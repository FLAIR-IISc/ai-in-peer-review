## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates the effect of stacking recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to create a hierarchical processing structure for time series data, termed a Deep RNN (DRNN). The core idea is to leverage the strengths of both deep neural networks (DNNs) – hierarchical feature extraction – and RNNs – temporal modeling. The authors hypothesize that this architecture will naturally capture the multi-scale temporal structure often present in time series, such as speech or text. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD) and provide an initial analysis of the emergent time scales within the DRNN.

The work builds upon a significant body of research in both DNNs and RNNs. The motivation is clearly situated within the context of limitations of standard RNNs in capturing hierarchical temporal dependencies, referencing prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and stacked LSTMs [8]. The paper appropriately acknowledges the existing literature and positions its contribution as a more in-depth analysis of the architectural properties of stacked RNNs, beyond simply achieving performance gains. 

**Strengths:** The paper is well-motivated and clearly articulates the potential benefits of the DRNN architecture. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods on a challenging task (character-level language modeling). The analysis of emergent time scales, while preliminary, is a valuable contribution, providing insight into how the DRNN learns to process information at different temporal resolutions. The inclusion of ablation studies (removing layer outputs) and generative examples further strengthens the analysis.

**Weaknesses:** The paper’s analysis, while insightful, feels somewhat limited. The experiments are primarily focused on a single task and dataset. Exploring the performance of DRNNs on other time series tasks (e.g., speech recognition, time series prediction) would broaden the scope and generalizability of the findings. The discussion of the training procedure could be more detailed; for example, the rationale behind the layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is not fully explained. While the authors acknowledge the computational cost, a more thorough discussion of scalability and potential optimizations would be beneficial. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization is mentioned but lacks detail on the practical differences and trade-offs.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Novel application of a stacked RNN architecture with a focus on emergent temporal hierarchies.
*   Strong experimental results demonstrating competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   Initial analysis of time scales provides valuable insights into the network’s behavior.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
*   Limited scope of experiments (single task and dataset).
*   Analysis of training procedure and scalability could be more thorough.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution to the field. The paper presents a well-motivated and empirically validated architecture for processing time series data. While the analysis could be expanded, the current work provides a valuable starting point for future research on deep recurrent neural networks. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
