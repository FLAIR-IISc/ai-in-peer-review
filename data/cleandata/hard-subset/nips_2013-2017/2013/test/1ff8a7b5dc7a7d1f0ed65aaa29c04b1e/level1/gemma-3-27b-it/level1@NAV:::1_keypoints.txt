- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) by stacking RNN layers to create hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Motivates DRNNs as addressing RNNs’ limited explicit modeling of temporal hierarchies in data like text and speech.  
- DRNN architecture: each layer is an RNN that takes the previous layer’s hidden state as input, creating multiple time scales.  
- Demonstrates competitive / state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using simple SGD training.  
- Provides initial analysis of emergent time scales within the network, showing some hierarchical temporal behavior.  
- Ablation studies (Figure 2) and parenthesis-completion experiments give insight into layer contributions and behavior.  
- Writing is clear, experiments are well-documented, and training details improve reproducibility.  
- Strength: achieves strong results without complex optimization (contrasted to Hessian-free methods).  
- Weakness: time-scale analysis is limited and the typo perturbation experiment is not fully revealing of dependencies.  
- Weakness: network sizes and configurations seem arbitrary; more exploration of layers/neurons is needed.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion and comparison to more recent sequence models (e.g., Transformers).  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with minor revisions to expand time-scale analysis and briefly discuss contemporary approaches.
