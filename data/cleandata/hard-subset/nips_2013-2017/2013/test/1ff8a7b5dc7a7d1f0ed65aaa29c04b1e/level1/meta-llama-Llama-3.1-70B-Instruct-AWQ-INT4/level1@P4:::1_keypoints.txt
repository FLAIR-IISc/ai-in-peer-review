- Paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, proposing hierarchical recurrent layers that take hidden states from the previous layer as input.  
- Claims traditional RNNs lack explicit temporal hierarchies and struggle to capture multiple time scales.  
- Positions work relative to prior hierarchical RNN research (e.g., [5], [8]) while claiming novel architecture contributions.  
- Provides analysis of DRNN hidden state evolution, output generation, and overall training setup.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent with a simple heuristic to prevent gradient explosions.  
- Evaluates on a Wikipedia text corpus and reports state-of-the-art results among recurrent networks for character-level modeling.  
- Demonstrates emergent time scales in DRNNs and improved ability to capture long-term dependencies.  
- Strength: introduces a novel architecture that addresses limitations of standard RNNs.  
- Strength: offers a thorough empirical and qualitative analysis of the architecture.  
- Strength: experimental results are well-presented and show clear performance gains.  
- Weakness: insufficient theoretical grounding and limited discussion linking to existing theory.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and training costs.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of DRNN applicability beyond the presented task and dataset.  
- Originality: presents a new DRNN design and insights into emergent temporal hierarchies.  
- Significance: contributes to discussions on training large-scale models and modeling temporal hierarchies in sequential data.  
- Quality: paper is well-written, organized, and demonstrates solid understanding of related work.  
- Clarity: explanations and experimental presentation are clear and easy to follow.  
- Pro-accept: novel approach, strong empirical performance, and valuable insights on time scales and long-term dependencies.  
- Con-accept: needs more theoretical analysis, complexity evaluation, and broader task validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; request deeper theoretical insight, complexity analysis, and broader application experiments.
