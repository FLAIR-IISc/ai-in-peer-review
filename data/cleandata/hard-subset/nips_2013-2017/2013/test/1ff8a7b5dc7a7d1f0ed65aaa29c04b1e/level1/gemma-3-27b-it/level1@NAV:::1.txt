## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates the effect of stacking recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to create a hierarchical processing structure for time series data, termed a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN). The authors motivate this approach by pointing out the limitations of standard RNNs in explicitly modeling the temporal hierarchies often present in real-world data like speech and text. They propose a DRNN architecture where each layer is an RNN receiving the hidden state of the previous layer as input, effectively creating multiple time scales. The work demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD) and provides an initial analysis of emergent time scales within the network.

The paper builds upon a rich history of work in deep learning and recurrent networks. It correctly positions itself within the landscape of deep neural networks, contrasting the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the memory-focused approach of standard RNNs. The authors acknowledge and cite relevant work on hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6, 8]), carefully differentiating their contribution through a focus on analysis alongside performance.  The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is valuable, demonstrating competitive results achieved with a simpler training method.

**Strengths:** The main strength lies in the successful demonstration of a DRNN achieving competitive results on a challenging task – character-level language modeling – without relying on complex optimization techniques. The analysis of emergent time scales, while preliminary, is a valuable contribution, providing insights into the internal dynamics of the architecture. The ablation studies (Figure 2) offer a clear understanding of layer contributions, and the experiments on parenthesis completion are insightful. The writing is clear and well-organized, and the inclusion of detailed training setup information enhances reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:** The analysis of time scales feels somewhat limited.  While the typo perturbation experiment is interesting, it doesn’t fully reveal the complete range of time dependencies captured by the network.  The discussion of limitations concerning nested parentheses and sentence boundaries is honest, but further exploration of these would be beneficial.  The network size chosen seems somewhat arbitrary; a more thorough exploration of different network configurations (number of layers, neurons per layer) would strengthen the conclusions.  The paper could benefit from a broader comparison to more recent advances in RNN architectures, such as Transformers, even if a direct comparison is not feasible.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Achieves competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   Provides an initial analysis of hierarchical time scale emergence in DRNNs.
*   Demonstrates state-of-the-art results using a simple training method.
*   Clear writing and detailed experimental setup.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited exploration of network configurations.
*   Analysis of time scales could be more comprehensive.
*   Lacks a discussion of more recent advances in sequence modeling (e.g., Transformers).



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution that demonstrates the potential of deep recurrent architectures. While not groundbreaking, the paper presents a clear and well-executed study with valuable insights. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, primarily focusing on expanding the time scale analysis and providing a brief discussion of related contemporary approaches.
