

Summary: This paper introduces a "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture, which stacks multiple RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue this architecture better captures temporal hierarchies in time series data than standard RNNs, which don't explicitly accommodate multiple time scales. They demonstrate DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia text corpus) using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), rather than complex Hessian-free optimization. They also provide analysis of time scales emerging across the layers. This builds on Graves et al. (2013) who used stacked bi-directional LSTMs, but provides deeper theoretical and empirical analysis of the architecture's temporal properties.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with clear theoretical analysis of the DRNN architecture and its hidden state evolution. The experimental design is strong, with careful comparison to standard RNNs and state-of-the-art results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of time scales across layers, including the insightful "typo-perturbation" experiment that demonstrates how different layers maintain different temporal contexts. They're honest about limitations, such as the need for layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, and provide appropriate ablation studies (e.g., removing layer outputs to measure importance). The paper presents a complete piece of work rather than a position paper.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with good use of figures to illustrate the architecture and experimental results. The DRNN concept is explained well through equations and diagrams. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility. The main weakness is inconsistent notation for DRNN variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO are not clearly defined until section 2.2), which could confuse readers. A clearer definition earlier in the paper would improve readability.

Originality: The DRNN architecture represents a novel combination of deep networks and RNNs, with the key insight that stacking RNNs automatically creates different time scales at different levels. While Graves et al. (2013) used stacked bi-directional LSTMs, this paper provides a more fundamental analysis of why this architecture works well for temporal tasks. The paper makes a clear distinction from previous work by explicitly analyzing time scales, which is not present in prior stacking approaches. The analysis of how layers process information at different time scales is particularly original.

Significance: Character-level language modeling is a challenging task with important applications in natural language processing. The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance with simple SGD (rather than complex Hessian-free optimization) is significant for practical applications. The analysis of time scales provides valuable theoretical insights into how recurrent networks process temporal information, addressing an important gap in understanding recurrent architectures. The paper's findings about DRNNs' ability to model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses over long sequences) suggest potential advantages over standard RNNs for tasks requiring extensive memory of past input.

Weaknesses: The paper could better articulate how DRNNs compare to other recent RNN architectures like LSTMs and GRUs, which have become standard for language modeling. While the authors acknowledge Graves et al. (2013), they don't sufficiently distinguish their contribution from this prior work. The comparison to the Hessian-free method in [19] is limited as the authors don't specify how much data their training algorithm went through in total, making a fair comparison difficult. The broader significance of DRNNs for other temporal tasks beyond language modeling is not thoroughly explored.

Overall, this is a strong contribution that makes a clear and valuable advance in understanding how deep architectures can be applied to recurrent networks. The analysis of temporal hierarchy is particularly insightful and addresses an important question in the field. While the paper could better distinguish its contribution from prior work and provide more comprehensive comparisons, the technical quality is high, the analysis is thorough, and the results are convincing.

Recommendation: Accept

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel architecture (DRNN) that combines deep networks with RNNs
- Clear theoretical analysis of temporal hierarchy and time scales
- State-of-the-art results on character-level language modeling using simple SGD
- Thorough analysis of layer contributions and time scales
- Well-written and technically sound paper

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited comparison to other recent RNN architectures (LSTMs, GRUs)
- Inconsistent notation for DRNN variants (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO)
- Broader significance beyond language modeling could be better articulated

This paper makes a significant contribution to understanding how deep architectures can be effectively applied to recurrent networks. The analysis of time scales emerging from the DRNN architecture is particularly valuable and addresses an important gap in the literature. The results are convincing, and the paper is well-written and technically sound. I strongly recommend acceptance.
