This paper proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture where multiple RNN layers are stacked hierarchically, with the hidden state of one layer serving as the input sequence for the next. The central hypothesis is that this structure allows the model to naturally learn a temporal hierarchy, with different layers operating on different time scales. This idea builds upon prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1995) and the use of stacked LSTMs (Graves et al., 2013). However, where previous work often focused on performance or artificial tasks, this paper's main contribution is a detailed analysis of the emergent properties of the DRNN on a challenging, real-world task: character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. The authors demonstrate that a 5-layer DRNN, trained with simple SGD, achieves state-of-the-art performance, comparable to models trained with more complex Hessian-free optimization.

The paper has several significant strengths.
*   **Quality & Significance:** The experimental results are compelling. Reaching state-of-the-art bits-per-character (BPC) with a standard SGD training regimen is a significant achievement, suggesting that architectural choices can be as important as sophisticated optimization algorithms.
*   **Clarity & Originality:** The paper's primary strength is its in-depth analysis of the model's internal dynamics. The authors go beyond simply reporting performance metrics and present a series of well-designed experiments to probe the learned representations. The "typo perturbation" and "context switch" experiments provide clear evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, and the analysis of parenthesis-closing demonstrates the model's capacity for learning long-range dependencies in a concrete, interpretable way. This analytical focus provides a novel and valuable contribution to the understanding of deep sequential models.

The paper also has minor weaknesses.
*   **Quality:** The experimental claims are based on a single training run for each of the three models. While the authors argue this is due to high computational cost and low variance in large models, the conclusions would be substantially stronger with results averaged over multiple initializations to ensure robustness.
*   **Clarity:** The layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O model, while effective, feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more detailed justification or ablation study on this training strategy would be beneficial.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a clear and well-supported contribution. The combination of state-of-the-art performance with a thorough and insightful analysis of the model's behavior makes it an excellent candidate for acceptance. The work not only demonstrates the effectiveness of DRNNs but also provides a valuable methodological template for analyzing and understanding deep learning models.
