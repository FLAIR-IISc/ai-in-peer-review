### **Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture for modeling time series with hierarchical temporal structures. The proposed DRNN is a stack of RNN layers where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input to the layer above it. The authors argue that this architecture is more suitable for hierarchical processing than a standard "shallow" RNN, which can be seen as deep only when unrolled in time. The paper evaluates DRNNs on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia dataset.

Two variants of the DRNN are explored: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The models are trained with a simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm with gradient normalization. Notably, the DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this benchmark, matching results previously obtained only with more complex models (Multiplicative RNNs) and sophisticated second-order optimization methods (Hessian-free).

The main contribution of the paper, however, extends beyond performance. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the trained models, demonstrating that a temporal hierarchy emerges naturally: higher layers in the network learn to operate on longer time scales than lower layers. This is shown through experiments measuring the decay of perturbations over time. Furthermore, the paper analyzes the model's ability to capture long-range dependencies, such as correctly closing parentheses over hundreds of characters, providing qualitative and quantitative evidence of the model's strengths. The work is well-situated in the context of prior research, acknowledging similar architectures used for speech recognition (e.g., Graves et al., 2013) but distinguishing itself by providing a much-needed in-depth analysis of the model's internal dynamics and capabilities.

### **Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results with Simple Methods:** The paper's primary result is very compelling. It demonstrates that a conceptually straightforward architecture (stacked RNNs) trained with a basic optimization algorithm (SGD with gradient clipping) can achieve state-of-the-art performance on a difficult, large-scale benchmark. This is significant because it challenges the prevailing notion at the time that complex second-order methods like Hessian-free optimization were necessary to train high-performing RNNs on such tasks.

2.  **Insightful Analysis of Model Dynamics:** The paper's greatest strength is its thorough analysis, which provides valuable insights into *why* deep RNNs are effective. The experiments to measure the emergent time scales in different layers (Figure 3) are well-designed and provide clear evidence for the temporal hierarchy hypothesis. The analysis of long-range dependencies through the parenthesis-closing task is also excellent, offering both a compelling qualitative example (Figure 4) and quantitative evaluation. This goes far beyond a simple "bake-off" of architectures and contributes to a deeper understanding of sequence modeling with neural networks.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clearly articulated, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup is detailed. The figures are informative and effectively support the paper's claims. The distinction between the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO architectures and their respective training schemes is explained well.

4.  **Significance and Impact:** This work provides strong evidence that architectural depth is a crucial ingredient for powerful sequence models, paralleling the concurrent findings in computer vision. By demonstrating the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, the paper provides a strong intuition for designing future recurrent architectures. The success of simple SGD also has practical implications, making these powerful models more accessible to researchers without access to massive computational resources or expertise in complex optimization. The paper is likely to inspire further research into both the architecture and analysis of deep recurrent models.

### **Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited Number of Experiments:** The main results are based on single training runs for each of the three architectures (RNN, DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO). While the authors justify this by the prohibitive training times (10+ days per model), it introduces uncertainty. Without multiple runs with different random initializations, it is difficult to assess the variance in performance and be fully confident that the reported differences are statistically significant and not an artifact of a fortunate initialization.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training for DRNN-1O:** The layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O model feels somewhat heuristic. While the authors provide a reasonable justification (ensuring lower layers receive a strong error signal during early stages of training), the method is not rigorously compared against a standard end-to-end training attempt for the same model. It would have been beneficial to show that end-to-end training of the DRNN-1O fails or performs significantly worse, which would more strongly motivate the proposed layer-wise scheme.

3.  **Interpretability of Layer Contributions:** The analysis of layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model by ablating a single layer's output (Figure 2) is interesting but, as the authors acknowledge, has limited interpretability due to potential complex interactions between layers. For instance, the lower contribution of intermediate layers to the final output does not necessarily mean they are less important; they could be performing crucial transformations for the higher layers. While a good first step, this analysis could be misinterpreted as a direct measure of layer importance.

### **Detailed Comments**

-   The comparison with Sutskever et al. (2011) is central to the paper's performance claims. It would be helpful to be more explicit about the differences in the datasets (95 vs. 86 characters). While the authors state in a footnote that the extra characters are rare and do not meaningfully affect the cross-entropy, this is a claim that would be stronger if supported by a brief analysis of character frequencies.
-   The analysis of parentheses is fascinating. The observation that the model "forgets" an open parenthesis after a full stop is a great example of learning complex, context-dependent heuristics. This suggests the model has learned something about sentence structure, not just a simple parenthesis-counting mechanism.
-   The discussion on future work is sound. Applying second-order methods to DRNNs would indeed be a valuable comparison to see if the architectural advantage and the optimization advantage are additive.

### **Conclusion**

This is an excellent and important paper. It presents a simple and effective architecture for a challenging problem, backs it up with state-of-the-art results, and—most crucially—provides a deep and insightful analysis that advances our understanding of recurrent neural networks. The weaknesses identified are minor relative to the paper's substantial contributions. The work is of high quality, clearly presented, and possesses significant originality and impact. I strongly recommend acceptance.
