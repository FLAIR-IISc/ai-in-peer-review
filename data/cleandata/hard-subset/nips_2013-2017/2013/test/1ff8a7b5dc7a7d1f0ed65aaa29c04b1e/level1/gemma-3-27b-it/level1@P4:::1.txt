This paper investigates the effect of a hierarchy of recurrent neural networks (RNNs) on processing time series data, specifically character-level language modeling. The authors propose a "deep recurrent neural network" (DRNN) where each layer is an RNN receiving the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture aims to naturally capture the temporal hierarchy inherent in time series. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), surpassing previous results obtained with more complex optimization methods like Hessian-free training. The paper also presents an analysis of emergent time scales within the DRNN layers.

The work builds upon the established success of deep neural networks (DNNs) and extends the concept to recurrent networks. Prior work in hierarchical RNNs, such as [5] and [8], is acknowledged. While [5] focuses on artificial tasks, and [8] primarily emphasizes performance gains without detailed architectural analysis, this paper delves into both performance and a characterization of the learned temporal dynamics. The DRNN architecture, combining DNN and RNN characteristics, has similarities to architectures explored in [8], but the emphasis on understanding the emergent time scales differentiates this work. The connection to existing literature is well-established.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The explicit investigation of hierarchical RNNs beyond simply achieving performance gains is a valuable contribution. Analyzing emergent time scales provides insight into the network's internal representation of temporal data.
*   **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results with a relatively simple training algorithm (SGD) is a significant achievement and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of the DRNN architecture and the motivations behind it are clear. Figures effectively illustrate the network structure.
*   **Analysis:** The investigation of time scales through perturbation experiments and analysis of parenthesis handling provides useful qualitative insights into the network's behavior. The inclusion of these analyses moves the paper beyond a purely empirical study.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While state-of-the-art performance is demonstrated on character-level language modeling, the paper lacks experiments on other time series tasks. This limits the generalizability of the findings. The discussion of potential applications to tasks requiring long-term memory (referencing [11]) feels somewhat cursory without actual experimentation.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The initialization of learning rates, especially for the top layer of DRNN-1O, is mentioned as being sensitive and requiring specific tuning. This suggests potential instability or difficulty in scaling the architecture, which isn't fully addressed.
*   **Output Connections:** The justification for using output connections at each layer, while presented (addressing pathological curvature and layer-wise training), feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more thorough investigation of the impact of different output connection strategies could be beneficial.
*   **Incomplete Comparison:** The comparison to other methods (e.g., [19], PAQ) is somewhat uneven, as performance metrics are not always directly comparable. It's difficult to fully assess the relative advantage of DRNNs without a standardized evaluation.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for hierarchical recurrent neural networks.
*   The experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task.
*   The analysis of emergent time scales provides valuable insights into the network's internal workings.
*   The clarity of writing and organization make the paper accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of experiments on diverse time series tasks limits the generalizability of the results.
*   The sensitivity to hyperparameter settings and the limited discussion of potential issues with scaling are concerns.
*   The comparison to existing methods could be more rigorous.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper is a solid contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The proposed DRNN architecture is novel, the experimental results are compelling, and the analysis provides valuable insights. While the limited scope of experiments and potential scaling issues are weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. I recommend acceptance.
