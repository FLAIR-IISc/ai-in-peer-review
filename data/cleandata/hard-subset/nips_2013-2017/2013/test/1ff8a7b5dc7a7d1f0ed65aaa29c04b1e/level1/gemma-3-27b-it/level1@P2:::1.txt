This paper investigates the effect of hierarchical recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on processing time series, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer, creating a hierarchy capable of capturing multi-scale temporal dependencies. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple SGD and provide an analysis of emergent time scales within the network.

This work builds upon a rich history of RNN research at NIPS. Papers like Hochreiter & Schmidhuber (1997) [11] introduced LSTM, addressing the vanishing gradient problem in RNNs. More recently, Sutskever et al. (2011) [19] showed strong results in language modeling with RNNs trained using Hessian-free optimization. The hierarchical aspect echoes earlier work by El Hihi & Bengio (2009) [5], although that work focused on artificial tasks, and Graves et al. (2013) [7] which used stacked bi-directional LSTMs. This paper aims to combine the depth of DNNs with the temporal processing capabilities of RNNs, and analyze the resulting structure.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The architecture is clearly defined, and the experimental setup is described in detail, making reproducibility plausible. The results demonstrate a significant improvement over a standard RNN and achieve comparable performance to more complex methods (MRNN with Hessian-free optimization) using a simpler training algorithm (SGD). The analysis of emergent time scales is a valuable contribution, providing insight into the internal dynamics of the DRNN. However, the analysis, while intriguing, could be more rigorous. The “typo” experiment, while illustrative, relies on Euclidean distance which may not be the most informative metric for measuring similarity in hidden state space. The ablation study removing layer contributions is reasonable, but deeper analysis of *why* certain layers contribute more would be beneficial.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly presented, and the concepts are explained in a straightforward manner. Figure 1 is helpful in visualizing the DRNN architecture. However, the notation is sometimes dense, and could benefit from more consistent use of subscripts and superscripts. The writing quality suffers slightly from some repetitive phrasing. A more concise and polished presentation would improve readability.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking RNNs isn't entirely novel (see [5], [8]), but this work provides a comprehensive investigation of this architecture, particularly its emergent time scales, and demonstrates strong performance with a simple training algorithm. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization is a strong point, highlighting the efficiency of the DRNN approach. The analysis of layer contributions and the experiments related to parenthesis completion add to the originality. 

**Significance:** The results are significant. Achieving state-of-the-art performance with SGD is a notable achievement. The insight into the hierarchical time scales is valuable for understanding how DRNNs process temporal data. This work could potentially influence future research in RNN architectures and training methods. The demonstrated ability to capture long-term dependencies, illustrated with the parenthesis example, is promising. This work provides a strong foundation for exploring more complex hierarchical RNNs and their applications to other sequential data tasks.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   State-of-the-art performance on a challenging task (character-level language modeling).
*   Demonstrates the effectiveness of a simple training algorithm (SGD) for a complex architecture.
*   Provides a novel analysis of emergent time scales in DRNNs.
*   Offers insights into the internal dynamics of hierarchical RNNs.
*   Well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The analysis of emergent time scales could be more rigorous.
*   The writing style could be more concise and polished.
*   The contribution, while significant, isn't radically groundbreaking - it builds on existing work in hierarchical RNNs.



**Overall Assessment:** I believe this paper is a strong contribution and should be accepted. The authors have presented a technically sound, clearly written, and insightful study of deep recurrent neural networks. The results are significant, and the analysis of emergent time scales is a valuable addition to the literature. While there are minor areas for improvement, the overall quality of the work justifies acceptance to NIPS.
