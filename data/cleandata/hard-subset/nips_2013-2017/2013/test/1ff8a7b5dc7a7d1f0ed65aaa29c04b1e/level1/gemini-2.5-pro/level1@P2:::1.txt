### Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes and investigates the use of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows for hierarchical processing of sequential data, enabling the model to learn representations at different time scales, which is a natural fit for data like text or speech. Each layer in the DRNN receives the hidden state sequence from the layer below it as input, creating a hierarchy of temporal representations.

The authors evaluate DRNNs on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They introduce two architectural variants: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O), and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). Using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient normalization, they achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this benchmark, matching results previously obtained only with a more complex model (multiplicative RNN) and a sophisticated second-order optimization method (Hessian-free).

A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to analyzing the trained models. The authors provide compelling experimental evidence that the DRNN layers learn a temporal hierarchy, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory and operating on longer time scales. This is demonstrated through experiments measuring the decay of perturbations (typos), the recovery time from a context switch, and a specific analysis of the model's ability to handle long-range dependencies like matching parentheses. This analytical work distinguishes the paper from prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1996) and more recent applications (e.g., Graves et al., 2013), which were often tested on simpler tasks or focused primarily on performance metrics rather than on understanding the model's internal mechanisms.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by a rigorous and comprehensive set of experiments on a large-scale, difficult benchmark. The achievement of state-of-the-art results is a strong piece of evidence for the model's effectiveness. More importantly, the paper does not stop at performance metrics; it includes a thorough analysis that provides qualitative and quantitative support for the central hypothesis that DRNNs learn a temporal hierarchy. The authors are careful in their experimental design and honest about limitations, such as the high computational cost of training and the model's inability to handle nested parentheses. The work is a complete and polished contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The motivation for deep recurrent architectures is articulated compellingly in the introduction. The model definitions are precise, and the training procedures are explained in sufficient detail. The figures and tables are informative and effectively communicate the results of the analysis (e.g., Figure 3, which visualizes the different time scales). The level of detail provided should be sufficient for an expert to reproduce the main results.

**Originality:** While the concept of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely novel (as acknowledged by the authors with citations to Graves et al. and earlier work), the paper's contribution is highly original in several respects. First, it demonstrates that this architecture can achieve state-of-the-art results on a very challenging task using a simple, standard training algorithm (SGD), which was an important finding at a time when more complex optimizers were thought necessary for training RNNs effectively. Second, and most importantly, the paper's primary originality lies in its deep, insightful analysis of the model's learned behavior. The systematic investigation into the emergent time scales in different layers provides a foundational understanding of *why* these models are effective, a contribution that goes far beyond a simple architectural proposal.

**Significance:** The paper is highly significant. It provides strong evidence that depth is as crucial for recurrent networks as it is for feedforward networks. The results helped establish stacked RNNs (and later, stacked LSTMs/GRUs) as a standard, powerful tool for sequence modeling tasks across the field. The finding that such models could be trained with simple SGD made them much more accessible to the broader research community. Furthermore, the analytical techniques and the core insight—that deep RNNs learn a hierarchy of time scales—have been influential, shaping how researchers understand, design, and analyze sequence models. This work is likely to be (and has been) widely cited and built upon by both researchers and practitioners.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  **State-of-the-Art Performance:** The paper achieves SOTA results on a difficult and established benchmark task for language modeling.
2.  **Simple, Effective Training:** It demonstrates that these excellent results can be obtained with a simple and widely-used optimizer (SGD), challenging the notion that complex second-order methods are required for high-performing RNNs.
3.  **Outstanding Analysis:** The core strength of the paper is its in-depth analysis, which provides compelling evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy in the DRNN. This provides significant scientific insight into the workings of deep sequence models.
4.  **High Clarity and Impact:** The paper is very clearly written and its findings have clear and broad implications for the field of sequence modeling. It provides both a powerful model and a deeper understanding of it.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
1.  **Incremental Architectural Novelty:** The core architectural idea of stacking RNN layers had been explored by others. However, this is a minor point, as the application, results, and particularly the analysis represent a major step forward.
2.  **High Computational Cost:** The experiments are computationally intensive, which limits the scope of architectural exploration (e.g., varying layer depths and widths). This is an understandable trade-off for working with a large-scale problem and is appropriately acknowledged by the authors.

**Conclusion**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning and sequence modeling. Its strengths—strong empirical results, exceptional analysis providing fundamental insights, and high clarity—far outweigh any minor weaknesses. The demonstration that deep recurrent networks can be trained with simple methods to achieve state-of-the-art performance, coupled with the clear evidence of a learned temporal hierarchy, makes this a foundational work. I strongly recommend acceptance.
