- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers hierarchically, with each layer's hidden state as the input sequence to the next, hypothesized to learn a temporal hierarchy.
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN and stacked LSTM work (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio 1995; Graves et al. 2013).
- Main contribution is a detailed analysis of emergent DRNN properties on a real-world task: character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus.
- Empirical result: a 5-layer DRNN trained with simple SGD achieves state-of-the-art bits-per-character, comparable to models trained with Hessian-free optimization.
- Strength — significance: shows architectural choices can match sophisticated optimization methods, reaching compelling performance with standard SGD.
- Strength — clarity/originality: provides in-depth, well-designed probes of internal dynamics beyond performance metrics.
- Strength — experiments: "typo perturbation" and "context switch" demonstrate emergent temporal hierarchy across layers.
- Strength — interpretability: parenthesis-closing analysis shows the model learns long-range dependencies in a concrete, interpretable way.
- Weakness — robustness: experimental claims are based on a single training run per model; results should be averaged over multiple initializations.
- Weakness — clarity: the DRNN-1O layer-wise training procedure appears ad-hoc and would benefit from further justification or ablation.
