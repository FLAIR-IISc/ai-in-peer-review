

This paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a temporal hierarchy for processing time series. The authors argue that standard RNNs implicitly handle temporal hierarchies through training dynamics, whereas DRNNs explicitly embed different time scales at different layers. They demonstrate DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks in character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD, with the DRNN-1O variant outperforming standard RNNs. The paper also provides analysis of emergent time scales across layers, including experiments on how each layer contributes to processing long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses).

The paper makes a significant contribution by formally analyzing temporal hierarchies in deep recurrent architectures, building on prior work such as [8] which used stacked LSTMs but focused on performance rather than architectural analysis. The experimental results are well-designed, showing DRNNs can capture multiple time scales with layer-specific memory properties, and the parentheses closing test provides compelling evidence of long-term dependency handling.

Strengths include strong technical quality with clear experimental validation, clear writing that effectively explains the architecture through equations and figures, and significant contribution to understanding how temporal hierarchies emerge in deep recurrent models. The paper also thoughtfully addresses limitations of standard RNNs for capturing multi-scale temporal structures.

Weaknesses include limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., transformers), and the architecture's novelty is somewhat constrained as similar stacked RNN approaches were explored in [8]. The paper could benefit from more thorough ablation studies on layer-specific contributions.

Pro: Novel analysis of temporal hierarchies, strong empirical results showing DRNNs can achieve SOTA performance with simple SGD, meaningful analysis of emergent time scales.

Con: Architecture is somewhat incremental given prior stacked RNN work, limited comparison to modern non-recurrent approaches.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding recurrent architectures, particularly regarding temporal hierarchy. While the architecture itself isn't entirely novel, the detailed analysis of time-scale emergence and performance gains with simple training methods is significant. The work advances the state of the art in understanding how to effectively model multi-scale temporal dependencies, which is important for real-world time series applications. I recommend acceptance.
