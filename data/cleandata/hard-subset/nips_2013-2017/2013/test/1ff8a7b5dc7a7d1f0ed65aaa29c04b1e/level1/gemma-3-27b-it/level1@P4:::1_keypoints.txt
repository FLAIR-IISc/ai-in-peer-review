- Proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) stacking RNN layers that receive the previous layer’s hidden state to capture temporal hierarchies in time series.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using simple SGD, outperforming methods that used more complex optimizers.  
- Analyzes emergent time scales in DRNN layers via perturbation experiments and parenthesis-handling behavior to interpret learned temporal dynamics.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work, differentiating itself by emphasizing both performance and characterization of temporal representations.  
- Strength: Novel investigation into hierarchical RNNs and explicit analysis of emergent time scales.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance achieved with a simple training algorithm (SGD).  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing with effective figures explaining the architecture.  
- Strength: Analysis moves the paper beyond purely empirical results by providing qualitative insights into network behavior.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to character-level language modeling, reducing generalizability to other time series tasks.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (notably learning rate initialization for top layers) suggests potential instability and scaling difficulties.  
- Weakness: Use of output connections at each layer is justified but feels ad-hoc and lacks thorough investigation of alternatives.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to other methods are uneven and not always directly comparable, complicating assessment of relative advantage.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, well-motivated architecture with strong results and insightful analysis.  
- Argument against acceptance: Limited task diversity, hyperparameter sensitivity, and insufficiently rigorous comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: Solid contribution with compelling strengths that outweigh weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
