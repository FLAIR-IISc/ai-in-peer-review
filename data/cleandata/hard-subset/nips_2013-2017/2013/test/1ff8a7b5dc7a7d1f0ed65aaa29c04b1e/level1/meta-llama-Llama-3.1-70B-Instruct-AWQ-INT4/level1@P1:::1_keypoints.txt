- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network receiving previous layer hidden states.  
- Designed to capture temporal hierarchies in time series, applied to character-level language modeling.  
- Presents analysis of emergent time scales within DRNNs.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus using simple SGD.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work but distinguishes itself by combining deep and recurrent network strengths.  
- Related to stacked bi-directional LSTMs used in prior speech recognition work.  
- Novel architecture is a primary strength.  
- Achieving strong empirical results with simple training is a notable strength.  
- Detailed emergent time-scale analysis is a valuable contribution.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Experiments are limited (only three network instantiations tested).  
- Lacks detailed comparisons to other architectures (e.g., LSTMs, Echo State Networks).  
- Training method (simple SGD) may be suboptimal; advanced methods like Hessian-free might improve results.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, SOTA results, and time-scale analysis.  
- Con-accept arguments: limited experiments, missing comparative baselines, and potentially weak training method.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported.  
- Clear presentation with sufficient detail for expert reproduction.  
- Paper is original and potentially significant for advancing NLP and RNN applications.
