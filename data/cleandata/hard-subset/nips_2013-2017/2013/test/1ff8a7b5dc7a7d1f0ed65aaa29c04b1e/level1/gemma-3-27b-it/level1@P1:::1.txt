This paper investigates the effect of a hierarchy of recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for processing time series data, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer, allowing for hierarchical processing and the capture of multiple time scales. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using stochastic gradient descent, outperforming previous recurrent network results that relied on more complex training algorithms like Hessian-free optimization. They also present an analysis of the emergent time scales within the network.

This work builds upon prior research in deep learning, particularly the success of DNNs in hierarchical feature extraction. While RNNs have been used for sequential data, the authors highlight that they don’t inherently provide the same hierarchical processing as DNNs. Previous attempts at hierarchical RNNs exist ([3, 5, 6]), but often focus on artificial tasks or lack detailed analysis. [8] used a stacked bidirectional LSTM, achieving good performance but with limited architectural analysis. The DRNN, as presented, combines the strengths of both DNNs and RNNs, and this work aims to analyze this combination. The paper references relevant work in language modeling [17, 19] and memory networks [11]. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The systematic exploration of stacking simple RNNs to create a deep recurrent architecture with inherent time-scale hierarchy is a significant contribution. While the idea of stacking RNNs isn't entirely new, this work provides a more focused investigation and analysis of the resulting dynamics.
* **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on a challenging character-level language modeling task using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD) is a strong empirical result. This suggests that the DRNN architecture itself contributes significantly to the performance gains.
* **Analysis:** The analysis of emergent time scales within the network layers provides valuable insights into the network's internal workings. The experiments with typo perturbations and parenthesis matching offer compelling evidence for the hierarchical nature of the learned representations.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the DRNN architecture and the experimental setup. The figures are helpful in visualizing the network structure and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison:** While the authors compare their results to existing work, the comparison is somewhat limited. It would be beneficial to include a more direct comparison with other deep learning architectures commonly used for language modeling (e.g., transformers).
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper briefly mentions the sensitivity of the learning rate, particularly for the top layer in the DRNN-1O configuration. A more thorough investigation of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance would strengthen the results.
* **Computational Cost:** The training process is computationally expensive, requiring 10 days for a single network. This limits the scalability and exploration of different architectures. While acknowledged, this is a significant practical limitation.
* **Interpretability:** While the time-scale analysis is insightful, further investigation into the learned representations within each layer would be beneficial. Visualizing the hidden states or analyzing the activation patterns could provide a deeper understanding of the network's behavior.
* **Output Connections:** The rationale for DRNN-AO using output connections from *all* layers, specifically citing pathological curvature and training efficiency, feels somewhat hand-wavy. A more rigorous justification or ablation study showing the specific benefit of each layer's output connection would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to deep recurrent neural networks.
* The empirical results are strong, achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task.
* The analysis of emergent time scales provides valuable insights into the network's internal dynamics.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The comparison to other state-of-the-art architectures is limited.
* The computational cost of training is high.
* The interpretability of the learned representations could be improved.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach for processing sequential data, and the analysis of emergent time scales provides important insights into the network's behavior. While there are some weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.

**Rating:** 7/10 (Strong Accept)
