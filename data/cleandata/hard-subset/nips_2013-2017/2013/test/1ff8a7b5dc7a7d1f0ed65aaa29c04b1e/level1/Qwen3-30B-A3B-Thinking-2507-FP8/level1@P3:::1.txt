

# Peer Review

This paper introduces "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" (DRNNs), a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this structure better captures temporal hierarchies in time series data compared to standard RNNs, which implicitly embed temporal structure rather than explicitly supporting multiple time scales. The paper demonstrates DRNNs' effectiveness on character-level language modeling, achieving state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on a Wikipedia text corpus using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), whereas previous state-of-the-art required more advanced Hessian-free optimization.

The paper builds upon several strands of prior work: it extends the stacked bi-directional LSTM approach of [8] with deeper analysis of the architecture, relates to hierarchical RNNs for time-scale decomposition [5], and connects to the broader deep learning literature on hierarchical processing [4, 17]. The authors' key contribution is demonstrating that DRNNs naturally develop different time scales at different layers, which they analyze through perturbation experiments and a parentheses-closing task.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper makes a compelling case for temporal hierarchy in RNNs, which is a significant conceptual contribution. The analysis of time scales through perturbation experiments (Figure 3) provides valuable insights into how DRNNs process information across different time scales.

2. The experimental results are well-designed and convincing. The DRNN-1O achieves state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on the language modeling task with SGD alone, which is notable given that previous work required more sophisticated training methods.

3. The parentheses-closing experiment (Figure 4) is particularly insightful, demonstrating that DRNNs can maintain long-term dependencies beyond the training sequence length, suggesting they learn to model parentheses as "pseudo-stable attractor-like states" rather than memorizing specific phrases.

4. The paper clearly distinguishes between DRNN-1O (output only at top layer) and DRNN-AO (output at all layers), and the analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) provides valuable mechanistic insights into the architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper lacks strong theoretical justification for why DRNNs should work better than standard RNNs. While the authors observe that DRNNs develop different time scales, they don't provide a formal analysis of how this architecture improves learning dynamics or generalization.

2. The comparison to previous work is somewhat superficial. The authors cite [8] as using stacked bi-directional LSTMs, but don't adequately distinguish their DRNN architecture from the layered RNNs in [5] or [3]. The paper's claim that DRNNs "embed different timescales directly in their structure" needs stronger theoretical grounding to differentiate from previous hierarchical approaches.

3. The experimental scope is limited. The paper only tests on character-level language modeling with a single corpus. To establish broader significance, the authors should have tested DRNNs on other temporal tasks (e.g., speech recognition, time series forecasting) to demonstrate wider applicability.

4. The training method for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training) is not well-justified. The paper notes this was "necessary to reach good performance," but doesn't explain why standard BPTT fails for this architecture. This raises questions about the scalability and robustness of the approach.

5. The performance gains are marginal (DRNN-1O achieves 1.50 BPC vs. 1.51 for Hessian-free RNNs [19]), yet the DRNN architecture is more complex. The paper doesn't sufficiently address the computational cost of the additional layers or the trade-off between performance and complexity.

**Critical Assessment:**

The paper makes an interesting contribution to understanding how RNNs process information across time scales, and the experimental evidence for temporal hierarchy in DRNNs is compelling. However, the work is largely empirical without sufficient theoretical foundation, and the performance gains are modest compared to the added architectural complexity. The paper's strongest contribution is the analysis of time scales, which provides valuable insights into RNN dynamics.

The claim that DRNNs "are well-suited to capture temporal hierarchies" is well-supported by the experiments, but the paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that this is a general advantage over standard RNNs. For instance, the paper states that DRNNs "can have extensive memory of several hundred characters," but the Hessian-free RNNs in [19] likely have comparable memory capacity.

The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to related work on hierarchical RNNs, particularly the time-scale decomposition approach in [5] which also uses multiple time scales. The authors should clarify how their approach differs fundamentally from existing work.

**Recommendation:**

The paper presents an interesting architectural innovation with compelling experimental results on temporal hierarchy. However, the marginal performance gains and lack of theoretical grounding limit its significance. The work would be stronger with a more comprehensive theoretical analysis, broader experimental validation across tasks, and clearer differentiation from prior hierarchical RNN work.

**Pro-Acceptance Arguments:**
- Novel analysis of time scales in RNNs
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for RNNs with simple SGD
- Provides valuable insights into RNN dynamics through perturbation experiments
- Clear and well-written paper with good experimental design

**Con-Acceptance Arguments:**
- Limited theoretical justification for the architecture
- Marginal performance gains compared to added complexity
- Superficial comparison to related hierarchical RNN work
- Narrow experimental scope (only character-level language modeling)
- Unjustified layer-wise training approach for DRNN-1O

Given the paper's strong experimental analysis of time scales and the significance of understanding RNN dynamics, I recommend acceptance. However, the authors should address the theoretical gaps and broaden their experimental scope in future work to strengthen the contribution. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding how RNNs process information across time scales, which is valuable for both theory and practice.
