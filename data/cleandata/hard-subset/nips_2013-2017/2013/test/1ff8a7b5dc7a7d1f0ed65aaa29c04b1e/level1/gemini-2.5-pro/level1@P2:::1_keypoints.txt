- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) formed by stacking multiple RNN layers to learn hierarchical temporal representations.  
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  
- Introduces two variants: DRNN-1O (output from top layer) and DRNN-AO (all layers contribute to output).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art recurrent network performance using plain SGD with gradient normalization.  
- Matches prior best results obtained with multiplicative RNNs and second-order optimization (Hessian-free).  
- Provides extensive analysis showing higher layers capture longer time scales and exhibit longer memory.  
- Analysis includes perturbation decay (typos), context-switch recovery, and tests on long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses).  
- Honest about limitations: high computational training cost and inability to handle nested parentheses.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, experiments are comprehensive, and claims are well-supported.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, precise model/training descriptions, and informative figures facilitating reproducibility.  
- Originality: stacking RNNs isn’t entirely new, but novelty lies in demonstrating SGD-trained stacked RNNs achieve SOTA and in-depth analysis of emergent time scales.  
- Significant impact: establishes depth as important for RNNs, influenced adoption of stacked recurrent architectures, and made training more accessible.  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA results, simple effective training, outstanding analysis, high clarity and broad impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental architectural novelty and high computational cost limiting broader exploration.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, polished contribution offering empirical and conceptual advances; strong recommendation for acceptance.
