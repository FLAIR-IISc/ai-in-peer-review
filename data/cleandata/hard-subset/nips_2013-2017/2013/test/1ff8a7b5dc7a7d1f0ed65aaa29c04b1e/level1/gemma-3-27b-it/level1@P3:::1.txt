This paper investigates the effect of a hierarchical architecture of recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on processing time series data, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, enabling hierarchical processing and capturing multi-scale temporal dependencies. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and provide an analysis of emergent time scales within the network.

The work builds upon a significant body of research in recurrent neural networks and deep learning. The motivation, rooted in the success of deep neural networks for hierarchical feature extraction, is well-articulated. The authors correctly point out that standard RNNs don't explicitly incorporate hierarchical processing, primarily focusing on memory capacity. They acknowledge related work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6, 8] but highlight the novelty of their analysis and strong performance on a real-world task without relying on advanced training algorithms like Hessian-free optimization [19]. The connection to DNNs and the analogy to processing pipelines is a helpful framing device. The authors appropriately cite key papers in the field, including those on LSTMs [11] and Hessian-free optimization.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Significance:** The DRNN architecture, while conceptually straightforward, is a valuable contribution. It provides a concrete way to integrate hierarchical processing into RNNs, addressing a noted limitation. Achieving state-of-the-art performance with simple SGD is significant, suggesting the architecture itself is powerful and less reliant on complex optimization schemes.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply presenting results. The analysis of emergent time scales (Figure 3) is a major strength, offering insight into how the hierarchical structure naturally encodes different temporal dependencies. The tests involving perturbations and parentheses (Figures 2 & 4) provide qualitative evidence supporting the theoretical claims.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly laid out, the architecture is explained with a helpful diagram (Figure 1), and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail.
* **Complete Experimentation:** The authors conducted a reasonable set of experiments, including comparisons to existing RNNs and ablation studies to evaluate the contribution of individual layers. The exploration of DRNN-1O vs. DRNN-AO is insightful.
* **Reproducibility:** The level of detail provided, coupled with the mention of Theano [1] as the implementation framework, increases the potential for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Exploration of Architectures:** While the authors tested a 5-layer DRNN, they do not systematically explore the impact of varying the number of layers or the number of neurons per layer. A more comprehensive architectural search could reveal optimal configurations.
* **Dataset Specificity:** The results are demonstrated solely on a character-level language modeling task. It would be beneficial to evaluate the DRNNs on other time series datasets with different characteristics (e.g., speech recognition, financial time series) to assess their generalizability.
* **Comparison to LSTMs/GRUs:**  The primary comparison is to a standard RNN.  A direct comparison to more sophisticated recurrent architectures like LSTMs or GRUs, *with the same number of parameters*, would strengthen the claims about the advantages of the hierarchical approach. While the authors mention LSTMs in the introduction, they aren’t benchmarked against directly.
* **Output Connection Strategies:** The justification for using output connections at each layer (DRNN-AO) is partially related to mitigating vanishing gradients. However, more modern techniques like gradient clipping and careful initialization are now widely used to address this issue. A more rigorous analysis of the impact of DRNN-AO vs. DRNN-1O on gradient flow would be valuable.
* **Training Details:** While sufficient, certain training details, such as the precise method for choosing the initial learning rate and the rationale behind the incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, could be expanded.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The DRNN architecture is a novel and potentially impactful contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks.
* The paper demonstrates strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task.
* The analysis of emergent time scales provides valuable insights into the behavior of the network.
* The writing is clear and the presentation is well-organized.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The lack of systematic architectural exploration and comparisons to more modern RNN variants (LSTMs/GRUs) limit the robustness of the conclusions.
* The results are currently limited to a single dataset, raising questions about generalizability.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS 2013. While there are areas for improvement, the novelty of the architecture, the strong experimental results, and the insightful analysis of emergent time scales make it a significant contribution to the field. The authors have identified a compelling approach to address a known limitation of standard RNNs, and the results are promising. Addressing the weaknesses highlighted above in future work would further strengthen the impact of this research. The paper is well-written and presents a clear and convincing argument for the benefits of deep recurrent neural networks.
