- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack RNN layers to model hierarchical temporal structures and multiple time scales.
- Shows state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus using SGD.
- Clearly defines the DRNN architecture and training methodology, including incremental layer-wise training (DRNN-1O) and gradient normalization.
- Provides empirical analysis of emergent time scales and layer contributions, demonstrating modeling of long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses).
- Claims novelty by explicitly addressing temporal hierarchy and offering a more comprehensive analysis than prior hierarchical RNN work.
- Argues significance for broader temporal reasoning tasks such as speech recognition and text generation.
- Evaluation scope is limited to character-level language modeling and should be expanded to tasks like speech or video to show generalizability.
- Lacks detailed quantitative comparison to prior hierarchical RNN architectures in terms of performance and computational efficiency.
- Training complexity: DRNN-1O’s incremental layer-wise training adds complexity relative to end-to-end methods and is under-discussed.
- Interpretability is somewhat qualitative; the paper needs quantitative metrics or visualizations (e.g., attention maps) to clarify hierarchical processing.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, but request broader evaluations and more thorough comparisons in the final version.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, solid theoretical/practical contributions, strong empirical results, and insightful time-scale analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow evaluation, added training complexity, and insufficient quantitative comparison to related work.
