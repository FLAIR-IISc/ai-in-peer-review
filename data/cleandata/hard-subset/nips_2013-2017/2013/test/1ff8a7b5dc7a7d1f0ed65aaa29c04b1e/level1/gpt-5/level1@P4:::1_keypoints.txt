- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) with two output variants: top-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO), trained with SGD + gradient normalization and incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O.  
- Evaluation is character-level language modeling (bits-per-character) on a large Wikipedia corpus at ~5M parameters.  
- Main contributions: simple stacked RNNs trained with vanilla SGD can match prior recurrent SOTA at similar parameter counts, and analyses indicate emergent layer-wise temporal hierarchies.  
- Strength: clear, simple architecture with reproducible training details.  
- Strength: competitive BPC comparable to multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free methods.  
- Strength: insightful analyses (layer ablation, perturbation/typo tests, context-switch experiments, parentheses closure probe) showing different layer roles and time scales.  
- Strength: AO variant both aids training lower layers and provides a tool to probe layer contributions.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—stacked RNNs and multi-timescale behavior are known; architectural novelty is modest.  
- Weakness: limited experimental breadth—only three model instantiations, single dataset, no LSTM or n-gram/KN baselines; vocabulary mismatch limits comparability.  
- Weakness: lack of statistical rigor—no reporting of variance across random seeds and few ablations on depth/width/training schedules at fixed parameter counts.  
- Weakness: differing training protocols across variants (e.g., incremental pretraining for DRNN-1O) may confound architecture comparisons.  
- Methodological choices need more justification (gradient normalization vs clipping, zero initialization of output layers, sensitivity to BPTT length).  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully designed analyses, but largely qualitative; quantitative summaries (decay constants with CIs) would strengthen claims.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with explicit model/training details and interpretable figures; some references incomplete and more training throughput/epoch reporting needed.  
- Originality: primarily empirical/analytical novelty—combines simple stacked tanh RNN, AO head, and focused temporal analyses showing SGD competitiveness.  
- Significance: practical value in showing SGD+gradient normalization suffices at scale and in revealing temporal hierarchies, but impact limited by missing baselines and incremental architecture.  
- Suggestions: add stacked LSTM/single-layer LSTM baselines at similar budgets, report seed variability, sweep depth (2–6 layers) at fixed params, standardize dataset/vocabulary, quantify time scales (fit decays + stats), and aggregate parentheses probe results by span/nesting.  
- Key question: explain why DRNN-AO slightly underperforms DRNN-1O—training schedule vs representational trade-offs?  
- Acceptance arguments: solid empirical performance and useful, careful analyses revealing emergent temporal hierarchy.  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty, missing strong baselines, and insufficient robustness checks.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — valuable empirical and analytical contributions but would benefit from stronger baselines and broader validation.
