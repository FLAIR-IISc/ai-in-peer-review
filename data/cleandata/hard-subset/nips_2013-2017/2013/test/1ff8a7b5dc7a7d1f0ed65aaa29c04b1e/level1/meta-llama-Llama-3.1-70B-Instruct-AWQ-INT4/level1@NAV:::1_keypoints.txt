- Introduces a novel hierarchical DRNN where each layer is an RNN that receives the previous layer’s hidden state, enabling hierarchical time-series processing.
- Claims the architecture naturally captures multiple time scales in sequential data.
- Provides a thorough analysis of architecture, training setup, and performance evaluation.
- Evaluates on character-level language modeling and reports state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia corpus.
- Outperforms a standard RNN and a multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization.
- Strength: well-motivated, novel architecture addressing limitations of common RNNs.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation, including emergent time-scale analysis.
- Strength: multiple experiments support the architecture’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: DRNN-1O uses incremental, layer-wise training that may be inefficient.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art architectures like LSTM.
- Weakness: some experiments (e.g., emergent time-scale analysis) are under-explained and under-motivated.
- Pro acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical results, and thorough analysis.
- Con acceptance: inefficient training method, missing comparisons to SOTA, and insufficient explanation in parts.
- Overall: well-written paper with impressive results and valuable contribution, but needs better comparisons and clearer experimental explanations.
