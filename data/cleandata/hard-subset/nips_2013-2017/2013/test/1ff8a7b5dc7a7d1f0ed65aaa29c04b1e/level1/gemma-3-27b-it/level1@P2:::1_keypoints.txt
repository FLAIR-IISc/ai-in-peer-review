- Studies hierarchical (stacked) RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling to capture multi-scale temporal dependencies.  
- Architecture stacks layers where each layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state; analyzes emergent time scales.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on a Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD and demonstrates reproducible experimental setup.  
- Builds on prior RNN work (LSTM, Hessian-free training, earlier hierarchical RNNs) and connects depth of DNNs with temporal processing of RNNs.  
- Architecture and experiments are technically sound and clearly defined, enabling reproducibility.  
- Significant improvement over a standard RNN and comparable performance to MRNN with Hessian-free optimization despite using SGD.  
- Analysis of emergent time scales is a valuable contribution but lacks sufficient rigor.  
- The “typo” experiment relies on Euclidean distance, which may be a suboptimal metric for hidden-state similarity.  
- Ablation study removing layer contributions is reasonable but needs deeper analysis of why certain layers matter more.  
- Writing is generally clear and organized; Figure 1 helps, but notation is sometimes dense and phrasing repetitive.  
- Stacking RNNs is not entirely novel, but the comprehensive investigation and emergent-timescale analysis add originality.  
- Comparison to Hessian-free methods highlights the efficiency and practicality of DRNNs trained with SGD.  
- Results are significant and potentially influential, demonstrating ability to capture long-term dependencies (e.g., parenthesis example).  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA results, simple effective training, novel time-scale analysis, insights into internal dynamics, generally well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for more rigorous analysis, writing polish required, and the contribution is incremental relative to prior hierarchical RNN work.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution—accept.
