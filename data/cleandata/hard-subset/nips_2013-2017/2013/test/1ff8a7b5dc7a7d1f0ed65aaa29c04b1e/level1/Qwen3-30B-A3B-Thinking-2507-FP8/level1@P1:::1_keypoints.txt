- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers so each layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state, creating a natural temporal hierarchy.
- Demonstrates DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results on character-level language modeling using simple SGD rather than Hessian-free optimization.
- Provides in-depth analysis showing different layers operate at different temporal resolutions via perturbation experiments (typos, context switching).
- Experimental design is technically sound, reproducible, and evaluated on a standard Wikipedia corpus with thorough comparisons.
- Perturbation/analysis experiments effectively demonstrate that temporal hierarchy is a learned property, not an architectural artifact.
- Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., layer-wise training for DRNN-1O) and present clear empirical evidence supporting claims.
- DRNN architecture is an original combination emphasizing temporal-processing implications of stacked RNNs distinct from prior hierarchical RNN work.
- Core insight—that stacking RNNs yields multiple emergent time scales—is a meaningful conceptual advance.
- Achieving strong performance with simpler training (SGD) is practically significant and may influence future RNN research.
- The parentheses-closing task gives concrete evidence of DRNNs’ ability to model long-term dependencies.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful figures (Fig.1, Fig.3) and reproducible experimental details.
- Explanation of DRNN-1O’s layer-wise training approach is confusing and insufficiently motivated.
- The counterintuitive performance gap (DRNN-1O slightly outperforming DRNN-AO) is not adequately explained.
- Paper lacks comparison with recent NIPS work on Hessian-free training of RNNs, weakening originality/contextualization claims.
- Computational cost and scalability of DRNNs are under-discussed despite long training times being mentioned.
- More detail is needed on how DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O differ in training dynamics and why incremental layer-wise training was required for DRNN-1O.
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to clarify DRNN-1O training, explain performance differences, and better situate the work against recent NIPS publications and computational-cost considerations.
