This paper introduces a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture that combines the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks. The authors propose a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent neural network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This allows for hierarchical processing of time series data and naturally captures the structure of time series.

The paper presents a thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture, including its training setup, performance on character-level language modeling, and an examination of the emergent time scales in the network. The results show that the DRNN achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia text corpus, outperforming a common RNN and a multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The introduction of a novel and well-motivated architecture that addresses the limitations of common RNNs.
* A thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture, including its training setup and performance on a challenging task.
* The use of multiple experiments to evaluate the performance of the DRNN, including an examination of the emergent time scales in the network.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The training method used for the DRNN-1O is incremental and layer-wise, which may not be the most efficient way to train the network.
* The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art architectures, such as long short-term memory (LSTM) networks.
* Some of the experiments, such as the analysis of the emergent time scales, could be more thoroughly explained and motivated.

Arguments pro acceptance:

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture. The results are impressive, and the paper provides a good contribution to the field of deep learning.
