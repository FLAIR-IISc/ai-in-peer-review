The paper "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" explores the concept of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) and their application to character-level language modeling. The authors argue that traditional recurrent neural networks (RNNs) do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies, and propose a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input.

The authors relate their work to previous research on hierarchical architectures employing RNNs, such as [5] and [8]. They also discuss the limitations of traditional RNNs, including their inability to capture multiple time scales and their reliance on implicit network dynamics to represent temporal hierarchies.

The paper presents an analysis of the DRNN architecture, including its hidden state evolution, output generation, and training setup. The authors use stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with a simple heuristic to prevent gradient explosions and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a Wikipedia-based text corpus.

The experimental results show that the DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on the character-level language modeling task, outperforming traditional RNNs and other architectures. The authors also analyze the emergent time scales in the DRNNs and demonstrate their ability to capture long-term dependencies.
