- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) by stacking RNN layers so each layer’s hidden states feed the next, to enable hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Motivates depth in recurrence to capture multi-scale temporal structure absent in standard "shallow" per-time-step RNNs.  
- Evaluates two variants: DRNN-1O (output from top layer) and DRNN-AO (all layers contribute to output).  
- Trained on large Wikipedia character-level language modeling; achieves state-of-the-art performance comparable to multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization.  
- Uses only standard SGD with gradient normalization, showing architecture can rival complex optimizers.  
- Extensive analysis shows emergent temporal hierarchy: higher layers have longer memory and slower dynamics.  
- Ablation studies and text-generation analyses demonstrate different layers capture structure at different scales (sub-word vs. long-term).  
- Demonstrates ability to model very long-range dependencies (e.g., correctly closing parentheses hundreds of characters later).  
- Technical quality high: simple, well-defined architecture and rigorous large-scale experimental setup with well-supported claims.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with helpful figures and sufficient methodological detail.  
- Originality: stacking RNNs is incremental but the framing of depth as a mechanism for temporal hierarchy and the detailed analysis are novel and insightful.  
- Significance: high — argues for architectural depth in sequence models and made deep recurrent architectures more accessible and influential.  
- Minor weaknesses: layer-wise training for DRNN-1O feels ad-hoc and would benefit from more justification or ablation.  
- Other limitations: reliance on gradient normalization trick, testing only a single run per architecture, and validation confined to one primary task.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — benefits (performance, analysis, clarity, impact) outweigh minor weaknesses.
