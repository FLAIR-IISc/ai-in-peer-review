This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers, where each layer receives the sequence of hidden states from the layer below it as input. The central hypothesis is that this hierarchical structure allows the model to naturally capture the temporal hierarchies present in sequential data by developing different time scales at different layers. The authors evaluate this architecture on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia dataset.

This work relates to several research threads. The idea of hierarchical RNNs has been explored previously, for instance by El Hihi & Bengio (1996), who used subsampling to create different time scales. More recently and concurrently, Graves et al. (2013) used a similar stacked LSTM architecture to achieve state-of-the-art results in speech recognition. However, that work focused primarily on performance. The key contribution of the present paper is a detailed analysis of the DRNN's properties, and a direct comparison on a task previously tackled by Sutskever et al. (2011) with a more complex multiplicative RNN (MRNN) trained with the Hessian-free (HF) second-order optimization method. This paper demonstrates that a simpler DRNN architecture trained with standard Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) can achieve comparable state-of-the-art performance.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Significance of Results:** The paper's primary strength is its demonstration that a conceptually simple architectural modification—stacking RNNs—can match the performance of more complex models (MRNN) and sophisticated training algorithms (Hessian-Free optimization). Achieving a BPC of 1.479 with a DRNN trained via SGD, compared to the 1.48 BPC of an MRNN trained with HF, is a significant result. It suggests that architectural design can be as, or more, important than the choice of a complex optimizer for tackling difficult sequential modeling problems. This finding provides a valuable data point in the ongoing discussion about the trade-offs between model complexity, architectural depth, and optimization methods.

2.  **Quality of Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply reporting performance metrics. It presents a thorough and insightful analysis of the trained models, providing strong evidence for its central claims.
    *   The experiments on emergent time scales (Figures 2 and 3) are particularly compelling. By measuring the decay of a perturbation (a typo), the authors clearly show that higher layers in the hierarchy learn to operate on longer time scales, thus validating the hypothesis that the architecture facilitates a temporal hierarchy.
    *   The analysis of layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model provides a window into the model's internal function, suggesting a division of labor where lower and upper layers handle direct output generation while intermediate layers focus on feature transformation for higher levels.
    *   The case study on closing parentheses is an excellent, concrete demonstration of the model's capacity for learning long-range dependencies over hundreds of time steps, showing that it has learned a generalizable rule rather than simply memorizing sequences.

3.  **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow. The DRNN architecture is explained clearly with both equations and a diagram. The training setup and experimental design are described with sufficient detail to understand the work's context. The analysis sections are structured logically, making the results and their interpretation highly accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Originality of the Architecture:** The core architectural idea of stacking RNNs is not entirely novel. The authors are transparent about this, citing prior work like El Hihi & Bengio (1996) and the concurrent work of Graves et al. (2013). While the analysis is novel and constitutes the paper's main contribution, the architectural concept itself is an incremental, though effective, extension of existing ideas.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training Procedure:** The best-performing model, DRNN-1O, required a specific incremental layer-wise training procedure to achieve good performance. The authors state this was "necessary," which implies that end-to-end training of the deep model from a random initialization was unsuccessful. This reliance on a specialized, somewhat heuristic pre-training scheme points to potential optimization difficulties (e.g., vanishing/exploding gradients through depth) that are not fully analyzed. A deeper investigation into why this procedure is necessary and how it compares to other initialization or training strategies would have strengthened the paper's technical contribution.

3.  **Limited Scope of Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments, while thorough, are focused on a single, albeit challenging, task. The authors briefly dismiss testing on synthetic long-memory tasks (e.g., from Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997), arguing they lack hierarchical structure. However, empirically verifying whether DRNNs perform comparably to or worse than standard RNNs or LSTMs on such tasks could provide a more complete understanding of the trade-offs introduced by the deep architecture. As it stands, the claims about the model's general applicability are supported by a single, strong data point.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark using a relatively simple model and first-order optimization.
*   Provides a novel and highly insightful analysis of how deep recurrent architectures learn temporal hierarchies.
*   The work is well-executed, clearly presented, and makes a significant contribution to the understanding of deep models for sequential data.
*   The findings are likely to influence future research in RNN architectures.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   The fundamental architecture is not completely novel.
*   The best-performing model relies on a specific, heuristic training procedure, hinting at unresolved optimization issues.
*   The empirical validation is confined to one task domain.

In summary, despite the architectural novelty being incremental, the paper's main contribution lies in its rigorous application and deep analysis. It provides strong evidence that stacking RNNs is an effective method for learning temporal hierarchies and achieving state-of-the-art performance. The quality of the analysis and the significance of the findings strongly support its acceptance.
