- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack RNN layers, using previous-layer hidden states as inputs.  
- Designed to capture hierarchical structures and multiple time scales in time series data.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level Wikipedia language modeling.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivates the architecture.  
- Provides thorough empirical analysis, including layer-removal ablations and tests of long-term dependency capture.  
- Results show DRNNs learn a hierarchy of time scales and outperform traditional RNNs on the task.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated architecture for time series processing.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis of model behavior and performance.  
- Strength: achieves strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark.  
- Weakness: DRNN-1O training method is complex and may be hard to replicate.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other deep learning architectures.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient discussion of potential applications and limitations.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, strong results, thorough analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: replication difficulty, limited baselines, and insufficient discussion of applicability/limitations.  
- Overall: strong contribution with minor revisions recommended to address weaknesses.
