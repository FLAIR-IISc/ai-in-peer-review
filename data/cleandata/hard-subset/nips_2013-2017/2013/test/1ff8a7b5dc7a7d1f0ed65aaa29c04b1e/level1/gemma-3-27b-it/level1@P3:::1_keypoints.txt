- Proposes Deep RNN (DRNN) stacking hidden states to enable hierarchical, multi-scale temporal processing in time series.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using simple SGD.  
- Provides analysis of emergent time scales showing layers encode different temporal dependencies.  
- Uses perturbation and parentheses tests to qualitatively support theoretical claims.  
- Well-motivated connection to deep feedforward hierarchies and existing RNN literature.  
- Clear presentation, helpful diagram, and sufficient experimental detail for reproducibility (Theano implementation noted).  
- Thorough experimentation including ablations and comparisons between DRNN variants (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO).  
- Novelty/significance: simple architecture yields strong performance without complex optimizers.  
- Limited architectural exploration: no systematic study of layer counts or layer sizes.  
- Results shown only on character-level language modeling, limiting demonstrated generalizability.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to LSTMs/GRUs with matched parameter counts.  
- Output-at-each-layer (DRNN-AO) justification tied to vanishing gradients but lacks rigorous gradient-flow analysis versus modern techniques.  
- Some training details (initial learning rate selection, layer-wise training rationale) are under-specified.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — promising, well-written contribution with impactful results, though further comparisons and broader evaluations are needed.
