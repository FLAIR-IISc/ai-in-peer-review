

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" (DRNNs), a novel architecture that combines deep neural networks with recurrent structures by stacking RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors claim that DRNNs naturally embed temporal hierarchies across layers, which would enable better modeling of long-term dependencies in sequential data. They evaluate their approach on character-level language modeling using the Wikipedia text corpus, claiming state-of-the-art results with only SGD training (unlike previous work requiring Hessian-free optimization).

The paper builds upon prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6, 8], but the authors' specific contribution appears to be a particular stacking implementation rather than a fundamentally new architecture. The DRNN-1O variant (with output connections only at the top layer) achieves a test BPC of 1.526, which is comparable to the Hessian-free optimized MRNN (1.516) from [19], but the difference is marginal and within the range of typical experimental variability. The paper's claim that DRNNs "embed different time scales directly in their structure" is supported by some analysis of layer-wise time scales, but this evidence is limited to one specific task (character-level language modeling) and lacks broader theoretical justification.

**Strengths:**
- The paper provides a clear and well-organized description of the DRNN architecture with appropriate equations and figures.
- The experimental setup is detailed and the training methodology is well-described.
- The analysis of layer contributions (removing individual layer outputs) provides some interesting insights into the architecture's internal functioning.
- The time scale analysis using typo perturbations and context-switching experiments is thoughtful and provides evidence for the claimed temporal hierarchy.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper fails to adequately compare against established RNN variants like LSTMs or GRUs, which have become standard in the field. The comparison to a Hessian-free method [19] is outdated as more recent work has shown that LSTMs with standard SGD training can achieve comparable results without specialized optimization.
- The marginal performance gains (0.01 BPC difference) do not justify the increased complexity of the architecture. The authors note that "this similarity might reflect limitations caused by the network size," suggesting that the observed results may not be due to the DRNN architecture itself.
- The paper does not provide sufficient evidence that the DRNN architecture is fundamentally better than standard RNNs for capturing temporal hierarchies. The time scale analysis is interesting but limited in scope, and the paper does not demonstrate that DRNNs can solve problems where standard RNNs fail.
- The paper claims DRNNs "embed different time scales directly in their structure," but this is not rigorously proven. The time scale differences observed could be simply due to the network's depth rather than any specific architectural property.
- The paper does not address whether the DRNN architecture would generalize to other sequential tasks beyond character-level language modeling, which limits its broader significance.

**Quality and Significance Concerns:**
The paper's claims about DRNNs' advantages are not sufficiently supported by evidence. The authors present marginal improvements over previous work but fail to demonstrate why these improvements are attributable to the DRNN architecture rather than to other factors (such as network size). The paper does not provide a clear theoretical framework explaining why DRNNs should outperform standard RNNs for modeling temporal hierarchies.

The significance of the work is limited by the marginal performance gains and lack of broader validation. The paper's main contribution appears to be a minor architectural variation that may offer no substantial advantage over existing methods, particularly considering the computational cost (the DRNN-1O required 10 training passes through the dataset, compared to 7 for DRNN-AO).

**Conclusion:**
While the paper presents a well-organized exploration of a stacked RNN architecture with some interesting observations about time scale differentiation, it fails to provide compelling evidence that the DRNN architecture represents a significant advancement over established methods. The performance gains are marginal and not convincingly linked to the proposed architectural innovation. The paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contribution from prior work on hierarchical RNNs, nor does it provide strong evidence that the claimed temporal hierarchy is a fundamental advantage of the architecture.

The paper would benefit from more extensive comparisons with standard RNN variants, a stronger theoretical justification for the claimed temporal hierarchy, and validation on additional sequential tasks beyond character-level language modeling. As currently presented, the work does not demonstrate a clear methodological advance that would warrant publication at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** Reject. The paper presents a marginal improvement over existing methods without sufficient evidence that the DRNN architecture is fundamentally better than established approaches. The claims are not adequately supported by the experimental evidence, and the work does not advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
