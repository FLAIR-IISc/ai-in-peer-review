

# Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that stacks RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this structure naturally creates a temporal hierarchy with different layers operating at different time scales, enabling more effective modeling of both short-term and long-term dependencies. They demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia text corpus) using only stochastic gradient descent, whereas previous state-of-the-art required more complex Hessian-free optimization. The paper provides theoretical analysis, experimental results, and a detailed analysis of time scales across layers.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of DRNNs (Section 2), including two variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO), and conduct thorough experiments on a standard language modeling benchmark. The experimental setup is appropriate, with careful attention to hyperparameters, training details, and metrics (BPC - bits per character).

The paper includes valuable ablation studies: analyzing the contribution of individual layers (Figure 2), demonstrating how the network handles context switches (Figure 3), and testing the model's ability to close parentheses after long sequences (Figure 4). The authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., DRNNs don't outperform standard RNNs on tasks requiring only long memory without hierarchical processing) and provide reasonable explanations for their results.

The training methodology is well-justified, particularly the incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, which addresses the common problem of poor training for lower layers in deep architectures. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization (which took 5 days on 8 GPUs) versus their SGD approach (10 days on a single GPU) is particularly compelling.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The introduction effectively establishes the problem and motivation, and the technical sections are logically presented. The figures (particularly Figure 1) effectively illustrate the DRNN architecture.

The paper could be improved by providing more detail about computational resources (how many GPUs were used for each experiment, exact training times for each model), and clarifying some experimental details (e.g., why the DRNN-1O used a different number of parameter updates per layer). However, the paper is still sufficiently clear for experts to reproduce the experiments.

## Originality
The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. While hierarchical RNN architectures have been explored before [3, 5, 6, 8], this paper provides a formalization of DRNNs as a specific type of architecture, demonstrates their emergence of temporal hierarchies, and provides empirical evidence of their benefits for language modeling.

The key novelty is the systematic analysis of how different layers operate at different time scales and how this enables better modeling of both short-term and long-term dependencies. Previous work [8] demonstrated good performance with stacked LSTMs but provided little analysis of the architecture's contribution. This paper fills that gap with careful experimental analysis.

The paper adequately references related work, including previous hierarchical RNN approaches and the Hessian-free optimization method they compare against.

## Significance
The results are significant for both theoretical and practical reasons. Theoretical significance comes from demonstrating that DRNNs naturally create temporal hierarchies, providing insight into how deep recurrent architectures process time series data. Practical significance comes from achieving state-of-the-art results on language modeling using only SGD, avoiding the need for more complex optimization methods.

The paper's demonstration that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses after long sequences) provides compelling evidence of their capability to handle complex temporal relationships. The comparison to previous work [19] (which required Hessian-free optimization) is particularly strong.

## Strengths
- Clear formalization of DRNNs as a specific architecture
- Strong empirical results on a standard benchmark
- Comprehensive analysis of temporal hierarchies across layers
- Demonstration of better performance with simpler training methodology
- Thoughtful ablation studies that provide insights into the architecture

## Weaknesses
- Limited comparison to more recent architectures (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs)
- The paper doesn't address whether DRNNs can scale to larger datasets or more complex tasks
- The computational cost comparison with Hessian-free methods is somewhat vague (the authors estimate "roughly 4 times" but acknowledge the difficulty of making an honest comparison)
- The analysis of time scales (Figure 3) shows that DRNN-AO has different time scales across layers, but it's unclear how this specifically contributes to better performance

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of recurrent neural network architectures. The DRNN concept is novel in its formalization and analysis, and the empirical results are compelling. The paper provides valuable theoretical insights into how temporal hierarchies emerge in deep recurrent architectures, which could inform future research on sequence modeling.

The paper meets NIPS standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The results advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way, and the analysis provides new theoretical insights that will be valuable to the community.

## Recommendation
Accept. This paper presents a well-motivated, technically sound contribution that advances our understanding of recurrent neural networks. The results are significant and well-supported, and the analysis provides valuable insights into how temporal hierarchies emerge in deep recurrent architectures. The paper is clearly written and addresses the key challenges in the field.
