

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

This paper introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture that combines elements of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The authors propose that by stacking RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, the network automatically creates a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers. This contrasts with standard RNNs that do not explicitly support multiple time scales. The paper applies this architecture to character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia text corpus, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), previously achieved with more advanced Hessian-free optimization. The authors also provide evidence for the temporal hierarchy through ablation studies, typo perturbation tests, and analysis of the network's ability to close parentheses in text.

The paper builds on previous work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5]) and stacked LSTMs [8], but makes a more focused contribution by analyzing the temporal hierarchy aspect of the architecture rather than primarily focusing on performance. The authors position their work as addressing a limitation of standard RNNs in handling multiple time scales, which is particularly relevant for character-level language modeling where both short- and long-term dependencies exist.

**Strengths:**
- The DRNN architecture is a technically sound and novel combination of DNNs and RNNs that directly addresses a limitation of standard RNNs in handling multiple time scales.
- The paper provides strong experimental evidence for the temporal hierarchy in DRNNs through multiple analyses (layer contribution tests, typo perturbation experiments, and parenthesis closing tests).
- The performance results are compelling, achieving state-of-the-art BPC (bits-per-character) for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia corpus using only SGD, which is significant given that previous state-of-the-art results required Hessian-free optimization.
- The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including mathematical formulations, experimental setup, and thorough analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main claim about temporal hierarchy is demonstrated primarily on character-level language modeling, making it unclear how generalizable this finding is to other sequence modeling tasks.
- The comparison with hierarchical RNN approaches (e.g., [5]) is not sufficiently detailed; the paper doesn't clearly articulate how DRNNs differ in architecture or training from these prior methods.
- The computational complexity analysis is limited; the paper doesn't provide a detailed comparison of training time or resource requirements between DRNNs and other architectures, which is important for assessing practical utility.
- The paper doesn't adequately address how the DRNN's temporal hierarchy relates to the theoretical properties of RNNs, such as the vanishing gradient problem.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Novel architecture that combines DNNs and RNNs with evidence of temporal hierarchy
- State-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using only SGD
- Thorough analysis of the architecture's properties through multiple experiments
- Well-written and clearly organized with sufficient detail for reproducibility

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- Limited generalizability of the temporal hierarchy finding beyond character-level language modeling
- Inadequate comparison with prior hierarchical RNN approaches
- Lack of detailed computational complexity analysis
- The contribution appears incremental compared to prior work on hierarchical RNNs

The paper meets NIPS's standards for quality, as it presents technically sound work with appropriate theoretical foundations and well-supported experimental results. The clarity is excellent, with a logical structure and sufficient detail for reproducibility. The originality is moderate, as the DRNN architecture builds on prior work but provides a novel analysis of temporal hierarchy that hasn't been previously demonstrated. The significance is strong for the specific task of character-level language modeling, but the broader impact beyond this task is less clear.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how hierarchical architectures can create temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks, but the limited scope of the analysis and lack of broader applicability reduce its significance compared to more generalizable contributions. The work is relevant to NIPS as it addresses a fundamental challenge in sequence modeling with a novel architecture that combines deep learning and recurrence.

Overall, the paper is technically sound, clearly written, and makes a meaningful contribution to understanding the properties of hierarchical recurrent architectures. While the scope is somewhat limited, the thorough analysis of temporal hierarchy and the state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling make it a worthy contribution to the field.
