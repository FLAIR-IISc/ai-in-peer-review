This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) – multilayer perceptrons with temporal feedback loops in each layer – for character-level language modeling. The authors aim to combine the benefits of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs), hypothesizing that DRNNs can capture temporal hierarchies present in language data more effectively than standard RNNs. They present experiments on a Wikipedia text corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), surpassing results previously obtained with more sophisticated optimization techniques like Hessian-free training. The paper also includes analysis of the learned time scales at different layers and the network's ability to handle long-range dependencies, specifically parenthesis matching.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Novel Architecture:** The DRNN architecture, combining the depth of DNNs with the temporal dynamics of RNNs, is a reasonable and interesting approach to language modeling. The idea of inherently creating multiple time scales through stacking recurrent layers is well-motivated.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a challenging character-level language modeling task, achieving state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks with a relatively simple training procedure (SGD). This is a significant accomplishment.
* **In-depth Analysis:** The authors go beyond simply reporting performance, providing analysis of the learned time scales and the network’s ability to capture long-range dependencies. The parenthesis matching experiment is particularly insightful.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow from introduction to experiments to conclusions.  The notation is clear and the explanations are accessible.
* **Thorough Experimental Setup:** The authors provide details about their experimental setup, including network architectures, training parameters, and evaluation metrics.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison to Modern Architectures:** While the paper achieves SOTA *for recurrent networks* with similar parameter counts, it doesn't compare against more modern architectures like Transformers, which currently dominate language modeling. The reference to Hessian-free optimization feels somewhat dated, as Transformers have largely superseded these techniques.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:** While the authors experiment with DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO, a more comprehensive ablation study varying the number of layers, hidden units, and connection schemes would provide deeper insights into the architecture’s behavior.
* **Training Details and Reproducibility:** The training process appears computationally expensive (10 days per network on a single GPU). While details are provided, reproducing the results may be challenging for researchers with limited resources.  The impact of different initializations isn’t fully discussed.
* **Interpretation of Time Scales:** The interpretation of the learned time scales is somewhat speculative. While the analysis of typo perturbation and context switching is valuable, it would be strengthened by more quantitative measures of the temporal dynamics.
* **Missing Related Work:** The paper touches on hierarchical RNNs but could benefit from a more comprehensive review of relevant work in hierarchical modeling and attention mechanisms.



**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The claims are well-supported by experimental results, and the authors demonstrate a careful evaluation of their approach, acknowledging both its strengths and limitations. The inclusion of the parenthesis matching experiment is particularly commendable as it provides a unique probe into the network's long-term dependency handling.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally very clear and well-organized.  The equations are well-defined, and the explanations are easy to follow.  However, the detailed training procedure and the computational cost involved could be further clarified.

**Originality:** The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of DNNs and RNNs. While the individual components are well-established, the specific way in which they are integrated and the analysis of the resulting temporal hierarchy are original contributions.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a difficult problem – language modeling – and offers a promising approach with competitive performance. The insights into the learned time scales and the ability to capture long-range dependencies are valuable contributions to the field.  However, the significance is somewhat tempered by the lack of comparison to more recent and powerful architectures.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a challenging task.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated architecture.
* Provides insightful analysis of learned time scales and long-range dependencies.
* Clear writing and thorough experimentation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Lacks comparison to more modern architectures (Transformers).
* Limited ablation studies to fully understand the architecture’s behavior.
* Computational cost and reproducibility concerns.
* The significance is lessened due to the dominance of other approaches in language modeling.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach to language modeling, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through rigorous experimentation and insightful analysis. However, the lack of comparison to state-of-the-art Transformer-based architectures and the limited ablation studies prevent me from giving a strong recommendation for acceptance. 

**Recommendation:** Reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Compare to Transformers:** Include a comparison to Transformer-based language models (e.g., GPT-2, BERT) to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the DRNN’s performance.
* **Conduct Ablation Studies:** Perform more extensive ablation studies, varying the number of layers, hidden units, and connection schemes to gain deeper insights into the architecture’s behavior.
* **Address Reproducibility:** Provide more detailed information about the training process and consider releasing code to facilitate reproducibility.
* **Expand Related Work:** Conduct a more thorough review of relevant work in hierarchical modeling and attention mechanisms.
* **Quantify Time Scales:** Explore more quantitative methods for measuring and analyzing the learned time scales.
