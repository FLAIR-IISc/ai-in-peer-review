**Summary**
This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers where the hidden state of a lower layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The central hypothesis is that this layered structure naturally creates a temporal hierarchy, enabling different layers to operate on different time scales and better model complex sequential data. The authors evaluate this architecture on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They introduce two variants: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). The paper's primary contribution is not the architecture itself, which is similar to prior work (e.g., Graves et al., 2013), but the in-depth analysis aimed at validating the temporal hierarchy hypothesis. The authors demonstrate that their DRNNs, trained with simple stochastic gradient descent, achieve performance competitive with the state-of-the-art at the time, which required a more complex Hessian-free optimization method.

**Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates impressive performance on a challenging, large-scale benchmark. Achieving a bits-per-character (BPC) score comparable to the state-of-the-art model of Sutskever et al. (2014) is a significant achievement, particularly given that the authors use a standard SGD-based training procedure rather than a more sophisticated second-order method like Hessian-free optimization. This suggests that the proposed architecture is effective and perhaps more amenable to simpler optimization schemes.

2.  **Thorough Analysis:** The main strength of this work lies in the extensive analysis that goes beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The authors conduct several well-designed experiments to probe the internal workings of the DRNN:
    *   The analysis of layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model provides valuable insight into the functional specialization of different layers.
    *   The typo-perturbation experiment offers compelling evidence for the emergence of different time scales across the layers, directly supporting the central hypothesis of a temporal hierarchy.
    *   The investigation of long-term dependencies through parenthesis-closing is a concrete and interpretable demonstration of the model's capabilities.

3.  **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and the proposed architecture and experimental setup are described clearly. The motivations for the design choices (e.g., adding outputs at all layers to aid gradient flow) are well-articulated.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited Architectural Novelty:** The concept of stacking recurrent layers is not new. As the authors themselves acknowledge, similar architectures have been used before. The novelty is framed as one of analysis, but this makes the core architectural contribution incremental. The paper primarily confirms and analyzes a known, effective architecture rather than introducing a fundamentally new one.

2.  **Methodological Concerns and Baselines:**
    *   A significant omission is the lack of a direct comparison to a deep network of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units. The LSTM is an established, standard method designed specifically to handle long-term dependencies, and it serves as a much stronger baseline than a "vanilla" RNN. It is unclear whether the performance gains observed are due to the deep structure itself or if a deep LSTM would perform even better. Without this comparison, the claim that the DRNN structure is particularly well-suited for this task is not fully substantiated against the most relevant established alternative.
    *   The comparison to the shallow RNN baseline is of limited value. While the parameter counts are matched, the computational structure and depth are fundamentally different, which heavily influences optimization dynamics. It is widely understood that depth can be beneficial, and simply showing a deep model beats a shallow one is not a surprising result.
    *   The training procedure for the DRNN-1O is ad-hoc and complex. The necessity of a custom, incremental layer-wise training scheme suggests that standard end-to-end training is unstable or ineffective for this variant. This detracts from the simplicity of the model and raises questions about its general applicability and robustness. A method that requires such careful, staged training is less appealing than one that can be trained directly.

3.  **Insufficient Experimental Validation:** The authors state that they only trained three network instantiations due to computational constraints. While understandable, this is a major weakness. Claims of state-of-the-art performance should be supported by multiple runs to demonstrate stability and rule out the possibility of a fortuitous random initialization. The assertion that "there is very little difference in performance for different initialisations" is not supported by evidence and is insufficient for a rigorous evaluation.

**Conclusion**
The paper presents a solid piece of empirical work that analyzes the properties of deep recurrent neural networks. The strong performance achieved with a simple optimizer is noteworthy, and the analyses supporting the temporal hierarchy hypothesis are insightful. However, the work is hampered by a lack of architectural novelty and several methodological weaknesses. The absence of a strong baseline like a deep LSTM is a critical flaw, and the reliance on single training runs for each model undermines the robustness of the performance claims. Furthermore, the specialized training procedure required for one of the models raises concerns about its practicality. While the analysis is valuable, the paper falls short of providing the rigorous, comprehensive validation needed to fully endorse this specific approach over other established deep recurrent models.
