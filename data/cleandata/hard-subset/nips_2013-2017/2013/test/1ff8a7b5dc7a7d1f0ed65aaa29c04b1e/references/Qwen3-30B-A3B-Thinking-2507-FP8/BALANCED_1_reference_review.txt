

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

## Summary
This paper introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture, which stacks RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this hierarchical structure naturally creates different time scales at different levels, allowing the network to model both short-term and long-term dependencies in sequential data. They evaluate DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, comparing to standard RNNs and state-of-the-art methods. The paper provides an analysis of time-scale hierarchies in DRNN layers and demonstrates the network's ability to model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses in long phrases).

## Strengths

1. **Clear and well-structured presentation**: The paper is well-organized with a logical flow from motivation to architecture, experiments, and analysis. The figures effectively illustrate the DRNN architecture and time-scale analysis.

2. **Thorough empirical analysis**: The authors provide detailed experiments including ablation studies (removing layer outputs), time-scale analysis via typo perturbation, and specific tests for long-term dependencies (parentheses closing). These analyses convincingly demonstrate the hierarchical time-scale properties of DRNNs.

3. **Competitive results**: The DRNN-1O achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on the character-level language modeling task using only SGD training, without the need for more complex optimization methods like Hessian-free training.

4. **Important insights**: The paper makes valuable contributions to understanding how hierarchical architectures process temporal information, demonstrating that DRNNs develop learned time-scale hierarchies rather than having them as fixed architectural properties.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited novelty of the architecture**: The DRNN architecture is a straightforward extension of prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5, 8]), and the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its contribution from these prior works. The claim that "stacking RNNs automatically creates different time scales" lacks theoretical justification and is not novel.

2. **Incomplete comparison to state-of-the-art**: The paper compares to standard RNNs and mentions prior work using Hessian-free optimization [19], but does not directly compare to more recent state-of-the-art architectures like LSTMs, GRUs, or Transformers, which would provide stronger context for the contribution.

3. **Insufficient theoretical analysis**: While the paper demonstrates that DRNNs develop time-scale hierarchies, it lacks a theoretical explanation for why this occurs. The claim that "stacking RNNs automatically creates different time scales" needs stronger justification beyond empirical observations.

4. **Limited discussion of computational cost**: The paper mentions training time (10 days on a single GPU) but doesn't provide a thorough comparison of computational efficiency relative to other architectures or training methods, which is important for practical adoption.

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Clarify the novelty**: The authors should more explicitly distinguish their work from prior hierarchical RNN approaches, particularly [8] which used stacked bi-directional LSTMs. A more detailed comparison of the DRNN architecture to existing hierarchical RNNs would strengthen the contribution.

2. **Expand the comparison to state-of-the-art**: Include direct comparisons to recent SOTA models (e.g., LSTMs, Transformers) on the same language modeling benchmark to better contextualize the DRNN's performance.

3. **Add theoretical justification**: Provide a more rigorous explanation for why stacking RNNs creates time-scale hierarchies, possibly through analysis of the dynamics of the hidden state evolution equations.

4. **Discuss practical implications**: Elaborate on the trade-offs between DRNNs and other architectures in terms of computational cost, training complexity, and applicability to other sequence tasks beyond character-level language modeling.

## Overall Assessment

The paper makes a valuable empirical contribution to understanding how hierarchical RNN architectures process temporal information, with convincing evidence that DRNNs develop time-scale hierarchies during training. The results on character-level language modeling are strong, particularly demonstrating that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance with simple SGD training. However, the paper's main contribution is somewhat limited in novelty, as it builds directly on prior work on hierarchical RNNs without sufficiently differentiating its approach or providing a theoretical foundation for the observed phenomena.

While the empirical analysis is thorough and the results are compelling, the paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of its specific contribution to the field and a more comprehensive comparison to the current state of the art in sequence modeling. With these improvements, the paper would make a stronger contribution to NeurIPS.

## Recommendation
Accept (with minor revisions)

The paper makes a solid contribution to understanding hierarchical RNN architectures, with strong empirical evidence supporting the claim that DRNNs develop time-scale hierarchies. While the novelty is somewhat limited, the thorough analysis and clear demonstration of the time-scale properties are valuable for the field. The paper meets the quality, significance, and clarity standards of NeurIPS, and with minor revisions to better position the work relative to prior art, it would be an excellent contribution.
