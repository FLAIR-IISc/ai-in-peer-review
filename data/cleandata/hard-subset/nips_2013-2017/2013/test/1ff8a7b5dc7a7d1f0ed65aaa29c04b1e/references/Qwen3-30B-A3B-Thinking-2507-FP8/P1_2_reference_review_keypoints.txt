- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked RNN layers forming a hierarchy of time scales, with two variants DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art recurrent network performance on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus) without Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides thorough experiments and ablation studies showing contributions of individual layers and emergence of temporal hierarchies.  
- Time-scale analysis (typo perturbation, context switch, parentheses task) gives empirical evidence that different layers capture different temporal dependencies.  
- Technical execution and experimental setup are strong and reproducible, using standard BPC metrics and appropriate baselines.  
- Lacks theoretical grounding explaining why DRNNs produce temporal hierarchies or outperform standard RNNs.  
- Paper does not fully address potential limitations: training issues for very deep stacks and scalability to longer sequences are underexplored.  
- Clarity is generally good with clear architecture description and figures, but distinctions from prior stacked RNN work need sharpening.  
- Needs clearer explanation of why DRNN-AO outperforms DRNN-1O beyond brief remarks about gradient flow and curvature.  
- The parentheses long-term memory experiment requires clearer motivation for its suitability as a test.  
- Original contribution is the analysis of temporal hierarchies in deep recurrent architectures, though related hierarchical RNN work (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio) is not thoroughly compared.  
- Significance: valuable for sequence modeling and suggests SGD-friendly training for deep recurrent nets, but empirical scope is mostly limited to character-level tasks.  
- Strengths: novel architecture, insightful temporal analysis, strong empirical results, good visualizations, and well-motivated problem statement.  
- Weaknesses: limited theory, superficial related-work comparison, unclear distinction from prior stacked RNNs, limited discussion of limitations, and constrained application scope.  
- Recommendation: accept—compelling empirical and analytical contributions, with weaknesses addressable in a camera-ready version.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify differences from stacked/hierarchical RNN prior work, add theoretical intuition for temporal hierarchies, expand limitations and scalability discussion, compare more thoroughly to El Hihi & Bengio, and discuss applicability to other sequence tasks.
