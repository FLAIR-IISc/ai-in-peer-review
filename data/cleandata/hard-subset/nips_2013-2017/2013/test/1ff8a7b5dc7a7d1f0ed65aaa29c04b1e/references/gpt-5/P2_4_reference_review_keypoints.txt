- Summary: Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) on large Wikipedia character LM, showing competitive BPC vs. MRNN+HF and that upper layers learn longer time scales via perturbation, context-switch, and parentheses analyses.

- Experimental protocol: Training details (BPTT truncation, parallel sequences, parameter counts, gradient normalization) are described clearly.

- Analyses: Thoughtful ablations (per-layer outputs), perturbation-based timescale measurement, context-switch recovery, and parentheses tracking support a temporal hierarchy claim.

- Practical result: SGD-trained stacked tanh RNNs (~5M params) approach MRNN+HF performance, suggesting second-order methods may not be necessary here.

- Strength: Comparison to prior MRNN on same corpus/parameter budget is informative and practically relevant.

- Weakness: Limited experimental breadth—only three models, single seeds, no hyperparameter sweeps or variance reporting.

- Missing baselines: No stacked LSTM or gated RNN comparison, making it hard to separate depth vs. gating benefits for long-term dependencies.

- Comparability issue: Character set differs (95 vs 86 chars) from prior work and the effect on BPC is not quantified.

- Training confound: DRNN-1O relies on incremental supervised pretraining (layer-wise output heads), confounding architectural vs. training contributions and suggesting end-to-end training may be difficult.

- Claim nuance: The “automatic” emergence of multi-scale processing is partly contradicted by similar timescales in top DRNN-1O layers due to training procedure; deeper causal analysis (gradients, Jacobians) is missing.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear with useful figures, but omits validation usage, precise gradient clipping/rescaling thresholds, bias handling, and contains minor typos.

- Originality: Architectural ideas are not new; novelty lies in combining simple tanh DRNNs, pragmatic training (gradient norm, incremental training), and specific behavioral analyses (parentheses, perturbations).

- Significance: Insights on depth enabling long-context modeling and SGD competitiveness are useful, but impact is limited by missing LSTM baselines and narrow experiments.

- Pros (summary): Depth helps over single-layer RNNs; convincing temporal hierarchy analyses; practical training tips (gradient normalization, layer-wise supervised training).

- Cons (summary): No LSTM baselines, few runs/no statistics, limited hyperparameter exploration, character set comparability caveats, and conflation of architectural and training effects.

- Questions for authors: How do stacked LSTMs/gated RNNs perform at same budget? Does end-to-end DRNN-1O training work without incremental pretraining? Quantify 95 vs 86-char effect on BPC. Do temporal hierarchies persist with different depths/hidden sizes?

- Suggestions: Add an apples-to-apples LSTM baseline, run multiple seeds with mean/std, include validation/early stopping details, report per-layer gradient norms, and release code for reproducibility.

- Recommendation: Weak accept (borderline); technically sound with useful analyses but needs broader baselines and tighter comparisons.
