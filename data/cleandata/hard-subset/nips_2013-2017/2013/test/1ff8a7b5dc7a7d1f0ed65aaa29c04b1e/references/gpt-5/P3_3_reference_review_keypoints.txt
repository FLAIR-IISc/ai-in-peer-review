- Studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level modeling, comparing DRNN-1O (top output) and DRNN-AO (all layers output) trained with SGD, gradient normalization, and linear LR decay.
- Shows simple stacked tanh RNNs trained with SGD can match prior MRNN+HF results at comparable parameter counts and suggests emergent temporal hierarchies across layers.
- All-outputs variant resembles deep supervision and is motivated as a way to alleviate vanishing gradients and improve interpretability.
- Methodology is clearly described: BPTT length 250, 50-step warmup, batch size 75, gradient L2 normalization, linear LR decay — but Eq.(3) likely has a typo.
- Needs clarification whether gradients are normalized to unit norm or clipped, since normalization implies a fixed step size per update and merits comparison to standard clipping.
- Experiments: trained single-layer RNN, 5-layer DRNN-1O, and 5-layer DRNN-AO (~4.9M params); DRNNs outperform single-layer RNN and approach MRNN+HF performance.
- Ablations (zeroing per-layer outputs) and perturbation/context-switch analyses are well-designed and indicate layer-wise differences in time scales and training-induced effects.
- Limitations: only one dataset and three model settings tested; no comparison to LSTM/stacked LSTM, which is a significant omission.
- BPC comparability to prior work is uncertain due to different character sets/splits; SOTA claims should be bounded by matching data/splits/budgets.
- No reporting of variance across random seeds, no validation set or early stopping, and no hyperparameter search or regularization analysis.
- Random sampling of sequences for BPTT breaks long-range continuity; unclear whether hidden states are reset or carried over between mini-batches.
- Time-scale experiments are strong, but Euclidean distance between hidden states is scale-dependent; recommend normalization or cosine distance.
- Parenthesis generation experiments are compelling; testing on synthetic data with known nesting distributions would strengthen claims.
- The “pseudo-stable attractor” interpretation of dynamics is plausible but remains speculative.
- Clarity: generally well-written with useful architectural/training detail, but fix Eq.(3), specify gradient handling, state reset behavior, and provide exact BPCs and stddevs.
- Originality: architectural contributions are incremental; main novelty is empirical evidence and detailed analysis of emergent time scales.
- Significance: useful empirical finding and insightful analyses for the community, but impact reduced by absence of LSTM comparisons.
- Pros: simple reproducible architecture, strong SGD performance at scale, thoughtful per-layer analyses, evidence of emergent temporal hierarchy.
- Cons: limited novelty, imperfect comparability to prior work, sparse hyperparameter variance reporting, methodological ambiguities, overstatements about “unlimited memory.”
- Suggestions: add LSTM/matched baselines, report multiple runs and stddevs, include validation/hyperparameter search, compare gradient normalization vs clipping, ablate incremental pretraining, standardize evaluation (e.g., enwik8), use normalized distance metrics and probing classifiers.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — modest novelty but valuable empirical analyses.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
