### NIPS Review

**Paper Summary:**

This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture that stacks multiple RNN layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to develop a temporal hierarchy, with different layers learning to operate on different time scales, which is beneficial for modeling complex sequential data like natural language. The authors propose and evaluate two variants: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O), and another where all layers contribute to the final output (DRNN-AO). They test these models on the challenging task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. The results show that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching a more complex model (MRNN) trained with a computationally expensive second-order method (Hessian-Free), using only standard SGD with gradient clipping. Crucially, the paper provides a detailed analysis that supports the core hypothesis, demonstrating that higher layers in the DRNN indeed learn longer-term dependencies.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Clear Motivation and Simple, Effective Architecture:** The motivation for DRNNs is intuitive and well-grounded in the hierarchical nature of sequential data. The proposed architecture is a simple and elegant extension of the deep learning paradigm to recurrent networks. The idea of stacking recurrent layers is a natural one, and this paper provides one of the first comprehensive investigations into its effectiveness and properties.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a large-scale, recognized benchmark (Wikipedia character prediction). Achieving a BPC of 1.48 with a relatively simple training procedure (SGD with momentum and gradient clipping) is highly significant, especially as it matches the performance of Sutskever et al. (2011) who used the much more complex Hessian-Free optimization. This result makes a strong case for the power of the architecture itself, separate from the optimization algorithm.

3.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** This is the paper's most significant contribution. Rather than just reporting a performance metric, the authors conduct a series of well-designed experiments to understand *why* the model works.
    *   The "typo" perturbation experiment (Fig. 3, left) provides compelling quantitative evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. The clear separation of decay times across layers directly validates the central hypothesis.
    *   The context-switching experiment (Fig. 3, right) connects this internal property to predictive performance, showing that the DRNNs leverage longer contexts more effectively than the shallow RNN.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis-closing is a superb qualitative example of the model learning non-trivial, long-range structural dependencies. It demonstrates a form of abstract reasoning that goes beyond simple n-gram statistics.
    *   The ablation study on the DRNN-AO model, which measures the performance drop when removing individual layer outputs, provides valuable insight into the functional role of each layer.

4.  **Clarity and Reproducibility:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The methodology, including architectural details and training procedures (like the layer-wise scheme for DRNN-1O), is described with sufficient detail to allow for reproduction. The honesty about training time and computational resources is also commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Lack of LSTM Comparison:** While the paper mentions LSTMs in the introduction as a way to handle long-term dependencies, the experimental comparison is only against a standard `tanh` RNN. The core problem addressed—learning long-range dependencies—is precisely what LSTMs were designed for. A critical missing baseline is a deep stack of LSTMs. It is unclear whether the observed benefits come purely from depth, or if a deep LSTM would perform even better, potentially rendering the standard DRNN obsolete. Comparing a deep `tanh` RNN to a deep LSTM would have clarified the relative contributions of architectural depth versus a specialized cell structure.

2.  **Training Procedure for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training scheme required to get the DRNN-1O to work well feels somewhat ad-hoc. While the authors are transparent about its necessity, this raises questions about the trainability of very deep RNNs. It suggests that a naive end-to-end training approach fails, likely due to vanishing/exploding gradients through depth (in addition to time). The DRNN-AO variant, which uses intermediate outputs as a form of "deep supervision," appears to be a more elegant solution to this problem. The paper could be strengthened by a more direct comparison and analysis of these two training paradigms.

3.  **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The choice of 5 layers and a constant width across layers is presented without much justification beyond "previous experiments (results not shown)." While perfectly acceptable for a focused study, some discussion on the sensitivity to depth or layer size would have been beneficial. For example, does the performance saturate at 5 layers, or does a 7- or 10-layer model continue to improve?

**Detailed Comments and Questions:**

*   The authors state that for DRNNs, "stacking RNNs automatically creates different time scales." The analysis of an *untrained* DRNN showed far smaller differences in time-scales, suggesting this hierarchy is a *learned* property, not an automatic one. This is a subtle but important distinction that strengthens the paper's claims about the model's learning capacity. This should perhaps be emphasized more.
*   The parenthesis-closing analysis is fascinating. The observation that the network "forgets" an open parenthesis after a full stop suggests it has learned a heuristic about sentence-level scope. This implies it's learning a rudimentary form of syntax. Have the authors considered other long-range syntactic structures, such as subject-verb agreement across long clauses?
*   Regarding the training of DRNN-1O, the learning rate for the top layer was halved. Was this necessary at each stage of the layer-wise training, or only for the final 5-layer model? This suggests a potential instability in the top layers that receive more abstract, processed input sequences.

**Recommendation: Accept**

This is a strong and influential paper. It introduces a simple yet powerful architectural principle for recurrent networks and validates it with state-of-the-art empirical results on a difficult task. The primary contribution, however, is the rigorous and insightful analysis that provides clear evidence for the model's ability to learn a temporal hierarchy of representations. While a comparison to deep LSTMs is a notable omission, the quality of the work, the clarity of the presentation, and the significance of the findings make it a valuable contribution to the field. The paper not only presents a better model but also deepens our understanding of how deep architectures function in the temporal domain.
