## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture combining the strengths of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors propose stacking RNN layers, creating a hierarchy of timescales suitable for modeling sequential data, and analyze its performance on a character-based language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus. They demonstrate state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks (matching MRNN performance achieved with Hessian-free optimization) using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), and provide an initial analysis of how the network learns and utilizes different timescales across its layers.

**Related Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature. It acknowledges the rise of DNNs and RNNs, highlighting the memory-focused function of RNNs compared to the hierarchical processing of DNNs.  It cites relevant work on hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6]), acknowledging the limitations of previous work (focus on artificial tasks) and the performance-focused nature of a related study using stacked LSTMs [8]. The connection to LSTM [11] is also well-established.  The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is appropriate and sets a useful benchmark.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Architecture:**  The DRNN architecture, stacking RNNs to inherently create multi-timescale processing, is a conceptually sound and potentially impactful idea. It offers a compelling bridge between DNN and RNN paradigms.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the Wikipedia text corpus *using only SGD* is a significant achievement.  This demonstrates the practical viability and efficiency of the proposed architecture.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply presenting results and attempts to analyze the internal workings of the DRNN.  The experiments examining the decay of typo perturbations and the contribution of individual layers are insightful and demonstrate a genuine effort to understand the learned representations.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with clear explanations of the DRNN architecture and training process.
* **Reproducibility:**  The authors provide sufficient detail regarding the network configurations and training procedures, enhancing reproducibility (though access to the linked data is required, which is good).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Analysis:** While the analysis is a strength, it feels somewhat preliminary. The experiments, while insightful, are limited in scope.  Further investigation into the learned representations (e.g., visualization of hidden states, analysis of weight matrices) would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions choosing initial learning rates carefully, with a different rate for the top layer of the DRNN-1O. It would be beneficial to discuss the sensitivity of performance to these hyperparameters and the methods used to select them.
* **Network Size Justification:** The authors aim for approximately 4.9 million parameters to match previous work. While reasonable, a more systematic exploration of network size and its impact on performance would be valuable.  The claim that more parameters offer more "raw storage power" feels slightly simplistic.
* **Output Connection Architectures:** The rationale for DRNN-AO's improved training due to direct error propagation to all layers is sound. However, the performance difference between DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O is modest, and a more detailed comparison of their learning dynamics could reveal valuable insights.
* **Generative Text Examples:**  The qualitative analysis of generated text, while interesting, is limited. A more systematic evaluation of the generated text's fluency and coherence (perhaps using automated metrics) would strengthen the claims.

**Quality:**

The paper is technically sound. The claims are supported by experimental results, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature. While not a complete “solved problem,” it’s a substantial and well-executed piece of work.

**Clarity:**

The paper is generally very clear. The architectural diagrams (Figure 1) are helpful. The mathematical notation is standard and well-defined.

**Originality:**

The DRNN architecture itself is a novel contribution. While building on existing concepts, the specific combination and the analysis of resulting timescales represent a genuine advance.

**Significance:**

The results are significant.  Achieving state-of-the-art performance with a simpler optimization method (SGD) is a notable contribution. The insights into the emergence of timescales within the DRNN could inform the design of future recurrent architectures. The work has the potential to influence both theoretical understanding and practical applications of RNNs.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
* Novel and potentially impactful architecture combining DNN and RNN principles.
* Insightful initial analysis of learned timescales.
* Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Analysis, while promising, is somewhat limited in scope.
* Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity and network size.
* Generative text analysis could be more systematic.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The DRNN architecture is a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The strong empirical results and initial analysis demonstrate the potential of this approach. While there are areas for further investigation, the paper represents a significant step forward and is of high quality.  I believe it will be of interest to the NIPS community.
