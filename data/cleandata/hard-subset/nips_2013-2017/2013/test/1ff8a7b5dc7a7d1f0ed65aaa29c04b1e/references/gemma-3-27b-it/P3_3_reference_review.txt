## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a solid contribution to understanding the behavior of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), particularly in the context of character-level language modeling. The authors successfully demonstrate competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods *without* relying on complex optimization techniques like Hessian-free training, and importantly, provide some initial analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies within the network. While the experiments are relatively limited in scope, the insights gained are valuable and warrant acceptance to NIPS. 

**Summary:**

The paper explores a DRNN architecture – essentially a multi-layered RNN where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors motivate this architecture by highlighting the limitations of traditional RNNs in capturing both hierarchical processing and multiple time scales inherent in sequential data like text.  They compare DRNNs to existing hierarchical RNN approaches and demonstrate strong results on a character-level language modeling task using a large Wikipedia corpus, achieving comparable performance to methods using more sophisticated optimization. Crucially, the authors go beyond performance numbers and attempt to analyze *how* the DRNN learns, examining the time scales represented by different layers and the contribution of each layer to the final output.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Motivation & Novelty:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind the DRNN architecture – bridging the gap between the hierarchical processing of DNNs and the temporal modeling of RNNs. While stacking RNNs isn't entirely novel (acknowledged in relation to [5, 8]), the authors' focus on analyzing the resulting temporal hierarchy *is* a valuable contribution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art (or near state-of-the-art) performance on a challenging task like character-level language modeling, *using only SGD*, is a significant accomplishment. This demonstrates the viability and potential of the DRNN architecture.
* **Insightful Analysis:** The attempt to analyze the time scales learned by different layers (using the typo perturbation and context switch experiments) is a key strength.  The visualizations and qualitative observations (e.g., top layer focusing on longer-range dependencies, bottom layer being crucial for basic statistics) provide valuable insights. The parenthesis closing experiment is a particularly clever way to probe long-term memory capabilities.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are concise and easy to follow.  The structure of the paper (motivation, architecture, experiments, analysis) is sound.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a reasonable overview of related work, acknowledging contributions from the speech recognition, RNN, and hierarchical modeling communities.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental evaluation is relatively limited. Only three network configurations were tested, making it difficult to draw broad conclusions about the robustness of the DRNN architecture. A more systematic exploration of hyperparameters (number of layers, units per layer, learning rate schedules) would strengthen the paper.
* **Lack of Theoretical Foundation:** While the authors hypothesize about the emergence of different time scales, a more formal theoretical analysis of this phenomenon would be beneficial. Exploring the relationship between layer depth and characteristic time scale could be a fruitful avenue for future research.
* **Comparison to Baselines:** While the performance is competitive, a more comprehensive comparison to a wider range of RNN variants (e.g., GRUs, LSTMs with different configurations) would provide a clearer picture of the DRNN’s advantages.
* **Training Details & Reproducibility:** The description of the training process (BPTT sequence length, gradient normalization) is adequate but could benefit from more detail.  Making code publicly available would greatly enhance reproducibility.  The authors mention difficulty exploiting parallel computing for SGD – addressing this limitation (even discussing potential approaches) would be valuable.



**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Demonstrates strong empirical results with a novel architecture using a simple optimization algorithm.
* Provides insightful analysis into the learned temporal hierarchies within DRNNs.
* Well-written, clearly motivated, and well-integrated into the existing literature.
* Addresses an important problem in sequential data modeling.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
* Lack of rigorous theoretical analysis.
* Could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to established RNN architectures.



**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **accepting** this paper to NIPS. While there are some areas for improvement, the work presents a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The authors successfully demonstrate a promising architecture, provide intriguing insights into its behavior, and achieve competitive performance without relying on complex optimization techniques. The analysis of learned temporal hierarchies is particularly compelling and justifies publication at a venue like NIPS.



**Specific Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Expand Experiments:** Conduct a more thorough hyperparameter search, explore different network configurations, and test on additional datasets.
* **Theoretical Analysis:** Investigate the theoretical relationship between layer depth and time scale.
* **Baseline Comparisons:**  Include more comprehensive comparisons to standard RNN variants (GRUs, LSTMs).
* **Code Release:**  Make the code publicly available to facilitate reproducibility.
* **Address Parallelization:** Briefly discuss potential approaches to address the parallelization challenges of SGD with DRNNs.
