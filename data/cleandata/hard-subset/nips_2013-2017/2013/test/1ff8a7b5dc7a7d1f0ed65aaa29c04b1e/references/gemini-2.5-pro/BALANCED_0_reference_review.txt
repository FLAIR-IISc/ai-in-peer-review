**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture, which is constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. In this model, the hidden state of a lower layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The authors argue that this structure naturally introduces a temporal hierarchy, allowing different layers to process information at different time scales, combining the hierarchical processing of deep feed-forward networks with the temporal memory of RNNs.

The authors evaluate this architecture on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They introduce two variants: one where only the top layer produces the output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). Empirically, both DRNN models significantly outperform a standard large RNN with a similar number of parameters. The proposed models achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex models (MRNN) and advanced second-order optimization methods (Hessian-free), while using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient clipping.

Beyond performance, the paper's main contribution is a thorough analysis of the DRNN's internal dynamics. Through a series of experiments, the authors demonstrate that a temporal hierarchy indeed emerges during training, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory timescales. They also provide a compelling case study on parenthesis matching, showing that the model can capture very long-range dependencies spanning hundreds of characters, suggesting it learns abstract structural rules rather than just memorizing sequences.

**Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results with Simple Methods:** The paper achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult, large-scale benchmark. A particularly strong point is that this performance is attained using a conceptually simple architecture trained with first-order SGD. This stands in contrast to the contemporary work by Sutskever et al. (2011), which required a sophisticated and computationally intensive Hessian-free optimizer to train RNNs effectively on this task. This finding is significant as it demonstrates that architectural choices can be as important as the choice of optimizer for overcoming training challenges.

2.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The primary strength of this paper lies in its detailed analysis, which goes well beyond simply reporting a performance metric. The authors provide compelling evidence for their central claim that DRNNs learn a temporal hierarchy.
    *   The "typo" perturbation experiment (Fig. 3, left) elegantly visualizes how different layers forget information at different rates, clearly showing that higher layers maintain memory of the perturbation for longer.
    *   The context-switching experiment (Fig. 3, right) connects this internal dynamic to predictive performance, confirming that DRNNs leverage a longer context than a standard RNN.
    *   The qualitative analysis of parenthesis matching is particularly impressive. It provides concrete, interpretable evidence of the model's ability to learn and track long-range, nested structures, a known challenge for sequence models.

3.  **Clarity and Motivation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated in the introduction by contrasting the roles of depth in feed-forward networks (hierarchical processing) and unfolded RNNs (memory). The architecture is explained clearly, and the experimental setup is detailed enough to facilitate reproducibility.

4.  **Novelty in Analysis and Application:** While the idea of stacking recurrent layers was not entirely new (as the authors acknowledge, citing Graves et al. and El Hihi & Bengio), this paper was one of the first to perform such a rigorous analysis of the *reasons* for its effectiveness on a large-scale, non-trivial task. The focus on demonstrating the emergent temporal hierarchy provides a foundational understanding that has been influential for subsequent work on deep sequence models.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Ad-hoc Training for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training procedure required for the DRNN-1O model feels somewhat heuristic. While effective, it complicates the training process and raises questions about the inherent trainability of very deep RNNs without intermediate supervision. The paper would be strengthened by a deeper investigation into why this procedure is necessary for the DRNN-1O but not the DRNN-AO, and whether other techniques (e.g., different initialization schemes, normalization) could achieve similar results with a standard end-to-end training protocol.

2.  **Limited Architectural Exploration:** Given the significant computational cost (10 days per model), the study is understandably limited to three specific network configurations (one RNN, two 5-layer DRNNs). This leaves some questions unexplored. For instance, how does performance scale with depth? Is there a point of diminishing returns? The choice of 5 layers is justified by "a previous set of experiments (results not shown)," which is not ideal.

3.  **Lack of LSTM Baseline:** The introduction appropriately mentions LSTM as a key architecture for capturing long-term dependencies. However, the experimental comparison is only against a "common RNN." While the parameter count is matched, an LSTM baseline would have provided a more direct comparison to the then-standard approach for mitigating vanishing gradient problems and modeling long-range context.

**Constructive Suggestions**

1.  To strengthen the claims about the DRNN-1O training procedure, it would be beneficial to include a brief experiment showing the performance of a DRNN-1O trained end-to-end from scratch, even if it performs poorly. This would more concretely justify the necessity of the layer-wise approach.

2.  The analysis of parentheses is excellent. To make it even more compelling, it would be useful to show the corresponding behavior of the baseline RNN's hidden state in the same analysis (e.g., in Figure 4). This would help clarify whether the shallow RNN fails to represent the open-parenthesis state or simply forgets it more quickly.

3.  The text generation examples provide good qualitative insight. The analysis of the "unstable" generation when the bottom layer's contribution is removed is interesting. This could be briefly connected to the idea that lower layers might be responsible for learning fundamental, low-level statistics of the character distribution (e.g., valid n-grams), which, if absent, lead to outputs that diverge from the training manifold.

**Decision**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. It presents a simple yet powerful architecture, demonstrates state-of-the-art empirical performance, and, most importantly, provides a deep and insightful analysis that advances our understanding of how deep recurrent models operate. The strengths, particularly the quality of the analysis and the strong results with a simple training algorithm, far outweigh the minor weaknesses. The work is technically sound, clearly presented, and has high significance for researchers working on sequence modeling. I strongly recommend acceptance.
