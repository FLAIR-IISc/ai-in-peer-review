### NIPS Review

**Summary**

This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The central hypothesis is that this architectural depth creates a functional temporal hierarchy, allowing different layers to operate on and learn dependencies across different time scales. The authors test this idea on the challenging task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. They introduce two variants: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). Impressively, they achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results obtained by more complex Hessian-Free optimization, using only standard stochastic gradient descent. The paper's main contribution, however, is not merely the performance but the series of insightful analyses designed to validate the temporal hierarchy hypothesis. Through novel experiments measuring perturbation decay, effective context length, and the handling of long-range dependencies like parentheses, the authors provide compelling evidence for the emergence of different time scales in different layers.

**Strengths (Arguments for Acceptance)**

1.  **Bold and Significant Conceptual Contribution:** The primary strength of this paper is its bold reframing of depth in recurrent models. Instead of simply stacking layers to increase parametric capacity, the authors propose that depth in an RNN creates a hierarchy of temporal processing. This is a powerful and intuitive idea that provides a new conceptual foundation for designing and understanding complex sequence models. It moves the conversation from "how deep can we train?" to "what does depth do?". This is precisely the kind of high-impact, foundational idea that NIPS should champion.

2.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The paper stands out for its commitment to analyzing the model's behavior, going far beyond reporting a final performance metric. The experiments designed to probe the internal dynamics are creative and revealing:
    *   The "typo" perturbation test (Fig. 3, left) provides clear visual evidence that higher layers maintain memory of a perturbation for longer, directly supporting the temporal hierarchy claim.
    *   The analysis of context-switching (Fig. 3, right) demonstrates that DRNNs utilize a longer effective context than shallow RNNs.
    *   The deep dive into how the model handles parentheses is a fantastic case study in long-term dependency learning. It shows the model has learned an abstract rule rather than just memorizing patterns, as evidenced by the sustained activation of the top layer's output contribution.
    This level of analysis provides genuine insight and is far more valuable than a paper that merely claims a new state-of-the-art score.

3.  **Strong Empirical Validation:** The conceptual claims are backed by very strong results. Achieving SOTA performance on a large-scale, real-world task like the Wikipedia character-level benchmark is a significant accomplishment. The fact that this is achieved with a relatively simple training procedure (SGD with gradient clipping) challenges the prevailing notion that complex second-order methods are a necessity for training RNNs on tasks with long-term dependencies. This has important practical implications for the field.

4.  **Novelty in Training:** The paper introduces two simple but clever techniques for training these deep models. The DRNN-AO architecture, with outputs from all layers, provides a direct error signal to lower layers, mitigating vanishing gradient issues in a practical way. The incremental layer-wise training for the DRNN-1O is also a novel and pragmatic approach to bootstrapping a very deep recurrent model.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited Experimental Runs:** The main methodological weakness is that the results are based on a single training run for each of the three main architectures. While the computational cost is acknowledged as a major constraint, this makes it difficult to assess the stability and variance of the results. Claims about performance would be significantly strengthened by reporting means and standard deviations over several runs with different initializations.

2.  **Incomplete Ablation Studies:** Certain hyperparameters, such as the choice of 5 layers, are justified by reference to prior experiments whose results are not shown. A more systematic study of the effect of depth would have been valuable to fully understand the trade-offs involved and to see how the temporal hierarchy develops as layers are added.

**Recommendation**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. While it has some methodological limitations, such as the single experimental runs, these are minor in the face of its significant contributions. This work presents a bold, novel, and highly influential idea—that depth in RNNs creates a temporal hierarchy. This concept is not only elegant but is also rigorously supported by a series of insightful and creative experiments that go well beyond standard practice. The paper advances the state of the art on a difficult task while simultaneously providing a new and valuable framework for understanding why the model succeeds. This is a foundational piece of work that is likely to inspire a great deal of future research into the architecture and analysis of deep sequence models. Its prioritization of conceptual understanding and deep analysis over sheer performance-chasing is exemplary.
