

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces a novel architecture called Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combines the hierarchical structure of deep neural networks with the temporal processing capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The authors argue that standard RNNs lack explicit temporal hierarchies, while DRNNs create different time scales at different layers through their stacked recurrent structure. The DRNN architecture consists of multiple layers, each of which is a recurrent neural network, where each subsequent layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The paper evaluates DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, comparing against standard RNNs and other approaches. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this task and provide evidence that DRNNs develop a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers, as evidenced by their analysis of the network's response to typos, context switches, and parenthesis closing tasks.

The paper builds on previous work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3,5,6,8]) but makes a clear distinction by focusing on the explicit temporal hierarchy created by the stacked architecture. It also relates to foundational RNN and LSTM work [11] and recent advances in language modeling [19].

## Evaluation of Review Criteria

### Quality

**Strengths:** The paper presents a technically sound architecture with clear mathematical definitions (Section 2.1). The experimental setup is well-designed, with appropriate baselines (standard RNN, DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) and a thorough evaluation using BPC (bits-per-character) as the primary metric. The authors provide multiple lines of evidence for their claims about temporal hierarchies: (1) analysis of layer importance through output contribution removal (Figure 2), (2) analysis of how quickly layers forget typos (Figure 3), and (3) the parenthesis closing task (Figure 4). The paper is careful to acknowledge limitations, such as the DRNN-AO performing slightly worse than DRNN-1O and the inability to handle nested parentheses.

**Weaknesses:** The paper claims "state-of-the-art performance" but doesn't provide sufficient comparison to recent work beyond [19]. The comparison to the Hessian-free approach [19] is valid, but the paper doesn't discuss how their approach compares to more recent methods (e.g., recent Transformer-based language models or advanced RNN variants). The paper also doesn't provide a clear theoretical explanation for why the time scales develop as they do in the DRNN - the experimental evidence is strong, but the underlying mechanism remains somewhat mysterious. The training time of "about 10 days" per network (Section 3.1) is quite long, and the paper doesn't address whether this is feasible for larger-scale applications.

### Clarity

**Strengths:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The architecture is well-defined with precise mathematical notation (Section 2.1), and the figures (Figures 1-4) effectively illustrate key concepts. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility (Section 3.1-3.2). The authors clearly explain the differences between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO, which is important for understanding the results.

**Weaknesses:** Some sections could be clearer. For instance, the distinction between "temporal hierarchy" and "hierarchical processing" in the introduction (Section 1) is somewhat confusing and could be better articulated. The paper claims that DRNNs "embed these different time scales directly in their structure," but the theoretical basis for this claim is not as clearly developed as the experimental evidence. The paper would benefit from a more explicit discussion of how the DRNN architecture differs from other hierarchical RNN approaches, particularly [5] which the authors cite.

### Originality

**Strengths:** The DRNN architecture represents a novel combination of deep neural networks and recurrent networks. While hierarchical RNNs have been explored before [3,5,6], the authors make a clear distinction by arguing that their architecture creates a *temporal hierarchy* rather than just a spatial hierarchy. The paper demonstrates how this temporal hierarchy manifests in the network's behavior (e.g., different layers forgetting typos at different rates, handling long-range dependencies like parentheses). The analysis of time scales through the typo perturbation experiment is particularly novel.

**Weaknesses:** The paper's claim of "state-of-the-art" performance might be overstated. The comparison to [19] is valid, but the paper doesn't show how their approach compares to more recent language modeling work. The paper also doesn't clearly distinguish between the novelty of the architecture itself versus the novelty of the application to language modeling. The DRNN architecture is a straightforward extension of previous hierarchical RNN approaches, and the paper could better articulate why this specific combination is novel and useful.

### Significance

**Strengths:** The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in recurrent networks: modeling long-term dependencies. The evidence that DRNNs can model dependencies spanning hundreds of characters (e.g., closing parentheses after long phrases) is significant. The paper also provides a clear example of how the DRNN's internal structure reflects the temporal structure of the language modeling task, which has broader implications for understanding neural network architectures. The layer-wise training method for DRNN-1O is a useful contribution to training deep recurrent architectures.

**Weaknesses:** The significance of the work is somewhat limited by the fact that the paper only evaluates on a single task (character-level language modeling). The paper doesn't explore whether DRNNs would generalize to other sequence modeling tasks (e.g., speech recognition, machine translation) where RNNs are commonly used. The paper also doesn't clearly articulate how DRNNs improve upon existing solutions to long-term dependency problems (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs, or attention mechanisms). The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is valuable, but the paper doesn't address how DRNNs might perform with more modern training techniques.

## Overall Assessment

This is a solid technical paper that makes a clear contribution to the understanding of recurrent architectures. The DRNN architecture is well-motivated, the experimental evaluation is thorough, and the analysis of temporal hierarchies provides valuable insights. However, the paper's claims of "state-of-the-art" performance are somewhat overstated, and it could have made a stronger case for the significance of the work by comparing to more recent approaches and demonstrating broader applicability.

## Recommendation

**Accept** (with minor revisions)

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field by providing a clear architecture (DRNN) that creates explicit temporal hierarchies, and by demonstrating through multiple lines of evidence how this architecture develops these hierarchies. While the significance could be strengthened by comparing to more recent work and demonstrating broader applicability, the paper is technically sound, well-written, and provides valuable insights into how recurrent networks process temporal information.

## Specific Comments for Authors

1. **Clarify the novelty:** The paper should more explicitly distinguish between the novelty of the DRNN architecture itself versus the novelty of applying it to language modeling. A clearer statement of how this specific architecture differs from [5] (which also uses hierarchical RNNs) would strengthen the paper.

2. **Strengthen the significance claims:** The paper claims "state-of-the-art performance," but doesn't provide a sufficient comparison to recent work. I recommend adding a brief comparison to more recent language modeling approaches (e.g., Transformer-based models or recent RNN variants) to better contextualize the results.

3. **Expand on theoretical understanding:** While the experimental evidence for temporal hierarchies is strong, the paper could benefit from a more theoretical discussion of *why* these time scales develop as they do. What properties of the DRNN architecture lead to this behavior?

4. **Address broader applicability:** The paper focuses solely on character-level language modeling. A brief discussion of how DRNNs might be applied to other sequence modeling tasks would strengthen the significance of the work.

5. **Clarify the DRNN-AO vs. DRNN-1O comparison:** The paper notes that DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O but doesn't provide a clear explanation. A brief discussion of this difference would be valuable for readers.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro (Arguments for Acceptance):**
- Clear, novel architecture (DRNN) that creates temporal hierarchies
- Thorough experimental evaluation with multiple lines of evidence
- Strong evidence for the development of different time scales at different layers
- Well-written, well-organized paper with clear figures and explanations
- Provides a useful training method for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training)

**Con (Arguments for Rejection):**
- Claims of "state-of-the-art" performance are not fully supported by comparison to recent work
- Limited to a single task (character-level language modeling), lacking broader applicability
- Theoretical explanation for temporal hierarchy development is insufficient
- No comparison to more recent language modeling approaches (e.g., Transformers)
- DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O without clear explanation

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how recurrent networks can model temporal hierarchies, and with minor revisions to strengthen the significance claims and broaden the context, it would be an excellent addition to the NeurIPS proceedings.
