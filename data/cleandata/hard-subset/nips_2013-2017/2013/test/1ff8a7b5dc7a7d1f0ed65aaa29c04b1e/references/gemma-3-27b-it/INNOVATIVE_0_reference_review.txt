This paper presents a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), essentially stacking RNNs to create a hierarchical temporal model. The core idea – combining the depth of DNNs with the temporal processing of RNNs to inherently model multiple timescales – is genuinely interesting and represents a novel architectural approach. While the experimental validation focuses on character-level language modeling, the potential for capturing hierarchical temporal dependencies across various domains is significant. The authors achieve state-of-the-art performance comparable to Hessian-free optimization techniques using only SGD, which is a noteworthy accomplishment.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture is a fresh perspective on combining deep learning with recurrent models. The inherent multi-timescale property is a strong conceptual contribution.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving competitive performance with SGD, rivalling Hessian-free methods, is impressive and demonstrates the viability of the architecture.
*   **Analysis of Time Scales:** The investigation into the differing time scales learned by each layer is a valuable analysis, supporting the core hypothesis of the paper. The visualization of parenthesis handling provides tangible evidence of long-term dependency capture.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of standard RNNs regarding hierarchical processing and multi-timescale dynamics, justifying the need for the DRNN.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The evaluation is almost entirely confined to character-level language modeling. While a good starting point, it doesn't fully demonstrate the generalization potential of DRNNs to other tasks with more complex hierarchical structures (e.g., speech, video).
*   **Ablation Study Depth:** While layer contribution is investigated, a more comprehensive ablation study could be beneficial. For instance, varying the number of layers or neuron count per layer could shed light on optimal configurations.
*   **Training Details & Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the training setup is described, a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity would be useful. The differing learning rates used for different layers and the layer-wise training method raise questions about optimal parameter tuning and potential instability.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper relies largely on empirical observation of timescale emergence. A more formal theoretical analysis of *why* this hierarchy develops would strengthen the contribution.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The DRNN presents a genuinely new architectural approach that addresses a known limitation of standard RNNs.
*   The empirical results are strong, achieving competitive performance with a simpler optimization algorithm.
*   The analysis of time scales provides insightful evidence supporting the core idea of the paper.
*   The work has the potential to inspire further research into hierarchical recurrent models.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single task, raising concerns about generalization.
*   The theoretical understanding of why the hierarchy emerges is incomplete.
*   The training details are somewhat opaque and could benefit from more detailed analysis.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field. The novelty of the DRNN architecture, combined with the strong empirical results and insightful analysis, outweighs the weaknesses. While the limited scope of experiments and lack of extensive theoretical grounding are concerns, the boldness of the approach and the potential for future research justify acceptance. This paper is a promising step towards building more powerful and interpretable recurrent neural networks capable of capturing complex temporal dynamics. 

**Recommendation:** Accept. I would encourage the authors to expand the experimental evaluation in future work to demonstrate the broader applicability of the DRNN architecture.
