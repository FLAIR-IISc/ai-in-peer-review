- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) to combine hierarchical processing with temporal memory, creating multi-timescale representations.  
- Applies DRNN to character-level language modeling on Wikipedia, achieving state-of-the-art performance comparable to Hessian-free optimized models using simple SGD.  
- Provides initial analysis showing different DRNN layers capture information at different temporal resolutions via typo and context-switching tests.  
- Situates work within DNN/RNN literature, cites prior hierarchical RNN efforts, and distinguishes itself by offering deeper analysis beyond performance.  
- Novel combination: stacking RNNs is an intuitive, potentially powerful way to induce multi-timescale processing.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive SOTA on a challenging benchmark with relatively simple training.  
- Valuable initial analysis of learned timescales that gives insight into internal temporal representations.  
- Clear presentation and helpful figures, especially Figure 1, improving readability and understanding.  
- Thoughtful discussion of output connections at each layer to mitigate vanishing gradients and enable layer-specific inspection.  
- Limited ablation studies; more experiments varying layer count and neuron numbers would strengthen claims.  
- Results may be sensitive to network size; scalability with larger networks is not fully explored.  
- Insufficient hyperparameter tuning detail beyond initial learning rate, reducing reproducibility and confidence.  
- Lacks comparison/discussion of more recent sequence models (e.g., Transformers) to better contextualize contributions.  
- Limited theoretical justification linking learned timescales to data or network parameters; analysis is preliminary.  
- For acceptance: presents a novel, effective architecture with strong empirical evidence and useful insights into DRNN design.  
- For acceptance: well-written and clearly presented, with promising direction for sequential modeling research.  
- Against acceptance: learned-timescale analysis is preliminary and needs more rigorous experiments.  
- Against acceptance: missing comprehensive ablation studies and detailed hyperparameter discussion.  
- Against acceptance: limited formal/theoretical underpinning for the DRNN design.  
- Overall: solid, well-motivated contribution with compelling results; should be accepted pending addressing ablation and hyperparameter concerns.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (with minor revisions).
