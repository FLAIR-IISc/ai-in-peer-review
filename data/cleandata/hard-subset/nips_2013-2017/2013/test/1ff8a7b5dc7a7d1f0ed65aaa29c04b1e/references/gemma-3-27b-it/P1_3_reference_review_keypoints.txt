- Summary: Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) for multi-timescale sequence modeling and shows strong character-level language modeling results on Wikipedia using SGD.  
- Related work: Positions the work among DNN/RNN literature, cites hierarchical RNNs and LSTM, and compares appropriately to Hessian-free optimization results.  
- Strength — Novel architecture: Stacking RNNs to create inherent multi-timescale processing is conceptually sound and bridges DNN and RNN paradigms.  
- Strength — Strong empirical results: Matches state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on Wikipedia using only SGD.  
- Strength — Detailed analysis: Provides initial analyses of learned timescales (typo decay experiments, layer contributions) to probe internal representations.  
- Strength — Clear writing: Paper is well-written, organized, and explains architecture and training clearly.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Sufficient experimental and configuration details provided to reproduce results (data access required).  
- Weakness — Limited analysis scope: Analytical experiments are preliminary and could be extended (hidden-state visualizations, weight analyses).  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Paper lacks systematic discussion of sensitivity to initial learning rates and selection methods.  
- Weakness — Network size justification: Matching prior parameter count is reasonable but more systematic exploration of size vs. performance is needed.  
- Weakness — Output connection architectures: DRNN-AO’s modest improvement over DRNN-1O needs deeper analysis of learning dynamics.  
- Weakness — Generative text examples: Qualitative samples are limited; more systematic evaluation of generated text fluency/coherence is needed.  
- Quality: Technically sound with supported claims and good understanding of literature.  
- Clarity: Generally very clear with helpful figures and standard notation.  
- Originality: DRNN architecture and analysis of emergent timescales represent a novel contribution.  
- Significance: Significant — achieves strong results with simpler optimization and offers insights that could influence future RNN design.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong empirical results, novel architecture, insightful timescale analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited scope of analysis, insufficient exploration of hyperparameters and network size, and weak systematic evaluation of generated text.  
- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance — valuable, well-executed contribution with room for further investigation.
