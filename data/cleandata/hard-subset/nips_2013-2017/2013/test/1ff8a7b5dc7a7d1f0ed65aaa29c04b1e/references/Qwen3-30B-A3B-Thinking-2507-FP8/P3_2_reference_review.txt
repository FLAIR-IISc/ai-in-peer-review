

# Peer Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces a novel "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture that stacks multiple recurrent layers, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this architecture creates a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers, which is particularly beneficial for tasks requiring modeling of both short-term and long-term dependencies, such as character-level language modeling. The paper presents two variants: DRNN-1O (output only from the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers), and evaluates them on a Wikipedia text corpus using bits-per-character (BPC) as the primary metric.

The paper relates to previous work by contrasting DRNNs with standard RNNs and deep neural networks (DNNs), noting that while standard RNNs have limited memory depth and DNNs lack temporal dynamics, DRNNs combine the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal memory of RNNs. The authors cite previous hierarchical RNN approaches [3, 5, 6] but argue their work differs in focusing on real-world language modeling tasks and providing deeper analysis of the temporal hierarchy.

## Strengths

1. **Clear theoretical motivation**: The paper provides a compelling argument for why a hierarchical architecture might be beneficial for modeling temporal dependencies, particularly the insight that "stacking RNNs automatically creates different time scales at different levels."

2. **Comprehensive analysis**: The paper goes beyond just reporting performance metrics by conducting several insightful analyses:
   - Layer contribution analysis (removing individual layer outputs)
   - Time-scale experiments (measuring how long perturbations persist in different layers)
   - Parentheses-closing task to evaluate long-term dependency modeling

3. **Well-executed experiments**: The experiments are well-designed and clearly presented, with appropriate metrics (BPC) and thorough analysis of the results.

4. **Practical implementation details**: The paper provides sufficient details about the network architectures, training procedures, and hyperparameters to allow for potential reproduction.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of strong theoretical justification**: While the paper argues that DRNNs create a temporal hierarchy, it doesn't provide a formal argument for why this should lead to better performance. The connection between the architectural design and the performance gains is somewhat intuitive but not rigorously established.

2. **Inadequate comparison to state-of-the-art**: The paper claims "state-of-the-art performance" but compares only to a 2010 Hessian-free optimization method [19] rather than more recent approaches. For example, the paper doesn't compare to:
   - Modern LSTM/GRU architectures with attention mechanisms
   - Transformer-based language models (which were already emerging in 2013)
   - More recent work on hierarchical RNNs like [5] or [6] that the paper cites

3. **Unjustified training method for DRNN-1O**: The paper states that "incremental layer-wise training was necessary to reach good performance" for DRNN-1O but provides no theoretical justification or ablation studies to support this claim. This is a significant methodological weakness.

4. **Limited scope of evaluation**: The paper only evaluates on character-level language modeling, which is a relatively narrow task. The significance of the work would be greatly enhanced if the authors demonstrated the architecture's benefits on more diverse tasks like speech recognition or machine translation.

5. **Vague claims about "state-of-the-art"**: The paper states "we are able to get state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks" but doesn't provide a thorough comparison to other recent recurrent network approaches. The comparison to [19] is outdated, and the paper doesn't acknowledge that their performance is comparable to a Hessian-free approach that was already known to be computationally expensive.

## Specific Comments

1. **Methodology**: The DRNN architecture is interesting, but the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the DRNN differs from existing stacked RNN approaches. For example, how is their architecture different from the stacked bidirectional LSTMs used in [8]?

2. **Experiments**: The time-scale analysis in Figure 3 is particularly strong and provides valuable insights into how DRNNs process information at different temporal resolutions. However, the paper doesn't adequately explain why the DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O exhibit different time-scale behaviors (the paper attributes this to training differences, but this deserves more detailed discussion).

3. **Parentheses-closing task**: This is a clever evaluation of long-term dependencies. However, the paper doesn't discuss why the DRNN-AO performs better than the DRNN-1O in this task, which would provide additional insight into the value of having output connections at all layers.

4. **Reproducibility**: The paper mentions using "a single GPU (NVIDIA GeForce GTX 680, 4GB RAM)" but doesn't specify how many epochs or how many examples per batch. For a 1.4 billion character corpus, these details would be valuable for reproducibility.

5. **Related Work**: The paper could better position itself against recent hierarchical RNN work. For example, the paper cites [5] as having "constructed a hierarchy of RNNs" but doesn't explain how their work differs from that. The authors should clarify what makes their DRNN approach distinct from other hierarchical RNN architectures.

## Overall Assessment

**Quality**: 6/10 - The paper is technically sound and the experiments are well-conducted, but the theoretical justification is weak and the comparison to state-of-the-art is inadequate.

**Clarity**: 8/10 - The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear figures and explanations. Some details about the training methodology could be clarified.

**Originality**: 5/10 - The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of existing ideas, but the paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate it from previous hierarchical RNN approaches. The core contribution is the analysis of time-scale hierarchy rather than the architecture itself.

**Significance**: 5/10 - The paper provides interesting insights into temporal hierarchies in RNNs, but the practical significance is limited by the modest performance gains and narrow task scope. The paper would be more significant if it demonstrated broader applicability beyond character-level language modeling.

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak rejection** for this paper. While the paper presents an interesting architectural idea and provides some valuable analysis of temporal hierarchies in RNNs, the paper has significant weaknesses that prevent it from making a strong contribution to the field. The most serious issue is the inadequate comparison to state-of-the-art methods, which undermines the paper's claim of achieving "state-of-the-art performance." The paper would need to substantially revise its experimental section to include comparisons with more recent methods before it could be considered for publication.

## Specific Recommendations for Revision

1. **Expand the comparison to state-of-the-art**: Include comparisons to more recent RNN-based language models (e.g., LSTM/GRU with attention, which were already being explored in 2013) and discuss why DRNNs might be competitive with or superior to these approaches.

2. **Justify the DRNN-1O training method**: Provide theoretical justification or ablation studies to explain why incremental layer-wise training is necessary for DRNN-1O.

3. **Clarify the novelty**: More clearly differentiate the DRNN architecture from existing hierarchical RNN approaches like [5] and [8].

4. **Expand the evaluation scope**: Include experiments on at least one additional task beyond character-level language modeling to demonstrate broader applicability.

5. **Strengthen the theoretical argument**: Provide a more rigorous explanation of why the temporal hierarchy created by DRNNs should lead to better performance on language modeling tasks.

The paper's core insight about temporal hierarchies in deep RNNs is valuable, but the current version doesn't sufficiently support this claim with evidence or properly position it within the broader literature. With these revisions, the paper could make a stronger contribution to the field.
