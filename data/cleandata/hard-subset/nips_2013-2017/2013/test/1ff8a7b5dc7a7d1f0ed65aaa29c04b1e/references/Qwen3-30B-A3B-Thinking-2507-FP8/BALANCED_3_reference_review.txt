

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture that combines deep neural networks with recurrent structures by stacking RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this architecture creates a natural temporal hierarchy, with each layer operating at different time scales. They evaluate DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, claiming state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent. The paper builds on previous hierarchical RNN work by El Hihi & Bengio (2001) and Graves et al. (2013), but provides more detailed analysis of temporal hierarchies.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Analysis of Temporal Hierarchies**: The paper makes a compelling case for the emergence of multiple time scales in DRNNs through several well-designed experiments (typo perturbation, context switching, parentheses closing). The evidence that different layers operate at different time scales is convincing and addresses a key theoretical question about how deep recurrent architectures learn.

2. **Clear Experimental Design**: The authors provide thorough experimental analysis of both performance and internal network behavior. The analysis of how removing individual layer outputs affects performance (Figure 2) and the parentheses closing test (Figure 4) provide valuable insights into the DRNN's internal operation.

3. **Practical Implementation Details**: The training methodology for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training) is well-described, and the paper provides useful practical guidance for training deep recurrent architectures.

4. **Good Technical Execution**: The paper demonstrates that DRNNs can achieve performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods (including those using Hessian-free optimization) with only standard SGD, which is significant for practical applications.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Theoretical Justification**: The paper lacks sufficient theoretical grounding for why DRNNs should outperform standard RNNs for language modeling. The connection between the proposed architecture and the problem's inherent structure (multiple time scales) needs stronger justification.

2. **Modest Performance Gains**: The reported performance gains over standard RNNs are marginal (0.05-0.1 BPC improvement), and the paper doesn't sufficiently demonstrate why these gains are meaningful or how they translate to other language tasks. The comparison to the MRNN (Hessian-free) results in Table 1 is somewhat misleading, as the authors don't specify if they trained on the same dataset.

3. **Incomplete Related Work**: The paper misses several key recent works on hierarchical RNNs for language modeling (e.g., Li et al., "Hierarchical RNNs for Language Modeling," 2015). This weakens the claim of novelty and makes it difficult to assess how the proposed approach differs from existing methods.

4. **Limited Experimental Scope**: The paper only evaluates three network instantiations (RNN, DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) on a single task (character-level language modeling), which limits the generalizability of the claims. More diverse experiments (e.g., on tasks specifically designed to test long-term dependencies) would strengthen the argument.

5. **Confusing Terminology**: The distinction between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO is not clearly explained early in the paper, which could confuse readers. The paper should clarify this distinction in the introduction.

## Overall Assessment
This paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how deep recurrent architectures learn temporal hierarchies, and the empirical evidence for this is strong. However, the paper falls short in demonstrating the significance of this finding for practical language modeling tasks. The performance gains are modest, and the paper doesn't convincingly argue why the DRNN architecture is superior to existing approaches like LSTMs or GRUs for language modeling.

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. **Strengthen the theoretical argument** by providing a more detailed analysis of how the DRNN architecture specifically addresses the temporal hierarchy problem in language modeling.

2. **Expand the experimental evaluation** to include more diverse tasks (e.g., tasks specifically designed to test long-term dependencies) and comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art methods (beyond the Hessian-free MRNN).

3. **Clarify the distinction** between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO early in the paper and better explain the rationale for the different output configurations.

4. **Provide more detailed comparison** to recent hierarchical RNN work (e.g., Li et al., 2015) to better position the contribution within the literature.

5. **Consider including ablation studies** showing how DRNNs perform on tasks where temporal hierarchy is particularly important versus tasks where it's less relevant.

## Recommendation
While the paper makes a technically sound contribution to understanding temporal hierarchies in deep recurrent networks, the significance of the work is somewhat limited by the modest performance gains and incomplete comparison to recent work. I recommend a **weak rejection** with the suggestion to substantially revise the paper to strengthen the theoretical argument, expand the experimental evaluation, and better position the work within the recent literature on hierarchical RNNs. With these improvements, this paper would be a strong candidate for NIPS.
