## NIPS Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a solid exploration of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling. The authors successfully demonstrate competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods while providing valuable insights into the hierarchical time-scale dynamics emerging within the DRNN architecture. While not a groundbreaking leap, the work is well-executed, clearly written, and makes a valuable contribution to understanding the behavior of deep recurrent models. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper investigates DRNNs, which combine the strengths of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) by stacking RNNs with temporal feedback loops in each layer. The authors position their work within the existing landscape of hierarchical RNNs (citing [3, 5, 6]), highlighting the limitations of previous approaches focused on artificial tasks or lacking in-depth analysis. They build upon the successful application of stacked bi-directional LSTMs in [8], emphasizing their focus on providing a more thorough analysis of the architecture’s behavior.  The central idea is that stacking RNNs naturally creates different time scales, enabling the model to capture dependencies at various levels of abstraction – a critical feature for complex sequential data like language. The paper also correctly identifies the challenge of training very deep recurrent networks and proposes solutions such as gradient normalization and layer-wise training.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on the Wikipedia text corpus, matching the results obtained with more complex training algorithms (Hessian-free optimization) using a simpler SGD approach. This is a significant accomplishment.
* **Insightful Analysis of Time Scales:** The investigation into the temporal dynamics within the DRNN layers is a key strength. The authors provide compelling evidence that different layers learn to operate at different time scales, supporting their hypothesis about the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. The typo perturbation and context switching experiments are well designed and effectively illustrate these dynamics.
* **Clear Explanation & Well-Written Paper:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The concepts are clearly explained, and the figures are helpful in visualizing the architecture and results.  The authors acknowledge the limitations of their analysis, adding to the paper’s credibility.
* **Practical Contributions:** The layer-wise training strategy for DRNN-1O and the gradient normalization technique are valuable practical contributions that could benefit other researchers working with deep recurrent models.
* **Analysis of Output Layer Contributions:**  Investigating the contribution of each layer’s output helps in understanding the role of each layer in the hierarchy, and demonstrates how the model utilizes the different layers.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Architectural Exploration:** While the paper thoroughly analyzes the DRNN architecture, the exploration of different network configurations is limited. They primarily compare a common RNN with two DRNN variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO). Exploring different numbers of layers, neuron counts, or recurrent cell types (e.g., GRUs) could provide further insights.
* **Dataset & Computational Constraints:**  The authors acknowledge the computational expense of training these large networks, limiting the number of experiments they could perform. This is understandable, but it restricts the scope of the investigation. The reliance on a single dataset (Wikipedia) might also limit the generalizability of the findings.
* **Superficial Comparison to LSTM:** While the paper draws parallels to LSTMs (especially in the introduction), it doesn’t offer a direct comparison of DRNNs to LSTMs *with equivalent model size and training budget*.  A more focused comparison would strengthen the argument for the benefits of the DRNN architecture.
* **Limited discussion of generalization:** While performance on a test set is reported, there is limited discussion of the generalization capabilities of the networks or potential overfitting.
* **Minor Issue:** The notation regarding bias terms being "augmented" is slightly unclear. A more explicit explanation would be beneficial.




**4. Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.
* Provides a novel analysis of time-scale dynamics in deep recurrent models.
* Clear, well-written, and insightful.
* Offers practical contributions to training deep recurrent networks.

**Against:**

* Limited architectural exploration.
* Dataset and computational constraints limit the scope.
* Lacks a direct, comprehensive comparison to LSTMs.



**5. Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Specific Suggestions for Revision:**

* **Expand Discussion of LSTM Comparison:** Add a paragraph discussing the potential advantages and disadvantages of DRNNs compared to LSTMs, considering factors like expressiveness, training complexity, and interpretability.
* **Address Generalization:** Include a brief discussion of the model's generalization capabilities and potential for overfitting, perhaps by reporting results on a held-out validation set.
* **Clarify Notation:**  Provide a more explicit explanation of how bias terms are “augmented” in the equations.
* **Consider adding a figure visualizing the learned timescales:** A diagram illustrating the relative timescales of each layer in the DRNN could further enhance the paper’s clarity.



This paper represents a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent neural networks. The authors have performed a thorough analysis of their architecture, and the results are both compelling and insightful.  Addressing the minor revisions suggested above would further strengthen the paper and solidify its position as a significant contribution to the NIPS community.
