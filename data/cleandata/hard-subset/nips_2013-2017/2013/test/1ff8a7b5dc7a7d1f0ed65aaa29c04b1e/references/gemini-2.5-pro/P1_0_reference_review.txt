**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The core hypothesis is that this deep structure combines the hierarchical processing of deep feedforward networks with the temporal memory of RNNs, naturally leading to a temporal hierarchy where different layers operate on different time scales. The authors apply this architecture to the task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus.

They propose two variants: one where only the top layer produces output (DRNN-1O) and one where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The DRNN-1O model requires a special layer-wise training procedure to perform well. The key contributions are twofold: 1) They achieve state-of-the-art performance on this benchmark for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient clipping, rivaling results previously obtained with the more complex Hessian-free optimization. 2) They provide a detailed and insightful analysis that offers strong empirical evidence for their temporal hierarchy hypothesis. Through a series of experiments, they demonstrate that higher layers in the DRNN learn longer time-scale dependencies, and they provide qualitative examples (such as tracking parentheses) that showcase the model's ability to capture long-range structure. This work builds on prior use of stacked RNNs (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) but distinguishes itself by providing a deep analysis of the model's internal dynamics, rather than focusing purely on performance.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by extensive and carefully designed experiments. The authors don't just present a final performance number; they perform ablation studies (removing layer outputs), perturbation analysis (introducing typos to measure memory decay), and context-switching experiments to rigorously test their hypotheses about the model's behavior. The analysis of parenthesis-closing is particularly compelling as a concrete demonstration of learned long-term dependencies. The authors are honest about the computational limitations that restricted them to three network configurations and acknowledge the specific training tricks required (layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, gradient normalization). The work is a complete and self-contained contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated by contrasting them with standard DNNs and RNNs. The model architecture is presented with clear equations and diagrams. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail to allow for reproducibility. The results are presented effectively through tables and well-chosen figures that directly illustrate the key findings, such as the different memory decay rates across layers. The writing is clear, concise, and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The core architectural idea of stacking RNNs is not entirely novel; as the authors note, stacked LSTMs had been successfully used before. However, the originality of this paper lies not in the architecture itself, but in the thorough and insightful analysis of its properties. While previous work showed *that* stacked RNNs work well, this paper provides a compelling explanation for *why* they work well, specifically by framing their function as a learned temporal hierarchy. The specific analysis techniques, such as measuring the decay of a typo-induced perturbation through the layers, are a novel and effective way to probe the model's dynamics.

**Significance:** The paper's contributions are highly significant.
1.  **Practical Significance:** It demonstrates that a relatively simple architecture trained with SGD can match the performance of more complex models and optimizers on a challenging large-scale task. This provides a strong data point in the ongoing discussion about the utility of second-order optimization methods for deep learning.
2.  **Scientific Significance:** The paper's primary contribution is the insight it provides into the functioning of deep recurrent models. The evidence for a learned hierarchy of time scales is a crucial finding that helps build a better theoretical and intuitive understanding of these models. This understanding is likely to influence future architectural design and analysis in sequence modeling. The work moves beyond just engineering better performance and contributes to the science of deep learning.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieves state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on a standard, difficult benchmark using a simpler optimization algorithm than prior work.
*   **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The paper's main strength is its deep dive into the model's internal workings. The experiments elegantly support the central hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy. This is a model of what good analysis in an empirical deep learning paper should look like.
*   **High Clarity and Readability:** The paper is very well-written, making its complex ideas and experiments accessible.
*   **Important Insights:** The findings about temporal hierarchies in deep recurrent models are significant and likely to be influential for researchers working on sequence modeling.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   **Limited Architectural Novelty:** The concept of stacking recurrent layers was previously established in the literature.
*   **Lack of LSTM Comparison:** The experiments use simple `tanh` RNN units. A comparison with a stacked LSTM architecture of a similar size would have been valuable, as LSTMs are specifically designed to handle long-term dependencies and would be a natural choice for this task.
*   **Reliance on Training Heuristics:** The success of the best-performing model (DRNN-1O) depends on a specific, somewhat ad-hoc, incremental training procedure. While the authors are transparent about this, it slightly complicates the story of the architecture's inherent power.

**Conclusion:**
This is an excellent paper that should be accepted. While the architectural novelty is modest, this is more than compensated for by the paper's primary contribution: a rigorous and insightful analysis that provides strong evidence for how and why deep recurrent networks are effective. The combination of state-of-the-art empirical results with a deep analysis of the model's internal mechanisms makes this a significant contribution to the field. It provides both a powerful practical model and, more importantly, a deeper scientific understanding that will be valuable to the NIPS community.
