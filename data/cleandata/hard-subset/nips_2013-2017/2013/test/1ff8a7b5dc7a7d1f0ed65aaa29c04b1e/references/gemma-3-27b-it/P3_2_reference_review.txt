## NIPS Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a solid investigation into Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), combining the strengths of both DNNs and RNNs. The authors demonstrate competitive performance on a character-level language modeling task, achieving state-of-the-art results for RNNs without relying on sophisticated optimization techniques like Hessian-free training. Importantly, they provide some initial analysis into the hierarchical time-scale behavior of DRNNs, which is a key motivation for this architecture. While the paper isn’t groundbreaking in its results (matching existing SOTA), the analysis and clear presentation make a valuable contribution. I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.

**Summary:**

The paper introduces DRNNs – a multi-layered architecture where each layer is an RNN with temporal feedback. This contrasts with standard RNNs which, the authors argue, primarily focus on memory rather than hierarchical processing. The key idea is that stacking RNNs inherently creates multiple time scales, allowing the network to capture dependencies at different granularities. The authors explore two DRNN variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) and evaluate them on a large-scale character-level language modeling task.  They present empirical evidence for the emergence of hierarchical timescales and demonstrate the impact of individual layers on the overall performance.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Motivation and Presentation:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind DRNNs – bridging the gap between DNNs' hierarchical processing and RNNs’ memory capabilities. The writing is concise and well-organized, making the concepts accessible.
* **Competitive Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance for *recurrent* networks on a challenging language modeling task using SGD is a significant accomplishment. The fact that they match results previously obtained with more complex optimization methods is particularly noteworthy.
* **Initial Analysis of Time Scales:** The analysis of time scales within the layers of the DRNNs is a valuable contribution. The experiments demonstrating the effect of perturbations and context switching provide some evidence for the emergence of a hierarchical temporal representation. The analysis of parenthesis handling is insightful and provides a concrete example of the network’s long-range dependency modeling.
* **Thorough Experimentation:** The authors explore different network configurations (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO), training strategies, and provide detailed experimental settings, aiding reproducibility.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper appropriately positions its contribution within the existing literature on hierarchical RNNs and language modeling.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Core Result:** While achieving SOTA *for RNNs* is positive, it doesn’t surpass the absolute best performance on the task.  The paper acknowledges this, but it diminishes the impact somewhat. The true novelty lies in the architectural analysis, not the performance breakthrough.
* **Analysis Depth:** While the time-scale analysis is a good start, it could be significantly deepened. The experiments are somewhat limited in scope. For example, a more systematic investigation of how network depth affects the time scales would be valuable.  The explanation for the differences in time-scales between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO is reasonable but could benefit from further exploration (e.g., analyzing the learned weights).
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  Beyond disabling layer outputs, more targeted ablation studies could strengthen the analysis. For example, what happens if the recurrent connections within a layer are removed? This would help isolate the contribution of the recurrent mechanism within each layer.
* **Computational Cost:** The authors mention the difficulty of parallelizing training with SGD. While acknowledged, a discussion of potential solutions or alternative training strategies (e.g., asynchronous SGD) would be beneficial.
* **Figure 3 Clarity:**  The y-axis labels in Figure 3 are slightly ambiguous. Clarifying the units and what is being measured would enhance readability.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a well-motivated and clearly explained architecture (DRNNs) that combines the strengths of DNNs and RNNs.
* It demonstrates competitive performance on a challenging task, achieving SOTA for recurrent networks with a relatively simple training method.
* It provides initial but insightful analysis of the hierarchical time-scale behavior of DRNNs.
* The experimental setup is thorough and facilitates reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The core performance result is not a significant leap beyond existing work.
* The time-scale analysis, while promising, lacks depth and could be further strengthened with more extensive experimentation.



**Specific Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Expand the time-scale analysis:** Investigate how network depth, layer size, and initialization affect the learned time scales.  Consider visualizing the learned weights to gain a deeper understanding of how information flows through the network.
* **Conduct more targeted ablation studies:** Explore the impact of removing recurrent connections or individual layers on the network’s performance and time-scale characteristics.
* **Discuss potential strategies for improving training efficiency:**  Address the computational cost of SGD and consider potential solutions.
* **Clarify Figure 3:** Add clear labels and explanations for the y-axis.




**Overall, this is a well-written and insightful paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent neural networks. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points outlined above.**
