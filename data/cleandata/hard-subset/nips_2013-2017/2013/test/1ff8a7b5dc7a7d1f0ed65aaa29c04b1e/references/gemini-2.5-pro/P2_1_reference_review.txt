### NIPS Review

**Summary**

This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The core hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to form a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process information at short time scales and higher layers capture longer-term dependencies. The authors test this hypothesis on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They propose two variants of the DRNN: one with an output connection only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with output connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). The results show that both DRNN models significantly outperform a standard RNN baseline with a similar number of parameters and achieve performance competitive with the state-of-the-art at the time (a multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization), despite using only simple stochastic gradient descent. The main contribution of the paper, however, is not just the performance but the in-depth analysis of the trained models. Through a series of clever experiments, the authors provide compelling evidence that a temporal hierarchy indeed emerges, and they demonstrate the model's ability to capture non-trivial, long-range dependencies, such as closing parentheses correctly over long distances. This work builds upon previous uses of stacked RNNs (e.g., Graves et al., 2013) by providing the first detailed analysis of *why* and *how* such architectures are effective.

---

**Strengths**

1.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The paper's primary strength is its thorough and insightful analysis, which goes far beyond simply reporting a performance metric. The experiments designed to probe the model's internal dynamics are particularly strong.
    *   The "typo test" and "context switch" experiments (Figure 3) provide clear, quantitative evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, directly supporting the paper's central claim. Visualizing how perturbations decay at different rates across layers is an elegant way to demonstrate this property.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis closing is a fantastic case study. It moves from a high-level statistical claim to a concrete, interpretable example of long-term dependency learning. Showing the top layer's sustained activation (Figure 4) and the quantitative generative test provide convincing evidence that the model has learned a meaningful structural rule rather than just surface statistics.
    *   The ablation study on the DRNN-AO model (Figure 2, Table 2) offers valuable insights into the functional roles of different layers, suggesting that intermediate layers act primarily as feature processors for higher layers, while the top and bottom layers are crucial for direct output generation at different levels of abstraction.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art or near state-of-the-art performance on a challenging, large-scale benchmark. Achieving this using only SGD with a simple gradient normalization trick is impressive, especially when the main competitor at the time relied on a much more complex and computationally expensive second-order method (Hessian-free optimization). This makes the proposed methods more accessible and practical.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clearly articulated in the introduction, distinguishing the DRNN from both standard DNNs and RNNs. The model is described concisely, and the experimental setup is detailed enough to understand the key choices. The progression from overall performance to deep analysis is logical and compelling.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited Comparison to Gated Architectures:** The paper introduces LSTMs in the introduction as a key architecture for handling long-term dependencies but does not include a stacked LSTM in its experimental comparison. Given that LSTMs are a dominant RNN variant, a comparison between a deep stack of simple RNN units and a deep stack of LSTM units would have been highly informative. It remains an open question whether the observed temporal hierarchy is an emergent property of depth alone, or if it interacts with or is rendered redundant by explicit gating mechanisms.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training Procedures:** The training methods, while effective, have some heuristic elements that are not fully justified. The layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O is presented as a necessity ("was necessary to reach good performance") without a deeper analysis of why it is required. Similarly, the gradient normalization is described as a "trick." While these techniques are common in practice, the paper would have been stronger with a more rigorous investigation or justification for these specific choices.

3.  **Limited Model Diversity:** The authors explicitly state that they only tested three network instantiations due to computational cost, arguing that model variance is low for such large networks. While understandable, this is still an empirical claim without direct support. Showing results from multiple random initializations would have strengthened the reliability of the reported performance numbers.

---

**Evaluation**

*   **Quality: Very Good.** The paper is technically sound. The experiments are well-designed and carefully executed, and the claims are strongly supported by the results. The analysis is a complete and satisfying piece of work. The authors are honest about the computational limitations they faced.

*   **Clarity: Excellent.** The manuscript is a model of clarity. It is well-organized, concepts are explained intuitively, and the figures are highly effective at conveying the key results. The writing makes a complex topic accessible.

*   **Originality: Very Good.** While the idea of stacking RNNs was not entirely new, this paper was the first to provide a deep and rigorous analysis of the properties of such models. The analytical techniques (e.g., the typo test) and the focus on understanding *why* deep recurrent models work represented a novel and important contribution that shifted the focus from architecture engineering to model understanding.

*   **Significance: Excellent.** This is a highly significant paper. It provides foundational evidence for the benefits of depth in recurrent models and popularized the idea of temporal hierarchies in deep learning. The strong results combined with insightful analysis likely inspired a great deal of follow-up work on deep recurrent architectures (including LSTMs and GRUs) for sequence modeling. The finding that deep architectures can learn complex temporal structures has had a lasting impact on fields like natural language processing and speech recognition.

**Recommendation:** Accept

**Justification for Recommendation:**

This paper is a clear accept. It presents strong empirical results on a difficult task and, more importantly, provides a deep and insightful analysis that advances our fundamental understanding of deep recurrent models. The strengths—particularly the quality of the analysis and the significance of the findings—far outweigh the minor weaknesses, such as the lack of an LSTM comparison. This work is a high-quality scientific contribution that has had a demonstrable and lasting impact on the field.
