**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this hierarchical structure is well-suited for modeling data with temporal hierarchies, such as natural language, by allowing different layers to operate on different time scales. The authors propose two variants: one where only the top layer produces an output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). They evaluate these models on a large-scale character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus. The key findings are that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results obtained with more complex Hessian-free optimization methods using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient normalization. More importantly, the paper provides a detailed analysis demonstrating that the layers of a trained DRNN indeed learn a temporal hierarchy, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory. The analysis also explores long-range dependencies, showing the model's ability to correctly handle structures like parentheses over hundreds of characters.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by extensive and carefully designed experiments on a challenging, large-scale benchmark.

*   **Strengths:**
    1.  **Thorough Empirical Evaluation:** The work goes far beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The analysis is the core strength. Experiments to measure the effective time scales of each layer (the "typo" and "context switch" experiments in Figure 3) provide convincing evidence for the central hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy.
    2.  **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of parenthesis-closing is an excellent case study that makes the abstract concept of "long-term dependency modeling" concrete and interpretable. It demonstrates that the model learns a generalizable rule rather than just memorizing long sequences.
    3.  **Complete Work:** This is a complete piece of research, not a preliminary report. It presents a clear hypothesis, designs experiments to test it, reports strong results, and provides analysis that yields valuable insights into the model's internal workings. The authors are also careful to compare their work to relevant benchmarks, including a non-neural compression algorithm (PAQ) and the previous state-of-the-art MRNN.

*   **Weaknesses:**
    1.  **Limited Number of Runs:** The authors state they only tested three network instantiations, justifying this by the high computational cost and arguing that performance variance is low for such large models. While understandable, this is a limitation. Reporting variance over a few runs with different random seeds would have strengthened the claims of robustness.
    2.  **Heuristic Training Methods:** The training procedure for the DRNN-1O model, which involves incremental layer-wise training, is somewhat ad-hoc and crucial for its success. While the authors provide a good justification (ensuring lower layers are effectively trained), this dependency on a specific training curriculum could be seen as a minor weakness. The gradient normalization is also a useful but heuristic trick.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.

*   **Strengths:**
    1.  **Clear Motivation:** The introduction clearly articulates the distinction between depth in feed-forward networks (hierarchical feature processing) and depth in RNNs (temporal hierarchy), setting up the motivation for the work perfectly.
    2.  **Well-Organized:** The paper flows logically from motivation and model definition to experimental setup, results, and detailed analysis. Figures and tables are clear, well-labeled, and effectively support the text. Figure 1 provides a good schematic of the proposed architectures.
    3.  **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail about the model architecture, training parameters, and data to allow an expert to understand and likely reproduce the work. The open-sourcing of the dataset is also commendable.

**Originality:** The paper makes several original contributions.

*   **Strengths:**
    1.  **Novel Analysis:** While the idea of stacking RNNs was not entirely new (as the authors appropriately cite), this paper is highly original in its rigorous application to a difficult, large-scale problem and, most importantly, its in-depth analysis of the emergent properties of the architecture. Prior work often focused on smaller or artificial tasks without providing such compelling evidence for the temporal hierarchy.
    2.  **Demonstrating SGD's Power:** At the time, achieving state-of-the-art on this task was thought to require complex second-order methods like Hessian-free optimization. A key contribution of this work is demonstrating that a carefully configured, deep architecture can achieve comparable results with a much simpler and more scalable optimizer (SGD).
    3.  **Conceptual Contribution:** The paper solidifies the concept of "temporal hierarchy" in deep recurrent models, providing a strong conceptual framework and vocabulary for understanding why stacking recurrent layers is beneficial.

**Significance:** The significance of this work is very high.

*   **Strengths:**
    1.  **Advancing the State of the Art:** The paper demonstrates a new state-of-the-art performance for RNNs on a standard benchmark, showing that architectural improvements (depth) can be as impactful as algorithmic ones (optimizers).
    2.  **High Impact on Future Research:** This work helped popularize and validate the use of deep, stacked RNNs (and LSTMs), which subsequently became a standard and dominant architecture for a vast range of sequence modeling tasks, including machine translation, speech recognition, and more. The insights from the analysis have informed countless subsequent studies.
    3.  **Practical Implications:** By showing that excellent results can be obtained with SGD, the paper provided a practical and scalable path forward for training very large sequence models, making this line of research accessible to a wider community.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult and widely recognized benchmark.
*   Provides a novel and highly insightful analysis that gives a deeper understanding of *why* deep recurrent models work, confirming the temporal hierarchy hypothesis.
*   Demonstrates that simple, scalable training methods (SGD) can be competitive with more complex second-order methods for these architectures.
*   The work is exceptionally clear, well-executed, and has had a significant and lasting impact on the field of sequence modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
*   The use of heuristic training methods and a very limited number of experimental runs are minor methodological weaknesses.
*   The core architectural idea of stacking recurrent layers has some precedent in prior, smaller-scale work.

**Conclusion:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses. It presents state-of-the-art empirical results and, more importantly, provides a deep and insightful analysis that advances our fundamental understanding of deep recurrent models. The paper is a model of clarity and high-quality research. It is a definite accept for NIPS.
