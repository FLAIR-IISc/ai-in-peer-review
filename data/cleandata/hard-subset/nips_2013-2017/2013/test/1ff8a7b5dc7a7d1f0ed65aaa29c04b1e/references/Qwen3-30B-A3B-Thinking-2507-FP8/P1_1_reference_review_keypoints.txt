- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): layered RNNs where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer, creating a temporal hierarchy.  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) and achieves state-of-the-art for recurrent networks using simple SGD.  
- Strength: Clear mathematical formulation and training details, including DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO distinctions.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments analyzing time scales, layer importance, and long-term dependencies.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstration that different layers learn different time scales rather than this being an artifact.  
- Strength: Practical result of matching or exceeding prior recurrent-network performance without complex optimizers.  
- Strength: Adequate comparison to standard RNN baselines and prior Hessian-free work.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification for why temporal hierarchies emerge in DRNNs.  
- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from prior hierarchical RNN work, especially [5] that also uses multiple time scales.  
- Weakness: Performance gains over standard RNNs are modest and not strongly motivated as significant.  
- Weakness: Incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is used without adequate theoretical or empirical justification.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to other deep recurrent architectures (e.g., stacked bidirectional LSTMs).  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of DRNN limitations, including computational cost and potential vanishing-gradient issues.  
- Quality: Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and experiments are well-supported, but weakened by limited theory and overlap with prior work.  
- Clarity: Writing and notation are clear; figures and experimental setup aid reproducibility, but time-scale analysis could be better explained.  
- Originality: Architecture is a novel combination of DNNs and RNNs, but core idea of temporal hierarchies overlaps with earlier work.  
- Significance: Shows that DRNNs can reach strong performance with SGD and offers insights into temporal hierarchies, but overall gains are modest.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with modest contribution; recommended for weak acceptance.  
- Suggestions: Clarify differences from prior hierarchical RNNs, provide theoretical justification for temporal hierarchies, compare to stacked bidirectional LSTMs, analyze computational cost and limitations, and justify incremental layer-wise training.
