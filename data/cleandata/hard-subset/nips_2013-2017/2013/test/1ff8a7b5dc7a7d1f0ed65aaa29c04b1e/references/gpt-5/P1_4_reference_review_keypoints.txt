- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked tanh RNNs) with two output schemes: DRNN-1O (top-only) and DRNN-AO (all layers contribute).
- Trains character-level language models on Wikipedia with SGD, gradient-normalization, and linear learning-rate decay; uses incremental supervised layer-wise training for DRNN-1O.
- With ~4.9M parameters, DRNN variants outperform single-layer RNNs and approach prior MRNN HF results on BPC.
- Empirically analyzes emergent temporal hierarchies via per-layer ablations, perturbation (typo/context-switch) tests, and parentheses long-range tracking.
- Relates to prior stacked/hierarchical RNN and deep supervision literature; novelty is systematic quantitative analysis of per-layer timescales on realistic text.
- Models and training are standard and well-implemented at scale; analyses use large test averages for stability.
- Eq. (3) likely contains a sign/typo (η0(- j/T) should be η0(1 − j/T)); needs correction.
- Gradient-normalization (dividing by ||∇θ||) is nonstandard—requires discussion of epsilon handling and stability/sensitivity.
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair: different alphabets for MRNN and HF training on different hardware; clearer apples-to-apples baselines needed.
- Experimental breadth limited: only three model instantiations, no error bars, no seed variance, and minimal hyperparameter exploration.
- Some training details are missing/ambiguous: hidden-state initialization, state resets across sequences, and effects of truncated BPTT length 200.
- Empirical evidence supports main claims: DRNNs beat single RNNs at equal parameter budgets and show layer-wise timescale differences.
- Paper is generally clear and well-written; analysis sections are insightful and accessible.
- Minor clarity fixes needed: correct Eq. (3), describe epsilon in gradient normalization, state hidden-state handling, complete bibliographic details, and clarify dataset preprocessing/alphabet differences.
- Originality is moderate: stacking and auxiliary outputs are not new, but the large-scale analysis of temporal hierarchies and training recipe are meaningful empirical contributions.
- Significance: character-level LM is relevant; showing SGD-trained vanilla DRNNs competitive with HF MRNNs is useful, but lack of LSTM/GRU comparisons limits impact.
- Strength: clear motivation for multi-timescale processing and practical architecture with solid large-scale experiments and insightful analyses.
- Strength: practical training insights including deep supervision (AO) and incremental supervised pretraining for 1O.
- Weakness: missing gated baselines (stacked LSTM/GRU) and multiplicative/gated DRNN variants to strengthen claims.
- Weakness: comparability issues with MRNN baseline; need matched character sets or toned-down SOTA claims.
- Weakness: lack of robustness metrics—report variance across seeds and ablate layer counts/widths to test hierarchy claims.
- Weakness: unclear training details—clarify learning-rate schedule formula, epsilon, hidden-state persistence, BPTT truncation, and provide dev curves.
- Suggest exploring regularization (dropout/weight decay) and whether AO acts as a regularizer beyond optimization aid.
- Suggest stronger analyses: task-relevant probes (e.g., linear classifiers for bracket depth), mutual information, or linear influence to quantify effective context per layer.
- Arguments for acceptance: advances understanding of deep recurrent architectures with careful large-scale analyses and practical training tricks.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited baselines, small model sweep, ambiguous methodological details, and incomplete references.
- Overall recommendation: lean weak accept contingent on clarifying training details, improving baseline comparisons, and adding robustness analyses.
