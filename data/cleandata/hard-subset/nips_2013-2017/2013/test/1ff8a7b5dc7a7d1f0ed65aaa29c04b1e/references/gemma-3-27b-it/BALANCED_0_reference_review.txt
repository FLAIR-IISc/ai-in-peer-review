This paper explores deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) as a means to combine the strengths of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) for sequential data modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs, creating a hierarchy that implicitly captures different timescales within the data, and applies this to the task of character-level language modeling. The authors demonstrate competitive results on a Wikipedia corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD).

This work builds on prior research in both DNNs [4] and RNNs [11, 17], attempting to bridge the gap between hierarchical processing (DNNs) and temporal modeling (RNNs). The authors appropriately acknowledge related hierarchical RNN architectures [3, 5, 6, 8], and position their contribution as providing a deeper analysis of the architectural properties and their relation to the task. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is well-placed, highlighting the effectiveness of their approach despite using a simpler training method. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture is a reasonable and potentially powerful combination of DNNs and RNNs. Stacking RNNs to create inherent multi-timescale processing is a compelling idea.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with SGD on a challenging character-level language modeling task is a significant accomplishment. This demonstrates the practical viability of the proposed architecture.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond merely presenting results, attempting to analyze the learned timescales within the DRNN and the contribution of different layers, including visualizations of output probabilities and forgetting behavior. The analysis of parenthesis matching is particularly insightful.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the DRNN architecture and training procedure clearly, including the nuances of the layer-wise training approach for the DRNN-1O configuration.
* **Thorough Experimentation:** The authors performed a reasonable set of experiments, including ablation studies (removing layer contributions) and tests to probe the network’s memory capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Comparison:** While the authors compare to existing work, the comparison is somewhat limited. Specifically, comparing solely to other *recurrent* network approaches obscures the broader landscape of language models.  More comparison to transformer-based models or other contemporary sequence modeling techniques would be valuable.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The tuning of learning rates, particularly for the top layer of the DRNN-1O, suggests sensitivity to hyperparameters. A more systematic exploration of these parameters, and potentially techniques for automatic learning rate tuning, could strengthen the results.
* **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** While the authors provide intuition for why DRNNs might be effective, the paper lacks a more formal theoretical analysis of the emergent timescales or the advantages of this architecture over simpler alternatives.
* **Computational Cost:**  Training these models takes a considerable amount of time (10 days for a single network). While acknowledged, this limits the scalability and exploration of more complex architectures and parameter settings. The authors mention a larger network trained for an extended period, but only present a single data point.
* **Interpretability Concerns:** The analysis of layer contributions, while insightful, is based on ablation studies, which can be misleading due to potential interactions between layers.  More robust methods for interpreting the internal representations of DRNNs would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for sequence modeling.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing recurrent network approaches.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of DRNNs and their ability to capture hierarchical temporal dependencies.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and results.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper’s scope is somewhat limited, and the comparison to the broader landscape of sequence modeling is lacking.
* The lack of a theoretical justification for the DRNN’s effectiveness is a weakness.
* The computational cost of training is high, limiting the scalability of the approach.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a compelling idea, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness on a challenging task. While the paper has some weaknesses, particularly in terms of theoretical justification and broader comparison, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses. I believe that this paper is **Accept** with minor revisions. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Expand the comparison:** Include a broader range of sequence modeling techniques in the experimental evaluation, including transformer-based models.
* **Investigate hyperparameter sensitivity:** Perform a more systematic exploration of hyperparameters, or consider using techniques for automatic learning rate tuning.
* **Consider a theoretical analysis:** Explore potential theoretical justifications for the DRNN’s effectiveness, such as an analysis of the emergent timescales or the information flow through the network.
* **Provide more details on the larger network:** Include more results from the larger network (9.6 million parameters) to demonstrate the scalability of the approach.
* **Explore alternative interpretability methods:** Investigate more robust methods for interpreting the internal representations of DRNNs, beyond simple ablation studies.
