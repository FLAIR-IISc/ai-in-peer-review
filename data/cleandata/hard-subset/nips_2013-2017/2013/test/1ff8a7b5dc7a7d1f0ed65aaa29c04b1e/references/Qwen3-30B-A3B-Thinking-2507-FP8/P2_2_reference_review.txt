

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture that combines the hierarchical structure of deep neural networks with the temporal processing capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The key insight is that stacking RNNs (where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input) naturally creates a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different levels. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs can capture both short- and long-term dependencies in language modeling tasks, outperforming standard RNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus. The paper makes several contributions: (1) proposing the DRNN architecture, (2) demonstrating its effectiveness through experiments, (3) analyzing the temporal hierarchy it creates, and (4) providing evidence of its ability to model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses after long sequences).

The work builds on previous hierarchical RNN approaches [3, 5, 6] but differs by focusing on the temporal hierarchy aspect rather than just stacking RNNs for performance gains. It also extends the work of [8] which used stacked bidirectional LSTMs for speech recognition but provided limited architectural analysis.

## Quality

**Strengths:**
- The paper is technically sound with well-defined architecture and training methodology. The DRNN equations (2.1) and output generation (2.2) are clearly presented with appropriate notation.
- The experimental design is rigorous, with careful attention to parameter settings, training protocols, and evaluation metrics (BPC).
- The authors provide multiple lines of evidence to support their claims: (1) performance comparisons, (2) layer contribution analysis (Figure 2), (3) time-scale analysis through typo perturbation (Figure 3), and (4) the parentheses closing task (Figure 4).
- The authors demonstrate honesty about limitations, noting that DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O, and acknowledging that the DRNN-1O training method requires layer-wise training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper claims "state-of-the-art performance" but doesn't fully compare with the most recent literature. The cited Hessian-free approach [19] is from 2013, while more recent works like [16] (which uses Hessian-free optimization) and various transformer-based approaches would provide a more current benchmark.
- The analysis of why DRNNs outperform standard RNNs could be more theoretically grounded. The explanation for the temporal hierarchy is somewhat intuitive but lacks deeper theoretical justification.
- The paper doesn't thoroughly address the computational cost trade-offs of DRNNs versus other architectures, though it mentions training time (10 days per network).

## Clarity

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-written, with clear organization and logical flow from problem statement to architecture to experiments.
- Figures 1 and 2 are particularly helpful in visualizing the architecture and layer contributions.
- The explanation of the DRNN architecture is accessible, with appropriate technical depth for the target audience.
- The training setup details (Section 3.3) are sufficient for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
- Some technical details could be more precise. For example, the training protocol for DRNN-1O (Section 3.1) mentions "incremental layer-wise training" but could more clearly explain why this is necessary compared to standard layer-wise pre-training.
- The paper could better clarify why the DRNN-AO architecture (all layers output) performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O, which seems counterintuitive to the authors' main thesis.

## Originality

**Strengths:**
- The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of existing concepts (DNNs and RNNs) that creates a natural temporal hierarchy. While hierarchical RNNs have been proposed before [5], the authors' specific approach to creating time-scale hierarchies through stacking is original.
- The paper's focus on analyzing the temporal hierarchy that emerges in the DRNN (rather than just demonstrating performance gains) is a significant contribution that differentiates it from previous work.
- The parentheses closing experiment (Section 3.5) provides a novel way to test long-term memory capabilities.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could better differentiate itself from related work. For instance, the paper cites [5] as having a similar hierarchical architecture but claims their approach is different. A more detailed comparison would strengthen the originality claim.
- The paper doesn't sufficiently engage with the broader literature on hierarchical sequence modeling, such as the work on hierarchical LSTMs [21] or the more recent Transformer-based approaches that also handle multiple time scales.

## Significance

**Strengths:**
- The paper demonstrates clear significance by showing that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling.
- The analysis of temporal hierarchies provides valuable insights into how RNNs can be structured to better model sequential data with multiple time scales.
- The parentheses closing experiment demonstrates practical utility for modeling long-term dependencies, which is a meaningful contribution to the field.
- The work suggests promising future directions for training algorithms (e.g., "applying second order techniques such as Hessian-free training on DRNNs").

**Weaknesses:**
- The significance is somewhat limited to character-level language modeling, with less clear applicability to other domains.
- The paper doesn't clearly articulate how DRNNs advance beyond existing approaches for tasks requiring long-term memory (e.g., LSTMs or Hessian-free trained RNNs).
- The claim that DRNNs "embed these different time scales directly in their structure" is interesting but could be better contextualized with respect to other architectures that also handle multiple time scales (e.g., dilated convolutions or Transformers).

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-executed paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of recurrent architectures. The DRNN architecture is novel in its specific implementation and the way it creates a natural temporal hierarchy. The experimental results are convincing and provide valuable insights into how deep RNNs process information at different time scales. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and addresses an important aspect of sequential modeling.

The main limitations are that the paper doesn't fully compare with the most recent state-of-the-art methods, and the significance could be strengthened by better contextualizing the work within the broader landscape of hierarchical sequence modeling.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how to structure recurrent networks to better capture temporal hierarchies, and the experimental results are compelling. The paper would benefit from:

1. A more thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods (beyond the 2013 Hessian-free approach)
2. A clearer discussion of how DRNNs relate to other approaches for handling multiple time scales (e.g., dilated convolutions, Transformers)
3. More detailed analysis of why DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O

The paper is well-suited for NeurIPS as it addresses a fundamental question about recurrent architecture design with both theoretical insights and empirical validation.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel architecture that creates a natural temporal hierarchy through stacking RNNs
- Rigorous experimental analysis showing DRNNs outperform standard RNNs on language modeling
- Insightful analysis of time scales across layers, demonstrating the architecture's key property
- Clear presentation of results with appropriate figures and metrics
- Demonstrates a practical application (parentheses closing) that validates long-term memory capabilities

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Limited comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods (only compared to 2013 Hessian-free approach)
- Doesn't thoroughly address why DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O
- The significance of the work could be better articulated in the context of modern sequence modeling approaches
- Some technical details (like the layer-wise training protocol) could be clearer

Overall, the strengths of this paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses, and it represents a solid contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks.
