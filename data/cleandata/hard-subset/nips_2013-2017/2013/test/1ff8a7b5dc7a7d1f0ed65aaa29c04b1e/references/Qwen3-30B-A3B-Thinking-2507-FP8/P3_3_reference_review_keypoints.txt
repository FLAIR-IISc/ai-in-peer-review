- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack RNN layers with temporal feedback loops to create a natural temporal hierarchy across layers.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using only SGD, without Hessian-free optimization.
- Provides multiple empirical analyses (perturbation experiments, layer contribution, parentheses-closing task) showing emergence of temporal hierarchies.
- DRNN architecture is a natural extension of standard RNNs and deep nets and is empirically well-evaluated and reproducible.
- Strong empirical evidence for hierarchical time-scales, but lacks theoretical justification for why DRNNs develop these hierarchies.
- Training methodologies differ (DRNN-1O layer-wise vs. DRNN-AO), making direct comparisons between variants problematic and insufficiently justified.
- Paper is generally clear, with good figures and equations, but could better distinguish DRNNs from similar stacked RNN approaches like [8].
- The distinction between “temporal hierarchy” and “hierarchical processing” is somewhat confusing and needs clarification.
- Novel contribution: first detailed empirical analysis showing stacked RNNs automatically develop temporal hierarchies.
- Related work coverage is incomplete; needs clearer comparison to prior hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5]) and recent hierarchical/attention RNNs.
- Significant findings: shows DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (hundreds of characters) and that SGD can achieve competitive performance for RNNs.
- Weaknesses: no theoretical grounding, inconsistent training between variants, insufficient differentiation from prior work, limited discussion of broader implications, and no comparison to Transformer models.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add theoretical discussion, clarify relation to prior stacked RNNs (especially [8]), and include a brief comparison to recent Transformer-based language models.
- Overall rating: Strong Accept.
