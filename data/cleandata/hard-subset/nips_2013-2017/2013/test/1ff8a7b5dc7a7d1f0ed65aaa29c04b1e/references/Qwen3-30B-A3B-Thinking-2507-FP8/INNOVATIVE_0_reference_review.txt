

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces a novel architecture called Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which stacks RNNs to create a temporal hierarchy where different layers operate at different time scales. The authors argue that traditional RNNs lack explicit support for multiple time scales, making them suboptimal for tasks with complex temporal dependencies like language modeling. Their key insight is that stacking RNNs inherently creates this temporal hierarchy, with bottom layers capturing short-term dependencies and top layers capturing long-term dependencies.

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, it presents a clear architectural innovation: DRNNs combine the hierarchical structure of DNNs with the temporal processing of RNNs, creating a natural temporal hierarchy without explicit time-scale engineering. Second, the authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling, reaching performance previously requiring more complex training methods like Hessian-free optimization. Third, they provide compelling empirical evidence for the temporal hierarchy through experiments measuring how different layers respond to perturbations and context changes.

The paper's strongest aspect is its novel theoretical insight about temporal hierarchy. The authors effectively demonstrate that DRNNs naturally create different time scales at different layers, which is a fundamental departure from previous hierarchical RNN approaches like [5] that required explicit time-scale engineering through subsampling. Their experiments on parenthesis closing and context length provide convincing evidence that the DRNN architecture genuinely learns to model long-term dependencies in a way that differs from standard RNNs.

The paper also makes a bold claim about the training simplicity of DRNNs: they achieve state-of-the-art results using only stochastic gradient descent, whereas previous work required more complex optimization techniques. This is significant because it suggests a simpler, more accessible path to high-performance sequential modeling.

Regarding weaknesses, the paper's experimental validation is somewhat limited (only three network instantiations, single GPU experiments), and the theoretical justification for the temporal hierarchy is primarily empirical rather than analytical. The paper also doesn't fully compare to the most recent work in the field (e.g., more recent language modeling papers beyond [19]). However, as a reviewer who prioritizes novel concepts over methodological perfection, these limitations don't outweigh the paper's significant contribution.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with good experimental design and appropriate metrics (BPC for language modeling). The analysis of individual layer contributions through the "output contribution removal" test is particularly insightful, providing a useful diagnostic tool for understanding deep recurrent architectures.

The significance of this work is substantial. The temporal hierarchy concept could influence future research on sequential modeling, potentially offering a more principled approach to handling long-term dependencies. The paper's demonstration that DRNNs can model complex temporal relationships (like remembering parentheses across long phrases) suggests they could be valuable for a range of sequential tasks beyond language modeling.

Compared to previous NIPS work, this paper stands out by focusing on the architecture's inherent temporal properties rather than just performance improvements. While [5] proposed hierarchical RNNs for artificial tasks, and [8] used stacked LSTMs for performance gains, this paper provides a deeper theoretical and empirical analysis of why the architecture works, particularly regarding temporal hierarchy.

The paper's main contribution is the conceptual insight that stacking RNNs creates a natural temporal hierarchy, which is a significant shift in how we think about recurrent architectures. This is not just an incremental improvement but a new direction for research. The authors effectively position their work as addressing a fundamental limitation of RNNs that has persisted since the early days of the field.

**Strengths:**
- Novel conceptual insight about temporal hierarchy in stacked RNNs
- Clear empirical evidence supporting the architecture's unique properties
- Achieves state-of-the-art results for RNNs using simple SGD
- Provides useful diagnostic tools for understanding deep recurrent architectures
- Well-motivated and well-executed experiments on language modeling

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited experimental scope (only three network instantiations)
- Lacks theoretical justification for the temporal hierarchy emergence
- Could have compared to more recent language modeling work
- Training methodology for DRNN-1O is somewhat ad hoc

**Recommendation:** Accept

This paper introduces a genuinely novel concept that has the potential to significantly influence how we design and understand recurrent architectures. The core insight about temporal hierarchy emerging naturally from the DRNN architecture is compelling and well-supported by experiments. While the paper has some methodological limitations, they don't undermine the significance of the conceptual contribution. The authors have made a strong case for a new direction in recurrent neural network research that addresses a fundamental limitation of current approaches. This work would be a valuable addition to NIPS, particularly for researchers working on sequential modeling and deep learning architectures.
