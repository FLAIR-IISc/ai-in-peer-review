### Summary and Relation to Prior Work

This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to develop a temporal hierarchy, with different layers learning to operate on different time scales, which is beneficial for tasks with complex, multi-scale temporal dependencies like character-level language modeling.

The authors propose two variants: one where only the top layer produces the output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). They evaluate these models on a large-scale Wikipedia character prediction benchmark. The key findings are twofold: 1) The DRNN architecture, trained with a simple SGD-based method (with gradient clipping), achieves state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained only with more sophisticated Hessian-free optimization on a multiplicative RNN [19]. 2) Through a series of well-designed experiments, the paper provides strong evidence that the DRNNs learn a functional hierarchy of time scales, with higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies.

This work builds upon prior ideas of hierarchical RNNs [5, 6], and particularly the stacked LSTM architecture used in [8] for speech recognition. However, whereas [8] focused primarily on achieving a state-of-the-art result, this paper's main contribution lies in its in-depth analysis of the model's internal dynamics. It provides a compelling explanation and empirical validation for *why* stacking recurrent layers is effective, connecting the architectural depth to the learning of a temporal hierarchy.

### Strengths

1.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The paper's primary strength is not just in achieving a strong result, but in the thorough and insightful analysis of the model's behavior. The experiments designed to probe the model's properties are exemplary:
    *   The "typo perturbation" experiment (Fig. 3, left) provides direct evidence of differing time scales across layers, showing that perturbations fade much more slowly in higher layers.
    *   The analysis of long-term dependencies via parenthesis-closing is particularly compelling. It demonstrates that the model learns something akin to a state-based understanding of syntax, rather than just memorizing sequences, as evidenced by the top layer's sustained activation and its ability to generalize to sequences longer than those seen in training.
    *   The ablation study on the DRNN-AO (Fig. 2) and the qualitative analysis of generated text (Table 2) effectively dissect the roles of different layers, suggesting that lower layers handle basic statistics while upper layers model higher-level structure.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a challenging, large-scale benchmark using a relatively simple optimization algorithm (SGD with gradient normalization). Achieving a BPC of 1.59, comparable to the 1.60 reported for the more complex MRNN with Hessian-free optimization [19], is a significant result. It suggests that architectural improvements can be as, or even more, important than sophisticated second-order optimizers for training RNNs.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clearly laid out, the model is defined precisely, and the experiments are logically structured to test the central hypotheses. The figures and tables are informative and effectively support the paper's claims.

4.  **Significance:** The work is highly significant. It provides a strong justification for using deep/stacked architectures for sequence modeling and offers a valuable set of techniques for analyzing the internal workings of such models. The finding that a simple SGD-based approach can be competitive with more complex optimizers had important practical implications for researchers and practitioners in the field. The analysis of learned temporal hierarchies has likely inspired much subsequent work on understanding representations in deep sequence models.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Confounded Training Procedures:** The best-performing model, DRNN-1O, uses a specialized incremental layer-wise training scheme, whereas the baseline RNN and the DRNN-AO are trained end-to-end from the start. This makes a direct comparison of the architectures somewhat difficult. The performance gain of the DRNN-1O could be attributed as much to this curriculum-learning strategy as to the architecture itself. The paper acknowledges this is "necessary to reach good performance," but it would strengthen the work to disentangle these two factors, for instance by applying a similar training budget but without the layer-wise staging.

2.  **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The paper only explores DRNNs with a fixed number of units per layer. While this simplifies the experimental setup, it leaves open questions about whether different layer sizes (e.g., a pyramidal or expansive structure) might be more effective. The choice of 5 layers is based on prior experiments that are not shown, which is reasonable given space constraints but slightly weakens the claim.

3.  **Novelty of the Architecture:** As the authors correctly point out, the idea of stacking recurrent layers was not entirely new at the time of writing [8]. The paper's primary novelty is therefore not in the architecture itself, but in the training methodology (for DRNN-1O) and, most importantly, the deep analysis. While this is a very strong contribution, the paper could be slightly more explicit in framing its novelty around the analysis rather than the architecture.

### Summary of evaluation

*   **Quality:** High. The claims are well-supported by extensive and carefully designed experiments. The analysis is technically sound and insightful.
*   **Clarity:** Excellent. The paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and easy to understand.
*   **Originality:** Good. While the base architecture is not entirely novel, the in-depth analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy, the specific training schemes, and the demonstration of SOTA performance with a simple optimizer represent a significant and original contribution.
*   **Significance:** High. The paper provides foundational evidence for the benefits of depth in recurrent models and offers a strong analytical framework for understanding them. The results have clear practical and scientific importance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a major benchmark with a simpler training algorithm than prior work.
*   Provides one of the first and most convincing in-depth analyses of how deep recurrent networks learn hierarchical temporal representations.
*   The experimental methodology for analyzing model internals (e.g., typo perturbation, parenthesis tracking) is exemplary and valuable to the community.
*   The paper is extremely well-written and presents a complete, self-contained piece of research.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   The comparison between the best model (DRNN-1O) and the baselines is confounded by a different training procedure, making it hard to isolate the effect of the architecture.
*   The architectural novelty is incremental, building directly on previously proposed stacked RNNs.

**Recommendation:** This is a strong paper that combines excellent empirical results with deep, insightful analysis. The strengths far outweigh the weaknesses. I would strongly recommend acceptance.
