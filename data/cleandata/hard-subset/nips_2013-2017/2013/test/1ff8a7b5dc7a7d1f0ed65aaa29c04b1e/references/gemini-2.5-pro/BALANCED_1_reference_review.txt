### Summary
This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which are constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The authors argue that unlike standard RNNs which primarily provide memory, this stacked architecture introduces a temporal and processing hierarchy, allowing different layers to operate on different timescales. The paper makes two primary contributions. First, it demonstrates that a 5-layer DRNN, trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and gradient clipping, can achieve state-of-the-art performance on a large-scale character-level language modeling task (Wikipedia corpus), matching results previously obtained with more complex Hessian-Free optimization methods. Second, and more central to the paper's thesis, it provides a detailed analysis of the trained models. Through a series of well-designed experiments, the authors show that a temporal hierarchy emerges during training, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory and capturing more abstract, long-range dependencies (e.g., parenthesis balancing), while lower layers handle more immediate, local statistics. This work builds upon prior uses of stacked RNNs, such as Graves et al. (2013) [8], but shifts the focus from purely performance-driven results to a more in-depth analysis of *why* such architectures are effective for hierarchically structured sequential data.

### Strengths (Arguments for acceptance)
1.  **Significant Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates that a relatively simple DRNN architecture trained with standard SGD can match the performance of a more complex multiplicative RNN trained with a sophisticated second-order method (Hessian-Free optimization [19]). This is a valuable and practical result, suggesting that architectural choices can be as important as the choice of optimizer for overcoming difficult training landscapes in large-scale sequence modeling.

2.  **Thorough and Insightful Analysis:** The main strength of this paper lies in its comprehensive analysis, which goes well beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The authors use several creative and effective methods to probe the internal workings of the DRNNs:
    *   The "typo perturbation" and "context switch" experiments (Figure 3) provide compelling quantitative evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, directly supporting the paper's central claim.
    *   The analysis of layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model (Figure 2, Table 2) gives valuable insight into the functional specialization of different layers (e.g., bottom layer for basic statistics, top layer for long-range structure).
    *   The parenthesis-closing task is an excellent case study demonstrating the model's ability to capture non-trivial, long-range dependencies over hundreds of time steps, linking its structure to a concrete linguistic phenomenon.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The motivation is well-established in the introduction, the model is defined precisely, and the experiments are described in sufficient detail to be understood. The figures and tables are informative and effectively support the paper's narrative.

4.  **Novelty of Contribution:** While the DRNN architecture itself is not entirely novel, the paper's primary contribution—the detailed analysis and evidence for an emergent temporal hierarchy—is original and significant. It provides a deeper understanding of deep sequential models and shifts the conversation from simply building deeper networks to understanding what they learn.

### Weaknesses (Arguments for rejection)
1.  **Missing Key Baselines:** The most significant weakness is the omission of a comparison to Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks [11], which are specifically designed to handle long-range dependencies. A comparison against both a single-layer large LSTM and a stacked LSTM with a similar parameter count is essential to contextualize the DRNN's performance and memory capabilities. Without this baseline, it is difficult to ascertain whether the observed benefits stem from depth in general or from the specific properties of stacked simple RNN units.

2.  **Limited Experimental Robustness:** The authors state that they only trained three network instantiations due to long training times, and argue that variance is low for such large models. While understandable, this lack of multiple runs with different random seeds makes it difficult to assess the statistical significance and robustness of the performance results. Reporting mean and standard deviation over even 2-3 runs would substantially strengthen the claims.

3.  **Under-analyzed Training Heuristics:** The paper introduces a layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O model, stating it was "necessary to reach good performance." This is an interesting finding, but it is presented as a heuristic without further analysis. An ablation study showing the performance degradation without this scheme would have been valuable. This leaves open questions about the training dynamics of these deep models and why this incremental approach is required.

### Constructive Suggestions for Improvement
-   **Include LSTM Baselines:** I strongly urge the authors to include experiments with a single-layer LSTM and a stacked LSTM, keeping the parameter count comparable to the DRNN models. This is a critical comparison that would significantly strengthen the paper by placing its results within the broader context of recurrent architectures designed for long-term memory.

-   **Report Variance:** Even if computationally expensive, running each experiment with 2-3 different random initializations and reporting the mean and standard deviation on the BPC metric would make the performance claims much more credible and robust.

-   **Elaborate on Training:** In Section 2.3, it would be beneficial to provide more justification for the layer-wise training of DRNN-1O. For instance, showing a learning curve for a DRNN-1O trained end-to-end from scratch that fails to converge would powerfully motivate the proposed incremental scheme.

-   **Minor Clarification:** The footnote regarding the character set (95 vs. 86 in [19]) claims the difference does not meaningfully affect cross-entropy. While likely true, providing a brief justification (e.g., citing the cumulative frequency of the extra characters) would add rigor.

### Decision
Overall, this is a well-executed and insightful paper that makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of deep recurrent models. The strengths, particularly the thorough and novel analysis of the model's internal dynamics, are significant. The main weakness is the lack of a crucial LSTM baseline. However, the analysis of the simple stacked RNN is compelling enough on its own to be of interest to the NIPS community. The paper successfully demonstrates not just that a deep architecture works, but provides strong evidence for *why* it works in the context of hierarchical data. Therefore, I lean towards acceptance.

**Recommendation: Weak Accept**
