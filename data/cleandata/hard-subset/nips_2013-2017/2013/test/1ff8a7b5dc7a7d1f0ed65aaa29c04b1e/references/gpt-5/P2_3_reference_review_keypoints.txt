- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) with two output topologies: DRNN-1O (top-layer output) and DRNN-AO (all-layer outputs summed).  
- Training uses SGD with per-update gradient normalization and an incremental layer-wise schedule for DRNN-1O.  
- Main task is large-scale character-level language modeling on Wikipedia with models matched at ~4.9M parameters.  
- DRNNs outperform plain RNNs and are competitive in bits-per-character (BPC) with multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization.  
- Analysis suggests a learned temporal hierarchy: upper layers capture longer-range statistics.  
- Empirical analyses include layer-output ablations, perturbation experiments (typo injection/context switching), and a generative-mode long-range parentheses test.  
- Paper connects to prior work on stacked recurrent nets and second-order RNN training; novelty is scaling stacked simple RNNs with SGD plus systematic time-scale analyses.  
- Strength: sensible experimental setup (parameter-matched baselines, explicit training schedule, large corpus).  
- Strength: strong analytical contribution triangulating temporal hierarchies across multiple probes.  
- Strength: transparent discussion of training dynamics and motivations for architectural choices.  
- Weakness: no LSTM/gated RNN baselines on the same task, limiting claims about long-range modeling advantages.  
- Weakness: very limited statistical support—only three networks trained, no seed variability or hyperparameter sensitivity reported.  
- Weakness: incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O likely induces long timescales, confounding architecture vs training effects.  
- Weakness: comparability issues (different character inventory, rough wall-clock comparisons, sparse reporting for larger models).  
- Weakness: truncated BPTT (200 effective steps) conflicts with claims about modeling long-range dependencies; sensitivity to truncation not explored.  
- Clarity suggestions: report exact BPCs with uncertainty, show validation curves, and include sensitivity analyses for gradient normalization vs clipping.  
- Clarity suggestions: provide more details on data preprocessing, vocabulary choices, BPTT windowing, and clarify axes/units in time-scale plots; fix incomplete references.  
- Originality: not a conceptual breakthrough but valuable demonstration that stacked simple RNNs + SGD can rival MRNNs; novelty in incremental training schedule and systematic analyses.  
- Significance: practical value for practitioners and insight into emergent temporal hierarchies, but impact is tempered by missing LSTM comparisons.  
- Pros: depth improves simple RNNs, competitive performance with simpler training, multifaceted time-scale analysis, and practical training insights.  
- Cons: missing gated baselines, potential confound from training schedule, sparse variability/sensitivity reporting, minor clarity/comparability issues.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—technically sound and insightful, but would be stronger with added LSTM baselines and clearer methodological reporting.
