- Investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) for character-level language modeling and claims they create a natural hierarchy of timescales.  
- Reports competitive/state-of-the-art performance for recurrent models on a Wikipedia corpus using only SGD.  
- Provides analysis of layer contributions and learned timescales, with illustrative figures (Figure 2 and Figure 3).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results despite relatively simple training methods.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and clear; equations and figures are helpful.  
- Core idea (stacking RNNs) is not entirely novel, so the contribution is somewhat incremental.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why DRNNs should exhibit hierarchical timescales.  
- Training details are under-specified, particularly the layer-wise training for DRNN-1O and the rationale for using five layers.  
- Relies on heuristics like gradient normalization without fully explaining their impact.  
- Experiments are limited by network size and computational resources, which constrains conclusions.  
- Results are demonstrated primarily on character-level language modeling, so generalizability to other sequence tasks is unclear.  
- Parenthesis-matching experiment is a clever analysis but not central to the main claim.  
- Strengths summarized: strong empirical performance, detailed temporal analysis, success with SGD, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: incremental novelty, limited theory, compute constraints, and task specificity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand theoretical discussion of timescale emergence, explicitly acknowledge and discuss network-size limitations, and clarify the layer-wise training procedure.
