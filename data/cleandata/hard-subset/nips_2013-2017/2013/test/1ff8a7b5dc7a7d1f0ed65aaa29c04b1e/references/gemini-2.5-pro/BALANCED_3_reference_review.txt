### Review

**Summary of the Paper**

This paper investigates the properties of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this depth introduces a temporal hierarchy, where different layers learn to operate on different timescales, making the model well-suited for complex sequential data like natural language. The authors motivate this by contrasting DRNNs with standard "shallow" RNNs, which lack an explicit hierarchical processing pipeline for each timestep, and with deep feed-forward networks, which lack memory.

The paper proposes and evaluates two DRNN variants: one where output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The latter is proposed to mitigate vanishing gradient issues during training. The models are trained on a challenging character-level language modeling task using a large Wikipedia corpus. Despite using a simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient clipping, the proposed DRNNs achieve performance competitive with the state-of-the-art at the time (Sutskever et al., 2011), which relied on a more complex model (MRNN) and a powerful second-order optimizer (Hessian-Free).

The primary contribution of this work is not just the performance, but a thorough and insightful analysis of the trained models. The authors conduct a series of diagnostic experiments to validate their temporal hierarchy hypothesis. These experiments demonstrate that (1) higher layers in the DRNN indeed develop longer memories, (2) the model effectively uses long-range context for prediction, and (3) it can capture specific long-term dependencies, such as tracking open parentheses over hundreds of characters. This work builds upon previous use of similar architectures (e.g., Graves et al., 2013), but provides a much-needed, in-depth analysis of *why* and *how* these deep recurrent structures are effective.

**Strengths**

1.  **Thorough and Insightful Analysis:** The paper's main strength lies in its exemplary analysis. The authors go far beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The experiments designed to probe the model's internal workings—such as the typo perturbation test to measure layer-wise timescales, the context-switching experiment to evaluate effective memory length, and the specific case study on parenthesis balancing—provide compelling evidence for the central claim that DRNNs learn a temporal hierarchy. This level of analysis is a significant contribution and sets a high standard for empirical deep learning research.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The DRNN models achieve performance that is on par with the state-of-the-art on a difficult benchmark, using only a simple SGD-based training procedure. This is a powerful result, as it demonstrates that much of the performance gain can be attributed to the architectural innovation rather than a sophisticated and computationally expensive optimization algorithm.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written. The motivation is clearly articulated, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup and results are presented in a logical and easy-to-follow manner. The figures are informative and effectively support the paper's narrative.

4.  **Significant Contribution:** This work provides a foundational understanding of deep recurrent models. By demonstrating and analyzing the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, it provides a clear conceptual framework for why stacking recurrent layers is beneficial. This insight has been influential and remains relevant for understanding modern, more complex recurrent architectures.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Comparison with Gated Architectures (e.g., LSTMs):** The experiments are conducted using simple `tanh` RNN units. While this serves to isolate the effect of depth, the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) architecture was already well-established for its ability to handle long-term dependencies. A direct comparison, such as a deep `tanh` RNN vs. a shallow LSTM, or a shallow `tanh` RNN vs. a deep LSTM, would have provided valuable context. It would help disentangle the benefits of architectural depth from the benefits of a specialized recurrent cell. The authors could consider this for future work to better situate the trade-offs between these two orthogonal approaches to improving RNNs.

2.  **Limited Number of Experimental Runs:** The authors state that due to long training times, each model was trained only once from a single initialization. While they argue that variance is low for such large models, this is a methodological limitation. Reporting results averaged over a few random seeds would have strengthened the claims of performance and made the comparisons more robust.

3.  **Training Details for DRNN-1O:** The layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O is an interesting heuristic that seems crucial for its success. However, the motivation for the specific training schedule (e.g., increasing the number of updates as layers are added) could be elaborated upon. While it is presented as a form of supervised pre-training, a more detailed discussion of why this particular approach was chosen and whether alternatives were considered would be beneficial.

4.  **Architectural Choices:** The paper uses a fixed number of layers (5) and a constant width across all layers. While this is a reasonable choice for a focused study, the work could be strengthened by a brief discussion or ablation study on the effect of depth (e.g., 2 vs. 5 vs. 10 layers) or width (e.g., constant vs. decreasing width in higher layers).

**Arguments Pro-Acceptance:**
*   Provides a seminal, in-depth analysis of deep recurrent neural networks, offering a clear explanation for their effectiveness.
*   Presents strong empirical results that are competitive with the state-of-the-art, achieved with a surprisingly simple training method.
*   The analytical techniques used in the paper are novel and serve as a model for how to rigorously evaluate and understand deep learning models.
*   The paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and addresses a significant and relevant problem.

**Arguments Con-Acceptance:**
*   The experiments are limited to a single type of recurrent unit (`tanh`) without comparison to LSTMs.
*   The results are based on single training runs, which raises minor concerns about robustness.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. Its primary value lies in the rigorous and insightful analysis that provides a clear conceptual understanding of deep recurrent architectures. The strengths, particularly the quality of the analysis and the strong empirical results, far outweigh the minor methodological weaknesses. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and its findings are of high importance. It clearly meets the standards for publication at NeurIPS.
