**Summary:**
This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The central thesis is that this depth induces a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process information on shorter timescales and higher layers capture longer-term dependencies. The authors test this hypothesis on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They propose and compare two variants: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and one with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). Through a series of well-designed analyses, they provide compelling evidence for the emergence of this temporal hierarchy and demonstrate that their models can capture very long-range dependencies, such as closing parentheses over hundreds of characters. Notably, using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), their models achieve performance comparable to the state-of-the-art at the time, which required a much more complex Hessian-free optimization method.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Novelty and Conceptual Contribution:** The primary strength of this paper lies in its bold conceptual claim and the rigorous analysis that supports it. While the idea of stacking RNNs was not entirely new (as the authors acknowledge, citing [8]), this work moves beyond a purely performance-driven application. It introduces the explicit and intuitive hypothesis of an emergent temporal hierarchy and then provides strong empirical evidence for it. The analysis of how different layers react to perturbations (the typo and context-switch experiments) is an excellent and novel way to probe the internal dynamics of the model. This shifts the focus from just "what works" to "why it works," which is a significant scientific contribution.

2.  **Significance of Results:** The paper demonstrates that a carefully chosen architecture can rival the performance of more sophisticated optimization algorithms. Achieving state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark using only SGD with gradient clipping is a powerful statement. It suggests that architectural innovation can be a more direct path to solving complex temporal tasks than solely focusing on overcoming optimization challenges in shallower models. This finding has important implications for the field, encouraging exploration of deep recurrent architectures.

3.  **Clarity and Quality of Analysis:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and the experiments are thoughtfully designed. The analysis goes far beyond simply reporting a final BPC score. The experiments to measure effective timescales (Figure 3) and the qualitative analysis of parenthesis closing (Figure 4 and subsequent test) are particularly insightful. The parenthesis example provides a concrete and easily understandable demonstration of the model's ability to learn non-trivial, long-range structural information, transforming an abstract concept into a tangible result.

4.  **Bold Approach:** The work prioritizes a new architectural idea and its analysis over exhaustive hyperparameter sweeps or multiple runs, which, while a weakness, also reflects a focus on the core scientific question. This focus on understanding the model's properties is refreshing and valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Limited Empirical Validation:** The most significant weakness is the limited number of experiments. The authors state they only trained three network instantiations (one for each architecture) due to computational cost. While their justification that large networks show little variance is plausible, it remains an unverified assertion. Reporting results from a single run makes it difficult to assess the stability and robustness of the training procedures and the final performance figures.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training Method:** The layer-wise training scheme required to get the DRNN-1O model to perform well feels somewhat ad-hoc. While it is an interesting finding in itself that this form of pre-training is effective, it detracts from the elegance of the architecture and suggests that training very deep RNNs without intermediate supervision is non-trivial. This point could have been explored in more detail.

3.  **Interpretability of the DRNN-AO analysis:** In the DRNN-AO model, the analysis of removing one layer's contribution to the output is interesting but potentially misleading, as the authors themselves note. The layers are trained jointly and their contributions to the softmax are not necessarily independent or additive in a simple way. The interpretation that intermediate layers are primarily for pre-processing is plausible but not definitively proven by this experiment.

**Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   The paper introduces a significant and intuitive concept: that architectural depth in RNNs leads to a functional hierarchy of timescales.
*   It provides strong, novel, and non-trivial empirical analysis to support this central claim, moving beyond simple performance metrics.
*   The results are highly significant, demonstrating that a novel architecture trained with a simple algorithm can be competitive with more complex approaches, advancing the state-of-the-art.
*   The work is a prime example of a bold idea-driven paper that opens up new avenues for research into understanding and designing deep temporal models.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   The experimental validation is thin, with results based on single training runs for each model configuration.
*   The training procedure for one of the main models relies on a specific, layer-wise heuristic, which raises questions about the general trainability of such deep architectures.

**Conclusion:**
Despite the limited number of experimental runs, the paper's strengths in novelty, conceptual depth, and the quality of its analysis are overwhelming. It presents a bold and compelling thesis about the function of deep recurrent networks and backs it up with well-designed experiments and impressive results. The weaknesses are minor in comparison to the significance of the contribution. This paper is not an incremental improvement; it provides a new lens through which to view and understand recurrent models, and is likely to inspire a great deal of follow-up work. It is an excellent fit for NIPS.
