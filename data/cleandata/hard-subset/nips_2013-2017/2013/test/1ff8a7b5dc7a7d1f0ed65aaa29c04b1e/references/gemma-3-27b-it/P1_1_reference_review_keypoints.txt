- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNN layers) that aim to create inherent multi-timescale temporal processing.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus using plain SGD, comparable to Hessian-free optimization.  
- Analyzes learned temporal hierarchies, showing different layers capture different time scales and can handle long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses matching).  
- Positions work appropriately relative to DNNs, RNNs, hierarchical RNNs, and LSTMs, and highlights relevance of comparison to Hessian-free methods.  
- Novel and intuitive architecture combining benefits of deep nets and recurrence to create multi-timescale representations.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrating practical effectiveness of DRNNs with simple training.  
- Provides in-depth analyses (typo perturbation, layer-wise time-scale examination, long-range dependency tests) beyond mere performance reporting.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures explaining architecture, training, and experiments.  
- Insight that DRNN-1O benefits from layer-wise output coupling and that deep training is challenging without such couplings.  
- Limited architectural exploration—focuses on fully connected tanh RNNs without systematic study of LSTM/GRU variants.  
- Scalability concerns due to testing only a few network sizes and anecdotal evidence that larger models still improve performance.  
- Analysis depth is limited—perturbation experiments could be broadened and time-scale quantification made more rigorous.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to other hierarchical RNN models to delineate relative strengths and weaknesses.  
- Insufficient discussion of training/hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rates, batch sizes, initialization).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, insightful analysis, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited architectural exploration, unclear scalability, and need for more comprehensive analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution that convincingly demonstrates DRNNs’ promise despite areas needing further work.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
