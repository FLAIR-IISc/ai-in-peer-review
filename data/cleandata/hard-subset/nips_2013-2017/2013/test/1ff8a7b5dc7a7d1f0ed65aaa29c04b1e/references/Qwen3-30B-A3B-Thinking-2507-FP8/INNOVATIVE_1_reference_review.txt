

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that stacks recurrent layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this creates a natural temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers, addressing a key limitation of standard RNNs that lack explicit multiple time scales. The paper is positioned as a synthesis of deep learning (DNNs) and recurrent networks (RNNs), with the claim that DRNNs combine the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal memory of RNNs.

The paper builds on prior work on hierarchical RNNs [5, 8] but makes a bold conceptual leap by framing DRNNs as a natural solution to the problem of modeling multiple time scales in sequential data. The authors' key insight is that stacking RNNs inherently creates different time scales at different layers, with lower layers handling short-term dependencies and higher layers handling longer-term dependencies. This is supported by experiments on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus, where DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD (while prior state-of-the-art required Hessian-free training).

The paper's most significant contribution is the conceptual framework of "temporal hierarchy" in DRNNs. The authors provide compelling evidence for this through several experiments: (1) measuring layer importance by removing output contributions, (2) analyzing how quickly layers forget perturbations (typo experiments), and (3) demonstrating the ability to close parentheses after long phrases. These experiments collectively show that DRNNs naturally develop different time scales across layers, which is a novel and important insight about how deep recurrent architectures function.

The paper's strengths lie in its bold conceptual contribution and the clear demonstration of the temporal hierarchy phenomenon. The authors successfully bridge the conceptual gap between DNNs (hierarchical processing) and RNNs (temporal memory), creating a new perspective on recurrent network architecture. The analysis of how different layers contribute to the task (e.g., bottom layers handling basic statistics, top layers capturing word-length sequences) is particularly insightful and provides valuable theoretical grounding for the DRNN concept.

While the paper is conceptually strong, it has several methodological limitations. The experimental validation is limited to a single task (character-level language modeling), and the authors only tested three network instantiations due to computational constraints. The performance gains over standard RNNs are modest (DRNN-1O achieves 1.449 BPC vs. RNN's 1.522 BPC), and the DRNNs match prior state-of-the-art achieved with much more sophisticated training methods (Hessian-free optimization). The paper also doesn't compare to more recent RNN variants like LSTMs or GRUs, which would have strengthened the comparison to current best practices.

The training methodology for DRNN-1O (incremental layer-wise training) is described as a practical workaround rather than a theoretically grounded approach. While the authors acknowledge this is a limitation, the paper doesn't explore why this approach works or how to make it more principled.

**Strengths:**
- Bold conceptual contribution: introduces the idea of temporal hierarchy as a natural property of stacked RNNs
- Clear experimental evidence for the temporal hierarchy phenomenon (typo experiments, parentheses closing)
- Insightful analysis of layer contributions to the task
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD
- Well-written and clearly organized

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited experimental validation (only one task, three network instantiations)
- Performance gains are modest compared to prior state-of-the-art
- Doesn't compare to more recent RNN architectures (LSTMs, GRUs)
- Training methodology for DRNN-1O is described as a heuristic rather than principled
- Limited theoretical justification for why temporal hierarchy emerges

**Recommendation:** Accept

This paper makes a significant conceptual contribution that aligns with NIPS' emphasis on novel ideas and bold approaches. While the experimental validation is somewhat limited and the performance gains are modest, the core insight about temporal hierarchy in DRNNs is novel, well-supported, and potentially transformative for how we think about recurrent architectures. The paper successfully bridges a conceptual gap between DNNs and RNNs, introducing a new way to think about hierarchical processing in sequential data.

The reviewer is willing to overlook the methodological limitations (limited experiments, modest performance gains) because the paper introduces a significant new concept that opens up new research directions. The temporal hierarchy insight has clear potential for application in other sequential tasks requiring multiple time scales, and the paper provides a solid foundation for future work in this area.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a strong case for the novelty of its approach. While not perfect, it represents a significant contribution to the field that advances our understanding of how deep recurrent architectures function. The authors have successfully demonstrated a new conceptual framework that could influence future work on recurrent neural networks.
