**Summary**

This paper investigates the properties of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), which are constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The authors propose that this architecture naturally facilitates a temporal hierarchy, with higher layers processing information over longer time scales. They introduce two variants: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). The models are evaluated on a large-scale character-level language modeling task (Wikipedia corpus), where they achieve state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), rivaling a previous, more complex model trained with Hessian-free optimization. The main contribution of the paper is not just the performance, but a detailed analysis of the trained models, providing empirical evidence that the stacked layers learn to operate at different time scales and can capture very long-term dependencies, such as closing parentheses over hundreds of characters.

**Strengths**

1.  **Thorough Analysis:** The paper's primary strength lies in its in-depth analysis of the DRNN's internal dynamics. Instead of merely presenting a final performance metric, the authors conduct a series of well-designed experiments to probe the learned representations. The "typo perturbation" and "context switch" experiments provide compelling quantitative evidence for the central hypothesis that DRNNs develop a temporal hierarchy. The qualitative analysis of parenthesis balancing is also an effective demonstration of long-term memory.
2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates that a relatively straightforward architecture (stacked RNNs) trained with a simple optimizer (SGD with gradient clipping) can match the performance of more complex models (MRNN) and advanced optimizers (Hessian-Free) on a difficult benchmark. This is a significant and valuable result for the community, suggesting that architectural depth can be a powerful alternative to sophisticated optimization techniques.
3.  **Clarity of Motivation:** The motivation for exploring DRNNs is clearly articulated in the introduction, contrasting them with standard RNNs and DNNs and hypothesizing their suitability for hierarchical temporal data. The two proposed output architectures (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) are well-justified.

**Weaknesses**

While the paper presents interesting ideas and strong results, it suffers from a substantial number of methodological and presentational flaws that undermine its overall quality.

1.  **Lack of Experimental Rigor:** The empirical claims are based on a single training run for each of the three models. The assertion in footnote 3 that "there is very little difference in performance for different initialisations" for such large networks is a strong claim made without any supporting evidence. For a result to be considered reliable, performance should be averaged over multiple runs with different random seeds, and standard deviations should be reported. Without this, it is impossible to know if the reported performance differences are statistically significant or merely due to fortunate initialization. Furthermore, several key experimental details are relegated to asides or omitted entirely (e.g., the results for an untrained DRNN to prove time scales are learned, the experiments justifying the choice of 5 layers).

2.  **Unjustified Methodological Choices:** Several crucial details of the training setup are non-standard and lack justification.
    *   The initialization of input weights `Z_1` from a `N(0, 1)` distribution is highly unusual. With a standard deviation of 1, this could easily lead to exploding activations in the first layer, especially before any normalization. Standard initialization schemes (e.g., Glorot or He) are designed to prevent such issues, and the deviation from these norms must be justified.
    *   The incremental layer-wise training for the DRNN-1O is presented as "necessary to reach good performance." However, no baseline experiment is shown where the DRNN-1O is trained end-to-end from the start. This makes it impossible to evaluate the actual impact or necessity of this complex training procedure.
    *   The choice of a linear learning rate decay is arbitrary. While it clearly works, there is no discussion of why this schedule was chosen over more common alternatives like step decay or exponential decay.

3.  **Poor Presentation and Attention to Detail:** The manuscript displays a notable lack of care in its preparation, which detracts from its scientific contribution.
    *   The **reference section** is in a state of disarray. Formatting is wildly inconsistent across entries. Many citations are incomplete, missing authors, years, conference/journal names, or page numbers (e.g., [1], [2], [3], [4]). Some entries contain stray commas (e.g., "In Proceedings...,, , ,"). Reference [7] is cited as "To appear" when the paper was published in 2013. This level of sloppiness is unacceptable for a top-tier conference publication and suggests a rushed submission.
    *   There are minor but frequent grammatical errors and awkward phrasings (e.g., "The last decade, machine learning has seen...", "we may need complex, hierarchical processing of the current network input, but this information only passes through one layer...", "an honest comparison is hard to make").
    *   Important details are hidden in footnotes. For instance, footnote 5 casually mentions a much larger model that achieves a better result (1.472 BPC), but this significant result is not properly presented or analyzed in the main text.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Presents strong empirical results on a standard benchmark, achieving SOTA with a simple method.
*   Provides a valuable and insightful analysis of the internal workings of deep RNNs, supporting the hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy.
*   The analysis techniques (e.g., typo perturbation) are sound and could be adopted by other researchers.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
*   Severe lack of experimental rigor (single runs, no error bars), making the central performance claims scientifically weak.
*   Key methodological choices (initialization, layer-wise training) are poorly justified and not compared to standard baselines.
*   The paper is marred by extremely poor formatting, especially in the reference section, indicating a lack of care unbecoming of a NIPS paper.

**Recommendation: Reject**

Despite the paper's novel analysis and impressive headline results, the numerous flaws in experimental rigor and presentation are too significant to overlook. The lack of multiple experimental runs makes the results difficult to trust, and the unsubstantiated claims about methodological necessity weaken the contributions. The abysmal state of the reference section is symptomatic of a general lack of attention to detail. The paper contains the seeds of an excellent contribution, but it requires a major revision to address these fundamental issues before it can be considered for publication.
