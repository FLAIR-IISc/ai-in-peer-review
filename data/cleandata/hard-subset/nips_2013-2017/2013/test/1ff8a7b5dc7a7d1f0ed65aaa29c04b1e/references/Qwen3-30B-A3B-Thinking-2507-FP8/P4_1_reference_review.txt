

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine the hierarchical structure of deep neural networks with the temporal processing capabilities of RNNs. The authors argue that standard RNNs lack explicit support for multiple time scales in temporal processing, while DRNNs naturally develop a temporal hierarchy where each layer operates at different time scales. The paper focuses on character-level language modeling as a task to validate this claim, using a Wikipedia text corpus. The authors propose two variants: DRNN-1O (output connections only at the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output connections at all layers), and provide theoretical analysis, training procedures, and extensive experimental validation.

The paper relates to previous work by distinguishing DRNNs from hierarchical RNN architectures proposed in [3,5,6], which focused on artificial tasks, and from [8], which used stacked bidirectional LSTMs for speech recognition but provided limited architectural analysis. The authors position their work as providing deeper understanding of the architectural benefits of stacking RNNs.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates strong technical soundness. The theoretical analysis of hidden state evolution (Section 2.1) is clear and well-defined. The experimental design is thorough, with appropriate baselines (standard RNN), metrics (BPC), and ablation studies to validate the importance of different architectural components. The authors conduct multiple experiments to verify the temporal hierarchy claim: typo-perturbation tests (Figure 3), context-switching experiments, and parentheses-closing tests (Figure 4). The paper acknowledges limitations, such as the need for layer-wise training in DRNN-1O, and the DRNN's inability to handle nested parentheses. The training methodology (Section 2.3) is well-described, and the use of stochastic gradient descent without second-order optimization is justified by their results. The experimental results are well-supported by the data presented in Table 1 and Figures 2-4.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The introduction effectively establishes the motivation by contrasting DRNNs with standard RNNs and DNNs. The architecture is clearly explained with mathematical formalism (Section 2.1) and visualized in Figure 1. The experimental setup (Section 3) is detailed enough for replication, including specific hyperparameters, training procedures, and dataset characteristics. The figures (Figures 1-4) are well-designed and effectively communicate the key findings. The writing is concise and technical without being overly dense, making the paper accessible to the NIPS audience.

**Originality:** The core idea of DRNNs as a natural way to create temporal hierarchies through stacking RNNs is novel. The paper makes a clear distinction from previous hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6,8] by providing detailed analysis of the temporal hierarchy rather than just achieving good performance. The authors' experimental validation of time-scale differentiation across layers through the typo-perturbation test (Figure 3) represents a significant contribution to understanding how stacked RNNs operate. The paper also contributes to the ongoing discussion about training algorithms by showing that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art results with standard SGD, whereas previous work required Hessian-free optimization [19]. This is a meaningful contribution to the field.

**Significance:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (Table 1), which is a significant result. The evidence for temporal hierarchy development in DRNNs (Figures 2-4) provides theoretical insights that could inform future work on sequence modeling architectures. The parentheses-closing test (Figure 4) offers compelling evidence of DRNNs' ability to model long-term dependencies, which is a significant advancement over standard RNNs. The work has clear potential to influence future research on training deep recurrent architectures and could be extended to other sequence modeling tasks beyond language modeling.

**Strengths:**
- Clear theoretical framework for DRNNs with well-defined mathematical formulation
- Comprehensive experimental validation of temporal hierarchy
- State-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using only SGD
- Thorough ablation studies to validate architectural choices
- Detailed analysis of time-scale differentiation across layers
- Appropriate comparison to relevant prior work

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper does not directly compare DRNNs to other hierarchical RNN variants beyond [3,5,6,8]
- The explanation of why DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O is not deeply explored
- The training time (10 days per network) is substantial, but the paper doesn't discuss potential optimizations for scalability
- The paper focuses on character-level modeling, but doesn't thoroughly discuss transferability to other sequence tasks

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The paper makes a clear, novel contribution to understanding recurrent network architectures
- The experimental validation of temporal hierarchy is thorough and convincing
- The results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD
- The paper provides significant theoretical insights beyond just achieving good performance
- The writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- The scope is somewhat narrow, focusing only on character-level language modeling
- The performance gains over standard RNNs are modest (BPC improvement of 0.05-0.1)
- The paper doesn't provide a comprehensive comparison to other hierarchical RNN approaches

The paper makes a significant contribution to the understanding of recurrent network architectures and their temporal processing capabilities. The experimental validation of the temporal hierarchy claim is particularly strong, and the results provide valuable insights into how stacked RNNs process information across different time scales. The work is technically sound, well-written, and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. The modest performance gains are justified by the significant theoretical insights provided. The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it advances our understanding of neural network architectures with clear experimental validation.
