- Paper studies stacked vanilla tanh RNNs with two output designs: top-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layer (DRNN-AO).  
- Trained with SGD, global gradient normalization, and linear learning-rate decay; DRNN-1O uses incremental layer-wise training.  
- On large Wikipedia character-level task, both DRNNs beat a matched-parameter single-layer RNN and approach MRNN+HF bpc.  
- Paper provides temporal analyses: per-layer time scales (typo perturbation, context-switch), per-layer readout ablations, and long-range dependency tests (parentheses).  
- Models and analyses are technically sound; training heuristics and diagnostics are sensible.  
- Empirical results consistently favor DRNNs over single-layer RNNs and support claims of multi-scale structure learning.  
- Novelty is limited architecturally—stacked RNNs and multi-layer readouts are incremental relative to prior work.  
- Key baseline omissions: no LSTM/GRU (single or stacked) comparisons under matched budgets.  
- MRNN+HF comparison is imperfect due to different training algorithms and unclear training/compute parity.  
- DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison may be unfair since they use different training schedules and data passes.  
- Limited robustness: only three model runs, no seed variance reporting, no extensive hyperparameter or regularization exploration.  
- Some design choices lack justification (zero-initialized outputs, differing weight initializations) and potential scale mismatches are not analyzed.  
- Truncated BPTT length is not clearly reported and may conflict with claims of very long-term modeling.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; architecture, training, and experiments are described precisely.  
- Suggested clarifications: exact epochs/data passes, wall-clock cost, rationale for initialization, and explicit BPTT truncation length.  
- Main novelty: empirical demonstration that deep vanilla RNNs trained with simple SGD can approach MRNN+HF, plus thorough layer-wise temporal analyses.  
- Significance: shows depth helps character-level modeling without gating and offers useful temporal diagnostics for the community.  
- Impact limited by missing LSTM/GRU baselines and modest architectural innovation.  
- Pros: solid empirical gains, competitive simpler training, insightful temporal analyses, and clear presentation.  
- Cons: limited novelty, missing key baselines, unequal training schedules, minimal hyperparameter/variance analysis, and unexplained design choices.  
- Questions for authors include comparisons to stacked LSTM/GRU, equalizing data passes between DRNN variants, exact truncated BPTT length and sensitivity, initialization rationale and alternatives, and result variability across seeds.  
- Suggested improvements: add LSTM/GRU baselines, report multiple seeds, disclose compute/data/pass budgets, explore per-layer sizes/residuals, compare gradient normalization vs clipping, and release code/models.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending stronger baselines and clarification of training budgets; reviewer confidence: medium.
