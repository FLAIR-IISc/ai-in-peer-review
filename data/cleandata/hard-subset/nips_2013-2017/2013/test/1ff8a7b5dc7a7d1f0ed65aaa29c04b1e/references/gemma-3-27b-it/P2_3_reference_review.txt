## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper explores the use of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) – essentially stacking RNNs to create a temporal hierarchy – for character-level language modeling. The authors argue that this architecture naturally captures multi-scale temporal dependencies in language, a weakness of standard RNNs. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia corpus using stochastic gradient descent, comparable to results previously achieved with more complex optimization methods (Hessian-free optimization).  Crucially, the paper goes beyond purely empirical results and attempts to analyze the learned temporal hierarchy within the DRNN, investigating time scales and the role of individual layers.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture, while building on established concepts (DNNs and RNNs), represents a reasonable and potentially fruitful combination, addressing a recognized limitation of standard RNNs regarding multi-scale temporal processing.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD) is a significant accomplishment. This suggests the architecture is effective and potentially easier to train than competing models.
* **Analysis of Learned Hierarchy:** The attempts to analyze the internal dynamics of the DRNN – identifying time scales at different layers, investigating the contribution of each layer to output, and examining its ability to handle long-range dependencies (parentheses) – are a key strength.  This goes beyond simply demonstrating good performance and provides some insight into *why* the architecture works.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, architecture, and experimental setup.
* **Thorough experimentation:** The authors perform several experiments, ablation studies, and analyses to back up their claims.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Novelty:** While the combination of DNN and RNN layers is a valid approach, the core idea of stacking RNNs isn't entirely novel (acknowledged by the authors referencing [5, 8]). The primary contribution lies in the analysis and empirical demonstration of its effectiveness in a specific task.
* **Modest Theoretical Foundation:**  The paper largely relies on intuition and empirical observations to justify the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. A more formal analysis of *why* stacking RNNs should lead to different time scales would strengthen the contribution.  The explanation that "older data passes through the recursion more often" feels somewhat superficial.
* **Comparison to Baselines:**  While the authors compare to previous work, the comparison to the RNN is somewhat limited.  A more comprehensive benchmark including different RNN variants (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs) trained with comparable resources would be valuable.
* **Training Details:** The descriptions of training parameter choices are fairly minimal. The justification for choosing the learning rate schedule and the initial learning rates for different layers is not fully explained.
* **Limited Generalizability:** The analysis is focused on a single task (character-level language modeling). It remains unclear whether the observed benefits of DRNNs would generalize to other sequence modeling problems.
* **Figure Quality**: Figure 3 could be clearer with more explicit labeling of the axes and units.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims based on experimental results. However, the lack of a strong theoretical underpinning prevents it from being truly exceptional. The work is complete within its chosen scope. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations. **Score: 4/5**
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally very clear and well-organized.  The notation is reasonable, and the explanation of the architecture is easy to follow. **Score: 5/5**
* **Originality:** The core idea has precedents, but the specific architecture and the depth of analysis contribute to a degree of novelty. The application to character-level language modeling and the insights gained are valuable. **Score: 3.5/5**
* **Significance:** The results are important, demonstrating a promising architecture for sequence modeling. The analysis provides valuable insights into the dynamics of deep recurrent networks. Other researchers are likely to build on this work, especially in the context of tasks requiring multi-scale temporal reasoning. **Score: 4/5**

**Arguments Pro & Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging task.
* Provides a thoughtful analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy within the DRNN.
* Offers a potentially useful architecture for sequence modeling.
* Well-written and clear presentation.

**Con:**

* Limited theoretical justification for the observed phenomena.
* Incremental advance over existing work, rather than a groundbreaking innovation.
* Limited generalization demonstrated beyond the single task.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. While it doesn't represent a radical departure from existing research, it makes a valuable contribution by demonstrating the effectiveness of DRNNs for character-level language modeling and providing a compelling analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy. The empirical results are strong, the writing is clear, and the insights gained are likely to be of interest to the NIPS community.  Addressing the minor weaknesses (e.g., strengthening the theoretical justification and providing more details on training) would further enhance the paper's quality.
