

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel architecture called Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combines elements of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The key idea is to stack RNNs such that each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a hierarchy of time scales where lower layers process shorter time scales and higher layers process longer time scales. The authors argue that this architecture better captures temporal hierarchies present in language data, such as the relationship between phonemes (short time scale) and sentences (long time scale). They evaluate their approach on character-level language modeling using the Wikipedia text corpus, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), whereas previous state-of-the-art results required more complex Hessian-free optimization. The paper also provides experimental evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy across layers through various experiments including typo perturbation analysis and parentheses closing tasks.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound and well-executed. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of the DRNN architecture (Section 2.1), including the update equations for hidden states and output generation. The experimental setup is detailed, with specific network configurations, training parameters, and evaluation metrics (BPC) that allow for reproducibility. The results are well-supported by multiple lines of evidence: performance comparisons, ablation studies on layer contributions, and experiments measuring temporal context lengths.

The authors are careful in their evaluation, acknowledging limitations such as the DRNN-AO (all layers connected to output) performing slightly worse than DRNN-1O (only top layer connected to output), and providing explanations for this counterintuitive result. They also conduct ablation studies to verify that the model is efficiently trained (by removing layer contributions and measuring performance degradation).

However, the paper lacks sufficient theoretical justification for why stacking RNNs creates the observed temporal hierarchy. While the experimental evidence is strong, the paper would benefit from a more formal explanation of how the architecture inherently develops different time scales across layers. This would strengthen the theoretical foundation of their claims.

## Clarity

The paper is well-written and well-organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem by highlighting the limitations of standard RNNs in handling temporal hierarchies, and the paper flows logically from problem statement to architecture to experiments. The figures (particularly Figure 1, which illustrates the DRNN architecture) are clear and helpful.

The authors provide sufficient detail about their experimental setup to allow for reproduction: specific network sizes, training parameters (learning rates, number of training iterations), and hardware specifications. The writing is clear and accessible to the NIPS audience, with technical details presented in a way that balances mathematical precision with readability.

One minor weakness is that the explanation for why DRNN-1O outperforms DRNN-AO (despite the authors' stated motivation for including multiple output connections) is not fully convincing. The authors suggest it might be related to the training methodology rather than the architecture itself, but this could be clarified.

## Originality

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks. While the idea of stacking RNNs isn't entirely new (the authors cite previous work [3, 5, 6, 8]), the specific focus on how this stacking creates a natural temporal hierarchy and the experimental evidence supporting this claim is novel.

The paper differentiates itself from previous work by explicitly analyzing how the architecture creates different time scales across layers, rather than just focusing on performance improvements. The comparison to hierarchical RNN approaches like [5] (which uses subsampling) is clear, and the authors provide a compelling argument for why their approach is better suited for capturing temporal hierarchies in natural language data.

However, the paper could have provided a more thorough comparison to recent hierarchical RNN work (e.g., [12, 13, 14] cited in the references but not fully compared). While the authors mention that the architecture has been used in [8], they don't clearly distinguish how their analysis differs from the previous work.

## Significance

The paper makes a significant practical contribution by demonstrating that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only SGD, whereas previous state-of-the-art results required more complex Hessian-free optimization. This simplification of the training process is valuable for practitioners.

The insights about temporal hierarchies in DRNNs have broader significance for understanding how deep architectures process temporal data. The paper provides concrete evidence that stacking RNNs naturally creates a hierarchy of time scales, which could inform future work on designing architectures for tasks with multi-scale temporal dependencies.

The parentheses closing experiment is particularly compelling as it demonstrates the DRNN's ability to model long-term dependencies beyond what's possible with standard RNNs. The fact that the DRNN-AO successfully closes parentheses in 88% of cases (compared to 72% for the standard RNN) is a strong demonstration of the model's ability to capture long-range dependencies.

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a strong contribution to the understanding of temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks. The experimental evidence supporting the claims about temporal hierarchy development is thorough and convincing, and the practical demonstration of state-of-the-art performance with a simpler training approach is significant.

The paper has several strengths: clear motivation, well-executed experiments, thorough analysis, and practical relevance. The main weaknesses are the lack of theoretical justification for the temporal hierarchy phenomenon and insufficient comparison to recent hierarchical RNN work.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS. It makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks, provides strong experimental evidence for its claims, and demonstrates practical benefits through state-of-the-art performance on a standard language modeling task. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important question in the field.

## Strengths
- Clear and well-motivated contribution to understanding temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks
- Thorough experimental validation with multiple lines of evidence (performance, ablation studies, temporal context analysis)
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD, a significant simplification over previous approaches
- Provides compelling evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy across layers through the typo perturbation and parentheses closing experiments
- Careful evaluation of both strengths and weaknesses of the approach

## Weaknesses
- Limited theoretical justification for why stacking RNNs creates different time scales across layers
- Insufficient comparison to recent hierarchical RNN approaches in the literature
- Unclear explanation for why DRNN-1O (only top layer connected to output) outperforms DRNN-AO (all layers connected to output)
- Limited discussion of scalability to larger datasets or more complex tasks beyond character-level language modeling

## Final Recommendation: Accept

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field with clear experimental evidence supporting its claims about temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks. The practical demonstration of state-of-the-art performance using only SGD is particularly significant. While there are some areas for improvement (primarily theoretical justification and comparison to more recent work), the paper's strengths substantially outweigh these weaknesses.
