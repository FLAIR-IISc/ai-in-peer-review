- Paper studies stacked vanilla RNNs (DRNN) with two output variants: top-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO).  
- Trained character-level language models on large Wikipedia with SGD, gradient-norm normalization, and linear LR decay.  
- Compared to same-parameter single-layer RNN and published HF-trained MRNN results; DRNNs outperform single-layer and approach MRNN.  
- Empirical analyses (typo perturbation, context switches, layer ablations, parentheses probe) support that depth induces a temporal hierarchy of time scales.  
- DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO differ in how time scales emerge across layers.  
- Main novelties: systematic comparison of output variants, an SGD training recipe (including incremental layer-wise training), and empirical time-scale analyses on real text.  
- Strength: solid empirical results showing depth helps at fixed parameter budget; DRNN-1O performs best.  
- Strength: careful and informative temporal analyses that collectively support claims about long-range modeling.  
- Strength: training details (batching, BPTT, init, counts) improve reproducibility.  
- Weakness: major missing baselines — no LSTM/GRU or stacked LSTM comparisons despite relevance.  
- Weakness: “state-of-the-art” claim is limited to similarly sized recurrent nets and confounded by dataset/training differences.  
- Weakness: lack of ablation isolating incremental layer-wise pretraining, gradient-norm normalization, and LR decay effects.  
- Weakness: no validation set or early stopping described; LR decaying to zero is unusual and concerning.  
- Weakness: limited experiments (only three models) and no variance across random seeds reported.  
- Presentation issues: ambiguous Equation (3), messy footnotes, and incomplete figure captions.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but needs fixes for Eq.(3), LR schedule description, and dataset preprocessing/details.  
- Originality: moderate — incremental contribution from simple DRNN variants and empirical analyses rather than novel architectures.  
- Significance: moderate — useful insights and tools, but limited impact without gated-architecture comparisons or stronger baselines.  
- Pros: demonstrates depth helps, offers insightful time-scale analyses, provides a practical training recipe, and evaluates on large realistic corpus.  
- Cons: missing strong baselines and ablations, reproducibility/rigor concerns (no dev set, limited runs), and overstated SOTA implications.  
- Suggested improvements: add stacked LSTM/GRU baselines, optimizer ablations (HF/Adam), ablate training tricks, use held-out validation and multiple runs, clarify Eq.(3)/initialization, and add quantitative time-scale metrics.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — useful empirical study with interesting analyses but needs stronger baselines and clearer ablations to be a stronger contribution.
