**NIPS Review**

**Summary:**
This paper proposes and analyzes the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture formed by stacking multiple RNN layers such that the hidden state of one layer serves as the input sequence for the next. The central hypothesis is that this layered structure naturally induces a temporal hierarchy, where deeper layers operate on longer timescales. The authors evaluate this architecture on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They introduce two variants: one with an output connection only at the final layer (DRNN-1O) and another with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). The results show that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex models and second-order optimization methods, using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The paper's main contribution lies not just in the performance but in the detailed analysis that provides strong evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy and the model's ability to capture long-range dependencies.

**Evaluation:**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by a series of thoughtful and insightful experiments. Rather than simply reporting a final bits-per-character (BPC) score, the authors conduct a thorough analysis to validate their architectural hypothesis.
*   **Strengths:** The analytical experiments are a standout feature. The "typo perturbation" test (Fig. 3, left) provides compelling quantitative evidence that higher layers learn longer timescales. The analysis of parenthesis closing (Fig. 4) is an excellent case study demonstrating the model's capacity to learn non-trivial, long-range structural dependencies. The qualitative analysis of generated text after ablating specific layers (Table 2) offers further insight into the functional specialization of the layers (e.g., the bottom layer handling basic statistics, the top layer handling longer-term structure).
*   **Weaknesses:** The empirical evaluation, while deep, is not particularly broad. The authors state they only tested three network instantiations due to long training times. While this is understandable for large-scale models, it makes the results susceptible to variance from initialization or other stochastic factors. The claim that "there is very little difference in performance for different initialisations" is an assertion that would be stronger if supported by data, even from smaller preliminary experiments. The layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O is presented as a practical necessity but feels somewhat ad-hoc; a more detailed analysis of why this specific curriculum is crucial would strengthen the paper.

**Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The motivation for DRNNs is well-articulated, contrasting them with standard RNNs and DNNs. The model equations are concise and the architecture is clearly depicted in Figure 1. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail to understand the methodology. The results are presented logically, building from overall performance to the detailed analysis of the network's internal dynamics. The figures and tables are informative and support the main arguments effectively. A minor point for improvement would be to provide more justification for certain hyperparameter choices, such as the number of layers (5), which is noted to be based on results not shown.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new, and the authors are honest about this, citing prior work [5, 6]. However, this paper's originality lies in several key aspects:
1.  **Scale and Task:** It is one of the first works to successfully apply and analyze such an architecture on a large-scale, real-world benchmark (character-level language modeling), demonstrating its practical utility.
2.  **In-depth Analysis:** The primary contribution is the novel and comprehensive analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy. Previous work often focused on performance, whereas this paper provides a deep dive into *how* and *why* the architecture is effective, connecting its structure to the hierarchical nature of the data.
3.  **Training Methodology:** While simple, the demonstration that these deep models can be trained effectively with basic SGD (and a gradient normalization trick) is a valuable contribution, especially at a time when more complex second-order methods were thought necessary for training deep RNNs on long sequences.

**Significance:** This work is highly significant.
*   It convincingly demonstrates that architectural depth (in the stacking sense) is a powerful mechanism for learning complex temporal dependencies in sequential data, much like spatial depth is for image data.
*   The results advanced the state of the art in language modeling, showing that a relatively simple architecture could match the performance of more complex models like the multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization. This makes high-performance sequential modeling more accessible.
*   The analytical techniques presented in the paper (e.g., measuring state perturbation decay across layers) provide a valuable blueprint for researchers seeking to understand the internal workings of their own deep sequential models. This emphasis on analysis and interpretation over pure performance is a major strength and has had a lasting impact on the field.
*   The paper's success helped popularize the concept of stacked/deep RNNs, which have since become a standard component in many state-of-the-art sequence processing systems (e.g., in translation, speech recognition).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  Achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult and widely recognized benchmark.
2.  Provides a novel and exceptionally thorough analysis that gives strong evidence for its core hypothesis about temporal hierarchies.
3.  Demonstrates that deep recurrent models can be trained with simple SGD, a significant practical contribution.
4.  The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides insights that are valuable to both practitioners and researchers.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
1.  The experimental validation is based on a very small number of runs (one per major architecture), which raises concerns about the robustness of the specific performance numbers.
2.  The incremental layer-wise training for one of the models is not deeply motivated, appearing more as a necessary trick than a principled method.
3.  The paper does not include a comparison to stacked LSTMs, which would have been a natural and highly relevant baseline given the focus on long-term dependencies.

**Overall Recommendation:**
Despite the limited number of experimental runs, the strengths of this paper far outweigh its weaknesses. The combination of strong empirical performance with a deep and insightful analysis of the model's internal dynamics makes it a landmark contribution. It not only presents a powerful model but also teaches us something fundamental about how deep architectures can learn hierarchical representations of time. This work has clear and lasting value for the NIPS community. I strongly recommend acceptance.
