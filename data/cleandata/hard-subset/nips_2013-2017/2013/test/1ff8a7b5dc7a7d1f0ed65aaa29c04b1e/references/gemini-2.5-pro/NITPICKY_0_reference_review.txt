**Summary**
This paper introduces and analyzes deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), which are constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The authors propose that this architecture naturally creates a temporal hierarchy, with different layers operating on different time scales, making it well-suited for tasks with complex temporal dependencies like character-level language modeling. Two variants are explored: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and one with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). Experiments on a large Wikipedia corpus show that both DRNN models outperform a standard RNN and achieve performance competitive with the state-of-the-art established by more complex Hessian-free optimization methods. The paper provides an in-depth analysis of the learned models, demonstrating the emergence of a layer-wise temporal hierarchy and the ability to capture long-range dependencies such as matching parentheses.

**Strengths**
- The paper presents a compelling motivation for deep recurrent architectures and provides a thorough empirical analysis that goes beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The investigation into the learned time scales of different layers is a significant contribution and provides valuable insight into the model's behavior.
- The performance achieved is impressive. Reaching a level of performance comparable to that of models trained with Hessian-free optimization using only SGD (with gradient normalization) is a noteworthy result and contributes to the discussion on the trade-offs between first- and second-order optimization methods for large-scale learning.
- The qualitative analyses, such as the text generation experiments and the study of parenthesis-closing, are well-conceived and provide concrete evidence for the model's ability to learn both short-term syntactic structures and long-term dependencies.

**Weaknesses**
Despite the interesting ideas and strong results, the paper is marred by a substantial number of flaws in its methodology, clarity, and presentation, which undermine the confidence in its conclusions.

**1. Clarity and Presentation:**
- **Mathematical Formalism:** There are critical errors and ambiguities in the mathematical descriptions. The learning rate update in Equation (3) is given as `η0 (- j/T)`, which is clearly incorrect and should presumably be `η0 (1 - j/T)`. Such a fundamental typo in a core equation is concerning. Additionally, the use of bar notation (e.g., `āi-1(t)`) is introduced for bias terms but is not used consistently, and its initial definition is slightly imprecise.
- **Anecdotal Claims:** The paper relies on unsubstantiated anecdotal claims. For example, Footnote 3 dismisses the need for multiple experimental runs by appealing to the authors' "experience." This is not a substitute for rigorous empirical validation. Similarly, Footnote 2 claims that using a different character set from the comparison work does not "meaningfully" affect the results, a claim that should be quantified.
- **Formatting:** The reference section is exceptionally poorly formatted, with inconsistent entries lacking critical information such as publication years, volume numbers, and page numbers. This falls far below the expected standard for a top-tier conference submission. Minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasing (e.g., "The last decade, machine learning has seen...") are also present throughout the text.

**2. Methodological Rigor:**
- **Confounded Experimental Design:** The paper's central comparison is severely confounded. The DRNN-1O model, which achieves the best performance, is trained using a completely different schedule than the other models. It uses an "incremental layer-wise method" and is trained for a total of 7.5 x 10^5 updates, while the standard RNN and DRNN-AO are trained for only 5 x 10^5 updates. It is impossible to determine whether the superior performance of the DRNN-1O is due to its architecture or simply because it received 50% more training and a specialized curriculum. This is a major flaw that invalidates the direct comparison between the models.
- **Lack of Statistical Validation:** The experiments are based on single runs for each of the three very large models ("only tested three network instantiations"). Without multiple runs with different initializations, it is impossible to assess the stability of the training process or the statistical significance of the reported performance differences. No error bars or confidence intervals are provided for any of the quantitative results.
- **Unjustified Hyperparameters:** Numerous critical hyperparameters appear to be chosen in an ad-hoc manner. For instance, the input weight matrix `Z1` is initialized from `N(0, 1)`, a non-standard choice with high variance that is not justified. The learning rate for the top layer of the DRNN-1O was specifically lowered to `0.25` based on an observation of saturation, but it is unclear why this was necessary only for this specific case. These details make the results difficult to reproduce and interpret.

**3. Originality and Significance:**
- The idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely novel, as the authors themselves cite Graves et al. [8]. The primary originality lies in the detailed analysis. However, the significance of this analysis is diminished by the aforementioned methodological shortcomings. While the results are strong, the lack of rigor makes it difficult to confidently accept the paper's conclusions about *why* the models perform well.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art-level results on a difficult benchmark using a relatively simple optimization algorithm.
- The analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy is insightful and novel, providing a valuable contribution to understanding deep models for sequential data.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
- The paper contains major methodological flaws, most notably a confounded experimental design that makes the comparison between the DRNN-1O and other models invalid.
- There is a severe lack of experimental rigor, with all conclusions based on single training runs without any measure of variance or statistical significance.
- The manuscript suffers from poor presentation quality, including a critical typo in a core equation, an unacceptably formatted reference list, and reliance on anecdotal claims over empirical evidence.

**Decision:**
I recommend **rejection**. While the paper addresses an important problem and presents promising results and interesting analyses, the sheer number of methodological and presentational flaws is too substantial to overlook. The confounded training schedule for the best-performing model is a critical issue that invalidates one of the paper's central comparisons. The lack of multiple runs, unjustified hyperparameter choices, and numerous presentation errors collectively fail to meet the high standards of quality, clarity, and rigor expected for a NIPS publication. The work shows promise, but it requires a major revision to address these fundamental issues before it can be considered a solid scientific contribution.
