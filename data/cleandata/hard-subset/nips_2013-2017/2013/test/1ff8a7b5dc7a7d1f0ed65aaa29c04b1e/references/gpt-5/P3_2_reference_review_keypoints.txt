- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs with two output schemes (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO), trained with SGD on character-level Wikipedia and compared to single-layer RNN and MRNN+HF.  
- Novelty lies mainly in training protocol and empirical analyses of temporal hierarchies rather than the architecture itself.  
- Clear motivation: stacking RNNs to induce hierarchical temporal representations addressing single-layer RNN limitations.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs trained with straightforward SGD approach MRNN+HF performance at comparable parameter counts.  
- Output-ablation analysis suggests lower layers capture short-term statistics while upper layers contribute more to longer-range predictions.  
- Hidden-state perturbation and context-switch experiments provide evidence for emergent, layer-wise time scales and differences between DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O.  
- Parentheses-closure experiments serve as a quantitative probe of long-term dependencies with reported success rates.  
- Training details (BPTT length, batch sizes, initialization, schedule) and code platform (Theano) are mostly provided.  
- Contribution/positioning concern: stacked RNNs/LSTMs are established; paper’s architectural novelty is incremental and related work coverage is sparse.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to stacked LSTM baselines or MRNN trained with SGD for fair contextualization.  
- Dataset/tokenization mismatch with prior MRNN work (different character sets) is hand-waved and should be quantified.  
- Compute/training budget comparisons (passes, wall-clock) are unclear and not normalized.  
- Training-protocol confound: DRNN-1O uses incremental supervised layer-wise training with output reinitialization, while DRNN-AO is end-to-end, complicating causal attribution of differences.  
- No validation set or clear hyperparameter tuning protocol; variance across random restarts is asserted but not reported.  
- Gradient normalization (unit-norm gradient with decaying scalar) is non-standard vs. clipping and lacks ablation to assess its impact.  
- Analyses need more quantification: report effective time constants with confidence intervals, normalize hidden-state distances, and provide fuller parentheses-closure statistics and failure modes.  
- Minor clarity issues: Eq. (3) typo, justification for zero-initialized output weights, and missing specifics (hidden-state initialization, sequence sampling, regularization details).  
- Overall quality: technically competent with reasonable experimental rigor but methodological choices limit causal conclusions.  
- Writing/clarity: generally well written and organized; figures are informative though some clarifications are needed.  
- Originality: moderate—straightforward stacking architecture with novel training practice and empirical analysis.  
- Significance: moderate—practical message that stacked simple RNNs with SGD can match HF-trained MRNNs; impact weakened by missing baselines.  
- Suggested improvements: add stacked LSTM/MRNN+SGD baselines, use a validation split and report run variance, ablate incremental training and gradient normalization, quantify time-scales and parentheses stats, and align character set with prior work.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—insightful analyses and useful practical result but incremental novelty and missing controls.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
