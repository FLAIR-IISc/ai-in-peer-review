

# NIPS Peer Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces a "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture, which consists of multiple stacked RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input time series. The authors argue that this architecture creates a natural temporal hierarchy, with different layers operating at different time scales. They test this architecture on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, comparing it to standard RNNs and demonstrating that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this task.

The paper makes several key contributions:
1. Proposing DRNNs as a way to explicitly model temporal hierarchies (e.g., phonemes, syllables, words, etc.)
2. Demonstrating experimentally that DRNNs develop different time scales at different layers
3. Providing evidence that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses after long phrases)
4. Showing that DRNN-1O (output only from top layer) achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD

The paper builds on prior work by Graves et al. (2013) who used stacked bidirectional LSTMs for speech recognition, and El Hihi & Bengio (1995) who proposed hierarchical RNNs with different time scales. However, the authors claim their work provides a more in-depth analysis of how the network architecture relates to the task.

## Quality Assessment

The paper is technically sound and well-executed, with appropriate experiments and analysis. The authors provide multiple lines of evidence supporting their claims about temporal hierarchy development in DRNNs through:
- Typo perturbation experiments showing different time scales across layers
- Context switching experiments measuring how long networks retain context
- Parentheses closing experiments demonstrating long-term memory capabilities

However, there are several important limitations:

1. **Lack of theoretical justification**: The paper claims DRNNs "embed different time scales directly in their structure" but provides no theoretical analysis to support this. The temporal hierarchy appears to be learned, not directly embedded, which is a significant distinction. The authors should clarify this.

2. **Training method concerns**: The DRNN-1O training procedure (incremental layer-wise training with full network training after each layer addition) is critical to their success, but the paper doesn't properly analyze whether this is a necessary component of DRNN success or just a training trick. This makes it difficult to separate the architectural contribution from the training methodology.

3. **Comparison to Hessian-free training is misleading**: The paper claims their DRNN-1O achieves "state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, which was previously only obtained using the far more advanced Hessian-free training algorithm." However, the Hessian-free results were obtained on a different dataset (86 characters vs. 95 characters), with different training procedures, and the DRNN-1O was trained for significantly more parameter updates (10x more than the Hessian-free method). This makes the comparison unfair.

4. **Lack of recurrent unit specification**: The paper doesn't specify what kind of recurrent units are used (LSTMs? GRUs? Simple RNNs?), which is a critical omission since performance would vary significantly depending on this choice.

## Clarity Assessment

The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear figures (Figures 1-4) that help illustrate key concepts. The main ideas are accessible to readers familiar with RNNs.

However, several areas could be improved for clarity:

1. The distinction between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO is not sufficiently explained early in the paper. This is a fundamental difference that impacts both the architecture and the results.

2. The training procedure for DRNN-1O is described in a confusing manner. The statement "When adding a layer, the previous output weights are discarded and new output weights are initialised connecting from the new top layer" suggests a training method that's different from standard layer-wise pre-training, but the paper doesn't adequately explain why this approach is necessary or how it differs from other methods.

3. The paper doesn't provide enough detail on the exact network architecture (e.g., number of layers, neuron counts per layer, activation functions), making it difficult to reproduce the experiments.

## Originality Assessment

The DRNN architecture is a reasonable extension of existing hierarchical RNN work, but the novelty is somewhat limited:

1. Stacked RNNs have been used before (e.g., Graves et al. 2013, El Hihi & Bengio 1995). The paper acknowledges this but doesn't sufficiently distinguish their specific approach.

2. The claim about temporal hierarchy is interesting but not entirely new. El Hihi & Bengio (1995) explicitly proposed hierarchical RNNs with different time scales using subsampling.

3. The paper doesn't adequately reference more recent work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., Fernández et al. 2007, Sutskever et al. 2011).

The most novel contribution appears to be the experimental evidence for temporal hierarchy development in DRNNs, but this is presented as a consequence of the architecture rather than the primary contribution.

## Significance Assessment

The significance of the work is somewhat limited:

1. The performance gains over standard RNNs are modest (BPC of 1.472 for DRNN-AO vs 1.51 for RNN, according to Table 1). This is a small improvement that may not be practically significant.

2. The paper doesn't compare to other recent state-of-the-art language modeling approaches (e.g., LSTMs with attention, Transformer-based models), making it difficult to assess how much DRNNs advance the field.

3. The paper claims DRNNs "embed these different time scales directly in their structure," but the experiments only show that time scales are learned, not directly embedded. This distinction is important for the significance of the claim.

4. The application to language modeling is somewhat narrow. The paper doesn't demonstrate how DRNNs might generalize to other tasks that require temporal hierarchies (e.g., speech recognition, video analysis).

## Strengths and Weaknesses

### Strengths:
- Well-executed experiments that provide multiple lines of evidence for the temporal hierarchy claim
- Clear demonstration of DRNNs' ability to model long-term dependencies (parentheses closing test)
- Interesting analysis of layer contributions to the output
- Good comparison to relevant baselines (standard RNN, Hessian-free results)
- Clear presentation of the DRNN architecture in Figure 1

### Weaknesses:
- Lack of theoretical justification for key claims
- Misleading comparison to Hessian-free training
- Critical omission of recurrent unit specification
- Unnecessary complexity in training procedure for DRNN-1O
- Overstated claims about "embedding" time scales in the structure
- Insufficient comparison to recent state-of-the-art language models

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak rejection** of this paper for NIPS. While the paper presents a technically sound approach with interesting experimental results, the novelty is limited and the significance of the contribution is questionable. The paper doesn't provide a substantial advance over existing work in the field, and the claims about temporal hierarchy development are somewhat overstated.

The paper would be strengthened by:
1. Providing a clear theoretical justification for the temporal hierarchy claim
2. Clarifying the recurrent unit type used in experiments
3. Making a fairer comparison to state-of-the-art methods (using the same dataset and evaluation metrics)
4. Better distinguishing the DRNN architecture from prior work on hierarchical RNNs
5. Providing more detail on the training procedure to enable reproducibility

The paper's contribution to the field is incremental rather than transformative, and it doesn't provide a compelling reason for the NIPS community to prioritize this work over other ongoing research in deep learning and recurrent networks.

## Specific Recommendations for Revision

1. **Clarify the key claim**: The paper must distinguish between "learning" temporal hierarchies versus "embedding" them directly in the architecture. The evidence presented shows the former, not the latter.

2. **Provide complete experimental details**: Include the specific recurrent unit type (LSTM? GRU?), the exact network architecture (number of layers, neurons per layer, activation functions), and complete training hyperparameters.

3. **Re-evaluate the comparison to Hessian-free training**: Either provide a fair comparison (same dataset, same number of parameter updates) or remove the claim about "previously only obtained using Hessian-free training."

4. **Compare to more recent state-of-the-art**: Include comparisons to recent language modeling approaches (e.g., LSTMs with attention, Transformer-based models) to better assess the significance of the results.

5. **Improve the discussion of related work**: Better distinguish the DRNN architecture from prior hierarchical RNN work, particularly El Hihi & Bengio (1995) and Fernández et al. (2007).

6. **Clarify the training procedure**: The DRNN-1O training method is confusing and critical to the results. Provide a clearer explanation of why this approach is necessary and how it differs from standard layer-wise pre-training.

This paper has the potential to be a solid contribution to the field with appropriate revisions, but as currently written, it doesn't provide sufficient novelty or significance to warrant acceptance at NIPS.
