**Summary**

This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to learn a temporal hierarchy, where different layers process information at different time scales, combining the hierarchical feature extraction of deep networks with the sequential memory of RNNs. The authors propose two variants: one with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with connections from all layers (DRNN-AO), the latter being proposed to improve gradient flow to lower layers.

The DRNN architecture is evaluated on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. The results demonstrate that a 5-layer DRNN, trained with simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) and gradient clipping, achieves performance comparable to the state-of-the-art established by more complex models (Multiplicative RNNs) trained with advanced second-order optimization (Hessian-Free). The paper's main contribution, however, lies in its in-depth analysis, which provides compelling evidence for the temporal hierarchy hypothesis. Through a series of carefully designed experiments, the authors show that higher layers learn to operate on longer time scales, and that the model can capture very long-range dependencies, such as closing parentheses over hundreds of characters.

**Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results with Simple Methods:** The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on a difficult, large-scale benchmark. A particularly strong point is that this is achieved using a standard SGD-based training procedure, in contrast to prior work that required computationally expensive Hessian-Free optimization. This result is significant as it demonstrates that architectural improvements (i.e., depth) can be a more practical path to better performance than more complex optimization algorithms.

2.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The paper goes far beyond simply reporting a new state-of-the-art BPC score. The analysis section is exemplary. The experiments designed to probe the network's internal mechanisms—measuring effective time scales per layer via perturbations, ablating layer outputs, and analyzing the handling of long-range syntax like parentheses—provide strong, convincing evidence for the authors' central claims. This analysis provides valuable insights into *why* deep recurrent architectures are effective and is a major strength of the work.

3.  **Clarity and Novelty:** The paper is very clearly written, well-motivated, and easy to follow. The idea of stacking standard RNNs to create a temporal hierarchy is conceptually simple yet powerful. While the use of stacked recurrent architectures had been explored before (e.g., Graves et al. 2013 for speech), this paper is novel in its focus on a deep analysis of the *properties* of this architecture and its application to character-level language modeling, providing a much clearer understanding of its benefits.

4.  **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail about their training setup, hyperparameters, and architecture to enable reproduction of their results. The description of the training procedure, including the layer-wise training for the DRNN-1O, is honest and clear.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Lack of Comparison to Deep LSTMs:** The introduction appropriately motivates the work by discussing the limitations of shallow RNNs and mentions the LSTM architecture. However, the experiments only compare the DRNN (using simple tanh units) to a shallow RNN. A crucial baseline would be a Deep LSTM network with a similar parameter count. LSTMs are explicitly designed to capture long-term dependencies, and it would be very informative to see if the architectural depth of the DRNN is complementary to, or a substitute for, the gating mechanisms of an LSTM. Does stacking simple RNNs sufficiently address the vanishing gradient problem to the same extent as LSTMs, or would a Deep LSTM perform even better?

2.  **Limited Exploration of Architecture and Training:** The paper presents results for a single depth (5 layers). While computationally expensive, some discussion or smaller-scale experiments on how performance varies with depth would strengthen the paper. Furthermore, the layer-wise training strategy for the DRNN-1O is described as "necessary to reach good performance." This suggests that training this variant end-to-end from a random initialization is difficult. This is an interesting finding in itself and warrants more discussion. It also highlights the practical advantage of the DRNN-AO architecture, which did not require this special procedure.

3.  **Analysis of Gradients:** The DRNN-AO is motivated as a way to ameliorate vanishing/exploding gradients by providing a more direct error signal to lower layers. This is a plausible and well-accepted rationale in deep learning. However, the paper does not present any empirical analysis to support this claim, such as measuring and comparing gradient norms at different layers for the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO during training. Such an analysis would provide direct evidence for the hypothesized training benefit and strengthen the paper's conclusions.

**Overall Assessment**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. It presents a simple yet powerful architectural principle—depth—and demonstrates its effectiveness on a challenging task. The key strength lies in the thorough and insightful analysis that provides a deeper understanding of how these models work, substantiating the claim that they learn a temporal hierarchy. The empirical results are strong and challenge the prevailing notion that second-order optimization methods are essential for training deep recurrent models. While a comparison with Deep LSTMs would have made the experimental section more complete, the paper as it stands is a well-executed and important piece of research. It is technically sound, clearly written, and the findings are likely to be influential and built upon by other researchers. I strongly recommend acceptance.
