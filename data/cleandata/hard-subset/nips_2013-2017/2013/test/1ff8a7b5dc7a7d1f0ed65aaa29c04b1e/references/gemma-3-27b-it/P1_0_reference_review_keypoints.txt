- Paper studies stacked RNNs (Deep Recurrent Neural Networks) for character-level language modeling and analyzes layer-wise behavior.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a large Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD, rivaling Hessian-free results.  
- Motivates DRNNs by combining DNN hierarchical processing with RNN temporal memory, arguing stacking yields multiple timescales.  
- Provides experiments analyzing layer-wise timescales, perturbation/ablation studies, and parenthesis handling to probe long-term dependencies.  
- Positions work relative to prior hierarchical RNN and Hessian-free literature, claiming simpler optimization with comparable or better results.  
- Strength: strong empirical results using straightforward training.  
- Strength: clear motivation and intuitive explanation for hierarchical temporal representations.  
- Strength: valuable attempt to analyze internal representations and output contributions.  
- Strength: well-written, organized, and reproducible (network/training details and code/data available).  
- Weakness: analysis is limited and mainly correlational, not establishing causation.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to more modern RNN variants (e.g., GRUs) or transformers.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies on layers, units, and output connection strategies; parameter sensitivity not fully explored.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns for SGD on very large models and parallel computing environments.  
- Weakness: some figures (e.g., Figure 3) need clearer labels and explanations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated architecture with strong results and initial representation analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited causal analysis, missing comparisons to newer architectures, and scalability questions.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution deserving acceptance despite analysis and comparison shortcomings.  
- Rating: Accept (7/10).  
- Recommendations: add deeper ablation/quantitative hidden-state analyses, compare to GRUs/Transformers, clarify scalability, and improve figure clarity.
