- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks recurrent layers to create a temporal hierarchy; presents DRNN-1O (top-layer output) and DRNN-AO (all-layers output) and evaluates on character-level Wikipedia data using BPC.

- Argues DRNNs combine hierarchical processing of DNNs with RNN temporal memory, enabling modeling of multiple time scales.

- Strength: clear theoretical motivation that stacking RNNs creates different time scales across layers.

- Strength: comprehensive analyses including layer contribution removal, time-scale perturbation experiments, and a parentheses-closing long-term dependency task.

- Strength: well-designed experiments with appropriate metrics and clear presentation.

- Strength: provides practical implementation and training details to aid reproducibility.

- Weakness: lacks formal theoretical justification linking the architectural design to observed performance gains.

- Weakness: inadequate comparisons to state-of-the-art — omits modern LSTM/GRU with attention, emerging Transformer models, and recent hierarchical RNN work.

- Weakness: unjustified incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O with no ablation or theoretical rationale.

- Weakness: narrow evaluation scope limited to character-level language modeling; no experiments on other tasks (e.g., speech, MT).

- Weakness: vague and unsupported claims of “state-of-the-art” performance, relying on outdated baselines.

- Specific comment: needs clearer distinction from existing stacked RNNs and stacked bidirectional LSTMs (e.g., how it differs from [8]).

- Specific comment: Figure 3 time-scale analysis is strong but the paper fails to explain why DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O show different time-scale behaviors.

- Specific comment: paper should explain why DRNN-AO outperforms DRNN-1O on the parentheses-closing task to illuminate the value of outputs at all layers.

- Specific comment: reproducibility is incomplete — missing epochs and batch-size details for training on the 1.4B character corpus.

- Specific comment: better positioning against recent hierarchical RNN work is needed to clarify novelty.

- Overall scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 5/10, Significance 5/10.

- Recommendation: weak rejection due to insufficient SOTA comparisons and limited evidence for broad impact.

- Revision suggestions: expand SOTA comparisons (LSTM/GRU/attention, Transformers), provide ablation/theory for incremental training, clarify novelty vs prior hierarchical RNNs, evaluate on additional tasks, and strengthen the theoretical argument for temporal hierarchies.
