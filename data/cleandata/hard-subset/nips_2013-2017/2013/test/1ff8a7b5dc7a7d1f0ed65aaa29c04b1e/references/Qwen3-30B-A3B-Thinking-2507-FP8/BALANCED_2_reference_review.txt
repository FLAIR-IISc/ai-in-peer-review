

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture that combines deep neural networks with recurrent networks by stacking RNNs such that each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that this structure naturally creates different time scales at different layers, forming a temporal hierarchy. They evaluate DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, comparing against standard RNNs and state-of-the-art methods. The paper builds on prior work in hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6] and stacked LSTMs [8], but claims their architecture explicitly creates temporal hierarchies rather than embedding them implicitly in network dynamics.

## Quality
The paper presents a technically sound architecture with thorough experimental evaluation. The authors provide detailed experimental setup, including network configurations, training parameters, and evaluation metrics. Their analysis of temporal hierarchies through typo perturbation, context switching, and parentheses closing is particularly compelling. However, the paper lacks theoretical analysis to support the claim that DRNNs "automatically create different time scales" - the evidence is purely empirical. The experimental scope is limited to a single dataset (Wikipedia text) and a single task (character-level language modeling), which weakens the generalizability of the findings. The paper could have strengthened its claims with more theoretical grounding or additional tasks demonstrating temporal hierarchy benefits.

## Clarity
The paper is well-written and logically organized, with clear equations and a helpful schematic (Figure 1). The experimental methodology is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. However, the explanation of why DRNN-AO (all layers output) underperforms DRNN-1O (only top layer output) is insufficient. This is a key finding that deserves more thorough analysis. The connection between the observed temporal hierarchies and the performance improvements could also be made clearer.

## Originality
The DRNN architecture is conceptually novel as a way to explicitly create temporal hierarchies in RNNs, though the authors could better distinguish it from prior hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6]. The paper claims their architecture "automatically creates different time scales," but it's unclear if this is truly novel or simply a natural consequence of stacking RNNs. The comparison to [5] is somewhat superficial, as [5] also explicitly creates multiple time scales through subsampling. The paper would benefit from a more precise articulation of what makes their approach distinct from existing hierarchical RNN work.

## Significance
The paper demonstrates that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling, which is significant. The analysis of temporal hierarchies provides valuable insights into how RNNs process information across time scales. However, the significance is limited by the narrow scope of experiments. The paper could have broader impact if they demonstrated the approach on other tasks with clear temporal hierarchies (e.g., speech recognition with phoneme/syllable/sentence-level processing). The claim that DRNNs "are able to model long-term dependencies" is somewhat overstated, as the paper shows they can handle parentheses spanning hundreds of characters but doesn't demonstrate this on more challenging long-term dependency tasks like those in [11].

## Strengths
- Clear and well-organized description of the DRNN architecture
- Thorough experimental evaluation with meaningful analysis of temporal hierarchies
- Good comparison with state-of-the-art methods
- Detailed experimental setup allowing for reproducibility
- Interesting generative text examples that illustrate the model's capabilities

## Weaknesses
- Limited theoretical analysis to support key claims
- Insufficient explanation of why DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O
- Overstated significance given the narrow experimental scope
- Inadequate differentiation from prior hierarchical RNN work
- Lack of experiments on more challenging long-term dependency tasks

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. Provide a more precise theoretical analysis of how the DRNN architecture creates temporal hierarchies, possibly through simple mathematical analysis of the hidden state evolution.
2. Conduct additional experiments on tasks with clearly defined temporal hierarchies beyond character-level language modeling (e.g., speech recognition with multiple time scales).
3. Expand the analysis of DRNN-AO vs. DRNN-1O, including ablation studies to understand why the all-layers output approach underperforms.
4. Better distinguish the work from prior hierarchical RNN approaches, particularly [5], by clearly articulating the methodological and conceptual differences.
5. Extend the analysis to more challenging long-term dependency tasks like those in [11] to more convincingly demonstrate the advantages of DRNNs.

## Conclusion
This paper presents an interesting DRNN architecture that demonstrates promise for character-level language modeling and provides valuable insights into temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks. While the experimental results are compelling for the specific task, the paper would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding, broader experimental scope, and clearer differentiation from prior work. With these improvements, the paper would make a significant contribution to the understanding of recurrent architectures and their temporal processing capabilities. As it stands, the work is technically sound and provides useful insights, but the scope and novelty claims limit its impact for NIPS.
