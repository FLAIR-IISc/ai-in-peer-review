### Summary of the Paper

This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the sequential input to the next. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure allows the network to develop a temporal hierarchy, with different layers learning to operate on different time scales. The authors apply this model to the task of character-level language modeling on a large-scale Wikipedia dataset.

Two variants of the DRNN are explored: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The DRNN-1O model is trained using a layer-wise incremental scheme, while the DRNN-AO is trained end-to-end. Using only stochastic gradient descent with gradient normalization, the proposed DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on the benchmark dataset, comparable to results previously obtained by a more complex multiplicative RNN trained with the advanced Hessian-free optimization technique [19].

Beyond performance, the paper provides a series of insightful analyses to validate the temporal hierarchy hypothesis. These experiments measure how long perturbations (like a typo) persist in each layer's hidden state, how quickly the models recover from an incorrect context, and how they handle long-range dependencies such as closing parentheses. The results suggest that higher layers in the DRNN indeed learn longer time scales and are crucial for capturing long-term structure in the data. This work builds upon prior uses of stacked LSTMs in speech recognition [8] but provides a much more detailed analysis of the architectural properties, which was absent in previous work.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The claims about performance are well-supported by experimental results on a large, standard benchmark. The comparison to the previous state-of-the-art [19] is direct and compelling, as the proposed method achieves similar performance with a simpler model and a more standard training algorithm (SGD). The analytical experiments designed to probe the internal dynamics of the network are a major strength. They provide strong, albeit indirect, evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, moving beyond simple performance metrics to offer insight into *why* the architecture is effective. The analysis of parenthesis-closing is particularly effective at demonstrating the model's capacity for learning long-range dependencies.

However, the empirical evaluation is somewhat limited. The authors report results for only one run of three network instantiations, citing computational cost. While the scale of the experiments makes this understandable, it leaves some uncertainty regarding the robustness of the results to initialization or minor hyperparameter changes. The assertion that large networks have little performance variance (footnote 3) is stated without supporting evidence.

**Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The DRNN architecture is explained concisely with clear equations and a helpful diagram (Figure 1). The training procedures, including the layer-wise scheme for DRNN-1O and the gradient normalization, are described in sufficient detail. The motivation for the analytical experiments is well-argued, and the results, presented in Figures 2, 3, and 4, are easy to interpret and effectively support the paper's main theses. The generated text samples in Table 2 provide a good qualitative illustration of the different roles played by the bottom and top layers.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new; the authors correctly cite its use in prior work [8]. However, the primary contribution of this paper is not the architecture itself but the in-depth analysis of its properties and the demonstration of its effectiveness for character-level language modeling using a simple training procedure. Previous work had used such architectures but had not systematically investigated the claim that they form a temporal hierarchy. The analytical experiments designed to probe these hierarchical dynamics are novel and provide a methodological contribution for future work on analyzing deep sequential models. The success of the layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O is also an interesting and non-obvious finding.

**Significance:** The results presented in this paper are highly significant. By showing that a straightforward DRNN trained with SGD can match the performance of a more complex model trained with a second-order method, the paper makes a strong case for the importance of architectural design. This finding was particularly relevant at a time of intense debate over the necessity of complex optimization algorithms for training deep models [2]. The paper's analysis provides a clear intuition for why depth is beneficial in recurrent models, linking it to the hierarchical temporal structure present in sequential data like language. This work has likely inspired further research into deep recurrent architectures and methods for analyzing their internal representations. The demonstration of successfully modeling very long-term dependencies (hundreds of characters) on a real-world task was a notable advance.

### Arguments For and Against Acceptance

**Arguments for acceptance:**
1.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on a difficult, large-scale benchmark using a relatively simple architecture and training algorithm.
2.  **Novel and Insightful Analysis:** The key contribution is the set of experiments that provide compelling evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy in the DRNN, offering a valuable understanding of why the model works. This goes substantially beyond prior work that simply used the architecture.
3.  **High Significance:** The findings contribute to the understanding of deep learning for sequential data and have important implications for both architectural design and the choice of optimization methods.
4.  **Excellent Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow, making its ideas and results accessible.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
1.  **Limited Architectural Novelty:** The concept of stacked RNNs was not invented in this paper, though its application and analysis in this context are new.
2.  **Limited Experimental Scope:** Due to computational constraints, the paper presents results from only a single training run for each model configuration, which slightly weakens the empirical claims. An ablation study on the necessity of the layer-wise training for the DRNN-1O would have been beneficial.

In conclusion, the paper's strengths, particularly its insightful analysis and significant results, far outweigh its weaknesses. It presents a complete and well-executed piece of research that advances the understanding of deep recurrent models.
