## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), constructed by stacking recurrent layers, for character-level language modeling. The authors argue that this architecture naturally introduces a temporal hierarchy, allowing it to capture both short- and long-term dependencies in text. They empirically demonstrate state-of-the-art performance (comparable to Hessian-free optimization methods) using only SGD on a Wikipedia corpus and provide an initial analysis of the learned timescales within the network’s layers.  They explore two DRNN variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) differing in how outputs are connected.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to understanding the dynamics of deep recurrent networks. The experimental results are compelling, showing competitive performance without relying on sophisticated optimization techniques. The analysis of learned timescales and the investigation of layer contributions are particularly valuable and offer insights into the behaviour of these networks.  However, the analysis remains somewhat superficial, and the lack of a comprehensive ablation study limits the strength of the conclusions. 

**Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound. The DRNN architecture is clearly defined, and the experimental setup is well-described. The results are convincing, and the comparison to existing work is fair. The use of BPC as a metric is standard. The gradient normalization technique is a practical addition. However, the analysis lacks statistical rigor (e.g., error bars on the timescale measurements would be beneficial). The claim of state-of-the-art performance needs to be qualified as being *for recurrent networks with the same number of parameters* – it’s not a global state-of-the-art claim.

* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the figures are helpful. However, some sections (particularly the discussion of training) could benefit from more detailed explanations. The motivation for the DRNN-AO architecture (addressing pathological curvature) is stated but not fully explained – a visual representation of the gradient flow would be beneficial. The connection between the chosen network size and the number of parameters is clearly articulated.

* **Originality (7/10):**  While stacking RNNs is not entirely novel (acknowledged by the authors, citing [5, 8]), the systematic analysis of the resulting temporal hierarchy and the demonstration of competitive performance with a simple training method (SGD) are original contributions.  The authors clearly position their work within the existing literature and address previous limitations (e.g., lack of analysis in [8]). However, the core idea of stacking RNNs to create hierarchies has been explored before.

* **Significance (8/10):** The results are significant because they demonstrate that deep recurrent architectures can be effectively trained with relatively simple methods. The analysis of the learned timescales provides valuable insights into the internal workings of these networks. This has implications for designing and understanding more complex recurrent models. The ability to achieve competitive performance with SGD is particularly noteworthy, as it makes these models more accessible to researchers without access to extensive computational resources.



**Strengths:**

*   Strong empirical results demonstrating competitive performance.
*   Interesting analysis of learned temporal hierarchies and layer contributions.
*   Clear explanation of the DRNN architecture and its motivation.
*   Demonstration that competitive results can be obtained with SGD.
*   Insightful experiments on parenthesis matching and typo recovery revealing long-term dependencies.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Analysis of learned timescales feels somewhat preliminary and lacks statistical robustness.
*   Ablation studies are limited. More systematic investigation of the impact of different hyperparameters (e.g., number of layers, neurons per layer) would be valuable.
*   The reasoning behind the architectural choices (e.g., the specific number of layers) isn’t fully justified.
*   The comparison to other state-of-the-art methods is limited to those with the same number of parameters. A broader comparison would be useful.
*   Discussion of limitations could be more comprehensive.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel analysis of a deep recurrent architecture.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate competitive performance.
*   The insights into learned temporal hierarchies are valuable for the community.
*   The authors address a relevant and important problem in recurrent neural networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The analysis, while interesting, lacks statistical rigor and depth.
*   The originality of the core idea is somewhat limited.
*   The ablation studies are not comprehensive enough to draw strong conclusions.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS. The work is of high quality, presents valuable insights, and addresses a relevant problem. The minor revisions suggested above (strengthening the statistical analysis, expanding the ablation studies, and providing a more thorough comparison to other methods) would significantly improve the paper. The paper is a good contribution to the field and has the potential to influence future research on deep recurrent networks.
