- Studies Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) for character-level language modeling and proposes they induce a temporal hierarchy; evaluates two variants (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO).  
- Shows competitive/state-of-the-art performance among recurrent models with the same parameter count on a Wikipedia corpus using only SGD (comparable to Hessian-free results).  
- Provides initial analysis of learned timescales and layer contributions, offering insights into temporal dynamics.  
- Experimental setup and DRNN definition are technically sound and clearly described; uses BPC metric and gradient normalization.  
- Analysis lacks statistical rigor (e.g., missing error bars on timescale measurements).  
- Paper is well-written and figures/notation are clear, but training discussion and DRNN-AO motivation need more detail and visualization (e.g., gradient flow).  
- Originality is moderate: stacking RNNs is known, but systematic temporal-hierarchy analysis and showing SGD effectiveness are novel contributions.  
- Results are significant: demonstrate deep recurrent architectures can be trained with simple methods and illuminate internal timescales.  
- Strong empirical evidence, clear architecture explanation, and insightful experiments (parenthesis matching, typo recovery) revealing long-term dependencies.  
- Timescale analysis is preliminary and not statistically robust.  
- Ablation studies are limited; more systematic exploration of layers, neuron counts, and hyperparameters is needed.  
- Architectural choices (e.g., number of layers) are not fully justified.  
- Comparisons are restricted to models with the same parameter count; broader benchmarking against state-of-the-art would strengthen claims.  
- Discussion of limitations is incomplete.  
- For acceptance: offers novel analysis, compelling empirical results, and useful insights into temporal hierarchies.  
- Against acceptance: analysis depth/statistical rigor is insufficient, core idea’s novelty is limited, and ablations are incomplete.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—strengthen statistical analysis, expand ablation studies, and provide broader comparisons.
