This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), combining the concepts of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks to capture temporal hierarchies in sequential data. The authors propose a DRNN architecture where each layer consists of a recurrent neural network, and subsequent layers receive the hidden state of the previous layer as input. They analyze this architecture in the context of character-based language modeling, aiming to demonstrate its ability to model both short- and long-term dependencies. The key contribution lies in demonstrating that stacking RNNs automatically creates different timescales, and experimentally verifying this claim. They achieve state-of-the-art results on a Wikipedia text corpus using only SGD, outperforming previous recurrent network results that relied on more advanced optimization techniques like Hessian-free training.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a clear mathematical formulation of the DRNN architecture and training process. The experiments are well-designed and provide convincing evidence for the benefits of the proposed architecture. The analysis of the learned timescales through typo perturbation experiments is particularly insightful. However, the ablation studies (removing layer outputs) feel somewhat superficial and lack deeper quantitative analysis of the performance impact. The authors acknowledge potential limitations related to network size and offer directions for future work, demonstrating honesty about the scope of their work. The normalization of gradients is mentioned as a heuristic, but further justification or discussion of its impact would be beneficial. While the results are strong, the paper feels descriptive rather than deeply analytical—exploring *why* DRNNs work as well as they do could elevate the contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for the DRNN architecture is clearly explained, and the mathematical notation is consistent. Figure 1 is helpful in visualizing the network structure. However, the explanations of the training setup, particularly the layer-wise training method for DRNN-1O, could be more detailed. While the description is present, it's a bit dense and would benefit from a simpler, more intuitive explanation. The numerous cross-references to previous work (e.g., citing results from [19] multiple times) sometimes disrupt the flow. The transitions between sections (particularly from results to conclusions) feel abrupt.

**Originality:** The DRNN architecture itself isn't entirely novel—combining stacked RNNs is a natural extension of existing work [3, 5, 6, 8]. However, the systematic analysis of the emergent timescales and the demonstration of state-of-the-art performance with a simpler training algorithm (SGD) represent a significant contribution.  The connection between the architecture and inherent time scale creation is well-articulated.  The authors appropriately position their work relative to existing literature, acknowledging prior efforts while highlighting their specific contributions. The focus on character-level language modeling is a good choice for demonstrating the benefits of the architecture due to its inherent temporal dependencies.

**Significance:** The results are significant. Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task using a relatively simple training method is a valuable contribution. The analysis of learned timescales provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent networks. The demonstrated ability to model long-term dependencies is promising. This work has the potential to influence future research in deep learning, particularly in the development of architectures capable of capturing complex temporal dynamics. The observations regarding parenthesis handling are interesting but could be explored in more detail to assess generalizability.




**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   State-of-the-art results on a challenging task.
*   Clear demonstration of emergent timescales in the DRNN architecture.
*   Technically sound and well-executed experiments.
*   Insightful analysis of network behavior.
*   Successfully employs SGD to achieve comparable performance to Hessian-free methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The DRNN architecture isn’t fundamentally novel, being a logical combination of existing concepts.
*   The analysis of layer contributions is somewhat superficial.
*   The paper is largely descriptive, lacking a deeper theoretical understanding of why the architecture works so well.
*   Some sections (training setup) could benefit from more detailed explanations.



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. While the architecture itself isn't revolutionary, the analysis of emergent timescales and the demonstration of competitive performance with SGD are significant achievements. The clarity of the paper could be improved with more detailed explanations in certain sections and smoother transitions between ideas. I recommend acceptance.
