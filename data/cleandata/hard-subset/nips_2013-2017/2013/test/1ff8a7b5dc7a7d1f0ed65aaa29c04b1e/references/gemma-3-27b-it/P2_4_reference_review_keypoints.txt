- Summary: Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) that create a temporal hierarchy and achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using only SGD.  
- Relationship to prior work: Positions work among DNN/RNN literature, differentiates by stacking RNNs with temporal feedback and providing deeper analysis than prior hierarchical RNN attempts.  
- Strength — Novel Architecture: Stacking RNNs is a well-motivated way to combine hierarchical processing with temporal memory.  
- Strength — Strong Empirical Results: Matches or beats prior recurrent-network results on a hard task without advanced optimization (Hessian-free).  
- Strength — Detailed Analysis: Provides layer-wise timescale analyses and contribution studies (including parenthesis-matching experiments).  
- Strength — Clear Writing: Paper is generally well-written with clear explanations of architecture and experiments.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Sufficient experimental details and parameters are provided to reproduce results.  
- Weakness — Limited Experiments: Only three network instantiations tested and limited architectural/hyperparameter exploration.  
- Weakness — Lack of Theory: Heavy reliance on empirical findings with no rigorous theoretical analysis of dynamics or why hierarchy emerges.  
- Weakness — Comparison Scope: Comparisons focus on older baselines; lacks evaluation against more recent architectures (e.g., Transformers).  
- Weakness — Training Details: Incremental layer-wise training (DRNN-1O) and differing learning rates are under-explained and convoluted.  
- Weakness — Failure-case Analysis: Limited discussion and analysis of failure modes (e.g., nested parentheses).  
- Qualitative — Quality: Technically sound and empirically supported, though stronger theory would help.  
- Qualitative — Clarity: Well organized and accessible, but some training specifics need elaboration.  
- Qualitative — Originality: Novel combination of techniques and significant contribution via temporal-hierarchy analysis.  
- Qualitative — Significance: Important empirical results and insights into RNN timescales; promising alternative to complex optimizers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel architecture, state-of-the-art results with SGD, detailed temporal analysis, strong empirical contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental scope, lack of theoretical justification, comparisons to somewhat dated benchmarks.  
- Overall assessment: Strong accept; merits minor revisions to expand benchmark discussion and clarify training procedures.
