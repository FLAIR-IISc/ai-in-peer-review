

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper proposes "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" (DRNNs), which combine hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal memory of RNNs. The authors claim that stacking RNN layers automatically creates multiple time scales, allowing for better modeling of long-term dependencies in character-level language modeling. The paper demonstrates this on Wikipedia text corpus, showing DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using only SGD.

## Strengths
The paper makes a clear and interesting theoretical contribution by formalizing DRNNs as a combination of DNNs and RNNs. The experimental results are generally sound, with DRNNs outperforming standard RNNs on character prediction tasks. The analysis of time scales through perturbation experiments is particularly insightful, demonstrating how different layers develop distinct temporal memory properties. The paper also provides a useful comparison to prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6] and the recent DRNN work [8].

## Weaknesses

### Technical Errors and Inconsistencies
1. **Equation Errors**: The update equation (2.1) contains multiple notational errors. The term "Wiai(t− 1)" should be "W_i a_i(t-1)" with proper subscripting. The output equation (2) has a mathematical formatting error: "L∑ i=1" should be "∑_{i=1}^L".

2. **Training Algorithm**: The training equation (3) contains a critical error: "η0 (− j T )" should be "η0 (1 - j/T)". This would significantly impact the training dynamics. The paper claims "using only stochastic gradient descent" but the DRNN-1O uses a layer-wise training scheme that's not standard SGD.

3. **Experimental Details**: The claim that "the DRNN-AO sees the full training set about 7 times in total" is inconsistent with the described training procedure (5×10^5 parameter updates with 75 sequences). The calculation doesn't add up, suggesting either a misunderstanding of training dynamics or a typo.

4. **Character Set Inconsistency**: The paper uses 95 characters (including 'unknown'), while the cited comparison [19] uses only 86 characters. The authors claim "cross-entropy is not affected meaningfully" but provide no evidence for this assertion.

### Clarity and Presentation Issues
1. **Figure References**: The paper references Figures 1-4 without providing them, making it impossible to verify the claims about the architecture and results.

2. **Confusing Methodology**: The description of the layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is particularly confusing. The phrase "When adding a layer, the previous output weights are discarded" contradicts the claim that "we always train the full network after each layer is added."

3. **Inadequate Comparison**: The paper compares to "state-of-the-art for recurrent networks" but doesn't clearly establish what this state-of-the-art is. The comparison to Hessian-free training [19] is mentioned but not properly contextualized.

4. **Reference Issues**: The reference section contains multiple formatting errors (e.g., "The tradeoffs of large-scale learning. Optimization for Machine Learning, page, L. Bottou, O. Bousquet, , , "). The PAQ compression algorithm [14] is cited as a comparison but its relevance to BPC is not explained.

### Theoretical and Experimental Concerns
1. **Overstated Claims**: The paper claims DRNNs "embed these different timescales directly in their structure" but the evidence (Figure 3) shows only that different layers develop different time scales during training, not that the structure inherently embeds them.

2. **Lack of Baseline**: The paper compares to a common RNN but doesn't include a comparison to standard LSTM, which is the current standard for such tasks.

3. **Inconsistent Results**: The DRNN-AO is described as "slightly worse" than DRNN-1O, but the text later states "the DRNN-AO sees the full training set about 7 times in total, and the DRNN-1O about 10 times" - the increased training time for DRNN-1O might explain its better performance, not the architecture itself.

4. **Misleading Figure 4 Description**: The text states "the output of the top layer for the closing parenthesis is increased strongly for the whole duration of the phrase" but doesn't provide evidence that this is due to the architecture rather than the training process.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper presents an interesting architectural idea and provides some evidence for the temporal hierarchy concept, numerous technical errors and inconsistencies undermine the credibility of the results. The paper contains multiple mathematical errors in key equations, confusing descriptions of methodology, and inconsistent experimental reporting. These issues make it difficult to evaluate whether the proposed DRNN architecture truly offers advantages over standard RNNs.

The paper would benefit from a thorough technical revision to correct these errors and provide clearer explanations of the methodology. Without these corrections, the claims about the DRNN's advantages over standard RNNs cannot be properly evaluated.

## Recommendation
Reject. While the paper addresses an interesting problem and contains potentially valuable insights, the numerous technical errors and inconsistencies significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The authors should address these issues before the work can be considered for publication. The paper would be a better fit for a venue with less stringent technical requirements, but for NIPS, the errors are too substantial to overlook.
