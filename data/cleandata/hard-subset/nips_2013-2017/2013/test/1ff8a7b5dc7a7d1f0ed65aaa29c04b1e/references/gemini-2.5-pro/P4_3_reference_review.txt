This paper introduces and analyzes the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The authors' central hypothesis is that this deep structure naturally facilitates the learning of a temporal hierarchy, with lower layers processing information on shorter time scales and higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies. This work positions itself as a more in-depth analysis of the stacked RNN architecture, which had been previously used to achieve state-of-the-art results in speech recognition [8] but without a detailed investigation into the source of its effectiveness.

The authors evaluate the DRNN on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They test two variants: one where the output is generated only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The key result is that both DRNN models, trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and gradient clipping, achieve performance competitive with the state-of-the-art at the time [19], which required a more complex multiplicative RNN model trained with a sophisticated second-order Hessian-free optimizer. Beyond performance metrics, the paper's main contribution is a series of well-designed experiments to analyze the internal dynamics of the trained models. These experiments measure the memory decay in each layer, the effective context length used for prediction, and the model's ability to handle long-range dependencies, such as closing parentheses over long distances. The results provide strong evidence for the emergence of a learned temporal hierarchy within the DRNN.

**Strengths:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Its main strength lies in the thorough and insightful analysis that goes beyond simply reporting performance scores. The experiments designed to probe the model's internal workings—specifically the "typo" perturbation test to measure memory decay across layers and the context-switching experiment—provide compelling empirical evidence for the central hypothesis that DRNNs learn a hierarchy of time scales. The case study on parenthesis-closing is particularly effective, offering a clear and concrete demonstration of the model's capacity to learn non-trivial, long-range dependencies.
*   **Significance:** The paper's finding that a relatively simple architecture (stacked RNNs) trained with a standard first-order method (SGD) can match the performance of more complex models [19] trained with computationally intensive second-order methods is highly significant. This result demonstrates the power of architectural priors and provides a more accessible path for practitioners to achieve state-of-the-art performance on sequential modeling tasks. The analytical techniques presented are also valuable contributions, providing a template for how to better understand the behavior of deep sequential models.
*   **Originality:** While the idea of stacking recurrent layers was not entirely new (e.g., [8]), this paper is one of the first to systematically investigate *why* this architecture is effective. The focus on analyzing the emergent temporal hierarchy as the primary source of the performance gain is a novel contribution. It shifts the focus from purely performance-driven engineering to a more scientific understanding of model behavior.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The DRNN architecture is explained concisely with equations and a helpful diagram. The experimental setup, including the training procedure and the specific analysis methods, is described in sufficient detail for the reader to understand the work. The results are presented clearly through tables and figures that effectively support the paper's claims.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Quality:** The empirical evaluation, while insightful, is based on a very small number of runs (one for each of the three main models). The authors justify this by the long training times, but it makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the results to different random initializations. The claim that network size mitigates this variance is anecdotal and would be strengthened by empirical validation, even on a smaller problem.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper claims that the incremental, layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O model was "necessary to reach good performance." This is a strong claim that is not substantiated with a control experiment. Showing that a DRNN-1O trained end-to-end from a random initialization performs significantly worse would be required to fully support this assertion. Without this comparison, it remains an unsubstantiated detail of the training protocol.
*   **Clarity and Scope:** The paper uses standard tanh RNN units. Given that LSTMs [11] were well-established and designed specifically to handle long-term dependencies—a key focus of the paper—a discussion on why they were not used is notably absent. Comparing a deep stack of simple RNNs to a shallow LSTM, or investigating a deep stack of LSTMs, would have provided a more complete picture and potentially stronger results.

**Summary of Arguments:**

**Arguments for acceptance:**
1.  Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a difficult benchmark using a simpler model and optimization algorithm than prior work, which is a significant practical contribution.
2.  Provides a novel and in-depth analysis of the internal dynamics of stacked RNNs, offering strong evidence for the emergence of a learned temporal hierarchy.
3.  The analytical methods proposed in the paper are valuable contributions in their own right and encourage a deeper understanding of model behavior beyond performance metrics.
4.  The paper is well-written, the claims are well-supported by the presented experiments, and the topic is of clear interest to the NIPS community.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
1.  The experimental results are based on single runs for each model, which raises concerns about the robustness of the findings.
2.  A key claim regarding the necessity of a layer-wise training scheme is presented without a proper ablation study.
3.  The omission of LSTMs as a recurrent unit is a limitation, as they are a highly relevant baseline and component for a study focused on long-term dependencies.

Overall, the strengths of this paper, particularly its combination of strong empirical results with insightful analysis, significantly outweigh its weaknesses. It presents a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep sequential models.
