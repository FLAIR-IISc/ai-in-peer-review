- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), stacking RNN layers to create explicit temporal hierarchies across layers.  
- DRNNs are evaluated on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus and compared to standard RNNs and variants.  
- Claims DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this task, with DRNNs showing different time scales per layer.  
- Architecture is clearly defined with precise mathematical notation and a well-designed experimental setup using BPC as the metric.  
- Multiple analyses support temporal hierarchy claims: output contribution removal, typo forgetting rates, and a parenthesis-closing task.  
- Paper acknowledges limitations such as DRNN-AO underperforming DRNN-1O and inability to handle nested parentheses.  
- Comparison to prior hierarchical RNN work is discussed, but distinction from related methods could be clearer.  
- Claim of state-of-the-art is overstated due to insufficient comparison with more recent models and methods.  
- No clear theoretical explanation for why time scales emerge in DRNNs; experimental evidence is strong but mechanistic understanding is lacking.  
- Training time (~10 days per network) is long and practical feasibility for large scale is not addressed.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible with effective figures and clear explanations of DRNN variants.  
- Some conceptual wording (temporal hierarchy vs. hierarchical processing) and distinctions from prior work need clearer articulation.  
- DRNN novelty lies in focusing on temporal hierarchies via stacked recurrent layers, with the typo perturbation analysis being particularly novel.  
- Novelty may be limited because DRNN is a straightforward extension of hierarchical RNNs; uniqueness versus prior art is not fully argued.  
- Significance: addresses long-term dependencies and shows DRNNs can model dependencies spanning hundreds of characters.  
- Significance is limited by evaluation on a single task and lack of comparison to alternatives for long-range dependencies (LSTM/GRU/attention/Transformers).  
- Layer-wise training method for DRNN-1O is a useful contribution for training deep recurrent architectures.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, provides valuable insights into temporal hierarchies, but significance claims need strengthening.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve comparisons, theoretical discussion, and clarity.  
- Specific suggestion: explicitly distinguish DRNN novelty from prior hierarchical RNNs, especially [5].  
- Specific suggestion: add comparisons to more recent language modeling approaches (e.g., Transformers, advanced RNNs).  
- Specific suggestion: expand theoretical discussion explaining why DRNN time scales develop.  
- Specific suggestion: discuss broader applicability to other sequence tasks (speech, MT) and explain DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O performance difference.  
- Pro arguments: clear novel architecture, thorough evaluation, multiple evidence lines for temporal hierarchies, strong presentation, and useful training method.  
- Con arguments: unsupported state-of-the-art claim, single-task evaluation, insufficient theoretical explanation, lack of comparisons to recent methods, unexplained DRNN-AO underperformance.
