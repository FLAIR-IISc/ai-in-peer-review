- Paper studies stacked vanilla RNNs (DRNNs) with two output schemes (top-only DRNN-1O vs aggregated-all DRNN-AO), claims stacking yields a temporal hierarchy, and evaluates on large-scale character-level language modeling.  
- Reports BPC comparable to MRNN trained with Hessian-free optimization, using plain SGD and gradient normalization.  
- Provides analyses: layer ablations, typo perturbation, context-swap experiments, and parentheses closing to probe layer-specific time scales and long-range signals.  

- Strength: Clear, simple architecture and careful comparison of output schemes.  
- Strength: Competitive large-scale results using standard SGD (no HF), suggesting careful design/training suffices.  
- Strength: Concrete, varied analyses offering evidence that higher layers capture longer contexts.  
- Strength: Practical incremental layer-wise training scheme that mitigates vanishing gradients.  
- Strength: Well written and situated in related hierarchical/stacked RNN literature.  

- Weakness: Missing strong baselines — no LSTM or stacked LSTM comparisons on the same dataset to contextualize results.  
- Weakness: BPC comparability confounded by different character vocabularies (95+UNK vs 86), claim of negligible difference is unverified.  
- Weakness: DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison confounded by different training curricula and unequal training budgets.  
- Weakness: Gradient normalization is unusual (appears to normalize to unit norm) and under-explained; unclear scope (global vs per-layer) and effects.  
- Weakness: Time-scale analysis uses Euclidean distances in tanh hidden space, which is hard to interpret due to saturation; context-swap BPC is more direct.  
- Weakness: Narrow experimental scope with limited hyperparameter exploration and some unusual initialization choices lacking ablation.  
- Weakness: State-of-the-art claims for “recurrent networks” are not substantiated without matched protocols and stronger baselines.  

- Quality: Technically sound modeling and clear analyses, but experimental evaluation needs stricter controls (matched budgets, vocab, BPTT details, gradient normalization).  
- Clarity requests: Clarify gradient normalization procedure, BPTT truncation/burn-in details, and data preprocessing/character mapping.  
- Originality: Incremental contribution—clean DRNN instantiation and useful analysis suite, but stacking and multi-depth outputs have precedents.  
- Significance: Moderate — useful empirical finding that stacked tanh RNNs can approach HF-trained MRNN and insightful analyses, but limited by missing baselines and protocol mismatches.  

- Suggested fixes: report results on the exact 86-character setup or provide a conversion; add LSTM baselines; equalize training budgets/curricula across variants; ablate gradient normalization and unusual initializations; complement hidden-state metrics with output-level or normalized measures; release code/models.  

- Pros for acceptance: strong large-scale results with a simple setup, insightful temporal-hierarchy analyses, and clear presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing LSTM baselines, comparability issues with MRNN, confounded DRNN variant comparison, and insufficient justification for some methodological choices.  

- Recommendation: Borderline accept — would be stronger if authors address matched vocab/protocol, add LSTM baselines, and clarify/ablate optimization details.
