- Proposes DRNNs by stacking RNN layers to create a hierarchy of time scales and applies it to character-level language modeling on Wikipedia, achieving state-of-the-art RNN performance using only SGD.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation of DRNNs and detailed experimental setup (network sizes, training parameters, metrics) enabling reproducibility.  
- Multiple experimental validations (performance comparisons, ablation studies, temporal context analysis) strongly support claims.  
- Empirical evidence (typo perturbation, parentheses closing) shows emergence of temporal hierarchy across layers.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses clear figures (e.g., architecture diagram); technical details are accessible to the audience.  
- Authors acknowledge and analyze counterintuitive result that DRNN-1O outperforms DRNN-AO but explanation (training vs architecture) is not fully convincing.  
- Novel contribution: focused analysis of how stacking RNNs creates temporal hierarchies, distinguishing it from prior stacking/subsampling approaches.  
- Lacks sufficient theoretical justification for why stacking produces different time scales across layers; needs more formal explanation.  
- Insufficient comparison to recent hierarchical RNN work and unclear distinction from some prior papers (e.g., [8]).  
- Significant practical impact: simplifies training (SGD vs Hessian-free) and improves long-range dependency modeling (parentheses closing: 88% vs 72% for standard RNN).  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution with thorough experiments; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, thorough experiments, SGD-based SOTA, compelling hierarchy evidence, careful evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical grounding, incomplete literature comparison, unclear DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO rationale, limited discussion on scalability to larger/complex tasks.
