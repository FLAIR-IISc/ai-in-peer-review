## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a hybrid architecture combining the hierarchical processing of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) with the temporal memory capabilities of Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors propose stacking RNNs to create a natural hierarchy of timescales and demonstrate strong performance on a character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus, achieving state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks *without* relying on advanced optimization techniques like Hessian-free training.  They also provide analysis into the timescales learned by different layers of the DRNN, and the contribution of each layer to the overall performance.

**Relationship to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing landscape of deep learning and recurrent neural networks. It acknowledges the success of DNNs, the memory-focused nature of RNNs, and previous attempts at hierarchical RNN architectures (e.g., [5, 8]). The authors differentiate their work by focusing on a specific architecture—stacking RNNs with temporal feedback in each layer—and providing a more in-depth analysis of the resulting temporal hierarchy, something notably lacking in [8]. They also demonstrate competitive performance with more complex optimization schemes.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Architecture:** The DRNN architecture is a reasonable and well-motivated approach to combining the strengths of DNNs and RNNs.  The idea of stacking RNNs to create inherent multi-timescale processing is intuitive and potentially powerful.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging character-level language modeling task *using only SGD* is a significant accomplishment, especially compared to previous work relying on more sophisticated optimization methods.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply reporting performance. The analysis of layer-wise timescales and contribution to output (Figures 2-4) provides valuable insights into how the network learns and operates.  The experiments relating to parenthesis matching are particularly insightful.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  The explanations of the architecture and experimental setup are clear and concise.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail regarding the experimental setup and network parameters to facilitate reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the results on the Wikipedia corpus are impressive, the experiments are somewhat limited.  Only three network instantiations were tested, and the architecture wasn't explored extensively with variations in layer size or connectivity. A more thorough hyperparameter search and architectural exploration would strengthen the claims.
*   **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** The paper relies heavily on empirical results. While the authors propose *why* a temporal hierarchy emerges, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the network's dynamics would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison to Modern Architectures:** The paper primarily compares itself to older results [19]. A comparison against more recent state-of-the-art architectures (e.g., Transformers) would provide a better context for evaluating the DRNN’s performance and limitations.
*   **Training Details:** While generally clear, the incremental layer-wise training method for the DRNN-1O is a bit convoluted and could be explained more clearly. The rationale for the differing learning rates is also slightly underspecified.
*   **Limited Discussion of Failure Cases:** The discussion of failure cases (e.g., nested parentheses) is brief. A more comprehensive analysis of the DRNN's limitations would strengthen the paper.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are generally supported by the experimental results, though further theoretical analysis would improve the robustness of the arguments. It's a complete piece of work presenting a novel approach and substantial analysis.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The explanations are generally accessible, though some training details could be elaborated.
*   **Originality:** The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of existing techniques. The detailed analysis of the temporal hierarchy is a significant contribution.
*   **Significance:** The results are important, demonstrating the potential of deep recurrent architectures and offering a competitive alternative to more complex optimization methods. The insights into learned timescales are valuable for understanding RNN behavior.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated architecture.
*   Provides detailed and insightful analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy.
*   Demonstrates strong empirical results without relying on complex optimization techniques.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited scope of experiments.
*   Lack of theoretical justification for the observed behavior.
*   Comparison to somewhat dated benchmarks.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. While there are some minor weaknesses, the novelty of the architecture, the strong empirical results, and the detailed analysis outweigh these concerns. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent neural networks and offers a promising new approach to sequential modeling.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above (e.g., adding a discussion of more recent benchmarks, elaborating on the training process for DRNN-1O).
