## NIPS Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

**Summary:**

This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a hybrid architecture combining the hierarchical processing of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) with the temporal memory capabilities of Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors argue that DRNNs naturally create multiple timescales for processing sequential data, addressing a limitation of standard RNNs. They present experiments on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance comparable to MRNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization, but using only Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD). Crucially, they also provide analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies within the DRNNs, demonstrating how different layers capture information at different timescales and contribute to the overall task.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the existing literature on both DNNs and RNNs, specifically Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. It acknowledges previous work on hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6]), but highlights its contribution as a more in-depth analysis and demonstration of the benefits of a specific DRNN architecture on a realistic task. It contextualizes their results relative to the work of Martens et al. ([19]) on RNNs and MRNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization, positioning their SGD-trained DRNNs as competitive and potentially more accessible.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination of Architectures:** The DRNN architecture is a logical and potentially powerful combination of DNNs and RNNs, explicitly addressing the need for hierarchical temporal processing.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging character-level language modeling task using a simpler training method (SGD) compared to previous work is a significant accomplishment.
* **Insightful Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply presenting results, offering valuable analysis of the learned timescales within the DRNN layers. The experiments relating to typo perturbation, context switching, and parenthesis handling provide concrete evidence for the emergence of temporal hierarchies.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the DRNN architecture and training process in a clear and understandable manner. The inclusion of Figure 1 is helpful for visualizing the network structure.
* **Thorough Experiments:** The authors conduct a variety of experiments, including ablation studies (removing layer outputs) and generative tests, to gain deeper insights into the DRNN's behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Network Instantiations:** Only three network configurations were tested. While the authors acknowledge the difficulty of training large models, more extensive experimentation with different layer sizes and architectures would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Lack of Comparative Analysis with LSTMs:** While the paper compares to other RNN variants, a direct comparison with LSTMs (especially with similar parameter counts) would be valuable. LSTMs are a strong baseline for sequential modeling.
* **Training Details Could Be More Detailed:** While the training setup is described, further details regarding hyperparameter tuning and the rationale behind specific choices (e.g., learning rate schedules) would be helpful for reproducibility.
* **Interpretability of Layer Contributions:** While the analysis of layer contributions is interesting, the authors acknowledge the limitations of interpreting them independently.  More sophisticated methods for analyzing layer interactions might provide further insight.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** The paper mentions the training time (10 days) but doesn't provide a detailed comparison of computational cost with other methods, especially Hessian-free optimization.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents well-supported claims. The experiments are well-designed and the analysis is insightful.  It's a complete piece of work within the scope defined.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clearly written, though some sections (e.g., the training setup) could benefit from more detailed explanations.
* **Originality:** The DRNN architecture and the detailed analysis of learned temporal hierarchies represent a novel contribution to the field.
* **Significance:** The results are important because they demonstrate the effectiveness of a DRNN architecture for a challenging task and provide insights into the emergent properties of these networks. The use of SGD to achieve state-of-the-art performance is also significant, potentially making this approach more accessible.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging task.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated architecture.
* Provides insightful analysis of the learned temporal dynamics.
* Offers a competitive alternative to more complex training methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited number of network configurations tested.
* Lack of a direct comparison with LSTMs.
* Some details of the training process could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach for modeling sequential data, and the authors have provided compelling evidence of its effectiveness and interesting insights into its behavior.  While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh them. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept** (with minor revisions - encouraging the authors to elaborate on training details and potentially include a comparison with LSTMs).
