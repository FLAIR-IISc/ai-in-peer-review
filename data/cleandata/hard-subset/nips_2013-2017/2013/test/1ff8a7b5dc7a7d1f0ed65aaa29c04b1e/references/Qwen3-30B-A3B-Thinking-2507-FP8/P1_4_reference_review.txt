

# Peer Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which extend standard RNNs by stacking multiple recurrent layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that DRNNs naturally create a temporal hierarchy with different layers operating at different time scales, which is beneficial for modeling language with both short and long-term dependencies. They compare DRNN variants (DRNN-1O with output connections only at the top layer, and DRNN-AO with output connections at all layers) against standard RNNs on a character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia text corpus. The paper demonstrates that DRNN-1O achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks (BPC = 1.488), outperforming standard RNNs, and provides analysis showing that bottom layers handle short-term dependencies while top layers handle longer-term ones. The authors also demonstrate DRNNs' ability to close parentheses after long intervening text, suggesting effective modeling of long-term dependencies.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Architecture with Strong Experimental Validation**: The DRNN concept is a meaningful extension of standard RNNs that creates a natural temporal hierarchy. The experimental results are compelling, showing DRNN-1O achieves better performance than standard RNNs using only SGD (avoiding complex Hessian-free optimization required by previous state-of-the-art work).

2. **Comprehensive Analysis**: The paper goes beyond performance metrics to provide insightful analysis of layer contributions through ablation studies and time-scale measurements. The parentheses-closing task is particularly clever for demonstrating long-term dependency modeling.

3. **Clear Writing and Organization**: The paper is well-structured with clear figures (Figures 1-4) that effectively illustrate the DRNN architecture and analysis results. The explanation of how DRNNs create temporal hierarchies is particularly accessible.

4. **Significant Practical Contribution**: The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance with simple SGD training is valuable for practitioners, as it avoids the computational complexity of second-order optimization methods.

## Weaknesses
1. **Critical Comparison Omission**: The paper fails to compare with LSTM networks, which are the standard for language modeling tasks. LSTMs are specifically designed to handle long-term dependencies, so the lack of comparison with this baseline is a major weakness. The authors cite Graves et al. [8] who used stacked bidirectional LSTMs for speech recognition, but don't compare their DRNN approach to this method on the same language modeling task.

2. **Limited Contextualization of Prior Work**: While the paper references hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6,8], it doesn't adequately position its contribution against the most relevant prior work. Graves et al. [8] already demonstrated stacked RNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance, so the paper should address how DRNNs differ from their approach and why DRNNs would be superior.

3. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The paper provides strong empirical evidence that DRNNs develop different time scales at different layers, but lacks theoretical justification for why this occurs. A brief theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper's contribution.

4. **Training Methodology for DRNN-1O**: The layer-wise training approach used for DRNN-1O (training one layer at a time, adding layers incrementally) is unconventional for deep architectures. The paper doesn't explore whether this is a fundamental requirement of the DRNN architecture or just a practical training strategy that might not generalize to other tasks.

## Overall Assessment
This is a solid contribution that demonstrates DRNNs can effectively model temporal hierarchies in language data. The core idea is novel and well-supported by experiments, and the performance improvements over standard RNNs are significant. However, the lack of comparison to LSTMs (the standard for language modeling) and insufficient contextualization against Graves et al.'s stacked RNN work are substantial weaknesses that prevent this from being a strong NIPS submission.

## Recommendation
I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding deep recurrent architectures, but the omission of critical comparisons (particularly with LSTMs) significantly limits the strength of the claims. If the authors could address these weaknesses in a revision (adding LSTM comparisons and clarifying how DRNNs differ from Graves et al.'s approach), this would be a strong candidate for acceptance.

## Pro and Con Arguments

**Pros:**
- Introduces a novel DRNN architecture that creates a natural temporal hierarchy
- Demonstrates clear performance improvements over standard RNNs using simple SGD
- Provides compelling analysis of layer contributions and time scales
- Well-written with clear figures and methodology
- Shows DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (parentheses-closing task)

**Cons:**
- No comparison to LSTMs (the standard for language modeling)
- Limited comparison to Graves et al.'s stacked RNN work on the same task
- Lacks theoretical justification for temporal hierarchy development
- DRNN-1O uses a special layer-wise training approach that may not generalize
- Does not address why DRNNs would be preferred over other deep RNN approaches

This paper has strong potential but requires addressing the critical comparison omissions to establish its significance in the context of existing language modeling approaches.
