## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), combining DNNs with RNNs to capture temporal hierarchies in character-based language modeling. The authors explore different output connection schemes (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) and demonstrate competitive performance on a Wikipedia text corpus, achieving state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks with a comparable parameter count, utilizing only stochastic gradient descent. The paper also provides some initial analysis of the learned time scales within the network layers.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture represents a plausible and potentially valuable combination of DNN and RNN paradigms. The idea of stacking RNNs to inherently create multiple time scales is intuitive and offers a potential advantage.
* **Competitive Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results with SGD is a notable accomplishment, particularly when compared to methods employing more sophisticated optimization techniques.
* **Initial Analysis:** The provided analyses regarding time scales and layer contributions, though preliminary, offer valuable insights into the network's behavior. The experiment with the typo perturbation and the parenthesis closure analysis are particularly insightful.
* **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind the DRNN architecture and its potential to address the limitations of traditional RNNs in capturing hierarchical temporal dependencies.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Rigorous Comparison:** While state-of-the-art *for recurrent networks with similar parameters* is claimed, the comparison to other strong baselines, like transformers or even more extensively optimized RNN variants (e.g., using techniques beyond Hessian-free optimization), is insufficient. The limitations of the parameter count comparison are acknowledged, but not adequately addressed.
* **Implementation Details & Reproducibility:** The description of the implementation is lacking crucial details. For example, the initialization schemes are described in broad strokes ("N(0, N^-1/2)"), but the specific values used in code are not provided. Furthermore, the statement that "We need time in the order of 10 days to train a single network" raises concerns about reproducibility, especially given the limited number of instantiations tested (only three).
* **Inconsistent Training Procedures:** The different training strategies employed for DRNN-1O (incremental layer-wise) and DRNN-AO are not fully justified. The rationale provided ("effectively trained") feels ad-hoc and a deeper investigation into the optimal training procedure is needed. The varying number of parameter updates per layer in DRNN-1O further complicates the comparison.
* **Superficial Analysis:** The analysis of time scales, while interesting, is limited. The conclusions drawn from the perturbation experiments are somewhat speculative and would benefit from more quantitative analysis. The observation regarding parenthesis closure, though intriguing, lacks deeper exploration and generalization.
* **Formatting & Minor Errors:** There are numerous minor issues throughout the paper. For instance, inconsistent notation (e.g., x̄ vs. ā), grammatical errors ("debatably falls into the category…"), and missing punctuation detract from the overall polish. The reference list formatting is also inconsistent. Specifically, some references are missing fields (e.g., page numbers).
* **Redundancy and Verbosity:** Some sections (particularly the introduction) are overly verbose and contain redundant statements. The discussion of related work feels cursory and could benefit from a more in-depth engagement with the relevant literature.
* **Ambiguous Claims:** The statement regarding "state-of-the-art performance" is qualified, yet still presented as a significant achievement. A more nuanced discussion of the limitations of this performance and the scope of the comparison is necessary.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The DRNN architecture is a novel and potentially valuable contribution.
* The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a challenging task.
* The initial analysis provides insights into the network's behavior.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of rigorous comparison to stronger baselines.
* Insufficient implementation details and concerns about reproducibility.
* Superficial analysis and lack of quantitative validation.
* Numerous minor errors and inconsistencies in the manuscript.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite presenting a novel architecture and achieving competitive results, this paper suffers from significant shortcomings in terms of rigor, reproducibility, and presentation. The analysis, while insightful, feels preliminary and lacks the depth expected for a NIPS publication. The numerous minor errors and inconsistencies detract from the overall quality of the manuscript. While the idea behind the DRNN is promising, the paper falls short of providing a convincing demonstration of its superiority over existing approaches. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**. While the core idea holds promise, the paper needs substantial revision to address the weaknesses outlined above before it would be suitable for publication at NIPS. The authors should focus on providing a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art baselines, improving the reproducibility of their results, and conducting a more rigorous analysis of the network's behavior. A careful review and correction of the minor errors and inconsistencies is also essential.
