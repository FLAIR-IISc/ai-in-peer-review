- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling on Wikipedia, comparing single-output (1O) and all-output (AO) variants and using SGD with BPTT truncated to 250 steps.
- Empirically, ~4.9M-parameter DRNNs outperform single-layer RNNs and approach HF-trained multiplicative RNN performance.
- Analysis includes perturbation experiments, layer ablations, and a parentheses-closing probe showing evidence of temporal hierarchies and long-range dependencies.
- Relation to prior work: builds on hierarchical/multi-timescale RNNs and stacked LSTMs; AO resembles deep supervision/skip connections and DRNN-1O incremental training is like supervised pretraining.
- Main contribution is empirical and analytical scale study of simple tanh DRNNs trained with straightforward SGD.
- Strength: clear problem motivation focusing on short- and long-term dependencies.
- Strength: solid engineering—large dataset, careful parameter budgeting, and consistent training setups.
- Strength: demonstrates competitive BPC with simple architectures and provides insightful temporal analyses.
- Strength: incremental training for DRNN-1O is practical and helps optimization of deep recurrent stacks.
- Weakness: missing important baselines—no gated RNN or stacked LSTM comparison, limiting assessment of practical value.
- Weakness: limited ablation experiments and single-seed runs raise concerns about robustness and variance.
- Weakness: unusual gradient normalization to unit norm lacks justification or comparison to clipping/adaptive optimizers.
- Weakness: no clear validation protocol, possible test-set leakage, and some analyses performed on the test set.
- Weakness: AO ablations measure direct output contribution but not preprocessing effects; hierarchy interpretation is partly driven by curriculum.
- Weakness: reporting lacks exact BPC numbers and some hyperparameter/training details needed for reproducibility.
- Weakness: no discussion of regularization; zero-initialized output weights are unconventional and possibly problematic.
- Quality: technically sound with careful experiments, but claims about induced time scales remain qualitative and lack theoretical backing.
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but missing validation details, exact metrics, and some reference formatting.
- Originality: moderate—stacking and AO connections are incremental; novelty lies in scale and empirical analyses.
- Significance: moderate—useful demonstration for practitioners but impact limited without gated baselines.
- Key questions: report exact test BPCs, validation protocol, sensitivity to gradient normalization, number of seeds, stacked LSTM baseline, and effect of BPTT truncation on long-range behavior.
- Suggested improvements: add stacked LSTM baseline or discuss comparable results, use a validation split, compare optimization methods, run multiple seeds, and analyze per-layer gradients/information flow.
- Recommendation: borderline accept pending stronger baselines and clarified reporting.
- Reviewer confidence: high.
