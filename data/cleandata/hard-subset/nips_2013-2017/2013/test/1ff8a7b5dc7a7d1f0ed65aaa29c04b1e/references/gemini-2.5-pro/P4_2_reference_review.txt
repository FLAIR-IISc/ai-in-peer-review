### Summary of the Paper

This paper introduces and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. In this model, the sequence of hidden states from a lower layer serves as the input sequence for the layer directly above it. The authors propose that this hierarchical structure is well-suited for modeling data with temporal hierarchies, such as natural language, by allowing different layers to operate on different time scales.

The paper investigates two variants of the DRNN: one where the output is generated solely from the top layer (DRNN-1O), and another where all layers contribute to the final output (DRNN-AO). The authors evaluate these models on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. Their primary contributions are twofold: first, they demonstrate that DRNNs trained with a simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) procedure can achieve state-of-the-art performance, comparable to more complex models trained with second-order methods like Hessian-Free optimization [19]. Second, they provide an in-depth analysis of the trained models, presenting empirical evidence that a temporal hierarchy emerges during training, with higher layers learning longer-range dependencies.

The concept of stacked or hierarchical RNNs is not entirely new and has been explored in prior work, notably in speech recognition [8]. However, this paper distinguishes itself by providing a detailed analysis of the model's internal dynamics and learned properties, which was largely absent in previous performance-focused studies.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:**
The paper is technically sound. The claims are well-supported by a series of carefully designed experiments. The core result—achieving state-of-the-art bits-per-character (BPC) on a standard benchmark with a simpler optimization method—is a strong and well-substantiated claim. The work appears complete, moving from model definition to training and then to a thorough analysis.

The authors are transparent about the training methods, including the use of gradient normalization to handle exploding gradients and a specific incremental layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O model. This layer-wise training, where the full network is retrained after adding each new layer, was noted as essential for good performance, which is an important practical finding. The analysis of the learned time scales via perturbation experiments (typo introduction and context switching) provides convincing evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. Further qualitative analysis, such as the parenthesis-closing experiment and text generation under layer ablation, effectively demonstrates the functional specialization of different layers.

**Clarity:**
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated in the introduction, contrasting them with standard RNNs and DNNs. The model architecture and training procedures are described with sufficient detail to enable reproducibility. The figures and tables are informative and effectively support the paper's narrative. For instance, Figure 3 clearly visualizes the different memory decay rates across layers, providing strong support for the central hypothesis of learned temporal hierarchies. The text generation examples in Table 2 offer intuitive insight into the roles of the bottom and top layers.

**Originality:**
The primary architectural idea of stacking recurrent layers has precedents in the literature (e.g., [8]). The authors correctly acknowledge this and frame their contribution not as the invention of the architecture, but as a rigorous analysis of its properties and a demonstration of its effectiveness on a challenging language modeling task with a simple training algorithm. The novelty, therefore, lies in the set of analytical techniques used to probe the network and the insights gained from them. The finding that different output connection schemes (DRNN-1O vs. DRNN-AO) and training procedures (end-to-end vs. incremental) lead to different internal dynamics is a valuable and original contribution to the understanding of these models.

**Significance:**
The paper's results are highly significant. By showing that a DRNN trained with SGD can match the performance of a multiplicative RNN trained with a powerful second-order optimizer [19], the work suggests that architectural choices can be as important as, or even substitute for, more sophisticated optimization algorithms. This has practical implications, as SGD is simpler to implement and parallelize.

The detailed analysis provides a compelling demonstration that deep recurrent architectures naturally learn to separate temporal dependencies across layers. This insight is valuable for researchers working on sequence modeling and is likely to inspire further work on hierarchical temporal models. The finding that higher layers capture longer-range context (e.g., sentence structure indicated by parentheses) while lower layers handle more local statistics is a concrete result that advances the community's understanding of how deep learning models process sequential data.

### Arguments for Acceptance:
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on a widely used character-level language modeling benchmark.
*   **Significant Finding on Optimization:** Demonstrates that a deep architecture trained with first-order SGD can match the performance of models requiring complex second-order methods, offering a simpler path to high performance.
*   **Excellent Analysis:** Provides a thorough and insightful analysis of the trained models, offering strong evidence for the emergence of a learned temporal hierarchy. This goes beyond a simple "SOTA" paper and contributes to the scientific understanding of deep learning models.
*   **Clarity and Reproducibility:** The paper is clearly written, and the experimental setup is detailed enough to facilitate the reproduction of its results.

### Arguments Against Acceptance:
*   **Limited Architectural Novelty:** The core idea of stacking RNN layers is not new, as acknowledged by the authors. The contribution is in analysis rather than architectural innovation.
*   ** reliance on Heuristics:** The success of the models relies on gradient normalization and, for the best-performing model, a specialized layer-wise training procedure. This indicates that training these deep architectures is non-trivial and may require problem-specific heuristics.
*   **Single-Task Evaluation:** The experiments are confined to a single task and dataset. While the task is challenging, the generality of the findings about learned hierarchies could be strengthened by evaluation on other sequence modeling domains.
