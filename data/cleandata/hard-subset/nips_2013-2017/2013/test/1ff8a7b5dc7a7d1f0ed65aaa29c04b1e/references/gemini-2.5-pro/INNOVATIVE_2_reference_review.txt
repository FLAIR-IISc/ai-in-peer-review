**Summary**

This paper introduces and analyzes the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure naturally induces a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process information on shorter time scales and higher layers capture longer-term dependencies. The authors test this hypothesis on the challenging task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. Through a series of insightful experiments, they provide evidence for the emergence of this temporal hierarchy and demonstrate that their model achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks of its size, impressively using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The paper’s main contribution is not merely the architecture itself, but the in-depth analysis of its internal dynamics, offering a compelling explanation for its effectiveness.

**Strengths (Reasons for Acceptance)**

1.  **Novel and Insightful Analysis:** The paper's greatest strength lies in its commitment to understanding *why* the proposed architecture works. Instead of simply presenting a new model and a benchmark score, the authors conduct a series of elegant experiments to probe the network's internal state. The "typo perturbation" test (Figure 3, left) provides compelling visual evidence for the core hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy, showing that higher layers retain memory of a perturbation for significantly longer. The analysis of parenthesis closing is particularly impressive, demonstrating that the model learns a robust, long-range dependency that generalizes beyond its training sequence length, suggesting it has captured a structural rule rather than just memorizing patterns. This type of deep analysis is often missing in performance-focused papers and represents a significant contribution to our understanding of deep sequential models.

2.  **Strong Conceptual Contribution:** The idea that stacking recurrent layers naturally creates a hierarchy of time scales is a simple, powerful, and intuitive concept. The paper successfully elevates this from a folk theorem to a well-supported scientific claim. This provides a clear conceptual motivation for building deep recurrent models beyond the simple intuition that "deeper is better." This direction—designing architectures with inherent inductive biases for hierarchical processing—is a promising avenue for future research.

3.  **Impressive Empirical Results with Simple Methods:** The paper achieves performance comparable to the state-of-the-art on a difficult benchmark (Sutskever et al., 2011), but does so using only SGD with gradient clipping. The prior work required a far more complex and computationally expensive Hessian-free optimizer. This result is significant in its own right, as it suggests that with the right architecture, simpler and more scalable training methods can be highly effective. It democratizes access to state-of-the-art sequence modeling by lowering the barrier of algorithmic complexity.

**Weaknesses (Minor Concerns)**

1.  **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The authors test only a small number of network configurations (a shallow RNN and two 5-layer DRNNs) with a fixed number of parameters. A more thorough study would have explored the effect of varying depth, the width of layers, or different connectivity patterns. While the computational cost is acknowledged as a major constraint, this narrow scope leaves some questions unanswered about the generality of the findings. However, given that the paper's focus is on analysis rather than an exhaustive architectural search, this is a forgivable limitation.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training scheme used for the DRNN-1O model feels somewhat heuristic. While it proves effective, it complicates the training process and makes a direct comparison with the jointly-trained DRNN-AO less straightforward. The paper could benefit from a brief discussion on why this was necessary and whether alternative solutions (e.g., careful initialization, different nonlinearities) were explored.

3.  **Incremental Aspect of the Architecture:** As the authors note, the idea of stacked or hierarchical RNNs is not entirely new (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1996; Graves et al., 2013). The architectural proposal itself could be seen as an incremental step. However, this weakness is almost entirely mitigated by the paper's true contribution, which is the novel analysis and the clear articulation and verification of the temporal hierarchy hypothesis.

**Recommendation: Accept**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. While the architecture itself builds on prior work and the empirical validation is not exhaustive, the paper's core contribution is exceptionally strong. It presents a bold and insightful analysis that moves beyond simply chasing state-of-the-art metrics to provide a deeper understanding of how deep recurrent models function. The experiments demonstrating the learned temporal hierarchy are novel and provide a compelling narrative that will likely influence how researchers think about designing and analyzing sequential models.

This paper is a perfect example of a submission that prioritizes a significant new concept—the emergence and role of a temporal hierarchy—over methodological perfection. The flaws are minor and do not detract from the importance of the central message. This is exactly the kind of thought-provoking, analysis-driven research that NIPS should champion.
