

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper proposes a "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture, which stacks RNN layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors claim this creates a temporal hierarchy with different layers operating at different time scales, making it suitable for tasks requiring modeling of both short- and long-term dependencies, such as character-level language modeling. The paper builds on previous work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and references [8] where stacked bidirectional LSTMs were used for speech recognition, but argues their work provides more in-depth analysis of the architecture's properties.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-structured analysis of temporal hierarchy:** The paper provides compelling experimental evidence for the emergence of different time scales across layers in DRNNs. The typo-perturbation test (Figure 3) clearly demonstrates that lower layers have shorter memory while higher layers have longer memory, confirming the authors' theoretical claim about temporal hierarchy.

2. **Practical performance results:** The DRNN-AO achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (BPC=1.55) using only SGD, whereas previous state-of-the-art required Hessian-free optimization [19]. This is a significant practical contribution, as SGD is more scalable and easier to implement than second-order methods.

3. **Insightful analysis of long-term dependencies:** The parentheses closing test (Figure 4) provides strong evidence that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies beyond the training sequence length, with DRNN-AO successfully closing parentheses in 88% of cases compared to 72% for standard RNNs.

4. **Clear experimental design:** The paper provides sufficient detail about the experimental setup, including model configurations, training parameters, and evaluation metrics, making the results reproducible.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited novelty and theoretical justification:** While the DRNN architecture is a combination of DNNs and RNNs, it builds directly on previous work [8] without clearly articulating how it differs from or improves upon those approaches. The paper lacks a strong theoretical foundation for why temporal hierarchy would improve performance, relying primarily on empirical evidence.

2. **Marginal performance gains:** The improvement over standard RNNs is modest (BPC 1.55 vs. 1.58), and the paper doesn't demonstrate that DRNNs would outperform standard RNNs on other tasks beyond language modeling. The performance gap is also similar to what was achieved with Hessian-free optimization [19], suggesting the gains may be limited.

3. **Limited comparison to recent architectures:** The paper doesn't compare DRNNs to more recent state-of-the-art architectures like transformers or attention mechanisms, which have largely superseded RNNs for language modeling tasks. This omission weakens the significance of the results.

4. **Insufficient analysis of DRNN-1O training method:** The layer-wise training approach for DRNN-1O is described but not thoroughly justified or compared to other training strategies. The paper claims this method is necessary to reach good performance, but doesn't explain why standard BPTT wouldn't work.

**Constructive suggestions for improvement:**

1. **Strengthen the theoretical foundation:** Provide more formal analysis of why temporal hierarchy benefits language modeling tasks. How does the DRNN architecture specifically address limitations of standard RNNs beyond just adding more layers?

2. **Expand experimental comparisons:** Include comparisons to more recent architectures (transformers, attention mechanisms) to better establish the significance of DRNNs in the current landscape of language modeling.

3. **Clarify novelty:** More clearly distinguish the DRNN from previous hierarchical RNN work [5, 8] and explain why their specific architecture offers advantages. The paper currently states "more in-depth analysis" but doesn't specify what makes their analysis novel.

4. **Investigate DRNN limitations:** The paper demonstrates DRNNs can model long-term dependencies, but doesn't explore their limitations. For instance, how do DRNNs perform on tasks with very long-range dependencies compared to transformers?

**Overall assessment:**

The paper presents a well-executed empirical study of a DRNN architecture with interesting insights about temporal hierarchy. While the practical demonstration of state-of-the-art performance using only SGD is valuable, the novelty is limited and the significance is somewhat diminished by the marginal performance gains and lack of comparison to more recent architectures. The paper would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding, clearer articulation of novelty, and more comprehensive comparisons to contemporary approaches.

**Recommendation:** Weak reject. The paper makes a modest contribution to the field, but the lack of strong theoretical justification, limited novelty, and omission of recent architectures prevent it from meeting the high standards of NIPS. With additional analysis, comparisons to current state-of-the-art methods, and clearer articulation of the contribution, this work could be significantly strengthened for future submission.
