

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal dynamics of recurrent networks. The key insight is that stacking RNNs inherently creates a temporal hierarchy, where each layer operates at a different time scale, with lower layers capturing short-term dependencies and higher layers capturing longer-term context. This is a significant conceptual contribution that challenges the conventional view of RNNs as simple sequential processors.

The paper makes two primary claims: (1) DRNNs create a natural temporal hierarchy without requiring explicit design, and (2) they achieve state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using only stochastic gradient descent, avoiding more complex training methods like Hessian-free optimization previously required for similar performance. The authors provide both theoretical analysis and experimental evidence to support these claims.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with strong connections to prior work. It properly situates itself within the literature on hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6], acknowledging that similar architectures have been used before (citing [8] for stacked bi-directional LSTMs), but emphasizes the novel insight about temporal hierarchy as the key contribution. The experimental section is thorough, with careful analysis of time scales at different layers, the role of each layer in the hierarchy, and specific tests on long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses).

The main strength of this paper is its conceptual novelty. While stacking RNNs has been done before, the authors' insight that this stacking creates an inherent temporal hierarchy is both elegant and significant. This is a bold approach that challenges conventional understanding of RNNs and could influence future architecture design. The paper provides compelling evidence for this claim through multiple experiments, including the time-scale analysis in Figure 3 and the parentheses-closing task in Figure 4.

The paper also demonstrates that DRNNs can achieve competitive performance with simpler training methods. The authors show that DRNN-AO reaches state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling with just SGD, while previous work required more advanced training techniques. This is an important practical contribution that could make deep recurrent architectures more accessible.

However, there are some limitations worth noting. The paper only evaluates the architecture on character-level language modeling, limiting the scope of its claims. While this is a well-chosen task for demonstrating temporal hierarchies, more diverse experiments would strengthen the case for broader applicability. The paper also doesn't fully address how DRNNs compare to other hierarchical approaches like LSTMs with attention mechanisms, which have been successful at handling long-term dependencies.

Despite these limitations, the paper's novelty and conceptual contribution stand out. The insight that stacking RNNs creates a natural temporal hierarchy is significant and could lead to new directions in recurrent architecture design. The experimental evidence supporting this claim is compelling, and the paper successfully demonstrates that this architectural insight translates to improved performance on a challenging task.

The paper is technically sound with clear methodology and sufficient detail for reproduction. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the need for layer-wise training in the DRNN-1O variant, and provide thoughtful analysis of the results. The writing is clear and well-organized, making the contribution accessible to readers.

In terms of significance, this paper addresses a fundamental question about how recurrent networks process temporal information and offers a novel architectural approach that could influence future research. The demonstration of temporal hierarchy in DRNNs is particularly important for understanding how deep architectures process sequential data, which is a core challenge in machine learning.

While the performance gains compared to prior work are not enormous (the DRNN-1O achieves 1.47 BPC vs. 1.46 for Hessian-free methods), the conceptual contribution and the fact that this performance was achieved with simpler training methods make this work significant. The paper's focus on understanding the architecture rather than just achieving state-of-the-art results is particularly valuable.

In my view, this paper represents a significant conceptual advance in understanding recurrent neural networks. The insight about temporal hierarchy in stacked RNNs is novel, well-supported by evidence, and has clear implications for future work. While the paper could be strengthened by more diverse experiments, the core contribution is substantial enough to warrant acceptance.

I recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS. The novelty of the DRNN architecture and the insight about temporal hierarchy provide a clear new direction for research in recurrent networks, which aligns well with NIPS's focus on significant conceptual contributions. The paper's technical quality is high, and it makes a meaningful contribution to our understanding of how recurrent networks process temporal information.
