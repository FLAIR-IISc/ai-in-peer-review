## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a sensible and well-executed investigation into deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), bridging the gap between traditional deep neural networks and recurrent architectures. While the ideas aren't radically novel – stacking RNNs and adding output connections have been explored – the paper offers a valuable analysis, solid empirical results on a challenging task (character-level language modeling), and a good discussion of the resulting temporal hierarchies. I believe this work is a **strong accept** for NIPS, although some minor clarifications and expansions would further strengthen the contribution.

**Summary of Ideas:** The paper proposes DRNNs, which are essentially multi-layered RNNs where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture aims to combine the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal modeling capabilities of RNNs, creating inherent multi-scale temporal hierarchies. The authors explore two output configurations – DRNN-1O (output from the top layer only) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers) – and analyze their performance on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  Crucially, they achieve state-of-the-art performance using only SGD, rivaling results obtained with more complex optimization algorithms like Hessian-free optimization.  The analysis focuses on the emergence of temporal hierarchies within the DRNNs, examining layer-wise time scales and the contribution of each layer to the overall performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:** The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature. It acknowledges the work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5]) and stacked bi-directional LSTMs [8], highlighting the lack of detailed analysis in the latter.  The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is well-placed and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors’ awareness of related work on language modeling, including compression algorithms like PAQ [14], is commendable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task using a relatively simple optimization algorithm (SGD) is a significant accomplishment. This immediately establishes the practical value of the proposed architecture.
*   **Detailed Analysis:**  The paper goes beyond simply presenting results. The analysis of layer-wise time scales, the influence of individual layers on performance, and the ability to model long-term dependencies (demonstrated through parenthesis prediction) provides valuable insights into the behavior of DRNNs.
*   **Clear Motivation and Justification:**  The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind DRNNs, explaining how they address the limitations of both traditional DNNs and standard RNNs.  The rationale behind the different output configurations (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) is also well-explained.
*   **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand the results.
* **Insightful Exploration of Temporal Hierarchies**: The investigation into different timescales inherent to each layer is a key contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The paper focuses solely on character-level language modeling. While this is a strong test case, it would be beneficial to see how DRNNs perform on other tasks that require temporal modeling, such as speech recognition or time series prediction.
*   **Network Size and Generalizability:** The experiments are limited to a specific network size (4.9 million parameters). It is unclear how the performance and the observed temporal hierarchies would change with larger or smaller networks.  The authors acknowledge this limitation.
*   **Lack of Ablation Studies:** While the authors analyze layer contributions, a more comprehensive ablation study exploring the impact of different hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, layer size) or architectural variations (e.g., different activation functions) would strengthen the analysis.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** The authors mention the training time (10 days on a single GPU), but a more detailed discussion of the computational cost, especially in comparison to other approaches, would be valuable. The limitations of single-GPU training should be highlighted further.
* **Minor Clarification Needed:** The discussion of training the DRNN-1O incrementally is somewhat confusing. More detail on the precise training schedule would improve clarity.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging task with a relatively simple optimization method.
*   Provides a detailed analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies within the DRNN architecture.
*   Offers valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different DRNN configurations (DRNN-1O vs. DRNN-AO).
*   The work is well-written, well-motivated, and clearly situated within the existing literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scope of experiments is limited to a single task.
*   The generalization ability of the results is unclear due to the limited number of network configurations tested.
*   The lack of comprehensive ablation studies limits the depth of the analysis.




**Overall, I strongly recommend accepting this paper for NIPS.** The work presents a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent neural networks, offering both practical insights and a compelling analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further enhance the impact of this research. I believe the strengths of this work significantly outweigh its weaknesses, and it is likely to be of interest to a broad audience within the NIPS community.
