- Explores Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, demonstrating competitive performance and analyzing hierarchical time-scale dynamics.  
- Situates work within hierarchical RNN literature and highlights training challenges; proposes gradient normalization and layer-wise training.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art recurrent-network results on Wikipedia using SGD, matching more complex Hessian-free methods.  
- Provides evidence that different DRNN layers learn different time scales, supported by typo perturbation and context-switching experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and uses helpful figures.  
- Practical contributions include a layer-wise training strategy for DRNN-1O and a gradient normalization technique.  
- Analyzes each layer’s contribution to outputs, clarifying the role of layers in the hierarchy.  
- Limited exploration of alternative architectures (few layer counts, neuron sizes, or recurrent cell types like GRUs).  
- Computational expense and reliance on a single dataset (Wikipedia) restrict experiment scope and generalizability.  
- Lacks a direct, size- and budget-equivalent comparison to LSTMs.  
- Limited discussion of generalization and potential overfitting.  
- Minor clarity issue: notation for “augmented” bias terms is ambiguous.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand LSTM comparison discussion, address generalization/overfitting, clarify bias notation, and consider adding a figure visualizing learned timescales.
