- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that create a temporal hierarchy with each layer operating at different time scales.  
- Evaluates DRNNs on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus) and compares output-from-top-layer (DRNN-1O) vs. outputs-from-all-layers (DRNN-AO).  
- Reports state-of-the-art recurrent network performance for this task (1.48 BPC vs. 1.58 BPC for standard RNNs).  
- Provides multiple complementary analyses showing distinct layer time scales: disabling layer outputs, perturbation decay, and long-term dependency tests.  
- Training methodology is well-described and includes a gradient normalization trick to prevent gradient explosions.  
- Experiments are technically sound and reproducible, though some training hyperparameters (e.g., BPTT batch size) could be clearer.  
- Theoretical justification for why DRNNs outperform standard RNNs is limited and underdeveloped.  
- Connection and distinction from prior hierarchical RNN work ([3,5,6]) are insufficiently explained.  
- Architecture and experiments are clearly presented with helpful figures and generative text examples.  
- Original contribution lies in demonstrating how deep recurrence naturally yields temporal hierarchies across layers.  
- Novelty is tempered by building on prior stacked LSTM work and lacking explicit architectural/training comparisons to related approaches.  
- Demonstrates compelling long-term dependency handling (e.g., closing parentheses) as evidence of hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Performance gains are modest and training is computationally expensive (≈10 days per network), limiting practicality.  
- Comparison to Hessian-free training is limited; authors acknowledge slower training without clear superiority.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theory and clarify relation to prior work.  
- Strengths: clear temporal-hierarchy evidence, state-of-the-art recurrent results, detailed layer analyses, and long-term dependency demonstrations.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical explanation, modest gains, long training times, and insufficient distinction from prior hierarchical RNNs.  
- Suggestions: expand theoretical discussion of hierarchy emergence, explicitly compare to other hierarchical RNNs, discuss computational trade-offs, and analyze scaling with network size.
