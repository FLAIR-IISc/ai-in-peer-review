- Paper studies stacked vanilla RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) for character-level language modeling on Wikipedia, achieving near-SOTA BPC with ~4.9M parameters.
- DRNN-1O connects only the top layer to output and uses incremental layer-wise training; DRNN-AO connects all layers to output.
- Training uses SGD with gradient normalization and linear learning-rate decay; DRNNs outperform a same-size single-layer RNN.
- Empirical analyses include typo perturbations, context-switch tests, per-layer output ablations, and a focused parentheses long-range dependency study.
- Main novelty: showing plain SGD can train deep vanilla RNN stacks competitively and demonstrating learned temporal hierarchies across layers.
- Strength: solid empirical result on a large corpus using simple, broadly applicable optimization tricks.
- Strength: insightful analyses that reveal different per-layer time scales and concrete long-range behavior (parentheses).
- Strength: clear writing and sensible experimental organization.
- Weakness: no comparisons to gated architectures (LSTM/GRU) or deep LSTM stacks, limiting significance.
- Weakness: experiments limited to one dataset and three model instantiations with minimal hyperparameter search due to compute constraints.
- Weakness: some training/evaluation details under-specified (state resets, exact gradient-normalization target, test BPC protocol), hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: DRNN-AO slightly underperforms DRNN-1O without sufficient diagnostics to explain why (e.g., gradient interference).
- Weakness: time-scale metric (Euclidean distance after perturbation) is heuristic and sensitive to scaling/saturation; complementary measures are needed.
- Quality: technically sound with credible results, but lacks multiple seeds/statistical variability and broader baselines.
- Clarity: generally clear but needs added implementation details (state handling, normalization, initialization, evaluation protocol).
- Originality: moderate—stacking and all-layer outputs have precedent; novelty lies in systematic analysis and showing SGD suffices.
- Significance: moderate-to-high if positioned properly, but practical impact depends on comparisons to gated RNNs.
- Suggestions: add LSTM/GRU baselines, clarify state/reset and test protocols, report BPC variance over seeds, and release code or pseudo-code.
- Suggestions: analyze DRNN-AO’s underperformance (per-layer gradients, regularization, learned gating) and use complementary time-scale diagnostics or synthetic tasks.
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical results, useful analyses of temporal hierarchies, and clear ablations.
- Cons against acceptance: missing gated baselines, limited experimental breadth, reproducibility gaps, and heuristic time-scale measures.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept; paper is promising but would be substantially strengthened by gated baselines and clearer reproducibility details.
