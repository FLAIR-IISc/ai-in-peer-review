Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies stacked recurrent neural networks in which each layer is itself a simple (tanh) RNN, and higher layers take the hidden state of the layer below as input. Two output variants are considered: top-layer-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO). The authors evaluate on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) with ≈5M parameters, trained with SGD using gradient normalization and linear learning rate decay. They compare to a single-layer RNN and to prior multiplicative RNN (MRNN) results trained with Hessian-free (HF), and provide analyses: (i) ablation of layer-wise output contributions, (ii) hidden-state perturbation and context-switch experiments to reveal emergent time scales, and (iii) qualitative/quantitative behavior on long-term dependencies (parentheses closure).

The work is related to hierarchical/stacked RNNs (El Hihi & Bengio ’96; Fernández et al.; Graves et al. stacked bi-LSTM) and to HF-trained MRNNs on the same dataset (Sutskever et al.). The novelty is not in the architecture per se—stacking RNNs is known—but in the careful training protocol for DRNN-1O and in the empirical analyses of temporal hierarchies and long-term interactions in a real-world corpus.

Strengths
- Clear motivation for stacking RNNs to induce temporal hierarchies; connects to known limitations of single-layer RNNs.
- Solid empirical result: DRNNs trained with straightforward SGD approach MRNN+HF performance with comparable parameter count; useful practical contribution given simpler training.
- Interesting analyses:
  - Output ablations suggest lower layers model short-term statistics while upper layers contribute more to longer-range predictions.
  - Hidden-state “typo” perturbation and context-switch experiments provide evidence for emergent, layer-wise time scales; the difference between DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O is insightful.
  - Parentheses closure experiments are a nice probe of long-term dependencies, with quantitative success rates.
- Training details are mostly provided (BPTT length, batch sizes, initialization, schedule), and code platform (Theano) is stated.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Novelty/positioning: Stacked RNNs (and stacked LSTMs) are established; the architectural idea is incremental. The main contribution is empirical analysis rather than a new model. Related work coverage is somewhat sparse on stacked RNNs in NLP/text; the contrast to stacked LSTM baselines is missing.
- Fairness of comparisons:
  - Dataset/tokenization mismatch with MRNN (95+1 vs 86 characters) is hand-waved; impact should be quantified (e.g., train both vocabularies).
  - Compute/training budget comparisons are unclear; performance vs. number of passes through data or wall-clock should be normalized where possible.
- Training protocol confounds: DRNN-1O uses a supervised layer-wise incremental scheme that reinitializes output weights when adding layers, while DRNN-AO is trained end-to-end. This likely contributes to the different time-scale profiles and makes a clean architectural comparison difficult. An ablation showing DRNN-1O without incremental training (or DRNN-AO with a comparable staged schedule) would clarify.
- Regularization/generalization: No validation set is described; test BPC appears to be monitored throughout. There is no discussion of overfitting, early stopping, or hyperparameter selection; variance across random initializations is asserted but not reported.
- Gradient normalization: The update normalizes the full gradient to unit norm with a linearly decaying scalar step size. This is a non-standard choice distinct from clipping; no ablation is provided to attribute gains or training stability to this heuristic.
- Analyses, while interesting, would benefit from more quantification:
  - Report effective time constants (e.g., half-life) per layer with confidence intervals, and normalize hidden-state distances by layer dimension/variance.
  - Parentheses analysis is partly anecdotal; the quantitative closure test is good, but distributions (lengths, failure modes) and comparisons to the single-layer RNN should be reported more fully.
- Clarity/minor issues:
  - Eq. (3) appears to have a sign/parenthesis typo as written in the text; please verify the intended schedule.
  - Zero-initializing output weights merits brief justification regarding symmetry breaking (it is safe but worth noting).
  - Important training details are missing or ambiguous: hidden state initialization; sequence sampling scheme (contiguous vs. random offsets), any gradient clip thresholds beyond normalization, weight decay or other regularization, and the exact dev/test split protocol.

Quality
Technically competent, with reasonable experimental rigor on a large corpus and thoughtful analyses. Some methodological choices (incremental training only for DRNN-1O; lack of dev set; atypical gradient normalization) limit the strength of causal conclusions.

Clarity
Generally well written and organized. Figures (referenced but not visible here) sound informative. Add clarifications noted above, and tighten comparisons to prior art.

Originality
Moderate. Architecture is a straightforward stacking of Elman RNNs. The contributions are in training practice (incremental supervised pretraining for DRNN-1O) and in empirical analyses of time scales and long-term dependencies on realistic data.

Significance
Moderate. Showing that stacked simple RNNs with SGD can match HF-trained MRNNs at similar scale is a useful practical message. The time-scale analyses are likely to interest researchers studying hierarchical temporal representations. However, absence of comparisons to stacked LSTMs (a prominent baseline) weakens the impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add a stacked LSTM baseline and/or MRNN with SGD to contextualize the claimed advantages.
- Provide a proper validation split, report variance across multiple runs, and avoid tuning on test.
- Factor the contribution of training protocol: compare DRNN-1O with/without incremental training, and DRNN-AO with a staged schedule; ablate gradient normalization.
- Quantify time-scale analyses (half-lives with error bars) and detail parentheses closure statistics.
- Align the character set with prior work for a direct BPC comparison, or report both.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The empirical analyses are insightful and the practical result is useful, but novelty is incremental and some comparisons/controls are missing.

Confidence
High.
