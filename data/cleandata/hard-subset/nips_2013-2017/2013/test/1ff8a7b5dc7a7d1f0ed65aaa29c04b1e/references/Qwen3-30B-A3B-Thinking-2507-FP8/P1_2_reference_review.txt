

# NIPS Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces a novel architecture called Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal memory of recurrent networks. The authors propose a stack of RNNs where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a hierarchy of time scales. The paper explores two variants: DRNN-1O (output only from the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers). Through extensive experiments on character-level language modeling using the Wikipedia text corpus, the authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks without requiring advanced training methods like Hessian-free optimization. The paper further analyzes the temporal hierarchies that emerge in DRNNs, showing that different layers operate at different time scales and that DRNNs can effectively model long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses in long phrases).

The work builds on previous research on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and stacked LSTMs [8], but makes a novel contribution by analyzing the temporal hierarchy that emerges when stacking RNNs. The paper also relates to the broader NIPS community's interest in understanding deep architectures and their relationship to temporal processing (e.g., [11, 19]).

## Quality

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with well-executed experiments and appropriate baselines. The authors provide thorough ablation studies to analyze the contribution of individual layers, which is particularly valuable for understanding the architecture. The time scale analysis through typo perturbation and context switch experiments provides compelling evidence for the emergence of temporal hierarchies in DRNNs.

However, the paper would benefit from more theoretical grounding. While the experimental results are convincing, the paper lacks a formal analysis explaining *why* the DRNN architecture works better than standard RNNs. The authors observe that different layers operate at different time scales but do not provide theoretical justification for this phenomenon. The paper also doesn't sufficiently address potential limitations of the DRNN architecture, such as training difficulties with very deep stacks or how the architecture scales to longer sequences.

The authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their approach (e.g., the DRNN-1O requires layer-wise training), and they compare their results with appropriate baselines including the state-of-the-art Hessian-free method [19]. The experimental setup is well-documented, and the performance metrics (BPC) are standard for language modeling.

## Clarity

The paper is generally well-written and well-organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and clearly states the contribution of the paper. The architecture is well-explained with equations (Section 2.1) and a schematic diagram (Figure 1). The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility.

There are a few areas where clarity could be improved:
1. The distinction between DRNNs and stacked RNNs (which have been used before, e.g., in [8]) could be more clearly articulated. The paper mentions that "the architecture we study in this paper has been used in [8]" but doesn't clearly explain how their analysis differs from the previous work.
2. The explanation of why DRNN-AO is better than DRNN-1O (beyond the technical reason about gradient flow) could be more explicit. The authors state it's for "pathological curvature in the cost function" but don't fully elaborate on why this is a critical issue for their specific architecture.
3. The "long-term interactions: parentheses" section would benefit from a clearer explanation of why closing parentheses is a good test of long-term memory.

## Originality

The DRNN architecture represents a novel combination of deep and recurrent neural networks that has not been previously explored in this way. While stacked RNNs have been used before [8], the paper's focus on analyzing the temporal hierarchy that emerges in such architectures is original. The authors provide a detailed analysis of how different layers operate at different time scales, which is not present in previous work on stacked RNNs.

However, the paper could better situate itself within the existing literature on hierarchical RNNs. Previous work by El Hihi and Bengio [5] also proposed hierarchical RNNs with different time scales, but the authors only briefly mention this work without clearly differentiating their approach. A more thorough comparison with [5] would strengthen the originality claim.

The paper's main contribution is not just the architecture but the analysis of temporal hierarchies that emerge in DRNNs. This analysis provides new insights into how deep recurrent architectures process temporal information, which is a significant contribution to the field.

## Significance

The paper's results are significant for the field of sequence modeling, particularly for tasks requiring long-term dependencies. The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling without requiring advanced training methods (like Hessian-free optimization) is valuable for practitioners. The paper also provides important theoretical insights about how deep recurrent architectures learn temporal hierarchies.

The significance is somewhat limited to character-level language modeling, and it's not clear how broadly applicable these findings are to other sequence tasks. The paper doesn't explore whether DRNNs would be beneficial for tasks like machine translation or speech recognition, which would strengthen the significance claim.

The paper contributes to the ongoing debate about training algorithms for deep networks, as the authors suggest that DRNNs might be better suited for training with SGD rather than second-order methods. This could have implications for future work on efficient training of deep architectures.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

### Strengths
- Novel architecture that combines deep and recurrent neural networks
- Thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies
- Insightful analysis of temporal hierarchies in DRNNs
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks without requiring advanced training methods
- Clear presentation of results with appropriate visualizations
- Well-motivated problem statement and analysis of temporal hierarchies

### Weaknesses
- Limited theoretical analysis to explain why DRNNs work well
- Superficial comparison with previous work on hierarchical RNNs
- Doesn't fully explain the distinction between DRNNs and stacked RNNs
- Limited discussion of potential limitations of the DRNN architecture
- The significance is somewhat constrained to character-level language modeling

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The work makes a valuable contribution to understanding how deep architectures can be combined with recurrent networks to model temporal hierarchies. The experimental results are compelling and the analysis of temporal hierarchies provides new insights that could guide future research in the field.

While the paper has some weaknesses in theoretical grounding and comparison with related work, these could be addressed in the camera-ready version. The results are significant for the sequence modeling community, and the paper's focus on understanding the architecture's behavior (not just achieving good performance) aligns well with NIPS's emphasis on scientific contributions.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. Clarify the distinction between DRNNs and stacked RNNs in the introduction and related work sections. Explicitly state how the current work differs from [8] and previous hierarchical RNNs [5].

2. Provide a more detailed theoretical explanation for why DRNNs create temporal hierarchies. Consider adding a brief analysis of how the layer-wise time scales emerge from the network dynamics.

3. Expand the discussion of limitations. For example, how does the DRNN architecture scale to very deep stacks? How does it perform on tasks with longer sequences than the Wikipedia corpus?

4. In the related work section, provide a more thorough comparison with El Hihi and Bengio's hierarchical RNNs [5], which also proposed different time scales at different layers.

5. Consider adding a brief discussion of how the DRNN architecture might be applied to other sequence tasks beyond character-level language modeling (e.g., speech recognition, machine translation).

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent architectures. With some minor revisions to address the weaknesses noted above, it would be an excellent addition to the NIPS proceedings.
