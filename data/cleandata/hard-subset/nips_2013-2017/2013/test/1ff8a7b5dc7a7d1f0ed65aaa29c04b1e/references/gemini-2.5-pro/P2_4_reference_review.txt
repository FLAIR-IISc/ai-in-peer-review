**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The core hypothesis is that this deep structure combines the hierarchical processing of deep feedforward networks with the temporal memory of RNNs, naturally inducing a temporal hierarchy where higher layers operate on longer time scales. The authors apply this model to the task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. They propose and evaluate two variants: one with an output connection only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with output connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). Empirically, the DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained only with complex second-order optimization methods (Hessian-free), while using only standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient clipping. A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to analyzing the trained models, providing evidence for the emergence of different time scales across layers and demonstrating an impressive ability to capture long-term dependencies, such as closing parentheses over hundreds of characters.

This work builds upon prior research in deep and hierarchical neural networks. It distinguishes itself from earlier hierarchical RNNs (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1996) by its simpler, more direct stacking of standard RNN layers and by its focus on a large-scale, real-world task. It directly compares its performance to the work of Sutskever et al. (2011), who used a more complex multiplicative RNN and Hessian-free optimization on the same dataset, providing a compelling argument for the architectural advantages of depth.

**Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The primary strength of this paper is its impressive performance on a challenging benchmark. Achieving state-of-the-art bits-per-character (BPC) on the Wikipedia dataset, and doing so with a relatively simple SGD-based training procedure, is a significant accomplishment. This result suggests that architectural improvements (i.e., depth) can be as, or more, impactful than sophisticated optimization algorithms for this class of problem.

2.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The paper goes far beyond simply reporting performance metrics. The authors conduct a series of well-designed experiments to probe the internal workings of the DRNNs.
    *   The "typo-perturbation" and "context-switch" experiments (Figure 3) provide compelling quantitative evidence for the core hypothesis: higher layers in the DRNN learn to operate on longer time scales than lower layers. This validates the idea that the architecture learns a temporal hierarchy.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis-closing is an excellent qualitative case study. Demonstrating that the top layer maintains a persistent state indicating an open parenthesis over hundreds of characters (Figure 4) gives a concrete and intuitive example of the model's capacity for long-term memory.
    *   The ablation study on the DRNN-AO model, which measures the performance impact of removing individual layer outputs, provides valuable insight into the functional specialization of the layers.

3.  **Clear Motivation and Hypothesis:** The introduction clearly motivates the need for DRNNs by discussing the limitations of shallow RNNs, namely the lack of explicit support for hierarchical temporal processing. The proposed architecture is a direct and intuitive solution to this perceived shortcoming.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Limited Experimental Scope:** The authors acknowledge that due to computational cost, they only trained three network instantiations (one shallow RNN, two 5-layer DRNNs). While the results are strong, this small number of experiments makes it difficult to assess the sensitivity of the architecture to hyperparameters like the number of layers, units per layer, or the choice of non-linearity. The decision for 5 layers is noted as being based on prior experiments, but these results are not shown.

2.  **Specialized Training for DRNN-1O:** The best-performing model (DRNN-1O) required a specific incremental layer-wise training procedure to achieve good results. While this is an interesting finding in itself (related to pre-training), it slightly weakens the argument for the overall simplicity of the approach, as the training is more complex than a standard end-to-end SGD run. An analysis of *why* this procedure was necessary would have strengthened the paper.

3.  **Lack of Theoretical Grounding:** The paper's claims are entirely supported by empirical evidence. While the evidence is strong, a more formal discussion or theoretical analysis of why stacking recurrent layers should inherently lead to a separation of time scales would have made the contribution more complete.

**Critique based on NIPS criteria**

*   **Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The experiments are well-designed and the claims are well-supported by the results presented. The authors are honest about the limitations of their work, such as the small number of models tested and the difficulty in making a precise computational cost comparison with prior work. The work is a complete and self-contained contribution.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The DRNN architecture is defined precisely, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail to understand the methodology. The figures are informative and effectively illustrate the key findings.

*   **Originality:** The idea of stacking neural network layers is not new. However, its specific application to RNNs for language modeling, combined with a dedicated analysis of the emergent temporal hierarchy, is novel and was not well-explored at the time. The paper clearly delineates its contribution relative to previous hierarchical RNN architectures and contemporary work using advanced optimizers.

*   **Significance:** The significance of this work is high. It provides strong evidence that depth is a crucial ingredient for powerful sequence models, a concept that has since become foundational in the field (e.g., in modern Transformers). The demonstration that a simple architecture with a standard optimizer can outperform more complex models with advanced optimizers is an important result that likely influenced subsequent research directions. Furthermore, the analytical techniques used in the paper provide a valuable template for understanding and interpreting the behavior of deep sequential models. Researchers and practitioners are very likely to build on these ideas.

**Recommendation:** Accept.

This is a strong paper that makes a clear and significant contribution. It presents a simple yet powerful architecture, demonstrates its state-of-the-art performance on a difficult task, and, most importantly, provides a thoughtful analysis that yields valuable insights into *why* the model is effective. Despite the limited number of experimental configurations, the quality of the results and the depth of the analysis make this a compelling and impactful piece of work suitable for NIPS.
