This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), combining aspects of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) to leverage both hierarchical processing and temporal memory. The authors propose a DRNN architecture where each layer is an RNN, allowing for the creation of different time scales within the network. They apply this architecture to the task of character-based language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus and demonstrate competitive performance with state-of-the-art results, achieved using only SGD. The paper also includes an analysis of the learned time scales within the DRNN and explores the network’s ability to handle long-term dependencies like parentheses.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach. The mathematical formulation of the DRNN is clear and concise. The experimental setup is well-defined, and the performance comparison with existing methods is fair. The use of SGD to achieve competitive performance is noteworthy, given the prior reliance on more complex optimization algorithms. The analysis of time scales and the parentheses experiment provide some insight into the network’s internal workings. However, the analysis, while interesting, remains somewhat superficial. The justification for the choice of network sizes (approximately 4.9 million parameters) feels arbitrary. The ablation study on layer contributions is useful, but a more detailed investigation of individual neuron or weight importance within each layer could be beneficial. The claim that the DRNN embeds different timescales "directly in its structure" requires more rigorous justification beyond the observation of decaying memory.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for combining DNNs and RNNs is clearly explained. The notation is consistent and understandable. However, some sections, particularly the description of the training setup, could be more concise. The discussion of the related work is adequate but could be more comprehensive, particularly regarding recent advances in RNN architectures and training techniques beyond those explicitly mentioned. Figure 1 is helpful in visualizing the DRNN architecture.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking RNNs to create a hierarchy of time scales is not entirely novel, as acknowledged by the authors referencing previous work [3, 5, 6, 8]. However, the specific DRNN architecture proposed, where each layer is a full RNN, differs from previous approaches. The detailed analysis of the learned time scales and the experiments on long-term dependencies, specifically the parenthesis example, add to the originality of the work. The achievement of competitive performance with SGD is also a noteworthy contribution, challenging the necessity of more sophisticated optimization methods for this type of architecture.

**Significance:** The results are significant in demonstrating that a relatively simple architecture, combined with careful training, can achieve state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task. The analysis of time scales and long-term dependencies provides valuable insights into the behavior of DRNNs. The success with SGD suggests that the DRNN architecture is potentially more accessible and easier to train than models requiring Hessian-free optimization.  The findings could have implications for other sequence modeling tasks where hierarchical temporal dependencies are important, such as speech recognition or natural language processing.  The observation regarding the effectiveness of DRNN-1O configuration potentially guides future architectural design.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Competitive performance on a challenging task using a simple optimization method (SGD).
*   Clear and technically sound presentation of the DRNN architecture.
*   Interesting analysis of learned time scales and long-term dependencies.
*   Demonstrates the potential of combining DNNs and RNNs in a novel way.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The core idea of stacking RNNs is not entirely novel.
*   The analysis of the learned time scales and dependencies is somewhat superficial.
*   The justification for the network size and specific design choices is limited.
*   The connection between structural properties and learned time scales isn’t fully explored.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a solid contribution that presents a well-motivated and technically sound approach to deep recurrent neural networks. The competitive performance and insightful analysis justify acceptance, despite the limited novelty of the core idea. I recommend **acceptance**.
