- Overall: Sensible, well-executed study showing stacked RNNs form a temporal hierarchy and achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling with SGD; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Evaluates two DRNN variants (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) on Wikipedia character LM; DRNN-1O performs best and layers capture different temporal dependencies.  
- Strength — Clear motivation: Good articulation of limitations of standard DNNs/RNNs and rationale for DRNNs, especially regarding time scales in language.  
- Strength — Strong empirical results: Achieves competitive/SOTA performance using only SGD, making the approach more accessible than Hessian-free methods.  
- Strength — In-depth analysis: Valuable analyses of layer contributions, time-scale dynamics, and handling of parentheses provide insight beyond raw numbers.  
- Strength — Presentation & clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, with clear equations and informative figures.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Sufficient architectural, training, and dataset details provided (noting long training time).  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: DRNN/stacked RNN idea isn’t radically new; main contribution is analysis and application.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient exploration of learning-rate sensitivity and other hyperparameters (e.g., neurons per layer).  
- Weakness — Missing LSTM comparison: Lacks direct comparisons to LSTMs/GRUs with similar parameter counts, which are widely used baselines.  
- Weakness — Analysis depth: Some findings are descriptive rather than explanatory (e.g., why the top layer dominates output).  
- Specific comment — Introduction: Claim that DNNs lack hierarchical feature extraction is overstated.  
- Specific comment — Section 2.3: Gradient normalization heuristic needs clearer justification for preventing explosions.  
- Specific comment — Section 3.2: Choice of 5 layers seems arbitrary; rationale or preliminary basis should be given.  
- Specific comment — Figure 3: Y-axis scale/units for distance between hidden states are unclear.  
- Specific comment — Parentheses example: Struggling with nested parentheses could stem from limited depth or training regime—needs investigation.  
- For acceptance: SOTA results, insightful analyses, thorough experiments, and clear writing support acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Architecture novelty is limited, analysis could be deeper, and more direct LSTM/GRU comparisons are needed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add LSTM comparisons, deepen explanatory analysis, and clarify the specific methodological points raised.
