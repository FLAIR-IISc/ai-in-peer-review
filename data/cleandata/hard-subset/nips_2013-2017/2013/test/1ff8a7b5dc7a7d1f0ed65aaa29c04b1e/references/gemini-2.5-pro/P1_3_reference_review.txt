This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which are constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this deep architecture naturally induces a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process information on shorter timescales and higher layers capture longer-term dependencies. The authors evaluate this hypothesis on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus.

The main contributions are twofold. First, the paper demonstrates that a 5-layer DRNN trained with simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) and gradient normalization can achieve state-of-the-art performance, comparable to that of a more complex multiplicative RNN trained with the advanced Hessian-free optimization technique on the same dataset. Second, and more importantly, the paper provides a series of insightful analyses to probe the internal dynamics of the trained DRNNs. These experiments provide compelling evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, analyze the functional role of different layers, and demonstrate the model's ability to capture very long-range dependencies, such as closing parentheses correctly over hundreds of characters. This work builds upon previous explorations of hierarchical RNNs (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, NIPS 1995) and the use of stacked LSTMs (Graves et al., 2013), but distinguishes itself by its focus on in-depth analysis of the learned representations and temporal dynamics on a challenging, large-scale benchmark.

### Strengths

1.  **Significant Empirical Results:** The paper presents very strong performance on a difficult benchmark. Achieving a BPC of 1.48 with a relatively straightforward DRNN architecture trained with SGD is a significant result. It provides evidence that for certain large-scale tasks, architectural choices (i.e., depth) can be as impactful as sophisticated second-order optimization methods, a point of considerable interest to the machine learning community.

2.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The paper's primary strength lies not just in its performance but in its careful and insightful analysis. The authors go beyond simply reporting a single performance metric. The experiments designed to test the model's properties are well-conceived and provide strong support for the main hypothesis:
    *   The analysis of layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model (Figure 2) gives a clear picture of functional specialization, suggesting intermediate layers act as feature processors for the top layer.
    *   The text generation experiments, particularly when ablating the bottom or top layer's output, provide compelling qualitative evidence for the roles of different layers in capturing n-gram statistics versus longer-term structure.
    *   The typo-perturbation and context-switch experiments (Figure 3) offer quantitative evidence that the DRNNs learn a hierarchy of timescales, with higher layers exhibiting much longer memory.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis-closing is a concrete and impressive demonstration of the model's ability to learn and maintain state over very long, non-fixed durations.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The motivation for DRNNs is well-established in the introduction, the model is described succinctly, and the experiments are structured logically to first establish performance and then to analyze the model's behavior. The figures and tables are informative and support the text well.

4.  **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail about their training setup (parameter counts, learning rates, gradient normalization, dataset, BPTT length) that an expert reader would have a good chance of reproducing the main results.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The authors state that due to high computational cost, they only trained three network instantiations (one RNN, two DRNN variants). While the results are strong, this raises minor concerns about the generality of the findings. The performance could be sensitive to initialization or other hyperparameter choices, and a single run for each architecture makes it difficult to assess this. Footnote 3, which claims "very little difference in performance for different initialisations," is a strong claim that is not substantiated with evidence.

2.  **Novelty of the Architecture:** The concept of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new, as the authors acknowledge. The novelty of the paper lies primarily in the demonstration of its effectiveness on a large-scale task and, most importantly, in the detailed analysis of its properties. This is a minor weakness, as the analysis itself is a significant and original contribution.

3.  **Unclear Justification for DRNN-1O Training:** The incremental, layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O is described as "necessary to reach good performance." This is an interesting finding, but the paper does not explore *why* it is necessary. A comparison with a DRNN-1O trained end-to-end from the start would have been valuable to quantify the benefit of this pre-training-like schedule and to provide more insight into the optimization challenges of such deep recurrent models.

### Overall Evaluation and Recommendation

This is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. It successfully demonstrates the power of deep recurrent architectures and provides a much-needed analysis of *why* they are effective. The experiments confirming the emergence of a temporal hierarchy are convincing and insightful. While the architectural novelty is moderate, the combination of strong empirical results on a benchmark task with a thorough, well-executed analysis of the learned model makes this a significant piece of work. The finding that simple SGD can be competitive with more complex optimizers through architectural improvements is particularly impactful. The weaknesses are minor and mostly relate to the computational expense of the experiments rather than fundamental flaws in the methodology or reasoning. The paper is well-written, the claims are well-supported by the evidence provided, and the results are likely to be of broad interest to researchers working on sequence modeling, deep learning, and neural network optimization.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Achieves state-of-the-art-level performance on a major benchmark with a simpler optimization method.
*   Provides excellent, insightful analysis that validates the hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy.
*   The analysis of long-term dependencies (parentheses) is particularly compelling.
*   The paper is clear, well-written, and the experiments are sound.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   Limited number of experimental runs for each model configuration.
*   The DRNN architecture itself is an extension of known ideas rather than a completely novel invention.

In conclusion, the strengths of this paper, particularly the quality and depth of its analysis, far outweigh its weaknesses. It presents an important result and provides valuable insights into the functioning of deep recurrent models.
