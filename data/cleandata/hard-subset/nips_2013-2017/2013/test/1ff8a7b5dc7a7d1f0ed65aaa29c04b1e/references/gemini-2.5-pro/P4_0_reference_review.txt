This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers, where the output of one layer serves as the input sequence for the next. The central hypothesis is that this depth creates a temporal hierarchy, allowing different layers to operate on different time scales, which is beneficial for modeling complex sequential data like natural language. The authors apply this architecture to character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. They compare a standard RNN, a 5-layer DRNN with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O), and a 5-layer DRNN with output connections from all layers (DRNN-AO).

The proposed architecture relates to prior work on hierarchical RNNs, such as El Hihi & Bengio (1995) who used subsampling to create different time scales, and more recent work by Graves et al. (2013) who successfully used stacked LSTMs for speech recognition. However, while previous work often focused on achieving state-of-the-art performance, this paper's main contribution is a detailed analysis of the DRNN's internal mechanisms. The performance of the DRNNs is benchmarked against the multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-free optimization by Sutskever et al. (2011) on the same dataset, which represented the state of the art.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments on a large-scale, challenging benchmark. The authors achieve state-of-the-art results (1.479 BPC) using only stochastic gradient descent with gradient normalization, which is a significant result compared to the previous state-of-the-art that required a complex second-order optimization method (Hessian-free). The analysis is the standout feature of the paper. The experiments designed to probe the model's behavior are insightful and provide strong evidence for the central claims:
1.  The typo-perturbation experiment (Figure 3, left) convincingly demonstrates that different layers learn different time scales, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory.
2.  The context-switching experiment (Figure 3, right) shows that DRNNs utilize a longer context for prediction than a standard RNN.
3.  The parenthesis-closing analysis provides a concrete example of the model learning long-range dependencies over hundreds of characters, linking it to an abstract grammatical structure.
The work is a complete piece of research, presenting a model, a training methodology, strong results, and a thorough analysis.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated in the introduction, contrasting them with both standard DNNs and shallow RNNs. The model architecture, equations, and training procedures are described with sufficient detail to allow for reproducibility. The figures and tables are clear, well-labeled, and effectively support the paper's narrative. For instance, the generated text samples in Table 2, along with the ablation analysis, provide a compelling qualitative illustration of the different roles played by the layers.

**Originality:** The core architectural concept of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely novel, as the authors acknowledge by citing Graves et al. (2013). However, the paper's originality lies in three key areas:
1.  It is one of the first works to provide a deep, systematic analysis of *why* such architectures are effective for sequence modeling. It moves beyond simply reporting performance metrics to investigating the learned internal representations and dynamics.
2.  It demonstrates that these deep architectures can be trained to a state-of-the-art level with a simple first-order optimizer (SGD with gradient clipping), which was a non-obvious finding at the time and contrasted with concurrent work advocating for more complex optimizers.
3.  The proposed layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O, where the full network is trained after adding each new layer, is a novel variant of pre-training for recurrent models.

**Significance:** The paper's contributions are highly significant.
1.  **Practical Impact:** It shows that deep recurrent models can achieve excellent performance without requiring complex and computationally expensive second-order optimization methods. This finding makes state-of-the-art sequence modeling more accessible to the broader research community.
2.  **Scientific Impact:** The analysis provides a foundational understanding of how depth in RNNs contributes to learning hierarchical temporal features. This insight has been influential and has informed the subsequent widespread adoption of deep stacked LSTMs and GRUs as a standard architecture in NLP and other sequence modeling domains.
3.  **Future Work:** The paper opens up several avenues for future research, such as exploring different pre-training schemes for DRNNs and comparing first-order vs. second-order optimization methods on these deeper architectures, as noted by the authors.

A minor weakness is the limited number of network instantiations tested (one for each of the three architectures), which is justified by the high computational cost. While the results appear robust, this limits the ability to draw general conclusions about the sensitivity of the models to initialization or minor hyperparameter variations.

### Arguments for Acceptance:
*   **Pro:** The paper presents state-of-the-art results on a difficult character-level language modeling benchmark.
*   **Pro:** It achieves these results with a conceptually simple architecture and a standard training algorithm (SGD), making the method highly practical and accessible.
*   **Pro:** The primary contribution—a detailed and insightful analysis of the model's learned temporal hierarchy and long-term memory capabilities—provides significant scientific value and a deeper understanding of deep sequential models.
*   **Pro:** The paper is extremely well-written, with clear motivation, methodology, and presentation of results.

### Arguments against Acceptance:
*   **Con:** The core architectural idea of stacking RNNs is not fundamentally new, which slightly lessens the novelty of the model itself. The main novelty is in the analysis and training methodology.

In summary, this is an excellent paper. While the architectural concept is an extension of prior ideas, the combination of achieving state-of-the-art performance with a simple optimizer and, most importantly, providing a rigorous and insightful analysis of the model's behavior constitutes a major contribution to the field. The paper is technically sound, clearly presented, and its findings have had a significant impact on subsequent research in sequence modeling.
