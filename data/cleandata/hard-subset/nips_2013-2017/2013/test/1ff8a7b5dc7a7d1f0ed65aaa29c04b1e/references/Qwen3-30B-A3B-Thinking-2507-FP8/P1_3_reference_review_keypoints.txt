- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers to create a temporal hierarchy with different time scales per layer.  
- DRNNs address RNN limitations by explicitly modeling multiple time scales and improving memory for long-term dependencies.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using only SGD (no advanced optimizers).  
- Paper provides clear mathematical formulation and comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple network configurations.  
- Analysis includes time-scale measurements and parentheses-closing tests that strongly support the temporal hierarchy claim.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical/stacked RNN work but contributes a novel theoretical and empirical explanation of why stacking creates multiple time scales.  
- Well-written and organized with reproducible experimental details and effective illustrative figures.  
- Could clarify distinctions between DRNNs and other hierarchical/stacked RNN architectures more explicitly.  
- Acknowledges limitations such as the need for layer-wise training in DRNN-1O and possible lack of benefit for tasks requiring only long-term memory.  
- Significant because a simple architectural change yields SOTA performance without complex optimization, offering new theoretical insights and future research directions.  
- Strengths: novel architecture, strong empirical evidence, insightful analyses, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited direct comparison to LSTMs, no discussion of computational efficiency, and limited connection to broader NLP/later architectures.  
- Recommendation: accept—solid technical contribution with minor suggestion to better compare to LSTMs.
