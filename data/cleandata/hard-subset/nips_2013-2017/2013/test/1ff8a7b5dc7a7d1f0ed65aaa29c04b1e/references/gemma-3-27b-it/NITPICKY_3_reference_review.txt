This paper explores Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, combining aspects of DNNs and RNNs to create a hierarchical temporal structure. While the core idea is interesting and the results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor, yet persistent, flaws that detract from its overall impact and technical rigor.

The introduction adequately motivates the study, framing DRNNs within the context of both DNNs and RNNs. However, the discussion of hierarchical processing in standard RNNs feels somewhat contrived. The assertion that older data doesn’t *require* more processing steps is debatable and doesn’t invalidate the established benefits of recurrent connections for capturing temporal dependencies. The comparison to speech processing levels, while illustrative, lacks depth. The authors appropriately acknowledge prior work ([5, 8]), but the description feels superficial.

Section 2, detailing the DRNN architecture, is generally clear, but marred by inconsistencies. The notation is occasionally imprecise (e.g., the definition of x̄ is confusingly placed). The equation for ai(t) when i>1 lacks a clear explanation of the dimensions of the matrices W and Z, making it difficult to verify its correctness. The motivation behind the two output connection scenarios (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) is well articulated, however, the rationale for adding output connections at each layer to mitigate vanishing gradients feels underdeveloped. While plausible, it isn’t thoroughly justified or compared to other gradient stabilization techniques.

The experimental setup in Section 3 is adequately described, but contains several issues. The justification for choosing a specific number of neurons (resulting in ~4.9 million parameters) is weak – why this particular number? The initial learning rate selection (0.5, except for the top layer of DRNN-1O) feels arbitrary and lacks a clear rationale. The description of the training process for DRNN-1O is convoluted and difficult to follow. The claim of “incremental layer-wise” training is misleading, as the *full* network is trained after each layer is added. This is not standard layer-wise training. Furthermore, the BPTT sequence length (250) and the discarded initial 50 characters are justified with insufficient explanation. The initialization schemes for weights are briefly described, but lack detail (e.g., why N(0, N−1/2) for Wi and Zi>1?). The text corpus details (Wikipedia) are adequate, though referencing the exact version/dump would be beneficial.

The results section (Section 3.2) presents performance metrics (BPC) and qualitative analyses. The comparison to existing work is reasonable, but lacks nuance. The statement that the DRNN performance is "roughly similar" to the MRNN feels dismissive, considering the MRNN was trained with a significantly more computationally intensive method. The analysis of layer contributions in DRNN-AO, while interesting, lacks statistical rigor. The visualizations in Figure 2 are unclear and lack error bars. The qualitative analysis of text generation (Table 2) is insightful, but the observations are largely anecdotal and could benefit from quantitative evaluation (e.g., perplexity). The experiments relating to time scales (Figure 3) are compelling, but the interpretation is speculative. The authors state the DRNN-1O shows "virtually the same, very long time scale" for the top three layers; the plots show noticeable differences. The discussion on parenthesis handling is overly optimistic and relies on anecdotal evidence. The specific behavior regarding sentence boundaries within parentheses is highlighted, but more systematic testing is required.

Throughout the paper, there are numerous minor stylistic issues: inconsistent use of boldface, imprecise language (“largely due to”), and occasional grammatical errors. The referencing style is inconsistent (e.g. [11] vs [13]).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of DNN and RNN architectures.
*   Competitive performance on a challenging benchmark.
*   Preliminary analysis of the temporal hierarchy in DRNNs.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Numerous minor technical flaws and inconsistencies.
*   Lack of rigorous experimental analysis.
*   Superficial justification for design choices.
*   Overstated conclusions based on anecdotal evidence.
*   Weak justification for specific parameters and hyperparameters.
*   Unclear and occasionally misleading descriptions of the training procedure.
*   Inconsistent notation and stylistic issues.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially interesting ideas presented, the numerous flaws outlined above raise significant concerns about the technical rigor and overall quality of this paper. While the DRNNs demonstrate competitive performance, the lack of thorough analysis and the abundance of minor errors make it difficult to confidently assess the true significance of the work. Therefore, I recommend **rejection**. The authors should address the identified flaws, provide more detailed justifications for their design choices, and conduct more rigorous experimental analyses before resubmitting. Addressing the comments regarding notation, clarity and statistical rigor would greatly improve the manuscript.
