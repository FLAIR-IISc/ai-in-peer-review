- Overall: Sensible, well-executed study of deep recurrent neural networks; reviewer strongly recommends accept.
- Proposal: Introduces DRNNs (stacked RNN layers) and two output schemes (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) to induce multi-scale temporal hierarchies.
- Empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using only SGD, matching more complex optimizers.
- Analysis: Provides layer-wise timescale analysis, measures each layer’s contribution, and demonstrates modeling of long-term dependencies (e.g., parenthesis prediction).
- Relation to prior work: Properly situates work relative to hierarchical RNNs, stacked bi-LSTMs, Hessian-free optimization, and language-modeling/compression baselines.
- Strength — strong empirical results: Simple optimization yields competitive performance on a challenging task.
- Strength — detailed analysis: Good investigation of temporal hierarchies and per-layer roles.
- Strength — clear motivation: Well-justified architecture and explanation of output configurations.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and organized.
- Weakness — limited tasks: Experiments only on character-level language modeling; generality to other temporal tasks is untested.
- Weakness — fixed network size: Results limited to a single network configuration (4.9M parameters); scalability/generalizability unclear.
- Weakness — missing ablations: Lacks comprehensive ablation studies over hyperparameters and architectural variants.
- Weakness — computational cost: Insufficient discussion of training time and resource comparisons (10 days on one GPU noted but not analyzed).
- Weakness — minor clarity: Incremental training procedure for DRNN-1O needs clearer description.
- Acceptance arguments: Strong empirical performance, insightful temporal-hierarchy analysis, clear comparison of DRNN variants, and solid writing.
- Rejection arguments: Narrow experimental scope, limited generalization evidence, and absence of thorough ablation analyses.
