This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) – multilayer perceptrons with temporal feedback loops in each layer – for character-level language modeling. The authors build on previous work employing stacked LSTMs [8] but aim to provide a more in-depth analysis of the architecture’s capabilities, particularly regarding its ability to capture temporal hierarchies. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD) on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, previously achieved with more advanced Hessian-free training [19].

The central idea is that stacking RNNs naturally creates different timescales at different levels, effectively embedding a temporal hierarchy within the network. The authors explore two DRNN variants – one with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another with output connections at all layers (DRNN-AO) – and analyze their performance and internal representations. The paper provides empirical evidence supporting the claim that DRNNs can learn and exploit such temporal hierarchies, demonstrating their ability to model long-term dependencies.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation for exploring DRNNs – bridging the gap between DNNs and RNNs to leverage the strengths of both. The discussion of time scales and hierarchical processing is well-defined.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple training method (SGD) is a significant contribution. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization is valuable.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis of layer contributions and time scales (e.g., typo perturbation experiments, parenthesis handling) goes beyond simply reporting performance numbers.  These experiments provide insights into *how* the DRNNs are functioning.
*   **Thorough Related Work:** The authors demonstrate awareness of relevant work in both deep learning and recurrent neural networks, including references to foundational papers (e.g., [4], [11], [19]) and more recent advances.
*   **Well-written and organized:** The paper is generally well-written and the logical flow is easy to follow.  Figures and equations are presented clearly.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Network Variety:**  Only three network instantiations were tested. While the authors acknowledge limitations due to computational cost, a wider range of architectures might provide more robust conclusions.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions specific initial learning rates for different layers (e.g., DRNN-1O top layer). It would be helpful to discuss the sensitivity of performance to these hyperparameters, or explain the methodology for their selection.
*   **Ablation Study Depth:** While the ablation study removing layer outputs is informative, a more systematic ablation study (e.g., varying the number of layers, neuron counts) could further refine understanding of the architecture’s behavior.
*   **Comparison Limitations:** Direct comparison to other state-of-the-art models is complicated by differences in datasets and evaluation criteria. While the authors attempt to address this, a more standardized benchmark might strengthen the claims.
*   **Missing Details on Training:** More details on the training process, such as batch size and precise hardware specifications (beyond GPU model) would enhance reproducibility.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel combination of DNN and RNN concepts, resulting in a promising architecture for sequential data modeling.
*   The empirical results are strong, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance.
*   The detailed analysis of layer contributions and time scales provides valuable insights into the inner workings of DRNNs.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The limited range of network configurations and the lack of a more comprehensive hyperparameter study might limit the generalizability of the findings.
*   The analysis, while insightful, could be further strengthened with more systematic ablation studies.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a compelling idea, and the authors provide convincing evidence of its effectiveness. While there are some limitations in terms of the scope of experimentation, the strengths of the paper – particularly the strong empirical results and detailed analysis – outweigh the weaknesses.  I recommend acceptance.
