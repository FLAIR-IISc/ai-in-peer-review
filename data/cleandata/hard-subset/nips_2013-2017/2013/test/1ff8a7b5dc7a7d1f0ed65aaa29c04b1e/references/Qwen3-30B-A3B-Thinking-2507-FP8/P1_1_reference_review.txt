

# NIPS Peer Review

## Summary

This paper introduces "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" (DRNNs), which combine the concepts of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The authors propose a network architecture where each layer is a recurrent neural network, and each subsequent layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This creates a temporal hierarchy where different layers operate at different time scales. The authors evaluate DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks with a simpler training method (SGD) compared to previous work that required Hessian-free optimization.

The paper relates to previous work on hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6] and stacked bi-directional LSTMs [8], but the authors claim their architecture provides a more natural way to create temporal hierarchies. They also build on the concept of Long Short-term Memory (LSTM) [11] and compare their results to the state-of-the-art Hessian-free trained networks [19].

## Strengths

1. **Clear Architecture and Training Method**: The paper provides a well-defined mathematical formulation of DRNNs (Section 2.1) and a detailed description of the training setup. The distinction between DRNN-1O (output only at top layer) and DRNN-AO (output at all layers) is useful for understanding the network's behavior.

2. **Comprehensive Experimental Analysis**: The paper includes thorough experiments on character-level language modeling, with careful analysis of time scales (Figure 3), layer importance (Figure 2), and the ability to handle long-term dependencies (parentheses closing test in Figure 4).

3. **Novel Insights on Temporal Hierarchies**: The demonstration that DRNNs naturally develop different time scales at different layers (Figure 3) is a valuable contribution. The paper provides empirical evidence that the time scale differences are learned properties rather than architectural artifacts.

4. **Practical Results**: The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only SGD, which is significant as previous state-of-the-art required more complex optimization techniques [19].

5. **Adequate Comparison to Baselines**: The paper compares with standard RNNs and previous work [19], showing clear performance improvements for DRNNs.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Theoretical Justification**: While the paper demonstrates that DRNNs develop temporal hierarchies, it lacks theoretical analysis to explain *why* this happens. The claim that DRNNs "embed different time scales directly in their structure" is not sufficiently supported by mathematical arguments.

2. **Overlap with Previous Work**: The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its approach from previous hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6]. The description of DRNNs seems very similar to the hierarchical RNNs described in [5], which also uses multiple time scales through subsampling. The authors should clarify how their work differs from prior hierarchical RNN approaches.

3. **Marginal Performance Gains**: The improvement over standard RNNs is modest (BPC of 1.472 vs. 1.55 for RNN). The DRNN-AO achieves slightly better performance than DRNN-1O, but the difference is not substantial. The paper doesn't convincingly argue why this marginal improvement is significant compared to previous work.

4. **Training Method Justification**: The incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is not well-justified. The authors claim it's necessary for "good performance," but don't provide sufficient theoretical or empirical evidence to support this approach over other training methods for deep recurrent networks.

5. **Lack of Comparison to Other Deep Recurrent Architectures**: The paper doesn't compare against other recent deep recurrent architectures (e.g., [8] which uses stacked bidirectional LSTMs), making it difficult to assess the relative merits of their DRNN approach.

6. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The paper doesn't adequately address the limitations of DRNNs. For example, it doesn't discuss the computational cost of training DRNNs compared to standard RNNs or other deep architectures, nor does it address potential issues like vanishing gradients in deeper DRNNs.

## Quality Assessment

The paper is technically sound and well-organized. The experimental results are well-supported by the data presented. The authors are careful to acknowledge both the strengths and limitations of their approach. However, the lack of theoretical analysis and the limited differentiation from previous work slightly weaken the quality of the contribution.

## Clarity Assessment

The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and appropriate for the subject matter. The figures (Figures 1-4) effectively illustrate key concepts. The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility. The paper would benefit from slightly more detailed explanations of the time scale analysis and the implications of the results for understanding DRNNs.

## Originality Assessment

The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of DNNs and RNNs, but the core idea of creating temporal hierarchies through layered RNNs is not entirely new. The paper would be stronger if it more clearly articulated how their specific DRNN architecture differs from previous hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6]. The novelty appears to be in the empirical demonstration of temporal hierarchies and the specific application to character-level language modeling.

## Significance Assessment

The results are significant in that they demonstrate that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD, avoiding the need for more complex optimization methods. The insights into temporal hierarchies provide valuable understanding of how these networks process information. However, the performance gains are modest, and the paper doesn't convincingly argue why the DRNN approach is substantially better than existing methods for practical applications.

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-executed paper that makes a modest but meaningful contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent architectures. The empirical results are solid, and the analysis of temporal hierarchies provides new insights. However, the paper would be significantly stronger with better theoretical justification, clearer differentiation from prior work, and a more compelling argument for the significance of the results.

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper presents a clear and well-executed study with valuable empirical insights, but the novelty is somewhat limited by overlap with previous work, and the performance gains are modest. The paper would benefit from addressing the weaknesses noted above before publication.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. More clearly distinguish DRNNs from previous hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6], especially [5] which uses a similar time-scale approach.

2. Provide theoretical justification for why DRNNs develop temporal hierarchies, not just empirical evidence.

3. Include a comparison with stacked bidirectional LSTMs [8] to better position the DRNN approach.

4. Discuss the computational cost of DRNNs compared to standard RNNs and other deep architectures.

5. Provide a more thorough analysis of the limitations of DRNNs, including potential failure cases.

6. Clarify the justification for the incremental layer-wise training method used for DRNN-1O.

## Final Thoughts

This paper makes a solid contribution to the field by empirically demonstrating the temporal hierarchy in deep recurrent architectures and providing state-of-the-art results for character-level language modeling using a simpler training method. While the novelty is somewhat limited by overlap with previous work, the thorough experimental analysis and clear presentation make it a valuable addition to the literature. With some revisions to address the weaknesses noted above, this paper would make an even stronger contribution to NIPS.
