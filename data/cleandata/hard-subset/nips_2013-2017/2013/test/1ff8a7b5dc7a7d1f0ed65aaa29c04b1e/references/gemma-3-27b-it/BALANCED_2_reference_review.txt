This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) – essentially DNNs with temporal feedback loops in each layer – for character-level language modeling. The core idea is to combine the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the memory capabilities of RNNs, creating a network with inherent multi-timescale processing. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on a Wikipedia text corpus, achieving comparable performance to Hessian-free optimization methods using only SGD. The paper also provides analysis into the learned temporal hierarchies within the DRNNs, examining layer contributions and timescale behavior.

This work builds upon a rich body of research in deep learning and recurrent neural networks. The foundational work on DNNs (Hinton et al., 2006) and RNNs (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997) are directly relevant. The idea of hierarchical RNNs has been explored previously (e.g., Chen et al., 2009; El Hihi & Bengio, 2009), however, these works largely focused on artificial tasks. More recently, Martens (2011, 2012) demonstrated the power of Hessian-free optimization for training RNNs, achieving strong results on language modeling. This paper differentiates itself by showcasing competitive performance with a simpler, SGD-based approach, while providing a more detailed analysis of the learned architecture.  The work also connects to the broader field of lossless data compression (Mahoney, 2008; Hutter, 2008), recognizing the connection between language modeling and compression.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Architecture:** The DRNN architecture is a reasonable and well-motivated combination of DNNs and RNNs, offering a potential pathway to capture multi-timescale temporal dependencies.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with SGD is a significant accomplishment, demonstrating the efficacy of the proposed architecture and training procedure.  The comparison to Hessian-free methods is valuable.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply presenting results, providing insightful analysis of layer contributions, learned timescales, and the network’s ability to handle long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses). The typo perturbation experiment and parenthesis handling tests are particularly illuminating.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the architecture and experiments in a clear and concise manner.
* **Thorough Experimentation:** The authors perform a range of experiments, including varying network configurations and output connections, to validate their claims.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Parameter Exploration:** Only three network instantiations were tested. While the authors acknowledge the computational cost, exploring a wider range of network sizes and hyperparameters would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Comparison Dataset Differences:**  While comparisons are made to other works, the discrepancies in the datasets used (different character sets or corpus sizes) make a direct comparison challenging. More standardized benchmarks would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies (beyond output connections):** While the authors analyze the impact of removing output connections from different layers, more comprehensive ablation studies (e.g., varying the number of layers, recurrent connections) could provide further insights into the importance of different architectural components.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper lacks a strong theoretical justification for *why* DRNNs should be well-suited for hierarchical temporal modeling. A more rigorous analysis of the information flow and representation learning within the network would be valuable.
* **Incremental Training & Generalizability:** The training regime for DRNN-1O is somewhat complex, involving incremental layer-wise training. It’s unclear how sensitive the performance is to the order in which layers are added and whether this method would generalize to other tasks.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for capturing temporal hierarchies.
* The empirical results are strong, demonstrating competitive performance on a challenging benchmark task.
* The detailed analysis provides valuable insights into the learned representations and behavior of the DRNNs.
* The work addresses an important problem in sequence modeling and contributes to the understanding of deep recurrent networks.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The limited parameter exploration and dataset discrepancies weaken the strength of the empirical findings.
* The lack of strong theoretical justification and comprehensive ablation studies limit the generalizability of the results.
* The complex training procedure for DRNN-1O raises concerns about its robustness and applicability to other tasks.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach for modeling hierarchical temporal dependencies, and the authors provide compelling evidence of its effectiveness. While there are some weaknesses in terms of parameter exploration and theoretical justification, the strong empirical results and detailed analysis outweigh these concerns.  

**Recommendation:**  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should:

1.  Expand the discussion of related work to include more recent advancements in hierarchical sequence modeling.
2.  Clarify the differences in datasets used for comparison and consider reporting results on more standardized benchmarks.
3.  Perform additional ablation studies to investigate the sensitivity of the results to different architectural choices.
4.  Discuss the theoretical limitations of the DRNN architecture and potential avenues for future research in this area.
5.  Provide more detail on the stability and generalizability of the incremental training procedure for DRNN-1O.
