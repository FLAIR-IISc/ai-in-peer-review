This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine aspects of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), for character-level language modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs to create a hierarchy, implicitly introducing multiple time scales for processing sequential data. The authors empirically demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus, comparable to results obtained with more sophisticated Hessian-free optimization techniques, using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD). They further analyze the resulting time scales within the network’s layers and explore the network’s ability to handle long-term dependencies like parenthesis matching.

The paper builds upon prior work in DNNs [4], RNNs [17], LSTM [11], and hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6, 8]. It distinguishes itself by proposing a specific architecture—a DNN with recurrent feedback loops in each layer—and providing a more in-depth analysis of its behavior in the context of language modeling than previous work [8], which focused primarily on performance. The authors acknowledge and compare their findings to related work utilizing multiplicative RNNs (MRNNs) and Hessian-free optimization [19].

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear and Well-Defined Architecture:** The DRNN architecture is clearly described and visually presented in Figure 1, making it easy to understand.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging text corpus with a relatively simple training algorithm (SGD) is a significant accomplishment.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The authors go beyond simply presenting results and attempt to analyze the learned time scales within the network and its ability to model long-term dependencies, demonstrating a deeper understanding of the model's behavior. The experiments with parenthesis matching are insightful.
*   **Thorough Related Work:** The paper cites relevant prior work and positions itself appropriately within the existing literature.
*   **Practical Insights:** The observation about the importance of output connections at each layer for training deep networks is a valuable practical insight.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** Only three network instantiations were tested, which limits the generalizability of the results. While the authors justify this limitation due to computational cost, a more systematic exploration of hyperparameter space would be beneficial.
*   **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** While the authors intuitively explain why DRNNs should capture temporal hierarchies, a more rigorous theoretical justification for this claim would strengthen the paper.
*   **Training Details:** The description of the layer-wise training approach for DRNN-1O is somewhat unclear and could benefit from more detail. The rationale for the specific learning rate adjustments is not fully explained.
*   **Comparison to Baselines:** Although the performance is compared to state-of-the-art, a more direct comparison to standard RNNs with similar parameter counts trained using advanced techniques (beyond Hessian-free) would be insightful.
*   **Figure Quality:** While the figures are helpful, they could be improved for clarity (e.g., larger font sizes).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and well-defined architecture (DRNN) that achieves compelling empirical results.
*   The analysis of learned time scales and long-term dependencies provides valuable insights into the behavior of the model.
*   The paper addresses a significant problem (language modeling) and advances the state-of-the-art, even if modestly.
*   The work is well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The limited scope of experiments and lack of rigorous theoretical justification weaken the paper's claims.
*   The training details could be more thoroughly explained.
*   The paper might benefit from a more comprehensive comparison to alternative approaches.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper represents a solid contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The proposed DRNN architecture is promising, and the empirical results are encouraging. While the paper has some weaknesses, the strengths outweigh them, and the insights provided are valuable. The paper is of sufficient quality for acceptance to NIPS, although addressing the weaknesses in a future revision would further strengthen the work.



**Rating:**  Accept (with minor revisions)
