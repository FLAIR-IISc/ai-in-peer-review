

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) as a combination of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks, with the claim that DRNNs naturally embed temporal hierarchies in their architecture. The authors present two variants: DRNN-1O (output connections only at the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output connections at all layers), and evaluate them on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.

## Major Technical Flaws

The paper contains a critical technical inconsistency in Section 3.1 that undermines the entire experimental setup. The authors state: "All our networks have a number of neurons selected such that in total they each had approximately 4.9 million trainable parameters," but then report "a common RNN (2119 units), a 5-layer DRNN-1O (727 units per layer), and a 5-layer DRNN-AO (706 units per layer)." This confuses the number of units (neurons) with the number of parameters. For a single-layer RNN with N units, the number of parameters is approximately N^2 + N (for weights and biases), not N. For the RNN with 2119 units, the parameter count would be approximately 4.5 million (close to 4.9 million), but for the DRNNs, the parameter count would be significantly higher than 4.9 million. The authors incorrectly report the number of units as the number of parameters, which is a fundamental misunderstanding of neural network architecture.

The paper further undermines its claims by comparing to results measured on different datasets and with different criteria (Section 3.2): "We also included two results which were not measured on the same dataset (or even using the same criteria), but which give an estimation of the true number of BPC for natural text." This invalid comparison weakens their claim of achieving "state-of-the-art performance." Additionally, they compare to PAQ compression algorithm [14], which is not a neural network model and provides no meaningful benchmark for neural network performance.

## Methodological and Formatting Issues

The training setup contains a significant error in Equation (3): "η0 (− j T )" should be "η0 (1 - j/T)." This is a critical mathematical error that would affect the training dynamics. The paper also inconsistently uses "DRNN" (singular) and "DRNNs" (plural) throughout, which is distracting and unprofessional.

The explanation for why DRNN-1O is trained for more iterations (10 times through the training set) than DRNN-AO (7 times) is absent, despite this being a key experimental design choice. The paper states "In the end the DRNN-AO sees the full training set about 7 times in total, and the DRNN-1O about 10 times" without explaining the rationale.

The generative mode description is insufficiently detailed. The authors state they "use the output probabilities of the DRNN-AO to recursively sample a new input character," but omit details on sampling strategy (greedy vs. stochastic), temperature, or how they handle the initial seed text. This makes replication difficult.

## Clarity and Presentation

The paper's figures and tables lack sufficient detail for reproducibility. For instance, Figure 2 shows "the increase in test BPC by removing a single layer's output contribution" without providing standard deviations or statistical significance tests. The description of text generation results (Table 2) is vague: "The text sample with the bottom layer output contribution disabled very rapidly becomes 'unstable'" without quantitative measures of instability.

The claim that DRNNs model parentheses as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state" (Section 3.2) is speculative and not adequately supported by the evidence. The paper acknowledges that the network might simply be memorizing training data, but doesn't provide evidence against this alternative explanation.

## Minor Inconsistencies

- In Section 2.1, the equation "ai(t) = tanh (Wiai(t− 1) + Ziāi−1(t))" has inconsistent spacing around the minus sign.
- The paper uses "BPC = −〈log2 pc〉" but doesn't define the angle brackets ⟨·⟩.
- The phrase "sees the full training set" is unprofessional; "processes" or "trains on" would be more appropriate.
- The reference to "95 of the most common characters" (Section 3.1) doesn't clarify how these were selected or why 95 was chosen.

## Strengths

The paper does make a clear distinction between DRNNs and traditional RNNs, and the concept of temporal hierarchy is potentially interesting. The ablation studies on layer contributions provide some insight into the network's internal functioning. The parentheses experiment is a creative way to test long-term memory.

## Conclusion

While the DRNN concept has potential, the paper suffers from multiple technical flaws, particularly the critical confusion between units and parameters, and invalid comparisons that undermine the main claim of state-of-the-art performance. The methodological inconsistencies and lack of sufficient detail for replication are serious issues that prevent the paper from meeting NIPS's standards for scientific rigor.

The paper's contributions are not significantly novel compared to prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6, 8], and the experimental results are not convincingly presented due to the technical errors. While the topic is relevant to NIPS, the execution is flawed.

**Recommendation: Reject**
