## NIPS Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

**Summary:**

This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine the hierarchical processing of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) with the temporal memory of Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors propose stacking RNNs, creating a hierarchy of timescales and argue this is well-suited for tasks with complex temporal dependencies, like language modeling. They apply DRNNs to character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance comparable to models utilizing more complex optimization techniques (Hessian-free optimization). Furthermore, they present an initial analysis of the learned timescales within the DRNN layers, demonstrating that different layers capture information at varying temporal resolutions.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature. It acknowledges the rise of DNNs and the established role of RNNs, particularly LSTMs, for sequential data. It also references prior work attempting hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6]) and acknowledges the state-of-the-art performance previously achieved by [8, 19] on the TIMIT and Wikipedia datasets, respectively.  The authors distinguish their work by providing a more in-depth *analysis* of the architecture, beyond simply demonstrating performance gains, a point the authors explicitly make concerning [8].

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture, while building on existing components, offers a sensible and potentially powerful combination of DNN and RNN principles. The idea of explicitly stacking RNNs to create inherent multi-timescale processing is intuitive and promising.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark (character-level language modeling) using relatively simple SGD is a significant achievement. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture and reduces the reliance on computationally expensive optimization methods.
* **Initial Analysis of Timescales:** The experiments analyzing the learned timescales within the DRNN layers provide valuable insight into the network’s internal representation of temporal information. The tests with typos and context switching are well-designed and offer compelling evidence for hierarchical temporal processing.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the DRNN architecture and training procedure.  The use of Figure 1 is particularly helpful.
* **Insightful Discussion of Output Connections:** The rationale behind using output connections at each layer (addressing vanishing gradients and providing layer-specific insights) is well-explained and demonstrates a thoughtful consideration of the architecture’s design.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Ablation Studies:** While the removal of individual layer outputs provides some insight, a more thorough ablation study would strengthen the analysis.  For example, investigating the impact of varying the number of layers or the number of neurons per layer could be informative.
* **Network Size Sensitivity:** The authors acknowledge that the results might be limited by the network size. A larger network (as briefly mentioned) should be investigated to assess the scalability and potential for further improvement.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper lacks detail regarding hyperparameter tuning beyond the initial learning rate.  More information on the search process for other hyperparameters (e.g., sequence length for BPTT, layer size) would increase confidence in the reported results.
* **Comparison to More Recent Baselines:** While the comparison to [19] is relevant, more recent advancements in recurrent neural networks (e.g., Transformers) should be discussed, even if a direct comparison is not feasible.  This would better contextualize the contribution of the DRNN.
* **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the intuition behind stacking RNNs for multi-timescale processing is reasonable, a more formal theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper.  Could the authors relate the learned timescales to properties of the input data or the network’s parameters?



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Demonstrates a novel and effective architecture for character-level language modeling, achieving state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple training procedure.
* Provides initial evidence for the emergence of a hierarchical temporal representation within the DRNN.
* Offers valuable insights into the design and training of deep recurrent neural networks.
* Is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The analysis of the learned timescales is preliminary and could be strengthened with more rigorous experiments.
* The paper lacks a comprehensive ablation study and detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning.
* The theoretical justification for the DRNN architecture is limited.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a clear contribution to the field. The DRNN architecture is a promising approach to modeling sequential data, and the experimental results are compelling. While the analysis could be more thorough, the authors have presented a well-motivated and well-executed study.  I believe this paper is a good fit for NIPS, with the caveat that the authors address the weaknesses outlined above in a future version.

**Rating:** **Strong Accept (with minor revisions)** – Addressing the suggested weaknesses, particularly regarding ablation studies and hyperparameter details, would elevate this paper to a truly excellent contribution.
