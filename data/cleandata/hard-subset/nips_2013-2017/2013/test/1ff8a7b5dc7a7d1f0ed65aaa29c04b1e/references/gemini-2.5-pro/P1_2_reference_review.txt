**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The authors hypothesize that this deep structure allows the network to learn a temporal hierarchy, where different layers process information at different time scales. They test this hypothesis on the challenging task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. Two DRNN variants are proposed: one with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) and one with output connections from all layers (DRNN-AO). The main contributions are: 1) Demonstrating that DRNNs trained with simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) can achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained with more complex models (MRNNs) and advanced second-order optimization methods (Hessian-free). 2) Providing a series of insightful analyses that offer strong evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy within the network's layers. The analysis explores layer-specific contributions to the output, the effective memory timescale of each layer, and the model's ability to capture long-range dependencies like closing parentheses.

This work builds upon prior research in hierarchical RNNs ([5], [6]) and, most notably, the use of stacked LSTMs by Graves et al. [8]. However, where Graves et al. focused primarily on achieving state-of-the-art performance on speech recognition, this paper's main contribution is its in-depth analysis of *why* such deep recurrent architectures are effective, using the task of language modeling as a testbed. It also provides a direct and strong comparison to the work of Sutskever et al. [19] on the same dataset.

**Strengths**

1.  **Excellent Analysis and Insight:** The paper's primary strength is not just in achieving a strong result, but in the thorough analysis that explains it. The authors go far beyond simply reporting a bits-per-character (BPC) score. The experiments designed to probe the model's internal workings are well-conceived and provide compelling evidence for their central hypothesis:
    *   The typo-perturbation experiment (Figure 3, left) is a clever and effective way to visualize the different memory timescales of each layer, clearly showing that higher layers retain information for longer.
    *   The qualitative analysis of generated text after ablating specific layers' outputs (Table 2) provides intuitive support for the idea that lower layers handle basic statistics while upper layers capture longer-term structure.
    *   The detailed investigation of parenthesis-closing (Figure 4) provides a concrete example of the model learning a complex, long-range dependency, framing it as learning a pseudo-stable state, which is a powerful insight.

2.  **Significant Empirical Results:** The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on a large-scale, standard benchmark using a relatively simple model (stacked standard RNNs) and a basic training algorithm (SGD with gradient normalization). This is a significant result, as it challenges the notion that more complex second-order methods like Hessian-free optimization are necessary to train RNNs effectively on tasks with long-term dependencies.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clearly articulated, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup and results are presented in a logical and understandable manner. The figures and tables are informative and support the text effectively. The authors are also transparent about their training setup, which aids in reproducibility.

4.  **Honesty About Limitations:** The authors are candid about the computational cost limiting the number of experiments, the heuristic nature of the layer-wise training for the DRNN-1O, and the model's failure cases (e.g., nested parentheses). This honesty strengthens the credibility of the work.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Moderate Architectural Novelty:** As the authors acknowledge, the idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new (e.g., [8]). The contribution is more in the popularization and deep analysis of this specific architecture for this task, rather than the invention of a fundamentally new model. However, the quality of the analysis largely compensates for this.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training Procedure for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training scheme used for the DRNN-1O model, while effective, feels somewhat heuristic. The paper states it was "necessary to reach good performance" but doesn't provide a deeper analysis of why this is the case or if other regularization/initialization schemes might have worked instead. This makes the training process for one of their key models seem less general.

3.  **Limited Scope of Experiments:** Due to the high computational cost, the study is limited to a single network size (matched by parameter count) and depth (5 layers). It would have been interesting to see an analysis of how performance and the temporal hierarchy scale with increasing depth or width, although this is understandable given the constraints.

**Detailed Comments and Questions**

*   The authors use the term "pathological curvature" as a motivation for the DRNN-AO architecture. While this is a common issue in deep networks, the primary problem in deep *recurrent* networks is often framed as the vanishing/exploding gradient problem. It would be slightly clearer to explicitly link the DRNN-AO's direct error signals to mitigating the vanishing gradient problem through time and depth.
*   The justification for the different initialization schemes for `W_i`, `Z_i>1` (scaled by `N^-1/2`) and `Z_1` (scaled by 1) is not provided. Could the authors comment on the motivation for this choice?
*   The comparison between the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO time scales in Figure 3 is fascinating. The authors attribute the DRNN-1O's uniformly long memory in its top layers to the incremental training scheme. This is a plausible explanation and a very interesting side-effect of the training method. It suggests that the training protocol itself can heavily influence the functional roles learned by different parts of the network.

**Recommendation: Accept**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Achieves a state-of-the-art result on a difficult and widely recognized benchmark.
*   The result is particularly significant because it is achieved with a standard training algorithm (SGD), suggesting that architectural choices can be as important as sophisticated optimizers.
*   Provides an exemplary, in-depth analysis of a deep learning model, offering valuable insights into *why* it works. This analytical contribution is a model for future research in the field.
*   The paper is extremely well-written, clear, and easy to understand.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
*   The core architectural idea of stacking RNNs has been previously explored.
*   The training procedure for one of the main models (DRNN-1O) is heuristic.

**Final Assessment**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a strong contribution to the field. While the architectural novelty is moderate, the combination of a significant empirical result with a deep, insightful analysis of the model's learned properties makes it an excellent submission for NIPS. The work not only advances the state of the art in language modeling but also provides a clear and compelling demonstration of how hierarchical structures in time can be learned by deep recurrent models. It is likely to be an influential paper that will inspire further research into the analysis and understanding of deep learning models.
