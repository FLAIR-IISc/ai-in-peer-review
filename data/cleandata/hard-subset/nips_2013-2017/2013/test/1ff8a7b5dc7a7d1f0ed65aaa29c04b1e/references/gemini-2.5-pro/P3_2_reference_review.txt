This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture formed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The central hypothesis is that this deep structure induces a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers capture short-term dependencies and higher layers learn to operate on longer time scales. The authors evaluate DRNNs on the challenging task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. The work provides both strong quantitative results, achieving state-of-the-art performance with a simple SGD-based training procedure, and a detailed qualitative analysis that supports the temporal hierarchy hypothesis.

This work builds upon prior research in hierarchical RNNs. While earlier works like El Hihi & Bengio (1995) and Fernández et al. (2007) explored similar concepts, they were often limited to smaller or artificial tasks. More recently, Graves et al. (2013) demonstrated the power of stacked LSTMs for speech recognition, but their work was primarily performance-oriented. This paper's main contribution is its shift in focus from simply achieving a high score to providing a thorough analysis of the internal mechanisms that make these deep architectures effective for sequence modeling. It directly compares its performance to the contemporary state-of-the-art by Sutskever et al. (2011), which used a more complex model (multiplicative RNN) and training algorithm (Hessian-free optimization).

**Strengths:**

1.  **Significant Analytical Contribution:** The paper's primary strength is its in-depth analysis, which goes well beyond simply reporting a final performance metric. The experiments are cleverly designed to probe the model's internal workings. The analysis of layer-wise time scales using perturbations (Figure 3) provides compelling evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. The study of parenthesis balancing is an excellent case study demonstrating the model's capacity for capturing long-range dependencies that are syntactically, not just statistically, driven.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates that a 5-layer DRNN, trained with a straightforward SGD procedure (with gradient normalization), can achieve performance competitive with a more sophisticated model (MRNN) trained with a powerful second-order method (Hessian-free). This is a significant finding, suggesting that architectural depth can, to some extent, compensate for less powerful optimization algorithms on complex sequence tasks. The result of 1.472 BPC with a larger model further reinforces the scalability and effectiveness of the approach.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated by contrasting them with both deep feedforward networks and shallow RNNs. The proposed architectures (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) are well-defined, and the justifications for each are sound (e.g., using all-layer outputs to mitigate vanishing gradients). The experiments are described in sufficient detail to understand the setup.

4.  **Novelty in Analysis:** The introduction and analysis of the DRNN-AO model is a clever methodological choice. It not only serves a practical purpose in aiding training but also provides a unique window into the function of each layer. The ablation studies where individual layer contributions to the output are removed (Figure 2 and Table 2) yield fascinating insights into the division of labor within the network (e.g., bottom layer for basic stats, top layer for long-range structure).

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Limited Comparison to LSTMs:** While the paper references Graves et al.'s work with stacked LSTMs, it does not include a direct comparison of its `tanh`-based DRNN against a stacked LSTM of equivalent size and depth on the same task. Given that LSTMs are explicitly designed to capture long-term dependencies via gating mechanisms, it would be crucial to understand how the benefits of architectural depth interact with or compare to the benefits of a more complex recurrent unit. Does depth in simple RNNs merely replicate the memory capabilities of a shallow LSTM, or do they offer complementary advantages?

2.  **Training Complexity Nuances:** The paper highlights the simplicity of using SGD, but the DRNN-1O model required a more complex, incremental layer-wise training scheme to achieve good performance. This suggests that end-to-end training of very deep RNNs from a random initialization is non-trivial, even with gradient normalization. While the authors are transparent about this, it somewhat tempers the claim of using a simple training method and hints at optimization challenges not fully addressed.

3.  **Analysis of Network Depth:** The experiments are conducted with a fixed depth of 5 layers. While this is justified by "a previous set of experiments," the paper would be strengthened by a more systematic study of how performance and the temporal hierarchy evolve as depth increases (e.g., from 2 to 7 layers). This would provide a clearer picture of the depth-performance trade-off and whether the observed hierarchical behavior continues to scale.

**Detailed Comments and Questions:**

*   The initialization scheme (input weights Z1 drawn from N(0, 1), others from N(0, N⁻¹/²)) is interesting. Is there a particular reason for using a larger variance for the input weights?
*   The analysis of the parenthesis-closing task is excellent. The observation that the network "forgets" the open parenthesis after a full stop is a fascinating and honest report of the model's limitations, suggesting it has learned sentence-level heuristics rather than a true context-free grammar.
*   The difference in learned time scales between the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO (Figure 3, left) is a key finding. The authors correctly attribute this to the layer-wise training of the DRNN-1O. This implies that the training methodology has a profound impact on the final learned representation, a point that could be emphasized further.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a very strong paper that makes a significant contribution to our understanding of deep learning models for sequential data. Its originality lies not in the invention of stacked RNNs, but in the rigorous and insightful analysis of their behavior. The work successfully validates its central hypothesis that depth creates a functional temporal hierarchy. The strong empirical results, combined with the high quality of the analysis and writing, make this a landmark paper in the field. The identified weaknesses are minor and represent avenues for future exploration rather than fatal flaws. I strongly recommend acceptance.
