## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which stack recurrent layers to create a hierarchical temporal model. The authors argue that this architecture naturally lends itself to modeling data with multiple timescales, unlike traditional RNNs. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus, achieving comparable results to more sophisticated methods like MRNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization, but using only SGD.  Crucially, the paper also includes analysis of the learned timescales within the DRNN layers, and how different layers contribute to prediction.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Originality:** While stacking RNNs isn’t entirely new (cited works [3, 5, 6, 8]), this work provides a more thorough analysis of the *inherent* hierarchical timescales created by such stacking. The DRNN architecture, explicitly combining DNN and RNN concepts, is well-motivated.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple training method (SGD) is a significant achievement. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
* **In-depth Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply demonstrating performance; it attempts to *understand* how the DRNN works. The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) and learned timescales (Figure 3) is valuable and insightful.  The parenthesis experiment provides a compelling qualitative demonstration of long-term dependency modeling.
* **Clear Motivation:** The introduction clearly articulates the motivation for DRNNs, contrasting them with traditional DNNs and RNNs and outlining the potential benefits of a hierarchical temporal model.
* **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is generally clearly written and follows a logical structure.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the results on character-level language modeling are impressive, the paper relies heavily on a single task.  Exploring other tasks (e.g., speech recognition, time series prediction) would strengthen the claims about the DRNN’s general applicability.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The tuning of learning rates, particularly the difference in learning rate for the top layer of the DRNN-1O, feels somewhat ad-hoc.  A more systematic exploration of hyperparameter space would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Comparison to LSTM/GRU:** The authors compare to a standard RNN and MRNN, but a direct comparison to LSTMs or GRUs (which are dominant in sequence modeling) would be valuable.  While these may require more sophisticated optimization, including them would provide a stronger baseline.
* **Scalability Concerns:** The authors mention the difficulty of parallelizing SGD for large models, and the 10-day training time is substantial. This raises concerns about the scalability of the approach to even larger datasets or models. The discussion of a larger model with 9.6M parameters briefly touches on this, but further discussion is warranted.
* **Interpretability Limitations:** While the layer contribution analysis is helpful, the interpretation of the learned timescales remains somewhat speculative. The authors acknowledge the potential for interdependence between layers.

**Quality:**

The paper is technically sound. The experimental setup is well-defined, and the results are presented clearly.  The analysis, while not exhaustive, is thoughtful and provides valuable insights. It’s a complete piece of work for its stated scope.

**Clarity:**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The figures are helpful and well-labeled. The notation is generally clear.

**Originality:**

The core idea of stacking RNNs isn’t entirely new, but the systematic analysis of the resulting hierarchical timescales and the achievement of strong results with SGD makes the work original.

**Significance:**

The results are significant, demonstrating a potentially effective architecture for modeling sequential data. The insights into learned timescales could be valuable for understanding and designing recurrent neural networks.  The success of SGD in achieving competitive performance is also noteworthy, suggesting a practical alternative to more complex optimization algorithms.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong empirical results on a challenging task.
* Novel analysis of learned timescales in stacked RNNs.
* Well-motivated and clearly explained architecture.
* Contribution to the understanding of how to train deep recurrent networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited scope of experiments.
* Lack of comparison to standard LSTM/GRU architectures.
* Scalability concerns.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. While there are some weaknesses, the strengths of the work – particularly the compelling empirical results and insightful analysis – outweigh them.  I believe this paper is suitable for acceptance at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** **Accept** (with minor revisions – addressing the comparison to LSTMs/GRUs would be particularly helpful).
