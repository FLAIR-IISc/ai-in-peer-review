Summary
The paper studies Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), defined as stacked vanilla RNN layers where each layer has its own recurrence and feeds its hidden state to the next layer. Two output schemes are considered: top-layer-only (DRNN-1O) and all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO). The authors train character-level language models on a large Wikipedia corpus using plain SGD with gradient norm normalization and a simple linear learning-rate decay. For DRNN-1O they use a supervised, incremental layer-wise training schedule while always training the full network. With ~4.9M parameters, both DRNN variants reach test bits-per-character comparable to prior state-of-the-art recurrent models (multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization), despite much simpler models and training. Beyond accuracy, the paper provides analyses suggesting emergent temporal hierarchies across layers: ablations of layer-wise outputs, perturbation/forgetting experiments that estimate layer time scales, and qualitative/quantitative evidence that higher layers capture long-range structure (e.g., closing parentheses over hundreds of characters).

Relation to prior work
The idea of hierarchical/stacked recurrent architectures is not new (e.g., hierarchical RNNs; stacked LSTMs in speech). Prior state-of-the-art on the same dataset used MRNNs trained with HF. This paper differs by (i) using simple stacked tanh RNNs without gating or multiplicative interactions, (ii) demonstrating that SGD with straightforward tricks suffices for competitive performance at this scale, and (iii) providing careful analyses linking layer depth to temporal scales and long-range dependencies in a real-world dataset.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity and simplicity: stacking vanilla RNNs plus deep supervision to outputs is a clean, broadly applicable design that works surprisingly well.
- Empirical significance: competitive BPC on a large, challenging corpus using SGD only, narrowing the perceived gap with second-order methods.
- Insightful analyses: multiple complementary probes (ablation, typo perturbation, context switch, parentheses tracking) provide convincing evidence of emergent temporal hierarchy and long-range memory.
- Practical training insights: the supervised incremental layer-wise schedule for DRNN-1O is a useful takeaway; gradient norm normalization and long BPTT are clearly described.
- Relevance: character-level modeling is a strong testbed for short/long dependencies; results and analyses will interest the RNN community.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Novelty is moderate: stacked RNNs and multi-scale/hierarchical RNN ideas predate this work; the main architectural contribution is incremental training and attaching outputs at each layer (deep supervision), which is more of an engineering/training strategy than a fundamentally new model.
- Limited experimental breadth: only three models on a single dataset, no statistical variation across seeds, and sparse hyperparameter exploration.
- Missing comparisons: no results for LSTM/stacked LSTM under similar parameter budgets and SGD training; no MRNN baseline trained with SGD; this limits conclusions about the advantages of deep vanilla RNNs versus gating or multiplicative interactions.
- Fairness of BPC comparison: the character set differs (95 vs 86 in prior work); while authors argue the effect is negligible, a matched-character-set number would strengthen the claim.
- Confounded analysis: the different training regimes for DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO make it hard to ascribe time-scale differences purely to architecture rather than training curriculum (authors acknowledge this).
- Reporting: some training choices (e.g., exact gradient norm used for normalization, effective BPTT length and truncation details) could be crisper; figures are essential for full assessment.

Quality
Technically sound overall, with sensible methodology and careful qualitative/quantitative probes. Claims about temporal hierarchy are supported empirically. However, the lack of stronger baselines and matched settings temper the strength of state-of-the-art claims and the generality of conclusions.

Clarity
Generally well-written and easy to follow; the training setup is described in enough detail for reproduction by an expert. Some typos and footnote clutter aside, the exposition is clear. It would help to precisely specify gradient normalization (norm target) and to consolidate training hyperparameters.

Originality
Moderate. The model is a straightforward composition of known elements. The most original aspects are (a) demonstrating competitive large-scale results with simple SGD on stacked vanilla RNNs, and (b) the careful, insightful analyses of layer time scales on a real dataset.

Significance
Reasonable. The work can influence practice by showing that simple stacked RNNs with SGD can be competitive, and by offering training tricks and diagnostic analyses. The temporal hierarchy findings are interesting and may motivate further research. The absence of stronger contemporary baselines limits the immediate impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add a matched-character-set BPC to ensure fair comparison to MRNN/HF results.
- Include a stacked LSTM baseline and, if possible, MRNN trained with SGD to clarify where DRNNs stand.
- Report variance across seeds and some hyperparameter sensitivity (depth, BPTT length, gradient norm threshold).
- Unify training regimes between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO for a cleaner time-scale comparison.
- Release code/scripts to facilitate reproduction.

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear, bold empirical message: simple stacked RNNs trained with SGD can achieve near-SOTA on a challenging corpus.
- Novel and useful analyses that reveal emergent temporal hierarchies and long-range behavior in deep RNNs.
- Practical training insights likely to be adopted by practitioners.

Arguments against acceptance
- Incremental architecture relative to prior hierarchical/stacked RNN work; novelty rests more on analysis than model.
- Limited experimental scope and missing key baselines reduce confidence in generality and strength of claims.
- Some fairness concerns in comparisons.

Recommendation
Weak accept. Despite moderate architectural novelty and limited breadth, the paper advances the conversation with a simple, effective approach and thoughtful analyses of temporal hierarchy in deep RNNs. I value the conceptual direction and practical insights, and I’m willing to overlook the experimental limitations if some clarifications are addressed.
