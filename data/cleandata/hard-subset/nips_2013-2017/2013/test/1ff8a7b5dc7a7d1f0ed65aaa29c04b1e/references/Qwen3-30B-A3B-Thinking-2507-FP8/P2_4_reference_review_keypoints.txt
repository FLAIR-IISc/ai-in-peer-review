- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack RNN layers receiving previous-layer hidden states, creating temporal hierarchies across layers.  
- Claims DRNNs model different time scales per layer, enabling better short- and long-term dependency modeling.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) using only SGD, avoiding complex Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides formal mathematical formulation and two variants (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO).  
- Experimental setup is thorough and appropriate, using bits-per-character (BPC) and careful hyperparameter/training details.  
- Includes valuable ablation studies: layer contributions, context-switch behavior, and long-range parentheses closing.  
- Acknowledges limitations: DRNNs do not outperform standard RNNs on tasks needing only long memory without hierarchical processing.  
- Introduces incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O to address poor training of lower layers.  
- Training efficiency comparison (SGD: ~10 days single GPU vs Hessian-free: 5 days on 8 GPUs) is compelling but approximate.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with effective figures, though some computational/training details need clarification.  
- Original contribution: formalizes DRNN architecture and systematically analyzes emergent temporal hierarchies, filling gaps in prior stacked-RNN work.  
- Significance: offers both theoretical insight into temporal hierarchies and practical SOTA results with simpler optimization.  
- Strengths: clear formalization, strong empirical results, comprehensive temporal analysis, simpler training, informative ablations.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to LSTMs/GRUs, unclear scalability to larger datasets/tasks, vague computational-cost comparison, unclear causal link between time-scale differences and performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and well-supported paper that meets NIPS standards.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
