

# Peer Review: "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

## Summary

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal dynamics of recurrent neural networks. The authors propose that stacking recurrent layers naturally creates a hierarchy of time scales, where lower layers process short-term dependencies and higher layers handle longer-term dependencies. The paper compares two variants of DRNNs: DRNN-1O (output connections only at the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output connections at all layers). The authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling tasks, specifically on a Wikipedia text corpus, using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD) without advanced optimization techniques like Hessian-free training.

The work builds on previous research on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3,5,6]) and stacked bidirectional LSTMs [8], but makes a distinct contribution by analyzing how the DRNN architecture creates natural temporal hierarchies. The paper provides experimental evidence for the emergence of different time scales across layers through several analyses: performance when removing individual layer contributions, text generation quality when disabling specific layers, and experiments measuring the temporal persistence of perturbations.

## Quality

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a technically sound architecture with clear mathematical formulation (Section 2.1).
- The experimental design is thorough, with multiple ablation studies and analyses of temporal properties.
- The results are well-supported by experiments on a standard language modeling task (character prediction on Wikipedia text).
- The paper provides meaningful analysis of how different layers contribute to the network's performance, particularly regarding time scales (Section 3.3).
- The authors carefully compare with relevant prior work, including the Hessian-free approach from [19].

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper doesn't sufficiently explain why the DRNN architecture would be particularly well-suited for language modeling beyond the theoretical argument about temporal hierarchies. While the Wikipedia text does contain hierarchical structure, the paper doesn't provide a detailed analysis of how the specific temporal hierarchies in the data map to the DRNN's layer structure.
- The training methodology for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training) is not thoroughly justified. The authors state it was "necessary to reach good performance," but don't provide evidence comparing the performance with and without this layer-wise training.
- The paper doesn't fully address why the DRNN-AO variant performs worse than DRNN-1O, despite having more output connections. This is a key point that could strengthen the analysis of the architecture.
- The claim that DRNNs "embed these different time scales directly in their structure" needs stronger empirical support. The paper shows that different layers have different time scales, but doesn't demonstrate that this is specifically due to the DRNN architecture rather than the training process.
- The comparison with previous work is good, but the paper doesn't sufficiently discuss how their approach relates to other hierarchical sequence modeling approaches like the hierarchical attention mechanisms in Transformer-based models (which have become dominant in language modeling since 2017).

## Clarity

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- The DRNN architecture is well-explained with equations (Section 2.1) and a helpful schematic figure (Figure 1).
- The experimental setup is described in sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- The analysis of temporal hierarchies through the typo-perturbation experiment is clearly presented (Section 3.3).

**Weaknesses:**
- Some technical details about the training process could be clearer. For example, the exact meaning of "incremental layer-wise method" (Section 2.3) could be better explained.
- The paper refers to "the same number of neurons for every layer" (Section 3.1) but doesn't explain why this was chosen over varying layer sizes, which might have been more effective.
- The description of the "generative mode" (Section 3.2) is somewhat vague; it would be helpful to know more about how the sampling was done (e.g., temperature parameter).
- The connection between the theoretical motivation and the empirical results could be made more explicit. The paper states the DRNNs "embed these different time scales directly in their structure," but the evidence for this is somewhat indirect.

## Originality

**Strengths:**
- The DRNN architecture itself is novel, combining DNNs and RNNs in a way that creates natural temporal hierarchies.
- The paper provides a fresh perspective on how hierarchical processing can be achieved in recurrent networks, going beyond just stacking LSTMs.
- The analysis of time scales across layers is original and provides valuable insights into how DRNNs process information.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's novelty is somewhat limited by the fact that stacked RNNs (e.g., [8]) have been used before, and the main contribution is the analysis of temporal hierarchies rather than the architecture itself.
- The paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate its approach from recent work on hierarchical attention mechanisms (e.g., Transformer-based models), which also create multiple time scales but through a different mechanism.
- The paper cites [5] as having "constructed a hierarchy of RNNs, which all operate on different time-scales (using subsampling)," but doesn't clearly explain how their approach differs from this previous work.

## Significance

**Strengths:**
- The paper demonstrates that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only SGD, which is significant because previous state-of-the-art results required more complex optimization techniques.
- The analysis of temporal hierarchies provides valuable insights into how deep recurrent architectures process information, which could inform future work on temporal modeling.
- The results on the parentheses-closing task (Section 3.4) are particularly compelling evidence for the DRNNs' ability to handle long-term dependencies.

**Weaknesses:**
- The performance gains over standard RNNs are modest (BPC of 1.52 vs. 1.57 for RNN), which limits the practical significance of the work.
- The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that DRNNs would outperform other state-of-the-art language models (like Transformers) on this task, which would be a more significant contribution.
- The paper doesn't address whether DRNNs would scale to larger datasets or more complex language modeling tasks, which is an important consideration for practical significance.
- The work is limited to character-level language modeling, which is less common in modern language modeling (which typically uses subword or word-level models).

## Specific Comments

1. The paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with the hierarchical attention mechanisms in Transformer models, which have become the dominant approach for language modeling. The authors should discuss how their approach differs from these more recent methods.

2. The claim that "DRNNs embed these different time scales directly in their structure" is central to the paper's contribution. The authors should provide more direct evidence for this claim, perhaps by showing that the time scales in the DRNN are more stable and predictable than those in a standard RNN.

3. The paper mentions that the DRNN-AO variant performed slightly worse than DRNN-1O, but doesn't provide a thorough analysis of why this might be the case. This is a significant point that should be addressed.

4. The training time (10 days on a single GPU) is quite long for such a relatively small task. The authors should discuss whether this is a fundamental limitation of the architecture or if it could be improved with better optimization techniques.

5. The paper would be strengthened by including a brief discussion of the computational complexity of DRNNs compared to standard RNNs or LSTMs, especially since the paper claims they are "well-suited to capture temporal hierarchies."

## Recommendation

The paper presents an interesting architecture that combines the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks, with valuable insights into how these networks create temporal hierarchies. The experimental results are generally sound and demonstrate a meaningful improvement over standard RNNs for character-level language modeling. However, the novelty of the architecture is somewhat limited, and the paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate its approach from more recent work on hierarchical sequence modeling.

I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper makes a modest but meaningful contribution to the understanding of how deep recurrent architectures process temporal information, but it falls short of making a major breakthrough in the field. The work is technically sound and well-executed, but the significance is somewhat limited by the modest performance gains and the fact that the approach doesn't clearly outperform more recent state-of-the-art methods.

## Arguments for and against acceptance

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Provides a novel perspective on temporal hierarchies in recurrent networks
- Demonstrates meaningful performance improvements over standard RNNs using only SGD
- Includes thorough experimental analysis of temporal properties
- Well-written and clearly organized

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The novelty is somewhat limited compared to recent work on hierarchical sequence modeling
- The performance gains are modest (BPC improvement of ~0.05)
- Doesn't sufficiently differentiate from more recent state-of-the-art approaches (e.g., Transformers)
- Doesn't address scalability to larger datasets or more complex tasks
- Some key claims (e.g., "embed these different time scales directly") aren't strongly supported

Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a modest contribution to the field, but it doesn't represent a major advance that would warrant strong acceptance at NIPS. It would be a good fit for a conference with a broader scope, but at NIPS, the bar for acceptance is higher.
