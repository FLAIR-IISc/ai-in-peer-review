This paper presents a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), essentially stacking RNNs to create a hierarchical temporal structure. The core idea – combining the depth of DNNs with the memory of RNNs – is compelling and intuitively addresses limitations of both architectures when dealing with complex time-series data. The authors motivate this work well, highlighting the need for both hierarchical processing *and* multi-scale temporal modeling, aspects not fully addressed by standard RNNs or existing hierarchical RNN approaches. The empirical results on character-level language modeling are strong, achieving state-of-the-art performance with a surprisingly simple training procedure (SGD) compared to previous work relying on Hessian-free optimization. The detailed analysis of layer contributions, time scales, and parenthesis handling provides valuable insights into the network’s learned representations.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture is a genuinely interesting combination of established techniques. It’s not merely an incremental improvement, but a conceptually different way to approach recurrent modeling, explicitly aiming for hierarchical temporal representation learning.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving SOTA performance on a challenging task (character-level language modeling) with a relatively straightforward optimization method (SGD) is a significant achievement. This lends credibility to the core idea.
* **In-depth Analysis:** The authors don’t just present results; they actively try to *understand* what the network is learning. The analysis of layer contributions, time scales (typo perturbation experiment), and parenthesis handling are particularly insightful. This level of analysis is often missing from papers focused purely on performance.
* **Clear Motivation & Connection to Existing Work:** The paper effectively situates the DRNN within the context of both DNNs and RNNs, articulating its advantages and limitations. It thoughtfully acknowledges related work and positions its contribution accordingly.
* **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is generally easy to follow, with a logical flow of ideas and clear explanations of the methodology.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the character-level language modeling task is well-chosen, the experiments are somewhat limited in scope. Testing on other tasks with different temporal dynamics (e.g., speech recognition, video processing) would strengthen the claims about the DRNN’s general applicability.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of performance to hyperparameters isn't explored thoroughly. While the authors mention some tuning, a more systematic investigation of hyperparameter settings (layer size, learning rate schedules, etc.) would be beneficial.
* **Theoretical Justification:** While the intuition behind the DRNN is strong, a more formal theoretical justification for *why* this architecture should be effective would be valuable.  For instance, a discussion of the representational capacity of DRNNs compared to standard RNNs could be added.
* **Comparison to LSTMs:** While the results are compared to other RNN variants, a more direct comparison to LSTMs with equivalent parameter counts (and ideally trained with SGD) would provide a clearer benchmark. The authors allude to LSTM challenges on certain tasks but don’t directly demonstrate a DRNN advantage.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The DRNN presents a genuinely novel architecture that addresses limitations of existing recurrent models.
* The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating SOTA performance on a challenging task.
* The detailed analysis provides valuable insights into the network’s learned representations and behavior.
* The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its contribution.
* The potential impact of this work is significant, as it opens up new avenues for hierarchical temporal modeling.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The experimental validation is somewhat limited, lacking tests on diverse tasks.
* The theoretical justification for the DRNN architecture could be strengthened.
* A more thorough comparison to LSTMs is needed to solidify the claim of superior performance.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and potentially impactful idea. While some further experimentation and theoretical justification would be beneficial, the originality, strong empirical results, and insightful analysis outweigh the weaknesses. I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field and warrants acceptance. I would rate this as a **Strong Accept**. The boldness of the idea and the preliminary success with a relatively simple approach make it a worthwhile addition to NIPS, even if some aspects remain to be fully explored. I would encourage the authors to expand the experimental validation and theoretical analysis in future work.
