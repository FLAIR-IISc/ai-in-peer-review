- Paper studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNNs) with two output schemes (top-layer-only DRNN-1O and all-layers-to-output DRNN-AO) for character-level language modeling under matched parameter budgets.
- Models trained with truncated BPTT and plain SGD with gradient normalization; report DRNNs outperform single-layer RNN and approach HF-trained MRNN performance.
- Analyses include layer ablations, per-layer perturbation/typo/context-switch responses, and long-range structure tests (parentheses) indicating emergent temporal hierarchies and multi-hundred-character dependencies.
- Relates to prior stacked/hierarchical RNN work and character-level MRNN research; main novelty is empirical demonstration and diagnostics with a layerwise pretraining recipe.
- Strength: Clear, timely question about whether stacking simple RNNs yields temporal hierarchies and improved modeling.
- Strength: Sound empirical methodology within constraints (parameter-matched models, reasonable BPTT length, insightful analyses).
- Strength: Practical training recipe (SGD + gradient normalization + incremental layerwise pretraining) valuable to practitioners.
- Strength: Useful qualitative diagnostics (per-layer contributions, generative samples) and evidence of long-range interactions.
- Strength: Ties performance claims to analyses of temporal context usage.
- Weakness: Confound between architecture and training curriculum—DRNN-1O uses layerwise pretraining while DRNN-AO does not, possibly explaining time-scale differences.
- Weakness: Missing strong baselines (single/multi-layer LSTM/GRU, stacked LSTM) under same dataset and parameter budget.
- Weakness: Limited statistical rigor—only single seeds reported, no error bars or seed variability evidence.
- Weakness: MRNN comparison uses a different character set; results should be reported on an identical setup or re-evaluated.
- Weakness: BPTT truncation (200 steps) conflicts with strong claims about modeling dependencies spanning several hundred characters; needs quantitative span-vs-performance analysis and span distribution in training data.
- Weakness: Optimization details unclear (apparent typo in Eq. 3; unusual gradient normalization); comparisons to clipping/RMSProp/AdaGrad/momentum needed.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—missing hyperparameters, exact gradient norm, batch details, seed info, and curriculum implementation specifics.
- Clarity: Generally readable with helpful figures and samples, but fix Eq. (3) and minor formatting/footnote issues; include BPC numbers in-text.
- Originality: Stacked RNNs are not novel; contribution is incremental empirical evidence and diagnostics plus a practical pretraining scheme.
- Significance: Practically relevant that simple stacked tanh RNNs approach MRNN/HF performance, but impact limited without stronger baselines and controls.
- Suggestions: Control curriculum confound, add LSTM/GRU baselines, report multiple seeds/error bars, provide learning curves and throughput.
- Suggestions: Quantify temporal analyses (per-layer perturbation half-lives, BPC recovery time constants, closure accuracy vs span, nested parentheses tests).
- Suggestions: Clarify Eq. (3), justify design choices (zero init output weights, input scales, depth choice), and evaluate on additional corpora for robustness.
- Acceptance stance: Well-executed empirical study with informative analyses; leans toward weak accept pending stronger baselines, clearer controls, and improved statistical/methodological detail.
