This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, aiming to combine the strengths of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors present a DRNN architecture and evaluate it on a Wikipedia text corpus, achieving state-of-the-art results compared to other recurrent networks utilizing the same number of parameters. They also provide an analysis of the learned timescales within the network layers.

While the paper tackles an interesting problem and presents promising results, a multitude of minor, yet pervasive, issues detract significantly from its overall quality and impact. The presentation suffers from inconsistencies, a lack of rigor in experimental details, and a tendency towards speculative interpretation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture, combining stacked RNNs with DNN-like hierarchical processing, is a reasonably novel approach.
*   **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance (albeit narrowly) with SGD is a positive result, especially considering previous reliance on more complex optimization techniques like Hessian-free training.
*   **Analysis Attempt:** The attempt to analyze learned timescales within the network is commendable, though the conclusions drawn are often weakly supported.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Formatting & Notation:** Numerous inconsistencies plague the manuscript. Equation (1) lacks a clear definition of `U`, which appears to change meaning between equations (1) and (2). The notation `s̄` is used without consistent definition and its meaning is unclear in the hidden state evolution equation. Bias terms are mentioned as being omitted from notation but aren’t consistently handled – sometimes implicitly accounted for, sometimes seemingly ignored. Variable definitions are often vague (e.g. 'Nin' is introduced but not clearly linked to the input sequence 's(t)').
*   **Experimental Detail:** Crucially, the experimental setup lacks essential details. The choice of 95 characters + ‘unknown’ is justified weakly, and the impact of this choice isn't discussed. While the authors mention a GPU model, they omit vital information like batch size, sequence length used during training (beyond the BPTT window), and the specifics of the initialization scheme beyond the general distribution. The layer-wise training approach for DRNN-1O feels ad-hoc and lacks sufficient justification; the choice of varying parameter update counts across layers is unexplained. The claim about needing 10 days for a single network training is unconvincing without providing more details on the computational setup.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The claim that stacking RNNs *automatically* creates different timescales is oversimplified. While plausible, it isn’t rigorously demonstrated. The discussion on hierarchical processing feels largely intuitive and lacks a solid theoretical grounding.
*   **Interpretation & Speculation:** The interpretations of the timescale analysis (Figure 3) are overly speculative. Correlating the decay of the perturbation signal with the ‘time scale’ of a layer is a reasonable hypothesis but is presented as fact. The analysis of parenthesis closing (Section 3.3) borders on post-hoc justification. Assertions that the DRNN learns ‘pseudo-stable attractor-like states’ for parenthesis matching are not supported by the presented evidence.
*   **Related Work:** The literature review is shallow. The comparison to [19] is framed solely around performance, neglecting potentially relevant methodological differences. The discussion of Hessian-free optimization feels cursory.
*   **Figure Quality:** Figure 1 is rather vague and doesn't clearly illustrate the temporal feedback loops within each layer.  The information density is too high, making it hard to grasp the DRNN architecture at a glance.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   The paper is riddled with minor, yet distracting, technical flaws and inconsistencies.
*   The experimental setup lacks critical details, hindering reproducibility and thorough evaluation.
*   The interpretations of results are often speculative and lack strong empirical support.
*   The clarity of presentation is hampered by inconsistent notation and vague descriptions.

**For:**

*   The DRNN architecture presents a potentially interesting combination of DNNs and RNNs.
*   The performance results, while incremental, are promising.
*   The attempt to analyze learned timescales demonstrates some insight into the network’s behavior.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the novelty of the DRNN architecture and the achievement of competitive performance, the numerous shortcomings in presentation, experimental detail, and rigorous analysis outweigh the strengths. The paper feels incomplete and rushed. While the ideas are interesting, they are not presented with the necessary clarity and precision expected for NIPS publication. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**. 

The authors should significantly revise the manuscript, addressing the issues outlined above. Specifically, they need to: (1) meticulously check and correct all inconsistencies in notation and formatting, (2) provide a complete and detailed description of the experimental setup, (3) strengthen the theoretical grounding of their claims, (4) temper their interpretations with greater caution and empirical support, and (5) improve the clarity of the figures. Without these substantial revisions, the paper falls short of NIPS standards.
