This paper explores a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), combining DNN and RNN concepts, for character-level language modeling. While the motivation – leveraging hierarchical processing and multi-timescale dynamics – is reasonable, the presentation feels somewhat iterative and lacks a truly compelling novelty beyond the straightforward stacking of RNN layers. The authors correctly identify limitations of standard RNNs regarding hierarchical representation and timescale handling, referencing prior work [3, 5, 6, 8], but the proposed DRNN appears largely as a natural extension, rather than a significant architectural innovation.

The technical details are presented clearly, and the mathematical formulation of the hidden state evolution is standard. However, the justification for the specific architecture choices – particularly the number of layers and units – feels ad-hoc. While they mention parameter counts aligning with previous work [19], a more thorough ablation study exploring the impact of these hyperparameters would be valuable. The training procedure, utilizing stochastic gradient descent with a linearly decaying learning rate and gradient normalization, is conventional and adequately described.

A significant weakness is the limited experimental evaluation. Only three network instantiations are tested, which, as the authors themselves acknowledge, restricts the ability to draw firm conclusions. The performance, achieving state-of-the-art results for *recurrent* networks with comparable parameter counts, is encouraging, but it is crucial to acknowledge that this is achieved using a relatively standard training procedure and doesn't inherently surpass non-recurrent deep learning approaches. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is interesting, but the context surrounding the computational cost of the latter is glossed over. The analysis of layer contributions, while insightful regarding the role of different layers, is limited in scope and lacks a more rigorous statistical validation. The text generation examples are qualitative, and the analysis of parenthesis handling is a specific case study, not a generalizable metric of language modeling capability.

The claim of inherent multi-timescale dynamics is supported by the typo-perturbation and context-switching experiments, showing differential time constants in different layers. However, this is not entirely surprising, as stacking layers naturally introduces different pathways for information flow. The question remains whether this emergent hierarchy is *superior* to, or even qualitatively different from, other techniques for capturing long-range dependencies. The analysis in Figure 3 feels somewhat post-hoc and requires stronger justification. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art performance *among recurrent networks* on a challenging task.
* Provides empirical evidence supporting the idea that stacking RNNs can induce hierarchical temporal dynamics.
* Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* Limited novelty: The DRNN is a relatively straightforward extension of existing architectures.
* Limited experimental evaluation: Only three network instantiations are tested.
* Insufficient comparison to non-recurrent deep learning methods.
* Analysis, while insightful, lacks rigor and broad applicability.
* The “state-of-the-art” claim should be qualified to “state-of-the-art among recurrent networks with comparable parameter counts.”



Overall, this paper presents a reasonable, well-executed experiment, but it falls short of making a truly significant contribution. The work feels incremental, and the claims of novelty and advantage are not sufficiently supported by comprehensive experimentation. It reads more like an engineering demonstration than a groundbreaking research advance. I recommend **rejection**, but encourage the authors to consider expanding the experimental evaluation, including comparisons to more sophisticated non-recurrent models, and providing a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the emergent hierarchical structure. A more detailed ablation study on the impact of architectural choices (layer depth, unit size) would also strengthen the work.
