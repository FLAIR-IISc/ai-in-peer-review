- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers forming a temporal hierarchy for short- and long-term dependencies.
- DRNN-1O achieves strong empirical performance (BPC = 1.488) on character-level language modeling using simple SGD.
- Analysis shows lower layers capture short-term dependencies and higher layers capture longer time scales; parentheses-closing task demonstrates long-term modeling.
- Architecture variants compared: DRNN-1O (output only at top) and DRNN-AO (outputs at all layers).
- Strength: novel, meaningful extension of RNNs with strong experimental validation and practical SGD training advantages.
- Strength: comprehensive analysis (ablation studies, time-scale measurements, and illustrative tasks) and clear writing/figures.
- Strength: practical contribution by avoiding complex second-order optimization while achieving state-of-the-art recurrent performance.
- Weakness: no comparison to LSTMs, the standard baseline for language modeling and long-term dependency handling.
- Weakness: insufficient contextualization versus prior stacked RNN work (notably Graves et al.) and unclear distinctions.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why temporal hierarchies emerge across layers.
- Weakness: unconventional layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is not analyzed for generality or necessity.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with significant empirical support but weakened by omitted critical comparisons.
- Recommendation: weak acceptance, contingent on adding LSTM comparisons and clarifying differences from prior stacked RNN work.
- Pros summary: novel architecture, clear performance gains with SGD, strong analysis, well-written, demonstrates long-term dependency modeling.
- Cons summary: missing LSTM and Graves comparisons, limited theory, potentially non-general layer-wise training, unclear preference over other deep RNNs.
