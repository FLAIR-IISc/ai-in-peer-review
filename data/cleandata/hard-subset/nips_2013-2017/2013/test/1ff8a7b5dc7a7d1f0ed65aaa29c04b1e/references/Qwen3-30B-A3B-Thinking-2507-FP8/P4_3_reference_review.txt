

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine deep neural network architecture with recurrent connections to create a temporal hierarchy where different layers operate at different time scales. The authors argue that standard RNNs lack explicit support for multiple time scales, while DRNNs naturally develop this property through their layered architecture. The paper focuses on character-level language modeling as a test case, demonstrating that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia text corpus using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), whereas previous state-of-the-art results required more complex Hessian-free optimization.

The paper builds upon prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and stacked LSTMs [8], but makes a key distinction by focusing on the temporal hierarchy aspect rather than merely achieving good performance. The authors compare two DRNN variants: DRNN-1O (output connections only at the top layer) and DRNN-AO (output connections at all layers), with the latter providing layer-wise insights into the network's operation.

Strengths:
1. The theoretical formulation of DRNNs is clear and well-motivated, with detailed equations for hidden state evolution and output generation. The authors effectively demonstrate how the architecture creates different time scales across layers.
2. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including performance metrics (BPC), ablation studies on layer importance, and specific tests for long-term dependencies (parentheses closing). The typo perturbation experiments provide strong evidence for the temporal hierarchy claim.
3. The paper achieves state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using simpler training methods (SGD vs. Hessian-free optimization), which has practical significance for practitioners.
4. The analysis of time scales across layers through perturbation experiments provides valuable theoretical insights into how DRNNs process temporal information.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper doesn't fully explain why DRNN-1O outperforms DRNN-AO, which is somewhat counterintuitive given the authors' motivation for DRNN-AO (to improve training and provide layer-wise insights).
2. The computational cost comparison with previous work is somewhat vague, with the authors noting "a computational cost of roughly 4 times ours" but not providing concrete details about the comparison.
3. The authors acknowledge they only tested three network instantiations due to computational constraints, which limits the statistical robustness of their results.
4. The related work section could be strengthened by discussing more recent papers on similar architectures, particularly those that have emerged since the cited works.

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper presents a well-motivated and novel architectural combination of DNNs and RNNs with clear theoretical justification.
- The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating clear advantages over standard RNNs in handling multiple time scales.
- The analysis of temporal hierarchies provides valuable theoretical insights into recurrent network behavior.
- The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance with simpler training methods, which is practically significant.

Arguments against acceptance:
- The lack of explanation for DRNN-1O's superior performance relative to DRNN-AO is a notable weakness.
- The computational cost comparison with prior work needs more concrete details.
- Limited number of experiments due to computational constraints affects statistical robustness.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by providing both a practical architecture for sequence modeling and theoretical insights into how recurrent networks can learn temporal hierarchies. The results advance the understanding of how recurrent architectures handle multiple time scales in sequential data, which is a fundamental challenge in sequence modeling. The work is technically sound, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for replication.

The paper meets all four NIPS criteria: it is of high quality with well-supported claims, clearly written with appropriate organization, novel in its focus on temporal hierarchy (distinct from prior hierarchical RNN work), and significant for both practitioners and researchers in sequence modeling. While the limited number of experiments is a minor concern, it's a common challenge in deep learning research that the authors acknowledge.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the understanding and application of recurrent neural networks for sequence modeling tasks. The combination of theoretical insight and practical results, along with clear experimental validation, makes it a strong candidate for NIPS.
