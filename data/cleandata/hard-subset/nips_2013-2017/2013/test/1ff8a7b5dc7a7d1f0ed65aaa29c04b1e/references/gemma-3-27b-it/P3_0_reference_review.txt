## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a sensible and well-executed investigation into Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling. It convincingly demonstrates that stacking RNNs creates a natural temporal hierarchy and achieves state-of-the-art results using a relatively simple training procedure (SGD) compared to prior work relying on more sophisticated optimization techniques. While the paper isn’t groundbreaking in terms of a completely novel architecture, its thorough analysis and empirical validation contribute meaningfully to our understanding of DRNNs. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**Summary:**

The authors explore DRNNs, which are essentially DNNs with recurrent connections at each layer, motivated by the limitations of standard RNNs in capturing both hierarchical processing and multiple time scales inherent in sequential data like language. They propose and analyze two DRNN variants – DRNN-1O (output from the top layer only) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers) – applied to a character-based language modeling task on a large Wikipedia corpus. They demonstrate that DRNNs, particularly DRNN-1O, achieve competitive performance with previous state-of-the-art methods while offering insights into the role of different layers in capturing varying temporal dependencies.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Motivation & Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of both standard DNNs and RNNs, providing a strong rationale for investigating DRNNs. The discussion of time scales in language is particularly insightful.
* **Strong Empirical Results:**  Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task using only SGD is a significant achievement. This makes the approach more accessible than methods requiring Hessian-free optimization.
* **In-Depth Analysis:** The authors go beyond simply reporting performance numbers.  The analyses of layer contributions (Figure 2), time scale dynamics (Figure 3), and the ability to handle parentheses are particularly valuable. These analyses provide genuine insight into how the DRNN architecture operates.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally clear, well-structured, and easy to follow. The equations are clearly presented and the figures are informative.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail regarding the network architectures, training parameters, and dataset, making it reasonable to believe the results could be reproduced.  (Although replicating a 10-day training run is a challenge!)

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** The DRNN architecture itself isn't radically new. Stacking RNNs has been explored before (mentioned in the related work, but not deeply discussed).  The main contribution lies in the detailed analysis and successful application of this architecture.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper touches upon the sensitivity of the learning rate, but a more thorough exploration of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance would strengthen the work.  For instance, a sensitivity analysis of the number of neurons per layer would be valuable.
* **Comparison to LSTMs:** While the paper compares to a MRNN, a more direct comparison to LSTMs (and perhaps GRUs) with similar parameter counts would be beneficial.  Given the widespread use of LSTMs, understanding their relative strengths and weaknesses against DRNNs is important.
* **Analysis Depth Limitations:** The analysis, while strong, sometimes feels descriptive rather than explanatory.  For example, the observation that the top layer contributes most to output is interesting, but further investigation into *why* this is the case would be more insightful. Is it simply due to the downstream task, or is there something inherent in the DRNN structure that promotes this behavior?



**Specific Comments & Questions:**

* **Introduction:** The comparison between DNNs and RNNs regarding hierarchical processing feels somewhat strained.  DNNs *do* perform hierarchical feature extraction, even if it's not explicitly recurrent.
* **Section 2.3:** The justification for the gradient normalization heuristic is weak. A brief explanation of *why* it prevents gradient explosions would be helpful.
* **Section 3.2:** The explanation for choosing 5 layers feels somewhat arbitrary. Was this based on preliminary experiments, or other considerations?
* **Figure 3:** The scale of the y-axis in Figure 3 is a bit unclear.  What units are being used for the distance between hidden states?
* **Parenthesis Example:** The observation that the network struggles with nested parentheses is interesting. Could this be related to the limited depth of the DRNN or potentially addressed by a different training regime?




**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging task.
* Provides valuable insights into the behavior and capabilities of DRNNs.
* The analysis is thorough and well-supported by experimental evidence.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The core architecture isn’t radically novel.
* The analysis, while good, could be deepened to provide more explanatory power.
*  A more direct comparison to LSTM variants would be beneficial.



**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more thorough comparison to LSTMs and deepening the analysis to explain the observed phenomena rather than simply describing them.  Addressing the specific comments regarding clarity and justification would also improve the paper. This is a solid contribution to the understanding of deep recurrent architectures and warrants inclusion in NIPS.
