

# Peer Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture that combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks (DNNs) with the temporal dynamics of recurrent neural networks (RNNs). The authors propose stacking RNN layers such that each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers. This architecture addresses two key limitations of standard RNNs: the inability to explicitly model multiple time scales and the limited memory depth for long-term dependencies. The paper demonstrates that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a character-level language modeling task using only stochastic gradient descent (without advanced optimization methods), and provides detailed analysis of the temporal hierarchies learned by the network.

The work builds upon previous research on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3, 5, 6]) and the successful application of stacked bidirectional LSTMs in [8], but makes a significant contribution by explicitly connecting the architecture to the temporal hierarchy inherent in language data. The authors provide compelling evidence that DRNNs naturally develop a hierarchy of time scales during training, which enables better modeling of long-term dependencies than standard RNNs.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound and well-executed. The authors provide clear mathematical formulation of the DRNN architecture (Section 2.1) and thoroughly evaluate it on a standard character-level language modeling task (Section 3). The experimental setup is comprehensive, with multiple network configurations (standard RNN, DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) and several insightful analyses (time scale measurements, parentheses closing capability). The results are well-supported by both quantitative metrics (BPC) and qualitative analysis (text generation samples).

The paper appropriately acknowledges limitations, such as the need for layer-wise training for DRNN-1O and the fact that DRNNs may not offer advantages for tasks requiring only long-term memory without hierarchical processing. The analysis of time scale hierarchies (Figure 3) and parentheses closing (Figure 4) provides strong evidence for the main claims of the paper.

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for the DRNN architecture is clearly articulated in the introduction, and the technical details are presented with sufficient mathematical precision. Figures 1-4 are particularly effective in illustrating the architecture and key findings. The authors provide enough detail for an expert reader to reproduce the experiments (e.g., network sizes, training parameters, evaluation metrics).

The paper could be slightly improved by clarifying the relationship between DRNNs and other hierarchical architectures (e.g., how DRNNs differ from the "stacked RNNs" in [5] or the "hierarchical LSTM" in [6]). However, these distinctions are sufficiently addressed in the related work discussion.

## Originality

The DRNN architecture represents a novel combination of DNNs and RNNs that has not been previously explored in depth. While previous work has used stacked RNNs (e.g., [8]), this paper provides a rigorous theoretical and empirical analysis of why this architecture works well for tasks with temporal hierarchies. The key insight that stacking RNNs inherently creates multiple time scales is both novel and well-supported by the experimental evidence.

The paper is significantly different from previous NIPS papers on RNNs, particularly those focusing on LSTMs [11] or echo state networks [13]. The authors clearly articulate how their work differs from and builds upon previous contributions.

## Significance

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field by demonstrating that a simple architectural modification (stacking RNNs) can yield state-of-the-art results on language modeling with standard training methods. This is particularly significant because it achieves performance previously requiring complex optimization techniques like Hessian-free training [19]. The insights into temporal hierarchies learned by DRNNs provide valuable theoretical understanding of how deep RNNs process temporal information.

The paper also opens up several promising avenues for future work, such as applying DRNNs to other sequence modeling tasks, exploring second-order optimization methods, and extending pre-training schemes to DRNNs. The demonstrated ability to handle long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses across long phrases) suggests DRNNs could be beneficial for a range of natural language processing tasks.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Novel architecture that effectively combines DNNs and RNNs
- Strong empirical evidence for the temporal hierarchy claim
- State-of-the-art results using only SGD (no complex optimization)
- Insightful analysis of time scales and long-term dependencies
- Clear, well-organized presentation

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited comparison to other standard RNN variants like LSTMs (which were dominant in 2013)
- No discussion of computational efficiency compared to other architectures
- The paper could better connect to the broader NLP landscape (e.g., how this relates to later transformer architectures, though this was not yet developed at the time)

## Recommendation

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of deep learning for sequence modeling. The DRNN architecture is novel, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-executed, and the insights about temporal hierarchies provide valuable theoretical understanding. The results are significant as they demonstrate that simple architectural modifications can achieve state-of-the-art performance without complex optimization techniques.

I recommend acceptance. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and makes a meaningful contribution that advances the state of the art in recurrent neural networks. The insights about temporal hierarchies in DRNNs are particularly valuable for understanding how deep recurrent architectures process sequential data.

The paper would benefit from slightly more discussion of how DRNNs compare to LSTMs (which were standard at the time), but this is a minor point given the paper's clear contribution. The authors have provided a complete and compelling case for their approach, and the results are robust across multiple analyses.
