

# Peer Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

## Summary
This paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which stack RNN layers with temporal feedback loops in each layer, creating a natural temporal hierarchy where different layers operate at different time scales. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus) using only SGD, without the need for advanced Hessian-free optimization. They provide evidence for the emergence of temporal hierarchies through perturbation experiments, layer contribution analysis, and a parentheses-closing task. The work builds on prior hierarchical RNN research [5,8] but focuses on analyzing the temporal hierarchy aspect rather than just achieving performance gains.

## Quality
The paper presents technically sound methodology and well-designed experiments. The DRNN architecture is a natural extension of standard RNNs and deep networks, and the authors provide convincing empirical evidence for the emergence of temporal hierarchies through multiple complementary experiments. The BPC results on character-level language modeling are solid and comparable to state-of-the-art methods. The layer contribution analysis (removing layer outputs) and time-scale perturbation experiments effectively demonstrate the hierarchical nature of DRNNs.

However, the paper lacks theoretical justification for why DRNNs naturally develop temporal hierarchies. While the empirical evidence is strong, the claim that "DRNNs embed different time scales directly in their structure" would be strengthened by more formal analysis. The training methodology for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training) differs significantly from DRNN-AO, making direct comparison challenging. The authors acknowledge this but don't fully justify why the different training approach is necessary for DRNN-1O.

## Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The architecture is clearly described with equations and a schematic (Figure 1). The experimental setup is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. The presentation of results is clear, with well-constructed figures (Figures 2-4) that effectively communicate the key findings about temporal hierarchies.

The paper could be improved by more clearly distinguishing the DRNN architecture from similar stacked RNN approaches like [8]. The authors cite [8] as using "stacked bi-directional LSTM networks" but don't explicitly clarify how their DRNN differs. The distinction between "temporal hierarchy" and "hierarchical processing" in the introduction is somewhat confusing and could be clarified.

## Originality
The core contribution is the empirical demonstration that DRNNs automatically develop temporal hierarchies, which is a novel insight not previously established in the literature. While stacked RNN architectures have been explored before [5,8], this paper provides the first detailed analysis of how these architectures create natural time-scale hierarchies. The authors successfully position their work as filling a gap between prior hierarchical RNN research and the need for understanding temporal hierarchies.

The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive related work section. The authors cite [5] as "constructing a hierarchy of RNNs operating on different time-scales" but don't adequately discuss how their approach differs from this prior work. Recent work on hierarchical RNNs with attention mechanisms [e.g., 21] should be referenced to better position this contribution.

## Significance
The paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding how recurrent networks process temporal information. Demonstrating that DRNNs naturally develop temporal hierarchies provides valuable theoretical insight that could guide future RNN architecture design. The achievement of state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using only SGD is significant, as it challenges the assumption that advanced optimization methods are necessary for good performance.

The implications for understanding long-term dependencies (through the parentheses-closing task) are particularly compelling. The finding that DRNNs can model dependencies spanning hundreds of characters is important for language modeling and potentially other temporal tasks. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently explore the broader implications beyond language modeling.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Clear empirical demonstration of temporal hierarchy in DRNNs
- State-of-the-art results on character-level language modeling with standard SGD
- Multiple complementary analyses (layer contribution, perturbation, text generation)
- Thorough exploration of time-scale dynamics

**Weaknesses:**
- Lack of theoretical justification for temporal hierarchy emergence
- Inconsistent training methodology for DRNN-1O vs. DRNN-AO
- Insufficient differentiation from prior stacked RNN work
- Limited discussion of broader implications beyond language modeling
- No comparison to recent Transformer-based language models

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how RNNs process temporal information, with strong empirical evidence supporting the key claim about temporal hierarchies. The main weaknesses are the lack of theoretical justification and insufficient differentiation from prior work on stacked RNNs. The authors should address these by:

1. Adding a brief theoretical discussion on why temporal hierarchies emerge in DRNNs
2. Clarifying the relationship to prior stacked RNN work, especially [8]
3. Adding a brief comparison to recent Transformer-based language models (even if results are not directly comparable)

The paper is technically sound, well-executed, and makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of RNNs. The empirical evidence for temporal hierarchies is compelling, and the results on character-level language modeling are significant. With minor revisions to strengthen the theoretical grounding and related work, this paper would make an excellent contribution to NIPS.

**Overall Rating: Strong Accept**
