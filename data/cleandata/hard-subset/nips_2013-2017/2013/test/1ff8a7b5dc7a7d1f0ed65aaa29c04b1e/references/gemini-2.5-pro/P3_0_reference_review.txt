**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces and analyzes the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple recurrent layers. The core hypothesis is that this deep structure inherently creates a temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process information on shorter time scales and higher layers capture longer-term dependencies. The authors propose two variants: one where only the top layer produces output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute (DRNN-AO). They evaluate these models on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. The key findings are: (1) DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained only with more complex Hessian-Free optimization, using only SGD with gradient clipping. (2) Through a series of insightful analyses (perturbation decay, context switching, parenthesis completion), the paper provides strong empirical evidence that trained DRNNs indeed learn a hierarchy of time scales.

This work builds upon prior research in hierarchical RNNs [5] and the application of deep LSTMs in speech recognition [8]. However, where [8] was primarily performance-focused, this paper's main contribution is its in-depth analysis, providing a much-needed understanding of *why* depth is beneficial in recurrent models for sequence modeling. It directly competes with and provides a simpler alternative to the training methods proposed in [19] for achieving top performance on this task.

**Strengths**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results with a Simpler Method:** The paper's most significant practical result is achieving state-of-the-art performance on a difficult, large-scale benchmark using standard SGD (with gradient normalization). This is a compelling result, as it rivals the performance of the MRNN trained with the far more computationally intensive Hessian-Free optimization [19]. This finding has important implications, suggesting that architectural choices (i.e., depth) can be as impactful as sophisticated second-order optimizers for overcoming training challenges in RNNs.

2.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The primary strength of this work lies not just in its performance but in its rigorous analysis. The authors go beyond simply reporting a BPC score.
    *   The "typo test" (Fig. 3, left) provides direct evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy, showing that higher layers retain memory of a perturbation for much longer than lower layers. The comparison between the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO training schemes and their effect on these time scales is particularly insightful.
    *   The parenthesis-closing experiments are a superb qualitative demonstration of long-range dependency modeling. Showing that the top layer maintains a high activation for the closing parenthesis over hundreds of characters (Fig. 4) provides a clear, interpretable example of the model learning abstract grammatical structure.
    *   The ablation study on layer contributions in the DRNN-AO model provides a good sanity check and hints at the functional specialization of different layers (e.g., the bottom layer handling basic statistics, top layer handling longer-term structure).

3.  **Novel Training Insights:** The paper introduces an effective incremental layer-wise training scheme for the DRNN-1O model. While related to greedy layer-wise pre-training [9], its application here (training the full stack at each stage) is a practical and effective method for ensuring gradients reach the lower layers in a very deep architecture, which is a well-known challenge.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Missing Comparison with LSTMs:** The most significant omission is the lack of a direct comparison with Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units [11]. The introduction correctly identifies the motivation for LSTMs as learning to select what information to pass on. Given that the paper's core theme is modeling long-term dependencies, a baseline of a single-layer, large LSTM and, more importantly, a stacked LSTM architecture are crucial missing comparisons. It is unclear whether the observed benefits (temporal hierarchy, long-term memory) are a unique property of stacked simple RNN units or a general property of deep recurrent architectures that would also be present (and perhaps stronger) in a stacked LSTM.

2.  **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The experiments are limited to three specific network instantiations, with the number of layers fixed at five. While the computational cost is acknowledged, this makes it difficult to assess the relationship between depth and performance. Does performance continue to improve with 6 or 7 layers? Is there a point of diminishing returns? A study on a smaller dataset exploring the effect of depth would have strengthened the conclusions.

3.  **Ambiguity in the "DRNN" Terminology:** While the architecture is clearly explained, the term "Deep RNN" for stacked RNNs is not entirely novel, as the authors' own citation of Graves et al. [8] on stacked LSTMs demonstrates. The contribution here is less the invention of the architecture and more the detailed analysis and demonstration of its effectiveness with vanilla RNN units and SGD. The paper could be clearer in positioning its novelty in the analysis rather than the architecture itself.

**Detailed Comments and Questions**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the experimental results appear solid and well-supported by the presented analysis. The work is a complete and valuable contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The methodology is described with sufficient detail, and the figures (as described) effectively illustrate the key analytical points. The motivation for each experiment is clear.
*   **Originality:** The originality is moderate in terms of the architecture itself but high in terms of the analysis. While stacked RNNs existed, this is one of the first works to systematically investigate *how* they function and provide evidence for the temporal hierarchy hypothesis on a real-world, complex task.
*   **Significance:** The paper is highly significant. It demonstrates that architectural depth is a viable path to modeling complex temporal structures and can mitigate some of the optimization difficulties traditionally associated with RNNs. The analytical techniques presented are valuable for understanding and interpreting deep sequential models. This work helped solidify the move towards deeper architectures in sequence modeling, which has become standard practice.

**Recommendation:** Accept.

This is a strong paper with significant contributions. The empirical results are impressive, and the in-depth analysis provides novel and important insights into the functioning of deep recurrent models. The main weakness—the lack of an LSTM comparison—is a limitation but does not invalidate the paper's core findings about stacked vanilla RNNs. The strengths in analysis and the demonstration of SOTA performance with a simple optimizer make this a valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
