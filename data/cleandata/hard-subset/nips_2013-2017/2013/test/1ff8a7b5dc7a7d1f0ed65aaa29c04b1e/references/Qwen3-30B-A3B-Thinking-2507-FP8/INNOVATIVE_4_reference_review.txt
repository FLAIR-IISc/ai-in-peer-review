

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that stacks recurrent neural network layers such that each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input time series. The authors argue that this architecture creates a temporal hierarchy where different layers operate at different time scales, addressing a key limitation of standard RNNs that struggle with modeling multiple time scales simultaneously. The paper demonstrates DRNNs on character-level language modeling, claiming state-of-the-art results using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD) - a simpler training method compared to previous state-of-the-art approaches that required Hessian-free optimization.

The paper's main novelty lies in the architectural insight that stacking RNNs inherently creates a temporal hierarchy. Previous work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3, 5, 6]) has focused on subsampling or artificial tasks, while this paper provides a comprehensive analysis of DRNNs on a real-world language modeling task. The authors make a compelling case that DRNNs embed temporal hierarchies directly in their structure, with evidence from experiments showing different time scales across layers, as demonstrated through perturbation tests and parenthesis-closing tasks.

The paper's strengths are significant. The core idea of DRNNs is genuinely novel and bold - it combines two major neural network paradigms (deep networks and recurrent networks) in a way that creates a new conceptual framework for temporal processing. The authors provide strong empirical evidence for their central claim about temporal hierarchy, with multiple experiments (typo perturbation, context switching, parenthesis closing) that collectively demonstrate the existence of different time scales across layers. The fact that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art results with simpler training (SGD instead of Hessian-free optimization) is particularly impressive and valuable for practical applications.

The paper also makes an important contribution to understanding how deep architectures function in the recurrent domain. The analysis of how individual layers contribute to output (Figure 2) and the generative text samples (Table 2) provide valuable insights into the internal functioning of these networks. The paper's claim that DRNNs model parentheses as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state" rather than memorizing phrases is particularly insightful.

While the paper has some limitations, they are largely acceptable given the paper's strong novelty. The experiments are limited to one dataset (Wikipedia character-level language modeling), but the authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest future work on other tasks. The computational constraints (only three network instantiations) are understandable given the resource demands, and the authors carefully note that more parameters (not just the architecture) improve performance. The comparison to Hessian-free methods is somewhat limited as the authors don't directly compare training time, but the key point about simpler training with SGD remains valid.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a strong case for its contributions. The figures (particularly Figure 1 showing the architecture and Figure 3 showing time-scale differences across layers) are essential for understanding the paper's core insights.

Pro-acceptance arguments:
- Introduces a genuinely novel architectural concept (DRNNs) that creates a temporal hierarchy
- Provides strong empirical evidence for the central claim about temporal hierarchy
- Achieves state-of-the-art results with simpler training (SGD vs. Hessian-free)
- Offers valuable insights into how deep recurrent architectures process information
- Makes a clear connection between architecture and temporal processing capabilities

Con-rejection arguments:
- Limited experimental scope (only one dataset/task)
- Some claims about the architecture's superiority could be strengthened with more diverse experiments
- The paper doesn't fully address whether the performance gains are due to architecture or simply more parameters

This paper represents a significant conceptual advance in recurrent neural network architecture. The DRNN concept is bold, novel, and well-supported by experiments. While the methodology has some limitations, the paper's core contribution - the architectural insight that stacking RNNs creates a temporal hierarchy - is substantial and likely to inspire future work. The paper advances the field by providing a new framework for understanding how temporal processing might be structured in deep architectures.

I recommend acceptance, as the paper introduces a significant new concept that addresses a fundamental limitation of RNNs and provides strong evidence for its theoretical claims. The novelty and boldness of the approach outweigh the methodological limitations, and the insights provided will be valuable to researchers working on temporal modeling.
