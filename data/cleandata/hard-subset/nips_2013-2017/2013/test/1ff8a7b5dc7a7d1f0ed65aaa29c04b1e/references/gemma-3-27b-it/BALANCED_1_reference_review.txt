This paper explores deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), combining the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks to capture temporal hierarchies in sequential data. The authors motivate their work by pointing out the limitations of standard RNNs in modeling multi-scale temporal dependencies, and propose a DRNN architecture where each layer is an RNN, allowing for different time scales to emerge. They apply this architecture to the character-level language modeling task, achieving state-of-the-art results with SGD and providing an analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies.

**Summary of Related Work:** The paper appropriately positions itself within the existing literature. It references foundational work on DNNs [4], RNNs [17], LSTMs [11], and hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6]. It acknowledges recent advances in training methods such as Hessian-free optimization [19] and accurately reflects the state-of-the-art performance achieved by these methods. The comparison to the work in [8] is useful, highlighting the novelty of the authors' in-depth analysis of the DRNN architecture.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture is a sensible and potentially powerful combination of DNNs and RNNs. The idea of implicitly creating different time scales through stacking RNNs is well-motivated and intuitively appealing.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using only SGD is a significant achievement. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture and its relative ease of training compared to methods relying on Hessian-free optimization.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply presenting results and attempts to understand *why* the DRNN works. The analysis of time scales, layer contributions, and the ability to handle parentheses provides valuable insights into the network's internal workings.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup. The inclusion of Figure 1 is helpful for understanding the DRNN structure.
* **Thorough Experiments:** The experiments include ablation studies (removing layer outputs) and tests of long-term dependencies (parentheses), which strengthen the analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scalability:** The authors acknowledge the computational cost of training, limiting them to only three network instantiations. While understandable, this hinders a more comprehensive exploration of hyperparameter space and architectural variations.  A larger scale study would be desirable.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The justification for certain hyperparameters (e.g., the learning rate for the top layer in DRNN-1O) is somewhat brief. A more systematic exploration and reporting of hyperparameter tuning would strengthen the results.
*   **RNN Comparison Details:** While the authors compare to a common RNN, further detail on the RNN’s architecture (number of layers, hidden units) would make the comparison more direct and informative.  Was this a simple single-layer RNN?
*   **Limited Scope of Analysis:** The analysis, while detailed, primarily focuses on the character-level language modeling task. It would be beneficial to explore the DRNN's performance on other tasks with different temporal dynamics.
* **Figure 2 Interpretation:** The interpretation of Figure 2 (layer contribution to performance) feels slightly hand-wavy. The authors state that the intermediate layers seem “more important in preprocessing data”, but this could be phrased more cautiously or supported with further analysis.

**Originality:** The combination of stacking RNNs to create a hierarchy of time scales is a novel approach, distinguishing this work from previous research on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6]. While the building blocks (DNNs and RNNs) are well-established, the DRNN architecture and the detailed analysis of its temporal properties represent a significant contribution.

**Significance:** The results are important because they demonstrate a promising new architecture for modeling sequential data. The ability to achieve state-of-the-art performance with a relatively simple training algorithm (SGD) makes the DRNN potentially accessible to a wider range of researchers and practitioners. The insights gained from the analysis of temporal hierarchies could inform the design of other deep learning architectures.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Strong empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
*   Novel combination of DNNs and RNNs with a clear motivation and intuitive appeal.
*   Detailed analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies provides valuable insights.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of the work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited scalability and hyperparameter exploration.
*   Analysis is primarily focused on a single task.
*   Some aspects of the analysis (e.g., interpretation of Figure 2) could be strengthened.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper with a novel and promising approach. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths outweigh them. I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field and is worthy of acceptance. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept**

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   Conduct a more systematic hyperparameter search.
*   Explore the DRNN's performance on additional datasets and tasks.
*   Provide more details on the architecture of the RNN used for comparison.
*   Elaborate on the interpretation of the layer contribution analysis (Figure 2).
*   Discuss potential avenues for scaling up the training process.
