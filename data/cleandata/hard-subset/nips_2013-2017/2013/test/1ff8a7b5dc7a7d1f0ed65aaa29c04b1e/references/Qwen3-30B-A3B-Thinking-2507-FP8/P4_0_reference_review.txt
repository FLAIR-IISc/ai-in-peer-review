

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

## Summary
This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that combines hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal memory capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The authors propose that stacking RNN layers with temporal feedback loops creates a natural temporal hierarchy, where lower layers handle short-term dependencies and upper layers capture long-term dependencies. The paper builds on prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5, 6]) and stacked LSTMs [8], but provides more thorough analysis of temporal hierarchy within the architecture. The authors evaluate DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus, comparing against standard RNNs and demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD, whereas prior state-of-the-art required Hessian-free optimization.

## Strengths
1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper presents a clear architectural definition with well-formulated equations (Section 2.1) and provides thorough experimental validation. The analysis of temporal hierarchies through typo-perturbation experiments (Figure 3) and parentheses-closing tests (Figure 4) provides compelling evidence for the claimed temporal hierarchy property.

2. **Novel Contribution**: While hierarchical RNNs have been explored before [5, 6], the DRNN architecture with explicit temporal feedback in each layer and the demonstration of automatic time-scale development across layers represents a novel contribution. The paper successfully demonstrates this through multiple experiments beyond just performance metrics.

3. **Significance**: The paper achieves state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only SGD, which is significant as prior state-of-the-art required more complex training methods. The demonstration of long-term dependency modeling (e.g., parentheses closing) provides concrete evidence of DRNNs' capability to model complex temporal structures.

4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-organized, with clear explanations of the architecture, training methodology, and experimental setup. The figures (not shown here) appear to effectively illustrate the key concepts. The paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Comparison to Recent Work**: The paper does not compare against more recent deep RNN architectures (e.g., deep LSTMs, hierarchical RNNs with different structures) that have emerged since the cited work [5, 8]. This makes it difficult to fully assess the novelty and relative advantages of DRNNs.

2. **Insufficient Training Time Comparison**: While the paper states DRNN-1O achieves state-of-the-art performance using only SGD (10 days on a single GPU), it doesn't provide a detailed comparison of training time versus the Hessian-free method (5 days on 8 GPUs). The claim that "only SGD" is used needs more context about computational cost.

3. **Limited Analysis of Temporal Hierarchy**: The paper demonstrates the existence of temporal hierarchies but doesn't thoroughly explain how the architecture learns these hierarchies or how they relate to language structure. The observation that DRNN-AO has short time-scales in lower layers and longer in upper layers is noted but not deeply analyzed.

4. **Implementation Details**: The paper mentions "very little difference in performance for different initializations" but provides no supporting evidence. The exact implementation details for the DRNN-AO (e.g., how layers interact during training) could be more detailed.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro Acceptance:**
- The paper makes a novel contribution to understanding how hierarchical RNN architectures can automatically develop temporal hierarchies.
- It provides comprehensive analysis beyond just performance metrics, including layer importance, time scales, and long-term dependency modeling.
- The state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks using only SGD are significant and reproducible.
- The analysis of temporal hierarchy addresses a fundamental question about how RNNs process temporal information.

**Con Acceptance:**
- The novelty claim is somewhat limited, as the architecture is a combination of existing concepts (DNNs + RNNs) without sufficient differentiation from recent work.
- The paper doesn't adequately compare DRNNs to more recent deep RNN architectures that may have similar or better performance.
- The computational cost analysis is insufficient to support the claim that DRNNs are more efficient than Hessian-free methods.
- The explanation for why the architecture develops temporal hierarchies remains somewhat superficial.

## Overall Assessment
This paper presents a technically sound and novel architecture (DRNN) with compelling evidence for temporal hierarchy development in recurrent networks. The experimental validation is thorough and demonstrates significant results for character-level language modeling. While the paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding how hierarchical RNNs can model temporal dependencies, it could be strengthened by more comprehensive comparison to recent deep RNN architectures and a more detailed analysis of why the temporal hierarchy emerges. The paper is well-written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.

The work advances the state of the art in recurrent network architectures by demonstrating how hierarchical structure can automatically create temporal hierarchies, which is a significant contribution to the understanding of RNNs. While the novelty is somewhat incremental (combining known concepts), the thorough analysis and state-of-the-art results justify publication. The paper meets all criteria for a high-quality NIPS submission, with particular strength in its experimental analysis of temporal hierarchies. The main limitations are the insufficient comparison to more recent architectures and incomplete computational cost analysis, but these do not undermine the core contribution.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the comparison to more recent work and provide more detail about the training time comparison.
