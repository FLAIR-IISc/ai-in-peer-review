- Proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers to create a temporal hierarchy capturing different time scales.  
- Demonstrates DRNNs outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus) and can model long-term dependencies (e.g., parentheses closing).  
- Contributions: DRNN architecture proposal, experimental validation, analysis of temporal hierarchy, and evidence of long-term dependency modeling.  

- Strength: Architecture and training methodology are technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Rigorous experimental design with proper parameter settings, training protocols, and evaluation (BPC).  
- Strength: Multiple analyses support claims (performance comparisons, layer contribution, time-scale perturbation, parentheses task).  
- Strength: Authors acknowledge limitations honestly (e.g., DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O, layer-wise training).  
- Weakness: Claims of state-of-the-art are not fully compared to recent literature and modern baselines.  
- Weakness: Explanations for why DRNNs outperform RNNs lack deeper theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: Computational cost trade-offs are not thoroughly addressed (training time mentioned but not fully analyzed).  

- Strength: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and figures effectively illustrate architecture and analyses.  
- Strength: Training setup details are sufficient for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Some technical details need clarity (e.g., necessity and explanation of incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O).  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation for why DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O.  

- Strength: DRNN is an original combination emphasizing temporal hierarchy emergence from stacking.  
- Strength: Focused analysis of temporal hierarchies and the parentheses experiment are novel contributions.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer differentiation from related hierarchical RNN work and broader hierarchical sequence-modeling literature.  
- Weakness: Limited engagement with more recent models like hierarchical LSTMs and Transformer-based approaches.  

- Strength: Shows significance by improving recurrent network performance on character-level modeling and providing insights into time-scale structuring.  
- Strength: Parentheses experiment validates practical long-term dependency modeling.  
- Weakness: Significance is mainly demonstrated on character-level tasks with unclear applicability to other domains.  
- Weakness: Does not clearly show advances over other long-term memory approaches (e.g., LSTMs, Hessian-free RNNs).  
- Weakness: Claim that DRNNs embed multiple time scales structurally needs better contextualization versus other architectures (dilated convs, Transformers).  

- Overall: Well-executed, novel, and insightful paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to include broader comparisons, contextualization with other multi-timescale approaches, and analysis of DRNN-AO underperformance.  

- Pro: Novel temporal-hierarchy architecture, rigorous experiments, insightful time-scale analysis, clear presentation, and practical long-term memory validation.  
- Con: Limited recent baseline comparisons, unclear reasons for DRNN-AO underperformance, significance not fully contextualized among modern sequence models, and some unclear technical details.
