

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal memory of recurrent neural networks. The authors propose a novel architecture where each layer is an RNN, and subsequent layers receive the hidden state of the previous layer as input time series. This structure automatically creates different time scales at different layers, forming a temporal hierarchy. The paper compares two DRNN variants: DRNN-1O (output only from top layer) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers), evaluating them on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia text corpus. The authors claim DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD (without Hessian-free optimization), and provide evidence that DRNNs develop a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers.

The paper makes a clear contribution to the understanding of how recurrent architectures can model temporal hierarchies. It builds upon prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6] and stacked LSTMs [8], but distinguishes itself by focusing on the temporal hierarchy aspect rather than just performance gains. The paper also relates to recent work on character-level language modeling [19] and the importance of long-term dependencies [11].

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical soundness:** The paper presents a clear mathematical formulation of DRNNs with well-defined equations for hidden state evolution and output generation. The experimental setup is thorough, with detailed network configurations, training procedures, and performance metrics (BPC). The authors provide convincing experimental evidence for their claims about temporal hierarchies through multiple tests (typo perturbation, context switch measurements, parenthesis closing).

2. **Clarity and organization:** The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of the architecture, training methodology, and experimental results. Figures 1-4 effectively illustrate key concepts and findings. The authors provide sufficient detail for reproducibility, including specific hyperparameters and training schedules.

3. **Novel insights:** The paper makes a valuable contribution by demonstrating that DRNNs automatically develop a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers, which is a significant insight for understanding how deep recurrent architectures process information. The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) and the parenthesis closing test (Figure 4) provide compelling evidence for this claim.

4. **Significance:** The results are significant as they achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on the Wikipedia corpus using only SGD, whereas previous state-of-the-art used more complex Hessian-free training [19]. The paper provides important insights into how DRNNs model long-term dependencies, which could inform future work on recurrent architectures.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited experimental scope:** Due to computational constraints, the authors conducted only three network instantiations (one RNN, one DRNN-1O, one DRNN-AO), which limits the statistical robustness of their claims. The paper could have provided more thorough ablation studies to better understand the relative contributions of different architectural choices.

2. **Incomplete explanation of DRNN-AO performance:** The paper notes that DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O but does not provide a thorough explanation for this difference, which would be valuable for understanding the trade-offs between the two variants.

3. **Limited comparison with related hierarchical RNN work:** While the paper acknowledges prior hierarchical RNN research [3,5,6], it could have provided a more detailed comparison of how DRNNs differ from these approaches, particularly regarding the temporal hierarchy aspect.

4. **Insufficient analysis of computational cost:** The paper compares performance metrics but doesn't provide a thorough analysis of the computational cost of DRNNs relative to other methods, making it difficult to assess practical trade-offs.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro Acceptance:* The paper presents a novel architecture with clear experimental evidence for its key claims about temporal hierarchies. The results are technically sound, the methodology is well-described, and the insights about how DRNNs model time scales have significant potential to inform future work on recurrent architectures. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of temporal processing in deep recurrent networks.

*Con Acceptance:* The limited number of experiments due to computational constraints weakens the statistical significance of the findings. The paper could have provided more thorough analysis of why DRNN-AO underperforms relative to DRNN-1O, which would strengthen the contribution. The computational cost analysis is insufficient for a full assessment of practical implications.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and makes a valuable contribution to understanding how deep recurrent architectures process temporal information. The evidence for the temporal hierarchy claim is compelling, and the results on character-level language modeling are significant. While the limited experimental scope is a drawback, the insights provided are substantial and likely to be of interest to the NIPS community. The paper meets the quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria for NIPS.

The paper is within scope for NIPS as it addresses fundamental questions about neural network architectures and their ability to model temporal dependencies, which is a core topic in machine learning research. The work builds upon but significantly extends previous research on hierarchical RNNs and deep learning for sequential data.

This paper should be accepted as it presents a novel architecture with strong experimental validation and important insights into how deep recurrent networks model temporal hierarchies. The work advances our understanding of recurrent architectures and provides a foundation for future research on deep temporal modeling.
