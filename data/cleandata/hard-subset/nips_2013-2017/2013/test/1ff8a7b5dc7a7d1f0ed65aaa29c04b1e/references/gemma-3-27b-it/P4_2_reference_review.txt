This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) – multilayer perceptrons with temporal feedback loops in each layer – for character-level language modeling. The authors aim to analyze the inherent temporal hierarchy created by stacking RNNs, suggesting different layers capture different time scales. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using stochastic gradient descent (SGD), comparable to results previously achieved with more sophisticated Hessian-free training.

The paper’s core idea – combining the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the memory capabilities of RNNs – is intuitively appealing and builds upon prior work in both areas. The comparison to hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and stacked bi-directional LSTMs [8] is appropriate, correctly positioning the DRNN as a distinct approach, despite its conceptual similarities. The work acknowledges existing performance benchmarks and strives to achieve comparable results with a simpler training algorithm (SGD). The experimental setup, utilizing a large-scale text corpus and established evaluation metrics (BPC), is well-defined and facilitates comparison with existing literature.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture represents a sensible and potentially impactful combination of DNNs and RNNs, offering a different perspective on hierarchical temporal modeling.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance with SGD is a significant accomplishment, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed architecture and training methodology.
*   **Analysis of Time Scales:** The investigation into the time scales learned by different layers is a valuable contribution, providing insights into the network’s internal workings. The experiments on typo perturbation and parenthesis matching are well-designed to explore these time scales.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear explanation of the DRNN architecture and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** The individual components (DNN layers, RNN cells) are well-established. The core novelty lies in their specific arrangement, which, while interesting, may not be revolutionary.
*   **Insufficient Comparison to Baselines:** While the authors compare to [8, 19], a more thorough comparison to more recent and relevant baselines (e.g., Transformers, more modern RNN variants) would strengthen the claims of state-of-the-art performance.  The field has rapidly evolved since many of the cited papers.
*   **Detailed Mechanistic Explanation Missing:**  While the paper demonstrates the existence of different time scales, a deeper explanation of *why* these time scales emerge during training is lacking. A theoretical analysis, even a simplified one, could enhance understanding.
*   **Training Details Lack Rigor:** The description of the training process, especially gradient normalization and the layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, is somewhat superficial. More detailed explanations and ablation studies would strengthen the reproducibility of the results.
*   **Interpretability Limitations:** The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) is presented cautiously, acknowledging its limitations. However, a more robust analysis of layer functionality would be beneficial.
*   **Limited scope of experiments**: The experiments are solely focused on language modeling. Exploring the DRNN’s performance on other sequential tasks could provide further validation and insights.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The DRNN architecture presents a novel and potentially valuable approach to hierarchical temporal modeling.
*   The experimental results are strong, demonstrating competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   The analysis of time scales provides insightful observations into the network’s internal dynamics.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The core components of the DRNN are not fundamentally new, and the novelty primarily resides in their combination.
*   The lack of a comprehensive comparison to more recent baselines weakens the claim of state-of-the-art performance.
*   The paper lacks a deeper theoretical understanding of why the observed time scales emerge.
*   The description of the training process lacks sufficient detail for easy reproducibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a solid contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. The DRNN architecture is a sensible and effective combination of existing techniques, and the experimental results are compelling. However, the limited novelty in the core components and the lack of a thorough comparison to recent baselines are areas for concern.  Given the strong empirical results and insightful analysis, I lean towards **acceptance**, but encourage the authors to address the weaknesses identified above in a revised version, particularly with regard to a more comprehensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art models.
