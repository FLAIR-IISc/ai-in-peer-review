## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the use of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), essentially stacking RNN layers, for character-based language modeling. The authors argue that this architecture naturally creates a hierarchy of timescales, allowing it to capture both short- and long-term dependencies in text. They present results on a Wikipedia corpus demonstrating state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks (though comparable to other methods) and provide analysis of layer contributions and learned timescales.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid, well-executed study. While the core idea of stacking RNNs isn't entirely novel (acknowledged by the authors), the detailed analysis of the resulting temporal hierarchies and the competitive performance achieved using only SGD are valuable contributions. The paper is well-written and largely clear. However, the significance of the findings, while positive, feels somewhat incremental.

**Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The technical approach is sound. The experimental setup is well-defined, and the results are presented clearly. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of RNNs and language modeling. The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) and timescales (Figure 3) adds significant value.  However, the paper relies heavily on empirical results without deeper theoretical justification for *why* DRNNs should exhibit these hierarchical properties.  The limitations of the network size are also acknowledged. Gradient normalization is mentioned as a heuristic; a more detailed explanation of its impact would be beneficial.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The equations are clearly presented, and the figures are helpful. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated. However, the description of the training procedure, especially the layer-wise training for DRNN-1O, could be more detailed.  The reasoning behind the choice of 5 layers isn’t fully explained, though the authors mention prior experiments.
* **Originality (6/10):** The idea of stacking RNNs has been explored previously (cited in the paper).  The novelty lies in the specific DRNN architecture, the detailed analysis of timescales, and achieving strong performance with a relatively simple training method (SGD).  The connection between the architecture and the emergence of timescales is the core contribution, but is not completely groundbreaking.  The paper does a decent job of referencing relevant work.
* **Significance (7/10):** The achieved performance is competitive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the DRNN architecture. The analysis of timescales provides insights into how these networks process sequential data.  However, the results don’t represent a paradigm shift. The state-of-the-art performance is comparable to other methods, albeit achieved with a simpler training scheme.  The specific task (character-level language modeling) is important, but the generalizability to other sequence modeling tasks isn’t fully explored. The exploration of parenthesis matching is a clever experiment, though not central to the core argument.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The DRNNs achieve competitive performance on a challenging task.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The investigation of layer contributions and timescales provides valuable insights into the network’s behavior.
* **SGD Training:** Achieving strong performance with SGD is a notable accomplishment.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Incremental Contribution:**  The core idea isn't entirely novel, and the performance gains are not dramatic.
* **Limited Theoretical Justification:**  The paper lacks a deeper theoretical explanation for the emergence of timescales.
* **Network Size Limitations:** The experiments are limited by the available computational resources.
* **Task Specificity:** The results are primarily demonstrated on character-level language modeling, and the generalizability to other tasks isn’t fully explored.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a solid empirical study with valuable analysis.
* It provides insights into the behavior of deep recurrent networks.
* The competitive performance achieved with SGD is a significant result.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The core idea is not entirely novel.
* The significance of the findings is somewhat incremental.
* The paper lacks a deeper theoretical justification for its claims.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The authors should consider:

* **Expanding the theoretical discussion:** Provide a more detailed explanation of why the DRNN architecture should naturally create a hierarchy of timescales.
* **Addressing limitations:** Acknowledge the limitations of the network size more explicitly and discuss potential future work with larger models.
* **Clarifying training details:** Provide a more detailed description of the layer-wise training procedure for DRNN-1O.
