This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), essentially stacking RNNs to create a hierarchy of timescales. The core idea – combining the depth of DNNs with the temporal modeling capabilities of RNNs – is quite compelling and represents a potentially significant shift in how we approach recurrent network architectures. While the experimental validation is focused on character-level language modeling, the motivation for exploring hierarchical temporal processing is well-articulated and resonates with known complexities in sequential data like speech. The authors achieve state-of-the-art results comparable to those obtained with more complex training schemes like Hessian-free optimization, using only SGD, which is a noteworthy achievement.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture is a genuinely interesting idea. Stacking RNNs to induce a timescale hierarchy is not a trivial extension of existing work and hasn't been thoroughly explored in the literature, especially not with this specific formulation.
*   **Performance:** Reaching SOTA performance with a relatively simple training method (SGD) is a strong positive signal. It suggests the architecture itself is contributing meaningfully to the results, and isn't simply benefiting from a superior optimization technique.
*   **Analysis:** The initial analyses on timescale emergence and layer contributions (Figures 2, 3, and 4) are valuable. The observation of different timescales within the layers, and the differing roles of layers in parenthesis matching, provides some insight into *how* the DRNN is functioning, beyond just demonstrating *that* it works.
*   **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a logical progression from motivation to architecture to experiments.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The character-level language modeling task, while a good starting point, is not overwhelmingly convincing as a definitive test of the DRNN's capabilities. The authors acknowledge that tasks focused specifically on long-term memory (like those introduced in [11]) may be more suitable, but don’t explore them.
*   **Incomplete Ablation Studies:** While layer contribution is analyzed for the DRNN-AO, a more systematic ablation study—e.g., varying the number of layers, neuron counts, or connection types—could provide more clarity on the architectural choices. The DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison isn't sufficient to fully dissect the impact of all layers having output connections.
*   **Training Details & Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The section on training setup is adequate, but lacks a detailed exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity. A more rigorous analysis of learning rate schedules and initialization strategies would strengthen the claims about the robustness of the approach.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the intuition behind timescale hierarchy is sound, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical justification for *why* this architecture should work. A more formal analysis of the emergent dynamics, perhaps drawing connections to signal processing concepts, would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The DRNN architecture is a genuinely novel contribution with the potential to influence future research in recurrent neural networks.
*   The empirical results are strong, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance without relying on complex optimization techniques.
*   The initial analyses provide some encouraging insights into the inner workings of the DRNN.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the motivation and rationale behind the proposed approach.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single task, and the paper lacks a more thorough investigation of the DRNN's generalizability.
*   The analysis, while promising, is relatively shallow and leaves many questions unanswered.
*   The lack of a strong theoretical foundation weakens the claims about the underlying mechanisms of the architecture.



**Overall Assessment:**

I lean strongly towards acceptance. The novelty of the DRNN architecture and its impressive performance are compelling enough to outweigh the weaknesses. While more extensive evaluation and analysis would be desirable, the paper presents a significant new idea that has the potential to move the field forward. The fact that SOTA results are achieved with SGD is a particularly strong selling point, demonstrating the inherent power of the architecture. It feels like a risk worth taking to include this work in NIPS, and the authors should be encouraged to expand on these findings in future research.



**Rating:** Accept (Strongly Consider)
