- Studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) trained with SGD, gradient-norm normalization, and incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O.
- Large-scale Wikipedia character-level LM (~1.4B chars) comparing single-layer RNN to 5-layer DRNNs with matched parameter counts; DRNNs outperform single RNN, DRNN-1O achieves best BPC and is competitive with prior HF-trained MRNNs.
- Analyses include perturbation experiments (typo/context) to estimate per-layer timescales, ablations of per-layer outputs in AO, and parenthesis tracking showing top-layer long-term retention.
- Relates to hierarchical/stacked RNN prior work but focuses on simpler stacked tanh RNNs trained with SGD and detailed temporal analyses.
- Strength: clear architectural question and empirical answer showing emergent multi-timescale dynamics and distinct layer roles (AO vs 1O).
- Strength: solid large-scale experiment demonstrating competitive performance with a simple optimizer.
- Strength: concrete analyses (typo/context perturbations, AO ablations, parenthesis tracking) yield interpretable insights.
- Strength: simple, potentially practical training recipe (gradient normalization, linear LR decay, incremental layering).
- Strength: paper is well written and candid about limitations.
- Weakness: missing canonical gated baselines (stacked LSTMs/GRUs) for direct empirical comparison.
- Weakness: limited experimental breadth and statistical rigor (only three instances, single seeds, no variance or validation-based hyperparameter selection).
- Weakness: state-of-the-art claims are hard to assess due to dataset/char-set/training differences across works.
- Weakness: nonstandard training choices (always normalizing by gradient norm, repeated output reinitialization) are unablated and may alter optimization dynamics.
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps (hidden-state reset behavior, sequence sampling protocol, exact BPCs, hyperparameters, code release).
- Quality: methodology and analyses are generally sound but empirical section is narrow and some choices unablated.
- Clarity: paper is readable but needs more reproducibility details (hidden-state handling, sampling, splits, exact metrics).
- Originality: stacking RNNs not new, but DRNN-AO, incremental training schedule, and large-scale temporal analyses are novel contributions.
- Significance: useful insights for character-level modeling and hierarchical RNNs, but impact limited by lack of gated baselines.
- Suggestions: add matched LSTM/GRU baselines, clarify hidden-state/reset and BPTT length effects, report exact BPCs/training details/variance, compare gradient-norm to clipping/other optimizers, describe validation/regularization, discuss output reinitialization, and release code.
- Pros for acceptance: empirical evidence of emergent temporal hierarchy, competitive performance, and well-executed analyses.
- Cons for acceptance: missing gated baselines, limited experiments/statistics, unusual unablated training choices, and insufficient reproducibility details.
- Recommendation: borderline accept contingent on adding gated baselines and clarifying training/reproducibility details.
