### Summary of the Paper
This paper investigates the properties of Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The authors hypothesize that this deep structure is well-suited for modeling data with temporal hierarchies, as different layers can learn to operate on different time scales. The core idea is that each layer receives the sequence of hidden states from the layer below it as input, allowing for progressively more abstract and longer-term temporal representations as one moves up the stack.

The authors evaluate this architecture on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus. They propose and compare two variants: one where only the top layer produces an output (DRNN-1O) and one where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The main contributions are:
1.  Achieving state-of-the-art performance on this benchmark for recurrent networks, matching results obtained with more complex Hessian-Free optimization by using only standard Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) with gradient normalization.
2.  Providing a detailed analysis that offers strong empirical evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy within the trained DRNNs. Higher layers are shown to have longer memory and operate on longer time scales.
3.  Demonstrating the model's ability to capture very long-term dependencies, such as correctly closing parentheses over hundreds of characters.

This work builds upon previous efforts in hierarchical RNNs [5, 6] and, more directly, on the use of stacked LSTMs by Graves et al. [8]. However, where [8] focused primarily on achieving state-of-the-art performance in speech recognition, this paper's main contribution lies in its in-depth analysis of *why* and *how* the deep architecture is effective for modeling temporal data.

### Strengths
1.  **Excellent Empirical Results with a Simpler Method:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art results (1.479 BPC) on a difficult benchmark. Crucially, it achieves this using a standard SGD-based training method. This is a significant result, as the previous best performance from a recurrent network [19] relied on the far more computationally expensive and complex Hessian-Free optimization. This suggests that the architectural prior of the DRNN is highly effective for this task, reducing the need for powerful second-order optimizers.

2.  **Thorough and Insightful Analysis:** The paper's primary strength is its analysis, which goes well beyond simply reporting a performance number.
    *   The experiments designed to measure the time scales of different layers (the "typo" perturbation test and the context-switch test) provide compelling evidence for the central hypothesis: that a temporal hierarchy is learned, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory.
    *   The analysis of parenthesis-closing is a concrete and intuitive demonstration of the model's ability to learn non-trivial, long-range dependencies. Showing that the top layer's output remains high for the closing parenthesis throughout a long parenthetical phrase is an excellent piece of qualitative analysis.
    *   The ablation study on the DRNN-AO model, showing the different roles of the bottom (basic statistics) and top (longer-term structure) layers, provides further insight into the functional decomposition learned by the network.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is very well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow. The distinction between depth in DNNs (hierarchical processing) and unfolded RNNs (memory) is well-articulated in the introduction. The model is described concisely, and the experimental setup is detailed enough to understand the methodology. The figures are clear and effectively support the paper's claims.

### Weaknesses
1.  **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The authors state that due to the high computational cost, they only trained three network instantiations. While the results are strong, the conclusions would be more robust if supported by multiple runs with different random initializations to provide a sense of variance. The claim in footnote 3 that "there is very little difference in performance for different initialisations" is an assertion that is not backed by evidence in the paper.

2.  **Confounding Training Procedures:** The best-performing model, DRNN-1O, is trained with a specialized incremental layer-wise procedure, whereas the DRNN-AO and the baseline RNN are trained end-to-end. This makes a direct architectural comparison between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO difficult. The superior performance of DRNN-1O could be attributed to this "pre-training" curriculum as much as to the architecture itself (i.e., having output only at the top). The paper acknowledges this training method was "necessary to reach good performance," which highlights the optimization difficulty but also complicates the analysis of the architectural trade-offs.

3.  **Minor Lack of Clarity on Hyperparameters:** While the training setup is mostly clear, some choices are not fully justified. For instance, the input weights Z1 are initialized with a different variance than the other inter-layer weights Wi and Zi>1. While there may be a good reason for this, it is not explained, slightly hindering full reproducibility. Similarly, the specific schedule for increasing the number of parameter updates for each stage of the DRNN-1O training seems somewhat arbitrary.

### Arguments For/Against Acceptance
**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   The paper presents state-of-the-art results on a recognized, challenging benchmark.
*   It achieves these results with a conceptually simple architecture and a standard optimization algorithm (SGD), which is an important contribution to the field.
*   The primary value lies in the excellent, in-depth analysis that validates the hypothesis of emergent temporal hierarchies in deep RNNs, providing valuable insights for the community.
*   The work is well-written, clearly presented, and provides a solid foundation for future research into deep sequential models.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   The experimental results are based on a very small number of runs (one for each model), making it difficult to assess their statistical significance.
*   The comparison between the two main DRNN variants is confounded by the use of different training procedures, which clouds the conclusions about the architectural design choices.

### Conclusion and Recommendation
Overall, this is a very strong paper. The weaknesses identified are relatively minor compared to the strengths. The achievement of state-of-the-art results with a simpler optimization method is a significant finding. More importantly, the paper provides a much-needed analysis of *why* deep recurrent architectures are effective, moving beyond mere performance benchmarking. The empirical evidence for learned temporal hierarchies is convincing and provides a clear intuition for practitioners and researchers. This work is a valuable contribution to our understanding of deep learning for sequential data. I strongly recommend acceptance.
