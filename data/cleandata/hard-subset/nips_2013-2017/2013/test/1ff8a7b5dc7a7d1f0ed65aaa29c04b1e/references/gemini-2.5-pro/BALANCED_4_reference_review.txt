### Summary of the Paper
This paper introduces and analyzes the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers. The motivation is to combine the hierarchical processing capabilities of deep networks with the temporal modeling of recurrent networks. The authors hypothesize that this architecture will inherently learn a temporal hierarchy, with different layers operating on different time scales. They propose two variants: one with an output only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The models are evaluated on the challenging task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia dataset. The DRNNs, trained with standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD), achieve state-of-the-art performance, matching results previously obtained by more complex models (Multiplicative RNNs) trained with advanced second-order optimization methods (Hessian-Free). The main contribution of the paper is not just the performance, but a thorough analysis that provides strong evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy within the DRNN. Through a series of well-designed experiments, the authors demonstrate how different layers capture dependencies of varying lengths and contribute to solving the task.

The work builds upon prior research in both deep feed-forward networks and recurrent networks. It directly relates to previous work on hierarchical RNNs [5, 6] and, more specifically, the stacked LSTM architecture used for speech recognition [8]. However, where prior work focused primarily on achieving state-of-the-art performance, this paper distinguishes itself by providing a detailed analysis of the internal dynamics and learned representations of the deep recurrent architecture, which was previously lacking.

### Strengths (Arguments for Acceptance)
1.  **Significant Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates impressive performance on a large-scale, difficult benchmark. Achieving state-of-the-art results for RNNs on the Wikipedia character modeling task using only SGD with gradient clipping is a significant achievement, especially when compared to the complex Hessian-Free optimization required by the previous state-of-the-art [19]. This shows the power and practicality of the proposed architecture.

2.  **Excellent and Insightful Analysis:** The core strength of this paper is its in-depth analysis of the DRNN's behavior. The authors go beyond simply reporting performance metrics.
    *   The experiment measuring the decay of a perturbation (a typo) provides compelling, quantitative evidence for the central hypothesis: that stacked RNN layers learn a temporal hierarchy with increasing time scales in higher layers (Fig. 3, left).
    *   The analysis of the model's ability to handle long-term dependencies, specifically by tracking open parentheses over hundreds of characters (Fig. 4), is an excellent and intuitive demonstration of the model's memory capabilities.
    *   The ablation studies on the DRNN-AO model, which analyze the contribution of each layer to the final output and generated text quality, provide valuable insights into the functional specialization of the layers.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The DRNN architecture is explained precisely, the experimental setup is detailed, and the analysis is presented in a logical and compelling narrative. The figures are informative and effectively support the paper's claims.

4.  **Originality and Significance:** While stacked RNNs are not an entirely new concept [8], this paper is the first to provide such a thorough investigation into *why* and *how* they work. The discovery and demonstration of the learned temporal hierarchy is a fundamental contribution to our understanding of recurrent models. This work provides a strong foundation for future research into deep recurrent architectures and their applications, and its findings are likely to be widely influential.

### Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement
1.  **Limited Comparison to LSTM:** The paper uses standard tanh RNN units. While this is sufficient to demonstrate the benefit of depth, the introduction explicitly mentions LSTMs [11] as a key architecture for handling long-term dependencies. A comparison to a deep (stacked) LSTM network would have been very valuable. It is possible that a deep LSTM would perform even better, or perhaps the hierarchical structure of the DRNN provides benefits that LSTMs achieve through their gating mechanisms. A discussion or experiment on this point would strengthen the paper's conclusions about the source of the performance gains.

2.  **Ad-hoc Training for DRNN-1O:** The incremental, layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O is effective but feels somewhat heuristic. Discarding output weights at each stage and using a different training schedule for each added layer makes the process complex and less elegant than end-to-end training. While the authors are transparent about this, it raises questions about whether this complexity is truly necessary or if a better end-to-end training strategy (e.g., with more careful initialization or normalization) could have achieved similar results. The DRNN-AO, which is trained end-to-end, provides a cleaner and more generalizable approach.

3.  **Limited Experimental Scope:** The authors acknowledge that due to computational constraints, they only tested three network configurations, with the number of layers (5) and neurons per layer fixed to match the total parameter count of a baseline. While understandable, this limits the generality of the findings. An exploration of how performance and the temporal hierarchy scale with the number of layers would have been a valuable addition. The claim that network performance is insensitive to initialization for such large models is plausible but would be stronger if supported by results from at least two different random seeds.

### Final Assessment
This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. It presents a simple yet powerful architecture, demonstrates its effectiveness with state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark, and—most importantly—provides a rigorous and insightful analysis that advances our understanding of deep recurrent models. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and the findings are of high importance for both researchers and practitioners working with sequence models. While a comparison with deep LSTMs and a broader set of experiments would have been welcome, the existing work is a complete and compelling piece of research. The analysis of the learned temporal hierarchy is a standout contribution that will likely be cited for years to come. I strongly recommend acceptance.
