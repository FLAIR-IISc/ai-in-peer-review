This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), essentially stacking RNNs to create a hierarchy of timescales, and demonstrates promising results on character-level language modeling. The core idea – combining the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal memory of RNNs – is genuinely novel and a compelling direction for research. While the analysis isn’t exhaustive, the authors provide initial evidence supporting their hypothesis about inherent timescale creation and demonstrate competitive performance using only SGD, a significant feat given prior reliance on Hessian-free optimization for comparable results. The exploration of layer contributions, and especially the analysis of parenthesis handling, are particularly insightful and suggest a richer internal representation than standard RNNs.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture is a fresh take on recurrent networks, and the idea of automatically inducing a temporal hierarchy through stacking is innovative.
*   **Competitive Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results (for recurrent networks) with SGD is a strong achievement, signaling the potential effectiveness of the architecture.
*   **Initial Analysis:** The authors go beyond simply presenting results, attempting to understand *how* the network operates through layer contribution analysis and investigation of parenthesis handling. This demonstrates a commendable effort towards interpretability.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of standard RNNs regarding hierarchical processing and multi-timescale signals, providing a solid rationale for the proposed architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While character-level language modeling is a good initial testbed, the results are primarily confined to this task. Further validation on other tasks exhibiting temporal hierarchies (e.g., speech recognition, video processing) would strengthen the claims.
*   **Modest Ablation Study:** The analysis of layer contributions is a good starting point, but more comprehensive ablation studies (e.g., varying network depth, neuron count, different output connection strategies) are needed to fully understand the architecture's sensitivity and optimal configuration.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the authors intuitively explain the emergence of timescales, a more formal theoretical treatment (e.g., analyzing the eigenvalue spectrum of the recurrent weight matrices) would bolster the argument.
*   **Training Details:** The explanation of the layer-wise training strategy for DRNN-1O is somewhat convoluted. A clearer description, and a comparison to other layer-wise training methods would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The DRNN architecture is a genuinely novel contribution with the potential to advance the field of recurrent neural networks.
*   The authors demonstrate competitive performance on a challenging task using a relatively simple optimization method.
*   The initial analysis provides intriguing insights into the network’s internal workings, hinting at a richer representational capacity.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly motivated, making the ideas accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation is limited, and the results may not generalize to other tasks.
*   The theoretical justification for the emergence of timescales is currently intuitive rather than rigorous.
*   The ablation studies are relatively modest, leaving open questions about the optimal configuration of the architecture.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a promising paper presenting a novel and potentially significant contribution. While the methodology isn’t perfect and further validation is needed, the originality and initial results are compelling enough to warrant acceptance. The authors have identified a worthwhile research direction, and the paper provides a strong foundation for future work. I would lean towards **Accept** despite the existing weaknesses. The novelty and potential impact outweigh the methodological limitations in this case. I encourage the authors to expand the experimental evaluation and pursue a more rigorous theoretical analysis in future work.
