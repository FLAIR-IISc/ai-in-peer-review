This paper proposes and analyzes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture constructed by stacking multiple RNN layers, where the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it. The authors hypothesize that this deep structure allows the network to develop a temporal hierarchy, with lower layers processing information at shorter time scales and higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies. This work builds upon prior research into hierarchical RNNs [5, 6] and the use of stacked recurrent architectures for speech recognition [8]. However, where previous work focused primarily on achieving state-of-the-art performance, this paper provides a detailed analysis of the internal dynamics and learned properties of the DRNN.

The authors evaluate the DRNN on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus, comparing it to a standard "shallow" RNN and previous state-of-the-art results from Sutskever et al. [19], who used a multiplicative RNN (MRNN) trained with the more complex Hessian-free optimization algorithm. Two DRNN variants are explored: one with output connections only from the top layer (DRNN-1O) and one where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The paper demonstrates that both DRNN variants, trained with simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD), achieve performance comparable to the Hessian-free trained MRNN. The core of the paper is a series of well-designed experiments aimed at verifying the temporal hierarchy hypothesis and understanding the role of each layer.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Thorough Analysis:** The primary strength of this work is its in-depth analysis, which goes beyond simply reporting performance metrics. The experiments designed to probe the model's internal state are insightful. The "typo perturbation" test (Figure 3, left) provides compelling quantitative evidence that higher layers in the trained DRNNs maintain memory of past events for longer periods than lower layers, directly supporting the central hypothesis of a learned temporal hierarchy.

2.  **Significant Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates that a relatively straightforward architecture (stacked RNNs) trained with a standard first-order method (SGD with gradient normalization) can achieve state-of-the-art performance on a challenging benchmark. This result is significant because it suggests that architectural depth, rather than sophisticated second-order optimization, may be a key factor for success on this task, simplifying the requirements for training such powerful sequence models.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The motivation for DRNNs is well-argued, the model is described precisely, and the experimental setup is detailed. The analysis is presented logically, starting with performance, moving to layer contributions and generative examples, and culminating in quantitative tests of the model's temporal properties and long-term memory.

4.  **Novelty in Analysis and Training:** While stacked RNNs were not entirely new at the time of this work [8], the detailed analysis of their emergent properties is novel and valuable. Furthermore, the incremental layer-wise training procedure used for the DRNN-1O model is an interesting contribution. This method, where layers are added sequentially and the full network is trained at each stage, proves effective and hints at the benefits of a curriculum-based or pre-training approach for deep recurrent models.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Limited Exploration of Hyperparameters:** The experiments are conducted on a small number of network configurations (one shallow RNN and two 5-layer DRNNs), with the total parameter count held constant. The choice of 5 layers is justified by referring to prior experiments whose results are not shown. A more systematic investigation of how network depth affects the learned time scales and overall performance would have strengthened the paper's conclusions.

2.  **Training Procedure for DRNN-1O:** The success of the DRNN-1O model relies heavily on a specific layer-wise training schedule. While effective, the schedule itself seems somewhat ad-hoc (e.g., the linearly increasing number of updates per stage). A more detailed justification or ablation study on the specifics of this training regime would have been beneficial for reproducibility and for understanding its importance relative to the architecture itself.

3.  **Lack of Comparison to Gated Architectures:** The paper focuses on simple tanh RNN units. A comparison with stacked LSTMs [11], which were specifically designed to handle long-term dependencies, would have been highly relevant. The authors dismiss testing on classic long-dependency tasks, arguing they don't require hierarchical processing, but a direct comparison on the main language modeling task would have provided a more complete picture of the benefits of deep recurrence versus specialized recurrent units.

**Summary of Arguments:**

**Pro-acceptance:**
*   Presents a strong and well-supported central thesis: that stacking RNNs leads to a learned temporal hierarchy.
*   Achieves state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark using a simple architecture and a standard optimizer, which is a significant practical and scientific contribution.
*   The analysis is thorough, insightful, and provides a valuable understanding of the model's internal workings, setting a good example for model-centric analysis papers.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.

**Con-acceptance:**
*   The experimental scope is somewhat limited, focusing on a single depth (5 layers) and not providing ablations on the specific layer-wise training scheme.
*   The absence of a direct comparison to stacked LSTMs is a notable omission, given the prominence of LSTMs for tasks requiring long-term memory.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution. The weaknesses identified are primarily related to the scope of the investigation and do not undermine the core findings. The paper successfully introduces the DRNN, demonstrates its effectiveness, and, most importantly, provides a clear and convincing analysis of its learned properties. The insights regarding the emergence of a temporal hierarchy and the success of simple SGD for training deep recurrent models are valuable to the community. This work is technically sound, clearly presented, and its findings are both original and significant.
