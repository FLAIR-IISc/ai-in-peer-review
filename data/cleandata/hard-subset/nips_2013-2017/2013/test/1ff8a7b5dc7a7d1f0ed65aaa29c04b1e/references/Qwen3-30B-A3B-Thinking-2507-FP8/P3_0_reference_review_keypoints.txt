- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked RNN layers intended to create a temporal hierarchy across layers.
- Claims DRNNs learn different time scales per layer and can model long-term dependencies in character-level language modeling.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over standard RNNs and reports state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia corpus.
- Provides multiple experimental analyses (typo perturbation, context switching, parentheses closing) supporting temporal hierarchy and memory claims.
- Paper is technically sound and well-executed with clear figures and accessible presentation.
- Major omission: no theoretical justification for the claim that DRNNs “embed” different time scales — evidence shows learned hierarchies, not embedded ones.
- Critical concern: the layer-wise incremental DRNN-1O training procedure may be responsible for gains, but its role isn’t properly analyzed or isolated from the architecture.
- Comparison to Hessian-free training is misleading: different datasets, differing character sets, and substantially more parameter updates for DRNN-1O.
- Fails to specify the type of recurrent units used (LSTM/GRU/simple RNN), a crucial omission for reproducibility and performance interpretation.
- Clarity issues: DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO distinction and the DRNN-1O training procedure are insufficiently explained.
- Reproducibility limited by missing architectural details (layer counts, neuron numbers, activation functions) and full hyperparameters.
- Originality is limited: stacked/hierarchical RNN ideas exist (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio 1995, Graves et al. 2013); novelty is mainly experimental evidence rather than a new architecture.
- Literature review is incomplete and should better acknowledge related hierarchical RNN work (e.g., Fernández 2007, Sutskever 2011).
- Significance is modest: performance gains are small (e.g., BPC improvements), and comparisons to modern state-of-the-art (LSTMs with attention, Transformers) are missing.
- Application scope is narrow — paper demonstrates character-level language modeling but not other temporal-hierarchy tasks like speech or video.
- Strengths: robust experiments, multiple evidence lines for temporal hierarchy, demonstration of long-term dependency modeling, and clear layer-output analysis.
- Weaknesses: lack of theory, misleading comparisons, missing unit specification, complex and under-explained training procedure, overstated claims about embedding time scales, insufficient SOTA comparisons.
- Recommendation: weak reject — incremental contribution, overstated claims, insufficient novelty and significance for NIPS acceptance.
- Revision suggestions: clarify learning vs embedding of time scales, specify recurrent unit and full architecture, provide fair comparisons (same dataset/training budget), compare to modern SOTA, and fully detail the DRNN-1O training protocol and rationale.
