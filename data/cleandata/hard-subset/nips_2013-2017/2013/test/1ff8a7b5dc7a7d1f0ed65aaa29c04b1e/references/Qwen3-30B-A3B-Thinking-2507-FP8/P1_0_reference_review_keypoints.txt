- Paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), stacking recurrent layers to create a hierarchy of time scales and compares DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO on character-level language modeling.
- Claims DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on Wikipedia character prediction using only SGD.

- Strength: Clear mathematical formulation and technically sound architecture (Section 2.1).
- Strength: Thorough experimental design with multiple ablation studies and temporal analyses.
- Strength: Results supported by experiments on a standard character-level language modeling task.
- Strength: Meaningful analysis of layer contributions and emergent time scales (Section 3.3).
- Strength: Careful comparison with relevant prior work, including Hessian-free approaches.

- Weakness: Insufficient explanation of why DRNNs are especially well-suited for language modeling beyond theoretical temporal-hierarchy arguments.
- Weakness: Layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is not well-justified or empirically compared to end-to-end training.
- Weakness: No thorough analysis explaining why DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O despite more outputs.
- Weakness: Claim that DRNNs “embed different time scales in their structure” lacks stronger causal empirical support.
- Weakness: Inadequate discussion of relation to hierarchical sequence models like Transformer-based attention.

- Strength: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes helpful schematic and equations.
- Strength: Experimental setup described sufficiently for reproducibility.
- Strength: Typo-perturbation experiment and temporal-hierarchy analysis are clearly presented.

- Weakness: Details of the training process (e.g., “incremental layer-wise method”) are ambiguous.
- Weakness: No rationale for using equal neuron counts per layer rather than varying layer sizes.
- Weakness: Vague description of generative sampling procedure (e.g., temperature not specified).
- Weakness: Connection between theoretical motivation and empirical evidence is indirect.

- Strength: DRNN architecture provides a novel perspective combining deep and recurrent processing to yield temporal hierarchies.
- Strength: Original analysis of time scales across layers yields useful insight beyond simple stacking of RNNs.
- Strength: Offers a fresh way to think about hierarchical processing in recurrent networks.

- Weakness: Novelty limited because stacked RNNs/LSTMs exist; main contribution is analysis more than architecture.
- Weakness: Insufficient differentiation from hierarchical attention mechanisms in Transformers.
- Weakness: Prior work that constructs RNN hierarchies via subsampling is not clearly distinguished from this approach.

- Strength: Demonstrates state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on character modeling using only SGD.
- Strength: Temporal-hierarchy analysis could inform future temporal modeling work.
- Strength: Parentheses-closing task results show DRNNs can handle long-term dependencies.

- Weakness: Modest performance gains over standard RNNs (BPC improvement ~0.05) limit practical impact.
- Weakness: No evidence DRNNs outperform modern state-of-the-art models (e.g., Transformers) on comparable tasks.
- Weakness: Scalability to larger datasets or more complex tasks is not addressed.
- Weakness: Focus limited to character-level modeling, which is less common in modern practice.

- Specific comment 1: Add detailed comparison with hierarchical attention/Transformer methods and clarify differences.
- Specific comment 2: Provide stronger, more direct evidence that DRNNs embed stable, architecture-driven time scales versus training artifacts.
- Specific comment 3: Analyze why DRNN-AO performs worse than DRNN-1O to strengthen architectural claims.
- Specific comment 4: Discuss long training time (10 days on one GPU) and whether optimization/architecture changes could reduce it.
- Specific comment 5: Include computational complexity comparison versus standard RNNs/LSTMs to support scalability claims.

- Recommendation: Weak acceptance — technically sound and insightful but modest novelty and limited practical impact.

- For acceptance: Offers novel perspective on temporal hierarchies, improves RNN performance using only SGD, includes thorough temporal analyses, and is well-written.
- Against acceptance: Limited novelty relative to recent hierarchical methods, modest empirical gains, unclear scalability, and some central claims lack strong empirical support.
