

# Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

## Summary
This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), which combine deep neural networks with recurrent architectures to create a temporal hierarchy where each layer operates at different time scales. The authors test DRNNs on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus, comparing variants with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) versus all layers (DRNN-AO). They demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this task and provide empirical evidence of how different layers develop distinct time scales through analysis of layer contributions, typo perturbations, and long-term dependency tasks like closing parentheses.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments that clearly demonstrate the DRNN's advantages. The authors provide thorough analysis of time scales using multiple complementary methods: disabling individual layer outputs, measuring decay of perturbation effects, and testing long-term dependency tasks. The training methodology is carefully described, including the gradient normalization trick to prevent explosions.

However, the theoretical justification for why DRNNs work better than standard RNNs is limited. While the empirical evidence of temporal hierarchy is strong, the paper lacks a clear theoretical explanation for why this hierarchy emerges during training or how it specifically benefits language modeling. The connection to prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3,5,6]) is underdeveloped; the authors mention [5] but don't clearly explain how their approach differs in terms of architecture or training.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized with a logical flow from motivation to experiments. The architecture is clearly described with equations and a helpful schematic figure. The experimental setup is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility, though some training parameters could be more explicit (e.g., batch size for BPTT). The writing is generally clear, though the motivation section could be more concise.

Figures 1-4 effectively communicate key findings about the architecture and time scales. The results presentation is logical, with performance metrics followed by meaningful analysis of layer contributions and time scales. The generative text examples (Table 2) provide intuitive validation of the model's capabilities.

## Originality
The DRNN architecture itself is a novel combination of deep and recurrent networks, though it builds on prior work using stacked LSTMs [8]. The key original contribution is the detailed analysis of how the architecture creates a temporal hierarchy with different layers operating at different time scales. This insight is valuable and not present in prior work.

The paper could better distinguish itself from other hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6] by explicitly comparing their architecture and training methodology. While the authors mention [5] uses subsampling for different time scales, they don't clarify how their approach differs in terms of how time scales emerge naturally through training rather than being imposed.

## Significance
The paper demonstrates that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (1.48 BPC vs. 1.58 BPC for standard RNNs), which is a meaningful contribution to the field. The analysis of time scales provides important insights into how deep recurrent architectures process information across different temporal resolutions.

The ability to handle long-term dependencies (e.g., closing parentheses after long phrases) is particularly compelling evidence of the DRNN's capabilities. However, the performance gains are modest, and the training time is very long (10 days per network), which limits practical applicability. The comparison to Hessian-free training [19] is somewhat limited; the authors acknowledge their method is slower but doesn't significantly outperform it.

## Recommendation
This is a solid contribution to understanding hierarchical recurrent architectures with strong empirical evidence. While the theoretical justification is limited, the paper makes a valuable empirical contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen the theoretical discussion and clarify the relationship to prior work.

**Strengths:**
- Clear demonstration of temporal hierarchy through multiple complementary analyses
- State-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling
- Detailed analysis of layer contributions and time scales
- Compelling evidence of long-term dependency handling

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited theoretical justification for why DRNNs work better
- Modest performance gains over standard RNNs
- Long training times (10 days per network)
- Insufficient distinction from prior hierarchical RNN work

## Specific Suggestions
1. Expand the theoretical discussion to explain why the temporal hierarchy emerges during training.
2. More explicitly compare DRNNs to other hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6] to clarify novelty.
3. Discuss the trade-offs between DRNNs and other methods more thoroughly, including computational cost.
4. Consider adding a brief analysis of how DRNN performance scales with network size.

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding deep recurrent architectures, and with these minor revisions, it would be a strong candidate for NIPS. The empirical analysis of time scales is particularly valuable for future work on neural network design.
