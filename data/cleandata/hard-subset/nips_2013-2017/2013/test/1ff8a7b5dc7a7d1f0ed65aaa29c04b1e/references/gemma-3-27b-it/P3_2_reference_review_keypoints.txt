- Overall recommendation: recommend accept with minor revisions; solid investigation combining DNN and RNN strengths.  
- Main contribution: introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that create hierarchical time scales by stacking RNN layers.  
- Performance: achieves state-of-the-art for recurrent networks on large-scale character-level language modeling using plain SGD.  
- Analysis: provides initial empirical evidence for hierarchical time-scale behavior and layer-specific roles (perturbation/context-switch experiments, parenthesis handling).  
- Presentation and reproducibility: clear motivation, concise writing, thorough experimental settings, and connections to prior work.  
- Novelty: core performance does not surpass absolute SOTA; main novelty is architectural analysis rather than a dramatic performance leap.  
- Analysis depth: time-scale investigation is limited and would benefit from more systematic exploration (e.g., effect of depth).  
- Ablation studies: lacks targeted ablations (e.g., removing recurrent connections) to isolate per-layer contributions.  
- Computational cost: training with SGD is hard to parallelize; discussion of alternative optimization/parallelization strategies is missing.  
- Figure clarity: Figure 3 y-axis labels are ambiguous and need clearer units/explanations.  
- Suggested improvements: expand time-scale analysis (depth, layer size, initialization, weight visualization).  
- Suggested improvements: conduct targeted ablation studies to isolate recurrence vs. stacking effects.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss strategies to improve training efficiency (e.g., asynchronous SGD).  
- Conclusion: well-written, insightful paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; accept after minor revisions addressing above points.
