

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper proposes a "Deep Recurrent Neural Network" (DRNN) architecture that combines deep neural networks with recurrent networks by stacking RNNs where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors claim this architecture creates a temporal hierarchy with different time scales at different layers, which they demonstrate through experiments on character-level language modeling. The paper builds on prior work on hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6,8] but claims their architecture is distinct because it "embeds different timescales directly in its structure."

The paper presents two DRNN variants: DRNN-1O (output connections only at top layer) and DRNN-AO (output connections at all layers). On the Wikipedia character prediction task, the authors report DRNN-1O achieves 1.45 BPC, outperforming a standard RNN (1.67 BPC) and approaching the state-of-the-art achieved with Hessian-free optimization [19]. They provide limited evidence for their claim of temporal hierarchy through typo-perturbation experiments and parentheses-closing tests.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with good mathematical formalization of the DRNN architecture.
- The experimental setup is reasonable, and the authors provide a detailed description of their training procedure.
- The exploration of how different layers contribute to the final output (through layer removal experiments) is a useful analysis.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Lack of rigorous comparison:** The paper only compares to a single prior method (standard RNN) and mentions Hessian-free training [19] as a benchmark without providing a fair comparison. The claim of "state-of-the-art" performance is misleading, as they don't compare against established architectures like LSTMs or GRUs, which have consistently outperformed standard RNNs on language modeling tasks. The marginal performance gain (0.22 BPC) over standard RNNs is not impressive given the complexity of the proposed architecture.

- **Insufficient evidence for novelty:** The core claim that DRNNs create a "temporal hierarchy" is not convincingly demonstrated. The authors show that different layers have different time scales, but this is exactly what one would expect from a hierarchical architecture. The paper fails to establish how this is fundamentally different from existing hierarchical RNN approaches [3,5,6,8], which also create multiple time scales through layer stacking. The paper doesn't provide theoretical justification for why this hierarchy should be beneficial for language modeling.

- **Methodological concerns:** The training procedure for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training with output connections only at the top layer) is unnecessarily complex and not well-justified. The authors claim this is "necessary to reach good performance," but provide no analysis of why standard training would fail. The paper doesn't address whether this training scheme would generalize to other tasks.

- **Limited experimental validation:** The authors only test on a single dataset (Wikipedia) with three network instantiations. Given the computational expense (10 days per network), they could have conducted more thorough experiments to validate their claims. The paper doesn't provide ablation studies to isolate the contribution of the temporal hierarchy versus other architectural features.

- **Overstated significance:** The paper claims DRNNs "embed these different timescales directly in their structure," but this is not a novel concept. The paper's contribution appears to be a minor variation of existing hierarchical RNN approaches, not a substantial advance. The claim that DRNNs "are able to model long-term dependencies" is not convincingly supported, as the paper doesn't compare against architectures specifically designed for long-term dependencies (like LSTMs).

**Recommendation:**
This paper presents an incremental variation of existing hierarchical RNN architectures without sufficient evidence of its superiority over established methods. The experimental validation is limited, the comparison to prior work is inadequate, and the claims about the temporal hierarchy are not convincingly demonstrated. The marginal performance gains over standard RNNs (which are already outperformed by LSTMs/GRUs) do not justify the complexity of the proposed architecture.

The paper would benefit from:
1. Comparing against standard LSTM/GRU architectures
2. Providing a clearer theoretical justification for why the temporal hierarchy is beneficial
3. Conducting more thorough experiments with multiple datasets
4. Better contextualizing the novelty compared to existing hierarchical RNN work

The paper's contribution appears to be incremental at best, and the evidence provided does not support the claims of significant advancement in the field. While the idea of stacking RNNs is interesting, the paper fails to rigorously demonstrate that this specific architecture provides meaningful advantages over simpler, well-established alternatives.

**Summary of strengths and weaknesses:**
*Strengths:* Clear writing, good formalization of the architecture, useful layer contribution analysis.
*Weaknesses:* Insufficient comparison to established methods, limited experimental validation, overstated novelty, inadequate justification for key design choices.

This paper does not meet NIPS standards for significance and quality. The contribution is not sufficiently novel, and the evidence provided does not convincingly support the claims. The paper should be rejected.
