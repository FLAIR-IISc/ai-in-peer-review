This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, presenting a hybrid architecture combining DNNs and RNNs to capture temporal hierarchies. While the idea of stacking RNNs to create multi-scale temporal processing is intuitive and the authors demonstrate competitive performance, the paper suffers from a plethora of minor, yet persistent, issues that ultimately detract from its overall quality.

The introduction adequately motivates the work, establishing the context of DNNs and RNNs and highlighting the potential benefits of combining their strengths. However, it's overly verbose and lacks conciseness. The discussion on hierarchical processing in RNNs feels unnecessarily drawn out and could be significantly trimmed. The comparison to existing hierarchical RNNs ([3, 5, 6]) is superficial. The statement that [5] limits itself to artificial tasks feels dismissive without further elaboration on *why* this limits the work’s relevance.

Section 2, detailing the DRNN architecture, is mathematically sound but lacks rigor in notation. The inconsistent use of overbars (e.g., x̄) is distracting, and the lack of explicit bias terms, while acknowledged, should be consistently reflected in the equations. The motivation for different timescales arising from stacking RNNs is reasonable, but could be strengthened with a more formal analysis. The two output configurations (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) are clearly explained. The rationale for adding output connections at each layer – addressing pathological curvature and providing a measure of layer contribution – is plausible, but feels somewhat ad-hoc and lacks strong justification.

The experimental setup (Section 3) is described with sufficient detail, but again suffers from minor inconsistencies and omissions. The choice of 95 characters plus an "unknown" character is reasonable, but the justification for this specific number is missing. The statement that training a single network requires 10 days, due to difficulties in parallelizing SGD, raises concerns. While acknowledged, this significantly limits the scope of experimentation to only three network instantiations. The initialization of weight matrices is clearly specified. The BPTT truncation length (50 characters) is appropriate, but the rationale for its selection isn’t provided. The statement regarding gradient normalization as a “trick” feels unprofessional and underplays its potential importance. The incremental layer-wise training strategy for DRNN-1O is described but feels unnecessarily complex and potentially suboptimal. Why not simply train all layers simultaneously with a lower learning rate?

The results section presents BPC scores and compares them to existing literature. The performance is competitive, but the comparison isn't entirely fair given the different datasets and experimental setups. The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) is interesting, but the interpretation remains largely descriptive. The text generation examples (Table 2) are illustrative, but lack quantitative evaluation. The analysis of typo perturbation (Figure 3) is insightful, but the interpretation of the observed time scales is somewhat speculative. The concluding observations about parenthesis handling are intriguing, but feel disconnected from the main thrust of the paper. The extended discussion of parenthesis handling and the details about nested parentheses and abbreviations feels excessively granular and detracts from the overall narrative.

The conclusions section summarizes the key findings but lacks a clear articulation of the limitations of the work. The suggested future directions are reasonable but generic. The acknowledgements are standard.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of DNNs and RNNs.
*   The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   The analysis of temporal hierarchies and layer contributions is insightful.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The paper is riddled with minor flaws in notation, presentation, and justification.
*   The experimental setup is limited by the computational cost of training.
*   The analysis is largely descriptive and lacks rigorous statistical validation.
*   The overall presentation is verbose and lacks conciseness.
*   The significance of the improvements over existing methods is not convincingly demonstrated.
*   The level of detail in certain sections (e.g., parenthesis handling) is disproportionate to its relevance.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the novel idea and competitive performance, the numerous minor flaws and lack of rigor make this paper unsuitable for NIPS. The paper feels incomplete and rushed, and the presentation detracts from the quality of the work. While the idea has potential, it requires further refinement and more thorough investigation. I recommend **rejection**. A significant revision addressing the points raised above would be necessary for reconsideration. The paper needs to be more concise, more mathematically rigorous, and more carefully evaluated.
