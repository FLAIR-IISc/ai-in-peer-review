- Overall: Solid contribution demonstrating competitive DRNN performance and initial analysis of learned temporal hierarchies; merits acceptance despite limited experiments.  
- Paper focus: Proposes and evaluates a multi-layered RNN (DRNN) for character-level language modeling, emphasizing hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Motivation: Aims to combine hierarchical processing of deep nets with RNN temporal modeling to capture multiple time scales in sequences.  
- Empirical result: DRNN achieves near state-of-the-art character-level modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus using only SGD.  
- Analysis: Authors probe temporal hierarchies via typo perturbation, context-switch, and parenthesis-closing experiments to show layer-wise time-scale specialization.  
- Novelty: Stacking RNNs is known, but the paper’s focused analysis of emergent temporal hierarchies is a valuable contribution.  
- Clarity: Well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically concise.  
- Related work: Provides a thorough and appropriate discussion of related literature.  
- Strength — optimization: Demonstrates strong results without complex optimizers (e.g., Hessian-free), relying on SGD.  
- Strength — insights: Visualizations and qualitative findings show top layers capture long-range dependencies while bottom layers handle local statistics.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Evaluation is limited (only three network configurations), hindering robustness claims.  
- Weakness — theory: Lacks a formal theoretical analysis linking layer depth to characteristic time scales.  
- Weakness — baselines: Needs broader comparisons to other RNN variants (e.g., GRUs, various LSTM setups).  
- Weakness — reproducibility: Training details are incomplete and code is not released; parallelization issues with SGD are not addressed.  
- For acceptance: Strong empirical performance with simple training, insightful analysis, clear writing, and importance to sequential modeling.  
- Against acceptance: Limited experiments, absence of rigorous theory, and insufficient baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Suggestions: Expand hyperparameter and architecture searches, add theoretical investigation of depth vs. time scale, compare to GRUs/LSTMs, release code, and discuss parallelization strategies.
