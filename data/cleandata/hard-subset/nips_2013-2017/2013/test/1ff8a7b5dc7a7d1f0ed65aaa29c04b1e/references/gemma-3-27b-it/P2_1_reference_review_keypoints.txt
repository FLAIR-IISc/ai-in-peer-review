- Summary: Paper studies Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), shows they model multiple timescales and achieve strong character-level language modeling results with SGD while analyzing learned timescales and layer contributions.

- Novelty & Originality: Provides a thorough analysis of inherent hierarchical timescales in stacked RNNs and motivates combining DNN and RNN concepts.

- Strong Empirical Results: Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling comparable to MRNNs/Hessian-free methods using only SGD.

- In-depth Analysis: Offers valuable analysis of layer contributions, learned timescales, and a qualitative parenthesis experiment demonstrating long-term dependency modeling.

- Clear Motivation: Introduction clearly contrasts DRNNs with traditional DNNs/RNNs and motivates hierarchical temporal modeling.

- Well-written and Organized: Paper is clearly written, logically structured, and figures are helpful.

- Limited Scope of Experiments: Heavy reliance on a single task (character-level language modeling) limits claims of general applicability.

- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Learning rate tuning, especially differing top-layer rates, appears ad-hoc and needs more systematic exploration.

- Lack of Comparison to LSTM/GRU: No direct baseline against dominant sequence models (LSTM/GRU), weakening comparative claims.

- Scalability Concerns: Long training time (10 days) and SGD parallelization issues raise concerns about scaling to larger models/datasets.

- Interpretability Limitations: Interpretation of learned timescales is somewhat speculative and may be confounded by interdependent layers.

- Quality: Technically sound with a well-defined experimental setup and clear presentation of results.

- Clarity: Notation and exposition are clear, making the paper easy to follow.

- Originality (reiterated): Stacking RNNs is not entirely novel, but the systematic timescale analysis and SGD success add originality.

- Significance: Results and insights into learned timescales are significant for designing and understanding recurrent models.

- Arguments For Acceptance: Strong empirical results, novel timescale analysis, well-motivated architecture, and contribution to training deep recurrent networks.

- Arguments Against Acceptance: Limited experiments, missing LSTM/GRU comparison, and scalability issues.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, complete work with compelling results and insightful analysis; weaknesses do not outweigh strengths.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, particularly to address comparison to LSTMs/GRUs.
