

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper proposes "Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" (DRNNs) as a novel architecture combining hierarchical processing of DNNs with temporal dynamics of RNNs. The authors claim that stacking RNNs creates a natural temporal hierarchy, where lower layers process short-term dependencies and higher layers handle longer-term dependencies, which they validate through character-level language modeling on Wikipedia text.

The paper's main contributions are: (1) proposing DRNNs as a hierarchical RNN architecture, (2) demonstrating their performance on character-level language modeling, and (3) providing evidence for temporal hierarchies within the network layers. The authors claim state-of-the-art results using only SGD, which they contrast with previous work requiring Hessian-free optimization.

**Quality:** The paper's technical execution is mostly sound, with appropriate use of standard techniques like BPTT and gradient normalization. However, the experimental validation is severely limited. The authors only test three network instantiations on a single dataset, with no ablation studies beyond removing layer outputs. The comparison to previous work is insufficient, as they compare to a single prior result (Hessian-free optimization) without testing against more standard approaches like LSTMs or GRUs, which have become the de facto standard for language modeling. The claim that "DRNNs embed different time scales directly in their structure" is based on a single perturbation test (typo introduction) and lacks rigorous statistical validation. The paper also fails to address the computational cost of DRNNs compared to standard RNNs, which is particularly concerning given the training time (10 days per network) and hardware constraints (single GPU).

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and well-organized. The figures (particularly Figure 1) effectively illustrate the proposed architecture. However, the paper lacks crucial implementation details necessary for reproducibility (e.g., exact hyperparameters for all experiments, specific training schedules, and detailed network configurations beyond "approximately 4.9 million parameters"). The explanation of the incremental layer-wise training for DRNN-1O is unclear and differs from standard layer-wise pretraining methods.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking RNNs is not novel. The paper acknowledges previous hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6,8], but the proposed DRNN appears to be a minor variation of existing approaches. The paper does not clearly differentiate its architecture from stacked LSTMs (which have been used in [8] for state-of-the-art performance on TIMIT). The paper's claim of "temporal hierarchy" is also not convincingly demonstrated as a novel property of DRNNs, as standard RNNs with sufficient capacity could potentially learn similar hierarchical representations.

**Significance:** The paper's claims of significance are overstated. The performance gains over standard RNNs are marginal (0.03 BPC improvement for DRNN-1O vs. RNN), and the results are not significantly better than prior work using standard RNNs with Hessian-free optimization. The paper does not demonstrate that DRNNs solve a problem that cannot be addressed by established methods. The text generation examples (Table 2) provide little insight into the claimed temporal hierarchy, as the qualitative differences are minimal and could be attributed to general network capacity rather than the proposed architectural innovation.

**Critical Weaknesses:**
1. The paper fails to compare against standard RNN variants (LSTMs, GRUs) that have dominated language modeling for years.
2. The limited experimental scope (only 3 network instantiations) cannot support broad claims about DRNNs' superiority.
3. The paper does not provide sufficient evidence that the observed temporal hierarchy is an inherent property of DRNNs rather than a result of training dynamics.
4. The claim of "state-of-the-art performance" is misleading, as they're comparing to a single prior result using a much more sophisticated optimization method.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper provides a clear architecture diagram (Figure 1) that effectively communicates the proposed DRNN structure.
2. The analysis of layer contributions (Figure 2) offers some interesting insights into how different layers contribute to the task.
3. The parentheses-closing experiment (Figure 4) provides a concrete example of long-term dependency learning.

**Recommendation:** The paper presents a minor variation of existing RNN architectures with limited evidence to support its claims of novelty and superiority. While the experimental results are reasonable, they do not demonstrate a significant advance over established methods. The paper would benefit from substantially more rigorous evaluation, including comparisons to standard RNN variants (LSTMs, GRUs), more extensive ablation studies, and a clearer theoretical justification for why DRNNs would outperform standard approaches. Given the limited evidence and overstated claims, I recommend rejection. The paper would be a better fit for a workshop or conference with less stringent requirements for empirical validation.
