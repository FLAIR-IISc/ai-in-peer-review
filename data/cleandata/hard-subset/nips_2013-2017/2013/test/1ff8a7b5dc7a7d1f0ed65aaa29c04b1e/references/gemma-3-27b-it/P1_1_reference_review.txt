## NIPS Paper Review: Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

**Summary:**

This paper investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), constructed by stacking RNN layers, and analyzes their ability to capture temporal hierarchies in sequential data. The authors argue that DRNNs combine the benefits of both Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) and RNNs, creating inherent multi-timescale processing. They apply this architecture to character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance with Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD), comparable to results obtained with more complex Hessian-free optimization.  Furthermore, the paper provides an initial analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies within the DRNNs, revealing different time scales at different layers and demonstrating the network’s ability to handle long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses matching).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The authors correctly position their work within the context of both DNNs and RNNs, acknowledging the limitations of each in modeling complex temporal dependencies. They cite relevant work on hierarchical RNNs ([5]) and stacked bidirectional LSTMs ([8]), pointing out the lack of detailed analysis in the latter. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is well-placed, highlighting the significance of achieving competitive results with a simpler training method. The paper builds on the established foundation of LSTM networks [11] and addresses a need for architectures that explicitly incorporate multi-timescale processing.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Combination:** The DRNN architecture is a reasonable and intuitive extension of existing neural network structures, blending the strengths of DNNs and RNNs. The idea of inherently creating different time scales through stacking is compelling.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task with a relatively simple training method (SGD) is a significant accomplishment.  This demonstrates the practical effectiveness of the DRNN architecture.
* **In-Depth Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply reporting results and attempts to analyze *how* the DRNN achieves its performance.  The analyses of layer-wise time scales (using typo perturbation) and the handling of long-range dependencies (parentheses matching) are valuable contributions.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the DRNN architecture, training process, and experimental setup. The figures are helpful in visualizing the network structure and analysis results.
* **Insightful Observations:** The observation that the DRNN-1O (top layer output only) benefits from layer-wise training, where each layer is directly coupled to the output, is important and highlights the challenges of training very deep networks.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The paper focuses on a specific DRNN configuration (fully connected layers, tanh activation).  A more systematic exploration of different layer types (e.g., LSTMs, GRUs) within the DRNN framework would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Network Size & Scalability:** While the authors acknowledge the difficulty of scaling to larger networks with SGD, the limited number of network instantiations tested (three) raises concerns about the robustness of the results. The anecdotal evidence of a larger network (9.6M parameters) performing better suggests that performance is still scaling with size.
* **Analysis Depth:** While the analyses are insightful, they are somewhat limited. The typo perturbation experiment, while informative, could be expanded to include more complex perturbations and different types of temporal structures.  A more rigorous quantification of the time scales observed in each layer would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Comparison to Other Hierarchical Models:** The paper mentions [5] but does not provide a detailed comparison of the DRNN to other hierarchical RNN architectures, especially regarding their respective strengths and weaknesses.
* **Training Details & Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameters, such as learning rate schedule, batch size, and initialization schemes.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for sequence modeling.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant contribution to the field.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the learned temporal hierarchies within the DRNN.
* The work is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The architectural exploration is limited.
* The scalability of the approach is unclear.
* The analysis could be more comprehensive and rigorous.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks.  The DRNN architecture is a promising approach to capturing temporal hierarchies, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through compelling experimental results and insightful analysis. While some areas could be strengthened (architectural exploration, scalability analysis, analysis depth), the overall quality of the work is high.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept.** I believe this paper meets the criteria for acceptance at NIPS.  It is technically sound, clearly written, original, and significant.  The strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses, and it has the potential to inspire further research in this area.
