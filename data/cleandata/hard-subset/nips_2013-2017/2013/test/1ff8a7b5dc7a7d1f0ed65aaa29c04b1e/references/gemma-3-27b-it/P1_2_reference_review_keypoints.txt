- Investigates Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that combine DNN hierarchy with RNN temporal memory and claim emergent multiple timescales, validated on character-level language modeling with SGD.  
- Positions work relative to prior hierarchical RNN and MRNN Hessian-free training literature, arguing DRNN+SGD is competitive and more accessible.  

Strengths:
- Novel DRNN architecture that explicitly supports hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Strong empirical performance: state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using only SGD.  
- Insightful analysis showing different layers capture different timescales (typo perturbation, context switching, parentheses tests).  
- Clear exposition and helpful visualization of the network structure.  
- Thorough experiments including ablation studies and generative tests.  

Weaknesses:
- Only three network configurations tested, limiting conclusions about architecture choices.  
- No direct comparison to LSTMs with comparable parameter counts.  
- Training/hyperparameter details (e.g., learning rate schedules) are insufficient for full reproducibility.  
- Layer contribution interpretability is limited; interactions not deeply analyzed.  
- Limited discussion of computational cost and comparison with Hessian-free methods beyond reported training time.  

Qualitative evaluation:
- Technically sound with well-designed experiments and coherent claims.  
- Generally clear writing, though some methodological sections need more detail.  
- Original contribution in architecture and temporal-hierarchy analysis.  
- Significant: demonstrates DRNN effectiveness and SGD competitiveness.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Achieves SOTA results, proposes a well-motivated architecture, provides insightful temporal analysis, and offers a simpler training alternative.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Restricted architectural exploration, missing LSTM baseline, and incomplete training details.  

Overall assessment / recommendation:
- Strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend accept with minor revisions to expand training details and consider LSTM comparison.
