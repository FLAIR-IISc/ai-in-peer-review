- Proposes DRNN: stacked recurrent layers where each layer uses the previous layer’s hidden state to model temporal hierarchies.
- Achieves state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using simple SGD and analyzes emergent time scales.
- Novelty: DRNN offers an intuitive hierarchical temporal processing extension to standard RNNs.
- Performance: Strong empirical results on a challenging task with a comparatively simple training algorithm.
- Analysis: Thorough exploration of emergent time scales, typo perturbation experiments, text generation, and layer output ablations.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explains architecture, methodology, and results clearly.
- Honesty & Thoroughness: Authors acknowledge limitations and outline future research directions.
- Gradient normalization: Role and necessity of gradient normalization are underexplained and may affect SGD averaging behavior.
- Missing comparison: Should discuss and compare results to Mikolov et al.’s work on similar Wikipedia compression tasks.
- Layer abstraction: More evidence needed to show higher layers truly capture more abstract/long-term properties versus inheriting lower-layer processing.
- Parameter-fairness: Comparison to standard RNN may be unfair due to differing parameter counts; fair comparisons should control parameters/units.
- Ablation study: Needs more systematic ablation (varying depths and widths) and performance-vs-depth curves to strengthen claims.
- Terminology: DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO are not defined in the main text before usage.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a relevant problem, proposes a promising architecture, provides compelling experiments and insightful analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient justification for gradient normalization, potentially unfair baseline comparisons, and incomplete evidence for layer abstraction.
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing gradient normalization and fairer baseline comparisons.
