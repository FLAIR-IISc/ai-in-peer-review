- With a fixed parameter budget, deeper recurrent networks outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling.
- Deeper DRNN layers capture more long-term dependencies; analysis shows deeper layers exhibit longer time scales.
- Standard BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the perceived need for Hessian-free training.
- The DRNN architecture itself is not novel; prior work (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) on stacked/deep RNNs exists.
- The paper’s main contribution is empirical analysis of DRNN behavior rather than an architectural innovation.
- Strong empirical results on a challenging character-level modeling task support the claims.
- The time-scale analysis provides valuable insight into how different layers process temporal information.
- The surprising BPTT result suggests re-evaluating training strategies for RNNs.
- The manuscript is clearly written and the experimental setup is well described.
- Limited generalizability: experiments are restricted to character-level language modeling and may not transfer to other sequential tasks (e.g., speech).
- Missing comparison to truncated BPTT; a reasonable truncation length (e.g., 30 steps) could be a competitive, cheaper baseline.
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge prior stacked RNN work and avoid claiming architectural novelty.
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on clarifying novelty and addressing or committing to tests of truncated BPTT and broader validation.
- Relevant references to consider: Graves et al. (2013) and Mikolov (PhD thesis) on truncated BPTT.
