- Introduces a deep recurrent architecture combining hierarchical representation learning with temporal modeling (HMM/RNN-like).
- Training stacks layers incrementally using supervised pretraining on a next-character prediction objective.
- Authors train very large models for ~10 days and report improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset used in prior work.
- Provides insightful analysis of model behavior: higher layers encode longer-range dependencies.
- Shows opening/closing parentheses are handled via a “pseudo-stable attractor-like” state.
- Writing is clear, experimental protocol is sensible, and figures effectively illustrate claims.
- Principal reservation: empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset with few prior benchmarks.
- Comparisons are mostly to RNN-family baselines and omit classical hierarchical models (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).
- Table 1 lists other techniques but on different corpora, weakening comparability.
- Main contribution is the convincing analysis of model operation rather than decisive empirical gains.
- Overall conclusion: well-written and thoughtful analysis, but empirical claims are somewhat equivocal.
