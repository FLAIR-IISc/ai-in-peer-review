- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) with multiple recurrent layers and shows effectiveness on text prediction and compression tasks.
- Claims higher DRNN layers learn more abstract, longer-term structures; experiments aim to support a functional hierarchy.
- Paper is well-written, transparent about limitations, and provides insightful analysis of internal network functions.
- Major concern: comparisons may be unfair—DRNN often has more parameters than baseline RNNs, so gains may reflect capacity not depth.
- Recommend parameter-matched comparisons (e.g., shallow RNNs with equal parameter count or wider layers) to isolate benefits of depth.
- Request more details on gradient normalization: how it prevents "bifurcations," its criticality for training, and relation to gradient clipping/SGD behavior.
- Caveat on ablation (“brain surgery”) experiments: interdependencies between layers make interpretation of layer roles inconclusive.
- Suggest evaluating alternative architectures, such as adding non-linear layers between timesteps within a single recurrent layer.
- Suggest comparing Wikipedia compression results to prior state-of-the-art (e.g., Mikolov et al.) for context.
- Suggest visualizing final benchmark performance vs depth (1–5 layers) with parameter-matched models to show how performance scales with depth.
- Minor issue: terms DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in Figure 1 are undefined in the text and should be clarified.
- Conclusion: paper is promising and thought-provoking; addressing baseline and experimental concerns (especially parameter-matched comparisons) would substantially strengthen the claims.
