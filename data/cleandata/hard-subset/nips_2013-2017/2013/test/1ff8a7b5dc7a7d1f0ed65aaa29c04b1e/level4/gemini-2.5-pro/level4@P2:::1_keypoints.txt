- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers, with each layer receiving the hidden sequence from the layer below to capture temporal hierarchies (lower layers short-term, higher layers long-term).  
- Evaluates on character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia corpus, with two variants: DRNN-1O (only top layer outputs) and DRNN-AO (all layers output).  
- Uses simple SGD with gradient normalization to achieve state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks, matching results from Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides thorough internal analysis showing layers learn different time scales, perturbations fade at different rates across layers, and the model captures long-range dependencies (e.g., matching parentheses over hundreds of characters).  
- Describes a compelling case study of parenthesis-closing behavior characterized as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, technically sound, and supported by effective figures and precise experimental details.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single task/dataset, reducing confidence in the generality of DRNNs across domains like speech or financial time series.  
- Comparisons are confined mainly to other RNNs; lacks broader discussion and empirical comparison to alternative hierarchical models (e.g., HHMMs) and structured-prediction approaches.  
- Inclusion of compression algorithm results is not directly comparable because they use a different dataset.  
- Need clarification on why incremental, layer-wise training was "necessary" for DRNN-1O—whether due to optimization issues like vanishing/exploding gradients or other pathologies.  
- Pro-accept: well-motivated architecture, strong empirical results with simple training, and valuable analysis of emergent temporal hierarchy.  
- Con-accept: limited dataset/task scope and narrow model-family comparisons weaken generality claims.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths (performance + analysis) outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
