- Introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) combining deep hierarchical processing with RNN temporal modeling, evaluated on character-level language modeling.  
- Motivation and literature review are well articulated, connecting to hierarchical RNNs, stacked LSTMs, deep belief nets, and autoencoders.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, with effective figures explaining architecture and results.  
- Thorough analysis of trained DRNNs, including time-scale investigations and parenthesis-matching behavior revealing pseudo-stable attractor-like states.  
- Achieves competitive, state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on a large Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD.  
- Paper feels complete: novel architecture, experiments, analysis, and discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset and a narrow set of recurrent baselines, reducing generality and comparative strength.  
- High computational cost (10 days on a GPU) hinders reproducibility and broader adoption.  
- Incremental layer-wise training (DRNN-1O) appears ad-hoc and lacks strong theoretical justification beyond mitigating vanishing gradients.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated architecture; insightful behavioral analysis; competitive results; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited dataset/baseline scope, high training cost, and weak theoretical grounding for layer-wise training.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions and insightful analysis, but experimental scope should be expanded in future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with encouragement to broaden evaluations and explore more principled training strategies.
