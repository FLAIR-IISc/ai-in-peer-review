- Proposes a novel hierarchical architecture combining deep nets and recurrent nets (stacked recurrent layers receiving previous hidden states).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent models on a character-level Wikipedia language modeling task.  
- Architecture captures temporal hierarchies; different layers operate on different time scales.  
- Well-motivated explanation of limitations of traditional RNNs.  
- Experiments are thorough, convincing, and results well-presented.  
- Paper is clear, readable, and intellectually honest about limitations.  
- Provides analysis of layer roles and how the network represents/processes data, but analysis is limited.  
- Comparison baseline is unfair: traditional RNN has fewer parameters, so parameter-matched baselines are needed.  
- Needs deeper analysis of higher layers to show they process more abstract/long-term properties in a non-trivial way.  
- Technically sound and well-explained methodology.  
- Original and significant contribution with potential impact on time-series and NLP modeling.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, strong empirical results, clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: unfair comparisons to baselines and insufficiently thorough layer-role analysis.
