- Explores DRNNs for text prediction/compression with emphasis on hierarchical layers interacting with temporal dynamics.  
- Core insight: older data benefits from deeper processing due to accumulated temporal context, aligning with hierarchical sequential representations.  
- DRNN architecture with inter-layer recurrent connections shows empirical advantages over standard RNNs, particularly for short-term dependencies.  
- Text generation results (Table 2) support functional specialization of higher layers, though interventions are hard to interpret due to layer interdependencies.  
- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and transparent about limitations.  
- Major concern: parameter-count comparison is unfair—DRNNs use more recurrent (and inter-layer) parameters, so gains may reflect capacity, not architecture.  
- Recommended fix: compare DRNNs to standard RNNs with matched total parameters or equivalent units/width.  
- Role and justification of gradient normalization (DRNN-AO) are unclear; the term “bifurcations” needs definition and connection to prior work (e.g., Bengio et al. ICML 2013).  
- Test whether gradient normalization alone (without DRNN structure) yields similar gains to isolate its effect from architecture.  
- Claim that higher layers encode abstract/long-term properties lacks causal evidence and may be merely correlative.  
- Suggested causal test: reposition high-layer units to lower layers (surgery/relocation) and measure impact on long-term tasks.  
- Minor fixes: define DRNN-AO/DRNN-IO in main text, compare to Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia compression, and show depth-scaling (1–5 layers) with width-adjusted baselines.  
- Strength: paper’s focus on mechanistic interpretation of deep temporal models is valuable and underexplored.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending revisions to address parameter fairness and causal evidence for abstraction; current proof of depth’s functional necessity is incomplete.
