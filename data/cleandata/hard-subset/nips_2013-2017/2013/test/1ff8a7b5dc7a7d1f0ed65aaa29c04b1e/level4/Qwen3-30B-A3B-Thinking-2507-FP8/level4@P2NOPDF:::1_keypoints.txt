- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical feature learning with HMM/RNN-inspired temporal modeling.  
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction task.  
- After extensive (~10 days) training, the model achieves state-of-the-art results on the Wikipedia dataset from Hinton et al.  
- Provides detailed internal analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and pairs of matching parentheses form a "pseudo-stable attractor" state.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology, robust experimental design, and supportive figures.  
- Primary experimental concern: evaluation is limited to the Wikipedia dataset with few prior baselines.  
- Comparisons are restricted mainly to RNN-based methods; the paper omits comparisons to hierarchical models like HMMs/hierarchical HMMs.  
- Some cited alternatives are evaluated on different corpora, making comparisons incomparable and weakening novelty claims.  
- Contribution mismatch: paper emphasizes a unified architecture but its strongest results are mechanistic analyses rather than empirical superiority.  
- Results are promising but inconclusive regarding generalization beyond the single dataset.  
- Recommendation: expand evaluations to include hierarchical HMMs and other relevant baselines on standardized benchmarks to strengthen claims.  
- Overall: technically sound model with valuable internal insights, but limited experimental breadth prevents a definitive contribution claim.
