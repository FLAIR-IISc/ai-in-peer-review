- Proposal: Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) — hierarchical stacked RNN layers that receive lower-layer hidden states to model temporal hierarchies.  
- Empirical result: DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art among RNNs on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) using only SGD.  
- Contribution: Empirical validation that temporal hierarchies emerge across layers.  
- Contribution: Layer-specific time-scale analysis via perturbation experiments (typo propagation, parenthesis closure).  
- Contribution: Higher layers capture long-term dependencies, retaining context for hundreds of characters.  
- Quality: Experiments are technically sound, with ablation studies, controlled perturbations, and generative tests; results robust across three instantiations.  
- Limitation: Primary comparisons only against RNN variants, omitting non-RNN hierarchical models (HMMs, CRFs, Echo State Networks).  
- Claim caveat: “State-of-the-art” is relative to RNNs and not benchmarked against broader language-modeling approaches (e.g., PAQ).  
- Efficiency concern: Large models required ~10 days training on one GPU with no efficiency comparison to alternatives (e.g., Hessian-free methods).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear architecture diagrams and compelling analyses; minor clarifications needed (DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O performance, SGD vs Hessian-free cost).  
- Originality: Architecture itself is not novel (prior use exists); main novelty is the empirical analysis of time-scale emergence.  
- Significance: Provides meaningful insights into RNN temporal dynamics and long-term memory, but impact limited by single-dataset evaluation and lack of generalization tests.  
- Strengths: Rigorous temporal-hierarchy analysis, strong visualizations, and practical demonstration that SGD suffices for training.  
- Weaknesses: Overstated architectural novelty, limited dataset scope and comparisons, potentially misleading SOTA claim, and uncontextualized computational cost.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to clarify novelty, expand comparisons to non-RNN hierarchical models, and contextualize computational costs.
