- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling, inspired by HMMs and RNNs.
- Training uses layer-by-layer construction with supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction objective.
- Experimental results on a Wikipedia dataset show improved performance, surpassing previous work after ~10 days of training large networks.
- In-depth analysis reveals higher layers model long-term dependencies and capture parentheses correspondence as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."
- Paper is well-written with well-designed experiments and effective figures supporting the findings.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, underused dataset.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical HMMs and other relevant approaches.
- Table 1 lists alternative methods but they are evaluated on different corpora, making comparisons incomparable.
- Contribution is stronger in providing analysis of network behavior than in definitive empirical achievements.
- Results are somewhat inconclusive and findings could be more comprehensive despite solid analysis and presentation.
