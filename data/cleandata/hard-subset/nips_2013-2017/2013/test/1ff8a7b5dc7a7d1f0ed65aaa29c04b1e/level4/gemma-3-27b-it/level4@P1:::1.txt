The authors propose a new deep architecture that combines the hierarchical structure of deep learning with the time-series modeling capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The proposed training algorithm builds the network layer-by-layer using supervised learning for a next-character prediction task. The experiments demonstrate that, after training very large networks for approximately 10 days, the network achieves state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, improving upon previous work. The authors then proceed to analyze and discuss details of how the network approaches its task, such as modeling long-term dependencies in higher layers and representing the correspondence between opening and closing parentheses as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”

The paper is of good quality. It is well-written, the experiments are well-designed, and the figures effectively support the findings. My main concern lies with the experimental evaluation. The comparison is performed on a single dataset, which doesn’t appear to have a substantial body of existing work. Direct comparisons are limited to other RNN-related models, rather than more common hierarchical approaches like Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), which are capable of solving similar tasks. (Table 1 lists other approaches, but on different corpora, rendering direct comparison difficult.)

The paper’s primary contribution lies in the analysis of *how* the network operates—an analysis that is conducted quite well—rather than in achieving a new state-of-the-art result, though this was a stated goal. A novel deep architecture is proposed, combining the hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling. The paper is well-written, the model is thoroughly analyzed, but the results are somewhat limited in scope.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel architecture that effectively combines deep learning and recurrent neural networks.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   The analysis of the network’s internal workings is insightful and provides a better understanding of how hierarchical recurrent networks process sequential data.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, making it difficult to assess the generalizability of the results.
*   The comparison to other relevant approaches (e.g., hierarchical HMMs) is limited.
*   While the analysis is strong, the performance improvements over existing RNN models are not dramatic.
