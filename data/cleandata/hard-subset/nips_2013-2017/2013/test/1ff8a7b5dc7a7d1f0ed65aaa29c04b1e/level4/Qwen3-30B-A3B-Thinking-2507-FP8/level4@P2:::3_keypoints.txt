- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): each layer is a recurrent network processing the previous layer’s hidden state to form a hierarchical temporal processor.  
- DRNNs naturally embed multiple time scales, allowing better modeling of long-term dependencies than standard RNNs.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work [3,5,6,8] but provides a more systematic analysis of temporal hierarchy and task relationship, especially for character-level language modeling.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using simple SGD, matching results previously requiring Hessian-free optimization.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and technically rigorous, with careful hyperparameter selection and clear training protocols.  
- Time-scale analysis via perturbation experiments (Figure 3) and text generation (Table 2) gives valuable insight into layer-specific temporal processing.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and reproducible; figures effectively illustrate concepts but some acronyms (e.g., DRNN-AO, DRNN-1O) should be defined earlier.  
- Originality is moderate: DRNN architecture is novel but closely related to [8] (stacked bi-directional LSTMs); main contribution is the systematic time-scale analysis.  
- Significance is notable: shows hierarchical RNNs can achieve strong results with simpler optimizers and provides theoretical insight into temporal processing in deep nets.  
- Concern 1: Comparisons may be unfair due to parameter-count differences (standard RNN: 2119 units; DRNN-1O: 5×727 units → different total recurrent parameters); recommend comparable-parameter/unit baselines.  
- Concern 2: Need experiments to verify higher layers benefit from hierarchical arrangement (e.g., moving same units to different positions or comparable flat architectures).  
- Concern 3: Should more thoroughly compare and discuss recent language-modeling work by Mikolov et al. as a relevant benchmark.  
- Text-generation ("brain surgery") experiments are convincing about layer roles (bottom layer for short-term stats, top for long-term patterns), but interpretations should remain cautious due to interdependencies.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-executed, meaningful insights; with revisions on parameter-comparisons and prior-work clarification, recommend acceptance.
