- Authors’ observation: older data benefits from deeper processing; proposal to use deep RNNs for short-term tasks and suggestion to explore adding extra nonlinear layers between time steps.  
- Question the role of gradient normalization in the method, how it prevents “bifurcations,” and whether it might interfere with SGD’s averaging; cite relevance of an ICML 2013 paper on gradient truncation/optimization in RNNs.  
- Recommend comparing Wikipedia compression results to prior work by Mikolov et al.  
- Find the text-generation experiment (Table 2) the most persuasive, but note that “brain surgery” interventions are hard to interpret due to interdependencies between layer outputs.  
- Major missing empirical test: whether higher layers genuinely process more abstract/long-term features non-trivially, versus simply relocating units to the first layer.  
- Point out an unfair comparison: the regular RNN has fewer parameters; urge comparisons where deeper models and baseline RNNs have matched parameter counts or units.  
- Suggest replacing Figure 2 with plots showing performance across DRNN depths when trained from scratch, possibly widening layers to equalize parameter counts.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the text rather than only in Figure 1.  
- Overall: paper is promising, well-written, and thorough, demonstrating DRNN effectiveness for text prediction/compression while acknowledging limitations.
