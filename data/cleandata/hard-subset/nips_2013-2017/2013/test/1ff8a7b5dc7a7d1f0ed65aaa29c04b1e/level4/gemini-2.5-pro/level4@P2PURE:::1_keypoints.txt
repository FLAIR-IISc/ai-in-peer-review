- Introduces a novel deep architecture integrating hierarchical deep learning with time-series models (HMMs/RNNs).  
- Proposes a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training algorithm using a next-character prediction objective.  
- Reports experiments on a Wikipedia dataset (used by Hinton et al.) where a very large network, after ~10 days of training, improves over prior work.  
- Provides detailed analysis of internal mechanisms, showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and matching parentheses form a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is well-written, experiments are methodologically sound, and figures effectively support findings.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset that is not a widely-used benchmark.  
- Empirical comparisons are restricted to other RNN-based models and omit relevant model classes (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).  
- Table 1 lists other approaches but uses a different corpus, preventing direct comparison.  
- Main contribution is the insightful analysis of network behavior rather than establishing a new state-of-the-art; empirical results are therefore inconclusive due to limited scope.
