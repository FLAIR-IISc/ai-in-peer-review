- Paper proposes a hybrid deep recurrent network (DRNN) and shows strong text prediction and compression performance.
- Experiments aim to show higher layers learn more abstract, longer-timescale representations.
- Reviewer finds the experiments interesting but raises reservations and requests additional experiments.
- Core motivation is compelling: DRNN processes each timestep through multiple layers to avoid arbitrary deeper processing of older inputs.
- Suggests authors consider/compare an alternative: adding non-linear layers between hidden-state updates at each timestep.
- Layer-role analysis is interesting and Table 2 (text generation) is persuasive, but lesion studies can be hard to interpret due to inter-layer dependencies.
- Key unanswered question: does hierarchy itself enable capturing long-term abstractions, or is this just a consequence of larger model capacity?
- Recommends comparing DRNN to a standard single-layer RNN with matched parameter count to disentangle depth vs. size effects.
- Notes current DRNNs likely have many more parameters than baselines; current comparisons may be unfair.
- Figure 2 should show performance of DRNNs of varying depths trained from scratch, with shallower models widened to match parameter budgets.
- Asks for clarification on gradient normalization, its relation to gradient clipping (Pascanu et al. 2013), and whether it affects SGD's implicit averaging.
- Requests comparative results for the Wikipedia compression task (e.g., Mikolov et al.) for context.
- Minor: explicitly define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the main text (they appear only in Figure 1).
