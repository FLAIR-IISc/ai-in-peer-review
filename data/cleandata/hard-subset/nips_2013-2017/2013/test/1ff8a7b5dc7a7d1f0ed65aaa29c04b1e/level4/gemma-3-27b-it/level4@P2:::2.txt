This paper investigates the utility of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), demonstrating that, with a fixed number of parameters, deeper recurrent networks outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling. Additional findings include an analysis revealing that deeper networks capture more long-term dependencies and the surprising result that standard backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses – a task previously thought to require more advanced optimization techniques like Hessian-free (HF) training. 

The authors rightly position their work within the context of the rise of deep neural networks and acknowledge that the DRNN architecture itself is not novel, citing related work such as Graves et al. (2013). While the paper doesn’t *introduce* a new architecture, it offers a valuable analysis of a previously explored concept. This focus on analysis is acceptable, but the paper would benefit from broader empirical validation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors demonstrate improved performance with deeper RNNs on a challenging character-level language modeling task.
*   **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of time scales within the DRNN layers provides valuable insight into how these networks process temporal data. The observation that deeper layers exhibit more long-range structure is particularly compelling.
*   **Surprising Result Regarding BPTT:** The finding that BPTT can effectively train DRNNs to balance parentheses challenges existing assumptions and suggests a re-evaluation of training strategies.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the experimental setup is clearly described.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Generalizability:** The analysis is currently limited to character-level language modeling. The findings may not generalize to other sequential tasks, such as speech recognition. Evaluating the DRNN architecture on a different task (e.g., speech) would strengthen the conclusions.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Truncated BPTT:** While the authors acknowledge the existence of truncated backpropagation through time (BPTT), a more direct comparison is warranted. Given its historical success in training RNN language models (Mikolov, PhD Thesis), truncated BPTT with a reasonable truncation length (e.g., 30 steps) is likely to achieve competitive performance at a significantly reduced computational cost.  This would provide a more practical benchmark and further validate the effectiveness of BPTT.
*   **Architectural Novelty:** The paper should explicitly acknowledge the prior work on stacked/deep RNNs (e.g., Graves et al., 2013) and refrain from claiming novelty in the architecture itself. The value lies in the analysis, not the architectural innovation.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a solid empirical evaluation of DRNNs and offers valuable insights into their behavior.
*   The finding regarding BPTT and parenthesis balancing is a significant and unexpected result.
*   The analysis of time scales within the DRNN layers is well-executed and contributes to our understanding of deep recurrent networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The limited scope of the analysis (single task) weakens the generalizability of the conclusions.
*   The absence of a comparison to truncated BPTT leaves a potentially important benchmark unexplored.
*   The claim of introducing a new architecture is inaccurate and should be removed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a good paper with interesting results. While the lack of broader generalization and the missing comparison to truncated BPTT are weaknesses, the strong empirical evidence and insightful analysis justify acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing these points in a rebuttal. Specifically, the authors should clarify that they are not introducing a new architecture and commit to exploring truncated BPTT in future work or as an addition to the submission.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.



**References:**
