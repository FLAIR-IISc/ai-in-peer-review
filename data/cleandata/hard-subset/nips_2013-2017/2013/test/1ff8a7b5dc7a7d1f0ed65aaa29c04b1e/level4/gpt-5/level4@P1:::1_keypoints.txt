- Paper studies stacked deep RNNs (DRNNs) with two output variants (top-only DRNN-1O and all-layers DRNN-AO) for temporal hierarchy.
- Trained with SGD, gradient normalization, and layer-wise supervised pretraining; models (~4.9M params) trained on ~1.4B-char Wikipedia for ~10 days on one GPU.
- Achieves strong bits-per-character (BPC) competitive with multiplicative RNNs trained with Hessian-free optimization.
- Main novelty is systematic analysis of emergent temporal behavior rather than a new architecture.
- Experiments analyze layer time scales, layer-ablation effects, generative samples, typo/context sensitivity, and long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses).
- Architecture is incremental relative to prior stacked/hierarchical RNN work; related work is cited but distinctions could be clearer.
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-described architecture and training setup.
- Strength: Careful experimental protocol, strong engineering, and informative figures.
- Strength: Insightful analyses (ablations, perturbations, parentheses) demonstrating multi-timescale behavior and long-range interactions.
- Strength: Simple SGD-based training achieves competitive performance versus more complex second-order methods.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single dataset and only three model instantiations, limiting robustness claims.
- Weakness: Comparisons include non-RNN methods on different corpora, making some numbers not directly comparable.
- Weakness: Missing strong baselines (e.g., stacked LSTMs, hierarchical/multiscale models) evaluated under the same conditions.
- Weakness: Incremental architectural contribution; framing as a new architecture is overstated.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of design choices, hyperparameter sensitivity, and variance across runs.
- Suggestion: Add results on additional benchmarks (text8/enwik8, PTB) and parameter-matched comparisons to LSTMs and multiscale models.
- Suggestion: Include non-RNN baselines (hierarchical HMM, strong n-gram/Kneser-Ney) on the same corpus and expand ablations (layers, outputs, pretraining).
- Suggestion: Report run variance, learning curves, training budget in tokens, and consider releasing code; clarify dataset preprocessing and vocabulary effects.
- Argument for acceptance: Valuable, careful analysis of how depth induces multi-timescale behavior and competitive empirical results with simple training.
- Argument against acceptance: Narrow empirical scope, modest architectural novelty, and some non–apples-to-apples comparisons weaken claims.
- Overall: Well-executed, clearly presented work with strong analytical insights but limited by scope and comparison breadth; broader experiments would strengthen impact.
