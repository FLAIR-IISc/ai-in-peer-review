- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNN layers) to create a temporal processing hierarchy that can capture multi-timescale structure.
- Applies DRNNs to character-level language modeling on Wikipedia and reports state-of-the-art recurrent network performance using plain SGD.
- Provides analytical experiments (text generation, typo perturbation, parenthesis task) suggesting different layers learn different timescales and abstractions.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and transparent about limitations and interpretability challenges.
- Major concern: baseline comparisons may be unfair because DRNNs appear to have far more trainable parameters than the single-layer RNN baseline.
- Needs a controlled comparison to a standard (wider) RNN matched for total parameter count to rule out capacity effects.
- Should compare DRNNs to single-layer RNNs matched for total units (not just parameters) to isolate the benefit of depth versus network size.
- Recommend plotting final test performance for networks of varying depths (1–5 layers) with widths adjusted to equalize parameters/units for a direct depth-impact analysis.
- Training detail gap: the gradient normalization trick is critical but insufficiently explained, including its relation to bifurcations, gradient clipping, and SGD dynamics.
- Missing citation/context: include related large-scale language modeling work (e.g., Mikolov et al.).
- Question for authors: have they considered alternative ways to add depth, such as extra feed-forward layers between time steps?
- Minor issue: acronyms DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO are used before being defined in the main text.
- Conclusion: strong and interesting work, but claims would be significantly strengthened by controlled baselines and additional experiments addressing parameter/unit matching.
