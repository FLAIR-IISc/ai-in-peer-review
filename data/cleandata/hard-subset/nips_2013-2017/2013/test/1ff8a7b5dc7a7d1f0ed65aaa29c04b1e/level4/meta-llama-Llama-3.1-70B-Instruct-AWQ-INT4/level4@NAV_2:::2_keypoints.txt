- Proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) where each layer is a recurrent network taking the previous layer’s hidden state, enabling hierarchical temporal processing.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling trained with simple SGD and analyzes emergent time scales.  
- Demonstrates that DRNNs can capture long-term dependencies and temporal hierarchies.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents results and analysis concisely.  
- Strength: Proposes a hierarchical DRNN architecture for time series processing.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough analysis and insightful empirical results on character-level modeling.  
- Weakness: Not novel—deep RNNs have been used previously (e.g., Graves 2013).  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a single task, so generalization to other domains is unclear.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other architectures or methods (e.g., truncated BPTT).  
- Originality: Contribution is primarily analytical/empirical rather than introducing a new architecture.  
- Significance: Findings are meaningful for NLP and may impact speech recognition and machine translation.  
- Quality: High quality of writing, organization, and experimental analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Thorough analysis, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: Limited novelty, narrow empirical scope, and missing baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but encourage broader evaluations, comparisons to alternatives, and more implementation/training details.
