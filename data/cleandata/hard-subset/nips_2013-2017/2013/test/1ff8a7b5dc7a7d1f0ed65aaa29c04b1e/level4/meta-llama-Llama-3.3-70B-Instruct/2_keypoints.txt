- Agree with the suggestion to focus the paper on a single task.  
- Recommend experimenting with truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) to potentially match entropies at much lower cost.  
- Paper examines the effectiveness of deep recurrent neural networks.  
- Key result: deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when the number of parameters is held constant.  
- Deeper networks retain more long-term information in higher layers.  
- Standard BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior assumptions.  
- Claiming to have introduced deep RNNs is inaccurate — prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) and earlier studies already used them.  
- The analysis would be stronger if validated on additional problems (e.g., speech recognition) to demonstrate generality.  
- Hierarchical framework experiments may have been unnecessarily costly; truncated BPTT (used since the 1990s and by Mikolov) should be tested as a cheaper alternative.  
- Concise contributions reported: deeper RNNs improve character-level modeling, deeper layers capture long-range structure, and BPTT can learn parentheses-balancing.
