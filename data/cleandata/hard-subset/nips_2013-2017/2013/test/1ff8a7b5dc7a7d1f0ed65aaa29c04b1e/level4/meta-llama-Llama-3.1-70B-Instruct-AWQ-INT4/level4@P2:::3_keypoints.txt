- Proposes DRNNs: hierarchical architecture combining deep networks and RNNs to better model temporal hierarchies in time series.
- Architecture: multiple recurrent layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks on a character-level Wikipedia language modeling task.
- Provides analysis showing higher layers capture longer-term dependencies and emergent multiple time scales.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and explains the architecture and advantages effectively.
- Experimental analysis is thorough and highlights both strengths and weaknesses of the approach.
- Main weakness: comparison to traditional RNNs may be unfair because DRNNs have more parameters.
- Suggested remedy: compare against standard RNNs with equal parameter counts or match units (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs single-layer RNN with same units).
- Lacks deeper insight into what higher layers represent and how they process more abstract, long-term text properties.
- Interpretability concerns: interventions like "brain surgery" are hard to interpret due to complex interdependencies across layers.
- Additional experiments and comparisons would strengthen the paper’s claims and clarify limitations.
- Originality: novel combination of DNN and RNN ideas applied to text prediction and compression.
- Significance: results have implications for improved language models and text compression algorithms.
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed work.
