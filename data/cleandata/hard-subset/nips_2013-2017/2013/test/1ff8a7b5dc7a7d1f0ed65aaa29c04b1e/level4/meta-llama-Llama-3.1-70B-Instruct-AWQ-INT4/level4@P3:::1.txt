This paper proposes a novel deep architecture, combining the hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling known from Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) or recurrent neural networks. The authors introduce a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks multiple layers of recurrent neural networks, allowing for hierarchical processing of time series data. The proposed training algorithm builds the network layer-by-layer using supervised (pre-)training with a next-letter prediction objective.

The paper is well-written, and the experiments are set up well. The figures effectively support the findings, providing valuable insights into the behavior of the DRNN. The authors demonstrate that after training very large networks for about 10 days, the network performance on a Wikipedia dataset published by Hinton et al. improves over previous work.

The authors then proceed to analyze and discuss details of how the network approaches its task. For example, they show that long-term dependencies are modeled in higher layers, and correspondence between opening and closing parentheses are modeled as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state". These analyses provide a good understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed architecture.

However, my main issue with the paper is the limited scope of the experiments. The comparison is done on one dataset only, which does not seem to have much work on it. Direct comparison is only with other RNN-related models, not with more common hierarchical HMMs or other architectures that are able to solve similar tasks. While Table 1 lists other approaches, they are evaluated on a different corpus, making them incomparable.

The contribution of the paper is more in the analysis of how the network operates than in what it achieves. While the paper sets out to propose a new deep architecture, the results are somewhat inconclusive in terms of its overall performance and generalizability. Nevertheless, the paper provides valuable insights into the behavior of DRNNs and their potential applications in time-series modeling.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The figures and tables are effectively used to support the findings.

**Originality:** The paper proposes a novel deep architecture that combines hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling. While the idea of stacking RNNs is not new, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the resulting architecture and its applications.

**Significance:** The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed architecture has the potential to advance the state of the art in time-series modeling and language modeling.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel deep architecture that combines hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the resulting architecture and its applications.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research.

**Arguments for rejection:**

* The experiments are limited to a single dataset, and the comparison is only with other RNN-related models.
* The results are somewhat inconclusive in terms of the overall performance and generalizability of the proposed architecture.

**Suggestions for improvement:**

* Provide a more comprehensive comparison with other architectures that are able to solve similar tasks.
* Evaluate the proposed architecture on multiple datasets to demonstrate its generalizability.
* Consider providing more details on the training algorithm and the hyperparameter tuning process.
