- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep models with time-series modeling, inspired by HMMs and RNNs.
- Uses layer-wise supervised pre-training with a next-character prediction objective.
- After ~10 days of large-scale training, outperforms existing methods on the Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset.
- Provides detailed internal analysis showing higher layers learn long-term dependencies and parentheses-like structures form "pseudo-stable attractor-like states."
- Manuscript quality is high: clear writing, sound experimental design, and effective figures.
- Major concern: evaluation is limited to a single, not widely benchmarked dataset.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-based models; missing relevant baselines such as hierarchical HMMs.
- Table 1 reports other approaches but on different corpora, preventing direct comparison.
- Contribution is stronger in insightful analysis of model behavior than in demonstrating a clearly superior high-performing architecture.
- Empirical results are thus somewhat inconclusive due to the limited experimental scope.
