- Stacking recurrent layers is sensible for giving more nonlinear processing to short-term signals; consider as baseline adding extra feedforward transforms between time steps.
- Have you explored the variant that inserts additional nonlinear (feedforward) layers within each time step?
- Clarify how critical gradient normalization was, precisely how it “avoids bifurcations,” and whether this relates to gradient clipping/truncation literature (e.g., ICML 2013 UMontreal); could scaling gradients harm SGD’s stochastic averaging?
- Compare results on Wikipedia-style compression to prior work by Mikolov et al.
- The text-generation ablation (Table 2) is the most compelling experiment, but interpreting “brain surgery” ablations is tricky due to interdependencies among layer outputs.
- Major empirical gap: show that higher layers genuinely gain from being higher rather than simply from having more units—i.e., test whether the same units placed in the first layer perform as well.
- The plain RNN in your setup appears to have fewer parameters, biasing the comparison; stronger evidence requires matching parameter or unit counts between models.
- Instead of only Figure 2, report results across multiple depths trained from scratch, widening shallower models to match parameters/units.
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O in the main text rather than only in Figure 1.
- Overall: DRNNs are a valuable direction and perform well on text prediction/compression; experiments suggest higher layers capture longer-term/abstract structure but further controlled experiments are needed.
