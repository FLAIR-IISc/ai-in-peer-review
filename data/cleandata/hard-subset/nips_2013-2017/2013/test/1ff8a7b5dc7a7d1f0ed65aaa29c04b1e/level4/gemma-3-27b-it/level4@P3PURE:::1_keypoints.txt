- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN concepts).  
- Training uses supervised layer-wise (pre-)training with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Empirical results: after ~10 days of training on large networks, outperforms prior results on a Wikipedia dataset (Hinton et al.).  
- Detailed analysis of network behavior: long-term dependencies encoded in higher layers.  
- Observed representations such as opening/closing parentheses forming a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Paper is well-written with well-designed experiments and supportive figures.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, relatively little-used dataset.  
- Comparisons are primarily against RNN-based models and omit hierarchical HMMs and related approaches.  
- Table 1 lists other methods but uses a different corpus, making direct comparison invalid.  
- Contribution is stronger in insightful analysis of how the model works than in definitive performance gains.  
- Overall conclusion: novel, thoroughly analyzed architecture but results are inconclusive due to limited experimental scope.
