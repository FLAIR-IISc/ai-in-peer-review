

This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), where each layer is a recurrent network that processes the hidden state from the layer below. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling when parameter counts are held constant, achieving state-of-the-art results with simple SGD training. They further analyze how DRNNs develop hierarchical time scales across layers, with deeper layers capturing longer-term dependencies, and show that DRNNs can effectively learn to balance parentheses without specialized training algorithms like Hessian-free optimization.

The paper builds on previous work in hierarchical RNNs, particularly Graves et al. (ICASSP 2013) who used stacked bidirectional LSTMs for speech recognition, and El Hihi & Bengio (NIPS 1996) who proposed hierarchical RNNs for long-term dependencies. While the architecture itself isn't novel (as acknowledged by the authors' reference to Graves et al.), the paper makes a valuable contribution by providing a detailed analysis of DRNNs' hierarchical time scale structure and demonstrating their effectiveness for language modeling with standard training methods.

Strengths: The paper offers compelling experimental evidence that DRNNs naturally develop multiple time scales across layers, with deeper layers processing longer-term dependencies. The analysis of parenthesis balancing demonstrates that DRNNs can learn complex long-term dependencies without specialized training algorithms, challenging previous assumptions that Hessian-free optimization was necessary for such tasks. The experimental design is sound, with careful attention to parameter matching and appropriate baselines.

Weaknesses: The paper's claim to "introduce" the DRNN architecture is problematic since it has been previously used (Graves et al., 2013). The authors should reframe their contribution as an analysis of an existing architecture rather than a novel proposal. While the character-level language modeling results are impressive, the paper would be significantly strengthened by extending the analysis to another domain like speech recognition, which would help establish whether the observed hierarchical time scale properties generalize beyond text. The paper also doesn't compare against truncated BPTT, which has been successfully used for language modeling (Mikolov, 2012), making it difficult to determine whether the DRNN's advantages stem from architecture or training methodology.

The paper excels in technical quality, with thorough experiments and appropriate baselines. The writing is clear and well-organized, though the claim about introducing the DRNN architecture undermines the paper's originality. The significance of the work is moderate; while the findings advance our understanding of RNN architectures and their temporal processing capabilities, the limited scope to character-level language modeling restricts broader impact. The paper would benefit from addressing the following questions: (1) Why do the time scales differ between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO architectures? (2) How do these findings relate to other hierarchical processing approaches in deep learning? (3) What are the computational trade-offs between DRNNs and standard RNNs?

Overall, this is an interesting analysis of DRNNs that provides valuable insights into how hierarchical RNN architectures process temporal information. While the architecture itself isn't novel, the detailed analysis of time scale emergence and the demonstration that simple SGD can achieve state-of-the-art results for character-level language modeling are meaningful contributions. To strengthen the paper, the authors should clarify their contribution as an analysis of an existing architecture rather than a novel proposal, and extend their evaluation to at least one additional domain like speech recognition to demonstrate broader applicability.

The paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, but it would be significantly improved with a more careful framing of its contributions and additional validation on other tasks. The findings are technically sound and well-supported, making them suitable for publication at NIPS with minor revisions. The paper would be particularly valuable to researchers interested in understanding the temporal processing capabilities of deep recurrent architectures.
