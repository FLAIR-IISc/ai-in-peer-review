- Reviewer agrees with the comment that the evaluation is limited to a single task.
- Recommend trying truncated backpropagation (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper alternative; it may not learn parentheses balancing but could achieve competitive entropies.
- Paper shows deeper recurrent networks outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is held constant on character-level language modeling.
- Analysis suggests deeper RNN layers retain more long-term information.
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs that HF was required.
- Paper incorrectly claims to have introduced the deep RNN architecture; prior work (e.g., Graves, ICASSP 2013) already used similar architectures.
- HF experiments were likely too costly and truncated BPTT (used successfully by Mikolov) should be evaluated as a practical alternative.
- The analysis would be more convincing if replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to rule out task-specific artifacts.
