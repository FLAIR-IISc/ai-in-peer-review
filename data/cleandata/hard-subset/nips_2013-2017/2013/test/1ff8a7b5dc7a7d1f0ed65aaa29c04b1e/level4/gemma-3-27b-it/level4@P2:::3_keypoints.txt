- Paper proposes deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) with recurrent connections at each layer to capture temporal hierarchies for text prediction and compression.
- Experiments on a Wikipedia corpus show competitive/state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks and analyze layer roles across timescales.
- Core idea: hierarchical deep recurrence can reduce unnecessary repeated processing of older data and better handle short- and long-term dependencies.
- Suggest exploring adding nonlinearities between timesteps and measuring their effect.
- The role and impact of gradient normalization (and its relation to “avoiding bifurcations”) need deeper discussion and citation of related ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/optimization.
- Consider whether gradient normalization changes the averaging behavior of stochastic gradient descent.
- Paper should compare to Mikolov et al.’s recent Wikipedia compression work for a more comprehensive benchmark.
- Text generation experiment (Table 2) is particularly compelling for understanding layer contributions.
- Ablation results are hard to interpret due to potential interdependencies among layers.
- Missing demonstration that higher layers genuinely process more abstract/long-term properties beyond post-hoc interpretation.
- Comparisons to standard RNNs may be unfair due to differing parameter counts; need matched-parameter baselines.
- Recommend comparing DRNNs to standard RNNs with equivalent parameter counts or unit counts (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs. single-layer RNN with same units).
- Instead of Figure 2, provide a benchmark of DRNNs with varying depths trained from scratch and balanced parameter counts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of DRNN architecture, strong performance, insightful layer analysis, clear writing, and honest discussion of limitations.
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter count discrepancy, lack of ablation proof that depth helps, limited comparison to prior work, and incomplete depth-vs-performance validation.
- Minor point: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the text rather than only in Figure 1.
- Overall recommendation: worthwhile contribution; accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing comparison and hierarchical-benefit concerns.
