- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with HMM/RNN-style time-series modeling.  
- Uses layer-by-layer supervised pre-training with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- After ~10 days of training on very large networks, achieves improved performance on the Wikipedia dataset, surpassing prior work.  
- Analysis shows higher layers model long-term dependencies and parentheses matching via a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, with well-designed experiments and supportive figures.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is narrow, conducted only on the Wikipedia dataset with limited prior benchmarks.  
- Benchmarking is restricted to RNN-related models and omits comparisons to established hierarchical methods (e.g., HMMs).  
- Table 1 lists alternatives evaluated on different corpora, making comparisons invalid.  
- Contribution mismatch: paper emphasizes a new architecture, but primary value appears to be the detailed analysis of network behavior.  
- Overall: clear writing and solid analysis, but results are inconclusive due to the limited experimental scope.
