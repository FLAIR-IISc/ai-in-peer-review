- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) claiming deeper layers learn more abstract, long-term dependencies; promising results on text prediction and compression.  
- Paper is well-written and candid about limitations, offering useful insights into deep temporal networks.  
- Suggests exploring additional non-linearities between timesteps as a natural extension; asks if this was investigated.  
- Gradient normalization is central but poorly explained; needs comparison to recent RNN optimization work (e.g., ICML 2013) and discussion of effects on SGD averaging.  
- Recommend including Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia compression work for a more comprehensive baseline comparison.  
- Ablation studies: text generation results are persuasive, but the difficulty of interpreting “brain surgery” interventions due to interdependent layer outputs requires deeper discussion.  
- Major concern: causal link between depth and abstraction is not established; current results only show higher layers appear more abstract.  
- Proposes a crucial experiment: move units from higher layers to the first layer to test if depth (preceding layers) is necessary for long-term dependency capture.  
- Experimental fairness issue: baseline RNN appears to have fewer parameters—compare DRNNs to standard RNNs with equal parameter counts or equal units.  
- For Figure 2, show DRNN performance across varying depths trained from scratch and consider widening deeper networks to match parameter counts for fairer architectural comparison.  
- Minor point: define acronyms DRNN-AO and IO in the text for clarity.
