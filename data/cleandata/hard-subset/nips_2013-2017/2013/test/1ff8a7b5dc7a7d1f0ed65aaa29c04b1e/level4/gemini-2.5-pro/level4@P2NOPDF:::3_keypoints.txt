- Introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) combining RNN temporal processing with deep hierarchical feature extraction, applied to text prediction and compression with strong reported performance.  
- Paper is well-written, acknowledges limitations, and presents a compelling, insightful motivation that depth mitigates uneven processing of old vs. recent inputs.  
- Experiments (especially text generation/Table 2) provide initial evidence that higher layers specialize in more abstract, longer-term dependencies.  
- Major concern: baseline single-layer RNNs have far fewer parameters than DRNNs, so gains may be due to capacity, not depth.  
- Requires direct comparison to parameter-matched or width-matched single-layer RNNs to validate that depth (not just parameter count) drives improvements.  
- Unresolved: whether higher-layer units truly gain from being deep vs. simply arising in a sufficiently large shallow network; causal role of depth not established.  
- Suggestion: systematic study of performance versus depth (1..N layers) with controlled total parameters/units to clarify depth vs. width trade-offs.  
- Question about gradient normalization: request more detail on how it prevents "bifurcations," its necessity, and whether it interferes with SGD averaging; cite relevant ICML 2013 work.  
- Query about alternative architectures: consider and compare adding hidden layers between timesteps within a single recurrent layer as another way to add depth/nonlinearity.  
- Missing comparison: Wikipedia compression results should be compared to prior work (e.g., Mikolov et al.) for proper contextualization.  
- Interpretation caveat: "brain surgery" layer-intervention experiments are hard to interpret due to complex learned interdependencies—authors rightly note this limitation.  
- Minor: terms DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO appear in Figure 1 but are not defined in the main text.  
- Overall: promising and thoughtful paper with valuable analyses, but central claims about the benefits of depth need stronger, fairer empirical validation to justify publication.
