- Deeper recurrent architectures outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling for a fixed parameter budget.
- Deeper networks capture more long-term dependencies, exhibiting emergent longer time scales in their dynamics.
- Plain backpropagation through time can train RNNs to balance parentheses, a task thought to require more advanced optimization.
- Empirical demonstration of depth benefits and analysis of emergent time scales is a key strength, with clear writing and appropriate citations.
- Claiming to introduce the “deep RNN” is inaccurate; prior hierarchical RNNs (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) should be acknowledged.
- Results are limited to character-level modeling, leaving the generalizability to other domains (e.g., speech recognition) unclear.
- The paper does not explore more computationally efficient training methods (e.g., truncated BPTT), which could reduce cost while remaining competitive.
- Recommendation: revise novelty claim, evaluate on additional tasks, and investigate efficient training methods to strengthen the contribution.
