- Reviewer agrees with concern about using a single task and suggests testing generality across tasks.
- Reviewer recommends experimenting with truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper alternative that might still achieve competitive entropies.
- Paper shows deep RNNs outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is held constant on a character-level language modeling task.
- Analysis indicates deeper layers capture more long-term information.
- Plain BPTT can train models to balance parentheses, countering the belief that HF optimization is required.
- Claim of introducing the deep RNN architecture is inaccurate; prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) predated it.
- Paper should reframe contribution as analysis of an existing architecture rather than architectural novelty.
- Experimental conclusions are limited by evaluation on a single task; results may be domain-specific and need validation on other tasks (e.g., speech).
- Reliance on Hessian-Free optimization is computationally excessive without comparison to standard baselines.
- A comparison to truncated BPTT (a common, efficient method) is necessary to contextualize performance and computational cost.
