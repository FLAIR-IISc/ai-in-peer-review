This paper introduces and analyzes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture that stacks multiple recurrent layers to form a temporal hierarchy. The core idea is that each layer operates on the sequence of hidden states from the layer below, enabling the model to process information at multiple timescales. The authors evaluate this architecture on a large-scale character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus. They demonstrate that a 5-layer DRNN, trained with a straightforward stochastic gradient descent (SGD) method, achieves state-of-the-art performance, comparable to previous results obtained with more complex Hessian-free optimization. A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to an in-depth analysis of the trained models, investigating the emergent temporal hierarchy across layers and the network's ability to capture long-range dependencies, such as balancing parentheses over long distances.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The DRNN architecture is explained concisely, and the experimental setup and results are presented in a manner that is easy to follow. The figures, particularly those analyzing the model's internal dynamics (Fig. 3 and 4), are highly effective at illustrating the core claims of the paper.
*   **Significance of Analysis:** The most compelling aspect of this work is the detailed analysis of the trained models. The experiments showing the emergence of different timescales in different layers provide strong evidence for the hierarchical processing hypothesis. The investigation of how the network handles long-term dependencies like parentheses offers valuable and concrete insights into the capabilities of such deep recurrent models, moving beyond simple performance metrics.
*   **Quality of Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on this challenging benchmark using only SGD is a notable result. It suggests that architectural improvements can be as impactful as advancements in optimization algorithms, which is an important contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Empirical Scope:** The paper's primary weakness is that its empirical claims are based on a single dataset. While the Wikipedia corpus is large and challenging, the lack of evaluation on other benchmark time-series or sequence modeling tasks makes it difficult to ascertain the general applicability of the DRNN's advantages over standard RNNs.
*   **Narrow Comparison:** The performance comparison is largely restricted to other RNN-based models. The work would be strengthened by relating the DRNN to other classes of models designed for hierarchical temporal data, such as Hierarchical Hidden Markov Models or other structured models, even if only through discussion. While Table 1 includes other methods, they are evaluated on different datasets, making a direct comparison impossible.
*   **Slightly Unfocused Contribution:** The paper presents itself as proposing a new architecture, but its strongest contribution lies in the analysis of how that architecture functions. The results are compelling but could be framed more decisively as either a breakthrough in model performance or as a case study for understanding hierarchical processing in neural networks.

In summary, this is a high-quality paper that presents a powerful deep recurrent architecture and, more importantly, provides a thorough and insightful analysis of its inner workings. While the experimental validation could be broader, the demonstrated performance and the detailed investigation into the model's learned temporal hierarchy make it a significant contribution.
