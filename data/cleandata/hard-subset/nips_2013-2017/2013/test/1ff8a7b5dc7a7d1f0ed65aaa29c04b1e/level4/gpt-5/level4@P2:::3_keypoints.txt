- Paper proposes stacked recurrent layers (DRNNs) with two output schemes (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO), trained with SGD and gradient normalization; achieves competitive BPC on Wikipedia and analyzes layer time-scales and long-range phenomena.  
- Connects to prior hierarchical/stacked RNN work (El Hihi & Bengio, Graves et al.) and HF-trained multiplicative RNNs; should also compare to Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia character models.  
- Strength: clear motivation that stacked recurrence induces temporal hierarchies and a simple architecture that attains strong results with plain SGD.  
- Strength: thoughtful analyses of emergent time scales and layer roles (perturbations, context switches, ablations, parentheses study).  
- Strength: well written and candid about limitations and interpretability challenges.  
- Main question: role and necessity of gradient normalization unclear—needs clarification of “bifurcations,” relation to gradient clipping/truncated BPTT, and ablation vs. clipping/none.  
- Main question: did authors consider deep transition RNNs (multiple nonlinearities per time step) to separate depth-in-time vs. depth-in-state effects?  
- Main question: layer ablation interpretations are confounded by interdependencies—report quantitative BPC impacts and confidence intervals rather than only qualitative samples.  
- Key missing test: show whether higher layers add capabilities beyond capacity by using parameter- and unit-matched baselines and depth sweeps trained from scratch.  
- Baseline fairness: report exact parameter breakdowns (input, recurrent, inter-layer, output) and include a single-layer RNN explicitly matched on total parameters.  
- Scope of baselines: include/discuss related Wikipedia-scale character/compression results (Mikolov et al.) to contextualize contributions.  
- Pros for acceptance: timely exploration, solid empirical performance with simple training, and insightful temporal analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient controlled comparisons disentangling depth vs. parameter count, missing ablations/theoretical grounding for training choices, and suggestive but inconclusive interpretability.  
- Suggestions: add ablations on gradient normalization vs. clipping/none, depth-from-scratch experiments with parameter matching, test deeper per-timestep transitions, expand related work, report quantitative BPC deltas with uncertainty, and clarify compute/data comparisons.  
- Minor comments: define DRNN-1O/DRNN-AO at first mention, tighten explanation of “bifurcations” and exact normalization rule, and verify dataset/character set/preprocessing consistency.  
- Overall: promising and well-presented study that would be significantly strengthened by stronger parameter-matched baselines, ablations, and broader depth comparisons.
