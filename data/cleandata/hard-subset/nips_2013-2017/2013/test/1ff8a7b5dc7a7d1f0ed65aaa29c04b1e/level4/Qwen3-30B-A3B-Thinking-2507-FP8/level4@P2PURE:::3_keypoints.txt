- Older data may receive more processing steps than newer data; deep RNNs are reasonable for short-term tasks and adding nonlinear layers between timesteps is a suggested alternative to explore.  
- The role and significance of gradient normalization and its connection to avoiding bifurcations need clearer explanation; cite recent ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/optimization.  
- Clarify whether gradient normalization disrupts SGD’s inherent “averaging” behavior.  
- Compare results on Wikipedia compression to Mikolov et al.’s findings.  
- Text generation results (Table 2) are the most convincing experimental evidence.  
- Interpreting “brain surgery” layer ablations is problematic due to complex interdependencies between layer outputs.  
- Major empirical gap: no definitive evidence that higher layers truly process more abstract/long-term properties versus identical units placed in the first layer.  
- Parameter-count imbalance: standard RNN uses fewer parameters here, making comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Stronger validation would show deeper RNNs outperforming standard RNNs with identical parameter or unit counts (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs. standard RNN with same units).  
- Figure 2 should be replaced or augmented with results showing performance across DRNN depths trained from scratch, adjusting layer widths to account for parameter/unit differences.  
- Minor: Define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the main text rather than only in Figure 1.  
- Overall: DRNN architecture shows strong performance and the paper is well-written and honest, but the above reservations should be addressed.
