

The authors propose a novel deep architecture that integrates hierarchical structures from deep learning with time-series modeling techniques derived from HMMs or RNNs. The training algorithm constructs the network layer-by-layer via supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction objective. Experiments demonstrate that after training extremely large networks for approximately 10 days, the model achieves improved performance on the Wikipedia dataset introduced by Hinton et al., surpassing prior work. The paper further provides a detailed analysis of the network's operational mechanisms, revealing that higher layers model long-term dependencies and that pairs of opening/closing parentheses are encoded as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."

The paper is well-executed: it is clearly written, experiments are rigorously designed, and figures effectively support the findings. However, my primary concern lies with the experimental evaluation. The comparison is conducted solely on one dataset (the Hinton Wikipedia corpus), which lacks substantial prior work for benchmarking. More critically, the baseline comparison is restricted to RNN variants, omitting hierarchical HMMs—standard approaches for similar sequence modeling tasks. Table 1 cites alternative methods, but they operate on different corpora, making them inherently incomparable. This limits the paper’s ability to establish the architecture’s relative advantages.

The paper’s strongest contribution is its insightful analysis of *how* the network operates, which is thorough and well-executed. However, this analysis diverges from the paper’s stated objective: proposing a new architecture that combines hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling. The empirical results, while suggestive, do not conclusively demonstrate the architecture’s superiority over established alternatives. The performance gain on a single dataset—without meaningful comparison to hierarchical HMMs or other relevant baselines—leaves the practical significance of the proposed model ambiguous. Consequently, the contribution appears more analytical than architectural, contradicting the paper’s primary claim. The analysis is valuable in its own right, but the results fail to robustly validate the architecture’s novelty or efficacy as presented.
