- Suggestion to try truncated backpropagation with more steps (e.g., 30) to potentially match entropies at much lower computational cost.  
- Paper shows deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is held constant.  
- Analysis indicates deeper networks retain more long-term information in their deeper layers.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the claim that HF optimization is necessary.  
- Claim of introducing the deep RNN architecture is incorrect; prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) and earlier studies exist.  
- The paper should remove the claim of introducing the architecture and instead acknowledge and build on prior work.  
- Results are interesting but possibly task-specific; the analysis should be validated on multiple problems to demonstrate generality.  
- Recommend validating findings on speech-processing tasks to strengthen generalizability claims.  
- HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT (used since the 1990s and by Mikolov) could likely yield similar results and should be tested.  
- Overall: two main contributions—deeper RNNs perform better under fixed parameter budgets, and deeper layers capture longer-range structure—yet broader validation and correct attribution are needed.
