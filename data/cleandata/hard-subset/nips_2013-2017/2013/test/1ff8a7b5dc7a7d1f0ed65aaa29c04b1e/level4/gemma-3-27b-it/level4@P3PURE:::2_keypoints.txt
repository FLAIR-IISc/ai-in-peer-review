- Deep RNNs outperform standard RNNs when holding the number of parameters constant (on the character-level language modeling task).  
- Deeper layers exhibit more long-range / long-term information.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the claim that Hessian-Free optimization is required.  
- Claiming to introduce the “deep RNN” is misleading; similar architectures exist (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) and prior work should be acknowledged.  
- The paper should emphasize its analysis and insights rather than presenting the architecture as novel.  
- The analysis is limited to a single character-level task, so findings may be task-specific artifacts.  
- Results would be more compelling if validated on other problems (e.g., a speech task) to demonstrate generalizability.  
- The Hessian-Free experiments may be needlessly expensive; truncated BPTT (TBPTT) has been effective historically (Mikolov) and might perform comparably.  
- Authors should evaluate TBPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) to test its ability to learn parentheses and to compare entropy vs computational cost.  
- Even if TBPTT fails on parentheses, it could still yield competitive entropies at much lower computational cost, so exploring it is worthwhile.
