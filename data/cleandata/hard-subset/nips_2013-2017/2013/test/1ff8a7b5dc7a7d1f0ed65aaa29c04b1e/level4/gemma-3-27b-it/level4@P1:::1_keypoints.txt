- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with recurrent time-series modeling.  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised training algorithm for next-character prediction.  
- Trained very large networks (~10 days) and reports state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus.  
- Analyzes internal mechanisms, showing higher layers model long-term dependencies.  
- Identifies representation of parentheses matching as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Paper is well-written, experiments are well-designed, and figures effectively support findings.  
- Main concern: evaluation is limited to a single dataset, hindering assessment of generalizability.  
- Comparisons are mostly to other RNNs; lacks direct comparison to hierarchical approaches like HMMs.  
- Table 1 lists other methods but on different corpora, making direct comparison difficult.  
- Primary contribution is insightful analysis of how the network operates, more than achieving a novel SOTA.  
- Results are somewhat limited in scope despite proposing a novel architecture.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, competitive results, strong internal analysis, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited dataset evaluation, insufficient comparison to relevant methods, modest performance gains.
