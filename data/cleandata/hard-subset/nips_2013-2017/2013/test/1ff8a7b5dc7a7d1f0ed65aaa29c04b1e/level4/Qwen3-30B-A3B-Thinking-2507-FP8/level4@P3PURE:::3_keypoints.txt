- Presents a well-motivated exploration of DRNNs for text prediction/compression and the idea that older data benefits from deeper processing.  
- Proposal to build deeper RNNs for improved short-term modeling is reasonable and well-argued.  
- Suggestion to test adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps (beyond standard DRNN) as a straightforward extension that may improve performance.  
- Gradient normalization needs clarification: explain why it “avoids bifurcations” and how it interacts with SGD averaging; cite relevant ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/optimization in RNNs.  
- Benchmarking is incomplete: compare results to Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia compression baseline.  
- Text generation experiments (Table 2) are the most persuasive evidence of layer roles in time-scale/abstraction.  
- Interpreting layer-removal “brain surgery” effects is problematic due to complex interdependencies formed during training.  
- Major omission: no direct evidence that higher layers specifically benefit from their hierarchical position (need relocation/reassignment experiments).  
- Parameter-count comparison is unfair: standard RNN has many more parameters; comparisons should match total parameters or unit counts.  
- Recommend comparing DRNNs of varying depth trained from scratch with width adjusted to equalize parameter budgets (Figure 2 should show this).  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the main text rather than only in Figure 1.  
- Overall: paper is honest, well-written, largely convincing, and a worthwhile contribution but requires additional analyses to address the reservations above.
