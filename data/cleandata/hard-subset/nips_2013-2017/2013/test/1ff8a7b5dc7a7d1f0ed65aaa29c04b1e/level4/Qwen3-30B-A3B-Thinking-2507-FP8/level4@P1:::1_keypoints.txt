- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the hidden state from the layer below.  
- Claims DRNNs naturally capture temporal hierarchies in time series (short-scale vs long-scale dependencies) better than standard RNNs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) using only SGD, avoiding Hessian-free optimization.  
- Empirically shows different layers encode distinct time scales: bottom layers model short-term statistics, top layers capture long-term dependencies.  
- Shows DRNNs learn "pseudo-stable attractor-like" states (e.g., parentheses handling), with DRNN-AO closing brackets correctly 88% vs 72% for RNNs.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work but focuses on analyzing emergent time scales rather than only improving accuracy.  
- Comparison to prior SOTA (Hinton et al.) is well-motivated; DRNN-1O outperforms Hessian-free MRNN while using simpler training.  
- Strength: rigorous technical analysis and convincing time-scale experiments (typo perturbation, context-switching, parentheses closure).  
- Strength: clear presentation and novel emphasis on emergent temporal processing; clear distinction and analysis of DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO.  
- Weakness: limited scope of comparison — lacks non-RNN hierarchical baselines (e.g., hierarchical/deep HMMs); PAQ/Shannon benchmarks not directly comparable.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in results — DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O without clear explanation of architectural vs optimization causes.  
- Weakness: reproducibility and robustness issues — long training (~10 days on one GPU for 1.4B chars) and missing ablation studies (layer/neuron counts).  
- Recommendation: Accept — important contribution demonstrating that stacking RNNs creates natural temporal hierarchies with sound results and clear writing.  
- Pro: novel architecture revealing emergent time-scale hierarchy, rigorous layer-specific analysis, SOTA results with simple SGD.  
- Con: limited non-RNN comparisons, unexplained DRNN-AO underperformance, high training cost.
