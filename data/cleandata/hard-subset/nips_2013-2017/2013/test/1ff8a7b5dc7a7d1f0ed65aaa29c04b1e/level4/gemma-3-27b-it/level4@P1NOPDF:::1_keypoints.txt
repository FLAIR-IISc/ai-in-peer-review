- Proposes a novel deep architecture that integrates hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-style).  
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Trained very large networks for ~10 days on a Wikipedia dataset (Hinton et al.) and reports improved performance over existing approaches.  
- Provides detailed analysis of the learned representations, showing long-term dependencies are captured in higher layers.  
- Finds specific phenomena such as opening/closing parentheses represented as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Paper is well-written, experiments are well-designed, and figures effectively support findings.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, not widely used dataset.  
- Comparisons are mostly against RNN-based models, omitting hierarchical HMMs and similar relevant methods.  
- Table 1 lists other methods evaluated on a different corpus, preventing direct comparison.  
- Contribution emphasizes analysis of how the network operates more than delivering definitive state-of-the-art results; results are somewhat inconclusive due to limited experimental scope.
