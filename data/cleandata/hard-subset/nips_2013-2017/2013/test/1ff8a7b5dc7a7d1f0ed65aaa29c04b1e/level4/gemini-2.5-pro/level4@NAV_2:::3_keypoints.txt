- Proposes stacking RNN layers (DRNN) to add intra-time-step hierarchical processing, addressing a perceived limitation of standard RNNs.  
- Strong empirical result: 5-layer DRNN trained with simple SGD achieves state-of-the-art on character-level Wikipedia modeling.  
- Thorough and insightful analyses showing emergent layer time scales and capability to capture long-term dependencies (e.g., matching parentheses).  
- Well-written and clear presentation, with transparent discussion of analysis limitations.  
- Baseline comparison weakness: matching parameter counts but different parameter distributions; unclear if benefits come from depth or parameter allocation.  
- Suggests need for comparisons to single-layer RNNs matched by number of units or tests with fewer DRNN layers to isolate the effect of depth.  
- Recommends training DRNNs of varying depths with constant parameter budgets to directly measure performance scaling with depth.  
- Requests more detail and intuition on the gradient normalization heuristic, its relation to exploding/vanishing gradients, and its effect on SGD averaging.  
- Asks whether alternative ways to add per-step depth (e.g., feed-forward layers inside the recurrent update) were considered and how they compare.  
- Suggests broader related-work comparisons for the Wikipedia task (e.g., Mikolov et al.) and a minor clarity fix: define DRNN-1O/DRNN-AO in the main text before figure captions.
