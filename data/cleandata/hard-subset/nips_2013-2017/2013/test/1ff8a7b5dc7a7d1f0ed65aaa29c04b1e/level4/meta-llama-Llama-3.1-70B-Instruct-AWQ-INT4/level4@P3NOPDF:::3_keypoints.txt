- Proposes a novel Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture combining deep and recurrent nets, demonstrating strong text prediction/compression results.  
- Key insight: older data may benefit from more layers of processing than newer data, motivating deep recurrence to improve short-term tasks and other benefits.  
- Suggests possible extension: add extra nonlinear layers between time steps; asks if authors explored this.  
- Highlights use of gradient normalization in DRNNs; requests elaboration on how it avoids bifurcations.  
- Notes related work on gradient truncation/optimization in RNNs (ICML 2013) should be cited and discussed.  
- Raises concern that gradient normalization might disrupt SGD’s implicit averaging behavior and requests discussion.  
- Recommends comparing results to Mikolov et al. on similar Wikipedia compression tasks for context.  
- Praises experiments probing layer roles (time-scale and abstraction) but warns interpreting them is problematic due to interdependencies.  
- Finds the text generation experiment persuasive but emphasizes its limitations.  
- Calls for experiments that directly test whether higher layers genuinely capture more abstract/long-term properties versus simply relocating units to lower layers.  
- Argues the DRNN vs RNN comparison may be unfair due to parameter count differences; requests comparisons with equal-parameter or equal-unit baselines.  
- Suggests replacing Figure 2 with plots showing DRNN performance vs depth (trained from scratch), potentially widening layers to match parameter counts.  
- Minor suggestions: define DRNN-AO and IO in the text and add more experiments to strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, honest about limitations, and a valuable contribution to studying deep temporal networks.
