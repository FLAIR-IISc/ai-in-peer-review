- Rebuttal focuses on a single task; suggest evaluating truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) which may yield competitive entropies at lower cost even if it struggles with parentheses.  
- Paper finds that, with fixed parameter count, deeper RNNs outperform standard (shallower) RNNs on character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper layers capture more long-range (long-term) information than shallower layers.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the belief that Hessian-free (HF) optimization is required.  
- Claiming to have introduced deep RNNs is incorrect; this architecture appeared in prior work (e.g., Graves ICASSP 2013) and should not be presented as novel.  
- The paper’s contribution should be framed as analysis of deep RNNs rather than invention of the architecture.  
- HF experiments may be overly expensive; truncated BPTT (per Mikolov) might achieve similar results and should be tested to confirm.  
- Impact would be stronger if the analysis were replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to demonstrate generality and rule out task-specific artifacts.
