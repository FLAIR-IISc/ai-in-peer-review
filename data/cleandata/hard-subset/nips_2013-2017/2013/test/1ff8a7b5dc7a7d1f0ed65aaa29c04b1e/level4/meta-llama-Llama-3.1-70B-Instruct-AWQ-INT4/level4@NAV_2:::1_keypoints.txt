- Proposes a novel Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that combines hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling.  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised (pre-)training algorithm using next-letter prediction.  
- Thoroughly analyzes DRNN behavior, showing modeling of long-term dependencies and temporal hierarchies.  
- Demonstrates ability to learn pseudo-stable, attractor-like states.  
- Experiments on a Wikipedia-based corpus show state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks.  
- Figures and visualizations effectively support and clarify findings.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Experimental design is strong but limited in scope.  
- Comparison is restricted to a single dataset (Wikipedia), limiting generality.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited to RNN-related models, omitting hierarchical HMMs and other relevant architectures.  
- Performance advantages of DRNN are not conclusively demonstrated; results are somewhat inconclusive.  
- The paper provides valuable analysis and potential significance for character-level language modeling.  
- Would benefit from more comprehensive evaluations and broader architectural comparisons.
