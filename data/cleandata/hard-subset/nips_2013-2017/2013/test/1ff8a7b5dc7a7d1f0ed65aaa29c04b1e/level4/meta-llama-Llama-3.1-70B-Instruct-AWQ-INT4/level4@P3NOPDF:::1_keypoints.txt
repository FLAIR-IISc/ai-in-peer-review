- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning structure with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-like).  
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- After ~10 days of training, the model outperforms prior work on Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia dataset.  
- Detailed analysis shows higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses pairing emerges as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like” state.  
- Paper is well written with clear presentation, thorough analysis, and effective figures.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, little-studied dataset.  
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models and omit hierarchical HMMs and other comparable approaches.  
- Table 1 reports other methods on a different corpus, making direct comparisons invalid.  
- Main contribution appears to be the behavioral analysis rather than conclusive performance gains.  
- Recommendation: strengthen empirical validation with more datasets and broader baseline comparisons.
