This paper introduces a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture that combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal modeling capabilities of recurrent networks. The authors investigate the effect of stacking recurrent layers, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, and demonstrate its application to character-level language modeling. They train these networks using stochastic gradient descent and report state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, alongside an analysis of the emergent time scales within the network.

The core idea of the DRNN – creating a hierarchy of recurrent processing – builds upon existing work in both deep learning and recurrent neural networks. Deep neural networks [4, 17] have demonstrated the power of hierarchical feature extraction, while recurrent networks [11] excel at processing sequential data. This work attempts to integrate these strengths, addressing a limitation of standard RNNs which often lack explicit hierarchical processing. Related work employing hierarchical RNNs has been explored previously [3, 5, 6], but often on artificial tasks or with a primary focus on performance rather than architectural analysis, as noted by the authors regarding [8].

The paper’s strengths lie in its clarity and thorough analysis. The writing is well-organized and explains the architecture effectively. The experimental setup is well-defined, and the figures appropriately support the findings. The analysis of time scales and the behavior of the network regarding long-term dependencies (e.g., parentheses matching) provides valuable insights into *how* the DRNN operates. The observation that higher layers model longer-term dependencies and the description of parentheses handling as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state” are particularly insightful. 

However, the evaluation could be strengthened. The experiments are currently limited to a single dataset, and direct comparison is primarily with other RNN-based models. While the authors acknowledge the existence of other hierarchical approaches like Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), a more direct comparison – even if on different datasets – would better establish the DRNN’s position within the broader landscape of sequential modeling techniques. The lack of comparison to established hierarchical HMMs or other relevant models limits the assessment of the novelty and significance of the approach. Furthermore, the significant training time (10 days) raises questions about computational cost and scalability, which could be addressed with a discussion of computational resources and potential optimization strategies.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated architecture (DRNN) combining the strengths of deep learning and recurrent neural networks.
*   The authors achieve state-of-the-art performance on a challenging character-level language modeling task.
*   The detailed analysis of the network's behavior, particularly regarding time scales and long-term dependencies, is a significant contribution.
*   The writing is clear and the presentation is well-structured.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, hindering broader generalization.
*   The comparison to alternative hierarchical models (e.g., HMMs) is lacking.
*   The computational cost of training is substantial and warrants further discussion.
*   While the analysis is strong, the ultimate impact of the architectural improvements could be more convincingly demonstrated with a wider range of experiments.



**Overall:** This is a solid paper with a clear contribution to the field. The analysis is particularly strong, providing valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent networks. While the evaluation could be broadened, the presented results are compelling. I believe this paper is suitable for NIPS, particularly given the strong analytical component.
