- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNNs) forming a temporal hierarchy and applies them to character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus.  
- Main contributions: state-of-the-art empirical results using simple SGD and an in-depth analysis of emergent hierarchical representations.  
- Strength — Strong empirical results: 5-layer DRNN with SGD matches prior SOTA that required Hessian-free optimization, suggesting easier training.  
- Strength — In-depth analysis: experiments (time-scale analysis, parenthesis closing, qualitative text samples) indicate higher layers capture longer-range, more abstract dependencies.  
- Strength — Clarity and honesty: well-written, well-organized, and transparent about analysis limitations (e.g., difficulties interpreting ablation).  
- Strength — Significance: validates stacking RNNs for hierarchical temporal processing and offers a foundation for further research.  
- Weakness — Baseline/parameter-count issues: parameter counting appears inconsistent and comparisons to the standard RNN may be unfair; need comparisons to shallow RNNs with comparable parameter counts.  
- Weakness — Depth analysis incomplete: experiments don’t prove hierarchy is necessary; should report performance vs. depth normalized by parameter count.  
- Weakness — Training justification weak: gradient normalization is treated as a heuristic and should be related to formal work (e.g., gradient clipping) and its effects discussed.  
- Weakness — Related work coverage incomplete: should include broader contemporaneous RNN LM results (e.g., Mikolov et al.) for fuller context.  
- Minor — Terminology: DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O are used before being defined; define in Section 2.2.  
- Minor — Architecture alternatives: authors should discuss how adding extra nonlinearities between timesteps in a standard RNN might compare to their approach.  
- Conclusion: Strong, timely paper with compelling results and analysis, but its claims would be strengthened by fairer baseline comparisons and deeper causal evidence for benefits of depth.
