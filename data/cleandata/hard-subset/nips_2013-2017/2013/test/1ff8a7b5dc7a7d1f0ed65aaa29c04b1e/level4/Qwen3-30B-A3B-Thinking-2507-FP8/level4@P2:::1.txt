

The authors propose Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a hierarchical architecture where each layer is an RNN receiving the hidden state of the layer below as input. This design explicitly models temporal hierarchies, enabling layered processing of time series across multiple time scales. The paper demonstrates that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus), using only SGD for training—previously requiring advanced methods like Hessian-free optimization. Key contributions include: (1) empirical validation of temporal hierarchy emergence across layers, (2) analysis of layer-specific time scales via perturbation experiments (e.g., typo propagation, parenthesis closure), and (3) demonstration that higher layers capture long-term dependencies (e.g., maintaining opening parenthesis context for hundreds of characters).

**Quality**: The work is technically sound. Experiments are well-designed: ablation studies isolate layer contributions (Fig. 2), time-scale analysis uses controlled perturbations (Fig. 3), and generative tests validate long-term memory (Table 2). Results are robust across three network instantiations. However, the primary comparison (Table 1) relies solely on RNN variants (e.g., MRNN), omitting established hierarchical models like HMMs or CRFs that solve similar tasks. The claim of "state-of-the-art" is relative only to RNNs, not the broader language modeling landscape (e.g., PAQ compression [14] achieves lower BPC but on different metrics). The 10-day training duration for large networks (4.9M parameters) also lacks efficiency analysis versus alternatives.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written. The DRNN architecture is clearly defined (Section 2), with Figure 1 effectively illustrating the hierarchical structure. The analysis of time-scale emergence (Section 3.3) is particularly compelling, using concrete experiments to explain *how* layers operate at different temporal resolutions. The generative text examples (Table 2) and parenthesis analysis (Fig. 4) further enhance readability. Minor improvements could include clarifying why DRNN-AO slightly underperforms DRNN-1O (Section 3.2) and explicitly stating the computational cost of their SGD approach versus Hessian-free methods.

**Originality**: The DRNN architecture itself is not novel (cited as used in [8] for TIMIT). The paper’s true innovation lies in *analyzing* how the architecture inherently creates temporal hierarchies—a departure from prior work focused solely on performance. While [5] proposed hierarchical RNNs for artificial tasks, this paper provides the first empirical analysis of time-scale emergence in real-world language modeling. However, the paper’s positioning as a *new architecture* misrepresents the contribution; the core advance is the analysis, not the architecture. Related work (e.g., [3, 5, 6]) is adequately cited, but the comparison to non-RNN hierarchical models (e.g., [13] Echo State Networks) is insufficient.

**Significance**: The analysis of temporal hierarchies offers meaningful insights for understanding RNN dynamics, potentially guiding future architecture design. The demonstration of long-term memory (e.g., parenthesis closure) validates DRNNs’ utility for tasks requiring multi-scale temporal reasoning. However, significance is limited by the narrow scope: results are confined to one dataset (Wikipedia), and the paper does not address whether DRNNs generalize to other temporal tasks (e.g., speech recognition [7, 17]). The claim that DRNNs "automatically create different time scales" is well-supported, but the broader impact on the field remains unclear without comparisons to non-RNN hierarchical approaches.

**Strengths**:  
- Rigorous analysis of temporal hierarchies via layer-specific perturbations.  
- Clear demonstration of DRNNs’ ability to model long-term dependencies (parentheses, context switching).  
- Well-structured experiments with strong visual support (Figures 2–4).  
- Practical validation of SGD sufficiency (vs. Hessian-free methods).  

**Weaknesses**:  
- Overstates architectural novelty; contribution is primarily analytical.  
- Limited dataset scope (only Wikipedia corpus) and lack of comparison to non-RNN hierarchical models.  
- "State-of-the-art" claim is misleading without broader benchmarking.  
- Training cost (10 days on a single GPU) is not contextualized against alternatives.  

**Recommendation**: Accept. The paper makes a valuable contribution by revealing *how* stacked RNNs naturally develop temporal hierarchies—a critical insight for RNN research. While the architectural novelty is overstated, the analysis significantly advances understanding of recurrent dynamics. The work is technically robust, well-written, and provides a foundation for future studies on hierarchical temporal modeling. To strengthen future versions, the authors should: (1) clarify the architecture’s novelty versus prior work, (2) expand comparisons to non-RNN hierarchical models, and (3) contextualize computational costs. This paper is a strong fit for NIPS, advancing the field’s mechanistic understanding of recurrent networks.
