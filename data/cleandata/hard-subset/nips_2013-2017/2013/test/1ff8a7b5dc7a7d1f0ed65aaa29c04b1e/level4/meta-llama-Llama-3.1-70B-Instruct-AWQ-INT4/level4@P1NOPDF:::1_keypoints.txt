- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-like).
- Training algorithm builds the network layer-by-layer using supervised next-letter prediction pre-training.
- Trained very large networks (~10 days) and achieved state-of-the-art performance on a specific Wikipedia dataset used by Hinton et al.
- Detailed analysis shows higher layers capture long-term dependencies and a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state" encodes matching parentheses.
- Writing is clear, experiments are well-designed, and figures effectively support findings.
- Major concern: experiments use only a single, little-studied dataset, limiting generality.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models and omit hierarchical HMMs and other relevant approaches.
- Table 1 lists other methods but on a different corpus, making direct comparison invalid.
- Main contribution is the thorough analysis of network behavior rather than conclusive empirical advances.
- Overall: well-written and well-analyzed, but results and experimental validation are somewhat inconclusive.
