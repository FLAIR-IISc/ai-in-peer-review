The authors propose a novel deep architecture that integrates the hierarchical structure of deep learning with the time-series modeling capabilities of recurrent neural networks (RNNs). This architecture, termed deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), is evaluated on the task of character-level language modeling. The proposed training algorithm involves building the network layer-by-layer using supervised (pre-)training with a next-letter prediction objective.

The experiments demonstrate that, after training very large networks for approximately 10 days, the network performance on a Wikipedia dataset published by Hinton et al. improves over previous work. The authors then proceed to analyze and discuss the details of how the network approaches its task. For example, they find that long-term dependencies are modeled in higher layers and that correspondences between opening and closing parentheses are modeled as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state".

The paper is of good quality, with clear writing, well-designed experiments, and supporting figures that effectively convey the findings. However, my main issue with the paper is the limited scope of the experiments. The comparison is only conducted on one dataset, which does not appear to have been extensively studied. Furthermore, the direct comparison is limited to other RNN-related models, without considering alternative hierarchical models, such as Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), that can solve similar tasks. Although Table 1 lists other approaches, they are evaluated on a different corpus, making them incomparable.

In my opinion, the contribution of the paper lies more in the analysis of how the network operates, which is conducted quite well, rather than in the results achieved. However, this is not the primary focus of the paper, which aims to propose a new deep architecture. While the paper is well-written, and the model is analyzed thoroughly, the results are somewhat inconclusive, and further experimentation on diverse datasets and comparison with other models would strengthen the paper's claims.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by experimental results. However, the evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which reduces the paper's impact.

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical. The figures effectively support the findings, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

**Originality:** The proposed architecture is novel, and the combination of hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling is an innovative approach. However, the analysis of the network's operation, while thorough, does not provide significantly new insights.

**Significance:** The results are somewhat inconclusive, and the impact of the paper is reduced due to the limited scope of the experiments. Further experimentation and comparison with other models would be necessary to demonstrate the significance of the proposed architecture.
