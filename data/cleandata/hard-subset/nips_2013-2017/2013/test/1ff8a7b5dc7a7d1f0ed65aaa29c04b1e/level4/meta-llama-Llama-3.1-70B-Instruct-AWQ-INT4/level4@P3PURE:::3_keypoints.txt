- Paper proposes deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) to give older inputs more layers of processing, potentially improving short-term tasks and other benefits.  
- Suggestion to try adding extra nonlinear layers between time steps as a potential extension.  
- Use of gradient normalization is notable, but its overall importance and claim of avoiding bifurcations need clearer justification and discussion.  
- Cite and relate to recent ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/optimization in RNNs.  
- Concern that gradient normalization might disrupt SGD’s averaging behavior and have unintended effects.  
- Recommend comparing results on Wikipedia compression to Mikolov et al.’s prior work.  
- Experiments probing layer roles are well-designed; text generation results (Table 2) are most persuasive.  
- Warning that “brain surgery” interpretations are problematic due to complex interdependencies across layers.  
- Missing empirical test: whether higher layers truly capture more abstract/long-term properties versus simply relocating units to the first layer.  
- Comparison to a regular RNN may be unfair due to parameter/size mismatch; recommend comparisons with equal-parameter or equal-unit baseline RNNs (and varying DRNN depth/width trained from scratch).  
- Minor fixes: define DRNN-AO and IO in the text (not only in Figure 1).  
- Overall assessment: well-written, honest, and valuable exploration of deep temporal networks deserving further empirical clarification.
