- Agree with focusing on a single task, but recommend trying truncated backpropagation (~30 steps) as a cheaper verification.  
- Truncated BPTT may not learn perfect parenthesis balancing but could reach competitive entropies at lower cost.  
- Main empirical finding: with equal parameter budgets, deeper RNNs outperform standard (shallower) RNNs in character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper layers in the network capture more long-range (long-term) information.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to the claim that HF optimization is required.  
- The paper’s claim to have introduced deep RNNs is incorrect—similar architectures appeared previously (e.g., Graves ICASSP 2013).  
- The analysis would be more convincing if applied to multiple problems (not just one), especially speech tasks, to show generality.  
- HF experiments may be computationally expensive; historically, truncated BPTT (used by Mikolov) has been effective and should be compared.  
- Recommend confirming whether truncated BPTT yields comparable results to HF to validate practical trade-offs.
