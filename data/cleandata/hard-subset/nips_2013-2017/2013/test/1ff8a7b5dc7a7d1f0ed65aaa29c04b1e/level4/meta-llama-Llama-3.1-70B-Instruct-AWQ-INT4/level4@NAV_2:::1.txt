This paper proposes a novel deep architecture that integrates the hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling, commonly found in Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) or Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The authors introduce a training algorithm that constructs the network layer-by-layer using supervised (pre-)training for a next-letter prediction objective.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture, exploring its capabilities in character-level language modeling. The experiments demonstrate that the DRNN achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, surpassing the results obtained by a common RNN. The authors also present an in-depth examination of the network's behavior, including its ability to model long-term dependencies, capture temporal hierarchies, and learn pseudo-stable attractor-like states.

The paper is well-written, and the experiments are well-designed. The figures effectively support the findings, providing valuable insights into the network's operation. However, the comparison is limited to a single dataset, and the authors only compare their results with other RNN-related models. A more comprehensive evaluation would benefit from comparisons with hierarchical HMMs or other architectures that can solve similar tasks.

While the paper's contribution lies in its analysis of how the network operates, the results are somewhat inconclusive. The proposed DRNN architecture is innovative, but its performance advantages are not conclusively demonstrated. Nevertheless, the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field, and its analysis of the DRNN's behavior is thorough and well-presented.
