- Studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) trained with plain SGD (gradient norm normalization, decaying LR) and incremental layer-wise training, achieving competitive BPC on Wikipedia.
- Situates work relative to hierarchical RNNs, deep LSTMs, and character-level RNNs; suggests citing Pascanu et al. (ICML 2013) on gradient issues.
- Strength: Clear architectural proposal with multiple probes showing layer-dependent temporal hierarchies.
- Strength: Competitive performance using simple SGD rather than Hessian-free optimization.
- Strength: Compelling qualitative evaluations (generative ablations, parentheses experiment) demonstrating long-term dependency capture beyond BPTT window.
- Strength: Sensible and careful incremental training procedure for DRNN-1O.
- Weakness: Unclear parameter accounting and baseline fairness—need clarification on how ~4.9M parameters were counted and stronger single-layer comparisons with matched units/params.
- Weakness: Missing controlled experiments on depth vs. width tradeoff (performance across 1–5 layers with matched parameter budgets).
- Weakness: Claims about gradient normalization preventing “bifurcations” need quantification and ablation (compare normalization vs clipping, with/without).
- Weakness: Layer ablation interpretations limited by interdependencies; need complementary probes (receptive-field masking, linear probes/CCA, single-layer-driven outputs).
- Weakness: Missing baselines such as deep-transition single-layer RNNs and gated models (e.g., LSTM) with comparable parameter counts.
- Weakness: Comparisons to prior LM/compression work incomplete; clarify differences with Mikolov et al. and characterize impact of using 96 vs 86 characters.
- Quality: Methodology and training described well; main methodological gaps are baseline controls, gradient normalization ablations, and parameter accounting.
- Clarity: Paper is well written but should define DRNN-AO/DRNN-1O at first mention, use consistent notation, and explain output-layer initialization.
- Originality: Stacking RNNs is not novel, but combining simple DRNN+SGD+systematic temporal analysis is a useful original contribution.
- Significance: Demonstrates that simple stacked RNNs can match more complex training methods on character-level modeling; stronger baselines would increase impact.
- Suggested fixes: Add depth-controlled comparisons, gradient normalization ablations, deep-transition and gated baselines, clarify parameter/training costs, and expand related-work discussion.
- Pros summary: Clear idea, solid time-scale analysis, competitive results, and well-written presentation.
- Cons summary: Insufficient depth/width controls, no gradient-normalization ablation, and limited interpretability of layer ablations.
- Overall: Strong, informative paper; recommend acceptance pending clarification of parameter accounting and, if possible, additional depth/normalization ablations.
