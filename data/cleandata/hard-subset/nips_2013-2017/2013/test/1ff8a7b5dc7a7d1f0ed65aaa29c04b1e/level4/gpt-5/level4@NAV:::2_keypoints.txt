- Reviewer agrees that evaluating on a single task is a limitation.
- Suggests trying truncated backpropagation (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper alternative worth testing.
- Paper studies the utility of deep recurrent neural networks.
- Finding: with a fixed parameter budget on character-level language modeling, deeper RNNs outperform standard (shallow) RNNs.
- Finding: deeper layers retain more long-term information.
- Finding: plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs that HF was required.
- Criticism: the paper incorrectly claims to introduce deep RNNs—similar architectures appeared earlier (e.g., Graves 2013).
- Suggestion: it is acceptable to focus on analysis rather than claim a new architecture.
- Concern: results may be specific to the chosen task; evaluation on additional tasks (e.g., speech) would strengthen the paper.
- Comment: HF experiments were likely more costly than necessary; truncated BPTT (used by Mikolov) might achieve similar results and should be confirmed.
