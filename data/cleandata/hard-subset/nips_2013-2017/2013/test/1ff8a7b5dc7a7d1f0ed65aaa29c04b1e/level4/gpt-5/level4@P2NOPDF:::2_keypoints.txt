- Reviewer agrees single-task evaluation is a concern and limits generality.
- Suggests trying truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) as a much cheaper baseline; it may match entropies even if it fails at strict parenthesis balancing.
- Paper's main empirical findings: deeper RNNs outperform single-layer RNNs under a fixed parameter budget.
- Deeper layers in the model capture longer-range information.
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting prior belief that HF was required.
- The paper should not claim architectural novelty—similar deep RNN architectures exist (e.g., Graves ICASSP 2013).
- Results shown on a single dataset; replicating on additional domains (ideally speech) would strengthen impact and rule out dataset-specific artifacts.
- HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; include truncated BPTT baselines and report compute/performance trade-offs.
- Recommendations to strengthen the paper: (a) correct novelty claims, (b) validate findings on another domain like speech, and (c) include truncated BPTT baselines to contextualize HF.
