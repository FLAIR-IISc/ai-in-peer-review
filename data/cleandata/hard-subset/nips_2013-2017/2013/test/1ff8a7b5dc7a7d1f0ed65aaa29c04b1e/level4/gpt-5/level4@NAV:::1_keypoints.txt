- Introduces a new deep architecture that merges hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (HMMs/RNNs).
- Training is incremental, layer-by-layer, using supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction objective.
- Large models trained for ~10 days achieve performance surpassing previous results on Hinton et al.'s Wikipedia dataset.
- Analysis shows higher layers capture long-range dependencies and that parentheses correspondence appears as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state.
- Paper is solidly written; experimental setup is sound and figures effectively support the claims.
- Principal concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset with relatively little prior work.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models and omit direct comparisons to hierarchical HMMs or other approaches on the same corpus.
- The main contribution is stronger in explaining how the network operates than in the magnitude of empirical advances.
- Empirical results therefore remain somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper’s stated emphasis.
