- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with HMM/RNN-style time-series modeling.  
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pre-training with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Experiments on a Wikipedia dataset (from Hinton et al.) show improved performance after ~10 days of training.  
- Provides detailed internal-analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses correspondence forms a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Paper is well-written, experimental setup is sound, and figures effectively support findings.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset studied by few others, restricting generalizability.  
- Comparisons are mostly to RNN-based models; relevant baselines (e.g., hierarchical HMMs) are omitted.  
- Table 1 lists other approaches but uses a different corpus, making results incomparable.  
- Main contribution appears to be the analysis of network behavior rather than demonstrably superior performance.  
- Conclusion: interesting model and thorough analysis, but limited experimental validation makes performance claims inconclusive.
