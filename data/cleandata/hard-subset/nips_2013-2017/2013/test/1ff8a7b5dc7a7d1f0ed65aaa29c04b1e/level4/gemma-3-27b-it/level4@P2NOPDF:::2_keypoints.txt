- Paper shows deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling when parameter counts are controlled.  
- Deeper layers within these RNNs capture more long-range dependencies in the data.  
- Plain Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contra claims that more complex methods are required.  
- Claim of introducing deep RNNs is inaccurate; such architectures were explored earlier (e.g., Graves et al., 2013).  
- Paper should reframe its contribution as a detailed analysis of deep RNNs rather than their introduction.  
- Analysis is valuable but limited in scope; results may be specific to character-level modeling and not broadly generalizable.  
- Extending experiments to other domains (e.g., speech recognition) would strengthen claims of generality.  
- Experiments with Highway Networks appear computationally expensive and may be suboptimal.  
- Truncated BPTT (well-established and used by Mikolov) could be a more efficient alternative and should be tested for comparable performance.  
- Main highlighted contributions: (1) depth advantage under fixed parameter budgets, (2) deeper layers model longer-range structure, (3) BPTT alone can solve parentheses balancing.
