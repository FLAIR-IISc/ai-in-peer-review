- Clear motivation and a straightforward, implementable DRNN architecture that deepens the recurrent transition.
- Strong empirical results on text prediction/compression with analyses; text-generation ablation most persuasive for layer specialization.
- Missing baseline: try adding depth inside the per-timestep transition (deeper nonlinearity) to separate “transition depth” from stacked recurrence.
- Clarify the role and necessity of gradient normalization with ablation tests (with/without normalization).
- Define “avoid the problem of bifurcations” and provide empirical signals that bifurcations are being avoided.
- Relate and compare conceptually and empirically to Pascanu/Mikolov/Bengio (ICML 2013) on exploding/vanishing gradients and clipping/truncation.
- Discuss whether gradient normalization alters SGD noise/averaging and provide experiments (update variance, convergence behavior).
- Ensure fairness: provide controlled comparisons matched for total parameter count and number of hidden units (e.g., widened single-layer RNN vs. DRNN).
- Show depth vs. width trade-offs from scratch: performance vs. depth with shallower models widened to match parameter counts.
- Provide stronger evidence that higher layers capture longer-term/abstract processing (unit relocation/shuffling, probing horizons, layer-freezing experiments, surrogate tasks).
- Compare more directly to prior Wikipedia compression work (e.g., Mikolov et al.) or normalize/discuss differences in datasets/preprocessing.
- Clarify terminology (define DRNN-AO and IO in main text) and give training details for gradient normalization (thresholds, norms, where applied).
- Report variance across runs for key results to account for sensitivity to initialization and optimization noise.
- Overall: interesting and timely work with thoughtful analyses, but central claims about depth need tighter, parameter-matched controls and deeper analysis of gradient normalization and higher-layer abstraction.
