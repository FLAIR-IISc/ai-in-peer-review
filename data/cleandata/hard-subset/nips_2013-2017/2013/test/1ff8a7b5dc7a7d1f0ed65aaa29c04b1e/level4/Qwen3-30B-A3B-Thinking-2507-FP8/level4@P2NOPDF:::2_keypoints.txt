- Agree with the authors' rebuttal on the single-task focus but recommend testing truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) to assess computational efficiency and possible competitive entropy with lower cost.  
- Paper’s three main findings: (1) deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs at fixed parameter counts on character-level LM; (2) deeper layers capture more long-range structural information; (3) standard BPTT enables character-level RNNs to balance parentheses previously attributed only to HF.  
- The claim of "introducing" the deep RNN architecture is incorrect—similar architectures appear in Graves et al. (ICASSP 2013) and earlier work; phrasing should acknowledge prior art.  
- Results are limited to character-level language modeling and may be task-specific; evaluating on a different domain (e.g., speech recognition) is needed to establish generality.  
- HF experiments appear computationally excessive; truncated BPTT (a long-established technique, used by Mikolov 2010) should be tested as a more efficient alternative for the parentheses task.  
- Recommended fixes: (1) correct the novelty claim, (2) expand evaluation to at least one additional task, and (3) compare HF vs. truncated BPTT for efficiency—without these, the work risks being viewed as a narrow case study.  
- The core insights about depth improving performance and long-range information retention are promising but require broader empirical validation to confirm wider relevance.
