- Paper studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network and shows SOTA character-level language modeling (1.472 BPC) with simple SGD.  
- Strong, technically sound analysis of emergent time scales using perturbation/typo experiments and ablations (Figures 2–4).  
- Empirical evidence that deeper layers retain longer-term context (e.g., parentheses context for 200+ characters).  
- Demonstrates plain SGD can train RNNs to model long-term dependencies without Hessian-free methods, with clear training methodology.  
- Novelty lies in systematic analysis of hierarchical time scales across layers, not in the architecture itself.  
- Incorrectly claims to “introduce” DRNNs—identical stacked bidirectional LSTMs were used by Graves et al. (ICASSP 2013); framing should be “revisiting/analyzing.”  
- Evaluation is limited to character-level language modeling and may not generalize; cross-domain experiments (e.g., TIMIT speech) are needed.  
- Methodological omission: no comparison to truncated BPTT (standard practice, e.g., Mikolov), weakening the efficiency/SGD claim.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept—valuable analysis but overstates novelty and scope; needs minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: reframe claims about novelty, add truncated BPTT comparison, and include at least one cross-domain experiment to show generality.
