- Agree with focusing the paper on a single task.  
- Suggest exploring truncated BPTT with fewer steps (e.g., ~30) as a lower-cost alternative that may be competitive.  
- Paper shows deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter count is fixed.  
- Deeper layers capture more long-range structure.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior belief.  
- Claim of introducing the deep RNN architecture is inaccurate—similar architectures exist (e.g., Graves 2013); revise the claim and focus on analysis.  
- Analysis would be more significant if demonstrated across multiple problems (e.g., speech) to show generality.  
- Hessian-free experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT (used by Mikolov) might yield similar results and should be tested.  
- Recommend extending experiments to speech tasks to strengthen evidence for deeper RNN benefits and long-range information in deeper layers.
