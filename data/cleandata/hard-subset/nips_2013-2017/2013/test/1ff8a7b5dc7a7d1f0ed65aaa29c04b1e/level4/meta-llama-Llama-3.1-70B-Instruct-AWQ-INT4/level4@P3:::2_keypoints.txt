- Paper analyzes deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling with a hierarchical recurrent layer architecture.  
- Each DRNN layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state, enabling hierarchical temporal processing and capturing time-series structure.  
- Evaluated on a Wikipedia text corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level modeling using simple SGD.  
- Provides analysis of emergent time scales, showing deeper layers capture longer-range structure.  
- Main contributions: (1) deep RNNs outperform standard RNNs at fixed parameter counts; (2) deeper layers exhibit more long-range dependencies; (3) plain BPTT can train RNNs to balance parentheses without HF optimization.  
- Strengths: thorough architectural analysis, real-world task evaluation, and detailed comparison with prior work.  
- Weakness: claim of introducing the DRNN architecture is incorrect—similar architectures exist in prior work.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single task (character-level language modeling), reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: Hessian-free (HF) experiments appear computationally expensive; truncated BPTT (TBPTT) would be a reasonable alternative to explore.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revising the claim about introducing the architecture.  
- Suggestion: encourage authors to extend analysis to other tasks (e.g., speech recognition) for broader validation.  
- Suggestion: consider using TBPTT instead of HF in future experiments to reduce computational cost.
