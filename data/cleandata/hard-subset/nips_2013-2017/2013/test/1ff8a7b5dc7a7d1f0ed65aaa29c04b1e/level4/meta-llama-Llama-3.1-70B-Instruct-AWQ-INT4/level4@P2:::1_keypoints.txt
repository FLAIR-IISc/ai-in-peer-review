- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) combining hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling.  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  
- Paper is well-written with well-designed experiments and clear, informative figures.  
- Provides thorough analysis of DRNN’s ability to model long-term dependencies and temporal hierarchies.  
- Shows DRNN outperforms a common RNN and achieves state-of-the-art for recurrent networks on the task.  
- Insightful behavioral analysis: emergence of different time scales across layers and parentheses as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Main limitation: evaluation limited to a single dataset, reducing generalizability.  
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models; lacks comparison to other hierarchical models (e.g., HMMs).  
- Contribution emphasizes analysis of network operation more than demonstrating clear performance gains.  
- Results are somewhat inconclusive regarding whether DRNN offers significant advantages over other models.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by experiments; organization and clarity are strong.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
