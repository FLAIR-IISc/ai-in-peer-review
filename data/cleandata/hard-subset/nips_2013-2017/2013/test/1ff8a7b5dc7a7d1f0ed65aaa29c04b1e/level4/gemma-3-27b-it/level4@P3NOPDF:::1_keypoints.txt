- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical feature learning with HMM- and RNN-like time-series modeling.  
- Trained layer-by-layer with supervised next-letter prediction pre-training.  
- After ~10 days of training on a large dataset, shows improved results on a Wikipedia text corpus.  
- Provides detailed analysis of internal representations, finding long-term dependencies in higher layers and balanced structures as “pseudo-stable attractor-like states.”  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and figures effectively support findings.  
- Experimental setup is robust but evaluation is limited to a single dataset.  
- Direct comparisons are mainly against RNNs; missing comparisons to hierarchical HMMs and other non-RNN sequential models.  
- Table 1 lists alternatives evaluated on different corpora, preventing meaningful direct comparison.  
- Main contribution is insightful analysis of model behavior rather than broad, state-of-the-art performance.  
- Unclear if gains stem from architectural novelties or from network scale and pre-training.  
- Very long training time (~10 days) raises practical concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, require additional comparisons to relevant baselines or justification, and discussion of training-time practicality.
