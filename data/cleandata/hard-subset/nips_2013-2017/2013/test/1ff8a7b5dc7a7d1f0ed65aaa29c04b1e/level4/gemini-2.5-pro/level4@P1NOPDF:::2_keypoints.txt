1. Paper shows deep RNNs can outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling at fixed parameter budgets.  
2. Analysis indicates deeper layers capture more long-term information.  
3. Demonstrates standard BPTT can train an RNN to solve a parenthesis balancing task previously thought to require HF optimization.  
4. Conclusion: depth can be more beneficial than width for a fixed parameter budget.  
5. Claim of introducing the deep RNN architecture is inaccurate; similar architectures (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) predate this work.  
6. Analysis is limited to a single task, so findings may not generalize beyond character-level language modeling.  
7. Recommend adding experiments on other domains (e.g., speech recognition) to test generality.  
8. Reliance on computationally expensive HF optimizer is a limitation; need baseline experiments with truncated BPTT.  
9. Reviewer still stands by the single-task critique and requests truncated BPTT results (e.g., 30-step truncation), noting it may not solve the parenthesis task but could match language-modeling performance at lower cost.
