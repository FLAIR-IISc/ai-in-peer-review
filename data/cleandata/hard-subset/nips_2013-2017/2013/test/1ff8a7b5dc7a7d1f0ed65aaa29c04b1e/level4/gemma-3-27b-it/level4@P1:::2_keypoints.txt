- Deeper recurrent networks outperform standard RNNs when the number of parameters is held constant.
- Deeper layers appear to capture longer-range dependencies in the data.
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, countering the need for advanced optimizers.
- The DRNN architecture itself is not novel (e.g., Graves et al., 2013).
- Analysis is limited to a single task (character-level language modeling) and may not generalize.
- Applying the analysis to other temporal tasks (e.g., speech recognition) would strengthen the claims.
- Authors acknowledge but do not run Hessian-free training due to computational cost.
- The paper omits experiments with truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps), which could be a cheaper competitive alternative.
- Strength: sound analysis with clear experimental evidence and useful insights into DRNN behavior.
- Weakness: limited novelty, narrow experimental scope, and missing TBPTT comparisons.
- For acceptance: provides valuable, well-supported insights and would improve with minor revisions.
- Against acceptance: limited impact from non-novel architecture, lack of generalization tests, and missed simpler-training baselines.
