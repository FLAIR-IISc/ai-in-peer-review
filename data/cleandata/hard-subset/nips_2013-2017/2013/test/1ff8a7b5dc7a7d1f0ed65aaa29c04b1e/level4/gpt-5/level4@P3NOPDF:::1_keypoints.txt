- Introduces a deep architecture combining hierarchical feature learning with time-series modeling, trained layer-wise on next-character prediction.
- Shows improved results on a Wikipedia dataset (Hinton et al.) after training very large networks for ~10 days.
- Provides insightful analysis of learned dynamics: higher layers capture longer-term dependencies and exhibit attractor-like states for structures like parentheses.
- Clear motivation for combining hierarchical representations with temporal modeling.
- Well-written paper with informative figures and careful presentation.
- Analyses effectively illuminate how different layers encode dependencies and long-range structure.
- Demonstrated empirical performance improvement on the referenced Wikipedia dataset.
- Layer-wise supervised pretraining is coherently described and appears practically useful for deep temporal models.
- Evaluation is limited to a single, uncommon dataset, making generality unclear.
- Baselines are mostly RNN-related; no direct comparisons to hierarchical/segmental HMMs or other structured sequence models.
- Table 1 mixes results from different corpora, complicating direct comparisons to the reported Wikipedia results.
- Performance gains are promising but not sufficiently contextualized to claim clear state-of-the-art advances.
- Training cost is very high (~10 days) and the paper lacks discussion on efficiency, scalability, and depth–capacity–accuracy trade-offs.
- Suggested broader evaluation on standard benchmarks and inclusion of non-RNN baselines (hierarchical HMMs, strong n-gram/neural LMs).
- Request to report detailed training settings, parameter counts, hardware, wall-clock time, learning curves, and multiple runs with variance.
- Recommend ablation studies isolating depth, layer-wise pretraining, and end-to-end fine-tuning effects.
- Ask for clarification on handling very long contexts and whether attractor-like states generalize across datasets.
- Key open questions: whether end-to-end fine-tuning is done and its effect; sensitivity to depth/hidden size/pretraining objective; direct comparisons to structured models; exact training resources and throughput; robustness of attractor states across domains.
- Overall: strong qualitative insights and clear writing, but empirical validation is too narrow; broader, more rigorous experiments would strengthen the contribution.
