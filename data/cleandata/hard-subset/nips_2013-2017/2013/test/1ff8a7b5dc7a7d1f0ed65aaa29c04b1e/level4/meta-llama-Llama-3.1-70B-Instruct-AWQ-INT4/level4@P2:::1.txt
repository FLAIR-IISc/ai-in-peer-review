This paper proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture that combines the hierarchical features of deep learning with time-series modeling. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia text corpus. The paper is well-written, and the experiments are well-designed, with clear and informative figures that support the findings.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of their proposed architecture, including its ability to model long-term dependencies and capture temporal hierarchies in the data. The results show that the DRNN outperforms a common recurrent neural network (RNN) and achieves state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on the given task. The analysis of the network's behavior, including the emergence of different time scales in the layers and the modeling of parentheses as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state," is particularly insightful.

However, the main limitation of the paper is the lack of comprehensive evaluation and comparison with other models. The experiments are limited to a single dataset, and the comparison is only made with other RNN-related models. It would be beneficial to include comparisons with other hierarchical models, such as Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), to provide a more complete picture of the DRNN's performance.

Additionally, the paper's contribution is more focused on the analysis of how the network operates rather than its overall performance. While the analysis is thorough and well-done, it is not entirely clear what the paper sets out to achieve in terms of performance. The proposed architecture is novel and interesting, but the results are somewhat inconclusive, and it is unclear whether the DRNN offers significant advantages over other models.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by experimental results. The writing is clear, and the organization is logical. However, the significance of the results is somewhat limited by the lack of comprehensive evaluation and comparison with other models.

Overall, I would rate the paper as follows:

* Quality: 8/10
* Clarity: 9/10
* Originality: 8/10
* Significance: 7/10
