- Reviewer agrees with the concern about the paper focusing on a single task.  
- Recommend experimenting with truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) to reduce computation and verify performance, including parentheses balancing.  
- Paper studies deep RNNs and finds deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter count is fixed.  
- Deeper networks are shown to retain more long-term information than shallower ones.  
- Contrary to prior claims, standard BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses (not only HF optimization).  
- The paper incorrectly claims to have introduced the deep RNN architecture; prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) exists.  
- The analysis is meaningful but risks being task-specific unless replicated on additional problems (suggested: speech).  
- HF experiments were likely computationally expensive and need comparison to cheaper alternatives.  
- Truncated BPTT has historical precedent (1990s, Mikolov) and may perform well here—this should be tested.  
- Paper’s primary contributions summarized: (1) deep RNNs beat standard RNNs for character-level modeling, (2) deeper layers capture longer-range dependencies, (3) truncated BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses.
