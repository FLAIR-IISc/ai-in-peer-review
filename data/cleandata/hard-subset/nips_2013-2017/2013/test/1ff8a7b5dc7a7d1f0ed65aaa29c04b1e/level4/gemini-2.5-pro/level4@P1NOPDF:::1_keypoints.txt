- Proposes a novel deep architecture that combines hierarchical feature learning with time-series modeling (like HMMs/RNNs).  
- Uses layer-by-layer supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction task.  
- Experiments on a large Wikipedia dataset (previously used by Hinton et al.) show the model outperforms prior work after extensive training (~10 days).  
- Provides detailed internal analysis: higher layers capture long-term dependencies and matching parentheses are handled via “pseudo-stable attractor-like states.”  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, with thoughtful experiments and clear, supportive figures.  
- Main concern: experimental evaluation is narrow, relying on a single dataset not widely used as a benchmark.  
- Direct performance comparisons are limited to other RNN-based models; relevant alternatives (e.g., hierarchical HMMs) are not directly compared.  
- Table 1 compares models evaluated on different corpora, preventing fair, direct comparisons.  
- Primary contribution appears to be the insightful analysis of how the network operates rather than clear advances in performance.  
- Framing the work as a new high-performing architecture is somewhat at odds with the emphasis on analysis.  
- Conclusion: interesting model and compelling analysis, but the narrow experimental scope undermines the conclusiveness of the results.
