- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks multiple RNN layers to combine hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling.
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining with a next-letter prediction objective.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses figures and tables effectively.
- Experiments are well set up and figures support the reported findings.
- After extensive training (~10 days), the DRNN outperforms previous work on a Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset.
- Analysis shows higher layers capture long-term dependencies and models like parentheses correspondence form pseudo-stable attractor-like states.
- Main weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset with little related prior work on it.
- Comparisons are restricted to other RNN models and omit hierarchical HMMs or other competitive architectures.
- Table 1 lists other approaches but those results are on different corpora, making comparisons invalid.
- Contribution is stronger in analyzing network behavior than in demonstrating clear performance or generalizability gains.
- Results are somewhat inconclusive regarding overall performance and generalizability of the architecture.
- Quality: technically sound and claims are well-supported by theory and experiments.
- Originality: proposes a novel architecture and provides thorough analysis, though stacking RNNs is not entirely new.
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and has potential to advance time-series and language modeling.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, thorough analysis, and improved results over prior work on the tested dataset.
- Arguments for rejection: limited experimental scope and inconclusive evidence of generalizability.
- Suggestions: include comparisons with other architectures, evaluate on multiple datasets, and give more details on training and hyperparameter tuning.
