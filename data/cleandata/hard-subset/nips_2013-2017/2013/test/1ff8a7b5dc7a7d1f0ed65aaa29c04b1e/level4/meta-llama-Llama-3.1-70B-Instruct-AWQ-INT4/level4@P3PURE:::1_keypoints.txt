- Proposes a novel deep architecture that combines hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (similar to HMMs/RNNs).  
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Large-scale experiments: after ~10 days of training, the model outperforms prior work on Hinton et al.'s Wikipedia dataset.  
- Provides in-depth analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and a “pseudo-stable attractor-like” state for matching parentheses.  
- Paper is well written with clear exposition, solid experiments, and supportive figures.  
- Primary concern: experiments are limited to a single, relatively understudied dataset.  
- Comparisons are narrow—only against RNN-related models; hierarchical HMMs and other alternative approaches are omitted or evaluated on different corpora.  
- Contribution is stronger in analysis and interpretation of model behavior than in conclusive empirical advances.
