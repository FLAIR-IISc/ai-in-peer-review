- Shows deeper recurrent networks (DRNNs) with a fixed parameter budget outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling.  
- Finds deeper networks capture more long-term dependencies; standard BPTT can train character-level tasks (e.g., parenthesis balancing) without Hessian-free optimization.  
- Situates work within substantial prior RNN/DNN literature and acknowledges related stacked LSTM results.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with intuitive explanation of multiple timescales.  
- Strength: Empirical results are competitive with methods using advanced optimization (Martens, 2012; Martens & Sutskever, 2013).  
- Strength: Insightful analysis (typo perturbation, parenthesis task) that reveals internal representations and long-range dependency learning.  
- Strength: Detailed architecture and training setup that facilitate reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Claimed novelty of the DRNN architecture is overstated given prior work (e.g., Graves et al., 2013).  
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to character-level language modeling; limited evidence of generalization to speech or other time-series tasks.  
- Weakness: Computationally expensive Hessian-free experiments; no comparison to truncated BPTT as a cheaper alternative.  
- Argument for acceptance: clear analysis, strong empirical performance, and valuable time-scale insights.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficiently novel framing, narrow evaluation scope, and missing truncated BPTT comparison.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on revisions to acknowledge prior DRNN work and, ideally, include or discuss truncated BPTT comparisons.  
- Overall rating: Weak Accept.
