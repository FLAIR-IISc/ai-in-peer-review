- Paper shows DRNNs achieve strong text prediction/compression and presents generally convincing experiments that higher layers capture more abstract, longer-term structure; writing is clear and limitations are acknowledged.
- Insight: in standard RNNs older inputs can be processed by more layers than newer ones; deeper RNNs can improve short-term modeling.
- Suggestion: try adding per-time-step depth (additional nonlinear transformations between successive timesteps).
- Question: how important was gradient normalization to results, by what mechanism does it “avoid bifurcations,” and could it interfere with SGD’s averaging; note relevance of a 2013 U. Montreal ICML paper on gradient truncation/optimization.
- Request: compare Wikipedia compression results to Mikolov et al.
- Praise: text generation experiment (Table 2) is the most persuasive probe of layer roles, but interpreting layer “surgery” is tricky due to complex interdependencies.
- Major missing experiment: show whether higher layers truly benefit from antecedent processing by comparing same units placed in lower vs higher layers (controlled test for nontrivial benefit of depth).
- Fairness concern: parameter counts appear mismatched across models; recommend matching models by total parameters or, preferably, by number of units.
- Experimental suggestion: train various DRNN depths from scratch and widen shallower models to compensate for parameter/unit differences, instead of (or in addition to) current Figure 2.
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and IO in the main text, not only in Figure 1.
