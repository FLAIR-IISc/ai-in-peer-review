- Agree with focusing on a single task but recommend testing truncated BPTT with fewer steps (e.g., ~30) as it may reach competitive entropies at lower computational cost.  
- When holding total parameters fixed, deeper recurrent networks outperform standard (shallow) RNNs on character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper networks tend to capture more long-term information / longer-range structure in their deeper layers.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the claim that Hessian-free optimization is required.  
- The paper’s claim to introduce deep RNN architectures is incorrect—similar architectures were explored previously (e.g., Graves 2013).  
- The HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT (used by Mikolov) likely produces similar results and should be tested.  
- The analysis would be more convincing if replicated on multiple problems, especially a speech task, to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Overall contribution: interesting analysis with two main findings (deep RNNs outperform when parameter-matched; deeper layers encode longer-range structure), but needs broader evaluation and correction of novelty claims.
