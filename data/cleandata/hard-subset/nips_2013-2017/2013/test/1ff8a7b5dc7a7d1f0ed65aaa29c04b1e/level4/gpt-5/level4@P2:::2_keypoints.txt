- Paper studies stacked RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) trained with SGD/BPTT on large Wikipedia char LM (~4.9M params) and matches prior HF-trained recurrent models.
- Main contribution is empirical analysis (layer-wise ablations, perturbation tests, temporal hierarchies) rather than architectural novelty.
- Must temper claims of introducing “deep RNN” and cite prior stacked/hierarchical RNN work more explicitly.
- Strength: deeper recurrent architectures outperform single-layer RNNs under a fixed parameter budget for char LM.
- Strength: simple SGD/BPTT training (with a practical layer-wise schedule) achieves competitive results to HF-trained models.
- Strength: insightful analyses reveal emergent layer specialization to different time scales and qualitative generation of long-range patterns.
- Weakness: all experiments are on a single dataset/task, so generalization across domains is unclear.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to LSTMs/gated RNNs and to truncated BPTT baselines.
- Weakness: training confound—DRNN-1O uses incremental layer-wise training while DRNN-AO and RNN do not, complicating attribution of gains.
- Weakness: limited ablation on depth vs width trade-offs and no reporting of variance across random seeds.
- Experimental quality is generally sound, but some claims need qualification and HF comparisons should report compute/data passes.
- Clarity issues: clarify gradient "normalization" method and justify the chosen BPTT truncation (250 steps with first 50 discarded).
- Clarity issue: explain difference in vocabulary size (95 vs 86) and its effect on BPC comparability.
- Originality is limited—stacked RNNs are known; contribution is systematic analysis showing learned temporal hierarchies and SGD effectiveness.
- Significance: practically useful results showing benefit of depth and that SGD suffices for large-scale char LM, but broader impact needs more tasks/baselines.
- Suggestions: add truncated BPTT baseline, include LSTM comparisons, control for incremental training schedule, explore depth/width trade-offs and seed variance, and test on a second domain.
- Suggestions: report compute/GPU-days and effective epochs, and release code/hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: clear empirical benefit of depth, strong analyses of temporal hierarchies, and showing SGD/BPTT can match HF results.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, single-task evaluation, and missing key baselines/confounded training procedures.
- Overall: informative empirical/analytical paper that leans toward accept if novelty claims are toned down and at least one strong baseline or second task is added.
