This paper proposes a novel deep architecture that integrates the hierarchical structure of deep learning models with the temporal modeling capabilities of methods like Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). The proposed training algorithm constructs the network in a layer-by-layer fashion, using a supervised pre-training scheme based on a next-letter prediction objective. Experiments on a Wikipedia dataset demonstrate that a large-scale version of this network, trained for approximately ten days, achieves improved performance over previous RNN-based approaches. A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to analyzing the network's internal mechanisms, revealing how it learns to handle complex temporal structures. For instance, the authors show that higher layers capture long-term dependencies, while phenomena like matching opening and closing parentheses are modeled through what they describe as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."

The paper is of high quality, characterized by clear writing, a sound experimental setup, and figures that effectively support the findings. The analysis of the model's internal representations is particularly insightful and well-executed.

However, the primary weakness lies in the experimental evaluation. The performance claims are based on a single dataset, which does not appear to be a widely used benchmark, making it difficult to contextualize the results and assess the generalizability of the proposed architecture. Furthermore, the direct comparison of results is restricted to other RNN-related models. The paper does not include a comparison with other relevant classes of models capable of solving similar tasks, such as Hierarchical HMMs. While Table 1 lists results from other methods, these are reported on a different corpus, which prevents a direct and meaningful comparison.

In conclusion, the paper's main contribution seems to be its detailed analysis of *how* the proposed network functions, rather than a decisive demonstration of its superior performance. While this analysis is valuable, it is somewhat misaligned with the paper's primary framing as a proposal for a new state-of-the-art architecture. The paper presents an interesting model and a compelling analysis, but the empirical results are inconclusive due to the limited scope of the experimental evaluation.
