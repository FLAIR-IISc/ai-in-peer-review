- Novel deep architecture that integrates hierarchical deep networks with time-series modeling techniques (HMM/RNN ideas).  
- Training algorithm builds the network incrementally via supervised, layer-wise pre-training using a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Experimental results show performance improvements on a Wikipedia dataset after training very large networks for ~10 days.  
- Detailed analysis indicates higher layers model long-term dependencies.  
- Analysis observes balanced parentheses encoded as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, and supported by clear figures and well-designed experiments.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, relatively understudied dataset.  
- Comparisons are confined to RNN-based models and omit relevant alternatives like hierarchical HMMs.  
- Table 1 lists other approaches but uses a different corpus, preventing direct comparison.  
- Main contribution is mechanistic analysis rather than clearly demonstrated performance gains.  
- Conclusion: architecture effectively combines hierarchical feature learning with time-series modeling, but results remain inconclusive due to limited experimental validation.
