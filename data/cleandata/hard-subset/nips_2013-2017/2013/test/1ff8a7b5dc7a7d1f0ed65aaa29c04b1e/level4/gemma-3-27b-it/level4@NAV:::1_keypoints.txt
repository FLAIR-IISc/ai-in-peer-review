- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep networks with time-series modeling techniques (HMM/RNN ideas).  
- Training uses layer-wise construction with supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Conducts extensive experiments (≈10 days training for large models) showing performance gains on a Wikipedia dataset.  
- Provides in-depth analysis: higher layers capture long-term dependencies and balanced structures form “pseudo-stable attractor-like states.”  
- Paper is well-written with high-quality experiments and clear, illustrative figures.  
- Major concern: evaluation is limited to a single, relatively underexplored dataset.  
- Comparisons are restricted mainly to RNN-based models; alternative methods (e.g., hierarchical HMMs) are evaluated on different corpora, preventing direct comparison.  
- Main contribution is detailed analysis of network behavior rather than a definitive performance breakthrough.  
- Results are promising but remain somewhat inconclusive due to the narrow scope of experimental validation.
