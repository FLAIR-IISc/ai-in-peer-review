- Novel deep architecture combining deep learning hierarchies with time-series modeling techniques (HMMs/RNNs).
- Training algorithm builds the network incrementally layer-by-layer via supervised next-letter prediction pre-training.
- Experiments show improved performance over prior work after ~10 days of training on the Wikipedia dataset.
- Network analysis finds long-term dependencies encoded in higher layers.
- Matching parentheses are represented as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state" in the model.
- Paper is high quality: clear writing, well-designed experiments, and supportive figures.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Wikipedia) with limited prior benchmarking.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-based models and omit hierarchical HMMs and other standard approaches.
- Table 1 cites methods on different corpora, making quantitative comparisons invalid.
- Contribution emphasizes analysis of network dynamics, but claims of a new architecture are not conclusively supported by the experiments.
