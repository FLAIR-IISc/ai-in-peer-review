The paper "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" studies the effect of a hierarchy of recurrent neural networks on processing time series. The authors propose a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture, where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture allows for hierarchical processing on difficult temporal tasks and more naturally captures the structure of time series.

The authors show that DRNNs reach state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks in character-level language modeling when trained with simple stochastic gradient descent. They also offer an analysis of the different emergent time scales in the DRNN. The results demonstrate that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies and capture temporal hierarchies.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture and its performance on character-level language modeling. The results are interesting and provide new insights into the capabilities of DRNNs.
