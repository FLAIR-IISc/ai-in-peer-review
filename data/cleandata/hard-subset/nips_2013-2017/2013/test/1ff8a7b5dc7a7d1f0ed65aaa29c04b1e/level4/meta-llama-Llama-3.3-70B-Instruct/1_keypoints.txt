- Introduces a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling, reminiscent of HMMs/RNNs.
- Proposes a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training algorithm using a next-letter prediction objective.
- Large networks trained (~10 days) outperform prior RNN-related work on a Wikipedia dataset originally published by Hinton et al.
- Analysis shows higher layers model long-term dependencies and that parentheses matching appears as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."
- Paper quality is high: clear writing, well-designed experiments, and supportive figures.
- Primary experimental concern: evaluation limited to a single dataset with little prior work for comparison.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models; hierarchical HMM baselines that could address similar tasks are omitted.
- Table 1 lists other approaches on different corpora, making those comparisons invalid.
- The main contribution is the in-depth analysis of network mechanisms rather than strong, generalizable empirical gains.
- Results are somewhat inconclusive given the paper's objective of introducing a new architecture.
