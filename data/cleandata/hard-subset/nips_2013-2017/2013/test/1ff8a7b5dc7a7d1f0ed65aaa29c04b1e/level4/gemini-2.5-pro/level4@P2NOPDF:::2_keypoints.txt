- Reviewer remains concerned about the limited scope (single task) and urges broader evaluation.  
- Recommends experiments with truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper baseline that may reach competitive entropy scores.  
- Paper finding: deep RNNs outperform shallower RNNs when total parameter count is held constant.  
- Finding: deeper layers appear to capture more long-range or "long-term" dependencies.  
- Finding: standard full BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting claims that HF is required.  
- Strength: demonstrates depth-over-width benefits and provides evidence for hierarchical temporal representations.  
- Strength: correcting the misconception that HF optimization is necessary for the parentheses task is noteworthy.  
- Weakness: claim of novelty is overstated; deep RNN architectures were explored in prior work (e.g., Graves et al., ICASSP 2013) and should be repositioned.  
- Weakness: limited generalizability—results are from character-level language modeling and should be validated on other sequential domains (e.g., speech).  
- Weakness: missing key baseline—truncated BPTT is widely used and computationally efficient and should be included for a practical comparison.
