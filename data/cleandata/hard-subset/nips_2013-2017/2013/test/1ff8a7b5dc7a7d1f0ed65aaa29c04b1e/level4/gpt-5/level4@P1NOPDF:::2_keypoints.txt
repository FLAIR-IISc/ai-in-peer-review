- Reviewer agrees with the single-task concern.  
- Recommend trying truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) as a lower-cost alternative that may achieve competitive entropies even if it cannot learn parentheses.  
- Paper finding: with constant parameter budget, deeper RNNs outperform single-layer RNNs on character-level language modeling.  
- Finding: deeper layers appear to capture more long-term information.  
- Finding: plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs favoring HF optimization.  
- Paper incorrectly claims to introduce the deep RNN architecture; prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) used stacked/deep RNNs.  
- Analysis is limited to a single task; results should be validated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to ensure generality.  
- HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT has prior success (Mikolov) and should be considered.  
- Recommend including experiments comparing truncated BPTT to full BPTT to evaluate accuracy–cost trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and useful analysis with compelling empirical depth advantage and insightful layer-wise long-range information results.  
- Suggested revisions: (a) remove claims of architectural novelty, (b) extend analysis to at least one additional task (ideally speech), (c) add careful comparison to truncated BPTT.
