- Proposes a deep architecture combining hierarchical feature learning with time-series modeling (inspired by HMMs and RNNs).  
- Trains the network layer-by-layer using supervised pretraining on a next-character prediction task.  
- Large models trained ~10 days achieve state-of-the-art performance on the Wikipedia dataset used by Hinton et al.  
- Provides detailed behavioral analysis showing higher layers capture long-range dependencies.  
- Demonstrates matching-parentheses representation via quasi-stable, attractor-like dynamics.  
- Paper is well written, experimental setup is sound, and figures effectively support claims.  
- Main empirical limitation: evaluation uses a single, infrequently used dataset, limiting generality of conclusions.  
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-style methods and omit classical hierarchical HMMs and related probabilistic sequence models.  
- Table 1 includes additional methods but those results are on a different corpus and thus not directly comparable.  
- Primary contribution is the thorough and insightful analysis of how the model solves tasks, not definitive empirical proof of architectural superiority.  
- Overall results are promising but inconclusive due to limited benchmark coverage and lack of diverse baselines.
