- Proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) stacking RNN layers hierarchically, each layer taking the hidden state from the layer below.
- Hypothesizes the architecture naturally captures temporal hierarchies at different time scales.
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks on this dataset using plain SGD with gradient clipping.
- Detailed analyses show different layers learn to operate on different time scales, with higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies.
- Case study demonstrates the model handles long-range structures (e.g., matching parentheses) via a “pseudo-stable attractor-like” state.
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well-presented, with effective figures and explanations.
- Strength: in-depth experimental analysis provides valuable insight into internal dynamics often missing in performance-focused work.
- Strength: strong empirical results using a simple training procedure make the architecture compelling.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset/task, leaving generality across sequential tasks unproven.
- Weakness: comparisons are mainly to other RNN variants; broader comparisons to other hierarchical sequence models are lacking and Table 1 mixes noncomparable results.
- Weakness: paper’s framing focuses on architectural novelty while its most significant contribution may be the representational analysis.
