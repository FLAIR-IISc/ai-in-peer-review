- Studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling and analyzes emergent time scales in deeper layers.  
- Shows DRNNs outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is fixed.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on a Wikipedia-based text corpus.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and provides insightful analysis of longer-range dependency capture.  
- Claim of having introduced the deep RNN architecture is incorrect (prior work such as Graves et al. 2013).  
- Analysis is limited to a single task, raising concerns about whether findings generalize to other domains (e.g., speech).  
- Lacks comparison with other common architectures such as LSTMs.  
- Does not discuss training limitations of plain BPTT, notably the vanishing-gradient problem for deep RNNs.  
- Originality: no new architectures/algorithms, but offers a novel analysis of emergent time scales.  
- Significance: demonstrates potential benefits of DRNNs for language modeling but generalization is unclear.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to correct claims, broaden evaluations or comparisons, and expand discussion of limitations and future work.
