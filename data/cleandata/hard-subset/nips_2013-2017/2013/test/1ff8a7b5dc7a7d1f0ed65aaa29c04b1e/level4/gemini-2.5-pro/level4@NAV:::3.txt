The authors make a compelling point regarding the asymmetry in standard RNNs, where earlier timesteps in a sequence undergo more layers of processing than later ones. The proposed deep RNN (DRNN) architecture is a logical approach to mitigate this issue and improve the modeling of short-term dependencies, among other potential benefits. As a point of clarification, have the authors considered or experimented with including additional nonlinear layers between each timestep, in addition to between the deep layers?

The paper should provide a more thorough motivation for the use of gradient normalization. What is its empirical importance to the final performance, and could the authors elaborate on the claim that this technique "avoids the problem of bifurcations"? In this context, it would be beneficial to discuss the relationship of this approach to recent work on gradient truncation and optimization in RNNs, such as the relevant ICML 2013 paper from the University of Montreal. Furthermore, is it possible that this normalization scheme could interfere with the averaging behavior inherent to Stochastic Gradient Descent?

For the Wikipedia compression tasks, the empirical evaluation would be better contextualized by a comparison to prior results from Mikolov et al. on similar benchmarks.

The series of experiments designed to probe the roles of different layers is insightful. The text generation experiment (Table 2) provides the most persuasive evidence for the hypothesis that layers specialize in different timescales and levels of abstraction. However, as the authors rightly acknowledge, interpreting the effects of such "brain surgery" on the network can be challenging due to the complex interdependencies that may have formed between layers during training.

A central empirical question that remains unanswered is whether the hierarchical structure itself is crucial for the observed specialization. Specifically, do the higher layers capture long-term, abstract properties more effectively *because* of their position in the hierarchy, or would a sufficiently wide single-layer RNN with a comparable number of units achieve a similar effect? The current evidence, while suggestive, is incomplete. The paper's claims would be significantly strengthened by an experiment demonstrating that higher layers benefit from the preceding levels of processing in a nontrivial way, beyond what could be achieved by simply increasing the capacity of the first layer.

The comparison with the standard RNN baseline appears to be confounded by a discrepancy in the number of parameters (e.g., a 5-layer DRNN has 5 * 2119^2 parameters versus a standard RNN with 2767^2 parameters, not accounting for input/output matrices). A more convincing comparison would benchmark the DRNN against a standard RNN with a matched number of parameters, or ideally, a matched number of units. For instance, comparing a 2-layer DRNN to a wider single-layer RNN with a similar parameter count would provide a much stronger validation of the architecture's benefits.

In lieu of Figure 2, a more informative analysis would present benchmark performance for DRNNs of varying depths, each trained from scratch. To ensure a fair comparison that isolates the effect of depth, shallower models in this analysis should be made wider to control for the total number of parameters and/or units.

Minor:
- The terms DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO are introduced in Figure 1 but should be explicitly defined in the main text.

This paper introduces a hybrid deep and recurrent architecture, the DRNN, which is shown to perform very effectively on text prediction and compression tasks. A significant portion of the work is dedicated to a series of experiments arguing that higher layers in the network learn to represent more abstract and long-term structures in the data. While these experiments are compelling, a few additional controls are needed to fully substantiate the claims. The investigation of such deep temporal models is a valuable research direction. The paper is well-written, clear, and demonstrates a commendable level of intellectual honesty by discussing its own potential shortcomings.
