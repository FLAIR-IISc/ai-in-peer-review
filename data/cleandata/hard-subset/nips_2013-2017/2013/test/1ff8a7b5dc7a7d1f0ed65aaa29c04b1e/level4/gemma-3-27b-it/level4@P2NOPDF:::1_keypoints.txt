- Novel architecture combining deep hierarchical representation learning with time-series modeling ideas from HMMs and RNNs.
- Layer-by-layer supervised training with per-layer next-letter prediction pre-training.
- Extensive training (~10 days on large compute) leads to improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset (surpassing prior reported results).
- Thorough analysis of internal network behavior showing long-term dependencies emerge in deeper layers.
- Discovery that matching parentheses are represented as “pseudo-stable attractor-like states.”
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, with clear experiments and illustrative figures.
- Major concern: evaluation is limited to a single dataset that lacks extensive prior work.
- Comparisons are largely to RNNs; missing comparisons to hierarchical HMMs and other sequential models.
- Table 1 comparisons are hard to interpret because different corpora were used.
- Main contribution is insightful analysis of network behavior rather than a large state-of-the-art performance gain.
- Results feel incremental given the high computational cost and long training time.
- Limited experimental scope and inconclusive performance gains reduce the overall impact.
