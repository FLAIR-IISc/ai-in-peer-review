- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers forming a temporal hierarchy that addresses multi-scale dependencies.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art recurrent-network performance on character-level language modeling using simple SGD.  
- Provides empirical analysis showing emergent layer-specific time scales via output perturbation, text generation, and context-switching tests.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work but contributes deeper empirical analysis distinct from performance-focused prior work.  
- Core insight—stacking RNNs creates temporal hierarchies—is compelling, technically sound, and well-motivated.  
- Results on parentheses closure and text generation convincingly show higher layers capture longer-term dependencies.  
- Parameter-count comparison is unfair: DRNN (~4.9M params) vs. smaller RNN (~2.3M) may conflate depth benefits with parameter scaling; suggest matching parameters/units.  
- Gradient normalization rationale is unclear/misstated (“bifurcations” vs. gradient explosions); authors should clarify mechanism and cite relevant work (e.g., Pascanu et al. ICML 2013).  
- Layer-removal interpretations risk overreach due to interdependencies; recommend acknowledging limitations and using complementary analyses (e.g., layer-wise input perturbations).  
- Experimental scope should include depth vs. width trade-offs (varying depths with matched parameter counts) to isolate benefits of depth.  
- Minor fixes: define acronyms (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) in main text, correct "IO" to "1O", and cite Mikolov et al. for language-modeling baselines.  
- Overall assessment: significant and novel contribution with strong experiments and clear writing; address fairness of comparisons and technical clarifications—Recommendation: Accept.
