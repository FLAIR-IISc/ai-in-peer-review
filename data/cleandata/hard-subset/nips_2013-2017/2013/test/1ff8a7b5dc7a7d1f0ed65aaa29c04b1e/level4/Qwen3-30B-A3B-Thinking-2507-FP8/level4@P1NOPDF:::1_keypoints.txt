- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN techniques).  
- Training algorithm builds the network layer-by-layer via supervised next-letter prediction pretraining.  
- Trained very large networks for ~10 days and achieved improved performance on the Hinton Wikipedia corpus, surpassing prior work.  
- Provides detailed analysis showing higher layers model long-term dependencies and parentheses pairs form a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is well-executed: clearly written, rigorously designed experiments, and effective figures.  
- Major concern: evaluation uses only a single dataset (Hinton Wikipedia), which lacks broad prior benchmarking.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited to RNN variants and omit hierarchical HMMs, a standard alternative for sequence tasks.  
- Table 1 cites methods on different corpora, making those comparisons inherently incomparable.  
- These evaluation limitations prevent establishing the architecture’s relative advantages or practical significance.  
- Strongest contribution is the operational analysis, which diverges from the paper’s primary claim of proposing a superior architecture.  
- Overall, the work is more analytical than architectural, and empirical results do not conclusively validate the model’s novelty or efficacy.
