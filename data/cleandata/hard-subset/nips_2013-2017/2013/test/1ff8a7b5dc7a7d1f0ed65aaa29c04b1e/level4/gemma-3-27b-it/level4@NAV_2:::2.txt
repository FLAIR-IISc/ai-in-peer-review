This paper investigates the usefulness of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), demonstrating that, with a fixed number of parameters, deeper recurrent architectures outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling. Key findings include an analysis revealing that deeper networks capture more long-term dependencies and the result that plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses – a task previously thought to require more advanced methods like Hessian-free optimization.

The paper’s strength lies in its empirical demonstration of the benefits of depth in recurrent networks and its analysis of the emergent time scales within these networks. The observation that DRNNs can learn to balance parentheses using a relatively simple training algorithm is particularly noteworthy. The authors connect their work to previous research in deep learning and recurrent neural networks, appropriately citing relevant publications. 

However, the claim of introducing the “deep RNN” is inaccurate. Hierarchical RNN architectures have been explored previously, for example, in Graves et al. (2013) [http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~graves/icassp_2013.pdf], and likely earlier. The paper would be strengthened by acknowledging this prior work and framing itself as an analysis of an existing architecture rather than its introduction. Furthermore, while the analysis on character-level language modeling is insightful, its generalizability is unclear. Conducting a similar analysis on another task, such as speech recognition, would bolster the conclusions and demonstrate whether the observed benefits extend beyond this specific problem domain. Finally, exploring more computationally efficient training methods, like truncated backpropagation through time (as successfully employed by Mikolov in his PhD thesis), would be valuable. Truncated BPTT could potentially achieve competitive performance at a reduced computational cost, and verifying this would add further practical insight.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Demonstrates performance improvements with increased depth in RNNs.
*   Provides analysis of emergent time scales within DRNNs, furthering understanding of their internal dynamics.
*   Successfully trains RNNs to perform a challenging task (parentheses balancing) with a simple optimization algorithm.
*   Clear writing and appropriate referencing.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The claim of introducing a novel architecture is inaccurate.
*   The analysis is limited to a single task, potentially limiting the generalizability of the findings.
*   Lack of exploration of more efficient training methods like truncated BPTT.

**Overall:** This work presents a valuable analysis of deep RNNs. Addressing the points raised regarding novelty, task diversity, and training efficiency would significantly strengthen the paper and enhance its contribution to the field.
