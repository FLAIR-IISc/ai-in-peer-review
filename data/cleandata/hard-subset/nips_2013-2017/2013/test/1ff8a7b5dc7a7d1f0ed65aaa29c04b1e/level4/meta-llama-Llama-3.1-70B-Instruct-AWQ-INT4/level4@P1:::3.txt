This paper explores the concept of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) and their application to character-level language modeling. The authors argue that common recurrent neural networks (RNNs) do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies, and propose a hierarchical architecture that combines the strengths of deep neural networks (DNNs) and RNNs.

The paper begins by introducing the concept of DRNNs, where each layer is a recurrent neural network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture allows for hierarchical processing of time series data and captures the structure of time series in a more natural way. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of DRNNs on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using simple stochastic gradient descent.

The authors also provide an in-depth analysis of the different emergent time scales in DRNNs. They show that the layer-wise time scales behave differently in the case of DRNNs with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O) and DRNNs with output connections at all layers (DRNN-AO). The DRNN-AO has very short time-scales in the three bottom layers and longer memory only appears for the two top ones, whereas in the DRNN-1O, the bottom two layers have relatively short time scales, but the top three layers have virtually the same, very long time scale.

The paper also examines the role of each layer in the DRNNs in terms of time-scale and abstraction. The authors perform several experiments, including text generation and analysis of the output probabilities of the DRNN-AO. The results suggest that the higher layers are processing more abstract and long-term properties of the text, but the authors acknowledge that this is incomplete evidence and that more experiments are needed to fully understand the benefits of the hierarchical architecture.

One of the strengths of the paper is its intellectual honesty and thoroughness in discussing its own potential problems and shortcomings. The authors acknowledge the limitations of their experiments and suggest several avenues for future research, including the application of second-order techniques such as Hessian-free training to DRNNs.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the comparison between the DRNNs and the regular RNN is not entirely fair, as the regular RNN has fewer parameters. A more convincing comparison would be between a DRNN and a regular RNN with the same number of parameters or units. Secondly, the paper could benefit from more experiments that demonstrate the benefits of the hierarchical architecture, such as training DRNNs with different depths and widths.

Minor suggestions include defining DRNN-AO and IO in the text, rather than just in Figure 1, and providing more details on the gradient normalization technique used in the paper.

Overall, the paper makes a strong contribution to the field of deep learning and provides a thorough analysis of the benefits and limitations of DRNNs. The authors' willingness to discuss the potential problems and shortcomings of their approach is particularly commendable.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for deep recurrent neural networks.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the benefits and limitations of DRNNs, including their application to character-level language modeling.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise presentation of the main ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The comparison between the DRNNs and the regular RNN is not entirely fair.
* The paper could benefit from more experiments that demonstrate the benefits of the hierarchical architecture.
* Some minor suggestions, such as defining DRNN-AO and IO in the text, could improve the clarity and readability of the paper.
