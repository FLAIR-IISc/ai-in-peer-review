- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): hierarchical RNN layers processing hidden states of the layer below to produce multiple temporal scales.  
- Claims DRNNs better model multi-scale time-series dependencies and demonstrates this on character-level language modeling with Wikipedia data.  
- Achieves competitive RNN performance (BPC ≈ 1.47) using simple SGD training instead of Hessian-free optimization.  
- Provides detailed analysis of emergent time scales via typo perturbations, context switching, and parenthesis-closing tasks.  
- Clear, rigorous presentation with well-motivated architecture, equations, and informative figures (e.g., Fig. 1–4).  
- Thorough experimental design including DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison, layer ablations, and layer-contribution analyses.  
- Strong evidence that higher layers capture longer-term dependencies (e.g., maintaining parenthesis-closing probability over hundreds of characters).  
- Training procedure is transparent and reproducible; claims about SGD practicality are a useful contribution.  
- Primary limitation: narrow evaluation—single dataset (Wikipedia) limits generalizability.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited to RNN variants; omission of standard hierarchical models (HMMs, hierarchical LSTMs) undermines the significance claim.  
- Use of PAQ compression as a benchmark is misleading because it is not a language model.  
- Originality is overstated: architecture is incremental and closely builds on Graves et al. (2013); the main novelty is the temporal-scale analysis, not a new model.  
- Framing mismatch: title/abstract present a new architecture while the main contribution is analytical insight into existing DRNN-style models.  
- Recommended revisions: add comparisons to standard hierarchical models, clarify that analysis (not a novel architecture) is the primary contribution, and discuss scalability and generalization limitations.  
- Verdict: borderline—accept if revisions address scope and originality concerns; otherwise reject.
