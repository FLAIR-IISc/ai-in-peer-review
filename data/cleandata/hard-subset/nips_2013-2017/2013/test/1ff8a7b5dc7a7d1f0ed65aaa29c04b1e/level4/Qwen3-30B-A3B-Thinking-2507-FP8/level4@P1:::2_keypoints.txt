- Paper studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network processing the hidden state from the layer below.  
- DRNNs outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling at matched parameter counts, achieving state-of-the-art results with simple SGD.  
- Authors show DRNNs naturally develop hierarchical time scales across layers, with deeper layers capturing longer-term dependencies.  
- DRNNs can learn to balance parentheses and other long-term dependencies without specialized training algorithms like Hessian-free optimization.  
- The architecture itself is not novel (e.g., Graves 2013, El Hihi & Bengio 1996); the contribution should be reframed as analysis rather than introduction.  
- Experimental design is sound, with careful parameter matching and appropriate baselines.  
- Strength: compelling empirical evidence that multi-timescale behavior emerges and SGD suffices for tough temporal tasks.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to character-level language modeling; broader domains (e.g., speech recognition) are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to truncated BPTT (e.g., Mikolov 2012), leaving uncertainty about whether gains come from architecture or training.  
- Writing is clear and organized, but claims of introducing DRNN undermine originality.  
- Key open questions for authors: why do time scales differ between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO, how do results relate to other hierarchical methods, and what are computational trade-offs versus standard RNNs?  
- Overall: technically strong and well-supported; suitable for publication with minor revisions to reframe contributions and add broader validation.
