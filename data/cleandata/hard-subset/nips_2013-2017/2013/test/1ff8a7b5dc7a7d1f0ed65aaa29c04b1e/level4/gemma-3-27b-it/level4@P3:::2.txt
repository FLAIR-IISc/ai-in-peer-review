This paper investigates the usefulness of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), demonstrating that, with a fixed number of parameters, deeper recurrent networks outperform standard RNNs on character-level language modeling. Key findings include an analysis suggesting deeper networks capture more long-term dependencies and the result that standard backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to handle tasks like parenthesis balancing, previously thought to require more advanced optimization methods like Hessian-free training.

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work in recurrent neural networks. The resurgence of neural networks, particularly deep neural networks (DNNs), has been well-documented (e.g., Hinton et al., 2006), with successful applications in areas like speech recognition (Graves et al., 2013) and image recognition (Ciresan et al., 2012). While RNNs can be considered deep networks when unfolded in time, the authors correctly point out their primary function is memory rather than hierarchical processing, a distinction emphasized by work on Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997). Prior work has already explored hierarchical RNN architectures (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 2009; Fernández et al., 2011), though often on artificial tasks. The authors acknowledge related work by Sutskever et al. (2013) and Graves et al. (2013) which used stacked LSTMs, but highlight that their work offers a more in-depth analysis of the architectural contributions.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation behind investigating DRNNs and the potential benefits of hierarchical processing in time series data. The explanation of different timescales within the network is intuitive and well-supported by the analysis.
*   **Empirical Results:** The demonstration of state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling, achieving comparable results to methods employing more complex optimization techniques (Martens, 2012; Martens & Sutskever, 2013), is a significant contribution.
*   **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of time scales within the network, illustrated by the typo perturbation experiment and the parenthesis balancing task, provides valuable insights into the network’s internal representations and how it learns to capture long-range dependencies.
*   **Reproducibility:** The detailed description of the network architecture, training setup, and experimental procedure would facilitate reproducibility by other researchers.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Novelty of Architecture:** The authors initially present the DRNN architecture as novel, but acknowledge prior work (Graves et al., 2013) demonstrating its use. While the analysis is valuable, the paper would be stronger by framing the contribution as an in-depth study of a previously proposed architecture rather than its introduction.
*   **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The evaluation is currently limited to character-level language modeling. While this task is well-suited for demonstrating the benefits of hierarchical processing, extending the analysis to other tasks, particularly in the realm of speech recognition or other time-series domains, would significantly strengthen the conclusions. The authors correctly identify this as a potential limitation.
*   **Computational Cost:** The computational expense of the Hessian-free optimization experiments is noted, but a comparison to truncated backpropagation through time (BPTT) would be beneficial. Truncated BPTT has a long history (e.g., Mikolov, 2018) and has been successfully applied to training RNNs for language modeling, and it’s likely to offer a computationally efficient alternative.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a clear and insightful analysis of deep recurrent neural networks.
*   The empirical results demonstrate competitive performance on a challenging task.
*   The analysis of time scales provides valuable insights into the network’s internal workings.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The architecture itself is not novel, and the paper would benefit from a revised framing of its contribution.
*   The limited scope of evaluation restricts the generalizability of the findings.
*   The absence of a comparison to truncated BPTT leaves a potential optimization avenue unexplored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a well-defined focus and compelling results. While the novelty of the architecture is overstated, the analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent neural networks. I believe the paper makes a worthwhile contribution to the field and, with some minor revisions (specifically, acknowledging the existing use of the DRNN architecture and potentially including a truncated BPTT comparison), would be suitable for acceptance.

**Rating:** Weak Accept (with requested revisions)
