- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) combining hierarchical deep learning structure with RNN time-series modeling.  
- Uses layer-by-layer supervised (pre-)training with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia dataset; very large networks trained for ~10 days.  
- Achieves improved performance over prior work on that specific Wikipedia dataset.  
- Network analysis finds long-term dependencies modeled in higher layers and parenthesis correspondences as pseudo-stable attractor-like states.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes effective figures.  
- Technically sound: claims are supported by the presented experiments.  
- Main limitation: experiments are confined to a single, not widely studied dataset, reducing impact.  
- Comparisons are limited to RNN-related models and omit alternative hierarchical models (e.g., HMMs); Table 1 entries use different corpora and are not comparable.  
- Contribution appears stronger in analysis of network behavior than in demonstrating the superiority of the proposed architecture.  
- Results are somewhat inconclusive; broader experimentation on diverse datasets and more model comparisons are needed.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel architecture, thorough analysis, clear writing/figures; Cons for acceptance: limited experiments, narrow comparisons, and inconclusive results.
