- Core findings: deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs at fixed parameter counts, deeper layers encode more long-term dependencies, and plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to solve parentheses balancing.  
- Strong analysis: the information-theoretic examination of long-range information in deeper layers is well-executed and valuable.  
- Overstated novelty: claiming to "introduce" deep RNNs is incorrect; deep/stacked RNNs have prior work (e.g., Graves 2013, Graves 2008).  
- Reframe contribution: the paper should be presented as an analysis of deep RNNs rather than an architectural innovation.  
- Limited validation: experiments are confined to character-level language modeling and may not generalize to other tasks.  
- Need broader tasks: add validation on a distinct task with known long-range dependencies (e.g., speech recognition or machine translation).  
- Inefficient methodology: using hierarchical feedback (HF) for the parentheses task is costly and unnecessary compared to truncated BPTT.  
- Truncated BPTT baseline: compare with truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) to verify whether it also solves the parentheses task and matches performance at lower cost.  
- Clarify BPTT terminology: explicitly state whether "plain BPTT" refers to full or truncated BPTT, since standard BPTT struggles with long sequences.  
- Overall assessment: analysis is interesting and well-done for LM, but misattributed novelty, narrow scope, and inefficient experiments weaken the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; revise to correct framing, add broader experiments (speech/translation), and replace HF with truncated BPTT to strengthen the paper.
