- Commendation: Authors' observation about older data needing more processing and proposal to use deep RNNs is reasonable.
- Suggestion: Consider adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps as an alternative approach.
- Question: Clarify the role of gradient normalization in avoiding "bifurcations" and discuss relevance of the ICML 2013 paper on gradient truncation/optimization.
- Concern: Normalizing gradients might disrupt SGD’s averaging behavior—needs discussion or evidence.
- Suggestion: Compare Wikipedia compression results to Mikolov et al.'s work.
- Strength: Text generation experiment (Table 2) is the most persuasive evidence for layer roles.
- Caveat: Interpreting “brain surgery” (layer manipulations) is problematic due to complex interdependencies between layers.
- Missing experiment: Test whether higher layers truly gain from preceding processing by comparing moving units to layer 1 vs. keeping them higher.
- Fairness concern: Parameter counts differ (regular RNN may have fewer parameters); compare deep RNNs to standard RNNs with equal parameters or units.
- Alternative experiment: Replace Figure 2 with benchmarks showing performance of various DRNN depths trained from scratch, possibly widening layers to match parameter/unit counts.
- Minor fix: Define DRNN-AO and IO in the text (not only in Figure 1).
- Overall: Paper is worthwhile, well-written, thorough, but needs additional experiments to strengthen empirical claims.
