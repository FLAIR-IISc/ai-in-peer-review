- Novel deep architecture that integrates hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-style).
- Training algorithm: layer-by-layer supervised pretraining using a next-letter prediction objective.
- Trained very large networks for ~10 days and achieved improved performance on the Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset, surpassing prior work.
- Detailed mechanistic analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses correspondence forms a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."
- Paper quality is high: well-written, well-designed experiments, and supportive figures.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset with few comparable prior works.
- Comparisons are restricted to other RNN-based models; no benchmarks against hierarchical HMMs or similar conventional approaches.
- Table 1 lists alternative methods but on a different corpus, making those comparisons incomparable.
- Stated contribution emphasizes the new architecture, but the paper's strongest, clearer contribution is the thorough analysis of network operation, causing a misalignment and making results somewhat inconclusive.
