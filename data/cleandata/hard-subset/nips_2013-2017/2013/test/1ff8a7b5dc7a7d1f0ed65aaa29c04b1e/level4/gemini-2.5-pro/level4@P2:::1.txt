This paper introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture for modeling time series with inherent temporal hierarchies. The core idea is to stack multiple RNN layers, where each layer receives the hidden state sequence of the layer below it as input. This design is proposed to naturally capture information across different time scales, with lower layers focusing on short-term patterns and higher layers integrating information over longer horizons.

The authors evaluate their model on the task of character-level language modeling using a large Wikipedia dataset. They present two variants: one where only the top layer produces an output (DRNN-1O) and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). Using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with gradient normalization, the proposed DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on this benchmark, matching results previously obtained with more complex Hessian-free optimization methods. A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to analyzing the trained models, providing compelling evidence that the layers indeed learn different time scales and can capture complex, long-range dependencies, such as matching opening and closing parentheses over hundreds of characters.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Originality and Significance:** The concept of stacking RNNs to form a deep temporal hierarchy is an intuitive and powerful extension of existing models. The paper's primary significance lies in demonstrating that this architectural choice, combined with a simple training procedure, can match the performance of more sophisticated optimization techniques on a challenging task. This finding contributes to the understanding of how architectural depth can benefit sequential modeling.

2.  **Thorough Analysis:** A major strength of the paper is its in-depth analysis of the network's internal workings. The authors go beyond simply reporting performance metrics. The experiments visualizing how perturbations (typos) fade at different rates across layers provide clear evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. Furthermore, the case study on parenthesis-closing offers a concrete and insightful example of the model's ability to learn non-trivial, long-range dependencies, which the authors compellingly describe as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state." This level of analysis is valuable to the community and provides insights into the properties of deep sequential models.

3.  **Clarity and Quality:** The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow. The model architecture is explained precisely, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail. The figures are particularly effective in illustrating the key findings, especially the visualizations of layer-wise time scales and parenthesis prediction probabilities. The work appears technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by the provided experimental results.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The empirical evaluation, while strong, is focused on a single task (character-level language modeling) on one specific dataset. While this is a challenging benchmark, the paper's claims about the general utility of DRNNs for hierarchical time series would be significantly strengthened by demonstrating their effectiveness on other domains, such as speech recognition or financial time-series prediction, where temporal hierarchies are also prominent.

2.  **Narrow Comparison to Other Model Families:** The performance comparison is made exclusively against other RNN-based architectures. While this is a fair comparison, the problem of modeling hierarchical sequences has been approached by other families of models, such as Hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HHMMs) or other structured-prediction models. A broader discussion of how the DRNN relates to these alternative approaches, and why it might be advantageous, would better contextualize the work within the broader machine learning literature. The inclusion of results from compression algorithms in Table 1 is noted, but their evaluation on a different dataset makes a direct comparison impossible.

3.  **Clarity on Training Procedure:** The paper states that an incremental, layer-wise training scheme was "necessary to reach good performance" for the DRNN-1O model. It would be beneficial for the authors to elaborate on why this was the case. Was it due to vanishing/exploding gradients in the deep structure, or another optimization pathology? Providing more intuition on this point would be a valuable practical contribution for others seeking to train similar deep architectures.

**Summary of Arguments:**

*   **Pro-acceptance:** The paper introduces a well-motivated deep architecture for sequential data, demonstrates state-of-the-art results on a difficult benchmark using a simple training algorithm, and, most importantly, provides an excellent and insightful analysis of the model's learned properties. The analysis of the emergent temporal hierarchy is a significant contribution.
*   **Con-acceptance:** The experimental validation is confined to a single dataset and task, which limits the generality of the conclusions. The comparison is also limited to the family of RNNs, with little discussion of alternative hierarchical models.

Overall, the strengths of this paper, particularly the combination of strong performance and detailed analysis, outweigh its weaknesses. It presents a valuable contribution to the field's understanding of deep models for sequential data. I recommend acceptance.
