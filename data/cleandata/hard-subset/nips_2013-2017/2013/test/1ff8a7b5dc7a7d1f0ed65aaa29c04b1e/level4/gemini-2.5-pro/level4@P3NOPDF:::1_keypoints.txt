- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical feature learning with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-like capabilities).  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training algorithm based on next-letter prediction.  
- Large-scale version trained extensively (~10 days) outperforms prior RNN approaches on a Wikipedia dataset used by Hinton et al.  
- Provides an in-depth analysis of internal mechanisms, showing long-term dependencies are modeled in higher layers.  
- Demonstrates phenomena like matching parentheses handled via a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, with thoughtful experiments and effective figures.  
- The representation analysis is particularly insightful and valuable to the community.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, not widely adopted dataset, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Comparative analysis is constrained to RNN-related models and lacks direct empirical comparisons to other relevant classes (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).  
- Reported results from other approaches are on different corpora (Table 1), making comparisons incomparable.  
- Main contribution appears stronger in analysis of how the model works than in conclusive state-of-the-art performance.  
- Recommendation: impact would increase with broader evaluation on multiple standard benchmarks and a wider set of comparison models.
