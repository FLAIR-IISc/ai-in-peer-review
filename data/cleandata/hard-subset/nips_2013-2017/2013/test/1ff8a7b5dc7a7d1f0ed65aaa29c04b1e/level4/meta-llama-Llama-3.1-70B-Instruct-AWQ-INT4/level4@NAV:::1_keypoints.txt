- Introduces a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling (HMM-/RNN-like).
- Proposes a layer-by-layer supervised (pre-)training algorithm using a next-letter prediction objective.
- After ~10 days of training large networks, reports performance that surpasses prior work on Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia dataset.
- Analysis shows higher layers model long-term dependencies and that parentheses matching appears as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like" state.
- Paper is well written with clear experiments and supportive figures.
- Main experimental concern: evaluation is limited to a single, relatively little-used dataset.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models, omitting other hierarchical HMM approaches that could address similar tasks.
- Table 1 lists alternative methods but evaluates them on a different corpus, preventing valid comparison.
- The paper’s primary contribution ends up being the in-depth analysis of network behavior rather than the novel architecture itself.
- As a result, the paper’s focus shifts from presenting a new architecture to analysis, leaving the overall conclusions somewhat inconclusive.
