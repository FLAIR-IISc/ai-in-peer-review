- Paper studies stacked recurrent layers (DRNNs) to induce multiple time scales and hierarchical sequence processing, showing strong character-level language-modeling results with SGD and analyses of emergent temporal hierarchies.
- Insight: stacking recurrent layers addresses the arbitrariness that older inputs get more unrolled processing in standard RNNs, potentially enabling distinct time scales per layer.
- Suggestion: compare to architectures that add extra nonlinear transformations within a single timestep (deeper transition functions) rather than stacking recurrent layers.
- Text-generation ablation (Table 2) is persuasive: removing bottom-layer outputs destabilizes local text, while removing top-layer outputs preserves local statistics but loses long-range coherence.
- Caution: ablation interpretation is tricky because layer outputs are interdependent and may compensate for one another.
- Temporal-scale analyses (typo perturbations, context switches) show lower layers learn short time constants and higher layers learn longer ones, with DRNNs recovering more slowly from context switches than single-layer RNNs.
- Question: how crucial was gradient normalization for stability and final performance, and what exact bifurcations or parameter jumps occurred without it?
- Request: relate the gradient-normalization approach to recent work on RNN optimization (e.g., Montreal group's ICML 2013) and discuss whether similar stability issues arise.
- Question: does gradient normalization interfere with SGD averaging or step-size adaptation, and can empirical evidence be provided?
- Request to compare against Mikolov et al.'s Wikipedia compression/text-prediction results in addition to MRNN and PAQ.
- Fairness concern: standard RNN baseline appears to have far fewer parameters than DRNNs, making comparisons potentially unfair.
- Suggested fairness experiments: compare DRNNs to (1) single-layer RNNs with the same total parameter count and (2) single-layer RNNs with the same number of units to decouple depth from capacity.
- Suggested experiment: report benchmarks for various DRNN depths trained from scratch, widening shallower models to match parameter count or unit count to test whether depth per se yields gains.
- Key missing test: move higher-layer units to the bottom layer (or equivalent capacity rearrangement) to verify whether deeper layers truly capture abstract longer-term properties beyond capacity/training dynamics.
- Overall: paper makes a solid case for stacked recurrent architectures and emergent temporal hierarchies, with thoughtful analyses and illustrative ablations, but baseline fairness and the missing capacity-control experiment are main reservations.
- Minor: explicitly define DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O in text (not just Figure 1).
- Minor: include comparisons to Mikolov’s Wikipedia language modeling/compression results where applicable.
