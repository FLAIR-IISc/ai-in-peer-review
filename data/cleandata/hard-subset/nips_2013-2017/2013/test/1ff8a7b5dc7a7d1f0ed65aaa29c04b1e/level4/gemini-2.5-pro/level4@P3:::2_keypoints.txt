- Paper studies stacked Deep RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, hypothesizing layers form a temporal hierarchy.  
- DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on a large Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD with gradient normalization.  
- Empirical analyses show deeper layers operate on longer time scales and capture longer-term dependencies.  
- Full BPTT training enables learning very long-range dependencies (e.g., balancing parentheses over hundreds of characters).  
- Contribution is primarily a novel, in-depth analysis of DRNN internal dynamics rather than a new architecture.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance achieved with simple optimization highlights the power of depth.  
- Strength: insightful, well-designed experiments (e.g., effective time-scale measures, parenthesis task) that improve scientific understanding.  
- Strength: clear, well-documented presentation supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: positioning overstates architectural novelty given prior stacked RNN work (El Hihi & Bengio, Graves et al.).  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to character-level language modeling—unclear generalizability to other sequential domains.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to the practical baseline of truncated BPTT (e.g., 30–50 steps) to assess performance–cost trade-offs.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant analysis with strong results, but needs clearer positioning and broader empirical validation.
