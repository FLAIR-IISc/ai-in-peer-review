- Strong empirical results: DRNNs trained with SGD achieve BPC competitive with prior Hessian-Free state-of-the-art on large-scale character-level language modeling.  
- Important finding: First-order SGD can match more complex second-order methods for this task, informing the optimization trade-off debate.  
- Insightful analysis: Experiments (typo perturbation, context-switch tests) show deeper layers capture longer time scales, supporting an emergent temporal hierarchy.  
- Demonstration of long-term dependency learning: The model reliably maintains parenthesis state over hundreds of characters, exceeding training sequence lengths.  
- Overstated novelty: Stacked RNN architectures are not new (e.g., Graves et al.); the paper’s main contribution is analysis and empirical validation, not a novel model.  
- Limited evaluation scope: Conclusions are drawn from a single task (character-level language modeling) and may not generalize to domains like speech or music.  
- Incomplete training comparison: The paper omits truncated BPTT baselines (common, efficient practice), leaving out a crucial comparison of training trade-offs.  
- Revision recommendation: Reframe the work as an in-depth analysis/empirical validation of DRNNs and better contextualize prior stacked-RNN literature.  
- Experimental recommendation: Discuss or include experiments on other sequential domains and compare full BPTT to truncated BPTT to strengthen generality and training-cost claims.
