- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks recurrent layers with two output configurations: DRNN-1O (top-only) and DRNN-AO (all layers).  
- Trains with SGD and gradient normalization; uses supervised incremental layer-wise pretraining for DRNN-1O to mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Evaluates on large Wikipedia character-level modeling with models constrained to ~4.9M parameters to match prior work.  
- Achieves bits-per-character comparable to best reported RNN results of similar size (e.g., multiplicative RNNs with Hessian-free optimization).  
- Provides analyses of emergent time scales per layer via perturbation tests, effective context length, generative behavior, and qualitative examples (e.g., long-range parentheses modeling).  
- Relates to prior hierarchical/stacked RNNs and stacked LSTMs; novelty mainly in analysis and training demonstration rather than architecture.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly described, and uses informative diagnostics (layer ablations, perturbation studies, qualitative generation).  
- Experimental design is coherent with matched parameter counts and controlled schedules, though some hyperparameter/initialization choices lack justification or ablation.  
- Claims about emergent time scales are supported by multiple measurements, but broader empirical validation is limited.  
- Manuscript is well organized and readable; figures support conclusions.  
- Needs clarification on gradient “normalization,” reporting of run-to-run variability, and explicit computational budget/data pass comparisons.  
- Architectural novelty is modest; incremental supervised layer-wise training is practical but not wholly new.  
- Significant in showing stacked RNNs trained with simple SGD can match complex training methods and in providing insights into depth-induced temporal hierarchies.  
- Empirical scope is narrow (single dataset) and baselines are limited, reducing confidence in generality and broader impact.  
- Strengths: clear writing, thoughtful analyses, competitive performance with simple optimizer, and useful diagnostics others can adopt.  
- Weaknesses: single-corpus evaluation, missing strong baselines (LSTM/stacked LSTM, high-order n-grams), limited exploration of design space, and no statistical variability reporting.  
- Comparisons may be unfair without clearer compute/data-pass accounting relative to prior work.  
- Suggested improvements: add enwik8/text8/PTB-char experiments, include LSTM/n-gram/compression baselines, ablate depth/width/output connections, compare layer-wise pretraining vs joint training.  
- Also suggested: report run-to-run variance and training cost (GPU-days/epochs), clarify gradient normalization and zero-output initialization, and release code/configs for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid analysis advancing understanding of temporal hierarchy, practical result that SGD can work well, and clear, instructive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, incremental architectural novelty, and unsupported claims of superiority without broader comparisons.  
- Overall: well-written, insightful, and borderline—valuable analysis but needs broader experiments and stronger baselines to be fully convincing.
