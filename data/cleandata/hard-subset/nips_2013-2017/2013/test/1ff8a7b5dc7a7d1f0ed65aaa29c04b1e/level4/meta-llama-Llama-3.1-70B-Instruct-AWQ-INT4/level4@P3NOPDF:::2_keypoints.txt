- Agree that focusing on a single task is limiting; recommend validating findings across multiple tasks.  
- Suggest testing truncated BPTT with fewer steps (e.g., ~30) to reduce compute and check if competitive entropies can be achieved.  
- Key result: with the number of parameters fixed, deeper RNNs outperform standard (shallower) RNNs on character-level modeling.  
- Deeper network layers tend to capture more long-term / long-range information.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting claims that HF optimization is required.  
- The paper’s claim of introducing “deep RNNs” is incorrect—similar architectures have been used previously (e.g., Graves 2013).  
- The analysis may be problem-specific; demonstrating results on additional domains (especially speech) would strengthen the claims.  
- HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; prior work (Mikolov) shows truncated BPTT often suffices and should be tested here.  
- Two main contributions: (1) depth helps when parameters are fixed, and (2) deeper layers encode longer-range structure; validating these on speech would make the paper significantly stronger.
