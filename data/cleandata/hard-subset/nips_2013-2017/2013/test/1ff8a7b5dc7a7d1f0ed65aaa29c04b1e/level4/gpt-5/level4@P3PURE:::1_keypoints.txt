- Proposes a new deep architecture combining hierarchical representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN spirit).
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining on a next-letter prediction objective.
- Experiments train very large networks (~10 days) and report improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset from Hinton et al.
- Provides detailed analysis showing long-term dependencies emerge in higher layers.
- Analysis finds parentheses correspondence manifests as a “pseudo-stable, attractor-like” state.
- Paper is well written, experimental setup is sound, and figures effectively support claims.
- Main concern: evaluation uses a single dataset with limited prior work, restricting generality.
- Comparisons are mainly to RNN-related models; conventional baselines like hierarchical HMMs are missing.
- Table 1 lists other approaches but those results are on a different corpus and not directly comparable.
- Overall contribution is stronger on analysis of model behavior than on conclusive empirical gains; empirical results are somewhat inconclusive.
