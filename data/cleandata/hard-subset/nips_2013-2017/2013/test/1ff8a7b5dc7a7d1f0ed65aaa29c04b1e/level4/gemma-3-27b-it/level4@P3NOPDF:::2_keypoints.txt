- Deep RNNs (with parameter count fixed) outperform standard RNNs on a character-level language modeling task.  
- Deeper RNN layers capture more long-term information than shallower ones.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs that more complex methods were required.  
- The claim of introducing the “deep RNN” is inaccurate; stacked/deep RNNs were used previously (e.g., Graves et al. ICASSP 2013) and the authors should reframe the contribution as analysis rather than a new architecture.  
- The analysis is meaningful but limited in scope; results may be task-specific and lack generalizability.  
- To strengthen the paper, evaluate on additional domains (e.g., speech recognition or generation) to confirm the benefits of depth.  
- Full BPTT experiments are computationally expensive; truncated BPTT (used by Mikolov) could likely yield competitive results at much lower cost and should be tested (e.g., 30 steps).  
- Clarify novelty, expand cross-domain validation, and explore truncated BPTT to improve experimental validation and efficiency.
