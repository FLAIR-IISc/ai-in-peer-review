- Post-rebuttal: add a truncated BPTT baseline (e.g., 30 steps) to test whether long unrolls are necessary given their cost.  
- Summary: paper empirically studies stacked tanh RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) trained with SGD and gradient norm normalization on Wikipedia character modeling.  
- Result: DRNNs match prior HF-trained models at similar parameter counts and outperform single-layer RNNs.  
- Analysis: includes per-layer ablations, perturbation/context-switch experiments, and qualitative generation showing long-range effects (e.g., parentheses closure).  
- Strength: clear empirical finding that depth improves performance at fixed parameter budget.  
- Strength: thorough diagnostics demonstrating plausible temporal hierarchies across layers.  
- Strength: shows SGD with BPTT (and gradient normalization) can learn long-range dependencies previously shown mainly with HF.  
- Strength: simple, reproducible incremental layer-wise training protocol for DRNN-1O.  
- Weakness: framing claims novelty for stacked architectures is misleading; stacked RNNs/LSTMs are established.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to one dataset/task; additional domains would support broader claims about temporal hierarchies.  
- Weakness: missing modern baselines (stacked LSTM/GRU, multiplicative RNN with SGD) and a TBPTT baseline to contextualize cost vs. accuracy.  
- Weakness: incremental training schedule may confound the observed long time scales; need controls (train without incremental reattachment or apply schedule to DRNN-AO).  
- Weakness: reporting details need clarification—exact character set BPC, effective epochs, and run variance.  
- Weakness: training is expensive (~10 days/GPU); include TBPTT baseline to quantify efficiency trade-offs.  
- Quality: technically sound with insightful time-scale analyses, but architectural claims need stronger controls and baselines.  
- Clarity: well written; must downplay novelty claims and expand related work and unroll/TBPTT details.  
- Originality/significance: moderate—main contribution is empirical/analytical study of depth rather than a new architecture.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on repositioning the paper as analysis, adding a lightweight TBPTT baseline, improving related work, and ideally adding one more task or a stacked LSTM baseline.
