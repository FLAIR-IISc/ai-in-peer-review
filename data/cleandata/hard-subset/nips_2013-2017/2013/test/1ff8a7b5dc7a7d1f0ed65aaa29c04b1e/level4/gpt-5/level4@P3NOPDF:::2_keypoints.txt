- Paper evaluates only one task, limiting generalizability of conclusions.  
- Try truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) to assess accuracy–efficiency trade-offs and whether parenthesis balancing persists under truncation.  
- Main reported findings: depth helps under a fixed parameter budget, deeper layers retain longer-range information, and plain BPTT can learn parenthesis balancing.  
- Clear, focused empirical question showing deeper RNNs outperform single-layer RNNs on the tested benchmark.  
- Analysis suggesting deeper layers capture longer-range temporal structure is informative and aligns with hierarchical intuition.  
- Demonstration that standard BPTT suffices for parenthesis balancing clarifies optimization assumptions.  
- Do not claim to introduce “deep RNNs”; stacked/multilayer RNNs have prior art and should be properly credited.  
- Single-task scope: results may be dataset- or task-specific; include at least one additional domain (e.g., speech) to strengthen claims.  
- HF experiments seem computationally expensive; authors should evaluate truncated BPTT, report entropies and wall-clock costs, and compare efficiency.  
- Long-term information analysis should be replicated across multiple datasets or modalities to establish generality.  
- Ensure experimental controls for fixed-parameter comparisons (regularization, initialization, training budgets) and report variance across seeds.  
- Minor: explicitly acknowledge prior stacked RNN work, detail HF vs BPTT training settings, and consider qualitative probes (unit visualizations).  
- Overall: a useful empirical study that needs reframing as analysis (not architectural novelty), broader empirical scope, and cost-aware comparisons to increase impact.
