- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep models with temporal modeling (HMM/RNN-like) capabilities.  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training algorithm using a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Large-scale model trained on a Wikipedia dataset (~10 days) reportedly outperforms prior RNN-based approaches.  
- Paper devotes substantial analysis to internal mechanisms, showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies.  
- Authors demonstrate specific behaviors (e.g., matching parentheses) via a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Writing, experimental setup, and figures are clear and high-quality.  
- Internal representation analysis is particularly insightful and well-executed.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation uses only a single, not widely adopted dataset, limiting generalizability.  
- Comparisons are largely restricted to RNN-related models and omit other relevant model classes (e.g., Hierarchical HMMs).  
- Table 1 cites results on a different corpus, preventing direct and meaningful performance comparisons.  
- Main contribution appears to be the detailed analysis of how the network functions rather than a decisive demonstration of superior performance.  
- Empirical claims are inconclusive and somewhat misaligned with the paper's framing as a new state-of-the-art architecture.
