- Reviewer agrees with the concern about the study being single-task focused.  
- Recommend testing truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper alternative that may still perform well.  
- Paper reports deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs at fixed parameter counts on character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper recurrent layers retain more long-term information.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior belief that HF methods were required.  
- The paper incorrectly claims to introduce the "deep RNN" architecture; similar architectures existed previously (e.g., Graves et al., 2013).  
- The analysis should be extended to additional tasks (e.g., speech recognition) to test generality beyond character-level modeling.  
- HF experiments appear unnecessarily costly; truncated BPTT (used historically and by Mikolov) likely suffices for parenthesis balancing.  
- Testing truncated BPTT would provide a more computationally efficient validation of the approach.  
- Overall: interesting analysis with noteworthy results, but limited by single-task scope and incorrect novelty claim.
