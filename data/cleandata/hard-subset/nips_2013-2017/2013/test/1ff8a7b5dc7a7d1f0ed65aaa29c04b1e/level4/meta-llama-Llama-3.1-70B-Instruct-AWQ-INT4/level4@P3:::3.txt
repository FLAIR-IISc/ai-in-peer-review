This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) and demonstrates its effectiveness in character-level language modeling tasks. The authors argue that common recurrent neural networks (RNNs) do not explicitly accommodate temporal hierarchies in time series data, and propose a hierarchical architecture where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture and its performance on a Wikipedia text corpus. The experiments designed to examine the different roles played by each layer in terms of time-scale and abstraction are generally convincing, although some reservations are noted below.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide insight into how the higher layers of the DRNN are processing and representing more abstract and long-term structures in the data. The text generation experiments, as shown in Table 2, are particularly persuasive in this regard. However, as pointed out by the authors themselves, it may be problematic to interpret the effect of this kind of "brain surgery" on RNNs due to the complex interdependencies that may have developed between the outputs of the various layers.

A significant concern is that the comparison between the DRNN and the regular RNN may be unfair due to the difference in the number of parameters. The regular RNN has fewer parameters, which may put it at a disadvantage. A more convincing comparison would be to compare the performance of the DRNN with a standard RNN that has the same number of parameters or units.

Additionally, it would be beneficial to see how well various depths of DRNN perform on the benchmarks when trained from scratch, possibly with wider layers than their deeper counterparts to make up for the difference in the number of parameters and/or units.

The use of gradient normalization is also noteworthy, and it would be helpful to provide more explanation on why this is necessary and how it avoids the problem of bifurcations. Furthermore, it would be interesting to see how this approach compares to other optimization techniques, such as gradient truncation, which has been explored in recent work.

In terms of previous results on these kinds of Wikipedia compression tasks, it would be beneficial to compare the performance of the DRNN with other approaches, such as those proposed by Mikolov et al.

Overall, the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and recurrent neural networks. The proposed architecture is novel and effective, and the analysis and experiments provide valuable insights into its performance and behavior.

Strengths:

* Novel architecture for deep recurrent neural networks
* Effective performance on character-level language modeling tasks
* Insightful analysis and experiments that provide understanding of how the higher layers of the DRNN process and represent more abstract and long-term structures in the data

Weaknesses:

* Comparison between DRNN and regular RNN may be unfair due to difference in number of parameters
* Lack of comparison with other approaches, such as those proposed by Mikolov et al.
* Limited explanation on the use of gradient normalization and its comparison to other optimization techniques
