- Proposes hierarchical DRNNs for character-level language modeling, claiming better temporal hierarchies and SOTA recurrent performance using SGD.
- Contribution 1: Shows DRNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter counts are fixed.
- Contribution 2: Analyzes how DRNN layers develop distinct time scales and capture longer-term dependencies.
- Contribution 3: Claims standard BPTT can train RNNs to balance parentheses (contradicting prior assumptions).
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that DRNNs beat standard RNNs on character prediction.
- Strength: Compelling layerwise analysis showing deeper layers capture longer dependencies.
- Weakness: Novelty claim is inaccurate—stacked/ hierarchical recurrent architectures (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) already exist.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to character-level language modeling, so generalizability is unclear.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification why truncated BPTT would be inadequate for the parentheses task.
- Requested correction: Acknowledge prior work and reframe the paper as analysis of the architecture rather than its introduction.
- Requested extension: Validate findings on additional tasks (e.g., speech recognition) to demonstrate generality.
- Requested analysis: Provide a thorough comparison with truncated BPTT and clarify its effectiveness or limitations.
- Quality assessment: Experiments are well-designed and results well-supported, but scope is limited.
- Clarity assessment: Paper is well-written with clear figures and explanations.
- Originality assessment: Core architecture not novel; temporal-hierarchy analysis is a useful contribution.
- Significance assessment: Insights are interesting but their impact is reduced by single-domain evidence.
- Recommendation: Reject pending corrections to novelty claims, broader experiments, and stronger comparisons with standard training methods.
