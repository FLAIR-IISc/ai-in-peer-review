- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN): stacked RNN layers where each layer’s outputs form the next layer’s input to build hierarchical temporal representations.  
- Empirical claim: 5-layer DRNN matches state-of-the-art on large-scale character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) using simple SGD.  
- Analysis shows higher layers learn longer timescales and more abstract features (e.g., long-range parenthesis tracking).  
- Strength: clear motivation that standard RNNs lack per-timestep processing depth and DRNN is a simple, natural solution.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance suggesting the architectural prior eases optimization.  
- Strength: thoughtful analyses (timescales, context recovery, parenthesis tracking) that illuminate model behavior.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and candid about analysis limitations.  
- Main weakness: lacks conclusive evidence that hierarchical structure (depth) rather than increased parameterization drives gains.  
- Need controlled comparisons against single-layer RNNs with equivalent hidden units (not just equal parameter count) to isolate depth vs. capacity.  
- Need ablation examining performance across DRNN depths (2–5 layers) with constant total parameter count to directly measure depth effect.  
- Request more details on gradient normalization: its necessity, effect without it, relation to gradient clipping (cite Pascanu et al. 2013), and impact on SGD averaging.  
- Question about alternative ways to add per-timestep nonlinearity (multiple non-linear layers within a timestep) and whether those were compared.  
- Missing baseline comparison: should compare against strong RNN language-model baselines such as Mikolov et al. for Wikipedia compression.  
- Minor issue: acronyms DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO are only in Figure 1 caption and should be defined in the main text.  
- Conclusion: interesting, well-written contribution highlighting architectural importance for temporal models, but needs more controlled depth vs. capacity experiments to solidify claims.
