- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-like).
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pre-training with a next-letter prediction objective.
- Large networks trained ~10 days achieve state-of-the-art on the Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset, outperforming prior work.
- Detailed analysis shows higher layers capture long-term dependencies.
- Analysis finds correspondence between opening/closing parentheses represented as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."
- Paper is well-written with well-designed experiments and supportive figures.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single, little-studied dataset.
- Comparison set is restricted to RNN-related models and omits hierarchical HMMs that could address similar tasks.
- Table 1 lists other approaches but they use different corpora, making comparisons incomparable.
- Primary contribution is deep behavioral analysis of the model rather than conclusive empirical results.
