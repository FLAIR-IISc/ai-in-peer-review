- Praises insight that assuming older data needs more processing layers is arbitrary.  
- Finds the proposed deep RNN architecture reasonable for enhancing short-term processing and other benefits.  
- Suggests exploring extra nonlinear layers between timesteps as an interesting extension.  
- Questions whether gradient normalization prevents bifurcation and if it disrupts SGD averaging; cites ICML 2013 Montreal work on gradient truncation.  
- Recommends including benchmark comparisons to Mikolov et al.'s Wikipedia compression results.  
- Notes text generation analysis (Table 2) is convincing but "brain surgery" experiment interpretations are difficult due to layer interdependencies.  
- Identifies major empirical gap: unclear if higher layers genuinely process more abstract, long-term properties versus simply acquiring roles post-training.  
- Points out unequal parameter counts between standard RNN and DRNN-1O; recommends comparisons with matched total units/parameters.  
- Advises Figure 2 should show DRNN performance across depths trained from scratch, possibly with wider layers to offset parameter differences.  
- Minor: DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O should be defined in the main text, not only in Figure 1.  
- Overall verdict: DRNNs are a promising hybrid with strong text-prediction performance and evidence of hierarchical time-scale processing; paper is well-written and candid about limitations.
