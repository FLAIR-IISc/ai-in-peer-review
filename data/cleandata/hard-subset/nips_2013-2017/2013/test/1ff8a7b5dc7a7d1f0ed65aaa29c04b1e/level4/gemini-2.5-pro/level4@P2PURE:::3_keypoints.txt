- Proposes a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that combines deep hierarchical processing with recurrent temporal modeling and shows strong results on text prediction and compression while analyzing layer abstractions.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and candid about limitations.  
- Claims older data points receive more non-linear processing in deeper layers, which may help short-term dependencies; asks whether adding extra non-linear layers between timesteps in a single recurrent layer was tested.  
- Requests clarification on the gradient normalization technique: how it prevents "bifurcations," relation to ICML 2013 work on gradient clipping/RNN optimization, and whether it interferes with SGD’s implicit averaging.  
- Recommends comparing Wikipedia compression results to prior work (e.g., Mikolov et al.) to strengthen empirical evaluation.  
- Finds layer-role analyses insightful; text generation (Table 2) is the most persuasive evidence that deeper layers capture longer-term, abstract structures, but lesioning interpretations remain challenging due to interdependencies.  
- Main unanswered question: is depth causally responsible for long-term/abstract processing, or would adding units to the first layer (wider/shallow network) yield similar effects?  
- Notes potential unfairness in DRNN vs standard RNN comparisons due to parameter mismatches; recommends comparing against RNNs with comparable parameter counts or the same total hidden units (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs single-layer RNN with same units).  
- Suggests running benchmarks for DRNNs of varying depths (2, 3, 4 layers), trained from scratch, with layer widths adjusted to control total parameters or units.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the main text instead of only in Figure 1’s caption.
