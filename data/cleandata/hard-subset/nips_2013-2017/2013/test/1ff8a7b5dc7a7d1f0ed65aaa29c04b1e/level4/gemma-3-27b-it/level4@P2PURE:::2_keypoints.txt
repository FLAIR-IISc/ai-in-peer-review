- Agree with authors on single-task limitation; suggest exploring truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps).
- Truncated BPTT may be less effective for balancing parentheses but could achieve competitive entropies with much lower computation—needs verification.
- Paper shows deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter counts are held constant on character-level language modeling.
- Deeper networks capture more long-range/structural information.
- Plain BPTT can successfully train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, challenging the supposed need for HF.
- Claiming novelty for the “deep RNN” architecture is inaccurate (e.g., Graves et al. 2013); remove or temper novelty claims.
- Analysis would be stronger if extended to other tasks (e.g., speech) to assess generalization beyond character-level modeling.
- HF experiments appear computationally expensive.
- Truncated backpropagation (well-established and used by Mikolov) likely would perform well here; empirical confirmation is recommended.
- Overall: interesting and meaningful results, but broader evaluation would make the paper considerably stronger.
