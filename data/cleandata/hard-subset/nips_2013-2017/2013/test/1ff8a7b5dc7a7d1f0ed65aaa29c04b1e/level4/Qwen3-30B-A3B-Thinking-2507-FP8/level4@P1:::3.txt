

The paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), where each layer is a recurrent network processing the hidden state from the layer below, forming a temporal hierarchy. This architecture explicitly addresses the limitation of standard RNNs in handling multi-scale temporal dependencies, as observed in tasks like language modeling. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using simple SGD, and analyze emergent time scales across layers. The work builds on prior hierarchical RNNs [3,5,6,8] but provides deeper empirical analysis of architectural effects, distinguishing it from [8]’s performance-focused approach.

**Strengths:**  
The paper’s core insight—that stacking RNNs inherently creates temporal hierarchies—is compelling and well-motivated. The experimental analysis of layer-specific roles (via output perturbation, text generation, and context-switching tests) provides strong evidence for emergent time-scale differentiation. The results on parentheses closure and text generation (Table 2) are particularly persuasive, revealing how higher layers capture long-term dependencies beyond standard RNNs. The analysis of DRNN-AO’s layer contributions (Figure 2) and the clear demonstration of hierarchical time scales (Figure 3) significantly advance understanding of RNN dynamics. The work is technically sound, well-written, and transparent about limitations (e.g., layer interdependencies in perturbation tests).

**Weaknesses and Suggestions:**  
1. **Parameter Count Comparison:** The comparison to a smaller RNN (2119 units vs. 5×727 units in DRNN-1O) is problematic. The DRNN has ~4.9M parameters vs. the RNN’s ~2.3M (since RNN parameters scale with *n*² for *n* units, while DRNNs add inter-layer weights). This favors DRNNs; a fairer comparison would use an RNN with equivalent parameters or units. For instance, a 2-layer DRNN vs. a single-layer RNN with matching units would better isolate architectural benefits. The current setup risks conflating parameter count advantages with depth.

2. **Gradient Normalization:** The rationale for gradient normalization (to avoid "bifurcations") is unclear. The phrasing "avoid the problem of bifurcations" is inaccurate—this likely refers to *gradient explosions*. The authors should clarify the technical mechanism (e.g., "prevents gradient explosions during training") and cite relevant work, such as the ICML 2013 paper by Pascanu et al. on gradient clipping in RNNs. Additionally, they should address whether normalization affects SGD’s averaging behavior, as noted in the draft.

3. **Layer Role Interpretation:** While the text-generation experiments (Table 2) are convincing, the authors rightly caution against overinterpreting layer removal (line 241). The draft’s concern about "complex interdependencies" is valid; the paper could acknowledge this limitation more explicitly and suggest complementary analyses (e.g., layer-wise input perturbations).

4. **Experimental Scope:** The paper focuses on DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO but doesn’t explore depth vs. width trade-offs. A comparison of DRNNs with varying depths (e.g., 2 vs. 5 layers) trained from scratch—using wider layers to match parameter counts—would strengthen the claim that depth, not just parameters, drives performance. This would address the draft’s suggestion for "deeper architectures" and clarify whether DRNNs outperform standard RNNs *beyond* parameter scaling.

5. **Minor Issues:**  
   - Define acronyms (DRNN-1O, DRNN-AO) in the main text, not just Figure 1.  
   - Correct "IO" to "1O" (one output) in the draft.  
   - Cite Mikolov et al.’s work on language modeling benchmarks for context.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
The paper makes a significant contribution by demonstrating how DRNNs naturally develop temporal hierarchies, with clear empirical validation. While the parameter count comparison weakens the performance claim, the architectural insight and analysis of time-scale emergence are novel and impactful. The work advances both practical modeling (state-of-the-art results) and theoretical understanding (layer dynamics). With minor revisions to address the fairness of comparisons and clarify technical points, this would be a strong candidate for NIPS. The authors’ self-awareness of limitations (e.g., layer interdependencies) and thorough experimental design align well with NIPS’ emphasis on scientific rigor.  

**Recommendation:**  
Accept. The paper’s originality (novel architecture + analysis of temporal hierarchies), significance (improved modeling of multi-scale time series), and quality (sound experiments, clear writing) meet NIPS standards. Addressing the parameter count concern would further strengthen it.
