- Proposes DRNN: a deep recurrent architecture combining hierarchical deep networks with recurrent temporal modeling.  
- Studies stacking recurrent layers where each layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state as input.  
- Applies DRNN to character-level language modeling and trains with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus.  
- Analyzes emergent time scales and long-term dependency behavior within the network.  
- Finds higher layers capture longer-term dependencies and describes parentheses handling as a “pseudo-stable attractor-like state.”  
- Situates work relative to prior deep and recurrent networks and previous hierarchical RNN efforts.  
- Writing, organization, experimental setup, and figures are clear and effective.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, reducing generalization claims.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to other hierarchical sequential models (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).  
- Training time is substantial (~10 days), raising concerns about computational cost and scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical results, and insightful behavioral analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow evaluation, missing baseline comparisons, high computational demands, and need for broader experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, analytically strong paper suitable for NIPS if evaluation is broadened.
