- Reviewer agrees with the “single-task” concern — evaluation is limited to one dataset/task.  
- Recommend testing truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) since it may match bits-per-character at much lower cost, even if it handles parentheses less well.  
- Main empirical findings: deeper recurrent architectures outperform standard RNNs under a fixed parameter budget on character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper layers appear to encode more long-range information than shallower layers.  
- Plain BPTT suffices to train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contradicting the claim that HF is required.  
- The paper should not claim architectural novelty: stacked/stacked recurrent models have been published previously (e.g., Graves 2013).  
- Analysis would be more compelling if replicated on additional domains (e.g., a speech benchmark) to rule out dataset-specific effects.  
- HF experiments seem unnecessary/overkill; prior work and Mikolov’s results suggest truncated BPTT would likely perform well and should be confirmed.
