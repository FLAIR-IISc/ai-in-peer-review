- Proposes a new deep architecture that combines hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-like).
- Training uses layer-by-layer supervised pretraining with a next-letter prediction objective.
- Experiments train very large networks (~10 days) and show improvement over prior work on a Hinton et al. Wikipedia dataset.
- Paper provides analysis: higher layers capture long-term dependencies; parentheses correspondence appears as a “pseudo-stable, attractor-like” state.
- Paper quality is high: well written, experiments well designed, and figures clearly support findings.
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset with little prior work and comparisons only to RNN-style models.
- Missing classical baselines (e.g., hierarchical HMMs and related sequence models) that could address similar tasks.
- Table 1 lists other approaches but reports on a different corpus, so results are not directly comparable.
- Overall contribution is stronger in careful analysis of network behavior than in definitive empirical evidence for a new deep architecture; empirical results are somewhat inconclusive.
