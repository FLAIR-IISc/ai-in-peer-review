1) Deep RNNs (controlled for parameter count) outperform shallower RNNs on character-level language modeling.  
2) Deeper layers in the network encode more long-term information.  
3) Standard BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, countering prior claims that HF optimization was necessary.  
4) The paper incorrectly claims to introduce the deep RNN architecture; stacked/deep RNNs exist in prior work (e.g., Graves ICASSP 2013) — the contribution should be framed as analysis, not architectural novelty.  
5) Experiments are limited to a single task (character-level LM), so the results may not generalize.  
6) The analysis should be replicated on at least one other domain (e.g., speech recognition) to strengthen generality.  
7) The training-method comparison omits a key baseline: truncated BPTT, which is widely used and more computationally efficient than full BPTT or HF.  
8) The authors should evaluate truncated BPTT (e.g., 30-step truncation) to report performance vs. computational cost tradeoffs (entropy vs. efficiency).  
9) Overall the analysis is valuable but the paper needs revision of claims, broader experimentation, and inclusion of important baselines.
