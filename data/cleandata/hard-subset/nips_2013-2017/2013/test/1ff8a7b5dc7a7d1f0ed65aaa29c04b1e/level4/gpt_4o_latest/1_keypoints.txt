- Proposes a novel deep architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN-style).  
- Uses incremental layer-by-layer supervised pre-training with a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Trained very large networks for ~10 days and outperforms prior approaches on a Wikipedia dataset from Hinton et al.  
- Provides detailed analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses correspondence as a "pseudo-stable attractor-like state."  
- Paper is high quality, well-written, and supported by well-designed experiments and informative figures.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset with sparse prior work.  
- Comparisons are confined to RNN-related models; alternative approaches (e.g., hierarchical HMMs) are not evaluated.  
- Table 1 lists other methods but on a different corpus, making direct comparisons infeasible.  
- Main contribution is the in-depth analysis of model behavior rather than definitive empirical superiority; results are somewhat inconclusive.  
- Overall: introduces a valuable architecture and insights but requires a more comprehensive experimental evaluation.
