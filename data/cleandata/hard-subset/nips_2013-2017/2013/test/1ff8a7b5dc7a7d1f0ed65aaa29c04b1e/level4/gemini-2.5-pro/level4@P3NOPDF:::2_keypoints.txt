- Main finding: With parameter count held constant, deep RNNs outperform shallower RNNs on character-level language modeling.
- Representation analysis: Deeper RNN layers encode longer-term dependencies than shallower layers.
- Optimization result: Standard BPTT can train RNNs to solve the parenthesis balancing task, contrary to prior assumptions favoring HF methods.
- Strength: The analysis offers meaningful insights into hierarchical temporal representations in deep recurrent architectures.
- Strength: The demonstration that BPTT succeeds on a long-range synthetic task is surprising and practically useful.
- Weakness: Incorrect claim of introducing deep RNN architecture; prior work (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) already explored deep RNNs.
- Recommendation: Reframe the paper’s contribution as a focused analysis of an existing architecture rather than proposing a new one.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to a single task (character-level language modeling), so generality of conclusions is uncertain.
- Recommendation: Add experiments on a second, distinct sequential task (e.g., speech) to test generality.
- Weakness: Missing a crucial baseline—truncated BPTT—which is widely used and computationally efficient.
- Recommendation: Include comparisons to truncated BPTT (e.g., moderate truncation like 30 steps) to assess practical trade-offs and competitive performance.
