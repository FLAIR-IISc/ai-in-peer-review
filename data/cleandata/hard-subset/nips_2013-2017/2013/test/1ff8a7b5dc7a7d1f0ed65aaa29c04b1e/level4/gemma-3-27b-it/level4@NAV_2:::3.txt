This paper investigates the effect of a hierarchy of recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on processing time series data, motivated by the observation that common RNNs do not explicitly model the temporal hierarchies often present in data. The core idea is to stack recurrent networks, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, effectively combining the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent networks. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling using stochastic gradient descent and provide an analysis of the emergent time scales within the network.

The paper builds upon the existing literature on deep and recurrent neural networks, referencing key works like those of Hinton [4, 8, 9], Hochreiter & Schmidhuber [11], Graves et al. [7], and El Hihi & Bengio [5]. It correctly identifies a gap in previous hierarchical RNN architectures [3, 5, 6, 8] – a lack of detailed analysis of the architectural contribution, often focusing instead on performance optimization. The proposed DRNN structure, described as a multilayer perceptron with temporal feedback loops, is clearly articulated and visually represented in Figure 1.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The DRNN architecture represents a reasonable and intuitive approach to incorporating hierarchical processing into recurrent networks, addressing a limitation of traditional RNNs.
*   **Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on a challenging language modeling task using a relatively simple training algorithm (SGD) is a significant accomplishment.
*   **Analysis:** The detailed analysis of emergent time scales, including experiments with typo perturbations and text generation, provides valuable insights into the internal dynamics of the DRNNs. The exploration of layer contributions through output ablation is also well-conceived.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed architecture, methodology, and results. The authors are forthright about the limitations of their work and potential areas for improvement.
*   **Honesty & Thoroughness:** The authors demonstrate intellectual honesty by acknowledging potential interpretations of their results and outlining future research directions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Gradient Normalization:** The role and necessity of gradient normalization, and its potential impact on the averaging behavior of SGD, is not fully explained. The mention of related work on gradient clipping/truncation from U of Montreal [ICML 2013] is appreciated but could be elaborated upon.
*   **Comparison to Mikolov et al.:** The authors should discuss and compare their results to the work of Mikolov et al. on similar Wikipedia compression tasks.
*   **Layer Abstraction:** While the text generation experiments are persuasive, further investigation is needed to definitively demonstrate that higher layers are truly processing more abstract and long-term properties of the text, rather than simply benefiting from the preceding layers’ processing.
*   **Parameter Count & Fairness of Comparison:** The comparison to the standard RNN might be unfair due to differences in parameter counts. A more compelling comparison would involve DRNNs and standard RNNs with the same number of parameters or units.
*   **Ablation Study Design:** A more systematic ablation study exploring the performance of DRNNs with varying depths and layer widths would be beneficial. Showing a curve of performance versus depth, potentially with wider layers to maintain parameter count, would strengthen the claims.
*   **Terminology:** DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO are not clearly defined in the main text before their use.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant problem in recurrent neural network design.
*   The proposed DRNN architecture offers a novel and promising approach to hierarchical temporal modeling.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.
*   The analysis provides valuable insights into the internal dynamics of the DRNNs.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and intellectually honest.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of a thorough justification for the gradient normalization technique.
*   The potentially unfair comparison to the standard RNN due to differences in parameter counts.
*   The need for further investigation to conclusively demonstrate the abstraction capabilities of the higher layers.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks. While some aspects could be further strengthened, the novelty of the approach, the compelling experimental results, and the thoughtful analysis make it worthy of acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding gradient normalization and a fairer comparison with the baseline RNN.
