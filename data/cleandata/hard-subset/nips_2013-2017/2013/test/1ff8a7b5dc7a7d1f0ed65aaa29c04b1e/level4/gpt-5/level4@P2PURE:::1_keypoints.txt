- Introduces a novel deep architecture that merges hierarchical deep representations with time-series modeling (HMM/RNN spirit).
- Training is layer-wise supervised pretraining using a next-letter prediction objective.
- Large networks trained (~10 days) show improved performance on Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia dataset.
- Detailed analysis shows higher layers capture long-range dependencies and parentheses form a pseudo-stable, attractor-like state.
- Paper is well written, experimental setup is sound, and figures effectively support findings.
- Primary concern: empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset with little prior work for comparison.
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-type models and omit alternatives like hierarchical HMMs that address similar tasks.
- Table 1 lists other approaches but reports results on a different corpus, making direct comparison invalid.
- Main contribution is the thorough analysis of how the network operates rather than a clear demonstration of the new architecture’s superiority.
- Empirical results are therefore somewhat inconclusive and do not fully align with the stated objective of proposing a new combined architecture.
