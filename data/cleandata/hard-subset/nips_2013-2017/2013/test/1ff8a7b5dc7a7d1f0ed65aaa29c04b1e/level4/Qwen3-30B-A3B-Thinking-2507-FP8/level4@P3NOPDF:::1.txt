

The paper introduces a novel deep architecture that integrates hierarchical feature learning from deep networks with temporal modeling inspired by HMMs and RNNs. The training procedure constructs the network layer-by-layer through supervised pre-training on a next-letter prediction task, culminating in a model that achieves improved performance on the Wikipedia dataset from Hinton et al. after approximately 10 days of training. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the network’s internal dynamics, demonstrating that higher layers capture long-term dependencies and that matching parentheses are represented as "pseudo-stable attractor-like states." The writing is clear, experiments are well-designed, and figures effectively support the key findings.

However, the primary limitation lies in the experimental evaluation. The results are reported exclusively on a single dataset (Hinton’s Wikipedia corpus), which lacks established benchmarks for meaningful comparison. Crucially, the comparison to prior work is restricted to RNN-based models (e.g., LSTMs), omitting relevant hierarchical approaches like HMMs or hierarchical HMMs that are standard for similar sequence modeling tasks. Table 1 cites other methods but on different corpora (e.g., Penn Treebank), rendering those comparisons invalid due to inconsistent evaluation settings. This omission undermines the paper’s central claim of a "new deep architecture" that combines hierarchical learning with time-series modeling, as the most directly comparable methods are excluded from the analysis.

Furthermore, the paper’s contribution is misaligned with its stated focus. While the analysis of the network’s operational dynamics is insightful and well-executed, the paper positions itself as introducing a novel architecture with demonstrable performance gains. The results, however, fail to establish clear superiority over established hierarchical models (e.g., hierarchical HMMs or structured RNNs) that solve analogous problems. The performance improvement over prior RNNs is marginal and unverified against the broader class of relevant models, leaving the architectural novelty unproven. The conclusion that the model "improves over previous work" lacks sufficient grounding, as the comparison set is both narrow and methodologically inconsistent.

The analysis of the network’s internal mechanisms—such as the layer-specific handling of dependencies and the attractor-like states for parentheses—adds significant value and would be a compelling contribution in its own right. Yet, this analysis is presented as secondary to the claimed architectural novelty, creating a disconnect between the paper’s execution and its primary thesis. A stronger submission would either demonstrate clear performance gains against a relevant baseline set (including hierarchical models) or refocus the contribution on the analytical insights, explicitly acknowledging the limited empirical validation of the architecture’s novelty.

In summary, the paper is well-written and offers a thoughtful analysis of the model’s behavior. The core weakness, however, is the lack of a rigorous experimental comparison against the most appropriate baseline methods for the claimed contribution. Without this, the paper’s central claim of a novel architecture that successfully merges hierarchical learning with temporal modeling remains unsubstantiated. The results are insufficient to support the paper’s primary contribution, though the analytical depth is commendable. To strengthen the work, the authors should either expand the experimental comparison to include hierarchical HMMs or similar methods on the same dataset, or reframe the contribution to emphasize the interpretative insights rather than architectural novelty.

Given these issues, the paper does not meet the bar for acceptance in its current form. The experimental design fails to validate the claimed contribution, and the narrow comparison set prevents a fair assessment of the architecture’s significance. While the analysis is promising, the lack of empirical evidence for the proposed architecture’s superiority over existing hierarchical approaches is a critical flaw for a venue like NIPS. The authors should address this limitation before reconsideration.
