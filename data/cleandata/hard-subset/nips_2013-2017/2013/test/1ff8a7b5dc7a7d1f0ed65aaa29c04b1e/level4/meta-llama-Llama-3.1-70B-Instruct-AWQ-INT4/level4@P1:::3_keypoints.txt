- Paper proposes deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) as a hierarchical architecture combining DNN and RNN strengths for character-level language modeling.
- Each DRNN layer is an RNN receiving the previous layer’s hidden state, enabling hierarchical temporal processing of sequences.
- DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple SGD.
- The authors analyze emergent layer-wise time scales and find different behaviors for DRNN-AO (outputs at all layers) versus DRNN-1O (output only at top).
- In DRNN-AO, bottom three layers have very short time-scales and only top two show longer memory; in DRNN-1O, top three layers share a very long time-scale.
- Experiments (text generation, output probability analysis) suggest higher layers encode more abstract, long-term properties, though evidence is incomplete.
- Authors transparently discuss limitations and propose future work, including second-order methods like Hessian-free training.
- Main strength: novel, well-motivated architecture with thorough analysis and clear presentation.
- Main weakness: comparisons to a regular RNN are unfair because the baseline has fewer parameters.
- Paper would benefit from additional experiments varying DRNN depth/width and matching parameter counts for baselines.
- Minor fixes: define DRNN-AO/IO in text and provide details on the gradient normalization technique.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10 and reviewer confidence 8/10.
