- Paper proposes hierarchical deep RNN (each layer an RNN receiving previous layer's hidden state) for character-level language modeling.
- Authors show DRNNs reach strong (near state-of-the-art for RNNs) performance on a Wikipedia text corpus using simple SGD.
- Provides insightful analysis of emergent time scales across DRNN layers.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of architecture, experiments, figures, and tables.
- Main contribution is analysis of DRNN behavior rather than a novel architecture.
- Claim of introducing deep RNNs is misleading because similar architectures (e.g., Graves et al. 2013) exist.
- Evaluation is limited to a single task (character-level language modeling), so generality to tasks like speech or vision is unclear.
- No empirical comparison to other training methods (e.g., truncated BPTT, Hessian-free optimization) despite using plain BPTT.
- Authors should clarify novelty and related-work positioning to avoid overstating contribution.
- Paper would be strengthened by experiments on an additional task (e.g., speech recognition) to demonstrate generality.
- Paper would benefit from comparisons to alternative training algorithms to contextualize results.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions addressing novelty clarification, training-method comparisons, and broader evaluation.
