- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) that stack multiple recurrent layers to form a temporal hierarchy.  
- Core idea: each layer processes sequences of hidden states from the layer below, enabling multiple timescales of processing.  
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  
- A 5-layer DRNN trained with plain SGD achieves state-of-the-art performance, comparable to prior Hessian-free optimized models.  
- Paper includes detailed analyses showing emergent temporal hierarchy across layers.  
- Shows the network captures long-range dependencies (e.g., balancing parentheses across long distances).  
- Strength: very clear writing and effective illustrative figures explaining architecture and dynamics.  
- Strength: in-depth analysis provides strong evidence for hierarchical temporal processing beyond raw metrics.  
- Strength: achieving SOTA with SGD suggests architectural gains can rival optimization advances.  
- Weakness: empirical claims rely on a single dataset, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: comparisons are mostly to RNNs; lacks relation to other hierarchical/structured temporal models.  
- Weakness: contribution framing is slightly unfocused—strongest value is analysis rather than clearly stated architectural breakthrough.
