- Proposes a novel hierarchical deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the previous layer's hidden state.  
- Demonstrates effective performance of the DRNN on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough analysis of architecture and experiments.  
- Experiments offer convincing insight that higher DRNN layers capture more abstract, long-term structures (text generation results particularly persuasive).  
- Caution that surgical layer analyses may be hard to interpret due to complex interdependencies between layer outputs.  
- Major concern: comparison to a standard RNN is potentially unfair because the regular RNN has fewer parameters.  
- Suggests evaluating DRNN against an RNN with matched parameter count or units for a fair comparison.  
- Recommends experiments varying DRNN depth and width (train from scratch) to assess depth vs. width tradeoffs.  
- Notes the use of gradient normalization is important but under-explained; request clarification on why it prevents bifurcations.  
- Asks for comparison of gradient normalization to other optimization methods (e.g., gradient truncation).  
- Requests comparison with prior approaches on Wikipedia compression tasks (e.g., Mikolov et al.).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and significant; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions: define DRNN-AO and IO in text, explain gradient normalization and alternatives, add same-parameter RNN baseline, and compare to Mikolov-style approaches.
