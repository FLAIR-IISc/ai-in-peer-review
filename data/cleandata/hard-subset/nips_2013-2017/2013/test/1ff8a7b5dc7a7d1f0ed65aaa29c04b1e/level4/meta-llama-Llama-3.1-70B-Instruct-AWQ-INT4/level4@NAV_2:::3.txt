The paper "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" proposes a novel architecture that combines the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on a character-level language modeling task, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia text corpus. The paper is well-written, and the experiments are thorough and convincing.

The main idea of the paper is to create a hierarchy of recurrent neural networks, where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This architecture allows for hierarchical processing of time series data, which is particularly useful for tasks that require modeling long-term dependencies. The authors show that this approach can capture temporal hierarchies in the data, and that the different layers of the network operate on different time scales.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional recurrent neural networks.
