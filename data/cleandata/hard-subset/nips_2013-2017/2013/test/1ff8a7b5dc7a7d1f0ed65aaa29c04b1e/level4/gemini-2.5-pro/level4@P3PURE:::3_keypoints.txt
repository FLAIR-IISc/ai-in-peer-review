- Introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) hybrid architecture and shows effectiveness on text prediction and compression tasks.  
- Experiments suggest higher DRNN layers learn more abstract, longer-term structures in data.  
- Paper is well-written, honest about limitations, and explores a worthwhile direction in deep temporal networks.  
- Main motivation: in standard RNNs older data receives more processing depth, so a deep RNN architecture is a sensible solution.  
- Concern: empirical comparisons may be unfair because the standard RNN baseline has far fewer parameters than the DRNNs.  
- Suggestion: compare DRNNs to single-layer RNNs with equivalent total units/parameters to control for capacity.  
- Key missing experiment: show higher layers’ advantage is due to depth (e.g., moving those units to the first layer should degrade long-term dependency performance).  
- Alternative/ablation suggestions: try extra intra-timestep non-linear layers within a single recurrent layer and train DRNNs of varying depths with capacity-matched shallower models.  
- Request clarification on the gradient normalization procedure, its role in avoiding bifurcations, and relevant citations (e.g., Pascanu et al. ICML 2013); question whether normalization interferes with SGD averaging.  
- Ask for more baselines on Wikipedia compression (e.g., Mikolov et al.).  
- Caveat: interpreting "brain surgery" layer interventions is difficult due to complex interdependencies—be cautious about strong claims.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the main text rather than only in a figure caption.
