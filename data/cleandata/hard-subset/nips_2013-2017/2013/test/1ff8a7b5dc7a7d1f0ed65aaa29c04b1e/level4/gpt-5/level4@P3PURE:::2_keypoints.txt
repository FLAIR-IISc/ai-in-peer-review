- Reviewer agrees with the critique that the paper evaluates only one task (one-task comment).  
- Suggests trying truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps); it may not learn parentheses but could match character-level entropies at far lower cost and should be tested.  
- Main result: deeper RNNs outperform shallow RNNs when the parameter budget is held constant on character-level language modeling.  
- Deeper layers appear to retain more long-range information than shallower layers.  
- Plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to the belief that HF optimization is required.  
- The paper should not claim to have introduced deep RNNs—similar architectures exist (e.g., Graves ICASSP 2013 and earlier).  
- The analysis might be dataset- or domain-specific; validating on additional tasks (e.g., speech) would make findings more compelling.  
- HF experiments were likely more computationally expensive than necessary; truncated BPTT (used historically, e.g., Mikolov) should be compared empirically.  
- Overall: interesting analytical work, but stronger if extended to other domains (especially speech) and if truncated BPTT comparisons are included.
