- Noted difference in processing older vs newer data; using a deep RNN to handle short-term dependencies is well-motivated and promising.
- Suggest exploring additional nonlinearities between timesteps and asks if this was investigated.
- Requests clarification on the role of gradient normalization in avoiding bifurcation and cites related ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/RNN optimization.
- Asks whether the proposed normalization might interfere with SGD’s averaging effect.
- Requests comparison to Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia compression approach and discussion of its relation to the present results.
- Finds the text generation experiments (Table 2) most convincing but warns about interpreting layer manipulations due to complex RNN interdependencies.
- Raises the key empirical question of whether higher layers truly process more abstract, long-term text properties versus simply emerging post-training.
- Points out a potential parameter-count imbalance between the regular RNN and deeper RNNs (2767^2 > 2119^2) that could bias comparisons.
- Recommends fairer comparisons by matching parameter/unit counts (e.g., two-layer DRNN vs standard RNN with equivalent units) to strengthen conclusions.
- Suggests replacing Figure 2 with results from DRNNs of varying depths trained from scratch, possibly using wider layers to equalize parameters/units.
- Minor request: define DRNN-AO and IO in the main text rather than only in Figure 1.
- Overall: praises the paper’s clarity and honesty, sees DRNNs as a valuable hybrid with strong text-prediction/compression results but wants additional analyses to address the reservations.
