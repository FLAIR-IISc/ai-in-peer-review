

I appreciate the insight regarding the arbitrariness of older data benefiting from potentially more layers of processing compared to newer data. Constructing a deep RNN appears plausible for improving short-term performance, with additional advantages possible. An obvious extension would be inserting extra nonlinear layers between timesteps—have you explored this approach?  

Regarding gradient normalization, how critical was this step, and why does it "avoid the problem of bifurcations"? A recent ICML 2013 paper by U of Montreal researchers on gradient truncation and RNN optimization may be relevant. Additionally, could this normalization disrupt SGD’s "averaging" behavior?  

For context, earlier work on Wikipedia compression tasks (e.g., Mikolov et al.) should be compared. Among experiments probing layer roles across timescales and abstraction, the text generation results (Table 2) are most convincing. However, as noted on line 241, interpreting "brain surgery" effects on RNNs is complicated by complex layer interdependencies.  

The most significant gap is whether higher layers genuinely process more abstract/long-term text properties than if units were relocated to the first layer. Their post-training emergence of such roles is suggestive but insufficient evidence of nontrivial benefit from hierarchical processing.  

The regular RNN uses fewer parameters (2767^2 5 > 2119^2, counting recurrent and inter-layer weights), potentially skewing comparisons. A fairer test would compare deeper RNNs to a standard RNN with identical parameters or units (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs. equivalent-unit RNN), which would strengthen the paper’s claims.  

Figure 2 would be more informative if it showed benchmark performance across DRNN depths trained from scratch, with wider layers compensating for parameter/unit differences.  

Minor: Define DRNN-AO and IO in the main text, not solely in Figure 1.  

This paper examines hybrid deep-recurrent networks (DRNNs) for text prediction/compression, demonstrating strong performance. A substantial portion details experiments arguing that higher layers capture abstract/long-term structures—these are largely convincing, though I have reservations outlined above. The work is intellectually honest, thorough about limitations, and well-written. I believe exploring such deep temporal architectures offers valuable insights into their trained dynamics.
