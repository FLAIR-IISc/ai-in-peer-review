This paper introduces a Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) architecture, aiming to combine the hierarchical processing of deep learning with the temporal modeling capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The authors investigate this architecture through character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus, demonstrating competitive performance and offering an analysis of the emergent temporal hierarchy within the network.

The DRNN builds upon existing work in both deep learning and recurrent networks. The motivation, drawing a contrast between the memory-focused nature of traditional RNNs and the hierarchical processing of DNNs, is well articulated. The authors appropriately reference related work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6] and stacked LSTMs [8], highlighting their contributions and limitations. The connection to deep belief networks [9] and autoencoders [10, 20] in the conclusions suggests an awareness of broader research directions.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The motivation is compelling, and the architecture is explained with sufficient detail. Figures 1 and 2 effectively illustrate the network structure and experimental results.
* **Thorough Analysis:** A significant strength lies in the analysis of the trained DRNNs. The investigation into time scales (Figure 3) and the examination of parenthesis matching demonstrate a genuine effort to understand *how* the network functions, rather than solely focusing on performance numbers. The observation of “pseudo-stable attractor-like states” for parenthesis matching is particularly insightful.
* **Competitive Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on the Wikipedia corpus is a notable accomplishment, especially considering the use of relatively simple stochastic gradient descent. The comparison to Hessian-free optimization [19] is valuable.
* **Complete Work:** The paper feels complete, presenting a well-defined problem, a novel architecture, experimental results, and insightful analysis. The discussion of limitations and future work is also commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Dataset & Comparisons:** The evaluation is primarily confined to a single dataset (Wikipedia) and a limited set of recurrent network baselines. While the comparison to [19] is relevant, a broader comparison to other hierarchical models – such as hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) – that tackle similar sequence modeling tasks would strengthen the paper. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but it remains a concern. The lack of comparability across datasets, as noted in the paper, reduces the impact of the performance gains.
* **Computational Cost:** Training these networks requires substantial computational resources (10 days on a GPU). This high cost limits reproducibility and makes it challenging for other researchers to build upon this work. While not a fatal flaw, it is a practical consideration.
* **Layer-wise Training Heuristic:** The incremental layer-wise training strategy (DRNN-1O) feels somewhat ad-hoc. While effective, the justification beyond alleviating the vanishing gradient problem could be expanded.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture.
* The analysis of the network's behavior is insightful and goes beyond mere performance reporting.
* The results are competitive and demonstrate the potential of DRNNs.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset and a narrow range of baselines.
* The computational cost of training is high.
* The layer-wise training strategy, while effective, lacks strong theoretical justification.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The novel architecture, combined with the thorough analysis, sets it apart. While the limited scope of the experiments is a concern, the insights gained from the analysis justify acceptance. The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the existing literature and proposes a compelling approach to addressing the challenges of modeling complex temporal dependencies.

**Recommendation:**

Accept. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS 2013. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and the paper offers a promising direction for future research. I encourage the authors to consider expanding the evaluation to other datasets and exploring more theoretically grounded training strategies in future work.
