- Paper explores DRNNs for text prediction, arguing hierarchical/deeper layers capture long-term dependencies while shallower layers handle short-term dynamics.  
- Observation that older data benefits from deeper processing is interesting; DRNN architecture is promising for mixed temporal contexts.  
- Suggest exploring extra nonlinear layers between timesteps as a natural architectural extension.  
- Request clarification on gradient normalization: why it’s necessary and the claim it “avoids bifurcations.”  
- Cite ICML 2013 U of Montreal work on gradient truncation for relevant context.  
- Concern that normalizing gradients could disrupt SGD’s averaging behavior and affect convergence or generalization.  
- Empirical comparison to Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia compression tasks would strengthen positioning.  
- Text generation results (Table 2) are most compelling, but “brain surgery” interventions are hard to interpret due to complex inter-layer dependencies, limiting causal claims.  
- Major gap: need to show higher layers require hierarchical processing—post-training role emergence isn’t proof; propose relocating higher-layer units to first layer (preserving total parameters) to test performance impact.  
- Parameter-count imbalance between RNN and DRNN makes comparisons unfair; recommend comparing with identical total parameters/units (e.g., single-layer RNN matching DRNN hidden sizes).  
- Figure 2 should include experiments training deeper DRNNs from scratch with wider layers to determine if depth itself, not size, drives gains.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and IO in the main text earlier rather than only in Figure 1.  
- Overall: valuable and accessible contribution with thorough experiments and honest limitations; suggested refinements would significantly strengthen the claims.
