- Suggest trying truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) as a cheaper baseline; it may match entropies and should be tested for parentheses balancing.
- Main finding: with fixed parameter count, deeper RNNs outperform shallower RNNs on character-level language modeling.
- Deeper networks capture more long-term information according to the paper's analysis.
- Plain BPTT can train these models to balance parentheses, counter to prior claims that HF-like optimizers are required.
- Paper incorrectly claims to introduce the deep RNN architecture; similar architectures appeared earlier (e.g., Graves et al., ICASSP 2013).
- Results may be domain-specific; demonstrating the findings on additional tasks (e.g., speech) would strengthen the conclusions.
- HF experiments were likely unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT is a well-established, efficient baseline that should be compared.
