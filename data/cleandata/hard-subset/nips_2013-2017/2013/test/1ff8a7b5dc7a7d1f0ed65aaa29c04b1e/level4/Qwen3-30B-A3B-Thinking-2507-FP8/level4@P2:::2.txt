

This paper investigates deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, proposing a hierarchical architecture where each layer processes information from the previous layer. The authors claim this architecture better captures temporal hierarchies in time series data, and demonstrate state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character prediction using simple stochastic gradient descent (SGD), rather than more complex methods like Hessian-free optimization.

The paper makes three main contributions: (1) demonstrating DRNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter counts are fixed; (2) analyzing how DRNNs develop distinct time scales across layers; and (3) showing that standard BPTT can train RNNs to balance parentheses (previously thought to require specialized methods).

The paper's most significant strength is its empirical evidence that DRNNs with fixed parameter counts achieve better performance than standard RNNs on character-level language modeling. The analysis of temporal hierarchies across layers is particularly compelling, showing how different layers process information at different time scales. The authors provide evidence that deeper layers capture longer-term dependencies, as demonstrated through the parentheses-balancing task where DRNNs outperform standard RNNs.

However, the paper has notable weaknesses. First, the claim that the DRNN architecture is novel is inaccurate. As noted in the rebuttal, Graves et al. (ICASSP 2013) used stacked bidirectional LSTM networks for speech recognition, and the architecture has been used in prior work. The authors should acknowledge this and reframe their contribution as an analysis of the architecture rather than its introduction.

Second, the paper's analysis is limited to character-level language modeling, which may not generalize to other domains. To strengthen their claims about temporal hierarchies, the authors should demonstrate similar findings on other tasks, such as speech recognition, where temporal hierarchies are well-documented. Without this, the observed phenomena might be specific to the language modeling task rather than a general property of DRNNs.

Third, the paper's claim that standard BPTT can train RNNs to balance parentheses is interesting but requires more context. The authors should address why truncated BPTT (a standard technique since the 1990s, successfully used by Mikolov for language modeling) wasn't sufficient for this task, or verify that it would be ineffective. The paper's dismissal of truncated BPTT as inadequate for this task lacks sufficient justification.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, with clear methodology and thorough experimental analysis. The results are well-supported by experiments, though the limited scope of the analysis weakens the generalizability of the conclusions. The authors provide appropriate references to related work, though they should correct the claim of novelty.

In terms of significance, the paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding how hierarchical processing in RNNs affects temporal modeling. The demonstration that DRNNs can learn to balance parentheses with standard BPTT is noteworthy, though the practical significance is limited by the narrow scope of the experiments. The paper's claim that DRNNs better capture temporal hierarchies is plausible but not fully validated by the current experiments.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by: (1) correcting the claim of novelty regarding the DRNN architecture; (2) expanding the analysis to additional tasks like speech recognition; and (3) providing a more thorough comparison with truncated BPTT approaches. Without these additions, the paper's contributions remain somewhat limited to a single task.

Regarding the four review criteria:
- Quality: The experiments are well-designed and results are well-supported, though the analysis scope is limited.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written with clear figures and explanations.
- Originality: The core architecture is not novel, though the analysis of temporal hierarchies is valuable.
- Significance: The insights are interesting but limited to one domain, reducing their broader impact.

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to understanding hierarchical processing in recurrent networks, but its claims are overstated due to the limited scope of the experiments and the inaccurate novelty claim. The core findings about temporal hierarchies are compelling, but the paper would benefit from broader validation across multiple domains to strengthen the generalizability of the results.

I recommend rejection due to the uncorrected novelty claim and limited scope of the analysis. The authors should revise to acknowledge prior work on the DRNN architecture, expand their experiments to additional tasks, and provide a more thorough comparison with standard training methods like truncated BPTT. With these revisions, the paper could make a stronger contribution to the field.
