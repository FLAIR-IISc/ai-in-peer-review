- DRNNs outperform shallow RNNs for a fixed parameter budget and achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling results.  
- The paper shows DRNNs can be trained with standard SGD and full BPTT to learn complex long-range dependencies (e.g., balancing parentheses) previously shown with HF methods.  
- Analysis reveals a temporal hierarchy: deeper layers operate on longer time scales and contribute differently to predictions.  
- Results are clearly motivated and well-presented, with convincing experiments supporting the hierarchical temporal hypothesis.  
- Claim of architectural novelty is overstated; the DRNN design has prior art (e.g., Graves et al., ICASSP 2013) and should be reframed as analysis of an existing architecture.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single task (Wikipedia character-level modeling), leaving generality to other domains (e.g., speech) untested.  
- Experimental comparison omits practical baselines like truncated BPTT (TBPTT), which may achieve similar results at lower computational cost.  
- Recommendation: reframe contribution as detailed analysis, add experiments on a second domain, and include TBPTT comparisons to strengthen the paper.
