- Authors’ observation that older data benefits from more extensive processing is valuable; suggest trying deeper RNNs and extra nonlinearities between timesteps.  
- Role of gradient normalization in avoiding “bifurcations” is unclear; request a detailed explanation and note relevant ICML 2013 work on gradient truncation/optimization.  
- Question whether gradient normalization might interfere with SGD’s “averaging” behavior.  
- For Wikipedia compression, compare results to Mikolov et al.  
- Text generation results (Table 2) are the most compelling, but “brain surgery” interpretations are problematic due to complex interdependencies.  
- Key empirical gap: unclear if higher layers truly learn abstract/long-term features or simply benefit from extra processing steps.  
- Current comparisons may be unfair because the regular RNN has fewer parameters; require comparisons with equal-parameter/-unit baselines (e.g., 2-layer DRNN vs standard RNN with same units).  
- Instead of Figure 2, show performance across DRNN depths trained from scratch, possibly widening layers to match parameters/units.  
- Minor: define DRNN-AO and IO in the text, not only in Figure 1.  
- Overall: DRNN hybrid approach is interesting and promising for text prediction/compression; paper is well-written and transparent but needs additional experiments to strengthen claims.
