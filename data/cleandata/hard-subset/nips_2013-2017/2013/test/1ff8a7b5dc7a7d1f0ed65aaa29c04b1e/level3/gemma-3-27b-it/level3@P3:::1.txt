This paper proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling (RNNs). The authors introduce a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure via next-letter prediction and demonstrate state-of-the-art character-level language modeling performance on a Wikipedia dataset, requiring substantial training time (≈10 days). A key strength is the detailed analysis of network behavior, revealing how layers capture different timescales and how parentheses matching emerges as an attractor-like state. The paper is well-written, clear, and the figures effectively support the claims.

The work builds upon the established success of deep neural networks (DNNs) in tasks like speech and image recognition (Hinton et al., 2006; Ciresan et al., 2012) by applying a hierarchical structure to recurrent networks. While RNNs offer memory capabilities, the authors argue they don’t explicitly capture temporal hierarchies. Previous work exploring hierarchical RNNs exists (Chen et al., 2009; El Hihi & Bengio, 2012), but often focuses on artificial tasks or performance without in-depth analysis. Specifically, the paper references work by Martens & Sutskever (2013) which achieved strong results on the same dataset but with a more computationally intensive Hessian-free optimization approach. The DRNN architecture aims to bridge this gap, offering competitive performance with a simpler training method (SGD).

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Architecture:** The DRNN architecture effectively combines the strengths of DNNs (hierarchical processing) and RNNs (temporal modeling), offering a potentially powerful approach to time-series analysis.
* **Strong Analysis:** The detailed analysis of network behavior – particularly the emergence of different timescales and the parentheses matching phenomenon – is a significant contribution. This provides valuable insight into *how* the model operates, beyond simply demonstrating performance gains. The visualization of layer contributions for parenthesis matching (Figure 4) is particularly compelling.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are accessible. Figures are well-designed and effectively illustrate key concepts.
* **Solid Empirical Results:**  Achieving state-of-the-art (for recurrent networks) performance on a challenging task (character-level language modeling) is a commendable achievement, even with the caveats discussed below.
* **Layer-wise Pre-training:** The layer-by-layer pre-training procedure offers a practical approach to training very deep networks and could be valuable to other researchers.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Evaluation:** The primary limitation is the evaluation on a single dataset (the Hinton et al. Wikipedia corpus). While a substantial dataset, it lacks diversity and makes it difficult to generalize the findings.
* **Restricted Comparison:** Comparisons are largely limited to RNN-related models. Crucially, the paper acknowledges the exclusion of hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs), a relevant alternative approach to hierarchical time-series modeling. The justification that other approaches use different corpora is weak, as BPC is a standard metric allowing for cross-corpus comparison.
* **Inconclusive Gains:**  While the DRNN achieves state-of-the-art *for recurrent networks*, the gains over previous work (Martens & Sutskever, 2013) are modest and achieved with a significantly longer training time. The paper doesn't definitively demonstrate a clear advantage over more sophisticated optimization techniques applied to simpler RNN architectures.
* **Training Cost:** The 10-day training time is a substantial barrier to reproducibility and wider adoption of the method. 



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The DRNN architecture is a novel and promising approach to hierarchical time-series modeling.
* The in-depth analysis provides valuable insights into the inner workings of deep recurrent networks.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.
* The experimental results, while limited, are encouraging.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset.
* The comparison to relevant non-RNN approaches is lacking.
* The performance gains are modest, especially considering the substantial training cost.
* The paper's central claim – that DRNNs represent a significant advance in character-level language modeling – is not entirely convincing based on the presented evidence.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution, particularly in its analysis of network behavior. However, the limitations in evaluation and comparison prevent it from being a truly groundbreaking work. While the performance gains are encouraging, they are not decisive. The paper feels more like a solid exploration of a promising architecture than a definitive breakthrough.  I would rate this as a **weak accept**. The strengths, particularly the insightful analysis, outweigh the weaknesses, but addressing the limitations (especially broadening the evaluation) would significantly strengthen the contribution.
