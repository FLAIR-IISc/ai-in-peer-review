- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture combining deep and recurrent nets.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on Wikipedia character-level language modeling.  
- Provides thorough experiments and convincing empirical results supporting the approach.  
- Gives an in-depth analysis of the network’s ability to capture temporal hierarchies and long-term dependencies.  
- Shows emergence of different time scales across DRNN layers.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible with a concise explanation of architecture and advantages.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; meets reviewer criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Lacks direct comparison to other state-of-the-art models (e.g., Mikolov et al.).  
- Insufficient insight into the role of gradient normalizations and how they prevent bifurcations.  
- Suggests exploring extra nonlinear layers between timesteps to potentially improve performance.  
- Unclear significance for broader NLP beyond character-level modeling; needs discussion of potential applications and wider impact.  
- Overall a strong contribution but could be strengthened by broader comparisons, deeper analysis of training mechanisms, and discussion of wider applicability.
