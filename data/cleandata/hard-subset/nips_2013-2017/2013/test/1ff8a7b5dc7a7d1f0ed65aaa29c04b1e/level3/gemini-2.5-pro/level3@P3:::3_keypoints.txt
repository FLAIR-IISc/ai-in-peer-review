- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN): stacked RNN layers where each layer receives hidden states from the layer below to enable hierarchical temporal processing.
- Motivation: combine RNN memory with deep hierarchical feature extraction to handle multi-scale temporal dependencies (e.g., language).
- Empirical result: 5-layer DRNN trained with simple SGD on Wikipedia character modeling is competitive with more complex models (MRNN) and second-order methods (Hessian-free).
- Representation analysis: different layers appear to operate at different time scales and capture very long-range dependencies.
- Strength: well-motivated, intuitive core idea that older data benefits from more recurrent iterations and depth can improve short-term processing better than width.
- Writing and analysis are clear; text generation ablation convincingly shows functional hierarchy across layers.
- Main experimental weakness: baseline comparison may be unfair despite matched parameter counts; depth vs parameterization trade-off not resolved.
- Missing experiments: compare to single-layer RNN with same hidden units per layer and to wider single-layer RNNs with comparable or greater parameter counts.
- Figure 2 (“surgery” analysis) is potentially misleading due to complex interdependencies; recommend plotting test performance vs depth with models trained from scratch and matched-width baselines.
- Training method concern: gradient normalization heuristic (Eq.3) is critical but under-justified; need explanation of why L2 scaling addresses bifurcation/large gradients.
- Related work omission: must cite and discuss Pascanu et al. (ICML 2013) on exploding/vanishing gradients and gradient norm clipping.
- Theoretical concern: normalizing gradients every step may change update directions and disrupt SGD’s averaging, especially with decaying learning rates.
- Architecture suggestions: consider adding intermediate nonlinearities between timesteps to allow more complex interactions.
- Additional comparisons requested: benchmark against Mikolov et al.’s Wikipedia language modeling work for direct state-of-the-art comparison.
- Interpretation caution: top-layer role may be confounded because it uniquely receives processed inputs from all layers; clarify DRNN-AO/DRNN-IO definitions in main text.
- Overall summary: promising and influential idea with strong results and analysis, but needs stronger controlled baselines and deeper discussion of the optimization technique to fully substantiate depth benefits.
