- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks multiple RNN layers into a temporal hierarchy, with each layer taking the hidden state of the layer below as input.  
- Novelty lies in combining deep hierarchical feature processing with sequential RNN modeling and emphasizing architectural innovation over optimization tricks.  
- Builds on prior RNN work (e.g., Sutskever et al. 2011) but distinguishes itself by focusing on model architecture and learned behavior analysis.  
- Proposes a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training scheme to ease training of the deep architecture.  
- Major strength is a thorough, insightful analysis of DRNN internal dynamics showing layers operate on different time scales.  
- Experiments demonstrate higher layers capture longer-term dependencies, and parenthesis-matching results suggest pseudo-stable, attractor-like states for long-range syntax.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear, supportive figures that illustrate model behavior.  
- Achieves competitive performance on the Wikipedia dataset using simpler SGD, comparable to Hessian-free optimization methods.  
- Limitation: evaluation on a single, not widely-used dataset limits assessment of generalizability.  
- Limitation: comparisons are restricted to RNN-based baselines and omit alternatives (e.g., hierarchical HMMs), making state-of-the-art claims inconclusive.  
- Overall: empirical superiority is not definitive, but the novel architecture and detailed analytical insights constitute a valuable contribution.
