- Proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) combining hierarchical deep learning with RNN time-series modeling.  
- Introduces layer-by-layer supervised pre-training via next-letter prediction.  
- Reports state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus (training ≈10 days).  
- Provides detailed analysis showing layers capture different timescales and parentheses matching emerges as an attractor-like state.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and effective figures.  
- Novel architecture that marries hierarchical processing (DNNs) with temporal memory (RNNs).  
- Layer-wise pre-training is a practical method for training very deep recurrent networks.  
- Empirical results are solid but evaluated on a single dataset, limiting generalization.  
- Comparisons are largely restricted to RNN models and omit relevant alternatives like hierarchical HMMs.  
- Performance gains over prior work (Martens & Sutskever) are modest and come at much higher training cost.  
- Long training time (~10 days) poses reproducibility and adoption challenges.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, insightful network analysis, clear presentation, encouraging results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation, missing non-RNN comparisons, modest gains vs high computational cost, central claim not fully convincing.  
- Overall: strong, insightful paper but not definitive; reviewer rates it as a weak accept and recommends broader evaluation to strengthen the contribution.
