- Investigates deep recurrent (hierarchical) RNNs for time-series, focusing on character-level language modeling.  
- Core idea: layered RNN architecture where each layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state to combine hierarchical feature extraction with temporal modeling.  
- Introduces layer-wise supervised pre-training using a next-letter prediction objective.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on a large Wikipedia corpus with very large networks and long training (~10 days).  
- Strong, detailed analysis showing higher layers capture longer-term dependencies.  
- Identifies emergent behavior (parentheses matching) resembling pseudo-stable attractor-like states.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented with supportive experiments and figures.  
- Main weakness: evaluation limited to a single, not widely adopted dataset (Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia corpus).  
- Comparisons restricted mainly to other RNNs, omitting hierarchical HMMs and other relevant architectures.  
- Performance gains are promising but inconclusive due to restricted benchmarking.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; authors should address dataset limitation and broaden comparative analysis.  
- Suggestion: further explore the attractor-like states and their implications to strengthen the contribution.
