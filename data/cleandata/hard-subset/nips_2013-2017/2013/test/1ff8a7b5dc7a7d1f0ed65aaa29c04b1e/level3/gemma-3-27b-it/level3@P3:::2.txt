This paper investigates the usefulness of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for processing time series, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is to stack RNNs, creating a hierarchy where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer, aiming to capture temporal hierarchies inherent in sequential data. The authors demonstrate that deeper RNNs, with a comparable number of parameters to standard RNNs, achieve state-of-the-art performance on character-level language modeling. They also present an analysis suggesting that deeper layers encode information over longer time scales, and surprisingly find that plain BPTT can successfully train the network to balance parentheses, a task previously thought to require Hessian-free training or similar advanced methods.

The work builds upon prior research in deep learning and recurrent neural networks. The resurgence of deep learning (e.g., [4, 17]) highlighted the benefits of hierarchical processing. While RNNs can be viewed as deep networks unfolded in time, the authors rightly point out their primary function is memory, not hierarchical processing. Previous hierarchical RNN architectures exist ([3, 5, 6]), but the authors claim novelty in their specific implementation. The paper acknowledges work by Graves [7] utilizing stacked bi-directional LSTMs, yet argues their work focused primarily on performance rather than architectural analysis. 

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a challenging task (character-level language modeling), achieving state-of-the-art results with a relatively simple training method (SGD).
* **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of time scales encoded within different layers of the DRNN is a key strength. The experiments showing different layers responding to perturbations at different rates, and the visualization of parenthesis balancing, offer valuable insights into the network's behavior.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, architecture, and experimental setup.
* **Relevance:** The investigation into the depth of RNNs is a relevant and important topic in the field of sequential data modeling.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Questionable Novelty:** The claim of introducing a new "deep RNN" architecture is weak. As the rebuttal acknowledges (and as pointed out by the authors themselves in the paper referencing [8]), similar architectures have been used previously, particularly by Graves [7]. The focus should be on the analysis rather than presenting the architecture as a novel contribution.
* **Task Specificity:** The results are demonstrated solely on character-level language modeling. The authors themselves acknowledge this limitation and suggest future work on other tasks. It is unclear whether the observed benefits of depth will generalize to other sequential data types (e.g., speech, audio, time series).
* **Potential Inefficiency of BPTT:** While the successful use of BPTT is a positive finding, the authors do not thoroughly investigate the computational cost. Truncated BPTT, as used by Mikolov et al., could likely achieve similar performance at a significantly lower cost. The authors should explore this possibility. Specifically, I suggest trying a truncated BPTT with around 30 steps. It might miss some very long-range dependencies encoded in the full BPTT, but it could dramatically reduce the computational burden without substantially impacting performance or entropy.
* **Limited Exploration of Output Connections:** The comparison between DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO is valuable, but further exploration of output connection strategies could be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents strong empirical evidence supporting the benefits of depth in RNNs for a challenging task.
* The analysis of time scales provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent networks.
* The work is well-written and clearly presented.
* Even if the architectural contribution is limited, the detailed analysis is a worthwhile contribution to the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The claim of architectural novelty is weak.
* The results may be task-specific and not generalize to other domains.
* The authors haven’t explored more efficient training methods like truncated BPTT.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to understanding the behavior of deep recurrent neural networks. While the claim of architectural novelty is overstated, the detailed analysis and strong empirical results justify acceptance. The authors should be encouraged to investigate the computational efficiency of truncated BPTT and explore the generalizability of their findings to other tasks. Replicating the analysis on, for example, speech recognition data would significantly strengthen the claims.

**Recommendation:** Accept. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the efficiency of BPTT and more clearly framing the work as an analysis of existing architectures rather than a novel architectural proposal.
