This paper, "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks," explores the concept of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) and their application to character-level language modeling. The authors propose a hierarchical architecture that combines the strengths of deep neural networks (DNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs), allowing for more natural capture of temporal hierarchies in time series data.

The paper's main contributions are threefold. Firstly, it demonstrates that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus, outperforming standard RNNs when the number of parameters is held constant. Secondly, it provides an analysis of the emergent time scales in DRNNs, showing that deeper layers encode more long-range information. Finally, it shows that plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs that Hessian-free (HF) optimization was necessary.

The paper's strengths include its thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture and its application to a real-world task. The authors provide a clear explanation of the architecture and its components, as well as a detailed description of the training setup and experimental results. The paper also includes a number of insightful experiments that shed light on the behavior of DRNNs, such as the analysis of time scales and the ability to model long-term dependencies.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. One criticism is that the authors claim to have introduced the DRNN architecture, when in fact similar models have been proposed in prior work (e.g., Graves, 2013). Additionally, the paper's focus on analysis rather than proposing a new architecture may limit its impact. Furthermore, the results may be task-specific, and it is unclear whether the findings would generalize to other tasks, such as speech recognition.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by experimental results. The authors are careful to evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it easy to follow for both experts and non-experts.

In terms of originality, the paper builds on existing work in the field, but the combination of DNNs and RNNs in a hierarchical architecture is novel. The paper provides a unique perspective on the strengths and weaknesses of DRNNs and their application to character-level language modeling.

In terms of significance, the paper's results are important, as they demonstrate the potential of DRNNs for modeling complex temporal hierarchies. The paper's findings may be of interest to researchers and practitioners working on natural language processing, speech recognition, and other applications involving time series data.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the DRNN architecture and its application to a real-world task.
* The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia-based text corpus.
* The paper sheds light on the behavior of DRNNs, including their ability to model long-term dependencies and their emergent time scales.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper's focus on analysis rather than proposing a new architecture may limit its impact.
* The results may be task-specific, and it is unclear whether the findings would generalize to other tasks.
* The authors claim to have introduced the DRNN architecture, when in fact similar models have been proposed in prior work.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the criticisms mentioned above.
