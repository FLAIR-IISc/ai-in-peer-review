- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers to capture hierarchical temporal processing and multiple time scales.  
- Each DRNN layer receives the previous layer’s hidden state, enabling hierarchical capture of temporal dependencies.  
- Analysis shows higher layers capture long-term dependencies and reveals parentheses-matching as a pseudo-stable attractor-like behavior.  
- Main contribution is the in-depth analysis of how the model operates and hierarchically processes time series.  
- Experiments and figures are clear, supportive, and demonstrate the DRNN can capture complex temporal relationships.  
- Shows improved performance versus other RNN-related models on a Wikipedia dataset.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, well-organized, and carefully evaluates strengths and weaknesses.  
- Originality stems from combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling and providing novel behavioral analysis.  
- Significance lies in contributing to training-algorithm debates and potential impact on NLP tasks needing complex temporal modeling.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single, not widely used dataset, reducing generalizability.  
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models and omit hierarchical HMMs and other approaches on different corpora.  
- Some results are inconclusive relative to stated goals, with performance gains not consistently demonstrated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, thorough behavioral analysis, clear experiments, and detailed RNN comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited dataset, narrow comparisons, incomparability with other corpora, and inconsistent performance evidence.
