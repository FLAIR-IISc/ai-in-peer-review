- Central result: Deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when parameter count is held constant, and plain BPTT can achieve state-of-the-art on character-level language modeling.
- Analysis finding: Deeper layers encode longer-range information; learned time scales and parentheses experiment convincingly demonstrate long-term dependency modeling.
- Motivation and positioning: Well-motivated work that situates itself relative to hierarchical RNNs and stacked LSTMs, though prior similar architectures exist.
- Experimental setup: Thorough experiments, including layer contribution analysis, time-scale visualizations, and long-range dependency tests (Figure 3 highlighted).
- Strength — empirical results: Strong performance gains and SOTA with simple SGD/BPTT.
- Strength — analysis: Insightful investigation of learned time scales and layer roles; visualizations are informative.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Strength — relevance: Character-level language modeling is an important and timely task.
- Weakness — novelty: Initial presentation overstated architectural novelty; similar models have been explored previously and should be acknowledged up front.
- Weakness — task specificity: Results are demonstrated mainly on one task, so generalization to other domains (e.g., speech) is uncertain.
- Weakness — computational cost: Full BPTT is expensive; truncated BPTT might be a more efficient alternative worth testing.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Limited discussion and analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters like learning rate and layer size.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound and claims are generally supported by experiments.
- Clarity assessment: Paper is well-organized and clear.
- Originality assessment: Main novelty is in the analysis of time scales and layer contributions rather than the architecture.
- Significance assessment: Significant because it shows deep RNN benefits and questions the necessity of Hessian-free optimization for this task.
- Arguments for acceptance: Demonstrates performance improvement, provides valuable analysis, challenges optimization assumptions, and is well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: Overstated novelty, limited task breadth, and computational concerns with full BPTT.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: Explicitly acknowledge prior related work in the introduction; discuss task generality and future evaluations on other domains; run experiments with truncated BPTT to compare efficiency and performance; add discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.
