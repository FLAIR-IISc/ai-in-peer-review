- Proposes a novel Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) combining deep layers where each layer is an RNN receiving the previous layer’s hidden state.
- Architecture enables hierarchical processing of time-series and captures temporal hierarchies across layers.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia character-level language modeling task.
- Provides in-depth analysis showing emergence of different time scales per layer and improved modeling of long-term dependencies.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture and experimental setup.
- Key strength: effectively captures temporal hierarchy and long-term dependencies better than traditional RNNs.
- Main limitation: lacks comparisons to other architectures matched for number of parameters or units, making effectiveness unclear.
- Insufficient analysis of gradient normalizations and how the approach avoids bifurcation issues.
- Missing experiments to test whether higher layers truly benefit from upstream processing versus merely reallocating units to lower layers.
- With additional fair comparisons and analyses, the work could make a significant contribution to deep learning.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical results, and insightful behavioral analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: unfair/insufficient baselines, limited analysis of training dynamics, and need for more targeted experiments.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
