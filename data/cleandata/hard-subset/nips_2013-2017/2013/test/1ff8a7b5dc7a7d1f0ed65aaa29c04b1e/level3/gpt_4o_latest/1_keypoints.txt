- Novel DRNN architecture that stacks recurrent layers to capture temporal hierarchies and combines DNN hierarchical processing with RNN temporal modeling.
- Evaluated on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia) achieving state-of-the-art for RNN-based models and providing in-depth analysis of network behavior.
- Strength: Introduces a novel approach bridging DNNs and RNNs with insightful analysis of emergent time scales and pseudo-stable states (e.g., parenthesis correspondence).
- Strength: Well-written and clearly presented with effective figures, tables, and reproducible technical details.
- Strength: Thoughtful experimental design (layer-wise output contributions, perturbation tests) demonstrating long-term dependency modeling and hierarchical processing.
- Strength: Competitive performance, outperforming standard RNNs and matching more complex architectures trained with advanced techniques.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single Wikipedia dataset, reducing generalizability to other domains like speech or other time series.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison scope—missing thorough baselines against hierarchical models and modern RNN variants (LSTM, GRU).
- Weakness: Performance improvements are marginal, with primary contribution being analysis rather than clear practical gains.
- Weakness: High training cost (10 days on a single GPU) raises concerns about scalability and computational efficiency.
- For acceptance: Novel, well-motivated architecture addressing hierarchical temporal modeling with analysis likely to inspire future work.
- For acceptance: Paper is well-documented and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
- Against acceptance: Limited evaluation and comparisons, modest gains, and high computational cost question broader impact.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing dataset diversity and expanding comparative analysis in future work.
