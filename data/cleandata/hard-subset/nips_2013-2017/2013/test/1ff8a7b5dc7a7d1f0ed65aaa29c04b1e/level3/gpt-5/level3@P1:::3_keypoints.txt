- Central claim: Stacking vanilla RNN layers yields emergent temporal hierarchies where lower layers capture short-range and higher layers capture longer-range dependencies.
- Empirical result: DRNNs trained with simple SGD achieve competitive bits-per-character (BPC) on large Wikipedia character-language modeling versus Hessian-free RNNs.
- Models: Two variants studied — single output head at top (DRNN-1O) and outputs at all layers (DRNN-AO).
- Analysis: Layer-wise time scales probed via perturbation experiments, parentheses-closing analysis, and text generation showing long-term structure.
- Novelty: Not the idea of stacking itself, but a systematic empirical study showing simple stacked tanh RNNs trained with SGD can match strong baselines and reveal interpretable time-scale separation.
- Quality: Architecture and training are clearly specified; credible baselines and parameter matching are provided.
- Clarity issues: Define DRNN-AO/DRNN-1O in main text, clarify “bifurcations” terminology, and report BPTT truncation length more prominently.
- Methodological gap: Gradient normalization scheme needs deeper justification and comparison to standard gradient clipping and unnormalized SGD.
- Confound: DRNN-1O used incremental layer-wise training; unclear if depth benefits persist when trained from scratch with identical schedules.
- Depth vs. width: Missing key baselines — single-layer RNNs with equivalent unit count/parameters and deep-transition RNNs to disentangle depth from unit allocation.
- Fairness of comparisons: Parameter matching is good but unit/fan-in distributions differ; need baselines matching unit counts to isolate architectural inductive bias.
- Layer ablation caveat: Layer removal results are suggestive but hard to interpret causally due to interdependencies; propose complementary diagnostics.
- Additional experiments suggested: Test deep-transition or residual per-step transformations, sensitivity to BPTT truncation, and variability across random seeds.
- Significance: Findings inform when simple RNNs suffice versus gating/advanced optimization, and the analysis techniques are likely adoptable by others.
- Strengths: Simple architecture, strong SGD performance, thorough time-scale analyses, and useful insights about intermediate outputs aiding optimization.
- Weaknesses: Limited ablations leave ambiguity about source of gains; missing comparisons to gated RNNs and gradient clipping literature.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical and analytical contribution addressing depth in recurrent architectures.
- Cons for acceptance: Remaining methodological questions and missing baselines reduce conclusiveness; some evaluation choices lack causal clarity.
- Overall: Worthwhile and largely convincing paper that would benefit from a few additional baselines, clarifications, and ablations to strengthen claims.
