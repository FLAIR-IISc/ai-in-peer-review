- Paper studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, arguing stacked RNNs capture hierarchical timescales.
- Shows competitive/state-of-the-art results on a Wikipedia corpus for recurrent networks with similar parameter count using only SGD.
- Provides useful analysis of emergent timescales across DRNN layers.
- Builds on prior hierarchical/stacked RNN work but goes further by analyzing how architecture influences processing.
- Writing, motivation, methodology, and results are clear and largely convincing.
- Authors mention adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps but do not report whether this was explored — needs clarification.
- Explanation of gradient normalization and how it “avoids bifurcations” is vague and requires a more detailed account of observed bifurcations and mechanisms.
- Should discuss related work on training instabilities, notably Sutskever et al. (ICML 2013) on gradient truncation/optimization as alternative context/solutions.
- Concern that gradient normalization may disrupt SGD’s averaging behavior — authors should address this potential downside.
- Comparison to Mikolov/Hutter on Wikipedia compression tasks would provide an alternative evaluation perspective and should be added.
- Text-generation samples (Table 2) are persuasive, but layer “surgery” interpretations are confounded by interdependencies and hard to isolate.
- Missing crucial experiment testing whether depth provides benefits beyond simply reallocating capacity (compare against a wider single-layer RNN with same parameter count).
- Current parameter-count comparisons may be unfair (RNN baseline has significantly fewer parameters); baselines should match parameters/units.
- Recommend plotting performance vs DRNN depth trained from scratch with matched parameters/units (instead of current Figure 2) to clarify depth benefits.
- Minor: definitions of DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO should be included in the main text, not only in Figure 1.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions provided suggested experiments, explanations, and comparisons are added.
