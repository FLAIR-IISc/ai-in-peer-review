- Proposes stacked deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) combining hierarchical feature processing with time-series modeling.
- Compares two output couplings: DRNN-1O (top-only) and DRNN-AO (all layers connect to softmax/deep supervision).
- Uses SGD with gradient-norm normalization and BPTT; introduces incremental supervised layer-wise pretraining for DRNN-1O.
- Experiments on the Wikipedia character-level corpus (~4.9M parameters, ~10 days/GPU) show DRNNs match or slightly improve HF-trained RNN baselines.
- Provides extensive analyses: layer-wise contributions, emergent timescales, typo/context sensitivity, and parentheses dynamics with attractor-like top-layer states.
- Situates work among prior hierarchical/stacked RNNs and HHMMs but differs via simple tanh stacks, all-layer outputs, and deep empirical analysis.
- Technical soundness: clear architecture/training spec and reasonable gradient normalization; pretraining empirically justified.
- Empirical support: solid large-scale results and carefully designed, convincing analyses.
- Key limitations: single dataset evaluation, limited baseline comparisons, and only three model runs with no seed/hyperparameter variance reported.
- Clarity: well-written, organized, reproducible details provided; analysis sections particularly insightful.
- Originality: stacking not novel, but incremental supervised pretraining and all-layer outputs are practical novelties; main originality is depth-to-timescale analysis.
- Significance: demonstrates SGD-trained stacked RNNs can rival HF methods and advances understanding of depth-induced multi-scale temporal processing.
- Suggested broadening: compare to HHMMs/HRNNs and standard char benchmarks (enwik8/text8, PTB char) for wider relevance.
- Recommended ablations: test all-layer vs top-only outputs, pretraining effect, gradient norm, and BPTT truncation length.
- Model variants to explore: stacked LSTMs/GRUs under comparable budgets and variable layer widths.
- Robustness needs: multiple random seeds and hyperparameter sweeps to assess stability and significance.
- Analysis extensions: relate timescales to linearized dynamics/eigenvalues and probe more hierarchical structures and failure modes.
- Reproducibility request: release code/models and detail preprocessing and vocabulary mapping.
- Reasons to accept: clear analyses, strong experimental effort, practical training insights, and convincing evidence of emergent temporal hierarchies.
- Reasons to reject: narrow empirical scope, missing baselines (HHMMs, LSTMs/GRUs), and limited exploration of hyperparameters and randomness.
- Overall: strong, well-executed paper with valuable analytical contributions but limited benchmarking makes performance claims not fully definitive; borderline accept depending on committee priorities.
