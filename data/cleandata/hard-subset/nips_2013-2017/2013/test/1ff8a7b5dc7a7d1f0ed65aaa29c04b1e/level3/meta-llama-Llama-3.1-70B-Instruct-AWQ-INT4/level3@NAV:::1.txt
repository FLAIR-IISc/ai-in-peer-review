The paper "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks" proposes a novel deep architecture that combines hierarchical deep learning features with time-series modeling, specifically using deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs). The authors introduce a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using a next-letter prediction objective, which allows them to train very large networks and achieve improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset. The paper provides a detailed analysis of the network behavior, showing that higher layers capture long-term dependencies and that parentheses matching appears as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed architecture is novel and well-motivated, addressing the limitations of common recurrent neural networks (RNNs) in processing time series data.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the network behavior, including experiments on time scales, long-term interactions, and parentheses matching.
* The paper is well-written, with clear and supportive figures and experiments.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which may not be representative of other tasks or domains.
* The comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical HMMs and other approaches that may be relevant.
* The results are somewhat inconclusive, with the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO models performing similarly, and the common RNN performing worse.

In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper scores well on:

* Quality: The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a complete piece of work.
* Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader.
* Originality: The proposed architecture and analysis are novel and contribute to the field.
* Significance: The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research.

However, the paper could be improved by:

* Evaluating the proposed architecture on additional datasets and tasks.
* Comparing the DRNNs to other relevant models, including hierarchical HMMs and other deep learning architectures.
* Providing more conclusive results, such as a clear advantage of one DRNN model over the other.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with revisions to address the limitations mentioned above.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated architecture that addresses the limitations of common RNNs.
* The analysis is thorough and provides new insights into the behavior of DRNNs.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, which may not be representative of other tasks or domains.
* The comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models, excluding other relevant approaches.
* The results are somewhat inconclusive, with the DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO models performing similarly.
