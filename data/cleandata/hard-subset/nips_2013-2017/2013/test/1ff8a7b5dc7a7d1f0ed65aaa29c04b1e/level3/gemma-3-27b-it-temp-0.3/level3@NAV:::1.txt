## Review of “Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks”

This paper investigates the impact of hierarchical recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on time series processing, specifically character-level language modeling. The authors propose a deep architecture combining recurrent layers to capture temporal hierarchies, coupled with a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using a next-letter prediction objective. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia dataset, achieved through training very large networks, and provide a detailed analysis of network behavior, revealing emergent time scales and attractor-like states related to parentheses matching. 

The work builds upon the established success of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks, addressing the limitation of standard RNNs in explicitly modeling temporal hierarchies. It acknowledges prior work in hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [5, 8]) but distinguishes itself through a more in-depth analysis of the resulting network dynamics. The proposed DRNN architecture, essentially a multi-layered RNN, is a natural extension of existing approaches and logically motivated by the hierarchical structure often present in time series data. 

The paper’s strengths lie in its clarity and thoroughness. The writing is excellent, the experiments are well-documented, and the figures effectively illustrate the key findings. The analysis of network behavior, particularly the observation of pseudo-stable attractor states for parentheses matching, is insightful and contributes to our understanding of how these networks operate. The successful training of large networks, despite the computational challenges, is also commendable.

However, the paper’s primary weakness is its limited experimental scope. Evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia corpus), which limits the generalizability of the results. Furthermore, comparisons are largely confined to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) which represent a relevant alternative. While the authors acknowledge this limitation, it weakens the claim of conclusive performance gains. The significance of the work, therefore, currently rests more heavily on the analysis of network behavior than on demonstrably superior performance. The results, while promising, are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper’s stated goals.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of hierarchical deep learning and time-series modeling.
*   Detailed and insightful analysis of network behavior, revealing emergent properties.
*   High-quality writing and clear presentation of results.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited evaluation on a single dataset.
*   Restricted comparisons to RNN-related models.
*   Performance gains, while present, are not definitively established as superior to all relevant approaches.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent neural networks. While the limited experimental scope prevents a definitive endorsement of its performance claims, the detailed analysis and clear presentation make it a worthwhile contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically encouraging the authors to broaden their experimental evaluation and comparisons in future work.
