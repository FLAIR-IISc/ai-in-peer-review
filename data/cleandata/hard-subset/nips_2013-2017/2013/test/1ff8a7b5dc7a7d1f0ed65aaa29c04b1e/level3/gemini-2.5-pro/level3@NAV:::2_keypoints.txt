- Studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) via empirical analysis on a large-scale character-level language modeling task.  
- Shows a 5-layer DRNN outperforms a standard shallow RNN with the same number of parameters.  
- Finds deeper DRNN layers operate on longer timescales, forming an emergent temporal hierarchy.  
- Demonstrates plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train RNNs to model long-range dependencies like parenthesis balancing.  
- Strength: provides detailed, valuable analysis explaining why stacked RNNs are effective and achieves near state-of-the-art results with simpler training.  
- Weakness: the DRNN architecture is not novel and the paper should be positioned as analysis of existing models rather than a new model contribution.  
- Weakness: conclusions about the benefits of depth are based on a single task and need replication on other sequential modalities (e.g., speech).  
- Critique/suggestion: comparison to Hessian-Free (HF) optimization lacks a practical baseline; recommend testing truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) to evaluate cost–performance trade-offs.
