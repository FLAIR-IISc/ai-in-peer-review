- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers receiving previous layer hidden states, forming a temporal hierarchy.  
- Demonstrates DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only SGD/BPTT (no Hessian-free methods).  
- Shows deeper layers encode longer time scales and the top layer captures information beyond word-length sequences.  
- Provides evidence DRNNs can model long-term dependencies (e.g., balancing parentheses).  
- Experimental design is thorough: ablation studies, parentheses task, and comparisons to prior work.  
- Challenges the assumption that complex optimization (Hessian-free) is necessary for training RNNs with long-term memory.  
- Paper emphasizes analysis of temporal hierarchies rather than proposing a fundamentally new model.  
- Fails to properly acknowledge that Graves et al. (2013) used similar stacked bidirectional LSTM architectures.  
- Results are limited to character-level language modeling and lack demonstrations on other tasks (speech, MT, etc.).  
- Does not quantify or compare computational training cost versus standard RNNs; only reports “about 10 days” on one GPU.  
- Did not evaluate truncated BPTT (~30 steps), which could be a more efficient alternative and relevant to claims about SGD sufficiency.  
- Claim of “state-of-the-art” needs qualification—no comparison to well-tuned LSTMs with similar parameter counts.  
- Recommended improvements: better contextualize prior work, expand experiments to other tasks, report training cost details, and compare to standard LSTMs with equal parameters.  
- Overall: valuable analysis of temporal hierarchies and demonstration that plain SGD/BPTT can work for long-term dependencies; recommend acceptance with revisions to acknowledge prior work and broaden evaluation.
