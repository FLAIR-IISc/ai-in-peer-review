This paper proposes a novel deep architecture that combines the strengths of hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling, specifically designed for processing temporal hierarchies in data. The authors introduce a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks multiple recurrent neural networks (RNNs) on top of each other, allowing for hierarchical processing of time series data. Each layer in the hierarchy is a recurrent neural network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, enabling the network to capture multiple time scales and temporal dependencies.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the network's behavior, demonstrating that higher layers capture long-term dependencies and that parentheses matching appears as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state. The paper is well-written, and the experiments and figures are clear and supportive of the authors' claims.

The main contribution of the paper lies in its analysis of how the model operates, providing insights into the hierarchical processing of time series data. The authors demonstrate that the DRNN is capable of capturing complex temporal dependencies, including long-term relationships between input characters. The paper also provides a detailed comparison of the DRNN's performance with other RNN-related models on a Wikipedia dataset, showing improved performance.

However, the paper has some limitations. The evaluation is restricted to a single dataset, which is not widely used, and comparisons are limited to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical HMMs. Other approaches listed in the paper are on different corpora and thus not comparable. Additionally, the results are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper's stated goals, as the performance gains are not consistently demonstrated across all experiments.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.
