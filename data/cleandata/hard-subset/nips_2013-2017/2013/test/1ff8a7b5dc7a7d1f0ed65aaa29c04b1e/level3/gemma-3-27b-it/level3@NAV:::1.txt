## Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper investigates the impact of hierarchical recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) on time series processing, specifically character-level language modeling. The core idea is to combine the benefits of deep learning’s hierarchical feature extraction with the temporal modeling capabilities of recurrent networks, creating a layered architecture where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous one. A layer-wise supervised pre-training procedure using a next-letter prediction objective is introduced, and the authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia dataset with very large networks requiring substantial training time (≈10 days). 

The work is strong in its detailed analysis of the network’s behavior. The authors observe that higher layers capture longer-term dependencies and that parentheses matching emerges as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state – valuable insights into the internal workings of these complex models. The paper is also exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, with supportive experimental results and figures. 

However, the paper’s primary weakness lies in the limited scope of its evaluation.  The results are demonstrated on a single, not widely adopted dataset (Hinton et al.’s Wikipedia corpus). Furthermore, comparisons are largely restricted to other RNN-related models, excluding potentially relevant hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) or other relevant architectural approaches. The authors acknowledge this limitation, noting that other comparable work uses different corpora, hindering direct comparison. While the performance gains are promising, they are somewhat inconclusive given this restricted benchmarking. The contribution feels stronger in *understanding* how the model operates than in demonstrating conclusively superior performance against a broader range of methodologies.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of hierarchical deep learning and recurrent modeling.
*   Demonstrated state-of-the-art results (within the limited comparison set).
*   Strong analysis providing valuable insights into network behavior and emergent properties.
*   High-quality writing and clear presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Evaluation limited to a single dataset.
*   Comparisons primarily restricted to RNNs, excluding other hierarchical models.
*   Performance gains, while promising, are not conclusive due to limited benchmarking.



**Overall:** This is a solid piece of work with a clear and novel contribution, particularly in the analysis of the resulting network dynamics. However, the limited experimental scope weakens the claim of performance superiority. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically encouraging the authors to address the dataset limitation (even with additional experiments) and broaden the comparative analysis. Further exploration of the observed attractor-like states and their implications could also strengthen the paper.
