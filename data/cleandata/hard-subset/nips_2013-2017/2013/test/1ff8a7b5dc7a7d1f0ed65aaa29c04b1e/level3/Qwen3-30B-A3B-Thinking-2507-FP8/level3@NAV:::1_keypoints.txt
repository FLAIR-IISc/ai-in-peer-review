- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the previous layer’s hidden state as input.  
- Proposes explicit hierarchical temporal processing, contrasting with standard RNNs that implicitly encode time scales.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work but offers a more systematic analysis of how temporal hierarchies emerge during training.  
- Shows DRNNs achieve near state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using only SGD, avoiding complex optimizers like Hessian-free methods.  
- Key innovation: detailed analysis of emergent time scales across layers, with higher layers capturing long-term dependencies.  
- Observes parentheses matching behavior as a pseudo-stable attractor state in the network dynamics.  
- Introduces a useful layer-wise pre-training procedure using next-letter prediction for training deep recurrent architectures.  
- Strengths: exceptionally clear exposition, well-designed experiments, insightful visualizations, and technically sound analysis.  
- Major limitation: evaluation on a single dataset (Wikipedia) limits generalizability.  
- Comparison scope is limited to RNN-related models and omits natural baselines like hierarchical HMMs on the same data.  
- Performance gains are marginal (1.47 BPC vs. 1.46 BPC for Hessian-free RNNs), weakening the strong “state-of-the-art” claim.  
- Overall contribution is stronger in advancing understanding of model behavior and temporal hierarchies than in conclusively demonstrating superior performance.
