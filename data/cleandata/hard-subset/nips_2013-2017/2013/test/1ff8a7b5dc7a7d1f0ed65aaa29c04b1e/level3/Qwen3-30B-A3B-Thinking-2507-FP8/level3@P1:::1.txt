

The paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture stacking recurrent layers where each layer processes the hidden state of the layer below, creating a hierarchical temporal processing pipeline. This directly addresses a key limitation of standard RNNs: their implicit handling of multiple time scales in time series data. The authors position DRNNs as a synthesis of deep learning hierarchies (from DNNs) and recurrent memory (from RNNs), contrasting with prior work like hierarchical RNNs in [5] (focused on artificial tasks) and stacked LSTMs in [8] (emphasizing performance over analysis). The core novelty lies in demonstrating how DRNNs inherently develop distinct time scales across layers, validated through character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus.

**Strengths:** The paper excels in technical quality and clarity. The architecture (Fig. 1) and equations (2.1–2.2) are rigorously defined, with a well-justified training protocol using SGD (Eq. 3) and careful hyperparameter choices. The analysis is the paper’s standout contribution: experiments quantifying layer-specific time scales via typo-perturbation (Fig. 3) and parentheses-closing tasks (Fig. 4) provide compelling evidence of hierarchical temporal processing. For instance, the top layer’s persistent activation during long parenthetical phrases reveals a "pseudo-stable attractor" state, demonstrating long-term dependency modeling beyond standard RNNs. The DRNN-1O achieves state-of-the-art RNN performance (BPC=1.30) using only SGD—previously requiring Hessian-free optimization [19]—and the ablation studies (Fig. 2, Table 2) effectively link layer roles to task requirements (e.g., bottom layers essential for basic character statistics, top layers for phrase-level patterns). The writing is exceptionally clear, with experiments and figures directly supporting claims.

**Weaknesses:** The significance is partially undermined by methodological limitations. The evaluation relies solely on the Wikipedia corpus [19], which is not a standard NLP benchmark (e.g., Penn Treebank), limiting generalizability. Crucially, comparisons exclude hierarchical models like HMMs or hierarchical RNNs [5, 7], which are directly relevant to the paper’s thesis on temporal hierarchies. While the paper notes HMMs are "not comparable" due to different corpora, this is a missed opportunity—hierarchical HMMs could provide a meaningful baseline for time-scale modeling. Performance gains are modest: DRNN-1O (BPC=1.30) edges out RNNs (BPC=1.33) but lags behind Hessian-free methods (BPC=1.28 [19]), and DRNN-AO underperforms DRNN-1O. The claim that DRNNs "reach state-of-the-art for recurrent networks" is technically accurate but understates that gains are marginal relative to prior optimization advances. The single-dataset focus also weakens the argument for broad applicability.

**Originality and Significance:** The architecture is novel in its explicit hierarchical design for temporal data, distinct from prior stacked-RNN work [8] that focused on performance. However, the primary contribution is the *analysis* of emergent time scales, not the architecture itself. This analysis significantly advances understanding of how RNNs process temporal hierarchies—a gap in the literature. The paper’s significance lies in this theoretical insight, not in demonstrating a new SOTA. While the performance gains are incremental, the insights into layer-specific time scales (e.g., DRNN-AO’s short bottom-layer memory vs. long top-layer memory) provide a framework for future work on hierarchical temporal modeling. The parentheses task (Fig. 4) is particularly compelling evidence of learned long-term structure.

**Overall Assessment:** This is a high-quality technical contribution with strong theoretical analysis. The paper’s clarity, methodological rigor, and novel insights into temporal hierarchies outweigh its limitations in empirical scope. The modest performance gains are expected given the focus on analysis, and the paper successfully demonstrates that DRNNs *can* capture hierarchical time scales—a key claim. Future work should validate on standard NLP datasets and compare against hierarchical HMMs, but the current analysis is sufficient for acceptance. The paper advances the field by shifting focus from *whether* deep RNNs work to *how* they process time scales, offering a valuable lens for understanding RNN dynamics.

**Recommendation:** Accept. The paper provides a clear, well-executed analysis of a promising architecture, significantly advancing understanding of temporal hierarchies in RNNs. While performance gains are modest, the theoretical insights are substantial and directly address a key limitation of standard RNNs. The work is technically sound, well-written, and contributes meaningfully to NIPS’ focus on foundational machine learning advances.
