## Review of Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks

This paper proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture that combines the hierarchical processing of deep neural networks with the temporal modeling capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The authors stack recurrent layers, with the hidden state of each layer serving as input to the next, effectively creating a hierarchy of timescales. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia corpus, achieved with simple stochastic gradient descent, and provide an analysis of the emergent timescales within the network.

The motivation behind this work is well-established. The authors correctly point out that standard RNNs, while capable of modeling temporal dependencies, don’t explicitly incorporate a hierarchical understanding of time, which is common in many real-world time series (e.g., speech).  This limitation contrasts with the success of deep neural networks in other domains, where hierarchical representations have proven crucial. The paper situates itself within a growing body of work on hierarchical RNNs [3, 5, 6], specifically acknowledging the work of Sutskever et al. [8] which previously used stacked LSTMs but lacked detailed analysis of the resulting architecture. This connection to previous work demonstrates a solid understanding of the field.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Architecture:** The DRNN architecture is a logical and well-motivated extension of both DNNs and RNNs. Combining the strengths of both approaches creates a potentially powerful model for complex temporal data.
* **Strong Empirical Results:**  The authors achieve state-of-the-art performance on a challenging character-level language modeling task, surpassing previous results obtained with more sophisticated optimization techniques (Hessian-free optimization [19]). This is a significant achievement.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply reporting results and provides a thorough analysis of the learned representations. The visualization of the network’s response to parentheses, and the analysis of timescales at different layers, are particularly insightful.  The demonstration of an "attractor-like state" for parentheses is a compelling finding.
* **Clear Writing and Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The figures are informative and support the main arguments. The mathematical notation is clear and concise.
* **Complete Piece of Work:** The experiments are well-designed, and the authors carefully consider the training setup and hyperparameter choices. The inclusion of ablative studies (removing layer contributions) adds to the robustness of the findings.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Dataset & Comparison:** The primary evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Wikipedia text corpus) which, while challenging, isn’t universally used within the community. The comparisons are primarily focused on other RNN-based models. A comparison against hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) or other non-RNN approaches capable of modeling hierarchical temporal structure would strengthen the paper. The authors acknowledge this in their discussion, but it remains a limitation.
* **Inconclusive Significance:** While the results are promising, the contribution in terms of purely *performance* gains isn't overwhelmingly significant.  The authors achieve state-of-the-art, but the improvement over existing RNNs is not dramatic. The primary strength of the paper lies in the analysis of the learned architecture and the insights into how it processes information.
* **Training Complexity:**  The training time (10 days) is substantial and may limit the accessibility of this approach for researchers with limited computational resources. While the authors address this to some extent, it’s worth noting.
* **Output Connection Strategies:** The exploration of DRNN-1O (output from the top layer only) and DRNN-AO (output from all layers) is valuable, but the rationale for the layer-wise training strategy used for DRNN-1O isn’t entirely clear, and may introduce a bias.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture that addresses a known limitation of standard RNNs.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach.
* The detailed analysis of the network’s behavior provides valuable insights into how it learns and processes temporal data.
* The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and represents a complete piece of work.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The limited dataset and comparison to related work may weaken the claims of broad applicability.
* The performance gains, while significant, are not overwhelmingly large.
* The substantial training time could be a barrier to adoption.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution and insightful analysis.  While the limited dataset and moderate performance gains are weaknesses, the thoroughness of the investigation and the compelling analysis of the learned representations make this a valuable contribution to the field.  I believe the paper is a good fit for NIPS and would recommend **acceptance**.  I encourage the authors to address the concerns regarding dataset diversity and comparison with non-RNN approaches in future work.
