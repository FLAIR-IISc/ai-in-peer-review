- Paper studies Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling, showing they capture temporal hierarchies and long-term dependencies.
- Authors claim DRNNs outperform standard RNNs on long-range memory tasks and achieve SOTA on a Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD.
- Show that plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) suffices for training DRNNs on tasks like balancing parentheses, challenging prior assumptions.
- Strength: Provides comprehensive analysis of temporal hierarchies and emergent time scales in DRNNs.
- Strength: Empirical results are competitive on a challenging character-level task, demonstrating practical utility.
- Strength: The finding about plain BPTT is noteworthy and challenges existing beliefs about training recurrent nets.
- Strength: Experimental setup and training details are clearly described, supporting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Lack of novelty—DRNNs/stacked recurrent architectures have been explored in prior work and contributions are not clearly differentiated.
- Weakness: Limited scope—evaluation confined to character-level modeling; other domains (speech, time-series) not tested.
- Weakness: Did not evaluate truncated BPTT, a cost-effective alternative relevant to practical training.
- Weakness: Paper emphasizes analysis over proposing new architectures, but this focus isn’t made explicit enough.
- Argument for acceptance: Rigorous analysis and insights into hierarchical temporal processing are valuable for future research.
- Argument against acceptance: Limited originality and narrow task focus reduce broader impact; missing truncated BPTT experiments weaken evaluation.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying contributions, extending evaluations to other tasks, and exploring truncated BPTT.
