- Main result: Deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when model size (number of parameters) is held constant.
- Experimental setup: Compared a common RNN, 5-layer DRNN with output only at top (DRNN-1O), and 5-layer DRNN with outputs at all layers (DRNN-AO) on Wikipedia text.
- Finding: Deeper layers in DRNNs encode more long-range information, supporting deeper networks capturing more complex patterns.
- Finding: Plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior beliefs favoring Hessian-free (HF) optimization.
- Finding: Layer-wise analysis shows DRNNs capture a hierarchy of time scales across layers.
- Presentation quality: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Experimental quality: Experiments are well-designed, thorough, and accompanied by good discussion of implications.
- Attribution issue: Claiming to have introduced deep RNN architecture is inaccurate given prior work (e.g., Graves 2013).
- Generalizability concern: Results may be task-specific and should be replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to strengthen claims.
- Optimization concern: HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT (as used by Mikolov) should be tested as a cheaper alternative.
- Argument for acceptance: Provides a thorough, valuable analysis of DRNN properties with interesting, well-presented results.
- Argument against acceptance: Perceived as incremental, possibly task-specific, and includes costly HF experiments that may be avoidable.
- Recommendation and score: Accept (rating 8/10) with encouragement to address the noted concerns in the final version.
