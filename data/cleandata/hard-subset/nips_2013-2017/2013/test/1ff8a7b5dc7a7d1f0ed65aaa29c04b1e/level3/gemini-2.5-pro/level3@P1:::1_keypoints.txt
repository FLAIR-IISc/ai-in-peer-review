- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN): stacked RNN layers to introduce explicit hierarchical processing at each timestep.  
- Motivation: enables different layers to operate at different time scales, combining temporal memory with hierarchical feature extraction.  
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling (Wikipedia dataset); compares DRNN-1O (top-layer output) and DRNN-AO (all-layer outputs).  
- Introduces a novel incremental, layer-wise supervised training procedure for DRNN-1O.  
- Achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art RNNs on the dataset using simple SGD (with gradient clipping) instead of complex second-order methods.  
- Main contribution is detailed analysis showing higher layers exhibit longer memory and capture temporal hierarchy.  
- Diagnostic experiments (typo-perturbation, long-range parenthesis matching) provide compelling evidence for pseudo-stable attractor-like states and long-range dependency handling.  
- Quality: technically sound, carefully executed experiments, and significant computational effort (~10 days per model).  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, precise model description, clear figures, and thorough experimental explanations.  
- Originality: stacking RNNs not novel, but novelty lies in large-scale application, the layer-wise training method, and comprehensive analysis of emergent temporal hierarchy.  
- Significance: provides valuable analysis techniques and evidence that depth in RNNs models multi-timescale structure; demonstrates practical SGD training for large RNNs.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation limited to a single dataset, reducing generalizability of performance claims.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit other hierarchical time-series baselines and non-RNN models, potentially misleading relative performance.  
- Weakness: performance gains over single-layer RNNs are modest; the analysis is the stronger contribution than raw performance improvements.
