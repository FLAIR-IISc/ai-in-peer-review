- Paper proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): each layer is a recurrent network processing the previous layer’s hidden states to capture hierarchical temporal processing and long-term dependencies.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia character-level language modeling task, shows emergent time scales in layers, and analyzes layer roles in text prediction (e.g., closing parentheses).  

Strengths:
- Novelty/significance: introduces a compelling hierarchical extension to RNNs addressing multi-scale temporal dependencies.  
- Experimental rigor: extensive experiments, baseline comparisons, and insightful layer-wise analyses.  
- Clarity/accessibility: well-written with detailed experimental setups that enhance reproducibility.  
- Performance: competitive SOTA results on a challenging text prediction task.  
- Intellectual honesty: authors acknowledge limitations like parameter mismatches and limited evidence for higher-layer abstraction.  

Weaknesses:
- Gradient normalization concerns: use of gradient normalization to prevent bifurcations may alter SGD averaging behavior and needs deeper analysis and relevant citations.  
- Fairness in comparisons: comparisons to regular RNNs may be biased by differing parameter counts; equal-parameter benchmarks are needed.  
- Lack of direct evidence for higher-layer abstraction: claims about higher-layer abstraction and long-term property processing are not sufficiently substantiated.  
- Limited interpretability of layer interdependencies: text generation results are persuasive but leave unclear how to interpret inter-layer dynamics.  
- Terminology definition: key terms (e.g., DRNN-AO, DRNN-1O) are defined only in a figure and should be introduced in the main text.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance contingent on revisions addressing gradient normalization concerns, fair parameter-budgeted comparisons, and stronger evidence for higher-layer abstractions.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel hierarchical contribution, rigorous experiments, and clear presentation.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Concerns about fairness of comparisons and gradient normalization, insufficient direct evidence for higher-layer abstraction, and limited interpretability of layer interactions.  

Overall:
- Paper advances understanding of deep recurrent architectures and their potential for modeling complex temporal hierarchies.
