This paper proposes a new deep architecture that combines hierarchical deep learning features with time-series modeling, specifically using deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs). The authors introduce a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using a next-letter prediction objective and train very large networks, achieving improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset compared to prior work. The paper provides a detailed analysis of network behavior, showing that higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses matching appears as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state.

The paper is well-written, with clear and supportive experiments and figures. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the subject matter and provide a thorough analysis of their results. The use of DRNNs to model temporal hierarchies is a novel approach, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how their architecture differs from previous contributions.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its analysis of network behavior. The authors provide a detailed examination of how the DRNNs learn to model long-term dependencies, including the ability to close parentheses and maintain a sense of context over long sequences. The use of visualization techniques, such as the plot of the Euclidean distance between the hidden states of the perturbed and unperturbed networks, helps to illustrate the authors' points and make the results more accessible to readers.

The paper also demonstrates a good understanding of the limitations of the current approach. The authors note that their evaluation is limited to a single dataset and that comparisons are restricted to RNN-related models, excluding hierarchical HMMs. They also acknowledge that the results are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper's stated goals, and that further research is needed to fully understand the capabilities and limitations of DRNNs.
