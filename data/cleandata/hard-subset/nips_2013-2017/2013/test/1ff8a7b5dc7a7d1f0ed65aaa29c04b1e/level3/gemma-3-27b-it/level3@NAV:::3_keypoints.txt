- Proposes Deep RNNs (DRNNs) by stacking RNN layers to form hierarchies that process multiple timescales.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using simple SGD, notable given prior reliance on complex training.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and largely convincing.  
- Key strength: deeper RNNs implicitly capture temporal hierarchies, supported empirically (e.g., Figure 3).  
- Ablation study (Table 2) provides useful, though complex-to-interpret, insight into layer-specific roles.  
- Text generation examples show the model captures long-range dependencies (e.g., parentheses matching).  
- Gradient normalization is mentioned to avoid bifurcations, but the mechanism and its impact on SGD are insufficiently explained.  
- Missing crucial control experiment: test whether increased capacity in the first layer can match gains attributed to depth.  
- Comparisons to Hessian-free baselines are undermined by parameter count differences; need baselines with comparable parameter budgets.  
- Recommend plotting performance versus depth while controlling for total parameters to isolate depth effects.  
- Presentation issue: DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO are shown in Figure 1 before being defined in the text.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of depth to RNNs, SOTA results with simple training, evidence for emergent temporal hierarchies, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of control experiments, unclear gradient normalization discussion, and potential unfair parameter-count comparisons.  
- Overall: worthwhile contribution that would be significantly strengthened by addressing the control experiments and clarifying gradient normalization.
