- Paper studies stacked deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) with two output coupling strategies: single top output (DRNN-1O) and summed-all-layers output (DRNN-AO).
- Trains large DRNNs on a Wikipedia character corpus using plain SGD with gradient normalization and a supervised incremental layer-wise pre-training schedule.
- Reports competitive bits-per-character (BPC) versus prior recurrent baselines and Hessian-free-trained multiplicative RNNs at similar parameter counts.
- Analysis shows emergent temporal hierarchies: higher layers capture longer time scales and can sustain attractor-like states (e.g., for open parentheses).
- Main contributions: explicit DRNN formulation with two output couplings and incremental supervised pre-training for 1O.
- Main contributions: empirical large-scale character modeling results improving over shallow RNNs and matching prior MRNNs.
- Main contributions: systematic probing/ablation suite (typo perturbations, context switches, parentheses probe) quantifying layer roles and time scales.
- Technical approach is sound and well-specified (equations, training details, truncation, gradient normalization) with convincing diagnostics and figures.
- Experimental limitations: single non-standard Wikipedia dataset, slight character-vocabulary differences from prior work, and only three model instantiations.
- Experimental limitations: no variance reported across random seeds and limited set of baselines (no LSTM/stacked LSTM comparisons).
- Limited ablations: little exploration of depth/width trade-offs, truncation length sensitivity, or impact of the incremental training schedule.
- Choice of five layers is not justified in the paper (motivated by “results not shown”).
- Clarity: manuscript is well written, organized, and reproducible for an expert reader; AO vs 1O rationale and training schedule are clear.
- Suggested clarifications: add pseudo-code for incremental training, precise compute/budget reporting, sensitivity analyses for truncation and gradient normalization, and impact of character-set differences on BPC.
- Originality: stacking RNN layers is incremental, but the supervised incremental training schedule, AO outputs for optimization/interpretability, and detailed temporal analyses are novel contributions.
- Significance: moderate — shows simple SGD can match prior RNN SOTA at similar sizes and provides useful analysis tools, but empirical breadth limits performance claims.
- Pros: clear, well-motivated study; simple effective training; insightful probes illustrating long-term behavior.
- Cons: limited dataset/baseline breadth, sparse ablations, no seed variance reporting, and unclear SOTA claims due to dataset differences.
- Recommended improvements: evaluate on at least one standard corpus (text8/enwik8/PTB-char) with strong LSTM/gated-RNN baselines and possibly hierarchical HMMs.
- Recommended improvements: run ablations across layers/units/AO vs 1O/truncation/gradient norm, report multiple seeds and parameter-efficiency plots, and consider second-order training comparisons.
- Overall verdict: careful and informative analysis-focused paper; leans toward acceptance if space allows analytical contributions, but authors should temper claims and broaden empirical evaluation.
