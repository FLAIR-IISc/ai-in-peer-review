This paper studies the usefulness of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling. The main result of the paper is that deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs when the number of parameters is held constant. The authors demonstrate this by comparing the performance of a common RNN, a 5-layer DRNN with output connections only at the top layer (DRNN-1O), and a 5-layer DRNN with output connections at all layers (DRNN-AO) on a Wikipedia text corpus.

The paper provides several key findings. Firstly, the authors show that deeper layers in the DRNNs encode more long-range information, which is consistent with the idea that deeper networks can capture more complex patterns in the data. Secondly, they find that plain backpropagation through time (BPTT) can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, contrary to prior belief that Hessian-free (HF) optimization was necessary. Finally, the authors provide an analysis of the time scales at which each layer in the DRNNs operates, which suggests that the networks are able to capture a hierarchy of time scales.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand their results. The experiments are well-designed and thorough, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of their findings.

One potential criticism of the paper is that it improperly claims to have introduced the deep RNN architecture, when in fact prior work (e.g., Graves 2013) already used similar models. However, this is not a major issue, as the paper is more focused on analyzing the properties of DRNNs rather than proposing a new architecture.

A more significant concern is that the results may be task-specific, and that the analysis should be replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech) to strengthen the claims made in the paper. Additionally, the HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive, and truncated BPTT (as used by Mikolov) may be sufficient and should be tested to confirm.

Overall, the paper is a solid contribution to the field of deep learning, and provides a good analysis of the properties of DRNNs. The results are interesting and well-presented, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the properties of DRNNs, which is a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning.
* The results are interesting and well-presented, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* The experiments are well-designed and thorough, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of their findings.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may be seen as incremental, as it builds on prior work on deep RNNs.
* The results may be task-specific, and the analysis should be replicated on additional tasks to strengthen the claims made in the paper.
* The HF experiments may be unnecessarily expensive, and truncated BPTT may be sufficient and should be tested to confirm.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept, but encourage the authors to address the concerns mentioned above in the final version of the paper.
