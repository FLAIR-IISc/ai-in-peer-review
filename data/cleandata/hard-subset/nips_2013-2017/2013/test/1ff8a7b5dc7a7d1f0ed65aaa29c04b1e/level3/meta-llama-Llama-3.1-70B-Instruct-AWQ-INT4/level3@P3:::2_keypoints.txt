Proposes a hierarchical deep recurrent neural network (each layer a recurrent net receiving previous layer's hidden state).  
DRNNs outperform standard RNNs when holding parameter count constant.  
Deeper RNNs capture longer-range dependencies and improve character-level language modeling.  
Provides analysis of emergent time scales across DRNN layers.  
Shows DRNNs model long-term structures (e.g., matching parentheses) and top layers capture statistics beyond word length.  
Experiments are clear, well-written, and results are presented concisely.  
Uses large-scale Wikipedia corpus and achieves state-of-the-art performance among recurrent networks.  
Major limitation: fails to properly acknowledge prior similar work (e.g., Graves 2013).  
Limited scope: evaluation only on character-level language modeling, so generalizability is unclear.  
Originality is incremental (variation on existing architectures) though time-scale analysis is a novel contribution.  
Recommendation: accept with revisions to add broader experiments and proper citations.  
Suggested revisions: include experiments on other tasks, cite and contrast prior work (Graves 2013), and consider truncated BPTT with fewer steps (e.g., 30) to reduce training cost.
