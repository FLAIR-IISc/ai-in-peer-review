- Paper studies stacked deep RNNs (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) on character-level language modeling and analyzes emergent time scales via perturbations and a parentheses probe.  
- Main empirical findings: (i) deeper RNNs beat single-layer RNNs at fixed parameter budgets; (ii) higher layers encode longer-range dependencies; (iii) vanilla SGD/BPTT with gradient clipping can learn long-term structure without HF.  
- Architectural novelty is limited: stacked RNNs and stacked LSTMs have prior work (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, Graves et al.), so claims should be moderated and prior work cited more prominently.  
- Strength: Clear empirical result that depth helps under a fixed parameter budget.  
- Strength: Thoughtful analyses (perturbation experiments, layer ablations, parentheses probe) and informative qualitative generations.  
- Strength: Demonstrates that simple SGD with gradient clipping suffices to learn long dependencies, reducing dependence on HF.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a single dataset/task, so conclusions about time-scale hierarchies may be task-specific.  
- Weakness: Training uses very long BPTT unrolls (250 steps), which is expensive and lacks comparison to truncated BPTT baselines (e.g., 20–30 steps).  
- Weakness: Limited ablations—only three model configs; lacks depth vs width sweeps, unroll length studies, gating (LSTM/GRU) comparisons, and variance across seeds.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to MRNN/HF are not fully normalized for data passes, compute, or wall-clock time; training budgets need clarification.  
- Quality: Experiments and analyses are generally sound and well-executed, but scope is narrow and some novelty claims overstate contributions.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written with effective figures; should explicitly state unroll length, training budgets, and the role of gradient clipping.  
- Originality: Main novelty lies in systematic time-scale analyses and AO vs 1O comparison; architectural ideas are not new.  
- Significance: Moderate—useful for practitioners and researchers studying temporal hierarchies, but broader impact depends on replication on other tasks and cheaper training.  
- Suggested experiments: add a truncated-BPTT baseline (e.g., 30 steps) matched for tokens seen; replicate on an additional dataset (PTB/enwik8 or TIMIT/WSJ); include depth/width trade-offs and seed variance; test gating baselines.  
- Suggested clarifications: moderate novelty claims, cite related stacked-LSTM work, report exact training budgets (updates/epochs/tokens), and clarify compute normalization vs HF/MRNN.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, practical finding that depth helps; insightful layer-wise time-scale analyses; shows SGD can learn long-range behaviors.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited architectural novelty, single-task evidence, missing truncated-BPTT baseline and broader ablations.  
- Overall recommendation: Leaning toward accept if authors tone novelty claims and add at least a truncated-BPTT comparison (ideally an extra dataset); otherwise solid but limited in scope.
