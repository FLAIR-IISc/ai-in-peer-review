- Main finding: A 5-layer deep RNN outperforms a single-layer RNN at equal parameter count on character-level Wikipedia language modeling.  
- Contribution: Shows deeper RNN layers learn to operate on progressively longer time scales, forming a useful temporal hierarchy.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates that plain BPTT + SGD can effectively train DRNNs, matching prior HF-optimized results.  
- Empirical highlight: A DRNN trained with BPTT can learn long-range parenthesis balancing previously thought to require HF.  
- Strength: Provides careful, insightful analysis of internal DRNN dynamics rather than only proposing an architecture.  
- Strength: Challenges the assumption that second-order optimizers are required for learning long-range dependencies.  
- Strength: Achieves strong state-of-the-art empirical results using a simple training algorithm.  
- Weakness: Novelty/positioning is overstated because stacked RNNs/LSTMs existed in prior work (e.g., Graves et al. 2013); should be framed as analysis not new architecture.  
- Weakness: Claims of general effectiveness are based solely on a single task (character-level LM) and need validation on other domains (e.g., speech or word-level LM).  
- Weakness: Training uses long BPTT windows (200 steps), raising questions about computational necessity and whether TBPTT with much shorter windows could match performance.  
- Suggested experiment: Include TBPTT baselines (e.g., 20–40 or ~30 steps) to evaluate trade-offs between training cost and long-range dependency capture.  
- Recommendation: Temper claims, better acknowledge prior work, and add cross-domain or TBPTT experiments to strengthen generality and efficiency arguments.
