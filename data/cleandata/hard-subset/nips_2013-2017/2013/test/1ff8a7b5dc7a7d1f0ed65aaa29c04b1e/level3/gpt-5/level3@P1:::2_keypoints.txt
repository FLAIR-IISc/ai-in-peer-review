- Paper studies stacked vanilla tanh RNNs (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling and compares top-layer-only (DRNN-1O) vs all-layers-to-output (DRNN-AO) outputs.  
- Trained with plain SGD (gradient normalization, linear LR decay), DRNNs match or slightly improve single-layer RNNs at fixed ~4.9M params and approach multiplicative RNNs trained with HF.  
- Core contribution: analysis showing deeper layers encode longer temporal dependencies and plain BPTT can learn long-range signals (e.g., balanced parentheses).  
- Architectural novelty is limited—stacked recurrent models have multiple precedents (El Hihi & Bengio, Graves et al., etc.).  
- The paper usefully complements prior performance-focused work by probing time scales, layer contributions, and qualitative behaviors.  
- Technical soundness: training setup and ablations are sensible; parentheses analysis and other probes are compelling.  
- Empirical limitations: results on a single large task, only three runs, and comparisons to MRNN+HF and compression baselines are not fully controlled.  
- Methodological concern: uses BPTT over 250 steps (first 50 ignored) but does not compare to truncated BPTT (TBPTT), which could offer large cost savings.  
- Clarity: exposition and experimental details are generally clear and reproducible, but effective BPTT truncation, DRNN-AO training schedule, and wall-clock vs data-pass normalization need explicit clarification.  
- Originality: main novelty is empirical analysis and training observation (SGD suffices), not the architecture itself; novelty claims should be toned down with proper citations.  
- Significance: finding that depth yields better performance at fixed params and the time-scale analyses are valuable, but generality beyond the single task is uncertain.  
- Suggested experiments: compare full BPTT to TBPTT (20–40 steps) for cost/accuracy trade-offs and report training time/BPC.  
- Suggested controls: apply the incremental layer-wise schedule to DRNN-AO and train DRNN-1O end-to-end to isolate curriculum vs connectivity effects.  
- Suggested robustness: include variance over multiple seeds and confidence intervals.  
- Suggested baselines: add stacked LSTM/GRU baselines to disentangle benefits of depth vs gating.  
- Suggested scope: evaluate on at least one additional domain (e.g., speech or another sequential task) or compare to stacked LSTM results to address single-task concerns.  
- Pros: clear empirical evidence that stacked vanilla RNNs help, insightful layer-wise/time-scale analyses, and demonstration that plain SGD can approach HF performance.  
- Cons: limited architectural novelty, single-task evaluation, non-ideal controlled comparisons, and high training cost without cost/accuracy analysis.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—valuable analytical insights and practical message but needs stronger positioning relative to prior work and additional TBPTT/robustness experiments.
