This paper proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture, combining the benefits of deep learning’s hierarchical representation learning with the temporal modeling capabilities of recurrent neural networks. The authors investigate the effect of stacking recurrent layers, where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, effectively creating a hierarchy of timescales. They demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on a character-level language modeling task, surpassing previous recurrent network results on the Wikipedia text corpus, and provide an insightful analysis of the emergent time scales within the network.

The work builds upon the established success of deep neural networks (DNNs) in various domains [4, 17] and acknowledges the limitations of standard RNNs in explicitly capturing temporal hierarchies. While RNNs provide memory, the authors argue they don’t inherently perform hierarchical processing in the same way as DNNs. They relate their approach to prior hierarchical RNN architectures [3, 5, 6], highlighting that their work offers a more in-depth analysis and achieves strong performance on a real-world task, unlike some prior work limited to artificial datasets. The architecture also shares similarities with stacked bi-directional LSTM networks [8], but differs in its emphasis on architectural analysis and achieving competitive results with simpler stochastic gradient descent (SGD).

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Originality:** The DRNN architecture, combining deep and recurrent layers in a specific manner, offers a novel approach to time series modeling. The investigation into the emergent time scales and the analysis of network behavior are particularly original.
* **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a clear and mathematically sound description of the DRNN architecture (Section 2). The training procedure and experimental setup are well-defined, and the use of gradient normalization is a practical detail that contributes to stable training.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The authors effectively explain the motivation behind their approach and the intuition behind the architecture. Figures are well-designed and aid in understanding the concepts.
* **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of the network’s behavior, especially the time scale analysis (Section 3.3) and the parenthesis matching experiment, provides valuable insights into how the DRNN learns and represents temporal dependencies. The observation that the network learns to treat parentheses as attractor-like states is particularly compelling.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the Wikipedia text corpus using only SGD is a significant accomplishment, especially considering prior work relied on more complex optimization methods [19].

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Dataset & Comparison:** The evaluation is confined to a single dataset (Wikipedia text corpus). While the authors acknowledge this, it limits the generalizability of the findings. Furthermore, comparisons are largely restricted to RNN-based models, neglecting potential comparisons with hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) or other relevant approaches. The different corpora used in cited papers make direct comparisons difficult and somewhat diminish the claim of conclusive performance gains.
* **Incremental Training Procedure:** The layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O, while effective, introduces a potential bias and might not be the most optimal training strategy. A more simultaneous training approach could be explored.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions some sensitivity in hyperparameter tuning, particularly the learning rate, and does not thoroughly investigate the impact of different parameter settings.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the paper notes the long training time (10 days), a more detailed discussion of the computational cost and scalability of the DRNN architecture would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The DRNN architecture represents a valuable contribution to the field of recurrent neural networks, offering a novel approach to capturing temporal hierarchies.
* The paper demonstrates strong empirical results on a challenging benchmark dataset, achieving state-of-the-art performance.
* The insightful analysis of the network's behavior provides a deeper understanding of how DRNNs learn and represent temporal dependencies.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and comparisons are primarily with RNN-based models, hindering the assessment of the DRNN’s broader applicability.
* The incremental training procedure introduces a potential bias.
* The paper doesn’t extensively investigate the impact of different hyperparameters.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution, clear presentation, and insightful analysis. While the evaluation is limited, the results are compelling and the analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep recurrent networks. I believe the paper significantly advances our understanding of hierarchical temporal modeling and warrants acceptance. I recommend a score of **7/10** (Strong Accept). I suggest the authors consider expanding their evaluation to other datasets and including comparisons with a wider range of models in future work. Further investigation into optimal training strategies beyond the layer-wise approach would also be beneficial.
