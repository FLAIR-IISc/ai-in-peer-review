This paper proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture, which combines the strengths of deep neural networks and recurrent neural networks. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs are well-suited for character-level language modeling, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on a Wikipedia text corpus. The paper also provides an in-depth analysis of the network's ability to capture temporal hierarchies and model long-term dependencies.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the DRNN architecture and its advantages. The experimental results are thorough and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the network's behavior, including the emergence of different time scales in the layers of the DRNN.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a clear and concise explanation of the DRNN architecture and its advantages. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the network's behavior, which helps to understand how the DRNN is able to capture temporal hierarchies and model long-term dependencies.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One potential weakness is the lack of comparison to other state-of-the-art models, such as those proposed by Mikolov et al.
