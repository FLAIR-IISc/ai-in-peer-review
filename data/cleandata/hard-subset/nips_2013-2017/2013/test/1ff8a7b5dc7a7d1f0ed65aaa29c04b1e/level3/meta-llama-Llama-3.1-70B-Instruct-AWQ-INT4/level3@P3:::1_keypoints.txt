- Proposes DRNN architecture combining hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling and layer-by-layer supervised pretraining using next-letter prediction.  
- Trains very large networks and reports improved/state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia dataset versus prior RNN work.  
- Novelty: modeling temporal hierarchies with DRNNs is a new approach and clearly differentiated from prior contributions.  
- Strong analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses-matching emerges as pseudo-stable attractor-like behavior.  
- Effective visualizations (e.g., Euclidean distance between perturbed/unperturbed hidden states) illustrate learned dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written; experiments, figures, and theoretical/empirical claims are clear and well-supported.  
- Authors candidly acknowledge limitations: evaluation limited to one dataset and comparisons restricted to RNN-related models.  
- Results are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper’s broader goals, indicating more work is needed to understand DRNN capabilities.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, detailed behavioral analysis, empirical improvement, and balanced discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow evaluation scope, lack of comparisons to hierarchical HMMs, and inconclusive general claims.  
- Recommendation: evaluate on multiple datasets to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Recommendation: add comparisons to hierarchical HMMs, more visualization techniques, and clearer discussion of implications for language understanding and applications.
