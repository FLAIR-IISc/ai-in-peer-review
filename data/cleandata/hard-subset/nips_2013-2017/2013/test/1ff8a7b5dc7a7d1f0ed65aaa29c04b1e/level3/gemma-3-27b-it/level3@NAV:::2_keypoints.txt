- Paper studies depth in RNNs by stacking layers for character-level language modeling to capture hierarchical temporal dependencies.  
- Deeper RNNs with comparable parameter counts outperform shallower models on a Wikipedia corpus.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results using simple SGD rather than more complex optimizers.  
- Analysis shows emergent timescales: different layers encode information over varying temporal ranges.  
- Plain BPTT surprisingly learns to balance parentheses without Hessian-free training.  
- Strengths: strong empirical results and insightful analysis of learned representations.  
- Claim of introducing a “deep RNN” is overstated; similar architectures existed (e.g., Graves 2013), weakening novelty.  
- Results are demonstrated only on character-level text, limiting generalizability to other sequence tasks.  
- Major computational concern: authors use full BPTT; truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) should be explored for efficiency.  
- Acceptance arguments: solid empirical evidence, useful analysis, and surprising capabilities.  
- Rejection arguments: limited originality, task-specific validation, and potentially unnecessary training cost.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance, but urge experiments on other tasks and with truncated BPTT to strengthen claims.
