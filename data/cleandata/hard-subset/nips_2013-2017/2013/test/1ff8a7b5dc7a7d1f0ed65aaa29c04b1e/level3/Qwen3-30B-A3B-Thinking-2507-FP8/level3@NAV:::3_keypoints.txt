- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the previous layer’s hidden state, aiming to capture temporal hierarchies.  
- Reports state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia with DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O architectures.  
- Strong contributions: clear architectural innovation combining depth and recurrence; convincing text-generation results showing long-range dependency handling (Table 2).  
- Provides analysis of emergent time scales across layers and a compelling explanation of how hierarchical processing emerges (Figure 3).  
- Comparison to prior work is incomplete: must include Mikolov et al. (2010) Wikipedia compression results for fair benchmarking.  
- Performance comparisons are potentially misleading due to parameter-count differences and differences in training (e.g., MRNN trained with Hessian-free optimization).  
- Missing critical experiment: test whether relocating units to the first layer (vs. higher layers) yields similar performance to rule out trivial parameter-allocation effects.  
- Gradient normalization (Eq. 3) is used without sufficient justification; authors should explain how it avoids “bifurcations” and whether it disrupts SGD averaging.  
- Suggest exploring nonlinear layers between timesteps as an architectural variant worth investigating.  
- Relevant prior work (Bengio et al., ICML 2013 on gradient truncation) is not cited but should be.  
- Minor issues: DRNN-AO/IO not defined in text (only in Figure 1); layer-surgery interpretation (Figure 2) may be confounded by complex interdependencies.  
- Recommend more thorough analysis of depth: parameter-matched networks trained from scratch to evaluate DRNN depth effects.  
- Overall: promising and well-written contribution, but needs additional experiments and clarifications to fully justify the hierarchical-architecture claims.
