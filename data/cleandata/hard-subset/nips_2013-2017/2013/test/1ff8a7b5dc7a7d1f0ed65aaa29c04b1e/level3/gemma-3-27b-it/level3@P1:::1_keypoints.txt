- Proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks recurrent layers to create a hierarchy of timescales.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on a Wikipedia corpus using simple SGD.
- Provides a thorough analysis of learned representations, including emergent timescales and an "attractor-like" response to parentheses.
- Motivates the work by highlighting RNNs’ lack of explicit hierarchical temporal modeling and connecting to prior hierarchical RNN research.
- Novel architecture: a logical, well-motivated combination of deep and recurrent networks for complex temporal data.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior methods, including models trained with Hessian-free optimization.
- Detailed analysis and visualization: insightful layer-wise timescale analysis and response visualizations strengthen the contribution.
- Clear writing, organization, and informative figures make the paper accessible and well-presented.
- Experiments are comprehensive with thoughtful training setup, hyperparameter choices, and ablative studies.
- Limited evaluation: primary results restricted to a single Wikipedia dataset not universally standard in the community.
- Comparisons focus on RNNs; lacks evaluation against non-RNN hierarchical temporal models (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).
- Performance gains, while state-of-the-art, are not dramatically larger than existing RNN approaches.
- High training cost (≈10 days) may hinder accessibility for researchers with limited compute.
- Some uncertainty in rationale for the layer-wise training strategy and potential bias in output-connection variants (DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong results, valuable behavioral analysis, and solid presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: limited dataset/comparisons, moderate performance improvement, and substantial training time.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with insightful analysis; recommended for acceptance with suggestions to broaden datasets and comparisons.
