- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers forming a hierarchical temporal processing pipeline.  
- Addresses RNN limitation of implicit multi-time-scale handling by explicitly encouraging layer-wise temporal hierarchies.  
- Positions DRNNs as synthesis of deep hierarchies and recurrent memory, contrasting prior hierarchical/staked RNN work.  
- Core novelty: empirical demonstration that DRNN layers develop distinct time scales.  
- Validated on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia corpus) with analyses quantifying layer-specific time scales.  
- Strong technical quality: clear architecture, rigorous equations, and well-justified SGD training protocol.  
- Standout analysis: typo-perturbation and parentheses-closing experiments show layer roles and long-term dependency modeling (pseudo-stable attractor).  
- DRNN-1O achieves near state-of-the-art RNN performance (BPC=1.30) using only SGD; ablation studies link layers to task functions.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single Wikipedia corpus, not standard NLP benchmarks, reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: missing direct comparisons to hierarchical baselines (e.g., HMMs, hierarchical RNNs), a missed opportunity for relevance.  
- Weakness: performance gains are modest and sometimes inferior to Hessian-free methods; some DRNN variants underperform.  
- Originality: architecture is novel, but primary contribution is analytical insight into emergent time scales rather than pure SOTA performance.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of how deep RNNs process temporal hierarchies and offers a framework for future work.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, rigorous, and clear paper; analysis outweighs empirical scope limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept; future work should evaluate on standard datasets and compare against hierarchical HMMs.
