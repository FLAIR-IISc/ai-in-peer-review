- Proposes hierarchical DRNN architecture where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the previous layer’s hidden state, applied to character-level language modeling with strong SGD results.
- Main contribution: analysis showing deeper DRNN layers capture longer-range temporal information and model long-term dependencies.
- Empirical finding: DRNNs outperform standard RNNs when number of parameters is held constant.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents thorough experiments.
- Provides a detailed analysis of time scales present in DRNN layers, enhancing understanding of these models.
- Weakness: claim of introducing deep RNN architecture is overstated given prior work (e.g., Graves 2013).
- Concern: results may be task-specific and should be replicated on additional tasks (e.g., speech).
- Concern: use of Hessian-free optimization may be unnecessarily expensive; truncated BPTT should be tested as an alternative.
- Arguments for acceptance: thorough analysis, promising performance, and insightful time-scale analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty in architecture, potential task-specificity, and costly optimization choices.
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
