- Proposes a novel Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers to model temporal hierarchies in time series data.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art recurrent network performance on character-level language modeling using a Wikipedia corpus.  
- Relates to prior hierarchical RNN work but focuses on isolating architectural contributions to performance.  
- Draws inspiration from successes of deep neural networks in other domains like speech and handwriting.  
- Paper quality is high: well-written, clear experiments, and supportive figures.  
- Provides detailed analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses matching behaves like a pseudo-stable attractor.  
- Introduces a novel layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using next-letter prediction.  
- Significance is high: offers a new perspective for processing temporal hierarchies and modeling complex temporal relationships.  
- Contributes to debate on training algorithms, comparing SGD and second-order methods.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single, not widely used dataset (Hinton et al. Wikipedia).  
- Comparisons omit relevant baselines such as hierarchical HMMs and non-RNN approaches.  
- Results are somewhat inconclusive: performance gains are not consistently demonstrated across all experiments.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, citing novelty and thorough analysis despite noted limitations.
