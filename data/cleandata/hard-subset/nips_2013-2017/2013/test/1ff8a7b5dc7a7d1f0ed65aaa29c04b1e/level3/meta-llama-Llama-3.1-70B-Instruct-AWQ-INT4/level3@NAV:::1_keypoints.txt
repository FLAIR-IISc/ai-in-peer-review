- Proposes a novel deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) architecture combining hierarchical deep features with time-series modeling.  
- Introduces a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training using next-letter prediction to enable training of very large networks.  
- Demonstrates improved performance on a Wikipedia dataset.  
- Provides detailed analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and parentheses matching forms attractor-like states.  
- Strength: architecture is novel, well-motivated, and addresses RNN limitations.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of time scales, long-term interactions, and syntactic behaviors.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear figures and experiments.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a single dataset and may lack generality.  
- Weakness: comparisons restricted to RNN-related models, omitting hierarchical HMMs and other relevant approaches.  
- Weakness: results are somewhat inconclusive—DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO perform similarly while common RNN underperforms.  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and complete experiments.  
- Clarity: clearly written and well-organized.  
- Originality: architecture and analysis are novel and contribute to the field.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and yields important insights.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate on additional datasets/tasks and compare against other model families (e.g., hierarchical HMMs).  
- Suggested improvements: provide more conclusive results or clearer advantages between DRNN variants.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted limitations.
