- Studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) where each layer is an RNN receiving the previous layer's hidden state, forming a temporal hierarchy for time-series processing.  
- Shows DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on Wikipedia using only SGD, challenging the notion that Hessian-free (HF) optimization is necessary.  
- Provides an insightful layer-wise analysis: bottom layers capture short-term dependencies while top layers encode longer-term context.  
- Builds on prior stacked-RNN/LSTM work (e.g., Graves et al. 2013, El Hihi & Bengio 1996) but overclaims architectural novelty.  
- Strength: deeper RNNs outperform standard RNNs with the same parameter count on language modeling tasks.  
- Strength: demonstrates DRNNs can solve long-term dependency tasks (e.g., balancing parentheses) without HF training.  
- Strength: perturbation experiments convincingly reveal layer-specific time scales and training dynamics.  
- Weakness: novelty claim is problematic—similar stacked architectures were used in prior work and should be acknowledged and differentiated.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to character-level language modeling, limiting the generality of conclusions about temporal hierarchies.  
- Weakness: does not evaluate truncated BPTT (a more efficient alternative to HF), despite literature showing competitive performance.  
- Weakness: high training cost (~10 days per network on a single GPU) raises practical concerns and lacks thorough cost comparisons.  
- Suggestion: replicate analyses on additional tasks (e.g., speech recognition) to validate the generality of the temporal hierarchy claim.  
- Suggestion: test and compare truncated BPTT and other efficient training methods, and provide detailed training cost comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: valuable analysis of temporal hierarchies and SGD training, but limited significance due to narrow scope and overstated novelty; acceptable for NIPS with revisions addressing novelty and expanded experiments.
