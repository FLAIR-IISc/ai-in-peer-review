- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN) that stacks RNN layers so each layer processes hidden states from the layer below to form a temporal hierarchy.  
- Evaluated on large-scale character-level language modeling (Wikipedia); achieves comparable performance to prior SOTA recurrent models.  
- Trained with simple SGD instead of complex second-order methods (Hessian-free) while matching SOTA results.  
- Provides in-depth empirical analysis showing different layers learn different time scales, with higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies.  
- Demonstrates ability to handle long-range structures (e.g., parenthesis matching) via pseudo-stable attractor-like states.  
- Examines two architectural variants: DRNN-1O (output from top layer) and DRNN-AO (outputs from all layers), plus a layer-wise training scheme for DRNN-1O.  
- High technical quality: well-described architecture, thorough experiments, careful training details, and honest reporting.  
- Exceptional clarity and organization; figures effectively illustrate key claims (time scales and parenthesis behavior).  
- Original contributions: first detailed analysis of why stacked RNNs work, strong large-scale results, and supervised layer-wise pretraining for recurrent nets.  
- Significant impact: shows depth improves modeling of complex temporal structure and that SGD suffices at large scale; insights likely to influence future sequential-model research.  
- Weakness: experimental validation limited to a single Wikipedia dataset, limiting generality claims.  
- Weakness: comparisons are narrowly focused on RNNs and lack broader hierarchical time-series model baselines.  
- Weakness: performance improvement over baseline RNNs is modest; main advance is analysis and optimizer simplicity rather than large accuracy gains.  
- Recommendation: overall excellent paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
