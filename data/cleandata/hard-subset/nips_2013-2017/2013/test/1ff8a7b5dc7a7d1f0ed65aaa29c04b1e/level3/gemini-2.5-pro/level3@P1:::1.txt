### **Summary**

This paper introduces the Deep Recurrent Neural Network (DRNN), an architecture that stacks multiple RNN layers to form a hierarchy. The motivation is that standard RNNs, which can be viewed as very deep networks unfolded in time, lack an explicit mechanism for hierarchical processing of information at each timestep. In a DRNN, the hidden state of each layer serves as the input sequence for the layer above it, allowing the model to combine the temporal memory of RNNs with the hierarchical feature extraction characteristic of deep feedforward networks. The authors hypothesize that this structure allows different layers to operate at different time scales, naturally capturing the temporal hierarchy present in many real-world sequences.

The authors evaluate this architecture on a large-scale character-level language modeling task using a Wikipedia dataset previously explored by Sutskever et al. (2011). They test two variants: one where only the top layer produces an output (DRNN-1O), and another where all layers contribute to the output (DRNN-AO). The DRNN-1O model is trained using a novel incremental, layer-wise supervised procedure. The results show that the DRNN models achieve performance comparable to the state-of-the-art for recurrent networks on this dataset, notably achieving this with simple Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) rather than more complex second-order methods like Hessian-Free optimization.

The paper's main contribution, however, is a detailed analysis of the trained models. Through a series of diagnostic experiments, the authors provide compelling evidence that the layers of the DRNN learn to operate on different time scales, with higher layers exhibiting longer memory. They further analyze the model's ability to handle long-range dependencies, such as matching opening and closing parentheses over hundreds of characters, and characterize this behavior as the network learning a pseudo-stable attractor-like state.

### **Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high quality. The proposed models are technically sound, and the experiments are conducted with care, especially considering the significant computational resources required (e.g., ~10 days of training per model). The most impressive aspect is the in-depth analysis of the network's internal dynamics. The authors go beyond simply reporting performance metrics and provide insightful experiments to understand *how* the model works. For instance, the typo-perturbation experiment (Figure 3) and the analysis of parenthesis matching (Figure 4) are excellent diagnostics that support the central claims about temporal hierarchies.

A potential weakness lies in the experimental evaluation of performance. While the comparison to the multiplicative RNN from Sutskever et al. (2011) is fair and direct, the evaluation is confined to a single dataset. The comparisons in Table 1 to other approaches (like PAQ) or results on different corpora are not direct and can be misleading. A more robust claim of state-of-the-art performance would require evaluation on more standard benchmarks and comparison against a wider class of models, including other hierarchical sequence models like hierarchical HMMs.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation for DRNNs is clearly articulated in the introduction. The model architecture is described precisely with equations and an effective diagram. The experimental setup and the analytical experiments are explained in sufficient detail. The figures are clear, well-designed, and effectively communicate the main findings of the analysis. The writing is a model of clarity for an empirical paper.

**Originality:** The core idea of stacking recurrent layers is not entirely new and is acknowledged by the authors with citations to prior work (e.g., El Hihi & Bengio, 1996; Graves et al., 2013). However, the paper's originality comes from several key aspects: (1) The application of this architecture to a very large-scale, challenging task. (2) The novel layer-wise training procedure for the DRNN-1O model. (3) Most importantly, the comprehensive and insightful analysis of the emergent temporal hierarchy within the network. While others may have proposed similar architectures, this paper is one of the first to so clearly demonstrate and analyze *why* such an architecture is effective, connecting the model's structure to the hierarchical nature of the data.

**Significance:** This paper is significant for several reasons. First, it provides strong evidence that adding depth to RNNs in this manner is a powerful way to model complex sequences with structure at multiple time scales. The analysis techniques presented are valuable contributions in their own right and provide a template for how to probe and understand the inner workings of deep sequence models. Second, it demonstrates that impressive results on large-scale RNN training can be achieved with a carefully tuned SGD procedure (with gradient clipping), offering a simpler alternative to the complex second-order methods that were gaining traction at the time. The insights from this work have likely influenced subsequent research into deep and hierarchical recurrent architectures, which have become standard in sequence modeling. The primary contribution is less about a definitive performance breakthrough and more about the fundamental analysis of how depth affects temporal processing in RNNs.

### **Arguments for Acceptance**

*   **Pro:** The paper presents a compelling combination of a simple, effective model architecture with a deep and insightful analysis of its learned behavior.
*   **Pro:** The analysis of the emergent temporal hierarchy is a strong and significant contribution that helps build a better understanding of deep learning models for sequential data.
*   **Pro:** It achieves results competitive with the state-of-the-art (at the time, on this dataset) using a much simpler optimization algorithm, which is an important practical result.
*   **Pro:** The paper is extremely well-written, clear, and serves as an excellent example of a high-quality empirical study.

### **Arguments against Acceptance**

*   **Con:** The experimental validation of performance is limited to a single dataset, making it difficult to generalize the performance claims.
*   **Con:** The comparison is restricted to other RNN-based models, omitting potentially relevant baselines from other families of hierarchical time-series models.
*   **Con:** The performance gains over the baseline single-layer RNN, while present, are not dramatic, which makes the analysis of the model's properties a much stronger contribution than the performance results themselves.

Overall, the strengths, particularly the quality of the analysis and its significance for understanding deep models, far outweigh the weaknesses related to the scope of the experimental evaluation.
