- Paper studies stacked deep recurrent neural networks (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) for character-level language modeling, reporting strong results with simple SGD and layer-wise training.
- Connects to prior work on hierarchical/stacked RNNs and MRNN/Hessian-free training; complements recent work on training pathologies and gradient clipping.

Strengths
- Stacking recurrent layers yields a plausible temporal hierarchy; analyses support lower layers for short-term and higher for longer contexts.
- Achieves competitive BPC among similarly sized RNNs using plain SGD without second-order methods.
- Thoughtful analyses (perturbations, context-length quantification, parenthesis case study) demonstrate emergent time scales and long-range behavior.
- Paper is well-structured and accessible.

Main weaknesses / questions
- Depth vs. width confound: unclear if gains come from depth or reallocating capacity to a single wider layer.
- No ablation quantifying the empirical importance of gradient normalization versus clipping or no normalization.
- Limited architectural comparisons: deep transition (within-time-step) variants not evaluated.
- Comparisons to standard Wikipedia compression/language benchmarks (enwik8/enwik9) are missing.
- Interpretation of layer “surgery” is confounded by interdependent readout contributions; conclusions about individual layer roles may be overstated.

Requested experiments / analyses
- Ablate gradient normalization: include no normalization, norm clipping (as in Pascanu et al.), and full renormalization; report learning curves, gradient norms, and final BPC.
- Define and provide evidence for “bifurcations” or clarify the term with trajectories/Lyapunov/activation stats.
- Discuss whether per-step renormalization disrupts SGD’s noise/averaging properties and compare to clipping with threshold c.
- Evaluate deep transition RNN variants (extra nonlinear layers or residual/MLP transitions per time step) under matched parameter budgets.
- Run depth-vs-width controls: single-layer wider baselines and families of models with constant total parameters across depths; conduct capacity-relocation experiments moving units from top to bottom layers.
- Report exact parameter counts per component (recurrent, inter-layer, output) and control for DRNN-AO vs DRNN-1O differences.
- Plot test BPC versus DRNN depth for several parameter budgets and include wider shallow baselines.
- Compare on canonical compression tasks (enwik8/enwik9) and, if possible, report compression bits via arithmetic coding for apples-to-apples with PAQ/RNN baselines.
- Use complementary diagnostics to interpret layer roles (e.g., CKA/CCA similarity, probing classifiers) in addition to readout-zeroing.

Presentation / reproducibility requests
- Define DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O explicitly on first mention and use consistent notation.
- Clarify “bifurcation” meaning in training and provide training details: number of data passes, batching, seeding.
- Improve figures (e.g., BPC vs depth) and report exact parameter breakdowns.

Quality / clarity / originality / significance
- Methodology and analyses are sound; main claims are supported but require extra ablations for stronger conclusions.
- Well-written and organized; adding requested definitions and training heuristics will improve clarity.
- Original in combining stacked RNNs with detailed emergent time-scale analyses; potentially influential with additional controls.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear motivation, simple effective architecture, strong SGD-based results, and insightful hierarchical time-scale analyses.

Cons for acceptance
- Missing depth-vs-width and parameter relocation controls, no gradient-normalization ablation, and limited benchmark comparisons.

Overall recommendation
- Worthwhile and largely convincing; lean to accept contingent on adding key ablations (gradient normalization, depth/width sweeps) and broader comparisons (Mikolov/PAQ on enwik8/enwik9).
