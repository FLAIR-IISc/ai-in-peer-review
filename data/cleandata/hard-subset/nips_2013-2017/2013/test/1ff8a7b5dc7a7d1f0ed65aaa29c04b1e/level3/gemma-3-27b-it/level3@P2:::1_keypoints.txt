- Proposes a deep recurrent neural network (DRNN) that stacks recurrent layers to create a hierarchy of timescales.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art character-level language modeling on the Wikipedia corpus, outperforming prior RNN results.  
- Provides original analysis of emergent time scales and parenthesis-matching behavior within the network.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN and deep learning work, stressing deeper analysis and real-world evaluation over prior artificial-data studies.  
- Architecture is clearly and mathematically described; training and experimental protocols are sound and include gradient normalization for stability.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, intuition, and helpful figures.  
- Insightful experiments reveal how DRNNs represent temporal dependencies and treat parentheses as attractor-like states.  
- Achieves strong empirical results using only plain SGD rather than complex optimizers.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Wikipedia), reducing generalizability and complicating cross-paper comparisons.  
- Comparisons focus mainly on RNN-based models and omit other hierarchical temporal models (e.g., HMM variants).  
- Layer-wise incremental training (DRNN-1O) may introduce bias and might not be optimal compared to simultaneous training.  
- Some sensitivity to hyperparameters (notably learning rate) is reported but not thoroughly investigated.  
- Computational cost and scalability are under-discussed despite long training times (~10 days).  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with compelling results and analysis; recommended for acceptance (score 7/10) with suggestions to expand datasets, broaden comparisons, and study alternative training and hyperparameter strategies.
