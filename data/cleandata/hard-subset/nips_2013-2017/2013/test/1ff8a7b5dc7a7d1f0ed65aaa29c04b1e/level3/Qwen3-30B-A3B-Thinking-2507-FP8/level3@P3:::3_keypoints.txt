- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) where each layer is a recurrent network receiving the previous layer’s hidden state, creating a natural temporal hierarchy.  
- Shows state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using only SGD, avoiding complex optimizers.  
- Text-generation results (Table 2) produce coherent, grammatically structured outputs, supporting practical effectiveness.  
- Perturbation experiments (Figure 3) convincingly demonstrate different layers operating at different temporal scales.  
- Parentheses-closing experiment (Figure 4) shows DRNNs can maintain memory over hundreds of characters.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and contributes to understanding hierarchical RNN architectures.  
- Suggest adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps to separate processing from temporal propagation and report whether this was tried.  
- Clarify the role and necessity of gradient normalization, explain how it prevents “bifurcations,” and relate it to prior formal work on gradient truncation.  
- Discuss whether gradient normalization disrupts SGD’s averaging behavior and potential effects on generalization.  
- Improve baseline comparisons (e.g., Mikolov et al.), and match parameter counts or units to fairly isolate architectural benefits (current DRNN 4.9M vs baseline 2.1M).  
- Run the key experiment testing whether depth provides benefits beyond parameter allocation (compare to a single-layer network with equal total units).  
- Replace Figure 2 with plots comparing various DRNN depths trained from scratch, including wider layers to match parameters/units for fair depth vs width evaluation.  
- Define terms like DRNN-AO and DRNN-IO in the text (they are only in Figure 1), to remove confusion.  
- Overall: compelling and valuable paper that would be suitable for NIPS; recommend acceptance with minor revisions pending the above experiments and clarifications.
