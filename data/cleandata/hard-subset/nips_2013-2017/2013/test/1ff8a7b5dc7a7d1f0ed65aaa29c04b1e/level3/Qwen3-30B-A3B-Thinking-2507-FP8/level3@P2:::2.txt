

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), where each layer is a recurrent network that receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input, creating a temporal hierarchy. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling using only stochastic gradient descent (SGD), without requiring advanced optimization techniques like Hessian-free training. They also analyze how different layers in the DRNN capture information at different time scales, and provide evidence that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies like balancing parentheses.

The paper builds on previous work in hierarchical RNNs (El Hihi & Bengio, 1995; Graves et al., 2013), though it properly acknowledges that stacked bi-directional LSTM networks were previously used in Graves et al. (2013) for speech recognition. The authors correctly note that their focus is on analyzing the architecture rather than proposing a fundamentally new model, which is acceptable given the emphasis on the analysis of time scales.

Strengths:
1. The paper provides compelling evidence that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using only SGD, challenging the assumption that more complex optimization techniques are necessary for such tasks.
2. The analysis of time scales across different layers is insightful, showing how deeper layers encode more long-range information, with the top layer specifically capturing information beyond word-length sequences.
3. The experimental design is thorough, including ablation studies on layer contributions, analysis of the network's ability to handle parentheses (a long-term dependency task), and comparisons to prior work.
4. The paper makes a valuable contribution by demonstrating that plain BPTT can effectively train RNNs for tasks requiring long-term memory, contrary to prior belief that Hessian-free optimization was necessary.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper improperly claims to have introduced the DRNN architecture, when Graves et al. (2013) already employed a similar stacked bidirectional LSTM architecture for speech recognition. This needs proper acknowledgment in the introduction and related work.
2. The results are limited to character-level language modeling, with no demonstration of generalizability to other tasks (e.g., speech recognition or machine translation). The authors should test on additional tasks to strengthen their claims about the architecture's broader utility.
3. The paper does not address the computational cost of training DRNNs compared to standard RNNs. The authors note training takes "about 10 days" on a single GPU, but don't compare this to training times for standard RNNs with similar parameter counts.
4. The paper could have tested truncated BPTT (with ~30 steps), as suggested in the rebuttal, which would be significantly more efficient while likely maintaining similar performance on entropy metrics.

The paper's main contribution is the analysis of temporal hierarchies in DRNNs and the demonstration that plain SGD can achieve state-of-the-art results for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling. The finding that DRNNs can model long-term dependencies like parentheses using standard training methods is particularly valuable.

However, the claim that DRNNs "reach state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks" needs qualification. The results are comparable to previous work using Hessian-free optimization, but the paper doesn't demonstrate that DRNNs are superior to other architectures (like LSTMs) with similar parameter counts. The paper would benefit from including a comparison to a well-tuned LSTM with the same number of parameters.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, though it could improve by:
1. More thoroughly contextualizing the architecture within prior work on hierarchical RNNs
2. Expanding the experimental evaluation to additional tasks
3. Providing more detail on the computational cost of training DRNNs
4. Including a comparison to standard LSTM architectures with similar parameter counts

Overall, the paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how hierarchical architectures can be used with recurrent networks to capture temporal hierarchies. The analysis of time scales is particularly insightful and could inform future work on RNN design. While the results are limited to character-level language modeling, the insights about temporal hierarchies likely generalize to other sequence tasks.

Pro: Demonstrates that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art results using only SGD, challenging assumptions about the need for complex optimization methods. The analysis of time scales across layers is novel and insightful.

Con: The architecture is not new (prior work by Graves et al. already used similar models), and the results are limited to one task (character-level language modeling) without demonstrating broader applicability. The paper could have tested truncated BPTT to validate the claim that simple SGD is sufficient.

The paper is of interest to the NIPS community as it provides new insights into how RNNs can be structured to capture temporal hierarchies, which is relevant for many sequence modeling tasks. While not groundbreaking, it makes a solid contribution to the understanding of recurrent network architectures and their training. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the paper should be revised to properly acknowledge prior work and expand the experimental evaluation.
