- Proposes a novel approach combining Bayesian word learning models with visual classifiers to infer generalization from few positive images.  
- Well-written paper with properly set up experiments, clear figures, and tables.  
- Outperforms baseline models on average precision and F1 scores.  
- Provides detailed analysis, including per-level analysis of generalization responses.  
- Introduces a new dataset for visual concept learning to be released to the community.  
- Experimental comparison is limited to a single dataset.  
- Comparisons are restricted to RNN-related baselines; lacks evaluation against other hierarchical models (e.g., HMMs).  
- Contribution is stronger in analysis of the network’s operation than in novel performance achievements.  
- Technically sound; claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Presents an original combination of existing techniques and situates work clearly relative to prior art.  
- Significant contribution: addresses a difficult problem, advances the state of the art, and is likely to be used by others.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but urges broader comparisons and multi-dataset evaluation.
