- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs) stacking RNN layers so each layer receives the previous layer's hidden state, forming a temporal hierarchy.  
- Claims DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art character-level language modeling using simple SGD, without advanced optimizers like Hessian-free methods.  
- Provides empirical evidence that DRNN layers develop distinct time scales: bottom layers handle short-term dependencies, top layers capture long-term patterns.  
- Parenthesis-closure experiments particularly demonstrate improved long-term dependency maintenance compared to standard RNNs.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and technically sound with experiments on a Wikipedia corpus and comparisons to prior work (e.g., Hessian-free MRNN).  
- Analysis of emergent time scales (Figure 3) gives valuable theoretical insight into temporal processing in DRNNs.  
- Comparison fairness concern: DRNN-AO (9.6M params) outperforms RNN (2.1M) but lacks matched-parameter baselines to isolate architectural benefits.  
- Authors should clarify whether they tried adding extra nonlinear layers between timesteps, as suggested could be beneficial.  
- Gradient normalization (Eq.3) is insufficiently justified; the term "bifurcations" is unexplained and necessity for convergence is not evidenced.  
- Recommend comparing gradient treatment to Pascanu et al. (ICML 2013) on gradient truncation/optimization.  
- Layer “surgery” experiments are interesting but potentially misleading because layer contributions are not independent; interpreting them as additive is confounded.  
- A stronger experiment would test if higher layers genuinely benefit from upstream processing versus simply relocating units to the first layer.  
- Paper needs experiments across varying DRNN depths (not just 5-layer) with parameter-matched architectures to establish depth-performance relationship.  
- DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O should be defined earlier in the text rather than only in Figure 1.  
- Text-generation examples (Table 2) are persuasive, showing clear degradation when bottom-layer contributions are removed.  
- Claim of “state-of-the-art” is misleading without comparison to best-performing models on this dataset (e.g., Mikolov et al.).  
- Overall: strong conceptual and empirical contribution on hierarchical RNNs and temporal structure, but needs fairer comparisons, more depth/parameter-controlled experiments, and clearer justification of optimization choices.
