

# Review of "Training and Analyzing Deep Recurrent Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), a novel architecture that stacks multiple recurrent layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. The authors argue that standard RNNs do not explicitly accommodate the temporal hierarchy present in many time series, and propose DRNNs as a solution that naturally creates different time scales at different hierarchical levels. The paper makes three main contributions: (1) a new architecture combining hierarchical deep learning with time-series modeling, (2) a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training procedure using next-letter prediction, and (3) detailed analysis of how the network captures temporal hierarchies.

The paper builds on previous hierarchical RNN work by [5, 8] but offers a more thorough analysis of the temporal properties. It extends the concept of deep learning to the temporal domain by explicitly creating a hierarchy of time scales, addressing a fundamental limitation of standard RNNs that process all temporal information through a single time scale. The authors train large DRNNs (approximately 4.9 million parameters) on a Wikipedia character-level language modeling task, achieving state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks using simple stochastic gradient descent, whereas prior work required Hessian-free optimization.

The paper's strongest contribution is its detailed analysis of the network's emergent properties. The authors demonstrate that higher layers capture long-term dependencies, with the top layer showing particularly strong memory of opening parentheses for hundreds of characters, suggesting the network learns to model parentheses as a pseudo-stable attractor-like state. This is supported by experiments showing DRNNs outperform standard RNNs in closing parentheses (12% failure rate vs. 28% for RNNs) and by time-scale analysis showing different layers operate at different temporal resolutions.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear figures and technical explanations. The training methodology is thoroughly documented, and the analysis of network behavior is particularly compelling. The authors carefully address limitations, including the single-dataset evaluation and restricted comparison to RNN-related models.

However, the paper has several limitations that affect its significance. The evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (Wikipedia text corpus) that is not widely used in the field, and comparisons are limited to RNN-related models (excluding hierarchical HMMs), which are not directly comparable to the DRNN approach. The performance gains over prior work are modest (e.g., DRNN-1O achieves 1.50 BPC vs. 1.49 for Hessian-free MRNN), and the authors acknowledge that the difference may reflect limitations caused by network size rather than the architecture itself. The paper's stated goal was to demonstrate that hierarchical processing improves performance on temporal tasks, but the results are somewhat inconclusive relative to this goal.

The main strengths of the paper are its insightful analysis of network behavior and the clear demonstration of temporal hierarchies in DRNNs. The weaknesses are the limited experimental evaluation and the modest performance gains that don't conclusively prove the superiority of the proposed architecture.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with well-executed experiments and clear theoretical foundations. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations, though the single-dataset evaluation weakens the generalizability of the claims.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally clear, with excellent figures and well-structured explanations of both the architecture and experimental results. The technical details are sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.

Originality: The DRNN architecture is a novel combination of hierarchical deep learning and time-series modeling. While hierarchical RNNs have been explored previously ([5, 8]), the paper's detailed analysis of temporal hierarchies and the specific pre-training method represent significant contributions.

Significance: The analysis of how the network operates (temporal hierarchies, attractor states) is highly significant and provides valuable insights into deep temporal modeling. However, the limited performance gains and narrow evaluation restrict the paper's immediate impact, as the claimed advantages over standard RNNs aren't conclusively demonstrated.

Arguments for acceptance: The paper offers a clear and insightful analysis of temporal hierarchies in deep RNNs, with compelling evidence that higher layers capture long-term dependencies. The demonstration of parentheses matching as an attractor-like state is particularly novel. The architecture and pre-training method are well-motivated and could be broadly applicable.

Arguments against acceptance: The evaluation is too limited to a single non-standard dataset, comparisons to relevant hierarchical HMMs are missing, and the performance gains over prior work are modest. The paper's stated goal of demonstrating architectural superiority isn't fully supported by the evidence.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper with significant theoretical insights about temporal processing in deep networks. While the experimental validation is limited, the analysis of network behavior provides valuable understanding that will inform future research on deep temporal modeling. The contribution is stronger in analysis than in demonstrating conclusive performance gains, but this analysis is sufficiently compelling to warrant acceptance, particularly given the paper's clarity and the current interest in temporal modeling architectures.
