- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers receiving previous layer hidden states, claimed to create a temporal hierarchy and achieve SOTA in character-level language modeling with simple SGD.  
- Provides analysis of emergent time scales across layers and shows DRNNs produce coherent long-term text (text-generation experiments).  
- Typos-perturbation test (Figure 3) supports the claim that different layers operate at different time scales.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and contributes to understanding multi-time-scale processing in RNNs.  
- Unfair comparison: baseline RNN has far fewer parameters (2,119 units) versus DRNNs (~4.9M params); need baselines matched for parameter count or units.  
- Missing critical experiment: test whether identical units placed in the first layer (vs higher layers) achieve similar performance to isolate architectural hierarchy benefits from mere capacity.  
- Gradient normalization is under-explained: clarify how it prevents "bifurcations," how it differs from clipping, and whether it affects SGD averaging behavior.  
- Missing relevant comparisons/citations: should compare to Mikolov et al. on Wikipedia compression and cite ICML 2013 work on gradient optimization/truncation.  
- Terminology clarity: define DRNN-AO and DRNN-1O in the text, not only in Figure 1.  
- Layer-removal (surgery) interpretation is confounded by interdependencies; claims about individual layer importance should be tempered.  
- Need experiments plotting performance vs DRNN depth trained from scratch (with widths adjusted to match total parameters/units) to separate depth effects from parameter count.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable architecture, technically sound and convincing but requires major revision to address parameter-comparison and missing-experiment concerns — recommendation: Major Revision.
