

# Review for NIPS 2013

The paper introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs), an architecture that stacks recurrent layers where each layer receives the hidden state of the previous layer as input. This creates a temporal hierarchy where different layers process information at different time scales, addressing a limitation of standard RNNs that do not explicitly accommodate hierarchical temporal structures. The authors demonstrate that DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance for recurrent networks on character-level language modeling (Wikipedia text corpus) using only stochastic gradient descent, previously requiring more complex Hessian-free optimization. They provide detailed analysis of the emergent time scales across layers, showing higher layers capture longer-term dependencies, and demonstrate DRNNs' ability to handle long-range dependencies like parentheses matching.

The paper builds upon prior work on hierarchical RNNs (e.g., [3,5,6,8]) but makes a significant contribution by systematically analyzing how DRNNs naturally develop different time scales across layers. The architecture is a natural extension of deep learning concepts to the recurrent setting, combining the hierarchical processing of DNNs with the temporal dynamics of RNNs. The authors relate their work to previous NIPS contributions such as Graves et al. (2013) on deep RNNs for speech recognition and Hochreiter & Schmidhuber's LSTM work.

**Strengths:**
1. **Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound architecture with clear theoretical justification. The experimental design is robust, with careful analysis of how different layers process information at different time scales. The use of perturbation experiments to measure time scale differences is particularly insightful. The results are well-supported by both quantitative metrics (BPC) and qualitative analysis (text generation, parentheses matching).

2. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear figures and explanations. The architecture is easy to visualize (Figure 1), and the explanation of how different layers process information at different time scales is particularly clear. The analysis of parentheses matching provides a concrete, intuitive demonstration of the model's long-term memory capabilities.

3. **Originality:** While hierarchical RNNs have been explored before (citing [5,8]), this paper makes a novel contribution by systematically analyzing the emergent time scales in the architecture and demonstrating how these scales correspond to different aspects of language structure. The demonstration that DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art results with simple SGD is a significant contribution to the training algorithm discussion in deep learning.

4. **Significance:** The work provides important insights into how hierarchical architectures process temporal information, with implications for time series analysis beyond language modeling. The demonstration that DRNNs can capture long-range dependencies without specialized memory mechanisms (like LSTMs) is particularly significant for understanding the nature of recurrent processing.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Limited Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is restricted to a single dataset (Wikipedia text) with comparisons limited to RNN-related models. The authors acknowledge that other approaches (like hierarchical HMMs) are excluded due to different corpora and metrics, making it difficult to assess the broader significance of the results. The paper would benefit from comparisons to more diverse datasets or architectures.

2. **Inconclusive Performance Gains:** While the paper claims state-of-the-art performance, the results are somewhat inconclusive relative to the paper's stated goals. The DRNN-AO performs slightly worse than DRNN-1O, and the performance gains over standard RNNs are modest (1.24 vs 1.33 BPC). The paper emphasizes the analysis of time scales more than conclusive performance gains.

3. **Training Methodology:** The training procedure for DRNN-1O (layer-wise training with incremental addition of layers) is complex and not clearly justified beyond "necessary to reach good performance." The authors acknowledge this is different from standard layer-wise training, but don't thoroughly explain why this specific approach is needed.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- Provides important theoretical and empirical insights into how hierarchical architectures process temporal information
- Demonstrates DRNNs can achieve state-of-the-art results with simple SGD, challenging assumptions about the need for complex training algorithms
- Well-written with clear analysis of time scales across layers, including the insightful parentheses matching example
- Makes a meaningful contribution to the understanding of temporal hierarchies in neural networks

*Con:*
- Limited experimental scope with evaluation restricted to a single dataset
- Performance gains are modest and not as conclusive as the paper suggests
- Training methodology for DRNN-1O is not fully justified or explained
- Does not adequately compare to hierarchical HMMs or other non-RNN approaches that might handle temporal hierarchies

**Recommendation:**

The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding how hierarchical architectures process temporal information, with strong theoretical motivation and insightful analysis. While the experimental results are somewhat limited in scope, the analysis of time scales across layers is particularly compelling and advances our understanding of how RNNs process temporal information. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a clear contribution to the field.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limited evaluation scope (e.g., adding a sentence about how results might generalize to other datasets or tasks) and to more thoroughly justify the training procedure for DRNN-1O. The paper's insights into temporal hierarchies are significant, and its contribution to understanding how RNNs process information across time scales makes it a valuable addition to NIPS.
