This paper investigates the properties of deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs), an architecture where multiple RNN layers are stacked hierarchically. The authors apply this model to the task of character-level language modeling on a large Wikipedia corpus. The central contributions are twofold: first, an empirical demonstration that DRNNs outperform a standard "shallow" RNN when the total number of parameters is held constant; and second, a detailed analysis of the trained DRNNs, providing evidence that deeper layers learn to operate on longer time scales and capture more abstract, long-range dependencies in the text. This work builds upon previous research in deep learning, which has shown the benefits of depth in feed-forward networks, and extends this line of inquiry to recurrent models. It also relates to prior work on character-level language modeling, particularly Sutskever et al. (2011), which tackled the same dataset using a multiplicative RNN trained with Hessian-Free (HF) optimization. A key finding of this paper is that a DRNN trained with simple stochastic gradient descent (using BPTT and gradient clipping) can achieve comparable state-of-the-art performance, suggesting that complex second-order methods may not be strictly necessary.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results and Analysis:** The paper presents compelling results on a difficult benchmark. The DRNN models significantly outperform the baseline shallow RNN in terms of bits-per-character (BPC), achieving performance competitive with the state of the art established by more complex models and training procedures. The analysis that follows is the paper's main strength. The experiments measuring the "memory" of each layer via typo-perturbation (Figure 3) provide clear, quantitative evidence for the emergence of a temporal hierarchy. The qualitative analysis of text generation and the specific investigation into long-range dependencies, such as balancing parentheses (Figure 4), are insightful and effectively demonstrate the model's capabilities.

2.  **Demonstration of Simpler Training Methods:** A significant contribution is showing that full backpropagation through time (BPTT), a first-order method, is sufficient to train these deep recurrent models to capture surprisingly long dependencies. The ability of the model to learn to close parentheses over hundreds of characters contradicts a common belief that such long-range structures are only learnable with more powerful optimizers like Hessian-Free. This is an important and practical result for the community.

3.  **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The architecture is explained well, the experiments are motivated, and the results are presented effectively through tables and figures.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Positioning and Originality:** The paper could be clearer about its novelty. The deep RNN architecture itself is not new; for instance, Graves et al. (ICASSP 2013, cited as [8]) used stacked LSTMs for speech recognition to great effect. While the paper's focus on analysis is perfectly acceptable and valuable, the current framing might lead readers to believe the architecture is a novel contribution of this work. The authors should more explicitly position the paper as an in-depth analysis and successful application of this architecture to a new domain, rather than the introduction of the architecture itself.

2.  **Generalizability of Claims:** The central claims about the benefits of depth and the emergence of a temporal hierarchy are supported by experiments on a single task: character-level language modeling. While this is a challenging task, the conclusions would be substantially stronger if they were validated in another domain where temporal hierarchies are known to exist, such as speech recognition or music modeling. As it stands, it is difficult to know if the observed phenomena are specific to language modeling or a more general property of DRNNs.

3.  **Efficiency of Training:** The experiments use BPTT over very long sequences (250 steps). This is computationally expensive and may be unnecessary for achieving good BPC scores, even if it is required for the parenthesis-balancing feat. Much prior work on RNN language models (e.g., by Mikolov et al.) has shown great success with truncated BPTT over much shorter horizons (e.g., 20-30 steps). It would be very useful to include a comparison with truncated BPTT to see how much performance (in BPC) is lost in exchange for a significant gain in computational efficiency. This would provide a more complete picture of the trade-offs and strengthen the paper's practical implications. It is plausible that truncated BPTT could match the reported entropies at a fraction of the computational cost.

**Summary of Evaluation:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The experiments are well-designed and support the main claims. The analysis is thorough. A comparison to a more efficient training baseline (truncated BPTT) would make the evaluation more complete.
*   **Clarity:** High. The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Originality:** The architectural contribution is low, as stacked RNNs have been explored before. The originality lies in the detailed analysis of the learned representations and the demonstration of what can be achieved with simple SGD on a complex task.
*   **Significance:** High. This work provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of a simple and intuitive principle (making RNNs deeper) and offers valuable insights into how these models work. The finding that SGD/BPTT is sufficient for training these models is of significant practical importance.

In summary, this is a strong empirical paper that makes a valuable contribution to our understanding of recurrent neural networks. The analysis of the learned temporal hierarchies is insightful, and the performance achieved is impressive. The primary weaknesses are the limited scope of the experiments (a single task) and a need for clearer positioning with respect to prior work. Despite these points, the paper's strengths are considerable, and it represents a solid contribution to the field.
