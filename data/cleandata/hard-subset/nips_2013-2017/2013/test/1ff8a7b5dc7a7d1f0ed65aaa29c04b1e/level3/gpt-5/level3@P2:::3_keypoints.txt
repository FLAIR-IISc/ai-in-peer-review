- Paper studies stacked recurrent nets (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) for character-level Wikipedia modeling and finds SGD-trained DRNNs reach competitive SOTA among comparable RNNs, with analyses of emergent time scales (typo perturbations, context-switch, parentheses).

- Connects to prior stacked/multi-timescale RNN work and to optimization (Hessian-free, Martens/Sutskever) and compression/language-modeling baselines.

- Strength: simple recurrent-stacked architecture trained with plain SGD achieves strong empirical results, showing depth improves character modeling.

- Strength: thoughtful analyses probing temporal hierarchies (perturbations, context-switch BPC, parentheses study) and persuasive text-generation examples.

- Strength: practical training insights (layer-wise supervised pretraining for DRNN-1O; gradient normalization) and clear DRNN-1O vs DRNN-AO comparison.

- Weakness: gradient normalization departs from standard clipping; paper must ablate normalization vs clipping and report effects on stability, convergence speed, and final BPC, and cite Pascanu et al. (ICML 2013).

- Weakness: unclear operational meaning of “avoiding bifurcations”; diagnostics (gradient norms, hidden-state stats) needed to show normalization is responsible.

- Weakness: missing depth vs width controls — need fixed-parameter sweeps varying layers (1,2,3,5) and widths, plus deep-transition (MLP-between-recurrence) baselines to isolate hierarchical vs capacity effects.

- Weakness: need experiments moving units between layers (e.g., more capacity in first layer) to test whether higher layers benefit from upstream processing or just capacity allocation.

- Weakness: fairness concerns — report exact parameter counts (including output heads) for each model and confirm matching tolerances; report results for unit-matched models.

- Weakness: compare or discuss differences with Mikolov et al.’s RNN/compression results and clarify dataset/evaluation differences (alphabet size, splits).

- Weakness: layer-ablation by zeroing outputs is confounded; recommend retraining without output heads, linear probes, leave-one-layer-out with shallow retraining, and representational analyses (CCA/SVCCA).

- Weakness: DRNN-1O relies on layer-wise supervised pretraining — report end-to-end-from-scratch results and quantify benefit; report variance across seeds and training-time/computation vs HF-MRNNs.

- Quality: technically sound with careful experiments; main concerns are missing optimization ablations, depth/width controls, and stronger baselines to isolate architectural claims.

- Clarity: well written but should define DRNN-AO/DRNN-1O in main text, state alphabet/test-split explicitly when comparing BPCs, and include a concise parameter-count table.

- Originality: stacking recurrent layers is not novel, but clean formulation, SGD success, and time-scale analyses provide useful empirical and practical contributions.

- Significance: demonstrates that simple SGD-trained DRNNs can be competitive and exhibit temporal hierarchies; with requested controls the work would be impactful for practitioners.

- Recommended experiments: ablate gradient normalization vs clipping (cite Pascanu et al.); depth/width sweep under fixed budgets including deep-transition and wider shallow baselines; stronger layer-output ablations/retraining; report seed variance and exact parameter counts; compare/discuss Mikolov et al. if possible.

- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical results, convincing time-scale analyses, relevance and clarity.

- Cons against acceptance: missing critical baselines (depth vs width, deep-transition), insufficient justification/ablation of gradient normalization, and potentially confounded interpretability claims.

- Overall: promising and largely convincing paper that needs the above additional experiments and clarifications to be a solid NIPS contribution.
