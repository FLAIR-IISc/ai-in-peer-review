- Main contribution: shows deeper stacked RNNs with comparable parameter counts obtain state-of-the-art character-level language modeling performance.
- Analysis finding: deeper layers encode longer-range dependencies and exhibit distinct time scales.
- Training insight: plain SGD/BPTT can achieve competitive results, challenging the need for more complex optimizers like Hessian-free.
- Empirical evidence: strong performance improvements with depth and illustrative examples (text generation, parenthesis balancing) demonstrating learned long-range structure.
- Novelty concern: the claim of introducing a new "deep RNN" architecture is inaccurate—similar stacked RNNs appeared in prior work (e.g., Graves 2013).
- Task-specificity concern: results are shown only on character-level language modeling and may not generalize to other time-series domains (e.g., speech).
- Computational cost: training is expensive using full BPTT; efficiency is a practical issue.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Revision 1: reframe contribution as analysis/performance study rather than introducing a novel architecture and cite prior stacked-RNN work more explicitly.
- Revision 2: expand discussion of limitations and outline future work to validate findings on other tasks/datasets.
- Revision 3: explore truncated BPTT (e.g., ~30 steps) as a potential cost-saving alternative, noting tradeoffs for extremely long-range dependencies.
