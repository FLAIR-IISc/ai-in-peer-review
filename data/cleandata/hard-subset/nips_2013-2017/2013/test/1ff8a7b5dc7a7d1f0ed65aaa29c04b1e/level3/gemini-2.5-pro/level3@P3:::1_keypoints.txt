- Proposes Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (stacked RNN layers) to explicitly learn hierarchical temporal features, with higher layers capturing longer-term dependencies.
- Contrasts DRNNs with standard RNNs that are shallow per time step, arguing spatial depth enables temporal hierarchy.
- Introduces two variants: DRNN-AO (outputs from all layers) and DRNN-1O (output from top layer) with layer-by-layer supervised pretraining.
- Evaluated on a large-scale character-level Wikipedia dataset previously used by Sutskever et al. (2011).
- Shows large DRNNs (~5M parameters) trained with simple SGD match state-of-the-art RNN performance achieved earlier with Hessian-free optimization.
- Provides thorough, well-designed internal analyses (typo-perturbation and parenthesis experiments) demonstrating layer-wise differences in time-scale and capture of long-range dependencies.
- Demonstrates practical training methodology—layer-wise pretraining helps mitigate vanishing gradients and improves reproducibility via clear experimental details.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and supported by informative figures.
- Main limitation: evaluation on a single, non-standard dataset limits generality and comparability.
- Comparison is narrowly focused on RNNs, lacking benchmarks against other hierarchical temporal models (e.g., Hierarchical HMMs).
- Performance gains over prior work are marginal, making the contribution stronger in analysis and training insight than in definitive performance improvement.
- Suggestion: visualize the untrained DRNN baseline to better contextualize learned temporal hierarchies.
- Overall: high-quality, analysis-focused paper that advances understanding of deep recurrent architectures and is suitable for NIPS.
