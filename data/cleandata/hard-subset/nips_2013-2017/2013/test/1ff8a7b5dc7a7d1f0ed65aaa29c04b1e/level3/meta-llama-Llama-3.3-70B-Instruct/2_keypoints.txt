- Novel approach: combines machine vision systems with Bayesian generalization to learn visual concepts from few positive examples.  
- Method: uses probabilistic predictions from visual classifiers as input to a Bayesian model to determine abstraction level.  
- Empirical contribution: collected and released a large-scale dataset and demonstrated the system outperforms several baselines.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written, clearly presented, and logically organized.  
- Related work: thorough review in vision and cognitive science, with clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Analysis: provides thorough experimental analysis, including qualitative per-level comparison to human performance.  
- Limitation: approach depends on pre-trained image classifiers, so performance may suffer if classifiers are unavailable or inaccurate.  
- Generalizability concern: the dataset may not represent all visual concepts, potentially limiting broader applicability.  
- Additional validation: paper could benefit from more analyses or experiments to further demonstrate robustness.  
- Evaluation: technically sound with well-supported claims; meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and significance.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strengths that outweigh the limitations.
