This paper studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) that stack recurrent layers to induce a temporal hierarchy, trained with SGD and a layer-by-layer supervised pre-training scheme (each layer temporarily connects to the output for next-character prediction). On character-level language modeling for the Hinton/Sutskever Wikipedia corpus, the authors report competitive bits-per-character relative to prior RNN work (e.g., multiplicative RNN with Hessian-free optimization), and provide detailed analyses showing that higher layers capture longer time scales and that phenomena like parentheses matching behave as pseudo-stable attractor-like states. The work relates to hierarchical RNNs (El Hihi & Bengio), stacked LSTMs (e.g., Graves et al.), and offers a complementary perspective to second-order training (Martens & Sutskever). It also touches the broader literature on hierarchical sequence modeling (e.g., hierarchical HMMs; Fine, Singer & Tishby, 1998), though empirical comparisons are limited.

Quality: The methodology is sound and carefully executed. Training details (architectures, update schedule, clipping/normalization) are described clearly, and analyses (perturbation/typo tests, context switches, layer ablations, generative probing) substantiate claims about emergent time scales and long-term dependencies. However, conclusions about state-of-the-art performance are weakened by evaluation on a single, not widely used corpus, limited hyperparameter exploration (only three model instantiations due to 10-day runs), and comparisons restricted to RNN variants; hierarchical HMMs and non-RNN neural baselines are absent.

Clarity: The paper is very well written with clear figures and experimental descriptions. It is reproducible in spirit, with most key hyperparameters and training choices specified.

Originality: While stacking RNNs is not new, the supervised layer-wise pre-training protocol and the depth of the behavioral analyses are novel and valuable. The work clearly differentiates itself from earlier results focused primarily on accuracy.

Significance: The strongest contribution is the analysis illuminating how depth induces temporal hierarchies and supports long-range structure (e.g., parentheses). The empirical gains, while promising, are not yet conclusive for broad impact due to limited benchmarking.

Pros for acceptance:
- Insightful, carefully executed analyses of DRNN dynamics and time scales
- Clear exposition and transparency of training procedure
- Practical supervised pre-training protocol for deep RNNs

Cons for acceptance:
- Single, non-standard dataset; limited generality
- Narrow baselines (no hierarchical HMMs or strong n-gram/compression baselines aligned to the same corpus)
- Heavy compute with few runs; limited ablations (depth vs width, pre-training vs none)

Suggestions: Add results on standard corpora (text8/enwik8, PTB), include hierarchical HMM and comparable compression-model baselines on the same data, and ablate the pre-training scheme. Overall: weak accept for the analytical insights into deep recurrent modeling.
