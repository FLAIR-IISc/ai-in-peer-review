- Paper studies deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) for character-level language modeling with a hierarchical DNN+RNN architecture.  
- Proposed architecture aims to capture temporal hierarchies in time series data.  
- Main contribution 1: DRNNs achieve state-of-the-art performance on a Wikipedia text corpus, outperforming standard RNNs with equal parameter counts.  
- Main contribution 2: Analysis shows emergent time scales—deeper layers encode longer-range dependencies.  
- Main contribution 3: Demonstrates plain BPTT can train character-level RNNs to balance parentheses, challenging the need for Hessian-free optimization.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-explained analysis of the DRNN architecture and components.  
- Strength: Detailed training setup and experimental results that support the claims.  
- Strength: Insightful experiments illuminating DRNN behavior and long-term dependency modeling.  
- Weakness: Claims novelty for DRNNs are overstated given prior similar models (e.g., Graves 2013).  
- Weakness: Emphasis on analysis over a truly new architecture may limit the paper's impact.  
- Weakness: Results may be task-specific and their generalization to other domains (e.g., speech recognition) is unclear.  
- Quality: Technically sound, claims well-supported, and paper is clearly written and organized.  
- Originality: Builds on prior work but presents a novel hierarchical combination of DNNs and RNNs and a unique analytical perspective.  
- Significance: Demonstrates DRNNs' potential for modeling complex temporal hierarchies, relevant to NLP, speech, and time-series research.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address novelty claims and clarify generality of results.
