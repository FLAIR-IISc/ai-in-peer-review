- Introduces deep recurrent neural networks (DRNNs) with a hierarchical RNN-per-layer architecture for character-level language modeling.  
- Argues DRNNs capture temporal hierarchies and different time scales in data.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains architecture and training.  
- Provides thorough empirical analysis on a Wikipedia corpus and shows emergent layer time scales.  
- Demonstrates DRNNs better capture long-term dependencies (e.g., modeling parentheses as attractor-like states).  
- Strength: DRNNs show significant improvement over common RNNs on long-range structure.  
- Weakness: No clear mechanistic explanation of why DRNNs outperform common RNNs.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are unfair because DRNNs use many more parameters than baseline RNNs.  
- Weakness: Lack of detailed analysis of how hierarchical architecture produces improved performance.  
- Unclear how layers interact and how information is propagated between layers.  
- Originality: Builds on hierarchical RNN work but offers a novel DRNN design and training perspective.  
- Significance: Shows DRNNs can learn temporal hierarchies and long-term dependencies in real-world text.  
- Recommendation: Provide explanation for DRNN advantages, same-parameter baseline comparisons, and deeper analysis of layer interactions.  
- Suggestion: Add more experiments on other real-world tasks to strengthen claims.
