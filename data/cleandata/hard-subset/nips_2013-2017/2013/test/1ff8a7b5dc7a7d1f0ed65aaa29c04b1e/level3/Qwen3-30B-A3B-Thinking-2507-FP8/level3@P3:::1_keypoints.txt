- Introduces Deep Recurrent Neural Networks (DRNNs): stacked recurrent layers where each layer receives the previous layer's hidden state.  
- Main contributions: novel hierarchical RNN architecture, layer-by-layer supervised pre-training (next-letter prediction), and detailed analysis of temporal hierarchies.  
- Claims DRNNs create different time scales across layers, addressing a limitation of standard single-timescale RNNs.  
- Builds on prior hierarchical RNN work ([5,8]) but offers more thorough temporal-property analysis.  
- Trains large DRNNs (~4.9M parameters) on Wikipedia character-level modeling, achieving competitive state-of-the-art results for RNNs using simple SGD.  
- Strongest contribution: detailed empirical analysis showing higher layers capture long-term dependencies and layer-specific time scales.  
- Demonstrates attractor-like memory for parentheses in top layers and improved parentheses-closing performance (12% failure vs. 28% for standard RNNs).  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and technically reproducible with clear figures and documented training methodology.  
- Limitations: evaluation uses a single, non-standard Wikipedia dataset, weakening generalizability.  
- Comparisons are limited to RNN-related models and omit relevant baselines like hierarchical HMMs.  
- Performance gains over prior work are modest and possibly due to network size rather than architectural advantages (e.g., 1.50 BPC vs. 1.49 HF MRNN).  
- Overall quality: technically sound with solid experiments and theoretical grounding.  
- Originality: novel combination of hierarchical deep learning and time-series modeling with significant analytical contributions.  
- Significance: analysis of temporal hierarchies and attractor-like states is highly valuable, but limited experimental validation reduces immediate impact.  
- Recommendation balance: strong analytical insights favor acceptance, while limited evaluation and modest gains argue against, overall leaning toward acceptance.
