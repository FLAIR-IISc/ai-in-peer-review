- Paper studies stacked recurrent networks (DRNN-1O and DRNN-AO) for character-level language modeling and compares depth under a fixed parameter budget (~4.9M).  
- Main empirical finding: deeper stacked RNNs outperform single-layer RNNs in bits-per-character on Wikipedia text when parameters are matched.  
- Analysis shows higher layers encode longer-range information and deeper layers develop longer time scales (layer-wise ablations and perturbation tests support this).  
- Demonstrates that plain SGD/BPTT (with gradient normalization) can learn long-range structures (e.g., balanced parentheses) without HF or special architectures.  
- Architectural novelty is limited—stacked recurrent models predate this work (e.g., Graves 2013, El Hihi & Bengio); paper’s contribution is empirical/analytic rather than a new architecture.  
- Strength: clear, parameter-controlled empirical result that depth helps under a fixed budget.  
- Strength: careful time-scale analyses and ablations convincingly identify layer roles.  
- Strength: practical takeaway that standard BPTT can capture long dependencies.  
- Weakness: evaluation on a single task/dataset limits generality; results may be task-specific.  
- Weakness: some wording overstates novelty; should position as analysis of stacked RNNs, not introduction of DRNN.  
- Weakness: heavy training cost (end-to-end BPTT ~200 steps, ~10 days/GPU) limits experiments, seeds, and ablations.  
- Weakness: missing stronger baselines—particularly (stacked) LSTMs with truncated BPTT—and too few random seeds/error bars to assess variance.  
- Weakness: parentheses experiment may be sensitive to BPTT horizon and its contribution to cross-entropy is unclear.  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully executed, but experimental choices (long BPTT, few runs) limit breadth of evidence.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; suggest explicitly stating BPTT sequence lengths and per-layer parameter counts and clarifying novelty claims.  
- Originality: limited architectural novelty; original value lies in systematic, parameter-matched analysis on character modeling and showing SGD/BPTT suffices for long-range effects.  
- Significance: moderate—useful empirical insight for practitioners but would be stronger with additional tasks and stronger baselines.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate truncated BPTT (e.g., 30 steps) and report trade-offs, add (stacked) LSTM baselines with comparable budgets, test at least one non-text task, run multiple random initializations, and better acknowledge prior stacked-RNN work.  
- Recommendation: leaning accept if positioned as an analysis paper; adding truncated BPTT comparisons and another task would substantially strengthen the submission.
