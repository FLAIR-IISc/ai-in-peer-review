- Addresses the flawed practice of treating entire datasets as single domains and proposes automatic discovery of latent domains.  
- Introduces two axioms for domain discovery: maximum distinctiveness (domains differ maximally) and maximum learnability (domains support strong classifiers).  
- Proposes a nonparametric, kernel-based method and a domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) procedure to determine the optimal number of domains K.  
- Validated on object and action recognition datasets (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam, IXMAS) showing consistent accuracy improvements over dataset-based adaptation and prior domain discovery.  
- Novelty: frames domain discovery as distinct from clustering and uses nonparametric methods instead of parametric Gaussian mixtures.  
- Significant practical extension: conditional reshaping of test sets to align with training domains, improving adaptation when test data are heterogeneous.  
- Strength: conceptual clarity—axioms are well-motivated and the critique of dataset=domain is compelling.  
- Strength: empirical rigor—reported 3–7% gains for training-side discovery and >5% gains from conditional test reshaping; DWCV is practical.  
- Weakness: optimization relaxation lacks justification and no analysis of solution fidelity or gap to integer optimum is provided.  
- Weakness: outdated image representation (800-bin BoW with SURF); stronger descriptors (spatial pyramid/Fisher Vectors) should be used or evaluated.  
- Weakness: Section 4.3’s conditional reshaping is poorly explained and needs a clear example distinguishing it from standard adaptation.  
- Weakness: DWCV uses learnability only to select K but does not integrate distinctiveness and learnability into a unified partitioning objective.  
- Recommendations: provide gap analysis for relaxation, evaluate or acknowledge stronger descriptors, clarify conditional reshaping and its requirements, and add visualizations of discovered domains.  
- Overall assessment: novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions.
