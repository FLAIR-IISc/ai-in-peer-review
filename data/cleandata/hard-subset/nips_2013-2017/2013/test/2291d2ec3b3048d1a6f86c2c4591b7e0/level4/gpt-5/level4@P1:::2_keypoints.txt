- Paper proposes discovering latent visual domains across datasets by enforcing maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Distinctiveness is measured nonparametrically via a kernel two-sample criterion with a continuous relaxation for domain assignments.
- Learnability is used via domain-wise cross-validation to select the number of domains K.
- Method can reshape heterogeneous test sets conditionally to match discovered training domains.
- Experiments on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and IXMAS show gains over treating datasets as domains and over Hoffman et al. (ECCV 2012).
- Method can be combined with multi-source adaptation techniques (e.g., geodesic flow kernel).
- Strong motivation to decouple datasets from domains in vision tasks.
- Nonparametric distinctiveness avoids brittle mixture assumptions and suits complex visual factors.
- Empirical results consistently improve over baselines and prior latent-domain discovery.
- Test-set conditional reshaping is a compelling and practically relevant idea.
- Optimization relaxation in Section 2 is not convincingly tied to the original discrete objective; relation to (2)+(3) is unclear.
- The two criteria are not integrated: learnability only used to pick K, not within the partitioning objective.
- Section 4.3 lacks clarity and should explicitly state the assumption that all test data are available simultaneously.
- Image representation (single bag-of-words on sparse SURF, 800-word codebook) is outdated and weak; histogram similarity metric is unspecified.
- Relaxation and constraints need more rigorous treatment and guarantees relative to the integer program; sensitivity to label-prior and δ is unclear.
- Paper is generally well written but would benefit from more figures showing discovered domains and a schematic of the reshaping pipeline.
- The nonparametric approach is original compared to Gaussian-mixture latent-domain methods and well motivated.
- Empirical gains indicate significance for adaptation performance, especially for heterogeneous targets.
- Experimental suggestions: clarify reshaping optimization and batch assumption; upgrade features to dense SURF + spatial pyramid, use histogram-appropriate similarity (chi-squared/intersection), or consider Fisher Vectors.
- Pros for acceptance: novel formulation, sound nonparametric distinctiveness, and consistent empirical improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: weak connection between relaxation and original objective, lack of joint learnability objective, and outdated/unspecified feature choices.
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to strengthen optimization analysis, integrate learnability into the objective, clarify test reshaping, and improve visual representation.
