- Proposes a convex framework to partition data into K latent domains by maximizing pairwise mean differences in RKHS and selecting K via average within-domain learnability.  
- Constraining class distributions to be similar within each domain avoids class-dominated clusters but may be unrealistic for tasks where within-class distributions vary (e.g., different poses of animals).  
- Latent domain discovery itself is not novel, but enforcing class-label distribution control within a relaxed convex formulation is a new contribution.  
- Using per-domain classification quality to determine the number of latent domains is a useful idea that ensures sufficient class samples per domain.  
- Terminology is unclear: dataset, domain, and latent domain are not explicitly defined and are easily confused, especially in section 4.2.  
- Section 4.2 is confusing because Si is referred to inconsistently as both datasets and source domains, and maxk r(U_k,B) used in eq.7 is not defined.  
- Experimental results show reasonable improvements over baselines, but the choice of source/target datasets is not justified.  
- Suggests alternative evaluation (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out adaptation) as a more reasonable benchmark; qualitative visuals are helpful.  
- Calling the problem “unsupervised” is misleading since the method uses semantic class labels.  
- Overall, the technical contribution is notable but requires clearer definitions and stronger experimental justification.
