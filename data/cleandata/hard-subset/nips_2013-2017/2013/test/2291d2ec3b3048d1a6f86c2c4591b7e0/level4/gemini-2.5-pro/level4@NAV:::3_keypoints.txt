- Proposes a convex framework to partition data into K latent domains such that each domain has similar semantic class distribution while being maximally distinct from others.  
- Distinctiveness is enforced by maximizing pairwise mean differences between subsets in an RKHS.  
- Automatically selects the optimal number of domains K by maximizing average "learnability" (how well a classifier can be trained on each latent domain).  
- Core assumption that class distributions should be similar across latent domains may be too restrictive and unrealistic for some tasks (e.g., pose-dependent class frequency differences in animals).  
- Main novelty is controlling class label distributions within a (relaxed) convex framework to guide latent domain discovery.  
- Positive point: choosing K by assessing per-domain classification quality ensures discovered domains are meaningful and have sufficient per-class samples.  
- Request for clearer definitions: distinction between "dataset," "domain," and "latent domain" is confusing and needs precise definitions and examples.  
- Section 4.2 needs clarification (uses "dataset" and "source domain" interchangeably) and Equation 7 references `maxk r(U_k,B)` without prior definition.  
- Experimental validation is adequate with reasonable improvements over baselines, and qualitative results provide helpful visual insight.  
- Rationale for dataset/source-target selection is unclear; recommends more standard protocols (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out) for stronger validation.  
- Labeling the problem as "unsupervised" is misleading because the method relies on semantic class labels.  
- Overall: technically notable contribution, but impact would increase with clearer conceptual definitions and stronger experimental justification.
