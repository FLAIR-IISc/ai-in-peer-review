- Introduces automatic partitioning of images across datasets into domains guided by feature-space separability and within-domain discriminative learnability to capture underlying factors rather than dataset boundaries.  
- Novel contribution: little prior work on automatic domain definition; promising for improving classifiers on the original task.  
- Major concern: optimization in Section 2 is unclear and may not closely solve the original (2)+(3) problem formulation.  
- Methodological issue: the two guiding criteria are not well integrated—learnability is used only to select the number of domains while separability drives the partitioning; a joint objective is preferable.  
- Overall recommendation: despite issues, the paper is a useful exploratory step and should be accepted.  
- Writing quality: well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify key concepts.  
- Experimental strength: shows significant gains using discovered domains over treating datasets as domains and over a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Experimental concern: the “reshaping” that includes the test set is not clearly presented and effectively assumes an imputation setting (all test data available), which should be stated.  
- Representation weakness: image descriptor is outdated and weak (single BoW over sparse SURF, 800 codebook); recommend spatial pyramid on dense SURF and proper histogram similarity (χ² or intersection), or Fisher Vectors.  
- Suggested references for improved descriptors: Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronin ECCV 2010.  
- Overall: interesting, novel ideas with good results but could be strengthened, especially by improving image representation and better integrating the two criteria.
