- Proposes a novel domain adaptation method that automatically partitions images into domains by maximizing feature-space separability and ensuring each domain supports an effective classifier.  
- Addresses the underexplored problem of automatic domain definition and offers a promising approach to discover visually meaningful domains.  
- The proposed optimization (Sec.2) needs further scrutiny for fidelity to the original formulations (Eqs.2 & 3).  
- Limitation: the maximal learnability criterion is used post-hoc to validate domain count rather than being integrated into a unified partitioning objective.  
- Paper is well-written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments show clear improvement over dataset-as-domain baselines and a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- The use of the test set during “reshaping” is interesting but needs clarification that it assumes an imputation setting where all test data are available simultaneously.  
- Image representation is outdated (sparse SURF bag-of-words with 800 codebook); recommend spatial pyramid + dense SURF, appropriate histogram similarity (chi-squared or intersection kernel), and exploring Fisher Vectors (refs suggested).  
- Overall conclusion: novel and interesting work with promising results; warrants acceptance pending suggested refinements.
