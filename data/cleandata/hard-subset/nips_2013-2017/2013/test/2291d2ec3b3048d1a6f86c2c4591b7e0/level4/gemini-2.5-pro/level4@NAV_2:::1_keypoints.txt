- Problem: paper argues whole datasets should not be assumed to be single "domains" and proposes discovering latent, coherent domains automatically.
- Core idea: optimize for "maximum distinctiveness" (domains have maximally different feature distributions) and "maximum learnability" (each domain is large/consistent enough to train a strong classifier).
- Method: use MMD (kernel-based) to measure pairwise domain differences and partition data to maximize sum of differences.
- Optimization: formulated as an integer program with label-prior constraints, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program.
- Model selection: determine number of latent domains K via a proposed Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) procedure with an upper bound K_max.
- Experiments: validated on object recognition and human activity recognition; discovered domains improve adaptation and outperform prior latent-domain method [20].
- Strength — originality: tackles the overlooked question of what constitutes a domain with a novel, principled formulation.
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, with clear motivation, technical exposition, and experimental setup.
- Strength — significance: empirical gains over baselines and prior work suggest practical value for multi-source adaptation.
- Weakness — motivation: claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains lacks empirical evidence or illustrative experiment.
- Weakness — distinctiveness criterion: unclear why maximum distinctiveness alone would avoid creating per-category domains; needs further explanation.
- Weakness — label prior constraint (LPC): forcing per-domain class priors to match the global prior is a strong, potentially unrealistic assumption and needs justification or ablation.
- Weakness — baselines: insufficient comparisons to simple partitioning baselines (e.g., k-means on features or random partitioning) to isolate the benefit of the optimization.
- Question 1: clarify whether "distribution" refers to marginal P(x) or joint P(x,y).
- Question 2: explain connection between integer-program constraints (Eq.3) and relaxed constraints (Eq.5), which appear different.
- Question 3: provide formal justification or proof for the DWCV upper bound K_max.
- Question 4: give reproducibility details for the geodesic flow kernel implementation and parameter settings.
- Request: include qualitative visualizations or failure-case examples showing why the proposed domains improve over baselines.
- Minor: probable typo in Eq.(1) — M'_k should likely be M_k'.
- Suggestion: cite and discuss Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) on dataset bias to contextualize the work.
