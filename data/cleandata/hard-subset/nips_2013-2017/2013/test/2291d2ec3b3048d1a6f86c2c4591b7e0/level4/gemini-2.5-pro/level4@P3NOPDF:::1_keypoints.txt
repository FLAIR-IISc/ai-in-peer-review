- Paper proposes automatic discovery of latent domains by maximizing inter-domain dissimilarity over feature vectors using a kernel.
- Discovered domains are used in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.
- Objective formulated as integer program with constraints: single-domain assignment per point and matching per-domain class label distribution to global prior.
- Integer program relaxed to a continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints; number of domains chosen via cross-validation.
- Empirical results on two datasets show improved performance over the baseline of Gong et al. [19].
- Paper is well-written and the method is clearly explained.
- Novel contribution: maximizing a dissimilarity metric with a label-prior constraint to find cross-category latent domains.
- Empirical results are promising and outperform a strong prior method.
- Initial concern 1: motivation that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains needed clearer evidence and explanation.
- Initial concern 2: necessity of the label-prior constraint questioned; suggested ablation and comparisons to simple baselines (k-means, unsupervised discovery, random partition).
- Authors’ rebuttal satisfactorily addressed both concerns, leading to an increased score and acceptance recommendation.
- Request to clarify what is meant by "distribution" (feature distribution or something else).
