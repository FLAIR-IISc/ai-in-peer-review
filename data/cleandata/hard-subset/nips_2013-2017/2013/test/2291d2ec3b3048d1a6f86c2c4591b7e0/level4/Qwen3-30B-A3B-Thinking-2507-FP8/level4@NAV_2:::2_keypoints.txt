- Proposes automatically discovering latent domains within datasets instead of treating each dataset as a domain, using maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability to partition images.

- Tackles an important, under-explored problem in domain adaptation and shows promise for improving performance.

- The optimization in Section 2 needs clarification on how well it solves the original objective (Equations 2–3).

- The learnability criterion is mainly used post-hoc to choose the number of domains rather than being integrated into the optimization; simultaneous optimization of both criteria would be stronger.

- Paper is well-written and logically structured but would benefit from more illustrative figures showing the domain discovery process and domain characteristics.

- Experiments show significant recognition gains over using datasets as domains and over Hoffman et al. [20]; reshaping test sets to match discovered domains is an interesting idea.

- Section 4.3 should clarify the imputation setting assumption that all test data are available simultaneously.

- The image representation (bag-of-visual-words with 800 SURF) is outdated; recommend spatial pyramids with dense SURF, X²/intersection kernels, or Fisher Vectors, citing relevant literature (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).

- Despite methodological and experimental shortcomings, the core idea is compelling and the paper makes a valuable contribution worthy of acceptance.
