- Proposes automatic cross-dataset image partitioning into domains using separability in feature space and learnability for the task, rather than using whole datasets as domains.  
- Novel and significant direction for domain discovery with potential to improve classifier performance.  
- Optimization in Section 2 appears loosely connected to the stated objective functions (2)+(3), raising methodological concerns.  
- The two criteria (separability and learnability) feel disjoint; learnability mainly acts as a post-hoc validation for choosing the number of domains.  
- Suggests tighter integration via directly optimizing a combined objective to better align partitioning with both criteria.  
- Paper is generally well-written but would benefit from illustrative figures and visualizations of domain partitions and feature spaces.  
- Experiments show clear advantage over treating datasets as domains and over a recent competing method (Section 4.2).  
- Using the test set during ‘reshaping’ is interesting but poorly explained and assumes simultaneous availability of all test data (an imputation setting) which must be stated.  
- Major limitation: image descriptor is outdated (single bag-of-words with sparse SURF and only 800 codewords).  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unspecified; Euclidean would be inappropriate—recommend Chi-squared distance or intersection kernel.  
- Recommend stronger representations (spatial pyramids on dense SURF, Fisher Vectors) and cite related works (Zhang IJCV 2006, Jurie CVPR 2005, Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronin ECCV 2010).  
- Overall: promising, valuable contribution with encouraging results, but improvements in descriptor choice and tighter methodological integration are recommended.
