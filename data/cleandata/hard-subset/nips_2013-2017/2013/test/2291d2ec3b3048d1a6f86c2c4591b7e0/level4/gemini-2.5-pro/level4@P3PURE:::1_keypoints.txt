- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains by partitioning data to maximize kernel-based discrepancy, formulated as integer programming relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with a class label distribution constraint; evaluated on two datasets and outperforms baseline [19].

- Strength: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Strength: Proposed approach for automatic latent-domain discovery appears novel.

- Strength: Reported performance improvements over a strong baseline are significant.

- Major concern: Motivation and problem formulation are unclear—no evidence that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains and unclear how the model prevents discovered domains from becoming class proxies.

- Major concern: The label prior constraint is not well-justified; its impact is unclear and an ablation without this constraint is needed.

- Major concern (baselines): Additional comparisons required—k-means on appearance features, an unsupervised object discovery method (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV 2005), and random equal-sized partitions.

- Clarify what "maximally different in distribution from each other" refers to (features, labels, or other distributions).

- Clarify why β_mk needs a projection step if it already lies on a simplex.

- Resolve apparent discrepancy between constraints presented at lines 153 and 182.

- Provide justification or proof for the bound stated at line 215.

- Give implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel (custom implementation or public code).

- Provide deeper analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines, including qualitative examples or systematic failure modes of baselines.

- Fix typo in Equation (1): "M'k" likely should be "Mk'".

- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias," CVPR 2011.
