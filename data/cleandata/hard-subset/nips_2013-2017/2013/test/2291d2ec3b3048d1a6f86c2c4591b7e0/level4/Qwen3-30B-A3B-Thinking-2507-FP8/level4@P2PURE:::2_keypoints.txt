- Proposes automatically partitioning the entire image set across datasets into meaningful domains instead of equating domains with datasets.  
- Partitioner guided by two principles: create distinct domains in feature space and ensure each domain supports training a strong classifier for original class labels.  
- Novel and significant contribution toward automatic domain definition and improved domain adaptation.  
- Optimization method (Section 2) is unclear about how closely it solves the original equations (2–3).  
- The two criteria are not well integrated: learnability is used post-hoc to pick the number of domains while partitioning relies solely on separability; an integrated objective is preferable.  
- Despite exploratory limitations, the work advances the field and merits acceptance.  
- Paper is well-written but needs illustrative figures to clarify key concepts.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over treating datasets as domains and over recent domain discovery method [19].  
- The inclusion of the test set in the “reshaping” process is not clearly explained and appears to assume an imputation setting (all test data available), which is not stated.  
- Image descriptor is overly simplistic/outdated (single bag-of-words on sparse SURF, 800 codebook); modern systems need dense features, spatial pyramids, or Fisher Vectors.  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unspecified; Euclidean is inappropriate—should use χ² or intersection kernel.  
- Recommends updating representation and citing relevant prior work (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).
