- Paper proposes automatic latent-domain discovery by maximizing a kernel-based distance between domains, relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with label-distribution constraints, and uses cross-validation to choose the number of domains; shows improved results on two datasets vs baseline [19].  
- Strength: paper is well-written and the automatic domain discovery approach appears novel.  
- Strength: empirical performance gains over a strong baseline are significant.  
- Major concern: motivation and problem formulation are insufficiently justified; the claim that appearance clustering yields per-category domains needs evidence.  
- Major concern: unclear how the objective prevents discovered domains from simply aligning with class categories.  
- Major concern: rationale for the label-prior constraint is unclear; need ablation study without this constraint to quantify its necessity.  
- Major concern: experiments lack comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., k-means on appearance, unsupervised object discovery like Sivic et al. ICCV 2005, or random equal-size partitions).  
- Comment: clarify what distribution is meant by “maximally different in distribution” (features, labels, etc.).  
- Comment: question whether the explicit simplex constraint on β_{mk} is necessary since formulation may already enforce it.  
- Comment: apparent discrepancy between constraints described in different parts of the paper—needs clarification.  
- Comment: provide justification or proof for the bound presented (line 215).  
- Comment: give implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel (re-implementation vs. public code).  
- Comment: include qualitative analysis or examples of failure modes of baselines that the method overcomes.  
- Comment: likely typo in Eq (1): "M'k" should be "Mk'".  
- Suggestion: cite Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias," CVPR 2011.  
- Post-rebuttal: rebuttal addressed motivation and label-prior concerns; reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
