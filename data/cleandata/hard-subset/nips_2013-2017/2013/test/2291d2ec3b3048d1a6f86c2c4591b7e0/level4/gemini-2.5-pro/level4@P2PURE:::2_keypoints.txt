- Introduces a method that automatically partitions images from all datasets into new domains using separability and learnability criteria.  
- Claims this partitioning better uncovers underlying generative factors (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, compression) than using dataset boundaries.  
- Novel and promising direction: relatively little prior work on automatically defining domains.  
- Concern: the proposed optimization (Section 2) is unclear and may not closely approximate the original problem in equations (2) and (3).  
- Concern: the two guiding criteria are poorly integrated—maximal learnability is used only as a wrapper/post-hoc validation while separability drives the partitioning.  
- Suggestion: a more integrated objective that directly optimizes both criteria simultaneously would be preferable.  
- Recommendation: despite methodological shortcomings, the paper is a valuable contribution and should be accepted.  
- Clarity: paper is well written but needs figures to visually illustrate core concepts.  
- Experimental strength: domains produced by the method outperform baselines that use datasets as domains and beat a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Methodological clarity: including the test set in the reshaping process is interesting but not clearly presented—should explicitly state it is a transductive/imputation setting.  
- Major downside: image descriptor is outdated and simplistic (single BoW of sparse SURF, 800-codebook), far weaker than modern representations.  
- Suggestion for experiments: use spatial pyramid BoW on dense SURF, appropriate histogram kernels (Chi-squared/intersection), or Fisher Vectors, and consider cited references (e.g., Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie & Triggs CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).
