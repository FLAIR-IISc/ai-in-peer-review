The paper "Reshaping Visual Datasets for Domain Adaptation" presents a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in a training set, which is then used in a domain adaptation framework to yield improved classification performance on a test set. The authors define a function that measures the difference between two feature vectors over a specified kernel and formulate the problem as an integer programming problem with two constraints: each point is assigned to exactly one domain and the distribution over class labels in each domain must match the input distribution over the entire point set. The problem is relaxed to a continuous optimization over a quadratic cost with linear constraints, and the number of domains is found via cross-validation.

The approach is evaluated on two datasets: static images and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset. A highlight is that improved performance is shown over the domain adaptation approach of Hoffman et al. [20]. The paper is well-written, and the approach appears to be novel.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the motivation for this paper is not entirely clear. The authors argue that simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains, but they do not provide evidence for this claim. Moreover, it is not clear how the model formulation in Section 2 overcomes this issue, i.e., how is not reshaping into per-category domains enforced?

Secondly, the second constraint ("label prior constraint") seems unnecessary, and it would be interesting to see the performance without this constraint. Additionally, a baseline where the data is partitioned using k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery over the appearance vectors should be shown. Furthermore, what is the performance when the dataset is randomly partitioned into equal sets?

Some minor comments include: the phrase "maximally different in distribution from each other" is mentioned throughout the paper, but it would be good to clarify what this means. Distribution over what? Also, the formulation of β_{mk} already lives on a simplex, so it is not clear why this is mentioned again. The constraints on line 182 seem to be different from those formulated before, and it would be good to clarify this. The justification/proof for the bound on line 215 is not provided, and it would be good to include this. The use of the geodesic flow kernel [4] is not clearly explained, and it would be good to provide more details on this. Finally, it would be good to show systematic failures of the baselines that the proposed approach overcomes and provide insights into the differences in performance.

In conclusion, while the paper presents a novel approach to domain adaptation, there are some concerns that need to be addressed before it can be accepted. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to domain adaptation that has shown improved performance over existing methods.
* The approach is well-motivated, and the problem formulation is clear.
* The paper is well-written, and the experiments are thorough.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The motivation for the paper is not entirely clear, and the authors do not provide evidence for their claims.
* The model formulation does not clearly overcome the issue of reshaping into per-category domains.
* The second constraint ("label prior constraint") seems unnecessary, and the performance without this constraint is not shown.
* The paper lacks a clear baseline comparison and does not provide insights into the differences in performance.

Recommendation:

The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field, but it needs some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper can be accepted.
