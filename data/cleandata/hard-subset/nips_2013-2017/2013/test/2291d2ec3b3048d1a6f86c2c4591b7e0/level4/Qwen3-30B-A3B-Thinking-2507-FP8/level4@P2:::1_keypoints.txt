- Paper addresses automatic discovery of latent domains within visual datasets to improve domain adaptation performance.  
- Method defines domains via two properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Domain discovery is formulated as an optimization maximizing pairwise distribution differences with label-prior constraints.  
- Number of domains is chosen by domain-wise cross-validation.  
- Uses nonparametric methods (contrast to parametric mixture models) to better handle visual data complexity.  
- Evaluated on Caltech-256/Amazon/DSLR/Webcam object recognition and IXMAS activity recognition datasets.  
- Empirical results show improvements over treating datasets as single domains and over naive clustering/baselines, with discovered domains matching meaningful visual factors (pose, background).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and technically sound with a well-motivated optimization procedure.  
- Strong empirical validation across multiple datasets supports the method’s effectiveness.  
- Reviewer requests stronger evidence that standard clustering (e.g., K-means) fails and tends to produce per-category domains.  
- Reviewer asks for an ablation showing the effect of removing the label prior constraint (LPC) to justify its necessity.  
- More analysis is needed on why the method outperforms baselines and why the optimal number of domains sometimes equals the number of input datasets.  
- Paper should clarify how it differs in practice from using multiple source domains in standard multi-domain adaptation.  
- Some technical details (e.g., the bound for Kmax in Section 2.2) lack sufficient justification.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution and recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
