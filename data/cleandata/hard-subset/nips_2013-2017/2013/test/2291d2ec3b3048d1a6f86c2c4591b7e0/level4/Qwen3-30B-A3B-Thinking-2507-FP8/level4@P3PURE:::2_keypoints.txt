- Introduces automatic image partitioning into meaningful domains (viewpoint, illumination, etc.) instead of treating datasets as monolithic domains.  
- Partitioning driven by two principles: creating separable feature-space domains and ensuring each domain enables a highly discriminative classifier.  
- This strategy better matches underlying factors of variation than dataset-based domain definitions.  
- The idea is novel, significant, and addresses a largely unexplored problem with potential community impact.  
- Optimization method (Section 2) is unclear about how well it approximates the combined objective (2)+(3).  
- The two driving criteria are not properly integrated: learnability is used only as post-hoc validation while partitioning relies solely on separability.  
- The authors should directly optimize a unified objective combining separability and learnability rather than using learnability as a secondary step.  
- Paper is well-written but omits illustrative figures that would clarify the domain partitioning concept.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over using datasets as domains and over prior domain discovery method [19].  
- Using the test set during partitioning is poorly explained and implies an unstated batch-processing assumption; clarify the evaluation protocol.  
- Image descriptor is outdated (sparse SURF bag-of-words with 800 codebook entries) and weak compared to modern representations.  
- Recommend modernizing descriptors (spatial pyramid, dense SURF, chi-square or intersection kernels, or Fisher Vectors) and cite relevant prior work.  
- Overall assessment: promising and valuable contribution with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions for representation and clarity.
