- Goal: Automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them in domain adaptation to improve test-set classification.
- Method: Measures feature-distribution differences with a kernel and partitions points into domains that maximize this difference.
- Formulation: Integer program with constraints for unique domain membership and matching label distributions per domain, relaxed to a continuous optimization.
- Model selection: Optimal number of domains chosen via cross-validation.
- Evaluation: Experiments on Saenko et al. image datasets and IXMAS action dataset show gains over Gong et al. [19].
- Strength: Clear writing and novel approach addressing domain discovery rather than assuming dataset-domain alignment.
- Strength: Demonstrated performance improvements over a strong baseline and tested on object and action recognition for generalizability.
- Weakness: Initial motivation is weakly articulated and needs strengthening.
- Weakness: Claim that simple clustering yields per-category domains lacks evidence and must be substantiated.
- Weakness: Need clearer explanation of how the proposed formulation avoids per-category clustering pitfalls.
- Weakness: The necessity and impact of the “label prior constraint” are not justified and should be analyzed.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to simpler baselines (e.g., k-means, unsupervised feature learning like Sivic et al. ICCV ’05).
- Detail: Specify which distribution aspects are compared (mean, variance, higher moments) for "maximally different in distribution."
- Detail: Clarify purpose of relaxation since β_mk already lies on a simplex (line 166).
- Detail: Resolve apparent differences between constraints at lines 153 and 182.
- Detail: Provide more explanation or proof for the bound on K_max (line 215).
- Detail: State whether the geodesic flow kernel implementation was reimplemented or used from public code (line 289).
- Detail: Include deeper analysis of performance differences and identify baseline failure cases (lines 313/340).
- Minor: Fix typo in Eq (1): M’_k should be M_k’.
- Related work: Consider including Torralba & Efros [2011] on dataset bias.
- Originality: Novel combination of maximizing distinctiveness and learnability for domain discovery.
- Significance: Problem is important and method could substantially improve robustness and generalization of domain adaptation.
- Quality: Technically sound with clear formulation and optimization; experiments are reasonable but need more baselines.
- Acceptance pros: Novel, clear, technically sound, shows empirical improvements across tasks.
- Acceptance cons: Motivation, the label prior constraint, and missing simpler-baseline comparisons remain concerns.
- Overall: Reviewer slightly leans toward acceptance pending strong rebuttal addressing motivation, label prior, and baseline comparisons.
