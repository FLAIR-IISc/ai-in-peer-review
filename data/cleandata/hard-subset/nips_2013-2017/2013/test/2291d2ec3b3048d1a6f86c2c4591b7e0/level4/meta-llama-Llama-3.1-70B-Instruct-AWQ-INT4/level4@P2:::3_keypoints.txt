- Proposes a convex framework to split datasets into K latent domains with similar class distributions and maximal pairwise distinctiveness in RKHS.  
- Maximizes pairwise mean differences in a chosen kernel to enforce "maximum distinctiveness" between latent domains.  
- Determines K by maximizing average learnability (how well a classifier can be learned) within each latent domain.  
- Uses a non-parametric measure for domain discrepancy and introduces a relaxed convex optimization formulation.  
- Experimental results show reasonable improvements over baselines, indicating adequate validation.  
- Assumes class distributions should be similar within each domain, which may not hold for tasks like pose-based latent domains.  
- Definitions of dataset, domain, and latent domain are unclear and easily confused; concept clarification needed (esp. Section 4.2).  
- Experimental setup lacks details on source/target dataset selection; leave-one-dataset-out adaptation could be more appropriate.  
- Qualitative results are useful but more analysis is needed on identified domains and choosing the optimal K.  
- Original idea of controlling class label distributions within a convex framework is novel, but latent domain discovery itself is not new.  
- Paper could better discuss distinctions from prior latent-domain discovery methods.  
- Significance: addresses an important domain-adaptation problem and may improve adaptation performance, but broader implications/applications need more discussion.  
- Overall technically sound and well-written, but has limitations that require minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity, experimental details, and additional analysis.
