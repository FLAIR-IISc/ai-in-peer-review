- Goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Method: define a kernel-based difference function between feature vectors and partition data to maximize between-domain differences.
- Formulation: integer programming with constraints (one domain per point; domain label distribution matches global label distribution), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints.
- Model selection: number of domains determined by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and shows improvement over domain adaptation method [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and appears novel with appreciated performance gains over [19].
- Main concern (i): unclear motivation — no evidence that appearance clustering reshapes datasets into per-category domains and unclear how the model prevents this.
- Main concern (ii): unclear necessity of the label-prior constraint; missing ablation showing performance without it.
- Missing baselines: no comparisons to k-means clustering, unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV '05), or random equal partitioning.
- Clarify: what does "maximally different in distribution" mean — distribution over what exactly?
- Technical question: does β_{mk} already lie on a simplex as formulated (line 166)?
- Consistency issue: constraints at line 182 appear different from earlier constraints (starting line 153).
- Theoretical question: request justification/proof for the bound stated at line 215.
- Implementation detail: clarify use of the geodesic flow kernel — reimplementation or public code?
- Explain: provide insights on why the proposed approach outperforms baselines and show systematic failure modes of baselines.
- Typo/correction: Eq (1) should read M'k => Mk'.
- Suggested citation: include Torralba & Efros "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011).
- Rebuttal effect: reviewer’s concerns about motivation and label-prior constraint were addressed, reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
- Strengths summary: novel approach, empirical improvements over a baseline, well-written paper.
- Weaknesses summary: unclear motivation, insufficient justification/ablation for label-prior constraint, missing relevant baselines, and some unclear equations/constraints.
