- Proposes a convex relaxation framework to partition data into K latent domains with similar class distributions and maximal RKHS-based inter-domain distinctiveness.
- Selects K by maximizing average per-domain learnability (classifier performance) to ensure useful, discriminative domains.
- Strength: principled convex formulation that enforces label-distribution control to avoid degenerate, class-dominated partitions.
- Strength: uses RKHS mean differences (kernel mean embedding) to encourage domain distinctiveness.
- Strength: practical K-selection criterion tied to downstream classification and per-domain sample sufficiency.
- Strength: empirical improvements over baselines and helpful qualitative visualizations.
- Concern: forcing similar class distributions across domains may be restrictive or bias discovery when nuisance factors are unevenly distributed across classes.
- Concern: manuscript conflates dataset, domain, and latent domain terms, causing conceptual ambiguity and interpretation difficulty.
- Concern: notation gap — max_k r(U_k, B) in Eq. 7 (and other symbols) are undefined and need explanation.
- Concern: supervision claim is misleading — method uses class labels for distribution constraints and learnability, so it is at least semi-/weakly-supervised.
- Concern: experimental dataset choices lack clear rationale and may suffer selection bias; a more systematic protocol is needed.
- Request: provide formal definitions and a running example clarifying dataset vs. domain vs. latent domain and how S_i maps to each.
- Request: fully describe the training/evaluation protocol, including data used for discovery, label usage, per-domain classifier training, and target evaluation.
- Request: define r(U_k, B), explain B, and state how pairwise RKHS mean differences are computed (relation to MMD) and any normalization for domain sizes.
- Request: ablate or relax the class-distribution regularizer and report effects on domain distinctiveness and downstream accuracy.
- Request: analyze sensitivity and stability of K selection (learnability-based) across initializations/folds and report sensitivity to kernel type/hyperparameters.
- Request: justify source–target dataset selection and, if possible, include leave-one-dataset-out evaluations to show generality; clarify representativeness of qualitative examples.
- Request: better position the work relative to prior latent domain discovery methods, highlighting novelty in convex relaxation and label-distribution control.
- Minor: improve consistent terminology, proofread for grammar, and ensure all equation symbols are defined and used consistently.
- Overall: technically solid and promising approach, but needs clearer definitions, fixed notation, calibrated supervision claims, and stronger, more systematic experimental validation.
