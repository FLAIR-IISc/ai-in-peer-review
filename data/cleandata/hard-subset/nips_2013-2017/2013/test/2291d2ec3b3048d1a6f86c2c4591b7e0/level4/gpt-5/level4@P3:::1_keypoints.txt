- Paper proposes nonparametric latent domain discovery using MMD for domain distinctiveness, DWCV for learnability, integer program relaxed to quadratic optimization, plus conditional test-set reshaping.
- Empirical results: improves over dataset-as-domain baselines and Hoffman et al. (ECCV’12) on Caltech-256/Amazon/DSLR/Webcam and IXMAS.
- Strength: principled formulation—MMD for distinctiveness and DWCV to trade off distinctiveness vs. learnability.
- Strength: nonparametric approach avoids misspecified mixture models and is broadly applicable.
- Strength: practical utility—reshaping training and test sets yields tangible gains, especially for cross-view action recognition.
- Strength: thorough experimental comparisons and qualitative analysis of discovered domains.
- Relevance: addresses core NIPS topics (domain adaptation, dataset shift, nonparametric testing).
- Clarify explicitly that “maximally different” means maximizing pairwise squared MMD between empirical distributions and state the role/implications of characteristic kernels.
- Risk of per-category clustering—LPC mitigates this but an ablation without LPC or with looser δ is needed to show robustness.
- Missing baseline results—include K-means over features, random equal-size partitions, and unsupervised object discovery baselines for context.
- Optimization details lacking—specify solver, initialization, runtime, convergence behavior, and sensitivity to kernel bandwidth and δ.
- Constraint reconciliation needed—clarify transition from integer to relaxed formulation and justify bounds (1/M, 1/C) and simplex/βmk handling to avoid degenerate allocations.
- K selection: justify Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}, provide sensitivity/variance plots of A(K) and accuracy vs. K across CV folds.
- Practical details: report GFK implementation/source, hyperparameters, and how reshaping integrates with GFK.
- Complexity: report per-dataset runtimes for training/test reshaping and scaling with M and K.
- Analysis: discuss systematic failure modes of baselines that method overcomes and situations where the method may fail (e.g., differing label priors across latent domains).
- Quality: technically sound and grounded; DWCV is sensible but needs more algorithmic details and ablations to strengthen claims.
- Clarity: generally well-written but fix terminology/notation (e.g., “maximally different”), reconcile constraint notation, and correct minor typos.
- Originality: novel application of MMD-based nonparametric domain discovery and conditional test reshaping compared to prior mixture-based work.
- Significance: meaningful empirical gains and practical impact for domain adaptation and dataset construction/evaluation.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, novel, strong empirical improvements, addresses important underexplored problem, applicable to image/video tasks.
- Cons for acceptance: missing ablations, limited optimization/sensitivity details, and some notational/constraint inconsistencies.
- Minor comments/typos: Eq. (1) M′k → Mk′; include Torralba & Efros citation; state kernel choice and report robustness to bandwidth selection.
- Post-rebuttal: rebuttal clarified LPC and per-category clustering concerns; reviewer now leans toward accept pending requested ablations and added clarifications.
