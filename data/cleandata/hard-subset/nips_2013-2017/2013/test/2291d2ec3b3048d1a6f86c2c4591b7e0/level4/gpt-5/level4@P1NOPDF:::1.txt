This paper aims to automatically discover latent domains in the training set and then use them within a domain adaptation framework to improve test-time classification. The authors define a kernel-based function that measures the discrepancy between two feature vectors and seek a partition of data points into domains that maximizes this discrepancy across pairs of domains. The problem is formulated as an integer program with two constraints: (1) each point is assigned to exactly one domain, and (2) the class-label distribution within each domain matches the global label distribution. The authors relax this to a continuous optimization with a quadratic objective and linear constraints, and select the number of domains via cross-validation. Experiments are conducted on two datasets: the static image dataset of [2] and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset [15]. Notably, the method outperforms the domain adaptation approach of [19].

Positives:
- The paper is clearly written, and the approach appears novel (though I am not a domain adaptation expert).
- The empirical gains over [19] are appreciated.

Negatives:
At this stage I slightly lean toward reject. I have two main concerns that I would like addressed in the rebuttal, which could change my evaluation:

(i) Motivation. On line 77, the paper states that “simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains.” What evidence supports this claim? Moreover, how does the formulation in Section 2 prevent this outcome? Concretely, what enforces that the discovered domains do not collapse into per-category groupings?

(ii) Necessity of the label prior constraint. On line 154, why is the second (“label prior”) constraint required? What is performance without this constraint? A baseline that partitions data using k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al., ICCV 2005) on the appearance features should be included. Also, what happens when the dataset is randomly partitioned into equal-sized subsets?

Additional comments:
- Line 82: “maximally different in distribution from each other” appears repeatedly. Please clarify what distribution is meant (e.g., distribution over features under the kernel, labels, or something else).
- Line 166: As formulated, doesn’t β_{mk} already lie on a simplex?
- Line 182: These constraints seem different from those introduced around line 153. Please reconcile the two formulations.
- Line 215: What is the justification or proof for this bound?
- Line 289: Please provide details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel [4]. Was this reimplemented, or did you use publicly available code?
- Lines 313/340: Please offer insights into why the proposed method outperforms the baselines. It would be helpful to show systematic failure modes of the baselines that your method avoids.
- Eq. (1): Typo: M'k should be Mk'.
- Suggested citation: A. Torralba and A. Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.

Post-rebuttal update:
The rebuttal adequately addressed my concerns about the paper’s motivation and the role of the label prior constraint. I now lean slightly toward accept.
