This paper proposes an approach to automatically discover latent domains in a training set, which is then used in a domain adaptation framework to improve classification performance on a test set. The method defines a function that measures the difference between two feature vectors over a specified kernel. The goal is to partition the data points into domains such that the function is maximized over the set of points across each pair of domains. This problem is formulated as an integer programming problem with two constraints: each point is assigned to exactly one domain, and the distribution over class labels in each domain must match the input distribution over the entire point set. The problem is relaxed to a continuous optimization over a quadratic cost with linear constraints. Finally, the number of domains is found via cross-validation.

The approach is evaluated on two datasets: static images of [2] and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset of [15]. Notably, improved performance is shown over the domain adaptation approach of [19].

The paper has several strengths. It is well-written, and the approach appears to be novel, although I am not an expert in domain adaptation. The performance gains over [19] are also appreciated.

However, I initially leaned towards rejecting the paper due to two main concerns that I would like to see addressed in the rebuttal. Firstly, the motivation for this paper is not entirely clear. On line 77, the paper argues that "simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains." However, I would like to see evidence supporting this claim and an explanation of how the model formulation in Section 2 overcomes this issue. Specifically, how does the formulation prevent reshaping into per-category domains?

Secondly, I am curious about the necessity of the second constraint, the "label prior constraint" (line 154). I would like to see the performance of the model without this constraint and a comparison with baseline methods, such as partitioning the data using k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV '05) over the appearance vectors. Additionally, it would be informative to see the performance when the dataset is randomly partitioned into equal sets.

I also have some additional comments and suggestions for improvement. On line 82, the phrase "maximally different in distribution from each other" is mentioned throughout the paper. It would be helpful to clarify what this means, specifically what distribution is being referred to. On line 166, it appears that β_{mk} already lives on a simplex, so further clarification would be appreciated. On line 182, the constraints seem to differ from those formulated earlier (starting on line 153), so a clearer explanation would be helpful. On line 215, I would like to see the justification or proof for the bound.

Furthermore, I would appreciate more details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel [4]. Specifically, was this a reimplementation or was publicly available source code used? On lines 313 and 340, it would be helpful to provide some insights into the differences in performance between the proposed approach and the baselines. I would like to better understand why the proposed approach performs better and see systematic failures of the baselines that the proposed approach overcomes.

Additionally, there is a typo in Eq (1), where M'k should be Mk'. It may also be relevant to include the citation "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" by A. Torralba and A. Efros (IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, 2011).

After reviewing the rebuttal, which addressed my concerns regarding the paper's motivation and the label prior constraint, I lean slightly towards accepting the paper.
