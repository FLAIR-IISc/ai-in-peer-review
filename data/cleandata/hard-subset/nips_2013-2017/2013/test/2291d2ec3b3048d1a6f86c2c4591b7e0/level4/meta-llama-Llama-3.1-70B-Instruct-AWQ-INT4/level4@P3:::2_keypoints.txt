- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition images across datasets into domains based on feature-space separability and per-domain discriminative learnability.  
- Valuable and original contribution with potential to improve domain adaptation by discovering more meaningful domains than dataset-based grouping.  
- Main optimization (sec.2) and how it approximates the original objective ((2)+(3)) is unclear.  
- The two driving criteria (maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability) are poorly integrated; learnability is used only as a post-hoc validation rather than within the partitioning objective.  
- The paper is well-written but would benefit from figures or visual aids to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments show significant gains using discovered domains over dataset-as-domain baselines and a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Image representation is outdated and weak (single bag-of-words over sparse SURF with 800 codebook entries); recommend dense SURF + spatial pyramids.  
- Unclear which similarity/kernel is used for comparing histograms; Euclidean would be inappropriate, X^2 or intersection kernel preferred.  
- The process of including the test set in the "reshaping" is interesting but not clearly presented; it resembles an imputation setting and needs clarification.  
- With improvements to the optimization, integration of criteria, stronger image features, and clearer presentation, the work could make a larger impact.  
- Pros: novel, promising idea with demonstrated empirical benefits.  
- Cons: unclear optimization and integration of objectives, weak image descriptors, and unclear test-set handling.
