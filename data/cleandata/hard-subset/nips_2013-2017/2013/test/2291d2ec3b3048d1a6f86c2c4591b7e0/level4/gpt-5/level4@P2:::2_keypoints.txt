- Addresses conflating datasets with domains and proposes automatic partitioning into latent domains using maximum distinctiveness (kernel two-sample measures) and maximum learnability (cross-validated accuracy), solved via a nonparametric continuous QP relaxation and K chosen by domain-wise CV.  
- Extends method to “conditional reshaping” of heterogeneous test data to match discovered training domains.  
- Clear motivation: datasets need not equal true underlying domain factors.  
- Principled criteria: distinctiveness measured nonparametrically and learnability via cross-validated accuracy.  
- Nonparametric formulation avoids restrictive mixture-model assumptions of prior latent domain work.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over treating datasets as domains and over a recent Gaussian-mixture latent domain method.  
- Test-set reshaping idea is promising for heterogeneous targets.  
- Optimization fidelity unclear—no analysis/bounds on how the relaxed continuous solution approximates the original combinatorial assignment.  
- Limited integration of criteria: learnability is used only to select K; the partition objective is driven solely by distinctiveness.  
- Conditional reshaping (Sec. 4.3) is tersely described and seems to assume batch access to all test data without discussion.  
- Weak image representation (single BoW of sparse SURF, 800 words) likely undercuts absolute performance and generality; similarity/kernel for histograms is unclear and Euclidean is inappropriate.  
- Paper is generally well written but needs more figures to illustrate principles, discovered domains, and reshaping.  
- Request more details on optimization (solvers, convergence, sensitivity to label-prior δ) and computational complexity.  
- Originality/significance: interesting nonparametric distinctiveness criterion and learnability-based model selection; could influence dataset construction/exploitation if strengthened.  
- Experiments: discovered domains yield significant gains and multi-source strategies approach oracle selection, but Sec. 4.3 needs clarification on test-time setting, matching objective, and supervision used.  
- Recommend updating representations/kernels (spatial pyramids, dense features, χ2 or intersection kernels, Fisher Vectors) and cite relevant prior work.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles important problem, sound idea, empirical gains.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient joint objective for distinctiveness/learnability, limited analysis of relaxation, outdated representation, underexplained conditional reshaping.  
- Recommendation: lean accept contingent on clarifying optimization and conditional reshaping and strengthening experiments with modern representations and appropriate histogram kernels.
