- Introduces a method to automatically partition an image collection into domains, instead of equating domains with source datasets.  
- Partitioning is guided by maximizing domain separability in feature space and ensuring high task learnability within each domain.  
- Aim is to recover partitions that reflect true underlying data-generating factors rather than arbitrary dataset boundaries.  
- Problem is novel and significant; automatic domain definition is underexplored and the approach is promising.  
- Connection between the proposed optimization algorithm and the original objective (Eqs. 2–3) is unclear, impeding assessment of solution quality.  
- The two guiding principles (separability and learnability) are not well integrated; learnability is used only as a post-hoc selector for number of domains.  
- A unified objective that directly incorporates both separability and learnability would be preferable.  
- Paper is well-written but would benefit from figures illustrating core concepts and the partitioning process.  
- Experiments show clear advantage over baselines: treating source datasets as domains and prior domain discovery method [19].  
- Inclusion of the test set in domain reshaping is effectively transductive and should be explicitly stated as such.  
- Experimental evaluation is weakened by outdated image features (sparse SURF BoW with 800-word codebook) that lag current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper omits the similarity measure used for descriptors; histogram-appropriate metrics (chi-squared, intersection kernel) should be used instead of Euclidean.  
- Recommend stronger representations (spatial pyramids on dense SIFT/SURF, Fisher Vectors) and cited references for improving features.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, important, and promising results but needs stronger feature representation and clearer methodological integration.
