- Proposes automatic partitioning of images into domains based on intrinsic aspects (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, compression) rather than treating datasets as domains.  
- Domains are chosen to be separable in feature space and conducive to training discriminative classifiers for the original task.  
- This aspect-based partitioning better aligns with underlying data variations than dataset-based domain definitions.  
- Addresses a significant gap in domain adaptation literature and offers a promising new direction.  
- Optimization method (Section 2) is unclear about how closely it solves the combined objectives (2)+(3).  
- The two criteria—maximal separability and maximal learnability—are not integrated; learnability is used only post-hoc while partitioning relies solely on separability.  
- An integrated objective combining separability and learnability would strengthen the contribution.  
- Paper is well-written but lacks illustrative figures to clarify domain partitioning and experimental setup.  
- Experiments show the proposed domains outperform dataset-based domains and prior domain discovery method [19].  
- The "reshaping" step includes the test set, which is conceptually interesting but poorly explained and implies an imputation setting (all test data available).  
- Major technical weakness: image representation is outdated—single BoW with sparse SURF and 800 codebook entries is simplistic.  
- Authors should use stronger descriptors (spatial pyramid BoW on dense SURF, appropriate histogram similarity like X²/intersection, or Fisher Vectors) and clarify the similarity metric.  
- Despite methodological and representation issues, the conceptual contribution is valuable and the paper merits acceptance pending revisions.
