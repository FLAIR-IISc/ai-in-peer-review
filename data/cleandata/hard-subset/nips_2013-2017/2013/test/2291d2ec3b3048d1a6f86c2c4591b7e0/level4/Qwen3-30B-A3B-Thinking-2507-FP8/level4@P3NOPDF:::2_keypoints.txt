- Proposes automatic partitioning of images into domains based on intrinsic visual aspects (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, compression) rather than dataset boundaries.  
- Core innovation: a partitioning algorithm driven by two principles—maximizing separability in feature space and ensuring domains support high discriminative accuracy.  
- Original/significant: fills a gap in domain adaptation by framing domain discovery as an optimization problem with dual criteria; shows advantages over dataset-based domains and a recent method [19].  
- Technical critique: the optimization is unclear—maximal learnability is used only as a post-hoc validation metric, not integrated into the partitioning objective, weakening conceptual coherence.  
- Recommendation: an integrated formulation where separability and learnability jointly guide partitioning would strengthen the method.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written but would benefit from visualizations (e.g., t-SNE of clusters, aspect distribution plots) to make concepts more accessible.  
- Experiments: results are compelling and consistently outperform baselines; however, the “reshaping” step uses test data and assumes all test samples are available (imputation setting), which must be explicitly stated and clarified.  
- Major limitation: uses an outdated BoW SURF descriptor and unspecified/likely inappropriate similarity measure; should use spatial pyramids/dense features, Fisher Vectors, and proper kernels (χ² or intersection).  
- Overall: conceptually sound and empirically promising; recommend acceptance contingent on refining the optimization, modernizing image representation, and clarifying test-data assumptions.
