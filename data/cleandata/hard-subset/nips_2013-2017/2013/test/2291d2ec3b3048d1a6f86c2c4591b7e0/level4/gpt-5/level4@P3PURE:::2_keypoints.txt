- Proposes a domain adaptation method that automatically partitions images across datasets into domains using separability and within-domain class learnability criteria.
- Novel idea with little prior work on automatic domain definition; promising for discovering domains that improve classifiers for the original task.
- Optimization in Section 2 is unclear; the proposed algorithm may not closely solve the original objective (Eqs. (2)+(3)).
- The two driving criteria are not well integrated: maximal learnability is used only as a post-hoc wrapper to pick the number of domains after separability-based partitioning.
- An integrated single-objective optimization combining separability and learnability would be preferable.
- Paper is generally well written but lacks figures that would clarify key concepts.
- Experiments show discovered domains significantly outperform treating datasets as domains and the recent domain discovery method [19].
- The “reshaping” experiment includes the test set in training-like processing; this imputation-like assumption (all test data available) is not clearly stated.
- Image representation is weak/outdated (single BoW over sparse SURF with 800-word codebook); recommend dense SURF spatial pyramids, chi-squared/intersection similarity, or Fisher Vectors.
- Provide and consider relevant references to improve descriptors and evaluation (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).
- Overall assessment: interesting, novel, and effective work that should be accepted but improved in optimization/integration and image representation.
