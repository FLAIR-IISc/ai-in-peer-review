This paper aims to automatically discover latent domains in the training set and then leverage them within a domain adaptation framework to improve test-time classification. The authors define a discrepancy function between feature vectors using a specified kernel and seek a partition of the data into domains that maximizes this discrepancy across domain pairs. The task is cast as an integer program subject to two constraints: (1) each instance is assigned to exactly one domain, and (2) the label distribution within each domain matches the global label prior. The problem is then relaxed to a continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints, and the number of domains is selected via cross-validation.

Experiments are conducted on two datasets: the static image dataset of [2] and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset of [15]. A notable result is improved performance over the domain adaptation method of [19].

Strengths:
- The paper is clearly written, and to the best of my knowledge the approach is novel (I am not a domain adaptation expert).
- The performance gains over [19] are encouraging.

Weaknesses and main concerns (leaning slightly toward reject pending rebuttal):
(i) Motivation. The claim on line 77 that clustering by appearance tends to produce per-category domains needs support. What empirical or theoretical evidence underpins this assertion? Moreover, how does the proposed formulation in Section 2 avoid this outcome—what mechanism explicitly prevents the domains from collapsing into per-category groupings?

(ii) Necessity of the label-prior constraint. Why is the second constraint (line 154) required? Please report performance without this constraint (an ablation). In addition, include baselines where the data are partitioned by simple k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al., ICCV 2005) on the appearance features. Also provide results when the dataset is randomly split into equal-sized partitions.

Additional comments and questions:
- Line 82: “maximally different in distribution” needs clarification. Distribution over what variables/features, and under which kernel/statistic?
- Line 166: As defined, doesn’t β_{mk} already lie on a simplex?
- Line 182: The constraints shown here seem to differ from those introduced around line 153. Please reconcile the formulations.
- Line 215: Please justify or prove the stated bound.
- Line 289: Provide implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel [4]. Was it reimplemented or taken from public code? Include parameter settings and any deviations.
- Lines 313/340: Offer a deeper analysis of why the proposed method outperforms baselines. Highlight systematic failure modes of the baselines that your approach avoids.
- Eq. (1): Typo—M'k should be Mk'.
- Consider adding: Torralba and Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.

Post-rebuttal: The rebuttal satisfactorily addressed my concerns regarding the paper’s motivation and the necessity of the label-prior constraint. I now lean slightly toward acceptance.
