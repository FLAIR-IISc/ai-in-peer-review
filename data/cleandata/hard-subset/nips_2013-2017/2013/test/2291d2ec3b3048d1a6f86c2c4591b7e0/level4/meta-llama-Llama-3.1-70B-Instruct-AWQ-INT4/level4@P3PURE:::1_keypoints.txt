- Paper goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them in a domain adaptation framework via a kernel-based pairwise feature-difference objective, formulated as an integer program relaxed to a quadratic continuous optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and outperforms the domain adaptation method of [19].
- Strength: well-written, appears novel, and shows empirical performance gains over prior work.
- Major concern 1: Motivation unclear—claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains needs evidence and explanation of how the proposed model prevents this reshaping.
- Major concern 2: Necessity of the label-prior constraint is questioned; reviewer requests results without it and with simple baselines (k-means, unsupervised object discovery like Sivic et al. ICCV ’05, and random equal partitioning).
- Clarify what “maximally different in distribution from each other” refers to (distribution over what features/labels/other statistics).
- Line 166 question: does β_{mk} already lie on a simplex as formulated?
- Line 182 issue: constraints presented here appear different from those earlier (around line 153); request clarification of the discrepancy.
- Line 215: ask for justification or proof for the stated bound.
- Line 289: request details on the geodesic flow kernel implementation—reimplementation or use of public code?
- Lines 313/340: ask for analysis explaining why the proposed method outperforms baselines and demonstration of systematic baseline failures the method overcomes.
- Eq (1) typo: M'k should be Mk'.
- Suggested citation to include: Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias” (CVPR 2011).
- Final assessment: after the authors’ rebuttal addressing motivation and the label-prior constraint, reviewer leans slightly towards acceptance.
