- Task: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Method: defines a kernel-based function measuring feature-vector differences and partitions data to maximize inter-domain differences.
- Formulation: integer programming with constraints (one domain per point; per-domain label distribution matches global prior), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and outperforms domain adaptation method [19].
- Strengths: well-written, appears novel, and shows noteworthy performance gains over [19].
- Main concern 1 (motivation): unclear evidence for the claim that appearance-based clustering produces per-category domains and unclear how the model prevents per-category partitions.
- Main concern 2 (label prior): unclear rationale for the label-prior constraint; reviewer requests ablation without this constraint and comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery, and random partition baselines.
- Clarify: what is meant by "maximally different in distribution" — distribution over features, labels, or something else?
- Clarify: does the definition of β_mk already place it on a simplex?
- Clarify: apparent mismatch between constraint formulations at different manuscript locations (lines ~153 vs ~182).
- Theory: request justification or proof for the bound stated around line 215.
- Implementation: detail whether the geodesic flow kernel was reimplemented or sourced from public code.
- Analysis: request deeper insights into why the method outperforms baselines and examples of baseline failures the method fixes.
