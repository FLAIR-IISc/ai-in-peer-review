- Proposes automated latent domain discovery using a kernel-based difference measure, formulated as an integer program relaxed to a quadratic continuous optimization; domain count chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on static images and IXMAS multi-view action dataset, reporting improvements over domain adaptation method [19].  
- Strength: Well-written and (apparently) novel approach with encouraging performance gains.  
- Initial overall decision: leaned toward rejection pending rebuttal, but open to persuasion.  
- Concern (i): Motivation unclear—claim that simple appearance clustering yields per-category domains lacks supporting evidence; need explanation of how the model prevents per-category domains.  
- Concern (ii): Role and necessity of the label prior constraint unclear—request ablation results without it and comparisons to baseline partitioning methods (k-means, Sivic et al. ICCV'05) and random equal partitioning.  
- Request clarification of the repeated phrase “maximally different in distribution”—define what “distribution” means here.  
- Question whether β_mk is already constrained to lie on a simplex as currently formulated.  
- Note apparent mismatch between constraints presented at lines ~153 and ~182; ask for reconciliation.  
- Ask for justification or proof for the bound stated at line 215.  
- Request details on geodesic flow kernel implementation (reimplementation vs. public code).  
- Ask for deeper analysis explaining why the proposed method outperforms baselines and for systematic failure cases of baselines that the method addresses.  
- Minor correction: Eq (1) typo—M'k should be Mk'.  
- Suggested related work to include: Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.  
- Post-rebuttal update: Rebuttal addressed motivation and label prior concerns; reviewer now slightly leans toward acceptance.
