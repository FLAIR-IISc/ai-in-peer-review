- Proposes a new domain-adaptation technique that automatically partitions images across datasets into domains driven by feature-space separability and per-domain discriminative learnability.
- Novel idea with limited prior work on automatic domain definition; promising for improving classifiers on original tasks.
- Concerns about the optimization in Section 2: unclear how closely it solves the original formulation (equations (2) + (3)).
- The two driving criteria are not well integrated: maximal learnability is used only as a wrapper/post-hoc validation to pick the number of domains.
- A more integrated objective that directly combines separability and learnability would be preferable.
- Paper is well-written but missing illustrative figures to clarify concepts.
- Experiments show significant gains over using datasets-as-domains and over a recent domain-discovery method [19].
- The inclusion of the test set in the reshaping process is interesting but poorly explained and should be explicitly stated as an imputation setting.
- Image representation is outdated (single bag-of-words on sparse SURF, 800 codewords); recommends spatial pyramid + dense SURF and appropriate histogram similarity (χ^2 or intersection), or Fisher Vectors for stronger results.
- Overall: interesting, novel work on an important problem with good results and should be accepted, but needs improvements in optimization clarity, integration of criteria, explanations, and image representation.
