- Paper proposes automatic discovery of latent domains and uses them in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.  
- Method: partition data to maximize a kernel-based pairwise feature-difference function, formulated as integer programming with class-label distribution constraint and relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on two datasets (static images and IXMAS) and shows improved performance over prior domain adaptation method [19].  
- Strength: well-written and appears novel; performance gains over [19] are convincing.  
- Major concern: motivation unclear—claim that appearance clustering yields per-category domains needs empirical evidence.  
- Major concern: unclear how the model formulation in Section 2 prevents reshaping into per-category domains.  
- Questioned necessity of the label prior constraint—request ablation showing performance without it.  
- Request comparisons to simple baselines: k-means on appearance vectors, unsupervised object discovery (Sivic et al. ICCV ’05), and random equal partitioning.  
- Clarify meaning of “maximally different in distribution from each other” (which distribution is referred to).  
- Clarify that β_{mk} appears to lie on a simplex (line 166) and reconcile differing constraint formulations (lines 153 vs. 182).  
- Request justification or proof for the bound mentioned on line 215.  
- Ask for details on geodesic flow kernel usage: reimplementation vs. public code.  
- Request analysis explaining why the proposed method outperforms baselines and examples of baseline failures.  
- Minor: typo in Eq (1) (M'k should be Mk') and suggest adding Torralba & Efros (2011) citation.  
- After rebuttal addressing motivation and label prior concerns, reviewer slightly leans toward accepting the paper.
