- Paper goal: automatically discover latent domains by partitioning training data to maximize a kernel-based distributional dissimilarity, then use domains in domain adaptation.  
- Formulation: integer program with constraints (exclusive domain membership; per-domain label distribution matches overall), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and shows improvement over baseline [19].  
- Strength: well-written, novel approach with encouraging empirical gains over [19].  
- Overall recommendation: reviewer leans toward rejection but open to persuasion via rebuttal.  
- Major concern 1: motivation unclear—need evidence that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains and explanation of how the proposed formulation avoids this.  
- Major concern 2: justification for the label-prior constraint is unclear; requester wants results without it and comparisons to simpler baselines (k-means, unsupervised object discovery, random partition).  
- Clarify what “distribution” refers to when saying domains are “maximally different in distribution.”  
- Mathematical/comments: β_mk variables already lie on a simplex (line 166).  
- Mathematical/comments: constraints at line 182 appear different from those at line 153—clarify consistency.  
- Provide justification/proof for the bound stated at line 215.  
- Implementation detail: clarify geodesic flow kernel implementation—public code used or reimplemented (line 289).  
- Analysis: give deeper insight into why the method outperforms baselines and provide examples of baseline failure modes (lines 313/340).
