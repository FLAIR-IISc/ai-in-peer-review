- Tackles domain adaptation by automatically partitioning image datasets into latent domains instead of equating domains with datasets.  
- Proposes partitioning guided by maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness and ensuring each domain supports a strong discriminative classifier.  
- Novel and significant: focuses on defining what constitutes a domain, an under-explored aspect of domain adaptation.  
- Well-motivated approach that could lead to more meaningful domains and improved classifier performance.  
- Technical formulation is clear, but the optimization method (Sec. 2; eqs. 2 & 3) needs clearer explanation of approximations.  
- The two criteria (distinctiveness and learnability) are applied somewhat sequentially; a unified objective combining both would be preferable.  
- Paper is generally well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to explain domain discovery and domain characteristics.  
- Experiments show clear gains from discovered domains over dataset-as-domain baselines and outperform Hoffman et al. [20].  
- Extension to reshape including the test set is interesting but should explicitly state the imputation/complete-access assumption.  
- Major weakness: outdated image representation (sparse SURF bag-of-words with small codebook) likely limits method performance.  
- Suggests modern representations (spatial pyramids, dense SURF, Fisher Vectors) would strengthen results.  
- Unclear which similarity measure is used for histogram comparisons; Euclidean is questionable—chi-squared or intersection kernels would be more appropriate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel method, strong empirical gains, thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient optimization detail, weak integration of core criteria, outdated image features, unclear test-set reshaping presentation.  
- Overall: valuable contribution with promising results; should be accepted pending revisions addressing representation and optimization clarifications.
