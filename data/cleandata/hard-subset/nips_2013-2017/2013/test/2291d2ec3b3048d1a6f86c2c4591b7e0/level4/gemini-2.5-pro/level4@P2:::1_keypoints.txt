- Paper tackles the flawed assumption that each dataset is a single domain and proposes unsupervised discovery of latent domains within datasets.
- Method optimizes two principles: "maximum distinctiveness" (domains maximally different via RKHS kernel-based measure) and "maximum learnability" (choose K via domain-wise cross-validation).
- Formulates domain discovery as integer programming, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program; includes a Label Prior Constraint (LPC) to preserve class distributions.
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition; shows improved adaptation performance and outperforms Hoffman et al. [20].
- Strength: original and significant problem framing that challenges common domain-adaptation assumptions.
- Strength: technically sound non-parametric kernel approach avoids strong distributional assumptions and provides a practical optimization.
- Strength: paper is generally clear, well-organized, and experiments are described in sufficient detail.
- Strength: experimental validation convincingly shows benefits of reshaping datasets into latent domains.
- Weakness: claim against simple appearance clustering lacks empirical evidence and should be validated with simple baselines (e.g., k-means).
- Weakness: unclear whether LPC is the main mechanism preventing category-based collapse; requires ablation showing performance without LPC.
- Weakness: recommend baseline of random partitioning (with LPC) to isolate TDD objective benefits from mere partitioning.
- Technical clarification needed on whether "maximally different in distribution" refers to P(x) or P(x,y).
- Technical clarification needed on definition and constraints of βmk (relationship to zmk/Mk and simplex constraint).
- Discrepancy between Eq. (5) constraints and the conceptual constraints described elsewhere needs reconciliation.
- Request justification for the chosen practical bound on Kmax in Section 2.2.
- Reproducibility request: state whether geodesic flow kernel [4] used public implementation or was re-implemented.
- Suggest deeper qualitative analysis: show failure cases of baselines and visualizations comparing mis-clustered vs correctly clustered domains.
- Minor: typo in Eq. (1) (M'k likely Mk') and suggest citing Torralba & Efros (2011) on dataset bias.
- Conclusion: paper is a novel, valuable contribution but currently leans toward rejection pending stronger empirical justification and clarification of technical details.
