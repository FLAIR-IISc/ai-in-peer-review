- Introduces a novel framework to discover latent domains in visual datasets to improve domain adaptation.  
- Proposes two axiomatic properties: maximum distinctiveness (domains are distributionally distinct) and maximum learnability (each domain has enough data for strong classifiers).  
- Formulates domain discovery as a nonparametric optimization maximizing pairwise domain differences in RKHS while enforcing balanced class distributions.  
- Determines the optimal number of domains via domain-wise cross-validation to ensure reliable classification in each domain.  
- Addresses the gap of treating entire datasets as single domains, capturing complex distributional variations instead.  
- Contrasts with Hoffman et al. (ECCV 2012): replaces parametric Gaussian mixtures with a more flexible nonparametric approach.  
- Methodologically sound with clear optimization and theoretical justification for the domain distinctiveness measure.  
- Comprehensive experiments across object and action recognition show consistent improvements and outperform Hoffman et al.’s method.  
- Qualitative analysis (Figure 1) provides useful insight into domain-separating factors like pose, background, and color.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, but terminology between “dataset,” “domain,” and “latent domain” is sometimes unclear.  
- Equation 6 contains an undefined term ("max_k r(U_k,B)") that needs clarification.  
- Main limitation: reliance on semantic class labels and balanced class priors, which may not hold when class distributions vary across latent domains.  
- Original contributions include a convex optimization framework for domain discovery and using domain-wise cross-validation for selecting domain count.  
- Overall significance is high with strong empirical validation; recommendation: accept for presentation at NIPS.
