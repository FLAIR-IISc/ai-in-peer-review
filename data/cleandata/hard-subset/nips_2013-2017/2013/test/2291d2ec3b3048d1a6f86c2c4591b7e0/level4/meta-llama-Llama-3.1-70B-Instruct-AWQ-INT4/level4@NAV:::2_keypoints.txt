- Proposes automatic partitioning of images across datasets into domains using separability in feature space and per-domain discriminative learnability.
- Novel and significant idea with limited prior work on automatic domain definition; a valuable contribution.
- Concern: the proposed optimization in Section 2 is not convincing and its relation to the original objective (2)+(3) is unclear.
- Concern: the two criteria (max separability and maximal learnability) are not well integrated; learnability is used post-hoc to choose the number of domains.
- Suggestion: a more integrated objective combining both criteria would be preferable.
- Paper is well-written but lacks illustrative figures to explain concepts.
- Experiments show the discovered domains outperform using datasets as domains and improve over recent domain discovery method [19].
- The "reshaping" that includes the test set is intriguing but poorly explained and resembles an imputation setting not explicitly stated.
- Image representation is outdated (single bag-of-words of sparse SURF, 800 codebook entries) and unsuitable for modern systems.
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unclear; Euclidean is inappropriate—recommend X^2, intersection kernel, spatial pyramids, dense SURF, or Fisher Vectors.
- Overall: interesting novel ideas, good results, warrants acceptance but can be improved in optimization integration, clarity, and image representation.
