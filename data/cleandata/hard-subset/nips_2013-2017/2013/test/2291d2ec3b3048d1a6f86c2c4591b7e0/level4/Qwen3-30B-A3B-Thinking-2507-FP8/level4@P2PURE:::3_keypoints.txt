- Proposes a convex optimization framework to partition data into K "latent domains" with (1) similar semantic class distributions within each subset and (2) maximal distinctiveness between subsets via RKHS mean differences.
- Selects the number of latent domains K by maximizing average learnability (classification quality) within each latent domain.
- Novelty: relaxing class-distribution control into a convex framework for latent-domain discovery.
- Novelty: choosing K based on per-domain classification quality to ensure sufficient samples per class.
- Limitation: enforcing similar class distributions within domains may be inappropriate for tasks where latent factors (e.g., pose) induce different class prevalences.
- Conceptual ambiguity: "dataset," "domain," and "latent domain" are used inconsistently and need rigorous definitions and examples.
- Experimental ambiguity: Section 4.2 and the experimental setup are unclear; notation confusion such as Si being called both "datasets" and "source domains."
- Technical ambiguity: Equation 7 references an undefined term "maxk r(U_k,B)" that must be explained.
- Experiments: empirical results show reasonable improvement over baselines and useful qualitative visualizations.
- Experimental design concern: unclear rationale for chosen source/target datasets; recommend leave-one-dataset-out adaptation for more robust evaluation.
- Incorrect claim: calling the problem "unsupervised" (line 74) is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.
- Overall: meaningful technical contribution but requires clarifications in definitions, equations, and experimental validation to strengthen the paper.
