- Proposes a convex framework to split a dataset into K latent domains with similar class distributions and maximal distinctiveness via pairwise mean differences in RKHS.
- Determines K by maximizing average learnability within each latent domain (how well a classifier can be learned).
- Forcing similar class distributions within domains may be limiting for tasks where domain-class distributions naturally differ (e.g., pose differences across classes).
- Novel contribution: controlling class label distributions in a relaxed convex framework for latent domain discovery.
- Novel contribution: choosing K based on per-domain classification quality to ensure sufficient samples per class for generalization.
- Paper needs clearer definitions and examples for key terms (dataset, domain, latent domain); terms are used interchangeably and cause confusion.
- Experiments (especially Sec. 4.2) are unclear; notation issues (e.g., maxk r(U_k,B) in Eq.7 undefined) need fixing.
- Experimental validation shows reasonable improvements but dataset/source-target selection is not justified; alternatives like leave-one-dataset-out suggested.
- Qualitative results are helpful visually.
- Claiming unsupervised latent domain discovery is misleading because the method relies on semantic class labels.
- Overall, technically notable contributions but several concepts and experimental validations require clearer explanation and justification.
