- Proposes a kernel-based method to automatically discover latent domains for improved domain adaptation, formulated as an integer program with a label-distribution constraint and relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on a static images dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset, showing significant gains over the domain adaptation method in [19].  
- Paper is well-written and presents a novel approach with well-supported performance improvements.  
- Reviewer initially leaned toward rejection pending clarification of two critical issues in the rebuttal.  
- Concern (i): The claim that appearance-based clustering reshapes datasets into per-category domains lacks supporting evidence and the paper does not explain how the proposed formulation prevents this.  
- Concern (ii): The necessity of the label prior constraint is not justified; need comparisons to k-means/unsupervised object discovery, random equal partitioning, and the model without the label prior.  
- Clarify what is meant by "maximally different in distribution"—distribution over which feature space or domain?  
- Question whether β_{mk} is already a simplex distribution by construction.  
- Apparent inconsistency between constraints described around lines 153 and 182 needs reconciliation.  
- Request justification or proof for the bound stated on line 215.  
- Ask whether the geodesic flow kernel was reimplemented or public code was used (line 289).  
- Request deeper analysis of performance differences and specific failures of baselines (e.g., [19]) to strengthen claims (lines 313/340).  
- Typographical correction requested for Equation (1): change M'k to Mk' (or vice versa as noted).  
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias," CVPR 2011.  
- Rebuttal addressed the main concerns and the reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
