- Paper proposes a novel method to discover latent domains in training data and use them for improved domain adaptation.
- Formulates domain discovery as an integer program (relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization) with constraints: one-domain-per-point and label-distribution matching.
- Number of domains is selected via cross-validation.
- Evaluated on static images and IXMAS multi-view action datasets, showing gains over Hoffman et al. [20].
- Paper is generally well-written and the approach appears novel.
- Motivation is unclear: no evidence that appearance-based clustering creates per-category domains as claimed.
- Unclear how the model formulation prevents reshaping into per-category domains in practice.
- The label-prior constraint may be unnecessary; performance without it is not reported.
- Missing baseline comparisons: k-means partitioning, unsupervised object-discovery over appearance vectors, and random equal partitioning.
- Need ablation showing effect of removing the second constraint and other components.
- Phrase “maximally different in distribution from each other” is vague—specify distribution over what.
- Redundant or unclear comments about β_{mk} already lying on a simplex need clarification.
- Constraints presented around line 182 differ from earlier formulation and should be reconciled.
- Bound claimed around line 215 lacks justification or proof.
- Use of the geodesic flow kernel [4] is not sufficiently explained—provide details.
- Should show systematic failure cases of baselines and offer insights into why the proposed method succeeds.
- Overall judgment: promising and potentially significant contribution but requires revisions to address motivation, baselines, ablations, and several technical clarifications.
