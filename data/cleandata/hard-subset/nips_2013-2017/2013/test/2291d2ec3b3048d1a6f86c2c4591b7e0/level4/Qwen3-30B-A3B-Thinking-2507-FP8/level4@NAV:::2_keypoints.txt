- Proposes automatic partitioning of an entire image collection into domains based on feature-space separability and domain learnability for the original task.  
- Fills a notable gap in automated domain definition and is a meaningful step toward discovering useful domains for classifier training.  
- Optimization method (Section 2) is unclear about how well it solves the combined objectives (separability + learnability).  
- Two driving criteria are insufficiently integrated: learnability is used post-hoc to pick domain count while partitioning relies solely on separability; a unified objective is preferable.  
- Paper is well-written but lacks illustrative figures to clarify key concepts.  
- Experiments show significant gains over dataset-based domains and a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Inclusion of the test set in the "reshaping" process is poorly explained and conflates an imputation setting with standard domain adaptation.  
- Uses an outdated image descriptor (single global BoW with sparse SURF); should adopt spatial pyramid BoW with dense SURF or Fisher Vectors.  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unspecified; Euclidean is inappropriate—suggest X² or intersection kernel.  
- Overall: valuable and empirically strong contribution but needs improved optimization integration and modern image representation methodology.
