- Paper goal: automatically identify latent training-set domains for domain adaptation to improve test-set classification.
- Method: kernel-based dissimilarity function used to partition data to maximize inter-domain dissimilarity.
- Formulation: integer program with constraints (unique domain assignment, per-domain class distribution matches overall) relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization.
- Model selection: optimal number of domains chosen via cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on a static-image dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset.
- Main contribution: improved performance over prior domain adaptation method [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and approach appears novel with noteworthy performance gains.
- Major concern 1: motivation unclear—no evidence that clustering by appearance leads to per-category domains or how Section 2 prevents this.
- Major concern 2: necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear—need ablation without it and comparisons to simple baselines.
- Requested baselines: k-means on appearance vectors, unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV’05), and random equal-sized partitions.
- Clarification requests: what distribution is meant by “maximally different in distribution” (Line 82)?
- Technical comments: check redundancy of β_mk simplex constraint (Line 166) and explain differing constraints between Lines 153 and 182.
- Proof and implementation queries: justify the bound at Line 215 and detail geodesic flow kernel implementation (reimplementation or public code).
- Analysis request: explain why baselines fail and how the proposed method systematically overcomes them (Lines 313/340).
- Minor edits: fix typo “M'k” to “M_k'” in Eq (1) and consider citing Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011).
- Outcome: rebuttal addressed motivation and label-prior concerns; reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
