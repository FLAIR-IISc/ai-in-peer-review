- Summary: Proposes kernel-based discovery of latent training domains with a label-prior constraint, relaxed QP optimization, and domain-adaptive classification showing gains over [19].
- Strength: Clear problem formulation and principled optimization framework.
- Strength: Novel idea of jointly discovering latent domains while constraining label priors.
- Strength: Empirical improvements on two datasets, suggesting practical value.
- Strength: Generally clear and well-structured presentation.
- Concern: Need empirical evidence that naïve appearance clustering yields per-category domains (e.g., MI between clusters and labels).
- Concern: Explain and ablate how the label prior constraint prevents per-category collapse; show results without it.
- Concern: Report performance with no constraint, a soft/penalized version, and sensitivity to constraint tightness.
- Concern: Add simple baselines: k-means on features, unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV 2005), and random equal-size partitions.
