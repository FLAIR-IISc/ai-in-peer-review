The paper proposes an approach to automatically discover latent domains in a training set, which is then used in a domain adaptation framework to improve classification performance on a test set. The method defines a function that measures the difference between two feature vectors over a specified kernel and aims to partition the data points into domains such that this function is maximized across each pair of domains. The problem is formulated as an integer programming problem with two constraints: each point is assigned to exactly one domain, and the distribution over class labels in each domain must match the input distribution over the entire point set. To solve this problem, the authors relax it to a continuous optimization over a quadratic cost with linear constraints and determine the number of domains via cross-validation.

The approach is evaluated on two datasets: static images of [2] and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset of [15]. Notably, the proposed method outperforms the domain adaptation approach of [19]. The paper is well-written, and the approach appears to be novel. The performance gains over [19] are also appreciated.

However, I initially lean towards rejection due to two main concerns that I would like to see addressed in the rebuttal. Firstly, the motivation for this paper is unclear. The claim on line 77 that "simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains" lacks evidence, and it is unclear how the model formulation in Section 2 overcomes this issue. Specifically, how does the model prevent reshaping into per-category domains?

Secondly, the necessity of the second constraint ("label prior constraint") on line 154 is unclear. I am curious about the performance without this constraint and suggest including a baseline where the data is partitioned using k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al ICCV '05) over the appearance vectors. Additionally, it would be helpful to evaluate the performance when the dataset is randomly partitioned into equal sets.

Some additional comments and suggestions for improvement include:

- Clarifying the meaning of "maximally different in distribution from each other" (line 82), specifically what distribution is being referred to.
- Noting that \beta_{mk} as formulated already lives on a simplex (line 166).
- Pointing out the difference in constraints between line 182 and the formulation starting on line 153.
- Providing justification or proof for the bound on line 215.
- Offering more details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel [4], including whether it is a reimplementation or publicly available source code.
- Providing insights into the differences in performance on lines 313/340, including systematic failures of the baselines that the proposed approach overcomes.
- Correcting the typo in Eq (1): M'k => Mk'.
- Considering the inclusion of the citation: Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias A. Torralba, A. Efros. IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2011.

After the rebuttal addressed my concerns regarding the paper motivation and the label prior constraint, I lean slightly towards acceptance.
