- Proposes automatic partitioning of images into meaningful domains (beyond dataset boundaries) using separability and learnability criteria.  
- Addresses a relatively underexplored problem; contribution is novel and significant.  
- Optimization in Section 2 is unclear; connection to original objective (eqns 2 and 3) and approximation quality is not established.  
- Separability and learnability are weakly integrated: learnability is used only to choose K post-hoc rather than influencing partitioning.  
- Suggests a joint objective that directly incorporates both separability and learnability would be stronger.  
- Paper is well-written but would benefit from figures/visualizations to illustrate partitioning and discovered domains.  
- Experiments show strong empirical gains over using datasets as domains and over prior domain discovery method [19].  
- Using the unlabeled test set in domain discovery is interesting but should be explicitly described as a transductive/imputation setting.  
- Image representation (sparse SURF bag-of-words, 800-codebook) is outdated and limits performance.  
- The similarity measure for histograms is unspecified; Euclidean is inappropriate—use Chi-squared or intersection kernel.  
- Recommend stronger features (spatial pyramids with dense sampling, Fisher Vectors) and cite Lazebnik et al. (CVPR 2006) and Perronnin et al. (ECCV 2010).  
- Despite methodological shortcomings, the paper’s conceptual advance and results justify acceptance.
