- Novelty: Proposes automatic discovery of domains across datasets to reflect underlying visual factors rather than dataset origin.  
- Contribution: Under-explored and promising direction likely to improve robustness of classifiers for target tasks.  
- Optimization concern: Fidelity of the proposed optimization (Section 2) in solving objectives (eqs. 2–3) is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Loose coupling: The two criteria (maximal separability and maximal learnability) are loosely coupled; learnability is mostly used as a post-hoc wrapper to pick domain count.  
- Recommendation for method: Prefer a unified objective that directly optimizes both separability and learnability together to produce final partitions.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is clearly written but would benefit from figures that visually illustrate core concepts.  
- Experimental strength: Empirical results show clear performance gains over using whole datasets as domains and over prior method [19].  
- Test-set reshaping: Use of the test set during "reshaping" is intriguing but unclear; it implies a transductive/imputation setup that must be explicitly stated.  
- Representation weakness: The image representation (sparse SURF BoW, 800-word codebook) is simplistic and outdated for modern vision benchmarks.  
- Specific representation fixes: Recommend spatial pyramid BoW on densely sampled SURF, specify proper histogram similarity (Chi-squared or intersection kernel rather than Euclidean).  
- Stronger encoding suggestion: Recommend adopting Fisher Vectors to align with state-of-the-art image encodings.  
- Missing details/references: Paper should state the similarity metric used and consider prior work (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).  
- Overall verdict: Despite methodological and representation issues, the work is a positive exploratory advance and merits acceptance.
