- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains and uses them in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.  
- Defines a kernel-based difference function over feature vectors and partitions data to maximize inter-domain differences.  
- Formulates the problem as integer programming with two constraints: each point assigned to exactly one domain, and each domain’s class-label distribution matches the global prior.  
- Relaxes the problem to continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on two datasets (static images [2] and IXMAS multi-view actions [15]) and shows improvements over the domain adaptation method of [19].  
- Positive: paper is well-written and the approach appears novel; performance gains over [19] are appreciated.  
- Major concern (i): motivation unclear—no evidence that clustering by appearance produces per-category domains and no clear explanation of how the model prevents that.  
- Major concern (ii): necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear—request ablation without it and baseline comparisons (k-means, unsupervised object discovery like Sivic et al. ICCV ’05, and random equal partitions).  
- Request to clarify the phrase “maximally different in distribution”—specify which variables/distributions are meant.  
- Technical query: confirm whether β_{mk} already lies on a simplex as formulated (line 166).  
- Clarify discrepancy between the constraints stated around line 153 and those at line 182.  
- Ask for justification or proof for the bound referenced at line 215.  
- Provide details on the use/implementation/source of the geodesic flow kernel (line 289)—reimplementation or public code?  
- Request analysis of why the proposed method outperforms baselines and examples of systematic baseline failures (lines 313/340).  
- Minor correction: Eq (1) M'k should be Mk'.  
- Suggest adding the citation: Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.  
- After rebuttal addressed motivation and label-prior concerns, reviewer now leans slightly toward accepting the paper.
