- Problem: Automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets to improve domain adaptation by avoiding treating heterogeneous datasets as a single source.  
- Method: Partition source data into K latent domains by maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness (pairwise MMD in RKHS) and ensuring learnability (cross-validated classifier performance to choose K).  
- Novelty: Non-parametric MMD-based formulation avoids distributional assumptions (unlike Hoffman et al.) and frames discovery as a constrained optimization.  
- Label Prior Constraint (LPC): Enforces similar class distributions across discovered domains to avoid trivial class-based clustering.  
- Strength — originality: Elegant, novel formulation; LPC is a clever use of label information to guide domain discovery.  
- Strength — technical quality: Sound relaxed convex optimization and a principled Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) to select K.  
- Strength — presentation & experiments: Generally clear writing, thorough experiments, ablation study, and informative qualitative results.  
- Weakness — LPC assumption: Strong and potentially unrealistic; may fail when true domain-specific class priors differ (e.g., class–pose correlations).  
- Weakness — terminology/notation: Inconsistent use of "dataset/domain/latent domain" and an undefined notation r(A → B) should be clarified.  
- Weakness — experimental protocol: Choice of source/target combinations not well motivated; recommend systematic evaluations like leave-one-dataset-out.  
- Weakness — characterization: Calling the method "unsupervised" is misleading since it uses class labels in LPC; better described as unsupervised w.r.t. domain labels but supervised w.r.t. class labels.  
- Overall: Significant, well-motivated contribution that would be strengthened by discussing LPC limitations and improving notation and experimental rigor.
