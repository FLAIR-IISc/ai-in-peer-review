- Paper proposes automatic discovery of latent domains by partitioning training data into maximally dissimilar domains using a kernel-based dissimilarity, formulated as an integer program relaxed to a quadratic program; number of domains chosen by cross-validation and evaluated on two datasets, outperforming [19].

- Positive: paper is well-written, appears novel, and shows significant performance gains over baseline [19].

- Reviewer currently leans toward rejection but is open to reconsidering after rebuttal addressing major concerns.

- Major concern 1 (motivation/formulation): unclear evidence for the claim that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains and unclear how the model prevents discovered domains from being proxies for class labels.

- Major concern 2 (label prior constraint): necessity of the label-prior constraint is not justified; request ablation without this constraint and comparison to simple baselines (k-means on appearance, unsupervised object discovery, random partitioning).

- Clarify what "maximally different in distribution" refers to (feature distribution, class distribution, etc.).

- Question why β_{mk} requires an additional simplex constraint (line 166) if it already satisfies non-negativity and sums-to-one.

- Point out apparent discrepancy between constraints stated around line 182 and those earlier (starting line 153) that needs clarification.

- Request justification or proof for the bound stated on line 215.

- Ask for details on geodesic flow kernel implementation (public implementation used or re-implemented) mentioned on line 289.

- Suggest the experimental analysis should explain why the approach outperforms baselines (e.g., qualitative results or baseline failure cases) as noted on lines 313/340.

- Note typo in Eq. (1): M'k should be Mk'.

- Suggest citing relevant dataset-bias work: Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias", CVPR 2011.
