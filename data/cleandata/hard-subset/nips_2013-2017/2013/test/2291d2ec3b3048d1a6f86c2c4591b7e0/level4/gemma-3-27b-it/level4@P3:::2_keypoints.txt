- Proposes automated discovery of latent domains to address distribution mismatches instead of treating entire datasets as single domains.
- Domain partitioning guided by two principles: maximize distinctiveness in feature space and maximize learnability for discriminative classifiers.
- Novel and significant focus on defining domains (the “what”) rather than only on adaptation methods (the “how”).
- Limited prior work on automated domain discovery makes this contribution valuable and potentially influential.
- Principled formulation with clear theoretical criteria (distinctiveness and learnability).
- Empirical results show clear advantages over treating datasets as single domains and outperform a recent related method.
- Introduces conditional reshaping of test datasets based on learned training domains, an insightful extension.
- Optimization method is complex and its effectiveness at solving the original formulation is unclear; learnability feels decoupled and used post-hoc.
- Feature representation is dated (sparse SURF bag-of-words, 800 bins); modern descriptors (spatial pyramids, dense SURF, Fisher Vectors) and better similarity measures are recommended.
- Description of test-set reshaping lacks clarity and should explicitly state assumptions (e.g., simultaneous access to all test data).
- Paper lacks visualizations of discovered domains; example images would aid understanding.
- Generally well-written and organized, but optimization details need clearer explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental problem, novel and theoretically sound approach, and promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear/weakly integrated optimization, outdated image representation, and insufficient explanation of test reshaping.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution warranting acceptance, contingent on revisions addressing optimization, features, and clarity.
