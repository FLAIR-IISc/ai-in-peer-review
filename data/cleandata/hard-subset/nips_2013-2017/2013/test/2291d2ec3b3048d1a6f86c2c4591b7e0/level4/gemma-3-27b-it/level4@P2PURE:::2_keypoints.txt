- Introduces a novel domain-adaptation technique that automatically partitions images into domains based on maximizing feature-space separation and ensuring domains support training of discriminative classifiers.  
- Emphasizes discovering domains aligned with underlying visual factors (viewpoint, illumination, clutter) rather than fixed dataset boundaries.  
- Addresses a relatively unexplored and significant problem—automated domain definition—and proposes a promising discovery approach.  
- Concerns about the proposed optimization: unclear how well it satisfies the combined objectives in equations (2) and (3).  
- The two criteria (maximal separability and maximal learnability) are not fully integrated; learnability is mainly used post-hoc rather than in a unified objective.  
- Paper is well-written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments show advantages over treating datasets as domains and outperform a recent method ([19]); including test set in reshaping is intriguing but under-explained and assumes an imputation setting.  
- Major drawback: image descriptor is simplistic/outdated (bag-of-words on sparse SURF with 800 codebook entries); recommend spatial pyramid, dense SURF, or Fisher Vectors.  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unclear; Euclidean distance is inappropriate—prefer X^2 or intersection kernels.  
- Overall verdict: interesting, novel, and promising results; despite limitations (particularly image representation and optimization integration), it’s a valuable contribution that merits acceptance.
