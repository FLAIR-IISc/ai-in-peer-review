- Goal: Automatically discover latent domains in training data for domain adaptation to improve test-set classification.
- Method: Defines a kernel-based function measuring differences between feature vectors and partitions points to maximize inter-domain differences.
- Formulation: Presents an integer program with constraints (unique domain assignment; domain class label distribution matches overall distribution).
- Optimization: Relaxes the integer program to a continuous quadratic program with linear constraints; selects number of domains via cross-validation.
- Evaluation: Tested on a static-image dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset.
- Main contribution: Shows improved performance over the domain adaptation method of [19].
- Positive: Paper is well-written and appears novel; performance gains are noteworthy.
- Primary concern (i): Motivation unclear—no evidence that appearance clustering yields per-category domains and unclear how Section 2 formulation prevents this.
- Primary concern (ii): Necessity of the label-prior constraint is questioned; request results without it and baselines using k-means or unsupervised object discovery and random partitioning.
- Clarification needed: Explain “maximally different in distribution from each other” (which distribution is meant).
- Clarification needed: Does β_{mk} as defined already lie on a simplex?
- Clarification needed: Constraints at line 182 appear different from earlier constraints (line 153); reconcile them.
- Technical request: Provide justification/proof for the bound stated at line 215.
- Implementation detail: Specify geodesic flow kernel [4] usage—reimplementation or public code.
- Analysis request: Explain why the method outperforms baselines and discuss systematic failure modes of baselines (lines 313/340).
- Minor correction: In Equation (1) change M'k to M_k'.
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.
- Final stance: Initial lean toward rejection, but after rebuttal concerns about motivation and label-prior constraint were addressed; reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
