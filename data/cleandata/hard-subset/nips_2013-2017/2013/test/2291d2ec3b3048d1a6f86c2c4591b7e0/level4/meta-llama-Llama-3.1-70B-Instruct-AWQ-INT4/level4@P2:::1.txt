The paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in a training set, which is subsequently used in a domain adaptation framework to yield improved classification performance on a test set. The authors define a function that measures the difference between two feature vectors over a specified kernel and aim to partition the data points into domains such that the function is maximized over the set of points across each pair of domains. The problem is formulated as an integer programming problem with two constraints: each point is assigned to exactly one domain, and the distribution over class labels in each domain must match the input distribution over the entire point set.

The paper is well-written, and the approach is novel. The performance gains over existing domain adaptation approaches are appreciated. However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed.

The motivation for this paper is not entirely clear. The authors argue that simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains. However, the evidence for this claim is not provided, and it is unclear how the model formulation overcomes this issue. Specifically, it is not clear how the model formulation prevents reshaping into per-category domains.

Additionally, the second constraint, the label prior constraint, is not well-justified. It is unclear why this constraint is necessary, and its impact on the performance of the model is not evaluated. A baseline where the data is partitioned using k-means clustering or unsupervised object discovery over the appearance vectors should be shown. Furthermore, the performance when the dataset is randomly partitioned into equal sets should be evaluated.

Some additional comments:

* The phrase "maximally different in distribution from each other" is mentioned throughout the paper. However, it is unclear what this means. Distribution over what?
* The formulation of βmk already lives on a simplex, so the relaxation to a continuous optimization over a quadratic cost with linear constraints may not be necessary.
* The constraints formulated in the relaxed optimization problem seem to be different from those formulated before.
* The justification or proof for the bound on the number of domains is not provided.
* More details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel should be provided. Is this a reimplementation or was publicly available source code used?
* The differences in performance between the proposed approach and the baselines are not well-understood. It would be good to show systematic failures of the baselines that the proposed approach overcomes.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in a training set.
* The approach is evaluated over two datasets, and improved performance is shown over existing domain adaptation approaches.
* The paper is well-written.

Weaknesses:

* The motivation for the paper is not entirely clear.
* The label prior constraint is not well-justified.
* The performance of the model without the label prior constraint is not evaluated.
* The differences in performance between the proposed approach and the baselines are not well-understood.
