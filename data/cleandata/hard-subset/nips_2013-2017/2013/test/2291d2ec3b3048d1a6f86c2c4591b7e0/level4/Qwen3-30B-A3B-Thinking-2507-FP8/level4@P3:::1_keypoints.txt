- Proposes a nonparametric framework to discover latent domains by enforcing maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Avoids assuming datasets = domains and does not rely on Gaussian-mixture models, explicitly handling heterogeneous test sets.  
- Novel contribution with no prior NIPS counterpart; significant for advancing domain adaptation.  
- Optimization is rigorous: label prior constraint (LPC) prevents domain collapse into per-category clusters.  
- Uses domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to select K, avoiding tiny overfit domains.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on object (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and action (IXMAS) recognition showing consistent gains.  
- Reshaping both training and test sets gives near-oracle performance; simple domain matching works without target labels.  
- Paper is clearly written, definitions are precise, and experiments/ablations support reproducibility.  
- Motivation for avoiding appearance-based clustering could better cite Torralba & Efros (2011); this was addressed in revision.  
- LPC necessity was questioned but empirically validated (Table 1); concern resolved.  
- Minor technical fixes: correct Eq.(1) notation (M_k') and clarify redundancy in simplex constraint on β_mk.  
- Bound on K_max (≤ min{M/(NC), C}) is reasonable but presented without proof; a brief citation (e.g., [20]) would help.  
- Geodesic flow kernel implementation citation is adequate.  
- Main remaining con: computational cost and scalability to large datasets not discussed.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, significant, and well-validated — recommend acceptance.
