- Introduces automatic domain partitioning across datasets using two principles: maximize feature-space separability and ensure each domain supports a strong discriminative classifier.
- Novel and significant contribution: tackles the relatively unexplored problem of automatic domain definition instead of relying on dataset labels.
- Optimization method is unclear: the connection between the proposed algorithm and the objective in equations (2) and (3) needs clarification.
- The two guiding criteria (separability and learnability) are not tightly integrated; learnability appears used post-hoc to select domain count rather than embedded in the partitioning objective.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear in concepts.
- Lacks illustrative figures—visual examples of partitioning and feature-space arrangements would aid comprehension.
- Experiments show clear advantages over treating datasets as domains and outperform the recent method in [19].
- Inclusion of the test set in the “reshaping” process is interesting but poorly presented; the implicit imputation setting (all test data available) should be explicitly stated.
- Critical weakness: uses an outdated image descriptor (single bag-of-words on sparse SURF with 800 codebook entries).
- Authors should explore stronger representations (e.g., spatial pyramid of dense SURF, Fisher Vectors) to match current state-of-the-art.
- Similarity measure for comparing descriptors is unspecified; Euclidean is inappropriate for histograms—clarify if chi-squared or intersection kernel was used.
- Recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the image-descriptor concerns and clarifying the optimization/integration of the two criteria.
