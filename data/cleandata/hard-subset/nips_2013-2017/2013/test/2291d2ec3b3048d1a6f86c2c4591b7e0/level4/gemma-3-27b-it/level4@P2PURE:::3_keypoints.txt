- Introduces a convex framework to partition datasets into K "latent domains" that maximize pairwise mean differences in RKHS, choosing K by maximizing average classifier learnability per domain.

- Enforces similar semantic class distributions within each domain while maximizing distinctiveness to avoid class domination in clusters.

- The class-distribution constraint may hinder discovery of meaningful latent domains in cases where class frequencies vary across latent factors (e.g., animal poses like horses vs cows).

- Controlling class label distributions within a (relaxed) convex framework and selecting K via within-domain classification performance are novel technical contributions.

- Terminology and definitions (dataset, domain, latent domain) are unclear and inconsistent (Section 4.2); examples and precise definitions are needed.

- Notation issues: 'Si' used ambiguously as dataset and source domain, and 'maxk r(U_k,B)' in Equation 7 is undefined.

- Experimental results show reasonable improvements over baselines and helpful qualitative visualizations, but dataset selection rationale is unclear.

- Recommends broader evaluation (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out adaptation) to strengthen empirical validation.

- Framing the problem as unsupervised is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.
