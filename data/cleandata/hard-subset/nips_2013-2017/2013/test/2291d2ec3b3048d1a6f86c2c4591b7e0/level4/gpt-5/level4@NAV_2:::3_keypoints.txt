- Proposes a relaxed convex formulation to partition datasets into K latent domains by enforcing similar class priors and maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness via MMD-style kernel mean embedding distances.  
- Chooses K by maximizing average within-domain learnability measured with domain-wise cross-validated classification performance, preventing overly small, unlearnable domains.  
- Matching class-prior distributions helps avoid clusters collapsing onto single classes but can be overly restrictive and mask meaningful domain structure when class-wise pose/occurrence varies (e.g., horses vs. cows).  
- The explicit label-prior control inside a relaxed convex objective is a novel contribution; validating K via within-domain discriminative performance is a useful design choice.  
- Terminology around dataset, domain, and latent domain is ambiguous and inconsistent (Si called both dataset and source domain); needs clear formal definitions and examples, especially in Section 4.2.  
- Eq. 7 uses maxk r(Uk, B) without an explicit definition and should be clarified.  
- Empirical validation is adequate and shows reasonable improvements over baselines, with informative qualitative visualizations.  
- The choice of source/target splits lacks full justification; a leave-one-dataset-out adaptation protocol would be a more standard, comprehensive evaluation.  
- Describing latent domain discovery as unsupervised is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.  
- Overall solid technical formulation but the paper would benefit from clearer concepts and a tightened experimental protocol.
