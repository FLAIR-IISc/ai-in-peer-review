- Proposes automatic partitioning of the union of images into domains guided by feature-space separability and domain “learnability” for original class labels, instead of equating domains with datasets.  
- Novel and promising idea with little prior work on automatic domain definition; can enable better classifiers for the original task.  
- Concern that the optimization strategy (Sec. 2) is unclear and may not closely address the stated objectives (2) + (3).  
- The two guiding criteria (separability and maximal learnability) are not tightly integrated: learnability is used mainly to select the number of domains rather than to shape the partition.  
- Recommendation to develop a unified objective that directly incorporates both criteria to produce the partition.  
- Paper is well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify main concepts.  
- Experiments show the discovered domains outperform treating datasets as domains and a recent domain-discovery baseline [19].  
- The “reshaping” procedure that incorporates the test set is unclear and appears to assume all test data are available (an imputation scenario); this should be explicitly stated.  
- Image representation is outdated (single bag-of-words over sparse SURF, 800 codebook); recommends spatial pyramid on dense SURF, appropriate histogram kernel (χ² or intersection), and/or Fisher Vectors to match modern practice.  
- Overall: interesting and novel work with good results, but optimization integration and image representation need improvement.
