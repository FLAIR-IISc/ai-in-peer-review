- Proposal: method to automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.
- Method: defines a kernel-based function measuring differences between feature vectors across domains and partitions points to maximize this function across domain pairs.
- Formulation: integer program with two constraints — each point assigned to exactly one domain, and class label distribution within each domain matches overall input distribution.
- Optimization: relaxes the integer program to a continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints; number of domains selected by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images [2] and IXMAS multi-view action [15]).
- Empirical result: demonstrates performance improvement over the domain adaptation approach in [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and appears novel in the domain adaptation literature; gains over [19] are noteworthy.
- Major concern 1 (motivation): claim that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains lacks supporting evidence and it’s unclear how the model in Section 2 prevents this.
- Major concern 2 (label-prior constraint): rationale is insufficient; need ablation showing performance without this constraint and comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery (Sivic et al. ICCV 2005), and random equal-sized partitions.
- Clarification needed: specify what is meant by “maximally different in distribution from each other” (which distribution is referenced).
- Technical question: does β_{mk} already lie on a simplex per its formulation?
- Consistency issue: constraints at line 182 appear inconsistent with those defined around line 153.
- Theoretical request: provide justification or proof for the bound stated at line 215.
- Implementation detail: clarify whether the geodesic flow kernel [4] was reimplemented or obtained from public code.
- Analysis request: explain performance differences and highlight systematic baseline failures that the proposed method overcomes.
- Minor correction: Equation (1) should use "M_k'" instead of "M'k".
- Citation suggestion: consider including Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias” (CVPR 2011).
- Rebuttal outcome: authors addressed motivation and label-prior concerns; reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
