- Paper goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them in a domain adaptation pipeline to improve test-time recognition.
- Method: introduce a kernel-based discrepancy using RKHS mean embeddings and seek a partition maximizing total pairwise discrepancy across domains.
- Optimization: pose as an integer program with two constraints—each instance assigned to one domain and a label prior constraint (LPC) enforcing domain class-frequency match to the global prior—then relax to a continuous quadratic program and choose K by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV).
- Experiments: evaluated on Saenko et al. image datasets and IXMAS multi-view action dataset, showing improved performance over prior work [19].
- Positive: manuscript is clearly written and the core idea appears novel.
- Positive: empirical improvements over [19] are encouraging.
- Concern (motivation): need evidence for the claim that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains and clarification of how the formulation prevents collapsing to per-category clusters.
- Concern (LPC): question necessity of the label prior constraint; request ablation reporting performance without LPC.
- Baseline request: ask for comparisons to simple partitioning strategies—k-means on appearance features, unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV’05), and random equal-size partition.
- Clarify what “maximally different in distribution” means—specify whether distributions over x, (x,y), or another quantity and the divergence/metric used.
- Clarify model details: role of the simplex constraint relative to βmk, and reconcile differing constraint formulations described in different lines.
- Theoretical detail: justify or prove the stated bound on K or cite a formal reference.
- Implementation detail: report how the geodesic flow kernel [4] was used—reimplementation or public code.
- Analysis request: explain why the proposed method outperforms baselines, identify baseline failure modes, and which ones the method addresses.
- Minor fixes: correct Eq. (1) M′k → Mk′ and consider adding Torralba & Efros “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias” (CVPR 2011) to references.
- Outcome: after rebuttal addressing motivation and LPC, reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
