- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in images/videos for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two desired properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to find domains satisfying those properties.  
- Splits datasets into K subsets (latent domains) with similar semantic class distributions and high mutual distinctiveness.  
- Achieves distinctiveness by maximizing pairwise mean differences in RKHS (kernel-based objective).  
- Selects K by maximizing average learnability (how well a classifier can be learned within each domain).  
- Demonstrates improved performance on object recognition and human activity domain adaptation tasks.  
- Provides qualitative results that help visualize what the algorithm discovers.  
- Concepts of dataset, domain, and latent domain are not clearly defined and are used inconsistently (section 4.2 confusing).  
- Notation issue: maxk r(Uk,B) in Eq. (6) is used but not defined.  
- Experimental choices (which source/target datasets reported) are not well justified; leave-one-dataset-out suggested as an alternative.  
- Claiming the problem is unsupervised is misleading because semantic class labels are used in the method.  
- Technical contribution noted: formulating latent-domain discovery via encouraging similar class distributions in a relaxed convex framework.  
- Overall: notable contribution but requires clarification on definitions, notation, and experimental validation.
