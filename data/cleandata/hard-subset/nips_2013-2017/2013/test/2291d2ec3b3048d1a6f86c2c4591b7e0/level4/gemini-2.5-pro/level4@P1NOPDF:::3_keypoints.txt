- Proposes a convex framework to split data into K "latent domains" that balance similar class distributions and maximal distinctiveness.
- Achieves distinctiveness by maximizing pairwise mean differences in an RKHS induced by a kernel.
- Chooses the optimal K by maximizing "average learnability" — classification performance within each discovered domain.
- Novel contribution: controlling class label distributions within a relaxed convex formulation to guide domain discovery.
- Strength: selecting K via within-domain classification ensures domains are distinct and contain enough class data for generalizable classifiers.
- Limitation: enforcing similar class distributions across all domains can prevent discovery of natural, class-specific groupings (pose example with horses vs. cows).
- Paper needs clearer definitions and consistent use of "dataset," "domain," and "latent domain," with examples to avoid confusion (Section 4.2 problematic).
- Equation 7 uses max_k r(U_k, B) but this term appears undefined and requires clarification.
- Experimental results show reasonable improvement over baselines and useful qualitative visualizations.
- Experimental design choices (specific source/target datasets) are not well-justified; suggest leave-one-dataset-out evaluation for better generalization assessment.
- Claiming latent domain discovery is unsupervised (line 74) is misleading since the method uses class labels to guide discovery.
- Overall: technically valuable contribution but would be stronger if core assumption limitations, terminology, and experimental justification were addressed.
