- Proposes a convex framework that partitions datasets into K “latent domains” with similar class distributions and maximizes inter-domain distinctiveness via RKHS mean differences.  
- Selects K by maximizing average learnability (within-domain classifier performance).  
- Enforcing similar class distributions helps avoid clusters dominated by a single class.  
- That constraint can be limiting: real latent factors (e.g., pose) may induce class-dependent domain distributions and be obscured by forcing similar class mixes.  
- Controlling class-label distributions within a relaxed convex formulation is a novel technical contribution.  
- Using within-domain classification quality to choose K is sensible and practical.  
- Manuscript is unclear about dataset vs. domain vs. latent domain; these terms need precise definitions and examples.  
- Section 4.2 is confusing: S_i is used inconsistently (datasets vs. source domains) and Eq.7 uses max_k r(U_k,B) without definition.  
- Experiments show reasonable improvements and helpful qualitative results, but the choice of source/target datasets lacks justification.  
- Recommends a leave-one-dataset-out adaptation protocol as a more natural evaluation.  
- Framing the problem as “unsupervised” is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.
