- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets, addressing the single-domain assumption in domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two domain-discovery criteria: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Develops a nonparametric optimization approach and domain-wise cross-validation to choose the number of domains.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements on object recognition and human activity recognition versus treating datasets as single domains and prior work [20].  
- Originality/significance: tackles a relatively unexplored problem with a principled approach that better matches real-world data structure.  
- Technical quality: well-motivated method and logically structured optimization with thorough experiments.  
- Integration issue: maximum learnability is primarily used post-hoc to select domain count rather than integrated into the discovery objective.  
- Optimization clarity: section 2 needs clearer explanation of how closely the method solves the original (2)+(3) problem.  
- Clarity/visualization: paper would benefit from more visualizations showing discovered domains and factors like viewpoint/lighting.  
- Section 4.3 (test-set reshaping) is confusing and needs a diagram or clearer explanation of conditional reshaping.  
- Missing experimental detail: insufficient description of the image representation used.  
- Representation concerns: uses outdated bag-of-visual-words (800 codebook entries); should use stronger features (SPM, dense SURF, Fisher vectors).  
- Similarity metric unspecified for histogram comparisons (likely X^2 or intersection kernel; Euclidean would be inappropriate).  
- Strengths: novel formulation, strong empirical gains, clear motivation, practical applicability across tasks.  
- Weaknesses: limited integration of criteria, outdated image representation, lack of visualizations, confusing presentation of test reshaping.  
- Overall recommendation: important contribution with compelling results; recommend acceptance pending addressing noted weaknesses.
