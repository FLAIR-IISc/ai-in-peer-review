- Problem addressed: datasets mix multiple latent factors; paper proposes automatic discovery of latent domains via two axioms: distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Distinctiveness: operationalized with nonparametric kernel mean embeddings and a sum over pairwise RKHS distances (akin to MMD) to partition data into maximally different distributions.  
- Learnability: enforced by domain-wise cross-validation to select the number of domains K, favoring partitions that support strong discriminative classifiers.  
- Test reshaping: introduces a conditional variant to align latent test partitions to discovered training domains (assumes access to entire test set).  
- Empirical results: shows improved transfer on object (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and action (IXMAS) recognition vs dataset-as-domain baselines and Hoffman et al. [20].  
- Compatibility: discovered domains integrate with downstream multi-source adaptation methods (UNION, ENSEMBLE, MATCHING) and often approach oracle performance.  
- Relation to prior work: positioned relative to domain adaptation, nonparametric two-sample testing, and latent-domain discovery; uses fewer distributional assumptions than parametric mixture models like [20].  
- Strength — formulation: clear, principled distinctiveness objective using kernel mean embeddings.  
- Strength — learnability: sensible practical mechanism (domain-wise CV) with informative A(K) analyses.  
- Strength — experiments: solid empirical evaluation with ablations against multiple baselines and extensions (test reshaping, multi-source strategies).  
- Weakness — optimization: discrete assignment relaxed to a nonconvex QP; lacks analysis of approximation quality, sensitivity to initialization, and stability across runs.  
- Weakness — coupling of objectives: learnability only affects K via outer CV and is not integrated into the partitioning objective; a unified trade-off would be cleaner.  
- Weakness — test-time assumption: conditional reshaping is transductive (requires full test set); needs explicit statement and discussion of online/streaming feasibility.  
- Weakness — methodological details: unclear choices for kernel on histogram descriptors, bandwidth selection, impact of label-prior constraint δ, and per-domain size bounds.  
- Clarity: generally well written and convincing, but conditional reshaping and some procedural details are underexplained; more illustrative figures (partitions vs K, t-SNE/RKHS visuals) would help.  
- Originality: novel nonparametric approach to latent domain discovery and test-time reshaping; conceptually original relative to prior parametric methods.  
- Significance: practical impact for multi-source adaptation with empirical gains that often match oracle selection; approach is generalizable to other modalities.  
- Experimental suggestions: update visual features (dense descriptors, Fisher Vectors), use χ2/histogram-intersection kernels for histograms, report explicit similarity measures, and ablate LPC and hyperparameters.  
- Scalability concerns: discuss kernel matrix costs M, runtime, and approximations (Nyström, random features) for larger datasets.  
- Pros summary: tackles an important problem, principled nonparametric formulation, consistent empirical improvements, practical extensions validated.  
- Cons summary: unquantified relaxation gap and potential instability, non-integrated learnability objective, transductive test assumption, outdated visual features and possibly suboptimal histogram kernels.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify optimization behavior and test-time assumptions and to strengthen experiments with modern representations and appropriate histogram kernels.
