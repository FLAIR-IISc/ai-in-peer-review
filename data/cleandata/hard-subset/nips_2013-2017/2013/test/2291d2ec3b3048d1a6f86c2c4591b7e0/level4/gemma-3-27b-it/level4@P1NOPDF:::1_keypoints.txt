- Goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Method: define a kernel-based difference function between features and partition data to maximize inter-domain differences.
- Formulation: integer program with constraints that each point has one domain and each domain’s label distribution matches the global class distribution.
- Optimization: relax integer program to continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints; select number of domains by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and shows improved performance over prior method [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and the approach appears novel with encouraging performance gains.
- Main concern 1: motivation unclear—no evidence shown that appearance-based clustering produces per-category domains, and unclear how the model prevents category-only partitions.
- Main concern 2: necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear—need results without it and comparisons to simple baselines (k-means, unsupervised object discovery, random partitions).
- Clarify: the phrase “maximally different in distribution” needs specification—distribution over which features/characteristics?
- Question: does β_mk as formulated already lie on a simplex?
- Clarify: constraints at line 182 seem different from those in line 153—please reconcile.
- Request: provide justification or proof for the bound stated on line 215.
- Implementation detail: specify whether the geodesic flow kernel [4] was reimplemented or used from public code.
- Analysis request: provide deeper insights into why the method outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failures that the method avoids.
- Minor correction: Eq (1) typo—M’_k should be Mk’.
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros (2011) "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" may be relevant.
- Final stance: after rebuttal, original concerns were addressed and reviewer now slightly leans toward acceptance.
