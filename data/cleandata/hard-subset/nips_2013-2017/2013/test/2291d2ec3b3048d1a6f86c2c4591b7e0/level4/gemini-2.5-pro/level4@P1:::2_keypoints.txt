- Presents a method to automatically discover latent domains within image collections for better domain adaptation.  
- Argues that treating entire datasets as domains is flawed because datasets can be heterogeneous mixtures of factors (viewpoint, illumination, background).  
- Core contribution: partition images into latent domains guided by two principles—maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Maximum distinctiveness: discovered domains should be maximally separable in feature space.  
- Maximum learnability: each discovered domain should support learning a strong discriminative classifier for object classes.  
- Goal: produce domain partitions that reflect true data-generating factors to improve adaptation performance.  
- Novelty/significance: tackles an important, underexplored problem and reframes datasets-as-domains, which is valuable for domain adaptation research.  
- Principled formulation: the two guiding principles are intuitive and provide a meaningful framework for domain discovery.  
- Strong experimental results: discovered domains yield significantly better adaptation than using original datasets or the Hoffman et al. baseline.  
- Integration concern: distinctiveness and learnability are not jointly optimized—learnability is applied post-hoc to select K rather than part of a unified objective.  
- Optimization concern: integer-program formulation is relaxed to a continuous problem, but the paper omits discussion of the relaxation’s approximation quality.  
- Feature representation concern: experiments use outdated BoW over sparse SURF (800 words); stronger modern features (spatial pyramid BoW on dense features, Fisher Vectors) and appropriate histogram metrics should be used.  
- Similarity metric unspecified: paper should clarify which histogram similarity (e.g., Chi-squared or intersection kernel vs L2) was used.  
- Test-set reshaping clarity: reshaping assumes access to the entire unlabeled test set (transductive setting) and this assumption should be explicitly stated.  
- Presentation: paper would benefit from illustrative figures showing feature-space distributions before and after domain discovery.  
- Overall: novel and compelling direction with promising results; recommend acceptance subject to addressing the noted methodological and experimental concerns.
