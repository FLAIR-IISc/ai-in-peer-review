- Paper goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test-set classification.
- Method: proposes a kernel-based distribution-difference measure and partitions data to maximize inter-domain difference, formulated as an integer program relaxed to continuous optimization.
- Model details: enforces that each instance belongs to one domain and that domain label distributions mirror the overall label distribution (label prior constraint); number of domains chosen via cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on object recognition datasets and IXMAS human activity dataset, showing improvements over the domain adaptation method of [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and presents a novel approach with encouraging performance gains and multi-task evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: reviewer leans toward rejection but is open to persuasion via rebuttal.
- Major concern — motivation: insufficient evidence that simple appearance clustering yields per-category domains and unclear how the formulation prevents collapsing into per-category domains.
- Major concern — label prior constraint (LPC): necessity unclear; reviewer requests ablation showing performance without LPC.
- Baseline comparisons requested: contrast with simpler baselines (k-means on appearance, unsupervised feature-learning/object discovery methods like Sivic ICCV ’05) and with random equal partitioning.
- Clarification needed: repeatedly used phrase “maximally different in distribution” must specify whether “distribution” refers to features, labels, or other quantities.
- Technical clarity: statement about β_mk “relaxed to live on the simplex” is misleading since they were already simplex-constrained.
- Constraint consistency: constraints at line 182 appear different from those at line 153 and need reconciliation/clarification.
- Theoretical justification: request for justification or theoretical basis for the bound on K_max (line 215).
- Implementation detail: clarify whether the geodesic flow kernel was reimplemented or a public implementation was used.
- Analysis depth: request for more insightful analysis explaining why the proposed method outperforms baselines and identifying systematic failure modes of baselines that the method addresses.
- Minor issues: typo in Equation (1) (M'_k should be M_k') and suggested relevant citation Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) on dataset bias/domain generalization.
