- Goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Method: define a kernel-based difference between feature vectors and partition data to maximize inter-domain differences.
- Formulation: cast as an integer program with constraints (one domain per point; per-domain label distribution matches global prior) and relax to a continuous quadratic program.
- Model selection: determine the optimal number of domains via cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on a static image dataset and IXMAS multi-view action dataset, showing improvements over the domain adaptation method of [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and presents a novel approach with noteworthy performance gains.
- Overall stance: initially leaning towards rejection but open to revision if key concerns are addressed.
- Main concern (i): unclear motivation—no evidence for claim that appearance clustering yields per-category domains and unclear how the formulation prevents this.
- Main concern (ii): unclear rationale for the label-prior constraint, need performance impact of removing it, and request baselines using k-means or unsupervised object discovery and random partitioning.
- Clarification request: explain what “distribution” refers to in “maximally different in distribution from each other.”
- Technical question: does β_mk already lie on a simplex as formulated (line 166)?
- Consistency issue: constraints at line 182 appear different from earlier constraints around line 153.
- Theoretical request: provide justification or proof for the bound stated at line 215.
- Implementation detail: clarify use of the geodesic flow kernel—reimplementation vs. public code.
- Empirical analysis: provide insight into why the proposed method outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failures that the method overcomes.
- Minor correction: notation in Eq (1) should be M'_k => M_k'.
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros (2011) “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias.”
- Outcome after rebuttal: the rebuttal addressed motivation and label-prior concerns, leading the reviewer to lean slightly towards acceptance.
