- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in a training set for improved domain adaptation performance.
- Formulates domain discovery as an integer programming problem with constraints: each point assigned to one domain and label distribution in each domain must match the global label prior.
- Paper is well-written and shows empirical improvements over existing domain adaptation approaches on two datasets.
- Motivation is unclear: claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains is not supported with evidence.
- Unclear how the model formulation prevents reshaping into per-category domains.
- The label-prior constraint is not well-justified and its necessity/impact is not evaluated.
- Missing ablations: performance without the label prior constraint is not reported.
- Missing baselines: should compare to k-means or unsupervised object-discovery on appearance vectors and to random equal partitions.
- Ambiguous terminology: “maximally different in distribution from each other” is not clearly defined (distribution over what?).
- Mathematical concerns: βmk already lies on a simplex so relaxation to continuous quadratic optimization may be unnecessary.
- The constraints in the relaxed optimization appear different from the original integer formulation and need clarification.
- No proof or justification provided for the bound on the number of domains.
- Implementation details lacking for the geodesic flow kernel (reimplementation vs. public code).
- Differences in performance between the proposed method and baselines are not well-explained; need examples of systematic baseline failures the method corrects.
- Recommendation: paper has promise but requires revisions addressing motivation, justification of constraints, additional baselines/ablations, and methodological clarifications.
