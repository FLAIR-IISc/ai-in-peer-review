- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition images into domains based on underlying aspects (viewpoint, illumination, clutter) rather than equating domains with datasets.  
- Partitioner is driven by two principles: maximize domain separability in feature space and ensure each domain allows training a good discriminative classifier (learnability).  
- Novel and significant idea with potential to improve classifier training; little prior work on automatic domain definition.  
- Optimization method (Sec. 2) is not fully convincing—unclear how closely it solves the original joint objective (equations 2+3).  
- The two driving criteria are poorly integrated: learnability is used only as a wrapper to choose the number of domains while partitioning relies on separability alone.  
- Experiments show clear gains using the discovered domains versus using datasets as domains and versus a recent domain discovery method [20].  
- Inclusion of the test set in the “reshaping” process is interesting but insufficiently explained; it effectively assumes an imputation setting that isn’t made explicit.  
- Lack of figures weakens the exposition and makes concepts harder to follow.  
- Image representation is outdated and weak (single bag-of-words of sparse SURF with 800 codewords); similarity measure for descriptors is not specified.  
- Recommend using modern representations (spatial pyramid BoW on dense SURF, Fisher V) and appropriate histogram similarities (χ² or intersection, not Euclidean).  
- Recommend integrating the two criteria into a single objective, clarifying the test-set reshaping protocol, and adding illustrative figures.
