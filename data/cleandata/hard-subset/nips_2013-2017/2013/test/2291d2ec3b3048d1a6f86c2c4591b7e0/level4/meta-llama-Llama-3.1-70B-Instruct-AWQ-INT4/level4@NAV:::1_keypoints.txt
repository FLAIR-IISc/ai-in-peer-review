- Paper goal: automatically identify latent domains in training data and use them in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.

- Method: introduces a kernel-based function measuring differences between feature vectors and partitions data to maximize between-domain differences.

- Optimization: formulates the partitioning as an integer program with two constraints (unique domain assignment per point; per-domain label distribution matches global label prior) and relaxes to a continuous quadratic program; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.

- Evaluation: tested on a static-image dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset, showing improved performance over the domain adaptation method of [19].

- Strength: well-written, appears novel, and demonstrates empirical gains over prior work.

- Reviewer stance: initially leaned toward rejection but, after rebuttal addressing concerns, now leans toward acceptance.

- Concern 1 (motivation): claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains is unsupported and it’s unclear how the model formulation addresses this issue.

- Concern 2 (label prior constraint): necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear; reviewer requests ablation without it and comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery baselines, and random equal partitioning.

- Clarification request: define “maximally different in distribution from each other” — specify the distribution being compared.

- Technical note: β_{mk} appears to already lie on a simplex as currently formulated.

- Consistency issue: constraints described around line 182 seem to differ from those earlier (around line 153).

- Theoretical request: provide justification or proof for the bound mentioned at line 215.

- Implementation detail: provide more information on the geodesic flow kernel usage and whether code is reimplemented or publicly available.

- Analysis request: give insights into why the proposed method outperforms baselines and detail systematic failures of the baselines.

- Minor correction: Eq (1) should read Mk' instead of M'k.

- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias” (CVPR 2011).
