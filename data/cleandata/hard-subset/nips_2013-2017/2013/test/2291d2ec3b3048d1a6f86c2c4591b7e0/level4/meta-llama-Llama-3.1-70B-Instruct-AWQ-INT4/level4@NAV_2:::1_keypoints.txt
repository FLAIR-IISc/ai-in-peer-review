- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains and use them for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Key idea: partition data to maximize pairwise distribution differences via a non-parametric formulation enforcing maximum distinctiveness and learnability.
- Paper is well-written and the approach is novel with promising performance gains over prior domain adaptation methods.
- Concern: motivation is unclear—claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains is unsupported by evidence.
- Unclear how the proposed formulation prevents reshaping into per-category domains; needs a detailed explanation.
- Necessity of the label prior constraint (LPC) is not demonstrated; evaluate and report performance without LPC.
- Recommend comparisons to simple baselines: k-means partitioning, unsupervised object discovery, and random equal partitions.
- Clarify what “maximally different in distribution” means—distribution over which features/representations.
- Note that β_mk already lies on a simplex, so the continuous relaxation may be unnecessary; justify the relaxation.
- Constraints on lines 182–183 appear inconsistent with earlier constraints; resolve and clarify.
- Missing justification for the bound introduced on line 215; provide derivation or rationale.
- Experimental detail missing: specify geodesic flow kernel implementation or cite publicly available code.
- Need deeper analysis of where and why the method outperforms baselines, including systematic failure cases of baselines.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and original, but motivation, constraint necessity, and experimental comparisons should be strengthened.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid technical basis, promising gains; arguments against: unclear motivation, unclear LPC necessity, inadequate baseline comparisons.
