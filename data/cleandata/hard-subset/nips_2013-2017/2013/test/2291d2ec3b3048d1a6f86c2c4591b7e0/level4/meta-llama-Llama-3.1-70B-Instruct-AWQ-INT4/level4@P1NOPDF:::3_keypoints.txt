- Proposes a convex framework to partition data into K latent domains with similar class distributions and maximum distinctiveness via RKHS mean differences, choosing K by maximizing average learnability.
- Enforces similar class distributions within domains to prevent single-class dominance, which may be limiting for tasks where class–domain distributions differ (e.g., pose differences across animal classes).
- Novel contributions: relaxed convex control of class-label distributions for latent-domain discovery and selecting K based on within-domain classification quality.
- Paper lacks clear definitions/distinctions between dataset, domain, and latent domain (confusing usage in Section 4.2) and omits definition of "maxk r(U_k,B)" in Equation 7.
- Experiments show reasonable improvements over baselines, but choice of specific source/target datasets is unclear; leave-one-dataset-out evaluation could be more comprehensive; qualitative results are useful.
- Calling the problem "unsupervised" is misleading since the method relies on semantic class labels.
- Overall technically notable contribution but requires clarification of concepts and experimental choices to strengthen the manuscript.
