- Introduces a convex optimization to partition datasets into K latent domains that enforce similar semantic class distributions while maximizing pairwise distinctiveness in an RKHS.

- Determines K by optimizing average learnability (classifier performance within each subset) to ensure enough per-class samples for discrimination.

- Constraining class distributions prevents single-class dominance but may be inappropriate when class distributions naturally differ across domains (e.g., pose-related class imbalance in animals).

- Novel contribution: relaxing control of label distributions in a convex framework and selecting K based on within-domain classification quality.

- Major conceptual clarity issues: “dataset,” “domain,” and “latent domain” are conflated (notably S_i in Sec. 4.2); definitions and examples are needed.

- Notational error: term max_k r(U_k,B) in Equation 7 is undefined and must be specified.

- Experiments show reasonable improvements over baselines, but the choice of source/target datasets is unjustified; a leave-one-dataset-out protocol would be more rigorous.

- Qualitative results effectively illustrate visual outcomes.

- Problem statement incorrectly labels latent domain discovery as unsupervised despite using semantic class labels.

- Overall assessment: convex formulation is a notable technical contribution, but substantial conceptual and experimental clarifications are required before publication.
