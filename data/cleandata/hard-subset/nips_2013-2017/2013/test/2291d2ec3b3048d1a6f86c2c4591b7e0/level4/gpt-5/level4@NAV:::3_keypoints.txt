- Proposes a convex formulation that partitions data into K “latent domains” by enforcing similar class distributions within each subset while maximizing pairwise distinctiveness in an RKHS.  
- Distinctiveness is defined as maximizing pairwise mean differences of subsets under a chosen kernel.  
- The number of latent domains K is selected by maximizing average within-domain learnability (classifier performance).  
- Enforcing similar class distributions prevents class-dominated clusters but can be too restrictive when latent factors (e.g., pose) do not align with class-wise distributions.  
- Novelty: constraining class-label distributions in a relaxed convex framework for latent-domain discovery is new.  
- Novelty: selecting K based on within-domain classification quality is a useful practice to ensure generalizable domains.  
- Terminology and concepts (dataset, domain, latent domain) are unclear and often conflated; explicit definitions and illustrative examples are needed.  
- Section 4.2 and the experimental setup are confusing—S_i is inconsistently referred to as both datasets and source domains.  
- Notation issue: the term max_k r(U_k, B) in Eq.7 is used but not defined.  
- Experimental validation shows reasonable gains and useful qualitative visualizations, but the choice of source/target datasets lacks clear motivation.  
- Suggest evaluating with a leave-one-dataset-out adaptation protocol as a potentially more appropriate benchmark.  
- Describing the problem as unsupervised (line 074) is misleading since semantic class labels are used.  
- Overall: technical contribution is notable, but conceptual definitions and certain experimental details require clarification.
