- Addresses automatic discovery of latent domains in images/videos by partitioning data to maximize distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Formulates domain discovery as an integer program with single-assignment and label-distribution constraints, relaxed to a continuous optimization; selects number of domains via domain-wise cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on object recognition and IXMAS activity datasets, showing improvements over prior domain adaptation method [19].  
- Novel contribution: discovers domains rather than relying on pre-defined dataset proxies, which is valuable for domain adaptation.  
- Paper is clearly written and the reported performance gains are encouraging.  
- Reviewer initially leaned to reject due to weak motivation for avoiding simple clustering and unclear necessity of components, but shifted to slight acceptance after rebuttal.  
- Concern (i): initial argument that clustering yields per-category domains was under-supported; rebuttal clarified that the distinctiveness objective discourages per-category partitioning.  
- Concern (ii): the label prior constraint (LPC) seemed unnecessary at first; rebuttal showed LPC acts as a regularizer and yields slight empirical improvement.  
- Rebuttal clarified that “distribution” refers to the joint distribution of features and labels.  
- Minor technical clarifications: simplex variable definition confirmed, constraint discrepancy was a typo, Kmax bound justification acceptable, and geodesic flow kernel used public code.  
- Rebuttal provided nuanced explanations for observed performance differences, emphasizing capture of within-category variations.  
- Typographical correction noted: "M'k" changed to "Mk'".  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, consistent empirical improvements, clarity after rebuttal, and sound theoretical/optimization approach.  
- Remaining concerns (weaker): optimization complexity may hinder reproducibility and baseline comparisons were limited (e.g., k-means), though rebuttal addressed some of this.  
- Overall recommendation: acceptance.
