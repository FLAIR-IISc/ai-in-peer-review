- Goal: Automatically discover latent domains in a training set to improve domain adaptation for test-set classification.  
- Method: Proposes a kernel-based pairwise feature-difference function and partitions data to maximize inter-domain differences.  
- Formulation: Casts domain discovery as an integer program with constraints that each point belongs to one domain and each domain matches the dataset label prior.  
- Optimization: Relaxes the integer program to a continuous quadratic program with linear constraints; selects the number of domains via cross-validation.  
- Evaluation: Tested on a static-image dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset, outperforming the baseline from [19].  
- Strengths: Clear writing, novel approach, and measurable performance gains over the compared baseline.  
- Major concern 1: Motivation unclear—no evidence that appearance-based clustering causes per-category domains or explanation of how the model prevents this.  
- Major concern 2: Necessity of the label-prior constraint is questionable; authors should show results without it and compare to simpler partitioning baselines.  
- Suggested baselines/ablations: k-means on appearance vectors, unsupervised object discovery (Sivic et al. ICCV 2005), and random equal partitioning.  
- Clarification requests: Define “maximally different in distribution” precisely (which distribution is meant?).  
- Technical comments: Note that β_{mk} appears to lie on a simplex already and point out inconsistent constraints between formulations on lines 153 and 182.  
- Theoretical request: Provide justification or proof for the bound referenced on line 215.  
- Implementation detail: State whether the geodesic flow kernel [4] was reimplemented or used from public code.  
- Analysis request: Explain why the proposed method outperforms baselines and identify systematic failure modes of those baselines.  
- Miscellaneous: Correct notation in Eq.(1) from M'k to Mk' and include Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) citation.  
- Final recommendation: After rebuttal addressing motivation and label-prior concerns, reviewer leans slightly toward acceptance.
