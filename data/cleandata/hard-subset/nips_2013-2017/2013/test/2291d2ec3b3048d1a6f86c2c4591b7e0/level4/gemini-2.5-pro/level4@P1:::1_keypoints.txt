- Paper proposes automatic discovery of latent visual domains to improve domain adaptation by maximizing domain distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Method maximizes total pairwise distribution differences via a kernel metric, formulates an integer program, relaxes it to a continuous QP, and enforces a label-prior constraint (LPC); K chosen by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV).  
- Strong empirical results: thorough experiments showing significant gains over adapting from monolithic datasets and outperforming prior work [20].  
- Novel formulation: discovering latent domains for domain adaptation is an important, underexplored problem and the objective is well motivated.  
- Primary concern: the motivation against simple clustering lacks empirical support—need evidence that k-means on features collapses to per-class clusters.  
- Unclear how maximizing distribution distinctiveness prevents collapse into per-category domains; LPC appears to be the main mechanism, raising questions.  
- LPC is a strong assumption requiring justification: intuition for enforcing global label priors per domain is missing and may be unrealistic.  
- Need ablation: report performance without LPC to quantify its impact.  
- Need baselines: include standard clustering (e.g., k-means on features) and random partition baselines to establish performance floors and isolate the benefit of the objective vs constraints.  
- Clarify that "maximally different in distribution" refers to feature distribution P(x) early in the paper.  
- Clarify relaxation steps: explain why βmk lives on the simplex given βmk = zmk/Mk and detail derivation of relaxed constraints (how (5) follows from (3)).  
- Provide justification or proof for the K bound Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}.  
- Reproducibility: specify whether geodesic flow kernel implementation was author-provided or public.  
- Add qualitative analysis: show examples of baseline failures that the method corrects to explain why it helps.  
- Minor fixes: typo in Eq. (1) (M'k → Mk') and add suggested citation Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011).  
- Reviewer leans toward rejection pending rebuttal that addresses motivation, LPC necessity, ablations, and additional baselines.
