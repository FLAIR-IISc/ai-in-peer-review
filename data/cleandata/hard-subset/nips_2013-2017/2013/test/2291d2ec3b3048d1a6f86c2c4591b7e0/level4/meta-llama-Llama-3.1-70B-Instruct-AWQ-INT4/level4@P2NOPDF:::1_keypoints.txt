- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains in training data and using them in a domain adaptation framework to improve test-set classification.  
- Method defines a kernel-based difference function between feature vectors and partitions data to maximize this function across domain pairs.  
- Formulation: integer program with two constraints (exclusive domain assignment; per-domain label distribution matches global prior), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Evaluated on two datasets (static images from [2] and IXMAS multi-view action [15]) and outperforms the domain adaptation method of [19]; paper is well-written and method appears novel.  
- Main concern 1: motivation unclear — claim that clustering by appearance produces per-category domains lacks evidence and it’s unclear how the model prevents such reshaping.  
- Main concern 2: necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear; need results without it and with simple baselines (k-means, unsupervised object discovery like Sivic et al. ICCV ’05, and random equal partitions).  
- Request clarification of “maximally different in distribution”: specify which distribution is being referred to.  
- Note that β_mk as formulated already lies on a simplex — clarify or correct.  
- Point out inconsistency between constraints at line 182 and the formulation starting on line 153 — reconcile them.  
- Ask for justification or proof for the bound stated on line 215.  
- Ask for details on the geodesic flow kernel [4] implementation (reimplementation vs. public code).  
- Request analysis explaining performance differences (lines 313/340), including systematic failures of baselines that the proposed method overcomes.  
- Minor corrections/suggestions: typo in Eq. (1) (M'k => Mk') and add citation for dataset bias (Torralba & Efros, CVPR 2011).  
- Reviewer stance: initially leaned toward rejection but after rebuttal would slightly lean toward acceptance if motivation and label-prior concerns are addressed.
