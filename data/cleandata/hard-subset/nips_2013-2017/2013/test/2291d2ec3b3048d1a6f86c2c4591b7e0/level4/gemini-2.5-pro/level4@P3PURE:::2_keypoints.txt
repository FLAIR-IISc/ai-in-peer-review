- Proposes automatic partitioning of pooled images into new domains, rather than equating domains with datasets, to reflect true data-generating factors.  
- Partitioning guided by two principles: maximize separability in feature space and ensure each domain supports a high-performing discriminative classifier.  
- Novel and significant contribution: underexplored problem of domain discovery and optimization for downstream classification.  
- Concern: proposed optimization method (Section 2) lacks sufficient justification and unclear approximation to objectives in equations (2) and (3).  
- Concern: separability and learnability criteria are not well integrated; learnability is used only to select domain count post-hoc rather than in a unified objective.  
- Recommendation: more principled objective that directly combines separability and learnability would strengthen the method.  
- Writing is generally clear, but inclusion of illustrative figures (e.g., for the partitioning process) would improve clarity.  
- Experiments show promising performance gains over baselines and a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Issue: use of the test set in reshaping (Section 4.3) is unclear and implies a transductive setting that is not stated or discussed.  
- Major weakness: reliance on outdated image features (sparse SURF BoW with 800-word codebook) weakens claims; stronger features needed.  
- Recommendation: adopt spatial pyramid/dense features, appropriate histogram similarity measures (Chi-squared/intersection), or Fisher V representations; suggested references provided.  
- Overall: paper makes a valuable conceptual contribution and merits acceptance despite methodological and experimental weaknesses.
