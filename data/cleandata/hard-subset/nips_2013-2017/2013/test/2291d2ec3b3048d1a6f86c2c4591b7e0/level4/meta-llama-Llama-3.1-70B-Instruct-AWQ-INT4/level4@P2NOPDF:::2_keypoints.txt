- Proposes automatic partitioning of images across datasets into domains using two principles: separability in feature space and per-domain learnability for original classes.  
- Novel contribution: automatic domain discovery is valuable and timely for domain adaptation.  
- Optimization concern: Section 2’s method is not convincing and it’s unclear how closely it solves the original objective (2)+(3).  
- Integration issue: the two criteria (maximal separability and maximal learnability) are poorly integrated—learnability is used post-hoc to pick domain count while partitioning uses only separability.  
- Suggested improvement: a joint objective that directly maximizes both separability and learnability would be more principled.  
- Overall recommendation: despite limitations, the work is a useful exploratory step and should be accepted.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written but would benefit from figures to illustrate concepts.  
- Experimental result: domains produced by the method outperform using datasets-as-domains and a recent domain discovery baseline [19].  
- Test-set issue: including the test set in the ‘reshaping’ process is interesting but presented unclearly and effectively treats the problem as an imputation setting—this should be clarified.  
- Representation critique: the image descriptor (single bag-of-words of sparse SURF, 800 codebook entries) is simplistic and outdated.  
- Representation suggestions: use spatial pyramid bag-of-words on dense SURF, appropriate histogram similarities (χ2 or intersection kernel), or Fisher Vectors to be competitive (see Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronin ECCV 2010).  
- Summary: novel and interesting results, but improvement needed in optimization, criterion integration, clarity about test usage, and modernizing image representation.
