- Problem: Automatically discover latent domains within visual datasets to improve domain adaptation.  
- Motivation: Datasets treated as single domains is a flawed simplification because real datasets are heterogeneous (lighting, pose, background).  
- Proposal: Partition datasets into latent domains that maximize distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Distinctiveness: Formulate an optimization to maximize distributional difference between discovered domains using a non-parametric RKHS kernel metric.  
- Learnability: Choose the number of domains K by selecting the K with highest cross-validation accuracy on partitioned data.  
- Optimization: Original integer program is NP-hard and relaxed to a continuous quadratic program for tractability.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on object and human activity recognition; using discovered domains with Geodesic Flow Kernel improves performance over original datasets and a competing method [20].  

Strengths:
- Originality/Significance: Addresses the fundamental question of what defines a domain and offers a principled preprocessing approach for multi-source adaptation.  
- Technical Quality: Well-motivated non-parametric metric and reasonable relaxation from integer program to continuous QP.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and organized.  
- Experimental Validation: Shows empirical gains on standard benchmarks and improves over at least one competing latent-domain method.  

Weaknesses / Major Questions:
- Need empirical evidence that appearance-based clustering produces per-category domains; include k-means baseline to support the central claim.  
- Role of Label Prior Constraint (LPC) unclear; ablation study required to show necessity and effect (what happens without LPC?).  
- Missing baseline: random equal-sized partitioning should be included to establish a lower bound.  
- Limited analysis: paper lacks qualitative examples and detailed error analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines and what failure modes are addressed.  

Minor Comments / Clarifications Requested:
- Clarify that "maximally different in distribution" refers to marginal P(x).  
- Clarify constraint details: whether βmk is already simplex-constrained and the derivation link between Eq.(3) and Eq.(5).  
- Justify or prove the bound for Kmax reported at line 215.  
- Provide implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel (public code or reimplementation).  
- Typo: M'k likely should be M_k'.  
- Suggest citing Torralba & Efros (2011) on dataset bias.  

Conclusion:
- Overall promising and novel but incomplete due to missing baselines and ablations; reviewer leans toward rejection but may reconsider if major concerns are addressed with additional experiments and analyses.
