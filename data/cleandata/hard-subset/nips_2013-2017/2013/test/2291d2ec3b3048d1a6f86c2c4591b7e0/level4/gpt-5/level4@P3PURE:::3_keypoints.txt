- Paper proposes a convex framework to partition data into K latent domains by encouraging similar class distributions within domains and maximizing pairwise mean differences in an RKHS for distinctiveness.  
- K is chosen by maximizing average learnability, i.e., within-domain classifier performance.  
- Novelty: explicitly controlling class-label distributions for latent domain discovery within a relaxed convex framework is technically interesting.  
- Sensible practice: selecting K based on within-domain classification quality helps ensure sufficient per-class samples for learning.  
- Concern: enforcing similar class distributions within latent domains can be restrictive and harm discovery when class–pose or class–domain correlations differ.  
- Need clearer definitions and consistent terminology for dataset, domain, and latent domain (Section 4.2 uses terms inconsistently).  
- Notational issue: max_k r(U_k, B) in Equation (7) is used but not defined.  
- Empirically, results show reasonable improvements and helpful qualitative examples.  
- Evaluation concern: motivation for chosen source/target dataset pairs is unclear; a leave-one-dataset-out protocol may be more systematic.  
- Minor: calling latent domain learning “unsupervised” is misleading since semantic class labels are used.  
- Overall: contribution is notable, but paper needs clearer concept definitions, resolved notation, and a more justified/broader evaluation protocol.
