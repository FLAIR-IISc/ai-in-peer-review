- Paper addresses latent domain discovery for better domain adaptation using two principles: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability, operationalized via RKHS mean embeddings and a relaxed integer quadratic program; K chosen by domain-wise CV; conditional test reshaping proposed.

- Experiments on object and cross-view action recognition show improvements over baselines and a prior latent-domain method [20], using geodesic flow kernel adaptation.

- Formulation is sensible, relaxation standard, DWCV heuristic reasonable, and empirical results promising.

- Need empirical evidence that naive appearance clustering collapses to per-category clusters (e.g., cluster purity metrics).

- Need explanation/ablation showing how the proposed objective prevents per-category clustering and whether prevention stems from the label prior or distinctiveness over P(x).

- Necessity and impact of the label prior constraint must be quantified; report performance without it.

- Additional baselines should be included: k-means on appearance features, random equal-size partitions, and unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al. ICCV’05).

- Clarify whether “maximally different in distribution” targets Pk(x) or Pk(x,y); current distance measures P(x) via kernel mean embeddings while P(x,y) is mentioned but unused.

- Align notation and constraints between integer and relaxed formulations; explicitly state βmk’s domain (simplex) constraints and reconcile inconsistencies.

- Clarify relaxation details and bounds (e.g., 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C), and justify the bound Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C} for N-fold CV.

- Provide implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel: reimplementation vs. public code, hyperparameters, and deviations from original.

- Analyze and explain why discovered domains improve adaptation: failure cases of baselines, what factors (pose, background, illumination) are captured.

- Fix minor notation issues (e.g., use Mk′ in Eq.1) and align line-referenced constraints (lines 153 vs 182).

- Suggested citation: Torralba and Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.

- Overall originality: combination of nonparametric RKHS distinctiveness, relaxed quadratic program with label prior, and DWCV is novel relative to parametric-mixture prior work.

- Significance: method could make adaptation more robust when datasets ≠ domains; conditional reshaping is practically relevant.

- Pros: clear problem setup, nonparametric distance, reasonable relaxation and K selection, empirical gains, outperforms prior [20] and dataset-bias baseline.

- Cons/questions reiterated for rebuttal: motivate avoidance of naive clustering with quantitative evidence; show necessity of label prior; compare to suggested baselines.

- Post-rebuttal: authors’ rebuttal addressed motivation and label prior concerns, reviewer now leans slightly toward acceptance.
