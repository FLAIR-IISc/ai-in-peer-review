- Goal: Automatically discover latent training domains and use them in domain adaptation to improve test-time classification.  
- Method: Define a kernel-based discrepancy between feature vectors and partition data to maximize inter-domain discrepancy.  
- Formulation: Integer program with two constraints (each point in one domain; per-domain class-label distribution matches global), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on a static image dataset and IXMAS multi-view action dataset; outperforms prior domain adaptation method [19].  
- Positive: Paper is clearly written and presents a seemingly novel approach; empirical gains are appreciated.  
- Main concern (motivation): Claim that appearance clustering leads to per-category domains lacks supporting evidence; need explanation of what prevents discovered domains from collapsing into per-category groupings.  
- Main concern (label prior): Need justification for the label-prior constraint, ablation results without it, and comparisons to simple baselines (k-means, unsupervised object discovery, random equal partitions).  
- Clarification request: Specify what “maximally different in distribution” refers to (feature/kernel distribution, labels, or other).  
- Technical query: Does β_{mk} already lie on a simplex as formulated?  
- Formulation consistency: Reconcile constraints stated around line 153 with those at line 182 (they appear different).  
- Theoretical detail: Provide justification or proof for the bound mentioned at line 215.  
- Implementation detail: Specify how the geodesic flow kernel [4] was used—reimplemented or public code?  
- Analysis request: Explain why the proposed method outperforms baselines and show systematic failure modes of baselines that this method avoids.  
- Typo: In Eq. (1), M'k should be Mk'.  
- Suggested citation: Add Torralba & Efros, “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias,” CVPR 2011.  
- Post-rebuttal: Rebuttal addressed the motivation and label-prior concerns; reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
