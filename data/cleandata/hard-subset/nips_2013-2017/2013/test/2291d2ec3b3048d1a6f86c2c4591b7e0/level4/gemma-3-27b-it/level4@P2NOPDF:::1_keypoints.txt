- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains for domain adaptation using a kernel-based dissimilarity and partitioning that is relaxed from integer programming to a continuous quadratic program, with domain count via cross-validation.

- Evaluated on a static image dataset and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset, showing improvements over baseline method [19].

- Strength: paper is clearly written and presents a novel contribution with encouraging performance gains over [19].

- Weakness: reviewer initially leans toward rejection but is open to reconsideration after a strong rebuttal.

- Major concern (i): motivation is unclear—need empirical evidence that simple appearance clustering yields per-category domains and explanation of how the proposed formulation prevents per-category domains.

- Major concern (ii): necessity of the label-prior constraint is not justified—request ablation without it and comparisons to simple baselines (k-means on appearance, unsupervised object discovery, random equal partitions).

- Clarify what specific "distribution" is meant by "maximally different in distribution from each other" (Line 82).

- Check whether β_mk already lies on a simplex as defined (Line 166).

- Reconcile apparent inconsistencies between the constraints in Section starting Line 153 and those at Line 182.

- Provide justification or proof for the stated bound (Line 215).

- Give implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel: reimplementation vs. publicly available code (Line 289).

- Provide deeper analysis of why the method outperforms baselines, including systematic failure cases of baselines (Lines 313/340).

- Correct apparent typo in Eq (1): M'_k likely should be M_k'.

- Suggest citing Torralba and Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias," CVPR 2011 for context.

- Post-rebuttal update: rebuttal addressed motivation and label-prior concerns, and the reviewer now slightly leans toward acceptance.
