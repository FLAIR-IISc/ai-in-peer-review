- Introduces a convex optimization method to partition a dataset into K “latent domains” that are internally semantically similar and maximally distinct from each other.  
- Distinctiveness is quantified as maximum distinctiveness via maximization of pairwise mean differences in an RKHS defined by a kernel.  
- The optimal K is chosen by maximizing average learnability/classification quality within each domain to ensure sufficient per-class samples.  
- Enforcing similar class distributions inside domains avoids single-class dominance but may hinder discovery when natural latent factors produce differing class distributions (pose example).  
- Novel contributions: controlling class-label distributions in a convex framework and using per-domain classification quality to select K.  
- Terminology is ambiguous—“dataset,” “domain,” and “latent domain” are used imprecisely; S_i’s meaning (dataset vs. source domain) is unclear and needs examples.  
- Equation/notation issues: terms like max_k r(U_k,B) appear without prior definition.  
- Experiments show reasonable gains and useful qualitative results, but the choice of source/target datasets lacks justification; a more comprehensive evaluation (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out) is recommended.  
- Framing the problem as purely unsupervised is misleading because the method relies on semantic class labels.
