- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains via a kernel-based distribution difference function, used for domain adaptation to improve test classification.
- Formulates domain discovery as an integer program with constraints (one domain per point; per-domain class distribution matches overall), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; selects number of domains via domain-wise cross-validation.
- Evaluated on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and human activity (IXMAS) datasets, showing improved performance over baseline [19].
- Strength: paper is well-written, appears novel, and reports significant, well-documented performance gains.
- Weakness: unclear motivation — claim that appearance clustering yields per-category domains needs evidence and explanation of how the method avoids this.
- Weakness: label prior constraint (LPC) is not well-justified; request comparisons without LPC and baselines like k-means, unsupervised object discovery (Sivic ICCV'05), and random equal partitions.
- Clarify what “distribution” refers to in “maximally different in distribution” (which features/variables).
- Point out redundancy: βmk already lies on a simplex, so a related statement is unnecessary.
- Note apparent inconsistency between constraints stated at lines 153 and 182; request clarification.
- Ask for justification or proof of the bound Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}.
- Request details on geodesic flow kernel implementation (reimplementation vs. public code).
- Ask for deeper analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines, including systematic failure analysis of competing methods.
- Minor fixes: correct M'k to Mk' in Eq.(1) and consider adding Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) reference.
- Rebuttal addressed concerns about motivation and LPC; reviewer’s stance moved from likely rejection to leaning slightly toward acceptance.
