- Proposes a novel method that automatically partitions images across datasets into domains based on feature separability and per-domain class learnability.  
- Avoids equating domains with datasets, aiming to discover underlying factors like viewpoint or illumination.  
- Original and significant contribution given limited prior work on automatic domain definition; paper merits acceptance.  
- Concern: optimization method (Section 2) is unclear and may not effectively solve the joint problem posed by criteria (2)+(3).  
- Concern: the two driving criteria are not cohesively integrated—separability drives partitioning while learnability is used post-hoc to choose domain count.  
- Suggestion: directly optimize a unified objective that balances separability and maximal learnability.  
- Paper is well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments show the discovered domains outperform using datasets-as-domains and a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- The use of the test set in "reshaping" is intriguing but poorly explained; it resembles an imputation setting and should be explicitly stated.  
- Major negative: image descriptor is outdated (sparse SURF bag-of-words, K=800); recommend dense SURF + spatial pyramid or Fisher Vectors for modern performance.  
- Specify and use an appropriate histogram similarity (e.g., χ² or intersection kernel) rather than Euclidean distance; cite relevant prior work (Zhang IJCV 2006, Jurie CVPR 2005, Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronnin ECCV 2010).
