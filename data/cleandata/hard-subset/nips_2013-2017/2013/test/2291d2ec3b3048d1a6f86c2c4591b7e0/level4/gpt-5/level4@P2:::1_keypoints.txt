- Proposes unsupervised discovery of latent visual domains that are maximally distinctive and maximally learnable, used for domain adaptation.
- Distinctiveness measured by a kernel-based distance between empirical distributions (MMD-like) summed over domain pairs; learnability enforced by training classifiers per domain.
- Domain assignment originally an integer program with constraints: one domain per instance and label proportions per domain match global label prior (LPC).
- Relaxation to a quadratic objective with linear constraints solved by off-the-shelf solvers; K chosen by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) weighted by domain size.
- Shows empirical improvements on Saenko et al. object datasets and IXMAS action dataset, outperforming several baselines including [19].
- Clear and relevant problem formulation addressing dataset ≠ domain in vision.
- Nonparametric distinctiveness avoids risks of misspecified mixture models.
- Jointly considers distinctiveness and learnability with a practical DWCV procedure for selecting K.
- Writing is clear and methods are described with sufficient detail for reimplementation.
- Reviewer initially leaned toward reject due to gaps but is open to change if addressed.
- Requests empirical evidence that appearance clustering produces per-category domains and underperforms for adaptation.
- Asks for concrete explanation of how the objective avoids per-category clustering beyond the LPC mechanism.
- Requests justification for LPC, plus ablation showing performance without LPC.
- Requests additional baselines: K-means on appearance, unsupervised object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al.), and random equal-sized partitions.
- Requests several clarifications: explicit statement of "distribution over x," relation to MMD, and notation/constraint reconciliation (β simplex, relaxed bounds, Mk ≥ C).
- Asks for justification of DWCV Kmax bound (Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}).
- Requests implementation details for geodesic flow kernel (dimensions, bandwidths, public code vs reimplementation).
- Requests analysis of why method outperforms baselines and typical failure modes of baselines that the method overcomes.
- Notes minor typo (M'k → Mk') and suggests citing Torralba & Efros 2011 for dataset-bias context.
- Pros: addresses practical domain adaptation issue, simple nonparametric approach, demonstrated improvements.
- Cons: insufficient motivation/evidence for avoiding per-class domains, dependence on LPC without ablations, and underexplained technical constraints.
- Post-rebuttal: rebuttal satisfactorily addressed motivation and LPC role; reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
