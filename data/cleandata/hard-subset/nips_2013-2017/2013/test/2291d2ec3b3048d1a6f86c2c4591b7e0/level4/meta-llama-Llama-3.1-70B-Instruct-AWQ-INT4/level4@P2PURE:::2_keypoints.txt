- Proposes automatic partitioning of images across datasets into domains based on separating domains in feature space and ensuring each domain is learnable for original classes.
- Novel contribution addressing an underexplored topic with potential to improve classifier training.
- Concern that the proposed optimization (Section 2) is unclear and may not closely approximate the original problem (equations (2)+(3)).
- The two driving criteria are poorly integrated: maximal separability is used for partitioning while maximal learnability is only a post-hoc validation for domain count.
- An integrated objective that combines both criteria directly would be preferable.
- Recommendation to accept despite exploratory limitations, as it moves the field forward.
- Paper is well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to improve clarity.
- Experiments show significant gains over using datasets as domains and outperform recent domain discovery method [19] (Section 4.2).
- Incorporating the test set in the reshaping process (Section 4.3) is interesting but poorly explained and effectively assumes an imputation setting that is not stated.
- Image descriptor is outdated and overly simplistic: single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords is weak by current standards.
- Strong suggestion to use spatial pyramids, dense SURF, and Fisher Vectors with references (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronin ECCV 2010).
- Unclear what similarity measure is used; histogram comparisons should use χ² or intersection kernel instead of Euclidean distance.
- Overall: novel and interesting with good results, but improvements needed in optimization clarity and image representation.
