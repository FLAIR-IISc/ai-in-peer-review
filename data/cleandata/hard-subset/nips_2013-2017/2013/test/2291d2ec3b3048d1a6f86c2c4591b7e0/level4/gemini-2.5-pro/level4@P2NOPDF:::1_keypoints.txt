- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains by partitioning training data to maximize a kernel-based distance between feature distributions for domain adaptation.
- Strength: paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Strength: the approach of maximizing distributional difference under a label-consistency constraint appears novel.
- Strength: empirical results show clear performance gains over the baseline from [19].
- Major concern: unclear motivation—need empirical/theoretical evidence that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains and explanation of how the model prevents this.
- Major concern: role and necessity of the label-prior constraint are insufficiently justified; request ablation and baseline comparisons to assess its impact.
- Requested baselines: remove label prior constraint (ablation), k-means on appearance features, a sophisticated unsupervised object-discovery method (e.g., Sivic et al. 2005), and random equal-size partitioning.
- Comment: clarify what "maximally different in distribution" refers to (features, labels, etc.).
- Comment: clarify whether β_mk already satisfies a simplex constraint making the explicit constraint redundant.
- Comment: explain apparent discrepancy between constraints stated around lines 153 and 182.
- Comment: provide justification or proof for the bound mentioned on line 215.
- Comment: detail usage/implementation of the geodesic flow kernel (public implementation or re-implemented).
- Comment: strengthen result analysis with qualitative examples showing why the method outperforms baselines and systematic failure modes it fixes.
- Minor: typo in Eq (1)—`M'k` likely should be `Mk'`.
- Suggestion: consider citing "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (Torralba & Efros, CVPR 2011).
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed main concerns about motivation and label prior; reviewer updated score and now leans toward acceptance.
