- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition combined image sets into meaningful domains based on underlying variation factors (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, compression).  
- Partitioning is guided by two principles: maximize separability in feature space and ensure each domain supports a high-performing discriminative classifier (learnability).  
- Paper is an original and valuable contribution to automatic domain discovery and is deemed worthy of acceptance.  
- The connection between the proposed optimization method and the original objective (equations (2)+(3)) is unclear.  
- The separability and learnability criteria are not well integrated; learnability currently acts as a wrapper that post-hoc determines the number of domains.  
- Recommends a more integrated objective that jointly optimizes separability and learnability rather than a two-step procedure.  
- Paper is well-written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify key concepts.  
- Experiments show significant gains over treating datasets as domains and outperform a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- The “reshaping” process includes the test set, which is not clearly explained and corresponds to an imputation setting that should be explicitly stated.  
- The image descriptor is outdated: single bag-of-words on sparse SURF with an 800-codebook is a significant weakness relative to modern methods.  
- Recommends improvements: spatial pyramid bag-of-words on dense SURF, χ² or intersection kernels for histogram comparison (not Euclidean), and exploring Fisher Vectors for stronger representations.  
- Suggests citing relevant prior work for better image representations (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).
