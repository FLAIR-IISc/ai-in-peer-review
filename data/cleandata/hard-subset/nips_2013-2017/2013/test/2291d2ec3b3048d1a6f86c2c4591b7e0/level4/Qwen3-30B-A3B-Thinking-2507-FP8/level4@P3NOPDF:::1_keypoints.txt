- Paper proposes discovering latent domains by partitioning training data to maximize kernel-based dissimilarity, formulated as an integer program with a label-prior constraint, relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization, and evaluated with CV for number of domains.  
- Positives: clearly written, appears novel, technically well-structured, CV selection for domain count justified, shows meaningful performance gains over baseline [19].  

Major concerns:
- Motivation lacking empirical support: no evidence that naive appearance clustering produces per-category domains on their datasets.  
- Unclear mechanism: paper does not explain how the label-prior constraint prevents domain partitions from aligning with class boundaries.  
- Necessity of label-prior constraint not demonstrated: need ablation showing performance without the constraint.  
- Missing baseline comparisons: include standard unsupervised partitioning (e.g., k-means or unsupervised object discovery) to isolate contribution of the constraint.  
- Missing random partition baseline: include equal-sized random partitions to establish a lower-bound performance.  

Minor comments / clarifications needed:
- Clarify "maximally different in distribution": specify which distribution and across what.  
- Explicitly state β_{mk} simplex constraint (Eq.12).  
- Reconcile differing constraint statements (Lines 153 vs. 182).  
- Provide justification/proof for the derived bound (Line 215).  
- Describe geodesic flow kernel implementation details (reimplementation vs. public code, parameters).  
- Provide deeper analysis of why the method outperforms baselines and identify failure modes of [19] or k-means.  
- Fix typo in Equation (1): "M'k" → "M_k'".  
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) on dataset bias.  

Recommendation:
- Current verdict: weak reject due to unsubstantiated motivation and missing baseline/ablation studies; would lean toward acceptance if authors provide compelling rebuttal and additional experiments.
