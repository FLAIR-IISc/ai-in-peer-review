- Proposes a novel method that automatically partitions images across datasets into domains rather than equating domains with datasets.  
- Partitioning is driven by two principles: creating separate domains in feature space and ensuring each domain supports a discriminative classifier for the original classes.  
- Originality/significance: contributes to the limited literature on automatic domain discovery and can help train better classifiers.  
- Concern: the proposed optimization (Section 2) is unclear and it’s not shown how closely it solves the original formulation (2)+(3).  
- Methodological issue: the two criteria (maximal separability and maximal learnability) are not well integrated—learnability is used as a wrapper/post-hoc validation rather than part of the partitioning objective.  
- Suggestion: a more integrated objective that jointly maximizes separability and learnability would be preferable.  
- Recommendation: despite shortcomings, the work is a promising exploratory contribution and warrants acceptance.  
- Presentation: paper is well written but lacks illustrative figures to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments: show significant advantage of discovered domains over using datasets as domains and over the recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Test-set handling: including the test set in the “reshaping” is intriguing but unclear; corresponds to an imputation setting that is not explicitly stated.  
- Visual representation critique: image descriptor is outdated (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codebook entries); authors should use spatial pyramid + dense SURF, appropriate similarity kernels (χ2 or intersection), or Fisher Vectors.  
- Overall: interesting and novel ideas with good results, but room for improvement especially in image representation and tighter integration of objectives.
