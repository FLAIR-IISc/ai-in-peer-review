- Proposes automatic partitioning of combined image sets into meaningful domains based on maximal separability in feature space and maximal learnability (trainable classifier per domain).
- Objective is to discover domains reflecting underlying factors of variation (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, compression) instead of using dataset boundaries.
- Novel and significant contribution to the limited literature on automatic domain definition; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Concern: optimization method (Section 2) is unclear—uncertain how closely the solution approximates the original objective ((2)+(3)).
- Concern: the two criteria (maximal learnability and separability) are not fully integrated; learnability currently acts as a post-hoc wrapper to pick the number of domains.
- Ideal approach would directly maximize a combined objective incorporating both criteria simultaneously.
- Paper is generally well written but would benefit from illustrative figures to clarify concepts.
- Experiments show clear advantage of the proposed domains over using datasets as domains and over prior domain discovery method [19].
- Using the test set in the ‘reshaping’ process is interesting but under-explained and implicitly assumes an imputation setting (all test data available at once).
- Image descriptor is outdated and weak: single bag-of-words of sparse SURF with only 800 codebook entries.
- Recommend stronger representations/similarity measures: dense SURF with spatial pyramid, χ² or intersection kernel for histograms, or Fisher Vectors; cite Zhang IJCV 2006, Jurie CVPR 2005, Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronnin ECCV 2010.
- Overall promising and improves classifier performance but could be strengthened by integrating objectives and using modern image representations.
