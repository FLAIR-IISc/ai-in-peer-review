- Proposes a method to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets for domain adaptation, arguing datasets ≠ domains in real-world settings.  
- Key insight: latent domains should maximize distinctiveness (different distributions) and learnability (support strong discriminative models).  
- Formulates domain discovery as a nonparametric optimization with a label prior constraint (LPC) and uses domain-wise cross-validation to select the number of domains.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, using kernel-based distribution comparison in RKHS to define domain distinctiveness.  
- Empirical evaluation on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and IXMAS action datasets shows consistent improvements over treating datasets as single domains.  
- Reshaping datasets into discovered latent domains yields substantially better adaptation performance, notably when using the MATCHING source-selection strategy.  
- Qualitative analysis (Figure 1) indicates discovered domains align with meaningful visual factors like pose and background.  
- Well-motivated, well-written, and technically solid with comprehensive experiments demonstrating practical utility.  
- Major concern 1: No concrete experimental evidence that standard clustering (e.g., k-means on appearance features) yields per-category clusters rather than domain-relevant clusters.  
- Suggestion: include a simple experiment showing k-means clusters by category and how the proposed method overcomes this.  
- Major concern 2: The necessity and impact of the label prior constraint (LPC) are not justified or ablated.  
- Suggestion: provide experiments comparing performance with and without LPC to show it is essential, not a technical convenience.  
- Additional suggestion: include baselines comparing to k-means on appearance features and random partitioning to isolate the benefit of the proposed domain discovery.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the above experimental concerns; authors’ responses will influence final decision.
