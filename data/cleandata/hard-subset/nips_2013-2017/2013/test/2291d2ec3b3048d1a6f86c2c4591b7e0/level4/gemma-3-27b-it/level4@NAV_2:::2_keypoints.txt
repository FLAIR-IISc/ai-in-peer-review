- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition image datasets into latent domains by maximizing domain distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Moves beyond treating entire datasets as domains to discover domains aligned with underlying factors (viewpoint, illumination, clutter).  
- Originality: tackles the fundamental and underexplored question of “what constitutes a domain” in domain adaptation.  
- Strength: compelling core idea that leverages distinctiveness and learnability to define domains.  
- Empirical strength: experiments show clear advantage over using original datasets as domains and outperform Hoffman et al. [20].  
- Extension: the conditional reshaping of test datasets (including test data in reshaping) is a promising direction.  
- Weakness: optimization method (continuous relaxation vs. original integer program) is insufficiently clarified.  
- Weakness: distinctiveness and learnability appear decoupled; learnability is mainly used as a validation metric rather than integrated into partitioning.  
- Suggestion: develop a more unified objective that directly incorporates both distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but would benefit from illustrative figures showing discovered domains.  
- Experiments: overall convincing, but presentation of the conditional reshaping/imputation setting should be clearer about assumptions.  
- Image representation weakness: reliance on sparse SURF bag-of-words with small codebook is outdated; recommend spatial pyramids, dense features, Fisher Vectors, and better similarity measures (χ² or intersection).  
- Recommend citing relevant prior works on improved representations (Zhang IJCV 2006, Jurie CVPR 2005, Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronnin ECCV 2010).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing image representation issues and clarifying/integrating the optimization strategy.
