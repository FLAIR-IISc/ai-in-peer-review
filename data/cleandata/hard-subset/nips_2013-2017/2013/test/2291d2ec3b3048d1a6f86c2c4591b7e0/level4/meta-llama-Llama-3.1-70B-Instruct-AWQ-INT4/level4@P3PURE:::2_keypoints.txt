- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition images across datasets into domains based on underlying aspects (viewpoint, illumination, clutter, etc.) rather than equating domains with datasets.  
- Partitioning is driven by two principles: maximizing separability in feature space and ensuring each domain supports a good discriminative classifier for the original classes.  
- Originality/significance: novel, relatively unexplored idea with potential to improve classifiers; paper merits acceptance despite exploratory limitations.  
- Methodological concern: optimization in Section 2 is unconvincing—unclear how closely it solves the original objective (eqs. 2+3).  
- Integration concern: the two criteria are poorly integrated; maximal learnability is used as a post-hoc wrapper/validation for separability rather than included in a unified objective function.  
- Suggestion: develop a partitioning that directly maximizes a combined goal function incorporating both separability and learnability.  
- Clarity: paper is well written but would benefit from figures to illustrate concepts.  
- Experiments: show clear advantage over using datasets as domains and over prior domain discovery [19]; including the test set in reshaping is interesting but presented unclearly and resembles an imputation setting that isn’t stated.  
- Representation weakness: uses an outdated, simplistic image descriptor (single BoW of sparse SURF, 800 codebook); recommends spatial pyramid of dense SURF, proper histogram kernels (χ² or intersection), or Fisher Vectors, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Overall: interesting and novel contributions with good results, but improvements needed in optimization integration and image representation.
