- Proposal: unsupervised latent-domain discovery maximizing a kernel-based dissimilarity, cast as an integer program relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with cross-validated domain count.  
- Evaluation: tested on static image datasets and IXMAS, showing improvements over domain-adaptation baseline [19].  
- Strength: paper is well-written, appears novel, and demonstrates clear performance gains over [19].  
- Major concern (motivation): Claim that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains is unsupported by evidence.  
- Major concern (mechanism): Unclear how the label-distribution constraint prevents per-category domains beyond matching label frequencies—needs explanation.  
- Major concern (necessity): The label-prior constraint’s necessity is unproven; an ablation study removing the constraint is required.  
- Baselines requested: compare against simpler alternatives (k-means on appearance features, unsupervised object discovery such as Sivic et al. 2005, and random equal-sized partitions).  
- Clarify language: specify what “maximally different in distribution” means (feature distribution, label distribution, or other).  
- Variable/constraint checks: confirm β_{mk} is enforced as a simplex and reconcile differing constraint statements (Lines 153–155 vs. Line 182).  
- Theoretical detail: provide justification/proof for the bound stated at Line 215 and correct notation in Eq. (1) (M'k → M_k').  
- Implementation detail: describe geodesic flow kernel implementation (code reuse vs. original) and explain why the method outperforms baselines (identify systematic failure modes of [19] or k-means).  
- Recommendation: currently leaning to reject unless authors provide compelling evidence for the motivation, ablation results for the label prior, and comparisons to the requested baselines—will reconsider if addressed.
