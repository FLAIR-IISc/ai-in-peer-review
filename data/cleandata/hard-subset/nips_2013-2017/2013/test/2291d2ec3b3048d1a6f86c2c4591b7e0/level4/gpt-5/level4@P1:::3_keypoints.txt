- Paper tackles discovering K latent domains in visual datasets to improve domain adaptation by enforcing maximum distinctiveness and learnability.
- Distinctiveness is measured nonparametrically via kernel two-sample statistics (RKHS mean embeddings / MMD).
- A label prior constraint prevents trivial per-class clusters by enforcing domain-level class proportions match the global prior.
- Domain assignments are relaxed to continuous simplex variables and optimized despite a hard max-quadratic objective.
- K is selected by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV), choosing domains that are both distinct and predictive.
- Experiments on object and cross-view action recognition show consistent improvements over original datasets and prior latent-domain methods.
- Qualitative results illustrate latent domains capture factors like pose, appearance, and background; method can reshape heterogeneous test sets.
- Claiming the approach is “unsupervised” is misleading since it relies on class labels via the LPC and DWCV—better termed semi-/supervised on the source.
- Terminology is inconsistent (dataset vs. domain vs. latent domain) and should be standardized with examples early on.
- Notation issues: e.g., max_k r(U_k, B) and r(A→B) are undefined when first introduced and need clear definition.
- Originality: combining kernel-based distinctiveness, an explicit label prior, and DWCV for K selection is a novel and practical contribution.
- Significance: automatically reshaping datasets into coherent domains is valuable for vision domain adaptation and has practical applicability.
- Main weakness: the label prior may be too restrictive when true domain factors induce class-dependent label distributions (e.g., pose varying by class).
- Experimental protocol choices lack justification; a leave-one-dataset-out protocol would be more standard and transparent.
- Reporting/exposition should be improved (clarify Section 4.2, DWCV role, and experimental setup).
- Suggested fixes: standardize terminology, define r(·) before use, correct “unsupervised” wording, analyze label-prior sensitivity (vary δ), and add leave-one-dataset-out experiments.
