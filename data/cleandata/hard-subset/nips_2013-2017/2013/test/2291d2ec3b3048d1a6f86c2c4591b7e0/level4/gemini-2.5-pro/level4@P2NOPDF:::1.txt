This paper proposes a method to automatically discover latent domains within a training dataset for use in a domain adaptation framework. The goal is to improve classification performance on a target test set. The core idea is to partition the training data into a set of domains such that a kernel-based distance function between the feature distributions of any pair of domains is maximized.

The problem is formulated as an integer program with two primary constraints: (1) each data point must be assigned to exactly one domain, and (2) the class label distribution within each discovered domain must match the global class distribution of the entire dataset. The authors relax this NP-hard problem into a continuous quadratic optimization with linear constraints, which can be solved efficiently. The optimal number of domains is determined via cross-validation. The approach is evaluated on a static image dataset from [2] and the IXMAS multi-view action dataset from [15], demonstrating improved performance over the domain adaptation method of [19].

**Strengths:**

*   The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
*   To my knowledge, the proposed approach of automatically discovering latent domains by maximizing a distributional difference under a label-consistency constraint is novel.
*   The empirical results are promising, showing clear performance gains over the relevant baseline method from [19].

**Major Concerns:**

Initially, I leaned towards rejection, but my score could be raised if the authors address the following two major concerns in their rebuttal.

1.  **Motivation and Problem Formulation:** The motivation for the specific formulation is not entirely clear. On line 77, the paper claims that "simply clustering images by their appearance is prone to reshaping datasets into per-category domains." First, what is the empirical or theoretical evidence supporting this assertion? Second, could the authors elaborate on the specific mechanisms within their proposed model (Section 2) that prevent this from occurring? How does the formulation explicitly enforce that the discovered domains are not simply proxies for object categories?

2.  **Necessity of the Label Prior Constraint:** Related to the first point, the role and necessity of the second constraint—the "label prior constraint" (line 154)—needs further justification. I am curious to see what the performance would be without this constraint. To better understand its impact and to justify the complexity of the proposed discovery method, I strongly recommend including the following baselines:
    *   An ablation study where the label prior constraint is removed.
    *   A comparison against partitioning the data using a standard k-means clustering on the appearance features.
    *   A comparison against a more sophisticated unsupervised method, such as object discovery (e.g., Sivic et al., ICCV 2005).
    *   A simple baseline where the dataset is randomly partitioned into sets of equal size.

**Additional Comments and Questions:**

*   **Line 82:** The phrase "maximally different in distribution from each other" is used frequently. Please clarify which distribution is being referred to (e.g., the distribution of features, labels, etc.).
*   **Line 166:** Regarding the formulation of β_mk, does this variable not already satisfy the simplex constraint by definition, making the explicit constraint redundant? Please clarify.
*   **Line 182:** The constraints presented here seem to differ from those introduced starting on line 153. Could you explain this apparent discrepancy?
*   **Line 215:** Please provide a justification or proof for the bound mentioned on this line.
*   **Line 289:** Could you provide more details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel [4]? Was a publicly available implementation used, or was it re-implemented for this work?
*   **Lines 313/340:** The analysis of the results would be significantly strengthened by a more in-depth discussion of *why* the proposed method outperforms the baselines. Providing qualitative examples that show systematic failure modes of the baselines which are overcome by your approach would be very insightful.
*   **Eq (1):** There appears to be a minor typo. `M'k` should likely be `Mk'`.
*   **Suggested Citation:** The authors might consider citing "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" by Torralba and Efros (CVPR 2011), as it seems highly relevant to the paper's motivation.

**Post-Rebuttal Update:**
The authors' rebuttal successfully addressed my main concerns regarding the paper's motivation and the role of the label prior constraint. I have updated my score and now lean towards acceptance.
