- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition images into domains based on underlying aspects (viewpoint, illumination, clutter) rather than datasets.  
- Partitioning is driven by two principles: maximizing distinctiveness in feature space and maximizing learnability for class discrimination.  
- Original and significant: addresses a scarce research area (automatic domain definition) and offers a valuable idea for domain adaptation.  
- Major concern: the proposed optimization (Sect. 2) is not clearly justified and may not solve the stated objective (eqs. 2+3).  
- Major concern: the two criteria are poorly integrated—learnability is used only as post-hoc validation instead of being part of the partitioning objective.  
- Paper quality: well-written but lacks figures and visual aids to clarify concepts.  
- Experiments: demonstrate significant improvements using discovered domains over dataset-based domains and prior method [20].  
- Methodology concern: inclusion of the test set in the reshaping process is interesting but unclearly presented and effectively assumes an imputation setting.  
- Implementation weakness: image representation is outdated (single BoW of sparse SURF, 800 codewords); similarity measure unspecified and Euclidean is inappropriate for histograms.  
- Suggested improvements: use spatial pyramids/dense SURF, appropriate histogram kernels (Chi2/intersection), or Fisher Vectors to modernize descriptors.  
- Overall recommendation: despite exploratory shortcomings, the paper is a promising direction and should be accepted.  
- Actionable recommendations: integrate learnability into the optimization, adopt modern descriptors, add visual illustrations, and clarify test-set reshaping and its assumptions.
