1. Method: Automatically partitions pooled images into latent domains by enforcing feature-space separability and that each domain supports a strong discriminative classifier on original categories.  
2. Novelty: Little prior work on automatic domain induction; promising direction for uncovering nuisance-factor domains that improve primary-task classifiers.  
3. Optimization concern: Unclear how closely the relaxed solver approximates the original constrained formulation in Section 2; lack of guarantees.  
4. Objective integration: Maximum learnability is used only to select K post-hoc, while partitions are determined solely by separability; a unified objective is preferable.  
5. Clarity: Paper is well written and organized but would benefit from additional illustrative figures.  
6. Experiments — positives: Discovered domains give clear gains versus treating datasets as domains and outperform recent domain-discovery method [19].  
7. Experiments — test-set reshaping: The reshaping idea is interesting but under-explained and implicitly assumes a batch/imputation setting that should be stated.  
8. Features critique: Image representation (sparse SURF BoW with 800 codebook) is outdated; recommend dense SURF, spatial pyramids, χ^2 or intersection kernels, or Fisher Vectors.  
9. References: Relevant prior work for feature/representation improvements includes Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronin ECCV 2010.  
10. Verdict: Despite limitations, the paper is a constructive contribution and merits acceptance.
