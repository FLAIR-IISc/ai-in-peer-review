- Introduces a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets, addressing a gap in domain adaptation research.  
- Defines two key properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness (domains maximally different) and maximum learnability (each domain supports strong discriminative models).  
- Formulates domain discovery as a nonparametric optimization using kernel-based measures in RKHS and a convex relaxation for efficient solution.  
- Determines the optimal number of domains via domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) that evaluates classification performance within candidate domains.  
- Extends the method to reshaping test datasets conditionally, useful for real-world multi-domain test data.  
- Avoids restrictive parametric assumptions, distinguishing it from prior latent-domain work (e.g., Hoffman et al. [20]).  
- Extensive experiments (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam, IXMAS) show consistent improvements over baselines and prior methods, with MATCHING achieving near-oracle accuracy without target labels.  
- Qualitative analysis (Figure 1) demonstrates discovered domains align with meaningful visual factors like pose and background.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound with clear mathematical formulations and computational feasibility considerations.  
- Terminology is inconsistent—“dataset,” “domain,” and “latent domain” are used interchangeably and need explicit early definitions and examples.  
- Equation 6 (max_k r(U_k,B)) and some technical details lack sufficient explanation.  
- The enforced class distribution (label prior) constraint may be unrealistic for tasks where domain-specific class distributions naturally differ and should be discussed or relaxed.  
- Experimental choices (specific source/target dataset pairs) lack justification; more systematic evaluations (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out) would strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: novel, significant, and practically effective contribution with minor revisions recommended; reviewer recommends acceptance.
