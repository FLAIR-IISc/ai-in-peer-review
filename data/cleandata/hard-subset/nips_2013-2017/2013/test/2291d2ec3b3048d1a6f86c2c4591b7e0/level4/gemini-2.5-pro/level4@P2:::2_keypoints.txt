- Summary: Proposes automatic discovery and partitioning of latent domains in visual datasets using Maximum Distinctiveness and Maximum Learnability, with a non-parametric distinctiveness measure and cross-validation to choose K, yielding improved domain-adaptation performance.

- Strength (Novel Problem): Novel, principled formulation addressing the question of "what constitutes a domain?" rather than assuming dataset-as-domain.

- Strength (Guiding Principles): Well-motivated criteria—maximize distinctiveness and learnability—to find partitions that are both separable and useful for classification.

- Strength (Empirical Results): Experiments show consistent improvement over using whole datasets as domains and outperform a recent related method (Hoffman et al.).

- Weakness (Decoupled Criteria): Distinctiveness and learnability are not integrated—partitioning optimizes only distinctiveness while learnability is used only to select K; a joint objective is preferable.

- Weakness (Relaxation Analysis): Integer-to-continuous optimization relaxation is applied without analysis of how well the relaxed solution approximates the original integer optimum.

- Weakness (Feature Representation): Uses outdated bag-of-visual-words with sparse SURF (800); stronger modern features (SPM, dense features, Fisher Vectors) and the histogram distance metric should be used and reported.

- Weakness (Clarity & Transductive Assumption): Presentation lacks illustrative figures; the "reshaping the test datasets" procedure is hard to follow and appears transductive without clear justification.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance for conceptual contribution but urge authors to acknowledge limitations and improve integration of criteria and empirical validation with stronger features.
