- Paper goal: discover latent domains in training data and use them for domain adaptation to improve test-time classification.
- Method: define a kernel-induced dissimilarity between features and seek a partition that maximizes pairwise domain dissimilarity.
- Formulation: integer program with constraints—each point assigned to exactly one domain and per-domain label distribution matches the global label prior.
- Optimization: relax to continuous quadratic objective with linear constraints; select number of domains by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and shows gains over prior domain adaptation method [19].
- Positives: clearly written, seemingly novel approach, and measurable performance improvements over [19].
- Main concern 1 (motivation): unclear evidence for the claim that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains and unclear how the proposed formulation prevents per-category clustering.
- Main concern 2 (label prior): need justification for the label-prior constraint; report performance without it and include baselines like k-means, unsupervised object discovery, and random equal partitions.
- Clarify what is meant by “maximally different in distribution” (which distribution is referenced).
- Question whether β_{mk} already lies on a simplex as defined (line 166).
- Reconcile apparent differences between constraints stated at line 153 and those at line 182.
- Request justification or proof for the bound claimed at line 215.
- Provide details on geodesic flow kernel usage (reimplementation or public code) and more insight into why the method outperforms baselines, including systematic failure cases.
- Minor fixes: correct Eq. (1) M'k => Mk' and consider adding Torralba & Efros “Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias” (CVPR 2011) citation.
- Final stance: initial lean toward reject, but after rebuttal concerns were addressed the reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
