- Proposes automatic partitioning of pooled datasets into domains reflecting latent factors (viewpoint, illumination, clutter) guided by domain separability and per-domain learnability.  
- Novelty/significance: timely and useful contribution—discovering domains that explicitly support class discrimination is novel and practically relevant.  
- Optimization unclear: derivation and how the proposed procedure approximates the original objective (2)+(3) need clarification.  
- Integration issue: separability and learnability are not fully integrated—learnability mainly used for selecting number of domains, not driving partitioning.  
- Request clearer derivation/justification of optimization steps, including assumptions, relaxations, and impact on objective gap.  
- Request sensitivity analysis to initialization and hyperparameters and reporting on convergence consistency of partitions.  
- Suggest ablation or alternative formulation where learnability directly contributes to the partitioning objective rather than only model selection.  
- Clarity/presentation: paper is generally well written but needs illustrative figures (pipeline schematic, visual examples of discovered domains, t-SNE/UMAP embeddings).  
- Experiments: discovered domains improve performance over dataset-as-domain baselines and outperform recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Transductive concern: “reshaping” appears to use full test set—must state evaluation protocol and whether test instances are available during partitioning.  
- If transductive info is used, provide a strictly inductive baseline, quantify benefit from transductive reshaping, and ensure no label leakage.  
- Image representation is weak (sparse SURF bag-of-words, 800-word codebook) and may underrepresent method’s potential.  
- Recommend stronger features: spatial pyramid BOW, dense features, histogram similarity measures (chi-square/intersection), or Fisher Vectors for competitive baselines.  
- Additional analyses requested: domain interpretability (correlation with known factors), sensitivity to number of domains, scalability/complexity, and robustness across feature types.  
- Overall: promising and novel approach with notable empirical gains, but needs clearer optimization, tighter integration of objectives, better feature baselines, and improved presentation; recommendation: weak accept.
