- Introduces automatic domain partitioning across datasets to avoid conflating dataset boundaries with true domain factors (viewpoint, illumination, etc.).  
- Partitioner enforces two principles: create distinct domains in feature space and ensure each domain supports training a strong task classifier.  
- Novel and promising direction for discovering useful domain structures to improve classifiers.  
- Main methodological weakness: optimization (Section 2) does not clearly solve the combined objective; learnability is used post-hoc rather than integrated into partitioning.  
- Paper is well-structured and clear but lacks illustrative figures demonstrating domain partitioning.  
- Experiments show clear gains over using datasets as domains and over a recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Unclear treatment of test set in the "reshaping" process (Section 4.3); seems to assume access to all test data and should be explicitly stated.  
- Image representation is weak/outdated: single BoW with sparse SURF (800 words) undermines rigor; recommends spatial pyramid BoW with dense SURF and appropriate histogram similarity (X² or intersection) or modern Fisher Vectors.  
- Overall recommendation: compelling contribution with strong empirical results but requires revisions to descriptor choice and clearer integration/clarification of optimization and experimental assumptions; recommend acceptance pending these fixes.
