- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition images across datasets into domains rather than equating domains with datasets.  
- Partitioning is driven by two principles: maximize domain distinctiveness in feature space and ensure each domain supports training a good discriminative classifier.  
- Contribution is significant and novel given limited prior work on automatic domain definition.  
- The optimization in Section 2 is unconvincing and it’s unclear how closely it solves the original problem.  
- The two driving criteria are not well-integrated; maximal learnability is used mainly as a post-hoc validation to choose the number of domains.  
- Recommends a more integrated objective that directly includes both distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Paper is a worthwhile contribution and should be accepted.  
- Writing is clear but would benefit from additional illustrative figures.  
- Experiments show substantial gains over treating datasets as domains and over a recent domain discovery method.  
- Including the test set in the “reshaping” process is interesting but unclear; it resembles an imputation setting and should be explicitly stated.  
- Image representation is outdated (single bag-of-words on sparse SURF with 800 codewords) and should be improved (e.g., spatial pyramid, dense SURF, Fisher Vectors).  
- Unclear which similarity measure is used for descriptors; recommend chi-squared or intersection kernels rather than Euclidean for histograms.  
- Suggests literature to improve representations: Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010.
