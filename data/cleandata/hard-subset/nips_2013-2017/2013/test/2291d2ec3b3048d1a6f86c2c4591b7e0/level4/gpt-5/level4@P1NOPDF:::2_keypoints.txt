- Proposes automatic partitioning of images across datasets into domains based on (1) separability in feature space and (2) ability to train a strong discriminative classifier for the original classes within each domain.

- Novel contribution: relatively little prior work on automatic domain definition; promising approach that can recover underlying factors of variation rather than dataset boundaries.

- Major concern: optimization in Section 2 is unclear and may not closely solve the stated objectives (2) and (3).

- Criteria not well integrated: maximal learnability is used only to choose the number of domains, while partitioning is driven solely by separability; a joint objective is recommended.

- Writing is clear but the paper would benefit from figures to illustrate main concepts.

- Experiments show discovered domains outperform treating datasets as domains and beat a recent domain discovery method [19].

- Methodology issue: including the test set in the “reshaping” process is interesting but under-explained and should be explicitly described as an imputation setting (all test data available).

- Major weakness in image representation: uses an outdated single bag-of-words on sparse SURF with 800 codebook entries; recommends spatial pyramid + dense SURF, χ2/intersection kernel for similarity, or Fisher Vectors, with relevant citations.

- Overall assessment: interesting and novel ideas with good results, worth acceptance but should be improved (especially image representation and joint optimization).
