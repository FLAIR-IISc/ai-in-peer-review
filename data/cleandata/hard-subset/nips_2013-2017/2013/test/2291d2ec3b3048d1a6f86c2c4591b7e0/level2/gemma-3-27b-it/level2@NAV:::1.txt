## Review of “Reshaping Visual Datasets for Domain Adaptation”

This paper tackles the important problem of domain adaptation in visual recognition, arguing that equating datasets with domains is a weak proxy for the complex factors causing distribution mismatches. The authors propose a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains within datasets by maximizing both distinctiveness and learnability. They formulate this as a non-parametric optimization problem and demonstrate its effectiveness on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.

The core idea of identifying domains based on both statistical difference *and* the ability to learn a good classifier within each domain is strong and logically sound. The paper clearly articulates the limitations of the common “dataset-as-domain” paradigm and offers a principled alternative. The use of kernel methods for measuring distribution discrepancies is well-justified, and the domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) strategy for determining the optimal number of domains is a practical contribution. 

The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate consistent improvements over baseline methods, including those proposed in prior work [20]. The ablation studies and analysis of identified domains (Figures 1 & 2) provide valuable insights into what the algorithm is learning. The extension to reshaping *test* datasets is also a valuable addition, addressing a less-explored aspect of domain adaptation. 

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the mathematical formulation is presented, the optimization process could benefit from more detailed explanation. Specifically, the practical aspects of scaling the optimization procedure to larger datasets are not fully addressed. The discussion of related work, while extensive, could benefit from a more critical evaluation of existing domain adaptation techniques beyond simply stating their limitations. A stronger comparison to recent adversarial domain adaptation methods might be useful. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and principled approach to domain discovery.
*   Strong experimental validation on multiple tasks.
*   Clear articulation of a key problem in domain adaptation.
*   Practical contribution with the DWCV strategy.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Optimization details could be more thoroughly explained.
*   Limited comparison to recent adversarial domain adaptation techniques.
*   Scalability to very large datasets is not fully addressed.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper with a well-motivated approach and compelling experimental results. While some areas could be strengthened, the contribution is significant and advances the field of domain adaptation. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the clarity of the optimization process and a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
