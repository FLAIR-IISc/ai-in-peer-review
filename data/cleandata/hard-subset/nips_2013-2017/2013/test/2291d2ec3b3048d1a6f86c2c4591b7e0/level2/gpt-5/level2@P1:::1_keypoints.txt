- Paper tackles the implicit assumption "datasets = domains" and aims to discover latent domains within visual datasets to improve domain adaptation.
- Proposes reshaping datasets into latent domains by optimizing two axioms: maximum distinctiveness (RKHS mean-embedding/MMD-style pairwise distances) and maximum learnability (select K via domain-wise cross-validation, DWCV).
- Optimization: integer program on binary assignments zmk with a label prior constraint (LPC) to avoid label-driven clustering, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program in βmk.
- Introduces conditional reshaping of test data to match discovered training domains and compares to parametric Gaussian-mixture latent-domain work (Hoffman et al. ECCV’12).
- Strength: well-motivated problem and novel nonparametric distinctiveness criterion combined with a label-aware constraint.
- Strength: DWCV provides a transparent criterion for selecting K and conditional test reshaping is a practical extension.
- Empirical evidence: outperforming original dataset partitions and ECCV’12 on Office/Caltech and IXMAS, improving downstream adaptation (with GFK).
- Major concern: LPC is a strong supervised assumption enforcing per-domain label proportions to match global priors (δ≈1%); real data often violate this.
- Question: sensitivity to LPC violations and δ; suggestion to consider a soft penalty formulation.
- Optimization/scalability concerns: maximizing a convex quadratic form is NP-hard; paper lacks solver details, runtimes, convergence, and scalability analysis.
- Practicality question: M×M kernel matrix limits M; need discussion of Nyström or random feature approximations and typical M, K in experiments.
- Objective structure note: pairwise-sum identity (∑_{k<k’}||μk−μk’||^2 = K∑k||μk−μ̄||^2) could simplify/offer spectral insights but is not discussed.
- Constraint concern: the unusual β constraints (1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C) need clearer derivation and role in preventing degenerate assignments.
- DWCV selection critique: limited evidence that in-domain accuracy A(K) correlates with target adaptation performance; needs more systematic analysis (multiple splits, variance).
- Experimental clarity lacking: MATCHING strategy and conditional test reshaping QP are underspecified; ablations (distinctiveness vs. learnability, δ and kernel bandwidth effects) are missing.
- External validity: datasets are small; unclear applicability to larger-scale recognition or modern deep features.
- Quality: technically sound with pragmatic model selection, but optimization and LPC need more detail and stress-testing.
- Clarity: generally well-written but some notational choices and constraints need clarification; add pseudocode and solver details.
- Originality: novel nonparametric framing relative to ECCV’12 and useful DWCV/test reshaping additions.
- Significance: could change how adaptation studies prepare sources/targets if robust; learning domains rather than assuming them is valuable.
- Suggestions: add solver/complexity details and scalability strategies, relax LPC to soft penalty, detail MATCHING/reshaping, exploit pairwise identity, report multiple-run statistics and feature variants.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, conceptually clean nonparametric approach, empirically promising improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: strong LPC/label reliance, sparse optimization/scalability details, underspecified components and limited sensitivity analysis.
- Overall verdict: solid, timely contribution leaning toward acceptance pending clearer optimization, assumptions testing, and stronger empirical analysis.
