- Addresses the overlooked problem that datasets often contain multiple latent domains rather than a single domain.  
- Proposes a novel latent-domain discovery method based on two axioms: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a kernel-based discrepancy measure to discover domains without assuming parametric distribution forms.  
- Introduces a practical DWCV procedure to determine the number of latent domains.  
- Demonstrates consistent adaptation performance gains on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and human activity (IXMAS) benchmarks.  
- Shows qualitative evidence that discovered domains correspond to meaningful factors (pose, appearance, background).  
- Technically sound presentation with clear organization and strong linkage to prior work, especially contrasting with [20].  
- Experimental focus is primarily on reshaping training data; test data reshaping is only briefly examined.  
- Lacks concrete historical examples showing how equating datasets to domains led to suboptimal prior results.  
- Limited discussion of the computational complexity and runtime implications for practitioners.  
- Method has practical value for varied-data applications (mobile, surveillance) and fits NIPS scope.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept — significant, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution to domain adaptation.
