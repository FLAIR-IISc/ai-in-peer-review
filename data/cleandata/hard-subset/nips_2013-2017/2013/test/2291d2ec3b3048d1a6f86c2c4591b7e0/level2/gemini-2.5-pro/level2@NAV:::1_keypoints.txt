- Problem: Challenges the common practice of treating whole datasets as discrete domains, arguing this ignores latent factors (pose, lighting) that shape distributions.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel non-parametric method to automatically discover latent domains within datasets.
- Objective: Optimizes for "maximum distinctiveness" (domains are distributionally different) and "maximum learnability" (each domain supports a strong classifier).
- Method novelty: Uses a kernel-based measure for domain distinctiveness and avoids restrictive parametric assumptions (improves on Hoffman et al., 2012).
- Model selection: Introduces domain-wise cross-validation to select the number of discovered domains, operationalizing "learnability".
- Empirical evaluation: Extensive experiments show clear benefits on object and action recognition, including reshaping the test set.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, methodology, and experiments.
- Weakness: Optimization is non-convex/NP-hard; paper lacks details on solver choice, initialization, and handling of local optima, hurting reproducibility.
- Recommendation: High-quality, significant contribution; minor fix needed on optimization details — strongly recommend acceptance.
