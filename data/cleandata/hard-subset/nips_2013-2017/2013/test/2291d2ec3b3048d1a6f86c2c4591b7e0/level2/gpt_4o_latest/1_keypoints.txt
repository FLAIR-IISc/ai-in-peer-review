- Contribution: Proposes a novel nonparametric method to automatically discover latent domains in images/videos using two principles—maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Optimization: Formulates a rigorous optimization framework and relaxes the NP-hard problem into a practical continuous form.
- Evaluation: Extensive experiments on object and activity recognition show discovered latent domains improve classification and outperform competing methods (e.g., Hoffman et al. [20]).
- Practical utility: Method can reshape both training and test sets into meaningful domains, improving domain adaptation for real-world applications.
- Presentation: Well-written, logically structured paper with clear math, methodology, experimental details, and qualitative analyses.
- Limitation — computational feasibility: The scalability and computational cost of the relaxed formulation, especially on very large datasets, need further discussion.
- Limitation — label prior assumption: The label prior consistency (LPC) assumption may not hold in highly imbalanced or certain real-world scenarios.
- Limitation — related work comparison: Comparison and discussion of other related methods (e.g., generalized clustering approaches [27]) is insufficiently detailed.
- Limitation — interpretability: The real-world interpretability of discovered domains (e.g., factors like lighting or pose) is not thoroughly explored.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the paper’s novelty, technical soundness, and strong empirical support, but urges future work on scalability and interpretability.
- Pro arguments: Novel and impactful contribution, rigorous methodology, and comprehensive evaluation demonstrating practical benefits.
- Con arguments: Insufficient discussion of scalability/computational feasibility and limited exploration of domain interpretability.
