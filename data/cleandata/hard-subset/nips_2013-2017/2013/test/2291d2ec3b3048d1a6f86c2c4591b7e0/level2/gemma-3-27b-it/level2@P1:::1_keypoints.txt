- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains in images and videos to replace dataset-as-domain proxy in domain adaptation.  
- Core idea: jointly maximize domain distinctiveness and learnability via a nonparametric optimization procedure.  
- Uses kernel-based distance measures to quantify domain discrepancy and guide discovery.  
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition, showing improved classification over baselines.  
- Well-motivated problem formulation addressing a significant limitation in domain adaptation research.  
- Conceptually sound and novel combination of distinctiveness and learnability criteria with a new nonparametric formulation.  
- Experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks is a strength, demonstrating promising results.  
- Extension to reshaping the test set based on discovered training domains is insightful and practical.  
- Connection between theoretical objectives and practical implementation is insufficiently explicit.  
- Needs a clearer, more intuitive explanation of why maximizing kernel distance produces better domains.  
- DWCV method for selecting the number of domains is defined but its sensitivity to parameters (e.g., CV folds) is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Writing is dense and clarity suffers; lacks visual aids such as a diagram of the optimization steps.  
- Related work discussion could be expanded, especially comparisons to Hoffman et al. [20], clustering, and subspace learning approaches.  
- Overall assessment: promising and significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, comparisons, and DWCV sensitivity.
