- Addresses the flawed assumption that datasets are single domains and proposes discovering latent domains within source datasets for visual domain adaptation.
- Method optimizes for Maximum Distinctiveness (maximize sum of pairwise MMDs) with a Label Prior Constraint to keep class priors per domain similar to the overall prior.
- Uses Maximum Learnability via domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to automatically select the number of domains K.
- Demonstrates improved target performance by adapting from discovered domains versus original monolithic datasets.
- Empirical evaluation on object and action recognition is thorough and convincing, including an oracle (GOURS) upper bound.
- Non-parametric MMD formulation is principled and avoids restrictive distributional assumptions used in prior work.
- DWCV is a creative, task-relevant model selection strategy that ties K selection to discriminative performance.
- Paper is well-motivated conceptually and likely to influence future domain adaptation work.
- Optimization details are vague: need specifics on solver, local vs global optima, initialization sensitivity, and reproducibility.
- The Label Prior Constraint may be unrealistic in some settings (class priors can vary by domain); an ablation or discussion is needed.
- Scalability concern: O(M^2) kernel computation is prohibitive for large datasets; discuss kernel approximations or scaling strategies.
- Details of conditional test-set reshaping are relegated to the supplement; include a brief formal objective in the main paper for clarity.
