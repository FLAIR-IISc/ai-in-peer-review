- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets, addressing the mistaken practice of equating datasets with domains.  
- Introduces two axiomatic properties for ideal domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Presents a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to discover domains without manual specification.  
- Empirically outperforms the standard dataset-as-domain baseline and the prior latent-domain method [20].  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and action recognition (IXMAS).  
- Proposes DWCV (domain-wise cross-validation) to determine the optimal number of domains; method is clever and well-justified.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical foundation, practical utility, comprehensive comparisons, ability to reshape training and test sets, and reproducibility.  
- Minor weakness: limited analysis of method limitations and failure cases, especially when domains are very similar.  
- Could more thoroughly connect and compare with multi-source domain adaptation literature.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, convincing experiments, significant advance over prior work, recommended for acceptance.
