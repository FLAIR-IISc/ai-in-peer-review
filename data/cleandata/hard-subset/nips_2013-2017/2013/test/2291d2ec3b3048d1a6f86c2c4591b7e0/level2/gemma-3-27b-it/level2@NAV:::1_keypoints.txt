- Addresses domain adaptation and argues that treating whole datasets as domains is a weak proxy for real distribution mismatches.  
- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains by maximizing both domain distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Frames the method as a non-parametric optimization problem.  
- Employs kernel methods to measure distribution discrepancies between discovered domains.  
- Introduces domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to select the optimal number of domains.  
- Validated on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.  
- Shows consistent improvements over baselines, including prior work [20].  
- Provides ablation studies and analyses of discovered domains (Figures 1 & 2) that offer interpretability.  
- Extends the approach to reshaping test datasets, addressing a less-explored aspect of domain adaptation.  
- Mathematical formulation is presented, but optimization procedure lacks sufficient practical detail and clarity.  
- Paper does not fully address scalability of the optimization to very large datasets.  
- Related work discussion is extensive but could be more critical and include stronger comparisons to recent adversarial domain adaptation methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify optimization and broaden comparisons.
