- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets for domain adaptation.
- Defines two key properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Develops a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization procedure to find domains satisfying those properties.
- Paper is well-written with clear and concise explanations.
- Extensive evaluation on object recognition and human activity recognition shows the discovered domains outperform original datasets and a competing method.
- Strength: addresses a crucial and novel problem in domain adaptation.
- Strength: formalizes what constitutes a good latent domain via two explicit properties.
- Strength: nonparametric approach and proposed optimization make the method flexible and scalable.
- Strength: strong empirical validation across multiple tasks.
- Weakness: the optimization problem is NP-hard and is relaxed to a continuous problem that may not yield optimal solutions.
- Weakness: kernel bandwidth is set heuristically (twice the median pairwise distance), which may be suboptimal.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale use.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add computational complexity analysis, consider alternative optimization techniques for scalability, and provide more insight/experiments on kernel-bandwidth choice.
