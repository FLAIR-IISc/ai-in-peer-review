## Review of "Reshaping Visual Datasets for Domain Adaptation"

This paper addresses the crucial problem of domain adaptation in visual recognition, arguing that equating datasets with domains is a weak proxy for real-world variations. The authors propose a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains within datasets by maximizing both distinctiveness and learnability. This is a compelling idea, as it moves beyond the limitations of manually defined or dataset-based domain assumptions. The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the shortcomings of current practices and positioning their work within the broader context of domain adaptation research, referencing key papers in the field (e.g., [2, 4, 9]).

The core contribution lies in formulating domain discovery as an optimization problem balancing distribution divergence and discriminative power. The use of nonparametric methods and the DWCV procedure for determining the optimal number of domains are sensible choices. The experimental results on object recognition and human activity recognition are promising, demonstrating improved performance compared to baseline approaches and, importantly, showing that the discovered domains can be effectively utilized for adaptation. The analysis of the identified domains (Figure 1) provides valuable insights into the factors driving domain separation, such as pose and illumination.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the mathematical formulation is presented, the explanation of the optimization procedure could be more detailed. Specifically, the connection between the relaxed optimization problem and the original integer programming formulation isn't fully clear. Furthermore, the impact of the kernel bandwidth selection on the results isn't thoroughly discussed. While the authors acknowledge limitations, a more in-depth discussion of the computational cost of the proposed method, especially for large datasets, would be beneficial. The comparison to Hoffman et al. [20] feels somewhat brief, and a more detailed analysis of the differences in their approaches would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and well-motivated approach to domain discovery.
*   Strong experimental results demonstrating improved adaptation performance.
*   Clear articulation of the problem and its limitations.
*   Sound theoretical foundation with a well-defined optimization problem.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Optimization procedure explanation could be more detailed.
*   Limited discussion of computational complexity.
*   Brief comparison to related work [20].
*   Kernel bandwidth sensitivity not fully explored.

**Overall:**

This is a solid contribution to the field of domain adaptation. The idea of automatically discovering latent domains is insightful, and the proposed method shows promising results. While some aspects could be further elaborated, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above, particularly regarding the optimization details and computational considerations. Encouraging the authors to expand the comparison with [20] would also be valuable.
