- Addresses the overlooked problem of defining a "domain" in domain adaptation and proposes discovering latent domains within datasets.
- Proposes two guiding principles: Maximum Distinctiveness (domains maximally different) and Maximum Learnability (domains yield strong classifiers).
- Implements Maximum Distinctiveness via a non-parametric, kernel-based distribution-difference measure (related to MMD) and a relaxed quadratic program with a Label Prior Constraint (LPC).
- Selects the number of latent domains K using Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) to maximize average classifier accuracy across discovered domains.
- Evaluated extensively on object and action recognition benchmarks, showing better adaptation performance using discovered domains than original datasets or competing methods.
- Introduces "conditional reshaping" for heterogeneous test sets as a practical extension.
- Novel and significant contribution that challenges the dataset-as-domain assumption and may influence dataset construction and domain adaptation research.
- Principled and elegant technical formulation that avoids restrictive parametric assumptions by using MMD and couples it with a learnability-based model selection.
- Strong, convincing experimental validation with clear qualitative analysis illustrating captured visual factors.
- Clear writing and organization, with logical exposition and easy-to-follow experiments.
