- Paper challenges the common "dataset-as-domain" assumption and argues datasets contain multiple latent domains (lighting, pose, background).
- Proposes automatically "reshaping" datasets by discovering latent domains to improve visual domain adaptation.
- Core partitioning principles: maximize distinctiveness (domains have different distributions) and maximize learnability (each domain can train a strong classifier).
- Maximum distinctiveness is implemented by maximizing sum of pairwise distribution distances using kernel-based MMD.
- The partitioning is posed as a constrained integer program, relaxed for continuous optimization.
- A label prior constraint (LPC) is introduced to prevent trivial class-based clustering.
- Maximum learnability selects the number of domains K via domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) maximizing average domain CV accuracy.
- Uses a non-parametric approach (MMD) rather than parametric domain models, offering flexibility.
- Strength: original and significant conceptual shift that could change data collection and evaluation practices.
- Strength: technically elegant formulation combining distinctiveness and learnability with appropriate non-parametric tools.
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well organized with good connection to prior work.
- Strength: thorough experiments across object and action datasets demonstrating generality.
- Strength: "oracle" evaluations show discovered domains improve adaptation over original partitions.
- Strength: practical multi-source adaptation experiments show benefits without target labels.
- Strength: reshaping the test set yields strong gains (IXMAS example) and qualitative analysis reveals meaningful factors.
- Weakness: scalability concerns due to O(M^2) kernel matrix computation and no discussion of approximations (Nystrom, RFF).
- Weakness: LPC assumes constant class priors across domains, which may be unrealistic and limits applicability.
- Weakness: optimization details are vague—solver used, local vs global optima, sensitivity to initialization not specified.
- Overall: strong novelty and empirical support favor acceptance, but practical scalability and optimization clarity are concerns.
- Questions for authors: clarify computational complexity and kernel approximations; detail the MATCHING strategy for source selection without target labels; specify solver, optimization behavior, and initialization details.
