- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains in image/video datasets for domain adaptation by reshaping datasets into domains.
- Introduces two objectives for discovered domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Develops a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization/relaxation to discover domains meeting these properties.
- Argues reshaping datasets into such domains improves adaptation, especially for heterogeneous test sets.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Experiments are extensive and cover object recognition and human activity recognition, demonstrating effectiveness.
- Strength: novel approach to handling heterogeneous datasets and domain adaptation without assuming specific distributions.
- Weakness: original optimization is NP-hard and relies on a relaxation that may not be optimal.
- Weakness: method depends on kernel choice and bandwidth, which are problem-dependent.
- Weakness: lacks a thorough analysis of time complexity.
- Overall assessment: significant and original work; recommend accept with minor revisions (Quality 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10, Overall 8.5/10).
- Requested minor revisions: analyze time complexity, discuss kernel and bandwidth selection, detail the optimization/relaxation, and add experiments to show robustness.
