- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains in visual datasets for domain adaptation using principles of maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability with a nonparametric, kernel-based optimization.  
- Motivation is strong: shifts focus from “how to adapt” to “what constitutes a domain,” arguing dataset-level domains are weak proxies.  
- Solid connection to prior work across domain adaptation, speech processing, and statistical learning.  
- Theoretical contribution: axiomatic foundation (maximum distinctiveness and learnability) and kernel formulation are well-developed.  
- Practical contribution: domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to determine the number of domains and efficient optimization.  
- Empirical results are promising, improving over baselines on object recognition and human activity datasets; qualitative examples (Fig.1) aid interpretation.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough empirical comparison to simple clustering baselines (e.g., k-means) and unsupervised object discovery methods.  
- Weakness: necessity and effect of the label-prior constraint (LPC) are unclear; results without LPC should be shown.  
- Clarification needed on what “maximally different in distribution” precisely means (feature distributions, label distributions, or both).  
- Technical clarifications requested: apparent simplex status and relaxation of β_mk (line 166), inconsistent constraints around line 182, and explicit justification for the bound on line 215.  
- Reproducibility concern: specify implementation details of the geodesic flow kernel (reimplementation vs. public code).  
- Request for deeper analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines and examples of baseline failures it resolves.  
- Minor errors: typo in Equation (1) (M'k should be Mk').  
- Suggested citation addition: Torralba & Efros (2011) on dataset bias.  
- Overall: valuable and novel contribution with strong theory and results; recommends acceptance with minor revisions after clarifications and additional baseline comparisons; confidence 7/10.
