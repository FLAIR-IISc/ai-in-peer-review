- Proposes a novel nonparametric method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets for domain adaptation.
- Introduces two desired properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Develops an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains satisfying those properties.
- Extensive evaluation on object recognition and human activity datasets showing the method outperforms original datasets and competing methods.
- Demonstrates ability to find latent domains driven by pose, appearance, and background not controlled by dataset collectors.
- Uses multiple evaluation metrics (recognition accuracy, domain-wise cross-validation) and comparisons to k-means and random partitioning.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and appears novel.
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting claims.
- Significant contribution to domain adaptation by revealing finer data structure and improving adaptation performance.
- Main concern: unclear motivation/evidence for claim that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains and how method prevents per-category clustering.
- Questionable necessity of the label-prior constraint; need ablation results without it and comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery, and random partitioning.
- Requests clarification/definition of "maximally different in distribution" and specification of which distribution (features, labels, etc.).
- Requests justification/proof for the referenced bound (line 215) and more detail on the use of the geodesic flow kernel.
- Some minor corrections and clarifications required to strengthen the paper.
