- Proposes a novel nonparametric method to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets using two properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Argues that equating datasets with domains is a weak proxy and that latent domains better capture real distributional shifts.  
- Introduces a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to discover domains across training and test data.  
- Positions work relative to prior methods (e.g., Hoffman et al.) by avoiding Gaussian-mixture assumptions.  
- Provides a relaxed optimization formulation usable with off-the-shelf solvers.  
- Strength: novel idea, well-written, and experimentally validated on two datasets showing improved performance.  
- Weakness: motivation unclear on how the method prevents per-category appearance-based clustering.  
- Weakness: the necessity of the label-prior constraint is not justified and lacks ablation results without it.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to simple baselines (k-means), unsupervised object-discovery methods, and random equal partitioning.  
- Quality: technically sound and clearly described, but would benefit from more theoretical analysis and justification.  
- Clarity: generally clear notation and exposition, though some equations and constraint explanations need improvement.  
- Originality: offers an original perspective by formalizing desired properties of latent domains and using a nonparametric approach.  
- Significance: has meaningful implications for domain adaptation and dataset construction; shows empirical gains on tested datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity, and supporting experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear motivation and missing ablations/comparisons, especially regarding the label-prior.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to provide more theoretical justification, ablations without label-prior, and baseline comparisons.
