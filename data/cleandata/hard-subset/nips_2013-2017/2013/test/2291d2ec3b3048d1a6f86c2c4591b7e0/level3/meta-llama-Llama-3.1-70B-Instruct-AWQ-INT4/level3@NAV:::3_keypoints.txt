- Proposes a novel method for automatically discovering latent domains in image/video datasets.  
- Introduces two desiderata for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Develops a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization procedure to identify domains.  
- Novelty: controls class-label distributions via a relaxed convex formulation and selects domain count by per-domain classification quality.  
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition; discovered domains outperform original datasets and a competing method.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanation of approach and advantages.  
- Experimental results are thorough and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Clarity issues remain, especially in the experimental section, making parts hard to follow.  
- Notation and terminology are inconsistent across the paper.  
- Claim of being unsupervised is problematic because the method uses semantic class labels (implicit supervision).  
- Dataset/source-target selection rationale is not clearly explained.  
- Qualitative experimental details are lacking and should be expanded.  
- Overall contribution is notable for domain adaptation, but revisions are needed; reviewer rating 7/10 and recommendation conditional acceptance with clarifications.
