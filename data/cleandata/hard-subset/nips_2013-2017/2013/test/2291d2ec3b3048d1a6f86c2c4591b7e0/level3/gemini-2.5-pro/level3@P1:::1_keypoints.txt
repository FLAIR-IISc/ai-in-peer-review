- Novelty/significance: Proposes learning latent domains within visual datasets rather than treating whole datasets as single domains, a potentially influential idea for domain adaptation.
- Technical quality: Uses a non-parametric, kernel-based distinctiveness measure and a practical relaxation of an NP-hard integer program.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and the principles of "maximum distinctiveness" and "maximum learnability" are intuitive and clearly presented.
- Empirical results: Shows consistent performance gains over dataset-as-domain baselines and prior method [20] on object and action recognition benchmarks.
- Baseline insufficiency: Lacks comparison to simple baselines (e.g., K-means on features) to validate the claim that naive clustering would fail by grouping by category.
- LPC ablation needed: The Label Prior Constraint (LPC) is central to preventing per-category domains but its necessity should be validated via an ablation study (what if LPC is removed).
- Random partition baseline: Should compare to random partitions that respect the LPC to isolate benefits of the distinctiveness objective.
- Ambiguity in distance measure: Clarify whether the inter-domain distance d(k,k') is computed on marginal P(x) or joint P(x,y), and justify the choice.
- Derivation clarity: The relaxed optimization constraints (e.g., 1/M <= sum_k(beta_mk) <= 1/C) need clearer derivation from the integer program.
- K_max justification: Provide a brief proof or justification for the bound K_max <= min{M/(NC), C}.
- Reproducibility detail: State whether the geodesic flow kernel implementation was public or custom.
- Deeper analysis requested: Provide systematic analysis explaining why the proposed method succeeds where dataset partitions or [20] fail, beyond anecdotal examples.
- Minor typo: Likely typo in Eq. (1): `M'k` should be `M_k'`.
