- Summary: Tackles latent domain discovery for vision via “maximum distinctiveness” using kernel mean embeddings (MMD), formulates domain assignment as an integer program relaxed to a QP, selects K by domain-wise CV (DWCV), and shows gains on object/action datasets.
- Relation to prior work: Related to kernel two-sample testing, generalized clustering, multi-source adaptation, and dataset-bias work; nonparametric and couples discovery with learnability compared to Hoffman et al.
- Main quality concern: Optimization/evaluation are generally sound but several clarifications, ablations, and reproducibility details are required.
- Clarify what “maximally different in distribution” means: is TDD over Pk(x), class-conditional Pk(x|y), or joint Pk(x,y), since labels currently only enter via LPC.
- Explain βmk/simplex and per-instance bounds: clarify whether βk is per-k simplex and how 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C follows from Mk ≥ C and Mk ≤ M.
- Reconcile constraint differences: reconcile relaxed constraints with original exact LPC/assignment and justify the δ tolerance used.
- Prove/justify DWCV bound: provide justification for Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}.
- Ablations/baselines needed: show results without LPC and compare to k-means, random equal-size partitions, and unsupervised object-discovery baselines.
- Reproducibility detail: fully specify geodesic flow kernel usage (public code vs reimplementation, parameters).
- Explain performance and failure modes: analyze why method outperforms baselines and show failure cases (e.g., appearance clustering collapsing to per-category groups) and how LPC/objective prevent that.
- Clarity: Paper is well written overall; minor typo: Eq. (1) M′k should be Mk′.
- Originality: Novel, thoughtful nonparametric formulation; should cite Torralba & Efros (CVPR’11).
- Significance: Addressing latent domain structure is impactful and results are promising.
- Pros: Clear problem formulation, principled objective and selection (DWCV), consistent empirical gains.
- Cons: Missing critical ablations/baselines, some theoretical justifications, and constraint/formulation ambiguities.
- After rebuttal: Authors addressed most concerns (including ablations and constraint clarifications); reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
