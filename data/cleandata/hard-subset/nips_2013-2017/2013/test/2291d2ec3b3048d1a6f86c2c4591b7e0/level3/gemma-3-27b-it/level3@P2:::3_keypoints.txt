- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains within image and video datasets to improve domain adaptation.
- Argues that treating entire datasets as single domains is a weak proxy and instead reshapes datasets into domains of maximum distinctiveness and learnability.
- Formulates domain discovery as a non-parametric optimization problem with an efficient kernel-based procedure and cross-validation for learnability.
- Automatically determines the number of latent domains (K) using domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV).
- Demonstrates effectiveness on object recognition and human activity recognition datasets with qualitative examples.
- Well-motivated formulation addressing an important problem in domain adaptation.
- Terminology is inconsistent—“dataset,” “domain,” and “latent domain” are used interchangeably, causing confusion.
- The claim of being “unsupervised” is misleading because the method depends on semantic class labels (label prior), so domains are discovered conditioned on class labels.
- Experimental section (4.2) lacks clarity on GOURS/GORIG/GOTHER definitions and the rationale for dataset/source-target selection; a leave-one-dataset-out scheme would be more systematic.
- Equation (7) uses notation (max_k r(U_k, B)) that is not defined, disrupting comprehension.
- The integer-program relaxation used for tractability introduces approximations whose impact on discovered domain quality is not discussed.
- Sensitivity to kernel parameter choices (e.g., bandwidth) is not analyzed, leaving robustness unclear.
- The label prior constraint enforcing similar class distributions within domains may be overly restrictive and could prevent discovering naturally imbalanced domains.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, principled maximum-distinctiveness/learnability formulation, automatic K selection, and demonstrated improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: inconsistent terminology, misleading unsupervised claim, unclear experiments, potentially restrictive label prior, and unaddressed optimization/kernel concerns.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on revisions addressing terminology, experimental clarity, and discussion of assumptions and methodological limitations.
