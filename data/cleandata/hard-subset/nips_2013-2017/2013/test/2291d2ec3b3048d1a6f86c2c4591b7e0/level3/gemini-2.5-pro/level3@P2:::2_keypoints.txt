- Problem: Datasets are commonly but incorrectly treated as domains; real-world domains are mixtures of factors like lighting, pose, and background.  
- Contribution: Proposes an automatic method to discover latent domains within image collections.  
- Core principles: Partitions data by (1) Maximum Distinctiveness between domain distributions and (2) Maximum Learnability to ensure classifiers per domain are trainable.  
- Maximum Distinctiveness: Formulated as maximizing sum of pairwise distribution distances using non-parametric kernel MMD.  
- Maximum Learnability: Used for model selection—choosing the number of domains K via domain-wise cross-validation accuracy.  
- Optimization: Original integer programming formulation is relaxed to a continuous problem for tractability.  
- Empirical results: Shows improved adaptation performance on object and human activity recognition versus using original datasets or prior method [20].  
- Strength — Originality: Tackles an under-explored, important problem and offers a principled, data-driven solution with potential broad impact.  
- Strength — Methodology: Elegant non-parametric distinctiveness formulation that avoids limitations of parametric approaches.  
- Strength — Experiments: Thorough validation with significant improvements and both oracle and practical accuracy metrics.  
- Weakness — Optimization clarity: The relaxation from integer to continuous formulation lacks justification and analysis of the relaxation gap.  
- Weakness — Integration of objectives: Distinctiveness and learnability feel disconnected; learnability is only a post hoc model-selection step rather than integrated into the partitioning objective.  
- Weakness — Test reshaping: The “reshaping the test datasets” procedure is under-explained and the experimental assumptions are unclear.  
- Weakness — Features: Uses outdated and weak BoW SURF features (800 codewords) instead of stronger descriptors (e.g., SPM, dense SIFT, Fisher Vectors), limiting generality.  
- Weakness — Similarity metric: Does not specify the histogram similarity measure (Euclidean is inappropriate); this affects reproducibility and correctness.  
- Presentation: Well-written overall but would benefit from additional visualizations (e.g., t-SNE of discovered vs. original domains).  
- Evaluation summary: Quality—good core idea but technical and experimental weaknesses; Clarity—mostly clear but needs more detail; Originality—high; Significance—high.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing methodological clarifications and, if possible, strengthening experiments with modern features.
