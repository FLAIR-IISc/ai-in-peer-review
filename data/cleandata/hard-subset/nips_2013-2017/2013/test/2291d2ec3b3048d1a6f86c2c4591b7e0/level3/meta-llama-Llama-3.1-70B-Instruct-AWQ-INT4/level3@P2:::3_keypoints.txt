- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two desired properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation with an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains in training and test data.  
- Employs a kernel-based discrepancy measure and relaxes the problem into a continuous optimization for tractability.  
- Paper is well-motivated, problem is well-defined, and the technical approach is sound.  
- Experiments show reasonable quantitative gains, but qualitative results are underinformative.  
- Claim of being “unsupervised” is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent terminology and confusing distinctions among datasets, domains, and latent domains.  
- Experimental section lacks detail on dataset/source-target selection rationale and qualitative analysis.  
- Authors should provide clear definitions and examples of datasets, domains, and latent domains.  
- Need more insights into the factors that dominate the identified domains and more informative qualitative results.  
- Related work is well-covered and distinctions from prior methods are clearly explained.  
- Overall contribution is notable; recommendation is acceptance contingent on revisions addressing clarity and experimental details.
