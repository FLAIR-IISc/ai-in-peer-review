- Central contribution: automatic discovery of latent domains for visual domain adaptation using principles of distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Method: nonparametric kernel-based MMD objective, continuous constrained QP relaxation for instance-to-domain assignment, DWCV for selecting K, and conditional reshaping of unlabeled test data; adaptation via GFK.  
- Relation to prior work: discovers domains rather than assuming them, avoids Hoffman et al.’s Gaussian-mixture parametric assumptions, and connects to established domain-adaptation and dataset-bias literature.  
- Strength: clearly motivates why “dataset = domain” is a weak proxy and provides an operational framework for more meaningful domains.  
- Strength: principled use of MMD for domain distinctiveness and an intuitive DWCV criterion tied to downstream utility.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and cross-view action recognition (IXMAS) over dataset-as-domain and prior latent-domain methods.  
- Strength: discovered domains integrate naturally with multi-source adaptation and outperform UNION/ENSEMBLE baselines.  
- Weakness/request: need systematic evidence that naive appearance clustering degenerates to per-category clusters (e.g., K-means with NMI/purity and downstream adaptation results).  
- Weakness/request: ablation for the label-prior constraint (LPC) — include results without LPC, random equal partition baseline, and comparisons to unsupervised object-discovery/segmentation to rule out mere regularization effects.  
- Weakness/request: clarify what distribution is being made “maximally different” — MMD operates on Pk(x) marginals; justify this and discuss incorporating label information (class-conditional MMD or joint kernels).  
- Weakness/request: clarify the relaxation and constraints derivation — explain βmk bounds, their link to Mk and minimum/maximum domain sizes, and reconcile with the simplex constraints.  
- Weakness/request: derive/justify the K bound (Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}) with explicit assumptions about per-fold per-class minimum counts for reproducibility.  
- Weakness/request: report optimization/scalability details — M×M kernel costs, computational complexity, runtimes, memory, solver choices, and whether low-rank approximations (Nyström, random features) were used.  
- Weakness/request: discuss sensitivity to kernel bandwidth (choice of 2×median distance) and include a brief robustness plot.  
- Weakness/request: specify GFK implementation details — code used, parameter settings beyond defaults, and how source/target matching was done without labels.  
- Weakness/request: provide systematic analyses explaining why the method works — inter-domain MMD vs intra-domain class-conditional variance, confusion-matrix shift pre/post reshaping, and t-SNE/UMAP visualizations showing nuisance-factor alignment.  
- Quality: technically sound overall with correct use of MMD and reasonable DWCV, but needs clearer derivations for relaxation/constraints and justification of the K bound; LPC ablations are important.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; fix notation errors (e.g., Eq.(1) M′k → Mk′), ensure consistent x vs (x,y) distribution notation, clarify QP constraint set, and add algorithm pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel combination of nonparametric domain discovery, label-prior constraints, DWCV for K selection, and conditional test reshaping relative to prior work.  
- Significance: approach can meaningfully improve multi-source adaptation and dataset design; conditional test reshaping shows practical impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important problem, methodologically principled, well-executed, and empirically strong across modalities.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacks key ablations/diagnostics (LPC, clustering baselines), has ambiguity in relaxation/constraint presentation and K bound, and limited discussion of scalability and parameter sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on adding requested clarifications, ablations, and minor fixes.
