- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains for domain adaptation, challenging the dataset=domain assumption.
- Introduces two guiding properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Uses a nonparametric optimization framework to maximize those properties.
- Demonstrates utility on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.
- Outperforms dataset-as-domain baselines and a recent latent domain discovery method.
- Well-motivated problem statement that clearly explains limitations of current practices.
- Novel and exploratory work with potential to inform dataset construction and adaptation research.
- Optimization lacks clarity on how separability and learnability interact and their relative importance.
- Uses outdated image representations (bag-of-visual-words with SURF), limiting applicability to modern datasets.
- Relies on Euclidean distance for histogram comparison; χ² or intersection kernels would be more appropriate.
- Inclusion of the test set in the reshaping/imputation process is intriguing but insufficiently explained for real-world use.
- Paper would benefit from illustrative figures (e.g., visualizations of discovered domains) to improve clarity.
- Overall assessment: novel and impactful but needs clearer optimization explanation and improved image/similarity choices.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address optimization clarity, image representation, similarity measures, figures, and test-set explanation.
