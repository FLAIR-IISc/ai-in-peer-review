- Paper tackles the overlooked problem of what defines a "domain" in visual domain adaptation and argues datasets may contain multiple latent domains.  
- Main contribution: a method to automatically discover K latent source domains that are maximally distinctive and "learnable" (strong classifier per domain).  
- Distinctiveness is measured by sum of pairwise kernel two-sample tests (MMD); partitioning is formulated as an integer program relaxed to a continuous quadratic program.  
- A Label Prior Constraint (LPC) enforces per-domain class label distributions match the global prior.  
- K is selected via a novel domain-wise cross-validation procedure to ensure learnability.  
- Experiments on object and action recognition show adapting from discovered domains improves performance over using original datasets and beats a prior latent-domain method.  
- Strength: original and significant problem formulation with potential practical impact.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written with well-structured motivation, method description, and experiments.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation using established kernel methods and reasonable optimization/K selection; empirical gains are encouraging.  
- Concern: maximizing kernel distance on features may cluster by object category rather than nuisance factors (viewpoint/illumination), and it's unclear how the model prevents this.  
- Concern: LPC importance is not experimentally verified—ablation without LPC is needed to justify complexity.  
- Concern: stronger baseline comparisons are needed (k-means on features, random equal partitioning, other unsupervised discovery methods) to validate superiority.  
- Clarification requested: specify whether "maximally different" refers to P(x), P(y|x), P(x,y), or other—formulation appears to focus on P(x).  
- Clarification requested: explain the β_k relaxation (β_mk = z_mk/M_k) and why β_k must be on a simplex.  
- Clarification requested: derive how integer constraint sum_k z_mk = 1 relaxes to 1/M <= sum_k β_mk <= 1/C in Eq. (5).  
- Clarification requested: justify/prove the K_max bound K_max <= min{M/(NC), C} used for search.  
- Clarification requested: detail use and implementation/source of the geodesic flow kernel for reproducibility.  
- Request for deeper analysis: provide qualitative examples or analyses showing why discovered domains overcome adaptation failures of original dataset partitions.  
- Minor: probable typo in Eq. (1): M'k likely should be M_k'.  
- Minor: suggest adding citation to Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011).
