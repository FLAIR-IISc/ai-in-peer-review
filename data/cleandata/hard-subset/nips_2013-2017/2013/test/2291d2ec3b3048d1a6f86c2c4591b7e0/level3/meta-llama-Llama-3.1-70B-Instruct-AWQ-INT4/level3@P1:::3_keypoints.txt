- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in images/videos using two properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Introduces a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to find domains across training and test data, improving adaptation on object and activity recognition.  
- Contribution: a convex framework to split data into K latent domains with similar within-domain class distributions and maximal pairwise distinctiveness in RKHS.  
- Contribution: a procedure to select K by maximizing average learnability.  
- Contribution: a relaxed convex formulation to control class-label distributions during latent-domain discovery.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem statement that highlights limitations of current domain adaptation methods.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation on multiple datasets demonstrating improved adaptation performance.  
- Strength: empirical comparison with existing methods, including recent latent-domain discovery approaches.  
- Weakness: confusing and inconsistent terminology (e.g., “dataset,” “domain,” “latent domain”); needs clear definitions and examples.  
- Weakness: experimental section (notably Section 4.2) is unclear and needs more detailed setup and results explanation.  
- Weakness: claiming the problem is unsupervised is misleading since the method uses semantic class labels and thus relies on labeled data.  
- Weakness: requires more conceptual and experimental clarification, especially regarding relation to existing methods.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical evaluation and favorable comparisons.  
- Against acceptance: unclear terminology, insufficient experimental clarity, misleading unsupervised claim, and need for further clarification.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
