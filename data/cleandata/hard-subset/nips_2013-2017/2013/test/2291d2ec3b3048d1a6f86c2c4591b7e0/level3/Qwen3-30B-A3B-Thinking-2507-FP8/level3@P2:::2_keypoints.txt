- Addresses latent-domain discovery in visual datasets instead of equating datasets with domains.  
- Proposes two axiomatic properties for domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses nonparametric kernel-based measures and continuous relaxation of an integer program for domain discovery.  
- Connects to prior cross-dataset domain adaptation work (e.g., Saenko et al., Hoffman et al.) but tackles a novel, harder problem.  
- Technically sound formulation and clear theoretical motivation for the approach.  
- Optimization procedure (Section 2.1) does not clearly demonstrate convergence to the original objectives.  
- The two criteria (distinctiveness and learnability) are not integrated into a single optimization; learnability is used mainly for model selection.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over treating datasets as domains and over Hoffman et al. (2012).  
- Image representation (BoW with sparse SURF and 800 codewords) is outdated and weakens experimental claims.  
- Paper fails to specify the appropriate histogram similarity measure (e.g., χ² or intersection kernel vs. Euclidean).  
- Description of test-set "reshaping" (Section 4.3) is poorly explained and seems to assume an imputation setting without stating it.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, but would benefit from more illustrative figures showing domain discovery and reshaping.  
- Novel contribution filling an underexplored gap: automatic latent-domain discovery with fresh axiomatic perspective.  
- Significant potential impact for real-world recognition tasks and future dataset construction.  
- Strengths: correctly identifies dataset-vs-domain problem, principled dual-property approach, empirical gains, and fills a literature gap.  
- Weaknesses: unclear optimization convergence, lack of unified objective, outdated features, poorly explained reshaping, and missing histogram-similarity details.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing methodological weaknesses in the camera-ready version.  
- Suggested fixes: update image representation (e.g., dense SURF/Pyramid or Fisher vectors), clarify histogram similarity, and better integrate the two domain properties into optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental overlooked problem, theoretically motivated, empirically effective, and practically relevant.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental validity weakened by old features, missing methodological details, and incomplete integration/explanation of core components.
