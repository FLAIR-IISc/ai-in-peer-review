- Proposes a novel method to automatically partition image datasets into domains for domain adaptation using feature-space separability and per-domain learnability.  
- Tackles an under-explored and important problem by moving beyond treating whole datasets as single domains.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical improvements over using datasets as domains and a recent domain-discovery baseline [20].  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents motivation, method, and experiments.  
- Lack of illustrative figures; visual examples of discovered domains would improve understanding.  
- Optimization (Section 2) is insufficiently justified and not clearly connected to the original objectives of maximizing separability and learnability.  
- The two driving criteria (distinctiveness and learnability) are not well integrated; learnability is handled externally via cross-validation rather than being built into the partitioning objective.  
- The relaxation to continuous optimization is standard but the chosen constraints and their impact on objectives aren’t fully articulated.  
- Uses an outdated image representation (sparse SURF BoW with 800 codewords); modern descriptors (spatial pyramids, Fisher Vectors, or deep features) should be used for better performance.  
- Important related works on improved descriptors (Zhang IJCV 2006, Jurie CVPR 2005, Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronnin ECCV 2010) should be considered and cited.  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unclear; Euclidean is inappropriate for histograms and alternatives like chi-squared or intersection kernel should be specified.  
- Use of the test set in the “reshaping” process is poorly explained and implies unstated assumptions (e.g., semi-supervised or imputation), requiring clear justification.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions to clarify test-set usage, justify optimization choices, and acknowledge feature-representation limitations.
