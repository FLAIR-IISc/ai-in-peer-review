- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image and video datasets, addressing the mismatch between datasets and true domains.  
- Method reshapes datasets into domains using two principles: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Formulates domain discovery as a non-parametric optimization problem and applies it to object and human activity recognition.  
- Important conceptual contribution: tackles "what constitutes a domain" rather than just "how to adapt."  
- Experiments show clear advantage over treating datasets as single domains and outperform a recent related work.  
- Interesting extension: reshaping test datasets to better match training domains.  
- Weakness: the relaxed continuous optimization is not convincingly shown to solve the original integer objectives (Eqs. 2 and 3).  
- Weakness: relationship between relaxed optimization and original integer program lacks sufficient justification.  
- Weakness: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability feel decoupled; learnability is used only as a wrapper for model selection (K) instead of being integrated.  
- Suggestion: develop a joint optimization that directly maximizes both distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Writing is generally good but paper needs more illustrative figures and visualizations of discovered domains.  
- Experimental weakness: uses an outdated BoW SURF descriptor with 800 codewords, limiting conclusions; should use modern descriptors (e.g., spatial pyramids, Fisher Vectors) and cite relevant prior work.  
- Methodological issue: histogram similarity uses an unclear metric—Euclidean distance is likely inappropriate; consider chi-square or intersection kernels.  
- Test-set reshaping is poorly explained and appears to assume known labels on the test set; this assumption must be clarified and the procedure detailed.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and promising work with novel idea and solid results; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing optimization justification, descriptor choice, and test-set reshaping clarity.
