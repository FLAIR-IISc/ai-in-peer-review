- Tackles automatic discovery of latent domains within datasets rather than treating each dataset as a single domain.  
- Proposes an unsupervised method that partitions images into domains that are maximally distinctive and maximally learnable.  
- Distinctiveness is measured nonparametrically using MMD (kernel-based two-sample test) to ensure separable feature distributions.  
- Learnability is used as a model-selection criterion (domain-wise cross-validation) to choose the number of domains K.  
- Differs from Hoffman et al. by using a nonparametric approach instead of a Gaussian mixture parametric model.  

Strengths:
- Novel and significant re-formulation of "what constitutes a domain" in domain adaptation.  
- Extensive experiments on object and action recognition showing adaptation from discovered domains improves performance and outperforms Hoffman et al.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and presentation.  

Weaknesses:
- Distinctiveness and learnability are decoupled: optimization maximizes distinctiveness only, with learnability applied post-hoc, rather than a unified objective.  
- Relaxation of the NP-hard integer program to a convex quadratic is not adequately justified or connected back to the original objectives.  
- Test-set "reshaping" procedure is poorly explained and implicitly assumes access to unlabeled test data (transductive setting) without stating or justifying this assumption.  
- Uses outdated BoW SURF features (800-word codebook), raising concerns about generalizability to modern representations (e.g., Fisher Vectors, deep features).  
- Missing detail on the kernel/similarity used for BoW histograms; Gaussian kernel on histograms (Euclidean) is inappropriate—chi-squared or intersection kernels should be considered.  

Suggestions:
- Develop an integrated objective that jointly optimizes domain distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Provide a clearer theoretical/empirical connection between the relaxed optimization and the original integer formulation.  
- Explicitly state and justify the setting for test-set reshaping (e.g., transductive) and move key methodological details from supplementary into the main paper.  
- Re-run experiments with stronger, modern feature descriptors (e.g., spatial pyramids, Fisher Vectors, deep features) to validate robustness.  
- Add illustrative figures showing examples of discovered domains for better intuition.  

Overall verdict:
- Despite shortcomings, the paper makes a valuable and timely contribution; recommendation: accept.
