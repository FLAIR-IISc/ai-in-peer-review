- Tackles automatic domain definition for domain adaptation, moving beyond equating datasets with domains.  
- Core idea: partition datasets into domains based on feature-space separability and per-domain discriminative learnability.  
- Proposes a nonparametric formulation and optimization procedure to discover domains.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.  
- Novel and important problem with clear motivation and well-defined objectives.  
- Experimental validation shows gains over dataset-as-domain baselines and a recent domain discovery method.  
- Interesting idea of reshaping the test dataset based on training-domain discovery, but the process is insufficiently explained.  
- Link between the proposed optimization and the original separability+learnability objectives is not fully elucidated.  
- Separability and learnability are not tightly integrated; learnability appears as a post-hoc model selection step.  
- Desirable to have a joint optimization that directly maximizes both separability and learnability.  
- Image representation (bag-of-words sparse SURF with 800 codewords) is outdated and weak compared to modern descriptors.  
- Recommend upgrading to spatial pyramids of dense SURF, Fisher Vectors, or learned representations.  
- Histogram similarity measure is unclear; Euclidean distance is inappropriate — use χ² distance or intersection kernel.  
- Paper would benefit from more illustrative figures showing the discovered domains and their characteristics.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but address integration, representation, similarity metric, and test-reshaping explanation in revisions.
