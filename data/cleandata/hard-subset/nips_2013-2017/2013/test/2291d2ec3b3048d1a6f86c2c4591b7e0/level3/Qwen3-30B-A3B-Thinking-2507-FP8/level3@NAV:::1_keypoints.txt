- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains to improve domain adaptation in visual datasets.  
- Introduces two key properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Formulates the problem using kernel-based distribution difference measures and relaxes it to a continuous quadratic program with label-prior constraints.  
- Determines the number of domains via domain-wise cross-validation.  
- Experimental results on object and action recognition datasets show improved adaptation performance using discovered domains.  
- Empirical analysis reveals discovered domains align with factors like pose, background, and color.  
- Paper is well-written, novel, and addresses an important problem in domain adaptation.  
- Motivation for avoiding per-category clustering lacks supporting evidence and explanation.  
- Unclear how the method prevents per-category clustering in practice.  
- Necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear; initial manuscript lacked an ablation study without it.  
- Missing comparisons to standard clustering baselines (e.g., k-means) and random partitioning to strengthen claims.  
- Ambiguity about what "maximally different in distribution" means (features, labels, or both).  
- Inconsistency noted in the formulation of β_{mk} relative to the simplex constraint.  
- Geodesic flow kernel usage requires more implementation detail.  
- Missing proof for the bound referenced at line 215.  
- Authors addressed most concerns in rebuttal (additional ablations and clarifications).  
- Overall contribution is meaningful and technically sound but some design choices and methodological details need stronger justification; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
