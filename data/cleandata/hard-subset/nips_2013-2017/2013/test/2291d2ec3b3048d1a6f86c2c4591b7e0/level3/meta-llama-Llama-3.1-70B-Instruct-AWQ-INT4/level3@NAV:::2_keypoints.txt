- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video data enforcing maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains across train and test data.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough problem analysis and motivation.  
- Experimental results are strong: outperforms existing methods on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.  
- Provides detailed analysis of identified domains and factors contributing to distinctiveness and learnability.  
- Optimization procedure is not clearly shown to solve the original objectives.  
- The two driving criteria (distinctiveness and learnability) are not well integrated.  
- Uses an outdated image representation (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords).  
- Does not clearly explain the similarity measure used for histogram comparison.  
- Reviewer considers the work a valuable contribution to domain adaptation and recommends acceptance.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify optimization-objective relationship, better integrate the two criteria, adopt modern image representations (e.g., dense SURF spatial pyramids, Fisher Vectors), and explain histogram similarity measure.
