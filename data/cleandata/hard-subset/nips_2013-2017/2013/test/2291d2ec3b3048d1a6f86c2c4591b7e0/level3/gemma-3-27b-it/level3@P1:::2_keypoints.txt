- Proposes a novel nonparametric method to automatically discover latent domains by maximizing domain distinctiveness and learnability.
- Argues that treating datasets as domains is a weak proxy and motivating automatic domain discovery for better domain adaptation.
- Core idea of reshaping datasets into meaningful domains is valuable and relatively underexplored.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains motivation, approach, and experiments.
- Experimental results on object recognition and human activity recognition show significant improvements over existing methods (including [20]).
- Positions the work well within prior literature (vision, speech, statistical learning) and relates to landmark detection and generalized clustering.
- Optimization formulation (Section 2) does not convincingly show joint optimization of distinctiveness and learnability; learnability is used mainly for model selection.
- Reliance on cross-validation to choose number of domains K feels disconnected from the core optimization objective.
- Test-set reshaping/assignment of test examples to discovered training domains is unclear and seems to implicitly assume imputation; needs explicit explanation.
- Uses an outdated image representation (BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords), limiting performance and robustness.
- Fails to specify histogram similarity measure; Euclidean is inappropriate—should consider chi-squared or intersection kernel.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting clearer test reshaping details and discussion/experiments with modern image descriptors.
- Main pros: novelty, significance, empirical gains, clarity; main cons: optimization integration, image features, unclear test assignment.
