This paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets, which is essential for domain adaptation in visual recognition problems. The authors introduce two key properties that latent domains should possess: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability. The method defines a kernel-based difference function between feature vectors and partitions data to maximize inter-domain differences. The formulation is an integer program with constraints, relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization, and the number of domains is chosen by cross-validation.

The paper is well-written, and the approach appears novel. The performance gains over prior domain adaptation methods are appreciated. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.

However, there are some concerns and questions that need to be addressed. The motivation behind the approach is unclear, and there is no evidence that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains. It is also unclear how the model prevents this. The necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear, and results without it, as well as comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery baselines, and random equal partitioning, would be helpful.

The definition of "maximally different in distribution" is unclear, and it would be helpful to clarify what distribution is being referred to (features, labels, or other). Additionally, it is unclear whether β_{mk} as formulated already lies on a simplex. The constraints stated around line 182 appear different from earlier constraints, and clarification would be helpful.

The authors should provide justification or proof for the bound referenced on line 215. Furthermore, details on the use of the geodesic flow kernel, including whether it was reimplemented or publicly available code was used, would be helpful. It would also be helpful to provide insights into why the proposed approach outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failures that the method overcomes.

A minor correction is needed in Eq (1), where M'k should be Mk'. Additionally, a suggested citation to Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011), would be relevant.

Overall, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of domain adaptation, but addressing the concerns and questions raised above would strengthen the paper and increase its impact.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to discovering latent domains in visual datasets
* Clear and concise explanation of the method
* Performance gains over prior domain adaptation methods

Weaknesses:

* Unclear motivation behind the approach
* Lack of evidence that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains
* Unclear necessity of the label-prior constraint
* Limited comparisons to other methods

Recommendations:

* Address the concerns and questions raised above
* Provide additional experimental results and comparisons to other methods
* Clarify the definition of "maximally different in distribution"
* Provide justification or proof for the bound referenced on line 215
* Detail the use of the geodesic flow kernel
* Provide insights into why the proposed approach outperforms baselines

Rating: 7/10

Confidence: 8/10
