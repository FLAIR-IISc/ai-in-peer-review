- Proposes automatically discovering latent domains within training data for domain adaptation rather than treating datasets as single domains.  
- Formulates domain discovery as an integer program, relaxed to a continuous quadratic optimization.  
- Uses domain-wise cross-validation to select the optimal number of domains.  
- Evaluated on object and action recognition tasks and shows improvements over a baseline domain adaptation method.  
- Paper is well-written and the methodology is technically sound.  
- Motivation for clustering by appearance leading to useful per-category domains lacks strong empirical justification.  
- The mechanism claimed to prevent clustering by category is not fully convincing.  
- Necessity and role of the label-prior constraint (LPC) are unclear; results without LPC are needed.  
- Missing comparisons to simpler unsupervised clustering methods (e.g., k-means) and object-discovery baselines to isolate LPC and method benefits.  
- Ambiguity in “maximally different in distribution” — unclear which distribution (features, labels, etc.) is meant.  
- Mathematical questions remain: does β_mk lie on a simplex, are constraints around line 182 consistent with earlier formulation, and justification for the bound at line 215 is needed.  
- More details required on the geodesic flow kernel implementation (reimplemented vs. public code).  
- Paper should provide deeper analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failures it overcomes.  
- Overall: novel and promising but needs empirical/technical clarifications; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending thorough rebuttal.
