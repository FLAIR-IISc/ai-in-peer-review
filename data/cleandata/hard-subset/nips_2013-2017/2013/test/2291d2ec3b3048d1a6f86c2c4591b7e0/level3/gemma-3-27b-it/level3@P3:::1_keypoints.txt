- Addresses latent-domain discovery for domain adaptation, avoiding reliance on pre-defined dataset boundaries.  
- Proposes maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness and intra-domain learnability via a relaxed quadratic optimization.  
- Relaxation of integer program to continuous optimization is sensible and clearly justified.  
- Experimental results on object and action recognition show promising gains over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and technically sound with consistent notation.  
- Ablation studies included but could be more extensive.  
- More rigorous analysis of the computational cost of the optimization is needed.  
- Kernel-based difference function (Eq. 1) needs a more intuitive explanation for non-kernel experts.  
- Related work discussion is adequate but could be expanded (additional citations suggested).  
- Novelty: automatic discovery of latent domains and combining distinctiveness with learnability is a significant contribution.  
- Novel aspects include the kernel-based difference function and the label-prior constraint (LPC).  
- Significance: reshaping datasets into latent domains can materially improve domain adaptation in practical settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, strong experimental validation, clear presentation, and relevance to real-world problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: motivation for avoiding per-category domains not fully convincing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: necessity of the label-prior constraint requires further justification and comparison to alternatives.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks comparison to unsupervised object discovery baselines.  
- Specific concern: paper explains that per-category clustering occurs but doesn't fully argue why it's undesirable in all scenarios.  
- Specific concern: need results without LPC and comparisons to k-means/unsupervised clustering to justify LPC.  
- Clarifications: “maximally different in distribution” refers to differences in feature distributions; β_{mk} lies on a simplex.  
- Clarifications: relaxation adds constraints (explained), but bound on line 215 lacks formal proof.  
- Implementation note: authors used an existing geodesic flow kernel implementation.  
- Suggested improvement: visualize discovered domains to better explain outperformance (e.g., on IXMAS).  
- Minor correction: Eq. (1) typo (M'k -> Mk') noted.  
- Suggested citation: include Torralba & Efros (2011) on dataset bias.  
- Overall recommendation: lean towards acceptance pending incorporation of suggested citation and additional analyses/experiments.
