- Tackles discovering latent domains in heterogeneous visual datasets to make domain adaptation meaningful and effective.
- Proposes reshaping datasets into domains satisfying maximum distinctiveness (maximize distributional differences) and maximum learnability (each domain supports a strong classifier).
- Operacionalizes distinctiveness via MMD between marginal feature distributions and formulates domain assignment as an integer program with one-domain-per-instance and label-prior constraints (LPC).
- Relaxes the integer program to a continuous quadratic program and selects K with domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) maximizing weighted within-domain CV accuracy.
- Includes conditional reshaping of test data to match discovered source domains and compares to parametric mixture models (Hoffman et al. ECCV’12).
- Well positioned within domain adaptation and related kernel-embedding clustering literature; motivation aligns with dataset bias work.
- Strength: addresses a practical gap where dataset ≠ domain and nuisance factors mix with semantics.
- Strength: principled nonparametric formulation using characteristic kernels avoids strong parametric assumptions.
- Strength: LPC discourages trivial per-category partitions; DWCV offers a simple data-driven K selection.
- Strength: solid empirical gains on object and cross-view action benchmarks; qualitative analyses are informative.
- Strength: clear writing and an easy-to-follow method with useful qualitative results.
- Weakness: needs empirical evidence that appearance-based clustering produces per-category domains (e.g., K-means label purity) and explanation of how LPC/objective prevent that failure.
- Weakness: missing ablations/comparisons — require results for (i) no LPC, (ii) K-means on features, (iii) random equal partitioning, (iv) generic clustering/unsupervised object discovery with same adaptation pipeline.
- Weakness: clarify that distinctiveness measures marginal Pk(x) via MMD (not joint Pk(x,y)) and state whether label info influences distinctiveness beyond LPC.
- Weakness: clarify βmk definition and how βk simplex constraint interacts with per-example constraints.
- Weakness: bridge derivation between original integer constraints and the relaxed constraints (including δ-relaxed LPC) and explain ∑k βmk relations.
- Weakness: K bound (Kmax ≤ min{M/(NC), C}) lacks formal justification; the C bound seems incorrect/confusing and needs correction (expect K ≤ M/C).
- Weakness: report GFK implementation details (public code or reimplementation), PCA/subspace dims, tuning, and sensitivity to Gaussian kernel bandwidth.
- Weakness: needs deeper analysis of why objective recovers nuisance-factor–aligned domains (quantitative diagnostics like correlation with known view angles, confusion reduction).
- Weakness: scalability/optimization concerns — maximizing a convex quadratic is NP-hard; report solver, runtimes, convergence, and scaling with M and K.
- Quality: formulation is sound and grounded in MMD; relaxations and DWCV are sensible, but more ablations and optimization/K-bound justification needed for reproducibility.
- Clarity: overall readable; clarify marginal vs joint distributions, relaxed constraints, fix Eq. (1) typo (Mk′), and add solver/parameter settings.
- Originality: novel nonparametric latent-domain discovery with learnability objective and useful conditional test reshaping, distinct from prior mixture models.
- Significance: likely useful to practitioners handling heterogeneous data; pairs well with many adaptation algorithms and shows consistent gains.
- Pros for acceptance: practical problem, principled objective, data-driven K selection, demonstrated benchmark improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: missing ablations/baselines, incomplete technical details (constraints, K bound, optimization, GFK), and light sensitivity analyses.
- Minor: fix Eq. (1) typo, explicitly cite Torralba & Efros (CVPR’11) in motivation, consider releasing code or pseudocode for optimization.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing requested clarifications and ablations for the final version.
