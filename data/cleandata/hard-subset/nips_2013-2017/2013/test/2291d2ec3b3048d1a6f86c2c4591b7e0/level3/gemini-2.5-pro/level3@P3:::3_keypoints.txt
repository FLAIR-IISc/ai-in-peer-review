- Summary: Proposes a convex, non-parametric framework to partition visual datasets into K latent domains by maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness in RKHS while enforcing class-label priors, selecting K via average within-domain cross-validation ("learnability").

- Strength: Originality — tackles the important problem that datasets are heterogeneous mixtures and offers a principled automated method to find coherent sub-domains.

- Strength: Novel formulation — introduces an elegant convex, kernel-based measure of domain distinctiveness and a class-prior constraint to avoid trivial class clustering.

- Strength: Model selection — chooses K using a sensible "maximum learnability" criterion tied to downstream classifier performance.

- Strength: Empirical validation — experiments on object/action benchmarks show gains over monolithic datasets and competing methods; qualitative results reveal meaningful visual factors.

- Weakness: Clarity/presentation — inconsistent use of "dataset/domain/latent domain" and unclear notation/metrics (GOURS, GORIG); oracle evaluation (max_k) and Equation 6 contain unexplained terms.

- Weakness: Label Prior Constraint (LPC) — LPC forces identical class proportions across discovered domains, an unrealistic and overly restrictive assumption that can prevent discovering class-dependent latent factors.

- Weakness: Supervision claim — calling the approach "unsupervised" is misleading because it relies on class labels for LPC and selecting K, making it semi- or weakly-supervised in practice.

- Weakness: Experimental rigor — unclear rationale for source/target dataset choices risks cherry-picking; a systematic protocol (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out) is needed.

- Weakness: MATCHING strategy — the critical MATCHING method for selecting sources for unlabeled targets is not described, undermining assessment of practical applicability.

- Overall assessment: A strong, timely contribution with technical merit; recommend acceptance contingent on major revisions to improve clarity, discuss LPC limitations, and provide full experimental details for reproducibility.
