- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains in visual datasets using two properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability, formulated as a nonparametric optimization with a continuous relaxation and domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV); shows improvements on object and activity recognition benchmarks.

- Technically sound: relaxation to continuous quadratic optimization and DWCV are well-motivated, but the necessity of the label-prior constraint (LPC) is not justified and needs testing.

- Claims it avoids per-category clustering (as in Hoffman et al.); this claim needs stronger evidence.

- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, but has ambiguities that require clarification.

- Key clarity issues: define "maximally different in distribution" (features, labels, or both); apparent inconsistency between constraints (lines 162–165 vs 182); β_{mk} may already lie on a simplex making the relaxation questionable.

- Originality: novel nonparametric formulation and addition of maximum learnability; sufficiently distinct from prior latent-domain work though related literature is acknowledged.

- Significance: addresses an important gap in domain adaptation, yields consistent empirical improvements, and offers insights into domain-defining factors (pose, appearance, background).

- Strengths: well-motivated problem, clear optimization framework, extensive evaluation across tasks, consistent improvements, and useful domain-structure insights.

- Weaknesses: insufficient justification for avoiding per-category clustering, unclear necessity of LPC, unresolved technical ambiguities, limited comparison to simple unsupervised baselines (e.g., K-means, random partitions), and inadequate explanation of why the method outperforms baselines.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — core idea and results are solid, but address remaining technical clarifications.

- Specific requested revisions: clarify "maximally different" meaning; provide ablation studies for the label-prior constraint; resolve the constraint discrepancy; include K-means and random partition baselines; explain mechanisms behind performance gains; provide details or code for geodesic flow kernel implementation.
