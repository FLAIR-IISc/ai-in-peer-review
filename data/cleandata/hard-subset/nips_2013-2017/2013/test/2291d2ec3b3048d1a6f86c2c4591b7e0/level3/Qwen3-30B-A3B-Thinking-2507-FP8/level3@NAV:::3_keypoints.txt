- Proposes a framework to discover latent visual domains by enforcing maximum distinctiveness (pairwise RKHS dissimilarity) and maximum learnability (each domain supports strong discriminative models).
- Introduces a convex optimization that splits data into K domains with similar within-domain class distributions, and selects K via domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to maximize average accuracy.
- Addresses the conceptual gap between dataset-level domains and true latent domains and extends Hoffman et al. (2012) using nonparametric methods.
- Provides a principled method to determine the number of domains and shows consistent empirical improvements on object and action recognition datasets.
- Claims of being “unsupervised” are misleading because the method uses semantic class labels via the label prior constraint (LPC).
- Significant clarity issues: inconsistent terminology (dataset/domain/latent-domain) and unclear experimental rationale (e.g., unexplained choice of source-target domains instead of leave-one-dataset-out).
- Presentation error: Equation 7 contains max_k r(U_k, B) without defining r(·), indicating a notation/definition bug.
- The constraint enforcing identical class distributions across domains is problematic for real-world data where class-specific latent factors (e.g., pose differences) are natural.
- DWCV for selecting K is novel, but the paper fails to demonstrate whether the identical-class-distribution constraint is necessary or beneficial in practice.
- Overall: technically sound with promising results, but weakened by the unsupervised mischaracterization, clarity/presentation issues, and inadequate justification of core constraints; authors should clarify label use, revise terminology, and justify constraints.
