- Addresses important, under-explored problem: automatically discovering latent domains for visual domain adaptation.  
- Proposes partitioning images to maximize feature-space separability and per-domain "learnability" (trainable discriminative classifiers).  
- Novel, non-parametric alternative to prior work that assumes pre-defined domains.  
- Strength: originality—learning domains rather than taking them as given could substantially impact domain adaptation research.  
- Strong experimental results: significant improvements over using original datasets as domains and over Hoffman et al. [20].  
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivates and explains the approach.  
- Major weakness: disconnect between stated goals and optimization—separability and learnability are not jointly optimized.  
- Learnability is only used for model selection (choosing K), not integrated into the primary partitioning objective.  
- Suggestion: a unified optimization that jointly maximizes separability and learnability would be more elegant and powerful.  
- Unclear whether the relaxed optimization in Section 2 accurately solves the original objectives.  
- Methodological weakness: uses outdated image features (single BoW of sparse SURF), which may limit generality.  
- Suggest using stronger descriptors (e.g., Fisher Vectors, spatial pyramids—Lazebnik CVPR 2006, Perronnin ECCV 2010).  
- Missing details: similarity measure for BoW histograms not specified—Chi-squared or intersection kernels would be more appropriate than Euclidean.  
- The "reshaping" of the test set is interesting but poorly explained and appears to assume an imputation setting that is not justified.  
- Overall verdict: despite representation and optimization issues, the core idea is novel, significant, promising, and the paper should be accepted.
