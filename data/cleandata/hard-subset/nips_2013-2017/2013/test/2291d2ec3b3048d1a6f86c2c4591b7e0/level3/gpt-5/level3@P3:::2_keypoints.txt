- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains across visual datasets, optimizing for feature-space distinctiveness (MMD/TDD) and learnability (DWCV for K).
- Formulates partitioning as an integer program with a label-prior constraint (LPC), relaxes to a continuous quadratic program, and introduces conditional test-time reshaping.
- Shows empirical gains over treating datasets as domains and over Hoffman et al. [19], with extra benefit from test reshaping on IXMAS.

- Novel framing: automatically finding domains for adaptation rather than assuming datasets are domains.
- Uses nonparametric distinctiveness via kernel mean embeddings/MMD, avoiding fragile parametric mixture assumptions.
- Provides a clear model-selection objective (DWCV) that ties K selection to downstream discriminative utility.
- Well written and easy to follow; LPC is a reasonable inductive bias.

- Learnability is not integrated into the partitioning objective and is only used as an outer-loop selector for K.
- The relaxed optimization maximizes a convex quadratic under constraints but lacks guarantees or diagnostics on rounding, integrality gaps, or stability.
- Conditional reshaping is poorly specified: unclear what test information is used and whether transductive access to all test instances is assumed.
- Uses outdated/weak representations (sparse SURF BoW with 800 codewords), which likely underestimates the method’s potential.
- Unclear which distance/kernel is used for histogram features; Euclidean may be inappropriate—needs χ2/intersection or Hellinger normalization and bandwidth tuning.
- Limited evaluation with alternative multi-source adaptation methods beyond GFK to demonstrate robustness.
- Missing illustrative figures and ablation plots for K selection, LPC sensitivity, and discovered domains.

- Technically sound and well-motivated, but rigor is reduced by the optimization–objective gap and an under-specified reshaping protocol.
- Generally clear writing, but needs more figures and explicit details on conditional reshaping and histogram-kernel choices.
- Highly original: automatic domain discovery and conditional test reshaping are novel contributions.
- Potentially impactful if validated with modern descriptors and clearer protocols.

- Suggest jointly optimizing separability and learnability (e.g., TDD − λ·classification loss) or using a bilevel/differentiable surrogate for learnability.
- Clarify and justify conditional reshaping assumptions and ensure fair comparisons under the same transductive access.
- Upgrade features to spatial pyramids/dense SIFT-SURF and Fisher Vectors and report whether results hold.
- Use χ2/intersection/Hellinger-normalized kernels for histograms and report sensitivity to kernel/bandwidth.
- Provide integrality/duality gap diagnostics, LPC sensitivity analyses, stability across seeds, and computational-cost reporting.

- Pros: tackles an important, under-explored problem with a principled nonparametric formulation and shows consistent empirical improvements.
- Cons: learnability not integrated, optimization–objective gap unaddressed, reshaping protocol under-specified, representations and kernel details are weak.
- Overall verdict: Accept — promising and valuable contribution that would be stronger with clearer optimization/test-protocol details and modern descriptors.
