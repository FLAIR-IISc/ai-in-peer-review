- Proposal: reshape visual datasets into K latent domains by optimizing maximal distinctiveness (RKHS mean embeddings) and maximal learnability (domain-wise classifier strength).
- Method enforces a label prior constraint (LPC) so within-domain class proportions match the global prior to avoid degenerate single-class domains.
- Uses nonparametric MMD-style RKHS distances and is positioned against parametric mixture and kernel-embedding clustering baselines.
- K is selected pragmatically by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) assessing per-domain learnability.
- Experiments on object and action recognition show gains over original partitions and a competing latent-domain method, with useful qualitative analyses.
- Strength: modeling distinctiveness via RKHS mean embeddings is theoretically sound and well-aligned with prior work.
- Strength: K-selection by per-domain cross-validation is a practical, data-driven approach.
- Concern: LPC may be too restrictive when real latent factors change class compositions; need discussion/ablation of δ and failure cases where LPC hurts.
- Concern: the relaxed optimization is a convex quadratic under linear constraints but remains nonconvex overall; solver, initialization, and robustness details are missing.
- Concern: method is not fully unsupervised because labels are used in LPC and DWCV; claim should be softened to weakly/semi-supervised.
- Clarity issue: inconsistent use of dataset/domain/latent-domain — provide clear definitions and a running example.
- Clarity issue: Section 4.2 notation (S, T, U, r) is hard to follow; explicitly define all symbols and fix presentation bugs.
- Bug: Eq.7 references max_k r(U_k, B) but r(U_k, B) is undefined; reconcile with r(A→B).
- Experimental issue: source/target choices and rationale are unclear; recommend leave-one-dataset-out protocols or clearer setup descriptions.
- Originality: novel combination of label-controlled domains and K-selection by learnability relative to prior RKHS clustering and latent-domain work.
- Significance: timely and practically relevant to domain adaptation and dataset bias; reported gains are promising but need stronger ablations.
- Suggested ablations: kernel choice/bandwidth, δ sensitivity, K-selection curves, and comparison to simple baselines like class-balanced K-means.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually clean nonparametric criterion, practical K selection, addresses unknown domains, and offers qualitative insights.
- Cons for acceptance: clarity/notation problems, LPC may limit applicability, insufficient optimization/reproducibility details, and misleading “unsupervised” claim.
- Overall: notable and promising contribution but requires conceptual, experimental, and presentation clarifications before publication.
