This paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in image or video datasets for domain adaptation tasks. The authors introduce two key properties that latent domains should possess: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability. Maximum distinctiveness requires the underlying distributions of the identified domains to be different from each other to the maximum extent, while maximum learnability ensures that a strong discriminative model can be learned from the domain.

The authors devise a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization procedure that can successfully discover domains among both training and test data. The proposed approach is extensively evaluated on visual object recognition and human activity recognition tasks, and the results show that the identified domains outperform not only the original datasets but also the domains discovered by a competing method.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach and its evaluation. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and the limitations of their approach. However, there are some areas that could be improved.

One major methodological weakness is the use of outdated image representation, which is a single bag-of-words (BoW) of sparse SURF with 800 codewords. This representation is too weak for current standards, and the authors should consider upgrading their descriptors to more modern and powerful representations, such as spatial pyramid of dense SURF or Fisher Vectors.

Another issue is the unclear similarity measure used for histogram comparison. The authors should specify whether they use Euclidean distance, which is inappropriate for histogram comparison, or a more suitable measure such as the X^2 distance or intersection kernel.

Furthermore, the paper could benefit from more illustrative figures to help explain the proposed approach and its evaluation. Additionally, the authors should clarify the imputation setting assumed in the "reshaping" process, which is not clearly stated.

Despite these shortcomings, the paper is a valuable and promising contribution to the field of domain adaptation. The proposed approach addresses the under-explored problem of automatically defining domains for domain adaptation, and the results show significant improvements over using datasets as domains and over a recent domain discovery method.
