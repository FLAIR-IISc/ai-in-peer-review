- Proposes automatic partitioning of image datasets into domains using feature-space separability and per-domain discriminative learnability, addressing domain definition for adaptation.
- Introduces two key properties: maximum distinctiveness (maximize inter-domain distribution differences) and maximum learnability (ensure strong discriminative models per domain).
- Uses a nonparametric optimization approach and reports experimental gains on object and action recognition vs. dataset-as-domain baselines and a recent domain discovery method [19].
- Paper is a valuable contribution with compelling empirical results and clear, well-organized writing; would benefit from more illustrative figures of domain partitions.
- Major methodological concern: optimization procedure (Sec. 2) does not clearly achieve the stated objectives and the two criteria are not properly integrated.
- Learnability is used only as a model-selection wrapper rather than being part of the partitioning objective; a joint optimization of both criteria is preferable.
- Image representation is outdated (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords); similarity measure for histograms is unspecified and Euclidean distance is inappropriate.
- Recommend using chi-square or intersection kernels (cite Zhang IJCV 2006, Lazebnik CVPR 2006) and upgrading to spatial pyramid dense SURF or Fisher Vectors.
- Use of test-set data in the “reshaping” process is poorly explained and appears to assume an unstated imputation setting.
- Despite weaknesses, the core idea is promising and the paper warrants acceptance if authors address representation and methodological clarifications in the camera-ready.
