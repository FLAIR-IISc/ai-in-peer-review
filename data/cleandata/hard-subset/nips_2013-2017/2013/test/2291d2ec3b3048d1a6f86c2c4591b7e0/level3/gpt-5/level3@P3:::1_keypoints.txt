- Paper tackles latent domain discovery to improve visual domain adaptation by partitioning training data to maximize inter-domain distributional distinctiveness (via MMD) and learnability (DWCV).
- Method: nonparametric RKHS mean-embedding/MMD objective, relaxed discrete assignment to a quadratic program, K chosen by domain-wise cross-validation favoring “maximum learnability.”
- Experiments on object recognition and multi-view action datasets show gains over prior latent-domain work [19]/[20] and original dataset partitions.
- Relates to MMD/two-sample tests (Gretton et al.), kernel clustering (Jegelka et al.), multi-source adaptation, and uses Geodesic Flow Kernel (GFK) for adaptation.
- Strength: clear problem motivation and well-written presentation.
- Strength: conceptual clarity in combining distinctiveness (distributional difference) and learnability (predictive utility).
- Strength: nonparametric approach avoids restrictive mixture assumptions of prior methods.
- Strength: practical K selection via DWCV linked to downstream discriminative performance.
- Strength: empirical improvements and demonstration that discovered domains can outperform original splits and prior methods.
- Strength: analysis of dominant factors in discovered domains and conditional reshaping of heterogeneous test sets.
- Weakness: risk of partitions simply splitting by object category rather than nuisance factors; mechanism preventing this is unclear.
- Weakness: necessity and effect of the label-prior constraint (LPC) not quantified; ablations without LPC missing in main text.
- Weakness: some notation/typos and imperfect alignment between integer and relaxed formulations (e.g., Eq. (1) typo).
- Weakness: optimization transparency lacking—relaxed problem remains NP-hard, solver choice, initialization, convergence not fully described.
- Question: clarify that “maximally different” optimizes differences over features Pk(x) (via MMD) not joint Pk(x,y), and discuss per-class split implications.
- Question: explain βmk/simplex behavior—what enforces peaked per-instance assignments and how per-instance 1/M ≤ Σk βmk ≤ 1/C interacts with simplex constraints?
- Question: reconcile constraint mismatch between discrete and relaxed formulations and justify the 1/M and 1/C bounds on Σk βmk.
- Question: justify/prove the K bound Kmax ≤ min{M/(N C), C} and clarify whether it’s sufficient or heuristic.
- Question: discuss bias introduced by βk ∈ Δ and sensitivity to class imbalance across latent domains.
- Question: detail GFK implementation/source, hyperparameters (subspace dimension, PCA), and multi-source target subspace estimation.
- Requested experiment: ablation study without LPC and sensitivity analysis over δ to test per-class clustering prevention.
- Requested baselines: k-means on x (with/without label balancing), unsupervised object discovery methods, and random equal partitioning using same adaptation pipeline.
- Requested analysis: failure analysis including cluster purity for nuisance vs class factors, class purity within domains, domain-size distributions, confusion matrices, and examples where k-means collapses.
- Requested optimization details: solver, initialization, runtime, and stability across random seeds.
- Minor issues: typo M'k should be Mk' in Eq. (1); cite Torralba & Efros 2011 for dataset bias context.
- Overall quality: sound core idea leveraging MMD with learnability-based model selection but weakened by technical ambiguities and missing ablations.
- Originality: novel nonparametric latent domain discovery with distinctiveness objective and learnability-driven K selection.
- Significance: addresses practical visual adaptation issues with empirical gains; potential broad impact.
- Pros: important problem, principled objective, practical K selection, empirical gains and conditional test reshaping.
- Cons: insufficient ablations/baselines, unquantified reliance on LPC, formulation/notation inconsistencies, and missing optimization details.
- Post-rebuttal: authors clarified that MMD is over Pk(x), reconciled constraints, provided ablations (no-LPC, k-means, random), justified K bound and GFK details; reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
