- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image and video datasets for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two desirable properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to identify domains satisfying those properties.  
- Finds latent domains that are more distinctive and learnable than original datasets, improving adaptation performance.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on object recognition and human activity recognition experiments.  
- Introduces domain-wise cross-validation to determine the number of latent domains.  
- Lacks clear definitions and distinctions between dataset, domain, and latent domain, causing potential confusion.  
- Selection of source and target datasets is unclear; alternative evaluations (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out) may be more appropriate.  
- Framing the problem as unsupervised is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels.  
- Paper quality is good with well-supported claims and a complete body of work.  
- Clarity is generally good but experimental sections need more detailed explanations.  
- Originality is high, proposing a new approach to latent domain discovery.  
- Significance is high, addressing a challenging problem in domain adaptation with a unique solution.  
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to clarify concepts and experimental setup.
