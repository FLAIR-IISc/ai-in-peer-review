- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in images/videos for domain adaptation.
- Introduces two domain properties: maximum distinctiveness (domains maximally different) and maximum learnability (each domain supports a strong discriminative model).
- Formulates the problem as a nonparametric optimization relaxed into a continuous quadratic optimization.
- Proposes domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to select the optimal number of domains.
- Evaluated extensively on object recognition and human activity recognition; discovered domains outperform original datasets and a competing method.
- Paper is well-written, appears novel, and shows meaningful performance gains.
- Motivation is unclear—no evidence that appearance-based clustering yields per-category domains or why the approach is necessary.
- Necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear; authors should show results without it and compare to k-means, unsupervised object discovery, and random partitioning.
- The phrase "maximally different in distribution" is vague—need to specify which distribution (features, labels, etc.).
- More details needed on the use of the geodesic flow kernel and the DWCV procedure.
- Some constraints and equations in the paper require clarification.
- Recommendation: address the above concerns and provide additional comparisons and clarifications; paper leans toward acceptance after revisions (rating 7/10).
