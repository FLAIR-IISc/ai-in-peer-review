- Problem: Challenges the common "dataset = domain" assumption and studies discovering latent domains for visual domain adaptation.  
- Proposal: Unsupervised method to reshape one or more datasets into latent domains.  
- Criteria: Two driving principles — Maximum Distinctiveness (maximize pairwise MMD in RKHS) and Maximum Learnability (ensure domains are learnable).  
- Optimization: Formulates an integer program with a Label Prior Constraint, then applies a continuous relaxation to solve it.  
- Model selection: Use Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) to pick the number of domains K by maximizing weighted domain-wise accuracy.  
- Experiments: Discovered domains lead to better adaptation performance and outperform prior latent-domain methods; qualitative analysis shows meaningful visual factors discovered.  
- Strength — originality: Tackles a fundamental, under-explored prerequisite for domain adaptation with a principled, data-driven approach.  
- Strength — soundness: The distinctiveness and learnability criteria are intuitive and well-motivated.  
- Strength — empirical evidence: Strong experimental gains validate the approach.  
- Weakness — criteria separation: Learnability is used only as a post-hoc model selection heuristic rather than integrated into the optimization.  
- Weakness — optimization analysis: No discussion of how well the continuous relaxation approximates the original integer problem or solver adequacy.  
- Weakness — outdated features: Uses BoW with 800 SURF descriptors; should use more modern representations (SPM, Fisher Vectors) and appropriate histogram distances.  
- Weakness — test-set reshaping clarity: The conditional reshaping procedure is under-explained and appears transductive; details are pushed to the supplement.  
- Overall: Recommends acceptance for its novel conceptual contribution, but urges addressing methodological shortcomings and updating feature representation.
