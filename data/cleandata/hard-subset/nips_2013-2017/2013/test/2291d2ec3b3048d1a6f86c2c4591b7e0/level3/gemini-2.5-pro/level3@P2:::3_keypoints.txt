- Addresses the overlooked problem of what defines a "domain" and proposes automatically discovering latent domains within collections of datasets.  
- Core idea: partition data into K domains by maximizing (1) distinctiveness (distributional separation in RKHS) and (2) learnability (each domain supports strong classifiers).  
- Selects K via Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) that maximizes domain-wise classifier accuracy.  
- Formulates discovery as a relaxed convex quadratic program using a non-parametric kernel-based measure related to MMD.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains in object and activity recognition: discovered domains improve oracle and multi-source adaptation performance.  
- Well-positioned relative to prior work; more flexible non-parametric approach compared to parametric GMM-based domain discovery (Hoffman et al.).  
- Strength: novel, important problem formulation that questions the dataset=domain assumption.  
- Strength: principled technical approach using non-parametric distinctiveness and convex relaxation.  
- Strength: model selection tied to learnability via DWCV, aligning discovery with classification utility.  
- Strength: promising qualitative and quantitative experimental results showing meaningful visual factors discovered.  
- Major weakness: Label Prior Constraint (LPC) forces per-domain class priors to match global priors, preventing discovery of semantically meaningful domains correlated with class.  
- Weakness: clarity and presentation issues—terminology inconsistent, experimental descriptions (e.g., GOURS, Eq.6 expectancy P) and notation have ambiguities/typos.  
- Weakness: calling the method "unsupervised" is misleading because it uses class labels for LPC and learnability evaluation.  
- Weakness: experimental protocol lacks systematic justification for dataset splits; recommend more systematic evaluations (e.g., leave-one-dataset-out).  
- Recommendation: notable contribution but requires major revisions—discuss and ablate the LPC, fix clarity/notation, and strengthen experimental design.
