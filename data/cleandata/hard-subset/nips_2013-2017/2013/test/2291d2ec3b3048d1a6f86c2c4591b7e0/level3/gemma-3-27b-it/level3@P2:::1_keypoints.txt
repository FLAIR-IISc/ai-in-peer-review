- Proposes automatically discovering latent domains within datasets for domain adaptation rather than treating each dataset as a single domain.  
- Formulates domain discovery via a principled optimization combining “maximum distinctiveness” and “maximum learnability.”  
- Solves the formulation with a nonparametric approach and an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Demonstrates consistent performance gains on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.  
- Paper is generally well-written and technical details are clearly presented.  
- Claim that appearance-based clustering leads to per-category domains lacks empirical evidence within the paper.  
- Necessity and impact of the label-prior constraint (LPC) are not thoroughly justified; results without LPC and comparisons to simple clustering are missing.  
- Definition of “maximally different in distribution” is ambiguous; more explanation of how feature/label distributions are characterized beyond the kernel metric is needed.  
- Choice and implications of the kernel (and kernel hyperparameters) are not discussed.  
- Minor clarity issues: β_{mk} simplex phrasing is confusing and Eq (1) has a typographical inconsistency (M'k vs Mk').  
- Selection of K and the justification for the stated Kmax bound are under-explained; need rationale for the limit.  
- Description of geodesic flow kernel usage is sparse — clarify whether a public implementation was used or reimplemented for reproducibility.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of why the method outperforms baselines and specific failure cases the method overcomes.  
- Reviewer leans slightly toward acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above concerns.  
- Suggests adding Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011) to related work.
