- Proposal: automatic discovery of latent domains in visual datasets to improve domain adaptation.  
- Two axioms: latent domains should have maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Method: formulate kernel-based domain discrepancy optimization, relax to continuous quadratic problem, use DWCV to select number of domains.  
- Motivation: challenges the dataset=domain assumption, noting datasets often contain multiple non-semantic latent domains (lighting, viewpoint, etc.).  
- Claim critique: assertion that appearance clustering yields per-category domains lacks empirical evidence; needs k-means comparison.  
- LPC concern: necessity of the Label-Prior Constraint is unclear; show ablations without LPC and comparisons to unsupervised baselines and random partitions.  
- Clarification request: specify what “maximally different in distribution” refers to (features, labels, or other).  
- β_{mk} question: determine whether β_{mk} already lies on a simplex; if so, β_k ∈ Δ may be redundant.  
- Constraint inconsistency: constraints around line 182 appear different from lines 165–166 and need to be reconciled.  
- Theoretical gap: bound referenced at line 215 is stated without justification or proof and should be provided.  
- Reproducibility: state whether the geodesic flow kernel was reimplemented or sourced from public code.  
- Insight gap: provide systematic analysis explaining why the method outperforms baselines and which baseline failures it overcomes.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly articulates the dataset bias problem.  
- Strength: the two axiomatic properties offer a principled approach to latent domain discovery.  
- Strength: experiments on object and action recognition show consistent improvements; DWCV is a practical contribution.  
- Minor corrections: fix Eq. (1) notation (Mk' vs M'k) and cite Torralba & Efros (CVPR 2011) on dataset bias.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel and significant contribution with clear experiments, though some methodological details require clarification.  
- Recommendation: accept — concerns are important but addressable, and the rebuttal addressed them adequately.
