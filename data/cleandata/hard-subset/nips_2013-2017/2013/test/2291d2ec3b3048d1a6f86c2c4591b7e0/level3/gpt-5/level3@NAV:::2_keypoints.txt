- Tackles automatic partitioning of mixed visual datasets into latent domains that are separable and discriminatively useful for class labels.  
- Proposes nonparametric distinctiveness criterion using kernel mean discrepancy (MMD) across partitions.  
- Selects number of domains K via a learnability criterion based on domain-wise cross-validation accuracy.  
- Contrasts with common “dataset = domain” assumption and relates to prior domain discovery and kernel two-sample tests.  
- Extensive object and action recognition experiments show clear gains over datasets-as-domains and prior work [19].  
- Conditional reshaping of heterogeneous test sets is practically relevant but effectively assumes a transductive/imputation setting.  
- Optimization: maximizing a convex quadratic under linear constraints is NP-hard; no approximation quality or convergence guarantees are provided.  
- The two criteria (separability and learnability) are not jointly optimized—learnability only used as a wrapper for K selection.  
- Risk of leakage and underspecified protocol in test-set reshaping; description and assumptions need clarification.  
- Paper is well written and organized but would benefit from illustrative figures showing visual factors captured by discovered domains.  
- Originality is high; automatic domain definition is novel and timely.  
- Significance: empirical gains are substantial, but results likely underestimated due to outdated image representation (single BoW of sparse SURF, 800 words).  
- Recommendation to upgrade features (spatial pyramid, dense SURF/SIFT, Fisher Vectors) and use appropriate histogram similarities (χ2 or intersection) instead of Euclidean.  
- Pros: clear novel problem, strong empirical improvements, nonparametric criterion avoids restrictive mixture assumptions.  
- Cons: lack of optimization guarantees, separation and learnability not jointly optimized, underspecified test reshaping, outdated features and missing histogram-similarity details.  
- Verdict: Accept — valuable and promising contribution despite methodological gaps.
