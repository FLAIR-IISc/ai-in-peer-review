- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two desired properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Develops a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization to discover domains across training and test data.  
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity datasets, showing identified domains outperform original datasets and a competing method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and thorough related work discussion.  
- Main methodological weakness: uses outdated image representation (sparse SURF BoW with 800 codewords).  
- Recommends upgrading descriptors to modern representations (e.g., spatial pyramid dense SURF or Fisher Vectors).  
- Unclear which similarity measure is used for histogram comparison—Euclidean would be inappropriate; suggest X^2 or intersection kernel.  
- Lacks illustrative figures to better explain the approach and evaluation.  
- Unclear imputation setting in the “reshaping” process; needs clarification.  
- Needs more thorough evaluation, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Should provide a more detailed discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Could strengthen discussion on potential real-world impact and significance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address representation, clarity, evaluation, and explanation issues.
