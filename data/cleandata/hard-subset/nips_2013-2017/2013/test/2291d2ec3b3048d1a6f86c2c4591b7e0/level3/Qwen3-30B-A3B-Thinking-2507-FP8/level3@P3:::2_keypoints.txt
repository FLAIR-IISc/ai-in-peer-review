- Addresses the underexplored problem of automatically defining meaningful domains within visual datasets rather than treating whole datasets as domains.

- Proposes reshaping datasets into latent domains optimized for two properties: maximum distinctiveness (kernel-based distribution difference) and maximum learnability (domain-wise cross-validation to choose K).

- Key insight: visual datasets contain hidden domains (pose, lighting, background, resolution) that affect adaptation and should be discovered, not assumed.

- Method contrasts with prior work (e.g., [20]) that used parametric mixtures and with naive practices of treating datasets as domains.

- Empirical results show significant improvements over using datasets as domains, random/K-means partitions, and method [20] on object and human activity recognition tasks.

- Experimental design is comprehensive and includes well-motivated DWCV for selecting the number of domains.

- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and explains technical details sufficiently.

- Original contribution is novel and addresses an important gap in domain adaptation literature.

- Main weakness: outdated image representation (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords) limits demonstrating the method’s full potential.

- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unclear and likely inappropriate (Euclidean distance used instead of X² or intersection kernels).

- Optimization concern: the two criteria (distinctiveness and learnability) are not jointly optimized—learnability is used only for model selection (K), not integrated into the partitioning objective.

- Explanation of the reshaping process for test sets (Section 4.3) is unclear and seems to assume an unstated imputation setting.

- Related work coverage is adequate but could better distinguish this approach from [20] and other clustering methods like [27].

- Pro arguments: important problem, principled method, strong empirical gains, clear writing, practical implications.

- Con arguments: outdated features, incomplete integration of objectives, unclear test-set reshaping, unclear histogram similarity treatment.

- Overall verdict: Accept with minor revisions — promising and valuable contribution that needs updated features and clarification of optimization and test-set reshaping.
