- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two desiderata for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Defines a kernel-based difference function between feature vectors and partitions data to maximize inter-domain differences.  
- Formulation is an integer program relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise explanation of the method.  
- Experimental results show performance gains on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.  
- Motivation for the approach is unclear and not well-justified.  
- No evidence that clustering by appearance produces per-category domains or that the model prevents per-category clustering.  
- Necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear; results without it are not provided.  
- Lacks comparisons to simple baselines such as k-means, unsupervised object discovery methods, and random equal partitioning.  
- The meaning of "maximally different in distribution" is ambiguous—unclear which distribution (features, labels, etc.) is referenced.  
- Unclear whether β_{mk} as formulated lies on a simplex and constraints around line 182 differ from earlier ones—needs clarification.  
- A bound referenced on line 215 requires justification or proof.  
- Details on use/implementation of the geodesic flow kernel (reimplementation vs. public code) are missing.  
- The paper should provide insight into why the method outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failure cases it addresses.  
- Minor correction: Eq (1) should use Mk' instead of M'k.  
- Suggested citation: Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011).  
- Overall potential for significant contribution to domain adaptation, but addressing the above concerns would strengthen the paper.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10, confidence 8/10.
