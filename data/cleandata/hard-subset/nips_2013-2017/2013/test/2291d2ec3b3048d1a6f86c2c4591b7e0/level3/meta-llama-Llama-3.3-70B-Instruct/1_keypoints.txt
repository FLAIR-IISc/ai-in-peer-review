- Proposes a novel method to discover latent domains for domain adaptation, formulated as an integer program relaxed to a quadratic continuous optimization.
- Evaluated on two datasets and reports improved classification performance over an existing domain adaptation method.
- Strength: effectively identifies latent domains that can boost classification accuracy.
- Strength: non-parametric nature increases flexibility and applicability across datasets.
- Strength: uses domain-wise cross-validation to select the number of domains in a principled way.
- Weakness: overall motivation and formulation of the model are not clearly explained.
- Weakness: claim that appearance-based clustering reshapes datasets into per-category domains is not convincingly justified.
- Weakness: the "label prior constraint" is introduced without sufficient intuition or explanation of necessity.
- Weakness: limited comparison to other baseline methods (e.g., K-means, random partitioning).
- Weakness: geodesic flow kernel use is under-explained; implementation details and parameter settings are missing.
- Suggestion: include deeper analysis of the discovered domains and the factors influencing their formation.
- Overall: promising and interesting approach but needs clearer motivation, more baseline comparisons, and deeper empirical analysis to strengthen the contribution.
