- Paper proposes discovering latent visual domains using two desiderata: maximum distinctiveness (MMD-based) and maximum learnability (DWCV-based).
- Distinctiveness is operationalized by maximizing pairwise RKHS mean-embedding discrepancies; discrete assignments relaxed to simplex weights β and solved as a constrained QP; K chosen via domain-wise cross-validation.
- Authors add a label-prior constraint forcing per-domain class proportions to match global priors and perform conditional test-set reshaping to align test data with discovered domains.
- Builds on kernel two-sample tests (MMD), multi-source adaptation, and extends prior latent-domain work (Hoffman et al. ECCV’12) by avoiding parametric GMM assumptions.
- Strength: addresses an important, practical problem of dataset≠domain with a principled, nonparametric formulation.
- Strength: clear formalization of distinctiveness and a pragmatic learnability-based K selection (DWCV).
- Strength: nonparametric approach handles complex, overlapping factors better than GMMs.
- Strength: conditional test reshaping is novel and yields empirical gains; qualitative analyses offer intuitive interpretations of discovered factors.
- Strength: empirical results compare favorably to baselines and relevant prior work.
- Weakness: method is not truly unsupervised—uses labels in LPC and DWCV despite occasional “unsupervised” wording.
- Weakness: label-prior constraint is a strong assumption (no label shift); may suppress real domain factors correlated with class frequency and needs sensitivity analysis (δ, missing classes).
- Weakness: optimization is non-convex in practice despite wording; paper lacks details on solver, initialization, restarts, complexity, and robustness across runs.
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology and notation (dataset/domain/latent-domain, r(·→·) usage); presentation and equation references need fixing.
- Weakness: experimental design under-specified (source/target splits, unclear MATCHING strategy); leave-one-dataset-out would be clearer.
- Weakness: potential double-dipping—DWCV selects K using the same labeled data used to form domains; nested CV or held-out validation needed.
- Weakness: limited ablations—no studies on kernel bandwidth sensitivity, δ values, removing LPC, or runtime/scalability.
- Quality: MMD-based discrepancy and QP reshaping are sound, but non-convex objectives, strong LPC assumption, and missing optimization/ablation details weaken the technical evaluation.
- Clarity: overall readable but marred by inconsistent terminology, notation lapses, and imprecise descriptions of multi-source strategies; suggest glossary, schematic, and pseudocode.
- Originality: novel combination of nonparametric distinctiveness and learnability-based model selection; conditional test reshaping is a meaningful addition.
- Significance: approach could impact adaptation and dataset construction if LPC is validated and optimization/experimental protocols are clarified.
- Suggestions: explicitly state supervised nature, provide ablations (no LPC, δ, label shift, bandwidth), detail optimization and runtimes, define MATCHING and protocol, unify notation, and use nested/held-out CV for K.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, principled nonparametric framework, coherent criteria combination, and empirical improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: mischaracterized supervision, strong LPC, non-convex optimization with limited details, insufficient experimental rigor and ablations, and notation issues.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying supervision/assumptions, tightening experiments and ablations, and improving optimization transparency and notation.
