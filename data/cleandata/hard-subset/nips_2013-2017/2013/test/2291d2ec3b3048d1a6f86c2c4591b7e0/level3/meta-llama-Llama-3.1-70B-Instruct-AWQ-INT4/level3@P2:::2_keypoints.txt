- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets, addressing domain adaptation needs.  
- Introduces two domain properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Presents a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains across training and test data.  
- Proposes domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to identify the optimal number of domains.  
- Extensive experiments on object recognition and human activity recognition show significant improvements over using datasets-as-domains and a recent domain discovery method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and strong motivations.  
- Technical contributions are substantial and clearly presented.  
- Optimization is not clearly shown to closely solve the original objectives.  
- Maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability are not well integrated; learnability is only used as a wrapper for model selection rather than part of partitioning.  
- An integrated optimization jointly maximizing separability and learnability would be preferable.  
- Image representation (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords) is outdated and likely too weak for current standards.  
- Recommend upgrading descriptors (e.g., spatial pyramid dense SURF or Fisher Vectors) to improve performance.  
- Similarity measure for histogram comparison is unclear; should consider X^2 or intersection kernel.  
- Use of the test set in the "reshaping" process is poorly explained and appears to assume an unstated imputation setting—needs clarification.  
- Overall evaluation: valuable and promising contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
