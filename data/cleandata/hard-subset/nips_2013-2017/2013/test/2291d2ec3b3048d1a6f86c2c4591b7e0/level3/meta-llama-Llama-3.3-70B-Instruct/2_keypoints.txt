- Proposes automatic partitioning of images into latent domains (e.g., viewpoint, illumination) for domain adaptation.
- Driven by two principles: creating distinct domains in feature space and making them trainable for discriminative classifiers.
- Novel and significant: little prior work on automatically discovering useful domains to improve classifier training.
- Well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough related-work review highlighting limitations of existing approaches.
- Extensively evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition, showing improved classification accuracy.
- Uses nonparametric methods to discover latent domains; introduces maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability as desirable domain properties.
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental support; authors are careful and honest about strengths and weaknesses.
- Main limitation: proposed optimization method is not fully convincing and the two driving criteria are not well integrated.
- Presentation weakness: lacks figures to illustrate concepts, reducing clarity.
- Major methodological weakness: uses simple/outdated image descriptors (bag-of-words with sparse SURF); should use modern representations (spatial pyramids, Fisher Vectors).
- Strengths summarized: novel approach, thorough multi-task evaluation, potential impact on dataset construction, and principled nonparametric discovery.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10, reviewer confidence 0.8.
