- Paper proposes automatically discovering latent domains within datasets using two principles: maximum distinctiveness (maximize pairwise distribution differences) and maximum learnability (choose K via domain-wise cross-validation).

- Method formulates distinctiveness as an integer program using a kernel two-sample test (MMD) with a label prior constraint, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program for tractability.

- Evaluated on object and action recognition datasets; discovered domains improve domain adaptation over treating datasets as single domains and outperform a competing method (Hoffman et al.).

- Strength: Novel and significant problem framing that challenges the dataset-as-domain assumption and could change domain adaptation practice.

- Strength: Principled, non-parametric technical approach with clear conceptual foundation in distinctiveness and learnability.

- Strength: Paper is well-written, well-structured, and clearly explains motivation, methods, and experiments.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains demonstrated on multiple standard datasets.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to simple but relevant baselines (K-means clustering, random partitioning) to isolate the benefit of the proposed optimization.

- Weakness: No ablation study to justify the necessity of the label prior constraint (LPC).

- Weakness: Several formulation clarifications needed (explicitly state which distribution is measured, clarify β_mk definition and simplex treatment, justify relaxation of constraints and the K_max bound).

- Weakness: Experimental reproducibility details missing (e.g., whether a public or custom implementation of the geodesic flow kernel was used) and deeper qualitative analysis (failure cases) is needed.

- Minor: Typo in Eq. (1) (`M'k` likely `M_k'`) and missing citation to Torralba & Efros (2011) which is relevant to dataset bias.

- Conclusion: Promising idea with solid results but currently weakened by missing baseline comparisons, required ablations, and clarity issues; reviewer is borderline and may raise score if concerns are addressed.
