- Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains by partitioning data to maximize inter-domain difference using a kernel-based metric and an integer program relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization with assignment and label-prior constraints.

- Evaluated on object and action recognition tasks, showing improvements over prior domain-adaptation method [19].

- Novel, well-written, non-parametric approach that challenges the dataset-as-domain assumption; related work (Torralba & Efros CVPR 2011) is relevant but uncited.

- Primary motivation concern: unclear that maximizing appearance-based distributional differences yields domains useful for adaptation rather than simply splitting categories by visual sub-types.

- Evaluation weaknesses: no ablation to show necessity of the label-prior constraint and missing comparisons to simple baselines (k-means on features, unsupervised object discovery, random equal-partition).

- Clarification needed on what “maximally different in distribution” means (feature distributions, label distributions, etc.).

- Technical clarifications requested: justify the bound on line 215; clarify constraint differences around line 182; confirm whether β_{mk} is already constrained to the simplex (line 166).

- Want more insight into why the method outperforms baselines, with examples of failure modes the approach avoids.

- Reproducibility request: include implementation details for the geodesic flow kernel.

- Minor fix: correct typo in Eq. (1) (M'k should be Mk').

- Overall assessment: promising and novel but requires stronger motivational and experimental support.
