- Paper proposes "reshaping": automatically discovering latent domains within visual datasets for better domain adaptation.  
- Core principles: maximize domain distinctiveness (sum of pairwise MMDs in RKHS) and maximize learnability (choose K via domain-wise cross-validation).  
- Optimization maximizes distinctiveness with a Label Prior Constraint (LPC) to preserve overall class priors and avoid clustering by category; integer program relaxed to a continuous quadratic program.  
- Extends method to conditionally reshape an unlabeled test set to match discovered source domains.  
- Uses non-parametric MMD (vs. prior parametric Gaussian-mixture approaches), argued to suit complex visual data better.  
- Experimental evaluation on object/action recognition shows reshaped domains improve multi-source adaptation and test-set reshaping yields significant gains.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and the formulation is principled and practical (relaxation + LPC).  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, with intuitive motivation and helpful qualitative analyses.  
- Contribution is original: reframes domain adaptation by learning domains from data rather than assuming pre-defined datasets as domains.  
- Work is significant and likely to influence how practitioners structure training data for robust recognition.  
- Main weakness: scalability — O(M^2) kernel matrix makes the method impractical for large-scale datasets, with no discussed approximations.  
- Missing optimization details: solver choice, runtimes, and convergence properties are not provided, hurting reproducibility.  
- LPC is a strong assumption (constant class proportions across domains) and may not hold in many real-world settings.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — novel, well-evaluated, and likely to inspire follow-up work despite scalability and minor-detail issues.
