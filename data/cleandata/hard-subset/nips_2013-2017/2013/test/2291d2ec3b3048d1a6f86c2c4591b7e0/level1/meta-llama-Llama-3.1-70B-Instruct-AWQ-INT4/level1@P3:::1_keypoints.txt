- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets, important for domain adaptation.  
- Introduces two properties for domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation with an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains in training and test data.  
- Paper is well-motivated and the latent domain discovery problem is clearly defined.  
- Clear explanation of challenges in visual domain adaptation and limitations of existing approaches.  
- Method and optimization problem are thoroughly described and well-formulated.  
- Experimental results are extensive and show effectiveness on object recognition and human activity recognition.  
- Provides detailed analysis of identified domains and methods for choosing the optimal number of domains.  
- Well-written paper with a thorough related-work comparison.  
- Strength: novel approach to latent domain discovery.  
- Strength: nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization.  
- Strength: extensive experimental validation and domain analysis.  
- Weakness: assumes number of domains is unknown, which may not hold in some applications.  
- Weakness: original optimization is NP-hard and relaxed to a continuous problem that may not be optimal.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of the method’s guarantees and limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experiments, and insightful analysis of domains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: assumption about unknown domain count, NP-hard relaxation concerns, and missing theoretical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested future work to address weaknesses.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
