This paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in image or video datasets, which is a crucial step in visual recognition problems where domain adaptation is essential. The authors introduce two desirable properties that latent domains should possess: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability. They devise a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization procedure to discover domains among both training and test data.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and concise language. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain their contributions. The proposed approach is extensively evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors address a significant problem in visual recognition, which is the mismatch between training and testing data distributions.
* The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the underlying assumptions and principles.
* The experimental results are thorough and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method on multiple datasets.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The optimization problem is NP-hard, and the authors relax it into a continuous optimization problem. While this is a common approach, it may not always yield the optimal solution.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach, which may be a concern for large-scale datasets.
* Some of the experimental results, such as the choice of kernel bandwidth, may be sensitive to hyperparameter tuning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses a significant problem in visual recognition and proposes a novel and effective approach to discover latent domains.
* The experimental results are thorough and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method on multiple datasets.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear structure and concise language.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The optimization problem is NP-hard, and the authors relax it into a continuous optimization problem, which may not always yield the optimal solution.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach, which may be a concern for large-scale datasets.
* Some of the experimental results may be sensitive to hyperparameter tuning.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, as it addresses a significant problem in visual recognition and proposes a novel and effective approach to discover latent domains. However, the authors should be encouraged to provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and to explore ways to improve the optimization problem.
