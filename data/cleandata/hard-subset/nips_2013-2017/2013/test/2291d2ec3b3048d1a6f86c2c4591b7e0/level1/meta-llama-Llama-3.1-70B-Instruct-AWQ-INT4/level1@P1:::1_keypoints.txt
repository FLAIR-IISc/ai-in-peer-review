- Proposes an approach to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets for visual recognition to improve domain adaptation.  
- Argues existing domain adaptation methods overlook complex distribution factors like pose, illumination, and background.  
- Introduces two properties for domains: maximum distinctiveness (domains differ greatly) and maximum learnability (strong discriminative model per domain).  
- Develops a nonparametric formulation and an efficient optimization procedure to discover domains.  
- Uses a kernel-based measure to quantify distribution differences and optimizes assignments to maximize total distinctiveness.  
- Proposes domain-wise cross-validation to determine the optimal number of domains.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the method and procedures.  
- Experiments on object recognition and human activity recognition show improved adaptation performance.  
- Provides thorough analysis of identified domains and how many domains are optimal.  
- Strength: novel, challenging unsupervised problem formulation with clear optimization and validation procedures.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate effectiveness across tasks.  
- Weakness: assumes data instances are annotated, limiting applicability to unannotated real-world data.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity for large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: does not compare against other state-of-the-art domain adaptation techniques.  
- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance but suggests addressing annotation assumption, complexity analysis, and comparisons in future work.
