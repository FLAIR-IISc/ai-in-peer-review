The paper "Reshaping Visual Datasets for Domain Adaptation" presents a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in image or video datasets. The authors propose two key properties for the identified domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability. The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly explained.

The authors provide a thorough review of related work in domain adaptation, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches that rely on manual definition of domains. They also discuss the challenges of visual domain adaptation, where images and videos are affected by various extraneous factors such as pose, illumination, occlusion, camera resolution, and background.

The proposed approach is based on a nonparametric formulation that imposes maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability on the identified domains. The authors devise an efficient optimization procedure that can successfully discover domains among both training and test data.

The paper presents extensive experimental results on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors evaluate the identified domains using various metrics, including the expected best possible accuracy of using the identified domains separately for adaptation.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in image or video datasets, which is a challenging unsupervised learning problem.
* The paper presents a thorough review of related work in domain adaptation, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches.
* The proposed approach is based on a nonparametric formulation that imposes maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability on the identified domains.
* The paper presents extensive experimental results on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The weaknesses of the paper include:
