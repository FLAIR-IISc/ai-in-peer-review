- Problem: Dataset ≠ domain — current domain adaptation often ignores overlapping visual factors causing domain shifts.  
- Goal: Automatically discover latent domains in visual datasets rather than assuming datasets are discrete domains.  
- Method: Nonparametric kernel-based formulation that maximizes domain distinctiveness and learnability, with convex relaxation and efficient optimization.  
- Model selection: DWCV procedure to determine the optimal number of domains K without arbitrary choice.  
- Relation to prior work: Contrasts with Hoffman et al.'s parametric GMM approach and distinguishes domain discovery from general clustering literature.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on multiple datasets (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and tasks (object/action recognition) showing consistent improvements.  
- Qualitative results: Visualizations show discovered domains align with meaningful factors (pose, color, background).  
- Strength: Well-motivated problem addressing a key limitation in domain adaptation practice.  
- Strength: Technically sound use of kernel-based discrepancy and practical convex relaxation.  
- Strength: DWCV is a thoughtful, non-arbitrary method for selecting K.  
- Weakness: Label prior constraint (LPC) lacks theoretical justification and may not hold in real datasets.  
- Weakness: “Nonparametric” claim is misleading because kernel bandwidth choices introduce parametric assumptions.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity and scalability (esp. equation 4 and DWCV N-fold CV) are not discussed.  
- Weakness: Limited comparative analysis showing clear cases where nonparametric approach outperforms [20] due to distributional assumptions.  
- Significance: Shifts focus from adapting between assumed domains to discovering meaningful domains, with practical performance gains.  
- Recommendation: Overall positive — recommend acceptance despite some limitations in LPC justification, nonparametric claims, and complexity discussion.
