This paper tackles a central gap in domain adaptation for vision: datasets are often heterogeneous mixtures of latent conditions (pose, illumination, background), yet most methods equate datasets with domains. The authors propose to automatically reshape datasets into latent domains by enforcing two axioms: maximum distinctiveness (domains should be distributionally far apart) and maximum learnability (each domain should support a strong discriminative model). Distinctiveness is operationalized via a kernel two-sample distance in RKHS (akin to MMD [21]) aggregated over all domain pairs, subject to a label-prior constraint that preserves class proportions. The combinatorial assignment is relaxed to a constrained quadratic program over simplex weights. Learnability selects K via domain-wise cross-validation on source labels. The method is evaluated on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and multi-view action recognition (IXMAS), showing improvements versus treating datasets as domains and against the latent-domain mixture model of Hoffman et al. [20]. The paper relates to domain adaptation [1–4, 9, 10], dataset bias [16], and generalized clustering with discriminability [27], and complements geodesic-flow adaptation [4] used in experiments.

Quality: The formulation is principled and empirically compelling across two tasks, and the conditional reshaping of test sets is well-motivated. However, optimization details (solver, initialization, runtime, scalability with M since K is M×M) are sparse; the continuous maximization of a convex quadratic under linear constraints remains NP-hard, so practical heuristics and sensitivity analyses are needed. The label prior constraint is strong and could fail under label shift; no ablation on δ or violations is provided. Statistical significance and variance across random splits are not reported.

Clarity: The exposition is generally clear, with useful intuition and ablations on K. Key details for conditional reshaping are deferred to the supplement; a concise algorithm box in the main text would help reproducibility.

Originality: Nonparametric latent-domain discovery via maximizing inter-domain MMD under supervised constraints, coupled with learnability-driven K selection, is novel relative to [20] and [27]. A comparison to kernel k-means or spectral clustering with similar constraints would strengthen claims.

Significance: Identifying latent domains is practically important and likely to influence multi-source adaptation pipelines. Demonstrated gains and qualitative analyses support impact, though scalability to larger, modern datasets remains to be shown.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, novel problem formulation with nonparametric objective (MMD-based)
- Consistent improvements on two benchmarks and scenarios (including test-time reshaping)
- Thoughtful analysis of K and qualitative insights into discovered factors

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited optimization/scalability details; unclear robustness to initialization
- Strong label-prior assumption; no study under label shift
- Missing comparisons to kernel k-means/spectral baselines and statistical significance tests
