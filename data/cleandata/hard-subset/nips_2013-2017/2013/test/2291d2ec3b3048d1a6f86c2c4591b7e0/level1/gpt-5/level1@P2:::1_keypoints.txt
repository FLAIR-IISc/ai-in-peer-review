- Problem & contribution: Proposes automatic discovery of latent domains in visual datasets by maximizing domain distinctiveness (pairwise MMD) and learnability (domain-wise CV), with a relaxed continuous QP assignment and conditional test-time reshaping.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on domain adaptation, MMD/kernel embeddings, and multi-source adaptation; differs from Hoffman et al. by being nonparametric, introducing learnability-based K selection, and adding test reshaping.

- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem—latent-domain discovery improves robustness of visual adaptation.

- Strength: Conceptually clean nonparametric objective using pairwise MMD as a distribution-free distinctiveness proxy.

- Strength: Practical K selection via domain-wise cross-validation to avoid over-fragmentation that harms training.

- Strength: Conditional reshaping of unlabeled test data is a useful extension for better alignment.

- Strength: Empirical gains shown on object and action recognition tasks; discovered domains qualitatively align with pose/background.

- Weakness: Optimization is a nonconvex QP with no approximation, convergence guarantees, or sensitivity analysis to initialization and restarts.

- Weakness: Label prior constraint (LPC) assumes identical class priors across domains and may bias discovery if priors differ.

- Weakness: Risk of per-class clustering remains because distinctiveness on P(x) can correlate with class structure; need ablation with class-conditional criteria.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—requires storing M×M kernel matrix and solving QP with O(MK) variables; no discussion of approximations for large datasets.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity not studied—kernel bandwidth, kernel choice, and LPC slack δ lack analysis.

- Weakness: K selection via domain-wise CV may overfit to labeled source; alternative model selection criteria (MDL, stability) not explored.

- Weakness: Evaluation could be broader—statistical significance, more baselines (e.g., balanced clustering, spectral clustering with regularizers), and clearer description of the MATCHING metric.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound with appropriate use of MMD and practical algorithms, but limited theoretical and robustness analyses.

- Clarity assessment: Generally clear and organized, but some constraints and mechanics (per-instance constraint, hard assignment) need more intuition and pseudocode.

- Originality assessment: Novel and notable—nonparametric latent-domain discovery with learnability-based K selection and conditional test reshaping.

- Significance assessment: Moderate–high potential impact if scalability and robustness issues are addressed.

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles underexplored problem, simple effective nonparametric method, useful test reshaping, solid empirical gains.

- Cons against acceptance: Limited theoretical/robustness analysis, unrealistic LPC assumption without sensitivity tests, and unclear scalability.

- Suggested improvements: Report complexity and runtimes; add ablations (no LPC, class-conditional MMD, kernel bandwidth, δ); test robustness to varying class priors and random restarts.

- Suggested improvements: Clarify MATCHING metric, include more baselines (e.g., spectral clustering with label regularization), and report statistical significance and confidence intervals.

- Recommendation: Weak accept.

- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
