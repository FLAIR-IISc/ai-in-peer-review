- Proposes a novel method to automatically discover latent domains in image/video datasets using two properties: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Paper is well-written and ideas are clearly explained.
- Thorough related-work review highlighting limitations of manual domain definitions and challenges in visual domain adaptation (pose, illumination, occlusion, etc.).
- Uses a nonparametric formulation that enforces maximum distinctiveness and learnability for discovered domains.
- Presents an efficient optimization procedure that can discover domains across training and test data.
- Extensive experiments on object recognition and human activity recognition demonstrate effectiveness.
- Evaluates discovered domains with metrics including expected best possible accuracy when adapting per domain.
- Strength: Addresses a challenging unsupervised problem with a novel approach.
- Strength: Comprehensive literature review situating the contribution well.
- Strength: Dual-property nonparametric formulation is conceptually strong.
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across tasks.
- Weakness: Optimization is NP-hard; continuous relaxation may not yield optimal solutions.
- Weakness: No clear justification or selection procedure for the number of domains used in experiments.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and runtime.
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, solid experiments, and thorough related work.
- Con-accept arguments: concerns about relaxation optimality, domain count choice, and missing complexity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
