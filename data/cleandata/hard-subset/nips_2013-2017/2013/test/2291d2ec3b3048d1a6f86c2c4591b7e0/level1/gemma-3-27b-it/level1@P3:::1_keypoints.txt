- Tackles domain adaptation by rejecting the dataset-as-domain assumption and proposing automatic discovery of latent domains.  
- Proposes principled objectives: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability to find latent domains.  
- Uses a nonparametric formulation with an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition, showing improvements over baselines.  
- Strong contribution: handling reshaped test datasets addresses a realistic scenario.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and reviews related work thoroughly.  
- Label Prior Constraint (LPC) explanation is hand-wavy; needs rigorous justification and analysis of violations/sensitivity.  
- Missing detailed scalability analysis—runtime scaling with number of instances (M) and latent domains (K) is unclear.  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel appears arbitrary; needs exploration or justification of other kernels.  
- Results lack statistical rigor—confidence intervals or p-values should be reported to confirm significance.  
- Comparison to Hoffman et al. [20] is superficial; requires deeper analysis of assumptions, costs, and differences (e.g., distributional assumptions).  
- Ablation studies are insufficient—need separate evaluations of distinctiveness-only and learnability-only.  
- Qualitative analysis is limited; include misclassified examples within each discovered domain to reveal limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focused on LPC, scalability, statistical significance, related-work comparison, and ablations.
