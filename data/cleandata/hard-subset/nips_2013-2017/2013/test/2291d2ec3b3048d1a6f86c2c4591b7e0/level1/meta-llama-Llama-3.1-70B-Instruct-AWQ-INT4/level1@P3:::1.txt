This paper proposes a novel approach to automatically discover latent domains in image or video datasets, which is essential for domain adaptation in visual recognition problems. The authors introduce two key properties for the identified domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability. The proposed method is based on a nonparametric formulation and efficient optimization procedure, which can successfully discover domains among both training and test data.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of discovering latent domains is well-defined. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the challenges in visual domain adaptation and the limitations of existing approaches. The proposed method is thoroughly described, and the optimization problem is well-formulated.

The experimental results are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the identified domains and the optimal number of domains, which sheds light on the main factors that determine the domains.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and the experimental results. The related work section is thorough, and the authors provide a clear comparison with existing approaches.
