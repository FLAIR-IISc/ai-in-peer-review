- Problem: Equating datasets with domains is a weak proxy for real-world domain shifts in visual recognition.
- Proposal: Automatically discover latent domains by optimizing for “maximum distinctiveness” and “maximum learnability.”
- Formulation: Cast as a non-parametric problem with an efficient optimization procedure and extension to test-set reshaping.
- Literature positioning: Builds on prior domain adaptation work but shifts focus from algorithms to discovering domains; relates to Hoffman et al. [20] while offering a nonparametric approach.
- Novelty: Fresh perspective of reshaping datasets into meaningful domains rather than assuming domains are given.
- Soundness: Principled framework with well-formulated optimization addressing key challenges.
- Empirical validation: Consistent improvements on object recognition and human activity recognition tasks.
- Comprehensive experiments: Includes test-set reshaping and analysis of identified domains (e.g., Figure 1) for stronger evaluation.
- Weakness — computational cost: Efficiency claims lack thorough discussion of scalability to large datasets.
- Weakness — kernel choice: Reliance on Gaussian kernels may limit applicability; kernel learning or alternatives deserve exploration.
- Weakness — DWCV: Domain-wise cross-validation for selecting domain number feels heuristic and needs theoretical justification.
- Weakness — clarity: Section 2’s mathematical notation is dense and would benefit from clearer explanations and stepwise breakdown.
- Acceptance argument: Addresses an important overlooked aspect, is novel and well-motivated, and shows compelling empirical results.
- Rejection argument: Potential scalability issues and heuristic methodological components warrant caution.
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and discuss computational considerations.
