- Problem: Treating entire datasets as single domains is a coarse approximation that hides latent sub-domains (illumination, pose, background) and can hurt adaptation.
- Contribution: Proposes an unsupervised method to automatically discover and "reshape" datasets into meaningful latent domains for better domain adaptation.
- Principle — Maximum Distinctiveness: Discover domains by maximizing Total Domain Distinctiveness (TDD), the sum of pairwise RKHS distances between domain distributions (non-parametric).
- Principle — Maximum Learnability: Choose the number of domains K by maximizing classifier performance within discovered domains to ensure sufficient data per domain.
- Optimization: Formulates domain assignment as an integer program maximizing TDD, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program for tractability.
- Label Prior Constraint (LPC): Enforces that class distributions within discovered domains match the overall prior to avoid trivial class-based clustering.
- K selection: Uses Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) to pick K by maximizing average within-domain cross-validation accuracy.
- Test-time extension: Proposes conditional reshaping of heterogeneous test sets to align with discovered source domains.
- Novelty: First concrete automated method to reshape datasets for domain adaptation, combining non-parametric distinctiveness with learnability for model selection.
- Difference from prior work: Uses non-parametric RKHS distances (MMD) instead of parametric assumptions like Gaussian mixtures (e.g., Hoffman et al.).
- Empirical results: Thorough experiments on object and activity recognition show significant gains over using original datasets and a competing method, with oracle GOURS establishing an upper bound.
- Technical strength: Method is well-grounded in kernel two-sample testing and the LPC is a well-justified safeguard against trivial solutions.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with intuitive motivation, consistent notation, clear methodology, and informative figures.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental overlooked assumption in domain adaptation and offers a practical pre-processing tool likely to impact the field.
- Scalability concern: Requires computing/storing an M x M kernel matrix, limiting applicability to very large datasets without approximation strategies.
- Optimization concern: Relaxed problem is a non-concave maximization (NP-hard); more details needed on the solver used, scalability in M and K, and solution quality (local vs global optima).
- Heuristic K-selection: DWCV is intuitive but heuristic and lacks theoretical justification linking it to improved adaptation on external targets.
- Reproducibility: More details on optimization procedure and solver choices would improve assessment of robustness and reproducibility.
