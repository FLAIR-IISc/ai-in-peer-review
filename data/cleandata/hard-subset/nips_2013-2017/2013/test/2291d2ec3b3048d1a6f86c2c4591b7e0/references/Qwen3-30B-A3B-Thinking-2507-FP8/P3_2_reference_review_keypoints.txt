- Proposes reshaping visual datasets into latent domains using two axioms: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability, via nonparametric kernel-based methods.  
- Addresses a compelling, understudied question: what constitutes a "domain" in visual recognition.  
- Introduces a novel, principled framework (two axioms) and avoids limitations of parametric clustering.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation across multiple datasets and tasks (object and action recognition).  
- Offers qualitative insights into factors driving domain boundaries (pose, appearance, background).  
- Reports an "oracle" MATCHING strategy that is misleading because it requires target domain labels.  
- Does not discuss computational complexity or scalability of the convex quadratic optimization.  
- DWCV procedure for selecting K lacks sufficient evidence of robustness and may be sensitive to input dataset count.  
- Risks overfitting when reshaping training data, especially with large K and continuous relaxations.  
- MATCHING reliance on target labels is unrealistic; paper should clarify operation without labels.  
- Claims about which visual factors matter are qualitative and lack supporting quantitative analysis.  
- Comparison to Hoffman et al. (2014) needs clearer examples showing where parametric approaches fail.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution, but requires minor revisions to address practical realism and robustness.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
