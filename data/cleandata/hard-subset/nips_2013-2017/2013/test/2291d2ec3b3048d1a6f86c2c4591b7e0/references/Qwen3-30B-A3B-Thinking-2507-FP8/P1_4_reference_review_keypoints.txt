- Addresses the misconception that entire visual datasets are single domains and proposes reshaping datasets into latent domains.
- Introduces two axiomatic properties for latent domains: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Develops nonparametric, kernel-based methods to identify latent domains and a domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to choose the number of domains.
- Evaluated on Caltech-256/Amazon/DSLR/Webcam and IXMAS, showing consistent improvements over original datasets and Hoffman et al.

- Technical approach is sound with clear theoretical foundation and well-justified claims.
- Nonparametric method avoids restrictive clustering assumptions and DWCV is a practical procedure for domain count selection.
- Novel contribution that fills a conceptual gap in domain adaptation by discovering latent domains within datasets.
- Significant empirical gains across multiple tasks, with implications for future dataset construction and adaptation research.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with informative figures and reproducible experimental details.

- Minor theoretical gap: needs stronger justification linking the two axioms to known domain adaptation theory and performance bounds.
- Practical limitation: assumes test domains can be reshaped conditionally on identified training domains, which may not hold when test domains are unknown.
- Comparison gap: lacks engagement with more recent multi-source/multi-domain adaptation methods beyond Hoffman et al.

- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, proposes a novel approach, is technically solid, empirically strong, and well-written.
- Against acceptance: theoretical justification for axioms could be stronger, practical assumptions about test-domain reshaping may limit applicability, and comparisons to recent related work are incomplete.

- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance (NIPS).

- Suggestion 1: Elaborate briefly on how the axiomatic properties relate to theoretical domain adaptation bounds.
- Suggestion 2: Discuss relations and differences with recent multi-source/multi-domain adaptation techniques.
- Suggestion 3: More fully address scenarios where test domains are completely unknown and conditional reshaping is infeasible.
- Suggestion 4: Add discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.
