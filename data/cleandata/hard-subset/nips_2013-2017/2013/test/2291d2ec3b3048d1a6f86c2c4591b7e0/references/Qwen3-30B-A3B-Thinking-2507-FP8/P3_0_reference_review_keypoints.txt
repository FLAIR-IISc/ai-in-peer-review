- Problem: Visual datasets often contain multiple latent domains; treating an entire dataset as a single domain is misleading for domain adaptation.

- Proposal: Reshape datasets into domains using two axioms—maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.

- Method: Nonparametric RKHS-based domain discrepancy, convex QP relaxation for optimization, and Domain-Wise Cross-Validation (DWCV) to select K.

- Evaluation: Tested on Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam, and IXMAS; shows significant improvements over original datasets and prior work.

- Strength — Novel framing: Correctly identifies and motivates the dataset-as-domain misconception in domain adaptation.

- Strength — Theoretical footing: Two axioms give a principled basis; nonparametric approach avoids parametric model limitations.

- Strength — Practical K selection: DWCV is a sound, effective method for choosing the number of domains without prior knowledge.

- Strength — Empirical thoroughness: Multiple datasets/tasks and conditional reshaping of test sets strengthen practical relevance.

- Strength — Clear critique of prior work: Demonstrates why appearance-based clustering (e.g., [20]) is insufficient.

- Weakness — Label dependency: DWCV requires labeled data, limiting applicability to unsupervised domain adaptation or unlabeled targets.

- Weakness — Scalability concerns: Computational cost of the convex QP for large datasets (M >> 10k) is not addressed.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Paper mainly compares to [20] and omits engagement with more recent domain-adaptation methods (e.g., [36–38]).

- Weakness — Theoretical gaps: No convergence analysis or bounds for the domain distinctiveness measure despite NP-hard origins.

- Weakness — Reproducibility details missing: Does not specify which solvers or implementation details are used.

- Weakness — Questionable assumptions: Label prior constraint assumes similar class distributions across domains, which may not hold in practice.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated contribution with empirical gains; limitations are important but addressable refinements.

- Recommendation: Accept — Strong Accept (8/10).
