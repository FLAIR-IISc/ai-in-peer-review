- Overall: Tackles definition/construction of “domains” in visual data and proposes an unsupervised framework to reshape datasets for domain adaptation; recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Main idea: Automatically discover latent domains by optimizing maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability in a kernel-induced feature space.

- Motivation: Argues that treating datasets as single domains is flawed due to latent variations (pose, illumination, background).

- Methodology: Uses kernel methods and a label prior constraint (LPC) to discover domains that are both separable and learnable.

- Related work: Thorough review; positions novel focus on “what” constitutes a domain vs. existing “how to adapt” methods and differentiates from Hoffman et al.

- Strength — Novelty: Explicit latent domain discovery is compelling and addresses a fundamental gap in domain adaptation.

- Strength — Theoretical: Solid formulation with distinctiveness/learnability objectives and effective LPC addition.

- Strength — Empirical: Strong results on object and action recognition, outperforming baselines and prior domain-discovery methods; valuable ablations.

- Strength — Presentation: Well-written, organized, with clear technical details, figures, and experimental setup.

- Strength — Practical impact: Suggests implications for dataset collection and domain-aware learning practices.

- Weakness — Kernel sensitivity: Performance heavily depends on kernel choice and bandwidth; insufficient exploration of alternatives and kernel learning.

- Weakness — Computational cost: Potentially expensive optimization; scalability and detailed runtime/complexity analysis are missing.

- Weakness — Limited domain analysis: Lacks quantitative characterization of discovered domains (e.g., distributions of pose/illumination/background).

- Weakness — Unlabeled-data assumption: Method needs large unlabeled data, which may not always be available.

- Questions/Suggestions: Request more kernel exploration/kernel learning, computational complexity and runtime scaling, deeper interpretation of discovered domains, and analysis of LPC weighting sensitivity.

- Acceptance arguments: Addresses overlooked problem, novel/theoretically sound approach, strong empirical evidence, clear writing.

- Rejection concerns: Kernel dependence, scalability, and limited in-depth analysis of discovered domains.

- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address kernel sensitivity and provide more domain analysis.
