- Summary: Proposes nonparametric domain discovery using two axioms (maximum distinctiveness and learnability), RKHS distance optimization, and DWCV to pick domain count; shows gains on object and action recognition tasks.

- Strength 1: Strong motivation—datasets often contain multiple latent domains and treating them as single domains harms adaptation.

- Strength 2: Novel methodology—axiomatic formulation, nonparametric RKHS approach avoids parametric assumptions, and DWCV is a practical model-selection procedure.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive evaluation—experiments on multiple datasets/tasks show clear improvements over baselines and prior latent-domain methods.

- Strength 4: Clear presentation—well-organized math and figures that intuitively illustrate discovered domains.

- Weakness 1: Limited comparison to more recent multi-source domain adaptation methods.

- Weakness 2: DWCV requires labeled data, limiting applicability in unsupervised domain adaptation.

- Weakness 3: Computational complexity and scalability (e.g., to millions of images) are not discussed.

- Weakness 4: Ambiguity in what factors define the discovered domains (e.g., pose, lighting) and limited qualitative analysis.

- Weakness 5: No reported quantitative comparison to simple clustering baselines like K-means.

- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).

- Suggested revisions: Add comparisons to recent multi-source methods, discuss scalability, analyze domain-defining factors, and report clustering-baseline results.

- Pro 1: Addresses an important overlooked problem in visual domain adaptation.

- Pro 2: Principled, nonparametric approach that avoids distributional assumptions.

- Pro 3: Demonstrates significant empirical improvements across datasets and tasks.

- Pro 4: Provides a practical DWCV method for choosing the number of domains.

- Con 1: Lacks comparison to recent multi-source adaptation techniques.

- Con 2: DWCV’s reliance on labels reduces usefulness in unsupervised settings.

- Con 3: Scalability and computational cost are unclear.

- Con 4: Qualitative analysis of discovered domains is shallow.
