- Paper proposes reshaping visual datasets into characteristic sub-domains for better domain adaptation using unsupervised learning.
- Method guided by principles of maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability to discover domains.
- Uses kernel-based measures of domain discrepancy and cross-validation to select the number of domains K.
- Evaluated on object recognition and human activity recognition, showing improved classification when using discovered domains.
- Correctly situates work within domain adaptation literature and relates to clustering and multi-source DA.
- Comparison to prior work (e.g., Hoffman et al.) is appropriate though could be introduced earlier.
- Strength: novel and important problem formulation addressing domain definition rather than just adaptation.
- Strength: principled, well-motivated approach with clear mathematical derivations.
- Strength: nonparametric formulation increases flexibility across datasets.
- Strength: strong empirical results and convincing ablation studies (reshaping train and test).
- Strength: paper is clear, well-written, and experiments are well-documented.
- Weakness: optimization is NP-hard and the continuous relaxation may be computationally costly for large datasets.
- Weakness: method performance depends on kernel choice and bandwidth; sensitivity analysis is limited.
- Weakness: limited systematic analysis of what factors (pose, illumination, background) drive discovered domains.
- Weakness: domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) for K selection can be expensive and may need efficient approximations.
- Weakness: Label Prior Constraint (LPC) is an assumption whose robustness to violations is unclear.
- For acceptance: addresses overlooked DA problem, offers a novel principled method, and presents compelling results.
- Against acceptance: scalability and kernel-sensitivity concerns and need for deeper analysis of domain characteristics.
- Overall: strong contribution with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance (8.5/10).
- Recommendations: provide runtime/scalability discussion, kernel sensitivity study, systematic domain-factor analysis, LPC robustness checks, and more efficient DWCV approximations.
