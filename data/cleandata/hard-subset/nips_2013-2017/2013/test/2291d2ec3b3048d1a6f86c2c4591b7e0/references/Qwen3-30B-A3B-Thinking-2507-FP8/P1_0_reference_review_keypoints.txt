- Summary: Proposes reshaping visual datasets into multiple latent domains using axioms of maximum distinctiveness and learnability with kernel measures and DWCV, improving domain adaptation on several benchmarks.

- Strength — Originality: Novel identification of latent domains within datasets and a principled reshaping framework differing from prior clustering methods.

- Strength — Significance: Addresses a fundamental domain-adaptation assumption and shows clear empirical gains, informing dataset construction and adaptation practice.

- Strength — Quality: Sound theoretical foundations and a clear optimization procedure with comprehensive experiments demonstrating robustness.

- Strength — Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, with clear motivation and effective experimental presentation.

- Weakness — Clarity: The relaxation from integer to continuous optimization (Eq.4-5) and the link between label priors and distinctiveness need clearer intuition.

- Weakness — Quality: Assumes homogeneous test domains; limited treatment of cases where both train and test contain multiple, misaligned latent domains.

- Weakness — Significance: Does not fully explore actionable guidance for dataset creators despite implications noted in the conclusion.

- Specific comment 1: Provide a brief intuitive justification for why the continuous relaxation preserves key properties of the original integer problem.

- Specific comment 2: Compare against more recent domain-adaptation methods that may perform domain discovery to better establish practical utility.

- Specific comment 3: DWCV depends on labeled training data to estimate domain-wise accuracy, limiting applicability when labels are scarce; suggest addressing or mitigating this.

- Specific comment 4: Provide deeper analysis of example domains (e.g., why certain domains capture colorful screens) to show discovered domains align with meaningful visual factors.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel contribution with solid empirical support; minor clarifications and broader analyses would strengthen the work.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Pro arguments: Tackles an overlooked fundamental problem, introduces principled axioms, provides a technically sound and empirically effective method, and yields insights into factors defining latent domains.

- Con arguments: Needs clearer optimization intuition, limited handling of heterogeneous test domains, and DWCV’s reliance on labeled data restricts applicability.
