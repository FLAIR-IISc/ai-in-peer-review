- Proposes reshaping datasets into latent domains using objectives of maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Identifies flaw in assuming datasets correspond to single domains; visual datasets often mix latent factors (pose, illumination, etc.).  
- Uses a nonparametric approach that improves on prior latent domain discovery methods with fewer distributional assumptions.  
- Theoretical foundation leverages kernel methods to measure domain discrepancies and introduces a label prior constraint (LPC).  
- Optimization is well-formulated with a practical relaxation to continuous optimization.  
- Conditional reshaping for test data is a notable contribution, though optimization details are in the supplement.  
- Strong experimental validation on object recognition and human activity recognition showing consistent improvements and informative ablations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents enough detail for reproducibility.  
- Major weakness: potential computational complexity and scalability concerns for large datasets.  
- Sensitivity to kernel bandwidth selection could impact results and needs further analysis.  
- Lacks discussion of failure cases where latent domains are too subtle to discover reliably.  
- Method requires labeled training data and does not address unsupervised domain adaptation.  
- Request for clearer intuition/visual example to illustrate how LPC constrains domain assignments.  
- Asks for better justification of the DWCV bound and rationale for setting Kmax = min{M/(NC), C}.  
- Suggests expanding main-paper details on conditional reshaping optimization and quantifying qualitative analyses (e.g., Fig. 1).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — novel, technically sound, and experimentally convincing despite noted limitations.
