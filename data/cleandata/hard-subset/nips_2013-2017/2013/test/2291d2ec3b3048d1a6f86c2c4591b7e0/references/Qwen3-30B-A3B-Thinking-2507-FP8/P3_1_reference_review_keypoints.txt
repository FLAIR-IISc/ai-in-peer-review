- Paper identifies the dataset-vs-domain mismatch in visual domain adaptation and motivates reshaping datasets into latent domains.  
- Proposes automatic domain discovery based on two axioms: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.  
- Uses nonparametric kernel-based RKHS measures for domain distinctiveness and domain-wise cross-validation to choose the number of domains.  
- Evaluated on object and action recognition, showing consistent improvements over baselines and Hoffman et al.’s Gaussian mixture approach.  
- Conceptual contribution: highlights a fundamental, often-overlooked problem in treating datasets as domains.  
- Methodology is well-motivated: distinctiveness and learnability criteria plus a label prior constraint (LPC) are justified.  
- Theoretical choice of kernel-based distances avoids parametric assumptions and is sound.  
- Experimental validation is strong, including practical conditional reshaping of test datasets.  
- Clear problem formulation with solid theoretical foundations.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to clustering baselines beyond K-means and to advanced clustering techniques.  
- LPC may be unrealistic when class distributions vary across domains and the paper doesn’t sufficiently address such cases.  
- Computational complexity and scalability of the continuous relaxation/optimization are not analyzed.  
- Claim that datasets are not collected with identifiable domains is overstated given existing domain-aware benchmarks (e.g., Office-31, VisDA).  
- Missing comparisons to recent domain adaptation methods (e.g., DANN, MMD, CORAL) to show practical integration/impact.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with consistent empirical gains, but needs more discussion/experiments on LPC, clustering baselines, complexity, and claims about datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: compare to more clustering methods, discuss LPC limitations with examples, add computational complexity analysis, and soften claims about dataset collection.
