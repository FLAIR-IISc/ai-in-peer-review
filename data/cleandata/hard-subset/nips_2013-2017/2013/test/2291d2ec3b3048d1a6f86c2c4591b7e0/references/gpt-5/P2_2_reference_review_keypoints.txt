- Summary: Proposes nonparametric discovery of latent domains by maximizing pairwise MMD between domain embeddings and selecting K via domain-wise cross-validation, plus conditional test-set reshaping; shows improved adaptation on Office-like and IXMAS datasets versus original datasets and a Gaussian-mixture baseline.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on kernel mean embeddings/MMD, GFK, multisource adaptation, and Hoffman's parametric latent-domain discovery; novelty is MMD-based discovery with an explicit learnability criterion and test reshaping.

Strengths:
- Clear, well-motivated problem formulation arguing datasets often contain multiple latent domains.
- Principled use of MMD to quantify domain distinctiveness without parametric assumptions.
- Practical, interpretable K selection via domain-wise cross-validation that links discovery to downstream utility.
- Thoughtful conditional test-set reshaping extension and demonstration on heterogeneous cross-view action recognition.
- Empirical improvements shown on both image and video benchmarks and over a relevant latent-domain baseline.

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Optimization under-specified and potentially intractable: relaxed problem remains an NP-hard quadratic program with no solver, initialization, stopping criteria, or runtime/memory reported.
- Strong label prior constraint (LPC) assumes similar class proportions across latent domains, undermining full unsupervised discovery and risking failure when priors vary.
- Risk of overfitting when choosing K via DWCV—may favor easily classifiable domains rather than those that transfer best.
- Limited baselines and reporting: KMeans/random partitions and additional adaptation methods omitted; sensitivity analyses missing.
- Notational and technical clarity issues (e.g., Eq.1, constraint bounds); conditional reshaping details and kernel bandwidth sensitivity deferred or unexplored.

Evaluation (NeurIPS criteria):
- Quality: Methodologically sound but optimization and theoretical guarantees are under-specified; more ablations and runtime data needed.
- Clarity: Generally clear high-level ideas; some notation and optimization details hinder reproducibility.
- Originality: Novel combination of MMD-based discovery, learnability-based K selection, and conditional test reshaping relative to parametric prior work.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with promising empirical gains; potential impact contingent on clarifying assumptions and scalability.

Pros for acceptance:
- Tackles an under-served real problem with a principled nonparametric approach.
- Empirically improves adaptation over strong baselines.
- Conceptually simple and broadly applicable; adds useful test-set reshaping idea.

Cons for acceptance:
- Unclear optimization tractability and scalability; limited practical guidance.
- Relies on strong LPC and label use in discovery; limited analysis of failure modes.
- Missing key ablations and omitted baselines reduce confidence in generality.
- Lacks theoretical analysis on when maximizing MMD improves transfer.

Key questions / suggestions:
- Specify solver, initialization, stopping criteria, and report runtime/memory for datasets.
- Provide ablations: without LPC, with learned/soft priors, sensitivity to δ and kernel bandwidth, and results with fixed K (no DWCV).
- Include KMeans/random partition baselines and at least one additional adaptation method to test generality.
- Clarify Eq.(1), the 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C constraint, and give a concise conditional reshaping formulation.
- Discuss robustness when class priors vary across domains and potential extensions (soft priors, entropic regularization).

Recommendation: Weak accept.
