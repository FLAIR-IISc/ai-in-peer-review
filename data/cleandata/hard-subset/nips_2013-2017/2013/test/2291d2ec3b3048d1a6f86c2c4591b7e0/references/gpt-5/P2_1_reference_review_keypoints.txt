- Problem: Discover latent visual domains within aggregated datasets to improve domain adaptation, instead of treating each dataset as a single domain.
- Method: Optimize for maximum distinctiveness (sum of pairwise MMD discrepancies in RKHS) and maximum learnability (choose K via domain-wise cross-validation).
- Optimization: Formulate distinctiveness as an integer program with a label prior constraint (LPC) to prevent class-separating partitions, relaxed to a continuous quadratic program.
- Test-time: Propose conditional reshaping of heterogeneous test data via a convex QP to align test samples to discovered training domains.
- Experiments: Show consistent gains on Office (Caltech/Amazon/DSLR/Webcam) and IXMAS actions and improvements over Hoffman et al. (ECCV’12).
- Soundness: Using MMD for discrepancy and DWCV for selecting K is principled and addresses trivial partitioning by large K.
- LPC concern: The label-prior constraint prevents class-based separation but is a strong assumption that may not hold when true domain label proportions vary.
- Scalability/optimization: The relaxed QP is non-convex/NP-hard in general; paper lacks solver details, convergence diagnostics, and runtime/memory analysis (M×M kernel limits).
- Reproducibility: Missing solver/initialization/stopping details and no public code referenced reduce reproducibility confidence.
- Empirical strengths: Practical multi-source strategies (UNION/ENSEMBLE/MATCHING) approach oracle performance without target labels; conditional reshaping is useful in IXMAS.
- Empirical weaknesses: Limited ablations (omitted KMeans/random), little sensitivity analysis to kernel bandwidth or δ, narrow baseline comparisons, and no statistical significance testing.
- Clarity issues: Some relaxation derivations (e.g., 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C) and the role/selection of δ need clearer explanation; conditional reshaping is relegated to supplement.
- Originality: Novel combination of nonparametric MMD-based domain discovery with supervised learnability criterion and test-set conditional reshaping.
- Significance: Addresses a practical problem with potential impact on preprocessing and handling heterogeneous test data, but LPC reliance and scalability caveats limit immediate broad applicability.
- Pros: Well-motivated problem, principled MMD use, sensible DWCV for K, demonstrated gains, and insightful qualitative analysis.
- Cons: Strong LPC assumption, non-convex optimization with little detail, limited ablations/baselines, notational ambiguities, and reliance on labels.
- Key open question 1: How sensitive are results to δ in LPC and to kernel bandwidth; what happens if LPC is relaxed or removed?
- Key open question 2: Please derive the constraint 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C and discuss feasibility when some classes are absent in a domain.
- Key open question 3: What solver/initialization was used for the QP and what are time/memory costs on the largest experiments?
- Key open question 4: How does performance vary with different adaptation backbones beyond GFK, and how gracefully does the method degrade when true domain priors differ?
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — interesting and timely contribution but would be stronger with optimization details, broader baselines/ablations, and robustness analysis.
