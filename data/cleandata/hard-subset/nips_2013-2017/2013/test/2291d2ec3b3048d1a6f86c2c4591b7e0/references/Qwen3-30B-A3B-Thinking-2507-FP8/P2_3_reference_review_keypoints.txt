- Problem: Datasets are often treated as single domains though they contain multiple latent domains, harming domain adaptation.
- Proposal: Automatically reshape visual datasets into characteristic domains using two axioms: maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability.
- Method: Nonparametric domain discrepancy measures and domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) to select the number of domains.
- Theoretical contribution: Formulates domain discovery as an optimization, provides a principled relaxation for tractability.
- Empirical results: Significant improvements on object and action recognition benchmarks (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam, IXMAS).
- Baselines: Outperforms prior latent-domain work and standard clustering baselines (K-means, random partitioning).
- Interpretability: Discovered domains align with meaningful visual factors (pose, background, color).
- Clarity: Well-organized and accessible presentation, with sufficient mathematical detail.
- Minor clarity issue: The relaxation (Section 2.1) needs a more intuitive justification and connection to the original problem.
- Originality: Novel nonparametric approach and DWCV; substantial advance over parametric Gaussian-mixture prior work.
- Significance: Substantial practical utility for domain adaptation and implications for dataset collection and evaluation.
- Limitations: Scalability to very large datasets not fully discussed.
- Limitations: Performance in fully unsupervised (no-label) settings not explored.
- Limitations: More discussion needed on kernel-based discrepancy measure limits and broader ML connections.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, novel, well-evaluated, and impactful.
