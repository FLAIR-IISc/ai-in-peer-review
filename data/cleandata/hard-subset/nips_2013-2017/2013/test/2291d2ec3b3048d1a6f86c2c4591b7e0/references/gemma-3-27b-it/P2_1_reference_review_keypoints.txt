- Summary: Identifies the problem of ill-defined domains in visual datasets and proposes an unsupervised method to reshape datasets into characteristic domains using maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability, relaxed to a continuous optimization and validated on object and activity recognition.

- Strength: Tackles an important, often-overlooked problem in domain adaptation.

- Strength: Novel nonparametric approach to automatically discover and reshape latent domains, emphasizing learnability.

- Strength: Methodology is sound—kernel-based formulation with intuitive distinctiveness and learnability objectives and a tractable relaxation.

- Strength: Strong empirical results outperform baselines across tasks, including compelling analysis of reshaping test sets.

- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well organized.

- Weakness: Results may be sensitive to kernel bandwidth; parameter sensitivity is not thoroughly analyzed.

- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability concerns remain despite continuous relaxation.

- Weakness: Limited comparisons to state-of-the-art multi-domain adaptation methods.

- Weakness: Qualitative analysis of discovered domains and failure cases could be deeper.

- Quality: Technically sound with comprehensive experiments and careful evaluation.

- Clarity: Clear exposition and logical organization.

- Originality: Novel contribution by automatically reshaping datasets into latent domains and distinguishing from prior work.

- Significance: Potential to impact domain adaptation by enabling automatic discovery and use of latent domain structure.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses critical problem, proposes a novel sound approach, demonstrates strong empirical gains, and is well written.

- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper kernel sensitivity analysis, better scalability discussion, and more comprehensive multi-domain comparisons.

- Overall assessment: Strong accept — important, well-executed work with minor weaknesses that do not undermine the contribution.

- Confidence: 9/10
