- Paper tackles latent domain discovery in visual datasets to improve downstream domain adaptation by maximizing inter-domain distinctiveness and learnability.
- Distinctiveness is measured via squared RKHS mean-embedding distances (MMD-style) summed over domain pairs (TDD) with label-prior constraints (LPC) to avoid trivial per-class clustering.
- Assignment indicators are relaxed to continuous variables and solved as a constrained quadratic program; K is chosen by domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV).
- Discovered domains are used with an off-the-shelf adaptation method (geodesic flow kernel); paper also proposes “conditional reshaping” of test sets via kernel discrepancy minimization.
- Strength: Addresses an important, underexplored problem relevant to robust recognition and multi-source adaptation.
- Strength: Nonparametric kernel mean-embedding formulation avoids restrictive parametric assumptions and connects to MMD/two-sample tests.
- Strength: DWCV is a simple, practical mechanism to select K that aligns discovery with classifier performance.
- Strength: Empirical gains shown on object recognition and cross-view action datasets; conditional reshaping appears useful for multi-view actions.
- Strength: Label prior constraint is a reasonable safeguard against trivial per-class partitions.
- Weakness: Optimization is non-convex and NP-hard; paper lacks solver details (initialization, convergence, scalability, runtime).
- Weakness: LPC assumption of identical class priors across domains is often unrealistic and sensitivity to this assumption (and δ) is not studied.
- Weakness: Maximizing marginal P(x) discrepancy can still yield category-driven partitions; per-class or dependence-penalty alternatives are not implemented.
- Weakness: DWCV optimizes in-domain accuracy, not target adaptation performance; correlation to adaptation success is asserted but not rigorously validated.
- Weakness: Missing ablations on kernel choice/bandwidth, δ, number of folds, effect of removing LPC, and no scalability/time/memory experiments.
- Weakness: Conditional test reshaping details are deferred to the supplement; main-text objective, solver, and complexity are unclear and potential label leakage is not discussed.
- Weakness: Experimental reporting lacks error bars/significance testing; MATCHING/UNION/ENSEMBLE methods are only sketched; dataset diversity is limited.
- Quality: Conceptually sound use of kernel discrepancies and sensible DWCV heuristic, but algorithmic guarantees and practical details are under-specified.
- Clarity: Generally readable and well-motivated, but notation/constraints need clearer justification (e.g., 1/M ≤ ∑k βmk ≤ 1/C) and solver/stopping criteria should be specified.
- Clarity: Define MATCHING and ENSEMBLE strategies clearly and present the conditional reshaping objective in the main text.
- Originality: Moderate—novel combination of nonparametric max-distinctiveness, LPC, and DWCV, but builds on prior latent domain discovery work (e.g., Hoffman et al.).
- Significance: Potentially impactful if made robust and scalable; could become a preprocessing step for multi-source adaptation and dataset bias analysis.
- Suggestion: Replace or complement marginal TDD with class-conditional discrepancy or penalize label–domain dependence (e.g., HSIC).
- Suggestion: Provide a practical algorithm with initialization strategies, iterative updates, convergence diagnostics, and runtime/memory benchmarks.
- Suggestion: Add sensitivity/ablation studies (kernel bandwidth, δ, folds, omitting LPC) and tests with domain-specific class priors.
- Suggestion: Include stronger baselines (balanced spectral clustering, class-conditional MMD methods), multiple runs with confidence intervals, and clearer experimental protocols.
- Pros for acceptance: Timely problem, principled nonparametric formulation, practical K selection idea, and promising empirical improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient optimization and algorithmic detail, brittle LPC assumption, heuristic K selection with limited validation, and missing baselines/ablations.
