- Addresses the overlooked problem that visual datasets are not naturally organized into discrete domains and proposes reshaping them for domain adaptation.
- Proposes two domain properties: maximum distinctiveness (domains should be maximally different) and maximum learnability (domains should permit strong discriminative models).
- Introduces nonparametric methods, including a kernel-based domain discrepancy measure, to identify characteristic domains.
- Proposes a domain-wise cross-validation (DWCV) procedure to determine the optimal number of domains balancing distinctiveness and learnability.
- Validated on object recognition (Caltech-256, Amazon, DSLR, Webcam) and human action recognition (IXMAS), showing significant improvements over baselines and Hoffman et al.
- Technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation and a convex relaxation solvable via quadratic programming.
- Experiments are comprehensive with appropriate baselines and consistent performance gains from reshaped domains.
- Demonstrates that discovered domains correspond to meaningful visual factors (pose, background, appearance), supporting practical utility.
- DWCV is well-justified as it directly addresses the trade-off between domain distinctiveness and learnability.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, though optimization details may challenge readers unfamiliar with convex optimization.
- Needs clearer explanation of why maximum learnability is preferable to simply maximizing TDD, and stronger links between theory and practical performance.
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, scalability, and failure cases or limitations.
- Original and novel by challenging the dataset-as-domain assumption and contrasting nonparametric discovery with prior parametric approaches.
- Significant impact: could change practices in domain adaptation and inform dataset construction by revealing main domain factors.
- Strengths: fundamental problem, theoretically sound method, thorough evaluation, practical improvements, and insightful domain analysis.
- Weaknesses: unclear necessity of learnability over TDD, limited complexity/scalability discussion, missing failure-case analysis, and could better connect to broader literature.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, novel, rigorously validated contribution likely to influence the field.
