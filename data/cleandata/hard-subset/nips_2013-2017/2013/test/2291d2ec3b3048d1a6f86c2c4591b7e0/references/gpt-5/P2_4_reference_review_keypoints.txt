- Paper tackles discovering latent domains in visual datasets to improve multi-source domain adaptation.
- Proposes two principles: maximum distinctiveness (MMD between domain marginals) and maximum learnability (select K via domain-wise CV).
- Introduces a label prior constraint (LPC) to discourage clustering by class identity.
- Relaxes integer assignments into a continuous quadratic program with simplex constraints.
- Evaluates on object recognition (Caltech-256/Amazon/DSLR/Webcam) and IXMAS action recognition, including test-time “conditional reshaping.”
- Reports improvements over original splits and Hoffman et al. (ECCV’12) Gaussian-mixture latent domains.
- Builds on kernel two-sample testing, kernel-embedding clustering, and multisource DA literature.
- Distinguishes from prior work by avoiding parametric mixture assumptions and focusing on discovering domains rather than adapting fixed ones.
- MMD-based discriminability is well-motivated and nonparametric, offering flexibility for complex image factors.
- LPC effectively addresses degenerate per-class clustering using available labels.
- DWCV for choosing K is intuitive and practically bounded by CV folds and class counts.
- Conditional test-set reshaping is an interesting, practically motivated extension.
- Objective targeting P(x) distinctiveness may still favor class-correlated or spurious factors; conditional alignment is suggested.
- Optimization is underspecified: relaxed QP is NP-hard, solver/initialization/stopping and guarantees are not provided.
- Notational overload and typos (e.g., K used twice, odd constraint bounds) hinder clarity.
- Scalability concerns: M×M kernel and QP per K raise complexity/memory questions not addressed.
- Sensitivity to kernel choice/bandwidth and LPC hyperparameter δ is not analyzed.
- “Maximum learnability” via within-source CV may pick easy-but-not-transferable domains; cross-domain proxies suggested.
- Conditional reshaping details are deferred to supplement, making correctness hard to assess.
- Experimental limitations: limited baselines (only [20] and GFK), few ablations, no variance/error bars or statistical tests.
- Missing diagnostics: inter/intra-class MMD, confusion changes, and robustness metrics would strengthen claims.
- Assessment: conceptually sound and novel but weakened by optimization details, limited ablations, and underspecified test reshaping.
- Pros: important problem, nonparametric label-aware formulation, practical K selection, promising empirical gains.
- Cons: NP-hard relaxed optimization without solver details, strong LPC assumptions, untested scalability/sensitivity, narrow baselines.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, with revisions to clarify optimization, add ablations (LPC/δ, bandwidth, K), consider conditional MMD, broaden DA backbones, report variability, and move key reshaping details into main text.
