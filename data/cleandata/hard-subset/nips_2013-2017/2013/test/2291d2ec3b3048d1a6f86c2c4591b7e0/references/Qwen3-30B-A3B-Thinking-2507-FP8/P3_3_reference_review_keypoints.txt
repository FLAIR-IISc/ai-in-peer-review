- Summary: Proposes automatically reshaping visual datasets into latent domains using two axioms—maximum distinctiveness and maximum learnability—via kernel-based discrepancy measures and DWCV to pick K.  
- Strength: Strong motivation highlighting that datasets often hide multiple domains (e.g., lighting, viewpoint) and that conflating datasets with domains is problematic.  
- Strength: Novel methodology: axiomatic formulation and nonparametric, kernel-based domain discrepancy approach that advances over parametric clustering.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on object and action recognition benchmarks showing consistent improvements and useful conditional reshaping of test sets.  
- Strength: Practical utility: DWCV provides a principled way to choose the number of domains without prior domain knowledge.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps: relaxation to continuous optimization lacks justification, convergence analysis, or guarantees relative to the integer formulation.  
- Weakness: Computational concerns: DWCV is computationally intensive, with no scalability analysis or runtime characterization for large datasets or large K.  
- Weakness: Limited generalization: assumes test domains are subsets of discovered training domains and does not address out-of-distribution test domains.  
- Weakness: Kernel selection justification is weak—uses Gaussian kernel with heuristic bandwidth (2× median) without theoretical or empirical sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons: only compares to older baselines (Hoffman et al. 2013) and omits recent adversarial domain adaptation methods (e.g., DANN, CDAN).  
- Significance: Shifts focus from adapting between given domains to discovering latent domain structures, potentially improving domain representations and adaptation performance.  
- Significance: Provides insights for dataset construction by identifying dominant visual factors (pose, background) and showing dataset-domain misalignment.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: add discussion of computational complexity and scalability; justify kernel choice; address out-of-distribution test domains; include brief comparisons with recent domain adaptation baselines.
