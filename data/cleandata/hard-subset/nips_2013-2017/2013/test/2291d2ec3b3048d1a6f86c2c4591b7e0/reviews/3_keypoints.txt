- Proposes a convex framework to split data into K “latent domains” with similar within-domain class distributions and maximal pairwise distinctiveness in RKHS; K chosen by maximizing average learnability.  
- Forcing similar class distributions per domain prevents single-class domination but can be limiting for real latent factors (e.g., pose distributions differ across classes like horses vs cows).  
- Novelty: controlling class-label distributions in a relaxed convex formulation for latent-domain discovery, and selecting K by checking per-domain classification quality.  
- Clarity issues: dataset/domain/latent-domain terminology is inconsistent and confusing—needs clear definitions and examples; experimental Section 4.2 is unclear.  
- Presentation bug: max_k r(U_k, B) is used in Eq.7 but not defined.  
- Experiments: validation is adequate and shows reasonable gains, but dataset/source-target selection rationale is unclear (leave-one-dataset-out suggested); qualitative results are useful.  
- Claiming the problem is unsupervised is misleading because the method uses semantic class labels; overall contribution is notable but several conceptual and experimental clarifications are needed.
