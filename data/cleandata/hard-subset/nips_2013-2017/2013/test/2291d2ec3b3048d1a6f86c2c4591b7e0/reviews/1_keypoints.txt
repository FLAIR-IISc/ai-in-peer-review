- Goal: automatically discover latent domains in training data to improve domain adaptation performance on a test set.
- Method: define a kernel-based difference function between feature vectors and partition data to maximize inter-domain differences.
- Formulation: integer program with constraints (each point assigned to one domain; per-domain label distribution matches global prior), relaxed to continuous quadratic optimization; number of domains chosen by cross-validation.
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets (static images and IXMAS multi-view actions) and shows improved performance over prior domain adaptation method [19].
- Positive: paper is well-written and appears novel; performance gains are appreciated.
- Main concern 1: motivation unclear—no evidence that clustering by appearance yields per-category domains and unclear how the model prevents this.
- Main concern 2: necessity of the label-prior constraint is unclear—need results without it and comparisons to k-means, unsupervised object discovery baselines, and random equal partitioning.
- Clarification requested: define “maximally different in distribution” — distribution over what (features, labels, other)?
- Question: does β_{mk} as formulated already lie on a simplex (line 166)?
- Question: constraints stated around line 182 appear different from earlier constraints—please clarify.
- Request: provide justification/proof for the bound referenced on line 215.
- Request: detail use of the geodesic flow kernel (reimplementation or publicly available code).
- Request: provide insights into why the proposed approach outperforms baselines and show systematic baseline failures the method overcomes.
- Minor correction: Eq (1) M'k should be Mk'.
- Suggested citation to include: Torralba & Efros, "Unbiased Look at Dataset Bias" (CVPR 2011).
- Outcome: rebuttal addressed concerns and reviewer now leans slightly toward accept.
