- Proposes automatic partitioning of all images across datasets into domains driven by feature-space separability and per-domain discriminative learnability.  
- Partitioner aims to produce domains that enable training strong classifiers for the original classes rather than separating datasets.  
- Novel contribution: addresses the under-explored problem of automatically defining domains for domain adaptation.  
- Criticism: the proposed optimization (Sec. 2) is not clearly shown to solve the original objectives (2)+(3) closely.  
- Criticism: the two driving criteria are not well integrated—maximal learnability is applied only as a wrapper for model selection, not as part of the partitioning objective.  
- Suggestion: an integrated optimization that jointly maximizes separability and learnability would be preferable.  
- Writing: paper is well written but would benefit from more illustrative figures.  
- Experiments: show significant improvements over using datasets as domains and over the recent domain discovery method [19].  
- Issue: using the test set in the “reshaping” process is interesting but poorly explained and effectively assumes an imputation setting that is not stated.  
- Major methodological weakness: image representation is outdated (single BoW of sparse SURF with 800 codewords) and too weak for current standards.  
- Missing detail: unclear what similarity measure is used for histogram comparison—Euclidean is inappropriate; should use X^2 or intersection kernel.  
- Recommendation: upgrade descriptors (spatial pyramid of dense SURF, Fisher Vectors) and consider cited works (Zhang IJCV 2006; Jurie CVPR 2005; Lazebnik CVPR 2006; Perronnin ECCV 2010).  
- Overall verdict: despite shortcomings, the paper is a valuable, promising contribution and should be accepted.
