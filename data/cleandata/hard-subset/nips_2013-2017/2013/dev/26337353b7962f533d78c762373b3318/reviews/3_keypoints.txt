- Paper proposes a novel multilevel sparse representation method via penalized low-rank matrix factorization with more than two factors representing hierarchical entities.

- Factors are estimated with block coordinate descent and sparsity-promoting penalties applied at each step.

- Application focus is large-scale calcium imaging to recover pixel→neuron→assembly hierarchy: neuron locations, assembly memberships, and assembly spikes.

- Authors claim better performance than other methods on realistic synthetic data and successful identification of assemblies in real data.

- Originality is praised: the three-way decomposition and enforcing consistency with a two-way decomposition is interesting and may improve robustness.

- Major concern about computational efficiency and scalability to tens of thousands of pixels and thousands of timesteps; algorithm may be slow and require many iterations despite parallelizable steps.

- Skepticism about the practical usefulness of explicit neural assemblies—temporal components of spatiotemporal matrices might already capture shared spikes without hierarchical modeling.

- Regularization details are unclear: definitions and examples of convex cell shape priors for ΩU and ΩA are not provided.

- Leaving regularization weight selection to users is problematic given many regularizers; the claim that rank overestimation is harmless relies heavily on appropriate sparsity penalties.

- Paper insufficiently addresses spike-to-calcium convolution and spike deconvolution literature; handling of overlapping transients (eliminating >20% overlap) seems to sidestep a central, active research topic.

- Presentation and clarity are poor: writing is accessible only to calcium imaging experts, main equations shown oddly as a figure, and methods/results need clearer exposition.

- Real data experiments are under-presented; Fig. 6 and lack of video support need more thorough explanation to be convincing.
