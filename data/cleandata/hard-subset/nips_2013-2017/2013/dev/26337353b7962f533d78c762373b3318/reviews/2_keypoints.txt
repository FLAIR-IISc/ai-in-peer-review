- Proposes a bilevel sparse heterarchical matrix factorization to jointly infer neurons (pixel correlates) and overlapping assemblies from calcium imaging, decomposing observations into a product of sparse matrices with decreasing rank.  
- Allows structured sparsity at every level and is not limited to strict hierarchies; does not require a priori number of assemblies (though results depend on user-set optimization parameters).  
- On synthetic data, it recovered assembly assignments with sensitivity exceeding MNMMF and KSVDS (all methods initialized with Adina).  
- Detected single-neuron calcium transients with higher sensitivity than MNMMF, Adina, or cell sorting.  
- Demonstrated on real rat hippocampus data, but reviewer unclear what conclusions to draw beyond neurons appearing less spatially sparse than the Adina-derived initialization.  
- Parallel inference of structures and time courses at every level is emphasized as improving robustness, yet the inferred dictionary (neuron images) is not discussed or compared to ground truth/other methods.  
- Paper is technically sound with detailed cost function and optimization in the supplement.  
- Missing methodological details: how trade-off parameters were chosen/optimized, how adjacency matrices were binarized for evaluation, and sensitivity to noise levels tested.  
- Paper is clearly written.  
- Approach appears novel and references relevant prior work.  
- Results are potentially important for labs doing calcium imaging where assemblies show coherent activation, and the method flexibly incorporates prior knowledge at different levels.  
- A limitation: method appears to require Adina-based dictionary initialization; authors could consider integrating Adina-like priors at the 0th inference level.
