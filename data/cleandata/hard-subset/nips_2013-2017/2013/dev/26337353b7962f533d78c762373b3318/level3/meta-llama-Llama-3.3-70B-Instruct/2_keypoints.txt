- Proposes a novel convex relaxation for sparse principal subspace estimation using the Fantope (convex hull of rank-d projection matrices).
- Formulates the problem as a semidefinite program with a Fantope constraint and provides an ADMM-based algorithm to solve it efficiently.
- Establishes near-optimal rates of convergence in subspace estimation without assuming known rank or spiked covariance models.
- Provides strong statistical guarantees that apply to a wide class of input matrices (e.g., sample covariance, Kendall's tau).
- Presents a general theoretical framework that can accommodate matrices beyond sample covariance.
- Numerical experiments show the proposed method outperforms deflation methods in computational and statistical efficiency.
- Claims improved robustness and flexibility for high-dimensional inference and the ability to parallelly optimize assembly assignments and time courses (useful for neural activation experiments).
- Weakness: assumes exact sparsity of the principal subspace, which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Weakness: requires careful selection of the regularization parameter and the subspace dimension d, which is challenging.
- Weakness: may be computationally expensive for very large datasets.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
