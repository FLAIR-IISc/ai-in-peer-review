- Proposes a novel convex relaxation for sparse principal subspace estimation using the Fantope (convex hull of rank-d projection matrices).
- Establishes a near-optimal convergence rate for principal subspace estimation without assuming a spiked covariance model.
- Provides a general theoretical framework applicable to arbitrary input matrices (e.g., sample covariance, Kendall’s tau).
- Offers an efficient ADMM algorithm to solve the resulting semidefinite program.
- Method handles high-dimensional data and comes with strong statistical guarantees.
- ADMM algorithm may be computationally expensive for very large datasets.
- Assumes the input matrix is symmetric, which may limit practical applicability.
- Lacks clear empirical or theoretical comparisons to existing sparse principal subspace methods.
- Paper is well-organized and explains the Fantope clearly, but notation and technical proofs may be challenging for non-experts.
- Originality is high, presenting a different approach from existing methods.
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should add comparisons to other methods and address algorithmic efficiency and the symmetry assumption.
