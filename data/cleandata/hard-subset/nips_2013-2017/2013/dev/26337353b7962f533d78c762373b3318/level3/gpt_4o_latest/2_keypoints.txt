- Introduces bilevel sparse heterarchical matrix factorization (SHMF) to jointly infer neurons, assemblies, and temporal dynamics from calcium imaging.  
- SHMF incorporates structured sparsity and does not require a preset number of assemblies.  
- Advances over prior methods (MNMMF, KSVDS) by avoiding separate clustering and predefined parameters.  
- Strong synthetic-data performance: better recovery of assembly assignments and calcium transients than baselines.  
- Parallel inference capability enhances robustness of neuronal time-course identification.  
- Novelty and technical rigor make SHMF a significant methodological contribution.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and well-referenced.  
- Real-data application (rat hippocampus) is less convincing; results lack clear interpretation.  
- The inferred dictionary is not adequately discussed, leaving its role and quality unclear.  
- Key technical details are missing: trade-off parameter optimization, adjacency matrix binarization, and noise sensitivity.  
- Reliance on Adina for dictionary initialization introduces external dependency and potential limitation.  
- Potentially impactful for neuroscience labs, but practical applicability may be hindered by missing details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address interpretability and technical/documentation gaps.
