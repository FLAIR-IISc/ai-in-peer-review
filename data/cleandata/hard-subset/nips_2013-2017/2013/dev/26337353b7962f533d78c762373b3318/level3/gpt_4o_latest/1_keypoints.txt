- Introduces SHMF: a multi-level sparse matrix factorization framework with hierarchical rank-decreasing structure for detecting neurons, assemblies, and temporal activities from calcium imaging.
- Proposes a bilevel formulation with structured sparsity that models both hierarchical and heterarchical relationships and jointly optimizes all decomposition levels.
- Differs from prior work by avoiding greedy level-by-level methods and performing simultaneous multi-level optimization.
- Validated on synthetic and real calcium imaging data, showing recovery of neuronal assemblies and inference of co-activation patterns.
- Originality: novel multi-level sparse factorization, enabling inference of hierarchical/heterarchical relationships and joint optimization—significant advance over existing methods.
- Clarity: well-written paper with clear mathematical formulations, helpful visuals, and good literature context.
- Experimental validation: thorough synthetic experiments with quantitative metrics (graph edit distance, sensitivity, precision) and real-data demonstrations showing superiority to baselines.
- Significance: likely valuable for calcium imaging analysis, capable of detecting overlapping and correlated neurons and assemblies.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of convergence properties and computational complexity, limiting assessment of scalability.
- Weakness: depends on user-specified tradeoff parameters (η, λ) with no explored data-driven or adaptive tuning strategies.
- Weakness: applicability to domains beyond calcium imaging is not explored, reducing perceived generality.
- Pros for acceptance: innovative, well-motivated framework with strong experimental support and clear presentation.
- Cons against acceptance: missing convergence/efficiency analysis, parameter-dependence without adaptive tuning, and limited cross-domain validation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational analysis and parameter-tuning concerns.
