- Proposes multi-level sparse matrix factorization (bilevel SHMF) that decomposes a matrix into a product of sparse matrices in a hierarchical, decreasing-rank manner.
- Enables level-specific regularization, offering advantages over traditional flat decompositions.
- Introduces a joint optimization scheme that simultaneously estimates inter-level relations and sparse approximations of raw data.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic data and real-world calcium imaging, accurately identifying neurons, assemblies, and temporal activities.
- Well-written with clear motivation and a thorough related-work review.
- Strength: handles hierarchical and heterarchical relations between concepts across levels.
- Strength: experimental results are impressive and empirically validate the method.
- Weakness: convergence properties of the learning algorithm (convergence guarantees, speed) are not thoroughly discussed.
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (e.g., η and λ) appear ad-hoc and lack data-driven tuning or justification.
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability analysis are insufficient.
- Recommendation: significant contribution to unsupervised learning and neural data analysis, but would benefit from revisions addressing convergence, hyperparameter tuning, and complexity.
