- Paper introduces Sparse Heterarchical Matrix Factorization (SHMF), a bilevel method for joint multilevel decomposition of calcium imaging data to identify neurons, assemblies, and dynamics.
- Novelty: Three-way decomposition with hierarchical and heterarchical relationships and joint optimization is original and departs from PCA/ICA/NMF approaches.
- Significance: Addresses an important neuroscience problem—interpreting calcium imaging to reveal neuronal assemblies and temporal dynamics.
- Performance: Shows improved assembly recovery on synthetic data and plausible interpretations on real data, suggesting practical utility.
- Flexibility: Framework supports different regularizers at each level, allowing adaptation to specific datasets or goals.
- Efficiency concern: Scalability and computational cost for large calcium imaging datasets and increasing hierarchy levels are not sufficiently addressed.
- Clarity issue: Paper is dense, assumes expert knowledge, and lacks clear explanation of key choices (e.g., regularizers ΩU, ΩA, their weights) and spike deconvolution details.
- Literature context missing: Inadequate discussion of prior work on spike deconvolution and related methods weakens positioning.
- Practical relevance questioned: Unclear whether identified assemblies provide novel insights beyond the temporal component of standard spatiotemporal decompositions.
- Presentation problems: Figures (notably Fig. 6) and equations are poorly explained and reduce accessibility.
- Recommendation: Promising and novel but requires major revisions—improve scalability analysis, clarify methodology and experiments, better situate in literature, and enhance figure/equation presentation.
