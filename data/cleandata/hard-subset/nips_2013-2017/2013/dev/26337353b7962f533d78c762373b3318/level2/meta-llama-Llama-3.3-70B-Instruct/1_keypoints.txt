- Proposes sparse heterarchical matrix factorization (SHMF) for unsupervised learning of hierarchical concepts.  
- Targets calcium imaging data to identify neurons, assemblies, and temporal evolution of both.  
- Main claim: SHMF can simultaneously infer neurons, assembly memberships, and time courses.  
- Empirical support from synthetic data (accurate recovery) and real data (plausible interpretations).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and method description.  
- Thorough review of related work, highlighting limitations of existing methods.  
- Clear mathematical formulation and optimization strategy.  
- Experiments are well-designed with detailed analysis and comparisons to other methods.  
- Limitation: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices (e.g., η and λ) and their effects.  
- Limitation: lacks analysis of computational complexity and comparative runtime.  
- Limitation: limited discussion of potential applications beyond calcium imaging.  
- Novelty: significant improvement in learning relations across concept levels and enforcing structured sparsity.  
- Significance: potential high impact for neuroscience analysis of calcium imaging.  
- Recommendation: accept; presented as a novel, significant contribution with strong presentation and experiments.
