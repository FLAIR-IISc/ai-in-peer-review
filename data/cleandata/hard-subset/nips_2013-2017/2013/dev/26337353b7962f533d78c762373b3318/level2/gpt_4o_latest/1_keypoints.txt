- Introduces Sparse Heterarchical Matrix Factorization (SHMF), a bilevel framework for joint multi-level sparse matrix decomposition focused on calcium imaging data.
- Jointly identifies neurons, their assemblies, and temporal activity by optimizing all levels simultaneously, unlike greedy level-by-level methods.
- Novelty: Significant advancement over existing bilinear and hierarchical decomposition methods with a bilevel joint-learning approach.
- Technical soundness: Well-motivated optimization framework with structured sparsity constraints and rigorous description.
- Experimental validation: Comprehensive synthetic and real-data experiments showing improved sensitivity and robustness in recovering assemblies and temporal patterns.
- Practical significance: Addresses a key neuroscience problem, enabling identification of overlapping and highly correlated neurons and their assemblies.
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and clearly written with detailed methodology and experimental setup.
- Weakness — Scalability: Computational complexity and scalability for large datasets are not discussed in detail.
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Requires multiple hyperparameters (η, λ, rank bounds) with insufficient guidance on selection and sensitivity.
- Weakness — Limited comparison: Some state-of-the-art matrix factorization and neural data analysis methods are not included in evaluations.
- Weakness — Real-world validation: Lack of ground truth for assemblies in real calcium imaging data limits rigorous validation.
- Suggestions: Discuss computational complexity, provide parameter selection guidance and sensitivity analysis, expand comparisons to more baselines, and explore extensions for larger or real-time datasets.
- Recommendation: Strong scientific contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability and parameter concerns.
