- Tackles inferring neurons and overlapping assemblies from calcium imaging using a "bilevel sparse heterarchical matrix factorization."  
- Decomposes observations into a product of sparse matrices with decreasing rank across levels, allowing structured sparsity at each level.  
- Extends beyond prior hierarchical methods and does not require knowing the exact number of assemblies (though results depend on optimization parameters).  
- Jointly and in parallel infers dictionaries (neurons), assembly assignments, and time courses across levels—claimed to improve robustness.  
- Strong synthetic-data performance: outperformed MNMMF and KSVDS in recovering assembly assignments (all initialized with Adina).  
- Higher sensitivity in detecting calcium transients than MNMMF, Adina, or cell sorting.  
- Real-data example (rat hippocampus) included but conclusions are unclear; neurons appeared less spatially sparse than Adina initialization.  
- The inferred dictionary (single-neuron images) is not discussed—reviewer recommends showing reconstructions vs ground truth and other methods.  
- Paper is technically robust with detailed cost function and optimization in supplements.  
- Missing methodological details: choice/optimization of trade-off parameters, how adjacency matrices were binarized, and sensitivity to noise.  
- Clearly written and appropriately cites relevant literature.  
- Novel approach with potential significance for labs doing calcium imaging; flexible incorporation of priors noted.  
- Concern: apparent reliance on Adina-derived initialization, though that prior knowledge could be integrated at the model's 0-th level.
