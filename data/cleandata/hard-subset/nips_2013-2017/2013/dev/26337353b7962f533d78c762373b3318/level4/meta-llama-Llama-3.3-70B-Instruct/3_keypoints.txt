- Proposes a novel multilevel sparse representation algorithm as a penalized low-rank matrix factorization with more than two factors, each corresponding to a hierarchical entity.  
- Factors are estimated via block coordinate descent with sparsity-promoting penalties applied per block.  
- Targets large-scale calcium imaging, modeling a hierarchy of pixels → neurons → assemblies to estimate neuron locations, memberships, and spike patterns.  
- Authors claim improved performance over existing methods on simulated and real data, correctly identifying neural assemblies.  
- Methodological originality is strong: three-way decomposition is noteworthy and adding a two-way decomposition with consistency constraints can increase robustness.  
- Paper raises scalability/efficiency concerns for large datasets; while dimensionality is reduced and some steps parallelizable, computational speed and required iterations are not discussed.  
- The practical prevalence and benefit of neural assemblies are unclear, so the multilevel decomposition’s real-world advantage is not convincingly established.  
- Regularizers (e.g., ΩU and ΩA) and the convex cell-shape priors need clearer definition and justification.  
- Choice of many regularization weights is left to the user and seems critical; reliance on penalty tuning undermines the claim that rank overestimation is harmless.  
- Claim that irrelevant components will automatically shrink to zero may heavily depend on appropriately chosen sparsity penalties.  
- Paper clarity is poor and inaccessible to non-experts in calcium imaging.  
- Important aspect of spike-to-calcium translation (spike deconvolution) is omitted and should be integrated into the algorithmic discussion.  
- Relationship to existing literature (e.g., Vogelstein et al. 2010, Grewe et al. 2010) is not established.  
- Presentation choices are unconventional (main equations shown as a figure), and real-data experiments, especially Figure 6, need better explanation and stronger results.  
- Overall: interesting and innovative approach but requires significant clarification on computational efficiency, regularization, biological relevance, and presentation.
