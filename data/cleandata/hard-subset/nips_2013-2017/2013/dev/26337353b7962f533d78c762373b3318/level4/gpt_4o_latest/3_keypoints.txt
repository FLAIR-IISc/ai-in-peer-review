- Proposes multilevel sparse representation via penalized low-rank matrix factorization with block coordinate descent and sparsity penalties.
- Targets calcium imaging data by modeling a pixel → neuron → assembly hierarchy to estimate neuron locations, assembly memberships, and assembly spike activity.
- Claims superior performance on realistic synthetic datasets and successful identification of neural assemblies in real data.
- Novelty: the three-way decomposition and enforcing consistency with a standard two-way decomposition are interesting and potentially robust.
- Major concern about computational efficiency given large numbers of pixels and time steps; parallelization helps but overall runtime and iterations are unclear.
- Questionable modeling assumption: unclear prevalence and advantage of neural assemblies versus low-rank temporal structure when neurons share spike patterns.
- Regularizers ΩU and ΩA need clearer specification (e.g., exact convex cell shape priors) and guidance on selecting many regularization weights.
- Claim that irrelevant components will be shrunk to zero may depend heavily on careful sparsity penalty tuning and thus needs discussion.
- Paper lacks clarity and is accessible mainly to calcium-imaging experts.
- Crucial spike-to-calcium convolution/deconvolution handling is insufficiently described and not positioned relative to existing work (e.g., Vogelstein 2010).
- Presentation issues: main equations included as a figure is odd stylistically.
- Real-data experiments and Figure 6 are not well explained or compelling; need more comprehensive discussion and interpretation.
