- Proposes a bilevel sparse heterarchical matrix factorization to infer neurons and multi-membership assemblies from calcium imaging.
- Decomposes observations into sparse matrices with decreasing rank across levels, allowing structured sparsity at each level.
- Not limited to strict hierarchies and does not require prior knowledge of the exact number of assemblies (though results depend on user-defined optimization parameters).
- Requires initialization with a dictionary from Adina, though authors claim similar prior knowledge could be incorporated directly.
- On synthetic data, outperforms MNMMF and KSVDS in recovering neuron-to-assembly assignments (all methods initialized with Adina).
- On synthetic data, detects single-neuron calcium transients with higher sensitivity than MNMMF, Adina, or cell sorting.
- Real-data application (rat hippocampus) is provided but interpretation is unclear beyond reduced spatial sparsity versus Adina initialization.
- Emphasizes parallel inference of structures and time courses at every level, which purportedly improves robustness of neuronal time-course identification.
- The inferred dictionary (single-neuron images) is not discussed in detail; reviewer suggests showing reconstructed dictionaries vs. ground truth and other methods.
- Paper is technically sound with a detailed cost function and optimization procedure; supplemental material provides additional details.
- Some methodological aspects need more elaboration: optimization of trade-off parameters, binarization of adjacency matrices, and sensitivity to noise.
- Manuscript is clearly written and accessible.
- Approach appears novel and adequately references relevant literature.
- Results are potentially significant for calcium-imaging labs, offering flexibility to impose priors at multiple levels and robustness for assembly-based experiments.
- Overall assessment: interesting, technically solid work with potential value for researchers handling structured neural imaging data.
