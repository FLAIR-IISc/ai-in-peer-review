- Introduces Sparse Heterarchical Matrix Factorization (SHMF), a novel multi-level matrix decomposition framework for hierarchical data.  
- Captures hierarchical and heterarchical relationships jointly across levels (pixels → neurons → assemblies).  
- Enforces structured sparsity constraints that align with biological properties of neural data.  
- Optimization uses block coordinate descent, described with technical rigor and literature support.  
- Validated on synthetic and real calcium imaging data, recovering assemblies and temporal dynamics.  
- Demonstrates higher sensitivity and comparable precision relative to existing methods.  
- Potentially significant tool for neuroscience, enabling joint inference of neurons, assemblies, and dynamics.  
- Presentation clarity is limited in sections on optimization and multi-level generalization, needing clearer explanations/diagrams.  
- Evaluation lacks analysis of computational efficiency, scalability, and robustness to hyperparameter choices.  
- Comparisons to prior methods (e.g., MNNMF+Adina, KSVDS+Adina) need deeper discussion of trade-offs like compute cost and interpretability.  
- Applicability beyond calcium imaging is not demonstrated, raising questions about generality to other hierarchical domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantive methodological advance, strong empirical results, and good literature positioning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity issues, incomplete evaluation of efficiency/scalability, and limited demonstration of generality.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand evaluation and discussion of broader applicability.
