- Proposes sparse heterarchical matrix factorization (SHMF), a novel unsupervised method for data with hierarchical concepts.  
- Demonstrates SHMF on synthetic and real calcium imaging data to identify neurons, assemblies, and their temporal evolution.  
- Introduces a principled multi-level decomposition that supports both hierarchical and heterarchical relations.  
- Method learns the nature of inter-concept relations rather than imposing them.  
- Develops a joint optimization scheme for all levels instead of a greedy level-by-level approach.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear overview of related work, method, and experiments.  
- Method complexity may make it hard to understand and implement without strong matrix factorization/optimization background.  
- Lacks clear comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, making result significance difficult to assess.  
- Evaluation is focused mainly on calcium imaging, raising concerns about generalizability to other applications.  
- Pro-accept: novel, principled approach with promising real-world results and clear presentation.  
- Con-accept: high complexity, missing SOTA comparisons, and limited evaluation scope.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10; overall positive but recommends clearer comparisons and broader evaluation.
