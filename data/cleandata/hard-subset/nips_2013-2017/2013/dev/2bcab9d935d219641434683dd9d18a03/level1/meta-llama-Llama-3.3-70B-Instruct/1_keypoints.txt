- Presents two approximate inference algorithms (variational and sampling) for odor presence detection using receptor activations and a spike-and-slab prior.  
- Derives neural implementations and maps algorithms to neural dynamics; simulations show correct inference in <100 ms.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Beck et al. 2012) and related NIPS contributions.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation that is easy to follow.  
- Strength: use of a more realistic spike-and-slab prior is a significant contribution.  
- Strength: derivation of neural implementations of both algorithms is noteworthy.  
- Strength: simulations give valuable insights and the results discussion is thorough.  
- Weakness: assumes a simple generative model that may not capture olfactory system complexity.  
- Weakness: algorithms require complex nonlinearities that may be difficult to realize biologically.  
- Weakness: focuses solely on inference, assuming pre-learned mappings and priors (learning not addressed).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address noted weaknesses.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
