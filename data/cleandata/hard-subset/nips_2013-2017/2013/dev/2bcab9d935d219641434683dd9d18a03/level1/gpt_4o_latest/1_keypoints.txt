- Proposes two biologically plausible algorithms (variational inference and sampling) for olfactory inference using a novel spike-and-slab prior and maps them onto neural dynamics.  
- Both algorithms infer odors rapidly (within ~100 ms) and are behaviorally indistinguishable but differ in connectivity requirements and activity predictions.  

Strengths:
- Introduces a realistic sparse (spike-and-slab) prior, improving over smooth priors in prior work.  
- Produces experimentally testable, distinct predictions about neural activity for the two algorithms.  
- Derivations of neural dynamics are thorough and simulations are well-designed to assess speed and accuracy.  
- Clearly demonstrates rapid inference consistent with behavior and contrasts coding strategies (log-probability vs. probability).  
- Paper discusses limitations and outlines promising future directions (hierarchical priors, learning mechanisms).  

Weaknesses:
- Generative model uses simplifying assumptions (linearity, binary connectivity, Poisson noise) that may limit biological realism.  
- Neural implementations require complex nonlinear computations (e.g., logarithms, digamma) that may be biologically implausible or need further justification.  
- Simulations are small-scale (40 receptor types, 400 odors) and may not capture full olfactory system complexity.  
- No experimental data are provided to validate the proposed algorithms.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important, underexplored problem in sensory neuroscience.  
- Offers a novel, biologically plausible framework with realistic priors and ecologically relevant timescales.  
- Results are clear, well-supported, and yield testable experimental predictions.  
- Advances the field beyond prior models by incorporating sparsity and neural mapping.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Dependence on simplifying generative-model assumptions undermines immediate applicability.  
- Biological plausibility of required nonlinear neural computations is not fully addressed.  
- Limited simulation scale and absence of experimental validation reduce impact.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to better address neural implementation feasibility and scalability of simulations.
