- Studies olfactory inference from receptor firing rates using a probabilistic model and two neurally-plausible algorithms (variational inference and sampling).  
- Extends Beck et al. (NIPS 2012) by introducing a spike-and-slab prior over odor concentrations.  
- Derives dynamical neural update rules to implement both inference algorithms.  
- Uses simulations to assess biological feasibility and concludes both algorithms could meet natural time constraints and be distinguishable by firing patterns.  
- Tackles the broader question of whether neural systems can perform probabilistic reasoning, offering novel and insightful approaches.  
- Proposes technically sound and substantially different sampling-based methods that contribute to the literature on neural probabilistic inference.  
- Provides little direct biological evidence that either algorithm is actually employed by real olfactory systems.  
- Does not fully assess the implementation-level, anatomical, and computational constraints required for biological realization; leaves this for future work.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and novel, but biological plausibility and experimental support remain questionable.
