- Manuscript compares two hypotheses for olfactory inference: variational inference vs. sampling from receptor neuron firing rates.  
- Authors present a probabilistic model with a spike-and-slab prior over odor concentrations, extending Beck et al.  
- Two neurally-plausible inference algorithms are derived, with dynamical update rules mapping to neural implementations.  
- Simulations assess biological feasibility and conclude both approaches could operate within natural time constraints.  
- The algorithms yield distinguishable outcomes in observed firing patterns, allowing potential experimental tests.  
- The topic is highly relevant to NIPS and computational neuroscience, tackling probabilistic computation in neural systems.  
- Methodologically innovative and technically sound, offering novel ideas distinct from prior sampling-based approaches.  
- Limited biological or experimental evidence is provided that real olfactory systems use these algorithms.  
- Further work is needed to evaluate computational and anatomical constraints required for biological implementation.
