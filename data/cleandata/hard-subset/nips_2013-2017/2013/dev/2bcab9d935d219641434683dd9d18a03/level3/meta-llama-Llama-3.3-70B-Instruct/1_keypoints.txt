- Proposes two new Bayesian entropy estimators (ĤDBer and ĤDSyn) using a hierarchical mixture-of-Dirichlets prior.  
- Uses the synchrony distribution as the Dirichlet base measure to incorporate prior knowledge about spike trains.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and explains methodology and results clearly.  
- Provides thorough discussion of limitations and potential applications.  
- Evaluates estimators on simulations and real neural data.  
- Estimators capture statistical structure of spike trains ignored by traditional methods.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over existing methods (e.g., NSB) in simulations and real data.  
- Choice of priors for hyperparameters (α and p) may affect estimator performance; sensitivity discussed.  
- Computational complexity could be problematic for large datasets.  
- Addresses an important neuroscience problem with implications for neural coding and information processing.  
- Overall recommendation in favor of acceptance.  
- Suggested cons: prior sensitivity, computational cost, and need for expanded discussion of limitations/applications.
