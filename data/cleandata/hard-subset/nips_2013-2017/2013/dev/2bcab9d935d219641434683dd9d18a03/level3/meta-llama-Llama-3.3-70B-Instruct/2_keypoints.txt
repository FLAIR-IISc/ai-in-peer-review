- Proposes two novel Bayesian entropy estimators (ĤDBer and ĤDSyn) for binary spike trains in neural systems.
- Introduces a hierarchical mixture-of-Dirichlets prior with a base measure to incorporate prior knowledge about spike trains.
- Uses a synchrony distribution as the base measure to capture statistical structure of simultaneously recorded spike responses.
- Novelty and technical soundness are strong: the synchrony-base idea is original and well-motivated.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with thorough explanations.
- Provides thorough comparisons against existing estimators (NSB and BUB).
- Validates methods using simulations and real neural data, demonstrating effectiveness at capturing correlations.
- Potential limitation: assumes the synchrony distribution is known or can be accurately estimated, which may not always hold.
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability to large neural datasets.
- Addresses an important neuroscience problem and offers a significant contribution to probabilistic inference in neural circuits.
- Overall recommendation: accept, given originality, clarity, and demonstrated effectiveness.
