- Proposes a novel transfer-learning approach that reuses reward functions across tasks instead of policies, value functions, or models.  
- Introduces a reward mapping function within the Optimal Rewards Problem (ORP) framework to convert task-specifying rewards into useful guidance rewards for subsequent tasks.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and related work coverage.  
- Technical contribution is sound and builds appropriately on existing ORP literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, well-designed, and shows substantial performance gains over baselines including a policy-transfer method.  
- Strength: clear motivation and concise presentation.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation.  
- Weakness: assumes the agent has perfect knowledge of task specifications and environment dynamics.  
- Weakness: Focuses narrowly on reward-function transfer, leaving unclear how it generalizes to other transfer types.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational overhead and scalability to larger, more complex tasks.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to transfer learning and reinforcement learning; strong candidate for acceptance.  
- Acceptance pros: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical results and theoretical grounding.  
- Acceptance cons: unrealistic assumptions about knowledge, limited scope of transfer, and missing scalability/overhead analysis.
