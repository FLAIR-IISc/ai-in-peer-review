- Proposes a novel transfer-learning approach for long-lived agents that reuses reward functions across tasks instead of re-learning policies or value functions.  
- Builds on the Optimal Rewards Problem (ORP) framework, separating task-specifying objective rewards from internal rewards that guide behavior.  
- Introduces a reward-mapping agent that learns to map objective reward parameters to internal reward parameters to initialize new-task internal rewards.  
- Paper is well-written and presents clear algebraic proofs explaining the method.  
- Empirical results show substantial performance gains over baselines, including a competing policy transfer method.  
- Evaluation is thorough and demonstrated across two different domains.  
- Claims promising applications in bioinformatics and other domains.  
- Lacks experiments on non-synthetic (real-world) data to validate practical applicability.  
- Limited comparison to existing genomics methods; needs more comprehensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art.  
- Scalability concerns: behavior and performance in higher-dimensional settings (>3 variables) are not explored.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and is technically sound with theoretical and experimental support.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses in a revision.
