- Introduces novel kernel-based tests for three-way interactions (Lancaster interaction and total independence) using RKHS embeddings of signed measures.
- Addresses an important gap: scalable, nonparametric detection of higher-order interactions.
- Theoretical development is rigorous and the methods are consistent against broad alternatives for many kernels.
- Experimental results are strong; Lancaster test outperforms competitors in detecting V-structures, especially in high-dimensional settings.
- Provides a robust tool for identifying complex dependencies where pairwise tests fail, with applications to graphical model learning.
- V-structure concept needs a clearer, intuitive explanation when first introduced for readers unfamiliar with graphical models.
- Recommend adding a pointer to V-statistics in the kernel-methods context for additional reader context.
- Equation (1) is unclear: the definition of P* and the phrasing after “where” require clarification and more precise exposition.
- Experimental sample sizes are not justified; discuss how they were chosen and their impact on test performance.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written contribution; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to address clarity and experimental justification.
