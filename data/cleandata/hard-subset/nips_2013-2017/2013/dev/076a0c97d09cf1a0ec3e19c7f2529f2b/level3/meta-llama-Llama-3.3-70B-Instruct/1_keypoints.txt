- Novel approach: reuse reward functions for transfer learning in long-lived agents solving task sequences.  
- Builds on the optimal rewards problem (ORP), separating task objective rewards from internal guiding rewards.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, background, and technical exposition.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation in grid world and networking routing domains.  
- Results show the reward-mapping approach improves performance versus other methods, including policy transfer.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem with a novel solution.  
- Strength: clear and concise writing makes the approach easy to follow.  
- Strength: thorough experiments provide strong empirical evidence.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis delineating when the approach is effective.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—only two domains and specific task sequences.  
- Weakness: implementation details (hyperparameters, learning algorithms for reward mapping) need clearer explanation.  
- Overall: significant contribution with potential broad applicability and a solid experimental foundation.  
- Pro acceptance: novel/significant contribution, strong experimental support, clear presentation.  
- Con acceptance: missing theory, limited generalization evidence, and reproducibility concerns due to sparse implementation details.
