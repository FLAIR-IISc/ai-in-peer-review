This article discusses the problem of creating a hypothesis test based on the kernel method to identify three-variable interactions. This work is an extension of the HSIC (Hilbert Schmidt Independence Criterion), which is designed to identify two-variable interactions based on the kernel method. There are various types of three-variable interactions, but the authors have attempted to identify an interaction called the Lancaster interaction, which is defined as ¥delta L = Pxyx -PxyPz - PxzPy - PyzPx - 2 PxPyPz. In the proposed method, the above-mentioned marginal and joint probabilities are embedded within the kernel Hilbert space, and are guaranteed to work as long as they satisfy the ISPD (Integrally Strictly Positive Definite) condition. In simulation tests, the proposed method's ability to identify interactions in three ways was demonstrated by performing a permutation test using ¥delta L as a test statistic.
