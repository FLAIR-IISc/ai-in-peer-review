This paper provides a very interesting non-parametric approach to testing three-variable interactions (and independence) using the embedding properties of the ISPD kernel. Overall, the paper is well-written, clear, and detailed in its coverage of the underlying algebraic proofs. Given the need for efficient statistical testing tools to detect complex interaction effects in bioinformatics, this work seems promising in its applications.

Given the stated objective of identifying gene interactions, it would be good to see comparisons of the performance of experiments on non-synthetic data and/or other genomics methods (such as based on partial correlations). Furthermore, the possibility raised in the final paragraph that the test could be extended to more than 3 variables seems somewhat contradictory to the point raised in section 4.3. Finally, it would be very interesting to see how this method performs in large-scale locations.

Minor issue: There is no reference to Figure 3 in the main text. A solid and novel statistical approach, with promising applications. A more detailed performance comparison with other cutting-edge methods for interaction testing would be beneficial.
