This paper addresses the problem of constructing hypothesis tests based on kernel methods to identify three-variable interactions. It extends the HSIC (Hilbert Schmidt Independence Criterion), which is designed to detect two-variable interactions based on kernel methods. While several types of three-variable interactions exist, the authors attempt to identify one called the Lancaster interaction, defined as a single measure of ¥delta L = Pxyx -PxyPz - PxzPy - PyzPx - 2 PxPyPz. In the proposed procedure, the above marginal and combined probabilities are embedded in all kernel Hilbert spaces, and this is guaranteed to work as long as they meet the ISPD (Integrally Strictly Positive Definite) condition. Simulation experiments demonstrate the proposed method's ability to identify three types of interactions using a combination test with ¥delta L as the test statistic.
