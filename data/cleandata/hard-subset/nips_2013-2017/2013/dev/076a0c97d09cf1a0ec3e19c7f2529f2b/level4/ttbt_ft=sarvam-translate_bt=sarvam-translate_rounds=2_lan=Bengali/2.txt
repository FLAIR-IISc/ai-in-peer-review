This paper provides a highly appealing nonparametric method for testing three-variable interactions (and independence) using the embedding feature of the ISPD kernel. Overall, the paper is well-written, clear, and has extensive coverage of underlying algebraic proof. With the need for efficient statistical testing tools to identify complex interaction effects in bioinformatics, this work seems promising in its application.

Considering the illustrative purpose described for identifying gene interactions, it would be good to see performance comparisons with tests on non-synthetic data and/or other genomics methods (such as linkage-based). Also, the possibility raised in the concluding paragraph that the test could be extended to more than three variables seems somewhat counterintuitive to the argument raised in section 4. Finally, it might be interesting to see how the method performs in large-scale settings.

Minor issue: There is no mention of Figure 3 in the main text. A powerful and novel statistical method, with promising applications. It could benefit from more extensive performance comparisons with other advanced methods for interaction testing.
