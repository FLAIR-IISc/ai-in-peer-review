- Paper proposes kernel-based hypothesis tests to detect three-way interactions among variables.  
- Builds on HSIC, extending two-variable kernel independence testing to three variables.  
- Focuses on detecting the Lancaster interaction defined by ΔL = P_xyz - P_xy P_z - P_xz P_y - P_yz P_x - 2 P_x P_y P_z.  
- Embeds marginals and joint distributions into an RKHS; method requires kernels to be ISPD.  
- Uses a permutation test with ΔL as the test statistic and shows effectiveness via simulations.  
- Minor error: the phrase "Figure 2 plots the Type II error" should refer to "Figure 3."  
- Paper is well-written, properly referenced, and provides rigorous proofs.  
- Notes extension to higher-order and structured interactions as valuable future work.
