- Proposes a novel kernel-based hypothesis test extending HSIC from pairwise to three-variable interaction detection.
- Targets Lancaster interactions, defined via the signed measure δL = Pxyz - PxyPz - PxzPy - PyzPx + 2PxPyPz.
- Method embeds joint and marginal distributions into an RKHS and relies on Integral Strictly Positive Definiteness (ISPD) for validity.
- Uses δL as the test statistic and employs permutation testing in simulations to demonstrate ability to detect three-way interactions.
- Minor correction: "Figure 2 plots the Type II error" should read "Figure 3" on page 8.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and supported by appropriate references and proofs.
- Noted that extensions to higher-order and structured interaction detection would be valuable.
