The paper expands the existing machine for testing kernel dependency into a three-way interaction. It's an interesting and well-executed work that attacks an important problem for which currently very limited tools exist. The paper is generally well-written.

A few minor comments:

*   Explain the V-structure more clearly in the first use.
*   Provide a pointer to V-statistics (I've heard of U-statistics, but not V-statistics).
*   The equation (1) doesn't make sense as a writing. You need to define the P more clearly. "Where" after (1) doesn't clarify it.
*   In the test, explain the sample size. A nice extension of the kernel dependency test into a three-way interaction.
