- Introduces a nonparametric test for three-variable interaction (Lancaster interaction) using kernel embeddings of signed measures into an RKHS.
- Test statistic is the RKHS norm of the embedding of the Lancaster interaction measure, which vanishes for non-trivially factorable joint distributions.
- Also proposes a test for total independence as a special case of the Lancaster interaction test.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise introduction and well-organized technical exposition of kernel embeddings and test derivation.
- Experiments show strong effectiveness in detecting three-variable interactions, especially where pairwise tests fail.
- Demonstrates superior performance in detecting V-structures compared to competing approaches like conditional independence tests.
- Strength: fills a gap by providing a new nonparametric test for three-variable interaction.
- Strength: kernel embedding of signed measures offers a flexible and powerful testing framework.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across various scenarios.
- Weakness: computational cost may be prohibitive for large datasets.
- Weakness: limited to three-variable interactions, restricting applicability to more complex datasets.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other nonparametric tests (e.g., mutual information, distance covariance).
- Pro-accept: novel, powerful test with convincing empirical results and clear exposition.
- Con-accept: concerns about scalability, limited scope (three variables), and insufficient baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, extend beyond three variables if possible, and include more comparisons to other nonparametric methods.
