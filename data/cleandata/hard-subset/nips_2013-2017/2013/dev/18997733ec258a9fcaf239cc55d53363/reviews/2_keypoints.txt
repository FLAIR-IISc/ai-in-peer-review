- Paper proposes a UCB-based contextual bandit algorithm that leverages a user graph structure for information sharing.  
- Model: contextual bandits with linear payoffs and user-dependent unknown coefficient vectors tied to a given user graph.  
- Algorithm uses the graph Laplacian to propagate information between users with similar coefficient vectors.  
- Authors provide a regret bound and numerical experiments supporting the method.  
- Contribution is a natural and relevant extension of contextual bandits to incorporate graph information.  
- Writing is generally good but there are several English/typo issues.  
- Intuition (especially Section 4) is not clearly explained and hard to follow.  
- Post-rebuttal, reviewer lowered score due to persistent intuition/exposition problems.  
- Reviewer finds it odd that the implemented algorithm (CB) differs from the one analyzed (TCB) and there is no discussion on whether CB can be analyzed.  
- Analogy noted: discrepancy similar to LinUCB (empirical) vs SupLinUCB (theoretical) without reconciliatory discussion.  
- Overall technically sound but not surprising or strongly executed; reviewer does not recommend acceptance.
