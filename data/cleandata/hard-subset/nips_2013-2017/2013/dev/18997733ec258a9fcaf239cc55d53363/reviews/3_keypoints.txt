- Paper studies contextual linear bandits on a graph with node weight-vectors smooth over neighbors—reasonable model for social recommendations.  
- Proposed algorithm runs per-node contextual bandits and shares updates across nodes via the graph Laplacian.  
- Experiments are strong: detailed, reproducible, and evaluated on real data.  
- The theoretical analysis is disappointing because it analyzes a different algorithm than the one implemented and evaluated.  
- No explanation or discussion of how the analyzed (TCB) vs. implemented (CB) algorithms differ in algorithmic behavior, evaluation, or deployment.  
- This mismatch raises the possibility that the analyzed algorithm performs poorly in experiments or that the implemented algorithm lacks provable guarantees.  
- Key terms in the regret bound (ln|M_t| and 2L) are not characterized: unclear dependence on T, n, and graph noise.  
- The paper does not provide guidance on choosing the exploration parameter α nor clarify how α affects the upper bound.  
- Suggestion/question: use regularization of the Laplacian (L + cI) to control “somehow”/“similar” update strength; current algorithm lacks an explicit parameter for this.  
- Question why the regret bound uses the unregularized Laplacian L when the algorithm regularizes it.  
- After rebuttal the reviewer recommends removing the theoretical analysis unless the relationship between the two algorithms is clarified; overall: good experiments but analysis undermines the contribution.
