- Introduces GOB.Lin, a novel global recommendation strategy for networked bandit problems that assigns a bandit to each node and shares signals with neighbors.  
- Leverages user similarities encoded as a graph and evaluates performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, thorough related work, organized technical sections, and easy-to-follow pseudocode.  
- Strength: Novel approach with potential to improve recommendation systems.  
- Strength: Algorithm is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: Algorithm has quadratic dependence on the number of nodes, limiting scalability.  
- Weakness: Assumes the graph structure is known, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive with additional datasets and baselines.  
- Pro-accept: Significant novel contribution to networked bandit literature with promising empirical results.  
- Con-accept: Scalability, reliance on known graph structure, and limited experimental breadth are important concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
