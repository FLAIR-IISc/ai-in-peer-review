- Novel integration of social network structures into contextual bandits via the GOB.Lin algorithm enabling signal sharing across neighboring users.
- GOB.Lin assigns a contextual bandit to each network node and leverages Laplacian-based coupling for multitask learning.
- Two clustering-based variants (GOB.Lin.MACRO and GOB.Lin.BLOCK) are proposed to address scalability and noise.
- The paper provides theoretical regret bounds for GOB.Lin, grounded in Laplacian and multitask analysis.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (Last.fm, Delicious) shows substantial gains over baselines that ignore network structure.
- Clustering-based methods offer a practical scalability trade-off and help mitigate noisy network effects.
- Theoretical and algorithmic sections are detailed but may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with advanced matrix operations.
- Baseline comparisons are limited to LinUCB-IND and LinUCB-SIN, lacking more recent or sophisticated contextual bandit and graph-based methods.
- GOB.Lin’s performance degrades with increasing graph noise and the paper explores clustering but not other robustness strategies.
- Scalability remains a concern due to quadratic dependence on the number of nodes, even with clustering.
- Strong argument for acceptance based on novelty, theoretical rigor, and empirical benefits for recommendation systems.
- Arguments against acceptance include limited baseline breadth, insufficient robustness analysis for noisy graphs, and presentation clarity issues.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand baseline comparisons.
