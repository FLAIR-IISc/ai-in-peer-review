- Proposes two novel Bayesian entropy estimators (ĤDBer and ĤDSyn) for binary spike trains using a hierarchical mixture-of-Dirichlets with a tailored base measure.
- Base measure uses a synchrony distribution to incorporate prior knowledge and capture sparsity and correlation in spike trains.
- Empirically outperforms traditional entropy estimators on simulated and real neural datasets.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a strong introduction and detailed methodology.
- Results section provides thorough evaluation demonstrating accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency.
- Computational efficiency makes the estimators practical for large-scale neural data analysis.
- Strength: effective integration of prior structure (synchrony) into the estimator.
- Weakness: minor grammatical mistakes and some figures lack clarity (noted on pages 2–3).
- Weakness: insufficient intuitive explanation of the algorithm and its interpretation (e.g., possible bandit-like analogy).
- Recommendation: accept with significant revisions to improve clarity and presentation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, incorporation of prior knowledge, strong empirical support, and computational practicality.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation issues and lack of algorithmic intuition.
