- Proposes two Bayesian entropy estimators (ĤDBer, ĤDSyn) using a hierarchical mixture-of-Dirichlets prior with a base measure tailored to spike-train structure.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with ideas and methods easy to follow.  
- Novel approach that outperforms existing methods in simulations and real neural-data experiments.  
- Base-measure/prior design is a key innovation enabling capture of higher-order dependencies in neural data.  
- Provides detailed analysis of estimator properties supporting empirical claims.  
- Regret analysis is problematic: it analyzes a different algorithm than the one evaluated, with no explanation of differences.  
- Regret bound lacks clarity on the behavior and variable dependence of certain terms, limiting insight.  
- No guideline for setting the exploration parameter and unclear dependence of the upper bound on that parameter.  
- Experiments are thorough and reproducible with real-data evaluations.  
- Use of graph and Laplacian regularization is questioned; alternative regularization (e.g., directly regularizing the Laplacian) may be preferable to clustering-based regularizers.  
- Theoretical analysis for a changed algorithm is confusing and may be unnecessary or counterproductive.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and significance; reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on addressing the regret-analysis and exploration-parameter issues.
