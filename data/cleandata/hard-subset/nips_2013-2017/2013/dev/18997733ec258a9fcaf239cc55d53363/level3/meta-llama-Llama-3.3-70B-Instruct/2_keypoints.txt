- Proposes two Bayesian entropy estimators (ĤDBer, ĤDSyn) using a hierarchical mixture-of-Dirichlets prior tailored for neural spike trains.  
- Introduces a synchrony distribution as the base measure to capture simultaneous spike-response structure.  
- Demonstrates ĤDBer and ĤDSyn outperform NSB and BUB estimators on multiple simulated and real neural datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanations and sound technical development.  
- Provides computational-complexity analysis and shows feasibility for large-scale neural data.  
- Strength: novel use of a synchrony base measure that leverages prior knowledge about spike trains.  
- Weakness: minor English errors and some sections (notably Section 4) lack clear intuition.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relationship to existing work and prior literature.  
- Weakness: limited insight or sensitivity analysis regarding choice of hyper-priors.  
- Originality: method is novel and interesting but not a major paradigm shift.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound contribution that advances entropy estimation for neural data.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, effective empirical performance, clear exposition, scalable.  
- Rejection concerns: not groundbreaking, minor clarity/language issues, and limited hyper-prior/related-work discussion.
