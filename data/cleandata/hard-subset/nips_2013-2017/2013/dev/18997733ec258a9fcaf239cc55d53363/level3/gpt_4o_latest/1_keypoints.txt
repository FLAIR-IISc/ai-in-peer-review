- Presents GOB.Lin, a graph-regularized linear contextual bandit that integrates social network information, with regret bounds and empirical validation.  
- Novelty: original extension of linear bandits to incorporate graph-based user relationships, timely given social data availability.  
- Theoretical rigor: solid regret analysis using the graph Laplacian, aligning with multitask learning literature.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic and real-world (Last.fm, Delicious) experiments show consistent improvements over non-graph baselines.  
- Scalability: clustering-based variants (GOB.Lin.MACRO, GOB.Lin.BLOCK) address scalability for larger graphs.  
- Notation issue: Algorithm 2 uses CB instead of TCB, which conflicts with the theoretical analysis and may confuse readers.  
- Unjustified assumption: requirement to know L(u1,…,un) or an upper bound is not adequately motivated and may be unrealistic.  
- Need clearer intuition: algorithm should be reframed (e.g., as n bandits with graph-influenced updates) to improve accessibility.  
- Writing and typos: minor errors ("distribuited", "similarly") and organization reduce clarity, especially in algorithm explanations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing notation/clarity and providing stronger justification/intuitions; overall a valuable contribution.  
- Pro/Con summary: pro — novel method, strong theory and experiments, scalable variants; con — misleading notation, unclear assumptions, minor clarity issues.
