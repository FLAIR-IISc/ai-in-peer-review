-------Original Comment-----------------
This paper provides a relevant bandit algorithm based on UCB that can use information provided in the form of a graph structure. Specifically, the authors consider a relevant bandit model with linear payoff and user-dependent unknown coefficient vectors. A graph structure is provided for the users, and an assumption of the relationship between the graph structure and the user's coefficient vectors ensures that the graph provides meaningful information about the coefficient vectors. The algorithm then uses this information to spread a user's information to other users with similar coefficient vectors using the graph's Laplacian matrix. The authors provide both a regret limit and numerical tests to support their proposed method.

This is a natural extension of currently available techniques and an important common area for combining bandit concepts in more realistic situations. Overall, the paper is well-written. However, there are some sentences where the English is not entirely correct (see some such typos below), and I did not easily understand the concept given in section 4.

--------After Rejection-----------------
After discussing this submission in detail with other reviewers, I decided to reduce my quality score. Initially, I thought that my lack of familiarity with graph-based methods hindered my understanding of the concept and the choices made in the paper. However, I am now convinced that the concept was not well presented. Also, the implementation of CBU is strange, but the analysis of TCB. There is no discussion on whether CBU analysis can be done. Although it is true that there is a difference between LNUCB and SupLNUCB, the former has been experimentally studied in detail, and the latter has been theoretically studied in a separate paper. There are no (technically) errors with the components of the contribution, I no longer consider it a strong candidate for acceptance.
