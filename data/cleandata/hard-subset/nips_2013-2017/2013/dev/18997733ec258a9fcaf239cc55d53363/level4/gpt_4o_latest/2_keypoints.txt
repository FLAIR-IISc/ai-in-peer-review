- Proposes a UCB-based contextual bandit algorithm that leverages a user graph and its Laplacian to transfer knowledge between users with similar linear payoff coefficients.
- Studies a contextual bandit setting with user-dependent unknown coefficient vectors and assumes the graph encodes meaningful similarity among these vectors.
- Provides a theoretical regret bound and supporting numerical experiments for the proposed method.
- Paper represents a natural, practically relevant extension of contextual bandits and is generally well written.
- The intuition and design choices—especially the exposition in Section 4—are unclear and difficult to follow.
- Reviewer lowered their score after rebuttal, concluding the intuition remains insufficiently presented.
- It is unusual that the implementation focuses on CB while the theoretical analysis addresses TCB, with no discussion of whether CB can be analyzed.
- Notes a discrepancy with prior practice (LinUCB studied empirically, SupLinUCB analyzed theoretically) but no resolution of that gap here.
- Technical components appear sound but the contribution lacks novelty and therefore is not a strong acceptance candidate.
- Several English typos are present and should be corrected (examples listed in the review).
