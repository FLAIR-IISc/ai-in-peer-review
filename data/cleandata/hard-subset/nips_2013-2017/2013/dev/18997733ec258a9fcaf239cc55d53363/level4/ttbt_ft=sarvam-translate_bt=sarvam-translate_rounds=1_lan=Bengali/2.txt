-------Original Comment-----------------
This paper provides a relevant bandit algorithm based on UCBs that can use information given in the form of a graph structure. Specifically, the authors consider a relevant bandit model with linear payoff and user-dependent unknown coefficient vectors. A graph structure is given for the users, and an assumption of the relationship between the graph structure and the user's coefficient vectors ensures that the graph provides meaningful information about the coefficient vectors. The algorithm then uses this information to disseminate information about a user to other users with similar coefficient vectors, using the graph's Laplacian matrix. The authors provide both a regret limit and numerical tests to support their proposed method.

This is a natural extension of currently available techniques and an important common ground for combining bandit concepts in more realistic situations. Overall, the paper is well-written. However, there are some sentences where the English is not entirely correct (see some such typos below), and I did not easily understand the concept given in section 4.

--------After Rejection-----------------
After discussing this submission in detail with other reviewers, I decided to reduce my quality score. Initially, I thought that my lack of familiarity with graph-based methods hindered my understanding of the concept and the choices made in the paper. However, I am now convinced that the concept was not well presented. Also, the implementation of the CBU is strange, but the analysis of the TCB. There is no discussion on whether the CBU analysis can be done. Although it is true that there is a difference between LinUCB and SupLinUCB, the former has been studied in detail empirically, and the latter has been studied theoretically in a separate paper. There is no (technically) error with the components of the contribution, I no longer consider it a strong candidate for acceptance.

The paper provides an interesting and natural extension of relevant bandits to include other information available in the form of graphs, which is of interest to the machine learning community. However, the work is not surprising, and several parts of the work, such as the concept, were not well done.
