-------Original Notes-----------------
This paper provides a UCB-based relevant bandit algorithm that can utilize information provided in the form of a graph structure. Specifically, the authors consider a relevant bandit model with linear pay-outs and unknown coefficient vectors that depend on the user. A graph structure is provided for the users, and an assumption on the relationship between the graph structure and the user coefficient vectors ensures that the graph provides meaningful information about the coefficient vectors. The algorithm then uses this information to convey information obtained about one user to other users with similar coefficient vectors, using the Laplacian matrix of the graph. The authors provide both an error bound and numerical experiments to support their proposed approach.

This is a natural extension of currently available techniques and is an important general direction for integrating bandit ideas into more practical scenarios. Overall, the paper is quite well-written. However, there are some sentences where the English is not entirely correct (see below for some of these typos), and I found the intuition given in section 4 to be not easy to understand.

--------After Rejection-----------------
After discussing this presentation in detail with other reviewers, I have decided to reduce my quality score. Initially, I thought that my unfamiliarity with graph-based methods hindered my understanding of the intuition and options presented in the paper. However, I now believe that the intuition is not well-provided. Furthermore, it is strange to see the implementation of the CB, but the analysis of the TCB. There is no discussion about whether the CB can be analyzed or not. While it is true that there is a discrepancy between LinUCB and SupLinUCB, the former was empirically studied in detail, and the latter in theory in a separate paper. Nothing (technically) is wrong with the elements of the contribution, but I no longer find it a strong candidate for acceptance.
