- Proposes a novel UCB-based contextual bandit algorithm that leverages graph-structured information to improve decisions.  
- Targets a contextual bandit model with linear payoffs and unknown user-dependent coefficient vectors.  
- Imposes a user graph with an assumption linking graph topology to coefficient similarity, enabling transfer of information.  
- Uses the graph Laplacian to propagate knowledge from one user to others with similar coefficients.  
- Provides a regret bound and numerical experiments to validate the approach.  
- Represents a logical extension of existing techniques toward more practical bandit settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains minor grammatical errors.  
- Intuition in Section 4 is unclear and not effectively conveyed.  
- Reviewer reassessed and lowered quality score after discussion with peers due to unclear intuition and methodological concerns.  
- Implementation of CB alongside analysis of TCB raises unanswered questions about the analyzability of CB.  
- Notes discrepancy between empirical study of LinUCB and theoretical use of SupLinUCB; technical parts are sound but not compelling for acceptance.  
- Work is interesting and natural but not particularly surprising, and several aspects are not well-executed.
