- Introduces GOB.Lin, a global recommendation strategy for networked contextual bandits that shares signals between neighboring nodes.
- Models networked bandits by leveraging user similarities represented as a graph.
- Proposes variants GOB.Lin.MACRO and GOB.Lin.BLOCK to reduce computational cost while retaining performance.
- Claims GOB.Lin outperforms state-of-the-art contextual bandits that ignore relational information.
- Provides a theoretical analysis including a regret bound for GOB.Lin.
- Evaluates performance on synthetic data and two real-world datasets (Last.fm and Delicious).
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and supports the paper’s claims.
- Algorithm and variants are clearly and thoroughly described, with sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- References are comprehensive, relevant, and correctly cited.
- Demonstrates high potential usefulness for recommender systems in social networks.
- Novel contribution to networked bandit problems with significant methodological innovation.
- Limitations are acknowledged and potential future work is discussed.
- Main weaknesses: potential scalability issues of GOB.Lin and limited evaluation on larger datasets.
- Recommendation: accept, given strong contribution, clarity, and solid empirical/theoretical support.
