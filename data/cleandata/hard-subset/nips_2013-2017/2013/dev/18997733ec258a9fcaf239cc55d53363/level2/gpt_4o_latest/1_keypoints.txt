- Novel approach: integrates social network structure into contextual multi-armed bandits to improve recommendations.
- Global strategy: assigns a contextual bandit to each user and enables signal sharing among neighboring nodes.
- Scalability/noise handling: proposes two clustering-based variants to address scalability and noisy graphs.
- Theoretical support: provides detailed regret analysis grounded in multitask learning and linear bandit theory.
- Empirical validation: shows superior performance on synthetic and real-world (Last.fm, Delicious) datasets.
- Clear presentation: algorithms and pseudocode (GOB.Lin and variants) are clearly described for reproducibility.
- Scalability limitation: GOB.Lin has quadratic time dependence on number of nodes, which remains a bottleneck.
- Limited limitations discussion: insufficient analysis of trade-offs and failure cases, especially with noisy or dynamic graphs.
- Missing comparisons: lacks experimental comparison with other social-information methods like feature hashing or graph neural networks.
- Dataset diversity: evaluation limited to two real-world and one synthetic dataset, reducing generalizability.
