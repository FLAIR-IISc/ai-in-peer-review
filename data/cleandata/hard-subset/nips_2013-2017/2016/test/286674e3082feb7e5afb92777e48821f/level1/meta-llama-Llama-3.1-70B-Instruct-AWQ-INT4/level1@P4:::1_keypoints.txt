- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that does not assume data is generated by a specific model.
- Provides a generic theorem: if clustering C fits data w.r.t. model class M, then any other clustering C' that also fits is close to C.
- Instantiates the theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).
- Approach is related to prior model-based clustering (SBM, degree-corrected SBM, PFM) but distinct because it relies on checkable data statistics rather than generative assumptions.
- Develops new algorithms and theoretical guarantees, with thorough analysis for PFM and SBM.
- Novel perspective that contributes to cluster validation, existing recovery theorems, and stability under graph perturbations.
- Applicability is limited to graphs well-represented by block models.
- Use of the spectral norm in assumptions may be overly restrictive; Frobenius norm might simplify proofs.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and needs expansion.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Overall rating: 8/10 with recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Minor revisions requested: expand experiments and dataset comparisons, consider Frobenius-norm assumptions, and clarify relation to existing model-based clustering work.
