- Presents a novel, model-free framework that guarantees graph clustering stability without assuming a specific generative block model.  
- Core idea: if clustering C and its block-model parameters fit graph G well, any other good-fitting clustering C' must be a small perturbation of C.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), linking to prior model-based recovery results.  
- Original and significant conceptual shift toward data-dependent validation, addressing a major practical limitation of block-model theory.  
- Technical approach is sound, leveraging matrix perturbation results (e.g., Davis–Kahan) and producing bounds computable from data.  
- Experiments validate the PFM-based theorem and demonstrate practical verifiability of derived quantities (goodness-of-fit, eigengaps).  
- Writing and organization are clear; the Generic Theorem blueprint effectively explains the methodology before instantiation.  
- SBM bounds (Theorem 9) are too loose in experiments (even on LFR benchmarks), limiting practical informativeness.  
- Paper should discuss why SBM bounds are not tight and propose avenues to tighten them.  
- Section 4.2’s presentation connecting to existing recovery theorems needs clearer structure; a comparison table of assumptions and bounds would help.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution that meaningfully bridges theory and practice in community detection, with only minor presentation and bound-tightness issues.
