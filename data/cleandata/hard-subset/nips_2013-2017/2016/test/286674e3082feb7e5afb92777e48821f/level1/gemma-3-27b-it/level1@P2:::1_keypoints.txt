- Proposes a novel framework for graph clustering that provides theoretical guarantees without assuming data is generated by a specific block model.  
- Core idea: guarantee recovery based on how well a clustering fits observed data rather than on a generative model.  
- Demonstrates the framework by deriving model-free guarantees for PFM and SBM and relating them to model-based results.  
- Well-motivated: addresses unrealistic assumption of specific generative models in prior work.  
- Background section concisely reviews relevant concepts and sets up the contribution.  
- Theorems are precisely stated and technically sound; proofs appear reasonable though many are deferred to the supplement.  
- Connection between model-free and model-based guarantees is interesting and well-justified.  
- Experimental evaluation provides initial support but is cursory and lacks breadth and systematic exploration.  
- Reliance on the supplement for core proofs is a weakness that limits thorough assessment.  
- Claims about computational tractability and applicability are not fully substantiated empirically.  
- Presentation issues: dense notation, terse explanations, and some disjoint organization (especially Section 4).  
- Specific clarity needed for Proposition 10 and clearer signposting/structure in proofs.  
- Original and significant: extends stability-style ideas to block models and could broaden real-world applicability.  
- Practical impact tempered by potentially restrictive conditions for guarantees; more empirical validation required.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to include full proofs, stronger experiments, and clearer exposition.
