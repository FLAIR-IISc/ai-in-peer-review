- Paper proposes a novel model-free framework (Generic Theorem) that converts model-based goodness-of-fit into guarantees about closeness of alternative clusterings, with instantiations for SBM and PFM and all quantities computable from data.

- Builds on prior SBM and clustering stability literature but uniquely connects model-based recovery theorems to model-free validation.

- Strength: Highly original idea of “turning around” model-based theorems to obtain model-free validation guarantees.

- Strength: Theoretical results are rigorous with clear proofs and concrete, computable instantiations for SBM and PFM.

- Strength: Experiments on synthetic and real data (including Political Blogs) show the framework is implementable and can yield practical bounds; authors improve an existing error bound by a factor of 128.

- Strength: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and reproducible detail.

- Strength: Authors honestly discuss limitations and suggest future directions (e.g., tighter SBM bounds, non-spectral thresholds).

- Weakness: Model-free guarantees are limited for SBM in practice (reported δ values are >1 and thus not informative).

- Weakness: Experimental validation focuses on demonstrating the framework rather than showing practical superiority over other validation methods (e.g., bootstrap or stability-based approaches).

- Weakness: Computational cost is not addressed; spectral computations for PFM may be expensive for large graphs.

- Weakness: Limited direct comparison to alternative model-free validation techniques in experiments and discussion.

- Arguments for acceptance: original and significant contribution, technically sound, immediately useful for practitioners, and advances connection between model-based and model-free approaches.

- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical applicability for SBM, weaker experimental demonstration versus alternatives, and unaddressed scalability concerns.

- Final recommendation: Recommend acceptance — significant contribution with strong PFM results and practical potential despite SBM limitations.
