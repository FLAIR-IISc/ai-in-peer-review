- Proposes a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Core contribution is a "Generic Theorem": if clustering C fits graph under model M, any other clustering C' that also fits must be close to C.  
- All guarantee-related quantities are computable from observed data rather than relying on model parameters.  
- Instantiates the Generic Theorem for SBM and PFM, linking and strengthening existing model-based results.  
- Provides precise, data-computable bounds for SBM and PFM, addressing a key limitation in prior work.  
- Proof techniques are elegant and simpler than many prior approaches.  
- Demonstrates improved bounds over prior work (e.g., improves [20] by factor of 128).  
- Experimental validation across multiple datasets shows practical applicability, especially for PFM.  
- Main weakness: model-free guarantees are much harder to satisfy for SBM than for PFM, limiting broad applicability.  
- Paper lacks sufficient explanation for why SBM bounds are weaker than PFM bounds.  
- Discussion of "sanity checks" (Section 4.3) is superficial and could be deepened.  
- Needs better comparison and contextualization with other model-free methods (e.g., [6], [11]).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and impactful; recommendation for acceptance despite applicability limitations.
