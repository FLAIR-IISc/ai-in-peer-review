- Introduces a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that provides correctness guarantees without assuming data follows a specific block model.  
- Core idea: enforce stability—any other clustering that fits the data well must be a small perturbation of a good clustering C.  
- Instantiates the framework for both PFM and SBM, linking model-free and model-based recovery guarantees.  
- Theorems 4 and 9 are central theoretical contributions offering provable stability and recovery results.  
- Uses prior results (e.g., [20]) to strengthen model-free PFM guarantees (Theorem 11).  
- Well-motivated and grounded in literature on block models and spectral clustering.  
- Rigorous presentation of “goodness of fit” and stability guarantees.  
- Paper is technically dense and requires substantial background in spectral graph theory and matrix perturbation to follow.  
- Computability of ε and δ is mentioned but lacks concrete discussion of practical values in real-world graphs.  
- Experimental section shows stability in some cases but is limited in scope and depth.  
- SBM-based guarantees appear highly restrictive, casting doubt on their practical applicability.  
- Related work discussion is thorough but could better integrate and contrast the proposed approach with existing methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, and successful bridging of model-based and model-free approaches.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high technical density, limited practicality of SBM results, and insufficient experimental evaluation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and discuss practical limitations of SBM results.
