- Introduces a novel model-free framework that provides theoretical guarantees for graph clustering via stability of model fit rather than assuming a generative model.  
- Core idea: if clustering C and its block-model parameters fit G well, any other good-fitting clustering C' must be a small perturbation of C.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for both PFM and SBM, deriving explicit, computable bounds on clustering distance.  
- All main quantities (goodness-of-fit ε, empirical eigengap, etc.) are computable from the observed graph and proposed clustering.  
- Paper is technically strong with sound proofs using matrix perturbation tools (e.g., Davis–Kahan).  
- Experimental results show PFM bounds are informative on test data, while SBM bounds are too loose to be practically useful.  
- Honest presentation of limitations increases credibility.  
- Bounds depend inversely on the square of the spectral eigengap, which can make guarantees vacuous for small eigengaps.  
- Some technical sections (especially PFM model construction) are dense and would benefit from more intuition for key steps.  
- Highly original: reframes block-model theory from recovery to validation and is the first formalization of this model-free validation idea for SBM-style clustering.  
- Cleverly repurposes existing model-based recovery proofs to derive model-free guarantees and can tighten constants in prior work.  
- Significant potential impact: addresses unverifiable generative assumptions and offers a practical tool for cluster validation.  
- Provides a solid foundation for future work (tighter SBM bounds, extensions to DC-SBM, model selection implications).  
- Main drawback: practical utility hinges on improving bound tightness, especially for SBM, to be informative across more real-world datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — originality, significance, and quality outweigh minor weaknesses.
