- Proposes a generic, model-free framework for graph clustering with a correctness theorem that does not assume SBM/PFM.
- Paper is well-organized with clear background, notation, and well-presented mathematical derivations.
- Theorem 1 is a significant contribution with clearly explained assumptions and proof.
- Provides concrete instantiations for PFM and SBM; Theorems 4 and 9 are well-explained and easy to follow.
- Key strength: enables clustering without assuming a specific generative model, increasing applicability.
- Key strength: establishes a clear connection between model-free guarantees and model-based recovery results.
- Experimental evaluation is well-presented, with clear data generation description and comparisons demonstrating effectiveness.
- Potential weakness: bounds in Theorems 4 and 9 may not be tight and could be improved.
- Potential weakness: reviewer notes parts of the paper assume data is sampled from a specific model, which may be unrealistic.
