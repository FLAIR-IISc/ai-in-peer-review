- Introduces a novel distribution-free theoretical framework for performance guarantees in graph clustering.  
- Provides stability guarantees showing a clustering that fits well implies any other good fit is structurally close, bridging model-free and model-based analyses.  
- Theoretical contributions for both the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and SBM are interesting and appear technically sound.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, effectively communicating complex arguments.  
- Empirical illustrations are helpful in showing how the derived bounds behave in practice.  
- The framework's "good fit" definition is restrictive, relying on a proxy-model Laplacian approximation that may not match other clustering objectives.  
- Main PFM result uses a non-standard degree-weighted misclassification error whose naturalness is debatable and seems driven by proof techniques.  
- Practical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing for theoretical conditions to hold, while classical algorithms can often recover structure without it.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should more explicitly discuss the practical implications and limitations of their framework.  
- Minor stylistic point: using "hats" on observed quantities is unconventional and potentially confusing.
