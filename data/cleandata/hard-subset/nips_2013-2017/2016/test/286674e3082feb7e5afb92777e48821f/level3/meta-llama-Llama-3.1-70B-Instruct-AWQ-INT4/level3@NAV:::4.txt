This paper proposes a framework for model-free clustering stability guarantees, which is a valuable contribution to both theory and practice. The authors provide a generic theorem that can be instantiated for different models, such as the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM). They also show that model-free and model-based results are intimately connected.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the proposed framework is general and can be applied to different models, making it a useful tool for clustering analysis. Secondly, the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their ideas, making it easy to follow their reasoning. Thirdly, they provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement. Firstly, the paper treats PFM and SBM differently, which may not be justified given that PFM includes DC-SBM/SBM. The authors should provide a clear explanation for this difference in treatment. Secondly, the estimator choices for PFM and SBM are inconsistent, and the authors should justify their choices. Thirdly, the paper assumes that the number of clusters is known, which may not always be the case in practice. The authors should consider extending their framework to handle unknown numbers of clusters.

Additionally, there are some minor issues with the paper. For example, the definition of <M', M>_F in Proposition 10 is unclear, and the authors should clarify this. Assumption 5 is also ambiguous, and the authors should specify whether it is ||Ĺ^2 - L^2||_F or ||Ĺ - L||_F^2. Finally, the paper could benefit from more comparisons with related work, such as the model-free stability results of Kumar & Kannan (2010) and Balcan et al. (2012/2013).

In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their ideas, and their approach is original and valuable. However, the paper could benefit from more comparisons with related work and a clearer explanation of the differences in treatment between PFM and SBM.
