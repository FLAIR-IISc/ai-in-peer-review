- Proposes a model-free framework for clustering stability guarantees with a generic theorem instantiable for SBM and PFM.  
- Core idea: any clustering that fits the data well must be a small perturbation of another good-fitting clustering.  
- Paper is well-organized and explains the framework and main theorems clearly.  
- Technical proofs are delegated to the supplement, improving readability.  
- Provides experimental results that demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: model-free approach increases flexibility versus model-specific guarantees.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive notation and well-organized, understandable proofs.  
- Limitation: framework requires the number of clusters to be fixed.  
- Limitation: assumes clusters are well-separated, which may not hold in practice.  
- Limitation: relies on weighted Misclassification Error (wME) distance, which may not suit all settings.  
- Related-work coverage is good but lacks deeper comparison to other model-free approaches.  
- Experiments are limited; more models, datasets, and algorithms are needed to show robustness.  
- Overall: significant contribution with notable limitations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Pro-accept arguments: model-free contribution, clear exposition, and solid proofs.  
- Contra-accept arguments: fixed-k and separation assumptions, limited experiments, and insufficient contextual comparison to prior work.
