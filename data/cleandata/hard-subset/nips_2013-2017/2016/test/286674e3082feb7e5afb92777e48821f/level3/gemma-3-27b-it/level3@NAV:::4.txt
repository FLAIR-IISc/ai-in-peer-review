## Review of "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results"

This paper presents a novel framework for obtaining clustering guarantees without assuming data originates from a specific model like the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) or Preference Frame Model (PFM). The core idea revolves around establishing stability: if a clustering fits the data well, any other clustering that also fits well must be a small perturbation of the original. The authors instantiate this framework for both PFM and SBM, deriving recovery results and demonstrating connections between model-free and model-based approaches. 

The contribution of providing model-free guarantees for clustering is significant, addressing a key limitation of existing work heavily reliant on model assumptions. The attempt to connect and strengthen existing model-based results using this framework is also commendable. The framework's reliance on computable quantities is a practical strength.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The treatment of PFM and SBM feels inconsistent. While the authors acknowledge PFM encompasses DC-SBM/SBM, employing different estimators (Laplacian for PFM vs. adjacency for SBM) and distance metrics (weighted ME vs. ME) without clear justification weakens the comparison. The absence of results for DC-SBM, given its inclusion within the PFM framework, is also a notable omission. The definition of <M', M>_F in Proposition 10 requires clarification – presumably it’s the Frobenius inner product. Furthermore, Assumption 5 regarding squaring Laplacians lacks intuitive explanation. 

The paper’s practical relevance is questionable, as simulations reveal the conditions for stability are extremely restrictive, particularly for the SBM. The claim regarding PFM sensitivity based on a large eigengap seems unsupported and even contradictory given observations about smoothing via powering the adjacency matrix. While the framework is theoretically interesting, the simulations suggest limited applicability to real-world datasets. 

Several minor issues exist: Figure 1’s caption is unclear, axes labels are missing, and there are inconsistencies in notation across sections (e.g., the usage of B, C, Y). Section 4.4 feels misplaced and would be better integrated into the related work. Additionally, the paper omits relevant related work on model-free stability (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and a clearer distinction between different notions of stability (perturbation, approximation, center-perturbation) is needed. Finally, the framework’s inability to handle cluster splits/merges (K vs. K+1) limits its applicability to scenarios where clustering solutions may differ in number of clusters.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel framework for model-free clustering guarantees.
*   Connects model-free and model-based recovery results.
*   Focus on computable quantities.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM.
*   Restrictive simulation results undermining practical relevance.
*   Omissions in related work.
*   Theoretical framework limited by inability to handle cluster splits/merges.



**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents an interesting theoretical contribution, but its limited practical applicability and several technical weaknesses necessitate a **reject** recommendation at this time. Addressing the inconsistencies, expanding the simulations, and clarifying the theoretical assumptions would significantly improve the work.
