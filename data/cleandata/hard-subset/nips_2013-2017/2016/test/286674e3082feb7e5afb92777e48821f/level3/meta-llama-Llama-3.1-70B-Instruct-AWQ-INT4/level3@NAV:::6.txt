I have reviewed the paper "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model-free results" submitted to NIPS 2016. The paper proposes a framework to provide theoretical guarantees for the results of model-based clustering algorithms without making any assumption about the data generating process. The authors claim to have developed a model-free theory for graph clustering, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, after carefully reading the paper, I have some concerns regarding the novelty, rigor, and presentation of the work.

Firstly, the paper's main contribution, the "Generic Theorem," seems to be a vague statement that lacks precision. The theorem states that if a clustering C fits the data G well, then any other clustering C' that also fits G well will be close to C. However, the notion of "close" is not clearly defined, and the theorem's statement is not rigorous.

Secondly, the paper relies heavily on informal propositions and assumptions, such as Proposition 3, which assumes "standard recovery conditions" without specifying what these conditions are. This lack of rigor makes it challenging to evaluate the validity of the results.

Thirdly, the paper's presentation is not clear, and the authors make unsubstantiated claims throughout the text. For example, the authors claim that their framework ties in with the existing model-based framework, but they do not provide sufficient evidence to support this claim.

Lastly, the paper's experimental evaluation is limited, and the results are not convincing. The authors only present a few examples, and the evaluation metrics are not clearly defined.

In conclusion, while the paper's idea is interesting, the execution falls short of expectations. The lack of rigor, precision, and clarity in the presentation makes it challenging to evaluate the validity of the results. I would recommend that the authors revise the paper to address these concerns before resubmitting it.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper's idea is interesting and has the potential to contribute to the field of graph clustering.
