- Introduces a novel model-free graph clustering framework that provides guarantees without assuming SBM/PFM generative models.
- Core idea: any clustering that fits the data well implies all equally well-fitting clusterings are close to it.
- Theoretical results are technically sound, interesting, and potentially impactful.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, though moving details to the supplement reduces accessibility.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and effectively illustrates the theoretical findings.
- Authors thoroughly explore implications, including connections to model-based recovery theorems and cluster stability.
- Stylistic concern: using hats (e.g., Ĥ) for observed quantities is unconventional and distracting.
- Main PFM result relies on a non-standard weighted Misclassification Error (wME) whose intuitive justification and consequences are unclear.
- The proxy-model approach (reconstructing PFM/SBM from a clustering) emphasizes Laplacian approximation and constrains what counts as a “good” clustering.
- Framework may miss meaningful clusterings that don’t align with standard block models, limiting practical applicability.
- Real-world graphs often require smoothing for recovery, effectively forcing data to fit framework assumptions.
- Empirical results show SBM bounds are often not satisfiable, indicating a gap between theory and practice for SBM.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, rigorous theory, significance, connections to existing work, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted applicability from proxy-model, questionable wME metric, overly restrictive SBM results.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept with minor revisions to justify wME, discuss proxy-model limitations, and address smoothing/real-world applicability.
