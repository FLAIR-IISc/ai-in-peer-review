This paper proposes a novel framework for obtaining model-free guarantees for graph clustering, specifically for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM). The authors provide a generic theorem that relates the goodness of fit of a model to the distance between two clusterings, and instantiate this theorem for SBM and PFM. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a model-free template for clustering stability guarantees. The authors argue that providing model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering is important and valuable for both theory and practice. They demonstrate the usefulness of their framework by deriving exact recovery results for SBM and PFM.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas. The writing is clear, and the notation is well-defined. The authors provide a good balance between theoretical and experimental results.

One of the strengths of the paper is the development of a generic theorem that relates the goodness of fit of a model to the distance between two clusterings. This theorem provides a general framework for obtaining model-free guarantees for graph clustering. The authors also provide a clear and concise presentation of their instantiation of this theorem for SBM and PFM.

However, there are some issues with the paper that need to be addressed. One of the main concerns is the inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM. The authors provide different estimators and metrics for PFM and SBM, which needs justification and comparison. Additionally, the results for DC-SBM are missing, which is a significant omission given that PFM includes DC-SBM/SBM.

Another issue is the definition of the Frobenius inner product in Proposition 10, which needs clarification. Additionally, Assumption 5 is ambiguous and needs clarification. The authors also need to explain the intuition behind squaring Laplacians.

The paper also raises some questions about the generality of the framework. The authors need to show how the template would apply to other settings where spectral clustering works, such as hierarchical clustering. Additionally, the framework requires C and C' to have the same number of clusters, which means it cannot handle splits/merges (K vs K+1) where clusterings are still “close.”

The related work section is also incomplete, omitting several model-free stability results. The authors need to clarify how this paper's notion of stability relates to perturbation/approximation/center-perturbation stability.

The simulations for PFM tolerate only tiny perturbations, which raises questions about the practical relevance of the results. The claim that large eigengap makes PFM(G,C) sensitive is also unqualified and contradictory to the smoothing-by-powering-A observation. Additionally, the SBM conditions appear very hard to satisfy, which undermines the practical relevance of the results.

Finally, there are some minor issues with the paper, such as the unclear caption for Figure 1, the mismatch between theory and experiments, and the missing labels for the axes in Figure 1.

In conclusion, the paper provides a novel framework for obtaining model-free guarantees for graph clustering, specifically for SBM and PFM. However, there are some issues with the paper that need to be addressed, including the inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM, the missing results for DC-SBM, and the incomplete related work section. The authors also need to clarify the definition of the Frobenius inner product and explain the intuition behind squaring Laplacians. Additionally, the simulations for PFM and SBM need to be improved to demonstrate the practical relevance of the results.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a novel framework for obtaining model-free guarantees for graph clustering.
* The authors demonstrate the usefulness of their framework by deriving exact recovery results for SBM and PFM.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM needs justification and comparison.
* The missing results for DC-SBM are a significant omission.
* The related work section is incomplete, omitting several model-free stability results.
* The simulations for PFM and SBM need to be improved to demonstrate the practical relevance of the results.
