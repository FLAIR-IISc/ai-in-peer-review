- Proposes a model-free framework for clustering stability with correctness guarantees based on data fit rather than assumed generative models.  
- Significant novelty: bridges theory and practice by providing model-free guarantees that can leverage and strengthen model-based results.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous definitions of goodness-of-fit and distance metrics, and sensible use of perturbation theory (Davis–Kahan).  
- Generality: presents a “Generic Theorem” template adaptable to different models and estimators.  
- Computability: emphasizes that the quantities used in guarantees are computationally accessible.  
- Inconsistent treatment of PFM vs. SBM: different estimators (Laplacian vs. adjacency) and distance metrics (weighted ME vs. ME) are not justified or compared.  
- Omission of DC-SBM results is a significant gap given its relation to both SBM and PFM.  
- Ambiguities needing clarification: notation for Frobenius inner product in Proposition 10 and Assumption 5’s Laplacian-squared term and its intuition.  
- Limited generalizability: framework requires same number of clusters K in both clusterings, excluding splits/merges.  
- Related work is incomplete: misses relevant model-free stability literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al.) and lacks comparison to other stability notions.  
- Simulation concerns: PFM appears sensitive only to extremely small perturbations, questioning practical robustness; SBM conditions seem hard to satisfy.  
- Contradiction in PFM claims: large eigengap claimed to increase sensitivity conflicts with common smoothing approaches (power iteration) that improve stability.  
- Figure and experiment clarity issues: unclear caption for Figure 1, missing axis labels, and ambiguous statement about “δ is always greater than δ0.”  
- Theory–experiment mismatch: theoretical guarantees use weighted ME for PFM while experiments perturb using ME without justification.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation across sections and incorrect wording about independence of A_{ij} (should be conditional on cluster assignments).  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: novel and theoretically sound contribution but practical relevance and methodological inconsistencies justify rejection with request for major revisions.
