Summary
This paper proposes a model-free template for certifying the stability (uniqueness up to small perturbations) of a given clustering C of a graph G. The framework: fit a model M(G, C) from a model family (PFM, SBM) to the observed graph; if the fit is good (a computable closeness measure between M(G, C) and G is small), then any other clustering C′ that also fits well must be close to C (in ME or weighted-ME). Instantiations are given for PFM (via a Laplacian-based estimator) and for SBM (via MLE on the adjacency), leading to explicit bounds δ ≤ δ0 that imply stability of C. The paper also shows how to “turn around” known model-based recovery results (e.g., Rohe–Chatterjee–Yu 2011; Qin–Rohe 2013; Wan–Meilă 2016) to yield model-free guarantees, and presents experiments on PFM-like data, a perturbed PFM, an LFR benchmark, and Political Blogs. Empirically, the PFM bounds certify stability only for tiny perturbations, and the SBM bounds are largely vacuous.

Relation to prior work: The paper connects to spectral recovery guarantees for block models, and to notions of clustering stability and resilience. However, several relevant model-free stability literatures (center perturbation stability, approximation stability, perturbation resilience) are not fully discussed.

Quality
Strengths
- Clear, general template with computable, data-dependent quantities (ε, eigengaps, δ, δ0), and careful, elementary proofs (Davis–Kahan + subspace geometry + local equivalence).
- Instantiations for both PFM and SBM, and a useful “bridge” showing how to reuse model-based analyses to certify model-free stability.
- Exact recovery-style corollaries are clean and interpretable when conditions hold.

Weaknesses and questions
1) Why different treatments for PFM vs. SBM? Since SBM ⊂ DC-SBM ⊂ PFM, it is unclear why the paper uses the Laplacian estimator and weighted-ME metric for PFM, but the adjacency MLE and unweighted ME for SBM. Please justify:
- Estimators: Could both be stated with a unified spectral object (e.g., normalized Laplacian or a regularized variant) to enable apples-to-apples bounds?
- Metrics: Theorem 4 uses weighted ME (wME), Theorem 9 uses ME. Provide a comparison or unify the metric (e.g., report both), and explain when wME is preferable.
- DC-SBM: Results are missing despite being the intermediate and practically important model. Please include a DC-SBM instantiation (e.g., via regularized Laplacian as in Qin–Rohe 2013) and compare bounds.

2) Clarify Proposition 10: Define ⟨M̂, M⟩F explicitly as the Frobenius inner product trace(M̂T M) and state the dimensions of M, M̂ to avoid ambiguity.

3) Assumption 5 ambiguity: As written, ||L̂2 − L2||F ≤ ε could be read either as squared Laplacians difference or squared norm of Laplacian difference. Please clarify:
- If you mean ||L̂2 − L2||F, explain the intuition (squaring corresponds to two-step diffusion/random walks; RCY 2011 analyzes L2 for concentration and spectral separation).
- If you meant ||L̂ − L||2F, say so and adjust constants accordingly.

4) Extensibility beyond block models: The template should apply wherever spectral clustering succeeds (e.g., planted hierarchical models, manifold or similarity graphs analyzed via Ng–Jordan–Weiss 2002; Rohe–Chatterjee–Yu 2011; Balakrishnan et al. 2011). Please illustrate how to instantiate M(G, C), the goodness-of-fit, and the stability bound in one such setting (e.g., hierarchical/similarity clustering with Laplacian eigenmaps). A section that walks through an example would strengthen the generality claim.

5) Same-K requirement: The framework enforces K for both C and C′, so it cannot capture “nearby” clusterings with splits/merges (K vs. K+1). Please discuss whether your local-equivalence approach (Meilă 2012) can be adapted to allow splits/merges, or give a negative result.

6) Relation to model-free stability notions: The discussion omits key lines of work.
- Center-perturbation and approximation stability: Kumar & Kannan (FOCS’10), Balcan et al. (SODA’12, JACM’13), Bilu & Linial (2010), Awasthi (survey).
- Please clarify how your stability notion (two model fits both close to G imply small ME/wME) compares to these notions (e.g., α-approximation stability, perturbation resilience, center stability) and whether implications in either direction can be stated.

7) Empirics and practical usefulness:
- PFM experiments certify stability only for tiny perturbations. The text states that a large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) sensitive; this needs qualification. Your ε = ||L̂ − L|| (spectral norm) scales like gap × sin θ by Davis–Kahan, so for a fixed subspace deviation θ (induced by perturbing C), ε indeed grows with the eigengap—explaining the tightness of δ. Please add this explanation and quantify.
- Smoothing-by-powering-A reduces ε in practice; reconcile this with the above and discuss as a recommended pre-processing step, with guidance on power choice.
- SBM conditions are “always above 1” in the plots and never certify stability, even for LFR. This undermines practical relevance; can constants be improved, or can adjacency be regularized (Qin–Rohe 2013) to yield informative δ?

8) Figure 1 issues:
- Caption: It says “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” but the figure doesn’t clearly show SBM. Please state explicitly which subplots correspond to SBM and which are omitted, and why.
- Axes: Add labels and units (x: perturbation level; y: δ, δ0, and indicate wME vs ME).
- Theory/experiment metric mismatch: PFM theory is in wME, but perturbations are in ME. Please either perturb in wME or report both to match the theorem.

9) Minor correctness and notation:
- Line 44: In block models, Aij for i > j are independent given cluster assignments (and node propensities for DC-SBM). Please correct wording.
- Notation varies between sections (B, C, Y, YZ). Consider a consistent symbol table (e.g., Y⊥, CD for cluster-degree matrix, λL vs λB) and avoid unnecessary YZ notation in SBM estimator.

10) Related work placement: Section 4.4 reads like related work; consider moving it as a subsection of Related Work and tightening.

Originality
- The “model-free from model-based” blueprint is a neat and original angle for graph clustering, with clean, computable conditions. The PFM instantiation and the subspace-geometry proof path are technically simple and elegant.

Clarity
- Generally well written, with helpful propositions. The issues above (definition ambiguities, metric mismatch, figure clarity, notation) should be addressed for reproducibility.

Significance
- If constants can be improved and DC-SBM included, this framework could become a practical validation tool. As-is, the SBM bounds are not informative in the experiments, which limits immediate impact.

Pros
- General, computable stability template; clear theory.
- Bridges model-based recovery to model-free validation.
- Exact recovery-style statements; simple proofs.

Cons
- Inconsistent treatment across models; missing DC-SBM.
- Ambiguities (Prop. 10, Assump. 5); metric mismatch.
- Empirical bounds mostly vacuous for SBM; PFM stability only for tiny perturbations.
- Missing connections to broader stability literature.

Recommendation
- Weak accept if the authors (i) unify metrics/estimators and include DC-SBM; (ii) clarify ambiguities (Prop. 10, Assump. 5); (iii) fix figures and experimental mismatch; and (iv) expand related work and extensions. Otherwise, borderline: the theoretical template is promising, but practical relevance and presentation need strengthening.
