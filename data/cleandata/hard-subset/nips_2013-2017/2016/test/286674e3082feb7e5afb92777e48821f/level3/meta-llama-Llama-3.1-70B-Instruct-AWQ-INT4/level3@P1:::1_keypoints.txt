- Proposes a novel graph-clustering framework that gives distribution-free theoretical guarantees without assuming a generative model.  
- Core idea: any clustering that fits the observed graph as well as a given clustering must be close to that clustering.  
- Theoretical contributions are novel and important for the field.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, making the material accessible.  
- Minor stylistic issues (e.g., use of "hats" on observed quantities) could be improved.  
- Some technical content is relegated to the supplement, which may impede reader comprehension.  
- Empirical results are well-commented and illustrate the framework’s effectiveness.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing for structure recovery, unlike classical algorithms.  
- Major limitation 1: main PFM result depends on a non-standard distance between clusterings whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Major limitation 2: use of a proxy-model approach (focused on normalized Laplacian approximation) strongly constrains what counts as a “good” clustering and may limit real-world applicability.  
- Related work review is thorough but lacks a detailed comparative analysis against existing methods highlighting pros/cons.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution worthy of acceptance, but authors should address stylistic issues, empirical limitations, and provide clearer comparisons to prior methods.
