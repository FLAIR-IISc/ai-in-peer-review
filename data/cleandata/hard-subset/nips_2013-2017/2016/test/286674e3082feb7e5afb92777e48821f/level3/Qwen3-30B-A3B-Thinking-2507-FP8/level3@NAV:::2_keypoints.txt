- Proposes a novel model-free graph clustering framework that yields theoretical guarantees without assuming SBM/PFM data-generating models.  
- Proves a locally convex-like property: if two clusterings fit the data well, they must be close in weighted misclassification distance.  
- Establishes equivalence between multiple clustering distance measures and shows how model-free guarantees follow from model-based recovery results.  
- Bridges model-based and model-free clustering by using model-based results to strengthen model-free guarantees.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical results for PFM and SBM, using relatively simple extensions of known techniques (e.g., Davis–Kahan bounds).  
- Delivers clear experimental validation on synthetic and real-world networks, demonstrating cluster stability validation without model assumptions.  
- Excellent clarity and organization: consistent notation, well-structured proofs, and precise exposition.  
- Notes limitations: SBM guarantees are more restrictive than PFM, and the framework lacks formal clusterability-detection results.  
- Theoretical novelty is somewhat incremental relative to recent prior work (e.g., Peng et al. 2015, Chao et al. 2015).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical foundation and experiments, minor novelty limits, recommended for acceptance.
