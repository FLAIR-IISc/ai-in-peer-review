- Presents a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives "correctness" guarantees based on data statistics.  
- Proposes clustering and cluster-similarity testing algorithms that are mostly modifications of existing methods, with novelty mainly in added constraints.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents main ideas and background on graphs, clusterings, and block models.  
- Generic Theorem 1 is well-explained and instantiated for PFM and SBM.  
- Experimental evaluation is clear, concise, and summarized effectively.  
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; results are reproducible for experts.  
- Original contribution: providing model-free guarantees for clustering and adapting existing model-based algorithms to this framework.  
- Significance: framework could impact clustering analysis and community detection by emphasizing stability and robustness.  
- Weak motivation and unclear presentation of algorithms presented as a list without sufficient purpose or connection.  
- Lacks detailed intuition behind algorithms and explanations of how they relate to one another.  
- Needs clearer context and comparison to prior work to better articulate advances over existing contributions.  
- Overall positive paper with significant potential, but would benefit from more intuition, motivation, and positioning relative to prior art.
