- Proposes a model-free stability framework: if a clustering and its block model fit the data well, any other good-fit clustering must be close to the original.
- Novelty/strength: elegant, important idea of turning model-based recovery theorems into model-free guarantees for real-world networks.
- Inconsistent treatment of PFM vs. SBM: different estimators (Laplacian vs. adjacency) and distance metrics (weighted ME vs. ME) used without justification.
- Presentation lacks rigor; several ambiguous definitions (e.g., inner product in Prop.10, norm in Assumption 5).
- Experimental validation is flawed: PFM theory targets weighted misclassification error but experiments use unweighted ME, invalidating PFM results.
- Related work is incomplete: omits key model-free stability literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2013) and doesn't relate to perturbation/approximation stability concepts.
- Practical significance doubtful: stability guarantees hold only for very small perturbations and SBM conditions are hard to satisfy, suggesting bounds may be too loose.
- Overall verdict: promising and original direction but requires major revision to fix technical inconsistencies, clarify definitions, expand literature discussion, and reconcile theory with experiments.
