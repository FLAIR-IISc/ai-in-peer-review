- Presents a novel framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming data from an SBM or its extensions.  
- Shows that any two clusterings that both fit the observed graph well (by a defined goodness-of-fit) must be close to each other.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM and connects to existing model-based recovery theorems.  
- Significant contribution: removes heavy reliance on generative model assumptions, broadening theoretical applicability to real-world networks.  
- Core idea—focusing on goodness-of-fit and clustering stability—is well-motivated and builds on substantial prior work.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and technically detailed, with proofs in the supplement.  
- Experiments provide illustrative examples showing applicability of the theory.  
- Stylistic issue: use of “hats” (e.g., Ĥ) for observed quantities feels arbitrary and jarring.  
- Technical concern: PFM main result uses a weighted Misclassification Error (wME) distance that may feel less natural than standard misclassification error.  
- Proxy-model limitation: framework reconstructs a model from a clustering, which biases guarantees toward clusterings well-approximated by the chosen models.  
- Focus on approximating the normalized Laplacian may exclude valid clusterings that don’t align with that representation.  
- Practical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing (e.g., matrix powers) before meaningful guarantees apply, which may distort original structure.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, strong connections to prior work, potential impact, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modeling restrictions from the proxy-model approach, practical need for smoothing, and the debatable choice of wME.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking authors to better discuss proxy-model limitations, justify the wME choice, and consider alternative distance metrics.
