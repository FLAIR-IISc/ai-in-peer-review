- Proposes a novel distribution-free framework for graph clustering that requires no generative model.  
- Core idea: any clustering that fits the graph equally well must be close to a truly good clustering.  
- Theoretical contributions are novel, tying distribution-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery results.  
- Framework can reproduce model-free guarantees for SBM and PFM and relates closely to model-based theorems.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with thorough empirical illustrations and analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, using simulated and real datasets and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Main limitation: relies on a non-standard distance between clusterings whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Analysis uses a proxy-model approach that strongly constrains what counts as a “good” clustering.  
- Some real-world graphs require smoothing for structure recovery under this framework, unlike classical algorithms.  
- Stylistic issue: use of “hats” on observed quantities (e.g., Ĥ) is questionable and should be addressed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; fix stylistic notation and clarify limitations.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
