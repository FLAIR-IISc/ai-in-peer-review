- Introduces a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives recovery guarantees without assuming data from a specific block model.  
- Core idea: any clustering that fits the data well under a model must be a small perturbation of the original clustering.  
- Framework instantiated for the planted partition model (PFM) and stochastic block model (SBM), with connections to existing model-based results.  
- Presentation is clear, mathematically rigorous, with consistent notation and well-defined algorithms/theorems.  
- Proofs appear sound and authors show strong command of relevant spectral clustering and SBM literature.  
- Primary weakness: weak motivation—paper does not convincingly explain why model-free guarantees are crucial in practice.  
- Paper reads as a set of algorithms/theorems without a cohesive, compelling narrative.  
- Contribution is largely incremental: adapts existing algorithms and adds constraints, with novelty concentrated in the final constraints.  
- Impact of the new constraints is not fully explored beyond theoretical bounds.  
- Empirical validation is limited and does not clearly demonstrate practical benefits of the framework.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, clarity, and potential to inspire future extensions of model-based guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of strong motivation, limited novelty, and insufficient empirical support.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline; weak accept conditional on strengthening the introduction and clarifying practical significance.
