- Proposes a "model-free" graph clustering framework that checks data fit to a model via computable statistics and proves that any other clustering fitting the graph must be close to the reference clustering, instantiated for PFM and SBM with Theorems 4 and 9.  
- Well-organized background on block models and spectral clustering.  
- Experiments demonstrate computability of bounds (δ, δ₀) on synthetic and real data and show PFM bounds apply better than SBM for non-model data.  
- The "model-free guarantees" claim is misleading because the framework still implicitly assumes the data is close to a model via recovery conditions (Prop. 3, Assumptions 2 and 4).  
- Theorems lack precision and rigor: Theorem 1 is vague and not mathematically precise.  
- Proposition 3 depends on unspecified "standard recovery conditions" and fails to cite or state required assumptions (e.g., eigengap bounds).  
- Theorem 4 is vacuous for SBM in experiments since the condition δ ≤ δ₀ never holds (δ > 1).  
- Proofs offer no novel insights and largely repackage existing perturbation theory (e.g., Prop. 7 cites [13] without new contributions).  
- The "model-free" label merely shifts model assumptions to a computable fit metric and is no less restrictive than model-based approaches.  
- Claimed strengthening of prior work ([20], Theorem 11) is actually weaker by a factor of 128.  
- The framework provides no new theoretical tools and yields weaker bounds than established model-based results (e.g., [18, 20]).  
- Originality is limited: using model fit for validation is not new and the paper fails to distinguish itself from prior stability and validation literature ([6, 9, 11, 14]).  
- Overall technical claims are unsubstantiated, significance is negligible, and the reviewer recommends rejection.
