- Presents a model-free framework that gives general concentration guarantees for community detection via spectral clustering, verifiable from data.  
- Strength: Moves beyond model-specific assumptions, broadening applicability of theoretical guarantees to real-world data.  
- Strength: Provides rigorous theoretical stability guarantees with proofs and instantiations for SBM and PFM.  
- Strength: Well-written and accessible, with clear motivations, assumptions, error definitions, and examples.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical relevance through experiments on real and simulated datasets (e.g., Political Blogs).  
- Strength: Establishes a foundation for future work, including extensions to non-spectral methods and tighter bounds.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on spectral clustering, which may be suboptimal or inefficient for sparse graphs.  
- Weakness: SBM bounds are weaker and harder to satisfy compared to PFM, indicating room for improvement.  
- Weakness: Lacks negative guarantees characterizing when clusterings are not stable.  
- For acceptance: Significant contribution addressing a core challenge in community detection with robust theory and experiments.  
- Against acceptance: Applicability limited by spectral-method dependence and unimpressive SBM guarantees.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address spectral reliance and improve SBM guarantees.
