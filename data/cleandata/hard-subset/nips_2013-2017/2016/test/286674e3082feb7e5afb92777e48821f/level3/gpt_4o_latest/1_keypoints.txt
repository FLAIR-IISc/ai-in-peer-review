- Introduces a novel, model-free framework for graph clustering that gives distribution-free theoretical guarantees.  
- Guarantees that any clustering fitting the graph well is close to other similarly fitting clusterings, without assuming SBM/PFM generative models.  
- Broadens applicability of clustering guarantees beyond model-based methods.  
- Theoretical results are well-supported with proofs and clear connections to model-based frameworks.  
- Computable quantities from data and use of spectral clustering as a baseline make results accessible and practical.  
- Experiments (Political Blogs, LFR benchmark, synthetic data) show practical utility but are limited in scope and diversity.  
- Writing is clear and self-contained, though some material is pushed to supplements.  
- Uses a non-standard distance (weighted misclassification error) for clustering comparisons, which may limit adoption.  
- Reliance on normalized Laplacian approximation and required smoothing restricts applicability to some real-world datasets.  
- Bounds for SBM are weaker/more informative for PFM, limiting theoretical usefulness in some scenarios.  
- Framework struggles to provide guarantees or validate stability on datasets that do not fit the assumed structure (e.g., LFR under SBM).  
- Minor notation issues (e.g., use of hats like \hat{L}) detract slightly but do not impede readability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify practical implications and limitations.
