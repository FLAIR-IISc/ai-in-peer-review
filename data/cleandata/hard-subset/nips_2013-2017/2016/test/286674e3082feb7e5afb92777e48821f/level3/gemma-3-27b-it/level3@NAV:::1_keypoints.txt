- Proposes a novel framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming a specific generative model (model-free approach).  
- Core claim: any clustering that fits the observed graph well implies all equally well-fitting clusterings are “close” to it.  
- Addresses an important limitation of prior work that relies on strong model assumptions (e.g., SBM, PFM).  
- Theoretical contributions are interesting and advance more robust clustering methodologies.  
- Paper is largely clear and well-written; many proofs are sensibly placed in the supplement.  
- Empirical evaluation is informative and demonstrates how to assess clustering stability in practice.  
- Notation choice: using “hats” for observed quantities (e.g., Ĥ) is stylistically questionable and needs clarification.  
- Major limitation: reliance on a non-standard weighted Misclassification Error for the PFM result, which may hinder comparability and acceptance.  
- Significant methodological constraint: the proxy-model approach reconstructs a model from a clustering and compares to the observed graph, restricting “good” clusterings to those approximable by the normalized Laplacian.  
- This modeling restriction may overlook valid clusterings not captured by the chosen proxy models, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Empirical drawbacks: some experiments required graph smoothing where classical algorithms worked without it.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and novel work worthy of acceptance pending minor stylistic fixes and a deeper discussion of the proxy-model limitations and distance-metric justification.
