This paper proposes a model-free template for certifying clustering stability using computable quantities. Given a graph G, a clustering C, and a fitted model M(G,C) from a chosen family (PFM or SBM), the authors prove that if M(G,C) is close to G, then any other clustering C′ whose fitted model is also close must be near C. Instantiations yield bounds for PFM (with Laplacian-based fits and weighted misclassification error, wME) and for SBM (adjacency-based fits and ME), and connect back to model-based recovery theorems. Experiments compute the stability certificates on synthetic PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, and Political Blogs. They generally find the PFM certificate tolerates very small perturbations, whereas SBM guarantees are rarely satisfied.

Quality: The theoretical template is sound and based on standard perturbation tools (Davis–Kahan plus a geometric equivalence of clustering distances). However, the treatment of PFM and SBM is inconsistent and under-explained: PFM uses Laplacian fits and wME whereas SBM uses adjacency fits and ME; please justify these choices, compare their induced bounds on the same datasets, and report DC-SBM results (PFM subsumes DC-SBM/SBM). Assumption 5 is ambiguous: is it ||Ĺ2−L2||F or ||Ĺ−L||2F? If the former, provide intuition for squaring Laplacians and discuss practicality. Clarify the definition of <M̂,M>F in Proposition 10 (Frobenius inner product). The framework requires C and C′ to have the same K; discuss whether the approach can handle near-splits/merges (K vs K+1). Experiments suggest limited practical usefulness: PFM certificates hold only for tiny perturbations; the statement that a large eigengap makes PFM(G,C) sensitive seems at odds with the stability improvement from smoothing by powering A—please reconcile. SBM conditions appear very stringent; comment on whether sharper analysis could yield informative bounds.

Clarity: The paper is generally readable but needs edits: Figure 1 axes lack labels; the caption is unclear about whether SBM is plotted and which subplot shows “for SBM, δ > δ0”; fix Line 44 (Aij, i>j independent given cluster assignments). Notation is inconsistent across sections (B,C,Y used differently for PFM vs SBM); adopt a unified notation (e.g., Y⊥, CD, λL/λB) and avoid unnecessary YZ in the SBM estimator. Section 4.4 reads like Related Work. Please add missing model-free stability literature and relate notions: Kumar & Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012/2013) on perturbation/approximation/center-perturbation stability.

Originality and significance: A computable, model-free certification template for graph clustering is valuable and novel in the block-model literature. Its impact would increase by (i) covering DC-SBM, (ii) aligning metrics across theory/experiments (PFM guarantees are in wME but experiments perturb with ME), and (iii) demonstrating applicability beyond block models to other spectral settings (e.g., hierarchical clustering; connections to Balakrishnan et al. 2011).

Pros:
- Clear, computable certification template; solid perturbation-based analysis.
- Bridges model-free and model-based guarantees; potentially broadly useful.

Cons:
- Inconsistent modeling choices and metrics; missing DC-SBM results.
- Experimental certificates often uninformative; theory/experiment mismatch (ME vs wME).
- Ambiguities (Assumption 5, Proposition 10), notation issues, missing related work, and figure shortcomings.

Overall: Promising framework but, in its current form, the guarantees’ practical relevance and presentation issues limit impact. I recommend rejection with encouragement to revise along the lines above.
