- Proposes a model-free framework for clustering graphs with theoretical stability guarantees, analyzed under SBM and PFM.  
- Validates cluster assignments using data-driven statistics instead of assuming a generative model.  
- Connects to and extends model-based recovery results, offering a novel perspective on model-free validation.

Strengths:
- Addresses dependence on model assumptions, expanding applicability to real-world datasets.  
- Provides rigorous, computable theoretical guarantees; introduces weighted misclassification error and spectral perturbation bounds.  
- Effectively ties results to prior work (e.g., spectral clustering), “turning around” model-based theorems for validation.  
- Experiments on synthetic and one real-world dataset (LFR, Political Blogs) show practical applicability and compare PFM vs SBM stability.

Weaknesses:
- Algorithmic novelty is limited; methods are mainly modifications of existing techniques with added constraints.  
- Manuscript is dense and could be better organized with clearer exposition and intuitive examples.  
- Empirical validation is narrow (mostly synthetic plus one real dataset); broader datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Presentation of algorithms as a list lacks clear motivation and a cohesive narrative linking them to theory.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and better articulate practical implications.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Significant theoretical contribution to model-free clustering, computable stability guarantees, and strong links to prior work.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited algorithmic novelty, dense presentation, and insufficient diverse empirical validation.

Summary:
- Advances model-free graph clustering with meaningful theory but needs clearer exposition and broader empirical support.
