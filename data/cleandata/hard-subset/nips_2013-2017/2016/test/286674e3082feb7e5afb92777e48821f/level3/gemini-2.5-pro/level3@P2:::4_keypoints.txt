- Proposes a novel model-free framework for graph clustering guarantees based on stability of a goodness-of-fit measure rather than assuming a data-generating model.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), deriving computable bounds on distance between good clusterings.  
- Connects the model-free framework to existing model-based recovery theorems, showing potential to strengthen prior results.  
- Original and significant conceptual advance that avoids strong sampling assumptions common in block model theory.  
- All key quantities in the theorems (goodness-of-fit ε, eigengap, cluster sizes) are computable from observed data and a candidate clustering.  
- Provides a clear technical approach using spectral perturbation theory (Davis–Kahan) and repurposes model-based results within a model-free context.  
- Treats PFM and SBM inconsistently (different estimators, goodness-of-fit metrics, and distance measures) without justification, making the framework feel ad-hoc.  
- Omits analysis of Degree-Corrected SBM, which lies between SBM and PFM and would help unify the treatment.  
- Experimental results suggest the derived bounds are extremely conservative for PFM and that SBM conditions are never met on tested datasets, questioning practical utility.  
- Theoretical PFM bounds are tested against unweighted ME perturbations despite being derived for weighted ME, creating a mismatch in experiments.  
- Fails to cite and compare with relevant model-free stability literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013), leaving the relation to prior stability notions unclear.  
- Framework is restricted to comparing clusterings with the same number of clusters K, limiting applicability to merges/splits and common clustering scenarios.  
- Recommends unifying PFM/SBM analysis or providing detailed justification for differences.  
- Requests experimental fixes: match theoretical metrics (weighted vs. unweighted ME) and report when SBM conditions are satisfied.  
- Asks for clarification of notation and statements (Frobenius inner product, whether ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F is intended, independence statement for A_ij) and better figure labeling.  
- Suggests extending discussion to other spectral clustering settings (e.g., hierarchical SBM) to clarify generality.  
- Advises strengthening and repositioning related work (move Section 4.4 into Related Work/Discussion) and harmonizing matrix notation across sections.  
- Overall evaluation: important and original idea but inconsistent execution, incomplete literature treatment, and weak experiments; borderline paper likely to be rejected unless practical applicability and consistency are substantially improved.
