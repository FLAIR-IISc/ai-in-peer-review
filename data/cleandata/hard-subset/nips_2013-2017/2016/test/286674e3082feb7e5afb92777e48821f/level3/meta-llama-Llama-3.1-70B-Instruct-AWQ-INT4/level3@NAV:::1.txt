This paper proposes a novel framework for graph clustering that provides distribution-free theoretical guarantees without assuming a generative model for the observed graph. The core idea is that if a clustering fits the graph well, any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it. The paper presents a generic theorem that instantiates this idea for various model classes, including the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM).

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The theoretical contributions are novel and interesting, and the empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of their approach, including the need for smoothing real-world graphs to recover structure under the proposed framework.

The paper's strengths include:

* Novel and interesting theoretical contributions that provide distribution-free guarantees for graph clustering.
* A clear and well-organized presentation that makes it easy to follow the authors' arguments.
* A thorough empirical evaluation that demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

However, the paper also has some limitations:

* The use of non-standard distance between clusterings (PFM) may limit the applicability and impact of the results.
* The analysis uses a proxy-model approach, which strongly constrains what counts as a "good" clustering.
* The results may not be directly comparable to classical algorithms that can recover ground truth without smoothing.
