- Proposes a generic framework for obtaining model-free guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific data-generating model.  
- Establishes relationships and equivalence between multiple clustering distance measures for network data.  
- Analyzes two network models—Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM)—and instantiates the generic theorem for each.  
- Proves a locally convex-like property: clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Derives model-specific stability results that bound clustering perturbation by model perturbation, useful for model-free and model-based guarantees.  
- Theoretical arguments are relatively simple extensions of known results rather than wholly new techniques.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and supports the theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, logically organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Results are novel in application to graph clustering but closely related to recent work and existing techniques.  
- Lacks formal results on potential applications (e.g., detecting clusterability) despite discussion of these topics.  
- Technically sound with well-supported claims via theory and experiments.  
- High originality in proposing a model-free guarantees framework and high significance for graph clustering and community detection.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
