This paper proposes a framework for graph clustering without assuming the data comes from a model. The authors aim to provide theoretical guarantees for the results of model-based clustering algorithms, focusing on the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM). While the paper's goals are ambitious, the execution falls short in several areas.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a "model-free" theory for graph clustering. However, the results presented are not convincing, and the theory lacks rigor. This makes it difficult to assess the validity of the results and the soundness of the theory.

In terms of originality, the paper's approach is not significantly different from previous contributions. The paper's results are also not particularly surprising, and the authors do not provide a clear explanation of how their work advances the state of the art.

The experimental evaluation is limited, and the results are not convincing. The authors only provide a few examples, and the evaluation is not systematic or comprehensive. The results are also not compared to existing methods, making it difficult to assess the paper's contributions.
