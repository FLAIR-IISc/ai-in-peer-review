- Introduces a novel "model-free" stability framework for community detection that derives guarantees from model fit to observed data rather than assuming a generative model.  
- Connects and translates existing model-based results into model-free guarantees, showing stability results can be "turned around."  
- Provides a general theoretical framework with concrete, computable guarantees applicable to popular models like SBM and PFM.  
- Proves results with mathematical rigor and tightens some existing bounds (e.g., improving error bounds in [20] by a factor of 128).  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written exposition with thoughtful motivation for error metrics and assumptions.  
- Experimental validation demonstrates the practical applicability of the framework.  
- Primary weakness: reliance on spectral algorithms that have non-tight guarantees and are problematic for very sparse graphs.  
- Lacks proposed alternative approaches for sparse settings, a significant gap given the prevalence of sparse networks.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance pending minor revisions to address sparse-graph limitations.
