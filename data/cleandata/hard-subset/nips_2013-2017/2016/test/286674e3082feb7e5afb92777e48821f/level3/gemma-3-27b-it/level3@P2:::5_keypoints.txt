- Proposes a novel framework giving theoretical guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Shows that if one clustering fits the data well, any other good-fit clustering must be close to it, enabling model-free guarantees.  
- Leverages existing model-based recovery theorems to derive model-free results and strengthen model-based statements.  
- Focuses on spectral clustering methods as the algorithmic backbone of the framework.  
- Theorems are clearly stated and proofs appear rigorous; relevant quantities (goodness-of-fit, clustering distance) are carefully defined.  
- Strong connection to prior work in spectral clustering, SBMs, degree-corrected SBMs, and the Preference Frame Model.  
- Contrasts with stability literature and emphasizes the model-based → model-free translation as a key insight.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic and real datasets and supports the theoretical claims, but is limited in scope.  
- Discussion candidly outlines limitations and suggests future research directions.  
- Major weakness: reliance on spectral algorithms and sensitivity to eigengap, limiting applicability for very sparse graphs.  
- Proposed bounds may not be the tightest compared to existing results (e.g., Peng et al., 2016).  
- Applicability is demonstrated mainly for SBM and PFM; broader model coverage or general conditions would strengthen the work.  
- Experiments need more comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art community detection methods and parameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: address spectral/eigengap limitations, explore potential tightening of bounds, and expand experimental comparisons.
