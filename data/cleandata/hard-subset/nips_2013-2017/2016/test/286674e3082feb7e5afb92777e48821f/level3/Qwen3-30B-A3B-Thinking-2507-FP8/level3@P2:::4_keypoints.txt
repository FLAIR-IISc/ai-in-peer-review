- Proposes a model-free framework that gives stability guarantees for graph clusterings by connecting model-based fit to proximity of clusterings.  
- Framework is computable: all theorem quantities can be calculated from data.  
- Successfully ties model-based and model-free results and can repurpose existing model-based recovery theorems.  
- Technical strengths: simpler, elementary proofs and improvements over prior bounds (e.g., factor 128 improvement for PFM).  
- Inconsistent treatment of PFM vs. SBM: different estimators (Laplacian vs. adjacency) and metrics (weighted ME vs. ME) without justification, undermining the unified- framework claim.  
- Omission of DC-SBM results despite DC-SBM being a PFM special case.  
- Experiments show PFM is extremely sensitive to small perturbations, contrary to claims about robustness from large eigengaps.  
- SBM conditions are overly restrictive in practice (e.g., cannot prove stability on LFR data), reducing practical relevance.  
- Theoretical/experimental mismatch: PFM theorems use weighted ME but experiments report ME, an inconsistency left unaddressed.  
- Related work section omits key model-free stability papers (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and doesn’t clarify connections.  
- Unclear extensibility: paper doesn’t address whether framework applies beyond block models or to other spectral clustering settings like hierarchical clustering.  
- Notational inconsistencies and ambiguous assumptions (e.g., inconsistent use of symbols and possible misuse of norms in Assumption 5).  
- Incorrect claim about handling splits/merges: theorems require same number of clusters, so K vs K+1 claims are unsupported.  
- Overall: conceptually valuable and technically sound, but practical limitations, inconsistencies, and restrictive SBM guarantees substantially reduce impact; reviewer recommends rejection.
