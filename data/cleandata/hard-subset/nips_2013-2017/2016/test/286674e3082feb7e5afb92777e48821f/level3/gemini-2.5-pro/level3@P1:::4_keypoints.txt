- Novel contribution: proposes a model-free framework showing that any clustering that fits the observed graph well must be close to a given clustering, shifting validation to post-hoc goodness-of-fit.  
- Conceptual value: avoids unverifiable data-generating assumptions and gives guarantees based on computable statistics like fit error and spectral gaps.  
- Clever technique: "turning around" model-based recovery theorems to derive model-free stability guarantees.  
- Practical advantage: derived bounds are explicit, free of unknown constants, and depend only on observable quantities (ε, spectral gaps).  

- Inconsistent treatment of PFM vs SBM: different estimators used (spectral for PFM, MLE for SBM) without clear justification despite SBM being a PFM subclass.  
- Metric inconsistency: PFM results use weighted misclassification error while SBM uses standard ME, but experiments test PFM with unweighted perturbations.  
- Missing DC-SBM: degree-corrected SBM is noted but no specific results are provided, leaving a key link unexplored.  

- Experimental concerns: PFM stability holds only for very tiny perturbations, raising doubts about bound tightness and practical relevance.  
- Contradiction in PFM interpretation: authors attribute instability to large eigengap, yet matrix smoothing (powering) appears to induce stability, which is not adequately explained.  
- SBM experiments fail to meet Theorem 9 conditions even on SBM-like benchmarks (LFR), questioning applicability to real data.  

- Related work omissions: fails to cite/compare to Kumar & Kannan (perturbation resilience) and Balcan et al. (stability notions), and does not position its notion relative to established stability definitions.  
- Limited scope: framework only compares clusterings with the same K, so it cannot validate merges/splits or solutions with different numbers of clusters.  

- Notational and presentation issues: undefined notation (e.g., Frobenius inner product <M̂, M>F), inconsistent symbols (Y, B, C used differently across sections), and unnecessary terms (YZ in SBM estimator).  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5: unclear whether ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F means difference of squared matrices or squared norm of the difference, and rationale for squaring Laplacians is not given.  
- Clarity of figures and results: Figure 1 is hard to read, axes unlabeled, and SBM results appear unplotted despite claims—caption and plots need correction.  
- Minor technical wording: statement that Â_ij are sampled independently should be qualified as conditional on cluster assignments.  
- Organization suggestion: Section 4.4 reads like related-work discussion and may be better placed under Related Work.  

- Overall verdict: promising and important idea but undermined by inconsistent theory/experiments, missing comparisons to prior stability work, limited applicability, and presentation/notation flaws that must be addressed.
