- Introduces a model-free framework for graph clustering that shows any other clustering fitting the data equally well must be “close” to the original, providing clustering stability guarantees.  
- Connects different distance measures and provides model-free guarantees for algorithms on Stochastic Block Models (SBM) and Preference Frame Models (PFM).  
- Situates work within and builds on recent model-based clustering literature, shifting focus from model parameters to data statistics.  
- Theorems (e.g., 4 and 9) are technically sound with rigorous proofs and careful use of perturbation bounds (Sinus Theorem).  
- Paper is clearly written, with consistent notation, precise definitions, and logical presentation of ideas.  
- The framework is original, reframing model-based recovery theorems to obtain new model-free guarantees.  
- Results are significant: broaden applicability of clustering algorithms by avoiding strict model assumptions and suggest directions for cluster validation and resilience.  
- Experimental validation supports theoretical claims by demonstrating clustering stability under perturbations, though not exhaustive.  
- Limited exploration of practical applications; claims about detecting clusterability are not demonstrated concretely.  
- SBM guarantees are restrictive and often uninformative in experiments, suggesting the approach is more effective for flexible models like PFM.  
- Results depend heavily on eigengap assumptions of the Laplacian, which may not hold in many practical settings.  
- Comparison to existing stability literature (e.g., Bilu & Linial, Meilă) is present but not sufficiently detailed regarding trade-offs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, well-written, impactful potential, and supported by experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical exploration, restrictive SBM bounds, reliance on eigengap, and relatively superficial comparison to prior stability work.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to expand real-world experiments, tighten SBM bounds, and relax eigengap assumptions in future work.
