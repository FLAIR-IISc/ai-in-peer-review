- Proposes a model-free stability framework for graph clustering aiming to guarantee that any two good-fit clusterings are “close.”
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM to connect model-free results with model-based recovery guarantees.
- Motivation to move beyond strict block-model assumptions is valuable and relevant.
- Core idea lacks novelty and largely restates existing concepts rather than offering a substantive advance.
- Theorem 1 is vague; the notion of “close” is ill-defined, preventing concrete application.
- Subsequent propositions (e.g., Proposition 3) depend on unspecified “standard recovery conditions,” weakening their impact.
- Technical execution is weak: proofs lack clarity and rely on handwaving rather than precise justification.
- Experimental validation is unconvincing and fails to demonstrate practical benefits or limitations of the theory.
- The connections to existing recovery theorems are more recapitulations of known results than synergistic contributions.
- Several cited references are not adequately engaged to clarify differences from prior work.
- Paper reads like a position paper—informal arguments and imprecise definitions reduce technical soundness and clarity.
- Overall contribution is incremental with limited significance; substantial theoretical strengthening and better experiments are needed.
- Recommendation: strong reject unless major revision with rigorous definitions, proofs, and compelling empirical evidence.
