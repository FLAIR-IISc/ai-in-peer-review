- Proposes graph clustering algorithms for SBM, DC-SBM, and PFM with theoretical guarantees that do not assume data is generated from those models.  
- Core idea: derive guarantees based on how well a chosen clustering and its parameters fit the observed data, using model-based recovery results.  
- Algorithms largely build on existing methods; novelty mainly in added constraints and analytical framing.  
- Strength: clear presentation, well-defined notation, concise algorithm descriptions, and clearly stated theorems.  
- Strength: explicitly connects model-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery literature.  
- Experiments are limited but attempt to validate theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: poor motivating narrative; paper doesn’t compellingly justify why model-free guarantees matter in practice.  
- Weakness: originality is limited—methods are mostly modifications of spectral clustering and MLE; “Generic Theorem” is more reinterpretation/strengthening than a breakthrough.  
- Weakness: extension to weighted graphs is incremental.  
- Significance is questionable: application conditions may be expensive or hard to verify, and empirical gains are not clearly demonstrated.  
- For acceptance: mathematically sound, clarifies model-free vs model-based links, and could provide theoretical stability insights.  
- Against acceptance: weak motivation, limited novelty, uncertain practical impact, unfocused collection of results, and weak experimental support.  
- Overall: technically correct but incremental; recommended rejection with suggestions to strengthen motivation, clarify novel contributions, and provide stronger experiments or application scenarios.
