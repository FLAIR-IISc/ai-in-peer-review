- Proposes a novel, model-free stability framework that gives data-driven, checkable guarantees on the uniqueness of a good graph clustering without assuming a generative model.  
- Central idea: if clustering C fits graph G well under a model class, any other well-fitting clustering C' must be a small perturbation of C.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), deriving explicit bounds on distances between well-fitting clusterings.  
- Bounds depend on computable data quantities like goodness-of-fit ε and the spectral eigengap of the graph Laplacian/adjacency.  
- Clever reuse of model-based recovery theorems to produce model-free, verifiable guarantees.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound and derived using appropriate matrix perturbation tools (Davis–Kahan).  
- All quantities in the bounds are computable from the input graph and clustering, enabling practical verification.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the framework’s application and utility.  
- Paper is very well written, organized, and clear, with strong motivation and thorough related work.  
- Main weakness: reliance on spectral perturbation bounds can yield loose or vacuous guarantees, especially in sparse graphs with small eigengaps.  
- SBM guarantees were too loose to be informative on some benchmark data, indicating limited practical applicability in challenging regimes.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel problem formulation, computable guarantees, high technical quality and clarity, and strong potential impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited tightness and applicability of bounds in sparse/community-detection-critical settings.  
- Overall conclusion: a strong, original contribution and promising first step despite limitations of spectral-based bounds; merits acceptance.
