- Proposes a novel model-free framework giving theoretical clustering stability guarantees, instantiated for PFM and SBM.  
- Leverages goodness-of-fit measures to derive exact recovery guarantees and connects model-free results to existing model-based theorems.  
- Extends applicability of clustering guarantees to real-world settings where model assumptions may not hold.  
- Novelty: bridges model-free and model-based clustering guarantees.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed derivations and computable data-driven quantities for PFM and SBM.  
- Practical validation: experiments on PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, and political blogs demonstrate implications.  
- Clear contribution: explains how existing recovery theorems adapt to model-free guarantees.  
- Practical limitations: goodness-of-fit measures and theorem conditions (especially for PFM) are sensitive to small perturbations.  
- SBM conditions are difficult to satisfy in practice, as shown by experiments.  
- Inconsistencies: different estimators/metrics for PFM vs SBM and omission of Degree-Corrected SBM (DC-SBM).  
- Notation and clarity issues: ambiguous notation (e.g., <M',M>F), unclear assumptions, and poorly labeled/ captioned figures.  
- Scope limitations: assumes same number of clusters and disjoint clusters, excluding varying cluster counts and hierarchical clustering.  
- Missing related work: omits several relevant model-free clustering contributions and lacks broader positioning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a challenging problem with a novel theoretical framework that could inspire future work and is empirically validated.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical utility, methodological inconsistencies, gaps in related work, and clarity problems.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending revisions to improve clarity, include DC-SBM, and better situate the work in the literature.
