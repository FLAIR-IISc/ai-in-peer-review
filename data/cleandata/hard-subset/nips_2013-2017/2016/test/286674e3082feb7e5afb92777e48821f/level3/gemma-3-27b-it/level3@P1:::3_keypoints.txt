- Proposes a model-free framework linking clustering quality to model goodness-of-fit, instantiated for SBM and PFM.  
- Novel perspective shifting theoretical guarantees from model assumptions to data-driven criteria.  
- Strong mathematical foundation using spectral graph theory and perturbation analysis with clear formal definitions.  
- Effectively connects model-free results to existing model-based recovery theorems and leverages prior bounds.  
- Detailed empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets analyzing stability and parameter effects.  
- Emphasis on computability: all theorem quantities are measurable from data and clustering outputs.  
- Lacks a clear overarching motivation or guiding narrative, feeling like a collection of results.  
- Algorithmic contributions are largely modifications of existing methods, making novelty incremental.  
- Results rely on strong assumptions (e.g., nonzero eigengaps) and sensitive parameter conditions not fully explored.  
- Dense presentation with many theorems makes the paper hard to follow without clearer exposition.  
- Insufficiently deep comparison to related work on robustness and model-free clustering approaches.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel framework, solid literature connections, and practical experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation, incremental algorithms, strong assumptions, and opaque presentation.  
- Overall: competent and interesting but limited impact due to motivation, novelty, and assumption concerns.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; suggest revisions to strengthen motivation, clarify presentation, and broaden related-work discussion.
