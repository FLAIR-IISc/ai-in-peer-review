- Proposes a "model-free" graph clustering framework where guarantees depend on how well a model fits observed data rather than assuming data is generated by that model.  
- Main theoretical contribution is Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem): if clustering C fits data well under model M, any other clustering C' that also fits well must be close to C.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for PFM and SBM, giving specific error bounds for those models.  
- Provides computable stability results for clustering under graph perturbations derived from spectral properties of the graph Laplacian.  
- Demonstrates how model-free guarantees can be obtained from existing model-based results (e.g., using Theorem 3 of [20] to derive Theorem 11).  
- Presents clear algorithms (PFM Estimation, SBM Estimation) and well-organized theorems and proofs with consistent notation.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets (PFM, LFR benchmark, Political Blogs) shows the computable quantities (ε, δ, δ0) can validate clusterings.  
- Technical soundness: proofs and theoretical results are correct and well-supported.  
- Strength: effectively connects model-based and model-free perspectives, clarifying how existing recovery results can imply model-free guarantees.  
- Weakness: contribution is largely a reinterpretation/refinement of existing model-based results rather than a novel conceptual advance.  
- Weakness: paper lacks clear motivation for why the model-free viewpoint is necessary or superior in practice.  
- Weakness: specific constraints in Theorems 4 and 9 are not convincingly distinguished from prior work (e.g., [18,17,20]).  
- Significance is limited: framework does not demonstrably solve new problems or offer clear practical advantages over established methods.  
- Overall: technically solid and well-written but conceptually incremental with limited practical impact; needs clearer narrative and stronger distinction from prior work.
