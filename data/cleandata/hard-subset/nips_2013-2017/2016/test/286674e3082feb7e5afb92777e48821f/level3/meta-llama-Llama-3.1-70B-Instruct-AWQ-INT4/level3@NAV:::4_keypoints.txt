- Proposes a general model-free framework for clustering stability guarantees with a generic theorem instantiable for models like SBM and PFM.  
- Shows intimate connections between model-free and model-based clustering results.  
- Framework is broadly applicable, making it a useful theoretical and practical tool.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, aiding reader comprehension.  
- Provides experimental results that support the proposed approach.  
- Treats PFM and SBM differently despite PFM including DC-SBM/SBM, and this distinction is not justified.  
- Estimator choices for PFM and SBM are inconsistent and need justification.  
- Assumes the number of clusters is known, limiting practical applicability; extension to unknown K is needed.  
- Definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F in Proposition 10 is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Assumption 5 is ambiguous—unclear whether it means ||Ĺ^2 - L^2||_F or ||Ĺ - L||_F^2.  
- Paper lacks sufficient comparisons with related model-free stability work (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013).  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality, and is recommended for acceptance with revisions to address the issues above.
