- Introduces a transformation/stability unsupervised loss that minimizes prediction differences across multiple stochastic passes of a sample.  
- Exploits stochastic mechanisms (randomized augmentation, dropout, random max-pooling) to enforce prediction consistency.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on MNIST, SVHN, NORB, CIFAR-10/100, and ImageNet, especially with few labeled samples.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and a thorough related-work review.  
- Proposed loss is simple, effective, and easily combined with any supervised loss.  
- Provides detailed empirical analysis of hyperparameters and benefits from combining with mutual-exclusivity loss.  
- Shows versatility by applying the method across different ConvNet architectures and datasets.  
- Main weakness: potentially high computational cost due to multiple network passes on large datasets.  
- Another weakness: limited theoretical justification and unclear relationship to existing semi-supervised methods.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions; reviewer rating 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with impressive experimental results and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational expense and lack of deeper theoretical insight.
