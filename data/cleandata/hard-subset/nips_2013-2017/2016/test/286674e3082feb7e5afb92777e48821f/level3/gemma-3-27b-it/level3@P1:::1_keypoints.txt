- Introduces a model-free theoretical framework: if a clustering fits the observed graph well, any equally good clustering must be structurally similar, enabling validation without assuming a generative model.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, yielding model-free guarantees and strengthening existing model-based recovery results.  
- Core technique is a proxy-model approach: reconstruct a model from a clustering and evaluate fit to link to recovery theorems.  
- Positions contribution within prior work on spectral clustering, clusterability, robustness, and stability, highlighting novelty over model-based methods.  
- Technical results are sound with rigorous theorems and proofs, though some details are deferred to the supplement.  
- Explicitly analyzes distance metrics (standard ME and weighted ME) and provides results for both PFM and SBM.  
- Experimental validation shows practical applicability but is limited in scope.  
- Strength: conceptually novel and elegant solution to a longstanding limitation in graph clustering.  
- Strength: connects model-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery literature, opening research avenues.  
- Minor issue: notational/stylistic confusion from using “hats” for observed quantities.  
- Major concern: proxy-model approach may bias the framework toward clusterings that fit the chosen models, limiting generality.  
- Concern: reliance on non-standard weighted Misclassification Error (wME) for PFM may be less natural and hinder adoption.  
- Practical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing (e.g., taking powers of adjacency) to meet theorem conditions, adding preprocessing that may mask structure.  
- Empirical critique: evaluation should be more comprehensive and include datasets where smoothing isn’t necessary.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on authors addressing wME concerns and discussing implications of using standard ME in the rebuttal.
