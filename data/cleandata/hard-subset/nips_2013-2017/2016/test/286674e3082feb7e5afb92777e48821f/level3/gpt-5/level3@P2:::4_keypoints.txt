- Proposes a model-free validation template: fit a model M(G, C) and certify clustering C is stable by showing any other good-fit clustering C′ must be close to C, using spectral/Frobenius goodness-of-fit and giving explicit misclassification bounds.  
- Main instantiation PFM: Laplacian-constrained estimator matching top-K eigenpairs, yields weighted ME bounds depending on the Laplacian eigengap.  
- Main instantiation SBM: adjacency-based MLE yields unweighted ME bounds assuming a nonzero K-th eigenvalue.  
- Shows how existing model-based recovery results can be turned into model-free guarantees under alternative goodness-of-fit assumptions.  
- Experiments: PFM guarantees sometimes hold but only for tiny perturbations; SBM bounds are very conservative and not informative in practice.  
- Relation to prior work: leverages standard Davis–Kahan/subspace tools and connects to stability literature, but omits and does not clearly map to several model-free stability notions.  
- Strength: clear, valuable goal — computable, data-driven validation with no hidden constants and technically sound, reusable analysis.  
- Weakness: inconsistent treatment across models (Laplacian+wME vs adjacency+ME) with no rationale or DC-SBM theorem provided.  
- Weakness: practical utility limited — bounds tolerate tiny perturbations or are too loose in experiments.  
- Weakness: framework fixes K and cannot handle near-merge/split (K vs K+1) scenarios where clusterings remain close.  
- Weakness: several definitions/assumptions ambiguous or under-explained (e.g., Assumption 5, inner product in Proposition 10).  
- Weakness: incomplete related-work coverage on model-free stability and unclear positioning relative to existing notions.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation across sections, missing matrix definitions, and unclear/ungraphical figures (missing axis labels, legends).  
- Requested clarifications: justify/unify metric choices (ME vs wME) and spaces (adjacency vs Laplacian), define ⟨M̂, M⟩F and δY, disambiguate Assumption 5 (squared Laplacian?), and align experiments with theoretical metrics.  
- Suggested extensions: provide a DC-SBM instantiation/theorem, consider rank-adaptive treatment for K vs K+1, and sketch extending the template beyond block models (e.g., hierarchical settings).  
- Originality: conceptual novelty in the model-free goodness-of-fit template; technique-level novelty modest.  
- Significance: potentially impactful but currently limited by conservative bounds, inconsistent choices, and mismatches between theory and experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: timely, practical validation goal with simple, reusable analysis and clear paths for extension.  
- Arguments for rejection: questionable practical usefulness, inconsistent modeling choices, ambiguities, and incomplete related-work placement.  
- Minor fixes: improve figures (labels/legends), report wME when Theorem 4 uses wME, state conditional independence of edges, standardize notation, and consider relocating Section 4.4 to Related Work.  
- Overall: promising conceptual contribution with careful theory, but needs unification (metrics/space), DC-SBM results, clearer assumptions/notation, and better-aligned experiments to be practically useful.
