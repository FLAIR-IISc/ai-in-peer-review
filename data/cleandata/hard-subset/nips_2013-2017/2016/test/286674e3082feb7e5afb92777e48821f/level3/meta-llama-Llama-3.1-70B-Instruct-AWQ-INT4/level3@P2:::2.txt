This paper proposes a framework for clustering with guarantees without model assumptions, providing a novel approach to model-free clustering. The authors establish relationships between different clustering distance measures for network data and analyze two network models: Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM). They prove a locally convex-like property for clustering under these models, showing that any two clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.

The paper is well-written and explains core ideas clearly. The authors provide a thorough background on graphs, clusterings, and block models, making the paper accessible to readers unfamiliar with the topic. The main theorem, Generic Theorem 1, is well-presented, and the authors provide a clear blueprint for instantiating the theorem for PFM and SBM.

The results are novel and contribute to the existing literature on model-free clustering. The authors demonstrate equivalence between multiple clustering distance measures for networks, which is a valuable contribution to the field. The paper also provides experimental validation, using standard spectral clustering and demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

The theoretical arguments are relatively simple extensions of known results, and the authors provide clear explanations and proofs. However, the paper lacks formal results in the direction of potential applications, such as detecting clusterability. The authors note that their framework can be used to prove model-free guarantees for clustering, but this is not fully explored in the paper.

In terms of originality, the paper builds upon recent works, such as Peng et al. (2015) and Chao et al. (2015), but provides a distinct contribution to the field. The authors' approach to model-free clustering is novel and provides a new perspective on the problem.

The paper's significance is high, as it provides a new framework for model-free clustering and demonstrates its effectiveness. The results have implications for various applications, including community detection, network analysis, and data mining.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide clear explanations and proofs. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough background on the topic. However, the paper could benefit from more formal results in the direction of potential applications.
