## Review of “Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results”

This paper presents a model-free framework for guaranteeing clustering stability, applicable to Stochastic Block Models (SBM) and Preference Frame Models (PFM). The core idea – establishing correctness without model assumptions by focusing on data statistics – is a valuable contribution to the field. The authors successfully derive exact recovery results under their framework and connect it to existing model-based approaches, potentially strengthening those results as well. 

However, the practical relevance of the proposed framework is questionable, as highlighted by the authors’ own simulations. A significant weakness lies in the inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM. While the authors acknowledge PFM encompasses SBM, the differing estimators (Laplacian vs. adjacency) and distance metrics (weighted ME vs. ME) lack sufficient justification. The absence of DC-SBM results, given its position between SBM and PFM, is a notable omission. 

Several technical points require clarification. The definition of <M', M>_F in Proposition 10 should explicitly state it represents the Frobenius inner product. Assumption 5’s notation is ambiguous; it needs to be clarified as either ||Ĺ^2 - L^2||_F or ||Ĺ - L||_F^2, with a clear explanation for squaring the Laplacians if the former is intended. Furthermore, the authors should discuss the potential extension of their techniques to other spectral clustering applications, such as hierarchical clustering (citing Balakrishnan et al. 2011 as a starting point). The framework’s limitation to equal cluster numbers (K vs. K+1) should be acknowledged as a significant drawback.

The related work section is incomplete, omitting relevant model-free stability results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013). A clearer articulation of how this paper’s notion of stability relates to existing concepts like perturbation, approximation, and center-perturbation stability is needed. 

The simulations reveal that PFM is sensitive to only tiny perturbations, contradicting the claim about a large eigengap. The SBM conditions appear overly restrictive, further undermining practical applicability. Figure 1’s caption is unclear regarding the SBM subplot, and the axes lack labels. A mismatch exists between the theoretical guarantees (weighted ME) and experimental perturbations (ME) for PFM, requiring a thorough explanation. Finally, minor notational inconsistencies and a misplaced section (4.4) should be addressed.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel model-free approach to clustering guarantees.
*   Connection to and potential strengthening of existing model-based results.
*   Clear theoretical framework with computable quantities.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Questionable practical relevance due to restrictive conditions.
*   Inconsistent treatment of SBM and PFM.
*   Technical ambiguities and omissions.
*   Incomplete related work.



**Overall Assessment:**  While the paper presents a theoretically interesting framework, its limited practical applicability and several technical shortcomings prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. Major revisions addressing the points above are necessary.
