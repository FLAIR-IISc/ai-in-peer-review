- Proposes a model-free framework: if clustering C and block parameters M(G,C) fit G well, any other good-fitting C' must be close to C.  
- Framework instantiated for PFM and SBM with derived stability bounds and connections to model-based recovery theorems.  
- Strength: novel, significant formulation moving beyond strict generative-model assumptions to observable-data guarantees.  
- Strength: bounds for δ and δ0 are computable from the graph and candidate clustering, making them practically checkable.  
- Strength: effectively connects and can improve constants in existing model-based analyses (e.g., Rohe et al., Wan & Meila).  
- Weakness: inconsistent treatment of PFM vs. SBM—different estimators (Laplacian vs. adjacency) and distance metrics (weighted vs. standard ME) lack justification.  
- Weakness: no evaluation of applying PFM analysis to SBM data or vice versa, and DC-SBM results are missing.  
- Weakness: simulations suggest PFM guarantees only hold for minuscule perturbations (δ ≤ δ0 fails quickly), questioning practical usefulness.  
- Weakness: claim that large eigengap increases sensitivity contradicts typical spectral-stability intuition and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: SBM conditions appear vacuous in experiments (δ > 1 always), undermining applicability of SBM guarantees.  
- Weakness: theoretical PFM bounds use weighted misclassification error but experiments report unweighted error, creating a critical mismatch.  
- Weakness: unclear definitions—e.g., notation for <M',M>_F not stated, and ambiguity in Assumption 5 (||Ĺ^2 - L^2||_F vs. ||Ĺ - L||_F^2) requires explanation.  
- Weakness: scope unclear—does the template extend beyond block models to other spectral-clustering settings (e.g., hierarchical)?  
- Weakness: framework requires same K for C and C', preventing assessment of stability under splits/merges.  
- Weakness: related work incomplete—omits relevant model-free clustering/stability papers (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2011, 2012) and lacks discussion of relations to perturbation/approximation stability.  
- Minor: Figure 1 axes unlabeled and caption confusing, especially regarding SBM claims.  
- Minor: wording error on line 44—edges should be “independent given cluster assignments.”  
- Minor: inconsistent notation across PFM and SBM sections (B, C, Y, etc.) and unnecessarily complex symbols (e.g., YZU).  
- Minor: section organization issue—Section 4.4 on clusterability/resilience fits better in Related Work.  
- Conclusion: idea is original and promising but the paper has substantial issues in consistency, clarity, experiments, and literature engagement requiring major revision.
