- Proposes a valuable model-free framework for clustering stability guarantees that provides "correctness" without model assumptions.  
- Core idea: use computable statistics to give guarantees and connect model-free results to PFM and SBM exact recovery.  
- Theoretical strengths: framework is sound with verifiable quantities (ε, δ, δ₀) and relatively elementary proofs.  
- Inconsistent methodology: PFM analysis uses Laplacian + weighted ME while SBM uses adjacency + standard ME without justification.  
- Omits DC-SBM results despite DC-SBM being a special case of PFM.  
- Technical ambiguities: unclear definition of ⟨M′,M⟩_F in Proposition 10 and ambiguous Assumption 5 (||Ĺ²−L²||_F vs ||Ĺ−L||_F²).  
- Framework limitation: cannot handle cluster splits/merges (K vs K+1 scenarios).  
- SBM conditions appear overly restrictive in simulations (all δ values > 1).  
- Mismatch between theory and experiments: theoretical PFM guarantees use weighted ME but experiments evaluate standard ME perturbations.  
- PFM simulations only tolerate tiny perturbations, contradicting the claim about eigengap sensitivity.  
- Omits key related work on model-free stability (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and fails to relate to existing perturbation-stability notions.  
- Conclusion: inconsistencies, omissions, and practical limitations reduce impact; recommends rejection absent substantial refinement.
