- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives guarantees when a clustering fits the data according to a computable goodness-of-fit measure.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, converting model-based recovery guarantees into model-free guarantees.  
- Conceptually novel: addresses the reliance on explicit model assumptions in clustering guarantees.  
- Theoretical results are sound with clear theorems and proofs (some relegated to the supplement).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with proper mathematical notation.  
- Lacks motivation: does not explain why model-free guarantees are needed or how they differ from existing validation methods like bootstrapping or stability-based approaches.  
- Limited novelty: algorithmic methods are mainly adaptations of existing techniques (e.g., spectral clustering); main contribution is the theoretical framing.  
- Insufficient experimental validation: experiments use generated data and do not demonstrate practical utility or compare to existing validation methods.  
- Ambiguous significance: paper does not clearly show how the model-free guarantees advance the state of the art or sufficiently connect to cluster stability literature.  
- Arguments for acceptance: represents a conceptual advance, provides sound theoretical results, offers a computable path to validating clusterings, and connects to model-based work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear motivation, limited methodological novelty, weak experimental evidence, and insufficient differentiation from related validation/stability work.  
- Suggested improvements: expand the introduction to motivate model-free guarantees, compare to existing validation methods, add real-world experiments, and discuss specific applications where model-free guarantees are valuable.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid but insufficiently motivated and validated; recommend rejection unless the authors better justify the approach and demonstrate practical advantages.
