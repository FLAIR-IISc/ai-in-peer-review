- Proposes a novel unsupervised transformation/stability loss that leverages the stochasticity of ConvNets to minimize prediction differences across multiple passes.  
- Introduces combination with a mutual-exclusivity loss that encourages one-hot classifier predictions.  
- Loss is designed to be used alongside standard supervised loss functions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering MNIST, SVHN, NORB, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet.  
- Demonstrates improved ConvNet accuracy, especially when labeled data is scarce.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on several datasets (notably CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100).  
- Provides detailed analysis of hyperparameters and the benefits of combining losses.  
- Technical implementation and experimental details are sufficient for reproducibility.  
- High originality by exploiting ConvNet stochasticity and proposing a new loss pairing.  
- Significant practical impact for semi-supervised learning scenarios with limited labels.  
- Lacks a thorough theoretical analysis to explain behavior and limitations of the loss.  
- Limited comparison with other semi-supervised approaches, especially generative and graph-based methods.  
- Could include a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential extensions.
