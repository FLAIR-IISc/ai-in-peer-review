- Proposes a novel framework for graph clustering that gives distribution-free theoretical guarantees without assuming a generative model.  
- Core idea: if a clustering fits the graph well, any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.  
- Provides a generic theorem instantiated for model classes including SBM and PFM.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Theoretical contributions are novel and interesting.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing.  
- Authors analyze limitations, notably the need to smooth real-world graphs to recover structure.  
- Limitation: use of a non-standard clustering distance (PFM) may reduce applicability and impact.  
- Limitation: reliance on a proxy-model approach strongly constrains what counts as a “good” clustering.  
- Limitation: results may not be directly comparable to classical algorithms that recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- NIPS criteria: scores well on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Suggested improvements: add more related-work context, discuss real-world implications and future directions, and address limitations with suggestions for follow-up work.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS.
