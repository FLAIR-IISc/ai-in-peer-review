- Proposes a novel model-free framework for clustering guarantees based on stability: any other good-fitting clustering must be a small perturbation of the original.  
- Instantiates the framework for both Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), deriving recovery results and linking model-free and model-based approaches.  
- Significant contribution: provides model-free clustering guarantees, addressing dependence on specific generative models.  
- Practical strength: framework relies on computable quantities.  
- Inconsistent treatment: uses different estimators (Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM) and distance metrics (weighted ME vs ME) without clear justification.  
- Omits results for Degree-Corrected SBM (DC-SBM) despite DC-SBM being encompassed by PFM.  
- Proposition 10 notation unclear: <M', M>_F likely the Frobenius inner product but needs clarification.  
- Assumption 5 about squaring Laplacians lacks intuitive explanation.  
- Simulations indicate stability conditions are extremely restrictive, especially for SBM, calling practical relevance into question.  
- Claim about PFM sensitivity to eigengap is unsupported and contradicted by observations about adjacency powering smoothing.  
- Framework appears theoretically interesting but may have limited applicability to real-world datasets per experiments.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 caption unclear and axes unlabeled; inconsistent notation across sections (e.g., B, C, Y).  
- Section 4.4 is misplaced and would fit better in related work.  
- Omits relevant model-free stability literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and lacks distinction between different stability notions.  
- Framework cannot handle cluster splits/merges (different K vs K+1), limiting applicability when cluster counts differ.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty of model-free guarantees, connection between model-free and model-based results, and computability focus.  
- Rejection arguments: inconsistent PFM/SBM treatment, restrictive simulations, omitted related work, and inability to handle cluster number changes.  
- Overall recommendation: reject pending fixes—address inconsistencies, expand simulations, and clarify theoretical assumptions to improve the paper.
