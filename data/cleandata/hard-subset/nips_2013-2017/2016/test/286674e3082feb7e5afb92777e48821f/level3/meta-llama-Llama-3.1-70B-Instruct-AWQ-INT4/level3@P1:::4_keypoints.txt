- Proposes a novel model-free framework for clustering stability applicable to multiple clustering models (PFM, SBM).  
- Framework measures distance between clusterings and provides theoretical stability guarantees.  
- Presents a unified template that can derive exact recovery results for PFM and SBM.  
- Simulations suggest practical concerns: PFM appears sensitive to tiny perturbations.  
- Inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM despite PFM including DC-SBM/SBM; this inconsistency is unjustified.  
- Uses different stability metrics for PFM and SBM, making comparisons difficult.  
- Proposition 10 lacks a clear definition/explanation of the Frobenius inner product crucial for the proof.  
- Unclear how the framework extends to other clustering settings where spectral methods work (e.g., hierarchical clustering).  
- Omits relevant model-free stability literature (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and does not relate the new notion of stability to prior work.  
- Simulations are limited to PFM and SBM; broader empirical validation (other models) is needed.  
- Paper makes a significant theoretical contribution and is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended minor revisions: define the Frobenius inner product, justify differing treatments of PFM vs SBM, add broader simulations, and address inconsistent model treatment.
