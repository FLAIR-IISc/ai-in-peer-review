- Introduces a framework for concentration guarantees in community detection using spectral clustering without relying on model parameters.  
- Proposes a generic theorem that can be instantiated for models like SBM and PFM.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and enjoyable to read.  
- Motivations, error definitions, and assumptions are clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Results are stated and proved in great generality and applied to specific popular graph models.  
- Addresses the important question of stability of community detection solutions.  
- Provides model-agnostic guarantees across different generative models, a significant contribution.  
- Thorough related-work analysis highlighting differences from existing methods.  
- Empirical evaluations on simulated and real-world datasets demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Main weakness: proofs rely on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight.  
- Spectral techniques may be nearly intractable for very sparse graphs, limiting applicability.  
- Overall technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques, distinct from prior work.  
- Results are significant and likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Recommendation: Accept (9/10, confidence 8/10), with minor revisions to address limitations and discuss applications/extensions.
