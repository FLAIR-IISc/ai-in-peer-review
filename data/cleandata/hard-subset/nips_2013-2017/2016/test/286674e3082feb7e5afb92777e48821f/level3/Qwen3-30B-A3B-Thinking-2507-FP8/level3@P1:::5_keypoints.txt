- Proposes a novel, model-free framework giving theoretical guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Establishes a connection between model-based and model-free approaches: if a clustering fits a model well, any other good fit must be close.  
- Main theoretical result is Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) asserting stability: any other clustering that fits well is close to the original under given conditions.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for PFM (Theorem 4) and SBM (Theorem 9), providing specific computable misclassification error bounds.  
- PFM results give weighted misclassification bounds in terms of spectral gap and goodness-of-fit; SBM bounds are similar but require stronger conditions.  
- Strong technical development using matrix perturbation theory (Davis–Kahan) instead of combinatorial counting, enabling cleaner proofs.  
- Demonstrates how existing model-based recovery theorems can be repurposed to yield model-free guarantees.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, with thorough background, notation, and comparisons to related work.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real networks show the bounds are computable and practically meaningful; PFM-based guarantees appear more practical than SBM for the same data.  
- Main limitation: reliance on spectral methods, which can be computationally challenging or less effective for very sparse graphs.  
- Contributions summarized: a framework for model-free guarantees, connection between model-based and model-free, specific computable error bounds, and empirical validation.  
- Original and significant relative to prior work (closer to [6] but stronger model-free guarantees); reviewer recommends acceptance.
