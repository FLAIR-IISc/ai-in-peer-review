- Proposes a “model-free” graph clustering framework claiming guarantees by checking model fit rather than assuming a generative model.  
- Main theoretical claim is Theorem 1: if a clustering fits the data well under a model, any other clustering that also fits well must be close to it.  
- Instantiates Theorem 1 for PFM and SBM but fails to clearly distinguish from prior stability-based approaches.  
- Theorem 1 is vague and unquantified; key terms like “fits G well” and “small perturbation” are undefined.  
- Proposition 3 is informal and depends on unspecified “standard recovery conditions,” preventing verification.  
- Claimed improvement in Theorem 11 (factor 128) actually weakens the bound, contradicting the paper’s claim.  
- Experimental results show SBM bounds (Theorem 9) are not satisfied even on LFR data, undermining practical usefulness.  
- Paper does not adequately discuss limitations of the proposed framework or its domain of applicability.  
- Definitions and key phrases are imprecise; the relation between model-based and model-free guarantees is unclearly articulated.  
- Proof techniques and the link between theory and experiments are insufficiently explained.  
- Core idea is not novel: closely resembles existing stability-based clustering work (Meilă 2003, Awasthi 2015).  
- Significance is limited: guarantees are not practically useful or generalizable, and offer no clear advance for practitioners.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-written, clear framework, and conceptually interesting connection between model-based and model-free views.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of rigor, unsubstantiated claims, questionable novelty, contradictory experimental validation — recommendation: reject.
