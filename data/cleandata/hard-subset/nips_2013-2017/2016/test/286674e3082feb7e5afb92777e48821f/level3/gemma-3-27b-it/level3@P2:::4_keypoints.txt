- Introduces a model-free stability framework: if a clustering C fits data well, any other well-fitting clustering C' must be “close” to C.
- Instantiates framework for PFM and SBM, relating model-free and model-based recovery and showing required quantities are computable.
- Addresses an important problem by extending clustering guarantees beyond strong generative model assumptions.
- Technical results for PFM are interesting and appear rigorous.
- Treatment of PFM and SBM feels disjointed despite PFM generalizing (D)C-SBM; a unified approach is expected.
- Different estimator choices (Laplacian for PFM, adjacency for SBM) and use of weighted vs standard ME need stronger justification.
- Missing results for DC-SBM are a significant omission given model hierarchy.
- Proposition 10’s <M',M>_F is unclear—should explicitly state Frobenius inner product.
- Assumption 5 is ambiguously worded; clarify whether it refers to ||Ĺ^2 - L^2||_F or ||Ĺ - L||_F^2 and justify squaring Laplacians.
- Stability conditions, especially for SBM, seem stringent and may limit practical applicability.
- Framework requires fixed K, preventing handling of splits/merges (K vs K+1) common in practice.
- Related work omitted several model-free stability results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013); comparison to other stability notions is missing.
- Experiments raise concerns: PFM simulations tolerate tiny perturbations, sensitivity claims about eigengap conflict with smoothing results, and SBM conditions seem hard to meet.
- Inconsistency between theoretical guarantees (weighted ME) and experimental perturbation metric (ME) needs resolution; figure axis labels and notation inconsistencies are minor but present.
- Recommendation: novel and potentially impactful but currently limited in practicality; reviewer suggests weak reject pending relaxed conditions, DC-SBM results, unified treatment, and improved experiments.
