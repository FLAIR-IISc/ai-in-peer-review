- Proposes a model-agnostic stability framework: if a clustering C fits graph G well, any other well-fitting clustering C' must be close to C, instantiated for PFM and SBM with computable bounds.  
- Strength: addresses important limitation of model-based guarantees by aiming for data-generating-process-agnostic validation.  
- Strength: derived bounds depend on data-computable quantities (ε, spectral eigengap), enabling practical cluster validation.  
- Strength: offers a novel perspective by reframing model-based recovery theorems as model-free validation via stability.  
- Weakness: overall lack of formal rigor; "Theorem 1" is a vague high-level statement rather than a formal theorem.  
- Weakness: Proposition 3 is informal and depends on unspecified prior assumptions, making it unverifiable as stated.  
- Weakness: results appear to be direct, possibly naive applications of standard matrix perturbation bounds (e.g., Davis–Kahan), yielding loose conditions.  
- Weakness: conditions like δ ≤ δ0 may be overly restrictive; Theorem 9 fails to give meaningful guarantees in some synthetic SBM-like experiments.  
- Weakness: experiments do not convincingly demonstrate utility on graphs not generated by PFM/SBM, weakening the claim of model-agnostic validation.  
- Weakness: presentation overclaims "model-free" while still fitting specific model classes; discussion of clusterability/resilience is largely rephrasing.  
- Detailed: mathematical steps (Sinus theorem, subspace-to-clustering distance) are sound, but may not yield practically relevant guarantees.  
- Detailed: experimental evaluation is limited and unsurprising (stability of algorithm’s own output); lacks tests on real-world non-PFM/SBM ground truths.  
- Detailed: Theorem 9’s dependence on |λ̂_AK|^2 in the denominator is concerning and can make bounds vacuous; needs more discussion.  
- Recommendation: reject as current work reads like a position/early-stage exploration; authors should formalize the framework, tighten bounds, and provide stronger real-world experiments.
