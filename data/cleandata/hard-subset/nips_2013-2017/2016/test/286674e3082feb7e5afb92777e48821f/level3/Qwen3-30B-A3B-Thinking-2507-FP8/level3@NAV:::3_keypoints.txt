- Proposes a "model-free" graph clustering framework that gives stability guarantees without assuming a specific generative model (SBM/DC-SBM/PFM).  
- Introduces conditions under which clustering stability holds when model parameters fit observed data, with theorems bounding clustering perturbations.  
- Connects model-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery theorems, showing how prior results can be repurposed.  
- Clear presentation of algorithms, theorems, and metrics (e.g., weighted ME distance).  
- Comprehensive experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Technical approach is sound with rigorous proofs for main results.  
- Offers a novel perspective linking model-based and model-free clustering approaches.  
- Fails to adequately motivate why this specific framework is needed; motivation is weak.  
- Paper reads like a list of algorithmic modifications rather than a cohesive theoretical contribution.  
- Novelty is limited—methods largely build on existing spectral clustering techniques without sufficient justification.  
- Connection to prior work is superficial, with brief references (e.g., [20]) and unclear differentiation.  
- Quality: theoretically sound but lacking depth on limitations; Clarity: excellent; Originality: weak; Significance: questionable.  
- Overall: technically competent but fails to establish compelling motivation or significant novelty; needs stronger theoretical grounding and clearer advancement over existing methods.
