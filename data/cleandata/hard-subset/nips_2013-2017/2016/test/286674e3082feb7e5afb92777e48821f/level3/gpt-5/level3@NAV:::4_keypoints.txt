- Proposes a model-free, computable certification template for clustering stability on graphs, instantiated for PFM and SBM.  
- Theoretical template is sound, using Davis–Kahan and geometric clustering-distance equivalence.  
- Treatment of PFM vs SBM is inconsistent: PFM uses Laplacian fits and weighted misclassification error (wME) while SBM uses adjacency fits and ME—justify and compare these choices.  
- Request to evaluate and report DC-SBM results since PFM subsumes DC-SBM/SBM.  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5: clarify whether the norm is ||Ĺ2 − L2||F or ||Ĺ − L||2F and explain the implication of squaring Laplacians.  
- Clarify notation and definitions, e.g., <M̂, M>F (Frobenius inner product) in Proposition 10.  
- Framework assumes same number of clusters K for C and C′; discuss handling near-splits/merges (K vs K+1).  
- Experiments show PFM certificates tolerate only tiny perturbations, questioning practical usefulness.  
- Contradiction: large eigengap claimed to increase PFM sensitivity vs empirical stability gain from smoothing/powering A—needs reconciliation.  
- SBM conditions are very stringent in practice; discuss whether sharper analysis could yield more informative bounds.  
- Notation inconsistencies across sections (B, C, Y used differently); recommend unified notation and removing unnecessary symbols (e.g., YZ in SBM estimator).  
- Clarity and presentation issues: Figure 1 axes/captions unlabeled/unclear, fix Line 44 (dependence statement for Aij).  
- Section 4.4 reads like Related Work; add missing model-free stability literature (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and relate to other stability notions.  
- Significance: the computable, model-free certificate is novel and promising for block models.  
- Suggested improvements to increase impact: (i) cover DC-SBM, (ii) align theoretical metrics and experimental perturbations (wME vs ME), (iii) demonstrate applicability beyond block models (other spectral/hierarchical settings).  
- Pros: clear, computable certification template; solid perturbation-analysis bridge between model-free and model-based guarantees.  
- Cons: inconsistent modeling choices and metrics, missing DC-SBM results, experimental-theory mismatch, ambiguities, notation and figure problems.  
- Overall recommendation: promising but current practical relevance and presentation issues warrant rejection with encouragement to revise along the above lines.
