The paper provides a framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a specific model, instead relying on the statistics of the data that can be checked. The authors propose a generic theorem that relates the goodness of fit of a model to the distance between two clusterings. They then instantiate this theorem for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), providing model-free guarantees for clustering. The paper also explores the connection between model-based and model-free results, showing that existing model-based recovery theorems can be used to prove model-free guarantees.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do a good job of explaining the core ideas and technical details. The use of notation and terminology is consistent throughout the paper. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

The main strength of the paper is its contribution to the field of graph clustering. The authors provide a new perspective on clustering, one that does not rely on specific models or assumptions about the data. The generic theorem and its instantiations for PFM and SBM provide a solid foundation for future work in this area.

One potential weakness of the paper is the reliance on existing model-based recovery theorems. While the authors do provide some new insights and results, the paper could benefit from more novel and original contributions. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results and their potential applications.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written, and the technical details are sound. The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of the results, and the experimental evaluation is thorough. However, the paper could benefit from more novel and original contributions to elevate it to the next level.
