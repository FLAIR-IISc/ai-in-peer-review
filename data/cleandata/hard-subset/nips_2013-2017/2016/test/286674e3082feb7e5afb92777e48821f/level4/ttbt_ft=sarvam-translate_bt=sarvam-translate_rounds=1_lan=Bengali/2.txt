This paper provides a relationship between the distance of clustering and the performance of a network model. The paper considers network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and proves that clustering has locally convex-like properties for the measurement of misclassification distance. It is shown that any two clusterability models that equally well estimate the parameters of the model fitted to the network data are also close to each other in terms of misclassification distance. This paper provides an excellent property of clustering in network data. The equivalence property between different measures of clustering distance for network data is stated and proven in this paper. This paper provides both theoretical and experimental results. The results presented in this paper are new, but some similar work has been done recently in network literature by Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015), Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical parts of this paper are also quite simple extensions of well-known results. As mentioned in this paper, this work can be used in some cases for clusterability identification, but formal results on that subject are not provided in this paper. This paper is well-written and explains the main concepts of the paper quite clearly.
