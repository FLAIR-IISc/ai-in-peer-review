- Provides broad concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering across many problem settings.  
- Establishes these guarantees under several generative models without requiring knowledge of model parameters.  
- Advances an important and popular area; work is interesting and relevant.  
- Raises the important question of stability of community-detection solutions.  
- Formulates and proves high-level, general results and instantiates them for widely studied graphical models.  
- Writing is clear and engaging; motivation for error definitions and assumptions is well explained.  
- Main substantive criticism: analysis relies on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can become nearly intractable in very sparse regimes.  
- Overall a strong starting point for a line of stability results with expectation of further progress.
