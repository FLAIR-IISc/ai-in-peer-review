This paper provides a relationship between the clustering distance and the performance of network models. The paper considers network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and proves that the misclassification of clustering has locally convex-like properties for measuring distance. It is shown that any two clustering models that equally well estimate the parameters of the model fitted to the network data are close to each other in terms of misclassification distance. This paper provides an excellent characteristic of clustering in network data. The equivalence properties between different measures of clustering distance for network data are stated and proven in this paper. This paper provides both theoretical and experimental results. The results presented in this paper are new, but similar work has been done recently in network literature by Peng, Sun, and Janety (COLT, 2015), Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Archiv, 2015). The theoretical parts of this paper are also quite simple extensions of well-known results. As mentioned in this paper, this work can be used in some cases for clustering identification, but formal results on this topic are not provided in this paper. This paper is well-written and explains the main concepts of the paper quite clearly.
