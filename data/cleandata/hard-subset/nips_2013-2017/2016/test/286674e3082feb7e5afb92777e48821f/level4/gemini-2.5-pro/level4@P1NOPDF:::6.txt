This paper proposes a model-free theoretical framework for graph clustering. However, the theoretical results presented are not sufficiently rigorous and are ultimately unconvincing.

The central issue is that the authors' proposed theory relies heavily on informal arguments and imprecise language, rather than formal mathematical definitions and proofs. While presented as a new theoretical framework, it currently reads more like a high-level conceptual sketch.

This lack of rigor is exemplified by the phrasing of the main results. For example, Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) makes the claim that a clustering "...which also fits G well is close to C," but the terms "fits G well" and "close to C" are not formally defined. This ambiguity makes the theorem's statement unfalsifiable and its contribution unclear. Similarly, Proposition 3 (Informal) is presented as holding "...under standard recovery conditions," but the paper fails to specify which conditions are being referenced or to justify their applicability in this context. Such statements are too vague to constitute a meaningful theoretical guarantee.

In its current form, the paper does not provide the robust, well-defined theory that it promises. The core claims are built on a foundation of "handwaving" rather than concrete, verifiable mathematics.
