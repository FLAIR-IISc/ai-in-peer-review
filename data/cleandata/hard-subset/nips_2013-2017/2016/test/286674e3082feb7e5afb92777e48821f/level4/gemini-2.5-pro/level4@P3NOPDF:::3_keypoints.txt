- Proposes algorithms for graph clustering and cluster comparison with clear, implementable descriptions.  
- Addresses relevant and important problems for the machine learning community.  
- Major weakness: manuscript lacks a unifying motivation and reads like a list of distinct algorithms rather than a cohesive contribution.  
- Authors should revise the introduction to frame a central research problem and explain why these algorithms belong together.  
- Novelty is not convincingly established; methods appear as modifications of existing techniques without sufficient contextualization.  
- Must clearly delineate precise contributions over prior work and expand the related work to highlight unique advantages.  
- The primary novel element seems to be the set of constraints introduced late in the paper; core algorithmic novelty needs stronger argument and validation.  
- Recommend reformulating the narrative to show how each algorithm contributes to the overall solution.  
- For each modified algorithm, explicitly state the exact modification and the practical/theoretical benefits.  
- Strengthen experimental validation with ablation studies on the new constraints and direct empirical comparisons against the baseline algorithms.
