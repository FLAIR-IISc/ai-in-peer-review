- Main contribution: establishes relationships between different clustering distance measures for network models (PFM and SBM).
- Key theoretical result: proves a locally convex–like property showing clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are also close in misclassification distance.
- Empirical support: provides theoretical and experimental validation of the equivalence between distance measures.
- Novelty: results are new but overlap with recent network literature (Peng et al., COLT 2015; Chao et al., arXiv 2015).
- Technical strength: theoretical components are relatively straightforward extensions of established results.
- Applications: mentions potential use for detecting clusterability but offers no formal results or guarantees.
- Presentation: paper is well written and clearly conveys its core ideas.
- Overall assessment: interesting contribution but impact may be limited by simplicity of theory and existence of similar prior work.
