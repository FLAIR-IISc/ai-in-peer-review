This paper establishes a key relationship between different distance metrics for clusterings in network data. Focusing on popular network models, namely the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a "locally convex-like" property for the clustering problem. Specifically, the paper demonstrates that any two clusterings that fit the observed network data equally well (in terms of model parameter estimation) must also be close to each other with respect to standard misclassification distance. This establishes a valuable equivalence between a model-fit-based distance and a clustering-based distance for network data. The authors support their claims with both theoretical proofs and experimental validation.

The primary strength of this work is its novel theoretical contribution, which provides a useful structural property for clustering on networks. The paper is well-written, and the core ideas are explained lucidly.

However, there are a few points that temper the overall contribution. While the results are novel, they follow a line of inquiry with a similar flavor to recent work in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). A more detailed comparison to these related works would help clarify the paper's unique contributions. Furthermore, the theoretical analysis, while sound, appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of well-known results.

Finally, the authors suggest potential applications in detecting "clusterability," which is an interesting direction. However, the paper would be more impactful if it included formal results or a more concrete methodology for this application, as it is currently left as a suggestion for future work.
