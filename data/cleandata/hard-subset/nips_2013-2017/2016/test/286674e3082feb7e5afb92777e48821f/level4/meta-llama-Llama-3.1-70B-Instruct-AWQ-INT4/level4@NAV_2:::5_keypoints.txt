- Provides general concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering techniques.
- Proves these guarantees for multiple generative models without relying on model-specific parameters.
- Poses and answers the novel question of stability of community-detection solutions.
- Frames and proves results in great generality while applying them to popular graphical models.
- Paper is well written, clear, and an easy, enjoyable read.
- Authors clearly explain motivations for their error definitions and proof assumptions.
- Main criticism: proofs depend on spectral algorithms and spectral results that are not tight.
- Spectral methods may be nearly intractable or perform poorly in very sparse graph regimes.
- Overall a strong starting contribution to stability results for community detection with expected further progress.
