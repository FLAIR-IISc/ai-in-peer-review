The paper establishes connections between different clustering distance notions. It studies network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and proves a local convexity–type stability property for clusterings. Specifically, it shows that any two clusterings that fit a model to the observed network data equally well must also be close in misclassification-based distance measures. The work thus identifies an appealing property of clusterings in network data. The equivalence among various clustering distance measures in this context is stated and proved. The paper includes both theoretical results and empirical demonstrations supporting these claims. The contributions are novel, though there are recent works of a similar flavor in the network literature, e.g., Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). Some of the theoretical arguments appear to be relatively direct extensions of established results. As noted by the authors, the framework could be applied to detecting clusterability, but formal results along those lines are not provided. The manuscript is clearly written and conveys the main ideas effectively.
