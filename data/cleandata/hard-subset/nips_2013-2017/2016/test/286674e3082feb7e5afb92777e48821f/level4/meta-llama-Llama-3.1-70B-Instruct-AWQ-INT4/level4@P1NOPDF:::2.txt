The paper explores the relationship between different clustering distances, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM). It establishes a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well are also close in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This result provides a valuable insight into the properties of clusterings in network data.

A key contribution of the paper is the equivalence property between different distance measures for clusterings in network data, which is both stated and rigorously proved. The authors provide both theoretical and experimental validation of their results, making the paper a comprehensive contribution to the field. While the results are novel, it is worth noting that similar ideas have been explored recently in the network literature, such as in the works of Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical aspects of the paper build upon well-known results, and although the extensions are straightforward, they are still valuable contributions.

One potential application of this work is in detecting clusterability, as mentioned in the paper. However, the authors do not provide formal results in this regard, which is a notable omission. Nevertheless, the paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the core ideas in a clear and concise manner. Overall, the paper makes a solid contribution to the understanding of clustering distances in network data, and its results are likely to be of interest to researchers in the field.
