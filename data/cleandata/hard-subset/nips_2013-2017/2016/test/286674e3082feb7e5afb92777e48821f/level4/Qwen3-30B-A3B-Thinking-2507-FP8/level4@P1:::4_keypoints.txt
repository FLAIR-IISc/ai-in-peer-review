- Proposes a general model-free clustering-stability theorem: if clustering C fits a model class M well, any other good-fitting clustering C' must be close to C.  
- Instantiates the framework for two block models (PFM and SBM) and shows required quantities can be computed from data.  
- Shows model-free guarantees can be derived from existing model-based recovery results, bridging the two approaches.  
- Framework is elegant, generalizable, and technically well-developed with clear definitions and proofs.  
- Provides computable stability bounds applicable to real data and supports them with experiments.  
- Treats PFM and SBM separately despite hierarchical inclusion (PFM ⊇ DC-SBM ⊇ SBM), introducing unnecessary complexity.  
- Choice of Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM and use of weighted ME vs standard ME hinder direct comparison and seem arbitrary.  
- Guarantee conditions are often too restrictive in practice; experiments show SBM conditions are almost always violated (δ > δ₀), making guarantees ineffective.  
- Claim about large eigengap causing sensitivity of PFM to small perturbations is insufficiently supported and practicality of such sensitivity is not addressed.  
- Related work is incomplete: omits key model-free results (e.g., Balcan et al. on perturbation resilience/approximation stability and Kumar & Kannan 2010).  
- Framework assumes fixed cluster counts and does not handle C and C' having different numbers of clusters, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Technical inconsistencies reduce clarity and credibility (undefined notation in Proposition 10; ambiguous norm in Assumption 5).  
- Overall: important theoretical contribution and useful connection to block-model literature, but restrictive conditions, incomplete related work, and practical limitations weaken impact.  
- Recommendation: clarify technical points, address SBM practicality, justify modeling choices, and extend to variable cluster counts to improve applicability.
