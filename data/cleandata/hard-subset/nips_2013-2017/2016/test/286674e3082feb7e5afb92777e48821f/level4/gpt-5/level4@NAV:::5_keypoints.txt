- Provides general concentration guarantees for community detection based on spectral clustering across many problem types.
- Proves these guarantees for multiple generative models without using model-specific parameters.
- The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular research direction.
- Raises a compelling question about the stability of community detection solutions.
- Results are stated and proved in great generality while also applied to specific, popular graphical models.
- Paper is very well written, clear, and an enjoyable read.
- Authors clearly explain the motivation behind their error definitions and the assumptions for proofs.
- Main substantive criticism: proofs depend on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can be nearly intractable for very sparse graphs.
- Nonetheless, this is a strong first step toward a body of stability results likely to spur further progress.
