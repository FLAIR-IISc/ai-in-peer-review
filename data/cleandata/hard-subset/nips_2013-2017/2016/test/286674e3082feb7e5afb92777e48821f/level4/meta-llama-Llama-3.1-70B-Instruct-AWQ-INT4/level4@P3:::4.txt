This paper presents a novel framework for providing theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well, without assuming that the data comes from a specific model. The stability is measured in terms of the distance between C and any other C' that fit the same data well. The authors provide specific examples of using this framework for two block models, PFM and SBM, and all the quantities involved in the guarantees can be computed explicitly from the data.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and understand the technical details. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work and sets the stage for the rest of the paper. The background section provides a concise overview of the necessary concepts and notation, and the main theorem is presented in a clear and concise manner.

The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-presented, and the results are clearly summarized in the text and figures.
