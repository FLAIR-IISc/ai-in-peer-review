- Presents novel, distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering not relying on generative assumptions like SBM.  
- Central thesis: any partition that fits the observed graph well implies all equally good partitions are structurally similar.  
- Tackles an important problem and provides compelling theoretical guarantees in a distribution-free setting.  
- Paper is well-written and clear; main text is self-contained despite some content moved to supplementary material.  
- Theoretical results are well-supported with commentary and illustrations; minor notational choice (use of hats for observed quantities) could be clearer.  
- Practical limitation: on some real-world datasets the framework requires graph smoothing to recover structure, whereas standard algorithms do not.  
- Theoretical limitation 1: main PFM result depends on a non-standard distance between clusterings whose naturalness is questionable.  
- Theoretical limitation 2: reliance on a "proxy" model (reconstruction via normalized Laplacian) enforces a strong, specific definition of a “good” clustering.  
- Concern that the proxy/model-based restrictions may explain the framework’s struggles on certain real-world examples.
