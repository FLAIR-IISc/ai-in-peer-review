This paper presents a general template for theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C, which fits well with the data. Stability is determined by measuring the distance between C and any other C, where C and other C's fit well with the same data. This paper also provides specific examples of using templates for two block models, PFM and SBM, and all the quantities involved in the guarantee can be clearly calculated from the data. Furthermore, it shows the correct recovery guarantee for models using the stability results obtained from this template. However, the simulations suggest that the results are not very useful due to the conditions of the given theorems or the goodness fit.

1) This paper considers the problem of providing a general template for theoretical guarantees for clustering without assuming that it is taken from a specific model. Its goal is to obtain guarantees using quantities that can be calculated from the data and compared with the outputs of the clustering algorithm. Providing such a model-free theoretical guarantee for clustering is important for both theoretical and practical purposes.

2) Since PFM includes DC-SBM as a special class, which in turn includes SBM, it is not clear why the two fields (PFM and SBM) are considered separately. Since spectral clustering works well for all models, why can't the same model estimator be used? In particular, it is not clear why the Laplacian is used for PFM, while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Also, the results for PFM are for weighted ME, while for SBM it is in terms of ME. How are they comparable to each other? Furthermore, why are the results for DC-SBM not given?

3) In proposal 10, what is the definition of < M', M >F?

4) In assumption 5: is it ||L^{hat}^2 - L^2||F or ||L^{hat} - L||F^2? If the first, what is the idea behind squaring the Laplacian?

5) This paper mainly focuses on disjoint clusters. The techniques/recipes used in this paper have been extended to other models outside the block model for which spectral clustering works well. For example, spectral clustering is known to work well for hierarchical clustering and also provides theoretical guarantees ("Spectral Clustering for Noise Threshold", S. Balakrishnan et al. 2011). How will the template work in this case?

6) Evaluation metrics are considered, and the estimators considered in both PFM and SBM are limited to C and C', where the number of clusters is the same. Different clustering algorithms can potentially give different numbers of clusters. For example, let's say the eigenvalue of A for C can propose K clusters and the Laplacian of A for C' can propose K' = K + 1 clusters, where all the clusters of C and C' are the same, except for one cluster that is divided into two parts. C' is still "close" to C, but it cannot be captured by the given structure.

7) Many model-free results are not reflected in the related work section: for example: "Spectral Rules and Clustering with K-Means Algorithms", Kumar and Kannan, 2010. "Spectral Clustering under Perturbation Resilience", Balkan et al. 2012. "Spectral Clustering under Approximation Resilience", Balkan et al. 2013. How does the concept of stability considered in this work relate to these works? In particular, how does stability in this paper relate to the measured stability for the perturbations of the centers for the two clusterings?

8) The perturbations that the simulations tolerate for PFM are very small. Line 252-253 generally says that "large eigenvalues distinguish PFM (G, C) from PFM (G, C0), even for very small perturbations", which does not explain the statement well. It is not clear why this is so. On the other hand, the political blog data is smoothed by increasing the power, which will further increase the distance of the eigenvalues, but does it seem to help increase stability in this case? Also, if such small perturbations can affect model fit, it is not very useful for practical purposes (which is one of the main selling points, because the quantities can actually be calculated from the data). Line 248-250 says that SBM guarantees seem to be very difficult to fulfill, which is unfortunate.

9) The caption of figure 1 mentions "For SBM, \delta is always greater than \delta0". Which of these subplots is being referred to? If SBM is not plotted, it should be clearly mentioned in the caption.

10) In the theorems of PFM, the guarantees are for weighted ME, while the perturbations in the experiment are for ME. Small details:

1) The labels of the axes are missing in figure 1.

3) For PFM, B, C, and Y are used with different definitions and later for SBM in 3.2. This can be generally confusing. Since there are many notations in the paper, it is good to maintain consistency. For example, Y{\orthogonals} and CDs can be used instead of B and C respectively for PFM. Also, YZ is not required for the estimator of SBM. It is better to use the expression YZ directly in proposal 8. Similarly, using \lamda^L and \lambda^B for \lambda in PFM and SBM will clearly understand which eigenvalue is being talked about.
