- Introduces a novel model-free framework for theoretical guarantees in graph clustering, removing reliance on data-generation assumptions.  
- Core contribution: a Generic Theorem showing that two clusterings that both fit the observed graph well must be close in weighted misclassification error.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, repurposing model-based recovery results to yield model-free guarantees.  
- All bound quantities (ε, δ, δ₀) are computable from the data and clustering, enabling direct empirical validation.  
- Extends Meilă’s distance-equivalence ideas to a model-free setting while avoiding restrictive prior assumptions.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous and well-motivated, with elegant proofs (e.g., Davis–Kahan perturbation analysis).  
- Computational nature of the guarantees is a significant practical advance over unverifiable model-based methods.  
- Experiments validate the framework: δ thresholds predict stability on PFM data; SBM bounds are conservative as expected.  
- Presentation is clear with precise definitions, contextualization of related work, and accessible algorithm descriptions.  
- The model-free perspective is original and significant, reframing recovery as model fitting rather than assuming a generative model.  
- Theorem 11 and clusterability discussion provide concrete improvements and directions for future work.  
- Weakness: SBM instantiation yields impractical bounds in some settings (e.g., vacuous δ for LFR data) and needs clearer guidance on when SBM is useful.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to simulated data and Political Blogs; lacks a real-world heterogeneous-degree network demonstration where model-based guarantees fail.  
- Weakness: Minor misattribution in the related work discussion (Peng et al. cited incorrectly), which slightly weakens originality claims.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong, actionable contribution with computable model-free guarantees despite SBM limitations.
