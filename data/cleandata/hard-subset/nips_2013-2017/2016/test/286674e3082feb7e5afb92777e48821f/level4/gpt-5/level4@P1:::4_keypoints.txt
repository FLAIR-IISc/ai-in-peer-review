- Summary: Proposes a model-free framework that fits a model M(G,C) to graph+clustering and uses a data-driven goodness-of-fit ε to certify that any other good-fit clustering C′ is close to C under spectral separation conditions.

- Summary: Instantiates the framework for PFM (Laplacian, wME) and SBM (adjacency, ME), proving computable bounds via Davis–Kahan and connecting model-free stability to model-based recovery results.

- Summary: Experiments on synthetic PFM/LFR graphs and political blogs show PFM-based bounds certify stability for very small perturbations, while SBM-based bounds are rarely informative.

- Strength: Timely question—certifying clustering quality without assuming the data-generating model.

- Strength: Provides a clean, computable blueprint applicable to multiple block-model families with data-driven quantities.

- Strength: Direct technical development using standard spectral tools with clear links to prior model-based results.

- Strength: Novel idea of turning model-based recovery theorems into model-free guarantees.

- Strength: Empirical illustrations demonstrate the certification pipeline in practice.

- Weakness 1: Practical value concerns—the computable conditions (ε/eigengap) appear too stringent, tolerating only very small perturbations in experiments.

- Weakness 2: Uneven treatment of models—PFM uses Laplacian/wME while SBM uses adjacency/ME; omission of DC-SBM is notable and hinders comparability.

- Weakness 3: Notational gap—Proposition 10 uses ⟨M̂, M⟩F without explicit definition (Frobenius inner product unclear).

- Weakness 4: Ambiguity in Assumption 5—unclear whether the fit uses ||L̂2 − L2||F or ||L̂ − L||2F and why Laplacians are squared in the criterion.

- Weakness 5: Scope question—do techniques extend beyond block models to other spectral-clustering settings and what goodness-of-fit would be appropriate?

- Weakness 6: Framework assumes equal K—does not handle differing numbers of clusters (refinement/coarsening or partial matching).

- Weakness 7: Related work omissions—missing positioning relative to model-free/stability literature (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and perturbation/resilience notions.

- Weakness 8: Experiment interpretation unclear—need explanation why large eigengap leads to sensitivity in some steps yet powering A (which increases eigengaps) appears to improve stability.

- Weakness 9: Figure 1 caption ambiguity—statement “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” is unclear which subplot this refers to; SBM results may be omitted.

- Weakness 10: Metric mismatch—PFM theorems bound wME but experiments measure ME; need discussion of implications and degree-weighted perturbation behavior.

- Minor: Figure 1 lacks axis labels and units—add for interpretability.

- Minor: Clarify model statement—Aij entries independent conditional on cluster assignments.

- Minor: Notation overload—B, C, Y reused across PFM/SBM; suggest clearer disambiguation and explicit notation in propositions.

- Minor: Section placement—Section 4.4 reads like related work on stability/resilience and should be moved under Related Work.

- Evaluation (Quality): Technically sound with elementary proofs but conditions appear stringent, limiting empirical utility.

- Evaluation (Clarity): Generally organized but hampered by notational inconsistencies, missing definitions, and poorly labeled figures.

- Evaluation (Originality): Model-free validation angle and inversion of model-based results are original and interesting.

- Evaluation (Significance): Potentially impactful if guarantees can be relaxed or tightened; current applicability is limited, especially for SBM.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel model-free validation framework, conceptual bridge from model-based recovery to certification, and clean spectral analysis likely to inspire follow-up work.

- Arguments against acceptance: Guarantees often too weak empirically, uneven model treatment and omission of DC-SBM, and underdeveloped related-work positioning and clarity.

- Overall: Promising core idea but requires substantial revision to strengthen guarantees, unify treatments across models, expand related work, and clarify experiments and notation.
