This paper establishes an important relationship between different measures of clustering quality for network data. Focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a property analogous to local convexity for clustering. They demonstrate that any two clusterings providing an equally good fit to the model parameters are necessarily close to one another as measured by misclassification distance. This equivalence between a model-fit criterion and a misclassification metric for clusterings on network data is a valuable finding. The paper supports its theoretical claims with experimental validation.

The contributions appear novel, though they follow a recent line of inquiry in the network literature, with related work including Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). A limitation is that the theoretical analysis appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of well-established results. Furthermore, while the authors suggest potential applications in detecting clusterability, formal results in this direction are not provided.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents its core ideas lucidly.
