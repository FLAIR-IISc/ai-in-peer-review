- Presents novel, distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering (no stochastic block model assumption).  
- Central thesis: a stability guarantee—any partition with similarly good fit must be structurally close to a found partition.  
- Addresses a fundamental problem; manuscript is well-written, largely self-contained, with technical details in the supplement.  
- Results are interesting, well-commented, and effectively illustrated.  
- Minor presentation issue: unconventional use of hat notation for observed quantities.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing before structure is recoverable, while classical algorithms can recover structure on raw graphs.  
- Theoretical limitation: main PFM result uses a non-standard clustering distance whose advantages over standard metrics are not fully convincing.  
- The analysis relies on a "reconstruction" approach—evaluating clusterings by how well a reconstructed model approximates the normalized Laplacian—which constrains the notion of a “good” clustering.  
- Trade-off concern: the framework’s strength comes from this specific approximation lens but may limit broader practical impact when other cluster-quality notions matter.
