This paper presents a generic template for deriving theoretical guarantees for the stability of a given clustering C. The proposed framework assesses stability by bounding the distance between C and any other clustering C' that also fits the data well. The authors demonstrate this template by applying it to two specific block models, the Planted Fragmentation Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), showing that all quantities involved in the guarantees can be computed explicitly from the data. Furthermore, these stability results are leveraged to establish exact recovery guarantees for the models. However, the simulation results suggest that the proposed goodness-of-fit measure or the theoretical conditions may be too restrictive, potentially limiting the practical utility of the results.

**Major Points**

1) The paper addresses the important problem of providing generic, model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering. The goal of developing guarantees based on quantities computable directly from the data and the clustering outputs is significant for both theory and practice.

2) The paper treats the PFM and SBM cases separately, which is confusing since PFM includes the Degree-Corrected SBM (DC-SBM) as a special case, which in turn includes the SBM. Given that spectral clustering works well for all these models, why not use the same estimation procedure? Specifically, it is unclear why the graph Laplacian is used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Furthermore, the theoretical results for PFM are expressed in terms of weighted Misclassification Error (ME), while the SBM results use the standard ME. The authors should clarify how these two error metrics compare. Finally, given the relationship between the models, why are results for DC-SBM not presented?

3) In Proposition 10, the notation `<M', M>_F` is used without definition. Please clarify if this is the Frobenius inner product.

4) In Assumption 5, is the term `||L̂² - L²||_F` or `||L̂ - L||_F²`? The current notation is ambiguous. If it is the former, please provide some intuition for squaring the Laplacians before taking the difference.

5) The analysis focuses on disjoint clusters derived from block models. Could the proposed template be extended to other settings where spectral clustering is effective, such as hierarchical clustering? For instance, how would this framework apply to cases with theoretical guarantees like those in "Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering" (Balakrishnan et al., 2011)?

6) The proposed framework, including the evaluation metric and estimators, is restricted to comparing clusterings C and C' that have the same number of clusters. However, different algorithms or model selection criteria can yield different numbers of clusters. For instance, the eigenvalues of the adjacency matrix might suggest K clusters, while the Laplacian eigenvalues suggest K' = K+1. In such a scenario, C' might be very similar to C (e.g., differing only by a single cluster being split), but this notion of "closeness" cannot be captured by the current framework. This seems to be a significant limitation.

7) The related work section could be strengthened by discussing other model-free stability results. For example, the paper does not mention work such as "Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm" (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), "Clustering under Perturbation Resilience" (Balcan et al., 2012), or "Clustering under Approximation Stability" (Balcan et al., 2013). How does the notion of stability proposed here relate to the concepts in these papers, particularly those based on the perturbation of cluster centers?

8) The simulation results raise concerns about the practical applicability of the proposed guarantees. For the PFM, the tolerated perturbations are extremely small. The paper states that a "large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C_0) even for very small perturbations" (lines 252-253) but does not adequately explain this phenomenon. This seems counter-intuitive, especially since for the political blog data, the authors raise the adjacency matrix to a power—a process that typically amplifies the eigengap—and this appears to *improve* stability. Could the authors clarify this apparent contradiction? If the goodness-of-fit measure is sensitive to such minor perturbations, it calls into question the practical utility of the guarantees, which is a major selling point of the paper. Furthermore, the authors note that the conditions for the SBM guarantee are "very hard to satisfy" (lines 248-250), which is also discouraging.

9) The caption for Figure 1 states, "For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0." It is not clear which subplot this refers to. If the SBM results are not shown in the figure, this should be explicitly stated.

10) There appears to be a mismatch between the theory and experiments for the PFM. The theoretical guarantees are for weighted ME, but the experiments evaluate performance using the standard ME. Please clarify or correct this inconsistency.

**Minor Points**

1) The axes in Figure 1 are unlabeled.

2) Line 44: The statement on block models should be more precise. The entries `A_ij` for `i > j` are independent conditional on the cluster assignments of nodes `i` and `j`.

3) The paper reuses notation (e.g., B, C, Y, λ) for different quantities in the PFM and SBM sections. This is confusing, especially in a notation-heavy paper. It would be better to use distinct notation. For instance, `Y_⊥` and `CD` could be used for the quantities currently denoted by B and C for the PFM. For the SBM estimator, the intermediate `YZ` term could be eliminated by substituting its expression directly into Proposition 8. Similarly, using `λ^L` and `λ^B` would help distinguish between eigenvalues of the Laplacian and the block probability matrix.

4) Section 4.4 seems more appropriate as a subsection within the Related Work section.
