This paper establishes very general concentration guarantees, derived from spectral clustering techniques, for all types of community detection problems. The authors prove these guarantees under multiple generative models without requiring access to any of the models’ parameters. The work is interesting, timely, and pushes a popular area in an important direction. It raises a compelling question about the stability of solutions in community detection and addresses it with results stated and proved in broad generality, while also demonstrating their applicability to several widely used graphical models.

The paper is very well written—clear, accessible, and enjoyable to read. The authors carefully motivate their choices of error definitions and the assumptions needed for their proofs.

My main criticism, which the authors themselves acknowledge in the conclusion, is that the proofs rely on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can be nearly intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems in the very sparse regime. Despite this limitation, the paper represents a strong first step toward a theory of stability for community detection, and I expect it will spur further progress in the near future.
