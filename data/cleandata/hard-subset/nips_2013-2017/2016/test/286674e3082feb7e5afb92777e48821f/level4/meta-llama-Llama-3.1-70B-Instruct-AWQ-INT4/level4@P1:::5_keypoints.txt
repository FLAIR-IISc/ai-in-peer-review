- Provides general concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering techniques.
- Proves guarantees across different generative models without using model parameters.
- Poses and rigorously analyzes the stability of community-detection solutions in great generality.
- Introduces a framework to obtain theoretical guarantees for model-based clustering without assuming a specific data-generating process.
- Main results are Theorems 4 and 9, giving stability guarantees for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM) instantiated from a generic theorem.
- Also offers alternative guarantees derived from existing model-based recovery theorems to obtain model-free results.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed method to existing approaches on several datasets.
- Empirical results show the method provides stability guarantees even when data deviates from the assumed model.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough analysis and discussion of implications for network data.
- Primary weakness: reliance on spectral algorithms and results, which may not be tight or tractable for very sparse graphs.
- Authors acknowledge the sparsity limitation and should discuss or address it in revisions.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with important implications; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 9/10).
