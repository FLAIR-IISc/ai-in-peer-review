Review- This paper offers very general concentration guarantees built on spectral clustering techniques across all types of community detection problems. The authors prove these guarantees for multiple generative models without using any parameters from those models. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular line of research in an important direction. The paper raises a compelling question about the stability of solutions for community detection. The results are stated and proved in great generality, while also being focused and applied to specific, popular graphical models of interest. The paper is very well written and an easy, enjoyable read. The authors clearly explain the motivation behind nearly all of their error definitions and the assumptions required for their proofs. My only substantive criticism—also acknowledged by the authors in their conclusion—is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and are even almost intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems in very sparse graphs. That said, this is a strong first step toward a body of stability results for community detection, which I hope and expect will progress further in the near future.
