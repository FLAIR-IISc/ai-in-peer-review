This paper presents broadly applicable concentration bounds for spectral clustering across a range of community detection problems. A key strength is the model-agnostic nature of the guarantees, achieving results without relying on specific parameters of the underlying generative models. The work is both interesting and relevant, representing a significant advance in a prominent research area. The authors tackle a compelling question regarding the stability of solutions in community detection, successfully formulating and proving highly general results with targeted applications to widely-studied graphical models. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clear, with thorough justification for all defined error metrics and assumptions. The primary limitation, acknowledged by the authors, lies in the reliance on spectral algorithms and associated bounds that can be loose and computationally challenging, particularly in the sparse graph regime. Nevertheless, this work establishes a strong foundation for future research into stability results for community detection, and promises to be a valuable stepping stone for further progress in the field.
