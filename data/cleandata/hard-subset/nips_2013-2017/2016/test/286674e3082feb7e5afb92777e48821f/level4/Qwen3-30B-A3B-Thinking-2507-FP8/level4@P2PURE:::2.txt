

The paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics for clustering in network data. It analyzes network models including the Preference-Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), proving a locally convex-like property for clustering. Specifically, it demonstrates that any two clusterings which estimate the model parameters equally well must also be close under misclassification distance measures. This equivalence between different clustering distance metrics for network data is rigorously stated and proved. The paper provides both theoretical and experimental validation for its results. While the core findings are novel, recent related work exists (e.g., Peng, Sun, and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou, arXiv 2015). The theoretical contributions are largely straightforward extensions of established results. Although the paper notes potential applications in clusterability detection, it lacks formal results in this direction. The paper is well-written and clearly conveys its central ideas.
