- Presents very general concentration guarantees for community detection based on spectral clustering.
- Establishes these guarantees across multiple generative models without using model parameters.
- Raises a compelling question about the stability of community detection solutions.
- States and proves results in broad generality while applying them to several widely studied graphical models.
- The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular research area.
- The paper is very well written and easy to read; authors clearly explain motivations for error definitions and assumptions.
- Main substantive criticism: reliance on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight.
- Spectral-based bounds can be nearly intractable in very sparse regimes for many important problems.
- Still a strong first step toward stability results for community detection and likely to spur further progress.
