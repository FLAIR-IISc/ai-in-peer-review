- Proposes distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that do not assume a specific generative model (e.g., SBM).
- Core idea: if a clustering fits the observed graph well (by their criterion), any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.
- Addresses an important problem with novel and interesting theoretical contributions.
- Paper is reasonably clear and readable despite supplementary material; minor notation critique about using hats for observed quantities.
- Results are well-commented and illustrated but have practical limitations shown in experiments.
- Experimental finding: some real-world graphs require smoothing for the framework to recover structure, whereas classical algorithms can recover ground truth without smoothing.
- Limitation 1: main PFM result uses a non-standard distance between clusterings whose naturalness is questionable.
- Limitation 2: framework compares a reconstructed proxy model (e.g., PFM) to the observed graph rather than to any true generative model, constraining the notion of a “good” clustering to normalized-Laplacian approximation.
- Reviewer questions the impact of these modeling restrictions given the framework’s limitations on real data.
