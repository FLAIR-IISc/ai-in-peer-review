- Novel theoretical results for graph clustering that avoid generative assumptions and focus on the observed graph's inherent structure.
- Core claim: if a clustering yields a good fit to the graph, any alternative clustering with comparable fit should be similar to it.
- Provides distribution-free guarantees for graph clustering and addresses an important problem.
- Paper is clear and comprehensible despite some material being moved to the supplement due to page limits.
- Notational concern: using "hats" to denote observed values (e.g., the Laplacian) is awkward.
- Results are well-explained and illustrated with examples.
- Empirical limitation: for some real-world data, graph smoothing is required to recover meaningful structure under the theory, whereas traditional algorithms can recover structure without smoothing.
- Limitation 1: the main PFM result uses a non-standard distance metric between clusterings, which the authors claim is intuitive but this is debatable.
- Limitation 2: the framework relies on a "proxy" model (reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing it to the graph) rather than the model that produced the clustering, restricting the notion of a good clustering and limiting applicability.
