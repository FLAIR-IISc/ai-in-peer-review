- Goal of a model-free graph clustering framework is admirable but the contribution is unconvincing and not novel.
- Paper reads as a set of intuitive observations rather than a rigorous theoretical advance.
- Mathematical rigor is lacking; key statements are unsupported by formal proofs or quantitative backing.
- Theorem 1 is vague: “fits” and “closeness” of a graph to a cluster are undefined and make the theorem uninterpretable.
- Proposition 3 is informal and cites “standard recovery conditions” without specifying them.
- No clear, formal definition of what constitutes a “good” clustering in the model-free setting.
- Modularity is mentioned but not formalized as an objective or evaluation metric.
- Theoretical guarantees are largely heuristic and need concrete bounds and precise assumptions.
- Experimental evaluation is limited, uses arbitrary datasets, and does not show clear improvement over established methods.
- Missing comparisons to strong baselines and lack of experiments on synthetic graphs with known communities.
- Requests for revision: formalize key concepts, define clustering quality, strengthen theoretical guarantees, expand experiments, and clarify novelty.
- Recommendation: reject in current form, but potentially publishable after substantial revisions addressing the above.
