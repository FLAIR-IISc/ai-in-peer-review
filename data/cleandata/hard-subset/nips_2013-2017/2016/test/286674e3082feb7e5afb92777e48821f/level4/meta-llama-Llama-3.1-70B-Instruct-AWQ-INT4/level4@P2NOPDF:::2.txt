This paper establishes a relationship between different distances in clustering, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The authors prove a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well are also close in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This result provides a valuable insight into the properties of clusterings in network data.

The paper presents a key property of equivalence between different measures of distance in clusterings for network data, accompanied by both theoretical and experimental validations. Although the results are novel, similar work has recently been published in the network literature, such as Peng et al. (COLT, 2015) and Chao et al. (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical components of the paper are relatively straightforward extensions of well-established results.

The authors mention potential applications in detecting clusterability, but unfortunately, the paper does not provide formal results in this regard. Nevertheless, the paper is well-written, and the authors effectively convey the core ideas in a clear and concise manner. Overall, the paper presents an interesting contribution to the field of network clustering, although its impact may be limited by the simplicity of the theoretical results and the existence of similar prior work.
