- Proposes a generic, model-free framework giving theoretical guarantees for clustering stability based on data fit rather than generative assumptions.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM) with explicit, computable guarantees.  
- Empirical results indicate the derived bounds are often difficult to satisfy, limiting immediate practical applicability.  
- Core contribution: providing recovery/stability guarantees without strict model assumptions and connecting model-free and model-based recovery.  
- Unclear rationale for using different estimators and distance metrics for PFM (Laplacian) vs SBM (adjacency); no justification for not using a consistent estimator like spectral clustering.  
- Absence of results for Degree-Corrected SBM (DC-SBM) is a notable gap given PFM’s generality.  
- Technical error: Definition of <M', M>_F is missing in Proposition 10.  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5 about whether the norm is ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2; need rationale if Laplacians are squared.  
- Framework restricted to disjoint clusters; should consider extensions to overlapping or hierarchical cluster structures.  
- Framework assumes clusterings have the same number of clusters; needs to address cases where algorithms output different cluster counts (e.g., splits).  
- Related work section omits important model-free stability literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and needs to relate proposed stability to perturbation resilience and approximation stability.  
- Simulations suggest PFM is highly sensitive to small perturbations (eigengap issues); this claim needs deeper analysis.  
- The smoothing operation on political blog data is counterintuitive—smoothing increases eigenvalue gaps yet improves stability—and requires explanation.  
- SBM conditions are consistently unmet in experiments; this undermines practical claims and should be addressed.  
- Figure 1 needs clarification (whether SBM data is plotted, statement about δ > δ0) and is missing axis labels.  
- Notation inconsistencies (redefinition of B, C, Y) reduce clarity; recommend consistent eigenvalue subscripts (λ^L, λ^B).  
- Subsection 4.4 belongs in related work rather than current placement; line 44 should state A_ij (i>j) independence is conditional on cluster assignments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers a novel theoretical framework with clear guarantees, and connects model-free to model-based results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: bounds appear overly conservative and impractical, comparisons to existing model-free results are insufficient, technical issues and limited scope require substantial revision.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject but encourage revision—strengthen theoretical justification, expand empirical evaluation, fix technical gaps, and better situate work in the literature.
