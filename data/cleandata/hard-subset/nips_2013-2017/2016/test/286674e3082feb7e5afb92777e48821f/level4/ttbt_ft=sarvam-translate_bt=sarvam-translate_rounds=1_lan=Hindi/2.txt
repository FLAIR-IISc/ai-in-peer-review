This paper describes a relationship between different clustering distances. It considers network models such as Preference Frame Models (PFMs) and Stochastic Block Models (SBMs) and proves a locally convex-like property for clustering. It is shown that for any two clusterings that equally estimate the parameters of models fitted to network data, they are also close to each other in terms of misclassification distance measures. This paper describes a good property of clustering in network data. The paper describes and proves the property of equivalence between different distance measures of clustering for network data. The results described in this paper provide both theoretical and experimental verification. The results presented in this paper are new, but there have been some similar works in network literature recently, such as Peng, Sun, and Zenety (COLT, 2015), Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical parts of this paper are also quite simple extensions of well-known results. As mentioned in this paper, this work may have some applications in determining clusterability, but formal results in that regard were not provided in this paper. This paper is well-written and explains the core ideas of this paper quite clearly.
