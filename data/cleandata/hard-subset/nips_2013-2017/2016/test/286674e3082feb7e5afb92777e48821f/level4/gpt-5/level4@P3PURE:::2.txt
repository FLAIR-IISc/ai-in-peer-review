This paper studies the relationship between different distance measures on clusterings in network models. Focusing on the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), it establishes a locally convex-like property for clustering: any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters from the observed network data equally well must be close to each other under misclassification-based distance measures. In other words, the paper proves an equivalence across multiple distance measures over clusterings for network data and shows that comparable model fit implies proximity in clustering assignments.

The paper provides both theoretical results and empirical validations supporting these claims. The contributions are novel, although there has been recent work of a similar flavor in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (arXiv, 2015). Moreover, the theoretical arguments appear to be relatively straightforward extensions of well-known results. As noted by the authors, the framework may have applications to detecting clusterability, but the paper does not provide formal results along those lines.

Overall, the paper is well written and presents its core ideas clearly.
