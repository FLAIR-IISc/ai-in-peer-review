- Establishes relationships between different distance metrics for comparing clusterings of network data.  
- Focuses on Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), proving a locally convex–like property for clustering solutions.  
- Shows that clusterings that fit data equally well (in parameter estimation) are close under various misclassification-based distance measures.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and experimental validation demonstrating equivalence between these distance measures for network clustering evaluation.  
- Results are novel but related recent work exists (Peng, Sun & Zanetti COLT 2015; Chao et al. ArXiv 2015).  
- Theoretical contributions build on well-established prior results in the field.  
- Proposed applications to detecting clusterability are suggested but lack formal supporting results in the paper.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains core concepts.
