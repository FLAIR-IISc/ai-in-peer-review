Establishes connections between different clustering distance notions.  
Studies network models including the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
Proves a local convexity–type stability property for clusterings in these models.  
Shows that any two clusterings that fit the observed network data equally well must be close in misclassification-based distance measures.  
Identifies an appealing stability/equivalence property of clusterings in network data.  
Formally states and proves the equivalence among various clustering distance measures in this context.  
Provides both theoretical results and empirical demonstrations supporting the claims.  
Contributions are novel, though related recent works exist (e.g., Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015).  
Some theoretical arguments appear to be relatively direct extensions of established results.  
Notes that the framework could be applied to detecting clusterability, but no formal results are provided.  
Manuscript is clearly written and effectively conveys the main ideas.
