- Proposes a framework for correctness guarantees of graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model by focusing on goodness-of-fit between clustering and observed data.  
- Shows that any alternative clustering with comparable fit must be structurally similar to the proposed clustering.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), connecting model-free and model-based recovery guarantees.  
- Strengthens some existing model-based results through the model-free perspective.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear problem setup, background, main theorems, and deferred proofs in the supplement.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with precise definitions, assumptions, and computable quantities (avoiding undetermined constants).  
- Experiments provide some validation but are limited in scope and dataset diversity.  
- Presentation is dense and can be difficult to follow; more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples are needed.  
- The connection between the general theorem and specific PFM/SBM instantiations is not always immediately clear.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive but would benefit from a more critical comparison and nuanced discussion (e.g., discussion of [16] feels dismissive).  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical soundness, novel framework linking model-free and model-based approaches, precise definitions, and potential for broader impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, dense exposition hindering accessibility, and unclear practical gains over existing methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand experiments with diverse datasets and state-of-the-art comparisons; improve exposition with intuitive explanations and examples; provide a more balanced, nuanced related-work discussion.
