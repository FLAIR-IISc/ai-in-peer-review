- Paper aims to develop a model-free theoretical framework for graph clustering but the results appear naive and unconvincing.
- Authors claim a new theory, but much argumentation reads as hand-waving rather than rigorous development.
- Theorem 1 uses vague phrasing (“fits G well is close to C”) with no formal definition, making it unclear.
- Proposition 3 invokes “under standard recovery conditions” without specifying those conditions.
- Undefined/imprecise notions and lack of explicit assumptions prevent interpretation, assessment, or verification of claims.
- While a model-free theory would be valuable, the current presentation lacks the precision and rigor to substantiate the contributions.
