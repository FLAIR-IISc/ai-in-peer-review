

The paper establishes a relationship between various distance metrics in clustering. It examines network models including the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), and demonstrates a locally convex-like property for clustering. Specifically, it proves that any two clusterings yielding equally good parameter estimates for the fitted network model must also be close under misclassification distance metrics. This equivalence property of clustering distance measures for network data is rigorously stated and proved. The work provides both theoretical and experimental validation for its claims. While the results are novel, recent related work exists in the literature (e.g., Peng, Sun, and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou, arXiv 2015). The theoretical contributions represent straightforward extensions of established results. Although the paper notes potential applications in clusterability detection, it lacks formal results in this direction. The paper is well-written and clearly conveys its core concepts.
