- Paper proposes a general, data-driven template for theoretical clustering stability guarantees measured as distances between well-fitting clusterings.  
- Conceptually appealing framework, but practical utility of the derived guarantees is questionable based on the simulations.  
- Inconsistent treatment of models: PFM and SBM are handled differently despite PFM encompassing DC-SBM and SBM.  
- Choice of estimators (Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM) is not adequately justified.  
- Guarantees use weighted ME for PFM and unweighted ME for SBM without comparing these measures.  
- Omission of results for the DC-SBM model is a notable gap.  
- Technical ambiguities: Proposition 10’s definition of <M’, M>_F is unclear.  
- Assumption 5 is ambiguous about whether it involves ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2, and the reason for squaring Laplacians is unexplained.  
- Framework is limited to disjoint clusters; extensibility to other clustering settings (e.g., hierarchical clustering) and links to existing spectral guarantees are not discussed.  
- Restrictive requirement that compared clusterings must have the same number of clusters fails to capture realistic scenarios (e.g., K vs K+1 splits).  
- Related work is incomplete; missing references and connections to model-free clustering and other stability/perturbation notions (e.g., Kumar & Kannan, Balcan et al.).  
- Simulation concerns: tested perturbations for PFM may be unrealistically small, smoothing may artificially inflate eigengaps, and small perturbations can overly affect model fit, reducing practical value of guarantees.  
- Authors acknowledge difficulty in satisfying SBM guarantee conditions, further undermining applicability.  
- Presentation and notation issues: Figure 1 caption and axes need clarification/labels; inconsistent notation (B, C, Y) across sections; unnecessary variables (YZ) in SBM estimator; eigenvalues should be distinguished with superscripts; Section 4.4 belongs in Related Work.
