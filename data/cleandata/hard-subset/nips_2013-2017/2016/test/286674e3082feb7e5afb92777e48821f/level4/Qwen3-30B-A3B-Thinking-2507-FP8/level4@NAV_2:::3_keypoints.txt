- Proposes a novel graph-clustering framework that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Derives correctness guarantees from computable statistics of the observed data rather than model assumptions.  
- Bridges model-based clustering results and model-free validation by repurposing model-based analyses for model-free guarantees.  
- Introduces a stability paradigm that validates clusterings via computable quantities, extending community-detection theory.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous mathematical analysis and clear presentation/notation.  
- Theorems 4 and 9 formally connect model-based and model-free guarantees for SBM and PFM, with detailed proofs.  
- Addresses practical computational considerations and demonstrates applicability across synthetic PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, and a political-blogs dataset.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and supplement contains detailed proofs.  
- Significant impact: enables model-free validation of existing clustering algorithms for applications where model assumptions fail.  
- Minor weakness: needs better contextualization relative to recent model-free validation work (e.g., Balcan et al., Airoldi et al.).  
- Minor weakness: could strengthen discussion of specific practical use cases and compare computational complexity to model-based methods.  
- For acceptance: novel, rigorous, and empirically validated framework; against: insufficient distinction from related stability/validation literature and limited concrete practical examples.
