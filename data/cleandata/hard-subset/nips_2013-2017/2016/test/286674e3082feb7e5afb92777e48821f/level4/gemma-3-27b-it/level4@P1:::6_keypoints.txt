- Proposes a model-free framework for providing theoretical guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.
- Demonstrates the approach by deriving model-free guarantees for the SBM and Preference Frame Model (PFM).
- Highlights and formalizes the connection between model-free and model-based recovery results.
- Well-motivated by limitations of existing model-based results and grounded in recent progress on block models and spectral methods.
- Novel contribution: addressing the open problem of model-free guarantees for graph clustering.
- Technically rigorous: clear notation, logical arguments, use of perturbation bounds and spectral graph theory.
- Strong connection to existing work: effectively relates and strengthens model-based results.
- Clear presentation and computability: definitions/theorems are clear and all quantities in theorems are data-computable.
- Applicability concerns: the Generic Theorem’s conditions (e.g., closeness of model fit) may be hard to verify in practice.
- Conservatism: derived bounds (notably for SBM) appear overly conservative and may lack practical usefulness.
- Formality issues: some statements (e.g., Proposition 3 labeled “Informal”) need more rigorous formulation.
- Literature overlap: the relation to existing clustering stability results is not fully clarified or explored.
- Repetition: some arguments are repeated in the conclusions.
- Recommendation: solid and promising theoretical paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to tighten bounds, formalize informal statements, and better situate against stability literature.
