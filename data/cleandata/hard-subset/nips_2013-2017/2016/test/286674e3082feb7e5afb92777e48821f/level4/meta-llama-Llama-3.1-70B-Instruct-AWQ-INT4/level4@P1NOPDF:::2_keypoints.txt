- Studies relationships between different clustering distances for network models (Preference Frame Model and Stochastic Block Model).  
- Proves a locally convex-like property: clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Establishes and rigorously proves an equivalence between different clustering distance measures for network data.  
- Provides both theoretical proofs and experimental validation of the results.  
- Results are novel but related to recent network literature (e.g., Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015).  
- Theoretical developments build on well-known results and are straightforward extensions, yet still valuable.  
- Suggests a potential application to detecting clusterability but does not provide formal results for this.  
- Paper is well-written and explains core ideas clearly and concisely.  
- Overall, a solid contribution likely to be of interest to researchers in clustering and network analysis.
