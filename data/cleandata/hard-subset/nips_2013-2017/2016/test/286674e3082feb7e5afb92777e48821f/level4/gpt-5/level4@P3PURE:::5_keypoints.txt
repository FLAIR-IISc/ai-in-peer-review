- Establishes broad concentration guarantees for community detection derived from spectral clustering techniques.
- Guarantees hold across multiple generative models without requiring access to model parameters.
- Addresses the stability of solutions in community detection with broadly stated and proved results.
- Demonstrates applicability to several widely used graphical models.
- Paper is well written, clear, accessible, and carefully motivated.
- Authors justify their choices of error definitions and the assumptions used in proofs.
- Main criticism: proofs rely on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight.
- Limitation: methods can be nearly intractable in very sparse regimes for important problems.
- Nonetheless, represents a strong first step toward a theory of stability and is likely to spur further progress.
