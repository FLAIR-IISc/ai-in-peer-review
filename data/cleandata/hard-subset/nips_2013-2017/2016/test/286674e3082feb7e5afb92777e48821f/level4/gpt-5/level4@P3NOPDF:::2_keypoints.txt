- Paper studies relationship between parameter-fit quality and partition similarity in network clustering for PFM and SBM, claiming a locally convex-like property: equal-fit clusterings must be close under misclassification distances.
- Formalizes and proves equivalences among several natural clustering distance measures.
- Provides theoretical proofs complemented by aligned experiments.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation and lucid exposition; paper is easy to follow and well motivated.
- Strength: Conceptual contribution gives a geometric view linking near-optimal parameter fits to partition proximity, aiding understanding of stability and identifiability.
- Strength: Results cover both PFM and SBM, suggesting model-agnostic phenomena.
- Strength: Theoretical and empirical validation are presented.

- Concern: Novelty relative to recent related work (Peng–Sun–Zanetti 2015; Chao–Lu–Ma–Zhou 2015) is not fully established; sharper positioning is needed.
- Concern: Theoretical techniques appear to be direct extensions of known methods; unclear which steps are nontrivial beyond standard concentration/coupling/perturbation arguments.
- Concern: Claims about detecting “clusterability” lack formal results, guarantees, or experiments, weakening practical impact.
- Concern: Scope and assumptions (sparsity/density regimes, block separation, degree heterogeneity, asymptotic vs nonasymptotic bounds, robustness to misspecification) are not explicitly discussed.
- Concern: Experiments would be more compelling with side-by-side comparisons of distance measures and stress tests across noise, block number, degree variability, and negative/boundary cases.

- Suggestion: Provide a direct comparison to Peng–Sun–Zanetti and Chao–Lu–Ma–Zhou, highlighting differences in assumptions, metrics, conclusions, and proof techniques.
- Suggestion: Tighten the locally convex-like statement with explicit finite-sample bounds, rates, and dependence on key parameters (n, K, separation), and state whether constants are uniform.
- Suggestion: Add at least one formal, computable clusterability detection result with guarantees, or tone down clusterability claims if infeasible.
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to compare multiple distance measures, vary network regimes (sparse/dense, balanced/unbalanced, degree correction), and include boundary cases.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential extensions beyond PFM/SBM (e.g., degree-corrected or mixed-membership models) or clarify obstacles.
- Suggestion: Connect results to algorithmic practice, explaining implications for why different methods with similar scores yield similar partitions or why consensus clustering works.

- Overall: Clean, well-written contribution that links fit to partition proximity, but appears incremental relative to prior work; needs clearer novelty, tighter formal statements, and at least one concrete application to raise significance.
