The paper establishes a connection among various clustering distance notions. It studies network models, specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), and demonstrates a local convexity-like property for clustering. It shows that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters from the network data equally well are also close in misclassification distance measures. This yields an appealing property of clusterings in network data. The paper formally states and proves an equivalence among different distance measures between clusterings for network data. Both theoretical analysis and experimental evidence are provided to support the stated results. The contributions are novel, though recent work of a similar nature in the network literature includes Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (arXiv, 2015). The theoretical components are also fairly straightforward extensions of well-known results. As noted in the paper, the work could have applications in detecting clusterability, but formal results in that direction are not included. The paper is well written and communicates the core ideas clearly.
