1. Theoretical conditions seem unrealistic: SBM guarantees require extremely small perturbations, undermining practical usefulness.  
2. Simulations show tolerated perturbations are excessively small, contradicting claims that guarantees are "useful" and data-computable.  
3. PFM eigengap claim (that small perturbations produce large differences) lacks justification and conflicts with empirical smoothing results.  
4. Using matrix powers to smooth real data (political blogs) improves stability, suggesting a disconnect between theory and practice.  
5. Unclear model hierarchy: why treat PFM and SBM separately when PFM generalizes DC-SBM/SBM.  
6. Choice of matrices/estimators (Laplacian for PFM, adjacency for SBM) is unexplained and inconsistent with spectral clustering practice.  
7. Omission of DC-SBM results weakens the claimed generality of the framework.  
8. Assumption 5 is ambiguous about whether the norm is ||L̂² - L²||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F² and needs intuition for squaring Laplacians.  
9. Proposition 10 lacks a clear definition of the inner product ⟨M', M⟩_F.  
10. Framework assumes same cluster count for C and C', excluding near-identical clusterings with different K (e.g., K vs K+1).  
11. Related work omits key model-free stability results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and should contrast the paper’s stability notion with perturbation-resilience/approximation stability.  
12. Framework’s extensibility is limited to disjoint block models and doesn’t address other spectral-success settings like hierarchical clustering.  
13. Figure 1 and caption ambiguous about SBM plotting; axes labels are missing.  
14. Notation is inconsistent across sections (B, C, Y, Z, eigenvalue labels), causing confusion and needing standardization.  
15. Minor clarifications: state independence of A_{ij} given clusters (Line 44) and move Section 4.4 into related work.  
16. Overall: conceptually interesting and well-structured for block models, but practical impact is limited by infeasible conditions, unclear model choices, missing related work, and unconvincing experiments.
