This manuscript proposes a collection of algorithms for clustering on graphs and for assessing the similarity between two cluster assignments. While the individual algorithms are presented with clarity, the paper suffers from two main weaknesses: a lack of a cohesive narrative to connect the different contributions and an insufficiently articulated claim of novelty over existing methods.

**Strengths**

The primary strength of this work lies in the clarity of the algorithmic descriptions. The proposed methods are detailed in a straightforward and understandable manner, which would likely make them easy for other researchers to implement. The problems addressed—graph clustering and cluster comparison—are relevant and important to the machine learning community.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement**

1.  **Lack of Unifying Motivation:** A significant weakness of the manuscript is the absence of a clear, overarching motivation that ties the various algorithms together. The paper currently reads as a list of distinct algorithmic contributions rather than a single, focused research paper. It is not clear to the reader why this specific set of algorithms is presented as a single package. The authors should substantially revise the introduction and narrative to frame a central problem or research question that each component of the work helps to solve. What specific gap in the literature does this collection of methods aim to fill? Without this unifying context, it is difficult to assess the paper's overall impact and contribution.

2.  **Limited Discussion of Novelty:** The novelty of the proposed clustering algorithms is not convincingly established. The paper suggests that the methods are modifications of existing techniques, but it fails to sufficiently contextualize these changes or argue for their significance. To strengthen the paper, the authors must clearly delineate their precise contributions over the prior art. The related work section should be expanded to more effectively position the proposed algorithms and highlight their unique advantages. For instance, what are the specific limitations of the baseline methods that the proposed modifications are designed to overcome?

    As presented, the primary novel contribution appears to be the set of constraints introduced at the end of the manuscript. While this is a potentially valuable addition, the paper would be much stronger if the novelty of the core clustering algorithms were also more thoroughly argued and empirically validated against their direct predecessors.

**Suggestions for the Authors**

*   **Refine the Narrative:** Consider reformulating the introduction to present a clear, compelling research problem. Then, structure the paper to show how each proposed algorithm contributes a necessary piece to the overall solution. This would transform the paper from a collection of methods into a cohesive and impactful contribution.

*   **Clarify Contributions:** For each proposed algorithm that is a modification of an existing one, please add a detailed discussion clarifying (a) the exact nature of the modification and (b) the practical and/or theoretical benefits gained from this change.

*   **Strengthen Experimental Validation:** The experimental setup should be designed to directly support the claims of novelty. For example, an ablation study demonstrating the specific impact of the new constraints would be highly beneficial. Likewise, direct empirical comparisons against the baseline algorithms that were modified are essential to demonstrate the superiority of the proposed methods.

In summary, while the paper presents a set of clearly described algorithms for an important problem, its contribution is obscured by a lack of motivation and an unclear discussion of novelty. Addressing these points would significantly improve the quality and potential impact of the manuscript.
