- Novel idea: proposes a model-free graph clustering framework, which is intriguing but underdeveloped.
- Generic Theorem (Thm 1) is vague: no formal definition of what it means for a clustering to "fit" a graph and relies on unspecified "technical conditions."
- Proposition 3 (Informal) claims PFM estimator consistency but lacks clear recovery-condition definitions and rigorous proof.
- Instantiations for specific models (PFM, SBM) are not convincingly proven and depend on informal arguments and handwaving.
- Overall lack of rigor: many statements are vague, undefined, or unsupported by formal derivations.
- Clarity issues: poor exposition and use of undefined terms make the claims hard to follow.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only a few synthetic datasets and one real dataset are tested.
- No comparisons to state-of-the-art methods in experiments, weakening empirical claims.
- Quality assessment: technically plausible but not convincingly proven.
- Originality/significance: conceptually novel but execution provides little new insight or strong evidence.
- Recommendation: paper not ready for acceptance in its current form due to lack of rigor, clarity, and convincing experiments.
