- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that provides theoretical guarantees without assuming a specific data-generating model.  
- Develops a generic theorem connecting model-based and model-free clustering results.  
- Instantiates the theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM), yielding model-free guarantees for these models.  
- Defines a goodness-of-fit measure between model and data and a distance metric between clusterings.  
- Proves that any two clusterings that fit the data well will be close under the proposed distance.  
- Provides a PFM estimation algorithm and several supporting propositions with proofs.  
- Presents experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explanations are clear and concise.
