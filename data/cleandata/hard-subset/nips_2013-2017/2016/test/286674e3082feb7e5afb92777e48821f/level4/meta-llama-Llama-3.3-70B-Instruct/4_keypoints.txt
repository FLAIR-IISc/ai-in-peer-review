- Proposes a general framework giving theoretical stability guarantees for clustering by bounding the distance between an original clustering and any other clustering that fits the data well, applied to PFM and SBM with computable quantities and exact recovery guarantees.  
- Simulation results indicate the chosen goodness-of-fit measure or the theorem conditions may limit practical usefulness.  
- Treating PFM and SBM as separate cases is questionable since PFM subsumes DC-SBM and SBM, raising concerns about presentation and hierarchy.  
- Use of different estimators (graph Laplacian for PFM, adjacency matrix for SBM) and different metrics (weighted ME vs ME) undermines comparability of results.  
- Technical ambiguities: undefined inner product <M',M>_F in Proposition 10 and unclear formulation of Assumption 5 (||L̂^2−L^2||_F vs ||L̂−L||_F^2), including unclear intuition for squaring Laplacians.  
- Focus on disjoint clusters limits scope; unclear whether techniques extend to other models like hierarchical clustering where spectral methods are effective.  
- Evaluation restricted to clusterings with the same number of clusters, which may not reflect practical scenarios.  
- Related work section is incomplete, omitting model-free results and lacking discussion of how the paper’s stability notion relates to existing stability measures (e.g., center perturbation).  
- PFM simulations show tiny perturbations can heavily affect model fit and SBM guarantees require strong conditions, limiting practical applicability.  
- Minor issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1, inconsistent notation, and suggestions to reorganize sections for clarity.
