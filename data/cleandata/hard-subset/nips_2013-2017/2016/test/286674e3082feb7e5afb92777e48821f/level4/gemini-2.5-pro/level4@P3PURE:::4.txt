This paper introduces a general template for proving theoretical guarantees on the stability of a given clustering. Stability is defined in terms of the distance between a clustering C that fits the data well and any other clustering C' that also provides a good fit. The authors instantiate this template for two block models, the Planted Final Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), and show that the quantities in the theoretical bounds can be computed directly from the data. These stability results are then leveraged to establish exact recovery guarantees for these models.

The primary contribution is the development of a model-free template for deriving theoretical guarantees for clustering stability. The goal of obtaining such guarantees using quantities that are computable from the data and the clustering output is commendable and has significant value for both theory and practice.

Despite the promising direction, there are several major concerns regarding the methodology, scope, and experimental validation that should be addressed.

**Major Points:**

1.  **Practical Utility of Guarantees:** The practical utility of the derived guarantees appears limited, as suggested by the simulation results.
    *   For PFM, the guarantees only hold for very small perturbations. The explanation that a "large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations" (lines 252-253) is not sufficiently justified. If the model fit is this sensitive, the guarantees may not be robust enough for practical use, which undermines one of the paper's key selling points.
    *   The authors note that the conditions for the SBM guarantee are very difficult to satisfy (lines 248-250), which also limits its applicability.

2.  **Inconsistent Treatment of Block Models:** The justification for treating PFM and SBM differently is unclear, especially since PFM includes DC-SBM, which in turn includes SBM.
    *   Given that spectral clustering is effective for all these models, why are different estimators used: the Laplacian for PFM and the adjacency matrix for SBM? A clear rationale for this choice is needed.
    *   The results for PFM are given in terms of weighted misclassification error (ME), while for SBM they are in terms of standard ME. The relationship and comparability between these two metrics should be clarified.
    *   Given the hierarchy of models, why are results for DC-SBM not presented?

3.  **Limited Scope of the Framework:**
    *   The analysis is restricted to comparing clusterings C and C' with the same number of clusters. However, different algorithms or model selection criteria (e.g., based on the eigengap) might suggest different numbers of clusters. The current framework cannot handle cases where, for instance, one cluster from C splits into two in C', even though C' might be considered qualitatively similar to C.
    *   The paper focuses on disjoint clusters and block models. Could the techniques be extended to other settings where spectral clustering is known to work, such as for hierarchical clustering (e.g., as in "Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering", Balakrishnan et al., 2011)?

4.  **Relation to Prior Work:** The related work section could be strengthened by discussing other model-free stability results, such as "Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm" (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), "Clustering under Perturbation Resilience" (Balcan et al., 2012), and "Clustering under Approximation Stability" (Balcan et al., 2013). A comparison of the stability notion proposed in this paper with the concepts in these prior works would be highly valuable.

**Questions and Minor Points:**

1.  In Proposition 10, the definition of `<M', M>_F` is missing.
2.  In Assumption 5, is the term `||L_hat^2 - L^2||_F` or `||L_hat - L||_F^2`? If it is the former, what is the intuition behind squaring the Laplacians before taking the difference?
3.  The experiment on the political blog data seems to contradict the earlier observation about sensitivity. Here, the adjacency matrix is raised to a power, which would exaggerate eigengaps, yet this appears to improve stability. This requires clarification.
4.  The caption for Figure 1 states, "For SBM, delta is always greater than delta0". It is not clear which subplot this refers to. If SBM results are not plotted, this should be clarified in the caption.
5.  There is a mismatch between theory and experiments for PFM. The theorems provide guarantees for weighted ME, but the experiments measure perturbations in terms of ME.
6.  Labels are missing on the axes in Figure 1.
7.  Line 44: The statement that A_ij for i > j are independent given cluster assignments is not strictly correct for block models; they are conditionally independent given the cluster assignments of nodes i *and* j.
8.  Several notations (e.g., B, C, Y, lambda) are reused with different meanings for PFM and SBM. This is confusing. Using distinct notation (e.g., `Y_perp` and `CD` for PFM; `lambda^L` and `lambda^A` for eigenvalues of the Laplacian and Adjacency matrix, respectively) would improve readability.
9.  The expression involving YZ in Proposition 8 could be written out directly to avoid introducing unnecessary notation.
10. Section 4.4 seems more appropriate as a subsection within the related work section.
