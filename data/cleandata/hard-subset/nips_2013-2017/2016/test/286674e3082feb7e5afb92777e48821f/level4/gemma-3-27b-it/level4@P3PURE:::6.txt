This paper attempts to develop a theoretical framework for model-free graph clustering. However, the presented results are naive and ultimately unconvincing. The authors claim to construct a new theory, but it largely amounts to handwaving arguments lacking sufficient rigor. To illustrate this, consider Theorem 1 (labeled as a “Generic Theorem”), which states that a graph well-approximated by a matrix *C* is “close to *G*.” Similarly, Proposition 3 is presented as “informal” and relies on unspecified “standard recovery conditions.” These instances, and others throughout the paper, demonstrate a significant lack of mathematical precision and leave the core theoretical contributions ill-defined.
