This paper develops broad, model-agnostic correctness guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering tools. The authors establish these guarantees across multiple generative settings without relying on model parameters. The work is interesting, timely, and moves a popular research direction forward in a meaningful way. It poses a compelling question about the stability of community detection solutions and presents results at a high level of generality while also instantiating them for specific, widely used graphical models. The manuscript is very clearly written and an easy, engaging read. The authors thoughtfully motivate their definitions of error and the assumptions needed in their proofs. My primary concern—also noted by the authors in the conclusion—is that the results depend on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can be nearly impractical in the very sparse regimes that often matter most. That said, this is a strong initial step toward a theory of stability guarantees for community detection, and I expect it will spur further progress in the near future.
