0) Paper proposes a general, data-computable template for clustering stability with instantiations for PFM and SBM and exact recovery guarantees, but simulations suggest the theorem conditions may limit practical utility.

1) Positively, model-agnostic theoretical guarantees based on quantities computable from data and algorithm outputs are valuable.

2) Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given PFM subsumes DC-SBM/SBM; question why different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency) and weighted vs unweighted ME are used, and request DC-SBM results.

3) Request clarification of the definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F in Proposition 10.

4) In Assumption 5, ask whether the norm is ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2, and if the former, why square the Laplacians.

5) Ask whether the framework and techniques extend beyond block models to other spectral-clustering-friendly models (e.g., hierarchical clustering) and how the template would adapt.

6) Framework restricts comparisons to clusterings with the same number of clusters, failing to capture close clusterings that differ by splits/merges or different K.

7) Related work is incomplete (missing Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013); request discussion of how this stability notion compares to existing perturbation/approximation-based stability measures.

8) Simulations use small perturbations yet show high sensitivity (PFM affected by eigengap); smoothing can help, but if small perturbations alter fit significantly the guarantees may lack practical relevance and SBM conditions are hard to satisfy.

9) Figure 1 caption is ambiguous about which subplot “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” refers to; clarify whether SBM data are plotted.

10) Theoretical PFM guarantees use weighted ME while experiments use unweighted ME, creating an inconsistency.
