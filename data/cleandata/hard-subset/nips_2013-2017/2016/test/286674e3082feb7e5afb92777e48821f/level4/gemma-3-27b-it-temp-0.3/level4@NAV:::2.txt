This paper establishes a relationship between various distance metrics used to compare clusterings. Focusing on network models – specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM) – the authors demonstrate a locally convex-like property for clustering solutions. They show that clusterings achieving comparable model fit to network data are also closely related according to common misclassification-based distance measures. This work presents a valuable property of clusterings within the context of network data, formally stating and proving the equivalence between different clustering distance measures. Both theoretical analysis and experimental validation support the presented results. While the findings are novel, it’s important to note related work in the network literature, such as that by Peng, Sun and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical contributions build upon established results in a straightforward manner. The authors suggest potential applications to clusterability detection, however, formal results in this area are not included. Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly explains its central concepts.
