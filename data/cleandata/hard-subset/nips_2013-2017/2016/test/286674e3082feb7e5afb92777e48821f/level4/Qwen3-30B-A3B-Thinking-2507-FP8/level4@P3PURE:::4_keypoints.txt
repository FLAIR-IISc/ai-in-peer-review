- Provides a model-free template for deriving data-computable stability guarantees for clustering solutions, which is theoretically meaningful and practically valuable.  
- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given inclusion relations (PFM ⊃ DC-SBM ⊃ SBM); questions why different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency) and different error metrics (weighted ME vs standard ME) are used and why DC-SBM results are omitted.  
- Proposition 10 omits the definition of the Frobenius inner product ⟨M', M⟩_F.  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5 about whether the norm is ‖ĤL^2 − L^2‖_F or ‖ĤL − L‖_F^2, and if the former, lack of intuition for squaring Laplacians.  
- Paper only treats disjoint block models; unclear if the template extends to other clustering settings where spectral methods work (e.g., hierarchical models).  
- Framework requires C and C' to have the same number of clusters, excluding close clusterings that split a cluster (K vs K+1 scenarios).  
- Related work omits key model-free clustering results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and lacks discussion connecting the paper’s stability notion to perturbation/center-based stability.  
- Practical utility concerns: claims about eigengap sensitivity are inadequately qualified, empirical manipulations (power-raising) contradict intuition, and SBM conditions appear hard to satisfy, undermining applicability despite computable quantities.  
- Figure 1 caption is ambiguous about δ vs δ0 for SBM and must clarify whether SBM is plotted.  
- Experiments use standard ME while PFM theory uses weighted ME, creating an inconsistency between theory and empirical evaluation.  
- Minor: Figure 1 axes labels missing.  
- Minor: Line 44 should clarify independence of A_ij given block assignments for block models.  
- Minor: Notation inconsistent across sections (B, C, Y definitions vary); recommend unified symbols and distinct eigenvalue notation for Laplacian vs adjacency.  
- Minor: Proposition 8’s estimator representation (YZ) should be expressed directly; Section 4.4 would fit better under Related Work.
