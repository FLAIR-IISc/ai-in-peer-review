This paper introduces a generic template for providing theoretical stability guarantees for a given clustering C. Stability is defined as the distance between C and any other clustering C' that also fits the data well. The authors demonstrate this template on two block models, the Planted Fragmentation Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), showing that all quantities in the guarantees can be computed directly from the data. These stability results are then used to derive exact recovery guarantees for these models. However, the simulation results suggest that the proposed goodness-of-fit measure or the theoretical conditions may be too restrictive, potentially limiting the practical utility of the guarantees.

**Major Points**

1.  The paper's goal of establishing a generic, model-free template for clustering stability is commendable. Developing theoretical guarantees based on quantities computable directly from the data and clustering outputs is an important problem with significant theoretical and practical implications.

2.  The paper treats PFM and SBM as separate cases, which is confusing given that PFM generalizes DC-SBM, which in turn generalizes SBM. Since spectral clustering is effective for all these models, it is unclear why different estimators are used. Specifically, why is the graph Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM? Furthermore, the stability results for PFM are given in terms of a weighted misclassification error (ME), whereas the results for SBM use the standard ME. The authors should clarify how these two error metrics compare. Finally, given the model hierarchy, why are specific results for DC-SBM not provided?

3.  In Proposition 10, the term `<M', M>_F` is used without a clear definition. Please define this notation.

4.  Assumption 5 contains the term `||L̂² - L²||_F`. Is this correct, or should it be `||L̂ - L||_F²`? If the former is intended, please provide intuition for squaring the Laplacians before taking the difference.

5.  The analysis focuses on disjoint clusters within block models. Could the proposed template be extended to other settings where spectral clustering is successful, such as hierarchical clustering? For instance, how would the framework apply to scenarios like those considered in "Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering" (Balakrishnan et al., 2011)?

6.  The proposed framework, including the evaluation metric and estimators, is restricted to comparing clusterings C and C' that have the same number of clusters. This is a significant limitation, as different algorithms or model selection criteria can yield different numbers of clusters. For example, one algorithm might suggest K clusters, while another suggests K+1 by splitting one of the original K clusters. Intuitively, these two clusterings are very similar, but the current framework cannot capture this notion of stability.

7.  The related work section could be strengthened by discussing other model-free stability results, such as "Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm" (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), "Clustering under Perturbation Resilience" (Balcan et al., 2012), and "Clustering under Approximation Stability" (Balcan et al., 2013). It would be beneficial to clarify how the stability notion in this paper relates to the concepts presented in these works, particularly with respect to stability under perturbations of cluster centers.

8.  The experimental results raise concerns about the practical applicability of the theoretical guarantees.
    *   For the PFM simulations, the tolerated perturbations seem very small. The statement on lines 252-253 that a "large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations" is presented without justification and seems counterintuitive. Why would a large eigengap—typically a sign of a stable cluster structure—lead to high sensitivity in the goodness-of-fit measure? This also seems to contradict the political blogs experiment, where increasing the eigengap by powering the adjacency matrix appeared to improve stability. If the model fit is indeed this sensitive to small perturbations, it undermines the practical value of the data-dependent guarantees.
    *   For the SBM, the authors acknowledge (lines 248-250) that the conditions for the guarantee are very difficult to satisfy, which is also a significant limitation.

9.  The caption for Figure 1 states, "For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0." It is unclear which subplot this refers to. If SBM results are not plotted, this should be explicitly stated.

10. There appears to be a mismatch between theory and experiment for the PFM case: the theoretical guarantees are for a weighted misclassification error (ME), but the experiments measure performance using the standard ME. Please clarify or correct this.

**Minor Points**

1.  The axes in Figure 1 are missing labels.

2.  On line 44, the statement about block models should be clarified: A_ij for i > j are independent conditional on the cluster assignments of nodes i and j.

3.  The paper reuses notation (e.g., B, C, Y, λ) with different meanings for the PFM and SBM sections, which can be confusing. To improve clarity, consider using distinct notation, such as Y_⟂ and CD for PFM, and λ^L and λ^A for eigenvalues of the Laplacian and adjacency matrix, respectively. Additionally, in Proposition 8, the expression for the SBM estimator could be written out directly instead of introducing the term YZ.

4.  Section 4.4 seems more appropriate as a subsection within the related work section.
