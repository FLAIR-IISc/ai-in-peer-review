- Proposes novel distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that do not assume a specific generative model (e.g., SBM).  
- Central claim: if a clustering fits the observed graph well, then any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.  
- Addresses an important problem and provides interesting, nonparametric guarantees for clustering.  
- Paper is clear and readable despite some material being in the supplementary due to page limits.  
- Notation choice (using “hats” for observed quantities, e.g., \hat{L}) is questionable and potentially confusing.  
- Results are interesting, well-commented, and supported by illustrations.  
- Experiments show limitations: some real-world graphs require smoothing for the framework to recover structure, whereas classical algorithms can recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- Limitation 1: main PFM result uses a non-standard distance between clusterings, which may be debatable compared to classical metrics.  
- Limitation 2: reliance on a “proxy” model (reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing it to the graph) constrains the notion of a good clustering (focus on normalized Laplacian approximation), raising concerns about applicability to real data.
