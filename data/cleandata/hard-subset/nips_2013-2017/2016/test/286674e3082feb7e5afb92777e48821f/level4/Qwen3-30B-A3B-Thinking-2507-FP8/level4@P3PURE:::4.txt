

The paper presents a generic template for deriving theoretical guarantees on the stability of a clustering solution \(C\) that fits the data well, measured by the distance between \(C\) and any other clustering \(C'\) that fits the data equally well. It demonstrates the template's application to two block models (PFM and SBM), with all guarantee quantities computable explicitly from the data. The stability results are then leveraged to establish exact recovery guarantees. However, the simulations suggest that either the goodness-of-fit measure or the theorem conditions render the results of limited practical utility.  

1.  **Model-Free Guarantees:** The paper's goal of providing model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering—where quantities are computable from the data and clustering outputs—is theoretically significant and practically valuable. This is a strong point of the work.  

2.  **Redundancy in Model Treatment:** PFM includes DC-SBM as a special case, which itself includes SBM. It is unclear why PFM and SBM are treated as distinct cases. Since spectral clustering works well for all specified models, why not use the same estimator? Specifically:  
    - Why is the Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM?  
    - Why are PFM results stated in terms of *weighted* misclassification error (ME), while SBM results use standard ME? How do these error metrics compare?  
    - Why are results for DC-SBM omitted?  

3.  **Notation Clarification (Prop. 10):** The definition of \(\langle M', M \rangle_F\) is not provided.  

4.  **Norm Ambiguity (Assump. 5):** The norm is stated as \(\|\hat{L}^2 - L^2\|_F\) or \(\|\hat{L} - L\|_F^2\)? If the former, what is the intuition behind squaring the Laplacians?  

5.  **Generalizability Beyond Block Models:** The paper focuses solely on disjoint clusters and block models. Do the techniques extend to other models where spectral clustering is effective (e.g., hierarchical clustering, as in Balakrishnan et al. 2011)? How would the template apply to such cases?  

6.  **Restrictive Cluster Count Assumption:** The framework assumes \(C\) and \(C'\) have the same number of clusters. However, different algorithms (e.g., spectral clustering using adjacency vs. Laplacian matrices) may suggest different cluster counts (\(K\) vs. \(K+1\)). A clustering \(C'\) that splits one cluster of \(C\) into two could be "close" to \(C\) but fall outside the framework.  

7.  **Related Work Gap:** The related work section omits key model-free clustering results:  
    - Kumar & Kannan (2010) on spectral norm and \(k\)-means.  
    - Balcan et al. (2012, 2013) on perturbation resilience and approximation stability.  
    *How does the stability notion in this paper relate to these works? Specifically, how does it connect to stability measured via perturbation of cluster centers?*  

8.  **Practical Utility of Perturbation Analysis:**  
    - The claim (Lines 252–253) that "large eigengap makes PFM\((G, C)\) differ from PFM\((G, C_0)\) even for very small perturbations" lacks qualification. The intuition is unclear.  
    - The political blog data analysis uses power-raising on \(A\) to exaggerate eigenvalue gaps, yet stability *improves*—this contradicts the small-perturbation sensitivity.  
    - If small perturbations significantly affect model fit (as suggested), the guarantees lose practical utility (a key selling point), especially since quantities are computable from data.  
    - Lines 248–250 note SBM conditions are "very hard to satisfy," which is unfortunate given the paper's focus on practical computation.  

9.  **Figure 1 Caption Ambiguity:** The caption states "\(\delta\) is always greater than \(\delta_0\) for SBM." If SBM is not plotted in the figure, this must be explicitly clarified.  

10. **Inconsistent Error Metrics:** Theorems for PFM use *weighted* ME, but experiments perturb standard ME. This disconnect needs resolution.  

**Minor Points:**  
- Axes labels missing in Figure 1.  
- Line 44: Clarify that for block models, \(A_{ij}\) (for \(i > j\)) are independent given cluster assignments of \(i\) and \(j\).  
- Notation inconsistency:  
  - \(B, C, Y\) have different definitions in PFM (Section 3.1) vs. SBM (Section 3.2). Use consistent symbols (e.g., \(Y^{\perp}\) and \(C_D\) for PFM instead of \(B\) and \(C\)).  
  - For SBM, the estimator \(YZ\) in Prop. 8 should be expressed directly (avoiding \(YZ\) as a separate definition).  
  - Distinguish eigenvalues via \(\lambda^L\) (Laplacian) vs. \(\lambda^B\) (adjacency) for clarity.  
- Section 4.4 is better placed as a subsection under "Related Work."
