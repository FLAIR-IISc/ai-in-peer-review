- Novel theoretical framework: provides distribution-free guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Core idea: if a clustering fits an observed graph well, any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.  
- Instantiations: framework is applied to SBM and PFM and shown to connect model-free and model-based recovery results.  
- Theorems: statements are clear and appear mathematically sound.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and accessible despite some material moved to the supplement.  
- Experimental finding: some real-world graphs require smoothing to meet theoretical recoverability conditions, whereas standard algorithms sometimes recover structure directly.  
- Distance measure concern: PFM result relies on a non-standard weighted Misclassification Error (wME) whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Proxy-model limitation: results use a reconstructed model from a clustering, which restricts the definition of a “good” clustering and may be overly restrictive.  
- Practicality issue: assumptions required for the theorems may be hard to verify in practice.  
- Sensitivity to eigengap: results depend on the Laplacian eigengap, which may be too small in many real graphs to guarantee stability.  
- SBM weakness: the SBM instantiation is weaker and more restrictive compared to the PFM results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important open problem, offers a novel, rigorous framework, connects to existing work, and is well written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability is limited by strong assumptions, non-standard distance, and the proxy-model approach; SBM results are less compelling.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better discuss limitations and possible extensions.
