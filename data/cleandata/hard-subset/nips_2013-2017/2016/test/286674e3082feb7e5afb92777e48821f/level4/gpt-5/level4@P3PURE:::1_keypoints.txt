- Presents novel, distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that make no generative assumptions.  
- Central claim: any clustering that fits the observed graph well implies any other equally good clustering must be close to it.  
- Addresses a fundamental question in graph clustering with rigorous, distribution-free guarantees.  
- Main paper is clear and self-contained despite some content in the supplement; results are well explained and illustrated.  
- Minor stylistic issue: using hats for observed quantities (e.g., \hat{L}) is confusing but not critical.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world datasets require graph smoothing for the framework to recover meaningful structure, unlike many classical algorithms.  
- Major concern: main PFM result depends on a non-standard distance between clusterings, complicating interpretation and comparison with standard metrics.  
- Major concern: guarantees rely on a proxy-model reconstruction (comparing a reconstructed model to the observed graph) rather than the model that produced the clustering, narrowing what counts as a “good” clustering.  
- Overall: well-written and thought-provoking with insightful theoretical contributions, but the non-standard distance, proxy-model approach, and need for smoothing limit perceived practical impact.
