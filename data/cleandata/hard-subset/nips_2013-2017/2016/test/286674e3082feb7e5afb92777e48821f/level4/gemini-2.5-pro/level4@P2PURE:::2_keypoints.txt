- Establishes relationships between different distance metrics for clusterings on network data.
- Focuses on the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Proves a "locally convex-like" property for these models.
- Core result: any two clusterings that give equally good parameter estimates are close in misclassification distance.
- Validates claims with theoretical analysis and experimental results.
- Findings are novel but follow a recent trend of similar work (e.g., Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. ArXiv 2015).
- Theoretical contributions seem to be straightforward extensions of well-known results.
- Suggests applications to detecting clusterability but provides no formal results in that direction.
- Paper is well-written and explains its core ideas lucidly.
