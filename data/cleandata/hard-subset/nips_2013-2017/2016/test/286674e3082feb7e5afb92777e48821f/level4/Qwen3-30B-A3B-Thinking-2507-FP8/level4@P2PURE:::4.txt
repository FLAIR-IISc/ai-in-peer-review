

The paper presents a generic template for providing theoretical guarantees on the stability of a clustering solution \(C\) that fits the data well. Stability is measured by the distance between \(C\) and any other clustering \(C'\) that similarly fits the data. The authors demonstrate the template's application to two block models—PFM and SBM—with all quantities in the guarantees computable explicitly from the data. Furthermore, they leverage the stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees for these models. However, the simulations indicate that either the goodness-of-fit measure or the theorem conditions render the results not practically useful.  

1) The paper addresses the problem of providing model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering, without assuming the data follows a specific generative model. The goal is to obtain guarantees using quantities computable from the data and the output of the clustering algorithms being compared. Such model-free guarantees are theoretically and practically significant.  

2) Since PFM includes DC-SBM as a special case, which in turn includes SBM, it is unclear why PFM and SBM are treated as distinct cases. Given that Spectral Clustering performs well across all specified models, why not use a consistent estimator? Specifically, it is unclear why the Laplacian is used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Additionally, the PFM results are presented in terms of weighted ME, whereas SBM results use ME—how do these comparisons relate? Why are DC-SBM results omitted?  

3) In Proposition 10, what is the definition of \(\langle M', M \rangle_F\)?  

4) In Assumption 5, is the norm \(\| \hat{L}^2 - L^2 \|_F\) or \(\| \hat{L} - L \|_F^2\)? If the former, what is the intuition for squaring the Laplacians?  

5) The paper focuses primarily on disjoint clusters. Do the techniques extend beyond block models to other settings where Spectral Clustering is effective, such as hierarchical clustering (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., 2011)? How would the template apply in such cases?  

6) The evaluation metric and estimators assume \(C\) and \(C'\) have the same number of clusters. However, different algorithms may yield different cluster counts (e.g., eigenvalues of \(A\) suggesting \(K\) clusters for \(C\), while the Laplacian suggests \(K' = K+1\) for \(C'\), with all clusters identical except one splitting into two). \(C'\) would be "close" to \(C\), but this scenario is unaddressed by the framework.  

7) The related work section omits key model-free results: Kumar & Kannan (2010) on clustering with spectral norms, and Balcan et al. (2012, 2013) on perturbation resilience and approximation stability. How does the stability notion here relate to these works, particularly regarding perturbation of cluster centers?  

8) The simulations for PFM tolerate only trivial perturbations. Lines 252–253 state casually that "large eigengap makes PFM\((G, C)\) differ from PFM\((G, C_0)\) even for very small perturbations" without justification. The intuition is unclear. Conversely, smoothing the political blog data via \(A^k\) exaggerates eigenvalue gaps but appears to improve stability—why? If small perturbations significantly affect model fit, the guarantees lose practical utility (a key selling point). Lines 248–250 note SBM conditions are "very hard to satisfy," which is unfortunate.  

9) Figure 1 caption states "For SBM, \(\delta\) is always greater than \(\delta_0\)"—which subplot does this refer to? If SBM is not plotted, the caption must clarify this.  

10) Theorems for PFM use weighted ME guarantees, but experiments perturb ME. This inconsistency requires resolution.  

**Minor Points:**  
1) Axes labels are missing in Figure 1.  
2) (Line 44) For block models, \(A_{ij}\) for \(i > j\) are independent given the cluster assignments of \(i\) and \(j\).  
3) Notation inconsistencies: \(B\), \(C\), and \(Y\) are redefined for SBM (Section 3.2) after being used in PFM. To avoid confusion (given the paper’s heavy notation), use consistent symbols (e.g., \(Y^\perp\) and \(C_D\) for PFM instead of \(B\) and \(C\)). For SBM, omit \(YZ\) and substitute its expression directly in Proposition 8. Similarly, distinguish eigenvalues using \(\lambda^L\) and \(\lambda^B\) for PFM and SBM, respectively.  
4) Section 4.4 is better suited as a subsection under Related Work.
