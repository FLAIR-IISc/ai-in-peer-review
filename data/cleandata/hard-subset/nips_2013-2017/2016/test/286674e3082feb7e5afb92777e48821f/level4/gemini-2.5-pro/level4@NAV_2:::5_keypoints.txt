- Introduces a model-free framework that provides correctness guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Guarantees depend on computable goodness-of-fit statistics measuring how well a clustering explains the observed graph.  
- Central claim: any other clustering that also fits the data well must be a small perturbation of the fitted clustering.  
- Framework is instantiated for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Shows a strong connection between model-free results and existing model-based recovery theorems.  
- Original and significant shift from model-based recovery to data-driven stability and validation.  
- Well-written and clearly organized; framework presented as a reusable "blueprint."  
- Carefully defines error metrics and goodness-of-fit assumptions with clear technical intuition.  
- Technical approach is sound, leveraging matrix perturbation theory (e.g., Davis–Kahan) in a novel context.  
- Provides explicit, computable bounds without unspecified constants.  
- Links to prior work (Rohe et al., Wan & Meila) strengthen both perspectives.  
- Main limitation: reliance on spectral methods and spectral-gap quantities, which can be weak in sparse regimes and make SBM conditions hard to meet in practice.
