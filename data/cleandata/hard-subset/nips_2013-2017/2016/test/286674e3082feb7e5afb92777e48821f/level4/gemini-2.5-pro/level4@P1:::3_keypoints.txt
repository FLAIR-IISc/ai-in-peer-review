- Proposes a novel model-free, data-dependent stability framework: if clustering C fits G well under a block-model class, any other well-fitting clustering C' is a small perturbation of C.  
- Shifts validation from unverifiable generative assumptions to a computable goodness-of-fit criterion.  
- Presents a generic theorem and concrete instantiations for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Defines fitting procedures and goodness-of-fit (spectral-norm distance between observed and model Laplacians/adjacency) and derives upper bounds on clustering distance.  
- Proofs rely on matrix perturbation theory, notably the Davis–Kahan theorem.  
- All main-theorem quantities are computable from data, making the framework practically applicable.  
- Demonstrates how the model-free framework can leverage and strengthen existing model-based recovery results.  
- Provides experiments on synthetic and real datasets to evaluate when theoretical guarantees hold.  
- Strength: significant and original conceptual shift addressing untestable model assumptions in community detection.  
- Strength: strong technical quality with clear, non-asymptotic bounds for PFM (Thm 4) and SBM (Thm 9).  
- Strength: clear writing and organization, with effective distinction between model-free and model-based paradigms.  
- Weakness: practical utility of SBM bounds is limited—conditions are hard to satisfy (δ > 1 in experiments), making SBM result loose.  
- Weakness: reliance on a non-zero eigengap (Assumptions 1 and 3); need more discussion of how bounds degrade as eigengap shrinks.  
- Weakness: choice of spectral norm for goodness-of-fit may limit tightness; trade-offs with Frobenius norm deserve deeper discussion.  
- Question: Is the looseness of SBM bounds a proof-technique artifact or a fundamental barrier for model-free guarantees on SBM?  
- Question: Why does "smoothing" (taking powers of adjacency) improve stability—request for more intuition/theory.  
- Clarification requested: notation for λ̂_AK—should this be the K-th largest eigenvalue (magnitude) of symmetric Â rather than singular value?  
- Clarification requested: why use MLE for SBM but a projection-based estimator for PFM—justify estimator choices and compare advantages.
