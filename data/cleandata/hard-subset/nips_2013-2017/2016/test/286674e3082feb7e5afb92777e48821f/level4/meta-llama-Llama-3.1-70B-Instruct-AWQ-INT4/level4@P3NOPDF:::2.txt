This paper explores the relationship between different distance measures for clustering in network models, specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The authors establish a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well are also proximal in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This property provides valuable insight into the behavior of clusterings in network data.

The paper's main contribution is the proof of equivalence between different distance measures for clusterings in network data. This result is supported by both theoretical and experimental validations, which strengthen the paper's claims. While the findings are novel, it is worth noting that recent works in the network literature, such as Peng et al. (COLT, 2015) and Chao et al. (Arxiv, 2015), have explored similar ideas. The theoretical components of the paper build upon well-known results, and the extensions presented are relatively straightforward.

The authors suggest that their work has potential applications in detecting clusterability, but unfortunately, the paper does not provide formal results in this regard. Nevertheless, the paper is well-written, and the core ideas are presented in a clear and concise manner. The authors' ability to lucidly explain complex concepts is a significant strength of the paper.

In terms of the paper's overall quality, the contributions are notable, but the simplicity of the theoretical extensions and the absence of formal results on detecting clusterability are drawbacks. However, the paper's well-organized structure, clear explanations, and validation of results through both theoretical and experimental means make it a valuable addition to the literature.
