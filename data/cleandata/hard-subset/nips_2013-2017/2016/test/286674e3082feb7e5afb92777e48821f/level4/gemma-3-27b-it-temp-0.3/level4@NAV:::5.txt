This paper presents broadly applicable concentration bounds for spectral clustering across a range of community detection problems. A key strength is the model-agnostic nature of the guarantees, achieving results without requiring parameters specific to the underlying generative model. The work is both interesting and relevant, representing a significant step forward in a crucial area of research. The authors address a compelling question regarding the solution stability of community detection, successfully formulating and proving results with impressive generality, and then thoughtfully applying them to well-known graphical models. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, with thorough justification provided for both error definitions and necessary assumptions. The primary limitation, acknowledged by the authors, lies in the reliance on spectral algorithms and bounds that can be loose and computationally challenging to apply in the sparse graph regime. Nevertheless, this work establishes a strong foundation for future research into stability results for community detection, and I anticipate further progress in this direction.
