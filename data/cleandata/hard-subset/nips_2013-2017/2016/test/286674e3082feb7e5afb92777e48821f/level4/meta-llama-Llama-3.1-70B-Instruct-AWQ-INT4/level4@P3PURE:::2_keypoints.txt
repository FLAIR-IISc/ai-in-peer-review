- Establishes a relationship between clustering distances for network models, specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Proves a locally convex–like property: two clusterings that fit the model equally well are close in misclassification distance.
- Shows equivalence between different clustering distance measures for network data.
- Provides both theoretical proofs and experimental validation of the results.
- Results are novel but related recent work exists (Peng, Sun, Zanetti COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou arXiv 2015).
- Theoretical contributions are relatively straightforward extensions of established results.
- Notes potential application to detecting clusterability but does not present formal results on this.
- Paper is well written, clear, and concise.
- Overall a valuable contribution whose impact could be increased by exploring implications for clusterability detection.
