- Introduces a novel, model-free framework giving theoretical stability guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a data-generating model.  
- Framework: if clustering C fits data well, any other clustering C' that also fits well must be close to C; instantiated for PFM and SBM with computable quantities.  
- Connects model-free guarantees to existing model-based results, strengthening prior analyses.  
- Strength: addresses an important gap by providing model-free theoretical guarantees useful for practitioners lacking strong data assumptions.  
- Strength: elegant, generalizable template with sound technical approach, clear definitions, rigorous proofs, and feasible computations shown experimentally.  
- Weakness: inconsistent treatment of models — PFM and SBM handled separately despite PFM encompassing SBM, with unjustified different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency) and distance metrics.  
- Weakness: technical ambiguities and unclear notation (e.g., undefined <M',M>_F, unclear use of ||L̂² - L²||_F vs ||L̂ - L||²_F).  
- Weakness: limited scope due to assumption of fixed cluster counts and disjoint clusters; does not handle refinements or varying k.  
- Weakness: insufficient related work coverage — omits key model-free clustering literature (e.g., Balcan et al., Kumar & Kannan) and lacks comparison to those stability notions.  
- Weakness: practical limitations — conditions often too restrictive (SBM δ values >1), small tolerated perturbations make guarantees uninformative for real data.  
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies across sections (B, C, Y) that hinder clarity.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation issues — unclear figure captions/axes and unexplained omission of SBM results from main figures.  
- Recommendation: conceptually promising but requires substantial revision to clarify technical points, standardize models/notation, expand related work, and improve practical relevance before acceptance.
