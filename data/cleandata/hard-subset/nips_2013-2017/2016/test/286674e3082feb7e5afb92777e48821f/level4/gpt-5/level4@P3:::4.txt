Summary
This paper proposes a model-free validation framework for graph clustering that provides a posteriori guarantees without assuming a generative model for the observed graph. Given a graph G, a clustering C, and a model class M (e.g., SBM, DC-SBM, PFM), the authors define an estimator M(G, C) and a data-driven goodness-of-fit criterion (e.g., spectral norm closeness between G and the fitted model). The main generic statement is: if C fits G well under M, then any other clustering C′ that also fits G well must be close to C in misclassification error (ME). The paper instantiates this template for PFM using the normalized Laplacian and for SBM using the adjacency matrix, proving stability bounds in terms of eigengaps and data-computable quantities. It further connects model-free and model-based results by reusing recovery theorems from prior work to derive alternative bounds. Experiments on synthetic PFM/LFR graphs and the Political Blogs network illustrate that PFM-based guarantees can be informative, while the SBM bounds are often too loose to certify stability.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on spectral clustering for block models (Ng et al., Rohe–Chatterjee–Yu, Qin–Rohe, Wan–Meila) and stability/clusterability notions (Bilu–Linial, Awasthi, Ben-David; Peng–Sun–Zanetti; Meilă 2007). Its main novelty is turning model-based perturbation analyses into model-free stability guarantees that depend only on computable goodness-of-fit metrics. Compared to stability results based on Cheeger constants or normalized cuts, the present guarantees focus on closeness of fitted models in operator norm and on the geometry of eigenspaces via Davis–Kahan.

Strengths
- Clear and useful framing: if two fitted models both approximate the data well, their clusterings must be close. All terms in the bounds are data-computable.
- Technically clean instantiations for PFM and SBM, with proofs leveraging Davis–Kahan and a local equivalence between subspace closeness and clustering error (Meilă, 2007).
- Conceptual bridge between model-free guarantees and classic model-based recovery theorems, including alternative bounds derived from prior results.
- Discussion of resilience/clusterability and how the framework could reuse existing recovery conditions.
- Experiments include both synthetic and real data, and report when guarantees fail.

Weaknesses and questions
1) Separation between PFM and SBM. Since SBM ⊂ DC-SBM ⊂ PFM and spectral methods are used in all cases, why use the Laplacian for PFM and the adjacency matrix for SBM, and why report wME for PFM but ME for SBM? A unified treatment (e.g., Laplacian for both) would help readers compare bounds. Please also include DC-SBM explicitly; it seems the framework should accommodate it.

2) Scope: fixed K. The template and estimators require the same number of clusters for C and C′. Many practical settings involve K′ ≠ K (e.g., a single cluster in C split into two in C′). Can the framework be extended to handle near-refinements/coarsenings (e.g., via merge–split–aware distances or by allowing K′ = K ± 1 when the “extra” cluster has small volume)?

3) Clarity of definitions. In Proposition 10, please define the inner product ⟨M̂, M⟩F explicitly (presumably trace(M̂TM)), and restate the definitions of M, M̂ there for self-containment.

4) Assumption 5. Is it ∥L̂2 − L2∥F or ∥L̂ − L∥2F? If the former, please provide intuition for squaring Laplacians (e.g., stabilizing signs, emphasizing the top subspace) and a short discussion of when this is preferable.

5) Relation to additional model-free stability literature. Please discuss how your “fit-then-stability” notion relates to perturbation resilience, approximation stability, and spectral-norm–based guarantees (e.g., Kumar–Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013). In particular, contrast your data-adaptive goodness-of-fit approach with instance-level stability assumptions and center-perturbation analyses.

6) Empirical usefulness and interpretation. The PFM guarantees tolerate only small perturbations, and the SBM bounds rarely certify stability. The statement that a large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) under small perturbations deserves a quantitative explanation: spell out how ε and the eigengap enter δ, and illustrate the sensitivity with a controlled example. The apparent contradiction that smoothing (A → (AT A)3) increases eigengaps yet seems to aid stability should be reconciled (e.g., smoothing may reduce noise, decreasing ε faster than it increases the gap term).

7) Experimental alignment. The PFM theorems bound weighted ME (wME), but the perturbations are generated in unweighted ME. Please either generate degree-weighted perturbations or report both wME and ME to align the theory and experiments.

8) Presentation and notation.
- Figure 1: add axis labels/units and clarify in the caption that SBM curves are omitted because δ > 1 (or δ > δ0) throughout; indicate which subplots correspond to which dataset. If SBM is not plotted, state this explicitly.
- Notation reuse: B, C, Y, and λ are overloaded across PFM and SBM. Consider distinct symbols (e.g., λL vs. λA; CD for D-weighted cluster volumes; Y⊥ for complements) to reduce confusion.
- Minor corrections: Aij are independent conditional on cluster labels for i > j; avoid introducing YZ in the SBM estimator unless necessary.

Quality
Technically sound and careful, with proofs that are clear and based on well-established perturbation tools. Some choices (adjacency vs. Laplacian; ME vs. wME) would benefit from justification and unification. The empirical section is honest about limitations but could be strengthened by aligning metrics and clarifying the effects of eigengaps and smoothing.

Clarity
Generally well organized, but notation overload and a few undefined symbols hinder readability. Improving the Related Work section and figure presentation would help.

Originality
The shift from model-based recovery to model-free stability via fitted models and data-driven ε is novel and interesting. The PFM estimator construction and the path from Davis–Kahan to ME via local equivalence are neat.

Significance
If extended to DC-SBM and to settings with K′ ≠ K, and if empirical tolerances improve or are better contextualized, this framework could be a useful tool for validating graph clusterings in practice. As is, the PFM results are promising; the SBM instantiation appears too conservative.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel, data-computable, model-free validation framework for graph clustering.
- Clear theoretical development with connections to existing recovery results.
- Potentially impactful perspective on a practical problem (post hoc validation).

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited empirical utility in the SBM case; small tolerated perturbations in PFM.
- Missing DC-SBM instantiation and lack of support for differing K.
- Clarity and notation issues; Related Work omits relevant stability literature.

Suggestions
- Add DC-SBM and unify the choice of operator (prefer Laplacians for both).
- Extend to near-refinements/coarsenings and report wME in experiments.
- Expand Related Work to include perturbation/approximation stability and spectral-norm clustering results, and compare notions of stability.
- Clarify Assumption 5 and Proposition 10; improve figure labeling and captions.

Minor comments
- Add axis labels to Figure 1; specify datasets and whether SBM curves are omitted.
- Move Section 4.4 into Related Work or a broader “Connections to stability literature” section.
- Be consistent about independence statements (i > j, conditional on labels).
