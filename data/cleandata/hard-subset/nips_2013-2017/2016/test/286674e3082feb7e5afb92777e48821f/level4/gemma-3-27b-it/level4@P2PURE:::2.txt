This paper establishes a relationship between various distance metrics used to compare clusterings. Focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors demonstrate a locally convex-like property for clustering solutions. Specifically, they show that clusterings which equally well estimate the model parameters from network data are also close to each other according to common misclassification-based distance measures. This establishes a useful property of clusterings in network data, proving the equivalence – under certain conditions – between different measures of distance for comparing these clusterings. The results are supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental validation. While the findings are novel, related work has recently appeared in the network literature (e.g., Peng, Sun and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou, Arxiv 2015). The theoretical contributions build upon well-established results in the field. The authors suggest potential applications to clusterability detection, but do not present formal results in this direction. Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly explains its core ideas.
