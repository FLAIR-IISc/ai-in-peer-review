1) Proposes a model-agnostic template for clustering stability guarantees based on data-computable quantities and uses it to obtain exact recovery results.  

2) Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated differently: PFM contains DC-SBM which contains SBM, so why different estimators (Laplacian for PFM, adjacency for SBM) and different error metrics (weighted ME vs ME); no DC-SBM results are provided.  

3) Proposition 10 uses <M', M>_F but the inner-product / definition is not given.  

4) Assumption 5 is ambiguous: is the bound on ||L_hat^2 − L^2||_F or on ||L_hat − L||_F^2, and if the former, what is the rationale for squaring Laplacians?  

5) Paper focuses on disjoint block models—does the template/techniques extend to other settings where spectral methods work (e.g., hierarchical clustering), and how would it specialize there?  

6) Evaluation and estimators assume equal K for C and C'—framework cannot handle differing cluster counts (e.g., one cluster split) even though such C' may be close to C.  

7) Related work omits several model-free stability/robustness frameworks (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013); the relation between this stability notion and those (perturbation/approximation resilience, center perturbation stability) is not discussed.  

8) Simulations suggest tolerated perturbations are tiny: large eigengaps supposedly make PFM sensitive to small perturbations (lines 252–253) but raising A to a power seems to improve stability paradoxically; SBM guarantee conditions also appear very hard to satisfy, calling practical utility into question.  

9) Figure 1 caption claims “For SBM, delta is always greater than delta0” but it’s unclear which subplot this refers to (or whether SBM is plotted).  

10) Theorems for PFM guarantee weighted ME but experiments perturb/report ME—there is a mismatch between theoretical metric and empirical evaluation.  

Minor 1) Figure 1 missing axis labels.  
Minor 2) Line 44: clarify that for block models A_ij for i>j are independent given cluster assignments.  
Minor 3) Notational reuse (B, C, Y, eigenvalues) across PFM and SBM is confusing; recommend consistent, distinct symbols (e.g., Y_perp, C_D, lambda^L, lambda^B).  
Minor 4) Section 4.4 belongs in Related Work as a subsection.
