This paper explores the relationship between various distance metrics for clustering within the context of network models, particularly the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The authors establish a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings which are equally effective at estimating the model parameters for the network data are also proximate in terms of mis-classification distance. This establishes a valuable equivalence between different clustering distance measures for network data, a property that is formally stated and proven. The work is substantiated by both theoretical proofs and experimental validation.

While the results presented are novel, they exist within a body of recent, similar research in network literature, including Peng, Sun and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). Additionally, the theoretical developments seem to be straightforward extensions of well-established principles. The paper suggests potential applications in detecting clusterability, as noted by the authors, but it does not provide formal results in this regard.

Overall, the manuscript is well-written, and its core concepts are explained with considerable lucidity.
