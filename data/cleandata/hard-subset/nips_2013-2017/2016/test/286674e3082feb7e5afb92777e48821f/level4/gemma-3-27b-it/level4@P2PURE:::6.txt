This paper attempts to develop a theoretical foundation for model-free graph clustering. However, the presented results are unconvincing and lack rigor. While the authors aim to construct a novel theory, it largely amounts to handwaving arguments. Specific examples illustrate this weakness: Theorem 1 (labeled as a “Generic Theorem”) relies on the vague condition that a fitted graph is “close” to a cluster structure C; and Proposition 3 (presented as “Informal”) invokes unspecified “standard recovery conditions.”
