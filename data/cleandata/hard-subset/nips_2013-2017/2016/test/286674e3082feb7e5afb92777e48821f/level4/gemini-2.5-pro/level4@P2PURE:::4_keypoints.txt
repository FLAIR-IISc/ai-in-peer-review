Major Points
1. Commends the proposal of a generic, model-free stability template with guarantees computable from data and clustering outputs.
2. Questions treating PFM and SBM separately despite model hierarchy; asks why different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency), why PFM uses weighted ME vs SBM standard ME, and why no DC-SBM results are given.
3. Requests definition of the notation <M', M>_F used in Proposition 10.
4. Flags Assumption 5’s term ||L̂² - L²||_F as suspicious and asks whether it should be ||L̂ - L||_F² or for intuition if squaring is intended.
5. Asks whether the template can be extended beyond disjoint block models to other spectral-clustering-successful settings (e.g., hierarchical clustering, Balakrishnan et al. 2011).
6. Notes a major limitation: the framework only compares clusterings with the same number of clusters, failing to capture near-equivalent splits (K vs K+1).
7. Suggests strengthening related work by discussing model-free stability results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and clarifying relations to perturbation/approximation stability notions.
8. Raises practical concerns from experiments: PFM tolerates only tiny perturbations and has confusing claims about eigengap sensitivity; SBM conditions are very hard to satisfy, undermining applicability.
9. Notes Figure 1 caption ambiguity about "For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0" and asks to specify the referenced subplot or state if SBM is not plotted.
10. Points out mismatch between theory (weighted ME for PFM) and experiments (standard ME) and asks for clarification or correction.

Minor Points
1. Figure 1 axes are missing labels.
2. Line 44’s statement about block models should clarify that A_ij for i ≠ j are independent conditional on node cluster assignments.
3. Criticizes reused notation across PFM and SBM sections (e.g., B, C, Y, λ) as confusing and suggests distinct symbols and expanding the SBM estimator expression in Proposition 8.
4. Recommends moving Section 4.4 to be a subsection of the related work.
