- Develops broad, model-agnostic correctness guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering tools.  
- Establishes guarantees across multiple generative settings without relying on model parameters.  
- Work is interesting, timely, and meaningfully advances a popular research direction.  
- Raises a compelling question about the stability of community detection and provides high-level general results plus instantiations for common graphical models.  
- Manuscript is very clearly written and engaging.  
- Authors thoughtfully motivate their error definitions and the assumptions used in proofs.  
- Primary concern: results rely on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can be nearly impractical in very sparse regimes.  
- Nevertheless, the paper is a strong initial step toward a theory of stability guarantees and is likely to spur further progress.
