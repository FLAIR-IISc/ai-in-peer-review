The paper presents a general template for theoretical stability guarantees for clustering algorithms, quantifying stability as the distance between a clustering C and any other clustering C' that fits the data equally well. The paper provides specific instantiations of this template for two block models, the Planted Function Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), with all quantities involved in the guarantees explicitly computable from data. Furthermore, it leverages these stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees for the models. However, the simulations suggest that the required goodness of fit or the conditions of the theorems may limit the practical utility of the results.

1) The paper tackles the important problem of providing model-agnostic theoretical guarantees for clustering, aiming for guarantees based on quantities computable from data and the output of clustering algorithms. Such model-free theoretical guarantees are valuable for both theoretical understanding and practical application.

2) Given that the PFM encompasses the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM) – which in turn includes the SBM – it’s unclear why PFM and SBM are treated as distinct cases. Since Spectral Clustering performs well across all specified models, why wasn’t a unified model estimator employed? Specifically, why is the Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM? Additionally, the PFM results are presented in terms of weighted Mutual Information (ME), while the SBM results use unweighted ME. A comparison of these two measures would be insightful. Furthermore, results for the DC-SBM are absent; it would be beneficial to see these included.

3) In Proposition 10, could the definition of <M', M><sub>F</sub> be clarified?

4) In Assumption 5: Is the condition ||L̂<sup>2</sup> - L<sup>2</sup>||<sub>F</sub> or ||L̂ - L||<sub>F</sub><sup>2</sup>? If the former, what is the rationale behind squaring the Laplacians?

5) The paper primarily focuses on disjoint clusters. Do the techniques and framework extend beyond block models to other models where Spectral Clustering is effective? For example, Spectral Clustering is known to perform well in hierarchical clustering, and theoretical guarantees exist (e.g., Balakrishan et al., 2011, “Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering”). How would the proposed template adapt to this scenario?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators considered for both PFM and SBM are limited to clusterings C and C' with the same number of clusters. Different clustering algorithms may yield differing numbers of clusters. For instance, the eigenvalues of A might suggest K clusters for C, while the Laplacian of A might suggest K' = K+1 clusters for C', where C' is similar to C except for a single cluster being split into two. While C' may be “close” to C, this scenario is not captured by the current framework.

7) The related work section lacks comprehensive coverage of existing model-free results. For example, relevant papers include Kumar and Kannan (2010, “Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm”), Balcan et al. (2012, “Clustering under Perturbation Resilience”), and Balcan et al. (2013, “Clustering under Approximation Stability”). How does the stability notion in this work relate to those presented in these papers? In particular, how does the stability metric here compare to stability measures based on perturbations of cluster centers?

8) The perturbations used in the PFM simulations appear small. Line 252-253 states that “large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C<sub>0</sub>) even for very small perturbations” without further qualification. The reason for this sensitivity is unclear. Conversely, smoothing the political blog data by raising the power of A seems to *help* stability, despite potentially exaggerating eigenvalue gaps. Furthermore, if even small perturbations can significantly affect model fit, the guarantee’s practical relevance is diminished, especially considering its dependence on calculable data quantities. Lines 248-250 note that the SBM guarantee conditions are difficult to satisfy, which is a significant limitation.

9) The caption for Figure 1 mentions “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ<sub>0</sub>.” Which subplot does this refer to? If SBM data isn’t plotted, the caption should clearly state this.

10) The theoretical guarantees for PFM are expressed in terms of weighted ME, whereas the experiments use perturbations for unweighted ME.

Minor Points:

1) Figure 1 lacks axis labels.

2) (Line 44) For block models, A<sub>ij</sub> for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments for i and j.

3) The symbols B, C, and Y are used with different meanings for PFM and SBM (Section 3.2). This inconsistency could be confusing. Given the paper’s extensive notation, maintaining consistency is crucial. Consider using Y<sub>⊥</sub> and C<sub>D</sub> instead of B and C for PFM. Furthermore, for the SBM estimator, YZ is unnecessary; directly substituting the expression for YZ into Proposition 8 would simplify the presentation. Similarly, using λ<sup>L</sup> and λ<sup>B</sup> would distinguish between the eigenvalues used for PFM and SBM.

4) Section 4.4 would be more appropriately positioned as a subsection within the related work section.
