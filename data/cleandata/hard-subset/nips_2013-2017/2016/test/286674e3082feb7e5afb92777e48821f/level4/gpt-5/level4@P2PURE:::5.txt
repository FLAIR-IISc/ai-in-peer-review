This paper presents very general concentration guarantees, based on spectral clustering techniques, across all types of community detection problems. The authors establish these guarantees for multiple generative models without using any of those models’ parameters. The work is interesting, relevant, and pushes a popular area forward in an important direction. The paper raises a compelling question about the stability of community detection solutions. The authors state and prove the results in broad generality while also focusing on and applying them to several widely studied graphical models. The paper is very well written—an easy and enjoyable read. The authors clearly explain the motivation for nearly all of their choices in error definitions and for the assumptions required by their proofs.

My only substantive criticism, which the authors themselves acknowledge in the conclusion, is the reliance on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight and can be nearly intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems in the very sparse regime. That said, this is a strong first step toward a field of stability results for community detection, and I hope and expect to see further progress soon.
