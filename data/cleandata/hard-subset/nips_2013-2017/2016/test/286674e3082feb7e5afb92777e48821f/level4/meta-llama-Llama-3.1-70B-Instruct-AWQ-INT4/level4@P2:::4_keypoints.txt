- Paper proposes a generic, model-free template giving theoretical stability guarantees for a clustering C based on distance to other clusterings C' that also fit the data well.  
- Authors apply the template to two block models (PFM and SBM), provide data-computable quantities, and derive exact recovery guarantees.  
- Main contribution ties a model-free framework to existing model-based recovery results, strengthening prior theoretical work.  
- Paper is well-written and explains technical details clearly.  
- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated differently: why use Laplacian for PFM but adjacency for SBM, and why different estimators (weighted ME vs ME)?  
- No discussion or results for DC-SBM despite relatedness to SBM.  
- Notation is inconsistent across sections (e.g., B, C, Y defined differently for PFM vs SBM).  
- Framework and evaluation restrict C and C' to the same number of clusters, failing to capture plausible near-miss cases where a cluster splits (K vs K+1).  
- Related work omits several model-free stability results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and does not relate the paper’s stability notion to those prior notions.  
- Minor issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1 and other notation inconsistencies.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially significant contribution that warrants acceptance after revisions addressing the above concerns.
