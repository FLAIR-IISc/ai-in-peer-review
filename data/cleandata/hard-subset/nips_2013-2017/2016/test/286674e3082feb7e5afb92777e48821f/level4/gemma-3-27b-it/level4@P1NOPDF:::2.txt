This paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics used to evaluate clusterings. Focusing on network models like the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a property resembling local convexity for clustering solutions. Specifically, they demonstrate that any two clusterings which fit the network data equally well – in terms of parameter estimation – are also close to each other when measured by various misclassification-based distance metrics. This constitutes a valuable property of clusterings within the context of network data. The paper clearly states and proves the equivalence between these different clustering distance measures. The authors support their theoretical findings with both experimental validation and empirical results. 

The results presented are novel, although recent work in network analysis, such as that by Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015), explores similar themes. The theoretical contributions largely build upon well-established results in the field, representing a logical and relatively straightforward extension of existing knowledge. While the authors suggest potential applications in detecting clusterability, the paper lacks formal results in this direction. 

Overall, the paper is well-written and effectively communicates the core ideas. The explanations are clear and lucid, making the concepts accessible to the reader.
