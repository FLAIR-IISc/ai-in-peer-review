The paper presents a generic template for theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well. Stability is measured in terms of the distance between C and any other C' that also fits the data well. The paper provides specific examples of applying this template to two block models, the Planted Function Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), where all quantities involved in the guarantees can be computed explicitly from the data. Furthermore, it leverages these stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees for the models. However, the simulations suggest that the required goodness of fit or the conditions of the theorems may limit the practical utility of the results.

1) The paper addresses the important problem of providing model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering, with a focus on quantities computable from data and the outputs of clustering algorithms. Such guarantees are valuable for both theoretical understanding and practical applications.

2) Given that PFM encompasses the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM), which in turn includes SBM, it is unclear why PFM and SBM are treated as distinct cases. Considering Spectral Clustering performs well across all specified models, why isn't a unified model estimator employed? Specifically, the use of the Laplacian for PFM while utilizing the adjacency matrix for SBM is unclear. Furthermore, the PFM results are presented in terms of weighted Mutual Information (ME), while the SBM results use standard ME. A comparison between these two metrics would be beneficial. The inclusion of results for DC-SBM is also missing.

3) In Proposition 10, the definition of <M', M>F requires clarification.

4) In Assumption 5, is the expression ||L̂² - L²||F or ||L̂ - L||F²? If the former, the intuition behind squaring the Laplacians should be explained.

5) The paper predominantly focuses on disjoint clusters. Do the techniques and framework extend to other models where Spectral Clustering is effective, such as hierarchical clustering (e.g., as discussed in Balakrishnan et al., 2011)? How would the proposed template apply in such cases?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators considered for both PFM and SBM are limited to clusterings C and C' with the same number of clusters. However, different algorithms may yield differing numbers of clusters. For instance, eigenvalues of A might suggest K clusters for C, while the Laplacian of A suggests K' = K+1 clusters for C', where C' is a minor refinement of C. This scenario, where C' is “close” to C, is not captured by the current framework.

7) The related work section lacks comprehensive coverage of existing model-free results, such as those by Kumar and Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012, 2013). How does the notion of stability employed here relate to these prior works? In particular, how does it compare to stability measures based on perturbation of cluster centers?

8) The simulations for PFM involve small perturbations. Line 252-253 states that “large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations” without sufficient qualification. The underlying reason for this behavior is unclear. Conversely, smoothing the political blog data by raising the power of A seems to improve stability despite exacerbating eigenvalue gaps. Additionally, if such small perturbations can significantly impact model fit, the guarantees lose practical relevance, particularly given the paper's emphasis on computability. Lines 248-250 indicate that the SBM guarantee conditions are difficult to satisfy, which is undesirable.

9) The caption of Figure 1 mentions “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0”. Clarify which subplot this refers to; if SBM is not plotted, this should be explicitly stated.

10) The theoretical guarantees for PFM are provided for weighted ME, whereas the experiments utilize standard ME.

Minor Points:
1) Figure 1 lacks axis labels.
2) (Line 44) For block models, Aij for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments for i and j.
3) The symbols B, C, and Y are defined differently for PFM and SBM, which can be confusing. Maintaining consistency is crucial, especially given the paper’s extensive notation. Consider using Y⊥ and CD instead of B and C for PFM. Similarly, for the SBM estimator, YZ is unnecessary; the expression of YZ can be used directly in Proposition 8. Using λL and λB to distinguish eigenvalues would enhance clarity.
4) Section 4.4 is more appropriately placed within the related work section.
