- Presents a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that gives stability guarantees without assuming a data-generating model.  
- Core idea: if a clustering fits the data well, any other equally good clustering must be close, enabling model-free guarantees.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, connecting to and strengthening some existing model-based recovery results.  
- Theorems are clearly stated, theoretically rigorous, and build on spectral graph theory and perturbation bounds.  
- Effectively relates to prior NIPS work and the literature on spectral clustering and community detection.  
- Experimental validation supports the theoretical claims and illustrates practical implications.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with consistent (though dense) notation.  
- Uses a weighted Misclassification Error (wME) for PFM, which may be less intuitive than standard Misclassification Error and needs more justification or comparison.  
- The proxy-model approach (fitting a model to a clustering) constrains the notion of a “good” clustering and may limit applicability when the true process differs from the model class.  
- Stability guarantees are sensitive to graph smoothing, indicating practical dependence on preprocessing.  
- SBM-related bounds are restrictive and often not satisfiable in experiments, suggesting the SBM formulation or bounds may need refinement.  
- The use of “hats” on observed quantities is unconventional and slightly increases reader cognitive load.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, addresses an important limitation, and backed by experiments and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on proxy models, weaker SBM results, contentious distance metric choice, and minor readability issues.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of acceptance with recommended revisions to justify the PFM distance metric, investigate parameter sensitivity, and improve notation.
