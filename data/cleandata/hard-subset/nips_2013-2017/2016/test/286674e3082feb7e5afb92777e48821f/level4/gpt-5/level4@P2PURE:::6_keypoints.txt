- Paper aims to develop a model-free theoretical framework for graph clustering.
- Results are naive and unconvincing.
- Authors claim a new theory, but the development is hand-waving and lacks rigor.
- Theorem 1 relies on a vague assertion that a solution “which also fits G well is close to C,” without formal justification.
- Proposition 3 appeals to unspecified “standard recovery conditions,” leaving key assumptions undefined.
- Overall informal formulations and lack of specificity make the contribution unpersuasive.
