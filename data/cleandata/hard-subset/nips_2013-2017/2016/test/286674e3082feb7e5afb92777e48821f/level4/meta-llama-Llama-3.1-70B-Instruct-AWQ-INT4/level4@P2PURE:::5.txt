This paper provides very general concentration guarantees based on spectral clustering techniques for all types of community detection problems. They prove these guarantees for different generative models without having to use any of the parameters from those models. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area of study in an important direction. The authors pose a very interesting question about the stability of solutions for the community detection problem, and they phrase and prove their results in great generality while also focusing and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. The paper is well-written and easy to read, with the authors explaining the motivation behind their definitions of error and assumptions required for their proofs. The only significant criticism is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms and results that are not tight and are even almost intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems when graphs are very sparse, a limitation the authors themselves acknowledge in their conclusion. Nevertheless, this is a great start in the field of stability results for community detection, and it is hoped that this line of research will be progressed further in the near future.
