- Proposes a novel, model-free framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming a specific data-generating model.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a generic theorem linking clustering quality to robustness under perturbations.  
- Introduces relevant block models (SBM, DC-SBM, PFM) as background and motivating examples.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for Preference Frame Models (PFM) and Stochastic Block Models (SBM).  
- Presents a PFM estimation algorithm and proves consistency under standard recovery conditions.  
- Presents an SBM estimation algorithm and shows it yields model-free clustering guarantees.  
- Demonstrates that existing model-based recovery theorems can be leveraged to obtain model-free guarantees.  
- Provides alternative model-free guarantees for PFM and SBM under different assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation on simulated and real datasets supports the effectiveness of the proposed framework.  
- Technical results are sound overall, offering a new perspective that bridges model-based and model-free clustering.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the framework’s limitations and practical scope.  
- Weakness: insufficient insight into the nuanced relationship between model-based and model-free approaches.  
- Weakness: some technical results need more detailed proofs and explanations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, sound theory, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient discussion of limitations, limited conceptual insights between paradigms, and incomplete technical exposition.
