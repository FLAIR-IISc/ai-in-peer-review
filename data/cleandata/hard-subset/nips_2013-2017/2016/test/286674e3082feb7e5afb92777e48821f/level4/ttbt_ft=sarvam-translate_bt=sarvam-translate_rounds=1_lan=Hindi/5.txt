This paper provides a very general concentration guarantee based on spectral clustering techniques for all types of community identity problems. They prove these guarantees for various generative models without using any parameters from those models. This work was interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area of study in a significant direction. This paper raises a very interesting question about the stability of the solution to the community identity problem. They expressed and proved these results in great generality, as well as focusing and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. This paper was very well written and easy and enjoyable to read. They explained the motivation behind almost all the methods chosen to define the error and create the necessary assumptions for their proofs. The only real criticism I had while reading this paper, which the authors themselves stated in their conclusion, is that these proofs relied on spectral algorithms and results that are not rigorous and are even almost impenetrable for many of the most interesting community identity problems when the graphs are very sparse. That being said, this was a great start in the field of stability results for community identity, which I hope will go even further in the near future.
