- Presents distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that avoid specific generative models like SBM.  
- Introduces a framework where any alternative clustering with comparable fit to the observed graph must be “close” to the original.  
- Provides mathematically rigorous, well-motivated theorems with clear explanations.  
- Paper is generally well-written and understandable even without the supplement; some material moved due to page limits.  
- Notation using “hats” for observed quantities is unconventional but not overly harmful to clarity.  
- Experiments show graph smoothing is necessary in some real-world cases for the framework to produce meaningful results.  
- Main concern: the PFM result uses a non-standard distance metric between clusterings, which may limit acceptance and is debatable in naturalness.  
- Secondary concern: approach relies on reconstructing a proxy model from a clustering, constraining the notion of a “good” clustering to normalized Laplacian approximation.  
- This proxy-model requirement may restrict practical applicability and raise questions on real-world impact.  
- Framework’s sensitivity to smoothing indicates limited robustness without preprocessing.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate practical advantages over existing methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, offers insights into model-free clustering guarantees, and has extension potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical limitations from proxy reliance and smoothing, non-standard metric, and insufficient experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the non-standard distance metric, discuss proxy-model limitations, and expand experimental comparisons.
