This paper establishes a relationship between different distances of clustering, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The authors prove a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that for any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well from network data, these clusterings are also near each other in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This result provides a useful property of clusterings in network data, showing the equivalence between different measures of clustering distance for network data.

The paper offers both theoretical and experimental validations of its results, making the findings more robust. Although the results are novel, similar work has recently appeared in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015), and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). Furthermore, the theoretical components of the paper are relatively straightforward extensions of well-established results. As noted by the authors, their work has potential applications in detecting clusterability, but the paper does not provide formal results in this regard.

The paper is well-written, effectively conveying its core ideas in a clear and concise manner. Overall, the authors have made a valuable contribution to the understanding of clustering distances in network data, although the impact of their work could be further enhanced by exploring its implications for clusterability detection.
