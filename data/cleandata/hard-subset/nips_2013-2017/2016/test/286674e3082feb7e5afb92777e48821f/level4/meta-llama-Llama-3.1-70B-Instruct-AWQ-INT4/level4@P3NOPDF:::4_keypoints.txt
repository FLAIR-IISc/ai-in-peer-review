- Proposes a generic template for provable clustering stability relative to other good-fitting clusterings and demonstrates it on PFM and SBM with computable data-dependent quantities.  
- Uses stability results to obtain exact recovery guarantees for the examined block models.  
- Simulations indicate the goodness-of-fit measure or theorem conditions make the practical usefulness of the guarantees questionable.  
- Unclear rationale for treating PFM and SBM differently (PFM uses Laplacian and weighted MSE, SBM uses adjacency and MSE) despite model containment and similar spectral methods.  
- Notational and definitional inconsistencies (e.g., missing definition of <M',M>_F in Proposition 10; ambiguous norm in Assumption 5).  
- Questions about extending the template beyond disjoint clusters to other settings where spectral clustering works (e.g., hierarchical clustering).  
- Framework assumes C and C' have the same number of clusters, limiting applicability when different algorithms produce different K.  
- Related work section omits relevant model-free stability/robustness results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and lacks comparison to those notions of stability.  
- Simulated perturbations tolerated for PFM are very small; claims about eigengap effects and smoothing (powering adjacency) are unclear and potentially undermine practical guarantees.  
- Minor presentation issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1, unclear block-model notation in line 44, and inconsistent use of symbols (B, C, Y) across PFM and SBM.  
- Overall: interesting and important template but needs clarification of notation, consistent treatment of models/estimators, discussion of limitations, and better connection to prior work.
