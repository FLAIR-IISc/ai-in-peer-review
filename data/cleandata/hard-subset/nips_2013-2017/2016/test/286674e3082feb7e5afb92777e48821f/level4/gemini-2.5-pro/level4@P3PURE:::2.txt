This paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics for clusterings in the context of network data. Focusing on the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a locally convex-like property for clustering. Specifically, they show that any two clusterings that explain the network data equally well (in terms of model parameter estimation) must also be close to one another with respect to misclassification distance. This equivalence between model fit and clustering similarity is an interesting property for network data, and the paper provides both theoretical and experimental validation for its claims. The paper is well-written and lucidly explains its core ideas.

However, the contribution has some limitations. While the results are novel, they follow a recent line of work with a similar flavor in the network literature (e.g., Peng, Sun and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou, Arxiv 2015). Furthermore, the theoretical analysis appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of well-known results. The paper also suggests potential applications in detecting clusterability, but formal results in this direction are not provided, which would have strengthened the paper's impact.
