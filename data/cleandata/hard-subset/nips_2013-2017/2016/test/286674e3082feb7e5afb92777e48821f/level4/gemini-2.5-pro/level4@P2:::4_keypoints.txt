- Paper proposes a model-free stability framework: if clustering C fits observed graph data well under a model class, any other clustering C' that also fits well must be structurally close to C.

- Strength — Novelty/importance: Addresses practical/theoretical need for model-free validation of clustering beyond assuming perfect generative models.

- Strength — Computable guarantees: Derived bounds are fully data-dependent and computable without unknown distributional constants.

- Strength — Elegant framework: Reuses model-based recovery logic to create an intuitive, verifiable goodness-of-fit–centered validation approach.

- Weakness — Practical utility: Experimental results suggest bounds are too loose — SBM conditions never met in experiments and PFM stability holds only for extremely small perturbations.

- Weakness — Counterintuitive sensitivity: Authors’ claim that a “large eigengap” causes sensitivity to perturbations is unclear and likely misleading.

- Weakness — Clarity/technical justification: PFM and SBM are analyzed differently without clear rationale; choices like using squared Laplacians in Assumption 5 are unexplained.

- Weakness — Notation issues: Reuse of symbols (B, C, Y, etc.) across sections creates avoidable confusion.

- Weakness — Limited scope: Framework requires same number of clusters K for comparisons and is restricted to block models, limiting practical applicability and extensions (e.g., splits or different K).

- Weakness — Missed literature: Related work insufficiently situates the paper within broader stability and model-free clustering literature (e.g., perturbation/approximation resilience, spectral-norm clustering).

- Minor point — Notation clarity: Define the Frobenius inner product notation <M̂, M>_F explicitly in Proposition 10.

- Minor point — Figures/visuals: Figures are hard to interpret (missing axis labels); unclear whether SBM results are plotted or omitted.

- Minor point — Metric mismatch: PFM theory uses weighted ME but experiments perturb/measure with standard (unweighted) ME without justification.

- Minor point — Organization: Section 4.4 (“Clusterability and resilience”) fits better as part of the introduction or related work rather than as a standalone section.
