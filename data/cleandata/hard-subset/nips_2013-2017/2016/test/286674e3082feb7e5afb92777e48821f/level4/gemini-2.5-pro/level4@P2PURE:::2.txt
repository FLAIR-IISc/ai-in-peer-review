This paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics for clusterings on network data. Focusing on models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a "locally convex-like" property. The core result shows that any two clusterings providing equally good parameter estimates for a fitted network model are also necessarily close in terms of misclassification distance. The authors validate these claims with both theoretical analysis and experimental results.

While the findings are novel, they align with a recent trend of similar work in the network literature (e.g., Peng, Sun, and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou, Arxiv 2015). Furthermore, the theoretical contributions appear to be straightforward extensions of well-known results. The authors suggest that this work could have applications in detecting clusterability; however, formal results in this direction are not provided.

Despite these limitations, the paper is well-written and explains its core ideas lucidly.
