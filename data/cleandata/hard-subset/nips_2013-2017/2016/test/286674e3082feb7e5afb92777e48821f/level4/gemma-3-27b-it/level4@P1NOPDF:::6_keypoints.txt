- Paper attempts a theoretical framework for model-free graph clustering but the results are unconvincing and naive.
- Proposed approach lacks rigor; statements in Theorem 1 and Proposition 3 are vague and imprecise.
- Core idea and key assumption are not clearly specified or validated, and their link to clustering performance is unclear.
- Theoretical claims are insufficiently mathematically grounded and need precise formulations and definitions.
- Paper does not convincingly differentiate itself from existing spectral clustering and related work.
- Empirical evaluation is inadequate (arbitrary datasets, insufficient baselines and metrics), failing to support the claims.
- Needed improvements: precise theorems, justification of assumptions, broader experiments with relevant baselines, and clearer exposition of advantages.
- Overall assessment: underdeveloped contribution lacking theoretical and empirical support; recommend rejection.
