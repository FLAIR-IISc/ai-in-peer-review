Paper links and compares various clustering distance metrics.  
Focuses on network models, specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
Demonstrates a locally convex-like property for clustering solutions.  
Key finding: clusterings that fit the network data equally well are close under common misclassification-based distance measures.  
Establishes an equivalence between different clustering distance measures in the network context.  
Provides both theoretical analysis and experimental validation supporting the results.  
Findings are novel but build on and reasonably extend prior work (Peng et al., COLT 2015; Chao et al., ArXiv 2015).  
Notes potential applications in clusterability detection, though formal results there are absent.  
Paper is well-written and clearly presents central concepts.
