This paper presents a general template for theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C, which fits well with the data. Stability is determined by measuring the distance between C and any other C', where C and other C's fit well with the same data. This paper also provides specific examples of using the templates for two block models, PFM and SBM, and all quantities involved in the guarantee can be clearly calculated from the data. Furthermore, it uses the stability results obtained from this template to show the correct recovery guarantee for the models. However, the simulations suggest that the results are not very useful due to the conditions of the goodness fit or the given theorems.

1) This paper considers the problem of providing a general template for theoretical guarantees for clustering without assuming that the data is taken from a specific model. Its goal is to obtain guarantees using quantities that can be calculated from the data and compared to the outputs of the clustering algorithm. Providing such model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering is important for both theoretical and practical purposes.

2) Since PFM includes DC-SBM as a special class, which in turn includes SBM, it is not clear why the two fields (PFM and SBM) are considered differently. Since spectral clustering works well for all models, why can't the same model estimator be used? In particular, it is not clear why a Laplacian is used for PFM, while an adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Also, the results for PFM are for weighted ME, while for SBM it is in terms of ME. How are they comparable to each other? Furthermore, why are the results for DC-SBM not given?

3) In proposal 10, what is the definition of < M', M >F?

4) In assumption 5: is it ||L^{hat}^2 - L^2||F or ||L^{hat} - L||F^2? If the first, what is the idea behind squaring the Laplacian?

5) This paper mainly focuses on disjoint clusters. Are the techniques/recipes used in this paper extended beyond the block model to other models for which spectral clustering works well? For example, spectral clustering is known to work well for hierarchical clustering and theoretical guarantees are also available ("Spectral Clustering for Noise Threshold", S. Balakrishnan et al. 2011). How will the template work in this case?

6) The evaluation metrics considered and the estimators considered in both PFM and SBM are limited to C and C', where the number of clusters is the same. Different clustering algorithms can potentially give different numbers of clusters. For example, let's say the eigenvalue of A can propose K clusters for C and the Laplacian of A can propose K' = K + 1 clusters for C', where all the clusters of C and C' are the same, except for one cluster that is divided into two parts. C' is still "close" to C, but it cannot be captured by the given structure.

7) Many model-free results are not reflected in the related work section: for example: "Spectral Norms and Clustering with K-Means Algorithms", Kumar and Kannan, 2010. "Clustering under Perturbation Resilience", Balkan et al. 2012. "Clustering under Approximation Stability", Balkan et al. 2013. How does the concept of stability considered in this work relate to these works? In particular, how is stability in this paper related to the measured stability for the perturbations of the centers for the two clusterings?

8) The perturbations that the simulations tolerate for PFM are very small. Line 252-253 generally states that "large eigenvalues separate PFM(G, C) from PFM(G, C0), even for very small perturbations", which does not explain the statement well. It is not clear why this is so. On the other hand, the political blog data A is smoothed by increasing the power, which will further increase the distance of the eigenvalues, but in this case it seems to help increase stability? Also, if such small perturbations can affect model fit, it is not very useful for practical purposes (which is one of the main selling points, because the quantities can actually be calculated from the data). Line 248-250 states that SBM guarantee conditions seem very difficult to meet, which is unfortunate.

9) The caption of figure 1 mentions "For SBM, \delta is always greater than \delta0". Which of these subplots is being referred to? If SBM is not plotted, it should be clearly stated in the caption.

Since there are many notations in the paper, it is better to maintain consistency. For example, Y{\orthogonal} and CD can be used instead of B and C for PFM respectively.
