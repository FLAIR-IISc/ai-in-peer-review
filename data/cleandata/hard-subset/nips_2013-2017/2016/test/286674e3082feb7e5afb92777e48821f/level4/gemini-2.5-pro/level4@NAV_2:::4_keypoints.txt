- Paper proposes a model-free framework proving clustering stability: if a clustering C fits data well, any other good-fit clustering C' must be a small perturbation of C.
- Novelty: shifts from model-based recovery guarantees to model-free stability using model-based analysis techniques.
- Practicality: all quantities in the guarantees (e.g., ε, δ) are computable from the observed graph and a given clustering.
- Methodology clarity concern: PFM and SBM are analyzed with different objects (normalized Laplacian vs adjacency) and different error metrics (wME vs ME) without clear motivation.
- Missing analysis: Degree-Corrected SBM (DC-SBM) is not treated despite its practical importance.
- Scope limitation: framework only compares partitions with the same number of clusters K, limiting applicability to common cases with different K.
- Related work omission: fails to discuss and compare to prior model-free stability notions (e.g., perturbation resilience, approximation stability from Kumar & Kannan, Balcan et al.).
- PFM experiments: guarantees seem informative only for extremely small perturbations, questioning practical usefulness.
- Eigengap paradox: authors' explanation that large eigengap causes rapid model change is unconvincing and conflicts with results where smoothing (increasing eigengap) improved stability.
- SBM experiments: stability conditions for SBM were never satisfied in experiments, making those bounds non-informative even on synthetic LFR data.
- Loose bounds concern: if SBM conditions fail on model-fitting synthetic data, the theoretical bounds may be too loose for practice.
- Notational and presentation issues: reuse of symbols across PFM and SBM sections confuses readers.
- Figure problems: Figure 1 lacks axis labels and fails to note that SBM results were not plotted because conditions weren't met.
- Theory-experiment mismatch: PFM theory bounds weighted ME but empirical perturbations use standard ME, creating inconsistency.
- Request for clearer rationale: authors should justify using Laplacian for PFM and adjacency for SBM and compare resulting wME vs ME bounds.
- Technical clarity needed: define inner product notation in Proposition 10 and give intuition for bounding ||L̂² - L²||F in Assumption 5.
- Relation to prior center-based stability: clarify how proposed stability connects to Balcan et al.'s center-based notions.
- Extensions and analysis requests: discuss extending techniques to other spectral-successful models (e.g., hierarchical clustering) and provide deeper analysis of the eigengap claim, especially for datasets like political blogs.
