The paper presents a general framework for theoretically guaranteeing the stability of a clustering *C* that well-fits the data. Stability is defined as the distance between *C* and any other clustering *C'* that also fits the data well. The framework is instantiated for two block models, the Planted Function Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), with all quantities involved in the guarantees explicitly computable from data. These stability results are then leveraged to derive exact recovery guarantees for these models. However, the simulations raise concerns about the practical utility of the results, potentially due to the strictness of the goodness-of-fit requirements or the conditions imposed by the theorems.

1) The paper addresses the important problem of providing model-agnostic theoretical guarantees for clustering, aiming for guarantees based on quantities computable from data and clustering outputs. This is valuable for both theoretical progress and practical application.

2) Given that the PFM encompasses the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM), which in turn includes the SBM, the differing treatment of PFM and SBM is unclear. Since Spectral Clustering performs well across all specified models, why were different model estimators chosen? Specifically, why is the Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM? Furthermore, the results for PFM are presented in terms of weighted Mutual Information (ME), while those for SBM use standard ME. A comparison of these metrics is needed. Also, results for DC-SBM are absent.

3) In Proposition 10, the definition of <*M’*, *M*>*<sub>F</sub> is unclear.

4) In Assumption 5, is the condition ||*L̂*<sup>2</sup> - *L*<sup>2</sup>||<sub>F</sub> or ||*L̂* - *L*||<sub>F</sub><sup>2</sup>? If the former, the rationale for squaring the Laplacians should be explained.

5) The paper primarily focuses on disjoint clusters. Does the proposed framework extend to other models where Spectral Clustering succeeds, beyond block models? For example, Spectral Clustering is effective for hierarchical clustering, with existing theoretical guarantees (Balakrishan et al., 2011). How would the template apply in this context?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators considered for both PFM and SBM are limited to clusterings *C* and *C'* with the same number of clusters. Algorithms can produce different numbers of clusters; for instance, eigenvalue analysis of *A* might suggest *K* clusters for *C*, while the Laplacian of *A* suggests *K’* = *K* + 1 clusters for *C’*, with *C’* being a refinement of *C*. The current framework cannot capture such scenarios.

7) The related work section inadequately reflects existing model-free results, such as Kumar & Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012, 2013). How does the stability notion in this work relate to those presented in these papers, particularly concerning the stability measured for perturbation of cluster centers?

8) The perturbations used in the PFM simulations are very small. The statement in lines 252-253 – that “large eigengap makes PFM(*G*, *C*) differ from PFM(*G*, *C*<sub>0</sub>) even for very small perturbations” – lacks sufficient justification. It's unclear why this occurs. Conversely, smoothing the political blog data by raising *A* to a power seemingly exacerbates eigenvalue gaps, yet improves stability. Moreover, if small perturbations can significantly impact model fit, the guarantees become less useful in practice, undermining a core motivation (data-computability). Lines 248-250 note the difficulty in satisfying the SBM guarantee conditions, which is also concerning.

9) The caption of Figure 1 states that “δ is always greater than δ<sub>0</sub>”. Which subplot does this statement refer to? If SBM is not plotted, this should be explicitly clarified in the caption.

10) The theorems for PFM provide guarantees for weighted ME, while the experiments perturb ME.

Minor Points: 1) Axes labels are missing from Figure 1. 2) (Line 44) For block models, *A<sub>ij</sub>* for *i* > *j* are independent given the cluster assignments of *i* and *j*. 3) The symbols *B*, *C*, and *Y* are defined differently for PFM and SBM, leading to potential confusion. Given the numerous notations, maintaining consistency is crucial. Consider using *Y<sub>⊥</sub>* and *C<sub>D</sub>* instead of *B* and *C* for PFM, respectively. Also, for the SBM estimator, *YZ* is unnecessary; directly substituting its expression into Proposition 8 would improve clarity. Similarly, using *λ<sup>L</sup>* and *λ<sup>B</sup>* would distinguish between eigenvalues in PFM and SBM. 4) Section 4.4 is more appropriately placed within the related work section.
