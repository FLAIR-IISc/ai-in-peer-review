- Presents novel, distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that do not assume a generative model like the SBM.  
- Main premise: any partition that fits the observed graph well implies all similarly good partitions are structurally close to it.  
- Paper is well-written, self-contained, and clear despite some material moved to the supplement.  
- Minor presentation quibble: unconventional use of “hat” notation for observed quantities (e.g., \hat{L}).  
- Theoretical results are significant and supported by useful commentary and illustrations.  
- Experiments show some real-world graphs require smoothing before a recoverable structure (per the theory) can be found.  
- Many standard algorithms can recover ground-truth structure on raw data where the proposed approach needs smoothing.  
- Key limitation: the PFM result uses a non-standard clustering distance whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Fundamental constraint: theory relies on reconstructing a proxy model from a clustering (e.g., PFM) and comparing that to the graph, restricting “good” clusterings to those approximating the normalized Laplacian well.  
- These restrictions are necessary for guarantees but raise concerns about broader applicability and performance on real-world datasets.
