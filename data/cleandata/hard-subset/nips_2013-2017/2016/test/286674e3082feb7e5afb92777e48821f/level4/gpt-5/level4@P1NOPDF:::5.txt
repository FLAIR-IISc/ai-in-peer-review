This paper provides broad concentration guarantees for community detection based on spectral clustering techniques, applicable across a wide range of problem settings. The authors establish these guarantees under several generative models without requiring knowledge of any model parameters. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area in an important direction.

The paper raises an important question about the stability of community-detection solutions. The authors formulate and prove results at a high level of generality, and also instantiate and apply them to specific, widely studied graphical models. The writing is clear and engaging. The motivation behind the chosen error definitions and the assumptions required for the proofs is carefully explained.

My only substantive criticism—also acknowledged by the authors in their conclusion—is that the analysis depends on spectral algorithms and bounds that are not tight, and can become nearly intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems in the very sparse regime. That said, this is a strong starting point for a line of stability results in community detection, and I hope and expect to see further progress in the near future.
