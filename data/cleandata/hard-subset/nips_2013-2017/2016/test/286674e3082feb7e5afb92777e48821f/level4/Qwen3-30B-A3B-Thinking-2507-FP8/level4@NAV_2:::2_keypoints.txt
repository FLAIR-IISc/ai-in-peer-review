Introduces a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees using computable data statistics instead of assuming a specific generative model.  
Demonstrates how existing model-based recovery theorems can be repurposed to yield model-free guarantees, bounding weighted misclassification error in terms of eigengap and goodness-of-fit.  
Theoretical results are technically sound with rigorous proofs using Davis–Kahan perturbation theory and detailed theorems for PFM and SBM, supported by experiments.  
All quantities in the bounds are computable from the data and the paper appropriately notes limitations when data poorly fits the model.  
Exceptionally well-written and organized, with consistent notation and clear explanations that make the technical content accessible; experiments concretely illustrate the framework.  
Novel in providing model-free guarantees for block models and extending understanding of clustering stability despite building on established spectral clustering theory.  
Significant for community detection and clusterability assessment, and can strengthen existing model-based results (e.g., improving an error bound by a factor of 128), though applications need more formal development.  
Strengths: conceptually elegant connection between model-based and model-free guarantees, rigorous proofs, computable bounds, and practical validation.  
Weaknesses: novelty somewhat limited by recent related work; extensions are relatively straightforward; connections to Peng et al. (2015) and Chao et al. (2015) need clearer distinction; more concrete practical examples would help.  
Arguments for acceptance: technically sound framework, clear exposition, and practical potential for network analysis.  
Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and depth relative to related work and insufficiently developed application impact.
