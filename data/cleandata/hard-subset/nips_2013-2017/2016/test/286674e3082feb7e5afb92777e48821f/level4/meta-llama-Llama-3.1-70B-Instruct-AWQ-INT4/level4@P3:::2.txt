This paper proposes a framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a specific model. The authors consider two popular network models, the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), and establish a locally convex-like property for clustering. Specifically, they show that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well are also close to each other in terms of mis-classification distance measures.

The paper provides a nice property of clusterings in network data, which has both theoretical and experimental validations. The results are novel, but there have been similar works in the network literature. The theoretical parts of the paper are simple extensions of well-known results. The authors also discuss potential applications in detecting clusterability, although formal results in this regard are not provided.

The paper is well-written and explains the core ideas clearly. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and position their contributions within the existing literature.
