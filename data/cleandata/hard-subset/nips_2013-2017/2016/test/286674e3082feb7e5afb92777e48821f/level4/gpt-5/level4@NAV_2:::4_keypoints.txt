- Proposes a general, model-free template for theoretical stability guarantees of a clustering C based on distance to any alternative C' that also fits the observed graph well.  
- Instantiates the template for two block-model families (PFM and SBM) with all quantities computable from data and derives exact-recovery-style implications.  
- Empirical results indicate the chosen goodness-of-fit measures or the stated conditions make the guarantees of limited practical utility.  
- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated asymmetrically despite model containment (PFM ⊃ DC-SBM ⊃ SBM); DC-SBM is omitted.  
- Inconsistency in estimators: Laplacian used for PFM vs adjacency for SBM, and PFM results use weighted ME while SBM uses unweighted ME — comparison unclear.  
- Request to define the inner-product notation ⟨M', M⟩_F precisely in Proposition 10.  
- Assumption 5 is ambiguous: is it ||L̂^2 − L^2||_F or (||L̂ − L||_F)^2, and if the former, why square the Laplacians?  
- The paper focuses on disjoint partitions — unclear whether the recipe extends to other settings where spectral methods succeed (e.g., hierarchical clustering).  
- Evaluation and estimators assume C and C' have the same number of clusters K, so the framework cannot capture natural split/merge closeness (e.g., K vs K+1 where one cluster is split).  
- Related Work omits relevant model-free lines (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013); need discussion of how this stability notion relates to perturbation resilience/approximate stability.  
- PFM simulations show tolerable perturbations are very small; claims about eigengap causing sensitivity need clarification, and smoothing observations (political blogs) seem contradictory — practical usefulness is questionable.  
- Figure 1 caption is unclear about which panels assert “For SBM, delta is always greater than delta0”; if SBM curves are omitted, caption should say so.  
- Theorems for PFM use weighted ME but experiments use unweighted ME — mismatch between theory and empirical evaluation.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels; line 44’s independence phrasing should be clarified; symbols B, C, Y are overloaded across sections and should be standardized; Section 4.4 might belong under Related Work.
