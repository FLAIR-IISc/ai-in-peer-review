- Claim of "model-free" guarantees is misleading: results are only applied to block models (PFM, SBM, DC-SBM) and rely on model-specific assumptions.  
- Unclear rationale for treating PFM, DC-SBM, and SBM as distinct cases despite subsumption relationships.  
- Inconsistent use of Laplacian (PFM) vs adjacency matrix (SBM) and mismatch between weighted ME (PFM) and standard ME (SBM) lack justification.  
- DC-SBM results are omitted, weakening the framework’s claimed generality.  
- Stability conditions are practically unverifiable and appear overly stringent (e.g., Assumption 5 for SBM).  
- Simulations contradict theoretical claims (e.g., eigengap effects and smoothing on political blog data), undermining practical relevance.  
- Undefined notation: inner product ⟨M', M⟩_F in Proposition 10 is not defined.  
- Ambiguous assumption: use of ||L̂² - L²||_F is unclear—possibly a typo or unjustified squaring of Laplacians.  
- Framework requires identical cluster counts for C and C', excluding realistic cases where spectral methods imply different K.  
- Inconsistent and redefined symbols across sections (B, C, Y), causing confusion and making propositions hard to follow.  
- Related work omissions: fails to cite or compare to model-free clustering/stability literature (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013).  
- Experimental/presentation flaws: incorrect Figure 1 caption, missing axes labels, and mismatch between experiments (ME perturbations) and theoretical guarantees (weighted ME).  
- Structural critiques: Section 4.4 belongs in Related Work; Line 44 should state conditional independence for block models.  
- Conclusion: contributions are theoretically interesting but practically limited due to overstated generality, unverifiable/contradictory conditions, technical gaps, and missing literature.  
- Recommendation: Reject—substantial revision needed (restrict scope, fix technical issues, add literature and real-data validation) before resubmission.
