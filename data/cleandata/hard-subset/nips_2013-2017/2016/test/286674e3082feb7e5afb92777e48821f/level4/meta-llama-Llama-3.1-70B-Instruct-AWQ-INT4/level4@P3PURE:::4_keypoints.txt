- Proposes a generic template for theoretical stability guarantees of a clustering C based on distance to any other clustering C' that also fits the data well.  
- Demonstrates template on two block models (PFM and SBM) and claims all quantities in the guarantees can be computed from data.  
- Uses stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees for PFM and SBM.  
- Simulation results indicate the goodness-of-fit measure or theorem conditions make the guarantees not very useful in practice.  
- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated differently despite PFM containing DC-SBM and SBM as special cases.  
- Questionable choice of estimators: Laplacian used for PFM but adjacency matrix for SBM, despite spectral methods working for both.  
- Results use weighted mean squared error (ME) for PFM and ME for SBM without comparison or justification.  
- Surprising omission of direct results for degree-corrected SBM (DC-SBM).  
- Missing/muddled technical definitions: e.g., inner product <M',M>_F in Proposition 10 is not defined.  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5 norm notation—unclear if ||Lhat^2 - L^2||_F or ||Lhat - L||_F^2 is intended and why Laplacians are squared.  
- Paper focuses on disjoint block models; unclear if template extends to other spectral-clustering-friendly models (e.g., hierarchical clustering).  
- Framework requires C and C' to have the same number of clusters, so it cannot capture near-equality cases where one clustering splits a cluster (K vs K+1).  
- Related work misses several model-free stability/robustness results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and lacks comparison to those stability notions.  
- Simulations suggest PFM tolerates only tiny perturbations; the claim about large eigengap causing instability is insufficiently explained and possibly contradictory to empirical smoothing results.  
- Conditions for the SBM guarantees appear very stringent and hard to satisfy in practice.  
- Minor presentation issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1, ambiguous caption references to SBM plots, and inconsistency about whether SBM is plotted.  
- Notational inconsistencies across models (e.g., B, C, Y having different meanings) creating confusion.  
- Section 4.4 placement is awkward and would fit better as a related-work subsection.
