This paper describes a relationship between various clustering distances. It considers network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and proves a property such as locally convex for clustering. It is shown that for any two clusterings that equally estimate the parameters of models fitted to network data, they are also close to each other in terms of misclassification distance measures. This paper describes a good property of clustering in network data. This paper describes and proves the property of equivalence between different distance measures for clustering in network data. The results described in this paper provide both theoretical and experimental verification. The results presented in this paper are new, but some similar work has been done recently in network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zenety (COLT, 2015), Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical parts of this paper are also quite simple extensions of well-known results. As mentioned in this paper, there may be some applications in determining clusterability in this work, but formal results in this regard were not provided in this paper. This paper is well-written and explains the core ideas of this paper quite clearly.
