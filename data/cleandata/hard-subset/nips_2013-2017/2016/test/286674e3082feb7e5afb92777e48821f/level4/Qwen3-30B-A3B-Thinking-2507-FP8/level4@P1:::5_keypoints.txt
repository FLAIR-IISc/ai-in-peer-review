- Introduces a novel framework for model-free theoretical guarantees in graph clustering that does not assume a specific generative model.  
- Shows how to establish empirically verifiable "correctness" guarantees based on data statistics instead of model assumptions.  
- Core theorem: any clustering that fits the data well relative to a model class must be close to a reference clustering that also fits well.  
- Instantiates the theorem for PFM and SBM, yielding concrete model-based and model-free guarantees.  
- Repurposes existing model-based recovery theorems to produce model-free guarantees, improving PFM error bounds by a factor of 128.  
- Clearly bridges and relates model-based (SBM, DC-SBM, PFM) and model-free clustering results.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with precise definitions (goodness of fit, clustering distance) and detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Strong technical quality: general framework applicable to multiple block models and providing computable, theoretically sound guarantees.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets (PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, Political Blogs) supports practical utility.  
- Highly original approach of "turning around" model-based recovery theorems to obtain model-free results.  
- Significant practical impact: provides a new, broadly applicable method for validating clustering stability and clusterability without model assumptions.  
- Main limitation: reliance on spectral methods which may lack tightness for very sparse graphs; authors acknowledge this and could discuss remedies.  
- Meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and reviewer recommends acceptance.
