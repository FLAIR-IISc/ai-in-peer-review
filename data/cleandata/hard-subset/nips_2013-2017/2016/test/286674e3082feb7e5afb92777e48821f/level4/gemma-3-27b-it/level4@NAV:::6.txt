This paper attempts to develop a theoretical foundation for model-free graph clustering. However, the presented results are ultimately unconvincing and lack rigor. While the authors aim to establish a novel theory, the approach feels underdeveloped and relies heavily on intuitive arguments without sufficient mathematical grounding. For instance, Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) contains the vague condition “…which also fits G well is close to C,” and Proposition 3 (Informal) invokes undefined “…standard recovery conditions.” These examples highlight a broader issue of insufficient precision and justification throughout the theoretical development.
