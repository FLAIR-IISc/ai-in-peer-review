- Proposes a novel, model-free stability framework: clusterings that fit data well must be small perturbations of each other.  
- Instantiates framework for PFM and SBM with all required quantities computable from data.  
- Significant theoretical contributions linking model-free guarantees to practical, computable checks.  
- Experimental results indicate the sufficient conditions for guarantees may be restrictive in practice.  
- Treatment of PFM and SBM feels disjoint despite PFM encompassing DC-SBM and SBM.  
- Needs clearer justification for different estimators (Laplacian for PFM, adjacency for SBM) and different distance metrics (weighted ME vs. ME).  
- Suggests deriving explicit results for DC-SBM as a natural intermediate case.  
- Paper mostly focuses on disjoint clusters; does not address differing cluster numbers or split-merge scenarios.  
- Missing definition: <M', M>_F in Proposition 10 is not specified.  
- Assumption 5 unclear: motivation for using ||L̂² - L²||_F instead of ||L̂ - L||_F² needs explanation.  
- Underdeveloped connection to prior model-free stability literature; should discuss Kumar & Kannan (2010) and Balcan et al. (2012, 2013).  
- Simulations suggest goodness-of-fit conditions are difficult to meet; PFM’s sensitivity to small perturbations needs more elaboration.  
- Figure 1 issues: caption should identify SBM-related subplots and axes lack labels.  
- Notation is inconsistent (e.g., B, C, Y redefined); recommend subscripts or more descriptive names for clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, rigorous math, practical relevance via computable quantities, and links to model-based recovery theorems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive conditions, limited scope (disjoint clusters only), incomplete literature review, and clarity/notation problems.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing literature, practical implications, extensions to broader clustering scenarios, and clarity fixes.
