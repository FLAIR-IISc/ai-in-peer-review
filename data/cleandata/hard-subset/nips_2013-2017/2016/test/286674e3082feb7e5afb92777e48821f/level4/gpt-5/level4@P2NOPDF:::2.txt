This paper studies relationships among several clustering distance measures in network models, specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM). It establishes a local convexity–type property: if two clusterings fit the network data equally well in terms of the model’s parameter estimation, then they must be close under standard misclassification-based distances. More broadly, the paper proves an equivalence (up to constants) among different clustering distance measures for network data under these models. The claims are supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evidence.

The main idea is appealing and provides a useful structural property of clusterings in common network models. The results are presented clearly, and the exposition is lucid. The combination of theory and experiments enhances the paper’s credibility.

In terms of novelty, while the results appear new, they are conceptually related to recent work in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (arXiv 2015). Moreover, parts of the theoretical development read as relatively straightforward extensions of known techniques. The paper mentions potential applications to detecting clusterability, which would be valuable in practice, but no formal statements or guarantees on this front are provided.

Overall, the paper is well written, the central property is interesting and potentially useful, and the theoretical and experimental validations are convincing. Clarifying the precise distinctions from closely related work and providing formal results on clusterability detection would further strengthen the contribution.
