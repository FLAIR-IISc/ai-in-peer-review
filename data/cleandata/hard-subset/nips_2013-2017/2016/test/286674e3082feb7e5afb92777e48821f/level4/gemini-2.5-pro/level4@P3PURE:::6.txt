The authors aim to develop a theoretical framework for graph clustering in a model-free setting. However, the presented theory is unconvincing and lacks formal rigor. While the paper purports to construct a new theory, its foundations rely on informal reasoning and imprecisely defined concepts.

This lack of precision is evident in several key results. For example:
*   Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) makes the claim that a clustering "...which also fits G well is close to C." The terms "fits well" and "close to" are not formally defined, rendering the statement ambiguous and difficult to verify.
*   Proposition 3 (Informal) is said to hold "...under standard recovery conditions." The paper fails to specify what these conditions are or justify why they should be considered "standard" in this context.

Without precise mathematical definitions for such crucial terms and conditions, the theoretical claims of the paper are not adequately supported.
