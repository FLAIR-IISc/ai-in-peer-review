- Establishes relationships between various clustering distance measures for network data.  
- Analyzes Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM) to show a locally convex-like property for clustering.  
- Proves that any two clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Demonstrates equivalence between different clustering distance measures in network settings.  
- Provides both theoretical proofs and experimental validation of the results.  
- Results are novel but related to recent work (Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015).  
- Theoretical contributions are relatively straightforward extensions of established results.  
- Mentions potential applications to detecting clusterability but offers no formal results in that direction.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents its core ideas.
