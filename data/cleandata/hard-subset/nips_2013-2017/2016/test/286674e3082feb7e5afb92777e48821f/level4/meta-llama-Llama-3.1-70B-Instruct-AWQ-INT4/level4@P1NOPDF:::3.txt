The manuscript presents a set of algorithms for clustering over graphs, as well as a method for evaluating the similarity between two cluster assignments. Although the algorithms themselves are clearly described, their underlying motivation and the rationale for presenting them as a collection are not well articulated. Upon closer inspection, it becomes apparent that the algorithms are modifications of existing ones, with the primary novelty being the constraints introduced at the end.

It would be beneficial for the authors to provide a clearer explanation of the context and inspiration behind their work, as well as a more detailed justification for the development of multiple algorithms. Additionally, the manuscript would benefit from a more cohesive narrative that ties the various algorithms together and highlights their unique contributions.
