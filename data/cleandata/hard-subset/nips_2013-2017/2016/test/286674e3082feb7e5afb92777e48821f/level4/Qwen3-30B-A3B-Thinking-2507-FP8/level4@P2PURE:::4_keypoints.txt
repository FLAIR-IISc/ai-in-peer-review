1) Paper aims to give model-free, data-computable theoretical guarantees for clustering stability and exact recovery without assuming a generative model.  

2) Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given PFM subsumes DC-SBM and SBM; inconsistent use of Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM, weighted ME vs ME, and omission of DC-SBM results.  

3) Proposition 10: the definition/meaning of the inner product ⟨M', M⟩_F is unspecified.  

4) Assumption 5: ambiguity whether the norm is ‖ĤL^2 − L^2‖_F or ‖ĤL − L‖_F^2, and if the former, no intuition given for squaring Laplacians.  

5) Paper focuses on disjoint block models—unclear if/how the template extends to other settings where spectral clustering works (e.g., hierarchical clustering).  

6) Framework assumes C and C' have the same number of clusters, ignoring practical cases where algorithms produce different K (splitting/merging) yet clusterings are still “close.”  

7) Related work omits key model-free results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013); relation between this stability notion and prior perturbation/approximation-stability results is not addressed.  

8) Simulations: PFM tolerates only trivial perturbations; claims about large eigengap causing sensitivity lack justification, and smoothing (A^k) effects on eigengaps and stability are unexplained—this undermines practical usefulness.  

9) Figure 1 caption incorrectly or unclearly references “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” without specifying which subplot or whether SBM is plotted.  

10) Theoretical guarantees for PFM use weighted ME but experiments perturb and report unweighted ME—this inconsistency must be resolved.
