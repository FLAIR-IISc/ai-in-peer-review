The manuscript presents a set of algorithms for clustering over graphs, as well as a method for testing the similarity between two cluster assignments. While the algorithms themselves are clearly described, the motivation behind their development and the reasoning for presenting them as a collection is not well explained. Upon closer examination, it becomes apparent that the algorithms are modifications of existing ones, with the primary novelty lying in the constraints introduced at the end.

The manuscript would benefit from a clearer explanation of the motivation and context for the proposed algorithms. Specifically, the authors should provide a more detailed discussion of the problem they aim to address, the limitations of existing approaches, and how their algorithms overcome these limitations. This would help to provide a clearer understanding of the significance and relevance of the proposed methods.

Furthermore, the presentation of the algorithms as a list could be improved by providing a more cohesive narrative that ties the different methods together. The authors could consider organizing the algorithms around a central theme or concept, and providing a clearer explanation of how each algorithm builds upon or differs from the previous ones.

In terms of the technical content, the algorithms themselves appear to be sound, and the modifications to existing methods are well described. However, the novelty of the proposed approach lies primarily in the constraints introduced at the end, which could be more thoroughly explored and motivated. The authors should provide a clearer explanation of the significance of these constraints, and how they contribute to the overall effectiveness of the algorithms.

Overall, while the manuscript presents some interesting ideas and contributions, it would benefit from a clearer and more cohesive presentation. With some revisions to address these issues, the manuscript has the potential to make a more significant impact in the field.
