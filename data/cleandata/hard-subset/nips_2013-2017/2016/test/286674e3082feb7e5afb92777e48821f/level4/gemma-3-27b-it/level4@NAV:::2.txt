This paper establishes a relationship between various distance metrics used to compare clusterings. Focusing on network models – specifically the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM) – the authors demonstrate a locally convex-like property for clustering solutions. A key finding is that clusterings providing equally good fits to the network data are also closely related according to common misclassification-based distance measures. This work presents a valuable characterization of clusterings within the context of network data, establishing an equivalence between different clustering distance measures. Both theoretical analysis and experimental validation support the presented results. While the findings are novel, they build upon related work in network analysis, such as that by Peng, Sun and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical contributions represent reasonable extensions of existing results. The authors note potential applications in clusterability detection, though formal results in this area are currently absent. The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its central concepts.
