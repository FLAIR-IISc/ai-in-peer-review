This paper provides general concentration guarantees based on spectral clustering techniques for various community detection problems. The authors prove these guarantees for different generative models without relying on any model-specific parameters, making their results widely applicable. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area of study in an important direction.

The paper poses a compelling question about the stability of solutions for community detection problems. The authors phrase and prove their results with great generality, while also focusing on and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. The paper is well-written, making it an easy and enjoyable read. The authors provide clear explanations for the motivation behind their definitions of error and assumptions required for their proofs.

One criticism of the paper is that the proofs rely on spectral algorithms and results that are not tight and can be almost intractable for many interesting community detection problems, particularly when dealing with very sparse graphs. The authors acknowledge this limitation in their conclusion. Nevertheless, this paper marks a great starting point for exploring stability results in community detection, and it is expected that this field will be further developed in the near future.
