- Proposes a model-agnostic framework for clustering graphs, analyzing clustering without assuming a specific data-generating model.  
- Studies two network models (Preference Frame Model and Stochastic Block Model) to demonstrate the framework.  
- Establishes a locally convex-like property: clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Provides theoretical and experimental validations of the claimed property.  
- Results are novel but relate to and extend existing work in network clustering literature.  
- Theoretical contributions are straightforward extensions of well-known results.  
- Discusses potential application to detecting clusterability but lacks formal results in that direction.  
- Well-written paper with clear exposition, consistent notation, and helpful figures.  
- Technically sound and clearly presented; contributes to network clustering theory.  
- Original in proposing a model-free clustering framework, though parts are incremental.  
- Significance: offers a useful clustering property with potential applications, but broader implications and applications are underexplored.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to expand applications and provide formal clusterability results in future work.
