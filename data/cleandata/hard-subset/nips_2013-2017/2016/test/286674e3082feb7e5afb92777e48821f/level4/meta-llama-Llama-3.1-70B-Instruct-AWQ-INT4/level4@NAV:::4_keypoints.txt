- Proposes a general, model-free framework for clustering stability that quantifies distance between well-fitting clusterings and applies to models like PFM and SBM, with quantities computable from data and yielding exact recovery guarantees.

- Simulations indicate the goodness-of-fit measure or theorem conditions may make the theoretical guarantees impractical.

- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given PFM subsumes DC-SBM and SBM, and why Spectral Clustering uses different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency) without justification.

- Proposition 10’s inner-product <M',M>_F needs clarification; Assumption 5 must specify whether the norm is ||L̂^2 − L^2||_F or ||L̂ − L||_F^2 and explain why Laplacians are squared if applicable.

- Paper focuses on disjoint clusters; it’s unclear if the methods extend to other models (e.g., hierarchical clustering) where spectral methods are also used.

- Evaluation metric and estimators assume the same number of clusters, failing to account for algorithms that produce different cluster counts.

- Related work omits key model-free results (e.g., clustering with spectral norm, perturbation resilience) and needs clearer comparison of stability notions to existing literature.

- PFM simulations appear to permit only very small perturbations; authors should explain this limitation in detail.

- Figure 1’s caption is unclear and its axes lack labels.

- Notation is inconsistent in places; symbols are reused with different meanings, harming readability.

- Section 4.4 would fit better as a subsection of the related work.

- Minor corrections needed (e.g., add axis labels to Figure 1, clarify block model notation).
