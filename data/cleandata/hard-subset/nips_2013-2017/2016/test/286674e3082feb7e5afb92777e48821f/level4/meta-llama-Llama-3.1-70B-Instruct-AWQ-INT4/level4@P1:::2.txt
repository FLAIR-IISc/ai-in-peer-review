The paper establishes a relationship between different distances of clustering, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM). It proves a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings that estimate the parameters of the model fitted to the network data equally well are also near each other in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This property of clusterings in network data is nicely presented, and the paper provides both theoretical and experimental validations of the results.

The paper's contributions are novel, but it is worth noting that similar work has recently appeared in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015), and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical parts of the paper are also quite simple extensions of well-known results. As mentioned in the paper, this work has potential applications in detecting clusterability, but the formal results in that regard were not provided.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the core ideas clearly. However, there are some areas that could be improved. For instance, the authors could provide more context and background information on the network models and clustering algorithms used in the paper. Additionally, the experimental section could be more detailed, with more information on the datasets used and the specific clustering algorithms employed.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and useful property of clusterings in network data, and the authors provide a good balance of theoretical and experimental results. With some minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
