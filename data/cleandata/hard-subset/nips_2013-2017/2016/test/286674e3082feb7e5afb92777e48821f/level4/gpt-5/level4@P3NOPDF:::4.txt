Summary:
The paper proposes a generic, model-agnostic template for proving stability guarantees for a clustering C that fits a given dataset well. Stability is defined as the distance between C and any other clustering C' that also fits the data well. The template is instantiated for two block models—PFM and SBM—with guarantees whose quantities are computable directly from the observed graph. The stability results are further leveraged to obtain exact recovery guarantees.

Strengths:
- Ambitious and timely goal: model-free guarantees that are interpretable and computable from data.
- Clear separation between a general template and concrete instantiations (PFM and SBM).
- Bridge from stability to exact recovery is valuable for both theory and practice.

Main concerns and questions:
1) Why treat PFM and SBM differently? Since PFM subsumes DC-SBM which subsumes SBM, it is unclear why the template is instantiated separately with different estimators and metrics. Given that spectral clustering is effective across these models, why not use a unified estimator? In particular:
   - Why use the Laplacian for PFM but the adjacency matrix for SBM?
   - PFM results are stated in terms of weighted misclassification error (ME), while SBM results use ME. How do these compare empirically and theoretically?
   - Since PFM includes DC-SBM, why are there no explicit DC-SBM results?

2) Clarify Proposition 10: What is the definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F? Please state explicitly that this is the Frobenius inner product (trace(M'^T M)) and specify any symmetry assumptions used.

3) Clarify Assumption 5: Is the intended quantity ∥L̂^2 − L^2∥_F or ∥L̂ − L∥_F^2? If the former, please explain the rationale for squaring the Laplacians (e.g., to enhance eigengaps or to linearize certain terms). If the latter, correct the notation.

4) Scope beyond disjoint block models: Do the techniques extend to other settings where spectral methods have guarantees, such as hierarchical clustering (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., 2011 “Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering”)? How would the template be adapted—what are the “fit” functionals and distances in that setting, and can bounds still be computed from data alone?

5) Different numbers of clusters: The theory and estimators assume C and C' have the same number of clusters. In practice, different algorithms (or different matrices—A vs Laplacian) may suggest different K. For instance, C' might split one cluster of C into two while being otherwise identical—intuitively close but outside your framework. Can the template be extended to:
   - allow K ≠ K', e.g., via merge-split operations or minimal refinement/coarsening distances;
   - use information-theoretic distances (e.g., variation of information) that handle differing K?

6) Relation to prior model-free guarantees: The related work misses several relevant lines of research on stability/robustness without specific generative models, such as:
   - Kumar and Kannan (2010): spectral norm conditions and k-means;
   - Balcan et al. (2012): perturbation resilience;
   - Balcan et al. (2013): approximation stability.
Please clarify how your stability notion compares. In particular, how does your “fit-based” stability relate to stability under center perturbations or perturbation-resilient clusterings? Are your conditions implied by or orthogonal to those frameworks?

7) Empirical concerns about practical utility:
   - The perturbations tolerated by PFM in simulations appear extremely small. Lines 252–253 state that a large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even under very small perturbations; please quantify this phenomenon and reconcile it with standard intuitions that larger eigengaps aid stability. What is the mechanism by which a large eigengap in the operator used for fitting increases sensitivity of the fit functional?
   - The political blogs experiment raises A to a power, which enlarges eigengaps; here, stability appears to improve. Please explain why this manipulation helps in practice but does not contradict the earlier observation.
   - If very small perturbations can significantly alter the “fit” and thus invalidate guarantees, the practical value of the template (one of the selling points being data-computable quantities) is undermined. Please discuss the range of perturbations under which the guarantees are informative, and report the typical magnitudes in real data.
   - For SBM, the paper notes that guarantee conditions are very hard to satisfy. Can you characterize when they hold (e.g., in terms of SNR, degree regime), and whether modest relaxations are possible?

8) Figure 1 caption: It states “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0,” but it is unclear which subplot this refers to. If SBM is not plotted, please revise the caption to avoid confusion or add the corresponding plot.

9) Theory–experiment mismatch: Theorems for PFM use weighted ME, but the experiments perturb ME. Please align the metric used in experiments with the theoretical statement or justify why the two are interchangeable in the tested regimes.

Clarity, notation, and presentation:
- Consistency of notation: B, C, and Y are reused with different meanings in PFM and SBM sections, which is confusing given the notation density. Consider using distinct, model-specific symbols (e.g., Y⊥, CD for PFM; explicit use of YZ in Proposition 8 rather than introducing YZ; λ^L vs λ^B for Laplacian vs adjacency eigenvalues).
- Independence statement (Line 44): For block models, A_ij for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments; please state this correctly.
- Organization: Section 4.4 reads as related work; consider moving it under the Related Work section for better flow.

Suggestions for strengthening the paper:
- Provide a unified instantiation across PFM/SBM (and, if possible, DC-SBM), ideally with the same spectral estimator and a common error metric, to highlight the generality of the template.
- Explicitly report eigengaps, noise levels, and the computed fit/stability quantities in each experiment; show how they predict observed recovery.
- Add experiments where K is misspecified or differs across methods to probe robustness of the framework and motivate extensions to K ≠ K'.
- Quantify the “small perturbation” regime in which guarantees remain nontrivial, and provide guidance on diagnosing when the template is likely to be informative on real data.
- Expand the related work discussion to connect your stability notion to perturbation resilience and spectral-norm-based guarantees, clarifying similarities and differences.

Overall assessment:
The paper presents a promising and broadly applicable template with computable guarantees and a compelling link to exact recovery. However, the differing treatments of related models, several unclear definitions/assumptions, limited empirical robustness (especially for PFM), and gaps in related work reduce clarity and practical impact. Addressing the above points—especially unifying the instantiations, clarifying assumptions, and strengthening the empirical section—would substantially improve the paper.
