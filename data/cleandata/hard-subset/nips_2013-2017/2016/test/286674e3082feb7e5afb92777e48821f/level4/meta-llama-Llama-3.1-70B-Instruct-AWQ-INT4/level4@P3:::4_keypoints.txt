- Proposes a novel, model-free framework giving theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well.  
- Measures stability by distance between C and any other clustering C' that also fits the data well.  
- Provides concrete applications to two block models (PFM and SBM).  
- All quantities in the guarantees can be computed explicitly from the observed data.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a clear introduction and accessible technical exposition.  
- Background and notation overview are concise and helpful; main theorem is clearly stated.  
- Authors analyze strengths and weaknesses and relate their results to existing literature.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are clearly presented in text and figures.  
- Major strength: provides model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering, a significant contribution.  
- Technical presentation is generally strong and easy to follow.  
- Notation is sometimes inconsistent and some definitions need clarification (e.g., <M', M>_F in Proposition 10).  
- Related work section should be expanded to include more model-free results referenced by reviewers.  
- Experimental section should include more dataset and experimental-setup details.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor revisions needed; recommended for acceptance (rating 8/10, confidence 8/10).
