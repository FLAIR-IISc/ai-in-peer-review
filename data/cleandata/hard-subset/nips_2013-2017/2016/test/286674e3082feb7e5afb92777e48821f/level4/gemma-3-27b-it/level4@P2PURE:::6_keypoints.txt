- Paper aims to build a theoretical foundation for model-free graph clustering but fails to deliver convincing results.
- Presented results lack rigor and largely amount to handwaving arguments rather than formal theory.
- Theorem 1 ("Generic Theorem") depends on the vague notion that a fitted graph is “close” to a cluster structure C without formal definition or measurable criterion.
- Proposition 3 is labeled “Informal” and relies on unspecified “standard recovery conditions,” leaving key assumptions undefined.
- Overall, claims are unsupported by precise statements, formal conditions, or rigorous proofs.
