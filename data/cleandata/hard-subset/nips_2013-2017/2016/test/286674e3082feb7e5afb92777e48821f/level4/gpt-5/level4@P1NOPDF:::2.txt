The paper establishes a relationship between different notions of distance between clusterings in network data. Working under the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), it proves a local convexity–like property for clustering: any two clusterings that fit the model parameters equally well are necessarily close to each other under standard misclassification distance measures. In effect, the paper demonstrates an equivalence among different clustering distance measures for network data in these models, and supports this claim with both theoretical analysis and empirical validation.

The contribution is interesting and useful: it identifies a desirable stability property of clusterings in network models, showing that near-optimal solutions in terms of model fit cannot be far apart in terms of assignment error. The results appear novel, although there is closely related work in the recent literature with a similar flavor (e.g., Peng, Sun, and Zanetti, COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou, arXiv 2015). Moreover, parts of the theory seem to be relatively straightforward extensions of well-known results.

The paper also mentions potential applications to detecting clusterability, which could be impactful, but it does not provide formal results in that direction.

Overall, the paper is well written and clearly explains the core ideas.
