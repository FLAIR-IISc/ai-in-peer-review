- Central contribution: a model-free framework showing that any clustering C' that fits the data as well as a given clustering C must be structurally close to C, instantiated for PFM and SBM with computable stability bounds.
- Originality/significance: shifts from model-based recovery to model-free validation by “turning around” recovery theorems for cluster validation.
- Practical potential: theoretical bounds use data-computable quantities (ε, δ, eigengaps), making the framework actionable for cluster validation.
- Technical contribution: combines matrix perturbation tools (Davis–Kahan) with recovery literature to derive elegant, rigorous stability proofs.
- Lack of unified treatment: PFM and SBM are treated differently (different estimators, goodness-of-fit, error metrics) despite SBM being a PFM special case; rationale is unclear.
- Missing DC-SBM: omission of Degree-Corrected SBM results is a notable gap.
- Weak empirical validation: PFM guarantees seem only valid for tiny perturbations, limiting usefulness in practice.
- SBM conditions too strict: Theorem 9’s conditions are reportedly not met even on synthetic LFR data, undermining practical applicability.
- Incomplete discussion of empirical limitations: discrepancies between theory and experiments are briefly treated and need deeper analysis.
- Insufficient related-work comparison: omits discussion of key model-free/stability works (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. perturbation-resilience/approximation-stability).
- Limited scope on K: analysis only compares clusterings with the same K, failing to address choosing K or comparing refinements/mergings.
- Question on methodological choices: request rationale for Laplacian-based PFM vs adjacency-based SBM and whether methods can cross-apply.
- Clarification needed on Assumption 5: ambiguity whether norm is ||L̂² - L²||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F² and why Laplacian squares are compared.
- Experimental puzzle: need explanation why increasing eigengap via adjacency powers seems to improve stability contrary to claims that large eigengap increases sensitivity.
- Extension request: ask how framework might be extended to compare clusterings with different numbers of clusters.
- Figure issues: Figure 1 axes unlabeled and caption unclear about omission of SBM plots.
- Notation confusion: reuse of symbols (B, C, Y, λ) across sections is confusing and should be disambiguated; define Frobenius inner product in Proposition 10.
- Metric mismatch in experiments: theory bounds weighted ME but plots appear to use standard (unweighted) ME—please clarify.
- Organization suggestion: Section 4.4 (“Clusterability and resilience”) fits better in Related Work and could be moved.
