1) Proposes a model-agnostic framework giving data-computable theoretical stability guarantees for clusterings that fit the data well.  
2) Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given PFM includes DC-SBM/SBM — questions on choice of Laplacian vs adjacency, weighted ME vs ME, and omission of DC-SBM results.  
3) Definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F in Proposition 10 is unclear.  
4) Assumption 5 is ambiguous: is it ||L̂^2 − L^2||_F or ||L̂ − L||_F^2? If the former, the intuition for squaring Laplacians is unexplained.  
5) Paper focuses on disjoint block models — asks whether the framework extends to other settings where spectral clustering works (e.g., hierarchical models).  
6) Framework requires C and C' to have the same number of clusters, so it cannot capture close clusterings that differ by splits/merges (K vs K+1).  
7) Related work is incomplete — missing model-agnostic results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and lacks comparison between this paper’s stability notion and perturbation/approximation-stability notions.  
8) Simulations show only tiny perturbations tolerated; the claim about large eigengap causing sensitivity is unqualified, and smoothing (raising A to a power) artificially improves stability, undermining practical utility.  
9) Figure 1 caption is ambiguous about which subplot "For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0" refers to and should clarify whether SBM is plotted.  
10) Theoretical guarantees use weighted ME for PFM while experiments perturb unweighted ME — this inconsistency must be addressed.  
Minor 1) Figure 1 axes are unlabeled.  
Minor 2) Clarify that for block models A_ij for i>j are independent given cluster assignments (Line 44).  
Minor 3) Notation B, C, Y differs between PFM and SBM and is confusing; suggest consistent symbols and clearer eigenvalue notation (e.g., λ^L vs λ^B).  
Minor 4) Section 4.4 should be moved or integrated into the related work section.
