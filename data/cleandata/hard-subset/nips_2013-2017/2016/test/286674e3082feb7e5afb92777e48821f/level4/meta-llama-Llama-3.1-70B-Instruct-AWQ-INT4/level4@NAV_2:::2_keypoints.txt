- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that uses data-checkable statistics rather than assuming a generative model.  
- Presents a generic theorem relating model goodness-of-fit to distance between two clusterings.  
- Instantiates the theorem for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), yielding model-free guarantees.  
- Demonstrates that existing model-based recovery theorems can be leveraged to derive model-free guarantees.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear explanation of core ideas and technical details.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are clearly presented.  
- Main strength: offers a new perspective on clustering independent of specific model assumptions.  
- Provides a solid foundation for future work in model-free graph clustering.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on existing model-based recovery theorems, which may limit originality.  
- Could benefit from more novel contributions and a deeper discussion of implications and applications.
