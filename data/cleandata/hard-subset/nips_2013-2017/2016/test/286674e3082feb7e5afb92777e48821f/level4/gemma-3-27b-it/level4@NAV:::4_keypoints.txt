1) Proposes a model-agnostic framework to quantify clustering stability as the distance to equally good alternative clusterings, with data-computable guarantees.

2) Unclear rationale for treating PFM and SBM differently given PFM contains DC-SBM and SBM; choice of Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM and weighted vs unweighted ME needs justification, and DC-SBM results are missing.

3) Proposition 10’s inner-product notation <M', M>_F is ambiguously defined and needs clarification.

4) Assumption 5 is ambiguous about whether the norm is ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2, and if the former, the intuition for squaring Laplacians should be explained.

5) Paper focuses on disjoint clusters; need discussion on extending the framework to other clustering models (e.g., hierarchical clustering) where spectral methods perform well.

6) Evaluation restricts to clusterings with the same number of clusters, preventing capture of close alternative clusterings that differ in cluster count (e.g., cluster splits).

7) Related work is incomplete: missing comparisons to model-free stability/perturbation results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and connection to center-perturbation notions.

8) Simulations suggest practical utility issues: small perturbations and eigengap sensitivity (and smoothing on political blog data) may undermine real-world relevance of the guarantees; SBM conditions look particularly stringent.

9) Figure 1 caption ambiguously states "δ is always greater than δ0" without specifying which subplot or whether SBM is plotted.

10) Theorems guarantee weighted ME for PFM but experiments perturb/use unweighted ME, creating a mismatch between theory and evaluation.

Minor 1) Figure 1 lacks axis labels.

Minor 2) Clarify that for block models A_ij entries for i>j are independent conditional on cluster assignments (line 44).

Minor 3) Notation is inconsistent across PFM and SBM (B, C, Y, eigenvalue labels); recommend consistent symbols and simplify SBM estimator exposition (e.g., distinct λ^L vs λ^B).

Minor 4) Section 4.4 belongs in the related work section rather than its current placement.
