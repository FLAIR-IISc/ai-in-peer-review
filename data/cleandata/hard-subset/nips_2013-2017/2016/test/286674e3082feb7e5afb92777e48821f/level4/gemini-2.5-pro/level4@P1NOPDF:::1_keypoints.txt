- Proposes novel distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering, avoiding assumptions like the stochastic block model.  
- Core claim: a clustering that fits the graph well implies any equally good-fitting clustering must be structurally similar.  
- Paper is well-written and clear; main text is largely self-contained despite some content moved to the supplement.  
- Minor notational issue: using hats for observed quantities (e.g., Ĥ) is unconventional and potentially confusing.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world graphs must be smoothed for the structure to be recoverable, whereas classical algorithms can recover structure on unsmoothed graphs.  
- Limitation 1: main Planted Frequency Model result depends on a non-standard distance between clusterings; the claim that this measure is more natural is debatable and under-supported.  
- Limitation 2: results rely on a proxy-model approach (reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing to the graph), which evaluates fit to the graph rather than fidelity to an underlying generative model.  
- Practical concern: the proxy/model-fit constraint is necessary for theoretical guarantees but raises questions about real-world applicability given the empirical shortcomings.
