- Novel theoretical results for graph clustering that do not assume a specific generative model.  
- Core contribution: if one clustering fits the graph well under the defined criterion, any other clustering with comparable fit must be close to it.  
- Provides distribution-free guarantees, addressing an important and relevant problem.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; some material moved to supplement due to page limits.  
- Notation using "hats" (e.g., observed Laplacian) is somewhat confusing but minor.  
- Results are interesting, clearly explained, and supported by illustrative examples.  
- Limitation: main PFM result uses a non-standard distance between clusterings; the claim that it is more natural is debatable.  
- Limitation: method relies on a proxy model reconstructed from a clustering and compared to the observed graph rather than the true generative process.  
- This proxy-based requirement restricts the notion of a “good” clustering (requires graph to be well-approximated in normalized Laplacian terms).  
- Applicability concern: framework may struggle on real-world data where smoothing is needed, whereas classical algorithms often recover ground truth without such preprocessing.
