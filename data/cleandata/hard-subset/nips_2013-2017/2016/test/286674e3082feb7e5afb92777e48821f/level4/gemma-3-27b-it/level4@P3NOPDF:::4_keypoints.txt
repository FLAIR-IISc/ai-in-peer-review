- Presents a generic, model-agnostic stability template for clustering and applies it to PFM and SBM with computable quantities.  
- Uses stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees, but practical utility appears limited by simulations and modeling choices.  
- Core contribution of a general clustering stability framework is valuable for theory and practice.  
- Inconsistent treatment of PFM and SBM; PFM uses Laplacian while SBM uses adjacency without clear justification.  
- Failure to provide a unified treatment including DC-SBM, despite model nesting, and no DC-SBM results.  
- Stability comparisons are hindered by using weighted ME for PFM but unweighted ME for SBM.  
- Technical ambiguities: unclear definition of <M’, M>_F in Proposition 10.  
- Ambiguity in Assumption 5: whether condition is ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or (||L̂ - L||_F)^2 and why Laplacians are squared.  
- Limited scope: focus on disjoint clusters only; unclear extension to hierarchical or other clustering regimes where spectral methods work.  
- Framework restricts to equal numbers of clusters in C and C’, failing to capture practical cases with different cluster counts or splits.  
- Related work is incomplete; missing connections to model-free clustering results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan; Balcan et al.) and prior stability/perturbation notions.  
- Simulation concerns: PFM tolerates only tiny perturbations; eigengap comments lack justification and smoothing experiments may undermine practical relevance.  
- SBM guarantees are acknowledged to be difficult to satisfy in practice.  
- Presentation and clarity issues: unclear Figure 1 caption and axes, inconsistent notation across sections, suggested notational fixes (e.g., λ^L vs λ^B), misplaced Section 4.4, and other minor notational/clarity fixes.
