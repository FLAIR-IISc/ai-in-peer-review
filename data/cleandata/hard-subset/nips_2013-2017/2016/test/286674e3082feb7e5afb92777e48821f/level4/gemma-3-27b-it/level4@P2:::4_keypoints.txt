- Introduces a general framework for clustering stability guarantees without strict model assumptions like SBM/DC-SBM/PFM.  
- Core idea: if clustering C fits data well, any other good-fitting clustering C' must be a small perturbation of C.  
- Defines a “goodness of fit” measure and derives bounds on distance between competing clusterings.  
- Framework instantiated for both PFM and SBM, showing connections and sometimes stronger results than existing model-based recovery.  
- Empirical results suggest the theoretical conditions may be difficult to satisfy in practice.  

- Valuable contribution: focuses on data fit rather than model adherence and gives explicitly computable quantities for guarantees.  
- Lacks convincing rationale for treating PFM and SBM separately despite hierarchical model relationship; a unified treatment is recommended.  
- Choice of Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM is insufficiently justified.  
- Using weighted ME for PFM and ME for SBM complicates comparison across models.  
- Omission of DC-SBM results is concerning given its position between PFM and SBM.  

- Technical issues: Proposition 10 misses the definition of <M', M>_F.  
- Notation ambiguity in Assumption 5: unclear whether ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F denotes squared Frobenius norm or Frobenius norm of squared difference; needs clarification/justification.  
- Framework currently focuses on disjoint clusters; extension to overlapping or hierarchical clustering would broaden applicability.  

- Evaluation concerns: conditions for guarantees appear restrictive and possibly impractical.  
- Statement about PFM sensitivity to small perturbations (lines 252–253) lacks quantification and needs elaboration.  
- Improvement from smoothing political blog data is shown but not explained theoretically.  
- Noted concern: δ > δ0 for SBM (Figure 1 caption) undermines the practical validity of SBM guarantees.  

- Related work is incomplete: key model-free clustering and stability papers (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) are missing.  
- Missing discussion comparing this stability notion to existing notions (e.g., perturbation of cluster centers).  

- Minor issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.  
- Line 44 phrasing about independence given cluster assignments could be clearer.  
- Reuse of symbols (B, C, Y) across sections causes confusion; consistent notation (e.g., Y_orthogonal) recommended.  
- SBM estimator definition could be simplified by substituting expression for YZ directly.  
- Use distinct eigenvalue notation for PFM vs SBM (e.g., λ^L vs λ^B) for clarity.  
- Section 4.4 reads like related work and might be better placed there.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel model-free stability framework, computable quantities, links to model-based recovery, and potential to strengthen existing results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions limiting applicability, limited empirical validation, incomplete related work, unclear technical details, and uneven treatment of models.  

- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical contribution but practical utility is questionable; recommend weak reject pending clarification of conditions, expanded experiments, fuller related work, and technical fixes.
