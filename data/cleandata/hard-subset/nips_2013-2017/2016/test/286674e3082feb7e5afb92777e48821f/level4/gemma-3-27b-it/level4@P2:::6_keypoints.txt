- Proposes a model-free clustering framework based on stability: clusterings that fit the graph well must be close to each other.  
- Demonstrates the framework with model-free guarantees for SBM and PFM and links to model-based recovery results.  
- Novel approach emphasizing stability and goodness-of-fit instead of assuming a data-generating model.  
- Effectively connects and leverages prior model-based clustering results to inform model-free guarantees.  
- Provides detailed technical development, definitions, theorems, and concrete estimation algorithms (PFM, SBM).  
- Claims all quantities in the bounds are computable from data, improving computational feasibility.  
- Generic Theorem 1 is broad in intent but its instantiations rely on model-specific assumptions (spectral decomposition, eigengaps).  
- Theoretical conditions (especially for SBM) are often stringent and may be unsatisfiable in practice.  
- The notion of “goodness of fit” remains abstract; specific measures lack clear intuition and practical interpretation.  
- Some results are presented informally and depend on “standard recovery conditions,” reducing rigor and clarity.  
- Related work section is thorough but lacks critical comparison and clear distinctions from prior stability results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a core problem, offers a new perspective linking model-based and model-free methods, and is technically rigorous.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability is questionable, framework feels underdeveloped, and comparative benefits over existing work are not convincingly shown.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and technically sound initial exploration but limited impact due to restrictive conditions and weak empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Weak Reject, with encouragement to relax theorem conditions and strengthen empirical and comparative evaluation.
