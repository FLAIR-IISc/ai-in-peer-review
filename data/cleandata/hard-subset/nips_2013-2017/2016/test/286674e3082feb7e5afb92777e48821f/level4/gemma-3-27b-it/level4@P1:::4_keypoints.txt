1) Proposes a generic, model-free theoretical framework for clustering stability based on data-computable quantities and algorithm outputs.  
2) Unclear why PFM, DC-SBM, and SBM are treated differently (different estimators, matrices, and ME metrics) despite PFM subsuming DC-SBM and SBM; DC-SBM results missing.  
3) Requests clarification of the definition of <M', M>_F in Proposition 10.  
4) Questions Assumption 5 notation: is it ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2, and asks for intuition if Laplacians are squared.  
5) Asks whether the framework and techniques extend to other clustering models (e.g., hierarchical clustering) where spectral methods have guarantees.  
6) Notes the framework only compares clusterings with the same K and fails to capture close clusterings with different numbers of clusters (e.g., one cluster split).  
7) Points out Related Work is incomplete — missing model-free results (Kumar & Kannan, Balcan et al.) — and asks how this stability notion relates to center-perturbation stability.  
8) Raises concerns that tolerated perturbations in PFM simulations are tiny and that eigen-gap behavior and smoothing effects are not well explained, undermining practical usefulness; SBM conditions also hard to satisfy.  
9) Requests clarification of Figure 1 caption statement “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” — specify which subplot or whether SBM is plotted.  
10) Notes mismatch between theoretical weighted ME guarantees for PFM and experiments using standard ME.  
Minor 1) Figure 1 axes lack labels.  
Minor 2) Suggests clarifying that for block models A_ij (i>j) are independent given cluster assignments (Line 44).  
Minor 3) Highlights inconsistent notation (B, C, Y) between PFM and SBM and recommends clearer, distinct symbols (e.g., Y_orthogonal, C_D, λ^L, λ^B).  
Minor 4) Recommends moving Section 4.4 into the Related Work section as a subsection.  
Overall assessment) Interesting and novel theoretical framework but practical relevance and impact are limited due to stringent conditions, insufficient experiments, missing comparisons, and clarity issues — recommendation: reject.
