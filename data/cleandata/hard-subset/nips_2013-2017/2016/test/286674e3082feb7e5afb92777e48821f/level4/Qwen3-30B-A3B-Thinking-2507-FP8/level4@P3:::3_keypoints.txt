- Introduces a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that provides theoretical guarantees without assuming data follows a specific block model.
- Defines a goodness-of-fit condition for a clustering and proves any two clusterings meeting it must be close.
- Bridges model-based and model-free approaches by repurposing model-based recovery theorems as model-free guarantees.
- Presents a generic theorem and instantiates it for PFM and SBM, giving a unified framework for model-free guarantees.
- Proofs are technically sound and leverage perturbation theory from spectral graph theory.
- Demonstrates practical utility: all quantities in the bounds are computable from data.
- Shows the framework can strengthen existing model-based results (e.g., improves error bounds in [20] by factor 128).
- Clarity and organization are generally good, with well-defined notation and connections to prior spectral clustering and SBM work.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets validate theoretical claims, though analysis of results could be deeper.
- Novelty: turning model-based recovery theorems into model-free guarantees is a fresh and original perspective.
- Significance: provides a practical way to validate clusterings without assuming a generative model, valuable for real-world applications.
- Weakness: focus is on clustering validation rather than proposing new clustering algorithms, limiting direct practical impact.
- Weakness: SBM guarantees (Theorem 9) are significantly more restrictive than PFM and often not satisfied empirically.
- Paper should more clearly explain differences from prior model-free approaches (e.g., [6]) and analyze the SBM vs. PFM discrepancy.
- Presentation could be improved with more intuitive explanations of eigengap conditions and stability; recommend stronger discussion of integration into clustering pipelines.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept with minor revisions to address SBM limitations and practical integration.
