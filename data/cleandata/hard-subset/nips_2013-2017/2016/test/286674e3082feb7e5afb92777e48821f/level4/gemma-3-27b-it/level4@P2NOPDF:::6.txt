This paper attempts to develop a theoretical foundation for model-free graph clustering. However, the presented results are ultimately unconvincing and lack theoretical rigor. The work feels less like the construction of a novel theory and more like a collection of intuitive, but unsubstantiated, claims. The theoretical contributions appear underdeveloped and rely heavily on imprecise language. 

For instance, Theorem 1, described as a “Generic Theorem,” includes the vague statement that graphs “which also fits G well is close to C.” The meaning of “fits well” is not clearly defined, and the connection to the clustering *C* remains insufficiently justified. Similarly, Proposition 3 is presented as an “Informal” result “under standard recovery conditions,” without specifying *which* standard conditions are being invoked or providing a rigorous demonstration of their applicability to this specific problem. This lack of specificity significantly weakens the proposition’s impact and makes it difficult to evaluate its validity.

The paper’s primary weakness lies in its lack of mathematical precision. Key concepts are not formally defined, and arguments often proceed through hand-waving rather than rigorous proof. While exploring model-free graph clustering is a valuable endeavor, this paper fails to deliver a compelling theoretical framework. The authors need to substantially strengthen the mathematical foundations of their claims and provide more concrete definitions and justifications for their proposed theory. Without these improvements, the paper's contribution remains limited.
