- Proposes a novel framework bridging model-based and model-free graph clustering with theoretical guarantees via a generic theorem.  
- Generic theorem: if a model M(G, C) fits graph G well, then any other clustering C' that also fits G well must be close to C.  
- Introduces model class M and notion of compatibility M(G, C) fitted to graph G and clustering C.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Presents a PFM estimation algorithm and proves consistency under standard recovery conditions.  
- Main PFM result: bounds distance between two clusterings in terms of the eigengap of the graph Laplacian.  
- Presents an SBM estimation algorithm with a main result bounding clustering distance via the eigengap of the graph incidence matrix.  
- Discusses practical implications: cluster validation, converting model-based recovery theorems to model-free guarantees, and possible extensions/sanity checks.  
- Experimental evaluation on simulations and real datasets (including Political Blogs) shows framework yields computable model-free guarantees.  
- Strength: provides a solid theoretical foundation and well-executed instantiations for PFM and SBM.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation that demonstrates applicability and computability of bounds.  
- Weakness: theoretical framework needs more detailed explanations and justifications for clarity.  
- Weakness: many proofs and derivations are deferred to the supplement, reducing paper readability.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons with existing methods and insufficient discussion of broader implications.  
- For acceptance: novel idea, well-developed theory, and solid experiments.  
- Against acceptance: exposition issues, supplementary proofs hinder accessibility, and inadequate empirical comparisons.
