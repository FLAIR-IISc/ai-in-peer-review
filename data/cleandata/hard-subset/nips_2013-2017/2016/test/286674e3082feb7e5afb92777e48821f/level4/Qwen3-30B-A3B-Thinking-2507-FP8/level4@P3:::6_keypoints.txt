- Addresses providing theoretical clustering guarantees without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Proposes a "Generic Theorem": if model M(G,C) fits G well (||ĤL − L|| ≤ ε), any other good-fit clustering C' is close to C in weighted misclassification error.  
- Instantiates framework for PFM and SBM (Theorems 4 and 9) linking goodness-of-fit ε to clustering stability.  
- Shows how existing model-based recovery results can be repurposed to yield model-free guarantees.  
- Demonstrates practical computability of theoretical conditions from data.  
- Improves some existing model-based bounds (e.g., a 128× improvement over [20]).  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real networks shows theoretical conditions correspond to observed stability.  
- Clear exposition: well-defined notation, distinct treatment of model-based vs. model-free, and more direct proof techniques.  
- Practical limitation: bounds can be too restrictive in practice (SBM conditions often fail, e.g., δ > 1 in experiments).  
- Limited scope: paper focuses on PFM and SBM and does not fully develop extensions to other models or poorly fitting data.  
- Missing comparisons to alternative model-free validation methods (e.g., bootstrap and other stability approaches).  
- Lacks systematic exploration of the trade-offs between model-based and model-free approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss when conditions hold, potential extensions, and comparisons to other model-free methods.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant contribution, though practical bounds need further refinement.
