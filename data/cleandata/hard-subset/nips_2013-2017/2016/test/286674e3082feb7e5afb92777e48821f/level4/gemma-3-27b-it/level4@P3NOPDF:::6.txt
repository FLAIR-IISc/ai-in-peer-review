## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission [Paper ID] - Graph Clustering in a Model-Free Framework

This paper attempts to develop a theoretical framework for graph clustering without relying on traditional model assumptions. While the goal of a model-free theory is admirable and potentially impactful, the presented results fall short of providing a convincing or novel contribution. The paper largely feels like a collection of intuitive observations rather than a rigorous theoretical advancement; the “theory” presented appears overly simplistic and lacks the necessary precision to be genuinely useful.

My primary concern lies with the lack of mathematical rigor throughout the paper. Several key statements are presented without sufficient justification or quantitative backing. For instance, Theorem 1, described as a “Generic Theorem,” relies on the vague notion of a graph “fitting” a cluster well and its closeness to a cluster *C*. The criterion for this “fit” and the meaning of “close” are not formally defined, making the theorem difficult to interpret and assess. This imprecision permeates other results as well. Proposition 3, labeled as “Informal,” invokes “standard recovery conditions” without specifying what those conditions are. This reliance on undefined terms undermines the theoretical claims and prevents a clear understanding of the assumptions required for the results to hold. 

Furthermore, the paper lacks a clear definition of what constitutes a “good” clustering in a model-free setting.  Without such a definition, it is difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed framework or to compare it with existing approaches. The authors allude to modularity but do not formalize it as an objective function or a metric for assessing clustering quality.  This absence is particularly problematic given the focus on theoretical guarantees.

The experimental evaluation, while present, doesn't adequately support the theoretical claims. The experiments are limited in scope and do not demonstrate significant improvements over established clustering algorithms, particularly when considering the complexity of deriving and applying this theoretical framework. The choice of datasets also seems somewhat arbitrary and does not cover a wide range of graph structures. A more comprehensive evaluation, including comparisons against strong baseline methods across diverse graph types and sizes, would be crucial to justify the paper's contributions. 

Specifically, I would like to see the authors address the following points:

* **Formalize Key Concepts:** Define rigorously what it means for a graph to “fit” a cluster, and quantify the notion of “closeness” used in Theorem 1. Similarly, explicitly state the “standard recovery conditions” referenced in Proposition 3.
* **Define Clustering Quality:** Provide a clear and mathematically sound definition of a good clustering in the model-free context.  This could involve adapting existing metrics or proposing a novel one.
* **Strengthen Theoretical Guarantees:**  Provide more concrete bounds and guarantees for the proposed framework. The current results feel largely heuristic.
* **Expand Experimental Validation:** Conduct a more thorough experimental evaluation with a wider range of datasets and baseline methods. Consider including synthetic graphs with known community structures to evaluate performance under controlled conditions.
* **Clarify Novelty:** Articulate more clearly how this work differs from, and improves upon, existing graph clustering techniques, particularly those that also aim for model-free performance.

In summary, while the motivation for this work is valuable, the current presentation lacks the mathematical rigor and experimental validation necessary for acceptance at NIPS. The theory, as presented, is insufficiently developed and relies on overly vague statements.  I recommend rejection in its current form.  However, I believe that with substantial revisions addressing the issues outlined above, the paper could potentially become a more valuable contribution to the field.
