- Paper proposes a generic, model-free template for clustering stability guarantees, with computable data-dependent quantities and examples for PFM and SBM, and derives exact recovery results.

- Simulations suggest the goodness-of-fit metric or theorem conditions make the theoretical results of limited practical use.

- Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately given PFM contains DC-SBM/SBM; choice of Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM needs justification.

- Results use weighted ME for PFM and ME for SBM without comparison; DC-SBM results are omitted but would be valuable.

- Several notational/definition clarifications are required (e.g., definition of <M',M>_F in Proposition 10; ambiguity in Assumption 5 whether ||Lhat^2−L^2||_F or ||Lhat−L||_F^2 is intended, and why Laplacians are squared).

- Paper focuses on disjoint clusters and does not discuss extension of the template to other models where spectral clustering works (e.g., hierarchical clustering).

- Framework assumes C and C' have the same number of clusters, so it cannot capture “close” clusterings that differ by a split/merge (different K).

- Related work is missing key model-free stability results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013); need to relate this stability notion to perturbation/approximation-stability literature.

- Simulations show PFM tolerates very small perturbations; the claim that large eigengap increases sensitivity is not well explained and powering A may artificially improve stability.

- Conditions for SBM guarantees appear very stringent and hard to meet in practice.

- Multiple minor presentation issues: ambiguous Figure 1 caption about δ, missing axis labels, mismatch between theoretical weighted ME and experimental ME, unclear wording about independence of A_ij, and inconsistent notation (B, C, Y) across sections.
