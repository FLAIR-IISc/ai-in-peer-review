This paper presents general concentration guarantees for spectral clustering across a wide range of community detection problems. A key strength is the model-agnostic nature of the results; the authors prove these guarantees without relying on specific parameters of the underlying generative model. The work is both interesting and relevant, representing a significant advance in a popular and important research area. The paper tackles a compelling question regarding the stability of solutions in community detection, achieving highly general results while also providing focused applications to commonly studied graphical models. Furthermore, the paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the rationale behind its definitions of error and required assumptions. The primary limitation, acknowledged by the authors, lies in the reliance on spectral algorithms and associated bounds that can be loose and computationally challenging, particularly for sparse graphs. Nevertheless, this work is a valuable contribution, initiating a promising line of research into stability results for community detection that I anticipate will be further developed.
