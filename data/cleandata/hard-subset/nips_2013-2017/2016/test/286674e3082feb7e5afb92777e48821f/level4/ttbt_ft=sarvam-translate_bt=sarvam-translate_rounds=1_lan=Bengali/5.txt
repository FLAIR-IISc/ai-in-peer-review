This paper provides a very general density estimate based on spectral clustering techniques for all types of community detection problems. They prove these estimates for various generative models without using any parameters of those models. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular field of study in an important direction. This paper raises a very interesting question about the stability of community detection problem solutions. They have published and proven these results very generally, focusing on and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. This paper is very well written and was an easy and enjoyable read. They explain the purpose behind almost all the ways they chose to define the errors and create the assumptions needed for their proof. My only real criticism while reading this paper, which the authors themselves mentioned in their conclusion, is that these proofs were dependent on spectral algorithms and results that are very difficult and almost impossible for many interesting community detection problems when the graphs are very thin. However, this was a great start in terms of stability results for community detection, which I hope and expect will improve further in the near future.
