- Proposes distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering without generative assumptions.
- Main idea: if one clustering fits the graph well, any other equally good clustering must be close to it.
- Uses a stability paradigm focusing on perturbations of a clustering rather than the clustering itself.
- Paper is well-written and clear, though some content is deferred to supplementary material.
- Notation choice (e.g., hats on observed quantities) could be improved.
- Experimental evaluation is helpful but reveals limitations of the framework in practice.
- Requires smoothing the graph to recover structure, whereas classical algorithms may not.
- Main PFM result relies on a non-standard distance measure between clusterings that may lack broad acceptance.
- Results depend on a "proxy" model reconstructed from a clustering, constraining what counts as a good clustering.
- Authors provide good discussion and commentary on implications of their theoretical results.
- Suggestions: expand discussion of implications, develop more robust distance measures, and relax proxy-model assumptions.
- Scores high on originality and significance; quality is good but clarity could be improved by addressing limitations.
- Reviewer concludes contributions outweigh limitations and recommends acceptance.
