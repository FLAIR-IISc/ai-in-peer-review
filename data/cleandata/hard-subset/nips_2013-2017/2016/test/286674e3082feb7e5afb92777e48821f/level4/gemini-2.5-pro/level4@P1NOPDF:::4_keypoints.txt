Major 1: Paper tackles an important goal of providing generic, model-free clustering guarantees computable from data and outputs.

Major 2: Treating PFM and SBM separately is confusing given model nesting; unclear why Laplacian used for PFM and adjacency for SBM, why weighted ME vs standard ME are used, and why DC-SBM results are omitted.

Major 3: Proposition 10 uses notation <M', M>_F without definition—clarify if this is the Frobenius inner product.

Major 4: Assumption 5 is ambiguous about whether the term is ||L̂² - L²||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F²; if the former, provide intuition for squaring Laplacians.

Major 5: Paper focuses on disjoint block models—ask whether the template extends to other spectral-clustering settings (e.g., hierarchical clustering or Balakrishnan et al. 2011 noise-threshold results).

Major 6: Framework only compares clusterings with the same number of clusters, failing to capture closeness when algorithms return different K (e.g., one cluster split), which is a significant limitation.

Major 7: Related work omits several model-free stability/perturbation results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013); need to relate the paper’s stability notion to these center-perturbation concepts.

Major 8: Simulations indicate the goodness-of-fit measure or theoretical conditions may be too restrictive—PFM tolerates tiny perturbations and SBM conditions are hard to satisfy; eigengap behavior and apparent contradictions need clarification.

Major 9: Figure 1 caption saying “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0” is unclear about which subplot or whether SBM results are shown.

Major 10: Theory-experiment mismatch for PFM: guarantees are for weighted ME but experiments use standard ME—clarify or correct.

Minor 1: Axes in Figure 1 are unlabeled.

Minor 2: Line 44’s statement about block models should be more precise: A_ij for i>j are independent conditional on node cluster assignments.

Minor 3: Notation is reused across PFM and SBM (B, C, Y, λ), which is confusing; recommend distinct symbols (e.g., Y_⊥, CD, λ^L, λ^B) and simplifying intermediate terms.

Minor 4: Section 4.4 would fit better as a subsection of Related Work.
