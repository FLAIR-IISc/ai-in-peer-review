- Establishes connections among various clustering distance notions.  
- Studies Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and proves a local convexity–like property for clustering.  
- Shows that clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Demonstrates an appealing implication for clusterings in network data.  
- Formally states and proves equivalence among different clustering distance measures for network data.  
- Provides both theoretical analysis and experimental evidence supporting the results.  
- Contributions are novel but related to Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT 2015) and Chao et al. (arXiv 2015).  
- Theoretical components are fairly straightforward extensions of known results.  
- Notes potential applications to detecting clusterability but lacks formal results in that direction.  
- Paper is well written and communicates core ideas clearly.
