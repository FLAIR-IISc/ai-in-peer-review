- Introduces novel, distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that do not rely on specific generative models.
- Core claim: any alternative clustering that fits the graph as well as a given good clustering must be similar to that clustering.
- Addresses a significant problem in the field and provides valuable theoretical insights.
- Paper remains clear and comprehensible despite some material being in the supplementary files.
- Minor notational gripe: using “hats” (e.g., \hat{L}) for observed quantities is awkward.
- Overall well-written; results are interesting, well-commented, and effectively illustrated.
- Empirical limitation: some real-world graphs require smoothing to recover structures per the theory, while classical algorithms can sometimes recover ground truth without smoothing.
- Limitation 1: the main PFM result uses a non-standard clustering distance measure, whose naturalness compared to classical measures is debatable.
- Limitation 2: analysis relies on a “proxy” model (reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing it to the observed graph), which constrains what counts as a good clustering.
- These proxy/Laplacian-approximation constraints are necessary for concrete results but may reduce applicability in some real-world cases.
