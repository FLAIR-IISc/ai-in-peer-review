This paper proposes a generic template for theoretical guarantees of clustering stability for a clustering C that fits the data well, measuring stability by the distance between C and any other C' that also fits the data well. The template is instantiated for two block models (PFM and SBM), with all quantities in the guarantees explicitly computable from the data, and the stability results are then used to derive exact recovery guarantees. However, the simulations and the strength of the required conditions suggest limited practical utility: either the chosen goodness-of-fit or the theorem conditions are too restrictive.

1) The paper tackles the important problem of model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering—i.e., guarantees that do not assume the data were generated by a specific model—while ensuring the relevant quantities are computable from the data and the clusterings being compared. Such guarantees are valuable both theoretically and practically.

2) Since PFM contains DC-SBM as a special case, which in turn contains SBM, it is unclear why the PFM and SBM cases are treated differently. If spectral clustering performs well for all these models, why not use a common estimator? In particular, why is the Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM? Also, the PFM results are stated in terms of weighted misclassification error (weighted ME), whereas the SBM results use unweighted ME. How should these be compared? Moreover, why are results for DC-SBM not provided?

3) In Proposition 10, what is the precise definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F?

4) In Assumption 5, is the intended quantity ||L̂^2 − L^2||_F or ||L̂ − L||_F^2? If the former, what is the intuition for squaring the Laplacians?

5) The paper focuses on disjoint clusters within block models. Do the techniques/recipe extend to other settings where spectral clustering works, beyond block models? For example, spectral clustering is effective for hierarchical clustering, for which there are theoretical guarantees (e.g., “Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering,” Balakrishnan et al., 2011). How would the template specialize to that case?

6) Both the evaluation metric and the estimators in the PFM and SBM cases require C and C' to have the same number of clusters. Different algorithms can return different numbers of clusters. For instance, the eigenvalues of A may suggest K clusters for C while those of the Laplacian of A suggest K' = K + 1 for C', with C' identical to C except that one cluster splits into two. C' is still “close” to C, but this notion of proximity is not captured by the current framework.

7) The related work omits several model-free results, such as: “Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm” (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), “Clustering under Perturbation Resilience” (Balcan et al., 2012), and “Clustering under Approximation Stability” (Balcan et al., 2013). How does the stability notion here relate to these? In particular, how does it compare to stability defined via perturbations of centers across two clusterings?

8) The perturbations tolerated in the PFM simulations are extremely small. Lines 252–253 state that “a large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations,” but this is not qualified or explained. Why should a large eigengap exacerbate this sensitivity? Moreover, for the political blog data the adjacency matrix is raised to a power, which should further enlarge eigengaps; paradoxically, this appears to improve stability. If such small perturbations materially change model fit, then the guarantees offer limited practical value, despite one of the main selling points being that all quantities are data-computable. Lines 248–250 also indicate that the SBM guarantee conditions are very hard to satisfy, which is unfortunate.

9) The caption of Figure 1 states, “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0.” Which subplot does this refer to? If SBM is not plotted, the caption should say so explicitly.

10) The PFM theorems provide guarantees in terms of weighted ME, but the experiments perturb ME. Please reconcile this mismatch.

Minor points:
1) Axis labels are missing in Figure 1.
2) Line 44: For block models, A_{ij} for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments of i and j.
3) The symbols B, C, and Y are reused with different meanings in the PFM section and again in Section 3.2 for SBM, which is confusing given the heavy notation. Please maintain consistent notation throughout. For example, use Y_⊥ and C_D instead of B and C for PFM. For the SBM estimator, YZ is not required; it would be clearer to substitute the expression for YZ directly in Proposition 8. Similarly, to distinguish eigenvalues, use λ^L and λ^B for Laplacian- and block-related quantities, respectively.
4) Section 4.4 would fit better as a subsection of Related Work.
