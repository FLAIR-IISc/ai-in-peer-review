- Proposes a novel model-free graph clustering framework that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming data is generated by SBM/DC-SBM/PFM.  
- Key idea: verify a fitted model's goodness-of-fit and derive recovery guarantees from that fit, bridging model-based and model-free approaches.  
- Main theorem: if clustering C fits the data well for model class M, any other clustering that also fits well must be close to C.  
- Instantiates the theorem concretely for PFM and SBM, providing explicit conditions for clustering stability.  
- Technical quality is strong: rigorous proofs, clear definitions (goodness of fit, clustering distance), and well-structured arguments.  
- Improves existing model-based guarantees in some cases (e.g., tightens an error bound from prior work by a factor of 128).  
- Experiments demonstrate the framework is practically checkable and guarantees hold when data fits the model (datasets: PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, Political Blogs).  
- Paper is well-written and organized with consistent notation and accessible background material.  
- Original and novel "turning around" of model-based recovery results to a model-free setting; situates contribution relative to prior work.  
- Potentially significant for practitioners: offers a principled way to validate clusterings without relying on generative model assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: lacks exploration of cases where data does not fit the model and comparisons with existing methods in such regimes.  
- Framework assumes an initial clustering is available and does not address how to find good clusterings.  
- Main weakness: paper does not convincingly argue why the model-free approach is superior to simply using model-based methods with a goodness-of-fit check; SBM conditions can be harder to satisfy.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better articulate practical advantages and broaden experimental evaluation.
