- Proposes a model-free certification framework: fit a proxy model to (G, C) and certify any equally good-fit clustering must be close to C.
- Instantiates the framework for two model classes: PFM (fitting Laplacians) and SBM (fitting adjacency matrices).
- Measures goodness-of-fit by spectral norms: Laplacian deviations for PFM and adjacency deviations for SBM.
- Uses subspace perturbation (Davis–Kahan) to bound distance between clusterings via a weighted misclassification error.
- Connects the model-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery results and sometimes sharpens constants.
- Shows required quantities for the bounds are computable from data and demonstrates empirical behavior.
- Empirically, PFM-based guarantees apply more often than SBM-based ones; smoothing helps on real graphs.
- Novel perspective: distribution-free, data-dependent clustering guarantees without assuming a generative model.
- General and computable criteria: eigengaps and spectral deviations derived directly from observed data and output clustering.
- Theoretical contribution: a clear Generic Theorem with concrete PFM and SBM instantiations and ties to prior results.
- Exposition is readable; technical choices are well motivated despite proofs being in the supplement.
- Main PFM bound uses a weighted misclassification error, which is nonstandard and may hinder comparisons to prior work.
- Guarantees rely on constructing a specific proxy model M(G, C), imposing a particular notion of “good clustering” that may exclude other meaningful criteria.
- Practical applicability is limited: meaningful guarantees on some real data require smoothing, which may change community semantics.
- SBM-based bounds are often loose and uninformative in practice; unclear if this is inherent or due to proof slackness.
- Minor presentation gripe: pervasive use of “hats” for observed quantities (e.g., L̂) is unconventional and distracting.
- Theoretical results appear sound and conditions are precise; experiments are illustrative but limited in scope.
- Clarity would improve with justification for the weighted misclassification metric and comparison to the standard metric.
- Original and timely idea: inverting recovery theorems to obtain distribution-free certification is a valuable conceptual shift.
- Potential significance: provides practitioners a way to certify clustering stability from data, though SBM limitations may limit impact.
- Suggested improvements: report unweighted misclassification or conversion bounds; expand experiments on unsmoothed graphs and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggested improvements: discuss when SBM guarantees can be informative (sparse/heterogeneous regimes) and clarify interpretability of Laplacian/adjacency goodness-of-fit.
- Acceptance arguments: tackles an important question, offers a clean computable framework, solid technical development, and candid discussion of limitations.
- Rejection arguments: relies on a nonstandard distance and proxy-fit notion, exhibits weak SBM guarantees, and depends on smoothing for real data.
