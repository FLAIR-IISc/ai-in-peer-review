- Ambition to develop a general model-free theory for graph clustering is commendable, but execution is unconvincing.  
- Primary weakness: lack of rigor and formal precision in core theoretical claims, which read like high-level intuitions.  
- Theorem 1 hinges on the undefined condition that a clustering "fits G well," requiring a precise, measurable definition.  
- As stated, Theorem 1 is tautological: it asserts that a good clustering is close to another good clustering without formal assumptions.  
- Proposition 3 invokes "standard recovery conditions" without specifying them, citing literature, or justifying applicability in a model-free setting.  
- Vagueness pervades the theoretical development; arguments appear as heuristics rather than a formal, verifiable framework.  
- The paper does not show that its conditions are non-vacuous or achievable under plausible data assumptions.  
- Recommendation: fundamentally rework the theory by providing precise definitions, formal theorem premises, and explicit assumptions for validity.
