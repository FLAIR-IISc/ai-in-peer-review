The paper introduces a general framework for establishing theoretical stability guarantees for clustering solutions, quantifying stability as the distance between a clustering *C* and any other clustering *C'* that equally well fits the data. This framework is then applied to two block models, the Planted Feature Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), with explicit formulas provided for all quantities needed to compute the guarantees directly from data. The resulting stability guarantees are leveraged to derive exact recovery results for these models. However, the simulations raise concerns about the practical utility of the results, potentially due to the strictness of the goodness-of-fit criteria or the theoretical conditions required for the guarantees to hold.

1) The paper tackles the important problem of developing model-agnostic theoretical guarantees for clustering, aiming for guarantees based on quantities computable from data and clustering outputs. This is a valuable contribution for both theoretical advancements and practical applications.

2) Given that the PFM encompasses the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM), which in turn includes the SBM, the differing treatment of PFM and SBM is unclear. Considering the demonstrated effectiveness of Spectral Clustering across all specified models, the rationale for employing distinct model estimators—the Laplacian for PFM versus the adjacency matrix for SBM—is not fully justified.  Furthermore, the PFM results utilize weighted mutual information (ME), while the SBM results employ unweighted ME; a comparison of these metrics would be beneficial. The absence of results for the DC-SBM is also noteworthy.

3) In Proposition 10, the definition of <M', M><sub>F</sub> requires clarification.

4) In Assumption 5, is the condition ||L̂<sup>2</sup> - L<sup>2</sup>||<sub>F</sub> or ||L̂ - L||<sub>F</sub><sup>2</sup>? If the former, the underlying intuition for squaring the Laplacians should be explained.

5) The paper primarily focuses on disjoint clusters. It would be valuable to understand whether the proposed techniques extend to other models where Spectral Clustering performs well, such as hierarchical clustering, for which theoretical guarantees already exist (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., 2011). How would the framework adapt to such cases?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators used for both PFM and SBM are limited to clusterings with the same number of clusters. This restriction prevents the framework from capturing scenarios where different clustering algorithms yield varying numbers of clusters—for instance, where one algorithm splits a cluster into two, resulting in a "close" but distinct clustering *C'*.

7) The related work section lacks comprehensive coverage of existing model-free results, such as those presented in Kumar & Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012, 2013).  A discussion of how the stability notion in this work relates to these prior works, particularly concerning perturbation of cluster centers, would strengthen the paper. 

8) The small perturbations used in the PFM simulations, combined with the statement regarding the eigengap’s sensitivity to even minor perturbations (lines 252-253), raise questions about the practical relevance of the guarantees. The smoothing applied to the political blog data, while seemingly stabilizing the results, may artificially exaggerate eigenvalue gaps.  If small perturbations can significantly impact model fit, the guarantee's usefulness is diminished. Similarly, the challenging conditions for SBM guarantees (lines 248-250) are discouraging.

9) The caption for Figure 1 mentions "\delta is always greater than \delta<sub>0</sub>". Clarification is needed regarding which subplot this refers to. If SBM data is not plotted, the caption should explicitly state this.

10) The theorems for PFM provide guarantees for weighted ME, while the experiments perturb the unweighted ME.

Minor Points:

1) Figure 1 is missing axis labels.
2) (Line 44) For block models, the entries A<sub>ij</sub> for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments of i and j.
3) The symbols B, C, and Y are defined differently for PFM and SBM, potentially causing confusion. Consistent notation throughout the paper would be preferred (e.g., Y<sub>⊥</sub> and C<sub>D</sub>).  The estimator for SBM doesn't require YZ, and expressing the result directly in Proposition 8 would be more concise. Distinguishing the eigenvalues for PFM and SBM as λ<sup>L</sup> and λ<sup>B</sup>, respectively, would enhance clarity.
4) Section 4.4 would be more appropriately placed within the related work section.
