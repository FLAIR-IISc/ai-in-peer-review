- Paper provides new distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering without assuming a generative model.  
- Main idea: if one clustering fits the graph well (under the authors' definition), any other equally good clustering must be close to it.  
- Results are interesting, well-commented, and illustrated, addressing an important problem.  
- Paper is clear and readable despite some content moved to the supplement.  
- Minor clarity issue: using "hats" (e.g., \hat{L}) for the observed Laplacian is questionable.  
- Empirically, some real-world graphs require smoothing for the framework to recover structure, while classical algorithms sometimes recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- Main theoretical result for PFM relies on a non-standard distance measure between clusterings, which is debatable.  
- Results depend on reconstructing a proxy model (e.g., PFM) from a clustering and comparing it to the graph, which constrains what counts as a good clustering.  
- These modeling choices (distance and proxy model) are necessary for provable results but may limit practical applicability.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance, provided authors address limitations and clarify implications of their framework.
