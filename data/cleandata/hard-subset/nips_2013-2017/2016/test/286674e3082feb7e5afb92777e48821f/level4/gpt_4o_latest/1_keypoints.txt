- Novel distribution-free theoretical contributions to graph clustering that do not assume a specific generative model.
- Central claim: if one clustering fits the graph well (by the paper’s definition), any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.
- Results are interesting, significant, and supported by illustrative examples.
- Paper is generally clear and comprehensible despite some material being in the supplement due to page limits.
- Notation concern: using “hats” for observed quantities (e.g., \hat{L}) is questionable.
- Empirical caveat: some real-world graphs need smoothing to reveal structure compatible with the theory, whereas classical algorithms can often recover ground truth without preprocessing.
- Limitation 1: main PFM result uses a non-standard distance between clusterings, and the claim that it’s more natural is debatable.
- Limitation 2: results rely on a “proxy” model (reconstruct a model from a clustering and compare it to the graph) rather than directly comparing clusterings to the graph or its generative process.
- The proxy approach requires the graph to be well-approximated in the normalized Laplacian sense, which limits broader applicability to some real datasets.
