- Addresses model-free theoretical guarantees for graph clustering, tying guarantees to data statistics and instantiating results for SBM and PFM.
- Introduces a general theorem: any clustering that also fits the data well must be a small perturbation of a well-fitting clustering.
- Clear introduction framing limitations of model-based clustering assumptions.
- Uses standard, well-defined mathematical notation and precisely defines goodness-of-fit and clustering distance measures (Misclassification Error and a weighted version).

Strengths:
- Novel framework offering model-free guarantees for clustering, addressing a key limitation of model-based methods.
- Demonstrates connections to and potential improvements of existing model-based recovery theorems.
- Mathematically rigorous and well-structured presentation.
- Concrete instantiations for SBM and PFM illustrate practical implications.
- Experimental validation on simulated and real data supports the theoretical stability guarantees.

Weaknesses:
- Motivation for this specific framework could be stronger and more clearly tied to practical clustering challenges.
- Theorems rely on potentially restrictive assumptions (e.g., eigengaps, goodness-of-fit) that may not hold in real-world graphs.
- Derived bounds are complex and involve many parameters, limiting interpretability and apparent practical usefulness.
- Insufficiently thorough comparison to existing graph stability results and related work.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, theoretically sound contribution with insightful links to model-based methods, clear mathematics, and supporting experiments.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Restrictive assumptions, complexity of results, and weaker motivation/comparison sections could limit impact.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, novel paper that merits acceptance, provided the authors address assumptions, clarify interpretation of bounds, and strengthen comparisons to related stability literature.
