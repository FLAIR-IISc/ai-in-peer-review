This paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics used to compare clusterings of network data. Focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), the authors prove a locally convex-like property for clustering solutions. Specifically, they demonstrate that clusterings which fit the network data equally well – in terms of model parameter estimation – are also close to each other according to various misclassification-based distance measures. This establishes a useful equivalence between these distance measures for evaluating clusterings in network data, and is supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental validation. While the results presented are novel, related work has recently appeared in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun and Zanetti (COLT, 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). Furthermore, the theoretical contributions build upon well-established results in the field. The authors suggest potential applications to detecting clusterability in networks, however, the paper does not include formal results supporting these applications. Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly explains its core concepts.
