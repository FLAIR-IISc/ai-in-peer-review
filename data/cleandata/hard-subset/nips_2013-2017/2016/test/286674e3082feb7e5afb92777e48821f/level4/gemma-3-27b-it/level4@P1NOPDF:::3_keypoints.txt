- Algorithms clearly described but overall motivation and rationale for presenting them as distinct methods is weak.  
- Novelty limited: largely modifications of existing approaches with main innovation in the later-introduced constraints.  
- Core contribution: relaxing balanced cluster-size assumption in spectral clustering—useful for real-world imbalanced graphs.  
- Paper fails to sufficiently explain why standard spectral methods specifically struggle with imbalance.  
- Theoretical error bounds are loose and rely on hard-to-estimate parameters (e.g., graph expansion).  
- Authors should provide guidance on approximating those parameters or relate bounds to observable graph properties.  
- Practical implications of the theoretical bounds (expected error levels) are not clearly articulated.  
- Experiments (synthetic and real) show improvements for imbalanced clusters but comparisons to recent SOTA (e.g., node embeddings/deep methods) are limited.  
- Sensitivity analysis of key hyperparameters is shallow and needs more thorough exploration.  
- Constraints are an interesting contribution but the paper lacks methods or guidelines for choosing/learning constraints in practice.  
- Notation mostly clear but some definitions (e.g., 'similarity' between clusterings) need more precision.  
- Figure 1 needs a more detailed caption explaining algorithms and their relationships.  
- Writing is repetitive in places; streamlining would improve readability.  
- Conclusion repeats benefits but omits discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Overall: technically sound and relevant; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above points.
