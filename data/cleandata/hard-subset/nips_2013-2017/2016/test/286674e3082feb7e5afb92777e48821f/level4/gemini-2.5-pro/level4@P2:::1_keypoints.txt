- Summary: Proposes a model-free stability framework that certifies uniqueness of well-fitting graph clusterings (PFM/SBM) without assuming a data-generating model, deriving explicit bounds and connecting to existing recovery results.

- Originality/significance: Tackles the reliance on model assumptions in block-model theory by turning model-based recovery theorems into practical, distribution-free validation tools.

- Technical quality: Solid theory using spectral perturbation (Davis–Kahan), with computable quantities (goodness-of-fit ε, eigengaps, clustering distance) making the framework practically checkable.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized; main manuscript is largely self-contained and effectively communicates ideas with proofs in the supplement.

- Strictness of conditions: Theoretical conditions can be too stringent for real-world graphs (e.g., required smoothing on Political Blogs), raising applicability concerns for noisy raw data.

- Constrained notion of “good clustering”: Defines goodness by fit to a reconstructed block model, limiting the framework’s ability to validate non-block-model community structures.

- Non-standard distance metric: Uses weighted misclassification error (wME) with degree weighting instead of the common unweighted ME, complicating direct comparisons to prior work.

- Minor notation point: Use of hats (e.g., Â, L̂) for observed quantities should be briefly clarified early (Section 2).

- Overall assessment: A significant, technically sound, and clearly presented contribution that advances model-free cluster validation, though practical utility is limited by strict bounds and block-model-centric scope.
