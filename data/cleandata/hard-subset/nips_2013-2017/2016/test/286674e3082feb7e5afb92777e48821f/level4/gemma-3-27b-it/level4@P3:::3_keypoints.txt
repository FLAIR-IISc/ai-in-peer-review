- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that guarantees stability based on goodness-of-fit rather than specific generative models.  
- Instantiates the framework for SBM and PFM, connecting to and sometimes strengthening existing model-based recovery results.  
- Presents rigorous theoretical treatment with theorems and proofs supporting the framework.  
- Demonstrates computational feasibility by emphasizing quantities in the theorems are computable.  
- Shows the framework’s generality by applying it to multiple models (SBM and PFM).  
- Novel approach addressing a significant limitation of model-based clustering methods.  
- Effectively situates the work within prior literature on spectral clustering and block models.  
- Motivation and narrative feel fragmented; unclear why results are presented as algorithms rather than a unified theory.  
- Theorems depend on restrictive assumptions (e.g., eigengaps, goodness-of-fit) whose practical implications are not fully discussed.  
- Proofs are dense and may be hard to follow; more intuitive explanations of key steps are needed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope, with brief discussion and insufficient comparisons or dataset diversity.  
- SBM guarantees appear harder to satisfy and need further investigation and explanation.  
- Related work citation is adequate but engagement with recent advances in robust clustering and stability analysis is shallow.  
- Overall: technically sound and potentially impactful, but clarity, practical discussion of assumptions, and experiments need improvement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, connects model-free and model-based recovery, and computationally feasible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity, restrictive assumptions, limited experiments, and insufficient engagement with recent related work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify motivation, expand experiments, provide intuitive proof explanations, and discuss SBM guarantee issues.
