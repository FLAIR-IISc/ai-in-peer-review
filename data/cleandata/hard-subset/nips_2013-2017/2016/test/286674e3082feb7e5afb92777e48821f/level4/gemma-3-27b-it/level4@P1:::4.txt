The paper presents a generic framework for theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering *C* that fits the data well. The stability is measured in terms of the distance between *C* and any other clustering *C'* that also fits the data well. The paper provides specific instantiations of this framework for two block models, the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), and demonstrates that all quantities involved in the guarantees can be computed explicitly from the data. Furthermore, it leverages these stability results to establish recovery guarantees for the models. However, the simulations suggest that the goodness-of-fit criteria used, or the conditions imposed by the theorems, render the results of limited practical use.

1) The paper addresses the important problem of providing generic theoretical guarantees for clustering without assuming the data originates from a specific model. The goal is to obtain such guarantees using quantities computable from the data and the output of clustering algorithms, offering both theoretical and practical benefits.

2) Given that the PFM encompasses both the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM) and the SBM as special cases, it is unclear why these three cases are treated differently. Spectral Clustering performs well for all the specified models; why not employ the same model estimator across the board? Specifically, the Laplacian is used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Additionally, the PFM results are expressed in terms of weighted Misclassification Error (ME) while the SBM results use standard ME. How do these different metrics compare? Furthermore, why are results for the DC-SBM not provided?

3) In Proposition 10, what is the definition of <*M'*, *M*><sub>F</sub>?

4) In Assumption 5: Is it ||L̂<sup>2</sup> - L<sup>2</sup>||<sub>F</sub> or ||L̂ - L||<sub>F</sub><sup>2</sup>? If the former, what is the intuition behind squaring the Laplacians?

5) The paper primarily considers disjoint clusters. Do the techniques/framework used in this paper extend to other models for which Spectral Clustering performs well, such as hierarchical clustering, for which theoretical guarantees exist (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., 2011)? How would the framework apply in such cases?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators considered for both PFM and SBM are limited to clusterings *C* and *C'* having the same number of clusters. Different clustering algorithms can potentially yield different numbers of clusters. For example, the eigenvalues of A might suggest *K* clusters for *C*, while those of the Laplacian of A might suggest *K'* = *K* + 1 clusters for *C'*, with all clusters in *C* and *C'* being identical except for one cluster splitting into two. *C'* is still "close" to *C*, but this scenario is not captured by the current framework.

7) The Related Work section lacks sufficient coverage of existing model-free results, such as Kumar & Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012, 2013). How does the notion of stability considered in this work relate to those in these works? Specifically, how does the stability presented here relate to the stability measured in terms of perturbation of cluster centers?

8) The perturbations tolerated in the simulations for PFM appear very small. Line 252-253 casually states that the large eigengap makes PFM(*G*, *C*) differ from PFM(*G*, *C*<sub>0</sub>) even for very small perturbations, without further qualification. It is unclear why this is the case. On the other hand, smoothing the political blog data by raising the power of A seems to help stability, despite potentially exaggerating eigenvalue gaps. Also, if such small perturbations can significantly affect the model fit, it diminishes the practical usefulness of the guarantee, which is a key selling point given the data-driven nature of the quantities involved. Lines 248-250 state that the SBM guarantee conditions are difficult to satisfy, which is unfortunate.

9) The Figure 1 caption mentions “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ<sub>0</sub>”. Which subplot does this refer to? If the SBM is not plotted, this should be clarified in the caption.

10) The PFM guarantees are for weighted ME, whereas the experiment involves perturbation using standard ME.

Minor Points:

1) Labels for the axes in Figure 1 are missing.
2) (Line 44) For block models, A<sub>ij</sub> for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments for i and j.
3) The symbols B, C, and Y have been used with different definitions for PFM and later in Section 3.2 for SBM. This can be confusing. Given the numerous notations in the paper, maintaining consistency is beneficial. Perhaps use Y<sub>orthogonal</sub> and C<sub>D</sub>, respectively, instead of B and C for PFM.  Also, for the SBM estimator, YZ is not required; using the expression of YZ directly in Proposition 8 would simplify the presentation. Similarly, using λ<sup>L</sup> and λ<sup>B</sup> would clearly distinguish between eigenvalues in PFM and SBM.
4) Section 4.4 is better suited as a subsection within the Related Work section.



**Overall Assessment:** The paper presents an interesting theoretical framework for model-free clustering validation. However, the practical relevance of the results is questionable given the stringent conditions required to satisfy the guarantees and the limited experimental validation. The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to existing work and a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the approach. While the ideas are novel, the current form of the results appears to offer limited immediate impact. I recommend rejection.
