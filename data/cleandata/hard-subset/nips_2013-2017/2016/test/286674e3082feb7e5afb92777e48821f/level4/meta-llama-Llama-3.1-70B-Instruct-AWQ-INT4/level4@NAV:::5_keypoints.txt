- Provides broad concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering across various generative models without depending on model parameters.
- Engaging, relevant work that significantly advances an important research area.
- Raises and addresses the important question of stability in community detection, proving general results and applying them to notable graphical models.
- Writing is clear and enjoyable, with well-explained motivations for error definitions and proof assumptions.
- Main limitation: proofs rely on spectral algorithms/results that are not tight and may be intractable for many sparse-graph community detection problems (noted by authors).
- Overall a promising first step in stability results for community detection, likely to spur further research.
