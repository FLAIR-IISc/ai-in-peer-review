- Paper proposes algorithms for graph clustering and a metric for comparing clusterings but lacks a clear motivating problem and narrative connecting methods.
- Procedures are clearly described, but most methods are minor variations of established algorithms with novelty mainly in a late-introduced set of constraints.

- Strength: Algorithms are described clearly with easy-to-follow procedures.
- Strength: Focus on practical graph clustering and comparison of outputs, relevant to many applications.
- Strength: Constraint-based extensions may be useful for domain-specific structure.

- Weakness: Motivation is weak and it’s unclear which concrete problems require this collection of algorithms.
- Weakness: Novelty is limited; most contributions seem adapted from known techniques without strong justification.
- Weakness: Paper reads like a list of algorithms rather than a unified framework or cohesive contribution.
- Weakness: Relationship to established clustering-comparison measures (e.g., ARI, NMI, VI) is not addressed.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees (complexity, convergence, approximation) and empirical validation.

- Suggestion: Precisely define the graph clustering tasks, application scenarios, and desired properties (scalability, robustness, interpretability).
- Suggestion: Present a unifying objective or optimization template and explain trade-offs between algorithmic variants.
- Suggestion: Formally state and analyze the proposed constraints, including feasibility and integration into base methods.
- Suggestion: Situate each algorithm relative to prior work (spectral clustering, modularity, SBMs, constrained clustering) and clearly delineate novelty.
- Suggestion: Relate the similarity test to ARI/NMI/VI, provide theoretical motivation (invariances, bias/variance), and state when it’s preferable.
- Suggestion: Provide time/space complexity, theoretical guarantees or approximation bounds, and discuss optimization hardness or relaxations.
- Suggestion: Strengthen empirical evaluation with synthetic and real networks, strong baselines, ablations, standard metrics, and scalability/robustness analyses.
- Suggestion: Improve clarity and organization: foreground contributions, move variants to appendix, ensure consistent notation, and add illustrative figures.

- Overall: Important area but currently reads as incremental tweaks with novelty confined to constraints; needs clearer problem statement, unified theory, stronger comparisons, and better theory/experiments — reviewer leans toward rejection but encourages revision.
