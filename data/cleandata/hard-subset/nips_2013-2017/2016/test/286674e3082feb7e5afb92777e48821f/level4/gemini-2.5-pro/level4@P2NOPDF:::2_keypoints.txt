- Establishes a key relationship between distance metrics for clusterings in network data, proving a "locally convex-like" property for PFM and SBM.  
- Shows that any two clusterings that fit the observed network data equally well must be close in standard misclassification distance, linking model-fit and clustering distances.  
- Supports claims with both theoretical proofs and experimental validation.  
- Novel theoretical contribution providing a useful structural property for network clustering; paper is well-written and clear.  
- Results resemble recent works (Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. ArXiv 2015) and require a more detailed comparison to clarify unique contributions.  
- Theoretical analysis appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of well-known results.  
- Suggests potential applications in detecting "clusterability" but offers no formal results or concrete methodology, leaving this to future work.
