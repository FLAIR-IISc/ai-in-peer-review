- Proposes a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that provides theoretical guarantees without assuming a data-generating model.  
- Introduces a generic theorem linking model-free analysis with existing model-based recovery results.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), yielding model-free guarantees for both.  
- Provides thorough background on graphs, clusterings, and block models for context.  
- Main theorem and its instantiations are well-explained and supported by propositions and proofs.  
- Experimental evaluation on multiple datasets demonstrates the approach’s empirical effectiveness.  
- Strength: novelty in providing model-free guarantees and strong analysis of PFM and SBM.  
- Strength: potential to advance the state of the art in graph clustering.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and assumptions of the proposed framework.  
- Weakness: limited insight into the relationship between model-based and model-free approaches.  
- Weakness: needs more context and comparisons to prior work in graph clustering.  
- Clarity issues: some long, dense paragraphs; suggested use of more headings/subheadings and clearer language.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with valuable contributions; recommend revisions to address noted weaknesses.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10, confidence 8/10.
