The paper introduces a general template for theoretically guaranteeing the stability of a clustering *C* that well-fits the data. Stability is defined as the distance between *C* and any other well-fitting clustering *C'*. The template is then applied to two block models – the Planted Function Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) – with all quantities involved in the guarantees explicitly computable from data. These stability results are further leveraged to derive exact recovery guarantees for both models. However, the simulations raise concerns about the practical utility of the results, potentially due to the strictness of the goodness-of-fit criteria or the conditions imposed by the theorems.

1) The paper tackles the important problem of providing model-agnostic theoretical guarantees for clustering, aiming for guarantees based on quantities directly computable from data and clustering outputs. Such model-free guarantees are valuable for both theoretical advancement and practical application.

2) Considering that the PFM encompasses the Degree-Corrected Stochastic Block Model (DC-SBM), which in turn includes the SBM, the differing treatment of PFM and SBM is unclear. Given the demonstrated effectiveness of Spectral Clustering across all specified models, the rationale for employing different model estimators – the Laplacian for PFM versus the adjacency matrix for SBM – is not fully explained. Furthermore, the PFM results utilize weighted Mutual Information (ME), while the SBM results employ unweighted ME; a comparison of these two measures would be beneficial. The absence of results for the DC-SBM is also noteworthy.

3) In Proposition 10, clarification is needed regarding the definition of <*M'*, *M*>*<sub>F</sub>.

4) In Assumption 5, it is unclear whether the condition is ||*L̂*<sup>2</sup> - *L*<sup>2</sup>||<sub>F</sub> or ||*L̂* - *L*||<sub>F</sub><sup>2</sup>. If the former, the intuition behind squaring the Laplacians warrants explanation.

5) The paper primarily focuses on disjoint clusters. It would be valuable to understand whether the proposed techniques extend beyond block models to other scenarios where Spectral Clustering performs well, such as hierarchical clustering (e.g., referencing Balakrishnan et al., 2011). How would the template adapt to such cases?

6) The evaluation metric and estimators considered are limited to clusterings *C* and *C'* with the same number of clusters. This limitation prevents capturing scenarios where different algorithms yield varying cluster numbers. For example, an eigenvalue analysis might suggest *K* clusters for *C* and *K'* = *K* + 1 clusters for *C'*, with *C'* closely resembling *C* except for a single cluster split. This framework cannot accommodate such situations.

7) The related work section lacks comprehensive coverage of existing model-free results, such as those presented in Kumar & Kannan (2010), Balcan et al. (2012, 2013). A discussion of how the stability notion in this work relates to these prior works – particularly concerning perturbation of cluster centers – would be beneficial.

8) The simulations for PFM utilize small perturbations. The statement in lines 252-253 regarding the sensitivity of PFM(G, C) to small perturbations due to a large eigengap requires further justification. Conversely, smoothing the political blog data by raising the power of *A* exacerbates eigenvalue gaps, yet appears to *improve* stability. If small perturbations significantly impact model fit, the practical relevance of the guarantees is diminished. The observation in lines 248-250 that the SBM guarantee conditions are difficult to satisfy is also concerning.

9) The caption of Figure 1 mentions “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ<sub>0</sub>”. It should be clarified which subplot this refers to, or the SBM data should be included in the plot.

10) The theorems for PFM guarantee stability for weighted ME, while the experiments perturb the unweighted ME.

Minor Points:

1) Figure 1 lacks axis labels.
2) (Line 44) For block models, *A<sub>ij</sub>* for *i* > *j* are independent given the cluster assignments for *i* and *j*.
3) The symbols *B*, *C*, and *Y* are redefined for PFM and SBM, potentially causing confusion. Maintaining consistency in notation throughout the paper is recommended (e.g., using *Y<sub>orthogonal</sub>* and *C<sub>D</sub>* for PFM).  The estimator for SBM does not require *YZ*; directly using its expression in Proposition 8 would be preferable. Similarly, using *λ<sup>L</sup>* and *λ<sup>B</sup>* would clearly distinguish between eigenvalues in PFM and SBM.
4) Section 4.4 is more appropriately placed within the related work section.
