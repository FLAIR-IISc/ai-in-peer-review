1. The goal of model-free, data-dependent theoretical guarantees for clustering is well-motivated and significant.

2. Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated separately when PFM includes DC-SBM/SBM and spectral methods work across models; questions about choice of Laplacian vs. adjacency, weighted vs. standard ME, and omission of DC-SBM results.

3. Proposition 10 references ⟨M', M⟩_F but the inner-product definition is unclear.

4. Assumption 5 ambiguity: is it ‖ĤL^2 − L^2‖_F or ‖ĤL − L‖_F^2, and if the former, why square the Laplacians?

5. Framework restricts to disjoint clusters; ask whether techniques extend to other settings where spectral clustering succeeds (e.g., hierarchical clustering) and how the template would apply.

6. Evaluation assumes identical cluster counts for C and C′, but real estimators may suggest different K (e.g., splits), leaving close-but-different clusterings outside the framework.

7. Related work omits key model-free clustering results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012/2013); request clarification on the relation between this stability notion and prior perturbation/center-perturbation results.

8. Simulations suggest PFM tolerates only tiny perturbations and SBM conditions are hard to satisfy; contradictory evidence that smoothing (A^p) can increase eigengaps and improve stability raises concerns about practical relevance of the guarantees.

9. Figure 1 caption claims "For SBM, δ > δ0" yet SBM is not plotted; caption needs clarification.

10. Theoretical results use weighted ME for PFM while experiments perturb standard ME, creating a mismatch that must be addressed.
