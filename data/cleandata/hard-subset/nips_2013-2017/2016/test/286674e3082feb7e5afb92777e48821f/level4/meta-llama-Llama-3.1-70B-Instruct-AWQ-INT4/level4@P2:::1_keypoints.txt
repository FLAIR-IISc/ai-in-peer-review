- Paper proposes distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering showing that any clustering fitting the graph well must be close to another good fit.
- Main idea is original and addresses an important problem in graph clustering without generative assumptions.
- Authors provide a clear summary, relate to prior NIPS work, and discuss strengths/weaknesses with pro/con arguments.
- Paper is well-written, results are well-commented and illustrated.
- Notation issue: using "hats" on the actual observation (e.g., \hat{L}) is not ideal and alternative notation should be considered.
- Experiments show limitations: some real-world graphs require smoothing for structure to be recoverable, while classical algorithms can recover ground truth without smoothing.
- Main limitation 1: the PFM result uses a non-standard distance between clusterings, whose naturalness is questionable.
- Main limitation 2: results rely on a "proxy" model approach—reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing it to the original graph—thereby constraining the notion of a good clustering.
- Overall contribution is valuable and significant, but limitations should be addressed in future work.
- Quality: 8/10; Clarity: 8/10; Originality: 9/10; Significance: 8/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and improve notation.
