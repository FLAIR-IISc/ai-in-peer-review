- Establishes relationships among different clustering distance measures in network settings.
- Focuses analysis on the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Proves a locally convex–like property: any two clusterings that fit the data equally well must be close in misclassification distance.
- States and proves equivalence or near-equivalence between several clustering distance measures for network data.
- Supports claims with both theoretical analysis and empirical experiments.
- Results are novel but related to recent works (Peng, Sun, Zanetti COLT 2015; Chao, Lu, Ma, Zhou arXiv 2015).
- Some theoretical arguments appear to be straightforward extensions of well-known results.
- Notes potential applications to detecting clusterability but provides no formal results on that front.
- Paper is well written and clearly conveys the central ideas.
