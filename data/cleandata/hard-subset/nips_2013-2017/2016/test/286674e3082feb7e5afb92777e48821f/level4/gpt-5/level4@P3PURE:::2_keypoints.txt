- Studies relationships between different distance measures on clusterings in network models (PFM and SBM).
- Proves a locally convex-like property: two clusterings that fit the data equally well must be close under misclassification-based distances.
- Establishes equivalence across multiple clustering distance measures: comparable model fit implies proximity of assignments.
- Provides both theoretical results and empirical validation supporting these claims.
- Contributions are novel but related to recent work (Peng et al., COLT 2015; Chao et al., arXiv 2015).
- Theoretical arguments are relatively straightforward extensions of well-known results.
- Notes potential applications to detecting clusterability but does not provide formal results on that. 
- Paper is well written and clearly presents its core ideas.
