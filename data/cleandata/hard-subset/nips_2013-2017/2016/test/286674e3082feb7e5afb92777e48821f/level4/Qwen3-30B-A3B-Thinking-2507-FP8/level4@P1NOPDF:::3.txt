

The manuscript introduces algorithms for graph clustering and for testing similarity between cluster assignments. While the algorithms themselves are clearly described, the motivation for presenting them as a disjointed list is inadequately justified. The core technical contributions are adaptations of established methods, with the primary novelty residing solely in the constraints introduced at the conclusion. The paper fails to articulate why these specific modifications are necessary or how they address gaps in prior work, leaving the reader to question the rationale for the algorithmic choices. Without a compelling narrative linking the proposed methods to the problem's underlying challenges, the contribution appears incremental rather than substantive. The constraints, though potentially useful, are presented without sufficient context regarding their theoretical or practical significance relative to existing approaches. This weakens the paper's overall impact, as the novelty is not convincingly framed as a meaningful advancement. The authors should strengthen the introduction and discussion to clarify the problem context, justify the algorithmic design choices, and explicitly position the constraints within the broader literature.
