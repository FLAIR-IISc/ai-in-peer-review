- Attempts to develop a theoretical framework for model-free graph clustering, but the approach is naive and unsuccessful.
- Results are unconvincing and do not substantiate the claimed contributions.
- Authors claim a “new theory,” but arguments are largely handwaving rather than rigorous.
- Theorem 1 (“Generic Theorem”) is vague: stating a graph approximated by matrix C is “close to G” without precise guarantees.
- Proposition 3 is informal and depends on unspecified “standard recovery conditions.”
- Repeated lack of mathematical precision throughout the paper.
- Core theoretical contributions are ill-defined and inadequately justified.
