- Introduces a model-free framework guaranteeing clustering stability: if clustering C fits data well under a model class, any other well-fitting C' is a small perturbation of C.  
- Instantiates the framework for SBM and PFM, deriving explicit, computable bounds.  
- All theoretical quantities are directly computable from the data, avoiding dependence on unknown ground-truth parameters.  
- Shows how the model-free perspective can strengthen existing model-based recovery results.  
- Strength: highly original and potentially significant; shifts focus from recovering a latent true clustering to guaranteeing stability of a good clustering.  
- Strength: practical usefulness because guarantees are expressed in data-computable quantities.  
- Weakness: SBM and PFM are treated disparately despite SBM being a special case of PFM; no unified treatment or justification for different analyses.  
- Weakness: Analysis uses Laplacian and weighted ME for PFM, adjacency and standard ME for SBM — missing comparison between wME and ME.  
- Weakness: Omission of Degree-Corrected SBM leaves a gap between SBM and PFM generality.  
- Weakness: Derived bounds appear too loose in experiments; PFM guarantees only hold for extremely small perturbations, and SBM conditions never held in tested data.  
- Weakness: Explanation attributing looseness to large eigengap is underdeveloped and counterintuitive.  
- Weakness: Framework only compares clusterings with the same number of clusters K, failing to capture refinements or splits.  
- Weakness: Overlooks related model-free stability literature (perturbation resilience, approximation stability) and spectral analyses like Kumar & Kannan (2010).  
- Question: Please justify analyzing squared Laplacians (||L̂² - L²||_F) instead of ||L̂ - L||_F and provide intuition.  
- Question: Define notation <M̂, M>_F explicitly (presumably the Frobenius inner product).  
- Question: Address mismatch between experiments measuring unweighted ME and theoretical PFM guarantees stated for weighted ME.  
- Question: Discuss whether the stability framework extends beyond block models to other spectral-method-applicable structures.  
- Minor: Figure 1 lacks axis labels and should clarify why SBM results are omitted.  
- Minor: Reuse of notation (B, C, Y, λ) across SBM and PFM is confusing; suggest distinct symbols for Laplacian vs adjacency eigenvalues.  
- Minor: Clarify that edges are conditionally independent given node partitions in block models.  
- Minor: Section 4.4 on clusterability and resilience should be moved into related work or significance discussion.
