

This paper establishes broad concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering, applicable across diverse community detection scenarios. The authors derive these guarantees for multiple generative models without relying on specific model parameters. The work is highly relevant, advances a significant area of research, and addresses a compelling question regarding solution stability in community detection. The results are presented with notable generality while being effectively applied to prominent graphical models of interest. The paper is exceptionally well-written, offering a clear and engaging exposition. The motivation for nearly all error definitions and proof assumptions is thoroughly explained. The sole limitation, which the authors acknowledge in their conclusion, is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms with non-tight theoretical bounds—these results become nearly intractable for sparse graphs, the most challenging cases in community detection. Despite this, the paper provides a strong foundation for future stability analysis in community detection, and I anticipate significant progress in this direction.
