This paper offers broad concentration guarantees for community detection problems using spectral clustering techniques, deriving these guarantees for various generative models without relying on their parameters. The authors' work is engaging, pertinent, and significantly advances a prominent area of study. The paper raises an intriguing question about the stability of community detection solutions, presenting and proving results with great generality while applying them to notable graphical models. The writing is clear and enjoyable, with well-explained motivations behind the definitions of error and assumptions used in the proofs. The primary criticism, acknowledged by the authors, is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms and results that are not tight and can be intractable for many interesting community detection problems with sparse graphs. Nevertheless, this paper marks a promising beginning in the field of stability results for community detection, and it is expected that this area will be further explored in the future.
