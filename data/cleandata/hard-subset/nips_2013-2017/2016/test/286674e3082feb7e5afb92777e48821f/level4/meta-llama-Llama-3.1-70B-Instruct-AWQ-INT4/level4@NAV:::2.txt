This paper establishes a connection between various clustering distances, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM). The authors demonstrate a locally convex-like property for clustering, showing that clusterings with comparable model fit to network data are also proximal in terms of mis-classification distance metrics. A notable contribution of the paper is the derivation of an equivalence property between distinct clustering distance measures for network data, supported by both theoretical and experimental evidence. While the results are innovative, they share similarities with recent studies in network literature, including Peng et al. (COLT, 2015) and Chao et al. (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical aspects of the paper build upon established concepts, albeit in a straightforward manner. Although the authors mention potential applications in detecting clusterability, formal results in this regard are not provided. The paper is well-structured and effectively communicates its core ideas.
