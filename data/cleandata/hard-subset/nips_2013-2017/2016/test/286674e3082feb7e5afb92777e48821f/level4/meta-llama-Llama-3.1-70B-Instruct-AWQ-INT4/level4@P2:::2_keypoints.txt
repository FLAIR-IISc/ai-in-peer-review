- Proposes a model-free framework for obtaining "correctness" guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific data-generating model.  
- Establishes a locally convex-like property: any two clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.  
- Bridges model-free and model-based methods, enabling use of model-based recovery results to strengthen model-free guarantees.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees specifically for the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM).  
- Demonstrates the approach empirically on simulated and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents novel contributions to understanding graph clustering.  
- Theoretical results are not highly complex and largely build on existing prior work.  
- Insufficient discussion of the wider implications (e.g., formal results on detecting clusterability).  
- Lacks a thorough comparison and discussion of differences/similarities with existing related methods.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
