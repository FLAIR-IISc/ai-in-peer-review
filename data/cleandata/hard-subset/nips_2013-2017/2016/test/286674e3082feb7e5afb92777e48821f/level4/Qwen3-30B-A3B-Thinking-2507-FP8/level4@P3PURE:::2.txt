

The paper establishes a relationship between different distance metrics for clustering in network data. It analyzes network models including the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM), proving a locally convex-like property for clustering objectives. Specifically, it demonstrates that any two clusterings which fit the model parameters equally well must also be close in terms of mis-classification distance. This equivalence between different clustering distance measures for network data is rigorously established and proved. The paper provides both theoretical analysis and experimental validation of its results. While the core findings are novel, recent work of similar nature has appeared in the literature (e.g., Peng et al., COLT 2015; Chao et al., arXiv 2015). The theoretical contributions constitute straightforward extensions of well-established results. Although the paper suggests potential applications in clusterability detection, it does not provide formal results supporting this application. The paper is well-written and clearly communicates its central ideas.
