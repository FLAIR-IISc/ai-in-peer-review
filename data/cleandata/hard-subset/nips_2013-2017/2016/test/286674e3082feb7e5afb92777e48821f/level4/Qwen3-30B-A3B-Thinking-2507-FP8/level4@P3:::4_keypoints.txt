- Introduces a model-free framework for graph clustering guarantees that does not assume data comes from a specific generative model.

- Core idea: any clustering that fits the data well (by a computable goodness-of-fit) must be close to a given clustering C.

- Framework instantiated for PFM and SBM, showing close connections to model-based guarantees and strengthening some (Theorem 11 improves prior bounds by factor 128).

- Strong technical quality with rigorous proofs; PFM analysis leverages spectral perturbation and uses weighted misclassification error (wME) to handle degree heterogeneity.

- High originality: shifts perspective from “data comes from a model” to “model fits the data,” a novel conceptual contribution for block models.

- Practical utility is questionable: experimental results indicate required conditions are very restrictive and often uninformative (e.g., SBM δ values > 1).

- Inconsistent treatment of models: PFM and SBM handled with different estimators and distance measures despite SBM ⊂ DC-SBM ⊂ PFM, and DC-SBM is omitted without justification.

- Related work section is inadequate, failing to cite and relate to model-free/stability literature (e.g., Balcan et al. 2012/2013, Kumar & Kannan 2010).

- Experimental evaluation limitations: PFM tolerated perturbations are extremely small and not quantified; political blogs smoothing (A^3) improves stability but is unexplained.

- Framework assumes fixed number of clusters K for C and C', which is restrictive and cannot handle splits/merges or differing cluster counts.

- Minor issues: inconsistent notation across sections, missing axis labels in Figure 1, unclear definitions (e.g., inner product in Proposition 10), and lack of explanation why SBM results are much more restrictive than PFM.

- Overall assessment: conceptually valuable and technically solid for PFM but limited practical applicability, unclear SBM treatment, and insufficient engagement with related stability literature; authors should clarify limitations and strengthen/justify SBM results.
