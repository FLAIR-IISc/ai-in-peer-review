Provides very general concentration guarantees for community detection using spectral clustering techniques.

Proves those guarantees for multiple generative models without using model-specific parameters.

Advances the field by formulating and addressing the stability of community detection solutions.

States results in great generality while also applying them to specific popular graphical models.

Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly motivates its error definitions and required assumptions.

Main criticism: proofs depend on spectral algorithms that are not tight and are nearly intractable for very sparse graphs (acknowledged by authors).

Overall positive assessment: a strong starting contribution to stability results for community detection with room for future improvement.
