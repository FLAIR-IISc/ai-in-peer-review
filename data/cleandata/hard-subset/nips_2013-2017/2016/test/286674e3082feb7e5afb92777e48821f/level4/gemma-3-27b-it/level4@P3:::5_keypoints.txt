- Presents a general, model-free framework giving theoretical guarantees for graph clustering without assuming a specific generative model.  
- Shows that if a clustering fits the data well, any other well-fitting clustering is a small perturbation of it (stability/robustness result).  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Connects model-free guarantees to existing model-based recovery theorems, bridging two strands of literature.  
- All quantities required to apply the theorems are computable from the data, addressing practicality concerns.  
- The framework is novel, broadly applicable, and offers a unified perspective on clustering validation.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous and well-proven.  
- Writing and exposition are clear and well-organized, making technical ideas accessible.  
- Experimental validation is limited and provides only preliminary assessment of theoretical claims.  
- Reliance on spectral algorithms is a key weakness, reducing applicability in sparse graph settings.  
- Theoretical bounds may be loose or conservative in some scenarios, as suggested by experiments.  
- Related work section could better cover recent advances in spectral clustering and stability results.  
- Discussion would benefit from exploring alternative goodness-of-fit measures or distances between clusterings beyond ME and WME.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and investigate extensions to non-spectral clustering methods.
