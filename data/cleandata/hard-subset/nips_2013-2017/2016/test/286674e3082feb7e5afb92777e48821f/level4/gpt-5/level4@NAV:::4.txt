The paper introduces a general template to establish theoretical guarantees on the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well, where stability is defined via the distance between C and any other clustering C' that also achieves good fit. The template is instantiated for two block models, PFM and SBM, with all relevant quantities in the guarantees computable explicitly from the data. The authors also leverage these stability results to obtain exact recovery guarantees. However, the simulations suggest that either the goodness-of-fit notion or the theorem conditions make the guarantees of limited practical utility.

1) The paper addresses the problem of providing a model-agnostic template for clustering guarantees without assuming a specific generative model. The aim is to derive guarantees in terms of quantities computable from the data and from the outputs of the clustering algorithms being compared. Such model-free guarantees are valuable for both theory and practice.

2) Since PFM contains DC-SBM as a special case, which in turn contains SBM, it is unclear why PFM and SBM are treated differently. Given that spectral clustering performs well across these models, why not use a single estimator? Specifically, why is the Laplacian used for PFM but the adjacency matrix for SBM? Moreover, the PFM guarantees are stated in terms of weighted ME, whereas for SBM they are in terms of ME. How should these be compared? Additionally, why are there no results for DC-SBM?

3) In Proposition 10, what is the definition of <M', M>_F?

4) In Assumption 5, is the intended quantity ||L_hat^2 − L^2||_F or ||L_hat − L||_F^2? If it is the former, what is the intuition for squaring the Laplacians?

5) The paper focuses primarily on disjoint clusters. Do the techniques/recipe extend beyond block models to other settings where spectral clustering is known to work? For example, spectral methods are effective for hierarchical clustering with existing guarantees (Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering, S. Balakrishnan et al., 2011). How would the template specialize to that case?

6) Both the evaluation metric and the estimators in the PFM and SBM cases assume that C and C' have the same number of clusters. Different clustering algorithms can return different numbers of clusters. For instance, eigenvalues of A might suggest K clusters for C, while the Laplacian suggests K' = K + 1 clusters for C', with C' identical to C except one cluster splits into two. C' is still close to C, but the current framework cannot capture this.

7) The related work section omits several model-free results, e.g., Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), Clustering under Perturbation Resilience (Balcan et al., 2012), and Clustering under Approximation Stability (Balcan et al., 2013). How does the stability notion here relate to these frameworks? In particular, how does it compare to stability measured under perturbations of centers across two clusterings?

8) The perturbations tolerated in the PFM simulations appear too small. Lines 252–253 state that “large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations,” but this is not properly qualified or explained. Conversely, the political blog data are smoothed by raising A to a power, which should further accentuate eigengaps, yet stability seems to improve—why? If such small perturbations materially affect model fit, the guarantees may have limited practical value (despite being touted as data-computable). Lines 248–250 also note that the SBM guarantee conditions seem very hard to satisfy, which is concerning.

9) The Figure 1 caption states, “For SBM, delta is always greater than delta0.” Which subplot does this refer to? If SBM is not plotted, the caption should make that explicit.

10) The PFM theorems provide guarantees for weighted ME, but the experiments perturb ME. Please reconcile this mismatch.
