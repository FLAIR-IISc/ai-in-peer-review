- Paper provides new distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering (no generative model assumptions).  
- Core idea: a clustering that fits the graph well implies any other equally fitting clustering is close to it.  
- Introduces a new framework to evaluate clustering quality and a new distance measure between clusterings.  
- Results are technically sound, well established, and illustrated with experiments.  
- Paper is well written and largely self-contained despite some material moved to the supplement.  
- Minor clarity concern: using "hats" on observed quantities (e.g., \hat{L}) is an odd choice.  
- Experimental finding: some real-world graphs require smoothing for structure to be recoverable under the framework, while classical algorithms can recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- Main limitation: PFM result relies on a nonstandard clustering distance that may be questionable as a replacement for classical measures.  
- Another limitation: analysis is based on reconstructing a proxy model from a clustering, which strongly constrains what counts as a good clustering (normalized Laplacian approximation).  
- Impact of these model/restriction choices on real-world applicability is unclear and may limit practical usefulness.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
