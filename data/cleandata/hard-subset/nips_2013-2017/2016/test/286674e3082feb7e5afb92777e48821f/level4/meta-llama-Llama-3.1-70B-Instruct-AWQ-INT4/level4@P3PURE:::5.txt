This paper provides very general concentration guarantees based on spectral clustering techniques for all types of community detection problems. The authors prove these guarantees for different generative models without relying on any of the parameters from those models. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area of study in an important direction.

One of the paper's key contributions is posing a very interesting question about the stability of solutions for the problem of community detection. The authors phrase and prove their results in great generality, while also focusing and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. The paper is well-written, making it an easy and enjoyable read. The authors provide clear explanations for the motivation behind their definitions of error and the assumptions required for their proofs.

The only notable criticism of this paper is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms and results that are not tight and can be intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems, particularly when dealing with very sparse graphs. The authors themselves acknowledge this limitation in their conclusion. Nevertheless, this work represents a great starting point for the field of stability results for community detection, and it is likely to be built upon in the near future.
