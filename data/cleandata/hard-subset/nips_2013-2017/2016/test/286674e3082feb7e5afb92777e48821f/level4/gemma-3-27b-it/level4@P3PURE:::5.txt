This paper provides general concentration guarantees for spectral clustering techniques applicable to a wide range of community detection problems. Notably, the authors prove these guarantees across different generative models without relying on knowledge of specific model parameters. This work is interesting, relevant, and represents a significant advance in a popular and important area of study. The paper poses a compelling question regarding the stability of solutions for community detection and addresses it with results of great generality, while also focusing on applications to specific, well-studied graphical models. Furthermore, the paper is exceptionally well-written and a pleasure to read; the authors clearly explain the motivation behind their choices for error definitions and assumptions underpinning their proofs. 

My primary criticism, acknowledged by the authors in their conclusion, concerns the reliance on spectral algorithms and bounds that are known to be loose and computationally intractable for many sparse graph settings of significant interest. However, this work constitutes a valuable starting point for the development of stability results in community detection, and I anticipate and hope to see further progress in this direction.
