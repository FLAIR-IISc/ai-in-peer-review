The paper establishes relationships among different clustering distance measures in network settings. Focusing on the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and the Stochastic Block Model (SBM), it proves a locally convex-like property for clustering: any two clusterings that fit the network data equally well (i.e., estimate the model parameters to a similar degree) must be close in terms of misclassification distance. In particular, the paper states and proves an equivalence, or near-equivalence, between several distance measures for clusterings in network data.

Both theoretical analysis and empirical experiments support the claims. While the results are novel, there are recent works of a similar flavor in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT 2015) and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (arXiv 2015). Moreover, some of the theoretical arguments appear to be relatively straightforward extensions of well-known results. As noted by the authors, the framework could have applications to detecting clusterability, but no formal results on that front are provided. The paper is well written and conveys the central ideas clearly.
