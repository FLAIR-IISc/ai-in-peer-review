- Introduces a model-free, data-dependent template for clustering stability with computable guarantees and demonstrates it on PFM and SBM to derive exact recovery results.

- Strength: Ambitious and valuable goal to provide theoretical guarantees based only on data and output, not on unverifiable generative assumptions.

- Inconsistent treatment of related models: PFM and SBM analyzed with different estimators (Laplacian vs adjacency) and different error measures (weighted ME vs standard ME) with no clear justification; DC-SBM omitted.

- Unconvincing empirical results: PFM guarantees hold only for tiny perturbations; apparent eigengap contradictions (manipulations that increase eigengap seem to help stability) are unexplained; SBM conditions admitted to be “very hard to satisfy.”

- Limited scope: framework compares clusterings only with the same K and is only demonstrated on disjoint block models, leaving extension to K±1, hierarchical, or overlapping clusters unclear.

- Insufficient comparison to related work: lacks discussion of model-free/perturbation-based stability notions (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010, Balcan et al. 2012/2013) and how the proposed measure relates to existing perturbation/stability definitions.

- Clarification needed: define the inner product notation <M',M>_F in Proposition 10.

- Clarification needed: specify whether Assumption 5 uses ||L_hat^2 - L^2||_F or ||L_hat - L||_F^2 and justify squaring Laplacians if applicable.

- Clarification needed: fix Figure 1 caption and clarify which subplot refers to “For SBM, delta is always greater than delta0,” since SBM results aren’t clearly plotted.

- Clarification needed: reconcile theory (weighted ME for PFM) with experiments (standard ME).

- Minor: Figure 1 missing axis labels.

- Minor: Clarify that A_ij for i>j are conditionally independent given cluster assignments in the block model description.

- Minor: Notation inconsistently redefined across sections (B, C, Y, lambda); use distinct, consistent symbols to improve readability.
