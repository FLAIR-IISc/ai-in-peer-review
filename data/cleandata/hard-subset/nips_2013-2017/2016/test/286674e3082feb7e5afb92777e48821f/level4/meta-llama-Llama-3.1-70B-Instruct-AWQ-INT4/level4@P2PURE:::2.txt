The paper establishes a relationship between different distances in clustering, focusing on network models such as the Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM). It proves a locally convex-like property for clustering, demonstrating that any two clusterings that estimate the model parameters equally well are also close in terms of mis-classification distance measures. This property provides valuable insight into the behavior of clusterings in network data. The paper formally states and proves the equivalence between different distance measures for clusterings in network data, supported by both theoretical and experimental validations. While the results are novel, similar work has recently appeared in the network literature, such as Peng, Sun, and Zanetti (COLT, 2015), and Chao, Lu, Ma, and Zhou (Arxiv, 2015). The theoretical contributions, although interesting, are relatively straightforward extensions of well-known results. Although the authors mention potential applications in detecting clusterability, they do not provide formal results in this regard. Nevertheless, the paper is well-written, effectively conveying the core ideas in a clear and lucid manner.
