This paper proposes a novel framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a model, providing theoretical guarantees for the results of model-based clustering algorithms. The authors introduce a generic theorem that ties in with the existing model-based framework and allows for the exploitation of results in model-based recovery. They instantiate this theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), obtaining model-free guarantees for these models.

The paper is well-organized, and the main ideas are clearly presented. The authors provide a thorough background on graphs, clusterings, and block models, which is helpful for understanding the context of the work. The main theorem, along with its instantiations for PFM and SBM, is well-explained and supported by propositions and proofs.

The strengths of the paper include its novelty in providing model-free guarantees for clustering algorithms, its thorough analysis of the PFM and SBM models, and its potential to advance the state of the art in graph clustering. The authors also provide experimental evaluation on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and the assumptions made. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the relationship between the model-based and model-free approaches, and how their work contributes to the broader field of graph clustering.

Furthermore, the paper could be improved by providing more context and comparisons to existing work. While the authors mention some related work, a more comprehensive discussion of the state of the art in graph clustering and how their work differs from or builds upon existing approaches would be helpful.

In terms of clarity, the paper is generally well-written, but there are some areas where the language could be improved for better readability. Some of the paragraphs are quite long and dense, making it difficult to follow the authors' arguments. Breaking up these paragraphs and using more headings and subheadings could improve the overall flow of the paper.

Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of graph clustering. With some revisions to address the areas mentioned above, it has the potential to be even stronger.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a model.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the PFM and SBM models and obtain model-free guarantees for these models.
* The paper has the potential to advance the state of the art in graph clustering.

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the limitations of the proposed framework and the assumptions made.
* The authors could provide more insights into the relationship between the model-based and model-free approaches.
* The paper could be improved by providing more context and comparisons to existing work.
