The paper proposes a novel framework for graph clustering in a model-free setting, which is an interesting and challenging problem. However, upon close examination, the results appear to be somewhat naive and unconvincing.

The authors' Generic Theorem (Theorem 1) claims that if a clustering C fits the graph G well, then any other clustering C' that also fits G well will be close to C. However, this statement is quite vague, and the authors do not provide a clear definition of what it means for a clustering to "fit" a graph well. Moreover, the theorem relies on unspecified "technical conditions" that are not explicitly stated.

Similarly, Proposition 3 (Informal) claims that the authors' PFM estimator is consistent under standard recovery conditions. However, the authors do not provide a clear definition of what these conditions are, nor do they offer any rigorous proof of consistency.

The rest of the paper is similarly plagued by vague statements and lack of rigor. The authors propose various instantiations of their Generic Theorem for specific models, such as the PFM and SBM. However, these results are not convincingly proven, and the authors rely heavily on informal arguments and handwaving.
