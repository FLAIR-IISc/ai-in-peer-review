This paper aims to develop a model-free theoretical framework for graph clustering. Unfortunately, the results come across as naive and unconvincing. The authors claim to construct a new theory, but much of the argumentation reads as hand-waving rather than rigorous development.

To give just a couple of examples of the vagueness: Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) includes the phrase “which also fits G well is close to C,” and Proposition 3 (Informal) invokes “under standard recovery conditions.” Such statements rely on undefined or imprecise notions (“fits G well,” “standard recovery conditions”), making the claims difficult to interpret, assess, or verify. Without clear definitions and explicit assumptions, it is not possible to evaluate the strength, novelty, or correctness of the results.

In short, while a model-free theory for graph clustering would be valuable, the current presentation lacks the precision and rigor needed to substantiate the claimed contributions.
