- Establishes relationships between different distance metrics used to evaluate clusterings.  
- Focuses on network models (PFM and SBM) and proves a local-convexity-like property for clustering solutions.  
- Proves that any two clusterings that fit network data equally well (in parameter estimation) are close under various misclassification-based metrics.  
- Clearly states and proves the equivalence between multiple clustering distance measures.  
- Supports theoretical findings with experimental validation and empirical results.  
- Results are novel but build upon recent related work (e.g., Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015).  
- Theoretical contributions are logical and relatively straightforward extensions of established results.  
- Suggested applications for detecting clusterability are mentioned but lack formal results.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and lucid explanations.
