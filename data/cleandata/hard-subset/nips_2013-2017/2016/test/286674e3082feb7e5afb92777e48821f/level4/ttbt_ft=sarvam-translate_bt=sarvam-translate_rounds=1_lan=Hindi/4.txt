This paper presents a general template for theoretical guarantees for the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well. Stability is measured in terms of the distance between C and any other C that fits the same data well. The paper also provides a specific example of using the template for two block models, PFM and SBM, and the calculation of all quantities involved in the guarantee can be clearly done from the data. Furthermore, it also uses the stability results obtained in this way to show accurate recovery guarantees for the model. However, simulations indicate that either the goodness fit used or the conditions of the theorems provided do not make the results very useful. 1) The paper considers the problem of providing a general template for theoretical guarantees for clustering, regardless of whether the data belongs to a specific model. The goal is to obtain guarantees that can be computed from quantities and compared to the output of the clustering algorithm. Providing such a model-free theoretical guarantee for clustering is important for both theoretical and practical purposes. 2) Given that DC-SBM is included as a special class in PFM that includes SBM, it is not clear why two cases (PFM and SBM) are considered separately. Given that spectral clustering works well for all specified models, why not use the same model estimator? In particular, it is not clear why the Laplacian is used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM. Furthermore, the results for PFM are for weighted ME, while for SBM, it is in terms of ME. How do they compare with each other? Also, why are the results not given for DC-SBM? 3) In proposal 10, what is the definition of < M', M >F? 4) In assumption 5: Is it ||L^{hat}^2 - L^2||F or ||L^{hat} - L||F^2? If the former, what is the intuition behind squaring the Laplacian? 5) The paper mainly focused on uncoupled groups. Are the techniques/recipes used in this paper extended from the block model to other models for which spectral clustering works? For example, spectral clustering is known to work well for hierarchical clustering and theoretical guarantees are available ("Spectral Clustering for Noise Limits", S. Balakrishnan et al. 2011). How will the template work for this case? 6) The evaluation metric that is assumed in the cases of both PFM and SBM is limited to C and C' in which the number of clusters is the same. Different clustering algorithms can potentially give different numbers of clusters. For example, suppose the eigenvalues of A suggest K clusters for C and the eigenvalues of A's Laplacian suggest K' = K + 1 clusters for C' as if all the clusters in C and C' are the same except for one cluster that splits into two. C' is still "close" to C, but it cannot be captured by the given framework. 7) The related work section does not reflect the many model-free results that exist: For example: "Spectral Norms and Clustering with k-Means Algorithms", Kumar and Kannan, 2010. "Clustering Under Decomposition Resilience", Balkan et al. 2012. "Clustering Under Estimation Stability", Balkan et al. 2013. How does the notion of stability considered in this work relate to these works? In particular, how does stability in this paper relate to the stability measured for the decomposition of centers for two clusterings? 8) The decomposition tolerated by the simulation for PFM is very small. Line 252-253 carelessly states "Large eigenvalues separate PFM(G, C) from PFM(G, C0), even for very small decomposition" without actually qualifying the statement. It is not clear why this is. On the other hand, the political blog data is smoothed by increasing the power of A which will further increase the gap in the eigenvalue, but in this case it seems to help stability? Furthermore, if such small decomposition can affect the model fit, it does not make the guarantee very useful for practical purposes (which is one of the main selling points because the quantities can actually be computed from the data). Lines 248-250 state that SBM guarantees seem very difficult to satisfy, which is unfortunate. 9) Figure 1 is mentioned in the caption "For SBM, \delta is always greater than \delta0". Which one is it referring to in the subplot? If SBM is not plotted, it should be clarified in the caption. 10) In the theorems for PFM, the guarantees are for weighted ME, while in practice, decomposition is done for ME. Minor details: 1) Labels for the axes in Figure 1 are missing. 2) (Line 44) For the block model, A{ij} are independent for i > j and cluster assignments are given for i and j. 3) B, C, and Y are used for PFM with different definitions and later for SBM in 3.2. This can be confusing in general. Given that there are many notations in the paper, it is better to maintain consistency throughout. For example, Y{\orthogonal} and CD can be used for PFM instead of B and C respectively. Furthermore, for the estimator of SBM, YZ is not required. It is better to use the expression of YZ directly in proposal 8. Similarly, for \lambda in PFM and SBM, using \lamda^L and \lambda^B will clearly distinguish which eigenvalues are being talked about. 4) 4.4 is better suited to be a subdivision in the related work.
