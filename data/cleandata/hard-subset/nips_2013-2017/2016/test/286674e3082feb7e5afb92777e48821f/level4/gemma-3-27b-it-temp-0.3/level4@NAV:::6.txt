This paper attempts to develop a theoretical foundation for model-free graph clustering. However, the presented results are ultimately unconvincing and lack theoretical rigor. While the authors aim to establish a novel theory, the approach feels overly simplistic and relies on imprecise reasoning. Specific instances highlight this concern; for example, Theorem 1 (Generic Theorem) includes the vague condition “which also fits G well is close to C,” and Proposition 3 (Informal) invokes undefined “standard recovery conditions.”
