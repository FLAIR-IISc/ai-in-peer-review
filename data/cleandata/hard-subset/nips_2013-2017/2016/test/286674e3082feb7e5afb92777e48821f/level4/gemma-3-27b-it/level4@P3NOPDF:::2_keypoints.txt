- Establishes relationships between different clustering distance metrics for network data (PFM and SBM).
- Proves a locally convex-like property: clusterings that fit data equally well are close under misclassification-based distances.
- Demonstrates an equivalence between likelihood/parameter-fit and misclassification distance measures.
- Supports theoretical findings with experiments and illustrative examples.
- Core result (consistency of clustering distance measures) is novel and insightful.
- Theoretical contributions build on existing results and are incremental rather than revolutionary.
- Related work (Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015) addresses similar themes and should be more thoroughly discussed.
- Potential application to detecting “clusterability” is noted but not developed or formalized.
- Paper is well-written with clear, accessible presentation of arguments.
- Experimental support lends credibility to the theoretical claims.
- Weakness: limited exploration or concrete methodology for using results in clusterability detection.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand related-work discussion, add a preliminary exploration/discussion of clusterability applications, and acknowledge the incremental nature of the theoretical contribution.
