- Studies relationships among clustering distance measures in network models (PFM and SBM).  
- Proves a local convexity–type property: equally good parameter fits imply clusterings are close under misclassification distances.  
- Shows equivalence (up to constants) among different clustering distance measures for these network models.  
- Provides both theoretical proofs and empirical experiments supporting the claims.  
- Exposition is clear and the combination of theory and experiments is convincing.  
- Results appear new but are conceptually related to prior work (e.g., Peng et al. COLT 2015; Chao et al. arXiv 2015).  
- Some theoretical arguments seem like straightforward extensions of known techniques.  
- Mentions applications to detecting clusterability but provides no formal statements or guarantees.  
- Recommendation: clarify distinctions from related work and add formal results on clusterability detection to strengthen the contribution.
