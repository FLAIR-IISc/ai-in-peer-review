This paper proposes a novel framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a model, instead providing guarantees based on the statistics of the data. The authors instantiate this framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), and show that their results tie in with existing model-based frameworks.

The main idea is to define a goodness of fit measure between a model and the data, and then show that if two clusterings fit the data well, they must be close to each other. The authors provide a generic theorem that captures this idea, and then instantiate it for SBM and PFM.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the algorithms and theorems. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and potential extensions of their results.

One of the strengths of the paper is that it provides a unified framework for clustering graphs, which can be applied to different models. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the algorithms and theorems, making it easy for readers to understand the main ideas.

However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the practical implications of the results. For example, how do the guarantees provided by the theorems translate to real-world clustering tasks? Are there any specific applications where the proposed framework is particularly useful?

Additionally, the paper could benefit from more comparisons with existing clustering algorithms. While the authors mention that their results can be used to provide model-free guarantees for clustering, it would be helpful to see more explicit comparisons with other algorithms.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and interesting framework for clustering graphs, and provides a thorough and well-written explanation of the algorithms and theorems. With some additional discussion on practical implications and comparisons with existing algorithms, the paper could be even stronger.
