This paper provides very general concentration guarantees based on spectral clustering techniques for all types of community detection problems. The authors prove these guarantees for different generative models without relying on any of the parameters from those models, making their results widely applicable. The work is interesting, relevant, and advances a popular area of study in an important direction.

The paper poses a very interesting question about the stability of solutions for the problem of community detection. The authors phrase and prove their results in great generality, while also focusing and applying them to specific popular graphical models of interest. The paper is well-written, making it an easy and enjoyable read. The authors provide clear explanations for the motivation behind their choices of error definitions and assumptions required for their proofs, helping readers understand the reasoning behind their approach.

The only significant criticism of the paper is that the proofs depend on spectral algorithms and results that are not tight and can be almost intractable for many of the most interesting community detection problems, particularly when dealing with very sparse graphs. However, the authors themselves acknowledge this limitation in their conclusion. Despite this, the paper makes a great start in the field of stability results for community detection, and it is expected that this line of research will be progressed further in the near future.
