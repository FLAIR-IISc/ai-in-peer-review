1) Proposes a model-agnostic framework giving data-computable theoretical stability guarantees for clusterings — a valuable contribution.

2) Unclear why PFM and SBM are treated differently (Laplacian for PFM vs adjacency for SBM), why PFM uses weighted ME vs SBM standard ME, and why DC-SBM is omitted despite PFM encompassing it.

3) Proposition 10: the definition/meaning of the inner product <M', M>_F is unclear.

4) Assumption 5: ambiguity whether the norm is ||L̂^2 - L^2||_F or ||L̂ - L||_F^2; if the former, justify squaring the Laplacians.

5) Scope question: framework focuses on disjoint clusters — does it extend to other settings where spectral clustering succeeds (e.g., hierarchical clustering)?

6) Limitation: evaluation assumes C and C' have the same number of clusters, so the framework cannot handle differing K or refinement relations between clusterings.

7) Related work gap: inadequate citation/discussion of model-free stability results (Kumar & Kannan 2010; Balcan et al. 2012, 2013) and unclear relation to cluster-center perturbation stability notions.

8) Simulation concerns: PFM perturbations are very small, contradictory/equivocal effects of eigengaps on stability, and difficulty satisfying SBM conditions call into question practical usefulness of the guarantees.

9) Figure 1 caption: statement “δ is always greater than δ0” is ambiguous about which subplot it refers to and should state if SBM is not plotted.

10) Inconsistency: theoretical guarantees target weighted ME for PFM while experiments perturb standard ME.

Minor 1) Figure 1 lacks axes labels.

Minor 2) Clarify line 44: for block models A_ij (i>j) are independent conditional on cluster assignments of i and j.

Minor 3) Notation confusion: B, C, Y are defined differently for PFM and SBM — suggest clearer, consistent symbols (e.g., Y_⊥, C_D, λ^L, λ^B) and simplify SBM estimator by eliminating unnecessary YZ.

Minor 4) Section 4.4 belongs in the related work section.
