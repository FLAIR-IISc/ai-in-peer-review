- Novel distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering that do not assume a specific generative model.  
- Central idea: if a clustering fits the graph well (under the authors’ fit definition), any other clustering with comparable fit must be close.  
- Addresses an important problem; the theoretical findings are interesting and advance the topic.  
- Results are well explained and well illustrated.  
- Paper remains readable despite some content moved to the supplementary material.  
- Minor exposition gripe: using “hats” for observed quantities (e.g., \hat{L}) is not ideal.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world datasets require graph smoothing for structure to become recoverable, whereas many classical algorithms recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- Major limitation 1: main PFM result relies on a nonstandard distance between clusterings, whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Major limitation 2: results use a proxy model reconstructed from a clustering (e.g., a PFM) and compare it to the observed graph, which constrains what counts as a good clustering (normalized Laplacian approximation).  
- While such restrictions may be necessary for theoretical guarantees, their practical impact is questionable given the framework’s limitations on some real-world examples.
