Summary and contributions
The paper proposes a generic template for theoretical guarantees on the stability of a clustering C that fits the data well, where stability is defined as the distance between C and any other C' that also fits the data well. The template is instantiated for two block models—PFM and SBM—with all quantities in the guarantees computable directly from the data. The stability results are further used to derive exact recovery guarantees. While the direction is important and the computability of the criteria is appealing, the empirical results and the conditions required by the theorems suggest that the guarantees may be of limited practical utility.

Major comments and questions
1) Scope and importance of model-free guarantees
- The paper tackles model-free theoretical guarantees for clustering, aiming for conditions and quantities that are data-driven and depend only on the outputs of clustering algorithms under comparison. This is both theoretically and practically significant.

2) Modeling choices and estimator inconsistency across cases
- Since PFM includes DC-SBM as a special case, and DC-SBM in turn includes SBM, it is unclear why the PFM and SBM cases are treated differently. If Spectral Clustering works across these models, why not use a unified estimator?
- Specifically, why is the Laplacian used for PFM while the adjacency matrix is used for SBM?
- The guarantees are stated in terms of weighted misclassification error (ME) for PFM and standard ME for SBM. How do these compare, and what is their relationship in practice?
- Given that DC-SBM is subsumed by PFM, why are there no explicit results for DC-SBM?

3) Proposition 10
- What is the precise definition of ⟨M', M⟩_F? Please specify whether this is the Frobenius inner product and how it is used in the proof.

4) Assumption 5
- Is the assumption ||L̂^2 − L^2||_F or ||L̂ − L||_F^2? If it is the former, please provide intuition for squaring the Laplacians and explain why this is the right quantity for the analysis.

5) Beyond disjoint block models
- The techniques focus on disjoint clusters within block models. Do the template and recipe extend to other models where Spectral Clustering is known to work, such as hierarchical clustering? For instance, there are theoretical guarantees in “Noise Thresholds for Spectral Clustering” (S. Balakrishnan et al., 2011). How would your template instantiate and specialize to this case?

6) Fixed number of clusters
- Both the evaluation metric and the estimators for PFM and SBM assume C and C' have the same number of clusters. In practice, different algorithms may return different numbers of clusters. For example, eigenvalues of A might suggest K clusters for C, whereas those of the Laplacian suggest K' = K + 1 for C', where C' differs from C only by splitting one cluster into two. Such C' is arguably “close” to C, but your framework cannot capture this. Can the framework be extended to allow comparisons across different K?

7) Related work and notion of stability
- The related work omits several model-free results, e.g., “Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm” (Kumar and Kannan, 2010), “Clustering under Perturbation Resilience” (Balcan et al., 2012), and “Clustering under Approximation Stability” (Balcan et al., 2013). Please clarify how your notion of stability relates to these frameworks. In particular, how does your stability compare to stability under perturbations of centers or inputs used in those lines of work?

8) Empirical evaluation and interpretability of conditions
- The perturbations tolerated by the PFM simulations appear extremely small. Lines 252–253 state that “large eigengap makes PFM(G, C) differ from PFM(G, C0) even for very small perturbations,” but this is not qualified or explained. Why does a large eigengap have this effect under your goodness-of-fit measure?
- In contrast, for the political blogs data, smoothing by taking a power of A should further enlarge eigenvalue gaps, yet this seems to help stability. Please reconcile these observations.
- If such small perturbations substantially affect the model fit, the practical usefulness of the guarantees is limited—even though a key selling point is that the quantities are computable from data.
- Lines 248–250 indicate that the SBM guarantee conditions are very hard to satisfy, which is unfortunate. Please discuss how often these conditions are met in realistic regimes, and whether the template can be relaxed or adapted.

9) Figure 1 caption
- The caption states, “For SBM, δ is always greater than δ0.” Which subplot shows this? If SBM is not plotted, the caption should say so explicitly.

10) Metric mismatch between theory and experiments
- The PFM theorems use weighted ME, whereas the experiments perturb ME. Please align the metrics or justify the mismatch and discuss implications for interpreting the results.

Minor points
1) Figure 1 is missing axis labels.

2) Line 44: For block models, A_{ij} for i > j are independent given the cluster assignments for i and j.

3) Notation consistency: B, C, and Y are used with different meanings for PFM and again in Section 3.2 for SBM, which is confusing given the heavy notation load. Consider maintaining consistent symbols throughout. For example, use Y^⊥ and C_D instead of B and C for PFM. For the SBM estimator, YZ is not required; use the expression for YZ directly in Proposition 8. Similarly, distinguish eigenvalues in PFM and SBM as λ^L and λ^B, respectively.

4) Section 4.4 reads more like related work and would be better positioned as a subsection in the Related Work section.

Overall assessment
- The paper tackles an important problem and offers a promising, computable template with applications to PFM and SBM and exact recovery results. However, the differing treatments of closely related models, unclear choices of estimators, lack of results for DC-SBM, metric inconsistencies, limited handling of differing numbers of clusters, missing connections to related model-free stability frameworks, and empirical issues (very small tolerated perturbations and hard-to-satisfy conditions) significantly limit clarity and practical impact in the current form. Addressing the conceptual and empirical concerns above would substantially strengthen the paper.
