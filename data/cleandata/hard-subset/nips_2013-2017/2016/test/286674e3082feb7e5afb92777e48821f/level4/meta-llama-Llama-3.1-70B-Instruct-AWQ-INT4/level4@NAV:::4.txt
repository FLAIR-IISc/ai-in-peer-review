The paper proposes a general framework for establishing theoretical guarantees on the stability of clustering algorithms, specifically focusing on the distance between two clusterings that fit the data well. This stability is quantified using a generic template that can be applied to various clustering models, including the popular PFM and SBM. Notably, the framework allows for the explicit computation of all quantities involved in the guarantees from the data. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate the applicability of their stability results to derive exact recovery guarantees for these models.

However, the simulation results suggest that either the goodness-of-fit measure employed or the conditions stipulated in the theorems may render the obtained guarantees less useful in practice.

Several aspects of the paper warrant further consideration:

1. The primary goal of the paper is to develop a model-free theoretical framework for clustering, which is crucial for both theoretical and practical purposes. However, it remains unclear why the authors treated PFM and SBM separately, given that PFM encompasses DC-SBM, which in turn includes SBM. Moreover, the choice of using Spectral Clustering for both models, but with different estimators (Laplacian for PFM and adjacency matrix for SBM), is not adequately justified.

2. The authors should clarify the definition of < M', M >F in Proposition 10. Additionally, in Assumption 5, it is essential to specify whether the norm is ||L^{hat}^2 - L^2||F or ||L^{hat} - L||F^2, and provide an intuitive explanation for squaring the Laplacians if the former is correct.

3. The paper primarily focuses on disjoint clusters, but it is unclear whether the proposed techniques can be extended to other models, such as hierarchical clustering, for which Spectral Clustering is known to be effective.

4. The evaluation metric and estimators used in the paper are limited to clusterings with the same number of clusters. This restriction may not capture scenarios where different clustering algorithms yield different numbers of clusters.

5. The related work section does not adequately acknowledge existing model-free results, such as those presented in "Clustering with Spectral Norm and the k-means Algorithm" and "Clustering under Perturbation Resilience." A more comprehensive discussion of how the notion of stability in this work relates to those in the existing literature is necessary.

6. The simulations for PFM appear to tolerate only small perturbations, which may not be practically useful. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation for this observation.

7. The caption for Figure 1 is unclear, and the labels for the axes are missing.

8. To improve readability, the authors should maintain consistency in notation throughout the paper, avoiding the reuse of symbols with different definitions.

9. Section 4.4 would be better suited as a subsection in the related work section.

10. Minor corrections, such as adding labels for axes in Figure 1 and clarifying the notation for block models, are also necessary.
