

# Review of "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results"

This paper proposes an interesting framework for providing theoretical guarantees for model-based clustering algorithms without assuming the data comes from a specific model. The main idea is that if a model fits the data well, then any other clustering that also fits the data well will be close to the original clustering. The authors instantiate this framework for PFM and SBM models.

## Major Concerns

The paper contains several technical inconsistencies that undermine its credibility. In Theorem 4, the authors define δ = (K-1)ε²/(|λ̂K| - |λ̂K+1|)² but then write the conclusion as "distd̂(C, C') ≤ maxk Ckk�k Ckk δ" - the notation "maxk Ckk�k Ckk" is completely unclear and appears to be a typo. This ambiguity makes it impossible to verify the claimed bound.

In Theorem 9, the authors define δ = 4Kε²/|λ̂AK|², but λ̂AK is never defined earlier in the paper. This appears to be a typo for λ̂K, the K-th eigenvalue, which would make the theorem consistent with standard spectral clustering literature.

The paper claims "the SBM is a subclass of the DC-SBM, and the latter a subclass of the PFM," but this is factually incorrect. While SBM is a special case of DC-SBM (when all node weights are equal), DC-SBM is not a subclass of PFM. The relationship between DC-SBM and PFM is more complex, and the paper fails to properly explain this hierarchy.

In the PFM Estimation Algorithm, the authors define YZ = D̂^(1/2)ZC^(-1/2) with C = Z^T D̂Z. However, C is a K×K matrix while Z is n×K, so C^(-1/2) is well-defined but the dimensions of the matrix multiplication don't align properly. This needs clarification to ensure the algorithm is correctly specified.

## Minor but Significant Issues

The paper contains several formatting and notation errors that suggest a lack of careful proofreading. For example, "At the central of intact are the so-called block-models" contains a clear typo. In Section 2, "In 1Rigorously speaking, the normalized graph Laplacian is I − L̂ [10]" has a misplaced "1" that should be removed.

The Misclassification Error (ME) distance is defined incorrectly in equation (2). The correct formula should sum over clusters k rather than nodes i: dist(C, C') = 1-1/n maxπ∈SK ∑k=1^K |k ∩ π(k)|. The current definition would evaluate to zero for any clustering, which is nonsensical.

The paper claims "The results of [13] is a local equivalence," but Theorem 9 of [13] is not cited in the paper, making it impossible to verify this claim. The reference should be properly included and explained.

## Other Concerns

The experimental section contains several unclear statements. The paper mentions "unweighted error" but never defines what this means. The claim "For �AT �A we find no guarantees" uses unclear notation (�AT likely should be A^T) and doesn't explain why no guarantees are found.

The paper states "The more recent results of [17] (with unspecified constants) in conjunction with our original Assumptions 3, 4, and the assumption that all clusters have equal sizes, give a bound of O(Kε²/λ̂²K) for the SBM." However, the authors don't provide a detailed comparison or show how their bound matches the model-based result.

## Strengths

The paper's main idea is theoretically interesting and potentially valuable for cluster validation. The framework could provide useful model-free guarantees for practitioners. The authors correctly identify that existing model-based results can be "turned around" to provide model-free guarantees.

## Conclusion

While the paper presents an interesting conceptual framework, it suffers from multiple technical errors, unclear notation, and poorly defined terms that make it difficult to verify the claimed results. The fundamental theorem statements contain critical ambiguities that prevent proper evaluation. The paper would benefit from significant revision to clarify these issues, correct the technical errors, and properly define all mathematical concepts.

The quality of the theoretical contribution is undermined by these inconsistencies, and the claims cannot be properly assessed without clarification. I recommend rejection due to these substantial technical flaws that prevent the reader from understanding and verifying the core results.
