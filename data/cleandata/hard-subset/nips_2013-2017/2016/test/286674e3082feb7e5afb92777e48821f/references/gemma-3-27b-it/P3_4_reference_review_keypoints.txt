- Proposes a model-free framework showing that any two clusterings that fit the data well must be close, providing meaningful structure recovery without assuming a specific block model.
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, yielding concrete bounds linking model-free and model-based recovery guarantees.
- Positions the work within block-model clustering literature and highlights the gap caused by strong model assumptions in prior results.
- Novelty: introduces an innovative model-free guarantee approach that leverages model-based analysis without requiring model fidelity.
- Generality: presents a “Generic Theorem” forming an abstract, widely applicable framework.
- Concrete results: provides computable guarantees for clustering stability useful to practitioners.
- Clear presentation: generally well-written with standard notation and clear definitions.
- Thorough experiments: empirical validation shows the theoretical bounds hold across datasets and representations.
- Connects to existing recovery theorems, repurposing them to derive model-free guarantees.
- Proofs: analysis appears rigorous and well-structured.
- Weakness: derived bounds (especially for SBM) are conservative and often not met in practice, indicating room to tighten analysis.
- Weakness: mathematical sections (PFM in particular) are dense and could be streamlined for readability.
- Weakness: lacks explicit discussion of computational cost for verifying goodness-of-fit (e.g., eigengap, SVD).
- Weakness: results depend on assumptions (like non-zero eigengap); sensitivity to these assumptions is not thoroughly explored.
- For acceptance: significant conceptual advance, strong theoretical foundation, practical relevance, and thorough evaluation support publication.
- Against acceptance: conservative bounds and dense presentation may limit practical utility and accessibility.
- Overall: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address bound tightness, computational costs, and assumption sensitivity.
- Requested minor revisions: discuss bound tightness and improvements, include computational complexity discussion, and clarify sensitivity to assumptions.
