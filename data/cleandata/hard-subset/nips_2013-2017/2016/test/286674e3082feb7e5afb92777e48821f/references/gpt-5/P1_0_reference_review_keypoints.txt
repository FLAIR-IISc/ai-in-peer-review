- Proposes a model-free validation framework: if a fitted model M(G,C) explains G well, any other clustering that also fits must be close to C, certifying uniqueness/stability without generative assumptions.  
- Instantiates framework for PFM using an estimator that aligns a constructed Laplacian L with the empirical spectral subspace and bounds clustering error via Davis–Kahan and Meilă’s local equivalence.  
- Instantiates framework for SBM using the MLE A = ZBZ^T and perturbation of the top-K singular subspace to derive unweighted misclassification bounds.  
- Shows how existing model-based recovery theorems can be “turned around” to give model-free guarantees, sometimes with improved constants.  
- Experiments on synthetic PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR, and political blogs show PFM certificates are computable and informative; SBM bounds are often too conservative.  
- Mathematical blueprint is sound: explicit estimator, computable ε and eigengap conditions, and conversion from subspace to clustering error.  
- PFM estimator is clever and implementable; key proofs (PFM validity, Proposition 3) are deferred to the supplement and should be restated inline.  
- SBM guarantee in operator norm is restrictive in sparse regimes and often yields δ > 1, limiting practical value.  
- Paper is generally well-organized but uses heavy notation and contains editorial issues and confusing Laplacian/normalized adjacency terminology.  
- Important details need clarification in the main text (PFM validity assumptions, whether A ∈ [0,1], how ε is quantified for differing fits).  
- The framing and alignment-based PFM estimator are novel; the idea of converting model-based results to agnostic guarantees is insightful.  
- Related work on stability/clusterability and graph model closeness is discussed and coverage is reasonable.  
- Framework is significant for practitioners as a computable certificate of clustering uniqueness among good-fitting models, especially for PFM/DC-SBM-like settings.  
- Current SBM result reduces significance; improving it via regularization, degree correction, or variance-aware norms would help.  
- Suggests clarifying Theorem statements (use ε = max{ε(C), ε(C′)}), discussing nonnegativity/boundedness of constructed A, and relaxing “probability” language if applying to similarity matrices.  
- Recommends providing a regularized/DC-SBM variant and bounds in terms of regularized norms to make SBM certificates practical on sparse graphs.  
- Asks for guidance on interpreting ε and proposes bootstrap or data-splitting to assess certificate robustness.  
- Requests at least one experiment where the SBM certificate succeeds, tighter exposition, moving key proof sketches into main text, and fixing typos.  
- Pros: novel model-free validation framework, solid perturbation-based proofs, practical PFM estimator, and informative PFM experiments.  
- Cons: conservative SBM instantiation, deferred technical details and notation issues, limited experiments and no comparisons to alternative validation methods.  
- Overall: promising and technically solid; recommend acceptance if authors clarify technical points, strengthen/regularize SBM results, and improve experiments and exposition.
