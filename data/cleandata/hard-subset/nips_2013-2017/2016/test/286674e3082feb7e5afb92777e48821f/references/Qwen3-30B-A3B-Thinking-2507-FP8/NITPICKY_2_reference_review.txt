

# Review of "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results"

This paper proposes a framework for providing theoretical guarantees for clustering algorithms without assuming the data comes from a specific model (SBM, DC-SBM, or PFM). The main idea is that if a clustering C fits the data well with respect to a model class M, then any other clustering C' that fits well must be close to C. The authors instantiate this framework for PFM and SBM, providing model-free guarantees.

## Major Concerns

The paper suffers from multiple technical inconsistencies and formatting errors that undermine its credibility. 

First, there is significant notational inconsistency. The paper defines the normalized Laplacian as L̂ = D̂⁻¹/²ÂD̂⁻¹/² in section 2, but then in a footnote states "the normalized graph Laplacian is I - L̂" without explaining why this is the case. This confusion persists throughout the paper, with inconsistent use of L̂ vs. L for the Laplacian matrix.

Second, the paper contains multiple mathematical errors. In Theorem 4, δ is defined as (K-1)ε²/(|λ̂K| - |λ̂K+1|)², but later in the proof (Proposition 5) the authors use ε/(|λ̂K| - |λ̂K+1|) without the square on ε. The authors also incorrectly define the Weighted ME distance in equation (3) with a missing denominator: "1− 1 �n i=1 d̂i" should be "1− 1 / (Σi=1^n d̂i)".

Third, the paper makes a misleading claim about the model-free nature of their results. The "model-free" guarantees still require assumptions about the Laplacian (Assumptions 1 and 2), which are not model-free but rather specific to the spectral properties of the data. This undermines their main contribution.

Fourth, the PFM Estimation Algorithm contains ambiguous notation. In step 1, they define "YZ = D̂ 1/2ZC−1/2" where C = ZTD̂Z, but C is a K×K matrix and C⁻¹/² is not well-defined for a general matrix. The paper should clarify whether they mean the matrix square root or the inverse of the diagonal elements.

Fifth, in Proposition 2, the authors claim "D̂ and L, or A define a PFM with degrees d̂1:n" but the PFM requires D = diag(A1), which is not verified in the construction. This is a significant technical oversight.

## Minor but Significant Issues

The paper contains several other minor but problematic issues:

1. Theorem 9 defines "λ̂AK as the K-th singular value of Â" when the paper has consistently used eigenvalues (not singular values) throughout. This is confusing and technically incorrect for the adjacency matrix.

2. The paper claims "the SBM is a subclass of the DC-SBM, and the latter a subclass of the PFM" but provides no justification or reference for this hierarchy, which is not standard in the literature.

3. In the experimental section, the authors state "the clustering C is ensured to be stable by Theorem 4 as the unweighted error grows to a breaking point, then the assumptions of the theorem fail." This is logically inconsistent - the assumptions should fail before the error reaches the breaking point, not after.

4. The paper uses inconsistent notation for the indicator matrix Z (sometimes using Z for the n×K matrix, sometimes for the K×n matrix).

5. The paper claims "all quantities in our theorems are computable from the data" but fails to specify how to compute the eigengap |λ̂K| - |λ̂K+1| in practice, which is a critical component of their bounds.

## Strengths and Overall Assessment

The paper presents an interesting conceptual framework that could be valuable for model-free validation in clustering. The connection between model-based and model-free guarantees is novel, and the technical approach using perturbation bounds is different from previous work.

However, the numerous technical inconsistencies, mathematical errors, and notational problems significantly undermine the paper's credibility. As a perfectionist reviewer, I cannot recommend acceptance because the paper fails to present a technically sound argument. The errors are not merely typographical but represent fundamental misunderstandings of the spectral properties and model definitions they are working with.

The paper's main contribution is overshadowed by these technical flaws. For instance, the claimed model-free guarantees depend on assumptions (like Assumptions 1 and 2) that are not truly model-free, and the mathematical derivations contain errors that would invalidate the main theorems.

I believe the paper would benefit from a thorough technical revision that addresses these issues, but as it stands, it does not meet NIPS's standards for technical rigor. The errors are too numerous and substantive to be considered merely "minor" by a perfectionist reviewer.
