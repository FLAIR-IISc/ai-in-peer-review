- Paper proposes a novel model-free framework showing that any clustering that fits the data well must be a small perturbation of another good fit, instantiating this for PFM and SBM.
- Major novelty: provides theoretical clustering guarantees without assuming a specific block model, addressing a key limitation of prior work.
- Framework is general and potentially applicable to various clustering algorithms and goodness-of-fit measures.
- Theoretical development is rigorous, leveraging perturbation bounds (Davis–Kahan) and well-defined clustering distances (ME, weighted ME).
- The paper explicitly connects model-free results to established model-based recovery theorems and shows how to improve existing bounds.
- Experiments provide some empirical support that the theoretical guarantees can hold in practice and illustrate applicability.
- Theorems still rely on assumptions (non-zero eigengap, specific goodness-of-fit conditions), so claims are not fully assumption-free.
- Derived bounds, especially for SBM, appear conservative; experimental conditions for guarantees are often unmet (e.g., δ > 1).
- Mathematical exposition can be dense and difficult to follow, particularly in the PFM sections; notation and definitions need clarification.
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope; more datasets and comparisons to model-based bounds are needed.
- Overall technically sound and original, representing a valuable proof-of-concept rather than a ready-to-use method.
- Potentially significant impact if bounds can be tightened and assumptions better contextualized for real data.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: investigate and tighten conservative bounds (SBM), clarify practical implications of assumptions, expand experimental evaluation and comparisons, and improve clarity and notation.
