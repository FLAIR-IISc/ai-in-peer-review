- Proposes a model-free stability framework: if a clustering fits the data well, any other good fit must be close, generalizing perturbation bounds.  
- Theorems are rigorous, leveraging Davis–Kahan and spectral graph theory, and instantiated for PFM and SBM.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show the framework is practically applicable and conditions can be computed from data.  
- Framework appears more readily applicable to PFM than to SBM; SBM stability conditions are stricter.  
- Novel contribution: bridges model-based theoretical guarantees and practical, model-free clustering validation.  
- Technical soundness: proofs are careful, though full verification requires deep review of supplementary material.  
- Generality: Theorem 1 is presented in a general form and successfully specialized to specific block models.  
- Clear definitions provided for goodness-of-fit and clustering distance metrics (ME and weighted ME).  
- Theoretical results are complex and rely on assumptions (eigengap, goodness-of-fit), limiting practitioner accessibility.  
- Bounds seem somewhat conservative—tight but potentially pessimistic; there may be room to relax them.  
- Experimental validation is limited in scope; more datasets, algorithms, and complex graph structures should be tested.  
- Computational cost of constructing PFM/SBM estimators and practical overhead are not discussed.  
- Open questions: robustness to eigengap violations, justification for weighted ME distance choice, and details of how principal subspace was perturbed in experiments.  
- Suggested future work: extend framework to Frobenius-norm perturbations and provide more intuitive/simplified conditions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking authors to simplify theory, broaden experiments, and clarify practical implications.
