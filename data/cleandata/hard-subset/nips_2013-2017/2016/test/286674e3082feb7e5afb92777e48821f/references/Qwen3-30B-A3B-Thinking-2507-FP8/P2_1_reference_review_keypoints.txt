- Introduces a novel framework that gives model-free theoretical guarantees for graph clustering by linking goodness-of-fit of a model to clustering stability.
- Formalizes a "generic theorem" and instantiates it for Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Shows that if a model-based algorithm fits the observed graph well, any other good-fitting clustering must be close to the original clustering.
- Technically rigorous: formal proofs for all main results and elegant use of tools like the Davis-Kahan sin-theta theorem.
- Assumptions (e.g., eigengap conditions) are clearly stated and bounds are interpreted carefully.
- Experiments on synthetic and real data validate that computed bounds correspond to observed clustering stability under perturbation.
- Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation; connection between model-based and model-free results is clearly articulated.
- Could use more intuitive explanation of technical concepts such as the weighted ME distance.
- Original contribution: novel notion of model-free guarantees that repurposes model-based recovery theorems for broader validation.
- Distinguishes itself from prior work that either assumes models or uses different clustering quality measures.
- Significant practical impact: allows validation of clustering without strong data model assumptions and applies beyond spectral clustering.
- Opens new research directions (e.g., tighter SBM bounds, non-spectral recovery thresholds).
- Strengths: novel framework, rigorous theory, practical experiments, clear presentation, and re-use of existing recovery results.
- Weaknesses: limited intuition for weighted ME distance, SBM bounds are much weaker than PFM, and links to practical non-spectral algorithms could be expanded.
- Recommendation: strong accept for NeurIPS — high quality, original, significant, with acknowledged limitations that can be addressed in future work.
