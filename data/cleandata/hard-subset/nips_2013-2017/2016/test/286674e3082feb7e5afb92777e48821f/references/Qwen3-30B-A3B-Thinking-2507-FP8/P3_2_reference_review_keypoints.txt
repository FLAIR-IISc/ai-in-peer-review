- Summary: Proposes a framework that gives model-free theoretical guarantees by showing any clustering that fits an observed graph under a model M must be close to a given clustering C; formalizes a Generic Theorem and instantiates it for PFM and SBM.

- Strength: Conceptual contribution bridging model-based recovery theorems and model-free validation, addressing a practical gap in network clustering.

- Strength: Strong technical execution using Davis–Kahan perturbation analysis and a justified weighted misclassification error for heterogeneous degrees.

- Strength: Experiments on simulated and real data validate the bounds and show PFM yields less restrictive guarantees than SBM.

- Weakness: Assumes clustering C (and K) is given; does not address how to select K or handle multiple good clusterings.

- Weakness: Bounds depend on ε but the paper gives no practical guidance for choosing ε; small perturbations can break guarantees.

- Weakness: Limited experiments — only spectral clustering and few datasets; lacks comparison to standard validation metrics (e.g., modularity, silhouette).

- Weakness: Theoretical treatment notes SBM bounds are more restrictive than PFM but does not analyze why or pursue tighter SBM bounds.

- Weakness: Focuses on positive guarantees without providing methods to detect when a clustering is unstable or assumptions fail.

- Suggestion: Add practical guidelines and empirical analysis for choosing ε and K across parameter ranges.

- Suggestion: Expand experiments to compare stability guarantees with standard validation metrics and more clustering algorithms.

- Suggestion: Test limits by considering data from models outside PFM/SBM (e.g., mixed-membership) to show framework boundaries.

- Suggestion: Include discussion and analysis of computational complexity for applying the bounds at scale.

- Significance: Novel and potentially influential approach that converts model-based recovery results into model-free validation tools for network clustering.

- Contribution: Advances understanding of clustering validation by providing a principled, computable route to certify cluster stability without strong generative assumptions.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address practical implementation challenges (choosing K and ε) and sensitivity issues for practitioner adoption.
