- Introduces a model-free framework: a Generic Theorem showing that if clustering C fits graph G well under a model, any other well-fitting clustering C' must be close to C.  
- Instantiates the theorem for PFM and SBM, turning model-based recovery theorems into model-free guarantees.  
- Achieves a technical improvement: PFM bound improved by a factor of 128 over prior work.  
- Technically rigorous with clear definitions, careful proofs, and a detailed supplement.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; consistent notation and accessible to those familiar with spectral clustering and block models.  
- Conceptually original in explicitly linking model-based recovery results to model-free clustering guarantees and showing model hierarchy (SBM ⊂ DC-SBM ⊂ PFM).  
- Significant practical implications for validating clustering without assuming a generative model; validated on synthetic and one real dataset (Political Blogs).  
- Limited experimental scope: primarily synthetic experiments and only a single real-world network.  
- SBM instantiation is weak/restrictive (Theorem 9 yields δ values > 1) and lacks explanation or remedies.  
- Reliance on eigengap assumptions (e.g., |λ̂K| > |λ̂K+1|) may be restrictive in real networks with small eigengaps.  
- Comparison with prior cluster stability work could be stronger—needs clearer distinction from related results (e.g., [16]).  
- Recommendation: strong accept—novel, sound, and significant despite noted limitations; lists concrete arguments for and against acceptance.
