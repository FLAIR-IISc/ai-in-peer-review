- Presents a novel model-free framework guaranteeing clustering quality without assuming a data-generating model.  
- Core idea: if a clustering fits data well, any other well-fitting clustering must be close to it.  
- Instantiates framework for SBM and PFM, proving model-free guarantees and linking model-based and model-free recovery.  
- Novelty/significance: removes strong model assumptions and broadens applicability of theoretical guarantees.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous theorems and structured proofs using standard perturbation tools (e.g., Davis–Kahan).  
- Generic Theorem offers a blueprint for extending the framework to other models and algorithms.  
- Practical considerations: quantities for verifying guarantees are computable; experiments show framework applicability.  
- Related work: comprehensive literature review situating the contribution within spectral clustering and stability analysis.  
- Conservatism of bounds: theoretical guarantees (especially for SBM) are overly conservative, limiting immediate practicality.  
- Complexity of assumptions: framework requires complex conditions (eigengap, goodness-of-fit) that may be hard to verify.  
- Clarity issues: some sections—particularly model estimation algorithms and the jump from Proposition 3 to Assumption 2—need clearer exposition.  
- Limited experiments: empirical evaluation is preliminary and lacks diverse datasets and comparisons to other methods.  
- Review scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 3.5/5, Originality 4.5/5, Significance 4/5.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, focusing on tightening bounds, expanding experiments, and improving exposition.
