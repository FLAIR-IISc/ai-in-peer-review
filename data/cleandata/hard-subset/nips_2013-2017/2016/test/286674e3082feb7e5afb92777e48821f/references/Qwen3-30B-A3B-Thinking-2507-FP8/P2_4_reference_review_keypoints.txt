- Proposes a novel model-free framework: if a fitted model explains the graph well, any other well-fitting clustering must be a small perturbation of it.
- Core theoretical contribution: a Generic Theorem instantiated for PFM and SBM giving model-free guarantees derived from model-based recovery results.
- Technically rigorous with clear, detailed proofs and careful distinction between model-based and model-free perspectives.
- Uses direct perturbation bounds (Davis–Kahan) rather than counting arguments, simplifying and clarifying proofs.
- Experimental validation on synthetic (PFM, perturbed PFM, LFR) and real (Political Blogs) datasets supports the theoretical claims.
- Empirical results show the theoretical bounds correctly predict clustering stability and the noise level at which clusterings break.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with consistent notation and clear definitions (goodness-of-fit, clustering distance).
- Justifies use of the weighted ME distance when node degrees differ, improving applicability.
- Original and novel: first to explicitly convert model-based recovery theorems into model-free clustering guarantees.
- Significant potential impact by enabling clustering validation without assuming a specific generative model.
- Demonstrates practical relevance (e.g., graph smoothing increases stability) but immediate practitioner utility is limited without more implementation details.
- Limitations: SBM-instantiated results are more restrictive/useful than PFM, which may limit applicability.
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity for large-scale graphs.
- Weakness: does not address how to select the best clustering among multiple candidate clusterings.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to discuss computational complexity and methods for selecting among multiple clusterings.
