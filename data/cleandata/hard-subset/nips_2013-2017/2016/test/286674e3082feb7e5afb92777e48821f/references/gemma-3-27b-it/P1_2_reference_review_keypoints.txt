- Novelty: Proposes a "goodness of fit" model-free framework for graph clustering that avoids strong data-generating assumptions.  
- Theoretical contributions: Presents rigorous theorems (Theorem 4, 9) proving conditions for clustering stability.  
- Instantiation: Framework is instantiated for PFM and SBM, showing how model-free guarantees follow from model-based results.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on spectral clustering and block-model literature while departing from assuming data is generated by a specific block model.  
- Connection to existing results: Demonstrates leveraging existing recovery theorems to obtain model-free guarantees.  
- Clear definitions: Provides precise definitions for goodness of fit, clustering distance (ME, wME), and model construction.  
- Experimental validation: Experiments provide empirical support and show bounds can be computationally checked, though scope is limited.  
- Discussion: Contains a detailed discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Weakness — conservative bounds: Derived bounds (especially for SBM) appear conservative and may limit practical applicability.  
- Weakness — residual assumptions: Theorems still rely on conditions like non-zero eigengaps and specific goodness-of-fit measures, introducing some model dependence.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: Experimental evaluation is not extensive and comparisons to other stability results are superficial.  
- Weakness — presentation: Paper is notation-dense and supplemental proofs need better organization and more explanatory text.  
- Pro-acceptance: Offers a novel, theoretically sound framework that bridges model-based and model-free approaches and can stimulate further research.  
- Con-acceptance: Conservatism of bounds, lingering model dependence, and limited empirical validation temper immediate impact.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions to tighten bounds and expand experiments.  
- Recommendation & confidence: Recommend Accept; reviewer confidence 8/10.
