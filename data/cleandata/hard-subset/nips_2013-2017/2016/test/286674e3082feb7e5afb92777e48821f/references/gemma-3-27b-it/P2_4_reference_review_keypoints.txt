- Proposes a model-free framework showing that any clustering that fits the data well must be close to any other well-fitting clustering.  
- Leverages existing model-based recovery results (SBM, DC-SBM, PFM) to obtain model-free guarantees.  
- Instantiates the framework for Preference Frame Models (PFM) and Stochastic Block Models (SBM), showing concrete connections.  
- Emphasizes computability: required parameters for guarantees can be estimated from the data.  
- Theorems and definitions (clustering distance, goodness-of-fit, model construction) are precise and technically sound.  
- Proofs appear rigorous, though many details are relegated to the supplement.  
- Experimental results validate applicability and illustrate limitations, including comparison of PFM and SBM bounds.  
- Novel and significant idea: turning model-based recovery theorems into model-free guarantees.  
- Presentation is dense with heavy notation; clearer, more intuitive exposition is needed.  
- Scope of instantiations is limited (primarily PFM and SBM); broader model coverage would strengthen the work.  
- Derived bounds can be conservative—particularly for SBM—limiting practical impact.  
- Key assumptions (e.g., eigengap) are not thoroughly analyzed for failure scenarios.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison to existing stability results and clearer articulation of advantages.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clarity, discussion of limitations/assumptions, tighter bounds, and better related-work comparison.
