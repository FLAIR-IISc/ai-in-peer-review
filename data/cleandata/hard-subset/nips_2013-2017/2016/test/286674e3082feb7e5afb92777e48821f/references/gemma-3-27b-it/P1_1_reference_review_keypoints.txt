- Proposes a model-free framework: if a clustering fits the data well then any other well-fitting clustering must be close, instantiated for PFM and SBM with experiments supporting theory.  
- Builds on spectral/block-model literature but moves beyond generative-model assumptions and links model-based recovery theorems to model-free guarantees.  
- Novel framework broadens applicability of theoretical guarantees for graph clustering.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with clear definitions of “fitting” and “closeness.”  
- Shows how model-based results can be leveraged to obtain model-free guarantees, creating a valuable connection between approaches.  
- Key verification quantities (ε, δ, δ0) are computable from data, suggesting practical tractability.  
- Provides initial experimental validation on LFR, PFM, and political blog datasets.  
- Theorems still depend on restrictive assumptions (eigengap conditions, goodness-of-fit, unique PFM eigenvectors) that may limit applicability.  
- Some bounds (notably SBM Theorem 9) are loose and may be impractical; limited discussion on improving tightness.  
- Perturbation analysis is relegated to the supplement, reducing clarity; main text needs concise explanation of key bounds (e.g., Sinus theorem).  
- Experimental scope is limited: few datasets, parameter settings, no extensive comparisons or sensitivity analyses.  
- The weighted ME (wME) distance is proposed but not convincingly demonstrated superior to standard ME in all cases.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important open problem, is novel and mathematically sound, and links model-based and model-free results with encouraging experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: loose theoretical bounds, restrictive assumptions, proofs hidden in supplement, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Overall: significant and original contribution with promising model-free guarantees but limited immediate practical impact due to bounds and assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required minor revisions: discuss assumptions and limitations more thoroughly; explore tightening SBM bounds if possible; clarify key perturbation analysis in main text; expand experimental evaluation, include comparisons, sensitivity analysis, and adversarial stability tests.
