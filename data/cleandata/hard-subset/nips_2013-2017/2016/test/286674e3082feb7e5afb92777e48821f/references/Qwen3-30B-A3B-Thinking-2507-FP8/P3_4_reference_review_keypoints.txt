- Summary: Proposes a model-free framework showing that any clustering that fits the data well must be a small perturbation of a model-based clustering; instantiated for PFM and SBM.

- Strength: Theoretical novelty — introduces a conceptually elegant model-free perspective for clustering validation.

- Strength: Technical soundness — rigorous proofs using perturbation theory (Davis–Kahan) and correct instantiation of the framework.

- Strength: Clear connections — relates to and improves existing model-based recovery results (e.g., tightens a bound from [20]).

- Strength: Computable guarantees — all theorem quantities (ε, δ, δ₀) are computable from data and clustering.

- Strength: Experimental validation — simulations and real datasets (LFR, Political Blogs) demonstrate practical relevance and when bounds fail.

- Weakness: SBM impracticality — Theorem 9 yields δ values >1, making SBM results too restrictive for practical use.

- Weakness: Limited scope — framework hinges on spectral methods (Laplacian/eigendecomposition), excluding many clustering algorithms.

- Weakness: Eigengap reliance — heavy dependence on a nonzero K-th eigengap, which may fail in real-world graphs.

- Weakness: Lack of real-world use case — experiments don't show a concrete workflow for using the framework to guide real clustering decisions.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison — paper does not adequately compare to existing validation methods (silhouette, modularity, stability/bootstrap).

- Suggestion: Explain why SBM bounds are so restrictive and whether alternative goodness-of-fit measures could improve them.

- Suggestion: Discuss potential extensions to non-spectral clustering methods, even if only as future work.

- Suggestion: Include comparisons with standard validation metrics to better position the contribution.

- Suggestion: Address practical implications of the eigengap assumption and provide heuristics for when to trust the bounds.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with sound technical work; PFM results promising but SBM limitations hamper practical impact.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
