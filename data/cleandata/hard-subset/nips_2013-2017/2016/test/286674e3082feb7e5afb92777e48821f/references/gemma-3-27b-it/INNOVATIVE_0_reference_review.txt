This paper presents a fascinating and broadly applicable framework for establishing guarantees for graph clustering *without* relying on specific model assumptions. This is a bold move, as the field has been heavily focused on model-based approaches (SBM, DC-SBM, PFM). The core idea – proving stability of a clustering based on goodness-of-fit rather than generative model adherence – is genuinely novel and has the potential to unlock a more practical understanding of clustering algorithms. The authors demonstrate this framework by successfully deriving model-free guarantees for both SBM and PFM, and importantly, connect these to existing model-based results. 

The paper is well-structured, and the mathematical presentation is generally clear, though dense. The theorems are stated precisely, and the connection between the generic theorem and the instantiations for PFM and SBM is logical. The inclusion of alternative results leveraging existing recovery theorems (e.g., from [20] and [18]) is a strength, demonstrating the versatility of the proposed framework.  The experimental evaluation, while not extensive, provides initial evidence supporting the theoretical findings. The comparison of δ values between SBM and PFM is insightful, highlighting the greater difficulty of establishing guarantees under the SBM framework.

However, the paper is not without its weaknesses. While the theoretical framework is elegant, the bounds derived (particularly for the SBM) appear quite restrictive. The authors acknowledge this, but it remains a significant limitation. The assumptions required for the theorems to hold, while technically sound, could be difficult to verify in practice. The experimental section feels somewhat cursory. While it demonstrates the applicability of the framework, more comprehensive evaluation on diverse datasets (including those where the model assumptions are clearly violated) would strengthen the claims. The connections to related work, while present, could be more thoroughly explored. For example, linking the stability results to robustness guarantees in machine learning could be fruitful.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** The model-free approach to clustering guarantees is genuinely novel and addresses a key limitation of existing research.
* **Significance:**  A general framework for validating clustering results without model assumptions has the potential to significantly impact the field.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the connection to existing recovery theorems is well-established.
* **Bold Approach:** The authors tackle a challenging problem with a creative and ambitious solution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* **Restrictive Bounds:** The derived bounds, particularly for SBM, are likely to be too restrictive to be practically useful in many scenarios.
* **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental section is somewhat weak and does not fully demonstrate the framework's applicability to real-world datasets.
* **Assumptions:** The assumptions required for the theorems to hold may be difficult to verify in practice.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the limitations, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The novelty and potential impact of the proposed framework outweigh the current weaknesses. The authors have taken a bold and innovative approach to a fundamental problem in graph clustering. While the current bounds may be restrictive, the framework itself provides a valuable foundation for future research. I believe the ideas presented in this paper will stimulate further investigation and ultimately lead to a more robust and practical understanding of graph clustering. The paper prioritizes a significant new direction over methodological perfection, which aligns with my reviewing preferences. Minor revisions addressing the experimental evaluation and a more thorough discussion of the assumptions would further strengthen the paper.
