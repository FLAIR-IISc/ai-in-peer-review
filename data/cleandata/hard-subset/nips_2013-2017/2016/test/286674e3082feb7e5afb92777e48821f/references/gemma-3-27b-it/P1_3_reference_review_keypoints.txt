- Introduces a model-free stability framework: if clustering C fits graph G well under model M, any other clustering C' that also fits G well must be close to C, instantiated for SBM and PFM.  
- Leverages and connects to prior block-model recovery and recent stability-based clustering work to repurpose existing theorems for model-free guarantees.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: provides a principled way to reason about clustering quality without strong generative-model assumptions.  
- Theoretical rigor: formal theorems and detailed proofs using spectral graph theory and perturbation analysis.  
- Effectively links new framework to established block-model results (Section 4.2) to obtain model-free corollaries.  
- Practical relevance: key quantities (ε, δ, δ0) are computable from data and experiments illustrate applicability.  
- Uses well-defined metrics (ME and wME) and provides a clear justification for preferring wME.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, aiding reader comprehension.  
- Bounds are conservative, particularly for SBM, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from more datasets, parameter sweeps, and comparisons to other model-free methods.  
- Proofs are lengthy and technically demanding, making core ideas less accessible to non-experts.  
- Limited development of potential extensions (e.g., Frobenius norm, other perturbations) that could broaden impact.  
- Results depend on a non-zero eigengap; behavior when the eigengap is small or zero is not well addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, rigorous analysis, bridges model-based and model-free approaches, computable guarantees, and strong potential for future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: conservative bounds, limited experiments, and accessibility concerns due to proof complexity.  
- Overall assessment: strong and original work whose strengths outweigh weaknesses and is a worthwhile NIPS contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
