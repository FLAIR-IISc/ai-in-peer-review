- Paper proposes a model-free framework showing that any two clusterings that fit the data well must be close, instantiated for PFM and SBM.  
- Novelty: introduces a model-free guarantee approach that departs from standard model-based clustering theory.  
- Generality: presents a “Generic Theorem” unifying model-based and model-free results.  
- Technical soundness: proofs are rigorous, leveraging spectral techniques and Davis–Kahan perturbation.  
- Presentation: generally clear and well-structured with intuitive motivation.  
- Experiments: brief empirical validation supporting theoretical claims and comparisons to existing bounds.  
- Assumptions: results still rely on conditions like eigengap and goodness-of-fit, limiting full model-free claims.  
- Conservatism: theoretical bounds (especially for SBM) are conservative and may lack practical tightness.  
- Experimental scope: experiments are limited (mostly synthetic PFM and LFR); more realistic datasets needed.  
- Proof complexity: lengthy proofs placed in supplement can hinder reader accessibility; some key steps should be highlighted in the main text.  
- Related work comparison: related work coverage is reasonable but lacks deeper discussion of practical implications versus algorithmic methods.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and complete, with appropriate caveats acknowledged.  
- Clarity assessment: well-written overall but proofs’ complexity could impede understanding.  
- Originality assessment: genuinely novel model-free approach that meaningfully combines existing techniques.  
- Significance assessment: potentially high impact for model-free community detection and future research.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, rigorous, clear presentation addressing an important problem.  
- Con acceptance: conservative bounds, restrictive assumptions, limited experiments, and complex proofs.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify assumptions, explore tighter bounds, expand experiments, and discuss sensitivity to assumption violations.
