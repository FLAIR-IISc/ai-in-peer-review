- Proposes a novel model-free framework for graph clustering that provides guarantees without assuming data comes from a specific block model.  
- Core idea: guarantees are based on how well a clustering fits the data rather than assuming the data is generated by a block model.  
- Theorem 1 provides an elegant, general statement of the framework.  
- Instantiates the framework for PFM and SBM, connecting model-free and model-based results.  
- Theoretical development appears rigorous and leverages existing results (e.g., from [20]) to strengthen guarantees.  
- Experimental validation supports the theory but is relatively limited in scope.  
- Well-motivated discussion highlighting limitations of model-based approaches and potential future work.  
- Practical relevance: framework could yield more robust clustering methods less sensitive to model misspecification.  
- Notation inconsistency (use of Â and A) can be confusing and should be fixed.  
- Computational concern: computing verification quantities (ε, δ, δ0) may be non-trivial for large graphs and cost is not analyzed.  
- Conservatism of bounds, especially for SBM, reduces practical applicability; SBM guarantees are often not met in experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation should be expanded to include broader datasets and comparisons with existing clustering algorithms.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses fundamental limitation of model assumptions, principled framework, strong theory and encouraging experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: practical applicability limited by computational cost and conservative bounds; experiments not comprehensive.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address complexity, bound tightness, experiments, and notation.
