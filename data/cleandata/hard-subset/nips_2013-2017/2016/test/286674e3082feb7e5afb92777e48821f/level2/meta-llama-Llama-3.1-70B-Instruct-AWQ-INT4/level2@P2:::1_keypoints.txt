- Proposes a novel, model-free framework for graph clustering that focuses on checkable data statistics rather than assumed generative models.  
- Provides model-free guarantees for clustering correctness without relying on strong model assumptions.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Demonstrates that the framework can strengthen existing model-based recovery theorems.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis with clear explanations of framework, models, and algorithms.  
- Well-written and easy-to-follow presentation.  
- Experimental evaluation is clear and well-described.  
- Experiments are limited to a small number of datasets, restricting empirical validation.  
- Scope is limited to graph clustering; applicability to other clustering problems is not shown.  
- The framework and derivations are complex and may be difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Overall originality and potential significance are high for graph clustering and related theory.  
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept.  
- Suggested improvements: apply the framework to other clustering settings, broaden experimental evaluation, and simplify technical presentation for accessibility.
