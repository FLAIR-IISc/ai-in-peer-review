- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives correctness guarantees checkable from data.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a generic theorem showing if clustering C fits graph G w.r.t. model M, any other clustering C' that also fits G well is close to C.  
- Instantiates the theorem for Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Connects the model-free framework with existing model-based recovery results and can exploit them.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations; technical proofs moved to the supplement for readability.  
- Provides experimental results demonstrating effectiveness of the approach.  
- Strength: novel model-free guarantee for clustering without assuming a data-generating model.  
- Strength: generic theorem is a significant theoretical contribution to clustering/community detection.  
- Weakness: assumes graph is represented as an adjacency matrix, which may not suit all graph types.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear prescription for choosing the model M and the goodness-of-fit measure.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparison with other clustering algorithms to benchmark effectiveness.  
- Originality: introduces a novel, model-agnostic clustering framework and theorem.  
- Significance: potential to impact graph clustering and community detection broadly via model-free guarantees.  
- Quality and clarity: well-organized, accessible exposition; experiments support claims.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; score 9/10.  
- Requested revisions: clarify how to choose model M and goodness-of-fit, and add comparisons to other clustering methods.
