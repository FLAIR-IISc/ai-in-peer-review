**Paper Review: Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a specific model. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the results of model-based clustering algorithms, focusing on the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM). They introduce a new approach to prove model-free guarantees for these models, which ties in with existing model-based frameworks. The paper also presents experimental results on various datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. A framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a specific model.
2. Theoretical guarantees for the results of model-based clustering algorithms, focusing on SBM and PFM.
3. Model-free guarantees for SBM and PFM, which can be used in conjunction with existing model-based recovery guarantees.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide a detailed theoretical analysis, including proofs and propositions, to support their claims. They also present experimental results on various datasets, which demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful, as they provide a new approach to clustering graphs without assuming a specific model. The authors also show how their framework can be used in conjunction with existing model-based recovery guarantees, which makes it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of graph clustering and model-based clustering. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature and provide proper citations.

**Novelty**

The work presents a significant improvement over existing approaches, as it provides a new framework for clustering graphs without assuming a specific model. The authors also introduce a new approach to prove model-free guarantees for SBM and PFM.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including a clear description of the algorithms and theoretical analysis.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the assumption of a specific model class (SBM and PFM) and the requirement of a large eigengap.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism is that the paper focuses on a specific model class (SBM and PFM), which may not be representative of all graph clustering problems. However, the authors do provide a clear explanation of how their framework can be extended to other model classes.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper presents a new and innovative approach to graph clustering.
* The authors provide a clear and detailed theoretical analysis.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

Con:

* The paper focuses on a specific model class (SBM and PFM), which may not be representative of all graph clustering problems.
* The requirement of a large eigengap may limit the applicability of the approach.

**Overall Assessment**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and detailed theoretical analysis. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of graph clustering. However, the focus on a specific model class (SBM and PFM) and the requirement of a large eigengap may limit the applicability of the approach.
