- Proposes a model-free clustering framework with theoretical guarantees based on data goodness-of-fit rather than assuming a specific block model.
- Instantiates the framework for both PFM and SBM and shows connections between model-free and model-based recovery results.
- Theorems (notably Theorems 4 and 9) are technically solid and use appropriate perturbation bounds.
- The approach is novel and addresses a major limitation of standard block-model-based guarantees.
- The framework is presented generally (Theorem 1) and demonstrates broad applicability by instantiation.
- The paper is well-written with clear, consistent notation and logical organization.
- Derived bounds are often very conservative in practice, especially for SBM, and may be less competitive than existing bounds (e.g., δRCY).
- Key assumptions (e.g., non-zero eigengap) may not hold in real networks and their practical implications are under-discussed.
- Computing the quantities required for the guarantees may be computationally expensive; this cost is not analyzed.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and cursory, focusing on perturbing clusterings rather than demonstrating usefulness on diverse real-world datasets.
- The connection to stability-based clustering results is mentioned but not fully explored or compared empirically.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, general, theoretically sound, connects to existing work, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: overly conservative bounds, limited experiments, and potentially unrealistic assumptions limiting practical impact.
- Overall assessment: a solid, novel contribution with practical limitations; reviewer leans toward accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested improvements: tighten bounds, expand empirical evaluation and baselines, discuss assumptions and computational cost, explore stability connections, and clarify practical scenarios of usefulness.
