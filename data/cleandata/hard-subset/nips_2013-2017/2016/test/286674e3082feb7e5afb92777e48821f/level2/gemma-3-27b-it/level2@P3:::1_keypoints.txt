- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives theoretical guarantees without assuming data from a specific block model.  
- Core idea: stability — any clustering that fits the data well must be close to another well-fitting clustering.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Establishes connections between model-free guarantees and existing model-based recovery results.  
- Novelty: expands applicability of clustering guarantees beyond model-generated data.  
- Theorems are mathematically well-formulated and appear rigorously proven.  
- Framework is general and applicable across different block models.  
- Bounds (ε, δ, etc.) are computable, giving potential practical relevance.  
- Linking model-free and model-based results provides a unified theoretical perspective.  
- Reliance on assumptions like non-zero eigengap and specific eigenvalue relationships may limit real-world applicability.  
- Derived stability conditions and bounds appear conservative, especially for SBM, reducing practical utility.  
- Experimental validation is limited in scope and parameter variety, offering weak empirical support.  
- Some proofs are dense and hard to follow, needing clearer intermediate steps and explanations.  
- Comparison to existing stability results is brief and not sufficiently comprehensive.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem with a novel, theoretically sound approach and clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: restrictive assumptions, limited empirical evidence, and complex proofs that hinder verification.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss assumption implications for real graphs; expand experiments across graph types/parameters; simplify/add steps in proofs; provide comprehensive comparison to prior stability work; explore tightening bounds (especially for SBM).
