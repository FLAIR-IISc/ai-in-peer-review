- Proposes a novel model-free clustering framework that provides theoretical guarantees based on goodness-of-fit to observed graph data rather than assuming a data-generating model.  
- Demonstrates the framework by deriving model-free guarantees for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Shows connections between model-free and model-based results, in some cases strengthening existing bounds.  
- Theorems (notably Thm. 4 and Thm. 9) are clearly stated and build on spectral graph theory and perturbation bounds.  
- Technically sound overall, addressing a major limitation of prior work that assumes model adherence.  
- Explicitly computes all parameters used in the theorems, avoiding hidden constants and enhancing practical applicability.  
- Experimental section provides initial validation and highlights differing stability profiles of SBM vs. PFM under perturbations.  
- Comparison to some existing bounds (e.g., δRCY) is uninformative and lacks compelling empirical support.  
- Clarity issues: mathematical notation and proof flow are sometimes abrupt and would benefit from more detailed derivations.  
- Experimental validation is limited in scope (few graph structures and parameter settings).  
- Paper engages related work well and offers a promising direction for future research.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve readability and expand experimental evaluation.
