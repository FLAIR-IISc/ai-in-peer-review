**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a framework for clustering graphs without assuming that the data comes from a model. The framework provides "correctness" guarantees based on the statistics of the data that can be checked. The authors show that this framework ties in with the existing model-based framework and can exploit results in model-based recovery. The paper focuses on the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM) and provides model-free guarantees for these models.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. The paper proposes a novel framework for clustering graphs without assuming a model, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide rigorous mathematical proofs for their claims, which adds to the credibility of the results.
3. The paper shows that the proposed framework can be used to obtain model-free guarantees for existing models, such as SBM and PFM.
4. The authors provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. The paper assumes that the graph is represented by an adjacency matrix, which may not be the case in all scenarios.
2. The authors do not provide a clear comparison with existing methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the performance of their approach.
