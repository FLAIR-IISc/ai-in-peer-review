- Proposes a model-free framework for clustering graphs that does not assume a specific generative model (e.g., SBM).
- Provides "correctness" guarantees that depend on data statistics and can be empirically checked.
- Framework connects to model-based approaches and can leverage model-based recovery results to strengthen those guarantees.
- Main theoretical contributions: a generic theorem for obtaining model-free clustering guarantees and instantiations for SBM and PFM.
- Empirical contributions: experiments on simulated and real-world networks demonstrating the framework’s effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, organized technical sections, and detailed proofs.
- Experiments are thorough and support the proposed method’s performance.
- Strength: novel model-free clustering framework addressing a gap in the literature.
- Strength: theoretical guarantees for correctness.
- Strength: practical validation across multiple datasets.
- Weakness: assumption that data can be represented as a graph may not always hold.
- Weakness: reliance on spectral clustering algorithms, which may not be optimal in all settings.
- Suggested improvement: provide a more detailed discussion of limitations and directions for future work.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
