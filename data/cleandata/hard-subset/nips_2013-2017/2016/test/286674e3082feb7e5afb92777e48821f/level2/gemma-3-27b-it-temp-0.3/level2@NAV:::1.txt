## Review of "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results"

This paper presents a novel framework for graph clustering that aims to provide theoretical guarantees without assuming the data originates from a specific block model (SBM, DC-SBM, PFM). The core idea is to establish stability: if a clustering fits the data well, any other clustering that also fits well must be a small perturbation of the original. The authors instantiate this framework for PFM and SBM, demonstrating connections between model-free and model-based recovery results, and even strengthening existing model-based bounds.

The paper’s strength lies in its attempt to bridge the gap between theoretical guarantees reliant on model assumptions and the practical reality of applying clustering algorithms to real-world graphs where model validity is uncertain. The “generic theorem” provides a clear blueprint for establishing these guarantees, and the application to PFM and SBM demonstrates its feasibility. The connection drawn between model-free and model-based results is insightful, potentially allowing leveraging of existing theory. The experimental validation, while limited, provides some initial support for the framework’s applicability.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. While the theorems are stated clearly, the technical details within the proofs (relegated to the supplement) are likely to be challenging for many readers. The assumptions required for the theorems to hold, particularly concerning eigengaps and goodness-of-fit measures, may be difficult to verify in practice. The experimental section is somewhat limited in scope and doesn't provide a comprehensive evaluation of the framework’s performance across diverse datasets. The comparison to existing stability results (e.g., [16]) feels cursory, and a more thorough benchmark would strengthen the contribution. The discussion of the limitations and potential extensions, while present, could be more detailed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel framework for model-free clustering guarantees.
*   Clear theoretical results and connection to existing model-based theory.
*   Potential to improve the practical applicability of theoretical clustering results.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Technical complexity and potentially restrictive assumptions.
*   Limited experimental evaluation.
*   Lack of a comprehensive comparison to existing stability results.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of graph clustering. While the technical details and assumptions require careful consideration, the proposed framework offers a promising direction for developing more robust and reliable clustering algorithms. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the assumptions and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation. The authors should also consider expanding the discussion of related work, particularly concerning stability results.
