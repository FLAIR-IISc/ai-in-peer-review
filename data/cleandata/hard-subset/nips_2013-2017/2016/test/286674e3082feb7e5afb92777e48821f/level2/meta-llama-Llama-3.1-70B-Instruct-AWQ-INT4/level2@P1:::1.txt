The paper "Graph Clustering: Block-models and model free results" proposes a framework for clustering graphs without assuming the data comes from a model. The authors obtain "correctness" guarantees that depend on the statistics of the data, which can be checked. They also show that this framework ties in with the existing model-based framework and can exploit results in model-based recovery.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a generic theorem that provides a model-free guarantee for clustering. The theorem states that if a clustering C fits the data G well with respect to a model M, then any other clustering C' that also fits G well is close to C. The authors instantiate this theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their framework and theorems. The technical proofs are relegated to the supplement, which makes the main paper more readable. The authors also provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
