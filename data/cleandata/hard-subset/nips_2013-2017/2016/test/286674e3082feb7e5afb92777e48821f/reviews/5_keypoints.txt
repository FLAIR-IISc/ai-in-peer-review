- Provides general concentration guarantees for community detection based on spectral clustering techniques.  
- Proves guarantees across different generative models without relying on model parameters.  
- Addresses an interesting and relevant question about the stability of community detection solutions.  
- Results are stated and proved in great generality and applied to specific popular graphical models.  
- Paper is well written, clear, and enjoyable to read.  
- Authors clearly explain motivations for their error definitions and assumptions.  
- Main criticism: proofs rely on spectral algorithms/results that are not tight and can be nearly intractable for very sparse graphs.  
- Overall a strong start in stability results for community detection with expected further progress.
