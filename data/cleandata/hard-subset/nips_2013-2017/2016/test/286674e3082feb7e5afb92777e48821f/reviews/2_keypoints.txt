- Establishes relationships between different clustering distance measures for network data.
- Analyzes two network models: Preference Frame Model (PFM) and Stochastic Block Model (SBM).
- Proves a locally convex–like property for clustering under these models.
- Shows that any two clusterings that fit model parameters equally well are close in misclassification distance.
- Demonstrates equivalence between multiple clustering distance measures for networks.
- Supports claims with both theoretical proofs and experimental validation.
- Results are novel but related to recent works (e.g., Peng et al. 2015; Chao et al. 2015).
- Theoretical arguments are relatively simple extensions of known results.
- Notes potential applications (e.g., detecting clusterability) but lacks formal results in that direction.
- Paper is well-written and explains core ideas clearly.
