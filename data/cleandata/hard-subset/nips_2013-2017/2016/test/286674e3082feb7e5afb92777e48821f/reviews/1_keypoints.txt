- Paper proposes distribution-free theoretical results for graph clustering, not assuming a generative model for the observed graph.  
- Core idea: if a clustering fits the graph well (by the paper's definition), any other clustering that fits equally well must be close to it.  
- The problem addressed is important and the theoretical contributions are novel and interesting.  
- The paper is mostly clear and readable despite some content moved to the supplement.  
- Minor stylistic concern: using "hats" on observed quantities (e.g., Ĥ for observed Laplacian) is questionable.  
- Results are well commented and illustrated empirically.  
- Empirical limitation: some real-world graphs must be smoothed for structure to be recoverable under the proposed framework, whereas classical algorithms can recover ground truth without smoothing.  
- Major limitation 1: the main result for PFM relies on a non-standard distance between clusterings, whose naturalness is debatable.  
- Major limitation 2: the analysis uses a proxy-model approach (reconstructing a model from a clustering and comparing it to the observed graph), which strongly constrains what counts as a “good” clustering (focused on normalized Laplacian approximation).  
- These modeling restrictions are necessary for results but may limit applicability and impact on real-world examples.
