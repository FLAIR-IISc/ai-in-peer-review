- Proposes LAND: a parametric Riemannian normal distribution metric linking metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Presents an MLE algorithm to estimate mean and covariance, learning a Mahalanobis distance on manifolds via geodesics and local metric inverses.  
- Uses inverses of per-point (diagonal) local covariances to define the local metric; also considers mixtures of LANDs.  
- Provides a useful parametric, generative model that can extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Primary concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because a diagonal covariance and its inverse are computed per point.  
- Lacks thorough complexity analysis and clarity on what counts as “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 5, 10, or 100 dimensions) and how dimensionality impacts performance.  
- Needs stronger positioning in metric-learning literature—related methods (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA) also learn per-point diagonal metrics and should be discussed.  
- Sensitivity to the Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is underexplored; robustness tests and density-adaptive choices of σ are missing.  
- Main-paper visual comparisons use GMM (a weak baseline); intrinsic-estimator visualizations (in the supplement and superior to GMM) should be moved into the main paper or shown alongside GMM.  
- Should include a computational complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to inform method choice for different data types.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are one of the most promising directions for real-world, multi-component data and warrant further exploration.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to read.
