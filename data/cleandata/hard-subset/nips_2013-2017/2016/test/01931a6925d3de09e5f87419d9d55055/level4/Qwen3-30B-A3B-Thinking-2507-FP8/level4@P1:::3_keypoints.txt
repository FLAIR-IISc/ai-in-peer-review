- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), generalizing multivariate normal to a Riemannian manifold via a locally adaptive metric.  
- Constructs a locally adaptive Riemannian metric (Eq. 7) based on local data density.  
- Introduces a Monte Carlo approach to estimate the normalization constant (Eq. 9–10).  
- Provides a maximum likelihood estimation algorithm for LAND parameters (Algorithm 1) and extends to mixture models.  
- Frames LAND as the maximum-entropy distribution under the learned metric, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Demonstrates improved modeling of nonlinear structures on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification compared to standard Gaussian models.  
- Uses tangent-space integration for normalization, which is clever and computationally efficient.  
- Metric in Eq. 7 is hand-crafted with fixed hyperparameters (ρ, σ); paper lacks discussion on choosing or learning σ and the effect of varying it.  
- Metric lacks invariance to coordinate transformations; authors should discuss whether a full (non-diagonal) metric would address this and provide more intuition.  
- Experimental comparison with GMMs is unbalanced (LAND allowed fewer components); recommend using model-selection criteria (e.g., BIC) to compare at equal complexity.  
- No quantitative analysis of computational cost or scalability; timing comparisons and experiments on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) are missing.  
- Builds on Pennec and Hauberg but novelty lies in adapting Riemannian normals to a data-learned metric; significance is meaningful though broader applicability could be better articulated.  
- Theoretical framework, MLE, and Monte Carlo integration are sound and well-developed.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, with consistent notation and useful supplementary material.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address metric learning, invariance, fair experimental comparison, and computational analysis.
