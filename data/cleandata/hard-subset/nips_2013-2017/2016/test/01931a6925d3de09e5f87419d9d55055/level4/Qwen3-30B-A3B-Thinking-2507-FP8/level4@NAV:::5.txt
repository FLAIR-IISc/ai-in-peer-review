

Review Summary: The paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) model utilizing a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from data, to replace the standard Mahalanobis distance in Gaussian distributions. The extension to mixtures of LANDs is also presented. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.  

Major Comments: The use of a geodesic distance, analogous to Isomap, represents an interesting theoretical contribution. However, the paper suffers from several clarity issues. First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) lacks clear justification—authors should provide deeper intuition for this choice. Second, the rationale for employing a Gaussian kernel in the weighted sum of local covariances requires further explanation. Third, the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Equation (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Equation (11) is unclear—do these denote the same quantity? Additionally, the experimental evaluation is insufficient, and the most pressing concern is the method’s scalability and computational efficiency: the computational complexity and runtime relative to standard EM must be explicitly addressed. While the theoretical contribution is valuable, the practical applicability appears limited.  

Minor Comments: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5: 'd' should be replaced with '\nabla'.
