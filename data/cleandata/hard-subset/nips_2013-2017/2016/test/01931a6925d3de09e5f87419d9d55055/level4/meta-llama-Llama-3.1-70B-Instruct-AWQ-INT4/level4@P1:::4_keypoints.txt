- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) grounded in Riemannian manifolds as an alternative to GMMs.  
- Introduces an MLE estimator for the parameters of the proposed distribution.  
- Provides sound theoretical foundations for the distribution and estimator.  
- Evaluates method with well-designed experiments on synthetic and real-world data showing effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written and clear in presenting theory and experiments.  
- Lacks clear novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19]; differences are not strongly established.  
- Key differences from state-of-the-art (Simo-Serra et al.) are only explained in the conclusion, not up front.  
- Reference [19] is missing from the introduction, hindering assessment of novelty.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison with Simo-Serra et al. [19] and other related work.  
- Potentially significant: could improve GMM performance in some applications and guide future research.  
- Overall high quality but would benefit from clearer positioning against related methods.  
- For acceptance: novel distribution, solid theory, and convincing experiments.  
- For rejection: insufficiently explained differences from prior work and inadequate related-work comparison.
