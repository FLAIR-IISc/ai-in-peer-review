The paper proposes a novel approach to modeling complex data distributions by introducing a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), which leverages Riemannian manifolds to capture non-linear relationships in the data. The authors provide a thorough theoretical foundation for the LAND, including a maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) scheme for parameter inference. The proposed method is evaluated through a series of experiments on both synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in modeling non-trivial probability distributions.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The theoretical background on Riemannian manifolds and the derivation of the LAND are sound and well-motivated. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering various aspects of the proposed method, including its performance on different types of data and its comparison to existing approaches.

However, the paper could benefit from a clearer discussion of its contributions and novelty, particularly in relation to existing work in the field. While the authors mention Simo-Serra et al. [19] in the conclusion, a more detailed comparison with this work and other related approaches would be helpful in understanding the significance of the proposed method. Specifically, the authors should clarify how their approach differs from existing methods and what advantages it offers.

In terms of the review criteria, the paper scores well on quality, clarity, and originality. The proposed method is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of its significance and impact, particularly in relation to existing work in the field.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to modeling complex data distributions using Riemannian manifolds.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical foundation for the LAND, including a maximum likelihood estimation scheme for parameter inference.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer discussion of its contributions and novelty, particularly in relation to existing work in the field.
* The authors should clarify how their approach differs from existing methods and what advantages it offers.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of its significance and impact, particularly in relation to existing work in the field.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but encourage the authors to revise their manuscript to address the above-mentioned concerns.
* Specifically, the authors should provide a more detailed comparison with existing work, clarify the advantages of their approach, and discuss the significance and impact of their method.
