This paper introduces a compelling generalization of the multivariate normal distribution by substituting the standard Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. This approach effectively extends the normal distribution’s applicability to manifold learning scenarios, a point well-supported by the presented experimental results. Notably, the paper concisely details a complete maximum likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture model – a Locally Adaptive Normal Density Mixture (LANDM) – positions it as a strong alternative to the widely used Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experimental findings are particularly promising and suggest numerous directions for future investigation. The manuscript demonstrates excellent clarity in language and structure, alongside a strong relevance to the NIPS conference scope, and features a comprehensive bibliography.
