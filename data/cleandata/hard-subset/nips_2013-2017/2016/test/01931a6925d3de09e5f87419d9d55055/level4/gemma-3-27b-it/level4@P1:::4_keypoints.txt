- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a Riemannian-manifold-based alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models.  
- Develops a maximum likelihood estimation procedure for LAND parameters.  
- Introduces a scalable Monte Carlo integration scheme to normalize the density.  
- Provides a gradient-based algorithm for MLE and extends LAND to mixture models with an EM algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage measurements.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry.  
- Novel idea: learning a Riemannian metric to adapt the normal distribution to local data structure.  
- Clear presentation and well-written exposition of math and algorithms.  
- Limited discussion of related work, notably insufficient comparison to Simo-Serra et al. [19]; novelty not clearly highlighted early.  
- Performance sensitive to the smoothing parameter σ; lacks detailed sensitivity analysis or adaptive selection strategies.  
- Computational cost remains a concern due to ODE solves for geodesics and log map evaluations in higher dimensions.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence or statistical consistency of the algorithms.  
- Empirical results show improvements over standard Gaussian mixture models.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on minor revisions to expand related work and clarify the method’s unique contributions.
