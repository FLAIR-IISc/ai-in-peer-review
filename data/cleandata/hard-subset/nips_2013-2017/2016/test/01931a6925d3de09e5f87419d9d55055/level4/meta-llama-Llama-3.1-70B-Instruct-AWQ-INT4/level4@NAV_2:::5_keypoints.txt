- Introduces LAND: replaces Euclidean distance in normal distribution with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance, inducing a smoothly changing Riemannian metric.  
- LAND generalizes the normal distribution to data assumed near a low-dimensional manifold embedded in R^D.  
- Proposes a maximum likelihood learning algorithm combining gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration; extends to LAND mixture models with an EM algorithm.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets and EEG sleep data, showing ability to fit non-trivial probability distributions.  
- Using geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is an interesting and novel idea.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) seems ad hoc; more intuition is requested for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances.  
- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear and confusing.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and lacks comprehensive comparisons with alternative methods.  
- Scalability and computational efficiency are not addressed; running time and complexity relative to standard EM are missing and are a major practical concern.  
- Reviewer view: theoretically interesting but practical applicability is uncertain; arguments both for acceptance (novelty, algorithm, empirical demo) and for rejection (clarity, experiments, scalability).
