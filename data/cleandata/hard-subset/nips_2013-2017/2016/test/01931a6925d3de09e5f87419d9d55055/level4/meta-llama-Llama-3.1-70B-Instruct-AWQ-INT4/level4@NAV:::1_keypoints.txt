- Introduces LAND, a parametric metric combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.
- Uses Pennec’s Riemannian normal distribution and MLE to estimate mean and covariance, yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.
- Distances computed via geodesics; novel aspect is learning the metric from inverses of local (diagonal) covariances.
- Proposes mixtures of LANDs as a useful extension for multi-component real-world data.
- Promising insight: parametric/generative models may generalize beyond training data.
- Major scalability concern: computing and inverting a (diagonal) covariance per data point may be impractical in high dimensions; complexity analysis is missing.
- Unclear at what dimensionality (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100) the method breaks down; needs empirical/analytical limits.
- Needs broader positioning within metric learning literature and clearer comparison to related work (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros).
- Influence and robustness of the kernel bandwidth sigma are not adequately analyzed; only discussed for one dataset.
- Should test sigma sensitivity and whether sigma should vary with local density.
- Visual comparisons to intrinsic estimators are incomplete in main figures (supplement shows some), include those visuals in main results.
- Compare computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators to guide method selection.
- Overall evaluation: paper is well-written and enjoyable.
