- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that generalizes multivariate normal to a Riemannian manifold with a locally adaptive metric favoring high-density regions.  
- Extends Pennec's Riemannian normal (which assumes a known manifold) to the manifold-learning setting where the metric is learned from data.  
- Develops maximum likelihood estimation using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and an EM algorithm for LAND mixtures.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage measurements.  
- Strong technical quality: thorough mathematical derivations of LAND, normalization constant, and optimization procedure.  
- Empirical results show LAND outperforms Gaussian Mixture Models and least-squares estimators, especially for nonlinear structures.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality concern: fails to clearly distinguish from Simo-Serra et al. [19], who study mixtures of Riemannian normals on known manifolds; lacks explicit comparison and missing citation in the introduction.  
- Significance demonstrated on EEG data but would be stronger with clearer articulation of practical differences and impacts of learning the manifold vs. assuming it known.  
- Overall: technically sound with good validation, but novelty claim is weakened by insufficient distinction from related work (Simo-Serra et al.).
