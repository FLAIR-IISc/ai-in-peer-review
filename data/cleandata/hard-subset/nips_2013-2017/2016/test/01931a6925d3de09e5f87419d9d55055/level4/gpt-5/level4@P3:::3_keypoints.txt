- Paper introduces LAND: a Riemannian normal on a data-adaptive metric replacing Euclidean distance with geodesic distance to capture nonlinear structure.  
- Metric is built nonparametrically via a Gaussian kernel and diagonal local covariances (Eq. 7); density uses Pennec’s Riemannian normal.  
- Contributions: tangent-space Monte Carlo estimate of the normalization constant, gradient-based ML for mean/covariance, and an EM algorithm for LAND mixtures.  
- Empirically, LAND models curved clusters more parsimoniously than GMMs and improves clustering on synthetic and EEG sleep data.  
- Conceptual strength: clean bridge between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics with a max-entropy interpretation.  
- Technical strength: elegant, practical Monte Carlo normalization avoiding repeated BVPs for Log maps.  
- Algorithmic strength: clear ML and mixture formulations with provided gradients and optimization recipe.  
- Empirical strength: avoids intrinsic-mean pathologies and fits curved structures with fewer components.  
- Main critique: calling the metric “learned” is misleading since it’s a fixed kernelized form with hyperparameters σ and ρ.  
- Hyperparameter concern: no principled selection or sensitivity analysis for σ/ρ; σ critically affects geodesics and normalization.  
- Suggestion: include cross-validation/plug-in/Bayesian methods or joint learning for σ, plus ablations showing geometry and likelihood vs σ.  
- Coordinate dependence: diagonal local covariances are axis-aligned and not invariant to linear reparameterizations; rotations can change results.  
- Suggestion: discuss/use full/local-whitened covariances, pre-whitening/local PCA, and provide empirical rotation tests.  
- Model-selection fairness: comparisons to GMMs should allow K selection via AIC/BIC or held-out likelihood and show LAND’s behavior as K grows.  
- Computational/scalability concerns: need runtime, per-iteration cost vs N,D,S, ODE tolerances, Monte Carlo variance/bias trade-offs, and feasibility strategies for larger datasets.  
- Mixture inference concern: responsibilities rely on approximate normalization constants—discuss numerical stability, convergence, and EM monotonicity if C is biased.  
- Related work: expand discussion of metric learning/probabilistic geometry (e.g., Tosi et al.) and other data-driven Riemannian metric constructions.  
- Quality: derivations are sound and MC trick is valuable, but missing hyperparameter, complexity, and sensitivity analyses; experiments are limited in scale.  
- Clarity: generally clear, but clarify “learned” vs “constructed” metric and provide practical guidance on σ/ρ selection plus runtime and ablation tables.  
- Originality: novel integration of data-adaptive Riemannian metric with ML for Riemannian normals and mixtures; metric construction itself is standard.  
- Significance: potentially impactful as a replacement for Gaussians on curved data, contingent on demonstrated scalability and robust hyperparameter selection.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled integration, practical normalization, implementable ML/EM, and clear empirical advantages on curved data.  
- Cons for acceptance: hand-tuned metric hyperparameters, unfair model-selection comparisons, missing scalability/runtime analysis, and unaddressed coordinate dependence.  
- Recommendation: Borderline — likely acceptable if revisions add principled σ/ρ selection, fair K selection experiments, runtime/scalability reports, and coordinate-invariance experiments.
