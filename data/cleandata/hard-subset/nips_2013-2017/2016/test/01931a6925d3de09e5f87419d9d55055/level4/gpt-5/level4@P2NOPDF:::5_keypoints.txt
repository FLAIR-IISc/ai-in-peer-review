- Paper proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using learned geodesic manifold distances, and extends it to mixtures; experiments on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Core idea (using geodesic distance / Isomap-style metric) is compelling but several presentation aspects are unclear.  
- The inverse metric tensor definition (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc; the reviewer asks for intuition for the Gaussian-kernel-weighted sum of local covariances, justification for Gaussian kernel, bandwidth selection, and sensitivity analysis.  
- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is confusing; clarify whether they are the same parameter at different stages or serve different roles and use consistent notation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; request comparisons to standard Gaussians and GMMs (Mahalanobis), more real datasets, and additional metrics (e.g., likelihood, calibration) beyond accuracy.  
- Major concern about scalability and efficiency; ask for computational complexity per E- and M-step, memory and wall-clock times versus standard EM, scaling of geodesic computations (graph construction, shortest paths), and whether costs can be amortized/approximated/sparsified.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but current clarity issues and limited experiments—especially on efficiency/scalability—limit assessment of practical applicability.  
- Minor comment: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace d with ∇.
