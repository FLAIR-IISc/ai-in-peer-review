- Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using geodesic distances from a data-dependent metric and an EM-style Mixture of LANDs, evaluated on a Swiss-roll and an EEG dataset.

- Strength: novel, principled approach to modeling densities on manifolds by replacing Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a locally adaptive metric; conceptually linked to manifold learning (e.g., Isomap).

- Major concern: definition of the inverse metric tensor G⁻¹(x) as a Gaussian-weighted sum of local covariances (Eq. 7) is insufficiently justified and appears ad hoc.

- Major concern: unclear role and estimation of local covariance matrices C_i — possible circular dependency between their use in Eq. (7) and their MAP estimation in Eq. (11); initialization and update procedure need clarification.

- Major concern: no computational complexity analysis—pairwise distances and geodesic computations likely scale poorly; requires theoretical complexity bounds and empirical timing comparisons to baseline EM-GMM.

- Major concern: limited experiments — evaluation only on a synthetic Swiss-roll and one EEG dataset; needs broader benchmarking against modern density estimation and manifold clustering methods on multiple datasets.

- Conclusion: promising theoretical contribution but not ready for publication until issues of clarity, scalability, and empirical evaluation are comprehensively addressed.

- Minor comment: Algorithm 1 likely has a typo where 'd' should be the gradient operator '∇'.
