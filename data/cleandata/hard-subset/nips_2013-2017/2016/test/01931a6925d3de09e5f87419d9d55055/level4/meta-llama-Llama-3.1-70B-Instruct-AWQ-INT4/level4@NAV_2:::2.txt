This paper extends the common multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting with wide applicability, as illustrated by the reported experiments. Importantly, it provides a complete account of a parameter estimation procedure of the maximum likelihood type. The extension to a mixture of LANDs makes it particularly practical as a competitor to the now standard GMM. The experiments are quite exciting and open a wide avenue for future research. Language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are excellent.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a novel generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting.
- The maximum likelihood estimation procedure is well-described and easy to follow.
- The extension to a mixture of LANDs is a significant contribution, making the model more practical and competitive with GMM.
- The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the potential of the proposed model.

Weaknesses:
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed approach.
- Some of the mathematical derivations and proofs could be included in the supplementary material to make the paper more concise.
- The relationship between the proposed model and other related work could be more thoroughly discussed.

Originality:
- The paper proposes a novel generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.
- The extension to a mixture of LANDs is also a new and important contribution.

Significance:
