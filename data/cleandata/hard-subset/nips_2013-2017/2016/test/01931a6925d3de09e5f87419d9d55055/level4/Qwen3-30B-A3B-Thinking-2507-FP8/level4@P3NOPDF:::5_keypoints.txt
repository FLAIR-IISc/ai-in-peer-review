- Paper proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distributions (LANDs) using a data-adaptive geodesic distance to generalize Gaussians and mixture models.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) is ad hoc and lacks justification, especially the choice of a Gaussian kernel for weighting local covariances.  
- Unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11); the paper must state whether they are the same quantity or derived differently.  
- Empirical validation is limited: synthetic data is too simple to test robustness.  
- Real-world evaluation uses only one modest EEG dataset and omits comparisons to standard baselines (GMMs, KDE, Isomap-based methods).  
- No scalability or computational complexity analysis: missing runtime comparisons and behavior for larger N (e.g., 10^3 vs 10^5).  
- Practical relevance is questionable without evidence that modeling gains justify computational overhead; trade-offs must be discussed.  
- For NIPS, the lack of scalability and computational benchmarks is a major shortcoming; a runtime comparison to EM on a medium dataset is essential.  
- Minor fixes: Algorithm 1 should use the gradient symbol (∇) instead of "d"; multiple typos (e.g., "gedesic", "compleixty", "covarance") need correction.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but unclear and under-validated; recommend Weak Reject pending clarifications and stronger empirical/scalability evidence.
