Summary: This paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) that leverages a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned directly from the data, in place of the standard Mahalanobis distance used in Gaussian distributions. The authors also extend this approach to a mixture of LANDs. They evaluate the performance of their method on both a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG measurement dataset. Major comments: The core idea of utilizing a geodesic distance, reminiscent of Isomaps, is compelling. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. Specifically, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears somewhat arbitrary. The authors should provide further intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight the local covariances in the sum. Furthermore, the presentation of the local covariance matrix definition in Eq. (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is confusing; it is unclear whether these refer to the same quantity. The experimental evaluation also feels limited. My primary concern, however, centers on the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. What is the computational complexity, and how does the running time compare to that of standard Expectation-Maximization (EM)? Overall, while the paper presents a theoretically interesting contribution, its practical applicability remains questionable. Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, replace 'd' with '\nabla'.
