- Proposes LAND: a parametric metric over non-parametric space using Riemannian normal distributions to learn a Mahalanobis-like distance measured via geodesics.  
- Learns a local metric at each point from the inverse of a diagonally-constrained local covariance matrix.  
- Extends the framework to mixtures of LANDs to model multi-modal data.  
- Strength: novel conceptual bridge between metric learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Strength: provides a parametric, generative model that can generalize beyond training data.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally well written and clear.  
- Scalability concern: computing and inverting a local covariance per data point is computationally intensive; no formal complexity analysis or guidance on practical dimensional limits.  
- Literature positioning: insufficient discussion of related local metric learning work (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Local PCA) to clarify novelty over prior methods.  
- Experimental weaknesses: qualitative comparisons use weak GMM baselines; intrinsic-model visualizations in the supplement should be in the main paper; need runtime comparisons to intrinsic estimators.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: inadequate analysis of the Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma, its selection, robustness, and whether an adaptive bandwidth is needed.  
- Mixtures of LAND: promising but underdeveloped; requires more experiments and exploration on multi-modal datasets.
