This paper extends the multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, which possesses wide applicability as illustrated by the reported experiments.

Importantly, the paper provides a complete and succinct account of a maximum likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture model makes this approach a particularly practical competitor to the now-standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are quite exciting and open a wide avenue for future research. The paper is excellent in terms of its language, structure, and bibliography, and is highly relevant to the NIPS conference.
