- Proposes LAND: a parametric metric on a nonparametric space using the Riemannian normal distribution and MLE for mean/covariance.  
- Defines distances as geodesics on the learned manifold using a local metric from inverses of diagonal local covariances; also considers mixtures of LANDs.  
- Strength: connects metric learning and Riemannian statistics and gives parametric generative models that extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Main concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data—per-point local covariance estimation and inversion is statistically and computationally challenging.  
- Request explicit analysis of algorithmic complexity, memory needs, numerical limitations, and a practical definition of “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dims).  
- Need clearer positioning within metric-learning literature, explicitly comparing to Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Hauberg et al., and other local-metric methods.  
- Ask for sharper conceptual and empirical comparisons to methods that learn a diagonal metric tensor per point and to local-PCA approaches.  
- Bandwidth σ concern: clarify how σ affects performance/stability, whether a global or locally adapted σ is used, and include robustness experiments.  
- Visualization critique: Figs. 5–6 compare only to GMMs; include intrinsic-model visualizations (from supplements) in the main paper since they outperform GMMs.  
- Request computational-complexity comparison (theoretical and empirical) between LAND and intrinsic estimators across data structures, sample sizes, and dimensions.  
- Mixture-of-LANDs needs more development: provide details on initialization, identifiability, convergence behavior, and scalability.  
- Overall summary: well-written and meaningful work; addressing scalability/dimensionality, σ role, stronger baselines, and expanded mixture analysis would increase impact.
