This brief paper extends the normal multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adapted Riemannian metric. This results in a generalization of the normal distribution for multi-variable education settings, which has broad applicability (as illustrated by published experience). Importantly, it provides a complete description of a parameter estimation method of the maximum likelihood type in a very concise manner. Its extension to the mixture of LANDS makes it particularly practical as a 'competitor' to the current standard GMM. The experiments (at least for this reviewer) are quite exciting and open a broad path for future research. Its relevance to the scope of Language, Structure, Bibliography, and NIPS is excellent.
