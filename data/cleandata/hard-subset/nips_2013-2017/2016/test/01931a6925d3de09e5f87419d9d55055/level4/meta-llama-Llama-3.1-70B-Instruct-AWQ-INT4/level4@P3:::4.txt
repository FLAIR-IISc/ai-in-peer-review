The paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs). The LAND is based on Riemannian manifolds and is designed to capture non-linear relationships in data. The authors provide a sound theoretical foundation for the LAND, including a maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) procedure for inferring its parameters. The paper is well-written, and the experiments on both artificial and real-world data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a parametric locally adaptive normal distribution that can be used for modeling data with non-linear relationships. The LAND is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold setting, where data is assumed to lie near a potentially low-dimensional manifold embedded in R^d. The authors show that the LAND is a maximum entropy distribution under a given metric, making it a theoretically interesting model.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical background and the proposed method. The experiments are well-designed, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the LAND in capturing non-linear relationships in data. The comparison with GMMs and other state-of-the-art methods is also informative and helps to highlight the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
