This concise paper extends the standard multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution for the manifold learning setting with broad applicability, as demonstrated by the reported experiments. Importantly, it provides, very succinctly, a complete account of a maximum-likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to mixtures of LANDS makes it particularly practical as a competitor to the now-standard GMM. The experiments are (at least for this reviewer) quite exciting and open a wide avenue for future research. Language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are excellent.
