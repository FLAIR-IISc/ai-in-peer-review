- Introduces a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and curved normal distribution to model observation-space geometry for statistical learning.
- The metric is not learned despite the title; it’s hand-crafted (Eq.7) and depends on fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ.
- Sensitivity to σ needs investigation, with more intuition and guidance for selecting σ; consider learning ρ and σ within LAND.
- The proposed metric lacks coordinate-invariance; different coordinate representations of the same data yield different metrics — this limitation needs discussion and analysis (including invariance of the full metric).
- Provide more intuition and justification for Equation 7 and relate the method to prior Riemannian metric-learning and manifold-learning literature.
- Experimental comparison is unconvincing: LAND’s flexibility may require fewer components but GMMs could match performance with more components; comparisons should use principled component selection (e.g., BIC).
- Test for LAND’s overfitting when increasing component count and characterize its susceptibility to noise relative to Gaussian models.
- Quantify computational cost and scalability of LAND versus standard Gaussian/Mixture estimation and demonstrate applicability on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST).
- Reviewer recommendation: revise experiments to reflect that LAND’s curved geodesics, while approaching straight lines, remain curved and can increase overfitting; redesign accordingly.
