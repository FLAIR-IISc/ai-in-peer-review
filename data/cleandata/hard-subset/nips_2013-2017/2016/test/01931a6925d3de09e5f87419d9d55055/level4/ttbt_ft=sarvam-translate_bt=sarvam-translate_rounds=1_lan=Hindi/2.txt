This brief paper expands the general multivariate normal density from the Euclidean metric to the locally adapted Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution for manifold learning settings (as demonstrated by the reported experiments). Importantly, it provides a complete description of the maximum likelihood type parameter estimation process, in a very synthetic manner. The extension to the mixture of LANDS makes it particularly practical as a 'competitor' to the standard GMM. The experiments are quite exciting (at least for this reviewer) and open a wide path for future research. The relevance to the scope of Language, Structure, Bibliography, and NIPS is excellent.
