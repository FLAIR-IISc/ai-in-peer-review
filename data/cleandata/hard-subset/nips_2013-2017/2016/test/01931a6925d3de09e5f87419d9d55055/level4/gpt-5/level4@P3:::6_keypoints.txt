- Proposal: LAND replaces the Euclidean metric with a learned Riemannian metric M(x) and defines a Riemannian normal using Logµ(x), normalized via tangent-space Monte Carlo.

- Metric learning: M(x) is estimated nonparametrically from local diagonal covariances weighted by a Gaussian kernel, producing geodesics attracted to high-density regions.

- Parameter estimation: MLE via block coordinate descent—steepest-descent on µ (exponential maps) and gradient descent on A with Σ−1 = AᵀA; EM derived for mixtures.

- Experiments: Synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage data show LAND (and mixtures) capture nonlinear structure better than Euclidean GMMs and Riemannian least-squares.

- Novelty: Integrates a learned continuous Riemannian metric, Pennec’s Riemannian normal, and practical normalization/estimation into a probabilistic generative model.

- Strength: Conceptually clean, maximum-entropy grounding favoring high-density geometry.

- Strength: Algorithmic contributions—scalable Monte Carlo normalization in tangent space, practical µ/Σ updates, and EM for mixtures.

- Strength: Strong exposition of Riemannian tools and intuitive visualizations of geodesics/density contours.

- Weakness: Model selection/overfitting risk—σ tuned on the same labels without held-out validation, producing potentially optimistic performance.

- Weakness: Missing control experiments on pure Gaussian or Euclidean GMM data to show when LAND degrades or is unnecessary.

- Weakness: No analysis of Monte Carlo normalization variance/cost (S=3000), or trade-off between S and accuracy; per-iteration complexity not given.

- Weakness: High-dimensional scalability and robustness unclear due to kernel smoothing with diagonal local covariances and lack of linear-transform invariance.

- Clarity issue: Notational/conceptual conflation of M(x) and Σ (occasional “M = Σ−1”); needs clearer separation and scope for any local inner-product statements.

- Clarity issue: Ambiguous clustering setup—unclear whether sleep-stage results use a LAND mixture with responsibilities via Logµk or k-means on geodesic distances.

- Clarity issue: σ results not clearly separated between fixed-σ and tuned-σ, and it’s not stated whether label information was used in tuning.

- Question: Clarify distinct roles of M(x) and Σ and whether Σ-preconditioning in µ-updates corresponds to steepest-descent under Σ−1 norm.

- Question: How are responsibilities computed in mixture EM in practice, are C(µk, Σk) recomputed each iteration, and how is Monte Carlo noise controlled?

- Question: Specify the train/validation/test protocol for σ in EEG experiments and report test F-measure with σ chosen on validation only.

- Question: Provide sensitivity analysis to S (Monte Carlo samples) and kernel bandwidth σ on synthetic and real data.

- Question: Provide computational complexity per iteration for mean and covariance updates as functions of N, D, and S.

- Pros for acceptance: principled generalization of Gaussians to learned geometries, practical normalization/estimation methods, empirical gains, and potential impact on generative modeling.

- Cons for acceptance: evaluation shortcomings (σ selection), missing Euclidean baselines, notation ambiguity, and incomplete computational/optimization characterization.

- Overall: Interesting and timely contribution; lean toward acceptance if notation is clarified and evaluation (σ selection, Euclidean baselines) plus a brief computational analysis are addressed.
