This paper introduces a novel extension of the multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, resulting in a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting. The experiments presented are exciting and showcase the potential of LANDs as a competitor to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM).

The paper is well-structured, and the language is clear and concise. The authors provide an excellent bibliography, demonstrating a thorough understanding of the relevant literature. The topic is highly relevant to the NIPS remit, and the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning.
