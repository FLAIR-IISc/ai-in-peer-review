- Proposes LAND, a novel parametric metric on a nonparametric space combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Defines the metric as a Riemannian normal distribution with ML-estimated mean and covariance, yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.  
- Distances computed via geodesics on the manifold, using local metrics from inverses of local diagonal covariances (akin to Hauberg et al.).  
- Explores mixtures of LANDs, addressing multi-component real-world data generation.  
- Argues parametric/generative models that generalize beyond training data are valuable.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional real-world data due to per-point covariance computation and inversion.  
- Authors note high-dimensional issues but need a thorough complexity analysis and clearer definition of what counts as “high-dimensional.”  
- Paper needs broader positioning in metric learning literature, citing related local-diagonal-metric and local-PCA methods (Frome, Malisiewicz, Kambhatla, etc.).  
- The choice of Gaussian kernel parameter sigma is under-discussed (only addressed for sleep data); requires robustness tests and clarification on whether sigma is constant across densities.  
- Comparisons are incomplete: Fig.4 compares to intrinsic estimators but Figs.5–6 only show GMM; visual comparisons to intrinsic estimators should be included.  
- Recommend analysis of computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators to guide method selection for different data structures.  
- Overall assessment: manuscript is well-written and enjoyable to read.
