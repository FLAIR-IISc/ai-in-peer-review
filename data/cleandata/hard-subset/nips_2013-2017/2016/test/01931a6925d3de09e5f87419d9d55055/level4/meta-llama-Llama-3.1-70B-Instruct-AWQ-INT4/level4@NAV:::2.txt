This concise paper presents a significant extension of the traditional multivariate normal density by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The outcome is a generalized normal distribution tailored to the manifold learning framework, showcasing broad applicability as evidenced by the presented experiments. Notably, the authors provide a succinct and comprehensive account of a maximum likelihood-based parameter estimation procedure. Furthermore, the extension to a mixture of locally adaptive normal distributions (LANDS) renders it a viable competitor to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experimental results are intriguing and unlock promising avenues for future research. The paper excels in terms of language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit.
