- Proposes a novel method using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal (LAND) to better model data manifolds.  
- Tackles the problem of defining a Riemannian metric from high-dimensional observations and estimating a curved Gaussian on that geometry.  
- The metric is not actually learned: equation (7) uses hand-set hyperparameters rho and sigma, contrary to the paper title.  
- Paper lacks detailed discussion on sigma’s effect, sensitivity, and principled selection; merging metric learning with LAND learning is suggested.  
- The proposed (diagonal) metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant; different coordinate rotations change the metric behavior.  
- A deeper discussion of invariance and the role of a full (non-diagonal) metric is needed for intuition and correctness.  
- The work should be connected to extensive literature on metric learning and manifold learning grounded in Riemannian geometry.  
- The first experiment’s comparison is flawed: LAND with few components outperforms GMM with few, but GMM can match with more components; fairness requires model selection (e.g., BIC).  
- Authors should examine and report how LAND overfits when using many components and why curved geodesics increase vulnerability to local noise.  
- Computational cost and scalability of LAND are unclear; provide quantitative runtime/memory comparisons and test limits (e.g., MNIST-scale experiments).
