- Paper proposes a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal (LAND) to better model data manifolds — an interesting application of Riemannian geometry to statistical learning.
- The metric is not learned as claimed: Equation 7 uses two hand-set hyperparameters (rho, sigma); sigma likely sensitive and the paper should give intuition and selection guidance.
- Ask whether metric construction can be integrated with LAND learning so rho and sigma are learned jointly.
- The proposed (diagonal) metric in Equation 7 is not invariant to coordinate transformations; discuss invariance, whether a full (non-diagonal) metric would be invariant, and justify the design.
- Connect the work to existing literature on metric learning and manifold learning in Riemannian contexts.
- Main experimental critique: first experiment is unfair — compare methods after each selects its preferred number of components (e.g., via BIC) and show how LAND behaves/overfits with many components.
- Request quantitative measurements of computational cost and scalability (practical limits, feasibility on datasets like MNIST) since LAND is more expensive than Gaussian estimation.
- After the authors’ response: acknowledge LAND’s flexibility but note that with many components local LANDs can overfit due to sensitivity to local noise; revise the first experiment accordingly.
