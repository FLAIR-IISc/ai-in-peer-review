- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using Riemannian metrics as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models for data on nonlinear manifolds.  
- Develops a maximum likelihood estimation algorithm for LAND combining gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, presented as practical and scalable.  
- Builds on a solid theoretical foundation in Riemannian geometry with sufficient mathematical detail.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and EEG data demonstrates promising improvements over standard GMMs.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Novelty relative to prior Riemannian statistics and manifold learning work is not sufficiently emphasized.  
- Insufficient comparison and differentiation from Simo-Serra et al. [19]; this connection should be clarified earlier in the paper.  
- Key advantages of LAND over existing Riemannian methods are left implicit and only made clear in the conclusion.  
- Sensitivity of key parameters (e.g., σ in the Gaussian kernel defining the metric) requires more detailed analysis and adaptive strategies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, practical algorithm, promising empirical results, strong theoretical basis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty relative to related work, limited introduction-level comparison, and incomplete sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better articulate novelty, expand discussion of related work (especially Simo-Serra et al.), and deepen parameter sensitivity analysis.
