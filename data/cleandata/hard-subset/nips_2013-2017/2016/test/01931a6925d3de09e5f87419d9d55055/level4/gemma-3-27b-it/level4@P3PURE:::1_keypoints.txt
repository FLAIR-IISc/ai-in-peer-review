- Proposes LAND: a novel parametric metric over a nonparametric space connecting metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics via a Riemannian normal distribution and ML estimation of mean/covariance, defining geodesic distances from local (diagonal) covariances; also explores mixtures.

- Main concern: scalability and applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because computing and inverting a per-point (diagonal) covariance can be computationally expensive and ill-conditioned; requires a thorough complexity and limitation analysis and a clear definition of “high-dimensional.”

- Needs stronger positioning in the metric-learning literature: related work (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) also learn per-point diagonal metrics and local covariances have been used in methods like local PCA.

- Local metric learning uses a Gaussian kernel; the influence and sensitivity of the kernel bandwidth σ need investigation (is σ uniform across points?), with robustness tests missing for the synthetic experiments.

- Empirical comparisons: Figs. 5–6 compare to GMMs (which perform poorly) rather than intrinsic estimators; visual results for intrinsic models are relegated to the supplement and should be included in the main paper, along with a computational-complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators.

- Mixtures of LANDs are a compelling extension for real-world, multimodal data and deserve further expansion.

- Overall assessment: paper is exceptionally well-written and provides valuable insights and useful parametric generative modeling contributions.
