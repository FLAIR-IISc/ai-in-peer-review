- Paper proposes LAND: a parametric generative model on a learned Riemannian manifold, instantiating Pennec’s Riemannian normal and ML estimation of mean/covariance.  
- Distances are geodesics on a manifold with local metric given by inverse pointwise (diagonal) local covariances; mixtures of LANDs are developed.  
- Strength: clarifies links between metric learning and Riemannian statistics and offers a generative model that can extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Primary concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data since LAND requires computing and inverting a local (diagonal) covariance per point.  
- Paper lacks concrete computational-complexity analysis and practical dimensionality limits (e.g., which d ranges are feasible: 3, 5, 10, 100?).  
- Need stronger contextualization in the metric-learning literature; related works (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) also learn per-point diagonal metrics.  
- Local covariances are common (e.g., local PCA); authors should position LAND relative to these methods.  
- Sensitivity to the Gaussian kernel width σ is insufficiently analyzed—robustness studies and whether σ is fixed across space/density are needed.  
- Experimental comparisons: Fig. 4 shows close performance to intrinsic estimators, but Figs. 5–6 only compare GMMs (weaker baseline); include intrinsic estimator visualizations from the supplement in the main paper.  
- Analyze computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators to guide which approach suits which data structures.  
- Extension to mixtures of LANDs is a compelling contribution and merits expansion.  
- Writing and presentation are strong; paper is pleasurable to read.
