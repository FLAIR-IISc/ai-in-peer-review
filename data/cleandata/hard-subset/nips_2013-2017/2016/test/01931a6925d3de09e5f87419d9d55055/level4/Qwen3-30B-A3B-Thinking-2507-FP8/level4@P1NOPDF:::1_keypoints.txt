- Introduces LAND: a parametric metric on a nonparametric manifold combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Models data as a Riemannian normal and derives an ML algorithm to estimate mean and covariance, yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.  
- Computes distances via geodesics with local metrics given by inverses of diagonal covariances; also proposes mixtures of LAND.  
- Offers valuable theoretical insights and highlights parametric generative models that generalize beyond training data.  
- Major scalability concern: computing a (diagonal) covariance and its inverse per point is computationally challenging in high dimensions.  
- Paper lacks concrete analysis of computational complexity and does not define what counts as "high-dimensional" (e.g., 3, 5, 10, or 100 dims).  
- Needs stronger contextualization in metric learning literature; related pointwise diagonal metrics and local covariance methods (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA) are not sufficiently distinguished.  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is inadequately justified and not tested for robustness, especially on synthetic data and across varying density regions.  
- Visual comparisons are weak: Figures 5–6 compare LAND only to GMM, which underperforms intrinsic estimators shown in Figure 4 and supplement.  
- Suggest replacing GMM comparisons in main text with intrinsic estimators (supported by Supplementary Figures 2 and 4).  
- Recommends a comparative computational complexity analysis between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify preferred methods for different data structures.  
- The mixture extension is compelling but needs deeper empirical validation and theoretical justification.  
- Technical content and writing are sound and clear, but addressing scalability, literature context, and experimental rigor would strengthen the contribution.
