- Introduces LAND: replaces Mahalanobis with geodesic distance on a learned Riemannian manifold, bridging manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Manifold built non-parametrically via a smoothly varying metric tensor = inverse of a local diagonal covariance computed with a Gaussian kernel.  
- Defines LAND as a Riemannian normal (per Pennec), uses MLE with gradient optimization plus Monte Carlo for normalization, and extends to a mixture model with an EM algorithm; evaluated on synthetic and EEG sleep data.  

Strengths:
- Originality/significance: novel, elegant synthesis producing a parametric generative model that adapts to local geometry and generalizes to new data.  
- Mixture extension: valuable for multi-modal real-world data, improving applicability for clustering and density estimation.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and concepts/algorithms are clearly presented.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Scalability/computational complexity: computing weighted local covariances over all N points and inverting matrices is likely prohibitive in large N or high D; lacks formal complexity analysis and practical limits.  
- Needs complexity comparison to intrinsic LS estimators to clarify trade-offs and applicability.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: critical dependence on Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ; lacks thorough sensitivity/robustness analysis and discussion of using a single global σ for non-uniform densities.  
- Experimental comparisons: qualitative visuals compare LAND to weak GMM baseline instead of stronger intrinsic LS estimators; recommend moving LS visualizations from supplement to main paper.  
- Positioning in literature: should better relate the learned metric to other local diagonal-metric and local-covariance methods (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Local PCA) to clarify novelty.  

- Overall: compelling and theoretically sound, but impact would increase by addressing scalability, hyperparameter analysis, and stronger experimental baselines.
