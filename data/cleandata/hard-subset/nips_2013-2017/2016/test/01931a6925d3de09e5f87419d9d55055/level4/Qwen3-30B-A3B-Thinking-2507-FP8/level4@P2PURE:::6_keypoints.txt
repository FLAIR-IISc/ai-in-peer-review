- Technical methods (Euler–Lagrange geodesics, logarithmic maps) are sound and well explained.  
- Monte-Carlo normalization and steepest-descent parameter estimation are cleverly designed and well described.  
- Sigma parameter selection appears to be done by optimizing on training data, and practical utility of the learned distribution is insufficiently justified.  
- Novelty is limited: mainly a combination of prior kernel-metric and geodesic methods with a new estimation algorithm, but this contribution is under-emphasized.  
- Evaluation on synthetic data compares favorably to GMM and LAND, but the method’s sigma selection is unclear.  
- Missing comparisons when data are generated from a single normal or GMM; this gap weakens empirical claims.  
- Clustering experiments choose sigma by optimizing F-measure on training data and report no held-out test results, raising overfitting concerns.  
- Testing on higher-dimensional data is needed to strengthen impact.  
- The paper assumes the manifold dimension equals the ambient dimension (M full rank), contradicting standard manifold-learning settings (D ≫ d).  
- Significant notational confusion over Σ and M (M = Σ⁻¹ vs. local metric M(x)); proofs assume a global M = Σ⁻¹ while M(x) should vary locally.  
- Cluster assignment procedure is unclear—appears to use minimal geodesic distance to means but is not explicitly stated.  
- Introduction to manifolds is clear for readers familiar with Riemannian geometry.
