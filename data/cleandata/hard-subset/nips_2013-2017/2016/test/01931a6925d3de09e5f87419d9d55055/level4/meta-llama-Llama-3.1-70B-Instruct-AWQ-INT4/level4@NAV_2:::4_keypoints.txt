- Proposes LAND: a manifold-generalized normal distribution using a learned locally adaptive nonlinear distance.
- Replaces Euclidean distance with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric that favors high-density regions.
- Provides a thorough theoretical review of Riemannian manifolds and their application to statistics.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and EEG sleep measurements.
- Experimental results show LAND outperforms GMM in capturing local data properties.
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the proposed method.
- Using Riemannian manifolds for data modeling is novel and interesting.
- Lacks a clear explanation of differences from existing state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Simo-Serra et al. [19]).
- Introduction could be more concise and clearer about the paper’s contributions.
- Originality: LAND is a novel manifold-based generalization of the normal distribution.
- Significance: shows practical gains in modeling complex, non-trivial distributions.
- Quality: experiments are thorough and well-presented.
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and technical concepts are accessible.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach and strong empirical evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to prior work and a weaker introduction.
