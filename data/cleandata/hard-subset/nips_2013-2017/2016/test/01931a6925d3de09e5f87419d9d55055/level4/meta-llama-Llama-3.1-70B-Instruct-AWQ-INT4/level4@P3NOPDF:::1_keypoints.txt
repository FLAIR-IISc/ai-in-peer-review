- Proposes LAND: a novel parametric Riemannian metric (Riemannian normal) over a nonparametric space combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Introduces an MLE algorithm to estimate mean and covariance of the Riemannian normal, yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.  
- Defines distances as geodesics along a learned local metric, using inverses of per-point diagonal covariances.  
- Considers mixtures of LANDs, a promising direction for modeling multimodal real-world data.  
- Strength: provides a generative, parametric model that can generalize beyond training data and links metric learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data is unclear since LAND requires computing and inverting a covariance per data point.  
- Requests a thorough complexity analysis and explicit guidance on feasible data dimensionalities (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dims).  
- Suggests better situating the work in metric learning literature, noting prior per-point diagonal metrics (Frome, Malisiewicz, Hauberg) and related local-covariance methods.  
- Notes local covariance use in methods like local PCA and asks for discussion of differences and precedents.  
- Calls for deeper analysis of the Gaussian kernel sigma choice, robustness tests, and whether sigma is uniform across density regions; synthetic-data sigma analysis is missing.  
- Recommends including visual results for intrinsic estimators (which outperform GMM) in the main paper rather than only GMM comparisons.  
- Asks for computational-complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify when to prefer each method.  
- Overall: paper is well-written and promising but needs revisions addressing scalability, related work, sigma robustness, visual comparisons, and complexity analysis.
