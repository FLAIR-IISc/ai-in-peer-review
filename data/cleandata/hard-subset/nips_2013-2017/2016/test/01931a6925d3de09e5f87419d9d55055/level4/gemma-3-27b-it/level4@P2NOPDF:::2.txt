This paper presents a compelling extension of the standard multivariate normal distribution by incorporating a locally adaptive Riemannian metric in place of the Euclidean metric. This results in a generalization of the normal distribution particularly well-suited for manifold learning, and demonstrates broad applicability, as evidenced by the reported experiments. 

The authors concisely and completely detail a maximum likelihood parameter estimation procedure for this novel distribution – termed LANDS (Locally Adaptive Normal DensityS). Notably, the extension to mixtures of LANDS positions it as a practical and competitive alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs), a widely used technique.

I found the experimental results particularly encouraging and believe this work opens up significant avenues for future research. The paper is exceptionally well-written, with excellent clarity, structure, and a comprehensive bibliography. Furthermore, the work is highly relevant to the NIPS conference remit.

Specifically, the replacement of the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric allows the distribution to better model data residing on non-Euclidean manifolds. The authors successfully demonstrate how this adaptation leads to improved performance across various experimental settings. The succinct presentation of the parameter estimation procedure is a strong point; it allows for immediate implementation and further exploration.  The potential to directly compare and contrast the performance of mixtures of LANDS with GMMs represents a significant contribution, potentially shifting the landscape of density estimation techniques.

While the paper is concise, it doesn’t sacrifice rigor or clarity. The mathematical formulation is presented clearly and the experimental setup is well-defined. The choice of datasets used in the experiments is appropriate and supports the claims made by the authors.

Overall, this is a strong paper with the potential to have a significant impact on the field. I recommend acceptance.
