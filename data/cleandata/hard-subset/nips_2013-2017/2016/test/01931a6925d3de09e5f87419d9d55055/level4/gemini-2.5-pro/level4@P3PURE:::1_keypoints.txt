- Introduces LAND, a novel parametric metric (Riemannian normal distribution) bridging metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.
- Presents a parametric, generative model that generalizes beyond training data and creates a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.
- Main concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data due to per-point covariance computation and inversion (even with diagonal assumption).
- Requests formal computational-complexity analysis and practical limits (feasible dimensionality: ~10, 100, or more).
- Needs stronger placement in metric-learning literature and direct contrasts with related local-diagonal metric methods (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros).
- Should discuss precedents using local covariances (e.g., Local PCA) to clarify novelty and context.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Gaussian kernel width sigma is critical; requires robustness analysis (including synthetic data) and discussion of whether sigma should vary with local density.
- Experimental comparisons are weak: main paper mostly compares to GMMs (a poor baseline); stronger intrinsic-model comparisons in the supplement should be moved to the main text.
- Requests complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify trade-offs and method preference by data structure.
- Mixtures of LANDs are a promising extension for real-world multimodal data and deserve further expansion.
- Overall the paper is well-written; addressing scalability, comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity would significantly improve impact.
