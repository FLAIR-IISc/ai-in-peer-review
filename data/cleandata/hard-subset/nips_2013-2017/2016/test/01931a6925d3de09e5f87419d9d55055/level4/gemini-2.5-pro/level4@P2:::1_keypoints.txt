- Introduces LAND: a Riemannian normal distribution learned via a locally adaptive metric for data on/near nonlinear manifolds.  
- Novel and significant synthesis of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics to produce a parametric generative model.  
- Provides a principled, maximum-entropy justification and ML estimation for the Riemannian normal — theoretically sound.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written exposition that makes complex geometry accessible.  
- Metric construction is non-parametric: local metric at x is inverse of local diagonal covariance using a Gaussian kernel.  
- Proposes ML algorithm combining gradient optimization and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to a LAND mixture with an EM algorithm.  
- Major scalability concerns: metric construction sums over all N points and covariance operations are costly in high dimensions — lacks formal complexity analysis.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends strongly on Gaussian kernel width σ; selection and global vs. local σ issues are underexplored.  
- Experimental comparisons are weak: main visual baselines use GMM (a poor baseline); more relevant LS mixture results are relegated to the supplement.  
- Missing analysis of computational trade-offs between ML estimators and simpler LS estimators.  
- Related-work positioning could be improved by comparing to other local/point-wise metric learning methods and clarifying differences.  
- Mixture-of-LANDs is promising but underdeveloped—EM details and more focused experiments are needed.  
- Pro-accept: novel, elegant, theoretically grounded generative model bridging manifold learning and Riemannian statistics; very clear presentation.  
- Con-accept: unresolved concerns about scalability, σ selection, and insufficiently challenging experimental baselines; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
