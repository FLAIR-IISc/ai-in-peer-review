- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance in place of the Mahalanobis distance, and extends to mixtures; tested on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Use of geodesic distance (similar to Isomap) is interesting but the exposition lacks clarity.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary; authors should justify the Gaussian kernel choice for weighting local covariances.  
- Transition between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is confusing—unclear if they refer to the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is relatively weak and does not convincingly demonstrate advantages.  
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency—need complexity/runtime comparison to standard EM and discussion of practical applicability.  
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace symbol "d" with "\nabla".
