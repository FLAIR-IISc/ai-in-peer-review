- Introduces a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution to model observation space and underlying data manifolds.  
- Metric in Equation 7 is hand-crafted (not learned) and depends on fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ, whose values are chosen empirically.  
- Sensitivity to σ is a major concern; the paper lacks intuition, analysis, and guidance on choosing σ and should consider learning ρ and σ during LAND training.  
- The defined metric is not coordinate-invariant (example: data on a line gives different metrics under rotations); the paper needs to discuss this limitation and whether a full non-diagonal metric would help.  
- Paper should better connect to metric learning and manifold learning literature, especially Riemannian geometry work.  
- First experiment is unconvincing: LAND’s greater flexibility explains better performance with few components; compare methods after model selection (e.g., BIC) and study overfitting as component count grows.  
- LAND is computationally heavier than a standard Gaussian, but the paper provides no quantitative cost or scalability analysis; experiments on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) and runtime comparisons to GMM are recommended.  
- Author response confirms LAND’s flexibility but also increased susceptibility to local noise and overfitting as components increase due to residual geodesic curvature; experiments should be revised accordingly.
