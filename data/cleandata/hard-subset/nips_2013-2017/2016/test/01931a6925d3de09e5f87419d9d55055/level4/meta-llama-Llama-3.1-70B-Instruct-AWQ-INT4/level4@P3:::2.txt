This paper extends the common multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting with wide applicability, as illustrated by the reported experiments. Importantly, it provides a complete account of a parameter estimation procedure of the maximum likelihood type. The extension to a mixture of LANDs makes it particularly practical as a competitor to the now standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM).

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear introduction that motivates the work and explains the main contribution. The authors provide a thorough review of the relevant literature, including previous work on Riemannian normal distributions and manifold learning. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a detailed derivation of the maximum likelihood estimation procedure for the LAND model.

The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the LAND model in capturing non-linear structure in the data. The comparison with the GMM is particularly useful, as it shows that the LAND model can outperform the GMM in certain cases. The use of real-world data, such as EEG measurements of human sleep, adds to the paper's impact and relevance.

The language is clear and concise, and the bibliography is comprehensive and relevant to the NIPS remit. The paper's relevance to the NIPS community is high, as it addresses a fundamental problem in machine learning and provides a new and useful tool for modeling complex data.

* The paper provides a novel and useful extension of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting.
