

This concise paper extends the standard multivariate normal density by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result generalizes the normal distribution to the manifold learning context, demonstrating broad applicability (as evidenced by the reported experiments). Critically, it provides, in a succinct manner, a complete account of a maximum likelihood-type parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture of LANDS renders it particularly practical as a 'competitor' to the now-standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are highly compelling and open significant avenues for future research. Language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are excellent.
