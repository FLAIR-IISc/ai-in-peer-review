This brief paper extends the common multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, with wide applicability as illustrated by the reported experiments. Importantly, it provides a complete account of a parameter estimation procedure of the maximum likelihood type in a concise manner. The extension to a mixture of Locally Adaptive Normal Distributions (LANDS) makes it a practical competitor to the now-standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are exciting and open a wide avenue for future research.

The paper's language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are all excellent. Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field, and its clarity and concision make it an enjoyable read. The authors have done a great job in presenting their work, and the reviewer is eager to see the impact of this research in the future.
