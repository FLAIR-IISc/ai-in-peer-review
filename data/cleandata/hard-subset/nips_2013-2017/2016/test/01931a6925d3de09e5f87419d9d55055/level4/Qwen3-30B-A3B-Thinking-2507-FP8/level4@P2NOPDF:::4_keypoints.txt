- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a Riemannian manifold-based alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, proposing an MLE and validating on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-structured with rigorous theoretical foundations and comprehensive experimental results demonstrating robust performance.  
- Major concern: novelty is not convincingly established relative to existing work, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19].  
- Simo-Serra et al. [19] is omitted from the introduction, depriving readers of essential context.  
- The key distinction between LAND and prior work (how local adaptivity differs from [19]) is only clarified in the conclusion rather than up front.  
- This structural oversight weakens the paper’s positioning and risks presenting the contribution as incremental.  
- Without early comparative analysis, readers cannot assess whether LAND offers meaningful theoretical or empirical advances over [19].  
- Revision requested: explicitly cite and contrast LAND with [19] and related work in the introduction.  
- Revision requested: articulate specific gaps in existing methods (e.g., limitations in adaptivity or geometric modeling) that LAND addresses.  
- Revision requested: position the MLE estimator as a targeted solution to those gaps in the foundational sections.  
- Conclusion: theoretical soundness and empirical results are strong, but the paper’s impact is diminished; recommend substantial revision before acceptance.
