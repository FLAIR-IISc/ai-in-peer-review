The paper proposes a novel Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a generalization of the normal distribution to the "manifold" setting. The authors replace the Euclidean distance in the ordinary normal distribution with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance measure, learned from the data. This is achieved by constructing a smoothly changing Riemannian metric that favors regions of high local density.

The authors provide a thorough review of the theoretical foundations of Riemannian manifolds and their application to statistics. They demonstrate the effectiveness of the LAND in modeling non-trivial probability distributions over both synthetic data and EEG measurements of human sleep. The experimental results show that the LAND outperforms the Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) in capturing the local properties of the data.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method. The use of Riemannian manifolds to model the data is a novel and interesting approach. However, the paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the differences between the proposed method and existing state-of-the-art methods, such as Simo-Serra et al. [19]. This would help to better situate the contribution of the paper and provide a more nuanced understanding of its novelty.
