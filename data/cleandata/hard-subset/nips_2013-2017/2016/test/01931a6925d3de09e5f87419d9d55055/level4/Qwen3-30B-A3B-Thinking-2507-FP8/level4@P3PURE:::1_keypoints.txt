- Introduces LAND: a parametric Riemannian metric (Riemannian normal distribution) with ML estimation of mean/covariance, geodesic distances, local diagonal covariance inverses, and mixtures.  
- Primary concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data due to per-point covariance and inverse computations; lacks detailed complexity analysis and a clear threshold for "high-dimensional."  
- Insufficient contextualization in metric learning literature — related prior work (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Hauberg et al.) also learn per-point diagonal metrics, reducing perceived novelty.  
- Local covariances are not novel per se (appear in methods like local PCA), and the paper should better situate its contributions relative to these approaches.  
- Gaussian kernel σ (locality parameter) is only analyzed on the sleep dataset and not on synthetic data; robustness across σ and density variations is not evaluated.  
- Visual comparisons are weak: main figures compare to GMM (known to underperform) rather than intrinsic estimators that perform similarly to LAND; supplementary shows intrinsic estimators outperform GMM and should be moved to main comparisons.  
- Missing computational complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice based on data structure.  
- Mixture-of-LANDs is a compelling extension deserving deeper exploration.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to read.
