- Proposes modeling observation space with a Riemannian metric and defines a curved normal distribution using that geometry.  
- Introduces the locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) to capture nonlinear data structure.  
- Provides a detailed derivation of LAND and a maximum likelihood estimation scheme for its parameters.  
- Demonstrates advantages of LAND over Gaussian mixture models on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of mathematical concepts and experiments.  
- Effectively bridges Riemannian geometry and statistical learning, offering a useful tool for nonlinear modeling.  
- Comparison to Gaussian mixture models may be unfair because LAND is more flexible; component selection is unclear.  
- Lacks quantitative analysis or experiments on computational complexity and scalability of LAND.  
- Needs clearer discussion of how the number of LAND components was chosen and its impact on results.  
- Should better relate LAND to existing metric learning and manifold learning literature.  
- Requires more intuition and guidance on selecting the parameter σ and on the invariance/intuitions of the metric (eq.7).  
- With revisions addressing these points, the paper could make a significant contribution to statistical learning.
