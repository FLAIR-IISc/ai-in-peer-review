

The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a Riemannian manifold-based alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, proposing a maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) and validating it on synthetic and real-world datasets. The work is well-structured, with rigorous theoretical foundations and comprehensive experimental results demonstrating robust performance. However, the paper fails to convincingly establish its novelty relative to existing state-of-the-art methods, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19]. Critically, [19] is omitted from the introduction, depriving readers of essential context to evaluate the contribution. The key distinction between LAND and prior work—specifically, how its local adaptivity mechanism differs from [19]'s manifold-based approach—is only clarified in the conclusion, rather than being explicitly framed in the introduction. This structural oversight significantly weakens the paper’s positioning. For example, [19] develops a similar framework leveraging Riemannian geometry for density estimation; without a clear comparative analysis early in the paper, the reader cannot assess whether LAND offers meaningful theoretical or empirical advances beyond this prior work. The current presentation risks misrepresenting the contribution as incremental rather than novel. To address this, the authors should revise the introduction to: (1) explicitly cite and contrast LAND with [19] and related work, (2) articulate the specific gaps in existing methods that LAND addresses (e.g., limitations in adaptivity or geometric modeling), and (3) position their MLE estimator as a targeted solution to these gaps. Without these revisions, the paper’s primary claim—that LAND represents a substantial improvement over current approaches—lacks sufficient justification. The experiments, while well-executed, do not compensate for the absence of a compelling novelty narrative. Theoretical soundness and empirical results are strong, but the paper’s impact is diminished by its failure to clearly differentiate itself from [19] in the foundational sections. A more focused introduction would align the contribution with the broader literature and clarify its significance. The reviewers recommend substantial revision to address this critical gap before acceptance.
