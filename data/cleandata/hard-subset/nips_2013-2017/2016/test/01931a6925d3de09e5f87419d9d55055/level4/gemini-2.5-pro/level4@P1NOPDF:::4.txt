This paper introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, a model based on Riemannian manifolds, as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) procedure for the model's parameters and evaluate their method on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper is generally well-written, with a sound theoretical basis and solid experimental validation. However, the primary weakness of this work is its limited novelty when compared to prior art, specifically the work of Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The distinction between the proposed method and this state-of-the-art approach is not clearly articulated until the conclusion. To properly contextualize the paper's contributions, these key differences should have been established much earlier, beginning in the introduction. Notably, reference [19] is not cited in the introduction at all, making it difficult for the reader to understand the specific gap in the literature that this paper aims to address.
