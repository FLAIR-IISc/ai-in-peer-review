- Novelty: Replaces Mahalanobis distance with a learned, data-driven geodesic distance, bridging manifold learning and probabilistic modeling.

- Theoretical foundation: Defines a parametric maximum-entropy distribution over a non-parametrically learned Riemannian space, grounded in geometry.

- Generative capability: Produces a full generative model that defines a continuous density over the ambient space, not just an embedding.

- Clarity and motivation lacking: Some modeling choices feel ad-hoc and insufficiently justified; roles of different covariance matrices are not clearly explained.

- Computational cost and scalability concerns: Inference requires repeated ODE solves and Monte Carlo integration, with no complexity or runtime analysis.

- Limited experimental validation: Experiments are preliminary, narrow in scope, and lack comparisons to a broader set of modern generative models.

- Metric construction question: The kernel-weighted local covariance metric in Eq. (7) needs stronger intuition or theoretical justification and comparisons to other metric-learning approaches.

- Covariance clarification needed: Distinguish the local diagonal covariance used to define M(x) from the LAND distribution covariance Σ on the tangent space and explain their relationship.

- Scalability/practicality requests: Provide complexity analysis in N, D, S and empirical runtime comparisons (e.g., vs. GMM) to assess practicality.

- Experimental improvement requests: Add comparisons to normalizing flows/VAEs, test on more diverse/higher-dimensional benchmarks, and include ablation/sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (notably kernel width σ).
