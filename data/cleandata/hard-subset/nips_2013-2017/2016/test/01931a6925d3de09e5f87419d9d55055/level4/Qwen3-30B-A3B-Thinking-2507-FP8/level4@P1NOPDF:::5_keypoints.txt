- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using learned geodesic manifold distances, replacing Mahalanobis distance and extended to mixtures.  
- Core idea of using geodesic distance is intriguing and reminiscent of Isomap.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) seems ad hoc; authors should justify the Gaussian-kernel weighting of local covariances.  
- Relationship between the local covariance in Eq. 7 and its MAP estimation in Eq. 11 is unclear—need clarification if they describe the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: synthetic results are underwhelming and EEG experiments lack comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Paper omits discussion of scalability and computational efficiency; must report computational complexity and runtime comparison to standard EM for Gaussian mixtures.  
- While theoretically interesting, practical applicability is questionable without addressing efficiency concerns.  
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, "d" should be replaced with "∇".
