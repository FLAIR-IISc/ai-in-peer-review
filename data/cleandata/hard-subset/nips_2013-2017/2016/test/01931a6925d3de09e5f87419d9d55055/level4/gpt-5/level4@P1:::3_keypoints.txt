- Introduces LAND: a Riemannian normal using a data-adaptive Riemannian metric and Monte Carlo normalization on tangent spaces.  
- Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning by learning a metric over RD rather than assuming a known manifold.  
- Uses gradient-based MLE for single components and an EM algorithm for mixtures; evaluated on synthetic and EEG sleep-stage data.  
- Strength: conceptually links manifold learning with generative modeling and provides practical estimation procedures.  
- Strength: sensible tangent-space Monte Carlo approach avoids boundary-value problems for log maps.  
- Strength: extends naturally to mixture models with an EM-style algorithm.  
- Strength: experiments show LAND captures nonlinear structure and avoids low-density mean issues.  
- Weakness: metric (Eq. 7) is hand-crafted with two hyperparameters (sigma, rho) fixed empirically and sensitive to sigma.  
- Weakness: lacks principled guidance or procedures for selecting/estimating sigma and rho, or for integrating metric learning into likelihood.  
- Weakness: chosen diagonal local covariance metric is not coordinate-invariant; rotations can change geodesics and densities.  
- Weakness: paper does not analyze the invariance issue or justify the diagonal restriction beyond efficiency concerns.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons may be unfair since LAND can fit manifold structure with fewer components while GMMs may need more.  
- Weakness: risk of overfitting when K is large or sigma is small; need to show behavior as K increases and use model-selection (AIC/BIC).  
- Weakness: computational cost is high (geodesic IVPs, MC normalization) and scalability limits (N, D, K, S) are not quantified.  
- Quality: method is technically sound but incomplete due to hyperparameter and invariance gaps and insufficient experimental control.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but needs deeper discussion of the metric, hyperparameter selection, and invariance; key supplement results should be in the main text.  
- Originality: novel application of Riemannian normals to a data-adapted metric over RD beyond known-manifold work.  
- Significance: potentially impactful as a Gaussian replacement for manifold-structured data, but needs stronger model selection, scalability analysis, and benchmarks.  
- Suggested improvement: provide principled selection/learning of sigma and rho with sensitivity analysis.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze invariance, consider full/low-rank local metrics, and relate to metric-learning literature.  
- Suggested improvement: redesign comparisons to let methods choose K via BIC/AIC and report overfitting behavior vs. K.  
- Suggested improvement: add runtime/scale studies and consider larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) to show limits and trade-offs.  
- Suggested improvement: visualize geodesics and densities under varying sigma to build intuition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant conceptual bridge, practical ML/EM algorithms, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: hand-crafted, non-invariant, hyperparameter-sensitive metric; uncontrolled experimental capacity comparisons; unquantified computational cost.  
- Post-rebuttal remark: even mild geodesic curvature can encourage overfitting for large K; recommend selecting K per method (e.g., BIC) and documenting LAND’s behavior as K increases.
