- Proposes LAND, a novel parametric metric over a nonparametric space connecting metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Learns a Riemannian normal distribution (per Pennec) with a maximum likelihood algorithm to estimate mean and covariance.  
- Effectively defines a Mahalanobis-like distance on Riemannian manifolds using geodesic distances and a learned local metric.  
- Local metric is learned from inverses of local (diagonal) covariance matrices; extension to mixtures of LANDs is presented.  
- Contribution: provides valuable insights linking metric learning and Riemannian statistics and yields a generative model that can generalize beyond training data.  
- Primary concern: scalability and applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data due to per-point local covariance computation and inversion (computationally expensive and potentially unstable).  
- Paper lacks formal computational complexity analysis and clarity on practical dimensionality limits (e.g., 10, 100, or more dimensions).  
- Needs better contextualization within metric learning literature and explicit comparisons to related methods (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Local PCA).  
- Kernel bandwidth (sigma) selection and its influence are inadequately analyzed; unclear if sigma is global or adapts to local density and robustness is not studied.  
- Experimental comparisons are weak: main figures compare against GMMs (a weak baseline) while intrinsic estimator results in the supplement outperform GMM and should be moved to the main paper.  
- Should include a computational complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify trade-offs for different data types and scales.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a compelling extension for modeling multimodal real-world data and merit further expansion.  
- Overall assessment: paper is exceptionally well-written and enjoyable to read despite the stated methodological and evaluation concerns.
