This paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as an alternative to Gaussian mixture models. The distribution is defined on Riemannian manifolds, using a locally adaptive metric, and the authors develop a maximum likelihood estimator for its parameters. They evaluate the approach on both synthetic and real-world data. Overall, the paper is clearly written, the theory appears sound, and the experiments are well executed.

Relation to prior work: While the paper is grounded in the literature on Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, its novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19] is not convincingly established. The differences from the state of the art are not clearly articulated until the conclusion, and [19] is not cited in the introduction. These comparisons should be made explicit much earlier to position the contribution.

Strengths:
- Clear presentation and generally good organization.
- Solid theoretical development, including an MLE procedure for the proposed distribution.
- Empirical evaluation on both artificial and real data that demonstrates the method’s practical utility.
- The approach is motivated by locally adaptive geometry, which is a natural fit for data near nonlinear manifolds.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The paper does not convincingly differentiate its contribution from prior mixtures of Riemannian normal distributions.
- The key distinctions from related work are deferred to the end of the paper. This diminishes the perceived contribution and makes it harder for readers to understand what is new.
- Reference [19] is missing from the introduction, where the related work context should be established.

Suggestions:
- Clearly state, in the introduction, how the proposed model differs from Simo-Serra et al. [19] and other closely related approaches. Explain the methodological distinctions and why they matter, and summarize any empirical advantages observed.
- Add [19] to the introduction and discuss it alongside other core references to situate the contribution early.
- Briefly preview (in the introduction) the main technical contributions and how they address limitations in prior work, so the reader can track these points throughout.

Overall assessment:
- Quality: Good theoretical grounding and careful empirical evaluation.
- Clarity: Generally clear and well written, but the positioning relative to [19] should be improved early in the paper.
- Originality: Weaker; the contribution over Simo-Serra et al. [19] is not sufficiently emphasized or demonstrated in the current presentation.
- Significance: Potentially useful, particularly for modeling data near nonlinear manifolds, but the paper would benefit from a sharper articulation of novelty and advantages over prior Riemannian mixture models.

Pros for acceptance:
- Sound methodology with a principled MLE estimator.
- Strong experimental section demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Clear and readable exposition.

Cons for acceptance:
- Insufficiently differentiated from closely related prior work, especially [19].
- Key comparisons and claims of novelty are presented too late in the paper.
- Missing critical citation in the introduction.
