- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold distance replacing Mahalanobis, and extends to mixtures; evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.
- Idea of using geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is interesting, but the paper suffers from clarity issues.
- The inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; the authors should provide more intuition for using Gaussian kernel weights in the weighted sum of local covariances.
- Need explanation why that particular kernel and weighting scheme is appropriate for defining the metric and how to interpret it geometrically.
- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is confusing; clarify whether they are the same object and align notation through the derivations.
- Experimental evaluation is limited; main concerns are scalability and computational efficiency—report computational complexity and actual running times versus standard EM for Gaussian mixtures.
- Practical utility is unclear without stronger empirical evidence on efficiency and more comprehensive experiments.
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, the notation should be changed from d to ∇.
