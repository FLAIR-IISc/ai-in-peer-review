

The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, grounded in Riemannian Manifold theory. It proposes a maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) for the distribution's parameters and validates the approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets. The manuscript is well-written, with sound theoretical foundations and well-designed experiments. However, the claimed novelty is insufficiently differentiated from the state-of-the-art. Specifically, the differences between the proposed method and Simo-Serra et al. [19] are not clearly articulated in the introduction or early sections, but only emerge near the conclusion. Reference [19] is absent from the introduction, which undermines the contextualization of the contribution. The authors should explicitly clarify the technical distinctions from prior work, particularly [19], at the outset to establish the novelty of their approach.
