- Presents a novel curved normal distribution using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric to better capture data manifolds.
- The claimed "learning a metric" is misleading: the metric is predefined in eq.7 with hyperparameters rho and sigma fixed a priori.
- Sigma is likely sensitive; the paper needs intuition, sensitivity analysis, and a clear selection procedure for sigma (or learning rho and sigma jointly with LAND).
- The metric in eq.7 is not coordinate invariant; the paper should discuss invariance properties and the intuition behind the chosen metric.
- The manuscript lacks connection to existing metric learning and manifold learning literature tied to Riemannian geometry.
- The first experiment’s comparison to GMM is flawed: LAND’s flexibility can outperform with fewer components but risks unfair comparison unless component counts are chosen optimally per method.
- Recommend redesigning experiments to select optimal component numbers (e.g., via BIC) and then compare performance, including studying LAND’s overfitting behavior as components increase.
- Need quantitative measurements of computational cost and scalability of LAND versus Gaussian mixtures; report limits of feasible scale.
- Suggest an experiment on MNIST (or similar) comparing LAND and GMM to illustrate performance and computational trade-offs.
- Even after author response, concern remains that increasing components makes local LANDs more prone to overfitting because geodesics, while nearly straight, are still curved—revise experiments accordingly.
