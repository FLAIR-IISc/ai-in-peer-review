## Review:

The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) based on geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance serves as a replacement for the traditional Mahalanobis distance within the Gaussian distribution, and the work extends this concept to a mixture of LANDs. The method is evaluated using both a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset. 

The core idea of employing a geodesic distance, reminiscent of Isomap, is genuinely interesting. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. Specifically, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears somewhat arbitrary. The authors should elaborate on the rationale behind utilizing a Gaussian kernel for weighting the local covariance sum. Furthermore, the distinction between the local covariance matrix defined in Eq. (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear; it should be explicitly clarified whether these represent the same quantity. 

The experimental evaluation presented is rather limited, but a significant concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. The authors should address the computational complexity and provide a comparative runtime analysis against standard Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms. While the paper presents a theoretically compelling contribution, its practical applicability remains questionable.

Finally, a minor point: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, 'd' should be replaced with '\nabla'.
