- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): a parametric Riemannian normal on a learned data-adaptive metric in RD.  
- Replaces Euclidean Gaussian metric with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric favoring high-density regions.  
- Defines a nonparametric metric M(x) as inverse of local diagonal covariances estimated via a Gaussian kernel.  
- Uses tangent-space Monte Carlo via exponential maps to estimate the normalization constant.  
- Derives gradient-based updates for mean and covariance and an EM algorithm for mixtures of LANDs.  
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning and relates to local metric learning and local PCA methods.  
- Conceptual novelty: principled maximum-entropy “Gaussianization” on a learned manifold.  
- Practical modeling choices: tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids expensive boundary-value problems for log maps.  
- Empirical evidence: fits nonlinear structure better than GMMs on synthetic data and improves EEG sleep-stage clustering.  
- Useful extension: mixture LANDs and EM formulation appropriate for complex real data.  
- Writing is clear and accessible.  
- Major scalability concern: computing M(x) and Monte Carlo evaluations imply O(ND) per sample without sparsification; complexity analysis and runtimes are missing.  
- High-dimensionality concern: method shown only in low D (2D, 5D); unclear practical limits and remedies for D≈10–100 or N≥1e5.  
- Sensitivity to kernel bandwidth σ and regularizer ρ: limited analysis; needs reporting of σ, ρ for all experiments and robustness curves.  
- Monte Carlo normalization adds variance to objective and gradients; variance vs. S, bias/unbiasedness of gradients, convergence behavior, and variance-reduction methods are not quantified.  
- Invariance issue: diagonal metric is not invariant to linear transforms and may underfit anisotropic local structure; prewhitening or full local covariances should be discussed.  
- Clarity gaps: EM algorithm details, handling of component-specific normalization constants, step sizes, stopping criteria, and initialization are relegated to supplement.  
- Numerical stability: need report on choice/impact of ρ and determinant computations for M(x).  
- Related work: expand discussion of prior local metric learning and local covariance methods to clarify differences (continuous generative density, max-entropy, normalization).  
- Significance: promising for structured generative modeling but needs computational comparisons to intrinsic estimators and GMMs and expanded mixture experiments (e.g., ARI, cluster purity).  
- Specific implementation questions: how neighbor search and sparsification (k-NN, KD-trees, approximate methods) are implemented.  
- Need to report typical Monte Carlo sample sizes S and their runtime/accuracy trade-offs; consider quasi-MC or control variates.  
- Potential failure modes: whether LAND mean can drift to low-density regions despite normalization penalty remains unexamined.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: consider learning or adapting σ (per-point or via marginal likelihood/cross-validation).  
- The reviewer’s recommendation: borderline accept conditional on added complexity analysis, sensitivity/runtimes/convergence studies, and moving key visualizations/mixture details into main text.
