- Novel and potentially useful application: proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distributions (LAND) to model observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and curved normal distribution.  
- Metric claimed as "learned" is actually hand-crafted (Equation 7) with hyperparameters ρ and σ; need discussion of σ sensitivity and principled selection.  
- Recommend joint learning: consider integrating estimation of ρ and σ into the main LAND optimization for a more data-driven model.  
- Invariance concern: the diagonal metric is not rotationally invariant—need discussion of rotational invariance, trade-offs, and intuition for choosing the diagonal form versus a full metric.  
- Unfair experimental comparison to GMMs: LAND’s flexibility vs. GMM with more components; suggest model selection (e.g., BIC) to compare each method’s best model.  
- Overfitting analysis requested: evaluate LAND behavior with many components to show whether residual curvature from local noise leads to overfitting.  
- Computational cost and scalability are not quantified: report algorithmic complexity, practical limits on dimensionality/sample size, and test on larger benchmarks (e.g., MNIST) with GMM comparison.  
- Literature positioning: more thoroughly relate the method to existing metric learning and manifold learning work to clarify novelty and contributions.
