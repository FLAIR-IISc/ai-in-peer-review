Summary: This paper proposes a locally adapted normal distribution based on geodesic manifold traversal distances, which is optimally learned from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. An extension for LANDs mixing is also provided. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset as well as a real-world dataset of EEG measurements. Key comments: The proposed idea of using geodesic distances, similar to iso-maps, is very interesting. However, there are some weaknesses in the clarity of the paper. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in equation (7) seems ad hoc. Can the authors provide more intuition on the idea of using a Gaussian kernel for weighting in the weighted sum of local सहप्रसरण? Furthermore, I got lost between the definition of the local सहप्रसरण matrix in equation (7) and the MAP estimate of the same quantity later in equation (11). Do these refer to the same quantity? Subsequently, the experimental evaluations are quite poor. But my main concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the method. For example, what is the computational complexity and running time compared to standard EM?
