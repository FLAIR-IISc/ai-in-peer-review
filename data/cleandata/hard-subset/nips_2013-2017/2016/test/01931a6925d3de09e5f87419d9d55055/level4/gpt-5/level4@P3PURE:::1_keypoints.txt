- Contribution: Introduces LAND, a parametric Riemannian metric based on the Riemannian normal (Pennec) with ML estimation of mean/covariance, geodesic distances, local diagonal covariances, and mixtures—providing fresh links between metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Positive: Well written; offers valuable parametric, generative models that can extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Main concern: Applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data—computing a covariance (and inverse) per point is challenging and the paper lacks explicit computational complexity analysis and practical limits (what counts as “high-dimensional”).  
- Literature positioning: Needs stronger positioning against metric-learning work (e.g., Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and methods using local covariances/local PCA to clarify similarities and differences.  
- Locality kernel σ: Unclear how choice of Gaussian bandwidth σ affects results, whether σ is constant across density regions, and robustness to σ—only discussed for one dataset; requires systematic analysis.  
- Figures/comparisons: Visual comparisons focus on GMMs (which perform poorly); intrinsic estimators outperform GMMs in the supplement, so main-paper visuals should include intrinsic model comparisons for more informative evaluation.  
- Complexity vs intrinsic estimators: The paper should analyze computational trade-offs between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice by data structure and conditions.  
- Mixtures of LANDs: The mixture model aspect is particularly interesting but underdeveloped—needs expanded conceptual discussion and more empirical evaluation.
