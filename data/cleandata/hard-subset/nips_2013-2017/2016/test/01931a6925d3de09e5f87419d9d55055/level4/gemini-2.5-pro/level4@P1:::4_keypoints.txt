- Introduces LAND: a generative model replacing Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance on a learned Riemannian manifold for data on/near non-linear manifolds.  
- Constructs a data-dependent, non-parametric Riemannian metric that is locally adaptive and favors high-density regions.  
- Derives the LAND probability density (Riemannian normal) and highlights the intractable normalization constant.  
- Proposes a scalable Monte Carlo integration scheme on the tangent space to estimate the normalization constant.  
- Develops an MLE procedure for mean and covariance using block coordinate descent and manifold-aware optimization.  
- Extends to mixtures of LANDs and derives an EM algorithm for inference.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification shows clear improvements over standard GMMs.  
- Strength: Conceptual novelty—synthesizes manifold learning and Riemannian statistics to build principled generative models on learned manifolds.  
- Strength: Technical soundness—thorough theory, max-entropy formulation, and a practical MLE with Monte Carlo for normalization.  
- Strength: Clarity and organization—well-written, includes a helpful Riemannian geometry primer and logical progression of ideas.  
- Strength: Convincing experiments—synthetic and real-world results demonstrate advantages over GMMs.  
- Weakness: Late positioning relative to prior work (notably Simo-Serra et al. [19])—distinction that the manifold is learned is not emphasized early.  
- Weakness: Novelty unclear in the introduction—readers may mistake the contribution as minor since related Riemannian-normal work is not introduced upfront.  
- Suggestion: Revise the introduction to clearly contrast with prior work on Riemannian normals (e.g., Pennec [15], Simo-Serra et al. [19]) and emphasize that the manifold geometry is learned from data.  
- Suggestion: Move the claim about Simo-Serra et al. [19] mishandling normalization constants earlier as motivation if accurate, since it differentiates and motivates the proposed MLE approach.
