- Introduces a novel, well-motivated extension of the multivariate normal by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric for manifold settings.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical foundation and a maximum-likelihood–type parameter estimation procedure.  
- Extends the approach to mixtures of Locally Adaptive Normal Distributions (LANDs).  
- Experiments are promising and show LANDs can compete with standard Gaussian Mixture Models.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and contains an excellent, comprehensive bibliography.  
- The topic is highly relevant to the conference and makes a significant contribution to machine learning.  
- Minor weakness: some mathematical derivations need more expansion or clarification for readers without strong differential geometry background.  
- Minor weakness: additional experiments or comparisons would further validate the efficacy of LANDs.  
- Overall judgment: strengths far outweigh weaknesses, with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
