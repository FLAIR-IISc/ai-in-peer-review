- Proposes LAND: a locally adaptive normal distribution on Riemannian manifolds using a smoothly learned local metric to model data near nonlinear manifolds.  
- Replaces Euclidean metric with a data-driven Riemannian metric to capture nonlinear, potentially low-dimensional structure.  
- Develops MLE using gradient descent combined with Monte Carlo integration to estimate normalization constants.  
- Extends the approach to mixture models via an EM algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and EEG sleep-stage data, outperforming Gaussian mixture models on studied datasets.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and grounded in Riemannian geometry and manifold learning with a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, offering a parametric distribution built on a non-parametric metric; Monte Carlo normalization is practical; mixture extension adds versatility.  
- Limitation: computational cost of evaluating logarithm maps is acknowledged and discussed.  
- Weakness: novelty relative to prior work (notably Simo-Serra et al. [19]) and differences from other metric-learning approaches (e.g., Hauberg et al. [9], [22]) are not clearly or early enough articulated.  
- Recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to better clarify novelty and expand related-work comparisons.
