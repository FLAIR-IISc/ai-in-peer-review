- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal (LAN) distribution using Riemannian manifolds as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models.
- Derives a Maximum Likelihood Estimator for LAN parameters and validates it on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-written with a clear mathematical formulation and theoretically sound derivations.
- Experimental results demonstrate LAN’s ability to model complex data effectively.
- Novelty over existing work, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19], is not clearly highlighted or positioned early in the paper.
- Introduction lacks context on non-parametric density estimation and omits discussion of Simo-Serra et al. [19].
- The paper fails to explicitly compare LAN to related methods in terms of methodology, assumptions, computational cost, or applicability.
- Needs a more intuitive explanation of why Riemannian-manifold modeling improves practical performance.
- Experimental section lacks statistical significance testing to support performance differences.
- Ablation study is limited; sensitivity analysis of key LAN parameters is missing.
- Strengths: solid Riemannian foundation, clear MLE derivation, demonstrated empirical performance, and readable presentation.
- Weaknesses: insufficient novelty positioning, poor contextualization in the introduction, no significance tests, and limited parameter sensitivity analysis.
- Overall: promising contribution but weakened by unclear novelty and lack of detailed comparisons; recommend weak accept with major revisions.
- Recommended revisions: expand the introduction and related work (include [19]), clearly state advantages/disadvantages vs prior methods, add statistical significance testing, and include an ablation/sensitivity study.
