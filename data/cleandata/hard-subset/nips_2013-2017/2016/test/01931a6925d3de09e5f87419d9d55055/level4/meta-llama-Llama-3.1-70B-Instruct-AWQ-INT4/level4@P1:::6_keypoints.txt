- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replacing Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance.  
- Defines a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x) and uses exponential/logarithm maps to compute geodesic distances.  
- Uses Euler–Lagrange equations to estimate geodesic distances along the manifold.  
- Applies geodesic-based Mahalanobis distances in a normal distribution framework.  
- Derives MLE estimators for mean and covariance; closed-form solutions unavailable.  
- Proposes a steepest-descent algorithm to estimate mean and covariance numerically.  
- Introduces a Monte‑Carlo approximation to estimate the normalization constant.  
- Evaluates LAND on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task versus standard GMMs.  
- Empirical results show LAND adapts to local manifold structure and improves modeling.  
- Technical approach is sound with well-explained manifold techniques and clever algorithms.  
- Novelty arises from combining metric estimation, geodesic computation, and distribution learning.  
- Potential impact is significant for modeling manifold-structured data.  
- Needs further evaluation, especially when data come from single normal distributions or GMMs.  
- Application analysis is lacking, particularly regarding choice and sensitivity of the sigma parameter.  
- Explanation of the practical usefulness of the learned distribution should be expanded.  
- Clarity is generally good but some notation needs fixing (e.g., overloaded Sigma).  
- Relationship between the kernel matrix and the fitted covariance matrix should be made explicit.  
- Suggestions: clarify notation, analyze sigma choice, expand usefulness discussion, and provide more comparative evaluations.
