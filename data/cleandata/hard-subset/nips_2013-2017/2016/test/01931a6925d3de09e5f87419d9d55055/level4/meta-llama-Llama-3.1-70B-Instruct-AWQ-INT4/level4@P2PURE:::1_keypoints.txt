1. Proposes LAND, a novel parametric metric combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics, estimating a Riemannian normal (mean and covariance) and using inverse local diagonals as the metric; also considers mixtures.

2. Major concern about scalability/applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because computing and inverting a per-point (diagonal) covariance is challenging — needs complexity analysis and a clear definition of "high-dimensional" (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100).

3. Paper needs a more comprehensive positioning within the metric learning literature, clarifying relations to prior works (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) that also learn per-point diagonal metrics.

4. The Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma used to define locality is insufficiently explored; robustness tests are needed and investigation of whether sigma should vary across density regions.

5. Comparisons and visualizations are weak: replace or supplement GMM visual results with intrinsic estimator results (which outperform GMM) in the main paper for more informative comparison.

6. An explicit analysis of computational complexity comparing LAND to intrinsic estimators is needed to determine method suitability for different data structures.

7. The mixtures-of-LANDs extension is promising for real-world multi-component data and deserves further development and emphasis.

8. Overall the paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.
