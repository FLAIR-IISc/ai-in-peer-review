- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in a normal distribution with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric using geodesic distance.  
- Presents MLE via gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to mixture models with an EM algorithm; shows results on synthetic data and EEG sleep stages.  
- The idea of using geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is an interesting theoretical contribution.  
- Equation (7)'s inverse metric tensor seems ad hoc; no sufficient intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances.  
- Presentation is confusing about whether the local covariance in Equation (7) is the same as the parameter estimated in Equation (11).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: lacks comparisons to relevant baselines and clear demonstration of practical advantages.  
- Major concern: no detailed computational complexity or run-time comparisons to standard EM, so scalability and efficiency are not assessed.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 uses "d" for gradients in lines 4–5; this should be replaced with "∇" for standard notation.
