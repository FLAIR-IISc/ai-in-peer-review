- Proposes LAND, a parametric Riemannian-normal metric linking metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics, with ML estimation of mean/covariance and geodesic distances from local inverse-diagonal covariances.  
- Introduces a mixture-of-LANDs extension to model multi-component real-world data.  
- Valuable contribution: a parametric, generative metric that can generalize beyond training data.  
- Major concern: scalability to high-dimensional data—per-point covariance computation and inversion may be infeasible; needs detailed complexity analysis and an indicated effective dimensionality limit.  
- Needs clearer positioning within the metric-learning literature, esp. relative to Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, and local-PCA-style methods that also use per-point diagonal metrics.  
- Missing parameter-sensitivity analysis for the Gaussian kernel bandwidth (sigma): impact on results and whether sigma is global or adaptive is not addressed.  
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete/misleading: strong intrinsic estimators are omitted from main visualizations despite outperforming GMM in supplements and should be included.  
- Should provide computational-complexity comparisons between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify trade-offs.  
- The mixture extension is promising and merits further expansion and analysis.  
- Overall: well-written and interesting idea, but practical impact is weakened by concerns about scalability, literature positioning, and experimental evaluation.
