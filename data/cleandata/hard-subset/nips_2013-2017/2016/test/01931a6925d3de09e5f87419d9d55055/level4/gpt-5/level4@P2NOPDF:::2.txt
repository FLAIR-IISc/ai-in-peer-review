This concise paper generalizes the multivariate normal distribution by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The resulting model extends the normal distribution to the manifold-learning setting and appears broadly applicable, as demonstrated by the reported experiments. Notably, the paper presents a succinct and self-contained maximum-likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to mixtures of LANDS makes the approach a practical alternative to the standard GMM. The experiments are compelling and, at least to this reviewer, open a promising avenue for future research. The language, structure, bibliography, and alignment with the NIPS remit are excellent.
