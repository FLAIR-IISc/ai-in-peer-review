- Proposes modeling data manifolds via a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution—interesting application of Riemannian geometry to statistical learning.  
- Metric is not learned as the title suggests but constructed via Eq.7 with two hyperparameters (ρ, σ) fixed empirically.  
- σ likely sensitive; paper lacks discussion of its intuitive effect or a principled selection method—method feels incomplete.  
- Recommend merging metric-definition with main learning so ρ and σ can be learned from data.  
- Proposed metric may not be invariant to coordinate transformations; authors should discuss coordinate invariance and whether the full (non-diagonal) metric is invariant.  
- More intuition for the choice of Eq.7 and stronger connections to metric learning and manifold learning literature are needed.  
- Experimental comparison to GMMs is unfair: LAND is more flexible so better fits with fewer components is expected and not insightful.  
- Suggest fair model selection (e.g., BIC to choose number of components per method) before comparing best-selected models.  
- Concern that LAND may overfit when using many components or few points per component due to curved geodesics capturing local noise.  
- Paper lacks quantitative analysis of computational cost and practical scalability limits (dimensionality and sample size).  
- Recommend demonstrating scalability on larger standard datasets (e.g., MNIST) with a GMM comparison.
