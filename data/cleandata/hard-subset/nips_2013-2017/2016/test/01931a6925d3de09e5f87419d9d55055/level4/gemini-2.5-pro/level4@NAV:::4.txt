This paper introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models grounded in the principles of Riemannian Manifolds. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimator for the distribution's parameters and validate their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The manuscript is well-presented, with rigorous theoretical underpinnings and a thorough experimental evaluation.

However, the main weakness of the work is its limited novelty when compared to prior art, specifically Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The paper does not adequately articulate the key distinctions between the proposed method and the state-of-the-art until the conclusion. To better frame the contribution, these differences should be clarified at the beginning of the paper. Notably, reference [19] is absent from the introduction, where this contextualization is essential.
