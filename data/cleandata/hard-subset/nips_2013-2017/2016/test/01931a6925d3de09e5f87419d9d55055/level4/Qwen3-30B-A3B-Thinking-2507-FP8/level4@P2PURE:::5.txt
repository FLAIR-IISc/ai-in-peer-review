

Summary: The paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance in Gaussian distributions, with an extension to mixtures of LANDs provided. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG measurement dataset.  

Major comments: The idea of using a geodesic distance (analogous to Isomaps) is intriguing. However, the paper lacks clarity in several key aspects. First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) appears ad hoc; the authors should provide greater intuition for employing a Gaussian kernel in the weighted sum of local covariances. Second, the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Equation (7) and its MAP estimation in Equation (11) is unclear—do these represent the same quantity? Third, the experimental evaluation is insufficient. Most critically, the method’s scalability and computational efficiency are unaddressed: what is its computational complexity, and how does its running time compare to standard EM? While the theoretical contribution is promising, the paper’s practical applicability appears limited.  

Minor comments: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5: replace "d" with "\nabla".
