- Introduces LAND, a novel parametric metric within a nonparametric framework combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Learns a Riemannian normal distribution (Mahalanobis on manifolds) via maximum likelihood to estimate mean and covariance.  
- Defines distances using manifold geodesics and learns the metric from inverses of local diagonal covariances.  
- Extends the framework to mixtures of LANDs, enabling generative modeling beyond training data.  
- Provides insightful connections between metric learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data due to per-point covariance computation and inversion.  
- Requests detailed computational complexity and scaling analysis, and a clear definition of “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dims).  
- Calls for stronger positioning within metric learning literature (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA/Kambhatla) and building on Hauberg et al.  
- Needs clarification and robustness analysis of the Gaussian kernel parameter sigma, and whether a uniform sigma is appropriate across densities.  
- Notes inconsistency: sigma discussed for sleep data but not for synthetic experiments.  
- Requests inclusion of comparisons to intrinsic estimators in Figs. 5 & 6 (as in supplementary material) rather than only GMM, and a computational complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators.  
- Recommends further development of the mixtures-of-LANDs extension given its promise.  
- Overall assessment: paper is exceptionally well-written and a pleasure to read.
