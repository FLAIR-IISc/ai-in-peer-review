The paper proposes an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, introducing a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution based on Riemannian Manifolds. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for the distribution's parameters and evaluate their method using experiments on both artificial and real-world data. The paper is well-written, with sound theoretical foundations and well-designed experiments.

However, upon comparison with Simo-Serra et al. [19], the paper's novelty appears somewhat diminished. The differences between the proposed method and the state-of-the-art are not clearly articulated until the conclusion. It would be beneficial to explicitly highlight these distinctions in the introduction, providing readers with a clearer understanding of the paper's contributions. Notably, reference [19] is absent from the introduction, which may leave readers unfamiliar with the relevant background and context.

To strengthen the paper, the authors could reorganize the introduction to include a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19]. This would help to establish the novelty and significance of the proposed Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, allowing readers to better appreciate the contributions of the paper. By addressing this issue, the authors can enhance the overall impact and clarity of their work.
