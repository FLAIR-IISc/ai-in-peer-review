- Proposes modeling observations with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal (LAND) to better capture data manifolds.  
- Addresses how to define a Riemannian metric from high-dimensional observations and how to define/estimate a curved Gaussian on that geometry.  
- The metric is not learned but hand-crafted (Eq.7) with hyperparameters ρ and σ fixed empirically; σ may be sensitive and needs discussion on its effect and selection.  
- Ask whether metric learning could be integrated with LAND so ρ and σ are learned jointly.  
- The metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant; paper should discuss invariance, whether a full non-diagonal metric would be invariant, and provide more intuition.  
- Paper should relate to prior literature on Riemannian geometry, metric learning, and manifold learning.  
- First experiment’s comparison is weak: LAND needs fewer components so unsurprisingly wins; GMM could match with more components and LAND may overfit.  
- Recommend using principled model selection (e.g., BIC) to choose component counts for fair comparison and test overfitting by increasing components.  
- LAND is more computationally expensive than a Gaussian; the paper should provide quantitative runtime/scalability results and limits (e.g., applicability to MNIST).  
- After author response, reviewer reiterates LAND’s greater flexibility leads to higher overfitting risk (curved geodesics), and urges experiment revision.
