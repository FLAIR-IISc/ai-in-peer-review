- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean Mahalanobis distance with a data-learned Riemannian metric.  
- Learns the metric via a Gaussian kernel-weighted local covariance and frames LAND as the maximum entropy distribution under that metric.  
- Provides MLE using Monte Carlo integration and an EM algorithm for LAND mixtures; builds on Riemannian statistics and manifold learning but learns the manifold from data.  
- Originality: novel integration of Riemannian statistics with data-driven metric learning, extending prior Riemannian normal work.  
- Theoretical quality: sound mathematical development and justification; normalization handled via Monte Carlo is practical and motivated.  
- Algorithmic approach: Algorithm 1 is well-justified and theoretical properties of LAND are supported.  
- Empirical significance: shows potential to model nonlinear-manifold data better than Gaussian mixtures, with promising EEG sleep-stage results.  
- Clarity issues: definition of the metric tensor (Eq.7) seems ad hoc and lacks intuition for the Gaussian kernel-weighted local covariance choice.  
- Confusion between covariances: relationship between local covariance (Eq.7) and covariance in likelihood (Eq.11) is unclear and possibly inconsistent.  
- Experimental limitations: synthetic experiments lack rigorous comparisons, statistical testing, and model-complexity analysis is relegated to the supplement.  
- EEG experiments: missing comparisons to other manifold methods (Isomap, LLE) and insufficient sensitivity analysis for σ, which is critical.  
- Computational concerns: scalability and complexity are not analyzed; log-map evaluation is cited as a bottleneck but no concrete runtime or complexity comparisons to EM/GMM.  
- Specific concern 1: need stronger justification and intuition for choosing the inverse of a Gaussian-weighted diagonal local covariance as the metric.  
- Specific concern 2: must clarify whether the covariances in Eq.7 and Eq.11 are the same or represent different model components.  
- Specific concern 3: provide complexity analysis and empirical runtimes to show how LAND scales with data size and dimensionality.  
- Specific concern 4: thoroughly explore σ sensitivity in EEG experiments across subjects and parameter ranges.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and novel theoretical contribution with promising results, but requires clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation to confirm practical utility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel integration of Riemannian and data-driven manifold learning, solid theory, promising empirical EEG performance, and justified estimation algorithms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient intuition for metric choice, limited experimental comparisons and statistical rigor, unclear scalability, and covariance representation confusion.  
- Recommendation: borderline—accept if authors address clarity, experimental rigor, and computational scalability concerns.
