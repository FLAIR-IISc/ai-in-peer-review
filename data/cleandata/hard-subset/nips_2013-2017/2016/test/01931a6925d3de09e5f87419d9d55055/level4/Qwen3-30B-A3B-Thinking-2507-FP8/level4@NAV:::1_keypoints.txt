- Proposes LAND: a parametric metric within a nonparametric framework, combining metric/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics and defining a Mahalanobis-like distance via Riemannian normal distributions.  
- Computes pointwise geodesic distances using a learned local metric derived from inverses of local diagonal covariance matrices and examines mixtures of LANDs.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data—computing a diagonal covariance and its inverse per point may be computationally prohibitive.  
- Paper lacks a rigorous computational-complexity analysis and practical limits; needs to define “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dimensions).  
- Literature review is incomplete: prior work (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA/Kambhatla) already employ diagonal pointwise metrics or local covariances and should be discussed.  
- The role of the Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is underexplored—only tuned for sleep data; robustness across σ values and different density regions is required.  
- Inconsistency in comparisons: Fig.4 includes intrinsic estimators, but Figs.5–6 omit them in favor of GMM; supplementary shows intrinsic outperform GMM, so main-text visuals should include intrinsic estimators.  
- A comparative runtime/complexity analysis between LAND and intrinsic estimators would clarify when to prefer each method.  
- The mixture-of-LANDs extension is promising and merits deeper investigation given real data’s multimodality.  
- Overall presentation is excellent and the paper is very well written.
