- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): extends multivariate normal to a Riemannian manifold by learning a local metric from data and develops MLE for the model, with mixture and application examples.

- Key contribution: adapts Riemannian normal distributions to manifold learning by learning the metric from data rather than assuming a known manifold.

- Theoretical strength: sound mathematical derivations and a theoretically grounded bridge between Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.

- Empirical strength: demonstrates LAND capturing nonlinear structure on synthetic data better than GMM and shows improved EEG sleep-stage cluster alignment.

- Weakness — incomplete metric learning: metric is hand-crafted (eq.7) with fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ, no principled method to select or learn them.

- Weakness — lack of coordinate invariance: the diagonal metric is not invariant to coordinate transformations; paper does not address this geometric limitation or consider full metrics.

- Weakness — flawed experimental comparison: synthetic comparisons are unfair (fixed K for LAND vs. unrestricted GMM); model selection (BIC/AIC) not used to compare optimal component counts.

- Weakness — scalability concerns: computational cost (geodesic solving, Monte Carlo integration) not analyzed, no runtime/memory comparisons or large-scale experiments.

- Originality/significance: novel extension to data-driven setting but metric construction closely follows prior work (Hauberg et al. 2013), yielding moderate overall significance.

- Clarity: generally well-written with clear derivations, but practical implementation details (hyperparameter selection, experimental design) are underexplained.

- Quality: rigorous theoretical development but incomplete empirical validation undermines key claims about learning a metric and computational feasibility.

- Recommendation: reject in current form but possibly accept after major revisions addressing metric learning, invariance, fair experimental comparisons, scalability, and discussion of metric limitations.

- Requested revisions: (1) develop principled learning/optimization for σ and ρ, (2) address coordinate invariance and consider non-diagonal metrics, (3) redesign experiments to select model complexity fairly (e.g., via BIC), (4) provide computational complexity analysis and scalability tests on larger datasets, (5) discuss metric limitations and potential improvements.
