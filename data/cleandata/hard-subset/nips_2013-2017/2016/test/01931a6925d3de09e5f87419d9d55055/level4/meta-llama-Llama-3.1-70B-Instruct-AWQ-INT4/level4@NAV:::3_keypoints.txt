- Introduces using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric to define a curved normal (LAND) that better captures data manifolds.
- Addresses the challenge of defining a Riemannian metric for high-dimensional real-valued observations and estimating a curved Gaussian.
- Claim of "learning a metric" is misleading because the metric in Eq.7 is predefined with hyperparameters rho and sigma fixed empirically.
- Paper should explain intuitive effects and selection of sigma and consider learning rho and sigma jointly with LAND.
- The metric in Eq.7 is not invariant under coordinate transformations; discuss this limitation and invariance of the full metric.
- Needs a more comprehensive literature review on Riemannian metric learning and manifold learning.
- First experiment comparison is flawed: LAND’s flexibility gives an advantage with few components, while GMM can outperform with many components; experiments should allow each method to choose optimal component number (e.g., BIC).
- Provide quantitative evaluation of computational cost and scalability of LAND versus Gaussian estimation (e.g., feasibility on MNIST).
- Even though LAND is more flexible, increasing components can make local LANDs vulnerable to local noise and overfitting because geodesics are curved; revise experiments accordingly.
