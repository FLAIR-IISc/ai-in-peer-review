This concise paper generalizes the standard multivariate normal density by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. This results in a novel distribution suitable for manifold learning, demonstrating broad applicability as evidenced by the presented experiments. Notably, the paper provides a succinct yet complete derivation of a maximum likelihood parameter estimation procedure. Extending this to a mixture of Locally Adaptive Normal DensityS (LANDS) makes it a compelling alternative to the widely used Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are particularly promising and suggest numerous directions for future research. The paper’s language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS conference are all excellent.
