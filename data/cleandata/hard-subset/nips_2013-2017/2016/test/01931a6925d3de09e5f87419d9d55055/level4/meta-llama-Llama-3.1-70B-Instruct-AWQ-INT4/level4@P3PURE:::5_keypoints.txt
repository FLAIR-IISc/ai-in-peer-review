- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance replacing the Mahalanobis distance.
- Extends LAND to a mixture model and evaluates on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.
- The geodesic-distance idea (similar to Isomap) is interesting and novel.
- Clarity issues: the inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and needs more justification.
- Authors should provide intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances in the weighted sum.
- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear — are they the same quantity?
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; complexity and runtime compared to standard EM are not reported.
- Practical applicability is uncertain despite the theoretical contribution.
- Minor fix: in Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, replace 'd' with '\nabla'.
