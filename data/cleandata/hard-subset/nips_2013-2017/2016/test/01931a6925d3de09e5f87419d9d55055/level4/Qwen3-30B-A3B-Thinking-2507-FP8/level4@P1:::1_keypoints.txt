- Introduces LAND: a Riemannian-generalized multivariate normal using a locally adaptive metric from smoothed local inverse covariances to favor high-density regions.  
- Derives MLE for LAND parameters, a scalable Monte Carlo scheme for normalization, and an EM algorithm for LAND mixtures.  
- Advances prior work by applying Riemannian statistics to a learned manifold rather than assuming a known manifold.  
- Theoretical strength: shows LAND is the maximum entropy distribution under the learned metric, providing a principled generative model for nonlinear data.  
- Technical strength: clear, sound derivations for normalization, gradient updates (Eq. 12–13), and the mixture EM algorithm.  
- Empirical strength: synthetic and EEG experiments demonstrate LAND outperforms GMMs and intrinsic least-squares in capturing nonlinear structure and clustering/F-measure.  
- Presentation strength: exceptionally well-written, precise notation, logical flow, and effective figures; supplementary material is useful.  
- Scalability weakness: computing local covariance/inverse per data point is computationally prohibitive in high dimensions; paper lacks concrete dimensionality limits or analysis.  
- Parameter-sensitivity weakness: Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is critical but only analyzed for EEG; synthetic experiments lack robustness checks or density-adaptive σ strategies.  
- Comparative-visualization weakness: main figures compare primarily to GMM; intrinsic estimator visualizations should be included in primary results to strengthen comparisons.  
- Mixture-model weakness: mixture extension is promising but underdeveloped—paper should explain why LAND mixtures outperform GMMs on real-world, non-convex clusters.  
- Originality/significance: distinct and valuable advance in learned-manifold Riemannian stats with practical impact (e.g., sleep staging), though scalability limits reduce broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address dimensionality analysis, σ robustness, and improved comparative visualizations.
