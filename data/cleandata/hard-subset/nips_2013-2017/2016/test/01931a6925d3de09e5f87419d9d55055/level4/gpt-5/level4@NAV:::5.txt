Review- Summary: The paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution constructed using a data-driven geodesic manifold traversal distance, which replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance in a Gaussian model. An extension to a mixture model of LAND components is also presented. The method is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real EEG dataset.

Major comments: The idea of employing a geodesic distance, akin to Isomap, is compelling. However, the paper has some clarity gaps. First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc. Could the authors provide more intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight a sum of local covariance matrices? Additionally, the connection between the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear—do these refer to the same quantity? The experimental evaluation is rather limited. My primary concern is scalability and computational efficiency: what are the computational complexity and wall-clock time compared to standard EM? Overall, the theoretical contribution is interesting, but the practical applicability seems limited.
