- Introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, grounded in Riemannian manifold theory.
- Proposes a maximum likelihood estimator for parameter estimation.
- Validates the approach on synthetic and real-world datasets with a well-executed experimental design.
- The theoretical foundation of the method is sound.
- Fails to establish clear novelty compared to Simo-Serra et al. [19].
- Key distinctions from the state-of-the-art are inadequately explained and only clarified in the conclusion rather than the introduction.
- Reference [19] is missing from the introduction despite being central to the comparison.
- Authors should explicitly position their contribution relative to [19] early in the paper to justify significance.
- Strong technical execution is undermined by the lack of an early, clear articulation of methodological differentiation, leaving the novelty claim unsubstantiated.
