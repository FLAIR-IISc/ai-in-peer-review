- Proposes LAND, a novel parametric metric linking metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics via Riemannian normal distribution and ML estimation of mean/covariance.  
- Defines distances as geodesics on the manifold (à la Hauberg et al.) but innovates by learning the metric from inverses of local (diagonal) covariances.  
- Provides a parametric, generative model that can generalize beyond training data and connects metric learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Major scalability concern: computing and inverting a (diagonal) covariance per data point is challenging for large-scale, high-dimensional data; complexity/limitations need analysis.  
- Related-work coverage is insufficient—should better situate LAND amid metric-learning literature (e.g., Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and local-covariance methods like local PCA.  
- Choice and influence of Gaussian kernel parameter sigma is underexplored; needs comprehensive analysis (including synthetic data) and clarification whether sigma is global or varies with density.  
- Comparisons are incomplete: intrinsic estimators perform similarly but their visual results are omitted from main figures (only GMM shown); intrinsic visualizations should be included in the main paper.  
- Recommend a computational-complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to determine method suitability for different data structures.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a promising direction for real-world multi-component data and warrant further exploration.  
- Paper is well-written and a pleasure to read.
