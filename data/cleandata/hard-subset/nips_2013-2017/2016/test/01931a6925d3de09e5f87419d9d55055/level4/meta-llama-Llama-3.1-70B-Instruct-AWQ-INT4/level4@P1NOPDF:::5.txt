The paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. The authors also provide an extension to a mixture of LANDs. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated on a synthetic dataset as well as a real-world dataset of EEG measurements.

Major comments: The proposed idea of using a geodesic distance, similar to Isomaps, is very interesting. However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved for clarity. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears to be somewhat ad hoc. The authors could provide more intuition behind the idea of using a Gaussian kernel for the weights in a weighted sum of local covariances. Furthermore, the distinction between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear. Specifically, do these two equations refer to the same quantity? 

Additionally, the experimental evaluations presented in the paper are limited. However, my primary concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. For instance, what is the computational complexity and running time of the method compared to the standard EM algorithm? In my opinion, the paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution, but its practical applicability is limited.

Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, 'd' should be replaced with '\nabla'.
