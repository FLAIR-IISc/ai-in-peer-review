Summary: This paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) that leverages a geodesic manifold traversal distance learned from the data, replacing the traditional Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. An extension to a mixture of LANDs is also provided. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world EEG measurement datasets.

Major comments: The idea of utilizing a geodesic distance, akin to Isomaps, is intriguing. However, there are several areas where the paper's clarity can be improved. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears to be somewhat arbitrary. The authors should provide more insight into the rationale behind using a Gaussian kernel for the weights in the weighted sum of local covariances. Furthermore, the distinction between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear. It is essential to clarify whether these two equations refer to the same quantity.

Additionally, the experimental evaluations presented in the paper are limited. A more significant concern, however, is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. Specifically, it would be beneficial to provide an analysis of the computational complexity and running time in comparison to the standard EM algorithm. In my opinion, while the paper offers an interesting theoretical contribution, its practical applicability may be limited.

Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, "d" should be replaced with "\nabla".
