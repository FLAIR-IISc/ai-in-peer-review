This brief paper presents an extension of the common multivariate normal density, replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The resulting generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting demonstrates wide applicability, as illustrated by the reported experiments. Notably, the paper provides a concise and complete account of a maximum likelihood-type parameter estimation procedure. Furthermore, the extension to a mixture of Locally Adaptive Normal Distributions (LANDS) renders it a practical competitor to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are particularly exciting, opening a wide avenue for future research. Overall, the language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are excellent.
