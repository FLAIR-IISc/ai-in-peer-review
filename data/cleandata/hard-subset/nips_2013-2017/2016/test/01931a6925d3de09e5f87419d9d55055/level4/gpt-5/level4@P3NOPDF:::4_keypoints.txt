- Summary: Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) on Riemannian manifolds, derives an MLE, and evaluates on synthetic and real data with solid theory and convincing experiments.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and principled Riemannian formulation suitable for manifold-structured data.  
- Strength: Derivation of an MLE and inference procedure that appear technically sound.  
- Strength: Experiments demonstrate practical viability on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: Empirical results suggest the method effectively captures local structure.  
- Weakness: Novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19] is not convincingly articulated and is only discussed late.  
- Weakness: [19] is omitted from the introduction and related work, weakening positioning.  
- Weakness: Unclear under what conditions LAND outperforms GMMs (expressiveness, sample/parameter efficiency, robustness).  
- Weakness: Lack of early intuition about the inductive bias introduced by the Riemannian structure versus Euclidean Gaussian mixtures.  
- Weakness: Computational aspects (optimization complexity, convergence, scalability) are insufficiently discussed.  
- Question: What is the precise technical distinction between LAND and [19] (metrics fixed vs learned, local covariance definitions, differing assumptions)?  
- Question: How does LAND compare to a well-tuned GMM with similar effective parameters and to [19] on identical benchmarks?  
- Question: What are training and inference computational costs vs GMMs and [19] — wall-clock times and asymptotic complexity?  
- Question: Is the MLE identifiable and under what regularity conditions; are there failure modes in low curvature or high noise regions?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to initialization, step sizes, and manifold-related hyperparameters?  
- Suggestion: Articulate contributions and compare to [19] early in the introduction, e.g., a table contrasting assumptions, parameterization, optimization, and guarantees.  
- Suggestion: Include direct empirical comparisons with [19] on the same tasks using common metrics and statistical significance reporting.  
- Suggestion: Add ablation/diagnostic analyses isolating the benefit of Riemannian components (e.g., Euclidean variant, disable adaptivity).  
- Suggestion: Report computational trade-offs: runtime, memory, scaling with dimension/dataset size, convergence criteria, and typical iteration counts.  
- Suggestion: Move essential explanations and intuition for LAND earlier in the paper to clarify novelty sooner.  
- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity analysis and practical guidance for choosing metrics, initialization, and hyperparameters.  
- Minor: Ensure consistent notation and define all symbols on first use.  
- Minor: Ensure references cited in conclusion/results (especially [19]) are introduced earlier.  
- Minor: Add a schematic figure early contrasting GMMs, [19], and LAND to improve accessibility.  
- Overall: Solid theory and promising empirics but under-positioned relative to [19]; currently borderline acceptance but could be strong with clearer positioning, head-to-head comparisons, and computational/diagnostic details.
