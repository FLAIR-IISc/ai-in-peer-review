- Proposes LAND, a manifold-aware generalization of the multivariate normal using geodesic distances from a data-adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Presents full framework: metric construction, Monte Carlo estimation of normalization constant, gradient-based MLE for parameters, and EM extension for mixtures.  
- Positive: novel and well-motivated application of Riemannian geometry to density estimation on manifolds.  
- Concern: metric construction depends on hyperparameters (sigma, rho) set empirically; sigma (kernel bandwidth) is crucial but not learned or thoroughly analyzed.  
- Suggestion: discuss intuitive effects of sigma/rho and consider incorporating their learning into the MLE framework.  
- Issue: proposed metric is not invariant to coordinate transformations; need discussion and whether using full local covariances restores invariance.  
- Experimental critique: GMM comparisons use fixed component counts, which may be unfair; recommend model selection (BIC/AIC) for each method before comparison.  
- Suggestion: analyze behavior with many components to assess overfitting risk and how local geodesics approximate straight lines.  
- Scalability concern: computational cost from solving ODEs for exponential/log maps is high; provide quantitative cost analysis and limits on dimensionality/sample size.  
- Suggestion: include experiments on larger benchmarks (e.g., MNIST subset) to demonstrate practical applicability and scalability.  
- Prior work connection: strengthen relation to metric learning literature to better situate contributions.  
- Summary: elegant and promising model, but needs improvements on hyperparameter selection, metric invariance discussion, fairer experiments, scalability analysis, and literature connections.
