- Introduces LAND: replacing Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a smoothly varying, data-driven Riemannian metric; also presents a mixture version with an EM-style procedure and evaluations on synthetic and EEG sleep-stage data.
- Central idea (using geodesic distance akin to Isomap) is compelling but presentation lacks clarity.
- Construction of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) seems ad hoc; authors should provide stronger intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariance estimates.
- Need discussion of alternatives to the Gaussian-weighted covariance and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters σ and ρ.
- Unclear relationship between the locally defined covariance in Eq. (7) and the covariance used in MAP estimation in Eq. (11)—are they conceptually the same or different?
- Experimental section is limited and does not demonstrate practical applicability convincingly.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; request reporting of computational complexity, memory usage, wall-clock time, and explicit comparisons to standard Gaussian mixture EM.
- Overall, theoretical contribution is interesting but practical usefulness remains uncertain without stronger evidence on efficiency and robustness.
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, replace d with ∇.
