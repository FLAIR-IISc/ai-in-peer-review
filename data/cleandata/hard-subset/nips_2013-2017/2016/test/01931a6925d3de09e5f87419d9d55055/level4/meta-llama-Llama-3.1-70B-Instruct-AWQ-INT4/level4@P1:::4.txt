The paper proposes an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, namely a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution. The proposed distribution is based on Riemannian Manifolds. The paper proposes an MLE estimator for the parameters of the distribution and evaluates the method using experiments on both artificial and real-world data. The paper is well-written, with sound theory and good experiments. However, compared to Simo-Serra et al. [19], the paper does not provide a potent novelty. The differences between the proposed method and the state-of-the-art are not well explained until the conclusion. These differences should have been made clear from the introduction (Note that ref [19] is absent from the introduction).

- The paper proposes a novel distribution, the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, which is based on Riemannian Manifolds.
- The paper provides a sound theoretical foundation for the proposed distribution and its estimator.
- The experiments on both artificial and real-world data are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Weaknesses:
