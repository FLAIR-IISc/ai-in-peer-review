- Introduces LAND: replace Mahalanobis distance with learned geodesic manifold traversal; extends to M-LANDs; evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Geodesic-distance idea is theoretically interesting and potentially impactful.  
- Equation (7)’s metric tensor inverse looks ad hoc; needs deeper intuition and relation to underlying manifold.  
- Unclear connection between local covariance in Eq.(7) and MAP estimate in Eq.(11); unclear if Eq.(11) redefines or estimates the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient—lacks comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., GMMs, Isomap-based methods).  
- Paper omits discussion of computational scalability; must address theoretical complexity vs. standard EM and provide empirical runtime comparisons.  
- Current results don’t convincingly demonstrate practical advantages of LANDs in real-world settings.  
- Minor fix: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5 should use the gradient symbol (∇) instead of "d".  
- Overall: novel concept but limited by unclear derivations, weak experiments, and unaddressed computational concerns; requires substantial revision before publication.
