- Introduces LAND, a generative model replacing the Euclidean metric with a learned Riemannian metric for data on/near manifolds.  
- Learns the metric non-parametrically via inverse local, kernel-weighted diagonal covariances (inspired by Hauberg et al.).  
- Defines a Riemannian normal distribution and derives ML estimation using gradient descent plus Monte Carlo integration.  
- Extends the framework to mixture models with an EM algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and an EEG sleep-stage classification task.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of data-driven manifold learning with parametric Riemannian generative modeling.  
- Advantage: generative approach can generalize beyond training data and mixtures capture multimodal structure.  
- Technically sound: clear derivations and well-motivated optimization/Monte Carlo methods.  
- Scalability concern: diagonal covariance estimation may be unreliable in high dimensions; lacks formal complexity analysis and clear dimensionality limits.  
- Experimental baseline concern: main-figure comparisons rely on GMM (a weak baseline); stronger intrinsic LS comparisons are only in the supplement.  
- Hyperparameter concern: model sensitive to Gaussian kernel bandwidth (sigma); robustness and local vs global sigma choices are underexamined.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible.  
- Suggestion: include a computational complexity analysis and compare complexity to intrinsic LS estimators.  
- Suggestion: replace GMM visualizations in main paper with intrinsic LS visualizations from the supplement.  
- Suggestion: add a sensitivity analysis of sigma on synthetic datasets and discuss region-varying bandwidths.  
- Suggestion: better position the metric construction within the broader metric learning literature (local diagonal metrics, local PCA, etc.).  
- Suggestion: expand experiments on mixture-of-LANDs with more complex clustering tasks to showcase potential.
