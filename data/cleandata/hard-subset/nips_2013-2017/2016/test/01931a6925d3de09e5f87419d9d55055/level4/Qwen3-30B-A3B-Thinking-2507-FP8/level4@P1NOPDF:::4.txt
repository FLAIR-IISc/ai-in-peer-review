

The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, grounded in Riemannian Manifold theory. It proposes a maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) for parameter estimation and validates the approach through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets. The theoretical foundation is sound, and the experimental design is well-executed. However, the work fails to establish clear novelty compared to Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The key distinctions between the proposed method and the state-of-the-art are inadequately explained until the conclusion, rather than being clarified in the introduction. Crucially, reference [19] is missing from the introduction, despite being central to the comparison. The authors should explicitly position their contribution relative to this prior work early in the paper to justify its significance. Without this, the paper's novelty claim remains unsubstantiated, and the reader cannot assess how the method advances beyond existing approaches. While the technical execution is strong, the lack of a clear, early articulation of the methodological differentiation undermines the paper's core contribution.
