- Introduces LAND: a parametric metric on a nonparametric space combining metric/manifold learning and Riemannian statistics, formulated as a Riemannian normal distribution with ML estimation of mean and covariance.  
- Defines distances as geodesics on the manifold using learned local metrics (inverses of local diagonal covariances) and considers mixtures of LANDs.  
- Provides valuable connections between metric learning and Riemannian statistics and promotes parametric generative models that generalize beyond training data.  
- Major concern: scalability and applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional real-world data due to per-point diagonal covariance computation and inversion.  
- Paper acknowledges high-dimensionality but lacks a thorough complexity analysis and no clear threshold for what constitutes "high-dimensional" (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100).  
- Insufficient contextualization within existing metric learning literature; similar ideas (point-wise diagonal metric tensors, local covariances, local PCA) exist in prior work (e.g., Hauberg, Frome, Malisiewicz & Efros, Kambhatla).  
- Locality depends on Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ, but robustness of σ is only tested on sleep data and not on synthetic data; needs robustness analysis across low/high-density regions.  
- Experimental/visualization critique: main paper compares LAND to GMM in figures where intrinsic estimators perform better (per supplementary figs); suggest replacing GMM visuals with intrinsic estimator comparisons.  
- Recommends adding a computational complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify method preference by data structure.  
- Mixture-of-LANDs is a promising direction for multi-component real-world data and deserves further expansion.  
- Overall evaluation: paper is exceptionally well-written and a pleasure to read.
