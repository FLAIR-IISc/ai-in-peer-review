### Review

**Summary**

This paper introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, a novel probability distribution based on Riemannian manifolds, intended as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for the distribution's parameters and provide an empirical evaluation of the method's performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Strengths**

1.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is well-written and the core concepts are explained clearly. The theoretical development of the proposed distribution and its parameter estimation framework is methodologically sound.
2.  **Empirical Evaluation:** The authors have conducted a good set of experiments to validate their approach. The inclusion of both artificial and real-world data helps to demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses**

The primary weakness of this work lies in its positioning with respect to existing literature and the clarity of its novel contributions.

1.  **Insufficient Differentiation from Prior Art:** The paper fails to sufficiently distinguish its contributions from closely related work, most notably Simo-Serra et al. [19]. While the technical approach is sound, the novelty is not made apparent. The reader is left questioning what significant advancement this method provides over the state-of-the-art.

2.  **Poor Narrative Structure:** The key differences between the proposed method and existing techniques are not discussed until the conclusion. To properly evaluate the paper's contribution, a reader must understand its context and advancements from the outset. Delaying this critical comparison until the final section prevents the reader from appreciating the potential novelty of the work as it is being presented.

3.  **Omission of Key References:** A critical issue is the absence of Simo-Serra et al. [19] from the introduction and related work sections. Given the strong overlap in the problem space, this work should be introduced and thoroughly discussed early on. This would allow the authors to frame their contributions effectively and explicitly highlight the unique aspects of their proposed distribution and estimation procedure.

**Conclusion**

In summary, this paper presents an interesting and technically solid method. The work is well-executed in terms of its theoretical formulation and experimental validation. However, its overall impact is significantly diminished by a failure to properly contextualize its contributions within the existing literature. The lack of a clear and early comparison to key prior work, especially Simo-Serra et al. [19], is a major flaw that obscures the paper's novelty.

I would be willing to reconsider my evaluation if the paper were substantially revised to address these points. A revised version must clearly articulate its unique contributions and differentiate itself from prior art, beginning in the introduction.
