- Paper introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a learned Riemannian manifold and local covariance for nonlinear distance measures.
- Authors give a clear introduction to Riemannian geometry and describe using LAND for density estimation and clustering.
- Technical methods are sound and well-explained, including Euler-Lagrange usage and logarithm map estimation.
- Monte Carlo estimation of normalization constant and steepest descent algorithms are clever and well described.
- Novelty stems from combining metric estimation, geodesic computation, and distribution learning into a locally adaptive normal.
- Potential impact is significant for manifold-based density estimation and clustering.
- Experiments are limited: synthetic evaluations are fair but sigma parameter choice is not well justified.
- Real-data evaluation is insufficient; more comparisons with other clustering methods and high-dimensional tests are needed.
- Application analysis is weak and the practical usefulness of the learned distribution needs clearer explanation.
- Notation issues (e.g., overloaded Sigma) and unclear relation between local diagonal kernel and fitted covariance should be fixed.
- Overall recommendation is positive but conditional on addressing clarity, evaluation breadth, and notation concerns.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel useful extension, high technical quality, comprehensive background and application framing.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak application analysis, limited real-data evaluation, and confusing notation.
