The paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. An extension to a mixture of LANDs is also provided. The performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset as well as a real-world dataset of EEG measurements.

The proposed idea of using a geodesic distance, similar to Isomaps, is very interesting. However, there are a few weak spots in the clarity of the paper. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) seems ad hoc. The authors should provide more intuition on the idea of using a Gaussian kernel for the weights in a weighted sum of local covariances. Additionally, the relationship between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear. Specifically, do these refer to the same quantity?

Furthermore, the experimental evaluations are rather limited. However, my main concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the method. For instance, what is the computational complexity and running time compared to standard EM? To fully appreciate the practicality of the proposed method, a more detailed analysis of its computational efficiency is necessary.

In my opinion, the paper has an interesting theoretical contribution, but its practical applicability is uncertain. The authors should address the concerns mentioned above to strengthen their work.
