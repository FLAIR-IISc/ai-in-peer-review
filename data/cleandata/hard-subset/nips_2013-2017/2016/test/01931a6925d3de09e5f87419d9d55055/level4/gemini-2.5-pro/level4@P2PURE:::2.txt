This paper proposes a generalization of the multivariate normal distribution by replacing the standard Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The resulting model effectively extends the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, and its wide applicability is well-illustrated by the reported experiments.

Importantly, the authors provide a complete and succinct maximum likelihood procedure for parameter estimation. The extension to a mixture of these distributions makes the approach a practical and compelling competitor to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experimental results are impressive and open up promising avenues for future research.

The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and highly relevant to the NIPS community. Its language, bibliography, and overall quality are excellent.
