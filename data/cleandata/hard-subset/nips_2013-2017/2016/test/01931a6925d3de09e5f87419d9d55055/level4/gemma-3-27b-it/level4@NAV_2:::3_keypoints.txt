- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in a normal distribution with a learned Riemannian metric to better model data manifolds.  
- Tackles the problem of defining a Riemannian metric from high-dimensional observations and estimating a curved Gaussian on that geometry.  
- Core idea of constructing a data-adaptive Riemannian metric is well-motivated and builds on manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Metric construction (Eq.7) is largely hand-crafted with hyperparameters ρ and σ chosen empirically; needs discussion of σ’s impact and principled selection.  
- Suggests integrating metric learning with LAND estimation so ρ and σ can be learned rather than fixed.  
- Proposed metric lacks coordinate invariance; paper should discuss this limitation and consider full (non-diagonal) metric alternatives and intuition.  
- Paper should better engage related literature connecting Riemannian geometry, metric learning, and manifold learning.  
- Experimental comparison to GMM is unfair as LAND is compared with fewer components; recommend letting methods select component count (e.g., via BIC).  
- Recommend testing LAND with more components to assess overfitting and reporting computational cost and scalability (e.g., MNIST).  
- Strengths: novel geometry-based distribution estimation, theoretical grounding, promising synthetic and EEG results, and thoughtful implementation (Monte Carlo integration).  
- Weaknesses: hand-crafted metric without optimization strategy, lack of coordinate invariance, weaker experimental validation, and incomplete literature connections.  
- Overall: technically sound and innovative; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing metric construction and experimental validation concerns.
