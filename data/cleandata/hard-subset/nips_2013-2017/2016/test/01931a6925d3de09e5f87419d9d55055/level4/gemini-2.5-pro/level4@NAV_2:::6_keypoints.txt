- Introduces LAND: a Riemannian-generalized normal distribution using a data-driven, locally adaptive metric learned from the data.
- Fits parameters via maximum likelihood with gradient-based optimization and Monte Carlo integration in the tangent space to estimate the intractable normalization constant.
- Novel and elegant synthesis of manifold learning and parametric density estimation providing a principled generative model that respects data geometry.
- Methodology is technically sound; derivations and Monte Carlo approach are well-motivated and clearly explained.
- Geometric concepts (exponential/log maps, geodesics) are presented clearly for readers with basic Riemannian geometry knowledge.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: hyperparameter (kernel bandwidth σ) selection is not properly justified and appears to be tuned on test data.
- Baseline comparisons are limited; need experiments on genuinely linear data (Gaussian/GMM) to reveal failure modes or overfitting tendencies.
- Scalability concerns: all experiments are low-dimensional (2D, 5D); need higher-dimensional tests to demonstrate broader applicability.
- Notational/clarity issues: relationship between local metric M(x) and global covariance Σ is unclear and contradictory (supplement claims M = Σ⁻¹), undermining gradient derivations.
- Clustering assignment procedure in the sleep-stage experiment is not specified and should be stated explicitly.
- Modeling assumption differs from typical manifold learning: assumes data lie on a D-dimensional manifold in R^D rather than a low-dimensional d ≪ D manifold; implications should be discussed.
- Overall: promising and technically clever work that would be strengthened by improved experimental rigor and clarification of notation and assumptions.
