This paper proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a density estimation model based on Riemannian manifolds, which is presented as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) framework for the model's parameters and evaluate its performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper is well-executed in several respects. It is clearly written, the theoretical foundations are sound, and the experimental results are compelling.

However, the primary weakness of the manuscript is its positioning with respect to prior work, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The paper does not adequately clarify its novel contributions over this highly relevant state-of-the-art method until the conclusion. As a result, the reader is left questioning the unique contributions and significance of the proposed method for most of the paper.

To properly frame the work, these critical differences should have been established from the outset. A thorough discussion and comparison with [19] belongs in the introduction and related work sections, not reserved for the final pages. The fact that reference [19] is absent from the introduction further highlights this lack of contextualization. Clarifying the distinctions early on would substantially strengthen the manuscript and better highlight its specific contributions to the field.
