- Proposes using a hand-designed Riemannian metric and curved Gaussian (LAND) to model observation space and better capture data manifolds.  
- Claims the metric is "learned," but Equation 7 shows a hand-designed metric with fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ set empirically.  
- Paper should explain the intuitive effect of σ, provide guidance for selecting it, and investigate jointly learning ρ and σ with the LAND model.  
- Equation 7’s metric appears not coordinate-invariant (diagonal in some bases, off-diagonal after rotation); the paper must clarify whether the full metric is invariant and give deeper intuition.  
- Recommend connecting to prior work on Riemannian metric learning and manifold learning to situate the approach.  
- First experiment is unbalanced: LAND’s flexibility vs GMM component count skews results; each model should select component count (e.g., via BIC) for fair comparison.  
- LAND tends to overfit with many components; this observation is useful but obscured by current experimental design and needs clearer treatment.  
- Paper lacks quantitative analysis of computational cost and scalability—how much more expensive is LAND and what are its limits?  
- Suggest evaluating on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) to benchmark practical feasibility against GMMs.  
- Authors’ response about geodesics and overfitting needs to be explicitly revised in the paper; the suggested experimental redesign using BIC remains essential.  
- Summary of required revisions: clarify metric hyperparameter dependence and joint learning, address coordinate invariance and intuition for Equation 7, redesign experiments for fair model comparison, and add computational scalability analysis.
