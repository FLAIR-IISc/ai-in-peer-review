- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean distance in normal distribution with geodesic distance on a learned manifold to capture non-linear structure.  
- Extends LAND to mixture models and derives an EM algorithm for parameter estimation.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and real EEG datasets, showing some improvements over standard GMMs.  
- Core idea (using geodesic distances, akin to Isomap) is appealing and theoretically interesting.  
- Paper suffers from clarity issues that hinder understanding of key methodological choices.  
- Definition of the metric tensor (Eq. 7) appears arbitrary and lacks intuition.  
- Choice of a Gaussian kernel for weighting local covariances needs justification and discussion of bandwidth effects.  
- Unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. 7 and its estimation in the maximum likelihood step (Eq. 11).  
- Synthetic experiments lack detail on data distributions and parameter settings.  
- Missing analysis of computational complexity and runtime; geodesic/manifold computations may be costly.  
- Scalability and practical efficiency concerns, especially for high-dimensional data.  
- Theoretical foundation (Riemannian geometry and maximum entropy) is sound and principled.  
- Results are promising but not sufficiently convincing empirically; more thorough experiments needed.  
- Reviewer leans to weak reject but would reconsider if the manuscript addresses clarity, experimental detail, and computational analysis.  
- Minor suggestions: fix notation in Algorithm 1 (replace "d" with "∇") and analyze sensitivity to Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ.
