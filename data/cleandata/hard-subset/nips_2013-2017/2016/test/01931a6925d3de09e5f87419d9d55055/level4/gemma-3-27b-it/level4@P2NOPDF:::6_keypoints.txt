- Introduces LAND: a locally adaptive normal using a data-dependent Riemannian metric M(x), computing geodesics via exponential/logarithm maps and Euler–Lagrange, then applying a normal on the tangent plane to measure distances along the manifold.  
- Uses maximum likelihood for mean and covariance but relies on a steepest descent algorithm (no closed-form) and Monte Carlo approximation for the intractable normalization constant.  
- Evaluated on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task with comparisons to GMMs; reviewer finds the approach compelling and recommends publication.  
- Technical strengths: sound manifold technique application and clear exposition of Euler–Lagrange and log-map estimation; Monte Carlo normalization and steepest descent are inventive and well-described.  
- Technical concerns: application analysis feels incomplete; sigma parameter chosen via training optimization raises overfitting concerns; practical utility of the learned distribution needs clearer justification.  
- Novelty: primarily a novel combination of existing tools (kernel-defined M(x), geodesic estimation, normal on tangent plane) with a new algorithm for parameter and normalization estimation.  
- Potential impact concerns: unclear sigma selection affecting interpretability; needs comparisons on data from standard normals/GMMs, held-out test reporting for clustering (to avoid overfitting), experiments on very high-dimensional data, and clarification of the unusual assumption that manifold dimension equals observation dimension.  
- Clarity issues: confusing notation/usage of Σ and M (paper states M = Σ⁻¹ but also fits a local diagonal kernel Σ), unclear whether kernel is just initialization, steepest descent proof reinforces confusion, and cluster membership assignment by geodesic distance is not explicitly stated.
