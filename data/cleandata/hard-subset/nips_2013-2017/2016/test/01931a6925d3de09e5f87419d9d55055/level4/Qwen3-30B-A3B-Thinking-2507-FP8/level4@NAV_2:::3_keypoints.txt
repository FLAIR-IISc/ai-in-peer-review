- Proposes LAND: replacing Euclidean metric in Gaussians with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model nonlinear data manifolds.
- Develops MLE for LAND using gradient descent plus Monte Carlo integration and extends to mixture models with an EM algorithm.
- Theoretically grounded: LAND is shown to be the maximum-entropy distribution under the learned metric.
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification shows LAND can capture nonlinear structures better than standard Gaussians.
- Major weakness: the Riemannian metric (Eq.7) is hand-crafted with hyperparameters ρ and σ fixed empirically and lacking justification.
- Paper lacks analysis of how σ affects the metric and guidance for selecting this sensitive parameter.
- Authors should consider jointly learning metric hyperparameters with LAND parameters rather than fixing them.
- The metric is not coordinate-invariant (depends on data embedding); the paper must discuss this and whether a full non-diagonal metric would help.
- Experimental comparison with GMMs is flawed: models were compared at fixed K instead of allowing each method to select K (e.g., via BIC), making the comparison unfair.
- LAND’s vulnerability to local noise increases with more components; experimental design should reflect optimal component selection for fair comparison.
- Missing quantitative analysis of computational cost: no measurements of training time, memory, or scalability limits.
- Recommend demonstrating practicality on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) to establish real-world applicability.
