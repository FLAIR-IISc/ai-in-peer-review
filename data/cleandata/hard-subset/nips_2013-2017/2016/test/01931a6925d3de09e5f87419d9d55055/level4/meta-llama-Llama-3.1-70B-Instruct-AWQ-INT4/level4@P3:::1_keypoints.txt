- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a novel parametric metric on a non-parametric manifold bridging metric learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Extends the traditional normal distribution to manifold settings to model nonlinear data structures.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of theoretical background (Riemannian geometry, manifold learning).  
- LAND model is well-motivated and shown effective on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Thorough evaluation and detailed related-work analysis positioning the contribution within metric learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity of presentation, and comprehensive experimental demonstration.  
- Main concern: applicability and limitations of LAND in high-dimensional real-world data are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Lacks detailed discussion and analysis of computational complexity of the introduced metric.  
- Choice of locality parameter sigma is insufficiently explored; robustness and impact analyses are needed.  
- Comparisons are limited; more comprehensive comparisons with other methods, especially intrinsic estimators, are required.  
- Visual comparisons against GMM are provided but more visual/in-depth comparisons with intrinsic estimators should be included in the main paper.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address complexity in high dimensions, sigma choice/robustness, and broader method comparisons.
