- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using geodesic distances from a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x).
- Geodesics computed via Euler-Lagrange ODEs and exponential/logarithm maps to map points to tangent spaces.
- Defines distribution by applying a Gaussian in tangent space using Mahalanobis distance from log-mapped points.
- Derives MLEs for mean and covariance, uses steepest descent for parameter estimation and Monte-Carlo for intractable normalization constant.
- Technical methods (Euler-Lagrange, log-map estimation, estimation schemes) are sound and clearly presented.
- Steepest descent and Monte-Carlo estimation procedures are clever and well-described.
- Hyperparameter sigma selection is poorly justified and appears tuned on training data, risking overfitting.
- Paper lacks discussion of practical advantages and specific use cases for the learned distribution.
- Novelty mainly in synthesizing existing techniques (kernel-based metric, geodesic estimation, tangent-space Gaussian) into a single probabilistic model and estimation pipeline.
- Evaluation omissions: unclear sigma selection procedure, no comparison showing robustness when data come from a standard Gaussian or GMM.
- Clustering experiments likely suffer from overfitting—sigma tuned on entire dataset and reported without held-out test/validation splits.
- Would benefit from evaluations on high-dimensional datasets to strengthen claims.
- Major modeling limitation: assumes manifold dimension equals ambient dimension (d = D) with full-rank M(x), which conflicts with typical low-dimensional manifold assumptions and may limit applicability.
- Clarity issues: overloaded "Sigma" notation—unclear relationship between local metric M = Sigma⁻¹ and the global fitted covariance matrix.
- Cluster assignment procedure (e.g., using geodesic-distance-to-mean) is not explicitly specified.
- Despite issues, the introduction to Riemannian manifolds is well-written and accessible, and the reviewer believes the work is worthy of publication.
