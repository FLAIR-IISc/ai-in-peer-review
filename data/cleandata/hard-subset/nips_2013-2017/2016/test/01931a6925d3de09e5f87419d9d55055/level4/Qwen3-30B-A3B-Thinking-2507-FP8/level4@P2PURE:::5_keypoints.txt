- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using geodesic manifold traversal distance, plus a mixture extension, evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Using a geodesic distance (Isomap-like) in place of Mahalanobis is an intriguing idea.

- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; authors should provide intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances.

- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear—are they the same quantity or different?

- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: scalability and computational efficiency are unaddressed.

- Missing analysis of computational complexity and runtime comparison to standard EM.

- Although theoretically promising, the paper's practical applicability seems limited without the above clarifications and experiments.

- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace "d" with "\nabla".
