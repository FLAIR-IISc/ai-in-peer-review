- Proposes a novel approach using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution to better capture data manifolds.
- Claims a useful tool and interesting application of Riemannian geometry in statistical learning.
- Despite the title, the metric is hand-crafted (Eq.7) with two hyperparameters (rho, sigma) fixed empirically rather than learned.
- Sigma likely sensitive; authors should discuss its intuitive effect and selection strategy.
- Recommend merging metric learning with LAND estimation so rho and sigma can be learned jointly.
- The proposed metric in Eq.7 is not invariant to coordinate transformations; a discussion of invariance (or lack thereof) is needed.
- Paper lacks a comprehensive review of related work in metric learning and manifold learning connected to Riemannian geometry.
- First experiment comparison is flawed: LAND’s flexibility favors fewer components while GMM can match with many components, so comparisons should allow each method to select component counts (e.g., via BIC).
- Suggest testing how LAND behaves and overfits with a large number of components.
- Request quantitative experiments on computational cost and scalability of LAND versus Gaussian estimation (e.g., limits and feasibility on MNIST).
- After authors’ response, concern remains that local LAND components may be more vulnerable to local noise and overfitting despite near-straight geodesics; revise experiments accordingly.
