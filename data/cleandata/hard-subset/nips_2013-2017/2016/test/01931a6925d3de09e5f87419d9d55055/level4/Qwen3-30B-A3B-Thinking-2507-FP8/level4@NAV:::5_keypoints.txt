- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance to replace Mahalanobis distance, and extends this to mixtures of LANDs.  
- Evaluations are performed on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.  
- The use of a geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is an interesting theoretical contribution.  
- Equation (7)’s inverse metric tensor is not clearly justified—authors should provide deeper intuition for this choice.  
- The choice of a Gaussian kernel for the weighted sum of local covariances lacks rationale and needs explanation.  
- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear—are they the same quantity?  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency—complexity and runtime compared to standard EM must be reported.  
- Practical applicability appears limited given the unclear efficiency and scalability.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5 contain a typo—use '\nabla' instead of 'd'.
