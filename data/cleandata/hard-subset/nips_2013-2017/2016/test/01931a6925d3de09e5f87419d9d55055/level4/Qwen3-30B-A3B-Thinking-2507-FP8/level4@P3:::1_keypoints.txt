- Introduces LAND, a novel Riemannian generalization of the multivariate normal using a locally adaptive metric from diagonal local covariances.  
- Replaces Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric while preserving the maximum entropy property.  
- Derives a maximum likelihood estimation framework using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration to handle the normalization constant.  
- Extends the model to mixtures and provides EM updates.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification, outperforming GMMs and intrinsic least squares estimators.  
- Strength: technically sound with rigorous theoretical grounding linking manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Strength: careful treatment of normalization constant via Monte Carlo integration (important contribution).  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with consistent notation, helpful figures, and reproducible algorithmic details.  
- Strength: original contribution by combining Riemannian normal and locally adaptive metrics to produce a parametric generative model over a learned manifold.  
- Strength: practical significance demonstrated by improved EEG classification results.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability, especially for high-dimensional data (cost of diagonal covariance computations).  
- Weakness: limited comparison with broader metric learning literature (e.g., Frome et al., local PCA) — discussion is superficial.  
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity analysis for kernel bandwidth σ is limited to EEG experiments; synthetic experiments lack systematic σ study.  
- Weakness: confusing comparison with intrinsic least squares estimators; visual comparisons largely omit intrinsic estimators and focus on GMMs.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel and well-founded combination of methods, clear derivations, practical utility, and a workable Monte Carlo normalization approach.  
- Argument against acceptance: needs better scalability analysis, more comprehensive parameter sensitivity studies, deeper literature comparisons, and more consistent baseline visuals.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability and parameter-sensitivity concerns.
