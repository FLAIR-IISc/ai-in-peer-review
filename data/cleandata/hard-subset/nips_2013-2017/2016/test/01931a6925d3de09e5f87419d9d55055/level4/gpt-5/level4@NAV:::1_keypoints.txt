- Introduces LAND, a parametric metric over a nonparametric space linking metric/manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Parametric model is the Riemannian normal distribution (Pennec); authors present MLE for mean and covariance.  
- Resulting model yields a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds using geodesic distances with a learned local metric.  
- Local metric is learned from inverses of per-point local (diagonal) covariance matrices.  
- Authors also explore mixtures of LANDs, which the reviewer views as a promising direction.  
- Paper offers fresh perspectives and valuable generative, parametric extensions beyond training data.  
- Primary concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data—computing and inverting per-point covariances is challenging.  
- Paper notes high dimensionality briefly, but needs a careful computational-complexity analysis and a clear definition of “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 10, 100 dims?).  
- More thorough positioning in the metric-learning literature is needed; related prior work (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA) learns per-point diagonal metrics.  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma needs analysis: how sigma affects results, robustness checks, and whether sigma is uniform across low/high density regions.  
- Visual comparisons omit intrinsic estimators in main figures (Figs. 5–6); intrinsic-model visuals in the supplement outperform GMMs and should be shown in the main paper or alongside GMMs.  
- Recommend comparing computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators to clarify practical trade-offs and preferable data regimes.  
- Overall assessment: paper is very well written and enjoyable to read.
