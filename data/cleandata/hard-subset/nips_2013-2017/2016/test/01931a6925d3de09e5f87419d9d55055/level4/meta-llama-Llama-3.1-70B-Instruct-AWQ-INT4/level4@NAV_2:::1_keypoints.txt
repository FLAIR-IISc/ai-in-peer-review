- Proposes LAND: a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution combining metric learning/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Defines a Riemannian normal distribution (per Pennec) and proposes MLE for manifold mean and covariance (Mahalanobis on manifolds).  
- Distances are geodesics on a learned local metric; the metric is built from inverses of local (diagonal) covariances; mixtures of LANDs are considered.  
- Brings useful insight connecting metric learning and Riemannian statistics and offers parametric generative models that generalize beyond training data.  
- Major concern about scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because computing and inverting a per-point covariance is costly; needs complexity analysis and clear definition of “high-dimensional.”  
- Paper should better situate itself in the metric learning literature—similar per-point diagonal metrics exist (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and local covariances appear in local PCA.  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma for locality is underexplored: influence on results, robustness tests, and whether sigma is uniform across density regions are missing.  
- Comparison/visualization issues: Figs. 5–6 show GMM comparisons (which perform poorly) instead of intrinsic estimators that perform similarly to LAND in Fig. 4.  
- Supplementary material shows intrinsic model visualizations that outperform GMM; those should be moved into or added to the main paper.  
- Paper should include an analysis of computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators to guide method selection by data structure.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a particularly promising direction and deserve further expansion in the paper.  
- Writing quality is very good and the paper is pleasant to read.
