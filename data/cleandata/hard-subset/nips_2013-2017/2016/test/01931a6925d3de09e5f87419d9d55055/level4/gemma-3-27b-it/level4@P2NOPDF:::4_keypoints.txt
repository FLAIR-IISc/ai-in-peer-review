- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models within a Riemannian manifold framework.  
- Develops an MLE for LAND parameters and validates performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, theoretically sound, and supported by compelling experimental results.  
- Novelty appears incremental with respect to Simo-Serra et al. [19]; lacks clear differentiation.  
- Simo-Serra et al. [19] is omitted from the introduction despite being a closely related work.  
- The paper only clarifies distinctions from prior work late (in the conclusion), forcing readers to infer advantages from experiments.  
- Recommend articulating LAND’s specific advantages and how it addresses limitations of existing methods (including [19]) earlier, ideally in the introduction.  
- Framing the contribution relative to existing literature earlier would improve impact, clarity, and accessibility.
