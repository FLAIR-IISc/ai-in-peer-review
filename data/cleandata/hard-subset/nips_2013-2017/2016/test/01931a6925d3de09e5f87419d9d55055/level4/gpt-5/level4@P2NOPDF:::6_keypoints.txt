- Proposal: Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) on Riemannian manifolds using a smoothly varying metric M(x), geodesics, and exponential/logarithm maps to define local Gaussians in tangent spaces.
- Method: Geodesics computed via Euler–Lagrange ODEs; logarithm/exponential maps used to move between manifold and tangent spaces.
- Modeling: Defines Gaussian in tangent space using Mahalanobis distance induced by M(x); replaces Euclidean distances with metric-derived distances.
- Estimation: Derives maximum-likelihood estimators for mean and covariance solved by steepest-descent; normalizing constant approximated by Monte Carlo.
- Empirical: Experiments on synthetic manifold data and clustering compared to standard GMM show promise; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Technical strengths: Manifold/geodesic components and optimization/Monte Carlo approximations are sound and well presented.
- Empirical concerns: Kernel bandwidth σ appears tuned on training/evaluation data and selection procedure is not made explicit, risking biased results.
- Comparative evaluation: Missing baseline tests on data from single Euclidean Gaussian or GMM to assess graceful degradation when no manifold structure exists.
- Validation protocol: In clustering, σ seems optimized to maximize reported F-measure without clear validation/test split; selection should be on validation data and final metrics on held-out test data.
- Sensitivity: Results would be more convincing with performance reported across a range of σ values and with cross-validation.
- Scalability/Generality: Need experiments or discussion in higher-dimensional settings where local metrics and geodesic computation are harder.
- Intrinsic dimension: Paper assumes full-rank M(x)=Σ^{-1} in ambient dimension D; lacks discussion of low-dimensional manifolds (d<<D) or rank-deficient metrics and remedies (e.g., dimensionality reduction).
- Clarity issues: Notation around Σ and M(x) is confusing—unclear whether Σ denotes global covariance, kernel bandwidth, or both, and how they relate.
- Algorithmic details: Section 4.2 lacks specification on cluster assignment procedure, number of clusters, distance computation (geodesic vs tangent Mahalanobis), and tie/ambiguity handling.
- Practical limitations: Recommend brief reminder/discussion of conditions for well-defined logarithm map (local injectivity, cut loci) to clarify applicability.
