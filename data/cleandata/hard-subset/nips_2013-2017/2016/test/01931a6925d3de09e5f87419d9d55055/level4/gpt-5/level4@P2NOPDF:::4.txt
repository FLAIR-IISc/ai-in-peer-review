The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, formulated on Riemannian manifolds. It derives a maximum likelihood estimator for the model parameters and evaluates the approach on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The manuscript is clearly written, the theoretical development appears sound, and the experiments are solid.

However, relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19], the novelty is not compelling as presented. The key distinctions from prior state-of-the-art are not clearly articulated until the conclusion. These differences should be made explicit at the outset—ideally in the introduction—so that readers can immediately understand what is new in the model, estimation procedure, or empirical findings. Notably, reference [19] is missing from the introduction and should be included. Establishing the gap with [19] early on would strengthen the paper’s contribution and better frame the experimental results.
