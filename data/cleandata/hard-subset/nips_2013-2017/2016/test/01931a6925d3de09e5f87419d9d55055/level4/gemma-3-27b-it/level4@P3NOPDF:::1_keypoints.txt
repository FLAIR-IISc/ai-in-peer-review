- Novelty: Proposes LAND, a parametric Riemannian metric on a nonparametric space, framing the learned metric as a Riemannian normal distribution (Mahalanobis-like) grounded in Pennec [15].  
- Method: Uses maximum likelihood estimation for mean and covariance with geodesic distances (following Hauberg et al. [9]) and learns local metrics from inverses of local diagonal covariances.  
- Extension: Introduces mixtures of LANDs as a way to model multi-component real-world data.  
- Theoretical strength: Clear conceptual link between metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics; promising generative, generalizable parametric models.  
- Scalability concern: Per-point (diagonal) covariance computation and inversion may be computationally prohibitive in higher dimensions; practical limits (e.g., 3–100 dims) are not specified.  
- Complexity analysis needed: Paper lacks a thorough computational complexity analysis and concrete threshold for “high-dimensional”; needs discussion of mitigation strategies (e.g., dimensionality reduction).  
- Literature positioning: Insufficient comparison to extensive metric learning work (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Kambhatla/local PCA); must clarify novel contributions relative to local diagonal metric methods.  
- Kernel sensitivity: Use of Gaussian kernel for locality raises questions about sensitivity to bandwidth σ; robustness tests and discussion of σ’s behavior across varying data densities are missing.  
- Experimental clarity: Synthetic-data experiments do not discuss σ choice; robustness to σ should be reported.  
- Presentation issues: Figs. 5–6 compare only to GMM (shown poor in Fig. 4); superior intrinsic-estimator comparisons in the supplement should be moved into the main paper.  
- Comparative evaluation: A complexity and performance comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators is needed to identify favorable scenarios and data structures.  
- Mixture models: Mixtures of LANDs are promising but under-explored—more detailed results and expansion on this aspect would strengthen the paper.
