- Proposes LAND: a parametric metric based on a Riemannian normal distribution that bridges metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Uses maximum likelihood to estimate Riemannian mean and covariance, yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds with geodesic distances via a learned local metric.  
- Learns the metric using inverses of local (diagonal) covariances and also considers mixtures of LANDs.  
- Provides novel insights linking metric learning and Riemannian statistics and offers parametric/generative models that can generalize beyond training data.  
- Main concern: applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional real-world data is unclear and likely problematic.  
- Computing a per-point (diagonal) covariance and its inverse is challenging in high dimensions; the paper notes this but lacks a thorough complexity/limitations analysis.  
- Requests explicit thresholds or experiments showing at what dimensionalities performance degrades (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100).  
- Paper should better situate itself in metric learning literature—prior work (Hauberg et al., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) also learns per-point diagonal metrics and local covariances appear in local PCA.  
- The choice and influence of the Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma is under-explored; needs robustness tests and clarification whether sigma is global or adaptive to density.  
- Comparisons are incomplete: intrinsic estimator results are omitted from main visualizations (Figs. 5–6) and weaker GMM baselines are used instead; suggest replacing or adding intrinsic-model visuals.  
- An analysis of computational complexity of LAND versus intrinsic estimators is needed to recommend methods for different data structures.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a promising and realistic extension that should be expanded.  
- Overall evaluation: paper is well-written and enjoyable to read.
