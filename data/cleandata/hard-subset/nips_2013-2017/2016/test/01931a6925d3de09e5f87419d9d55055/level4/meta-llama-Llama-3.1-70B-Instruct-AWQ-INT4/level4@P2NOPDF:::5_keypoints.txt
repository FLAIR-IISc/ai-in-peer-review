- Proposal: Introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance replacing the Mahalanobis metric, with a mixture extension and evaluations on synthetic and EEG data.

- Strength: Using a geodesic/Isomap-like distance is intriguing and shows potential.

- Clarity issue: The inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and needs more justification and intuition.

- Method detail: Authors should explain why a Gaussian kernel is used for weights in the weighted sum of local covariances.

- Notation/consistency: Unclear whether the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11) refer to the same quantity—needs clarification.

- Experiments: Empirical evaluation is somewhat lacking and should be expanded.

- Scalability: Major concern about computational efficiency and scalability—report complexity and runtimes compared to standard EM.

- Practicality: While theoretically interesting, the method may have limited practical applicability without efficiency improvements.

- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace 'd' with '\nabla'.
