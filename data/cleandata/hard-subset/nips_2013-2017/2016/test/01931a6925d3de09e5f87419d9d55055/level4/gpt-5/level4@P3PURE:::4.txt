This paper proposes an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, namely the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND). The distribution is defined on Riemannian manifolds. The authors derive a maximum likelihood estimator for its parameters and evaluate the method on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The paper is well written, the theoretical development appears sound, and the experiments are solid.

However, relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19], the paper does not present sufficiently strong novelty. The distinctions between the proposed approach and the state of the art are not clearly articulated until the conclusion. These differences should be made explicit from the outset—ideally in the introduction—to help the reader understand the contribution and its relationship to prior work (notably, [19] is not cited in the introduction).
