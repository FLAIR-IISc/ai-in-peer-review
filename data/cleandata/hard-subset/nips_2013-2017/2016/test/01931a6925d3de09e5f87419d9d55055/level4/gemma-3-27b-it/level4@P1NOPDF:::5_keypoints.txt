- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold distance instead of the Mahalanobis distance, and extends to mixtures of LANDs; evaluated on synthetic and EEG datasets.  
- The use of geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is interesting and novel.  
- Paper has clarity issues: the inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and needs more intuition, particularly why a Gaussian kernel weights local covariances.  
- Confusion between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11)—unclear if they denote the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing and not particularly compelling.  
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency: missing complexity analysis and runtime comparison to standard EM.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but practical applicability is questionable.  
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace “d” with “∇”.
