The paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a new parametric metric defined over a non-parametric space, by bridging concepts from metric learning/manifold learning and Riemannian statistics. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical background, including Riemannian geometry and manifold learning. The proposed LAND model is well-motivated, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in capturing nonlinear data structures through experiments on both synthetic and real-world data.

The strengths of the paper include its novel contribution to the field of machine learning, its clear and concise writing style, and its thorough evaluation of the proposed model.
