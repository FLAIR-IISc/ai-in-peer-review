- Proposes a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution to better capture data manifolds—an interesting application of Riemannian geometry in statistical learning.

- The metric is not actually learned but hand-crafted via Eq.7 with hyperparameters ρ and σ fixed empirically; the paper needs discussion of σ’s effect and a principled selection or joint learning of ρ and σ.

- The proposed metric lacks invariance to coordinate transformations; the authors should discuss this limitation, whether a full (non-diagonal) metric would restore invariance, and provide more intuition for Eq.7.

- The paper needs to better position itself relative to existing Riemannian metric learning and manifold learning literature.

- Experimental comparison to GMM is unfair because LAND has more flexibility per component; allow each method to select its optimal number of components (e.g., via BIC) and compare best models.

- Increasing components may cause LAND to overfit local noise despite geodesics becoming nearly straight; the authors should demonstrate how LAND overfits as component count grows.

- Computational cost appears substantially higher than standard Gaussians; include quantitative complexity/runtime analysis and discuss scalability limits (dataset size, dimensionality).

- Add larger-scale experiments (e.g., MNIST) with comparisons to GMMs to demonstrate practical applicability and scalability.
