This paper introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, a probability distribution based on Riemannian Manifolds, presented as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for the distribution's parameters and evaluate the method through experiments on both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper is well-written, with a sound theoretical foundation and well-executed experiments. However, the primary weakness lies in its limited novelty when compared to the work of Simo-Serra et al. [19]. A significant issue is that the distinctions between the proposed method and this state-of-the-art approach are not clarified until the conclusion. To properly frame the paper's contribution, these differences should have been established at the beginning of the paper. This lack of clear positioning is underscored by the absence of reference [19] in the introduction, which makes it difficult for the reader to assess the specific advancements offered by the proposed approach.
