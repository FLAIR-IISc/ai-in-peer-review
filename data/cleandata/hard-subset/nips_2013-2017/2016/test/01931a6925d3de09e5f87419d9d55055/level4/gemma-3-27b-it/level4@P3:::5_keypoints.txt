- Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces the Euclidean metric with a learned Riemannian metric to model data on nonlinear manifolds.
- Core idea leverages geodesic distances in a continuous, parametric form suitable for generative modeling, reminiscent of Isomap.
- Develops maximum likelihood estimation for LAND and extends it to mixture models with an EM algorithm.
- Demonstrates the method on synthetic datasets and EEG data.

- Novelty: combines Riemannian statistics and adaptive manifold learning to define a new distribution.
- Strong mathematical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry and maximum entropy principles.
- Provides a generative model enabling density estimation and sampling on manifolds.
- Proposes a practical estimation approach using Monte Carlo integration and gradient-based optimization.
- Experimental results show LAND can outperform standard Gaussian mixture models in some scenarios.

- Metric definition (Eq. 7) using a Gaussian-kernel-weighted local covariance lacks justification and alternative schemes aren’t explored.
- Notation inconsistency: unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its estimation in the MLE (Eq. 11).
- Experimental evaluation is limited—needs more comparisons to competing and state-of-the-art manifold learning methods.
- Missing reporting of computational running times and a detailed complexity analysis.
- Scalability concerns: computational cost of geodesic evaluation and Monte Carlo integration is not adequately addressed.
- Parameter sensitivity: the effect of the smoothing parameter σ is not sufficiently explored or guided.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; principled alternative to Gaussian models; practical implementation; promising empirical performance.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear explanations and inconsistent notation; insufficient experimental validation; unresolved scalability and computational-efficiency concerns; lack of thorough complexity analysis and method comparisons.

- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution with potential, but clarity, experimental depth, and scalability issues currently prevent acceptance.
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision—authors should clarify the metric and notation, expand experiments and comparisons, analyze complexity and runtimes, and study sensitivity to σ.
