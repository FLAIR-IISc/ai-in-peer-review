- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance replacing the Mahalanobis distance; includes a mixture extension and tests on synthetic and EEG data.
- Novelty: using a geodesic (Isomap-like) distance to define a probability distribution is interesting.
- Clarity issue: the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and needs justification.
- Needs more intuition for using a Gaussian-kernel weighted sum of local covariances to form the metric.
- Confusing relation between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11); unclear if they denote the same quantity.
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing and limited in scope.
- Major missing analysis on scalability and computational efficiency; no algorithmic complexity provided.
- No runtime comparisons to standard baselines (e.g., EM for Gaussian mixtures), hindering assessment of practical applicability.
- Minor: in Algorithm 1 (lines 4–5) the notation 'd' should likely be the gradient operator '∇'.
