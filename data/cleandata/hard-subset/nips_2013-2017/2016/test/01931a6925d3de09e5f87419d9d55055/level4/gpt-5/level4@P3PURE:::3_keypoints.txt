- Paper proposes modeling observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and defining a curved Gaussian (LAND) to better capture data manifolds.
- Strength: clear motivation to use geometry to capture manifold structure in high-dimensional data.
- Strength: coherent framework combining an observation-space metric with a curved Gaussian family.
- Strength: potentially broadly applicable in statistical learning.
- Concern: metric is hand-crafted (Eq.7) with hyperparameters ρ and σ fixed empirically, so “learning a metric” is overstated.
- Concern: σ likely sensitive; paper needs detailed discussion of how σ affects local curvature, neighborhood size, geodesics, and guidance for selecting it.
- Suggestion: consider jointly learning ρ and σ with LAND (e.g., likelihood maximization with regularization) or allowing partial adaptation per dataset/component.
- Concern: metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant; rotations or coordinate changes can yield different metrics and distortions.
- Request: explain why a diagonal metric is appropriate, what is lost by this choice, and how it affects geodesics and densities.
- Suggestion: clarify whether a full metric would mitigate invariance issues and discuss trade-offs (estimation stability, sample complexity, computation).
- Concern: weak positioning relative to rich prior work in metric learning and manifold learning; need to compare objectives, invariance, and learning procedures.
- Concern: experimental comparison is unbalanced—GMM can match LAND with more components; recommend allowing each method to select component number (e.g., BIC).
- Suggestion: study how LAND behaves as components increase, including overfitting scenarios, and revise first experiment accordingly.
- Concern: LAND is more computationally expensive; paper should report runtimes, memory, scaling with samples/dimensions/components, and dominant bottlenecks.
- Suggestion: evaluate practical scale limits (e.g., experiment on larger dataset like MNIST) and compare to GMM baselines for empirical scalability.
- After authors’ response: residual curvature can amplify sensitivity to local noise, making LAND components potentially more prone to overfitting than local Gaussians.
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful, but needs clearer metric treatment/learning, discussion of invariance, stronger related-work positioning, balanced experiments, and quantitative scalability analysis.
