- Proposes LAND: a parametric Riemannian-normal-like distribution using geodesic distance under a learned, smoothly varying metric M(x).
- Metric M(x) is built from local, kernel-weighted diagonal covariances so geodesics favor high-density regions.
- Defines density pM(x|µ,Σ) ∝ exp(−1/2 ⟨Logµ(x), Σ−1 Logµ(x)⟩) and computes the normalization via a tangent-space integral with Jacobian √|M(Expµ(v))|.
- Uses tangent-space Monte Carlo (v ∼ N(0,Σ)) and Exp-map IVP solves for efficient normalization estimation.
- Parameters estimated by ML with block coordinate descent: manifold steepest descent for µ and factorized optimization for Σ−1 = AᵀA; EM extension for mixtures provided.
- Empirically outperforms GMMs and LS Riemannian baselines on synthetic nonlinear manifolds and improves EEG sleep-stage F-measure when tuning σ.
- Geometric setup, Euler–Lagrange geodesic derivation, and tangent-space Monte Carlo are technically sound and well-motivated.
- Optimization scheme is reasonable given nonconvexity; gradients and mixture extensions are consistent with the probabilistic model.
- Key experimental concerns: σ appears tuned on evaluation data (risk of overfitting) and needs held-out or nested cross-validation.
- Missing sanity-check baseline: need experiments on Euclidean Gaussian/GMM data to show LAND does not degrade when manifold assumptions are unnecessary.
- Scalability is underexplored: computational cost per iteration, number of Exp evaluations, sensitivity to Monte Carlo samples S, and high-D behavior are not quantified.
- Modeling assumption critique: diagonal, non-invariant metric may limit expressiveness; ablation or discussion needed.
- Notation/confusion: Σ (model covariance) vs. M(x) (learned metric) is sometimes conflated (e.g., “M = Σ−1”); clarify roles and symbols.
- Clustering ambiguity: clarify whether labels come from posterior responsibilities or nearest-mean under geodesic distance.
- Suggestions: report held-out/nested-CV EEG results, add Euclidean/GMM baselines, provide time/complexity scaling, sensitivity studies for S and σ, and discuss non-diagonal/local PCA metrics and intrinsic-d < D extensions.
- Overall: technically solid and practically useful synthesis of prior Riemannian ideas into a tractable learned-manifold normal; merits acceptance after clearer notation and stronger, unbiased empirical validation.
