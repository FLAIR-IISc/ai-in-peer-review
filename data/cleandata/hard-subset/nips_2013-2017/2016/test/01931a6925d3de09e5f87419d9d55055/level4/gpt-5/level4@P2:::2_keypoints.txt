- Proposal: Introduces LAND, a generalization of the multivariate normal using a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric so the density lives on a data-adaptive manifold while keeping mean and covariance parameters.  
- Metric construction: Metric built from local (diagonal) covariances weighted by a Gaussian kernel, biasing geodesics toward high-density regions.  
- Technical contributions: Monte Carlo normalization by integrating in tangent space via Exp maps, gradient-based ML for µ and Σ (with stable descent and Σ−1 = AᵀA factorization), and an EM algorithm for LAND mixtures.  
- Experiments: Synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage features show improved modeling and clustering compared to GMMs and intrinsic least-squares estimators.  
- Relation to prior work: Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning by learning the metric and fitting a maximum-entropy normal; unlike mixtures on known manifolds it estimates per-component normalization constants.  
- Theoretical quality: Formulation is consistent with Riemannian statistics; tangent-space integral and importance sampling with N(0,Σ) is practical and avoids expensive Log maps.  
- Optimization quality: Optimization scheme is reasonable for a nonconvex problem; steepest-descent on Σ helps mitigate conditioning issues.  
- Empirical strengths: Requires fewer components than GMMs to fit nonlinear densities and avoids intrinsic-mean pathologies; EEG results show higher F-measure when σ is tuned.  
- Missing practical details: Many implementation specifics (initialization, EM details, complexity) are relegated to the supplement.  
- Ablation gaps: No ablation on Monte Carlo sample size S and no quantitative check of normalization accuracy.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Acknowledged sensitivity to σ and ρ but lacks systematic study; per-subject σ tuning may inflate reported performance.  
- Baseline comparisons: Evaluation limited to GMMs and LS estimators; stronger baselines (manifold mixtures on known manifolds, kernel density, spectral clustering, other metric-learning) are missing.  
- Metric limitations: Learned metric is diagonal and not invariant to linear transforms, reducing robustness to rotations or scaling of coordinates.  
- Scalability concerns: Scalability to high-dimensional data is questioned and not thoroughly addressed.  
- Clarity: Paper is largely clear and well organized with concise derivations and good high-level geometric exposition.  
- Suggested clarity additions: Report per-iteration computational complexity, provide guidance and sensitivity curves for σ and ρ, clarify convergence and step-size rules, and move EM summary into main text.  
- Reproducibility: Recommend releasing code or detailed pseudo-code to aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: The specific combination—learned data-adaptive Riemannian metric, tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization, and full ML/EM treatment—is novel relative to existing manifold and statistics literature.  
- Significance: Offers a principled, practical way to add local geometric adaptivity to Gaussian models with potential impact on density modeling, clustering, and classification; EEG use-case is promising.  
- Pros (summary): Principled Riemannian grounding, practical tangent-space normalization, full ML/EM framework, empirical gains on synthetic and real data, and a coherent bridge between manifold learning and generative modeling.  
- Cons (summary): Sensitivity to σ and diagonal metric choice, limited ablations and runtime analysis, missing stronger baselines, nonconvex optimization without initialization/local-minima analysis, and key algorithmic details in supplement.  
- Improvements suggested: Run systematic sensitivity analyses for σ, ρ, and S; estimate normalization errors; compare to stronger baselines; explore learning σ and richer local metrics (low-rank/full) with regularization; report complexity and wall-clock times; discuss approximate/cached Exp/Log computations; show robustness to initialization and consider stochastic variants; add more diverse and higher-dimensional datasets.  
- Overall assessment: Well-motivated and thoughtfully executed contribution that advances generative modeling on learned manifolds but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer guidance on hyperparameters and scalability to be fully compelling.
