- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using geodesic distance instead of Mahalanobis, and a mixture extension evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.
- Conceptually interesting contribution to density estimation but hampered by clarity and insufficient experimental validation.
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; needs stronger justification and intuition.
- Need motivation for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances and how this reflects manifold structure.
- The relation between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear; authors must clarify whether they are the same and why MAP is used.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic data is insufficient and EEG results need comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- Major concern about scalability: geodesic distance computation may be expensive, and the paper lacks complexity analysis and runtime comparisons.
- Should compare computational cost to standard EM for GMMs and report running times on datasets of varying sizes.
- Suggest reporting or developing approximations/optimizations to reduce geodesic computation cost.
- Minor: replace “d” with gradient symbol (∇) in Algorithm 1 for clarity.
- Minor: add visual aids (diagrams) to show manifold traversal and Gaussian adaptation.
- Minor: improve notation consistency, e.g., boldface for vectors and matrices.
- Questions for authors: justify Gaussian kernel weighting; clarify Eq. (7) vs Eq. (11); provide computational complexity and EM comparison; propose any geodesic approximations.
- Recommendation: lean towards reject but encourage revision pending clarity, scalability analysis, and stronger experimental validation.
