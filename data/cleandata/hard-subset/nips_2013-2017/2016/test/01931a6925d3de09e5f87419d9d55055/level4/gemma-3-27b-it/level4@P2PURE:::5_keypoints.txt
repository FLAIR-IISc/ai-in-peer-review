- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance instead of Mahalanobis distance.  
- Extends the approach to mixtures of LANDs and evaluates on synthetic and real-world EEG data.  
- Positive: the core idea of using a geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is compelling.  
- Clarity issue: the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary and needs justification.  
- Needs intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances in the metric sum.  
- Presentation confusion: unclear whether the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) refer to the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and could be expanded.  
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency—missing complexity analysis and runtime comparison to standard EM.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but practical applicability is questionable.  
- Minor: in Algorithm 1, lines 4–5 should replace 'd' with '\nabla'.
