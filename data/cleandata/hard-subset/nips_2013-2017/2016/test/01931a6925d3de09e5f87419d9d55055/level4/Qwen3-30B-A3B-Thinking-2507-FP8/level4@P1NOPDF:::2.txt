

This paper extends the standard multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, yielding a natural generalization of the normal distribution for manifold learning. The approach demonstrates broad applicability, as evidenced by the accompanying experiments. The authors provide a succinct, comprehensive treatment of maximum likelihood parameter estimation for this model. The extension to a mixture of LANDS further enhances practical utility, positioning it as a compelling alternative to the now-standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experimental results are promising and open significant avenues for future research. The paper’s language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to NIPS’ scope are all excellent.
