- Proposes LAND, a parametric metric in a nonparametric space combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- LAND corresponds to a Riemannian normal distribution (Pennec) and the authors derive an MLE algorithm for mean and covariance (Riemannian Mahalanobis).  
- Uses geodesic distances (Hauberg et al.) and learns the metric via inverses of local diagonal covariance matrices; also extends to mixtures of LANDs.  
- The approach provides useful insights at the intersection of metric learning and Riemannian statistics and offers parametric/generative extrapolation benefits.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional datasets due to per-point covariance estimation and inversion.  
- Paper needs a more thorough computational-complexity analysis and a clear definition of what “high-dimensional” means in this context (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dims).  
- The manuscript should better situate itself within the broader metric learning literature (e.g., works that learn per-point diagonal metric tensors and local PCA approaches).  
- The Gaussian kernel bandwidth (sigma) used to define locality is not fully explored; robustness tests and whether sigma is uniform across density regions are needed.  
- Comparisons: Fig. 4 includes intrinsic estimators, but Figs. 5 and 6 compare only to GMM; intrinsic-estimator visual comparisons should be moved into or added to the main text.  
- Recommend adding a computational-complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice across data structures.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a compelling contribution and expanding this application would increase the paper’s impact.  
- Overall assessment: manuscript is exceptionally well written and enjoyable to read.
