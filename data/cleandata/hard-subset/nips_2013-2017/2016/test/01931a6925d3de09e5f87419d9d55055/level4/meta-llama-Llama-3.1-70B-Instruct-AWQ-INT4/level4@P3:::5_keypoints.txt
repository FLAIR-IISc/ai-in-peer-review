- Proposes LAND: replaces Euclidean distance in Gaussian with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance.
- Learns a non-parametric, smoothly changing metric that induces a Riemannian manifold for modeling.
- LAND is a parametric distribution defined over a non-parametric manifold — a natural Gaussian extension.
- Paper gives a clear, concise summary and situates the work relative to prior NIPS and other literature.
- Authors explicitly discuss strengths and weaknesses and provide a helpful pro/con list for the PC.
- Technically sound: develops an MLE algorithm using gradient descent combined with Monte Carlo integration.
- Extends LAND to mixture models and derives the corresponding EM algorithm.
- Experiments are limited but demonstrate effectiveness on synthetic data and EEG sleep measurements.
- Empirical comparisons include GMM and a mixture of LANDs using least-squares estimators.
- Strong theoretical contribution with a clear introduction to Riemannian geometry and links to methods like Isomap.
- Weakness: definition of the inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc and needs more intuition.
- Weakness: choice of Gaussian kernel weights for local covariances needs better justification.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks breadth and detailed runtime/computational-complexity comparisons to standard EM.
- Clarity issues: notation and some equation explanations could be improved; Algorithm 1 has a gradient-symbol typo.
- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical work but practical applicability limited by computational complexity; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Rating: 7/10.
