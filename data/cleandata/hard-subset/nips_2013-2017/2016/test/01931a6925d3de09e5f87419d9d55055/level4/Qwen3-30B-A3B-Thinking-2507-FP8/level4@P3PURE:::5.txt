

Summary: The paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance in the Gaussian distribution. The extension to mixtures of LANDs is also provided. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.  

Major Comments: The core idea of employing a geodesic distance, analogous to Isomap, is highly interesting. However, several weaknesses undermine clarity. First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) appears ad hoc. The authors should provide deeper intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight the sum of local covariances. Second, the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Equation (7) and its MAP estimate in Equation (11) is unclear. Do these equations refer to the same quantity? Third, the experimental evaluation is insufficient; results on the synthetic dataset lack statistical significance, and the EEG analysis offers minimal insight into practical utility. Most critically, the paper omits any discussion of scalability and computational efficiency. What is the computational complexity and runtime relative to standard EM? While the theoretical contribution is promising, the method’s practical applicability is questionable due to these omissions.  

Minor Comments: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5: "d" → "∇".
