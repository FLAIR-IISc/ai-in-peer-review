- Summary: Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using learned geodesic manifold distances instead of Mahalanobis distance, and extends it to mixtures; evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Interesting idea: Using geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is promising but the paper lacks clarity in places.

- Inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc; authors should justify using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariance sums.

- Unclear relation between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate in Eq. (11); it’s not clear if they represent the same quantity.

- Experimental evaluation is limited and needs to be expanded to demonstrate broader empirical effectiveness.

- Major concern: scalability and computational efficiency are not addressed; need complexity analysis and runtime comparison to standard EM.

- Overall: Theoretical contribution is interesting but practical applicability may be limited.

- Minor: Typo/notation error in Algorithm 1 (lines 4–5): "d" should be "∇".
