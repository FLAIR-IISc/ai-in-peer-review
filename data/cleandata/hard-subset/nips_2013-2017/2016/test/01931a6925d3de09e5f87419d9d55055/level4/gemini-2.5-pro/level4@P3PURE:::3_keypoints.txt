- Proposal: model observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and define a curved normal (LAND) to better capture data manifolds — an interesting application of Riemannian geometry.

- Metric definition & hyperparameters: the metric (Eq.7) is hand-crafted and depends on ρ and σ fixed empirically; σ likely sensitive and the paper lacks discussion on its effect, selection, or joint learning with LAND.

- Invariance of the metric: the diagonal metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant (results change under rotation); the paper should discuss this and whether a full (non-diagonal) metric or alternative form would be more appropriate.

- Relation to existing work: the paper is insufficiently situated in the metric learning and manifold learning literature and should cite and connect to related methods.

- Experimental evaluation: first experiment is unconvincing—compare fairly using model selection (e.g., BIC) to choose component counts, analyze complexity vs. performance trade-offs, and explicitly demonstrate LAND’s tendency to overfit with many components.

- Computational cost & scalability: LAND is computationally more expensive than standard Gaussians; the paper needs quantitative runtime/scalability analysis and experiments on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) comparing to GMMs.
