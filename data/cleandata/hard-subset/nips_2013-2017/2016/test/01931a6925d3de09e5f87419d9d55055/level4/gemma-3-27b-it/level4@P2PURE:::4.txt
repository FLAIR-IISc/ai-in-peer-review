This paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, grounded in the framework of Riemannian Manifolds. The authors derive a maximum likelihood estimator for the distribution’s parameters and demonstrate its performance through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and supported by solid experimental results. However, the novelty relative to existing work, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19], feels incremental. The key distinctions between the proposed method and the current state-of-the-art are not sufficiently highlighted until the conclusion; these differences should be clearly articulated earlier, ideally beginning with the introduction – notably, Simo-Serra et al. [19] is not mentioned in the introduction despite its relevance.
