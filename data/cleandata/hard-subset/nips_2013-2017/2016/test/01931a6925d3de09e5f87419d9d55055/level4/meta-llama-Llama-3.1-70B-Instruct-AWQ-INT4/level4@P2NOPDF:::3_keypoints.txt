1. Proposes a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal (LAND) to better capture data manifolds—interesting application of Riemannian geometry in statistical learning.

2. Title is misleading: the metric is not learned but hand-crafted (Eq.7) with two hyperparameters (rho, sigma) fixed empirically.

3. Paper lacks discussion on sigma’s sensitivity and principled methods for selecting it; authors should consider learning rho and sigma jointly with LAND.

4. The metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant; the paper needs discussion of invariance of the full metric and intuition behind Eq.7.

5. Must relate work to the extensive literature on metric learning and manifold learning grounded in Riemannian geometry.

6. First experiment’s comparison is unfair: LAND uses fewer components while GMM can outperform with more components; propose selecting component counts by a criterion (e.g., BIC) for fair comparison.

7. Recommend experiments showing overfitting behavior of LAND with many components, since local LANDs may be more sensitive to noise than local Gaussians.

8. Need quantitative evaluation of computational cost and scalability—report run-time, limits, and whether LAND can scale to datasets like MNIST compared to GMMs.

9. Authors’ response confirms LAND’s flexibility but also the risk of overfitting with increased components; suggest revising experiments accordingly.
