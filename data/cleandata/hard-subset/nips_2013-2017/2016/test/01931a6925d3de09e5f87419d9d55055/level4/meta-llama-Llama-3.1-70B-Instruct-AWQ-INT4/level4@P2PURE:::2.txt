This brief paper extends the common multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, resulting in a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting with wide applicability, as illustrated by the reported experiments. Notably, it provides a concise and complete account of a maximum likelihood-type parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture of LANDS makes it a practical alternative to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are exciting and open up a wide avenue for future research. The language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are all excellent.
