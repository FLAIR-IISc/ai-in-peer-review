

This paper extends the multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, resulting in a generalization of the normal distribution suitable for manifold learning with broad applicability, as demonstrated by the reported experiments. It provides a succinct, complete account of a maximum likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture of LANDS makes it particularly practical as a viable alternative to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are compelling and open significant avenues for future research. The language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS scope are excellent.
