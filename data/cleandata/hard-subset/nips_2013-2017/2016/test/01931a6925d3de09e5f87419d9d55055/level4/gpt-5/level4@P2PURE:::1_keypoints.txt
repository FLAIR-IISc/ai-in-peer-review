- Introduces LAND: a parametric metric on nonparametric spaces combining metric/manifold learning and Riemannian statistics, with learned Riemannian normal distributions and MLE for mean/covariance yielding a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds.  
- Distances computed as geodesics using a locally learned metric from inverses of local (diagonal) covariances; also explores mixtures of LANDs.  
- Positive: offers fresh perspective linking metric learning and Riemannian statistics and provides parametric generative models that can extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Main concern: applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data—computing per-point (diagonal) covariances and inverses may be infeasible; needs detailed computational complexity and practical limits (what counts as “high-dimensional”?).  
- Literature positioning: should better situate within metric learning literature—Hauberg et al. and prior work (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) already learn similar per-point diagonal metric tensors; local covariances relate to local PCA (Kambhatla).  
- Gaussian kernel locality (sigma): unclear how sigma choice affects results; needs robustness analysis and clarification whether sigma is constant across low/high-density regions.  
- Evaluation/figures: comparisons inconsistent—Fig.4 includes intrinsic estimators but Figs.5–6 compare to GMM (known poor performer); suggest replacing or adding intrinsic-model visualizations from supplement and comparing computational cost against intrinsic estimators.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a compelling direction and deserve further development.  
- Writing: paper is very well written and pleasant to read.
