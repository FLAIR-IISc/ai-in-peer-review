- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance to replace Mahalanobis distance, with an extension to mixtures and evaluation on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Core idea of using a geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is interesting.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; authors should justify using a Gaussian kernel to weight the sum of local covariances.  
- Relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) is unclear—are they the same quantity?  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: synthetic results lack statistical significance.  
- EEG analysis provides minimal insight into practical utility.  
- Major omission: no discussion of scalability or computational efficiency—need complexity and runtime comparisons to standard EM.  
- Minor comment: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5: replace "d" with "∇".
