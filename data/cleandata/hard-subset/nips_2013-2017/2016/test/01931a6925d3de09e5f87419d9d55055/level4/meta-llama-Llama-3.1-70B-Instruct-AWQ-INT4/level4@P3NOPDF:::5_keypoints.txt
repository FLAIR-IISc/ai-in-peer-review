- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance in place of Mahalanobis distance.  
- Extends approach to a mixture of LANDs and evaluates on a synthetic dataset and an EEG dataset.  
- Strength: using geodesic distance (Isomap-like) is an intriguing and potentially valuable contribution.  
- Clarity issue: inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; authors should give intuition for using a Gaussian kernel for weights.  
- Ambiguity: unclear whether the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) refer to the same quantity—needs clarification.  
- Limited experimental evaluation; more datasets and ablation studies are needed.  
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; paper lacks complexity and runtime comparison to standard EM.  
- Practical applicability is currently limited; addressing theoretical clarifications and expanding experiments would strengthen the paper.  
- Minor: In Algorithm 1 (lines 4–5) replace 'd' with 'nabla' (∇).
