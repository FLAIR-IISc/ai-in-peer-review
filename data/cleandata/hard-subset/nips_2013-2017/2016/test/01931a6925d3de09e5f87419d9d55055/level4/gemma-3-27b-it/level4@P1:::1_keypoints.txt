- Proposes LAND: a novel parametric distribution over a non-parametric space combining metric learning/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics (Riemannian normal + learned locally adaptive metric).  
- Develops an MLE algorithm that effectively defines a Mahalanobis-like distance on manifolds with distances computed as geodesics and a local metric from inverse local (diagonal) covariances (Hauberg-inspired).  
- Extends the model to mixtures of LANDs, enabling multi-component generative modeling.  
- Strength: novel bridge between metric learning and Riemannian statistics, yielding a parametric generative model that can extrapolate beyond training data.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation with connections to maximum entropy and demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic and EEG datasets; paper is well written.  
- Primary concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional datasets due to per-point covariance and inverse computations.  
- Needs a detailed computational complexity analysis and a clear definition of what “high-dimensional” means in this context (e.g., 3, 10, 100 dims).  
- Insufficient positioning within the wider metric learning literature—similar per-point diagonal metrics and local-covariance approaches (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA) should be better discussed.  
- Bandwidth σ (Gaussian kernel) influence and robustness require deeper investigation; unclear if σ is constant across varying point densities and not tested on synthetic data.  
- Visualization critique: Figs. 5–6 compare to GMMs (which perform poorly) rather than intrinsic estimators; suggest including intrinsic estimator visualizations from the supplement or replacing GMMs.  
- Recommend comparative analysis of computational complexity and practical trade-offs between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify when each is preferable.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are particularly promising for real-world data and warrant further expansion and exploration.
