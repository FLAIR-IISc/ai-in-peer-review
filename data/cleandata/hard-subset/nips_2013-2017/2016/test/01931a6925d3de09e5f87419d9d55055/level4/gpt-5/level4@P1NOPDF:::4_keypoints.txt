- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution grounded in Riemannian manifold geometry as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models.
- Derives a maximum likelihood estimator for the distribution’s parameters.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets with convincing experimental results.
- Manuscript is clearly written and the theoretical development appears sound.
- Contribution lacks strong novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19].
- Distinctions from [19] are not clearly articulated until the conclusion.
- [19] is not cited in the introduction, making it hard to position the work early.
- Recommendation to cite and provide a concise, technical comparison to [19] in the introduction.
- Request to clearly state what is new (model formulation, parameterization, optimization, theoretical guarantees) relative to [19].
- Suggestion to explain early when and why the proposed distribution is preferable to GMMs and prior manifold-based density models.
- Overall assessment: well executed but appears incremental; clarifying novelty and positioning earlier would improve impact.
