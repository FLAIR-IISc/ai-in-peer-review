- Introduces LAND, a parametric Riemannian normal distribution framework linking metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Core innovation: learning a local Mahalanobis metric per point via inverses of diagonal covariances to enable a generative model beyond training data.  
- Proposes mixtures of LANDs to model multi-component data.  
- Theoretical connection between metric learning and Riemannian statistics is well articulated and paper is clearly written.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data is unaddressed and critical for practical use.  
- Missing complexity analysis: no quantification of computational cost, dimensionality limits, or practical thresholds for infeasibility.  
- Need precise definition of "high-dimensional" (e.g., 3D, 10D, 100D) and how cost scales with dimensionality and dataset size.  
- Literature positioning is weak: must clarify novelty relative to Hauberg [9], Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, and local PCA.  
- Hyperparameter σ (kernel bandwidth) is inadequately studied; sensitivity, density dependence, and optimal strategies are not analyzed beyond one dataset.  
- Visualization and empirical comparisons are inconsistent: main paper compares to GMM but omits stronger intrinsic estimator results shown in supplementary material.  
- Mixture extension is underdeveloped: lacks analysis of convergence, component selection, and scalability.  
- Required revisions: add complexity analysis, clarify literature contributions, perform σ sensitivity/density-aware tests, include intrinsic estimator visualizations, and expand mixture learning details.
