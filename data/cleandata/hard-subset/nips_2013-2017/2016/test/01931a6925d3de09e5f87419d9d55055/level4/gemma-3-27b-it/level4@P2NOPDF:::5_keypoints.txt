- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using geodesic distances on learned manifolds, extended to mixtures, evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Core idea of using geodesic distances (akin to Isomap) is intriguing and potentially valuable.  
- Paper has clarity issues that hinder understanding and assessment.  
- Main concern: justification for the metric tensor inverse calculation in Eq. (7) is weak; using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances seems arbitrary and lacks intuition.  
- Presentation is confusing about the local covariance definition (Eq. 7) versus its MAP estimation (Eq. 11); unclear if they are the same or distinct steps.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: results are not compelling and experimental setup lacks detail.  
- Practical limitations are not addressed, especially scalability and computational efficiency.  
- Reviewer requests computational complexity comparison to standard EM and running times on datasets of varying sizes.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but, without clarity and scalability fixes, practical applicability is questionable and unlikely to be widely adopted.  
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, replace ‘d’ with the gradient symbol (∇).
