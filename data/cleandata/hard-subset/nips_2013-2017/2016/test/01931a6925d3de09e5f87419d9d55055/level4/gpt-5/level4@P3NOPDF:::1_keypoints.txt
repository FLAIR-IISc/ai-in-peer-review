- Paper summary: Introduces LAND, a parametric generative metric linking metric/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics, estimating per-point diagonal covariances and defining distances via manifold geodesics; includes mixture models.

- Strength: Clear, well-written exposition with strong conceptual motivation and links to Riemannian statistics.

- Strength: Principled parametric Riemannian-normal formulation that unifies metric learning and generative modeling.

- Strength: Natural and practically important mixture extension for multi-modal data.

- Strength: Empirical results are suggestive and the approach is elegant.

- Main concern 1 (Scalability/high-d): Per-point (diagonal) covariance estimation and geodesic computations scale at least linearly in N and d and may be statistically and computationally challenging in high dimensions.

- Request: Provide explicit big-O complexity (N, d, k, C, I) including geodesic cost/gradients and memory-footprint estimates.

- Request: Clarify practical regime for “high-dimensional” (e.g., up to tens, hundreds, thousands) and report runtime/accuracy scaling with d and N.

- Request: Discuss numerical stability and regularization for local covariances (shrinkage, variance floors) in sparse/high-d neighborhoods.

- Main concern 2 (Relation to prior work): Position more clearly relative to prior local metric-learning literature and local PCA approaches, and articulate novelty beyond Hauberg et al. and how ML framing affects estimation/regularization/generalization.

- Main concern 3 (Kernel bandwidth σ): Clarify how σ is chosen (global vs adaptive), provide robustness/sensitivity analyses, and offer data-driven selection procedures.

- Main concern 4 (Baselines/visualizations/fairness): Include intrinsic estimators in main visualizations and runtime comparisons, ensure fair geodesic infrastructure and regularization, and add/compare to local metric learning baselines.

- Main concern 5 (Mixture of LANDs): Expand experiments on number of components, initialization, regularization to avoid collapse, sensitivity to σ, and provide EM/optimization details and per-iteration complexity.

- Additional suggestions: Discuss smoothness of per-point metric field and consider spatial smoothing/shared low-rank structure for stability; propose practical accelerations (approx k-NN, anchor/region-wise metrics, shrinkage, multi-scale σ, GPU).

- Additional suggestions: Provide guidance on regularization hyperparameters, defaults, reproducibility and code availability.

- Minor comments: Clarify notation for geodesic computation and assumed/induced manifold structure; report confidence intervals/variance across runs.

- Overall assessment: Conceptually strong and promising; recommend acceptance conditional on clearer complexity/scalability analysis, inclusion of intrinsic estimator visualizations/runtimes, and expanded mixture/σ evaluation.
