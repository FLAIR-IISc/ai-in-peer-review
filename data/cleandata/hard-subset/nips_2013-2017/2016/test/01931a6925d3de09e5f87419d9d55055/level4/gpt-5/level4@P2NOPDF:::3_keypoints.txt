- Paper proposes modeling observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution (LAND) to better capture data geometry.

- Major concern: the “metric learning” is not learned—Equation (7) uses a hand-crafted form with fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ.

- σ likely sensitive; the paper lacks intuition, guidance for choosing σ, and robustness analysis.

- Suggestion: consider jointly learning ρ and σ with LAND (e.g., via marginal likelihood or cross-validation) instead of fixing them.

- Invariance issue: the metric in Equation (7) does not appear invariant to coordinate transformations (e.g., rotations); needs analysis of what transforms preserve it.

- Question whether moving from a diagonal to a full metric would restore invariance or at least approximate it; provide more intuition for the chosen form.

- Related work is under-discussed; connect to Riemannian metric learning, information geometry, and graph/Laplacian manifold methods to clarify novelty.

- Experimental concerns: first experiment favors LAND but is not convincing—compare methods with principled model-selection (BIC/AIC/held-out likelihood/CV) so each can choose its number of components.

- Need to show LAND’s behavior as component count grows (overfitting curves, noise sensitivity) because local LANDs may be more prone to noise than local Gaussians.

- Computational cost unquantified: report complexity in n, d, components, wall-clock times, memory, acceleration strategies, practical scale limits (e.g., MNIST feasibility) and compare to Gaussian mixtures.

- Overall required improvements: principled treatment/learning of ρ and σ; invariance analysis and justification of Equation (7); rigorous experimental control of model complexity; quantitative scalability assessment; and broader related-work positioning.
