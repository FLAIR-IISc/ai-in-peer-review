- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic distance instead of Mahalanobis to capture nonlinear data structure; extended to mixtures and evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Core idea of using geodesic distance (similar to Isomap) is compelling and bridges Riemannian statistics with manifold learning, offering a solid theoretical foundation.

- The inverse covariance weighting in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary; the paper needs more intuition for choosing a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances.

- The relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its use in the MLE (Eq. 11) is unclear—are they the same estimate or distinct quantities?

- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency: the paper should provide detailed computational complexity and runtime comparisons (e.g., versus standard EM).

- Experimental evaluation is limited; the paper should discuss trade-offs for the smoothing parameter σ and include a more thorough ablation study.

- Overall, the contribution is theoretically interesting with potential impact, but practical relevance is uncertain without further investigation into computational feasibility.

- Minor issues: Algorithm 1 line 4 should use “∇” instead of “d”, and notation should be made consistent (e.g., clarify when tangent vectors are column vectors).
