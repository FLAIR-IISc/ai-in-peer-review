Summary: This paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in Gaussian distributions. The authors also extend the method to a mixture of LANDs. Performance is evaluated on both a synthetic dataset and a real-world dataset of EEG measurements.

Major comments: The proposed idea of using a geodesic distance, similar in spirit to Isomap, is very interesting. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. First, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) seems somewhat ad hoc. Could the authors provide more intuition regarding the use of a Gaussian kernel for the weights in the weighted sum of local covariances? Second, I found myself confused by the distinction between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Eq. (11). Do these equations refer to the same quantity? 

Furthermore, the experimental evaluations are not particularly compelling. My main concern, however, is the scalability and computational efficiency of the method. For example, what is the computational complexity, and how does the running time compare to that of standard Expectation-Maximization (EM)? In my opinion, the paper presents an interesting theoretical contribution, but its practical applicability is questionable. 

Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, please replace “d” with “∇”.
