1. Introduces LAND: a parametric Riemannian normal model yielding a Mahalanobis-type distance with learned local (diagonal) covariances and a mixture extension.  
2. Main concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data due to per-point local covariance computation and inversion.  
3. Paper needs concrete computational and memory complexity analysis (per-iteration and overall) as functions of data size, intrinsic dimension, neighborhood size, and number of components.  
4. Clarify what “high-dimensional” means in practice (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100+ dims) and provide guidance on feasible regimes.  
5. Discuss numerical stability and regularization of local covariances (shrinkage, conditioning of inverses).  
6. Positioning relative to metric learning literature is weak—compare conceptually and empirically to per-point/local-metric methods (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, local PCA).  
7. Investigate sensitivity to kernel bandwidth σ, provide robustness analyses, and clarify whether σ is global or locally adapted.  
8. Experimental presentation needs improvement: include intrinsic estimators in main visual comparisons and add computational comparisons (wall-clock time, scaling, convergence).  
9. Mixture extension is promising but requires more detail on initialization, component selection/model selection, identifiability, and interaction between local metric estimation and assignments.  
10. Overall positive: well written; addressing scalability, literature connections, kernel choice, and enhanced experiments would strengthen the paper.
