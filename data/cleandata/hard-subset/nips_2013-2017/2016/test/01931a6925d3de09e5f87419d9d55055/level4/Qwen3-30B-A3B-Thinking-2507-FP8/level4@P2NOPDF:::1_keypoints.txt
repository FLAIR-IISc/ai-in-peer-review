- Introduces LAND: a novel parametric metric on nonparametric spaces combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics using a Riemannian normal and per-point diagonal covariances with geodesic distances.  
- Proposes mixtures of LANDs, a promising extension for multi-component data that needs deeper exploration and validation.  
- Major concern: scalability to high-dimensional data—per-point covariance computation/inversion is computationally expensive and statistically unstable, with no rigorous complexity analysis or definition of “high-dimensional” thresholds.  
- Lacks positioning relative to prior metric-learning work that addresses scalability and pointwise diagonal metrics.  
- Gaussian kernel σ is under-analyzed—only tuned for one dataset, with no sensitivity tests or treatment of varying-density regions, leaving robustness unclear.  
- Experimental inconsistencies: visuals compare to GMM despite intrinsic estimators outperforming GMM; intrinsic estimator visualizations should replace or accompany GMM results.  
- Missing computational-complexity comparison between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice by data structure and dimensionality.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and well-written, but requires stronger analysis of scalability, σ, and experimental design to increase impact.
