- Proposes a novel locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using Riemannian geometry to model high-dimensional nonlinear data.  
- Defines a curved normal distribution based on a hand-crafted Riemannian metric to better capture the data manifold.  
- Well-structured and clearly written paper with a clear introduction to limitations of multivariate normal and Riemannian concepts.  
- Technical derivation is sound and thorough, introducing a local covariance matrix to represent local data structure.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of LAND in capturing nonlinear relationships.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Comparison with Gaussian mixture models highlights LAND’s advantage on nonlinear structures.  
- Reliance on a hand-crafted Riemannian metric may limit applicability across datasets.  
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity; potential scalability and computational cost concerns for large datasets.  
- Authors suggest learning metric parameters (rho and sigma) jointly with LAND as future work.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address metric choice and scalability in future research.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
