Summary: The paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) that replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance with a geodesic, manifold-traversal distance learned from data. The approach is also extended to mixtures of LANDs. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and on a real-world EEG dataset.

Major comments:
- The idea of using a geodesic distance, similar to Isomap, is very interesting. However, there are some issues with clarity.
- The definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc. Could the authors provide more intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight the sum of local covariance matrices?
- I am confused about the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimate given in Eq. (11). Are these intended to be the same quantity?
- The experimental evaluation is rather limited.
- My main concern is scalability and computational efficiency. What are the computational complexity and running times compared to standard EM?

Overall, the paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution but may have limited practical applicability.
