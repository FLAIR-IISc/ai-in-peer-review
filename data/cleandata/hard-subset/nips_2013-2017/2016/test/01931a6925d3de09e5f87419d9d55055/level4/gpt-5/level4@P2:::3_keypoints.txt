- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in a Gaussian with a data-driven Riemannian metric to adapt density contours and geodesics to local data structure.  
- Constructs a smoothly varying metric using a kernel-weighted diagonal local covariance with hyperparameters σ (locality) and ρ (regularization).  
- Estimates normalization constant via Monte Carlo sampling in the tangent space.  
- Performs maximum-likelihood estimation of mean and covariance with practical gradients.  
- Extends the model to mixtures using an EM-like procedure.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage features show LAND fits nonlinear manifold structure better than standard Gaussians/GMMs.  
- Conceptually appealing unification of manifold learning and parametric generative modeling, keeping simple parameters (mean, covariance) while adapting to nonlinearity.  
- Clear algorithmic contributions: tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization, ML gradients, and a mixture extension.  
- Maximum-entropy perspective gives a principled justification for the distribution given a metric.  
- Main weakness: the metric is closer to hand-crafted than learned, relying on fixed σ and ρ chosen a priori.  
- Need sensitivity analysis and selection strategy for σ and ρ (trade-off between smoothing and overfitting).  
- Should explore joint estimation or Bayesian integration of σ and ρ (hierarchical priors or bilevel optimization).  
- Invariance concern: diagonal local covariance is not affine-invariant and depends on coordinate system (rotation can change metric).  
- Ask whether a full local covariance would restore affine invariance and discuss computational/overfitting trade-offs.  
- Recommend preprocessing guidance (e.g., whitening) to mitigate coordinate dependence and more intuition for diagonal choice.  
- Experimental fairness issue: fixing K across methods biases comparison because LAND is more flexible per component.  
- Suggest model selection over K (BIC/AIC or held-out likelihood) and reporting performance at chosen K; show LAND behavior as K grows.  
- Computational cost and scalability are not quantified; request runtime and memory vs N, D, S and cost of Exp/Log maps and geodesics.  
- Ask for practical limits (e.g., feasibility on MNIST-like datasets) and possible accelerations (subsampling, sparse kernels, low-rank, fast ODE solvers).  
- Related-work positioning should be deeper: compare to metric learning and probabilistic geometry literature and highlight differences (diagonal covariances, tangent-space normalization, mixture EM).  
- Evaluation summary: sound formulation and motivation, but fixed σ and missing scalability analysis limit practical guidance; clarity is good but needs deeper discussion of key issues.  
- Originality: moderately high—novel to adapt Riemannian normals to a data-defined metric with practical ML/EM procedures.  
- Significance: potentially impactful as a flexible Gaussian replacement on manifolds, but needs broader comparisons and scalability evidence.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled extension, practical algorithmic framework, empirical gains on manifold-structured tasks.  
- Cons for acceptance: hand-specified metric with sensitive hyperparameters, lack of invariance discussion, unfair experimental comparisons, and unquantified computational cost.  
- Suggested revisions: add procedure for selecting/learning σ and ρ with sensitivity plots, include model selection over K, provide runtime/complexity benchmarks vs GMM, expand related-work, and consider full local covariance or preprocessing recommendations.  
- Post-rebuttal caution: even with large σ, geodesics can introduce curvature that—combined with many components—may overfit local noise; revise experiments to let each method choose K and show LAND’s behavior as K increases.
