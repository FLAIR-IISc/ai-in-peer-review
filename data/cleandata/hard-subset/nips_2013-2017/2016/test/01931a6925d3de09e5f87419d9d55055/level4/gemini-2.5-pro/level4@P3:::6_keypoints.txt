- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian generalization of the multivariate normal that uses a data-driven, locally adaptive metric so geodesics follow manifold structure.  
- Defines the distribution on the manifold via a mean and covariance, with an intractable normalization constant estimated by Monte Carlo integration on the tangent space.  
- Develops a maximum likelihood estimation procedure using block coordinate descent and extends to a LAND mixture with an EM algorithm.  
- Demonstrates benefits on synthetic manifold data and EEG sleep-stage clustering, claiming improvements over standard GMMs.  

Strengths:
- Novel and elegant combination of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics to generalize the Gaussian.  
- Methodology is technically sound; clear gradient derivations and a pragmatic Monte Carlo scheme make the approach feasible.  
- Potentially broad impact: a data-adaptive normal could improve many models relying on Gaussian assumptions.  

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Hyperparameter selection (σ) appears to be chosen by testing on the full dataset, risking overfitting and invalidating generalization claims.  
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete: no experiments showing performance when data are generated by a true GMM or single Gaussian.  
- Unconventional assumption: metric M(x) is full-rank in D-dimensional ambient space (manifold dimension = ambient dimension), conflicting with typical d << D manifold assumptions and raising scalability concerns.  
- Notational and conceptual ambiguity: unclear relationship between spatially-varying metric M(x) (used for geodesics/Log maps) and the constant covariance Σ used in the Riemannian normal and gradient derivations.  

Requests / Questions for authors:
- Clarify hyperparameter selection protocol and report results on held-out test data or via cross-validation.  
- Explain precisely how M(x) relates to the constant covariance Σ and whether gradients assume a fixed metric.  
- Justify the D-dimensional manifold-in-D-space assumption and discuss scalability to high-dimensional settings with d << D.  
- Provide experiments where data are generated from a standard GMM to assess whether LAND degrades on simpler linear data.  
- State how cluster assignments were determined for the F-measure (e.g., max posterior vs. minimum geodesic distance).  

Overall:
- Promising and interesting direction, but the paper needs stronger experimental rigor and clearer exposition of key theoretical/notation points.
