This short paper extends the standard multivariate normal density by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The outcome is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold-learning setting with broad applicability (as evidenced by the reported experiments). Importantly, it offers, very succinctly, a complete account of a maximum-likelihood-type parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture of LANDS makes it particularly practical as a competitor to the now-standard GMM. The experiments are (at least for this reviewer) quite exciting and open a wide avenue for future research. Language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are excellent.
