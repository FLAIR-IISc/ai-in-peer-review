This concise paper augments the standard multivariate normal by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive, smoothly varying Riemannian metric. The resulting model effectively generalizes the normal distribution to a manifold-learning framework with broad applicability, as evidenced by the presented experiments. Notably, it offers a very compact yet complete description of a maximum-likelihood parameter estimation procedure. The extension to mixtures of LANDs makes the approach particularly practical as a direct alternative to the widely used GMM. The experiments are, in this reviewer’s view, compelling and suggest numerous promising directions for future work. The writing, organization, bibliography, and fit to the NIPS scope are excellent.
