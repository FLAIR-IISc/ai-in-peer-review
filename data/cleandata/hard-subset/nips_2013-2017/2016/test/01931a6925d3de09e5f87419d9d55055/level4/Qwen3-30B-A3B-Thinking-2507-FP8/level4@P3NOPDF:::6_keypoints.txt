- Introduces LAND: a locally adaptive normal distribution using a position-dependent Riemannian metric M(x) and geodesic distances via exponential/log maps.  
- Geodesic distances are estimated by solving Euler–Lagrange equations; Mahalanobis distance is computed in tangent space using the log map.  
- Parameters fitted by maximum likelihood with a steepest-descent algorithm; normalization constant approximated by Monte Carlo.  
- Evaluated on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task, compared to Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs).  
- Strength: Technical contributions are sound, coherent integration of metric learning, geodesic computation, and distribution estimation.  
- Strength: Euler–Lagrange derivation, steepest-descent fitting, and Monte Carlo approximation are clearly described and rigorous.  
- Strength: Synthetic experiments show LAND models manifold-structured data well; clustering performance is competitive with GMMs.  
- Strength: Exposition of Riemannian concepts is accessible for readers familiar with manifold learning.  
- Weakness: Notation/assumptions unclear—Σ is overloaded; relation between global Σ, local M(x), and the kernel initialization is ambiguous.  
- Weakness: Manifold assumption (data lies on a D-dimensional manifold embedded in R^D) is atypical; paper should address applicability to d << D settings.  
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks rigor—σ is tuned on training set risking overfitting; no held-out test results for clustering.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison on non-manifold data (e.g., standard Gaussians) to justify advantage over GMMs when manifold assumption is violated.  
- Recommendation: Clarify M(x)/Σ/kernel relationship, report test-set results, compare to GMMs on non-manifold and higher-dimensional settings, and discuss scalability to d << D.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions pending clarifications and stronger evaluation.
