- Proposes using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution to better model data manifolds.
- Addresses defining a Riemannian metric from high-dimensional observations and estimating a curved Gaussian on that geometry.
- Methodological issue: the metric (Eq. 7) is hand-crafted, not learned, relying on fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ set empirically.
- The paper lacks discussion of σ’s intuitive effect, selection procedure, and sensitivity, leaving the approach incomplete.
- Unclear whether metric learning could be integrated to jointly optimize ρ and σ with LAND.
- The proposed metric is not coordinate invariant; the paper should discuss invariance and whether a full (non-diagonal) metric would resolve this.
- Needs stronger connection to existing Riemannian metric learning and manifold learning literature.
- Main experimental criticism: the initial comparison is uninformative because LAND’s flexibility can be matched by GMM with more components.
- Recommend redesigning experiments so each method selects its optimal number of components (e.g., via BIC) for a fair comparison of manifold modeling ability.
- Must quantify LAND’s computational cost, scalability limits, and compare runtime/complexity versus Gaussian estimation.
- Suggest demonstrating applicability on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) and addressing overfitting observed with excessive LAND components; note geodesics remain slightly curved.
