This paper introduces an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, namely the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, which is based on Riemannian Manifolds. The authors propose a Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for the distribution's parameters and evaluate their method using experiments on both artificial and real-world data. The paper is well-written, with a solid theoretical foundation and effective experiments.

However, compared to Simo-Serra et al. [19], the paper's novelty is not substantial. A major concern is that the differences between the proposed method and the state-of-the-art are not clearly explained until the conclusion. To improve the paper's impact, these differences should be explicitly stated in the introduction, which currently lacks a reference to [19]. By doing so, the authors can provide a clearer motivation for their work and help readers understand the significance of their contributions.
