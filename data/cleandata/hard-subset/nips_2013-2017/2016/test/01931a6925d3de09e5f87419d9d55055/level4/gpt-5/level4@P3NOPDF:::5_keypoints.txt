- Summary: Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a data-driven Riemannian metric (kernel-weighted local covariances), extended to mixtures and evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Strength: Conceptually appealing link between Gaussian modeling and manifold geometry by substituting Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance.

- Strength: Spatially varying metric can capture local anisotropy and curvature, improving modeling on nonlinear manifolds.

- Strength: Natural extension to mixture models broadens applicability.

- Strength: Timely topic given interest in geometry-aware probabilistic models.

- Major concern 1 (Eq.7): Construction of inverse metric as kernel-weighted sum of local covariances seems ad hoc—needs stronger intuition/theoretical justification and conditions for smoothness and positive-definiteness.

- Major concern 1 (kernel): Clarify role of Gaussian kernel—bandwidth selection, dependence on dimension/local density, weight normalization, and sensitivity of results.

- Major concern 2 (consistency Eq.7 vs Eq.11): Unclear whether “local covariance” in Eq.7 is the same as the MAP-estimated quantity in Eq.11; unify notation and explicitly distinguish empirical local covariances, inverse metric tensor, and global parameters.

- Major concern 2 (exposition): Add a concise generative-model paragraph explaining the role of each parameter before the MAP objective.

- Major concern 3 (geodesics): Explain how geodesics are computed in practice (k-NN/shortest paths vs ODE integration), their fidelity to the continuous metric, and whether distances are differentiable w.r.t. parameters and usable in optimization.

- Major concern 4 (complexity): Provide per-iteration complexity in N, D, K, k (neighborhood construction, geodesic evaluation, precomputations, normalization), memory footprint, and compare wall-clock times and convergence against standard GMMs.

- Major concern 4 (scalability): Discuss scalability strategies (landmarks, Nyström, mini-batching, sparse updates) for larger datasets.

- Major concern 5 (experiments): Current experiments are too limited—add strong baselines (GMM diag/tied/full, t-mixtures, KDE, Isomap+GMM, recent manifold-aware models), held-out log-likelihood, classification/clustering metrics, sensitivity analyses, ablations isolating geodesic benefit, and runtime/memory comparisons with CIs.

- Major concern 5 (EEG): Clarify EEG preprocessing, dimensionality reduction, task definition, and report multiple runs/confidence intervals.

- Major concern 6 (statistical/numerical): Address degeneracy/overfitting in local covariances (regularization/shrinkage, eigenvalue floors), show how LAND normalization constant is computed/approximated, and detail initialization and convergence behavior.

- Minor comments: Fix notation (use ∇ for gradient), correct numerous typos, define symbols on first use and avoid overloading, and improve figure readability and captions.

- Overall assessment: Intriguing and principled idea but requires much stronger justification, clearer algorithmic exposition, complexity/runtime analysis, and comprehensive experiments; current form raises practical applicability concerns and leans toward rejection unless addressed.
