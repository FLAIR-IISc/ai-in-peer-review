This paper presents a generalization of the multivariate normal density by substituting the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. This approach effectively extends the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, and its wide applicability is well-supported by the reported experiments. A crucial contribution is the paper's succinct yet complete account of a maximum likelihood procedure for parameter estimation. Furthermore, the extension of this method to a mixture model makes it a particularly practical and compelling alternative to the standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experimental results are compelling, highlighting numerous promising directions for future work. The manuscript's language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the conference are all excellent.
