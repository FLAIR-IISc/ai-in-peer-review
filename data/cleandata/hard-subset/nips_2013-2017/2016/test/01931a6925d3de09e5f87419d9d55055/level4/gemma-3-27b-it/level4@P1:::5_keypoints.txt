- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic distance on a manifold, replacing the Mahalanobis distance; extended to mixtures and evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Interesting idea: using geodesic distance (Isomap-like) to model data on nonlinear manifolds.

- Eq. (7)’s inverse metric tensor formulation appears ad hoc; authors should provide intuition for the Gaussian-kernel weighting of local covariance contributions.

- Unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MLE in Eq. (11): are they the same estimate or distinct quantities?

- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; paper lacks complexity/runtime analysis compared to standard EM.

- Need explicit accounting of computational cost for solving ODEs to compute geodesics and evaluating exponential/logarithmic maps.

- Theoretical contribution is interesting but practical viability remains uncertain without computational evidence.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel Riemannian approach, maximum-entropy theoretical foundation, demonstrated improvements over GMMs, and clear mathematical presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: missing detailed computational analysis, limited experiments and ablations, clarity issues in derivations, and insufficient justification for using a Gaussian kernel.

- Minor correction: Algorithm 1, line 4 should use ‘∇’ (gradient) instead of ‘d’.

- Suggestion: expand related work to cover recent advances in manifold learning and generative models.

- Recommendation: accept conditionally if authors address scalability/runtime, clarify derivations (Eq. 7 vs Eq. 11), and justify kernel choice with additional experiments/analysis.
