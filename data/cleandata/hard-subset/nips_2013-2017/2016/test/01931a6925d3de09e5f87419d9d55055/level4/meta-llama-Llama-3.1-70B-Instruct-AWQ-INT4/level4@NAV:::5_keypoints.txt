- Paper proposes locally adaptive normal distributions using learned geodesic manifold traversal distance, and extends to a mixture of LANDs, evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Using geodesic distance (like Isomap) is an interesting idea but needs clearer justification.  
- The inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary and requires a more detailed explanation.  
- Authors should justify using a Gaussian kernel for weighting local covariances in the weighted sum.  
- Clarify whether the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11) denote the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; main concern is scalability and computational efficiency of the method.  
- Report computational complexity and running time comparisons to standard EM.  
- Practical applicability may be limited despite the theoretical contribution.  
- Minor correction: in Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5 replace 'd' with '∇'.
