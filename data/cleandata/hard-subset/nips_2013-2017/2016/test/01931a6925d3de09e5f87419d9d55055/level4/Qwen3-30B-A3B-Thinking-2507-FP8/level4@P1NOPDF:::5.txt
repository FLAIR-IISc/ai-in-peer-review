

Summary: This paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) model based on geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from data. The proposed distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance in Gaussian distributions, with an extension to mixtures of LANDs. Performance is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.  

Major comments: The core idea of using a geodesic distance (reminiscent of Isomap) is intriguing. However, several aspects of the paper lack clarity. First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) appears ad hoc. The authors should provide deeper intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances in the weighted sum. Second, I am confused by the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Equation (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Equation (11). Do these equations describe the same quantity? Clarification is essential.  

The experimental evaluation is insufficient. The synthetic dataset results are underwhelming, and the EEG evaluation lacks meaningful comparison to relevant baselines. More critically, the paper omits any discussion of scalability and computational efficiency. The authors must address: What is the computational complexity of the proposed method? How does its runtime compare to standard EM for Gaussian mixtures? Without this analysis, the practical viability of LANDs remains questionable. While the theoretical contribution is valuable, the method may have limited practical applicability due to these unresolved efficiency concerns.  

Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, "d" should be replaced with "∇".
