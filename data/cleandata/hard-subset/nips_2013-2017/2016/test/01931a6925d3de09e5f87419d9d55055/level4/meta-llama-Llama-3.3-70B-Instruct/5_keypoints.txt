- Summary: proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance (replacing Mahalanobis), with a mixture extension evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.
- The idea of using a geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is intriguing.
- The paper suffers from clarity issues throughout.
- The inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary and needs clearer justification.
- The choice of a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances requires a stronger rationale.
- There is a potential discrepancy between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11); clarify whether they denote the same quantity.
- Experimental evaluations are somewhat lacking in rigor and scope.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; provide complexity analysis and runtime comparisons to standard EM.
- Practical applicability may be limited unless computational and clarity issues are resolved.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 lines 4–5 should use ∇ (nabla) instead of d.
