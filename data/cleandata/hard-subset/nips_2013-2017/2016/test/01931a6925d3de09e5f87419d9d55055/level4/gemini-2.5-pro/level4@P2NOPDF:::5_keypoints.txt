- Novel contribution: defines a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using learned geodesic distances and proposes a mixture-of-LANDs extension.  
- Clarity issues: the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and lacks theoretical justification, particularly the use of a Gaussian kernel for weights.  
- Unclear relations: the connection between the local covariance definition (Eq. (7)) and its MAP estimation (Eq. (11)) is not clearly explained—are they the same quantity and how is the transition justified?  
- Limited experiments: evaluation is restricted to one synthetic and one EEG dataset, insufficient to demonstrate generalizability or advantages over established methods.  
- Scalability concerns: no formal computational complexity analysis is provided for the algorithm with respect to dataset size and dimensionality.  
- Empirical efficiency missing: the paper lacks runtime comparisons against standard baselines (e.g., EM for GMMs) to assess practical viability.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and theoretically appealing idea but practical applicability is questionable without clearer formulation, broader experiments, and complexity/runtime analysis.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1 likely contains a typo—`d` should be `∇` to denote the gradient.
