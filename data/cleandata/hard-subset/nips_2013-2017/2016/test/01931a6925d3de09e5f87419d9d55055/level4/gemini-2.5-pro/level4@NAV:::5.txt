**Summary:**
This paper introduces a novel probability distribution, termed the locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND), which is defined using a geodesic manifold traversal distance. This distance is learned adaptively from the data and replaces the conventional Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. The work also describes an extension to a mixture of LANDs model. The performance of the proposed method is assessed using both a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.

**Major comments:**
The central idea of employing a geodesic distance, in a manner similar to Isomap, is highly interesting and presents a novel contribution. However, the manuscript suffers from a few points of unclarity.

First, the formulation for the inverse of the metric tensor in Equation (7) lacks clear motivation and seems ad hoc. Could the authors provide more intuition regarding the use of a Gaussian kernel for weighting the sum of local covariances?

Second, I found the relationship between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Equation (7) and the subsequent MAP estimation of the same term in Equation (11) to be confusing. It is unclear if these equations refer to the same quantity, and this should be clarified.

Furthermore, the experimental validation is underdeveloped. My most significant concern, however, is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. The paper is missing a critical analysis of its computational complexity. For instance, what is the runtime of the algorithm in comparison to a standard Expectation-Maximization implementation?

In conclusion, while the paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution, its practical applicability remains questionable without a more thorough analysis of its computational performance and more extensive empirical evaluation.

**Minor comments:**
There appears to be a minor typographical error in Algorithm 1. In lines 4 and 5, the variable `d` should be replaced with the gradient symbol `\nabla`.
