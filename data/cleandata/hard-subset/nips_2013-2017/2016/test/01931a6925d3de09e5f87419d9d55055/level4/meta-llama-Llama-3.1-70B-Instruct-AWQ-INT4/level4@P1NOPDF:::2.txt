This brief paper extends the common multivariate normal density by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric. The result is a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting, with wide applicability as illustrated by the reported experiments. Importantly, it provides a concise and complete account of a maximum likelihood-type parameter estimation procedure. The extension to a mixture of LANDS makes it a practical competitor to the now-standard Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM). The experiments are (at least for this reviewer) quite exciting and open a wide avenue for future research. The language, structure, bibliography, and relevance to the NIPS remit are all excellent.
