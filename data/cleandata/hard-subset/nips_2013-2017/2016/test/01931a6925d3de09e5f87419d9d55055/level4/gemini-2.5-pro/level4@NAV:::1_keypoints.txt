- Introduces LAND: a parametric metric on a nonparametric space combining metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics via a Riemannian normal distribution.  
- Presents maximum likelihood estimation for the mean and covariance defining a Mahalanobis distance on manifolds, with geodesic point-to-point distances.  
- Core learning approach: use the inverse of a local, diagonal covariance matrix at each point; framework extended to mixtures of LANDs.  
- Significant contribution: a generative, parametric model that can generalize beyond the training set and links metric learning to Riemannian statistics.  
- Primary concern: scalability and stability — computing and inverting a local covariance per data point is computationally challenging and unstable in high dimensions.  
- Requests formal analysis of computational complexity and clearer definition of “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 10, or 100+ dimensions).  
- Literature positioning is weak: several prior methods also learn point-wise diagonal metrics or use local covariances (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros, Local PCA) and should be better related.  
- Sensitivity to the Gaussian kernel width sigma is not adequately addressed; need robustness analysis and clarification whether sigma is global or adapts to local density.  
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete/imbalanced: visualizations omit strong intrinsic competitors and favor GMM (a weak baseline); stronger results from the supplement should be moved into the main paper.  
- Recommend adding computational-complexity comparisons between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify trade-offs for different data regimes.  
- The mixture-of-LANDs extension is promising for multimodal real-world data and deserves more detailed exploration.  
- Paper is overall very well written and clear.
