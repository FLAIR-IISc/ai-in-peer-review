The paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned adaptively from the data. This distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. An extension to a mixture of LANDs is also provided. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world dataset of EEG measurements.

The idea of utilizing a geodesic distance, similar to Isomaps, is intriguing and demonstrates potential. However, there are several areas where the paper's clarity can be improved. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears to be ad hoc. The authors should provide more intuition behind the use of a Gaussian kernel for the weights in a weighted sum of local covariances. Additionally, the distinction between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear. It would be beneficial to clarify whether these refer to the same quantity.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluations are somewhat lacking. A more significant concern, however, is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. For instance, it would be helpful to provide the computational complexity and running time compared to standard EM algorithms. In the reviewer's opinion, the paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution but may have limited practical applicability.

Minor comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, 'd' should be replaced with '\nabla'.
