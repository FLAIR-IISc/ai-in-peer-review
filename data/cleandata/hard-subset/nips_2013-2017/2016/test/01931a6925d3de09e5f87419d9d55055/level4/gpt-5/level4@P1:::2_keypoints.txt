- Summary: Proposes LAND, a multivariate normal using a learned Riemannian metric, with ML estimation (gradient + Monte Carlo normalization) and a mixture-EM extension; outperforms GMMs on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Quality: Technically sound formulation; normalization constant handled via scalable Monte Carlo sampling in tangent space; ML algorithm and block-coordinate updates are well motivated.  
- Mixture extension: Natural and practical; EM derivation accounts for LAND-specific normalization.  
- Experiments: Concise but compelling—captures nonlinear structure with fewer components than GMMs and improves EEG sleep-stage alignment.  
- Clarity: Clearly written, well organized, with adequate geometric preliminaries and appropriate references; fits NIPS scope.  
- Originality: Novel adaptation of the Riemannian normal to a learned metric, yielding a parametric generative model on manifolds without explicit embedding.  
- Significance: Broad applicability where Gaussian assumptions fail; mixture LAND provides a practical alternative to GMMs and opens further research directions.  
- Pros: Conceptually clean generalization, complete/practical ML procedure, scalable normalization, usable EM mixture, strong empirical results, excellent writing and relevance.  
- Cons: Reviewer notes no major weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—well motivated, technically solid, practically implementable, and empirically effective; valuable alternative to GMMs.
