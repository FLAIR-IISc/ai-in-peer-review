- Proposes using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved Gaussian (LAND) to better model data manifolds — an interesting application of Riemannian geometry.  
- The metric is not learned: Eq. (7) uses fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ chosen empirically, making the “learning a metric” claim misleading.  
- The paper must explain the intuitive effect of σ, provide a principled selection method, and explore joint optimization of ρ and σ with LAND.  
- The proposed diagonal metric lacks coordinate invariance (e.g., behavior changes under rotation); discuss invariance, whether a full non-diagonal metric fixes this, and provide deeper intuition for Eq. (7).  
- The paper should engage more thoroughly with existing Riemannian metric learning and manifold learning literature.  
- The first experiment is flawed: comparisons with GMM are uninformative unless each method can select its optimal number of components (e.g., via BIC); LAND’s apparent overfitting needs proper contextualization.  
- Provide quantitative measures of computational cost and scalability (runtime scaling, max dataset size/dimensionality) and compare LAND vs GMM under equivalent computational constraints (e.g., MNIST).  
- After authors’ response, note that geodesics approaching straight lines increase vulnerability to local noise and overfitting remains a concern; experiments must be redesigned and these issues addressed in revision.
