Summary: This paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance that is learned adaptively from the data, replacing the traditional Mahalanobis distance used in Gaussian distributions. The authors also extend this concept to a mixture of locally adaptive normal distributions (LANDs). The proposed method is evaluated using both synthetic and real-world EEG measurement datasets.

Major Comments: The idea of utilizing geodesic distance, similar to Isomaps, is intriguing. However, there are several areas that require clarification. Firstly, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears to be somewhat arbitrary. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation for employing a Gaussian kernel for the weights in the weighted sum of local covariances. Furthermore, the distinction between the local covariance matrix defined in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear. It is essential to establish whether these refer to the same quantity. Additionally, the experimental evaluations are limited, and the primary concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. For instance, what is the computational complexity and running time compared to standard EM? In my opinion, the paper offers an interesting theoretical contribution but may have limited practical applicability.

Minor Comments: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5 should be corrected to replace 'd' with '\nabla'.
