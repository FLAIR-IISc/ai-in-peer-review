- Novel idea: leveraging Riemannian geometry to define a LAND is theoretically interesting but underdeveloped due to technical and experimental gaps.  
- The metric in Eq.7 is hand-crafted with fixed hyperparameters (ρ, σ) instead of being learned or justified.  
- The sensitivity and intuitive effect of σ (and ρ) on local curvature are unexplored, with no guidance on how to select them.  
- The authors miss jointly optimizing ρ and σ with LAND parameters as a natural and necessary extension.  
- Eq.7 appears to violate Riemannian coordinate invariance—rotating coordinates yields qualitatively different geometry.  
- The paper must clarify whether a full non-diagonal metric would resolve invariance and provide intuition for Eq.7’s design, plus cite metric-learning literature.  
- Experimental comparison is unfair: fixing K for LAND vs GMM biases results since LAND can model manifolds with fewer components.  
- A fair evaluation should use model-selection (e.g., BIC) and show performance vs K for both models, including demonstrating LAND’s overfitting at large K.  
- Missing scalability analysis: no runtime or complexity benchmarks (e.g., geodesic computation cost), and no tests on larger datasets like MNIST.  
- Required revisions to accept: justify/learn hyperparameters, resolve coordinate-invariance concerns, redesign experiments with model selection, and provide scalability/runtime analysis.
