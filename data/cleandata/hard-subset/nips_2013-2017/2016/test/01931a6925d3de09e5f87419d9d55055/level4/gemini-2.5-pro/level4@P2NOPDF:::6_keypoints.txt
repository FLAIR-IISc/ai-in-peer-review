- Novelty: Proposes LAND, a locally adaptive normal distribution that models data with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x) on the data space.  
- Core idea: Defines density in the tangent space at a mean via the logarithm map and applies a Mahalanobis normal on geodesic-projected points.  
- Technical approach: Uses Euler–Lagrange ODEs to estimate exponential/log maps, steepest descent for parameter estimation, and Monte Carlo to approximate the normalization constant.  
- Clarity: Manifold background and learning-algorithm derivations are generally well explained for the target audience.  
- Experimental concerns: Hyperparameter tuning (kernel width σ) is unclear and appears to be chosen to optimize reported metrics, risking overfitting and inflated results.  
- Baselines: Comparison only to GMM on manifold-structured data is limited; need experiments where GMM is ground truth to reveal failure modes.  
- Scalability: Experiments are low-dimensional; no evaluation on high-dimensional data to demonstrate scalability or practical applicability.  
- Manifold assumption: Assumes intrinsic dimension d equals ambient dimension D (full-rank M(x)), which departs from common d << D settings and limits applicability.  
- Notation/interpretability issue: Ambiguous use of Σ—unclear relationship between local metric M(x)=Σ⁻¹ (kernel-based) and the learned global covariance Σ; needs disambiguation.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify whether M(x) and Σ are the same or distinct, how cluster assignments were determined (e.g., nearest geodesic mean), and how the method extends to d << D.  
- Recommendation: Technically sound and promising; publishable if authors strengthen experimental rigor, clarify Σ/metric notation, and address the d=D assumption.
