- Introduces LAND: replaces Euclidean distance with geodesic distance from a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x).  
- Defines a normal on tangent space via the logarithmic map and Mahalanobis distance.  
- Derives MLEs for mean/covariance, using steepest descent for non-closed-form updates.  
- Approximates the intractable normalization constant with a Monte Carlo method.  
- Evaluated on synthetic manifolds and a real clustering task, compared to a standard GMM.  
- Strength: technically solid use of Euler–Lagrange for geodesics and log map formulation.  
- Strength: practical parameter estimation (steepest descent for parameters, Monte Carlo for normalization).  
- Strength: effective synthesis of metric learning, geodesic computation, and probabilistic modeling.  
- Major concern: unclear/likely flawed hyperparameter selection (sigma appears tuned on training and reported directly, risking overfitting).  
- Major concern: incomplete baselines—no experiments showing LAND vs GMM when data are truly Gaussian or Gaussian mixtures.  
- Major concern: experiments limited to low dimensions; no high-dimensional evaluations to assess scalability.  
- Modeling concern: restrictive assumption d = D (full-rank M(x)), limiting applicability to typical low-dimensional manifold settings (d < D).  
- Notational/clarity issue: ambiguous use of Sigma—unclear relationship between local metric M(x), kernel estimate, and the learned global covariance.  
- Minor clarity issue: clustering assignment procedure (presumably min geodesic distance) is not explicitly stated.
