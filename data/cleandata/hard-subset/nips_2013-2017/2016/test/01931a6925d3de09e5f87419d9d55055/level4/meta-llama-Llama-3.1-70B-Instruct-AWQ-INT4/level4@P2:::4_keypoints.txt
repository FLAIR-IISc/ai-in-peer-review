- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that uses Riemannian manifolds to model complex, non-linear data relationships.  
- Presents a thorough theoretical foundation for LAND, including a maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) scheme for parameter inference.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates effectiveness at modeling non-trivial probability distributions.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the methodology easy to follow.  
- Theoretical background on Riemannian manifolds and derivation of LAND are sound and well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and covers multiple data types and comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Lacks a clear, detailed discussion of contributions and novelty relative to prior work.  
- Needs a more explicit comparison with Simo-Serra et al. [19] and other related methods to clarify differences.  
- Should clarify the specific advantages of the proposed approach over existing methods.  
- Requires a more detailed discussion of the significance and broader impact of the method.  
- Review scores the paper highly on quality, clarity, and originality but requests stronger positioning against prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on manuscript revisions to address comparisons, clarify advantages, and discuss significance.
