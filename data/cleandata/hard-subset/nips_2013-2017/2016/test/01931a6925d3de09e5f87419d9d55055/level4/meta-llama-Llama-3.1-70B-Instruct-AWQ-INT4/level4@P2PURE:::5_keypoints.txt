- Proposes LAND: a locally adaptive normal using a learned geodesic manifold distance (vs. Mahalanobis), with a mixture extension and tests on synthetic and EEG data.
- Using a geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is an intriguing idea.
- The inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears arbitrary and needs justification.
- Authors should explain the rationale for using a Gaussian kernel for weights in the weighted sum of local covariances.
- Unclear distinction between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11); clarify whether they are the same.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and should be expanded.
- Scalability and computational efficiency are a major concern; provide complexity and runtime comparison to standard EM.
- Practical applicability may be limited despite the theoretical contribution.
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, "d" should be replaced with "\nabla".
