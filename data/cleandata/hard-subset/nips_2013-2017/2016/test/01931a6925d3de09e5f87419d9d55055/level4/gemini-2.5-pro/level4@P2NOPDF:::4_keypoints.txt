- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a Riemannian-manifold-based density estimator as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models.  
- Derives a Maximum Likelihood Estimation framework for LAND parameters.  
- Evaluates LAND on synthetic and real-world datasets with compelling experimental results.  
- Paper is clearly written and grounded in sound theoretical foundations.  
- Main weakness: poor positioning relative to prior work, especially Simo-Serra et al. [19].  
- Novel contributions over [19] are not clarified until the conclusion, leaving readers unsure of significance for most of the paper.  
- Critical differences from [19] should be established early; discussion/comparison belongs in the introduction and related work.  
- Absence of [19] from the introduction highlights the lack of contextualization; clarifying distinctions earlier would strengthen the manuscript.
