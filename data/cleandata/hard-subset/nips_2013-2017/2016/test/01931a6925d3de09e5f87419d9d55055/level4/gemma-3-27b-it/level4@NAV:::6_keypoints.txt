- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a data-dependent Riemannian metric M(x) and geodesic distances via exponential/log maps.  
- Computes geodesic distances by solving Euler–Lagrange equations; logarithmic map projects points to tangent planes.  
- Applies a Mahalanobis distance on mapped points to define a normal distribution measured along the manifold.  
- Uses maximum likelihood to estimate mean and covariance; proposes a steepest descent algorithm for absence of closed-form solutions.  
- Introduces a Monte Carlo approximation for the intractable normalization constant.  
- Evaluated on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task, compared against standard GMMs; reviewer recommends publication.  
- Technical methods are sound and clearly explained, but application analysis is somewhat incomplete.  
- Sigma (kernel) parameter selection appears to be optimized on training data, raising concerns about overfitting.  
- Novelty consists mainly of combining existing techniques (kernel-defined M(x), Euler–Lagrange geodesics, manifold-aware normal) with a tailored estimation algorithm.  
- Evaluation should include cases where data come from standard normals/GMMs and performance on very high-dimensional data.  
- The assumption D = d (data space dimensionality equals manifold dimensionality) is restrictive and should be discussed.  
- Clarity issues: Sigma notation is overloaded and the relationship between M(x) and Sigma (global vs. local/kernel-initialized) is ambiguous.  
- Repeated statements “M = Sigma^-1” in proofs reinforce ambiguity about whether M varies with x while Sigma is a single parameter.  
- Cluster membership assignment method (minimizing geodesic distance to cluster means) should be explicitly stated and validated on held-out data.  
- Introduction to manifold concepts is well-written and accessible for readers familiar with Riemannian geometry.
