- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) on Riemannian manifolds with a locally adaptive metric and develops an MLE estimator.
- Paper is clearly written with solid theoretical development and well-executed experiments on synthetic and real data.
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates practical utility, especially for data near nonlinear manifolds.
- Grounded in Riemannian statistics and manifold learning literature, but novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19] is not convincingly established.
- Key distinctions from prior mixtures of Riemannian normals, especially [19], are not clearly articulated until the conclusion.
- Reference [19] is missing from the introduction, weakening early positioning and context.
- Suggest adding explicit comparisons to Simo-Serra et al. [19] and other related work in the introduction.
- Suggest previewing main technical contributions in the introduction to highlight how they address prior limitations.
- Quality: strong theoretical grounding and careful empirical work.
- Clarity: generally good writing, but needs earlier positioning relative to [19].
- Originality: perceived as weaker due to insufficiently emphasized differences from [19].
- Significance: potentially useful for manifold-modeled data but requires sharper articulation of novelty and advantages.
- Pros for acceptance: principled MLE, strong experiments, clear exposition.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient differentiation from related work (especially [19]), delayed comparison/claims, and missing critical citation in the introduction.
