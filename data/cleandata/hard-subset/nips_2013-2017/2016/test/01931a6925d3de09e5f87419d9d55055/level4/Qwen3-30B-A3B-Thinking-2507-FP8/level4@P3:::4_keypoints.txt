- Paper proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing Euclidean metric with a data-dependent Riemannian metric to model distributions on learned manifolds.  
- Metric tensor is constructed from a local covariance (Gaussian kernel); parameters are fit by MLE using gradient descent plus Monte Carlo normalization estimation.  
- Method extends to mixture models and is evaluated on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage data, showing improved modeling of nonlinear structure.  
- Builds on Riemannian statistics and manifold learning literature, connecting to Riemannian normal distributions and manifold learning methods.  
- Strong technical quality: rigorous Riemannian derivations and clear exposition of equations and algorithms.  
- Implementation details are thorough (e.g., Monte Carlo sample size, computational considerations) aiding reproducibility.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and well-justified; synthetic and EEG results convincingly show advantages over GMMs and Riemannian least-squares.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and supported by effective figures and consistent notation.  
- Main weakness: insufficient differentiation from Simo-Serra et al. [19]; the distinction that LAND learns the manifold is not emphasized early enough.  
- Significance could be strengthened by explicit concrete examples or evidence of how LAND benefits broader applications (e.g., Naive Bayes, LDA, PCA).  
- Limited analysis of computational complexity and scalability, especially for high-dimensional data where exponential cost of logarithm map is a concern.  
- The maximum-entropy connection is noted but not deeply explored in terms of practical importance.  
- Overall originality/significance: novel and meaningful to apply Riemannian normals to learned manifolds but presentation underplays this novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better position the contribution, especially by comparing to Simo-Serra et al., clarifying scalability, and giving concrete application examples.
