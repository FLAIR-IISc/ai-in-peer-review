- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a geodesic manifold traversal distance and extends it to mixtures; evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.
- The idea of replacing Mahalanobis distance with a learned geodesic distance (similar to Isomap) is interesting and promising.
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc; the authors should provide more intuition for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances.
- The connection between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its use in the MAP estimation in Eq. (11) is unclear—are they the same quantity?
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comprehensive comparisons with other methods.
- Main concern: scalability and computational efficiency are not addressed—need complexity analysis and running time comparison to standard EM.
- Theoretical contribution is interesting but practical applicability may be limited without addressing scalability and clarity.
- Minor: Algorithm 1 should use ∇ instead of d to denote gradients in lines 4 and 5.
- Strength: novel approach to modeling data with a locally adaptive normal distribution.
- Strength: use of geodesic distance has potential cross-domain applications.
- Strength: extension to mixtures of LANDs is a useful contribution.
- Weakness: paper lacks clarity in defining and using the local covariance matrix.
- Weakness: limited experimental validation and comparisons.
- Weakness: scalability and computational efficiency concerns are not well addressed.
- Recommendation: currently leans toward rejection, but could be acceptable after revisions improving clarity and expanding experiments.
