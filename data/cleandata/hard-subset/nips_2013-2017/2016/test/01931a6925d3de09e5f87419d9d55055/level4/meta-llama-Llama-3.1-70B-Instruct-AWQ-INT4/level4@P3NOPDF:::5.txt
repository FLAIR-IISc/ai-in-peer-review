This paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance learned adaptively from the data. The geodesic distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance used in the Gaussian distribution. The authors also extend their approach to a mixture of LANDs. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world dataset of EEG measurements.

The idea of using geodesic distance, similar to Isomaps, is intriguing and represents a valuable contribution to the field. However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved for better clarity. Firstly, the definition of the inverse of the metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears to be somewhat ad hoc. The authors should provide more intuition behind the use of a Gaussian kernel for the weights in the weighted sum of local covariances. This would help readers understand the reasoning behind this choice.

Additionally, the definition of the local covariance matrix in Eq. (7) and its later MAP estimation in Eq. (11) are not clearly connected. It is unclear whether these equations refer to the same quantity. The authors should clarify this relationship to avoid confusion.

The experimental evaluations presented in the paper are limited. However, my primary concern lies in the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. Specifically, it would be beneficial to know the computational complexity and running time of the method in comparison to standard EM algorithms. This information is crucial in assessing the practical applicability of the proposed approach.

In my opinion, the paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution, but its practical applicability is limited. To strengthen the paper, the authors should address the concerns mentioned above and provide more comprehensive experimental evaluations.
