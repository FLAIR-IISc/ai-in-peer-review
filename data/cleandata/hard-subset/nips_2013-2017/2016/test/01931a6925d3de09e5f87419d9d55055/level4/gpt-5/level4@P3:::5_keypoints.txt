- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian-normal parametric density with a learned, data-adaptive metric replacing Euclidean/Mahalanobis distances.
- Key technical idea: learn a smoothly varying local-diagonal metric via kernel-smoothed local covariances and use geodesic distances in the Gaussian density.
- Derives ML estimation for mean/covariance using gradient descent plus tangent-space Monte Carlo for the normalization constant and extends to mixtures with EM.
- Strength: elegant maximum-entropy grounding and principled generalization of Gaussians to learned Riemannian geometries.
- Strength: practical ML estimation using tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids expensive boundary-value problems for Log maps.
- Strength: mixture model properly accounts for component-specific normalization constants in EM.
- Strength: empirical demos show improved fit to nonlinear structure and more meaningful cluster contours than GMMs.
- Metric construction appears ad hoc: diagonal inverse local covariance and isotropic Gaussian kernels need more justification and ablations (k-NN, full vs diagonal, pre-whitening).
- Existence/uniqueness of geodesics and behavior of Log/Exp maps on a learned metric are nontrivial and not sufficiently discussed (cut loci, numerical failures).
- Computational complexity and runtimes are missing; per-evaluation metric and Exp computations are expensive and likely scale poorly with N, D, S, K.
- Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization C(μ,Σ) may have high variance as Σ adapts; variance-reduction strategies and sensitivity to S are needed.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow: one synthetic setup and one real dataset; lacks additional baselines (KDE, spectral clustering, geodesic mixtures without normalization) and held-out metrics.
- Clarify relationship between Eq. 7 (metric learning) and Eq. 11 (ML estimation on fixed manifold); state Eq. 11 is ML, not MAP.
- Notation issues (dμφ vs ∇μφ) and Algorithm 1 ambiguities; explicitly state steepest descent direction and use consistent symbols.
- Recommend figure(s) illustrating tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization and ML mean vs intrinsic mean for clarity.
- Originality: contribution is meaningful in bringing Riemannian normals to learned manifolds and practical ML via tangent-space Monte Carlo, but must better delineate novelty vs related work (Hauberg, Tosi, Simo-Serra).
- Significance depends on scalability and robustness to higher dimensions; experiments or strategies (local neighborhoods, inducing points) for higher-D would strengthen impact.
- Specific requests: provide per-iteration complexity, wall-clock runtimes, and whether Eq. 7 is restricted to k-NN for efficiency.
- Specific requests: explain how σ and ρ are chosen and whether σ can be learned (e.g., marginal likelihood or cross-validation).
- Specific requests: report whether Monte Carlo samples are reused via reparameterization and whether variance reduction is used.
- Specific requests: quantify IVP solver failure rates, detection, and handling of inaccurate geodesics.
- Specific requests: provide ablation on diagonal-metric assumption and sensitivity analyses.
- Pros for acceptance: principled theoretical framing, correct normalization in mixtures, and evidence of empirical benefit on nonlinear data.
- Cons against acceptance: limited experiments, missing scalability/runtime analysis, heuristic metric design, and clarity issues.
- Minor comments: fix typos/grammar, consider pre-whitening or alternative local metrics, and report AIC/BIC and held-out likelihoods with error bars across initializations.
