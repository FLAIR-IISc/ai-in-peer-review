The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models, grounded in Riemannian manifold geometry. It derives a maximum likelihood estimator for the distribution’s parameters and evaluates the approach on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The manuscript is clearly written, the theoretical development appears sound, and the experiments are convincing.

However, relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19], the contribution does not present a strong novelty. The distinctions between the proposed method and the state of the art are not clearly articulated until the conclusion. These differences should be made explicit much earlier—ideally in the introduction—together with a clear statement of the paper’s specific contributions beyond [19]. Notably, [19] is not cited in the introduction, which makes it difficult for readers to position the work from the outset.

To strengthen the paper, I recommend:
- Introducing [19] in the related work section of the introduction and providing a concise, technical comparison.
- Clearly highlighting what is new: e.g., any differences in the model formulation, parameterization, optimization procedure, or theoretical guarantees relative to [19].
- Briefly explaining early on when and why the proposed distribution is preferable to GMMs and to prior manifold-based density models.

Overall, the work is well executed but appears incremental with respect to [19] as currently presented. Clarifying the novelty and positioning earlier in the paper would substantially improve its impact.
