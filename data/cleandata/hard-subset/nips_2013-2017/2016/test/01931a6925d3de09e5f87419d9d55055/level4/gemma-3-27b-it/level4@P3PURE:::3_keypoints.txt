- Proposes a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved Gaussian (LAND) to better model data manifolds — a useful application of Riemannian geometry.  
- Title is misleading: the metric is not learned but hand-crafted in Eq. 7 using hyperparameters ρ and σ.  
- σ is likely sensitive; the paper omits discussion of its intuitive effect and how to choose it.  
- Consider jointly learning the metric hyperparameters (ρ, σ) with the LAND model instead of fixing them.  
- The metric in Eq. 7 lacks coordinate-invariance; this issue needs analysis and clarification.  
- Examine whether a full (non-diagonal) metric would restore invariance and provide more intuition for Eq. 7.  
- The paper should situate itself within the substantial literature on Riemannian geometry, metric learning, and manifold learning.  
- First experiment is flawed: direct comparison is unfair because LAND’s flexibility lets it fit with fewer components while a GMM with more components could match performance.  
- Redesign experiments to let each method choose component count via principled criteria (e.g., BIC) and then compare performance.  
- Show how LAND behaves with many components to demonstrate potential overfitting.  
- Provide quantitative measurements of computational cost and scalability (practical data size limits, possible MNIST-scale experiments).  
- Note that increasing components makes LAND more vulnerable to local noise and overfitting because geodesics, though nearly straight, still curve and contribute to overfitting; revise experiments to reflect this.
