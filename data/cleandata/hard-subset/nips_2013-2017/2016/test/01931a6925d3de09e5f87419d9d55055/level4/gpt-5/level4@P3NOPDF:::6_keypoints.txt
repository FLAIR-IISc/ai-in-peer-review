- Proposes LAND: a normal-like density using geodesic distances from a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x), with geodesics computed via Euler–Lagrange ODEs and log/exp maps.
- Defines Gaussian-like density by applying a Mahalanobis norm in tangent space, and derives ML estimators optimized by steepest descent with Monte Carlo normalization.
- Strength: technically sound and clearly explained use of Riemannian geometry, geodesic computation, and log map.
- Strength: sensible, well-motivated optimization (steepest descent) and Monte Carlo partition-function estimation described carefully.
- Strength: natural and potentially impactful idea for manifold-concentrated data; manifold preliminaries are clearly presented.
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection (notably kernel bandwidth σ) is under-documented and appears ad hoc/tuned on test data, risking overfitting and biased comparisons.
- Weakness: lacks control experiments on truly Euclidean data (single Gaussian/GMM) to show LAND does not degrade performance when curvature is unnecessary.
- Weakness: practical usefulness is underdeveloped—unclear when LAND provides clear advantages for downstream tasks or likelihood evaluation.
- Weakness: model assumes full-rank ambient-dimension metric (intrinsic dimension d assumed equal to D), unclear how method handles d ≪ D or exploits low intrinsic dimensionality.
- Weakness: confusing notation around Σ and M(x); ambiguous whether Σ is global covariance, local metric inverse, or both, especially in gradient derivations.
- Weakness: clustering assignment rule is not fully specified—unclear whether assignments use geodesic distance, tangent-space Mahalanobis distance, or mixture likelihood.
- Weakness: computational aspects (cost of ODE solves for log maps, Monte Carlo sample counts, runtimes, scalability) are underreported.
- Novelty: conceptually incremental but nontrivial combination of kernel-induced metrics, geodesic computation, and tangent-space Gaussian modeling into a practical estimator.
- Suggestion: clearly specify σ and other hyperparameter selection (use cross-validation/held-out sets) and report sensitivity curves.
- Suggestion: add baselines/control experiments with Euclidean Gaussian and GMM data and report log-likelihoods and clustering performance.
- Suggestion: disambiguate notation for Σ vs M(x), revise derivations (or state assumptions if M constant), and adopt distinct symbols.
- Suggestion: precisely describe clustering assignment rule and ensure training/evaluation consistency.
- Suggestion: report computational complexity, convergence criteria, Monte Carlo sample sizes, wall-clock runtimes, and scaling experiments.
- Suggestion: discuss or explore extensions to low intrinsic-dimension manifolds (e.g., low-rank metrics or local PCA), or state this limitation.
- Suggestion: broaden baselines (e.g., kernel density estimation, spectral clustering) to better contextualize performance.
- Overall: promising, well-developed methodological contribution that needs stronger experimental validation, clearer notation, and computational analysis before publication.
