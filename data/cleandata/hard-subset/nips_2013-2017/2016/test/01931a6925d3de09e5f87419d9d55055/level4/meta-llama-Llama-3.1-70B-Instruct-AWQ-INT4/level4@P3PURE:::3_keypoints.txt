- Proposes modeling observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and defining a curved normal (LAND) to capture data manifolds.
- Addresses the problem of defining a Riemannian metric from high-dimensional observations and estimating a curved Gaussian on that geometry.
- The metric is hand-crafted (Eq.7) with hyperparameters rho and sigma fixed empirically, contrary to the paper’s “learns a metric” title.
- Needs a detailed discussion of sigma’s intuitive effect, sensitivity, and guidance for selecting it.
- Should explore jointly learning the metric hyperparameters (rho, sigma) with LAND rather than fixing them.
- The presented metric is not coordinate-invariant; the paper should discuss invariance (including full, non-diagonal metrics) and provide intuition.
- Related work in metric learning and manifold learning with Riemannian geometry should be cited and connected.
- First experiment comparison is flawed: LAND’s flexibility favors fewer components, while GMM can outperform with more components; experiments must allow each method to choose component count (e.g., via BIC).
- Recommend redesigning experiments to compare methods at their optimal model complexity and to study LAND’s overfitting when using many components.
- Quantify computational cost and scalability of LAND (runtime, limits, possible training on datasets like MNIST) since it is more expensive than Gaussian estimation.
- After authors’ response, concern remains that increased LAND components lead to vulnerability to local noise and overfitting due to curved geodesics; experiment revision is strongly suggested.
