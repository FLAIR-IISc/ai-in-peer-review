**Summary**

This paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) based on a geodesic manifold traversal distance that is learned adaptively from the data. This geodesic distance replaces the standard Mahalanobis distance within the Gaussian probability density function. The authors also develop an extension for a mixture of LANDs. The proposed model is evaluated on a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.

**Major Comments**

The central idea of employing a geodesic distance, similar to the Isomap algorithm, is intriguing. However, the paper's presentation has several points that lack clarity.

First, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) appears ad hoc. It would be beneficial for the authors to provide more intuition for their choice of a Gaussian kernel to create a weighted sum of local covariances.

Second, the relationship between the definition of the local covariance matrix in Equation (7) and its subsequent MAP estimation in Equation (11) is unclear. Do these two equations refer to the same underlying quantity?

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation is not sufficiently thorough. A major concern is the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method. The authors should provide an analysis of the computational complexity and compare the runtime of their algorithm to that of a standard EM implementation.

In its current form, the paper presents an interesting theoretical contribution, but its practical applicability is not well-established due to the aforementioned issues.

**Minor Comments**

In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, the symbol 'd' should be replaced with '∇'.
