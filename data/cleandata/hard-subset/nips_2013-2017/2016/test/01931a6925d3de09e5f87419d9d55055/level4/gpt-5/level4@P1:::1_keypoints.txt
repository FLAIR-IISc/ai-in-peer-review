- Introduces LAND: a Riemannian normal using a learned, smoothly varying metric built from local inverse diagonal covariances, with Monte Carlo normalization, gradient ML (alternating mean via Exp/Log and covariance updates), and a mixture EM; shows better capture of nonlinear structure than GMMs on synthetic and EEG data.

- Positions the work at the intersection of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics; novelty is learning the metric and incorporating it into a normalized generative model, but should be better situated among local metric learning and local covariance methods.

- Strength: elegant conceptual bridge between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics by defining a maximum-entropy Riemannian normal with a learned metric.

- Strength: practical inference via integration in the tangent space avoids repeated boundary-value solves; algorithmic choices (S samples, IVPs) make the approach implementable.

- Strength: empirical evidence that ML estimates of LAND means avoid low-density regions better than intrinsic least-squares means.

- Strength: natural mixture-of-LANDs extension increases applicability to multimodal data.

- Strength: paper is clearly written and generally easy to follow.

- Weakness: scalability concerns—computing local covariances and inverses for every point raises N, D, and memory complexity questions; need complexity analysis and practical tractable dimensional ranges.

- Weakness: pivotal kernel bandwidth σ requires robustness/sensitivity analysis and possibly data-driven selection; current experiments insufficiently explore σ sensitivity across datasets.

- Weakness: baselines and visualizations are incomplete—intrinsic least-squares estimators are competitive but not prominently shown in main figures (only GMMs), hindering fair comparison and discussion of computational trade-offs.

- Weakness: lack of empirical time/memory comparisons between LAND (Monte Carlo normalization and geodesic computations) and intrinsic least-squares estimators.

- Weakness: mixture model evaluation is limited—needs expanded experiments on model selection (K), σ sensitivity within mixtures, and comparisons to intrinsic-estimator-based mixtures.

- Weakness: learned metric is not invariant under linear transformations; paper should discuss implications and preprocessing recommendations (e.g., whitening).

- Weakness: implementation details missing—sensitivity to Monte Carlo sample size S and wall-clock runtimes for experiments should be reported.

- Quality: methodology and derivations are sound, but the paper requires more thorough analyses of computational complexity, scalability, parameter sensitivity, and stronger baselines.

- Clarity: well organized; suggested improvements include adding a σ-selection subsection, moving key intrinsic-estimator visualizations into the main text, and including runtime/scaling plots.

- Originality: novel and valuable contribution adapting the Riemannian normal to a learned manifold with normalization and mixture modeling.

- Significance: potentially impactful generative maximum-entropy model for nonlinear structure, but practical impact depends on addressing scalability and robustness.

- Pros for acceptance: clear, elegant formulation with solid grounding; practical inference procedure; demonstrated improvements over GMMs and EEG application; natural extension to mixtures.

- Cons for acceptance: unclear scalability to higher dimensions/large datasets; insufficient σ robustness evaluation; main-text baselines underemphasize intrinsic estimators; limited empirical exploration of mixtures.

- Overall recommendation: strong, well-written paper recommended for acceptance if authors address complexity/scalability analysis, expand σ robustness studies, include intrinsic baseline visuals in the main text, and strengthen mixture-model evaluation.
