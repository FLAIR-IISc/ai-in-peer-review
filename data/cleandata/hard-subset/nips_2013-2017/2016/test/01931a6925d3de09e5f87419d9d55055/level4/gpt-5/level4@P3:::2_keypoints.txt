- Summary: Proposes LAND, a locally adaptive normal using a learned Riemannian metric (from kernel-weighted local variances) with Monte Carlo normalization, gradient ML estimation, and an EM mixture extension; shows improved modeling of nonlinear structure vs GMMs on synthetic and EEG data.

- Relation to prior work: Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, differing from prior work by learning the metric from data and explicitly handling normalization constants for mixtures.

- Strength: Conceptual clarity — replacing Euclidean metric with a learned Riemannian metric is geometrically grounded and links to maximum-entropy.

- Strength: Sound algorithmic design — tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator scales practically, explicit gradients for µ and Σ, and Σ−1 factorization ensures positive-definiteness.

- Strength: Practicality — mixture extension and EM make LAND a feasible alternative to GMMs.

- Strength: Empirical evidence — captures curved structures with fewer components and yields higher clustering alignment on EEG data.

- Strength: Good fit to conference topics — combines manifold learning, probabilistic modeling, and Riemannian geometry with application value.

- Weakness: Computational complexity and scalability — normalization requires S samples and ODE integrations with kernel-weighted metric evaluations, needing complexity analysis and wall-clock comparisons.

- Suggestion: Provide per-iteration complexity in N, D, S, integration steps, and discuss approximation speedups (KD-trees, truncation, inducing points, mixtures of metrics).

- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters — performance depends on kernel width σ and Monte Carlo sample size S, with unclear selection strategy.

- Suggestion: Add principled σ/S selection (cross-validation, AIS/BIC), sensitivity plots, Monte Carlo variance analysis, and variance-reduction techniques (QMC, control variates).

- Weakness: Metric design limitations — diagonal local covariances break linear invariance and may miss correlated local structure.

- Suggestion: Discuss when diagonal metrics suffice, consider full local covariances with regularization, or evaluate whitening/local PCA preprocessing.

- Weakness: Optimization and convergence — non-convex subproblems need clarity on initialization, step sizes, and stability.

- Suggestion: Provide initialization heuristics, step-size and stopping criteria, training curves, and comparisons to natural or Riemannian gradient methods.

- Weakness: Evaluation breadth — limited baselines; should compare to embeddings+GMMs, manifold-aware density estimators, mixtures of factor analyzers/warped mixtures, and report held-out log-likelihoods.

- Weakness: Theoretical guarantees — lacks discussion of statistical properties (identifiability, consistency) beyond maximum-entropy.

- Quality: Technically sound derivations and reasonable approximation for normalization; main concerns are scaling and hyperparameter sensitivity.

- Clarity: Paper is readable and organized but needs clarification on ρ in the metric, µ/Σ initialization, ODE numerical settings, and EM update details (in supplement).

- Originality: Novel combination of Riemannian normal with a learned data-adaptive metric and explicit normalization handling for mixtures.

- Significance: High potential impact as a manifold-aware alternative to Gaussians with practical extensions and promising application results.

- Arguments for acceptance: Elegant integration of geometry and probabilistic modeling, practical algorithm, and empirical gains over GMMs.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited analysis of computational scaling, ad-hoc hyperparameter selection, restricted diagonal metric class, and missing ablation.

- Overall assessment: Strong, timely contribution leaning toward acceptance, but would be more compelling with additional scalability analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity studies, and broader baselines.
