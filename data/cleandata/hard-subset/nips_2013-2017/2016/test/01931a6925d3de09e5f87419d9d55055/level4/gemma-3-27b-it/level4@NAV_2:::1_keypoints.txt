- Proposes LAND: a novel parametric, generative model defined on a learned Riemannian manifold using the Riemannian normal distribution and geodesic distances with ML estimation of mean and covariance.  
- Learns a local metric via inverses of local (diagonal) covariances and extends to mixtures of LANDs.  
- Strong contribution bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, with clear writing and empirical results on synthetic and real data.  
- Major concern: scalability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because covariances and their inverses must be computed per data point.  
- Paper lacks a detailed computational complexity and scalability analysis, including a practical dimensionality threshold for applicability.  
- Needs better contextualization in the metric learning literature; related prior work (e.g., Frome, Malisiewicz, Hauberg) also learns per-point diagonal metrics.  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel parameter σ is underexplored: robustness/sensitivity tests and its behavior on synthetic data are missing.  
- Unclear whether σ is uniform across points or should adapt to local data density.  
- Comparisons: intrinsic estimators give comparable results but are visualized only in Fig.4; visual comparisons with intrinsic estimators are missing in Figs.5–6.  
- Should include computational-complexity comparisons between LAND and intrinsic estimators to clarify method preference under different conditions.  
- Mixtures of LANDs are a promising direction and merit further exploration.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, generative extension beyond training data, clarity, demonstrated effectiveness; Cons: scalability, limited complexity discussion, insufficient literature contextualization, lack of visual comparisons.
