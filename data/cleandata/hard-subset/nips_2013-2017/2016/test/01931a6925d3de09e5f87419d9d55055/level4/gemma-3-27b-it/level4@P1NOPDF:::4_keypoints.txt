- Proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution grounded in Riemannian manifolds as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models.  
- Derives a maximum likelihood estimator for the distribution’s parameters with rigorous mathematical formulation.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets show competitive and convincing performance.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound with solid experimental design.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty compared to prior work, notably Simo-Serra et al. [19].  
- Key distinctions from existing methods are not presented early enough; reference [19] is missing from the introduction.  
- The Riemannian formulation is mathematically elegant but its practical advantages over prior methods are not clearly demonstrated.  
- Paper explains what was done but lacks a compelling explanation of why the approach matters in practice.  
- Needs clearer discussion of how the locally adaptive distribution differs from and improves upon existing adaptive mixture models.  
- Should quantitatively compare with Simo-Serra et al. [19] on the same datasets and analyze cases of over/underperformance.  
- Introduction should acknowledge and position the work relative to related literature (especially [19]) to clarify contributions upfront.  
- Minor writing/style fixes suggested (e.g., more concise phrasing), but these are not critical.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on clearer justification of Riemannian approach, inclusion/discussion of [19] in the introduction, and a focused quantitative comparison to [19].
