- Proposes a novel method using a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and a curved normal distribution to better model data manifolds.  
- Title is misleading: the metric is not learned but hand-crafted in Eq.7 with hyperparameters rho and sigma.  
- Sigma likely sensitive; paper lacks discussion on its intuitive effect and guidelines for selecting it.  
- Recommend merging metric learning with LAND so rho and sigma can be learned jointly.  
- Metric in Eq.7 is not coordinate-invariant (diagonal form); need discussion of invariance and the full-metric case.  
- More intuition and explanation of the specific metric form (Eq.7) are needed.  
- Paper should better connect to the extensive metric-learning and manifold-learning literature.  
- First experiment comparison is flawed: LAND uses fewer components to fit manifold, but GMM can match with more components; experiments should allow model selection (e.g., BIC).  
- Authors should demonstrate LAND’s tendency to overfit with many components and its vulnerability to local noise.  
- Need quantitative measurements of computational cost, scalability limits, and experiments on larger datasets (e.g., MNIST) comparing LAND vs GMM.  
- After authors’ response, concern remains that curved geodesics increase overfitting risk; experimental revision is strongly suggested.
