This paper introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution, a model based on Riemannian Manifolds proposed as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models. The authors derive a Maximum Likelihood Estimator for the model's parameters and evaluate their method with experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper is well-written, with a sound theoretical basis and strong experimental support. However, its primary weakness is the limited novelty when compared to the work of Simo-Serra et al. [19]. The authors fail to clearly articulate the differences between their proposed method and the state-of-the-art until the conclusion. This critical comparison should have been established from the outset; notably, reference [19] is absent from the introduction.
