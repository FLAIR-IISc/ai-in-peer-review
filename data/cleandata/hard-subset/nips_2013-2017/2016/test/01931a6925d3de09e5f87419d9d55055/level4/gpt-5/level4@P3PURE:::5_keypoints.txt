- Paper proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using geodesic manifold distances and extends it to mixtures, evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.
- Central idea of using geodesic distance (similar to Isomap) is compelling and theoretically interesting, but the presentation lacks clarity.
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) seems ad hoc; authors should justify using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances and assess sensitivity to kernel bandwidth.
- Unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimation in Eq. (11); clarify whether they are the same quantity or serve different roles in learning.
- Experimental evaluation is weak in scope and depth and does not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits of LANDs over standard methods.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; the paper needs a clear complexity analysis and running-time comparisons (e.g., versus EM for Gaussian mixtures).
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but currently limited applicability due to issues with clarity, scalability, and empirical validation.
- Minor: In Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, replace d with ∇.
