1. Originality: Proposes a novel combination of a data-dependent Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal distributions to build a flexible generative model for nonlinear manifolds.

2. Theoretical foundation: Method is well-grounded in differential geometry and information theory; LAND is the maximum-entropy distribution for given mean/covariance on the learned manifold.

3. Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and provides a concise introduction to required Riemannian concepts before the model and inference.

4. Metric construction & hyperparameter sensitivity: The learned metric depends on two empirically set hyperparameters (especially kernel bandwidth σ), which appear highly sensitive; needs intuition, principled selection (e.g., CV/marginal likelihood), ablation, or integrated hyperparameter learning.

5. Lack of invariance: The diagonal metric (Eq.7) is not rotation-invariant, causing different metrics for rotated data; authors should discuss trade-offs and consider whether full local covariance would resolve this.

6. Experimental rigor: GMM comparison is not fully fair—need model selection (BIC/AIC) for both methods, and experiments addressing overfitting (mixtures curving to noise) to provide objective assessment.

7. Computational cost & scalability: Estimation uses ODE solves and Monte Carlo and is more expensive than Gaussians; paper should report complexity, runtime comparisons, and scalability experiments on larger datasets.

8. Positioning in related work: Needs stronger connection to the metric learning literature to clarify novelty and relation to existing distance-metric/Riemannian methods.

9. Pro-acceptance summary: The idea is novel, elegant, and theoretically sound with potential broad impact as a building block for generative models on manifolds.

10. Con-acceptance summary: Practicality is limited by empirical σ selection, non-invariant metric, and unaddressed computational/experimental concerns; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
