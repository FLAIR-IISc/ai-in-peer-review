## Review:

This paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) defined using a geodesic manifold traversal distance, learned directly from the data. This distance effectively substitutes the traditional Mahalanobis distance within the Gaussian framework, and the authors further extend the approach to a mixture of LANDs. The method’s performance is demonstrated through evaluation on both a synthetic dataset and a real-world EEG dataset.

The core idea of leveraging a geodesic distance, reminiscent of techniques like Isomap, is compelling. However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. Specifically, the definition of the inverse metric tensor in Equation (7) appears somewhat arbitrary. The authors should elaborate on the rationale behind employing a Gaussian kernel for weighting the local covariances in the weighted sum. Furthermore, the presentation creates confusion regarding the relationship between the local covariance matrix defined in Equation (7) and its subsequent Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) estimation in Equation (11) – it is unclear if these represent the same quantity. 

The experimental validation is currently unconvincing. More significantly, the scalability and computational efficiency of the proposed method are major concerns. The authors should address the computational complexity of LAND and provide a comparative runtime analysis against standard Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms. While the paper presents a potentially valuable theoretical contribution, its practical applicability remains questionable.

Finally, a minor point: in Algorithm 1, lines 4 and 5, “d” should be replaced with “∇”.
