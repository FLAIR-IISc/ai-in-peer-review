- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replace Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance in Gaussian with geodesic distance on a learned, density-adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Metric is nonparametric: diagonal local covariance estimated via Gaussian kernels; manifold is learned from data.  
- Defines Riemannian normal using Log/Exp maps and develops Monte Carlo estimator for normalization constant, block coordinate ML for µ/Σ, and EM for mixtures.  
- Positions work as bridging manifold learning and statistics on manifolds; novelty is end-to-end ML estimation on a learned manifold.  
- Main technical flaw: normalization constant derivation omits Jacobian |det dExpµ(v)| (volume density), so estimator and gradients are biased unless justified.  
- Gradients (Eq.12–13) are incomplete because they ignore derivatives of the exponential map Jacobian.  
- Computational cost is high: many Log (BVP) and Exp (IVP) solves per iteration plus Monte Carlo sampling, but no complexity or runtime analysis provided.  
- Potential scalability issues with large datasets, high dimension, or many mixture components (e.g., S=3000 samples).  
- Optimization is nonconvex; paper lacks convergence analysis, ablations on step sizes, conditioning, or sensitivity to initialization.  
- Using a diagonal metric limits local anisotropy and may introduce bias; implications not studied.  
- Synthetic experiments are illustrative, but EEG clustering protocol uses label-informed σ tuning, inflating results versus baselines.  
- Baselines are limited; missing comparisons to KDEs, full-covariance mixtures, spectral/diffusion clustering, and manifold-aware KDEs.  
- Clarity issues: change-of-variables for C needs precise statement (injectivity radius, cut locus, normal coordinates) and distinction between Euclidean/Riemannian gradients.  
- Implementation details for Log/Exp solvers, counts of IVPs/BVPs per iteration, and algorithmic costs are missing.  
- Originality: novel combination of learned density-adaptive Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal and ML estimation; EM with component-specific normalization is useful.  
- Significance is limited currently by the normalization omission, computational cost, and limited evaluation; could be valuable if corrected and validated.  
- Pros: conceptually appealing bridge between manifold learning and manifold statistics; clear algorithmic recipe; empirical gains on curved synthetic data; includes normalization in mixture likelihood.  
- Cons: missing exponential map Jacobian in normalization and gradients; heavy computational burden without timing/complexity analysis; diagonal metric restrictive and sensitive to scaling; EEG hyperparameter tuning uses labels.  
- Suggested fixes: incorporate the exponential map Jacobian (via Jacobi fields or autodiff through solver) or adopt a pullback metric/normalizing-flow-style correction.  
- Suggest stating theoretical conditions (injectivity radius, curvature bounds) or quantifying approximation error for tangent-space integral.  
- Recommend analyzing and reporting computational complexity and runtimes; consider amortizing Log computations or approximation schemes.  
- Suggest exploring richer local metrics (full local covariances/local PCA) and discussing invariance to linear transforms.  
- Advise principled hyperparameter selection (unsupervised criteria, held-out likelihood) and stronger baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising idea but not acceptable in current form due to the serious normalization omission and evaluation/computational shortcomings.
