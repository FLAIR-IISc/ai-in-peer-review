- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): a Riemannian-generalized normal with a data-driven, smoothly varying metric.  
- LAND is parametric in mean/covariance while learning a nonparametric local metric via Gaussian-kernel-smoothed local covariances.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous connection to Riemannian statistics and manifold learning; LAND is the maximum-entropy distribution under the learned metric.  
- Practical algorithm: MLE via gradient descent with Monte Carlo integration in tangent spaces and block coordinate descent for stability.  
- Extension to mixtures: EM algorithm developed for LAND mixtures.  
- Strong experimental validation: synthetic examples show advantages over GMMs; real EEG sleep-stage classification shows clear improvement.  
- Good literature context: thorough relation to prior Riemannian and manifold-learning work and clear differentiation from past methods.  
- Scalability concern: evaluating logarithm maps is computationally expensive and paper lacks analysis of scaling with data size/dimensionality.  
- Bandwidth selection issue: no principled, data-driven method for choosing the Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ; current approach is ad-hoc.  
- Limited high-dimensional evaluation: experiments limited to low-dimensional data (EEG = 5D); performance in high dimensions not demonstrated.  
- Incomplete comparisons: lacks evaluation against other manifold-learning methods (e.g., Isomap, LLE, deep methods) that could be efficient alternatives.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, novel contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address bandwidth selection and scalability analysis.  
- Pro-accept points: novel data-driven Riemannian normal, strong theory, practical Monte Carlo-based algorithm, compelling synthetic and EEG results, clear gains over standard Gaussians.  
- Con-accept points: unresolved computational scalability in high dimensions, unprincipled bandwidth choice, limited high-D analysis, and insufficient comparison to alternative manifold methods.
