- Introduces the locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND), a parametric generalization of the normal distribution to data near manifolds using a locally adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Presents an ML algorithm for LAND parameters using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Builds on Riemannian normal distribution literature (e.g., Pennec, Zhang & Fletcher) but novelly adapts the metric for manifold learning.  
- Technically sound derivations and well-motivated ML algorithm; Monte Carlo is appropriate for normalization constant estimation.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show LAND captures non-linear structure and outperforms standard Gaussian mixtures.  
- Well-written with consistent notation and helpful figures; some derivations (e.g., intrinsic least squares estimators) need more explanation and examples.  
- Original contribution: adapting the normal distribution to the manifold setting is novel and promising for multiple applications.  
- Significant results offering a new perspective connecting manifold learning and Riemannian statistics with potential broad impact.  
- Pro: Novel methodology that effectively models non-linear manifolds and improves over Gaussian mixtures.  
- Pro: Offers a new theoretical and practical link between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.  
- Con: Some sections lack sufficient explanation and illustrative examples.  
- Con: Assumes substantial background in Riemannian geometry/statistics, limiting accessibility.  
- Con: Experiments could be more comprehensive with additional datasets and comparisons to more baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to clarify technical sections and expand experimental comparisons.
