- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replace Euclidean Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x).
- Learns M(x) nonparametrically from data via Gaussian-kernel-weighted local (diagonal) variances to bias geodesics toward high-density regions.
- Uses Pennec’s Riemannian normal p(x|µ,Σ) ∝ exp(−1/2 ⟨Logµ(x), Σ−1 Logµ(x)⟩) on the learned manifold.
- Key technical contributions: (i) Monte Carlo estimator for normalization constant C(µ,Σ) over the tangent space using Expµ(v) samples.
- Key technical contributions: (ii) gradient-based maximum likelihood for µ and Σ (Σ−1 factorized as AᵀA).
- Key technical contributions: (iii) EM algorithm for mixtures of LANDs including component-dependent normalization constants.
- Empirical results: improved density modeling and clustering on synthetic curved data and EEG features vs GMMs and Riemannian least-squares.
- Positive: construction of a nonparametric metric and use of Riemannian normal is principled; mixture extension with proper normalization is sound.
- Major concern: change-of-variables for C omits the Jacobian det(DExpµ(v)); omission likely biases the estimator unless justified or corrected.
- Major concern: gradient w.r.t. µ appears incorrectly derived; derivation yielding an E[m(µ,v) v] term is non-obvious and needs rigorous justification.
- Concern: metric restricted to diagonal form and not invariant to linear transforms, limiting modeling fidelity and introducing preprocessing sensitivity.
- Concern: computational cost high—each m evaluation requires solving geodesic IVPs; uses large S (e.g., 3000) with no runtime/complexity analysis or scalability study.
- Concern: critical kernel bandwidth σ is chosen heuristically or by per-subject grid search, lacking principled or automatic selection.
- Concern: theoretical properties missing—no consistency guarantees, bias analysis for MC approximation, or robustness to metric misspecification.
- Clarity issues: need explicit derivation of the tangent-space volume element, clearer gradient derivations in main text, and ODE/optimization hyperparameter details.
- Originality: novelty lies in learning a nonparametric Riemannian metric and estimating normalization in tangent space; synthesis of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics is new.
- Significance: potentially impactful alternative to GMMs for manifold-concentrated data, but heavy computation and hyperparameter tuning may limit adoption.
- Pros: principled framework, proper mixture normalization, better capture of non-ellipsoidal structure, clear algorithm combining geodesics and MC.
- Cons: likely omission of Exp map Jacobian, insufficient gradient justification, diagonal metric limitation, high computational cost, limited baselines/ablations.
- Suggestions: rigorously derive/include det(DExpµ(v)) or justify omission, validate gradients and reduce MC variance, provide runtime/scaling and sensitivity analyses, add baselines and ablation studies, and offer automatic σ selection.
