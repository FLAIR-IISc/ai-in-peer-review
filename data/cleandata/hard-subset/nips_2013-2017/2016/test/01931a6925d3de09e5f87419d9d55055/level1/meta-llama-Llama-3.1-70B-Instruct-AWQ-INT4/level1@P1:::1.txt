This paper introduces a novel distribution, the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), which generalizes the normal distribution to the "manifold" setting. The LAND is a parametric distribution that depends only on a mean and a covariance, but it is defined on a non-parametric Riemannian manifold. The authors develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, which relies on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration. They also extend the LAND to mixture models and provide the corresponding EM algorithm.

The main idea of the paper is to replace the linear Euclidean distance in the ordinary normal distribution with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance measure. This is achieved by learning a Riemannian manifold that adapts to the data. The authors show that the LAND is a natural model that is the maximum entropy distribution for a fixed mean and covariance. They also demonstrate the efficiency of the LAND to fit non-trivial probability distributions over both synthetic data and EEG measurements of human sleep.
