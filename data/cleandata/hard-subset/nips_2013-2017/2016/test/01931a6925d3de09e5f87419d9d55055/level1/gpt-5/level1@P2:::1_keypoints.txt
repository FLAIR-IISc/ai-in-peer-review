- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance in a Gaussian with geodesic distance from a learned, smoothly varying diagonal Riemannian metric estimated via local Gaussian-weighted covariances (with regularizer ρ).  
- Instantiates Pennec’s Riemannian normal on the learned manifold, derives ML estimation for mean and covariance, and extends to a mixture model with an EM-like procedure.  
- Estimates normalization constant C(µ,Σ) by Monte Carlo in the tangent space using exponential maps and the induced measure √|M(Expµ(v))|.  
- Optimization alternates manifold steepest-descent updates for µ and factorized updates for Σ via Σ−1 = AᵀA; exponential maps computed via IVPs.  
- Technical construction (metric, geodesics, density) and change-of-variables to tangent space are sound; MC estimator and gradient expressions are plausible.  
- Metric is diagonal and not invariant under linear transforms, which can bias modeling unless data is prewhitened.  
- Kernel width σ and regularizer ρ critically affect smoothness/curvature; selection is ad hoc and tuned per EEG subject, risking overfitting.  
- No theoretical analysis of ML consistency, convergence of alternating optimization, or MC estimator variance as dimensionality grows.  
- Scalability to high dimensions is unclear because each MC sample needs an Exp map and determinant evaluation; S=3000 may be insufficient in high D.  
- Synthetic experiments show LAND models curved manifolds with fewer components and outperforms LS intrinsic estimators.  
- EEG clustering shows improved F-measure and sensitivity to σ, indicating capture of nonlinear structure.  
- Baselines are limited: missing comparisons to graph-geodesic GMMs, spectral clustering, kernel density estimators, and other adaptive generative models.  
- Runtime and computational cost are not reported; ablation studies (diagonal vs full local covariance, sensitivity to S) are lacking.  
- Paper is generally well written and self-contained on geometry; optimization algorithm and role of C(µ,Σ) are clearly explained.  
- Some implementation/experimental details are under-specified: systematic σ/ρ selection, ODE solver stability, stopping criteria, and per-iteration complexity.  
- Novelty: combines learned Riemannian metric with normalized generative modeling and a tractable normalization estimator; pragmatic diagonal kernel-smoothed metric.  
- Mixture extension correctly incorporates component-specific normalization, differing from some prior Riemannian mixture work.  
- Potential significance: could be a practical replacement for Gaussians on manifold-structured data, reducing needed mixture components; demonstrated results are encouraging but limited.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled bridge between learned geometry and normalized generative models, practical MC estimator, empirical benefits on curved data, and topical relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on diagonal kernel metric with ad hoc hyperparameter tuning, limited baselines/domains, no scalability/theory analysis, and sparse reproducibility details.  
- Suggestions: provide principled σ selection (e.g., CV), analyze sensitivity to σ and ρ, report computational cost and ablations on S and ODE tolerances.  
- Suggestion: consider preprocessing (whitening) to address non-invariance and discuss extensions to non-diagonal local metrics.  
- Suggestion: compare to spectral clustering and GMMs using graph-based geodesic distances to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel, practical, and likely to stimulate follow-up work despite empirical and hyperparameter/scale limitations.
