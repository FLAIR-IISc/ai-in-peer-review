- Summary: Proposes LAND — a Riemannian generalization of the multivariate normal using a learned local metric, Monte Carlo tangent-space normalization via Exp maps, and an EM for mixtures; shows gains over GMMs and LS-based mixtures on synthetic and EEG data.

- Strength: Clear, conceptually appealing link between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics producing a “manifold-aware Gaussian.”

- Strength: Tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization using IVP-based Exp maps is a sensible computational strategy.

- Strength: EM for mixtures with per-component normalization constants fills a gap in prior Riemannian mixture work.

- Strength: Empirical results show LAND captures non-elliptical structure missed by GMMs; synthetic examples and EEG application are relevant.

- Major technical concern: The normalization integral appears to omit the Exp-map Jacobian (|det d Expµ(v)|), which undermines correctness of the normalization constant, gradients, and ML estimates.

- Major technical concern: The derivation of the µ-gradient (Eq. 12) is unclear/inconsistent — the E[m(µ,v) v] term is unexplained given v ~ N(0,Σ) independent of µ.

- Computational concern: High cost and scalability issues — each evaluation needs many geodesic IVPs and Log map BVPs, but runtime, scaling with N/D/S, and wall-clock comparisons to GMMs are not reported.

- Hyperparameter concern: Performance is sensitive to σ (and ρ); selection protocol for EEG (fixed vs grid best) is unclear and risks optimistic bias if not validated properly.

- Robustness concern: Learned diagonal metric is not invariant to linear transformations; expressiveness trade-offs and alternatives (full covariances, metric mixtures) are not explored.

- Baselines concern: Comparisons are limited (GMM, LS mixtures); stronger density and manifold-aware clustering baselines (adaptive KDEs, normalizing flows, spectral methods) are missing.

- Quality: Interesting idea with promising results, but theoretical derivations and experimental methodology (runtime, hyperparameter selection) need clarification.

- Clarity: Generally well written, but crucial measure-change details and algorithmic specifics are deferred to the supplement; complexity discussion is lacking.

- Originality: Novel combination of a learned Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal ML estimation and mixture modeling — original for the venue.

- Significance: Potentially impactful if theoretical issues are fixed and computational/hyperparameter challenges addressed.

- Arguments for acceptance: Elegant bridge of two fields, general-purpose distribution with ML/EM, demonstrated empirical benefits.

- Arguments against acceptance: Apparent omission of Exp-map Jacobian and unclear µ-gradient, limited baselines, missing runtime analysis, σ sensitivity, diagonal-covariance restriction.

- Questions for authors: Does the estimator include or approximate |det d Expµ(v)| and under what assumptions? Provide full derivation of Eq.(12). Report per-iteration IVP/BVP counts and wall-clock times vs GMMs. How were σ and ρ selected for EEG (validation without labels)? How sensitive are results to S and can variance reduction help?

- Suggestions: Fix or justify the normalization estimator (include Jacobian or provide approximation analysis) and revise gradients; report ablations for σ, S, and metric expressiveness and runtime/scaling; add stronger baselines; consider metric mixtures or learned σ.
