- Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a parametric normal generalization defined on a learned non-parametric Riemannian manifold.  
- Replaces Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance by learning a Riemannian metric from data.  
- Derives a maximum-likelihood algorithm for LAND parameters using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration.  
- Extends LAND to mixture models and provides an EM algorithm for mixtures.  
- Shows LAND is the maximum-entropy distribution for fixed mean and covariance on the manifold.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic datasets and EEG sleep data, outperforming Gaussian mixture models.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and gives a clear introduction to Riemannian manifolds and related work.  
- Thorough literature review and clear presentation of contributions.  
- Assumes substantial background in differential geometry, which may limit accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large datasets.  
- Does not clearly explain how to choose the σ parameter used to construct the metric tensor.  
- Novelty: introduces a new manifold-aware distribution and an associated inference algorithm.  
- Significance: offers a new modeling perspective with potential applications in clustering, dimensionality reduction, and generative modeling.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address accessibility, complexity analysis, and guidance for choosing σ.
