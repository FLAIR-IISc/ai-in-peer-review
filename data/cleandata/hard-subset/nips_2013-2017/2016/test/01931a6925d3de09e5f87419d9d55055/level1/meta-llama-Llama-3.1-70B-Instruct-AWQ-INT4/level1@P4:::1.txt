**Summary**

The paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a generalization of the normal distribution to the "manifold" setting. The LAND is a parametric distribution that depends only on a mean and a covariance, and is the maximum entropy distribution under a locally adaptive, smoothly changing Riemannian metric. The authors develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, which relies on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration. They also extend the LAND to mixture models and provide the corresponding EM algorithm.
