This paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that generalizes the normal distribution to a Riemannian manifold setting. The authors replace the Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive, smoothly changing Riemannian metric that favors regions of high local density. The LAND is parametric, depending only on a mean and a covariance, and is the maximum entropy distribution under the given metric. The paper develops a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters and extends the LAND to mixture models.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to Riemannian geometry and the LAND. The notation is well-defined, and the mathematical derivations are thorough. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the efficiency of the LAND in fitting non-trivial probability distributions.
