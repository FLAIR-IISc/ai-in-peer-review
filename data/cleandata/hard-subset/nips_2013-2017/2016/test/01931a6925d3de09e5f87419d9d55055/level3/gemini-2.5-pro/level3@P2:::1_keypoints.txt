- Core idea: Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance on a data-driven Riemannian manifold whose metric is the inverse local diagonal covariance computed via a Gaussian kernel.
- Originality/significance: Novel synthesis of non-parametric manifold learning with a parametric generative model, enabling principled generation that respects intrinsic non-linear geometry.
- Generative capability: LAND defines a proper probability distribution over the ambient space, supports sampling and density estimation, and extends to mixture models to capture complex multi-modal clusters.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presents the required Riemannian geometry and motivations.
- Scalability/computational complexity: Major concern—metric construction sums over all N points and computing geodesics requires solving BVPs; need detailed complexity analysis and practical limits for N and D.
- Metric definition and hyperparameters: Choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is critical but under-addressed; requires guidance on selection, sensitivity, and handling varying densities (global σ may be inappropriate).
- Experimental comparisons: Evaluation is weak—qualitative comparisons use only GMM (a poor baseline); should compare against intrinsic LS estimators in visuals and report computation times.
- Positioning in metric learning: Lacks discussion of related work using local per-point diagonal metrics (e.g., local PCA, Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) to clarify novelty and differences.
- Overall assessment: Highly original and promising but practical impact limited by scalability, hyperparameter robustness, and weaker experimental baselines; recommends acceptance if authors address these concerns.
