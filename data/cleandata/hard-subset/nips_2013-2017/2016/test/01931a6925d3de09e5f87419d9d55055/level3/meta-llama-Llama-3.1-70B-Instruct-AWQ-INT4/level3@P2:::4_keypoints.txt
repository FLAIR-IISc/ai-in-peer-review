- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs), grounded in Riemannian manifold theory.
- Replaces Euclidean distance in a normal distribution with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance learned via a non-parametric metric.
- Constructs local metric as the inverse of a local covariance matrix with locality defined by a Gaussian kernel (kernel smoothing view).
- Introduces a Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for LAND parameters and derives it theoretically.
- Provides thorough theoretical development and discusses relations to other Riemannian normal distributions.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets show LAND captures nonlinear structures and often outperforms GMMs.
- Paper is well-written and the theoretical and experimental work is of high quality.
- Main weakness: novelty is unclear relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19], which is not introduced early and differences are not well explained.
- Lacks clear presentation of how the method advances the state-of-the-art and specific distinctions from prior work.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty and explicitly highlight differences from Simo-Serra et al. [19].
- Scores: Originality 6/10, Quality 8/10, Significance 7/10, Clarity 8/10.
