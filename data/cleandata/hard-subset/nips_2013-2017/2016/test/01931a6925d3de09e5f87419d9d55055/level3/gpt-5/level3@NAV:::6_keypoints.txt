- Proposes LAND: replaces Euclidean distance with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x) and computes distances via geodesics and exponential/log maps.
- Applies a Mahalanobis inner product in the tangent space and derives maximum-likelihood estimators for mean and covariance, which lack closed form.
- Introduces a steepest-descent algorithm with Σ−1 = AᵀA reparameterization to estimate parameters.
- Uses a Monte Carlo normalization trick by integrating over the tangent space TµM for tractable likelihood computation.
- Extends the model to mixtures with an EM-like procedure and evaluates on synthetic manifolds and an EEG sleep clustering task.
- Builds on prior Riemannian statistics and learned-metric manifold learning, focusing on learned/adaptive geometries rather than fixed manifolds.
- Technical construction, tangent-space MC normalization, and steepest-descent updates appear sound and well justified.
- Integrating in TµM is a clever variance-reduction and computationally favorable alternative to manifold-space integration.
- Experimental evaluation issues: σ (kernel metric) appears tuned on training data with no held-out likelihood or stability analysis.
- Missing baseline experiments when data are truly Euclidean (to assess bias under wrong manifold assumptions).
- Scalability and computational complexity are not characterized for higher-dimensional data; assumes full-rank M(x).
- Clarity concerns: notation conflates Σ (model covariance) and M(x) (kernel-induced metric) and should be disentangled.
- Unclear cluster assignment rule in clustering experiments (nearest geodesic mean vs posterior responsibilities); sensitivity to S (MC samples) and computational cost need reporting.
- Originality is incremental—combines known ingredients (data-driven metric, geodesic maps, Riemannian normal) but executed well with a useful MC trick.
- Significance: practical and principled generalization of Gaussians to learned geometries; useful for modeling nonlinear, non-ellipsoidal structure.
- Pros: sound geometric formulation, clever tangent-space MC, clear derivations, mixture extension, better empirical capture of nonlinear structure than GMMs.
- Cons: unclear σ selection and potential overfitting, no held-out tests, missing Euclidean baseline, no high-D experiments, and notation/assignment ambiguities.
- Overall: interesting and useful; recommendation leans toward acceptance conditional on clarifying σ selection/evaluation and strengthening empirical validation.
