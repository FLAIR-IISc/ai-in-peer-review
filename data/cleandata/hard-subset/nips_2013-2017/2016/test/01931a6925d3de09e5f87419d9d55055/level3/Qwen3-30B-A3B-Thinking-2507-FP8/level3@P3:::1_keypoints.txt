- Proposes LAND: a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution that generalizes multivariate normal to a Riemannian manifold using a locally adaptive metric built from Gaussian-weighted per-point diagonal covariances.  
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning, yielding a parametric distribution (mean, covariance) over a nonparametric space and shown to be the maximum entropy distribution under the learned metric.  
- Develops an MLE estimation scheme using Monte Carlo integration for the normalization constant and gradient descent, and extends the model to mixtures.  
- Experiments on synthetic data demonstrate LAND captures nonlinear structures better than GMMs; EEG sleep-stage classification shows practical utility.  
- Writing is exceptionally clear and concepts are well explained for the ML community.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical grounding, elegant connection between fields, careful estimation algorithm, convincing synthetic experiments, and a promising mixture extension.  
- Main weakness: computational complexity analysis is insufficient—per-point covariance estimation and inversion costs are not quantified and "high-dimensional" is undefined.  
- Poor literature positioning: fails to adequately discuss related metric learning and local PCA approaches (e.g., per-point diagonal metrics from Frome, Malisiewicz & Efros).  
- Choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is unexplained and untested; no robustness analysis or discussion of whether σ should vary across the data space.  
- Visual comparisons against only GMMs are misleading; should compare to intrinsic estimators (found in supplement) that outperform GMMs.  
- Authors mention possible extensions (learning σ, adaptive schemes) and computational bottlenecks (log map evals) but provide no complexity comparison with intrinsic methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, theoretically sound, and practically promising; revision should address scalability, σ justification, and positioning in related work, and emphasize the mixture extension.
