- Proposes HOPS: a novel homotopy smoothing algorithm combining Nesterov smoothing with a homotopy strategy, achieving iteration complexity Õ(1/(1−θ)) under a local error bound.
- Paper is well-structured and explains the algorithm and theoretical guarantees clearly.
- Technical quality is strong; manifold techniques and algorithmic ideas are sound and well-explained.
- Provides a comprehensive analysis of the local error bound condition and its relation to the Kurdyka-Łojasiewicz property.
- Experimental results indicate HOPS is effective compared to several state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Clarity issues exist, notably overloaded Sigma notation and insufficient explanation of the sigma parameter choice.
- Evaluation is limited: raises concerns about potential overfitting and lack of justification for sigma selection.
- Comparisons are not comprehensive, particularly missing strong baselines or results on high-dimensional data.
- Novelty mainly stems from combining prior techniques and offering a parameter estimation algorithm rather than wholly new theory.
- Offers an interesting extension adapting the normal distribution to local manifold structure via metric estimation, geodesic distances, and distribution learning.
- Overall contribution to non-smooth optimization is significant but impact is unclear due to evaluation and comparison limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental/comparative evaluation.
