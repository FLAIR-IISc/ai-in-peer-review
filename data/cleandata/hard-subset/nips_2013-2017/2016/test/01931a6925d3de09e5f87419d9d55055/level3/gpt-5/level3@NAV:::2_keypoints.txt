- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replaces Euclidean/Mahalanobis metric with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric and defines a parametric maximum-entropy density on the induced manifold.  
- Learning/estimation: metric learned nonparametrically; ML via gradient descent with Monte Carlo estimation of the normalization constant; EM derived for mixtures of LANDs.  
- Technical soundness: careful derivations; tangent-space Monte Carlo using exponential maps is sensible and more scalable than methods requiring log maps.  
- Gradient derivations: clean gradients for mean and covariance (using Σ−1 = AᵀA); mixture EM is consistent with the full probabilistic model.  
- Empirical results: clear advantages over Euclidean models and least-squares manifold baselines on synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage features.  
- Practical weaknesses: sensitivity to smoothing parameter σ, use of diagonal local covariances (no linear/affine invariance), and computational overhead from repeated geodesic solves.  
- Missing/weak analyses: timing and convergence diagnostics are limited; theoretical properties (consistency, identifiability) and Monte Carlo variance/control variates discussion are lacking.  
- Clarity and presentation: very well written and organized; notation, algorithmic steps, and bibliography are appropriate.  
- Originality: novel application of a properly normalized Riemannian normal to the learned-manifold setting with ML and mixture estimators, improving on prior work that assumed known manifolds or ignored normalization.  
- Significance: broadly applicable as a drop-in replacement for Gaussians on nonlinear data; mixture extension is compelling and experiments suggest meaningful practical gains.  
- Pros summary: elegant max-entropy generalization, complete ML/EM procedures with practical Monte Carlo normalization, strong empirical benefits, and high relevance to NeurIPS.  
- Cons summary: scalability and computational cost not fully quantified, dependence on σ and diagonal covariances, limited theoretical guarantees and ablations.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper; reviewer recommends acceptance.
