- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance to replace Mahalanobis distance, extended to mixtures and tested on synthetic and EEG data.
- The use of a geodesic distance (similar to Isomap) is an interesting and promising idea.
- Paper is well-written with a thorough introduction to Riemannian manifolds and geodesic distances.
- Mathematical derivations are sound and the maximum likelihood estimation algorithm is clearly explained.
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and requires further justification.
- Use of a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances is not well motivated and needs more intuition.
- Confusion between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimation in Eq. (11); unclear whether they refer to the same quantity.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic results are encouraging but EEG experiments are limited and insufficiently thorough.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; no complexity or runtime comparisons to standard EM algorithms.
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but practical applicability is questionable without further refinement and more extensive evaluation.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing scalability and computational-efficiency concerns, expanding experimental evaluation, and clarifying the Gaussian kernel choice and covariance vs. MAP estimation.
