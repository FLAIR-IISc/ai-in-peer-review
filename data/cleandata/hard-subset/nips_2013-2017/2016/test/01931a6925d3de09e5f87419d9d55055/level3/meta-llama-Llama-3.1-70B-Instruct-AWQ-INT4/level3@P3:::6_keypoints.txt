- Proposes LAND: a locally adaptive normal distribution that generalizes the normal to Riemannian manifolds (parametric mean/covariance on a non-parametric manifold).  
- Develops a maximum likelihood algorithm for LAND using gradient descent combined with Monte Carlo integration.  
- Reviews multivariate normal limitations and introduces Riemannian normals with a brief summary of Riemannian geometry and intrinsic least squares estimators.  
- Replaces Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive Riemannian distance by constructing a data-driven metric that enables geodesic computation.  
- Defines LAND as the Riemannian normal distribution using the learned local metric.  
- Extends LAND to mixture models and derives a corresponding EM algorithm.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and EEG sleep measurements show LAND fits complex distributions, learns faster than GMMs, and aligns better with sleep stages.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and introduces a novel use of Riemannian geometry for modeling nonlinear data.  
- Sigma parameter selection is unclear and appears possibly tuned on training data.  
- Paper lacks sufficient analysis and explanation of the practical usefulness of the learned distribution.  
- Main contribution partly combines prior work (kernel metric, geodesic estimation, Riemannian normal) with estimation algorithms rather than wholly novel theory.  
- Evaluation is limited and misses comparisons when data are generated by standard Gaussians or GMMs.  
- Scalability is a concern: experiments are limited to low-dimensional settings.  
- Notation/confusion around Sigma vs M = Sigma^-1 and the role of the local kernel matrix versus a fitted covariance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10); authors should clarify sigma selection, practical usefulness, and expand evaluation.
