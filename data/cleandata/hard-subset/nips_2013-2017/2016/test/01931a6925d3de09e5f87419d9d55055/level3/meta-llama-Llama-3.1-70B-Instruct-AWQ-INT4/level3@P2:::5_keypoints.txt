- Paper proposes LAND: a generative model replacing Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance with a learned, locally adaptive Riemannian metric and geodesic distance.  
- LAND is parametric (mean and covariance) and is the maximum-entropy distribution under the learned metric.  
- Authors give a clear, concise summary and position the work well within manifold learning and Riemannian statistics literature.  
- Main theoretical strength: using geodesic distance instead of Mahalanobis is well-motivated and promising.  
- The inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and lacks sufficient justification or intuition.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only a few synthetic and EEG examples and no broad dataset testing.  
- No comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, making significance and advantages unclear.  
- Minor issues: typo/symbol error in Algorithm 1 (lines 4–5: "d" should be "∇").  
- Confusion between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11); unclear if they refer to the same quantity.  
- Scalability and efficiency not addressed: no complexity or runtime comparison to standard EM, raising concerns about applicability to large-scale data.  
- Practical applicability is questionable given computational concerns and limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: interesting ideas but requires major revision to justify Eq. (7), expand experiments, and address scalability before acceptance.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7, Clarity 8, Originality 8, Significance 7.
