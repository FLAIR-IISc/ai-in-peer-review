- Proposes a novel locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) using a smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model data near nonlinear manifolds.
- Replaces Euclidean distance with geodesic distances computed via exponential and logarithm maps.
- Provides a clear, accessible introduction to Riemannian manifolds and geodesics for the target audience.
- Mathematical derivations are sound and well-explained.
- Proposes an MLE scheme combining gradient-based optimization with Monte Carlo integration for the normalization constant.
- Uses a steepest-descent algorithm for parameter estimation that is well-described.
- Monte Carlo approximation for the intractable normalization constant is a reasonable practical choice.
- Evaluation on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task is thorough and informative.
- Comparison to standard Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) is useful but limited.
- Unclear choice and tuning of the sigma parameter, with risk of tuning on training data and overfitting.
- Practical usefulness and interpretation of the learned distribution are not thoroughly analyzed or explained.
- Evaluation lacks held-out test results and comparisons when data are generated by standard Gaussians/GMMs.
- Novelty is questionable since the method combines existing ideas (kernel metrics, geodesic estimation, normals) without clearly quantified advancement.
- Scalability is not thoroughly evaluated, raising concerns for high-dimensional datasets.
- The assumption that manifold dimension equals ambient dimension (full-rank M(x)) is unusual and inadequately justified.
- Notation issues: ambiguity between Sigma and M = Sigma^-1 and unclear distinction between local kernel matrix and fitted covariance.
- Cluster membership assignment procedure is not explicitly stated, causing potential confusion.
- Recommendations: clarify sigma choice and overfitting risk, analyze practical usefulness, add held-out/GMM comparison experiments, evaluate scalability on high-D data, fix notation ambiguities, and explicitly state cluster assignment.
