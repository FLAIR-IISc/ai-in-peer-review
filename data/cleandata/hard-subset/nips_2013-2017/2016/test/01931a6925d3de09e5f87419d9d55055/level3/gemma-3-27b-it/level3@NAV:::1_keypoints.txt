- Proposes LAND: a novel model learning a Riemannian metric that adapts to local data density, bridging metric learning/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Models the metric using a Gaussian kernel and estimates Riemannian normal distributions via maximum likelihood, extended to mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in Riemannian geometry provides a solid foundation for the approach.  
- Writing is excellent—clear structure and sufficient mathematical detail.  
- Experiments on synthetic data show the model can capture non-trivial, nonlinear distributions.  
- Real-world application to EEG data demonstrates promise for practical use.  
- Insufficient positioning within the broader metric-learning literature, lacking discussion of per-point diagonal metric methods and local PCA approaches.  
- Computational complexity concerns: per-point covariance estimation and inversion may not scale to large/high-dimensional data; the paper needs a detailed complexity analysis and a clear definition of “high-dimensional” (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100).  
- Lack of sensitivity analysis for the Gaussian kernel bandwidth (sigma) and no exploration of whether a uniform sigma is appropriate across varying-density regions.  
- Visual comparisons (Figs. 5–6) use GMMs as weak baselines; should include intrinsic estimators (some shown only in supplement) in main comparisons.  
- Missing computational complexity comparisons between LAND and intrinsic estimators to help users choose methods based on data characteristics.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with promising results; accept with minor revisions addressing scalability, related-work positioning, sigma sensitivity, and stronger experimental baselines.
