- Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), generalizing the multivariate normal by replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric.
- LAND is parametric in mean and covariance but defined on a non-parametric Riemannian manifold.
- Presents a maximum likelihood parameter estimation algorithm using gradient descent combined with Monte Carlo integration.
- Extends LAND to mixture models and derives the corresponding EM algorithm.
- Experiments on synthetic data and EEG sleep measurements show LAND outperforms GMM in mean negative log-likelihood and aligns better with sleep stages.
- Paper is well-written and provides clear background on Riemannian geometry and manifold learning.
- Strength: novel distribution generalizing the normal to manifold settings.
- Strength: development of a new maximum likelihood inference algorithm.
- Strength: extension to mixture models with an EM algorithm.
- Strength: empirical demonstration of practicality and improved performance on real and synthetic data.
- Weakness: reliance on a non-parametric Riemannian manifold may limit applicability in high-dimensional spaces.
- Weakness: further work needed to improve the manifold learning component and algorithmic aspects of LAND.
- High marks for originality, significance, and clarity (rated 9/10 each) and overall recommendation to accept for NIPS 2016.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, new MLE algorithm, strong experimental results; arguments against: scalability/applicability concerns and need for further methodological improvements.
