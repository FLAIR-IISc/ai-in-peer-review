- Paper proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal distribution using a learned diagonal, kernel-weighted local metric with tangent-space Monte Carlo, gradient ML for mean/covariance, and EM for mixtures.  
- Key technical contributions: tangent-space Monte Carlo normalizer estimator, Exp-based Monte Carlo sampling to avoid costly BVPs, gradient formulas, and mixture-EM derivation.  
- Strength: conceptually elegant link between local metric/manifold learning and Riemannian maximum-entropy modeling, with clear motivation about intrinsic estimator pitfalls.  
- Strength: sound algorithmic design—tangent-space integration and Exp-map Monte Carlo reduce computational cost relative to full manifold integration.  
- Strength: natural and useful mixture extension; generative extrapolation via geodesics is compelling.  
- Strength: paper is very well written and clearly organized.  
- Major weakness: scalability to larger N and higher D is not quantified; no detailed complexity analysis or empirical runtime/memory scaling provided.  
- Missing complexity breakdown: costs of metric evaluation M(x), its derivatives, geodesic IVP (Exp), and log map BVP (Log) are not fully analyzed or reported.  
- Specific cost concerns: naive M(x) is O(ND) (or O(kD) with kNN), geodesic IVP ~ L·(cost M + cost ∂M), Log (BVP) is far more expensive and dominates mixture E-steps requiring NK Log computations.  
- Normalizer C(µ,Σ) cost is O(S·Tgeo) for S samples with additional gradient costs; overall per-iteration costs not empirically characterized.  
- Reviewer requests wall-clock/runtime vs (N,D,K,S,L,k) plots and memory scaling, and a concrete definition/experiments for “high-dimensional” (e.g., D ∈ {3,5,10,50,100}).  
- Positioning: the diagonal per-point metric closely relates to local metric learning/local PCA; paper should discuss connections to local Mahalanobis, per-exemplar models, LMNN/NCA variants, and tangent-space alignment.  
- Kernel bandwidth σ is critical; reviewer asks whether σ is uniform or adaptive, and requests sensitivity/robustness analyses of likelihood/clustering vs σ and of ρ.  
- Baselines: main comparisons against GMMs are weak; reviewer asks to include intrinsic-estimator visualizations, local-PCA generative baselines, and local metric-learning baselines.  
- Figures: supplement intrinsic-estimator fits should be moved into main figures for fair qualitative comparison.  
- Computational comparisons: report runtime and #ODE/BVP solves between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice.  
- Invariance: learned diagonal metric is not invariant to linear scalings/rotations; recommend evaluating preprocessing (whitening) or jointly learning a global linear transform.  
- Mixtures: request deeper experiments on mixtures—model selection over K, geodesics within/between components, and comparisons to mixtures of intrinsic Riemannian normals.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound overall, but missing complexity/bottleneck analysis and limited informative baselines in main text.  
- Clarity suggestions: add a dedicated complexity section, computational flow schematic (where Log vs Exp used), and a practical guide to choosing σ.  
- Originality: novel combination of learned Riemannian metric with maximum-entropy normals and ML/EM, but diagonal local metric relates to prior local metric learning—need clearer positioning.  
- Significance: promising if computational issues and baselines are addressed; could be a useful generative building block for manifold-structured data.  
- Suggested improvements: add formal complexity analysis and empirical scaling, define “high-dimensional,” provide robustness/adaptive σ strategies, include stronger baselines and intrinsic visuals, report computational comparisons, expand mixture experiments, and address invariance.  
- Reasons to accept: elegant bridge between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics, practical ML estimator, generative extrapolation, and strong presentation.  
- Reasons to reject: missing complexity analysis and unclear scalability, weak qualitative baselines, and unaddressed sensitivity to σ that may dominate performance.
