- Summary: Proposes LAND — a “curved Gaussian” using a data-adaptive Riemannian metric (inverse local diagonal covariance) with ML estimation via tangent-space Monte Carlo for normalization and gradient/EM updates; shows improved fit on synthetic manifolds and EEG features.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Riemannian statistics and learned-metric manifold methods, extending to data-adaptive metrics with explicit normalization; related to diffusion/anistropic metrics and metric learning.

- Strength — idea: Novel and useful concept of a density-sensitive, smoothly varying Riemannian metric defining a max-entropy “local Gaussian.”

- Strength — technical: Practical ML scheme for mean/covariance, Monte Carlo partition-function estimation, and an EM for mixtures.

- Strength — empirical: Clear visualizations and improved clustering alignment on EEG versus GMM in presented setups.

- Strength — breadth: Bridges manifold learning and Riemannian statistics with potential reuse in generative models.

- Weakness 1 (metric “learning” claim): The metric is hand-crafted with fixed σ and ρ, not learned by likelihood; title/narrative overstates “learning a metric.”

- Weakness 2 (σ sensitivity): Method is highly sensitive to kernel width σ; lacks intuitive regimes, principled selection, sensitivity analysis, or options like local/adaptive σ or learning σ via differentiating through ODEs.

- Weakness 3 (coordinate invariance): Using diagonal local covariances is not rotation/scale invariant; paper should discuss invariance, diagonal vs full local covariance trade-offs, and robustness to preprocessing/rotations.

- Weakness 4 (related literature): Missing discussion/citations for metric learning (LMNN, ITML, etc.) and diffusion geometry/diffusion maps; positioning of Eq.7 needs broader context.

- Weakness 5 (experimental comparison & model selection): Comparisons favor LAND with small K while GMMs can match with more components; fairness requires same model-selection criteria (BIC/held-out likelihood) and reporting NLL/BIC vs K.

- Weakness 6 (overfitting risk): LAND’s geometric flexibility may overfit; need train/test curves and demonstrations of overfitting vs GMM under comparable capacity.

- Weakness 7 (computational cost & scalability): High cost from ODE solves and Monte Carlo; paper lacks runtime/memory scaling (N, D, K, S), accuracy vs S, and practical acceleration strategies or medium-scale benchmarks.

- Quality: Sound derivations and reasonable Monte Carlo approach, but overstated metric-learning claims and insufficient experimental rigor.

- Clarity: Well-written overall but needs clarity on invariance, justification for diagonal M, σ intuition, and implementation details (GMM covariance type, ODE tolerances, S sensitivity).

- Originality: Novel combination of data-adaptive Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal and practical ML estimation, though built from known components.

- Significance: Potentially useful primitive for manifold generative modeling; impact depends on robustness, fair baselines, and scalability evidence.

- Pros (summary): Elegant, principled idea with clear algorithms and compelling qualitative fits.

- Cons (summary): Hand-crafted, non-invariant metric with sensitive hyperparameters, limited guidance, and experimental comparisons not fully fair or scalable.

- Key revision suggestions: Reframe claims or learn σ/ρ (and richer M) via likelihood, provide σ sensitivity plots and guidance, test invariance/full vs diagonal M, use principled K selection, show train/test overfitting curves, and present scaling/runtime trade-offs.

- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to σ across datasets? Can σ be differentiated/learned through the ODE/Monte Carlo? What GMM covariance type was used? How do results change under random rotations and what is per-iteration complexity (N, D, K, S) and runtime?

- After-rebuttal note: Even with near-straight geodesics, LAND can overfit more than GMMs; revise experiments to include principled model selection, train/test curves, and explicit overfitting characterization.
