- Proposal: Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a learned Riemannian metric and geodesic distance in Gaussians, extended to mixtures with EM.  
- Novelty: Learns a nonparametric metric from data and fits a parametric Riemannian normal on that geometry, unlike prior work assuming known manifolds.  

Strengths
- Conceptually interesting: bridges manifold learning and Riemannian statistics, generalizing Gaussians to curved domains.  
- Solid technical choice: clean tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for the normalizer and IVP-based Exp avoids BVPs.  
- Natural extension: mixture model and EM formulation are reasonable and potentially useful.  
- Empirical promise: qualitative visuals show LAND captures non-ellipsoidal structure better than GMMs.  

Weaknesses / Major concerns
- Metric definition ad hoc: Eq. (7) (Gaussian-weighted local diagonal covariance inverted to form M(x)) lacks principled derivation or justification.  
- Lack of invariance: using diagonal entries in ambient coordinates makes geometry sensitive to rotations/scaling; PCA/whitening or full local covariance could help.  
- Missing theoretical links: need relation to local likelihood, Fisher information of KDE, or prior probabilistic geometry work (e.g., Tosi et al.).  
- Bandwidth choices unclear: no discussion of bandwidth selection, sensitivity to σ and ρ, or use of compact-support kernels to reduce cost.  
- Confused notation/objectives: conflation between nonparametric local covariance (metric) and global model covariance Σ; Eq. (11) is ML not MAP and the manifold metric is fixed, not jointly learned.  
- Monte Carlo estimator issues: variance/control details sparse; noisy normalizer evaluations in mixtures may hurt responsibilities and EM stability.  
- Integrability/geodesic completeness not addressed: behavior of metric far from data and role of ρ need analysis to ensure well-posedness.  
- Algorithmic notation inconsistent: Algorithm 1 uses dµφ = −Σ∇µφ but notation in lines 4–5 is inconsistent.  
- Scalability concerns: Exp requires solving IVPs per sample, M(x) and derivatives are O(ND) per evaluation, making per-iteration costs high with large S, N, D, K.  
- Missing complexity/runtime analysis: no big-O per iteration/component, no wall-clock comparisons vs GMM-EM, no ablations on S or neighborhood approximations.  
- Experimental evaluation limited: synthetic examples okay but EEG experiment lacks breadth, more baselines (manifold-aware methods) and held-out log-likelihood comparisons needed.  
- Sensitivity and robustness absent: need studies on σ, ρ, diagonal vs full local covariance, sample size S, and cross-subject stability.  
- Sampling from LAND nontrivial: practical sampling difficulties due to m(µ,v) term not discussed or experimentally evaluated.  

Questions for authors (summarized)
- Can Eq. (7) be derived from local likelihood or Fisher information, and is full local covariance with shrinkage feasible?  
- How sensitive are results to σ and ρ, and can σ be learned?  
- What are per-iteration and total runtimes vs GMM-EM and practical limits on N, D, K?  
- Do you reuse MC samples or use variance reduction during mixture EM?  

Pros for acceptance
- Elegant theoretical framing combining learned geometry with Riemannian normals and a clear path to mixtures.  
- Promising qualitative performance on nonlinear manifolds.  

Cons for acceptance
- Metric choice appears ad hoc and non-invariant; lacks principled justification.  
- Weak experimental validation and missing scalability/runtime analysis reduce practical credibility.  

Minor issues
- Fix Algorithm 1 notation consistency (d vs ∇) and small grammar errors (e.g., “replace” → “replaces”).  

Overall
- Interesting and promising theoretical contribution but needs stronger justification of the metric, clearer distinction of covariance roles, comprehensive scalability analysis, and broader empirical validation to demonstrate practicality.
