- Proposes LAND: a parametric distribution on a data-adaptive Riemannian manifold replacing Mahalanobis with learned geodesic distances.  
- Metric is inverse of a diagonal, kernel-weighted local covariance that attracts geodesics to high-density regions.  
- Uses Pennec’s Riemannian normal (max-entropy) and derives ML estimators with block coordinate descent and Monte Carlo normalization in tangent space.  
- Extends to mixtures of LAND components with an EM algorithm and applies to synthetic data and EEG sleep staging.  
- Bridges Riemannian statistics on known manifolds and manifold/metric learning literature; should also cite broader local metric-learning work.  
- Construction is technically principled; gradient derivations and tangent-space Monte Carlo for normalization are sensible.  
- Empirical results are encouraging but main-text baselines rely mostly on GMMs; intrinsic least-squares (LS) Riemannian estimators should be featured.  
- Paper lacks concrete complexity analysis; naive implementation can be very costly due to geodesic ODEs and kernel-weighted metrics.  
- Needs quantification of per-iteration complexity (N, D, S, K), wall-clock times, and practical limits.  
- Should discuss and evaluate accelerations (kNN sparsification, kd-tree, neighbor caching, fast Gauss transforms) and their accuracy impact.  
- Monte Carlo variance for C and gradients requires discussion; adaptive S, control variates, or common random numbers could help.  
- Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ is crucial; robustness analysis, adaptive (kNN) bandwidths, or learning σ are needed.  
- Mixture experiments are promising but should compare to intrinsic LS mixtures and include an ablation without normalization constants.  
- Writing and exposition are clear; Riemannian preliminaries and algorithm descriptions are well presented.  
- Clarify ODE solver details, step sizes, convergence criteria, sample reuse, and quantify “smooth enough” in terms of σ and ρ.  
- The integration of learned Riemannian metrics with Riemannian normals and tractable normalization is novel and interesting.  
- If made scalable and robust, LAND could replace Gaussians in many models for curved data structures.  
- Pros: principled max-entropy generalization, avoids LS estimator pitfalls, probabilistically consistent mixtures, clear writing.  
- Cons: limited scalability analysis, heavy reliance on σ without robust study, underrepresented stronger baselines, mixture experiments could be expanded.  
- Suggested improvements: add complexity/runtime studies and ablations, include intrinsic LS baselines and BIC/AIC/log-likelihoods, study/adapt σ, expand mixture results, position within local metric-learning, and discuss metric invariance and remedies.  
- Overall: strong, well-written, and novel; lean toward acceptance pending added complexity analysis, stronger baselines, and systematic σ treatment.
