- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replacing Euclidean metric with learned Riemannian metric to capture manifold structure.
- Extends LAND to mixtures and derives an EM algorithm.
- Core idea of using geodesic distances and connecting manifold learning with Riemannian statistics is interesting and novel.
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and lacks sufficient justification.
- Rationale for using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances (Eq. 7) is not well motivated or analyzed.
- Ambiguity between Eq. (7) and Eq. (11): unclear if they define the same local covariance or if one is a MAP estimate of the other.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: synthetic experiments are limited and not comprehensive.
- Real-world EEG experiments lack comparisons to standard methods beyond a single GMM baseline.
- No discussion or empirical evidence of computational complexity, runtime, or claimed scalability via tangent space integration.
- Crucial missing comparison to standard EM for GMMs on comparable datasets to assess practical benefits.
- Overall recommendation: promising theoretical contribution but practical applicability unproven; recommend rejection pending major revisions.
- Minor correction: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, should use ∇ instead of d.
