- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean distance in Gaussian with a locally adaptive nonlinear (geodesic-like) distance to model data on a manifold.  
- Introduces an ML algorithm combining gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration to infer LAND parameters.  
- Clear, concise introduction to Riemannian geometry and its application to manifold learning.  
- LAND is a natural and novel extension of the normal distribution to manifold settings.  
- Definition of the inverse metric tensor (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc and needs stronger justification and intuition.  
- Use of a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances in the weighted sum is insufficiently motivated.  
- Confusion between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and its MAP estimate in Eq. (11); unclear if they denote the same quantity.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only limited synthetic and EEG experiments, not comprehensive enough to assess performance.  
- Scalability and computational efficiency are not addressed; no complexity analysis or runtime comparisons provided.  
- No comparison of algorithm runtime/complexity to standard EM, making practical applicability uncertain.  
- Overall contribution is theoretically interesting but practical utility is questionable without further work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution to manifold learning, with potential applications in vision, robotics, and signal processing.  
- Arguments for rejection: lacks comprehensive experiments and scalability analysis; requires significant revisions.  
- Recommended improvements: justify Eq. (7), clarify relation between local covariance and MAP estimate, expand experiments, analyze scalability, and compare complexity/runtime to standard EM.
