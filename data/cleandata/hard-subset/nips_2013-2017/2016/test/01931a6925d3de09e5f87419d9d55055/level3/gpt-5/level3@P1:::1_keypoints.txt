- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replaces Euclidean/Mahalanobis metric with a learned smooth Riemannian metric M(x) (inverse of diagonal local covariance via Gaussian kernel weights) in a Riemannian normal density.  
- Uses geodesic distances computed by solving ODEs for Exp/Log maps; normalization integrates over tangent space via Monte Carlo samples v ~ N(0, Σ) with volume correction √|M(Expµ(v))|.  
- Derives ML algorithm via block coordinate descent: update µ by steepest descent on the manifold and update Σ using factorization Σ−1 = AᵀA.  
- Extends to mixtures with a consistent EM algorithm that includes per-component normalization constants C(µk, Σk).  
- Empirically, LAND better captures curved clusters and improves EEG sleep-stage clustering alignment compared to GMMs, and avoids low-density bias of intrinsic least-squares estimators.  
- Situates work between manifold learning (Isomap, LLE, Laplacian Eigenmaps) and Riemannian statistics, differing by learning a continuous metric over R^D rather than embedding or fixed manifolds.  
- Novelty: meaningful combination of a learned Riemannian metric with maximum-entropy Riemannian normals plus ML/EM estimators accounting for metric-induced measure.  
- Formulation and normalization are principled; tangent-space Monte Carlo and IVP-based geodesics are efficient choices; optimization derivations are clear.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; geometric preliminaries and algorithm presentation are concise and easy to follow.  
- Missing complexity analysis: per-iteration computational costs for µ and Σ updates are not quantified.  
- Scalability concern: evaluating m(µ,v) requires Expµ(v) ODE solves and M(x) evaluation (naively O(ND)); practical runtime/memory and feasible regimes for N and D are unspecified.  
- High-dimensional behavior unclear: diagonal M(x) and ODE solver stability for D ≈ 50–100 need empirical characterization.  
- Bandwidth σ is critical: unclear if σ is global or adaptive; robustness to σ and selection strategy not systematically studied.  
- Monte Carlo sensitivity: impact of sample size S and variance-reduction strategies (control variates, quasi-MC) not evaluated.  
- Weak baselines: primary comparisons to GMMs are insufficient; intrinsic least-squares estimators and other geometric baselines should be shown in main text with AIC/BIC.  
- Invariance issue: learned metric not invariant to linear transforms; preprocessing (e.g., whitening) effects should be discussed.  
- Positioning with metric learning: needs clearer citations and comparisons to local metric learning and per-point diagonal metrics (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and local PCA methods.  
- Suggested additions: computational complexity section with empirical runtimes across N, D, K, S, T and comparisons to intrinsic estimators and GMMs.  
- Suggested additions: systematic σ selection (cross-validation, plug-in, or learned/adaptive bandwidths) and expanded mixture experiments with K>2 and harder nonconvex mixtures.  
- Suggested accelerations: approximate M(x) and derivatives via k-NN truncation, KD-trees, Nystrom, precomputed grids, or learned/approximate ODE solvers.  
- Recommend discussing failure modes and diagnostics (sparse sampling, noisy high-D data, geodesic distortion metrics).  
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually novel, well-grounded, solid derivations, promising empirical results, and potential broad impact as a geometry-aware Gaussian replacement.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of computational scalability, limited strong geometric baselines, and ad hoc σ selection.  
- Overall: strong and polished submission that would be substantially improved by addressing scalability, σ selection, and stronger baselines.
