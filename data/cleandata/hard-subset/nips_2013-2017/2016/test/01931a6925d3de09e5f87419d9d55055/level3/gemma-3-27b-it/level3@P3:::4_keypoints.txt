- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a Riemannian-generalized Gaussian.
- Develops a gradient-based MLE and uses Monte Carlo integration to normalize the density.
- Learns a locally adaptive metric via a Gaussian kernel, producing a smooth differentiable distance for generative modeling.
- Extends the approach to mixture models for greater flexibility.
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous, clear, and well-grounded in Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.
- Experiments on synthetic and EEG data demonstrate effectiveness; comparisons with GMMs and least-squares LAND are fair and informative.
- Novelty is incremental, particularly relative to Simo-Serra et al. [19], and key differences from prior work are not clearly presented.
- Simo-Serra et al. [19] is omitted from the introduction and not sufficiently contrasted; the normalization-constant difference is overstated.
- Computational cost (geodesic evaluation and Monte Carlo integration) may be a bottleneck for high-dimensional data and is not fully addressed.
- Sensitivity to the smoothing parameter σ and its impact on the learned manifold needs more thorough analysis beyond the EEG experiment.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, empirically validated, and introduces a scalable Monte Carlo integration scheme.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unclear distinction from prior work, missing early discussion of related work, and insufficient treatment of complexity and parameter sensitivity.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: explicitly discuss and contrast Simo-Serra [19] in the introduction, moderate claims about normalization differences, and provide detailed discussion of computational complexity and parameter sensitivity.
