- Proposes a novel locally adaptive Riemannian metric (LAND) to model observation spaces and capture data manifolds.

- Replaces Euclidean distance in the normal distribution with a locally adaptive nonlinear distance; idea is well-motivated and promising.

- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and easy to follow.

- Title is misleading because the metric is hand-crafted (Eq. 7) rather than learned.

- The metric in Eq. 7 uses hyperparameters (rho, sigma) fixed empirically with no clear learning procedure.

- The paper lacks discussion on the intuitive effect of varying sigma and guidance for selecting its value.

- Sigma is likely sensitive and can significantly affect LAND performance; this sensitivity is not analyzed.

- The Eq. 7 metric is not coordinate-invariant, which may produce inconsistent results and needs discussion.

- Experimental comparison with GMMs is unfair: LAND is advantaged with fewer components while GMMs could match with more components.

- Authors should use model selection (e.g., BIC) to choose the number of components per method for fair comparisons.

- The paper does not demonstrate overfitting behavior of LAND when using many components.

- Quantitative measures of computational cost and scalability (limits, runtime, experiments on larger datasets like MNIST) are missing.

- Related work on metric learning and manifold learning (especially Riemannian geometry connections) is insufficiently cited and discussed.

- Overall potential is high, but the paper requires revisions to address misleading claims, metric issues, hyperparameter sensitivity, fair baselines, scalability, and related-work coverage.
