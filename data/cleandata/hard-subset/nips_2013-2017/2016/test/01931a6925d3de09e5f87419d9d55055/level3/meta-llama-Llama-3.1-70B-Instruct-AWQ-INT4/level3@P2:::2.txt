This paper introduces a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that generalizes the normal distribution to the manifold learning setting. By replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, the authors extend the normal density to capture nonlinear structures in data. The proposed distribution is a maximum entropy distribution under the given metric, making it a natural model for manifold-valued data.

The authors provide a comprehensive overview of the mathematical foundations of Riemannian geometry and its application to manifold learning. They develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, relying on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration. The algorithm is extended to mixture models, and the corresponding EM algorithm is provided.

The experiments demonstrate the efficiency of the LAND in fitting non-trivial probability distributions over both synthetic data and EEG measurements of human sleep. The results show that the LAND learns faster and more accurately than the Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) and outperforms the least squares estimators. The authors also illustrate the advantages of the LAND in capturing nonlinear structures in data, particularly near the boundaries of the data manifold.
