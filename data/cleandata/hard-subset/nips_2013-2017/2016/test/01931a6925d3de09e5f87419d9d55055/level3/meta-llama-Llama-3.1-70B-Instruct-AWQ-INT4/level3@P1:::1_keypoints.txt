- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a parametric model linking metric and manifold learning using Riemannian statistics.  
- Models the metric as a Riemannian normal distribution and learns mean and covariance by maximum likelihood.  
- Defines distances as geodesics on the learned Riemannian manifold using local metrics from inverse diagonal covariances.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear, accessible introduction to Riemannian geometry and manifold learning.  
- Development and motivation of the LAND model are clear; use of local metrics and geodesics is a natural extension.  
- Strong contribution in connecting metric learning, manifold learning, and Riemannian statistics.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data are promising and demonstrate effectiveness of LAND.  
- Comparison with GMMs is informative but insufficient; additional comparisons with other intrinsic estimators are recommended.  
- Reviewer requests more details and comparisons regarding computational complexity versus other methods.  
- Major concern about the choice and influence of Gaussian kernel bandwidth (sigma); robustness tests and analysis needed.  
- Paper is technically sound, original, and potentially significant for applications in data analysis and vision.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear development, promising experiments, and strong exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to sigma, missing complexity analysis, and limited baseline comparisons.
