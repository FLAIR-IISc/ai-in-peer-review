- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces the Euclidean metric with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model data on nonlinear manifolds.  
- Develops an MLE procedure combining gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends it to mixtures via an EM algorithm.  
- Builds on Riemannian geometry and manifold learning (cites Hauberg et al. and Riemannian statistics) to justify the approach.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over Gaussian Mixture Models on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage measurements.  
- Paper is theoretically rigorous, clearly presented, and provides sufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Inclusion of both synthetic and real-world experiments strengthens empirical validation.  
- Extension to mixture models increases practical applicability.  
- Novelty is limited by related work, particularly Simo-Serra et al. [19], which is not sufficiently distinguished.  
- The paper omits citing Simo-Serra et al. in the introduction and fails to clearly state the key difference (learned metric vs. assumed known manifold).  
- Needs clearer early articulation of the advantages of learning the Riemannian metric.  
- Lacks systematic sensitivity analysis for regularization parameter ρ and kernel bandwidth σ; σ selection for EEG was done by picking best from a range.  
- Scalability to high-dimensional data is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: explicitly compare/contrast with Simo-Serra [19] in the introduction, analyze parameter sensitivity (σ and ρ), and add the missing reference [19].
