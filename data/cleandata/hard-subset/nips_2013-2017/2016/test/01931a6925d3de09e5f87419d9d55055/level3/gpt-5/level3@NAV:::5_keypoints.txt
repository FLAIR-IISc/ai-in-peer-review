- Proposal: LAND replaces Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance in a Gaussian with geodesic distance induced by a learned Riemannian metric built from kernel-weighted local covariances.  
- Metric design concern: Eq. (7)’s inverse metric (diagonal kernel-smoothed variance with additive ρ) appears ad hoc; justify diagonality, additive term, and discuss invariance to scaling/rotation and alternatives (full local covariances, metric mixtures).  
- Kernel weighting question: provide intuition for Gaussian kernel choice, how σ and ρ are set, and sensitivity of results to these hyperparameters.  
- Notation/semantics confusion: clarify distinction between the “local covariance” defining the geometry (Eq. 7) and the model covariance Σ estimated in Eq. (11), and whether the metric is learned or fixed (MAP implications).  
- Computational trick praised: tangent-space Monte Carlo for normalization avoids expensive boundary-value problems for log maps.  
- Mixture extension: EM-like mixture formulation and high-level algorithmic details are clearly presented.  
- Experimental limitations: evaluation is limited—compare to Isomap+GMM, Riemannian GMMs on known manifolds, spectral/kernel mixtures, and modern density models; include out-of-sample tests.  
- Ablation/sensitivity requests: run ablations on σ and S, and report sensitivity to ambient dimension D and sample size N.  
- Scalability/runtime concern: no complexity or wall-clock analysis; per-iteration cost likely scales with S geodesic IVPs per component—report asymptotic complexity in N, D, K, S and empirical runtimes vs standard EM.  
- Minor fix: Algorithm 1 should use ∇ (gradient) instead of d or explicitly define the steepest-descent direction.  
- Decision summary: strengths — novel/elegant bridge between manifold learning and generative modeling, principled maximum-entropy view, strong mathematical framing; weaknesses — ad hoc metric choice, weak empirical validation, unclear scalability, and notation clarity issues.
