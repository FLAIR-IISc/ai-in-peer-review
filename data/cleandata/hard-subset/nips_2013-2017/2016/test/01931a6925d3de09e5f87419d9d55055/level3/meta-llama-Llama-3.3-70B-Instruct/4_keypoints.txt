- Proposes a novel homotopy smoothing (HOPS) algorithm for a family of non-smooth optimization problems.  
- Achieves improved iteration complexity Õ(1/(1−θ)) with θ ∈ (0,1] under a mild local error bound.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and provides a clear, detailed convergence proof.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world data show the method is effective.  
- Strength: lower iteration complexity compared to existing methods (e.g., Nesterov’s smoothing).  
- Strength: applicable to a broad class of non-smooth optimization problems.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and claims are supported by theory and experiments.  
- Weakness: limited novelty, notably similar to Simo-Serra et al. [19].  
- Weakness: introduction does not clearly highlight differences from prior work; related work is only referenced late.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed empirical and theoretical comparisons with existing methods and clearer articulation of advantages.  
- Originality is constrained because underlying ideas/techniques are not substantially new despite improved complexity.  
- Significance: potentially makes a meaningful contribution by providing a faster, more efficient algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify novelty and provide stronger comparisons to prior work.
