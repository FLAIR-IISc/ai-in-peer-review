- Proposal: Introduces LAND, a Riemannian-generalized Gaussian using a learned, data-adaptive metric M(x) and density based on geodesic distance to a global mean µ with covariance Σ.
- Normalizer estimator: Derives a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator C = Z E[v∼N(0,Σ)] m(µ,v) to avoid expensive boundary-value geodesic solves.
- Optimization: Uses steepest-descent updates on µ (via Expµ) and A-factorization gradient updates for Σ, extendable to mixtures with correct per-component normalizers.
- Relation to prior work: Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, differing by learning the metric and handling normalization consistently.
- Strength — conceptual: Principled, well-motivated generalization of Gaussians to adaptive geometries.
- Strength — estimator: Tangent-space Monte Carlo normalizer is clever, geometrically consistent, and computationally attractive.
- Strength — algorithms: Sensible optimization choices and a mixture EM that properly accounts for normalization are valuable.
- Strength — experiments: Demonstrates improved fit and clustering on nonlinear synthetic manifolds and EEG features versus Euclidean GMMs and intrinsic moment estimators.
- Strength — exposition: Geometric preliminaries and algorithm descriptions are generally clear for readers familiar with Riemannian geometry.
- Weakness — hyperparameters: σ (kernel bandwidth) selection is critical but unclear and appears hand-tuned per subject, risking overfitting.
- Weakness — evaluation: No held-out test log-likelihoods, limited generalization checks, and missing comparisons to stronger baselines (cross-validated GMM, spectral clustering, KDE, HMM).
- Weakness — scalability: Computing m(µ,v) aggregates over all N via kernel weights and uses many MC samples (S≈3000), raising runtime concerns and lacking complexity analysis or approximations.
- Weakness — intrinsic dimension: Keeps full ambient dimension D (full-rank M(x)), potentially mismatched when data lie on low-dimensional manifolds; sensitivity to this is unexamined.
- Weakness — clarity: Confusion between global Σ and local M(x), missing unified notation, lack of affine invariance discussion, and truncated algorithm box (missing initialization, ρ, stopping criteria).
- Weakness — estimator diagnostics: Paper lacks reports on variance/accuracy of C(µ,Σ) estimates, S sensitivity, and convergence behavior.
- Question 1: How to select σ unsupervisedly; can you show held-out log-likelihood vs σ and recommend a procedure?
- Question 2: What are wall-clock computational costs per EM/MLE iteration as functions of N, D, K, S; any use of approximate neighbors or inducing points?
- Question 3: How sensitive are results to S; can variance reduction (quasi-MC, control variates, antithetic sampling) reduce S substantially?
- Question 4: Do you observe regressions on data generated by Euclidean Gaussians; provide this sanity check.
- Question 5: Characterize limiting cases (σ→∞ → Euclidean Gaussian; σ→0 behavior) and discuss ρ regularization.
- Question 6: How are mixture components initialized and how robust is EM to poor starts?
- Suggestion: Use cross-validated σ via held-out likelihood and report sensitivity curves.
- Suggestion: Add runtime/complexity tables, high-dimensional experiments, and approximate-neighbor or sparse-kernel strategies for M(x).
- Suggestion: Include comparisons on Euclidean Gaussian data and to nonparametric baselines (KDE) and manifold clustering (spectral clustering).
- Suggestion: Improve notation clarity, explicitly state cluster assignment rules, recommend pre-whitening for affine issues, and provide full algorithm details.
- Suggestion: Explore variance reduction for normalizer estimation and reuse of Expµ(v) samples across iterations where feasible.
- Pros: Principled formulation, elegant tangent-space normalization, practical MLE/EM, and strong qualitative performance on nonlinear structures.
- Cons: Hyperparameter tuning and evaluation risk overfitting, missing scalability/high-dimensional analyses, and some clarity/notation shortcomings.
- Overall: Technically sound and potentially impactful; recommend acceptance conditional on tightened evaluation (held-out likelihoods, fair σ selection), complexity analysis, and minor clarity fixes.
