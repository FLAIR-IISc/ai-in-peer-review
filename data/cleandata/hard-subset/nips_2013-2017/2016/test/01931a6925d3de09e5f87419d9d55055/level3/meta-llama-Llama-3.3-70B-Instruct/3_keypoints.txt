- Proposes a novel Homotopy Smoothing (HOPS) algorithm for a family of non-smooth optimization problems.  
- Achieves improved iteration complexity Õ(1/(1−θ)) for θ ∈ (0,1] under a mild local error bound condition.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence guarantees and iteration complexity.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a coherent introduction and explanation.  
- Experimental results show HOPS converges consistently faster than existing methods, especially when the optimality gap is small.  
- Method handles a broad range of applications (machine learning, image processing, cone programming).  
- Includes detailed comparisons with Nesterov’s smoothing and primal–dual methods, highlighting advantages.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion and empirical analysis on hyperparameter choices (smoothing parameter µ and iteration count t).  
- Needs additional comparisons with other relevant algorithms (e.g., restarted subgradient method) for completeness.  
- Could broaden discussion of potential applications beyond the provided examples to show wider impact.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance and is suitable for publication.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel/effective algorithm, better complexity under mild conditions, strong analysis, convincing experiments.  
- Rejection concerns: insufficient hyperparameter analysis, limited algorithmic comparisons, and limited application discussion.
