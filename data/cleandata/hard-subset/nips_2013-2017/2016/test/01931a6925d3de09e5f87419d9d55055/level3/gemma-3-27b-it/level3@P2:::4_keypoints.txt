- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing Euclidean metric with a smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model data near nonlinear manifolds.  
- Develops MLE for LAND parameters and extends to LAND mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage recordings demonstrate LAND’s ability to capture complex distributions and outperform GMMs in some cases.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry, with sound derivation of the MLE estimator.  
- Clear exposition and accessible presentation despite mathematical complexity; Algorithm 1 clarifies the MLE procedure.  
- Appropriately cites manifold learning literature (e.g., Isomap) and prior Riemannian normal work.  
- Paper’s originality feels incremental; adapting statistical models to learned manifolds is not entirely novel.  
- Missing citation and comparison to Simo-Serra et al. [19]; distinction between learning vs. known manifold not emphasized enough.  
- Lack of comparative analysis against closely related methods weakens novelty and empirical claims.  
- Limited discussion of practical limitations and scalability for high-dimensional inputs.  
- Sensitivity to smoothing parameter σ is noted but not thoroughly investigated.  
- Connection to maximum entropy distributions is mentioned but not fully exploited in experiments.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, practical limitations, and computational cost analysis.  
- Specific requested revisions: explicitly compare to Simo-Serra et al. [19], detail scalability and parameter sensitivity, and include computational cost discussion.
