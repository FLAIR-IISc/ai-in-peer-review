- Introduces LAND: a locally adaptive normal distribution replacing Euclidean Mahalanobis distance with a learned Riemannian geodesic distance.  
- LAND is parametric (mean and covariance) and identified as the maximum entropy distribution under the learned metric.  
- Develops MLE via gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Theoretical foundations and mathematical development (geodesics, exp/log maps, normalization) are clear and sound.  
- Novelty: adapts Riemannian normal distributions to a learned-manifold setting, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Metric tensor definition (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc; lacks justification for Gaussian kernel choice and diagonal covariance restriction.  
- Ambiguity between local covariance in Eq. 7 and covariance parameter in MLE (Eq. 11); relationship is not clarified.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: comparisons only to GMM and LS, missing manifold-learning and density-estimation baselines.  
- Synthetic experiments limited to a single dataset structure; robustness to varied distributions not demonstrated.  
- EEG sleep analysis lacks statistical validation (no significance tests or error bars) and systematic parameter selection (σ).  
- No scalability or runtime analysis despite noting exp/log map expense; practical computational cost is unassessed.  
- Claims of superiority over standard GMMs are not convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Clarity is generally good, but confusion around metric definition and covariance weakens the core contribution.  
- Originality is moderate: a natural but relatively straightforward extension of prior Riemannian mixture work.  
- Overall significance is questionable due to unclear design choices, insufficient experiments, and missing computational analysis.
