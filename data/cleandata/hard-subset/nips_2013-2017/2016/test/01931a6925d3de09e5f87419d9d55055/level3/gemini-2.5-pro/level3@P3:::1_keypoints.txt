- Introduces LAND: a parametric generative model on a data-driven Riemannian manifold replacing Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance; local metric is inverse of a Gaussian-kernel-weighted diagonal covariance.

- Derives MLE for mean and covariance via gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to mixture models with an EM algorithm.

- Experiments (synthetic + 5D EEG) show LAND captures nonlinear manifold structure better than standard GMMs.

- Strength — Originality: principled synthesis of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics yielding a parametric model over a non-parametric space.

- Strength — Clarity: well-written, accessible exposition of Riemannian concepts and motivation.

- Strength — Generative capability: provides a full probability model that can extrapolate and generate samples along the learned manifold; mixtures handle multimodality.

- Weakness — Computational complexity/scalability: metric computation sums over all N, geodesic computations require solving ODEs, and repeated Monte Carlo/optimization is expensive; no formal complexity analysis provided.

- Weakness — Dimensionality concerns: only a 5D real experiment shown; reliance on diagonal local covariances may fail in moderate/high D (e.g., 10–100), and practical limits are unclear.

- Weakness — Experimental baselines: comparisons mainly to GMMs are insufficient; intrinsic least-squares estimators are more relevant but under-displayed visually; no runtime/computational-cost comparisons.

- Weakness — Literature positioning: insufficient discussion of prior local/metric-learning work (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and how this differs in probabilistic/Riemannian framing.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sigma: kernel bandwidth critically shapes learned geometry; lacks sensitivity analysis and consideration of adaptive/local sigma choices.

- Detailed question 1: Provide a detailed complexity analysis — cost per gradient step for µ and Σ as functions of N, D, and Monte Carlo samples S.

- Detailed question 2: How sensitive were EEG results to sigma tuning and did optimal sigma correlate with subject data properties?

- Detailed question 3: Have scalable alternatives for constructing M(x) been considered (e.g., neural-network-based metrics or mixtures of local metrics)?

- Detailed question 4: Elaborate on computational challenges and stability of the EM algorithm for LAND mixtures as component count increases.
