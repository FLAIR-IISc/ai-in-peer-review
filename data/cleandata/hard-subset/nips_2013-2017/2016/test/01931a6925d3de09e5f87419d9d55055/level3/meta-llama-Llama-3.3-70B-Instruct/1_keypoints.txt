- Proposes a novel Homotopy Smoothing (HOPS) algorithm for a family of non-smooth optimization problems.  
- Achieves improved iteration complexity Õ(1/ε^{1−θ}) under a mild local error bound condition (θ ∈ (0,1]).  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis including convergence guarantees and iteration complexity.  
- Well-written and easy to follow with clear explanations of algorithm and analysis.  
- Detailed comparisons to existing methods (Nesterov smoothing, primal-dual) are provided.  
- Experimental results on three tasks demonstrate HOPS’ effectiveness versus baselines.  
- Strength: novel algorithm with formal iteration-complexity guarantees.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis and convergence proof.  
- Strength: empirical validation showing improved performance.  
- Weakness: relies on a local error bound condition that may not hold for all problems and needs discussion/examples.  
- Weakness: choice of Gaussian kernel parameter σ (locality) is not well justified and needs robustness tests.  
- Weakness/suggestion: include visual results using intrinsic estimators and analyze computational complexity versus intrinsic methods.  
- Suggestion: expand treatment of mixtures of LANDs since real data may come from multiple components.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses; rating 8/10.
