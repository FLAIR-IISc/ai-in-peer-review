- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian generalization of the multivariate normal using a learned, smoothly varying metric that favors high-density regions.  
- Metric M(x) constructed from kernel-weighted local diagonal covariance estimates; defines Riemannian normal with geodesic distance and measure from M.  
- Practical normalization via Monte Carlo integration on tangent space (sample v ~ N(0,Σ) and weight by √|M(Expµ(v))|).  
- ML procedure: gradient descent on µ using Exp/log maps and factorized parameterization Σ−1 = AᵀA; extended to mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Experiments show LAND better fits non-elliptical, manifold-like structures than GMMs and intrinsic least-squares on synthetic data and EEG features.  
- Situates contribution relative to prior work: Riemannian normal on known manifolds (Pennec) and data-driven manifold/metric learning (Isomap, Hauberg, Tosi).  
- Strength: elegant unification of a parametric normal with a data-adaptive Riemannian geometry, preserving max-entropy characterization.  
- Strength: sound geometric implementation—tangent-space integration avoids BVPs; clear gradients; reasonable Σ−1 factorization.  
- Strength: natural mixture extension with properly normalized components addressing issues in prior unnormalized Riemannian mixtures.  
- Strength: qualitative results show contours aligning with nonlinear manifolds; EEG example indicates biomedical potential.  
- Weakness: metric construction (Eq.7) is ad hoc—uses inverse diagonal of kernel-smoothed variances; needs justification.  
- Weakness: diagonal-only metric—questions about ignoring local cross-covariances and alternatives (regularization, low-rank/shrinkage).  
- Weakness: metric not affine-invariant—ask whether to whiten inputs or adapt metric to be affine-invariant.  
- Weakness: isotropic kernels used—why not anisotropic kernels aligned with local structure?  
- Weakness: text blurs distinction between learned manifold metric M(x) (nonparametric, fixed) and model covariance Σ (parametric); suggests renaming (e.g., G(x)).  
- Weakness: no complexity/scalability analysis—need Big-O in N, D, K, S and cost of IVP solves; provide runtimes and iteration counts vs GMM EM.  
- Weakness: Monte Carlo normalization variance—S=3000 used; request estimator variance for C(µ,Σ) and gradients, and exploration of variance-reduction (control variates, quasi-MC, importance sampling).  
- Weakness: potential overfitting/double use of data—metric learned from full dataset then used to fit same data; recommend leave-one-out or train/validation for σ and ρ.  
- Clarity: provide more intuition for Eq.7 and a schematic showing how σ affects geodesic flow and normalization.  
- Clarity: Algorithm 1 typo (d should be ∇ or explicitly state steepest-descent notation).  
- Clarity: notation overload (M used for manifold and metric)—tighten notation.  
- Originality: novelty is coupling a learned data-adaptive metric with a Riemannian normal plus a workable ML/EM algorithm and tangent-space MC normalization.  
- Significance: potentially high but empirical evidence limited—needs comparisons to embeddings + GMMs, GP-LVM, manifold-regularized estimators, and Riemannian mixtures on known manifolds.  
- Evaluation requests: include held-out log-likelihoods, clustering metrics, calibration of responsibilities, ablations on σ, S, diagonal vs full local covariance, sensitivity to initialization, and scaling experiments across D and N.  
- Suggestions: learn σ (and ρ) via validation or empirical Bayes; consider multi-scale metrics.  
- Suggestions: explore low-rank local covariances with shrinkage or learned local principal directions.  
- Suggestions: use variance-reduced/quasi-MC estimators for C(µ,Σ); precompute metric fields or use neural ODE solvers with adjoints for faster Exp/Log and gradients.  
- Suggestions: consider natural gradient or trust-region methods for µ to mitigate conditioning, and report effects.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled link between Riemannian statistics and manifold learning; practical algorithmic contributions with clear derivations; compelling qualitative fits and promising EEG application.  
- Cons for acceptance: heuristic metric design and invariance concerns; insufficient experiments, baselines, ablations, and runtime analysis; open questions about estimator variance, convergence, and scalability; clarity and notation issues.  
- Minor: fix Algorithm 1 notation, clarify whether σ is per-dataset or per-component and its selection procedure, and consider renaming the metric tensor to avoid ambiguity.
