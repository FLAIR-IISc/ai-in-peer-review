This paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that generalizes the normal distribution to the "manifold" setting, where data is assumed to lie near a potentially low-dimensional manifold embedded in R^D. The LAND is a parametric distribution that depends only on a mean and a covariance, and is the maximum entropy distribution under a given metric. The authors develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, which relies on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the concepts of Riemannian geometry and manifold learning. The proposed LAND model is a novel contribution to the field, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on both synthetic and real data.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a thorough introduction to the concepts of Riemannian geometry and manifold learning, making the paper accessible to readers who may not be familiar with these topics.
* The proposed LAND model is a novel contribution to the field, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on both synthetic and real data.
* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the maximum likelihood algorithm used to infer the distribution parameters.

However, there are also some weaknesses and concerns:

* The paper assumes that the manifold dimension equals the ambient dimension D, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how to select the parameter σ, which is used to construct the metric tensor.
