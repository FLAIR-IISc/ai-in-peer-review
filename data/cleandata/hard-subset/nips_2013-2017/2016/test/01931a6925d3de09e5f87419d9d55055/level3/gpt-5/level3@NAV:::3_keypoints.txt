- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replace Euclidean metric in a Gaussian with a smoothly varying, data-adaptive Riemannian metric and fit Riemannian normals/mixtures via MLE with Monte Carlo normalization.  
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning by defining a continuous geometry favoring high-density regions and using geodesics in density evaluation and learning.  
- Compelling applications shown on EEG sleep staging and synthetic manifolds; attractive maximum-entropy characterization.  
- Technical development and algorithms are mostly sound and clearly specified.  
- Major critique: the “learned metric” is actually hand-crafted (Eq. 7) with fixed hyperparameters ρ and σ chosen empirically.  
- Paper lacks sensitivity analysis for σ and provides no principled procedure for selecting σ or ρ.  
- Eq. 7 is diagonal in ambient coordinates and not coordinate-invariant; paper should discuss diagonal vs full metric and invariance under linear transforms.  
- Experimental comparisons appear unfair: LAND is shown superior with few components, but GMMs can match with more components—model order should be chosen per method (e.g., BIC/AIC or validation).  
- No demonstration of overfitting behavior as K grows or quantitative reporting of computational cost and scalability (complexity in N, D, K; timings; limits).  
- Absence of larger benchmarks (e.g., MNIST) to evaluate scalability and practical performance.  
- Clarity is good overall, but the title is misleading (metric is constructed, not learned) and manuscript needs intuition on how σ deforms geometry/density and how to select it.  
- Originality: useful synthesis of hand-crafted Riemannian metric with normalized Riemannian normal/mixture and MLE, but related work on metric learning and manifold-based density modeling should be expanded (e.g., LMNN, ITML, NCA, Bellet surveys).  
- Significance: promising for generative modeling on manifolds, but practical impact depends on robust metric specification/learning and fair evaluation.  
- Pros: appealing conceptual bridge, maximum-entropy rationale and properly normalized EM, interesting applications and clear algorithmic recipe.  
- Cons: metric not learned (framing overstates), σ/ρ sensitivity and invariance unaddressed, fairness and overfitting analyses incomplete, no scalability/cost quantification.  
- Post-rebuttal requests: revise experiments to select K per method via model selection, include overfitting curves for large K, add σ-sensitivity analyses, and report runtime/scaling results.
