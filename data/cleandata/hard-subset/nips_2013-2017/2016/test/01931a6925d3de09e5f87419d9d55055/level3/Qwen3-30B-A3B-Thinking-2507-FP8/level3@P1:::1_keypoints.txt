- Introduces LAND, a parametric maximum-entropy generalization of the multivariate normal using a data-adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Constructs the metric from local inverse diagonal covariances via Gaussian-kernel smoothing, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Derives normalization via tangent-space integration and provides a gradient-descent MLE with Monte Carlo normalization.  
- Extends LAND to mixtures with an EM algorithm, enabling generative modeling beyond the training manifold.  
- Paper is very well-written and makes Riemannian concepts accessible.  
- LAND mixtures outperform Gaussian mixtures on synthetic data and achieve higher F-measure on EEG sleep-stage classification.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong: LAND is the maximum-entropy distribution under the learned metric and has an elegant normalization derivation.  
- Major scalability concerns: per-point covariance estimation and O(D^3) metric inversion limit applicability for D > 10.  
- Lacks concrete complexity analysis and empirical runtime evaluations for higher dimensions.  
- Incomplete baseline comparisons: main figures compare only to GMM; intrinsic least-squares estimators (stronger baselines) are relegated to supplement.  
- Bandwidth (σ) sensitivity is unaddressed: fixed empirically with no analysis or adaptive selection.  
- Positioning vs. prior metric-learning work is unclear; needs clarification of differences from Frome et al. (2013) and Malisiewicz & Efros (2011).  
- Contribution is original in using a learned metric to define a probabilistic model and the mixture extension is a notable innovation.  
- Recommendation: promising and solid contribution but requires scalability analysis and stronger empirical comparisons before being top-tier.  
- Pro: theoretical novelty, elegant mixture model, clear EEG application, excellent presentation; Con: unquantified scalability, insufficient baselines, bandwidth sensitivity, unclear relation to metric-learning literature.
