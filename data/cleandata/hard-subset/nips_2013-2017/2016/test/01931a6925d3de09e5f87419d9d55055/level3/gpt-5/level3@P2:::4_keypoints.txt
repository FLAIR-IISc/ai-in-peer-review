- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal with a data-adaptive metric M(x) (inverse local diagonal covariance) whose geodesics follow high-density regions.  
- Defines pM(x|µ,Σ) with volume measure induced by M and approximates normalization constant C(µ,Σ) by Monte Carlo in the tangent space using Expµ(v).  
- Develops a mixture of LANDs with an EM algorithm that correctly incorporates component-specific normalization constants.  
- Construction is principled: metric induces geodesics, Riemannian normal is max-entropy given mean/covariance, and tangent-space MC is a sensible variance-reduction strategy.  
- Gradients for µ and Σ are derived and used in a block-coordinate scheme; using Exp avoids boundary-value ODE solves.  
- Experiments show improved likelihood and F-measure on synthetic nonlinear data and EEG clustering versus GMMs and Riemannian least-squares.  
- Paper lacks runtime/complexity reporting, sensitivity analyses for σ and regularizer ρ, convergence diagnostics for MC (S=3000), and broader comparisons.  
- Learned metric is diagonal and not invariant to linear transformations, which can bias geodesics and densities; implications not fully explored.  
- Potential scalability issues in high dimensions due to many ODE solves for Exp evaluations; practical performance and approximations not detailed.  
- Writing and exposition are clear, with a helpful geometric prelude and a clear tangent-space MC explanation.  
- Suggest moving contrast with Simo-Serra et al. to the Introduction, adding computational cost summaries, main-text EM pseudo-code, and initialization details.  
- Contribution is combining known ingredients into a practical ML framework on a learned manifold with an estimator for normalization constants, but novelty over prior Riemannian mixtures is incremental.  
- Significance: useful modeling primitive and practical benefits shown, but impact limited without stronger baselines, more datasets, and ablations (e.g., diagonal vs full covariances).  
- Theoretical concerns: identifiability and consistency given the data-dependent geometry are not addressed.  
- Pros: principled Riemannian formulation, practical tangent-space MC estimator, correct mixture EM, empirical gains on tasks, and clear presentation.  
- Cons: incremental novelty, diagonal-metric and linear-invariance issues, limited baselines and scalability analysis, and incomplete sensitivity/MC robustness characterization.  
- Key requests for authors: move and expand contrast with prior work; report complexity and runtimes; add sensitivity analyses for σ and ρ; discuss/mitigate lack of linear invariance and non-diagonal covariances; include stronger baselines and more datasets; report MC convergence and justify S; clarify initialization strategies.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — solid and useful but incremental without sharper differentiation and stronger empirical/complexity evaluation.
