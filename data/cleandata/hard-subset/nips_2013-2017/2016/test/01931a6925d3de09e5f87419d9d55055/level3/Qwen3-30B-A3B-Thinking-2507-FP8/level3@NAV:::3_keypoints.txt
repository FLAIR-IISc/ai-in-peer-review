- Introduces LAND: replaces Euclidean metric in Gaussian with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric depending on local density via a Gaussian kernel.  
- Presents MLE estimation using Monte Carlo integration and extends model to mixtures.  
- Well-motivated connection between Riemannian statistics and manifold learning; relates to Pennec [15] and Hauberg [9].  
- Literature review is incomplete; should cite more recent metric learning work (e.g., [22, 24]).  
- Strength: elegant theoretical foundation for a locally adaptive normal distribution.  
- Strength: practical Monte Carlo scheme for normalization.  
- Strength: synthetic examples show LAND captures nonlinear structures better than standard GMMs.  
- Major concern: title claim of "learning a metric" is misleading—metric is hand-crafted with fixed hyperparameters (ρ and σ).  
- Critical omission: no sensitivity analysis or guidance for selecting σ, despite its strong influence on metric behavior.  
- Technical concern: metric tensor (Eq.7) is not coordinate-invariant; no justification for choosing a diagonal metric over a full metric.  
- Experimental flaws: comparisons unfair—LAND used fewer components than GMM; model selection (e.g., BIC) was not used to choose component counts.  
- Missing evaluations: no analysis of overfitting with many components and no computational scalability metrics (e.g., runtime on MNIST).  
- Required revisions: clarify metric is hand-crafted, provide σ sensitivity analysis, run fair model-selection-based comparisons, and discuss metric invariance and computational costs.  
- Overall verdict: promising idea but current presentation lacks rigor and completeness to merit acceptance.
