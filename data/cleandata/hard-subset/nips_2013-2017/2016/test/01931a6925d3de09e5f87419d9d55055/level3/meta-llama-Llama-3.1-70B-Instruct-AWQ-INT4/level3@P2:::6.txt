This paper introduces a locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) as a generalization of the normal distribution to the manifold setting. The authors replace the Euclidean distance with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric that favors regions of high local density. The resulting LAND is parametric, depending only on a mean and a covariance, and is the maximum entropy distribution under the given metric.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear introduction to Riemannian geometry and manifold learning. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the proposed estimation algorithms are clever and well-explained. The use of a Monte Carlo approximation to estimate the intractable normalization constant is a nice touch.

The authors evaluate their method on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task, comparing it to standard Gaussian mixture models. The results are encouraging, and the LAND is shown to be better at capturing the local properties of the data.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. The selection of the sigma parameter appears unclear and may be tuned on training data. The authors should provide more analysis and explanation of the practical usefulness of the learned distribution. Additionally, the novelty of the contribution is mainly in combining prior work (kernel metric, geodesic estimation, normal) and providing estimation algorithms.

The evaluation of the method is also limited. The authors should provide more experiments, including those on high-dimensional data, and address the assumption that the manifold dimension equals the ambient dimension D (full-rank M(x)). The comparison to standard Gaussians/GMMs when the data are generated by these models is also missing. Furthermore, the authors should provide more details on the cluster membership assignment, which is not explicitly stated.

The notation around Sigma vs M = Sigma^-1 and the role of the local kernel matrix versus a fitted covariance is also confusing and should be clarified. However, overall, the paper is interesting, useful, and worthy of publication. The introduction to manifold learning is well-explained for readers familiar with Riemannian manifolds.
