- Proposes LAND, a locally adaptive normal distribution generalizing the Gaussian to Riemannian manifolds with a learned manifold metric.  
- Builds on Riemannian statistics (Pennec 2006; Zhang & Fletcher 2013) but adapts these ideas to the manifold learning setting.  
- Strong technical development, including an MLE using gradient descent combined with Monte Carlo integration for normalization.  
- Experiments (synthetic and EEG sleep-stage) show LAND captures nonlinear structure better than GMMs, with compelling visualizations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Major weakness: lack of clear novelty relative to Simo-Serra et al. (2015); the introduction omits this key reference.  
- Differences between LAND and Simo-Serra are only discussed in related work and not clearly distinguished, making the novelty claim unclear.  
- Significance is limited because the paper does not convincingly show improvements are due to LAND’s specific innovations rather than general benefits of Riemannian geometry.  
- Revision needed: explicitly compare to and differentiate from Simo-Serra et al. to strengthen the novelty and contribution.
