This paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as an alternative to Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) for modeling data that lies near a nonlinear manifold. The LAND is grounded in Riemannian manifold theory and uses a locally adaptive metric to capture the nonlinear structure of the data. The authors introduce a maximum likelihood estimator for the distribution parameters and evaluate the LAND on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper is well-written and provides a sound theoretical development of the LAND. The experiments demonstrate the efficiency of the LAND in fitting non-trivial probability distributions and show that it outperforms GMMs in capturing the local properties of the data.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a novel approach to modeling data that lies near a nonlinear manifold.
* The LAND is grounded in Riemannian manifold theory, which provides a strong mathematical foundation for the model.
* The experiments demonstrate the efficiency of the LAND in fitting non-trivial probability distributions.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the theoretical development of the LAND.

Weaknesses:

* The paper lacks clear, strong novelty compared to Simo-Serra et al. [19], which also considers mixtures of Riemannian normal distributions.
* The differences between the LAND and GMMs are not clearly explained early in the paper, and ref. [19] is missing from the introduction.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other related work, such as [22] and [9].
