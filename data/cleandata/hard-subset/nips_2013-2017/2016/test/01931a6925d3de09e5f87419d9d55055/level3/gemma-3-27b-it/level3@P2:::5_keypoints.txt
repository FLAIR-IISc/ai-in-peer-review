- Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) by replacing Euclidean metric with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model data near nonlinear manifolds.  
- Theoretical development is mathematically sound and connects maximum entropy distributions with Riemannian geometry.  
- Novel and compelling idea bridging manifold learning and statistical modeling.  
- Metric tensor definition (Eq. 7) using inverse local covariance feels ad hoc and needs stronger justification.  
- Use of a Gaussian kernel for weighting local covariances is not explained; alternatives and bandwidth selection not explored.  
- Confusion between the local covariance used to define the metric and the covariance Σ estimated in maximum likelihood—relationship unclear.  
- MLE algorithm derived using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration; extended to mixtures via EM.  
- Experiments on synthetic data show some improvement over GMMs but lack depth across manifold types and noise levels.  
- Baseline comparisons (e.g., "least squares" LAND) are not clearly motivated.  
- EEG sleep experiments are interesting but reported only via F-measure; lacks deeper physiological interpretation of fitted distributions.  
- No discussion or analysis of computational complexity or runtime; Monte Carlo and geodesic computations likely expensive.  
- Scalability concerns: solving ODEs for geodesics may be a bottleneck, especially in higher dimensions, limiting practicality.  
- Recommendation: weak reject—interesting theoretical contribution but insufficient experimental validation and unresolved methodological/scalability issues.  
- Suggested revisions: expand experiments, justify metric/kernel choices, clarify covariance roles, and provide runtime/complexity analysis.  
- Minor comment: probable typo in Algorithm 1 (lines 4–5): "dµφ" should likely be "∇µφ".
