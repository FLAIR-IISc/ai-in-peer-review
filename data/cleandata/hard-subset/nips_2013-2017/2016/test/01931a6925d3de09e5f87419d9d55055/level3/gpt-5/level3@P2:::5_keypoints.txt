- Proposal: Introduces Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a data-driven Riemannian metric (local covariance) and geodesic distances in a Riemannian normal (and mixtures) with Monte Carlo tangent-space normalization.

- Novelty: Bridges manifold learning and Riemannian statistics by learning a smoothly varying metric from data and performing likelihood-based inference with normalization and gradients.

- Monte Carlo normalization: Tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant is reasonable but its variance and sample complexity are not analyzed; practical guidance beyond S=3000 is missing.

- Gradients and optimization: Gradients for µ and Σ are derived and plausible; steepest descent for µ to improve conditioning is sensible.

- Metric construction concerns: The inverse metric (Eq.7) uses diagonal local covariances with Gaussian kernel weights that are ad hoc, not affine-/rotationally-invariant, and insufficiently justified.

- Sensitivity to hyperparameters: No analysis or guidance on sensitivity to σ and ρ or on how to choose/adapt bandwidths; current advice risks hidden tuning and instability.

- Notational confusion: The paper blurs the distinction between the local metric M(x) and the global model covariance Σ; notation should be clarified (e.g., G(x)) and roles contrasted.

- Computational cost and scalability: Method likely requires many expensive Log/Exp ODE solves and O(N) kernel evaluations per operation; no complexity analysis, runtime/memory comparisons, or profiling provided.

- Experimental weaknesses: Limited evaluation — few datasets, no held-out likelihoods, no runtime comparisons, no ablations (σ, ρ, S), no sensitivity to initialization, and few/weak baselines.

- Baselines and comparisons: Lacks comparisons to stronger methods (MFA, normalizing flows, GMMs on learned embeddings, Riemannian mixtures on known manifolds).

- Practical implementation tips missing: No discussion of speedups (k-NN truncation, caching, sparse kernels, batching, GPU) or ODE solve counts per iteration.

- Invariance and alternatives: Should discuss alternatives to Eq.7 (full covariances with shrinkage, k-NN/adaptive kernels, mixtures of local metrics, probabilistic metrics) and consider pre-whitening or learning σ.

- Statistical/variance reduction suggestions: Recommend studying estimator variance for C(µ,Σ) and exploring variance reduction (control variates, importance sampling, reuse of samples).

- Clarity and minor fixes: Paper is readable but needs clearer intuition for Eq.7, corrected notation/typographical issues (Algorithm 1 ∇ vs d), and EM details from supplement brought into main text.

- Overall assessment: Conceptually significant and elegant idea with moderate-to-high originality, but practical utility unclear without stronger empirical evidence, metric justification, and scalability/complexity analysis.
