- Summary: Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a data-driven Riemannian metric, Riemannian normal density, Monte Carlo normalization in the tangent space, and an EM for mixtures; shows improved fit on curved synthetic data and EEG features.

- Relation to prior work: Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning and contrasts with embedding-based methods, building on ideas like geodesics-follow-data.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: Principled way to impose a data-adaptive geometry and define a maximum-entropy normal under that metric.
- Solid geometric formulation: Correct use of Exp/Log maps and recognition that normalization C(µ,Σ) depends on curvature; tangent-space Monte Carlo is sensible.
- Practical algorithms: Derives gradients for µ and Σ and an EM for mixtures that properly accounts for per-component normalizers.
- Empirical indications: Demonstrates ability to capture nonlinear cluster shapes and improves sleep-stage clustering F-measure for some subjects.

Weaknesses — Quality / Evaluation
- Metric construction not learned: Eq.7 uses a fixed-form, diagonal kernel smoother; the paper overstates “learning” the metric and depends on hyperparameters σ and ρ.
- Sensitivity to σ: Results depend on σ with little analysis or principled selection strategy; robustness curves and bias-variance discussion are needed.
- Coordinate dependence: Diagonal metric is not invariant under linear reparameterizations; requires discussion and guidance (e.g., whitening or full local covariance).
- Model selection / overfitting: K is not consistently selected per method; need held-out model selection, analysis of LAND behavior as K grows, and generalization diagnostics.
- Computational characterization lacking: Main cost (many IVPs inside Monte Carlo) needs runtime breakdowns, scaling with N,D,K,S, and analysis of Monte Carlo variance and convergence.
- Limited baselines: Should compare to nonlinear-aware alternatives like embedded-space GMMs or mixtures of local PCA to separate curved structure from mixture expressivity.

Clarity and presentation
- Clarify “learning” vs constructing the metric: Update title/abstract/text to reflect that the metric is constructed, not optimized.
- Intuition for Eq.7: Explain choice of diagonal form, role of m(µ,v)=|M(Expµ(v))|^1/2, and effects of coordinate rescaling.
- Expand related work on metric learning/invariance: Discuss connections to Xing, ITML, LMNN, NCA and prior probabilistic geometries.
- Implementation details missing: Provide ODE solver settings, backprop through IVPs, step sizes, initialization, and stopping criteria.

Originality
- Novel combination: Extends Riemannian statistics into manifold learning by using a data-adaptive ambient metric with a tractable MLE and tangent-space Monte Carlo.

Significance
- Potentially useful for generative modeling and clustering, but empirical scope is limited to low-D data and scalability is unclear.

Pros for acceptance
- Elegant, principled formulation and correct normalization handling; clear advantage on curved structures and promising real-data application.

Cons for acceptance
- Fixed-form, coordinate-dependent metric; insufficient σ/invariance analysis; weak experimental model selection and scalability evaluation.

Questions / suggestions for authors
- Can σ and ρ be learned (e.g., via EM, evidence maximization, or bilevel optimization)?
- Provide robustness curves over σ and feature rescaling, and recommend preprocessing or invariant alternatives.
- Report per-method K chosen by BIC/AIC/CV and show results for larger K to assess overfitting.
- Add runtime/scaling plots, analyze S vs accuracy, and consider variance reduction techniques.
- Include additional baselines like embedded-space GMMs and mixtures of local PCAs.
- Clarify the scope of the maximum-entropy claim when the metric is data-induced.

Overall assessment
- Interesting, well-founded idea with promise, but currently overclaims “metric learning,” is hyperparameter-sensitive, and requires stronger experimental/model-selection and computational analysis; reviewer leans to weak reject pending revisions.
