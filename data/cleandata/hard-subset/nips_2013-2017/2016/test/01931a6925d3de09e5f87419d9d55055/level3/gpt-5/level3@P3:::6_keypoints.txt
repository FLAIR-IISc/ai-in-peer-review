- Proposal: Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing global Euclidean metric with a data-adaptive Riemannian metric M(x) and defining densities via Riemannian normal with geodesic Log/Exp maps.
- Normalizer estimation: Uses tangent-space Monte Carlo sampling reweighted by √|M(Expµ(v))| to estimate the intractable normalizer C(µ,Σ).
- Parameter estimation: Derives steepest-descent update for µ and a block-coordinate update for Σ (via Σ−1 = AᵀA); extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm that includes component normalizers.
- Strength: Principled formulation linking manifold learning to generative modeling and clear pedagogical intro to Riemannian tools.
- Strength: Clever, well-explained algorithms—tangent-space MC and computationally motivated choice of IVPs for Exp vs BVPs for Log.
- Strength: Practical mixture extension with responsibilities that account for proper normalization.
- Strength: Empirical results show LAND captures curved structures better than Euclidean GMMs on synthetic and EEG data.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter σ (metric bandwidth) selection is ad hoc and may lead to tuning on training data and optimistic results.
- Weakness: Lack of analysis of practical gains for downstream tasks; missing quantitative ablations on NLL, clustering metrics, and computational cost vs σ and MC samples S.
- Weakness: Novelty is moderate—paper combines known ideas; needs clearer unique technical contributions (e.g., variance reduction, convergence).
- Weakness: Evaluation design problematic—no held-out likelihoods, potential σ overfitting, no sanity checks on Euclidean-Gaussian data, and no high-dimensional scalability experiments.
- Weakness: Modeling assumptions restrictiveness—assumes full-rank M(x) in ambient D and diagonal local covariances, which may misrepresent lower-dimensional data.
- Clarity issue: Notation conflates Σ (model covariance) and local Σloc(x)=M(x)−1; assignment rules and σ-selection protocol need explicit statement.
- Quality: Technically sound and carefully derived, but steepest-descent for µ lacks convergence guarantees and needs empirical characterization (step-size, restarts).
- Originality: Thoughtful synthesis of known methods applied to learned manifolds; moderate novelty.
- Significance: Potentially useful for manifold-aware generative modeling but broader impact limited by evaluation and σ guidance.
- Suggestions: Provide principled σ-selection (cross-validation, Bayesian, or learn σ via reparameterized MC and adjoint ODEs) and held-out NLLs/clustering metrics.
- Suggestions: Add experiments on Euclidean-ground-truth and higher-dimensional data (D=20–50) with runtime vs S/N comparisons and baselines.
- Suggestions: Clarify notation and cluster-label assignment; discuss diagonal vs off-diagonal/local low-rank metric extensions.
- Suggestions: Consider variance-reduction for the normalizer (common random numbers, control variates, Laplace init) and report step-size sensitivity and stopping criteria.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled extension, solid algorithmic contributions, clear derivations, and convincing qualitative gains on curved data.
- Cons for acceptance: Unclear σ selection, incomplete evaluation (no held-out tests or high-D), moderate novelty, and restrictive modeling assumptions.
- Overall: Interesting and useful paper that would benefit from clearer σ selection, stronger evaluations, and improved clarity; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
