- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), a generalization of the multivariate normal for data on or near non-linear manifolds using a learned locally adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Replaces the Euclidean metric with a data-driven Riemannian metric, grounded in manifold theory and prior manifold-learning/statistics work.  
- Presents a full MLE procedure that uses Monte Carlo integration over the tangent space for parameter estimation.  
- Extends the model to mixtures via an EM algorithm.  
- Validated on synthetic and real EEG datasets, showing advantages over standard Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs).  
- Strength: technically sound with clear theoretical derivations and solid experimental support.  
- Strength: manuscript is exceptionally well-written and organized, with helpful Riemannian geometry background and reproducible algorithmic details.  
- Strength: significant contribution—provides a parametric density model for non-parametric manifold-structured data, useful for practitioners and future work.  
- Weakness: insufficiently distinguishes from prior work (Simo-Serra et al. [19]); failing to highlight the key novelty (learning the manifold vs. assuming it known) early weakens perceived originality.  
- Recommendation: revise introduction and related work to explicitly contrast this approach with [19] and emphasize the originality of bridging data-driven manifold learning with Riemannian density estimation.
