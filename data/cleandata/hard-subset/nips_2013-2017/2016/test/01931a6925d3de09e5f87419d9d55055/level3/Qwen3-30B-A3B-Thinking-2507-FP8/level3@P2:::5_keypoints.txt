- Summary: Proposes LAND, a maximum-entropy generalization of the multivariate normal using a locally adaptive Riemannian metric, with MLE via gradient descent/Monte Carlo and an EM for mixtures, linking Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.

- Strength: Theoretical novelty—extends normal distributions to a learned local Riemannian metric setting.

- Strength: Clear mathematical formulation with careful Riemannian geometry definitions and connections.

- Strength: Provides a practical estimation algorithm (gradient descent + Monte Carlo) and an EM for mixtures.

- Weakness: Metric construction (Eq. 7) appears ad hoc with little theoretical justification or discussion of alternatives.

- Weakness: Notation/confusion between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the quantity estimated in Eq. (11), creating ambiguity about the model.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is weak—limited datasets, lack of proper baselines (e.g., other manifold methods), no statistical-significance analysis, and sensitivity to σ not explored.

- Weakness: No runtime or complexity analysis; evaluating the log map is reported expensive but not quantified.

- Recommendation: The idea is theoretically interesting but lacks convincing empirical evidence and practical validation, so the reviewer does not recommend acceptance.

- Suggested fixes: Provide stronger justification for the metric, clarify notation/relationship between local covariance and MLE, add comprehensive experiments with baselines and significance tests, and include runtime/complexity comparisons.
