- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric with a smoothly varying, data-driven Riemannian metric to define a locally adaptive normal distribution for density estimation.
- Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, offering a principled parametric model built on a non-parametric metric.
- Provides MLE procedure for LAND using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration, and extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic data and real-world EEG experiments.
- Novelty: a compelling, principled combination of Riemannian statistics and manifold learning for non-parametric density estimation.
- Strong theoretical foundation leveraging Riemannian manifold properties and maximum-entropy distributions.
- Technical soundness: estimation and inference methods appear well-motivated and correctly derived.
- Clear presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written with informative figures and accessible exposition.
- Generative capability: LAND can extrapolate beyond training data, unlike purely non-parametric methods.
- Computational complexity concern: per-point covariance estimation and inversion is costly; lacks detailed complexity analysis and a clear definition of “high-dimensional.”
- Related work positioning needs strengthening: insufficient comparison to per-point diagonal metric learning and local PCA methods.
- Bandwidth sensitivity: choice of Gaussian kernel bandwidth (σ) is critical; no systematic robustness study or adaptive bandwidth strategy presented.
- Limited empirical baselines: comparisons focus on GMMs (which perform poorly); should include intrinsic least squares estimators and other stronger baselines.
- Missing complexity trade-off discussion: needs analysis comparing computational/resource trade-offs between LAND and intrinsic estimators (e.g., Pennec).
- Recommendation: overall strong and recommend acceptance conditional on addressing computational analysis, including intrinsic estimator comparisons in main paper, and investigating bandwidth robustness.
