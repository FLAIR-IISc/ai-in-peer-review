- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replacing Euclidean metric with a data-learned Riemannian metric to model data on nonlinear manifolds.  
- Builds method on Riemannian manifold theory and derives an MLE using gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration.  
- Validated on synthetic data and real EEG sleep-stage data, showing improvements over standard GMMs in some scenarios.  
- Paper is very well-written with clear derivations and solid theoretical grounding; connects to Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Experimental validation and the sleep stage analysis are strong practical contributions.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty—method is very closely related to Simo-Serra et al. [19].  
- Significant omission: citation to Simo-Serra et al. [19] is missing in the introduction.  
- Paper does not clearly delineate differences from [19], particularly regarding normalization constants and non-parametric metric learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requiring more thorough comparison to [19] and adding the citation in the introduction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, clarity, solid experiments, and potential improvement over GMMs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty relative to prior work, unclear articulation of differences, and missing key citation.
