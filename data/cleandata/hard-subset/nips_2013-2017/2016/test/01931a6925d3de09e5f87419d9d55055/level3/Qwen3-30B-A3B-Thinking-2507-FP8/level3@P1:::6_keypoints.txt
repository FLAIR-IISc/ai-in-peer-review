- Proposal: Introduces LAND — a locally adaptive Riemannian normal that replaces Euclidean metric with a data-learned Riemannian metric M(x) defined as the inverse of a local diagonal covariance.  
- Technical methods: Derives MLE via gradient descent with Monte Carlo integration for the normalization constant, provides a steepest-descent optimizer (Alg. 1) and an EM algorithm for mixtures.  
- Monte Carlo contribution: Cleverly transforms the normalization integral to the tangent space to avoid expensive geodesic computations.  
- Validation: Evaluated on synthetic manifold data and EEG sleep staging, showing improved clustering and capturing nonlinear sleep-stage distributions.  
- Strengths: Sound technical execution, well-motivated geometric intuition, effective optimization, and clear connections to Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Major weakness — sigma tuning: Kernel bandwidth σ, crucial to the learned metric, is ambiguously handled and tuned on training data, risking overfitting and invalidating evaluation.  
- Missing baseline: No comparison to GMMs on data generated from standard Gaussians, so unclear if gains come from modeling nonlinearity or overfitting.  
- Practical utility under-explored: Improvements shown qualitatively (contours/clustering) but downstream task benefits (e.g., sleep-stage classification F1) are not quantified.  
- Scalability limitation: Assumes full-rank M (manifold dim = ambient dim D), limiting applicability to high-dimensional data and lacking experiments in such settings.  
- Originality/significance: Synthesizes existing ideas into a novel parametric model; learning the metric is incremental but implementation details are solid.  
- Significance undermined: Lack of rigorous comparisons and limited real-world impact evidence weaken the claimed superiority over GMMs.  
- Clarity issues: Notation is confusing (uses Σ for covariance then M = Σ^{-1}), potentially misleading; cluster assignment procedure is not explicitly described.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — clarify σ selection/hyperparameter sensitivity, add Gaussian-data GMM comparisons, quantify downstream task gains, and fix notation/clarity.
