- Proposes LAND: a parametric distribution over a learned nonparametric Riemannian metric, linking metric/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Metric defined as inverse of a local diagonal covariance from a Gaussian kernel, producing geodesics that favor high-density regions.  
- Fits a Riemannian normal by MLE using tangent-space Monte Carlo for the normalization constant and gradient-based updates; trains mixtures with an EM-style scheme.  
- Empirically models nonlinear structure, extrapolates beyond training support, and outperforms GMMs on synthetic and EEG clustering tasks.  
- Relates to prior work on Riemannian statistics (Pennec; Zhang & Fletcher; Simo-Serra et al.) and manifold learning (Isomap), but uses a learned continuous metric.  
- Connects to local/diagonal per-point metrics and local-PCA metric learning; novelty in “parametric over nonparametric space” and maximum-entropy interpretation.  
- Formulation is technically sound and well-motivated; tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization is elegant.  
- Major scalability concern: computing M(x) needs O(ND) kernel-weighted accumulators per evaluation and per ODE step, plus geodesic IVPs/BVPs and exponential-map calls, with mixtures compounding cost.  
- Reviewer requests explicit asymptotic and wall-clock complexity analysis as functions of N, D, S, path-solver accuracy, and K, and clarification of “high-dimensional” (examples like d=3,5,10,100).  
- Paper acknowledges non-invariance to linear transforms; reviewer asks for discussion and a simple whitening pre-processing baseline.  
- Clarity is strong, but more detail needed on σ selection: whether σ is uniform across heterogeneous densities and robustness to σ (suggest variable bandwidth/kNN and sensitivity plots).  
- Better positioning required relative to local-metric and local-PCA literatures to strengthen contribution.  
- Significance: promising for generative modeling on manifolds and mixtures are compelling; reviewer requests more visual/quantitative comparisons to intrinsic estimators and computational timings across dimensions.  
- Pros: elegant theoretical link, sound ML/EM derivations with generative extrapolation, and very clear writing.  
- Cons: insufficient complexity analysis/scalability evidence, limited positioning vs related work, sensitivity to σ not thoroughly evaluated, and comparisons rely on weak GMM baselines rather than intrinsic estimators.
