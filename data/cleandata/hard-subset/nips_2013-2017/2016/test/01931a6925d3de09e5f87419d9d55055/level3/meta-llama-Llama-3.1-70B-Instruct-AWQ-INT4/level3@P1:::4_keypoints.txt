- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean distance with a learned local nonlinear distance to model complex data.  
- Introduces a maximum likelihood estimator for LAND parameters grounded in Riemannian manifold theory.  
- Theoretical development is sound and accessible, with a clear introduction to Riemannian statistics for non-experts.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world data show LAND outperforms GMMs and LAND mixtures (least squares) on mean negative log-likelihood and clustering accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and figures effectively illustrate key points.  
- Omits early discussion and comparison to Simo-Serra et al. [19], which limits clarity about novelty.  
- Differences from Simo-Serra et al. are not clearly explained, weakening the paper’s claimed originality.  
- Overall quality rated 8/10; originality 6/10; impact 7/10; clarity 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theoretical grounding, and strong experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited clear novelty relative to prior work and missing/late citation of closely related reference.
