- Proposes LAND (Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution): parametric distribution over a nonparametric metric using learned Riemannian normal distribution with geodesic distances from local inverse diagonal covariances; extends to mixtures and generative extrapolation.

- Formalizes a maximum-entropy distribution on learned Riemannian manifolds, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.

- Writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized, making complex geometric concepts accessible.

- Strong theoretical foundation and elegant formulation.

- Practical Monte Carlo integration for normalization is a useful implementation choice.

- Promising empirical results on synthetic and EEG data; mixture extension is compelling.

- Lacks clarity on scalability: “high-dimensional” is undefined and no computational complexity analysis versus intrinsic estimators is provided.

- Experimental comparisons are misleadingly limited to GMMs; should compare to intrinsic estimators that reportedly outperform GMMs (per supplementary material).

- Insufficient positioning within metric learning literature; similar per-point diagonal metrics and local PCA methods (e.g., Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) are not adequately contrasted.

- Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma treated as fixed with no robustness analysis or discussion of spatially varying bandwidths across density regions.

- Omissions in scaling analysis, comparative baselines, and visualizations limit the paper’s impact despite sound theory and clear writing.
