- Introduces the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replace Euclidean Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a smoothly varying, data-dependent Riemannian metric M(x).  
- Learns M(x) nonparametrically via kernel-smoothed, diagonal local covariances (kernel bandwidth σ is critical).  
- Computes geodesic Exp/Log maps by integrating an ODE, enabling distances and tangent-space calculations.  
- Defines the Riemannian normal density pM(x|µ,Σ) ∝ exp(−½⟨Logµ(x), Σ−1 Logµ(x)⟩) w.r.t. the manifold measure.  
- Normalization constant C(µ,Σ) is intractable; authors derive a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator C = Z · EN(0,Σ)[m(µ,v)] with m(µ,v)=|M(Expµ(v))|1/2.  
- Propose ML estimation: steepest-descent updates for µ and parameterization Σ−1 = AᵀA for stable optimisation, with gradients including normalization terms.  
- Extend to mixtures and give an EM scheme that includes per-component normalization constants in responsibilities.  
- Experiments (synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage clustering) show LAND better captures nonlinear structure vs GMMs and least-squares Riemannian baselines and avoids low-density means.  
- Builds on Riemannian statistics and manifold learning; novelty is integrating a learned nonparametric metric into a maximum-entropy Riemannian normal with a scalable MC normalization.  
- Tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids expensive boundary-value problems for Log and is a practical computational choice.  
- Formulation and gradients are technically sound; normalization term explanation is convincing and empirically supported.  
- Major weakness: kernel bandwidth σ selection is ad hoc and potentially overfit (grid search per subject); no principled σ strategy or sensitivity analysis.  
- No held-out test likelihoods or separate validation/test splits reported for model selection and evaluation.  
- Scalability is unaddressed: each C(µ,Σ) needs S IVPs for Expµ(v); per-iteration cost and empirical runtime vs N, D, S, solver tolerances are not analyzed.  
- The learned metric M(x) is full-rank in RD (i.e., an ambient-space metric, not a lower-dimensional manifold); this assumption and its high-D consequences should be emphasized.  
- Clarity issues: notation confuses local metric M(x) and model covariance Σ; recommend using Λ for precision and clearer separation of terms.  
- Mixture algorithm details need clearer exposition: explicit E-step responsibilities, M-step updates, and how C is estimated per component per iteration (reuse of samples, variance control).  
- Limited ablations: no study of S vs accuracy/runtime, no baseline where data come from Gaussians/GMMs to test whether LAND reduces to standard Gaussian.  
- Cons: diagonal M(x) implies lack of linear invariance; scalability and high-dimensional behavior untested.  
- Requests: provide principled σ selection (CV likelihood, median k-NN, or learning σ), held-out NLLs, runtime complexity and wall-clock times, solver details, variance reduction strategies, sparsification/k-NN for m, and affine-invariance tests.  
- Suggest ablation removing normalization term to quantify its effect on mean placement.  
- Minor: explicitly state the “manifold” is RD with a learned metric (not necessarily lower-dimensional); fix typos and notation consistency.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, practically useful contribution with novel integration of learned metrics and normalized Riemannian Gaussians; lean accept pending stronger validation (σ selection, held-out evaluation, scalability analysis).
