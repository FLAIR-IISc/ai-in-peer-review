- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replacing Euclidean metric with a hand-crafted local Riemannian metric for density estimation on manifolds.  
- Core idea is interesting and promising, bridging Riemannian geometry and manifold learning as a parametric extension to nonparametric methods.  
- Metric is not learned: Eq.7 uses hand-chosen hyperparameters (ρ, σ), so the claim of “learning a metric” is misleading.  
- Sensitivity to σ is not analyzed; no intuition or systematic selection procedure for σ is provided.  
- Recommendation to learn ρ and σ jointly with model parameters to properly merge metric learning with LAND.  
- Lack of discussion on coordinate invariance and implications of using a diagonal vs. full metric.  
- Insufficient connection to existing metric-learning and Riemannian manifold literature.  
- Experimental comparison is flawed: GMM with more components can match LAND, so reported advantages may be artificial.  
- Recommend fairer model selection (e.g., BIC) per method and demonstrations of LAND’s overfitting behavior with many components.  
- Missing quantitative analysis of computational cost and scalability; suggest larger-scale experiments (e.g., MNIST).  
- Pros: novel, well-motivated idea with demonstrated gains over GMMs in some settings.  
- Cons: misleading title, unclear σ treatment, flawed experiments, limited scalability analysis, weak literature integration.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; address metric-learning claim, analyze σ, and perform fairer, scalable evaluations to strengthen the paper.
