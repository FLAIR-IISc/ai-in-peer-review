- Introduces LAND: a generalization of the multivariate normal using geodesic distance on a learned, data-dependent Riemannian manifold.  
- Constructs a non-parametric metric that favors high-density regions and defines a parametric distribution (mean, covariance) on this manifold.  
- Derives an MLE procedure using Monte Carlo integration on the tangent space to estimate the normalization constant.  
- Extends the model to mixtures of LANDs with an EM algorithm.  
- Bridges manifold learning (learning a Riemannian metric) with statistical methods on known manifolds.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic non-linear data and EEG sleep-stage clustering, outperforming GMMs.  
- Strength: principled, theoretically grounded formulation (maximum entropy under learned metric).  
- Strength: complete technical development, including gradient-based optimization and practical Monte Carlo scheme to avoid expensive BVPs.  
- Strength: strong experimental visualizations and real-world results showing clear advantages over GMMs.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and figures effectively support claims.  
- Weakness: insufficient early positioning vs prior work (Simo-Serra et al. [19]); the novelty of learning the manifold vs fixed-manifold methods is not emphasized up front.  
- Weakness: scalability and parameter selection concerns—kernel bandwidth σ is chosen heuristically and performance is sensitive, and metric computation sums over all N points (potentially expensive).  
- Detailed comment: the claim that [19] is "inconsistent with the probabilistic formulation" should be elaborated to clarify implications of ignoring normalization constants.  
- Detailed comment: the choice of diagonal local covariance (Eq.7) is for efficiency; it’s unclear if full covariance was tried and what trade-offs arise.  
- Detailed comment: optimization is non-convex and initialization (in supplements) may affect solutions; a brief main-text note on initialization strategy would help.  
- Summary eval — Quality: high (technically sound with rigorous derivations).  
- Summary eval — Clarity: excellent (well-written and organized).  
- Summary eval — Originality: good but presentation weakens perceived novelty; main novelty is learning the manifold plus MLE.  
- Summary eval — Significance: high (LAND is a powerful, flexible alternative to GMMs with broad applicability).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, lean toward acceptance if the authors revise to clearly and early articulate novelty over prior fixed-manifold methods.
