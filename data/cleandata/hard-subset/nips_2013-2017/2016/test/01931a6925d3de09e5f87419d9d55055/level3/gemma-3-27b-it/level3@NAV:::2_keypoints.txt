- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing the Euclidean metric with a locally adaptive Riemannian metric.  
- Core idea: adapt the shape of the normal density to better reflect the underlying data manifold.  
- Grounded in Riemannian geometry and manifold learning; positioned relative to methods like Isomap.  
- Develops a maximum likelihood estimation procedure on the learned manifold.  
- Introduces a scalable Monte Carlo integration scheme for normalization and a gradient-based optimization algorithm.  
- Extends LAND to mixture models and derives an EM algorithm as a practical alternative to GMMs.  
- Strong experimental results on synthetic data and real-world EEG sleep-stage analysis, showing improvements over GMMs.  
- Ablation study on the σ parameter demonstrates model sensitivity and adaptability.  
- Theoretical justification via maximum entropy is convincing.  
- Limitations: scalability concerns for high-dimensional data due to metric learning and ODEs for geodesics.  
- Suggests need for deeper discussion of assumptions behind the chosen Riemannian metric and better σ optimization.  
- Reviewer conclusion: technically sound, novel, well-presented work with strong potential; recommends acceptance.
