- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal density using a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric (inverse of a locally smoothed diagonal covariance) instead of Euclidean/Mahalanobis metric.  
- Derives ML estimators for mean and covariance using tangent-space Monte Carlo for the normalization constant and manifold-aware gradient updates; extends to an EM algorithm for mixtures.  
- Empirical results: synthetic curved-manifold data and EEG sleep-stage clustering show LAND (and mixtures) capture non-ellipsoidal structure better and outperform GMMs and intrinsic least-squares estimators.  
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning by moving from distributions on known manifolds to data-adaptive learned metrics.  
- Closest prior work is Simo‑Serra et al. [19] (mixtures on known manifolds with geodesic EM); the paper differs by learning the manifold and handling normalization but doesn’t emphasize this early or cite [19] in the introduction.  
- Technical soundness: formulation is coherent, tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator is sensible, and gradients/optimization are implemented with manifold-aware steps.  
- Computational concerns: Monte Carlo (S=3000) and repeated Exp/Log ODE solves may be costly and unstable in higher dimensions; more scaling/time/memory details needed.  
- Metric expressivity: using a diagonal, kernel-smoothed metric limits expressivity, breaks linear invariance, and may oversimplify local anisotropic structure.  
- Baselines are limited: primarily compared to GMMs and LS estimators; should compare to graph geodesic/kernel-based or other manifold-aware clustering methods and ensure fair GMM tuning.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: σ selection is important and currently manual; automated selection (ML, CV, plug-in) would be valuable.  
- Clarity is good overall; algorithm and supplement are clear and derivations helpful.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: cite and contrast [19] in the introduction, summarize sensitivity to S and σ in main text, give per-iteration complexity (D, N, S, K) and runtimes, and clarify ODE solver choices/tolerances and any caching/approximations.  
- Originality: moderate—combines known ingredients (Riemannian normals, learned metrics, mixtures) but contributes a practical normalized ML estimator, tangent-space Monte Carlo for the partition function, and a usable optimization scheme.  
- Significance: addresses limitations of Euclidean Gaussians for curved data and could influence generative modeling on manifolds, but practical impact may be limited by computation and diagonal metric assumption.  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated Riemannian formulation, sound ML estimator with normalization, mixture EM, and convincing synthetic/real-data improvements.  
- Cons for acceptance: novelty vis-à-vis [19] not clearly presented early, restrictive diagonal metric, limited baselines and scaling discussion, and manual σ selection.  
- Key author requests: add [19] to introduction and contrast methods; report runtimes and scaling (D, N, S, K) vs. GMM EM; show sensitivity of results to σ and S and consider learning σ; discuss whether full local covariances (with shrinkage) help; explore caching/approximate Exp/Log to reduce cost.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper with clear theory and empirical support; lean toward acceptance pending improved framing regarding [19] and added complexity/sensitivity details.
