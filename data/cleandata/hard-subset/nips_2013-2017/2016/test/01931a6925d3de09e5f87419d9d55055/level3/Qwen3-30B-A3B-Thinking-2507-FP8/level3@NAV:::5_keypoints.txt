Introduces LAND: replaces Mahalanobis distance with geodesic distance from a learned Riemannian metric.  
Constructs a smoothly varying metric using local covariance estimated via a Gaussian kernel to adapt to nonlinear data.  
Develops MLE training with gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration; extends to mixtures (mLANDs).  
Theoretically grounded in Riemannian statistics and connects Pennec’s Riemannian normal to data-adaptive manifold learning.  
Use of geodesic distances (Isomap-like) is well-motivated to capture nonlinear structure.  
Inverse metric tensor definition in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and lacks sufficient theoretical justification.  
Unclear relationship between the local covariance in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimation in Eq. (11)—potentially inconsistent.  
Experimental evaluation is weak: insufficient baselines and lack of parameter sensitivity analysis.  
No discussion of computational complexity or runtime, and no comparison to EM—raises scalability concerns.  
EEG experiment shows marginal improvement over GMMs but uses ad hoc σ selection and lacks rigorous evaluation metrics.  
Applicability to high-dimensional data is questionable given the missing computational analysis.  
Minor typo in Algorithm 1 (d → ∇).  
Overall: strong theoretical contribution but needs much stronger experimental validation and computational analysis for practical impact.
