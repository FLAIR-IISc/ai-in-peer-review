- Proposes HOPS (homotopy smoothing) for a family of non-smooth optimization problems.  
- Achieves iteration complexity Õ(1/(1−θ)) with θ ∈ (0,1] under a mild local error bound condition.  
- Uses a homotopy strategy to improve smoothing-algorithm convergence — an interesting and novel idea.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical convergence analysis and guarantees.  
- Experimental results show HOPS outperforms state-of-the-art methods.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear overview of problem, related work, and algorithm.  
- Needs clearer explanation of the local error bound condition and its relation to other conditions.  
- Should give more insight into choosing the smoothing parameter and number of iterations per stage.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent notation and some poorly formatted equations.  
- Originality is strong: new algorithm plus novel convergence analysis.  
- Significance: faster convergence for many non-smooth problems with broad applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above issues.
