- Proposes modeling observation space with a hand-crafted Riemannian metric and defining a curved normal (LAND) to better capture data manifolds — useful and interesting application.  
- Title misleading: claims to "learn a metric" but metric is hand-crafted in Eq.7 with hyperparameters rho and sigma fixed empirically.  
- Sigma likely sensitive; paper lacks discussion on the intuitive effect of varying sigma and how to select it.  
- Suggests possibly merging metric learning with LAND learning so rho and sigma can be learned rather than fixed.  
- Eq.7 metric is not coordinate-invariant; needs discussion on invariance (diagonal vs full metric) and more intuition about the chosen metric.  
- Should relate and cite richer literature on metric learning and manifold learning connected to Riemannian geometry.  
- Experimental comparison flawed: LAND is advantaged with few components but GMM can match with more; recommend choosing number of components per method via model selection (e.g., BIC) for fair comparison.  
- Recommend demonstrating LAND overfitting with many components and providing quantitative measures of computational cost and scalability (e.g., limits, experiments on MNIST).  
- After authors' response, reviewer reaffirms LAND’s flexibility makes it more prone to overfitting (even if geodesics are nearly straight) and urges revising experiments accordingly.
