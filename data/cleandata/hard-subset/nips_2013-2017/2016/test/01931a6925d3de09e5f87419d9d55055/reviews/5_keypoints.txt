- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution using a learned geodesic manifold traversal distance replacing the Mahalanobis distance, with an extension to mixtures (LANDs) and evaluation on synthetic and EEG data.
- The idea of using a geodesic distance (akin to Isomap) is interesting and promising.
- The definition of the inverse metric tensor in Eq. (7) appears ad hoc and needs more justification.
- Request for intuition on using a Gaussian kernel to weight local covariances in the weighted sum.
- Confusion between the local covariance defined in Eq. (7) and the MAP estimation of that quantity in Eq. (11); unclear if they refer to the same thing.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and insufficiently thorough.
- Major concern about scalability and computational efficiency; no complexity or runtime comparison to standard EM provided.
- Overall interesting theoretical contribution but questionable practical applicability.
- Minor issue: Algorithm 1, lines 4–5, typo/symbol correction d -> ∇.
