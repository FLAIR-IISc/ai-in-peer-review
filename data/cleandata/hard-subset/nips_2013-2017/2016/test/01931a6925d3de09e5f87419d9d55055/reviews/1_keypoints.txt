- Proposes LAND, a parametric metric over a nonparametric space linking metric learning/manifold learning with Riemannian statistics.  
- Models the metric as a Riemannian normal distribution (Pennec [15]) and learns mean/covariance via maximum likelihood.  
- Defines distances as geodesics on the learned Riemannian manifold, using local metrics from inverse diagonal covariances.  
- Also considers mixtures of LAND components and highlights generative extrapolation beyond training data.  
- Raises concern about applicability to large-scale, high-dimensional data because per-point covariance estimation and inversion are computationally challenging.  
- Requests analysis of complexity and clarification of what "high-dimensional" means (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 100 dimensions).  
- Calls for better positioning within the broader metric learning literature, citing similar per-point diagonal metrics (Frome et al., Malisiewicz & Efros) and local PCA approaches.  
- Questions the choice and influence of the Gaussian kernel bandwidth sigma for defining locality and requests robustness tests and whether sigma is uniform across density regions.  
- Notes Fig. 4 shows LAND comparable to intrinsic estimators, but visual comparisons in Figs. 5–6 are against GMMs only, which perform poorly and are less informative.  
- Recommends replacing or supplementing GMM visualizations with intrinsic estimator visualizations (some appear in supplementary material and outperform GMM).  
- Suggests adding computational-complexity comparisons between LAND and intrinsic estimators to guide method choice by data structure.  
- Highlights mixtures of LAND as a particularly interesting direction that should be expanded.  
- Praises writing quality: paper is very well written and pleasant to read.
