- Proposes a locally adaptive normal distribution using a smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x) instead of Euclidean distance.  
- Computes geodesic distances via exponential/logarithm maps and Euler–Lagrange ODEs to map points to tangent planes.  
- Applies a Mahalanobis distance on log-mapped points and uses that inner product to measure distance to the mean.  
- Derives maximum likelihood estimators for mean and covariance but solutions lack closed form.  
- Introduces a steepest-descent algorithm to estimate parameters.  
- Uses a Monte Carlo approximation to estimate the intractable normalization constant.  
- Evaluates on synthetic manifold data and a clustering task, comparing to standard Gaussian mixture models.  
- Reviewer finds the manifold techniques, Monte Carlo method, and steepest-descent algorithm sound, clever, and well explained.  
- Concern: sigma parameter selection appears unclear and may be tuned on training data.  
- Concern: insufficient analysis and explanation of the practical usefulness of the learned distribution.  
- Novelty concern: contribution mainly combines prior work (kernel metric, geodesic estimation, normal) and provides estimation algorithms.  
- Evaluation concern: unclear sigma choice, potential overfitting, lack of held-out test results, and missing comparisons when data are generated by standard Gaussians/GMMs.  
- Scalability concern: no experiments on high-dimensional data and an unusual assumption that manifold dimension equals ambient dimension D (full-rank M(x)).  
- Clarity issue: confusing notation around Sigma vs M = Sigma^-1 and the role of the local kernel matrix versus a fitted covariance.  
- Clarity issue: cluster membership assignment is not explicitly stated (presumably nearest geodesic distance to means).  
- Positive overall assessment: paper is interesting, useful, and worthy of publication; manifold introduction is well explained for readers familiar with Riemannian manifolds.
