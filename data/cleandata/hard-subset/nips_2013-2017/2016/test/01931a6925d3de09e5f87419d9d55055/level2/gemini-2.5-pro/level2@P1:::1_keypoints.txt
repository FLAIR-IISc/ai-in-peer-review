- Introduces LAND: a novel generalization of the multivariate normal using a locally adaptive Riemannian metric learned non-parametrically, yielding a max-entropy distribution on the learned manifold.

- Replaces the Mahalanobis (Euclidean) distance with geodesic distance under a data-dependent metric that warps space toward high-density regions.

- LAND is parametric (mean and covariance on the manifold) and extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm.

- Derives MLE with an intractable normalization constant handled via Monte Carlo integration over the tangent space (using the exp map) to avoid expensive boundary-value problems.

- Parameter inference uses block coordinate descent with gradient-based updates for mean and covariance; gradients appear correctly derived.

- Empirical results: convincing synthetic demonstrations of non-linear structure recovery and improved EEG sleep-stage clustering versus GMMs.

- Novelty/significance: elegantly unifies Riemannian normal distributions with learned data-dependent metrics, enabling parametric models on non-parametric manifolds.

- Technical soundness: formulation of normalization as a tangent-space expectation is clever and makes Monte Carlo practical; derivations are solid.

- Clarity/organization: well-written, includes helpful Riemannian background and Algorithm 1 summarizing estimation.

- Major weakness — computational cost/scalability: metric computation sums over N and inference requires many exponential/logarithm map ODE solves per data point and MC sample, limiting N and D scalability.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: model performance depends on kernel width σ with no principled selection or learning strategy presented.

- Optimization concerns: non-convex objective, potential local minima, and unstable mean gradients due to Σ; authors use steepest descent but could consider more advanced Riemannian optimizers.

- Questions for authors: provide formal complexity analysis in N, D, S; discuss using full local covariance vs diagonal; quantify impact of number of MC samples S on gradient variance and convergence.

- Overall verdict: excellent, original, and technically sound paper with convincing experiments and a strong acceptance recommendation despite practical limitations.
