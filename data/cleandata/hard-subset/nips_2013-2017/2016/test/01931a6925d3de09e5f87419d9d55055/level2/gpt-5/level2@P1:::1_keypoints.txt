- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian generalization of the multivariate normal using a data-driven local metric M(x) and geodesic distance in the Gaussian exponent.
- Uses diagonal local covariance from Gaussian kernel smoothing so geodesics favor high-density regions; density is the Riemannian normal with max-entropy on the learned manifold.
- Introduces a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant C(µ, Σ) using exponential maps (IVPs) to avoid expensive log maps (BVPs).
- Derives gradients for µ and Σ and presents a block coordinate descent scheme with preconditioning.
- Extends LAND to mixtures with an EM algorithm that accounts for component-specific normalization constants.
- Empirically shows better fit and clustering than GMMs and intrinsic least squares on synthetic curved data.
- Demonstrates improved alignment with sleep stages on 5D EEG NMF features, with further gains by tuning kernel width σ.
- Conceptually strong: bridges learned-manifold metric learning with Riemannian statistics and preserves maximum-entropy properties.
- Tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization is elegant and practically important.
- Derivations and practical optimization (steepest descent, factorized Σ) are sound and attentive to conditioning issues.
- Experiments provide clear synthetic illustrations and a real-data proof of concept.
- Metric design concern: diagonal local covariance with isotropic kernel is not linear-invariant and may underfit anisotropic local structure.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on σ (and ρ) and is tuned per subject; lacks systematic selection or methods to learn σ.
- Computational cost and scalability: evaluating C(µ, Σ) and gradients requires S exponential maps (S=3000), raising concerns for larger D, N, K; lacks runtime/scaling plots.
- Gradient-of-log-normalization concern: Eq.12’s gradient term EN[m(µ,v) v] needs clearer justification and validation (e.g., finite-difference checks) due to dependence of m on Expµ(v).
- Evaluation breadth limited: missing baselines like mixtures of factor analyzers, PPCA mixtures, GPLVM generative models, or spectral clustering for clustering comparison.
- Statistical interpretation: learning the metric from the same data used to fit densities risks overfitting; needs discussion of sample-splitting or regularization.
- Overall quality: technically sound with reasonable estimators, but some derivational details (especially ∇µ log C) need clearer justification.
- Clarity: paper is well organized; should move key derivation steps (Eq.12/13) from supplement, add algorithmic complexity, runtimes, and clarify initialization/convergence.
- Originality: novel adaptation of Riemannian normal to a learned metric and tangent-space normalization; extends prior Riemannian statistics to data-driven manifolds.
- Significance: promising parametric, interpretable manifold-aware generative model if computational issues are addressed.
- Question: Can you validate the gradient of log C wrt µ (e.g., finite differences), and do you use common random numbers to reduce MC variance?
- Question: What are typical runtimes per iteration for D=5 and synthetic datasets, and how do runtimes scale with K and S?
- Question: Have you tried local full covariances with shrinkage for M(x), and does that materially improve performance?
- Question: How do results change if the metric is estimated on a separate subset to avoid “double dipping”?
- Suggestion: Add ablations on S and σ showing likelihood vs. runtime trade-offs.
- Suggestion: Include at least one additional baseline (e.g., MFA or spectral clustering) and report standard errors.
- Suggestion: Discuss invariance and recommend simple preprocessing (e.g., linear whitening) to mitigate sensitivity to rotations.
- Suggestion: Consider adaptive or learned bandwidth σ (e.g., local bandwidths based on kNN distances).
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — promising but needs stronger gradient justification, runtime/scaling analysis, and investigation of metric sensitivity.
