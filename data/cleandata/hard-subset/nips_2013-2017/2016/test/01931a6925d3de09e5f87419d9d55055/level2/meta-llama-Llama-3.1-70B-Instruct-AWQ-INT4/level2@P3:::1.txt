**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) as a generalization of the normal distribution to the "manifold" setting. The LAND is a parametric distribution that depends on a mean and a covariance, but the underlying metric is non-parametric. The authors develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, which relies on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration. The LAND is extended to mixture models, and the corresponding EM algorithm is provided.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to modeling data that lies near a nonlinear manifold embedded in a high-dimensional space.
* The LAND is a parametric distribution that can be used as a building block in various machine learning algorithms.
* The authors provide a maximum likelihood estimation scheme for the LAND, which combines a gradient-based optimization with a scalable Monte Carlo integration method.
* The paper demonstrates the efficiency of the LAND in fitting non-trivial probability distributions over both synthetic data and EEG measurements of human sleep.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the data lies near a nonlinear manifold embedded in a high-dimensional space, which may not always be the case.
* The LAND depends on the parameter σ to construct the metric tensor, which can be difficult to select in practice.
* The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.
* The LAND is a relatively simple extension of the normal distribution, and it may not be sufficient to capture complex data distributions.
