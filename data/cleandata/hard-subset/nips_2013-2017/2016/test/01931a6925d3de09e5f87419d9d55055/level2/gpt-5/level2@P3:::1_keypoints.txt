- Proposal: introduce LAND, a learned Riemannian-metric generalization of the multivariate normal pM(x|µ,Σ) ∝ exp(−1/2⟨Logµ(x),Σ−1Logµ(x)⟩) with respect to the metric-induced volume form.  
- Core idea: replace Euclidean metric with a smooth, data-adaptive Riemannian metric to align geodesics with the data manifold.  
- Contribution 1: a locally defined diagonal metric based on kernel-weighted local covariance that encourages geodesics to stay near high-density regions.  
- Contribution 2: a Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant via integration over the tangent space using exponential maps, avoiding repeated Log map BVPs.  
- Contribution 3: MLE procedure for µ and Σ with derived gradients and a mixture extension using an EM algorithm.  
- Contribution 4: empirical demonstrations showing LAND captures non-ellipsoidal structures better than GMMs and improves EEG sleep-stage clustering.  
- Strength: principled Riemannian construction yielding a maximum-entropy model relative to the learned metric.  
- Strength: practical, scalable normalization scheme using tangent-space Monte Carlo and Exp maps.  
- Strength: derived gradients and EM that include per-component normalization constants.  
- Strength: empirical evidence of better modeling of curved densities and improved clustering in EEG data.  
- Strength: honest discussion of limitations (σ choice, computational costs, high-D scaling).  
- Weakness: normalization derivation (Eq.9) may not clearly account for the Jacobian of Expµ; needs clarification or citation.  
- Weakness: diagonal learned metric is not linear-transform invariant and may bias results depending on coordinates.  
- Weakness: missing computational complexity and empirical runtime reporting (costs scale with S, D, N, K; Exp/Log costs).  
- Weakness: lack of sensitivity analyses for σ and Monte Carlo sample size S; no variance-reduction strategies discussed.  
- Weakness: limited baselines — lacks comparisons to other nonlinear density/clustering models (adaptive KDE, t-mixtures, spectral methods).  
- Weakness: mixture EM details deferred to supplement; convergence behavior, initialization robustness, and local optima issues not discussed in main text.  
- Quality: methodology is technically sound overall, but normalization clarity and complexity reporting would improve rigor and reproducibility.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, though Eq.9 and EM derivations need more detail/citations in the main text.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of Riemannian normal to a learned metric with a tractable normalization estimator; tangent-space Monte Carlo is a practical novelty.  
- Significance: advances manifold-aware generative modeling and shows practical utility, but broader comparisons and runtime analysis are needed to assess impact.  
- Essential suggestions: clarify the role of the Exp Jacobian in Eq.9; report computational complexity and empirical runtimes/scaling; include sensitivity analyses for σ and S and discuss variance-reduction.  
- Nice-to-have suggestions: evaluate robustness to linear transformations or add pre-whitening/non-diagonal local metrics; add broader baselines; summarize EM convergence and initialization strategies in main text.  
- Pro-accept arguments: principled, novel model with practical inference and evidence of better modeling of nonlinear structures than GMMs.  
- Con-accept arguments: ambiguous normalization derivation, missing runtime/complexity and sensitivity analyses, and reliance on a coordinate-sensitive diagonal metric.
