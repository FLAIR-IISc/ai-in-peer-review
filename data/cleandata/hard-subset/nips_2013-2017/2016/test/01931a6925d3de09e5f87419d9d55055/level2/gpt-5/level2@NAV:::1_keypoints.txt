- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): a Gaussian with mean/covariance on a data-learned Riemannian metric that favors high-density regions.  
- Contributions: tangent-space Monte Carlo normalizer using Exp maps, gradient-based MLE with stable descent, and an EM algorithm for mixtures.

- Conceptually clean generalization separating parametric density from nonparametric metric.  
- Careful normalization via tangent-space integration and practical choice of Exp maps over Log maps.  
- Well-motivated MLE with explicit gradients and mixture EM accounting for component-dependent normalizers.  
- Empirical gains: avoids intrinsic-mean pathologies and captures non-ellipsoidal structure; improved EEG clustering.

- Learned metric is diagonal and not invariant to linear transforms, risking misspecification for correlated/high-D data.  
- Metric is estimated on the same data as density fitting; no held-out likelihoods or calibration to assess overfitting.  
- Theoretical conditions (cut loci, injectivity radius) for replacing manifold integral by tangent-space integral are not discussed.  
- Monte Carlo normalizer’s finiteness, bias, and estimator variance lack analysis and control variates.  
- Strong sensitivity to σ, which is hand-tuned or grid-searched rather than learned or integrated into training.  
- Computational complexity and scalability not rigorously profiled; costs scale with many Exp/Log map solves.

- Overall quality: sound algorithms but would benefit from variance/bias analyses and variance reduction methods.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; some references malformed and implementation/geodesic solver details missing.  
- Originality: novel combination of learned Riemannian geometry with properly normalized Riemannian Gaussian and EM mixtures.  
- Significance: promising for generative modeling on nonlinear structures and manifold-aware clustering.

- Suggestions: learn σ (and possibly full local metrics) via marginal likelihood or bilevel optimization; consider mixtures of metrics.  
- Suggestions: report held-out log-likelihoods, timing, and comparisons to other flexible densities (GP-LVMs, diffusion distances, flows).  
- Suggestions: add variance-reduction (common random numbers, control variates) and study convergence vs. sample S.  
- Suggestions: discuss geometric conditions (cut loci/injectivity radius) ensuring validity of tangent-space integrals.

- Arguments for acceptance: principled model and algorithms with solid empirical improvements addressing a timely gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical/empirical analysis of estimator variance/consistency and scalability, plus reliance on hand-tuned σ and diagonal metrics.
