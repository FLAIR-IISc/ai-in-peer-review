- Proposal: Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replaces Euclidean Mahalanobis metric with a learned Riemannian metric; ML estimation via gradient descent + Monte Carlo, EM for mixtures, tested on synthetic data and EEG.

- Strength: Well-motivated with strong Riemannian geometry foundation; novelty in learning the manifold metric rather than using a predefined manifold.

- Strength: Monte Carlo integration for normalization is a practical solution; experiments show improvement over standard GMM.

- Weakness: Choice of diagonal covariance (Eq. 7) is arbitrary, limits linear invariance, and tradeoffs/alternatives are not fully explored.

- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity (notably Gaussian kernel width σ) is under-analyzed; lacks guidelines and thorough sensitivity studies.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—computational cost of logarithm maps and lack of detailed complexity analysis for high-dimensional data.

- Weakness: Limited benchmarking against modern manifold learning and probabilistic methods (e.g., UMAP, t-SNE, other density estimators).

- Weakness: Mathematical exposition (especially Algorithm 1 and related derivations) needs clearer intermediate steps and explanations.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination of Riemannian geometry and manifold learning, solid theory, practical MLE algorithm, empirical gains.

- Arguments against acceptance: scalability and computational issues, sensitivity to parameters, limited comparisons, and arbitrary covariance choice.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on added discussion of limitations, complexity analysis, extended sensitivity analysis, and broader comparisons.
