This paper proposes a Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that generalizes the normal distribution to the manifold setting. The LAND is a parametric distribution that depends on a mean and a covariance, and is the maximum entropy distribution under a given metric. The authors develop a maximum likelihood algorithm to infer the distribution parameters, which relies on a combination of gradient descent and Monte Carlo integration. They also extend the LAND to mixture models and provide the corresponding EM algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the Riemannian geometry and the LAND. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the optimization scheme. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the LAND in capturing non-linear structures and outperforming traditional Gaussian mixture models.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting extension of the normal distribution to the manifold setting.
* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the Riemannian geometry and the LAND.
* The mathematical derivations are sound, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the optimization scheme.
* The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the LAND in capturing non-linear structures and outperforming traditional Gaussian mixture models.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with Riemannian geometry and manifold learning, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other manifold learning algorithms, such as Isomap or Laplacian Eigenmaps.
* The computational complexity of the algorithm is not thoroughly discussed, which may be a concern for large-scale datasets.
