- Summary: Proposes LAND, replacing Euclidean metric in a Gaussian with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric derived from local diagonal covariances, and fits parameters via ML using Monte Carlo estimates of the normalization constant and gradients.  
- Summary: Extends LAND to mixtures with an EM algorithm and shows improved clustering vs GMMs on synthetic data and EEG sleep staging.

- Strength: Conceptually elegant unification of Riemannian normal modeling with a data-adaptive learned metric and a conditional maximum-entropy interpretation.  
- Strength: Practical algorithm combining tangent-space Monte Carlo for C(µ,Σ) with gradient-based ML; IVP-based exponential maps scale better in practice.  
- Strength: Empirical evidence that LAND avoids intrinsic-mean-in-low-density pathology and captures non-ellipsoidal structure, improving clustering on reported tasks.  
- Strength: Authors clearly state limitations and outline promising applications.

- Major concern: Change-of-variables in Eq. (9) omits the Jacobian |det dExpµ(v)|; without justification this biases the C(µ,Σ) estimator and invalidates inference.  
- Major concern: Gradient w.r.t. µ (Eq. 12) is not correctly derived given the missing Jacobian and the µ-dependence of Expµ in m(µ,v); optimization direction may be wrong.  
- Major concern: Paper assumes global injectivity of Expµ (D(µ)=Rd); learned metrics may have cut loci, causing double-counting and invalid change-of-variables unless addressed.  
- Major concern: Evaluation metric on synthetic data estimates E_model[−log q_true] rather than the standard test NLL E_true[−log p_model], which can be misleading for density quality.  
- Major concern: Scalability issues: computing m(µ,v) requires IVP solves and O(N) metric sums; no complexity analysis, accelerations, or runtimes are provided, raising practicality concerns.  
- Major concern: Hyperparameter σ is tuned by hand and (in EEG) selected via label-based F-measure, risking optimistic results and unfair tuning.  
- Major concern: Modeling limitation: local covariance is diagonal and metric not affine-invariant, limiting applicability when local anisotropy or linear transforms matter.

- Clarity: Generally well-written with good intuition, but critical derivations (tangent-space integral, gradients) are terse and too reliant on supplements; some references are incomplete/broken.

- Originality: Novel combination of a learned Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal ML estimation; mixture extension is useful though straightforward.

- Significance: Potentially valuable primitive for manifold-aware generative models if measure/gradient issues and computational constraints are resolved; empirical scope and comparisons are currently limited.

- Suggestion: Provide a correct derivation of Eq. (9) including the exponential map Jacobian or explicitly state and quantify any approximation used.  
- Suggestion: Re-derive ∇µ log C including the correct measure, and describe variance-reduction strategies for Monte Carlo gradients.  
- Suggestion: Discuss injectivity radius/cut-locus issues, restrict integration domain or provide practical diagnostics/safeguards.  
- Suggestion: Report computational complexity and wall-clock times, and evaluate accelerations (sparse neighborhoods, caching, mini-batching).  
- Suggestion: Use standard evaluation (test NLL on held-out data) and unsupervised hyperparameter selection (likelihood/AIC/BIC) applied fairly to baselines.  
- Suggestion: Compare to stronger baselines (mixtures of factor analyzers/PPCA, adaptive KDE, graph-based geodesic KDE, probabilistic manifold models).  
- Suggestion: Consider learning full local covariance (or low-rank+diagonal) and explore affine-invariance strategies or sensitivity to linear transforms.

- Minor comments: Fix incomplete/malformed references, clarify µ/Σ initialization and sensitivity, and state the role/effect of regularizer ρ.

- Pro for acceptance: Conceptually appealing, novel, and promising empirical results with a practical algorithmic framework.  
- Con for acceptance: Potentially incorrect/brittle treatment of normalization integral and gradients, nonstandard evaluation, and unresolved scalability/hyperparameter issues.

- Overall: Interesting and promising idea but serious correctness and evaluation concerns must be addressed to validate the approach.
