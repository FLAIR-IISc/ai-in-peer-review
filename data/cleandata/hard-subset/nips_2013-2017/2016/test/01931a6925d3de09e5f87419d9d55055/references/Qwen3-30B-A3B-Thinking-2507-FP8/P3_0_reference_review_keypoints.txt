- Summary: Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean distance with a data-driven Riemannian metric built from local diagonal covariances and uses Monte Carlo to normalize and estimate parameters; demonstrated on synthetic and EEG data.

- Strength 1 (Theoretical Foundation): Strong theoretical grounding extending Riemannian normal distributions to a data-adaptive metric, building on Pennec and Hauberg.

- Strength 2 (Practical Innovation): Clever, practical Monte Carlo integration over tangent space to estimate the intractable normalization constant.

- Strength 3 (Clear Exposition): Well-written, well-organized paper with consistent notation and helpful figures illustrating concepts.

- Strength 4 (Empirical Validation): Experiments on synthetic and EEG sleep-stage data show clear advantages over standard GMM and Riemannian least-squares.

- Strength 5 (Novel Integration): Successfully bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning to produce a useful probabilistic model.

- Weakness 1 (Computational Scalability): Method may not scale to high-dimensional data; Gaussian kernel locality and S=3000 MC samples are computationally expensive and no complexity analysis is provided.

- Weakness 2 (Parameter Selection): Bandwidth σ is critical but lacks a principled selection method; experiments rely on brute-force tuning.

- Weakness 3 (Diagonal Covariance Restriction): Restricting to diagonal covariances for efficiency is a significant limitation and its impact versus full covariances is unexplored.

- Weakness 4 (Limited Comparison): Comparisons are mainly to GMM and Riemannian least-squares; missing comparisons to manifold learning methods (Isomap, LLE, t-SNE).

- Weakness 5 (Theoretical Justification): No thorough theoretical justification for choosing the metric as the inverse of local diagonal covariance.

- Quality Assessment: Technically sound and empirically supported, but needs stronger theoretical justification for metric choice and better high-dimensional scalability analysis.

- Clarity Assessment: Clear and reproducible in exposition overall, but some implementation details (e.g., solving IVPs for the exponential map) need more detail.

- Originality Assessment: Novel idea of a locally adaptive metric for a normal distribution; significant extension beyond prior Riemannian and manifold-learning work.

- Significance Assessment: Potentially impactful probabilistic framework for modeling nonlinear manifolds and extendable to other generative models.

- Specific Comment 1 (Diagonal Covariance): Diagonal covariance constraint should be more thoroughly acknowledged and compared to full covariance alternatives.

- Specific Comment 2 (Parameter Selection): Need a principled approach to selecting σ rather than grid search.

- Specific Comment 3 (High-Dimensional Data): Must address or mitigate scalability issues for NIPS-relevant high-dimensional settings.

- Specific Comment 4 (Normalization Constant): Provide more detail on convergence properties of the Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant.

- Specific Comment 5 (Comparisons): Add comparisons with other manifold learning methods to better position LAND.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request detailed complexity/scalability analysis, experiments comparing diagonal vs full covariances, and additional comparisons with manifold learning methods.

- Pro: Strong theory, novel integration, practical normalization solution, clear empirical gains, and good presentation.

- Con: Scalability concerns in high dimensions, unjustified diagonal covariance restriction, lack of principled σ selection, and limited comparisons to other manifold methods.
