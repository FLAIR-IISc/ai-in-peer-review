- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal distribution using a learned, smoothly varying local (diagonal) covariance metric on R^D and ML/EM estimation for mixtures.
- Key technical components: Monte Carlo estimator of normalization constant by sampling in tangent space, gradient-based ML for µ and Σ, and an EM-like algorithm for mixtures.
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning and learned metrics; novelty is learning a Riemannian geometry from data for generative modeling.
- Strength: principled density on a learned geometry that adapts geodesics to high-density regions.
- Strength: practical algorithmic ideas that avoid repeated boundary-value Log solves by sampling in the tangent space.
- Strength: empirical evidence that LAND avoids intrinsic-mean drift and fits nonlinear structures qualitatively well.
- Strength: promising EEG clustering alignment in fixed-σ experiments versus GMMs.
- Weakness 1: Change-of-variables/volume element appears incorrect—omits Jacobian det(DExpµ(v)) and ignores injectivity/cut-locus issues, affecting C and gradients.
- Weakness 2: Gradient w.r.t. µ seems to ignore differentiation through Expµ(v) (Jacobi fields), so reported µ-gradients are likely biased.
- Weakness 3: Use of diagonal local covariances makes the metric coordinate-dependent/non-invariant to linear transforms and may distort geodesics; trade-offs not analyzed.
- Weakness 4: Scalability concerns—each m evaluation requires solving ODEs per Monte Carlo sample and summing over all N points, with no complexity or runtime analysis.
- Weakness 5: Experimental issues—unusual synthetic NLL metric, label-informed hyperparameter selection for EEG, limited baselines; ablations and proper held-out likelihoods missing.
- Weakness 6: Theoretical gaps—no guarantees on Exp/Log existence, geodesic completeness, identifiability, consistency, or EM monotonicity with approximate C.
- Clarity: paper is readable but derivations for normalization constant/gradients and numerical/ODE details need clarification.
- Originality: concept of Riemannian normal on a learned manifold is novel; diagonal-kernel metric itself is not new but its ML use is a fresh angle.
- Significance: promising if volume/gradient issues and scalability are addressed; current theoretical gaps limit impact.
- Pros for acceptance: interesting direction, implementable algorithms, bridges manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.
- Cons for acceptance: likely incorrect volume/gradient derivations, unaddressed scalability, and weakened experimental methodology.
- Key questions for authors: can you derive/incorporate the correct pullback volume factor and justify approximations?
- Key questions for authors: how do you differentiate C w.r.t. µ without Jacobi fields—can adjoint sensitivity be used?
- Key questions for authors: what are computational costs per iteration and how do they scale with D, N, S?
- Key questions for authors: how sensitive are results to rotations/rescalings of data?
- Recommendation: borderline reject unless volume/gradient derivations are fixed and experiments are made principled (no label-informed tuning).
