- Proposal: Introduces LAND — a Gaussian that uses geodesic distance from a learned Riemannian metric built from local inverse diagonal covariances.
- Normalizer: Approximates the metric-dependent normalization constant via Monte Carlo integration in the tangent space using samples v ~ N(0, Σ).
- Estimation: Derives gradients and uses block coordinate descent with exponential/logarithm maps (solving ODEs) for ML estimation; extends to mixtures with EM.
- Relation to prior work: Connects Riemannian statistics to manifold learning but differs by learning the metric and addressing the intractable normalizer.
- Strength — conceptual: Principled way to make Gaussians locally adaptive while preserving a maximum-entropy characterization.
- Strength — geometric: Sound use of Exp/Log maps, tangent-space change of variables, and explicit dependence of the normalizer on curvature.
- Strength — practical algorithm: Block coordinate descent, EM extension, tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids full manifold integration.
- Strength — empirical: Shows LAND avoids intrinsic-mean pathology and captures non-elliptical clusters better than GMMs on synthetic data.
- Strength — transparency: Clear articulation of limitations and future work.
- Weakness — metric design: Restrictive diagonal local covariances and isotropic kernel make the metric non-invariant and sensitive to feature scaling/linear transforms.
- Weakness — scalability: Requires many Logµ(xn) evaluations (BVP solves) per iteration; no runtime or complexity analysis or engineering optimizations provided.
- Weakness — hyperparameter tuning: σ is tuned per subject using clustering performance, favoring LAND; no unsupervised or cross-validated selection or joint learning of σ.
- Weakness — baselines: Missing comparisons to manifold-aware clustering and graph-based density models; no held-out likelihoods on real data.
- Weakness — Monte Carlo estimator: No analysis of variance, bias, or convergence with sample size S and no explored variance-reduction techniques.
- Quality — correctness: Formulations align with Riemannian theory; tangent-space integration and gradients are plausible though some details deferred to supplements.
- Quality — empirical support: Synthetic results convincing where nonlinearity matters; real-data evidence suggestive but limited and weakened by post-hoc σ tuning.
- Quality — missing details: Lacks complexity/runtime, robustness to σ/ρ, and numerical-stability analysis.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with sufficient geometric background, but reproducibility hampered by missing step-size, stopping criteria, and geodesic solver details.
- Originality: Novel combination of learned metric + Riemannian normal + tangent-space Monte Carlo normalizer + ML/EM estimation.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if computational and invariance issues are addressed; current significance moderate.
- Suggestions: Report runtime/complexity, recommend preprocessing (whitening) and sensitivity analysis, study normalizer variance and reduction techniques, and consider learning σ/ρ.
- Suggestions: Explore approximations or caching for Logµ(x) (shooting with autodiff, cached geodesics, local expansions) to reduce BVP cost.
- Suggestions: Add stronger real-data baselines and held-out likelihood evaluations.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled contribution at the intersection of fields, novel implementable normalizer estimator, solid synthetic demonstrations.
- Cons for acceptance: Unaddressed scalability and efficiency, sensitivity to σ and non-invariant metric design, limited baselines and favorable hyperparameter selection.
- Overall recommendation: Leaning toward weak accept, contingent on better treatment of efficiency, hyperparameter selection, and stronger baselines.
