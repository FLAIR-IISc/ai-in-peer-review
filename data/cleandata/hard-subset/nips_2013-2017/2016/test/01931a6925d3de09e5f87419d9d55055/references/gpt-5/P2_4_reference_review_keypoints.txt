- Proposal: Introduces LAND — a Riemannian normal distribution on a learned manifold induced by a smoothly varying metric M(x) from local diagonal covariances.  
- Metric construction: M(x) is set as the inverse of a locally estimated (diagonal) covariance using Gaussian kernel smoothing.  
- Distribution definition: pM(x|µ, Σ) uses geodesic distances via Log/Exp maps on the learned manifold.  
- Normalization estimator: Proposes a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant C(µ, Σ) using m(µ, v) = sqrt(|M(Expµ(v))|).  
- Parameter optimization: Uses block coordinate descent MLE with derived gradients for µ (preconditioned/steepest descent) and Σ (via Σ−1 = AᵀA).  
- Mixtures: Presents an EM-like algorithm for mixtures of LANDs with per-component normalization constants.  
- Empirics: Shows LAND fits complex synthetic nonlinear shapes with fewer components than GMMs and outperforms GMMs on EEG clustering (F-measure).  
- Relation to prior work: Connects Riemannian statistics (Pennec) with learned-metric manifold learning (Hauberg, Tosi), novelty is normalization on a learned manifold.  
- Conceptual strength: Principled replacement of Euclidean with geodesic distance yields a coherent generative model consistent with Riemannian normal theory.  
- Algorithmic strength: Tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids manifold BVPs; gradients and implementation details are reasonably derived.  
- Practical strength: Provides mixture modeling and EM extension addressing a gap in manifold mixture literature.  
- Empirical strength: Promising results on synthetic and real EEG data indicate captured nonlinear structure.  
- Honesty: Paper candidly discusses limitations (non-invariance, diagonal metric, computation).  
- Critical gap — change-of-variables: The use of m(µ,v)=sqrt(|M(Expµ(v))|) lacks a full derivation/justification for the Jacobian of Exp; needs clarification or quantification of the approximation.  
- Metric invariance concern: Diagonal local covariances make the model non-invariant to linear transforms (rotations/whitening); robustness remedies (prewhitening, shrunk full covariance) are not explored.  
- Bandwidth σ sensitivity: Bandwidth choice is ad hoc and tuned per-subject for EEG; requires principled selection (cross-validation) and sensitivity analysis.  
- Monte Carlo variance and cost: Fixed S=3000 lacks bias/variance analysis; need study of Ĉ and gradient variance vs S and wall-clock scaling (N, D, K).  
- Computational bottleneck: Many Exp evaluations dominate cost; empirical timing and scalability comparisons to GMMs are missing.  
- Optimization details missing: Step sizes, convergence criteria, stopping conditions, and Algorithm 1 completion are under-specified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Experimental issues: Synthetic evaluation metric is ambiguous; should report held-out test log-likelihoods/KL estimates and clearer descriptions.  
- Baselines limited: EEG experiments lack manifold-learning and embedding baselines (Isomap/LLE/KernelPCA + GMM); per-subject variability and statistical tests are not reported.  
- Presentation and reproducibility: Generally well written but notational density and missing solver/optimizer tolerances impede full reproducibility.  
- Originality and significance: Moderate–high originality; moderate significance contingent on addressing computational and hyperparameter sensitivity concerns.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled extension of Gaussians to learned Riemannian geometries, handles normalization, provides MLE/mixtures, and shows empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: Unjustified change-of-variable, σ sensitivity, diagonal-metric invariance limits, limited baselines, missing timing/scaling and optimization details.  
- Key author questions: Provide derivation/justification for tangent-space integral weight, sensitivity to S/σ/initialization, per-iteration computational cost vs GMMs, and whether prewhitening or shrunk full local covariance helps.
