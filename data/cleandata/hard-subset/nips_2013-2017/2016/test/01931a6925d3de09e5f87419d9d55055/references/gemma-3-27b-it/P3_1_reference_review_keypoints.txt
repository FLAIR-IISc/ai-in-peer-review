- Main contribution: Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that learns a Riemannian metric to model data on non-linear manifolds.  
- Theoretical foundation: Rigorous grounding in Riemannian geometry with connections to maximum-entropy distributions.  
- Novelty: Learns the metric directly from data and provides a practical inference scheme for a generative model on manifolds.  
- Monte Carlo integration: Uses tangent-space Monte Carlo integration to approximate the intractable normalization constant efficiently.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear notation, and accessible explanations of geometric concepts.  
- Empirical results: Promising gains over Gaussian Mixture Models on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Performance depends on bandwidth σ; current approach relies on search rather than adaptive selection.  
- Computational cost: Solving geodesic ODEs and exponential maps is expensive; paper lacks detailed complexity and scalability analysis.  
- Limited evaluation: Experiments are relatively narrow (simple synthetics and one EEG dataset); broader datasets and tasks needed.  
- Covariance assumption: Diagonal covariance simplifies computation but may reduce expressiveness; full covariance exploration suggested.  
- Missing error analysis: No thorough quantification of density estimation error relative to ground truth in synthetics.  
- Metric invariance concern: Metric is not invariant to linear transformations—implications and mitigations should be discussed.  
- MC sample choice: Justification for S=3000 samples in Monte Carlo is unclear; sensitivity analysis requested.  
- Optimization detail: Rationale for using steepest descent instead of full gradient for µ updates needs explanation.  
- EEG factor choice: Choice of 5 factors in EEG decomposition appears arbitrary; impact should be analyzed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, including complexity analysis, adaptive σ methods, exploring full covariances, expanded empirical evaluation, and responses to detailed comments.
