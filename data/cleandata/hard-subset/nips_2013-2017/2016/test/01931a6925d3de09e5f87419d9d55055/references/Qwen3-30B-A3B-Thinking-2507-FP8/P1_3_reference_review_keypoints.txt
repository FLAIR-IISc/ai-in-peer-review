- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing Euclidean distance with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric.  
- Learns the metric from data via a local covariance defined with a Gaussian kernel.  
- Defines a Riemannian normal distribution on the learned manifold and extends it to mixture models.  
- Provides theoretical foundations from Riemannian geometry and derives necessary math for the model.  
- Introduces a scalable Monte Carlo integration method to compute the normalization constant.  
- Develops a gradient-based maximum likelihood estimation algorithm for parameter learning.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic data shows LAND captures nonlinear structures better than alternatives.  
- Real-world EEG sleep-stage experiments demonstrate practical utility and improved modeling over GMMs.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-supported, and carefully addresses normalization and estimation challenges.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear notation, helpful figures, and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Novel contribution: first to learn a Riemannian manifold from data for Riemannian normal distributions.  
- Correctly differentiates from prior work that ignored normalization, improving probabilistic consistency.  
- Significant impact potential by extending the fundamental multivariate normal to nonlinear manifolds and enabling downstream methods (e.g., Naive Bayes, LDA, PCA).  
- Strengths: solid theory, clever Monte Carlo normalization, practical algorithm, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: computational bottleneck due to logarithm map (boundary value problem) and limited scalability to high-dimensional data.  
- σ (Gaussian kernel width) selection is somewhat ad-hoc and could affect results.  
- Could include more diverse real-world applications beyond EEG to broaden empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial, well-executed contribution with clear advantages over baseline methods despite acknowledged limitations.
