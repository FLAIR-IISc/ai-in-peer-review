- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal distribution on a learned manifold with metric M(x) built from local diagonal covariances via Gaussian kernels.  
- Density pM(x|µ,Σ) uses Logµ(x) and a normalization constant C(µ,Σ) dependent on manifold geometry; ML estimation done via Monte Carlo integration over the tangent space.  
- Main algorithm: sample v ∼ N(0,Σ) in tangent space, evaluate m(µ,v)=√|M(Expµ(v))| to estimate C(µ,Σ), and compute gradients for µ and Σ (Σ−1 = AᵀA); EM derived for mixtures.  
- Empirically, LAND outperforms GMMs and intrinsic least-squares estimators on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage features.  
- Connects prior Riemannian-normal work (Pennec) with learned metrics (Hauberg et al.) and differs from graph-geodesic and known-manifold mixture approaches by learning the metric and accounting for normalization constants.  
- Positive: ML objective and gradients are derived; tangent-space Monte Carlo avoids repeated Log-map BVPs; block-coordinate scheme is practical and captures non-elliptical clusters.  
- Major technical concern: change-of-variables for C(µ,Σ) seems to omit det(d Expµ(v)), likely biasing normalization and gradients unless justified or corrected.  
- The omitted Jacobian is only equal to 1 at v=0 (to second order), so the paper must justify omission, prove it for their metric class, or include det(d Expµ(v)).  
- Additional technical limits: metric restricted to diagonal entries and not invariant under linear transforms; kernel bandwidth σ chosen manually.  
- Missing analyses: estimator variance for C(µ,Σ), convergence behavior, computational complexity/runtime, and numerical stability of ODE solvers and step-size selection.  
- Clarity is generally good, with helpful exposition and an algorithm box, but the measure transformation is oversimplified and needs conditions/Jacobian discussion.  
- Implementation details are insufficient: ODE solver/tolerances, choice S=3000, sample reuse across iterations, step sizes, and stopping criteria should be specified or moved from supplement.  
- Limitations are acknowledged but practical implications (non-invariance, diagonal metric) should be emphasized more.  
- Originality: novel combination of normalized Riemannian densities with learned metrics and tangent-space MC normalization; the metric learning itself is simple but the overall idea is fresh.  
- Significance: could be a useful replacement for Gaussians on curved data, with promising demos, but tempered by scalability concerns and limited empirical breadth.  
- Pros: principled density on learned manifolds, practical tangent-space Monte Carlo idea, derived ML gradients and EM, and strong empirical advantages on curved data.  
- Cons: probable omission of Jacobian in normalization, heavily engineered diagonal metric with manual tuning, limited empirical evaluation, and unclear scalability to high D and large N.  
- Key questions for authors: justify Eq. (9) and Jacobian omission; state whether MC samples are reused or variance reduction used; provide wall-clock times and scaling with D, N, S; report sensitivity to σ and diagonal restriction and potential for low-rank/full covariances; detail numerical stability choices for geodesic IVPs and optimization.  
- Minor comments: suggest discussing whitening to mitigate non-invariance, clarify initialization and EM responsibility computation, and fix typographical/notation inconsistencies (bold vectors, inner products, define Z).  
- Recommendation: borderline reject due to unresolved measure-theoretic issue in normalization and limited empirical validation; could be strong if Jacobian issue and computational robustness are addressed.
