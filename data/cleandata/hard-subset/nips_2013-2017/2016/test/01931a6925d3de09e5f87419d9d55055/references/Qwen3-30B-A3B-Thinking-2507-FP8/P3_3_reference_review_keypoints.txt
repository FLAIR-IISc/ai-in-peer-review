- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND), replacing Euclidean/Mahalanobis distance with a data-learned Riemannian distance; contributions: metric construction, Monte Carlo normalization, and gradient ML estimation.  
- Builds on Riemannian geometry and extends prior Riemannian normal work (Pennec) to a data-adaptive setting.  
- Demonstrates LAND better captures nonlinear manifold structure than standard Gaussian/GMM in synthetic experiments.  
- Introduces a practical Monte Carlo integration via tangent-space transformation to compute the normalization constant, avoiding expensive boundary-value problems.  
- Empirical results show LAND outperforms GMM and least-squares estimators on synthetic and sleep EEG data.  
- Provides a valuable link between manifold learning and generative modeling, offering a continuous generative model on learned manifolds.  
- Major weakness: metric uses only diagonal local covariances, severely limiting ability to model anisotropic structures and not adequately justified.  
- Scalability concern: Gaussian-kernel-based metric construction is problematic in high dimensions (curse of dimensionality) with no convincing alternatives discussed.  
- Limited experimental comparisons: only against GMM and least-squares, omitting recent manifold-based generative approaches.  
- Hyperparameter issues: kernel bandwidth σ chosen arbitrarily in EEG experiments, lacking a principled selection method or robustness analysis.  
- Robustness concerns: paper doesn’t address handling of varying data density or outliers.  
- Practical limitations (diagonal assumption + scalability) substantially weaken real-world applicability despite theoretical interest.  
- Recommended improvements: experiments with full covariances, detailed complexity/scalability analysis, comparisons to recent methods, and principled σ selection.  
- Overall recommendation: reject with major revision—interesting theoretical contribution but requires significant work to address practical limitations.  
- Final note: conceptually valuable for Riemannian statistics/manifold learning intersection, but needs fixes to be a strong ML contribution.
