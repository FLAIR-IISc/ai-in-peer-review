- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in a multivariate normal with a data-driven Riemannian metric learned from local diagonal covariances and kernel smoothing.  
- Defines Riemannian normal pM(x|µ,Σ) using Logµ and includes the induced volume element; approximates normalization constant C(µ,Σ) by MC in the tangent space using v ∼ N(0,Σ) and weights m(µ,v)=√|M(Expµ(v))|.  
- ML estimation via gradient-based block coordinate descent for µ and Σ (Σ−1 factorized as AᵀA); extends to mixtures with an EM algorithm.  
- Empirically improves fit and clustering on synthetic curved clusters and EEG sleep-stage features compared to GMMs and Riemannian least-squares.

- Connects manifold learning and Riemannian statistics to yield a generative model adapting to nonlinear structure without embedding.  
- Practical estimation scheme: integrates on tangent space and uses IVPs for Exp maps to avoid BVPs.  
- Well-motivated gradient updates: µ update matches empirical tangent means to model moments; Σ update stabilized via factorization.  
- Natural and useful mixture extension.  
- Experiments demonstrate ability to capture non-elliptical clusters and improve EEG clustering.

- Major theoretical concern: normalization constant derivation appears to omit the Jacobian |det d Expµ(v)|; change-of-variable should include this volume density.  
- Global integration over TµM may double-count beyond injectivity radius; assumption D(µ)=RD needs justification or bounds on mass outside injectivity radius.  
- Metric is diagonal and kernel-smoothed, not invariant to linear transforms, so geodesics are sensitive to scaling/rotation and may miss local correlations.  
- σ selection is unclear and reporting best result over σ risks optimistic bias; needs principled selection or joint optimization.  
- Computational cost unclear: complexity scales with S IVP solves per gradient and per component; no runtime comparison vs GMMs or ablation on S.  
- Experimental clarity issues: ambiguous evaluation protocol (which expectation used), missing figures/tables, and incomplete reporting of implementation details.

- Approach is technically sound and gradients plausible, but normalization omission is a substantive theoretical issue needing resolution or justification.  
- Experimental evidence promising but limited in scope and lacking computational and evaluation detail.

- Generally clear and organized; some typos/formatting and missing figure/table details.  
- Recommend adding complexity discussion and explicit algorithmic details for ODE solvers and step-size selection.

- Novelty: combining learned Riemannian metric with ML estimation of Riemannian normal and practical MC normalization is new relative to prior work assuming known manifolds.  
- Mixture extension practical but not conceptually novel.

- Significance: conceptually significant for bringing Gaussian-based generative tools to learned manifolds; practical promise for low- to moderate-D but scalability concerns remain.

- Key suggestions: clarify role of |det d Expµ(v)| or quantify approximation bias; discuss injectivity radius/cut locus and heuristics to restrict integration; provide runtime comparisons and sensitivity to S; consider learning or cross-validating σ and testing preprocessing (e.g., whitening) or richer local metric classes.

- Pros for acceptance: novel principled link between manifold learning and Riemannian generative modeling; clear algorithm with encouraging results; useful building block for manifold-aware models.  
- Cons for acceptance: possibly incorrect/incomplete normalization due to missing Jacobian; limited analysis of computational cost, scalability, and hyperparameter sensitivity; some evaluation and clarity issues.

- Recommendation: borderline accept if authors clarify/correct normalization (or justify approximation) and add computational analysis; otherwise borderline reject.
