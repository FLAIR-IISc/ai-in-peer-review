- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in Gaussian density with a learned Riemannian metric, use geodesics to define a Riemannian normal and MC-estimate normalization.  
- Conceptual novelty: bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning by learning the metric to build a principled generative density.  
- Methodological clarity: pipeline (metric → geodesics → Riemannian normal → MC normalization → gradients) is well motivated and computationally sensible.  
- Practical relevance: can fit non-ellipsoidal clusters and avoid placing means in low-density regions; natural mixture extension.  
- Empirical evidence: synthetic examples and EEG experiment show local adaptation and potential real-data benefits.  
- Key technical concern 1 — normalization: change of variables omits Jacobian |det d(Expµ)v|; ignoring it biases C(µ,Σ) unless justified (flat manifold or small-v).  
- Key technical concern 2 — µ-gradient: derivation replacing ∇µ log C by (Z/C)E[m(µ,v)v] is not justified; m depends on µ via Exp and M and requires ∂Exp/∂µ and ∂M/∂x.  
- Key technical concern 3 — manifold assumptions: assumes global Exp/Log and unique geodesics on learned metric; needs conditions on σ, ρ, M to guarantee solvability and single-valued maps.  
- Key technical concern 4 — metric design and invariance: diagonal metric is not rotation/affine invariant and may fail with correlated features; need discussion or comparison to full/low-rank metrics.  
- Key technical concern 5 — evaluation protocol: reported metric estimates KL(pθ||ptrue) via sampling from models, not held-out test log-likelihood; recommend standard held-out likelihood comparisons.  
- Key technical concern 6 — computational cost and scaling: MC normalization requires many exponential map ODE solves (S≈3000), potentially expensive; need runtimes, sensitivity to S, and variance-reduction.  
- Key technical concern 7 — hyperparameter selection: σ is critical; EEG tuning raises overfitting concerns—recommend CV, marginal likelihood, or joint learning of σ.  
- Key technical concern 8 — missing details: EM updates, stepsizes, stopping criteria, ODE tolerances, and sensitivity to ρ are sparsely described.  
- Quality assessment: core idea is sound and promising but significant technical gaps in normalization and µ-gradient could affect correctness.  
- Clarity: generally well written; key math steps (Eqs. 9–13) need clearer derivations and stated assumptions.  
- Originality: novel combination of learning a Riemannian metric and ML estimation with MC normalization in generative modeling.  
- Significance: if technical issues are resolved, LAND could be a useful method for adapting Gaussian models to nonlinear structure.  
- Main actionable suggestions: correct or justify normalization Jacobian, rigorously derive Eq. (12) or state approximations, report held-out likelihoods and runtimes, include MC variance and sensitivity to S.  
- Additional suggestions: discuss preprocessing (scaling/whitening), ablate diagonal vs full/low-rank metrics, explore automated σ selection, and clarify geodesic uniqueness conditions and numerical handling of violations.  
- Pros: elegant idea, practical ML estimator, clear empirical advantages on nonlinear clusters and a real dataset.  
- Cons: potential incorrect treatment of volume element and µ-gradient, heavy computational burden, and insufficiently rigorous evaluation and hyperparameter selection.
