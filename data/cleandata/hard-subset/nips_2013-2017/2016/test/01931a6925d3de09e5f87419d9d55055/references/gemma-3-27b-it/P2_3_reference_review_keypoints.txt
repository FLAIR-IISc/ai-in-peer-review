- Summary: Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) using a learned Riemannian metric to adapt Gaussian models to nonlinear manifolds, with ML estimation, Monte Carlo normalization, and an EM for mixtures; evaluated on synthetic and EEG data.  
- Relationship to prior work: Builds on Riemannian normal distributions (Pennec) and learned metrics (Hauberg et al.), positioning itself between manifold learning and statistics and highlighting continuous metric advantages over methods like Isomap.  
- Novelty: Integrates a learned Riemannian metric directly into a parametric Gaussian-like distribution, a novel contribution.  
- Theoretical foundation: Strong grounding in Riemannian geometry and statistical results.  
- Practical algorithm: Provides scalable Monte Carlo normalization and gradient-based optimization to handle practical challenges.  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrates improved performance over standard GMMs on synthetic and EEG datasets; includes a σ bandwidth ablation for EEG.  
- Clear writing: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and detailed enough for informed readers.  
- Computational cost: Solving ODEs for geodesics (exp/log maps) is computationally heavy and a potential bottleneck, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Metric invariance: Learned metric is not invariant to linear transformations, which may limit applicability; impact and remedies are insufficiently explored.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Performance depends on kernel bandwidth σ; more comprehensive tuning strategies across datasets are needed.  
- Limited comparisons: Lacks comparisons to other modern nonlinear generative models (e.g., VAEs, normalizing flows).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, practical algorithms, promising empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Computational cost concerns, non-invariance to linear transforms, and limited comparison to advanced generative models.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with solid theory, practical methods, and promising results despite some limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested minor revisions: Expand discussion of computational costs and mitigations, detail impact and remedies for lack of metric invariance, and add brief comparisons to other nonlinear generative models.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
