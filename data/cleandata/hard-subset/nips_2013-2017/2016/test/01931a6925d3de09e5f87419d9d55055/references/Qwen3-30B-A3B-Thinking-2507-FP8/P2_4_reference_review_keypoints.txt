- Proposal: Introduces LAND—a Riemannian normal distribution using a locally adaptive metric from local covariances (Gaussian kernel), with Monte Carlo normalization, gradient MLE, and EM for mixtures; evaluated on synthetic data and EEG.

- Technical soundness: Mathematically rigorous metric construction (Eq.7), clever Monte Carlo normalization (Eq.10) avoiding BVPs, and practical gradient-based optimization (Algorithm 1); empirically outperforms GMM on nonlinear structure and EEG.

- Clarity and organization: Exceptionally well-written with a concise Riemannian geometry review, clear derivations, and thorough experimental description.

- Significance: Provides a principled framework for generative modeling on nonlinear manifolds with potential to extend many standard models and demonstrated real-world utility.

- Theoretical depth (weakness): Lacks analysis of estimator properties (consistency, convergence rates), limited discussion of metric choice scalability to high dimensions, and no theoretical treatment of kernel bandwidth σ.

- Comparative analysis (weakness): Baselines are limited—missing comparisons to manifold learning methods (e.g., Isomap, Laplacian Eigenmaps) and alternative EEG feature pipelines.

- Originality (weakness): Does not sufficiently distinguish from related work like Simo-Serra et al.'s Geodesic Finite Mixture Models and omits comparison to other probabilistic manifold methods (Hauberg 2013; Zhang & Fletcher 2013).

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance at NIPS—solid, practical contribution with strong experiments despite theoretical and comparative gaps.

- Suggested improvements: add theoretical analysis of estimator consistency/convergence; expand experiments to include other manifold learning baselines; clarify distinction from Simo-Serra et al.; discuss extensions for high-dimensional data (e.g., dimensionality reduction first).
