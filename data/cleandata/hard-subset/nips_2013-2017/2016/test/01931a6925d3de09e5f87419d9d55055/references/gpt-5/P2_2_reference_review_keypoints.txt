- Summary: Proposes LAND — a Riemannian normal on a learned locally-weighted diagonal metric, with Monte Carlo estimation of the intractable normalizer and ML/EM learning for mixtures; shows synthetic and EEG improvements over GMM/LS.

- Key technical concern: The normalization derivation likely omits the Jacobian |det DExpµ(v)| when changing variables x = Expµ(v), which could bias the normalizer and gradients.

- Gradient derivations: The µ-gradient result is unclear and depends on exchange of derivatives and the possibly-missing Jacobian; full, verifiable derivations are needed.

- Computational cost: Estimating C requires many exponential map ODE solves per eval and M(x) needs sums over all data; no complexity, timing, or scalability analysis provided.

- Experiments: Synthetic results are convincing; EEG real-data test shows gains but evaluation is limited (one dataset, 10 subjects, no significance tests, few baselines).

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Model depends strongly on σ (chosen per subject by grid search), raising concerns about optimistic tuning and lack of learning for σ.

- Clarity: Paper is readable but defers crucial measure-change and gradient derivations to the supplement; key math (volume form under Exp) should be in the main text.

- Metric limitations: Metric is diagonal and not affine-invariant; paper should state conditions for geodesic completeness and numerical stability.

- Originality: Novel integration of Riemannian normal with learned metrics and practical tangent-space MC normalization and ML estimation, though metric choice follows prior work.

- Significance: Potentially impactful if technically correct, enabling geometry-aware components, but practical utility hinges on resolving normalization, scalability, and hyperparameter issues.

- Pros for acceptance: Principled bridging of Riemannian statistics and learned geometry, ML treatment with normalization, and promising synthetic/real results.

- Cons for acceptance: Potentially incorrect measure-change, limited empirical scope and baselines, heavy computation without accelerations, and sensitivity to σ with restricted diagonal metric.

- Suggested fixes: Explicitly derive the pullback of the Riemannian volume under Exp (include |det DExp|), correct gradients, add complexity/timing/ablation studies (S, σ, ODE settings), consider neighbor truncation/caching, strengthen baselines and statistical tests, and explore learning σ or using off-diagonal local covariances.

- Overall recommendation: Promising idea but currently on the fence (leaning negative) pending resolution of the normalization/gradient correctness and stronger experimental validation.
