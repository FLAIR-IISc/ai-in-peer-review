- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in multivariate normal with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric to model densities on Rd.  
- Metric constructed from diagonal, locally weighted covariance via Gaussian kernels so geodesics favor high-density regions.  
- Defines Riemannian normal pM(x|μ, Σ) ∝ exp(−1/2⟨Logμ(x), Σ−1 Logμ(x)⟩) and develops practical estimation methods.  
- Introduces an MC estimator for the normalization constant by integrating over the tangent space using the exponential map and volume element m(μ, v).  
- Derives gradient-based MLE for μ and Σ with reparameterization Σ−1 = AᵀA and an EM-style algorithm for LAND mixtures.  
- Experiments on synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage data show improved fit and clustering over GMMs and intrinsic least squares.  
- Builds on prior Riemannian statistics (Pennec, Karcher) and prior probabilistic geometries (Hauberg, Tosi) but novelly learns the metric and estimates normalizers.  
- Provides a generative, continuous-metric alternative to graph-based manifold methods like Isomap/LLE.  
- Theory and algorithms are soundly grounded in Riemannian geometry; change-of-variables and induced measure use are appropriate.  
- Gradients for μ and A align with intrinsic gradient expressions; using steepest descent to mitigate Σ conditioning is sensible.  
- MC normalization C(μ, Σ) ≈ Z·E[m(μ, v)] is simple and scalable since it uses exponential maps only, but Logμ(xn) remains a computational bottleneck.  
- Paper lacks complexity/runtime and numerical stability analysis for Log and Exp map computations.  
- No variance analysis or convergence diagnostics for the MC estimator; choice S = 3000 is empirical and unexplained.  
- EM for mixtures uses numerically approximated normalizers; monotonic likelihood increase is not guaranteed under MC noise and stability evidence is missing.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly show LAND adapts to curvature and outperforms GMMs with fewer components.  
- EEG results show improved clustering (F-measure) when σ is tuned, but baselines are limited and computational costs and uncertainties are not reported.  
- Paper is well written with concise Riemannian background; supplement contains key derivations but main text could include EM sketch and complexity discussion.  
- Metric choice (diagonal local covariance) and non-invariance are acknowledged, but guidance on preprocessing (e.g., scaling) is missing.  
- The combination of Riemannian normal on a learned manifold and tangent-space MC normalization is novel and fills a gap between manifold learning and generative modeling.  
- If scalable, LAND could replace Gaussians in models for data on curved supports; impact depends on addressing computational and hyperparameter issues.  
- Strengths: bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, principled normalization for ML and mixtures, simple MC scheme using Exp maps, and empirical gains over GMMs.  
- Weaknesses: scalability questions (N log maps + S exp maps per update) and missing runtime/complexity metrics.  
- Weaknesses: lack of MC variance/bias analysis and no variance-reduction strategies discussed.  
- Weaknesses: sensitivity to σ lacks principled selection; suggestion to use CV or learn bandwidths.  
- Weaknesses: diagonal metric is restrictive and not invariant; suggest local PCA/anisotropic metrics or input standardization.  
- Weaknesses: limited baselines—should compare to adaptive KDEs, mixtures with local covariance, and embedding-based density estimators.  
- Weaknesses: EM monotonicity under MC noise not addressed; recommend practical safeguards (increase S, common random numbers).  
- Weaknesses: missing citation/justification for the volume element in exponential coordinates (Eq. 9).  
- Questions for authors: report typical runtimes and scaling with N, D, K, S, and practical D limits for geodesic ODEs.  
- Questions: how are Log maps computed (shooting vs. BVP solvers) and what heuristics/approximations are used?  
- Questions: sensitivity of results to S and whether an adaptive S scheme was tried.  
- Questions: can you report held-out log-likelihoods for EEG beyond F-measure?  
- Questions: did you try anisotropic local covariance (low-rank or shrunk full covariance) for M(x)?  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—conceptually clean and promising but needs more scalability, variance analysis, stronger baselines, and hyperparameter treatment.
