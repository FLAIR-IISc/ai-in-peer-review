- Overall: Proposes a novel, mathematically grounded locally adaptive normal distribution (LAND) by learning a Riemannian metric; reviewer gives a strong positive assessment.  
- Core idea: Replace Euclidean distance with a learned Riemannian distance to model data on nonlinear manifolds.  
- Technical contributions: construction of the metric, scalable Monte Carlo normalization via tangent-space integration, gradient-based parameter estimation, and an EM algorithm for mixtures.  
- Experimental scope: Evaluated on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage data, with LAND outperforming standard GMM in presented cases.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry and linked to maximum entropy principles.  
- Strength: Novel approach that bridges Riemannian geometry and manifold learning and provides a practical estimation scheme.  
- Strength: Scalable Monte Carlo integration is a key computational innovation.  
- Strength: Clear writing and logical organization.  
- Weakness: Using a diagonal covariance for the learned metric is restrictive and lacks invariance to linear transformations.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to the Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ; more thorough sensitivity analysis and adaptive selection needed.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental validation and lack of comparisons to modern generative models (e.g., VAEs, normalizing flows).  
- Weakness: Potential gradient instability due to covariance condition number; optimization strategies need exploration.  
- Weakness: Computational cost concerns from solving ODEs for geodesics and exponential map evaluations, especially in high dimensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretical novelty, bridging fields, Monte Carlo solution to normalization, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Metric expressiveness limits, limited experiments, parameter sensitivity, and optimization stability concerns.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestions to address metric choice and parameter sensitivity in revision.  
- Requested revisions: expand discussion on metric alternatives, perform thorough σ sensitivity/adaptive selection, compare to VAEs/flows, investigate optimization/regularization to stabilize gradients, detail computational complexity, and enrich supplementary implementation details.
