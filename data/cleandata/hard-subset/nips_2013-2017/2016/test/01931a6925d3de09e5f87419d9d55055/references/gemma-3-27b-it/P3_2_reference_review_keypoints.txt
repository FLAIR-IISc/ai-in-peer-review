- Overall assessment: novel, well-motivated approach bridging manifold learning and Riemannian statistics; weak accept with request for major revisions.  
- Problem tackled: adapt a standard normal to data on a nonlinear manifold via a learned Riemannian metric and Riemannian normal distribution.  
- Theoretical grounding: solid Riemannian geometry foundation and connection to maximum-entropy principles.  
- Novelty: principled and original combination of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics for density estimation.  
- Mathematical rigor: clear derivations and clever Monte Carlo normalization method.  
- Empirical promise: outperforms standard GMMs on synthetic and EEG sleep-staging data.  
- Exposition: generally well-written and clearly motivated.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance likely depends strongly on kernel bandwidth (σ) and regularizer (ρ); lacks systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational complexity: insufficient discussion—geodesic ODE solves and exp/log maps may be expensive; scaling with D and N is unclear.  
- Limited baselines: missing comparisons to other manifold-based density estimators (e.g., KDE on learned manifolds, Isomap/LLE-based methods).  
- Optimization details: unclear choice and sensitivity of stepsizes (αµ, αA) in Algorithm 1.  
- Overstated claims: assertion that LAND “always” avoids low-density regions is too strong and should be softened.  
- Specific methodological queries: need justification for diagonal covariance choice in the metric tensor and its tradeoffs.  
- Experimental details requested: report standard deviations, learning curves, and more info on EEG feature extraction.  
- Related work suggestion: cite and compare to kernel density estimation techniques applied after manifold learning.
