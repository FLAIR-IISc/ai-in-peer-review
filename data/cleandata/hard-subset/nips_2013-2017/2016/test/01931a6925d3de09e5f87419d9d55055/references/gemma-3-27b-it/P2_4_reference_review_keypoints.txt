- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean distance with a learned Riemannian metric to model data near nonlinear manifolds.  
- Introduces a scalable Monte Carlo integration scheme to normalize the density and a gradient-based MLE algorithm, with an extension to mixtures of LANDs.  
- Builds on Riemannian statistics and manifold learning, differing by learning the metric and offering a fully probabilistic generative model.  
- Novelty: a principled, theoretically sound extension of the normal distribution to non-linear data structures.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry and linked to maximum entropy principles.  
- Practical contributions: Monte Carlo normalization, gradient optimization, and an EM algorithm for mixtures.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification show promising results and outperform GMM in some cases.  
- Writing is clear and accessible, though background in Riemannian geometry helps.  
- Computational complexity: solving ODEs for geodesics and evaluating exp/log maps is costly, especially in high dimensions; diagonal covariance may limit expressiveness.  
- Parameter sensitivity: model performance depends on kernel bandwidth σ and lacks a systematic selection procedure.  
- Scalability discussion is limited and offers no concrete remedies for high-dimensional settings.  
- Baseline comparisons are weak: needs evaluation against modern manifold-aware generative models (e.g., VAEs, GANs with manifold regularization).  
- Ablation study is missing, making it hard to isolate the impact of model components (metric vs. standard normal).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-founded approach with practical methods and promising empirical evidence bridging theory and manifold learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about computational cost, σ sensitivity, and insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand scalability discussion, provide automated σ selection, include broader baseline comparisons, and add an ablation study.
