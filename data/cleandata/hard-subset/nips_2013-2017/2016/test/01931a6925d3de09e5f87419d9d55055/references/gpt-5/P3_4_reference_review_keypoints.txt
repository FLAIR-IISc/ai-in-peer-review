- Proposes LAND: a Riemannian normal on a learned data-adaptive manifold with a kernel-smoothed diagonal precision metric.  
- Approximates normalization constant by Monte Carlo in the tangent space and derives gradients for ML and an EM-like mixture algorithm.  
- Novelty: uses a learned metric as base geometry for a normalized generative model, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Strength: conceptually appealing—distance follows data geometry and avoids straight-line Gaussian bias.  
- Strength: methodologically nontrivial—differentiating through Exp/Log with Monte Carlo is interesting and useful.  
- Strength: normalization constant acts like regularization, penalizing means in low-density regions and large covariances.  
- Strength: mixture extension properly accounts for per-component normalization constants.  
- Empirical: shows good qualitative fits on synthetic curved data and improved clustering alignment on EEG features.  
- Major theoretical concern: tangent-space normalization appears to omit the |det dExpµ(v)| Jacobian factor, biasing C(µ,Σ) and its gradients.  
- Metric design is ad hoc and fragile: diagonal, kernel-weighted local covariance is not rotation-invariant and ignores local cross-correlations.  
- Computational scalability unclear: each iteration needs many ODE solves (Log/Exp) and metric evaluations, with S≈3000 MC samples per step being potentially prohibitive.  
- Gradient correctness/stability concerns: derivations rely on gradient identities that may not hold on learned geometries; details deferred to supplement.  
- Experimental scope limited: comparisons only to GMMs and LS-based estimators; stronger baselines and principled validation (especially for EEG σ tuning) are missing.  
- Reproducibility issues: heavy dependence on ODE solvers and hyperparameters with missing timing/robustness analyses.  
- Quality: technically ambitious but correctness hinges on the missing Jacobian and optimization is nonconvex and expensive.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written but crucial derivations (normalization change of variables, gradients) and computational details are relegated to the supplement.  
- Originality: combining a learned metric with a normalized Riemannian normal and ML/EM procedures is novel; metric choice itself is not new.  
- Significance: potentially useful if normalization and scalability are fixed, but concerns currently temper impact.  
- Key questions for authors: clarify Eq.(9)/Jacobian, Log solver details and costs, sensitivity to σ/ρ/S and whether σ can be learned, rationale for diagonal covariance vs local PCA/aniso, and EEG evaluation protocol.  
- Minor comments: avoid inverting AᵀA in Algorithm 1, discuss numerical stability of det M and m(µ,v), and fix incomplete references.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong idea, inclusion of normalization in mixtures, and promising empirical visualizations.  
- Cons for acceptance: potentially incorrect normalization, limited baselines/analysis, scalability concerns, and heavy dependence on hyperparameters.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending corrected derivation/justified approximation and stronger empirical validation (confidence: high).
