- Summary: Proposes LAND — a Riemannian-normal generative model using a locally adaptive metric from smoothed local covariances, with MLE via Monte Carlo normalization and applications on synthetic and EEG data.

Strengths
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in Riemannian geometry and statistics, linking to prior work and maximum-entropy properties.
- Novel integration of manifold learning and Riemannian statistics via a data-adaptive metric construction (Equation 7).
- Practical algorithmic contributions: Monte Carlo scheme for the normalization constant and a gradient-based optimization (Algorithm 1).
- Clear experimental validation showing improved modeling of nonlinear structures on synthetic data and EEG classification versus GMM and least-squares estimators.
- Authors explicitly discuss limitations and potential extensions (e.g., scalability) in the paper.

Weaknesses
- No theoretical justification that the LAND mean "generally avoids low density regions"; evidence is limited to one synthetic example.
- Scalability concerns are acknowledged but not quantified; computational cost of exponential/logarithm maps and scaling with dimension/dataset size is not analyzed.
- Insufficient comparison to related Riemannian and metric-learning work (e.g., [9, 19, 22]); novelty vs prior adaptive-metric approaches is not clearly distinguished.
- Critical parameter σ (kernel bandwidth) selection is ad-hoc (fixed or manually tuned) with no principled selection method described.
- Limited empirical validation: EEG experiments use only 10 subjects and 5D features and lack comparisons to more advanced baselines (e.g., VAEs, GANs, t-SNE/UMAP + GMM).
- Monte Carlo normalization lacks error bounds or convergence analysis; choice of S=3000 is arbitrary and unjustified.

Specific methodology comments
- Metric construction uses diagonal covariance simplification without discussing the impact on datasets with correlated manifold structure.
- Normalization constant approximation (Equation 10) error and its effect on likelihood optimization are not analyzed.
- Optimization uses steepest descent due to instability but lacks convergence guarantees or empirical convergence evidence across datasets.

Specific experiment comments
- Synthetic experiments are illustrative but lack statistical significance testing (e.g., paired tests on negative log-likelihoods).
- EEG results may confound model performance with feature extraction (NMF to 5 factors); no ablation study on feature processing.
- Baselines are limited; should include manifold-preprocessing pipelines (Isomap, Laplacian eigenmaps) and stronger generative baselines.

Overall assessment
- Interesting, theoretically sound extension with promising results but insufficient theoretical and empirical support for key claims and scalability.

Recommendation
- Weak Accept (borderline).

Specific recommendations for revision
- Provide theoretical analysis for why the LAND mean avoids low-density regions and compare to intrinsic least-squares mean.
- Include complexity analysis and empirical scalability results on larger/higher-dimensional datasets.
- Introduce a principled method for selecting σ (e.g., cross-validation or Bayesian optimization) and report sensitivity.
- Expand experiments to more challenging datasets, stronger baselines (VAEs, GANs), and include ablation studies on feature extraction.
- Clarify and compare more thoroughly with related work ([9,19,20,22]) to highlight fundamental differences.
- Provide error analysis and convergence discussion for the Monte Carlo normalization and sensitivity to sample size S.
