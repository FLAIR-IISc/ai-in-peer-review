- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean distance with a data-driven Riemannian distance.  
- Builds the Riemannian metric from data using local covariance with a Gaussian kernel and provides Monte Carlo normalization, gradient MLE, and EM for mixtures.  
- Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning by learning the metric from data rather than assuming it known.  
- Mathematical derivations are correct, well-explained, and properly referenced.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage classification show clear advantages over standard GMMs and least-squares estimators.  
- Provides a complete practical framework (metric construction, normalization estimation, parameter inference, mixture modeling).  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical framework and thorough experimental validation.  
- Weakness: scalability to high-dimensional data is not adequately addressed.  
- Weakness: bandwidth parameter σ requires manual tuning and is not automatically selected.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear exposition, helpful figures, and reproducible algorithm pseudocode.  
- Could better explain computational cost of the logarithm map evaluation and potential optimizations in the main text.  
- Novelty: first to learn a Riemannian metric from data to define a normal distribution; LAND is max-entropy for fixed mean and covariance.  
- Limitation: insufficient comparison to other manifold-based generative models (e.g., GANs on manifolds).  
- Significance: extends the fundamental normal distribution to nonlinear manifolds with potential for broad impact and numerous extensions.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: strong, well-presented contribution—recommend accept despite scalability and σ-tuning concerns.
