- Summary: Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) that replaces Euclidean distance with geodesic distance from a learned Riemannian metric, plus Monte Carlo normalization, gradient MLE, and an EM for mixtures; shows gains over GMMs on synthetic and EEG data.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Riemannian geometry, manifold learning (Isomap), learned metrics (Hauberg et al.), and Riemannian statistics (Pennec), bridging parametric models and non-parametric manifolds.

- Novelty: Combines Riemannian geometry with manifold learning in a parametric density framework, adapting the Riemannian normal to a learned manifold.

- Theoretical foundation: Grounded in solid mathematical principles and maximum-entropy reasoning.

- Practical algorithm: Provides a scalable Monte Carlo integration for normalization and gradient-based parameter estimation.

- Empirical validation: Demonstrates improved modeling of nonlinear structure versus GMMs on synthetic data and EEG sleep-stage measurements.

- Presentation: Generally well-written with clear explanation of math and algorithms.

- Computational complexity: Computing geodesics via ODEs (exp/log maps) is potentially expensive and may not scale well to high dimensions.

- Monte Carlo sensitivity: Scalability concerns remain and performance depends on choice of Monte Carlo sample size S.

- Bandwidth sensitivity: Performance is sensitive to the Gaussian kernel bandwidth σ and lacks a thorough tuning/robustness analysis.

- Metric invariance: The learned metric is not invariant to linear transformations, which may limit applicability and should be discussed.

- Limited baselines: Comparisons are mainly to GMMs and least-squares LAND; missing evaluations against state-of-the-art nonlinear density estimators (e.g., normalizing flows, GANs).

- Overstated claims: Some assertions (e.g., learning speed) are stronger than warranted and should be qualified.

- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound and complete for a conference contribution, though some explanations could be made more intuitive.

- Originality and significance: Original approach with potential significance, especially for applications like EEG analysis and extensions of normal-based models.

- Pro acceptance: Novel, theoretically strong, empirically promising, well-written, tackles a challenging density estimation problem.

- Con acceptance: Scalability issues, parameter sensitivity, limited comparisons, and lack of metric invariance are notable drawbacks.

- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: Expand scalability analysis; provide parameter-tuning strategies and σ selection methods; compare to more nonlinear density estimators; moderate strong claims; discuss metric invariance implications; study the effect of Monte Carlo sample size S.
