- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): a data-adaptive Riemannian normal using a learned local metric from inverse local covariances and geodesic distances.  
- Introduces a Monte Carlo integration scheme for normalization and a gradient-based MLE procedure, plus a mixture model extension.  
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets and EEG sleep-stage classification, showing improvements over standard GMMs.  

- Theoretically grounded in Riemannian geometry and maximum-entropy distributions on manifolds.  
- The Monte Carlo normalization avoids expensive boundary-value problems and makes the method computationally feasible in practice.  
- Experiments and figures clearly demonstrate LAND’s ability to capture nonlinear structure and improve EEG classification.  
- Paper is well-organized with consistent notation and clear presentation.  

- Computational bottleneck in the logarithm map is noted but no scalability analysis for large/high-dimensional datasets is provided.  
- Critical sensitivity to the kernel bandwidth σ with no principled selection method; σ fixed arbitrarily in EEG experiments despite intersubject variability.  
- Technical ambiguity in Eq.7: metric tensor definition appears scalar/diagonal but is presented as a full metric—needs clarification.  
- Limited empirical comparisons: lacks baselines from other metric-learning and modern deep manifold-learning approaches.  
- Insufficient theoretical justification for why the LAND mean avoids low-density regions versus the intrinsic least-squares mean.  

- Notation and structure are clear overall, and figures effectively illustrate results.  
- Explanations of metric construction and the inverse-covariance choice are confusing or incomplete.  
- Monte Carlo integration is described but lacks discussion of convergence properties or error bounds.  

- Novel contribution in adapting Riemannian normal distributions to learned manifolds, bridging Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.  
- Approach resembles prior metric-learning work (Hauberg et al.) and mixture extensions (Simo-Serra et al.), and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish from these.  
- Restriction to diagonal covariances for efficiency is a significant limitation not explored further.  

- Potentially useful for modeling nonlinear structures and grounded by maximum-entropy rationale, but practical impact is limited by scalability and parameter issues.  
- No evidence provided for applicability in large-scale machine learning settings; claims of bridging fields may be overstated.  

- Specific technical concerns: Eq.7 ambiguity (diagonal vs full metric), insufficient justification for integrating over tangent space vs manifold, no method for selecting σ, and unclear maximum-entropy status when the manifold is learned.  

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying Eq.7, providing a principled σ-selection method, and including a comparison to at least one metric-learning baseline.  
- Final scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 5/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 5/10; overall recommendation: Weak Accept.
