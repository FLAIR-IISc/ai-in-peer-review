- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric of a Gaussian with a learned, smoothly varying diagonal Riemannian metric M(x) and use the Riemannian normal distribution for modeling data.  
- Approximates the normalizing constant C(µ,Σ) by Monte Carlo in the tangent space sampling v∼N(0,Σ) and evaluating m(µ,v)=√|M(Expµ(v))|.  
- Performs MLE with gradients for Σ and geodesic updates for µ; sketches a mixture model and EM extension.  
- Empirical results show improved fit and clustering versus GMMs and least-squares Riemannian estimators on synthetic manifolds and EEG features.  
- Bridges Riemannian statistics with manifold learning by learning the metric rather than assuming known manifolds, building on Hauberg et al. and Pennec.  
- Strength: conceptually simple route to “nonlinear Gaussians” with maximum-entropy properties when manifold is fixed.  
- Strength: practical sampling-based approximation for C(µ,Σ) that avoids boundary-value problems by working in tangent space.  
- Strength: empirical evidence that LAND captures non-elliptical clusters and can outperform standard GMMs; mixture extension is natural.  
- Major concern: Eq. (9) appears to omit the Jacobian |det DExpµ(v)| in the change-of-variables, biasing C(µ,Σ) and its gradients.  
- If omission is an approximation, it must be stated, justified (e.g., small curvature), and its impact quantified.  
- Gradient concern: Eq. (12) seems to neglect µ-dependence through Expµ(v) and M(Expµ(v)); derivations are missing and expressions may be incorrect.  
- The “steepest descent” preconditioning using Σ instead of M(µ) is ad hoc and needs justification (e.g., natural gradient interpretation).  
- Metric design: diagonal, uncentered local second moments are not affine-invariant and cannot capture local anisotropy/coupling, potentially distorting geodesics.  
- Suggests evaluating alternatives or ablations (e.g., local PCA/full metrics or whitening) to address metric limitations.  
- Global geometry: paper assumes Logµ(x) globally defined, but learned metrics can have cut loci/non-unique geodesics; conditions for completeness/uniqueness are not stated.  
- Computational scalability: Monte Carlo estimation of C(µ,Σ) needs many Exp evaluations (S≈3000), with no timing, scaling, or variance-reduction strategies reported.  
- Evaluation methodology: EEG σ selection uses labels (best F-measure), which is label-informed and optimistic; label-free model selection and stronger baselines are needed.  
- Theoretical positioning: maximum-entropy claim is weaker because the manifold is learned from data rather than fixed—this nuance should be emphasized.  
- Quality: potentially serious technical flaw or undocumented approximation around normalization and gradients; empirical results are promising but not decisive.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but key equations (Eq. 9, gradients) need precise derivations or explicit approximation statements; hyperparameter choices and costs should be detailed.  
- Originality: novel combination of a learned Riemannian metric with per-component normalizer estimation; related work cited appropriately.  
- Significance: could be useful for generative modeling on data manifolds if normalization/gradient issues and evaluation gaps are resolved.  
- Questions for authors: derive Eq. (9) including Exp Jacobian or justify omission; provide full derivation of Eq. (12) and µ-derivatives; explain label-free selection of ρ and σ and sensitivity.  
- Questions for authors: report computational costs per iteration/component and any variance-reduction; comment on observed non-uniqueness of Log/Exp and safeguards.  
- Questions for authors: robustness to centered local covariance or full local metric?  
- Pros: clear idea, accounts for per-component normalizers, models non-elliptical clusters.  
- Cons: likely incorrect/incomplete change of variables; simplistic diagonal metric; unclear scalability and label-informed hyperparameter selection.  
- Recommendation: weak reject unless normalization/gradient derivations are corrected or justified with empirical validation and the evaluation protocol is tightened.
