- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in a Gaussian with a data-adaptive Riemannian metric learned from local covariances, compute geodesics via ODEs, and define a Riemannian normal density.  
- Normalizing constant C(µ,Σ) is intractable and estimated by Monte Carlo sampling in the tangent space using Expµ(v) and m(µ,v)=√|M(Expµ(v))|.  
- MLE is performed by block coordinate descent: update µ via steepest-descent in TµM using Log maps and Monte Carlo term, and update Σ via factorization; mixtures and an EM sketch are provided.  
- Connects prior work: extends Pennec’s Riemannian normal to learned metrics, relates to Hauberg-style local covariance metrics and Isomap-like geodesic approaches.  
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution bridging manifold learning and Riemannian statistics to yield a geometry-adaptive generative model.  
- Strength: MLE approach addresses intrinsic least-squares mean pitfalls (means falling in low-density regions).  
- Strength: Practical algorithmic choices (work in tangent space, use IVPs for Exp, block updates) are sensible.  
- Empirical results: synthetic curved-cluster experiments show improvements over GMMs; EEG clustering shows promise.  
- Major technical concern: Eq. (9) appears to omit the Jacobian |det DExpµ(v)| — replacing √|G(v)| by √|M(Expµ(v))| is not generally correct and affects C and its gradients.  
- If the Jacobian omission is an approximation, it must be stated, theoretically justified (e.g., small-Σ regime), and empirically quantified.  
- Statistical concern: metric is learned nonparametrically from the same data used for MLE, coupling metric estimation and likelihood and complicating consistency/identifiability and MLE interpretations.  
- Metric design issue: M(x) is diagonal in ambient coordinates and not affine-invariant, which can fail under strongly correlated local structure; whitening or richer metrics should be considered.  
- Computational scalability: computing m(µ,v) requires solving ODEs for Exp and O(ND) kernel sums; Monte Carlo with S≈3000 per eval and mixtures yields heavy per-iteration costs with no complexity/runtime analysis.  
- Monte Carlo/optimization concerns: normalizer and gradient estimators may have high variance; no variance-reduction (reparameterization, control variates), sample reuse, or convergence analysis provided.  
- Evaluation issues: EEG σ tuned using ground-truth labels (uses supervised signal in unsupervised task); baselines are limited (no KDE, MFA, flows), few ablations and no runtime/std dev reporting.  
- Reproducibility gaps: missing details on how Log is computed (BVP solver/tolerances), caching strategies, gradient variance mitigation, and exact EM steps.  
- Quality judgement: idea is compelling but missing Jacobian is a serious flaw; experiments are suggestive but need stronger baselines and hyperparameter handling.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear but omits crucial implementation and algorithmic details needed for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel combination of learned metric + maximum-likelihood Riemannian normal beyond prior work on known manifolds.  
- Significance: potentially useful if made robust and scalable, but current computational/theoretical gaps limit practical impact.  
- Suggested fixes: correct or justify Eq.(9) and quantify error; add complexity/runtime analysis and neighbor truncation; consider richer/local low-rank metrics or affine pre-whitening.  
- Suggested fixes (cont.): apply variance-reduction for Monte Carlo, analyze S’s effect, choose σ via unsupervised criteria (held-out likelihood/stability), add stronger baselines and report uncertainties.  
- Suggested theory: discuss asymptotics, consistency as N→∞ and σ→0, and conditions for MLE existence/uniqueness under learned metric.  
- Pros summary: elegant bridge between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics; addresses intrinsic mean issues; strong synthetic results.  
- Cons summary: probable incorrect normalization (missing Jacobian), heavy computation and limited scalability, diagonal non-invariance, and evaluation hyperparameter bias.  
- Recommendation: weak reject contingent on correcting/justifying Eq. (9) and strengthening experiments.
