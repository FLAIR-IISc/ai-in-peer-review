- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND) replacing Euclidean distance with a learned Riemannian metric for multivariate normals.  
- Learns the Riemannian metric from data via local covariance with Gaussian kernel smoothing rather than assuming a known manifold.  
- Provides scalable Monte Carlo integration for normalization, gradient-based MLE, and an EM extension for mixtures.  
- Demonstrates improved modeling on synthetic data and better EEG sleep-stage clustering compared to standard Gaussian mixtures.  
- Builds on and bridges Riemannian statistics (Pennec 2006) and manifold learning (e.g., Hauberg et al. 2013).  
- Technically sound with mostly correct mathematical derivations and clear geometric definitions.  
- Experimental evidence supports claims but lacks detailed statistical significance testing.  
- Paper is largely complete, covering theory, algorithms, and experiments, while acknowledging limitations like σ choice and geodesic cost.  
- Comparison to alternative metric-learning approaches is somewhat superficial and could be deeper.  
- Well-written and well-organized, with accessible exposition of Riemannian concepts for ML readers.  
- Would benefit from more implementation details for exponential/logarithmic map solvers for reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution: first to learn manifold metric for a generative Riemannian normal, using local covariance smoothing.  
- Related work is adequately referenced and the distinction from prior methods is clearly stated.  
- Significant because it addresses generative modeling on nonlinear manifolds and has potential applications (e.g., Naive Bayes, PCA).  
- Key limitations: scalability to high dimensions, computational cost of geodesic ODE solvers, and limited real-world evaluations.  
- Strengths summary: strong theoretical foundation, novel metric learning for generative models, practical normalization method, empirical gains, and compelling max-entropy interpretation.  
- Weaknesses summary: sensitivity and selection of σ, geodesic computation cost, scalability concerns, limited dataset evaluation, and shallow comparisons to other metric-learning methods.  
- Recommendation: accept—technically solid, novel, and significant despite computational and evaluation caveats.  
- Suggested improvements: add σ-selection analysis, discuss computational complexity and scalability, compare to other metric-learning approaches, show σ sensitivity on EEG, and detail numerical solvers used.
