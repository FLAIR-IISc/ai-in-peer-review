- Proposes the Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): a Riemannian normal using a learned, smoothly varying metric from local kernel-smoothed covariances.
- Metric defined as inverse of local (diagonal) covariance estimated with Gaussian kernels, making geodesics attracted to high-density regions.
- Density pM(x|µ,Σ) uses Riemannian Log map and has a normalization constant that depends on µ and manifold curvature.
- Introduces a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant C(µ,Σ) by sampling v ∼ N(0,Σ) and evaluating m(µ,v)=√|M(Expµ(v))|.
- Derives gradients for ML estimation of µ and Σ (with Σ−1 = AᵀA) and provides a block-coordinate descent solver.
- Extends the model to mixtures with an EM algorithm.
- Empirical results show improved likelihood and clustering F-measure over GMMs and LS-based Riemannian estimators on synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage features.
- Novelty: learns the manifold/metric from data and accounts for the µ,Σ-dependent normalization, bridging manifold learning and Riemannian statistics.
- Technical contribution: avoiding expensive Log maps inside the integral via tangent-space MC and providing practical gradients.
- Strong exposition, clear figures, and honest discussion of limitations.
- Major scalability concern: computing m(µ,v) requires solving ODEs for Expµ(v) and summing kernel weights over N, with S≈3000 MC samples per iteration—runtime and scaling not reported.
- Metric limitation: using diagonal local covariances ignores local correlations and is not invariant to linear transforms, potentially reducing modeling power.
- Kernel bandwidth σ selection unclear: grid search may have used labels (F-measure), risking bias; unsupervised selection methods are not explored.
- Gradient w.r.t. Σ is delicate because the sampling distribution depends on Σ; unbiasedness and variance-reduction (e.g., common random numbers) are not clarified.
- Optimization concerns: non-convexity with no convergence analysis or empirical sensitivity studies (initialization, step sizes, S); reuse of MC samples across iterations is unclear.
- Limited baselines: comparisons exclude manifold-aware clustering, kernel GMMs, mixtures of factor analyzers, and related probabilistic geometry methods.
- Evaluation breadth limited: real-data experiments on 10 subjects with 5D features are promising but modest; held-out log-likelihoods and more datasets are missing.
- Quality: technically sound and motivated, but key derivations are in the supplement and computational burden is not quantified.
- Clarity: generally clear, but needs explicit computational complexity, Log-map implementation details, and transparency on σ selection and label use.
- Originality: placing a Riemannian normal on a learned manifold with tangent-space normalization is novel and nontrivial.
- Significance: could be a useful replacement for Gaussians in moderate dimensions if computational issues are addressed.
- Suggested improvements: scalable approximations (local neighborhoods, inducing points, Nystrom/RFF), reuse of CRNs, control variates, and small-v expansions for m(µ,v).
- Recommend learning σ by ML or EB and exploring low-rank or shrinkage local covariances to capture correlations.
- Recommend stronger baselines, held-out likelihoods, and wall-clock time reporting.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-motivated model; solid algorithmic contribution; empirical gains; bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning.
- Cons for acceptance: computational scalability concerns, limited baselines, potential tuning bias for σ, and supplemental-only derivations.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending stronger experimental validation (scaling, baselines, σ selection) and clarification of gradient derivations.
