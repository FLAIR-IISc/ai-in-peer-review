- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean Mahalanobis distance with a learned Riemannian metric M(x) and fit a Riemannian normal (and mixtures) using tangent-space MC normalization.
- Novelty: learns the manifold metric from data (diagonal, kernel-smoothed SPD field) and does end-to-end ML estimation of Riemannian normals and mixtures.
- Main technical concern: the normalization change-of-variables appears to omit the Jacobian of the exponential map (volume distortion), potentially biasing the MC estimator in curved regions.
- The paper does not justify conditions (e.g., small v or weak curvature) under which ignoring the Exp Jacobian is acceptable or quantify the resulting error.
- Gradients and optimization for µ and Σ (via factorization) are derived and a block-coordinate / EM-like mixture scheme is provided; derivations are deferred to the supplement.
- Computational burden: requires many Log (BVP) and Exp (IVP) solves plus S Monte Carlo samples per step, with no reported runtimes or scaling analysis.
- Stability: steepest descent for µ helps condition-number issues for Σ, but overall computational practicality in higher D or large N is unclear.
- Experiments: synthetic data convincingly show curved clusters captured with fewer components than GMMs.
- EEG experiments are suggestive but limited: per-subject σ tuning may be optimistic and baselines are not equivalently tuned or sufficiently strong.
- Missing empirical ablations: effect of S (MC samples), σ selection, diagonal vs full M(x), and normalization cost/accuracy trade-offs are not quantified.
- Clarity: exposition is generally good, but the key measure transformation for C(µ,Σ) needs a precise derivation and explicit statement of any approximations.
- Paper should add algorithmic details: ODE solver choices, step sizes, initialization, convergence criteria, complexity analysis, and wall-clock times.
- Originality: moderate–good; combining learned Riemannian metric with Riemannian normal fitting is novel and pragmatic.
- Significance: potentially impactful as a way to “manifoldize” Gaussian models, but immediate practical impact is weakened by the normalization issue and scalability concerns.
- Pros: clear idea, principled normalization attempt, bridges manifold learning and Riemannian statistics, provides usable gradients and mixture extension.
- Cons: likely incorrect/uncontrolled approximation in normalization, computationally heavy and limited to diagonal M and low D, limited experimental rigor and baselines.
- Key questions for authors: Is the Exp Jacobian accounted for in Eq.(9)? If not, under what assumptions is dropping it acceptable and what is the empirical impact?
- Key questions for authors: Provide runtimes and scaling with N, D, S, K and count of Log/Exp solves per iteration.
- Key questions for authors: How sensitive are results to σ and S, and is there an automated σ selection?
- Recommendation: Borderline / weak reject pending a justified correction or approximation for the normalization constant and stronger empirical comparisons with runtime reporting.
