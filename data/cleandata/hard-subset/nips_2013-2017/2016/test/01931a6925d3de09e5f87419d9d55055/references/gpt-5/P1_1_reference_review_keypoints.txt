- Proposes LAND: replace Euclidean metric in multivariate normal with a learned, smoothly varying Riemannian metric M(x) built from local (diagonal) covariance estimates via Gaussian kernels.  
- Defines Riemannian normal density pM(x|µ,Σ) ∝ exp(−1/2 ⟨Logµ(x), Σ−1 Logµ(x)⟩) with a normalization constant depending on curvature and µ.  
- Key technical contribution: scalable Monte Carlo estimator for the normalization constant C = Z Ev∼N(0,Σ)[√|M(Expµ(v))|], avoiding boundary-value problems by using only Exp (initial-value geodesic ODEs).  
- Derives gradients for MLE with block coordinate descent: manifold-aware µ updates and Σ updates via Σ−1 = AᵀA factorization; develops an EM-like mixture-of-LANDs algorithm.  
- Experiments on synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage clustering show LAND better captures curved structures than GMMs and outperforms least-squares intrinsic Riemannian estimators, improving clustering F-measure.  
- Situates work relative to prior Riemannian normals and manifold learning (Pennec; Zhang & Fletcher; Hauberg et al.; Tosi et al.); novelty is combining learned metrics with a practical normalization estimator.  
- Strength: principled bridge between manifold learning and Riemannian statistics yielding a flexible, locally adaptive generative model.  
- Strength: clever tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization and clear, implementable gradient formulas.  
- Strength: MLE formulation mitigates intrinsic-mean drift into low-density regions.  
- Strength: natural mixture extension and convincing empirical advantages over simple baselines.  
- Strength: likely broad interest for “manifoldizing” Gaussian-based models.  
- Weakness: metric uses only diagonal local covariances, lacks affine invariance, ignores correlations, and can be brittle under rotations.  
- Weakness: kernel width σ is critical, tuned per subject in EEG experiments, risking overfitting; no procedure to learn σ or thorough sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: tangent-space change-of-variables assumes well-behaved Exp/Log and injectivity radius; paper lacks discussion on cut loci or when the integral over TµM is valid.  
- Weakness: computational cost high—each µ-update requires N Log maps (BVPs); no runtime, complexity, or scaling studies provided; MC normalization uses S=3000 Exp maps which may not scale to higher D.  
- Weakness: limited baselines (no PPCA/factor analyzers, kernel density, warped mixtures, normalizing flows, etc.); evaluation choices (e.g., mean NLL of true generative distribution) are unconventional.  
- Weakness: missing ablations on hyperparameters ρ, σ, S and no stability/optimization analysis.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees (consistency/identifiability) and lacks analysis of MC estimator variance/bias or variance-reduction techniques.  
- Clarity: generally well written, but lacks detail on convergence criteria, step-size selection, initialization, runtime stats, dimensionalities, and sensitivity plots; some derivations deferred and references misformatted.  
- Originality: moderately original—combines known ideas but contribution of tractable normalization and mixture EM is nontrivial.  
- Significance: potentially impactful as a manifold-aware replacement for Gaussians, but practical adoption hinges on scalability and robust hyperparameter selection.  
- Questions posed: report runtimes/scaling with N,D,K,S; sensitivity of results to σ and ρ and whether σ can be learned; use of variance-reduction for C; numerical handling of Exp/Log (solvers, tolerances, retractions); whether metrics are shared or component-specific in mixtures.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled model, elegant normalization trick, clear algorithm, and empirical gains on nonlinear structure.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak baselines/evaluation depth, simplistic metric design, and insufficient justification of normalization assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on stronger empirical comparisons, runtime/scaling analysis, and better justification of geometric and computational assumptions.
