- Proposes Locally Adaptive Normal Distribution (LAND): replaces Euclidean distance with geodesic distance from a learned, locally estimated Riemannian metric and uses Monte Carlo normalization and gradient MLE; extends to mixtures via EM and shows gains on synthetic and EEG data.

- Bridges Riemannian statistics and manifold learning by building a continuously defined metric (vs. discrete geodesic distances) and jointly learning manifold and distribution parameters.

- Novelty: principled, innovative adaptation of the normal distribution to nonlinear manifolds.

- Theoretical foundation: grounded in Riemannian geometry with a clear mathematical framework.

- Practical algorithm: scalable Monte Carlo integration, gradient-based optimization, and an EM extension make the method applicable to real data.

- Empirical validation: outperforms GMMs and a least-squares LAND variant on synthetic and EEG tasks, capturing non-ellipsoidal distributions.

- Presentation: generally clear exposition of math and algorithms.

- Computational cost: geodesic ODE solves and log/exp maps are expensive; σ affects smoothness and compute time.

- Smoothing parameter σ: choice impacts performance and computation; needs more adaptive/automated selection.

- Metric invariance: learned metric is not invariant to linear transformations, limiting applicability.

- Sensitivity to initialization: gradient optimization can be initialization-sensitive; supplement discussion is brief.

- High-dimensional scalability: local covariance estimation via Gaussian kernel may fail in very high dimensions (curse of dimensionality).

- Limited comparisons: lacks evaluation against modern manifold/generative methods (e.g., VAEs, normalizing flows on manifolds).

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid, practical algorithm with promising empirical results that fill a gap between Riemannian stats and manifold learning.

- Arguments against acceptance: computational scalability concerns and lack of linear invariance could limit real-world applicability.

- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with acknowledged limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance.

- Recommendations: provide detailed initialization strategies; explore adaptive σ or alternative metrics; investigate approximate or specialized solvers to reduce geodesic costs; compare to advanced generative/manifold methods; expand discussion of metric invariance and workarounds.
