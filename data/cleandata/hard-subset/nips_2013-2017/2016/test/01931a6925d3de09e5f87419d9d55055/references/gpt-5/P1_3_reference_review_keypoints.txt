- Proposes LAND: a parametric Riemannian-normal density using geodesic distances on a learned data-adaptive metric M(x).
- Metric M(x) is a smoothly varying diagonal tensor from Gaussian-kernel-smoothed local diagonal covariances with ridge ρ, encouraging geodesics through high-density regions.
- Uses Pennec’s Riemannian normal pM(x|µ,Σ) and addresses the normalization constant C(µ,Σ) that depends on manifold geometry and µ.
- Introduces a tangent-space Monte Carlo estimator for C by sampling v ~ N(0,Σ) and computing Expµ(v), avoiding expensive Log map BVPs.
- Derives gradients for ML: steepest-descent update for µ and factorized Σ−1 = A⊤A gradient for Σ; sketches a mixture-of-LANDs with EM-like procedure.
- Empirically shows improved fit and clustering over GMMs and least-squares Riemannian estimators on synthetic manifolds and EEG sleep-stage data.
- Connects Riemannian statistics with manifold learning and differs from embedding-based methods by learning a continuous ambient-space metric.
- Novelty: learning a Riemannian metric for a normalized Riemannian normal and tangent-space Monte Carlo normalization are notable contributions.
- Strength: clear conceptual bridge and principled maximum-entropy formulation on a learned metric.
- Strength: practical and elegant normalization scheme using Exp maps that is computationally preferable to Log-based integration.
- Strength: provides full ML treatment, algorithmic recipe, and normalized mixture components.
- Strength: demonstrates that LAND captures nonlinear cluster structure with fewer components than GMMs and avoids intrinsic-mean drift.
- Weakness: metric limited to diagonal local covariances, losing invariance to linear transforms and inability to model arbitrary local anisotropy.
- Weakness: strong dependence on kernel width σ with ad-hoc tuning per subject; lacks principled selection or learning of σ.
- Weakness: computational cost concerns—C evaluation needs S Exp solves and gradients need N Log maps—without runtime, scaling, or sensitivity analysis to S.
- Weakness: µ-gradient derivation (Eq.12) is unclear; dependence of m on µ suggests missing derivative terms and deferred supplement derivations need clarification.
- Weakness: geometric well-posedness not addressed—learned metrics may be incomplete or have cut loci, risking non-unique Exp/Log evaluations.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (low-D, modest N) and missing comparisons to nonparametric baselines (KDE, diffusion distances, GP-LVM).
- Weakness: mixture EM implementation details (C estimation per component, responsibility stabilization, resampling v) are relegated to supplements and need main-text clarity.
- Quality: idea is plausible and well motivated but key derivations, complexity, and stability are underexplored.
- Clarity: paper is readable with good geometry intuition, but notation (factors of 1/2, domains) and algorithmic pseudo-code/complexity section should be tightened.
- Significance: potentially useful if made scalable and robust, with likely impact on manifold-aware generative modeling.
- Questions for authors: request full Eq.12 derivation, numerical handling of Logµ(x) and cut loci, runtime/scaling data, Monte Carlo variance reduction, and whether v are resampled when Σ changes.
- Pros for acceptance: novel conceptual bridge, practical normalization, ML estimation with mixtures, and empirical gains on nonlinear data.
- Cons for acceptance: unclear gradient/theoretical guarantees, limited experiments and scalability analysis, and restrictive diagonal metric.
- Recommendation: borderline accept pending clarification of derivations (especially Eq.12), scalability evidence, and more principled σ handling.
