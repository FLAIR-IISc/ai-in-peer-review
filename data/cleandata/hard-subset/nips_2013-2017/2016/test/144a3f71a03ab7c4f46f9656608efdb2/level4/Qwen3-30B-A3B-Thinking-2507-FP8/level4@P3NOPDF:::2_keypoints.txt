- Proposes leveraging ancestral constraints to improve heuristics for exhaustive search; idea is theoretically sound.  
- Experimental presentation is ambiguous and fails to demonstrate the incremental value of the proposed method.  
- Tables 1 and 3 are confusing: it's unclear whether Table 3 reports base EC vs GOB or includes the proposed method.  
- The authors do not present a direct comparison between EC (baseline) and EC + proposed method—this is a key omission.  
- Comparing (EC + method) to (GOB + method') conflates the method’s effect with EC’s inherent superiority.  
- Small absolute improvements on the strong EC baseline are more meaningful in admissible heuristics/exhaustive search, but are not reported.  
- The paper lacks analysis of why the method performs differently on EC vs GOB and has no sensitivity analysis to constraint quality.  
- Required fixes: clarify experimental setups in Tables 1/3, add EC vs EC + method comparison, and analyze performance variation with constraint characteristics.  
- Conclusion: theoretical contribution is promising but experimental shortcomings leave the paper’s practical significance unproven; major revision needed.
