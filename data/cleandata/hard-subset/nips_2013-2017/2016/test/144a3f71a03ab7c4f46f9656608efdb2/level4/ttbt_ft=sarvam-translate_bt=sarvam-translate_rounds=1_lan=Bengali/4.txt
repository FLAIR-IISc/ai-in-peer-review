This paper proposes a method for converting non- separable ancestral constraints into separable constraints, enabling separable-score-based learning methods to incorporate such domain knowledge and thereby improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper lies primarily in Section 5, where the authors present their proposed method for converting non- separable ancestral constraints into edge-based and topological ordering constraints, which are separable. The paper is well-written and clearly illustrates their work in both theory and experiment. Here are some of my concerns and suggestions: i. Table 3 shows that the projection of ancestral constraints can only improve the performance of EC trees, not the ILP method. Does this mean that projecting ancestral constraints into separable constraints only works for EC trees and that its generalization ability is weak for other learning methods? The main innovation of this paper is inferring separable constraints from non- separable ancestral constraints. It would be more convincing if the authors could show that incorporating such separable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of specific learning methods that cannot handle non- separable constraints. ii. Table 2 shows a simple-hard-easy pattern of EC tree performance with increasing ancestral constraint ratio. Why does this happen? Does this mean that the effect of reducing the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints is quite sensitive to the domain knowledge it incorporates compared to the ILP method? Therefore, is it necessary to carefully determine the number of ancestral constraints to achieve ideal performance? iii. Table 2 only shows the results of structural Hamming distance from EC trees. But, how does this compare to the ILP method? iv. A small suggestion: I understand that the theoretical basis of EC trees is in the BN graph, as the EC tree extends from it. However, since the experimental part only considers the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints applied, I will skip section 4.1 and spend more time explaining the EC tree and the A* algorithm in EC tree settings.
