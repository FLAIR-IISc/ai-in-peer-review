- Presents a novel method to learn Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s framework and extends the EC tree search space to incorporate ancestral constraints via sound pruning and constraint projection.  
- Enables efficient, optimal structure learning by allowing existing decomposable-score oracles to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Addresses an important practical problem: integrating cause-and-effect background knowledge into optimal Bayesian network learning.  
- Advances beyond prior work (constraint-based, ILP/CP) by empirically demonstrating efficient optimal learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Strong theoretical development: well-motivated constraint projection algorithms with formal correctness proofs.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation showing orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP-based approaches across benchmarks and constraint settings.  
- Results are clearly presented with appropriate statistics (50 datasets) and relevant metrics (structural Hamming distance).  
- Clarity is generally good, but the link between the BN graph (Sec. 4.1) and EC trees (Sec. 4.2) should be made more explicit.  
- Main concern 1: Constraint projection improves EC tree performance significantly but has limited impact on ILP; authors should analyze whether this is inherent to ILP or due to encoding.  
- Main concern 2: The easy–hard–easy pattern in EC tree performance as constraint proportion increases is observed but not explained; authors should discuss causes and practical implications.  
- Main concern 3: Table 3 reports EC tree SHD but lacks direct comparison to ILP quality; include ILP vs EC tree accuracy comparison to assess trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: Consider omitting Sec. 4.1 (BN graphs) or shorten it and expand explanation of EC trees and A* search to aid readers unfamiliar with prior work.  
- Overall strengths: technical rigor, clear presentation, novel constraint projection, and compelling empirical gains; main weaknesses are limited discussion on ILP differences, unexplained performance pattern, and missing comparative quality metrics.  
- Recommendation: With minor revisions addressing the above concerns, the paper is a strong contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
