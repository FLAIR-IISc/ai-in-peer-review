- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into ordering constraints and integrating with an EC tree algorithm; shows strong performance vs a modified Gobnilp.

- Paper is well-written and clear; core idea is conceptually straightforward with moderate novelty (score 3) but limited practical impact (score 2) because ancestral constraints usually arise in broader causal settings with unobserved confounders.

- Key technical contribution: encoding intractable ancestral constraints as decomposable ordering constraints via a MaxSAT solver, aiming to explicitly capture all implied constraints.

- Constraint-rule errors: Rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion (inconsistency), and Rule 4 may wrongly exclude valid “X and Y do not cause each other” constraints.

- Questionable assumption: sparse ancestral constraints may not meaningfully reduce search space—10–25% constraints would already specify most edges, contradicting typical use cases.

- Experimental baseline is misleading: Gobnilp with added linear constraints is not designed for ancestral constraints, so the comparison overstates advantages.

- Recommends a more meaningful baseline such as oracle-guided GES (greedy search), which, while not optimal, would be a realistic benchmark and could recover the true structure with oracle constraints.

- Theorem 1 needs clarification that the CPDAG-derived constraint set A is a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG; otherwise the iff claim is incorrect.

- Theorem 3’s practical utility is doubtful for sparse ancestral constraints, because few positive edge implications would be produced.

- Acyclicity condition (line 267) is unclear and appears to conflict with earlier statements about constraints—needs clarification.

- The claim that 10–25% ancestral constraints are “not typical” requires justification, since that level is highly informative.

- Minor editorial fixes: clarify "standard" vs "omniscient" oracle (line 30), rephrase "empower the oracle" (line 38), and state that optimization minimizes the score (line 90).

- Overall assessment: technically valid and of niche interest to BN structure-learning specialists, but modest novelty and impact; strengthen by fixing rule/theory issues and using a more appropriate experimental baseline.
