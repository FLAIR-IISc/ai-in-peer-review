- Tackles learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAGs.  
- Search uses symmetry breaking and A* with the URLearning heuristic (details deferred to prior work).  
- Central result (Lemma 3) appears correct but its proof needs clearer justification—specifically the claim “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” via EC tree edge-generation rules.  
- Uses implied/projected constraints: ancestral constraints are converted to edge constraints for pruning.  
- Derives ordering constraints on topological orders, but the inference method is insufficiently explained.  
- The phrase “can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is ambiguous—authors actually add many consistent (not logically entailed) ordering constraints.  
- They use a MAXSAT-based approach to select which ordering constraints to add; this procedure needs clearer exposition.  
- Experiments vary variables, sample size, and ancestral constraints and show large speedups over GOBNILP.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is unclear because GOBNILP typically struggles without a parent-set size cap; poor GOBNILP performance may reflect that rather than ancestral-constraint handling.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints via a MAXSAT formulation from Cussens (2008), which is a weak ILP formulation for solvers.  
- Recommend introducing binary A(X,Y) ancestry variables with transitivity plus linkage constraints (as in Cussens 2010 Sec.3.1.1) to get a much tighter linear relaxation.  
- Even if the proposed method still outperforms GOBNILP with a stronger ILP, comparing against a weak formulation weakens the empirical claims.  
- Suggest comparing to van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP; that work is not cited.
