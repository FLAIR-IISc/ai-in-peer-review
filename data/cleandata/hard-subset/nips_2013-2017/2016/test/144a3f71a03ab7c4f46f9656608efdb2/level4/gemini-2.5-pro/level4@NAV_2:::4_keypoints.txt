- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures from data with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that break many efficient, score-decomposable algorithms.  
- Background: builds on Chen et al.’s A* search over equivalence-class (EC) trees using an oracle optimal learner for decomposable scores.  
- Main contribution: method to infer decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints from ancestral constraints and supply them to the oracle.  
- Effect: inferred constraints improve the oracle’s heuristic and substantially accelerate the EC-tree search.  
- Empirical result: extensive experiments on standard benchmarks show orders-of-magnitude speedups versus an ILP baseline (GOBNILP).  
- Originality/significance: novel “projection” of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable form, bridging rich background knowledge and efficient learning machinery.  
- Technical quality: methods are well-founded, experiments thorough, and results convincingly demonstrate performance gains.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and presents background and contributions clearly.  
- Weakness 1 / Q: Adding inferred constraints sometimes slows the ILP solver (e.g., n=12); authors should explain why redundant constraints can hurt ILP performance and general applicability.  
- Weakness 2 / Q: The “easy–hard–easy” pattern as constraint percentage increases needs deeper discussion of practical implications, especially for moderate constraint levels.  
- Weakness 3 / Q: Recommend including ILP baseline SHD in Table 3 to verify the speed advantage does not sacrifice model accuracy.  
- Weakness 4 / Suggestion: streamline the intro by condensing BN-graph material and expanding the EC-tree/A* search discussion.  
- Overall: strong, significant, and scalable contribution; remaining issues are minor requests for clarification and additional experimental detail.
