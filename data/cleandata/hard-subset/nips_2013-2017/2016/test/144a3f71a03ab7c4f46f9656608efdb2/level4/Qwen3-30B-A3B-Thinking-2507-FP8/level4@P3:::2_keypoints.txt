- Problem: Incorporates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Main contribution: Projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Framework: Builds on EC tree (Chen et al.) and leverages decomposable-score methods (Koivisto, Parviainen, Yuan & Malone) and constraint/path-based literature.  
- Theoretical soundness: Provides rigorous proofs for the projection method (Theorems 2–4) and pruning soundness (Theorem 1).  
- Experimental design: Uses standard BN benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) with systematic variation of n, N, p and reproducible details.  
- Empirical results: EC tree with projected constraints is orders-of-magnitude faster than ILP-based GOBNILP, with efficiency gains increasing with problem size.  
- Accuracy impact: 10–25% ancestral constraints can yield accuracy improvements comparable to increasing data from 512 to 8192.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and explanations are clear, though some experimental tables (1–3) are confusing and need clarification.  
- Missing comparison: Does not explicitly present EC vs EC+proj to isolate the benefit of the projection method (Table 2 partially indicates projection helps).  
- Originality: First comprehensive evaluation showing ILP/CP inefficiency for ancestral constraints and a novel projection enabling efficient oracles to handle them.  
- Significance: Addresses a practical gap—enabling domain causal knowledge in optimal learning with large runtime reductions.  
- Strengths: Strong theoretical contribution, comprehensive empirical validation, practical relevance, and clear connection to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: Experimental comparisons could be clearer; limited discussion of incomplete/conflicting constraints and extreme scaling; MaxSAT connection needs stronger motivation.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, original, and practically significant with clear efficiency gains.
