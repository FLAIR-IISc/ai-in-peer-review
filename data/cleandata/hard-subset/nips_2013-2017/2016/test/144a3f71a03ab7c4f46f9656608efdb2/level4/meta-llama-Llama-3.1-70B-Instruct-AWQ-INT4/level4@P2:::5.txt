This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. The authors build upon a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning based on non-decomposable scores, which can accommodate ancestral constraints. The proposed approach leverages oracles for learning structures using decomposable scores, which cannot handle ancestral constraints directly. By inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, the authors empower these oracles to efficiently search for optimal Bayesian network structures.

The paper provides a thorough review of the problem of Bayesian network structure learning, including existing approaches based on score-based methods, integer linear programming (ILP), and constraint programming (CP). The authors highlight the limitations of mainstream approaches in handling non-decomposable constraints, such as ancestral constraints, and demonstrate how their approach can overcome these limitations.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors introduce a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, based on ancestral constraints. They also propose a method for inferring decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which can be used to empower the oracle during structure search.

The empirical evaluation of the proposed approach against ILP-based approaches shows impressive results, with orders-of-magnitude improvements in runtime. The authors also demonstrate the scalability of their approach, with results reported for up to 20 variables.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical concepts and contributions. The empirical evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner.
