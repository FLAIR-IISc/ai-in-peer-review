This paper presents a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms, specifically edge and topological ordering constraints. The goal is to enable decomposable score-based structure learning algorithms to incorporate this type of domain knowledge, thereby enhancing learning efficiency. The core novelty of the work is detailed in Section 5, where this projection technique is introduced. The manuscript is well-written, and both the theoretical and experimental sections are presented with clarity.

However, I have the following concerns and suggestions:

i. According to Table 3, the constraint projection method improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. This raises questions about the method's generalizability. Is the benefit of projecting ancestral constraints limited to specific algorithms like EC trees? The paper's main contribution would be more compelling if the authors could demonstrate that their method improves the performance and efficiency of a learning algorithm that was fundamentally unable to handle non-decomposable constraints in their original form.

ii. Table 2 reveals an "easy-hard-easy" performance pattern for EC trees as the proportion of ancestral constraints is increased. Could the authors provide an explanation for this observation? It may suggest that the impact of search space reduction via ancestral constraints is particularly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge supplied, potentially more so than in the ILP approach. This would imply that the number of constraints needs to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance.

iii. The results in Table 2 are incomplete, showing only the Structural Hamming Distance for the EC tree method. To facilitate a direct comparison, the corresponding results for the ILP approach should also be included.

iv. A final suggestion on the paper's structure: While Section 4.1 on BN graphs provides foundational context, the experiments are focused entirely on EC trees. I would suggest omitting Section 4.1 and using the reclaimed space to offer a more thorough explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm as applied in the paper's experimental setting, as these elements are more central to the empirical contributions.
