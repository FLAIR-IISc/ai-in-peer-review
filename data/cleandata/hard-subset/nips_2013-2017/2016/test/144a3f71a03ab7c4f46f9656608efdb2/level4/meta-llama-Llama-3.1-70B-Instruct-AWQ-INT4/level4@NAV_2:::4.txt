This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a type of non-decomposable constraint that is challenging for mainstream structure learning methods. The authors leverage a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning based on non-decomposable scores, which can accommodate ancestral constraints. They show how to empower an oracle for learning structures with decomposable scores by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints. The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning.

The main contribution of this paper lies in its ability to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. This allows the authors to incorporate domain knowledge into decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches, leading to improved learning efficiency. The authors provide a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, based on ancestral constraints.

The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it is orders-of-magnitude more efficient than alternative frameworks based on integer linear programming. The authors also investigate the impact of ancestral constraints on the efficiency of search and the scalability of their approach as the number of variables increases.
