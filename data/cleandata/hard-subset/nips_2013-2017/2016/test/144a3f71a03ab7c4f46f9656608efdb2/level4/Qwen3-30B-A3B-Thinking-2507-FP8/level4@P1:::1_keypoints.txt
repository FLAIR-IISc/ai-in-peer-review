- Addresses learning Bayesian network structure under ancestral (cause-effect) constraints, extending Chen et al.'s EC tree to non-decomposable scores.  
- Key insight: ancestral constraints, though non-decomposable, can imply decomposable edge and ordering constraints to empower an efficient URLearning oracle.  
- Shows large empirical gains — orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP and scales to larger networks (up to ~20 variables).  
- Provides a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree using ancestral constraints, crucial for non-decomposable background knowledge.  
- Develops a systematic method to infer decomposable edge constraints from ancestral constraints to tighten the search heuristic.  
- Introduces a MaxSAT-based approach to infer ordering constraints that maximizes consistency without creating cycles.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear theoretical foundations, robust experimental design, and reproducible technical detail.  
- Strengths: novel handling of non-decomposable constraints, thorough theoretical analysis, convincing empirical evidence, and clear writing.  
- Weakness: explanation of how ancestral constraints imply edge constraints (Theorems 2–3) is opaque and needs a concrete illustrative example.  
- Weakness: comparison to GOBNILP uses a suboptimal ILP/MAXSAT encoding for ancestral constraints; a tighter ILP with A(X,Y) variables and linking/transitivity constraints should be considered.  
- Weakness: omits comparison to constraint programming approaches (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann 2015), which often outperform GOBNILP and could handle ancestral constraints.  
- Needs clearer explanation that inferring ordering constraints from non-ancestry (e.g., Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y) is a design choice to add consistent constraints, not a logical entailment.  
- Significance: enables optimal structure learning with rich causal background knowledge and yields practical efficiency gains, especially with limited data.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing clarifications and stronger baseline comparisons.
