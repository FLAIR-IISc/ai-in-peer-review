

This paper addresses the challenge of learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable constraints, specifically ancestral constraints. The authors extend Chen et al.'s [2015, 2016] structure learning framework by integrating these constraints, reformulating the problem as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. Their empirical evaluation demonstrates significant computational advantages over ILP-based approaches, with runtime reductions on the order of multiple magnitudes. While my expertise in MaxSAT formulations is limited, the runtime results presented are compelling and clearly highlight the efficiency gains of their approach. The method effectively handles constraints that previous techniques struggle with, and the performance improvements are substantial enough to warrant serious consideration in the field. The technical contribution is well-motivated, and the experimental results provide strong evidence for the practical viability of their method.
