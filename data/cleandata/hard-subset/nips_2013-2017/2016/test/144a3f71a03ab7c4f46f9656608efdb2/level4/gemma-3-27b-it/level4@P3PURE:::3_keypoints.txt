- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal BN learning by translating them into ordering constraints used with an EC-tree score-based algorithm.
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to compute a maximal set of ordering constraints so all implied ancestral constraints are explicitly represented.
- Experiments show significant performance improvements over a straightforward ILP-modified Gobnilp baseline in the tested setting.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained, though the solution is conceptually straightforward.
- Novelty assessed as moderate (3): the problem/solution pairing is interesting and new but not groundbreaking.
- Impact assessed as low-moderate (2): in many cases general causal discovery methods handling latent confounders would be preferable.
- Practical concern that typically only a limited number of ancestral constraints will be provided, so search-space reduction may be small.
- Some inference rules (lines 257–261) appear strange or incomplete (e.g., acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion).
- Rule 4 seems to incorrectly exclude ancestral constraints that state "X and Y do not cause each other."
- Gobnilp baseline is not ideally targeted to this problem; recommend benchmarking against greedy algorithms like GES under oracle-consistent ancestral constraints.
- Theorem 1 (line 164) should explicitly state that constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG on the EC-tree are only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G.
- Theorem 3 (lines 225–227) is unlikely to yield many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.
- The relationship to "acyclic" (line 267) is unclear and needs clarification (see related rule concerns).
- Minor wording/clarity fixes needed: line 30 (oracle vs omniscient oracle), line 38 ("empower the oracle" wording), line 90 ("optimizing" should mean minimizing).
- Practical note (line 310): 10–25% ancestral constraints imply substantial prior knowledge of the target graph, which is atypical in real scenarios.
- Overall conclusion: novel problem + straightforward mapping + promising empirical gains make the work of definite interest to a specialist audience.
