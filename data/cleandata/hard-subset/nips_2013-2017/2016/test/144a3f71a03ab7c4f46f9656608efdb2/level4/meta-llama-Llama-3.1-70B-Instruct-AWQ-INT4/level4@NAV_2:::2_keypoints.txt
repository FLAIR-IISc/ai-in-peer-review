- Proposes using ancestral constraints to inform an admissible heuristic for exhaustive search in Bayesian network structure learning.
- Builds on a recent framework for optimal learning with non-decomposable scores that can incorporate ancestral constraints.
- Leverages oracles for decomposable-score structure learning by converting ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable constraints the oracles can handle.
- Introduces a method to empower existing decomposable-score oracles via inferred decomposable constraints.
- Provides a sound and complete pruning method for the equivalence-class (EC) search tree based on ancestral constraints.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough review of problem and prior work.
- Clear explanation of the proposed approach and its advantages over existing methods.
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over alternatives such as integer linear programming.
- Experiments are well-designed and provide thorough comparisons with existing methods (except EC baseline).
- Strengths: clear writing, comprehensive literature review, solid experiments, and the EC-tree pruning contribution.
- Weaknesses: lacks explicit experimental comparison to the existing EC approach.
- Weaknesses: insufficient detail on computational complexity and comparative complexity analysis.
- Overall assessment: significant and technically sound contribution to Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral constraints.
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, well-designed and executed method, technically sound exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing EC-baseline comparison and more detailed complexity analysis.
