- Tackles Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable (ancestral) constraints.  
- Extends Chen et al.'s [2015, 2016] structure learning framework.  
- Reformulates the problem as a MaxSAT optimization with carefully designed constraints.  
- Enables efficient incorporation of complex, non-decomposable requirements.  
- Empirical evaluation shows runtime reductions of multiple orders of magnitude versus ILP-based methods.  
- Significantly outperforms alternatives in scalability.  
- Reviewer’s limited MaxSAT expertise noted, but runtime comparisons are compelling and well-supported.  
- Method handles non-decomposable constraints without sacrificing efficiency, addressing a common bottleneck.  
- Paper clearly explains the reformulation and provides thorough experimental validation across diverse datasets.  
- Establishes a strong baseline and positions MaxSAT as a promising, practical alternative to ILP for large-scale constrained structure learning.
