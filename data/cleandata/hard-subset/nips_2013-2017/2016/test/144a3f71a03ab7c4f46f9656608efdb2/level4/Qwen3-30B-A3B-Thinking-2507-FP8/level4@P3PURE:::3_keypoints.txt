- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into maximal ordering constraints.  
- Integrates these ordering constraints into a score-based learning algorithm using the EC tree framework (Chen et al., 2015).  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to encode all implied ancestral constraints explicitly.  
- Empirical results show significant performance gains compared to a modified ILP-based Gobnilp that includes linear constraints.  
- Paper is clearly written and explained, though the core idea is conceptually straightforward.  
- Novelty is limited because there is no standard solution for this specific problem (novelty score = 3).  
- Impact is limited since the problem setting is somewhat artificial; more general causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders may be more appropriate (impact = 2).  
- Certain logical rules (notably Rule 3 and Rule 4) appear problematic or incomplete—Rule 3’s symmetric premise vs asymmetric conclusion and Rule 4 excluding mutual non-ancestry cases.  
- The acyclicity condition and some rule formulations (lines cited) are not clearly recognizable or may be incorrect.  
- In practice, typically only a small number of ancestral constraints are provided, so they may have minimal effect on search-space reduction.  
- The comparison to modified Gobnilp is weak because Gobnilp is not designed for this problem; a more meaningful baseline would be a greedy method like GES with oracle input.  
- Theorem 1 needs to explicitly state that constraints A satisfied by CPDAG P on the EC tree are a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G for the iff to hold.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications under sparse ancestral constraints.  
- Practical comment: 10%–25% ancestral constraints imply extensive knowledge of the target graph and are atypical in realistic settings.  
- Minor wording/clarity fixes: clarify distinction between a standard and omniscient oracle (L30); rephrase "Empower the oracle" (L38); use "minimizing" instead of "optimizing" (L90).
