- Problem & contributions: Learns optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral constraints via EC-tree A* search, with node pruning and projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Main technical idea: Project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints to tighten the A* heuristic (oracle) and improve search efficiency.  
- Empirical result: Shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and that small amounts of expert knowledge can match large data increases in accuracy.  

Strengths:
- Significance/originality: Addresses a practical, important problem and introduces a novel idea of inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones.  
- Technical approach: Builds on solid exact-learning foundations; edge and ordering projection techniques are clever and technically sound.  
- Empirical performance: Strong experimental gains demonstrating substantial runtime improvements and effectiveness of expert knowledge.  

Weaknesses:
- Clarity (ordering constraints): Claim that "Z not ancestor of Y implies Y < Z" is misleading; the method finds maximal consistent ordering constraints, not logically entailed ones, and this distinction needs clarification.  
- Clarity (proof): Proof of Theorem 1 / Lemma 3 is hard to follow; the statement about EC tree edge generation (Gk contains Z -> W) needs more detailed justification.  
- Experimental baseline (architectural bias): A*-based methods naturally outperform ILP without bounded parent sets; experiments should include bounded in-degree to disentangle gains from the novel method versus A* advantages.  
- Experimental baseline (ILP encoding): The ILP encoding of ancestral constraints used for GOBNILP is suboptimal (cubic MAX-SAT style); a tighter ILP linking ancestor vars to parent-indicator vars would be stronger and may reduce the reported gap.  
- Missing related work: Omits van Beek & Hoffmann (2015) CP approach, which is relevant and competitive and should be discussed or compared.  

Summary / recommendation:
- Pro: Novel, elegant method with impressive empirical speedups.  
- Con: Needs clearer technical exposition and a stronger, fairer experimental comparison (better ILP encoding and CP baseline).  
- Overall: Strong paper recommended for acceptance if authors address clarity and baseline concerns.
