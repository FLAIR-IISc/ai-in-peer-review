This paper presents a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, allowing decomposable-score-based structure learning algorithms to incorporate domain knowledge and potentially improve learning efficiency. The primary novelty resides in Section 5, where the authors detail their approach to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into equivalent edge and topological ordering constraints. The paper is well-written and clearly explains both the theoretical contributions and experimental results. However, I have several concerns and suggestions for improvement.

i. Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether the benefits of projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints are specific to EC trees and do not generalize well to other learning methods. Given that the core innovation of this work is inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral ones, it would be more compelling to demonstrate that incorporating these decomposable constraints enhances the performance and/or efficiency of a learning approach that is *incapable* of directly handling the original non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 reveals an easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This is puzzling. Does this suggest that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space through ancestral constraints is particularly sensitive to the amount of incorporated domain knowledge, in contrast to the ILP approach? If so, careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints may be crucial for achieving optimal performance.

iii. Table 2 only reports the structural Hamming distance results for EC trees. A comparison with the ILP approach on this metric would provide a more complete picture of the results.

iv. While I understand the theoretical foundation of EC trees lies within Bayesian Networks (BNs), as EC trees represent an extension of BNs, the experiments focus solely on the EC tree search space with applied ancestral constraints. Therefore, I suggest removing Section 4.1 and allocating more space to a clearer explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm *within the specific context of the EC tree settings*.
