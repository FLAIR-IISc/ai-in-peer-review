- Addresses optimal Bayesian network configuration and extends Chen et al.’s framework to handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Key contribution: direct incorporation of ancestral constraints into structure learning.  
- Formulates the problem as a tailored MaxSAT instance to leverage SAT solvers.  
- Empirical results show significant runtime improvements, with orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP methods.  
- Results are compelling despite reviewer’s limited expertise in the specific approach.  
- Paper would benefit from a clearer discussion of limitations, especially scalability to very large networks.  
- Recommend investigating how performance degrades as the number of variables increases.  
- Suggest evaluating the impact of different constraint types on runtime.  
- Experimental details missing: specify SAT solver used, version, and key parameters for reproducibility.  
- Recommend assessing sensitivity of results to choice of SAT solver.  
- Suggest including comparisons to other structure-learning algorithms (e.g., score-based or greedy methods) beyond ILP.  
- Presentation issues: clarify figure y-axis units and reduce repetitive phrasing; overall a strong paper with recommendation for acceptance.
