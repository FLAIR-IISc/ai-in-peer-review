- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints prevents use of decomposable-score frameworks.  
- Framework: Builds on EC tree search which can handle non-decomposable scores via an "oracle" for decomposable parts.  
- Core contribution: Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to empower the oracle.  
- Methods: Provides an algorithm for inferring edge constraints and a MaxSAT formulation to infer a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints.  
- Empirical result: Shows orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over an ILP-based approach (GOBNILP).  
- Strength — Significance: Addresses a practical and important problem of incorporating expert causal/temporal knowledge into structure learning.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel and general strategy to convert global non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones for oracle-based search.  
- Strength — Quality: Technically sound, exact method with solid theoretical justification (Theorems 2–4) and extensive empirical evaluation.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is generally well-written with clear motivation and logical presentation of technical material.  
- Weakness — Experimental clarity: Presentation of experiments (Tables 1–2) is confusing and could be more direct to show projection benefits.  
- Weakness — Missing baseline: Lacks a critical ablation comparing EC tree search with and without passing projected constraints to the oracle.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant problem, novel idea, exact method with large empirical gains, and shows value of a few constraints on model accuracy.  
- Argument against acceptance: Experimental section missing the key ablation, making it hard to precisely quantify the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper recommended for acceptance, contingent on clarifying experiments and, if possible, adding the ablation baseline.
