- Tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable constraints, focusing on ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes a MaxSAT formulation that encodes standard structure/score components plus global constraints, building on Chen et al.  
- Empirically outperforms ILP baselines by large margins while producing optimal, constraint-consistent solutions.  
- Strong motivation: supports domain knowledge (e.g., ancestral relations) not handled by decomposable local constraints.  
- Technical contribution: principled MaxSAT encoding that appears general enough to capture other global constraints.  
- Reported empirical speedups over ILP are striking and suggest MaxSAT is a competitive substrate for constrained BN learning.  
- Lacks clarity in the encoding; needs explicit separation of base acyclicity/parent-set selection, scoring, and global constraints with concrete examples.  
- Claims of generality beyond ancestral constraints are under-supported; more examples and characterization of other non-decomposable constraints are needed.  
- Evaluation fairness concerns: ILP baseline versions/settings, parent-set bounds, scoring functions, and hardware/time limits must be precisely specified.  
- Unclear scalability boundaries; requires sensitivity analysis over number of variables, max parent-set size, and number/type of global constraints.  
- Theoretical soundness/completeness and optimality guarantees should be stated formally, with any assumptions made explicit.  
- Practical utility needs demonstration via a case study showing how ancestral constraints change learned structures in meaningful ways.  
- Requests details on MaxSAT solver(s), configurations, and any solver-specific heuristics or preprocessing used.  
- Asks for clarity on scoring functions and priors used and how equivalence across baselines was ensured.  
- Queries about parent-set bounds and their effect on solution quality and runtime compared to ILP.  
- Requests examples and empirical results for non-ancestral, non-decomposable constraints supported by the encoding.  
- Wants information on handling inconsistent or nearly inconsistent constraints, early infeasibility detection, and support for soft constraints with penalties.  
- Suggests releasing code to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Recommends adding a worked example of the MaxSAT encoding for ancestral constraints and including ablations (base vs ancestral, varying constraint counts).  
- Advises reporting memory usage and time-to-first-feasible vs time-to-optimal, and expanding related work to include CP/SAT/ASP and how this encoding differs from prior approaches.  
- Overall: well-motivated, technically solid, and empirically promising work that needs clearer exposition and a more thorough, balanced evaluation to strengthen claims of generality and scalability.
