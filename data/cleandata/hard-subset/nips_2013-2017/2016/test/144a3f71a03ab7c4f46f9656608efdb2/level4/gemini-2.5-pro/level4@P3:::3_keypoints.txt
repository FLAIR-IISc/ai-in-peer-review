- Summary: Proposes an algorithm to learn optimal Bayesian network structure under user-provided ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for use in an EC tree search oracle.
- Summary: Introduces a MaxSAT formulation to infer topological ordering constraints and shows large empirical speedups over an ILP-based baseline.

- Strength: Originality — novel method to convert non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints enabling use of efficient existing solvers.
- Strength: Technical quality — sound use of EC tree search, clever MaxSAT reduction, formal results (Theorems 1–4), and solid empirical comparisons showing substantial performance gains.
- Strength: Clarity — paper is well-written, clearly organizes the algorithm-oracle interaction and explains the main ideas.

- Weakness: Problem positioning — needs broader discussion of relation to causal discovery and limitations when unobserved confounders or alternative models (e.g., MAGs) are relevant.
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation clarity — the acyclicity hard constraint appears asymmetric and needs justification; the soft constraint `(X 6 Y) ⇒ (Y < X)` can conflict under mutual non-ancestry and requires explanation of solver handling.
- Weakness: Experimental baseline — should compare runtime and solution quality to a strong heuristic baseline (e.g., GES modified to respect ancestral constraints) to contextualize practical efficiency.

- Minor: Make "oracle" vs "omniscient oracle" terminology consistent or clarify any difference.
- Minor: State explicitly that optimizing the score corresponds to minimizing it (L90).
- Minor/Theorem 1: Clarify the precise scope of Theorem 1 regarding which constraints descendants are guaranteed to satisfy.
- Minor/Theorem 3: Comment on practical utility since inference of positive edge constraints requires strong conditions across all other variables, which may be limiting when prior knowledge is sparse.
- Minor: Provide experiments with smaller percentages of known ancestral relations to show performance scaling with amount of background knowledge.
