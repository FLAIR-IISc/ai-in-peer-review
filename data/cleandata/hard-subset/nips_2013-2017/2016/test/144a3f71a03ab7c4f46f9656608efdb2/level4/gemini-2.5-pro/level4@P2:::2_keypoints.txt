- Addresses learning Bayesian network structure with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that break standard decomposable-score methods.  
- Builds on EC-tree A*-search over equivalence classes and proposes projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Introduces a sound algorithm to infer required/forbidden edges from ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes a novel MaxSAT reduction to infer a maximal, globally consistent set of ordering constraints.  
- Uses inferred decomposable constraints to empower the heuristic oracle in A*, producing a much tighter lower bound and faster search.  
- Empirically outperforms an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) by orders of magnitude and scales up to ~20 variables for exact learning.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance: ancestral constraints are common expert knowledge and can greatly improve learned model accuracy and search efficiency.  
- Strength: novel, principled projection idea that bridges non-decomposable constraints and efficient decomposable solvers.  
- Strength: technically sound methods with appropriate theoretical results (Theorems 1–4) and well-motivated MaxSAT formulation.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation lacks clarity—key ablation (`EC tree` vs `EC tree + projection`) is split across tables making impact hard to read.  
- Weakness: Table 3’s setup/identity (whether it reports `EC tree + projection`) is ambiguous and should be explicitly labeled.  
- Weakness: framing overemphasizes win over slower ILP; should focus on how much projection improves the best existing method (EC tree).  
- Detailed: paper is high quality, generally clear and original, and significant for making exact structure learning with ancestral knowledge more scalable.
