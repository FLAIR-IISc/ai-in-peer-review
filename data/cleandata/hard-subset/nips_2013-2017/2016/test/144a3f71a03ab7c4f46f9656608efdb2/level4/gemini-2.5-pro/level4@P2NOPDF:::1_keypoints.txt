- Proposes branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs for Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral constraints, using symmetry-breaking and a heuristic from URLearning (details cited elsewhere).

- Main contribution: extra pruning from ancestral constraints supported by Lemma 3, but the lemma’s proof is hard to follow and one claim about EC tree edges needs stronger justification.

- Uses “projected constraints” (implied constraints) and infers ordering constraints, but the inference description is imprecise and underdeveloped; MAXSAT-based inference procedure is insufficiently explained.

- Experimental results show the method is faster than GOBNILP, but the comparison is weakened because A* methods naturally excel without parent-set cardinality limits, which the experiments allow.

- The GOBNILP baseline appears handicapped by a suboptimal MAXSAT encoding of ancestry; a tighter ILP formulation (e.g., Cussens 2010 with ancestry variables and transitivity constraints) would likely improve its performance.

- The paper omits discussion and comparison to the competitive CP-based approach by van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), which should be acknowledged and evaluated.

- Overall assessment: promising idea, but clarity issues in theoretical/methodological exposition and a weak experimental baseline undermine the strength of the paper.
