- Novel contribution: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to enable decomposable-score-based structure learning.  
- Main technical innovation presented in Section 5: method to convert ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical and experimental explanations.  
- Concern i: Table 3 shows projection improves EC trees but not ILP, raising questions about generalizability to other learning methods and whether decomposable constraints help methods that cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern ii: Table 2 shows an easy-hard-easy pattern for EC trees as constraint proportion increases, requiring explanation and suggesting sensitivity/tuning of the number of ancestral constraints.  
- Concern iii: Table 2 lacks a structural Hamming distance comparison with the ILP approach; adding this would give a more complete performance picture.  
- Suggestion iv: Consider removing Section 4.1 and reallocating space to better explain EC trees and the A* algorithm in that context.
