- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into a maximal set of ordering constraints.  
- Integrates these ordering constraints into a score-based learner using the EC tree structure (Chen et al., 2015).  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to derive the complete set of ordering constraints implied by initial ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation versus a modified ILP-based Gobnilp shows the proposed method performs significantly better on this task.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained, though conceptually straightforward and aimed at a specialist audience.  
- The problem setting (learning with ancestral constraints) is novel but may be somewhat artificial; general causal discovery methods that handle latent confounders might be more natural in many applications.  
- Some translation rules (lines 257–261) appear questionable or incomplete (e.g., acyclicity rule 3 is asymmetric in conclusion despite symmetric premise; rule 4 may prevent expressing mutual non-ancestry).  
- With typically few ancestral constraints in practice, the translation may not prune the search space enough to yield big gains.  
- Comparing only to a modified Gobnilp (not designed for this task) is limited; a greedy baseline like GES would be a more informative benchmark despite its non-guarantees.  
- Theorem 1 (line 164) needs precision: the set of ancestral constraints satisfied by a CPDAG in the EC tree is a subset of those satisfied by a full DAG, so the stated iff may not hold.  
- Theorem 3 (lines 225–227) likely overstates the number of compelled edges inferable from a small, sparse set of ancestral constraints.  
- Acyclicity formulation (line 267) is unintuitive and relates to the concerns about rule 3.  
- Experiments use a large fraction of ancestral constraints (10–25% of pairs), implying unrealistic prior knowledge for typical moderately sized graphs.  
- Minor clarifications requested: distinguish "standard oracle" vs "omniscient oracle" (line 30); clarify "empower the oracle" phrasing (line 38); state that "optimizing" the score means minimizing (line 90).
