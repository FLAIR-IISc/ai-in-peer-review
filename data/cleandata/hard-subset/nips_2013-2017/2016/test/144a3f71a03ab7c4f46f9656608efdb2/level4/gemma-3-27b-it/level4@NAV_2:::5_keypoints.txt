- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral constraints, extending Chen et al.'s non-decomposable score framework.
- Infers decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to leverage decomposable-score oracles.
- Formulates constraint projection as a MaxSAT problem to efficiently find additional useful decomposable constraints.
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based formulations.
- Experiments vary network size, dataset size, and constraint percentage, strengthening empirical conclusions.
- Analysis includes structural Hamming distance, showing improved model accuracy.
- Finds that a small number of ancestral constraints can substantially improve accuracy, comparable to increasing dataset size.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and technically thorough (EC trees, constraint projection).
- Strong theoretical foundation supported by Theorems 1–4.
- Connection to MaxSAT is clever and well-executed.
- Weakness: limited discussion of recent advances in ILP and CP related work.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to simulated data; lacks real-world dataset experiments.
- Weakness: brief description of MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) configuration and performance details.
- Weakness: scalability claims (up to n=20) need more detail on memory usage and computational limits.
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address the points above.
