- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, incompatible with standard decomposable-score methods.  
- Proposes translating ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints handled by existing oracles.  
- Leverages the EC tree search space (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) with pruning and oracle empowerment via inferred decomposable constraints.  
- Uses MaxSAT to compute the translation/projection of ancestral constraints into ordering constraints.  
- Provides a formal framework and proves soundness of the pruning approach (Theorem 1).  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude empirical speedups over ILP-based methods like GOBNILP across benchmarks.  
- Shows that small percentages (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can substantially improve learning accuracy.  
- Builds on prior optimal structure learning work but extends it novelly to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Technically sound with clear methodological contribution and rigorous evaluation.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with effective use of examples and explanation of EC tree representation.  
- Some MaxSAT technical details (Section 5.2) need clearer exposition for readers unfamiliar with constraint techniques.  
- Should better explain why causal discovery methods with unobserved confounders do not suffice for this setting.  
- The claim that 10–25% ancestral constraints imply extensive graph knowledge is questionable and needs justification.  
- Additional comparisons (e.g., against GES) would provide more context for empirical efficiency claims.  
- Strengths: novel practical solution, strong theory and proofs, comprehensive experiments, and solid implementation.  
- Weaknesses: conceptually straightforward idea, some unclear technical details, and debatable claims about the practical informativeness of constraint percentages.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid contribution, technically sound, and practically significant for NIPS.
