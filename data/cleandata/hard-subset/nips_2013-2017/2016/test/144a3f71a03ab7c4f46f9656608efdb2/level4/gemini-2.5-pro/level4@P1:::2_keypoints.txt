- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures from data with non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on A* search over equivalence-class (EC) tree and integrates ancestral constraints into that search.  
- Main contributions: a sound EC-tree pruning rule and a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Infers ordering constraints via a reduction to MaxSAT to obtain a maximal consistent set.  
- Passing inferred decomposable constraints to the heuristic yields a tighter A* heuristic while preserving exactness (optimality).  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over the ILP baseline GOBNILP.  
- Constraint projection is the primary driver of performance gains; even few ancestral constraints substantially improve model accuracy.  
- Strengths: tackles an important practical problem, presents a novel projection technique, is technically sound, and uses an elegant MaxSAT solution.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation and organization are unclear, which obscures the core contribution.  
- Suggestion: present a side-by-side ablation comparing EC tree with vs. without projection to clearly show projection’s impact.  
- Current table setup (Table 1 vs Table 2 comparisons) is confusing and mixes comparisons across methods, making the main ablation hard to follow.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and well-supported paper with minor presentation issues that are easily fixable.
