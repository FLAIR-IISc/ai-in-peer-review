- Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into score-based Bayesian network learning by projecting them into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints.  
- Novel projection method with clear theoretical foundations and potentially significant technical contribution (notably Section 5).  
- Paper is well-written, motivated, and presents a clear empirical evaluation.  
- Primary concern: projection improves EC tree performance but not ILP (Table 3), raising questions about generalizability.  
- Authors should demonstrate benefits on learning algorithms that originally could not handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints to support broad applicability.  
- EC tree exhibits an “easy-hard-easy” performance pattern as constraint proportion varies (Table 2); this sensitivity needs investigation and guidance on choosing constraint levels.  
- Need analysis of whether intermediate constraint proportions over-constrain the search space and why performance degrades.  
- Evaluation currently focuses only on SHD for EC trees; compare SHD between EC tree and ILP to assess relative benefits of projection.  
- Suggest removing or shortening Section 4.1 on general Bayesian networks and reallocating space to a detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* implementation.  
- Request clearer description of how projected constraints interact with A* search, how the heuristic is affected, and the practical impact of reduced search space.
