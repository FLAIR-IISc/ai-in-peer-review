- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning with ancestral (background) constraints, an important practical problem.
- Core idea: convert non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints and use them in an EC tree-based oracle (building on Chen et al.).
- Uses MaxSAT to project ancestral constraints into ordering constraints within the EC tree framework.
- Empirical results show significant computational efficiency gains over an ILP-based baseline (GOBNILP).
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains methodology and prior work.
- Originality is moderate: novel integration of existing components (EC trees, MaxSAT, ancestral constraints) rather than new foundational techniques (Originality: 3/5).
- Significance is limited by reliance on having a substantial number of strong ancestral constraints in practice (Significance: 2/5).
- Technical soundness is generally strong, but the constraint-projection rules (lines 257–261) appear potentially incomplete or asymmetric and need stronger justification.
- Specific concerns: the acyclicity rule’s symmetry is questionable, and rule 4’s exclusion of mutual non-causality may be overly restrictive.
- The approach’s effectiveness depends on the (sometimes unrealistic) availability of many ancestral constraints; the discussion of this assumption should be expanded (lines 310–312).
- Clarity notes: minor phrasing improvements suggested (e.g., replace “empower the oracle”), and Theorem 1 should clarify CPDAG constraint relationships to DAGs in its equivalence class.
- Empirical evaluation is convincing against ILP but could be strengthened by adding a more relevant baseline such as a greedy algorithm (e.g., GES) with oracle input.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, technically sound and novel integration, strong empirical speedups, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical applicability due to constraint assumptions, incomplete justification of projection rules, and need for broader experimental baselines.
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing completeness/justification of projection rules and possibly adding an additional baseline comparison.
