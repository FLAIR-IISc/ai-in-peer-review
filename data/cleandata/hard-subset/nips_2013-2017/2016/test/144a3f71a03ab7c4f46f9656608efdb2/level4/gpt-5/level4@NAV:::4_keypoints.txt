- Contribution: Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-order constraints (Section 5) so decomposable score–based learners can use ancestral knowledge.  
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written and effectively presents theory and experiments.  
- Concern 1: Table 3 shows projection helps EC-tree but not ILP, raising doubts about whether the projection generalizes beyond EC-tree learners and whether it benefits methods that cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern for EC-tree as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; the cause is unclear and suggests EC-tree pruning may be highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge and may require careful tuning.  
- Concern 3: Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance (SHD) only for EC trees—need SHD comparison versus the ILP approach under the same conditions.  
- Suggestion: Since experiments focus on EC-tree search with ancestral constraints, consider dropping Section 4.1 and reallocating space to better explain EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree context.
