- Presents an efficient method for incorporating ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into maximal ordering constraints and using an EC-tree score-based algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make all implied ancestral constraints explicit when converting to ordering constraints.  
- Empirical results show significant outperformance of a modified ILP-based Gobnilp baseline in the tested setting.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, though the solution is conceptually straightforward.  
- Problem is novel and of interest to specialists (novelty score 3) but the setting is somewhat artificial and limited in practical impact (impact score 2).  
- In practice users typically provide only few ancestral constraints, so the method may not substantially reduce the search space.  
- Some encoding rules appear incomplete or inconsistent (e.g., symmetric premise but asymmetric conclusion in acyclicity rule 3; rule 4 excludes some ancestral constraints).  
- The comparison to Gobnilp is not entirely fair since Gobnilp is not designed for this specific problem.  
- A more meaningful baseline would be a greedy algorithm like GES (assuming constraints align with the optimal model) to judge practical efficiency.  
- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that the EC-tree CPDAG constraints are a subset of constraints satisfied by the full DAG G.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to yield many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Notation issues: line 267’s notation for 'acyclic' is not easily recognizable.  
- Minor wording/clarity fixes suggested: distinguish standard vs omniscient oracle (line 30), clarify “empower the oracle” (line 38), and note optimizing as minimizing (line 90).  
- For reasonably sized graphs, 10–25% ancestral constraints already imply extensive knowledge of the target, which is atypical in real applications.
