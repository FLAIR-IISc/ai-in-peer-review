The paper "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints" presents an efficient approach to incorporate ancestral constraints in finding optimal Bayesian networks from data. The authors translate a set of ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints, which are then used in a score-based learning algorithm based on the EC tree. This approach is evaluated against a straightforward modification of the ILP-based Gobnilp algorithm and shown to outperform it significantly.

The problem tackled in this paper is interesting and new, as optimal Bayesian network learning in combination with ancestral constraints has no readily available 'standard solution'. However, it is worth noting that the situation is somewhat artificial, as ancestral relations often imply the need for more general causal discovery algorithms that also allow for unobserved confounders.

The approach taken is based on translating hard-to-use ancestral constraints into sets of ordering constraints that are more easily expressed in terms of absence of edges, which fit decomposable scores. The mapping is done through a MaxSAT solver, which ensures that all implied ancestral constraints are made explicit. However, some of the rules seem strange or incomplete, and it is unclear how they would perform in practice with only a few ancestral constraints.

The comparison against Gobnilp with added linear constraints looks impressive, but it is not clear if this is a fair comparison, as Gobnilp is not developed to tackle this problem. A more meaningful indication of the efficiency achieved by the method in the paper would be to compare against a greedy algorithm like GES, which for oracle input should produce the same output.

The paper is well-written and clearly explained, although the solution is conceptually straightforward. The results are of interest to a specialist audience, and the approach is novel and useful. However, the impact of the paper is somewhat limited by the artificial nature of the problem setting.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to incorporating ancestral constraints in Bayesian network learning
* Efficient algorithm for translating ancestral constraints into ordering constraints
* Good performance compared to Gobnilp

Weaknesses:

* Artificial problem setting that may not reflect real-world scenarios
* Some rules seem strange or incomplete
* Limited comparison to other algorithms
