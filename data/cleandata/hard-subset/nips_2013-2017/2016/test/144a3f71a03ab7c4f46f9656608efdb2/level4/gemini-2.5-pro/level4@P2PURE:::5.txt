This paper presents an approach for learning the optimal structure of a Bayesian network. The key contribution is an extension of the structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] to incorporate non-decomposable constraints, with a particular focus on ancestral constraints. To achieve this, the authors formulate the structure learning task as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) problem.

The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed approach is significantly more efficient than competing Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods, achieving runtime reductions of several orders of magnitude. While the reviewer has limited expertise with MaxSAT-based formulations, these performance gains appear very impressive.
