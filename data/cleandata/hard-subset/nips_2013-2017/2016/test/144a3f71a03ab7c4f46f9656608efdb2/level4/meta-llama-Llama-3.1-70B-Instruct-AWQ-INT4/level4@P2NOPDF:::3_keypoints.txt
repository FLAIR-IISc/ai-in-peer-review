- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into maximal ordering constraints and using a score-based EC tree algorithm with MaxSAT.
- Shows strong empirical performance versus a modified ILP-based Gobnilp.
- Paper is well-written and clear, though the solution is conceptually straightforward.
- Problem is novel but somewhat artificial; ancestral constraints often call for causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders, so impact may be limited to specialists.
- Mapping ancestral constraints to ordering/absent-edge constraints fits decomposable scores and uses MaxSAT to make implied constraints explicit.
- Some presented rules (e.g., 257–261) seem incomplete or incorrect: acyclicity rule 3 has an asymmetric conclusion despite a symmetric premise, and rule 4 may wrongly exclude certain ancestral constraints.
- In practice, only a few ancestral constraints are typically available, so the method may not substantially reduce the search space.
- Comparison to Gobnilp is not fully appropriate because Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this problem; a comparison to greedy methods like GES would be more informative.
- Minor issues to address: clarify the difference between standard and omniscient oracle (p.30); rephrase “empower the oracle” (p.38); state that optimizing means minimizing (p.90).
- Theorem/notation concerns: Theorem 1 should state the CPDAG-satisfied constraints are a subset of DAG-satisfied constraints; Theorem 3 may yield few positive edge implications with sparse constraints; notation on p.267 doesn’t clearly capture acyclicity.
- Practical note: 10–25% ancestral constraints would imply extensive knowledge of the target graph, which is uncommon in real applications.
- Overall: novel and useful for a niche setting, but impact limited and evaluation should include more suitable baselines.
