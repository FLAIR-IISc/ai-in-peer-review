- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral constraints that are non-decomposable and incompatible with standard score-based algorithms.  
- Extends Chen et al.'s EC-tree A*-like search framework that uses an oracle for a decomposable subproblem.  
- Core contribution: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by the oracle.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to infer a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints as part of the projection.  
- Projection yields a much tighter heuristic for the oracle, enabling more aggressive pruning of the search space.  
- Empirical results: method is orders of magnitude faster than a state-of-the-art ILP-based exact approach.  
- Constraint projection is critical to the observed runtime improvements and scalability to previously infeasible problem sizes.  
- Approach is elegant, well-motivated, and generalizable—translates global constraints into local ones for powerful solvers.  
- Reviewer notes limited personal expertise with MaxSAT but finds the MaxSAT-driven results compelling.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant, well-supported contributions and thorough empirical validation.
