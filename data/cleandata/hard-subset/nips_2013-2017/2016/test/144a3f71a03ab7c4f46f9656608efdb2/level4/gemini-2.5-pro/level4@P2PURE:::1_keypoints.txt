- Proposes branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs to learn Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, using symmetry-breaking and a URLearning-derived heuristic.

- Core contribution: pruning the search space via ancestral constraints formalized in Lemma 3; lemma seems correct but its proof is hard to follow and needs clearer justification (notably the "Gk contains edge Z -> W" claim).

- Uses "projected" (implied) constraints for extra pruning, but the procedure for inferring ordering constraints is inadequately explained and ambiguous (e.g., "can infer Y < Z" from "Z not an ancestor of Y").

- Authors appear to select consistent ordering constraints via a MAXSAT approach, but this crucial step must be explicitly clarified and justified.

- Experimental results show large speedups over GOBNILP, but this may be confounded because GOBNILP is known to be weaker than A*-based methods in the unlimited parent-set setting used.

- The GOBNILP baseline was encoded using a MAXSAT-based formulation (Cussens 2008) that is weak for ILP; a stronger ILP encoding exists (Cussens 2010 Sec. 3.1.1) and should have been used for fairer comparison.

- While the proposed method might still outperform GOBNILP with a stronger ILP encoding, the current evaluation’s strength is diminished by the suboptimal baseline choice.

- The paper omits citation and empirical comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), a constraint-programming approach that outperforms GOBNILP and could likely handle ancestral constraints—this is a notable omission.
