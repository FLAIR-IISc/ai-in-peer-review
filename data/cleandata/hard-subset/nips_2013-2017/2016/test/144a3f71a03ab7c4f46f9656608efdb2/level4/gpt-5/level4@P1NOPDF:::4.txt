This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints so that decomposable score–based structure-learning methods can incorporate domain knowledge and thereby improve learning efficiency. The main novelty lies in Section 5, where the authors project ancestral constraints into edge constraints and topological-ordering constraints, both of which are decomposable. The paper is clearly written and presents both the theoretical development and the experiments well. I have the following concerns and suggestions:

i. Table 3 shows that projecting ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. Does this mean that projection to decomposable constraints is effective only for EC trees and has limited generalization to other learning methods? Since the central contribution is inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating the projected constraints improves the performance and efficiency of at least one learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy–hard–easy pattern in EC-tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. Why does this occur? Does it suggest that the benefit of search-space reduction via ancestral constraints is quite sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge in EC trees, especially compared to the ILP approach? If so, does the number of ancestral constraints require careful tuning to achieve good performance?

iii. Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance (SHD) only for EC trees. How do these results compare with the ILP approach under the same conditions?

iv. A minor suggestion: I understand that the BN graph provides the theoretical foundation for EC trees, which extend it. However, since the experiments focus solely on the EC-tree search space with ancestral constraints, I would consider dropping Section 4.1 and using the space to better explain EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree setting.
