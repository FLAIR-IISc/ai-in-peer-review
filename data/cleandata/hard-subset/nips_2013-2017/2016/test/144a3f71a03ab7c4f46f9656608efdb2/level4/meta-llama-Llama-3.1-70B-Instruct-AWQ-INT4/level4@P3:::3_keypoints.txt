- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints when learning optimal Bayesian networks.
- Tackles a novel problem with no standard existing solution.
- Approach is conceptually straightforward but significant in results.
- Translates ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints for use in an EC-tree score-based learner.
- Uses an oracle empowered by decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Develops a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints.
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude performance improvements over the ILP-based Gobnilp algorithm.
- Evaluation lacks comparisons to other algorithms (e.g., greedy methods like GES).
- Practical implications (typical number of ancestral constraints and their effect on search space) are not fully explored.
- Minor clarity issues: distinction between standard and omniscient oracle and phrasing like "empower the oracle" could be improved.
- Paper is well-written and accessible to a specialist audience.
- Reviewer scores: Originality 3, Impact 2, Quality 4, Clarity 4, Significance 3.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem/solution, strong empirical performance, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited algorithmic comparisons, somewhat artificial setting, and minor clarity problems.
