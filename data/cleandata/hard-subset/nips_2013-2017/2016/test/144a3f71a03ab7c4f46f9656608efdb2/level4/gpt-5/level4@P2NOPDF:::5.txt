This paper addresses the problem of optimal Bayesian network structure learning. The authors extend the approach of Chen et al. (2015, 2016) to accommodate non-decomposable constraints, with a focus on ancestral constraints, and reformulate the task as a MaxSAT problem with carefully designed constraints. The empirical evaluation against ILP-based methods shows substantial runtime gains, often by orders of magnitude. Although my expertise with MaxSAT-based formulations is limited, the reported comparisons appear sound and the performance improvements are striking. Overall, the work presents a clear and effective way to incorporate challenging constraints into Bayesian network learning while achieving significant computational benefits over existing alternatives.
