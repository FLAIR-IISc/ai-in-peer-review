- Proposes an exact A*-based search over EC trees (CPDAGs) for Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral (path) constraints with sound pruning of infeasible classes.
- Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge-presence/absence and ordering constraints to strengthen a decomposable-score oracle.
- Infers entailed edge constraints via rule-based closure and extracts many entailed orderings via a MaxSAT reduction.
- Empirical results show large speedups over the ILP solver GOBNILP on several benchmarks.
- Builds on prior EC-tree/BN graph frameworks (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) and contrasts with ILP/CP approaches that can encode ancestral constraints.
- Strength: tackles a practically important class of non-decomposable constraints that current decomposable-score methods cannot enforce directly.
- Strength: provides sound pruning conditions and clear criteria for when a CPDAG satisfies ancestral constraints.
- Strength: introduces a novel, well-motivated MaxSAT-based projection to derive ordering constraints.
- Strength: consistent empirical benefits from projected constraints across experiments.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are unclear—tables do not explicitly state which components (ancestral, projected edges, projected orderings, oracle inputs) are enabled.
- Weakness: missing critical ablations, particularly EC with projection vs EC without projection, and breakdown of projection overhead vs search.
- Weakness: limited scalability—experiments only up to n≈20; unclear memory/runtime bottlenecks (CPDAG enumeration, MaxSAT size).
- Weakness: no robustness analysis for noisy or inconsistent ancestral constraints or cost/guarantees of inconsistency detection/repair.
- Weakness: minor presentation issues—typos, dangling footnotes, inconsistent notation hindering readability.
- NIPS-quality assessment: algorithms and pruning are sound and original; experiments reasonable but need clearer setup, ablations, and robustness/overhead analyses.
- Requested clarification: explicitly state per-table which components are active (EC vs GOBNILP, ancestral constraints, projected edges/orderings, oracle input, time/memory/family bounds).
- Requested clarification: add ablations (EC + ancestral only, +edge projection, +ordering projection, +both) and report counts/effects of projected constraints on oracle runtime.
- Requested clarification: report projection overhead (closure and MaxSAT times) and total runtime composition.
- Requested clarification: evaluate robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints and describe inconsistency detection/handling.
- Requested clarification: clarify when CPDAG pruning requires enumerating class(P), report frequency/cost and average/maximum enumerations.
- Requested clarification: discuss scalability limits and memory consumption; consider experiments with larger n and/or bounded parent sets.
- Pros for acceptance: principled exact handling of ancestral constraints, elegant projection leveraging decomposable oracles, and substantial empirical speedups.
- Cons for acceptance: confusing experimental presentation, missing ablations and overhead/robustness analyses, and unquantified reliance on potentially expensive CPDAG-class enumeration.
- Overall: strong and timely contribution that would be substantially strengthened by clearer experiments, targeted ablations (especially EC+projection vs EC), and robustness/scalability analyses.
