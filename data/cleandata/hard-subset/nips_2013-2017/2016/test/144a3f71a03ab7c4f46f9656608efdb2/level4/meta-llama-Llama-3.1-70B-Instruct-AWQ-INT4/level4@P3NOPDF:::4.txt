This paper proposes a novel approach to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning methods to incorporate domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The primary contribution of this work lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable.

The paper is well-written, clearly illustrating the theoretical and experimental aspects of the work. However, I have some concerns and suggestions for improvement.

Firstly, Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints only improves the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. This raises questions about the generalizability of the proposed method. Does the projection of ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints only work for EC trees, and does it have limited applicability to other learning approaches? Given that the main innovation of this paper is to infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating such decomposable constraints improves the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

Secondly, Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This phenomenon warrants further explanation. Does it suggest that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, compared to the ILP approach? If so, does this imply that the number of ancestral constraints needs to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance?

Thirdly, while Table 2 presents results on the structural Hamming distance for EC trees, it would be beneficial to include a comparison with the ILP approach. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

Lastly, I offer a minor suggestion. Although the BN graph provides theoretical foundations for EC trees, the experimental section primarily focuses on the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints applied. Considering this, I would recommend removing Section 4.1 and allocating more space to explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm in the context of EC trees. This would enhance the clarity and focus of the paper.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field, and addressing these concerns would further strengthen the work.
