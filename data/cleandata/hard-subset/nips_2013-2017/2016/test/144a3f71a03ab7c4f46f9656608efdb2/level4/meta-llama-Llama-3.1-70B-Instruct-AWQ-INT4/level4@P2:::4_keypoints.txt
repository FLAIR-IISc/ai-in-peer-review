- Proposes a method for learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by leveraging a framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores.  
- Main contribution (Section 5): a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Projection enables using decomposable-score-based oracles and incorporating domain knowledge into structure learning.  
- Demonstrates how to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints to make the problem tractable.  
- Experiments on standard BN benchmarks vary number of variables, dataset size, and proportion of ancestral constraints.  
- Results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based GOBNILP; the EC tree approach is consistently faster.  
- The projection of constraints has a significant impact on learning efficiency.  
- Concern: projection may only be effective for the EC tree approach and might not generalize to other learning methods like ILP.  
- Concern: an unexplained easy–hard–easy performance pattern appears as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases, suggesting possible sensitivity to constraint proportion.  
- Concern: comparisons with ILP focus on structural Hamming distance; additional metrics (runtime, model accuracy) should be reported.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, technically sound, and experimentally thorough.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address generalizability, explain the easy–hard–easy pattern, and expand comparisons.
