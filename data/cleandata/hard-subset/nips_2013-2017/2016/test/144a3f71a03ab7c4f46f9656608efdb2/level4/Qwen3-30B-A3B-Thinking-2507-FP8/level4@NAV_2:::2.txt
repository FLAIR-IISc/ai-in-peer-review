

The authors present a novel approach for learning Bayesian network structures under ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and typically challenging for standard structure learning methods. Building on Chen et al.'s framework for non-decomposable scores, the paper introduces a method to project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, enabling the use of efficient oracles for decomposable scores. The approach leverages the EC tree search space, which allows for sound pruning based on ancestral constraints while maintaining the ability to utilize existing structure learning oracles. 

The paper demonstrates significant empirical advantages over ILP-based methods, with results showing orders-of-magnitude improvements in efficiency across various problem sizes. The experimental design is well-considered, with careful evaluation of how ancestral constraints affect search efficiency and scalability. The paper effectively addresses the problem of integrating non-decomposable background knowledge into optimal structure learning, which is an important practical consideration in real-world applications where domain knowledge is often available.

The main weakness lies in the presentation of experimental results. Table 1 compares (EC + new work) against (GOB + new work'), while Table 3 appears to compare EC against GOB without the new constraints. This creates some confusion about the relative benefits of the proposed constraint projection method. A clearer presentation would explicitly compare (EC + new work) against EC alone, as this would better demonstrate the specific contribution of the constraint projection technique. While the paper shows EC is substantially better than GOB regardless of the constraints, it would strengthen the argument to isolate the impact of the constraint projection method on EC's performance.

Strengths:
- Addresses an important practical problem (integrating non-decomposable ancestral constraints)
- Provides a theoretically sound method for projecting constraints
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over existing methods
- Well-motivated and well-structured paper

Weaknesses:
- Experimental comparisons could be presented more clearly to isolate the contribution of constraint projection
- The paper could better emphasize how the constraint projection method differs from existing approaches to constraint handling

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field by enabling efficient structure learning with ancestral constraints, which was previously challenging for most mainstream approaches. The results are significant, showing that background knowledge can have a comparable impact on model accuracy as substantially larger datasets. The approach advances the state of the art in Bayesian network structure learning by expanding the range of background knowledge that can be incorporated into optimal structure learning procedures.
