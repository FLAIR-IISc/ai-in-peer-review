This paper proposes a method for converting non-decisive parent constraints into decisive constraints, so that decision-score-based learning approaches can incorporate domain knowledge of such domains and thereby improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper lies mostly in Section 5, where the authors present their proposed method for converting non-decisive parent constraints into edge and topological order constraints, which are decisive. The paper is well-written and clearly demonstrates their work in both theoretical and experimental sections. My concerns and suggestions are as follows: i. Table 3 shows that projecting parent constraints can only improve the performance of EC trees, but not in the ILP approach. Does this mean that projecting parent constraints into decisive constraints only works for EC trees and has poor generalization capacity for other learning approaches? Considering the main innovation of this paper to be inferring decisive constraints from non-decisive parent constraints, it would be more reliable if the authors could show that incorporating such decisive constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of some learning approaches that are not originally capable of handling non-decisive constraints. ii. Table 2 shows an easy-difficult-easy pattern on EC tree performance with an increase in the proportion of parent constraints. Why does this happen? Does this mean that the effect of reducing the search space of EC trees with parent constraints is significantly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge included compared to the ILP approach? Therefore, the number of parent constraints needs to be carefully adjusted to achieve ideal performance? iii. Table 2 only shows the results of structural Hamming distance from EC trees. But, how does this compare with the ILP approach? iv. A small suggestion: I understand that the theoretical basis of EC trees is in BN graphs because EC trees expand from it. However, since the experimental section only considers the search space of EC trees with applied parent constraints, I will skip section 4.1 and spend more time explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm in EC tree settings.
