- Addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs.  
- Search progression models moving from CPDAG1 to CPDAG2 by selecting a parent set for a variable not yet present.  
- Employs effective symmetry breaking in the search.  
- Uses A* search with a heuristic from the URLearning system (implementation described in prior work).  
- Leverages ancestral constraints to prune the search tree, with Lemma 3 being central to this pruning.  
- Lemma 3’s proof (supplementary material) lacks clarity—specifically the claim “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs more explanation.  
- Considers implied/projected constraints and ordering constraints, but the derivation and terminology are not fully explained.  
- The claim “we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is imprecise—Z not being an ancestor allows adding Y<Z but does not entail it.  
- Authors use MAXSAT to infer many consistent ordering constraints while avoiding over-constraining the search.  
- Experiments (varying variables, data, constraints) show large speedups over GOBNILP, but the comparison is unclear because GOBNILP degrades without parent-set cardinality limits.  
- The supplemental encoding of ancestral constraints uses a MAXSAT formulation (Cussens 2008) that is suboptimal for ILP; a better ILP would use binary A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables to avoid cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables and tighten the relaxation.  
- Even with a better ILP encoding the authors’ method would likely outperform GOBNILP, but comparing to a suboptimal encoding weakens the empirical claim.  
- Missing citation and empirical comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to ancestral constraints.
