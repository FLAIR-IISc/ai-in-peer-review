This paper tackles the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network configuration. The authors show how to incorporate non-decomposable constraints—particularly ancestral constraints—into the structure-learning approach of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. They reformulate the task as a MaxSAT problem with specific constraints. In empirical comparisons against ILP-based methods, their approach achieves orders-of-magnitude improvements. While my expertise with this approach is limited, the reported runtime results appear quite impressive, demonstrating reductions by orders of magnitude relative to alternative methods.
