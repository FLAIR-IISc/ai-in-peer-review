- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs with iterative parent-set expansion.  
- Symmetry breaking techniques are used; a URLearning-derived heuristic guides search but integration details are deferred to a prior publication.  
- Ancestral constraints are used to prune the search tree; Lemma 3 is the core theoretical contribution but its proof is unclear, specifically the claim that “By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W.”  
- The paper discusses “implied constraints” (called “projected constraints” here) and “ordering constraints,” but the method for inferring ordering constraints is under-specified.  
- The claim “we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is imprecise; non-ancestry does not logically entail a topological order without the caveat that the constraint is added only when consistent.  
- Authors use a MAXSAT approach to conservatively infer consistent ordering constraints, avoiding over-constraining the solution space.  
- Experiments vary variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints; the proposed method outperforms GOBNILP in speed under these settings.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is confounded because GOBNILP struggles with unbounded parent set cardinality used in the experiments, so performance differences may reflect that rather than handling of ancestral constraints.  
- The ancestral-constraint encoding uses a MAXSAT formulation from Cussens (2008) instead of the ILP encoding in Cussens (2010), which is suboptimal for ILP integration.  
- The reviewer suggests a better ILP encoding: add binary A(X,Y) ancestor variables, keep I(Y,U) parent-set variables, remove cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables, enforce transitivity on A and link A and I via A(X,Y) ≥ Σ_{U:X∈U} I(Y,U) for tighter relaxations.  
- That ILP approach (inspired by Cussens 2010) should adapt to partial ancestor orders by removing one constraint and would likely improve performance.  
- The comparison to a weaker encoding weakens the paper’s conclusions; a reasonably implemented ILP would likely outperform GOBNILP.  
- The reviewer recommends comparing to the constraint programming approach of van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP; that paper is not cited and adapting it to ancestral constraints seems feasible.
