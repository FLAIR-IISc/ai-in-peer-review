This paper addresses the problem of learning the optimal structure of a Bayesian network subject to non-decomposable constraints. The authors' main contribution is a method for incorporating such constraints, particularly ancestral constraints, into the structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. This is achieved by reformulating the problem as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) problem.

The empirical evaluation shows that the proposed approach yields orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements compared to Integer Linear Programming (ILP)-based methods. While my expertise with MaxSAT-based approaches is limited, the reported performance gains are quite impressive. Specifically, the authors demonstrate a runtime reduction of several orders of magnitude relative to alternative methods.
