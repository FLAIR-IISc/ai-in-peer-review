- Tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable constraints, focusing on ancestral constraints.  
- Reformulates the problem as a MaxSAT instance to enforce desired network structures.  
- Main contribution: bridges complex non-decomposable constraints and a tractable computational framework.  
- Improves scalability over prior ILP-based methods, which suffered significant scalability issues.  
- Empirical results show runtime reductions of orders of magnitude compared to ILP baselines.  
- Experimental comparisons against relevant baselines are clear and convincingly showcase efficiency gains.  
- Problem definition, motivation, and connection to Chen et al. are clear and well-established.  
- Translation from Bayesian network learning to MaxSAT appears sound and sufficiently detailed.  
- Paper would benefit from analysis of which network topologies or constraint patterns yield the largest gains.  
- Should discuss limitations, especially solver sensitivity to the structure of generated SAT instances and challenging constraint configurations.  
- Recommend broader comparisons to other state-of-the-art structure learning methods (beyond ILP) for fuller context.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, effective approach with significant empirical improvements; recommend acceptance.
