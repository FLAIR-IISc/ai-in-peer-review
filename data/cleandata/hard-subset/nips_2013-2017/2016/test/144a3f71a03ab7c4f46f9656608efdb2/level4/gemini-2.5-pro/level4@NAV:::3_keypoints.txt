- Presents an effective method for learning optimal Bayesian networks under ancestral constraints by translating those constraints into ordering constraints.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral relations explicit and integrates the ordering constraints into a score-based EC-tree learning algorithm.  
- Empirical results show a significant performance advantage over an augmented version of the ILP-based Gobnilp algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations; the approach is conceptually straightforward but novel and yields solid results for a specialized audience.  
- The problem/formulation is novel, but practical impact may be limited because the setting can seem artificial and real-world use may favor general causal discovery methods that handle hidden confounders.  
- Concern that only a small number of available ancestral constraints in practice may be insufficient to meaningfully prune the search space.  
- Several MaxSAT encoding rules (lines 257–261) are unclear or incomplete: acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion, and rule 4 appears to preclude specifying mutual non-causation.  
- The acyclicity formulation (line 267) is not intuitive and ties back to concerns about the encoding rules.  
- Baseline choice: comparison to augmented Gobnilp is impressive but that baseline was not designed for this problem; a comparison to a greedy method like GES with oracle constraints would be a more informative efficiency benchmark.  
- Theorem 1 needs a more precise statement—clarify that the CPDAG on the EC tree satisfies a subset of the constraints satisfied by the complete DAG (line 164).  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to imply many positive edges given only sparse ancestral constraints (lines 225–227).  
- Using 10–25% of all possible ancestral constraints in experiments implies a very high and possibly unrealistic level of prior knowledge (line 310).  
- Minor wording/clarity issues: distinguish "standard" vs "omniscient oracle" (line 30); rephrase "empower the oracle" (line 38); explicitly state "optimizing = minimizing the score" (line 90).
