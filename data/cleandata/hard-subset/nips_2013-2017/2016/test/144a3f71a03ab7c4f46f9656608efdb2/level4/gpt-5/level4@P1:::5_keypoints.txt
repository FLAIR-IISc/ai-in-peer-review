- Addresses optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints using EC-tree/BN-graph frameworks.
- Two key ideas: prune EC-tree by testing CPDAG extendability under ancestral constraints, and project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints for an oracle.
- Provides sound inference rules for edge projection (Theorems 2–3).
- Formulates a MaxSAT encoding for ordering projection with hard/soft clauses and correctness stated in Theorem 4.
- Empirically achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) across benchmarks, with extra gains when projected constraints are given to the oracle.
- Extends Chen et al. by enabling non-decomposable ancestral constraints within exact score-based search frameworks.
- Complements ILP/CP and constraint-based path-constraint methods; the use of projected decomposable constraints to empower oracles is novel.
- Strength: principled mechanism with soundness theorems for pruning and projection.
- Strength: practical impact—substantial speedups and compatibility with any decomposable-score oracle and standard scores.
- Strength: clear separation between non-decomposable handling (EC-tree pruning) and decomposable oracle empowerment (projection).
- Weakness: scalability/worst-case behavior unclear—CPDAG pruning can require enumerating class(P); needs quantification, complexity bounds, or heuristics.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (n ≤ 20, only BDeu); should evaluate larger n, other scores (e.g., MDL), and parent-size constraints.
- Weakness: narrow comparisons—only GOBNILP evaluated; recommend adding CP baselines and end-to-end tests without precomputed parent scores.
- Weakness: assumes consistent ground-truth sampled constraints; lacks robustness tests for noisy or inconsistent background knowledge and conflict resolution.
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding details lacking—need encoding size, solver settings, runtime breakdown, sensitivity to solver choice, and guarantees on completeness/optimality.
- Weakness: presentation issues—typos, inconsistent notation, and missing pseudocode/proof sketches for edge projection and CPDAG pruning hurt reproducibility.
- Quality: appears technically sound with admissible oracle-induced heuristic preserving optimality, but worst-case complexity remains uncertain.
- Clarity: generally well organized but requires polishing and more explicit algorithmic descriptions for reproducibility.
- Originality: high—novel integration of ancestral constraints into exact score-based learning, especially the MaxSAT-based ordering inference.
- Significance: high—important for applications needing causal/ancestral knowledge; projection idea likely useful beyond ancestral constraints.
- Reasons to accept: novel, principled treatment of non-decomposable constraints with strong empirical speedups and theoretical backing.
- Reasons to reject: limited experimental breadth, under-specified algorithmic/MaxSAT details, and presentation flaws.
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity, experimental breadth, and complexity/MaxSAT specification concerns.
