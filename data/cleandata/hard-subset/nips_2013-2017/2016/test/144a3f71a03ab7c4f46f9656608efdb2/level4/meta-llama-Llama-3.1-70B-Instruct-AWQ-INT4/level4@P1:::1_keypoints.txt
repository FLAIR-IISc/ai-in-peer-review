- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using a framework for non-decomposable scores.
- Key idea: convert ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints and use an oracle for decomposable-score structure learning.
- Problem is well-motivated and important for Bayesian network learning.
- Technical contributions are sound and the approach is thoroughly analyzed.
- Major strength: orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvement over ILP-based alternatives.
- Demonstrated scalability: significantly faster than GOBNILP across experiments varying variables, datapoints, and constraints.
- Evaluation is thorough, comparing runtime and behavior under different settings.
- Main limitation: comparison to GOBNILP uses a poor formulation of ancestral constraints, weakening that baseline.
- Unclear whether GOBNILP’s poor performance stems from many parent sets or poor handling of ancestral constraints.
- A more sensible baseline (e.g., Cussens 2010 formulation) is needed for a fair comparison.
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of trade-offs between number of ancestral constraints and learned model quality.
- More implementation details are needed on how decomposable constraints are inferred from ancestral constraints.
- Writing and clarity are strong overall but could improve in implementation exposition.
- Recommendation summary: technically strong, novel, and significant, but needs better baseline comparison and deeper analysis of implications.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
