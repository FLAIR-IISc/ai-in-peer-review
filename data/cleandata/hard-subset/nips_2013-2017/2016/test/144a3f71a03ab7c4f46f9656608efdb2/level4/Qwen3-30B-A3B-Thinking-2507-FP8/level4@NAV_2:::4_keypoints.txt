- Problem: learning Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break assumptions of existing decomposable-score methods.  
- Method: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by standard oracles.  
- Theoretical contribution: sound and complete constraint-projection method with clear foundation (Section 5).  
- Algorithmic contribution: pruning the EC tree search space using ancestral constraints and empowering oracles with decomposable constraints.  
- Empirical result: orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based methods, especially as variable count grows (Tables 1–3).  
- Practical impact: demonstrates that background causal knowledge can substantially improve learning accuracy with minimal data (Table 3).  
- Relation to prior work: builds on EC tree search and extends constraint handling beyond earlier decomposable-score approaches.  
- Presentation: well-organized, clearly written, with appropriate technical depth and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: novel projection technique enabling non-decomposable constraint handling while preserving optimality.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation and comprehensive ILP comparison.  
- Weakness: unclear why projected constraints help EC trees more than ILP; generalizability to other approaches not shown.  
- Weakness: easy–hard–easy pattern in Table 3 is under-explained regarding practical implications.  
- Weakness: missing structural Hamming distance (accuracy) comparison between EC trees and ILP approaches.  
- Weakness: Section 4.1 on BN graphs is somewhat tangential and could be condensed or removed.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution; acceptance favored pending minor revisions to address generality, easy–hard–easy explanation, and accuracy comparisons.
