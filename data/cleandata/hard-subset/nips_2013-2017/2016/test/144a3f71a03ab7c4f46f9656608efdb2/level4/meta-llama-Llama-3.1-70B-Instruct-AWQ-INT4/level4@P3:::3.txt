The paper "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints" proposes an efficient approach to incorporate ancestral constraints in finding optimal Bayesian networks from data. The authors tackle an interesting and novel problem, as optimal Bayesian network learning in combination with ancestral constraints has no readily available 'standard solution'. The solution is conceptually straightforward, yet the results are significant, showing that the proposed approach outperforms a straightforward modification of the ILP-based Gobnilp algorithm.

The paper is reasonably well-written and clearly explained, making it accessible to a specialist audience. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, discussing the importance of ancestral constraints in practice and the limitations of existing approaches. They then present their approach, which involves translating a set of ancestral constraints into a (maximal) set of ordering constraints that are subsequently used in a score-based learning algorithm based on the EC tree.

The authors evaluate their approach against the ILP-based Gobnilp algorithm and demonstrate significant performance improvements. However, it would be beneficial to compare their approach against other algorithms, such as greedy algorithms like GES, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the efficiency achieved by the method. Additionally, the authors could provide more insight into the practical implications of their approach, such as the typical number of ancestral constraints that would be provided in practice and how this would impact the search space.

Some minor details could be improved for clarity. For example, the distinction between a (standard) oracle and an omniscient oracle could be clarified. Additionally, the phrase "empower the oracle" could be rephrased to "use in combination with the oracle" for better understanding.

The paper's main contribution is the development of a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints. The authors also show how the employed oracle can be empowered by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed approach is orders-of-magnitude more efficient compared to learning systems based on ILP.

Overall, the paper presents a novel solution to an interesting problem, and the results are significant. However, some minor improvements could be made to enhance clarity and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approach.

Strengths:

* Novel problem and solution
* Significant performance improvements over ILP-based approach
* Clearly explained and well-written

Weaknesses:

* Limited comparison to other algorithms
* Some minor details could be improved for clarity
* Practical implications could be further explored
