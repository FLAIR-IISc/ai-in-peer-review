- Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints so decomposable-score-based structure learning can incorporate domain knowledge and improve efficiency.  
- Main novelty (Section 5): projection from ancestral constraints to edge and topological-ordering constraints, both decomposable.  
- Paper is clearly written and provides solid theoretical development and experimental evaluation.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows projection helps EC trees but not ILP — raises question whether the approach generalizes beyond EC trees and if it benefits learners that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern: Table 2’s easy–hard–easy pattern for EC trees as ancestral-constraint proportion increases — unclear why this occurs and whether performance is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge (may require tuning).  
- Concern: Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees — missing SHD comparison for the ILP approach.  
- Suggestion: Drop Section 4.1 and instead expand the explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree setting for clarity.
