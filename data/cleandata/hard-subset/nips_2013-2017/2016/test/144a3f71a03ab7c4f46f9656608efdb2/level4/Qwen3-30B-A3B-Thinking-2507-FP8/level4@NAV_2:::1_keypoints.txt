- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break standard decomposable-score methods.
- Method: Extends Chen et al.'s EC tree framework with a projection that infers decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.
- Oracle use: Leverages a decomposable-score oracle (e.g., URLEARNING) guided by inferred constraints to search the EC tree more efficiently.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on EC tree literature (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) and ancestral-constraint work (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos), providing a systematic way to handle non-decomposable constraints.
- Theoretical contribution: Provides a sound foundation for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints, with correctness proved (Theorem 1).
- Practical technique: Introduces a method to infer decomposable constraints that improves the oracle’s heuristic and search efficiency.
- Empirical results: Shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP when ancestral constraints are present.
- Scalability and accuracy: Demonstrates good scaling to 20-variable networks while maintaining solution accuracy across benchmarks.
- Data-efficiency insight: 10–25% ancestral constraints can have an effect comparable to increasing training data by 4–16×.
- Strength: High technical quality with clear proofs and a well-presented adaptation of the EC tree framework.
- Strength: Significant and consistent empirical improvements over ILP baselines as problem size grows.
- Strength: Practical significance for applications with available background ancestral knowledge.
- Weakness: Proof of Lemma 3 in the supplement lacks sufficient explanation—specific edge-generation logic needs clarification.
- Weakness: Discussion of inferred ordering constraints is imprecise; the manuscript should better explain consistency limitations and the MAXSAT-based inference.
- Weakness: ILP comparison uses a suboptimal ancestral-constraint encoding (Cussens 2008) instead of a more efficient 2010 formulation, possibly understating ILP performance.
- Overall quality: Technically sound, thorough experiments, and well-organized presentation with a few clarifications needed.
- Originality: Meaningful and novel extension enabling non-decomposable ancestral constraints in structure learning.
- Significance: Strong practical impact for structure learning with background knowledge; advances state of the art.
- Pro accept: Novel, theoretically sound, and empirically compelling approach that extends recent EC tree work.
- Con accept: Paper should clarify Lemma 3 and ordering-constraint nuances and use a stronger ILP encoding for fairer comparison.
