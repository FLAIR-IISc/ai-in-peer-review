- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into ordering constraints within the EC tree framework.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to ensure explicit representation of all implied ancestral constraints.  
- Evaluated against a modified ILP-based GoBNILP algorithm and shows significant performance improvements in tested settings.  
- Well-written, conceptually straightforward, and of definite interest to a specialist audience.  
- Problem is novel and interesting, but the practical relevance is questionable because real-world causal discovery often requires handling unobserved confounders, not only constrained structure learning.  
- Correctness and completeness of the translation rules need further scrutiny—specific concern about asymmetry in acyclicity rule 3 (premise symmetric, conclusion asymmetric).  
- Rule 4 appears to exclude ancestral constraints that indicate mutual non-causation between variables.  
- Comparison to GoBNILP is useful but insufficient since GoBNILP wasn’t designed for this constrained learning problem.  
- A comparison to greedy algorithms like GES (given oracle-consistent constraints) would be more informative about practical efficiency gains.  
- Clarify terminology: distinguish “standard” vs “omniscient” oracle (line 30).  
- Rephrase “empower the oracle” to a more precise phrase such as “use in combination with the oracle” (line 38).  
- Explicitly state that “optimizing” refers to “minimizing” the score (line 90).  
- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that the set of constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG P is a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G to justify the iff claim (line 164).  
- Theorem 3 may generate few positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse (lines 225–227).  
- The link between the cited rule and acyclicity is unclear; earlier comment on rule 3 applies here (line 267).  
- Using constraint sets of 10–25% of possible ancestral constraints implies a large amount of prior knowledge, which is atypical in many practical scenarios (line 310).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising results, but addressing translation-rule correctness and strengthening the evaluation methodology would improve the paper.
