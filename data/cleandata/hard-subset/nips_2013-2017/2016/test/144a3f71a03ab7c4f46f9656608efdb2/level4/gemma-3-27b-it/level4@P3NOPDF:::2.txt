## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission [Paper ID]

This paper presents a novel approach to informed exhaustive search, leveraging ancestral constraints to guide the admissible heuristic used by the search algorithm. The core idea – incorporating problem structure to prune the search space – is promising and potentially valuable for challenging combinatorial optimization problems. However, the experimental presentation and resulting clarity of the performance gains are areas requiring significant improvement.

My primary concern lies with the experimental setup and the interpretation of the results presented in Tables 1 and 3. The comparison structure feels unnecessarily convoluted. Table 1 appears to contrast the performance of Exhaustive Chain (EC) combined with the proposed method against Guided Optimization by Branching (GOB) combined with a modified version of the proposed method (“new work’”). This setup makes it difficult to isolate the benefit of the proposed technique. 

Subsequent analysis hinges on Table 3, which then seems to compare EC directly to GOB. This raises the question: is Table 3 comparing the *baseline* EC to the *baseline* GOB, or is it implicitly referencing EC + the proposed method versus GOB + the modified proposed method? This ambiguity needs to be explicitly addressed. 

It would be substantially more informative to see a direct comparison between EC *with* the proposed method and EC *without* it. While the comparison of GOB with and without the modification (“new work’”) is interesting, the inherent superiority of EC over GOB suggests that even a substantial relative improvement to GOB might not outweigh the absolute performance of EC augmented with the proposed method. A clear demonstration of the absolute benefit conferred by the proposed method *on top of* a strong baseline like EC is crucial.

Beyond the comparison structure, the paper lacks sufficient detail to fully understand the modifications applied to the method when combined with GOB (“new work’”). The authors should clarify precisely what changes were made and *why* they were necessary for GOB. Without this explanation, it’s difficult to assess whether the observed performance differences are due to the core proposed method or these implementation-specific adaptations.

Regarding the technical aspects of the paper, the theoretical justification for the heuristic's admissibility, given the ancestral constraints, appears sound. However, the authors could strengthen this section by providing a more intuitive explanation of how these constraints effectively reduce the search space. A visual illustration demonstrating the pruning effect of the heuristic could be highly beneficial.

The related work section provides a reasonable overview of existing approaches, but could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how this work differentiates itself from other heuristic search methods. Specifically, a clearer articulation of the unique advantages conferred by the ancestral constraint-based heuristic would be valuable.

**Strengths:**

*   The core idea of leveraging ancestral constraints for informed exhaustive search is novel and potentially impactful.
*   The theoretical justification for the heuristic’s admissibility is presented.
*   The paper addresses a challenging problem in combinatorial optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The experimental setup and presentation are confusing and hinder clear interpretation of the results.
*   Lack of a direct comparison between EC with and without the proposed method.
*   Insufficient detail regarding the modifications applied when integrating with GOB (“new work”).
*   The related work section could benefit from a more focused discussion of the paper's novelty.



**Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Re-structure the experiments:** Present results comparing EC + proposed method to EC alone, and GOB + modified method to GOB alone. Add a final comparison of the best performing methods (likely EC + proposed method).
*   **Clarify “new work”:** Provide a detailed explanation of the modifications made to the method when applied to GOB, and the rationale behind these changes.
*   **Provide a visual illustration:** Demonstrate the pruning effect of the heuristic using a diagram or example.
*   **Expand related work discussion:** Explicitly articulate how this work differs from and improves upon existing heuristic search methods.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a promising idea, but the confusing experimental presentation and lack of clarity regarding the modifications for GOB significantly detract from its impact. If the authors can address these concerns by restructuring the experiments and providing more detailed explanations, the paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field. Currently, I lean towards a **weak reject** due to the difficulties in evaluating the true benefit of the proposed method.
