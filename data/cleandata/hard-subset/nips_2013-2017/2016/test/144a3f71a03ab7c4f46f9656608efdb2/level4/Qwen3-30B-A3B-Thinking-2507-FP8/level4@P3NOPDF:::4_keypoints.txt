- Proposes a novel projection method to convert non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, enabling decomposable-score structure learning methods to use ancestral constraints.  
- Core technical contribution is formalized in Section 5 and the paper is well-structured with clear theory and comprehensive experiments.  
- Table 3 shows improvements for EC trees but no benefit for ILP, raising concerns about the method’s claimed generality to “any” decomposable-score method.  
- Authors must explain why ILP fails to benefit (incompatibility with projected constraints) or provide additional experiments showing broader applicability beyond EC trees.  
- Table 2 exhibits an “easy-hard-easy” performance pattern as constraint proportion increases, indicating sensitivity to constraint density that needs investigation (over- vs under-constraining) and discussion of practical tuning.  
- Need to establish whether the easy-hard-easy pattern generalizes across datasets and structural complexities and whether it limits real-world deployment.  
- Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees; missing SHD comparisons with ILP is a significant omission—add direct SHD comparisons to contextualize EC tree gains versus state-of-the-art.  
- Section 4.1 on BN fundamentals is tangential and should be removed or shortened; reallocate space to explain how projected constraints integrate with EC tree search (pruning rules, scoring, A* mechanics).  
- Overall: technique is sound and promising, but limited empirical validation, unexplained performance patterns, and missing ILP comparisons weaken claims of broad applicability; addressing these would strengthen the paper.
