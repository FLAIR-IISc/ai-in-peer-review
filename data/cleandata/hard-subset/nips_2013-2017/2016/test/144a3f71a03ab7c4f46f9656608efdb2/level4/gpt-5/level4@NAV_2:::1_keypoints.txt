- Paper tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral constraints via search over CPDAGs with symmetry breaking.  
- Search transitions pick parent sets for variables not yet in the CPDAG; A* guided by URLEARNING heuristic (building on prior work).  
- Ancestral constraints allow pruning the search space; Lemma 3 is central to this pruning but its proof has unclear steps (specifically justification that Gk contains edge Z -> W).  
- Authors derive “projected/implied” constraints from ancestral constraints, including edge constraints for decomposable-score oracles and ordering constraints on topological orders.  
- Treatment of ordering constraints is underspecified; the phrase “can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is ambiguous and overclaims logical entailment.  
- The authors resolve over-commitment of ordering constraints using a MaxSAT formulation.  
- Experiments vary variables, data size, and ancestral constraints, comparing the method to GOBNILP and showing substantial speedups.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is confounded because parent-set size is unrestricted; ILP methods typically struggle with large parent-set spaces.  
- The ILP encoding of ancestral constraints used is adapted from a MaxSAT formulation and is not a native, tighter ILP encoding.  
- A stronger ILP formulation would introduce binary ancestry variables A(X,Y), impose transitivity, and link ancestry to parent selection (yielding a tighter LP relaxation).  
- Although the proposed method likely still outperforms a stronger ILP encoding, the current unfavorable ILP formulation weakens the empirical comparison.  
- The authors should compare to or cite the constraint programming approach of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which is often competitive and adaptable to ancestral constraints.
