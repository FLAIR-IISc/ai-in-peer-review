

The paper presents a novel approach for optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and typically incompatible with mainstream structure learning methods. The authors leverage a recently proposed EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores, introducing a sound method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints. This enables the oracle to be effectively empowered for constrained structure learning, resulting in significant efficiency gains.

The main strengths of the paper include a well-motivated technical contribution that addresses a meaningful limitation in current approaches, a clear theoretical foundation for constraint projection, and comprehensive empirical evaluation demonstrating orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP-based methods. The paper's organization is logical, with a thorough presentation of both theoretical and experimental results. The authors effectively demonstrate how background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints can substantially improve learning efficiency, with experiments showing that a small number of constraints (10%-25%) can have a similar impact on model accuracy as significantly larger datasets.

The primary weakness lies in the presentation of experimental results, which creates unnecessary confusion. Specifically, Table 1 compares (EC + new work) against (GOB + new work'), while Table 3 appears to compare EC against GOB directly. This structure obscures the relative impact of the constraint projection technique. It would be significantly more informative to explicitly compare (EC + new work) against EC alone, as EC already substantially outperforms GOB in most scenarios. The current presentation makes it difficult to assess how much of the performance improvement stems from the new constraint projection method versus the inherent advantages of the EC approach over ILP-based methods. A clearer experimental design would strengthen the paper's contribution by isolating the specific impact of the constraint projection technique.
