- Proposes translating ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints and using them in a score-based EC-tree learning algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method significantly outperforms a straightforward ILP-modified Gobnilp baseline in the reported setting.  
- Paper is clearly written but the solution is conceptually straightforward.  
- Novel problem (ancestral constraints + optimal BN learning) but somewhat artificial; more general causal discovery methods allowing latent confounders may be more appropriate.  
- Practical impact is limited because realistic settings will likely provide few ancestral constraints, so search space reduction may be small.  
- Some logical rules (rules 257–261) appear strange or incomplete—e.g., asymmetric acyclicity rule and exclusion of mutual non-causation.  
- Notation and presentation of "acyclic" constraints are unclear (including rule-related and notation issues around line 267).  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is not fully convincing since Gobnilp is not designed for this problem.  
- A more meaningful baseline would be a greedy algorithm like GES assuming ancestral constraints align with the optimal model.  
- Theorem 1 should clarify that constraints A satisfied on the EC tree are only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to yield many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Several minor editorial/clarity issues: clarify "standard vs omniscient oracle" (line 30), rephrase "empower the oracle" (line 38), state that "optimizing" means minimizing (line 90), and comment on unrealistic proportions of ancestral constraints (line 310).
