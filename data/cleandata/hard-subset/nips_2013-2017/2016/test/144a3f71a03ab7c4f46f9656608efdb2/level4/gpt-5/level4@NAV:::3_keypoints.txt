- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by converting them into ordering constraints used with EC-tree search.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit and form a maximal collection of ordering constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation shows substantial gains over an ILP-based Gobnilp baseline adapted for the task.  
- Manuscript is clear and well organized, though the core idea is conceptually simple.  
- Novel problem and solution; relevant to specialists but not groundbreaking (novelty=3).  
- Impact limited because ancestral-relation setups are somewhat artificial and broader causal discovery methods often more appropriate (impact=2).  
- Translation into ordering constraints leverages the fact that absence-of-edge statements integrate with decomposable scores.  
- Some proposed logical rules (lines 257–261) appear odd or incomplete (e.g., asymmetric conclusions, potentially incorrect rule 4).  
- In realistic settings one would expect only a few ancestral constraints, so the search space may not shrink substantially.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is strong but Gobnilp is not designed for this task; a baseline like GES with oracle access would be more informative.  
- Theorem 1 needs clearer statement that CPDAG-satisfied constraints are only a subset of DAG-satisfied constraints (clarify the iff).  
- Theorem 3 likely yields few positive edge implications with sparse ancestral constraints.  
- Acyclicity encoding (e.g., line 267) is not easily recognizable or may be unclear.  
- Minor editorial/clarity issues: clarify “oracle” terminology (line 30), rephrase “empower the oracle” (line 38), note “optimizing = minimizing” (line 90).  
- Claim that 10%–25% ancestral constraints already imply substantial knowledge of the target graph may be unrealistic for typical practice (line 310).
