- Problem: optimal Bayesian network structure learning with background ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Core challenge: incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable-score-based learning frameworks.  
- Contribution: method to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints so existing efficient oracles can be used, building on Chen et al.  
- Empirical result: orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods across experiments varying network size, data size, and constraint density.  
- Motivation and context: problem is well-motivated and situated within existing literature on BN structure learning and constraint handling.  
- Strength — importance: tackles a practically important problem (incorporating causal/background knowledge).  
- Strength — novelty: clever projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable form.  
- Strength — empirical rigor: strong, well-designed experiments demonstrating significant performance gains.  
- Strength — related work: thorough and appropriate literature review and positioning.  
- Strength — presentation: generally clear writing and formalization via theorems.  
- Weakness — MaxSAT detail: reduction to MaxSAT is glossed over; clause construction/details need more explanation.  
- Weakness — scalability: experiments scale to 20 variables only; limits and future scaling directions should be discussed.  
- Weakness — oracle dependence: performance heavily relies on chosen oracle (URLEARNING); sensitivity analysis across oracles is missing.  
- Weakness — SHD interpretation: discussion of Structural Hamming Distance and effects of Markov equivalence is insufficiently detailed.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, novel approach with strong empirical support and clear writing; theoretical projection idea has broader value.  
- Against acceptance: incomplete MaxSAT exposition, scalability concerns, and reliance on a specific oracle reduce confidence.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with compelling results; weaknesses are notable but not fatal.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
