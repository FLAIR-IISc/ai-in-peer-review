- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Extends Chen et al.'s framework to handle ancestral constraints via a novel constraint projection technique.  
- Converts ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1–4) supporting the method.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude search efficiency improvements without loss of solution quality.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive across benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) and varied parameters.  
- Results validated using structural Hamming distance and show scalability to 20 variables for exact learning.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-organized with reproducible implementation details.  
- MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints is accessible and effectively presented.  
- Novel projection approach fills a gap by enabling score-based learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Shows practical significance: leveraging background ancestral knowledge can greatly reduce data needs.  
- Strengths include novel technique, solid theory, thorough experiments, and large efficiency gains over ILP methods.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of trade-offs between constraint density and search efficiency in MaxSAT.  
- Weaknesses: limited exploration of handling very complex ancestral constraint patterns.  
- Pro-accept: meaningful overlooked problem, strong practical and theoretical contributions, convincing results.  
- Con-accept: approach specialized to ancestral constraints and could better contextualize scalability vs. recent alternatives.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; paper recommended for acceptance.
