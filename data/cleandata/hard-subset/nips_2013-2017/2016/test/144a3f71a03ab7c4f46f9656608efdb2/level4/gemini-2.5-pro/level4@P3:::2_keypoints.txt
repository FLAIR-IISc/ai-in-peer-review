- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints that are non-decomposable and incompatible with many efficient decomposable-score methods.  
- Novel idea: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (edge constraints and pairwise orderings) to enable efficient search.  
- Builds on EC-tree A* search over equivalence classes of DAGs and uses the inferred decomposable constraints to strengthen the admissible heuristic.  
- Provides sound theorems for projecting ancestral constraints to edge constraints.  
- Introduces a MaxSAT-based reduction to infer a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints from ancestral information.  
- Empirically shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) for exact structure learning.  
- Demonstrates that even a small fraction of ancestral constraints can significantly improve model accuracy, comparable to much larger increases in data.  
- Technical approach is sound and guarantees optimality when combined with EC-tree search.  
- Experimental presentation is unclear: lacks a direct ablation comparing EC-tree with vs. without the projection.  
- Narrative around the tables is confusing; results should be reorganized to clearly show baseline EC-tree vs. ILP, then the added benefit of projection.  
- Needs discussion of MaxSAT preprocessing overhead relative to the main search and whether it scales to very large variable sets.  
- Recommendation: revise evaluation to include explicit ablation and clearer framing; overall paper is strong and recommended for acceptance.
