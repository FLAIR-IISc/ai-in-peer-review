- Presents an efficient method to learn optimal Bayesian networks under ancestral constraints by converting them to ordering constraints via MaxSAT and integrating into an EC-tree based score search; shows strong performance vs a modified Gobnilp baseline.  
- Paper is well-written, conceptually straightforward, and novel since there's no standard solution for optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Concern about problem motivation: if rich causal/ancestral knowledge exists, general causal discovery methods that handle latent confounders may be more appropriate, making the setting feel artificial.  
- Core technical contribution (translation from ancestral to ordering constraints) needs clarification: potential logical issues in the SAT rules (lines 257–261) undermine confidence in correctness.  
- Specific issue: 'acyclicity rule 3' has symmetric premises but an asymmetric conclusion, which appears inconsistent.  
- Specific issue: Rule 4 cannot represent constraints like "X and Y are not ancestors of each other," so the rule set may be incomplete.  
- Experimental baseline is weak: comparing to a modified Gobnilp (not designed for this task) is unconvincing; a greedy baseline like GES would provide a more meaningful efficiency comparison.  
- Practicality concern: real applications usually provide only sparse ancestral constraints, so the method’s pruning/efficiency gains may be limited; experiments using 10–25% of all ancestral constraints are unrealistic.  
- Theorem 1 needs clearer phrasing: state that constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG on the EC tree are a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G to avoid incorrect iff claims.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when initial ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Several minor clarity/editing points: clarify "standard" vs "omniscient oracle" (Line 30), rephrase "empower the oracle" (Line 38), state that "optimizing" means "minimizing" (Line 90), and reconsider naming "acyclic" for the rule (Line 267).
