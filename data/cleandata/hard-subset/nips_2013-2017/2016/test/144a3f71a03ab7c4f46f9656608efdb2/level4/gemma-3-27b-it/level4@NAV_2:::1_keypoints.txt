- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable forms.  
- Core idea: empower decomposable-score oracles with constraints they can handle, derived from ancestral constraints they cannot.  
- Uses EC tree search space and A* search with a heuristic from the URLearning system.  
- Key contribution: infers both edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints to improve search efficiency.  
- Provides clear technical background and motivation, linking to prior work (Chen et al., 2015/2016).  
- Novel projection approach effectively handles intractability of non-decomposable constraints.  
- Theorem 1 establishes soundness of pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints.  
- Experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups compared to the ILP-based GOBNILP system.  
- Analysis of how ancestral constraints affect learning efficiency and scalability is insightful.  
- Thoughtful use of MaxSAT to infer ordering constraints and maximize background-knowledge benefits.  
- Weakness: clarity issues around Lemma 3—edge generation rule reasoning needs clearer exposition.  
- Weakness: initial explanation of “can infer” is potentially misleading; needs clearer framing of consistent vs. entailed constraints.  
- Major concern: comparison with GOBNILP uses a suboptimal MAXSAT encoding instead of a more efficient ILP formulation (Cussens 2010).  
- Missing comparison to van Beek and Hoffmann (2015) constraint programming approach, which should be cited and discussed.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong theoretical foundation, solid empirical gains, and practical significance.  
- Rejection concerns: clarity issues and incomplete comparison to state-of-the-art ILP/CP methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity (Lemma 3 and inference wording) and expand related-work/comparison.
