- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them to ordering constraints used in an EC tree-based score algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make all implied ancestral constraints explicit.  
- Experiments show significant performance gains over a modified ILP-based solver (Gobnilp) in the evaluated setting.  
- Paper is well-written, conceptually straightforward, and of interest to a specialist audience.  
- Novelty rated moderate: the problem is interesting and novel because no standard solution exists (novelty=3).  
- Impact limited: the experimental setting is somewhat artificial and broader causal discovery methods that handle confounders may be more appropriate (impact=2).  
- The transformation effectively converts ancestral constraints into a form compatible with decomposable-score-based algorithms.  
- Some presented rules (lines 257–261)—notably acyclicity rule 3 and rule 4—appear incomplete or questionable (symmetry/implication issues and exclusion of mutual non-causation).  
- In practice, users may supply few ancestral constraints, so the method might not substantially reduce search space; 10–25% constraints already imply strong prior knowledge.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is impressive but limited, since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this problem; a comparison assuming oracle-consistent constraints against greedy algorithms (e.g., GES) would be more informative.  
- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that the EC-tree CPDAG satisfies only a subset of the full DAG’s ancestral constraints to justify the “if and only if.”  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Several minor clarifications and wording fixes are requested (Lines 30, 38, 90, 164, 225–227, 267, 310).
