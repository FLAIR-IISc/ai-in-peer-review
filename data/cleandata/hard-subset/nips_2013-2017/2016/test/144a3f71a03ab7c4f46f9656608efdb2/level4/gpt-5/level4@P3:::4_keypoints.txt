- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints for oracle-guided EC-tree/A* search.  
- Key technical contribution: method to project ancestral constraints into safe edge and ordering constraints, tightening admissible heuristics.  
- Provides pruning rules for CPDAG nodes in the EC tree to enforce ancestral constraints.  
- Presents an algorithm to infer all entailed edge constraints from a maximally closed ancestral constraint set (Theorems 2–3).  
- Gives a MaxSAT reduction to extract a maximally large, safe set of ordering constraints (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks, especially when using projected constraints.  
- Observes an easy–hard–easy runtime pattern for EC-tree search as constraint proportion increases and improved SHD with more data or constraints.  
- Strength: clear problem motivation — ancestral constraints are valuable but non-decomposable for standard learners.  
- Strength: principled, exact EC-tree search framework augmented with decomposable-score oracle and sound pruning.  
- Strength: novel projection approach and practical MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints.  
- Strength: strong empirical scalability and informative analysis of constraint/data effects on runtime and SHD.  
- Weakness: unclear why projection benefits heuristic search much more than ILP; need clarification on tighter heuristics vs. redundant linear constraints.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of stronger ILP integrations (cutting planes, parent pruning, order-based formulations) to close the performance gap.  
- Weakness: easy–hard–easy phenomenon not quantified; request for node expansions, oracle calls, branching factor, and pruned CPDAG counts versus constraint proportion.  
- Weakness: missing ILP accuracy comparison — report ILP SHD or best-feasible SHD and optimality gaps under time limits.  
- Weakness: CPDAG pruning may require enumerating class(P) (potentially exponential); need stats on how often and at what cost enumeration occurs.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT scaling concerns — report variable/clause counts, solver time vs. n and p, projection overhead vs. search savings.  
- Weakness: need robustness analysis to inconsistent/noisy ancestral constraints and whether MaxSAT can select maximal consistent subsets.  
- Experimental detail request: clarify how ancestral constraints are sampled (positive/negative proportions and uniformity) and sensitivity to noisy constraints.  
- Presentation: condense BN-graph preliminaries and expand EC-tree/A* mechanics with a worked example showing pruning and oracle guidance.  
- Presentation: fix reference placeholders, stray text, and tighten theorem/notation definitions (α, β sets; “maximum set of ancestral constraints”).  
- Quality: technically sound with useful theorems, but some complexity and robustness details need clarification to support claims.  
- Originality: novel combination of EC-tree search with projected decomposable constraints to enable oracle tightening.  
- Significance: method enables exact BN learning with causal background knowledge and shows practical efficiency gains; constraints can rival increased data for SHD improvements.  
- Recommendations: report ILP SHD/gaps, breakdown search/projection costs, analyze projection benefits for ILP/CP, provide enumeration and MaxSAT statistics, and clean up presentation.
