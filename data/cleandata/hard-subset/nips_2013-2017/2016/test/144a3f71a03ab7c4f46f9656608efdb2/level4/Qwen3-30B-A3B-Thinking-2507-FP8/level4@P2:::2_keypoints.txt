- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break standard decomposable-score structure learning methods.  
- Main idea: Use the EC tree search space plus an oracle for decomposable scores and project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Novelty: First method to incorporate ancestral constraints into score-based BN structure learning via projection to decomposable constraints.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proofs showing projection correctness and a sound pruning method for the EC tree.  
- Built on prior work: Extends Chen et al.’s BN graph and EC tree framework to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical contribution: First empirical evaluation comparing EC-based approach to ILP and constraint-programming methods for ancestral constraints.  
- Performance: Orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP approaches, consistent across network sizes and constraint densities.  
- Practical impact: Even modest amounts of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match large increases in sample size for model accuracy.  
- Scalability: Demonstrated scaling to 20-variable networks, comparable to state-of-the-art score-based methods.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written paper with helpful figures and detailed experimental setup averaged over 50 datasets.  
- Weakness — experiment clarity: Table 1 comparison is confusing; unclear how the ILP “new work'” differs from the EC projected constraints.  
- Weakness — complexity explanation: Needs clearer analysis why EC tree scales better than ILP and explicit computational complexity discussion.  
- Weakness — robustness: Does not evaluate performance when ancestral constraints are noisy or partially incorrect.  
- Recommendation: Technically sound, significant, and practically valuable work; minor clarifications needed but overall recommendation for acceptance.
