- Core contribution: translate ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints and incorporate them into score-based BN learning via the EC tree framework.  
- Empirical result: shows significant runtime improvements over a modified ILP baseline (Gobnilp).  
- Novelty: addresses a relatively unexplored problem with a novel constraint-transformation idea (novelty ~3/5).  
- Practical relevance concern: when ancestral knowledge exists, causal discovery methods for hidden confounders might be more appropriate, limiting applicability.  
- Technical approach detail: uses a MaxSAT solver to enumerate implied ordering constraints to make non-decomposable ancestral constraints compatible with score-based methods.  
- Rule correctness issue: some translation rules (e.g., acyclicity symmetry, mutual non-causality) appear incomplete or potentially flawed.  
- Search-space assumption risk: benefits rely on ancestral constraints sufficiently reducing the search space, which may fail with sparse prior knowledge.  
- Baseline weakness: comparison to Gobnilp is not a strong or fully fair baseline for this specialized problem.  
- Suggested stronger baseline: compare against a greedy algorithm (e.g., GES) given the true ancestral constraints to better gauge efficiency gains.  
- Strengths summary: tackles an important problem, proposes a novel transformation, provides empirical validation, and is clearly written.  
- Weaknesses summary: limited practical relevance, questionable translation rules, weak baseline, and unclear scalability with sparse constraints.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with clear exposition and empirical efficiency gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact, potential rule errors, insufficient baseline comparisons, and scalability concerns.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution warranting acceptance, but with fixable issues that should be addressed in revision.  
- Recommendations: clarify motivation and limits, re-examine and correct translation rules, add greedy-baseline comparisons with true constraints, and evaluate scalability under varying constraint sparsity.
