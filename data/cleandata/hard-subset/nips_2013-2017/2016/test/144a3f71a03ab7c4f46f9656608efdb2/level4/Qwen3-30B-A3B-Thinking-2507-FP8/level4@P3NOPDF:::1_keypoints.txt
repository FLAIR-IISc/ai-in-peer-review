- Tackles constrained Bayesian network learning via branch-and-bound over CPDAGs with ancestral constraints, symmetry-breaking, and a URLearning heuristic.
- Lemma 3 is the key theoretical foundation and appears correct, but its proof lacks justification for the claim that "Gk also contains edge Z → W" under the edge-generation rules.
- The paper incorrectly phrases ordering inference: "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" conflates choosing a consistent ordering constraint with logical entailment.
- The process of inferring all consistent ordering constraints via MAXSAT is under-described and the rationale for MAXSAT over other constraint-propagation methods is unclear.
- Experiments report speedups over GOBNILP across sizes, but the comparison is undermined by the encoding of ancestral constraints.
- The adopted MAXSAT encoding introduces O(n³) E(X,Y,Z) variables, which is suboptimal for ILP solvers compared to an O(n²) ILP formulation (Cussens 2010) that encodes A(X,Y) and I(Z,U).
- A tighter ILP formulation yields stronger linear relaxations by directly encoding ancestor-parent relations; the current weak encoding likely exaggerates GOBNILP’s poor performance.
- Because of the encoding choice, it is unclear whether observed speedups are due to better ancestral-constraint handling or simply a weaker baseline encoding.
- The paper fails to compare against a sensible ILP baseline (e.g., Cussens 2010), weakening the experimental contribution.
- The work also omits comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), a stronger CP-based baseline that could be adapted to ancestral constraints.
- Strengths: symmetry-breaking techniques and the CPDAG branch-and-bound framework are well-motivated and promising.
- Major required fixes: clarify Lemma 3’s proof, correct the constraint-inference logic/terminology, justify or replace the MAXSAT encoding with a standard ILP formulation, and add van Beek & Hoffmann comparisons.
- Recommendation: promising direction but major revisions needed; current theoretical ambiguities and flawed experimental setup significantly undermine the paper’s validity.
