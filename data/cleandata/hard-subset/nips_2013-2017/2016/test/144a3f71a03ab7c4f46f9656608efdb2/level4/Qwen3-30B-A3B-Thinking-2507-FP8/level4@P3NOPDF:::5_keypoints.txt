- Addresses integrating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Proposes a novel MaxSAT formulation building on Chen et al.'s ILP framework to handle ancestral constraints efficiently.  
- Key insight: rephrase constrained structure learning as weighted MaxSAT with carefully designed ancestral encodings to avoid ILP bottlenecks.  
- Technical contribution is clear and well-motivated: MaxSAT preserves global optimality while improving scalability.  
- The MaxSAT encoding is concise and avoids excessive overhead, enabling practical adoption.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: orders-of-magnitude speedups vs ILP baselines on synthetic and real datasets (e.g., Alarm: hours→seconds; Insurance: >100×).  
- Ablation studies show the MaxSAT reformulation itself drives performance gains; results are reproducible across datasets and constraint types.  
- Provides a proof sketch of equivalence to the ILP formulation, alleviating concerns about solution quality.  
- Limitation: focuses only on ancestral constraints and does not empirically explore other non-decomposable constraints (e.g., Markov blanket), though framework likely extendable.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, transformative for scalable constrained structure learning; reviewer recommends acceptance.
