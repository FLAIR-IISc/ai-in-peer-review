- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints via branch-and-bound over CPDAGs, using symmetry breaking and A* with a URLearning heuristic.  
- Lemma 3 (supplement) is the key pruning result; likely correct but the proof is hard to follow and the step claiming “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs fuller justification.  
- The treatment of implied (“projected”) constraints and inferred ordering constraints is unclear; the wording “can infer Y < Z” is misleading and the MAXSAT selection method needs clearer explanation.  
- Experiments show the proposed method is substantially faster than GOBNILP, but since no parent-set bound is used, it’s unclear if GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints or handling many parent sets.  
- The GOBNILP baseline uses a MAXSAT-based encoding from Cussens 2008 that is ill-suited for ILP (introduces cubic E(X,Y,Z) vars); a stronger ILP would use binary ancestry A(X,Y) vars with transitivity and linking constraints, yielding a tighter relaxation.  
- Even with a stronger ILP baseline the authors’ method likely still outperforms GOBNILP, but using a poor encoding weakens the empirical comparison.  
- The paper omits comparison to the CP approach of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often beats GOBNILP and could handle ancestral constraints; this related work should be cited and compared.  
- Overall: promising A*-based framework with sound symmetry breaking and pruning; main weaknesses are clarity gaps (Lemma 3 proof and projected/ordering constraint procedure) and an inadequate experimental baseline.
