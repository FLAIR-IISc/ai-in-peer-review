- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAG nodes, selecting parent sets for variables not present in the initial CPDAG.
- Employs effective symmetry breaking to reduce the search space.
- Search is guided by the URLearning heuristic, but the paper omits details (referenced to an earlier work).
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning; Lemma 3 underlies this pruning but its proof is unclear—specifically the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs clarification.
- Paper uses the term "projected constraints" for what is standardly called "implied constraints" derived from ancestral constraints for pruning.
- Inferring ordering constraints is inadequately explained; the claim "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading—adding Y < Z is possible when Z is not an ancestor of Y, but inference must be consistent and maximal.
- Authors use a MAXSAT approach to infer many consistent ordering constraints while avoiding overcommitment.
- Experiments vary variables, samples, and ancestral constraints and show the method is much faster than GOBNILP.
- Comparison to GOBNILP is confounded because GOBNILP performs poorly without parent-set cardinality limits, so it’s unclear if poor performance is due to ancestral constraints handling or general limitations.
- The supplementary encoding of ancestral constraints uses a MAXSAT encoding (Cussens 2008) rather than the ILP encoding of Cussens 2010, which is unsuitable for ILP systems.
- Proposes a better ILP formulation introducing A(X,Y) binary ancestor variables with transitivity and linking constraints A(X,Y) ≥ ∑{U:X ∈ U} I(Y,U), yielding a tighter linear relaxation and likely better performance.
- Claims that with a sensible ILP formulation the proposed method would still outperform GOBNILP, but comparing to a poor ILP formulation weakens the paper.
- Recommends comparing to van Beek and Hoffmann’s approach (often better than GOBNILP), notes it’s not cited and could likely be adapted to ancestral constraints.
