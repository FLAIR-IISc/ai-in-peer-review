This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, allowing decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches to incorporate such domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The novelty primarily lies in Section 5, where the authors present a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. The paper is well-written and clearly illustrates the work, both theoretically and experimentally. However, I have some concerns and suggestions.

First, Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. Does this suggest that projecting constraints to decomposable forms is specifically beneficial for EC trees and doesn’t generalize well to other learning approaches? Given that the core innovation of this paper is inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating these decomposable constraints improves the performance and efficiency of learning approaches that *cannot* natively handle the non-decomposable constraints.

Second, Table 2 reveals an easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. What explains this pattern? Does it indicate that the effectiveness of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is more sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge than in the ILP approach? Consequently, does the number of ancestral constraints require careful tuning to achieve optimal performance?

Third, Table 2 only presents structural Hamming distance results for EC trees. How does the EC tree approach compare to ILP in terms of structural Hamming distance?

Finally, a minor suggestion: while the BN graph provides the theoretical foundation for EC trees, as the experiments focus solely on the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints, I suggest streamlining the paper by omitting Section 4.1 and dedicating more space to explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm within the EC tree setting.
