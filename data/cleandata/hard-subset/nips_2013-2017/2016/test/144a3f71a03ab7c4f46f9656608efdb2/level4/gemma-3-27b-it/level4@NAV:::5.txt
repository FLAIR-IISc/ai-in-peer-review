This work addresses the challenging problem of optimal Bayesian network configuration. The authors build upon the structure learning framework introduced by Chen et al. [2015, 2016] by demonstrating a method for integrating non-decomposable constraints, notably ancestral constraints. Their empirical assessment demonstrates substantial performance gains – improvements of several orders of magnitude in runtime – when compared to existing Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods. The core innovation lies in reformulating the problem as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. While my familiarity with this specific MaxSAT approach is limited, the reported runtime results are compelling, indicating a significant reduction in computational cost relative to current state-of-the-art alternatives.
