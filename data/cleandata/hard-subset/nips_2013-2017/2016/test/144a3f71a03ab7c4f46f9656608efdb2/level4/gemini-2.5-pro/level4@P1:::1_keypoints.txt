- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Method: extends an A*-based EC-tree search over CPDAGs to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Key technique 1: prune CPDAG nodes inconsistent with ancestral constraints.  
- Key technique 2: infer decomposable “projected” constraints (edges/orderings) from ancestral constraints to improve the A* heuristic (URLEarning).  
- Empirical claim: method yields massive speedups (orders of magnitude) vs ILP-based GOBNILP on tested problems (up to 20 variables).  
- Strength: tackles an important, practical problem of incorporating expert causal knowledge.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated extension of the EC-tree search framework.  
- Strength: projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones is a key insight that greatly boosts heuristic accuracy and search speed.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation shows strong scalability/efficiency on the reported benchmarks.  
- Weakness: unclear technical justification for inferring ordering constraints from ancestral constraints—ambiguous whether added orders are entailed or merely consistent; goal of finding a maximal consistent set should be made explicit.  
- Weakness: proof of Lemma 3 (supplementary) is hard to follow; specific step about EC tree edge generation (Z -> W) needs more justification.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison confounded—A*-based methods naturally outperform ILP when no parent-set limit is used, so superiority over GOBNILP may reflect this rather than constraint handling.  
- Weakness: ILP baseline encoding appears suboptimal (based on Cussens 2008/MAX-SAT); a tighter ILP formulation (e.g., Cussens 2010 or using binary ancestry A(X,Y) and parent-set I(Z,U) vars with transitivity) would be a stronger baseline.  
- Weakness: missing related work—no citation/discussion of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which uses constraint programming and is a strong competitor to ILP approaches.  
- Summary: promising and fast algorithm, but empirical claims are weakened by potential confounds and a suboptimal ILP baseline; clearer technical exposition and stronger baselines/comparisons are needed.
