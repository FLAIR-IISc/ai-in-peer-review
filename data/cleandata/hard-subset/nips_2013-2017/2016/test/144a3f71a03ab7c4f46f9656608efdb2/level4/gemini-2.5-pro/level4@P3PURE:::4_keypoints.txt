- Proposal: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to let decomposable-score-based structure learners use ancestral knowledge and improve efficiency.  
- Novelty and clarity: main contribution is the projection method in Section 5, which is well-explained theoretically and empirically.  
- Concern 1: Table 3 shows improvement for EC tree but not ILP, raising questions about the projection’s generalizability and suggesting a need to test on algorithms that only accept decomposable constraints.  
- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an "easy-hard-easy" pattern for EC tree as constraint proportion increases; the authors should explain this and whether EC tree performance is highly sensitive to constraint quantity.  
- Concern 3: Table 2 omits a direct SHD comparison with the ILP approach under the same conditions; including ILP results would give a fuller comparison.  
- Suggestion 4: Rather than Section 4.1 on BN–EC tree theory, reallocate space to a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* search used in experiments to better match the empirical focus.
