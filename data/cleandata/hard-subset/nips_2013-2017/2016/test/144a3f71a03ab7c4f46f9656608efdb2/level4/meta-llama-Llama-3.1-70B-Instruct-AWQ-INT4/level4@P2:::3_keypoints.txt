- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints by translating them into maximal ordering constraints using a MaxSAT solver and a score-based EC-tree learner.
- Empirical evaluation shows the method significantly outperforms a straightforward ILP-based (Gobnilp) modification.
- The problem (optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints) is interesting and novel with no standard solution.
- The problem setting is somewhat artificial; real causal discovery often requires handling unobserved confounders.
- Translation maps hard-to-use ancestral constraints into ordering constraints that fit decomposable scores.
- MaxSAT mapping makes implied ancestral constraints explicit.
- Some mapping rules seem strange or incomplete, raising concerns about practical behavior.
- Unclear how the method performs when only a few ancestral constraints are provided.
- Comparison to Gobnilp may be unfair because Gobnilp was not designed for this problem.
- A more meaningful baseline would be a greedy algorithm like GES, which could match oracle outputs.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and supported by good examples/illustrations.
- Solution is conceptually straightforward but novel and useful for specialists.
- Main weaknesses: artificial setting, odd/incomplete rules, and limited comparisons to other algorithms.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the listed concerns.
