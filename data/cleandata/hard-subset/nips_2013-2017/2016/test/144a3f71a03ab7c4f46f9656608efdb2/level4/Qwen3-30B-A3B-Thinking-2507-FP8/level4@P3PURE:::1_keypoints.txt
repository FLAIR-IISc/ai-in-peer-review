- Paper proposes branch-and-bound BN structure learning under ancestral constraints using CPDAG nodes, parent-set selection transitions, symmetry breaking, and a URLearning heuristic.  
- Ancestral-constraint-based pruning is enabled by Lemma 3 (supplementary); the lemma seems correct but its proof needs clearer explanation (specifically the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gₖ also contains edge Z → W").  
- Authors distinguish "projected/implied constraints" and "ordering constraints" from ancestral constraints, but phrase "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading — such ordering constraints may be added (not entailed) and the inference method is insufficiently explained.  
- Experiments vary variables, data size, and ancestral constraints and report large speedups over GOBNILP.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is problematic because without a parent-set size limit GOBNILP’s poor performance is expected; it’s unclear whether the performance gap is due to GOBNILP’s general inefficiency with large parent sets or its handling of ancestral constraints.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints for GOBNILP using a MAXSAT formulation from Cussens (2008), which is suboptimal for ILP solvers; a simpler ILP encoding using ancestor variables A(X,Y) and parent indicators I(Z,U) (avoiding O(n³) E variables) yields a tighter relaxation and better performance (as in Cussens 2010).  
- Using a poor MAXSAT encoding for GOBNILP weakens the empirical claims— a sensible ILP encoding would be a stronger baseline, though the proposed method would likely still outperform GOBNILP.  
- The paper omits comparison to van Beek and Hoffmann (2015), a method that often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted for ancestral constraints; this is a notable missing benchmark.
