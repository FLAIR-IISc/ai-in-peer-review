- Paper tackles constrained optimization for learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAG nodes.  
- Search moves from CPDAG₁ to CPDAG₂ by selecting parent sets for variables missing in CPDAG₁, with sensible symmetry breaking.  
- URLearning provides heuristic guidance for the search (details deferred to earlier work).  
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning; Lemma 3 (supplementary) is pivotal but its proof is unclear—specifically the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gₖ also contains edge Z → W" needs elaboration.  
- The term "implied constraints" corresponds to standard "projected constraints" (edge constraints derived from ancestral constraints) used for pruning.  
- "Ordering constraints" (e.g., inferred from "Z not an ancestor of Y") are also used to prune the search.  
- The statement "can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading; one can add a consistent ordering like Y<Z, but the authors intend to infer all consistent ordering constraints via MAXSAT to avoid over-inference.  
- Experiments show large speedups over GOBNILP, but it is unclear whether this is due to ancestral-constraint handling or because GOBNILP typically underperforms A-based methods without parent-set limits.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints using a suboptimal MAXSAT formulation from Cussens (2008) instead of the stronger ILP encoding from Cussens (2010), unnecessarily introducing cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables.  
- A tighter ILP would use binary ancestor A(X,Y) and parent I(Z,U) variables with constraints A(X,Y) ≥ Σ_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U) plus transitivity on A, yielding better relaxations and performance; comparing against the weaker encoding weakens the claims.  
- A comparison to van Beek and Hoffmann’s CP-based method (which often outperforms GOBNILP) would be valuable, and adapting it to ancestral constraints appears feasible but was omitted.
