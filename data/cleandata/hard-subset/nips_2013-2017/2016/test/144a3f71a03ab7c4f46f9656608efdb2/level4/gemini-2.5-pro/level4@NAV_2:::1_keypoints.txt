- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which breaks standard DP/heuristic methods.  
- Core contributions: Extend A* over equivalence-class (EC) tree with (1) pruning by satisfiability checks on CPDAGs and (2) “empowering” the A* heuristic by projecting decomposable edge/ordering constraints (using MaxSAT) from ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical claim: Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on tested problems.  

Strengths:
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem (incorporating ancestral/causal/temporal background knowledge).  
- Originality: Novel integration of non-decomposable constraints into A* via pruning and heuristic strengthening; MaxSAT-based maximal consistent ordering inference is clever.  
- Quality: Methodology appears sound and experiments show strong practical improvements.  

Weaknesses:
- Clarity: Key technical details need clearer exposition (e.g., Lemma 3 proof; justification for EC tree edge statement).  
- Constraint inference explanation: Confuses inferring entailed orderings with finding a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints; needs clearer distinction.  
- Evaluation setting: Experiments omit parent-set cardinality limits, a setting that favors A* over ILP and confounds attribution of gains to the ancestral-constraint handling.  
- Baseline ILP encoding: The GOBNILP encoding of ancestral constraints appears suboptimal; a tighter ILP (e.g., linking ancestor variables to parent-set indicators per Cussens 2010) could yield a stronger baseline.  
- Related work omission: Missing discussion of van Beek & Hoffmann (2015) constraint-programming approach, which is a relevant competitor.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, technically sound method for a difficult and important problem.  
- Powerful idea of empowering decomposable-score oracles with constraints inferred from non-decomposable ones.  
- Empirical results indicate substantial practical speedups.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Experimental comparison is weakened by a favorable A* setup and a potentially weak ILP formulation, making the claimed advantage less convincing.  
- Key proofs and the description of constraint inference lack clarity needed for reproducibility and verification.  
- Important related work in constraint-based learning is not discussed.  

Summary: Strong, novel contribution with promising empirical gains, but needs clearer technical exposition, a stronger/more fair baseline, and discussion of related constraint-programming work to fully substantiate claims.
