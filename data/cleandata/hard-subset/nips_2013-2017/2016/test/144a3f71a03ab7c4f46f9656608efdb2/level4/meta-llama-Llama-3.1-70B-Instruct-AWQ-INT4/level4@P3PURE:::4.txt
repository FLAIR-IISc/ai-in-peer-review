This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, thereby enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches to incorporate domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper primarily lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce their proposed method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable. The paper is well-written and clearly illustrates the authors' work in both the theoretical and experimental sections.

I have some concerns and suggestions as follows:

i. Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints only improves the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. Does this imply that projecting ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints is only effective for EC trees, and has limited generalization ability to other learning approaches? Given that the main innovation of this paper lies in inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating such decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in the EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. What is the reason behind this phenomenon? Does it suggest that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, compared to the ILP approach? Consequently, does the number of ancestral constraints need to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance?

iii. Table 2 only presents the results of the structural Hamming distance for EC trees. How does this compare to the ILP approach?

iv. A minor suggestion: while I understand that the BN graph contains the theoretical foundation of EC trees, as EC trees are an extension of it, I would recommend removing Section 4.1 and allocating more space to providing a clearer explanation of EC trees and the A\* algorithm in the context of EC trees.
