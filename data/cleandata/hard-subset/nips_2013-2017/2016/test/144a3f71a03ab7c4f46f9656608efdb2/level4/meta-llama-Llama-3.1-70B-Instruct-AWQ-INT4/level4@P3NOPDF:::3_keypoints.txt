- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by converting them to maximal ordering constraints used in an EC-tree score-based algorithm.
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit by translating hard-to-use ancestral constraints into ordering constraints compatible with decomposable scores.
- Evaluated against a modified ILP-based Gobnilp and shows significant outperformance in the given experiments.
- Paper is clearly written and explained, but the solution is conceptually straightforward.
- Problem is novel and of specialist interest since no standard solution exists for combining optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints (novelty=3).
- The experimental setting may be artificial because real ancestral relations often require causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders (impact=2).
- Some formal rules (e.g., rules 257–261) appear incomplete or incorrect—acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but asymmetric conclusion, and rule 4 wrongly excludes mutual non-causation.
- In practice only a few ancestral constraints are typically available, so they are unlikely to substantially reduce the search space.
- Comparison to Gobnilp is not fully convincing because Gobnilp was not designed for this specific problem; a comparison to greedy algorithms like GES under oracle-aligned constraints would be more informative.
- Theorem 1 should clarify that the set of constraints A satisfied by CPDAG P on the EC tree is only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G.
- Theorem 3 may produce few positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.
- Notation for expressing acyclicity (line 267) is unclear and may not be easily recognizable.
- For reasonable graph sizes, 10%–25% ancestral constraints would already imply extensive knowledge of the target graph, which is atypical in practice.
- Minor editorial suggestions: clarify standard vs omniscient oracle (line 30), rephrase “empower the oracle” (line 38), and specify that “optimizing” refers to minimizing (line 90).
