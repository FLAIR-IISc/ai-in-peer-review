- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into ordering constraints and integrating into an EC tree score-based algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to ensure all implied ancestral constraints are explicitly captured in the translation.  
- Empirical evaluation against a modified ILP-based Gobnilp shows significant performance improvements.  
- Paper is well written and clearly explained, though the solution is conceptually straightforward.  
- The problem is novel but somewhat artificial; when ancestral relations are considered, more general causal discovery methods handling unobserved confounders may be more appropriate (low impact).  
- The translation maps hard-to-use ancestral constraints into ordering/edge-absence constraints compatible with decomposable scores.  
- Several logical rules (lines 257–261) appear problematic: Rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion, and Rule 4 may wrongly exclude "X and Y do not cause each other" constraints.  
- In practice, users typically provide few ancestral constraints, so these are unlikely to substantially reduce the search space.  
- The reported comparison to Gobnilp is not ideal since Gobnilp is not designed for this problem; a more meaningful benchmark would be a greedy algorithm like GES under oracle-aligned constraints.  
- Theorem 1 needs clarification that the set of constraints A satisfied by CPDAG P on the EC tree is a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G to validate the iff claim.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- The constraint at line 267 is not clearly recognizable as encoding acyclicity and needs clarification.  
- Minor wording/clarity fixes: Line 30 distinguish standard vs omniscient oracle; Line 38 rephrase "Empower the oracle"; Line 90 specify that optimization means minimization.  
- Practical note: 10%–25% ancestral constraints would imply extensive knowledge of the target graph and are unlikely in typical scenarios.
