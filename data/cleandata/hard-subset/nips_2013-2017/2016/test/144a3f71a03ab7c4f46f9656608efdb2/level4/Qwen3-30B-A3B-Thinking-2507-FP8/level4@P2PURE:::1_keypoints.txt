- Proposes branch-and-bound search over CPDAG nodes for Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints, with symmetry breaking and parent-set-driven transitions.
- Uses system A with a URLearning-provided heuristic; heuristic details are referenced to prior work rather than fully explained here.
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning; Lemma 3 is the key theoretical result but its proof is unclear—specifically the step "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gₖ also contains edge Z → W" needs elaboration.
- The paper uses the term "projected constraints" where "implied constraints" is standard terminology.
- Inferred ordering constraints aid pruning, but the method for inferring these constraints is inadequately explained.
- The claim "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is problematic; authors appear to mean they add many consistent (not only entailed) ordering constraints and avoid over-inference via a MAXSAT approach.
- Experiments (varying variables, data points, ancestral constraints) show the proposed system is significantly faster than GOBNILP.
- Because parent-set cardinality is unbounded in experiments, it is unclear whether GOBNILP's poor performance is due to ancestral-constraint handling or difficulty with large parent sets.
- The supplementary-material encoding of ancestral constraints uses Cussens (2008) MAXSAT instead of the tighter ILP encoding from Cussens (2010), which is suboptimal for ILP comparison.
- A better ILP formulation would use A(X,Y) ancestor and I(Z,U) parent-set variables (omitting cubic E(X,Y,Z)), enforce transitivity and linking constraints, and yield a tighter linear relaxation and much better ILP performance.
- The comparison is weakened by using a poor MAXSAT encoding for GOBNILP; even so, the proposed system would likely outperform a sensible ILP baseline.
- Recommend comparing against van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and could be straightforwardly adapted for ancestral constraints, but was not included.
