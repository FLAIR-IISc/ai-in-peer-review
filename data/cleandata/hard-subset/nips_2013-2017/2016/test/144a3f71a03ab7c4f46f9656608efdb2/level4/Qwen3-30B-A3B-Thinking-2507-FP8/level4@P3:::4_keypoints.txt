- Paper proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to enable efficient Bayesian network structure learning within the EC tree search framework.  
- Uses Chen et al.’s EC tree space and shows projection yields large efficiency gains over ILP-based methods like GOBNILP.  
- Core contributions: sound and complete inference of edge constraints (Theorems 2–3) and ordering constraints via a MaxSAT reduction (Section 5.1–5.2).  
- Addresses a literature gap: extends score-based optimal learning to handle ancestral (non-decomposable) background knowledge.  
- Theoretical foundation is rigorous with clear completeness proofs (Theorems 2–4).  
- Experimental design is thorough, using standard benchmarks with controlled variations in size, data amount, and constraint density.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups (Tables 1–2) and improved structural Hamming distance equivalent to increasing data by ~4× (Table 3).  
- Paper is very clear and well-written, with precise definitions, intuitive examples, and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- The constraint projection technique and the MaxSAT reduction for ordering constraints are novel and not present in prior ILP/CP approaches.  
- The work is practically significant, enabling scalable incorporation of causal knowledge and showing that modest constraint sets can substantially improve learning.  
- Weakness: Claim of broad generalizability is overstated because Table 2 shows no ILP efficiency gains from projection; authors should clarify projection mainly benefits oracle-based methods and explain why ILP can worsen.  
- Weakness: EC tree exhibits an "easy-hard-easy" performance pattern across constraint densities; authors should analyze why intermediate densities peak and implications for unknown real-world constraint density.  
- Weakness: Comparative results omit ILP’s structural Hamming distance in Table 3, leaving unclear whether EC tree’s speedups trade off accuracy; include ILP Hamming distances.  
- Weakness: Section 4.1 on BN graphs is largely redundant and should be condensed to focus the paper on the EC tree contributions.  
- Overall conclusion: Strong, technically sound, novel, and well-validated contribution that merits acceptance after clarifying generalizability and analyzing the constraint-density behavior.
