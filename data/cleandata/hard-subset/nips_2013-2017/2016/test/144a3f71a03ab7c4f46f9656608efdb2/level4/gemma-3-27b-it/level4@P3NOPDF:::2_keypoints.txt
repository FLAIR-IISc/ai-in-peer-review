- Novel idea: leveraging ancestral constraints to guide an admissible heuristic for informed exhaustive search, promising for hard combinatorial problems.
- Theoretical justification for the heuristic’s admissibility appears sound but would benefit from a more intuitive explanation.
- Experimental setup and presentation are confusing, making it hard to interpret performance gains.
- Table 1 mixes EC + proposed method vs GOB + modified method, preventing isolation of the proposed method’s effect.
- Table 3 is ambiguous about whether it compares baseline EC vs baseline GOB or their modified versions.
- The paper needs a direct comparison of EC with and without the proposed method to demonstrate absolute benefit.
- Insufficient detail about the modifications labeled “new work” when integrating the method with GOB and the rationale for those changes.
- A visual illustration or example showing how ancestral constraints prune the search space would improve clarity.
- Related work is adequate but should more clearly articulate how this approach differs from and improves upon existing heuristics.
- Strengths: novel constraint-based heuristic idea, theoretical admissibility, tackles a challenging problem.
- Weaknesses: confusing experiments, missing EC ablation, unclear GOB-specific modifications, weakly differentiated related work.
- Suggested fixes: restructure experiments to show with/without comparisons, clarify “new work” changes, add pruning visualization, expand related-work discussion.
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but currently hard to evaluate; reviewer leans toward weak reject pending clarifications and stronger experimental evidence.
