This paper addresses the problem of learning optimal Bayesian network structures under complex, non-decomposable constraints. The authors propose a novel approach that extends the structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] to incorporate such constraints, with a particular focus on ancestral constraints. The core contribution lies in formulating the score-based structure learning problem as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) problem. This formulation allows for the efficient encoding of both the standard acyclicity and parent-set constraints, as well as the more complex non-decomposable constraints.

The primary strength of this work lies in its impressive empirical performance. The authors conduct a thorough experimental evaluation, comparing their MaxSAT-based solver against state-of-the-art Integer Linear Programming (ILP) methods, which are a common choice for this exact optimization problem. The results clearly demonstrate that the proposed approach is significantly faster, achieving runtime improvements of several orders of magnitude on a variety of benchmark datasets. This performance gain is a substantial practical contribution, as it potentially makes exact structure learning feasible for problem instances that were previously intractable with ILP-based techniques.

The technical approach of reframing the problem in terms of MaxSAT is both novel and elegant for this application. While my own expertise is not primarily in satisfiability solving, the empirical evidence presented is compelling. The paper successfully makes the case that MaxSAT solvers are a powerful and highly efficient alternative to ILP for Bayesian network structure learning, especially when dealing with challenging side constraints.

Overall, the paper is well-motivated and addresses a significant problem in the field. The proposed method is technically sound, and the experimental results are exceptionally strong, highlighting a clear and impactful advantage over existing methods. This work is a valuable contribution to the literature on graphical model learning.
