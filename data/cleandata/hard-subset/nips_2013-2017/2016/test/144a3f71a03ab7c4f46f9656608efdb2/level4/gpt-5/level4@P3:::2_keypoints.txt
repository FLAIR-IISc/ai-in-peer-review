- Problem: exact BN structure learning with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Contribution (i): pruning EC-tree (CPDAG) nodes that cannot satisfy ancestral constraints, with efficient checks and fallback to enumerating class(P).  
- Contribution (ii): projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge-presence/absence and ordering constraints to improve the oracle used in A*.  
- Contribution (iii): MaxSAT-based inference of a large, provably safe set of ordering constraints.  
- Empirical claim: orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) and clear benefits from projection.  
- Relation to prior work: extends EC/BN search to non-decomposable constraints, complements DP/A* and ILP/CP approaches, and provides an exact score-based alternative to path-constraint heuristics.  

- Strength: addresses a practical, important form of prior knowledge (ancestral/path constraints) in exact learning.  
- Strength: sound EC-tree integration with intuitive pruning rules and Theorem 1.  
- Strength: useful projection rules for negative-edge entailment (Theorem 2) and reasonable sufficient conditions for positive edges (Theorem 3).  
- Strength: MaxSAT formulation provides a flexible, provably safe way to infer many ordering constraints (Theorem 4).  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating significant speedups and sensible easy–hard–easy behavior; good SHD analysis.  

- Weakness: experimental presentation is unclear—need explicit labeling of EC vs. EC+Proj and ILP vs. ILP+Proj and whether Table 3 uses projections.  
- Weakness: lack of ablation within EC (pruning only vs. pruning+edge vs. pruning+ordering vs. both) to quantify projection contributions.  
- Weakness: reporting omissions—absolute runtimes, node expansions/A* queue sizes, timeouts, and runtime breakdowns missing.  
- Weakness: insufficient solver details for GOBNILP (acyclicity/constraint encodings, solver versions, parameters).  
- Weakness: MaxSAT reporting incomplete—need instance sizes and solve times relative to total runtime.  
- Weakness: completeness/admissibility clarification required—must state only guaranteed-entailed constraints are added and note MaxSAT may be only maximal under sufficient conditions.  
- Weakness: complexity/scalability concerns—need worst-case cost discussion for enumerating class(P) and MaxSAT construction/solving as n grows and report how often enumeration fallback occurred.  
- Weakness: robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints not addressed—need discussion, infeasibility detection, and ideally experiments.  
- Weakness: notational and presentation issues—typos, stray text, incomplete citations, inconsistent notation, unclear definition/complexity of closure for “maximum set of ancestral constraints.”  

- Quality: technically sound with theorems guiding pruning and projection; full proofs or expanded sketches in supplement would help.  
- Clarity: core ideas well-motivated but experiments, CPDAG pruning explanation, and MaxSAT encoding require clearer organization and succinct summaries.  
- Originality: novel projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints and MaxSAT use for safe ordering inference within EC-tree.  
- Significance: meaningful practical contribution enabling real prior knowledge use with large empirical gains.  

- Pros for acceptance: enables exact handling of non-decomposable ancestral constraints; strong empirical performance; clean conceptual integration of pruning and safe projection.  
- Cons against acceptance: experimental/ablation clarity lacking; limited discussion of worst-case costs and MaxSAT scaling; no evaluation under noisy or inconsistent constraints.  

- Suggested revision 1: reorganize experiments to explicitly compare EC vs. EC+Projections and ILP vs. ILP+Projections and add edge vs. ordering projection ablation.  
- Suggested revision 2: report runtime breakdowns, node expansions/A* queue sizes, timeouts, and full solver configurations.  
- Suggested revision 3: add a subsection on computational complexity and frequency/cost of CPDAG enumeration and MaxSAT as n grows.  
- Suggested revision 4: discuss handling of inconsistent/noisy constraints and infeasibility detection; include a small robustness experiment if possible.  
- Suggested revision 5: clean up notation, citations, typos, define “maximum set of ancestral constraints” precisely, and state closure algorithm complexity.  

- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying experiments, addressing presentation/complexity/noise concerns, and strengthening supplementary proofs/documentation.
