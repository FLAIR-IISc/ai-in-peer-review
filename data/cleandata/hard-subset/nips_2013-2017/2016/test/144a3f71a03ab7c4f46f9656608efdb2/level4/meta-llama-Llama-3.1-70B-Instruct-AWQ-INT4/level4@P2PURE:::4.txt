This paper proposes a novel method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches to incorporate domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The key contribution of this paper lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce their proposed method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable. The paper is well-written, clearly illustrating the theoretical and experimental aspects of their work.

I have some concerns and suggestions as follows:

i. Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints only improves the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether the proposed method of projecting ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints is specific to EC trees and lacks generalizability to other learning approaches. Given that the main innovation of this paper is inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating such decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in the EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. What causes this phenomenon? Does it imply that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, compared to the ILP approach? If so, this suggests that the number of ancestral constraints needs to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance.

iii. Table 2 only presents results on the structural Hamming distance for EC trees. How does this compare to the ILP approach?

iv. A minor suggestion: while it is understood that the BN graph provides the theoretical foundation for EC trees, as EC trees are an extension of BN graphs, the experiments only consider the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints applied. Therefore, it might be beneficial to remove Section 4.1 and allocate more space to explaining EC trees and the A\* algorithm in the context of EC trees.
