- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) framework for non-decomposable scores and extends it to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Key technical contribution: sound and complete pruning method for the EC tree search space using ancestral constraints.  
- Introduces a way to pass decomposable constraints to the oracle inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, improving pruning.  
- Technical presentation is rigorous, with thorough analysis of the proposed approach.  
- Empirical evaluation shows significant efficiency gains over ILP-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and concise in exposition.  
- Originality: distinct extension of existing work with meaningful improvements in efficiency.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and has implications for efficient structure-learning algorithms.  
- Concern: approach may be too specialized and technically challenging for non-expert readers.  
- Concern: empirical evaluation is limited to specific benchmarks; generalizability to other datasets is unclear.  
- Concern: would benefit from additional comparisons to other state-of-the-art structure-learning methods.
