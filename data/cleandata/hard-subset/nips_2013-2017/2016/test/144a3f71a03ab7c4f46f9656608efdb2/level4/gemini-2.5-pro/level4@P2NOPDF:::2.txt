This paper proposes a method for improving exhaustive search algorithms by incorporating a given set of ancestral constraints to guide the admissible heuristic.

The experimental evaluation, particularly the comparison between baselines, could be clarified. The paper introduces two existing approaches, EC and GOB, as baselines. My understanding is that Table 1 compares the proposed method applied to each of these baselines (i.e., EC + proposal vs. a variant of GOB + proposal). Subsequently, Table 3 appears to present a direct comparison between the original, unmodified baselines (EC vs. GOB). It would be helpful if the authors could confirm this interpretation.

To convincingly demonstrate the value of the proposed technique, a direct comparison between the enhanced EC algorithm (EC + proposal) and the original EC baseline is essential. This ablation study would isolate the contribution of the ancestral constraints and quantify their impact on what appears to be the state-of-the-art method. While the comparison showing the impact on GOB is interesting, the existing results suggest that EC is a substantially stronger baseline. Consequently, even if the proposed method yields a larger relative improvement when applied to GOB, the most critical result for the community is the absolute performance of the best available system, which would be the enhanced EC. The key question the experiments should answer is whether the proposed framework improves upon EC, and by how much. Without this direct comparison, it is difficult to assess the practical significance of the work.

I would strongly recommend the authors include this direct comparison (EC + proposal vs. EC) in a revised manuscript, as it would significantly strengthen the paper's claims and clarify the contribution.
