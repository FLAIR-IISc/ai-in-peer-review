This paper addresses the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network configuration, focusing on the incorporation of non-decomposable constraints, specifically ancestral constraints, into the structure learning approach of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. The authors reformulate the problem as a MaxSAT problem with specific constraints, which enables them to efficiently handle these complex constraints.

The empirical evaluation of the proposed approach against ILP-based methods demonstrates significant improvements, with orders of magnitude reductions in runtime. Although my expertise in this specific approach is limited, the results presented, particularly the runtime comparison, appear impressive. The authors show that their method achieves substantial runtime reductions compared to alternative methods, making it a promising solution for optimal Bayesian network configuration.

The paper's contribution lies in its ability to effectively incorporate non-decomposable constraints into the structure learning process, which is a challenging task. The authors' reformulation of the problem as a MaxSAT problem allows for efficient handling of these constraints, leading to improved performance. The empirical evaluation provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach, demonstrating its potential for real-world applications.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning, offering a novel approach to handling non-decomposable constraints. The results are well-presented, and the comparison with ILP-based methods provides a clear understanding of the proposed approach's advantages.
