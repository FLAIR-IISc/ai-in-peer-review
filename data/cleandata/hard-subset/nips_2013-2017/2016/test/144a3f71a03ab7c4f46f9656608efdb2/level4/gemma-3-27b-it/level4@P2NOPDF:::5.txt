This paper addresses the challenging problem of optimal Bayesian network structure learning, specifically extending the work of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] to incorporate non-decomposable constraints, including ancestral constraints. The key contribution lies in formulating this constrained structure learning problem as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. 

The empirical evaluation demonstrates substantial performance gains over existing Integer Linear Programming (ILP)-based methods. The reported results indicate runtime improvements of several orders of magnitude, suggesting a significant advancement in practical applicability. While my familiarity with the MaxSAT formulation is limited, the compelling runtime comparisons strongly support the authors' claims. 

The reformulation as a MaxSAT problem appears to be a crucial innovation, enabling efficient handling of complex constraints that are difficult to represent within traditional ILP frameworks. The magnitude of the observed speedup is particularly noteworthy and suggests that this approach could unlock the feasibility of Bayesian network structure learning for larger and more complex datasets where existing methods are computationally prohibitive. 

Further detail on the specific MaxSAT encoding and constraint satisfaction strategies employed would be beneficial for readers less familiar with this approach. However, the robust empirical results presented provide strong evidence of the method's effectiveness.
