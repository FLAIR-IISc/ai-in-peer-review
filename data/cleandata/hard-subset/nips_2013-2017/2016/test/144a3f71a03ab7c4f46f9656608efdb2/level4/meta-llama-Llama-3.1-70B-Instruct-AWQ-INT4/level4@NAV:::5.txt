This paper tackles the problem of determining the optimal configuration of a Bayesian network. Specifically, the authors demonstrate how to integrate non-decomposable constraints, particularly ancestral constraints, into the structure learning approach proposed by Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. The empirical evaluation of the proposed approach reveals significant improvements over ILP-based methods, with the results showing substantial reductions in runtime. By reformulating the problem as a MaxSAT problem with specific constraints, the authors achieve notable runtime enhancements, exhibiting orders of magnitude improvements compared to alternative methods, as evidenced by the runtime comparison.
