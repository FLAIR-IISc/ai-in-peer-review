The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm.

I found the experimental evaluation somewhat confusing. As I understand it, Table 1 compares two existing approaches, EC and GOB, after both have been enhanced by the proposed method. Table 3 then appears to compare the original EC and GOB baselines directly. If this interpretation is incorrect, the experimental setup could be described more clearly.

For the paper's contribution to be evaluated effectively, a direct comparison between the enhanced EC and the original EC baseline is essential. While the comparison demonstrating the method's impact on GOB is interesting, the results suggest that EC is a significantly stronger baseline. Consequently, even if the proposed method provides a much larger *relative* improvement to GOB, the *absolute* performance of the enhanced EC system is likely to be the most relevant practical outcome. Therefore, the most critical comparison needed to assess the value of this work is the one that quantifies the improvement over the stronger EC baseline.
