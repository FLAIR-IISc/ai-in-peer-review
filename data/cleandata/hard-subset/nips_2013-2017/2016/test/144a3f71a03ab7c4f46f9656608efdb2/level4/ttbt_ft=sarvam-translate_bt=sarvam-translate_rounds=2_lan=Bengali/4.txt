This paper proposes a method for converting non-distinct ancestor constraints into distinct constraints, enabling distinct-score-based learning methods to incorporate such domain knowledge and thereby improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper lies primarily in Section 5, where the authors present their proposed method for converting non-distinct ancestor constraints into edge-based and topological ordering constraints, which are distinct. The paper is well-written and clearly illustrates their work on theory and experimentation. Here are some of my concerns and suggestions: i. Table 3 shows that the projection of ancestor constraints can only improve the performance of the EC tree, not the ILP method. Does this mean that the projection of ancestor constraints in distinct constraints only works for the EC tree and its generalization ability is weak for other learning methods? The main innovation of this paper is estimating distinct constraints from non-distinct ancestor constraints. This would be more convincing if the authors could show that incorporating such distinct constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of specific learning methods that non-distinct constraints cannot handle. ii. Table 2 shows a simple-hard-easy pattern of EC tree performance with increasing ancestor constraint ratio. Why does this happen? Does this mean that the effect of reducing the search space of the EC tree with ancestor constraints is quite sensitive to the domain knowledge it incorporates compared to the ILP method? Therefore, is it necessary to carefully determine the number of ancestor constraints to achieve ideal performance? iii. Table 2 only shows the results of structural Hamming distance from the EC tree. But, how does this compare to the ILP method? iv. A small suggestion: I understand that the theoretical basis of the EC tree is in the BN graph, because the EC tree expands from it. However, since the experimental part only considers the search space of the EC tree with the application of ancestor constraints, I will skip section 4.1 and spend more time explaining the EC tree and the A* algorithm in the EC tree settings.
