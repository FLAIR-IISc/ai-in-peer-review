- Method: branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs with symmetry breaking, A* search using URLearning heuristic (details deferred to prior work).
- Central theoretical claim (Lemma 3) appears correct but the proof is unclear; the step “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs justification.
- The paper uses “projected”/implied ancestral and ordering constraints, but the explanation is unclear and the wording (“can infer Y < Z”) is misleading about whether constraints are entailed or chosen.
- Authors use a MAXSAT approach to select many consistent ordering constraints, but the selection rationale and limits are not clearly explained.
- Empirics: proposed method is much faster than GOBNILP, but the comparison may be unfair because GOBNILP is known to struggle without a parent-set cardinality bound used in these experiments.
- The MAXSAT-derived MAXSAT/ILP encoding of ancestral constraints is weak for ILP solvers; a stronger ILP with ancestor variables A(X,Y) and transitivity/linking constraints (as in Cussens 2010) would give a tighter relaxation.
- Because the authors did not compare against a stronger ILP formulation, their empirical claims are weakened even if the new method would likely remain superior.
- Related work omission: the van Beek & Hoffmann CP-based BN learning approach (often outperforming GOBNILP) is not cited or compared and could likely be adapted to ancestral constraints.
