This paper proposes a novel approach to projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, thereby enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning methods to incorporate domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The primary contribution of this work lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce their method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable. The paper is well-written and effectively conveys the theoretical and experimental aspects of the work.

I have several concerns and suggestions to further enhance the paper:

i. Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints only improves the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. This raises questions about the generalizability of the proposed method. Does it only work for EC trees, and does it have limited applicability to other learning approaches? To strengthen the paper's main contribution, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in the EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. What causes this phenomenon? Does it suggest that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, unlike the ILP approach? If so, does this imply that the number of ancestral constraints needs to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance?

iii. Table 2 only presents the results of the structural Hamming distance from the EC trees. How do these results compare to those obtained using the ILP approach?

iv. A minor suggestion: while the BN graph provides theoretical foundations for EC trees, the experimental section focuses solely on the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints. I recommend removing Section 4.1 and dedicating more space to explaining EC trees and the A\* algorithm in the context of EC trees, as this would provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.
