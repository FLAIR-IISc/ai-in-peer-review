- Main contribution: Section 5 proposes a novel method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints for use in decomposable-score-based structure learning.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly communicates theory and experiments.
- Concern: Table 3 shows projection helps EC trees but not ILP, raising doubts about the method’s generalizability beyond EC trees.
- Request: Provide results showing performance improvements or enabled constraint handling for a learning approach that cannot natively process the original non-decomposable constraints.
- Observation: Table 2 shows an unusual easy–hard–easy EC tree performance pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; explanation is needed.
- Implication: The benefit of reducing the EC tree search space via ancestral constraints may be highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge, implying careful tuning is required.
- Missing comparison: Table 2 reports only EC-tree structural Hamming distance—add ILP comparisons for a fuller performance picture.
- Suggestion: Remove Section 4.1 and instead expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm within the EC-tree experimental context.
