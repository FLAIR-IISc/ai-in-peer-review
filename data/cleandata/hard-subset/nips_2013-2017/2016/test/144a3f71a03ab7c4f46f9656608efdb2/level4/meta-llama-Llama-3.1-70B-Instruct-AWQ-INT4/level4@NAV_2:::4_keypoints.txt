- Introduces a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Uses a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores to accommodate ancestral constraints.  
- Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints.  
- Enables decomposable-score oracles by supplying decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints.  
- Presents a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree search space using ancestral constraints.  
- Experimental evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based alternatives.  
- Evaluates impact of ancestral constraints on search efficiency and scalability as variable count grows.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow with helpful figures and tables.  
- Technically sound with thorough analysis and sufficient background/related work.  
- Original contribution: novel use of non-decomposable scores and projection to decomposable constraints.  
- Significant potential to improve efficiency and accuracy in domains where ancestral constraints are common.  
- Limitation: approach may not generalize beyond the EC tree search space to other learning methods.  
- Empirical evaluation could be strengthened with more baseline comparisons and a wider range of datasets.  
- Paper would benefit from more analysis of limitations and avenues for extension.  
- Summary decision rationale: strong contribution with clear strengths (novelty, pruning method, experiments) but concerns about generalizability and experimental breadth.
