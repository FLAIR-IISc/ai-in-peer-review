This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to ancestral constraints. The authors utilize a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree, and employ an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. They also propose a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, based on ancestral constraints. Furthermore, they show how the employed oracle can be empowered by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach. The authors provide a thorough review of the existing literature on Bayesian network structure learning and highlight the limitations of current methods in handling ancestral constraints. They also provide a detailed description of their approach, including the EC tree search space, the pruning method, and the oracle empowerment technique.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors compare their method to the GOBNILP system and show that it is orders-of-magnitude faster. They also vary the number of variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints to demonstrate the scalability and robustness of their approach.

However, there are a few limitations and potential improvements that can be noted. Firstly, the comparison to GOBNILP is not entirely fair, as the encoding of ancestral constraints used in GOBNILP is not optimal. A more sensible ILP formulation of ancestral constraints, as suggested by the reviewer, could potentially improve the performance of GOBNILP. Secondly, the paper does not provide a detailed comparison to other existing methods, such as the approach by van Beek and Hoffmann, which could be adapted to handle ancestral constraints.

In terms of the quality, clarity, and originality of the paper, I would rate it as follows:

* Quality: 8/10 (the paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach, but there are some limitations and potential improvements that can be noted)
* Clarity: 9/10 (the paper is well-organized and easy to follow, but some of the technical details may be challenging for non-experts to understand)
* Originality: 8/10 (the paper proposes a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to ancestral constraints, but some of the underlying ideas and techniques may not be entirely new)

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper for publication, subject to minor revisions to address some of the limitations and potential improvements noted above.
