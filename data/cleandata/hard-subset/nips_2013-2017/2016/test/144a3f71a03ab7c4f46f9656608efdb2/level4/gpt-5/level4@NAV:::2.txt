The authors propose a method that uses a specified set of ancestral constraints to guide an admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm. I found the experimental evaluation somewhat confusing. Given the existing methods EC and GOB, Table 1 appears to compare (EC + new work) with (GOB + new work'); if I’m interpreting this correctly, Table 3 then compares EC to GOB (or am I mistaken?). It seems advisable to include an explicit comparison between (EC + new work) and EC—although (GOB + new work') vs. GOB is informative, EC appears so much stronger than GOB that, even if GOB benefited more from the new work, (EC + new work) would still be the preferable choice.
