

The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm. The experimental comparisons are somewhat unclear. Specifically, Table 1 compares (EC + new work) against (GOB + new work'), yet Table 3 appears to compare EC against GOB (or is this misinterpretation?). An explicit comparison between (EC + new work) and EC would be more meaningful, as EC is significantly superior to GOB. Consequently, even if GOB experienced a larger relative improvement from the new work compared to EC, the overall performance of (EC + new work) would still dominate due to EC's strong baseline advantage.
