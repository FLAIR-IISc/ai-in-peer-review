- Main contribution: novel method (Section 5) to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints.  
- Paper quality: well-written with clear theoretical and experimental exposition.  
- Table 3 concern: projection improves EC trees but not ILP, raising questions about generalizability.  
- Applicability question: need to show projection benefits learning approaches that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Table 2 phenomenon: EC tree performance shows an easy–hard–easy pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases, requiring explanation.  
- Sensitivity/tuning: suggests the effect of ancestral constraints on EC tree search space may be highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge and may require careful tuning.  
- Missing comparison: Table 2 reports EC tree SHD but should include ILP comparison for a more complete evaluation.  
- Minor suggestion: remove Section 4.1 and allocate more space to explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm for clarity and focus.  
- Overall: valuable contribution, but addressing the above concerns would strengthen the work.
