- Paper proposes translating ancestral constraints into maximal ordering constraints and using them in an EC-tree score-based algorithm to learn optimal Bayesian networks.  
- Translation is performed with a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit.  
- Empirical results show strong outperformance over a modified ILP-based Gobnilp, though Gobnilp is not designed for this task.  
- The solution is conceptually straightforward, well-written, and of interest to a specialist audience, but not spectacular.  
- Novelty: addresses a new problem (optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints) lacking a standard solution (novelty=3).  
- Impact: limited because the setting is somewhat artificial and real problems often require causal discovery that handles unobserved confounders (impact=2).  
- Practical concern: in realistic settings only few ancestral constraints are given, so search-space reduction may be limited.  
- Evaluation issue: a more meaningful baseline would be greedy algorithms like GES (assuming constraints align with the optimal model) rather than Gobnilp.  
- Specific technical concerns: several presented rules (e.g., 257–261) seem unusual or incomplete and need clarification.  
- Acyclicity rule 3 appears to have a symmetric premise but a non-matching conclusion (possible logical error).  
- Rule 4 may wrongly exclude constraints expressing that two variables do not cause each other.  
- Theorem 1 should be restated to clarify that constraints A satisfied by a CPDAG on the EC tree are only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G.  
- Theorem 3 may yield few positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse; this limitation should be noted.  
- Presentation fixes and wording improvements suggested: clarify standard vs omniscient oracle, replace “empower the oracle,” specify “minimizing,” improve acyclicity-rule presentation, and acknowledge that 10–25% ancestral constraints already imply extensive knowledge of the target graph.
