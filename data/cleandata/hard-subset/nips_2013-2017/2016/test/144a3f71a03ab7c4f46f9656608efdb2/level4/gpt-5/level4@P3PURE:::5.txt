This paper addresses the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network structure and shows how to incorporate non-decomposable constraints—particularly ancestral constraints—into the structure learning framework of Chen et al. (2015, 2016). The authors reformulate the problem as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. Empirical evaluation against ILP-based approaches demonstrates orders-of-magnitude improvements in runtime. While my expertise with this approach is limited, the runtime comparisons appear compelling, with substantial reductions relative to alternative methods.
