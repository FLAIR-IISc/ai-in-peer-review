- Paper’s contribution: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints (novelty in Section 5), enabling decomposable-score-based learners to use this domain knowledge.  
- Presentation: well-written with clear theory and experiments.  
- Concern 1: Table 3 shows benefit for EC trees but not ILP—raises question whether projection benefits generalize beyond EC trees and whether it improves approaches that originally couldn’t handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an unusual easy–hard–easy pattern for EC tree performance as constraint proportion increases; needs explanation and suggests sensitivity to amount of constraints and potential need for careful tuning.  
- Concern 3: Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees—missing SHD comparison for the ILP baseline.  
- Minor suggestion: remove Section 4.1 and instead provide a deeper explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm within the EC tree setting.
