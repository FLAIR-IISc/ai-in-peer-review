This paper addresses the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network configuration, focusing on the incorporation of non-decomposable constraints, particularly ancestral constraints, into the structure learning approach of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. The authors propose a novel approach that reformulates the problem as a MaxSAT problem with specific constraints. They then evaluate their method empirically against ILP-based approaches, demonstrating significant improvements in terms of runtime, with orders of magnitude reductions compared to alternative methods. Although my familiarity with the MaxSAT approach is limited, the results of the runtime comparison appear impressive, showcasing the potential of this method for efficient Bayesian network configuration.
