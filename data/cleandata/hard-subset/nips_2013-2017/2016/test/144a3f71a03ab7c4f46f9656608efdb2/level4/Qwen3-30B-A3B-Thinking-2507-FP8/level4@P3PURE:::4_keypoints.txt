- Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable (edge and topological ordering) constraints to enable decomposable-score-based structure learning; main novelty in Section 5.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents theoretical and experimental contributions.  
- Table 3: projection improves performance only for EC trees, not ILP — questions the method’s generalizability to other learning frameworks.  
- Table 2: EC trees show an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases — needs explanation and suggests sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge (possibly requiring tuning).  
- Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees — missing direct comparison to ILP, which is needed to contextualize EC tree results.  
- Section 4.1 on BN theory appears redundant given experiments focus on EC trees; suggest removing it and elaborating on EC trees and the A* algorithm within that framework.
