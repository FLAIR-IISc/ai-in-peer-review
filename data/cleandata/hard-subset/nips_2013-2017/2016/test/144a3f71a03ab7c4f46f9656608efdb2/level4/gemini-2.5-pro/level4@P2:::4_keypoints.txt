- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by making them usable in decomposable-score algorithms.  
- Main contribution: project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-order constraints so an A* oracle can exploit them.  
- Core methods: logical derivation for edge constraints and a MaxSAT reduction to infer consistent ordering constraints.  
- Integrates projected constraints into an Equivalence Class (EC) tree search with an empowered A* oracle for exact structure learning.  
- Empirical claim: orders-of-magnitude speedup over a strong ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on the same problem.  
- Strength — significance/originality: novel bridge between expressive non-decomposable constraints and efficient decomposable-score learners.  
- Strength — technical quality: sound theorems for edge inference and well-founded MaxSAT reduction; complete EC-tree + A* framework.  
- Strength — clarity: well-written with clear background and presentation of contributions.  
- Strength — empirical evaluation: thorough experiments varying variables, dataset sizes, and constraint percentages, showing dramatic runtime improvements.  
- Weakness — projection generality: need discussion on why projected constraints benefit the A* oracle far more than an ILP solver.  
- Weakness — easy-hard-easy pattern: more detailed analysis needed of the runtime sensitivity and practical implications of intermediate constraint densities.  
- Weakness — solution quality comparison: missing SHD comparison for the ILP baseline to confirm identical optimal solutions.  
- Weakness — presentation: suggest condensing BN/EC-tree intro (Section 4.1) and expanding implementation details of the A* search within EC trees.
