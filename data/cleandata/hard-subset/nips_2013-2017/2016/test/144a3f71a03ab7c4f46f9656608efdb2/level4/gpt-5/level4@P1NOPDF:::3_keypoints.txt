- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal BN learning by converting them to ordering constraints and enforcing these in an EC-tree score-based search.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit and produce ordering constraints compatible with decomposable scores.  
- Evaluated against a modified ILP-based Gobnilp and shows clear performance gains in that setting.  
- Paper is well written and ideas are clearly explained, though the core idea is conceptually straightforward.  
- Novelty: addresses a new gap (optimal BN learning under ancestral constraints) but the setting is somewhat artificial (novelty = 3).  
- Impact: limited because practitioners often prefer causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders (impact = 2).  
- Concern: The rule system (lines 257–261) contains odd or incomplete rules—e.g., acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion, and rule 4 seems to exclude mutually noncausal constraints.  
- Practicality: Typical use will involve few ancestral constraints, so the search space may not shrink dramatically.  
- Baseline critique: Gobnilp with linear constraints may be an unfair or weak baseline; suggest comparing to greedy methods such as GES (under oracle input) to better assess practical efficiency.  
- Theorem 1 needs clearer wording: the set of constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG P on the EC tree should be stated as a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G.  
- Theorem 3 likely yields few positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Rule at line 267 is not clearly recognizable as encoding acyclicity and needs clarification.  
- Line 310’s claim that 10%–25% of ancestral constraints is realistic is misleading—this would encode extensive knowledge of the target graph.  
- Minor wording edits requested: clarify “oracle” vs “omniscient oracle” (L30), rephrase “empower the oracle” (L38), and state that “optimizing” means “minimizing” (L90).  
- Overall: A clear, well-executed contribution of definite interest to specialists, but evaluation could be strengthened and the rule set clarified.
