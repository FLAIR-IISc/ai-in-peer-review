- Paper tackles Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound on CPDAGs and A* search with URLearning heuristic, with reasonable symmetry breaking.  
- Authors exploit ancestral constraints for additional pruning; Lemma 3 is the key result but its proof is unclear (specifically the claim “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs more explanation).  
- The discussion of implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained; the phrase “we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is misleading—authors are choosing consistent ordering constraints rather than entailed ones.  
- The authors use a MAXSAT selection of ordering constraints, but the procedure for inferring and selecting these constraints is not well described.  
- Experiments show the proposed system much faster than GOBNILP, but it’s unclear if this is due to handling ancestral constraints or GOBNILP’s known weakness with unbounded parent-set size.  
- The supplementary MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints (from Cussens 2008) is a weak ILP formulation; a stronger ILP would use ancestor indicator variables A(X,Y) with transitivity and linking constraints to I(Y,U), avoiding cubic E(X,Y,Z) vars.  
- The paper is weakened by comparing against a poor formulation of GOBNILP rather than a stronger ILP encoding; reviewer believes the proposed method would still win but the comparison should be stronger.  
- Recommend comparing to van Beek & Hoffmann’s CP-based BN learning method (often outperforming GOBNILP), which is not cited and could likely be adapted to handle ancestral constraints.
