- Paper tackles Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAGs with A* guided by the URLearning heuristic and effective symmetry breaking.  
- Lemma 3 (supplement) is a key contribution enabling search pruning, but its proof is unclear—specifically the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs elaboration.  
- The paper introduces "projected" (implied) and ordering constraints, but the method for inferring ordering constraints is under-specified and the statement inferring Y < Z from Z not being an ancestor of Y is imprecise.  
- The authors seem to intend optional addition of Y < Z and use a MAXSAT approach to infer a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints to avoid over-constraining, but this process needs clearer explanation.  
- Experiments show large speedups over GOBNILP, yet it’s unclear whether this is due to GOBNILP’s general weakness with unbounded parent sets or specific difficulty handling ancestral constraints.  
- The ancestral-constraint encoding uses a MAXSAT formulation (Cussens 2008) which is suboptimal for ILP systems; a better ILP encoding (inspired by Cussens 2010) would use binary A(X,Y) ancestor variables, eliminate cubic E variables, enforce transitivity, and link A and I variables for a tighter relaxation.  
- The comparison to GOBNILP is weakened by using a suboptimal encoding; even so the authors likely outperform GOBNILP, but a stronger baseline or improved ILP encoding is needed.  
- The paper omits comparison and citation of van Beek & Hoffmann’s constraint-programming approach, which often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to handle ancestral constraints.
