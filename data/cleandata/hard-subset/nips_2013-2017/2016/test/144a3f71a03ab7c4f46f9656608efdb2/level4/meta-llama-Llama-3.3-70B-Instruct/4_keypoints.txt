- Proposes a method to transform non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to enable decomposable-score-based structure learning.  
- Main technical contribution (Sec. 5) is projection of ancestral constraints onto edges and topological orderings.  
- Experimental results: projection improves EC-tree performance but not ILP, raising questions about generalizability to other learners.  
- EC-tree results (Table 2) show an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion increases, implying sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge and need for careful tuning.  
- Missing comparison of structural Hamming distance between EC trees and the ILP approach.  
- Suggest reducing focus on the BN graph (Sec. 4.1) and expanding exposition of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the experimental context.
