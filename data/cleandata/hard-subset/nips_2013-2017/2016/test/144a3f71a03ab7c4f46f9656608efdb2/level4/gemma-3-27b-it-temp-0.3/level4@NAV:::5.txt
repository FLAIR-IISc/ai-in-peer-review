This paper addresses the challenge of optimal Bayesian network configuration, building upon the structure learning framework introduced by Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. The key contribution lies in the incorporation of non-decomposable constraints – notably, ancestral constraints – into this learning process. The authors demonstrate substantial performance gains, with empirical results revealing runtime improvements of several orders of magnitude compared to existing Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods. They achieve this by reformulating the problem as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. Although my familiarity with this specific MaxSAT approach is limited, the reported runtime comparisons are compelling and indicate a significant advancement in efficiency. The authors convincingly demonstrate a considerable reduction in computation time relative to alternative techniques.
