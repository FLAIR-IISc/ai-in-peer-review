- Proposes a method to learn Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints.  
- Framework leverages existing oracles for decomposable-score structure learning by passing them inferred decomposable constraints.  
- Main technical contribution (Section 5) is the projection of ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints.  
- Empirically compared to ILP-based systems and shown to be orders-of-magnitude more efficient, especially as variable count grows.  
- Demonstrates scalability to about 20 variables, comparable to modern score-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents both theory and experiments thoroughly.  
- Results indicate the projection improves performance for EC-tree search but not for the ILP approach, raising generalization concerns.  
- EC-tree performance shows an easy-hard-easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints changes, indicating sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge.  
- Experiments report structural Hamming distance only relative to EC trees, not compared to ILP results.  
- Suggestion to show that decomposable-constraint projection improves other learning methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Suggestion to drop Section 4.1 and instead expand explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree setting, since experiments focus on EC-tree search.  
- Overall contribution is novel and significant for incorporating non-decomposable constraints into decomposable-score learners, but further validation and comparisons are needed.
