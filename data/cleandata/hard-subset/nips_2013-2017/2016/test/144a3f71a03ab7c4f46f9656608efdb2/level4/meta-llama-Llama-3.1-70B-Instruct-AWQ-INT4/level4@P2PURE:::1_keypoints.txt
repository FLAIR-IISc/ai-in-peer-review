- Addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores with ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound search over CPDAG nodes.
- Search uses A* with symmetry breaking; heuristic provided by URLearning but details are omitted from the paper.
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning of the search tree (Lemma 3), but the proof is unclear—specifically the claim "Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs more explanation.
- Discusses implied ("projected") constraints and ordering constraints to allow further pruning, but the inference method is inadequately explained.
- The statement "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading; one can choose to add Y < Z, not universally infer it.
- Uses a MAXSAT approach to infer as many consistent ordering constraints as possible without over-constraining the problem.
- Experiments vary number of variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints and show the proposed method is significantly faster than GOBNILP.
- Comparison to GOBNILP is weakened because GOBNILP typically underperforms A* without parent-set limits and the MAXSAT encoding from Cussens 2008 is unsuitable for ILP.
- Proposes a better ILP formulation using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and linking constraints (A(X,Y) ≥ Σ_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U)) to tighten the linear relaxation, similar to Cussens 2010 but for a partial order.
- Reviewer believes the proposed method would still outperform a sensible ILP, but recommends comparing to van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often beats GOBNILP and could be adapted to ancestral constraints.
- Notes missing/unclear citations: Cussens 2010 (James Cussens) and van Beek & Hoffmann 2015 should be referenced when discussing alternative methods.
