- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by pruning the EC tree and projecting constraints onto a decomposable oracle.  
- Builds on BN graph and EC tree search spaces (Chen et al.) to allow shortest-path/A* search when scores/prior are non-decomposable.  
- Defines when a CPDAG satisfies ancestral constraints and proves sound pruning of CPDAG nodes and descendants (Theorem 1).  
- Proposes procedures to infer necessary negative and sufficient positive edge constraints from a closure of ancestral constraints (Theorems 2 & 3).  
- Formulates inference of a maximal cycle-free set of ordering constraints from ancestral constraints as a MaxSAT problem with transitivity and acyclicity hard clauses (Theorem 4).  
- Empirically compares to an ILP-based method (GOBNILP) and reports orders-of-magnitude speedups and dramatically tightened oracle heuristics.  
- Related to order-graph exact methods, ILP/CP formulations, and path-constraint constraint-based approaches; novelty is integrating ancestral constraints with EC-tree search and projection into decomposable constraints.  

Strengths:
- Tackles a practical, important class of non-decomposable knowledge with a principled exact method.  
- Provides sound EC-tree pruning rules that leverage equivalence-class search to avoid redundant exploration.  
- Clever projection of ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints; MaxSAT approach effectively selects large, consistent orderings.  
- Strong empirical results and ablations showing the value of projections.  
- Insightful observation of an easy–hard–easy pattern and meaningful accuracy gains from modest path knowledge.  

Weaknesses / Clarifications needed:
- Lemma 3 and the EC-tree edge-generation argument require more detailed justification and a concrete example application of the rules.  
- Statements implying logical entailment of orderings (e.g., “infer Y < Z”) are misleading; clarify these are safe augmentations chosen by MaxSAT, not logical entailments.  
- MaxSAT formulation is terse: provide full clause set, variable definitions, and a proof sketch that any solution yields O with G(A) ⊆ G(O), plus runtime/complexity data.  
- The ILP baseline encoding for ancestral constraints seems weak; a tighter ILP with ancestor variables and transitivity would strengthen comparisons.  
- Missing comparison to a CP baseline (van Beek & Hoffmann 2015), which may outperform ILP and is a relevant exact competitor.  
- CPDAG feasibility tests fall back on enumerating class(P) in some cases (potentially exponential); report how often this occurs, pruning used, and possible efficient partial checks.  

Quality:
- Overall technically sound and empirically validated, but some proofs and algorithmic details need more exposition for reproducibility.  

Clarity:
- Generally clear but needs consistent terminology (“projected constraints” vs “implied constraints”), explicit distinction between entailment and safe augmentation, pseudocode for key procedures, and typographical/freference fixes.  

Originality:
- Novel and non-trivial combination of EC-tree pruning under ancestral constraints and projection of non-decomposable path constraints into decomposable constraints.  

Significance:
- High practical relevance; observed speedups and accuracy improvements suggest potential for adoption in exact BN learning toolkits.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, useful problem with a principled exact solution, strong empirical improvements, and novel integration of non-decomposable constraints with equivalence-class search.  

Cons against acceptance:
- Insufficient detail in key proofs and MaxSAT encoding, reliance on a relatively weak ILP baseline, lack of CP baseline, and scalability concerns for CPDAG enumeration.  

Key suggestions for revision:
- Expand proof of Lemma 3 and detail the EC-tree edge-generation argument.  
- Provide complete MaxSAT encoding, variables/clauses, proof of safety (G(A) ⊆ G(O)), and runtime discussion.  
- Re-implement or justify the ILP baseline using ancestor variables and transitivity constraints to strengthen comparisons.  
- Add or discuss a CP baseline and its expected performance/feasibility.  
- Report frequency of class(P) enumeration fallbacks and any mitigations or more efficient feasibility checks.
