- Proposes branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs with symmetry breaking and A* guided by URLearning for ancestral-constrained BN structure learning.  
- Lemma 3 (pruning via inferred constraints) is plausible but its proof lacks clarity, particularly the justification that "Gk contains edge Z → W" via EC tree edge rules.  
- Distinguishes ancestral (given) vs implied/projected constraints, and introduces inferred ordering constraints, but incorrectly presents "Z not an ancestor of Y implies Y < Z" as entailed.  
- Clarify that ordering constraints inferred from absence of ancestry are optional additions that must be consistent, not deductive consequences; MAXSAT is used to avoid over-constraining.  
- Experiments report speedups over GOBNILP, but this comparison is confounded because GOBNILP underperforms when parent-set cardinality is unconstrained.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints via a cubic MAXSAT formulation (E(X,Y,Z) vars), which is suboptimal compared to a binary ILP encoding (A(X,Y), I(Z,U) plus transitivity) from Cussens (2010).  
- Using the MAXSAT encoding weakens the GOBNILP baseline and may explain its poor performance, so conclusions about speedups are unsubstantiated.  
- Important prior work (van Beek & Hoffmann 2015, CP methods) is omitted and should be included as a competitive baseline that can handle ancestral constraints.  
- Recommendations: revise and clarify Lemma 3 and entailed vs chosen constraints, replace MAXSAT with Cussens (2010) ILP encoding, and compare against properly encoded GOBNILP and CP baselines.  
- Overall: core idea is valuable but theoretical exposition, encoding choice, experimental design, and missing baselines currently undermine the paper’s claims.
