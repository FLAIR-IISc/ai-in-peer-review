- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral (ancestor/descendant) constraints.  
- Proposes leveraging Chen et al.'s EC tree search space to incorporate non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Key insight: infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to use existing decomposable-score oracles.  
- Main contributions: (1) sound EC-tree pruning mechanism based on ancestral constraints, (2) projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound; Theorem 1 proves pruning soundness.  
- Minor theoretical clarity issue: Lemma 3 was initially hard to follow but is verifiable by experts.  
- Strong empirical results: orders-of-magnitude faster than ILP-based GOBNILP across network sizes and constraint densities.  
- Experimental design is robust (varied variables, dataset sizes, constraint density; 50 dataset averages).  
- Shows ancestral constraints can improve learning accuracy comparably to large increases in training data (e.g., 512 → 8192).  
- Well-written, logically organized, and connects appropriately to prior work (EC tree, constraint-based and order-constraint literature).  
- Originality: novel and practical projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones; fills a gap vs. ILP feasibility-only approaches.  
- Significance: important for practitioners with domain knowledge; improves efficiency and accuracy when data is scarce.  
- Main weakness: comparison to GOBNILP may be unfair—ILP encoding used (MAXSAT-based) is not the most efficient; should compare to a direct ILP encoding with ancestral variables and transitivity or to van Beek & Hoffmann's CP approach.  
- Minor issue: clarity needed on the MAXSAT encoding for ordering constraints and the wording around inferring/order choices vs. entailment.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify ILP comparison and MAXSAT encoding details; well-suited for NIPS.
