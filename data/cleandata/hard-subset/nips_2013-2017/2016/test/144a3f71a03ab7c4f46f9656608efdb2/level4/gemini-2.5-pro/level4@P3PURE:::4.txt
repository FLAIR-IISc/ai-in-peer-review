This paper proposes a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning algorithms to incorporate this form of domain knowledge and thereby improve learning efficiency. The primary novelty lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce their method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into a set of decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. The paper is well-written, and the proposed method is clearly explained both theoretically and through empirical evaluation.

I have the following concerns and suggestions:

1.  According to Table 3, the proposed projection of ancestral constraints improves the performance of the EC tree approach but not the ILP approach. This raises a concern about the generalizability of the proposed projection method: is its benefit limited to EC trees? The paper's central contribution is the projection method itself. To strengthen this contribution, it would be more compelling if the authors could demonstrate its effectiveness on a learning algorithm that can *only* handle decomposable constraints. Showing that the projected constraints improve the performance and efficiency of such an algorithm would provide a stronger validation of the proposed technique.

2.  Table 2 reveals an "easy-hard-easy" performance pattern for the EC tree method as the proportion of ancestral constraints is increased. The authors should provide an explanation for this phenomenon. Does this pattern suggest that the performance of the EC tree search is highly sensitive to the quantity of ancestral constraints provided, perhaps more so than the ILP approach? If so, this would imply that the number of ancestral constraints must be carefully tuned to achieve optimal results, which could be a practical limitation.

3.  Table 2 presents the Structural Hamming Distance (SHD) results for the EC tree method but omits a direct comparison with the ILP approach under the same experimental conditions. Including the ILP results in this table would provide a more complete picture of the relative performance.

4.  A minor suggestion regarding the paper's structure: While the theoretical connection between Bayesian Networks (BNs) and EC trees is understood (Section 4.1), the experiments focus exclusively on applying the proposed constraints within the EC tree search space. The authors might consider removing Section 4.1 and allocating that space to a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* search algorithm as applied in their experiments. This would improve the paper's focus and better align the background material with the empirical evaluation.
