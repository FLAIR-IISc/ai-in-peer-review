The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm. While the approach is intriguing, I found the experimental design and presentation to be somewhat confusing.

The comparison between existing approaches, EC and GOB, in Table 1 is not entirely clear. It appears to compare the combination of EC with the proposed method (EC + new work) to the combination of GOB with a modified version of the proposed method (GOB + new work'). However, it would be more informative to include a direct comparison between EC and EC + new work, as this would allow readers to assess the impact of the proposed method on the performance of EC. Although the comparison between GOB + new work' and GOB is also of interest, the results suggest that EC outperforms GOB to such an extent that even if GOB were to benefit significantly more from the new method, EC + new work would likely remain the preferred choice.

Furthermore, the presentation of results in Table 3 could be clarified. It is unclear whether this table compares EC to GOB or serves a different purpose. Providing a clear and explicit comparison between the performance of EC and GOB, both with and without the proposed method, would strengthen the experimental evaluation and facilitate a more accurate assessment of the method's effectiveness.
