- Paper tackles constrained optimization for learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound with CPDAG nodes.  
- Search moves by selecting parent sets for variables absent in the initial CPDAG and includes sensible symmetry-breaking techniques.  
- Search is guided by an A* approach using the URLearning system as a heuristic, but integration specifics are not detailed in this paper.  
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning of the search tree; Lemma 3 in the supplement is central to this pruning.  
- The proof of Lemma 3 is unclear, especially the claim that EC tree edge generation rules imply the presence of a particular edge.  
- The paper introduces "projected constraints" (implied constraints) to further prune edges based on ancestral constraints.  
- Authors infer "ordering constraints" on permissible topological orderings, but the inference methodology is not fully explained.  
- Ordering constraints are inferred by optionally adding order constraints when one variable is not an ancestor of another, aiming to maximize consistent constraints without over-constraining.  
- The inference of ordering constraints is implemented via a MAXSAT formulation.  
- Experiments vary number of variables, data points, and ancestral constraints, comparing performance to GOBNILP.  
- Results show the proposed system is significantly faster than GOBNILP.  
- The observed weakness of GOBNILP may stem from handling many parent sets rather than specifically from ancestral constraints.  
- The comparison encodes ancestral constraints via a MAXSAT formulation from prior work, which may be suboptimal for an ILP solver like GOBNILP.  
- A potentially better ILP formulation would use binary variables for ancestral relations with linking constraints to tighten the linear relaxation and improve performance.  
- The paper would be stronger if it compared against a more appropriate GOBNILP formulation or other methods (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann), and the omission of such comparisons weakens its conclusions.
