- Addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs with symmetry breaking and a URLearning-derived heuristic.

- Lemma 3 (supplement) is a key result but its proof is hard to follow; the step “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs clearer justification.

- Inferring implied/projected ordering constraints is under-explained; the claim “we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is imprecise because non-ancestry allows but does not entail that ordering.

- Authors appear to aim to infer a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints and use MAXSAT to avoid over-constraining, but this process needs clearer exposition.

- Experiments vary variables, data size, and ancestral constraints and show the proposed method is much faster than GOBNILP.

- The comparison to GOBNILP may be unfair: experiments do not bound parent set size, and without ancestral constraints GOBNILP is known to perform poorly versus A* when parent sets are unbounded, so it’s unclear whether performance differences stem from ancestral constraints or large parent sets.

- The paper encodes ancestral constraints using Cussens (2008) MAXSAT instead of the more appropriate ILP-style encoding from Cussens (2010), which is suboptimal for an ILP system.

- A stronger ILP formulation is recommended: add binary ancestor variables A(X,Y), remove the cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables, add transitivity on A, and link A and parent indicators I via A(X,Y) ≥ Σ_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U), producing a tighter relaxation and better performance.

- The use of a suboptimal encoding weakens the empirical comparison; the reviewer believes a sensible ILP encoding would likely still have the proposed system outperform GOBNILP but demands a fairer baseline.

- Recommend comparing to van Beek & Hoffmann’s constraint-programming BN learning method (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and is not cited; adapting it to ancestral constraints should be feasible.
