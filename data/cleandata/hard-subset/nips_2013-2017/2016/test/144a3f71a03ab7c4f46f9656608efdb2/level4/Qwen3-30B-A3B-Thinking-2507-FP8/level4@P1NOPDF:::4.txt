

This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches to incorporate domain knowledge and improve efficiency. The core novelty lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce a projection technique converting ancestral constraints into edge and topological ordering constraints—both decomposable. The paper is well-written, with clear theoretical exposition and experimental validation. However, the following points require clarification:

First, Table 3 indicates that constraint projection only enhances EC tree performance, not the ILP approach. This suggests the projection may be particularly effective for EC trees but lacks generalization to other methods. Given the paper’s central contribution is deriving decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more compelling to demonstrate that incorporating these constraints improves performance *and* efficiency for methods originally unable to handle ancestral constraints. Without this, the practical impact of the projection technique remains limited.

Second, Table 2 reveals an "easy-hard-easy" performance pattern for EC trees as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This pattern warrants explanation: does it indicate that EC tree search space reduction via constraints is highly sensitive to the quantity of domain knowledge relative to the ILP approach? If so, this implies careful tuning of constraint quantities is necessary for optimal results—a critical consideration for real-world application.

Third, Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance (SHD) for EC trees but omits comparison with the ILP approach. Without SHD results for ILP under the same constraint settings, it is unclear whether the observed EC tree improvements (e.g., in Table 3) represent relative gains or merely absolute improvements within EC trees. A direct comparison between EC trees and ILP would strengthen the evaluation.

Finally, Section 4.1 discusses Bayesian networks (BNs) as the theoretical foundation of EC trees. However, since the experiments exclusively apply ancestral constraints to EC trees (not BNs), this section is largely redundant. Given the paper’s focus on EC trees, it would be more effective to omit Section 4.1 and dedicate additional space to clarifying EC trees and the A* algorithm within this specific setting. The current framing risks diluting the paper’s core contribution by overemphasizing BNs, which are not utilized in the experimental evaluation.
