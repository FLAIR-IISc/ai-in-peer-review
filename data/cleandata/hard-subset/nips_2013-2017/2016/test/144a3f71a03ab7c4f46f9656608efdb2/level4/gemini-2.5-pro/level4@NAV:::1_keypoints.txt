Paper addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints via an A* branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs.  
Search refines CPDAGs by selecting parent sets, uses effective symmetry-breaking, and a heuristic derived from URLearning (integration details in prior work).  
Ancestral constraints enable additional pruning; Lemma 3 underpins this but its proof is unclear—the claim "Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs more explanation.  
Method uses implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints to prune, but the inference procedure is inadequately explained and ambiguous (e.g., the meaning of "can infer Y < Z").  
Authors seem to seek a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints and use MAXSAT to avoid over-constraining, but rationale and details are lacking.  
Experiments vary variables, samples, and constraints and show the proposed method is substantially faster than GOBNILP.  
Comparison to GOBNILP is limited because GOBNILP is known to struggle with unlimited parent-set cardinality, so poor performance may reflect that rather than handling of ancestral constraints.  
The GOBNILP ancestral-constraint encoding uses a MAXSAT-based formulation (Cussens 2008) that is suboptimal for ILP; a better ILP encoding (e.g., Cussens 2010) should be used.  
Recommend using binary ancestor variables A(X,Y) with transitivity and linking constraints to replace cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables, yielding a tighter linear relaxation and better ILP performance.  
Even if the new method would still win, comparing to an inefficient baseline weakens the paper's contribution.  
Recommend comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann's constraint-programming approach (often outperforming GOBNILP) and note that their work is missing from citations.
