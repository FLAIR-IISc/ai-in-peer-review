This paper addresses the problem of learning optimal Bayesian network structures under a challenging class of constraints. The primary contribution is a novel method for incorporating non-decomposable constraints, and ancestral constraints in particular, into the score-based structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. To achieve this, the authors formulate the structure learning problem as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) problem.

The empirical evaluation provides compelling evidence for the efficacy of this approach. When compared against state-of-the-art methods based on Integer Linear Programming (ILP), the proposed MaxSAT-based solver demonstrates a significant performance advantage, achieving runtime reductions of several orders of magnitude. While my expertise with MaxSAT solvers is limited, the empirical results are impressive and suggest a substantial practical contribution to the field.
