This paper addresses the problem of learning an optimal Bayesian network structure subject to a set of constraints. The main contribution is a novel method for incorporating non-decomposable constraints, and ancestral constraints in particular, into the structure learning framework previously developed by Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. To achieve this, the authors formulate the problem as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) instance with specialized constraints.

The paper provides an empirical evaluation comparing the proposed approach against established methods based on Integer Linear Programming (ILP). The results are impressive, demonstrating a reduction in runtime by several orders of magnitude relative to these alternatives. While my own expertise with MaxSAT-based approaches is limited, the performance gains shown in the runtime comparisons appear to be a significant improvement over the state of the art.
