- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound A*-style search over CPDAGs.  
- Search expands CPDAGs by resolving parent sets for one variable at a time, using a URLearning heuristic and symmetry-breaking techniques.  
- Ancestral constraints enable extra pruning; Lemma 3 is central to this pruning but its proof is hard to follow and contains an unexplained claim about edge presence.  
- The paper discusses "projected constraints" (i.e., implied constraints) and inferred ordering constraints, but the method for inferring ordering constraints is ambiguous.  
- The phrase "can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is unclear and incorrect as a logical entailment; intent seems to be adding many consistent ordering constraints, but details are missing.  
- Authors mention using MAXSAT to select ordering constraints, yet provide insufficient explanation of this MAXSAT strategy and how inconsistency is avoided.  
- Experiments show the proposed method is much faster than GOBNILP, but this comparison is confounded because GOBNILP is known to perform poorly with unbounded parent set cardinality used here.  
- The ILP encoding used for GOBNILP’s ancestral constraints appears weak (based on a MAXSAT encoding) and a stronger formulation from Cussens (2010) using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity/linking constraints would give tighter relaxations.  
- Even if GOBNILP were improved, the authors’ claim of superiority is weakened by comparing to a known weak baseline without trying stronger ILP formulations.  
- The paper omits a comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (2015)’s constraint programming approach, which is relevant and often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely handle ancestral constraints.
