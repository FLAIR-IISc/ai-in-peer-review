- Paper proposes a novel projection method that converts non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints, enabling score-decomposable structure learners to incorporate complex priors.  
- Primary contribution is the projection technique detailed in Section 5; paper is well-written and clear on theory and experiments.  
- Generalizability concern: Table 3 shows improvement for EC trees but not ILP, so the method should be validated on at least one other score-decomposable learning algorithm to demonstrate broad utility.  
- Performance sensitivity: EC trees exhibit an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases (Table 2); the paper lacks explanation and should discuss why this occurs and its practical implications.  
- Incomplete comparison: Table 2 reports SHD for EC trees but omits ILP results under the same conditions—ILP metrics should be included for fair comparison.  
- Minor suggestion: Reduce general BN background (Section 4.1) and expand the explanation of EC trees and the A* search specifics to better align with the experimental focus.
