- Introduces a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints (novelty in Section 5).  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear theoretical foundations and experimental presentation.  
- Results show performance improvement from projection only for the EC tree learner, not for ILP, raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Recommend demonstrating benefit on algorithms that cannot directly handle the original non-decomposable constraints to better justify the contribution.  
- EC tree exhibits an unexpected “easy-hard-easy” performance pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases, indicating sensitivity to amount of prior knowledge.  
- Suggest investigating and discussing whether careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints is necessary and why EC tree sensitivity differs from ILP.  
- Evaluation uses structural Hamming distance only for EC trees; recommend comparing EC tree and ILP with the same metric for a more complete assessment.  
- Section 4.1 (BN connection) feels tangential given the EC-tree-focused experiments; suggest removing it and expanding explanation of EC trees and the A* implementation instead.
