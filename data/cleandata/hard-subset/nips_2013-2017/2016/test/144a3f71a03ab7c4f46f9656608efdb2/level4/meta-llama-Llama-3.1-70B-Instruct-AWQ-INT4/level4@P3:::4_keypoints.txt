- Proposes a novel method to learn Bayesian network structure with ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Main technical contribution is the projection method described in Section 5.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical exposition and experimental illustration.  
- Experiments show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based structure learning.  
- Results indicate a small number of ancestral constraints can improve learned model quality similarly to increasing data.  
- Table 3 suggests the projection only improves EC-tree methods, not ILP, raising concerns about generalization to other learners.  
- Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern in EC-tree performance as constraint proportion varies, indicating sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge.  
- More analysis is needed on how to tune the number of ancestral constraints and why the easy–hard–easy phenomenon occurs.  
- Missing comparison of structural Hamming distance between EC-tree and ILP approaches limits assessment of structural quality differences.  
- Suggestion to remove Section 4.1 (BN graph) and instead provide a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree context.
