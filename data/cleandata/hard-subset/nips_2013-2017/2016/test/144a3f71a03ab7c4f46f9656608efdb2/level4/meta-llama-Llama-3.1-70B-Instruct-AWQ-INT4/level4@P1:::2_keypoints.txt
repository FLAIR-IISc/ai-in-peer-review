- Presents a method that uses ancestral constraints to inform an admissible heuristic for exhaustive search in Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Builds on a framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores that can accommodate ancestral constraints.  
- Empowers the framework's oracle by inferring and passing decomposable constraints derived from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Provides a clear, thorough introduction and literature review on learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints.  
- Gives a detailed explanation of the EC tree search space and the technique for inferring decomposable constraints.  
- Experimental comparisons are confusing because different tables compare different combinations of methods (EC + new work vs GOBNILP+new work; EC vs GOBNILP without new work).  
- Lacks an explicit comparison between the proposed approach (EC + new work) and the EC tree search space without the new work, which would clarify impact.  
- Demonstrates the proposed method is significantly more efficient than existing approaches.  
- Experiments are thorough but could be clarified and better organized to support claims.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the approach.  
- The approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential future work.
