This paper introduces a technique that incorporates a given set of ancestral constraints to guide the admissible heuristic within an exhaustive search algorithm.

The experimental section was somewhat unclear. As I interpret it, Table 1 compares two baseline methods, EC and GOB, after each has been enhanced with the proposed contribution (or a variant of it). It then appears that Table 3 provides a direct comparison between the original EC and GOB methods; I would appreciate clarification if this understanding is incorrect.

A more direct and informative experiment would explicitly compare the performance of (EC + proposed method) against the standalone EC baseline. Although the comparison involving GOB is interesting, EC is clearly the superior baseline. Therefore, even if the proposed work provides a larger relative improvement to GOB, the absolute performance of (EC + proposed method) is likely to be the most relevant configuration. For this reason, quantifying the specific gain over the original EC method is essential to evaluate the practical impact of this contribution.
