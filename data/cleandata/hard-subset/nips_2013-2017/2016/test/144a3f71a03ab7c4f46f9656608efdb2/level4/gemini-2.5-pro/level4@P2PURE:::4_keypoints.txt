1. Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to let decomposable-score learners use domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency.

2. Primary novelty is the projection technique described in Section 5.

3. Paper is well-written with clear theoretical exposition and experimental results.

4. Concern: Table 3 shows improvement for EC trees but not ILP, raising questions about the method's generalizability beyond EC trees; need evidence it helps learners that cannot natively handle non-decomposable constraints.

5. Concern: Table 2 shows a non-monotonic "easy-hard-easy" pattern for EC trees as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; authors should explain whether this indicates sensitivity to the amount of constraints and the need for tuning.

6. Concern: Table 2 reports SHD for EC trees but omits corresponding ILP SHD results; include ILP results for a complete comparison.

7. Suggestion: Remove or shorten Section 4.1 on Bayesian Networks and use the space to better explain EC trees and the A* search algorithm, since experiments focus on the EC tree search space.
