- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints is hard for decomposable-score methods and ILP scales poorly.
- Core contribution: Integrates ancestral constraints into the EC-tree A*-based search by projecting non-decomposable constraints onto decomposable ones the oracle can handle.
- Key technique: Project ancestral constraints into required/forbidden edges and ordering constraints to tighten the A* heuristic and prune search.
- Edge inference: Proposes a simple, efficient algorithm to infer decomposable edge constraints from ancestral information.
- Ordering inference: Reduces finding a consistent set of variable ordering constraints to a MaxSAT instance.
- Oracle empowerment: Uses the projected decomposable constraints to “empower” an existing optimal learner for decomposable scores without modifying it.
- Theoretical support: Provides theorems formalizing constraint projection and correctness within the EC-tree framework.
- Empirical results: Thorough experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP solver GOBNILP, especially as variable count grows.
- Practical impact: Demonstrates that a small fraction of ancestral constraints can yield accuracy gains comparable to large increases in training data.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient background and experimental detail.
- Novelty: The projection approach enabling decomposable oracles to handle non-decomposable constraints is a new and significant idea.
- Weakness — scope: Focuses only on ancestral constraints; lacks discussion on generalizing projection to other non-decomposable constraint types.
- Weakness — preprocessing cost: Uses MaxSAT for ordering inference (NP-hard) but does not analyze the runtime or scalability of this preprocessing step.
- Evaluation: Overall assessment is very positive — high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; strong recommendation for acceptance.
