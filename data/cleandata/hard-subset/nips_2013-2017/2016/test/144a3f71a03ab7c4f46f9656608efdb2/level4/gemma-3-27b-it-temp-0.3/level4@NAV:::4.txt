This paper presents a method for translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into a decomposable form, enabling their integration into decomposable-score-based structure learning algorithms and potentially improving learning efficiency. The primary contribution of this work resides in Section 5, where the authors detail their approach to projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into equivalent edge and topological ordering constraints. The paper is well-structured and clearly explains both the theoretical foundations and experimental results. However, I have several concerns and suggestions for improvement:

i. The results in Table 3 indicate that projecting ancestral constraints improves performance for EC trees, but not for the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether the benefits of this projection are specific to EC trees and do not generalize well to other learning methods. Given the core innovation of this paper – inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints – it would be more compelling to demonstrate that incorporating these decomposable constraints enhances the performance and/or efficiency of a learning approach that *could not* originally handle the non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 reveals an unusual easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This suggests that the effectiveness of using ancestral constraints to reduce the EC tree search space is highly sensitive to the amount of incorporated domain knowledge, potentially more so than with the ILP approach. Consequently, careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints may be necessary to achieve optimal performance.

iii. Table 2 currently presents only the structural Hamming distance results for EC trees. A comparison of these results with those obtained using the ILP approach would provide a more complete evaluation.

iv. While I acknowledge the theoretical connection between Bayesian Networks and EC trees, as EC trees can be viewed as an extension of the former, the experimental focus is solely on EC tree search spaces with applied ancestral constraints. Therefore, I suggest removing Section 4.1 to allow for a more detailed and thorough explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm specifically within the EC tree framework.
