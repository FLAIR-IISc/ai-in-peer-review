- Paper addresses optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by searching EC trees and pruning infeasible CPDAGs.
- Authors project ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints: inferred edge constraints via sound rules and ordering constraints via a MaxSAT that finds many cycle-free orderings.
- Theorems 1–4 provide conditions for EC-tree pruning and valid constraint projection.
- Empirical results show substantial speedups (often orders of magnitude) over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP), especially when using projected constraints.
- Method reuses strong decomposable oracles (order-graph solvers) by “empowering” them with projected constraints.
- Paper is well situated relative to prior score-based and ILP/CP approaches and extends Chen et al.’s non-decomposable-search perspective.
- Strength: clear problem statement and generally clear writing; pipeline (constraints → projection → oracle-empowered EC-tree search) is easy to follow.
- Strength: sound EC-tree pruning leveraging CPDAG structure.
- Strength: practical projection strategy that tightens the oracle’s heuristic and reduces search.
- Empirical study examines effect of constraint density and data size on search and structural accuracy.
- Concern: conceptually straightforward—main idea is pruning CPDAGs and projecting constraints to help decomposable solvers.
- Concern: exact ancestral constraints may be artificial; methods handling latent confounding (MAGs/PAGs) may be more relevant in practice.
- Concern: limited practical impact when only sparse ancestral constraints are available, reducing projected constraints and pruning.
- Evaluation concern: GOBNILP may be an incomplete baseline; a constrained greedy baseline (e.g., GES respecting constraints) would be a useful complementary comparison.
- Technical concern: some MaxSAT rules (lines 257–261) seem strange or incomplete and may lack symmetry/completeness.
- Specific MaxSAT issues: acyclicity rule (3) appears asymmetric despite symmetric premises; rule (4) may not express mutual non-ancestry constraints.
- Theorem 3’s conditions for inferring positive edges are stringent and may rarely hold with sparse constraints.
- Acyclicity encoding (line 267) is not clearly justified; a brief proof sketch or revision is suggested.
- Quality: approach appears sound overall but MaxSAT constraint set and Theorem 1’s statement need clarification.
- Clarity suggestion: explicitly state that EC-tree pruning tests existence of a DAG in class(P) satisfying constraints (existential check).
- Clarity suggestion: give an intuitive explanation of MaxSAT hard constraints and ensure symmetry of rules.
- Minor wording suggestions: clarify “omniscient oracle,” rephrase “empower the oracle,” and note score optimization corresponds to cost minimization.
- Practical note: 10–25% ancestral constraints can encode substantial knowledge for moderate graphs—discuss typical availability in practice.
- Overall originality: moderate—combination is novel and useful but straightforward.
- Significance: moderate—of interest to exact BN learning researchers but limited practical impact in some settings.
- Recommendation: borderline accept for a specialist audience; improve MaxSAT clarity and add a complementary baseline to strengthen the paper.
