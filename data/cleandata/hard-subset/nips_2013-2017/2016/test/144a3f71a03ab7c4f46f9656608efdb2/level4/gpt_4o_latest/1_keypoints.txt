- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs with parent-set selection transitions.  
- Sensible symmetry-breaking techniques are implemented.  
- Search follows an A* framework using the URLearning system heuristic (heuristic details deferred to prior work).  
- Ancestral constraints enable pruning; Lemma 3 is central but its proof is unclear, specifically the claim that "By the EC tree edge generation rules, G_k also contains edge Z -> W."  
- Authors use the term "projected constraints" for what are standardly called "implied constraints."  
- Ancestral constraints imply edge constraints that help pruning.  
- Authors infer ordering constraints, but the derivation is insufficiently explained and includes ambiguous statements (e.g., "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y").  
- The goal is to infer many consistent ordering constraints (beyond strictly entailed ones) via a MAXSAT formulation while avoiding over-inference.  
- Experiments vary variable count, data points, and ancestral constraints; proposed method significantly outperforms GOBNILP in speed.  
- It’s unclear if GOBNILP’s poor performance stems from large parent sets or specific handling of ancestral constraints, since no parent-set limit was imposed.  
- Ancestral constraints were encoded in MAXSAT following Cussens (2008), which is suboptimal for ILP systems; a better ILP formulation would use A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and linkage constraints for tighter relaxations (per Cussens 2010).  
- Comparing against a suboptimal encoding weakens the paper; authors should compare to a stronger ILP encoding and to van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and is not cited.
