The authors propose a method that uses a given set of ancestral constraints to construct an admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm. I found the experimental section somewhat confusing. As I understand it, EC and GOB are the existing baselines. In Table 1, you compare (EC + proposed method) to (GOB + a variant of the proposed method). If this interpretation is correct, then Table 3 is comparing EC to GOB without the new method (or have I misunderstood this?).

If so, it would be helpful to include an explicit ablation comparing (EC + proposed method) directly to EC alone. While the (GOB + variant) vs. GOB comparison is interesting, EC appears to substantially outperform GOB. Consequently, even if GOB benefited more from the new method than EC does, the preferred choice would likely still be (EC + proposed method). Clarifying these comparisons and adding the EC ablation would make the empirical evaluation much clearer.
