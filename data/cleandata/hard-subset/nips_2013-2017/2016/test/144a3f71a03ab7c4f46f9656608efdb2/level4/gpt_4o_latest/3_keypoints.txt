- Paper proposes converting ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints and using them in a score-based EC-tree learning algorithm (MaxSAT used for the translation).  
- Empirical results show large speedups over a straightforward adaptation of ILP-based Gobnilp for this setting.  
- Method is conceptually straightforward, well-written, novel for this specific problem, but not groundbreaking and mainly of interest to a specialized audience.  
- The problem setting may be somewhat artificial: when ancestral relations matter, general causal discovery methods that handle confounding may be more appropriate (limits impact).  
- Translating ancestral into ordering constraints makes them compatible with decomposable scores, but in practice few ancestral constraints may not much reduce the search space.  
- Certain MaxSAT-derived rules (lines 257–261) appear unusual or incomplete: acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an unexpected conclusion, and rule 4 seems to exclude mutually non-causal constraints.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is impressive but not fully convincing since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for ancestral constraints; a more meaningful baseline would be greedy algorithms (e.g., GES) given oracle constraints.  
- Suggest comparing against GES with oracle-aligned constraints to assess efficiency relative to a greedy method that would find the same model under oracle input.  
- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that CPDAG-satisfied constraints are only a subset of the DAG-satisfied constraints (otherwise the iff is incorrect).  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse or small.  
- The assumption that practitioners would provide 10–25% of ancestral constraints is unrealistic; such levels imply substantial prior knowledge of the true graph.  
- Minor clarifications requested: distinction between “standard oracle” vs “omniscient oracle” (L30), wording of “empower the oracle” (L38), clarify “optimizing” means minimizing (L90), and clarify acyclicity rule wording (L267).
