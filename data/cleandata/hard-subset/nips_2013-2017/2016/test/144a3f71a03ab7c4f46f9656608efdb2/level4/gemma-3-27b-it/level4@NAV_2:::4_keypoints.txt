- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable forms.  
- Core idea: project ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints usable by decomposable-score algorithms within the EC tree search space.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly presenting problem, approach, and experiments, and builds logically on prior optimal-learning work.  
- Appropriately references related ILP-based and other prior methods.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation for the projection method.  
- Strength: the projection method appears novel and bridges non-decomposable constraints and decomposable algorithms.  
- Strength: empirical results show substantial efficiency gains, particularly with the EC tree/A* framework.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and constraint levels.  
- Weakness: benefits of projected constraints are primarily realized only within the EC tree framework, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: unclear why ILP approaches do not gain similar benefits—could be solver encoding, solver limitations, or constraint characteristics.  
- Weakness: the “easy–hard–easy” pattern in EC tree performance as constraint proportion varies is unexplained and needs analysis.  
- Weakness: lack of direct structural-accuracy comparison (e.g., SHD) between EC tree and ILP-learned networks.  
- Suggestion: streamline Section 4.1 on BN graphs and expand exposition of the EC tree, A* algorithm, and how projected constraints affect search.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound projection method with strong empirical validation and thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited established generalizability, unexplained EC tree sensitivity pattern, and missing direct accuracy comparisons with ILP.
