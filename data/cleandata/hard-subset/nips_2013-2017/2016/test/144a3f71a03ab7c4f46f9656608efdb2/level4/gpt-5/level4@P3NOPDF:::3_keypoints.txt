- Paper proposes translating ancestral constraints into maximal ordering constraints via MaxSAT and using them in EC-tree exact BN search, yielding empirical speedups over a modified Gobnilp baseline.
- Clear, well-written exposition with a clean separation between constraint translation and exact search.
- Conceptual contribution: mapping ancestral constraints to order constraints that fit decomposable scores and EC-tree search.
- Principled use of MaxSAT to compute closure of implied ancestral relations.
- Novelty: explicit integration of ancestral constraints into optimal BN learning where no standard solution exists.
- Empirical results against the adapted Gobnilp baseline are favorable.
- Practical scope is limited when latent confounding is plausible; methods for equivalence classes with hidden variables may be more appropriate.
- Unclear pruning benefits when only a small number of ancestral constraints are available; needs systematic analysis of pruning vs. number/type of constraints.
- Baseline choice concern: Gobnilp was not designed for these constraints; additional baselines (e.g., GES with oracle scores) are needed for calibration.
- Rule set (lines 257–261) appears asymmetric or incomplete; needs clarification of directionality and potential symmetric conclusions.
- Rule 4 may improperly exclude mutual non-ancestry constraints; clarify intent and expressibility conditions.
- Request for formal soundness and completeness statement (or precise delimitation) for the rule system.
- Theorem 1 (line 164) likely overclaims equivalence; the CPDAG-satisfied constraints are generally a subset of DAG-satisfied constraints—restate or add conditions.
- Theorem 3 (lines 225–227) may overstate practical gains for sparse constraints; quantify implied edge count vs. input constraints empirically.
- Acyclicity encoding (line 267) is hard to follow; add intuition and a correctness argument showing ordering constraints prevent cycles without overconstraining.
- Evaluation needs a greedy baseline (e.g., GES with oracle scores) in regimes where constraints match ground truth.
- Evaluation should include sensitivity analysis over number, type, and noise level of ancestral constraints to assess robustness and pruning effectiveness.
- Discuss how realistic constraint structures (pairwise, chain-like, etc.) affect performance.
- Terminology/clarity fixes: consistent use of “oracle,” rephrase “empower the oracle,” and clarify scoring optimization direction (minimize vs. maximize).
- Temper language and provide supporting evidence for claims about CPDAG vs. DAG relationships and Theorem 3.
- Clarify or reference standard acyclicity-to-ordering reductions for the encoding.
- Justify or revise the claim that 10%–25% of ancestral constraints is typical for moderate/large graphs.
- Overall: solid, niche contribution that bridges ancestral to order constraints and enables EC-tree optimal BN learning, but needs theoretical clarifications and stronger, more appropriate empirical baselines and sensitivity analyses.
