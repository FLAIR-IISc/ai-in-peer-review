- Novel approach: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints using the EC tree search space + a decomposable-score oracle for constrained Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Sound method: projection is theoretically justified and enables effective oracle-based constrained learning.  
- Efficiency gains: empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods.  
- Strong motivation and organization: technically meaningful contribution with clear theoretical foundation and thorough presentation.  
- Empirical insight: a small fraction of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can yield accuracy improvements comparable to much larger datasets—background knowledge greatly helps.  
- Primary weakness: experimental presentation is confusing and obscures the specific contribution of the constraint-projection technique.  
- Key suggestion: explicitly compare (EC + new method) to EC alone to isolate the benefit of the constraint projection (current comparisons mix EC vs GOB and hide this effect).
