This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches to incorporate domain knowledge and improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper lies primarily in Section 5, where the authors introduce their proposed method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable. The paper is well-written and clearly illustrates the authors' work in both the theoretical and experimental sections. However, I have some concerns and suggestions as follows:

i. Table 3 shows that projecting ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether projecting ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints is specific to EC trees and lacks generalizability to other learning approaches. Given that the main innovation of this paper is to infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating such decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of certain learning approaches that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. What is the cause of this phenomenon? Does it imply that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, in contrast to the ILP approach? Consequently, does the number of ancestral constraints need to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance?

iii. Table 2 only reports the results of the structural Hamming distance from the EC trees. How does this compare to the ILP approach? It would be beneficial to include a comparison to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the results.

iv. A minor suggestion: While I understand that the BN graph provides theoretical foundations for EC trees, as EC trees are an extension of BN graphs, I would recommend removing Section 4.1 and dedicating more time to explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm in the context of EC trees, as the experimental section focuses primarily on the search space of EC trees with ancestral constraints applied.
