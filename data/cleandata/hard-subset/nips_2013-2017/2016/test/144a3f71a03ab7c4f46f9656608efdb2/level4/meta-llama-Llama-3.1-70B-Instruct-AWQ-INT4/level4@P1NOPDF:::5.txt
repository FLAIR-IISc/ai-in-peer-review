This paper tackles the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network configuration. Specifically, the authors demonstrate how to incorporate non-decomposable constraints, particularly ancestral constraints, into the structure learning approach proposed by Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. The empirical evaluation of the proposed approach against ILP-based methods reveals significant improvements, with orders of magnitude reductions in runtime. The authors reformulate the problem as a MaxSAT problem with specific constraints. Although my familiarity with this approach is limited, the results, particularly the runtime comparison, appear impressive. Notably, the authors show a substantial reduction in runtime by orders of magnitude compared to alternative methods.
