This paper proposes a novel approach for learning the structure of Bayesian networks optimally, subject to ancestral constraints. The authors introduce a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints, which can be utilized to improve the efficiency of structure learning.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear illustration of the proposed approach, both in the theoretical and experimental sections. The main contribution of the paper lies in Section 5, where the authors present their method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints.

The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it is orders-of-magnitude more efficient than learning systems based on integer linear programming (ILP). The results also highlight the impact of background knowledge on the quality of the learned model, demonstrating that a relatively small number of ancestral constraints can have a similar impact on the quality of the observed network as increasing the amount of data available.

However, there are some concerns and suggestions that need to be addressed. Firstly, the results in Table 3 suggest that the projection of ancestral constraints only improves the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. This raises questions about the generalization ability of the proposed method to other learning approaches. It would be more convincing if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of learning approaches that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

Secondly, the results in Table 2 show an easy-hard-easy pattern in the EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This suggests that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated. It would be helpful to provide more insight into this phenomenon and discuss the implications for tuning the number of ancestral constraints to achieve ideal performance.

Thirdly, while the paper reports the structural Hamming distance for the EC tree approach, it would be useful to compare this with the ILP approach to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the performance differences between the two methods.

Finally, a minor suggestion is to consider removing Section 4.1, which discusses the BN graph, and instead focus on providing a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC tree setting.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints.
* The method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints is well-explained and demonstrated to be effective.
* The experiments provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach, including a comparison with ILP-based systems.

Weaknesses:

* The generalization ability of the proposed method to other learning approaches is unclear.
* The sensitivity of the EC tree performance to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated needs to be further explored.
* A comparison of the structural Hamming distance between the EC tree and ILP approaches is missing.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints.
* The method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints is original and contributes to the field of Bayesian network structure learning.

Significance:

* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach in improving the efficiency of structure learning with ancestral constraints.
* The results highlight the impact of background knowledge on the quality of the learned model, which is a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning.

Quality:

* The experiments are comprehensive and provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed approach.
* The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the underlying theory and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.

Clarity:

While there are some concerns and suggestions that need to be addressed, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning.
