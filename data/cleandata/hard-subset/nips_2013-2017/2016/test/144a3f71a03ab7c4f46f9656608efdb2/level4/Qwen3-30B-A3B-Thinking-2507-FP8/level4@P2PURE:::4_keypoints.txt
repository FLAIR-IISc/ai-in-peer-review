- Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning to use domain knowledge.  
- Main novelty in Section 5: projecting ancestral constraints into edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Paper is well-written and presents clear theoretical and experimental results.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows projection improves EC tree but not ILP—questions about generalizability and whether projection benefits learners that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern for EC tree performance as constraint proportion varies—needs explanation; may indicate high sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge and require careful tuning.  
- Concern: Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees—need comparison with ILP performance.  
- Suggestion: Consider removing Section 4.1 and instead expand clarification of EC trees and the A* algorithm within the EC tree framework.
