- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that standard decomposable-score methods cannot handle.  
- Approach: extend A*-based search over equivalence classes (EC tree) using an oracle for decomposable scores and pruning CPDAGs that cannot meet ancestral constraints.  
- Key contribution 1: sound pruning test to discard EC tree nodes that cannot satisfy ancestral constraints.  
- Key contribution 2: projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (required/forbidden edges and ordering constraints) to tighten the oracle heuristic.  
- Empirical result: orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on benchmark networks and improved model accuracy with few constraints.  
- Strength — significance: tackles an important practical problem of incorporating ancestral prior knowledge into exact structure learning.  
- Strength — originality: novel and elegant idea to convert non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones to empower the heuristic.  
- Strength — quality: technically sound framework and well-designed experiments varying variables, data size, and constraints.  
- Weakness — clarity: explanation of inferred topological ordering constraints is ambiguous; method appears to select a maximal consistent set (via MaxSAT) rather than only strictly entailed orderings.  
- Weakness — ILP baseline: the chosen GOBNILP/MAX-SAT encoding may be weak (cubic auxiliary variables); a direct modern ILP encoding (e.g., Cussens 2010) could be a stronger baseline.  
- Weakness — experimental setup: no bound on parent set size favors A*-based methods, so adding bounded in-degree experiments would give a fairer comparison.  
- Weakness — missing related work: lacks discussion and comparison to Constraint Programming approaches (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann 2015) that naturally express ancestral constraints and can outperform GOBNILP.
