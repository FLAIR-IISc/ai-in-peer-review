This paper proposes a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints so that decomposable-score-based structure learning methods can incorporate such domain knowledge and thereby improve learning efficiency. The main novelty lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce a projection from ancestral constraints to edge and topological-ordering constraints, both of which are decomposable. The paper is clearly written and presents both the theoretical development and the experimental evaluation effectively.

Concerns and suggestions:
i. Table 3 indicates that projecting ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. Does this imply that projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints is effective primarily for EC trees and does not generalize well to other learning methods? Given that the core contribution is to infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating these projected constraints improves the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. In Table 2, the EC-tree results exhibit an easy–hard–easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. Why does this occur? Does it suggest that the impact of reducing the EC-tree search space via ancestral constraints is quite sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge, especially in comparison to the ILP approach? If so, does the number of ancestral constraints require careful tuning to achieve strong performance?

iii. Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees. How does this compare with the ILP approach?

iv. A small suggestion: while I understand that BN graphs provide the theoretical foundation for EC trees, the experiments focus solely on the EC-tree search space with ancestral constraints. I would recommend dropping Section 4.1 and instead spending more space on a clearer explanation of EC trees and of the A* algorithm in the EC-tree setting.
