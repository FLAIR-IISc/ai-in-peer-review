- Tackles optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints by adapting a decomposable-score oracle to non-decomposable constraints.  
- Searches the CPDAG space with A* guided by a heuristic that leverages the URLearning oracle.  
- Introduces a pruning strategy for the search space based on ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes “projecting” ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to empower the oracle.  
- Uses MaxSAT to infer ordering constraints, balancing constraint tightness and feasibility.  
- Well-motivated: incorporating ancestral/background knowledge is important for practical BN learning.  
- Core idea of decomposing non-decomposable constraints is sound and potentially impactful.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical speedups compared to the used ILP baseline (GOBNILP with a suboptimal encoding).  
- Theoretical pruning foundation appears solid, but Lemma 3’s proof is referenced and not fully explained.  
- Explanation of how projected constraints are passed to and used by URLearning lacks sufficient detail.  
- Description of ordering-constraint inference is initially confusing and should be clarified.  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: a stronger ILP encoding (e.g., Cussens 2010) should be used.  
- Fails to compare against van Beek & Hoffmann’s constraint programming approach, a notable omission.  
- Clarity issues and missing implementation details hinder reproducibility and full understanding.  
- Recommends addressing comparisons, clarifying oracle integration, and providing the full Lemma 3 proof.  
- Reviewer verdict: promising contribution but weaknesses warrant a weak reject pending fixes.
