This paper addresses the problem of optimal Bayesian network configuration. The authors demonstrate how to incorporate non-decomposable constraints – notably ancestral constraints – into the structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. Empirical evaluation of the proposed approach reveals order-of-magnitude improvements in performance compared to existing Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods. The authors reformulate the problem as a MaxSAT problem with tailored constraints. While my familiarity with this specific approach is limited, the reported results – particularly the runtime comparison – are highly impressive. Specifically, the authors demonstrate a runtime reduction by orders of magnitude relative to alternative methods.
