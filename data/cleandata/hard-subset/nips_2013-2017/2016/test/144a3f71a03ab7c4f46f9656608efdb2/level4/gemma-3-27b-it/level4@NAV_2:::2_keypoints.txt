- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints, a hard problem due to non-decomposability.
- Builds on recent non-decomposable-score framework and leverages decomposable-score oracles by projecting ancestral constraints into equivalent decomposable constraints.
- Projection idea is novel and potentially impactful for improving structure learning efficiency.
- Theoretical foundation (Theorems 1–4) is solid and supports correctness of the method.
- Experiments show significant speedups versus GOBNILP, especially as variable count increases.
- Analysis of how constraint projection affects performance is insightful and demonstrates practical benefits.
- Connection to MaxSAT for inferring ordering constraints is a clever and promising direction.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: varied network size, data size, and constraint percentage for comprehensive assessment.
- Structural Hamming distance analysis provides useful accuracy insights alongside efficiency results.
- Presentation of experimental results is somewhat convoluted—Tables 1 and 2 mix methods with/without projection, reducing clarity.
- Recommend a direct comparison between full approach (EC tree + constraint projection) and EC tree alone to isolate projection benefits.
- Reproducibility concern: equivalent sample size for the BDeu score is not specified and should be reported.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of projection approach, demonstrated efficiency gains, theoretical soundness, and practical significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experimental presentation and missing detail for reproducibility.
