This paper proposes a method for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and thus pose a challenge for traditional score-based approaches. The authors build upon a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, and demonstrate how to incorporate ancestral constraints into this framework. They also show how to infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which can be used to empower an oracle for structure search.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed approach, and the experimental evaluation. The experiments compare the proposed approach to a state-of-the-art ILP-based system, and demonstrate significant improvements in efficiency.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive. For example, it would be interesting to see a comparison between the proposed approach and other existing methods for learning Bayesian networks with constraints. Additionally, the authors could provide more insight into the types of problems for which the proposed approach is particularly well-suited.

Overall, I think the paper is a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning, and I would recommend its acceptance.
