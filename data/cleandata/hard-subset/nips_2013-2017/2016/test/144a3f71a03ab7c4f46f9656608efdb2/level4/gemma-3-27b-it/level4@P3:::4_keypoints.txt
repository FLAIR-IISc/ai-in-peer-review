- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable form for existing oracles.  
- Core contribution: method in Section 5 for inferring edge and topological ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on recent framework for non-decomposable scores and leverages EC tree search space with A* for optimization.  
- Well-situated in literature, citing foundational and recent relevant works.  
- Novelty: projecting ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable constraints is a new and useful idea.  
- Theoretical soundness: Theorems 1–4 provide a solid foundation, especially for pruning EC tree and inferred constraints.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on standard benchmarks show notable efficiency gains over ILP methods when using projected constraints.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, making methods and results easy to follow.  
- Weakness: projected constraints mainly benefit EC tree and do not consistently improve ILP performance, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: observed “easy-hard-easy” performance pattern across constraint proportions is unexplained and needs analysis.  
- Weakness: Table 2 lacks a Structural Hamming Distance comparison between EC tree and ILP, limiting accuracy evaluation.  
- Weakness: Section 4.1 on BN graph feels redundant given experiments focus solely on EC trees; could be streamlined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, potential impact on BN learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability of projected constraints, unexplained performance pattern, incomplete comparative accuracy metrics.  
- Overall: strong paper warranting acceptance with minor revisions addressing ILP generalizability, easy-hard-easy explanation, SHD comparison, and Section 4.1 focus.
