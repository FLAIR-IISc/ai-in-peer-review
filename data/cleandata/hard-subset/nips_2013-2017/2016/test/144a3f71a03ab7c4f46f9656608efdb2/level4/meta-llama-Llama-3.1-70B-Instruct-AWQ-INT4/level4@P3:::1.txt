This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The authors propose a framework that leverages a recently developed search space for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree. The approach empowers an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through empirical evaluations, showing that it is orders-of-magnitude more efficient than existing methods based on integer linear programming (ILP).

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its underlying principles. The technical content is sound, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature. The empirical evaluations are thorough and well-designed, providing a comprehensive comparison of the proposed approach with existing methods.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle the challenging problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The authors provide a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of MaxSAT to infer ordering constraints is also a clever approach that allows for the efficient incorporation of ancestral constraints into the learning process.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. The authors' comparison with the GOBNILP system is somewhat limited, as they use a poor formulation of the ILP encoding. A more sensible formulation, such as the one proposed in [Cussens, 2010], would provide a tighter linear relaxation and potentially better performance. Additionally, the authors do not compare their approach with other relevant methods, such as the constraint programming approach of van Beek and Hoffmann [2015]. Including such comparisons would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, and the technical content is sound. The paper presents a novel and original approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, and the empirical evaluations demonstrate its significance and potential impact on the field.
