This paper proposes a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones, specifically edge and topological ordering constraints. This projection allows structure learning algorithms that use decomposable scores to incorporate this form of domain knowledge, thereby improving learning efficiency. The primary novelty lies in the projection technique itself, which is detailed in Section 5. The paper is well-written, clearly presenting both the theoretical framework and the experimental results.

I have the following concerns and suggestions:

i. Table 3 indicates that the proposed projection of ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether the method's benefits are specific to EC trees, potentially limiting its generalizability. Given that the paper's main contribution is the inference of decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones, the argument would be significantly strengthened if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating these projected constraints improves the performance and efficiency of a learning algorithm that cannot natively handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 reveals a non-monotonic "easy-hard-easy" performance pattern for the EC tree method as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. The authors should provide an explanation for this phenomenon. Does this suggest that, compared to the ILP approach, the effectiveness of using ancestral constraints to prune the EC tree search space is highly sensitive to the quantity of domain knowledge provided? If so, does this imply that the number of ancestral constraints must be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance?

iii. Table 2 presents the Structural Hamming Distance (SHD) results for EC trees, but the corresponding results for the ILP approach are missing. Including the ILP results in this table would allow for a more direct and complete comparison.

iv. A minor suggestion regarding the paper's structure: While Bayesian Networks (BNs) provide the theoretical foundation for EC trees, the experiments focus exclusively on applying ancestral constraints within the EC tree search space. Therefore, the authors might consider removing Section 4.1 and using the space to provide a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* search algorithm in that context.
