- Problem and contribution: Learn optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten an A* oracle.  
- Novelty/significance: The projection technique for converting ancestral constraints into decomposable ones is novel and addresses a practical need for incorporating expert knowledge.  
- Main technical ideas: Theorems for inferring edge constraints and a MaxSAT reduction for ordering constraints are well-defined and theoretically grounded.  
- Empirical result: Projected constraints yield dramatic runtime improvements for the EC-tree A* method and outperform a strong ILP baseline in experiments.  
- Methodological insight: The approach “empowers” a fast but limited oracle within a general A* search, yielding a tighter heuristic and faster search.  
- Clarity and presentation: The paper is well-written, logically organized, and experimental results are clearly presented.  
- Easy–hard–easy pattern: Request for deeper explanation of the observed runtime “easy-hard-easy” pattern as constraint density varies and its relation to A*-based pruning versus ILP behavior.  
- Solution quality/SHD: Ask to clarify that both EC-tree and ILP are exact (thus should yield identical SHD when both finish) and note whether ILP timed out on instances where EC-tree succeeded.  
- Generalizability of projection: Ask why projected constraints help A* much more than ILP (sometimes small overhead for ILP), suggesting the technique mainly tightens A* heuristics.  
- Presentation suggestion: Recommend condensing Section 4.1 (BN graph) and reallocating space to better explain A* operation in the EC-tree framework.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, novel, well-evaluated, with only minor clarifications requested.
