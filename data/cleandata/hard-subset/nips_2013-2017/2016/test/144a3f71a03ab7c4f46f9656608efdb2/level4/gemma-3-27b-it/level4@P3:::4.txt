This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral constraints, a challenging task due to the non-decomposable nature of these constraints. The authors build upon a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, focusing on how to leverage oracles designed for decomposable scores by projecting the ancestral constraints into a decomposable form. The core contribution lies in Section 5, detailing a method for inferring both edge and topological ordering constraints from the ancestral constraints. The paper is well-written and clearly presents both the theoretical foundations and empirical results. 

The work is situated well within the existing literature on Bayesian network structure learning, referencing key papers in the field (Darwiche, 2009; Koller & Friedman, 2009; Murphy, 2012) and recent advances utilizing non-decomposable scores (Chen et al., 2015, 2016). It also correctly positions ancestral constraints as challenging for traditional approaches relying on decomposable scores (Koivisto & Sood, 2004; Parviainen & Koivisto, 2013) and acknowledges alternative methods like Integer Linear Programming (Jaakkola et al., 2010; Bartlett & Cussens, 2015; van Beek & Hoffmann, 2015). The use of the EC tree as a search space is appropriate given the focus on non-decomposable priors.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms is a novel approach that effectively bridges the gap between existing oracles and practical constraints.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The theorems presented (Theorems 1-4) provide a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed approach, particularly regarding the soundness of pruning the EC tree and the constraints that can be inferred.
* **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experiments demonstrate significant performance improvements compared to an ILP-based approach, particularly with the projected constraints. The chosen benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) are well-established in the field.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to understand the concepts and methodology.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Generalizability of Projected Constraints:** The empirical results (Table 3) indicate that the projected constraints primarily benefit the EC tree approach and do not consistently improve the ILP performance. This raises a concern about the generalizability of the core contribution – inferring decomposable constraints – to other learning algorithms. Further investigation into why this occurs would be valuable.
* **Easy-Hard-Easy Pattern:** The observed “easy-hard-easy” pattern in Table 2, where EC tree performance fluctuates with the proportion of ancestral constraints, is not fully explained. Understanding this sensitivity would strengthen the analysis. Is this specific to the EC tree structure and the inference method?
* **Missing Comparison of Structural Hamming Distance:** While Table 2 presents the structural Hamming distance for the EC tree, it lacks a corresponding comparison with the ILP approach. Including this comparison would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the accuracy of both methods.
* **BN Graph Section:** While the BN graph provides historical context, its limited role in the experimental evaluation (only EC trees are considered) makes Section 4.1 somewhat redundant. A more focused discussion of the EC tree and the A* algorithm within that context would be more effective.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem in Bayesian network structure learning.
* The empirical results demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods, particularly in terms of efficiency.
* The work is well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to a broad audience.
* The findings could have a significant impact on the development of more efficient and effective Bayesian network learning algorithms.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The limited generalizability of the projected constraints raises questions about the broader applicability of the core contribution.
* The unexplained “easy-hard-easy” pattern requires further investigation.
* The lack of a direct comparison of structural Hamming distance between the EC tree and ILP approaches limits the comprehensive evaluation.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution and compelling empirical results. While the limited generalizability of the projected constraints is a concern, the overall quality of the work and the potential impact on the field warrant acceptance. 

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the following points:

1.  Investigate the reasons why the projected constraints do not consistently improve ILP performance.
2.  Provide a more in-depth explanation of the “easy-hard-easy” pattern observed in Table 2.
3.  Include a comparison of the structural Hamming distance between the EC tree and ILP approaches in Table 2.
4.  Consider streamlining Section 4.1 to focus more directly on the EC tree and A* search within that context.
