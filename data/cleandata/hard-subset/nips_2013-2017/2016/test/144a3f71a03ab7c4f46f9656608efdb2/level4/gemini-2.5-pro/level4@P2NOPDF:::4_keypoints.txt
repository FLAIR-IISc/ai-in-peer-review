- Core contribution: a projection technique that converts non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, enabling decomposable-score structure learners to use richer domain knowledge.

- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written and clear; theoretical framework and experiments are easy to follow.

- Major concern — generalizability: projection improves EC tree but not ILP (Table 3), raising questions whether the method works beyond specific algorithms; need demonstration on at least one additional learner.

- Major concern — non-monotonic behavior: EC tree results show an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as constraint proportion increases (Table 2); authors should analyze why performance is non-monotonic and how sensitive results are to constraint quantity.

- Major concern — incomplete comparison: Table 2 reports SHD for EC tree but omits ILP SHD under the same conditions; include ILP results for proper accuracy comparison.

- Minor suggestion — paper focus: consider shortening the BN background (Section 4.1) and instead expand on EC tree details and the A* implementation used in experiments.
