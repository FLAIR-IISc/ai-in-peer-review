Summary:
The paper tackles exact structure learning for Bayesian networks under non-decomposable constraints, with a focus on ancestral constraints. Building on the framework of Chen et al. (2015, 2016), the authors present a MaxSAT formulation that encodes these constraints alongside standard structure and scoring components. Empirically, the approach substantially outperforms ILP-based baselines on runtime, reportedly by orders of magnitude, while yielding optimal solutions consistent with the specified constraints.

Strengths:
- Clear problem motivation: incorporating domain knowledge that is not decomposable over local parent sets (e.g., ancestral relations) is both practically important and insufficiently supported by existing exact learners.
- Technical contribution: a principled MaxSAT encoding that supports non-decomposable constraints within an exact optimizer. The emphasis on ancestral constraints is well-justified, and the formulation appears general enough to capture other global constraints.
- Empirical performance: the reported speedups over ILP approaches are striking, with substantial reductions in runtime while maintaining optimality.
- Potential impact: the work enables richer forms of prior knowledge in exact BN structure learning and suggests MaxSAT as a competitive substrate for constrained learning problems.

Weaknesses and concerns:
- Clarity of the encoding: while the paper outlines the MaxSAT formulation, several components are described at a high level. It would help to clearly separate (i) base acyclicity and parent-set selection constraints, (ii) scoring representation, and (iii) global non-decomposable constraints, and to give concrete examples of each.
- Generality beyond ancestral constraints: the paper emphasizes ancestral constraints but claims support for wider classes of non-decomposable constraints. More explicit characterization and examples (e.g., forbidden substructures, separation constraints, or interventional constraints) would strengthen the generality claim.
- Evaluation details and fairness: the baseline ILP methods should be precisely specified (e.g., GOBNILP version/settings, parent set size bounds, cutting planes, preprocessing). It is important to ensure equivalent candidate parent sets, scoring functions (BIC/MDL), and hardware/time limits to make the comparisons fully convincing.
- Scalability boundaries: the paper reports strong results, but it is not entirely clear how performance scales with (a) number of variables, (b) maximum parent set size, and (c) number and type of global constraints. A sensitivity analysis would help locate break-even points relative to ILP and to alternative SAT/MaxSAT encodings.
- Completeness and optimality guarantees: the theoretical argument that the MaxSAT formulation is both sound and complete for the specified constraints should be stated formally. If optimality hinges on particular assumptions (e.g., bounded parent set sizes or specific scoring decompositions), those should be made explicit.
- Practical utility: while runtime is the focus, a small case study demonstrating how ancestral constraints meaningfully alter the learned structure (e.g., aligning with known causal orderings) would better demonstrate the practical benefit of supporting non-decomposable constraints.

Questions for the authors:
- Which MaxSAT solver(s) were used, and with what configurations? Were any problem-specific heuristics or preprocessing steps critical to the observed speedups?
- What scoring function and priors were used (e.g., BDeu, BIC/MDL), and how were equivalent settings ensured across baselines?
- Are parent set sizes bounded? If so, how do bounds affect solution quality and runtime in your formulation compared to ILP?
- Can you provide examples of non-ancestral, non-decomposable constraints supported by your encoding, along with any empirical results for them?
- How does the approach behave when constraints are inconsistent or nearly inconsistent? Do you detect infeasibility early, and can the framework accommodate soft constraints with penalties?
- Is the code available to facilitate reproducibility?

Suggestions for improvement:
- Add a small, self-contained example that walks through the encoding of ancestral constraints into MaxSAT, including how violations are penalized or prohibited.
- Include ablations: (i) MaxSAT with only base constraints vs. with ancestral constraints, and (ii) varying the number of ancestral constraints to show scaling.
- Report memory usage and time-to-first-feasible vs. time-to-optimal to illuminate solver behavior.
- Expand related work to include constraint programming and SAT-based BN learners, clarifying how your encoding differs from prior SAT/ASP/CP formulations and from ILP approaches like GOBNILP.

Overall evaluation:
This is a well-motivated and technically solid contribution that advances exact Bayesian network structure learning under rich, non-decomposable constraints. The MaxSAT formulation is promising and empirically compelling, with notable runtime advantages over ILP baselines. The paper would benefit from clearer exposition of the encoding, a more detailed and balanced evaluation, and stronger evidence for generality beyond ancestral constraints. With these improvements, the work has strong potential for publication.
