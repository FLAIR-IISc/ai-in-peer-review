- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using a branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs.  
- Transitions in the search involve selecting a parent set for a variable not present in the initial CPDAG.  
- Symmetry breaking is implemented effectively to reduce redundant search.  
- Search is guided by a heuristic from the URLearning system.  
- Ancestral constraints are used to prune the search tree; Lemma 3 is a key theoretical result enabling this pruning.  
- Proof of Lemma 3 is unclear, notably the unexplained claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W."  
- Authors use implied or "projected" constraints to infer edge restrictions and permissible topological orderings.  
- The method for inferring projected constraints is under-explained and the claim "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading about certainty.  
- The authors actually aim to infer consistent ordering constraints and solve this with a MAXSAT encoding.  
- Experiments vary number of variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints, and show substantial improvements over GOBNILP.  
- The GOBNILP comparison is compromised because the authors use a suboptimal earlier MAXSAT encoding for ancestral constraints instead of a tighter ILP encoding.  
- A revised encoding using A(X,Y) binary variables and omitting E(X,Y,Z) would give a tighter linear relaxation and likely better performance.  
- The paper should compare to van Beek and Hoffmann’s method, which often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted for ancestral constraints.
