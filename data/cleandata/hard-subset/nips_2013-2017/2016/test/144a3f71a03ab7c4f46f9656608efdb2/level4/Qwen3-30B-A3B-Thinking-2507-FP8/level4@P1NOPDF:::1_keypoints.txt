- Addresses Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints via branch-and-bound on CPDAGs, selecting parent sets to move between CPDAG nodes.  
- Uses the URLearning system as a heuristic for search; details are deferred to a prior paper.  
- Lemma 3 is the key pruning result and likely correct, but its proof needs clarification—specifically the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z → W" requires justification.  
- Uses "projected constraints" (aka implied constraints) and derives ordering constraints, but the phrasing that "Z not an ancestor of Y implies Y < Z" is misleading and needs precision.  
- The method actually adds consistent ordering constraints via MAXSAT, but the explanation of this MAXSAT-based inference process is insufficiently detailed.  
- Experiments show the proposed system is much faster than GOBNILP, but it’s unclear whether GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints or the lack of a parent-set cardinality limit.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints using Cussens (2008) MAXSAT instead of the ILP-style encoding from Cussens (2010), which is suboptimal for ILP solvers.  
- A better ILP encoding would keep A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables, drop cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables, enforce transitivity on A and A(X,Y) ≥ sum_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U), yielding a tighter relaxation and superior performance.  
- Comparing against the MAXSAT encoding weakens the evaluation and is misleading; the authors should compare to a sensible ILP formulation.  
- The paper omits comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann’s CP approach (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted to ancestral constraints—this benchmark should be included.  
- Overall: core idea of using ancestral constraints for pruning is sound, but unclear Lemma 3 proof, imprecise constraint-inference exposition, and suboptimal comparison choices undermine the technical evaluation.
