This paper proposes a method for converting non-decidable parent constraints into decidable constraints, so that decidible-score-based learning approaches can incorporate domain knowledge of such domains and thereby improve learning efficiency. The novelty of this paper lies mostly in Section 5, where the authors present their proposed method for converting non-decidable parent constraints into edge and topological order constraints, which are decidible. The paper is well-written and clearly demonstrates their work in both the theoretical and experimental sections. My concerns and suggestions are as follows: i. Table 3 shows that projecting parent constraints can only improve the performance of EC trees, but not in the ILP approach. Does this mean that projecting parent constraints into decidible constraints only works for EC trees and has poor generalization capacity for other learning approaches? Considering the main innovation of this paper to be estimating decidible constraints from non-decidable parent constraints, it would be more convincing if the authors could show that incorporating such decidible constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of some learning approaches that are not originally capable of handling non-decidable constraints. ii. Table 2 shows an easy-difficult-easy pattern on EC tree performance as the proportion of parent constraints increases. Why does this happen? Does this mean that the effect of reducing the search space of EC trees with parent constraints is significantly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge included compared to the ILP approach? Therefore, the number of parent constraints needs to be carefully adjusted to achieve ideal performance? iii. Table 2 only shows the results of structural Hamming distance from EC trees. But, how does this compare with the ILP approach? iv. A small suggestion: I understand that the theoretical basis of EC trees is in the BN graph because EC trees expand from it. However, since only the search space of EC trees with applied parent constraints is considered in the experimental section, I will skip Section 4.1 and spend more time explaining the EC tree and A* algorithm in the EC tree settings.
