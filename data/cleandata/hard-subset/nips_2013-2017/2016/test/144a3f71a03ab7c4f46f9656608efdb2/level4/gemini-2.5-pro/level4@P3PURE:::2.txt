The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm.

The experimental evaluation, however, was difficult to follow. As I understand it, Table 1 compares two existing approaches, EC and GOB, after both have been augmented with the authors' proposed method. It then appears that Table 3 compares the original EC and GOB baselines. This experimental design makes it difficult to isolate the precise contribution of the new method.

To demonstrate the value of this work more clearly, the evaluation would be significantly strengthened by a direct comparison between a baseline and that same baseline augmented with the proposed method (e.g., EC vs. EC + proposed method). While comparing the relative gains for GOB is interesting, the results indicate that EC is a substantially stronger baseline. Consequently, even if GOB benefits more from the proposed method, the final performance of the enhanced EC system is what ultimately matters. A direct comparison against the standalone EC algorithm is therefore crucial for assessing the practical utility of this work.
