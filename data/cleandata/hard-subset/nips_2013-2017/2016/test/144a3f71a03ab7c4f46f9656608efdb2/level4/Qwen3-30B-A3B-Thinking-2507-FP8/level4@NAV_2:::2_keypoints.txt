- Introduces a novel method to learn Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s framework to enable use of efficient oracles for decomposable scores.  
- Leverages the EC tree search space to allow sound pruning based on ancestral constraints while retaining oracle compatibility.  
- Provides a theoretically sound projection technique for integrating non-decomposable background knowledge.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude empirical efficiency improvements over ILP-based (GOB) methods across problem sizes.  
- Experimental design carefully evaluates how ancestral constraints affect search efficiency and scalability.  
- Shows EC outperforms GOB substantially regardless of constraints.  
- Claims background knowledge can improve model accuracy comparably to much larger datasets.  
- Main weakness: experimental presentation is confusing—comparisons mix (EC + new) vs (GOB + new') and EC vs GOB, obscuring the isolated impact of the constraint projection.  
- Recommendation: explicitly compare (EC + new method) against EC alone to isolate the projection method's benefit.  
- Additional weakness: paper could better emphasize how the constraint projection differs from existing constraint-handling approaches.  
- Overall: valuable contribution that expands practical, optimal structure learning to include ancestral constraints and advances the state of the art.
