- Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to inform an admissible heuristic for exhaustive search.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s oracle framework to enable guidance of decomposable-score structure learning via inferred constraints.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound, with clear theorems proving correctness of the constraint projection.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based methods (GOBNILP).  
- A small number of ancestral constraints can substantially improve learned-network accuracy, sometimes comparable to adding more data.  
- Experimental presentation is unclear; Tables 1 and 3 need clearer explanations of what each comparison demonstrates.  
- A crucial missing experiment is a direct comparison of the EC tree approach with and without constraint projection to isolate the projection’s benefit.  
- Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver for inferring ordering constraints may introduce a performance bottleneck and external dependency.  
- Scalability is demonstrated up to 20 variables, but limits for larger networks are not investigated.  
- Novel and important contribution to learning Bayesian networks with background knowledge.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve experimental clarity.  
- Main weaknesses: unclear experimental comparisons and potential MaxSAT-induced overhead.
