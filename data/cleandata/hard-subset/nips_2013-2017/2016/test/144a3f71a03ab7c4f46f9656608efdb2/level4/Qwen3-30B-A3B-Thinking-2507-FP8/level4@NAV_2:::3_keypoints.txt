- Proposes translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints for efficient score-based Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Builds on the EC tree search space framework (Chen et al.) and uses a MaxSAT solver to pass inferred ordering constraints to oracles.  
- Empirically outperforms an ILP-based Gobnilp implementation with linear ancestral constraints by orders of magnitude.  
- Addresses an important gap: most optimal learning methods assume decomposable scores and cannot directly handle ancestral constraints.  
- The translation approach is technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Empirical results clearly demonstrate substantial efficiency gains.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and relation to prior work.  
- Problem setting is somewhat artificial because ancestral constraints are usually used alongside causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders, which the paper omits.  
- Practical impact may be limited since only 10%–25% ancestral constraints are reported to achieve improvements, which may not reflect real-world sparsity.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp lacks more meaningful baselines; comparisons to greedy algorithms like GES would contextualize practical efficiency (despite their lack of optimality guarantees).  
- Several technical details need clarification: the distinction between "standard" and "omniscient" oracles is unclear.  
- The phrase "empower the oracle" should be clearer.  
- The optimization direction should be explicitly stated as minimization.  
- Theorem 1 should clarify that CPDAG constraints are a subset of the corresponding DAG’s constraints.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- The acyclicity condition (line 267) is not clearly captured by the presented rule and needs clarification.  
- Overall: technically sound, niche but important contribution; would be stronger by addressing practical limitations and adding more relevant baseline comparisons.
