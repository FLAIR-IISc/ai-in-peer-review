This work addresses the task of identifying an optimal Bayesian network structure. More specifically, the authors demonstrate how to integrate non-decomposable constraints—especially ancestral constraints—into the structure-learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. An empirical comparison with ILP-based methods indicates improvements by orders of magnitude. The core idea is to cast the problem as a MaxSAT formulation with tailored constraints. Although my familiarity with this methodology is limited, the results (in terms of runtime) appear quite compelling. In particular, the authors report order-of-magnitude speedups over competing approaches.
