- Proposes a novel method to learn optimal Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints by inferring decomposable edge and ordering constraints for an exact oracle.  
- Builds on the EC tree search space to handle non-decomposable scores and projects ancestral constraints into a decomposable form.  
- Passes inferred constraints to an exact learner (oracle) to prune the search space and accelerate learning.  
- Empirically demonstrates orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over an adapted ILP (GOBNILP) baseline.  
- Strength: original, well-founded solution to incorporating ancestral constraints into score-based exact learning.  
- Strength: manuscript is generally clear and well organized; core ideas are communicated effectively.  
- Strength: algorithm is conceptually sound and experimental results are impressive.  
- Weakness: practical impact is questionable since causal discovery methods that handle latent confounding may be more natural when strong ancestral knowledge exists.  
- Weakness: method’s efficiency appears to depend on dense prior constraints (10–25% of ancestral relations), which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison to an adapted ILP may not be representative; missing comparisons to fast greedy baselines like GES.  
- Weakness: technical clarity issues in the MaxSAT projection, especially the acyclicity constraint (rule 3), which is hard to parse and appears asymmetric.  
- Weakness: rule 4 may only handle one type of negative ancestral constraint and might not cover mutual non-ancestry; needs clarification.  
- Suggestion: replace the term “omniscient oracle” with “exact oracle” and avoid repetitive phrasing like “empower the oracle.”  
- Suggestion: explicitly state that “optimizing” the score means finding the minimum-scoring structure.  
- Suggestion: clarify Theorem 1 regarding checking constraints A only for variables present in a CPDAG P and how this projects to descendants.  
- Suggestion: discuss how frequently Theorem 3’s strict conditions for inferring edge constraints are met in practice, particularly with sparse initial constraints.  
- Suggestion: authors should comment on applicability in more realistic settings with only a few ancestral constraints rather than the dense settings used in experiments.
