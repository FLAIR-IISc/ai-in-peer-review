- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints, building on Chen et al.'s framework.  
- Shows how to incorporate ancestral constraints into the EC tree search space.  
- Method: infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints, then use an oracle for decomposable scores.  
- Contribution: sound and complete EC tree pruning method based on ancestral constraints.  
- Contribution: systematic projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration of orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based methods.  
- Provides formal theorems establishing correctness of the constraint projection methods.  
- Thorough experiments on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) with varied sizes and constraint proportions.  
- Shows that a small fraction (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can substitute for large increases in data (e.g., 512 → 8192 samples).  
- Strength: addresses a practical limitation in structure learning and provides a solid theoretical bridge to decomposable-score oracles.  
- Strength: compelling empirical speedups and scalability (up to 20 variables) for exact BN learning with constraints.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation presentation could be clearer for non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit discussion of the computational complexity of the constraint projection algorithms.  
- Weakness: would benefit from deeper analysis of when the method is most effective (constraint types, network structures).  
- Overall: well-aligned with NIPS topics, advances the state of the art, and is recommended for acceptance.
