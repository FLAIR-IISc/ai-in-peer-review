- Presents an efficient method translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints used with EC tree, outperforming ILP (Gobnilp) on simulations.  
- Addresses a relevant but underexplored problem; originality rated moderate (3/5) with sound motivation to integrate prior knowledge.  
- Projection technique is clever and enables use of existing efficient score-based learning algorithms.  
- Empirical results convincingly show substantial speedups vs ILP.  
- Practical impact may be limited when only a small number of ancestral constraints are known, yielding little search-space reduction (significance 2/5).  
- Method may be less appropriate when strong causal assumptions or unobserved confounders require general causal discovery algorithms.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically builds on the EC tree framework.  
- Constraint projection rules (lines 257–261) need clarification; potential acyclicity symmetry flaw and restrictive exclusion of reciprocal ancestral constraints.  
- The relation between the “omniscient oracle” and a standard oracle is unclear; phrase “empower the oracle” is vague.  
- Minor wording fix: “optimizing” is generally equivalent to “minimizing” (line 90).  
- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that constraints satisfied by a CPDAG are a subset of those satisfied by the corresponding DAG.  
- Theorem 3 may produce limited benefits when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Evaluation should include a greedy baseline (e.g., GES with constraints) as a more relevant comparator than ILP.  
- Discussion should justify the practical prevalence of 10–25% constraint levels, since that implies substantial prior knowledge.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions if authors clarify projection rules and strengthen evaluation.
