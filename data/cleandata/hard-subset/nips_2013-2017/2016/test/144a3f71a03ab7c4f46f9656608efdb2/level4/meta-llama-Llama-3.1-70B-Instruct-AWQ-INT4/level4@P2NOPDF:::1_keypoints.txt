- Paper addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints via constrained optimization.  
- Uses a branch-and-bound search where nodes are CPDAGs and expansion selects a parent set absent from the current CPDAG.  
- Applies sensible symmetry breaking to reduce the search space.  
- Search guided by a heuristic from the URLearning system (as in prior work).  
- Incorporation of ancestral constraints enables pruning; Lemma 3 in the supplement is central to this pruning.  
- Proof of Lemma 3 is not entirely clear (notably the claim “By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W”), though it appears correct.  
- Authors consider implied/projected constraints (standard terminology) to further prune the tree.  
- They infer ordering constraints on topological orderings, but the explanation is unclear and the phrasing “we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is misleading.  
- Correct interpretation: if “Z is not an ancestor of Y” then the constraint Y < Z may be added; authors use MAXSAT to infer a maximal consistent set without over-constraining.  
- Experiments vary number of variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints and compare to GOBNILP, showing the proposed method is significantly faster.  
- GOBNILP struggles with unlimited parent sets, so it’s unclear whether the speedup is due to handling ancestral constraints or overall algorithmic efficiency.  
- The ancestral-constraint encoding used for comparison is a MAXSAT encoding from Cussens (2008), which is ill-suited for ILP systems.  
- A better ILP encoding would use A(X,Y) binary variables plus I(Z,U), omit E(X,Y,Z), enforce transitivity on A and link A–I, yielding a tighter linear relaxation (similar to Cussens 2010 §3.1.1 adapted for partial orders).  
- Even with a sensible ILP formulation, the proposed method would likely still outperform GOBNILP, but the current comparison is weakened by the suboptimal encoding; a stronger comparison would be to van Beek & Hoffmann’s method, which the authors do not cite.
