This paper presents a novel approach to projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints, thereby enabling decomposable-score-based structure learning methods to incorporate domain knowledge and enhance learning efficiency. The primary innovation of this work is outlined in Section 5, where the authors introduce a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto edge and topological ordering constraints, which are decomposable. The paper is well-written, providing clear explanations of both the theoretical and experimental aspects of their work.

However, I have several concerns and suggestions for improvement:

i. The results in Table 3 indicate that projecting ancestral constraints only enhances the performance of EC trees, but not the ILP approach. This raises questions about the generalizability of this method to other learning approaches. Given that the paper's main contribution lies in inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints, it would be more convincing to demonstrate that incorporating these decomposable constraints can improve the performance and efficiency of learning approaches that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern in EC tree performance as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. This pattern warrants further explanation. Does it suggest that the effect of reducing the EC tree search space with ancestral constraints is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge incorporated, particularly when compared to the ILP approach? If so, this implies that the number of ancestral constraints requires careful tuning to achieve optimal performance.

iii. While Table 2 presents results on the structural Hamming distance for EC trees, a comparison with the ILP approach is missing. It would be beneficial to include this comparison to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's performance.

iv. A minor suggestion: Although the BN graph provides theoretical foundations for EC trees, the experimental section primarily focuses on the search space of EC trees with applied ancestral constraints. Consider removing Section 4.1 and allocating more space to explaining EC trees and the A* algorithm in the context of EC trees.
