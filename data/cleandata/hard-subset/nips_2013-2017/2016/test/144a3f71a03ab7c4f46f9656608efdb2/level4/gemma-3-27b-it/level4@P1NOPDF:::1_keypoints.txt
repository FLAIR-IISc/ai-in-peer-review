- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and A* search with a heuristic from prior URLearning work.  
- Heuristic integration is deferred to a prior publication, so details are not fully presented here.  
- Ancestral constraints enable search pruning; Lemma 3 is central but its proof is hard to follow—specifically the claim “Gk also contains edge Z -> W” needs clarification.  
- The paper uses “projected” (implied) constraints from ancestral constraints to prune, and also infers ordering constraints, but the inference method is imprecisely described (e.g., “can infer Y < Z” is ambiguous).  
- The authors aim to add as many consistent ordering constraints as possible (not just logically entailed ones) and use MAXSAT to avoid over-constraining, but the procedure is not fully explained.  
- Experiments vary variables, data points, and ancestral constraints and show large speedups over GOBNILP, but without parent-set limits it’s unclear if GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to large parent sets or ancestral-constraint handling.  
- The supplemental encoding of ancestral constraints uses a MAXSAT formulation from Cussens (2008) rather than the ILP encoding in Cussens (2010), which appears suboptimal for ILP solvers.  
- Reviewer proposes a tighter ILP formulation using binary ancestor variables A(X,Y) plus I(Z,U) and transitivity/linking constraints (e.g., A(X,Y) ≥ Σ{U:X∈U} I(Y,U)) to remove cubic E-variables and improve relaxations.  
- Comparing against a suboptimal ILP/formulation weakens the evaluation; recommend comparing to van Beek & Hoffmann’s CP-based method (often better than GOBNILP), which is not cited and could likely be adapted to ancestral constraints.
