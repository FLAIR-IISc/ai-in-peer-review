- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral/path constraints by pruning EC-tree search at the CPDAG level and projecting constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints.

- Projection uses closure over ancestral relations for edge constraints and a MaxSAT formulation to extract a sound, maximized set of topological-order constraints.

- Theorems are provided that justify the pruning and projection rules; empirical results show substantial runtime gains over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP), often by orders of magnitude.

- Experiments explore scaling with variable count and constraint density, revealing an easy–hard–easy trend and accuracy improvements as more constraints or data are available.

- Builds on Chen et al.’s BN-graph and EC-tree frameworks; novelty lies in exact EC-tree pruning under ancestral constraints and MaxSAT-based projection to tighten decomposable oracles.

- Prior exact score-based methods typically require decomposable scores; ILP/CP can handle non-decomposable constraints but lacked systematic evaluation for ancestral constraints.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem—ancestral constraints are practically useful and non-decomposable.

- Strength: Methodologically solid—pruning criteria and entailment theorems are well specified and formalized.

- Strength: Projected constraints meaningfully improve the oracle’s heuristic quality, yielding practical speedups.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation is thorough and shows consistent, large speedups plus useful analyses of constraint/data effects.

- Weakness: Some technical details are omitted (closure rules, complexity of enumerating class(P)), making worst-case behavior and completeness hard to assess.

- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology—e.g., “maximum set of ancestral constraints” needs precise definition and complexity analysis.

- Weakness: Limited baselines—only ILP compared; CP-based methods are mentioned but not experimentally evaluated.

- Weakness: No robustness study for noisy or misspecified ancestral constraints, reducing practical relevance.

- Weakness: Unclear how the projection framework generalizes to other non-decomposable constraints (e.g., path bans over sets, v-structure constraints).

- Weakness: Editorial and notation issues (stray text, missing footnotes, α/β inconsistencies) that require proofreading.

- Evaluation (quality/clarity): Technically sound with formal support; clarity good overall but needs tightened definitions and more algorithmic detail.

- Originality/significance: Novel and useful combination of EC-tree pruning with provably safe projection and MaxSAT ordering inference; results are significant for practitioners.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical and empirical contribution, dramatic speedups, and a reusable MaxSAT-based ordering technique.

- Cons for acceptance: Missing ablations/baselines, incomplete algorithmic detail and complexity discussion, and lack of robustness experiments.

- Recommendation: Weak accept—solid, original work with strong empirical support; revise to clarify technical details, add baselines/robustness studies, and fix editorial issues.
