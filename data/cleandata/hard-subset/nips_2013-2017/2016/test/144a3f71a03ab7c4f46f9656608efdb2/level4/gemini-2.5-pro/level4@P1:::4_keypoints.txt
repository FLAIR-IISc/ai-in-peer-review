- Tackles learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to enable oracle-based A* EC-tree search.
- Proposes a novel constraint-projection technique that transforms non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones, with potential broader impact.
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, explaining BN graphs, EC trees, and the projection method.
- Empirical results show substantial runtime improvements (often orders of magnitude) over ILP solver GOBNILP and practical gains from incorporating background knowledge.
- Unclear generalizability: projection greatly aids EC-tree/A* search but shows mixed benefits for ILP (overhead on small problems, moderate gains on large); need clarity on which algorithms benefit and why.
- Observed "easy-hard-easy" runtime pattern as ancestral-constraint density changes; authors should provide intuition and discuss sensitivity to constraint density.
- Table 3 omits SHD for the ILP baseline; a direct accuracy comparison is needed, especially for larger instances where the proposed method is faster.
- Suggest condensing BN-graph background and expanding the EC-tree/A* search exposition to better align with the experimental focus.
