The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm.

The experimental evaluation was somewhat unclear. My understanding is that Table 1 compares two existing approaches, EC and GOB, after each has been augmented with the proposed method. In contrast, Table 3 appears to compare the original EC and GOB baselines directly, though clarification on this would be helpful.

A key comparison that seems to be missing is an explicit ablation study showing the performance gain of the proposed method when applied to the stronger baseline. Specifically, a direct comparison between EC with the proposed method versus the original EC would be highly valuable. While the analysis of the method's impact on GOB is interesting, EC appears to be a significantly superior baseline. Consequently, even if the proposed method yields a greater relative improvement for GOB, the absolute performance of the enhanced EC system is likely the most relevant. Quantifying the improvement over the standalone EC baseline is therefore crucial for assessing the practical utility of this work.
