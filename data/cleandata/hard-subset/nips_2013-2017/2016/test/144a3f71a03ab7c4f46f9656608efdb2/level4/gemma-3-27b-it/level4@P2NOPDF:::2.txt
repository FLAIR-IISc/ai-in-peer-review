The authors introduce a novel method that utilizes ancestral constraints to refine the heuristic function guiding an exhaustive search algorithm. My primary concern lies with the presentation of experimental results, which I found difficult to interpret. The comparisons in Table 1 appear structured as (EC + proposed method) versus (GOB + a modified proposed method – “new work'”). If my understanding is correct, Table 3 then attempts a direct comparison between EC and GOB. 

However, a more insightful evaluation would include an explicit comparison between EC *with* the proposed method and EC alone. While the contrast between (GOB + new work') and GOB is interesting, the inherent superiority of EC over GOB is substantial. Consequently, even a significant performance improvement in GOB resulting from “new work’” would likely not outweigh the benefits of applying the proposed method to EC. In other words, demonstrating a substantial benefit *on top of* a strong baseline (EC) is more convincing than showing a larger relative gain on a weaker baseline (GOB).

To clarify, it’s unclear *why* “new work’” differs from the proposed method, and how this impacts the fairness of the comparison. Is “new work’” specifically tailored to GOB, potentially inflating its relative gains? Addressing this ambiguity would greatly improve the clarity of the experimental analysis. A direct assessment of the proposed method’s impact when integrated with both EC and GOB would provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness and generalizability.
