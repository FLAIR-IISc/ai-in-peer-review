- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral (non-decomposable) background constraints.  
- Builds on a framework for non-decomposable scores and leverages decomposable-score oracles by inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Introduces a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree search space using ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes a method to infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to empower oracles.  
- Uses MaxSAT to encode the problem, providing a creative encoding of constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based approaches.  
- Demonstrates scalability with experiments up to 20 variables.  
- Thorough review of related work (score-based methods, ILP, CP) and clear identification of limitations of mainstream approaches.  
- Technical contributions are significant and well-explained.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with easy-to-understand results presentation.  
- Strengths: novel solution to non-decomposable constraints, significant technical contributions, thorough empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: assumes reader familiarity with Bayesian network structure learning and MaxSAT; limited discussion of approach limitations and future work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—significant contribution with potential impact on the field.
