- Proposes translating ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints to integrate with decomposable score-based BN learning on the EC tree (uses MaxSAT).

- Empirically outperforms a straightforward ILP-modified Gobnilp baseline in the tested setting.

- Paper is clear and well structured, but the core idea is conceptually straightforward.

- Novelty present (no standard solution for ancestral-constraint BN learning) but broader impact limited because settings with ancestral relations often favor more general causal discovery (novelty=3, impact=2).

- Method maps ancestral constraints to absent-edge/order constraints so they fit decomposable scoring; MaxSAT used to make implied constraints explicit.

- Several rule statements (lines 257–261) appear strange or incomplete and need fixing/justification.

- Acyclicity rule 3 is asymmetric in conclusion despite symmetric premises; this is puzzling and likely incorrect.

- Rule 4 seems to exclude plausible “X and Y do not cause each other” constraints and should support that important class.

- Practicality concern: realistically only few ancestral constraints are available, so pruning of the search space may be limited.

- Gobnilp baseline is not purpose-built for this task; stronger/more informative comparison would be to a greedy method (e.g., GES) under oracle-consistent constraints.

- Theorem 1 should explicitly state that constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG on the EC tree are only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G.

- Theorem 3 likely yields few positive edge implications when constraint sets are small/sparse.

- The acyclicity encoding is not clearly recognizable as ensuring acyclicity (reinforces concern about rule 3).

- Claim that specifying 10%–25% of ancestral constraints is reasonable is misleading—such amounts imply extensive prior knowledge and are atypical.

- Overall: technically sound and of specialist interest, but strengthen by fixing rule inconsistencies, clarifying theoretical statements, and adding a greedy-baseline comparison under oracle conditions.
