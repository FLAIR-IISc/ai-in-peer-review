- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break standard score-based learning efficiency.
- Core contribution: Adapts EC-tree/A* search to handle ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten the admissible heuristic.
- Technical novelty: Formal inference rules for projecting constraints and a MaxSAT reduction to infer a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints.
- Theoretical soundness: Pruning rules and projection methods are proven (Theorems 1–4).
- Empirical result: Method yields orders-of-magnitude speedups over a state-of-the-art ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on benchmark networks.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem—integrating expert background knowledge (ancestral constraints) into optimal structure learning, useful with limited data.
- Originality: Novel and elegant strategy of converting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones to empower an optimal learner’s heuristic.
- Quality and clarity: Rigorous methodology, solid comparisons, and a well-written, organized presentation.
- Weakness — MaxSAT preprocessing: No analysis of the computational cost of the MaxSAT step; it may become a bottleneck for large-variable problems.
- Weakness — ILP comparison: Lacks experiments in bounded parent-set settings where ILP methods are typically more competitive.
- Weakness — Generality: Focused only on ancestral constraints; more discussion needed on applicability to other global or non-decomposable constraint types.
