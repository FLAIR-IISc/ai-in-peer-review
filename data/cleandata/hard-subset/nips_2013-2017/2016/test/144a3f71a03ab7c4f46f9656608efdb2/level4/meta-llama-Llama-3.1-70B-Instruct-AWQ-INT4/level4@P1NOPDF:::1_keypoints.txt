- Paper tackles BN structure learning with ancestral constraints via branch-and-bound search over CPDAGs.  
- Nodes are CPDAGs; transitions pick a parent set for a variable not present in the current CPDAG.  
- Symmetry breaking in the search is implemented effectively.  
- Search uses a heuristic from the URLearning system, but heuristic details are omitted here and referenced to prior work.  
- Lemma 3 (supplement) is key for pruning under ancestral constraints; reviewer accepts it but finds the proof unclear.  
- Specific proof gap: the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs further explanation.  
- Authors use the term "projected constraints" for what is standardly called "implied constraints."  
- Ancestral constraints imply edge constraints that can be used for pruning.  
- Paper also infers ordering constraints, but the inference method is not properly explained.  
- The statement "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading—such an inference is not always entailed.  
- Likely intent is to add consistent ordering constraints opportunistically, not only entailed ones; authors aim to infer as many consistent constraints as possible.  
- Authors use a MAXSAT formulation to avoid inferring too many ordering constraints.  
- Experiments vary variables, data points, and ancestral constraints; the proposed system significantly outperforms GOBNILP.  
- However, since parent-set cardinality was unlimited and GOBNILP is known to struggle with many parent sets, it’s unclear if the gap is due to ancestral constraints handling or parent-set scaling.  
- The paper encodes ancestral constraints with a MAXSAT encoding from Cussens (2008) rather than the stronger ILP encoding from Cussens (2010); the MAXSAT encoding is a poor ILP formulation.  
- Reviewer recommends an ILP formulation using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables (omit E vars), transitivity on A, and linking constraints A(X,Y) ≥ Σ_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U) for a tighter relaxation.  
- Even with a sensible ILP comparison, reviewer believes the proposed system would still outperform GOBNILP, but the current comparison is unfair.  
- Suggests comparing to van Beek and Hoffmann’s method (not cited), which often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to ancestral constraints.
