- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal BN learning by translating them to ordering constraints and integrating with the EC tree framework; clear and well written but conceptually straightforward.  
- Problem is novel (no standard solution) but somewhat artificial; when ancestral relations are known, more general causal discovery accounting for latent confounders would be preferable, limiting impact.  
- Core approach (translate constraints to ordering constraints compatible with decomposable scores using a MaxSAT solver) is sound.  
- Constraint translation rules contain logical issues: Rule 3’s symmetric premise leads to an asymmetric (inconsistent) conclusion, and Rule 4 wrongly excludes mutual non-causation constraints.  
- The assumption that ancestral constraints are sparse (10–25%) is questionable—real-world constraints would likely encode much more structure, so actual search-space reduction may be minimal.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is misleading because Gobnilp wasn’t optimized for ancestral constraints; a more meaningful baseline would be a greedy method like GES in an oracle setting.  
- Minor issues to fix: clarify “standard oracle” vs “omniscient oracle”; rephrase “empower the oracle”; state optimization is minimization; Theorem 1 must assert the constraint set A is a subset of constraints satisfied by full DAG G; note Theorem 3’s edge implications are negligible under sparse constraints; clarify the “acyclic” rule.  
- Overall: technically sound and of interest to BN specialists, but practical relevance and impact are modest due to problem formulation and weak experimental baselines.
