This paper tackles the problem of identifying an optimal Bayesian network structure. The authors show how to integrate non-decomposable constraints—specifically ancestral constraints—into the structure-learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. Their empirical study against ILP-based approaches reports improvements by orders of magnitude in runtime. The method also frames the task as a MaxSAT instance with tailored constraints. While my familiarity with this specific approach is limited, the reported runtime comparisons are compelling, demonstrating order-of-magnitude speedups over competing methods.
