- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into ordering constraints and using an EC-tree based score search.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to convert ancestral constraints into a (maximal) set of ordering constraints, ensuring implied ancestral relations are explicitly handled.  
- Empirical results show significant performance gains versus a straightforwardly modified ILP-based Gobnilp baseline.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained, though the core idea is conceptually straightforward.  
- The problem and solution are novel, but the experimental setting is somewhat artificial and limits broader impact.  
- When unobserved confounders are present, more general causal discovery methods would likely be more appropriate than the proposed approach.  
- Some inference rules (lines 257–261) appear strange or incomplete (e.g., acyclicity rule 3’s asymmetric conclusion; rule 4 excluding mutual non-causation).  
- In practice users are likely to provide only a small number of ancestral constraints, which may not substantially reduce the search space.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is impressive but not ideal since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this task; benchmarking against greedy algorithms like GES with oracle input is suggested.  
- Theorem 1 needs a clearer statement: constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG P on the EC tree are only a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G (otherwise the iff is invalid).  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- The formulation claimed to capture acyclicity (line 267) is not immediately convincing and needs clarification.  
- Practical concern: 10–25% ancestral constraints represents a large amount of prior knowledge for reasonably sized graphs and is atypical in real applications.  
- Minor wording/clarity issues: distinguish “oracle” vs “omniscient oracle” (line 30), clarify “empower the oracle” (line 38), and state that “optimizing” means “minimizing” (line 90).
