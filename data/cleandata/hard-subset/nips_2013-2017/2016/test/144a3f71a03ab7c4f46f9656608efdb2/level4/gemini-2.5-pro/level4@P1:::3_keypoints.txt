- Paper proposes translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into a maximal set of decomposable ordering constraints to prune score-based BN structure search using the EC tree framework.  
- Empirical evaluation shows significant performance gains over a modified ILP baseline (Gobnilp) on the authors' tasks.  
- Strength: Addresses a novel, clearly defined problem gap in optimal BN learning under ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written, organized, and the method is conceptually straightforward.  
- Strength: Experimental results are compelling and demonstrate clear improvements over the chosen baseline.  
- Weakness: Problem setting is specialized; authors should better motivate when optimal BN scoring under ancestral constraints is preferred over causal discovery methods that handle confounding.  
- Weakness: Baseline choice—Gobnilp—was not designed for this task; comparing to practical greedy methods like GES would provide a more informative benchmark.  
- Weakness: Experimental constraint densities (10–25% of possible ancestral relations) are unrealistically large; performance with much sparser, more realistic constraints should be reported.  
- MaxSAT concern: Rule 3’s acyclicity premise is symmetric but its conclusion is asymmetric (includes Y ⇹ Z but not W ⇹ X); logic needs clarification.  
- MaxSAT concern: Rule 4 infers Y < X from X ⇹ Y, which seems to prevent expressing mutual non-ancestry (X and Y unrelated); clarify handling of "no causal relation" constraints.  
- MaxSAT concern: Theorem 3 may rarely infer positive edges (X -> Y) with sparse initial constraints; provide empirical data on how often this rule fires.  
- Suggestion: Replace vague phrases like "omniscient oracle" and "empower the oracle" with consistent, precise terminology (e.g., "oracle" or "passed to the oracle").  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state optimization direction (minimize score) when introducing the score function to avoid ambiguity.  
- Suggestion: Clarify Theorem 1’s conditions—specify how the constraints A on the CPDAG P relate to the full set A on the final DAG G (projection vs. full set).
