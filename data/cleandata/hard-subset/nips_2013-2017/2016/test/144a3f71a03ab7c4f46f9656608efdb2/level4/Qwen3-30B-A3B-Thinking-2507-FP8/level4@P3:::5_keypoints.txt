- Addresses Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints and extends Chen et al.'s framework to handle them.  
- Main contribution: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Provides soundness proofs for constraint projection (Theorems 1–4) and integrates ancestral constraints into the EC tree search.  
- First empirical evaluation comparing ILP/CP approaches (GOBNILP) to their projection-based method for ancestral constraints.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across standard BN benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2), dataset sizes, and constraint proportions.  
- Results show orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based methods, with gains increasing for larger problems.  
- Demonstrates that modest numbers of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can have effects on model quality comparable to much larger datasets.  
- Clear, well-organized exposition with precise definitions, illustrative examples, and effective figures (notably EC tree visualizations).  
- Strong originality: novel projection method filling a gap left by prior non-decomposable score frameworks and order-graph approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: enables incorporating domain causal knowledge efficiently, improving both speed and learned-model quality.  
- Strengths summarized: novel solution, solid theoretical foundation, comprehensive experiments, practical insights, and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints needs more discussion on computational complexity.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to constraint-based learning methods that also handle ancestral constraints (e.g., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos 2012).  
- Weakness: Evaluation emphasizes runtime; network quality comparisons beyond structural Hamming distance are limited.  
- Con-acceptance concerns: uncertain scalability beyond ~20 variables and potential overlap with existing constraint-based methods.  
- Pro-acceptance points: meaningful problem solved, significant efficiency gains, theoretically sound, well-validated, and clearly presented.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with practical significance; the paper should be accepted.
