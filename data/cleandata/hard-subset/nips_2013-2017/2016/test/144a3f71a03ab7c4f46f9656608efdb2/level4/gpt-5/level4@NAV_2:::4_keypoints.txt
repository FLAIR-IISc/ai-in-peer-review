- Main novelty: Section 5 introduces projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Paper is clearly written and explains theory and experiments well.  
- Concern 1: Table 3 shows projection improves EC-tree but not ILP, raising whether projection benefits only EC-trees and fails to generalize to other learning paradigms; need evidence it helps methods that cannot natively handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern for EC-tree as ancestor-constraint proportion increases; the cause and whether EC-tree performance is highly sensitive to constraint amount (requiring careful tuning) should be explained.  
- Concern 3: Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC-trees—compare SHD with the ILP approach.  
- Minor suggestion: Shorten or remove Section 4.1 on BN graphs and instead expand on EC trees and the use of A* in the EC-tree setting.
