- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using a CPDAG-based branch-and-bound search and A* with URLearning heuristics.  
- Branching chooses parent sets for undecided variables and updates CPDAGs; includes symmetry breaking and URLearning heuristics.  
- Main contributions: pruning rules exploiting ancestral constraints (Lemma 3), projected/implied edge constraints, and inferred ordering constraints via a MAXSAT procedure.  
- Empirical results report large speedups over GOBNILP on the tested settings.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem and integrates constraints into a principled A* framework with practical heuristics.  
- Strength: derives additional constraints from ancestry that substantially reduce search.  
- Lemma 3’s proof in the supplement is hard to follow; the EC tree edge-generation rules and the step showing Z -> W in Gk need precise restatement and step-by-step justification.  
- Terminology confusion: “projected constraints” should align with standard “implied constraints”; clearly distinguish entailed (sound) constraints from optional, consistent ordering constraints.  
- Unsound phrasing on ordering constraints: “infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” is incorrect as stated; clarify that ordering constraints are optional choices consistent with ancestral info, not entailments.  
- Explain the MAXSAT formulation used to select ordering constraints and the optimization criterion (e.g., maximize number or weight of consistent constraints).  
- CPDAG branching description is confusing—clarify that branching targets variables whose parent sets are not yet fixed by previous orientations.  
- Provide clear soundness/completeness claims and proofs/pointers that pruning rules do not eliminate optimal solutions and that URLearning remains admissible/consistent in the constrained setting.  
- Quantify pruning impact: report how many nodes/edges are pruned by each component (implied edges, ordering constraints, Lemma 3 pruning).  
- Explain interactions between ancestral constraints and CPDAG symmetry breaking and confirm no contradictions.  
- Experimental comparison to GOBNILP may be unfair: no parent-set cardinality bounds favor A* methods and inflate GOBNILP difficulty.  
- The GOBNILP encoding used is a MAXSAT-style formulation (Cussens 2008) which is weak for ILP solvers; a stronger ILP encoding would be preferable for fair comparison.  
- Recommend a tighter ILP encoding using binary ancestor variables A(X,Y), transitivity linearizations, and linking parent selection I(Y,U) to ancestry without cubic variables (cf. Cussens 2010).  
- Suggest comparing or discussing the constraint programming approach of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015) as a stronger baseline.  
- Experimental reporting should include parent-set statistics (avg/max per node), memory usage, timeouts, anytime bounds, and results varying parent-set caps to separate effects of ancestry vs parent-set explosion.  
- Minor fixes: unify terminology (implied/projected), define all supplement symbols at first use, and clarify URLearning version/settings used for heuristics.  
- Overall assessment: promising and well-motivated approach with strong empirical performance, but needs clearer proofs, terminology separation, and stronger/fairer baseline comparisons; reviewer leans positive pending these fixes.
