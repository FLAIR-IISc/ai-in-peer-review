- Paper proposes a branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs to learn Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints.  
- Algorithm refines CPDAGs via parent-set selection and uses symmetry-breaking plus URLearning as an admissible heuristic (details deferred to prior work).  
- Main contribution: deriving "projected/implied constraints" and "ordering constraints" from user-specified ancestral constraints to prune the search space.  
- Experimental claim: the proposed system is substantially faster than GOBNILP on tested benchmarks.  
- Major concern: the proof of Lemma 3 (key to pruning) is hard to follow and requires more detailed justification.  
- Major concern: the method for inferring ordering constraints is imprecisely described; it appears to impose a consistent ordering (via MAXSAT) rather than derive entailments, and the rationale/methodology needs formalization.  
- Major concern: experimental comparison may be unfair because A*-based methods inherently do better without a parent-set cardinality limit, confounding the source of speedup.  
- Major concern: the ILP/MAXSAT encoding used to give constraints to GOBNILP is suboptimal; stronger ILP formulations exist and should be used for a fair baseline.  
- Major concern: by comparing against a potentially weak GOBNILP formulation, the reported performance gap is less credible.  
- Missing related work: the paper omits citation/comparison with van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), a relevant constraint-programming competitor.  
- Minor suggestion: use standard terminology ("implied constraints" instead of "projected constraints") for clarity.  
- Conclusion: promising approach with encouraging results, but needs clearer proofs, a formal description of ordering inference, fairer/better baselines, and inclusion of related work before acceptance.
