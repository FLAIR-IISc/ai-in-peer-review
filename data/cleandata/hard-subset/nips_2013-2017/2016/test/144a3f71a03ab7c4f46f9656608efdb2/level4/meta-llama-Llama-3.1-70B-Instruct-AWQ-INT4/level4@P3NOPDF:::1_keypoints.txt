- Tackles Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints via branch-and-bound over CPDAG search nodes.  
- Search expands by selecting parent sets for variables absent from current CPDAG; symmetry breaking reduces search space.  
- Uses URLearning heuristic for A* search but paper omits integration details (referenced in earlier work).  
- Ancestral constraints allow pruning; Lemma 3 is central but its proof is unclear (specific edge-generation claim needs clarification).  
- Authors introduce implied/"projected" constraints and inferred "ordering constraints," but the inference procedure is not fully explained.  
- The statement "we can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y" is misleading—should state that Y < Z can be added when Z is not an ancestor of Y.  
- MAXSAT is used to infer a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints.  
- Experiments vary variables, datapoints, and constraints and show the proposed method is much faster than GOBNILP.  
- GOBNILP’s poor performance may be due to unrestricted parent-set cardinality rather than handling of ancestral constraints, leaving the cause ambiguous.  
- Supplementary material uses a MAXSAT encoding from Cussens (2008) instead of the ILP encoding from Cussens (2010), which is ill-suited for ILP solvers.  
- Reviewer proposes a better ILP formulation: include A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables, omit E(X,Y,Z), enforce transitivity on A and link A and I with A(X,Y) >= sum{U:X in U} I(Y,U).  
- The suggested ILP formulation gives a tighter relaxation and would likely improve performance, weakening the current GOBNILP comparison.  
- Recommend comparing to a sensible ILP formulation and to the van Beek & Hoffmann approach for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: promising approach but needs clearer proofs/explanations and stronger, fairer empirical comparisons.
