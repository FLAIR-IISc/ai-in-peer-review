- Paper tackles optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable forms (edge and topological ordering constraints).  
- Core contribution (Section 5) is a method to project ancestral constraints so decomposable-score-based algorithms can incorporate them.  
- Theoretical justification for the projection method appears sound and clearly explained.  
- Empirical results show substantial performance improvement for EC tree search when using projected constraints.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, with practical relevance for incorporating domain knowledge into BN learning.  
- Table 3 shows projection improves EC trees but not ILP, raising questions about generalizability beyond EC trees.  
- Reviewer recommends demonstrating the projection’s benefit on learning methods that originally couldn’t handle non-decomposable constraints to strengthen broad applicability.  
- Table 2 reveals an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as constraint proportion increases, suggesting sensitivity of search-space reduction to constraint amount.  
- Reviewer asks for analysis of why the easy-hard-easy pattern emerges and guidance on tuning the number of constraints.  
- Missing direct comparison of structural Hamming distance (accuracy) between EC trees and ILP in Table 2; reviewer requests this for completeness.  
- Experimental focus is entirely on EC trees; Section 4.1 (BN graph theory) may be extraneous and could be streamlined or removed.  
- Reviewer requests a deeper dive into EC tree implementation and A* interaction with projected constraints for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practical contribution worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
