- Paper tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge/order constraints for use with order-graph oracles.  
- Builds on BN-graph and EC-tree search spaces and supplies pruning in the EC-tree plus projection to tighten A* heuristics.  
- Proposes an algorithm to infer entailed positive/negative edge constraints from a closed set of ancestral constraints (Theorems 2–3).  
- Proposes a MaxSAT reduction to infer a large, cycle-free set of ordering constraints entailed by ancestral constraints, with sufficient conditions in Theorem 4.  
- Describes and justifies a pruning criterion for ancestral constraints within CPDAG nodes, with immediate accept/reject tests and a fallback to enumerating DAGs in the equivalence class (Theorem 1).  
- Empirical evaluation compares to ILP (GOBNILP) on standard benchmarks using synthetic data and constraints, showing orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements and accuracy gains from projection.  
- Shows that modest amounts of background knowledge can substitute for substantial increases in sample size (improved SHD).  
- Technical approach is principled and plausible, leveraging established search spaces and providing useful inference/pruning conditions.  
- Some aspects are underspecified: construction of the “maximum” closure of ancestral constraints is only sketched.  
- Fallback CPDAG enumeration may be expensive; frequency and mitigation strategies are not discussed.  
- MaxSAT formulation needs more detail on correctness, completeness, and computational cost.  
- Experimental setup is careful (same scores, precomputed local scores, ablations), but reporting variance, solver stats, and MaxSAT times would give deeper insight.  
- Clarity issues: typographical artifacts, undefined “maximum set of ancestral constraints,” and unclear “closed set” in Theorem 4.  
- Paper should include complexity discussion and practical checks (or citations) to avoid CPDAG enumeration.  
- Recommend adding pseudocode/procedural descriptions for the edge-inference algorithm and the MaxSAT encoding.  
- Novelty: projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints for EC-tree/oracle integration is original and interesting.  
- Significance: important problem with substantial reported speedups; demonstrated scale (~20 variables) is modest but in line with exact methods.  
- Pros: strong motivation, principled projection, sound pruning, effective oracle integration, and solid empirical gains.  
- Cons: limited scalability demonstration, need for practical bounds/efficiencies for CPDAG fallback, limited comparisons (only ILP), and unclear MaxSAT runtime impact.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity, inference-detail, and MaxSAT-cost concerns.
