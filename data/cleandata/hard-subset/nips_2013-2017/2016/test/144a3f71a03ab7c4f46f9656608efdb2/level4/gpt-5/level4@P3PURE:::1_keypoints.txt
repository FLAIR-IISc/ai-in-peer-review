- Paper addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound on CPDAGs, with symmetry breaking and an A* search heuristic from prior work.  
- Main contribution: additional pruning under ancestral constraints (Lemma 3), but the proof is hard to follow—especially the step claiming “By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W.”  
- Authors use the nonstandard term “projected constraints” instead of the standard “implied constraints.”  
- Paper infers ordering constraints from ancestral constraints, but the method is inadequately explained and imprecise (e.g., claiming “can infer Y < Z from Z not an ancestor of Y” without clarifying they add consistent but not entailed constraints).  
- The authors use a MAXSAT-based approach to infer many consistent ordering constraints (guided by MAXSAT), but this inference strategy needs clearer exposition and justification.  
- Experiments vary variables, datapoints, and ancestral constraints and show the proposed system much faster than GOBNILP, but this comparison is confounded because GOBNILP typically struggles without parent-set cardinality bounds.  
- The supplementary encoding of ancestral constraints is based on the MAXSAT encoding from Cussens (2008), which is a poor ILP formulation and likely weakens the comparison.  
- Recommend a better ILP formulation: add A(X,Y) ancestor binaries, omit E(X,Y,Z), enforce transitivity and link A and I via A(X,Y) ≥ ∑_{U: X∈U} I(Y,U) for a much tighter relaxation (akin to Cussens 2010 but for partial orders).  
- Author believes the proposed system would still outperform GOBNILP with a sensible ILP encoding, but the current benchmarking against a poor formulation weakens the claim.  
- Suggest comparing against van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and is not cited but could likely be adapted to support ancestral constraints.  
- References highlighted: Cussens (2010) and van Beek & Hoffmann (2015).
