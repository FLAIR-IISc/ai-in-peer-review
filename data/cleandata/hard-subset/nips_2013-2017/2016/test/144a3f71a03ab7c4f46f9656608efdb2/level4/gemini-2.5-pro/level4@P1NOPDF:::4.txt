This paper proposes a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints. This allows score-based structure learning algorithms, which rely on decomposable scores, to incorporate this form of domain knowledge to improve learning efficiency. The primary novelty lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce their method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into a set of decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. The paper is well-written, and the authors clearly present their contributions in both the theoretical and experimental sections.

I have the following concerns and suggestions:

i. Table 3 shows that the proposed projection of ancestral constraints improves the performance of EC trees but not the ILP approach. This raises the question of whether the projection method is specific to EC trees and has poor generalizability to other learning algorithms. Since the main contribution of this paper is the inference of decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones, the argument would be more convincing if the authors could demonstrate that incorporating these projected constraints improves the performance and efficiency of a learning approach that cannot originally handle non-decomposable constraints.

ii. In Table 2, the performance of the EC tree method exhibits an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. Could the authors offer an explanation for this phenomenon? Does this imply that the effect of search space reduction for EC trees is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge provided, especially when compared to the ILP approach? If so, this would suggest that the number of ancestral constraints must be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance.

iii. Table 2 only presents the Structural Hamming Distance results for the EC tree method. For a complete comparison, the corresponding results for the ILP approach should also be included.

iv. A final suggestion regarding the paper's structure: I understand that Bayesian Network (BN) graphs provide the theoretical foundation for EC trees. However, since the experiments focus exclusively on applying ancestral constraints within the EC tree search space, I suggest removing the general background on BN search in Section 4.1. The space could be used more effectively to provide a more detailed explanation of EC trees and the A* search algorithm as applied in that context.
