- Paper addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (reachability) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints.  
- Builds on EC-tree/CPDAG search (Chen et al.) and prunes equivalence classes incompatible with ancestral constraints.  
- Projects ancestral constraints into (i) edge presence/absence via entailment rules and (ii) topological order constraints via a MaxSAT formulation selecting a maximal consistent subset.  
- Empirical comparison to an ILP (GOBNILP) with linear ancestral encodings shows orders-of-magnitude runtime speedups, especially when projected constraints are supplied to the oracle.  
- Observes an easy-hard-easy pattern in search difficulty as the proportion of ancestral constraints varies.  
- Technical soundness: EC-tree pruning and constraint projection are well motivated and sound; edge-entailment rules are clear.  
- The MaxSAT encoding for order constraints is interesting but contains rules that appear asymmetric or insufficiently justified.  
- Empirical support is strong against the ILP baseline, and ablations on projected constraints are informative.  
- Baseline choice is limited: GOBNILP is not tailored for ancestral constraints and greedy/constraint-based baselines (e.g., GES, CP methods) are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written, but Theorem 1 and certain rules (lines 257–261) need clarification.  
- Originality: synthesis of EC-tree pruning, constraint projection, and MaxSAT order selection is novel and practically useful despite relying on known components.  
- Significance: method is relevant for settings with reachability/temporal knowledge and yields substantial speedups, but practical impact may be moderate since practitioners often use methods handling latent confounding (MAGs/PAGs).  
- Strength: principled projection of non-decomposable constraints and effective EC-tree pruning leading to strong empirical gains.  
- Weakness: experimental regimes often assume 10–25% pairwise ancestral relations, which may be unrealistically dense and reveal much of the structure.  
- Suggestion: report results with small and noisy constraint sets and quantify how many edge/order constraints are inferred.  
- MaxSAT rule concerns: rule 3’s asymmetry needs clarification/proof for acyclicity sufficiency; rule 4’s handling of mutual non-ancestry (negative relations) is unclear.  
- Theorem 3 likely yields few positive edge entailments when ancestral information is sparse—please quantify empirically and ablate edge-only vs order-only constraints.  
- Theorem 1 wording should state that a CPDAG P is retained iff at least one DAG in class(P) satisfies the ancestral constraints.  
- Scope limitation: paper does not address latent confounders or path constraints in MAGs; discuss whether framework can extend to those cases.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear problem formulation, sound pruning, clever constraint projection, and strong empirical speedups.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited baselines, unclear MaxSAT rule set, possibly overstated experimental assumptions, and limited discussion of latent confounding.  
- Minor editorial comments: clarify “omniscient oracle” terminology, rephrase “empower the oracle,” note score minimization, clarify acyclicity encoding intuition, and discuss typical availability of ancestral relations in practice.  
- Overall: solid, well-presented contribution with moderate novelty and compelling specialist utility; stronger baselines, MaxSAT clarifications, and sparse/noisy experiments would improve the paper.
