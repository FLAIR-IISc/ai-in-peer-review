- Main contribution: novel method (Section 5) to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to enable decomposable-score-based structure learning.  
- Paper quality: well-written with clear theoretical and experimental exposition.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows improvement only for EC trees, not ILP, raising questions about the method’s generalizability beyond EC trees.  
- Request: demonstrate that decomposable constraint incorporation improves performance/efficiency for a learner that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Observation: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern in EC tree performance as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; cause of this phenomenon is unexplained.  
- Concern: the EC tree benefit may be sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge, possibly requiring careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints.  
- Missing comparison: Table 2 reports SHD for EC trees only—need corresponding ILP SHD results for fair comparison.  
- Minor suggestion: remove Section 4.1 and instead expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm to better situate the proposed method.
