- Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal BN learning by translating them into maximal ordering constraints used with an EC-tree score-based algorithm; well written but conceptually straightforward.

- Novelty: addresses a new problem (no standard method for ancestral constraints) but the setting feels artificial since broader causal discovery methods that handle latent confounding are often more appropriate.

- Method: converts ancestral constraints into ordering constraints via a MaxSAT solver so they can be expressed as absent edges and used with decomposable scores.

- Experimental results: shows substantial improvements over a modified ILP-based Gobnilp, but Gobnilp is not designed for this task, so the baseline is weak.

- Baseline suggestion: compare to greedy algorithms like GES with oracle input (which should recover the same solution) to better quantify efficiency gains.

- Practical concern: in practice only a small number of ancestral constraints are expected, so search-space reduction may be limited.

- Rule correctness: some MaxSAT rules (lines 257–261) appear odd or incomplete—acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but an asymmetric conclusion.

- Rule 4 issue: rule 4 seems to incorrectly exclude ancestral constraints of the form “X and Y do not cause each other.”

- Theorem 1 needs clarification: must state that constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG P on the EC tree are a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG G; the current iff is unclear.

- Theorem 3 concern: unlikely to produce many positive edge implications when ancestral constraint sets are sparse.

- Encoding transparency: the encoding claimed to enforce acyclicity (line 267) is not easily recognizable and may be problematic.

- Realism of constraint rates: claiming 10%–25% of ancestral constraints is substantial prior knowledge and atypical for realistic problems.

- Minor wording/clarity issues: Line 30 unclear distinction between “oracle” and “omniscient” oracle; Line 38 “empower the oracle” phrasing unclear; Line 90 should state that “optimizing” means minimizing.
