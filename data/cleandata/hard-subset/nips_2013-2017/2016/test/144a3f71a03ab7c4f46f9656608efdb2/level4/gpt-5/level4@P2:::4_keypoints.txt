- Paper addresses optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by pruning EC-tree search and projecting constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints for a decomposable-score oracle.

- Main technical contributions: CPDAG pruning test (Theorem 1), edge entailment rules (Theorems 2–3), and MaxSAT-based sufficient conditions for entailed orderings (Theorem 4).

- Novel projection from ancestral constraints to decomposable edge and ordering constraints, using MaxSAT to feed existing decomposable-score learners.

- Theoretical results justify pruning and characterize what edge/order constraints can be inferred; edge entailment rules are intuitive and complete for the stated closure.

- Empirical results show large runtime speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP), especially for larger n, and analyze an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion varies.

- Strength: principled integration of non-decomposable knowledge into EC-tree search with sound pruning and an oracle-based heuristic.

- Strength: MaxSAT reduction for ordering projection is sensible and leverages strong solvers.

- Strength: Theoretical clarity on pruning and projection procedures and demonstrated empirical gains.

- Weakness: Unclear impact of projected constraints on non-EC-tree learners; projected constraints sometimes add overhead for ILP at small n.

- Weakness: Need experiments showing benefits of projection for other decomposable-score exact methods (DP over order graphs, A*).

- Weakness: The easy–hard–easy phenomenon lacks deeper explanation (pruning vs heuristic fragmentation) and guidance on robustness to noisy/partial/conflicting constraints.

- Weakness: Missing accuracy comparisons (e.g., SHD) for ILP to assess tradeoffs between speed and solution quality.

- Weakness: Need quantification of computational cost and completeness of projection—cost of computing the “maximum” closure and practical runtime of edge checks.

- Weakness: Clarify whether MaxSAT yields a maximal set of entailed orderings or only a maximal set under the sufficient-condition encoding, and any entailment gaps.

- Weakness: Scalability questions beyond n≈20 and with bounded parent sizes; request runtime breakdown among pruning, MaxSAT, and oracle calls.

- Weakness: Framework’s robustness to noisy/incorrect background knowledge and support for soft/weighted constraints is not addressed.

- Clarity: Define “maximum set of ancestral constraints” and specify the closure procedure and complexity earlier in the paper.

- Clarity: Clarify CPDAG pruning test complexity and when enumeration is required; separate hard vs soft clauses in the MaxSAT encoding and state global maximality for entailments.

- Originality: Projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints within EC-tree is novel and well motivated.

- Significance: Approach can materially reduce computation/data needs when domain knowledge is available and has potential impact beyond EC-tree learners.

- Pros for acceptance: solid theory, novel projection method, strong empirical speedups, and practical relevance.

- Cons against acceptance: limited evidence of generalization to other learners, missing ILP accuracy comparisons, unclear projection/MaxSAT overhead and scalability, and gaps on ordering maximality.

- Minor suggestion: shorten general BN graph background and expand on EC trees, A* setup, heuristic behavior under constraints, and MaxSAT encoding details in experiments.
