- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into maximal ordering constraints used with an EC-tree score-based algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to convert ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints, making implied ancestral constraints explicit.  
- Empirical results show substantial outperformance over a modified ILP-based Gobnilp in the evaluated settings.  
- Paper is well written and clearly explained, though conceptually straightforward.  
- Novelty is moderate (novel to specialists; no standard solution exists for optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints).  
- Impact is limited because the scenario is somewhat artificial—ancestral relations typically call for causal discovery methods handling unobserved confounders.  
- Some translation rules (e.g., rules 257–261) appear unusual or incomplete, with concerns about an asymmetric acyclicity rule and a rule that may exclude certain ancestral constraints.  
- In practice, only a few ancestral constraints are likely to be available, so the approach may not substantially reduce the search space (Theorem 3 may yield few positive edge implications).  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is not entirely fair since Gobnilp was not designed for this problem; a more meaningful baseline would be a greedy algorithm like GES under compatible constraints.  
- Minor revision suggestions: clarify distinction between standard and omniscient oracle; change “empower the oracle” phrasing; state that optimizing means minimizing; revise Theorem 1 to state A(P) ⊆ A(G); fix notation for “acyclic” (line 267); and acknowledge that 10–25% ancestral constraints implies unrealistically extensive knowledge of the target graph.
