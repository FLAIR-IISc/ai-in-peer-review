- Addresses incorporating ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.

- Core contribution: translate ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints enabling use of efficient decomposable-score oracles.

- Leverages the EC tree search space (Chen et al.) with an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores while respecting ancestral constraints.

- Builds on and clearly situates itself relative to ILP (GOBNILP) and constraint programming approaches and prior non-decomposable-score frameworks.

- Introduces constraint projection via MaxSAT to convert ancestral constraints into ordering constraints for practical use.

- Theoretical contributions include formal soundness and completeness results (Theorems 2–4) for the constraint projection method.

- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP-based methods and careful analysis of how constraints affect search.

- Demonstrates scalability to ~20 variables, comparable to state-of-the-art score-based methods for BDeu scores.

- Shows that even modest numbers of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can significantly improve learning accuracy or reduce required data.

- Practical significance: highly useful for applications where domain causal knowledge is available, despite not addressing unobserved confounders.

- Writing and organization are strong; technical details are reproducible, though some parts could use more intuitive explanation.

- Weakness: needs more discussion on realistic availability and prevalence of ancestral constraints in practice.

- Weakness: clarify ambiguous terminology such as "omniscient oracle" and "empowering the oracle."

- Weakness: clarify the relationship between CPDAG constraints (Theorem 1) and full DAG constraints to avoid confusion.

- Overall assessment: novel, well-executed contribution filling a literature gap; recommendation for acceptance (NIPS 2016).
