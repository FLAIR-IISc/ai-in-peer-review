This paper presents an efficient method for incorporating ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network (BN) learning. The core idea is to translate ancestral constraints into a (maximal) set of ordering constraints, which are then leveraged within a score-based learning algorithm utilizing the EC tree framework introduced by [Chen et al., 2015]. Empirical evaluation against a modified integer linear programming (ILP) approach (Gobnilp) demonstrates significant performance improvements. The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains a conceptually straightforward solution. 

The problem addressed – optimal BN learning subject to ancestral constraints – is interesting and relatively unexplored, lacking a standard solution (novelty: 3/5). However, the practical relevance is somewhat limited; when ancestral knowledge is available, causal discovery algorithms designed to handle unobserved confounders may be more appropriate. The approach centers on transforming non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints, facilitating compatibility with existing score-based methods. This transformation is achieved using a MaxSAT solver to explicitly represent all implied constraints. However, some of the rules described (lines 257-261) appear incomplete or potentially flawed. For example, the acyclicity rule’s symmetry isn't reflected in the conclusion, and another rule potentially excludes mutual non-causality. Furthermore, the practical impact hinges on the assumption that ancestral constraints sufficiently reduce the search space, which may not hold in scenarios with limited prior knowledge. The comparison against Gobnilp, while demonstrating performance gains, lacks a strong baseline. A more informative comparison would involve evaluating against a greedy algorithm (like GES) with access to the true ancestral constraints, offering insight into the efficiency gains achieved by the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important and under-explored problem:** Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints is a challenging task with practical relevance.
*   **Novel Approach:** The translation of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ordering constraints is a novel idea.
*   **Empirically Validated:** The approach demonstrates significant performance improvements over an ILP-based baseline.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The paper is well-written and explains the concepts and methodology clearly.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Practical Relevance:** The setting may be somewhat artificial; in many cases, causal discovery algorithms designed for hidden confounders might be more suitable when ancestral information is available.
*   **Questionable Rules:** Some of the rules used for constraint translation appear incomplete or potentially incorrect.
*   **Weak Baseline Comparison:** The comparison against Gobnilp is not entirely fair, as it is not optimized for this specific problem. A comparison with a greedy algorithm utilizing the ground truth constraints would be more insightful.
*   **Scalability Concerns:** The impact of ancestral constraints on search space reduction needs further investigation, particularly with sparse constraint sets. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a novel and important problem in Bayesian network learning.
*   The proposed approach is theoretically sound and empirically validated.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explained.
*   The results demonstrate a significant improvement in efficiency compared to existing methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The practical relevance of the problem is somewhat limited.
*   Some of the underlying rules used for constraint translation appear questionable.
*   The baseline comparison is not sufficiently strong.
*   The scalability of the approach with sparse constraints needs further investigation.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network learning. While the practical relevance of the problem is debatable and the baseline comparison could be stronger, the novelty of the approach and the empirical results warrant acceptance. The identified weaknesses are relatively minor and could be addressed in a revised version of the paper.

**Recommendations:**

*   Clarify the rationale for focusing on this specific problem setting and discuss the limitations of the approach.
*   Re-examine and potentially revise the rules used for constraint translation.
*   Include a comparison against a greedy algorithm that utilizes the ground truth ancestral constraints.
*   Investigate the scalability of the approach with varying degrees of constraint sparsity.
