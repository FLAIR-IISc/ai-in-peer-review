- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a challenging and important problem.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and infers decomposable constraints to empower existing structure-learning oracles.  
- Projects ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints, enabling integration with decomposable methods.  
- Formulates constraint inference as a MaxSAT problem to compute the projection.  
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods on benchmark datasets.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, varying network size, dataset size, and constraint percentage.  
- Clear motivation and strong theoretical framing; presentation of preliminaries is concise and accessible.  
- Thorough literature review and proper positioning relative to recent related work.  
- MaxSAT formulation is sensible and demonstrates technical depth.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of MaxSAT’s scalability and scenarios where projection may be computationally expensive.  
- Weakness: lacks a more intuitive explanation of why projected constraints yield large performance gains (pruning vs. oracle effectiveness).  
- Weakness: the connection and comparison to Borboudakis and Tsamardinos (2012, 2013) is brief and could be expanded.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, empirically strong, minor clarifications needed; reviewer recommends acceptance.
