- Paper's contribution: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints (novelty in Section 5) so decomposable score–based learners can use domain knowledge.

- Presentation: clearly written with solid theoretical development and experiments.

- Concern 1: Table 3 shows improvement for EC trees but not ILP—questions whether projection generalizes beyond EC trees and asks for evidence it helps at least one method that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern for EC-tree performance as ancestral-constraint proportion increases—asks why this occurs and whether EC-tree benefits are highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge (requiring tuning).

- Concern 3: Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees—requests SHD comparisons with the ILP approach under the same conditions.

- Suggestion: Drop or shorten Section 4.1 on BN graph and use the space to better explain EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree setting, since experiments focus on EC-tree search space.
