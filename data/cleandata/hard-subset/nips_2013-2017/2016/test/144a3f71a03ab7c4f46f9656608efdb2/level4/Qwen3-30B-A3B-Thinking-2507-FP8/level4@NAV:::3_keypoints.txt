- Contribution: Presents an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network learning by translating them into a maximal set of ordering constraints integrated into the EC tree framework.  
- Implementation detail: Uses a MaxSAT solver to enforce all implied ancestral constraints via edge absence encoding.  
- Empirical result: Evaluation vs a modified ILP-based Gobnilp shows significant performance improvements in the tested setting.  
- Writing and novelty: Paper is well-written and clear; solution is conceptually straightforward but addresses a novel gap (novelty=3).  
- Impact concern: Practical impact limited (impact=2) because ancestral constraints are an artificial restriction compared to causal discovery methods that handle unobserved confounders.  
- Key technical insight: Effectively maps non-decomposable ancestral constraints into ordering constraints usable by score-based algorithms.  
- Theoretical issue: Rule 3 appears problematic—premise is symmetric but conclusion (Y ↛ Z) is asymmetric and may not follow.  
- Theoretical issue: Rule 4 wrongly excludes ancestral constraints expressing mutual non-causation between X and Y.  
- Practical limitation: Sparse ancestral constraints are unlikely to substantially reduce the search space or yield many positive edge implications.  
- Baseline choice: Comparison against Gobnilp is unfair because Gobnilp isn’t designed for this problem; stronger benchmark would compare to greedy algorithms (e.g., GES) under oracle input.  
- Suggested evaluation: Compare runtime/efficiency to a greedy method with oracle constraints, which should produce the same output as the optimal method for clearer efficiency assessment.  
- Minor correction: Line 30—clarify distinction between “oracle” and “omniscient oracle.”  
- Minor wording: Line 38—“Empower the oracle” should mean “use in combination with the oracle.”  
- Clarification: Line 90—state that “optimizing” refers to minimization.  
- Theorem clarification: Line 164—Theorem 1 must state that the constraint set A satisfied by CPDAG P on the EC tree is a subset of that satisfied by the full DAG G.  
- Theorem consequence: Lines 225–227—Theorem 3 likely produces few positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.  
- Clarity issue: Line 267—the stated constraint is unclear as capturing acyclicity.  
- Practical realism: Line 310—claiming 10%–25% ancestral constraints implies substantial target-graph knowledge and is unrealistic for typical applications.
