This paper proposes a method for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and thus challenging for traditional structure learning approaches. The authors leverage a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, which can accommodate ancestral constraints. They demonstrate how to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, enabling the use of an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including theoretical foundations and experimental evaluations.

The main contribution of this paper lies in Section 5, where the authors introduce a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. This allows the incorporation of domain knowledge into decomposable-score-based structure learning approaches, improving learning efficiency. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach, showing orders-of-magnitude improvements in efficiency compared to alternative frameworks, such as those based on integer linear programming (ILP).

The authors evaluate their approach using simulated structure learning problems from standard Bayesian network benchmarks, varying the number of variables, training dataset size, and proportion of ancestral constraints. The results show that their approach is consistently faster than the ILP-based system, GOBNILP, and that the projection of constraints has a significant impact on the efficiency of learning.

However, there are some concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the results suggest that projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints may only be effective for the EC tree approach, and not for other learning methods, such as ILP. It would be more convincing to demonstrate the generalizability of this approach to other learning methods. Secondly, the results show an easy-hard-easy pattern in the performance of the EC tree approach as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. It is unclear why this pattern occurs, and whether it implies that the number of ancestral constraints needs to be carefully tuned to achieve optimal performance. Finally, the comparison between the EC tree approach and ILP is limited to the structural Hamming distance, and it would be useful to include additional metrics, such as computational time or model accuracy.

Overall, this paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, and demonstrates its effectiveness through experimental evaluations. However, further work is needed to address the concerns and suggestions mentioned above, and to fully explore the potential of this approach.
