- Proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints to enable decomposable-score-based structure learning.  
- Main novelty is Section 5: projecting ancestral constraints onto edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical and experimental exposition.  
- Table 3: projection improves EC trees but not ILP—raises concern about limited generalization to other learning approaches and asks for evidence that decomposable constraints help methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Table 2: EC trees show an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion increases—asks for explanation and whether EC tree performance is highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge (implying need for careful tuning).  
- Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees—requests a comparison with ILP.  
- Minor suggestion: remove Section 4.1 and use the space to better explain EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree context.
