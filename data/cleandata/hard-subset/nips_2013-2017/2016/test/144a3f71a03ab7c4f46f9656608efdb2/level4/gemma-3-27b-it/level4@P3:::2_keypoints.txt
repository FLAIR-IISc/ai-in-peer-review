- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints and the challenge they pose for standard decomposable-score methods.
- Core idea: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to enable use of existing decomposable-score learning oracles.
- Builds on prior framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores and offers a practical mechanism to leverage decomposition.
- Theoretical contribution: formal justification of constraint projection (Theorems 1–4) that supports the method’s soundness.
- Clear positioning within related work (e.g., Darwiche, Koller & Friedman, ILP and constraint programming approaches) and useful technical preliminaries.
- Introduces and leverages BN graphs and EC trees to implement the projection approach in a comprehensible way.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: experiments across benchmark networks, varying data sizes and constraint levels.
- Demonstrates consistent orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over GOBNILP, showing strong practical benefits.
- Analysis includes effects on EC tree search and ILP performance, easy–hard–easy behavior with constraint percentages, and structural Hamming distance insights.
- Presentation issues: experimental comparison setup (Tables 1 and 3) is unclear—specifically what “new work” denotes in GOBNILP comparisons.
- Suggests directly comparing EC tree with and without constraint projection to isolate the projection’s impact.
- Recommends more detailed analysis identifying scenarios where gains are most pronounced.
- Calls for expanded explanation of the MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) for readers unfamiliar with it.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of projection idea, significant speedups, technical soundness, and strong empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation of experimental results and limited discussion of MaxSAT’s role.
- Overall: strong paper with minor revisions needed for clarity; recommendation to accept.
