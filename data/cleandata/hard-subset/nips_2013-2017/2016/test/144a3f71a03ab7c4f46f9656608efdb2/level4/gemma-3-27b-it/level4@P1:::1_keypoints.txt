- Paper addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints via branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, adding parent sets, using symmetry breaking and the URLearning heuristic.

- Lemma 3 is central but its proof is hard to follow; the claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z → W" needs clearer justification.

- The paper uses implied (termed “projected”) constraints inferred from ancestral constraints for additional pruning, but renaming standard terminology is confusing.

- Ordering-constraint inference is under-explained and potentially misleading: “Z not an ancestor of Y” does not logically entail Y < Z, rather one can choose to add consistent ordering constraints; MaxSAT is used to avoid overconstraining.

- Experiments vary number of variables, data points, and ancestral constraints and report the proposed method is significantly faster than GOBNILP.

- The GOBNILP comparison is inconclusive because with no limit on parent set cardinality GOBNILP is known to underperform, so the speed gap may reflect parent-set handling rather than handling of ancestral constraints.

- The ancestral-constraint encoding used for GOBNILP is a MAXSAT-based encoding (Cussens 2008) that is suboptimal for ILP systems; a better ILP formulation would introduce binary ancestor variables A(X,Y) and linking/transitivity constraints to avoid cubic E variables.

- A tighter ILP with A(X,Y) and linking constraints would give a stronger linear relaxation and likely improve GOBNILP’s performance, so comparing to a suboptimal encoding weakens the empirical claim.

- The paper omits comparison to van Beek and Hoffmann (2015), a relevant prior method that often outperforms GOBNILP and should be considered.
