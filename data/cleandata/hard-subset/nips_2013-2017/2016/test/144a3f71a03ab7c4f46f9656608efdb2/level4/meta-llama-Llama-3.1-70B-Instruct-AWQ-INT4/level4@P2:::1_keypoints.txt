- Tackles optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral (non-decomposable) background constraints.  
- Builds on recent optimal-structure framework for non-decomposable scores and shows it can accommodate ancestral constraints.  
- Key technical idea: prune the EC search tree using ancestral constraints to reduce search space.  
- Shows how to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints to empower decomposable-score oracles.  
- Experimental claim: method is orders-of-magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially for many variables.  
- Well-motivated problem with a thorough literature review and clear exposition.  
- Technical contribution is sound and the paper is well-written and organized.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough but has limitations in baseline selection and encoding choices.  
- Unclear whether GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints or lack of parent-set cardinality limits.  
- Authors used Cussens 2008 MAXSAT encoding for ancestral constraints, which may be suboptimal.  
- A stronger comparison using a better ILP formulation (e.g., Cussens 2010) or other baselines (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann’s CP approach) is needed.  
- Novelty: presents a new combination of techniques for learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Significance: approach could substantially improve structure-learning efficiency under ancestral constraints.  
- Acceptance arguments: relevant problem, sound technical contribution, thorough experiments; Rejection arguments: experimental comparisons and encoding choices need improvement.
