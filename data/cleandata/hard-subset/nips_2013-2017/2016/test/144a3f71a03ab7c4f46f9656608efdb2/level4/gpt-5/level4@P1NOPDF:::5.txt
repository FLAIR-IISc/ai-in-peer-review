This paper addresses the problem of finding an optimal Bayesian network structure. The authors show how to incorporate non-decomposable constraints—particularly ancestral constraints—into the structure-learning framework of Chen et al. (2015, 2016). They reformulate the task as a MaxSAT problem with carefully designed constraints. 

The empirical evaluation compares the proposed approach against ILP-based methods and reports orders-of-magnitude improvements in runtime. While my expertise in this area is limited, the runtime results appear quite impressive. Specifically, the authors demonstrate runtime reductions by orders of magnitude relative to alternative methods.
