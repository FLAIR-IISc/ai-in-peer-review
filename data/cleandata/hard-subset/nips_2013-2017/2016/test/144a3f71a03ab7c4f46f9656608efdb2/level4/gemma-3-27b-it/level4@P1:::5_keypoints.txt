- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable constraints, focusing on ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) to leverage decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints to empower an oracle.  
- Prunes the EC tree search space using inferred constraints; BN graph and EC tree explanations are clear and easy to follow.  
- Presents key theorems (1, 3, 4) providing theoretical justification for the methodology.  
- Proposes projection of constraints by inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Transforms the constraint projection problem into a MaxSAT formulation to find a maximal set of ordering constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation against ILP-based approaches shows orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Uses standard Bayesian network benchmarks and varies n, N, p in experiments, demonstrating robust testing.  
- Discusses impact of ancestral constraints on learning efficiency and model accuracy.  
- Clear problem motivation and thorough literature grounding (Darwiche, Koller & Friedman, Murphy).  
- Well-organized, well-written presentation with helpful technical preliminaries (decomposable scores, K2, ILP).  
- Strength: novel method for incorporating non-decomposable constraints into EC tree search.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and solid theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability beyond n = 20; large-network behavior not fully explored.  
- Weakness: dependence on MaxSAT solver could become a bottleneck; more discussion of MaxSAT instance characteristics and optimizations needed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, technically sound and empirically validated — recommend acceptance.
