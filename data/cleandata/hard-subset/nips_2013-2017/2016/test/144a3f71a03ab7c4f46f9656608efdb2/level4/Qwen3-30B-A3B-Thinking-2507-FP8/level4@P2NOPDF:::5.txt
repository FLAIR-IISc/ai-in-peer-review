

This paper tackles the challenge of learning Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable constraints, specifically ancestral constraints, by extending Chen et al.'s [2015, 2016] structure learning framework. The authors formulate the problem as a MaxSAT optimization task with carefully designed constraints, enabling efficient incorporation of these complex requirements. Their empirical evaluation demonstrates that this approach achieves runtime reductions of multiple orders of magnitude compared to existing ILP-based methods, significantly outperforming alternatives in scalability. While my expertise in MaxSAT-based optimization is limited, the presented runtime comparisons are compelling and well-supported by the experimental results. The method's ability to handle non-decomposable constraints—typically a computational bottleneck—without sacrificing efficiency represents a meaningful advancement for practical Bayesian network learning. The paper clearly articulates the problem reformulation, provides thorough experimental validation across diverse datasets, and establishes a strong baseline for future work. The results suggest that MaxSAT offers a promising alternative to ILP for constrained structure learning, with potential implications for large-scale applications where runtime efficiency is critical. The contribution is both technically sound and practically relevant, addressing a well-known limitation in the literature.
