- Addresses learning Bayesian network structure with ancestral background knowledge by projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones for use with oracle-based search.
- Builds on EC tree framework and prior work (Chen et al.) to enable efficient handling of ancestral constraints.
- Well-motivated literature positioning, citing key works (Darwiche; Koller & Friedman; Murphy) and ILP-related approaches (Jaakkola et al., Bartlett & Cussens).
- Core theoretical contribution: projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable constraints, supported by Theorems 1–4.
- Experimental results show substantial performance gains over ILP-based GOBNILP, especially when using projected constraints.
- Scaling experiments indicate the method can handle networks with a reasonable number of variables, comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Empirical finding: even a small percentage of ancestral constraints can improve model accuracy.
- Presentation of experimental results is somewhat convoluted and needs clearer separation of effects (projection vs. EC baseline).
- Recommend clearer comparison of (EC + projection) directly against EC to isolate the contribution of projection.
- Request clarification on the choice of BDeu score and the equivalent sample size used in experiments.
- Dependence on a specific MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) raises concerns about reproducibility and generalizability.
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, empirical validation, practical relevance, and novelty of the projection idea.
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental clarity issues and solver dependence.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve experimental clarity and address solver-dependence concerns.
