This work addresses the problem of learning an optimal Bayesian network structure. The paper's main contribution is extending the structure learning framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] to incorporate non-decomposable constraints, with a specific focus on ancestral constraints. The empirical evaluation against Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods reveals that the proposed approach yields improvements of several orders of magnitude. To achieve this, the authors reformulate the problem as a Maximum Satisfiability (MaxSAT) problem with a specialized set of constraints. Although my familiarity with this specific methodology is not extensive, the results from the runtime comparison are highly compelling. Specifically, the paper reports a runtime reduction of several orders of magnitude relative to competing approaches.
