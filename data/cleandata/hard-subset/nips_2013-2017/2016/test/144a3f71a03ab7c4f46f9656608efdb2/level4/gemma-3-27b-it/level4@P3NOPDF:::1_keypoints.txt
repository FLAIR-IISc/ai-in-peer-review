- Addresses learning Bayesian networks from local scores under ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound over CPDAGs.  
- Search expands by selecting a parent set for a variable absent from the current CPDAG, with symmetry breaking and a URLearning-derived heuristic.  
- Leveraging ancestral constraints enables pruning of the search tree, improving efficiency.  
- Core theoretical result is Lemma 3 (supplement), which appears correct but has an unclear proof step about EC tree edge generation.  
- Treatment of implied constraints and ordering constraints is insufficiently detailed and potentially misleading.  
- Claim that "Z not an ancestor of Y implies Y < Z" is overstated; non-ancestry permits but does not entail an ordering.  
- Authors mitigate over-constraining by using MAXSAT to infer only consistent ordering constraints.  
- Experiments vary variables, sample size, and constraints and show much faster performance than GOBNILP.  
- Unclear whether GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints or the unlimited parent-set cardinality used in experiments.  
- The MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints is likely suboptimal and introduces unnecessary cubic E(X,Y,Z) variables.  
- A tighter ILP-style encoding using binary A(X,Y) ancestor variables plus transitivity and linking constraints would be more efficient.  
- Even with a better ILP encoding, the authors’ system would likely still outperform GOBNILP, but comparing to a weak encoding weakens the claim.  
- The paper omits comparison to or citation of van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), a more competitive constraint-programming approach that should be included.  
- Overall: promising approach with speed gains, but needs clearer theoretical exposition, a stronger/alternative encoding of ancestral constraints, and comparisons to stronger baselines.
