- Novelty: Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints (Section 5), enabling integration with decomposable-score-based structure learning.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and clearly explains theory and experiments.  
- Concern 1 (Table 3): Projection improves EC-tree performance but not ILP, raising doubts about whether the approach generalizes beyond EC trees or benefits learners that cannot handle original non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern 2 (Table 2): EC-tree shows an unexplained easy-hard-easy pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases, suggesting EC-tree search may be highly sensitive to amount of domain knowledge and may require careful tuning.  
- Concern 3 (metrics): Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees — authors should report the same metric for ILP to enable a fair comparison.  
- Concern 4 (experiments/structure): Experiments focus solely on EC-tree search space; recommend removing Section 4.1 and expanding the description of EC trees and the A* algorithm within the EC-tree setting.
