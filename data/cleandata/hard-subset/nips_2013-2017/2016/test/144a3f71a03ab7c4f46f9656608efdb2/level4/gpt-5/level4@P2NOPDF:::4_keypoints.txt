- Paper proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints, enabling score-decomposable learners to use ancestral knowledge.  
- Main novelty is Section 5: the projection to edge and topological order constraints.  
- Writing, theory, and experiments are clear and well presented.  
- Concern: empirical gains appear limited to EC-tree; projection may not benefit other paradigms like ILP or standard score-based search.  
- Suggestion: show improvements for at least one prominent method that cannot natively encode ancestral constraints, and explain why ILP fails to benefit.  
- Observation: EC-tree shows an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion increases; mechanism behind this non-monotonicity is unclear.  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to constraint quantity, consistency, and strength, and include controlled ablations or guidance for selecting/validating constraints.  
- Missing comparison: SHD for ILP under the same settings (with and without projected constraints) should be reported for direct accuracy comparison.  
- Presentation suggestion: streamline or remove Section 4.1 and instead provide a clearer EC-tree exposition, A* search details, and how projected constraints affect branching/pruning and heuristic tightness.  
- Overall: idea is interesting and well-motivated; addressing generality and the easy–hard–easy behavior would strengthen the contribution.
