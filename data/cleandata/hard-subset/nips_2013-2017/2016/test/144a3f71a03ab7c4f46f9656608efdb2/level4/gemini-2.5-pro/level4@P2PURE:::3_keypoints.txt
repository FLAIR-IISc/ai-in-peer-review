- Proposes translating ancestral constraints into a maximal set of ordering constraints and integrating them into an EC-tree score-based BN learning algorithm.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to derive the maximal implied ordering constraints compatible with decomposable scores.  
- Empirical results show significant performance improvements over a modified ILP-based Gobnilp in this specific setting.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained; conceptually straightforward and of interest to specialists despite modest results.  
- Practical relevance is debatable: ancestral-constraint scenarios often imply causal discovery settings that may require handling hidden confounders, which this method does not address.  
- Translation is necessary because ancestral constraints are hard to integrate with decomposable scores, while ordering constraints (absence of edges) are compatible.  
- MaxSAT translation rules are questionable: acyclicity rule 3 is asymmetric despite a symmetric premise, and rule 4 may exclude common constraint types (e.g., mutual non-ancestry).  
- The acyclicity encoding (line ~267) and the MaxSAT rules are not intuitive and need clearer justification.  
- Sparse ancestral constraints (common in practice) likely provide little pruning, limiting practical benefit.  
- Baseline choice is problematic: Gobnilp was not designed for this problem, so the comparison may be uninformative.  
- Recommends comparing against greedy methods like GES as a more meaningful efficiency benchmark when constraints are correct.  
- Theorem 1 needs a more precise statement clarifying that constraints A satisfied by the CPDAG on the EC tree should be a subset of those satisfied by the full DAG.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many positive edge implications in practical (sparse-constraint) scenarios.  
- Experimental setup using 10%–25% ancestral constraints is unrealistic for reasonably sized graphs and implies extensive prior knowledge.  
- Minor clarity/editorial issues: clarify "omniscient oracle" (line 30), rephrase "empower the oracle" (line 38), and specify that "optimizing" the score means "minimizing" (line 90).
