- Presents a novel method for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using a framework for non-decomposable scores.  
- Exploits existing oracles for decomposable-score structure learning by converting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints.  
- Introduces use of EC trees and describes how to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Provides a thorough literature review and clear exposition of the problem and limitations of mainstream decomposable-score approaches.  
- Theoretical analysis supports the technical soundness of the method.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and compares the method to an ILP-based system, GOBNILP.  
- Results show the proposed approach is orders-of-magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially as variable count increases.  
- Demonstrates that projecting constraints significantly improves learning efficiency.  
- Lacks a direct experimental comparison to the baseline EC tree approach, which would isolate the new method’s contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses effective figures/tables; references are comprehensive.  
- Scores well on quality, clarity, and originality per NIPS criteria.  
- Significance could be strengthened by more practical examples or case studies illustrating real-world benefits.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and promising, with minor experimental and application-focused improvements suggested.
