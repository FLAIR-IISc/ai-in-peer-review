- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s non-decomposable score framework and leverages the EC-tree search space to prune DAG search while maintaining soundness.  
- Main novelty: algorithms to project ancestral constraints into decomposable forms so standard structure-learning methods can incorporate them.  
- Strong theoretical foundations with well-justified theorems (Theorems 1–4) proving soundness of the approach.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) showing orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) and scaling to ~20 variables.  
- Clear presentation and examples; constraint-projection algorithms are well-motivated though Section 4.1 (BN graphs) is somewhat redundant.  
- Original extension of prior work on constraint incorporation to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Practically significant: 10–25% ancestral constraints can yield accuracy gains comparable to large increases in training data.  
- Concern: Claim that projection “empowers” the oracle is weakly supported—projection helps EC trees but has minimal impact on ILP; needs demonstration on other frameworks (e.g., A* with order graphs).  
- Concern: The easy–hard–easy runtime pattern as constraint proportion increases is unexplained and should be analyzed and compared to ILP behavior.  
- Concern: Missing structural Hamming distance (SHD) comparison between EC trees and ILP in Table 2 to validate accuracy parity.  
- Suggestion: Move BN-graph material to the introduction and expand EC-tree and A* algorithm details in the main text.  
- Overall: Strong, novel, and practically important work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing generalizability, easy–hard–easy explanation, SHD comparison, and section reorganization.
