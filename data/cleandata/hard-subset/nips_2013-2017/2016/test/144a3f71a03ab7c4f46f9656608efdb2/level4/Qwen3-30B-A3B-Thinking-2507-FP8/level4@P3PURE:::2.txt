

The authors present a method that leverages a given set of ancestral constraints to inform the admissible heuristic for an exhaustive search algorithm. The experimental comparisons, however, are unclear. Specifically, Table 1 compares (EC + new work) against (GOB + new work), while Table 3 appears to compare EC against GOB directly—this distinction is ambiguous and requires clarification. An explicit comparison between (EC + new work) and EC alone would be more meaningful, as EC consistently outperforms GOB by a significant margin. Even if the new work yields a larger relative improvement for GOB compared to EC, the absolute performance of (EC + new work) would remain superior to (GOB + new work), making the latter comparison less critical for evaluating the method's contribution.
