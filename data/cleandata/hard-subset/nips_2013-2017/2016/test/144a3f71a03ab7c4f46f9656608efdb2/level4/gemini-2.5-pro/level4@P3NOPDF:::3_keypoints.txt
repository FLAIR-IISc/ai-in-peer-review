- Presents a method to convert ancestral constraints into maximal ordering constraints and integrate them into score-based BN learning using the EC-tree, improving efficiency over an adapted Gobnilp baseline.
- Well-written paper with a clear, conceptually clean MaxSAT-based translation approach and strong empirical results for the studied setting.
- Novel problem formulation (optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints) of interest to specialists.
- Practicality concern: the setting may be artificial and limited by the Bayesian network assumption of causal sufficiency versus more general causal discovery needs (e.g., unobserved confounders).
- MaxSAT formulation lacks clarity: several rules (lines 257–261) are incomplete or confusing and need clearer exposition.
- Rule 3 (acyclicity) is asymmetric and unintuitive—authors should clarify the logic behind concluding `Y -/-> Z` from `(X < Y) and (Z < W)`.
- Rule 4 appears to exclude certain constraints (e.g., mutual non-causation between X and Y) and needs explanation.
- The acyclicity encoding (line 267) is not a standard representation and requires further justification or clarification.
- Effectiveness with sparse constraints is questionable: small expert-provided constraint sets may not sufficiently prune search space; Theorem 3 may rarely apply.
- Experimental setup may be optimistic by using 10%–25% of all possible ancestral constraints, which is unlikely in practice.
- Baseline choice: comparing only to an adapted Gobnilp (not designed for this task) is limiting; comparison to greedy methods like GES would give a more informative benchmark.
- Minor issues and clarifications needed: wording (L30, L38), explicitly state score optimization direction (L90), and tighten Theorem 1’s statement regarding constraint subsets (L164).
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and of specialist interest, but practical impact and experimental design limitations reduce broader significance.
