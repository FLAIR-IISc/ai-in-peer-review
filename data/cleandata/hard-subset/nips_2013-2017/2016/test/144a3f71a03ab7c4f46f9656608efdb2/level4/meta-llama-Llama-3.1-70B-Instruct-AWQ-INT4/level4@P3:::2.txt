The authors present a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. They leverage a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning based on non-decomposable scores, which can accommodate ancestral constraints. The proposed approach exploits oracles for learning structures using decomposable scores, which cannot handle ancestral constraints directly. Instead, the authors show how to empower these oracles by passing them decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints.

The paper provides a thorough review of the problem of Bayesian network structure learning and the challenges posed by ancestral constraints. The authors discuss the limitations of mainstream approaches, which assume decomposable scoring functions, and how these approaches can be modified to accommodate ancestral constraints. They also provide a detailed explanation of their proposed approach, including the use of EC trees and the inference of decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing the proposed approach to an ILP-based system, GOBNILP. The results show that the proposed approach is orders-of-magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially when the number of variables increases. The authors also evaluate the impact of projecting constraints on the efficiency of learning and show that it has a significant impact on the proposed approach.

However, the experiments could be improved by including a direct comparison between the proposed approach and the baseline EC tree approach. This would help to isolate the contribution of the new method and provide a clearer understanding of its benefits.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of figures and tables is effective in illustrating the key concepts and results. The references are comprehensive, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work.

In terms of the NIPS review criteria, the paper scores well on quality, clarity, and originality. The approach is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical and easy to follow. The approach is novel and addresses an important problem in Bayesian network structure learning.

However, the significance of the paper could be improved by providing more insights into the practical applications of the proposed approach. While the authors mention that ancestral constraints are important in practice, they could provide more examples or case studies to illustrate the benefits of their approach.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the approach is novel and technically sound. With some minor improvements to the experiments and a clearer discussion of the practical applications, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning.
