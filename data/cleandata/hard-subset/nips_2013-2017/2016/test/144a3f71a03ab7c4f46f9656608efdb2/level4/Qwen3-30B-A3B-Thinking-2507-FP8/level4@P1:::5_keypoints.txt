- Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s framework that uses decomposable-score oracles to search EC tree space.  
- Proposes a method to convert ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Provides algorithms for inferring decomposable constraints and proves soundness and completeness (Theorems 2–4).  
- Uses a MaxSAT formulation to encode ordering constraints for the projection step.  
- Empirically shows orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based GOBNILP, especially as variable count increases.  
- Demonstrates scalability to ~20 variables and significant runtime advantages on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, etc.).  
- Shows ancestral constraints improve learning accuracy (structural Hamming distance), with small constraint sets comparable to much larger datasets.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper with accessible technical preliminaries and EC tree exposition.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior literature (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos, Cussens) and extends Chen et al.'s methods.  
- Novel contribution: systematic projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms.  
- Practical significance: makes ancestral-constraint-informed optimal learning tractable for real problems.  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion on how approach relates to constraint-based methods (e.g., PC algorithm).  
- Minor weakness: MaxSAT ordering formulation is technically sound but could use more intuitive explanation for non-experts.  
- Minor weakness: paper does not fully explore trade-offs between constraint-projection complexity and runtime gains.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-quality contribution recommended for acceptance.
