- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Builds on a framework that leverages decomposable-score oracles by projecting ancestral constraints into equivalent decomposable constraints.  
- Core contribution: demonstrating effective use of existing oracles with projected constraints, yielding large efficiency gains over ILP approaches.  
- Well-structured paper with clear problem statement, approach, and experimental results.  
- Good technical preliminaries covering decomposable scores and relevant algorithms (e.g., K2).  
- Clear description of BN graphs, EC trees, and method for incorporating constraints.  
- Detailed constraint-projection method, including use of MaxSAT for ordering constraints.  
- Experimental setup shows substantial performance improvements (orders of magnitude) over GOBNILP.  
- Use of standard benchmarks and direct comparison to a relevant baseline strengthens empirical validity.  
- Analysis of the impact of constraint projection is a valuable component.  
- Strength: comprehensive approach to handling ancestral constraints and making oracles more efficient.  
- Weakness: lacks an intuitive explanation of why constraint projection so significantly improves oracle efficiency.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT reduction discussion is brief; complexity and potential bottlenecks need more detail.  
- Weakness: choice of BDeu score is mentioned without discussion of its suitability or limitations.  
- Weakness: selection and representativeness of sub-networks from benchmarks are not well justified.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies on structural Hamming distance and could be strengthened with additional accuracy metrics.  
- Comment: the paper’s statement on scalability to n=20 variables seems understated relative to prior work limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel, technically sound work with minor issues; recommended for acceptance.
