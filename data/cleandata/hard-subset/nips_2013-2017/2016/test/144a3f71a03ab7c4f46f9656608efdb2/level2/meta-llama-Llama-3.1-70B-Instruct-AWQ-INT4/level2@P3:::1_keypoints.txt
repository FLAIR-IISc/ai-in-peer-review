- Proposes learning Bayesian networks optimally under non-decomposable ancestral constraints using an EC tree search space plus an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores, with EC-tree pruning and inferred decomposable constraints.
- Builds on prior work (Chen et al. 2015/2016) and draws from constraint-based methods (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos) and ILP approaches (Jaakkola, Cussens).
- Claims orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvement over ILP-based systems like GOBNILP for ancestral-constraint problems.
- Demonstrates scalability up to ~20 variables, comparable to modern score-based approaches.
- Shows that incorporating ancestral constraints can improve model accuracy, especially with limited training data.
- Still requires significant computational resources for larger problem sizes despite being faster than ILP.
- Method is specialized to ancestral constraints and may not generalize to other constraint types or learning problems.
- Depends on availability and efficiency of an oracle for decomposable-score optimization, which may not always be practical.
- Presents original contributions: the EC tree search space and empowering the oracle via inferred decomposable constraints.
- Paper is well-written, technically thorough, and backed by a comprehensive experimental evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful approach with strong empirical support and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability and potential computational concerns at larger scales.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
