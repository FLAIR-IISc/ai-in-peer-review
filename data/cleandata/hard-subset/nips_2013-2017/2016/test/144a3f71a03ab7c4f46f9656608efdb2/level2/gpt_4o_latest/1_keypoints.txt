- Tackles optimal Bayesian network learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints using the EC tree framework.  
- Proposes pruning the EC tree and inferring decomposable constraints to strengthen the oracle for structure learning.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based methods (e.g., GOBNILP), especially on larger networks/datasets.  
- Novel contribution: extends EC tree framework to handle ancestral constraints and infers decomposable constraints.  
- Strong empirical validation across benchmarks, dataset sizes, network sizes, and varying proportions of ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides formal theorems and proofs for pruning and constraint inference, ensuring correctness.  
- Practical significance: shows background ancestral knowledge can substantially improve learning efficiency and accuracy.  
- Clarity issues: paper is dense and hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with EC tree or BN structure learning.  
- Limited comparative analysis: lacks benchmarks against non-ILP heuristics or dynamic programming methods.  
- Scalability concerns: demonstrated up to ~20 variables; performance for much larger networks (50+) is unclear.  
- Reproducibility gaps: insufficient implementation details, parameter settings, and dataset specification; code/pseudocode not provided.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, gives significant theoretical and empirical contributions, and achieves major efficiency improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs improved clarity/accessibility and broader evaluation against other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to improve clarity and add additional comparative analysis and reproducibility details.
