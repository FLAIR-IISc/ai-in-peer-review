This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and cannot be handled by mainstream approaches that rely on decomposable scores. The authors propose a framework that leverages a search space called the EC tree, which is designed for learning with non-decomposable scores. They also introduce a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and ordering constraints, which can be used to empower an oracle during structure search.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the technical concepts and a well-organized structure. The authors provide a thorough review of the related work and clearly position their contribution within the existing literature.

The technical content of the paper is sound, and the authors provide a rigorous treatment of the problem. The proposed framework is based on a solid theoretical foundation, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the EC tree and how it can be used for learning with ancestral constraints. The method for projecting non-decomposable constraints onto decomposable ones is also well-motivated and clearly explained.

The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, with a clear description of the setup and the results. The authors compare their approach to a state-of-the-art ILP-based system and show that their approach is orders-of-magnitude faster for most of the evaluated scenarios.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning and provides a novel approach to handling non-decomposable constraints. The technical content is sound, and the experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed.
