- Introduces a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Leverages the EC tree search space and an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores.
- Proposes inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints to “empower” the oracle.
- Main technical contribution: a sound and complete method to prune the EC tree using ancestral constraints.
- Additional contribution: a procedure for deriving decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Experimental results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based learning systems.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically well-presented with thorough related-work discussion.
- Strengths: tackles a challenging, under-addressed problem with solid theoretical grounding.
- Strengths: experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing.
- Limitation: lacks discussion of scalability/behavior when the number of variables is very large.
- Limitation: needs more implementation details about the oracle and how decomposable constraints are applied.
- Limitation: would benefit from additional comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., constraint programming).
- Overall recommendation: accept; reviewer score 8/10.
