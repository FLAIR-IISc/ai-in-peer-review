This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a practically relevant scenario where prior knowledge about causal relationships exists. The authors build upon a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores [Chen et al., 2015], which is key as ancestral constraints are inherently non-decomposable. The core idea is to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, allowing them to leverage oracles designed for decomposable scores. The paper provides a theoretical justification and empirical evaluation demonstrating significant efficiency gains compared to ILP-based approaches.

**Summary of strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important and practical problem:** Incorporating background knowledge into Bayesian network learning is crucial for many real-world applications. The focus on ancestral constraints is well-motivated.
*   **Sound technical approach:** The framework of leveraging oracles for decomposable scores, combined with the projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones, is a clever and well-reasoned approach. The theoretical basis, including theorems 1-4, lends credibility to the method.
*   **Strong empirical results:** The empirical evaluation consistently demonstrates significant speedups compared to ILP-based methods, particularly as the network size increases. The comparison to GOBNILP is compelling. The analysis of how the projected constraints impact efficiency is valuable.
*   **Clear exposition:** Despite the technical complexity, the paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and outlines the contributions.
*   **Thoroughness:** The inclusion of MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints is a thoughtful addition, demonstrating a comprehensive approach to the problem.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited discussion of related work on constraint-based methods:** The paper mentions Borboudakis and Tsamardinos [2012, 2013] briefly, but doesn't fully contextualize the contribution within the broader field of constraint-based structure learning. A more detailed comparison to methods like PC algorithm with path constraints would be valuable. It would be useful to understand why the proposed score-based approach with constraint projection is preferable.
*   **Complexity of MaxSAT implementation:** The dependence on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) introduces a potential bottleneck and reliance on external software.  While the authors acknowledge this, further discussion on the practical implications of the MaxSAT solver’s performance would be helpful. What happens if the MaxSAT solver fails to find a solution or takes an excessive amount of time?
*   **Limited analysis of structural Hamming distance:** The analysis of the structural Hamming distance (∆) is somewhat cursory. While the authors observe that a small number of ancestral constraints can have a similar impact to increasing data size, a more in-depth analysis of the trade-off between constraints and data would strengthen the results. The connection between the quality of inferred constraints and resulting model accuracy needs clarification.
*   **Oracle dependency:** The performance heavily relies on the oracle’s efficiency. While they use URLEARNING, the paper doesn't sufficiently discuss how different oracle choices might impact the overall performance. Is the approach sensitive to the choice of oracle?
*   **Scalability beyond n=20:** While the results show scalability up to n=20, it is not clear if the approach can scale to much larger networks. A discussion of potential bottlenecks and future work towards scaling up the method would be valuable.



**Overall assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network learning. The proposed approach is technically sound, well-motivated, and empirically validated. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and the paper is likely to have a positive impact on the field.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important practical problem.
*   Presents a novel and technically sound approach.
*   Demonstrates significant performance gains compared to existing methods.
*   Clear and well-written exposition.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of related constraint-based methods.
*   Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver.
*   Scalability to very large networks is not fully explored.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.** The authors should address the weaknesses by providing a more comprehensive discussion of related work, elaborating on the practical considerations of the MaxSAT solver, and conducting a more detailed analysis of the structural Hamming distance. Addressing the oracle dependency and scalability concerns would further strengthen the paper.
