**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper presents an approach for learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. Ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, which poses a challenge for learning approaches based on decomposable scores. The authors propose a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree, and employ an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. They also introduce a method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints and show how to empower the oracle by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon recent work on optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable scores [Chen et al., 2015, 2016]. The authors also draw inspiration from previous work on constraint-based learning methods [Borboudakis and Tsamardinos, 2012, 2013] and integer linear programming (ILP) approaches for structure learning [Jaakkola et al., 2010, Cussens, 2008].

2. **Scalability**: The approach scales to larger problem sizes, with results reported up to 20 variables, comparable to the scalability of modern score-based approaches.

**Weaknesses**

2. **Limited applicability**: The approach is specifically designed for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints and may not be directly applicable to other types of constraints or learning problems.
3. **Dependence on oracle**: The approach relies on an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores, which may not always be available or efficient.
