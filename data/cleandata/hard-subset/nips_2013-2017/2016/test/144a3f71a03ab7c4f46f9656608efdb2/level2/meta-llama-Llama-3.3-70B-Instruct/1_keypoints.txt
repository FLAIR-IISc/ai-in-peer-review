- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral constraints.  
- Leverages a non-decomposable-score framework that can accommodate ancestral constraints.  
- Transforms ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to empower decomposable-score oracles.  
- Claims the approach efficiently learns networks with ancestral constraints.  
- Claims it outperforms ILP-based methods, showing orders-of-magnitude speedups.  
- Empirical evaluations on standard benchmarks demonstrate efficiency and scalability.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem statement emphasizing practical importance of ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: sound and complete EC-tree pruning method based on ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: efficient algorithm for inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Weakness: assumes ancestral constraints are given, without discussing how to obtain them in practice.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation limited to a specific benchmark set and may not generalize.  
- Weakness: lacks more detailed comparisons with other approaches such as constraint programming (CP).  
- Overall assessment: significant and useful contribution with strong empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
