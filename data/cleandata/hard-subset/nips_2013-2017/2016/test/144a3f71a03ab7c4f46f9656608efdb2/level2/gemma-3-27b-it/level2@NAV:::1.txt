## Review of “Learning Bayesian Networks with Ancestral Constraints”

This paper tackles the problem of learning Bayesian networks when provided with ancestral constraints, a common scenario in applications where domain knowledge exists. The core issue addressed is that standard optimal structure learning techniques often rely on decomposable scores, which are incompatible with the non-decomposable nature of ancestral constraints. The authors build upon their previous work utilizing non-decomposable scores and an oracle-based approach, proposing a method to infer decomposable constraints from the ancestral ones, effectively enabling the oracle to be used. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing approaches and the practical relevance of incorporating background knowledge. The proposed solution – projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable forms – is a clever approach to bridge the gap between the constraints and existing learning algorithms. The theoretical framework is presented concisely and convincingly, with theorems establishing the soundness of the projection process. 

The empirical evaluation provides strong evidence of the method’s effectiveness. The comparison against an ILP-based approach demonstrates significant speedups, especially as the network size increases, and the ablation study highlighting the benefit of constraint projection is particularly valuable. The experiments demonstrate that adding ancestral constraints can improve accuracy comparable to increasing the dataset size. The scaling to 20 variables is a good result given the computational complexity of the problem.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the constraint projection technique. While the authors demonstrate its effectiveness, a more thorough analysis of scenarios where projection might be less successful (e.g., highly complex constraint sets) would strengthen the paper. Additionally, the choice of BDeu score is mentioned, but a brief discussion of the impact of different scoring functions on the performance would be insightful. Finally, while the code is not required for a conference submission, mentioning its availability would increase reproducibility. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to handling non-decomposable constraints in Bayesian network learning.
*   Strong theoretical grounding with clear theorems.
*   Empirically validated with substantial speedups compared to ILP.
*   Demonstrates the value of incorporating background knowledge.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the limitations of the constraint projection technique.
*   Lack of experimentation with different scoring functions.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper presenting a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network learning. The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. Despite some minor weaknesses, the potential impact of this work justifies acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations discussed above.
