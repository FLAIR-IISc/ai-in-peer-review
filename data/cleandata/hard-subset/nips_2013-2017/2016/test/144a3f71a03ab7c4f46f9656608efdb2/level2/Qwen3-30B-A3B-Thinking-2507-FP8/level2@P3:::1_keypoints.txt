- Problem: tackles Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints incompatible with standard decomposable-score methods.  
- Contribution 1: introduces a pruning mechanism for the EC tree that leverages ancestral constraints.  
- Contribution 2: presents a projection technique converting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides formal theorems proving the pruning approach and constraint projection are correct and preserve the solution space.  
- Practical transformation: shows ancestral constraints can be efficiently transformed into decomposable constraints enabling use of existing oracles.  
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive experiments varying number of variables, dataset size, and constraint proportion across multiple benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2).  
- Empirical results: outperforms ILP-based methods by orders of magnitude, with efficiency gains growing with problem size and scales to 20 variables.  
- Impact on learning: ancestral constraints can improve learning accuracy comparably to large increases in data (e.g., 10–25% constraints ~ increase from 512 to 8192 samples).  
- Quality and clarity: rigorous theoretical development, clear writing, good notation and illustrations, and logical organization relating to prior work.  
- Originality: novel constraint projection approach and first demonstration of practical inefficiency of ILP for these constraints compared to the proposed framework.  
- Significance: meaningful advancement that is compatible with existing oracles and has practical utility for incorporating background causal knowledge.  
- Limitations: lacks comparison to constraint-based approaches and could give more detail on the MaxSAT implementation (EVASOLVER cited).
