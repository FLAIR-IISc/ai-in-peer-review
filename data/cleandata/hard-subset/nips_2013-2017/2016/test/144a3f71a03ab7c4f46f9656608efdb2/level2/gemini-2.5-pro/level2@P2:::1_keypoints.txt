- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (ancestor / non-ancestor relations).
- Non-decomposable constraints break standard decomposable-score algorithms, creating the main technical challenge.
- Builds on EC-tree A* search framework that needs a decomposable-score oracle for heuristics.
- Introduces pruning of the EC tree directly using ancestral constraints to reduce search space.
- Proposes a projection technique that converts ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (edge presence/absence and ordering).
- Uses MaxSAT to compute a large, consistent set of ordering constraints for the projection.
- Passing projected constraints to the oracle yields a much tighter A* heuristic and dramatic speedups.
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP and scales to networks up to 20 variables.
- Experiments demonstrate that a small amount of background knowledge can substantially improve learning accuracy.
- Technical contributions are sound, well-motivated, and supported by correct theorems and proofs for edge constraint projection.
- Writing and presentation are clear, with effective exposition from EC-tree foundations to main methods.
- The MaxSAT formulation is clever but its third hard constraint needs more intuition to justify acyclicity enforcement.
- The preprocessing assumption that ancestral constraints are closed under inference should be referenced or detailed in an appendix.
- Suggests an ablation study separating edge vs. ordering projection effects to better understand their individual impacts.
- Minor presentation fix: align the Δ symbol for structural Hamming distance in Table 3 caption and table.
- Work is original and significant for graphical-models practitioners, enabling practical exact learning with ancestral knowledge.
- Potential criticism: perceived as an extension of Chen et al. (2016), but the extension is substantial and non-trivial.
