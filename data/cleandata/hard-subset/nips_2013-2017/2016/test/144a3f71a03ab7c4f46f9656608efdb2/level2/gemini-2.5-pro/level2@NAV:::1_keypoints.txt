- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Builds on the EC-tree A* search framework guided by a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Introduces pruning of the EC-tree using ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes a novel technique to infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- The inferred constraints improve the oracle heuristic, yielding much more efficient search.  
- The approach is backed by theorems formalizing the constraint inference.  
- Empirical evaluation vs. ILP shows orders-of-magnitude performance gains, with a convincing ablation study.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the base framework and extensions.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational cost and scalability of the constraint-projection step (reduction to MaxSAT).  
- Weakness: unclear performance and scalability beyond ~20 variables and across different graph densities/types of ancestral constraints.  
- Overall: high-quality, novel, practically important work with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
