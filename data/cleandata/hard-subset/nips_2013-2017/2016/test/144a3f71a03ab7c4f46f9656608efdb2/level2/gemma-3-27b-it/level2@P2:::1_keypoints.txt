- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a practically important setting.  
- Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones to leverage existing oracles.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015) framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Theorems 1–4) supporting the projection approach.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical speedups over ILP-based methods, especially for larger networks.  
- Compares favorably to GOBNILP and analyzes how projected constraints affect efficiency.  
- Includes a MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints, adding methodological completeness.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly motivated, and organized despite technical complexity.  
- Limited discussion/comparison with broader constraint-based methods (e.g., PC algorithm variants).  
- Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) raises practical bottleneck and failure-mode concerns.  
- Structural Hamming distance analysis is brief; needs deeper study of constraint vs. data trade-offs and accuracy impact.  
- Performance heavily depends on the chosen oracle (e.g., URLEARNING); sensitivity to oracle choice is underexplored.  
- Scalability beyond n=20 is unclear; potential bottlenecks and scaling strategies should be discussed.  
- Overall positive evaluation: novel, sound, empirically strong; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
