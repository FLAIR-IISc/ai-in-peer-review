- Presents a novel method to learn Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by inferring decomposable constraints for existing oracles.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework to enable optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores.  
- Key technical contribution: a sound and complete projection of ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and ordering constraints, improving search efficiency.  
- Argues ancestral constraints are practically important because they encode causal relations and reduce search space.  
- Notes most existing methods cannot handle ancestral constraints due to non-decomposability; ILP could in principle but lacked empirical evaluation for this use.  
- Theoretical strength: clear theorems (1–4) establishing soundness of the constraint projection approach.  
- Experimental strength: thorough comparisons to GOBNILP across multiple benchmarks, dataset sizes, and constraint percentages.  
- Empirical result: projected constraints yield orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains (see Table 1).  
- Practical finding: a small fraction (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can have similar impact on model accuracy as much larger datasets.  
- Weakness: scalability claims limited—experiments only up to n=20 variables; lacks comparison to methods scaling to larger networks (e.g., 26 variables).  
- Weakness: claim that ILP/CP approaches were not evaluated is imprecise given Cussens (2008) used ancestral relations for acyclicity; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding for ordering constraints is treated as a black box; paper should detail its computational complexity and scaling.  
- Weakness: paper does not fully explain why decomposable scores are pervasive; should briefly discuss their computational advantages.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons with recent state-of-the-art learners for larger networks to bolster scalability claims.  
- Suggestion: clarify relationship to Cussens (2008) and whether that work evaluated learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Suggestion: analyze complexity of the constraint projection step and when ancestral constraints are likely available in real-world applications.  
- Overall judgment: strong, novel, and well-executed contribution with sound theory and convincing empirical gains; recommended for acceptance.
