- Proposes a novel framework for Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints within Chen et al.'s EC tree search space.  
- Claims efficient incorporation of ancestral constraints that yields orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods.  
- Provides strong theoretical foundation: algorithms/theorems (2–4) for projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Introduces a well-motivated and sound EC tree pruning mechanism.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to GOBNILP across benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) and varying sizes, showing consistent large speed improvements, growing with problem size (e.g., n=20).  
- Shows practical impact: 10–25% ancestral constraints can have effects similar to quadrupling training data (Table 3).  
- Original and fills a gap: handles non-decomposable ancestral constraints that most structure-learning methods cannot, distinct from prior ILP/CP approaches.  
- High quality and clarity: rigorous theoretical claims, careful experiments, and clear exposition of EC tree and constraint projection methods.  
- Significant contribution: makes optimal structure learning with background knowledge practically feasible for larger problems.  
- Minor limitation: lacks detailed exploration of trade-offs when ancestral constraints are sparse or inconsistent and more discussion of constraint quality vs. quantity (and ILP overhead for small n) would strengthen the paper.
