- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints.  
- Core idea: infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones to enable efficient oracle-based structure learning.  
- Builds on prior optimal structure learning work and formalizes the projection with theorems (1–4).  
- Well-structured and clearly written, with clear motivation and connections to prior work (Chen et al.).  
- Empirical evaluation shows substantial speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP, especially as variable count grows.  
- Experiments use benchmark datasets, vary relevant parameters, and analyze the impact of constraint projection.  
- Finding: a small number of ancestral constraints can improve model accuracy more than additional training data.  
- Weakness: intuition for why projection prunes the search space so effectively is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints is discussed briefly and lacks encoding/scalability details.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations and scalability of the EVASOLVER MaxSAT solver as problem size increases.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion of ILP limitations beyond computational cost and when ILP may struggle structurally.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss scalability bottlenecks beyond 20 variables and possible future improvements.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical speedups, solid theoretical foundation, valuable insight on background knowledge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient intuition for projection, brief MaxSAT treatment, incomplete discussion of ILP and scalability limits.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions to address intuition, MaxSAT details, and scalability discussion.
