- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by pruning the EC-tree and projecting constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints usable by standard oracles.
- Presents simple edge-inference tests (Theorems 2–3) and a MaxSAT formulation to infer a large sound set of ordering constraints (Theorem 4).
- Empirically outperforms ILP (GOBNILP) by orders of magnitude on synthetic benchmarks and shows projected constraints greatly tighten search and improve accuracy with more data/constraints.
- Extends prior exact learning (order-graph DP/A*) limitations by incorporating ancestral/path constraints within the EC-tree framework.
- Novelty: projection of ancestral constraints to decomposable constraints and MaxSAT-based extraction of safe orderings.
- Strength: clear integration of non-decomposable ancestral constraints with sound pruning on CPDAG nodes in the EC-tree.
- Strength: practical and effective MaxSAT approach to derive ordering constraints that boost standard oracles.
- Strength: thorough empirical ablations showing projected constraints are critical and revealing easy–hard–easy constraint-density trends.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 is stated unclearly/counterintuitively and needs precise restatement and proof or intuition.
- Weakness: Fallback exhaustive enumeration of class(P) for CPDAG testing can be exponential; need empirical frequency and cost analysis or alternative SAT/ILP/Meek-rule checks.
- Weakness: Ancestral closure details are omitted—specify closure rules, completeness, complexity, and whether closure inference is iterative to a fixpoint.
- Weakness: Theorems 2–3 require formal proofs, complexity bounds, and clarity on whether tests are applied iteratively with feedback.
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding uses a nonstandard acyclicity constraint (3); justify/cite it, define “closed” order sets, ensure transitive closure and clarify whether inferred orderings are maximal or conservative.
- Weakness: Evaluation fairness concerns—report GOBNILP version, parameters (e.g., parent bounds), and results with typical in-degree limits; provide runtime breakdowns and counts of oracle calls/CPDAG enumerations.
- Weakness: Lacks experiments on robustness to noisy/incorrect ancestral constraints and handling of soft constraints or penalties.
- Scope limitation: experiments up to n=20; discuss scaling under common in-degree restrictions.
- Quality: Generally sound and motivated, but theoretical statements and CPDAG enumeration bottleneck need clarification and quantification.
- Clarity: Mostly clear with helpful examples, but several formal definitions and statements (Theorem 1, closure rules, MaxSAT encoding) require precision.
- Originality: Novel combination of EC-tree pruning with projection to decomposable oracles and MaxSAT-based ordering inference.
- Significance: Enabling exact BN learning with practically relevant non-decomposable knowledge is significant and shows real practitioner impact.
- Acceptance pro: fills an important gap, strong empirical gains, and broadly useful projection technique.
- Acceptance con: under-specified theoretical/algorithmic details, potential worst-case exponential step, and limited robustness/CP comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
