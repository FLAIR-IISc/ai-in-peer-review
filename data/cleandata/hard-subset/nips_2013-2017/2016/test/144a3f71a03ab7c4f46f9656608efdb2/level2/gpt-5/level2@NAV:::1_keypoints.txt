- Problem: Optimal BN structure learning with ancestral (path) constraints that are non-decomposable and incompatible with decomposable-score learners.  
- Method: Prune the EC-tree using CPDAG-based tests to enforce ancestral constraints.  
- Method: Project non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones via closed-form edge inference rules (Theorems 2–3).  
- Method: Extract a maximal acyclic set of ordering constraints with a MaxSAT formulation (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical result: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks.  
- Empirical result: Projection of constraints yields clear benefit; experiments include multiple networks, dataset sizes, and constraint densities, and report SHD.  
- Technical quality: Core theory (CPDAG pruning criterion, projection rules) is sound and well-motivated.  
- Omitted details: Closure/inference rule specifics and proofs are omitted and should be provided (e.g., in supplementary material).  
- Complexity concern: Enumerating class(P) when local tests are inconclusive can be exponential; no practical safeguards or heuristics are discussed.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-organized with good prior-work connections; citation formatting needs cleanup.  
- Originality: Novel combination of EC-tree pruning, projection to edge/order constraints, and MaxSAT reduction relative to ILP/CP and prior path-belief work.  
- Significance: Practically valuable for optimal BN learning with hard causal knowledge; observed easy–hard–easy constraint trend is insightful.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: Principled integration of non-decomposable constraints, strong empirical gains, and relevance to domains with causal priors.  
- Anti-acceptance arguments: Missing proofs/algorithmic details, limited baselines (no CP comparison), no study of noisy/incorrect constraints, and scalability shown only up to ~20 nodes.  
- Suggestions: Add proofs and full closure-rule specifications in supplement.  
- Suggestions: Analyze worst-case and practical complexity of CPDAG enumeration and add heuristics or bounds.  
- Suggestions: Evaluate robustness to incorrect/partial constraints and to other scores/ESS choices.  
- Suggestions: Compare to CP approaches and modern GOBNILP settings; break down contributions of edge vs ordering projections.  
- Suggestions: Release code and statistics on projected constraints.
