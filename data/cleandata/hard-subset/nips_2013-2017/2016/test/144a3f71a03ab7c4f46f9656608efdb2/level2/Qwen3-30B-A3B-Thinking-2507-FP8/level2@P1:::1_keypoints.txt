- Addresses incorporating ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning, a practically important problem.  
- Notes mainstream decomposable-score methods cannot directly handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) framework that uses a decomposable-score oracle for non-decomposable scores.  
- Key contribution: method to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints so the oracle can handle them efficiently.  
- Formalizes pruning of the EC tree search space using ancestral constraints with a sound and complete approach.  
- Projects ancestral constraints into edge constraints (efficient algorithms) and ordering constraints (MaxSAT formulation).  
- Provides formal theoretical support (Theorems 2–4) establishing correctness of the approach.  
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods like GOBNILP across benchmarks and sizes.  
- Experiments include runtime and model-quality metrics (structural Hamming distance), demonstrating practical benefits.  
- Insight: a small fraction of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match the effect of substantially more training data on accuracy.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with helpful figures and well-cited related work.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation and its implementation details could be clarified.  
- Weakness: unclear how often exhaustive enumeration of class(P) is needed and how that scales with CPDAG size.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, empirically convincing, significant advance over existing ILP approaches; recommended for acceptance.
