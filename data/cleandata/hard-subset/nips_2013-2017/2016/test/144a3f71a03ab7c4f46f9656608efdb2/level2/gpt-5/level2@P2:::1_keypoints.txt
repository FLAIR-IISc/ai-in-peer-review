- Tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral (non-decomposable) positive/negative constraints.  
- Enforces ancestral constraints in the EC-tree by pruning CPDAG nodes that cannot extend to any satisfying DAG.  
- Projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge constraints and topological ordering constraints to empower a decomposable-score oracle (URLearning).  
- Theorems 2–3 give necessary-and-sufficient conditions for inferring negative/positive edges from ancestral constraints; negative-edge rule is especially strong.  
- Ordering projection uses a MaxSAT formulation to extract a safe (conservative) set of entailed order constraints; Theorem 4 supplies sufficient conditions.  
- Empirically often achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP, particularly when using projected constraints.  
- Well positioned relative to prior exact, ILP/CP, and constraint-based work; combining EC-tree and projection appears novel.  
- EC-tree pruning strategy is sound (Theorem 1) under the presented expansion policy.  
- Edge-projection criteria are plausible, tight, and practically useful.  
- Many proofs and the closure rules for generating a “maximum set” of ancestral constraints are omitted.  
- CPDAG-node ancestral-satisfaction test uses two quick tests plus exhaustive enumeration of class(P) when inconclusive; worst-case enumeration can be exponential and is unanalyzed.  
- Experiments compare fairly to an ILP baseline and show clear benefits and informative accuracy analyses; scalability demonstrated up to ~20 variables.  
- Paper is generally clear but defers technical details (proofs, closure procedure, enumeration complexity, MaxSAT encoding specifics).  
- Original contributions: integrating ancestral constraints into EC-tree search, edge-projection with tight criteria, and MaxSAT-based ordering extraction.  
- Significance: ancestral background knowledge is common in causal discovery; projection idea is general and impactful despite scale limits.  
- Pros: clear motivation, theoretically supported pruning, effective projection methods, and strong empirical results.  
- Cons: missing proofs/closure algorithm, potentially expensive CPDAG enumeration without mitigation, MaxSAT only gives sufficient guarantees and runtime not reported.  
- Additional cons: evaluation limited to ILP baseline, only BDeu (ESS=1), limited to ~20 variables, and no study of noisy/inconsistent constraints.  
- Suggested improvements: add formal proofs (appendix), analyze/enforce bounds or caching for enumeration, report MaxSAT runtime and ablation of edge vs ordering impacts.  
- Further suggestions: test robustness to imperfect/contradictory constraints, try other scores/parent limits, include a CP baseline, and clarify URLearning integration details.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated, technically sound contribution with a compelling and empirically effective projection mechanism; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity and missing-detail issues.
