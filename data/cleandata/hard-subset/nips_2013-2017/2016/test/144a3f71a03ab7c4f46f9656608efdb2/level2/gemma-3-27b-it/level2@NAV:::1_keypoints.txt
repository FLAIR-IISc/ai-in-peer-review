- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, where standard decomposable-score methods fail due to non-decomposability.
- Proposes inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral ones to enable oracle-based optimal structure learning.
- Builds on prior non-decomposable score/oracle approach and introduces a projection technique to bridge the gap.
- Well-motivated and practically relevant for incorporating domain background knowledge.
- Theoretical framework includes theorems proving the soundness of the constraint projection process.
- Empirical evaluation shows significant speedups over an ILP-based approach, especially as network size grows.
- Ablation study demonstrates the benefit of constraint projection and shows ancestral constraints can boost accuracy comparably to more data.
- Scales experiments up to 20 variables, a reasonable achievement given computational complexity.
- Weakness: limited discussion and analysis of scenarios where constraint projection may fail or perform poorly (e.g., very complex constraint sets).
- Weakness: experiments use only the BDeu score; impact of other scoring functions is not explored.
- Minor reproducibility concern: no mention of code availability.
- Recommendation: strong paper with theoretical and empirical contributions; accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
