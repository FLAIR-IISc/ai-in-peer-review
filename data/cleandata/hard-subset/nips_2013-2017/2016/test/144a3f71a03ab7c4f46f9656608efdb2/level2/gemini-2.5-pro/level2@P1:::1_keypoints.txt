- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures from data given non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Challenge: Ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, breaking compatibility with efficient decomposable-score learning algorithms.  
- Framework: Builds on A*-based EC-tree search that uses a decomposable-score oracle to compute an admissible heuristic.  
- Main contribution: Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (edge and ordering) to tighten the oracle heuristic.  
- Edge constraints: Provides an efficient algorithm to infer decomposable edge constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Ordering constraints: Introduces a novel, sound reduction to MaxSAT to find a maximally large set of compatible topological ordering constraints.  
- Empirical comparison: Evaluated against GOBNILP (ILP); method is orders of magnitude faster and scales to problems with up to 20 variables.  
- Accuracy impact: Even a small number of ancestral constraints substantially improves learned network accuracy.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound work with thorough experiments that support the claims.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, though some algorithmic details and proofs are omitted for space.  
- Originality: Novel projection approach and the MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints are original contributions.  
- Significance: Makes optimal learning with ancestral constraints much more practical and highlights that constraints can substitute for more data.  
- Strengths: Addresses a practical problem, elegant technical solution, strong empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: Some missing details (proofs, full entailment algorithm) and no discussion of the computational overhead of the constraint projection (MaxSAT) step.  
- Questions/suggestions: Clarify what "maximally large set" means (maximum cardinality and uniqueness), report runtime overhead of constraint projection, and discuss potential bottlenecks from enumerating DAGs in large CPDAG equivalence classes.
