- Problem: Enables optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor/not ancestor of Y).
- Approach: Uses EC-tree/BN-graph search with A* guided by a decomposable-score “oracle.”
- Pruning: Prunes CPDAG nodes that cannot satisfy ancestral constraints using sound necessary/sufficient tests, falling back to enumerating DAGs in a CPDAG when needed.
- Projection: Projects ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints via (i) sound-and-complete edge inference rules (Theorems 2–3) and (ii) a MaxSAT reduction to obtain a maximal, sound set of ordering constraints (Theorem 4).
- Soundness: Emphasizes sound pruning and projection; theorems formalize when edges and orders are entailed.
- Empirics: Shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks, especially when using projected constraints.
- Scalability/accuracy: Explores up to 16–20 variables and reports improved SHD as data size and constraint proportion increase.
- Practical relevance: Projected constraints can be used by many decomposable-score learners, not just the chosen oracle.
- Novelty: Novel combination of EC-tree pruning and projection of non-decomposable ancestral knowledge into decomposable constraints.
- Weakness: CPDAG pruning can require exponential DAG enumeration in the worst case; frequency and runtime impact are not quantified.
- Weakness: MaxSAT ordering projection is sound but not complete; “maximally large” claim needs clarification as maximal under sufficient constraints only.
- Weakness: Construction of an ancestral-closure (closure under entailment) is assumed but rules and complexity are omitted.
- Weakness: No study of robustness to inconsistent or noisy background constraints; detection/handling not discussed.
- Weakness: Empirical comparison limited to ILP; constraint programming baselines (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann) are not evaluated.
- Presentation: Paper is readable and well-organized but needs more detail on closure procedure, CPDAG enumeration frequency/cost, and MaxSAT guarantees; minor formatting/typo issues exist.
- Suggestions (essential): Add formal ancestral-closure description/complexity, quantify CPDAG enumeration frequency and cost, and refine maximality claims for MaxSAT.
- Suggestions (nice-to-have): Test robustness to noisy/conflicting constraints, compare or justify omission of CP-based baselines, report numbers of projected constraints and correlate with runtime, release code/pseudocode.
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses an important problem, shows clear novelty and strong empirical gains, and produces practically useful projected constraints.
- Rejection arguments: Missing technical details affecting reproducibility, incomplete MaxSAT guarantees, and lack of robustness/breadth in baselines.
