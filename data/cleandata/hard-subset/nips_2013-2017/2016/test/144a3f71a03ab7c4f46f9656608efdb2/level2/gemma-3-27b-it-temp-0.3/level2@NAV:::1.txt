## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) optimally under ancestral constraints, a crucial aspect for incorporating domain knowledge. The core contribution lies in adapting a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores to handle these constraints, which are inherently non-decomposable. The authors achieve this by cleverly inferring decomposable constraints from the ancestral ones, enabling the use of efficient oracles previously limited to decomposable scores. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing approaches that struggle with non-decomposable constraints. The proposed approach builds upon the existing work of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] and extends it in a meaningful way. The theoretical foundation is solid, with theorems establishing the soundness of the constraint projection process. The empirical evaluation is a significant strength, demonstrating orders-of-magnitude speedups compared to ILP-based methods, particularly when leveraging the projected constraints. The experiments cover a range of benchmark datasets and varying constraint levels, providing a comprehensive assessment.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the constraint projection technique. While the authors acknowledge the potential for information loss, a deeper analysis of scenarios where projection might be ineffective or lead to suboptimal solutions would be valuable. Additionally, the reliance on the EVASOLVER for MaxSAT introduces an external dependency and its performance could be further discussed. The presentation of the experimental results, while thorough, could be streamlined for better readability. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important problem with practical relevance.
*   Presents a novel and theoretically sound approach.
*   Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over existing methods.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the limitations of constraint projection.
*   Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver.
*   Experimental results could be presented more concisely.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach is innovative, well-supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results, and addresses a significant challenge in incorporating prior knowledge. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations of constraint projection and streamline the presentation of experimental results.
