- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Adapts Chen et al.'s oracle-based framework to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Core contribution: projects ancestral constraints into equivalent decomposable constraints to empower the oracle.  
- Introduces a MaxSAT formulation to infer ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups versus ILP-based methods.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation and adequate background on decomposable scores and existing methods.  
- Explanation of why ancestral constraints are incompatible with decomposable scores is clear and well-motivated.  
- Use of EC trees (from Chen et al.) provides a sound technical framework.  
- Strength: tackles an important, practically relevant problem of integrating prior causal knowledge.  
- Strength: novel and clever technique with convincing empirical performance across varied experimental settings.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including structural Hamming distance and varied network/dataset sizes and constraint percentages.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with Bayesian networks; some technical details may be hard for newcomers.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation discussion feels rushed and lacks intuitive explanation of trade-offs.  
- Weakness: comparisons are mainly against ILP; lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art constraint-based or hybrid algorithms.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability limits of the MaxSAT solver as variable count grows.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
