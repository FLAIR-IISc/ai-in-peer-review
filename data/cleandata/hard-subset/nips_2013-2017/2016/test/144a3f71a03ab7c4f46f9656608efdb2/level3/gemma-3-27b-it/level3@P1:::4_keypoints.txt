- Introduces a method to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Leverages the EC-tree search space with an oracle for decomposable scores and prunes using projected constraints.  
- Provides theoretical justification (soundness theorems) for the pruning and constraint inference steps.  
- Empirically shows large speedups over ILP on benchmarks, especially when using projected constraints (Tables 1–2).  
- Builds on prior work (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) and addresses an important practical problem in BN learning.  
- Key concern: unclear generalization — projected constraints speed up EC-tree strongly, but Table 3 shows limited benefit for ILP as variable count grows.  
- Requests more evidence that projection benefits methods that cannot natively handle ancestral constraints, to validate broader applicability.  
- Notes an unexplained “easy–hard–easy” runtime pattern as constraint proportion varies; asks for deeper analysis of causes (pruning efficiency, dataset traits, or EC-tree limits).  
- Criticizes limited accuracy evaluation: SHD reported only for EC-tree; asks for SHD comparison between EC-tree and ILP to assess trade-offs.  
- Suggests trimming Section 4.1 (BN graph) as tangential and reallocating space to expand EC-tree and A* heuristic discussion.  
- Recommends expanding explanation of the A* heuristic and EC-tree representation details used in experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, clear writing, and significant empirical speedups.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evidence of generalizability, unexplained runtime pattern, incomplete accuracy comparison, and extraneous material.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing generalizability, explaining the easy–hard–easy phenomenon, and adding comparative SHD results.
