- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structure under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints using the EC tree search space and a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Key idea: infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints to pass to the oracle and prune the EC tree.  
- Strength: Novel application of the EC tree/Markov equivalence-class structure to handle non-decomposable constraints; empirically shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over the tested ILP baseline.  
- Weakness: Lemma 3 (central to pruning) lacks a rigorous proof and fails to handle cases where some DAGs in a CPDAG’s equivalence class violate ancestral constraints.  
- Weakness: The claim that EC tree edge generation implies specific edges (e.g., “Gk also contains Z → W”) is asserted without explanation, leaving the pruning soundness unclear.  
- Weakness: The treatment of “inferred” ordering constraints is misleading — authors infer consistent (not necessarily entailed) constraints and do not clarify theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness: The MAXSAT formulation for selecting ordering constraints is under-explained; unclear how it ensures consistency and maximal informativeness.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparison to GOBNILP is insufficient — it’s unclear if poor GOBNILP performance is due to ancestral constraints or its known weakness with unbounded parent sets.  
- Weakness: Comparison against an outdated/weak ILP/MAXSAT encoding (Cussens 2008) weakens empirical claims of orders-of-magnitude improvement.  
- Weakness: Missing discussion and comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015), a stronger CP approach that could handle ancestral constraints.  
- Quality: Technical content is promising but undermined by missing proofs and ambiguous/insufficient explanations.  
- Clarity: Generally readable but critical gaps exist (lemma proof, EC tree edge rules, MAXSAT details, “inferred” vs entailed constraints).  
- Originality: Valuable extension of EC tree framework, but relation to prior constraint-projection/ILP work is not fully clarified.  
- Significance: Problem is important and results promising, but significance is reduced by weak baselines and theoretical gaps.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; require rigorous proof for Lemma 3, clarification of EC tree edge rules, detailed MAXSAT treatment and constraint semantics, and stronger comparisons (van Beek & Hoffmann CP 2015 and a tighter ILP formulation).
