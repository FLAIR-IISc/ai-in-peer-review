- Novel contribution: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints to enable efficient algorithms.  
- Builds on prior EC tree search + oracle work (Chen et al.); empowers the oracle with inferred decomposable constraints for efficiency gains.  
- Theoretical framework is sound; Theorems 1–4 give a solid foundation.  
- Paper is well-written and connects clearly to prior Bayesian network structure learning work.  
- Experiments on standard BN benchmarks show significant speedup over ILP (GOBNILP) when using projected constraints (Table 1).  
- ILP performance remains largely unchanged by projected constraints (Table 3), questioning broad applicability.  
- Key question: does projection generalize to improve all methods lacking direct handling of non-decomposable constraints, or mainly benefit the EC tree framework?  
- Observed “easy-hard-easy” pattern as ancestral constraint proportion increases (Table 2) needs investigation and explanation.  
- Possible sensitivity of EC tree to amount of domain knowledge; may require careful tuning of constraint quantity.  
- Table 2 lacks SHD comparison with ILP, limiting assessment of accuracy trade-offs between methods.  
- Suggest removing tangential Section 4.1 and expanding details on EC trees, A* implementation, and heuristic use in experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, demonstrated efficiency, theoretical soundness, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear generalizability, sensitivity concerns, and incomplete empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address generalizability, explain the easy-hard-easy pattern, and include SHD comparisons with ILP.
