- Proposes EC-tree A* search with symmetry breaking and a URLearning heuristic to learn Bayesian networks under ancestral constraints by projecting decomposable constraints.  
- Novel idea: project ancestry into entailed edges and a maximized set of compatible ordering constraints via MaxSAT to empower a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Pruning strategy over EC trees is appealing and the projection of constraints is practically useful, yielding reported large speedups over GOBNILP.  
- Builds on Chen et al. and A* BN search literature; positions against ILP/MAXSAT and CP encodings.  
- Lemma 3 (pivotal for ancestry pruning) is underspecified—proof sketch unclear and needs expansion plus explicit conditions for completeness and A* admissibility.  
- The claim “By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W” lacks a precise reference/derivation or illustrative figure to show the orientation must appear in all completions.  
- The discussion of implied/projected constraints conflates logical entailment with consistent augmentation; “can infer” is misleading and should be clarified.  
- Must prove adding consistent (but not entailed) ordering constraints cannot exclude any A-feasible DAG during EC-tree search.  
- MaxSAT formulation details are missing: variables, hard/soft clauses, weights, acyclicity encoding, and the computational overhead of solving MaxSAT need to be fully described.  
- Experimental comparison is suggestive but not conclusive—GOBNILP’s weakness may be due to unbounded parent sets rather than ancestral constraints.  
- Baseline ILP follows Cussens (2008) which is loose; a fairer ILP would use A(X,Y) and I(X,U) variables with transitivity and A–I linking constraints.  
- Omission of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015) is a notable gap since CP often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted to ancestry constraints.  
- Need for ablations and clearer attribution of speedups: test bounded parent sizes, URLearning heuristic alone, with/without projected constraints, and measure MaxSAT overhead.  
- Generally well-written, but reproducibility suffers from insufficient rigor in proofs and lack of algorithmic detail for the projection pipeline.  
- Originality: thoughtful synthesis of EC-tree search with constraint projection and novel MaxSAT-based selection of compatible orderings.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for incorporating causal background knowledge, but claims require stronger baselines, proofs, and ablation studies.
