- Problem addressed: optimal Bayesian network structure learning with background ancestral constraints.  
- Builds on prior work for non-decomposable scores and infers decomposable constraints to enable oracle-based algorithms.  
- Core contribution: projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints usable by existing learners.  
- Technical tools: uses the EC tree and introduces Theorem 1 (pruning) and Theorems 2–4 (inferring edge and ordering constraints).  
- MaxSAT formulation: casts finding a maximal set of inferrable ordering constraints as a MaxSAT instance to empower the oracle.  
- Empirical results: orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, etc.).  
- Ablation study: projected constraints significantly improve performance (shown in Tables 1 & 2).  
- Model quality analysis: structural Hamming distance examined relative to data size and constraint proportion.  
- Motivation and positioning: well-motivated problem and well-situated within relevant literature.  
- Technical rigor: clear explanations, sound methodology, and formal theorems.  
- Strengths summarized: important problem, novel approach, strong empirical gains, and thorough evaluation.  
- Scalability concern: limited demonstrated scalability beyond ~20 variables.  
- MaxSAT dependency: potential bottleneck from reliance on the MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) not deeply explored.  
- Oracle reliance: performance depends on the decomposable-score oracle (URLEARNING); its impact needs more analysis.  
- Scope limitation: focus mainly on ancestral constraints; applicability to other non-decomposable constraint types is limited.  
- Overall judgment: significant, well-executed contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance despite noted limitations.
