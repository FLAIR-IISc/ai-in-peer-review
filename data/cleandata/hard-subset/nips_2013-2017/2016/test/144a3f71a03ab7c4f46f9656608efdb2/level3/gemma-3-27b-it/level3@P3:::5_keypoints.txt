- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Extends prior EC-tree/oracle framework to incorporate ancestral constraints via projection into decomposable constraints.  
- Core technical approach uses a MaxSAT formulation to infer topological ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based approaches.  
- Highly relevant to the probabilistic graphical models and structure learning community.  
- Correctly identifies prior limitation that many methods require decomposable constraints and offers a compelling solution.  
- Connects well to prior ILP-based structure learning work and to causal-knowledge integration literature.  
- Technical soundness: framework, theorems, and constraint-projection approach appear rigorous.  
- Significant empirical validation with comprehensive experiments varying network size, dataset size, and constraint percentage.  
- Novelty: first practical incorporation of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal structure learning via projection.  
- Clear writing, organized presentation, and helpful examples.  
- Paper is complete with clear problem statement, detailed approach, and thorough evaluation.  
- Reviewer notes limited personal expertise in the specific subarea, which constrains deep assessment.  
- Scalability shown up to n=20; experiments on larger, more realistic networks would strengthen claims.  
- Method performance depends on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER); sensitivity to solver choice is unexamined.  
- Structural Hamming distance reporting is useful but its interpretation is nuanced; more error-type analysis would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, solid theory, strong empirical gains, novel technique, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependency on external MaxSAT solver and open questions about scalability to very large networks.  
- Overall: strong paper with significant practical impact; recommend acceptance with further investigation into solver dependence and larger-scale scalability.
