- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints by inferring decomposable constraints to enable an oracle-based search.  
- Well-motivated problem: ancestral constraints are common and hard to incorporate efficiently.  
- Promising approach: leveraging a decomposable-score oracle adapted for non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical results show substantial speedups over GOBNILP.  

- Critical weakness: Lemma 3 (foundational to pruning) lacks a rigorous, complete proof.  
- EC tree edge generation (how edges "Z -> W" are created) is explained vaguely and needs clearer elaboration.  
- Misleading terminology: authors say they “infer” ordering constraints but actually add constraints consistent with ancestral constraints rather than logically entailed ones.  
- The MAXSAT encoding for selecting ordering constraints is clever but under-explained; rationale and trade-offs are missing.  

- Experimental limitations: unclear whether speedups stem from ancestral constraints or from GOBNILP’s weakness with unbounded parent sets.  
- Comparison to Cussens (2008) is weak; authors should compare against a tighter ILP encoding (e.g., A(X,Y)/I(Z,U) with transitivity and linking constraints).  
- Omits important related work: van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), which may outperform GOBNILP and be adaptable to ancestral constraints.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, practical relevance, significant empirical speedups, clever oracle + constraint projection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient proof for Lemma 3, unclear explanations, misleading wording about inference, weak empirical comparisons, omitted related work.  

- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage resubmission after (1) providing a full rigorous proof of Lemma 3, (2) clarifying EC tree edge generation and constraint inference, (3) detailing the MAXSAT encoding, and (4) performing stronger empirical comparisons including van Beek & Hoffmann and a tighter ILP baseline.
