- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by extending the EC tree search space via constraint projection.  
- Key insight: ancestral constraints can be projected into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to leverage standard decomposable-score oracles.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) EC tree framework and extends it to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong: clear theorems and sound proofs for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Novel, practical projection methods; MaxSAT-based approach for ordering constraints is elegant.  
- Empirical results claim significant speedups over GOBNILP (orders of magnitude faster in Table 1).  
- Experimental design is unclear: ambiguous whether constraint projection was applied to GOBNILP the same way as to EC.  
- Role and comparisons in Table 3 are unclear due to time/memory limits and lack of clear baseline comparisons.  
- Major missing experiment: no direct comparison of EC tree with vs. without constraint projection, undermining claims about projection’s benefit.  
- Without the EC-without-projection baseline, it's impossible to isolate the impact of constraint projection.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; theoretical results are well-motivated and proofs appear sound.  
- Significance is high: ancestral constraints are common in practice and small amounts of constraints can rival larger datasets in improving accuracy.  
- Originality is strong; first work to efficiently incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal non-decomposable-score structure learning.  
- Overall quality: strong theory but weakened empirical evaluation due to experimental clarity and missing comparisons.  
- Recommendation: major revision—add EC with/without projection comparison, clarify how projection was applied to GOBNILP, and strengthen experimental section.
