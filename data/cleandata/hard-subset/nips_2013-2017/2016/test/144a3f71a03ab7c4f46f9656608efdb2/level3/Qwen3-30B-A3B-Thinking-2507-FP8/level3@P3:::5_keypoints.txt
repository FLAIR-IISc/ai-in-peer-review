- Problem: optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints (cause-effect relations).  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s framework, extending it to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Main contribution: transform non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Introduce a MaxSAT formulation to infer the largest possible set of ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Use inferred ordering constraints to prune the EC tree search space, leveraging Chen et al.'s efficient EC tree.  
- Theorems 1–4 establish correctness of the constraint projection method.  
- MaxSAT formulation is designed to avoid cycles that would eliminate valid networks.  
- Empirical results: orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods (GOBNILP), with larger speedups as problem size grows.  
- Empirical insight: a small fraction (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can yield accuracy gains comparable to much larger training sets.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear explanations and illustrative examples.  
- Originality: novel and first efficient exact approach to incorporate ancestral constraints into optimal structure learning.  
- Significance: practical impact—enables feasible optimal learning with background knowledge for larger problems (scales to ~20 variables).  
- Strengths: methodological novelty, theoretical rigor, dramatic empirical improvements, and practical relevance.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of cases where constraint projection fails, handling of conflicting ancestral constraints, and MaxSAT implementation details.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
