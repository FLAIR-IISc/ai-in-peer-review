- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with background knowledge in the form of ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Main contribution: incorporates ancestral constraints into Chen et al.'s non-decomposable score structure-learning framework.  
- Uses the EC tree search space plus an oracle for decomposable scores, enabling sound and complete pruning based on ancestral constraints.  
- Empowers the oracle by inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints via a MaxSAT formulation.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough literature review (ILP, CP, prior work).  
- Empirical evaluation shows the approach is orders-of-magnitude faster than ILP-based alternatives (GOBNILP) across variables, data size, and constraint proportions.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate improved efficiency and scalability when incorporating ancestral constraints.  
- Strengths: strong technical contribution, solid empirical evidence, and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: overall performance depends on the quality of the decomposable-score oracle.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and future work directions.  
- Weakness: could provide deeper analysis of how ancestral constraints impact the learning process.  
- Originality: novel extension to incorporate non-decomposable ancestral constraints and use of MaxSAT to inform the oracle.  
- Significance: has potential to make structure learning more practical and to highlight the value of background knowledge.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel contribution; thorough and convincing experiments; clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on oracle quality; insufficient discussion of limitations and deeper constraint impact analysis.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
