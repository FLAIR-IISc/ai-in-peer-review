- Problem: Exact BN structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor/not ancestor of Y).
- Approach: Extend EC-tree / BN-graph shortest-path framework with pruning and admissible heuristics using a decomposable-score oracle.
- Key idea: Project ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten the oracle heuristic.
- Pruning: Sound CPDAG pruning rules with Theorem 1 ensuring no descendant solutions are discarded.
- Edge inference: Algorithms infer entailed negative and positive edges using α/β ancestor/descendant closures and a two-step detour condition.
- Ordering inference: MaxSAT formulation to compute a maximal cycle-free set of ordering constraints entailed by ancestral constraints.
- Heuristic: Admissible oracle-induced heuristic; projection enriches oracle constraints to reduce search substantially.
- Empirics: Reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) and improved scalability as constraint proportion increases.
- Practical impact: Demonstrates that modest ancestral knowledge can substitute for data and improve accuracy.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Chen et al. (2015/2016) EC-tree search and complements prior ILP/MaxSAT and path-prior approaches.
- Originality: Novel combination of projection rules, pruning theory, and MaxSAT-based ordering inference within EC-tree framework.
- Strength: Clear conceptual separation between search space, oracle heuristics, and projection machinery.
- Strength: Theorems and pruning logic are well-motivated; projection rules are intuitive and efficiently checkable after closures.
- Weakness: Experimental presentation and table labels (Tables 1–3) are confusing and need unification and clarity.
- Weakness: Missing essential ablation comparing EC-tree with vs. without projection (and edge-only vs ordering-only).
- Weakness: Baseline coverage limited—no CP (van Beek & Hoffmann) or BN-graph (without EC merging) baselines to isolate contributions.
- Weakness: CPDAG class enumeration can be exponential; frequency, depth, and wall-clock cost of enumeration are unquantified.
- Weakness: MaxSAT entailment role unclear—are maximized sets merely sufficient/conservative or true logical entailments; MaxSAT runtimes and acceptance fractions not reported.
- Weakness: Unclear whether the heuristic is consistent in the EC-tree and the empirical impact of possible A* reopenings.
- Reporting issues: Missing timeouts/failed runs, memory usage, dispersion metrics (medians/IQR); formatting and reference artifacts exist.
- Requested additions: Provide EC ablation (with/without projection, edge vs ordering), clarify table comparisons, and report costs/benefits of MaxSAT and CPDAG enumeration.
- Overall assessment: Strong, timely, and practically significant contribution with solid theory and impressive empirical gains, contingent on clearer experiments and quantified algorithmic costs.
