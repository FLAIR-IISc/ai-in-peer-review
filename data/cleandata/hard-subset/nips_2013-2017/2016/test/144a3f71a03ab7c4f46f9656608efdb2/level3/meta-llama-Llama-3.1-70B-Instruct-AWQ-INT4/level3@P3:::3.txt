This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The authors present a framework that translates ancestral constraints into ordering constraints, which are then used in an EC-tree score-based algorithm to learn optimal Bayesian networks. The approach is evaluated against a modified version of the GOBNILP system, and the results show that the proposed method outperforms GOBNILP in terms of efficiency.

The paper is well-written and clearly explained, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The concept of translating ancestral constraints into ordering constraints is straightforward and novel, and the use of an EC-tree score-based algorithm is a nice touch. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the technical details, including the rules for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.

The empirical evaluation of the approach is also thorough, with a comparison to GOBNILP on several benchmark datasets. The results show that the proposed method is orders-of-magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially for larger networks. The authors also investigate the impact of ancestral constraints on the efficiency of search and the scalability of the approach.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, and they explain the technical details in a way that is easy to understand. The paper is also well-organized, with a logical flow of ideas and a clear structure.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One of the main limitations is that the setting is somewhat artificial, as ancestral constraints would often motivate broader causal discovery methods that allow unobserved confounders. Additionally, the comparison to GOBNILP is not entirely fair, as GOBNILP was not developed for this specific problem. The authors also note that the impressive results may reflect the mismatch between the two approaches.

Another limitation is that the approach may not be practical for real-world problems, as it requires a large number of ancestral constraints to be effective. The authors note that in practice, only a few ancestral constraints are likely to be available, which may not substantially reduce the search space.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The idea of translating ancestral constraints into ordering constraints is new and interesting, and the use of an EC-tree score-based algorithm is a nice touch. However, the paper builds on previous work in the area, and the authors acknowledge the contributions of other researchers.

In terms of significance, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network learning. The approach presented in the paper has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications, from bioinformatics to social network analysis. The paper also highlights the importance of considering ancestral constraints in Bayesian network learning, which is a key aspect of causal discovery.
