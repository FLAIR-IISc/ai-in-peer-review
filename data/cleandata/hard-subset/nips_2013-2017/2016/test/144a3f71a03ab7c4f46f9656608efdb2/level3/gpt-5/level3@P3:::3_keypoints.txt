- Tackles exact BN structure learning with ancestral (path) constraints by pruning EC-tree nodes and using a decomposable-score oracle to guide A* search.
- Translates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones via edge implication rules and MaxSAT-derived topological ordering constraints.
- Empirically shows large speedups over an ILP baseline (Gobnilp), especially when projected constraints are provided to the oracle.
- Builds on EC-tree and CPDAG frameworks (Chen et al.) and relates to ILP/CP and constraint-based prior work.
- Well-organized presentation with clear definitions and an understandable EC-tree adaptation.
- Conceptually simple approach: feasibility pruning plus empowering a decomposable-score oracle with projected constraints.
- Effective integration of MaxSAT for extracting ordering constraints; edge-implication rules are well-motivated.
- Strong empirical evidence of speedups and analysis of easy–hard–easy behavior with constraint density.
- Acyclicity/compatibility encoding in the MaxSAT reduction is unclear and needs a clearer derivation or alternative encoding.
- Specific rule issues: asymmetry in Acyclicity Rule 3 needs justification or symmetric closure; Rule 4 may mishandle mutual non-ancestry unless clarified.
- Theorem 1 is imprecise about what a CPDAG guarantees; statement risks conflating CPDAG-level and DAG-level satisfaction of constraints.
- Theorem 3’s positive edge-implication result may be weak in sparse-constraint regimes, limiting practical usefulness.
- Practical concern: typical applications have few ancestral constraints, so projected constraints and search tightening may be limited; quantify projections under sparse A.
- Comparison to Gobnilp may be unfair since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this projection/oracle setting; make this limitation explicit.
- Recommend additional baselines (e.g., GES or order-graph solvers with the same projections) to better isolate the contribution.
- Request empirical ablations including MaxSAT overhead and EC-tree runs with/without projections and with only edge or only ordering projections.
- Minor clarity fixes: distinguish “oracle” vs “omniscient oracle,” rephrase informal wording, and clarify “optimize” and the atypicality of 10–25% constraints.
- Overall pros: clear extension of EC-tree, sound CPDAG pruning, neat MaxSAT use, and strong reported speedups.
- Overall cons: limited practical impact due to few real-world ancestral constraints and latent confounding, insufficiently compelling baselines, and technical/encoding details needing clarification.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending tighter theory/encoding, more realistic sparse-constraint experiments, and stronger baselines.
