- Proposes translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering constraints to incorporate them into EC-tree optimal structure learning.  
- Uses a MaxSAT-based mapping to "empower the oracle" within the EC-tree framework.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC-tree framework for non-decomposable scores and connects to prior constraint-based and ILP approaches.  
- Novelty is significant for specialists (reviewer novelty rating: 3).  
- Paper is well-written and explanations make the approach conceptually straightforward.  
- Method appears technically sound and empirically more efficient than GOBNILP as variable count increases.  
- Demonstrated empirical gains over ILP-based methods, especially in scalability.  
- Main weakness: the experimental setting is somewhat artificial and ignores common issues like unobserved confounders.  
- The approach does not naturally extend to broader causal discovery scenarios with latent confounding.  
- Constraint translation has specific concerns: Rule 3’s asymmetric conclusions from symmetric premises are counterintuitive.  
- Rule 4 may be overly restrictive and could exclude valid constraints (e.g., mutual non-causation between X and Y).  
- These issues raise questions about the completeness and optimality of the translation process.  
- Practical utility is limited when only a few ancestral constraints are available, since modest prior knowledge yields limited search-space reduction.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP feels unfair because GOBNILP wasn't designed for this problem; a comparison to greedy methods like GES would be more appropriate.  
- Theoretical issues: Theorem 1’s “iff” is incorrect—constraints satisfied by the CPDAG are a subset of those for the full DAG and need clarification.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to provide many positive edge implications when constraints are sparse, reducing practical value.  
- The acyclicity encoding is not immediately intuitive and needs clearer explanation.  
- Minor wording and presentation issues throughout (several line-specific suggestions).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, clear presentation, empirical efficiency gains, addresses EC-tree limitation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-world impact, concerns about translation completeness/optimality, limited utility with few constraints, potentially unfair baseline comparison, minor theoretical inaccuracies.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix constraint translation rules, clarify Theorem 1, and provide a more balanced experimental comparison.
