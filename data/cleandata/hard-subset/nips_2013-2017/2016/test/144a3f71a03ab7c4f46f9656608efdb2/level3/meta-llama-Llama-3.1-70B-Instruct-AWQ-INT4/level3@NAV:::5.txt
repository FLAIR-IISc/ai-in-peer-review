This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. The authors' main contribution is incorporating non-decomposable constraints, particularly ancestral constraints, into the Chen et al. [2015, 2016] structure learning approach. This is achieved by rephrasing the problem as a MaxSAT formulation with specific constraints.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their approach, including a thorough explanation of the technical preliminaries, the problem formulation, and the proposed solution. The writing is concise, and the use of notation and terminology is consistent throughout the paper.

The approach presented in this paper is original and novel, as it addresses a specific limitation of existing structure learning approaches, namely, their inability to handle non-decomposable constraints. The authors' solution is a significant contribution to the field, as it enables the incorporation of background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints, which can improve the efficiency of the learning process.

The empirical evaluation of the proposed approach is thorough and convincing, demonstrating orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based alternatives. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the results, highlighting the impact of ancestral constraints on the efficiency of the learning process.

The significance of this work is evident, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed approach has the potential to advance the state of the art in Bayesian network structure learning, particularly in scenarios where background knowledge is available.

Arguments pro acceptance:

1. Original and novel approach to incorporating non-decomposable constraints into structure learning.
2. Thorough and convincing empirical evaluation demonstrating significant runtime improvements.
3. Clear and well-organized presentation of the technical approach.
4. Significant contribution to the field, enabling the incorporation of background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints.

Arguments con acceptance:

1. Limited expertise of the reviewer with the specific approach, which may have led to a lack of detailed feedback on certain aspects of the paper.
2. No major criticisms or concerns were raised, but the reviewer would have liked to see more discussion on the potential limitations and future directions of the proposed approach.

Overall, this paper is a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning, and its acceptance would be a valuable addition to the conference program.
