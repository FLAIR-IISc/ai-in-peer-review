- Main contribution: novel projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to enable decomposable-score oracles.  
- Context: builds on EC-tree framework (Chen et al., 2016) and offers a practical alternative to ILP for constrained BN structure learning.  
- Strength: original, clearly presented technical idea with theoretical explanation and empirical claims of orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP.  
- Concern 1: experiments only demonstrate the projection within EC-tree; need evidence it improves standalone methods (e.g., DP or A*).  
- Concern 2: Table 2 shows an unexplained "easy–hard–easy" runtime pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion varies; authors should explain this sensitivity.  
- Concern 3: empirical evaluation incomplete—SHD reported only for EC trees; include SHD comparison with ILP to assess solution quality vs runtime.  
- Minor suggestion: condense BN background (Sec. 4.1) to expand details on the specific EC-tree and A* implementations used in experiments.
