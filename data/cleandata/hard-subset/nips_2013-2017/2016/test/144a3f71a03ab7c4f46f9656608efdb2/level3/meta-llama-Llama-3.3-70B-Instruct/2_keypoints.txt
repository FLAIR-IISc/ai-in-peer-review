- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using the EC tree and an oracle for decomposable scores.  
- Utilizes the EC tree search space for non-decomposable scores while empowering an oracle by passing decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints.  
- Key insight: convert non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to enable oracle-based optimization.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement, method description, and experimental setup.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based learning systems.  
- Experiments are thorough and results are presented clearly.  
- Major strength: handles non-decomposable constraints and provides a sound and complete EC tree pruning method based on ancestral constraints.  
- Technical soundness: theoretical analysis and experimental results support the claims.  
- Originality: novel combination of existing techniques to address ancestral constraints in structure learning.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and shows scalability to larger networks and many variables.  
- Main weakness: experimental comparisons are confusing, especially in Tables 1 and 3.  
- Missing a direct comparison between the original EC approach and the new method combined with EC.  
- Paper would benefit from more detailed explanations of the method and experimental results.  
- Additional comparisons with existing approaches are needed to better demonstrate advantages.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify experiments and add needed comparisons and explanations.
