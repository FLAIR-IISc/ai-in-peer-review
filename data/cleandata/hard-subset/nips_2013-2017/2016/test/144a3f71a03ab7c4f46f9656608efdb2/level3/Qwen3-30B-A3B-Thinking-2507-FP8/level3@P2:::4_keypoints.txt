- Addresses incorporation of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning via EC trees.  
- Introduces a novel, theoretically sound method to project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Core technical contribution: algorithms in Section 5 for inferring positive/negative edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical support: formal proofs for constraint projection (Theorems 2–4).  
- Practical impact: enables use of decomposable-score oracles for problems with non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Strong empirical results: orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) across network sizes and constraint proportions, especially when constraints are projected.  
- Demonstrates that 10–25% of ancestral constraints can yield model-accuracy gains comparable to substantially larger datasets.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with thorough experimental design and evaluation.  
- Weakness: limited evidence that projection method generalizes beyond the EC tree approach; ILP benefits inconsistently.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of the "easy–hard–easy" performance pattern in Table 2 and lack of comparison to ILP behavior.  
- Weakness: incomplete experimental comparison—SHD reported only for EC tree, not ILP, hindering assessment of accuracy vs. efficiency trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Section 4.1 on BN graphs is largely redundant; paper should expand EC tree and A* implementation details in experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address generalizability, explain performance patterns, complete SHD comparisons, and remove/replace redundant content.
