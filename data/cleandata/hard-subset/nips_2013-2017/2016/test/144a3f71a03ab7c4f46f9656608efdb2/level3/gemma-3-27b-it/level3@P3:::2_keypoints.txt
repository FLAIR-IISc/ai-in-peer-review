- Presents a method to incorporate ancestral constraints into Bayesian network learning by projecting them onto decomposable constraints for oracle-based methods.  
- Motivation is clear: ancestral constraints are useful but hard to integrate with decomposable scoring functions.  
- Builds on and properly cites prior work (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) and related ILP approaches.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound: Theorems 1–4 establish correctness of pruning and constraint inference.  
- Experiments show the approach scales reasonably to networks up to ~20 variables.  
- Potential impact is high: leveraging background knowledge can improve structure learning efficiency.  
- Experimental evaluation is confusing and undermines the paper’s claims.  
- Table 1 compares EC+projection to GOBNILP+different-projection, making it unclear whether gains come from EC, projection, or both.  
- A direct controlled comparison of EC with and without the proposed projection is missing and is essential.  
- Comparing against GOBNILP with different constraints is misleading since EC is generally superior to GOBNILP.  
- The role and relevance of Table 3 to the main claim (benefit of projection) are unclear and not well explained.  
- Authors note an "easy-hard-easy" trend but fail to explain why projection helps in easy regimes and not in hard regimes.  
- Use of MaxSAT for inferring ordering constraints may be costly for very large networks, but this is not empirically evaluated.  
- Limited exploration of constraint density/accuracy/noise; more systematic investigation is needed.  
- Recommendation: reject as submitted but encourage resubmission after adding a direct EC vs. EC+projection experiment and clarifying Table 3’s role.
