- Paper addresses exact Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral (path) constraints, which are non-decomposable and practically important.  
- Builds on EC-tree/A* search with a decomposable-score oracle and integrates ancestral constraints via CPDAG pruning and projection to decomposable constraints.  
- CPDAG pruning: remove CPDAG nodes that cannot realize any DAG consistent with ancestral constraints (Theorem 1).  
- Edge projection: entailment rules after closing ancestral constraints under transitivity/inconsistency provide decomposable edge constraints (Theorems 2–3).  
- Ordering projection: MaxSAT formulation infers a large consistent set of X < Y orderings (sufficient conditions summarized in Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results show large runtime improvements over GOBNILP (ILP) across variable counts and constraint densities, with easy–hard–easy behavior.  
- Strong motivation and clear conceptual advance: integrating non-decomposable constraints into EC-tree search and tightening the heuristic oracle.  
- Technical contributions include edge-entailment rules, MaxSAT ordering extraction, and using these to improve A* heuristic admissibility.  
- Experimental promise: substantial speedups and informative behavior with respect to constraint density.  
- Key novelty: handling ancestral constraints in EC-tree search and projecting them into decomposable constraints for the oracle.  
- Major missing ablations: need EC search without projection (EC alone) vs EC+projection, and separate edge-only vs ordering-only projections.  
- Tables/experiments unclear: unclear comparisons across Tables 1–3 and whether projections are enabled in scaling studies.  
- Theorem 1 may rely on enumerating class(P) in inconclusive cases; enumeration can be exponential—need statistics on how often it occurs and practical mitigations (polynomial tests, caching).  
- MaxSAT/order projection is conservative (sufficient conditions only); report how many orderings are inferred vs entailed and MaxSAT runtime and solver sensitivity/scalability.  
- Ancestral-closure rules are omitted but crucial for Theorems 2–3; authors must provide exact rules, complexity, and correctness/termination guarantees.  
- Robustness to noisy or inconsistent constraints is not assessed; need analysis or extension to soft constraints.  
- Generality: experiments use BDeu (ESS=1) with no parent-set cap; report results for BIC/MDL and with parent caps which affect ILP baselines.  
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: citation/typesetting glitches, inconsistent notation for constraints, and need for pseudo-code for edge-entailment and closure procedures.  
- Theoretical parts are mostly sound but omit derivations/closure rules and fallback enumeration is not quantified.  
- Empirical reporting should include node expansions, memory, and time splits (projection/MaxSAT vs search).  
- Originality: novel combination of EC-tree pruning and projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones; prior work hasn’t used this EC+oracle framework.  
- Significance: relevant to practitioners with background causal knowledge; reported speedups are substantial though scale is similar to prior exact learning (≈20 vars).  
- Acceptance pros: addresses an important form of background knowledge, integrates theory and practice, and shows strong empirical performance vs ILP.  
- Acceptance cons: missing critical ablations, potential exponential enumeration fallback unquantified, and lack of tests with noisy or real expert constraints.  
- Required camera-ready changes: add EC-alone vs EC+projection and edge-only vs ordering-only ablations; provide closure rules/proofs and quantify CPDAG enumeration and MaxSAT costs; clarify experiments/tables and report search statistics.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing ablations, clarity, and methodological details; reviewer confidence high.
