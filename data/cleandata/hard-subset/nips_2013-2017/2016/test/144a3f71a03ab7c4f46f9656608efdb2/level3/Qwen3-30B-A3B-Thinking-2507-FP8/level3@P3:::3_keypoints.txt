- Presents a novel method to learn optimal Bayesian networks with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable ordering and edge constraints for an EC-tree oracle.  
- Uses MaxSAT to infer maximal sets of ordering constraints and algorithms to infer edge constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP-based implementation (Gobnilp) in experiments.  
- Technical approach is sound, well-motivated, and grounded in clear theoretical foundations for constraint projection.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses helpful examples and figures.  
- Translating ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints is an original and significant contribution for structure learning efficiency.  
- Impact may be limited because the ancestral-constraint setting is somewhat artificial and often motivates broader causal discovery methods instead.  
- Theorem 1 contains an incorrect "iff" claim about CPDAG constraint satisfaction; the stated equivalence is not generally true.  
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to produce many edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse, limiting its practical usefulness.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is potentially unfair since Gobnilp was not designed for this specific problem; a baseline like GES with constraints would be more appropriate.  
- The "acyclic" encoding (line 267) is unclear and needs clarification to show it captures acyclicity.  
- The paper overstates the typical availability of ancestral constraints and should clarify how many such constraints are realistic in practice.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, addressing theoretical issues (Theorem 1/3), clarifying implementation and practical relevance, and including a more appropriate baseline.
