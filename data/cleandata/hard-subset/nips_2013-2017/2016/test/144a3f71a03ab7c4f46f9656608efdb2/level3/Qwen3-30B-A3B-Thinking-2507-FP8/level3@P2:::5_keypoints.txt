- Problem: Incorporates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network (BN) structure learning.  
- Context: Builds on Chen et al.'s non-decomposable scores and BN graph / EC tree search spaces.  
- Limitation of prior work: Methods like K2 and order-graph approaches assume decomposable scores and cannot handle ancestral constraints directly.  
- ILP alternative: Integer linear programming can encode ancestral constraints but is empirically inefficient.  
- Main contribution: A framework enabling optimal BN learning with ancestral constraints using the EC tree, pruning via ancestral constraints (Theorem 1), and inferring decomposable constraints.  
- Theoretical advance: Formal projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints (Theorems 2–4).  
- Oracle/optimization: Uses inferred decomposable constraints to empower an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores.  
- Empirical gains: Orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over GOBNILP across multiple datasets and constraint sizes.  
- Data-efficiency finding: Small fractions (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can improve model quality comparable to large increases in training data (e.g., 512→8192 samples).  
- Experimental rigor: Comprehensive evaluation across 5 benchmark networks, varying n, N, and constraint percentage p, with SHD used for model-quality assessment.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, clear distinction between ancestral vs. decomposable constraints; helpful figures; MaxSAT formulation presented clearly but could be more intuitive.  
- Originality: Novel approach for incorporating ancestral constraints into optimal score-based learning, distinct from constraint-based methods.  
- Practical significance: Background ancestral knowledge is common in applications; approach scales to ~20 variables comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks direct empirical comparison to other non-decomposable-score approaches (e.g., constraint programming).  
- Weakness — datasets: Evaluation limited to simulated benchmark networks; no real-world case study provided.  
- Weakness — complexity analysis: Needs clearer analysis of computational complexity and runtime impact of the constraint-projection / MaxSAT step.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, novel, and significant contribution with strong empirical support; recommend acceptance.  
- Pro-accept points: Addresses a clear problem, strong empirical improvements, rigorous theoretical justification, and clear practical implications.  
- Con-accept points: Reliance on simulated data, limited differentiation from constraint-based learning, and the MaxSAT formulation could be made more intuitive.
