- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and a URLearning-based heuristic.  
- Tackles the hard problem of non-decomposable ancestral constraints within a framework for non-decomposable scores.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear introduction and thorough related-work review (including ILP and CP approaches).  
- Presents a sound and complete pruning method for the EC tree that leverages ancestral constraints.  
- Shows how the oracle is strengthened by adding decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Provides detailed procedures for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, including assessment of how ancestral constraints affect search efficiency and scalability.  
- Reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP across varied n, N, and p settings.  
- Lemma 3, which enables pruning with ancestral constraints, has an unclear and insufficiently justified proof.  
- The claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" needs more explanation.  
- Treatment of implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained; "can infer" is misleading without clarifying consistency vs. entailment.  
- The MAXSAT procedure used to control added ordering constraints lacks sufficient detail and rationale.  
- It is unclear whether GOBNILP’s poor performance is caused specifically by ancestral constraints or by general weakness with unbounded parent-set size.  
- Comparison against Cussens (2008) MAXSAT/ILP encoding is weak; a tighter ILP using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and linking constraints should be considered.  
- The omission of van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), a method that often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted to ancestral constraints, weakens empirical comparisons.  
- Overall: promising and efficient approach but needs clearer proofs, better explanation of constraint inference and MAXSAT use, and stronger empirical baselines.
