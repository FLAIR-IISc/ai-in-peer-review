- Summary: Exact BN structure learning under ancestral/path constraints using EC-tree A* with a decomposable-score oracle empowered by projected edge and ordering constraints (edge closure + MaxSAT); empirical speedups over GOBNILP on synthetic benchmarks.

- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem—exact BN learning with path knowledge.

- Strength: EC-tree search over CPDAGs provides symmetry breaking and often reduces the state space versus DAG-space search.

- Strength: Clear separation of concerns—pruning with non-decomposable constraints while strengthening the decomposable-score oracle.

- Strength: Intuitive and potentially useful edge-projection rules.

- Strength: Strong empirical speedups and informative ablation studies on projected constraints.

- Weakness: Soundness/admissibility of the heuristic with ordering projections is unclear—paper alternates between inferring and maximizing orderings, risking A* optimality.

- Weakness: Theorem 4 and MaxSAT claims need precision: are only entailed orderings passed to the oracle, or is optimality sacrificed?

- Weakness: Lemma 3 (key pruning result) has an unclear/incomplete proof and requires a rigorous, self-contained statement and proof.

- Weakness: The EC-tree orientation step (“Gk also contains edge Z -> W”) is not justified; need explicit EC-tree edge-generation and orientation-propagation rules and a minimal example or lemma.

- Weakness: Paper conflates implied/entailed vs merely consistent projected constraints; must explicitly distinguish and state consequences for admissibility.

- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation (clause (3)) lacks rationale and proof that selected orderings are entailed; need instance size, solver times, and ablation between different ordering-selection methods.

- Weakness: CPDAG satisfaction test may require exponential enumeration of class(P); quantify frequency/average class size and consider consistent-extension or possible-path tests to avoid enumeration.

- Weakness: Experimental baseline concerns—GOBNILP’s poor runtime may be due to unbounded parent sets or a weak ILP encoding; include bounded-indegree runs and a tighter ILP with A/I variables and transitivity/linking constraints.

- Weakness: Missing comparison to CP approach (van Beek & Hoffmann 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and could handle path constraints.

- Weakness: Many operational details (URLearning heuristic, EC-tree construction, symmetry breaking) are deferred to prior work; needs a short recap for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Notation and definitions (e.g., “maximum set of ancestral constraints”) are underspecified; provide closure rules and complexity.

- Quality: Technically promising but underspecified at key theoretical points (Lemma 3, orientation argument, admissibility under ordering projections).

- Clarity: Readable and well-motivated overall, but several core arguments are too terse and must explicitly distinguish entailed vs consistent projections.

- Originality: Novel synthesis of EC-tree A* search with projected constraints to empower a decomposable oracle under path constraints.

- Significance: Could be a useful exact framework for path knowledge if theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation are strengthened.

- Revision suggestion: Provide rigorous proof of Lemma 3 and detailed explanation/lemmas for the Z->W orientation step.

- Revision suggestion: Clarify whether ordering projections are entailed; if so, fix MaxSAT and prove entailment; if not, state loss of optimality and present appropriate results.

- Revision suggestion: Report MaxSAT sizes/solve times and add ablations isolating edge vs ordering projection contributions.

- Revision suggestion: Strengthen baselines—bounded indegree GOBNILP, tighter ILP encoding with A/I variables, and include or discuss the CP baseline.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a relevant constraint type with a principled search framework, offers strong empirical speedups, and introduces useful practical ideas (edge projections).

- Arguments against acceptance: Key theoretical guarantees are underjustified, baselines are weak/missing, and algorithmic details are deferred limiting reproducibility.
