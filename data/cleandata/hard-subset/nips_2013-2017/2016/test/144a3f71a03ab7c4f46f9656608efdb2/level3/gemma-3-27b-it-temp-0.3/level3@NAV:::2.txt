## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper tackles the problem of learning Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral constraints, a crucial aspect for incorporating domain knowledge into structure learning. The core idea – leveraging a recently proposed framework based on non-decomposable scores and empowering oracles with decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral ones – is a sensible and potentially impactful approach. The work builds upon the existing literature on optimal structure learning, particularly the work of Chen et al. on BN graphs and EC trees, and correctly identifies the limitations of traditional decomposable score-based methods when dealing with non-decomposable constraints like ancestral relations. 

The strengths of the paper lie in its theoretical grounding and the clear articulation of how ancestral constraints can be projected into decomposable constraints usable by existing oracles. The proposed method offers a pathway to efficiently incorporate background knowledge, which is a significant advantage. The empirical evaluation, while somewhat convoluted (see below), demonstrates improvements over an ILP-based approach, suggesting the practical benefits of the proposed technique. 

However, the presentation of experimental results is a major weakness. The comparison in Table 1 appears to be between (EC + new work) and (GOB + new work’), making it difficult to isolate the contribution of the proposed method. The role of Table 3 is unclear without more context, and the discussion of its results is limited. Critically, the paper lacks a direct comparison of (EC + new work) versus EC *alone*. This is the most relevant comparison, as it demonstrates the benefit of the constraint projection technique. Even if GOB benefits more from the “new work” (the constraint projection), EC is already a superior method, so understanding the improvement *within* the EC framework is paramount.

Furthermore, while the paper mentions the scalability of the approach, a more detailed analysis of computational complexity and memory usage, especially as the number of variables increases, would be beneficial. The reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) also introduces a dependency that could impact reproducibility and performance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to incorporating non-decomposable constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.
*   Sound theoretical basis and clear explanation of the constraint projection technique.
*   Demonstrated performance improvements over an ILP-based approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Confusing presentation of experimental results.
*   Lack of a direct comparison between (EC + new work) and EC alone.
*   Limited discussion of computational complexity and scalability.



**Overall:** I believe this paper has merit and presents a valuable contribution to the field. However, the clarity of the experimental results needs significant improvement. I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors providing a clear comparison of (EC + new work) versus EC alone, clarifying the purpose and results of Table 3, and providing a more detailed analysis of the computational aspects of the proposed method.
