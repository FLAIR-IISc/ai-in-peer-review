- Paper addresses exact BN structure learning with ancestral (path) constraints by pruning EC-tree and projecting constraints to decomposable forms for oracle tightening.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC-tree and ties to DP/A*/ILP work in score-based BN learning and prior path-constraint work.  
- Overall architecture is sensible, but Theorem 1 needs clearer quantifiers and assumptions to avoid misreading.  
- Pruning claim is plausible under clarified assumptions: A over full variable set, EC-tree adds only a leaf, and “P satisfies A” means ∃G ∈ class(P) satisfying A.  
- Negative-edge inference (Theorem 2) appears correct and useful in practice.  
- Positive-edge inference (Theorem 3) is strong but will rarely apply under sparse constraints, limiting practical impact.  
- MaxSAT ordering encoding is interesting but has two issues: Rule 3’s asymmetric conclusion needs rigorous justification, and Rule 4’s soft clauses require explanation to avoid cyclical order selections.  
- Empirical results show large speedups vs GOBNILP, but GOBNILP may be an unfair baseline since it wasn't designed for ancestral constraints.  
- Recommend adding greedy/CP baselines (e.g., GES, greedy local search) and experiments with very sparse constraints (1–5% or few constraints).  
- Empirical ablations are good, but the paper should report counts of projected edge vs ordering constraints and their separate runtime impacts.  
- Clarity: be consistent about minimize vs maximize, prefer “oracle for decomposable scores,” and rephrase “empower the oracle.”  
- MaxSAT acyclicity constraint needs clearer explanation, intuition, reference, or proof.  
- Theorem statements (1, 3, 4) should include explicit quantifiers, assumptions (closed/maximal A), and brief proofs or an appendix.  
- The claim that 10–25% ancestral constraints is realistic is optimistic; acknowledge sparser realistic priors and test them.  
- Originality is moderate: combining EC-tree pruning with constraint projection is novel for ancestral constraints though related ideas exist elsewhere.  
- Significance is mixed: large gains in tested regime but limited by likely few available constraints, focus on fully observed DAGs (no latent confounding), and reliance on strong precomputed-score oracles.  
- Pros: sound framework, clever projection to decomposable constraints, strong empirical speedups, and clear writing.  
- Cons: some theoretical components need clarification, positive-edge rule limited under sparsity, baseline comparison may be unfair, limited experiments for sparse priors, and terse MaxSAT presentation.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify theorems and MaxSAT rules with proofs/appendix, report projected-constraint statistics, add greedy/CP baselines, include sparse-constraint experiments, and tone down density claims.  
- Overall: solid, promising extension of EC-tree to non-decomposable ancestral constraints with strong empirical gains but requires clearer theory and broader, sparser baselines; borderline accept.
