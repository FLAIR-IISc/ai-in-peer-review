

This paper addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian network structure learning: incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimization frameworks. The authors propose a novel method to project these non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints, enabling standard learning algorithms (which require decomposable constraints) to handle ancestral constraints. The core contribution, detailed in Section 5, demonstrates how to infer sound decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, with empirical results showing orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based approaches.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains both theoretical foundations and experimental results, making it accessible to readers. The authors relate their work to prior contributions (e.g., Chen et al. [2015, 2016] on EC trees) and clearly articulate how their method extends existing frameworks for structure learning with constraints.

**Strengths:** The method is technically sound, with rigorous theoretical foundations (Theorems 2-4) supporting the constraint projection approach. The empirical evaluation demonstrates significant efficiency gains (Table 1), and the analysis of the easy-hard-easy pattern in Table 2 provides valuable insights into constraint sensitivity. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to a challenging problem in structure learning.

**Weaknesses:** The main concerns relate to experimental validation. The paper claims general applicability of the constraint projection method, but Table 3 shows no improvement for ILP when constraints are projected, raising doubts about whether the method truly generalizes to other learning approaches. The authors should provide evidence that the inferred constraints improve performance for methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints. Additionally, the easy-hard-easy pattern in Table 2 requires explanation—why does EC tree performance peak at moderate constraint proportions? The omission of SHD comparisons for ILP in Table 2 is problematic, as it limits the ability to assess relative model quality. Finally, Section 4.1 on BN graphs is tangential to the main contribution and should be removed to allow deeper exploration of EC trees and the A* algorithm.

**Recommendation:** This paper makes a valuable contribution to Bayesian network structure learning with constraints. However, the current experimental evidence is insufficient to fully validate the generalizability claim. Addressing the concerns about ILP performance, explaining the easy-hard-easy pattern, and adding SHD comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper. I recommend **weak acceptance** with major revisions to address these points.
