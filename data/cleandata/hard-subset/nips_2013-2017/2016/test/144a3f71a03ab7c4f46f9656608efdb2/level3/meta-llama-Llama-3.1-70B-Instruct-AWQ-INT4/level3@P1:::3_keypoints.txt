- Proposes a novel method for learning Bayesian networks under non-decomposable ancestral constraints using a framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores.  
- Leverages an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores by supplying it decomposable constraints inferred from ancestral constraints.  
- Key technical contribution: mapping ancestral constraints to ordering constraints via a MaxSAT solver, enabling explicit representation of implied ancestral constraints.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over a modified GOBNILP (ILP-based) implementation.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to specialists, with a clear and straightforward presentation of the idea.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and experimentally shown to scale and be efficient.  
- Concern: the experimental setting is somewhat artificial since ancestral constraints usually arise in broader causal discovery contexts with unobserved confounders.  
- Practical limitation: in real problems only a few ancestral constraints may be available, which might not sufficiently reduce the search space.  
- Suggestion/limitation: evaluation should include additional baselines (e.g., greedy algorithms like GES) under assumptions where ancestral constraints align with the optimal model.  
- Specific technical concern about certain inference rules (e.g., acyclicity rule) having symmetric premises but asymmetric conclusions that need clarification.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent use of the term “oracle” (sometimes “omniscient oracle”) and awkward phrasing like “empower the oracle” should be revised.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel contribution addressing a challenging problem and strong empirical efficiency; rejection arguments: limited impact due to setting, potentially unfair baseline comparison, and need for broader evaluation.
