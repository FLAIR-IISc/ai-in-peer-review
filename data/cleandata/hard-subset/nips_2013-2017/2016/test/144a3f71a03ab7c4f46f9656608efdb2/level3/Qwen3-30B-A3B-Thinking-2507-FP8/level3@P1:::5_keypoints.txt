- Addresses Bayesian network structure learning with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints, a largely overlooked problem.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework and oracle-based optimal structure learning for non-decomposable scores.  
- Shows decomposable edge and ordering constraints can be inferred from ancestral constraints and passed to the oracle.  
- Provides a polynomial-time algorithm to derive decomposable edge constraints from ancestral relations.  
- Formulates ordering constraint extraction as a MaxSAT problem to find maximal compatible ordering constraints.  
- Integrates inferred constraints into EC tree search to prune invalid structures while preserving optimality.  
- Theoretical claims are rigorously supported by formal proofs (Theorems 2–4).  
- Empirical evaluation compares against GOBNILP across multiple benchmarks and variable sizes (n=12–20).  
- Results show orders-of-magnitude speedups, particularly as problem size increases.  
- Demonstrates that modest amounts of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match large increases in dataset size for accuracy.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized with intuitive examples and helpful figures explaining EC trees and MaxSAT.  
- Original contribution: first to handle ancestral constraints within the EC tree framework by projecting non-decomposable to decomposable constraints.  
- High practical significance for incorporating expert causal knowledge, improving efficiency and accuracy in structure learning.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of cases where constraint projection is inconsistent or too weak, and potential MaxSAT scalability issues for very large constraint sets.  
- Lacks comparison to constraint-based (approximate) methods, though authors note these are typically non-optimal.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution suitable for acceptance.
