- Presents a novel method to learn Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Projection technique (Section 5) enables an oracle to search the EC tree and is the paper’s main contribution.  
- Claims large efficiency improvements for EC-tree search compared to ILP-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and situates itself with a thorough related-work review.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments are technically sound and support the paper’s claims.  
- Concern: Table 3 suggests the projection improves EC trees but not ILP, raising doubts about generalizability to other learning methods.  
- Request: Provide evidence that inferred decomposable constraints improve performance of methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Observation: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; authors should explain this behavior and EC-tree sensitivity to domain-knowledge amount.  
- Request: Discuss tuning implications for the number of ancestral constraints to achieve optimal EC-tree performance.  
- Request: Add structural Hamming distance (SHD) comparison between EC trees and ILP in Table 2 for a more complete evaluation.  
- Suggestion: Consider dropping Section 4.1 on BN graph and expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree experimental setting.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with revisions to address the generalizability, sensitivity explanation, SHD comparison, and clarity of EC-tree/A* exposition.
