- Paper addresses learning Bayesian networks under non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a significant problem for incorporating domain knowledge.  
- Builds on prior search framework (A* over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking) and uses URLearning as an oracle.  
- Main contribution: projecting ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable constraints usable by the oracle.  
- Projection technique is novel and enables leveraging existing efficient algorithms.  
- Experimental results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP.  
- Authors correctly note Cussens (2008) ILP baseline used is suboptimal.  
- Lemma 3, central to pruning, lacks a clear proof and needs substantial clarification.  
- The use of "can infer" ordering constraints is unclear—authors appear to add consistent but not necessarily entailed constraints, which is potentially misleading.  
- MaxSAT approach for inferring ordering constraints is under-described; implementation choices and rationale are missing.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is insufficiently nuanced; speedups may reflect issues with unbounded parent sets rather than ancestral-constraint handling.  
- Omission of van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), a competitive CP-based baseline, is a significant empirical oversight.  
- Recommendation: promising work but should be accepted only after major revisions addressing theoretical clarity and a more thorough experimental comparison.
