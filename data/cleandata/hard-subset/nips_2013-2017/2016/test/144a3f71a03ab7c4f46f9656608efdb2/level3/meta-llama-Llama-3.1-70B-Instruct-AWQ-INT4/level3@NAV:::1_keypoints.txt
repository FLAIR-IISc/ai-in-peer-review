- Tackles the challenging problem of incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints in Bayesian network learning.  
- Introduces a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, a significant technical contribution.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation showing orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based systems.  
- Well-written with clear explanation of approach and theoretical foundations.

- Lemma 3, central to pruning with ancestral constraints, lacks a clear proof and needs stronger justification.  
- The claim "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" is unclear and requires elaboration.  
- Treatment of implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained; "can infer" is misleading without clarifying added constraints are consistent but not entailed.  
- Description of the MAXSAT approach for selecting ordering constraints lacks detail on the inference procedure and rationale.  
- Unclear whether GOBNILP's poor performance is due to handling ancestral constraints specifically or its weakness with unbounded parent set size.  
- Comparison to a MAXSAT encoding (Cussens 2008) is weak; a tighter ILP using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and A–I linking constraints would be preferable.  
- Fails to discuss van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), which often outperforms GOBNILP and might be adaptable to ancestral constraints.

- Proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, marking a significant contribution.  
- New combination of branch-and-bound A* over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and a heuristic from URLearning.

- Results demonstrate substantial efficiency improvements over existing ILP-based approaches.  
- Addresses a hard problem in Bayesian network structure learning (non-decomposable ancestral constraints).  
- Approach has broad potential applications (classification, clustering, feature selection, causal discovery).

- Paper is technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Authors present an honest and careful evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.

- Paper is well-written, clear, and concise in explaining methods and theory.  
- Logical organization makes the paper easy to follow.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address Lemma 3 proof, clarify EC tree edge claim, detail MAXSAT method, compare to tighter ILP, and discuss van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015).
