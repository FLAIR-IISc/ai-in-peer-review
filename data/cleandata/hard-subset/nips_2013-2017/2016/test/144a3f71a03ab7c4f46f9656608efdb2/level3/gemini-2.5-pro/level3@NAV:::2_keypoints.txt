- Presents a method for learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints using the EC tree search framework.  
- Core contribution: uses an exact solver for decomposable scores as an admissible heuristic for A* on the EC tree.  
- Key technical idea: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to "empower" the oracle and tighten the heuristic.  
- Positioned as a more efficient alternative to ILP-based methods, building on Chen et al. (2015, 2016).  
- Strength — Significance: addresses an important practical problem; small numbers of ancestral constraints can markedly improve model accuracy (Table 3).  
- Strength — Originality: novel and generalizable technique to infer decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones for heuristic search.  
- Strength — Quality: technically sound approach with empirical orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP on the evaluated task.  
- Weakness — Experimental clarity: comparisons are confusing because the projection technique is applied to the baseline, confounding the evaluation.  
- Weakness — Missing baselines: needed comparisons include (EC tree + projection) vs un-enhanced GOBNILP and vs EC tree without the empowered heuristic.  
- Overall: promising, potentially high-impact method, but empirical claims are not well-supported by the current experimental design and should be revised.
