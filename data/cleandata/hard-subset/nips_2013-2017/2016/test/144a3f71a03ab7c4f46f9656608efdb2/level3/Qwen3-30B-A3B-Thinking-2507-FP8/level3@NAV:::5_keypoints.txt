- Problem: tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a challenging and practical problem.  
- Approach: transforms ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints that existing oracles can handle.  
- Main technical contribution: a MaxSAT formulation to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical quality: sound foundations with formal proofs for the constraint inference methods.  
- Empirical evaluation: thorough experiments varying network size, dataset size, and constraint density across multiple benchmarks.  
- Performance: shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods, especially for larger networks and higher constraint density.  
- Practical viability: runtime improvements make optimal learning with ancestral constraints feasible for larger problems.  
- Impact of constraints: small amounts of ancestral constraints (10%–25%) yield efficiency and accuracy gains comparable to much larger datasets.  
- Relation to prior work: well-motivated extension of Chen et al.'s framework, clearly distinguished from decomposable constraint methods.  
- Presentation and significance: well-written, clearly organized, and represents a novel, meaningful advance for structure learning.
