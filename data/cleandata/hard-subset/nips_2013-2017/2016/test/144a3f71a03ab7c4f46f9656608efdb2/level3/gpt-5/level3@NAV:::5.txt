This paper tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints. Building on Chen et al. (2015, 2016), the authors search in the BN/EC-tree spaces while delegating decomposable subproblems to an oracle (e.g., order-graph–based solvers). The key contribution is to make ancestral constraints usable in this framework via two ideas: (i) safe pruning of the EC tree using constraint satisfaction tests (with quick checks and, if needed, CPDAG-class enumeration), and (ii) “projection” of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to empower the oracle. The latter uses a complete rule-based procedure for inferring edge presence/absence and a MaxSAT formulation to select a maximal, cycle-free set of ordering constraints. Empirically, the approach yields orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks. This extends earlier work on non-decomposable priors and k-best enumeration, and relates to ILP/CP formulations that can encode path constraints (e.g., Cussens 2008; Jaakkola et al. 2010) and to constraint-based causal prior work (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).

Quality: The technical development appears sound, with formal theorems for pruning and entailment (edge and ordering constraints). The empirical study is careful about data generation and includes ablations on projected constraints. A possible concern is the fallback to enumerating DAGs in a CPDAG class when quick tests are inconclusive; the worst-case cost of this step and its frequency in practice should be quantified. The ILP comparison is compelling, though fairness and tuning (e.g., indegree bounds, cutting planes) should be discussed more explicitly.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written. Some definitions (e.g., “maximum set” of ancestral constraints) would benefit from precise algorithms/pseudocode. The MaxSAT encoding deserves a concise, self-contained specification and complexity discussion. Minor reference formatting issues exist.

Originality: Incorporating ancestral constraints into the EC-tree/oracle framework and projecting them via MaxSAT is, to my knowledge, novel. Prior ILP/CP work supports such constraints but lacks the proposed projection-empowerment mechanism and the empirical evaluation presented here.

Significance: If the reported speedups hold more broadly, this is a meaningful advance for exact structure learning with background knowledge, with potential impact on practical causal discovery workflows.

Pros:
- Clear, principled integration of non-decomposable constraints into an exact search framework
- Effective projection (edge rules + MaxSAT) that materially accelerates search
- Strong empirical gains over ILP on standard benchmarks

Cons:
- Potential bottleneck in CPDAG-class enumeration not fully analyzed
- Limited scale (n ≤ 20) and focus on BDeu; broader settings would strengthen claims
- Baseline tuning details could be expanded

Caveat: I have limited expertise with this specific approach; nonetheless, the runtime improvements and conceptual clarity are impressive.
