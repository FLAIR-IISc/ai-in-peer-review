- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (ancestor/not-ancestor) that break decomposability assumptions of many efficient learners.  
- Main idea: Improve A* search over equivalence-class (EC) trees by empowering its heuristic via projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones.  
- Heuristic oracle: Use an optimal decomposable-score learner as an "oracle" whose heuristic is tightened by inferred decomposable constraints.  
- Constraint projection: Provide a direct algorithm to infer edge presence/absence constraints from ancestral constraints (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Topological constraints: Formulate inferring a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints as a novel MaxSAT problem.  
- Technical novelty: The projection methodology is a non-trivial, original contribution that extends Chen et al.’s EC-tree framework.  
- Empirical evaluation: Compared to ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on benchmarks, datasets, sizes, and constraint densities.  
- Results: Reports orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over the ILP solver and shows small numbers of constraints can match large data increases in accuracy.  
- Analysis insight: Observes and explains an "easy–hard–easy" runtime pattern as constraint density varies.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized overall, with clear motivation and method exposition.  
- Clarity issue: MaxSAT formulation (Section 5.2) is dense and would benefit from intuition or a worked example.  
- Incrementality concern: Work is an extension of an existing EC-tree/A* framework rather than an entirely new structure-learning paradigm.  
- Dependence: Performance relies on the underlying A* EC-tree search and the chosen decomposable-score oracle (URLEARNING).  
- Reviewer caveat and verdict: Reviewer notes limited expertise in exact-learning/ILP subtleties but finds results convincing and recommends acceptance.
