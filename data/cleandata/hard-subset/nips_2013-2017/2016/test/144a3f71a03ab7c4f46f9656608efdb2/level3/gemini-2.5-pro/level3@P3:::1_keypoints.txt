- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a practically important problem.  
- Builds on A*-based search over CPDAGs (EC tree) and handles ancestral constraints by pruning EC-tree nodes and projecting constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints.  
- Projected constraints are supplied to the heuristic oracle (URLEARNING) to tighten bounds and guide search.  
- Empirically reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and observes an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as constraint count varies.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem with practical relevance for incorporating causal background knowledge.  
- Strength: elegant, novel combination of pruning the search space and strengthening the heuristic to handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Strength: strong empirical speedups suggesting promise of the search-based approach.  
- Weakness: key pruning lemma (Lemma 3/Theorem 1) lacks a clear, complete proof; the claim that EC tree edge generation preserves certain edges is non-obvious.  
- Weakness: "Projecting Constraints" section is confusing and conflates entailment with consistency; it may add non-entailed constraints that prune valid DAGs.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation is described too abstractly, lacking sufficient detail for reproduction or full understanding.  
- Weakness: ILP baseline (GOBNILP) is weak in the unbounded parent-set setting, so speedups may reflect baseline limitations rather than the proposed method's handling of ancestral constraints.  
- Weakness: compared ILP encoding for ancestral constraints is outdated/weak; stronger ILP formulations using explicit ancestry variables and transitivity constraints were not considered.  
- Missing related work: fails to cite/compare against van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015) and CP-based solvers that often outperform GOBNILP and can naturally handle complex constraints.  
- Overall: promising and elegant idea but undermined by unclear technical details and an unconvincing empirical evaluation, leaving the contribution not fully convincing.
