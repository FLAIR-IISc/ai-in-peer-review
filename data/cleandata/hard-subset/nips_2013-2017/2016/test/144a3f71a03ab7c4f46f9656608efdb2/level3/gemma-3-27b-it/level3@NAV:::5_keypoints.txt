- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints.  
- Core contribution: Extends Chen et al. framework to handle ancestral constraints by rephrasing the problem as a MaxSAT formulation.  
- Key technical idea: Infer decomposable constraints from ancestral ones to enable use of decomposable-score oracles.  
- Theoretical support: Entailment theorems justify the projection from ancestral to decomposable constraints.  
- Presentation: Technical approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Empirical results: Orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based methods.  
- Experimental design: Thorough evaluation across network size, dataset size, and constraint density, with SHD analysis for accuracy.  
- Limitation: Reviewer has limited expertise in this subarea, requiring some reliance on authors’ claims.  
- Comparison gap: Paper needs more comprehensive comparisons to other non-decomposable-constraint methods (e.g., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos’ path-constraint work).  
- Suggested improvement: Better contextualization and discussion relating to alternative constraint-handling approaches.  
- Acceptance arguments: Significant speedups, clear technical contribution, thorough experiments, and relevance to real-world problems.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
