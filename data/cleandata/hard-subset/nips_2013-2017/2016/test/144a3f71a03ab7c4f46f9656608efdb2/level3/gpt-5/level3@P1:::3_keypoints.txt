- Main contribution: extends EC-tree and decomposable-score oracle to handle non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints via EC-tree pruning and safe projection to decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Edge-projection: derives negative and some positive edge implications from closure of ancestral constraints (Theorems 2–3); positive-edge rule (Theorem 3) is strong and may fire rarely when constraints are sparse.  
- Ordering-projection: uses a MaxSAT formulation to infer ordering constraints enforcing acyclicity and transitivity (Theorem 4).  
- MaxSAT acyclicity rule unclear: Rule 3’s symmetric premise vs. asymmetric conclusion needs a proof sketch or illustrative example to show it prevents cycles.  
- Handling symmetric negative knowledge unclear: Rule 4 infers Y < X from X 6 Y but seems to preclude expressing mutual non-ancestry—clarify how mutually non-ancestral pairs are represented.  
- Theorem 1 wording ambiguous: “P satisfies A iff its descendants satisfy A” needs a precise definition of “satisfies” for CPDAGs (should reference existence in class(P)).  
- Empirical results: EC-tree with projected constraints is often orders of magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially as n grows; reports an easy–hard–easy trend and accuracy gains from moderate background knowledge.  
- Fairness of GOBNILP comparison debatable: ILP strengths (family-size bounds, constraint types) differ, potentially explaining large speed gaps.  
- Missing ablations: need experiments separating EC-tree pruning only vs. pruning+edge projection vs. pruning+ordering projection to quantify each component’s contribution.  
- Quantify projections: report how many edge and ordering implications are inferred as a function of constraint density p (empirically quantify positive-edge inference frequency).  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and organized, but include minor wording fixes (e.g., optimize=minimize, avoid “omniscient oracle”).  
- Acyclicity encoding: should be explicitly motivated and accompanied by a small worked example for reader intuition.  
- Scope claims need moderation: the 10–25% ancestral-constraint density claim should be toned down or supported by citations.  
- Originality: moderate — novelty is in combining EC-tree pruning with safe projection/MaxSAT to make non-decomposable ancestral constraints usable in score-based optimal BN learning.  
- Significance: useful for practitioners with trusted path knowledge, but less applicable when latent confounders or MAGs are relevant; practical benefit depends on availability and accuracy of ancestral constraints.  
- Robustness concerns: limited discussion of mis-specified or noisy constraints and how the method behaves when constraints are wrong; consider soft-constraint approaches.  
- Theorem/encoding clarifications needed: tighten theoretical statements (Theorem 1, acyclicity rule) to remove ambiguity.  
- Baselines to add: compare to greedy/equivalence-class search (e.g., GES variants), local search with penalties, and constraint-based methods that accept path constraints (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).  
- MaxSAT solver properties: specify whether MaxSAT is solved to optimality and what happens if the solver returns suboptimal solutions.  
- Overall assessment: solid, careful contribution with promising empirical speedups; with clearer theoretical justification for ordering constraints and broader/ablative experiments, it would be a strong conference paper.
