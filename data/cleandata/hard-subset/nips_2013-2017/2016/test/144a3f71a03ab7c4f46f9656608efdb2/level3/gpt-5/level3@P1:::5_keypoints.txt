- Problem: Enforces non-decomposable ancestral constraints in exact Bayesian network structure learning, which mainstream decomposable-score methods cannot handle directly.
- Core idea: Combine EC-tree pruning with projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by a decomposable-score oracle to produce strong admissible A* heuristics.
- EC-tree pruning: Prunes CPDAG nodes when no DAG in the equivalence class satisfies ancestral constraints, using efficient necessary checks and falling back to enumeration when inconclusive.
- Edge projection: Provides necessary and sufficient tests (Theorems 2–3) to infer entailed negative and positive edges using ancestor/descendant closures and forbidding mediating paths.
- Ordering projection: Encodes ordering inference as a MaxSAT problem to maximize entailed order relations under transitivity and acyclicity, with Theorem 4 giving sufficient entailment conditions.
- Integration: Projected edge and ordering constraints tighten the decomposable oracle (URLEARNING), yielding stronger admissible heuristics while preserving optimality.
- Theoretical soundness: Correctness arguments and theorems justify pruning and edge-projection; MaxSAT formulation is reasonable though some theoretical details are limited.
- Empirical performance: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP), especially as the number of variables increases and when projected constraints are provided.
- Empirical patterns: Shows an easy–hard–easy behavior as constraint proportion varies and generally improves SHD with more data and constraints.
- Relation to prior work: Novelly enables non-decomposable ancestral constraints in EC-tree/A* search unlike order-graph methods, and differs from ILP/CP by delegating global reasoning to pruning plus projection.
- Originality: Novel and elegant combination of EC-tree pruning with projection to decomposable constraints and new use of MaxSAT for ordering inference.
- Practical significance: Useful for practitioners with prior causal knowledge; projection approach could generalize to other non-decomposable priors.
- Quality concerns: Some implementation and algorithmic details omitted; complexity bounds for key subroutines are not analyzed.
- Scalability concerns: Experiments limited to n ≤ 20; unclear behavior at n≈25–30 and impact of parent-set size caps.
- CPDAG enumeration: Frequency and cost of fallback CPDAG-class enumeration are not quantified, raising scalability questions.
- MaxSAT questions: No reported sizes of MaxSAT instances, solver sensitivity, or guarantees on maximality of the resulting ordering set O.
- Baselines and ablations: Missing comparisons to CP-based methods, ILP variants, order-graph A*, and ablation studies (edge-only vs order-only projection; heuristic effect).
- Robustness: Handling of inconsistent/noisy (soft) ancestral constraints and whether MaxSAT can support soft constraints is not addressed.
- Reproducibility: Some inference-rule details and proofs omitted; code/data and full algorithmic details are requested.
- Overall assessment: Strong, exact, and practically impactful contribution with convincing speedups; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications on scalability and projection details.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate (familiar with BN structure learning and ILP/A* literature; less familiar with EC-tree internals).
