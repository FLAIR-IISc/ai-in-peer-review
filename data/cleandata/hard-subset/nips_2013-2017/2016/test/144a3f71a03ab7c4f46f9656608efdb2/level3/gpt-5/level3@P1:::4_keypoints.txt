- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral/path constraints, an important practical class of causal background knowledge.
- Builds on BN-graph and EC-tree shortest-path frameworks to search DAG/CPDAG space while using a decomposable-score oracle.
- Main technical novelty: “projection” method that infers decomposable constraints (edges and pairwise orders) from ancestral constraints to tighten the oracle.
- Provides sound pruning rules for CPDAG nodes in the EC tree under ancestral constraints.
- Gives efficient algorithms to infer all entailed edge constraints via α(·)/β(·) reachability sets (Theorems 2–3).
- Introduces a MaxSAT formulation to extract a maximally large acyclic set of order constraints consistent with ancestry (Theorem 4).
- Empirically, EC-tree with projected constraints is orders-of-magnitude faster than an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks, especially for larger n.
- Projection is shown to be crucial for EC-tree efficiency and often substantially reduces SHD to ground truth, sometimes matching large data increases.
- Relation to prior work: extends methods that cannot encode ancestry (DP/order-graph/A*) and complements ILP/CP approaches that encode non-decomposable constraints but scale poorly.
- Novelty claim: projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints to tighten a decomposable oracle appears new, as do the α/β tests and MaxSAT ordering extraction.
- Theoretical quality: pruning and entailment results are sound and well-motivated, but completeness/complexity of α/β-closure and enumeration costs are deferred and need analysis.
- Empirical quality: speedups are compelling, but missing ablations (no projection, edge-only, order-only), MaxSAT overheads, and A* expansion statistics to clarify the easy–hard–easy pattern.
- Clarity: paper is well written; suggests dropping Section 4.1 to expand on the A* + oracle interface and fast CPDAG path tests to reduce class(P) enumeration.
- Specific concern 1: need evidence that projected constraints help other learners (order-graph DP, A*, CP, alternative ILP) beyond EC-tree.
- Specific concern 2: easy–hard–easy behavior needs deeper analysis on how constraint proportion p affects pruning, heuristic tightness, and branching/expansions.
- Specific concern 3: report ILP accuracy/SHD and/or best feasible solutions at timeout to compare optimality and anytime behavior.
- Specific concern 4: discuss robustness to noisy/inconsistent ancestral knowledge and whether projection/MaxSAT can be softened (e.g., penalties) while preserving admissibility.
- Specific concern 5: report projection costs (edge entailment and MaxSAT wall-clock and fraction of total) and provide complexity bounds for α/β closure and Theorems 2–3.
- Pros: practically impactful problem, elegant theory, novel projection technique, strong empirical speedups, good writing and relevance to prior work.
- Cons: limited evidence of generality beyond EC-tree, sensitivity to amount of domain knowledge not fully analyzed, missing ablations/overhead/accuracy metrics, and deferred algorithmic detail.
