- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a challenging and important problem.  
- Proposes a branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, building on the EC tree framework (Chen et al., 2016).  
- Core idea: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to prune search and tighten an oracle heuristic (URLearning).  
- Novelty: integrates global, non-decomposable constraints into a decomposable-score search framework in a clever way.  
- Empirical claim: shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over the ILP solver GOBNILP.  
- Technical concern: Lemma 3’s proof contains an unsubstantiated claim about EC tree edge generation that needs rigorous justification.  
- Conceptual concern: treatment of inferred ordering constraints is confusing—“can infer” suggests added constraints are only consistent with, not entailed by, the ancestral knowledge.  
- Reproducibility issue: the MAXSAT formulation for deriving constraints is under-specified and hard to reproduce.  
- Baseline selection concern: unclear if GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints or its weakness with unbounded parent sets.  
- Comparative weakness: authors use a known-weak MAXSAT-based ILP encoding (Cussens, 2008); a tighter ILP baseline is missing.  
- Literature omission: fails to compare with relevant constraint programming approaches (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann CP 2015) that may outperform GOBNILP.  
- Overall: idea is promising but hampered by clarity, theoretical justification, and experimental rigor issues that must be addressed.
