- Proposes a novel method to learn Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by translating them into ordering constraints.
- Integrates the ordering constraints into an EC-tree score-based algorithm to learn optimal networks.
- Uses a MaxSAT solver for the mapping from ancestral to ordering constraints.
- Evaluated against a modified Gobnilp and shows significant efficiency improvements.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian networks.
- Novelty lies in handling non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a challenging problem in structure learning.
- Experiments are extensive across benchmark datasets and demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.
- Impact limited by an artificial problem setting and omission of methods that handle unobserved confounders.
- MaxSAT-based mapping may not scale well to large problems.
- Some methodological rules (e.g., acyclicity rule) and exclusion of certain constraints raise concerns.
- Only a few ancestral constraints are likely available in practice, potentially limiting search-space reduction.
- Comparison to Gobnilp may be unfair since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this specific problem; suggest comparing to greedy baselines like GES.
- Minor wording and clarity issues (e.g., use of “oracle” and “omniscient oracle”) need correction.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability, fairness of comparisons, rule clarifications, and wording.
