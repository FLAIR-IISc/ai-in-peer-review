- Problem & contribution: Addresses exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by pruning the EC-tree and projecting constraints into decomposable forms for a decomposable-score oracle.
- Key technical ideas: Sound edge-entailment rules (Theorems 2–3) and a MaxSAT formulation to extract safe ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Chen et al.’s non-decomposable framework and contrasts with DP/A*, ILP/CP, path-constraint, and MaxSAT-based BN methods.
- Empirical results: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard benchmarks, with larger gains from projection and an observed easy–hard–easy trend as constraint density varies.
- Structural accuracy: Shows improved SHD with constraints as data size and proportion of constraints vary.
- Strength — motivation: Clearly motivated by practical causal/temporal prior knowledge (ancestral constraints).
- Strength — integration: Elegant integration of non-decomposable constraints into an EC-tree search that leverages decomposable-score oracles for pruning.
- Strength — projection: Technically neat projection approach with sound edge rules and a principled MaxSAT ordering extraction.
- Strength — empirical rigor: Strong empirical findings and informative analysis (constraint density effects, runtime gains).
- Strength — positioning: Good connections to prior frameworks and a reasonable ILP baseline for comparison.
- Weakness — pruning completeness/scalability: When CPDAG path tests fail, the method may require exponential enumeration of class(P); frequency and mitigation strategies are unclear.
- Weakness — MaxSAT sufficiency: Ordering projection constraints are sufficient but not necessary; it’s unclear how much entailment remains unexploited in practice.
- Weakness — experimental scope: Evaluations limited to n ≤ ~20; more discussion on oracle and MaxSAT scaling/memory is needed.
- Weakness — baseline fairness: ILP baseline may be disadvantaged without indegree bounds; comparisons should include bounded-indegree and CP baselines.
- Weakness — robustness to noisy constraints: All experiments use constraints consistent with ground truth; handling misspecified or soft constraints is not explored.
- Weakness — generality: Results shown only for BDeu/discrete data; applicability to other scores, continuous/mixed data, or missing data is not addressed.
- Weakness — theoretical clarity: Complexity and completeness of Theorem 3 (positive edge entailment) and a proof sketch for Theorem 4 (MaxSAT sufficiency) are needed.
- Quality assessment: Technical claims are plausible and mostly supported, but omitted empirical details (CPDAG enumeration frequency, MaxSAT overhead) should be reported.
- Clarity assessment: Paper is generally clear; would benefit from an explicit algorithmic loop, MaxSAT instance-size reporting, and pseudocode for edge projection.
- Originality: Novel integration of ancestral constraints into the Chen et al. non-decomposable framework plus MaxSAT-based ordering projection.
- Significance: High practical impact; method likely useful to practitioners and to be adopted/extended.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important limitation, conceptually clean with theoretical backing, strong empirical speedups, advances EC-tree/oracle-guided search line.
- Cons for acceptance: Limited scalability evidence beyond n≈20, lack of CP baselines and robustness experiments, and unquantified risk of exponential CPDAG enumeration.
- Suggestions: Report CPDAG enumeration frequency/times, provide ablations for edge vs ordering projections and MaxSAT overhead, add bounded-indegree/CP baselines and robustness tests with noisy constraints, and release code plus MaxSAT instance details.
- Recommendation: Leaning toward accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
