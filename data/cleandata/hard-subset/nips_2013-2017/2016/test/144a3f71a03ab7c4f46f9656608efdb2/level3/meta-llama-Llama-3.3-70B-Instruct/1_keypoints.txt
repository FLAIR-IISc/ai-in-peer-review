- Presents a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes the EC tree search space for non-decomposable scores and leverages an oracle for decomposable scores.  
- Main contributions: sound and complete pruning of the EC tree using ancestral constraints, and inferring decomposable constraints to empower the oracle.  
- Paper is well-written and explains technical details clearly.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Extensive experiments demonstrate the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains versus ILP-based learning systems (GOBNILP).  
- Strength: significant contribution to structure learning with ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation confirming efficiency and effectiveness.  
- Weakness: GOBNILP comparison is weakened by a poor MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints; an ILP encoding might be fairer.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed time and space complexity analysis and broader comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Suggestion: compare to van Beek and Hoffmann’s approach, which often outperforms GOBNILP and could be adapted for ancestral constraints.  
- Overall: with minor improvements and additional analyses, the paper is a strong NIPS candidate.
