- Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework and extends it by inferring decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Provides clear theoretical results (Theorems 2–4) establishing how to infer decomposable constraints.  
- Empowers decomposable-score oracles by passing inferred constraints, enabling EC tree to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Claims orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based methods like GOBNILP.  
- Experimental design is problematic: Table 1 mixes “EC + new work” vs “GOB + new work’,” preventing isolation of the authors’ contribution.  
- Lacks the most relevant baseline comparison: EC with constraint inference versus EC alone.  
- Role and interpretation of Table 3 (scalability and accuracy) are unclear and don’t directly compare the core method to baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written with consistent notation, but the experimental section needs clearer presentation of compared components.  
- The constraint inference method is novel, well-motivated, and appropriately cited relative to prior work.  
- The problem is significant for practical applications; small amounts of ancestral constraints can substitute for much more data.  
- Significance is reduced by unclear experimental comparisons, making the magnitude of improvements hard to assess.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, sound theory, substantial efficiency gains, useful constraint inference method.  
- Arguments against acceptance: confusing experiments, missing EC vs EC+inference comparison, unclear Table 3, and insufficient explanation of why EC is preferable to ILP beyond speed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper but accept with major revisions to clarify experiments and include the missing EC-without-inference comparison.
