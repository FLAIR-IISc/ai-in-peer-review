- Addresses incorporation of ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning by inferring decomposable constraints for EC-tree/URLEARNING instead of ILP.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework and proposes using inferred decomposable constraints to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Main technical contribution is Lemma 3 enabling pruning with ancestral constraints, but its proof and justification are unclear and insufficiently explained.  
- Claim that EC tree edge generation implies certain edges (e.g., "Gk contains Z → W") is not well justified and needs elaboration.  
- Experiments show large speedups over GOBNILP when constraints are present, suggesting practical benefits.  
- Experimental comparison is weakened by using a suboptimal ILP encoding (Cussens 2008) rather than a tighter formulation with transitivity/A-I linking constraints.  
- Paper fails to compare against van Beek and Hoffmann (2015), a stronger baseline that could likely handle ancestral constraints.  
- Presentation and clarity are poor, especially around "implied/projected constraints" and whether inferred constraints are entailed or merely consistent.  
- The MAXSAT approach for ordering constraints is mentioned but not explained well enough to understand how ordering constraints are chosen.  
- Section 5 ("Projecting constraints") lacks clear algorithms and sufficient justification for how inferred constraints are identified; Theorems 2 and 3 lack context and practical explanation.  
- Originality: a non-trivial and practical extension of EC tree for ancestral constraints, but novelty limited by heavy reliance on prior work and incomplete engagement with recent literature.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and shows that modest background knowledge can greatly improve efficiency and model quality, but impact is limited by unclear theory and unfair baselines.  
- Pros: tackles a practical problem, demonstrates strong empirical gains, builds on solid theory, and shows background knowledge can greatly help learning.  
- Cons: key lemma inadequately justified, constraint projection poorly explained, unfair ILP comparisons, missing relevant baselines, and theoretical advance depends on prior work.  
- Recommendation: reject — promising empirical results but undermined by unclear technical justification, poor exposition of constraint projection, and insufficiently rigorous experimental comparisons.
