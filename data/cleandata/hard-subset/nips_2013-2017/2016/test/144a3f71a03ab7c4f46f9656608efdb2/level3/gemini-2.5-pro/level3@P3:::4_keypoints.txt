- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that standard decomposable-score learners cannot natively handle.  
- Main contribution: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge presence/absence and ordering constraints to empower a decomposable-score oracle in an A* EC-tree search.  
- Methods: algorithm to infer all entailed edge constraints and a MaxSAT formulation to find many entailed ordering constraints, tightening the A* heuristic and pruning search.  
- Empirical result: proposed method is orders of magnitude faster than a state-of-the-art ILP solver (GOBNILP) adapted for ancestral constraints.  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel, effective bridge between decomposable-score learners and rich non-decomposable domain knowledge.  
- Strength — technical quality: sound theorems, clever use of MaxSAT, and strong empirical evidence supporting efficiency claims.  
- Strength — clarity/presentation: well-written, organized, and explains problem, solution, and experiments clearly.  
- Weakness — generalizability: unclear whether constraint projection benefits methods beyond the A*-and-oracle EC-tree framework; authors should discuss or test broader applicability.  
- Weakness — sensitivity to constraint amount: EC-tree shows an "easy-hard-easy" runtime pattern as constraint density varies, unlike ILP’s monotonic improvement; requires explanation and practical guidance.  
- Weakness — incomplete comparison: SHD reported only for EC-tree; include SHD for GOBNILP to verify comparable solution quality.  
- Weakness — paper organization: condense BN graph preliminaries (Sec. 4.1) and expand implementation details of EC-tree A* and oracle (URLEARNING) to improve reproducibility.
