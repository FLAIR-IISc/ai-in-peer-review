- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints, which standard exact methods cannot natively handle.
- Contribution: Extends Chen et al.'s A* search over Markov equivalence classes (EC-tree) to efficiently incorporate ancestral constraints.
- Core technical idea: Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints to improve heuristics and prune search.
- Novelty detail: Uses MaxSAT to infer a maximal set of consistent ordering constraints — a particularly clever formulation.
- Empirical strength: Strong experiments showing orders-of-magnitude speedup over a state-of-the-art ILP baseline.
- Experimental thoroughness: Evaluation across varying network sizes, data amounts, and constraint densities.
- Significance: Enables practical, exact incorporation of expert background knowledge (ancestral constraints) in Bayesian network learning.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and clearly situates prior work.
- Weaknesses: May be seen as incremental extension of Chen et al.; reviewer has limited expertise with A* on EC-tree but finds empirical comparisons convincing.
