- Paper addresses Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by translating them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for an EC-tree + oracle framework.  
- Novel framework leverages EC tree search and a decomposable-score oracle to handle ancestral constraints efficiently.  
- Introduces a sound pruning method for the EC tree using ancestral constraints with theoretical backing.  
- Proposes a practical MaxSAT-based method to infer decomposable ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP, especially for n=12–20.  
- Small fractions (10–25%) of ancestral constraints substantially improve model accuracy, comparable to much larger increases in training data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains technical contributions, and situates work relative to Chen et al. (2015, 2016).  
- Strengths: theoretical soundness, clear constraint projection method, straightforward framework, and strong empirical performance.  
- Main weakness: problem setting is artificial—ancestral constraints in the assumed form are rarely available in practice.  
- Claim that 10–25% of ancestral relations is “small” is misleading because that fraction of n(n−1)/2 relations is extensive and uncommon in applications.  
- Comparison to GOBNILP is potentially unfair since GOBNILP wasn’t designed for ancestral constraints; should compare to more appropriate baselines (e.g., GES).  
- Technical clarification needed for Theorem 1: statement about CPDAGs and descendant DAGs is imprecise and should be corrected.  
- Acyclicity rule (line 267) is poorly named/explained and does not clearly capture acyclicity.  
- Theorem 3’s conditions for inferring edge implications are very restrictive, limiting practical utility of edge constraint inference.  
- Novelty is reasonable within optimal structure learning with ancestral constraints but broader impact on causal discovery (especially with latent confounders) is limited.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify theoretical statements, address practical applicability and baselines, and temper claims about required prior knowledge.
