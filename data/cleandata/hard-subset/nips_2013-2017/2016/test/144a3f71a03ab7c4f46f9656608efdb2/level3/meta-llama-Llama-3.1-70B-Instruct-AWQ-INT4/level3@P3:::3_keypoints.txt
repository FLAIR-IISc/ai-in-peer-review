- Proposes a novel method that translates ancestral constraints into ordering constraints and learns Bayesian networks with an EC-tree score-based algorithm.  
- Method evaluated against a modified GOBNILP and shown to be orders-of-magnitude faster, especially on larger networks.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explained, with a logical flow and strong organization.  
- Provides thorough technical details and explicit rules for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, using benchmark datasets and investigating how ancestral constraints affect search efficiency and scalability.  
- Strength: clarity and accessibility of the presentation.  
- Limitation: setting is somewhat artificial—ancestral constraints often motivate causal methods that account for unobserved confounders.  
- Limitation: comparison to GOBNILP may be unfair because GOBNILP was not designed for this specific problem, possibly inflating performance differences.  
- Limitation: approach may require many ancestral constraints to be effective, which may be unrealistic for real-world applications where few constraints are available.  
- Originality: the translation of ancestral to ordering constraints is a new and interesting idea, though it builds on prior work.  
- Significance: makes a notable contribution to Bayesian network learning with potential applications (bioinformatics, social networks, etc.).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address practicality and include comparisons to more appropriate alternative methods.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
