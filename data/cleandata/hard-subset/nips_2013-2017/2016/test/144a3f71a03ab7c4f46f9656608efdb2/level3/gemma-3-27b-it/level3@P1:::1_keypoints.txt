- Problem: Learn Bayesian network structures optimally under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints for an existing oracle and pruning the search space.  
- Motivation: Ancestral constraints are important in practice and typically break standard decomposable-score methods.  
- Prior work: Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and references core BN and constraint-learning literature.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: Introduces a new method to incorporate ancestral constraints via projection into decomposable constraints usable by an oracle.  
- Empirical gains: Reports orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over the ILP-based baseline (GOBNILP).  
- Theoretical soundness: Pruning step is supported by formal result (Theorem 1).  
- Clarity of problem: Problem statement and limitations of prior approaches are well articulated.  

Weaknesses:
- Proof clarity: Justification for Lemma 3 and the EC tree edge generation rule is insufficiently detailed.  
- Terminology ambiguity: “Can infer” about ordering constraints is unclear—entailed vs merely consistent needs distinction.  
- Constraint inference detail: MAXSAT formulation and rationale for using MAXSAT (vs simpler heuristics) lack explanation and specifics.  
- Baseline choice: GOBNILP may be a weak baseline; its poor performance could stem from unbounded parent sets rather than ancestral constraints.  
- Missing related work: Fails to discuss van Beek & Hoffmann (2015), a relevant CP approach often outperforming GOBNILP.  
- Scalability concerns: Experiments scale to n=20 only; implications for larger networks and complex constraints are not discussed.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses an important problem with a novel, theoretically grounded approach and strong empirical speedups.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Weaknesses in proof clarity, empirical baselines, missing related work, and insufficient constraint-inference detail hinder reproducibility and evaluation strength.  

Overall assessment:
- Valuable contribution but requires improvements in theoretical exposition, experimental comparisons, and detail on constraint inference.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions.  

Requested revisions:
- Provide a more rigorous proof/justification for Lemma 3.  
- Clarify whether inferred ordering constraints are entailed or only consistent.  
- Fully detail the MAXSAT formulation and justify its choice over alternatives.  
- Include or justify the exclusion of comparison to van Beek & Hoffmann (2015).  
- Discuss scalability limitations and future research directions.
