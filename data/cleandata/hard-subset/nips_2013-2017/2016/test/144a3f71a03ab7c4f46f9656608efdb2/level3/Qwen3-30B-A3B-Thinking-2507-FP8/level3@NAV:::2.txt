

This paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints by leveraging the EC tree search space and projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints that can be used to empower an oracle for heuristic search. The work builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework (2016) for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, extending it to handle ancestral constraints that are otherwise incompatible with mainstream approaches. The key innovation lies in the sound method for inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, which significantly improves search efficiency.

The paper demonstrates promising results showing orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP-based approaches like GOBNILP. However, the experimental evaluation contains significant clarity issues that undermine the claimed contributions. Table 1 compares (EC + new work) to (GOB + new work'), creating confusion about what exactly is being compared. The role of Table 3 is unclear, as it reports structural Hamming distance without properly contextualizing how this relates to the main efficiency claims. Most critically, the paper fails to provide an explicit comparison between (EC + new work) versus EC alone, which is essential given the claim that EC is fundamentally superior to GOB-based approaches. As the authors acknowledge, EC is "much better than GOB," making the comparison between (EC + new work) and EC alone the most relevant evaluation.

The technical contribution is sound and well-motivated, with clear theoretical foundations for the constraint projection method. The approach represents a meaningful extension to existing work on non-decomposable scoring in structure learning. However, the experimental section lacks the necessary clarity to properly validate the claimed efficiency gains. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a direct comparison showing how much the new projection method improves upon EC alone, as this would directly address the core claim about the value of the constraint projection technique.

Strengths: Novel constraint projection method, sound theoretical foundation, meaningful improvement over ILP-based approaches. Weaknesses: Confusing experimental setup, missing critical comparison between EC+new work and EC alone, unclear role of Table 3. The paper has potential significance for Bayesian network learning, but the experimental evaluation must be clarified to support the claims.
