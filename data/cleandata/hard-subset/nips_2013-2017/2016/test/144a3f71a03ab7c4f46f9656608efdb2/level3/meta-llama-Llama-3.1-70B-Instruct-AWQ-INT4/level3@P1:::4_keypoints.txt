- Proposes a novel method to learn Bayesian networks optimally with ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable constraints onto decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Uses the projection to empower an oracle-based structure search, building on a framework for non-decomposable scores.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theory, experiments, and a thorough problem review.  
- Experimental results report orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based alternatives.  
- Projection of ancestral constraints onto decomposable constraints is shown to substantially improve learning efficiency.  
- Concern: Table 3 improvements appear for EC trees but not for ILP, casting doubt on projection generalization to other solvers.  
- Request for evidence that inferred decomposable constraints improve performance/efficiency of methods that cannot natively handle non-decomposable constraints (e.g., ILP).  
- Observation: Table 2 shows an easy-hard-easy pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; authors should explain why and whether EC trees are particularly sensitive to domain-knowledge amount.  
- Suggest clarifying whether EC trees require careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints to achieve best performance.  
- Missing evaluation: Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees; add SHD comparison with ILP for a more complete assessment.  
- Suggestion to drop Section 4.1 on BN graph and instead expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree experiments.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, original, and potentially high-impact across applications like classification, clustering, feature selection, and causal discovery.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, solid technical basis, thorough evaluation, and convincing experimental comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Incomplete evidence of generalizability to other learning approaches and need for additional explanations/evidence to bolster claims.
