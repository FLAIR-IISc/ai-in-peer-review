- Main contribution: method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints (Section 5).  
- Novelty: enables incorporation of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Methodology: leverages a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores and empowers a decomposable-score oracle by passing inferred decomposable constraints.  
- Experimental result: approach is orders-of-magnitude more efficient than ILP-based alternatives.  
- Experiments also evaluate how ancestral constraints affect search efficiency and scalability.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written, clear, and provides enough detail for an expert to reproduce results.  
- Technical quality: paper is technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Originality/significance: novel combination of techniques that advances state of the art and is likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows improvement only for EC trees and not ILP, raising doubts about whether the projection generalizes to other learning approaches.  
- Suggestion: provide evidence that inferred decomposable constraints improve performance/efficiency of methods that cannot originally handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern: Table 2 exhibits an unexplained easy-hard-easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases; need explanation and discussion of EC-tree sensitivity to amount of domain knowledge.  
- Suggestion: include SHD comparison with the ILP approach (Table 2 currently reports SHD only for EC trees).  
- Suggestion: drop Section 4.1 on BN graph and expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree experimental setting for clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant contribution; accept with revisions to address evaluation generalization, explanation of observed patterns, and expanded methodological clarity.
