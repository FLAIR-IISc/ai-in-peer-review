- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and incompatible with many score-based algorithms.  
- Builds on EC-tree A* search over Markov equivalence classes to handle non-decomposable constraints by pruning violating CPDAGs.  
- Main technical contribution: projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to strengthen the oracle’s admissible heuristic.  
- Infers edge constraints directly and reduces ordering constraint inference to MaxSAT to find a maximal consistent set.  
- Empowering the oracle with projected constraints yields a tighter heuristic and more efficient A* search.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP.  
- Strength — Significance/Originality: well-motivated problem; novel method to translate non-decomposable constraints for exact solvers.  
- Strength — Technical quality: sound projection techniques and elegant MaxSAT reduction.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is generally well-written with clear motivation and technical detail.  
- Strength — Empirical performance: dramatic speedups against the ILP baseline.  
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is confounded and lacks a crucial ablation comparing EC-tree with vs. without the projection to quantify projection’s contribution.  
- Specific criticisms: Table 1’s comparison to GOBNILP+projection doesn’t isolate projection benefit; Table 2 still focuses on ILP; Table 3’s scalability analysis is useful but premature without the core ablation.
