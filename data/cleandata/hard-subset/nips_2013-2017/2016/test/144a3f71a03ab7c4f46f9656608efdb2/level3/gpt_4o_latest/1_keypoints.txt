- Presents a novel branch-and-bound approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using CPDAG search and A* guided by URLearning heuristics, projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.
- Strength — Technical innovation: introduces a sound and complete pruning method for the EC tree and a novel inference of decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Strength — Efficiency gains: empirical results show the method is significantly faster than existing methods, especially on larger networks and datasets.
- Strength — Scalability: the system scales to problems with up to 20 variables, matching state-of-the-art Bayesian network learners.
- Strength — Practical impact: incorporating ancestral background knowledge can reduce required data and improve model accuracy.
- Weakness — Lemma 3 clarity: the central Lemma 3 lacks an adequately explained proof, weakening theoretical rigor.
- Weakness — Projected constraints underexplained: the process and role of inferring projected constraints and how they empower the oracle are insufficiently detailed for reproducibility.
- Weakness — Missing related-work comparison: no comparison to van Beek and Hoffmann’s approach, which could be adapted for ancestral constraints, leaves evaluation incomplete.
- Weakness — MAXSAT formulation suboptimal: using MAXSAT to encode ancestral constraints is criticized as less efficient than a tighter ILP formulation.
- For acceptance — Tackles a challenging, relevant problem and contributes novel methods for ancestral constraints in Bayesian network learning.
- For acceptance — Demonstrates substantial empirical improvements and practical applicability in data-limited scenarios.
- For acceptance — Innovative integration of EC tree search and projected constraints enhances the field.
- Against acceptance — Theoretical gaps (e.g., Lemma 3) and underexplained projected-constraint inference hinder reproducibility.
- Against acceptance — Omission of a relevant related-work comparison (van Beek & Hoffmann) weakens the evaluation of novelty and effectiveness.
- Against acceptance — Reliance on a MAXSAT encoding suggests potential for further optimization with ILP formulations.
- Recommendation — Accept with minor revisions: address theoretical clarity, expand explanation of projected constraints, and include broader related-work and formulation comparisons.
