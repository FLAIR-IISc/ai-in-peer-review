- Introduces a novel method to learn Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable ones within an enhanced EC tree search.
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and extends the EC search space to infer decomposable constraints that enable effective heuristics.
- Empirically outperforms ILP-based methods (e.g., GOBNILP), showing orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.
- Original and practically relevant contribution by enabling ancestral constraints in structure learning.
- Demonstrates substantial efficiency gains compared to ILP approaches in experiments.
- Theoretical framework is sound with complete and correct pruning methods and a clear constraint-projection approach.
- Scales to problems of up to 20 variables, matching state-of-the-art capability.
- Experimental results presentation is unclear, with ambiguous interpretation of tables and unexplained trends (e.g., easy-hard-easy pattern).
- Missing direct comparison between (EC + new work) and EC alone, limiting assessment of the incremental benefit.
- Structural Hamming distance results are reported but not thoroughly analyzed, especially for p = 1.
- Writing and organization need improvement, particularly in the experimental section and discussion of projected constraints on ILP.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers novel and sound theoretical contributions, and shows large empirical efficiency gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation of experimental results and missing direct baseline comparison to isolate contributions.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify experiments and add baseline comparisons.
