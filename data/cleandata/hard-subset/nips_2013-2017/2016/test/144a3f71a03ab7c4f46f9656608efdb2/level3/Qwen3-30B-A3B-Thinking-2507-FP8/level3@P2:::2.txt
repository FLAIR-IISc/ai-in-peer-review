

# Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper addresses an important problem in Bayesian network structure learning: incorporating ancestral constraints (specifying cause-effect relationships between variables) into optimal structure learning. Ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, making them incompatible with standard structure learning approaches that rely on decomposable scores. The authors build upon Chen et al.'s EC tree framework (2015, 2016), which enables optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, and extend it to handle ancestral constraints by inferring decomposable constraints (edge and ordering constraints) from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints. The authors demonstrate their approach is orders-of-magnitude more efficient than ILP-based methods like GOBNILP.

**Quality**: The technical approach is sound, with clear theoretical results (Theorems 2-4) for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints. The paper correctly identifies that ancestral constraints are non-decomposable and cannot be handled by standard structure learning methods that rely on decomposable scores. The authors provide a clear explanation of how to empower an oracle for decomposable scores by passing it the inferred decomposable constraints. However, the experimental design is problematic. Table 1 appears to compare "EC + new work" against "GOB + new work'" (where "new work'" refers to the authors' constraint inference method), making it impossible to isolate the impact of the authors' contribution. The paper does not provide a clear comparison between their full approach (EC + constraint inference) versus EC alone, which would be the most relevant comparison to demonstrate the value of their constraint inference method. The role of Table 3 is also unclear, as it reports results on scalability and accuracy but doesn't directly compare the core method against a baseline.

**Clarity**: The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the problem, the EC tree framework, and the constraint inference method. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined. However, the experimental section would benefit from clearer presentation of the comparisons being made. The authors should explicitly state which components they are comparing (e.g., EC with and without constraint inference) and provide a direct comparison of these variants.

**Originality**: The paper makes a valuable contribution by showing how to incorporate non-decomposable ancestral constraints into the EC tree framework, which previously could not handle them. The method for inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints is novel and well-motivated. The paper appropriately cites related work, including the EC tree framework (Chen et al., 2016) and constraint-based learning approaches that use ancestral constraints (Borboudakis and Tsamardinos, 2012, 2013). The approach differs significantly from previous work, as it addresses the specific challenge of non-decomposable constraints within an optimal structure learning framework.

**Significance**: The problem addressed is important for real-world applications where domain knowledge about cause-effect relationships is available. The paper demonstrates significant efficiency gains (orders of magnitude) over ILP-based methods, which is valuable for practitioners. The results also show that a small number of ancestral constraints (10%-25%) can have a similar impact on model quality as substantially more training data (e.g., increasing from 512 to 8192 samples), highlighting the value of incorporating background knowledge. However, the significance is somewhat diminished by the unclear experimental comparisons, which make it difficult to fully assess the magnitude of the improvement.

**Arguments for acceptance**:
- The paper addresses an important problem in Bayesian network structure learning with practical relevance.
- The method is technically sound and builds upon a solid theoretical foundation.
- The experimental results demonstrate significant efficiency gains over state-of-the-art ILP-based approaches.
- The constraint inference method provides a useful way to incorporate non-decomposable constraints into existing structure learning frameworks.

**Arguments against acceptance**:
- The experimental design is confusing and does not clearly isolate the contribution of the constraint inference method.
- The paper fails to provide the most relevant comparison: (EC + constraint inference) versus EC alone.
- The role of Table 3 is unclear, making it difficult to assess the scalability and accuracy claims.
- The paper does not sufficiently explain why the EC tree approach is better than ILP approaches beyond the speed comparison.

Overall, this is a strong paper with an important contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The technical approach is sound, and the results demonstrate significant efficiency improvements. However, the confusing experimental design and lack of clear comparison between the authors' full method and the EC tree without constraint inference are significant weaknesses that need to be addressed. I recommend acceptance with major revisions to clarify the experimental comparisons and provide the missing comparison between EC with and without constraint inference.
