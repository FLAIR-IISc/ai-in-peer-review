This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The authors propose a branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, leveraging symmetry breaking and a heuristic from URLearning. The key result enabling pruning with ancestral constraints is Lemma 3, which requires further clarification and justification.

The paper's main contribution lies in its ability to efficiently incorporate non-decomposable ancestral constraints into the structure learning process. The authors demonstrate how to project these constraints onto decomposable edge and ordering constraints, which can be used to empower the oracle and improve the efficiency of structure search.

However, several aspects of the paper require improvement. The treatment of implied and projected constraints, as well as the inference procedure for ordering constraints, is not clearly explained. The use of "can infer" is misleading without proper clarification that the authors add consistent but not necessarily entailed ordering constraints.

The MAXSAT approach used to control which ordering constraints are added is not sufficiently detailed, and the inference procedure and rationale behind it are unclear. Furthermore, the experimental comparison to GOBNILP shows large speedups, but it is uncertain whether GOBNILP's poor performance is due to ancestral constraints specifically or its general weakness with unbounded parent set size.

Additionally, the paper compares against a MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints, which is a poor ILP formulation. A tighter ILP using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and A–I linking constraints would be preferable. The comparison to a weak encoding weakens the empirical claim, and it is unclear whether the authors' method would still outperform a stronger ILP formulation.

Finally, the work of van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015) is not discussed, which often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to handle ancestral constraints. A comparison to this work would strengthen the empirical claim and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.
