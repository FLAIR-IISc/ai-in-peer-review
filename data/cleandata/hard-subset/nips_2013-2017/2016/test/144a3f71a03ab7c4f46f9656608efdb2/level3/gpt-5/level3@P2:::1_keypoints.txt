- Paper addresses exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by pruning the EC-tree and projecting constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints to guide A* search over CPDAGs.
- Builds on Chen et al.’s BN-graph/EC-tree framework and introduces edge entailment theorems plus a MaxSAT-based selection of ordering constraints to strengthen a decomposable-score oracle (URLEARNING).
- Empirically shows large speedups and better scaling versus a standard ILP (GOBNILP) encoding, with improved structural accuracy and an easy-hard-easy pattern as constraint count varies.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation: supports causal background knowledge (ancestral constraints) not handled by decomposable methods.
- Principled integration: pruning CPDAG nodes in the EC tree is a sound way to incorporate non-decomposable constraints into A*.
- Practical projection idea: converting ancestral constraints to implied edges/orders to strengthen the oracle is impactful and backed by crisp edge-entailment theorems.
- Strong empirical trends: orders-of-magnitude speedups vs. the chosen ILP baseline and demonstrated benefits from added constraints.
- Relevant and timely: extends recent exact BN learning work and connects to ILP/CP literature.

Weaknesses / Detailed concerns:
- Pruning lemma/theorem proof is unclear; the CPDAG extension step claiming an edge must exist needs explicit justification and illustration.
- Ordering projection is conflated between “entailed” and “selected consistent”; MaxSAT may be conservative and lacks formal guarantees about completeness or soundness.
- MaxSAT formulation lacks detailed rationale for hard clauses (especially clause (3)) and needs a proof sketch that selected orders won’t eliminate valid DAGs in G(A).
- Edge-inference algorithm is only sketched; needs pseudocode, complexity bounds, and clarity on transitivity/closure handling in ordering selection.
- Experimental baseline fairness: the ILP encoding used (Cussens 2008) is weak; stronger ILP/CP baselines (explicit ancestor vars, transitivity constraints, or van Beek & Hoffmann 2015 CP) should be compared.
- Confounding factors: unbounded parent set size favors heuristic A* over ILP; experiments should include degree bounds to isolate effects of ancestral constraints.
- Missing ablations separating effects of EC-tree pruning, projected edge constraints, and projected ordering constraints.
- Reproducibility: code, scripts, and exact encodings (including MaxSAT templates) are needed.

Quality:
- Overall technically sound, but key pruning proof and MaxSAT encoding require clearer proofs and guarantees; edge-entailment theory is well specified while ordering selection is less rigorous.

Clarity:
- Writing is generally clear, but some steps are elided; authors should expand proofs, add pseudocode, and consistently distinguish “entailed” vs. “consistent” orderings.

Originality:
- Novel integration of ancestral constraints into EC-tree/A* with projection to decomposable oracles and original edge-entailment theory.

Significance:
- Valuable advance if empirical gains hold against stronger ILP/CP baselines; enables exact BN learning with common causal background knowledge.

Suggestions:
- Provide full formal proof of the pruning lemma with CPDAG extension details and figures.
- Strengthen baselines: tighter ILP with A(i,j) variables and CP comparison.
- Add ablations for pruning vs. edge projection vs. ordering projection, and report node expansions and heuristic error.
- Clarify MaxSAT clause rationale, sensitivity to solver choices, and include pseudocode and complexity bounds.
- Report results under maximum in-degree constraints and share code/templates for reproducibility.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Fills an important gap by supporting non-decomposable ancestral constraints in exact BN learning with a principled algorithm and strong empirical gains.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Incomplete proofs and unclear MaxSAT guarantees, reliance on a weak ILP baseline, and insufficient ablations reduce confidence in theoretical and empirical claims.

Recommendation:
- Weak accept (borderline); promising work but needs tightened theory and stronger baselines to be a clear accept.
