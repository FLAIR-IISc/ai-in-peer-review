- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral constraints (non-decomposable background knowledge).  
- Method: Uses the EC tree search space plus an oracle for decomposable scores to navigate/prune DAGs violating ancestral constraints.  
- Novelty: Specifically targets non-decomposable ancestral constraints and combines EC tree with an oracle for efficient pruning.  
- Relation to prior work: Related to learning with non-decomposable priors/scores but distinct in focusing on ancestral constraints.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a thorough literature review.  
- Empirical results: Strong evaluation showing orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP-based frameworks (e.g., GOBNILP).  
- Analysis: Detailed experimental analysis highlighting how ancestral constraints impact learning efficiency.  
- Key theoretical concern: Proof of Lemma 3 (enabling pruning) is unclear and needs more detailed justification.  
- Constraint handling issue: Treatment of implied/projected and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained; “can infer” is ambiguous about entailment vs consistent addition.  
- MAXSAT concern: Use of MAXSAT to control added ordering constraints is not fully justified; inference procedure and rationale need clarification.  
- Experimental comparison weakness: GOBNILP comparison may be inconclusive—poor performance could stem from unbounded parent-set handling, not ancestral constraints specifically.  
- Suggested experiments: Compare against tighter ILP formulations and other alternative methods to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Missing related work: Does not discuss or compare to van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015), a relevant competing approach.  
- Overall judgment: Novel, promising approach but needs revisions addressing theoretical clarity and constraint-inference explanation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 7/10).
