- Paper addresses optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints usable by decomposable-score oracles.  
- Builds on BN graph and EC-tree search spaces (Chen et al.) using shortest-path/A* guided by an oracle for decomposable subproblems.  
- Main technical contribution: infer edge presence/absence via closure rules (Theorems 2–3) and infer safe topological orderings via a MaxSAT formulation with soundness guarantees (Theorem 4).  
- Projected edge and ordering constraints are fed to a decomposable-score oracle (URLearning/A* over order graphs) to tighten heuristics and speed up search.  
- Extends EC-tree pruning: prune CPDAG nodes if no DAG in equivalence class satisfies ancestral constraints, with enumeration of class(P) when quick tests are inconclusive.  
- The pruning criterion and projection theorems are sound; ordering projection is sufficient but not proven complete.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) in many settings and sensible SHD improvements as N and p increase.  
- Experiments explore effects of number of constraints p, dataset size N, and number of variables n.  
- Paper is generally clear and well written; helpful examples illustrate non-decomposability and theorems.  
- Presentation issues: placeholder references, stray characters, and an arguably unnecessary Section 4.1 on BN graphs.  
- Novelty: principled projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints appears original and well-motivated.  
- Significance: integrating causal ancestry into exact BN learning is important, and reported speedups suggest practical value and potentially broader applicability.  
- Strength: unified, principled framework enabling decomposable oracles to use ancestral constraints, with sound theory and strong empirical performance.  
- Weakness: limited evidence that projections broadly benefit other paradigms (mixed effects on ILP and limited oracle diversity).  
- Weakness: missing ablations—need edge-only, ordering-only, and combined projection impacts on ILP and another decomposable oracle.  
- Weakness: unclear whether projection time is included in reported runtimes for EC-tree and ILP.  
- Weakness: easy–hard–easy runtime pattern for EC-trees as p varies is unexplained; needs deeper analysis and practical guidance on sensitivity to p.  
- Weakness: SHD reported only for EC trees; need SHD for ILP to rule out accuracy/runtime trade-offs.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantification of CPDAG-class enumeration frequency, average class sizes, and runtime cost (caching/early stopping not detailed).  
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding and solver details, solver time breakdown, and contribution of inferred orderings vs edges are not fully reported.  
- Weakness: robustness to incorrect/noisy ancestral constraints not evaluated; framework’s handling of soft/uncertain constraints not discussed.  
- Recommendation: paper is solid and impactful; lean toward acceptance if authors address empirical generality, SHD comparisons, and analyses clarifying easy–hard–easy behavior and implementation details.
