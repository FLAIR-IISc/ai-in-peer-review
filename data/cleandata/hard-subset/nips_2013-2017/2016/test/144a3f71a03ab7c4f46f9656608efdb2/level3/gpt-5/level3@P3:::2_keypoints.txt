- Paper addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by searching CPDAGs with A* and projecting constraints into decomposable forms to guide an admissible oracle heuristic.

- Main technical contributions: sound CPDAG pruning rule, edge-constraint entailment procedures (Theorems 2–3), and a MaxSAT formulation to select a maximal, cycle-free set of ordering constraints (Theorem 4).

- Projecting ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints yields a tighter admissible heuristic that speeds up EC-tree search.

- Empirically shows substantial speedups over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) and ablation indicates projection materially helps EC search.

- Provides useful qualitative findings: easy–hard–easy behavior with constraint density and improved SHD with modest background knowledge.

- Experimental design and presentation are confusing; ablations do not cleanly isolate the projection’s effect on EC (need EC vs EC+projection side-by-side).

- Role and goals of Table 3 are unclear and should be explicitly tied to core claims (scalability/effect of constraints).

- Fairness to GOBNILP is questionable: clarify which projected constraints were added, whether they are safely entailed, and report GOB vs GOB+projection ablation.

- CPDAG pruning completeness relies on exponential enumeration fallback; report how often enumeration is needed, average class sizes, and runtime impact.

- MaxSAT overhead and benefit are not quantified; report number of inferred orderings, MaxSAT time fraction, and incremental speedups from edges vs orderings vs both.

- Proof coverage needs improvement: be precise about pruning completeness conditions and provide proof sketches or appendix for Theorems 2–4.

- Overall technically sound and novel: principled integration of non-decomposable constraints into exact EC-tree search with admissible heuristic tightening.

- Clarity is good for algorithms, but experimental section should be reorganized: state hypotheses, define configurations, present consistent ablation grid, and summarize numeric findings.

- Requested revisions: add controlled comparisons (EC alone vs EC+edge vs EC+ordering vs both), clarify Table 3, ablate GOBNILP with/without projections, and report projection/MaxSAT/enumeration statistics.

- Recommendation: lean toward accept after revision; strengthen empirical ablations and clarify complexity/implementation caveats.
