This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and pose a challenge to traditional structure learning methods. The authors propose a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints, which can be used to empower an oracle for searching the EC tree.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the theory and experiments. The authors provide a thorough review of the related work and position their contribution within the existing literature.

The main contribution of the paper is the method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, which is presented in Section 5. The authors show that this method can be used to improve the efficiency of structure learning by orders of magnitude compared to ILP-based approaches.

However, there are some concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, Table 3 shows that the proposed approach improves the efficiency of EC trees but not ILP, raising doubts about whether the projection generalizes to other learning approaches. The authors should provide evidence that the inferred decomposable constraints improve performance/efficiency of methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.

Secondly, Table 2 exhibits an easy-hard-easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases. The authors should explain why this is the case and whether EC trees are highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge compared to ILP. This could have implications for the tuning of the number of ancestral constraints to achieve ideal EC tree performance.

Thirdly, Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees. The authors should add a comparison of SHD with the ILP approach to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

Finally, the authors may consider dropping Section 4.1 on BN graph and instead expanding explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree experimental setting. This could provide more insight into the proposed method and its application.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints is new and significant.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The results have implications for the efficiency of structure learning and the use of ancestral constraints in Bayesian networks.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns and suggestions mentioned above.

**Arguments Pro:**

* Novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints
* Significant improvement in efficiency compared to ILP-based approaches
* Well-written and well-organized paper
* Thorough review of related work

**Arguments Con:**

* Limited generalizability of the projection method to other learning approaches
* Sensitivity of EC trees to the amount of domain knowledge
* Limited comparison of SHD with ILP approach
* Potential for improvement in explanation of EC trees and A* algorithm
