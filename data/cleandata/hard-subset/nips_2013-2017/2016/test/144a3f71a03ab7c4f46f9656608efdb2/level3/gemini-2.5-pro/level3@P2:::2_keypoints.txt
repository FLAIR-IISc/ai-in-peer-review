- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structure under non-decomposable ancestral constraints that break local score decomposability.
- Builds on EC-tree A* search over Markov equivalence classes using an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Core contribution: project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge presence/absence and ordering constraints that the oracle can use.
- Projection yields a tighter heuristic for A* search, pruning the search space and speeding up learning.
- Provides theorems and algorithms for projection, including a MaxSAT reduction to infer ordering constraints.
- Empirical results claim orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP and improved structural accuracy with even few ancestral constraints.
- Strength: novel, practical, and elegant approach for incorporating causal priors into exact structure learning.
- Strength: technically sound, principled, and builds on solid prior work.
- Weakness: experimental presentation is unclear and hides the central comparison (EC tree with vs. without projection).
- Important experimental comparison (EC tree baseline vs. EC tree + projection) is present but not highlighted as the main result.
- Framing issue: readers may incorrectly interpret the contribution as merely outperforming GOBNILP rather than demonstrating the projection technique’s effect.
- Suggested fix: reorganize experiments to present a direct table comparing EC tree (baseline) vs. EC tree + projection, then show comparisons to GOBNILP as secondary.
- Suggested fix: explicitly state each table’s purpose (e.g., scalability, effect on structural Hamming distance).
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem, novel and sound method, strong empirical gains.
- Argument against acceptance: confusing experimental presentation obscures the main contribution.
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on revising the experimental section for clarity and emphasis on the projection’s impact.
