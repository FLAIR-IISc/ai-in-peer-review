## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper addresses the important problem of learning optimal Bayesian network structures when incorporating background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. The authors build upon the recent framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, which is crucial as ancestral constraints themselves are non-decomposable. The core contribution lies in effectively re-framing the problem as a MaxSAT formulation, allowing for the inference of decomposable constraints that can then empower the underlying oracle used in the Chen et al. approach.

The technical approach is well-motivated and clearly presented. The authors convincingly argue why existing methods struggle with ancestral constraints and demonstrate a clever way to bridge the gap by projecting the non-decomposable constraints into a decomposable form. The theoretical justification, including theorems regarding constraint entailment, adds rigor to the work. The experimental evaluation is a significant strength. The reported orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based alternatives are impressive and strongly suggest a practical benefit to this approach. The systematic evaluation across different benchmark networks and varying dataset/constraint sizes provides a solid empirical foundation.

While my expertise in this specific area of Bayesian network structure learning is limited, the paper is remarkably clear and self-contained. The authors do a good job of contextualizing their work within the broader field and referencing relevant literature. The connection to MaxSAT is a novel and effective technique. One minor point is that the discussion of related work could be slightly expanded to include more recent advances in constraint-based structure learning with causal constraints (e.g., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos, 2012, 2013, mentioned in the paper but not deeply discussed). 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Significant speedup:** The empirical results demonstrate a substantial performance improvement over existing methods.
*   **Novel approach:** The combination of non-decomposable constraints, MaxSAT formulation, and oracle empowerment is a novel and effective strategy.
*   **Theoretical soundness:** The work is grounded in a solid theoretical framework with clear justifications for the proposed methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited scalability discussion:** While the experiments show scalability to 20 variables, a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential bottlenecks for larger networks would be beneficial.
*   **Dependency on Oracle:** The performance relies on the quality of the underlying oracle. Further analysis of the oracle's impact would be valuable.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The clear presentation, solid technical approach, and compelling empirical results make it a worthwhile addition to NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
