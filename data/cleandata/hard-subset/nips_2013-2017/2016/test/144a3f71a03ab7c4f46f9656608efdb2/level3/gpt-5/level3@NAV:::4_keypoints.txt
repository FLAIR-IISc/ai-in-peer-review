- Addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral (path) constraints; main contribution is a projection technique that infers decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to tighten A*/EC-tree heuristics.  
- Provides correctness results for EC-tree pruning (Theorem 1) and for projected constraints (Theorems 2–4), plus a MaxSAT reduction for ordering inference.  
- Experimental evaluation is systematic across benchmarks, data sizes, and constraint densities.  
- Results show large speedups for EC-tree search but little improvement for the ILP baseline (GOBNILP), leaving generalization unclear.  
- Reviewer requests evidence that projected decomposable constraints improve other learners (dynamic programming, order-graph A*, CP) and analysis of why ILP benefits little.  
- Technical core appears sound with clear entailment conditions and a MaxSAT construction for ordering inference.  
- Recommend dropping Section 4.1 (BN graph) to expand the EC-tree/A* setup and the oracle interface for clarity.  
- Suggest adding pseudo-code or more detail for the edge-inference procedure and the MaxSAT encoding to improve reproducibility.  
- Novelty: projection of ancestral constraints into edge/order constraints, especially the completeness of the negative-edge test and the MaxSAT ordering construction.  
- Significance: high if projection generalizes across learners, since ancestral knowledge is common and often non-decomposable.  
- Request to explain the easy–hard–easy pattern in Table 2 and whether EC trees are more sensitive to domain-knowledge amount than ILP, including practical guidance on tuning p.  
- Request to report SHD for ILP alongside EC trees (Table 2 and elsewhere) to compare accuracy under time/memory limits.  
- Request ablation studies showing oracle node expansions, heuristic tightness, and runtime with/without projected edge vs ordering constraints across learners.  
- Pros: clear formulation, novel projection, strong EC-tree speedups, soundness guarantees, and good related-work positioning.  
- Cons: limited evidence of generalization beyond EC trees, missing SHD comparisons for ILP, limited analysis of constraint-density effects, and space spent on BN graph that could be reallocated.
