- Introduces a novel framework for incorporating ancestral constraints into Bayesian network learning by translating non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ordering constraints.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to infer ordering constraints, making implied constraints explicit and aiding search efficiency.  
- Integrates inferred constraints into the EC tree search space, enabling efficient pruning and strengthening the oracle used by the search.  
- Empirical results show large computational improvements over ILP-based methods like Gobnilp, especially at scale.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear technical exposition, theorems, and detailed experiments.  
- Problem setting appears somewhat artificial; real-world causal discovery methods might be more appropriate for leveraging background knowledge.  
- Ancestral constraints are typically sparse in practice, which may limit the method’s practical impact on search-space reduction.  
- Translation rules (e.g., for acyclicity) seem incomplete or inconsistent in some cases, raising theoretical concerns about general applicability.  
- Comparison to Gobnilp is favorable but possibly unfair since Gobnilp is not tailored to this problem.  
- A comparison with greedy methods like GES under oracle input would be a more relevant benchmark.  
- The method assumes extensive prior knowledge of ancestral constraints, which may be unrealistic in many real-world scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound solution with strong empirical speedups and interest for a niche BN-learning audience.  
- Arguments against acceptance: artificial problem framing, limited practical impact due to sparsity and assumptions, and needing stronger theoretical validation and benchmarks.  
- Recommendation: promising contribution but better suited to a specialized venue unless theoretical and practical limitations (translation rules, benchmarks, assumptions) are addressed.
