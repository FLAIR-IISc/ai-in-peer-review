- Paper proposes projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints to enable existing structure-learning algorithms to handle them.  
- Method builds on EC tree approaches (Chen et al.) and presents a sound projection technique (Section 5).  
- Theoretical contribution is sound and clearly presented.  
- Experiments (Table 1) show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods (GOBNILP) when using projected constraints.  
- Results indicate a small fraction of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match the accuracy gains of much larger increases in training data.  
- Paper is very well written; explanations of EC tree search space and the projection method are particularly strong.  
- Concern: Projection improvements appear significant for EC trees but not for ILP (Table 3), raising questions about generalization to other learning approaches.  
- Concern: Table 2 shows an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion increases; authors should explain this and whether EC trees are sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge.  
- Concern: Structural Hamming distance (SHD) is reported only for EC trees in Table 2; SHD comparisons with ILP are missing.  
- Suggestion: Section 4.1 on BN graphs seems redundant and could be dropped or shortened in favor of expanding EC-tree and A* algorithm explanations.  
- Requested revisions: provide evidence that projected constraints benefit ILP, explain the easy–hard–easy pattern and sensitivity, add SHD comparisons with ILP, and streamline/expand relevant sections.  
- Overall judgment: original and significant contribution but requires major revisions to demonstrate generalization and complete evaluation; recommend acceptance with major revisions.
