- Proposes learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints by projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints within the EC tree search space.  
- Extends Chen et al.'s EC tree framework (2016) to handle ancestral constraints incompatible with mainstream approaches.  
- Key innovation: sound method for inferring decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints to empower an oracle for heuristic search.  
- Theoretical contribution is sound and well-motivated, providing clear foundations for the constraint projection method.  
- Claims orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP-based approaches like GOBNILP.  
- Experimental evaluation has significant clarity issues that undermine the claimed contributions.  
- Table 1 comparison is confusing (mixes EC+new work vs GOB+new work'), making it unclear what is being compared.  
- Table 3’s role is unclear: reports structural Hamming distance (SHD) without contextualizing its relevance to efficiency claims.  
- Critically missing: no explicit comparison between (EC + new work) and EC alone, which is essential given the paper’s claims that EC outperforms GOB-based methods.  
- Strengths: novel constraint projection method, meaningful extension to non-decomposable scoring, and improvements over ILP approaches.  
- Weaknesses: confusing experimental setup, missing critical EC+new vs EC comparison, and unclear presentation of results (e.g., Table 3).
