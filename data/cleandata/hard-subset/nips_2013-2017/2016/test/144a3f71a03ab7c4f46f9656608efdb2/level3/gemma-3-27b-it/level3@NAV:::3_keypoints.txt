- Proposes a novel method to learn Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints by translating them into ordering constraints and using an EC-tree score-based algorithm with decomposable-score oracles.
- Uses a MaxSAT solver for the mapping and demonstrates significant speedups in experiments versus a modified GOBNILP.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and presents an intuitively appealing, novel contribution with solid related-work coverage.
- Practical significance is limited: the setting feels artificial and real-world causal discovery often requires handling unobserved confounders beyond restricting BN search space.
- Strong assumption of a perfectly known set of ancestral constraints reduces real-world applicability.
- Technical concerns about the MaxSAT-based mapping: rules (lines 257–261) need closer scrutiny, Rule 3’s asymmetry is problematic, and Rule 4 excludes “no-causation” constraints.
- Theorem statements (1 and 3) require clarification and refinement for mathematical correctness.
- Comparison to GOBNILP is potentially unfair since GOBNILP wasn’t designed for this problem; a baseline like GES adapted to use ancestral constraints would be more appropriate.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel integration of non-decomposable constraints, clear presentation, and demonstrated performance gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-world applicability, potential technical correctness issues, and unfair experimental comparison.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing technical issues and broadening discussion of real-world applicability.
