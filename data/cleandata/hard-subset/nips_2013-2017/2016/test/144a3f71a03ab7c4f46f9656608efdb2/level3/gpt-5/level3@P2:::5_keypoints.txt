- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with hard ancestral (path) constraints via EC-tree pruning and projection into decomposable constraints for a decomposable-score oracle.
- Uses EC-tree shortest-path view to prune DAGs/CPDAGs violating ancestral constraints, building on Chen et al.
- Proposes edge projection via closure over ancestral implications (Theorems 2–3) and ordering projection via a MaxSAT formulation (Theorem 4).
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and an easy–hard–easy trend as constraint density varies.
- Connects to prior exact BN learning (DP/A* order-graph and ILP/CP methods); projection for path constraints appears novel.
- EC-tree pruning rule is formalized (Theorem 1) and projection rules are plausible and sound under stated conditions.
- Key technical details are omitted: closure algorithm for maximal ancestral set and proof sketches/complexity for Theorems 2–4.
- Potential bottleneck: CPDAG-level tests can be inconclusive, possibly requiring exponential enumeration of class(P); frequency/cost not reported.
- Experiments are systematic (five benchmarks, varying n, N, p) with precomputed BDeu scores, but capped at n=20.
- Comparison to GOBNILP is appropriate, but CP-based baselines are not evaluated.
- Reproducibility gaps: ILP solver parameters, MaxSAT instance sizes, EC-tree node expansion statistics, and code/data availability missing.
- Clarity is generally good, but needs precise definition of “maximum set of ancestral constraints” and the closure inference rules and complexity.
- MaxSAT encoding needs more exposition: the “sufficient condition for acyclicity” and intuition for Theorem 4 should be elaborated.
- Request to quantify how often CPDAG-level ancestral tests are inconclusive and force full enumeration.
- Originality: novel combination of EC-tree pruning with projection pipeline and MaxSAT-based safe ordering projections.
- Significance: practically relevant for constrained exact learning on small-to-moderate networks; could influence practice despite scalability limits.
- Strengths: sound formulation, principled projection methods, strong empirical gains, and good alignment with existing literature.
- Weaknesses/questions: missing algorithmic proofs, possible exponential CPDAG enumeration, lack of CP baseline, and fairness concerns about ILP settings (no indegree bounds).
- Robustness concern: handling noisy or contradictory ancestral constraints and extension to soft/path priors not addressed.
- Scaling/detail requests: provide clause/variable counts and runtimes for MaxSAT, EC-tree node expansions, and heuristic accuracy with/without projections.
- Suggested improvements: add appendix with closure rules and proofs, include ablations (edge-only vs edge+ordering), report CPDAG/EC-tree stats, and release code.
- Minor editorial fixes needed: placeholder citations, stray non-English characters, and formatting artifacts.
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) with moderate confidence.
