- Proposes learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using EC trees and A* search, leveraging an oracle for decomposable scores.  
- Key innovation: infers decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to enable the oracle.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based approaches like GOBNILP, indicating ancestral constraints can improve efficiency.  
- Makes a valuable practical contribution by integrating background knowledge into optimal BN structure learning, a neglected problem.  
- EC tree representation yields a more compact search space than BN graph, aiding search efficiency.  
- Theorems 2–3 provide theoretical support for the constraint inference technique for edge constraints.  
- Empirical results are compelling: ancestral constraints reduce search space size and improve learning accuracy.  
- Lemma 3 (pruning with ancestral constraints) lacks a clear proof and needs stronger justification.  
- Explanation of how EC tree edges are generated (e.g., “Gk also contains edge Z → W”) is insufficiently detailed.  
- Treatment of inferred ordering constraints is misleading: authors add consistent but not necessarily entailed orderings without clarifying this.  
- The MAXSAT formulation for selecting ordering constraints is poorly explained and lacks rationale.  
- Experimental comparison is flawed: GOBNILP was compared using a weak ILP encoding (Cussens 2008) instead of a tighter formulation with transitivity, and the omission of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015) is a significant oversight.
