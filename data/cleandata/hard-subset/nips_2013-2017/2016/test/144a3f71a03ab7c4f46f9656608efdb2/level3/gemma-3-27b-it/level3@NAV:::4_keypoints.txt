- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, an important problem using domain knowledge.
- Core idea: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms usable by existing efficient algorithms.
- Novel extension of the optimal structure learning framework (builds on Chen et al., 2015) using decomposable-score oracles.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear exposition of theory and experiments.
- Technically sound and shows potential for significant efficiency gains, empirically outperforming ILP in some settings.
- Constraint projection is a compelling contribution that bridges expressiveness and computational tractability.
- Experimental improvements appear specific to the EC-tree framework; gains for ILP are less conclusive, raising generalizability concerns.
- Lack of evidence that inferred decomposable constraints help methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.
- “Easy–hard–easy” pattern in Table 2 is unexplained; EC trees seem highly sensitive to the proportion of ancestral constraints.
- Sensitivity implies careful tuning of the number of ancestral constraints may be required; this needs deeper analysis and comparison to ILP.
- Structural Hamming Distance (SHD) is reported only for EC trees; lacking SHD for ILP prevents comprehensive accuracy comparison.
- Section 4.1 on BN graphs feels peripheral and detracts from the EC-tree/A* focus; suggest expanding EC-tree and A* explanations instead.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear presentation, strong EC-tree empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evidence of generalizability, unexplained performance pattern, and incomplete SHD comparison.
