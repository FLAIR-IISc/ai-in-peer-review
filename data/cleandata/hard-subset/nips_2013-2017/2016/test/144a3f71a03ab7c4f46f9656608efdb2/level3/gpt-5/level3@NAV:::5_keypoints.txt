- Paper addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by searching BN/EC-tree spaces and delegating decomposable parts to an oracle.
- Main contributions: (i) safe EC-tree pruning via constraint-satisfaction tests (quick checks + CPDAG-class enumeration) and (ii) projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.
- Projection method combines a complete rule-based procedure for inferring edge presence/absence with a MaxSAT formulation to choose a maximal, cycle-free set of ordering constraints.
- Theoretical contributions include formal theorems for pruning and entailment of edge and ordering constraints.
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over an ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks.
- Experiments are careful about data generation and include ablation studies on the effect of projected constraints.
- Relation to prior work: extends Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and connects to ILP/CP encodings of path constraints and constraint-based causal prior literature.
- Concern: fallback to enumerating DAGs in a CPDAG class may be a bottleneck; worst-case cost and practical frequency of this step need quantification.
- Concern: fairness and tuning of the ILP baseline (e.g., indegree bounds, cutting planes) should be discussed and justified.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but some definitions (e.g., “maximum set” of ancestral constraints) need precise algorithms/pseudocode.
- Clarity: MaxSAT encoding should be presented concisely and include a complexity discussion; minor reference formatting issues exist.
- Originality: integrating ancestral constraints into the EC-tree/oracle framework and using MaxSAT projection appears novel compared to prior ILP/CP work.
- Significance: if speedups generalize, this is a meaningful advance for exact structure learning with background knowledge and causal discovery workflows.
- Limitations: experiments limited to n ≤ 20 and BDeu score; broader scales and scoring settings would strengthen claims.
- Pros summarized: principled integration of non-decomposable constraints, effective projection that speeds search, and strong empirical gains over ILP.
- Cons summarized: potential CPDAG-enumeration bottleneck, limited scale and scoring variety, and incomplete baseline-tuning details.
- Reviewer caveat: reviewer has limited expertise in this specific approach but finds the runtime improvements and conceptual clarity impressive.
