This paper presents a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. The authors tackle the problem of incorporating non-decomposable constraints, specifically ancestral constraints, into the structure learning approach of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. This is a significant contribution, as ancestral constraints are essential in practice for expressing background knowledge in the form of cause-and-effect relations between variables.

The authors' technical approach involves rephrasing the problem as a MaxSAT formulation with specific constraints. This allows them to leverage the power of MaxSAT solvers to efficiently search for optimal Bayesian network structures. The authors also propose a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, based on ancestral constraints.

One of the most impressive aspects of this paper is the empirical evaluation of the proposed approach against ILP-based alternatives. The authors demonstrate orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based systems, which is a significant advancement in the field. The experiments are thorough and well-designed, covering various scenarios and evaluating the approach's scalability.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the technical details of their approach. The text is clear, concise, and well-organized, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the subject matter. The authors also provide sufficient background information and references to related work, which helps to situate their contribution within the broader context of Bayesian network structure learning.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of techniques from constraint programming and Bayesian network structure learning. The authors' use of MaxSAT solvers to efficiently search for optimal Bayesian network structures is a unique contribution that sets their approach apart from existing methods.

The significance of this paper lies in its potential to advance the state of the art in Bayesian network structure learning. The authors' approach has the potential to be widely adopted in various applications, such as classification, clustering, feature selection, and causal discovery. The paper's findings on the impact of ancestral constraints on the efficiency of structure learning are also valuable, as they highlight the importance of incorporating background knowledge into the learning process.

Overall, this paper is a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The authors' technical approach is sound, and their empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing. The paper's originality, significance, and clarity make it a valuable addition to the literature.
