- Presents a method to incorporate ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into Bayesian network learning by projecting them into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.  
- Leverages efficient oracle-based learning frameworks (e.g., Chen et al.) to enable fast structure learning with these projected constraints.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear theory, experimental setup, and appropriate positioning relative to ILP and constraint-programming approaches.  
- Originality is strong: translating non-decomposable constraints into decomposable form for score-based optimal learning is a clever contribution.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups over ILP, demonstrating practical benefits of the projection approach.  
- Related work on path constraints is correctly cited; this work distinctively targets score-based and optimal-structure methods.  
- Significance: broadens applicability of efficient BN structure learning by enabling common domain knowledge (ancestral constraints) without costly ILP solvers.  
- Concern: Table 3 shows improvements for EC trees but not ILP, raising questions about the projection method’s generalizability across learning algorithms.  
- Authors should provide evidence that the inferred decomposable constraints improve efficiency for methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.  
- Concern: Table 2 shows an unexplained "easy-hard-easy" pattern as ancestral-constraint proportion increases; authors should explain this sensitivity and whether EC trees are especially affected.  
- Method may require careful tuning of number of ancestral constraints; this needs clarification and guidance.  
- Concern: Table 2 reports SHD only for EC trees; SHD for ILP should be included for a balanced accuracy comparison.  
- Section 4.1 (BN graphs) appears tangential and could be removed to focus the paper; expand EC-tree and A* algorithm explanation instead.  
- Recommend expanding details on how inferred constraints integrate into the A* search within the EC-tree framework.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying generalizability, explaining experimental patterns, and adding missing comparisons (e.g., ILP SHD).
