- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor or not ancestor of Y).
- Core contributions: (1) pruning EC-tree nodes inconsistent with ancestral constraints, (2) projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints to strengthen the A* oracle.
- Empirical claim: orders-of-magnitude speedups over the ILP baseline GOBNILP on several benchmarks.
- Clarity issue: presentation lacks sufficient technical detail, making soundness hard to assess.
- Theorem 1 (pruning justification) is inadequately explained and its proof is omitted; key reasoning about descendants/edge additions preserving or violating constraints is unclear.
- Projection method confusing: the paper conflates "inferring" entailed ordering constraints with adding a large set of consistent constraints, risking elimination of valid DAGs.
- MaxSAT formulation for deriving ordering constraints is presented without sufficient justification or analysis of its guarantees (entailed vs heuristic).
- Originality: builds on Chen et al.'s EC-tree framework; novelty is the idea of projecting non-decomposable constraints to empower a decomposable oracle.
- Missing comparisons: fails to discuss or compare with relevant recent work, notably the constraint programming approach of van Beek & Hoffmann (CP 2015).
- Baseline choice concern: using GOBNILP as the main comparator is questionable, especially in an unbounded in-degree setting where ILP methods scale poorly.
- ILP encoding critique: the ancestral-constraint ILP is based on a weak Cussens (2008) formulation; stronger ILP encodings using ancestorship variables and transitivity constraints exist and were not used.
- Experimental validity: reported speedups may reflect baseline weaknesses or encoding choices rather than superior handling of ancestral constraints.
- Strengths: tackles an important practical problem, proposes a clever/generalizable hybrid technique, and shows promising empirical performance.
- Weaknesses: significant clarity/theoretical gaps, unclear MaxSAT properties, incomplete related-work discussion, and an unconvincing experimental comparison.
- Overall verdict: promising idea but requires major revision to clarify theory, justify the projection method, and provide fairer/more complete empirical comparisons.
