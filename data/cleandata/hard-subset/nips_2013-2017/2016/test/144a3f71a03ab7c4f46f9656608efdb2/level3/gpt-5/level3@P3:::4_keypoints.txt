- Paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints usable by existing oracles.
- Builds on EC-tree/BN-graph shortest-path framework and prunes search space using ancestral constraints (Theorem 1 sound pruning).
- Main technical contribution: sound rules to infer negative/positive edge constraints from ancestral constraints (Theorems 2–3).
- Introduces a MaxSAT-based method to infer a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints from ancestral constraints (Theorem 4 gives sufficient conditions).
- Novelty: general method to project path constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for reuse inside decomposable-score learners.
- Strength: clear problem motivation—ancestral constraints are practical but non-decomposable.
- Strength: solid integration into EC-tree with substantial empirical speedups and orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP in many cases.
- Strength: presentation is clear; theory and experiments are easy to follow and implementable.
- Weakness: unclear generality—projection shows large gains for EC-tree but mixed/limited improvements for ILP, casting doubt on broader applicability.
- Request: demonstrate benefits of projected constraints across other methods (ILP/GOBNILP with runtime/cut stats, dynamic programming/order-graph, precedence-constraint DP, A* order-graph).
- Request: report counts of inferred constraints (edge/order) and correlate these counts with runtime reductions across methods.
- Concern: EC-tree shows easy–hard–easy runtime pattern as number of constraints p varies; need explanation and diagnostics (nodes expanded, heuristic errors, projected constraints vs p, pruning fractions).
- Suggestion: provide diagnostic plots and compare EC-tree behavior to ILP’s cut generation and LP gaps; discuss guidance for selecting p or adaptive projection strategies.
- Concern: SHD reported only for EC-tree; request SHD for ILP on common solved instances and investigate any mismatches.
- Scope/space: condense BN graph section and expand EC-tree/A* mechanics, exact heuristic/oracle interface, how projections are injected, plus edge-only vs ordering-only ablations.
- Request: expand MaxSAT encoding details—soft clause weights, solver settings, complexity, and justify the “maximally large set” objective.
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness to noisy/incorrect ancestral constraints and support for soft path constraints in MaxSAT/framework.
- Clarify completeness limits of projection rules—are there entailed edge constraints not captured? Prove G(A)⊆G(O) preservation for MaxSAT output.
- Request end-to-end statistics: number of inferred edges/orderings, percent parent sets ruled out, reduction in candidate orders, per-dataset.
- Fairness to ILP: ensure strong GOBNILP settings, report nodes/cuts/runtime, and consider CP baselines.
- Reproducibility: release code, experiment scripts, and MaxSAT instances.
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and potentially high-impact if authors strengthen generality evidence, add requested comparisons, SHD checks, and easy–hard–easy analysis; reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on addressing these concerns.
