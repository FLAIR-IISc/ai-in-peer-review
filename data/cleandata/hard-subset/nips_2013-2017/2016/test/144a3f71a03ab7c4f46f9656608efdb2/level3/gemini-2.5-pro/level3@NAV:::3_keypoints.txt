- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral constraints.
- Core contribution: translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ordering and edge constraints.
- Method: use a MaxSAT solver to make implied constraints explicit.
- Application: use decomposable constraints to prune an EC-tree search, enabling an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Relation: extends framework for learning with non-decomposable scores (Chen et al.).
- Strength: paper is clear, conceptually straightforward, and well explained.
- Strength: novel projection of ancestral constraints into a state-of-the-art optimal structure learning algorithm.
- Concern: significance limited because ancestral constraints often imply causal settings with unobserved confounders, which this score-based framework does not handle.
- Experimental concern: Gobnilp baseline may be inappropriate and performance gains could be exaggerated.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a constrained greedy search like GES modified to incorporate constraints.
- Practicality concern: experiments rely on unrealistically large amounts of prior constraints (10–25%); benefits for sparser, realistic constraint sets are unclear.
- Technical issues: Theorem 1 needs clarification about CPDAGs satisfying only a subset of consistent-DAG constraints.
- Technical issues: MaxSAT acyclicity encoding unclear—rule 3 yields an asymmetric conclusion from a symmetric premise, and rule 4 may wrongly exclude mutual non-causation constraints.
- Recommendation: addressing technical clarifications and providing stronger, more realistic experimental baselines would strengthen the paper.
