- Novelty: Introduces a new method projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, enabling use of decomposable score-based methods.  
- Method: Leverages the EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores to improve structure learning efficiency.  
- Empirical performance: Experiments show orders-of-magnitude computational gains over ILP-based approaches across diverse benchmarks and settings.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear theory and experiments; Section 5 clearly explains the constraint projection.  
- Significance: Addresses an important challenge in Bayesian network learning and could influence future work on incorporating background knowledge.  
- Limited generalizability: Improvements are mainly demonstrated on EC trees (Table 3), raising questions about applicability to other frameworks.  
- Sensitivity to constraints: Performance shows an easy-hard-easy pattern (Table 2), indicating high sensitivity to the number of ancestral constraints.  
- Incomplete comparisons: Table 2 lacks direct EC tree vs ILP comparisons, limiting assessment of relative strengths.  
- Irrelevant content: Section 4.1 on BN graphs appears unnecessary and distracts from the EC tree/A* focus.  
- Suggestion: Add direct EC tree vs ILP comparisons in Table 2 to better contextualize results.  
- Suggestion: Remove Section 4.1 and expand discussion of EC trees and the A* algorithm in experiments.  
- Suggestion: Discuss tuning strategies for the number of ancestral constraints to mitigate sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: Investigate generalizability to other structure learning frameworks beyond EC trees.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance if authors address concerns about generalizability and sensitivity.
