- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints for existing oracles.
- Core idea of empowering existing decomposable-score oracles with pre-processed ancestral constraints is reasonable and potentially impactful.
- Theoretical contribution: formal exploration of projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable forms and its effects on learning efficiency.
- Practical method: inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints, with a useful connection to MaxSAT for ordering constraints.
- Strength: well-motivated problem, solid theoretical foundation, and novel projection approach.
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is confusing and disorganized, undermining the claimed contributions.
- Table 1 is unclear about what “new work’” or other variants mean and which changes are being compared.
- Table 3 lacks clarity on which variables are being varied, and the role of structural Hamming distance is not explained.
- Crucial missing baseline: no direct comparison of “EC + new work” versus EC alone, which is necessary to demonstrate benefit from the projection.
- The claim that GOB is inferior to EC is insufficient; the paper must show that the projection meaningfully improves EC’s performance.
- The assertion of orders-of-magnitude speedup over ILP is not convincing without a transparent, focused evaluation.
- Overall assessments: technically sound (quality), generally well-written but experimental section poor (clarity), idea is novel (originality), and potentially significant if experiments are fixed (significance).
- Recommendation: weak reject pending major revisions; authors must add the EC-alone baseline and clarify experimental setup/results.
