- Addresses learning Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, a challenging and important problem.
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) by projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.
- Key technical contribution: novel projection method (Section 5) enabling existing decomposable-score oracles to handle ancestral constraints.
- Empirical contribution: EC tree approach with projected constraints achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods (e.g., GOBNILP).
- Paper is well-written, technically precise, and situates itself clearly within prior work.
- Main strength: elegant, practical solution to incorporate common domain ancestral constraints into optimal structure learning.
- Experiments (Tables 1–3) are convincing about speed improvements across sizes and constraint proportions.
- Concern: Table 2 shows ILP (GOBNILP) does not benefit from projected constraints like EC tree, so generalization to other frameworks is unclear.
- Concern: Unexplained easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion varies, suggesting sensitivity of EC trees to amount of domain knowledge.
- Concern: SHD reported only for EC tree; need SHD comparison with ILP to ensure speedups do not degrade model quality.
- Presentation issue: Section 4.1 on BN graphs seems redundant and could be removed to expand EC tree/A* explanation in experiments.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address generalization to other frameworks, explain the easy–hard–easy pattern, and include SHD comparisons with ILP.
