- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian networks under non-decomposable ancestral constraints is challenging for score-based methods.  
- Method: Translate ancestral constraints into ordering constraints via a MaxSAT solver and use them in an EC-tree score-based search.  
- Empirical claim: The proposed method outperforms a modified Gobnilp implementation by orders of magnitude.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, technically clear, and the constraint-translation idea is novel and of interest to BN structure-learning specialists.  
- Theoretical foundation: EC-tree search adaptation is well-presented, but several formal issues need clarification.  
- Theorem concerns: Theorem 1’s “iff” is incorrect (CPDAG constraints are a subset of DAG constraints); Theorem 3 offers limited positive implications when constraints are sparse.  
- MaxSAT encoding issues: The encoding labeled “acyclic” is misleading and rules (lines 257–261) contain logical inconsistencies.  
- Baseline concern: Comparison to Gobnilp is questionable since Gobnilp wasn’t designed for this problem; greedy baselines like GES with ancestral constraints would be more appropriate.  
- Practical relevance: Limited—ancestral constraints often arise with unobserved confounders (not addressed), and 10–25% constraint availability may not sufficiently reduce search space.  
- Minor issues: Several wording/typo problems throughout (e.g., lines 30, 38, 90, 310).
