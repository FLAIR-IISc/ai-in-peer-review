- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structure with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break local-score-based algorithms.  
- Main contribution: Project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable required/forbidden edges and ordering constraints to tighten A* heuristics.  
- Method: Logical inference for edge constraints and a MaxSAT formulation to infer maximal consistent ordering constraints.  
- Integration: Uses projected constraints to "empower the oracle" in an EC-tree A* search framework for exact learning.  
- Empirical claim: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over a competitive ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on benchmarks.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel and significant bridge between non-decomposable domain knowledge and decomposable search heuristics.  
- Strength — Technical quality: Sound theorems characterizing safe inferences; appropriate MaxSAT formulation.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, organized paper with clear motivation and methodology.  
- Strength — Empirical results: Thorough experiments convincingly showing large practical speedups.  
- Weakness — Generalizability: Projection benefits shown mainly for EC-tree; limited impact on ILP and unclear applicability to other algorithms (e.g., DP).  
- Weakness — Easy-hard-easy pattern: Observed performance trend is noted but lacks deeper analysis and explanation.  
- Weakness — Incomplete comparison: SHD reported for proposed method but not explicitly for the ILP baseline as a sanity check.  
- Weakness — Presentation: Section on BN graph could be condensed into EC-tree discussion to free space for more A* implementation details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses important problem, offers novel technically sound method, shows dramatic empirical improvements, and is well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Main contribution demonstrated primarily within one algorithmic framework and experimental analysis could be strengthened.  
- Conclusion: Strong paper with minor addressable weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
