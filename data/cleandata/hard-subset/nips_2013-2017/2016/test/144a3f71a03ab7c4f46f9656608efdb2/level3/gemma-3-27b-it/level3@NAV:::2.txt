## Review of "Learning Bayesian Networks with Ancestral Constraints"

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian network structures optimally when incorporating background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. The authors build upon a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, focusing on how to leverage oracles designed for decomposable scores by projecting the non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms. The core idea – empowering existing oracles with pre-processed constraints – is a reasonable and potentially impactful approach. The paper is well-motivated, connecting the problem to practical applications where prior knowledge is available, and correctly identifies the limitations of standard decomposable-score approaches. 

The strength of this work lies in the theoretical exploration of projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints and demonstrating how this impacts the efficiency of structure learning. The proposed method for inferring edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints seems promising, and the connections made to MaxSAT for the latter are a nice touch. 

However, the experimental evaluation is quite confusing and hinders a clear assessment of the contributions. Table 1 compares "EC + new work" to "GOB + new work'", and it’s unclear what “new work’” entails – is it just the constraint projection, or other improvements to GOBNILP? The role of Table 3 is also murky; it's not readily apparent *what* is being varied (beyond n and p) to observe the scaling behavior, and the relevance of the structural Hamming distance isn't fully explored. Critically, the paper *lacks a direct comparison of "EC + new work" versus EC alone*. This is the most important experiment to demonstrate the benefit of the proposed constraint projection. While the authors argue GOB is inferior to EC, the value of their contribution hinges on showing that adding the projection significantly improves EC’s performance. The claim that their approach is orders of magnitude faster than ILP is only persuasive with a more transparent and focused evaluation.

**Quality:** Technically sound, but the empirical evaluation weakens the support for claims. The completeness is questionable without the key EC-alone baseline.
**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, but the experimental section is disorganized and difficult to interpret.
**Originality:** The idea of projecting constraints is novel and a sensible direction to explore.
**Significance:** If the experimental shortcomings can be addressed, this work could be significant. Efficiently incorporating prior knowledge into Bayesian network structure learning is an important problem.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem.
*   Presents a novel approach to incorporate non-decomposable constraints.
*   Theoretical foundation appears solid.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Confusing and incomplete experimental evaluation.
*   Missing crucial baseline comparison (EC alone).
*   Claims of significant speedup are not fully justified by the presented results.



I recommend a **weak reject** pending major revisions. The authors *must* include the requested EC-alone baseline and clarify the experimental setup and results to convincingly demonstrate the benefits of their approach.
