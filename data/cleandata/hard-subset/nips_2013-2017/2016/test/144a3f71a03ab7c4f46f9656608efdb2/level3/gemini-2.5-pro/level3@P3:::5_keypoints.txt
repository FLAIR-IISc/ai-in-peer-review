- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X is/is-not ancestor of Y).  
- Challenge: ancestral constraints break decomposability required by standard score-based learning heuristics.  
- Framework: builds on Chen et al.'s A* search over Markov equivalence classes (EC tree) using a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Main contribution 1: prune EC-tree nodes directly that violate ancestral constraints.  
- Main contribution 2: project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints consumable by the oracle.  
- Ordering inference: reduce inferring ordering constraints to a MaxSAT problem and solve with a MaxSAT solver.  
- Impact of projection: inferred decomposable constraints substantially improve heuristic accuracy and pruning power.  
- Empirical result: orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on benchmarks.  
- Ablation: experiments show projected constraints are essential for the performance gains.  
- Scalability: competitive exact-learning scalability up to 20 variables in experiments.  
- Practical value: a few ancestral constraints can improve model accuracy as much as much larger increases in dataset size.  
- Strength: tackles an important, well-motivated problem of integrating expert knowledge into BN learning.  
- Strength: elegant, principled technical approach bridging expressive constraints and efficient decomposable-oracle methods.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation against a state-of-the-art baseline with thorough experiments and ablation.  
- Weakness: relies on an external oracle (URLEARNING) and lacks a clear, self-contained discussion of its properties and integration.  
- Weakness: runtime of the MaxSAT preprocessing (EVASOLVER) is not reported; could be a bottleneck for larger problems.  
- Weakness: scope limited to ancestral constraints; no discussion of extending projection framework to other non-decomposable constraints.  
- Assessment: technically sound, clearly written, original extension of prior work, and highly significant due to dramatic performance gains.  
- Reviewer caveat and recommendation: reviewer notes limited personal expertise in A*-EC-tree specifics but finds the empirical evidence convincing and recommends acceptance.
