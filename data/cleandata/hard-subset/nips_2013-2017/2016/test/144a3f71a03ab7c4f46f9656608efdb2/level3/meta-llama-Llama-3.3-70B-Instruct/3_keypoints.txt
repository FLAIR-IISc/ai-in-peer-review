- Proposes an efficient method to incorporate ancestral constraints into Bayesian network learning by translating them into ordering constraints.  
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to map ancestral constraints to ordering constraints; this mapping is a key innovation.  
- Translation and pruning of the search space can yield orders-of-magnitude speedups over alternative frameworks.  
- Tackles a novel problem with no standard solution; approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the rules and constraints used in the mapping process.  
- Empirical evaluations demonstrate effectiveness but may be limited to the experimental setting used.  
- Comparison against Gobnilp may be unfair because Gobnilp was not designed for this specific problem.  
- The experimental setting may be somewhat artificial; general causal discovery algorithms might be more appropriate for real-world use.  
- Practical utility is limited when only a few ancestral constraints are available, since they may not sufficiently reduce the search space.  
- Minor issues with notation, terminology, and clarity (e.g., unclear rules, inconsistent terminology) need fixing.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow overall, with detailed comparisons to prior work.  
- Novelty and impact are moderate (novelty score 3, impact score 2); results are solid but not groundbreaking.
