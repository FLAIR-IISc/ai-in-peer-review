This paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and a heuristic from URLearning. The authors demonstrate significant improvements in efficiency compared to existing ILP-based approaches.

Strengths:

1. The paper tackles a challenging problem in Bayesian network structure learning, namely, incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
2. The proposed approach is based on a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation, comparing their approach to ILP-based systems and demonstrating orders-of-magnitude improvements in efficiency.
4. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and its theoretical foundations.

Weaknesses:

1. Lemma 3, which is a key result enabling pruning with ancestral constraints, lacks a clear proof and requires better justification.
2. The explanation of the statement "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" is unclear and needs more elaboration.
3. The treatment of implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained, and the use of "can infer" is misleading without clarifying that the authors add consistent (but not necessarily entailed) ordering constraints.
4. The MAXSAT approach used to control which ordering constraints are added lacks sufficient detail on the inference procedure and rationale.
5. The experimental comparison to GOBNILP shows significant speedups, but it is unclear whether GOBNILP's poor performance is due to ancestral constraints specifically or its general weakness with unbounded parent set size.
6. The comparison to a MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints (Cussens 2008) is weak, and a tighter ILP using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and A–I linking constraints would be preferable.
7. The work of van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015) is not discussed, which often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to handle ancestral constraints.

Originality:

1. The paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The use of branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and a heuristic from URLearning is a new combination of techniques.

Significance:

1. The results are important, as they demonstrate a significant improvement in efficiency compared to existing ILP-based approaches.
2. The paper addresses a difficult problem in Bayesian network structure learning, namely, incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
3. The approach has the potential to be used in various applications, such as classification, clustering, feature selection, and discovering causes and effects.

Quality:

1. The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
2. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Clarity:

1. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and its theoretical foundations.
2. The organization is logical, and the paper is easy to follow.

Recommendation:

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly providing a clear proof for Lemma 3, elaborating on the statement "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W", and providing more detail on the MAXSAT approach. Additionally, the authors should consider comparing their approach to a tighter ILP and discussing the work of van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015).
