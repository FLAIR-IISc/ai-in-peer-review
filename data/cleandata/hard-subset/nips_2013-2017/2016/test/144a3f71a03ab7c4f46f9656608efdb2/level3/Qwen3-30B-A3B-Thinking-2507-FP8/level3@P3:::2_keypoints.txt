- Paper tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for EC tree search.  
- Key insight: use constraint projection to prune EC search space and improve A* heuristics while leveraging a decomposable-score oracle (e.g., URLEARNING).  
- Main theoretical contributions: formal methods to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints (Theorems 2 and 3) with detailed proofs.  
- Practical contributions: MaxSAT-based approach for inferring ordering constraints (Section 5.2).  
- Empirical claim: method outperforms ILP-based GOBNILP by orders of magnitude (reported in Tables 1 and 2).  
- Authors show that a small number of ancestral constraints can substantially improve learning accuracy, comparable to larger datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and connects clearly to prior work (Chen et al. 2015/2016, Yuan & Malone 2013).  
- Critical experimental flaw: presentation of results is confusing—Table 1 compares "EC + new work" vs "GOB + new work'" but "new work'" is undefined, obscuring the main contribution.  
- Most important missing experiment: no explicit comparison of EC with constraint projection versus EC alone, so the value of projection for the EC approach is unquantified.  
- Table 2 improvements with ILP do not address whether projection benefits the EC tree method itself.  
- Role and purpose of Table 3 (n=16,18,20 with limits) are unclear and do not fill the missing EC vs EC+projection comparison.  
- Because the key comparison is absent, claims of orders-of-magnitude efficiency are only supported against ILP, not against the base EC approach, weakening the paper's significance.  
- Strengths summarized: solid theoretical foundation, clear relation to EC trees and non-decomposable scores, and demonstrated practical value of ancestral constraints.  
- Weaknesses summarized: inadequate experimental validation of core contribution, confusing result presentation, missing critical EC baseline comparison, and unclear Table 3 role.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient experimental validation; paper would be acceptable if authors add the missing EC vs EC+projection comparison and clarify results.
