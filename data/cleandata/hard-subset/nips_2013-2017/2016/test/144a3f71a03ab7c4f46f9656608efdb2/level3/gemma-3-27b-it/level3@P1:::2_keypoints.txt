- Paper introduces a novel method to learn Bayesian networks with ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by inferring decomposable constraints to “empower” an existing oracle.  
- Builds on prior optimal structure learning work and leverages the EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores.  
- Addresses an important problem: incorporating background knowledge (ancestral relations) into Bayesian network learning.  
- Core technical contribution: projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms to improve oracle efficiency.  
- Theoretical contributions include soundness of pruning and completeness of the constraint projection.  
- Empirical results (Tables 1–3) show large efficiency gains over ILP-based GOBNILP.  
- Ablation study indicates projection significantly improves efficiency, but its presentation is unclear/muddled.  
- Scaling analysis demonstrates applicability up to 20 variables.  
- Experimental presentation flaw: lack of a direct comparison between EC with and without the projected constraints (confusing comparisons in Tables 1 and 3).  
- MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints is sound but needs discussion of computational cost and scalability.  
- Paper needs clearer discussion of limitations: which ancestral constraints benefit most and scenarios where projection may fail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, practical significance, theoretical soundness, and strong empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experimental presentation and insufficient analysis of MaxSAT scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; specifically add a table isolating EC performance with vs. without projected constraints.
