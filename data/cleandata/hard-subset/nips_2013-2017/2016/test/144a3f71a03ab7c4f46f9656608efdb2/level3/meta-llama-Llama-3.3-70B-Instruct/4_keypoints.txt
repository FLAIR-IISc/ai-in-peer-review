- Proposes a novel method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge and topological-order constraints for Bayesian network learning.  
- Enables incorporation of domain knowledge into decomposable-score-based structure learning by empowering an oracle for EC-tree search.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical development and experimental illustrations.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over alternative frameworks (e.g., ILP) for the EC-tree approach.  
- Projection improves performance for EC-tree methods but does not benefit the ILP approach.  
- Comparison with ILP is incomplete—Table 2 reports EC-tree SHD only and lacks a direct ILP comparison.  
- Observes an easy-hard-easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints varies, indicating sensitivity to the amount of domain knowledge.  
- Recommends dropping section 4.1 and expanding explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in EC-tree settings for clarity.  
- Overall a significant, practically useful contribution, but concerns remain about generalization ability and the thoroughness of comparative evaluation.
