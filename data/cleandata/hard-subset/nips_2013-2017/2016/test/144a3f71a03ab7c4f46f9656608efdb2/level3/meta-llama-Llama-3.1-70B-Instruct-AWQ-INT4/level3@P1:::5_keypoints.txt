- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) framework and extends it to handle ancestral constraints.
- Main idea: empower a decomposable-score oracle by passing decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Rephrases the problem as a MaxSAT formulation with specific constraints to implement the approach.
- Provides a clear, well-organized, and readable exposition of the method and underlying concepts.
- Technical approach is sound and methodologically well-justified.
- Detailed explanation of how to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using benchmarks that vary variables, dataset sizes, and constraint proportions.
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based alternatives.
- MaxSAT formulation is argued to be well-suited to the problem and effectively implemented.
- Comprehensive literature review highlighting limitations of existing methods and motivation for the work.
- Potential limitation: assumes reader familiarity with the Chen et al. framework.
- Could benefit from additional explanations or examples to aid readers less familiar with the prior framework.
- Overall assessment: high scores for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; strong candidate for acceptance.
