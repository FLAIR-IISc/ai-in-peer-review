- Problem: Learns Bayesian networks optimally under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints to encode cause-and-effect relationships.
- Method: Extends Chen et al.'s framework with a novel MaxSAT-based approach to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.
- Key innovation: Inferring decomposable constraints enables use of efficient oracles and ordering constraints, allowing MaxSAT to replace ILP.
- Empirical results: Shows orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over ILP-based GOBNILP, especially for larger variable counts and with ancestral constraints.
- Technical soundness: Provides clear theoretical foundations with soundness and completeness in search-space pruning.
- Evaluation quality: Thorough empirical comparison across datasets and configurations against a strong baseline.
- Significance: Makes structure learning scalable and enables effective incorporation of expert ancestral constraints, reducing data needs.
- Originality: Novel adaptation of MaxSAT to infer ordering/decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones.
- Clarity: Well-organized, clearly written, with transparent experimental setup and background.
- Generality concern: Experiments are limited to specific benchmarks; broader real-world dataset evaluation is needed.
- Implementation complexity: Practical use may be hindered by multiple components (EC tree, MaxSAT solver, decomposable-score oracle) and tuning effort.
- Missing limitations discussion: Paper lacks explicit discussion of scenarios where inferred constraints may not help or MaxSAT may not scale.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem, shows substantial improvements, and has strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical complexity and limited evaluation breadth could limit adoption.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and practical implementation considerations.
