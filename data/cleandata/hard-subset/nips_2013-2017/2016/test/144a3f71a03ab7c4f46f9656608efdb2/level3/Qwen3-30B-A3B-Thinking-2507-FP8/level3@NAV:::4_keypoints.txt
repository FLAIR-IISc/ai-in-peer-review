- Problem: Incorporates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.
- Proposed solution: Project non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints.
- Core contribution: Section 5 shows how to infer sound decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints, enabling standard learning algorithms.
- Empirical claim: Orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based approaches.
- Clarity and positioning: Well-written, clearly explains theory and experiments, and relates to prior work (e.g., Chen et al.).
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical foundations (Theorems 2–4) supporting the projection approach.
- Strength: Strong empirical efficiency gains demonstrated (Table 1).
- Strength: Analysis of an easy-hard-easy pattern (Table 2) gives useful insight into constraint sensitivity.
- Weakness: Experimental validation is insufficient—Table 3 shows no ILP improvement after projection, questioning generalizability.
- Weakness: Lacks evidence that inferred constraints improve performance for methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.
- Weakness: Easy-hard-easy EC tree performance peak at moderate constraint proportions is unexplained and needs justification.
- Weakness: Missing SHD comparisons for ILP in Table 2, limiting assessment of relative model quality.
- Weakness: Section 4.1 on BN graphs is tangential and should be removed to allow deeper exploration of EC trees and A*.
- Recommendation: Weak accept with major revisions to address ILP performance concerns, explain the easy-hard-easy pattern, and add SHD comparisons.
