- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that break decomposable-score algorithms.  
- Contributions: Proposes EC-tree pruning to check CPDAG compatibility with ancestral constraints and a projection method to convert ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints for the oracle.  
- Empirical claim: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP on benchmarks, scaling to ~20 variables and showing an "easy-hard-easy" pattern as constraints vary.  
- Strength — Significance/Originality: Tackles an important, challenging problem and integrates ancestral constraints into A*-based EC-tree search in a novel way.  
- Strength — Performance: Empirical results are impressive, demonstrating large speedups and competitive scaling.  
- Strength — Constraint projection idea: Inferring decomposable constraints from non-decomposable ones is powerful and potentially broadly applicable.  
- Weakness — Theoretical clarity: The soundness proof for the EC-tree pruning (Theorem 1) is omitted and the provided justification is insufficient.  
- Weakness — Pruning detail: Missing rigorous argument that adding variables/parents along EC-tree edges preserves ancestral constraint satisfaction.  
- Weakness — Projection clarity: Section 5 blurs entailed vs. merely consistent constraints; the MaxSAT formulation is presented without sufficient rationale or correctness argument.  
- Weakness — Baseline selection: GOBNILP performs poorly with unbounded max in-degree, so the speedup may partly reflect an unfair baseline.  
- Weakness — ILP encoding: The ILP used for ancestral constraints is not the tightest possible encoding; stronger ILP formulations could narrow the reported gap.  
- Weakness — Missing comparisons: The paper omits comparison to other relevant methods (e.g., van Beek & Hoffmann CP 2015 constraint programming approach).  
- Summary: Ideas are promising and original but key technical proofs and the MaxSAT rationale are unclear, and the empirical evaluation is weakened by baseline and encoding choices.  
- Recommendation: Lean reject pending major revision to clarify theoretical claims and strengthen experimental comparisons; open to reversal if authors address these issues.
