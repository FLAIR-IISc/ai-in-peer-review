- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints using an EC-tree search and an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Translates ancestral constraints into ordering constraints and integrates them into an EC-tree score-based algorithm.
- Introduces a sound and complete pruning method for the EC-tree based on ancestral constraints.
- Uses a MaxSAT solver to make implied ancestral constraints explicit and pass decomposable constraints to the oracle.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms a modified GOBNILP in the presented experiments.
- Comparison to GOBNILP is questionable because GOBNILP was not designed for this specific problem.
- A more appropriate baseline would be greedy algorithms like GES, assuming ancestral constraints align with the optimal model.
- The setting is somewhat artificial and impact is limited because real causal discovery often involves unobserved confounders.
- The assumption that many ancestral constraints are available is unrealistic; sparse constraints may not sufficiently reduce search space.
- Concerns about the mapping rules: acyclicity rule 3 has a symmetric premise but asymmetric conclusion, and rule 4 may exclude “X and Y do not cause each other” constraints.
- Theorem 1 needs clearer statement about A being only a subset of constraints satisfied by the full DAG G.
- Theorem 3 is unlikely to yield many positive edge implications when ancestral constraints are sparse.
- The encoding labeled “acyclic” is not clearly recognizable as capturing acyclicity.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow but contains minor wording and clarity issues.
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 6/10.
