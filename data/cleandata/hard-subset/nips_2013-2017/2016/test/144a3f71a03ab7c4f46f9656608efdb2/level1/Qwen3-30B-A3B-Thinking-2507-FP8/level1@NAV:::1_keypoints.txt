- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by extending Chen et al.'s (2015) optimal-structure framework.  
- Proposes converting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Leverages the EC tree search space and demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods.  
- Strong theoretical foundations with theorems on constraint projection and pruning soundness.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) and varied parameters (variables, data size, constraint percentage).  
- Shows practical value: small fractions of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match improvements from much larger datasets.  
- Well-situated in related work, building on Chen et al. and distinguishing from constraint-based methods like PC.  
- Main strength: novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated constraint projection method.  
- Primary limitations: scalability to very large networks (experiments up to n=20) and potential implementation complexity of constraint projection.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-quality contribution warranting acceptance for NIPS.
