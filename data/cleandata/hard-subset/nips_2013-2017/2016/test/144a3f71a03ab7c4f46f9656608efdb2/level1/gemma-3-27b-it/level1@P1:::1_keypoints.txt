- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints to incorporate domain knowledge into structure learning.  
- Builds on prior work for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores using a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Key contribution: projects ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable constraints to leverage the oracle and improve efficiency.  
- Demonstrates significant empirical speedups over ILP-based methods (notably GOBNILP).  
- Theorems 1–4 provide a sound theoretical foundation for the pruning and projection techniques.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and connects appropriately to prior literature (Chen et al., ILP, constraint programming).  
- Experiments show practical effectiveness and scalability up to 20 variables.  
- Novelty: the projection strategy for non-decomposable constraints is new and effective.  
- Efficiency: empirical results indicate substantial performance improvements making the approach practical for larger networks than ILP.  
- Clarity: technical concepts, including ancestral constraints and non-decomposability, are concisely explained.  
- Weakness — scalability: unclear how the method performs on much larger networks (hundreds/thousands of variables).  
- Weakness — MaxSAT dependency: reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) creates a potential performance bottleneck and dependency.  
- Weakness — projection completeness: claim of complete projection needs clarification on limits and possible cases causing information loss or degraded performance.  
- Weakness — limited baselines: comparisons are mainly against GOBNILP; additional baselines or workarounds for other methods would strengthen evaluation.  
- Weakness — heuristic analysis: more in-depth discussion of the heuristic’s quality and impact on search efficiency is needed.  
- Recommendation: overall strong and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability, MaxSAT dependency, projection completeness, and heuristic analysis.
