- Problem summary: Tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by pruning EC-trees and projecting constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Key technical contributions: Sound EC-tree pruning for CPDAGs (Theorem 1), edge entailment via α/β reachability with necessary-and-sufficient conditions (Theorems 2–3), and a MaxSAT formulation to harvest safe ordering constraints (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical claim: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP), especially when using projected constraints; scaling experiments up to n=20 with BDeu.  

Strengths:
- Motivation: Clear and practical motivation—ancestral constraints capture causal/temporal knowledge that should improve learning.  
- Theoretical soundness: Pruning criteria and edge-entailment theorems are technically solid and well-motivated.  
- Novelty: Projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/order constraints is original and useful.  
- MaxSAT idea: Principled MaxSAT-based selection of compatible ordering constraints to maximize decomposable information.  
- Empirical support: Strong runtime gains and ablation showing projected constraints dramatically tighten the oracle.  
- Practical impact: Demonstrates improved runtime and estimation accuracy; small amounts of trusted knowledge can substitute for more data.  

Weaknesses / Questions:
- CPDAG pruning scalability: When tests are inconclusive the method enumerates class(P), which can be exponential; frequency and cost of this are unquantified.  
- Alternative checks: Could SAT/ILP checks or Meek-rule orientation extensions avoid exponential enumeration?  
- MaxSAT maximality claim: Theorem 4 provides only sufficient (not necessary) conditions, so the MaxSAT optimum is conservative and not guaranteed globally maximal — clarify the exact guarantee.  
- Experimental limitations: Evaluations use noiseless constraints on small networks (n ≤ 20); robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints is not tested.  
- Missing baselines: No constraint programming (CP) baseline and lack of experiments in bounded in-degree regimes where ILP is competitive.  
- Missing methodological details: Key procedures (closure to maximum ancestral set, edge projection algorithm, MaxSAT encoding) are only sketched; proofs for Theorems 2–3 are absent.  
- Presentation issues: Notation for α(·), β(·), Xa/Yb is unclear; the MaxSAT acyclicity condition is nonstandard and needs motivation/proof; minor typos/references incomplete.  

Quality assessment:
- Core ideas are sound and the ordering component is conservative but correct; experimental methodology is reasonable but would benefit from more analyses (enumeration frequency, MaxSAT overhead).  

Clarity:
- Overall organization is good, but important algorithms and pseudocode are omitted; include appendices with algorithms, proofs, and runtime breakdowns (projection vs search).  

Originality:
- Using EC-trees with sound pruning for ancestral constraints and projecting to decomposable constraints, plus the MaxSAT selection, is novel in this context.  

Significance:
- Advances exact BN learning with non-decomposable priors and shows substantial practical gains; relevant given common causal/temporal priors.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Well-motivated problem, novel principled methods, strong empirical gains, and clear practical impact; extends influential prior work.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Incomplete technical details and proofs, unquantified potential scalability bottlenecks (CPDAG enumeration), and limited experimental scope (noise, CP baseline, in-degree regimes).  

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide full algorithms and proofs (closure, edge projection, MaxSAT encoding) with complexity analysis.  
- Replace or mitigate CPDAG enumeration with satisfiability-based checks or report statistics on enumeration frequency and cost.  
- Clarify the exact optimality guarantee of the MaxSAT approach and consider strengthening the encoding (e.g., reachability variables).  
- Add experiments with bounded in-degree, a CP baseline, projection/MaxSAT runtime breakdowns, and robustness to noisy constraints.  
- Tighten notation, justify the MaxSAT acyclicity clause, and fix typos/references.
