- Proposes an approach for optimal Bayesian network learning with ancestral constraints using an EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Builds on a recent framework for optimal learning with non-decomposable scores, general enough to handle ancestral constraints.
- Provides a clear, concise summary of the problem and strong connection to prior work.
- Gives a thorough review of technical preliminaries (Bayesian networks, structure learning, ancestral constraints).
- Main contribution: a sound and complete method to prune the EC tree based on ancestral constraints.
- Enhances the oracle by translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints for efficiency.
- Empirical results show the method is orders-of-magnitude faster than an ILP-based system across varied n, N, and p.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and well-presented technical details.
- Strength: novel and effective approach for learning with ancestral constraints.
- Strength: thorough preliminaries and strong empirical evaluation.
- Weakness: assumes substantial background knowledge, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and future research directions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical evidence, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility and insufficient discussion of limitations/future work.
