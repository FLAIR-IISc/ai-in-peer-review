- Addresses learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor/not ancestor of Y), which breaks local verification used by many exact learners.
- Builds on the EC-tree A* search over equivalence classes to handle non-decomposable scores and adapts it to enforce ancestral constraints via sound pruning of CPDAG nodes.
- Introduces "constraint projection": infers decomposable edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints to tighten the A* oracle heuristic and speed up search dramatically.
- Provides formal methods for projection, including an efficient algorithm to infer all entailed edge constraints.
- Uses a MaxSAT formulation to compute a maximal set of sound topological ordering constraints for further pruning.
- Theorems and pruning rules are sound and appear correct; algorithms are technically solid.
- Empirical evaluation is rigorous, compares against ILP, shows orders-of-magnitude speedups and scalability to networks up to 20 variables.
- Ablation study demonstrates the large impact of constraint projection on performance.
- Well-written and organized; distinguishes clearly between non-decomposable ancestral constraints and inferred decomposable constraints.
- Minor clarity suggestions: give more intuition/example for the complex MaxSAT hard constraint and a brief proof sketch for Theorem 1 (e.g., in an appendix).
- Original contribution: the projection idea and using MaxSAT for ordering constraints are novel and non-obvious extensions of prior EC-tree work.
- Significant practical impact: enables exact incorporation of expert ancestral knowledge and shows a small number of constraints can match large increases in data (e.g., ~4×).
- Pros: solves an important problem, elegant technique, strong empirical gains, technically sound, and practically valuable.
- Cons: inherent scalability limits of exact score-based methods (experiments limited to n=20) and some MaxSAT technical details need clearer explanation.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.
