- Paper addresses optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by searching BN/EC-tree spaces and using a decomposable-score oracle heuristic.  
- Core idea: project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten admissible heuristics and prune search.  
- Presents CPDAG feasibility checks with quick necessary tests and enumeration of class(P) when inconclusive.  
- Derives edge-entailment conditions (Theorems 2–3) using ancestor/descendant closures after saturating A under transitivity and simple rules.  
- Proposes a MaxSAT reduction to infer a maximally large set of safe ordering constraints, with hard constraints for transitivity/acyclicity and soft clauses for preferred orders.  
- Theorem 4 gives sufficient conditions for correctness of the MaxSAT-based ordering inference.  
- Empirical evaluation: EC-tree+oracle (URLearning oracle) vs ILP baseline (GOBNILP) shows orders-of-magnitude speedups, especially when using projected constraints.  
- Experiments examine effects of constraint density and dataset size on runtime and accuracy, showing easy–hard–easy behavior and that modest prior knowledge can match substantial data increases.  
- Strength: conceptually clear and well-motivated problem with principled integration of non-decomposable pruning and oracle heuristics.  
- Strength: edge-entailment rules are simple to implement and effective in practice.  
- Strength: MaxSAT formulation is practical and treating inferred orders as soft objectives is reasonable.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains and ablation studies demonstrating projection value.  
- Weakness: proofs and completeness details for Theorems 2–4 are thin or omitted; some iff claims lack justification.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3’s necessity condition and Theorem 4’s “closed set” need clearer definitions and rigorous arguments.  
- Weakness: CPDAG feasibility enumeration can be exponential; frequency and runtime impact of enumeration are not quantified.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding rationale, asymptotic size (variables/clauses), and empirical solve times are not analyzed.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines limited to ILP; missing comparisons to constraint programming and bounded-indegree regimes.  
- Weakness: robustness to noisy or conflicting prior constraints is not studied; no soft/slack handling presented.  
- Minor issues: some theorem statements/montonicity phrasing and formatting/typo problems hurt clarity.  
- Quality: overall technically sound but several proofs, derivations, and complexity analyses are deferred or missing.  
- Clarity: generally readable but needs tighter formal definitions, theorem statements, and pseudocode for closures and edge entailment.  
- Originality: novel combination of EC-tree search, projection of path constraints, and MaxSAT-based ordering inference in exact BN learning.  
- Significance: potential meaningful advance if speedups generalize; projection idea may transfer to other frameworks.  
- Suggested improvements: add proofs or sketches for key theorems, define “closed” O, quantify CPDAG enumeration costs, report MaxSAT sizes/times, add CP and bounded-fanin baselines, and study noisy constraints.  
- Pro accept: fills important gap and shows strong empirical improvements over a solid ILP baseline.  
- Con accept: incomplete theoretical details, possible exponential CPDAG enumeration, and limited experimental regimes.
