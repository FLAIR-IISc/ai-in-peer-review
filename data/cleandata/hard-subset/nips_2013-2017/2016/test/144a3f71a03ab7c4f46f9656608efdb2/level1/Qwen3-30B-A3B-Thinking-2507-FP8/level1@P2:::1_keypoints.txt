- Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning by inferring decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree for non-decomposable scores and enables it to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1–4) proving soundness of the constraint projection methods.  
- Novel method that transforms non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones usable by existing oracles.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation comparing EC tree approach to GOBNILP, showing orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements.  
- Demonstrates scalability to ~20 variables and that EC tree scales where ILP struggles.  
- Shows that 10–25% ancestral constraints can match the effect of quadrupling training data on model accuracy.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, with helpful examples and reproducible experimental setup averaged over 50 datasets.  
- Original contribution distinct from prior ILP, CP, and path-constraint based methods (e.g., Cussens; Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).  
- Practical significance for real-world applications where background knowledge is available but data collection is costly.  
- Limitation: insufficient analysis of robustness to incorrect or partially incorrect ancestral constraints.  
- Limitation: MaxSAT encoding for ordering constraints lacks discussion of computational complexity and scaling trade-offs.  
- Overall conclusion: strong technical and empirical contribution; recommendation to accept.
