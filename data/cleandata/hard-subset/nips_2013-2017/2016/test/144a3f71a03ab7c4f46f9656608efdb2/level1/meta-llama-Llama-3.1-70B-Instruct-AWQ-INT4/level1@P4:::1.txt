This paper proposes an approach for learning Bayesian networks optimally with ancestral constraints. The authors utilize a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree, and employ an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. The approach is based on a recently proposed framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, which is general enough to accommodate ancestral constraints.

The authors provide a clear and concise summary of the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints and relate it to previous work in the field. They also provide a thorough review of the technical preliminaries, including the definition of Bayesian networks, the problem of structure learning, and the concept of ancestral constraints.

The paper's main contribution is the proposal of a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints. The authors also show how the employed oracle can be empowered by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints. This is a significant contribution, as it allows for the efficient learning of Bayesian networks with non-decomposable scores.

The authors provide empirical evidence for the effectiveness of their approach, comparing it to an ILP-based system. The results show that the proposed approach is orders-of-magnitude faster than the ILP-based system for almost all values of n, N, and p that were varied.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The technical details are well-presented, and the empirical evaluation is thorough and well-executed.
