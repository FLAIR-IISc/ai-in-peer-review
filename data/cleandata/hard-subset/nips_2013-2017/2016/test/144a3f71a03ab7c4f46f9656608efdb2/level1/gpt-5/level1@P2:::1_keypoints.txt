- Summary: Extends EC-tree shortest-path exact BN learning to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints by pruning CPDAG nodes and projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten an oracle heuristic.  
- Strength: Clear focus on exact BN learning with hard ancestral (causal) constraints, a practically relevant problem.  
- Strength: Principled CPDAG-level pruning (Theorem 1) that leverages equivalence classes.  
- Strength: Effective, checkable edge-entailment projection using α/β closure with necessary-and-sufficient conditions (Theorems 2–3).  
- Strength: Scalable extraction of safe precedence/orderings via a MaxSAT reduction (Theorem 4) to augment the oracle.  
- Strength: Strong empirical speedups over GOBNILP and ablations showing projected constraints substantially tighten the heuristic.  
- Strength: Good positioning relative to prior work (order-graph, ILP/CP, CB path constraints).  
- Weakness: Scalability modest (≈20 variables) and depends on oracle limits; inherits A*-style memory/time profile and precomputed family scores.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT-based ordering projection uses sufficient (not necessary) conditions; impact of suboptimality and MaxSAT runtime overhead not quantified.  
- Weakness: No robustness experiments with noisy, inconsistent, or partially incorrect constraints common in practice.  
- Weakness: Some theoretical/algorithmic details deferred or informal (closure rules, complexity of maximal ancestral sets).  
- Weakness: Complexity bounds missing for edge entailment and pruning when enumeration of class(P) is required; exponential enumeration mitigation not detailed.  
- Weakness: Narrow baseline comparison (only GOBNILP); ILP tuning and alternative baselines (CP) underexplored.  
- Quality: Technical claims largely sound; pruning and projection theorems appear correct, but reliance on class(P) enumeration and missing robustness tests are gaps.  
- Clarity: Well-written overall with clear distinction between ancestral and projected decomposable constraints; some algorithms, pseudocode, and complexity analyses would improve readability.  
- Originality: Novel integration of ancestral constraints into EC trees and creative MaxSAT reduction for safe precedence extraction.  
- Significance: Practically meaningful contribution with large empirical gains that could broaden applicability of exact learning with causal priors.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints and propose conflict-resolution or soft-penalty mechanisms.  
- Suggestion: Provide detailed algorithms and complexity bounds for ancestral closure, edge entailment, and pruning tests that avoid full class enumeration.  
- Suggestion: Report MaxSAT runtime overhead and ablation separating edge-only, order-only, and combined projection effects.  
- Suggestion: Expand baselines (e.g., constraint programming), detail ILP tuning, and test different family-size limits and scores (e.g., MDL).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, principled method with sound theory and substantial empirical speedups; useful projection ideas for hybrid pipelines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited scalability, incomplete complexity characterization, missing robustness evaluation, and underreported MaxSAT/ILP details.  
- Overall: Solid, technically sound contribution that advances exact BN learning with ancestral constraints and merits acceptance despite gaps in robustness and scalability analysis.
