- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and incompatible with many exact structure-learning algorithms.  
- Challenge: Ancestral constraints cannot be checked locally, breaking decomposability assumptions used by DP/A*-over-orderings methods.  
- Framework used: Builds on EC-tree A* search over equivalence classes with an "oracle" for decomposable-score optimization to compute admissible heuristics.  
- Contribution 1: Develops sound pruning rules for CPDAG nodes in the EC tree that are inconsistent with ancestral constraints.  
- Contribution 2: Introduces a method to infer decomposable constraints (edge presence/absence and pairwise orderings) from ancestral constraints to tighten the oracle's heuristic.  
- Technical novelty: Formalizes constraint projection with theorems (2–4) and reduces inference of maximal consistent ordering constraints to MaxSAT.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) across networks, dataset sizes, and constraint densities (Tables 1–2).  
- Practical benefit: Shows improved learning accuracy when incorporating background knowledge (structural Hamming distance results in Table 3).  
- Quality: High technical soundness, well-justified methods, and thorough experimental validation.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; clearly distinguishes what the EC search and oracle handle.  
- Originality: Novel "empower the oracle" projection idea bridging non-decomposable and decomposable methods; MaxSAT formulation is creative.  
- Significance: Makes exact, constrained BN learning substantially more practical (scales to >20 variables) and suggests a general algorithmic paradigm.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong contribution, solid theory, state-of-the-art empirical gains, clarity, and broader applicability.  
- Arguments against / limitations: Minor concern about incremental nature relative to prior EC-tree work and inherent scalability limits of exact methods.
