- Summary: Proposes a novel method to optimally learn Bayesian network structure under non-decomposable ancestral constraints using an EC tree search and a decomposable-score oracle augmented with inferred decomposable constraints.

- Strength: Novel approach tackling the challenging problem of ancestral constraints in BN structure learning.

- Strength: Solid theoretical foundations with proofs of correctness and completeness.

- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation comparing to existing methods and demonstrating effectiveness.

- Strength: Shown to scale to larger networks, suggesting practical applicability.

- Weakness: Relies on a complex search space and oracle that may be hard to implement and understand.

- Weakness: Assumes ancestral constraints are consistent and acyclic, which may not hold in practice.

- Weakness: Empirical comparison is limited (only compared to ILP), possibly insufficient to assess relative performance.

- Quality: Well-written with clear explanations; strong theory and empirical work despite noted drawbacks.

- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and accessible to researchers, though technical.

- Originality: Original use of the EC tree and empowering the oracle with inferred decomposable constraints.

- Significance: Addresses an important problem with a method that could be widely adopted due to demonstrated effectiveness and scalability.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel method addressing ancestral constraints in BN learning.

- Argument for acceptance: The approach is both theoretically sound and empirically validated.

- Argument for acceptance: Demonstrates scalability to larger networks.

- Argument for rejection: Complexity of the approach may hinder implementation and adoption.

- Argument for rejection: Key assumptions (consistency/acyclicity) may not hold in real-world cases.

- Argument for rejection: Limited experimental baselines reduce confidence in comparative performance.
