- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints by using an oracle for decomposable scores within a framework handling non-decomposable constraints.
- Main contribution: efficiently infers decomposable constraints from ancestral ones to enable the oracle, improving over ILP approaches.
- Builds on Chen et al. [2015, 2016] and combines existing techniques in a novel, theoretically grounded way (Theorems 1–4).
- Explores both edge and ordering constraints derived from ancestral constraints.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides necessary technical preliminaries.
- Experimental setup is thorough, comparing to a strong baseline (GOBNILP) across network sizes and constraint levels.
- Empirical results show convincing benefits, notably significant speedups from constraint projection.
- Demonstrates that a few ancestral constraints can have an effect comparable to increasing data size.
- Significance: practical impact for real-world tasks where domain knowledge provides ancestral constraints.
- Related work is adequately cited, including ILP and constraint-based learning references.
- Weakness: MaxSAT reduction discussion is brief; needs more on practical challenges and solver bottlenecks.
- Weakness: Choice of BDeu score seems arbitrary; should examine other scoring functions (e.g., MDL).
- Weakness: Structural evaluation relies on SHD; additional metrics like edge precision/recall would strengthen claims.
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of limitations and scaling behavior with complex/higher-density ancestral constraint networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, empirically supported, novel combination with theory, significant performance gains, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: brief MaxSAT discussion, need for more structural metrics, and missing limitation analysis.
- Overall assessment: Strong recommendation for acceptance; significant, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution.
