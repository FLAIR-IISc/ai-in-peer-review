- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints.  
- Leverages a framework for non-decomposable scores that can incorporate ancestral constraints.  
- Key idea: convert non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints and use an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores.  
- Presents an EC tree search space and a pruning method based on ancestral constraints.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, well-organized technical exposition.  
- Empirical evaluation compares to a state-of-the-art ILP system and shows significant efficiency gains.  
- Results demonstrate better scalability and efficiency, especially as variable count increases.  
- Strength: novel and efficient approach tailored to ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation supporting effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear, concise writing makes the work accessible.  
- Weakness: method depends on availability and efficiency of a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Weakness: inferred decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints may be incomplete or inaccurate.  
- Weakness: empirical benchmarks are limited and may not generalize across domains.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept; quality 9/10, clarity 9/10, originality 8/10, significance 9/10, overall score 8.8/10.
