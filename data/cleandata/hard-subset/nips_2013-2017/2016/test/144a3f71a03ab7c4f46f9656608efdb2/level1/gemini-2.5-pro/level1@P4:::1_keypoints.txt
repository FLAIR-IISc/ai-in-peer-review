- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, which break standard decomposability assumptions used by efficient exact learners.  
- Prior work: Builds on an A*-based EC-tree search framework that uses an oracle decomposable-score learner to compute admissible heuristics.  
- Main contribution: Adapt the A* EC-tree framework to handle ancestral constraints via pruning and constraint projection to decomposable constraints.  
- Pruning: Use ancestral constraints to directly prune EC-tree nodes that contain no valid DAGs (Theorem 1).  
- Constraint projection: Infer edge presence/absence and pairwise ordering constraints from ancestral constraints to empower the oracle and tighten the heuristic.  
- Ordering inference: Formulate finding a maximal consistent set of ordering constraints as a MaxSAT problem (Theorem 4).  
- Theoretical support: Provide formal results for edge inference (Theorems 2 and 3) and soundness of the methods.  
- Empirical results: Compared to ILP (GOBNILP), the method achieves orders-of-magnitude speedups across varied settings (variables, data size, constraints).  
- Ablation: Studies show constraint projection is critical for the large speedups reported.  
- Practical benefit: Background ancestral constraints can substantially improve learned structure accuracy (SHD), comparable to having much more data.  
- Weakness — MaxSAT overhead: The runtime cost of the MaxSAT projection step is not measured or discussed and may be a bottleneck.  
- Weakness — Scalability: Method scales to ~20 variables but exhibits an "easy-hard-easy" behavior as constraint density changes, suggesting practical limits and intermediate-case difficulty.  
- Weakness — Oracle dependency: Overall performance relies on the chosen decomposable-score oracle (URLEARNING); interaction with other oracles is not explored.  
- Overall: A novel, technically sound, and well-validated approach that makes optimal learning with ancestral constraints feasible for problem sizes previously out of reach, with minor caveats about preprocessing cost and scalability.
