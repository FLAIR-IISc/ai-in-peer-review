- Addresses learning Bayesian networks under ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) framework to enable use of oracles for decomposable scores.  
- Proposes a novel method to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral ones.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based frameworks.  
- Experiments are thorough and vary dataset size, number of variables, and proportion of constraints.  
- Method scales well to problems with up to 20 variables and exhibits an easy-hard-easy trend.  
- Demonstrates practical value of incorporating background ancestral knowledge for efficiency and accuracy.  
- Technical contribution is significant and impactful in addressing a key limitation of existing BN learning methods.  
- Empirical validation is robust and well-designed.  
- Scalability is comparable to state-of-the-art Bayesian network learning approaches.  
- Paper is dense and parts are challenging to follow; needs more examples or visual aids (e.g., for projection and MaxSAT).  
- Comparison to related methods is limited—insufficient discussion of constraint-based or greedy search alternatives.  
- Unclear how well the approach generalizes to other non-decomposable constraint types.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated problem, novel/technically sound method, strong empirical improvements, practical implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues and limited evaluation scope.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve readability and contextualization.
