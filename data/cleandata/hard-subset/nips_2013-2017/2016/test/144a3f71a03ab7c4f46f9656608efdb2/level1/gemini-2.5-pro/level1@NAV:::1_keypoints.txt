- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures under ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable.
- Challenge: mainstream exact learning algorithms depend on decomposable scores and cannot directly handle ancestral constraints.
- Approach: builds on EC-tree A*-search and projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.
- Technical novelty: reduces ordering-constraint inference to MaxSAT, enabling constraint projection to feed a decomposable-score oracle.
- Effect: projected constraints tighten the heuristic used by the oracle, significantly accelerating search.
- Empirical results: thorough experiments show orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and strong practical gains.
- Key finding: adding a small percentage of constraints can match accuracy improvements equivalent to quadrupling dataset size (Table 3).
- Quality & significance: technically sound, addresses a relevant practical problem, and offers important advances for exact structure learning.
- Originality: core search framework builds on prior work, but the projection technique and MaxSAT reduction are novel and non-trivial.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, logically organized, and explanations are easy to follow.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: elegant, effective solution with dramatic empirical improvements and clear advancement of capabilities.
- Con-acceptance argument: primarily an extension of existing search framework rather than a brand-new algorithm, though the extension is significant.
