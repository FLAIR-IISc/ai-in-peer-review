This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a valuable and practical extension to standard structure learning. The core idea – leveraging an oracle for decomposable scores via projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints – is clever and well-motivated. The authors build upon their previous work [Chen et al., 2015, 2016], which introduced a framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, and successfully adapt it to incorporate ancestral constraints.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions the work within the existing literature. The technical preliminaries provide necessary background on Bayesian network structure learning and related concepts. The description of the proposed approach is concise and understandable. The theoretical results (Theorems 1-4) are clearly stated and appear correct, though a more detailed proof sketch for Theorem 4 (regarding the MaxSAT reduction) would be beneficial. The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the proposed approach against an ILP-based method and demonstrating significant performance gains. The analysis of the results, including the impact of constraint projection and the effect of constraint density, is insightful.

A key strength of the paper is its practical relevance. Ancestral constraints are common in many real-world applications, and the ability to efficiently incorporate them into the structure learning process is significant. The empirical results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods, particularly in terms of computational efficiency. The exploration of the trade-offs between constraint density and learning performance is also valuable. The theoretical justification for constraint projection and the soundness arguments for pruning the search space are well-presented. The connections to existing work (e.g., ILP, constraint programming, order graphs) are appropriately acknowledged and discussed.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. While the experimental setup is comprehensive, the choice of datasets (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) feels somewhat limited. These are standard benchmarks, but their representativeness of real-world scenarios might be questioned. Exploring datasets with different characteristics (e.g., higher dimensionality, more complex dependencies) could strengthen the evaluation. The discussion of the MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) is brief. More details on the solver's parameters and performance would be useful.  Although the authors mention scaling to n=20, a more detailed scalability analysis, perhaps with a graph showing runtime versus network size, would be helpful to understand the limitations of the approach. The discussion of the structural Hamming distance could be expanded, explaining its interpretation and significance in the context of the experiments. While the authors mention the importance of edge constraints, the discussion of ordering constraints felt less developed. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to empower an oracle is novel and addresses a significant limitation of existing methods.
* **Significance:** The ability to efficiently learn Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints is practically important and has the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in causal inference and probabilistic modeling.
* **Soundness:** The theoretical results and the soundness arguments for the pruning and projection steps are well-established.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* **Dataset Limitations:** The evaluation is based on a limited set of datasets.
* **MaxSAT Solver Details:** Lack of detailed information about the MaxSAT solver.
* **Scalability Analysis:** Limited scalability analysis.
* **Ordering Constraints:** Less development of ordering constraints compared to edge constraints.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The approach is novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. The paper addresses a practically important problem and has the potential to have a significant impact. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding dataset limitations, MaxSAT solver details, and scalability analysis. Specifically, adding a scalability plot and perhaps including results on a different dataset or two would strengthen the paper considerably.
