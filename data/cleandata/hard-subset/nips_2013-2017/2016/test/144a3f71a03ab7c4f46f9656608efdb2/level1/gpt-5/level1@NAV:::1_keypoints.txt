- Tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with hard (non-decomposable) ancestral constraints by pruning EC-tree nodes and projecting constraints to decomposable forms to empower decomposable-score oracles.  
- Technical contributions: CPDAG pruning criterion; edge entailment via closure (Thms. 2–3); ordering selection via MaxSAT (Thm. 4) — sound but conservative.  
- Risk: fallback to exhaustive enumeration of class(P) when path tests are inconclusive may be exponential; paper should bound how often this occurs and its impact.  
- MaxSAT conservativeness: constraints are sufficient but not necessary, potentially leaving useful ordering information unused; requires analysis of tightness.  
- Experiments: show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) and accuracy gains from modest ancestral knowledge across datasets and constraint densities.  
- Experimental limitations: evaluations limited to n ≤ 20, only ILP baseline (no CP baseline), and no robustness study to noisy constraints.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: generally clear and well organized; would benefit from pseudocode for edge-closure and a precise complexity discussion.  
- Originality: high — novel, elegant projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints to decomposable edge/order constraints.  
- Significance: promising for broadening practical exact learning with realistic causal priors if scalability holds.  
- Pros summarized: principled integration, strong empirical speedups, clear ablations, and useful theoretical characterizations.  
- Cons summarized: potential exponential CPDAG enumeration, conservative MaxSAT lacking tightness analysis, limited baselines/scale, and no noise robustness evaluation.
