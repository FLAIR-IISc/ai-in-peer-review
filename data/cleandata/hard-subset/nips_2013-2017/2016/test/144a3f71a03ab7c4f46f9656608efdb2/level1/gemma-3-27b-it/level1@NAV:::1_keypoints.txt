- Addresses optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints to incorporate prior knowledge.
- Leverages a recent framework for optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores and adapts it to ancestral constraints.
- Key technical contribution: infer decomposable constraints from ancestral ones to enable efficient oracle use.
- Novel pragmatic bridge between theoretical non-decomposable frameworks and practical implementations.
- Connects constraint inference to MaxSAT, providing a solid reduction for ordering constraint inference.
- Well-motivated positioning within literature, citing foundational and recent related works.
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over an ILP-based baseline (GOBNILP).
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, varying network size, data size, and constraint percentage.
- Structural Hamming distance analysis demonstrates accuracy and highlights impact of few ancestral constraints.
- Weakness: scalability beyond n=20 networks is not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness: complexity of the constraint projection step and MaxSAT reduction lacks deep analysis.
- Weakness: limited direct empirical comparison to alternative methods handling ancestral constraints (e.g., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).
- Minor issue: notation could be improved for readability.
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths and empirical gains outweigh the identified weaknesses.
