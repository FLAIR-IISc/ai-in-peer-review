- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a practical extension to structure learning.
- Core idea: project ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to leverage an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Builds on prior optimal structure learning framework (Chen et al. 2015, 2016) and adapts it to ancestral constraints.
- Well-structured and clearly written paper with effective motivation and literature positioning.
- Technical preliminaries are adequate and the proposed approach is concisely described and understandable.
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–4) are clearly stated and appear correct.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed proof sketch for Theorem 4 (MaxSAT reduction).
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares favorably against an ILP-based method, showing significant performance gains.
- Analysis of constraint projection and the effect of constraint density is insightful.
- Practical relevance is high: ancestral constraints are common and the method efficiently incorporates them.
- Soundness arguments for pruning and projection are well-presented.
- Appropriate connections to related work (ILP, constraint programming, order graphs) are acknowledged.
- Dataset choice (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) is limited and may not represent varied real-world scenarios.
- More details are needed on the MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) parameters and performance.
- Scalability analysis is limited; recommend a runtime vs. network size plot and tests beyond n=20.
- Structural Hamming distance discussion should be expanded to clarify interpretation in experiments.
- Ordering constraints are less developed compared to edge constraints and need more discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (more datasets, solver details, scalability plot).
