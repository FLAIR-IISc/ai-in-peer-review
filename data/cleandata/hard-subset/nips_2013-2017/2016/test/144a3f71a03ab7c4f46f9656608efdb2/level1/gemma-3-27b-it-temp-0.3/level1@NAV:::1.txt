## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper tackles the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) optimally under ancestral constraints – a crucial aspect for incorporating domain knowledge. The core idea is to leverage a recent framework for optimal structure learning based on non-decomposable scores, which naturally accommodates these constraints, but requires an oracle for learning with decomposable scores. The authors cleverly address this by inferring decomposable constraints from the ancestral ones, effectively empowering the oracle. This approach is positioned against existing methods like Integer Linear Programming (ILP), and the paper demonstrates significant efficiency gains empirically.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of traditional decomposable score-based methods when dealing with non-decomposable constraints like ancestral relations. The connection to prior work in BN structure learning (Darwiche, Koller & Friedman, Murphy) and constraint-based approaches (Koivisto & Sood, Borboudakis & Tsamardinos) is appropriately established. The proposed method, building upon the EC tree framework (Chen et al., 2015, 2016), is logically presented, and the projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable forms is a novel and insightful contribution. The theoretical grounding, including theorems regarding constraint entailment, adds rigor.

A key strength is the empirical evaluation, which convincingly demonstrates the orders-of-magnitude speedup compared to ILP-based methods. The experiments are well-designed, varying network size, dataset size, and constraint density. The analysis of structural Hamming distance provides valuable insight into the accuracy of the learned models. The discussion of the "easy-hard-easy" trend with increasing constraint density is particularly insightful.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the constraint projection technique. While the authors demonstrate its effectiveness, a deeper analysis of scenarios where projection might be ineffective or lead to a loss of information would be valuable.  Furthermore, the reliance on the EVASOLVER for MaxSAT introduces an external dependency, and a discussion of its potential impact on scalability and robustness would be beneficial. The notation is dense and could be improved for readability. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to incorporating non-decomposable constraints into optimal BN structure learning.
*   Significant empirical improvements in efficiency compared to existing methods (ILP).
*   Sound theoretical foundation with clear theorems and proofs.
*   Well-designed experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the limitations of the constraint projection technique.
*   Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER).
*   Dense notation impacting readability.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach is novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated. Despite minor areas for improvement, the paper meets the criteria for acceptance to NIPS.
