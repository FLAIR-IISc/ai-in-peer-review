This paper tackles exact Bayesian network structure learning with hard ancestral constraints, a non-decomposable form of background knowledge that standard decomposable-score methods cannot natively handle. Building on Chen et al.’s BN graph and EC tree spaces for non-decomposable scores, the authors (i) prune EC-tree nodes (CPDAGs) that cannot realize the ancestral constraints, and (ii) “empower” a decomposable-score oracle (order-graph methods such as URLearning) by projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable edge and ordering constraints. Edge entailments are derived via closure over ancestral relations (Thms. 2–3), while ordering constraints are selected via a MaxSAT formulation (Thm. 4) that is sound though conservative. Experiments compare against ILP (GOBNILP) and show orders-of-magnitude speedups across datasets and constraint densities, and demonstrate that modest amounts of ancestral knowledge can substantially improve accuracy, echoing earlier observations on the value of priors (e.g., Koivisto & Sood; Yuan & Malone). The work complements prior use of path constraints in constraint-based and heuristic settings (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos) and extends exact learning beyond decomposable priors (Chen et al., 2015, 2016) and ILP/CP formulations (Jaakkola et al., Cussens; van Beek & Hoffmann).

Quality: The approach is technically sound overall. The pruning criterion for CPDAG nodes and the edge/ordering projections are well motivated and formalized. However, the fallback to exhaustive enumeration of class(P) when path tests are inconclusive may be exponential; the paper should bound how often this happens and its impact. The MaxSAT constraints are sufficient but not necessary, potentially leaving useful ordering information on the table; an analysis of tightness would help. Experiments are solid but limited to n ≤ 20 and to ILP as the sole baseline.

Clarity: Generally clear and well organized; the connection between theorems, closure to a “maximum” constraint set, and the oracle interface is understandable. Pseudocode for edge-closure and a precise complexity discussion would improve reproducibility.

Originality: High. The projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints to decomposable edge/order constraints for empowering an exact oracle is novel and elegant.

Significance: Promising. If scalable, this could broaden practical exact learning with realistic causal priors. The empirical gains over ILP are substantial.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and principled integration of ancestral constraints into exact learning via EC trees and projections.
- Strong empirical speedups and clear ablation showing projection benefits.
- Useful theoretical characterizations (Thms. 2–4).

Cons for acceptance:
- Potential exponential CPDAG enumeration without quantified frequency.
- Conservative MaxSAT formulation; missing analysis of tightness and ablations (edge vs order projections).
- Limited baselines (no CP) and modest scale; no robustness study to noisy constraints.
