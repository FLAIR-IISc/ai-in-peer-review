- Problem: learning optimal Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral constraints is challenging for standard decomposable-score methods.  
- Framework: builds on an A*-based EC-tree search guided by a decomposable-score oracle.  
- Contribution 1: adapts the EC-tree search to correctly prune branches violating ancestral constraints.  
- Contribution 2: projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints via rule-based edge inference and a MaxSAT reduction for ordering.  
- Empirical result: method is orders of magnitude faster than ILP (GOBNILP) and scales to ~20 variables for exact learning.  
- Strength: high significance and practicality—ancestral constraints substantially improve structural accuracy, comparable to much larger datasets.  
- Strength: novel, elegant methodology—constraint projection is generalizable and the MaxSAT ordering formulation is insightful.  
- Strength: strong technical soundness and thorough, systematic experimental evaluation.  
- Weakness: clarity issues—MaxSAT’s third hard constraint needs better justification or examples.  
- Weakness: potential computational bottleneck—CPDAG pruning may require exhaustive enumeration of equivalence class DAGs; need discussion of frequency and runtime impact.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—missing comparison to Constraint Programming (CP) solvers.  
- Weakness: deeper analysis needed on why projection benefits A* far more than ILP (hypothesis: heuristic tightening aids A* pruning).  
- Overall: paper is original, technically strong, significant, and recommended for acceptance.
