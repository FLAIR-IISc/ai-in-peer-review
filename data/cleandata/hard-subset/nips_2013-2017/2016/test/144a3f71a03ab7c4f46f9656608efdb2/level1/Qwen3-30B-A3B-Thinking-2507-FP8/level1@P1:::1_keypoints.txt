- Problem: Learning optimal Bayesian network structures under non-decomposable ancestral constraints, extending Chen et al.'s EC tree framework.  
- Approach: Prune the EC tree using ancestral constraints and infer decomposable edge/ordering constraints to enable efficient search.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorems 1–4 give a sound and complete method for EC-tree pruning and decomposable constraint inference.  
- Novelty: First to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones and evaluate this projection for efficiency gains.  
- Empirical evaluation: Compared to ILP (GOBNILP) on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) across sizes and constraint levels.  
- Empirical result: Orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP-based methods.  
- Practical significance: Small percentages (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can match the effect of increasing training data by 4–16×.  
- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous proofs and thorough experimental design.  
- Clarity: Well-organized, clearly written, with helpful figures and reproducible detail.  
- Minor clarity issue: Theorem 4’s explanation is dense and would benefit from a more intuitive justification.  
- Limited comparison: Does not sufficiently position against recent score-based constraint-handling methods (e.g., Parviainen & Koivisto 2013).  
- Practical limitation: No discussion on handling inconsistent or contradictory ancestral constraints (e.g., X→Y and Y→X).  
- Pro acceptance: Novel, strong theory, significant empirical gains, and practical relevance.  
- Con acceptance: Needs better handling/discussion of inconsistent constraints, clearer positioning vs related work, and more accessible exposition of some theory.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
