This paper addresses the important problem of learning optimal Bayesian network structures when subject to ancestral constraints. The authors recognize that such constraints are non-decomposable and thus cannot be handled by mainstream exact learning algorithms that rely on decomposable scores. The work builds upon the recent A*-based search framework over equivalence classes (the EC tree by Chen et al., 2016), which uses an "oracle" for decomposable scores to compute its search heuristic. The paper's key contribution is a method to "project" the non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological ordering constraints. These inferred constraints are then passed to the oracle, significantly tightening the heuristic and accelerating the search. This approach is compared empirically against an Integer Linear Programming (ILP) formulation, a standard alternative for handling such constraints.

**Quality & Significance:** The paper is technically sound and makes a significant contribution. The problem of incorporating expert knowledge is highly relevant, and ancestral constraints are an intuitive and powerful form of such knowledge. The experimental evaluation is thorough and provides compelling evidence of the proposed method's effectiveness. The results, showing orders-of-magnitude speedups over the GOBNILP system, clearly demonstrate a substantial advance in the state of the art for this problem. The analysis in Table 3, which highlights that adding a small percentage of constraints can have a similar impact on accuracy as quadrupling the dataset size, underscores the practical importance of this work.

**Originality:** While the EC tree search algorithm itself is from prior work, the core novelty—projecting non-decomposable constraints to empower a decomposable-score oracle—is a clever and original idea. The reduction of the ordering constraint inference problem to MaxSAT is particularly insightful and constitutes a non-trivial contribution. The paper is clear about its relationship to previous work and effectively differentiates its contributions.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The motivation is strong, and the technical sections build clearly on one another. The explanation of how ancestral constraints are used to prune the EC tree is easy to follow.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   Provides an elegant and effective solution to the important and practical problem of learning with ancestral constraints.
*   The novel constraint projection technique results in dramatic, empirically verified performance improvements over a strong ILP baseline.
*   The work advances the capabilities of exact score-based structure learning.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   The core search framework is an extension of existing work, rather than a completely new algorithm. However, the extension itself is significant and novel.

Overall, this is a strong paper with clear contributions and impressive results. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses, and it represents a valuable addition to the field.
