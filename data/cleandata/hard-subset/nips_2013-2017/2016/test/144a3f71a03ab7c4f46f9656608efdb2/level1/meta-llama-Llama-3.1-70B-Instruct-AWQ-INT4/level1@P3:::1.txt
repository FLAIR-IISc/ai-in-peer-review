**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel approach for learning Bayesian networks optimally, subject to background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints. The authors utilize a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree, and employ an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. They propose a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree based on ancestral constraints and show how the employed oracle can be empowered by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints.

**Related Work**

The authors build upon their previous work on learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable scores [Chen et al., 2015, 2016]. They also draw connections to other approaches, such as integer linear programming (ILP) and constraint programming (CP), which can represent ancestral constraints but have not been evaluated for learning structures with these constraints.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient handling of ancestral constraints**: The authors propose a novel approach for incorporating ancestral constraints into the structure learning process, which is more efficient than existing approaches based on ILP.
2. **Empowering the oracle**: By passing decomposable constraints inferred from non-decomposable ancestral constraints to the oracle, the authors demonstrate a significant improvement in the efficiency of structure search.
3. **Scalability**: The approach scales to larger networks (up to 20 variables) and is comparable to the scalability of modern score-based approaches.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach against ILP-based systems and do not compare it to other state-of-the-art methods, such as dynamic programming or heuristic search.
2. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the ancestral constraints are consistent and do not consider the case where the constraints are inconsistent or incomplete.
3. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The authors do not provide a theoretical analysis of the complexity of their approach or the optimality of the learned structures.

**Originality**

The authors propose a novel approach for incorporating ancestral constraints into the structure learning process, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of the EC tree and the empowerment of the oracle by passing decomposable constraints are also original contributions.

**Significance**

The authors demonstrate the significance of their approach by showing that it can be orders-of-magnitude more efficient than alternative frameworks, such as those based on ILP. They also highlight the impact that background knowledge can have on the quality of the learned model.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach and the underlying concepts. However, some of the technical details, such as the proof of Theorem 1, are not provided in the paper and may require additional background knowledge to fully understand.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro acceptance**:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient approach for incorporating ancestral constraints into the structure learning process.
* The approach scales to larger networks and is comparable to the scalability of modern score-based approaches.
* The authors demonstrate the significance of their approach through empirical evaluation.

**Con acceptance**:

* The paper only evaluates the approach against ILP-based systems and does not compare it to other state-of-the-art methods.
* The authors assume that the ancestral constraints are consistent and do not consider the case where the constraints are inconsistent or incomplete.
* The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the complexity of the approach or the optimality of the learned structures.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above. The authors should consider evaluating their approach against other state-of-the-art methods and providing a theoretical analysis of the complexity of their approach. Additionally, they should consider discussing the limitations of their approach, such as the assumption of consistent ancestral constraints.
