- Proposes a novel method for optimal Bayesian network learning under ancestral constraints using the EC tree and an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Builds on authors' previous non-decomposable score work and relates to ILP/CP approaches that can represent ancestral constraints but were not evaluated here.
- Strength: efficiently incorporates ancestral constraints into structure learning, outperforming ILP-based approaches.
- Strength: empowers the oracle by translating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, improving search efficiency.
- Strength: demonstrates scalability to larger networks (up to 20 variables), comparable to modern score-based methods.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to comparisons with ILP-based systems and omits other state-of-the-art methods like dynamic programming or heuristic search.
- Weakness: assumes ancestral constraints are consistent and does not address inconsistent or incomplete constraint scenarios.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of algorithmic complexity and formal guarantees on optimality of learned structures.
- Originality: introduces a novel use of the EC tree and oracle-empowerment to handle ancestral constraints in structure learning.
- Significance: shows orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains over ILP frameworks and highlights the value of background knowledge.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but omits some technical details (e.g., proof of Theorem 1) that may require extra background.
- Pro-accept: novel, efficient approach that scales and is empirically significant.
- Con-accept: limited baseline comparisons, strong assumptions about constraints, and missing theoretical analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to include broader comparisons, theoretical complexity analysis, and discussion of constraint limitations.
