**Summary**

The paper "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints" proposes a novel approach to learning the structure of Bayesian networks optimally, subject to ancestral constraints. Ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, posing a particular difficulty for learning approaches for decomposable scores. The authors utilize a search space for structure learning with non-decomposable scores, called the EC tree, and employ an oracle that optimizes decomposable scores. They also show how the employed oracle can be empowered by passing it decomposable constraints inferred from the non-decomposable ancestral constraints.

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which is a challenging problem in the field.
2. **Theoretical soundness**: The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their approach, including proofs of correctness and completeness.
3. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper includes a thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed approach, comparing it to existing methods and demonstrating its effectiveness.
4. **Scalability**: The approach is shown to be scalable to larger networks, making it a promising solution for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The approach relies on a complex search space and oracle, which may be difficult to implement and understand.
2. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the ancestral constraints are consistent and acyclic, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Limited comparison**: The empirical evaluation only compares the proposed approach to one existing method (ILP), which may not provide a comprehensive view of its performance.
