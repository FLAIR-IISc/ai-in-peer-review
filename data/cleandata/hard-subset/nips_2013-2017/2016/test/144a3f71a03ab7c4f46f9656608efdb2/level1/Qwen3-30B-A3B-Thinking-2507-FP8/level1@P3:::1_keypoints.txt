- Addresses incorporating ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning by projecting them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework to enable use of existing oracles for decomposable scores.  
- Theoretically sound: Theorems 2–4 give sound and complete methods for deriving edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Empirically strong: Shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP across benchmarks and sizes.  
- Practical impact: Small fractions (10–25%) of ancestral constraints can improve model accuracy equivalently to 4× more data (512→2048 samples).  
- Well-organized exposition with clear background, problem statement, methods, and illustrative figures (BN graph and EC tree).  
- High practical relevance for incorporating domain causal knowledge into optimal structure learning.  
- Lacks detailed computational complexity analysis of the constraint projection algorithms, especially the MaxSAT formulation.  
- Missing empirical comparison to other non-decomposable approaches such as constraint programming (CP) methods.  
- Does not discuss handling of inconsistent or conflicting ancestral constraints in practice.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments up to 20 variables and EC tree’s exponential space complexity are not fully addressed.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-executed contribution warranting acceptance, with noted areas for further analysis and comparison.
