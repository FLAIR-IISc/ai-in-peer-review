- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a practically important but computationally hard problem.
- Leverages recent non-decomposable-score optimal-structure-learning framework to handle ancestral constraints.
- Core contribution: method to project ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into decomposable constraints usable by an existing oracle.
- Builds on Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and correctly identifies ancestral constraints as non-decomposable obstacles to traditional algorithms.
- Positions related work (ILP, CP, constraint-based methods) appropriately and motivates the new approach clearly.
- The projection idea is novel and bridges non-decomposable and decomposable frameworks effectively.
- Theoretical justification (Section 5) and pruning strategy for the EC tree (Section 4.3) appear technically sound and well-defined.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, compares against GOBNILP, varies n, N, p and shows significant speedups and clear impact of constraint projection.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses consistent, clear notation.
- Reduction to MaxSAT for inferring ordering constraints is reasonable but may not scale for very large networks.
- Paper focuses mainly on ancestral constraints and could discuss extending the projection method to other non-decomposable constraint types.
- Performance depends heavily on the chosen oracle (URLEARNING); sensitivity analysis over different oracles is lacking.
- Discussion of Structural Hamming Distance (Δ) is brief and could better relate the metric to constraints and network complexity.
- Overall: strong, novel, technically and empirically validated contribution with some concerns about MaxSAT scalability and oracle dependence; recommended for acceptance.
