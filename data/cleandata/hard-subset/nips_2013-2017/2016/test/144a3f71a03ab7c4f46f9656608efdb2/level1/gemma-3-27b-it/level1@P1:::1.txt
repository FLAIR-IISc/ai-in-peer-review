This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a crucial aspect for incorporating domain knowledge into structure learning. The authors build upon their previous work on optimal structure learning with non-decomposable scores, leveraging an oracle for decomposable scores. The key contribution lies in demonstrating how to project ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints, empowering the oracle and significantly improving efficiency compared to ILP-based approaches.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the problem, related work, and proposed solution. It correctly identifies the limitations of traditional approaches that rely on decomposable scores when dealing with non-decomposable constraints like ancestral ones. The connection to previous work, particularly [Chen et al., 2015, 2016] and the related literature on ILP and constraint programming, is appropriately established. The introduction clearly motivates the work and highlights the practical importance of incorporating background knowledge. The explanation of ancestral constraints and their non-decomposability is concise and effective. The theoretical contributions, particularly Theorems 1-4, are stated clearly and appear sound. The experimental evaluation demonstrates a substantial performance improvement over ILP, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones to empower a decomposable-score oracle is a novel and effective strategy.
*   **Efficiency:** The empirical results clearly demonstrate significant speedups compared to ILP-based methods, making the proposed approach practical for larger networks.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The theorems presented provide a solid foundation for the pruning and projection techniques.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, despite the technical complexity of the topic.
*   **Significance:** Incorporating background knowledge into Bayesian network learning is important, and this paper provides a practical and efficient method for doing so. The authors demonstrate this with compelling experiments.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability Limitations:** While the results show scalability up to 20 variables, it remains unclear how well the approach scales to much larger networks (e.g., hundreds or thousands of variables) commonly encountered in real-world applications.
*   **MaxSAT Solver Dependency:** The reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) introduces a dependency and potential performance bottleneck.  The performance of the overall system is tied to the efficiency of the MaxSAT solver. A more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential optimizations for this step would be beneficial.
*   **Projection Completeness:** The paper claims a *complete* projection of constraints. It would be valuable to discuss any limitations on *which* ancestral constraints can be effectively projected, or any cases where projection might lead to a loss of information or sub-optimal results. Is there a scenario where inferring *too many* decomposable constraints actually hurts performance?
*   **Limited Comparison:** The comparison is primarily against GOBNILP. While this is a good baseline, it would be beneficial to compare against other state-of-the-art structure learning algorithms, even if they don't directly support ancestral constraints (and thus require a workaround).
*   **Detailed Heuristic Analysis:** While the authors mention the heuristic function's role, a more in-depth discussion on the quality of the heuristic and its impact on search efficiency would be helpful.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in Bayesian network structure learning.
*   The proposed approach is novel, theoretically sound, and empirically effective.
*   The performance improvements over existing methods are significant.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability limitations to very large networks remain a concern.
*   The dependency on an external MaxSAT solver might be a bottleneck.
*   The completeness of constraint projection isn’t thoroughly explored.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach is innovative and provides a significant improvement in efficiency compared to existing methods. While some limitations exist, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above regarding scalability, MaxSAT solver dependency, projection completeness, and a more detailed analysis of the heuristic function.  Specifically, the authors should discuss potential strategies for improving scalability (e.g., parallelization) and mitigating the dependency on the MaxSAT solver.
