- Overall: Significant, technically sound contribution for learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints; strong positive recommendation for acceptance.  
- Problem: Tackles optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints, common in real-world causal/domain knowledge.  
- Approach: Adapts Chen et al.’s EC-tree/oracle framework, prunes EC trees using constraint satisfaction, and projects ancestral constraints into decomposable forms for the oracle.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides formal results (Theorems 1–4) supporting pruning and constraint projection techniques.  
- Constraint projection: Novel and impactful technique that converts ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to empower the oracle.  
- Empirical results: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and scalability demonstrated up to 20 variables.  
- Positioning: Well-contextualized with relevant prior work (Darwiche, Koller & Friedman, ILP methods, Chen et al., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).  
- Strength — gap addressed: Directly resolves a major limitation of existing optimal structure learning methods regarding non-decomposable constraints.  
- Strength — technical soundness: Elegant adaptation of EC trees and a sound pruning strategy with theoretical support.  
- Strength — presentation: Clear, well-organized writing and formalization via theorems.  
- Weakness — oracle reliance: Overall performance depends heavily on the underlying oracle (URLEARNING) and its limits.  
- Weakness — complexity: Framework complexity (EC trees, oracles, projection) may hinder adoption; implementation or simplification could help.  
- Weakness — MaxSAT dependency: Efficiency of constraint projection hinges on MaxSAT solver performance (EVASOLVER used), which may vary.  
- Weakness — scope: Focuses mainly on ancestral constraints; limited exploration of other non-decomposable constraint types.  
- Originality: High — extends prior work to ancestral constraints and introduces the constraint projection idea.  
- Significance: High — enables incorporation of domain/causal knowledge into optimal BN learning with practical speedups.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Solves an important open problem, technically sound, empirically strong, novel projection technique, and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on external oracle, complex framework, and reliance on MaxSAT solver performance.  
- Final recommendation: Strongly accept.
