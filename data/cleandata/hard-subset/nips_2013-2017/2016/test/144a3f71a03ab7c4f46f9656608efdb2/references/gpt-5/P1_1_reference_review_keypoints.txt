- Problem: optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor/not ancestor of Y).
- Approach: extend EC-tree framework to prune search using ancestral constraints and project them into decomposable edge/order constraints for a decomposable-score oracle (URLearning).
- Theoretical contributions: rules to infer negative/positive edge constraints (Theorems 2–3) and a MaxSAT formulation to derive safe ordering constraints (Theorem 4).
- Empirical claim: large (often orders-of-magnitude) speedups over GOBNILP on standard BN benchmarks, with analysis of accuracy (SHD) and search hardness as constraints vary.
- Relation to prior work: fills gap left by order-graph DP/A* methods (which can’t enforce ancestral constraints) and complements ILP/CP encodings and path-belief priors.
- Strength: addresses an important practical class of background knowledge that prior optimal score-based methods handle poorly.
- Strength: solid conceptual integration—CPDAG-based pruning of the EC tree plus heuristic strengthening via projected decomposable constraints.
- Strength: clear theorems for edge entailment and a principled MaxSAT approach for ordering constraints.
- Strength: strong empirical performance and useful analysis of easy–hard–easy behavior and accuracy improvements.
- Strength: exact algorithm that builds on widely used components (URLearning, GOBNILP, public MaxSAT solvers).
- Weakness: missing proofs and incomplete theoretical justification for key results (especially Theorems 3–4 and the sufficiency clause for acyclicity).
- Weakness: MaxSAT ordering projection is sufficient but not complete; the paper overstates “maximally large set” without clarifying the gap to true entailment.
- Weakness: CPDAG pruning resorts to enumerating all DAGs when tests are inconclusive, which can be exponential—no polynomial-time criterion or practical bounds given.
- Weakness: experiments use noiseless, simulated constraints only—no evaluation of robustness to misspecified or conflicting constraints.
- Weakness: limited baselines—only compares to ILP (GOBNILP) and omits CP-based encodings despite discussing them.
- Weakness: indegree-unrestricted experiments bias against ILP baselines; controlled comparisons with indegree caps are needed.
- Weakness: reported scalability only up to n≈20; node/edge expansions and memory footprints are not reported to contextualize limits.
- Missing detail: no complexity analysis for the edge-projection algorithm, closure process, or worst-case bounds.
- Missing detail: no measurement of MaxSAT and projection overhead vs. observed speedups; ablation experiments (“no projection,” “edge only,” “ordering only”) are missing.
- Missing detail: unclear how ordering constraints are integrated into URLearning and whether all projected constraints are supported.
- Clarity: some notation/definitions (e.g., “partially directed path” test) lack precise references; some references are garbled and figures/tables are insufficiently described.
- Quality assessment: technically sound with compelling design and solid experiments, but theoretical claims and worst-case complexities need strengthening.
- Originality: novel and meaningful integration of ancestral constraints into EC-tree search and projection into decomposable constraints via MaxSAT.
- Significance: high practical impact for users with causal/temporal prior knowledge; methods likely transferable to other non-decomposable priors.
- Suggestions: provide proofs/appendix for theorems and MaxSAT encoding, clarify completeness vs. sufficiency.
- Suggestions: replace or bound CPDAG enumeration, report how often enumeration occurs in practice.
- Suggestions: evaluate robustness to noisy/incorrect constraints and add diagnostics for inconsistent constraint sets.
- Suggestions: add ablation studies, report counts of projected constraints and MaxSAT/runtime overhead.
- Suggestions: compare to a CP baseline and run experiments with indegree limits to control for ILP weaknesses.
- Suggestions: detail how ordering constraints are enforced in the oracle and include pseudocode for projection procedures.
- Editorial: fix formatting/typos, clean up references, and include clearer figures/tables.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — promising and effective approach but needs stronger theoretical justification and more comprehensive experimental analysis.
