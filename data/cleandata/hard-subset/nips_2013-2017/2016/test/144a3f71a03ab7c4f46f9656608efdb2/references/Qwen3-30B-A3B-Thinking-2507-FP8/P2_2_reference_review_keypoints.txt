- Paper addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Key contributions: EC-tree pruning for ancestral constraints, projection of ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge/ordering constraints, and empirical methods showing large speedups.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework and contrasts with decomposable-score approaches.  
- Theorems 1–4 provide rigorous correctness guarantees for pruning and constraint projection.  
- Constraint projection algorithms are sound and well-motivated with formal proofs.  
- Experiments are comprehensive: benchmark networks, variations in variables/data/constraint percentages, 50 trials, and clear statistical reporting.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP-based GOBNILP (with/without projection).  
- Small fractions of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can yield model-quality gains comparable to much larger increases in training data (SHD results).  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; explains decomposable vs non-decomposable constraints and EC-tree intuitively with helpful figures.  
- Originality: novel projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into edge and ordering constraints (including MaxSAT for orders).  
- Significance: practical for incorporating domain causal knowledge and enabling existing decomposable-score algorithms to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Weakness: needs stronger justification/comparison for using the EC tree over other search spaces (order graphs, ILP).  
- Weakness: limited discussion on scalability and behavior when the number of ancestral constraints grows or when constraints are inconsistent.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could include more baselines (e.g., constraint programming approaches).  
- Weakness: could better relate to recent advances in BN structure learning such as deep-learning–based methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, well-evaluated work that advances the state of the art.  
- Recommendation: accept — paper makes a substantial contribution with minor areas for improvement.
