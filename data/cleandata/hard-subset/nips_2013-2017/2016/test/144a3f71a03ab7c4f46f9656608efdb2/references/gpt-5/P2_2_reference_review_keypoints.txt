- Summary: Proposes A* search over CPDAGs for exact BN structure learning under hard ancestral/path constraints by pruning infeasible nodes and projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.
- Method: Uses a decomposable-score oracle (URLearning) as an admissible heuristic, projects ancestral constraints to edge/order constraints, and prunes EC-tree nodes via CPDAG path-feasibility tests.
- Theory: Presents entailment characterizations for negative/positive edge constraints (Theorems 2–3) and a sufficient MaxSAT encoding to infer many safe ordering constraints (Theorem 4).
- Practical contribution: Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP baseline GOBNILP on benchmarks, especially when using projection, and shows an easy–hard–easy runtime trend with constraint density.
- Relation to prior work: Extends BN/EC-tree search to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints and complements ILP/CP approaches by systematically projecting constraints into a decomposable form for heuristic guidance.
- Technical soundness: Pruning via CPDAG path feasibility and the edge-entailment theorems are sound; MaxSAT projection is intentionally sufficient (not complete) to preserve admissibility.
- Theorem 1 issue: Theorem 1 appears misstated (likely should state monotonicity of violation); needs correction/clarification.
- Experimental support: Comparisons to GOBNILP are appropriate and show strong empirical gains; accuracy improves with more data and constraints.
- Missing runtime analyses: Paper omits runtime/overhead details for CPDAG class enumeration when tests are inconclusive and for MaxSAT solving; frequency and cost of exponential enumeration are not reported.
- Robustness concern: No experiments or discussion on handling inconsistent or noisy constraints; the method assumes a maximum consistent constraint set.
- Clarity issues: Need precise definition of “no partially directed paths,” explicit algorithm for CPDAG tests, and statement of any practical heuristics/limits for enumeration.
- Omitted details: Closure rules for ancestral constraints are omitted for space and should be summarized in an appendix; proofs or sketches for Theorems 2–4 should be included.
- Reproducibility gaps: Code availability unclear; MaxSAT solver parameters, time limits, and pseudocode for edge-projection and MaxSAT encoding should be reported.
- Originality: Novel integration of hard ancestral constraints into EC-tree search, exact edge-projection theory, and MaxSAT-based safe ordering inference represent a non-trivial, original combination.
- Significance: Practically valuable for causal discovery and exact BN learning under constraints; shows that projecting non-decomposable constraints can greatly improve search efficiency.
- Strengths: Clear motivation, sound pruning, admissible heuristic via oracle, theoretical edge-entailment results, and strong empirical performance with informative ablations.
- Weaknesses: Theorem 1 wording and omitted details hurt clarity; missing analyses on CPDAG enumeration and MaxSAT bottlenecks; no treatment of inconsistent/noisy constraints and limited baselines.
- Questions for authors: Provide/correct Theorem 1, include proofs/sketches for Theorems 2–4, quantify CPDAG/MaxSAT costs, discuss handling inconsistent constraints and possible soft relaxations, and clarify how derived constraints are injected into the oracle.
- Baselines: Recommend comparisons to MaxSAT-based BN learning (Cussens 2008) and CP-based methods to strengthen empirical claims.
- Implementation detail request: Explain exactly how derived edge/order constraints prune parent sets or constrain ordering while preserving heuristic admissibility.
- Pros: Addresses an important gap, offers solid theory and empirically effective projection, and delivers substantial speedups vs. a leading ILP baseline.
- Cons: Some theoretical and clarity gaps, limited analysis of potential bottlenecks, and no evaluation of noisy/inconsistent constraints.
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on fixing clarity issues and adding modest empirical analyses of projection overhead and enumeration costs.
