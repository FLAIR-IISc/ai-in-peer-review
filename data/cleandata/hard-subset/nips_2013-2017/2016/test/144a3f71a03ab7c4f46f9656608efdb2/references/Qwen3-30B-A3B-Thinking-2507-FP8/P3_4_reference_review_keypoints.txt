- Paper extends the EC tree search to incorporate non-decomposable ancestral constraints and provides a sound pruning method (Theorem 1).
- Introduces novel constraint projection techniques to transform ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints (Theorems 2 and 3).
- Enables use of standard decomposable-score oracles for non-decomposable constraints via the projection approach.
- Empirical evaluation shows orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP across network sizes and constraint densities.
- Demonstrates that modest amounts of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can match large increases in training data for model accuracy.
- Clarifies the important conceptual distinction between decomposable and non-decomposable constraints in structure learning.
- Builds on and connects to prior work (Chen et al. 2016, Yuan & Malone 2013), but could better position relative to more recent constraint-based methods.
- Main scalability concerns: claim of scaling to 20 variables lacks analysis of how projection complexity grows with number of constraints.
- MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints is introduced but its computational complexity and scaling with constraint density are not discussed.
- Experiments are limited to simulated benchmark data and lack real-world case studies with domain-specific ancestral constraints.
- The paper does not address handling inconsistent or contradictory ancestral constraints and how to detect unsatisfiable constraint sets.
- Technical content and proofs are sound and presentation is clear, though Section 5 could be more concise and practical limitations of projection need more detail.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to (1) discuss constraint projection complexity and limitations, (2) compare to recent constraint-based NIPS methods, and (3) describe handling of inconsistent constraints.
