This paper addresses the important problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a scenario frequently encountered in applications where prior knowledge exists about causal relationships. The core idea of leveraging an oracle for decomposable scores within a non-decomposable constraint setting is a sound one, building on the authors' prior work. The paper aims to integrate these ancestral constraints into the EC tree search space, demonstrating improved efficiency over ILP-based approaches.

However, while the ideas presented are potentially valuable, the paper suffers from a significant number of shortcomings in both presentation and technical detail, ultimately leading to a negative recommendation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Relevant Problem:** Learning with constraints is crucial for practical Bayesian network applications.
*   **Sound Core Idea:** The approach of utilizing an oracle within a constrained search space is a logical extension of prior work.
*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The comparison against ILP-based methods provides some evidence of the approach's efficiency.
*   **Theoretical Framework:** The theorems regarding constraint projection are conceptually interesting.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Lack of Clarity & Organization:** The paper is difficult to follow. The connections between sections are often unclear, and the flow of ideas feels disjointed. For instance, the jump from the introduction of the EC tree (Section 4.2) to its adaptation for ancestral constraints (Section 4.3) is abrupt.
*   **Insufficient Detail:** The descriptions of algorithms and procedures lack necessary detail for reproducibility. For example, the MaxSAT reduction described in Section 5.2 is presented at a very high level, without sufficient information on how the clauses are constructed or optimized. What specific MaxSAT solver was used, and what parameters were tuned?
*   **Overstated Claims:** Claims of "orders-of-magnitude improvements" (Introduction and Section 6) need rigorous substantiation. The tables present relative speedups, but their statistical significance is not assessed. Are these speedups consistent across all experiments, or are they heavily influenced by specific parameter settings?
*   **Inconsistent Notation:** The use of notation is inconsistent. For example, the definition of 'X' and 'bold X' is unclear and not consistently applied. The meaning of 'G(A)' and 'G(O)' in section 5 is not immediately clear from earlier definitions.
*   **Missing Experimental Details:** The experimental setup lacks critical details. How were the sub-networks N selected? How were the ancestral constraints randomly generated? What were the exact parameter settings for both the EC tree and ILP approaches? Reporting the range of values and the default values would significantly improve the replicability and interpretability of the results.
*   **Weak Comparison:** The comparison to GOBNILP is limited. The authors state they provided both systems with projected constraints, but a more thorough ablation study would be beneficial, showing the impact of projection alone. Was the ILP implementation optimized? The paper does not provide sufficient detail to judge the fairness of the comparison.
*   **Lack of Discussion:** The discussion of results is superficial. For instance, the “easy-hard-easy” trend observed in Table 3 is not adequately explained. What is causing this pattern?
*   **Minor Errors:** A few minor errors are present. For instance, “th Conference” in the Introduction.

**Pro & Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Addresses a relevant and challenging problem.
*   Demonstrates potential performance gains over existing methods.

**Con:**

*   Significant clarity and presentation issues.
*   Lack of technical detail hinders reproducibility.
*   Overstated claims and insufficient statistical analysis.
*   Weak experimental setup and comparison.
*   Inconsistent notation and minor errors.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite addressing a worthwhile problem, the paper falls short of NIPS standards due to its numerous weaknesses in clarity, detail, and rigor. The empirical results, while promising, are not presented convincingly enough to outweigh these shortcomings. The lack of sufficient detail makes it impossible to assess the validity of the claims and prevents reproducibility. The numerous minor issues further detract from the paper's quality.

**Recommendation:** **Reject**. The authors should significantly revise the paper, addressing the clarity and detail issues, providing a more rigorous experimental evaluation, and toning down overstated claims. A more thorough comparison to alternative approaches and a more in-depth discussion of the results are also crucial for acceptance.
