- Paper extends EC-tree search for exact BN structure learning to enforce hard ancestral constraints via pruning and projection.  
- Main contributions: pruning non-extendable CPDAGs, projecting ancestral constraints to entailed edges and orderings, and a MaxSAT formulation for maximal consistent ordering constraints.  
- Builds on Chen et al.'s BN/EC graphs and oracle-guided A*; contrasts with DP/order-graph methods (assume decomposability) and ILP/CP approaches (can encode constraints).  
- Pruning strategy is conceptually sound with efficient sufficient checks and a complete fallback of enumerating class(P) when inconclusive.  
- α/β-based projection rules (Theorems 2–3) for entailed edges are well-motivated and appear correct under maximal closure assumptions.  
- MaxSAT encoding enforces transitivity/acyclicity sufficiency (Theorem 4) and produces a safe but possibly conservative set of ordering constraints O.  
- Empirical evaluation is careful: diverse benchmarks, varied n/data/constraint density, ablations, time/memory limits, and SHD reporting.  
- Reported results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP (ILP) and an easy–hard–easy difficulty profile as constraint density varies.  
- Theorem 1 and the monotonicity/pruning claim are unclear; need precise definitions for partial CPDAG satisfaction vs. full graphs.  
- The exponential fallback (enumerating class(P)) is potentially costly; paper should report frequency and mitigation heuristics or bounds.  
- Details omitted on computing maximal closure of ancestral constraints limit reproducibility; provide full inference rules and pseudocode.  
- The MaxSAT acyclicity condition is non-standard and only sufficient; more intuition, derivation, and an ablation of its impact are needed.  
- Method assumes hard/correct constraints only; no handling or evaluation of inconsistent/noisy (soft) constraints.  
- Baselines limited to ILP; missing comparisons to CP-based methods and order-graph approaches augmented with constraints.  
- Scalability is modest (results up to ~20 variables); discuss bottlenecks (oracle cost, projection, CPDAG enumeration) and limits as n grows.  
- Writing is generally clear, but Theorem 1, closure rules, MaxSAT encoding, and definition of a “closed” set O need clarification and added pseudocode/appendix.  
- Integration of ancestral constraints into EC-tree/A*-based exact learning and the MaxSAT projection approach is novel and significant for causal discovery.  
- Recommendation: lean accept — solid, novel approach with compelling empirical gains, but needs clearer theory details, reproducibility aids, and broader baselines.
