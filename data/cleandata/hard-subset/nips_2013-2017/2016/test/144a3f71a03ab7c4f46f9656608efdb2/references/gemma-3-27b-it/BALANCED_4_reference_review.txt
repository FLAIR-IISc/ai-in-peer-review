This paper addresses the important problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, which represent background knowledge about causal relationships. The authors acknowledge the limitations of existing approaches that struggle with non-decomposable constraints and propose a novel framework building upon the work of Chen et al. [2015, 2016]. They leverage the EC tree search space and demonstrate how to incorporate ancestral constraints by pruning the search space and projecting them into decomposable constraints to empower an oracle used within the search process. The empirical evaluation compares their approach against an ILP-based method, showcasing significant performance improvements.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Incorporating background knowledge into Bayesian network structure learning is crucial for real-world applications, and ancestral constraints are a natural and expressive way to represent such knowledge.
* **Novel approach:** The adaptation of the EC tree framework to handle non-decomposable constraints is a significant contribution.  The idea of projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable ones to guide the search is particularly clever and appears effective.
* **Strong empirical results:** The experimental results demonstrate a clear and substantial performance advantage over the ILP-based approach, especially as the network size increases. The scalability analysis is also insightful.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The theorems providing soundness guarantees for the pruning step and the constraint projection are valuable.
* **Clear writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, approach, and results.  The organization is logical, and the technical details are presented with sufficient clarity.
* **Thorough comparison:**  The comparison against the ILP approach provides a strong baseline for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited discussion of related work:** While the introduction mentions some related work, the discussion feels somewhat superficial.  For instance, the connection to constraint-based algorithms like PC (and the work of Borboudakis and Tsamardinos [2012, 2013]) could be more thoroughly discussed, along with the differences in their approaches (e.g., handling of uncertainty in constraints, scalability).  Similarly, there's room for more discussion on how this work compares to other approaches for incorporating prior knowledge beyond ancestral constraints.
* **Oracle Dependence:** The performance heavily relies on the oracle (URLEARNING) used within the EC tree search. While the authors demonstrate its effectiveness, the robustness of the approach to different oracle choices isn’t fully explored.  A sensitivity analysis or discussion of potential oracle limitations would be beneficial.
* **MaxSAT Solver Choice:** The choice of EVASOLVER for MaxSAT is stated but not justified. Are there other solvers that could potentially provide better performance or quality of projected constraints? A brief discussion would strengthen the presentation.
* **Dataset Generation:** The experimental setup relies on simulated data.  While a reasonable starting point, it would be valuable to evaluate the method on real-world datasets to demonstrate its practical applicability.
* **Structural Hamming Distance Interpretation**: The interpretation of the structural hamming distance is limited. Providing more context around what constitutes a "good" or "bad" result would aid the reader.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a challenging and important problem.
* The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.
* The theoretical grounding adds credibility to the work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discussion of related work could be more comprehensive.
* The dependence on the oracle and the choice of MaxSAT solver could be addressed with further analysis.
* The lack of evaluation on real-world datasets limits the assessment of practical applicability.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach for incorporating ancestral constraints is novel, well-motivated, and supported by compelling empirical results. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS, but suggest the authors address the concerns regarding related work, oracle dependence, and dataset evaluation in a revised version.

**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions).
