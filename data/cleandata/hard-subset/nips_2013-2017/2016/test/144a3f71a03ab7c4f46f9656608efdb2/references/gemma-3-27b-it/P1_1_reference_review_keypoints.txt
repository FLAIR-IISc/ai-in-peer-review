- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints that specify ancestor–descendant relations.  
- Builds on prior work to integrate ancestral constraints into an EC tree search space and prune that space accordingly.  
- Introduces a method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for use by an oracle.  
- Reduction to MaxSAT is used within the approach, but implementation details (solver, clause construction) are insufficiently described.  
- Empirical evaluation shows significant performance improvements over an ILP-based baseline, especially as network size grows.  
- Theoretical results (projection theorems and pruning soundness) strengthen the paper’s rigor.  
- Demonstrates impact: even a small fraction of ancestral constraints can notably improve model accuracy.  
- Paper is generally well-written and uses examples to clarify complex concepts.  
- Lacks higher-level motivation for choosing EC trees, BN graphs, and MaxSAT rather than other search spaces.  
- Comparisons are limited—mainly against a single ILP method—and do not include baselines that ignore ancestral constraints or other state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Scalability remains limited (experiments up to ~20 variables) and the paper does not explore further scaling strategies (approximation, parallelization).  
- Experiments use standard benchmarks; results on larger or more complex real-world datasets are not presented.  
- Practical implementation could be challenging for practitioners due to approach complexity.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, primarily to add MaxSAT implementation details and broader comparisons.
