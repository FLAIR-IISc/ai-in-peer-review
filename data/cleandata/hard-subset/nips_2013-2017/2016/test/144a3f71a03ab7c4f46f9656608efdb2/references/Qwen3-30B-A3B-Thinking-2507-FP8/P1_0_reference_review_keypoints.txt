- Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Main contributions: (1) pruning EC tree by ancestral constraints, (2) projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, (3) empirical speedups versus ILP.  
- Extends Chen et al.'s BN graph and EC tree search spaces to handle ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical development is sound with formal proofs for Theorems 1–4.  
- Provides efficient algorithms for edge-constraint projection and a MaxSAT-based approach (EVASOLVER) for ordering constraints.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) varying variables, sample sizes, and constraint percentages.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over the ILP-based GOBNILP system.  
- Shows 10–25% ancestral constraints can yield accuracy gains comparable to substantially increasing dataset size.  
- Notes limitation: potential computational cost when enumerating structures in Markov equivalence classes for CPDAG pruning.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible with clear figures; could better explain why ancestral constraints are non-decomposable and expand comparisons.  
- Novelty: first to project ancestral constraints into decomposable forms to leverage existing decomposable-score oracles.  
- Significance: practical impact—incorporating domain knowledge improves efficiency and accuracy and may generalize to other non-decomposable constraints.  
- Strengths: fills a gap, rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, practical methodology, careful discussion of limitations.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of projection-step complexity for large networks, incomplete comparison with other constraint-based methods, reliance on simulated data, unclear real-world availability of ancestral constraints.  
- Pro arguments: important problem, solid theory, strong empirical gains, practical framework for domain knowledge.  
- Con arguments: heavy reliance on prior EC-tree work, simulated-data focus, missing complexity analysis of projection step.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
