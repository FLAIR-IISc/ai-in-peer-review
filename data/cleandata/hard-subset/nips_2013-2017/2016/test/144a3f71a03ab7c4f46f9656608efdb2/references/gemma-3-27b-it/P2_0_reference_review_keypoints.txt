- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral (ancestor/descendant) constraints by adapting a framework for non-decomposable priors.  
- Uses EC-tree pruning and projects ancestral constraints into decomposable forms to enable an oracle-based optimal search.  
- Empirically outperforms ILP (GOBNILP) by orders of magnitude and scales to 20 variables.  
- The projection idea is novel and effective, enabling handling of previously intractable non-decomposable constraints.  
- The method is theoretically sound with pruning correctness (Theorem 1) and completeness of constraint projection (Theorem 4).  
- Builds clearly on prior work and is well-written and organized.  
- Addresses an important, practical problem of incorporating background knowledge into BN learning.  
- Strong empirical evaluation demonstrating the benefit of constraint projection and significant speedups.  
- Framework complexity (BN graphs, EC trees, oracles) may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with prior work; more intuitive explanation/examples would help.  
- Heavy reliance on the oracle (URLEARNING) without thorough sensitivity analysis to different oracles.  
- Comparison against ILP is limited to one solver (GOBNILP); broader ILP comparisons would strengthen claims.  
- Dependence on an external MaxSAT solver for ordering projection adds complexity and may limit scalability.  
- Quality scored 9/10, clarity 8/10, originality 9/10, significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel/effective approach, strong empirical and theoretical support, extends promising prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex framework, reliance on MaxSAT solver, and oracle dependence affecting generalizability.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor concerns; recommendation: Strong Accept.
