- Problem: tackles optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor of Y / not), which mainstream exact learners poorly support.
- Method: adapts EC-tree/DAG search to prune CPDAG nodes using ancestral constraints and projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to strengthen oracles.
- Edge projection: provides necessary-and-sufficient conditions (Theorems 2–3) to infer positive/negative edge constraints via ancestral closure.
- Ordering projection: encodes topological ordering constraints as a MaxSAT problem with soundness guarantees (Theorem 4).
- Empirics: reports orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) across benchmarks, dataset sizes, and constraint densities with an easy–hard–easy trend.
- Practicality: uses standard benchmarks and existing tools (URLEARNING, EVASOLVER); approach can extend to k-best enumeration.
- Strength: clear motivation—ancestral constraints are important but non-decomposable and under-served.
- Strength: novel integration of ancestral constraint handling into exact BN learning via EC-tree pruning and projection.
- Strength: empirical ablation shows projection greatly improves heuristic quality and runtime.
- Strength: theoretical contributions for edge entailment and sound ordering projection.
- Weakness: EC-tree may fallback to enumerating all DAGs in a CPDAG, which can be exponential—frequency and overhead not quantified.
- Weakness: MaxSAT ordering projection is claimed “maximally large” but Theorem 4 only gives sufficient (sound) conditions; completeness/maximality guarantees unclear.
- Weakness: algorithm for computing ancestral closure ("maximum set") is omitted—closure rules, soundness, and complexity missing.
- Weakness: experiments limited to small variable counts (n ≤ 20); should include parent-size caps to scale and to give ILP a fairer comparison.
- Weakness: only ILP baseline evaluated; CP-based approaches that can encode non-decomposable constraints are omitted.
- Weakness: missing experimental details—GOBNILP/SCIP versions, acyclicity constraint handling, time/memory limits, and treatment of projected constraints in ILP.
- Weakness: need ablations separating edge vs ordering projection effects, counts of inferred constraints, node expansion impact, and MaxSAT runtime.
- Clarity: notation like “maximum set” should be defined precisely (closure); proofs or appendices for Theorems 2–4 requested.
- Presentation: several typos, formatting glitches, and incomplete references need cleanup.
- Quality: technically sound overall; EC-tree pruning and oracle admissibility are reasonable; ordering projection sound but completeness unclear.
- Originality: novel combination of EC-tree pruning and projection of ancestral constraints; prior work used path constraints in other settings.
- Significance: meaningful advance for incorporating domain causal knowledge into exact BN learning with substantial empirical gains.
- Pros for acceptance: principled framework, solid theory for projections, strong empirical improvements, practical relevance.
- Cons for acceptance: missing algorithmic/proof details, ambiguous completeness of ordering projection, limited baselines and modest problem sizes, unquantified exponential fallback.
- Suggestions: add proofs/appendix, closure algorithm and complexity, quantify CPDAG enumeration frequency/cost, consider stronger CPDAG tests, add CP baseline or justify omission, include parent caps, and report MaxSAT overhead and ablations.
- Recommendation: weak accept (high confidence in contribution, medium in empirical generality).
