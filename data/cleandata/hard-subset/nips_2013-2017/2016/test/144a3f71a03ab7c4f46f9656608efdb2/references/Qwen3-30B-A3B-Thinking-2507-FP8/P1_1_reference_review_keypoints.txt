- Problem: Incorporates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning, a gap in existing methods.  
- Approach: Leverages the EC tree search space to efficiently handle ancestral constraints.  
- Contributions: Sound EC-tree pruning method based on ancestral constraints.  
- Contributions: Technique to infer decomposable edge and ordering constraints from non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Contributions: Empirical demonstration of orders-of-magnitude speedup versus ILP-based GOBNILP.  
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous theoretical foundations with formal proofs (Theorems 1–4).  
- Empirical quality: Comprehensive experiments on multiple benchmark networks, dataset sizes, and constraint percentages.  
- Baselines and metrics: Careful comparisons with GOBNILP (with/without projected constraints) using structural Hamming distance and resource details.  
- Clarity: Well-written, well-organized, consistent notation, and helpful figures for understanding.  
- Reproducibility: Sufficient experimental detail for expert reproduction.  
- Originality: Novel projection of ancestral constraints onto decomposable edge/order constraints and novel use of EC tree for this problem.  
- Significance: Practically important—small numbers of ancestral constraints can match large increases in data and yield substantial efficiency gains.  
- Scalability: Demonstrated scalability to ~20 variables, competitive with state-of-the-art score-based approaches.  
- Strength: Strong practical impact and meaningful application to causal discovery and knowledge incorporation.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation and clear demonstration of efficiency gains.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison with constraint-based methods (e.g., PC algorithm) and insufficient exploration of differences.  
- Weakness: Complexity analysis of the constraint projection algorithms (particularly MaxSAT reduction) is limited.  
- Weakness: Assumes BDeu score with ESS=1; extension to other scoring functions not discussed.  
- Weakness: Handling of many or inconsistent ancestral constraints is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Acceptance arguments: Sound theory, novel method, strong empirical speedups, practical relevance, and clear presentation support acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited comparisons to other paradigms, incomplete complexity analysis, and unclear handling of inconsistent constraints temper acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Overall recommendation to accept for NIPS due to significant contribution and strong results.  
- Suggested improvements: Add comparison with constraint-based methods, provide complexity analysis of MaxSAT reduction, show handling of inconsistent constraints, discuss extensions to other scores, and compare with newer ILP methods.
