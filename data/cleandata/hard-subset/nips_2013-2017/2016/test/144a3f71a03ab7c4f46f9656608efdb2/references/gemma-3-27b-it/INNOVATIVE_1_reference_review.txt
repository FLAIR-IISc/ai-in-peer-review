This paper tackles the challenging problem of learning Bayesian networks under ancestral constraints, a practically relevant scenario where prior knowledge needs to be integrated into structure learning. The key novelty lies in adapting the recent framework of Chen et al. (2015, 2016) – based on navigating the space of DAGs with an oracle – to handle the non-decomposable nature of ancestral constraints. The authors cleverly propose projecting these constraints onto decomposable ones to empower the oracle, significantly improving efficiency. This is a bold approach, as it directly addresses a fundamental limitation of existing methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of integrating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into the EC tree framework is genuinely novel. Existing methods, including ILP-based ones, struggle with this directly.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The theorems presented regarding constraint projection and soundness of pruning are strong indicators of a well-grounded approach.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling. The reported orders-of-magnitude improvement over GOBNILP is substantial and demonstrates the practical benefits of the proposed approach. The impact of constraint projection is also clearly highlighted.
* **Clear Connection to Prior Work:** The paper carefully positions its contribution within the existing landscape of Bayesian network structure learning, referencing relevant work and explicitly highlighting the limitations of previous approaches.
* **Scalability:** Demonstrating scalability up to 20 variables, and acknowledging the limitations while comparing favorably to existing methods, is realistic and valuable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments demonstrate significant improvements, the simulated datasets are relatively simple. Evaluating the approach on real-world datasets with complex dependencies and noisy data would strengthen the findings.
* **MaxSAT Solver Dependence:** The efficiency gain from constraint projection relies heavily on the performance of the MaxSAT solver. A more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the projection step itself would be beneficial.
* **Oracle Performance:** The performance is directly tied to the oracle’s (URLEARNING) performance. While a strong oracle is desirable, acknowledging potential bottlenecks or limitations of the oracle itself would be helpful.
* **Incomplete Validation of Projected Constraints:** The paper showcases benefits from the projected constraints, but doesn’t delve deeply into *why* projecting specific constraints led to the observed gains. More analysis of which projected constraints were most effective, and why, would be insightful.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* This paper presents a genuinely novel approach to a challenging problem in Bayesian network structure learning.
* The theoretical foundation is strong, and the empirical results are convincing.
* The approach has the potential to significantly improve the efficiency of learning Bayesian networks with prior knowledge.
* The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its contributions and limitations.
* The work opens up new avenues for research in incorporating non-decomposable constraints into probabilistic graphical models.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The experiments are limited to simulated data.
* Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver introduces a potential dependency and performance bottleneck.
* The analysis of the constraint projection process could be more in-depth.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that I recommend accepting. While the experiments could be broadened and a deeper analysis of the constraint projection process would be valuable, the novelty and potential impact of this work are significant. The authors have identified a fundamental limitation of existing methods and proposed a clever and effective solution. The orders-of-magnitude speedup compared to ILP is particularly impressive. I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field and has the potential to influence future research in Bayesian network structure learning. I value the boldness of the approach and willingness to tackle a difficult problem head-on. The minor weaknesses do not detract from the overall strength of the paper.
