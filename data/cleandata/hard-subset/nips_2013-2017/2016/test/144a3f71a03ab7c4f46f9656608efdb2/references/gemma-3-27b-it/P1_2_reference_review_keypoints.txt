- Problem: Learns Bayesian networks under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by projecting them into decomposable constraints so an existing “oracle” can be used.  
- Novelty/approach: Combines search-space pruning with constraint projection (edge/ordering) and theoretical results (Theorems 1–4) to enable optimal learning.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on the authors’ BN graph and EC tree search spaces and contrasts with K2, dynamic programming, ILP, constraint-based and MaxSAT/ILP approaches.  
- Strength — importance: Tackles a significant practical barrier—incorporating ancestral background knowledge into BN learning.  
- Strength — technique: Constraint projection is a clever, theoretically grounded method to handle non-decomposability.  
- Strength — empirical: Shows large speedups (orders of magnitude) over ILP methods and ablation confirms the value of projection.  
- Strength — scalability & writing: Scales to networks up to 20 variables and is clearly written with solid theoretical foundations.  
- Weakness — practical constraints: Limited discussion on how ancestral constraints are identified/represented in practice and robustness to noisy or incomplete constraints.  
- Weakness — oracle dependence: Heavy reliance on the URLEARNING oracle; sensitivity to oracle efficiency is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Experiments rely mainly on simulated data; lacks validation on real-world datasets.  
- Weakness — MaxSAT dependency: Uses an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER); its computational cost and role as a bottleneck are insufficiently explored.  
- Weakness — theorem applicability: Conditions in Theorem 4 appear restrictive; unclear how often they hold or how violations are handled.  
- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound solution to a hard problem with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Against acceptance: Limited generalizability due to simulated data, oracle dependence, and incomplete analysis of practical and MaxSAT-related issues.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper, minor weaknesses do not outweigh contributions—recommended for acceptance.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).  
- Recommendations: Add real-world experiments, discuss constraint elicitation and noise robustness, analyze sensitivity to different oracles and MaxSAT costs, and study cases where Theorem 4 conditions fail.
