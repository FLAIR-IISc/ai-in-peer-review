- Paper addresses exact Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral constraints by adapting EC-tree search and projecting constraints to decomposable forms.
- Proposes CPDAG-level pruning and projection algorithms: edge inference (Theorems 2–3) and a MaxSAT-based method to infer maximal, cycle-free ordering constraints (Theorem 4).
- Projected constraints are used to empower an admissible decomposable-oracle heuristic, substantially tightening A* search over the EC tree.
- Experiments on standard networks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) show large speedups vs GOBNILP and an easy-hard-easy pattern as constraint proportion varies.
- Paper clearly positions itself relative to DP/A*, ILP/MaxSAT, and constraint-based prior work; core novelty is integrating ancestral constraints into EC-tree and deriving decomposable projections.
- Strength: tackles a practical problem important for causal discovery and knowledge integration.
- Strength: elegant, principled integration of CPDAG pruning with exact decomposable-oracle heuristics.
- Strength: projection theorems and MaxSAT encoding are useful and well-motivated.
- Strength: empirical results include informative ablations and show consistent orders-of-magnitude improvements.
- Weakness: proofs for Theorems 2–4 and for the closure algorithm are omitted, and complexity analyses are missing.
- Weakness: CPDAG feasibility test falls back to exhaustive class(P) enumeration in inconclusive cases, which can be exponential and may limit scalability.
- Weakness: MaxSAT projection is conservative (sufficient but not necessary), potentially leaving many entailed orderings unused; gap not quantified.
- Weakness: no evaluation of robustness to noisy, inconsistent, or soft background constraints.
- Weakness: limited baselines (only GOBNILP); missing comparisons to CP-based methods, other ILP formulations, or order-modular approaches.
- Weakness: experiments only up to ~20 variables; need reporting of memory usage, EC nodes expanded, and projection costs.
- Quality: method is generally sound and well-motivated but needs proof sketches, complexity, and algorithmic details for reproducibility.
- Clarity: mostly clear and organized, but omits important algorithmic details, pseudo-code, and precise definitions (e.g., closure, closed set O).
- Originality: novel integration of ancestral constraints into EC-tree and the MaxSAT reduction for order projections.
- Significance: advances exact BNSL under practical non-decomposable constraints with strong empirical gains, though practical reach depends on constraint accuracy and scalability.
- Suggestions: add proof sketches and complexity bounds, report enumeration frequency/cost, separate empirical contributions of edge vs order projections, test robustness to noisy constraints, add more baselines, and clean up minor textual issues.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses underexplored setting, methodologically solid, strong empirical improvements, and introduces useful projection techniques.
- Rejection arguments: missing theoretical proofs/complexity, potential exponential enumeration, limited robustness and baseline evaluation, and modest empirical scale.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (high confidence in novelty and empirical value; medium concern about theoretical completeness).
