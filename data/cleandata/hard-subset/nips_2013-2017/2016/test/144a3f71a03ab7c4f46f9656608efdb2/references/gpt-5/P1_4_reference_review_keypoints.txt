- Problem: Exact Bayesian network structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints.  
- Approach: Extend EC-tree (CPDAG-based) A* search with pruning of CPDAGs that cannot satisfy ancestral constraints.  
- Oracle strengthening: Project ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints to improve oracle pruning.  
- Edge projection: Algorithm (Theorems 2–3) infers entailed positive/negative edge constraints via ancestor/descendant closures.  
- Order projection: MaxSAT formulation finds a maximal acyclicity-safe set of precedence constraints entailed by ancestral constraints (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) on several benchmarks and varied settings.  
- Empirical trends: Easy–hard–easy runtime behavior as constraint density varies and SHD improves with more constraints.  
- Relation to prior work: Fills gap where existing exact methods assume decomposability; integrates non-decomposable constraints into EC-tree framework.  
- Strength: Addresses practically important causal/temporal background knowledge enabling exact constrained learning.  
- Strength: Principled integration of pruning and projection to exploit decomposable oracles.  
- Strength: Theoretical contributions include simple, checkable entailment rules and a useful negative-edge entailment rule.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence and useful framing of EC-tree as a meta-framework using powerful oracles.  
- Weakness: Pruning condition (Theorem 1) is confusing/mistated; monotonicity claims need correction and clearer variable-set disambiguation.  
- Weakness: Completeness claim for pruning is questionable unless P and A are over the same variable set; needs precise statement/proof.  
- Weakness: Missing formal proofs and algorithmic details for key results (Theorems 2–4, complexity bounds).  
- Weakness: MaxSAT acyclicity clause (Constraint 3) is unusual and needs justification and discussion of tightness.  
- Weakness: Potentially expensive CPDAG class(P) enumeration when tests are inconclusive; frequency and impact not quantified.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited—constraints sampled noiselessly from ground truth; no study of noisy/conflicting constraints or soft constraints.  
- Weakness: Baselines limited to ILP; missing comparison to order-modular/A* baselines augmented with projected ordering constraints.  
- Weakness: Missing runtime breakdowns (EC-tree search vs oracle vs MaxSAT) and other implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Presentation: Generally clear but needs tightened definitions, precise pruning safety description, pseudo-code, and corrected citations/notation.  
- Quality assessment: Technically promising with strong empirical support, but requires clarification/corrections to pruning theorem and added proofs/details.  
- Originality: Novel and useful combination of EC-tree search with projection of non-decomposable constraints to decomposable forms.  
- Significance: High practical impact—background knowledge can greatly reduce search and improve accuracy; methods may generalize to other learners.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify Theorem 1/pruning policy, frequency/size of class(P) enumeration, complexity of edge inference, robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints, and comparison to order-graph/A* baseline.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying/correcting pruning theorem, providing formal proofs/appendix, and expanding implementation/evaluation details.
