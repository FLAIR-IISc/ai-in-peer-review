- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks with prior ancestral (ancestor/descendant) constraints, which are non-decomposable and hard for optimal structure learning.
- Method: Adapts an EC tree framework to prune search space and incorporate ancestral constraints via an oracle for decomposable scores.
- Innovation: Projects non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable (edge/ordering) constraints to empower the oracle and improve efficiency.
- Theory: Provides pruning and constraint-projection theorems that justify the approach.
- Empirics: Strong performance gains (orders of magnitude) over an ILP-based baseline across experiments.
- Ablation: Study shows clear benefits of the constraint-projection step.
- Writing: Paper is well-structured and technical concepts are clearly explained.
- Dependency concern: Method performance depends heavily on the chosen oracle (URLEARNING here) and its speed/quality.
- Scalability concern: Experiments only reach ~20 variables; larger real-world networks remain untested and scalability is uncertain.
- Trend note: Observed "easy-hard-easy" scaling behavior needs further explanation.
- Baselines: Comparison limited to ILP; lacks comparison with other relevant methods (e.g., constraint-based algorithms like PC with path constraints).
- MaxSAT overhead: Reduction to MaxSAT for inferring ordering constraints may add computational cost; more analysis needed.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with convincing theory and empirical support, though oracle and scalability limits should be discussed more.
- Originality/significance: Novel extension of EC tree framework to non-decomposable constraints with potential high impact for BN learning with background knowledge.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: (1) Discuss oracle limitations and alternatives, (2) add comparison to a relevant constraint-based BN algorithm, (3) provide detailed analysis of MaxSAT computational cost.
