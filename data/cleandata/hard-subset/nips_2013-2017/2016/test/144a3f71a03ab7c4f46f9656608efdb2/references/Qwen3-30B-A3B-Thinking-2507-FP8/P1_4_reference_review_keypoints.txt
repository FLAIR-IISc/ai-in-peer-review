- Problem: Incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints (e.g., "X is an ancestor of Y") into Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Key contributions: a sound pruning strategy for the EC tree and a method to project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Methodology: integrates projection output with an oracle for optimizing decomposable scores.  
- Empirical claim: orders-of-magnitude faster than ILP-based approaches (e.g., GOBNILP), especially when using projected constraints.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree framework and situates within prior BN structure learning literature (Koivisto & Sood; Yuan & Malone).  
- Quality: technically sound with Theorems 1–4 supporting pruning and projection claims.  
- Experiments: thorough, varying variables, dataset sizes, and constraint percentages across standard benchmarks with results averaged over 50 datasets.  
- Completeness: full contribution with theoretical backing and empirical validation; authors acknowledge limitations.  
- Clarity: clearly written, well-organized, figures and pseudocode aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones to empower existing oracles.  
- Distinction: differentiates itself from Chen et al. and from ILP/CP by demonstrating superior efficiency for this problem.  
- Significance: significant practical impact—large speedups over ILP and finding that 10%–25% constraints can substitute for large increases in data.  
- Strength: addresses an identified gap in literature on ancestral constraints in structure learning.  
- Strength: provides solid theoretical foundations and well-motivated theorems.  
- Strength: strong empirical advantages and evidence that small amounts of background knowledge greatly improve learning.  
- Strength: well-documented and reproducible presentation.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of projection approach limitations, e.g., handling inconsistent constraints and scaling of the projection step.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to constraint-based methods (e.g., PC algorithm variants and Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).  
- Weakness: MaxSAT encoding for ordering constraints is complex and lacks a simple illustrative example.  
- Weakness: assumes ancestral constraints are correct; robustness to noisy/incomplete background knowledge is not evaluated.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, well-supported contribution with clear theoretical and empirical value suitable for NIPS.
