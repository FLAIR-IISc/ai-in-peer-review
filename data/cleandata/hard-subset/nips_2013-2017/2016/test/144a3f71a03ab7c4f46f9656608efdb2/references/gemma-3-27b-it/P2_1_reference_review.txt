## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) optimally while incorporating background knowledge in the form of ancestral constraints (relationships between variables asserting cause-effect or lack thereof). The core challenge tackled is that ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, rendering them incompatible with traditional BN structure learning approaches relying on decomposable scoring functions. The authors build upon their prior work using EC trees (a search space for non-decomposable scores) and propose methods for incorporating these constraints, including pruning the search space and *projecting* the constraints into decomposable forms that can empower an oracle used within the EC tree search.  They demonstrate empirically that their approach significantly outperforms Integer Linear Programming (ILP)-based methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Incorporating prior knowledge into BN structure learning is crucial for real-world applications. Ancestral constraints represent a meaningful and common form of such knowledge.
* **Novel approach:** The combination of EC trees with constraint propagation and projection is a novel and clever way to tackle the non-decomposability issue.  The idea of projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones to improve oracle performance is particularly insightful.
* **Strong empirical results:**  The experimental evaluation convincingly demonstrates the superior performance of the proposed approach compared to ILP, often by orders of magnitude. The scaling experiments and analysis of the impact of constraints are well-presented.
* **Theoretical grounding:** The paper provides a theoretical justification for the pruning strategy and establishes conditions for constraint projection. Theorems 1-4 add rigor to the presented methods.
* **Clear connections to prior work:** The paper builds logically upon previous work by the authors and others in the field (e.g., Chen et al., Cussens, Koivisto). Related work is appropriately referenced.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the method:** While the core ideas are clearly explained, the overall system seems complex, involving multiple steps (EC tree search, constraint propagation, oracle calls). This complexity might limit its practical adoption. The paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the interplay between these components.
* **Oracle dependency:** The performance relies heavily on the quality of the oracle (URLEARNING in their experiments). While the authors acknowledge this, the sensitivity of the approach to the oracle's performance is not thoroughly explored. Different oracle choices could impact results.
* **Limited scope of experimental evaluation:** The experiments are performed on simulated data. While this allows for controlled evaluation, it doesn’t directly assess the performance on real-world datasets, which could reveal further challenges.
* **MaxSAT solver reliance:**  The use of an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) introduces an external dependency and potential bottleneck.  The performance of the overall system is linked to the efficiency of this solver.
* **The presentation of the experimental setup could be better.** Specifically, describing the method for creating "random sub-networks" of the benchmark networks would enhance reproducibility.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis is well-presented, and the experimental results convincingly support the claims. The completeness of the work is good, although exploring the oracle's sensitivity would strengthen it.  The authors are careful about acknowledging limitations.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, as mentioned above, some sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations, particularly regarding the interaction of the different components. The notation is clear and consistent.
* **Originality:** The approach is original. While EC trees and constraint-based structure learning are not new, the combination with constraint projection and the specific application to ancestral constraints are novel.
* **Significance:** The results are significant. The demonstrated performance improvements over ILP are substantial, and the ability to incorporate ancestral constraints effectively addresses a crucial challenge in BN structure learning. The findings are likely to be useful to researchers and practitioners working in this area.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*  Solves an important problem with a novel and effective approach.
*  Strong empirical validation, demonstrating significant performance gains.
*  Solid theoretical foundation.
*  Clear writing and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  The method's complexity could hinder practical adoption.
*  Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver and an oracle introduces potential bottlenecks and dependencies.
*  Evaluation is limited to simulated data.




**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper.  The work is of high quality, original, and addresses a significant problem in the field of Bayesian network structure learning. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall contribution. The paper presents a valuable advancement in the field and is likely to stimulate further research in this area.



**Confidence:** 9/10
