- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral (ancestor/descendant) constraints that are non-decomposable and hard for standard optimal structure learning.
- Method: Builds on EC-tree search and introduces pruning plus projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints to feed an oracle.
- Empirical result: Method substantially outperforms ILP-based approaches in speed and scalability across varied network sizes, data sizes, and constraint percentages.
- Theoretical contribution: Proves soundness of EC-tree pruning and feasibility of constraint projection.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Chen et al.'s optimal BN learning framework and contrasts with ILP and path-constraint methods, positioning as a scalable solution for ancestral constraints.
- Strength — practical importance: Addresses a meaningful real-world need for incorporating background ancestral knowledge in BN learning.
- Strength — novelty: Constraint projection into decomposable forms is a clever, enabling idea for efficient search.
- Strength — empirical strength: Experiments are well-designed and show clear performance gains.
- Strength — theoretical soundness: Theorems provide a solid foundation for the approach.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized, making core ideas accessible.
- Weakness — ILP comparison limited: Comparison uses GOBNILP briefly; broader ILP solver comparisons would strengthen claims.
- Weakness — MaxSAT details lacking: EVASOLVER use and parameter tuning are under-discussed; sensitivity to MaxSAT quality is unclear.
- Weakness — scalability: Method scales to ~20 variables, which is modest; bottlenecks for larger networks need discussion.
- Weakness — SHD analysis shallow: More in-depth analysis of remaining structural errors at higher constraint levels would help.
- Qualitative — quality: Technically sound with strong experimental support, though some implementation details need fleshing out.
- Qualitative — clarity: Overall clear, but dense sections (e.g., MaxSAT formulation) could be expanded.
- Qualitative — originality: Novel combination of EC-tree search and constraint projection; significant contribution.
- Qualitative — significance: Addresses an important challenge with practical impact for researchers and practitioners.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, effective approach; strong empirical improvements; sound theory; practically relevant problem.
- Rejection arguments: Limited ILP comparison, brief MaxSAT discussion, and potential scalability concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address ILP comparison and MaxSAT implementation details.
