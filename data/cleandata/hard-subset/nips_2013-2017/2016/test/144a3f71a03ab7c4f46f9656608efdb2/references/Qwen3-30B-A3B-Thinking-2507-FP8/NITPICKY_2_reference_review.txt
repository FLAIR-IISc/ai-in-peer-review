

# Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

This paper proposes an approach for learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and pose challenges for standard score-based learning methods that rely on decomposable scores. The authors leverage the EC tree search space and an oracle-based approach to efficiently incorporate ancestral constraints, and they introduce a method for projecting these non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints.

## Strengths
The paper makes a theoretically sound contribution to Bayesian network structure learning by addressing a practical limitation in existing methods. The EC tree framework is well-motivated and the constraint projection technique is novel and well-executed. The experimental results demonstrate significant efficiency gains (orders of magnitude) compared to ILP-based approaches, which is a substantial contribution to the field. The paper also provides a clear technical foundation for understanding how ancestral constraints interact with structure learning.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Technical Inconsistencies
The paper contains numerous minor but troubling formatting inconsistencies that undermine its professionalism. The paper uses inconsistent notation for negative edge constraints (e.g., "X 6 Y" vs "X 6→ Y" vs "X 6 Y" in different places). In Section 3, the paper states "X 6 Y" for negative ancestral constraints but later uses "X 6→ Y" for negative edge constraints, creating confusion. The references have inconsistent formatting (e.g., "Bartlett and Cussens.,? \Q2015\E," instead of standard citation formats).

### Technical Errors
The paper contains a critical error in Section 5.2: "To see this, consider any DAG G satisfying Z 6 Y. We can construct another DAG G′ from G by adding the edge Y → Z, since adding such an edge does not introduce a directed cycle." This is incorrect - adding Y → Z to a DAG that satisfies Z 6 Y (meaning no path from Z to Y) could create a cycle if there was already a path from Y to Z. This fundamental error undermines the paper's theoretical foundation.

### Experimental Details
The paper claims "orders-of-magnitude improvements" but provides insufficient statistical evidence to support this claim. The experiments report average results over 50 datasets but omit standard deviations or error bars, making it impossible to assess the significance of the results. The paper states "we assumed BDeu scores with an equivalent sample size of 1" without justification for this choice, which is critical for Bayesian network learning.

### Theoretical Claims
The paper claims "a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree" but provides no formal proof of completeness. Theorem 1 states "a CPDAG P satisfies ancestral constraints A iff its descendants satisfy A" but doesn't provide a proof or sufficient explanation of why this holds. The paper also lacks a formal proof of the soundness of the constraint projection method.

### Related Work
The paper mentions Borboudakis and Tsamardinos [2012, 2013] but doesn't adequately contrast their constraint-based learning approach with the current paper's score-based approach. The paper doesn't sufficiently explain how the proposed method differs from existing constraint-based approaches that also handle ancestral constraints.

### Clarity Issues
The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout (e.g., "X" for variables vs "X" for sets of variables). The EC tree is introduced without sufficient explanation of its relationship to the BN graph in Section 4.1. The paper claims "the K2 algorithm and approaches based on the order graph cannot enforce ancestral constraints" but doesn't provide a clear explanation of why this is a practical limitation rather than just a theoretical curiosity.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses a meaningful problem and demonstrates promising empirical results, the numerous technical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and theoretical gaps significantly undermine the paper's quality. The critical error in Section 5.2 is particularly concerning as it suggests a fundamental misunderstanding of graph constraints. The paper's claims of "orders-of-magnitude improvements" are not sufficiently supported by the experimental results provided.

The paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution to Bayesian network structure learning, but the numerous minor flaws (which collectively constitute substantial issues in the context of a perfectionist review) prevent it from being accepted. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to address these technical inconsistencies and provide more rigorous theoretical justification for the proposed methods.

## Recommendation

Reject. The paper contains multiple technical errors, inconsistent notation, and insufficient experimental justification that prevent it from meeting NIPS standards for quality and clarity. The minor flaws collectively outweigh the paper's potential contributions to the field.
