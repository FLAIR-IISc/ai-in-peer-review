- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks optimally while incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints.

- Core challenge: Ancestral constraints are non-decomposable, breaking standard decomposable-score BN learning methods.

- Method: Builds on EC trees and introduces constraint propagation, pruning, and projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable forms to aid oracle calls.

- Oracle use: The approach relies on an oracle (URLEARNING) aided by projected constraints for efficient EC-tree search.

- Theoretical contribution: Provides pruning strategy justification and conditions for constraint projection (Theorems 1–4).

- Empirical results: Significantly outperforms ILP-based methods, often by orders of magnitude, with scalable experiments.

- Novelty: The projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones within EC-tree search is a novel idea.

- Clarity: Generally well-written with clear notation, but some components (interaction of parts) need more intuitive explanation.

- Complexity: The overall system is complex (multiple interacting steps), which may limit practical adoption.

- Oracle sensitivity: Performance heavily depends on oracle quality; sensitivity to different oracles is not fully explored.

- External dependency: Relies on a MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER), introducing a potential bottleneck and external dependency.

- Experimental scope: Evaluation is limited to simulated data; real-world dataset performance is not assessed.

- Reproducibility concern: Experimental setup description (e.g., creating random sub-networks) could be clearer.

- Significance: Results are important and likely useful to researchers and practitioners in BN structure learning.

- Recommendation: Strong accept—high quality, original, and significant contribution despite minor weaknesses.

- Counterpoints for rejection: Method complexity, reliance on external tools/oracles, and limited experimental scope. 

- Overall confidence: Reviewer confidence rated 9/10.
