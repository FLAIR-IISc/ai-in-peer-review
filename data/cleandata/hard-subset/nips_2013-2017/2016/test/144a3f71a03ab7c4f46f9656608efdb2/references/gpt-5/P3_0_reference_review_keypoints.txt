- Summary: Integrates hard ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints into exact BN structure learning via EC-tree pruning, projection to decomposable edge/order constraints, and MaxSAT for ordering inference, yielding large speedups over ILP (GOBNILP).

- Contribution (i): Prunes CPDAG nodes in EC-tree search using ancestral constraints to eliminate graphs that cannot satisfy them.

- Contribution (ii): Projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints, enabling standard oracles (order-DP/A*) to exploit them.

- Contribution (iii): Provides necessary-and-sufficient tests for entailed edge presence/absence (Theorems 2–3) and a MaxSAT encoding to infer a maximally consistent set of ordering constraints (with a sufficient soundness condition, Theorem 4).

- Empirical claim: Orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP across networks, data sizes, and constraint densities, with an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraints increase.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on EC-tree BN learning (Chen et al.) and classical order/DP methods, filling a gap by handling ancestral constraints not addressed by prior exact methods.

- Novelty: Novel marriage of EC-tree pruning with projection techniques to feed decomposable constraints into oracles with soundness guarantees.

- Strength: Addresses an important, practically useful class of background knowledge (ancestral relations) that existing decomposable methods do not support.

- Strength: Clean CPDAG-based pruning rules leveraging standard CPDAG semantics for must-directed and possibly-directed paths.

- Strength: Theorems characterizing entailed edge absence/presence are insightful; MaxSAT adds a large safe set of ordering constraints.

- Strength: Empirical results convincingly demonstrate significant practical speedups and accuracy benefits from modest constraints.

- Weakness: Missing proofs and underspecified theoretical details (closure procedure, proofs of Theorems 2–4, correctness/complexity of pruning tests).

- Weakness: Theorem 1 is confusingly stated and needs precise restatement about monotonicity/leaf additions.

- Weakness: MaxSAT acyclicity constraint (constraint 3) is nonstandard and insufficiently motivated; the method may be conservative and miss many safe orderings.

- Weakness: Pruning via enumeration of class(P) may be exponential; the frequency, class sizes, and runtime impact are not reported.

- Weakness: Experimental scope limited to n ≤ 20 and unbounded indegree; lacks bounded-indegree experiments, DP/A* baselines enhanced by projected constraints, and other baselines (CP/MaxSAT-based learners).

- Weakness: No study of robustness to noisy or inconsistent background knowledge or soft-constraint variants.

- Weakness: Presentation issues—notation slips, missing proofs in appendix, unclear figures/tables, and reference formatting problems.

- Quality: Technically promising and empirically strong, but missing theoretical proofs and complexity discussions weaken confidence.

- Clarity: Generally organized but needs clearer theorem statements, precise closure algorithm description, and better intuition for MaxSAT constraints.

- Significance: High relevance to exact BN learning; ideas likely to influence exact and approximate learners by enabling useful background knowledge.

- Suggestions: Provide full proofs, clarify Theorem 1 and closure algorithm, quantify CPDAG enumeration costs, add ablations (edge-only/order-only/both), report MaxSAT overhead, include more baselines and bounded-degree experiments, and consider soft/noisy constraints.

- Pros (summary): Tackles important constraint class, principled pruning/projection, and strong empirical speedups with clear practical impact.

- Cons (summary): Missing theoretical details/proofs, potential exponential steps without analysis, conservative MaxSAT encoding, limited baselines and scalability/robustness evaluation.

- Overall: Solid and promising; likely accept if authors add proofs, clarify algorithms, and extend experiments as requested.
