- Paper addresses optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints (X ancestor/not ancestor of Y).  
- Builds on BN-graph and EC-tree search to directly search DAGs/CPDAGs and prune nodes violating ancestral constraints.  
- Proposes projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to tighten oracles.  
- Infers edge constraints via closure over α(X) (forced ancestors) and β(X) (forced descendants); presents Theorems 2 and 3 for negative/positive edge entailment.  
- Infers ordering constraints by solving a MaxSAT to select maximal pairwise orderings while enforcing acyclicity via a sufficient condition (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP (GOBNILP) on synthetic constraints, scalability up to ~20 variables with BDeu scores.  
- Positions work relative to decomposable-score learners, ILP/CP methods, and path-constraint priors; extends Chen et al.’s EC-tree framework.  
- Importance: supports natural causal/ancestral domain knowledge that is not local, filling a practical need.  
- Methodological contribution: EC-tree pruning plus projection into local constraints is insightful and implementable.  
- Theorems for edge entailment and MaxSAT projection are practically useful and intuitive.  
- Empirically, projection significantly improves solver performance and yields better model accuracy with modest constraints.  
- Practical approach preserves benefits of decomposable-score oracles while enabling global constraints.  
- Theorem 1 is unclearly stated and needs clarification and a proof sketch (variable-set precision and monotonicity).  
- Closure rules that define the “maximum” ancestral constraint set are omitted; their specification and complexity are needed.  
- Completeness of Theorem 3 (positive edge entailment) is nontrivial and needs proof or detailed intuition, especially for CPDAG/collider corner cases.  
- The MaxSAT acyclicity sufficient condition is nonstandard and requires justification or references; define “closed set of ordering constraints.”  
- Pruning fallback may require exhaustive enumeration of class(P) (potentially exponential); frequency and mitigations (e.g., SAT checks) should be discussed.  
- Experimental limitations: only ILP baseline used; CP/SAT baselines and robustness to noisy/inconsistent constraints are missing.  
- Scalability remains modest (≈20 vars); report wall-time/memory scaling and time breakdown (pruning vs oracle vs MaxSAT).  
- Need more detail on how projected constraints are injected into the oracle (parent-set or predecessor restrictions) for reproducibility.  
- Overall technically sound but key claims (Thms 1–4) need proofs/precise conditions; enumeration bottleneck is a concern.  
- Readability is good but several formal statements, definitions, and pseudocode should be tightened and added.  
- Originality: novel use of EC-trees for ancestral constraints and projection (MaxSAT) is new and useful.  
- Significance: could become a standard way to incorporate causal background knowledge into exact BN learning if made robust.  
- Pros: addresses an under-served problem, elegant integration, strong empirical gains, clear practical implications.  
- Cons: underspecified theory/proofs, potential exponential fallback, limited baselines, and modest scalability.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept leaning accept; improve proofs, clarity, and experiments (robustness and additional baselines).  
- Suggested improvements: provide proofs/sketches for Theorems 1–4, formalize closure rules, replace enumeration with SAT/ILP feasibility checks if possible, evaluate noisy constraints, clarify oracle injection, and release code/pseudocode.
