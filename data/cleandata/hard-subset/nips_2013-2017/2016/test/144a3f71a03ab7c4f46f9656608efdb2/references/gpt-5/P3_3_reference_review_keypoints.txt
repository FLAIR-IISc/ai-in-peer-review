- Paper addresses exact Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by using EC-tree/CPDAG search and projected constraints to strengthen a decomposable oracle.

- Core contributions: EC-tree pruning for ancestral constraints, projection of ancestral constraints into edge/ordering constraints for a decomposable oracle, efficient edge-constraint inference (Theorems 2–3), and a MaxSAT reduction for ordering constraints (Theorem 4).

- Empirical claim: large speedups over ILP baseline (GOBNILP) on standard BN benchmarks, especially with projected constraints.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated combination of non-decomposable search space with a tightened decomposable oracle via projections.

- Strength: Clear explanation of why ancestral constraints are non-decomposable and why order-graph methods struggle with them.

- Strength: Edge-constraint inference rules are simple, low-overhead, and practically useful.

- Strength: Conservative MaxSAT-based ordering inference is sound and avoids eliminating feasible DAGs.

- Strength: Compelling empirical gains, observation of easy–hard–easy behavior with constraint density, and demonstration that modest background knowledge can substitute for more data.

- Weakness: Theorem 1 appears incorrect as stated; monotonicity claim about CPDAG satisfaction of ancestral constraints is flawed and needs correction.

- Weakness: CPDAG pruning may require exhaustive enumeration of class(P) in inconclusive cases; no analysis or mitigation of this potentially exponential bottleneck.

- Weakness: Theorems 2–3 rely on an unspecified “maximum” closure of rules; completeness and complexity of computing this closure are not provided.

- Weakness: Ordering projection (Theorem 4) is sufficient but not necessary, potentially missing many useful constraints; no ablation separating edge vs ordering projection effects.

- Weakness: Experimental scale tops at n=20; stronger scalability claims would require results for n≥26 and comparisons to precedence-constrained A* and CP-based solvers.

- Weakness: Unclear whether projection computation time (edge closure and MaxSAT) is included in reported runtimes; needs clarification and separate reporting.

- Weakness: No study of robustness to incorrect/noisy ancestral constraints, which is important in practice.

- Quality: Approach is sound and experiments careful, but theoretical rigor weakened by Theorem 1 error, missing proofs, and lack of complexity analysis.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written but has typos, missing derivations/proofs, and figures that should be more self-contained; Theorem 1 and closure/MaxSAT descriptions need expansion.

- Originality: Combining EC-tree search with projection of ancestral constraints and a MaxSAT formulation for safe ordering inference appears novel and distinct from prior soft-prior or constraint-based work.

- Significance: Method could substantially improve exact BN learning with background knowledge; edge inference is particularly practical.

- Suggested fixes: Correct Theorem 1 and provide formal proofs/appendix for Theorems 2–4 and closure rules with complexity analysis.

- Suggested fixes: Replace exhaustive class(P) enumeration with SAT/MaxSAT feasibility checks or empirically quantify enumeration frequency and cost.

- Suggested fixes: Add ablation (EC-tree only; +edge projection; +ordering projection), include projection time and memory usage, and compare to CP-based and precedence-constrained order-graph methods.

- Suggested fixes: Evaluate robustness to a fraction of incorrect constraints.

- Reasons to accept: Addresses an important gap, technically sound core idea, strong empirical gains, and reusable ideas (projection, MaxSAT).

- Reasons to reject: Theorem 1 likely incorrect, missing proofs/closure details, potential CPDAG enumeration scalability issues, and limited experimental scope/ablations.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on fixing Theorem 1, adding proof details and complexity analyses, and clarifying/expanding experiments.
