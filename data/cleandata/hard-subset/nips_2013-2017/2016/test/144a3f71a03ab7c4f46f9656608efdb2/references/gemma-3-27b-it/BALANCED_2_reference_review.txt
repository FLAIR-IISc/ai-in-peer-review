This paper tackles the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a practically relevant task that existing optimal structure learning methods struggle with due to the non-decomposable nature of these constraints. The authors build upon their previous work leveraging the EC tree search space and propose a method to incorporate ancestral constraints by pruning the search space and, crucially, projecting these constraints into decomposable forms to empower the oracle used within the EC tree framework. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents a significant contribution towards handling non-decomposable constraints in Bayesian network structure learning.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Learning with ancestral constraints is a valuable problem with real-world applications, as it allows incorporating prior knowledge and potentially improving learning efficiency.
* **Novel Approach:** The combination of pruning the EC tree with ancestral constraints and projecting those constraints into decomposable forms to enhance the oracle is a novel and effective strategy.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach compared to an ILP-based method, showcasing orders of magnitude improvements in efficiency. The scalability to 20 variables is also a significant achievement.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The theorems regarding constraint projection and soundness of pruning are well-defined and contribute to the theoretical understanding of the method.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the complex concepts accessible. The inclusion of theorems and related proofs (though brief) is appreciated.
* **Comprehensive Related Work:** The authors thoroughly review relevant literature on Bayesian network structure learning, constraint handling, and related approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

* **MaxSAT Solver Dependency:** The reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) for constraint projection introduces a dependency that could be a bottleneck or limitation in certain scenarios.  The performance of the overall system is thus coupled to the performance of the MaxSAT solver.  A more detailed analysis of the impact of the MaxSAT solver's performance would be valuable.
* **Projection Overhead:** While the empirical results demonstrate the overall benefit of projection, the computational cost of the projection step itself isn't thoroughly analyzed.  Understanding the trade-off between projection time and the subsequent speedup in the EC tree search is important.
* **Limited Dataset Diversity:** The experiments are based on a relatively limited set of benchmark datasets. Evaluating the approach on a wider range of datasets, including those with different characteristics (e.g., different network densities, variable types) would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Scalability Beyond 20 Variables:** While reaching 20 variables is a good start, further investigation of scalability to larger networks is needed. It’s unclear how the performance will degrade as the number of variables increases significantly.
* **Discussion of Alternatives to MaxSAT:** The paper doesn’t discuss alternative methods for constraint projection. Exploring other approaches and comparing their performance would be useful.
* **Heuristic Dependence:** The efficiency of the EC tree search relies heavily on the quality of the oracle (URLEARNING). The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t fully explore the potential impact of using different oracles.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in Bayesian network structure learning.
* The proposed approach is novel, well-motivated, and theoretically sound.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a substantial improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents the technical details.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The dependency on a MaxSAT solver and lack of detailed analysis of its impact.
* The limited diversity of the experimental datasets.
* The relatively modest scalability demonstrated (limited to 20 variables).



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach effectively tackles the challenge of incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints, and the empirical results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Analyze MaxSAT Performance:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the performance of the MaxSAT solver, including runtime and scalability as the number of constraints and variables increase.
* **Discuss Projection Cost:**  Quantify the computational cost of the constraint projection step and analyze its trade-off with the speedup in the EC tree search.
* **Expand Dataset Evaluation:** Evaluate the approach on a wider range of datasets, including those with different characteristics.
* **Explore Scalability:** Investigate the scalability to larger networks, potentially using parallelization or other optimization techniques.
* **Discuss Alternative Projection Methods:** Briefly discuss alternative approaches to constraint projection and their potential advantages and disadvantages.
* **Oracle Sensitivity:** Briefly explore the sensitivity of the performance to the choice of the oracle used in the EC tree search.
