- Summary: Addresses incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into optimal Bayesian network structure learning by extending EC trees and projecting constraints into decomposable forms, showing large speedups over ILP methods.

- Strength 1 (Technical soundness & novelty): Provides theoretical results (Theorems 1–4) and a novel constraint projection technique to handle ancestral constraints.

- Strength 2 (Empirical rigor): Thorough experiments on standard benchmarks with varied parameters and 50 repetitions, demonstrating orders-of-magnitude speedups vs GOBNILP.

- Strength 3 (Practical impact): Shows that modest numbers of ancestral constraints can yield accuracy gains equivalent to 4–16× more data.

- Strength 4 (State-of-the-art comparison): Fills a gap by empirically evaluating ancestral constraints where ILP/CP methods have not been assessed for this problem.

- Weakness 1 (Constraint projection limits): Lacks discussion of cases where projection could be too restrictive and exclude valid networks.

- Weakness 2 (Scalability): Does not analyze scaling beyond ~20 variables or explore behavior at larger sizes (e.g., 50+ variables).

- Weakness 3 (Oracle dependency): Overreliance on the chosen oracle (URLEARNING) without evaluating sensitivity to different oracles.

- Weakness 4 (Comparative methods): Omits comparison with recent constraint-based/path-constraint methods that also tackle ancestral relations in other paradigms.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound with solid theory and experiments, representing a complete research contribution.

- Clarity assessment: Well-organized and clearly written, though some technical sections (e.g., MaxSAT reduction) may be hard for non-specialists.

- Originality assessment: Novel contribution extending EC tree framework and introducing constraint projection for non-decomposable constraints.

- Significance assessment: High practical significance due to large speedups and the value of incorporating domain knowledge.

- Overall recommendation: Strong paper, recommend acceptance (NIPS); contributions outweigh the noted limitations.

- Suggestions for improvement: Discuss projection limitations and remedies, analyze scalability to larger networks, compare with constraint-based methods like PC/path constraints, and clarify the relationship between "maximum set of ancestral constraints" and projection in Section 5.1.
