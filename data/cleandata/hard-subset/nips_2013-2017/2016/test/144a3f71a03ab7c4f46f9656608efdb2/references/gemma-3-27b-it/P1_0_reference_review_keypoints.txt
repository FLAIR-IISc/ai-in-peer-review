- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with prior ancestral constraints, a practical and expressive form of prior knowledge.  
- Builds on EC tree and BN graph frameworks [Chen et al.] to integrate non-decomposable priors via pruning and projection.  
- Core technical idea: prune the EC tree for constraint satisfaction and project non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones for an oracle.  
- Leverages existing decomposable-score learning oracles (e.g., order-graph based methods) to gain efficiency.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP approaches and scalability up to ~20 variables.  
- The pruning theorem guarantees soundness and completeness; MaxSAT reduction gives a principled projection to decomposable constraints.  
- Writing and exposition are clear; explanations of BN graph, EC tree, and constraint projection are understandable.  
- Addresses an important practical problem of incorporating prior knowledge into BN learning.  
- The combination of pruning plus constraint projection is novel and cleverly reuses existing efficient algorithms.  
- MaxSAT reduction complexity is under-analyzed and may be a computational bottleneck in larger problems.  
- Heavy dependence on the chosen decomposable-score oracle (URLEARNING) could affect performance and is not fully discussed.  
- Limited comparisons: lacks broader evaluation against other state-of-the-art score-based methods and ablation showing benefits without ancestral constraints.  
- Theoretical analysis focuses on algorithms; deeper theory on limits of constraint projection is missing.  
- Experimental setup relies on simulated data and would benefit from more diverse real-world benchmarks.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, empirically validated, and worthy of acceptance; reviewer rating 7/10 (Strong Accept).
