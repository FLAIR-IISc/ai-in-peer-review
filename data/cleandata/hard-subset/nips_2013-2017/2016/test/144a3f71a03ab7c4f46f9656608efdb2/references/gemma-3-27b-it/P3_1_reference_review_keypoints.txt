- Tackles learning Bayesian Networks with ancestral (ancestor-descendant) constraints, extending EC trees and an oracle to incorporate non-decomposable constraints via pruning and projection.  
- Projects ancestral constraints into equivalent decomposable constraints to enable efficient optimal structure search and oracle use, showing large empirical gains over ILP methods.

- Correctly positions the work relative to key BN literature (Darwiche, Koller & Friedman, Murphy) and builds on Chen et al.’s EC-tree approach.  
- Acknowledges related constraint-based, MaxSAT, ILP/CP, and previous BN-graph works.

- Addresses an important practical gap: integrating non-decomposable prior knowledge into optimal BN learning without sacrificing optimality.  
- Provides a theoretically sound framework with EC-tree adaptation and a solid pruning soundness theorem.  
- Introduces constraint projection as a key innovation that significantly improves oracle performance and search efficiency.  
- Delivers strong empirical results showing orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP across networks and dataset sizes.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured with accessible technical detail.

- MaxSAT reduction details are limited; more explanation and examples needed, and MaxSAT could be a bottleneck for very large networks.  
- Heavy dependence on the oracle (URLEARNING) with limited exploration of sensitivity to different oracle implementations.  
- Limited direct comparison to state-of-the-art constraint-based algorithms (e.g., PC, FCI) adapted for ancestral constraints.  
- Experimental setup lacks some reproducibility details (sub-network selection, generation of ancestral constraints).

- For acceptance: solves an important, previously unsolved problem with theoretical soundness, a novel projection technique, and strong empirical gains.  
- Against acceptance: potential scalability issues from MaxSAT, oracle dependence, and insufficient comparison to constraint-based baselines.

- Overall: strong and valuable contribution; novel constraint projection and empirical evidence justify acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (add MaxSAT details and compare to constraint-based methods).
