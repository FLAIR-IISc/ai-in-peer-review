This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints, a challenging task due to the non-decomposable nature of these constraints. The authors build upon their previous work on optimal Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable priors, proposing a method to incorporate ancestral constraints by pruning the search space and inferring decomposable constraints from the ancestral ones. The paper is well-structured, clearly presenting the problem, related work, and their proposed solution.

The core idea of leveraging the EC tree search space and pruning based on ancestral constraints is sound and logically presented. The theoretical results (Theorems 1-4) solidify the approach and demonstrate its completeness. The concept of projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable ones to empower the oracle is insightful and potentially significant for improving efficiency.  The experimental evaluation, comparing their method with an ILP-based approach, provides empirical evidence of the advantages of their approach, particularly in terms of speed. The observation of an "easy-hard-easy" trend with increasing constraint percentage is interesting and offers a nuanced understanding of the impact of constraints.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. While the empirical results demonstrate speed improvements, the experiments are limited in scope. The evaluation focuses primarily on synthetic data and a relatively small number of benchmark networks. The effect of data size *N* isn't fully explored – while variations are used, a more detailed analysis of scaling with data volume would be valuable. Further, the paper states that the proposed approach “scales to n=20 variables,” but doesn’t fully contextualize this within the broader field of Bayesian network structure learning; comparing the scalability to state-of-the-art *unconstrained* learning methods would be helpful.  The comparison with GOBNILP seems somewhat unfair, as the authors mention that the ILP approach benefits from the projected constraints, yet the ILP approach is ultimately slower. A fair comparison might involve running GOBNILP *without* the projected constraints to establish a baseline. The description of the MaxSAT implementation is brief and lacks details, making it difficult to assess the computational cost of the constraint projection step. Also, while the authors acknowledge existing constraint-based methods like PC algorithm, the comparison is limited to Borboudakis and Tsamardinos' work. A broader comparison with established constraint-based methods would be beneficial. Finally, the notation could be improved for clarity.  For instance, the use of both uppercase and bold uppercase for sets is confusing, and the mathematical notation isn't always consistent.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of integrating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into the EC tree search space is novel and addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian network structure learning.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical foundations are solid, with theorems justifying the correctness of the pruning and constraint projection steps.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments demonstrate the speed advantages of the proposed method compared to an ILP-based approach.
*   **Significance:**  The ability to efficiently learn Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints has practical implications for domains where prior knowledge is available.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimental Scope:** The evaluation is limited to synthetic data and a relatively small set of benchmark networks.
*   **Incomplete Comparison:** The comparison with GOBNILP is potentially biased, and a more thorough comparison with other relevant methods is needed.
*   **Lack of Detail:** Insufficient detail is provided regarding the MaxSAT implementation and the scaling behavior of the approach with increasing data size.
*   **Minor Clarity Issues:** The notation and presentation could be improved for better readability.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints. While the experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, the results are promising and suggest that the proposed method can significantly improve efficiency.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the concerns regarding the experimental scope, the comparison with other methods, and the clarity of the presentation. The authors should expand the experimental section to include more diverse datasets and a more thorough analysis of scalability. They should also clarify the computational cost of the constraint projection step and provide a more balanced comparison with existing approaches.
