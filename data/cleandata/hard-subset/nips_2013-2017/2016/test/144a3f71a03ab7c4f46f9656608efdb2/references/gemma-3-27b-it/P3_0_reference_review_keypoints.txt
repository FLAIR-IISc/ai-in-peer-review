- Tackles learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral (ancestor/descendant) constraints that are non-decomposable.  
- Builds on EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores to incorporate ancestral constraints.  
- Key innovation: pruning the EC tree and projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints usable by the oracle.  
- Experimental results show orders-of-magnitude speedup over ILP-based (GOBNILP) baselines.  
- Systematic experiments vary network size, data size, and constraint percentage for robust evaluation.  
- Theorems provide pruning guarantees and conditions for valid constraint projection.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and uses consistent notation.  
- Demonstrates scalability up to 20 variables, competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Reduction to MaxSAT for projection is complex and may be computationally expensive; runtime of this step is under-analyzed.  
- Heavy reliance on the chosen oracle (URLEARNING); sensitivity to different oracles is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Limited comparisons to other modern Bayesian network learning methods (approximate or heuristic approaches).  
- Experiments use simulated data; lack of real-world dataset evaluation limits applicability claims.  
- Structural Hamming distance analysis is brief; more detailed error analysis relative to constraints is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, novel and sound approach, strong empirical gains, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: MaxSAT complexity, oracle dependence, and lack of real-world evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance despite noted weaknesses.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
