- Problem: Learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints is challenging for standard decomposable-scoring structure learning algorithms.  
- Main idea: Use EC-tree search with pruning and project ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to enable oracle-based search.  
- Technical novelty: The projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (via a MaxSAT reduction) is a key novel contribution.  
- Theoretical support: The paper provides theorems on constraint entailment and projection to justify correctness.  
- Empirical results: Method outperforms ILP-based baselines by orders of magnitude and scales to networks up to ~20 variables.  
- Accuracy gains: Using ancestral constraints improves learned network quality (shown via structural Hamming distance).  
- Connection to prior work: Clear situating within existing BN structure learning literature and authors’ prior EC-tree work.  
- Strength — practical importance: Incorporating ancestral (causal) prior knowledge is a valuable practical contribution.  
- Strength — clarity and organization: Paper is generally well-written and technically sound.  
- Weakness — projection details: Description of the MaxSAT reduction and practical implementation is under-specified and feels hand-wavy.  
- Weakness — scalability of projection: Unclear how projection running time scales with number/complexity of constraints and MaxSAT solver performance.  
- Weakness — limited baselines: Comparison is primarily against a single ILP method; other constraint-based or hybrid baselines are missing.  
- Weakness — oracle dependence: Heavy reliance on the oracle (URLEARNING) warrants more discussion of its limitations and impact.  
- Weakness — dataset/constraint generation: Insufficient detail on how simulated data and ancestral constraints were generated.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, subject to addressing minor weaknesses (MaxSAT details, broader comparisons, oracle and dataset clarification).
