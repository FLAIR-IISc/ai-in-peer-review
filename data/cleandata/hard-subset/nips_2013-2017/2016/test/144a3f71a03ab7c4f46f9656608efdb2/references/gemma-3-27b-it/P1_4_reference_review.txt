## Peer Review for "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) with ancestral constraints – prior knowledge specifying ancestor/descendant relationships between variables. The authors highlight that standard optimal structure learning approaches struggle with these non-decomposable constraints. They build upon recent work utilizing a search space based on EC trees and propose methods to incorporate ancestral constraints through pruning this search space and, crucially, by projecting the ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (edge and ordering constraints) that can empower an oracle used within the EC tree search. They demonstrate empirically that their approach significantly outperforms Integer Linear Programming (ILP)-based methods.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon the framework introduced by Chen et al. (2015, 2016) for optimal BN structure learning with non-decomposable scores. It acknowledges previous attempts to incorporate constraints using ILP (Jaakkola et al., 2010; Bartlett & Cussens, 2015; van Beek & Hoffmann, 2015) but points out the lack of empirical evaluation for these methods in the context of ancestral constraints. It also relates to constraint-based learning methods utilizing path constraints (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos, 2012, 2013).  The authors correctly position their contribution as improving upon existing approaches by providing a scalable and demonstrably effective method for dealing with ancestral constraints specifically.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important practical problem:**  Incorporating background knowledge is crucial for BN learning in many real-world scenarios, and ancestral constraints are a natural and expressive way to represent such knowledge.
* **Novel approach:**  Projecting non-decomposable constraints into decomposable forms is a clever idea that allows leveraging existing efficient algorithms (the oracle) and significantly speeds up the search.
* **Strong empirical results:** The paper presents compelling evidence of the superiority of their approach over ILP, demonstrating substantial speedups and scalability.  The experiments are well-designed, varying network size, data size, and constraint percentage.
* **Theoretical soundness:** The theorems establishing the soundness of pruning the EC tree and the feasibility of constraint projection provide a solid foundation for the approach.
* **Clear presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the core ideas.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited scope of ILP comparison:** While the authors demonstrate performance gains over ILP, the ILP implementation used (GOBNILP) is mentioned only briefly. A more detailed comparison, potentially with other competitive ILP solvers, would strengthen the claims.
* **MaxSAT implementation details:** The use of EVASOLVER for MaxSAT constraint projection is mentioned, but the parameter tuning and potential impact of the MaxSAT solver’s performance aren't thoroughly discussed.  Understanding how sensitive the approach is to the quality of the inferred ordering constraints is important.
* **Scalability limitations:** While the approach scales to 20 variables, this is still relatively modest for many real-world applications. Discussing the potential bottlenecks as the number of variables increases and potential future work addressing these limitations would be beneficial.
* **Structural Hamming Distance Discussion:** The discussion of structural Hamming distance (∆) could be strengthened. While the authors mention it improves with more data and constraints, a more in-depth analysis of the types of errors remaining at higher constraint levels would be valuable.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical justification for the proposed methods. The experimental results strongly support the claims made. The completeness of the work is good, though some details (mentioned above) could be further fleshed out.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized.  However, some sections (e.g., the MaxSAT formulation) are dense and could benefit from further explanation.
* **Originality:**  The combination of EC tree search with ancestral constraint incorporation and, especially, the constraint projection technique is novel and represents a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** The results are important and address a real-world challenge in BN learning.  The potential for leveraging background knowledge to improve learning efficiency and accuracy is valuable for both researchers and practitioners.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach for incorporating ancestral constraints into BN structure learning.
* The empirical results demonstrate significant performance gains over existing methods.
* The theoretical foundations are sound and well-explained.
* The work addresses an important problem with practical relevance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The comparison with ILP is somewhat limited.
* The discussion of the MaxSAT implementation and its impact on performance is brief.
* Scalability could be a concern for very large networks.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach is novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above—particularly providing more detail on the ILP comparison and MaxSAT implementation—would further strengthen the paper.  I am confident that this work will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
