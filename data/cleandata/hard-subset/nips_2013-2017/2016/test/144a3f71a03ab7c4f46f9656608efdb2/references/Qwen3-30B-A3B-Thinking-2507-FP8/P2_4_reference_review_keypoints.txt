- Addresses the challenge of incorporating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Proposes a novel EC-tree search-space approach plus methods to infer decomposable constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Provides formal proofs (Theorems 2–4) establishing the soundness of the constraint inference methods.  
- Reduces topological ordering constraints to MaxSAT and gives algorithms for edge constraints.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: varies variables, dataset size, and constraint density across benchmarks.  
- Compares fairly against GOBNILP (ILP-based) using the same datasets and scores.  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP methods when handling ancestral constraints.  
- Shows constraint inference step adds negligible overhead relative to structure search.  
- Empirically shows modest numbers of ancestral constraints can match large increases in dataset size for accuracy.  
- Scales to around 20 variables, comparable to state-of-the-art score-based approaches.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written with reproducible experimental setup.  
- Novel contribution: first empirical demonstration that EC-tree plus constraint projection efficiently handles ancestral constraints.  
- Differentiates clearly from prior work (Chen et al., ILP/CP approaches) and situates contributions in related work.  
- Weaknesses: does not handle inconsistent ancestral constraints (e.g., X→Y and Y→X).  
- Weaknesses: evaluation uses synthetic benchmark data; lacks a real-world case study.  
- Weaknesses: does not explicitly compare to other constraint-based/constraint-programming approaches beyond ILP.  
- Overall recommendation: strong acceptance — significant, original, and practically valuable contribution.
