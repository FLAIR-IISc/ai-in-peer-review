- Problem addressed: learning Bayesian network structures with non-decomposable ancestral (ancestor–descendant) constraints, building on EC tree search space.  
- Method: prune EC tree using ancestral constraints and project them into decomposable edge and ordering constraints usable by existing oracles.  
- Practical result: significant efficiency improvements over ILP-based methods (e.g., GOBNILP).  
- Theoretical soundness: Theorems 1–4 prove pruning soundness and projection correctness.  
- Technical derivation: EC tree adaptation is logically and technically well-founded based on prior BN/EC work.  
- Experimental setup: comprehensive evaluation on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) across n=12–20, N=512–8192, and constraint rates 0–100%.  
- Empirical findings: projected constraints yield orders-of-magnitude speedups and maintain model quality.  
- Limitations acknowledged: some pruning cases require enumerating structures; authors note other constraints.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, with effective figures and sufficiently detailed experimental description (though newcomers may need background reading).  
- Originality: first efficient implementation and empirical study integrating ancestral constraints via projection to decomposable constraints.  
- Significance: small amounts of ancestral knowledge (10–25%) can match large increases in data and substantially reduce computation.  
- Scalability: approach scales to 20 variables, competitive for BDeu scoring.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient explanation of why ILP struggles with ancestral constraints, limited complexity analysis of projection, incomplete exploration of projection failures, and weaker domain-specific examples.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-quality, significant contribution — recommended for acceptance.
