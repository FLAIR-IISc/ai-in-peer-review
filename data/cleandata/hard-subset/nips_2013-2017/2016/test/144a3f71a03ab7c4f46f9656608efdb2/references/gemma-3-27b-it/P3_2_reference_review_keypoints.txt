- Problem addressed: learning Bayesian networks with background ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable and challenge existing optimal structure learning methods.  
- Method: extends the EC tree search space and projects ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to enable an oracle-based search.  
- Key technical contribution: a projection method that translates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable forms usable by the EC search/oracle.  
- Theoretical support: Theorems 1–4 provide conditions and proofs for the correctness and validity of the constraint projection.  
- Empirical results: the proposed method substantially outperforms ILP-based approaches, often by orders of magnitude, and shows good scalability to larger networks.  
- Related work: situates the contribution within BN structure learning literature, acknowledges prior optimal learning methods, path-constraint work, ILP, and MaxSAT for ordering.  
- Strength: addresses a practically important problem (incorporating background knowledge) with a novel, clever approach (EC + projection).  
- Strength: strong empirical validation and scalability analysis strengthen practical impact.  
- Strength: clear presentation and illustrative examples make the approach accessible.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on the oracle (URLEARNING); performance may degrade with a weaker oracle and oracle sensitivity is insufficiently explored.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT reduction for ordering constraints may become a bottleneck for very large networks; scalability of the EVASOLVER is not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: experiments are mainly on synthetic/benchmark data; evaluation on real-world datasets with natural ancestral constraints is limited.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of how the method handles Markov equivalence classes when multiple DAGs satisfy the constraints and whether any DAG is preferred.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective paper with solid theory and empirical gains; minor issues do not undermine the main contribution.  
- Pro arguments: practical relevance, novel/theoretically sound method, significant performance improvements, and clear writing.  
- Con arguments: reliance on oracle performance, potential MaxSAT scalability problems, and limited real-world evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestions to address oracle sensitivity, MaxSAT scalability, and real-world experiments in future work.
