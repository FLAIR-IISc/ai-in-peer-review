## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) when prior knowledge is available in the form of ancestral constraints (relationships specifying ancestor/descendant relationships between variables). Existing optimal structure learning methods struggle with these non-decomposable constraints. The authors build upon a recent framework leveraging an "oracle" for decomposable scores and demonstrate how to incorporate ancestral constraints by pruning the search space within an EC tree structure. They also present a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable (edge/ordering) constraints to further empower the oracle and improve efficiency.  Empirical evaluation against an ILP-based approach demonstrates significant performance gains.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Integrating background knowledge into BN learning is crucial for real-world applications, and ancestral constraints represent a natural and powerful form of such knowledge.
* **Novel approach:**  Leveraging the existing EC tree framework and adapting it for non-decomposable constraints is a clever idea. The projection of constraints is particularly insightful and demonstrably effective.
* **Strong empirical results:** The performance gains over the ILP-based method are substantial (orders of magnitude) and consistently observed across various experimental settings. The ablation study clearly highlights the benefit of constraint projection.
* **Sound theoretical foundation:** The theorems regarding pruning and constraint projection provide a solid grounding for the proposed methods.
* **Clear writing & Organization:** The paper is well-structured and the technical concepts are explained clearly. The progression from problem definition to solution and evaluation is logical.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Dependency on the oracle:** The performance is heavily reliant on the quality and speed of the "oracle" (URLEARNING in this case). The paper briefly acknowledges this but doesn't extensively discuss the limitations or potential bottlenecks introduced by the oracle itself.  How robust is the approach to different oracle choices?
* **Scalability limitations:** While the experiments demonstrate scalability up to 20 variables, this is still relatively small for many real-world BN applications. While acknowledged, further investigation into scalability with larger networks would be beneficial.  The trend observed in Table 3 is intriguing (easy-hard-easy) but needs further explanation.
* **Limited comparison:** The ILP approach is the primary baseline, but it is not necessarily the most competitive or commonly used method for constrained BN learning.  Including comparisons with other relevant approaches (e.g., constraint-based methods like PC with path constraints) would strengthen the paper.
* **MaxSAT complexity:** The reduction to MaxSAT for inferring ordering constraints might introduce computational overhead. While the empirical results suggest it’s beneficial, a more detailed analysis of the computational cost and its impact on overall performance would be valuable.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical arguments are convincing, and the empirical results are well-supported. The completeness is good, although the limitations of the oracle and scalability could be addressed more explicitly. The authors are careful to evaluate the impact of constraint projection.
* **Clarity:** The paper is very well written and clearly organized. The complex technical details are explained in a way that is accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian networks and structure learning.
* **Originality:** The approach of adapting the EC tree framework and projecting constraints is novel. While building upon prior work (Chen et al., 2015, 2016), it significantly extends these ideas to handle non-decomposable constraints, a key limitation of existing methods.
* **Significance:** This work has the potential to be highly significant.  Enabling efficient learning of BNs with complex background knowledge can open up new applications in various domains. The demonstrated performance gains and the potential for integrating diverse types of prior knowledge make this a valuable contribution.



**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Solves an important and challenging problem.
* Presents a novel and effective approach.
* Demonstrates strong empirical results.
* Solid theoretical foundation.
* Clear and well-written.

**Con:**

* Reliance on the oracle’s performance.
* Scalability remains a concern.
* Limited comparison to other relevant baselines.
* MaxSAT overhead could be a bottleneck.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field of Bayesian network structure learning. The proposed approach is novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated. While there are some limitations regarding scalability and the reliance on the oracle, the benefits clearly outweigh the drawbacks. I recommend **acceptance** to NIPS with minor revisions.  Specifically, I suggest:

1.  Discussing the limitations of the oracle and potential alternatives.
2.  Adding a comparison to a relevant constraint-based BN learning algorithm.
3. Providing a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of the MaxSAT reduction.
