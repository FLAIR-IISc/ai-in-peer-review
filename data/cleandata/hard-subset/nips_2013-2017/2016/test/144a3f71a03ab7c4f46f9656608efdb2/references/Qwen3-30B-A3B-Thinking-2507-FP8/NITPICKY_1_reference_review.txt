

# Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

## Summary
This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian network structures with ancestral constraints, which are non-decomposable constraints that specify cause-and-effect relationships between variables. The authors propose an approach that utilizes the EC tree search space and an oracle for decomposable scores, along with a method for projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints. The paper shows theoretically sound pruning techniques for the EC tree and empirically demonstrates orders-of-magnitude improvements over ILP-based approaches.

## Strengths
The paper makes a valuable contribution to the Bayesian network structure learning literature by addressing a practical problem (incorporating ancestral constraints) that has been largely overlooked in the context of optimal structure learning. The theoretical framework for projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints is sound and complete. The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating significant efficiency improvements over ILP-based approaches across multiple datasets and constraint configurations. The paper also provides clear explanations of the technical concepts, which should be accessible to the NIPS community.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Presentation Issues
1. The paper contains multiple inconsistent citation formats. For example, references like "Borboudakis and Tsamardinos.,? \Q2012\E" and "Chen et al\.,? \Q2015\E" have inconsistent punctuation and backslashes that should be removed. The references section should follow a standard format without these artifacts.

2. The paper states "th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2016), Barcelona, Spain" which should be "The Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2016), Barcelona, Spain."

3. In section 4.3, the paper claims "A CPDAG P with no partially directed paths from X1 to X2 violates the given constraint" but this is incorrect. A CPDAG with no partially directed paths from X1 to X2 could still have structures in its equivalence class containing a path from X1 to X2. The paper should clarify this point or correct the statement.

4. The paper uses "6" to denote "not" (e.g., "X 6 Y"), which is inconsistent with standard notation. This should be replaced with "X ↛ Y" or similar standard notation to avoid confusion.

### Technical Issues
1. In section 5.2, the MaxSAT encoding contains a typo: "for all X < Y and Z < W : (X < Y ) ∧ (Z < W )⇒ (X Y ) ∨ (Z W ) ∨ (X Z) ∨ (Y W ) ∨ (X W ) ∨ (Y 6 Z)". The "Y 6 Z" should be "Y ↛ Z" or "Y not Z" for clarity, and the encoding should use consistent notation.

2. The paper states "We first test for these two cases, which can be done efficiently" in section 4.3 but does not explain how these tests are performed efficiently. A brief explanation of the algorithm or complexity would strengthen this claim.

3. The paper mentions "URLEARNING as an oracle" but does not define what URLEARNING is or provide a reference for it. The reader should be able to understand what this oracle is and why it's appropriate.

4. In the experiments, the paper states "We selected random sets of nodes and all their ancestors, up to a connected sub-network of a given size" but this is ambiguous. How are these sub-networks generated? What is the precise definition of "up to a connected sub-network"? This ambiguity makes it difficult to reproduce the experiments.

5. The paper claims "our approach is consistently orders-of-magnitude faster than GOBNILP" but doesn't provide specific numbers or error margins in Table 1. The reader cannot assess the magnitude of the improvement without seeing actual values.

### Minor Inconsistencies
1. The paper refers to "EC trees" without ever defining what "EC" stands for (likely "Equivalence Class" but this should be explicitly stated).

2. The reference "Bartlett and Cussens.,? \Q2015\E" contains an erroneous backslash in the citation.

3. The URL for the Bayesian network benchmarks is provided as "http://www.bnlearn.com/bnrepository" but this is not formatted as a proper hyperlink in the text.

## Recommendation
While the paper presents a theoretically sound approach with promising empirical results, the numerous minor formatting inconsistencies and technical ambiguities undermine the paper's quality. The paper should be revised to address these issues before publication. The inconsistencies in citation formatting, the unclear definition of "EC," the ambiguous sub-network selection method, and the incorrect technical statement in section 4.3 are particularly problematic.

The authors should clarify the notation for ancestral constraints, define the EC tree explicitly, explain the sub-network selection process, and correct the technical statement about CPDAGs. The references should be cleaned up to follow a consistent format.

Although the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field, these minor issues collectively detract from the paper's quality and reproducibility. Given NIPS's high standards for technical precision and clarity, I recommend rejection. The authors should address these issues in a revised version that better meets NIPS's standards for presentation and technical detail.
