- Technical contribution: Provides a sound and complete method for pruning the EC tree with ancestral constraints and principled projection of non-decomposable constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints (Theorems 1–4).  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on multiple benchmarks showing the method is orders of magnitude faster than GOBNILP, especially at larger variable counts (e.g., 100–1000x faster at n=20 with projected constraints).  
- Practical significance: Demonstrates that modest ancestral constraint amounts (10–25%) can improve model accuracy comparably to large increases in training data, highlighting value of domain knowledge.  
- Clarity and organization: Well-written and logically structured with clear explanations of concepts and reproducible experimental details.  
- Narrow scope: Focuses only on ancestral constraints and does not directly handle other non-decomposable constraint types, limiting general applicability.  
- Oracle dependency: Relies on a perfect decomposable-scoring oracle (URLEARNING) without evaluating sensitivity to oracle suboptimality or heuristic alternatives.  
- Scalability limits: Shows scalability up to 20 variables but does not analyze limits or why it may not scale to larger problems (e.g., 26+ variables reported elsewhere).  
- Missing comparisons: Does not compare against constraint-based methods (e.g., PC with ancestral constraints), leaving out relevant baselines.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant, and practically valuable contribution that is theoretically sound and empirically compelling.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance; suggests adding brief discussion on extending the approach to other constraint types.
