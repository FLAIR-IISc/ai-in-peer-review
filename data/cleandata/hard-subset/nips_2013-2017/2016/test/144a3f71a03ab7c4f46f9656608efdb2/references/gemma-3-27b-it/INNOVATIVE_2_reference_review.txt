This paper tackles the important problem of learning Bayesian networks under ancestral constraints, a scenario frequently encountered in real-world applications where prior knowledge exists. The core idea – leveraging the recent framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] and adapting it to incorporate non-decomposable constraints – is strong and potentially impactful. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing approaches reliant on decomposable scores and offer a compelling alternative. The projection of ancestral constraints to infer decomposable constraints is a clever addition, showing a deep understanding of the underlying search space.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach is genuinely novel. Existing methods struggle with non-decomposable constraints, and this work provides a direct solution within a recently established, powerful framework. The idea of projecting constraints is particularly insightful.
* **Significance:**  Learning with constraints is critical for practical applications.  The empirical results demonstrating orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods are highly significant, suggesting a real advantage in scalability.  The potential impact on incorporating domain knowledge into BN learning is substantial.
* **Technical Soundness:** The adaptation of the EC tree framework appears well-reasoned, and the proposed theorems (1-4) provide theoretical backing for the pruning and constraint projection techniques. The connection to MaxSAT for ordering constraint inference is a clever and theoretically grounded approach.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and highlights the limitations of current approaches. The technical details are presented in a logical manner.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Validation – Limited Scope:** While the empirical evaluation demonstrates impressive speedups, the experiments are somewhat limited in scope. The benchmarks used (ALARM, ANDES, etc.) are standard, but the variations in dataset size and constraint percentage feel a little ad-hoc.  A more systematic exploration of parameter space and a deeper analysis of the structural Hamming distance (beyond the averages reported) would strengthen the evaluation.
* **MaxSAT Solver Dependency:** The reliance on an external MaxSAT solver (EVASOLVER) introduces a dependency and potentially a bottleneck. The paper doesn't fully discuss the scalability or limitations of the MaxSAT solver itself.  It’s not clear how performance degrades as the number of ancestral constraints increases and the MaxSAT instance grows.
* **Oracle Performance:** The paper relies heavily on the performance of the oracle (URLEARNING). While acknowledged, a more in-depth discussion of the oracle’s limitations and sensitivity to different datasets would be beneficial.  Could a different oracle significantly impact performance?
* **Missing Related Work:** While the paper references relevant work, there's a slight under-acknowledgement of research leveraging constraint satisfaction and integer programming *specifically* for Bayesian network learning *with* constraints, particularly more recent developments.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a truly novel approach to a challenging problem.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a significant speedup over existing methods.
* The theoretical foundations are sound and the proposed techniques (constraint projection, EC tree adaptation) are well-motivated.
* The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of Bayesian network learning, making it more practical and applicable to real-world problems.
* The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the complexities involved in learning with non-decomposable constraints.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The empirical evaluation could be more comprehensive and systematic.
* The reliance on an external MaxSAT solver is a potential concern.
* The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of the oracle’s limitations and related work in constraint-based BN learning.



**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend acceptance. Despite some minor weaknesses in the empirical validation and discussion, the novelty and potential significance of this work are exceptional. The authors have tackled a difficult problem with a clever and effective solution. The observed speedups are substantial and the theoretical grounding is solid. I’m willing to overlook the somewhat limited scope of the experiments given the importance of the problem and the boldness of the approach. This paper represents a significant contribution to the field and has the potential to inspire further research in learning Bayesian networks with complex constraints.
