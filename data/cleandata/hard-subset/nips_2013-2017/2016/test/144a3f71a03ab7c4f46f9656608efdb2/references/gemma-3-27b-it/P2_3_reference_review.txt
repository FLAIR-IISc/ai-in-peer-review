## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) when prior knowledge is available in the form of ancestral constraints (specifying ancestor/descendant relationships between variables).  The core challenge is that these constraints are non-decomposable, making them difficult to integrate with standard BN structure learning algorithms that rely on decomposable scoring functions. The authors build upon their previous work using EC trees (a search space for optimal structure learning) and propose a method to incorporate these constraints by pruning the search space and, crucially, projecting the non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints (edge and ordering constraints) to empower an oracle used within the EC tree search. They demonstrate empirically that their approach significantly outperforms ILP-based methods for learning BNs with ancestral constraints.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Incorporating prior knowledge into BN learning is a crucial practical need. Ancestral constraints represent a common type of prior knowledge (e.g., causal relationships) that is often available.
* **Novel Approach:**  The combination of using EC trees, pruning based on ancestral constraints, and *projecting* those constraints into a decomposable form to improve oracle performance is a novel and intelligent approach. The projection step is a particularly clever idea.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate substantial performance gains (orders of magnitude faster) compared to ILP-based methods.  The scalability tests show the approach can handle reasonably sized networks (up to 20 variables), and the results on structural Hamming distance demonstrate the benefits of using ancestral constraints for improving the accuracy of the learned networks.
* **Clear Connection to Prior Work:** The paper clearly situates itself within the existing literature on BN structure learning, particularly their own previous work and related approaches using ILP and constraint programming.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The theorems provided (regarding constraint entailment and projection) help establish the correctness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Projection Step:** While the idea of projecting constraints is compelling, the description of the MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints feels a bit hand-wavy.  More detail on the practical implementation of this projection step would be helpful, particularly regarding the efficiency of the MaxSAT solver.  How does the running time of the projection scale with the number of constraints?
* **Limited Comparison:** The comparison is primarily against a single ILP-based method.  It would be valuable to compare against other state-of-the-art constraint-based or hybrid BN structure learning algorithms.
* **Oracle Dependence:**  The approach relies heavily on the performance of the oracle (URLEARNING in the experiments). The paper acknowledges this, but a more detailed discussion of the oracle’s limitations and how they might impact the overall performance would be beneficial.
* **Dataset Generation:** The details of how the simulated datasets and ancestral constraints are generated could be more thorough.  What distribution is used to simulate the data? Are the ancestral constraints generated randomly, or based on some specific criteria?



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with a novel and effective solution. The approach is theoretically sound, well-motivated, and supported by compelling empirical results.  The projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints is a particularly clever idea that significantly improves the efficiency of the search process. While the complexity of the MaxSAT reduction and the limited comparison to other methods are minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** Excellent. The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by experimental results.
* **Clarity:** Good. The paper is generally well-written and organized, although some sections (e.g., the description of the MaxSAT reduction) could benefit from more detail.
* **Originality:** High. The combination of techniques presented is novel and represents a significant contribution to the field.
* **Significance:** High. The results are important and have the potential to be widely used by researchers and practitioners working on Bayesian network learning.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and effective approach to incorporating ancestral constraints into BN learning.
* Strong empirical results demonstrating significant performance improvements.
* Clear and well-motivated work with a strong connection to prior research.
* Addresses an important practical problem.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
* Some details of the MaxSAT projection step could be more thoroughly explained.
* Oracle dependence could be further discussed.

**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**  This paper represents a significant contribution to the field and is well-deserving of acceptance to NIPS.  I would encourage the authors to address the minor weaknesses mentioned above in a revised version of the paper.
