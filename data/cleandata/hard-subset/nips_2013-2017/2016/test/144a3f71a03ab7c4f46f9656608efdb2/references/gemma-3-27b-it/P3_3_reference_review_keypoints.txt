- Overall: significant contribution addressing non-decomposable ancestral constraints in BN learning; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Main idea: uses Chen et al.'s DAG search framework, pruning EC trees and projecting non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable ones to enable an oracle-based search.  
- Projection step: projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints is the paper’s key novel insight and enables large efficiency gains.  
- Theoretical soundness: pruning strategy for the EC tree (Theorem 1) and the MaxSAT reduction for inferring ordering constraints (Theorem 4) are well-founded.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over GOBNILP and provides a convincing ablation showing the benefit of constraint projection.  
- Scaling behavior: observes an "easy-hard-easy" trend as constraint density varies.  
- Practical significance: even a small amount of ancestral knowledge can yield benefits comparable to increasing dataset size.  
- Relation to prior work: thoughtfully builds on Chen et al. (2015,2016), contrasts with ILP/CP approaches and constraint-based ancestral methods, and emphasizes optimal structure learning.  
- Strength — Novelty: combining the Chen et al. framework with ancestral-constraint projection is genuinely novel.  
- Strength — Technical/Empirical: solid theoretical development and compelling experimental validation.  
- Weakness — Exposition: EC tree explanation is somewhat rushed and would benefit from clearer intuition and a pruning diagram/example.  
- Weakness — MaxSAT details: MaxSAT instance construction is described at a high level; implementation choices (EVASOLVER) and scalability concerns need more discussion.  
- Weakness — Comparisons/datasets: limited comparison to other relevant techniques (e.g., hybrid methods) and no discussion of potential limitations of benchmark datasets for ancestral-constraint challenges.  
- Final recommendation: strongly accept — strengths (novelty, soundness, empirical gains) outweigh minor clarity and comparison issues.
