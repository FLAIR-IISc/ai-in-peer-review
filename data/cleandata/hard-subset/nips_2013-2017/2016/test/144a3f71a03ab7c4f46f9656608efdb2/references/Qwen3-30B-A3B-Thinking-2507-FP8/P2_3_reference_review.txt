

# Review: "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

## Summary
This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian network structures when provided with ancestral constraints (non-decomposable constraints specifying ancestor-descendant relationships between variables). The authors build upon Chen et al.'s EC tree search space for learning with non-decomposable scores, introducing methods to prune the search space based on ancestral constraints and project these constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints that can be leveraged by existing oracles. The approach demonstrates significant efficiency improvements over ILP-based methods like GOBNILP.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-justified theoretical contributions. The authors prove Theorems 1-4 establishing the soundness of their pruning approach and the correctness of their constraint projection methods. The EC tree adaptation for ancestral constraints is logically derived from prior work on BN graphs and EC trees. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using standard Bayesian network benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2) across varying problem sizes (n=12-20 variables), dataset sizes (N=512-8192), and constraint percentages (p=0-100%). The results consistently show orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over ILP approaches when using the projected constraints. The paper correctly acknowledges limitations, such as the need to enumerate structures in some pruning cases, and properly evaluates both strengths and weaknesses.

## Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with a logical flow from problem statement to technical background, proposed method, and evaluation. The figures (Figures 1a-b) effectively illustrate the BN graph and EC tree concepts. The authors provide sufficient background on Bayesian network structure learning, ancestral constraints, and related work. The technical details are presented accessibly, though some readers unfamiliar with Bayesian network structure learning may need to consult additional references for full understanding. The paper adequately informs the reader about the methodology and results, and the experimental setup is described with enough detail for reproducibility.

## Originality
The paper makes a clear and significant contribution by addressing a gap in the literature: integrating non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning. While prior work (Chen et al., 2015-2016) established search spaces for non-decomposable scores, and ILP/CP approaches can theoretically handle such constraints, this paper is the first to provide an efficient implementation and empirical evaluation of these approaches for ancestral constraints. The key novelty lies in their method for projecting ancestral constraints into decomposable constraints that can be leveraged by existing oracles. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from related work, including constraint-based methods (Borboudakis & Tsamardinos, 2012-2013) and ILP approaches (Cussens, 2008).

## Significance
The significance of this work is substantial. The paper demonstrates that a relatively small number of ancestral constraints (10-25%) can have a similar impact on model accuracy as increasing training data from 512 to 8192 samples, highlighting the value of incorporating domain knowledge. The orders-of-magnitude efficiency improvements over ILP approaches make this method practically valuable for real-world applications where ancestral constraints are available. The approach scales to 20 variables, which is competitive with state-of-the-art methods for BDeu scores. The results provide strong evidence that incorporating background knowledge through ancestral constraints can dramatically reduce the computational burden of structure learning without sacrificing model quality.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Theoretical foundation is rigorous with well-proven theorems
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple benchmarks and problem sizes
- Demonstrates significant practical advantages (orders-of-magnitude speedups)
- Clear demonstration of how background knowledge can reduce data requirements
- Well-motivated extension of prior EC tree work (Chen et al., 2015-2016)

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could better explain why ILP approaches struggle with ancestral constraints despite being theoretically capable (Section 3)
- Limited analysis of the computational complexity of the constraint projection step
- The paper doesn't fully explore the limitations of the constraint projection method (e.g., when it might fail to infer useful constraints)
- The connection to practical applications could be strengthened with more domain-specific examples

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to Bayesian network structure learning. The authors address a well-motivated problem with a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution. The work advances the state of the art in structure learning with non-decomposable constraints, with clear practical implications. The paper meets all four NIPS review criteria at a high level, with particular strengths in quality and significance.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it addresses a fundamental problem in probabilistic graphical models with clear theoretical and practical contributions. The results are well-supported by experiments, and the work builds upon but significantly extends prior research in the field. The authors have demonstrated a clear understanding of the relevant literature and have made a valuable contribution to the field.

**Recommendation: Accept** - This paper makes a significant contribution to Bayesian network structure learning with non-decomposable constraints, demonstrated through rigorous theory and comprehensive experiments. The work is of high quality, clear, original, and significant for the machine learning community.
