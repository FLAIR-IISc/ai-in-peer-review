- Addresses incorporation of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.
- Proposes an EC tree search space plus projection of ancestral constraints into decomposable edge/ordering constraints to use existing optimal learners.
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based GOBNILP on multiple benchmark networks.
- Builds on prior work (Chen et al.) and fills a noted limitation: most learners assume decomposable scores and can’t enforce ancestral constraints.
- Theoretical foundation is strong: Theorems 2–4 justify constraint projection and EC tree pruning ensures completeness.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and systematic (benchmarks, varying variables, data sizes, constraint percentages).
- Results show small numbers of ancestral constraints can improve model quality comparably to large increases in training data.
- One technical limitation: the paper does not address handling inconsistent/contradictory ancestral constraints.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear definitions, examples, and illustrative figures.
- Methods (constraint projection algorithms, MaxSAT formulation) are described in sufficient detail for experts to reproduce.
- Original contribution: first comprehensive, efficient solution for ancestral (non-decomposable) constraints in structure learning.
- Distinguishes clearly from order-graph methods and ILP/CP approaches; constraint projection technique is novel and general.
- Significant practical impact for domains (medical, biological) where domain knowledge is available and data are costly.
- Scalability is competitive (demonstrated up to 20 variables) and applicable to larger problems than prior non-decomposable approaches.
- Strengths: novel problem focus, rigorous theory, strong empirical speedups, practical relevance, and clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: no treatment of inconsistent constraint handling, lack of analysis on constraint quality effects, missing direct comparison to constraint-based methods, limited intuition/scalability discussion for MaxSAT, and limited theory on constraint density vs. search reduction.
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful problem, theoretically sound and efficient solution, strong empirical gains, extensible framework, and high-quality writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved inconsistent-constraint issue, incomplete comparisons, perceived MaxSAT complexity without more justification, and unexamined dependence on constraint quality.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; suggest a brief revision adding discussion and potential remedies for inconsistent ancestral constraints.
