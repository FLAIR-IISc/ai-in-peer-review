- Tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral (ancestor-descendant) constraints, a non-decomposable and challenging prior.  
- Builds on EC tree search for non-decomposable scores and extends it to handle ancestral constraints via pruning.  
- Introduces projection of non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints to strengthen the oracle.  
- Empirically shows large speedups and better performance than an ILP-based approach, with an ablation study highlighting projection’s impact.  
- Provides theoretical backing (Theorems 1–4) for the projection and pruning mechanisms.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear technical exposition overall.  
- Addresses an important problem: incorporating prior knowledge improves BN accuracy and interpretability.  
- Novel contribution: constraint projection is a clever method that materially improves search efficiency.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrate orders-of-magnitude improvements in many cases.  
- Limited baselines: comparison is mainly to ILP; lacks comparisons to modern scalable score-based or heuristic ordering methods.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments scale to 20 variables but more discussion and analysis of bottlenecks are needed.  
- Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver may limit performance; sensitivity analysis of solver effects is missing.  
- Experiments use simulated data only, raising questions about real-world generalization under noise and data quality issues.  
- Minor clarity issues: proofs/discussion of Theorems 2 and 3 could be clearer, and an illustrative example would help.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand baseline comparisons, deepen scalability discussion, and comment on data/noise impact.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
