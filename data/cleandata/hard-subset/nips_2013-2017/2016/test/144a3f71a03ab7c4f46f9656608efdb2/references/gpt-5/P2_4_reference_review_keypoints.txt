- Tackles exact BN structure learning with non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints by adapting EC-tree/A* search and pruning.
- Empowers a decomposable-score oracle via projection of non-decomposable constraints into entailed edges and precedence (MaxSAT) constraints.
- Theorems 2–3 give rules for inferring entailed edge presence/absence; Theorem 4 presents a MaxSAT encoding for sound precedence constraints.
- Pruning on the EC tree is justified by monotonicity: adding leaves cannot fix violated ancestral relations among existing variables.
- Projection rules for negative and positive edges are intuitive and preserve admissibility when passed to the oracle.
- MaxSAT formulation is conservative (sufficient conditions), maintaining heuristic admissibility.
- Several proofs and key algorithmic details (closure procedure, proofs of Theorems 2–4, CPDAG pruning test) are omitted, limiting reproducibility.
- The need to enumerate class(P) in inconclusive cases could be expensive; frequency and runtime impact are not quantified.
- MaxSAT runtime and projection overheads are not reported.
- Empirical comparison to GOBNILP is fair and extensive (multiple networks, data sizes, constraint densities, 50 replicates).
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP and an easy–hard–easy pattern as constraint proportion varies.
- Accuracy (SHD) improves with more data and constraints; scaling demonstrated up to n=20 variables.
- Paper is mostly clear; high-level ideas, oracle role, and benefits of projection are well explained.
- Some statements (e.g., Theorem 1) are confusing or misstated and need clarification.
- Presentation issues include omitted algorithmic details and formatting/citation glitches.
- Novel integration of EC-tree pruning with oracle-guided A* to handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.
- Projection of ancestral constraints into edges and large precedence sets via MaxSAT is original and practically useful.
- Impactful for exact BN learning and practitioners needing causal background knowledge; wider adoption would benefit from code release.
- Pros: clear problem formulation, elegant integration, theoretically guaranteed projection, strong empirical gains.
- Cons: omitted theoretical details, unquantified class(P) enumeration cost, no CP-based comparisons, and some presentation shortcomings.
- Suggested improvements: add proofs/appendix, report class(P)/MaxSAT stats and ablations, compare to CP/MaxSAT-based BN learning, and release code.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid, novel, and effective work but needs stronger theoretical details and clarifications.
