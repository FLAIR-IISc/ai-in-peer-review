- Addresses limitation of optimal Bayesian network structure learning by enabling non-decomposable ancestral constraints via projection into decomposable constraints.
- Builds on Chen et al.'s EC tree search space and prunes it to incorporate ancestral constraints, improving search efficiency.
- Introduces a novel projection technique that converts ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and ordering constraints for an oracle learner.
- Theoretical contributions: Theorems 1–4 prove correctness of pruning and constraint projection, with careful handling of transitivity and cycles.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: standard benchmarks, varied parameters, 50 dataset averages, and comparisons to ILP (GOBNILP).
- Results show orders-of-magnitude speedups over ILP-based methods while maintaining comparable accuracy; speedup grows with problem size.
- Novelty: first systematic method for ancestral constraints in non-decomposable-score optimal BN learning and first practical ILP vs non-decomposable comparison.
- Technical quality is high: sound proofs, careful consideration of edge cases, and well-motivated solutions.
- Clarity is good: well-organized, accessible to BN readers, and effective figures; MaxSAT ordering formulation may challenge some readers.
- Significance is high: practical importance, demonstrates that modest constraints can substitute for much larger datasets, and scales competitively (to ~20 vars).
- Strengths include addressing an overlooked practical problem, solid theory, extensive experiments, major speedups, and an elegant EC tree adaptation.
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of constraint projection (especially MaxSAT), and scalability of that step.
- Additional weaknesses: no comparison with recent constraint-programming approaches, no discussion of inconsistent/contradictory constraints, unexplained ILP overhead at small n, limited analysis of constraint percentage vs accuracy.
- Recommended improvements: discuss projection complexity and scaling, compare to recent CP methods, explain handling of inconsistent constraints, and analyze constraint-count vs accuracy trade-offs.
- Final recommendation: Accept — contributions are significant, novel, and well-supported by theory and experiments.
- Acceptance arguments: solves a significant practical problem, sound theoretical basis, strong empirical speedups, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against (minor): incomplete discussion of projection complexity, missing comparisons to all relevant CP approaches, and lack of treatment for inconsistent constraints.
