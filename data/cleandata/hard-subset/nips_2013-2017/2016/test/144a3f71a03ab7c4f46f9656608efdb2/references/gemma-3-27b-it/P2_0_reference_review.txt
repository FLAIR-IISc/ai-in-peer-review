## Review of "Learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning Bayesian networks (BNs) when prior knowledge is available in the form of ancestral constraints (specifying ancestor/descendant relationships between variables).  Existing optimal structure learning methods struggle with these non-decomposable constraints. The authors build upon their previous work [Chen et al., 2015, 2016] which introduced a framework for optimal BN learning with non-decomposable priors, adapting it to incorporate ancestral constraints. They achieve this by pruning the search space (using an EC tree) based on the constraints and, crucially, by *projecting* these constraints into decomposable forms to empower an oracle used within their search process.  The paper demonstrates, through empirical evaluation, that this approach significantly outperforms Integer Linear Programming (ILP) based methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Incorporating background knowledge into BN learning is crucial for real-world applications, and ancestral constraints are a natural and expressive form of this knowledge.
* **Novel approach:** Extending the framework of Chen et al. [2015, 2016] to handle non-decomposable constraints is a significant contribution.  The idea of *projecting* constraints is clever and demonstrably effective.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, showing orders-of-magnitude speedups compared to ILP.  The results also highlight the benefit of constraint projection.  Scaling to 20 variables is impressive.
* **Soundness and Completeness:** The theoretical guarantee of pruning correctness (Theorem 1) and the completeness of the constraint projection (Theorem 4) add confidence in the approach.
* **Clear Connection to Prior Work:** The paper builds directly on previous work, clearly explaining the extensions and improvements.
* **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is logically structured, making it easy to follow the technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Framework:** The core framework (BN graphs, EC trees, oracles) is quite complex and may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with the prior work to fully grasp. A more detailed explanation or intuitive example of EC trees would be beneficial.
* **Oracle Dependence:**  The performance relies heavily on the effectiveness of the “oracle” (URLEARNING in their experiments).  The sensitivity to oracle choice isn't thoroughly investigated.  While they acknowledge this, a discussion of the implications of using different oracles would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Scope of ILP Comparison:** While the ILP comparison is compelling, it's limited to a single ILP solver (GOBNILP).  Comparing against a broader range of ILP solvers could provide more comprehensive insights.
* **MaxSAT Solver:** The use of an external MaxSAT solver for ordering constraint projection adds complexity and a potential bottleneck.  A discussion of the scalability of the MaxSAT solver and its impact on the overall approach would be useful.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (9/10):** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical justification for the proposed methods. The empirical results strongly support the claims. The completeness proofs for the constraint handling are impressive.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The writing is generally clear, though the complexity of the underlying framework could be a barrier for some readers.  More illustrative examples could improve understanding.
* **Originality (9/10):** The extension of the existing framework to handle ancestral constraints and the introduction of constraint projection are novel and represent a significant contribution.
* **Significance (9/10):** This work has the potential to significantly impact the field of Bayesian network learning, making it possible to incorporate more realistic and useful prior knowledge. The demonstrated speedups over ILP are highly significant.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Solves an important problem with a novel and effective approach.
* Strong empirical results demonstrating significant performance improvements.
* Sound theoretical foundations and completeness guarantees.
* Builds upon and extends a promising line of research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The framework is complex and may be difficult for some readers to understand.
* Reliance on an external MaxSAT solver could be a potential limitation.
* Oracle dependence could affect generalizability.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian network learning. The proposed approach is novel, well-justified, and demonstrably effective. While the complexity of the framework and reliance on an oracle are minor concerns, the overall quality and significance of the work outweigh these drawbacks.

**Recommendation:**  **Strong Accept**
