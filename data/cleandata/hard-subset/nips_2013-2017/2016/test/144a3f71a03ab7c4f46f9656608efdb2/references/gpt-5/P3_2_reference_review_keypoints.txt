- Addresses exact BN structure learning under non-decomposable ancestral (path) constraints using EC-tree search and pruning.
- Leverages EC-tree over CPDAGs and "projection" methods to derive decomposable constraints for a decomposable-score oracle.
- Provides pruning rules: negative constraint prunes if P has directed X→…→Y; positive constraint prunes if no partially directed path exists; otherwise enumerate DAGs in class(P).
- Pruning rules are sound, but the fallback enumeration can be exponential and its frequency/cost are not quantified.
- Theorem 1 is misstated; correct monotonicity is: if P violates A then all descendants violate A, but the converse does not hold.
- Projection to edge constraints uses a closure (transitive closure and inconsistency propagation) that is referenced but not specified in detail or complexity.
- Theorem 2 (negative edge inference) is intuitive and likely correct; Theorem 3 (conditions for inferring positive edges) is plausible but needs proof sketch and edge-case examples.
- Projection to ordering constraints is implemented via a MaxSAT encoding that yields a safe but conservative set of orderings entailed by A.
- Theorem 4 gives a sufficient condition for order entailment; discussion of tightness/completeness and trade-offs between maximality and safety is missing.
- Need empirical reporting of MaxSAT overhead and its contribution to total runtime.
- Overall methodology (pruning + projection) appears sound aside from Theorem 1 and omitted proofs/algorithms, harming reproducibility.
- Experimental setup uses standard BN benchmarks, BDeu score, URLearning as oracle, EVASOLVER for MaxSAT, and comparisons to GOBNILP.
- Results show large runtime speedups over ILP, increasing with n, and projected constraints substantially help both methods.
- Observe an easy–hard–easy runtime pattern versus constraint proportion p; SHD improves with more data and more constraints.
- Missing analyses: runtime breakdown (projection vs search), ablations (edge-only vs order-only vs pruning-only), robustness to noisy constraints, CP/ILP comparisons with parent-set bounds, and larger-n bounded in-degree experiments.
- Clarity problems: formatting issues, broken references, omitted algorithmic details, missing figures, and imprecise statements reduce readability.
- Originality: novel integration of ancestral constraints into EC-tree pruning and new projection rules to derive decomposable edge/order constraints for oracles.
- Significance: practically important and shows substantial exact-method speedups, but limited scale (n≈20) constrains impact.
- Pros: sound/practical pruning, clever projection to empower decomposable oracles, and strong empirical speedups with clear qualitative insights.
- Cons: misstated theoretical points, omitted crucial details and proofs, limited evaluation scope, and unquantified projection/MaxSAT overhead.
- Suggested fixes: correct Theorem 1, provide proofs/sketches for Theorems 2–4, detail the closure algorithm and complexity, report enumeration frequency/cost, give runtime breakdowns and ablations, test robustness to noisy/soft constraints, and include bounded in-degree experiments.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful technical and empirical advance but needs improved clarity, proofs, and additional experiments.
