- Problem: Incorporates non-decomposable ancestral constraints into Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Method: Extends the EC tree with a sound pruning mechanism by projecting ancestral constraints to decomposable edge and ordering constraints.  
- Empirical result: Orders-of-magnitude faster than ILP (GOBNILP) on benchmarks; small numbers of constraints greatly improve model quality (comparable to large increases in data).

- Strength: Clearly identifies an important gap—mainstream learners can't handle non-decomposable ancestral constraints.  
- Strength: Provides sound and complete theoretical results for pruning and constraint projection (Theorems 1–4).  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple benchmarks and settings.  
- Strength: Shows practical significance—few constraints can substantially reduce data needs and improve models.  
- Strength: Technical rigor with clear definitions and well-structured arguments.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to related constraint-based methods (e.g., Borboudakis & Tsamardinos).  
- Weakness: Lacks time-complexity analysis for the constraint projection algorithms.  
- Weakness: MaxSAT formulation for ordering constraints is under-explained and lacks discussion of alternatives.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations (scalability with many or inconsistent/incorrect constraints).  
- Weakness: Doesn't adequately explain why ILP approaches are comparatively slow when enforcing ancestral constraints.

- Quality: Technically sound with well-justified theoretical results and comprehensive experiments.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written, but Section 4.3 (EC trees + pruning) and the MaxSAT section need clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Originality: Novel extension of EC tree and a new constraint projection technique addressing an overlooked problem.  
- Significance: Practical and theoretical advance—enables efficient optimal learning with ancestral constraints and reduces data requirements in practice.  
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS.

- Suggestion: Expand comparisons with constraint-based approaches like Borboudakis & Tsamardinos (2012, 2013).  
- Suggestion: Add complexity/scale analysis for projection algorithms (Theorems 2–3).  
- Suggestion: Analyze and explain why ILP encodings are computationally expensive for ancestral constraints.  
- Suggestion: Include a limitations section covering inconsistent constraints, sensitivity to noisy constraints, and scalability with many constraints.  
- Suggestion: Elaborate and motivate the MaxSAT formulation and discuss trade-offs with alternatives.

- Final: Strong, complete contribution with sound theory and convincing experiments; a valuable addition to the literature.
