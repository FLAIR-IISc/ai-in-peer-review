- Addresses learning Bayesian networks with non-decomposable ancestral constraints by extending EC tree search with pruning and decomposable-constraint inference.  
- Develops a sound and complete constraint projection method (Theorems 2–4) to infer edge and ordering constraints from ancestral constraints.  
- Uses the projection to enable a decomposable-score oracle, yielding dramatic efficiency improvements over ILP (GOBNILP).  
- Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude speedups on standard benchmarks (ALARM, ANDES, CHILD, CPCS54, HEPAR2).  
- Experimental design is comprehensive (varying n, N, and constraint percentage p) and results are reproducible with implementation/hardware details.  
- Technically rigorous with formal proofs and well-supported empirical evidence; acknowledges limitations like "easy-hard-easy" search trends.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; EC tree representation and algorithms are well-explained with helpful figures and examples.  
- Novel contribution: first to show EC tree plus constraint projection handles ancestral constraints far more efficiently than prior ILP/CP approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: small fractions of ancestral constraints (10–25%) can boost model accuracy similarly to much larger datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed complexity analysis of how the projection step scales with number of ancestral constraints.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other constraint-based approaches (e.g., constraint programming) beyond ILP.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical implementation issues (eliciting constraints, conflicts, noisy constraints).  
- Weakness: explanation of why EC tree outperforms ILP is underdeveloped; needs deeper computational-complexity comparison.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and well-supported paper suitable for NIPS.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestion to address projection complexity and broader method comparisons in future work.
