- Main contribution: method to project non-decomposable ancestral constraints into decomposable edge and topological-ordering constraints (novelty concentrated in Section 5).
- Presentation: paper is well written and clearly explains theory and experiments.
- Concern 1: Table 3 shows improvement for EC trees but not ILP, raising doubts about whether the projection generalizes to other learning approaches.
- Request: provide evidence that the inferred decomposable constraints improve performance/efficiency of methods that originally cannot handle non-decomposable constraints.
- Concern 2: Table 2 exhibits an easy–hard–easy pattern as the proportion of ancestral constraints increases; authors should explain why and whether EC trees are highly sensitive to the amount of domain knowledge compared to ILP.
- Implication: the number of ancestral constraints may need careful tuning to achieve ideal EC tree performance.
- Concern 3: Table 2 reports structural Hamming distance only for EC trees; add comparison of SHD with the ILP approach.
- Suggestion: drop Section 4.1 on BN graph and instead expand explanations of EC trees and the A* algorithm in the EC-tree experimental setting.
