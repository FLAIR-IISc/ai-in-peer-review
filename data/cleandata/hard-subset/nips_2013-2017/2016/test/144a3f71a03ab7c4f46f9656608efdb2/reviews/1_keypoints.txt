- Paper tackles learning Bayesian networks with ancestral constraints using branch-and-bound A* search over CPDAGs, symmetry breaking, and a heuristic from URLearning (details deferred to prior work).  
- Lemma 3 is the key result enabling pruning with ancestral constraints, but its proof is unclear and needs better justification.  
- The statement "By the EC tree edge generation rules, Gk also contains edge Z -> W" requires more explanation.  
- The treatment of implied/projected constraints and inferred ordering constraints is poorly explained; the use of "can infer" is misleading without clarifying that authors add consistent (but not necessarily entailed) ordering constraints.  
- The authors use a MAXSAT approach to control which ordering constraints are added, but the inference procedure and rationale are insufficiently detailed.  
- Experimental comparison to GOBNILP shows large speedups, but it is unclear if GOBNILP’s poor performance is due to ancestral constraints specifically or its general weakness with unbounded parent set size.  
- The paper compares against a MAXSAT encoding of ancestral constraints (Cussens 2008), which is a poor ILP formulation; a tighter ILP using A(X,Y) and I(Z,U) variables with transitivity and A–I linking constraints would be preferable.  
- Comparing to a weak encoding weakens the empirical claim; even if the suggested ILP were used, the authors’ method may still win, but the current comparison is not fully convincing.  
- The work of van Beek and Hoffmann (CP 2015) is not discussed; it often outperforms GOBNILP and could likely be adapted to handle ancestral constraints and should be compared.
