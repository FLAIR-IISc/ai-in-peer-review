The paper "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit" proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs when cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors formalize this task as a multi-fidelity bandit, where at each time step, the forecaster may choose to play an arm at any one of M fidelities, with each fidelity providing a biased estimate of the highest fidelity. They develop a novel upper confidence bound procedure, MF-UCB, which adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs, achieving better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their approach. The MF-UCB algorithm is well-explained, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its performance, including a regret bound and a lower bound. The paper also includes some simulations on synthetic problems, which demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel and interesting framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the presence of cheaper approximations.
* The MF-UCB algorithm is well-designed and effectively adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs.
* The analysis of the algorithm's performance is thorough and provides valuable insights into its behavior.
* The simulations on synthetic problems demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes that the lower fidelities provide biased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a clear discussion of the limitations of their approach and how it can be extended to more general settings.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the practical implications of the results and how they can be applied to real-world problems.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas. The MF-UCB algorithm is a valuable contribution to the field, and the analysis of its performance provides valuable insights into its behavior. However, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations of the approach and its practical implications.
