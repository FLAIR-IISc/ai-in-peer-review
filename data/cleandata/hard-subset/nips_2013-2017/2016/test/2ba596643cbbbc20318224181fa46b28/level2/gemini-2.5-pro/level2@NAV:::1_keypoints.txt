- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where arms can be observed at multiple fidelities that trade off cost and biased approximation.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an intuitive extension of UCB that uses cheap, low-fidelity pulls to prune arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including a regret upper bound (Theorem 2) showing advantage over naive UCB.  
- Proves a lower bound (Theorem 4), demonstrating near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-designed, and the algorithmic ideas are clear and principled.  
- Writing and presentation are excellent: clear motivation, notation, algorithm description, and honesty about limitations.  
- Assumption 1 (fast decay of bias terms ζ(m)) is critical for the best theoretical results and deserves discussion about milder conditions.  
- Experiments are synthetic only; semi-synthetic or real-world evaluations with natural fidelities would strengthen practical claims.  
- Notes an open gap between upper and lower bounds for a specific class of arms, left for future work.  
- High potential impact: formulation addresses common practical challenges and could inspire follow-up research.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper, strengths outweigh minor weaknesses, strong recommendation for acceptance.
