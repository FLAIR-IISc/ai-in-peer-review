- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem where arms can be pulled at different fidelities that trade cost for biased estimates.  
- Proposes the Multi-Fidelity UCB (MF-UCB) algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity samples to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides an upper bound on MF-UCB regret showing it outperforms naive fidelity-ignorant strategies.  
- Supplies a problem-dependent lower bound (Theorem 4) establishing near-optimality of MF-UCB for a broad class of instances.  
- Decomposes arms into partitions K(m), giving clear intuition that many arms are explored primarily at low fidelities.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong and technically sound, with comprehensive regret decomposition.  
- Proof sketches and fidelity-switching rationale are clear and helpful for understanding the analysis.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, notation, and algorithm presentation.  
- Original contribution: first formal analysis of multi-fidelity bandits within the regret-minimization/UCB framework.  
- Significant practical relevance to settings with cheap biased approximations (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, simulations).  
- Weakness: analysis assumes known bias bounds ζ(m), which may be unrealistic in practice and requires discussion or estimation methods.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 on ζ(m) decay is technical and mainly simplifies bounds; performance without it needs clearer discussion.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic problems and would be stronger with semi-synthetic or real-world experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated problem; elegant algorithm; strong theoretical guarantees including lower bound; excellent exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on known ζ(m) parameters and limited empirical validation.
