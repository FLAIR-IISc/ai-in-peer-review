- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple evaluations with different costs and biases.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms before costly high-fidelity tests.  
- Provides strong theoretical results: a regret bound showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when few arms are promising, plus a near-matching lower bound.  
- Clearly situates the work within bandit literature and distinguishes it from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with well-motivated assumptions and careful handling of lower-fidelity bias.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear with precise pseudocode.  
- Synthetic experiments robustly support theory, showing consistent performance gains over UCB across configurations.  
- Writing quality and clarity are professional and accessible to bandit researchers.  
- Novelty is significant: extends classical bandits to practical multi-fidelity cost–benefit trade-offs, properly referencing prior related work.  
- Practical significance is strong, with relevant applications (e.g., online advertising) that can reduce resource use.  
- Main limitation: MF-UCB may be suboptimal for arms with misleading lower-fidelity estimates (those in K(m)7), leaving a gap between upper and lower bounds.  
- Minor improvement suggested: briefly discuss computational complexity relative to standard UCB.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with strong theory and empirical validation; recommended for acceptance.
