- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm has multiple biased, costed fidelities.  
- Motivates the problem with practical applications (e.g., online advertising) where cheaper biased estimates exist.  
- Formalizes the setting with a cost-weighted regret that balances fidelity cost against estimate quality.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive algorithm that uses lower fidelities for exploration and higher fidelities for exploitation.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: upper bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound, showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Shows MF-UCB yields significant regret improvements over standard UCB when few arms are promising relative to total arms.  
- Carefully handles fidelity biases (not just variance) in analysis and proofs, with clear intuition in proof sketches.  
- Empirical (synthetic) results support theory: MF-UCB consistently outperforms UCB and the paper visualizes fidelity allocation.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, though notation is dense for readers new to bandits.  
- Related work is well-positioned; distinguishes this setting from budgeted bandits and other multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Acknowledges limitations: a gap remains between the algorithm's regret bound and the lower bound for a subset of arms (K(m)).  
- Minor suggestions: give more intuition for the choice of γ(m), discuss real-world algorithm behavior beyond synthetic/advertising, and clarify the practical impact of Assumption 1 on ζ(m) decay.  
- Overall: original, significant, technically sound contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support; recommends acceptance.
