- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms have costly true rewards and cheaper biased fidelities.  
- Defines a cost-aware regret notion suitable for a fixed-budget (capital) setting.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm extending UCB that selects both arm and fidelity by trading off information gain, cost, and bias.  
- Provides an upper bound on MF-UCB’s regret showing adaptation to problem structure and improved performance over always using highest fidelity.  
- Proves a problem-specific lower bound and shows MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Theorem 2 insight: regret for a suboptimal arm depends on the cost of the fidelity that can identify it as poor, not uniformly on the highest fidelity.  
- Transparently discusses a gap between upper and lower bounds for a subset of arms and explains the algorithmic trade-off causing it.  
- Assumption 1 (fast decay of fidelity biases ζ(m)) may be strong; authors should discuss how relaxing it affects regret.  
- Synthetic experiments corroborate theoretical claims but empirical validation is limited to simple simulations.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-motivated, and clearly written with helpful illustrations and proof sketches.  
- Partitioning arms into K(m) is a key conceptual and analytical tool, explained effectively.  
- Originality: first formalization of multi-fidelity in stochastic bandits with rigorous regret analysis and a novel fidelity-selection rule.  
- Significance: practically important for tasks like hyperparameter tuning and experimentation; framework likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Recommendation: minor weaknesses outweighed by strengths; strongly recommend acceptance.
