- Introduces multi-fidelity K-armed bandits with M fidelities, known bias bounds ζ(m), and capital-weighted regret based on highest-fidelity means per unit cost.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-fidelity UCBs for μ(M)k using empirical means + ζ(m), selects arm by tightest UCB and escalates fidelity when confidence radius < γ(m).  
- Proves an upper regret bound showing most low-cost effort is spent on obviously suboptimal arms at low fidelities and high-fidelity plays concentrate on near-optimal arms.  
- Provides a lower bound (Bernoulli rewards, sub-polynomial play assumption) matching the upper bound on a large subset of instances, with an explained gap on a specific set K(m)7.  
- Positions work distinct from budgeted bandits and weak/strong-label active learning, complementing multi-fidelity optimization and connecting to UCB and GP-based multi-fidelity bandits.  
- The per-fidelity UCB construction and fidelity-selection rule are principled; main regret decomposition via K(m) is informative.  
- Assumes known ζ(m) and uniform concentration ψ across fidelities—useful for tight analysis but a practical limitation.  
- Assumption 1 (ζ(m) decay) is a technical regularity simplifying analysis but not essential for correctness; lower-bound Assumption 3 is standard.  
- Proof approach and lemmas controlling per-fidelity play counts and total plays are convincing, with role of ρ > 4 explained.  
- Writing is clear and organized; heavy notation is mitigated by narrative and figures; regret metric is nonstandard but well motivated.  
- Suggests clarifications: explicitly state ζ(M)=0 and that min-over-m UCB bounds μ(M)k, guidance on choosing ρ and ζ mis-specification sensitivity, and move experimental setup details to main text.  
- Originality: formalization and MF-UCB fidelity rule are novel and provide new interpretative insights.  
- Significance: addresses realistic settings, algorithm is simple and practical given ζ(m), and analysis shows tangible gains; likely to spur follow-up work.  
- Strengths: clear formulation, simple algorithm with finite-time guarantees, adaptive regret bounds, matching lower bound on many instances, experiments align with theory.  
- Weaknesses: requires known ζ(m) with no robustness study, Assumption 1/γ(m) choices may be restrictive, lower bound limited to Bernoulli, only synthetic experiments, unresolved gap on K(m)7.  
- Key suggestions: explore online or robust learning of ζ(m), empirically test sensitivity to ζ mis-specification, consider cheap hypothesis tests to handle K(m)7, and report full experimental settings and errors in main text.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel formulation, strong theoretical results, simple practical algorithm, well-situated literature review.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on known bias bounds, limited lower-bound scope and open optimality gap, lack of real-world experiments.  
- Overall: high-quality, original, and significant paper that would benefit from minor clarifications and expanded empirical description.
