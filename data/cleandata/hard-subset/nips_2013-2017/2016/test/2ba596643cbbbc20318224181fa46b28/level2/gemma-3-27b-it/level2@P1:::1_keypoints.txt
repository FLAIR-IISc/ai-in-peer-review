- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit problem where precise rewards are costly but cheaper noisy approximations exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper confidence bound algorithm tailored to multi-fidelity settings.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees demonstrating near-optimal regret with rigorous analysis.  
- Problem is well-motivated with examples from online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and formalization/notation is consistent.  
- Fidelity selection criterion based on γ(m) is well-explained and intuitive.  
- Key contribution: quantifies bias of lower-fidelity approximations via ζ(m) and incorporates it into confidence bounds.  
- Includes both upper and complementary lower regret bounds, showing deep theoretical understanding.  
- Simulations support theoretical claims and show benefits over standard UCB.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and appropriately cites literature.  
- Main concern: assumption of known ζ(m) values may be unrealistic; need discussion of estimation or handling unknown ζ(m).  
- Notation and dense analysis can be hard to follow; more intuitive explanations or examples would improve readability.  
- Only proof sketches are provided; full proofs would increase confidence in results.  
- Lower bound discussion is somewhat disconnected from the main analysis and needs tighter integration and gap discussion.  
- Experiments are synthetic; including real-world datasets or applications would strengthen empirical validation.  
- The separation argument between identifying good arms and fine-tuning them is somewhat hand-wavy and needs more justification.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically relevant contribution with solid theory and empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite minor issues.
