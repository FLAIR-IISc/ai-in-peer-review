- Proposes a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple fidelities providing biased estimates of the highest fidelity.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Claims MF-UCB attains better regret than naive UCB that ignores multi-fidelity information.  
- Claims MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Provides an upper bound on MF-UCB regret and a lower bound for any strategy in this setting.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem formulation with thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: MF-UCB intuitively uses low fidelities to eliminate bad arms and reserves high fidelity for promising arms.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis (upper and lower regret bounds).  
- Weakness: assumes lower fidelities provide biased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental evaluation on real-world problems.  
- For acceptance: novel problem formulation and strong theoretical support.  
- For acceptance: well-designed, intuitively appealing algorithm (MF-UCB).  
- For acceptance: lower bound supports near-optimality claim.  
- Against acceptance: fidelity-assumption may limit practical applicability.  
- Against acceptance: absence of real-world experiments makes practical effectiveness unclear.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested future work to relax fidelity assumptions and add real-world experiments.
