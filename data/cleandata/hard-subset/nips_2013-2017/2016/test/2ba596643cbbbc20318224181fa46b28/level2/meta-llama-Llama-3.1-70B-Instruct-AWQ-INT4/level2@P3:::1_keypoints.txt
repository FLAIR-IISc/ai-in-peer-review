- Summary: Proposes MF-UCB for multi-armed bandits with cheaper low-fidelity evaluations to reduce expensive high-fidelity queries, showing improved regret and near-optimality via a lower bound.

- Novel framework: Introduces a new multi-fidelity bandit setting, a significant conceptual contribution.

- Efficient algorithm: MF-UCB uses cheap plays to eliminate bad arms and reserves costly queries for promising arms.

- Theoretical guarantees: Provides regret bounds for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound indicating near-optimality.

- Simulations: Empirical results validate the practical effectiveness of MF-UCB.

- Complexity: The multi-fidelity, costed setting is complex and potentially hard to analyze and understand.

- Assumptions: Relies on concentration inequalities and regularity conditions that may not hold universally.

- Limited scope: Focuses on a specific setting, which may limit applicability to other bandit problems.

- Originality/significance: Original and significant, offering a foundation for further research in multi-fidelity bandits.

- Clarity/organization: Well-organized and clearly written, with supportive tables, figures, and appendices.

- Related work: Thorough literature review that clearly differentiates the paper’s contributions from prior work.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel framework and algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and supporting simulations.

- Arguments for rejection: Complexity of the setting and limited generalizability to other applications.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should consider addressing complexity and scope in future work.
