- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple fidelities with different costs and biases, motivated by practical settings (e.g., online advertising).
- Formalizes the problem using cost-weighted regret and bias parameters ζ(m).
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that dynamically selects fidelities based on confidence intervals and known bias parameters.
- Shows MF-UCB achieves improved regret O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) by using low-fidelity observations to eliminate suboptimal arms.
- Provides a matching lower bound demonstrating near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear assumptions (notably decay of ζ(m)) and careful proofs.
- Clearly distinguishes this work from prior multi-fidelity and budgeted bandit literature.
- Synthetic experiments validate theoretical insights and show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; notation consistent and algorithm reproducible; figures effectively illustrate key ideas.
- Originality is significant as the first formal regret study for multi-fidelity bandits addressing bias explicitly.
- Contribution is practically significant for settings with costly evaluations (advertising, algorithm selection, clinical trials).
- Minor weaknesses: assumes known bias parameters ζ(m), experiments are relatively brief, and there remains a gap between lower bound and MF-UCB for some arms (noted as open problem).
- Overall assessment: strong paper that meets NIPS criteria and should be accepted.
