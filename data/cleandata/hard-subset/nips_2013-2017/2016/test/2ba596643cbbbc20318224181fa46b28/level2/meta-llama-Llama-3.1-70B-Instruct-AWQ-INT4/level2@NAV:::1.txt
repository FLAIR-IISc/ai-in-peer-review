This paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in multi-armed bandit problems where cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors introduce the concept of multi-fidelity bandits, where each arm has multiple fidelities that serve as approximations to the desired outcome. They develop a new algorithm, MF-UCB, which adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategy to the multi-fidelity setting.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm, including a regret bound and a lower bound. The regret bound shows that MF-UCB achieves better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations. The lower bound demonstrates that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper also presents simulation results that compare MF-UCB with UCB on synthetic problems. The results show that MF-UCB outperforms UCB on all problems, with a smaller gradient of the curve, corroborating the theoretical insights.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits by introducing a new framework and algorithm that can effectively handle multi-fidelity evaluations.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in multi-armed bandit problems with multi-fidelity evaluations.
* The MF-UCB algorithm is well-motivated and effectively adapts the classical UCB strategy to the multi-fidelity setting.
* The analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm is thorough, including a regret bound and a lower bound.
* The simulation results provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of MF-UCB.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the lower fidelities are more attractive than higher fidelities in terms of cost, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm relies on certain assumptions about the distribution of the rewards, which may not hold in all scenarios.
* The paper does not provide a clear discussion of the computational complexity of the MF-UCB algorithm.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Consider relaxing the assumption that the lower fidelities are more attractive than higher fidelities in terms of cost.
* Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the MF-UCB algorithm.
* Consider exploring other applications of the multi-fidelity bandit framework, such as clinical trials or robotics.

Originality and significance:

* The paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in multi-armed bandit problems with multi-fidelity evaluations.
* The MF-UCB algorithm is a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm, including a regret bound and a lower bound.

Clarity and presentation:

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* The notation is clear and consistent throughout the paper.
* The figures and tables are well-presented and help to illustrate the results.

Overall, I would rate this paper as 8/10. It makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits and provides a thorough analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm. However, there are some minor weaknesses and suggestions for improvement that could be addressed in future work.
