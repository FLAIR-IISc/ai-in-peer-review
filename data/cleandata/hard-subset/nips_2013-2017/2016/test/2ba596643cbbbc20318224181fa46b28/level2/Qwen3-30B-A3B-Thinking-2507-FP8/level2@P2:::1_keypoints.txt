- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit problem and proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively selects fidelities with theoretical regret guarantees.  
- Well-motivated by applications (online advertising, algorithm selection) where high-fidelity evaluations are costly and cheaper approximations exist.  
- Main strengths: clear formalization, novel algorithm design, and strong theoretical results.  
- Regret analysis is technically sound; upper bound shows MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when lower fidelities are much cheaper.  
- A complementary lower bound indicates MF-UCB is nearly optimal for many arms.  
- Good positioning within related work, distinguishing from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Paper clarity is generally good: well-structured presentation and clear explanation of technical innovations.  
- Regret definition appropriately accounts for fidelity costs and is thoughtfully motivated.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data support the theoretical claims; MF-UCB outperforms UCB in those settings.  
- Major concern: assumption that ζ(m) (bias bounds between fidelities) are known is strong and unrealistic; need discussion of estimating ζ(m) and its effect on performance and guarantees.  
- The gap between upper and lower bounds for arms in K(m)7 is not fully explained or quantified; authors should clarify its practical significance and whether it can be closed.  
- Empirical validation is limited to synthetic data; paper would benefit from at least one real-world experiment (e.g., ad-campaign optimization).  
- Minor point: the choice of qt in the regret definition is important and differs from alternatives (qt = µ(m)t); the paper could further elaborate on how this compares to other cost-sensitive bandit formulations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address ζ(m) estimation, clarify the bound gap, and add an empirical example.
