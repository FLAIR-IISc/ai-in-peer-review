- Problem: Introduces stochastic multi-fidelity K-armed bandits with cheaper biased lower-fidelity feedback and a cost-weighted pseudo-regret under budget Λ.  
- Regret benchmark: Reward always measured w.r.t. highest-fidelity mean µ(M), regardless of fidelity played.  
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB using bias-corrected upper confidence bounds and adaptive fidelity switching via thresholds γ(m).  
- Bias correction: UCBs are corrected by known fidelity bias bounds ζ(m) to account for systematic approximation error.  
- Fidelity selection: γ(m) thresholds trade off cost and informativeness based on cost ratios and concentration ψ.  
- Upper-bound theory: Proves regret upper bound concentrating expensive plays on a small “hard” subset of arms near-optimal at lower fidelities.  
- Lower-bound theory: Gives a Bernoulli lower bound matching the upper bound up to a gap on a specific subset, highlighting non-optimality of the switch criterion on those instances.  
- Assumptions: Analysis requires known bias bounds ζ(m), concentration function ψ, and Assumption 1 enforcing fast decay of ζ across fidelities.  
- Novelty: Formalization and MF-UCB design (bias-corrected UCB + cost-aware switching) are novel relative to budgeted bandits and GP multifidelity work.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, practically relevant formulation (e.g., ads, sim-to-real) with a clear, simple algorithmic idea.  
- Strength: Solid, explicit theoretical analysis with clean partition-based regret decomposition and explained gaps.  
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments support improved regret scaling and show fidelity usage patterns consistent with theory.  
- Weakness: Relies critically on tight, known ζ(m); real applications may have unknown or arm-specific biases and robustness is unanalyzed.  
- Weakness: Dependence on ψ and γ(m); sensitivity to misspecified ψ, heavy tails, or tuning choices is not studied.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 may be stronger than necessary; unclear behavior when fidelities are dense or redundant.  
- Weakness: Lower bound limited to Bernoulli rewards and uses Assumption 3 to exclude pathological policies; general sub-Gaussian extension is missing.  
- Weakness: Experiments limited to synthetic settings and only compare to standard UCB; lacks multifidelity baselines and real or semi-synthetic tasks.  
- Regret definition concern: Competing against Λµ* is reasonable but other objectives (fidelity-specific utilities) are not explored.  
- Quality: Proofs are technically sound under stated assumptions, though some constants are hidden and could hinder reproducibility.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written; suggests adding the algorithm box in main text, restating assumptions near theorems, and more intuition for γ(m).  
- Significance: Addresses an important setting and likely to spur follow-up work on unknown-bias learning and robust variants.  
- Suggested improvements: Analyze robustness to ζ misspecification, provide data-driven skipping of redundant fidelities, extend to heavy-tailed rewards, broaden lower-bound scope, and expand experiments and baselines.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance given novelty and theory, conditional on stronger empirical validation and discussion of robustness to unknown ζ/ψ.
