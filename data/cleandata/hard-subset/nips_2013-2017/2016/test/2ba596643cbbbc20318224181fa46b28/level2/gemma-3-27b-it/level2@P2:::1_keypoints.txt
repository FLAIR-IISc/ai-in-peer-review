- Introduces a practical multi-fidelity bandit framework with varying reward costs and cheaper biased approximations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a sensible UCB-based algorithm that uses cheap fidelities to prune suboptimal arms.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including a regret bound and a lower bound showing near-optimality under conditions.  
- Well-motivated applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) and clear connection to UCB literature.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB improves over standard UCB in controlled settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written and well organized.  
- Theoretical results rely on potentially restrictive assumptions (notably Assumption 1 on ζ(m) decay) whose practical validity isn’t fully explored.  
- There is a gap between the algorithm’s performance and the provided lower bound for certain cases, indicating room for improvement.  
- The chosen form of γ(m) (Eq. 4) is justified only heuristically and needs stronger derivation or explanation.  
- Simulations are limited in scope; they should cover more cost structures, reward distributions, and sensitivity to ρ.  
- Related work discussion could better incorporate active learning and Bayesian optimization multi-fidelity methods (e.g., GP-based approaches).  
- Suggest providing examples and sensitivity analysis for when Assumption 1 fails to assess practical impact.  
- Recommend investigating/tightening the lower-bound gap or designing algorithms to close it.  
- Advise a more detailed derivation or a more general formulation and trade-off discussion for γ(m).  
- Recommend expanding experiments to varied scenarios (highly variable costs, non-Bernoulli rewards, different ρ values).  
- Suggest expanding discussion and analysis of alternative choices for qt.  
- Minor notation improvements recommended (e.g., define 'Kg' earlier).  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, sensible algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and promising initial experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, lower-bound gap, and limited experimental evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; accept with minor revisions to address Assumption 1, γ(m) justification, and expanded experiments.
