- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where cheap approximations supplement costly rewards.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that uses lower-fidelity estimates to eliminate suboptimal arms early.  
- Clear problem formulation motivated by applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Theoretical analysis provides a regret bound showing MF-UCB adapts to available approximations.  
- Presents a lower bound indicating near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations corroborate theoretical gains over standard UCB.  
- Assumption 1 on the decay of ζ(m) is somewhat restrictive and may limit applicability.  
- Implications of violating the decay assumption are not fully explored.  
- Regret definition and the choice of qt are discussed only briefly and need expansion.  
- A gap remains between the MF-UCB bound and the lower bound for certain arms and warrants further investigation.  
- Some notational inconsistencies (e.g., T(m)k,t vs. T(>m)k,t) should be cleaned up.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address assumptions, lower-bound gap, and notation.
