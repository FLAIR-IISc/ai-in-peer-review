- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework for exploration–exploitation when cheaper approximations exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an UCB-based algorithm that uses low-fidelity plays to eliminate bad arms and reserves high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and well-organized technical sections.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and a lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal.  
- Shows MF-UCB achieves better regret than strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information.  
- Includes synthetic simulations illustrating MF-UCB’s performance versus UCB.  
- Main weakness: assumes lower fidelities provide unbiased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may be unrealistic.  
- Lacks guidance on choosing algorithm parameters (e.g., threshold γ(m)).  
- Empirical evaluation limited to synthetic problems; needs real-world experiments.  
- Recommendation: provide parameter-selection guidance, relax unbiasedness assumption, and add real-world simulations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, well-motivated algorithm, thorough analysis and near-optimality proof.  
- Arguments for rejection: unrealistic unbiasedness assumption, unclear parameter choices, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; rating 8/10 (confidence 9/10).
