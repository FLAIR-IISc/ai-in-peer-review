- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms have cheaper approximate fidelities of the desired experiment.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an adaptation of the UCB algorithm designed for multi-fidelity evaluations.  
- Provides a regret bound showing MF-UCB outperforms naive strategies that ignore approximations.  
- Derives a lower bound indicating MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Simulation results on synthetic problems show MF-UCB consistently outperforms standard UCB.  
- Strength: novel framework addressing exploration-exploitation with multi-fidelity evaluations.  
- Strength: MF-UCB is well-motivated and effectively adapts classical UCB to the new setting.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis including both upper (regret) and lower bounds.  
- Strength: Empirical results strongly support the theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: Assumes lower fidelities are cheaper than higher fidelities, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Analysis depends on specific reward-distribution assumptions that may be restrictive.  
- Weakness: Lacks a clear discussion of the computational complexity of MF-UCB.  
- Suggestion: Relax the cost-ordering assumption across fidelities in future work.  
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed computational-complexity analysis of MF-UCB.  
- Suggestion: Explore additional applications (e.g., clinical trials, robotics) for the multi-fidelity framework.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear notation, and helpful figures/tables.  
- Overall assessment: Significant contribution; rates 8/10 with minor weaknesses to address.
