- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem with cost-aware regret and bounded fidelity bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that selects both arms and fidelity levels by checking confidence intervals from cheap to expensive.  
- Provides an upper-bound regret analysis showing cheap fidelities eliminate many suboptimal arms, saving high-fidelity budget.  
- Derives a matching lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Paper is technically strong, with full proofs and principled algorithm design grounded in UCB literature.  
- Regret analysis is thorough and highlights that many arms incur lower-fidelity costs λ(m) rather than λ(M).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data, though brief, supports theoretical advantages of MF-UCB over standard UCB.  
- Concerns about assumptions: fast decay of bias bounds ζ(m) and the need to know ζ(m) a priori may be strong or unrealistic.  
- Suggests elaborating robustness or estimation methods when ζ(m) is unknown and relaxing Assumption 1.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and effectively motivated with practical examples.  
- Figures are helpful; analytical sections are dense and could use a concrete step-by-step example for arm partitioning K(m).  
- Recommends more upfront intuition for the role of γ(m) as a “switching threshold.”  
- The problem formulation and fidelity selection mechanism are highly original and non-trivial.  
- Contributions are significant for real-world settings (hyperparameter tuning, simulations vs. real tests) and likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel practical problem, elegant algorithm with strong theory, near-matching lower bound, clear writing.  
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on potentially strong assumptions about known and fast-decaying bias bounds.
