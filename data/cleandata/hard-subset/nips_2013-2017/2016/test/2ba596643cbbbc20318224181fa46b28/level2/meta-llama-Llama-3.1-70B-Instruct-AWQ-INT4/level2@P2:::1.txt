The paper "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit" introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs when cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, based on upper confidence bound techniques, which uses cheap lower fidelity plays to eliminate several bad arms and reserves expensive high fidelity queries for a small set of arms with high expected reward.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their ideas. The introduction effectively sets up the problem and provides a good overview of the related work. The technical sections are also well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their algorithm.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of the MF-UCB algorithm, which is shown to achieve better regret than strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information. The authors also provide a lower bound, which demonstrates that MF-UCB is near-optimal. The paper includes some simulations on synthetic problems, which illustrate the performance of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors introduce a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs with multi-fidelity evaluations.
* The MF-UCB algorithm is well-motivated and effectively uses cheap lower fidelity plays to eliminate bad arms and reserve expensive high fidelity queries for promising arms.
* The analysis of the algorithm is thorough, and the authors provide a lower bound that demonstrates the near-optimality of MF-UCB.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes that the lower fidelities provide unbiased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the parameters of the algorithm, such as the threshold γ(m).
* The simulations are limited to synthetic problems, and it would be useful to see the performance of MF-UCB on real-world problems.

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The authors effectively introduce a novel framework and develop an algorithm that achieves better regret than existing strategies.
