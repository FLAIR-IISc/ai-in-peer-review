- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelities with increasing costs and decreasing known bias bounds, objective is cost-aware regret minimization under budget Λ.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, extending UCB to choose both arms and fidelity levels, using cheap low-fidelity evaluations to prune arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for ambiguous arms.  
- MF-UCB maintains per-arm per-fidelity confidence bounds and a principled fidelity-switching rule based on reduced uncertainty at lower fidelities.  
- Provides an upper-regret bound showing MF-UCB leverages fidelity structure to outperform naive high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Derives a problem-specific lower bound and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal for arms not misleading at low fidelities.  
- Theoretical claims are supported by simulations on synthetic problems.  
- Distinct from prior budgeted bandits and general multi-fidelity optimization; related to active learning with weak/strong labelers but formalized within bandits.  
- High technical quality: sound, well-motivated formulation and careful, honest analysis (including discussion of gaps between bounds).  
- Main practical caveat: assumes known bias bounds ζ(m), which may be unrealistic and would require estimation in practice.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization: motivating examples, consistent notation, helpful figures, and proof sketches with full details in the appendix.  
- Original contribution: novel problem formulation and a non-trivial, tailored algorithmic and theoretical treatment of fidelity selection within UCB.  
- Significant impact: first formal regret analysis for this setting, likely useful across domains (AutoML, experiments, robotics) and to inspire future work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
