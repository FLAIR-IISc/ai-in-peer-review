- Summary: Proposes multi-fidelity bandits and MF-UCB, using cheap low-fidelity plays to eliminate bad arms and reserving expensive high-fidelity queries; provides regret bound and compares to a lower bound.

- Strength 1: Introduces a novel, practically relevant multi-fidelity bandit framework.

- Strength 2: MF-UCB is well-motivated, easy to understand, and theoretically characterized.

- Strength 3: Regret bound for MF-UCB is competitive with the provided lower bound, showing near-optimality.

- Strength 4: Simulations on synthetic problems demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.

- Weakness 1: Assumes lower fidelities are unbiased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may be unrealistic.

- Weakness 2: Choice of γ(m) in MF-UCB is insufficiently justified and its impact is underexplored.

- Weakness 3: Lower bound is proven only for a specific set of Bernoulli distributions, so generality is unclear.

- Weakness 4: Lacks thorough comparison with other methods (e.g., variance-based algorithms or Thompson sampling).

- Originality: Novel framework and algorithm; regret and lower bounds are original contributions.

- Significance: Potentially significant for applications (online advertising, finance, robotics) with cheaper approximations.

- Clarity: Well-written, consistent notation, and helpful figures/tables.

- Quality: Technically sound with proofs provided in the appendix.

- Pro arguments: Novel framework, well-motivated MF-UCB with theoretical guarantees, and original regret/lower bound analysis.

- Con arguments: Unchecked unbiasedness assumption for lower fidelities, unclear choice and role of γ(m), and limited lower-bound generality.

- Recommendation: Recommend accept, with revisions to justify γ(m), assess biased fidelities, and provide broader algorithm comparisons.
