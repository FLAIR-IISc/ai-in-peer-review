- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm has multiple biased, cost-differing fidelities.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive UCB algorithm that selects fidelity levels to minimize regret.  
- Provides a formal regret analysis showing MF-UCB outperforms naive UCB when lower fidelities are sufficiently cheap and informative.  
- Proves an upper bound for MF-UCB and a complementary lower bound (near-optimality under certain conditions).  
- Distinguishes from prior multi-fidelity and budgeted bandit work by offering formal regret guarantees for the bandit setting.  
- Uses a partitioning of arms into K(m) sets as a key theoretical insight guiding fidelity switching.  
- Empirical validation via synthetic experiments aligns with theoretical predictions.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible with a detailed appendix.  
- Main limitation: assumes known biases ζ(m) and relies on Assumption 1 (fast decay of ζ(m)), which may not hold in practice.  
- Does not address how to estimate ζ(m) from data, limiting practical applicability.  
- Experiments are synthetic only; lacks real-world validation.  
- No discussion of computational complexity for large-scale problems.  
- Strong contribution to theory and practice with clear significance for expensive high-fidelity evaluation domains.  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality theoretical contribution, well-written, and practically motivated despite noted limitations.
