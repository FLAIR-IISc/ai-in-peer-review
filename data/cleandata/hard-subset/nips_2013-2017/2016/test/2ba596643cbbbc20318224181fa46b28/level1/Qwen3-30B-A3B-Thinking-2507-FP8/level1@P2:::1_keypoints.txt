- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms have multiple fidelities with costs λ(m) and biased estimates of true reward.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses cheap low fidelities to eliminate arms and high fidelities for promising arms.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: regret bound (Theorem 2) showing improvement over naive UCB.
- Derives a matching lower bound (Theorem 4) demonstrating near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Carefully accounts for fidelity biases and justifies parameter choices (e.g., γ(m)); appendix contains technical details for reproducibility.
- Synthetic experiments validate theory: MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB across scenarios.
- Novel contribution: formalizes regret in the multi-fidelity bandit setting, distinct from budgeted bandits and prior multi-fidelity optimization work.
- High practical significance for domains like online advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics where cheaper approximations exist.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation, with helpful illustrations (e.g., K(m) partition) and a clear algorithm description.
- Weakness: gap between MF-UCB and the lower bound for arms in K(m)7; practical frequency of this case not thoroughly discussed.
- Weakness: assumes known fidelity biases ζ(m), which may be unrealistic and is not explored experimentally.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation limited to synthetic problems; no real-world case study included.
- Pro-acceptance: novel, practically relevant extension of bandit theory with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Con-acceptance: unsatisfying lower-bound gap for some arms, reliance on known ζ(m), and lack of real-world validation.
