- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit setting where each arm can be pulled at multiple fidelities with cheaper but biased estimates.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm tailored to exploit low-fidelity cheap biased observations.  
- Formalizes bias ζ(m) and cost λ(m) to distinguish biased lower fidelities from high-fidelity observations.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: upper bound on MF-UCB regret and a matching lower bound.  
- Regret analysis partitions arms into sets K(m) by fidelity to show how lower fidelities eliminate suboptimal arms.  
- Empirically demonstrates MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems with regret curves matching theory.  
- Practical motivations include online advertising and algorithm selection where cheap approximations exist.  
- Careful related work placement, distinguishing from budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, and active learning with weak labelers.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with clear proof sketches and intuition for algorithm behavior.  
- Strength: convincing practical motivation and illustrative examples (e.g., ad short displays).  
- Strength: empirical validation showing fidelity usage patterns and improved scaling.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical implementation issues, e.g., estimating ζ(m) or robustness to violated bias assumptions.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (fast decay of ζ(m)) may be restrictive and limits scope of theoretical comparison to UCB.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: strong, meaningful advance in bandit theory with clear applications; reviewer recommends acceptance.
