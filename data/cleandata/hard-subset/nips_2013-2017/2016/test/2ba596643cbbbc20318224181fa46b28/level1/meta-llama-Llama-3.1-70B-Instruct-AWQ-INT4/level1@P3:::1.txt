**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in multi-armed bandit problems when cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors introduce the concept of multi-fidelity bandits, where each arm has multiple fidelities that serve as approximations to the desired outcome. They develop a new algorithm, MF-UCB, based on upper confidence bound techniques, which uses the cheap lower fidelity plays to eliminate several bad arms and reserves the expensive high fidelity queries for a small set of arms with high expected reward. The authors provide a regret bound for MF-UCB and show that it is near-optimal by comparing it to a lower bound.

**Strengths**

1. The paper introduces a novel framework for studying multi-fidelity bandits, which is relevant to many practical applications.
2. The MF-UCB algorithm is well-motivated and easy to understand, and its performance is theoretically characterized.
3. The regret bound for MF-UCB is competitive with the lower bound, demonstrating its near-optimality.
4. The paper includes simulations on synthetic problems, which demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the lower fidelities are unbiased estimates of the highest fidelity, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The choice of γ(m) in the MF-UCB algorithm is not thoroughly justified, and its impact on the algorithm's performance is not fully explored.
3. The lower bound is only proven for a specific set of Bernoulli reward distributions, and it is unclear whether it holds for more general distributions.
4. The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with other existing algorithms, such as those that use variance estimates or Thompson sampling.

**Originality**

The paper introduces a new framework for studying multi-fidelity bandits, which is a significant contribution to the field. The MF-UCB algorithm is also novel and builds upon existing upper confidence bound techniques. The regret bound and lower bound are original contributions that demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF-UCB algorithm.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for many practical applications, such as online advertising, finance, and robotics, where cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The MF-UCB algorithm provides a new approach to balancing exploration and exploitation in these settings, and its near-optimality makes it a promising solution.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the MF-UCB algorithm and its theoretical characterization. The notation is consistent, and the figures and tables are helpful in illustrating the concepts.

**Quality**
