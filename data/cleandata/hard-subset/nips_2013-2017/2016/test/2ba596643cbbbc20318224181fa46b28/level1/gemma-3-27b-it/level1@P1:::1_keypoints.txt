- Introduces a multi-fidelity variant of K-armed bandits where cheaper approximations to costly evaluations are available.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB with a fidelity-switching rule based on confidence interval widths.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with regret bounds showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in the tested settings.  
- Positions the work relative to prior multi-armed bandit and multi-fidelity literature and fills a gap in bandit-specific multi-fidelity analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents the algorithm concisely.  
- Novel and relevant problem motivated by real applications (online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- The assumption that lower-fidelity means are within known ζ(m) of high-fidelity means may be limiting and sensitivity to misestimation is not analyzed.  
- Regularity condition (decay rate of ζ(m)) is not fully justified and its impact (including possible cases where MF-UCB underperforms UCB) is unclear.  
- There is a gap between the provided upper bounds and the derived lower bound; explanation and remedies are insufficient.  
- Simulations are limited in scope; more experiments across different λ, K, ζ, and reward distributions are needed.  
- The chosen regret definition (qt = µ(M)It) is somewhat restrictive and alternative definitions are not explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, rigorous theory, supportive empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, upper/lower bound gap, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommendation: Strong Accept (with suggested further analysis and expanded simulations).
