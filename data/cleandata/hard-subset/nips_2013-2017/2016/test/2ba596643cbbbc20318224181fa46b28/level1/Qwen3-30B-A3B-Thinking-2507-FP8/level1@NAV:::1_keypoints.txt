- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can be observed at M fidelities with increasing costs and biased lower-fidelity estimates.  
- Formalizes the setting with fidelity costs λ(1) < ... < λ(M) and biased observations, extending classical K-armed bandits.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting UCB to use low fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and high fidelities for promising ones.  
- Provides a regret bound O(∑_k∈K(m) Δ^(M)_k λ^(m)/ψ(Δ^(m)_k)) showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB under certain conditions.  
- Supplies a lower bound analysis demonstrating near-optimality of the proposed algorithm.  
- Builds on classic bandit literature and multi-fidelity optimization, but contributes a novel regret formalization and principled algorithm.  
- Theoretical analysis carefully addresses bias–cost trade-offs and is technically thorough.  
- Empirical results confirm theoretical advantages of MF-UCB.  
- Strengths: clear and relevant problem formulation, elegant algorithm design, strong theoretical support, and good writing/organization.  
- Weakness: a minor gap in the lower bound for arms where lower fidelities misleadingly suggest optimality (K^(m)_7), arising from algorithm design choices.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
