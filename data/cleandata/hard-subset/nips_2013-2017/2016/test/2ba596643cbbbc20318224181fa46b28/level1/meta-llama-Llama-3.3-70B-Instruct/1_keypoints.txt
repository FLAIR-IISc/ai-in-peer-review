- Proposes a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit framework where each arm has cheaper, biased lower-fidelity approximations.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting classical UCB to exploit multi-fidelity information.  
- Provides a regret bound showing MF-UCB outperforms naive UCB that ignores fidelity structure.  
- Gives a lower bound showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal, though the lower bound is not tight for all arms.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement, organized technical sections, and thorough proofs.  
- Strength: introduces a novel problem setting with broad applications (online advertising, finance, robotics).  
- Strength: presents a new algorithm with theoretical guarantees (regret and lower bound).  
- Weakness: assumes biases of lower fidelities are bounded and known, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental evaluation on real-world problems to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion that authors address the noted assumptions, experiments, and tightness issues in future work.
