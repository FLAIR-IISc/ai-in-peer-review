- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms can be queried at multiple fidelities that are cheaper but biased approximations of true reward.  
- Formalizes the problem using a cost-weighted regret metric.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm, an adaptation of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- MF-UCB leverages cheap, low-fidelity pulls to quickly eliminate poor arms and reserves expensive, high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.  
- Situates the work relative to existing literature, distinguishing it from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Novel and practically relevant formulation for domains like A/B testing and hyperparameter tuning.  
- MF-UCB is an elegant, intuitive algorithmic approach.  
- Provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis with an upper regret bound for MF-UCB.  
- Supplies a problem-specific lower bound, showing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Synthetic experiments, though simple, effectively corroborate theoretical insights and show MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB.  
- Minor clarity issue: the partitioning of arms into K(m) could use more intuition in the main text.  
- Authors acknowledge a gap between upper and lower bounds for a subset of arms (K(m)7); a deeper discussion on whether this gap is analytic or fundamental is recommended.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant contribution; strong recommendation for acceptance.
