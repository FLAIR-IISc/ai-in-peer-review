- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) formulation and the MF-UCB algorithm for using cheaper approximations.  
- Provides rigorous regret analysis (Theorem 2) and a matching lower bound (Theorem 4) showing near-optimality.  
- Technical approach builds on standard UCB techniques and is mathematically sound; upper and lower bounds increase confidence.  
- Key assumption on ζ(m) is clearly stated, but the impact of violating Assumption 1 is not fully explored.  
- Proofs are rigorous but placed in the appendix, making core technical details harder to assess quickly.  
- Simulations support the theoretical results and demonstrate practical behavior.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized; motivation and algorithm description are strong.  
- Notation with multiple indices (m, k, t, s) can become cumbersome and reduce readability.  
- The jump between equations (1) and (5) is abrupt; a brief explanation linking regret to cost is needed.  
- The intuition behind the lower bound is insufficiently explained and should be clarified.  
- Novelty: the MF-MAB formulation and adaptation of UCB to multi-fidelity settings is a significant contribution.  
- Clever treatment of biased low-fidelity estimates via the ζ(m) parameter differentiates the work.  
- Strong practical relevance for domains like online advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical foundation, practical relevance, clear presentation, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: proof complexity residing in appendix, limited discussion of assumption violations, and weak intuition for the lower bound.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity of the lower bound and provide a high-level overview of core proofs.
