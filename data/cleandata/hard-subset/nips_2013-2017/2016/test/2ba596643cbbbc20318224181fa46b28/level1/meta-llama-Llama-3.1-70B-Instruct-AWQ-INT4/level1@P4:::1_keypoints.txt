- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, which uses cheap low-fidelity plays to eliminate bad arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and helpful figures/tables.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review that situates the contribution.  
- Technically strong: rigorous analysis with regret bound and a lower bound.  
- Proofs are well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Balances technical rigor with intuitive explanations; accessible to a broad audience.  
- High originality: novel algorithm and analysis with potential to influence future work.  
- Significant potential impact across domains (advertising, finance, healthcare); effectiveness shown in simulations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, MF-UCB algorithm, rigorous theory, empirical demonstrations, broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: assumes unbiased lower fidelities which may be unrealistic; lacks guidance for choosing parameters (e.g., ρ); no real-world dataset evaluation.  
- Suggestions for improvement: provide parameter-selection guidance, evaluate on real-world datasets, consider relaxing unbiasedness assumption, and expand related-work discussion.  
- Overall strong recommendation for NIPS acceptance.
