- Introduces a multi-fidelity extension of the K-armed bandit problem, motivated by costly evaluations with cheaper approximations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that adaptively selects fidelity levels.  
- Provides rigorous regret analysis showing near-optimality and adaptation to fidelity costs and approximation quality.  
- Differentiates from prior multi-fidelity and budgeted bandit works and generalizes beyond GP assumptions.  
- Empirical simulations support theoretical claims and show improvements over naive UCB.  
- Well-written and clearly organized presentation.  
- Assumption 1 (decay of ζ(m)) is somewhat restrictive and effects of violating it are underexplored.  
- There is a gap between the achieved regret bound and the lower bound in some scenarios, especially with suboptimal fidelity switching.  
- Simulation study is limited; more varied experiments and comparisons to other bandit algorithms are needed.  
- Discussion of alternative choices for qt is brief and could be expanded for more insight.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, strong theoretical guarantees, intuitive algorithm, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumption, lower-bound gap, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution and recommended for acceptance.
