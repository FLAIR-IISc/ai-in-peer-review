This paper introduces the concept of multi-fidelity bandits, where each arm can be played at multiple fidelities, each with a different cost and accuracy. The authors propose a novel algorithm, MF-UCB, which adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) algorithm to this multi-fidelity setting. They prove that MF-UCB achieves better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations, and provide a lower bound that shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance, including a regret bound and a lower bound. The paper also includes simulations that demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a new and interesting problem setting, which is relevant to many real-world applications.
* The proposed algorithm, MF-UCB, is well-motivated and easy to understand.
* The analysis of the algorithm's performance is thorough and well-done, including a regret bound and a lower bound.
* The simulations provide a clear demonstration of the effectiveness of MF-UCB compared to UCB.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the cost of playing each arm at each fidelity is known, which may not be the case in practice.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the parameter ρ, which is used in the algorithm.
* The simulations are limited to a few specific problem instances, and it is not clear how well MF-UCB will perform on other problems.
