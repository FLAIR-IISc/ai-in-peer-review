- Introduces a new multi-fidelity MAB framework and MF-UCB algorithm that leverages cheaper approximations and shows improved regret with a near-optimal lower bound.  
- Extends UCB to handle multiple fidelities, drawing from multi-fidelity optimization and RL work.  
- Novel framework for studying exploration–exploitation with multiple fidelity levels.  
- Theoretical guarantees: regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching-ish lower bound.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems.  
- Relies on assumptions about the decay rate ζ(m) and concentration inequalities for fidelity distributions.  
- Applicability is limited: framework/algorithm are tailored to multi-fidelity MABs and may not generalize broadly.  
- Open problem: gap remains between the lower bound and MF-UCB performance for certain arms.  
- Quality: technically sound, rigorous analysis, and well-organized presentation.  
- Clarity: well-written with adequate background for readers new to MABs and UCB.  
- Originality: significant novel contribution introducing multi-fidelity MAB framework and algorithm.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential applications in advertising, finance, and robotics.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, theoretical results, and empirical validation.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong assumptions and limited domain scope.  
- Recommendation: accept.
