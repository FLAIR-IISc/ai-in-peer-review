- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit problem where true rewards are costly but cheaper approximations exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm adapted to exploit lower-fidelity information.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees, including a regret bound and a lower bound showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Problem formulation and motivation are clear and practically relevant (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- MF-UCB is a logical extension of UCB that leverages cheaper fidelities to reduce cost.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous: careful regret definition accounting for play costs and fidelity biases.  
- Connection between MF-UCB regret and standard UCB regret is clearly articulated.  
- Inclusion of a lower bound strengthens the claim of near-optimality.  
- Assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1) are clearly explained and their implications discussed.  
- Simulations support theoretical findings and show MF-UCB outperforms naive UCB.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and should include more diverse settings and baselines.  
- Choice of γ(m) appears ad-hoc and requires better justification or exploration of alternatives.  
- Notation is occasionally dense and some equations need clearer spacing or explanations.  
- Related work coverage is incomplete; could include Bayesian optimization and active learning literature.  
- Recommend expanding discussion of future work and potential applications beyond the examples given.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and theoretically sound paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
