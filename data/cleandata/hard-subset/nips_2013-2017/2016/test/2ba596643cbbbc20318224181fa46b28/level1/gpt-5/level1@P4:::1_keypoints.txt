- Introduces a stochastic K-armed multi-fidelity bandit where M fidelities give biased, cheaper estimates of the highest-fidelity reward with known bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m).  
- Defines a capital-based pseudo-regret comparing total highest-fidelity reward per unit capital to an oracle that always plays an optimal arm.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: maintain fidelity-specific UCBs for the highest-fidelity mean (X̄(m)+conf.+ζ(m)), take the min across fidelities for arm selection, and use thresholds γ(m) to decide fidelity upgrades.  
- Chooses thresholds γ(m)=ψ^{-1}((λ(m)/λ(m+1))ψ(ζ(m))) to balance informativeness and cost.  
- Proves an upper-regret bound: MF-UCB pays logarithmic exploration mainly at the lowest fidelity that can eliminate each arm, yielding speedups roughly λ(low)/λ(M) versus single-fidelity UCB.  
- Provides a lower bound (Bernoulli case, mild visitation assumption) showing near-optimality except for a subset of arms (the gap set K(m)) where conservative switching makes MF-UCB pay more.  
- Analysis is technically sound (UCB construction, arm partitioning, play-count control); requires ρ>4 and relies on Assumption 1; full proofs in appendix.  
- Key assumptions: known bias bounds ζ(m), known concentration ψ, and a regularity condition to avoid near-duplicate fidelities — reasonable but restrictive.  
- Empirics: synthetic simulations match theory, but no real-world experiments or extensive ablations in main text.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and organized; notation can be heavy and dependence on ζ/ψ should be emphasized earlier; residual regret term r̃ needs more exposition.  
- Originality: formalizes biased multi-fidelity bandits with capital-regret and a novel min-over-fidelities UCB with fidelity-adaptive switching; lower bound and gap characterization are original.  
- Significance: practically relevant to advertising, algorithm selection, sim-to-real transfer; shows concrete gains and could influence budgeted evaluation research.  
- Pros: well-motivated problem, simple implementable algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, clear intuition via partition analysis, supportive simulations.  
- Cons: requires known ζ/ψ and Assumption 1, lower bound limited to Bernoulli and a strategy class, MF-UCB can be suboptimal on K(m) arms, limited empirical breadth, and little guidance on tuning ρ or handling unknown ψ/ζ.  
- Suggested improvements: investigate online estimation/robustness for ζ and ψ, ablation/sensitivity studies for γ(m), compare to more baselines (Thompson, elimination methods), extend/tighten lower bound beyond Bernoulli, add real-world experiments.  
- Overall assessment: clear, technically solid, and novel contribution with strong theory and practical relevance despite some restrictive assumptions and limited empirical evaluation.
