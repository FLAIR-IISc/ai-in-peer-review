- Introduces a stochastic K-armed multi-fidelity bandit with M fidelity levels, known bias bounds ζ(m), per-pull costs λ(m), and a cost-weighted pseudo-regret measured against the highest fidelity.
- Proposes MF-UCB: maintain per-arm per-fidelity UCBs B_k^m,t = empirical mean + bonus + ζ(m), pick arm maximizing min_m B_k^m,t, and query the lowest fidelity whose uncertainty exceeds γ(m)=ψ^{-1}((λ(m)/λ(m+1))ψ(ζ(m))).
- Theorem 2 (upper bound): MF-UCB spends most effort on cheap fidelities to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves expensive pulls for a small near-optimal set, yielding provable cost savings (λ(J_k)/ψ(Δ) vs λ(M)/ψ(Δ)).
- Theorem 4 (lower bound for Bernoulli): matches MF-UCB on a broad subset of arms under an efficiency assumption, but identifies a gap class where MF-UCB may over-invest at high fidelities.
- Assumptions are explicit: sub-exponential concentration, known ζ(m), and a technical Assumption 1 to avoid near-duplicate fidelity behavior.
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show consistent improvements and expected “flatter” regret, with per-fidelity pull counts illustrating algorithm behavior.
- Experiments are limited: only synthetic cases, no comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., TS variants, hierarchical elimination) or real data.
- Proof sketches are convincing and full proofs are deferred to the appendix; the regret notion and its limitations are carefully motivated.
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, though fidelity partitioning and the role of r̃ should be made explicit earlier; notation is heavy but standard.
- The problem formulation and MF-UCB design are original for the K-armed multi-fidelity setting, and the tailored lower bound is novel.
- The model is significant for realistic scenarios with cheap biased proxies and offers meaningful gains when K is large and many arms can be cheaply eliminated.
- Major limitation: the algorithm requires known bias envelopes ζ(m) and lacks analysis of robustness to misspecification.
- Assumption 1’s practical relevance is unclear; the lower bound is restricted to Bernoulli rewards and excludes the identified gap class K_m^7.
- Suggested improvements: discuss practical ways to obtain or estimate ζ(m), perform sensitivity analyses for misspecified ζ or ψ, and consider adaptive calibration of ζ within MF-UCB.
- Suggested experiments: compare to additional baselines (TS-style, hierarchical elimination, cost-aware best-arm ID), and run ablations varying λ, ζ, and M to validate Assumption 1 and γ(m).
- Clarify guidance on choosing ρ and constants, state computational complexity per step (O(KM)), and discuss extensions that exploit cross-fidelity correlations instead of worst-case bias bounds.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance — solid theory, clear writing, novelty and likely influence — tempered by reliance on known ζ(m) and a modest empirical section.
