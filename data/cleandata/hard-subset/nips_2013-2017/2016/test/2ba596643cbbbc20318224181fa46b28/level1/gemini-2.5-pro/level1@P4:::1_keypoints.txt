- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: each arm has M fidelities with cost λ(m), known bias bounds ζ(m), and defines cost-aware pseudo-regret for budget Λ.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: maintains UCBs per fidelity, selects the arm with the tightest overall upper bound, and escalates fidelities from cheap to expensive based on uncertainty.  
- Theoretical upper bound: regret for a suboptimal arm scales with the cost of the fidelity that identifies it as suboptimal, not always the most expensive fidelity.  
- Provides a lower bound showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal for a broad class of instances.  
- Proof technique uses a clever partitioning of arms (K(m)) that clarifies algorithm behavior and supports the regret analysis.  
- Strength: rigorous, comprehensive theoretical analysis with both upper and lower bounds.  
- Strength: honest discussion of the gap between upper and lower bounds and its causes.  
- Weakness: relies on knowing bias bounds ζ(m); robustness to misspecification is not analyzed.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and explains algorithmic choices and thresholds clearly, with helpful figures.  
- Weakness: presentation is dense and full proof understanding requires careful reading of the appendix.  
- Originality: novel formalization of multi-fidelity evaluations within regret-minimization bandits and a non-trivial UCB extension.  
- Significance: addresses important practical cost–accuracy tradeoffs and likely to inspire extensions (e.g., contextual/linear settings).  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited to synthetic data; no real-world or benchmark application shown.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, principled algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and clear insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions (known ζ(m)) and limited empirical evaluation.
