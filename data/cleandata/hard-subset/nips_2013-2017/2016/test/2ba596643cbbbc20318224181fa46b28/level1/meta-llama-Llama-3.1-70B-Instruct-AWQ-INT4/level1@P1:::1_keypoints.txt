- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit setting where each arm can be played at multiple costs and accuracies.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for multi-fidelity choices.
- Proves MF-UCB achieves improved regret over naive single-fidelity strategies.
- Provides a lower bound showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with consistent notation.
- Thorough theoretical analysis including regret bounds and a matching lower bound.
- Simulations demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on tested instances.
- Assumes known costs for each fidelity, which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Lacks guidance on how to choose the algorithm parameter ρ.
- Simulations are limited to a few specific instances, leaving generalizability unclear.
- Novel problem setting and a new algorithmic adaptation contribute originality.
- The work is potentially significant and broadly applicable to real-world problems.
- Overall quality is high with solid analysis and empirical support.
- Recommendation: accept, despite limitations that do not outweigh contributions.
