- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit where each arm-pull has a fidelity m with error zeta_m and cost, proposes MF-UCB, gives a regret lower bound, and claims improvements over standard high-fidelity UCB.

- Addresses an interesting, relevant problem and provides a potentially useful benchmark; paper appears technically solid.

- Major concern: regret is defined with multiplicative cost, which may be non-general; an additive cost model might be more natural and should be analyzed.

- Major concern: Assumption 1 (constraints on zeta_m decay) seems artificial and lacks justification; unclear if one can select a subset of fidelities to satisfy it.

- Major concern: Assumption 1 appears to influence MF-UCB’s design and performance, but the paper does not justify focusing on those zeta_m configurations.

- Major concern: notable gap between upper and lower regret bounds, suggesting the algorithm may be suboptimal and could be redesigned for tighter bounds.

- Clarity/presentation: main theoretical results (Theorem 2) are overly technical and hard to interpret; paper should state the exact UCB baseline and give intuitive explanations of terms like Delta, [[k]], and the sequence of sets K^(m).

- Minor comments: wording/notation issues—hyphenate “near-optimal,” remove the extra period before “for all m < M,” and use curly braces for the definition of [[k]].
