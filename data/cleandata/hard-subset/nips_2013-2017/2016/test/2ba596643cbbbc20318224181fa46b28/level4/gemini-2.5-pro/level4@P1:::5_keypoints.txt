- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation where cheaper, biased reward approximations can be used to reduce evaluation cost.  
- Core idea: use low-fidelity evaluations to quickly eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, including a regret upper bound for MF-UCB.  
- Derives a problem-specific lower bound and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations on synthetic problems corroborate the theoretical claims and show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with helpful intuition and the arm-partitioning (K(m)) device aiding comprehension.  
- Honest discussion of a remaining gap between the upper and lower bounds for a subset of arms (K(m)7), flagged as an open problem.  
- Notes Assumption 1 on the decay rate ζ(m) simplifies analysis; suggests discussing how relaxing it would affect performance and bounds.  
- Overall assessment: significant, technically strong contribution that opens a promising research direction.
