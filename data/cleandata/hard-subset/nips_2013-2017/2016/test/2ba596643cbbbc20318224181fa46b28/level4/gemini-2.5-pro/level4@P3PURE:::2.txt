This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be pulled with varying levels of accuracy, or fidelity. In this framework, the cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost and the standard pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, to address this setting and provide corresponding upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.

The main paper is clear, well-written, and effectively presents the problem and algorithm. From a theoretical perspective, this work provides valuable insights into the multi-fidelity bandit problem, supported by interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, this is a significant and interesting contribution.

However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved:

1.  **Clarity of Theoretical Results:** The use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation in the main paper can be somewhat misleading, as these constants sometimes hide important algorithm parameters. For instance, in Theorem 2, a factor of `ρ` (a parameter of the algorithm) is absorbed into the constant. Using the exact inequalities, which are provided in the supplementary materials, directly in the main paper would significantly improve the clarity and precision of the theoretical results.

2.  **Practical Applicability:** It is not immediately clear how readily the MF-UCB algorithm can be applied to real-world problems. The core strategy of using low-accuracy pulls to eliminate highly suboptimal arms could be sensitive to inaccurate estimations of `ζ` (the fidelity accuracy parameter). The paper would benefit from a discussion of such practical limitations and potential strategies to overcome them.

3.  **Quality of Supplementary Materials:** While the main paper is clear, the same cannot be said for the supplementary materials, which contain the majority of the proofs. The appendix suffers from numerous typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. Addressing these issues would greatly increase the readability and overall quality of the paper. A non-exhaustive list of noted errors is provided below.
