- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where observations have fidelity-dependent costs.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that allocates exploration across fidelity levels to focus high-fidelity evaluations on promising arms.
- Provides regret analysis and a corresponding lower bound, claiming near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in the tested settings.
- Problem formulation is relevant and potentially impactful for real-world cost-sensitive decision problems.
- Notational presentation is disorganized; definitions are scattered and some variables (e.g., ‘s’) are poorly introduced.
- The online-advertising example is misleading and introduces tangential concepts (e.g., non-stationarity) without justification.
- The regret objective ignores cost-weighting and doesn’t address reward-per-unit-cost, making the analysis feel artificial.
- The paper lacks an explicit cost/sample complexity analysis quantifying high-fidelity sampling vs. overall cost.
- Writing style and phrasing are unwieldy and need substantial revision for clarity and concision.
- Suggest reframing motivating examples toward product development or clinical trials where fidelity hierarchy is natural.
- Recommend exploring a cost-aware regret definition that incorporates fidelity costs directly.
- Advise investigating cost complexity: how many high-fidelity samples are needed to reach specified confidence.
- Pros: novel extension, well-motivated algorithm, theoretical guarantees, and supportive empirical evidence.
- Cons: poor notation/organization, weak motivating example, questionable regret formulation, missing cost analysis, and writing issues.
- Overall: promising idea but paper is not ready—recommend rejection pending substantial revisions.
