- Problem: Introduces a multi-fidelity variant of stochastic K-armed bandits where cheap approximations to arms exist.  
- Novelty: Formalizes a novel and interesting multi-fidelity bandit setting.  
- Main contribution: Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for multi-fidelity problems.  
- Theory: Provides regret bounds and lower-bound analysis showing theoretical improvement and near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Experiments: Simulations demonstrate effectiveness and encouraging results of MF-UCB.  
- Presentation: Clear, concise, well-organized writing with good intuition for the algorithm.  
- Balance: Good balance between theoretical analysis and practical demonstrations.  
- Related work: Discusses related work and clearly highlights differences from existing approaches.  
- Applications: Mentions potential applications such as online advertising and clinical trials.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation paired with effective empirical validation.  
- Weakness: Assumes cheap approximations to arms are available, which may be unrealistic in some settings.  
- Weakness: Performance may degrade if approximations are insufficiently accurate.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper discussion of limitations and future research directions.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: Significant contribution, novelty, solid theory supported by simulations, and clear exposition.  
- Counterpoints against acceptance: Practical applicability concerns due to reliance on available/accurate approximations and limited discussion of limitations.
