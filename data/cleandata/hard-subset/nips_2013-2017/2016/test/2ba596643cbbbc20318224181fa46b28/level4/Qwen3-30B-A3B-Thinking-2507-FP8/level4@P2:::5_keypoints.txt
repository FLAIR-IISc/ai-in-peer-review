- Introduces a multi-fidelity K-armed bandit setting where each arm has M fidelities with increasing cost and decreasing bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper confidence bound algorithm that uses low-fidelity observations to eliminate suboptimal arms before high-fidelity pulls.  
- Proves upper regret bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when lower fidelities are substantially cheaper.  
- Provides a matching lower bound establishing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Theoretical analysis rigorously accounts for fidelity costs and bias; proofs are technically sound.  
- Experimental results on synthetic tasks corroborate theoretical improvements over standard UCB.  
- Builds on classical bandit literature and differs from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization by integrating exploration-exploitation trade-offs.  
- Extends prior multi-fidelity bandit work (e.g., Gaussian-process based) with a more general theoretical framework.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible; figures effectively illustrate concepts.  
- Novel contribution: formalizing multi-fidelity bandits and leveraging low-fidelity elimination is a clear and useful insight.  
- High potential significance for applications with expensive high-fidelity observations (online ads, algorithm selection, clinical trials).  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated problem, rigorous theory, solid algorithm design, supportive experiments, excellent presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: assumes known bias parameters ζ(m), relies on regularity Assumption 1, and experiments are limited to synthetic data.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—significant, well-executed contribution with minor limitations.
