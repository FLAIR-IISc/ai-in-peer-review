- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be pulled at multiple fidelities with cost increasing with accuracy.  
- Regret is defined as the product of pull cost and standard pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written; supplementary materials contain numerous typographical and minor technical errors that impair readability.  
- Excessive use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is potentially misleading because hidden algorithmic parameters (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2) are absorbed into constants; state exact inequalities instead.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: using low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms may fail if the fidelity parameter ζ is inaccurately estimated; recommend discussing limitations and mitigation strategies.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong: novel theorems and rigorous proofs constitute a significant advance.
