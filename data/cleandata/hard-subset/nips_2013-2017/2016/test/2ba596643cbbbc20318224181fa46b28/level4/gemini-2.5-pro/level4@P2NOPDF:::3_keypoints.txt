- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can be sampled at M fidelities with lower fidelities cheaper but biased estimates.  
- Models low fidelity as bounded bias (mean shift) rather than increased variance.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Theoretical analysis yields a complex regret bound that is hard to interpret.  
- Authors claim the bound is often much better than running UCB only at highest fidelity.  
- Primary contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a straightforward optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty adaptation.  
- Motivating examples (e.g., ad display duration, hyperparameter tuning) are plausible but not fully convincing or well-connected to the model.  
- Minimizing cumulative regret may not align with practical ML objectives like final model performance.  
- Empirical results show MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB by factors of 3–4 in simulations.  
- Experimental parameter choices (e.g., bias decay ζ^(m)) are given but not justified, raising concerns about result generality.  
- Paper would be strengthened by clearer intuition for the theoretical bound and by relating experimental parameters to realistic scenarios.
