- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with M fidelity levels where cheaper, lower-fidelity queries can prune arms and expensive, high-fidelity queries refine the best ones.  
- Motivated by practical settings (e.g., online advertising) where short/cheap tests proxy long-run performance.  
- Proposes a new cost-aware multi-fidelity regret notion, an algorithm with theoretical guarantees, a regret lower bound, and simulation results.  
- Analysis is novel and technically interesting.  
- Key assumption: the deviations ε^{(m)} between fidelity m means and true means are known a priori.  
- Major critique: need to clarify how ε^{(m)} are obtained in practice (domain knowledge, calibration, side data) and whether the algorithm can tolerate misspecification.  
- Suggest extending/robustifying the method to estimate ε^{(m)} online (e.g., via confidence bounds or adaptive calibration) and analyze the impact on guarantees.
