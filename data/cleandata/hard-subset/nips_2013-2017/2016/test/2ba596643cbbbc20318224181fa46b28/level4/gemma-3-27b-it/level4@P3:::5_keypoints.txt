- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit problem: cheaper noisy approximations available for costly arm evaluations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm designed to leverage multi-fidelity information.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound indicating near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations support MF-UCB’s effectiveness compared to naive UCB.  
- Well-motivated application scenarios (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) and clear connection to bandit literature.  
- Novel contribution that extends the traditional K-armed bandit framework to a practical setting.  
- Theoretical rigor is strong; matching lower bound strengthens optimality claims.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with generally understandable notation and algorithm description.  
- Thorough related-work discussion situating the paper within multi-fidelity optimization and active learning.  
- Analysis relies on assumptions (e.g., regularity of ζ(m), concentration bounds) whose violation and robustness are not fully explored.  
- Gap remains between the provided regret bound and the lower bound for certain arms (K(m)7), with limited discussion on closing it.  
- Intuition behind the choice of γ(m) is insufficiently explained; an example would help.  
- Simulation details (parameters, number of runs, statistical significance) are sparse and should be expanded.  
- Discussion of choosing qt (alternatives to µ(M)It) is abrupt and needs more exploration of implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically supported, and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions and unresolved regret-bound gap.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
