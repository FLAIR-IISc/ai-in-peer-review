- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where cheaper approximations to experiments are available.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses low-fidelity plays to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity queries for promising arms.
- Provides a thorough analysis of MF-UCB, including regret bounds compared to standard UCB.
- Shows MF-UCB can outperform UCB, especially when fidelity costs vary significantly.
- Presents a lower bound on regret, indicating MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.
- Clear, concise presentation and consistent notation throughout the paper.
- Good discussion of related work and connections to areas like online advertising and machine learning.
- Connection between the model and motivating applications is not entirely convincing and some examples seem contrived.
- The performance guarantee of MF-UCB is somewhat hard to interpret.
- Simulation results on synthetic problems are not thoroughly justified.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and potential real-world applications.
- Overall contribution is novel and valuable; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, well-motivated algorithm, strong analysis (regret and lower bound), good related work.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak application linkage, interpretability issues, inadequate simulation justification, limited practical discussion.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
