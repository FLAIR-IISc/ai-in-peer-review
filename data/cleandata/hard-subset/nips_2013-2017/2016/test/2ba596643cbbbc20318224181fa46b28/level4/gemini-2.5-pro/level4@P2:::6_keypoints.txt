- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms can be pulled at M fidelities trading cost for bias/noise, motivated by real-world cheap approximations (e.g., ads, algorithm selection).  
- Main contributions: formal problem definition with cost-aware regret, MF-UCB algorithm, regret upper bound and matching lower bound showing near-optimality, and simulations validating performance.  
- Strength: originality — novel, well-motivated problem that fills a gap in bandit literature and could inspire further work.  
- Strength: technical quality — comprehensive, sound theoretical analysis with problem-specific lower bound demonstrating near-optimality.  
- Strength: clarity — well-written, organized presentation; intuitive algorithm description and supportive empirical results.  
- Main weakness: reliance on knowing bias parameters ζ(m) a priori; practical feasibility of this assumption is unclear.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss how ζ(m) might be estimated or bounded in practice, analyze sensitivity to ζ(m) mis-specification, and consider relaxing assumption by learning biases online.
