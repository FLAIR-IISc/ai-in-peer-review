- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: each arm has M fidelity levels where lower fidelity is cheaper but incurs bias bounded by fidelity level.  
- Proposes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to compensate for bias from low-fidelity samples.  
- Provides theorems with complex regret bounds that are difficult to interpret but claims often substantially better regret than always using highest fidelity.  
- Simulation results (brief) support the claim of improved regret.  
- Initially skeptical about modeling low fidelity as bias rather than variance, but some motivating examples (e.g., algorithm selection) partly justify the choice.  
- Certain motivations are unconvincing: shorter ad displays may not induce bias, and regret minimization’s relevance in algorithm selection is unclear.  
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a standard optimism-based method, and the performance guarantees are hard to parse.  
- Simulation study lacks justification for chosen parameters (Appendix C) and it’s unclear if observed 3–4× improvement is typical or an artifact of parameter choices like ζ(m).
