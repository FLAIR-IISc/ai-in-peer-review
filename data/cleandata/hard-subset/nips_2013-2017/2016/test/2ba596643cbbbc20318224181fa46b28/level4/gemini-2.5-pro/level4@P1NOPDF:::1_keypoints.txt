- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic bandit model, MF-UCB algorithm, and a regret lower bound.  
- Problem is interesting and relevant as a benchmark for cost-quality trade-offs in online learning.  
- Primary weakness: unclear explanation of core assumptions and theoretical results.  
- Regret definition penalizes cost multiplicatively, which seems unnatural; authors should discuss additive-cost alternatives and implications.  
- Assumption 1 on fidelity errors (zeta_m) appears artificial and is not well-justified for general problems.  
- Unclear whether results extend when zeta_m do not satisfy the assumed decay; need to address selecting subsequences of fidelities.  
- Authors claim the assumption is noncritical, but it appears used in proofs, raising concern it may drive algorithm design and performance.  
- The algorithm seems tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification for their practical relevance.  
- Gap between the MF-UCB regret bound and the lower bound suggests the need for algorithmic refinement or tighter analysis.  
- Theorem 2 is hard to interpret; lacks an intuitive comparison to a baseline (e.g., standard UCB at highest fidelity).  
- Recommend more explicit, intuitive exposition of results, plus illustrative examples or simplified corollaries.  
- Minor edits: hyphenate "near-optimal"; remove unnecessary period before "for all m < M"; use curly braces for [[k]].
