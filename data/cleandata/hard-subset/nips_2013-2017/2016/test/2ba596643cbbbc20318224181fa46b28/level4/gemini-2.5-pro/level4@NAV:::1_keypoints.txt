- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity bandit model and proposes MF-UCB with a regret lower bound.
- Problem is interesting, relevant, and could serve as a benchmark for future work.
- Technical contributions appear sound but core assumptions and results lack sufficient justification and explanation.
- Regret definition is unnatural—penalizing cost multiplicatively is unclear; additive cost formulation seems more natural and should be analyzed.
- Assumption 1 on fidelity error decay (zeta parameters) appears artificial and lacks motivation; its necessity is unclear despite appearing in proofs.
- Authors claim Assumption 1 is non-essential, but the paper does not show how results change when it is violated or how to select a valid subset of fidelities.
- Algorithm seems tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification for their general relevance.
- Significant gap remains between the regret upper bound and the provided lower bound, suggesting possible need to redesign the strategy for optimality.
- Presentation of Theorem 2 is hard to interpret; comparison to standard UCB is ambiguous (is baseline UCB at highest fidelity?).
- Performance expressions use complex parameters (Delta, [[k]], sets K^(m)) without intuitive explanation of their effect.
- Overall clarity of theoretical results and assumptions is insufficient, obscuring the impact of contributions.
- Minor edits: hyphenate "near-optimal", remove unnecessary period before "for all m < M", and use standard curly-brace set notation for [[k]].
