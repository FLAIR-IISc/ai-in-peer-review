- Paper defines a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit where each arm has M fidelities; lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bias bounded by fidelity level.  
- Proposes a UCB-style algorithm with widened confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are provided but are cumbersome and difficult to interpret.  
- Authors claim the bounds often outperform always-sampling-at-highest-fidelity UCB; a short simulation section is offered as support.  
- Reviewer questioned modeling low fidelity as bias rather than increased variance, though some examples justify bias modeling.  
- The ad-duration example as a source of bias is unconvincing, and algorithm-selection’s regret objective is not clearly motivated.  
- Overall connection between model and motivating applications is weak, but not a fatal flaw.  
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm itself is a fairly standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.  
- Performance guarantees are hard to interpret and offer limited practical insight.  
- Synthetic experiments lack justification for parameter choices (Appendix C lists them but doesn’t justify).  
- Observed empirical improvements (3–4x) may be driven by specific parameterization (e.g., chosen ζ^(m)) rather than being generally representative.
