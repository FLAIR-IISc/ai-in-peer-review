- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit problem using low-cost approximations for arms.  
- Formalizes the setting with rigorous theoretical development.  
- Provides a tight regret upper bound that matches a derived lower bound.  
- Shows the method achieves optimal regret scaling and improves over standard bandits when using fidelity trade-offs.  
- Lower bound analysis is strong, confirming asymptotic optimality under the problem constraints.  
- Empirical simulations across synthetic and benchmark tasks validate theoretical claims.  
- Results consistently show reduced regret versus single-fidelity baselines, especially when low-fidelity evaluations are cheap.  
- Experimental design effectively isolates the impact of fidelity levels and presents clear results.  
- Paper is well-structured, accessible, and avoids unnecessary jargon.  
- Notes future work could extend to contextual or non-stationary bandits, but current scope is solid.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, novel, meets NIPS standards, recommend acceptance.
