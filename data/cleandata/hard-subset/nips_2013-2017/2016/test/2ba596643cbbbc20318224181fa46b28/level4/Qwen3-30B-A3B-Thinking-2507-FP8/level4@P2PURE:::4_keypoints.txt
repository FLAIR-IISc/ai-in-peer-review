- Proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandits framework where arms have multiple fidelity levels with different costs; presents an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and experiments showing it beats UCB, but the paper is judged not ready for publication.

- Notation is scattered and confusing (key symbols like s are defined long after first use).

- The advertising example is misleading—discussion of non-stationarity is irrelevant and a different motivating example is needed.

- The regret formulation is questionable: optimal policy ignores cost (chooses highest mean reward), and forcing low-fidelity sampling for the optimal arm obscures unnecessary low-fidelity costs.

- A cost/sample-complexity analysis would be more appropriate and practically motivated (e.g., testing prototypes under constraints requiring low-fidelity tests).

- Writing is awkward in places and needs thorough revision to improve flow and clarity.
