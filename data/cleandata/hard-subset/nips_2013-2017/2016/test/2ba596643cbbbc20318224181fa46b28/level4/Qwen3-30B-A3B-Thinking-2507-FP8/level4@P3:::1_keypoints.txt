- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where cheaper, lower-fidelity observations approximate expensive high-fidelity outcomes.
- Formalizes cost-weighted regret for multi-fidelity selection but uses a multiplicative cost formulation (Equation 1).
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting UCB to exploit lower fidelities for exploration and higher fidelities for promising arms.
- Provides theoretical upper bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB that ignores fidelity structure.
- Offers a lower bound indicating MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.
- Builds on existing bandit and multi-fidelity literature but makes a significant contribution by analyzing multi-fidelity in the bandit context.
- Empirical results on synthetic tasks convincingly demonstrate MF-UCB’s superiority over standard UCB.
- Major critique: the multiplicative regret definition is unnatural compared to a more standard additive cost formulation.
- Assumption 1 on the decay rate of ζ(m) appears arbitrary, lacks practical justification, and is crucial for proving MF-UCB’s advantage.
- Theoretical results may be limited to a narrow class of problems unless Assumption 1 can be justified or relaxed.
- Notation and partitioning (K(m) sets) obscure the practical interpretation of the regret bound; more intuition is needed.
- There is a gap in the lower bound analysis where MF-UCB may not be optimal for certain arms, which is not fully addressed.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—suggest adopting additive regret, justifying/relaxing Assumption 1, and clarifying intuition linking theory to practice.
