- Problem setup: Introduces multi-fidelity K-armed bandits where each arm has M fidelity levels with lower cost but potential bias.
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that enlarges confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.
- Exploration/Exploitation behavior: MF-UCB uses low fidelities to explore and reserves high-fidelity plays for arms near optimal.
- Theoretical results: Provides regret lower bound and shows MF-UCB is near-optimal.
- Experiments: Synthetic simulations where MF-UCB consistently outperforms standard UCB.
- Strength: Novel formalism for multi-fidelity bandits.
- Strength: Clear adaptation of UCB and solid technical analysis.
- Strength: Empirical results supporting the theoretical claims.
- Weakness: Weak connection between the model and motivating applications.
- Weakness: Performance guarantees are hard to interpret.
- Weakness: Simulation parameter choices are unjustified, making results hard to assess.
- Weakness: Limited originality—routine application of optimism principle.
- Weakness: Limited practical significance—mostly theoretical contribution.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to strengthen application links, clarify guarantees, and justify simulation parameters.
