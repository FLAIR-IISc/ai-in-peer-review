- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where cheaper, lower-fidelity estimates are used to prune arms before using costly high-fidelity evaluations.  
- Well-motivated by applications (e.g., online advertising) where short/cheap measurements can approximate long-term outcomes under cost constraints.  
- Proposes a new regret formulation, an algorithm with theoretical guarantees, and provides a regret lower bound.  
- Analysis is technically sound and offers novel insights compared to standard UCB that ignores fidelity trade-offs.  
- Major concern: the paper assumes the fidelity deviations ε^(m) are known a priori; this is unrealistic and needs discussion on how to obtain or relax (e.g., adaptive estimation or confidence-based approaches).  
- Suggest authors address practical estimation of ε^(m) or analyze robustness of the framework under uncertainty in these values.
