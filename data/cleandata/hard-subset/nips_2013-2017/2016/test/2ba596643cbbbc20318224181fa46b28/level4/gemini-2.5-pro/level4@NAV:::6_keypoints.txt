- Introduces a novel M-fidelity multi-armed bandit where cheaper, lower-fidelity samples can rapidly eliminate suboptimal arms and high-fidelity samples are reserved for promising arms; motivated by online advertising proxies.  
- Main contributions: a new regret definition for this setting, an algorithm with rigorous theoretical analysis (including a regret lower bound), and simulation validation.  
- The analysis is novel and constitutes a significant contribution.  
- Major concern: the method assumes known deviations ε^(m) between fidelity means and the highest fidelity; reviewer asks how these would be obtained in practice and suggests discussing relaxing this assumption or estimating them online.
