- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be sampled at multiple fidelities with different costs.  
- Formalizes a cost-weighted regret measure combining estimation quality and sampling cost.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity samples to eliminate suboptimal arms before high-fidelity evaluations.  
- Extends classical UCB to incorporate multi-fidelity structure, addressing practical settings like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Provides rigorous regret analysis with near-optimal upper bounds and a matching lower bound, demonstrating tightness.  
- Theoretical analysis is careful and well-supported by mathematical proofs.  
- Main paper is exceptionally clear and accessible; supplementary materials contain numerous typos and inconsistent notation that need proofreading.  
- Original contribution distinguishes this work from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization by focusing on regret minimization across fidelities.  
- Significant practical impact: MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB, especially when few arms are promising relative to the total.  
- Empirical results (Figure 2) corroborate theoretical claims, showing consistent improvement over UCB on synthetic tasks.  
- Notational/precision issue: frequent "up to a multiplicative constant" hides dependence on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities should be stated in the main text.  
- Practicality concern: method assumes known fidelity bias ζ(m); paper should discuss estimating ζ(m) and robustness to estimation errors.  
- Overall assessment: strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve theoretical precision and address practical implementation considerations.
