- Formalizes the multi-fidelity bandit problem, capturing practical settings like hyperparameter tuning and simulation-based science.  
- Presents a principled algorithm that leverages low-cost approximations to reduce cumulative regret.  
- Provides strong theoretical results: regret upper bounds that quantify multi-fidelity benefit and a near-optimal lower bound.  
- Paper is clearly written, with intuitive algorithm description and accessible presentation of results.  
- Empirical simulations support theory: MF method outperforms baselines, especially when high-fidelity evaluations are much more costly.  
- Relies on assumed relationships between fidelities; sensitivity and robustness to misspecification should be discussed.  
- Algorithm appears to require known cost/statistical parameters; practical schemes for online estimation are needed.  
- Should compare and contrast more explicitly with related multi-fidelity work (e.g., Bayesian optimization with multi-fidelity GPs).  
- Overall a strong, well-executed contribution with minor clarifications suggested; recommend acceptance.
