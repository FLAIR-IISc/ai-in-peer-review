- Paper studies multi-fidelity stochastic bandits where each arm pull at fidelity m gives biased rewards within known zeta_m of the true mean; lower fidelities are cheaper; authors propose a sequential arm–fidelity algorithm and give a lower bound.  
- The problem is interesting and relevant, explicitly capturing trade-offs among information, cost, and reward and serving as a useful benchmark.  
- Major shortcoming: results and assumptions are not clearly explained.  
- The chosen regret definition (multiplicative penalty for resource use) feels unnatural; authors should justify it and compare to an additive-cost formulation.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque; it's unclear why it should hold generally.  
- If zeta_m do not decay as Assumption 1 requires, the paper lacks a general procedure for selecting a subsequence of fidelities that would preserve results.  
- Although claimed non-crucial, Assumption 1 is used in proofs; concern that it may critically affect algorithm design and performance.  
- The algorithm appears tuned to specific zeta configurations without justification for why those sequences are “right.”  
- The gap in the lower bound needs further investigation and may require redesigning the strategy.  
- The main theorem (MF-UCB regret) is hard to interpret and compare to single-fidelity UCB because gains depend on Delta and the intricate sets K^(m).  
- Overall: promising model but presentation and interpretation of results lack clarity.  
- Minor typos: l.56 “near-optimal”; l.126 remove period before “for all m < M”; l.179 use set braces when defining [[k]].
