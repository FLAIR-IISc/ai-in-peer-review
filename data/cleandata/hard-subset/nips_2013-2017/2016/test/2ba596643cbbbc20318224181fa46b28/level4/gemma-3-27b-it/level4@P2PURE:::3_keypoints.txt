- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits: K arms each playable at M fidelities with lower fidelities cheaper but potentially biased observations.  
- Models lower-fidelity effects as bounded bias (not increased variance), which initially seemed questionable to the reviewer.  
- Provides motivating examples; algorithm-selection example supports bias model, advertising example is unconvincing.  
- Objective of minimizing regret for an algorithm designer in the algorithm-selection setting is not clearly motivated.  
- Proposes an MF-UCB algorithm: a UCB adaptation that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity bias.  
- Main technical results are rigorous regret bounds, but the bounds are complex and difficult to interpret.  
- Claims substantial improvement over standard UCB (often 3–4x lower regret) and supports this with simulations.  
- Simulation study uses synthetic problems and Appendix C lists parameter settings but gives no justification for those choices.  
- Unclear whether observed empirical gains reflect typical behavior or are artifacts of the chosen parameters (e.g., ζ^(m)).  
- Primary contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard application of optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty.  
- Overall, the connection between model and motivating applications feels somewhat tenuous, though not fatal to the work.
