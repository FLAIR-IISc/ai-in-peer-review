- Introduces a novel Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelities with different costs and lower fidelities approximate the true distribution.  
- Presents an algorithm with regret analysis and a problem-specific lower bound; synthetic simulations show better performance than a standard UCB baseline.  
- Presentation is unclear and poorly organized: notation and definitions are scattered and hard to follow.  
- The online-advertising motivating example is misleading because it suggests non-stationarity, which contradicts the paper’s stationary bandit setting.  
- The chosen regret framework is questionable: the optimal policy ignores sampling costs, weakening the benchmark for cost-sensitive decisions.  
- A cost-complexity analysis (e.g., motivated by prototype testing in product design) may better capture the problem’s objectives and practical constraints.  
- Overall writing and section structure need substantial revision for clarity and conciseness.
