Paper studies K-armed bandits with M biased, costed fidelities and proposes MF-UCB using low-fidelity plays to eliminate arms and save high-fidelity cost.  
Defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret tied to highest-fidelity mean and proves upper bound for MF-UCB plus a matching lower bound under conditions.  
Empirically, MF-UCB outperforms a highest-fidelity-only UCB baseline, especially when many arms are clearly suboptimal.  

Strength: models trade-off between information quality, cost, and reward in online learning with biased proxies.  
Strength: algorithmic novelty via fidelity-specific confidence bounds and switching thresholds γ(m).  
Strength: theoretical regret bounds showing cost reductions and near-optimality under assumptions.  
Strength: simulations demonstrate consistent cost savings versus highest-fidelity UCB.  

Weakness: pseudo-regret formulation (multiplicative highest-fidelity mean minus costs) is application-specific; additive-cost formulations are not analyzed.  
Weakness: Assumption 1 on ζ(m) decay seems opaque and possibly tailored to analysis without guidance on when it holds.  
Weakness: no constructive procedure to select/prune fidelities when ζ(m) decays slowly or fidelities are redundant.  
Weakness: algorithm and thresholds critically rely on known ζ(m); sensitivity to misspecification is not studied.  
Weakness: lower-bound gap for arms in K(m)7 suggests MF-UCB may overuse high fidelities; switching criterion might be improvable.  
Weakness: main results (Theorem 2, K(m) partitioning) are hard to parse and need clearer exposition and intuition.  

Requests: explicitly state that the UCB comparator runs only at highest fidelity and give a small worked example illustrating K(m) partitions and cost savings.  
Requests: discuss additive-cost regret and whether MF-UCB or variants can be extended with similar guarantees.  
Requests: provide a method to prune/subsample fidelities to satisfy Assumption 1 and analyze performance loss.  
Requests: analyze robustness to misspecified or slowly decaying ζ(m) and include a sensitivity experiment.  
Requests: consider modifying the switching rule to close the lower-bound gap for K(m)7 arms.  

Quality: technically sound with reasonable proofs, but key assumptions and choices need clearer justification.  
Clarity: presentation becomes dense; needs more intuition, examples, and explicit comparisons.  
Originality: novel formalization of multi-fidelity bandits with biased lower fidelities and regret analysis.  
Significance: practically relevant framework and near-optimality result, though lower-bound gaps limit completeness.  

Minor: several small editorial fixes (l.56, l.126, l.179) suggested.
