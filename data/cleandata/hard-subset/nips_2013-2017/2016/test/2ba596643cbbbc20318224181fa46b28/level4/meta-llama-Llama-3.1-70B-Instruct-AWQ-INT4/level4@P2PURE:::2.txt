This work introduces a novel multi-arm bandit setting, where each arm can be drawn with varying accuracies or fidelities, and the cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy. The authors define the regret as the product of the cost and the usual pseudo-regret, and propose the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting. They provide both upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The main paper is well-written and clear; however, the supplementary materials, which contain most of the proofs, require improvement. The proofs are marred by numerous typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader (see below for a non-exhaustive list). Correcting these errors would significantly enhance the readability of the paper.

Furthermore, I think the use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signs in the paper can be misleading, as the constants are not truly constant. For instance, in Theorem 2, there is a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden in the constant. Using the exact inequalities, as written in the supplementary materials, could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical perspective, it is unclear whether this algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For example, the strategy of MF-UCB, which uses low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms, may suffer from inaccurate estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion on the practical limitations and potential solutions would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides valuable insights into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I believe that this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
