- Formalizes a stochastic K-armed multi-fidelity bandit with biased low-fidelity observations and a cost-weighted regret targeting highest-fidelity reward.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an intuitive UCB-style algorithm that adaptively selects both arm and fidelity, using cheap low-fidelity pulls to screen arms and costly high-fidelity pulls for promising ones.
- Proves an upper-regret bound showing gains over single-fidelity UCB when low fidelities are informative and cheaper.
- Provides a lower bound indicating near-optimality under certain conditions, while noting a gap for a class of arms (K(m)_7).
- Strength: clear, well-motivated and novel formalization distinct from prior UCB, budgeted bandits, and multifidelity optimization.
- Strength: simple algorithm with fidelity-dependent arm partitioning that gives insight into where budget is spent.
- Strength: simulations corroborate theory, showing reduced regret and sensible allocation of high-fidelity evaluations.
- Weakness: assumes known fidelity biases ζ(m); needs discussion or methods for estimating/adapting to unknown or misspecified biases.
- Weakness: key results hide dependence on constants/parameters (e.g., ρ, λ(m), ζ(m), ψ) in “up-to-constants” notation; main text should expose these dependences.
- Weakness: lower-bound gap for some arms; paper should discuss whether more aggressive or data-driven early-fidelity testing could close the gap.
- Weakness: supplement contains numerous typos and minor errors that impair readability and must be corrected.
- Quality: technical development, assumptions, and proofs are sound and carefully argued, aside from supplement errors.
- Clarity: main exposition is clear, but asymptotic/≈ notation and constant hiding can obscure parameter dependence; bring key constants into main statements or references.
- Significance: setting is applicable to online advertising, algorithm selection, and simulation-based evaluation, offering actionable insights and potential large gains.
- Recommendation: accept after fixing supplement errors and expanding discussion on parameter dependence and robustness to unknown fidelity biases.
