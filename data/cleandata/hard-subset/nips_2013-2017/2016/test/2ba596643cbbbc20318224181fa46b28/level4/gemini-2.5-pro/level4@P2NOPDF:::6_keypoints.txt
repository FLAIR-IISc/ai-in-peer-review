- Introduces a multi-armed bandit framework with M fidelity levels where higher fidelity gives closer-to-true rewards at higher cost and lower fidelity is cheaper but noisier.  
- Motivates using low-fidelity pulls to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising arms, with applications like short vs. long ad displays.  
- Contributions: (1) new regret definition for cost-vs-accuracy setting.  
- Contributions: (2) a novel algorithm exploiting fidelity structure with a rigorous regret upper bound.  
- Contributions: (3) a matching lower bound establishing near-optimality, supported by simulations.  
- Strength: formalizes a practical multi-fidelity MAB problem with an elegant algorithm and comprehensive, novel theoretical analysis.  
- Main theoretical assumption/concern: fidelity bias values ε^{(m)} are assumed known a priori, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: authors should discuss how to estimate ε^{(m)} in practice and analyze robustness to misspecified biases.  
- Future work suggestion: relax the known-bias assumption and develop algorithms that learn these biases online, with discussion of potential challenges.
