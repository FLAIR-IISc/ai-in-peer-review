This paper formulates a multi-armed bandit setting in which each arm can be queried at multiple accuracies (fidelities) with increasing costs, and defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret tailored to this setting. The authors propose MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB that leverages inexpensive, biased approximations to screen out clearly suboptimal arms before committing cost at higher fidelities, and they establish both upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret. The main manuscript is clear and well organized. However, the supplementary material, where most proofs reside, contains numerous typos and minor, easily fixable mistakes that can mislead readers (see below for a non-exhaustive list). Correcting these would substantially improve readability. In addition, the pervasive “up-to-constant” notation can be confusing, as the hidden constants sometimes depend on algorithmic parameters; for example, in Theorem 2, a dependence on ρ (an algorithm parameter) is absorbed into the constant. Presenting explicit inequalities, as already done in the supplement, would make the claims crisper. On the practical side, it is not entirely clear how readily the method transfers to real-world deployments. In particular, MF-UCB’s elimination logic hinges on the fidelity biases ζ, and mis-specification of these quantities could degrade performance; a discussion of practical limitations and potential mitigation strategies would strengthen the paper. From a theoretical standpoint, the paper offers useful insights into the multi-fidelity exploration–exploitation trade-off, together with interesting theorems and generally solid proofs. Overall, this is a significant and interesting contribution.
