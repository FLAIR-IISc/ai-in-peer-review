- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelity levels where lower fidelity reduces cost but introduces bounded bias in reward estimates.  
- Proposes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for bias from low-fidelity pulls.  
- Provides complex theoretical regret bounds (theorems) claiming typical improvement over always-using highest fidelity UCB.  
- Regret guarantees are hard to interpret and lack intuitive clarity.  
- Main contribution is the multi-fidelity formulation; the algorithm itself is a straightforward application of optimism/standard UCB.  
- Simulation section offers empirical support but is brief and parameters in Appendix C lack justification.  
- Reported empirical improvements (MF-UCB beating UCB by ~3–4×) may be sensitive to chosen parameters (e.g., fidelity bias values ζ^(m)).  
- Questions the modeling choice: bias-based low-fidelity model is plausible but a variance-based model could also make sense.  
- Some motivating applications are convincing (e.g., ML algorithm selection), but others (ad display / interval bias claim) are unconvincing.  
- Overall model-application connection is weak but not disqualifying; clarity and broader empirical validation are needed.
