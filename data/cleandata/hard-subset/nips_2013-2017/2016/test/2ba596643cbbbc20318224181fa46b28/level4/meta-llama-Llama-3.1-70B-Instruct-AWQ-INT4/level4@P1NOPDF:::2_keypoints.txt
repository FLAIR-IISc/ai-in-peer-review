- Introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls where pull cost grows with accuracy and regret is cost × pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written, but most proofs in the supplementary have many typos and small errors that hurt readability.  
- The supplementary contains a long, specific list of notation/typo errors (e.g., incorrect symbols, missing parentheses, wrong inequalities and indices) that should be corrected.  
- The "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden constants (e.g., the algorithm parameter ρ) are not true constants; using exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-fidelity pulls to eliminate arms may fail if fidelity accuracy ζ is estimated poorly; a discussion of practical limitations and remedies is needed.  
- Theoretical contribution is solid and interesting, with useful insights, theorems, and proofs, making the work a significant contribution despite presentation issues.
