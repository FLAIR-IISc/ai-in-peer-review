- Studies a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be played at multiple fidelities with increasing cost and accuracy.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Provides theoretical analysis of MF-UCB, including regret bounds.
- Proves a regret lower bound via a change-of-measure argument, showing near-optimality in some cases.
- MF-UCB exploits cheap, low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate many suboptimal arms and uses expensive fidelities only for promising arms.
- Simulation experiments demonstrate MF-UCB’s empirical performance and comparisons to standard UCB.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Main limitation: assumes the deviations of fidelity means from the highest fidelity are known a priori, which may be unrealistic.
- The paper does not discuss how to estimate or relax the known-deviation assumption or its effect on performance.
- Lists several minor technical/notation errors needing correction (specific line-numbered fixes provided).
- Originality: novel algorithm and problem setting.
- Significance: potentially useful for practical applications (e.g., online advertising).
- Quality/clarity rated highly; reviewer scores: originality 8/10, significance 8/10, clarity 9/10, quality 9/10.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
