- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be sampled at varying accuracies with higher fidelity costing more, and defines cost-aware regret.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that uses cheap, low-fidelity pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms before high-fidelity evaluation.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: upper bounds and near-matching lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, characterizing interactions between fidelity, cost, and confidence.  
- Main paper is clear, well-structured, and gives good intuition for the multi-fidelity strategy.  
- The theoretical contributions are solid and give a meaningful picture of problem difficulty.  
- Supplementary material contains many typos and minor errors that hinder readability and verification of proofs.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated formulation reflecting realistic tunable-accuracy evaluations.  
- Strength: principled algorithm design (MF-UCB) and strong theoretical analysis (upper and nearly matching lower bounds).  
- Weakness: key constants are hidden in asymptotic notation, obscuring dependence on important parameters (notably ρ).  
- Weakness: practical applicability limited by reliance on known fidelity accuracies ζ; misspecification could cause irreversible arm-elimination errors.  
- Weakness: many typos/inconsistencies in appendix impede careful checking and confuse readers.  
- Suggestion: surface constants or add remarks summarizing dominant parameter dependencies in main theorems.  
- Suggestion: analyze or discuss robustness to misspecification of ζ, propose conservative/validation-based variants, and include sensitivity experiments.  
- Suggestion: provide guidance on selecting ρ and setting/learning fidelity thresholds or cost schedules in practice.  
- Suggestion: thoroughly revise and proofread the supplementary material and add a proof roadmap to aid navigation.
