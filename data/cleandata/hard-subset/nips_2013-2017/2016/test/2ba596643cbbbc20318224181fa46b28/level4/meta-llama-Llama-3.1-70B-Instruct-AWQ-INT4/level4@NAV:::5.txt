This paper tackles a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where low-cost approximations for each arm are readily available. The authors have formalized this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem. The paper presents theoretical results on regret bounds and validates these findings through simulations that effectively illustrate the impact of multi-fidelity. The presentation is clear and accessible. The simulations successfully demonstrate the efficacy of the multi-fidelity bandit method. Furthermore, the authors have provided a thorough theoretical analysis of the lower bound of the proposed method, thereby establishing its theoretical superiority.
