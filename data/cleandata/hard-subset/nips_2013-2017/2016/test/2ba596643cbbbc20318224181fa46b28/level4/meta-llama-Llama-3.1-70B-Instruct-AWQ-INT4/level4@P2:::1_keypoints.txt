- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity framework for exploration–exploitation in multi-armed bandits.  
- Introduces the notion of "fidelity" and the MF-UCB algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity plays to prune bad arms and reserves costly high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- Paper is technically solid and studies a relevant online learning problem.  
- Provides a lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal.  
- Regret definition is unconventional, penalizing resource use multiplicatively rather than additively, and may not be generally appropriate.  
- The ad-display example motivates the regret choice but alternative additive-cost formulations should be considered.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque, with unclear general applicability to real problems.  
- Unclear how to handle fidelity sequences that don't satisfy Assumption 1 or whether a suitable subsequence selection is possible.  
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific zeta (fidelity) configurations without justification that these are the correct sequences.  
- A gap in the lower bound needs further investigation and may require redesigning the strategy.  
- The main theoretical result (Thm. 2) is highly technical and difficult to interpret or compare to standard UCB.  
- Dependence of MF-UCB’s gains on Δ and the sets K^(m) is complicated and needs clearer, more explicit explanation.  
- Presentation is overly technical and should be simplified for broader accessibility.  
- Suggested fixes: better motivate/clarify regret, justify Assumption 1, address lower-bound gap, provide intuitive interpretation of Thm. 2, and simplify exposition.
