- Introduces a novel framework for exploration–exploitation when cheaper approximations to experiments are available.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound-based algorithm for the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides both upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret of MF-UCB.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written.  
- Supplementary materials contain many typos and small errors that hinder readability.  
- Many inequalities are presented up to multiplicative constants; using exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear—MF-UCB may suffer when the fidelity accuracy ζ is inaccurately estimated.  
- The paper lacks a discussion of practical limitations and strategies to mitigate fidelity estimation issues.  
- Strength of the work lies in its theoretical insights and solid proofs.  
- Arguments for acceptance emphasize the novel framework, the MF-UCB algorithm, and regret bounds.  
- Arguments against acceptance cite the supplementary errors and practical applicability concerns.  
- Suggested improvements: fix supplementary typos, use exact inequalities, add practical limitations/mitigations, and provide more experimental details to demonstrate applicability.
