- Studies a new multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelities and proposes MF-UCB that adapts to approximations/costs, improving regret over naive strategies.  
- Provides a lower bound on regret and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Problem is well-motivated and relevant to applications like online advertising.  
- Paper gives a clear, concise problem statement and proposed solution.  
- Theoretical analysis is novel and thorough, offering solid understanding of algorithm performance.  
- Major practical concern: assumes known deviations between fidelity means (mth vs highest fidelity); unclear how to obtain these in real applications and should be relaxed.  
- Multiple minor/math errors need correction (grouped): H→K at lines 326/357/386; "s"→μ* at 343; γ→γ^(m) at 345; two "≤"→"≥" at 346–347; summation over “above”→“below” K^(m) at 384; δ^(m)→δ^(M) and drop μ* in μ*κλ^(m) at 407; "for l ≥ m"→"for l ≥ p" at 431 and eq.(17) at 440; P(A_{n,3})→P(A_{n,2}) at 450.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm/setting, strong theoretical results, clear motivation and exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: impractical fidelity-deviation assumption and the listed minor errors.  
- Recommendation: accept subject to minor revisions addressing the practical assumption and the enumerated errors.
