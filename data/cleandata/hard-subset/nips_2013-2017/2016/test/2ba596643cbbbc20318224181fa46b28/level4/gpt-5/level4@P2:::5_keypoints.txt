- Introduces multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where each arm can be queried at M fidelities with known fidelity-wise bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m), and defines cost-weighted pseudo-regret against the highest-fidelity mean µ(M).  
- Proposes MF-UCB: selects arms using min over fidelity-specific UCBs (inflated by ζ(m)) and chooses fidelity via thresholds γ(m) that trade off bias, estimation error, and cost.  
- Theoretical upper bound shows MF-UCB concentrates high-fidelity budget on promising arms and prunes suboptimal arms cheaply; a Bernoulli lower bound shows near-optimality for most arms with an identified gap for a subset.  
- Empirical simulations on synthetic data demonstrate improved regret over naive high-fidelity UCB and align with theoretical fidelity usage intuition.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated formalization with a regret definition appropriate for long-term/high-fidelity objectives.  
- Strength: algorithmic novelty in integrating fidelity-specific UCBs with a principled fidelity-switching rule γ(m)=ψ−1(λ(m)/λ(m+1)·ψ(ζ(m))).  
- Strength: strong, interpretable regret analysis via K(m) partitioning and explicit account of where the lower-bound gap remains.  
- Strength: general concentration framework (ψ) covering sub-Gaussian and beyond and anytime guarantees without knowing capital Λ.  
- Strength: simulations consistently support theoretical claims about fidelity exploitation.  
- Weakness: assumes known, arm-uniform bias bounds ζ(m); lacks discussion or experiments on misspecification, arm-dependent ζ, or adaptive estimation.  
- Weakness: relies on Assumption 1 for favorable comparisons; needs sensitivity analysis when fidelities are dense or M is large and clarity on degraded guarantees if assumption fails.  
- Weakness: identified lower-bound gap where MF-UCB unnecessarily escalates to higher fidelity; suggests adaptive thresholds, occasional cheap re-checks, or arm-dependent γ but lacks empirical fixes.  
- Weakness: regret model fixed to evaluating by µ(M); should formalize alternative regret choices and how MF-UCB adapts to other regimes.  
- Weakness: limited implementation guidance (selecting ρ, unknown ψ, computational overhead) and lacks sensitivity analyses for these parameters.  
- Weakness: empirical baselines limited to standard UCB; needs comparisons to Thompson sampling variants, cost-aware elimination, UCB-V, ablations, misspecification tests, and a real-data case study.  
- Quality: analysis appears technically sound with careful concentration and standard, well-argued lower-bound construction.  
- Clarity: generally well organized; could add a two-fidelity walkthrough and a small numerical example to illustrate K(m) and γ(m).  
- Originality: formalism and MF-UCB design are novel relative to prior bandit work on surrogates and costs.  
- Significance: likely impactful for domains using surrogate evaluations (ads, AutoML, sim-to-real) and offers a practical blueprint.  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated, elegant algorithm with strong theory and consistent empirical gains.  
- Cons against acceptance: reliance on known ζ(m) and Assumption 1, unresolved optimality gap, and limited empirical breadth/realism.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance, contingent on clarifying assumptions, expanding empirical comparisons, and addressing robustness to misspecification.
