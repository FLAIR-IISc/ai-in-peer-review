This paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the multi-armed bandit setting, where each arm can be drawn with different accuracies (or fidelities). The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost by the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, which addresses this setting and provide an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The paper is well-structured and clear in its presentation. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the multi-fidelity bandit setting, the MF-UCB algorithm, and the theoretical analysis of its performance. The use of figures and tables helps to illustrate the key concepts and results.

The theoretical analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm is solid, and the authors provide a comprehensive proof of the upper bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret. The lower bound, which is proved using a change of measure argument, provides a useful benchmark for evaluating the performance of the MF-UCB algorithm.

The paper also includes some simulations on synthetic problems, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF-UCB algorithm in practice. The results show that MF-UCB outperforms the standard UCB algorithm on all the problems considered.

However, there are some minor errors and typos in the supplementary materials, which contain most of the proofs. These errors can be easily corrected, but they may confuse the reader.

From a practical point of view, it is not clear whether the MF-UCB algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB, which uses low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms, could suffer from inaccurate estimation of the fidelity accuracy. A discussion on the practical limitations and how to overcome them would benefit this work.

Overall, this paper provides a significant and interesting contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The authors' use of a novel framework, combined with solid theoretical analysis and simulations, makes this paper a valuable addition to the literature.
