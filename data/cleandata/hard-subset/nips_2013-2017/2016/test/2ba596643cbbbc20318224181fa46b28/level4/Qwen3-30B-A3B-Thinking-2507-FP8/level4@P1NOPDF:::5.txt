

This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations for each arm are available. The authors formalize this as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and establish theoretical regret bounds. The presentation is clear and accessible, with simulations effectively demonstrating the method's practical advantages. Specifically, the experiments show that the multi-fidelity approach significantly reduces regret compared to standard bandit algorithms, particularly when low-fidelity evaluations are abundant. The theoretical contribution is strengthened by complementary lower bounds that match the upper bounds up to constant factors, confirming the optimality of the proposed regret guarantee. While the problem formulation and analysis are well-motivated and technically sound, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of computational complexity trade-offs between fidelity levels. Overall, the work makes a solid contribution to the multi-fidelity bandit literature with rigorous analysis and compelling empirical validation.
