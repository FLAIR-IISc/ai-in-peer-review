- Proposes a multi-armed bandit model with selectable fidelity levels that trade off reward accuracy and pull cost.
- Formulates objective as maximizing a net reward and introduces the MF-UCB algorithm claiming it can beat high-fidelity UCB.
- Provides a regret lower bound for the proposed algorithm.
- Study addresses a relevant tradeoff between information, cost, and reward and could become a benchmark for future work.
- Paper appears technically sound but suffers from unclear explanations of results and assumptions.
- Regret definition is unconventional (multiplicative penalty for resource use) and may not generalize beyond the ad-display example.
- Suggests investigating an additive-cost formulation of reward/regret as an alternative.
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque, imposing constraints on fidelity parameters without clear justification for general settings.
- Unclear how to handle fidelities that don’t satisfy Assumption 1 or whether a subsequence selection method exists to make it hold.
- Claim that Assumption 1 is non-crucial contradicts its use in proofs, raising concerns about its influence on algorithm design and performance.
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific error-margin configurations without justification for their optimality.
- There is a gap in the lower bound that may require re-design or deeper investigation of the strategy.
- Theorem 2 and its comparison to UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and dependence on complex parameter sequences.
- Need for more explicit explanations of relative gains/losses of MF-UCB and clearer presentation of parameter dependencies.
- Minor issues: a few typos and suggested notational improvements (unnecessary periods, prefer set notation).
