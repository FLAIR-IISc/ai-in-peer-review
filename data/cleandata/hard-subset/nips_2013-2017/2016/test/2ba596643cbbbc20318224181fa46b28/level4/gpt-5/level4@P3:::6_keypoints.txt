- Paper introduces a finite-armed, stochastic multi-fidelity MAB where fidelities have known uniform bias bounds ζ(m) and differing costs, optimizing cumulative high-fidelity reward under budget Λ with a cost-weighted pseudo-regret.
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-fidelity UCBs augmented by ζ(m), pick arm by min over fidelity-specific UCBs, and use lowest fidelity whose uncertainty exceeds γ(m) to screen arms cheaply.
- Provides regret upper bounds showing MF-UCB concentrates spending on the minimal fidelity needed to eliminate arms and a lower bound that nearly matches the upper bound except for a specific conservative regime.
- Empirical simulations support the theory but are limited to synthetic experiments.
- Builds on classical UCB analyses and differs from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity GP work by focusing on finite arms with explicit bias bounds and a new regret notion.
- Modeling and regret analysis are technically solid and clearly presented.
- Key assumptions: known bias bounds ζ(m), sub-Gaussian–type concentration via ψ, and a regularity condition (Assumption 1) preventing near-duplicate fidelities.
- Notation is heavy; a short symbol table would improve clarity.
- Formalization and the MF-UCB design are novel and an elegant adaptation of UCB ideas to multi-fidelity settings.
- Potentially impactful in domains with cheap proxies (ads, A/B testing, AutoML, simulators) where many arms can be screened cheaply.
- Main practical limitation: requirement to know ζ(m) and ψ, and sensitivity to their mis-specification.
- Strengths: clear formalization, simple and interpretable algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees including a lower bound, and clear insights on when multi-fidelity helps.
- Weaknesses/questions: practicality of knowing ζ(m) — suggested remedies include pilot sampling, adaptive/robust inflation of ζ(m), and sensitivity analyses.
- Weaknesses/questions: Assumption 1 (fast decay of ζ) may be unrealistic — recommend empirical study of mild violations and possible fidelity merging.
- Weaknesses/questions: lower-bound gap suggests the switching rule may be conservative; propose refining γ(m) to be arm-specific or data-driven to allow cheap resolution of small gaps.
- Weaknesses/questions: experiments need real or semi-synthetic studies and robustness tests to ζ(m), λ(m), and ψ; compare against heuristic low-fidelity screening baselines.
- Practical guidance requested: how to choose parameters (e.g., ρ) and effect of ψ mis-specification.
- Minor issues: several typos and notation errors (listed by line numbers) needing correction.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — compelling formulation and strong theory, but needs better treatment of unknown ζ(m) and expanded experimental validation.
