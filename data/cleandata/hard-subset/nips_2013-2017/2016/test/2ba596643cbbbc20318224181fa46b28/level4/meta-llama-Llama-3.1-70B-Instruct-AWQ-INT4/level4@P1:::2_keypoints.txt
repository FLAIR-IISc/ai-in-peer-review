- Introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting with fidelity-dependent pull accuracies and costs, and defines regret as cost times pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written; key ideas and results are effectively conveyed.  
- Supplementary materials contain many typos and small errors that hamper readability and should be corrected.  
- The paper often uses "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation that hides important parameters (e.g., ρ), which can mislead; presenting exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-fidelity pulls to eliminate arms may fail if fidelity accuracies ζ are inaccurately estimated.  
- Recommends adding a discussion of practical limitations and ways to mitigate issues with fidelity estimation.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong: provides useful insights, interesting theorems, and generally solid proofs.  
- The reviewer provided a detailed list of specific typographical and technical errors in the appendix that need correction.
