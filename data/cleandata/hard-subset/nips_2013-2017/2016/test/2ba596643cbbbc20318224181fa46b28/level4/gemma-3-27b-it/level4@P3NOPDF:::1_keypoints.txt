- Addresses a relevant problem: multi-armed bandits with fidelity-cost tradeoff and models arms with fidelity-dependent stochastic rewards and costs.  
- Proposes a novel algorithm MF-UCB and provides a regret bound showing potential improvements over standard high-fidelity UCB.  
- Paper is technically sound but suffers from unclear presentation and weak justification of key modeling choices.  
- Regret definition is questionable: multiplicative net reward by fidelity seems less natural than an additive cost; exploring additive cost formulations would broaden applicability.  
- Assumption 1 on decay of fidelity error parameters (zeta_m) feels artificial and is not convincingly justified; its necessity and generality are unclear.  
- If zeta_m do not satisfy Assumption 1, authors should propose selecting a suitable subsequence of fidelities to meet the assumption for practical use.  
- The lower bound is loose; a tighter lower bound or strategy redesign informed by its limitations is needed to strengthen the contribution.  
- Theorem 2 is hard to interpret: dependence on Delta and complex sets K^(m) obscures when and by how much MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Authors should explicitly state conditions and quantitative gains for MF-UCB vs UCB and provide concrete examples for different zeta configurations.  
- Minor editorial/notation fixes: revise “near-optimal” (l.56); remove unnecessary period (l.126); use curly braces for [[k]] (l.179).
