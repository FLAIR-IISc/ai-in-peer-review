- Studies a variant of the stochastic K-armed bandit problem with low-cost, low-fidelity reward approximations.
- Formalizes the problem as a multi-fidelity bandit setting.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Theoretical analysis is a major strength, supplying rigorous regret bounds for the proposed algorithm.
- Authors derive a lower bound that quantifies the theoretical gains from using multiple fidelities.
- Theoretical claims are validated by simulations that demonstrate practical advantages of the method.
