- Studies multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits with biased cheap approximations and defines a cost-weighted regret metric.  
- Proposes MF-UCB to allocate plays across fidelities and proves near-optimal regret bounds, plus a lower bound highlighting where gaps occur.  
- Strong practical motivation (e.g., efficient ad testing) and significance for resource-constrained learning.  
- Novel formalism modeling biased approximations and a cost-aware regret, filling gaps in prior multi-fidelity work.  
- Theoretical rigor: tight regret bound (Thm. 2), matching lower bound for most arms (Thm. 4), and insight into fidelity allocation.  
- Excellent clarity and organization with precise definitions, intuitive figures, accessible algorithm description, and proof sketches.  
- Empirical validation: simulations show MF-UCB outperforms UCB and exhibit predicted fidelity-use behavior.  
- Limitation: Assumption 1 (fast-decaying ζ^(m)) may restrict applicability and needs deeper discussion for slow decay regimes.  
- Limitation: MF-UCB does not match the lower bound for arms in K^{(m)}_7; adaptive switching criteria (e.g., adaptive γ^(m)) are not explored.  
- Limitation: Related work comparison (notably Kandasamy et al. [8]) is underdeveloped; clearer distinctions would strengthen novelty claims.  
- Pro: Novel, practical problem formulation with rigorous analysis and supportive simulations — strong case for acceptance.  
- Con: Scope limited by Assumption 1 and the K^{(m)}_7 gap; related-work distinctions should be improved.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution with high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; recommend acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
