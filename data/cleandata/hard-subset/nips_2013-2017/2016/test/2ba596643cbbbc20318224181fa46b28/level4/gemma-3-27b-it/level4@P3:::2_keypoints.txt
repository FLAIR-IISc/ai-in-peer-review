- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be evaluated at different-cost fidelities to speed elimination of suboptimal arms.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adapts UCB to choose fidelity levels per arm pull.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: upper and lower regret bounds and near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Clear presentation and intuitive motivation, with good connections to prior work in bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, and active learning.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typographical errors and inconsistencies that hinder readability and could cause confusion.  
- Use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” hides important practical dependencies; more precise bounds or clarity on dependence (e.g., on ρ) are needed.  
- Lacks in-depth discussion of practical limitations and robustness, especially sensitivity to fidelity-accuracy estimates (ζ) and mitigation strategies.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic simulations; more real-world or complex experiments would strengthen validation.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, theoretical rigor, clear exposition, and potential impact in several application areas.  
- Weaknesses summarized: poor appendix quality, hidden constants in results, insufficient practical discussion, and limited empirical validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novel problem, strong theoretical analysis, potential impact, and good literature understanding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: numerous appendix errors, missing practical considerations and mitigations, and limited empirical evidence.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix appendix errors, tighten bounds where possible, and expand practical discussion.  
- Appendix error list: reviewer provides a detailed enumerated list of specific typos and formula corrections in the supplementary material.
