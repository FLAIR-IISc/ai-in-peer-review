- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic bandit model where each fidelity has known cost and error bound, and proposes MF-UCB with regret analysis and a lower bound.  
- Strength: Formalizes the cost–precision tradeoff in a practical setting (simulations, medical tests, ads) with solid technical proofs.  
- Concern: Regret defined multiplicatively (cost model) is unconventional; an additive-cost formulation is more standard and should be justified or compared.  
- Concern: Assumption 1 (geometric decay of ζ_m) feels artificial and under-motivated; need clarity on how essential it is to the algorithm and proofs.  
- Question: Does Assumption 1 bias MF-UCB’s design to a narrow class of ζ configurations, and can a valid subsequence be chosen if the full set fails the assumption?  
- Clarity issue: Theorem 2 and the regret bound are presented opaquely, using complex recursive quantities without explicit comparison to a high-fidelity UCB baseline.  
- Suggestion: Provide intuitive discussion and illustrative examples showing when and by how much MF-UCB outperforms single-fidelity UCB (dependence on gaps, costs, ζ_m).  
- Theoretical gap: There is a gap between the upper and lower bounds; the paper should discuss whether this is an artifact of analysis or indicates possible algorithmic suboptimality.  
- Overall: Interesting and potentially valuable contribution but limited by unclear assumptions and hard-to-interpret main results; better exposition would strengthen impact.  
- Minor fixes: hyphenate "near-optimal" (l.56), fix stray period before "for all m < M" (l.126), and use standard set notation for [[k]] (l.179).
