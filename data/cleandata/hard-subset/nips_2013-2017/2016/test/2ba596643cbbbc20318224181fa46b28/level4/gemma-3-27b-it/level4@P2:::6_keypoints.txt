- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where evaluations have multiple cost-accuracy fidelities.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm tailored to multi-fidelity bandits.  
- Proves regret bounds for MF-UCB and shows near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Extends the classical UCB framework to handle biased, cheaper approximations.  
- Core idea: use low-cost biased approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve costly evaluations for promising arms.  
- Formalization and regret definition for the multi-fidelity setting are clear and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical analysis including a lower bound is a significant contribution.  
- Simulations on synthetic problems support the theoretical results and outperform naive UCB baselines.  
- Related to budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization but specifically addresses biased approximations in bandits.  
- Strength: problem novelty and practical relevance (e.g., online advertising).  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and clear presentation.  
- Strength: convincing empirical validation (synthetic).  
- Weakness: assumption that fidelity deviations ζ(m) are known a priori is a major limitation.  
- Weakness: dense, technically complex analysis may limit accessibility.  
- Weakness: simulations are limited to synthetic datasets; real-world evaluation is lacking.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, practically relevant problem with significant theoretical and empirical support.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited applicability due to strong assumptions and accessibility concerns.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of acceptance, but should address the known-deviation assumption in future work.  
- Minor comments: multiple typographical/notation errors and incorrect indices/equations listed for correction (lines 326–450 and others).
