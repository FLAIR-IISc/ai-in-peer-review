This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting that incorporates varying levels of accuracy, or fidelity, for each arm. The cost associated with sampling an arm scales with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of this cost and the standard pseudo-regret. The authors present an algorithm, MF-UCB, designed to address this setting, and establish both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret. 

The main body of the paper is clearly written and well-organized. However, the supplementary materials, which contain the majority of the proofs, suffer from numerous typos and minor, correctable errors that hinder readability and could potentially cause confusion (a detailed list is provided below). Rectifying these issues would substantially improve the clarity of the presentation. 

Furthermore, the use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation throughout the paper feels somewhat imprecise, as the constants often depend on algorithmic parameters – for example, the parameter ρ in Theorem 2. Presenting the more precise inequalities, as found in the supplementary materials, would enhance the transparency of the results.

From a practical standpoint, the immediate applicability of this algorithm to real-world problems is not immediately obvious. Specifically, the initial phase of MF-UCB, which relies on low-accuracy samples to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms, might be vulnerable to inaccuracies in the estimation of ζ (fidelity accuracy). A discussion of these practical limitations and potential mitigation strategies would strengthen the work.

Theoretically, this paper offers valuable insights into the problem and features interesting theorems supported by rigorous proofs. Overall, this represents a significant and worthwhile contribution to the field.
