- Models multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelity levels per arm (known zeta_m error bounds, fidelity-dependent costs), proposes MF-UCB algorithm and gives a regret lower bound.  
- Studies the tradeoff between information, cost, and reward in stochastic bandits; potentially a useful benchmark.  
- Technical development appears sound.  
- Regret definition seems unnatural (multiplicative penalty for fidelity) and lacks justification; an additive-cost formulation should be discussed.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial/opaque—unclear why the zeta_m constraints should hold in general.  
- No clear method for selecting a subsequence of fidelities if zeta_m do not satisfy Assumption 1; this weakens the results' applicability.  
- It’s unclear whether Assumption 1 critically influences algorithm design or performance.  
- Algorithm appears tailored to certain zeta configurations without justification of why those sequences are appropriate.  
- The lower bound has a gap that needs further investigation and may require redesign of the strategy.  
- Main theoretical result (Thm. 2) is hard to interpret and not clearly contrasted with standard UCB at highest fidelity.  
- Dependence of MF-UCB’s performance on Δ and the complex sets K^(m) is not made explicit; interpretation is difficult.  
- Paper starts with an interesting model but loses clarity when presenting results.  
- Pros for acceptance: interesting/relevant problem, technically sound algorithm, lower bound is a good starting point.  
- Cons for acceptance: unnatural regret definition, opaque Assumption 1, unresolved lower-bound gap, overall lack of clarity.  
- Minor typos: l56 "near-optimal"; remove period before "for all m < M" (l126); use curly braces for [[k]] definition (l179).
