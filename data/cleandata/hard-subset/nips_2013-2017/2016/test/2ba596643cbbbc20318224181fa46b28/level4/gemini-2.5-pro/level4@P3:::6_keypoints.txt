- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: each arm can be sampled at M fidelities with cheaper but biased low fidelities and expensive accurate high fidelity (highest = true reward).
- Motivated by practical applications (e.g., online advertising, hyperparameter tuning, experiment design) where cheap approximate evaluations are available.
- Formalizes the setting and proposes a cost-weighted regret that accounts for sampling capital.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses low fidelities to quickly eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity plays for promising arms.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: an upper bound for MF-UCB showing adaptive behavior and a corresponding lower bound, establishing near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Distinct from prior work like budgeted bandits or active learning with weak/strong labelers; addresses a different problem structure.
- Strength: original, significant formulation capturing a practical and underexplored aspect of sequential decision-making.
- Strength: technically sound, with an intuitive algorithm design and clever fidelity-switching criterion.
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition; useful analysis tools (e.g., partitioning into K(m) sets).
- Main weakness: assumes bias bounds ζ(m) are known a priori, which is unrealistic in many applications and affects the fidelity-switching mechanism γ(m).
- Suggests need for discussion or methods on sensitivity to ζ(m) misspecification or for learning these bias parameters online.
- Recommendation: overall strong paper with substantial contributions; recommend acceptance.
- Minor issues: several typos/notation errors in the appendix (e.g., H→K; s→μ*; γ→γ^(m); reversed inequality signs; index and probability term corrections) that should be fixed.
