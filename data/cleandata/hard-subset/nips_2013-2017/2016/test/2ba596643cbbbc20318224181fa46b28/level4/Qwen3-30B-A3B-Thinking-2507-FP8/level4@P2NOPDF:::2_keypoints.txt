- Introduces a novel cost-aware multi-armed bandit model where pull cost scales with fidelity and defines regret as cost times pseudo-regret; proposes MF-UCB.  
- Provides matching upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret; theoretical contributions are novel and significant.  
- Main text is well-written and clearly presents the theoretical results.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typographical errors, inconsistent notation, missing symbols, incorrect indices, and some logical gaps that impair readability and confidence in proofs.  
- Specific ambiguous notation: inconsistent symbols like H^{(m)} vs K^{(m)}, \hat{\gamma} vs \gamma^{(m)}, and index mix-ups.  
- The use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” hides dependence on algorithm parameter \rho (e.g., Theorem 2); constants should be made explicit.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-fidelity pulls may be sensitive to misestimation of \zeta and needs discussion of robustness.  
- Recommend adding mitigation strategies and practical guidance (e.g., adaptive fidelity scheduling, robustness analysis for \zeta estimation errors).  
- Required revisions: (1) fix all typos and notation errors in the supplement, (2) replace asymptotic/hidden-constant statements with explicit dependence on parameters like \rho, (3) add discussion on practical implementation and \zeta sensitivity.  
- Overall assessment: promising and theoretically strong work; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
