This paper studies a multi-armed bandit setting in which each arm can be queried at multiple fidelities (accuracies), with higher fidelity incurring higher cost. Regret is defined as a cost-weighted pseudo-regret. The authors propose MF-UCB for this setting and provide both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well organized, and the theoretical development is insightful with solid proofs.

However, the supplementary material, where most proofs reside, contains numerous typos and minor errors that impede readability. Correcting these would significantly improve the paper’s accessibility (a non-exhaustive list is provided below).

I am also concerned that the “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation can be misleading because some “constants” actually hide dependence on algorithmic parameters. For example, in Theorem 2 the factor rho is absorbed into the constant. Stating the exact inequalities (as already given in the supplement) in the main text would enhance clarity about parameter dependence.

On the practical side, it is not yet clear how easily MF-UCB can be deployed in real applications. The algorithm’s strategy—using low-fidelity pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms—may be sensitive to mis-specification or noisy estimation of the fidelity-accuracy parameter zeta. A focused discussion on (i) how zeta is obtained or estimated in practice, (ii) robustness of MF-UCB to misspecified fidelities, and (iii) possible mitigation strategies (e.g., adaptive calibration of fidelity levels, conservative elimination, or confidence-adjusted thresholds) would make the contribution more actionable.

Overall, this paper offers a valuable theoretical treatment of multifidelity bandits with meaningful insights and strong results. With improved presentation of constants and a clearer account of practical limitations and robustness, it would be a significant and impactful contribution.
