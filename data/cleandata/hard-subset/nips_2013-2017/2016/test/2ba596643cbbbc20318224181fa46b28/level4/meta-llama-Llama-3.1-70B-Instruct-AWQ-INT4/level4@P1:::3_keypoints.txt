- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where arms can be played at M fidelities with cost-bias tradeoffs.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.  
- Provides regret theorems showing MF-UCB often outperforms always-playing-highest-fidelity UCB.  
- Includes simulations that support the theoretical claims.  
- Clear and well-motivated problem formulation; main novelty is the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Analysis is thorough, technically sound, and well-executed.  
- MF-UCB itself is a standard optimism-based approach rather than a radical algorithmic innovation.  
- Connection between the model and motivating applications is weak and needs strengthening.  
- Simulation parameter choices are not well-justified, making empirical results harder to assess.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and results are relevant to the field.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to justify simulation parameters and better relate the model to applications.
