- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelities where lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce increasing bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm with modified confidence bounds to account for fidelity-dependent bias.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds claiming significant improvement over always-highest-fidelity UCB, though the bounds are complex.  
- Presents synthetic simulations showing ~3–4× regret improvement versus standard UCB.  
- Modeling choice: lower fidelity modeled as bias (not variance); justified for some examples (e.g., algorithm selection) but questionable for others (e.g., short ad display CTR).  
- Unclear why regret minimization is the primary objective in the algorithm-selection motivation.  
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a natural application of optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty.  
- The derived regret bound is difficult to interpret and assess practically.  
- Simulation parameter choices are not fully justified (Appendix C), raising concern that improvements may be parameter-dependent.  
- Strengths: novel formulation, straightforward algorithm, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: complex/opaque regret bound, weak links to some motivating examples, insufficient simulation-parameter justification, and initial bias-vs-variance modeling concern.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/impactful problem, reasonable algorithm, theoretical insights, and supportive simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: hard-to-interpret bounds, tenuous motivations for some examples, and inadequately justified simulation setup.  
- Overall assessment: worthwhile contribution and recommend acceptance, conditional on clarifying the regret bound and better justifying simulation parameters.
