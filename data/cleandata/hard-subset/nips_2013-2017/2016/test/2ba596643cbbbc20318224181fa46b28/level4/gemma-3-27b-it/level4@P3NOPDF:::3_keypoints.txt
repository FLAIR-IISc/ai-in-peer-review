- Introduces a multi-fidelity MAB where each arm can be played at M fidelities; lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded bias in rewards.  
- Proposes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Main contribution is the problem formulation and theoretical analysis for multi-fidelity MAB.  
- Resulting regret bounds are complex and difficult to interpret or extract practical insight from.  
- Authors claim the algorithm significantly outperforms always using highest fidelity; simulations report such gains.  
- Modeling low fidelity as bias (not variance) is debatable; justified for some motivating examples but not all.  
- The ad display interval example used to justify bias modeling is unconvincing.  
- In algorithm selection, focusing on regret minimization may not match practical objectives of designers.  
- The algorithm is a straightforward application of optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty (standard UCB adaptation).  
- Simulation experiments are on synthetic problems and lack justification for chosen parameter values (Appendix C).  
- Unclear whether reported 3–4× regret improvement is general or an artifact of specific parameter settings (e.g., ζ^(m)).  
- Paper does not fully explore consequences of modeling low fidelity as bias versus variance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing parameter-selection concerns and clarifying model limitations.  
- Suggestion: include sensitivity analysis of simulation parameters to strengthen empirical validation.
