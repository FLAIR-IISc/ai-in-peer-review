- Proposal: Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where cheaper low-fidelity samples approximate high-fidelity arms; presents an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments outperforming UCB.  
- Confusing notation: Key symbols (e.g., s) are introduced without clear definitions or consistent use, making the paper hard to follow.  
- Misleading example: The advertising use case incorrectly invokes non-stationarity; a domain like engineering prototyping (low-fidelity tests before costly validation) would better motivate the setting.  
- Flawed regret formulation: Regret is treated incorrectly—optimal policy should maximize reward per unit cost, but the paper assumes selecting the highest-mean arm regardless of cost, invalidating the analysis.  
- Missing cost-complexity analysis: The manuscript lacks an analysis of cumulative testing cost for identifying the best arm with high probability, which is crucial for practical applications.  
- Unpolished exposition: Redundant phrasing, inconsistent terminology, and abrupt transitions require substantial editing to improve clarity and flow.  
- Overall judgment: Core idea is promising but underdeveloped; must rework the cost-aware regret model, replace the motivating example, add cost-complexity results, and improve exposition before being publication-ready.
