- Proposed multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework: arms can be sampled at different fidelity levels with costs; authors give an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB.

- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., s used before being defined and then barely used), hindering readability and verification.

- The advertising motivating example is misleading by invoking non-stationarity, which is outside the paper’s scope and distracts from the core setting.

- The regret formulation is problematic: it ignores cost (optimizes highest mean regardless of cost) and forces low-fidelity sampling of the best arm, weakening the benchmark and obscuring true cost trade-offs.

- A sample-/cost-complexity (high-probability identification under a cost budget) perspective would be more natural and practically relevant, e.g., for product prototyping.

- Presentation needs substantial editing to streamline exposition, consolidate notation, and remove extraneous references.

- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful framework but currently unfit for publication; authors should adopt a cost-complexity view, tighten notation/exposition, and realign performance criteria.
