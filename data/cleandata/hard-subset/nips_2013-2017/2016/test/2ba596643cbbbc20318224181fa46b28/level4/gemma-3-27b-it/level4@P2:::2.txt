This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit (MAB) setting where each arm can be evaluated with varying levels of fidelity, trading off accuracy against cost. The regret is defined as a cost-weighted pseudo-regret. The authors propose the MF-UCB algorithm for this setting and provide both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative regret. The paper is generally well-written and clear, though the supplementary material, containing the proofs, suffers from numerous minor errors that detract from readability. Correcting these errors would significantly improve the paper’s accessibility. Furthermore, the frequent use of “up-to-a-constant” notation could be refined; explicitly stating the dependencies (e.g., the parameter ρ in Theorem 2) would enhance clarity.

From a practical standpoint, the applicability of this algorithm to real-world problems is not immediately obvious. The performance of MF-UCB relies on accurate estimation of the fidelity accuracy parameter ζ. Inaccurate estimates could severely impact the algorithm’s effectiveness. A discussion addressing potential practical limitations and mitigation strategies would strengthen the work. However, theoretically, this paper offers valuable insights into the problem, along with interesting theorems and robust proofs.

Overall, this is a significant and interesting contribution to the field.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The multi-fidelity bandit setting is a novel and potentially important extension of the classical bandit framework, relevant to numerous applications where obtaining precise evaluations is costly.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The algorithm is rigorously analyzed, with both upper and lower bounds established on its regret.
*   **Clarity (Main Text):** The core ideas and contributions are clearly presented in the main body of the paper.
*   **Significance:** The work advances the state of the art in bandit algorithms and addresses a practically relevant trade-off between cost and accuracy.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Supplementary Material Quality:** The numerous errors in the supplementary material significantly hinder understanding and reproducibility.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The paper lacks a thorough discussion of the practical challenges of applying the algorithm, particularly regarding the estimation of the fidelity accuracy parameter ζ.
*   **Constant Factors:** The frequent use of "up-to-a-constant" notation obscures the precise dependencies and may limit the paper's practical impact.
