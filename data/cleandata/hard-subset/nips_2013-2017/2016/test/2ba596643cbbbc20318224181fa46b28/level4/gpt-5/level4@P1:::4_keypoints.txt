- Paper introduces multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits with biased, cheaper low-fidelity observations and defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret measured against highest-fidelity means under capital Λ.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: fidelity-aware UCBs that include known bias bounds and a fidelity-selection rule trading off cost and information.  
- Theoretical results: upper bound showing low fidelities prune many arms and limit high-fidelity plays to promising arms, plus a lower bound and near-optimality under regularity conditions.  
- Empirical results on synthetic tasks show MF-UCB outperforms vanilla UCB and exhibits aggressive low-fidelity pruning.  
- Strength: timely, interesting formalization of biased cheap approximations with costs.  
- Strength: simple, implementable algorithm extending UCB with principled fidelity switching.  
- Strength: nontrivial theory (upper and lower bounds) that improves over standard UCB in favorable cases.  
- Strength: simulations align with theory and intended behavior.  
- Weakness: notation is dispersed and confusing; key symbols are defined late and should be consolidated.  
- Weakness: advertising example is misleading because it invokes nonstationarity not covered by the paper’s stationary model.  
- Weakness: regret objective (pseudo-regret measured against highest mean regardless of cost) is poorly justified and misaligned with cost-aware applications.  
- Weakness: missing alternative evaluation—fixed-confidence cost-complexity (best-arm identification under cost) would be a more natural objective for many applications.  
- Weakness: writing and organization need tightening, clearer stationarity assumptions, and explicit motivation for the chosen instantaneous reward qt = µ(M)It.  
- Quality: technically careful within the chosen framework but undermined by weak justification of the central modeling choice.  
- Clarity: below conference standards due to scattered notation and confusing examples; needs editorial revision.  
- Originality: novel multi-fidelity formalism distinct from budgeted or weak/strong labeler settings; fidelity-aware UCB is a natural but nontrivial extension.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for real-world proxy settings but limited by the mismatch between the regret objective and realistic cost-aware goals.  
- Pros for acceptance: interesting setting, clean algorithm, nontrivial theory, and empirical support.  
- Cons against acceptance: weakly justified regret notion, clarity issues, and missing analysis for more relevant objectives like cost-complexity.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; revise by consolidating notation, replacing misleading examples, justifying or changing the regret objective, and adding a cost-complexity identification analysis.
