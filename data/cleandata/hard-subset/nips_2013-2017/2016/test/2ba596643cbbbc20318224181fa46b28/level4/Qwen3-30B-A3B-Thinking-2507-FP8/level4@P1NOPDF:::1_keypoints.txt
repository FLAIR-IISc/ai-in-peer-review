- Addresses multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity levels (known fidelity biases ζ_m, lower-fidelity cheaper), proposes MF-UCB and gives a regret lower bound.  
- Problem is timely and could serve as a useful benchmark for cost–information–reward tradeoffs.  
- Significant clarity issues around core assumptions and presentation of results.  
- Regret formulation penalizes cost multiplicatively (reward × cost^{-1}), which seems unnatural; authors should justify this over an additive penalty (reward − cost) and strengthen the ad-display motivation.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on ζ_m decay rates) appears ad hoc and unjustified; unclear how to handle ζ_m sequences that do not meet it or how to choose a subsequence to restore guarantees.  
- Concern that the algorithm’s design/performance may critically depend on the specific ζ_m structure, limiting generality.  
- The regret lower bound may be loose; if so MF-UCB might not be near-optimal and requires deeper analysis or a revised strategy.  
- Interpretation and comparison of MF-UCB vs high-fidelity UCB are opaque: dependence on complex parameters (Δ, [k], sets K^{(m)}) needs clearer, explicit scaling under varying ζ_m.  
- Overall promising modeling but technical presentation, justification of assumptions, and result interpretation need substantial revision.  
- Minor corrections: fix "near-optimal" usage, remove extra period at Line 126, and use curly braces for set notation around [k].
