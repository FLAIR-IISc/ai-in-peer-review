- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelity levels where higher fidelity gives more accurate but costlier feedback, proposing policies that use cheap fidelities to prune arms and expensive ones only on promising arms.  
- Introduces a new regret notion, presents an algorithm with theoretical guarantees, proves a regret lower bound, and supports claims with simulations; analysis is original and relevant to applications like online advertising.  
- Major concern: the paper assumes known ε^(m) (deviation of m-th fidelity mean from highest-fidelity mean); reviewer asks how these are determined in practice (e.g., advertising) and whether this assumption can be relaxed.
