This paper investigates a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, considering scenarios where inexpensive approximations of each arm’s reward are readily available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical regret bounds. Simulations are presented to demonstrate the benefits of this multi-fidelity approach. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, and the simulations effectively illustrate the performance of the proposed method. Importantly, the authors include sufficient theoretical analysis, establishing a lower bound that demonstrates the theoretical advantages of their approach.
