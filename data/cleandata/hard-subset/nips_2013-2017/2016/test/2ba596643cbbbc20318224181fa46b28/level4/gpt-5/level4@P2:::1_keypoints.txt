- Paper studies K-armed stochastic bandits with M biased, cheaper fidelities, defines cost-weighted pseudo-regret, proposes MF-UCB, and proves upper/lower bounds with simulations showing benefits.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical cost–information trade-off setting.  
- Strength: Gives a clear algorithmic contribution (MF-UCB) with nontrivial analysis.  
- Strength: Presents both upper and lower bounds and supportive simulations.  
- Strength: Offers a useful benchmark formulation for multi-fidelity decision-making.  

- Concern: Regret definition multiplies reward by cost; an additive reward-minus-cost model may be more natural in many applications.  
- Suggestion: Discuss or analyze how results would change under an additive-cost objective or justify the multiplicative choice and its applicability.  

- Concern: Assumption 1 (ζ(m) decay) is opaque and restrictive yet used in proofs and algorithm design.  
- Suggestion: Clarify exactly where Assumption 1 is used and whether failing it only affects constants or degrades bounds.  
- Suggestion: Provide a principled procedure to select a subsequence of fidelities satisfying the assumption (e.g., skip near-duplicates) and show MF-UCB’s guarantees on that subsequence.  
- Suggestion: Include sensitivity analysis (theoretical or empirical) showing how regret scales with slower ζ-decay.  

- Concern: Theorem 2’s advantage expressed via complicated sets K(m) and gaps is hard to parse.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state the UCB baseline (highest-fidelity-only) and give a simplified corollary comparing terms side-by-side, highlighting λ(Jk)/λ(M) savings.  
- Suggestion: Add an intuitive explanation, small numerical example, and diagram illustrating the partition K(1)…K(M) and which arms are eliminated at which fidelities.  
- Suggestion: Summarize in prose that “far-from-optimal arms are eliminated at low fidelities; near-optimal arms consume high-fidelity budget.”  

- Concern: Lower bound is tight for some arm classes but loose for others due to MF-UCB’s switching criterion causing premature escalation.  
- Suggestion: Discuss whether modified switching rules or confidence-based decisions at cheaper fidelities could close the gap or provide impossibility results.  
- Suggestion: Provide guidance on parameter choices (e.g., γ(m)) or heuristics to reduce unnecessary high-fidelity escalation.  

- Quality: Analysis appears technically sound but some assumptions and design choices need clearer justification; main bound interpretability could improve.  
- Clarity: Problem setup is clear; presentation of results is dense and needs more intuition on Assumption 1 and the K(m) partition.  
- Originality: Novel formulation of multi-fidelity stochastic bandits with biased proxies and cost-aware UCB-style method.  
- Significance: Relevant to applications with evaluative proxies and could be a reference model; empirical and theoretical results suggest practical gains.  

- Pro: Interesting, relevant formulation capturing information–cost trade-offs.  
- Pro: Concrete algorithm with theoretical guarantees and empirical support.  
- Pro: Provides upper and lower bounds; near-optimal under conditions.  
- Con: Regret model choice may be too narrow; additive-cost formulations not analyzed.  
- Con: Assumption 1 feels ad hoc and needs clarification for practice.  
- Con: Main bounds are hard to interpret; baseline comparison should be clearer.  
- Con: Lower bound gap raises concerns about the switching strategy.
