This paper investigates a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, specifically addressing scenarios where cheap approximations for evaluating each arm are available. The authors formalize this as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical regret bounds. Simulations are presented to demonstrate the benefits of incorporating multi-fidelity information. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The simulations effectively illustrate the performance of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method. Importantly, the authors include sufficient theoretical analysis, including a lower bound, which demonstrates the theoretical improvements achieved by their approach.
