- Proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: each arm has M fidelities with lower cost but potential bias in rewards.  
- Presents a UCB-based algorithm that adjusts confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.  
- Supplies theoretical regret bounds, but they are complex and difficult to interpret.  
- Reports simulation gains of roughly 3–4× over standard UCB.  
- Models low fidelity as increased bias (not variance); initially counterintuitive but partly justified by examples like ML algorithm selection.  
- Some motivating examples (e.g., ad display time) and the use of regret minimization for algorithm selection are unconvincing.  
- The link between the model and its motivating applications is somewhat tenuous, though not fatal.  
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.  
- Simulation results lack justification for chosen parameter values, raising concern the improvements may be artifacts.  
- Overall: interesting and novel formulation, but theoretical/empirical clarity and motivation need strengthening.
