- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity levels, each giving noisy rewards within known zeta_m of the true mean and incurring known costs.  
- Authors propose a novel sequential-fidelity algorithm (MF-UCB) to maximize net reward and show it can outperform standard UCB when restricted to high fidelity.  
- A lower bound on regret for the proposed setting is provided.  
- The topic is timely and interesting, addressing the tradeoff between information, cost, and reward in stochastic bandits and could serve as a benchmark.  
- Presentation is technically sound but suffers from insufficient explanation of results, assumptions, and intuition.  
- The regret formulation penalizes cost multiplicatively rather than additively; this choice is unconventional and may not generalize beyond the ad-display example.  
- Assumption 1 on fidelity parameters (zeta_m) appears artificial/opaque and its general validity is unclear; authors should show how to pick a subsequence of fidelities if the assumption fails.  
- It is unclear how crucial Assumption 1 is to algorithm design and performance; the algorithm seems tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification.  
- The gap in the lower bound needs further investigation and might require rethinking the proposed strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and comparisons to UCB are hard to interpret because performance depends on complex quantities (Delta, [[k]], and sets K^(m)); clearer exposition is needed.  
- Minor typos: Line 56 "near-optimal"; Line 126 remove the period before "for all m < M"; Line 179 use curly braces for [[k]].
