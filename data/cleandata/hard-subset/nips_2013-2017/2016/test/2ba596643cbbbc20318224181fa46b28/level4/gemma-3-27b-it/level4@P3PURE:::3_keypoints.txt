- Problem setup: Introduces multi-fidelity MAB with K arms and M fidelity levels where lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded, fidelity-dependent bias in observed rewards.  
- Modeling choice questioned: Reviewer doubts bias (vs. variance) modeling, though accepts it for some applications.  
- Motivation: Algorithm-selection examples support the bias model; ad display duration example is unconvincing and minimizing regret may not match designer goals.  
- Algorithm: Proposes a natural UCB-based method that enlarges confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.  
- Novelty: Primary innovation is the multi-fidelity problem formulation; the algorithm is a standard optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty application.  
- Theoretical results: Provides regret theorems that are technically correct but complex and difficult to interpret.  
- Claimed improvement: Authors argue the bounds often imply significant gains over always-playing highest-fidelity UCB.  
- Experiments: Synthetic simulations show MF-UCB outperforming UCB (often by a factor of 3–4).  
- Experimental shortcomings: Appendix C lists parameters but gives no justification; unclear whether empirical gains generalize or are sensitive to parameter choices (e.g., ζ^(m)).  
- Overall stance: Connection between model and motivating applications is somewhat tenuous but not disqualifying; contribution is mainly problem formulation with limited algorithmic novelty.
