- Introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be pulled at multiple fidelities with costs increasing with accuracy, and regret is defined as cost-weighted pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting and provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well written.  
- Supplementary materials (proofs) contain many typos and small errors that hinder readability.  
- Reviewer provides a non-exhaustive list of specific appendix errors (incorrect symbols, missing parentheses, wrong inequalities, swapped variables, mislabeled lemmas/theorems, and typos).  
- Use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is potentially misleading because hidden “constants” depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., rho); exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB may be sensitive to misestimation of fidelity accuracy (zeta) and needs discussion of limitations and mitigation strategies.  
- Theoretical contribution is valuable and interesting, with solid theorems and proofs (subject to fixing the supplementary errors).
