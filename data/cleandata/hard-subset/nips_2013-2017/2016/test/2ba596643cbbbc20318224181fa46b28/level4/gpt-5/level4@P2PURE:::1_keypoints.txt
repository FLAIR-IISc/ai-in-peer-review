- Problem: studies multi-armed bandits where each pull also selects a fidelity level; fidelity m gives i.i.d. reward with known bias ζm and lower fidelities are cheaper.  
- Contribution: proposes MF-UCB to maximize net reward and provides a lower bound on regret.  
- Relevance: interesting and useful model for the trade-off between information, cost, and reward; could serve as a benchmark.  
- Presentation: paper is technically solid but lacks sufficient explanation of results and assumptions.  
- Regret definition: multiplicative penalty for cost seems unnatural; additive incorporation of fidelity cost into reward/regret should be considered.  
- Assumption 1: feels artificial/opaque—unclear why constraints on ζm should hold generally and the assumption appears used in proofs.  
- Robustness question: if ζm do not decay as required, authors should show how to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption and preserve results.  
- Algorithm design: MF-UCB appears tailored to specific ζ configurations; justification for these being the “right” sequences is missing.  
- Lower bound: gap in the lower bound requires further investigation and may necessitate redesigning the strategy.  
- Interpretation difficulty: Thm.2 and comparisons to UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and complicated dependence on Δ and the sets K^(m); need a high-level explanation.  
- Overall verdict: promising model but paper loses clarity in presenting and interpreting results; needs clearer explanations and justification.
