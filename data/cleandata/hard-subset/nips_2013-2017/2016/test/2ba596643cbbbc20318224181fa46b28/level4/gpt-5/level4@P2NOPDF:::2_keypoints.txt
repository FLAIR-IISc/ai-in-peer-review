- Contribution: Studies multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelities per arm, defines cost-weighted pseudo-regret, and proposes MF-UCB with upper and lower cumulative pseudo-regret bounds.  
- Main paper quality: Clear, well-organized, and theoretically insightful with solid proofs.  
- Supplement issues: Supplementary material contains numerous typos and minor errors that impede readability; a non-exhaustive list of corrections is provided.  
- Notation/constant concern: “Up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is misleading because some constants hide dependence on algorithmic parameters (e.g., rho); recommend stating exact inequalities in the main text.  
- Practical deployment concern: Unclear how easily MF-UCB can be used in real applications; algorithm may be sensitive to misspecification or noisy estimation of the fidelity-accuracy parameter zeta.  
- Requested practical discussion: Ask for details on (i) how zeta is obtained/estimated in practice, (ii) robustness to misspecified fidelities, and (iii) mitigation strategies (adaptive calibration, conservative elimination, confidence-adjusted thresholds).  
- Overall verdict: Valuable and strong theoretical contribution; with clearer constant presentation and a clearer account of practical limitations/robustness, it would be a significant and impactful paper.  
- Example supplement errors: Many specific corrections across pages 10–17 (equation/index/notation fixes, missing parentheses, wrong inequalities, swapped symbols) and a spelling error in Section C.
