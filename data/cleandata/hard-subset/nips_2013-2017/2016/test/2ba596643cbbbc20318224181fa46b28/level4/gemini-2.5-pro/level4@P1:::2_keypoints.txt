- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be pulled at different accuracies (fidelities) with fidelity-dependent costs.  
- Defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret to formalize trade-offs between exploration, exploitation, and budget.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm for this setting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with both upper and lower bounds on cumulative regret and solid proofs.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written; formulation is significant and original.  
- Supplementary material contains many typos and minor errors that hinder readability and should be fixed.  
- Use of asymptotic/proportional notation can be misleading because hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities should be stated in the main text.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB may be sensitive to misspecified fidelity-accuracy parameters (ζ) and needs a discussion of robustness and mitigation strategies.  
- Overall assessment: important and interesting contribution despite appendix presentation issues and limited discussion of practical limitations.  
- (Appendix) Reviewer provides a non-exhaustive, detailed list of typographical and mathematical errors to correct.
