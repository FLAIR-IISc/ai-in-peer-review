- Problem setup: Studies a variant of stochastic K-armed bandits where inexpensive approximations of arm rewards are available, formalized as a multi-fidelity bandit.  
- Approach: Leverages cheaper, lower-fidelity evaluations to accelerate learning.  
- Main theoretical contribution: Derives regret bounds for the proposed multi-fidelity bandit algorithm.  
- Interpretation of bounds: Regret bounds quantify the benefit of using lower-fidelity data and provide formal performance guarantees.  
- Lower bound analysis: Presents a detailed lower bound showing theoretical improvement over existing methods.  
- Empirical validation: Simulations convincingly demonstrate practical performance gains, especially when high-fidelity evaluations are costly.  
- Presentation quality: Paper is clear and accessible, with core ideas easy to grasp.  
- Overall assessment: Well-defined problem, sound theoretical analysis, and compelling empirical validation; formalization and regret results are valuable.
