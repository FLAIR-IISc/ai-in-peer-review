- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity choices per pull, each fidelity giving noisy rewards within known zeta_m of true mean and with different costs.
- Problem is relevant and interesting for tradeoffs between information, cost, and reward in online learning.
- Authors propose a new MF-UCB algorithm to maximize net reward and provide a regret lower bound.
- Main shortcoming: results and assumptions are inadequately explained, hurting clarity and accessibility.
- Regret definition is unnatural/limited: multiplicative penalization of cost is used instead of a seemingly more natural additive cost penalty.
- Assumption 1 (constraints on zeta_m) is opaque and appears artificial; its general applicability is unclear.
- Unclear whether one can select a subsequence of fidelities when zeta_m do not satisfy Assumption 1, and how that would affect results.
- Algorithm design appears tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification for why those sequences are appropriate.
- The lower bound has gaps and may require revisiting or redesigning the proposed strategy.
- Theorem 2 and comparisons to single-fidelity UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and dependence on complex sets K^(m).
- Overall promising model, but paper loses clarity in presenting results; several minor typos noted.
