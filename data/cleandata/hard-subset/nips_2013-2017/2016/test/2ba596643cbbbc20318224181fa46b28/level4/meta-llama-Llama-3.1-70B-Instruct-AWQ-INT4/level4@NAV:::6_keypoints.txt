- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where M fidelities trade accuracy for cost.  
- Strategy uses low-cost fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and high-cost fidelities for promising arms, potentially outperforming standard UCB.  
- Practical motivation and example given (online advertising: short displays approximate long-term CTRs at lower cost).  
- Introduces a new regret definition, provides an algorithm, theoretical analysis, and a regret lower bound.  
- Includes simulation results supporting the analysis and provides a fresh perspective.  
- Major concern: the analysis assumes each ε^{(m)} (deviation of mth-fidelity mean from highest fidelity mean) is known a priori, which is unrealistic in practice and needs discussion or relaxation.
