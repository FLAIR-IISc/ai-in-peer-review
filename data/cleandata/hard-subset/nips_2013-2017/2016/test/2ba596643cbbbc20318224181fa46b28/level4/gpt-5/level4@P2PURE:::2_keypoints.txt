- Introduces a new multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be pulled at multiple accuracy (fidelity) levels and regret is cost-weighted pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides matching upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well written.  
- Supplementary material is poorly polished with many typos and minor errors that impede readability.  
- Excessive use of “up to a multiplicative constant” is misleading because hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities should be stated.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may be sensitive to misestimates of fidelity ζ; discussion of limitations and remedies is needed.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and interesting, with valuable insights and solid proofs.  
- Reviewer provides a long, specific list of appendix typos/errors that should be corrected to improve clarity.
