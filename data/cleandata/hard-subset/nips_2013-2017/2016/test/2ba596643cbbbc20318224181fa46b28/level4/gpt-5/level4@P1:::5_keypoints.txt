- Problem: Studies multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where lower-fidelity observations are cheaper but biased, with fidelity-dependent costs and a cost-weighted regret metric.
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound method that uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate poor arms and reserves high-fidelity plays for promising ones.
- Theoretical upper bounds: Provides regret bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms naive strategies that ignore lower fidelities.
- Lower bound: Presents a complementary lower bound indicating MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Empirical validation: Simulations substantiate the theory and demonstrate reduced regret by leveraging lower fidelities.
- Clarity: Writing and presentation are clear; problem setup, algorithmic idea, and empirical results are easy to follow.
- Originality: Formalizes a distinct multi-fidelity bandit setting with biased approximations and fidelity costs; MF-UCB is a natural novel adaptation of optimism-based methods.
- Significance: Method can substantially reduce expensive high-fidelity evaluations with practical relevance (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).
- Overall: Clear, well-motivated work presenting a simple effective algorithm with matching theoretical and empirical support.
