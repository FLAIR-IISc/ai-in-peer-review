This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting, where a learner can choose from $M$ different fidelities to play an arm. Each successive fidelity provides a better approximation of the rewards, but at a higher cost. The key insight is that a strategy that leverages lower, cheaper fidelities to eliminate sub-optimal arms and reserves higher, more expensive fidelities for a small subset of promising arms may outperform the standard UCB algorithm, which neglects these characteristics of the fidelities.

This setting has practical applications, such as online advertising, where displaying ads for a short period can approximate long-term click-through rates while minimizing costs. To address this problem, the authors propose a new regret definition, develop an algorithm with theoretical analysis (including a regret lower bound), and conduct simulations. The analysis is novel and contributes to the field.

The authors assume that the deviations of the $m$-th fidelity mean from the highest fidelity mean, denoted as $\varepsilon^{(m)}$, are known in advance. However, it is unclear how these values can be determined in practice, such as in the online advertising application. Relaxing this assumption would enhance the practicality of the approach.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their contributions. However, there are a few minor errors and suggestions for improvement:

* In lines 326, 357, and 386, "H" should be replaced with "K" in the expression "arm k \in H^{(m)}".
* In line 343, the argument "\Beta{k^\ast,t} \leq s" should be "\Beta{k^\ast,t} \leq \mu{\ast}".
* In line 345, "\gamma" should be replaced with "\gamma^{(m)}".
* In lines 346-347, the inequalities should be reversed, and "\leq" should be replaced with "\geq".
* In line 384, the summation over all k \in those "above" K^{(m)} should be over all k \in those "below" K^{(m)}.
* In line 407, "\delta^{(m)}" should be replaced with "\delta^{(M)}", and the last term "\mu{\ast} \kappa \lambda^{(m) }" should be "\kappa \lambda^{(m)}".
* In line 431, "for l \ge m" should be replaced with "for l \ge p".
* In line 440, in equation (17), "l \geq m" should be replaced with "l \geq p".
* In line 450, "P(A{n,3})" should be replaced with "P(A{n,2})".

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and novel approach to the multi-armed bandit problem. With some minor corrections and clarifications, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
