- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be sampled at multiple cost-accuracy fidelities and defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate poor arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Main contributions: problem formalization, MF-UCB algorithm, a regret upper bound, and a nearly matching lower bound showing near-optimality.  
- Builds on UCB literature by adding a practical cost/approximation dimension relevant to applications like advertising, trials, and algorithm selection.  
- Originality/significance: the multi-fidelity formulation is novel, well-motivated, and likely to inspire further work.  
- Technical quality: the algorithm is intuitive and the theoretical analysis (upper and lower bounds) is rigorous and shows near-optimality, despite minor proof errors.  
- Clarity of main paper: the main body is well-written and organized; the K(m) partitioning effectively explains algorithm behavior and regret structure.  
- Major weakness: the supplementary material contains numerous typos and minor errors that hinder readability and verification of proofs.  
- Theoretical clarity issue: asymptotic notation in theorems hides dependencies on important constants/parameters (e.g., ρ); full explicit inequalities should be included.  
- Practical limitation: algorithm assumes known fidelity bias bounds ζ(m); need discussion on robustness to misspecification or methods to estimate ζ in practice.  
- Appendix-specific problems: many concrete errors in notation, equations, and statements across pages 10–17 (e.g., wrong variables, missing parentheses, incorrect inequalities) that require thorough proofreading.  
- Miscellaneous appendix fixes: interchange of variables p and m, missing probability terms, incorrect section/lemma/theorem labels, and typos like “distribtuions” → “distributions.”  
- Overall summary: significant and interesting contribution with an intuitive algorithm and solid theory, but needs careful proofreading of the appendix and a deeper discussion of practical considerations.
