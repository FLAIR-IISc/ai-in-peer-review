- Proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework with fidelity-dependent costs, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations showing better performance than UCB.  
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting but unfit for publication in its current form.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s is defined late and then scarcely used).  
- The advertising example is misleading due to correlations and non-stationarity; recommend replacing it with a more relevant example like prototype/product testing.  
- The regret formulation is questionable: the paper’s objective effectively always favors the arm with the highest mean regardless of cost, which may hide the cost of unnecessary low-fidelity sampling.  
- Recommends a sample-complexity or cost-complexity analysis instead of (or in addition to) regret, motivated by practical product-development testing constraints.  
- Several sections are unwieldy and need revision to improve clarity, flow, and coherence.  
- Overall framework has potential but requires substantial clarification and stronger theoretical/empirical justification.
