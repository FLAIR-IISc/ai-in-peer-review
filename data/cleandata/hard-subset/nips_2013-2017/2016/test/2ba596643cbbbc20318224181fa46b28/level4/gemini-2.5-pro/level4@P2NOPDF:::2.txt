This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be pulled at varying levels of accuracy, or fidelity. In this framework, the cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired fidelity, and the regret is defined as the product of the cumulative cost and the standard pseudo-regret. The authors propose the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting and provide corresponding upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.

The main paper is clear, well-written, and presents a significant and interesting contribution to the field. The theoretical perspective offers valuable insights into the problem, supported by interesting theorems and what appear to be solid proofs.

However, the paper's overall quality could be substantially improved by addressing a few key areas, primarily concerning the supplementary material.

The primary issue lies with the appendix, which contains the bulk of the proofs. Unfortunately, it is marred by numerous typos and minor, albeit easily correctable, errors that hinder readability and make the proofs difficult to follow and verify. Addressing these issues would greatly increase the quality and impact of the paper. A non-exhaustive list of errors I identified includes:

*   **Page 10, Equation (8):** `k` should likely be `k-1`.
*   **Page 10, Section A1:** `H^(m)` should be `K^(m)`.
*   **Page 10, Line 328:** The expression `ν/(ρ-2)` should be `ν/2`.
*   **Page 11, Equation after Line 343:** `s` should be `μ`.
*   **Page 11, Equation (10):** `γ` should be `γ^(m)`.
*   **Page 11, Equation (11):** There is an extraneous comma.
*   **Page 12, Line 375:** "Lemma" should be "Theorem".
*   **Page 12 (last line) & Page 13 (first equation):** Parentheses appear to be missing.
*   **Page 13, Definition of xn:** `K^2` should be `K`.
*   **Page 13, Definition of yn:** The exponent `^{1/2}` should be `^{1/(ρ-2)}`.
*   **Page 13, Lines 388-389:** The sequence of logical implications seems incorrect; the result appears to be a more direct consequence of `ρ > 4`.
*   **Page 13, Equation (15):** The variables `x`, `y`, and `δ` should be indexed as `x_{n,δ}`, `y_{n,δ}`, and `δ_{n,δ}`.
*   **Page 14, Line 406:** `N` should be `log(N)`.
*   **Page 14, Equation after Line 406:** The inequality `N < 2n` should be `N > 2n`.
*   **Page 14, Equation before Line 408:** The `μ*` term should be removed, and the additive constants `+1` and `κ_k` are missing.
*   **Page 15:** The indices `p` and `m` are interchanged several times.
*   **Page 15, Line 432:** The opposite inequality should also be shown to hold.
*   **Page 15, Equation after Line 445:** The tilde accents (`~`) are missing on several variables.
*   **Page 15, Line 448:** The union symbol (`∪`) should be an intersection (`∩`).
*   **Page 15, Equation after Line 448:** The `^(l)` superscripts have been dropped.
*   **Page 16, Equation after Line 451:** The probability operator `P(...)` is missing from the rightmost term.
*   **Page 17, Line 454:** "Theorem" should be "Lemma".
*   **Section C:** "distribtuions" should be "distributions".

Secondly, the use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation in the main paper can be misleading, as these "constants" can hide important dependencies on algorithm parameters. For instance, in Theorem 2, a factor of `ρ` (a key parameter of the algorithm) is absorbed into the constant. Presenting the full, exact inequalities from the supplementary material directly in the main paper would significantly improve the clarity and precision of the stated results.

Finally, from a practical standpoint, it is not clear how readily this algorithm could be applied to real-world problems. The core strategy of MF-UCB—using low-accuracy pulls to quickly eliminate substantially suboptimal arms—could be sensitive to inaccurate estimations of the fidelity-accuracy relationship (i.e., the parameter `ζ`). The paper would benefit from a discussion on such practical limitations and potential strategies to overcome them, which would strengthen the work's connection to real-world applications.

In summary, this paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the multi-armed bandit literature. While the core ideas are strong and well-presented in the main text, the numerous errors in the supplementary material detract from the overall quality. I believe that with careful proofreading of the appendix and the inclusion of a discussion on practical limitations, this could be an excellent paper.
