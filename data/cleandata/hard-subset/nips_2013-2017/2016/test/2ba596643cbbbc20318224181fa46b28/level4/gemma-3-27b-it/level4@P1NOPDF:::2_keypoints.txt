- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with arms sampled at multiple fidelities and cost growing with accuracy, defining regret as cost times pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written, but most proofs in the supplementary materials are less polished.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and minor errors (author provides a detailed list) that should be corrected to improve readability.  
- The ubiquitous “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is misleading because some hidden “constants” depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); presenting exact inequalities would clarify results.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may be fragile to misestimation of fidelity parameters like ζ and needs discussion of limitations and remedies.  
- Theoretical contributions are valuable with interesting theorems and sound proofs; overall a significant and interesting contribution.
