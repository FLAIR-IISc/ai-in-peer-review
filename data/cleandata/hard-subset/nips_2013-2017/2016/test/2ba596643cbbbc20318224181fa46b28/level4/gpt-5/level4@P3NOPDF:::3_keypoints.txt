- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits with cheaper, biased lower-fidelity pulls and proposes MF-UCB to widen confidence intervals for bias and uncertainty.
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and synthetic simulations showing potential multi-fold improvements over high-fidelity-only UCB.

Strengths:
- Timely, natural formulation for settings with cheaper biased proxies (e.g., truncated training, coarser simulations).
- MF-UCB is conceptually simple, principled, and easy to implement (UCB augmented for fidelity-aware confidence).
- The paper supplies theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence that lower-fidelity data can reduce regret under suitable conditions.

Weaknesses/concerns:
- Modeling fidelity only as bias (not variance) is a strong assumption and is not convincingly justified for all motivating examples.
- Cumulative regret may be the wrong objective for some applications (algorithm selection); connection to practical goals is weak.
- Regret bounds are technically correct but messy and hard to interpret for practitioners.
- Experiments rely solely on synthetic settings with unexplained parameter choices and no sensitivity or robustness analyses.

Clarity and presentation:
- High-level intuition is useful but buried; a short worked two-arm, two-fidelity example and simplified bound would help.
- The paper should explicitly define bias-adjusted confidence intervals and succinctly state the fidelity-switching decision rule.

Questions for authors:
- How are bias bounds zeta^(m) obtained in practice, and what if they are misspecified?
- Can the analysis handle fidelity-dependent variances or modest violations of the bias-only model?
- Guidance on choosing the number of fidelities, costs, and bias targets; are there diminishing returns?
- Are there simple instance conditions where MF-UCB offers no benefit over high-fidelity UCB?
- How does computational overhead scale with M, and how should budgets be fairly compared?

Comments on experiments:
- Justify parameter choices (zeta^(m), cost ratios), include sensitivity analyses over bias, costs, and gaps.
- Report robustness to misspecified bias bounds and fidelity-dependent variances.
- Add a real or semi-real benchmark (e.g., early-stopping hyperparameter tuning) and clarify metrics (wall-clock cost vs. regret).
- Compare against elimination-style or best-arm identification baselines tailored to cost constraints.

Overall assessment:
- Formalizing multi-fidelity MAB and a clean UCB-style solution is an interesting, relevant contribution with careful theory.
- Practical impact is limited by opaque regret expressions, weak application links, and limited empirical validation.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide interpretable corollaries of regret bounds under stylized assumptions and rules of thumb for when MF-UCB helps.
- Clarify or empirically validate the bias-only fidelity model and discuss extensions to variance changes.
- Strengthen experiments with justified parameters, sensitivity studies, and at least one realistic case study.
- Discuss objective choice (regret vs. fixed-budget identification) and give practical guidance on estimating zeta^(m), tuning confidence inflation, and selecting fidelities.
