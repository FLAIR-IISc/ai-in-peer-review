- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm can be observed at M fidelities with cost λ(m) and biased estimates of the highest fidelity.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses low-fidelity observations to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms before expensive high-fidelity evaluations.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: an upper bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound showing near-optimal regret compared to standard UCB.
- Builds on MAB, UCB, multi-fidelity optimization, and budgeted bandits literature while formalizing a novel multi-fidelity bandit problem.
- Problem formulation is well-motivated with clear applications in online advertising and algorithm selection.
- Theoretical analysis is strong and rigorous, with mostly solid proofs.
- Main paper is clearly written and well-organized; algorithm design intuitively captures the cheap-versus-accurate trade-off.
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and errors (e.g., missing subscripts, incorrect notation/exponents) that reduce readability.
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden parameters (e.g., ρ) are not truly constant and should be clarified.
- Practical applicability is a concern: performance depends on estimating ζ(m) (bias between fidelities), but the paper lacks guidance on estimation or robustness to misspecification.
- Needs more discussion of practical limitations and potential solutions for real-world fidelity-bias estimation challenges.
- Quality: technically sound overall, but supplementary corrections are required.
- Clarity: main text clear; supplementary material clarity is poor due to errors.
- Originality: novel and meaningful extension of the standard bandit problem to multiple fidelities.
- Significance: addresses important resource-constrained learning problems and offers valuable insights for practitioners.
- Overall: strong paper with minor revisions (fix supplements, clarify constants, address ζ(m) estimation) would be an excellent fit for NIPS.
