- Paper proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework with per-arm fidelity levels and costs, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis and lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB — interesting but not ready for publication.
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s defined after first use and barely used thereafter).
- The advertising example is misleading by invoking non-stationarity, which is outside the paper’s scope; a different motivating example is recommended.
- The regret formulation is hard to justify: it favors the arm with highest mean regardless of cost, and enforced low-fidelity sampling can obscure true costs.
- A cost-complexity (sample-complexity) analysis may be more appropriate for multi-fidelity settings (e.g., prototype testing where low-fidelity tests are required).
- The manuscript is unwieldy and needs careful editing to streamline presentation.
