- Proposes the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit (MF-MAB) framework: arms can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with different costs and approximations to the high-fidelity distribution; offers an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, a lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB.

- Presentation and notation are unclear and scattered; key variables appear before definition and the writing is unwieldy, hindering comprehension.

- The chosen motivating example (online advertising) is misleading because it implies non-stationarity, which contradicts the paper’s stationary bandit setting.

- The regret metric is poorly justified for a cost-sensitive problem; optimizing cumulative reward ignores cost trade-offs and can penalize economically rational avoidance of expensive high-fidelity samples.

- The reviewer recommends reframing the problem as a cost-complexity or best-arm identification with a budget task (e.g., prototype testing), which better matches the multi-fidelity setting and practical objectives.

- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; invite revision that fixes clarity, replaces the motivating example, and adopts a more appropriate cost-aware theoretical framework.
