- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where higher fidelities give more accurate but costlier reward approximations.  
- Main insight: use cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve expensive high-fidelity evaluations for promising arms, improving over standard UCB that ignores fidelity costs.  
- Motivated by practical applications (e.g., online advertising) where short-duration tests approximate long-term behavior at lower cost.  
- Proposes a new regret definition, an algorithm, and provides theoretical guarantees including a regret lower bound.  
- The theoretical analysis is novel and well-executed.  
- Major concern: assumption that all ε^{(m)} (deviations of m-th fidelity means) are known a priori is unrealistic; authors should explain how to obtain or relax these values.
