- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where arms can be pulled at varying-cost, varying-bias fidelities and evaluation uses a cost-weighted regret under a capital budget.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that leverages cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with an upper bound on MF-UCB regret and a lower bound, showing near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Main paper is well-written and clearly motivates the problem and algorithm.
- Originality and significance: novel, well-motivated extension with clear real-world relevance (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection).
- Technical quality: elegant UCB extension, rigorous regret analysis, and inclusion of both upper and lower bounds.
- Clarity: core ideas and main body are well-organized and accessible.
- Weakness: supplementary material (proofs) has numerous typos and minor errors that hinder readability and verification.
- Weakness: asymptotic notation in main theorems hides dependencies (e.g., constants depending on algorithm parameter œÅ); main results should state explicit dependencies.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical robustness‚ÄîMF-UCB depends on known fidelity bias bounds Œ∂(m), and sensitivity to misspecification or adaptation strategies is not addressed.
- Overall assessment: significant and interesting contribution with strong core ideas despite appendix presentation issues and limited practical robustness discussion.
