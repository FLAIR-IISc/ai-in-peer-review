- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm has M fidelity levels trading cost for accuracy.
- Lower fidelities are modeled as cheaper but potentially biased estimates, with bias bounded by fidelity level.
- Proposes MF-UCB: an extension of UCB that widens confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.
- Provides theoretical regret bounds for MF-UCB that are complex and hard to interpret.
- Authors claim the bounds are often much better than always-playing-highest-fidelity UCB.
- Simulations show MF-UCB can yield roughly 3–4× improvement in regret in certain scenarios.
- Reviewer finds the bias-based modeling choice initially counterintuitive; variance-based alternatives seem plausible.
- Some motivating examples (e.g., algorithm selection) help justify bias modeling, but others (e.g., short ad display causing bias) are unconvincing.
- In the algorithm selection context, the reviewer questions why minimizing regret is the primary design objective.
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.
- Performance guarantees and simulation results are unclear—improvement may depend on chosen parameter values rather than being generally representative.
