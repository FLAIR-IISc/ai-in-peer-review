- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms have multiple fidelities with cheaper but biased lower-fidelity rewards.
- Formalizes a new regret definition that accounts for both evaluation costs and bias from lower-fidelity approximations.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive algorithm that uses low fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves high fidelities for promising arms.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees: Theorem 2 gives regret scaling ~λ^(m)/ψ(Δ^(m)_k) for arms at fidelity m, improving over standard UCB when lower fidelities are cheaper; Theorem 4 gives a near-optimal lower bound.
- Differentiates from prior work by explicitly modeling multiple fidelities per arm within the bandit regret framework and accounting for bias bounds |μ^(M)_k - μ^(m)_k| ≤ ζ^(m).
- Empirical results (Section 6) strongly support theory: MF-UCB consistently outperforms UCB on synthetic problems and visualizations show fidelity-aware allocation behavior.
- Main limitation: the analysis assumes known ζ^(m) bounds; the paper does not show how to estimate or adapt to unknown ζ^(m), which is important in practice.
- Paper notes ζ^(m) estimation as an open problem but should at least discuss potential approaches or test robustness to estimation errors.
- Lists nine specific minor technical/notation errors (e.g., H^(m)→K^(m); β^(k*,t) bound; γ→γ^(m); inequality directions; summation/index fixes; δ^(m)→δ^(M); μ*κλ typo; index l ranges; P(A_{n,3})→P(A_{n,2})) needing correction.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution with clear formulation, solid theory, and convincing experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix technical issues and discuss ζ^(m) estimation.
