- Problem: Studies stochastic K-armed bandits with M biased, costed fidelities and goal of minimizing cost-weighted pseudo-regret under budget Λ.  
- Contribution: Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm adjusting UCBs by fidelity bias ζ(m) and selecting fidelity via cost-information thresholds γ(m).  
- Theoretical results: Provides instance- and cost-dependent regret upper bound showing cheap fidelities eliminate many arms and costly pulls concentrate on near-optimal arms.  
- Lower bound: Gives a complementary lower bound proving near-optimality on many instances and identifying a gap when low-fidelity signals are only slightly informative.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on classic UCB and relates to budgeted bandits, weak/strong labelers, and multi-fidelity optimization, but formalizes biased multi-fidelity bandits.  
- Technical soundness: Algorithm and analysis appear principled and careful; arm partitioning K(m) clarifies cost allocation.  
- Key assumption: Assumption 1 (decay of ζ(m)) is important for clean comparisons to UCB but needs clearer interpretation and practical plausibility.  
- Known ζ: Analysis assumes known bias envelopes ζ(m); robustness to misspecification and adaptive estimation are not addressed.  
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments support theory but are limited in scope and lack key ablations and sensitivity analyses.  
- Regret definition concern: The chosen regret R(Λ)=Λµ*−Σ λ(mt) q_t deviates from standard additive cost-penalty objectives and needs broader justification and comparison.  
- Clarity issues: Presentation could better contrast regret/oracle with alternatives, and explain K(m) via concrete two- or three-fidelity examples and tables.  
- Baseline clarity: The baseline should be explicitly stated as “UCB using only the highest fidelity.”  
- Assumption exposition: The role and consequences of Assumption 1 should be highlighted where used and discussed if it fails.  
- Lower-bound gap question: The gap for arms with small positive low-fidelity gaps suggests potential algorithmic improvements or heuristics for safe early elimination.  
- Practical suggestions: Consider subsequence selection/pruning of fidelities, and propose robustness measures (e.g., inflating ζ adaptively) for practical use.  
- Empirical suggestions: Add sensitivity to (ζ, λ), compare to two-phase low-to-high-fidelity baselines, and test non-sub-Gaussian noise.  
- Presentation fixes: Add worked example tracing K(1), K(2), state baseline explicitly, and include a plain-language interpretation of Theorem 2.  
- Notation/typos: Define JkK on first use, use curly braces for sets, ensure consistent use of Δ(m) and K(m), and fix minor typographical issues.  
- Strengths: Well-motivated formalism, simple scalable algorithm, instance- and cost-dependent guarantees, and clear conditions when multi-fidelity helps.  
- Weaknesses: Regret notion and assumptions may limit applicability, known-ζ requirement, empirical scope limited, and lower-bound gap leaves potential suboptimality.  
- Significance: Practically relevant setting (advertising, sim-to-real), with potential to seed extensions (contextual, nonstationary, GP-based).  
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses important problem, provides clean algorithm and analysis, and identifies open theoretical questions.  
- Rejection concerns: Dense presentation, limited experiments, and unresolved lower-bound gap that may indicate suboptimality on some instances.
