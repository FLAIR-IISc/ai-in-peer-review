- Strong, relevant model: studies multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelity levels (known ζm, cheaper lower fidelities, highest fidelity ζ=0), proposes a net-reward objective, a new algorithm, and provides a regret lower bound.  
- Explanations are insufficient: the adopted regret definition (multiplicative penalty for resource use) seems unnatural; an additive cost model would be more intuitive and should be analyzed.  
- Assumption 1 is opaque and seemingly artificial—constraints on ζm are unexplained and may not hold in practice.  
- No method given for selecting a subsequence of fidelities if ζm do not satisfy Assumption 1; such a principled selection would strengthen the work.  
- Authors’ claim that Assumption 1 is noncritical is contradicted by its use in proofs; unclear whether the algorithm is overly tailored to certain ζ configurations.  
- The lower bound has a gap that requires further investigation and might necessitate redesigning the proposed strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and MF-UCB vs. UCB comparison are hard to interpret: dependence on Δ, [[k]], and the sets K^(m) is technical and needs clearer definitions, intuition, and concrete examples.  
- Overall: appealing model but clarity and presentation of main results are lacking.  
- Minor typos: l.56 “near-optimal”; l.126 remove period before “for all m < M”; l.179 use curly braces to define [[k]].
