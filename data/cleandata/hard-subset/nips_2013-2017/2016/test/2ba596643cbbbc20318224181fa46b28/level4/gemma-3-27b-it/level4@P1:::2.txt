This work introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be evaluated with different accuracies (or fidelities). The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as a function of this cost and the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm to address this setting, MF-UCB, and provide upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm. The main paper is clear and well-written. However, this is not the case for the supplementary materials, where most of the proofs are located. They contain numerous typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader (see below for a non-exhaustive list). Correcting these errors would greatly increase the readability of the paper. Additionally, I think the “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” signs used in the paper can be somewhat misleading, as the constants are not truly constant; for instance, in Theorem 2, there is a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden within the constant. Presenting the exact inequalities (which are written in the supplementary materials) could improve the clarity of the results. From a practical point of view, it is not immediately clear how easily this algorithm can be applied to real-world problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB (using low-accuracy evaluations to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms) could be sensitive to inaccurate estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). I believe that a discussion of practical limitations, and potential methods to overcome them, would benefit this work. From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides valuable insights into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I think that this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
