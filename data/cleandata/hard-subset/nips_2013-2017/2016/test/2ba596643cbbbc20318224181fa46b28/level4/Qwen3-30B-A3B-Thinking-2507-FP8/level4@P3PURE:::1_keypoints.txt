- Paper studies a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit, proposing MF-UCB to maximize net reward with fidelity-dependent costs and noise bounds—an interesting and relevant model for online learning.  
- Regret/net-reward definition penalizes cost multiplicatively (reward × (1−cost)), which appears unnatural; additive penalty (reward − cost) is more standard and should be considered.  
- Authors must clarify whether an additive cost model changes problem formulation or their results, and justify the choice of multiplicative penalty beyond the ad-display example.  
- Assumption 1 imposes constraints on fidelity error parameters {ζ_m} that seem artificial and lack general justification; its necessity is unclear despite being used in proofs.  
- No guidance is provided for cases where ζ_m decays slower than required—how to select a subsequence of fidelities to preserve results is not addressed.  
- The algorithm appears tailored to specific ζ_m sequences without justification for why those sequences are appropriate or realistic.  
- The lower bound analysis has a gap that requires further investigation and might force redesign of the proposed strategy.  
- Theorem 2 (MF-UCB regret bound) and its comparison to UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and implicit assumptions (e.g., UCB at highest fidelity).  
- Relative performance depends on Δ and index sets [k] derived from complex sets K^(m); the paper needs clearer, explicit explanation of these dependencies and expected gains.  
- Overall: promising model and benchmark potential, but paper loses clarity in results presentation and assumption justification.  
- Minor corrections: fix "near-optimal" consistency (Line 56), remove period before "for all m < M" (Line 126), and use curly braces for set notation (Line 179).
