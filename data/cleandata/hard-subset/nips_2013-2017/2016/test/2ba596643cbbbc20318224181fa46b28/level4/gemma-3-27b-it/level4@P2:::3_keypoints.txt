- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation where lower fidelities are cheaper but produce biased reward estimates.  
- Models fidelity-induced error as bias (bounded by ζ^(m)) rather than variance, which initially seems counterintuitive.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm adapted to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB can outperform standard high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Regret bounds are complex and difficult to interpret, offering limited practical insight.  
- Simulation results show improvements (often 3–4×) over standard UCB but may depend on specific parameter choices.  
- Choices of simulation parameters are not well-justified, raising concerns about result generalizability and robustness.  
- Motivating examples: algorithm selection in ML supports bias model, but the online advertising example is less convincing.  
- The link between minimizing regret and an algorithm designer’s objective in the ML context is unclear.  
- Strengths: novel problem formulation, sensible algorithm extension, rigorous theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Weaknesses: weak application connections for some domains, hard-to-interpret regret bounds, questionable simulation parameter selection, and incomplete bias-vs-variance rationale.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important novel problem, well-motivated algorithm, solid theory, and promising empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: tenuous application ties, limited practical usefulness of complex bounds, and inadequate simulation justification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with revisions to make regret bounds more intuitive and to better justify simulation parameter choices.
