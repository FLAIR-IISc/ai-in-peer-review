This work introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be evaluated with different fidelities, incurring costs proportional to the accuracy of the evaluation. The authors define a regret measure that incorporates these costs and propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, along with upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret. The paper is generally clear and well-written, though the supplementary material suffers from numerous minor errors that detract from readability (detailed list below). Addressing these errors would significantly improve the manuscript. The use of “up-to-a-constant” notation can also be misleading, as the hidden constants often depend on algorithmic parameters like ρ. Presenting the more precise inequalities found in the supplementary material would enhance clarity. From a practical standpoint, the applicability of MF-UCB to real-world problems is questionable. The performance relies on accurate estimation of ζ, the fidelity accuracy, and inaccurate estimations could hinder the algorithm's effectiveness. A discussion of potential limitations and mitigation strategies would strengthen the work. Theoretically, this paper offers valuable insights into the problem, presenting interesting theorems supported by solid proofs. Overall, this is a significant and worthwhile contribution to the field.
