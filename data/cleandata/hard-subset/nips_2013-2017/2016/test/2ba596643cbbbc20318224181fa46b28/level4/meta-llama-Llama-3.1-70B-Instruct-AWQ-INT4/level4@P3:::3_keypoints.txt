- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit formulation where each arm can be sampled at multiple fidelity/cost levels with fidelity-dependent bias and variance.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that inflates confidence widths to account for bias from low-fidelity samples.  
- Provides clear and concise problem formulation, assumptions, objectives, and related-work positioning.  
- Delivers theoretical analysis of MF-UCB including a regret upper bound and a matching lower bound.  
- The regret bound is complex and hard to interpret, though key influencing factors are explained.  
- Simulation results on synthetic tasks show MF-UCB reduces regret relative to standard UCB.  
- Simulation parameter choices are not fully justified, leaving representativeness of results unclear.  
- The connection between the model and motivating real-world applications is not sufficiently explained.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a thorough technical treatment overall.  
- Contribution is significant: new problem formulation and an effective algorithmic solution.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, solid analysis (upper and lower bounds), and empirical gains.  
- Rejection arguments: interpretability of regret bounds, unclear simulation parameter validity, and limited application grounding.
