- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be pulled at varying fidelity with cost increasing with accuracy.  
- Defines regret as the product of pull cost and the standard pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written.  
- Supplementary materials are poorly presented with numerous typos and minor errors that could confuse readers; fixing them would improve readability.  
- Use of "up to constant factors" is potentially misleading because those constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., Theorem 2 hides a factor of ρ).  
- Recommends using exact inequalities (available in the supplement) to improve clarity of results.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may fail if ζ (fidelity accuracy) is estimated inaccurately.  
- Suggests adding a discussion of practical limitations and possible mitigation strategies.  
- Theoretical contribution is valuable with interesting theorems and solid proofs.  
- Overall assessment: significant and interesting contribution.
