- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity M-armed bandit framework with M fidelities where higher fidelities give more accurate rewards at higher cost.  
- Suggests using cheap, low-fidelity plays to eliminate many suboptimal arms and reserving expensive, high-fidelity plays for promising arms to outperform standard UCB.  
- Notes practical application (e.g., online advertising: short displays to cheaply estimate long-term CTRs).  
- Contributions: introduces a new regret definition tailored to fidelities.  
- Contributions: presents an algorithm with theoretical analysis and a regret lower bound.  
- Contributions: validates the approach via simulations.  
- Strength: theoretical analysis is innovative.  
- Major concern: the analysis assumes known deviation parameters ε^(m) between fidelities; unclear how these would be obtained in practice and whether the assumption can be relaxed.
