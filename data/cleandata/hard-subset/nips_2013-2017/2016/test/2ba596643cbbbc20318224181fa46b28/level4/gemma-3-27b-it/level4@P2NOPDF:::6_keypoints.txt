- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelity levels where higher fidelities give more accurate but costlier reward estimates.
- Motivates the setting with online advertising: short-duration ad displays as low-cost proxies for long-term CTRs.
- Core idea: use low-cost, low-fidelity evaluations to rapidly eliminate suboptimal arms and devote expensive high-fidelity evaluations to promising arms, exploiting the fidelity–cost trade-off.
- Claims potential to outperform standard algorithms (e.g., UCB) that ignore fidelity-cost structure.
- Proposes a new regret definition tailored to the fidelity setting, an algorithm that leverages fidelity structure, and provides theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound.
- The theoretical analysis is novel and represents the paper’s main contribution.
- Major limitation: the algorithm assumes the deviations ε^(m) between fidelity m and highest fidelity are known a priori, which harms practical applicability.
- Recommendation: evaluate robustness to inaccurate ε^(m) and/or develop methods to estimate or relax this assumption (e.g., initial exploration phase).
- Experimental evaluation supports theory but needs more extensive comparisons with other bandit algorithms and fuller details on simulation setup and parameter choices for reproducibility.
