- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where pull costs increase with fidelity and regret is cost-weighted.
- Proposes a new algorithm, MF-UCB, designed for this multi-fidelity setting.
- Provides a comprehensive upper-bound proof on MF-UCB’s cumulative pseudo-regret.
- Derives a lower bound via a change-of-measure argument as a benchmark for algorithm performance.
- Simulation results on synthetic problems show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB across considered cases.
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and uses figures/tables effectively to illustrate concepts and results.
- Theoretical analysis is solid and proofs (especially for the upper bound) are comprehensive.
- Supplementary materials contain minor errors and typos in the proofs that need correction.
- Practical applicability is unclear—MF-UCB may suffer from inaccurate fidelity estimation and lacks discussion of real-world limitations.
- Overall contribution is significant and interesting for the multi-armed bandit literature.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to fix supplementary errors and add discussion on practical limitations.
- Reviewer scores: Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Quality 9/10.
