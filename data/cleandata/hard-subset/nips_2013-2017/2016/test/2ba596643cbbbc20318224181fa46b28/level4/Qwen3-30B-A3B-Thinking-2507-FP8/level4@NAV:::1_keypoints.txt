- Models multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity choices: fidelity m yields rewards within ζ_m of the true mean, lower fidelities cost less, highest fidelity has ζ=0.
- Proposes MF-UCB to maximize net reward, claiming it outperforms UCB at highest fidelity, and provides a regret lower bound.
- Tackles a relevant low-information/cheap vs high-information/expensive tradeoff and could serve as a useful benchmark.
- Technically sound but explanations of results and assumptions are insufficient.
- Regret definition penalizes cost multiplicatively, which seems unnatural; additive cost penalties are more general than the ad-display motivation given.
- Necessity and generality of Assumption 1 (constraints on ζ_m) are unclear and seemingly essential in proofs despite claims otherwise.
- If ζ_m decays too slowly, the paper lacks a procedure to select a valid fidelity subsequence to preserve results.
- No justification given for the specific ζ_m sequences used in the algorithm.
- The lower-bound gap requires further investigation and might necessitate redesigning the strategy.
- Theorem 2 (MF-UCB regret bound) is difficult to interpret relative to high-fidelity UCB because performance depends on complex parameters Δ and 𝒦^(m).
- Overall: promising model but the presentation and exposition need substantial clarification.
- Minor typos: Line 56 "near-optimal"; Line 126 extra period before "for all m < M"; Line 179 use curly braces for set notation defining 𝒦.
