This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be sampled with varying accuracies (or fidelities). The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost and the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, to address this setting and provide both an upper and a lower bound on its cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well-written. However, this clarity does not extend to the supplementary materials, which contain the majority of the proofs. These proofs include numerous typos and minor, easily correctable errors that impede readability (see below for a non-exhaustive list). Correcting these errors would significantly improve the paper’s accessibility. 

Furthermore, the “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation used throughout the paper can be somewhat misleading, as the constants are not truly constant. For instance, Theorem 2 includes a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden within the constant. Presenting the exact inequalities, as they are written in the supplementary materials, would enhance the clarity of the results.

From a practical perspective, the applicability of this algorithm to real-world problems is not immediately clear. The MF-UCB strategy, which relies on low-accuracy pulls to efficiently eliminate suboptimal arms, could be susceptible to inaccuracies in the estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion of these practical limitations and potential strategies to mitigate them would strengthen this work. 

From a theoretical standpoint, this paper provides valuable insight into the problem, along with interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I believe this work represents a significant and interesting contribution.
