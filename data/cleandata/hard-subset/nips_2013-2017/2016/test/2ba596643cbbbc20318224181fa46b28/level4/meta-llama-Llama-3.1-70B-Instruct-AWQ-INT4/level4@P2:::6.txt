This paper studies a new multi-armed bandit setting, where the learner can choose to play an arm at one of $M$ fidelities, each providing a better approximation to the rewards but expending larger cost. The authors propose a novel algorithm, Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB), that adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs, achieving better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations. The paper also provides a lower bound on the regret and shows that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem setting is relevant to many applications, such as online advertising. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The theoretical analysis is novel and provides a thorough understanding of the algorithm's performance.

However, there are some minor issues with the paper. The authors assume that the deviations of the mth fidelity mean from the one of the highest fidelity are known in advance. It is unclear how these values can be obtained in practice, such as in the application to online advertising. Relaxing this assumption would make the algorithm more practical.

Additionally, there are some minor errors in the paper. For example, on line 326, 357, and 386, 'H' should be 'K'. On line 343, the argument "s" should be \mu{\ast}. On line 345, \gamma should be \gamma^{(m)}. On line 346-347, the two '\leq's should be '\geq's. On line 384, the summation over all k \in those "above" K^{(m)} should be over all k \in those "below" K^{(m)}. On line 407, \delta^{(m)} should be \delta^{(M)}, and the last term \mu{\ast} \kappa \lambda^{(m) } should be \kappa \lambda^{(m)}. On line 431, "for l \ge m" should be "for l \ge p". On line 440, in eq(17), "l \geq m" should be "l \geq p". On line 450, P(A{n,3}) should be P(A{n,2}).

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithm. With some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
