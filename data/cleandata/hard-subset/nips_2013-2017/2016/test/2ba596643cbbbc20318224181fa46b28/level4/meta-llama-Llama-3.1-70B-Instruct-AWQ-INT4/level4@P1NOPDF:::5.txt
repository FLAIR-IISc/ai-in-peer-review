This paper presents a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem. They provide theoretical results on regret bounds and perform simulations to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity. The presentation is clear and easy to understand. The simulations effectively demonstrate the impact of the multi-fidelity bandit method. Furthermore, the authors provide sufficient theoretical analysis on the lower bound of the proposed method, which highlights its theoretical improvement.
