- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be pulled at variable accuracies with costs increasing with fidelity.  
- Defines regret as the product of pull cost and standard pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm tailored to this multi-fidelity bandit setting.  
- Provides theoretical results: upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typographical and notation errors that hinder readability (detailed list provided by reviewer).  
- Use of the phrase "up to a multiplicative constant" is misleading because constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); explicit inequalities should be reported.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may be sensitive to misestimation of fidelity parameter ζ and needs discussion of limitations and mitigations.  
- Theoretical contributions are valuable, well-motivated, and rigorously proven; overall a significant and interesting contribution to bandit literature.
