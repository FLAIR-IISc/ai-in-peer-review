- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit where each arm can be queried at M fidelities with known bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m), and defines cost-aware regret under a capital budget Λ.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: bias-corrected per-fidelity UCBs that estimate the highest-fidelity mean, selects arms by lower UCB and fidelities via thresholds γ(m) trading bias vs cost.  
- Theoretical upper bounds show cheap fidelities concentrate expensive queries on few near-optimal arms, giving improvements over single-fidelity UCB; a lower bound shows near-optimality in certain regimes.  
- Empirical simulations on synthetic problems corroborate the theoretical gains.  
- Positions work relative to UCB, multi-fidelity optimization, budgeted bandits, weak/strong labeler active learning, and multi-fidelity RL, highlighting use of structured fidelity approximations.  
- Strength — Originality: formalizes multi-fidelity bandits with biased costed approximations and a principled highest-fidelity regret notion.  
- Strength — Quality: MF-UCB is natural and well-analyzed; upper bound decomposes by fidelity and lower bound isolates necessary regimes; partition K(m) and γ(m) design are insightful.  
- Strength — Significance: framework models practical settings (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection) and shows concrete gains when cheap fidelities can eliminate many arms.  
- Strength — Clarity: organized presentation with clear algorithmic description and intuitive notions like fidelity information caps via ζ(m).  
- Weakness — Assumes known ζ(m): lacks guidance on obtaining or estimating bias bounds in practice and on robustness to misspecification.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1: needs discussion on realism and consequences if fidelity staging regularity fails (e.g., similar costs/biases).  
- Weakness — Regret definition: always crediting highest-fidelity mean may not suit all applications; more discussion on alternatives and their algorithmic/analytical impact is needed.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: dependence on γ(m), ψ, λ(m), and tuning ρ needs empirical sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — Empirics: experiments are synthetic only; needs semi-synthetic/real-case studies and comparisons to other baselines (e.g., Thompson sampling variants).  
- Weakness — Gap to lower bound for some K(m): small but positive lower-fidelity gaps leave a regret gap; heuristics to cheaply validate suboptimality could be explored.  
- Pros for acceptance: timely, well-scoped, strong theoretical contributions, clear intuition, and empirical alignment with theory.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on known ζ(m) and Assumption 1, limited empirical validation, and some notational/presentation issues.  
- Suggestions — add methods or guidance to estimate ζ(m) from pilot data or design an adaptive/robust MF-UCB that learns upper bounds online.  
- Suggestions — provide sensitivity experiments for mis-specified ζ(m), λ(m), ψ, and tuning parameter ρ to assess robustness.  
- Suggestions — include a semi-synthetic or real-case study and broaden baseline comparisons (e.g., TS variants).  
- Suggestions — expand discussion on alternative regret notions where low-fidelity outcomes have different utility or risk.  
- Minor typos to fix: multiple notation errors and indices (Lines 326/357/386, 343, 345, 346–347, 384, 407, 431, 440, 450) as listed by reviewer.  
- Overall evaluation: strong quality, good clarity, high originality, solid significance; recommendation to accept after addressing practical ζ(m) issues and minor corrections.
