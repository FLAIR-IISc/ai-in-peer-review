

The authors propose a novel framework termed Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandits, where arms can be sampled at varying fidelity levels with associated costs. Lower-fidelity samples approximate the highest-fidelity distribution at reduced cost. They introduce an adaptive algorithm, provide regret analysis, a problem-specific lower bound, and synthetic simulations demonstrating superior performance over UCB. While the framework holds potential for impact upon deeper development, the current submission is unfit for publication. Key concerns are:  

1.  **Confusing Notation:** Definitions are scattered and difficult to locate. For instance, the symbol $s$ (used early on) is defined only one page later and subsequently appears infrequently, causing significant confusion.  
2.  **Misleading Advertising Example:** The advertising application introduces irrelevant concepts like correlations between ad display time and non-stationarity, which are outside the paper's scope. A more appropriate example should replace this.  
3.  **Flawed Regret Justification:** The regret analysis is difficult to justify. Regret minimization inherently requires maximizing long-term gain, i.e., selecting the arm with the best reward-to-cost ratio. However, the optimal policy here is simply to select the arm with the highest mean reward, regardless of cost. The framework's constraint of *always* sampling the best arm at lower fidelity obscures the true cost of low-fidelity sampling when it is unnecessary.  
4.  **Missing Cost Complexity Analysis:** A sample complexity analysis (more accurately, cost complexity) is essential for this setting. It would directly address practical scenarios like prototype testing in product development, where the goal is to identify the best product with high probability while minimizing cumulative test costs before mass production. Companies often face regulatory, contractual, or facility constraints mandating the use of low-fidelity approximations prior to costly high-fidelity testing.  
5.  **Awkward Wording:** The text contains several convoluted passages. A thorough revision to improve flow and clarity is strongly recommended.
