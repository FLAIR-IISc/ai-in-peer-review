- Novel framework: multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit where arms can be sampled at different fidelity levels with associated costs and low-fidelity samples approximate high-fidelity distributions.  
- Proposed method: adaptive algorithm that handles sequences of approximations and costs, with regret analysis and a lower bound.  
- Empirical claim: synthetic simulations show the proposed algorithm outperforms UCB.  
- Overall verdict: interesting and potentially impactful, but currently unsuitable for publication.  
- Notation/organization issue: notation is scattered and confusing (e.g., variable s defined after first use and then rarely used).  
- Motivating example issue: online advertising example is misleading—ties to display time and non-stationarity are outside the paper’s scope.  
- Regret formulation concern: focusing on identifying the arm with highest mean (regardless of cost) is questionable and may obscure the cost impact of low-fidelity sampling.  
- Suggested alternative analysis: a sample-/cost-complexity framework (minimize testing cost to identify best arm with high probability) would be more relevant for practical multi-fidelity settings.  
- Writing quality: parts of the text are unwieldy and need streamlining for clarity and conciseness.  
- Pros summary: novel problem formulation, algorithm with theoretical guarantees, empirical improvement over baseline.  
- Cons summary: confusing notation/organization, questionable motivating example, unconvincing regret justification, missing cost-complexity analysis, and overall writing deficiencies.
