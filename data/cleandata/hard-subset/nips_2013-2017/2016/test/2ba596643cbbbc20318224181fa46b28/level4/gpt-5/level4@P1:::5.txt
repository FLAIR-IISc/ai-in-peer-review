Summary
The paper studies a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit in which each arm can be evaluated at multiple fidelities: lower-fidelity observations are cheaper but biased approximations of the highest-fidelity reward. The authors formalize this multi-fidelity setting with fidelity-dependent costs and define an appropriate cost-weighted notion of regret. They propose MF-UCB, an upper confidence bound algorithm designed to exploit cheap low-fidelity evaluations to quickly eliminate poor arms while reserving expensive high-fidelity plays for promising ones. Theoretical results provide regret bounds showing that MF-UCB improves over naive strategies that ignore lower fidelities. The paper also presents a complementary lower bound, demonstrating that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions. Simulations illustrate the benefits of the multi-fidelity approach.

Evaluation

Quality
- The paper provides theoretical regret bounds for the proposed algorithm and includes a lower bound analysis. The lower bound supports the claim that MF-UCB is near-optimal, highlighting the theoretical improvement over strategies that only use the highest fidelity.
- The simulations substantiate the theoretical claims by demonstrating the effect of leveraging lower fidelities to reduce regret.

Clarity
- The presentation is easy to follow. The problem setup, the algorithmic idea of using lower fidelities for elimination, and the connection to cost-weighted regret are clearly explained.
- The empirical section clearly demonstrates the effect of the multi-fidelity method.

Originality
- The paper formalizes a multi-fidelity bandit framework in which lower fidelities provide biased but informative approximations, coupled with fidelity-dependent costs. Within the bandit literature, this is a distinct setting compared to standard K-armed bandits and budgeted bandits.
- The MF-UCB algorithm adapts the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty paradigm to the multi-fidelity context in a natural and novel way.

Significance
- By exploiting cheap, biased approximations, MF-UCB can substantially reduce the number of expensive high-fidelity evaluations required. The theoretical analysis (upper and lower bounds) and simulations together indicate that this approach can deliver meaningful improvements in regret compared to approaches that ignore multi-fidelity information.
- The framework is motivated by relevant applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection), suggesting practical impact.

Overall assessment
This paper provides a clear and well-motivated formalization of multi-fidelity bandits, proposes a simple and effective algorithm (MF-UCB), and backs it with both regret upper bounds and a lower bound that together demonstrate near-optimality. The simulations further show the practical effect of the approach. The writing is clear and the contributions are well presented.
