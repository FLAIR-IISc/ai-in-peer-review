- Models multi-armed bandits with selectable finite fidelities per pull; lower fidelities are cheaper and produce i.i.d. rewards within known ζ_m of the true mean, highest fidelity has ζ=0.  
- Authors formulate sequential arm+fidelity selection, introduce an MF-UCB algorithm, and provide an upper regret bound plus a lower bound.  
- Strength: tackles a relevant cost–information–reward tradeoff in online learning and could serve as a useful benchmark.  
- Main weakness: explanations of results and underlying assumptions are insufficient and the presentation lacks clarity.  
- Regret definition is unintuitive—penalizing cost multiplicatively is questionable; additive cost/regret formulation may be more natural.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on known ζ_m) appears artificial and opaque; its general applicability is unclear.  
- No guidance on handling ζ_m sequences that don’t satisfy Assumption 1 (e.g., selecting a subsequence to meet the assumption).  
- Algorithm seems tailored to specific ζ configurations without justification for those being the “right” choices.  
- The gap in the provided lower bound needs more investigation and possible refinement of the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and comparisons to UCB are hard to interpret because they depend on Δ and the intricate sequence of sets 𝒦^(m); a more explicit explanation is needed.  
- Minor typos: l56 “near-optimal”; l126 unnecessary period before “for all m < M”; l179 use curly braces for [[k]] (e.g., {k}).
