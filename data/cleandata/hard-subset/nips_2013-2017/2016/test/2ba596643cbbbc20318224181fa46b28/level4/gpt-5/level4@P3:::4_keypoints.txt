- Summary: Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits (MF-MAB) with fidelity-dependent costs and bias bounds, defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret evaluated at high fidelity, and proposes MF-UCB with per-fidelity UCBs and fidelity-switching thresholds γ(m).  
- Theoretical claims: MF-UCB uses cheap fidelities to eliminate bad arms, concentrates high-fidelity pulls on near-optimal arms, has a regret upper bound improving over high-fidelity-only UCB, and a near-matching lower bound under assumptions.  
- Empirics: Synthetic simulations align with theory, showing concentration of high-fidelity budget on promising arms.  

Strengths:
- Novel, well-motivated formulation modeling biased cheaper approximations distinct from budgeted bandits and variance-only models.  
- Simple, principled adaptation of UCB with interpretable fidelity-switching thresholds balancing cost and information.  
- Nontrivial analysis: arm partitioning by fidelity, cost-sensitive regret upper bound, and informative lower bound.  
- Empirical results corroborate theoretical trends.

Weaknesses / suggestions:
- Clarity/organization: notation introduced piecemeal (e.g., s) — recommend a consolidated notation table and clearer exposition of regret and qt.  
- Examples/motivation: online-ad example implies non-stationarity not modeled; suggest revising examples or adding caveats and using engineering/robotics simulators instead.  
- Objective/regret notion: justify and position the high-fidelity-evaluated pseudo-regret versus budgeted MAB and pure-exploration, and consider sketching a cost-complexity identification analysis.  
- Assumptions/practicality: algorithm assumes known ζ(m) and fast-decay assumption; discuss estimating ζ(m), robustness to misspecification, and what if assumptions fail; provide guidance and sensitivity analysis for ρ and γ(m).  
- Empirical evaluation: limited scope — add ablations on ζ(m), λ(m), M; stronger baselines (e.g., fixed low-fidelity pulls); and per-fidelity play distributions to mirror theory.

Quality:
- Technical development is careful with meaningful regret analysis and informative lower bound, but presentation and empirical breadth weaken the contribution.

Clarity:
- Readable in parts but unwieldy; consolidate notation, streamline introduction, and add intuition for γ(m) and arm partitions.

Originality:
- Setting and analysis are original relative to UCB and budgeted MAB, explicitly modeling biased approximations with known bounds.

Significance:
- Potential impact for staged cost-tiered evaluations; theory is solid but needs broader empirical validation.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Timely, practical formulation with rigorous analysis, simple implementable algorithm, and clear theoretical improvement over naïve high-fidelity UCB.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Presentation and notation issues reduce accessibility; objective needs stronger justification versus alternatives; practical aspects (ζ estimation, tuning, robustness) under-discussed; experiments limited to synthetic data and lack ablations/baselines.

Overall assessment:
- Interesting, potentially impactful paper with solid theory but not yet meeting conference bar due to clarity, empirical limitations, and insufficient justification of the regret objective; recommend revision and resubmission after addressing major concerns.
