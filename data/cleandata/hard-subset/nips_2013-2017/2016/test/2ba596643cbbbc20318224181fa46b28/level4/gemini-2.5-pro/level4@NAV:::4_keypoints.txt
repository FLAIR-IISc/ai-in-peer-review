1. Novel Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework and algorithm with cost-aware sampling; shows improved empirical performance over UCB but not yet ready for publication.

2. Notation and definitions are poorly organized and scattered, causing confusion (e.g., variable s used before definition).

3. Advertising motivating example is misleading (invokes non-stationarity and temporal correlations) and does not fit the paper's stationary-bandit setting.

4. Regret formulation is inadequately justified: optimal policy ignores sampling costs and may misrepresent cost–reward trade-offs.

5. Cost-complexity (cost-to-identify best arm) is a more natural objective for many multi-fidelity applications (e.g., prototype testing) than the current regret view.

6. Manuscript needs a thorough revision for clarity and conciseness; several sections are hard to follow and should be streamlined.
