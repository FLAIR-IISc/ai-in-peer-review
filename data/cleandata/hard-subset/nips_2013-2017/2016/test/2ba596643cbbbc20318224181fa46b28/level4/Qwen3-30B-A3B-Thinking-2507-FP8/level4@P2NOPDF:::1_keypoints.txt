- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem balancing cost, information quality, and reward.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and claims it outperforms a high-fidelity UCB, supported by a regret lower bound.
- Problem formulation is relevant and could serve as a useful benchmark for online learning.
- Major clarity and justification gaps undermine the technical contribution.
- Regret definition uses a multiplicative cost penalty that seems unnatural; an additive cost penalty is more standard and should be considered.
- Authors must justify why multiplicative cost is preferable and analyze the additive case.
- Assumption 1 (constraints on ζₘ decay) is arbitrary, lacks justification, and is crucial in proofs despite claims otherwise.
- No method is provided to handle cases where ζₘ decays slower or to select a valid subsequence of fidelities, weakening applicability.
- Theorem 2’s regret bound is overly technical and hard to interpret due to complex parameters and nested sets.
- Comparison to UCB is unclear (UCB fidelity not explicitly specified) and needs a more intuitive, structural analysis of gains.
- The gap in the lower bound requires deeper analysis and may indicate the strategy needs revision.
- Minor issues: typos/notation problems (Line 56 "near-optimal", Line 126 extra period, Line 179 curly braces for sets).
- Recommended fixes: justify or provide fallback for Assumption 1; compare additive vs. multiplicative costs; simplify Theorem 2 and UCB comparison; clarify role of fidelity sequences in design.
