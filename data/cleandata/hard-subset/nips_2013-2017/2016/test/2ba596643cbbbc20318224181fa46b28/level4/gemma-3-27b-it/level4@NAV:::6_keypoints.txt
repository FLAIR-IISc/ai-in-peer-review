1) Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model with M fidelity levels where higher fidelities give more accurate but costlier reward estimates.  
2) Proposes an algorithm that uses low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate poor arms and reserves high-fidelity evaluations for promising arms, aiming to outperform standard UCB-like methods.  
3) Presents online advertising as a motivating application (low-cost initial displays to estimate long-term CTRs).  
4) Defines an appropriate regret metric for the multi-fidelity setting and provides a corresponding algorithm.  
5) Offers theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound and supports claims with simulation results—analysis is a novel contribution.  
6) Major concern: the algorithm assumes advance knowledge of ε^(m) (the deviation of mean reward at fidelity m from highest fidelity); practicality and methods to relax this assumption need discussion.
