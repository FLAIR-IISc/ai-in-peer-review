- Proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit (MF-MAB) framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with costs; presents an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations outperforming UCB.  
- Framework is promising and potentially impactful but the paper is not ready for acceptance in its current form.  
- Notation and presentation are scattered and unclear (e.g., variable s used before definition); need a centralized notation table or earlier consolidated definitions.  
- The advertising motivating example is misleading by invoking non-stationarity and correlations that conflict with the paper’s (apparently) stationary model; replace with a scenario matching assumptions.  
- Regret formulation is questionable: benchmark ignores cost (uses highest-fidelity mean), and algorithm’s forced low-fidelity sampling of best arm can mask true exploration cost; need justification or a cost-aware benchmark.  
- A cost/sample-complexity objective (e.g., best-arm identification under budget) may be more natural for MF-MAB; product prototyping is a compelling application example.  
- Writing quality and organization are unwieldy; require thorough editing to streamline, remove redundancy, and introduce assumptions/notation before use.  
- Recommendations: (i) reorganize and clarify notation and assumptions, (ii) replace the advertising example with a stationary-consistent example, (iii) justify the current regret or adopt a cost-aware regret or cost-complexity objective.
