- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (M-armed) setting where higher fidelities give more accurate but costlier reward estimates.
- Proposes a strategy that uses cheap, low fidelities to eliminate many suboptimal arms and reserves expensive, high fidelities for promising arms, outperforming standard UCB that ignores fidelity structure.
- Motivated by applications like online advertising where short displays approximate long-run performance at lower cost.
- Defines a new fidelity-tailored regret notion, proposes an algorithm, provides theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound, and shows simulation results.
- Analysis is claimed novel.

- Main concern: analysis assumes the fidelity deviations ε^(m) (difference between fidelity-m mean and highest-fidelity mean) are known a priori; question practicality in applications and whether this assumption can be relaxed.
