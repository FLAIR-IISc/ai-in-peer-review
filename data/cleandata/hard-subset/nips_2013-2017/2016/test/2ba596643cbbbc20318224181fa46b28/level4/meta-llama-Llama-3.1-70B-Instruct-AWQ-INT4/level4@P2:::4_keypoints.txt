- Proposal of a new Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where each arm can be played at M fidelities with cheaper biased approximations.  
- Introduction of MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Theoretical results: regret upper bound for MF-UCB, matching lower bound, and near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical results: synthetic simulations where MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered across the paper; some variables (e.g., "s") are defined late.  
- The advertising example is misleading because it suggests non-stationarity which is not part of the model.  
- The presented regret analysis is not fully convincing: optimal policy reduces to highest-mean arm regardless of sampling costs.  
- The focus on regret may hide the cost of unnecessary low-fidelity sampling; a sample/cost complexity analysis might be more appropriate.  
- Paper is thorough in analysis but certain sections are unwieldy and need streamlining.  
- Strengths: novel framework, solid theoretical analysis, and supportive simulations.  
- Weaknesses: unclear notation, misleading example, unconvincing regret framing, and presentation issues.  
- Recommendations: clarify notation, strengthen or rethink the regret analysis, consider sample complexity results, and add more motivating examples/applications.
