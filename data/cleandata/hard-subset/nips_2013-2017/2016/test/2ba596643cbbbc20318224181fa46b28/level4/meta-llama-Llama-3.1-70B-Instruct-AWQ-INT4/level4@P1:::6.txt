This paper studies a new multi-armed bandit setting where there are M fidelities at which the learner can choose to play an arm, each providing a better approximation to the rewards but expending larger cost. The authors propose a novel algorithm, Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB), that adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategies to this multi-fidelity setting. They provide a theoretical analysis, including a regret lower bound, and conduct simulations to demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB.

The setting considered in this paper could be applied to online advertising, where one may want to display ads for a few minutes to approximate the clicks in the long run, for cost concerns. The authors consider a new regret definition that accounts for the cost of the fidelity at which an arm is played. They also provide an algorithm with theoretical analysis, including a regret lower bound, and conduct simulations to demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB.

The analysis in this paper is novel and provides a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The authors show that MF-UCB can outperform the standard UCB algorithm that neglects the characteristics of the fidelities. They also provide a lower bound on the regret, which demonstrates that MF-UCB is near-optimal.

However, there are some minor issues with the paper. For example, the authors assume that the deviation of the mth fidelity mean from the one of the highest fidelity, ε(m), is known in advance. It is not clear how this value can be obtained in practice, such as in the application of online advertising. It would be useful to relax this assumption or provide guidance on how to estimate ε(m) in practice.
