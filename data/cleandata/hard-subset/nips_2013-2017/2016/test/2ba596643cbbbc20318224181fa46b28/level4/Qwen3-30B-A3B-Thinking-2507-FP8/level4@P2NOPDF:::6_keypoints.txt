- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework that trades off fidelity accuracy and cost, using low-cost fidelities to eliminate arms and high-cost fidelities to refine promising arms.  
- Motivation: online advertising where short ad displays (low fidelity) approximate long-term click behavior (high fidelity) at lower cost.  
- Proposes a new regret definition, an algorithm, theoretical guarantees (including a regret lower bound), and empirical validation.  
- The theoretical analysis is novel and well-executed.  
- Major limitation: assumes the fidelity deviations ε^(m) are known a priori, with no practical method given to obtain them.  
- Recommendation: authors should either relax this assumption (e.g., via adaptive estimation) or justify feasibility of knowing ε^(m) in target domains.
