- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity extension to multi-armed bandits allowing cheaper, biased evaluations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm adapted for multi-fidelity observations.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and a lower bound showing improved performance over naive high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Builds on MAB and UCB literature and connects to multi-fidelity optimization and RL, but fills a gap by formalizing regret in this setting.  
- Related work (e.g., Kandasamy et al.) complements but differs in focus and theoretical results.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and a natural, well-motivated algorithmic extension.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating algorithm performance.  
- Strength: synthetic simulations support theoretical findings.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s regret bound is complex and hard to interpret intuitively.  
- Weakness: Motivating applications (e.g., biased short ad displays) are not convincingly connected to the model.  
- Weakness: Simulations lack justification for parameter choices and no sensitivity analysis or real-world experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with caveats—ask authors to simplify presentation, justify simulation parameters, and explore practical robustness.
