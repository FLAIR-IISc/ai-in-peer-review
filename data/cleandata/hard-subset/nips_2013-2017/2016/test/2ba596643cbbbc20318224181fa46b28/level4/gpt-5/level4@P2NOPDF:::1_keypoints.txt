- Studies multi-fidelity stochastic MAB, proposes MF-UCB with regret guarantees and a lower bound, and claims MF-UCB can beat running UCB only at highest fidelity.  
- Problem is interesting, relevant, and technically sound; could serve as a benchmark for future work.  
- Major clarity issues around assumptions, modeling choices, and result interpretation.  
- Regret is defined with a multiplicative penalty on resource use (nonstandard); an additive-cost objective (subtracting costs) seems more natural—need discussion of how algorithm/analysis change under additive costs.  
- Assumption 1 on the ζm sequence is opaque and possibly artificial; unclear how to handle sequences that don’t satisfy it or whether selecting a subsequence can preserve guarantees.  
- Gap between upper and lower bounds needs more discussion: is the gap intrinsic or can the algorithm be improved; how do bounds scale with fidelity costs and ζm.  
- Theorem 2 is hard to parse and compare to UCB—must state the exact UCB baseline used and make comparisons explicit.  
- Need a high-level explanation of how K^(m) is constructed and how it affects exploration.  
- Provide intuition for Δ and [[k]] via simple canonical examples (e.g., two fidelities with geometric ζ decay, uniform costs) and state when MF-UCB is provably better, tied, or worse than high-fidelity UCB.  
- Overall: solid technical ingredients but presentation needs clearer motivation, transparent assumptions, examples, and a discussion of the additive-cost variant.  
- Minor issues: wording “near-optimal” (l.56); remove period before “for all m < M” (l.126); use curly braces to define [[k]] (l.179).
