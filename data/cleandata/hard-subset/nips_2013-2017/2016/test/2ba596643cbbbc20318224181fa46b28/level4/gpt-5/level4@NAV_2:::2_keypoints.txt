- Problem formulation: introduces a multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be queried at multiple fidelities with increasing costs and defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret.  
- Algorithm: proposes MF-UCB, a UCB adaptation that uses inexpensive biased approximations to screen out clearly suboptimal arms before using higher-fidelity, costlier queries.  
- Theoretical results: provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret and useful insights into the multi-fidelity exploration–exploitation trade-off.  
- Proof quality: proofs are generally solid, but many supporting details and corrections are in the supplement.  
- Manuscript clarity: main manuscript is clear and well organized.  
- Supplement quality issues: supplementary material contains numerous typos and minor errors (non-exhaustive list provided) that can mislead readers and should be fixed to improve readability.  
- Notation concern: pervasive “up-to-constant” notation is confusing because hidden constants sometimes depend on algorithmic parameters (e.g., ρ); presenting explicit inequalities would make claims crisper.  
- Practical limitation: unclear how well MF-UCB transfers to real-world deployments because the elimination logic depends on fidelity biases ζ; mis-specification could degrade performance and discussion of mitigation strategies is needed.  
- Overall assessment: significant and interesting contribution.
