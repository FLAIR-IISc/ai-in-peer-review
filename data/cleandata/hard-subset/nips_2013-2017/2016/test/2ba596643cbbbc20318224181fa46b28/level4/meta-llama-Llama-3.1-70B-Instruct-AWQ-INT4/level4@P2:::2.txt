This paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs when cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors propose an algorithm for this setting, MF-UCB, based on upper confidence bound techniques, and provide an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The main paper is clear and well-written, but the supplementary materials, where most of the proofs are, contain many typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. Correcting these errors would greatly increase the readability of the paper. Additionally, using exact inequalities instead of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signs could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical point of view, it is not clear that this algorithm can easily be applied to real-life problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB (using low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms) could suffer from inaccurate estimation of the ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion on the practical limitations and how to overcome them would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides a nice insight into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I think that this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
