1. Proposes a Multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework (arms sampled at different fidelity/costs), gives an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB.  
2. Notation is confusing and inconsistently introduced (e.g., variable s used before definition), making the technical content hard to follow.  
3. The motivating online-advertising example is misleading because it evokes non-stationarity, while the paper assumes stationary distributions.  
4. Regret as the performance metric is not well-justified: the optimal policy ignores sampling costs, so the regret formulation decouples reward and cost and may not capture the practical cost–accuracy trade-off.  
5. A cost-complexity / best-arm identification formulation (e.g., prototype testing in product development) would be a more natural and practically relevant alternative.  
6. Overall writing and presentation need substantial revision to improve clarity and readability.
