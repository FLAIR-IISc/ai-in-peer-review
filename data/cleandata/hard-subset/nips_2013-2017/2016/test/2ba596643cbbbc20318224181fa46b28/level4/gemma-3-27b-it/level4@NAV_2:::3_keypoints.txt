- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be played at M fidelities trading cost vs biased reward estimates.  
- Lower fidelities are cheaper but produce potentially biased (not just noisy) estimates of true rewards.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm with fidelity-dependent uncertainty bounds applying optimism in the face of uncertainty.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB can improve over always playing at highest fidelity.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid but yields complex, hard-to-interpret regret expressions.  
- Simulations support the theoretical gains, reporting ~3–4x regret improvements in the presented settings.  
- Motivation and connection to applications (e.g., algorithm selection, advertising) feel weak or insufficiently justified.  
- Modeling choice of bias at lower fidelities (vs. variance) is counterintuitive for some applications and not fully convincing.  
- Simulation parameter choices lack justification and sensitivity analysis, raising concerns about representativeness of results.  
- Overall assessment: novel and valuable contribution with reasonable algorithmic design and guarantees; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify application links and simulation setup.
