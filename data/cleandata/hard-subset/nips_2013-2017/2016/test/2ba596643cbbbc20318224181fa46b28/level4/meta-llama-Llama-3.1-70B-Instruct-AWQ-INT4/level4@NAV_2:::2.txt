This paper introduces a new multi-arm bandit setting where each arm can be drawn with different accuracies (or fidelities), with the cost of pulling an arm increasing with the desired accuracy. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, to address this setting, and provide an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The paper is clear and well-written, but the supplementary materials, which contain most of the proofs, have many typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. Correcting these errors would greatly increase the readability of the paper. Additionally, the use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signs can be misleading, as constants are not really constants. Using the exact inequalities could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical point of view, it is not clear that this algorithm can easily be applied to real-life problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB, which uses low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms, could suffer from inaccurate estimation of the fidelity accuracy. A discussion on the practical limitations and how to overcome them would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides a nice insight into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. The authors relate their work to previous studies on multi-armed bandits and provide a clear motivation for their new setting. The analysis of the algorithm's performance is thorough, and the comparison with the UCB algorithm is helpful in understanding the benefits of the new approach.

The paper's main contributions are the introduction of the multi-fidelity bandit setting, the proposal of the MF-UCB algorithm, and the analysis of its performance. The authors also provide a lower bound on the regret, which shows that MF-UCB is near-optimal in certain cases.

In terms of the criteria for evaluation, the paper scores well on quality, clarity, and originality. The technical content is sound, and the writing is clear and well-organized. The paper presents a new and interesting problem, and the proposed algorithm is novel and well-motivated. The analysis of the algorithm's performance is thorough, and the results are significant.

However, the paper could be improved by addressing the practical limitations of the algorithm and providing more discussion on how to overcome them. Additionally, the supplementary materials need to be corrected to eliminate errors and improve readability.

Overall, I think that this work is a significant and interesting contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The paper provides a new perspective on the problem, and the proposed algorithm is a valuable addition to the existing literature.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper introduces a new and interesting problem setting, which is well-motivated and relevant to the field of multi-armed bandits.
