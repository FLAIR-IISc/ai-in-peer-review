This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit (MAB) setting incorporating variable fidelity rewards. In this framework, $M$ levels of fidelity are available for evaluating each arm, with higher fidelities providing more accurate reward estimates at increased cost. The core idea is that an effective strategy should leverage low-cost, lower-fidelity evaluations to rapidly eliminate suboptimal arms, concentrating more expensive, high-fidelity evaluations on a promising subset. This approach has the potential to outperform standard algorithms like UCB, which don’t account for the fidelity-cost trade-off. The authors motivate this setting with an application to online advertising, where initial low-duration ad displays can provide quick, inexpensive approximations of long-term click-through rates.

The paper presents a new regret definition tailored to this fidelity setting, proposes an algorithm designed to exploit the fidelity structure, and provides a theoretical analysis – including a regret lower bound – alongside simulation results. The analysis appears novel and constitutes the paper’s main contribution. 

However, a key limitation lies in the assumption that the deviation $\varepsilon^{(m)}$ between the mean reward at fidelity $m$ and the highest fidelity is known *a priori*. This assumption significantly impacts the practical applicability of the algorithm. Determining these deviations in real-world scenarios, such as the online advertising example, is unclear. It would be valuable to explore the robustness of the algorithm to inaccurate estimates of $\varepsilon^{(m)}$, or to investigate the possibility of relaxing this assumption – perhaps by estimating these deviations during the initial exploration phase.

The experimental evaluation, while providing some support for the theoretical results, could benefit from more extensive comparisons with existing bandit algorithms, especially those that incorporate exploration-exploitation trade-offs. Further detail on the simulation setup and parameter choices would also increase reproducibility.
