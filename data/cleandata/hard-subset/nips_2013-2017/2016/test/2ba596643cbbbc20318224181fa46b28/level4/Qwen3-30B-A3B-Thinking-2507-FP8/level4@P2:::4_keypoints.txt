- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with different costs and biases.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively selects arms and fidelity levels.  
- Provides theoretical upper regret bounds and a matching lower bound, extending classical UCB to multi-fidelity settings.  
- Distinguishes this work from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization by formalizing and analyzing regret in this setting.  
- Empirical results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistently introduced (e.g., s, T^{(m)}_{k,t}, Q^{(m)}_{k,t} defined late or without context).  
- The advertising example is misleading by introducing non-stationarity not addressed by the theoretical model.  
- The regret formulation R(Λ)=Λμ*−∑λ(m_t)q_t is conceptually flawed and does not capture the cost–exploration trade-off or allow oracle fidelity choices.  
- Paper lacks a cost-complexity analysis aligned with practical applications like sequential testing of low- and high-fidelity prototypes.  
- Writing and organization need streamlining; formal problem definition should introduce all symbols before use and better connect theory to applications.  
- Reviewer recommendations: clarify and consistently define notation, replace the advertising example, revise regret formulation, add cost-complexity analysis, and streamline writing.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but potentially reconsider if the above issues are addressed.
