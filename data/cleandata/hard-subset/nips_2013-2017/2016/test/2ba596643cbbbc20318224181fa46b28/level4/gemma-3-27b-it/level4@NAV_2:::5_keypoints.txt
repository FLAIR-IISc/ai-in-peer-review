- Introduces a multi-fidelity K-armed bandit setting with costly high-fidelity evaluations and cheaper biased approximations.
- Formalizes costs (λ high-fidelity, λ' lower-fidelity) and bias parameters ζ(m) for approximations.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm adapted to multi-fidelity evaluations.
- Provides a regret bound for MF-UCB (Theorem 2) with detailed proofs and comparison to standard UCB.
- Establishes a lower bound (Theorem 4) showing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.
- Includes Assumption 1 and discussion of its implications and algorithmic limitations.
- Builds on and distinguishes itself from prior work in budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, and active learning.
- Motivates the problem with practical applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear proof sketches in the appendix.
- Strength: logical adaptation of UCB principles and coherent methodology.
- Empirical validation shows promise but is limited in scope and diversity of instances.
- Lacks detailed simulation parameter settings and a broader range of problem instances.
- Unclear sensitivity of performance to inaccuracies in ζ(m); recommends a sensitivity analysis.
- Notation is dense and can be hard to follow for non-expert readers; γ(m) choice needs more intuition.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor clarity and empirical evaluation improvements; recommends acceptance.
