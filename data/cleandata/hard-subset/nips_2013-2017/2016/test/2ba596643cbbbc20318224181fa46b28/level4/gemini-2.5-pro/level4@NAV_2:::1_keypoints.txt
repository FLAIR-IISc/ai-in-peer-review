- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit setting with fidelity-dependent costs and biased low-fidelity rewards, formalized via a cost-weighted regret and the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB leverages cheap, low-fidelity pulls to eliminate highly suboptimal arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.  
- The problem formulation is novel and highly relevant to real-world settings with approximate or preliminary evaluations.  
- The MF-UCB algorithm is an intuitive UCB extension and the paper provides technically solid theoretical analysis (upper bound for the algorithm and a matching lower bound for the problem).  
- Presentation of the main theoretical results is unclear; Theorem 2 and the role of the sets K(m) are difficult to interpret.  
- The paper would benefit from an intuitive walkthrough or a simplified concrete example to illustrate the regret bound and practical gains of MF-UCB.  
- The multiplicative cost-weighted regret definition needs stronger motivation and discussion of alternatives (e.g., additive penalties) and their effects.  
- Assumption 1 (decay structure of fidelity approximation errors ζ(m)) seems artificial; authors should justify its realism and clarify how critical it is to the method.  
- There is an observed gap between the algorithm's upper bound and the lower bound for certain classes of arms, requiring more discussion on whether this reflects a fundamental limitation or room for improved algorithms.  
- Overall: technically strong and sets a benchmark, but hindered by clarity issues and insufficient justification of key modeling assumptions.
