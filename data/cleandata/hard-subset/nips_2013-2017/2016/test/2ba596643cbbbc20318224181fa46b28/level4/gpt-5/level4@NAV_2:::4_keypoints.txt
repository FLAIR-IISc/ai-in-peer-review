- Proposes a new Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where arms can be queried at multiple fidelity levels with differing costs and biased low-fidelity approximations.  
- Introduces MF-UCB algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs, with regret analysis, a problem-specific lower bound, and synthetic simulations outperforming UCB.  
- Overall assessment: framework is interesting and potentially impactful but paper not ready for publication in current form.  
- Notation is confusing: symbols are scattered and some (e.g., s) are defined after first use and seldom used thereafter.  
- Advertising example is misleading and invokes non-stationarity/correlations outside the paper’s scope; a different motivating example is preferable.  
- The chosen regret notion is hard to justify: benchmarking by highest mean regardless of cost ignores reward/cost trade-offs and may hide costs of low-fidelity sampling.  
- Recommends a sample- (cost-) complexity analysis for multi-fidelity bandits, motivated by prototype testing where low-fidelity tests may be required before expensive tests.  
- Writing is unwieldy and needs a thorough editorial pass to streamline presentation and improve clarity.
