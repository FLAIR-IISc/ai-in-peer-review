- Paper proposes a multi-armed bandit model with discrete fidelity levels, where fidelity affects reward noise (zeta) and lower fidelities are cheaper.  
- Authors optimize a net-reward objective via sequential arm pulls with chosen fidelities and present a new algorithm (MF-UCB) plus a regret lower bound.  
- Claim that MF-UCB can outperform standard high-fidelity UCB.  
- Reviewer finds the problem interesting and a useful benchmark for cost–information tradeoffs.  
- Paper lacks clear explanation of results and assumptions, reducing accessibility.  
- The adopted regret definition is unnatural—penalizes resource use multiplicatively—and may not be generally appropriate.  
- Suggests investigating additive incorporation of fidelity costs into reward/regret as an alternative.  
- Assumption 1 is presented as artificial and opaque, with unclear general validity or intuition.  
- Unclear how to handle cases where zeta values do not decay as required or how to choose a subsequence to satisfy Assumption 1.  
- Concern that Assumption 1, though claimed non-crucial, appears used in proofs and may critically affect algorithm design/performance.  
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification for those configurations.  
- The gap in the provided lower bound needs further analysis and may require redesigning the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and comparisons to UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and dependence on complex sets (Δ, [[k]], K^(m)).  
- Paper needs clearer, more explicit explanation of relative gains/losses of MF-UCB versus UCB.  
- Minor corrections: wording “near-optimal” (line 56), remove extraneous period before “for all m < M” (line 126), and use curly braces for [[k]] (line 179).
