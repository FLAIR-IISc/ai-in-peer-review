- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where arms can be observed at M fidelities with differing costs and biased estimates.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper confidence bound algorithm tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: an upper-regret bound for MF-UCB and a lower bound, claiming near-optimality and improved regret vs. naive strategies.  
- Problem is relevant to real-world tasks (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) where cheaper approximations exist.  
- Positions work within bandit literature and connects to multi-fidelity optimization and RL, highlighting novelty in applying regret minimization.  
- Strength: novel formulation that formalizes cost–information trade-offs.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating performance guarantees.  
- Strength: potential for practical impact across several application domains.  
- Strength: clear motivation and accessible motivating example.  
- Weakness: regret definition (multiplicative cost) feels unnatural and lacks strong justification; additive penalty may be more intuitive.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 on fidelity regularity appears artificial, limiting generality and lacking guidance if it fails.  
- Weakness: technical presentation hampers intuition and makes comparisons to standard UCB hard to interpret.  
- Weakness: gap between upper and lower bounds suggests the algorithm or analysis may be improvable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated algorithm with sound theory that can serve as a benchmark.  
- Arguments for rejection: unnatural regret metric, restrictive assumption, poor clarity, and unresolved bound gap reduce practical applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; requested fixes include justifying the regret definition, discussing Assumption 1 in depth, improving clarity of presentation, and investigating the upper–lower bound gap.
