- Paper studies multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits: learner picks arm and fidelity, fidelity m gives rewards with mean within zeta_m of true mean (zeta_M=0), fidelities have known decreasing zeta_m and differing costs; authors define a budgeted objective, propose MF-UCB, and give a regret lower bound.

- Problem is interesting and timely, explicitly modeling information–cost–reward trade-offs and useful as a benchmark.

- Major limitation: insufficient explanation of several key algorithmic and modeling choices and of key results.

- Regret notion is questionable: authors couple reward and resource multiplicatively; reviewer asks why not an additive cost penalty and requests clarification of implications if cost were added to reward/regret.

- Assumption 1 is opaque and ad hoc, imposing constraints on fidelity parameters; unclear why it should hold generally or how to choose a subsequence of fidelities when zeta_m does not decay as required.

- Although authors claim Assumption 1 is not crucial, it appears in proofs and may critically shape algorithm design and performance; justification for the chosen zeta sequences is missing.

- The lower-bound gap suggests further investigation is needed and may motivate refining the strategy.

- Theorem 2 and comparisons to standard (high-fidelity-only) UCB are hard to interpret: advantage depends on Delta and [[k]] via nested sets K^(m); paper needs clearer, reader-friendly conditions quantifying when and by how much MF-UCB improves.

- Overall: compelling modelling start but clarity is lost in presenting and interpreting theoretical results.

- Minor typos/presentation issues: l56 “near-optimal”; l126 superfluous period before “for all m < M”; l179 consider using curly braces to define [[k]].
