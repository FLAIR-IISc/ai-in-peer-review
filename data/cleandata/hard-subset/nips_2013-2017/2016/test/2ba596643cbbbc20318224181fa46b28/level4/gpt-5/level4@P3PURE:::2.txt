This paper introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting in which each arm can be pulled at different accuracies (fidelities), with the cost of a pull increasing with the desired accuracy. Regret is defined as the product of the cost with the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose MF-UCB to address this setting and provide both an upper bound and a lower bound on the algorithm’s cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well written. However, the supplementary material, where most of the proofs appear, contains numerous typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader (a non-exhaustive list is provided below). Correcting these issues would greatly improve readability.

I also find the “up to a multiplicative constant” notation used throughout the paper potentially misleading, as important parameter dependencies are hidden in the constants. For example, in Theorem 2 the factor ρ (an algorithm parameter) is absorbed into the constant. Presenting exact inequalities, as given in the supplementary materials, would considerably improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical standpoint, it is not obvious that the algorithm can be easily applied to real-world problems. For instance, the MF-UCB strategy—using low-accuracy pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms—may be sensitive to inaccurate estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion of such practical limitations and possible ways to mitigate them would strengthen the paper.

From a theoretical perspective, the paper provides a nice insight into the problem together with interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I view this work as a significant and interesting contribution.
