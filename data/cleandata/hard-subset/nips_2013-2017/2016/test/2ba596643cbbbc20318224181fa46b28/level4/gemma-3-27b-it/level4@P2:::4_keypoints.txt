- Introduces a novel Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework addressing varying evaluation costs and approximate low-fidelity evaluations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB algorithm with regret analysis and a matching lower bound.  
- Empirical synthetic results show MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB.  
- Paper currently not suitable for publication in present form.  
- Major clarity and presentation issues: notation introduced fragmentedly, variables (e.g., ‘s’) appear before definition and are sparsely used.  
- Illustrative online-advertising example is distracting and introduces irrelevant non-stationarity concerns.  
- Suggest replacing the motivating example with cleaner analogies (e.g., algorithm selection or engineering design).  
- Regret formulation is questionable for cost-sensitive settings: optimizing mean reward ignores reward-to-cost ratio and may misguide fidelity exploration.  
- Missing cost (or sample) complexity analysis that would strengthen practical relevance (e.g., prototype testing scenarios).  
- Writing is unwieldy and would benefit from streamlining and clearer organization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: the framework is novel, theoretical contributions are solid, and empirical validation is promising.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, questionable regret justification, missing cost-analysis, and a misleading motivating example.
