- Paper introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms can be sampled at M fidelities with higher fidelity more costly but less biased; proposes cost-aware regret and the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB strategy: use cheap, low-fidelity samples to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserve expensive, high-fidelity samples for promising candidates.  
- Theoretical contributions: regret analysis for MF-UCB, a lower bound for the problem, and near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions; supported by synthetic simulations.  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel formulation addressing practical cost vs. information-quality trade-offs in real-world experiments.  
- Strength — technical soundness: well-motivated algorithm with complete theoretical treatment (regret guarantees and lower bound).  
- Strength — intuition: the core idea of pruning with cheap information before costly commitment is clear and compelling.  
- Weakness — regret formulation: regret is measured against an oracle that always plays the highest-fidelity optimal arm, ignoring costs and reward-to-cost trade-offs.  
- Weakness — alternative framing: a best-arm-identification or cost-complexity framework might be more natural and practically relevant than cumulative regret.  
- Weakness — clarity/presentation: notation is scattered (e.g., variable s used before definition), the motivating online-ad example is confusing due to stationarity mismatch, and parts of the text need rewriting for flow.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: promising idea but recommend rejection in current form; advise reframing (e.g., BAI with cost budget) and substantial manuscript revision for clarity.
