This work introduces a new multi-arm bandit setting where each arm can be pulled with varying precision (or fidelity). The cost of pulling an arm with the desired precision increases, and regret is defined as the product of the cost and the general pseudo-regret. The author proposes an algorithm that addresses this setting, MF-UCB, and provides an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm. The main paper is clear and well-written. However, it is not a case of supplementary material, where there is most of the evidence. It contains several typos and minor, easily fixable errors that can confuse the reader (see below for a non-exhaustive list). Correcting those errors would greatly improve the paper's readability. Additionally, I think the up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signals used in the paper may be slightly misleading, as the constants are not actually constants: for example, in theorem 2, there is a factor \rho (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden in the constant. Using precise inequalities (which are written in the supplementary material) could improve the clarity of the results. From a practical standpoint, it is not clear to me that this algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For example, the strategy of MF-UCB (using less precise bridges to eliminate very few sub-optimal arms) may suffer from an incorrect estimate of \zeta (fidelity accuracy). I think a discussion on the practical limits, and how to overcome them, would benefit this work. From a theoretical point of view, this paper provides a good insight into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid evidence. Overall, I think this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
