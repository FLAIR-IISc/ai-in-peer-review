- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms can be sampled at varying fidelities with different costs, formalizing the cost–quality trade-off.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a generalization of UCB that leverages cheaper low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms before costly evaluations.  
- Proves near-optimal regret bounds that scale with lower-fidelity costs and improve over naïve UCB in favorable cost/arm-structure regimes.  
- Provides a lower bound (Theorem 4) demonstrating near-optimality of MF-UCB for arms in K^{(m)}_3.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic problems shows MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB and clearly exhibits fidelity-specific play patterns.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and practically motivated (online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- Regret bound strictly improves on naive UCB when low-fidelity cost λ^{(1)} ≪ λ^{(M)} and |K^{(M)}| ≪ K.  
- Supplementary material has multiple technical errors (e.g., incorrect indices, mislabelled sets, wrong constants) that undermine reproducibility.  
- Use of asymptotic notation (≲) hides dependence on parameter ρ (requires ρ > 4); sensitivity to ρ is not discussed.  
- Practical applicability is underdeveloped because MF-UCB assumes known fidelity bias bounds ζ^{(m)}, and robustness to estimation errors is not analyzed.  
- Required revisions: correct supplementary errors and add discussion/strategies for estimating or robustifying ζ^{(m)}.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, significant advance with strong empirical support; merits acceptance after minor revisions.
