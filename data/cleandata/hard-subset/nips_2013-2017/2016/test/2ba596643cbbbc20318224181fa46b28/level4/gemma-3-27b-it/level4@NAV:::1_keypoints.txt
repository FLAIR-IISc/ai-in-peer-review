- Presents a novel multi-fidelity bandit model where each fidelity has known error bounds and higher fidelity can be error-free.  
- Proposes MF-UCB algorithm to maximize net reward by balancing information gain and fidelity costs, and provides a regret lower bound.  
- Addresses an important trade-off between information, cost, and reward in stochastic bandits and could be a useful benchmark.  
- Overall technically sound but the presentation and clarity of results and assumptions are weak.  
- The chosen multiplicative penalty in the regret definition seems unnatural; an additive cost penalty may be more intuitive and widely applicable.  
- Assumption 1 on fidelity parameter decay appears artificial, unjustified, and its necessity and impact on results and algorithm design are unclear.  
- Unclear behavior if zeta_m do not satisfy Assumption 1; authors should consider selecting a suitable subsequence of fidelities to broaden applicability.  
- The gap in the provided lower bound needs further investigation and may indicate room to refine the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and comparison to UCB are hard to interpret because performance depends on complex quantities (Delta, [[k]], and the set sequence K^(m)); more explicit discussion is needed.  
- Minor presentation issues and typos (e.g., "near-optimal" typo, stray period before "for all m < M", and set notation formatting).
