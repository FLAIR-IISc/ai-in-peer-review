- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework introducing "fidelity" with cost–reward trade-offs.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB to multi-fidelity settings, with provable regret improvements over naive strategies.  
- Provides a regret lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Technical analysis is solid but overall presentation and assumptions lack clarity.  
- Regret definition penalizes resource consumption multiplicatively, which may be unnatural; additive penalization should be considered.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on fidelity parameters) appears artificial/opaque, is used in proofs, and needs justification or relaxation.  
- There is a gap in the lower bound: MF-UCB may not meet the lower bound on some arms, requiring further investigation or redesign.  
- Presentation is overly technical; more explicit explanations and examples are needed to show MF-UCB’s relative gains vs UCB.  
- Minor typos/suggestions: hyphenate "near-optimal" (Line 56); remove period before "for all m < M" (Line 126); use set notation for [[k]] (Line 179).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel framework, thorough analysis, and a lower bound indicating near-optimality.  
- Cons for acceptance: questionable regret formulation, unclear/possibly crucial Assumption 1, unresolved lower-bound gap, and weak exposition.
