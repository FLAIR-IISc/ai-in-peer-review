- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with fidelity choices, formalizes net-reward objective, proposes MF-UCB, and proves a lower bound.  
- Modeling trade-off among information, cost, and reward is interesting and relevant.  
- Main shortcoming: results and assumptions are not clearly explained.  
- Regret definition concerns: penalizing cost multiplicatively (rather than additively) seems unnatural; an additive-cost formulation should be considered or discussed.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque; its general applicability is unclear and it is used in proofs despite the claim it is not crucial.  
- If zeta_m’s do not decay as Assumption 1 requires, the paper should provide a principled way to select a subsequence of fidelities that does, to preserve results.  
- The algorithm appears tailored to particular zeta configurations without justification for why those sequences are the “right” targets.  
- The gap in the lower bound vs. algorithm performance needs further investigation and may require redesigning the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and the MF-UCB vs. UCB comparison are hard to interpret: dependence on Δ and the sets K^(m) is opaque and a clearer high-level comparison is needed.  
- Overall: promising modeling choice but weakened by lack of clarity in presentation and interpretation of main results.  
- Minor typos: line 56 “near-optimal”; remove period before “for all m < M” at line 126; use curly braces to define [[k]] at line 179.
