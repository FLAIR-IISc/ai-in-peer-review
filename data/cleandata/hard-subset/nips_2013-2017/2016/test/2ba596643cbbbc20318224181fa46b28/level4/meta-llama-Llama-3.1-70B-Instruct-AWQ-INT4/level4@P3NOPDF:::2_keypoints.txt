- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls where cost increases with accuracy and regret is cost × pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written, making the contributions easy to follow.  
- Supplementary materials contain most proofs but suffer from numerous typos and small errors that hinder readability.  
- Reviewer provides a non-exhaustive, page-by-page list of specific corrections needed in the supplementary material.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is potentially misleading because constants hide algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may fail if fidelity accuracy ζ is poorly estimated; discussion of limitations and remedies is needed.  
- Theoretical contributions are valuable, with interesting theorems and solid proofs, making this a significant and interesting work.
