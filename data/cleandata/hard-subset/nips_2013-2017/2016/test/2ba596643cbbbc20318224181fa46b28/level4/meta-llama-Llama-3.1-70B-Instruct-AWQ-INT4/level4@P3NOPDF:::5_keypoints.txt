- Proposes a multi-fidelity variant of the stochastic K-armed bandit with cheap approximations to each arm.  
- Formalizes the problem and provides a theoretical framework for exploration–exploitation trade-offs.  
- Derives regret bounds that characterize the algorithm's performance.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of a lower bound for the proposed method.  
- Runs simulations that demonstrate the effects of multi-fidelity on performance.  
- Empirical results show the method balances exploration and exploitation more efficiently than traditional bandit algorithms.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible to a broad audience.  
- Overall, a well-motivated and well-executed combination of theoretical and empirical work.
