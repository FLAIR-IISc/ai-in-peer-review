- Introduces a formal multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where arms can be evaluated at varying cost–accuracy fidelities.
- Proposes MF-UCB, extending UCB optimism to use cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and save high-fidelity cost for promising arms.
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with an upper bound on cost-weighted pseudo-regret for MF-UCB.
- Proves a corresponding lower bound that establishes near-optimality of the algorithm under specific conditions.
- Positions the work within UCB literature and relates it to budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.
- Strength: Well-motivated, practically relevant problem formulation applicable to settings like advertising and algorithm configuration.
- Strength: Strong technical quality — derivation of both upper and problem-specific lower regret bounds.
- Strength: Main paper is clear, well-organized, and accessible.
- Weakness: Appendix (proofs) contains numerous typos and minor errors that impede readability and should be carefully proofread.
- Weakness: Main theorem statements hide important parameter dependencies via "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation (e.g., dependency on ρ); make these explicit in the main text.
- Weakness: Practical limitation — algorithm requires prior knowledge of fidelity accuracy parameters ζ; discuss robustness or methods to learn/estimate ζ.
- Overall: Significant and interesting contribution; would be stronger after fixing appendix errors and discussing practical assumptions.
