This paper studies a new multi-armed bandit setting where the learner can choose to play an arm at multiple fidelities, each providing a better approximation to the rewards but expending larger cost. The authors propose a new algorithm, Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB), that adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategies to this multi-fidelity setting. They provide a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a regret lower bound, and conduct simulations to demonstrate its performance.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem setting, the algorithm, and the theoretical analysis. The simulations are also well-presented and provide useful insights into the performance of the algorithm.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of the MF-UCB algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The authors show that MF-UCB can outperform the standard UCB algorithm by using the lower and cheaper fidelities to eliminate several sub-optimal arms, while reserving higher and more expensive fidelities for a small subset of good arms.

The paper also provides a regret lower bound, which shows that MF-UCB is near-optimal in certain cases. The lower bound is based on a change of measure argument and provides a useful benchmark for evaluating the performance of the algorithm.

One potential limitation of the paper is the assumption that the deviations of the mth fidelity mean from the one of the highest fidelity are known in advance. In practice, these values may not be known, and it may be necessary to estimate them. The authors do not discuss how to relax this assumption, and it is not clear how the algorithm would perform in this case.
