- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with different costs.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively leverages fidelity approximations and costs.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including an upper bound on regret and a matching lower bound.  
- Empirically shows MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems.  
- Novel problem formulation with clear practical relevance (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- MF-UCB is well-motivated and effectively uses lower-fidelity information to reduce regret.  
- Theoretical guarantees are comprehensive and include both upper and lower bounds.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistently introduced (e.g., variable 's' used before definition).  
- Advertising example is misleading by introducing non-stationarity unrelated to the studied bandit setting.  
- Regret definition/analysis is flawed: optimal policy should consider reward-to-cost ratio, not just highest reward, so the regret formulation may be incorrect.  
- Lacks a cost-complexity analysis, which would be more appropriate for multi-fidelity objectives focused on minimizing testing cost.  
- Paper contains awkward phrasing and presentation issues that make it difficult to follow.  
- Overall recommendation: reject pending substantial revision to fix notation, regret formulation, examples, and presentation.
