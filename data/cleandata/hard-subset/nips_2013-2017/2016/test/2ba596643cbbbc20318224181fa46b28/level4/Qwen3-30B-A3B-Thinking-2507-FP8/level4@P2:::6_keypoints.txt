- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm can be played at multiple fidelities with costs λ(m) and biases ζ(m).  
- Formalizes the problem using M successively better approximations and defines a cost-aware regret metric.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that allocates plays across fidelities to reduce cost-weighted regret compared to standard UCB.  
- Provides strong theoretical results: regret upper bound showing MF-UCB’s advantage when |Kg|<K and λ(1)<λ(2), plus a matching lower bound indicating near-optimality under conditions.  
- Analysis is thorough and well-structured, with clear theorems and proofs.  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms UCB, with smaller regret gradients consistent with theory.  
- Problem formulation is well-motivated by practical applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) and shows how low fidelities can prune suboptimal arms.  
- Main limitation: the assumption that bias parameters ζ(m) are known is unrealistic; the authors should discuss estimating ζ(m) or adapting MF-UCB to unknown biases.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, reproducible, and figures (notably Fig.1) effectively illustrate fidelity allocation.  
- Related work is properly contextualized and the contribution is distinct from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity GP optimization.  
- Minor corrections: several notation/typo fixes listed (e.g., H^{(m)}→K^{(m)} at lines 326/357/386; Beta{k*,t}→μ* at 343; gamma→γ^{(m)} at 345; inequality directions at 346–347; summation index at 384; delta/λ term corrections at 407; index fixes at 431 and 440; P(A{n,3})→P(A{n,2}) at 450).
