- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity (MF) extension of the stochastic K-armed bandit where low-cost, low-fidelity reward approximations are available.
- Formalizes the MF bandit setting and proposes an algorithm to exploit multiple fidelities.
- Derives regret bounds for the proposed algorithm.
- Provides a thorough lower-bound analysis, strengthening the theoretical results.
- Rigorously shows performance gains from leveraging multiple fidelities versus single-fidelity bandits.
- Clear and accessible presentation of problem formulation and methods.
- Solid empirical evaluation with simulations validating reduced regret through intelligent allocation between high- and low-fidelity pulls.
