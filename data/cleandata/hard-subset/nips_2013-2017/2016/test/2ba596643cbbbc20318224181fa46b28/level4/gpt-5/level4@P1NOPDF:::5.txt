This paper studies a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem in which inexpensive approximations are available for each arm. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide regret bounds. They present simulations that demonstrate the impact of leveraging multiple fidelities. The presentation is clear and easy to follow. The experiments illustrate the effect of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method. There is sufficient theoretical analysis of lower bounds for the proposed method, which indicates theoretical improvements.
