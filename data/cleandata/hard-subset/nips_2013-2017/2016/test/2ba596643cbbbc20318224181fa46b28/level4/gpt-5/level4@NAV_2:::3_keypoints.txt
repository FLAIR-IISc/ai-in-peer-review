- Proposes a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit where each arm can be queried at M fidelity levels with fidelity-dependent bounded bias relative to high-fidelity rewards.  
- Develops a UCB-style algorithm that adjusts confidence bounds to account for fidelity-induced bias (not just statistical variance).  
- Theoretical regret guarantees are provided and claimed to be strictly better than running standard UCB only at the highest fidelity, though the bounds are algebraically complex and hard to parse.  
- Empirical synthetic experiments report encouraging improvements (often 3–4× regret reduction for MF-UCB), supporting the theoretical claim.  
- Primary modeling choice treats low fidelities as introducing bounded bias rather than added variance; reviewer questions this modeling choice but accepts it for some motivating settings.  
- One motivating example (short-time ad displays allegedly bias long-term effectiveness) is unconvincing to the reviewer.  
- For algorithm selection, regret minimization may not align with practitioner objectives, weakening application relevance.  
- Main novelty is formalizing the multi-fidelity bandit problem; the algorithmic approach is a straightforward adaptation of optimism-under-uncertainty.  
- Simulation details in Appendix C specify parameters but lack justification, making it unclear whether reported gains are typical or artifacts of chosen settings (e.g., ζ(m)).
