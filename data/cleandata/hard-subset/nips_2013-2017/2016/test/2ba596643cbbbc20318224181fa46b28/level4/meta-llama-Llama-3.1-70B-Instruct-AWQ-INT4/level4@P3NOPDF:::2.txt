This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be drawn with varying accuracies, or fidelities. The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost and the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, to address this setting and provide an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The main paper is clear and well-written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. However, the supplementary materials, which contain most of the proofs, require more attention. Unfortunately, they are plagued by numerous typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. A non-exhaustive list of these errors is provided below. Correcting these errors would significantly increase the readability of the paper.

Additionally, I believe that the use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signs in the paper can be misleading, as constants are not truly constant. For instance, in Theorem 2, there is a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden in the constant. Using the exact inequalities, which are written in the supplementary materials, could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical perspective, it is unclear whether this algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB, which uses low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms, may suffer from inaccurate estimation of the fidelity accuracy ζ. A discussion on the practical limitations and how to overcome them would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides valuable insights into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I believe that this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
