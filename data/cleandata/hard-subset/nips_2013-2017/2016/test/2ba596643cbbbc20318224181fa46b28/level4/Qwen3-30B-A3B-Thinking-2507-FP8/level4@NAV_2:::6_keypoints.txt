Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm can be observed at M fidelities with increasing cost and better approximations of the true reward.

Formalizes strategies that choose arm and fidelity, with higher fidelities giving better estimates at greater cost.

Proposes MF-UCB, which leverages cheap low fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and uses expensive high fidelities only for promising arms.

Demonstrates MF-UCB attains better regret than standard UCB that ignores fidelity information.

Introduces a cost-aware regret definition and provides theoretical analysis including an upper bound and a lower bound.

Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.

Extends classical bandit literature to settings with cheaper approximations of outcomes; potential applications include online advertising and algorithm selection.

Theoretical development is sound, building on UCB methods while introducing meaningful algorithmic and theoretical innovations.

Main concern: the paper assumes the deviation ε^(m) between the m-th fidelity mean and the highest-fidelity mean is known a priori, which is a strong and potentially unrealistic assumption.

Addressing or providing methods to estimate ε^(m) would materially strengthen the paper’s practical applicability.
