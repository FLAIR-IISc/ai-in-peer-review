- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where sampling cost scales with fidelity, regret is cost × pseudo-regret, and proposes MF-UCB with matching upper and lower cumulative pseudo-regret bounds.  
- Main paper is clearly written and well-organized, but the supplementary proofs contain numerous typos and minor errors that hinder readability (a detailed list is provided).  
- The frequent use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is imprecise because those constants depend on algorithmic parameters (e.g., ρ); presenting the exact inequalities would improve transparency.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy samples to eliminate arms may be vulnerable to misestimation of ζ (fidelity accuracy); discussion of limitations and mitigation strategies is needed.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong — interesting theorems supported by rigorous proofs — and represent a significant contribution to the field.  
- Appendix contains many specific typographical/math errors across pages 10–17 and Section C (detailed corrections enumerated in the review).
