- Paper introduces Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit and MF-UCB: handles varied evaluation costs and biased cheap approximations, with regret analysis and lower bound.
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Notation is confusing and definitions are dispersed (e.g., variable s introduced without immediate definition), harming clarity and reproducibility.
- The online-advertising motivating example is misleading (mixes display time and non-stationarity); a more focused motivation (e.g., ML algorithm selection) is preferable.
- The chosen regret objective is unclear/inappropriate: framework seems to target highest mean reward rather than best reward-to-cost ratio, reducing emphasis on avoiding unnecessary low-fidelity sampling.
- A sample/cost-complexity analysis is missing and would be valuable for practical scenarios where testing cost matters.
- Writing is unwieldy and needs streamlining for conciseness and clarity.
- Main reasons for rejection: confusing notation, misleading motivation, questionable regret justification, missing cost-complexity analysis, and overall writing quality.
- Reasons for potential acceptance: framework is novel and promising; MF-UCB shows empirical gains; theoretical regret bound and matching lower bound are provided.
