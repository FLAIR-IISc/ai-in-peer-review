- Studies K-armed bandits with cheaper, lower-fidelity approximations and proposes a multi-fidelity (MF) bandit framework, algorithms, regret guarantees, and simulations.
- Problem formulation is clear and practically relevant (e.g., simulation-based optimization, hyperparameter tuning with proxies).
- Provides theoretical contributions: upper and lower regret bounds quantifying gains from low-fidelity information.
- Empirical simulations support theory and show performance gains from using multiple fidelities.
- Guarantees depend on assumptions about fidelity relationships (bias, variance, stochastic dominance); these assumptions should be made more explicit and their practical validity discussed.
- Unclear whether algorithms require prior knowledge of fidelity biases, noise levels, or costs, or if these are estimated online—clarify what is known vs. learned.
