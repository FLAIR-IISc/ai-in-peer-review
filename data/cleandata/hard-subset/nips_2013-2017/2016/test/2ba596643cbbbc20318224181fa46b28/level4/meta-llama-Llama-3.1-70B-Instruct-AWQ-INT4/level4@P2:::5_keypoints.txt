- Addresses multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where cheap approximations to arms are available.
- Proposes the Multi-Fidelity UCB (MF-UCB) algorithm adapting classical UCB to multi-fidelity settings.
- Proves MF-UCB achieves lower regret than naive strategies that ignore approximations.
- Provides a regret lower bound and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Theoretical analysis includes detailed comparison with classical UCB.
- Simulations demonstrate MF-UCB’s effectiveness across various scenarios and practical relevance.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Strengths: novel framework, new algorithm, thorough theory including lower bound, and convincing simulations.
- Weaknesses: assumes known costs for playing each arm, which may be unrealistic.
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed analysis of MF-UCB’s computational complexity.
- Suggestions: extend analysis to unknown/uncertain arm costs.
- Suggestions: provide detailed computational-complexity analysis and explore real-world applications (e.g., advertising, clinical trials).
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS 2016 subject to minor revisions addressing the suggestions.
