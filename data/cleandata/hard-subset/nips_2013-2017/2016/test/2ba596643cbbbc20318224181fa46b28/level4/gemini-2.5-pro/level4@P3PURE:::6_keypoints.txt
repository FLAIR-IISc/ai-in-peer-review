- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be played at M fidelities with increasing accuracy and cost.
- Motivates the setting with online advertising (short displays approximate long-term click rates cheaply).
- Proposes a new regret definition tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Presents a novel algorithm with theoretical analysis, including a regret lower bound.
- Provides simulations that support the theoretical findings.
- Analysis appears novel and is the paper's key contribution.
- Main concern: the theoretical analysis assumes known fidelity deviations ε^(m); it's unclear how these would be known in practice.
- Requests the authors to discuss practical implications of knowing ε^(m) or show how to relax/estimate this assumption.
