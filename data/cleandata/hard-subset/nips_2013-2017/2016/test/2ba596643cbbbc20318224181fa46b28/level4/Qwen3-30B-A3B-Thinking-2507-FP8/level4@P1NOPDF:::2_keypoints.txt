- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls, where cost increases with fidelity and regret is defined as cost × standard pseudo-regret.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.
- Main paper is well-written and clear; supplementary materials contain numerous typos/minor errors that impair readability.
- Frequent use of "up to a multiplicative constant" is potentially misleading because hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); recommend stating exact inequalities.
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB relies on accurate estimation of ζ to eliminate arms, and misestimating ζ could undermine performance; discuss limitations and mitigation strategies.
- Theoretical results are well-motivated and rigorously proved, representing a significant and interesting advancement.
