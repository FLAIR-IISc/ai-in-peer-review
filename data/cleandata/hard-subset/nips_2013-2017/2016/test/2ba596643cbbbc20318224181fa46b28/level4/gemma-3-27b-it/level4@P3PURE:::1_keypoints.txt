- Models multi-armed bandits with a novel fidelity component (choose precision/cost for each pull), proposes an algorithm (MF-UCB), and gives a regret bound plus a lower bound.  
- Addresses a relevant tradeoff between information, cost, and reward and could serve as a useful benchmark for future work.  
- Paper seems technically sound but provides insufficient explanation of results and assumptions.  
- The regret definition (multiplicative cost penalization) feels unnatural; an additive cost formulation should be considered and its effect on results analyzed.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on fidelity parameters ζ_m) appears artificial and opaque and lacks justification for general problems.  
- It’s unclear what to do if the ζ_m do not decay as Assumption 1 requires; no principled method is given to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption.  
- Although authors claim Assumption 1 is not crucial, it appears integral to proofs and may fundamentally influence algorithm design and performance.  
- The algorithm seems tailored to specific ζ_m configurations without clear justification, and the gap in the provided lower bound needs further investigation.  
- Theorem 2 and the comparison to UCB are hard to interpret: gains/losses depend on Δ and |K| through complicated sets K^(m), so a more explicit comparison is needed.  
- Overall presentation loses clarity when explaining results; clearer exposition would substantially strengthen the paper.  
- Minor typos: l.56 “near-optimal”; l.126 remove unnecessary period before “for all m < M”; l.179 use curly braces for |K|.
