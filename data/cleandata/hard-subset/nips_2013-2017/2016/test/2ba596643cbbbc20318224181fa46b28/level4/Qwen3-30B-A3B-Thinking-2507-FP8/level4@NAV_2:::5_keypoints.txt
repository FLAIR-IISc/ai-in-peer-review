- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework extending K-armed bandits with multiple fidelity levels (low-fidelity cheap but biased; high-fidelity expensive and accurate).  
- Formalizes the problem via a cost-weighted regret definition.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for multi-fidelity settings.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and a matching lower bound, demonstrating near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Builds on prior UCB and multi-fidelity optimization work but focuses on exploration-exploitation trade-off unique to bandits.  
- References recent related NIPS work [8] but could compare formulations/results more explicitly.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis; regret bounds are well-motivated and proven.  
- Carefully addresses biases introduced by lower-fidelity approximations—a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Exposition and clarity are excellent; formalism, intuition, and figures (e.g., Figure 1) are effective.  
- Originality is strong: formalizes a multi-fidelity bandit setting not previously analyzed in bandit literature.  
- High practical significance (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) where cheaper approximations exist.  
- Theoretical results show MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when lower fidelities are sufficiently cheap and informative.  
- Empirical results corroborate theoretical insights but are limited to synthetic data.  
- Main weakness: theoretical analysis depends on specific assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1 on bias decay) that may not hold in practice.  
- Clarity issue: paper should provide a more intuitive explanation for the choice of γ(m) in Equation 4.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution; accept with minor revisions addressing γ(m) clarity and clearer comparison with [8].
