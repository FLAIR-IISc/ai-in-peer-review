- Introduces a new MAB setting with fidelity-varying feedback where sampling cost increases with accuracy and regret is cost times pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower cumulative pseudo-regret bounds.  
- Main paper is exceptionally clear and well-written.  
- Supplementary material contains numerous typos and minor errors that reduce readability and risk confusion; fixing them is necessary.  
- Frequent use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” bounds is misleading because the constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); presenting precise inequalities would improve transparency.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB relies on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms but depends critically on accurate estimation of the fidelity parameter ζ.  
- Recommends discussing practical limitations and mitigation strategies for ζ estimation to strengthen the work.  
- Overall, the paper is a significant theoretical contribution with interesting theorems and rigorous proofs despite the above issues.  
- Appendix contains a detailed list of specific typos/errors (equation labels, variable substitutions, missing parentheses/superscripts, incorrect inequalities, and misspellings) that should be corrected.
