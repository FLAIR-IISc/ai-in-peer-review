This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where low-cost approximations of each arm are available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem. The work includes a theoretical analysis that provides regret bounds for the proposed method. A sufficient analysis of the lower bound is also presented, which demonstrates a clear theoretical improvement. The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The empirical evaluation, conducted through simulations, effectively demonstrates the practical benefits of the multi-fidelity bandit approach.
