- Introduces a new multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where pull accuracy (fidelity) and cost trade off.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret for MF-UCB.  
- Shows MF-UCB is near-optimal in certain cases via a lower bound.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, with solid proofs (mostly in the supplement).  
- Supplementary materials contain many typos and small errors that hurt readability.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading; exact inequalities would be clearer.  
- Practical applicability is unclear—MF-UCB may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity accuracies.  
- Paper lacks discussion of practical limitations and how to mitigate them.  
- Scores well on quality, clarity, and originality overall.  
- Acceptance pros: novel setting, sound algorithm/analysis, and significant theoretical contribution.  
- Acceptance cons: supplement errors, unclear practical applicability, and notation issues that need fixing.
