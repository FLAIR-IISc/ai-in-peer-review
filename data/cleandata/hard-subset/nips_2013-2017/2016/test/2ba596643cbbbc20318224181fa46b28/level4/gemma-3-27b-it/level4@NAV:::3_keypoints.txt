- Introduces a multi-fidelity extension of multi-armed bandits where each arm has M fidelity levels, with lower fidelities cheaper but potentially biased reward observations.
- Models lower-fidelity pulls as introducing bounded bias in expected reward (dependent on fidelity m) rather than increased variance.
- Proposes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.
- Provides formal regret bounds for the algorithm, but the bounds are complex and hard to interpret.
- Authors claim substantial improvement over standard UCB played at highest fidelity; simulations are presented to support this.
- Reviewer initially questioned the bias modeling choice (preferring variance), but found algorithm-selection examples reasonably justify bias.
- Skeptical of the advertising example and of using regret minimization in the algorithm-selection context.
- Finds the connection between the model and motivating applications somewhat tenuous, though not decisively flawed.
- Views the primary contribution as the problem formulation rather than novel algorithmic ideas.
- Notes the proposed algorithm is a fairly standard optimistic-exploration application.
- Finds theoretical performance guarantees difficult to readily interpret in practical terms.
- Critiques simulations as synthetic and hard to evaluate; Appendix C lists parameter settings but lacks justification.
- Raises concern that the reported 3–4× MF-UCB improvement may be due to specific parameter choices (e.g., ζ^(m)) rather than typical performance.
