- Introduces a new Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where arms can be sampled at different fidelities with associated costs and low-fidelity samples approximate high-fidelity distributions.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that adapts to available approximation sequences and costs, with regret analysis and a problem lower bound.
- Provides synthetic simulations showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Notation introduction is confusing and scattered, making definitions hard to find (e.g., the variable "s" is unclear and scarcely used).
- The advertising example is misleading and invokes non-stationarity inappropriately; a more fitting illustrative example is needed.
- The regret analysis is insufficiently justified because the optimal policy is taken as the arm with highest mean regardless of cost, potentially hiding sampling costs of low fidelities.
- Recommends adding a sample complexity or cost-complexity analysis, motivated by practical testing scenarios (e.g., prototype testing).
- Some parts of the text are unwieldy and need rewriting for clarity and flow.
- Paper has strong potential and interesting contributions but requires revisions on notation, examples, and theoretical justification.
- Overall recommendation: revise and resubmit after clarifying notation, replacing the example, strengthening regret justification, and considering sample/cost complexity analysis.
