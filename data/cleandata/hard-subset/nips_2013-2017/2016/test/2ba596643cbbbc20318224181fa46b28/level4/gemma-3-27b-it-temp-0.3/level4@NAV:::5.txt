This work investigates a variation of the standard stochastic K-armed bandit problem, specifically addressing scenarios where low-cost approximations for each arm’s reward are accessible. The authors formulate this as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical regret bounds for their approach. Supporting simulations validate the benefits of utilizing multi-fidelity information. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, with simulations effectively illustrating the performance of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method. Importantly, the theoretical analysis includes a lower bound demonstrating the method’s potential for improvement over existing techniques.
