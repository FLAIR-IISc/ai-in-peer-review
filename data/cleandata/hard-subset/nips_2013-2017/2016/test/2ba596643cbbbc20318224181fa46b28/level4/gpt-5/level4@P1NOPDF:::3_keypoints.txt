- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelity levels where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased estimates of true means.  
- Models low fidelity as introducing bias (bounded deviation), not increased variance.  
- Reviewer initially puzzled by bias modeling but largely convinced by examples; some specific justifications (e.g., short ad windows) remain unconvincing.  
- Unclear why cumulative regret is the right objective for some motivating applications like algorithm selection.  
- Main methodological novelty is the multi-fidelity bandit formulation rather than novel algorithmic ideas.  
- Proposes a natural UCB-style algorithm with confidence intervals widened to account for low-fidelity bias.  
- Theoretical contribution: regret bounds for the algorithm, claimed to often outperform always-highest-fidelity UCB.  
- Regret bounds are complicated and hard to interpret; theorems do not clearly quantify when and by how much the method improves.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited, with parameter choices in Appendix C given but not justified.  
- Reported empirical gains (MF-UCB reducing regret by 3–4×) may reflect specific parameterization (ζ^(m), cost/bias trade-offs) rather than general performance.  
- Lacks systematic exploration of parameter regimes, making practical relevance of results hard to assess.  
- Recommendations: improve interpretability of theoretical results, justify experimental parameters, and strengthen application-driven motivations.
