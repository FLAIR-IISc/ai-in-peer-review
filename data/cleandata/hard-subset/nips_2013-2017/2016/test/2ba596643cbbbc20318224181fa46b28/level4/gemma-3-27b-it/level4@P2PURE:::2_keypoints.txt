- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with varying sampling fidelities where sampling cost rises with accuracy and regret is cost × pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is clearly written and well-organized; supplementary material (major proofs) contains numerous typos/minor errors that hinder readability.  
- The reviewer provides a non-exhaustive list of specific appendix errors (misplaced symbols, missing parentheses, incorrect indices/inequalities, swapped variables, and typos).  
- Frequent use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” bounds is potentially misleading because hidden “constants” depend on parameters (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2).  
- Recommends presenting exact inequalities (as in the supplement) to improve transparency of results.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls may be sensitive to misestimation of ζ (fidelity accuracy).  
- Suggests discussing practical limitations and mitigation strategies for inaccurate ζ estimation to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting, rigorous, and a significant contribution, but clarity and practical discussion need improvement.
