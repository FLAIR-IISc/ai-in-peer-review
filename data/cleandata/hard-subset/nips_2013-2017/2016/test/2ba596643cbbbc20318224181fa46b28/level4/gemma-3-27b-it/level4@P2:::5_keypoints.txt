- Studies a multi-fidelity extension of the K-armed bandit where cheaper, lower-accuracy evaluations are available.  
- Formalizes arms playable at multiple fidelities with different costs and accuracies.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm extending UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides a theoretical regret analysis showing improved performance over fidelity-ignoring baselines.  
- Includes a lower bound that strengthens the theoretical contributions.  
- Demonstrates empirical support via simulations on synthetic settings.  
- Core idea: use cheap approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and concentrate expensive evaluations on promising ones.  
- Regret analysis shows near-optimality under certain conditions and quantifies multi-fidelity benefits.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Related work section acknowledges prior UCB and multi-fidelity work but could better cover recent contextual bandit and active learning advances.  
- The connection to active learning with weak/strong labelers (Zhang & Chaudhuri, 2016) is noted but deserves deeper treatment.  
- Key assumption that lower-fidelity means are within known ζ(m) of the highest-fidelity mean may be restrictive.  
- Needs discussion on sensitivity to misestimated ζ(m) and methods to adapt when ζ(m) is unknown.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address ζ(m) assumptions and expand related work.
