- Addresses multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits, proposes MF-UCB, and provides a regret lower bound.  
- Problem is interesting and relevant; could serve as a benchmark for future work.  
- Presentation lacks clear explanation of core assumptions and results, reducing clarity.  
- Regret definition penalizes resource consumption multiplicatively and seems unnatural; additive-cost analysis would be more standard and valuable.  
- Ad-display motivational example may be too narrow to justify the chosen reward/cost model.  
- Assumption 1 on fidelity errors (zeta_m) appears artificial, under-motivated, and potentially unrealistic.  
- Authors claim Assumption 1 is noncritical but still use it in proofs and algorithm design, creating a dependence that is not justified.  
- No general method given to select a subsequence of fidelities satisfying Assumption 1 when the original set does not—algorithm seems tailored to special zeta configurations.  
- Theorem 2’s interpretation is unclear; comparison baseline (UCB at highest fidelity) is not explicitly stated.  
- Performance characterization depends on complex quantities (Delta, [[k]], K^(m)) that are not explained intuitively, making benefits of MF-UCB hard to assess.  
- Noted gap in the lower bound needs further investigation and may indicate the need to redesign the strategy.  
- Minor editorial points: use "near-optimal" (l.56), remove unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (l.126), and use standard curly-brace set notation for [[k]] (l.179).
