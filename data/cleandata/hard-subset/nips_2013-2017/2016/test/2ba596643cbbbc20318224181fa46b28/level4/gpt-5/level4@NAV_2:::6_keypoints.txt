- Paper studies a new stochastic K-armed bandit where each arm can be queried at M fidelities with a cost-accuracy tradeoff.  
- Key idea: use cheap biased, low-fidelity pulls to prune clearly suboptimal arms and reserve costly high-fidelity pulls for promising arms, potentially outperforming standard UCB.  
- Motivating example: online advertising — short displays or restricted audiences approximate long-run click behavior under tight budgets.  
- Contributions: tailored regret notion for multi-fidelity, MF-UCB algorithm, theoretical guarantees including a lower bound, and simulation results; analysis appears original.  
- Major concern: modeling assumes bias terms ε^{(m)} (difference between mth-fidelity mean and highest-fidelity mean) are known a priori — unclear how to obtain these in practice.  
- Open question: can ε^{(m)} be learned or adapted online, or can the approach work under weaker/no assumptions about ε^{(m)}?
