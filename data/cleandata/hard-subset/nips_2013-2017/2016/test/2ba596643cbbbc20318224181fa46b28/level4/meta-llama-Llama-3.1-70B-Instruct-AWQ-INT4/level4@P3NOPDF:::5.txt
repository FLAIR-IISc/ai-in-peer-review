This paper proposes a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem, providing a theoretical framework for analyzing the trade-offs between exploration and exploitation in this setting.

The paper presents theoretical results on regret bounds, which provide a useful characterization of the algorithm's performance. In addition to the theoretical analysis, simulations are performed to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity on the algorithm's performance. The presentation is clear and easy to understand, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

The simulations effectively demonstrate the benefits of the multi-fidelity bandit method, showcasing its ability to balance exploration and exploitation in a more efficient manner than traditional bandit algorithms. Furthermore, the paper provides sufficient theoretical analysis on the lower bound of the proposed method, which highlights the theoretical improvements achieved by the multi-fidelity approach.

Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and well-executed study on the multi-fidelity bandit problem. The combination of theoretical and empirical results provides a convincing case for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
