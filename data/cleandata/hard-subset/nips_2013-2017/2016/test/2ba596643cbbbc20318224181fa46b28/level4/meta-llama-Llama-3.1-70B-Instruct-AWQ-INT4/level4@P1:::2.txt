This work introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be drawn with different accuracies (or fidelities). The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost by the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm that addresses this setting, MF-UCB, and provide an upper bound and a lower bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The main paper is clear and well-written, effectively conveying the main ideas and results. However, the supplementary materials, where most of the proofs are presented, contain many typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. Correcting these errors would greatly increase the readability of the paper.

Additionally, the use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant signs in the paper can be a bit misleading, as constants are not really constants. For instance, in Theorem 2, there is a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) that is hidden in the constant. Using the exact inequalities (which are written in the supplementary materials) could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical point of view, it is not clear that this algorithm can easily be applied to real-life problems. For instance, the strategy of MF-UCB (using low-accurate pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms) could suffer from inaccurate estimation of the ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion on the practical limitations and how to overcome them would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides a nice insight into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I think that this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
