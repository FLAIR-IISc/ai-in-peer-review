- Studies a multi-fidelity variant of stochastic K-armed bandits where high-fidelity outcomes are costly but cheaper biased approximations exist.  
- Formalizes the problem as a multi-fidelity bandit and introduces a cost-weighted regret metric capturing fidelity trade-offs.  
- Proposes a novel algorithm, MF-UCB, that uses cheap approximations to focus resources on promising arms.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical regret bounds for MF-UCB, including a compelling bound O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))).  
- Presents a matching lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Assumptions (Assumption 1) are clearly stated and justified, though their practical implications need more discussion.  
- Simulations effectively demonstrate empirical benefits and support theoretical claims.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well-organized.  
- Builds appropriately on classic bandit literature (e.g., Robbins, UCB) and motivates the approach with practical applications.  
- Connection to existing multi-fidelity optimization and RL work is acknowledged but lacks detailed comparison.  
- Discussion of alternative choices for qt in the regret definition is brief and could be expanded.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, practically relevant work with minor areas for improvement; recommendation to accept.
