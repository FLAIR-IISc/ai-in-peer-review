- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with M fidelities where higher fidelities are more accurate but costlier.  
- Proposes leveraging cheap, low fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserving high fidelities for promising arms.  
- Application example: online advertising using short-duration displays as cheap proxies for long-term CTRs.  
- Introduces a new regret definition, an algorithm, and provides theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound.  
- Simulation results support the theoretical claims; analysis appears novel and significant.  
- Major concern: assumption that fidelity deviations ε^(m) (deviation of mean at fidelity m from highest fidelity) are known a priori, which is unrealistic in practice; relaxing this would strengthen the paper.
