- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms can be evaluated at multiple fidelity levels with cheaper but biased lower-fidelity estimates.  
- Motivates practical scenarios (advertising, algorithm selection) where high-fidelity evaluations are costly and cheap biased proxies exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that dynamically selects fidelity levels to minimize cost-weighted regret.  
- Proves a regret bound showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when lower fidelities are significantly cheaper and per-fidelity gaps are large.  
- Provides a matching lower bound establishing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Builds on classic bandit theory while extending it to multi-fidelity settings distinct from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization literature.  
- Strength: high originality—fills a gap in bandit literature by formalizing cost-bias trade-offs across fidelities.  
- Strength: strong theoretical quality—rigorous regret analysis and elegant proof techniques (arm partitioning into K^(m) sets).  
- Strength: moderate-high significance—clear application potential and empirical results showing 3–4× improvement in synthetic tests.  
- Strength: moderate clarity—well-structured presentation, clear notation, precise algorithm description, helpful figures.  
- Weakness: regret bound is complex and the paper insufficiently highlights when it outperforms UCB in practical parameter regimes.  
- Weakness: application motivations (e.g., advertising bias) are plausible but not rigorously justified; objective choice in algorithm selection could be clarified.  
- Weakness: simulation parameters (e.g., ζ^(m) values) lack justification and sensitivity analysis; lower bound tightness gaps for bias-misled arms remain unresolved.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, original, and significant—minor clarifications suggested but recommends acceptance.
