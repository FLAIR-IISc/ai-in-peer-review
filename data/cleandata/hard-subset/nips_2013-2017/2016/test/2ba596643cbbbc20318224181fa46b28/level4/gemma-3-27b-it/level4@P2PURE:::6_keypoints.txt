- Studies a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be sampled at M fidelities with increasing accuracy and cost.  
- Argues leveraging low fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserving high fidelities for promising arms can outperform standard UCB-like algorithms.  
- Presents online advertising as a motivating application (low-cost early ad displays estimate long-term CTRs).  
- Defines a new regret measure tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Proposes an algorithm for this setting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound; analysis is novel.  
- Supports theoretical claims with simulations.  
- Main concern: assumption that deviations ε^(m) between fidelity means and highest fidelity are known a priori is unrealistic; unclear how to determine in practice.  
- Suggestion: explore relaxing or removing the known-ε^(m) assumption to strengthen the work.
