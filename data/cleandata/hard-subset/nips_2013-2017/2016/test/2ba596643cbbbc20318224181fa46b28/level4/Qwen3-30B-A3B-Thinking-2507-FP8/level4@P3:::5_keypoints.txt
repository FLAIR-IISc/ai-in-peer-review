- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework with cost-weighted regret where lower fidelities give cheaper but biased approximations of true rewards.  
- Formalizes sequential fidelity choices and introduces MF-UCB, an algorithm that dynamically allocates fidelities to balance exploration and exploitation.  
- Theoretically derives a tight regret bound for MF-UCB that matches a lower bound for most arms, showing strategic use of low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity queries.  
- Empirical synthetic results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, especially when low-fidelity costs are much smaller than high-fidelity costs.  
- Situates contribution against prior work: fills gap in cost-aware sequential fidelity modeling, complementary to GP multi-fidelity work and distinct from budgeted bandits, robotics MDPs, and active learning labeler setups.  
- Strength — Quality & Originality: novel formalism, technically rigorous regret analysis (Theorem 2) and near-optimality (Theorem 4), with principled algorithm design using fidelity-specific confidence bounds and switching criterion.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear intuition (K(m) partitions), reproducible algorithm and regret analysis.  
- Strength — Significance: addresses practical need to reduce costly evaluations with improved regret scaling and validated empirical gains.  
- Weakness — Assumption Restriction: reliance on Assumption 1 (ζ^(m) decay) limits applicability; if ζ^(m) decays slowly the regret advantage may disappear and needs further discussion/weaker conditions.  
- Weakness — Empirical Scope: experiments limited to Bernoulli rewards and do not test non-sub-Gaussian/heavy-tailed cases despite theoretical concentration assumptions.  
- Weakness — Lower Bound Gap: lower bound is tight for some arm groups but loose for others (e.g., K^(m)_7); paper should discuss how this gap might be closed in future work.  
- Overall verdict: strong, high-quality contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
