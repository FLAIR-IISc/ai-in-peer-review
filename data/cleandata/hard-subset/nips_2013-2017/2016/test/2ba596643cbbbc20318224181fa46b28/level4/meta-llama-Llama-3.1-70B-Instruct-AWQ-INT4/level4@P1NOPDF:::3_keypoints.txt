- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelities; lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded bias in reward estimates.

- Proposes and analyzes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.

- Provides mathematically rigorous regret bounds that the reviewer finds difficult to interpret.

- Claims MF-UCB often outperforms always-highest-fidelity UCB; supported by simulations.

- Reviewer questioned modeling low fidelity as bias (vs. variance); some motivating examples justify bias but others (e.g., short ad displays) do not.

- Unclear why regret minimization is the right objective in the algorithm-selection motivating example.

- Reviewer sees a weak (but not fatal) connection between the formal model and real motivating applications.

- Main novelty is the multi-fidelity problem formulation; the algorithm itself is a standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty approach.

- Simulation methodology: parameter settings are listed in Appendix C but not justified; observed 3–4× improvement may be an artifact of chosen parameters and may not generalize.
