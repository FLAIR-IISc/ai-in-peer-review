- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic multi-armed bandit where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased (error bounds zeta_m) and highest fidelity is unbiased.
- Proposes a multiplicative cost-weighted regret metric and a new algorithm MF-UCB claiming better performance than standard high-fidelity UCB.
- Provides regret analysis and a lower bound, arguing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.
- Strength: models cost–quality trade-offs for practical settings (ads, hyperparameter tuning, experiments) and appears technically solid.
- Concern: the multiplicative, cost-weighted regret may not be natural for all applications; authors should compare/justify against additive cost penalties.
- Concern: Assumption 1 (decay rate constraint on zeta_m) seems artificial and under-motivated; unclear if algorithm/performance depends critically on it.
- Suggestion: explain how to handle violations of Assumption 1 (e.g., choosing a subsequence of fidelities) or better justify the assumption.
- Concern: Theorem 2 is highly technical and opaque; need a high-level explanation of the regret bound, the arm partition K^(m), J_k^K, and when/how much MF-UCB outperforms UCB.
- Concern: there is a gap between the algorithm’s upper bound and the lower bound that needs further analysis and may motivate algorithmic improvements.
- Overall: compelling model and promising start, but impact limited by unclear assumptions and presentation; clarity and stronger justification needed.
- Minor comments/typos: hyphenate "near-optimal" (Line 56); remove extra period (Line 126); clarify J_k^K notation (Line 179).
