- Problem introduced: MF-MAB — K arms, M fidelities; lower fidelities are cheaper but provide biased approximations with known bias bounds ζ(m).

- Main contributions: formal MF-MAB formulation and cost-aware regret, MF-UCB algorithm, and upper and lower regret bounds showing near-optimality.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated problem formulation capturing cost–information trade-offs relevant to real applications.

- Strength: MF-UCB is an intuitive, principled extension of UCB that accounts for statistical uncertainty and fidelity bias; fidelity-switching criterion is well-justified.

- Strength: Comprehensive theoretical analysis with an upper regret bound and a problem-specific lower bound demonstrating substantial improvements over naive high-fidelity UCB.

- Weakness: Main regret bound (Theorem 2) is complex and hard to interpret; partitioning into K(m) obscures dependence on core parameters (λ(m), ζ(m), Δ_k^(m)).

- Weakness: Connection between the formal model and motivating applications is sometimes tenuous (e.g., claim that shorter ad displays induce bias rather than variance); alternative fidelity models and objectives (e.g., best-arm identification) are not explored.

- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation on synthetic tasks with unexplained parameter choices (costs λ(m), biases ζ(m)), so reported 3x–4x gains may not generalize.

- Overall: Well-written, significant and technically strong paper likely to inspire follow-up work, despite clarity and empirical limitations.

- For acceptance: introduces a novel practical problem, proposes an elegant algorithm, and provides strong theoretical near-optimality results.

- Against acceptance: theoretical bounds are hard to interpret, application links need stronger justification, and experiments lack parameter-justification to assess generality.
