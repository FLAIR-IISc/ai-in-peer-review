This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem, providing a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the presence of multiple fidelities.

The paper's main contribution is the development of the Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB) algorithm, which adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategy to the multi-fidelity setting. The authors prove that MF-UCB achieves better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations. They also provide a lower bound on the regret and show that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper is well-written, and the presentation is easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithm, and the theoretical analysis. The simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF-UCB algorithm in various scenarios, showcasing its potential in real-world applications.

The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed comparison of MF-UCB with the classical UCB algorithm. The lower bound on the regret is also a significant contribution, as it provides a benchmark for evaluating the performance of multi-fidelity bandit algorithms.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical rigor with practical relevance. The authors provide a clear motivation for the problem, and the simulations demonstrate the potential of the MF-UCB algorithm in real-world applications.

Overall, the paper is well-structured, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The paper is a valuable addition to the literature, and it has the potential to inspire further research in this area.

Strengths:

* Novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the presence of multiple fidelities
* Development of the MF-UCB algorithm, which adapts the classical UCB strategy to the multi-fidelity setting
* Thorough theoretical analysis, including a lower bound on the regret
* Simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF-UCB algorithm in various scenarios

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the cost of playing each arm is known, which may not always be the case in practice
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the MF-UCB algorithm

Suggestions for improvement:

* Consider extending the analysis to the case where the cost of playing each arm is unknown or uncertain
* Provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the MF-UCB algorithm
* Explore the application of the MF-UCB algorithm to real-world problems, such as online advertising or clinical trials

* The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits
* The paper has the potential to inspire further research in this area
