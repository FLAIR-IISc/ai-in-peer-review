- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework where pull cost scales with fidelity and regret is cost × pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm with an upper bound and a matching lower bound on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant: novel formulation, tight bounds, and well-structured proofs in the main text.  
- Main text is clear and compelling; overall work is a meaningful advancement in cost-aware bandits.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typographical and notational errors that impair clarity (e.g., misplaced index in Eq. (8), inconsistent H^(m) vs. K^(m), missing denominator, mislabeled variables).  
- A comprehensive proofreading and correction of the supplementary proofs and notation are required.  
- Asymptotic notation (“up to multiplicative constants”) is potentially misleading because the hidden constant absorbs the algorithm parameter ρ, which varies with the problem.  
- Authors should state explicit inequalities (as in the supplement) rather than relying on vague multiplicative-constant phrasing.  
- Practical applicability concerns: MF-UCB’s arm-elimination via low-fidelity pulls depends on accurate estimation of ζ (fidelity parameter).  
- Need analysis/discussion of sensitivity to ζ misestimation and mitigation strategies (e.g., adaptive fidelity scheduling or robustness guarantees).  
- Connection to application domains should be strengthened to demonstrate real-world utility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on fixing supplementary errors, refining asymptotic notation, and addressing practical robustness.
