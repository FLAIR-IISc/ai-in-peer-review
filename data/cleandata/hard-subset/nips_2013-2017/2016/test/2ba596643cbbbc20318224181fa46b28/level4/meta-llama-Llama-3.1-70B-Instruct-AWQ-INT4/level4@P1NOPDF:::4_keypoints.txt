- Paper proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments where the method outperforms UCB, but is not ready for publication.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s is undefined at first use and barely used later).  
- The advertising example is misleading—drawing on non-stationarity is out of context and inappropriate for this problem.  
- The regret formulation is questionable: optimal policy always picks the highest-mean arm regardless of cost, forcing low-fidelity samples of the best arm and obscuring sampling costs.  
- A sample (cost) complexity analysis would be more appropriate/valuable, motivated by practical testing scenarios with mandated low-fidelity tests.  
- The manuscript is unwieldy in places and needs revision for clarity and streamlining.
