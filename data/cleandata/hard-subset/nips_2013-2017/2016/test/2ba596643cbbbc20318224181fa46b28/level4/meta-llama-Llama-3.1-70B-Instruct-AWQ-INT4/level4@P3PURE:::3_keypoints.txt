- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can be played at M fidelities, with lower fidelities cheaper but inducing bounded bias in the reward estimate.
- Proposes a UCB-based algorithm that increases confidence-interval widths to account for bias from low-fidelity samples.
- Provides regret bounds for the algorithm, but the bounds are somewhat difficult to interpret.
- Claims the algorithm often achieves significantly better regret than always playing at highest fidelity with standard UCB.
- Simulation results are presented to support the claim, but parameter choices used in simulations are not justified.
- Concern that observed 3–4× improvement in simulations may be due to chosen parameter settings (e.g., ζ^(m)) rather than reflecting typical practical gains.
- Initially puzzled by modeling low fidelity as added bias rather than added variance, but some motivating examples (e.g., algorithm selection) make the bias model plausible.
- One motivation (shorter ad display producing biased long-interval estimates) is unconvincing.
- In the algorithm-selection example, it’s unclear why minimizing regret is the appropriate objective for an algorithm designer.
- Overall, the main contribution is the multi-fidelity MAB formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.
- Connection between the model and motivating applications is somewhat weak but not a disqualifying flaw.
