- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit where each arm has M fidelities: lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded bias from the true reward.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-dependent bias.  
- Regret analysis is theoretically sound but yields complex, hard-to-interpret bounds.  
- Paper claims MF-UCB typically outperforms standard UCB (which always uses highest fidelity).  
- Simulations report MF-UCB beating UCB by ~3–4×, but results are brief.  
- Modeling low fidelity as bias (not variance) is initially counterintuitive though partly justified by ML algorithm-selection examples.  
- The ad-display example and the use of regret minimization for algorithm selection are unconvincing.  
- Connection between the model and real applications is weaker than claimed, though not a fatal flaw.  
- Main contribution is formalizing the multi-fidelity bandit problem—novel and well-motivated.  
- The algorithm itself applies standard “optimism under uncertainty” and lacks major methodological innovation.  
- Regret bounds are cumbersome and provide limited practical insight.  
- Reported empirical gains depend on specific fidelity-bias parameters (ζ^(m)), with Appendix C offering no justification.  
- Unclear whether the 3–4× improvement generalizes or is sensitive to chosen ζ^(m) values; simulations don’t test robustness.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to justify simulation parameter choices and clarify motivation for bias-focused modeling.
