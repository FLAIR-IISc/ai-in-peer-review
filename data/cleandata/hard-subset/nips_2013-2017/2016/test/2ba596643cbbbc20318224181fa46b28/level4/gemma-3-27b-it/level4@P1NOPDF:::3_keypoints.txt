- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem: K arms, M fidelities; lower fidelities are cheaper but yield biased estimates with bounded deviation.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence bounds to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Proves regret bounds that are technically correct but complex and difficult to interpret.  
- Claims MF-UCB often substantially outperforms always-playing-highest-fidelity UCB.  
- Provides synthetic simulations that support the claim but are brief and hard to fully assess.  
- Appendix C lists simulation parameter values but gives no justification, raising concern results may be artifacts of chosen parameters.  
- Reported empirical gains are substantial (roughly 3–4× regret reduction) but it’s unclear if this is typical in practice.  
- Modeling low fidelity as bias (rather than variance) was initially questionable; algorithm-selection examples make the bias model plausible.  
- The advertising example for bias is unconvincing, and minimizing regret may not be the right objective in the algorithm-selection setting.  
- Main technical contribution is the formalization of the multi-fidelity MAB; the algorithm itself is a fairly standard application of optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty.  
- Overall, the link between the model and motivating applications is somewhat weak, though not critically so.
