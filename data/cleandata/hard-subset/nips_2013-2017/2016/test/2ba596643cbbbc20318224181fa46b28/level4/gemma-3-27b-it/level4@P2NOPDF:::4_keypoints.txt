- Paper proposes a novel MF-MAB framework and an adaptive algorithm with regret analysis and a lower bound; empirical results show gains over UCB, but the reviewer finds it unsuitable for publication now.
- Notation is confusing and disjointed (definitions scattered); variables like ‘s’ are introduced without immediate definition and seldom used—requires centralized, consistent notation.
- The advertising motivating example is misleading; invoking non-stationarity is out of place and distracts from the core MF-MAB problem—use a more focused example (e.g., iterative prototyping).
- The regret formulation is not convincingly justified: it emphasizes long-term reward and may ignore the cost–reward trade-off, potentially favoring high-mean arms irrespective of fidelity cost.
- The paper lacks a sample/cost-complexity analysis; a cost-focused guarantee (identify the best arm with high probability while minimizing testing cost) is practically important and should be included.
- Prose and wording are awkward in several sections and need a thorough revision to improve clarity and presentation.
