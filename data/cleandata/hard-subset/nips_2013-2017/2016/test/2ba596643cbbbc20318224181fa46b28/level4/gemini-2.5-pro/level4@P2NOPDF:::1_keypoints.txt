- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model where each fidelity m has cost c_m and error bound zeta_m, proposes MF-UCB, and analyzes regret claiming improvements over single-fidelity UCB.

- The problem formulation is interesting and practically relevant, capturing the trade-off between information cost and value in online learning.

- Major concern: the paper uses a multiplicative cost-based regret without sufficiently justifying it or comparing to the more natural additive (total reward − total cost) formulation.

- Major concern: Assumption 1 (constraints on decay of zeta_m) seems artificial and under-motivated; its role in proofs and whether the algorithm relies on it is unclear.

- Major concern: Theorem 2 and related bounds are hard to interpret—baseline specification is unclear, dependence on Delta, [[k]], and K^(m) is complex, and practical conditions for MF-UCB’s advantage are not illustrated.

- Suggestion: provide simplified corollaries or illustrative examples showing how problem parameters (Delta, costs, zeta_m) affect MF-UCB vs. baselines, and discuss the gap between upper and lower bounds.

- Minor: hyphenate "near-optimal" (l.56), remove unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (l.126), and use standard set notation (curly braces) for [[k]] (l.179).
