- Paper models multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity levels: cheaper, biased low-fidelity pulls (known error ζm) versus costly unbiased high-fidelity pulls, and proposes MF-UCB plus a regret lower bound.  
- Problem is interesting and relevant for trading information, cost, and reward in online learning.  
- Presentation and explanations are insufficient; results and assumptions are not well motivated or clearly communicated.  
- Regret definition is questionable: authors use a multiplicative cost model, but additive cost penalization may be more natural and should be explored.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on ζm) appears artificial and opaque; its general validity is unclear.  
- It’s unclear how to proceed if ζm do not decay as required — authors should show how to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption.  
- Algorithm design seems tailored to particular ζ configurations without justification that these are the “right” sequences.  
- The gap in the lower bound needs more investigation and may require redesigning the proposed strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and the regret bound are hard to interpret; gains depend on Δ and complex sets K(m), which should be explained more explicitly.  
- Overall the paper starts well conceptually but loses clarity in formal results and their implications.  
- Minor typos: line 56 “near-optimal”; line 126 extra period before “for all m < M”; line 179 suggest using curly braces for [[k]].  
- For acceptance: interesting problem and potentially useful benchmark. Against acceptance: weak explanations, questionable regret model, opaque assumption, lower-bound gap, and overly technical presentation.
