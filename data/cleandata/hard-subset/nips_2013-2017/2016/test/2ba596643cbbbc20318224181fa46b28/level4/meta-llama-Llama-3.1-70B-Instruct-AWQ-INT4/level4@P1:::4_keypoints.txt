- Proposes a new multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where arms can be sampled at different fidelities with associated costs.  
- Provides an algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs, plus regret analysis and a lower bound.  
- Empirical simulations on synthetic problems show the proposed algorithm outperforms UCB.  
- Notation introduction is confusing and scattered; key symbols (e.g., s) are undefined where first used.  
- Using advertising as the motivating example is misleading and introduces irrelevant non-stationarity discussion.  
- Regret analysis is questionable because the optimal policy is defined by highest mean regardless of cost, masking the cost of unnecessary low-fidelity sampling.  
- The paper lacks a sample-complexity (cost-complexity) analysis appropriate for multi-fidelity settings and practical testing scenarios.  
- Writing is unwieldy and many parts need streamlining for clarity.  
- The framework is original and potentially impactful if further developed.  
- Significance is plausible for applications like prototype testing constrained by regulations and costs.  
- Overall quality is mixed: solid technical contributions but missing important analysis and clarity.  
- Recommendation: major revisions — clarify notation, replace or fix motivating example, add cost/sample-complexity analysis, and improve exposition.
