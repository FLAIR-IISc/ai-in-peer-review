- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be observed at M fidelities with increasing cost and decreasing bias ζ(m), ζ(M)=0.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a modified UCB algorithm that adaptively selects fidelity levels to minimize regret.  
- Defines regret to account for the cost of using lower fidelities and provides theoretical guarantees showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when high-reward arms are few.  
- Formalizes a previously under-addressed extension of classical bandits, distinct from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Builds on UCB methodology but addresses new algorithmic challenges specific to multi-fidelity observations.  
- Strong technical contribution: clear regret upper bound and a matching lower bound under certain conditions.  
- Assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1) are reasonable and well-justified.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes helpful figures illustrating algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show consistent improvements over standard UCB.  
- Main motivation is practical (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) where high-fidelity observations are costly.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for modeling low fidelities as biased rather than higher-variance estimates.  
- Weakness: the regret bound is complex and hard for practitioners to interpret; the paper should clarify when and by how much gains occur.  
- Weakness: simulation parameter choices lack justification, so the reported 3–4× improvement may be sensitive to these choices.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel contribution with solid theory and empirical support; minor issues do not undermine acceptance recommendation.
