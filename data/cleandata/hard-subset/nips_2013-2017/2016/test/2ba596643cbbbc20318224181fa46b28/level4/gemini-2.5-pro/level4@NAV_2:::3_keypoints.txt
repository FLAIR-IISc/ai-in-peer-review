- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms can be pulled at M fidelities with lower-cost but biased estimates bounded by known ζ(m).
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that incorporates fidelity-dependent bias to use cheap pulls for elimination and expensive pulls for promising arms.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: an upper regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound showing near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Extends classical UCB literature by explicitly trading off cost and information quality through biased approximations, distinct from budgeted or random-cost bandits.
- Strength — originality/significance: novel, well-motivated formulation with potential impact on cost-varying evaluation tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, simulations).
- Strength — technical quality: solid theoretical foundations with upper and lower regret bounds.
- Strength — clarity of algorithm: intuitive, natural extension of UCB with a clear fidelity-selection mechanism.
- Weakness — clarity of theoretical results: main regret bound (Theorem 2) is complex and hard to interpret without a simplified corollary or breakdown.
- Weakness — connection to applications: assumptions (e.g., bias vs. variance, known ζ(m)) and choice of regret objective need stronger justification for motivating examples.
- Weakness — experiments: empirical evaluation uses synthetic settings with unexplained parameter choices; lacks sensitivity analysis or real-world-driven experiments to validate reported gains.
