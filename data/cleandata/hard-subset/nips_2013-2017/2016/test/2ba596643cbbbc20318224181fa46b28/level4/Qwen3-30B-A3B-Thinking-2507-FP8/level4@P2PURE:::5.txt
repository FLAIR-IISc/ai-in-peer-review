

This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem in which low-cost approximations for each arm are available. The authors formalize this as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical regret bounds. The presentation is clear and well-structured. Simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the multi-fidelity bandit method in reducing regret. The work includes sufficient theoretical analysis establishing the lower bound for the proposed approach, confirming its theoretical optimality.
