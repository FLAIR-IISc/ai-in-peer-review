- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: each arm can be sampled at M fidelities with lower cost but fidelity-dependent bounded bias.  
- Proposes a UCB-style algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for bias-induced uncertainty.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are complex and hard to interpret, though claimed to often beat standard high-fidelity UCB.  
- Simulations show MF-UCB outperforming UCB by ~3–4× in many synthetic cases.  
- The choice to model low fidelity as bias (rather than variance) is questioned; some motivating examples support it, others (ad display) do not.  
- The link between the model and practical applications is tenuous, and in ML algorithm selection it's unclear why regret is the right objective.  
- Main contribution is formalizing the multi-fidelity bandit problem rather than methodological novelty.  
- Methodology is a fairly standard application of optimism/UCB, lacking significant innovation.  
- Regret guarantees suffer from interpretability issues and limited practical insight.  
- Simulation details (Appendix C) lack justification for parameter choices (e.g., ζ^(m)), so observed gains may be artifacts and not generally representative.
