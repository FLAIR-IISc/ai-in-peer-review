- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where arms can be evaluated at differing costs (fidelities).  
- Core insight: use cheap, low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms early and reserve expensive high-fidelity evaluations for promising arms.  
- Formalizes a new fidelity-aware regret definition and proposes a corresponding algorithm.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, including a regret upper bound and a matching lower bound.  
- Empirical simulations align with theory and demonstrate regret improvements over standard UCB.  
- Setting is well-motivated for cost-sensitive applications (e.g., online advertising with short- vs. long-term evaluations).  
- Major practical limitation: assumption that fidelity deviations ε^(m) are known a priori is unrealistic in many real-world scenarios.  
- Suggests need to relax this assumption via adaptive online estimation of ε^(m) or algorithmic robustness to estimation errors.  
- Manuscript should discuss practical trade-offs between fidelity cost, regret reduction, and computational overhead compared to standard UCB.  
- Minor but essential technical corrections listed (nine specific line/equation fixes provided by reviewer).  
- Overall assessment: strong conceptual contribution and solid analysis; recommend acceptance pending resolution of the ε^(m) assumption and the listed technical corrections.
