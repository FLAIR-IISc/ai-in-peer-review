This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be drawn with varying accuracies or fidelities. The cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy, and the regret is defined as the product of the cost by the usual pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, to address this setting and provide both upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret of this algorithm.

The main paper is well-written and clear. However, the supplementary materials, which contain most of the proofs, require improvement. They are marred by numerous typos and minor, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader. A non-exhaustive list of these errors is provided below. Correcting these errors would significantly enhance the readability of the paper.

Furthermore, the use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant notation in the paper can be misleading, as constants are not truly constant. For instance, in Theorem 2, there is a factor ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) hidden in the constant. Using the exact inequalities, as written in the supplementary materials, could improve the clarity of the results.

From a practical perspective, it is unclear whether this algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For example, the strategy of MF-UCB, which uses low-accuracy pulls to eliminate greatly suboptimal arms, may suffer from inaccurate estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion on the practical limitations and potential solutions would benefit this work.

From a theoretical perspective, this paper provides valuable insights into the problem, along with interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, this work is a significant and interesting contribution.
