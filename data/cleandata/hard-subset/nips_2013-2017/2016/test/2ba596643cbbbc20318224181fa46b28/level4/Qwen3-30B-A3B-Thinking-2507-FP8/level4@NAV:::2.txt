

This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be pulled at varying accuracies (fidelities), with pull cost increasing with desired accuracy. The regret is defined as the product of cost and standard pseudo-regret. The authors propose the MF-UCB algorithm, establishing both upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well-written, though the supplementary materials suffer from numerous typographical and minor technical errors that impair readability (see specific corrections below). Addressing these would significantly enhance the paper's presentation. Additionally, the pervasive use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is potentially misleading, as hidden algorithmic parameters (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2) are absorbed into constants. Explicitly stating the exact inequalities (as provided in the supplementary materials) would improve result clarity. From a practical standpoint, it remains unclear how readily MF-UCB can be applied to real-world scenarios; the strategy of using low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms may be compromised by inaccurate estimation of the fidelity parameter ζ. A discussion of practical limitations and mitigation strategies would strengthen the contribution. Theoretically, the work offers valuable insights, novel theorems, and rigorous proofs, constituting a significant and interesting advance.
