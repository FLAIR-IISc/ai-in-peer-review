- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting: K arms, M fidelity levels where lower fidelity is cheaper but biased.
- Fidelity model: a pull at level m yields a sample whose expectation deviates from the true reward by a bounded amount depending on m.
- Proposes and analyzes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for low-fidelity bias.
- Theoretical results give regret bounds, but the bounds are complex and hard to interpret.
- Authors claim the regret bound often outperforms always-playing-highest-fidelity UCB.
- Short simulation section is provided to support empirical claims.
- Reviewer initially questioned modeling low fidelity as added bias rather than variance, but found the paper’s motivating examples largely convincing.
- Reviewer is unconvinced by the ad-duration example and questions whether regret minimization is the right objective for ML algorithm selection.
- Overall connection between model and motivating applications is tenuous but not critically flawed.
- Main contribution is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.
- Difficulty interpreting regret bounds is a notable drawback.
- Simulations use synthetic problems and Appendix lists parameters but does not justify the parameter choices.
- Empirical results show MF-UCB improving over UCB by factors of 3–4 in many cases, but it’s unclear if this reflects typical behavior or specific parameter tuning (e.g., values of ζ^(m)).
