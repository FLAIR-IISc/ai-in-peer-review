This paper proposes a novel variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem, which is a significant contribution to the field. The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including a regret bound for the proposed Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB) algorithm. The authors also derive a lower bound, which demonstrates the near-optimality of MF-UCB.

The presentation is clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning. The simulations performed to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity are convincing and provide a practical validation of the theoretical results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its thorough theoretical analysis, which provides a solid foundation for the proposed algorithm. The authors have done an excellent job of analyzing the regret bound and deriving a lower bound, which demonstrates the theoretical improvement of MF-UCB over traditional UCB algorithms.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to make the content accessible to a broad audience. The use of figures and tables is helpful in illustrating the key concepts and results.

In terms of the conference's criteria, the paper scores high on quality, clarity, and originality. The problem formulation is novel, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous and thorough. The paper is well-written and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it proposes a new algorithm that can be applied to a wide range of problems, including online advertising, finance, and other fields. The authors have demonstrated the practical relevance of their work through simulations, which shows the potential impact of their research.
