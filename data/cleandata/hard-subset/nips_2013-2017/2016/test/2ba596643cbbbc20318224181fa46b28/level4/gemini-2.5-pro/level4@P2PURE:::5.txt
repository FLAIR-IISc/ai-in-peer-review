This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where low-cost, low-fidelity approximations for each arm are available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem. The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including regret bounds and a lower bound for the proposed method, which demonstrates a theoretical improvement over existing approaches. The effectiveness of the multi-fidelity bandit method is validated through simulations. Overall, the presentation is clear and easy to understand.
