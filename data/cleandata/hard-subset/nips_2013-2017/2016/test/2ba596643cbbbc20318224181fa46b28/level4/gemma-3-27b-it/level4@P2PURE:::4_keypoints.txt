- Paper proposes a novel Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with different costs and low-fidelity samples approximate high-fidelity distributions.  
- Authors present an adaptive algorithm for varying sequences of approximations and costs, provide regret analysis, and prove a lower bound.  
- Numerical simulations on synthetic problems show the proposed algorithm outperforms UCB.  
- Overall assessment: interesting framework with potential impact, but currently unsuitable for publication.  
- Notation and definitions are confusing and dispersed (e.g., variable s is used before being defined and then rarely used).  
- The advertising motivating example is misleading—ties to non-stationarity are outside the paper’s scope; recommend revisiting or replacing the example.  
- The regret analysis is questionable: standard regret targets long-term reward/cost ratio, but the paper’s formulation favors the highest mean reward regardless of cost, obscuring low-fidelity sampling costs.  
- Recommend a sample-complexity or cost-complexity analysis (motivated by prototype testing/product development) to better capture multi-fidelity trade-offs.  
- Parts of the manuscript are unwieldy and need streamlining to improve clarity.
