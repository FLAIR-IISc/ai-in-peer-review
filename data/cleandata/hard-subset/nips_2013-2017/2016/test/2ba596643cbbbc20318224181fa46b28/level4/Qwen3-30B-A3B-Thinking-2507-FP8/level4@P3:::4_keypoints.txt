- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms have multiple biased, cheaper fidelity levels.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively selects arms and fidelity levels to minimize regret under a budget.
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and a lower bound showing near-optimality under certain assumptions.
- Empirical synthetic results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB and uses lower fidelities strategically.
- Builds on classical MAB and UCB literature and is distinct from prior multi-fidelity optimization/active learning work by formalizing regret in this bandit setting.
- Related to budgeted bandits but differs by treating fidelities as biased approximations rather than cost-reward ratios.
- Strength: original and well-motivated formulation with real-world relevance (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection).
- Strength: technically sound regret analysis with tight bounds demonstrating MF-UCB’s advantage when lower fidelities are informative and cheap.
- Strength: clear empirical validation illustrating algorithmic benefits.
- Weakness: inconsistent and poorly defined notation (e.g., undefined/late-defined variables like s; scattered symbols for fidelities, costs, and regret components).
- Weakness: problematic regret formulation—conceptual inconsistency about incorporating cost versus selecting the arm with highest mean.
- Weakness: advertising example mentions non-stationarity, which is irrelevant to the paper’s stationary bandit model.
- Weakness: lacks practical cost-complexity analysis and detailed discussion of real-world constraints (e.g., regulatory testing).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, strong theory, and empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/notation issues, flawed regret formulation, misaligned examples, and missing practical analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Reject pending substantial revisions to fix conceptual/regret formulation, streamline notation, and align examples with the model.
