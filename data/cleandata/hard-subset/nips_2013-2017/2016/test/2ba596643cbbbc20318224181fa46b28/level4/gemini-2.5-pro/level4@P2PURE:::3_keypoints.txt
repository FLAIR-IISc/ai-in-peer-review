- Problem: Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits where arms can be played at cheaper, lower-fidelity levels that induce bounded bias in rewards.
- Method: Proposes a UCB-style algorithm that inflates confidence bounds to account for bias from low-fidelity samples.
- Theory: Provides complex regret bounds that are hard to interpret but claimed to often improve over standard high-fidelity UCB.
- Experiments: Brief synthetic simulations support the claimed 3–4x improvement but are limited in scope.
- Novelty vs. simplicity: The multi-fidelity formulation is novel, but the algorithm is a straightforward application of optimism in the face of uncertainty.
- Motivation concerns: The connection to applications is tenuous—modeling lower fidelity as bias (not variance) is debatable and some motivating examples (e.g., ad display duration, ML model selection) are unconvincing.
- Evaluation concerns: Empirical parameter choices (e.g., bias bounds ζ^(m)) are unexplained, so reported improvements may reflect favorable parameterization rather than typical performance.
