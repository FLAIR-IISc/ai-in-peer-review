- Paper proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework and algorithm with regret analysis and synthetic gains, but reviewer finds it currently unsuitable for publication.

- Notation is unclear and fragmented (e.g., variable s used before definition and then barely discussed), hindering comprehension.

- The advertising illustrative example is misleading by invoking non-stationarity, which is not central to the problem; a more relevant example is needed.

- The regret analysis is questionable: standard regret optimizes reward/cost ratio, but the presented framework favors the highest-mean arm regardless of cost, potentially hiding costs of low-fidelity sampling.

- A cost-complexity (sample complexity) analysis is missing and would be valuable for practical settings where the goal is cost-efficient identification of the best design via low-fidelity proxies.

- Portions of the text are cumbersome and require revision for clarity and streamlining.
