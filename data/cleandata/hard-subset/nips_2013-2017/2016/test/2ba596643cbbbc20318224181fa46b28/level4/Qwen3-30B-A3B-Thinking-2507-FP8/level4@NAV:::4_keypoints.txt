- Contribution: Proposes "Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandits" where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelity levels with differing costs, plus an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, and a problem-specific lower bound.  
- Experiments: Synthetic experiments claim the proposed algorithm outperforms UCB.  
- Overall verdict: Potentially impactful but currently unsuitable for publication.  
- Notation: Definitions are scattered and confusing (e.g., variable s introduced without definition and only defined later, then barely used).  
- Example: The advertising example is misleading by invoking non-stationarity irrelevant to the setting; a more appropriate real-world example is needed.  
- Regret analysis: Lacks justification—policy should consider reward/cost trade-offs (reward/cost ratio), but the paper treats the optimal arm as highest mean reward independent of cost, obscuring costs of unnecessary low-fidelity sampling.  
- Cost analysis: A cost-complexity analysis (rather than sample complexity) is highly relevant and should be included/motivated (e.g., product prototyping with mandatory low-fidelity tests before costly validation).  
- Presentation: Several sections are unwieldy and require streamlining for clarity and conciseness.
