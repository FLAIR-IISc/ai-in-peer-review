This paper studies a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem in which inexpensive, lower-fidelity approximations are available for each arm. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity (MF) bandit problem and provide theoretical results on regret bounds. They also present sufficient theoretical analysis of the lower bound for the proposed method, demonstrating a theoretical improvement. Simulations are conducted to illustrate the impact of leveraging multiple fidelities, and they clearly demonstrate the benefits of the MF bandit approach. The presentation is clear and easy to follow.
