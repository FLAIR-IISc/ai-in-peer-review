- Paper proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework (arms sampled at varying fidelities with costs), an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments showing gains over UCB, but is judged unsuitable for publication.

- Confusing notation: definitions are scattered and some variables (e.g., s) are introduced without timely definition, harming readability.

- Misleading motivating example: using advertising and non-stationarity as motivation is inappropriate since the paper assumes a stationary setting; a different example (e.g., product prototyping) is suggested.

- Flawed regret analysis: the formulation treats the optimal arm as highest mean reward regardless of cost, ignoring reward-to-cost tradeoffs and weakening the optimal policy definition when low-fidelity sampling is forced.

- Metric mismatch: the regret metric does not properly incorporate sampling costs, obscuring the true cost of using lower fidelities versus unnecessary higher-fidelity samples.

- Need for cost-aware sample complexity: reframe analysis around minimizing cumulative testing cost to reach a target confidence (e.g., find best product with 95% confidence), which better matches multi-fidelity applications.

- Unpolished writing: sections are awkwardly phrased and lack flow; the paper needs thorough revision for clarity.

- Overall: core idea is promising but requires fixing notation, replacing the misleading example, reworking the analysis to incorporate cost (or use sample complexity), and polishing prose before publication.
