- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity stochastic multi-armed bandit: each arm has M fidelity levels with known bias bounds ζ(m) and increasing costs λ(m), and defines a cost-weighted regret with the MF-UCB algorithm and matching lower bound.  
- Novelty: first formalization of regret with theoretical guarantees for multi-fidelity bandits in the bandit literature.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical problem where high-fidelity samples are expensive; well-motivated by applications like advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Strength: MF-UCB is well-designed and the theoretical analysis is technically sound, showing improved regret over UCB when K(m) sets are small.  
- Strength: Lower bound analysis supports near-optimality of the proposed approach.  
- Weakness: Regret definition uses a multiplicative cost term λ(m)·r_t that is less intuitive than a standard additive cost penalty and needs justification.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (rapid decay of ζ(m)) appears arbitrary, is undocumented in general settings, and is crucial only for comparisons to UCB.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks methods for handling cases where Assumption 1 is violated; an automatic fidelity-selection mechanism would strengthen the work.  
- Weakness: Main regret bound (Theorem 2) is technically complex and hard to interpret; dependence on partition sets K(m) and indices obscures practical implications.  
- Weakness: Unexplained gap between the lower bound and MF-UCB performance for certain arms (in K(m)); practical impact of this gap is unclear.  
- Quality: Theoretical results are rigorous and thorough but need more discussion on the practical applicability of Assumption 1.  
- Clarity: Well-structured but overly technical presentation; fails to clearly communicate when and why MF-UCB outperforms UCB to non-specialists.  
- Originality: The multi-fidelity bandit framework is a meaningful and original extension of standard bandit problems.  
- Significance: Potentially important for cost reduction in real applications, but practical significance would be stronger with real-world experiments beyond synthetic tests.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; revise to (1) reconsider additive regret formulation, (2) justify or relax Assumption 1, (3) simplify and better explain the regret bound, and (4) address the lower-bound performance gap.
