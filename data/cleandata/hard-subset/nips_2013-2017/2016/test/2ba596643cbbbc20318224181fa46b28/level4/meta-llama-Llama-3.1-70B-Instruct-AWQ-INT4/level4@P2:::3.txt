The paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the multi-armed bandit problem, where cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. This "multi-fidelity" setting is formalized, and a new algorithm, MF-UCB, is proposed based on upper confidence bound techniques. The algorithm uses cheap lower fidelity plays to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive high fidelity queries for a small set of arms with high expected reward.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of MF-UCB, including a regret bound that is compared to the standard UCB algorithm. The results show that MF-UCB can achieve better regret than UCB, especially when the cost of playing arms at different fidelities varies significantly. The paper also presents a lower bound on the regret, which demonstrates that MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.

The strengths of the paper include its clear and concise presentation of the multi-fidelity bandit problem, the proposed algorithm, and the analysis of its performance. The paper also provides a good discussion of the related work and the connections to other areas, such as online advertising and machine learning.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The connection between the model and the motivating applications is not entirely convincing, and some of the examples seem contrived. The performance guarantee of MF-UCB is also somewhat hard to interpret, and the simulation results on synthetic problems are not thoroughly justified. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the practical implications of the results and the potential applications of the multi-fidelity bandit problem.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and interesting framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the multi-armed bandit problem. While there are some weaknesses in the paper, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field.
