This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where inexpensive, low-fidelity approximations of each arm's reward are available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The theoretical analysis is a significant strength, providing rigorous regret bounds for the proposed algorithm. Crucially, the authors derive a lower bound that quantifies the theoretical improvement gained by leveraging multiple fidelities. These theoretical claims are well-supported by simulations that effectively demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method.
