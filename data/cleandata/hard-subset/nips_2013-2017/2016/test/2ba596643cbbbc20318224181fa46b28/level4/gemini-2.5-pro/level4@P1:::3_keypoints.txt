- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic multi-armed bandit problem where each arm has M fidelity levels with cheaper, potentially biased lower-fidelity samples bounded by known fidelity-dependent bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that adjusts confidence bounds to account for bias from lower-fidelity samples.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and claims MF-UCB can significantly outperform standard high-fidelity-only UCB, supported by synthetic simulations.  
- Main contribution: formulation of the multi-fidelity bandit problem, a well-motivated and practically relevant extension of classical bandits.  
- Weakness: connection between the theoretical bias model and motivating applications is tenuous; some examples (e.g., short ad displays) are unconvincing.  
- Weakness: appropriateness of regret minimization for applications like algorithm selection is not well-justified.  
- Technical critique: MF-UCB is a straightforward optimism-based extension; the presented regret bounds are complex and hard to interpret for scaling insights.  
- Experimental critique: simulation parameter choices are listed but unjustified, leaving it unclear whether reported 3–4× improvements are typical or result from favorable parameterization.
