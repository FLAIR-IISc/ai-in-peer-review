- Introduces a multi-fidelity variant of the stochastic K-armed bandit problem where cheap approximations for arms are available.  
- Formalizes the problem and provides a theoretical framework to analyze exploration–exploitation trade-offs in this setting.  
- Derives regret bounds that give insights into the proposed method’s performance.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical lower-bound analysis showing limitations and improvements over existing approaches.  
- Conducts simulations that demonstrate the practical effectiveness and impact of the multi-fidelity bandit method.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible, and the paper offers a valuable contribution to the multi-armed bandit literature.
