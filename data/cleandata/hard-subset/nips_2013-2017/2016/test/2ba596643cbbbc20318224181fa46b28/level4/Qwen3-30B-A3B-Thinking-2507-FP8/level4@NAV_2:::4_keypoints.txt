- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelities with biased cheaper estimates and proposes MF-UCB to minimize regret.  
- Proves near-optimal regret bounds (Theorem 2) and a lower bound (Theorem 4), and validates MF-UCB empirically against naive UCB on synthetic data.  
- Novelty: formalizes a distinct multi-fidelity bandit setting different from prior budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous analysis and algorithm design that leverages low-fidelity approximations to reduce high-fidelity queries.  
- Significance: addresses practical cost-constrained exploration-exploitation problems with relevance to domains like advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Clarity: problem formalization and algorithm presentation are well-structured, though some notational issues exist.  
- Notational confusion: key symbols (e.g., s in X_k^{(m),s}) are defined non-locally and underutilized, hindering reproducibility.  
- Example misalignment: advertising example overemphasizes non-stationarity which is irrelevant; better examples (e.g., simulation prototyping) should illustrate cost-fidelity trade-offs.  
- Regret interpretation critique: reviewer notes criticism in related work misinterprets the role of low-fidelity sampling—oracle uses highest fidelity, low-fidelity is a cost-saving strategy and analysis accounts for this.  
- Missing context: lacking a cost-complexity perspective (e.g., minimizing total test cost to identify best product) that would strengthen practical significance.  
- Writing flow: some sections (e.g., Section 4.1) are dense and need streamlining for accessibility.  
- Recommendation: overall a valuable contribution with strong theory and empirical support but requires revisions on notation, examples, and cost-complexity motivation to meet conference standards.
