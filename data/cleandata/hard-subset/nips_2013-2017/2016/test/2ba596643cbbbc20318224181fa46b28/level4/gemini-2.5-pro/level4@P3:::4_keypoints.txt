- Introduces the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit problem where each arm can be played at M fidelities with differing costs and bounded approximation error ζ(m).
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that chooses arm and fidelity to use cheaper evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms.
- Provides a theoretical upper bound on cost-weighted regret showing MF-UCB reserves expensive plays for promising arms.
- Derives a corresponding lower bound, arguing MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Empirical simulations on synthetic data show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Novel and practically motivated formulation applicable to hyperparameter tuning, simulations vs. prototypes, and advertising.
- Technically complete and sound: intuitive algorithm and thorough theoretical analysis (upper and lower bounds).
- Core idea—using low-fidelity cheap information to prune arms before high-fidelity evaluation—is clear and well demonstrated.
- Presentation suffers from scattered notation and organizational issues; definitions (e.g., sample index s) appear late.
- The choice of cost-weighted regret is not fully justified versus alternative objectives like reward-to-cost ratio.
- The problem might better suit a best-arm identification (pure exploration) or cost-complexity analysis for some applications.
- The online advertising motivating example conflicts with stationarity assumptions and could confuse readers.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance but require revisions to streamline notation, justify the formulation, and refine motivating examples.
