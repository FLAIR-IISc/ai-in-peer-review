This paper proposes a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical results on regret bounds. Simulations are performed to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity, and the presentation is easy to follow. The simulations effectively demonstrate the benefits of the multi-fidelity bandit method. Furthermore, the paper provides sufficient theoretical analysis on the lower bound of the proposed method, which confirms its theoretical improvement.
