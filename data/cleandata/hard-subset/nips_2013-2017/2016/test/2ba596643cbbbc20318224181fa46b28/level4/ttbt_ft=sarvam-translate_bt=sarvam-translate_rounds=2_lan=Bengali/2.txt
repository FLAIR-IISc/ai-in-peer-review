This work presents a new multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be drawn with varying precision (or fidelity). The cost of drawing an arm with the desired precision increases, and regret is defined as the product of the cost and the natural pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm that solves this setting, MF-UCB, and provides an upper bound and a lower bound on the algorithm's increasing pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well-written. However, this is not true for the supporting materials, where there is mostly evidence. These contain many typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse the reader (see below for an incomplete list). Correcting those errors would greatly improve the readability of the paper. Furthermore, I believe that the multiplier-constant symbols used in the paper may be somewhat misleading, as the constants are not actually constants: for example, in theorem 2, a factor \rho (a parameter of the algorithm) is hidden within the constant. Using the correct inequalities (which are written in the supporting materials) can improve the clarity of the results. From a practical standpoint, it is not clear to me that this algorithm can be easily applied to real-life problems. For example, the MF-UCB's technique (using less accurate draws to eliminate extremely weak arms) may be subject to an incorrect assumption of \ zeta (fidelity precision). I believe that a discussion of practical limitations and how to overcome them would be beneficial for this work. From a theoretical point of view, this paper provides a beautiful insight into the problem, as well as interesting theorems and rigorous proofs. Overall, I believe this work is an important and interesting contribution.
