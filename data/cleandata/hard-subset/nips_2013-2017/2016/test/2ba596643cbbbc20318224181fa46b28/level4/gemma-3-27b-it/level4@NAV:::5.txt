This work investigates a variation of the standard stochastic K-armed bandit problem, specifically addressing scenarios where low-cost approximations for each arm’s evaluation are accessible. The authors formulate this as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and provide theoretical regret bounds for their approach. Empirical evaluation through simulations validates the benefits of utilizing a multi-fidelity framework. The paper is clearly written and readily understandable, and the simulations effectively illustrate the performance gains achieved by the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method. Furthermore, the inclusion of sufficient theoretical analysis concerning a lower bound demonstrates a clear theoretical advantage for the presented methodology.
