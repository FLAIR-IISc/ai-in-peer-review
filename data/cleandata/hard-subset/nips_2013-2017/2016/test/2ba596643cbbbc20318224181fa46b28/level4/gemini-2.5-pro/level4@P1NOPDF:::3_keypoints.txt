- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can be evaluated at M fidelities with lower fidelities being cheaper but biased.
- Modeling choice: lower fidelity induces a bounded, fidelity-dependent bias (not increased variance).
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that inflates confidence bounds to account for biased low-fidelity samples.
- Theoretical analysis yields complex regret bounds that are hard to interpret.
- Authors claim the regret bounds are often significantly tighter than standard UCB restricted to highest fidelity.
- Primary contribution is the novel formulation; the algorithm is a relatively straightforward optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty application.
- Connection to motivating applications is tenuous: ad-duration example’s bias claim is not substantiated.
- Questionable applicability: unclear why regret minimization is the right objective for algorithm-selection tasks.
- Simulation section is brief and uses synthetic data with parameter choices detailed in Appendix C but without justification.
- MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB by factors of ~3–4 in many simulations, but this improvement may be an artifact of specific parameter (ζ⁽ᵐ⁾) choices.
- Overall, the bias-based model is reasonable for some examples but the linkage to practical scenarios and interpretability of guarantees are disappointing.
