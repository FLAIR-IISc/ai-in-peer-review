This paper presents a novel multi-armed bandit framework incorporating variable fidelity, where the cost of sampling an arm is linked to its accuracy. The authors define regret as the product of this cost and the standard pseudo-regret, and propose the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting. They provide both upper and lower bounds on the algorithm’s cumulative pseudo-regret. The core of the paper is clearly articulated and well-written. However, the supplementary materials, which contain the majority of the proofs, suffer from numerous typos and minor errors that detract from readability and could potentially cause confusion (a detailed list is provided below). Rectifying these issues would significantly improve the paper’s clarity. 

Furthermore, the use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” bounds feels somewhat imprecise, as the constants often conceal algorithm-dependent parameters – for example, the parameter ρ in Theorem 2. Presenting the more precise inequalities, as found in the supplementary materials, would enhance the transparency of the results. 

From a practical standpoint, the algorithm’s applicability to real-world scenarios is not immediately obvious. The initial phase of MF-UCB, which relies on low-accuracy pulls to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms, may be sensitive to inaccuracies in the estimation of ζ (fidelity accuracy). A discussion of these practical limitations, and potential mitigation strategies, would strengthen the work.

Despite these points, the paper offers valuable theoretical insights into this problem, along with interesting theorems and rigorous proofs. Overall, this is a significant and worthwhile contribution to the field.
