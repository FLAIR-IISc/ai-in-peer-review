- Studies multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits, introduces MF-UCB, and proves regret bounds — a novel, practically relevant extension of bandits.  
- Problem formulation and motivation (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) are clear and compelling.  
- MF-UCB builds on the UCB framework; algorithm is clearly presented.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorem 2 upper bound, Theorem 4 lower bound) and supporting simulations demonstrate technical soundness.  
- Connection to existing bandit literature is appropriate and well-positioned.  
- The adopted regret definition is unclear and feels artificial; an additive resource-penalty might be more intuitive.  
- Assumption 1 (decay rate of fidelity parameters zeta_m) appears arbitrary and needs stronger justification and discussion of practical implications.  
- The authors claim Assumption 1 is noncritical, but it seems integral to proofs and requires explanation of alternatives (e.g., selecting a subsequence of fidelities).  
- There is a gap between the proved regret bound and the lower bound, suggesting the strategy may not be optimal and warranting further investigation/improvements.  
- Theorem 2’s dependence on Δ, |K_g|, and the sequence of sets K^(m) is technically complex and hard to interpret in practice.  
- The paper needs more intuitive explanations and concrete examples to illustrate gains of MF-UCB over standard UCB.  
- Simulations indicate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, supporting empirical relevance.  
- Presentation and notation are complex, hindering accessibility and broader impact.  
- Recommendation is rejection with major revisions: clarify regret, justify Assumption 1, bridge the theoretical gap, and improve interpretability.  
- Minor comments: reconsider "near-optimal" wording, fix punctuation at Line 126, and use curly braces for sets at Line 179.
