- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework with fidelity-dependent costs, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB.  
- Paper is intriguing and potentially impactful but currently unsuitable for publication without further development.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s defined long after first use and rarely used); needs a more organized, concise presentation.  
- The advertising example is misleading because it introduces irrelevant non-stationarity; replace with a more appropriate example such as prototype testing.  
- The regret formulation is questionable: standard regret optimizes reward-to-cost trade-offs, but here the optimal policy always selects the highest mean regardless of cost, which can hide sampling costs.  
- The analysis should be made more nuanced to explicitly account for reward-versus-cost trade-offs.  
- A sample-complexity or cost-complexity analysis (identify best arm with high probability while minimizing cumulative testing cost) would be valuable and practically motivated.  
- Several sections are unwieldy and need thorough revision to improve clarity, coherence, and flow.
