- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem where each arm can be sampled at M fidelities trading cost vs bias.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that uses low-fidelity samples to discard poor arms and reserves high-fidelity samples for promising arms.
- Formalizes a cost-aware regret notion and supports the algorithm with simulations.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: regret upper bounds for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound showing near-optimality.
- Strength: novel and practically significant problem formulation capturing cost–accuracy trade-offs in real-world tasks.
- Strength: intuitive and effective MF-UCB algorithm with fidelity-switching based on confidence intervals.
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical guarantees including both upper and lower bounds.
- Main weakness: assumes known bias bounds ζ^(m) for each fidelity; unclear how to obtain or how sensitive results are to misspecification—extension to unknown ζ^(m) is needed.
