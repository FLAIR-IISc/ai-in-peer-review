The paper "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit" studies a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalize this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem and develop a novel upper confidence bound procedure, MF-UCB, which adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs. The paper provides theoretical results on the regret bound of MF-UCB and demonstrates its near-optimality under certain conditions. Simulations are performed to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity on the regret of MF-UCB compared to the standard UCB algorithm.

The main idea of the paper is to use cheaper approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve expensive higher-fidelity plays for a small set of promising arms. The authors formalize this intuition by defining a new notion of regret that accounts for the cost of the fidelity at which an arm is played. The MF-UCB algorithm is designed to minimize this regret by maintaining an upper confidence bound for each arm at each fidelity and playing the arm with the highest upper bound.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the regret bound of MF-UCB, including a lower bound that demonstrates the near-optimality of the algorithm. The analysis is based on a partitioning of the arms into sets based on their fidelity means and the use of concentration inequalities to bound the probability of playing suboptimal arms.

The simulations demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity on the regret of MF-UCB compared to the standard UCB algorithm. The results show that MF-UCB outperforms UCB on all the simulated problems, with a smaller gradient of the regret curve. The number of plays by MF-UCB and UCB at each fidelity is also illustrated, showing that most of the very suboptimal arms are only played at the lower fidelities.
