

This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be pulled at varying accuracy levels (or fidelities), with pulling cost increasing with desired accuracy. The regret is defined as the product of cost and standard pseudo-regret. The authors propose the MF-UCB algorithm, establishing both upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is well-written and clear. However, the supplementary materials contain numerous typos and minor errors that impair readability (see list below). Correcting these would significantly enhance the paper’s accessibility.  

The paper frequently uses "up to a multiplicative constant" notation, which is potentially misleading since the hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2). Explicitly stating the exact inequalities (present in the supplementary material) would clarify the results.  

Practically, it remains unclear how readily MF-UCB applies to real-world problems. The algorithm’s strategy—using low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms—relies on accurate estimation of ζ (fidelity accuracy). If ζ is misestimated, this could undermine performance. A discussion of practical limitations and mitigation strategies would strengthen the contribution.  

Theoretically, the work offers valuable insights, with well-motivated theorems and rigorous proofs. Overall, it presents a significant and interesting advancement.
