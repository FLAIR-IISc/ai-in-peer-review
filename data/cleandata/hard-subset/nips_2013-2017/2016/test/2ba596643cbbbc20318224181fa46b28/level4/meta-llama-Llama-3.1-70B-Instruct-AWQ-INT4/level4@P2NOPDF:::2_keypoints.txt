- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with arms drawable at varying fidelities, where pull cost increases with accuracy and regret is cost times pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- Main paper is well-written and clear; supplementary materials contain many typos/minor errors that impede readability.  
- A non-exhaustive list of appendix errors is provided; fixing them would significantly improve clarity.  
- The use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because constants hide parameter dependence (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2); recommend using exact inequalities.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may fail if ζ (fidelity accuracy) is estimated inaccurately; discussion of limitations and remedies is needed.  
- Theoretically solid contribution with interesting theorems and proofs; overall a significant and interesting paper.
