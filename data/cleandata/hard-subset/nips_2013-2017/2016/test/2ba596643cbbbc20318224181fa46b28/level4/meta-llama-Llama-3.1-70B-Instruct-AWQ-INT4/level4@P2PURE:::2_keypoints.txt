- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be pulled at varying fidelities and higher accuracy incurs higher cost.  
- Defines regret as the product of cost and the usual pseudo-regret and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret of MF-UCB.  
- Main paper is well-written and clear.  
- Supplementary materials (proofs) require improvement due to numerous typos and small errors that confuse the reader.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden constants depend on the algorithm parameter ρ; exact inequalities would improve clarity.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s strategy of using low-accuracy pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms may fail if ζ (fidelity accuracy) is inaccurately estimated; a discussion of limitations and remedies is needed.  
- Theoretical contribution is valuable, with interesting theorems and generally solid proofs.
