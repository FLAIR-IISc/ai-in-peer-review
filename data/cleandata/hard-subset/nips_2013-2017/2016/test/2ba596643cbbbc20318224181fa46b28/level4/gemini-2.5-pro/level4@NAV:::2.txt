The paper presents a novel multi-armed bandit framework where arms can be pulled at varying levels of accuracy, or fidelity. In this setting, the cost of an arm pull is coupled with its fidelity, and a new regret metric is introduced, defined as the standard pseudo-regret multiplied by the associated cost. To address this problem, the authors develop the MF-UCB algorithm and support it with a theoretical analysis that establishes both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.

The main body of the paper is commendably clear and well-articulated. Unfortunately, this level of quality does not extend to the supplementary materials, which house the majority of the proofs. The supplement is marred by numerous typographical and minor errors. While these appear to be easily fixable, in their current state they significantly impede the reader's ability to follow the technical arguments. A thorough correction of these issues, a non-exhaustive list of which is provided below, would substantially improve the paper's overall readability.

Additionally, a point of concern is the use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation in the main results. This can be somewhat misleading, as these "constants" conceal dependencies on key parameters. A notable example is in Theorem 2, where a factor of ρ, an algorithm parameter, is absorbed into the constant. The clarity of the results would be enhanced by presenting the full, exact inequalities from the supplementary materials directly in the main paper.

From a practical standpoint, it remains unclear how readily the proposed algorithm can be deployed in real-world scenarios. For example, the core strategy of MF-UCB—leveraging low-fidelity pulls to quickly discard highly suboptimal arms—seems potentially sensitive to inaccuracies in the estimation of the fidelity-accuracy parameter, ζ. The paper would benefit from a dedicated discussion on such practical limitations and potential strategies to mitigate them.

Despite these points, the theoretical contributions of this paper are strong. It offers valuable insights into the multi-fidelity bandit problem, backed by interesting theorems and fundamentally sound proofs. Overall, this work represents a significant and compelling contribution to the field.
