

This work introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting in which each arm can be pulled with varying accuracy (or fidelity), where the cost of pulling an arm increases with the desired accuracy. The regret is defined as the product of the cost and the standard pseudo-regret. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, and establish both an upper bound and a lower bound on its cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well-written. However, the supplementary materials are poorly presented, with most proofs containing numerous typos and minor, easily correctable errors that could confuse the reader (see the non-exhaustive list below). Correcting these errors would significantly improve the paper's readability. Additionally, the use of "up to constant factors" notation is potentially misleading, as the constants depend on algorithm parameters. For instance, Theorem 2 hides a factor of ρ (a parameter of the algorithm) within the constant. Using the exact inequalities (provided in the supplementary materials) would enhance the clarity of the results. From a practical perspective, it is unclear whether this algorithm can be readily applied to real-world problems. Specifically, MF-UCB's strategy (using low-accuracy pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms) may suffer from inaccurate estimation of ζ (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion of practical limitations and potential mitigation strategies would strengthen the paper. Theoretically, the work provides valuable insights, interesting theorems, and solid proofs. Overall, this is a significant and interesting contribution.
