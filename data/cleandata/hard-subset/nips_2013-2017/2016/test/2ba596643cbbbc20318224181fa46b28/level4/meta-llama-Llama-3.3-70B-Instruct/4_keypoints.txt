- Introduces a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework with fidelity-dependent sampling costs, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, a lower bound, and simulations showing improvement over UCB.  
- Overall promising but currently unsuitable for publication without further development.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s used before defined and then rarely used).  
- Advertising example is misleading or poorly matched to the paper’s scope; a different motivating example is recommended.  
- Regret analysis is hard to justify here because the optimal policy always picks the arm with highest mean, potentially masking costs of unnecessary low-fidelity sampling.  
- Recommends adding a sample/cost complexity analysis (e.g., motivated by product-testing before mass production) to better capture practical trade-offs.  
- Paper is wordy and cumbersome in places and needs revision for clarity, conciseness, and flow.
