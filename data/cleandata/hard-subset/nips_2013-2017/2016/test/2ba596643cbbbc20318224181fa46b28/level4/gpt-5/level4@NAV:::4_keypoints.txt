- Overall: Proposes a Multi-fidelity MAB framework and adaptive algorithm with regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments outperforming UCB — interesting and potentially impactful, but currently unsuitable for publication.

- Notation: Presentation is confusing and fragmented; key symbols (e.g., s) are defined after use and scarcely employed.

- Motivation/example: The advertising example is misleading — linking ad display time to non-stationarity is out of scope; a different motivating example is needed.

- Regret formulation: The regret analysis is hard to justify — it benchmarks the highest-mean arm regardless of cost and forces low-fidelity sampling of the optimal arm, obscuring true cost trade-offs.

- Alternative analysis: A cost/sample-complexity perspective may be more appropriate (e.g., prototype testing in product development where low-fidelity tests are required before expensive tests).

- Presentation: Several parts are unwieldy and the paper needs a thorough editing pass to streamline and clarify.
