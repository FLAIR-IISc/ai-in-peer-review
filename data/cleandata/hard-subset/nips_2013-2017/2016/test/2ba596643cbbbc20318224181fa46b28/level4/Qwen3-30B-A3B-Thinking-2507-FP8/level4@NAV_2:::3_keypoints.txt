- Introduces a multi-fidelity MAB framework: each arm has M fidelities with known bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m).
- Defines a cost-weighted regret metric that incorporates sampling cost and bias.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that dynamically selects fidelity levels to minimize regret.
- Proves a regret bound: MF-UCB achieves O(∑_k Δ_k^(M) λ(J_k^K)/ψ(Δ_k^(J_k^K))) and can outperform standard UCB when low fidelities are sufficiently cheap and accurate.
- Provides a matching lower bound for certain classes of arms.
- Empirical synthetic results show MF-UCB outperforms UCB, using low fidelities for suboptimal arms and high fidelities for near-optimal arms.
- Strength: original, practically motivated problem formulation filling a gap in bandit literature.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis establishing near-optimality under assumptions.
- Strength: situates well against related work (budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization).
- Weakness: main regret bound is technically intricate and hard to interpret without deep proof study.
- Weakness: modeling low fidelity as bias (vs. variance) needs stronger real-world justification; algorithm-selection motivation is thin.
- Weakness: simulation parameter choices (e.g., ζ values) are insufficiently justified, so reported improvements may be sensitive.
- Weakness: practical significance of improvements in real-world settings is not convincingly argued; clearer interpretation and parameter justification needed.
