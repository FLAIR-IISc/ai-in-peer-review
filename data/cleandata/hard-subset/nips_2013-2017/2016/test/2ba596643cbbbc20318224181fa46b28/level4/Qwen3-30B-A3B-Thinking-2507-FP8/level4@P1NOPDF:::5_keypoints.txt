- Introduces a multi-fidelity variant of the stochastic K-armed bandit where cheap approximations of arms are available.  
- Formalizes the problem and derives theoretical upper bounds on regret for the proposed algorithm.  
- Provides complementary lower bounds that match the upper bounds up to constant factors, supporting optimality of the regret guarantee.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible.  
- Simulations empirically demonstrate substantial regret reduction compared to standard bandit algorithms, especially when low-fidelity evaluations are abundant.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of computational-complexity trade-offs across fidelity levels.  
- Overall, a solid, technically sound contribution with rigorous analysis and convincing empirical validation.
