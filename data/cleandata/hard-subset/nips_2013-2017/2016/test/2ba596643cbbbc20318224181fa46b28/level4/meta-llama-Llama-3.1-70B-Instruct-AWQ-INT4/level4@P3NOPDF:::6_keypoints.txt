- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelities where cheaper, lower-fidelity plays approximate rewards at lower cost.  
- Key strategy: use low-cost fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-cost fidelities for promising arms, potentially outperforming standard UCB.  
- Presents practical motivation (e.g., online advertising: short displays approximate long-term CTRs while minimizing cost).  
- Proposes a new regret definition, an algorithm, theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound, and simulation results; analysis is novel and valuable.  
- Major modeling concern: assumes fidelity deviations ε^{(m)} are known a priori; unclear how to obtain these in practice and relaxing this would improve practicality.
