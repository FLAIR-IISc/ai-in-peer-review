This paper introduces a novel and practical variant of the stochastic multi-armed bandit problem, termed the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit. In this setting, each arm can be pulled at various "fidelities," where lower fidelities provide cheaper but more biased estimates of the true reward. The authors propose a cost-weighted notion of regret appropriate for this framework, where a strategy is evaluated against a total capital budget.

To address this new problem, the authors develop the Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB) algorithm. This algorithm intelligently leverages cheap, low-fidelity pulls to quickly discard suboptimal arms, reserving expensive, high-fidelity pulls for a smaller set of promising candidates. The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including an upper bound on the regret of MF-UCB and a corresponding lower bound, demonstrating the near-optimality of the proposed approach under certain conditions. The main paper is well-written, clearly motivating the problem and presenting the proposed algorithm and its theoretical guarantees.

**Strengths:**

*   **Originality and Significance:** The multi-fidelity formulation is a novel and well-motivated extension of the classical bandit problem, with clear connections to real-world applications like online advertising and algorithm selection. The work addresses an important gap by formalizing the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in the presence of variable-cost, variable-accuracy information sources.
*   **Technical Quality:** The paper provides a solid theoretical contribution. The MF-UCB algorithm is an elegant and intuitive extension of the UCB principle to the multi-fidelity setting. The regret analysis is rigorous, and the inclusion of both upper and lower bounds provides a comprehensive understanding of the problem's complexity and the algorithm's performance.
*   **Clarity:** The main body of the paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the core ideas accessible.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

1.  **Clarity of Supplementary Material:** While the main paper is clear, the supplementary material, which contains the core of the theoretical proofs, would benefit significantly from careful proofreading. It currently contains numerous typos and minor errors that can hinder readability and make the arguments difficult for a reader to verify. Correcting these issues would greatly improve the overall quality and completeness of the work. A non-exhaustive list of suggested corrections is provided at the end of this review.

2.  **Clarity of Theoretical Results:** The use of asymptotic notation (`.=` and `.&`) in the main paper, while useful for conveying intuition, can be slightly misleading as it hides dependencies on key parameters. For instance, in Theorem 2, the hidden "constant" in the regret bound depends on the algorithm's parameter ρ. While the full inequalities are present in the appendix, stating the main theorems with their explicit dependencies would improve the precision and clarity of the results for the reader.

3.  **Discussion of Practical Limitations:** From a practical standpoint, the paper could be strengthened by a discussion on the algorithm's robustness and limitations. The performance of MF-UCB relies on prior knowledge of the fidelity bias bounds (ζ(m)). In many real-world scenarios, these values may not be known precisely. A discussion on the sensitivity of the algorithm to misspecified ζ values, and potential strategies for estimating or adapting to these parameters, would greatly enhance the paper's practical significance and provide valuable guidance for practitioners.

**Overall Assessment:**
This paper presents a significant and interesting contribution to the multi-armed bandit literature. The problem formulation is novel, the proposed algorithm is intuitive, and the theoretical analysis is solid. Despite some issues with the presentation of the proofs in the appendix and a lack of discussion on practical robustness, the core ideas are strong and well-supported. I believe this work is a valuable addition to the field.
