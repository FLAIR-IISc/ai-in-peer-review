- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem: K arms, M fidelity levels with lower cost but bounded bias in reward estimates.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Provides mathematically rigorous regret bounds, but the bounds are difficult to interpret.  
- Argues MF-UCB can outperform always-highest-fidelity UCB based on the regret analysis.  
- Simulation results show MF-UCB reduces regret in some scenarios.  
- Simulation parameter choices (Appendix C) are not well-justified, limiting confidence in generality.  
- Modeling low fidelity as increased bias (rather than variance) is plausible for some examples but may not fit all applications.  
- The connection between the model and motivating applications is weak.  
- Main novelty is the problem formulation; the algorithm is a fairly standard optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty approach.  
- Performance guarantees need clearer interpretation to improve practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to justify simulation parameters, clarify applicability, and make guarantees more interpretable.  
- Strengths: novel formulation and rigorous analysis.  
- Weaknesses: weak application linkage, under-justified simulations, and hard-to-interpret guarantees.
