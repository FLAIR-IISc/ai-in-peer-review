- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: K arms, M fidelity levels where lower fidelity reduces cost but introduces bias in reward estimates.  
- Bias is modeled as a bounded deviation in expected reward at lower fidelities rather than increased variance.  
- Authors propose a UCB-based algorithm that expands confidence intervals to account for bias-induced uncertainty.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are presented but are complex and difficult to interpret.  
- Authors claim the multi-fidelity approach typically outperforms high-fidelity UCB in regret.  
- A brief simulation section supports the claimed empirical improvement.  
- Reviewer questions the choice of bias (vs. variance) to characterize low fidelity, noting variance-based models also seem plausible.  
- Some motivating examples (e.g., ML algorithm selection) partially justify the bias model, but other examples (short ad display intervals) are unconvincing.  
- It is unclear why regret minimization is the appropriate objective for ML algorithm designers, weakening the application link.  
- Main technical contribution is the multi-fidelity formulation; the algorithm is essentially a standard application of optimism/UCB.  
- Disappointment with the complexity of the regret guarantees and concern that simulation results (3–4× improvement) may depend on unexplained parameter choices (sensitivity to ζ^(m)).
