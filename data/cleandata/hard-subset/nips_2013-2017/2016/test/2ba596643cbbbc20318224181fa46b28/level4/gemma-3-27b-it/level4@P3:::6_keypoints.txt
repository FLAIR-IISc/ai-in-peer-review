- Studies a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where evaluations have different cost-accuracy trade-offs, motivated by applications like online advertising.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that uses low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate arms and high-fidelity checks for promising ones.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: a regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound.  
- Regret definition is adapted to incorporate fidelity-dependent evaluation costs.  
- Simulation results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in the presented scenarios.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and connects to classical bandit literature.  
- Core idea is intuitive and practically impactful if fidelity biases are known.  
- Major practical limitation: assumes known fidelity deviations ζ(m), which is unrealistic in many real-world settings.  
- Suggestion: explore relaxing the known-ζ assumption or methods to learn ζ(m) online.  
- Lower bound discussion is terse; tightness and discrepancies (Section 5.2) need more thorough explanation.  
- Explanation of how the lower bound is achieved lacks clarity and could be expanded.  
- Simulations are helpful but limited in scope; more diverse experiments would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, strong theoretical results, rigorous algorithm design, and supportive simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic strong assumption on ζ(m) and insufficient lower-bound discussion.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with clear theoretical guarantees; practical limitation noted but not fatal — recommendation to accept.  
- Numerous specific typos/notation errors flagged (lines 326, 343, 345, 346–347, 357, 384, 386, 407, 431, 440, 450) that should be corrected for clarity.
