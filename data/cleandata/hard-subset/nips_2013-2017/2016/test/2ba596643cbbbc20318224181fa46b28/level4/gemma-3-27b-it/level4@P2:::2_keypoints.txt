- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity MAB setting where arms can be evaluated at varying fidelities trading accuracy for cost.  
- Defines regret as a cost-weighted pseudo-regret appropriate for the fidelity-cost trade-off.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on cumulative regret.  
- Main paper is generally well-written and clearly presents core ideas.  
- Supplementary material (proofs) contains numerous minor errors that hurt readability and reproducibility.  
- Frequent use of “up-to-a-constant” notation obscures precise dependencies; parameters like ρ should be stated explicitly.  
- Practical applicability is unclear because MF-UCB relies on accurate estimation of the fidelity accuracy parameter ζ.  
- Paper should discuss practical limitations and mitigation strategies for misestimation of ζ.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong, offering valuable insights and robust theorems.  
- Overall assessment: a significant and interesting contribution to the field.  
- Appendix issues (non-exhaustive): many typographical/math errors (wrong symbols, missing parentheses, incorrect inequalities, swapped variables, dropped terms, and spelling mistakes).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of setting, rigorous theoretical analysis, clear main text, and overall significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor quality of supplementary material, insufficient practical discussion (ζ estimation), and unclear constant factors.
