- Paper studies multi-fidelity stochastic bandits, proposes MF-UCB, proves an upper regret bound and a lower bound, and shows regimes where MF-UCB can beat high-fidelity-only UCB.

- Strength: Proposes a meaningful, practically motivated fidelity–cost extension of stochastic bandits.

- Strength: Provides a concrete algorithm (MF-UCB) with provable guarantees and nontrivial analysis.

- Strength: Supplies a lower bound and comparisons suggesting advantages over highest-fidelity baselines in some regimes.

- Strength: Has potential to serve as a benchmark for multi-fidelity decision making.

- Concern: Regret objective uses multiplicative penalization of resources; need stronger justification and comparisons to the more standard additive reward-minus-cost objective.

- Request: Explain how analysis/results would change under an additive regret objective or provide intuition/propositions for that variant.

- Request: Clarify the baseline used in comparisons (e.g., UCB at highest fidelity under the same regret objective) and state it near Theorem 2.

- Concern: Assumption 1 on the ζm sequence is opaque and possibly restrictive; need intuition and examples of satisfying ζm sequences.

- Request: Describe algorithm sensitivity to violations or misspecification of Assumption 1 and which proof parts depend critically on it.

- Suggestion: Provide a principled or heuristic method to prune fidelities (subsequence selection) if ζm do not decay fast enough, with supporting lemmas if possible.

- Request: Clarify whether MF-UCB requires exact knowledge of ζm and costs or can work with upper bounds and discuss robustness to errors.

- Concern: Theorem 2’s presentation is hard to parse; provide a plain-language corollary showing how regret scales with Δk, ζm, and costs.

- Request: Intuitively define K(m) and [[k]] (which arms fall into K(m), how [[k]] picks fidelity balancing bias, variance, and cost).

- Request: Explicitly state the baseline (UCB at highest fidelity) and summarize regimes where MF-UCB improves versus not, ideally with a compact table/figure.

- Concern: There is a gap between the lower bound and MF-UCB’s upper bound; quantify the gap and identify whether it arises from analysis looseness or algorithm suboptimality.

- Request: Indicate whether tighter analysis could close the gap or a different algorithmic strategy is needed in certain ζm/cost regimes.

- Scope question: Discuss extensions to unknown/partially known ζm and costs, nonuniform arm variances, and non-Gaussian rewards; state which results require sub-Gaussianity.

- Practicality: Comment on per-round computational complexity and any tuning parameters beyond standard UCB constants.

- Presentation: Exposition is very technical early on; add a running example with 2–3 fidelities illustrating MF-UCB decisions to build intuition.

- Presentation: Include an “intuition box” before Theorem 2 explaining roles of Δk, ζm, and costs and how fidelities are chosen adaptively.

- Suggestion: Give examples of ζm sequences that satisfy/violate Assumption 1 and explain consequences; state explicitly whether ζm are absolute biases or upper bounds and whether they vary by arm.

- Experiments: Recommend small-scale empirical comparisons vs high-fidelity UCB, low-fidelity-only, and an additive-cost-optimized policy, including cases violating Assumption 1 and with/without pruning.

- Minor edits: Fix typos and formatting (e.g., Line 56 “near-optimal,” remove period before “for all m < M” at Line 126, use curly braces for [[k]] at Line 179).

- Overall: Solid and timely contributions, but clearer justification of the regret objective, transparent treatment of Assumption 1 (and pruning), and a reader-friendly summary of Theorem 2 are needed to strengthen impact.
