- Addresses multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where cheap approximations of arm rewards are available.  
- Formalizes the multi-fidelity bandit problem cleanly and with clear motivation.  
- Proposes an approach with theoretical regret bounds and establishes a matching lower bound.  
- Regret analysis appears sound and bounds are reasonably tight for the setting.  
- Simulations clearly demonstrate performance gains from leveraging multi-fidelity information.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Key idea—using cheaper, lower-fidelity evaluations to guide exploration and reduce high-fidelity costs—is valuable.  
- Recommendation to compare against other bandit algorithms adapted to multi-fidelity (e.g., UCB, Thompson Sampling) for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Practical guidance on choosing fidelity levels (cost vs. accuracy trade-offs) is lacking and should be discussed.  
- Need more analysis of how performance degrades as noise in low-fidelity observations increases.  
- Overall a solid, practically relevant contribution with rigorous theory and convincing empirical support; recommend acceptance.
