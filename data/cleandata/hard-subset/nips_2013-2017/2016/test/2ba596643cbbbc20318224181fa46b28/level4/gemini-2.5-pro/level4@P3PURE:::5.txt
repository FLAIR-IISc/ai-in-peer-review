This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where cheap approximations for each arm are available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity bandit problem. The paper provides theoretical results on the regret bound, including a thorough analysis of the method's lower bound, which demonstrates a theoretical improvement over existing approaches. The simulations effectively demonstrate the benefits of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit method. Overall, the paper is well-written and the presentation is clear and easy to understand.
