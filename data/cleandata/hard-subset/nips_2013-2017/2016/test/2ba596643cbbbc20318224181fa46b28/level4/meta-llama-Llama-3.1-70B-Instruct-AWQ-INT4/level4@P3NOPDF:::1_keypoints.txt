- Proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model with fidelity-dependent costs, defines net reward, introduces MF-UCB algorithm and provides a regret lower bound.  
- The problem is interesting and relevant and can serve as a useful benchmark for future work.  
- Regret definition is unintuitive: penalizes resource consumption multiplicatively rather than additively, which may not be generally appropriate.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque—constraints on fidelity parameters lack justification and general applicability.  
- Unclear how to handle cases where the zeta_m decay condition fails or how to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy Assumption 1.  
- Authors claim Assumption 1 is noncritical, but it appears used in proofs; unclear whether it critically affects algorithm design or performance.  
- Algorithm design seems tailored to specific zeta configurations without sufficient justification for those configurations.  
- Lower bound has a gap that requires further investigation and might necessitate redesigning the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and the MF-UCB regret bound are hard to interpret and compare to high-fidelity UCB due to technical presentation and complex dependence on Δ, [[k]], and sets K^(m).  
- Paper loses clarity in presenting results; needs more intuitive explanations of assumptions and outcomes.  
- Minor typos: Line 56 "near-optimal"; Line 126 unnecessary period before "for all m < M"; Line 179 use curly braces for [[k]].
