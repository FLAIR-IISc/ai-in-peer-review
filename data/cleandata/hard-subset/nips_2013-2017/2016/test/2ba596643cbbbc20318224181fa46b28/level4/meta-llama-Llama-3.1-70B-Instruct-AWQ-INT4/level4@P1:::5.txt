This paper aims at a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem where cheap approximations to each arm are available. The authors formalise this problem as a multi-fidelity bandit problem, which is a novel and interesting take on the traditional bandit setting. The theoretical results on the regret bound are provided, and simulations are performed to demonstrate the effect of multi-fidelity.

The presentation is easy to understand, and the authors do a good job of explaining the intuition behind their algorithm, MF-UCB. The simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF bandit method, and the results are encouraging. There is sufficient theoretical analysis on the lower bound of the proposed method, which shows the theoretical improvement.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theory and practice. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their algorithm and also demonstrate its effectiveness in simulations. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of the MF-UCB algorithm, which adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategies to the multi-fidelity setting. The algorithm is shown to be near-optimal under certain conditions, and the theoretical results are supported by simulations.

The paper also provides a good discussion on the related work and highlights the differences between the proposed method and existing approaches. The authors also discuss the potential applications of their method, including online advertising and clinical trials.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of bandit problems. The paper is suitable for publication in a top-tier conference like NIPS.

Strengths:

* Novel and interesting take on the traditional bandit setting
* Solid theoretical foundation for the algorithm
* Effective simulations demonstrating the effectiveness of the MF bandit method
* Good balance between theory and practice
* Clear and concise writing

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the cheap approximations to each arm are available, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The algorithm may not perform well if the approximations are not accurate enough.
* The paper could benefit from more discussions on the potential limitations of the method and future directions for research.
