1) Paper introduces fidelity-extended bandits, net reward objective, and MF-UCB; problem is relevant and technically sound.  
2) Regret is multiplicatively scaled by cost, which seems arbitrary; authors should justify this choice and analyze additive-cost alternatives.  
3) Assumption 1 (ζₘ ≤ C/m^α) is unmotivated and crucial to regret bounds; authors must justify when it holds or provide a method to select conforming fidelities.  
4) MF-UCB appears tailored to sequences satisfying Assumption 1 with no argument that such sequences are natural; need robustness analysis for slow-decaying ζₘ (e.g., 1/√m).  
5) There is a gap between the lower bound (Thm.1) and upper bound (Thm.2); the paper should tighten the lower bound or explain whether the gap is inherent.  
6) Presentation is overly technical and obscures insights; provide concrete examples (specific ζₘ) showing when MF-UCB improves over high-fidelity UCB and clarify roles of Δ and [[k]].  
7) Minor fixes: correct typo at Line 56, remove extra period at Line 126, and use curly braces instead of [[k]] at Line 179.  
8) Overall: important problem and solid approach, but issues in regret formulation, Assumption 1, robustness, and clarity make the contribution unconvincing for publication.
