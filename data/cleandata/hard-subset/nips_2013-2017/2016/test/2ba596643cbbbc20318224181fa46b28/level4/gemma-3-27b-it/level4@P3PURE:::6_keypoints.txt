- Problem: introduces a multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelities where higher fidelities yield more accurate but costlier reward estimates.  
- Core idea: use cheap, low-fidelity evaluations to quickly eliminate bad arms and reserve expensive high-fidelity evaluations for promising arms, outperforming fidelity-agnostic algorithms like UCB.  
- Application: motivated by online advertising (short, inexpensive initial displays to estimate long-run CTRs).  
- Contribution: proposes a new regret definition tailored to multi-fidelity MABs.  
- Contribution: presents an algorithm for this setting with theoretical analysis and a regret lower bound.  
- Empirics: simulation results support the theoretical claims.  
- Novelty: analysis appears novel and advances theoretical understanding of variable-fidelity MABs.  
- Major concern: assumes known deviations ε^{(m)} between fidelity means and the highest-fidelity mean a priori; unclear how these would be obtained in practice and should be relaxed or investigated.
