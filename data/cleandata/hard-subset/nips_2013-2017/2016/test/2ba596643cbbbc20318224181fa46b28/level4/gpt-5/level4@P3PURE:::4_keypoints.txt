- Paper proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework with fidelity-dependent costs and an algorithm with regret analysis, lower bound, and simulations outperforming UCB on synthetic tasks.  
- Notation and presentation are confusing and dispersed, making definitions hard to find and hindering readability and verification.  
- The advertising motivating example is misleading due to references to non-stationarity, which is outside the paper’s scope.  
- The chosen regret benchmark is questionable for a cost-sensitive setting because it ignores sampling costs and forces sampling the best arm at low fidelities, obscuring true cost–decision trade-offs.  
- The paper lacks a cost/sample-complexity analysis, which would better capture practical multi-fidelity goals like identifying the best option under testing cost constraints.  
- Writing is unwieldy and needs careful editing to streamline exposition, consolidate notation, and improve flow.  
- Overall: interesting and promising results but not ready for publication; recommend tightening notation, replacing the motivating example, reconsidering the regret benchmark or adding cost-complexity analysis, and revising the writing.
