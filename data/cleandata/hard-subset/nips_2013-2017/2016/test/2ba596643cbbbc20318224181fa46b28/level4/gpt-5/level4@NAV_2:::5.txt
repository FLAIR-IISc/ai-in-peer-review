This paper investigates a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem in which inexpensive approximations for each arm are available. The authors formalize this as a multi-fidelity bandit setting. Theoretical results on regret bounds are provided. Simulations are conducted to illustrate the impact of leveraging multiple fidelities. The presentation is clear and easy to follow. The experiments highlight the behavior and benefits of the multi-fidelity bandit (MF-UCB) approach. There is sufficient theoretical analysis of lower bounds related to the proposed method, supporting its theoretical improvements.
