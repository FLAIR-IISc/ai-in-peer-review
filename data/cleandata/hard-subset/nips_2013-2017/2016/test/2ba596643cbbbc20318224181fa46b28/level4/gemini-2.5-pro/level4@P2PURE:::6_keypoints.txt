- Introduces a multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelities where higher fidelities are more accurate but costlier.
- Central idea: use low-cost, low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.
- Motivated by applications like online advertising where short displays approximate long-term metrics more cheaply.
- Proposes a new, setting-specific regret definition and a novel algorithm.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including regret upper and lower bounds, supported by simulations.
- Analysis is novel and represents a significant contribution.
- Major concern: assumes known biases ε^{(m)} (deviation of m-th fidelity mean from highest fidelity), which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Suggestion: discuss whether the ε^{(m)} assumption can be relaxed or how these bias parameters could be estimated in real applications.
