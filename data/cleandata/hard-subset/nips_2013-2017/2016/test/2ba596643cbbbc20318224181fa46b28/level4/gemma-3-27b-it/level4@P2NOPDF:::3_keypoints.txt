1. Proposes a multi-fidelity MAB formulation where each arm has M fidelity levels; lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded bias in observed rewards.  
2. Adapts a UCB algorithm by widening confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
3. Provides mathematically sound regret bounds that are complex and difficult to interpret.  
4. Claims the multi-fidelity approach often outperforms always-using highest fidelity; supports this claim with simulations.  
5. Reviewer initially questions modeling lower fidelity as bias (vs. increased variance); motivating examples partially justify the bias model.  
6. Skepticism about the advertising example’s plausibility and about regret minimization aligning with algorithm-selection objectives.  
7. Overall connection between model and motivating applications is tenuous but not fundamentally flawed.  
8. Primary contribution is the multi-fidelity MAB formulation; the algorithm is a standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty application.  
9. Theoretical guarantees’ complexity hinders clear understanding of the algorithm’s practical strengths.  
10. Simulation results show substantial improvements but are hard to evaluate due to unexplained parameter choices in Appendix C.  
11. Large empirical gains (3–4×) may stem from specific parameter settings (e.g., ζ^(m)); robustness of improvements is unclear and needs further validation.
