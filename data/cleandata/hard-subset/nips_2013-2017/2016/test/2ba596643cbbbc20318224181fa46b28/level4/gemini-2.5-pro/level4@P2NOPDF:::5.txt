This paper addresses a variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, extending it to a setting where low-cost, low-fidelity approximations of each arm's reward are available. The authors formalize this novel setting as a multi-fidelity (MF) bandit problem.

The paper makes several well-supported contributions. On the theoretical side, the authors derive regret bounds for their proposed algorithm and provide a thorough analysis of the corresponding lower bound. This analysis is a key strength of the work, as it rigorously establishes the theoretical performance gains achievable by leveraging multiple fidelities compared to traditional single-fidelity bandit methods.

The presentation is clear and easy to follow, making the problem formulation and the authors' approach accessible. The theoretical claims are complemented by a solid empirical evaluation. The simulations effectively demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed multi-fidelity bandit algorithm, validating its ability to reduce regret by intelligently allocating pulls between high-cost, high-fidelity arms and their low-cost approximations.
