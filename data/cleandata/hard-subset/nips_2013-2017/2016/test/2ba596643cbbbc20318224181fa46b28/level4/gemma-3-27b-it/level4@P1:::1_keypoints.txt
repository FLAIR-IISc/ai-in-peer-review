- Paper studies multi-fidelity stochastic multi-armed bandits where pulls at lower fidelities are cheaper but biased by known ζ_m, proposing MF-UCB and proving regret bounds and a lower bound.  
- The problem is interesting and relevant for modeling cost–information tradeoffs in online learning and could be a useful benchmark.  
- The technical analysis appears sound and simulations show the algorithm can outperform high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- The chosen regret/objective definition (multiplicative treatment of fidelity cost) seems unnatural; additive cost penalization might be more intuitive and should be discussed.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on ζ_m) is opaque and appears artificial; its generality and necessity are unclear.  
- It is unclear whether one can select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy Assumption 1 when ζ_m do not decay as required—providing a general procedure would strengthen the paper.  
- The algorithm seems tailored to specific ζ configurations; the authors should clarify how assumption-driven the algorithm design and performance are.  
- The gap between the provided lower bound and the algorithm’s performance needs more investigation and possibly algorithm redesign.  
- Theorem 2 and the regret comparison to single-fidelity UCB are hard to interpret; the dependence on Δ and |K_g| (via complicated K^(m) sets) should be explained more explicitly.  
- The presentation is highly technical and loses clarity quickly; more intuitive explanations and simplified statements would help.  
- Practical connection between theoretical bounds and empirical gains is not sufficiently clarified.  
- Minor typos and notation issues (e.g., “near-optimal”, punctuation at l.126, set notation for K_g) should be fixed.  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision to improve clarity, justify modeling choices, explore Assumption 1 and alternative cost formulations, and tighten the lower-bound analysis.
