- Paper studies a novel multi-fidelity MAB where each fidelity gives a cheaper, noisier reward estimate and higher fidelities are costlier but more accurate.
- Application motivates setting (e.g., online advertising: short displays approximate long-term CTRs cheaply).
- Authors introduce a new regret definition, propose an algorithm, provide theoretical analysis including a regret lower bound, and run simulations.
- Analysis yields novel insights into using cheap fidelities to eliminate arms and reserving expensive fidelities for promising arms.
- Main concern: the analysis assumes prior knowledge of each ε^(m) (deviation of fidelity m mean from highest fidelity mean); how to estimate these in practice or relax this assumption is unclear and critical.
