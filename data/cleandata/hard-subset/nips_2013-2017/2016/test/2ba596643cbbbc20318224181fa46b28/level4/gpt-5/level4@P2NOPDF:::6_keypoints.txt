- Paper studies multi-armed bandits with M fidelity levels where higher fidelities are more accurate but costlier, enabling low-fidelity screening and high-fidelity confirmation.  
- Natural application: online advertising using short-run engagement (clicks) as a low-cost proxy for long-run performance.  
- Introduces a fidelity-aware regret notion that accounts for fidelity-specific costs and bias.  
- Proposes an algorithm with theoretical guarantees and provides a regret lower bound.  
- Presents simulations; analysis appears novel and technically sound.  
- Major practical concern: theory assumes known biases epsilon^(m) (difference between m-th fidelity mean and highest-fidelity mean); unclear how these are specified in practice (e.g., advertising).  
- Requests whether epsilon^(m) assumption can be relaxed, e.g., by estimating/upper-bounding epsilon^(m) online, using data-driven calibration phases, or constructing adaptive confidence sets while retaining regret guarantees.
