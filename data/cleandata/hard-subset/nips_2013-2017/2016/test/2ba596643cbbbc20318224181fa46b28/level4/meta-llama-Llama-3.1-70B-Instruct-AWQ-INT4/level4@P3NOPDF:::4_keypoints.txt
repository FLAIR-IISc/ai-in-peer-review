- Proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework with fidelity-dependent costs, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, a lower bound, and simulations showing better performance than UCB.  
- Paper is interesting and potentially impactful but not ready for publication without revisions.  
- Notation is confusing and scattered (e.g., variable s is defined late and scarcely used).  
- The advertising example is misleading—linking display time to non-stationarity is out of scope; a different motivating example is needed.  
- The regret formulation is questionable: standard regret considers reward-to-cost ratio, but here the optimal policy always picks the highest mean arm, potentially hiding costs of unnecessary low-fidelity samples.  
- A sample-complexity or cost-complexity analysis is recommended (e.g., motivated by prototype testing constraints and practical use of low-fidelity approximations).  
- Several sections are unwieldy and should be streamlined to improve clarity and readability.
