- Problem: studies multi-fidelity K-armed bandits where cheap approximations to arms are available.  
- Main idea: use low-cost approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity plays for promising arms.  
- Algorithm: MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound procedure maintaining bounds per arm per fidelity and selecting highest bound.  
- Regret: defines a cost-aware regret that accounts for the fidelity at which arms are played.  
- Theoretical results: provides upper regret bounds and a matching lower bound showing near-optimality under assumptions.  
- Analysis techniques: partitions arms by fidelity means and uses concentration inequalities to bound mistakes.  
- Experiments: simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB and largely confines very suboptimal plays to low fidelities.  
- Strength: formalizes a new bandit variant and develops an adaptive algorithm that leverages approximations and costs.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis including lower bound demonstrating near-optimality.  
- Strength: empirical results consistently support the benefit of multi-fidelity evaluation.  
- Weakness: relies on distributional concentration inequalities that may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Weakness: paper omits a clear discussion of MF-UCB’s computational complexity.  
- Originality: introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation and a tailored MF-UCB algorithm.  
- Significance: demonstrates practical potential of cheaper approximations to reduce expensive evaluations and improve performance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear, consistent notation.  
- Quality: combines solid theoretical guarantees with supporting simulations.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel problem + algorithm, strong theory, and supportive experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: restrictive concentration assumptions and missing complexity analysis.
