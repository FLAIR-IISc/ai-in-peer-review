- Paper proposes a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis and lower bound, with synthetic experiments outperforming UCB.  
- Reviewer finds the submission currently unsuitable for publication despite the idea’s potential.  
- Major presentation/notation problems: symbols are introduced inconsistently (e.g., s), definitions scattered — recommend a dedicated notation section.  
- The advertising illustrative example is misleading: it emphasizes non-stationarity which is irrelevant and distracts from the fidelity–cost tradeoff.  
- The regret analysis is questionable: the formulation appears to always favor the arm with highest mean regardless of cost, so low-fidelity sampling may artificially deflate regret and penalize exploration to higher fidelity.  
- Recommend adding a cost- or sample-complexity analysis framed by practical scenarios (e.g., iterative prototype testing) to better motivate real-world constraints.  
- Writing is cumbersome in places; needs revision for conciseness and clearer logical flow.  
- Overall score 4/10: interesting idea but requires significant work on notation, theoretical motivation, and application framing.
