- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit formulation where lower fidelities reduce cost but introduce bounded bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that adjusts confidence intervals to account for fidelity-dependent bias.  
- Main contribution is the multi-fidelity framework, which is a meaningful extension to the literature.  
- The core assumption that lower fidelity increases bias rather than variance is questionable and needs stronger justification.  
- Examples (algorithm selection, ad display) do not convincingly show why lower fidelity induces bias or why regret minimization is the right objective.  
- Regret analysis (Theorems 1–2) is mathematically complex and lacks intuitive interpretation or simple asymptotic forms.  
- It is unclear how regret scales with M, K, or the bias-variance tradeoff; readers would benefit from simpler bounds.  
- Simulation claims of 3–4× improvement over UCB lack explanation and may be sensitive to chosen fidelity parameters.  
- Simulation parameter choices (e.g., ζ^(m)=1/m^1.5, fidelity costs) are unexplained and may be tuned to favor MF-UCB.  
- The paper does not test robustness across broader parameter ranges or more realistic cost structures.  
- Claiming novelty for "optimism in the face of uncertainty" is misleading; the confidence-interval adjustment is standard.  
- The terms "multi-fidelity" and "multi-resolution" are sometimes conflated, risking reader confusion.  
- No comparisons to alternative multi-fidelity models, especially variance-based approaches, are provided.  
- Overall, the paper’s main value is formalization, but theory is hard to interpret, empirical validation is weak, and the algorithm is a straightforward UCB adaptation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on substantial revisions to clarify theoretical results, justify the bias model, and strengthen empirical validation.
