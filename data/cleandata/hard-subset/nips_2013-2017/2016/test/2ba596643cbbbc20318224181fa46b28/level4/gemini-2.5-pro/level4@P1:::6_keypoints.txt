- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where arms can be played at M fidelities with increasing accuracy and cost, motivated by applications like online advertising.
- Proposes a cost-weighted notion of regret tailored to multi-fidelity evaluation costs.
- Develops the MF-UCB algorithm and provides a theoretical upper bound on its regret.
- Derives a problem-specific lower bound showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Supports theoretical claims with simulations comparing MF-UCB to standard UCB.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated extension with clear practical relevance to cost-constrained experimentation.
- Strength: strong theoretical depth—rigorous upper and lower regret bounds and insightful analysis.
- Strength: generally clear and well-organized exposition with good motivation and algorithm intuition.
- Main weakness: analysis assumes known bounds ζ^(m) (max deviation between fidelity means), which is unrealistic in many applications and needs sensitivity analysis, estimation methods, or robustness to misspecification.
