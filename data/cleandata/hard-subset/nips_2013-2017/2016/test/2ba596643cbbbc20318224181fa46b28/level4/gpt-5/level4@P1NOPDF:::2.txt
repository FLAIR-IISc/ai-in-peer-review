This paper introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting in which each arm can be pulled at multiple fidelities (accuracies). The cost of a pull increases with the desired accuracy, and regret is defined as cost-weighted pseudo-regret (the product of the pull cost and the usual pseudo-regret). The authors propose MF-UCB to address this setting and provide both upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret. The main paper is clear and well written.

However, the supplementary materials—where most of the proofs are located—are not at the same level. They contain many typos and small, easily correctable errors that can confuse readers (see the non-exhaustive list below). Correcting these would substantially improve readability.

I also find the use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation potentially misleading, because the “constants” are not truly constant: for example, in Theorem 2 a factor of \rho (an algorithm parameter) is hidden in the constant. Presenting the exact inequalities (as already given in the supplementary materials) would improve the clarity and interpretability of the results.

From a practical standpoint, it is not clear that MF-UCB can be readily applied to real-world problems. The strategy—using low-accuracy pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms—may be sensitive to misestimation of \zeta (the fidelity accuracy). A discussion of these practical limitations, and possible ways to mitigate them, would strengthen the paper.

From a theoretical perspective, the paper offers valuable insight into the multi-fidelity bandit problem, with interesting theorems and solid proofs. Overall, I consider this a significant and interesting contribution.
