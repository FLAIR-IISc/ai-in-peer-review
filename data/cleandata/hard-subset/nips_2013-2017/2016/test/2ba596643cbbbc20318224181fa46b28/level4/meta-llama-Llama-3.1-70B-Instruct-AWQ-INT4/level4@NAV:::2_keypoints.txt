- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework with arms sampled at varying accuracy levels and cost increasing with accuracy.
- Defines regret as cost times standard pseudo-regret and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm to address this setting.
- Provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret and presents interesting theorems with solid proofs.
- Main manuscript is well-structured and clearly written.
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and minor errors that impede readability and should be corrected.
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is potentially misleading because hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities would improve clarity.
- Practical applicability is unclear; MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate bad arms may be vulnerable to misestimation of fidelity ζ and needs discussion of limitations/solutions.
- Overall, the work is a significant and intriguing contribution.
