- Models multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity levels where lower fidelities are cheaper and yield biased rewards within known zeta_m errors.
- Proposes a new MF-UCB algorithm to maximize net reward and provides a regret upper bound and a lower bound for the problem.
- Studies an interesting and relevant tradeoff between information, cost, and reward, offering a potentially useful benchmark.
- Paper is technically solid but lacks adequate explanation and intuition for results and assumptions.
- Regret definition (multiplicative penalty) seems unnatural; reviewer suggests considering additive cost penalization and asks for analysis under that model.
- Assumption 1 (constraints on fidelity errors zeta_m) appears artificial, opaque, and unjustified for general problems.
- Unclear how to proceed if the zeta_m sequence does not meet Assumption 1; need a method to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption.
- Assumption 1 seems used in proofs and may critically influence algorithm design and performance; this dependence must be clarified.
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific zeta configurations without justification that these are the “right” sequences.
- Gap in the lower bound requires further investigation and possibly redesign of the strategy.
- Main result (Theorem 2) and comparison to standard high-fidelity UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and dependence on Delta and the complex sets K^(m); needs clearer, explicit explanation.
- Minor corrections: fix "near-optimal" (line 56), remove unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (line 126), and use curly braces for [[k]] definition (line 179).
