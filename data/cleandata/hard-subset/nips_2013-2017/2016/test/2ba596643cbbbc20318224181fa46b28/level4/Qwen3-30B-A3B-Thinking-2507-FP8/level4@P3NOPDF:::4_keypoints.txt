- Paper proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit with costed fidelities, an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments, but requires major revision.

- Notation clarity and presentation: symbols are scattered/poorly defined (e.g., s), breaking readability; a centralized notation table and consistent usage are needed.

- Misleading motivating example: the advertising example invokes non-stationarity that is not in the model/analysis and should be replaced with a fidelity-cost appropriate scenario (e.g., simulation before physical testing).

- Flawed regret analysis justification: regret is treated w.r.t. highest mean arm while the true optimum should account for reward-to-cost ratio; the regret objective and optimal policy must be made cost-aware.

- Missing cost complexity analysis: no bounds on expected total cost to identify the best arm with high probability; the paper should provide cost-complexity guarantees and argue why they matter over standard regret.

- Writing and structure: prose is verbose, disorganized, and contains grammatical errors; transitions (e.g., problem setup → algorithm) and the relation of the lower bound to regret are unclear and need substantial editing.

- Recommendation / required fixes: redefine the regret objective to be cost-aware and re-derive bounds, replace the advertising example, add cost-complexity analysis motivated by practical constraints, and comprehensively revise notation and writing.

- Overall: promising idea but currently theoretically flawed and poorly presented; not suitable for publication without major revision.
