- Presents a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where arm pulls have varying accuracies and costs, and regret is cost × pseudo-regret.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides upper and lower bounds on its cumulative pseudo-regret.
- Main paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Supplementary materials contain most proofs but have numerous typos and minor errors that hinder readability.
- Use of up-to-a-multiplicative-constant notation is misleading because hidden constants can depend on parameters (e.g., ρ in Theorem 2).
- Recommends using exact inequalities from the supplement to improve clarity.
- Practical applicability is unclear; MF-UCB’s reliance on low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may suffer from inaccurate ζ estimates.
- Suggests adding discussion on practical limitations and strategies to mitigate ζ-estimation issues.
- Theoretically valuable work with interesting theorems and solid proofs.
- Overall, a significant and interesting contribution.
