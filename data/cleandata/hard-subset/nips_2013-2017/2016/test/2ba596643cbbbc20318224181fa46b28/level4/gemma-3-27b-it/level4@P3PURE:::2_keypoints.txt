- Introduces a multi-armed bandit setting with varying sampling accuracies (fidelities); pull cost increases with accuracy and regret is defined as cost × pseudo-regret.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.
- Main paper is clear and well-written, but the supplementary proofs lack clarity and contain many typos/minor errors that impede readability.
- Reviewer provides a non-exhaustive list of numerous specific typos and mistakes in the appendix that need correction.
- The “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is misleading because hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); exact inequalities should be shown.
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s use of low-accuracy pulls to eliminate arms may be vulnerable to errors in estimating fidelity ζ.
- Recommends discussing practical limitations and potential mitigation strategies for fidelity-estimation inaccuracies.
- Theoretical contribution is valuable with interesting theorems and generally solid proofs; overall a significant and interesting contribution.
