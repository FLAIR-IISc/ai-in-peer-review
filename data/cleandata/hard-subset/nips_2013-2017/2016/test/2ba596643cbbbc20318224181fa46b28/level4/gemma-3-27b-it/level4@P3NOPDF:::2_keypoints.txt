- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting where arms offer multiple fidelities at increasing cost and defines regret as cost times pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm and provides both upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret.  
- The paper is generally clear and provides a valuable theoretical contribution with well-motivated theorems and mostly solid proofs.  
- The supplementary material (where most proofs live) contains numerous typos and minor errors that reduce readability and require substantial correction.  
- The frequent use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” bounds is misleading because the hidden constants depend on algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); the reviewer recommends showing the precise inequalities.  
- Practical applicability is unclear: MF-UCB’s reliance on accurate estimation of the fidelity parameter ζ could severely impair performance if ζ is misestimated.  
- The reviewer urges adding discussion of practical limitations and mitigation strategies (robust ζ estimation, adaptive accuracy selection, etc.).  
- Overall assessment: significant and interesting theoretical contribution, but needs error corrections and clearer treatment of constants and practical concerns.  
- Appendix contains a detailed list of specific typos/errors to fix (many equation/variable corrections, missing parentheses, swapped indices, incorrect inequalities, and section typos).
