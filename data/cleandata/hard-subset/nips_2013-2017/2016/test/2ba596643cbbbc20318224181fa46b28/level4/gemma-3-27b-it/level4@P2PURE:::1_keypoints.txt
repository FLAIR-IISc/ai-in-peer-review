- Studies multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelities per arm where lower fidelities give noisy, cheaper rewards and goal is to maximize net reward; proposes an algorithm and regret lower bound.  
- Problem is interesting and relevant, modeling tradeoffs between information, cost, and reward and providing a useful benchmark.  
- Paper is technically sound but gives insufficient explanation of results and underlying assumptions.  
- The chosen regret definition (additive penalty for cost) feels unnatural; multiplicative or alternative formulations might be more intuitive and should be explored.  
- The ad-display motivation for the regret choice may not generalize; authors should analyze implications of additive incorporation of fidelity costs.  
- Assumption 1 (constraints on fidelity parameters ζ_m known to the learner) appears artificial and lacks clear justification.  
- It is unclear how to proceed if ζ_m do not decay as required—need a principled method to select a subset of fidelities that satisfy the assumption.  
- Authors claim the assumption is noncritical, but it seems integral to proofs and possibly to algorithm design and performance.  
- The algorithm appears tailored to specific ζ_m configurations without rationale for why those configurations are appropriate.  
- There is a gap in the lower bound that requires further investigation and possibly refinement of the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and comparisons to UCB are hard to interpret; performance depends on Δ and ||k|| via complex sets K^(m), so a clearer, more transparent explanation is needed.  
- Minor editorial issues: line 56 “near-optimal”; line 126 an extra period before “for all m < M”; line 179 prefer curly braces for defining ||k||.
