- Introduces the novel multi-fidelity bandit setting: arms have costly high-fidelity observations plus cheaper biased lower-fidelity approximations, with a cost-weighted regret objective.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses cheap fidelities to quickly eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves expensive pulls for promising candidates.  
- Builds on the classical UCB framework to handle a new problem structure with significant algorithmic and theoretical challenges.  
- Theoretical analysis gives regret bounds showing cost for a suboptimal arm scales with the lowest fidelity that can identify it as poor.  
- Provides a lower bound demonstrating near-optimality of MF-UCB under specific conditions.  
- Paper is very clearly written, with effective figures and the K(m) partitioning to convey intuition.  
- Assumes known bias bounds ζ(m) for each fidelity, which may be impractical and raises questions about sensitivity or online estimation.  
- There is a gap between upper and lower bounds for a certain class of arms (K(m)7), suggesting the fidelity-switching rule may not be universally optimal.  
- Experiments are only on synthetic data; lack of semi-synthetic or real-world validation limits demonstrated practical impact.  
- Well-motivated with clear real-world relevance (online advertising, hyperparameter tuning, scientific experiments).  
- Claims are well-supported by rigorous analysis and results.  
- Overall a high-quality, significant contribution worthy of acceptance, subject to caveats about ζ(m) assumptions and limited empirical evaluation.
