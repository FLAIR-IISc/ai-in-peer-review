- Introduces MF-MAB: arms sampled at multiple fidelities with costs; proposes an adaptive algorithm, regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic experiments showing improvement over UCB.

- Regret formulation is questionable because optimality is defined by highest high-fidelity mean regardless of sampling cost, which misaligns with cost-sensitive multi-fidelity goals and can hide true exploration costs.

- The paper should consider alternative objectives (fixed-confidence/budget, cost-complexity, or reward-to-cost ratio) that better capture practical, cost-aware decision goals like prototype testing.

- Presentation and notation lack clarity: definitions are scattered (e.g., variable s undefined initially), making the paper hard to follow and in need of a thorough rewrite.

- The motivating example (online advertising) is misleading by implying non-stationarity while the theory assumes stationarity; a cost-centric, stationary example like prototype testing would be more appropriate.
