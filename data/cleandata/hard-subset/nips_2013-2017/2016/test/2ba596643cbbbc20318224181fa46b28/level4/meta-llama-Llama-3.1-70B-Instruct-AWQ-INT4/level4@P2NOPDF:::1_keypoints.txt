- Presents a stochastic multi-armed bandit with discrete fidelity choices where lower fidelities are cheaper but yield rewards within a known error zeta_m of the true mean.  
- Proposes MF-UCB algorithm to maximize net reward and provides a regret lower bound, claiming improvements over high-fidelity UCB.  
- Problem formulation is interesting and relevant and could serve as a benchmark for future work.  
- Paper lacks clear explanations of results and assumptions, reducing technical clarity.  
- Regret definition penalizes resource use multiplicatively, which seems unnatural; an additive cost model may be more intuitive and generalizable.  
- Assumption 1 is artificial and opaque; its practical validity and necessity are unclear, yet it is used in proofs.  
- Unclear how to handle fidelity sequences that do not satisfy Assumption 1 or how to choose subsequences to meet it.  
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific zeta configurations without sufficient justification for those configurations.  
- The lower-bound gap needs further investigation and may require redesigning the strategy.  
- Theorem 2 and regret comparison to UCB are hard to interpret due to technical presentation and dependence on complicated sets K^(m), Delta, and [[k]].  
- Paper rapidly loses clarity in presenting results; more explicit explanations of gains/losses are needed.  
- Minor corrections: fix "near-optimal" (line 56), remove period before "for all m < M" (line 126), and use curly braces for set notation when defining [[k]] (line 179).
