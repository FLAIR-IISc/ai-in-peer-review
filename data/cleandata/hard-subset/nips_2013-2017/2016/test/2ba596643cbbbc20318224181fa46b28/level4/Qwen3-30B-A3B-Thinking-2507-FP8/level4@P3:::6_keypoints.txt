- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm can be evaluated at multiple fidelities with different costs and accuracies.  
- Formalizes a cost-weighted regret measure that accounts for evaluation cost and approximation quality.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: a regret upper bound and a matching lower bound.  
- Motivated by practical applications (e.g., online advertising) where low-fidelity checks can prune bad arms cheaply.  
- Extends bandit literature beyond single-fidelity and contrasts with prior multi-fidelity optimization work lacking regret analysis.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with well-structured proofs demonstrating advantages over naive UCB.  
- MF-UCB uses lower-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms early, improving performance over standard UCB.  
- Empirical results on synthetic tasks corroborate theoretical improvements across settings.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with clear definitions, logical flow, and effective illustrative figures.  
- Main weakness: assumption that fidelity deviations ζ(m) are known a priori, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Paper lacks discussion or methods for estimating ζ(m) from data or handling unknown deviations.  
- Minor issues: multiple notational inconsistencies and some incorrect inequalities/indexing that need correction.  
- Evaluation on criteria: high quality, clear, original, and significant for practical domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, rigorous theory, practical motivation, and supporting experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability limited by the ζ(m) assumption and limited discussion on its estimation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address ζ(m) assumption discussion and fix notation errors.
