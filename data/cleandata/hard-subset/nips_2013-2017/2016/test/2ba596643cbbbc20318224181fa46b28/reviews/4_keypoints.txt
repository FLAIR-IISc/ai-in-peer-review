1. Paper proposes a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelities with different costs and lower-fidelity draws approximate the highest-fidelity distribution.

2. Authors provide an adaptive algorithm for available approximation sequences, a regret analysis, and a lower bound; experiments on synthetic problems show the algorithm outperforms UCB.

3. Reviewer finds the paper interesting and potentially impactful but not ready for publication in its current form.

4. Notation is confusing and dispersed (e.g., variable s is defined after first use and then barely used), harming readability.

5. The advertising example is misleading; references to display time correlations and non-stationarity are inappropriate for the paper’s scope.

6. The regret analysis is questionable: optimal policy should maximize reward/cost, but the paper treats the best arm as highest mean regardless of cost, potentially hiding costs of low-fidelity sampling.

7. Reviewer suggests a sample-complexity (cost-complexity) analysis focused on finding the best arm with high probability under budget/constraints would be more appropriate and motivated by real-world testing scenarios.

8. Writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization.
