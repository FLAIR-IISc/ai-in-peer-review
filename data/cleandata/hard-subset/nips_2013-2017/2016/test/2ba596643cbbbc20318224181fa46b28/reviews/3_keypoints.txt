- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model: K arms, M fidelities where lower fidelity is cheaper but introduces bounded bias in estimates.  
- Proposes and analyzes a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret.  
- Paper argues the MF-UCB regret is often much better than always-using highest fidelity UCB.  
- Provides simulation results claiming empirical improvement of MF-UCB.  
- Reviewer questions modeling low fidelity as increased bias rather than increased variance.  
- Some motivating examples (e.g., algorithm selection) partially justify bias model, but others (e.g., short ad displays) are unconvincing and the regret objective is unclear for certain applications.  
- Overall link between model and motivating applications is weak but not fatal.  
- Main novelty is the problem formulation; the algorithm itself uses standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty ideas.  
- Simulation parameter choices (Appendix C) lack justification, so the reported 3–4× improvement might reflect chosen parameters (e.g., ζ^(m)) rather than typical performance.
