- Paper studies a new multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting with M fidelities where higher fidelities give better reward approximations at higher cost.  
- Practical motivation provided (e.g., online advertising: short displays to approximate long-run clicks).  
- Authors propose a new regret definition, give an algorithm with theoretical analysis (including regret lower bound), and validate via simulation; analysis is novel.  
- Major concern: the paper assumes known ε^(m) (deviation of mth-fidelity mean from highest-fidelity mean); unclear how these values are obtained in practice and whether the assumption can be relaxed.
