- Problem setup: models multi-armed bandits with discrete fidelity levels, known fidelity errors zeta_m (highest fidelity zeta=0), and fidelity-dependent costs.  
- Contribution: proposes MF-UCB algorithm to maximize net reward, provides a regret bound and a lower bound, and claims improvement over high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Strength: studies an interesting, relevant tradeoff between information, cost, and reward in stochastic bandits; technically appears solid.  
- Clarity issue: paper lacks adequate explanation of results and assumptions, making interpretation difficult.  
- Regret formulation concern: penalizing cost multiplicatively is unnatural; additive cost penalization should be considered and discussed.  
- Motivation limitation: ad-display example may not generalize; need broader motivation for the chosen reward/cost model.  
- Assumption 1 critique: constraint on zeta_m is artificial/opaque and its general validity is unclear.  
- Robustness question: if zeta_m don’t decay as required, authors should show how to select a subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption.  
- Algorithm dependence worry: algorithm design seems tailored to specific zeta configurations; unclear whether Assumption 1 critically affects performance.  
- Lower bound gap: the gap in the lower bound warrants further investigation and might require redesigning the strategy.  
- Result interpretation difficulty: Thm.2 and the comparison with UCB are hard to parse; dependence on Delta and the complicated sets K^(m) needs clearer, more explicit explanation.  
- Minor issues/typos: "near-optimal" (l.56), unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (l.126), use curly braces for [[k]] set notation (l.179).
