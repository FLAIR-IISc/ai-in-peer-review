- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework and the MF-UCB algorithm that uses cheaper, lower-fidelity evaluations to guide high-fidelity sampling.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical regret bounds showing improvement over standard UCB under specific conditions.  
- MF-UCB is intuitive and implementable, with a well-explained switching criterion based on γ(m).  
- Simulations validate the theoretical results but are relatively basic and limited in scope.  
- Assumption 1 (ζ decay) is restrictive and its practical implications and violations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- There is a gap between the provided regret bound and the lower bound, limiting claims of near-optimality.  
- Choice of qt (set to µ(M)) is reasonable but alternative choices and their implications need more discussion.  
- Notation can be overly dense in places, reducing readability.  
- Originality: novel formalization of multi-fidelity bandits with significant practical relevance.  
- Technical quality: proofs and analysis appear sound and convincing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant new problem, strong theoretical guarantees, intuitive algorithm, and empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumption, lower-bound gap, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss practical effects of violating Assumption 1, investigate/clarify the lower-bound gap, and expand empirical evaluation with more realistic datasets and parameter settings.
