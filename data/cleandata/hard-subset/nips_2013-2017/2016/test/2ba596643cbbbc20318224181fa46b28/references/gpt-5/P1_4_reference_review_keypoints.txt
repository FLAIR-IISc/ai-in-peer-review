- Problem: Introduces stochastic K-armed bandits with multiple biased, costed fidelities and a cost-weighted pseudo-regret measured against the high-fidelity mean under a capital budget Λ.  
- Assumptions: Requires known fidelity bias envelopes ζ(m) with monotone decay and a general ψ-based concentration condition (beyond sub-Gaussian).  
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB, which uses fidelity-specific UCBs extended by bias terms and selects the cheapest fidelity whose uncertainty exceeds a cost/bias-tuned threshold γ(m).  
- Intuition: Cheap fidelities eliminate clearly suboptimal arms; expensive fidelities are reserved for promising arms.  
- Upper bound: Proves expected regret decomposes by fidelity partitions K(m), showing most cost is spent at the lowest fidelity that can eliminate an arm and yields speedups over single-fidelity UCB under a regularity condition.  
- Lower bound: Provides a Bernoulli-specific lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal for many arms but identifies a class (K(m)7) where a gap remains.  
- Empirics: Synthetic simulations show MF-UCB outperforms naive single-fidelity UCB in regret.  
- Novelty: First formal regret analysis for multi-fidelity stochastic bandits with biased approximations and fidelity-dependent costs.  
- Strengths: Well-motivated problem, simple principled algorithm, interpretable theory (decomposition by fidelity), general ψ-analysis, and supportive simulations.  
- Main limitation: Reliance on known ζ(m); lacks guidance on estimating/robustness to misspecification.  
- Theoretical limits: Lower bound restricted to Bernoulli rewards and additional technical assumptions; extension to general sub-Gaussian families is missing.  
- Empirical limits: Experiments limited to synthetic data, few baselines (only naive UCB), no ablations/sensitivity analyses or realistic applications.  
- Presentation issues: Notational density, some typos/stray fragments, unclear constants/ρ roles, and certain sets/thresholds poorly motivated early.  
- Suggestions: Address learning/robustness of ζ(m), extend lower bound beyond Bernoulli, explore switching refinements to reduce K(m)7 gap, add baselines/sensitivity/realistic experiments, and improve exposition.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, practically relevant formulation with a simple algorithm and strong, interpretable theoretical contributions showing substantial gains in many instances.  
- Rejection risks: Dependence on known bias bounds, limited lower-bound scope and experiments, and polish/clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer leans toward acceptance but requests stronger empirical validation and clarification of assumptions for final version.
