- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits, defines a cost-weighted regret, and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm with theoretical guarantees.  
- Derives regret bounds showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when the set of good arms is small and cheaper fidelities exist.  
- Provides a matching lower bound demonstrating near-optimality of the approach.  
- Quality: technically rigorous analysis with careful handling of biases from lower-fidelity approximations.  
- Quality: synthetic experiments corroborate the theoretical claims.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and the algorithm/figures effectively illustrate the ideas.  
- Originality: first formal regret analysis for multi-fidelity bandits and a novel MF-UCB adaptation.  
- Significance: practically relevant to domains with cheap approximations (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection) and promising when few good arms exist.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited to synthetic problems; lacks real-world experiments.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis assumes known bias parameters ζ(m), with no practical estimation strategy discussed.  
- Weakness: lower bound analysis has a gap—MF-UCB may be non-optimal for certain arms (acknowledged by authors).  
- Weakness: scope is limited to stochastic bandits; extension to adversarial or other variants is not addressed.  
- Overall: high-quality contribution that meets NIPS criteria; recommend acceptance with high confidence (pros: motivation, rigor, clarity; cons: limited experiments, ζ(m) assumption, minor lower-bound gap).
