- Introduces stochastic K-armed multi-fidelity bandits where lower fidelities give biased, cheaper approximations to the highest-fidelity reward and defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret over a capital budget Λ.  
- Assumes known, arm-independent bias bounds |µ(M)k − µ(m)k| ≤ ζ(m) (ζ(m)→0) and common concentration function ψ for empirical means.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-fidelity UCBs for the target mean µ(M)k that add empirical means, an exploration term, and the bias ζ(m), selecting arms by the minimum UCB across fidelities.  
- Introduces a fidelity-selection rule that escalates to higher fidelities when the confidence radius at fidelity m drops below γ(m)=ψ−1((λ(m)/λ(m+1))ψ(ζ(m))), balancing cost and bias.  
- Intuition: use cheap fidelities to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserve expensive fidelities for arms that are hard to distinguish at lower fidelities.  
- Upper bound: E[R(Λ)] scales (up to logs) like ∑k∉K* Δ(M)k λ(Jk)/ψ(Δ(Jk)k), where Jk is the highest fidelity that can eliminate arm k, improving over uniform-high-fidelity UCB.  
- Lower bound (Bernoulli): any reasonable strategy must incur, up to constants, ∑k Δ(M)k min` λ(`)/Δ(`)2, showing MF-UCB is near-optimal for a large subclass of arms.  
- Identifies a gap for arms that are slightly suboptimal at some lower fidelity (the K(m)7 subset), acknowledged as nontrivial to close.  
- Distinguishes this work from bandits with knapsacks, multi-labeler/weak supervision, and GP-based multi-fidelity BO by offering finite-time, frequentist, per-arm guarantees for biased proxies.  
- Recommends explicitly citing and contrasting BwK/knapsack bandits, structured/correlated bandits with side observations, and cascade/linked bandits.  
- Soundness: algorithm design and analysis are principled; per-fidelity UCBs with ζ(m) offsets yield valid high-probability bounds on µ(M).  
- Upper-bound analysis: arm partitioning K(m) matches intuition and the ψ dependence covers general sub-exponential tails (specializable to sub-Gaussian).  
- Lower-bound analysis: change-of-measure argument is standard and well-adapted; Assumption 3 excludes pathological strategies and is reasonable.  
- Assumptions: strong but explicit—known, arm-independent ζ(m); common ψ; Assumption 1 on ζ decay is necessary for key comparisons.  
- Clarity: generally well written; recommends earlier motivation for the unconventional regret definition and a small example/table for the sets J(·) and K(·).  
- Ask to explicitly state the high-probability event/union bound for Bk,t and the role of ν in equation (2).  
- Request discussion on sensitivity to ζ(m) mis-specification and whether algorithm is robust to upper bounds or arm-varying ζ.  
- Originality: novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation and elegant MF-UCB adaptation of optimism with new partition-based analysis.  
- Significance: clean framework and near-optimal guarantees for practical settings; theoretical insights likely to influence follow-ups, though empirical validation is limited.  
- Pros: principled formulation, simple implementable algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, clear discussion of limitations.  
- Cons: unconventional regret notion may mismatch some applications; strong assumptions; unresolved lower-bound gap for certain arms; limited synthetic experiments and missing robustness/real-data validation; limited discussion of related resource-constrained models.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze sensitivity to ζ mis-specification or provide adaptive estimation, incorporate occasional high-fidelity probes, expand experiments (vary λ, ζ, K; robustness; semi-synthetic/real data), and clarify relation to BwK/side-observation bandits.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with novel formulation and solid analysis; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity, related-work gaps, and expanding empirical validation.
