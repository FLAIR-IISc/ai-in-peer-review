- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity extension of K-armed bandits and proposes MF-UCB to leverage cheap, biased low-fidelity estimates to reduce regret.  
- Motivated by practical settings (online ads, algorithm selection) where high-fidelity feedback is costly.  
- Novel and significant generalization of classical bandits with clear real-world relevance.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous: defines regret, provides upper bound for MF-UCB, and includes lower-bound discussion.  
- Careful treatment of bias from lower fidelities and convincing proof sketches.  
- Algorithm and notation are clearly presented; paper is well-written and organized.  
- Simulations show MF-UCB outperforming naive UCB, supporting theory.  
- Detailed arm-partition analysis (K(m) sets) gives insight into algorithm behavior and limits.  
- Assumes known ζ(m) bias values, which may be unrealistic and lacks sensitivity analysis to estimation errors.  
- Gap remains between the provided upper bound and the lower bound, notably for misidentified arms.  
- Simulation experiments are limited; need broader parameter ranges, reward distributions, and fidelity counts.  
- Related work discussion could better connect to Bayesian optimization and surrogate-model literature.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel algorithm with theory, and supportive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive ζ(m) assumption, upper-lower bound gap, and narrow simulation scope.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request sensitivity analysis for ζ(m), expanded simulations (more fidelities/distributions), and added discussion of Bayesian optimization links.
