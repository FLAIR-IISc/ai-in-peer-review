This paper introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem, motivated by scenarios where obtaining precise reward estimates is costly, but cheaper, approximate estimates are available. The authors propose a Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB) algorithm and provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating improved regret bounds compared to standard UCB when lower fidelities are sufficiently cheaper and informative. The work is well-motivated, clearly written, and addresses a relevant and practically important problem.

**Summary of Ideas and Relation to Prior Work:**

The core idea of leveraging cheaper, approximate evaluations in the MAB setting is novel, though it builds upon the extensive literature of MABs (Robbins, 1952; Auer, 2002; Bubeck & Cesa-Bianchi, 2006) and UCB algorithms. The paper appropriately positions itself within the broader context of multi-fidelity optimization (Huang et al., 2006; Rajnarayan et al., 2011) and related work on active learning (Zhang & Chaudhuri, 2016) and budgeted bandits (Tran-Thanh et al., 2013).  However, these related works do not explicitly formalize regret bounds in the multi-fidelity bandit setting, which is the primary contribution of this paper.  The authors also correctly differentiate their work from multi-fidelity reinforcement learning (Cutler et al., 2015), which frames the problem as a Markov Decision Process. The recent work by Kandasamy et al. (2016) is also noted and acknowledged, representing a very close predecessor. The paper builds upon this earlier work, providing more comprehensive theoretical analysis and addressing additional practical considerations like the impact of biased lower-fidelity estimates.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Relevance:** The multi-fidelity bandit framework is a valuable generalization of the standard MAB problem, with direct applications in various fields like online advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the MF-UCB algorithm, establishing regret bounds that demonstrate its advantages over naive UCB strategies. The lower bound analysis strengthens the theoretical results.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized. The notation is generally clear, and the algorithm is described in a concise and understandable manner. The intuition behind the algorithm and its analysis is effectively conveyed.
* **Comprehensive Related Work:**  The authors provide a thorough review of relevant literature, properly contextualizing their contributions and highlighting the gaps in existing research.
* **Empirical Validation:** The simulations, while not extensive, support the theoretical findings and demonstrate the practical benefits of MF-UCB.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on several assumptions, including the concentration inequalities (Equation 2) and Assumption 1 regarding the decay of ζ(m). While these are standard in bandit analysis, it would be beneficial to discuss the sensitivity of the results to violations of these assumptions.  Specifically, the impact of a slower decay in ζ(m) could be further explored.
* **Lower Bound Gap:** The authors acknowledge a gap between their upper bound for MF-UCB and the lower bound, particularly for arms that are initially misidentified due to noisy lower-fidelity estimates. Addressing this gap, even partially, would significantly strengthen the theoretical contribution.
* **Limited Simulations:** The simulation results are somewhat limited in scope. It would be valuable to include experiments with more complex reward distributions, different cost structures (λ(m)), and a larger number of arms. A comparison with other bandit algorithms beyond standard UCB (e.g., Thompson Sampling) could also be insightful.
* **Regret Definition:** While the regret definition (Equation 1) is motivated, the choice of using the highest fidelity reward (µ(M)k) for all fidelity plays might not be appropriate in all scenarios.  The discussion about alternative choices for *qt* is brief and could be expanded.
* **Algorithm Complexity:** While not a major concern, the algorithm involves nested loops in its implementation, and a discussion about its computational complexity would be helpful, especially for a large number of arms and fidelities.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The problem is novel and relevant, addressing a gap in the existing MAB literature.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides strong guarantees on the performance of the proposed algorithm.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.
* The empirical results support the theoretical findings.
* The work has the potential to impact a wide range of applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis relies on potentially restrictive assumptions.
* There is a gap between the upper and lower bounds on regret.
* The simulation results are limited in scope.
* The regret definition might not be universally appropriate.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the weaknesses, I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits. The problem is well-motivated, the proposed algorithm is theoretically sound, and the results are promising. The weaknesses are not critical and could be addressed in future work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Sensitivity Analysis:**  Discuss the robustness of the results to violations of the assumptions (concentration inequalities, Assumption 1).
* **Lower Bound Analysis:** Investigate potential approaches to close the gap between the upper and lower bounds.
* **Expanded Simulations:** Include more comprehensive simulations with different reward distributions, cost structures, and a larger number of arms.
* **Regret Definition Discussion:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the implications of the chosen regret definition and potential alternatives.
* **Computational Complexity:** Briefly analyze the computational complexity of the MF-UCB algorithm.
