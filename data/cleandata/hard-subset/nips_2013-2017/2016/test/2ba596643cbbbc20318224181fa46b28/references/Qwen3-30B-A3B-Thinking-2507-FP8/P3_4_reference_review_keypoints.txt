- Introduces a multi-fidelity extension of multi-armed bandits where arms can be sampled at multiple costs and biases, with a new cost-aware regret notion.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: a UCB-based algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity samples to eliminate bad arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity samples for promising arms.  
- Provides theoretical regret upper bound O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) and a matching lower bound for some arm categories.  
- Well-motivated problem formulation with concrete applications (online advertising, algorithm selection) and an elegant cost-aware regret metric.  
- MF-UCB is a natural, intuitive extension of UCB that leverages multi-fidelity structure effectively.  
- Technical analysis is solid: insightful partitioning of arms, sound regret bound, and useful comparison to standard UCB.  
- Synthetic experiments support theoretical claims, showing MF-UCB outperforms UCB under predicted conditions.  
- Major weakness: assumes known fidelity biases ζ(m), limiting practical applicability and no analysis of bias estimation or misestimation effects.  
- Strong regularity assumption (Assumption 1) requiring fast/possibly exponential decay of ζ(m) may not hold in practice and is only weakly justified.  
- Lower bound gap: matching lower bound holds for some arm sets (K(m)3) but not for others (K(m)7), and no solution is proposed to close this gap.  
- Algorithm depends on parameter ρ (ρ > 4) with no guidance on practical selection or sensitivity analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic data; no realistic benchmarks to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Overall: technically sound, original extension with moderate significance, clear writing and related work, but practical applicability limited by assumptions and theoretical gaps.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting handling of unknown ζ(m), justification/relaxation of Assumption 1, addressing lower-bound gap, guidance on choosing ρ, and more realistic experiments.
