- Introduces multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where lower fidelities are cheaper, biased approximations with known bias envelopes ζ(m), and formulates a cost-weighted pseudo-regret under fixed capital Λ.
- Proposes MF-UCB: UCBs on highest-fidelity means using all fidelities with bias correction Bk,t = minm[X̄(m)+conf+ζ(m)] and an adaptive fidelity choice via threshold γ(m) balancing cost and informativeness.
- Proves an upper regret bound showing expensive (high-fidelity) queries concentrate on few near-optimal arms while cheap fidelities eliminate clearly suboptimal arms.
- Provides a lower bound for Bernoulli rewards (with a mild subpolynomial-pulls assumption) that captures the λ/Δ^2 dependence and shows near-optimality on many instances, while identifying a class (K(m)7) where a gap remains.
- Simulations on synthetic problems show consistent gains of MF-UCB over a naive high-fidelity UCB baseline and match theoretical trends.
- Situates work relative to prior literature: extends UCB methodology, distinct from budgeted bandits, and novel formalization of biased multi-fidelity stochastic bandit regret.
- Technical soundness: UCB construction and fidelity-selection rule are principled; regret decomposition and comparisons to standard UCB are convincing; proofs use standard concentration/change-of-measure tools and appear consistent.
- Requires known monotone bias envelopes ζ(m) and a regularity condition (Assumption 1) relating their decay to ψ, which is reasonable for theory but potentially strong in practice.
- Lower bound is specialized to Bernoulli rewards and assumes Assumption 3 to exclude degenerate strategies; this specialization should be discussed more.
- Experiments are limited: only synthetic data, single baseline (high-fidelity UCB), no tests of sensitivity to ζ(m), λ(m), number of fidelities, or mis-specification, and no real-world datasets.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, but the pseudo-regret definition, the role of ψ, instantiation in sub-Gaussian settings, constants (ρ>4), and minor editorial issues need clarification.
- The problem formulation and MF-UCB algorithm are original and nontrivial, and the theoretical analysis (upper and lower bounds) is novel for this setting.
- Significance: setting is practically relevant (ads, subsampling, simulators) and the results provide a principled foundation likely to influence follow-on work, albeit with practical caveats.
- Pros: clear, original formulation; simple, intuitive algorithm; strong theoretical guarantees including matching lower bounds on many instances; empirical-theory alignment.
- Cons: pseudo-regret and oracle model need deeper discussion; depends on known ζ(m) and ψ with no robustness analysis; Assumption 1 may be restrictive; lower bound limited to Bernoulli and leaves a gap for some instances; empirical validation is thin.
- Key suggestions: analyze sensitivity to ζ(m) mis-specification or methods to estimate/upper-bound ζ(m); extend analysis to unknown variance/heavy tails; quantify degradation when Assumption 1 fails; expand experiments (robustness, more baselines, real/semi-real data).
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline to weak accept) — original and theoretically strong contribution with limited empirical validation and some strong assumptions.
