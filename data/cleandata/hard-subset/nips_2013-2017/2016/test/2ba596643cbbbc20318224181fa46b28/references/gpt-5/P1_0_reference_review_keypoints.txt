- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit where fidelities trade off known bias bounds ζ(m) and increasing costs λ(m).  
- Defines a capital-constrained cost-weighted pseudo-regret that credits each play with the arm’s highest-fidelity mean μ(M).  
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-arm upper bounds on μ(M) formed by combining fidelity-specific UCBs plus ζ(m), and a fidelity-selection rule using thresholds γ(m).  
- Theoretical guarantees show MF-UCB concentrates low-fidelity exploration on clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity plays for near-optimal arms.  
- Regret bounds match or improve single-fidelity UCB depending on problem structure; a lower bound shows near-optimality except on a characterized subset.  
- Novelty: first formalization of multi-fidelity stochastic MAB with biased approximations, costs, and regret guarantees.  
- Builds on classical UCB/regret work and differs from budgeted-bandit and multi-fidelity BO/RL literature by providing formal regret analysis.  
- Strength: well-motivated, realistic problem formulation capturing cheap biased approximations in practice.  
- Strength: simple, practical algorithm with principled fidelity selection and minimal tuning (ρ and known ζ, λ).  
- Strength: transparent, non-asymptotic bounds parameterized by ψ and a matching lower bound on many instances.  
- Insight: multi-fidelity helps when many arms are clearly bad at low fidelity but offers limited benefit when many arms are near-optimal.  
- Weakness: strong assumption of known, uniform bias bounds ζ(m) across arms; sensitivity to misspecification not studied.  
- Weakness: regret metric assumes low fidelities provide no intrinsic reward, limiting applicability to settings where lower fidelities also yield payoffs.  
- Weakness: reliance on Assumption 1 (ζ decay relative to ψ) for qualitative improvements; main results without it are not fully stated.  
- Weakness: lower bound proven only for Bernoulli rewards with technical conditions (μ* > 1/2, ζ(1) < 1/2); extension to sub-Gaussian families not detailed.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to synthetic data and lacking stronger baselines (e.g., screen-then-UCB, multi-fidelity Thompson sampling) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Quality: technically careful with clear assumptions and informative proof sketches; full proofs in appendix.  
- Clarity: well organized but notation is dense; some simulation parameter details and sub-Gaussian specialization could be presented earlier.  
- Originality and significance: high—setting is relevant, algorithm and analysis likely to stimulate follow-up work.  
- Suggestions: add stronger baselines, sensitivity to ζ mis-specification and schedules, a small real/semi-synthetic experiment, and a theorem variant without Assumption 1.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept to accept, contingent on stronger empirical validation and robustness/clarity improvements.
