- Introduces MF-UCB, a multi-fidelity extension of multi-armed bandits that uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to reduce costly high-fidelity plays.  
- Core idea: leverage lower fidelities to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity plays for promising arms.  
- Provides a theoretical regret bound showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB under certain conditions.  
- Presents a lower bound to argue near-optimality of the proposed approach.  
- Builds on UCB literature and distinguishes itself from existing multi-fidelity optimization and budgeted bandit work.  
- Missing a useful comparison/contextualization with Thompson Sampling for budgeted bandits (Xia et al., IJCAI 2016).  
- Novel and practically relevant formulation with applications in advertising, algorithm selection, and experimental design.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and the regret derivation appears sound.  
- MF-UCB algorithm is clearly presented and implementable; fidelity switching criterion γ(m) is well-motivated.  
- Simulations show MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB, supporting theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Assumption 1 (e.g., exponentially decaying ζ(m)) is restrictive and performance gains rely on it; applicability may be limited.  
- A gap remains between the MF-UCB upper bound and the lower bound, especially for arms with similar rewards.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; needs more diverse scenarios, cost ratios, reward distributions, and sensitivity analysis of ρ.  
- Regret definition (qt = µ(M)It) prioritizes high fidelities and alternatives are not fully explored.  
- Lacks direct comparison/discussion with active learning setups using cheap and expensive labelers (Zhang & Chaudhuri, NeurIPS 2016).  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to MAB literature with solid theory and practical relevance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, rigorous theory, well-designed algorithm, empirical validation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumption on ζ(m), upper-lower bound gap, and insufficient empirical breadth.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address Assumption 1, elaborate on the lower-bound gap, expand experiments, and discuss regret definition implications.
