- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting with multiple approximations per arm and a cost-weighted regret metric.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, which leverages cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms before expensive high-fidelity plays.  
- Derives an upper-bound on cost-weighted regret showing improvement over standard UCB when low fidelity is much cheaper and |K_g| < K.  
- Proves a matching lower bound (near-optimality) and provides comprehensive theoretical analysis under clear assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1).  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems align with theory and demonstrate MF-UCB’s superiority over standard UCB.  
- Novelty: first formal treatment of multi-fidelity bandits with regret analysis, distinct from budgeted bandits and GP multi-fidelity work.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, well-motivated with real-world examples and helpful illustrations/notation.  
- Significance: highly relevant to practical domains (online advertising, algorithm selection, robotics) and provides actionable guidance on when multi-fidelity helps.  
- Minor theoretical limitation: lower bound proven only for Bernoulli distributions, limiting generality and leaving a gap for certain arm-classes as an open problem.  
- Practical limitation: analysis assumes known bias terms ζ(m) between fidelities; estimation of ζ(m) from data is not addressed.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: technically strong, original, and significant — strongly recommended for acceptance as a meaningful conceptual advance.
