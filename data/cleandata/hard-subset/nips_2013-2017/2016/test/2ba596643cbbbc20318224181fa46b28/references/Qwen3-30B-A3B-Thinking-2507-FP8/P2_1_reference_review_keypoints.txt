- Introduces multi-fidelity bandit setting where each arm has multiple cost-quality reward approximations and proposes MF-UCB to use cheap approximations to eliminate arms and reserve expensive evaluations for promising arms.  
- The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis with matching upper and lower regret bounds, showing improved regret when low fidelities are cheap and informative.  
- MF-UCB is an elegant, natural extension of UCB with a well-motivated fidelity-switching criterion balancing exploration across fidelities.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with useful notation and conceptual partitioning of arms (K(m)) to explain algorithm behavior.  
- Synthetic experiments show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, achieving lower regret and faster convergence in the presented settings.  
- Experimental details are sparse; more results are needed on sensitivity to ζ(m) values and fidelity cost ratios.  
- Assumes known bias bounds ζ(m), which is impractical; the paper does not study estimation or robustness to unknown ζ(m).  
- Relation to prior multi-fidelity/GP work (e.g., Kandasamy et al.) is not clearly differentiated or positioned.  
- Lacks discussion of practical implementation issues when fidelities differ in real computational characteristics (e.g., cheap but slow).  
- Quality: technically sound with complete theoretical analysis and appropriate caveats about limitations near-optimal arms.  
- Clarity: writing and notation are precise and accessible to bandit experts.  
- Originality: novel and meaningful extension of bandit literature to multi-fidelity settings with practical relevance.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem and demonstrates potential for substantial regret reduction in applications.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to add experimental detail and clarify relation to Kandasamy et al.
