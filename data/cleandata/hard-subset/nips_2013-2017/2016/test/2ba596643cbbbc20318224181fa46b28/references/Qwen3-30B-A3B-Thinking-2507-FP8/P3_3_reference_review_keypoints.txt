- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework with cost-weighted regret and MF-UCB that uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.
- Main theoretical result: MF-UCB achieves O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) regret, improving over standard UCB under certain conditions, with a near-optimal lower bound provided.

Strengths:
- Problem relevance: multi-fidelity setting is practically important and well-motivated (e.g., online ads, algorithm selection).
- Theoretical contribution: strong, technically sound regret analysis showing advantage over standard UCB.
- Algorithm design: elegant fidelity-aware extension of UCB with a well-justified γ(m) trade-off.
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments convincingly show MF-UCB outperforms UCB across settings.

Weaknesses:
- Relies heavily on Assumption 1 (fast decay of ζ(m)), which may not hold in many practical cases and is not well-handled when unknown or time-varying.
- Lower bound gap: performance gap for arms in K(m)7 is acknowledged but not addressed or quantified in practice.
- Limited engagement with recent multi-fidelity optimization literature and insufficient analysis of closely related follow-up work.
- Practical implementation gaps: no treatment of unknown or changing ζ(m), limiting real-world applicability.
- Computational complexity: paper omits discussion of MF-UCB’s computational cost for large K or many fidelities.

Overall assessment:
- Technically strong, well-written paper with meaningful theoretical and empirical contributions, but practical applicability is limited by strong assumptions and some theoretical gaps.

Recommendation:
- Accept.
