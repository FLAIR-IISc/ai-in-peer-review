- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity extension to the multi-armed bandit (MAB) problem where cheaper, approximate evaluations complement expensive accurate ones.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that uses lower fidelities for early exploration and higher fidelities for promising arms to reduce overall cost.  
- MF-UCB’s switching criterion uses confidence intervals and fidelity cost differences (γ(m)); design is logical and intuitive.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with a regret bound showing improved performance over standard UCB under certain conditions.  
- Includes a lower-bound analysis that supports near-optimality but acknowledges a gap in some scenarios.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms naive UCB in the presented settings.  
- Formalization of regret for the multi-fidelity setting is a significant contribution.  
- Writing and organization are clear overall, though theoretical derivations (regret bound) are dense and could be simplified.  
- Analysis depends on assumptions (e.g., ζ(m) decay) that may not always hold; sensitivity to these assumptions is not fully explored.  
- A gap remains between the theoretical lower bound and MF-UCB’s regret for arms with small ∆(m)k, which weakens optimality claims.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and uses relatively simple problem settings; more complex, real-world datasets and varied ζ(m) decay rates are needed.  
- The chosen pseudo-reward definition (qt = µ(M)It) may not be universally appropriate; alternative choices and implications should be discussed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, relevant contribution with sound theory and promising results.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify assumption sensitivity, discuss the lower-bound gap, and expand empirical validation.
