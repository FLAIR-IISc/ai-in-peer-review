- Introduces a multi-fidelity extension of the multi-armed bandit problem and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB leverages cheap, lower-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides a regret analysis for MF-UCB and claims near-optimal performance with an explicit theorem (Theorem 2).  
- Includes a lower bound that grounds the theoretical claims.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements of MF-UCB over naive UCB in simulations.  
- Builds on classic UCB work (Auer, 2002) and distinguishes itself from multi-fidelity optimization (Huang et al.) and budgeted bandits (Tran-Thanh et al.).  
- Appropriately cites related recent work on multi-fidelity bandits with GPs (Kandasamy et al., 2016).  
- Novel formalization opens a new avenue within the MAB framework.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-presented, clearly contrasting MF-UCB with standard UCB.  
- Introduction provides clear motivation and intuition for the approach.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and clearly written.  
- The assumption that lower fidelities are biased but informative (|µ(M)k − µ(m)k| ≤ ζ(m)) is crucial and potentially restrictive.  
- The regularity condition on ζ(m) seems somewhat arbitrary and its impact on performance is not fully articulated.  
- There is a noted gap between the provided lower bound and MF-UCB’s performance for certain arms (e.g., K(m)7) that is not fully resolved.  
- Simulations are relatively simple and would benefit from exploring varied fidelity costs, biases, and noise levels.  
- Discussion of the choice qt = µ(M)It is good, but alternatives and their regret implications are insufficiently explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, sound theory, supporting empirical results, clear writing, and novelty.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions/regularity, unresolved lower-bound gap, and limited simulation scope.  
- Overall assessment: solid and novel contribution to MAB research with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses (Reviewer: Strong Accept).  
- Recommendations: analyze sensitivity to violations of the ζ(m) assumption, investigate closing the lower-bound gap (especially for K(m)7), and expand simulations to more complex scenarios.
