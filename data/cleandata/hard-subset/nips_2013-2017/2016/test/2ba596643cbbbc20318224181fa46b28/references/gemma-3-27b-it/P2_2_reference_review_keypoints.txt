- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework with cost-weighted regret and the MF-UCB algorithm balancing fidelity-specific evaluations.  
- MF-UCB uses cheap, low-fidelity evaluations to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive, high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB, especially with many arms and few optimal ones.  
- Formalizes a regret metric tailored to multi-fidelity settings and gives rigorous lower-bound analysis.  
- Positions work relative to prior MAB and multi-fidelity literature, arguing a distribution-agnostic, bandit-focused contribution.  
- Acknowledges related GP-based multi-fidelity bandit work but claims broader applicability.  
- Strength: novel, realistic problem formulation applicable to domains like advertising and robotics.  
- Strength: strong theoretical analysis and well-developed lower bounds.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive MF-UCB algorithm and fidelity-switching criterion.  
- Strength: empirical simulations that corroborate theoretical claims.  
- Strength: thorough proofs and detailed analysis increasing confidence in results.  
- Weakness: assumption that lower-fidelity means lie within known bounds ζ(m) may not hold in practice and sensitivity is unexplored.  
- Weakness: the regularity/decay condition on ζ(m) feels restrictive and its performance impact when violated is unclear.  
- Weakness: gap remains between the algorithm’s regret bound and the theoretical lower bound due to the switching rule.  
- Weakness: choice and motivation for the specific γ(m) (Equation 4) is not fully justified or intuitively explained.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with contributions outweighing weaknesses; recommended for acceptance (Strong Accept, 8/10).
