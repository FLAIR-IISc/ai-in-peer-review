- Introduces a multi-fidelity K-armed bandit with cost-weighted regret and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB leverages cheaper, lower fidelities and uses a switching criterion based on confidence bounds and fidelity costs γ(m).  
- Theoretical analysis gives a regret upper bound and shows MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when lower fidelities are informative and cheaper.  
- Provides a lower bound suggesting near-optimality of the approach.  
- Simulated experiments support the theoretical findings.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear.  
- Assumes known bias bounds ζ(m) between fidelities; robustness to large bias is not explored.  
- Regularity assumption on decay of ζ(m) (Assumption 1) seems arbitrary and may limit applicability; analysis without it is lacking.  
- A gap remains between the lower and upper bounds (notably for arms in K(m)) with no clear route to close it.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs more parameter sweeps, complex reward models, real-world datasets, and ablation on γ(m).  
- The choice qt = µ(M)It is plausible but not fully justified; alternatives are not investigated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with rigorous analysis and practical relevance; recommended for acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-defined relevant problem, sensible algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees (including lower bound), empirical support, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on restrictive assumptions, unresolved gap between bounds, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
