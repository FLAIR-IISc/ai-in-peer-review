- Summary: Proposes MF-UCB for multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelities, introduces cost-weighted regret, and proves near-optimal regret bounds improving over standard UCB in favorable regimes.

- Strength: Strong motivation and problem formulation with realistic examples and a well-motivated cost/ζ(m) assumption structure.

- Strength: Significant theoretical contribution—regret bounds showing clear benefits from lower-fidelity approximations and near-optimality via a lower bound.

- Strength: Clean algorithmic insight—MF-UCB is a natural UCB extension with a justified fidelity-switching rule and well-designed γ(m).

- Strength: Empirical validation—synthetic experiments convincingly show lower regret and efficient fidelity use versus standard UCB.

- Weakness: Assumption restrictiveness—ζ(m) decay requirement may not hold in practice and the paper does not analyze effects of violations.

- Weakness: Lower-bound gap—the analysis reveals a gap for arms in K(m)7 where lower fidelities mislead, and no remedy is proposed.

- Weakness: Limited related-work comparison—the distinction from budgeted bandits and other multi-fidelity optimization approaches is not fully clarified.

- Weakness: Practical implementation concerns—not addressed are scenarios where lower fidelities are misleading beyond the ζ(m) model.

- Evaluation (Quality): Technically sound with rigorous theory and experiments; claims well supported.

- Evaluation (Clarity): Exceptionally well-written, organized, and clearly notated with effective figures.

- Evaluation (Originality): Novel and meaningful extension of UCB to multi-fidelity settings with solid theoretical grounding.

- Evaluation (Significance): High practical relevance across domains; demonstrates substantial regret reductions in resource-constrained settings.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: Expand related work to distinguish budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity methods, analyze impacts of violating Assumption 1, and detail practical implications/possible fixes for the K(m)7 gap.

- Final assessment: Excellent paper that advances multi-armed bandit theory and practice; appropriate for NIPS acceptance.
