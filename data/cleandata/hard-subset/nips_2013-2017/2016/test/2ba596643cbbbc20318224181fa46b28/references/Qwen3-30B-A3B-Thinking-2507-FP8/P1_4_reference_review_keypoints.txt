- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm using cheaper approximations to guide expensive evaluations.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when low fidelities are sufficiently cheaper and few arms are high-performing.  
- Offers rigorous technical analysis with clearly stated assumptions and both upper and lower regret bounds (Theorems 2 and 4).  
- Empirical synthetic results support the theoretical claims, showing MF-UCB reduces regret compared to UCB.  
- Novel contribution: first formalization of regret guarantees in a multi-fidelity bandit setting, distinct from budgeted bandits and prior multi-fidelity optimization work.  
- Well-written and organized manuscript with clear notation, motivating examples, and detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Significant practical relevance for domains with costly evaluations (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Minor clarity issues: some sections could be more concise and guidance on choosing parameters (e.g., ρ) is vague.  
- Limited empirical validation: experiments are synthetic and lack real-world application results.  
- Theoretical gap: lower bound may be loose for arms in K(m)7; authors explain but do not resolve this limitation.  
- Review recommends acceptance based on novelty, rigorous analysis, and demonstrated advantages over standard UCB.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, strong theoretical guarantees, clear advantages and practical motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: synthetic-only experiments, minor presentation issues, and an acknowledged theoretical gap for certain arms.  
- Final verdict: strong recommend accept; paper is timely, technically solid, and suitable for NIPS.
