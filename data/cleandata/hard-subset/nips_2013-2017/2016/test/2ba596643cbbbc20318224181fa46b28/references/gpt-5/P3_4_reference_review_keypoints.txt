- Summary: Proposes a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with M fidelities (costs λ(m), biases ζ(m)), defines cost-weighted pseudo-regret measured against highest fidelity, and introduces MF-UCB using per-fidelity UCBs + bias correction and fidelity thresholds γ(m); analysis partitions arms into K(m), gives improved regret bounds, and provides a Bernoulli lower bound; simulations support theory.

- Relation to prior work: Distinct from budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, simulator-based RL, and weak/strong labeler active learning; methodologically closest to UCB/elimination literature but novel in multi-fidelity bias+cost setting.

- Strength: Clear, explicit modeling of fidelity biases and costs with a principled regret definition aligned to high-fidelity outcomes.

- Strength: Simple, implementable algorithm (per-fidelity UCBs with ζ(m) inflation and intuitive γ(m) escalation rule).

- Strength: Strong theoretical results—arm partition K(m), regret improvements over single-fidelity UCB, and matching lower bounds for a large arm subset.

- Strength: Careful cost accounting (capital Λ control and negligible residual term) and simulations that concentrate high-fidelity pulls on promising arms.

- Weakness: Assumes known, uniform ζ(m) and regularity Assumption 1; no treatment of arm-dependent or unknown/mis-specified ζ.

- Weakness: Regret notion alternatives are only briefly discussed; adaptation of MF-UCB to other regret definitions is not developed.

- Weakness: Lower bound restricted to Bernoulli rewards (µ* > 1/2, ζ(1) < 1/2); extension to general sub-Gaussian ψ is not shown.

- Weakness: Identified gap for borderline arms in K(m)7; potential practical fixes (e.g., more low-fidelity sampling) are not explored.

- Weakness: Experiments limited to synthetic data and single-fidelity UCB baseline; missing baselines (flat UCB over (k,m), adapted elimination, Thompson Sampling) and sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness: Minor clarity/notation inconsistencies (e.g., Kg vs K(m)) and some proofs/constants relegated to appendix without main-text intuition for Assumption 1.

- Quality: Technically solid with careful, high-probability analyses, explicit assumptions, and candid discussion of limitations.

- Clarity: Generally well written and organized; main intuitions (γ(m), K(m)) are clear despite minor notational issues.

- Originality: Novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation, regret definition tied to highest-fidelity reward, and MF-UCB design/analysis in the bandit literature.

- Significance: High relevance to many applications (ads, algorithm selection, sim-to-real); likely to spur follow-up work on contexts, priors, unknown ζ, and Thompson sampling.

- Suggestions: Test robustness to ζ mis-specification and arm-dependent ζk(m); add additional baselines and sensitivity studies; give practical guidance for choosing ρ and handling general ψ; sketch adaptation to alternative regret definitions.

- Pros (summary): Novel formulation, simple practical algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and supportive empirical evidence.

- Cons (summary): Requires known/uniform ζ and Assumption 1, lower-bound scope limited, gap for some arms, and limited empirical validation.

- Recommendation: Accept (score ~7–8/10, reviewer confidence high).
