- Introduces a multi-fidelity extension to multi-armed bandits where each arm has multiple approximations with different costs and accuracies.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate bad arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Main theoretical result: regret bound O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) showing potential improvement over standard UCB when λ(1) << λ(2) and |Kg| << K.  
- Provides matching lower bound, arguing near-optimality of the approach.  
- Problem is well-motivated by real-world applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- Algorithm design is natural and intuitively justified; paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Restrictive Assumption 1 (fast decay of ζ(m)) is required for strong regret comparison and is not well-motivated or empirically validated.  
- Choice of γ(m) depends on ζ(m) via ψ^{-1}(·) and may be difficult to implement without knowing true ζ(m).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic problems and lacks discussion of practical implementation challenges.  
- Does not consider adaptive fidelity selection over time, which could be important in practice.  
- Related work is discussed, but the paper could better distinguish itself from recent multi-fidelity GP bandit work (Kandasamy et al. 2016).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with clear practical relevance; recommendation: accept.  
- Pro arguments: novel formulation, rigorous analysis with matching bounds, natural algorithm, clear presentation.  
- Con arguments: restrictive assumption, limited experiments, missing implementation details, no adaptive fidelity consideration.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss practical settings where Assumption 1 holds, add implementation guidance for estimating ζ(m) and setting γ(m), expand empirical evaluation (including real datasets), and consider extending to adaptive fidelity selection.
