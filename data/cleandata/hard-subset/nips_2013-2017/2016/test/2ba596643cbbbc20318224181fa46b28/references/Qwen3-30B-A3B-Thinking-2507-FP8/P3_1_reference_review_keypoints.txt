- Proposes MF-UCB, a multi-fidelity extension of multi-armed bandits that uses cheaper low-fidelity evaluations to prune arms before costly high-fidelity pulls.  
- Formalizes the multi-fidelity setting and defines a regret metric that accounts for evaluation costs and rewards.  
- Uses arm partitioning into sets K(m) to intuitively show how the algorithm allocates evaluations across fidelities.  
- Proves a regret bound (Theorem 2) of O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) showing potential improvement over standard UCB when lower fidelities are informative and cheap.  
- Strong theoretical analysis including a lower bound (Theorem 4) that shows near-optimality for arms in K(m)₃; proof sketches give insight into algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results on synthetic tasks corroborate theory: MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Major weakness: Assumption 1 (rapid decay of ζ(m)) is strong and may not hold in practical applications, with insufficient justification provided.  
- Theoretical gap: MF-UCB fails to meet the lower bound for arms in K(m)₇, and the paper does not propose fixes or alternative algorithms.  
- Related work section is superficial and lacks thorough comparison to broader multi-fidelity optimization literature.  
- Practical implementation issues are under-discussed, notably how to choose ρ and how to handle unknown ζ(m) values in real settings.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution with clear intuition and validation, but limited immediate practical applicability due to strong assumptions and unresolved theoretical gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss when Assumption 1 is realistic, address the K(m)₇ gap, expand related-work comparisons, and add practical implementation guidance.
