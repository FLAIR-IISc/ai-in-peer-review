

# Review of "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit"

This paper introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple approximations (fidelities) with different costs and biases. The authors propose an MF-UCB algorithm that uses cheaper lower-fidelity approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity plays for promising arms. The paper provides a regret bound showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when lower fidelities are significantly cheaper and the optimal set is small.

**Strengths:**
- The problem formulation is novel and well-motivated by practical applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.
- The theoretical analysis provides a clear regret bound (Theorem 2) and a matching lower bound (Theorem 4), with careful comparison to UCB.
- The algorithmic intuition is sound, and the simulations demonstrate MF-UCB's superiority over UCB on synthetic problems.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Notational Inconsistencies:** The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout. For example, "µ?" for the optimal mean (instead of µ*) is confusing. The regret definition (equation 1) uses confusing tilde notation (r̃(Λ,A) and R̃(Λ,A)) that's not properly defined. The notation ∆(m)k = µ? − µ(m)k − ζ(m) is inconsistent with later usage of ∆(M)k.

2. **Formatting and Typos:** The paper contains multiple formatting errors. In the introduction: "setK = {1, . . . ,K}" should be "set K". The conference name "th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2016)" appears mid-sentence. The phrase "expends a capital th Conference" is clearly a typesetting error.

3. **Technical Errors:** Assumption 1 states "∑m i=1 1 ψ(ζ(i)) ≤ 1 ψ(ζ(m+1))" which is missing parentheses and summation notation (should be ∑_{i=1}^m 1/ψ(ζ(i)) ≤ 1/ψ(ζ(m+1))). The regret bound in Theorem 2 incorrectly writes "E[R(Λ)] log(nΛ) . ∑" instead of "E[R(Λ)] . log(nΛ) · ∑".

4. **Missing Details:** The proof sketch (Section 5.1) refers to "Lemma 5" and "Lemma 6" but doesn't provide formal statements of these lemmas. The paper claims "the proof is given in Appendix A" but the excerpt provided doesn't include the appendix.

5. **Ambiguous Claims:** The paper states MF-UCB is "at most a constant times worse than UCB" but doesn't specify what this constant is. For practical applications, the constant could be significant.

6. **Related Work:** The paper cites Kandasamy et al. [8] as having "extended ideas in this work" but [8] is listed as a reference. If this is prior work, it should be cited as such in the related work section, not described as extending their own work.

7. **Figure Reference:** The paper references Figure 1 with "K(1),K(2),K(3),K(4) are shown in yellow, green, red and purple respectively" but the figure is not included in the provided text.

**Overall Assessment:**
While the paper presents a novel framework and provides theoretically sound analysis, the numerous technical and formatting errors significantly undermine the paper's quality. As a perfectionist reviewer, I find these minor flaws substantial enough to justify rejection, as they indicate insufficient attention to detail that could lead to confusion for readers and reviewers. The paper's contribution is meaningful, but the presentation issues make it difficult to assess the technical merits fully.

The paper would benefit from careful proofreading, consistent notation, and proper formatting before reconsideration. The core idea has merit, but the current presentation does not meet NIPS standards for technical precision and clarity.

**Recommendation:** Reject (due to multiple minor but significant technical and formatting issues that compromise the paper's credibility and readability).
