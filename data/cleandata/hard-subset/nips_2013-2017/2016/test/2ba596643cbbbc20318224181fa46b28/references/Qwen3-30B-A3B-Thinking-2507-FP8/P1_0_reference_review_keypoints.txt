- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits, formalizes a cost-aware regret, and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB extends UCB to exploit cheaper, lower-fidelity evaluations with different costs and biases.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds (Theorem 2) showing gains over standard UCB when low fidelities are cheaper and promising arms are few.  
- Proves a near-optimal lower bound (Theorem 4), with a gap only for arms that are misleading at low fidelities.  
- Paper is technically sound, with rigorous analysis and proofs in the appendix.  
- Well-written and well-organized; notation and key concepts (fidelities, regret, arm partitioning) are clear.  
- Empirical validation is limited to synthetic experiments; no real-world experiments are provided.  
- Assumes known fidelity biases ζ(m), which may be unrealistic and is not addressed (estimation/robustness absent).  
- The choice and intuition behind the γ(m) parameter need clearer motivation and connection to regret trade-offs.  
- Lacks discussion of practical implementation details, especially how to transition between fidelities when optimal fidelity is unknown.  
- Original contribution: first formalization of multi-fidelity regret in bandits and a novel algorithm with theoretical guarantees.  
- Significant practical relevance for domains with expensive experiments and cheaper approximations (advertising, robotics, algorithm selection).  
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical contribution, well-presented, minor practical weaknesses expected for a theory paper.
