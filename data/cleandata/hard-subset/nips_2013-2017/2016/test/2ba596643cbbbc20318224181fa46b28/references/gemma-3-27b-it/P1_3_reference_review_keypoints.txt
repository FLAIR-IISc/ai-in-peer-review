- Proposes a multi-fidelity extension to multi-armed bandits with cost-weighted regret and a Multi-Fidelity UCB (MF-UCB) algorithm.
- Proves a regret bound for MF-UCB that can outperform standard UCB by using cheaper low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate suboptimal arms.
- Positions the work relative to MAB, multi-fidelity optimization, RL, and budgeted bandits, noting broader setting than GP-based prior work.
- Strength: introduces a novel, well-motivated multi-fidelity bandit formulation with clear practical relevance.
- Strength: provides theoretical guarantees specifying conditions (λ(1) < λ(2) and |Kg| < K) under which MF-UCB helps.
- Strength: MF-UCB is conceptually simple and uses adaptive fidelity selection via confidence bounds.
- Strength: simulations support the theory, showing MF-UCB outperforming naive UCB in tested scenarios.
- Strength: paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear organization.
- Weakness: analysis assumes knowledge of ζ(m) and decay Assumption 1, but sensitivity to violations and estimation issues are not explored.
- Weakness: gap exists between the provided lower bound and the algorithm’s regret bound for certain arms (K(m)), tied to the switching criterion.
- Weakness: simulation scenarios are limited; more diverse costs, fidelities, and complex environments would strengthen empirical claims.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical implementation choices (e.g., selecting ρ and γ(m)) and real-world applicability challenges.
- Weakness: the chosen regret definition is somewhat convoluted and could be simplified for clarity.
- Quality: technically sound with solid theory and supporting experiments, though reliant on assumptions.
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but regret definition and parts of the theoretical analysis could be simplified.
- Originality: novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation and original adaptation of UCB with new theoretical insights.
- Significance: addresses an important, widely applicable problem and offers a promising approach to leverage cheaper approximations.
- For acceptance: novel problem, sound algorithm with regret bound, empirical support, and practical importance.
- Against acceptance: reliance on strong assumptions, theoretical lower-bound gap, and limited simulation scope.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to discuss assumptions and investigate the lower-bound gap.
