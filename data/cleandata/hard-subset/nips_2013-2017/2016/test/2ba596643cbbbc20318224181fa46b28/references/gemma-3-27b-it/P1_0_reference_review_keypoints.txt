- Paper introduces MF-UCB: a multi-fidelity extension of multi-armed bandits using cheap low-fidelity evaluations to guide expensive high-fidelity pulls.  
- Theoretical regret bound shows MF-UCB can outperform naive UCB under certain fidelity-structure conditions, supported by empirical simulations.

- Builds on UCB literature and contrasts with multi-fidelity work in optimization/RL; properly situates itself relative to budgeted bandits, active learning, and GP multi-fidelity bandits.  

- Novel problem formulation with clear, practical motivation for multi-fidelity bandits.

- Solid theoretical analysis including regret bound and lower bound discussion; proof sketch gives intuition on leveraging fidelities.

- MF-UCB algorithm is clearly described with intuitive use of low fidelities for elimination and high fidelities for confirmation.

- Empirical results demonstrate practical benefits over a standard UCB baseline.

- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses clear notation.

- Key assumption: lower-fidelity means are within known bounds ζ(m) of true means, which may be unrealistic in some applications and lacks discussion of violations.

- Assumption 1 (decay of ζ(m)) is not fully justified; the paper doesn't show cases where violating it degrades performance significantly.

- There is a gap between the presented regret bound and the derived lower bound for certain arms (K(m)7) that is not closed or deeply discussed.

- Simulations are limited in scope; more arms and varied cost/fidelity tradeoffs would strengthen empirical claims.

- Choice of qt = µ(M)It is justified but alternative choices and their impact on regret are not explored.

- Arguments for acceptance: relevant novel problem, sound theory, effective algorithm, and clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: strong/practicality-questionable assumptions and incomplete theoretical tightness; empirical validation could be broader.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; concerns (assumptions and minor theoretical gaps) do not outweigh contributions.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: discuss impact of violating |µ(M)k - µ(m)k| ≤ ζ(m); justify or show counterexamples for Assumption 1; expand discussion of regret vs. lower bound gap and future work; consider other qt choices and their implications; include more varied simulation scenarios.
