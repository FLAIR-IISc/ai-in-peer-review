- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework and MF-UCB algorithm that leverages cheap low-fidelity evaluations to focus high-fidelity pulls on promising arms, with improved regret under reasonable fidelity approximations.
- Builds on UCB literature while distinguishing itself from multi-fidelity optimization, simulator-based RL, and budgeted bandits; relates to prior GP multi-fidelity work.
- Novel and practically relevant problem setting that generalizes classic bandits.
- MF-UCB is clearly described and intuitively motivated via fidelity-switching based on confidence bounds.
- Provides rigorous regret analysis showing MF-UCB’s benefits and includes a lower bound suggesting near-optimality.
- Empirical simulations indicate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.
- Relies on potentially strong assumption that lower-fidelity means are within known ζ(m) of high-fidelity mean, with limited exploration of misspecification effects.
- Impact of the regularity condition (Assumption 1) on performance and sensitivity to its violation is not fully clarified.
- A gap remains between the theoretical lower bound and achieved regret for some arms, indicating room for improvement.
- Simulations are limited in scope and some setup details are confined to the appendix, reducing empirical clarity.
- Alternative regret definitions are mentioned but not thoroughly analyzed or justified.
- Overall assessment: strong technical quality, originality, and potential significance; recommended as Strong Accept.
