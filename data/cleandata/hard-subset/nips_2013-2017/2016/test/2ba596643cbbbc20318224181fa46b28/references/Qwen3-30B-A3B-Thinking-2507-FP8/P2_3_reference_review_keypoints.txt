- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm has multiple-cost fidelity evaluations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, which uses cheap low-fidelity evaluations to eliminate bad arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides theoretical upper-regret bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when lower fidelities are informative and cheap.  
- Establishes a matching lower bound, demonstrating MF-UCB is near-optimal overall.  
- Formalism and assumptions (fidelity costs, approximation quality) are clearly defined and technically sound.  
- Algorithm design is careful (including the γ(m) choice) and balances exploration across fidelities.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, with proofs for Theorem 2 (upper bound) and Theorem 4 (lower bound).  
- Synthetic experiments corroborate theory, showing lower regret growth than UCB.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful figures and intuitive explanations; Theorem 2’s regret bound could use more intuition in the main text.  
- Original contribution: first formal treatment of multi-fidelity regret in bandits and clear distinction from related budgeted/multi-fidelity work.  
- High practical significance for domains with costly evaluations (advertising, algorithm selection, robotics).  
- Main weaknesses: limited discussion of practical issues when ζ(m) (fidelity biases) are unknown, and a theoretical gap for arms in K(m)7 that could be better addressed.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically solid, well-motivated, empirically supported, with minor issues that don’t undermine the contribution.
