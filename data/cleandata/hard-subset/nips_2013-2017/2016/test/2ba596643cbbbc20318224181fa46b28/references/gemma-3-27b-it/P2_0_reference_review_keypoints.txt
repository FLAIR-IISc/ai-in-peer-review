- Summary: Introduces a multi-fidelity MAB framework and MF-UCB algorithm that leverages cheap, low-fidelity evaluations to prune arms, with theoretical regret bounds and supportive simulations.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on UCB bandits and connects to multi-fidelity optimization, budgeted bandits, and active learning, while emphasizing unique bandit-specific bias challenges.  
- Novel framework: Presents a practically relevant and well-defined extension of standard MABs to multi-fidelity settings.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides a meaningful regret bound O((λ1K + λ2|Kg|) log(Λ/λ2)) showing gains when low fidelities are much cheaper and few optimal arms exist.  
- Algorithm design: MF-UCB is an intuitive UCB adaptation with a theoretically motivated fidelity-switching criterion γ(m).  
- Empirical validation: Simulations demonstrate MF-UCB outperforming naive UCB; visualization of multi-fidelity exploration is insightful.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is clear, well-structured, and uses consistent notation.  
- Assumption 1 concern: The ζ(m) decay regularity assumption seems artificial and its effect when violated is not examined.  
- Lower-bound gap: A discrepancy remains between the provided regret upper bound and the proposed lower bound without deeper resolution.  
- Limited simulations: Experiments are narrow; varying cost ratios, |Kg|, and noise would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Regret definition discussion: The choice to compete against high-fidelity optimum is justified but alternatives (e.g., using current fidelity) are not fully explored.  
- For acceptance: Novel problem, solid theoretical insights, supporting experiments, and clear presentation argue in favor.  
- Against acceptance: Restricted assumption, unresolved lower-bound gap, and limited empirical scope are concerns.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
