- Problem: Introduces stochastic K-armed bandits with M biased, costed fidelities per arm and known bias bounds, optimizing high-fidelity utility under a capital budget.  
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB using per-fidelity UCBs inflated by bias bounds and a cost-informed fidelity escalation rule via thresholds γ(m).  
- Key idea: Use cheap, biased fidelities to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserve expensive high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- Main theoretical result: Finite-capital instance-dependent regret bound scaling like O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|K2| + …) log(Λ/λ(M))) where Km are arms surviving lower-fidelity elimination.  
- Comparison to UCB: MF-UCB is never much worse than high-fidelity UCB and can be substantially better when many arms are identifiable as bad at low fidelities and costs rise.  
- Lower bound: Provides a Bernoulli-specific lower bound under a subpolynomial-pulls assumption and identifies a constant-factor gap for a subset of arms due to the switching rule.  
- Experiments: Synthetic experiments support theoretical improvements but are limited in scope and baselines.  
- Novelty: First rigorous treatment of multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits with biased fidelities, bias-aware UCB design, instance-dependent analysis, and a complementary lower bound.  
- Assumptions: Relies on known bias bounds ζ(m), generic concentration ψ, and a regularity condition on ζ decay (Assumption 1).  
- Proof quality: Clean, standard UCB-style proof strategy (arm partitioning, per-fidelity pull bounds) and appropriate change-of-measure lower bound.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well organized and mathematically clear; MF-UCB and γ(m) are well motivated though some descriptions and notation could be streamlined.  
- Regret notion: Thoughtful for high-fidelity utility applications but nonstandard; additional discussion of alternative objectives would help.  
- Practical limitations: Strong reliance on known ζ(m) and ψ, no robustness analysis to misspecification, and limited empirical validation.  
- Experimental limitations: Only synthetic settings, no ablations or sensitivity analyses, and comparisons limited to naive UCB rather than stronger baselines.  
- Suggested experiments/baselines: Recommend two-phase screening baselines, Thompson sampling variants, ablations over K, M, λ ratios, ζ decay, and a semi-real experiment (e.g., algorithm selection with subsampling).  
- Robustness suggestion: Investigate sensitivity to misspecified ζ(m), online estimation of ζ, or adaptive/safe variants of γ(m).  
- Behavior under violated assumptions: Clarify degradation when Assumption 1 fails and whether fidelities can be merged or algorithm degrades to standard UCB.  
- Significance: Practically relevant setting with provable gains; ideas could influence use of approximate evaluations in bandits if empirical and robustness gaps are addressed.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid, novel theoretical contribution but needs stronger empirical validation and robustness analysis.
