- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits where cheaper lower-fidelity evaluations trade accuracy for cost.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively balances exploration across fidelities to minimize regret.  
- Formalizes the multi-fidelity bandit problem, filling a practical gap in bandit literature.  
- Builds on UCB methodology while incorporating fidelity-aware decision rules.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: regret bound showing improvement over naive UCB when lower fidelities are cheap and informative.  
- Lower bound analysis indicates MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Identifies a minor gap in the lower bound for specific arms (k ∈ K(m)), noted as an open problem.  
- Clear, well-structured exposition with helpful figures.  
- Experimental results on synthetic tasks demonstrate practical gains, especially when the optimal arm set is small.  
- Empirical evaluation could be strengthened with more diverse, real-world benchmarks.  
- Broad applicability to domains like online advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant, and a strong candidate for acceptance.
