

This paper introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be played at multiple fidelities, with higher fidelities providing more accurate but more expensive observations. The authors formalize a cost-weighted regret metric where regret is defined as cost × pseudo-regret, and propose the MF-UCB algorithm that adaptively selects fidelities to minimize this regret. The theoretical contribution is significant, with tight upper and lower bounds demonstrating MF-UCB's near-optimality under certain conditions. The paper builds on UCB methods but introduces substantial new challenges in the multi-fidelity setting, differentiating it from prior work on budgeted bandits or multi-fidelity optimization.

The main paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured, making the complex concepts accessible. The theoretical contribution is strong, with solid proofs supporting the regret bounds. The algorithm is well-motivated by practical examples like online advertising and algorithm selection, where cheaper approximations of outcomes are available.

However, several weaknesses require attention. The supplementary materials contain numerous typos and small errors (e.g., page 10 equation 8: typo in k/K, page 11 equation 10: γ should be γ^(m), page 15 equation: missing tildes) that would confuse readers and undermine confidence in the proofs. The paper's use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading, as hidden constants like ρ are algorithm parameters that should be presented explicitly. Additionally, the practical applicability is unclear; the authors should discuss how MF-UCB would handle inaccurate estimation of fidelity parameters ζ and potential mitigations for this limitation.

The paper's strengths in theoretical contribution and clarity outweigh its weaknesses, which can be addressed with careful revision. The problem is original and significant, addressing an important gap in multi-armed bandit literature. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides meaningful insights into how multi-fidelity information can improve regret bounds. While the practical discussion could be strengthened, the core theoretical contribution is substantial and would be valuable to the community.

Pro: Strong theoretical contribution, well-written main paper, significant problem formulation, tight bounds. Con: Appendix errors, misleading notation, unclear practical limitations. With the appendix errors corrected and practical considerations expanded, this would be an excellent contribution to NIPS.
