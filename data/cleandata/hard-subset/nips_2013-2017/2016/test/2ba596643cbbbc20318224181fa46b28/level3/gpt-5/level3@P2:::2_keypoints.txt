- Introduces stochastic K-armed bandits with multiple fidelities per arm, where lower fidelities are biased but cheaper and bias envelopes ζ(m) shrink with fidelity.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: fidelity-specific UCBs adjusted by ζ(m), selects arm with highest upper bound, and adaptively chooses lowest informative fidelity via thresholds γ(m).  
- Provides an upper bound on cost-weighted pseudo-regret under capital budget Λ and a complementary lower bound, showing near-optimality except for a characterized set of “borderline” arms.  
- Synthetic experiments show consistent gains over standard UCB that ignores lower fidelities.  
- Builds on classical UCB/regret literature and budgeted/multi-fidelity work, but novel in formal regret analysis for this exact biased multi-fidelity stochastic setting.  
- Related to GP multi-fidelity bandits but distinct by focusing on unstructured stochastic K-armed setting with explicit bias envelopes and costs.  
- Strength: Clear, principled modeling with explicit bias envelopes and costs.  
- Strength: MF-UCB is simple, intuitive, and grounded in UCB principles; switching rule γ(m) well-motivated by cost–information trade-off.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical analysis—upper bound decomposes by arm/fidelity and a lower bound demonstrates near-optimality; identifies when multi-fidelity helps or not.  
- Strength: Analysis explains cost savings and when multi-fidelity offers no benefit (arms near-optimal); experiments align with theory.  
- Weakness: Assumes known ζ(m) and Assumption 1, which may be unrealistic; robustness to misspecification is not addressed.  
- Suggestion: Discuss robustness when ζ(m) are only upper bounds, methods to estimate ζ(m) online, and sensitivity to ζ(m)/λ(m) misspecification with ablation plots.  
- Weakness: Regret notion fixed to highest-fidelity reward; suggest discussing alternative regret definitions for other domains (simulators, clinical trials).  
- Empirics weakness: Only synthetic data; recommend semi-synthetic/real evaluations and stronger baselines (cost-aware UCB, Thompson sampling variants, heuristics).  
- Empirics suggestion: Include sensitivity analyses for M, ζ decay rates, and λ ratios.  
- Presentation weakness: Overuse of “up-to-constant” notation hides dependence on parameters like ρ; recommend explicit constants or a lemma isolating ρ-dependence.  
- Quality: Technical development is sound; partitioning by K(m) is insightful and lower bound adaptation is appropriate; practical implications of Assumption 1 need brief discussion.  
- Clarity: Main paper is readable and algorithm intuition clear; supplementary has many glitches and would benefit from fewer asymptotic shorthands and explicit constants.  
- Originality: Novel formulation and algorithm for biased multi-fidelity observations with cost-weighted regret; theoretical characterization and lower bound are new.  
- Significance: Relevant setting (ads, AutoML, simulators); advances understanding of cost-reducing low-fidelity information and invites extensions (unknown ζ, contextual bandits, Thompson sampling).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Well-motivated problem, simple principled algorithm, strong near-optimal theory, and experimental corroboration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on known ζ and regularity assumption, limited empirical evaluation, and numerous supplementary typos.  
- Key questions for authors: Sensitivity to ζ(m) misestimates; whether γ(m) can be adapted online; extensions to contextual bandits/Thompson sampling; narrowing the K(m) gap via validation checks.  
- Appendix typos and errors listed (multiple equation/notation issues, wrong variables, missing terms, and spelling mistakes) that need correction.
