- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm can be pulled at M fidelities trading cost vs. bias/noise.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity budget for promising arms.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: upper bound on cumulative pseudo-regret for MF-UCB and a matching problem-dependent lower bound, showing near-optimality under conditions.  
- Connects to prior multi-fidelity optimization work while novelly applying the idea to exploration–exploitation bandits.  
- Strength: original and significant problem formulation with clear practical motivations (A/B testing, hyperparameter tuning, clinical trials).  
- Strength: strong technical quality — intuitive algorithm and sound regret analyses with well-structured proofs at a high level.  
- Strength: main paper is very clear and well-written; algorithm mechanics and fidelity-switching intuition are well explained.  
- Weakness: supplementary proofs contain numerous typos and minor errors (many specific equation/notation mistakes listed) that hinder verification and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: theoretical bounds are presented up to multiplicative constants; hiding a tunable parameter ρ in these constants obscures quantitative implications — exact non-asymptotic bounds should be shown.  
- Weakness: practical applicability limited by assumption that fidelity bias parameters ζ(m) are known; need discussion on robustness to misspecification and possibilities for online estimation.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require thorough proofreading of the appendix and added discussion on estimating/robustness of fidelity parameters.
