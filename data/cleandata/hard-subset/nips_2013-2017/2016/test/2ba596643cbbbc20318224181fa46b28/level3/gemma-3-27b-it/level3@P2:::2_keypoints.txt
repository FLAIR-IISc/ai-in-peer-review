- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity MAB setting with cheap biased approximations and costly accurate observations, and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- MF-UCB uses low-fidelity evaluations to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity trials for promising arms; defines a cost-weighted regret.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: an upper bound on MF-UCB regret and a matching lower bound, showing near-optimality.  
- Builds on the UCB framework and connects clearly to existing bandit and multi-fidelity optimization literature.  
- Novel and practically relevant generalization of classical MABs (applications: online advertising, algorithm selection, robotics).  
- Strong theoretical analysis with rigorous derivation of upper and lower bounds.  
- Paper is clearly written; motivation, formulation, algorithm, and analysis are well presented.  
- Significant contribution with potential to advance bandit optimization.  
- Lacks discussion on practical estimation and robustness of fidelity bias ζ(m).  
- Performance likely sensitive to parameters (e.g., ρ) but paper gives no practical tuning guidance.  
- Appendix contains numerous typos/minor errors (e.g., eq. (8) "k/K" typo, Lemma 5 mislabeled), reducing readability and trust.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation obscures dependence on algorithm parameters and practical implications.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing appendix errors and adding discussion on ζ(m) estimation and parameter-tuning guidance.
