- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) setting with cheap biased approximations and costly precise feedback.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm to balance exploration and exploitation across fidelities.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, showing near-optimality under conditions.  
- Builds on UCB and multi-fidelity optimization literature, uniquely framing multi-fidelity within bandits.  
- Related work (budgeted bandits, active learning) is discussed but distinguished by different formulations.  
- Strength: problem is relevant and practically motivated as an extension of classical bandits.  
- Strength: MF-UCB is intuitive and supported by rigorous theoretical analysis and sound (though lengthy) proofs.  
- Strength: Simulation results demonstrate empirical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Many typographical errors, incorrect lemma references, and inconsistent notation in the appendix hinder readability.  
- Weakness: Use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is ambiguous; explicit constants would improve clarity.  
- Weakness: Strong assumption that fidelity bias ζ(m) is known; no strategy given for estimating or handling inaccurate biases.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of robustness to noisy/inaccurate fidelity information and of computational costs.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with revisions—fix appendix errors and add discussion on practical limitations and mitigation strategies.
