**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the context of multi-armed bandits, where arms can be sampled at multiple fidelities with different costs. The authors propose an adaptive algorithm, called Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB), which leverages the structure of the multi-fidelity setting to achieve better regret than traditional UCB algorithms. The paper provides a regret analysis and a lower bound, and experiments on synthetic problems demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithm.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a new and interesting framework for studying multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelities.
* The authors provide a thorough regret analysis and a lower bound, which demonstrates the optimality of their algorithm.
* The experiments on synthetic problems show promising results, with MF-UCB outperforming traditional UCB algorithms.

Weaknesses:

* The notation is confusing and dispersed, which harms readability.
* The advertising example is misleading, and references to display time correlations and non-stationarity are not relevant to the paper's scope.
* The regret analysis is questionable, as it treats the best arm as the one with the highest mean regardless of cost, potentially hiding the costs of low-fidelity sampling.
* The writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper introduces a new framework for studying multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelities, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors' algorithm, MF-UCB, is a novel approach that leverages the structure of the multi-fidelity setting to achieve better regret than traditional UCB algorithms. The paper's results have the potential to impact various applications, such as online advertising, finance, and healthcare.

**Quality and Technical Soundness**

The paper's technical soundness is good, with a thorough regret analysis and a lower bound. However, the notation is confusing, and the writing needs improvement. Additionally, the reviewer raises concerns about the regret analysis, which may not accurately capture the costs of low-fidelity sampling.

**Clarity and Organization**

The paper's clarity and organization are compromised by the confusing notation and unwieldy writing. The authors could improve the paper's readability by streamlining the notation, reorganizing the sections, and providing clearer explanations of the technical concepts.
