- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- Well-motivated problem: precise measurements are costly while cheaper approximations exist.  
- Core idea: use lower-fidelity information to efficiently eliminate suboptimal arms.  
- Paper clearly presents formulation, algorithm, and theoretical analysis in the main text.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with upper and lower cumulative pseudo-regret bounds showing near-optimality.  
- Analysis is sound and connects clearly to classical UCB algorithms.  
- Writing in the main paper is clear and accessible to bandit researchers.  
- Major weakness: supplementary proofs contain numerous typos and errors that hinder reproducibility and understanding.  
- Examples of errors noted (e.g., typos in equations and notation) indicate need for thorough proofreading and correction.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading since the constant depends on algorithm parameters like ρ.  
- Recommend presenting exact inequalities to improve clarity and precision.  
- Practical applicability is unclear because MF-UCB requires accurate estimation of fidelity ζ, which may be difficult in practice.  
- Paper lacks discussion of sensitivity to ζ estimation errors and of adaptive methods to learn ζ online.  
- Simulations show gains but are limited and don’t fully explore behavior in complex scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, practical significance, and clear main text.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor-quality supplementary materials, ambiguous notation, and insufficient treatment of practical challenges.  
- Overall assessment: substantial theoretical contribution but acceptance should be conditional on major revisions to proofs, notation clarity, and discussion of practical issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions, prioritizing correction of supplementary materials and addressing practical concerns.
