- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls where pull cost increases with accuracy.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that adapts to sequences of available approximations and costs.  
- Provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Theoretical analysis and regret proofs are solid and thorough.  
- Paper is well-written and clear; algorithm and motivation are easy to follow.  
- Supplementary proofs contain many typos and small errors that hinder readability.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters.  
- Practical applicability is unclear—MF-UCB may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity ζ.  
- Needs discussion of practical limitations, mitigations, and guidance for choosing algorithm parameters.  
- Results have potential applications in online advertising, finance, and other bandit domains.  
- Overall contribution is significant and novel.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing typos, presenting exact inequalities, and adding practical guidance.
