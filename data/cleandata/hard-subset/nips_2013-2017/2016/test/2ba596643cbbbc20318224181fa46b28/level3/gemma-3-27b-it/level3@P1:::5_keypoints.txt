- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit: use cheaper, noisier reward approximations to speed learning and reduce cost.  
- Motivated by practical settings (online advertising, algorithm selection) where low-fidelity signals exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB tailored to multi-fidelity observations.  
- Defines a cost-weighted notion of regret appropriate for multi-fidelity decisions.  
- Proves an upper regret bound for MF-UCB (Theorem 2) showing advantages over naive strategies.  
- Provides a lower bound (Theorem 4) establishing near-optimality of the approach.  
- Introduces K(m) partitions to categorize arms across fidelities, aiding analysis and intuition.  
- Theoretical analysis carefully accounts for approximation biases and fidelity trade-offs.  
- Paper is clearly written with consistent notation and accessible presentation.  
- Simulation results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in tested scenarios.  
- Discusses limitations and future work (misleading low fidelities, alternative regret definitions).  
- Concerns about reliance on assumptions (e.g., concentration inequalities, Assumption 1) that may not hold broadly.  
- Simulations are limited in scope and could include more benchmarks and baselines.  
- Noted gap between upper and lower bounds for arms within certain K(m) sets needs further study.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
