- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model where each arm has multiple fidelities with different costs and biases.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs.  
- Provides regret analysis showing MF-UCB outperforms naive strategies that ignore approximations.  
- Presents a lower bound and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Main contribution is the new problem formulation, a significant departure from classical K-armed bandits.  
- Modeling cheaper approximations via fidelity-dependent bias is a valuable and novel perspective.  
- MF-UCB is a natural, well-analyzed extension of the classical UCB algorithm.  
- Writing and organization are clear and concise, facilitating reproducibility.  
- Empirical simulations demonstrate a reported 3–4x improvement of MF-UCB over UCB.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret, limiting clarity and impact.  
- Questionable modeling choice: low fidelity treated as increased bias rather than increased variance.  
- Some motivating examples (e.g., short ad displays) are unconvincing and the regret objective is unclear for certain applications.  
- Link between the formal model and motivating applications is weak but not fatal.  
- Simulation parameter choices (Appendix C) lack justification and may affect reported gains.  
- Technically sound and complete work, but would benefit from more extensive experiments and algorithmic variations.  
- Originality is high due to the new formulation; significance is moderate and somewhat application-specific.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address interpretation of bounds, fidelity modeling, simulation justification, and expanded experiments.
