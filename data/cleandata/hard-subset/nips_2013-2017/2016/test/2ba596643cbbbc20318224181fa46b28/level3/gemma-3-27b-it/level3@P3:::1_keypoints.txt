- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem with varying costs/accuracies and proposes MF-UCB with regret bound, lower bound, and empirical gains over standard UCB.  
- Problem setup is relevant and models practical cost-information-reward trade-offs.  
- Technical analysis is strong: provides regret upper bound, lower bound, and conditions where MF-UCB outperforms UCB.  
- Paper is complete: offers algorithm, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Explanations lack clarity, making results and assumptions (e.g., the K^(m) sets, dependence on Δ) hard to interpret.  
- Regret formulation uses multiplicative cost penalization, which feels unnatural; an additive cost penalty should be considered/discussed.  
- Motivation is limited to an ad-display example and should include broader real-world scenarios.  
- Assumption 1 (constraint on ζ_m) appears artificial and needs stronger justification or discussion of violations.  
- No guidance on robustness if ζ_m do not decay as required; need methods to select a suitable fidelity subsequence.  
- Algorithm design seems highly tailored to a specific ζ configuration; unclear if benefits persist under weaker assumptions.  
- Gap between upper and lower bounds indicates potential for further optimization and should be acknowledged.  
- Minor editorial issues: clarify "near-optimal", fix punctuation, and use correct set notation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practically relevant problem; rigorous analysis; empirical support; expands bandit algorithms’ scope.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, unusual regret choice, questionable assumption, and upper-lower bound gap.  
- Overall recommendation: reject with major revisions—address clarity, justify regret formulation and Assumption 1, investigate lower-bound gap, and improve robustness discussion.
