- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework and the MF-UCB algorithm for better exploration-exploitation under differing sampling costs.  
- Provides regret analysis and a lower bound that claim optimality of MF-UCB.  
- Synthetic experiments show MF-UCB outperforms traditional UCB algorithms.  
- Novelty: the multi-fidelity framework and algorithm are a significant and original contribution with potential application impact.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis including a lower bound.  
- Strength: promising empirical results on synthetic benchmarks.  
- Weakness: confusing, inconsistent, and dispersed notation harms readability.  
- Weakness: misleading advertising example and irrelevant references to display time correlations and non-stationarity.  
- Weakness: regret analysis may be flawed by treating the best arm as highest mean regardless of cost, possibly hiding low-fidelity sampling costs.  
- Weakness: writing is unwieldy and the organization needs streamlining.  
- Technical soundness is generally good but tempered by concerns about the regret formulation and presentation clarity.  
- Clarity and organization require improved notation, clearer explanations, and reorganized sections.  
- For acceptance: novel framework, solid analysis, and promising experimental performance.  
- Against acceptance: notation/readability issues, misleading examples, questionable regret accounting, and poor writing.  
- Recommended improvements: fix and unify notation, remove misleading examples, revise regret analysis to account for low-fidelity costs, streamline writing, and consider adding a cost/sample-complexity analysis for best-arm identification.
