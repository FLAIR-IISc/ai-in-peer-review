- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity K-armed bandit variant where cheap approximations for arms are available.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of the UCB strategy to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical results including regret bounds for MF-UCB.  
- Offers a lower-bound analysis showing theoretical improvement from the multi-fidelity approach.  
- Presents simulations demonstrating the benefits and behavior of MF-UCB.  
- Situates the work relative to prior bandit and multi-fidelity optimization literature, arguing a distinct focus on exploration–exploitation.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem presentation and novel algorithm design.  
- Strength: strong technical soundness and well-organized, readable exposition.  
- Weakness: key assumptions (availability of cheap approximations and reward-structure) may not hold in many practical settings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical comparison with existing K-armed bandit algorithms.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to synthetic problems; real-world applications or datasets are missing.  
- Suggestion: paper should discuss practical limitations, challenges, and directions for future work more thoroughly.  
- Evaluation: scores well on quality, clarity, and originality but significance could be improved with applied results.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel problem, solid theory, and informative simulations support contribution.  
- Con-acceptance: practical applicability and comparative evaluation shortcomings weaken impact.
