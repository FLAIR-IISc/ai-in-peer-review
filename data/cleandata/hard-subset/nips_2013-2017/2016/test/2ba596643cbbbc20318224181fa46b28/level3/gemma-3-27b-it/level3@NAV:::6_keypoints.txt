- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where reward estimates at different fidelities have different costs, motivated by practical scenarios like online advertising.  
- Proposes a new regret definition tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Presents MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound based algorithm that leverages cheaper noisy approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including regret upper bounds and a matching lower bound, indicating rigorous bandit-theoretic treatment.  
- Simulation results empirically support MF-UCB’s effectiveness but are limited in diversity and scope.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a comprehensive related work section.  
- Core contribution: demonstrating how cheaper fidelities can be used to focus resources on promising arms.  
- Major concern: the analysis assumes known ζ^(m) (deviation of mth-fidelity mean), which may be impractical in real applications.  
- Suggestion: relax the ζ^(m) assumption or introduce adaptive estimation techniques to improve practicality and impact.  
- Theoretical exposition could benefit from more intuitive explanations of how the algorithm adapts to varying costs and fidelities.  
- Multiple minor typographical and notation errors are present (specific line/item corrections listed by reviewer).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem and solution, strong theoretical foundation, clear presentation, and practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumption of known ζ^(m) limiting applicability and presence of minor typos.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the ζ^(m) assumption and correct typographical errors.
