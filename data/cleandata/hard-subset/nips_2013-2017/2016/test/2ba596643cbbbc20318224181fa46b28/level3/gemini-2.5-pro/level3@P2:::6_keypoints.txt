- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms playable at M fidelities where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased/noisy estimates of the true high-fidelity reward.  
- Strong practical motivation with examples like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Main contributions: formal problem definition with cost-aware fixed-budget regret.  
- Main contributions: proposes MF-UCB, extending UCB to exploit cheap low-fidelity evaluations to prune arms.  
- Main contributions: rigorous theoretical analysis including an upper bound for MF-UCB and a near-matching lower bound, plus supporting simulations.  
- Quality: high technical quality—algorithm is intuitive and regret analysis is comprehensive and insightful.  
- Primary weakness: analysis assumes known bias bounds ζ(m) for all fidelities, which is unrealistic and not addressed for misspecification or online learning.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with clear formulation and interpretation; supplementary material contains minor errors/typos.  
- Originality: highly original—first formalization and regret analysis of multi-fidelity stochastic bandits.  
- Significance: important and impactful for areas like hyperparameter tuning and A/B testing, though tempered by the ζ(m) assumption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, elegant algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and promising new research direction.  
- Arguments for rejection: major practical limitation due to assuming known ζ(m) without discussion, relaxation, or sensitivity analysis.  
- Main suggestion: discuss/relax ζ(m) assumption, test robustness to misspecification, or outline methods to learn ζ(m) online.  
- Additional suggestion: carefully proofread and correct supplementary material (several specific line-by-line errors noted).  
- Overall verdict: strong, near-optimal contribution and clear candidate for acceptance if the ζ(m) assumption and supplement errors are addressed.
