This paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the stochastic K-armed bandit problem where cheaper approximations to the desired outcome are available. The authors formalize this task as a multi-fidelity bandit problem, where at each time step, the forecaster may choose to play an arm at any one of M fidelities. The highest fidelity (desired outcome) expends cost λ, while the m fidelity (an approximation) expends λ < λ and returns a biased estimate of the highest fidelity.

The authors develop MF-UCB, a novel upper confidence bound procedure for this setting, and prove that it naturally adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs, thus attaining better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations. The theoretical results, including regret bounds for the proposed method, demonstrate that MF-UCB outperforms traditional UCB algorithms. Additionally, the authors provide a lower-bound analysis showing that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper is well-written and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the multi-fidelity bandit problem and the proposed MF-UCB algorithm. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance, including regret bounds and a lower-bound analysis. The simulations presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of MF-UCB in practice, showing that it outperforms traditional UCB algorithms in various scenarios.
