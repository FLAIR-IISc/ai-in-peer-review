- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm has M fidelities with increasing accuracy and cost.  
- Proposes a new cost-aware regret definition that accounts for differing fidelity costs.  
- Presents MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm that uses low-fidelity observations to eliminate suboptimal arms before expensive high-fidelity pulls.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: an upper bound on regret and a matching lower bound.  
- Simulation results demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB by strategic fidelity allocation.  
- Well-motivated by practical applications (e.g., online advertising) where low-fidelity proxies are available.  
- Theoretical contributions are technically sound and novel within bandit literature.  
- Major concern: assumption that ε^(m) (deviation between fidelity means) is known — no discussion or method for estimating or relaxing this in practice.  
- This unknown-ε^(m) assumption significantly limits practical applicability and should be addressed or discussed.  
- Paper contains minor notational/indexing/typographical errors that need correction.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to handle the ε^(m) issue and fix minor errors.
