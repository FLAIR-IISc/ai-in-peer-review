- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit formulation where lower fidelities are cheaper but induce bounded bias.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Main contribution is the new problem formulation, a significant departure from traditional bandit models.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and regret bounds for MF-UCB.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret, making performance implications unclear.  
- Simulation results show MF-UCB can outperform highest-fidelity UCB (≈3–4x) in some scenarios.  
- Simulation parameter choices lack justification; reported gains may reflect chosen parameters rather than typical performance.  
- Modeling low fidelity as increased bias (rather than increased variance) is questioned and may not fit all applications.  
- Link between the model and motivating applications is weak; some examples (e.g., short ad displays) are unconvincing.  
- Algorithmic ideas use standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty; novelty is in applying them to multi-fidelity settings.  
- Paper is technically sound but clarity and notation need improvement, and regret presentation could be clearer.  
- Originality and potential significance are high, with possible impact on advertising, finance, and robotics.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, contingent on addressing modeling, application linkage, and clarity concerns.
