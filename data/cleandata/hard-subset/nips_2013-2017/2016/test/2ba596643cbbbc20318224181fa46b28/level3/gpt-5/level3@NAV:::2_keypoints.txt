- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with M fidelities, cost-weighted pseudo-regret, and proposes MF-UCB that adaptively selects arm and fidelity, with upper bounds and near-optimal lower bounds.  
- Setting is distinct from budgeted bandits and connects to weak/strong labeler and multi-fidelity optimization work, offering a new bandit-theoretic formalization.  
- Theory is interesting and largely sound, with a clear decomposition explaining when low fidelities help and well-motivated upper/lower bounds.  
- Authors acknowledge a gap for certain K^(m) cases; overall near-optimality holds up to multiplicative constants under mild conditions.  
- Main weakness: supplementary proofs contain many typos that impede verification and need substantial proofreading.  
- Concern about pervasive “up-to-constant” notation: hidden constants include algorithm parameters (e.g., ρ); reviewers ask for explicit inequalities carrying ρ, ζ^(m), and λ^(m) through.  
- Clarity: main paper is clear and well organized; supplement requires substantial cleanup to improve readability.  
- Originality: high — the variable-fidelity pull formulation, cost-weighted regret, and MF-UCB design are novel.  
- Significance: theoretically significant and likely influential for resource-aware exploration; practical impact unclear because MF-UCB assumes known fidelity biases ζ^(m).  
- Requests for authors: discuss robustness to ζ misspecification, propose/experiment with procedures to estimate or adapt ζ online (cross-fidelity calibration, conservative γ^(m) inflation, joint estimation-and-control), and include sensitivity analyses.  
- Pros for acceptance: new, well-motivated problem; near-optimal analysis; clear main presentation and insightful partitioning argument.  
- Cons against acceptance: unclear practical applicability (no robustness study for ζ), heavy reliance on “up-to-constant” statements hiding algorithmic parameters, and numerous appendix typos.  
- Numerous specific typos/errors in the supplement cited (non-exhaustive list: p10 eq.(8) k/K, Sec A1 H^(m)→K^(m), p10 l.328 ν/(ρ−2)→ν/2, p11 variable/symbol mistakes, multiple equation/labeling mistakes through Secs A–C).
