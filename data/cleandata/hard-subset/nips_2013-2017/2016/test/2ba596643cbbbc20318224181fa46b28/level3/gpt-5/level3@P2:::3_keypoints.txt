- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with M fidelities, fidelity-specific costs λ(m), known bias bounds ζ(m) (decreasing with m), and objective to minimize cost-weighted pseudo-regret under budget Λ.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-fidelity UCBs widened by bias bounds, with active arm upper bound = min over fidelities and a fidelity-escalation rule using thresholds γ(m).  
- Theory: upper bound shows MF-UCB can outperform naive high-fidelity UCB when lower fidelities prune many arms; lower bound shows near-optimality except for “borderline” arms.  
- Empirics: synthetic simulations report several-fold improvement over high-fidelity UCB and match the theory qualitatively.  
- Strength: novel, timely formalization bridging bandits and multi-fidelity evaluation with per-fidelity costs and bias bounds.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, intuitive algorithm adapting UCB with fidelity-aware confidence sets and escalation criterion.  
- Strength: nontrivial analysis decomposing regret by fidelity-based arm partitions and providing a meaningful lower bound.  
- Strength: sound intuition and empirical behavior that cheap fidelities are used to prune clearly suboptimal arms.  
- Weakness: strong modeling assumption of known, uniformly valid bias bounds ζ(m); no robustness study to misspecification.  
- Weakness: pseudo-regret definition (scoring highest-fidelity reward always) may be inappropriate in some motivating applications and is discussed only briefly.  
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty is limited (direct UCB adaptation); main contributions are problem setup and analysis.  
- Weakness: upper-bound expressions are intricate and lack interpretable corollaries or simple special-case results.  
- Weakness: theoretical gains depend on Assumption 1 (rapid ζ decay) and known costs; scope when relaxed is unclear.  
- Weakness: simulations lack justification for parameter choices (ζ, λ, noise) and limited sensitivity analyses; reported gains may rely on favorable settings.  
- Suggestions: provide sensitivity analyses or algorithms that learn/upper-bound ζ online and evaluate misspecification robustness.  
- Suggestions: expand experiments and discussion of alternative regret notions, include interpretable special cases (e.g., M=2, sub-Gaussian), justify simulation parameters, and add additional baselines.  
- Suggestions: clarify when Assumption 1 holds, show empirical behavior when it does not, and discuss extensions to unknown costs or adaptive budgets.  
- Pros summary: novel problem, simple algorithm with solid analysis, informative lower bound, supporting empirical results.  
- Cons summary: strong assumptions (known bias), hard-to-interpret bounds, modest algorithmic novelty, and limited experimental justification.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
