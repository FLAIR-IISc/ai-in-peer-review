This paper introduces a compelling multi-fidelity extension to the classic K-armed bandit problem, motivated by practical scenarios where obtaining precise rewards is costly, but cheaper approximations are available. The authors formalize this as a multi-fidelity bandit and propose MF-UCB, a novel algorithm building on the UCB framework. The theoretical analysis, including regret bounds and a lower bound, is rigorous and demonstrates the potential benefits of exploiting fidelity variations. Empirical results further support these findings.

The work builds naturally on the extensive literature surrounding K-armed bandits [9, 11, 12] and UCB algorithms [2, 3, 5]. It differentiates itself by acknowledging the inherent cost structure and approximation possibilities present in many real-world applications. While multi-fidelity optimization has been explored in other domains [7, 10, 6], this paper is the first to rigorously analyze it within the exploration-exploitation framework of bandits, specifically addressing the regret minimization problem. Related work on budgeted bandits [13, 15] and active learning [16] are correctly positioned as tackling different problems, despite some surface-level similarities. The recent work by Kandasamy et al. [8] is also appropriately cited, demonstrating awareness of concurrent related research.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Significance:** The multi-fidelity bandit formulation is a significant contribution, broadening the applicability of bandit algorithms to a wider range of practical problems. The potential for cost savings by intelligently utilizing cheaper approximations is substantial.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The regret analysis, including the lower bound, is well-developed and provides strong theoretical justification for the proposed approach. The proof sketches are helpful in understanding the key ideas.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem is clearly defined, the algorithm is presented logically, and the analysis is accessible. The notation is consistent and well-explained.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The simulations provide compelling evidence that MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in the proposed setting. The visualization of arm plays across fidelities offers valuable insights.
* **Honest Discussion of Limitations:** The authors acknowledge potential tightness of the lower bound and discuss the assumptions made, contributing to the paper's credibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumption 1:** While the authors state Assumption 1 isn't *necessary*, its impact on performance isn’t fully explored. A discussion of performance degradation when this assumption is violated would be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Simulations:** While the presented simulations are convincing, exploring a wider range of problem instances, particularly with varying cost ratios and fidelity accuracies, would strengthen the empirical validation.
* **Regret Definition:** The chosen regret definition, focusing on the highest fidelity reward, is justified but might not be optimal for all applications. The authors briefly mention this, but a more in-depth discussion of alternative regret metrics could be included.
* **Computational Complexity:** The algorithm's complexity isn't explicitly discussed. A brief analysis of its computational cost relative to standard UCB would be helpful.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound solution to a relevant problem.
* The writing quality is excellent, making the work accessible to a broad audience.
* The empirical results support the theoretical findings.
* The authors acknowledge limitations and potential avenues for future work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  The impact of violating Assumption 1 is not fully explored.
*  The simulations, while strong, could benefit from broader scope.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of bandit algorithms. The problem formulation is insightful, the proposed algorithm is well-motivated and analyzed, and the presentation is exemplary. The minor weaknesses identified do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I strongly recommend acceptance.



**Quality:** Excellent. The paper is technically sound, rigorously analyzed, and presents a complete piece of work.
**Clarity:** Excellent. The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized.
**Originality:** High. The multi-fidelity bandit formulation is novel and represents a significant extension of existing work.
**Significance:** High. The results have the potential to be impactful in a variety of applications where obtaining precise rewards is costly.
