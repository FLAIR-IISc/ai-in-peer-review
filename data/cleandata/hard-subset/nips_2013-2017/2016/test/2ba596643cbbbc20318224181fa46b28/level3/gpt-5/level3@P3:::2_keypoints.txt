- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with biased low-fidelity observations, cost λ(m), bias ζ(m), and a cost-weighted pseudo-regret for budgeted evaluation.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: fidelity-specific UCBs that cheaply eliminate bad arms using low fidelities and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Proves near-optimal upper bounds on cost-weighted pseudo-regret and a matching lower bound (up to constants) for a broad arm subset.  
- Novelty: formalizes biased low-fidelity observations with cost-aware regret, fidelity-switching rules, partition K(m) based on ζ(m)/γ(m), and Λ-budget analysis.  
- Relation to prior work: extends classical UCB, budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity GP optimization, and weak/strong labeler active learning by handling biased, costed fidelities.  
- Technical soundness: main results are well-motivated and largely correct; proof ideas adapt standard techniques to the biased, costed setting.  
- Identified gap: arms in a particular partition (K(m)7) are not fully resolved and are discussed as an open problem.  
- Presentation: main paper is clear, but supplementary proofs contain many typos and small errors that hinder verification.  
- Notation/clariy issue: pervasive “up-to-constant” shorthand hides algorithm- and problem-dependent constants (ρ, ν, ψ) that affect γ(m) and fidelity switching; exact inequalities are needed.  
- Practicality concern: algorithm assumes known ζ(m); paper should discuss estimation, robustness to mis-specification, and provide sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggested practical fixes: use conservative ζ̂(m) bounds, adaptively calibrate γ(m) via pilot samples, and add robustness experiments for ζ(m) and λ(m).  
- Experiments: synthetic experiments illustrate mechanisms but stronger empirical validation and ablations (ζ, λ, ρ) or real-world/simulator-to-real cases are recommended.  
- Assumptions: request clarification on practical reasonableness of Assumption 1 (ζ decay) and Assumption 3 for the lower bound; provide ψ examples for non-sub-Gaussian tails.  
- Notation fixes: clarify where ρ>4 vs ρ>2 is required and make dependence on ρ, ν, ψ explicit in theorems/lemmas.  
- Numerous specific typos and errors listed in the appendix (equations, variable names, inequality directions, missing symbols) that must be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: strong, timely theoretical contribution with near-optimal guarantees; recommend acceptance conditional on fixing appendix issues, clarifying constants, and addressing ζ(m) estimation/robustness.
