- Paper formulates multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where each arm has M fidelities with increasing costs and known bias bounds ζ(m) to the highest-fidelity mean.  
- Defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret using highest-fidelity means as rewards while allowing plays at cheaper, biased fidelities.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: bias-corrected per-fidelity UCBs, arm selection by optimistic bound, and fidelity escalation using threshold γ(m)=ψ^{-1}((λ(m)/λ(m+1))ψ(ζ(m))).  
- Theoretical upper bound shows cost-weighted regret ∑_k Δ_k^(M)·λ(J_k)/ψ(Δ_k^(J_k)) (up to logs), exploiting cheap fidelities to prune suboptimal arms.  
- Provides a Bernoulli lower bound showing MF-UCB is near-optimal except for a gap on a subset K(m)_7 where lower fidelities can be slightly misleading.  
- Synthetic experiments validate that MF-UCB uses low fidelities to prune arms and attains lower regret than standard high-fidelity UCB.  
- Relates to classical UCB, budgeted bandits, and multi-fidelity optimization/RL literature, complementing GP-based multi-fidelity bandits by focusing on finite arms and regret.  
- Technical soundness: algorithm design and regret analysis are well motivated and carefully account for fidelity-specific costs via the K(m) partition.  
- Key assumptions include known bias bounds ζ(m), known concentration function ψ, and a ζ-decay regularity (Assumption 1); authors note when Assumption 1 can be relaxed.  
- Empirics are consistent with theory but limited to synthetic data and a single baseline (highest-fidelity UCB); more baselines and sensitivity analyses are needed.  
- Limitations: regret definition is one of several choices, lower bound restricted to Bernoulli with specific parameter ranges, and robustness to misspecified ζ(m)/ψ is unexplored.  
- Clarity: paper is well written with precise formulation and intuition; suggests earlier example for γ(m), a sub-Gaussian simplification of bounds, and expansion on residual term and anytime behavior.  
- Originality: first regret-minimization treatment of per-arm multi-fidelity biased/costed evaluations with a novel bias-aware UCB and fidelity-selection rule.  
- Significance: important for practical settings with cheap biased proxies (e.g., short-run metrics, subsampling), and likely to inspire work on learning ζ(m), richer models, and broader validation.  
- Pros: clear formulation, simple principled algorithm, strong theoretical upper/lower bounds, and simulations aligning with theory.  
- Cons: requires known ζ(m)/ψ, limited empirical breadth, unresolved near-optimality gap for K(m)_7, reliance on Assumption 1 for clean comparisons, and limited discussion of alternative regret notions.  
- Key questions/suggestions: can γ(m) be adapted online; sensitivity to misspecified ζ(m); comparisons to hierarchical racing baselines; provide sub-Gaussian specializations and quantify when MF-UCB matches UCB; characterize or mitigate K(m)_7.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance—solid, novel, strong theory despite empirical and assumption-related limitations.  
- Reviewer confidence: high.
