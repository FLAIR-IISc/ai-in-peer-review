- Proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework and the MF-UCB algorithm leveraging cheaper approximations to speed learning.  
- Models discrete fidelity levels per arm with known fidelity errors and costs; lower fidelities give biased estimates of the highest fidelity.  
- MF-UCB adapts UCB by maintaining arm-fidelity upper confidence bounds and choosing fidelity based on uncertainty and cost.  
- Proves a regret bound O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) where Kg is a small set of arms identifiable via low fidelities.  
- Provides a lower bound via change-of-measure and arm partitioning, claiming near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems.  
- Strength: Tackles a relevant trade-off between information, cost, and reward with technically solid analysis.  
- Weakness: Explanations of results and assumptions are inadequate and make interpretation difficult.  
- Weakness: Regret penalizes cost multiplicatively; additive cost penalization is not considered and should be discussed.  
- Weakness: Limited motivation (mainly an ad-display example); broader real-world motivations needed.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (decay of fidelity errors) appears artificial and opaque and requires clarification/justification.  
- Weakness: Algorithm may be tailored to specific fidelity configurations; unclear how crucial Assumption 1 is to performance.  
- Weakness: Gap in the lower bound suggests the strategy or analysis may need redesign; further investigation required.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 and the comparison with UCB are hard to parse; clearer, more explicit explanations needed.  
- Minor: Uses "near-optimal" without a clear definition.  
- Minor: Typo/punctuation—unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (line 126).  
- Minor: Notation suggestion—use curly braces for set notation (line 179).  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel framework, provided regret and lower bounds, and empirical gains over UCB.  
- Argument against acceptance: Poor explanation, questionable cost model, limited motivation, and opaque key assumptions.  
- Overall: Interesting and technically solid contribution that needs clearer exposition, stronger motivation, and justification of assumptions.
