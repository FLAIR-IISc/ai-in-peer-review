- Introduces the multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple fidelities with different costs and accuracies.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides regret upper bound showing improvement over naive single-fidelity strategies.  
- Provides a lower bound and argues MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear and detailed derivations.  
- Lacks intuitive explanation of results, especially the regret formulation and assumptions.  
- Penalizing cost multiplicatively in the regret is questioned; additive cost penalization should be considered.  
- Motivation for the specific reward/cost model is narrow (ad-display example) and may not generalize.  
- Assumption 1 (fast decay of fidelity errors zeta_m) is artificial/opaque and its general validity is unclear.  
- Authors should discuss robustness if zeta_m do not decay and provide guidance for selecting a satisfying subsequence.  
- Algorithm design appears tailored to specific zeta configurations; unclear how critical the assumption is to empirical performance.  
- Gap in the lower bound needs further investigation and might imply strategy redesign.  
- Theorem 2 and UCB comparison are hard to parse; dependence on Δ and complex sets K^(m) needs clearer, explicit explanation.  
- Overall, the paper presents an interesting, relevant trade-off and a valuable foundation for further work.  
- Concise arguments for acceptance: novel framework, thorough analysis, and technical rigor.  
- Concise arguments against acceptance: poor interpretability of results/assumptions, questionable cost model and fidelity-decay assumption, and potential overfitting of algorithm to specific fidelity structures.
