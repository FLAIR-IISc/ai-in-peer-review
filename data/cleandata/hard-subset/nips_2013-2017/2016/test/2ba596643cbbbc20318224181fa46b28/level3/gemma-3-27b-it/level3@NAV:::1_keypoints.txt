- Introduces multi-fidelity bandit problem and MF-UCB algorithm with regret bound, lower bound, and empirical improvements over high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Problem is relevant for applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Technical analysis (regret and lower bound) appears generally solid and builds on existing bandit literature.  
- Significant clarity issues impede full comprehension and assessment of the paper.  
- Justification for modeling choices is weak, particularly the multiplicative cost penalization in the regret formulation; additive cost should be discussed.  
- Ad-display example motivation is limited and does not convincingly demonstrate broader applicability of the reward/cost model.  
- Assumption 1 constraining fidelity errors (zeta_m) is problematic: its necessity and general validity are unclear and unexplored.  
- Paper does not address handling violations of Assumption 1 (e.g., selecting suitable subsequences of fidelities).  
- Algorithm design appears heavily tailored to the specific zeta configuration, raising robustness concerns if fidelities do not decay as assumed.  
- Gap between the derived lower bound and MF-UCB performance suggests potential room for improvement and needs further investigation.  
- Presentation of Theorem 2 and comparison with UCB is hard to parse; dependence on Delta and sets K^(m) requires clearer explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated, impactful problem and largely correct technical contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear assumptions and modeling, restrictive Assumption 1, and lower bound gap limit practical applicability and optimality.  
- Overall: technically sound but significant clarity and assumption issues warrant major revisions before acceptance.  
- Minor issues: wording ("near-optimal"), punctuation error (extra period), and set notation formatting.
