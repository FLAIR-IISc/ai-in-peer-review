- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where lower fidelities give cheaper but biased approximations of high-fidelity rewards and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm.  
- Formalizes regret in the multi-fidelity context and provides thorough theoretical analysis with regret upper bounds and a matching lower bound.  
- Extends classical bandit literature by incorporating a cost structure across fidelity levels, relevant to applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- MF-UCB is a natural UCB extension and is shown to be nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, supporting the theory.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, logical organization, and helpful figures illustrating key concepts.  
- Major weakness: assumption that ζ^(m) (bias of each fidelity) is known a priori is unrealistic and not addressed.  
- The paper does not discuss how to estimate ζ^(m) in practice or analyze the impact of estimation error on performance and guarantees.  
- Minor weakness: several typographical and notation errors that hurt readability.  
- Pro arguments: novel problem formulation, strong theoretical analysis, empirical support, and practical relevance.  
- Con arguments: problematic ζ^(m) assumption, lack of methods/experiments for estimating ζ^(m), and limited empirical validation (only synthetic data).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address ζ^(m) estimation and fix typos to strengthen practical relevance.
