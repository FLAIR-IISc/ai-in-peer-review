- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit formulation and the MF-UCB algorithm that leverages cheaper, biased lower-fidelity observations.
- MF-UCB is a novel adaptation of UCB to exploit fidelity-specific costs/biases for cheaper exploration.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with regret upper bounds showing improvement over naive UCB.
- Includes a lower bound, arguing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.
- Empirical simulations validate MF-UCB’s practical benefits and align with theoretical predictions.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful examples and arm partitioning by fidelity.
- Analysis depends on specific assumptions (e.g., decay of fidelity biases ζ(m) and Assumption 1), limiting generality.
- Focuses on a regret notion that assumes all fidelities yield meaningful rewards, which may not hold in some practical settings.
- There is a gap between the MF-UCB upper bound and the lower bound for certain arms (e.g., in K(m)7), left as an open problem.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a practical, underexplored problem with a novel algorithm, strong theory (upper/lower bounds), and supportive simulations.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, unresolved bound-tightness for some arms, and limited treatment of fidelity-specific reward settings.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to address limitations and explore broader practical extensions in future work.
