- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) model where cheap, biased approximations supplement expensive high-fidelity observations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that leverages lower fidelities to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms and focus costly evaluations on promising ones.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and a lower bound showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Clear motivation and real-world connections (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- Comprehensive related work situating the contribution relative to bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, and budgeted bandits.  
- Strength: Novel and practically relevant problem formulation capturing fidelity trade-offs.  
- Strength: MF-UCB is a natural, well-motivated extension of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Strength: Theoretical analysis adds rigor despite complex bounds.  
- Strength: Simulations show MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB in tested scenarios.  
- Weakness: Modeling lower fidelities as biased (rather than higher-variance) is questionable and may not fit many applications.  
- Weakness: The distinction between maximizing short-run outcomes vs. long-run rates is not convincingly bridged.  
- Weakness: Regret bound is complex and hard to interpret; needs simplification or intuition.  
- Weakness: Simulation details (especially ζ^(m) choices) lack justification and results may be parameter-sensitive.  
- Weakness: Core algorithmic idea is incremental—primarily adapts existing optimism/UCB principles.  
- Overall assessment: Solid paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses but requires better justification of modeling and more robust empirical evidence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, theoretically analyzed algorithm, empirical gains, and contribution to exploration–exploitation understanding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable bias modeling, opaque regret bounds, weak simulation justification, and limited novelty.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: discuss bias vs. variance modeling trade-off, simplify or interpret regret bound, justify and broaden simulation parameters, and clarify practical regimes where MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
