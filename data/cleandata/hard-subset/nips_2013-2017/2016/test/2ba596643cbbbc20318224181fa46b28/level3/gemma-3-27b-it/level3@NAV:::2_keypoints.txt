- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where higher-fidelity pulls incur greater cost.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that allocates higher-fidelity resources to promising arms.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees with upper and lower regret bounds and shows near-optimality.  
- Problem setting is well-motivated and relevant to applications like online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Paper is well-written, with a clear presentation and effective introduction.  
- Notation using "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" obscures dependence on parameters (e.g., ρ); explicit inequalities are needed.  
- Practical applicability is limited by reliance on accurately estimating fidelity ζ; discussion of limitations and adaptive estimation strategies is required.  
- Supplementary material (proofs) contains numerous typos and inconsistencies (e.g., eq.(8) k/K typo, Section A1 H^(m) vs K^(m)) that hinder readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel setting, solid theoretical foundation, clear presentation, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved practical fidelity-estimation issues, appendix errors, and unclear constant notation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on fixing appendix errors and clarifying practical considerations.
