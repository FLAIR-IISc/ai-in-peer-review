- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework where each arm has multiple-cost, biased approximations, motivated by applications like online advertising.  
- Formalizes the problem and develops the MF-UCB algorithm to exploit cheaper, biased fidelities.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis including an upper-regret bound O((λ(1)K + λ(2)|Kg|) log(Λ/λ(2))) and a matching lower bound.  
- Explicitly accounts for bias from lower fidelities in algorithm design and analysis.  
- Shows MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB by leveraging multi-fidelity information.  
- Lower-bound analysis shows near-optimality, with a gap only for arms in a specific set K(m)7 due to the switching criterion.  
- Simulations on synthetic problems corroborate theory, showing consistent improvements and predicted fidelity allocation.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-motivated formulation, consistent notation, precise algorithm description, and accessible theoretical discussion (Figure 1 helps).  
- Originality is strong: conceptual advance over classical bandits and clear differentiation from prior multi-fidelity and active learning work.  
- Significance is high: practical impact for domains with expensive evaluations (advertising, algorithm selection, experimental design).  
- Main weakness: the unexplained performance gap for arms in K(m)7 (open problem) and limited focus to the stochastic setting.  
- Overall verdict: technically rigorous, well-executed, and a significant contribution to bandit literature.
