- Introduces a new variable-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where pull cost increases with accuracy.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, adapting UCB to use low fidelities to eliminate arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis with upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret and near-optimality under conditions.  
- Shows MF-UCB outperforms naive strategies that ignore fidelity approximations.  
- Strength: novel, relevant problem setting with practical real-world applications.  
- Strength: a tailored, new algorithm (MF-UCB) for the setting.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical results and regret analysis.  
- Weakness: supplementary proofs contain numerous typos and small errors that reduce readability.  
- Weakness: use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is misleading because hidden constants are algorithm parameters; exact inequalities are preferred.  
- Weakness: practical applicability is unclear; paper should discuss limitations and mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: needs more empirical comparisons to other algorithms and techniques.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to fix proofs, clarify notation, discuss practicality, and add comparisons.
