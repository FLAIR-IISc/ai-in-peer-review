This paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where cheaper approximations to the desired outcome are available. The authors formalize this setting as a multi-fidelity (MF) bandit problem and propose a new algorithm, MF-UCB, based on upper confidence bound techniques. The algorithm uses cheap lower-fidelity plays to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity queries for a small set of arms with high expected reward, achieving better regret than naive strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information.

The paper provides theoretical results, including regret bounds for the proposed method, and offers a lower-bound analysis showing the theoretical improvement of the approach. The authors also present simulations that demonstrate the effect and benefits of the MF bandit method. The writing and presentation are clear and easy to understand.

The main contributions of this paper are:

1. A novel formalism for studying bandit tasks when one has access to multiple fidelities for each arm, with each successive fidelity providing a better approximation to the most expensive one.
2. A new algorithm, MF-UCB, that adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategies to the multi-fidelity setting.
3. A theoretical characterization of the performance of MF-UCB, showing that the algorithm uses the lower fidelities to explore all arms and eliminates arms with low expected reward, and reserves the higher fidelity plays for arms with rewards close to the optimal value.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithm, and the theoretical results. The simulations presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the MF bandit method and provide insights into its behavior.
