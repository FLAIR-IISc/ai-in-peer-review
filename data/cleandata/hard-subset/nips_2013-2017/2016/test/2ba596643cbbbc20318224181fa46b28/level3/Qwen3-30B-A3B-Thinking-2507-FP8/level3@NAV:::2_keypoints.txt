- Introduces a novel multi-armed bandit framework where each arm can be played at multiple fidelities with higher fidelities being more accurate but more expensive.  
- Formalizes a cost-weighted regret metric defining regret as cost × pseudo-regret.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that adaptively selects fidelities to minimize cost-weighted regret.  
- Provides strong theoretical contributions with tight upper and lower bounds showing MF-UCB's near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Builds on UCB methods but addresses substantial new challenges unique to the multi-fidelity setting, distinct from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization.  
- Main paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured, making complex concepts accessible.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with solid proofs supporting the regret bounds.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated by practical examples (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) where cheaper approximations exist.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and errors in equations that could confuse readers and undermine confidence in the proofs.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters that should be explicit.  
- Practical applicability is unclear—paper lacks discussion on handling inaccurate estimation of fidelity parameters ζ and possible mitigation strategies.  
- Overall strengths (originality, significance, tight analysis, clarity) outweigh weaknesses; with appendix corrections and expanded practical discussion, it would be an excellent NIPS contribution.
