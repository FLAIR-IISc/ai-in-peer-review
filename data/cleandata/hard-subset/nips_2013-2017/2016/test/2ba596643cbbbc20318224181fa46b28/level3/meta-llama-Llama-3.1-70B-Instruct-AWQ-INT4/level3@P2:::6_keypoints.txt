- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm can be played at M fidelities with cheaper biased approximations.  
- Formalizes the problem with fidelity-dependent costs and biased estimates, and defines the desired highest-fidelity outcome.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm, extending UCB to decide both arm and fidelity choices.  
- Provides theoretical characterization of MF-UCB, including regret bounds and a lower bound.  
- Empirically validates MF-UCB through simulations that support the theoretical claims.  
- Writing is clear and the paper is well-organized, making the main ideas easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis is novel, rigorous, and well-executed.  
- Simulations effectively demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed algorithm.  
- Assumes known fidelity bias parameters ε(m), which may be unrealistic or hard to obtain in practice.  
- Paper contains several minor errors (typos and incorrect notation) that need correction.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound work with meaningful contributions to bandit problems.  
- Quality: claims are well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Clarity: presentation and notation are generally clear and concise.  
- Originality: introduces a new formalism, algorithm, and analysis for multi-fidelity bandits.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging problem and has potential impact on online learning research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formalism, solid algorithmic/theoretical contributions, and supporting simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on known ε(m) limits practicality, plus minor presentation errors.
