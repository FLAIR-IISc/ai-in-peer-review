- Proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) framework and MF-UCB algorithm, with regret analysis, lower bound, and synthetic empirical gains over UCB.  
- MF-MAB is a natural, practical extension addressing costly arm evaluations; MF-UCB’s adaptive fidelity selection is intuitively appealing.  
- Theoretical results (regret bound and lower bound) are solid; empirical validation gives preliminary support.  
- Related work is cited, but connections to budgeted bandits and active learning are not made explicit enough.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistent (variables often defined after first use, some scarcely used), harming clarity.  
- Overall presentation and logical flow are poor, making the argument hard to follow.  
- The motivating online-advertising example is misleading because cited non-stationarity aspects conflict with the paper’s stationary assumptions.  
- The treatment of lower fidelities as approximations fails to address potential bias and the consequences of misleading low-fidelity signals.  
- Regret analysis is problematic: optimal arm is chosen by mean reward ignoring costs, so cost-effectiveness (reward-to-cost) is not considered.  
- By ignoring cost in optimal-arm selection, the analysis may misestimate benefits of low-fidelity sampling and actual regret.  
- A more appropriate analysis would study sample/cost complexity under a budget (identify best arm with high probability for fixed cost) rather than cumulative regret.  
- Writing is unwieldy and sentences/organization need substantial streamlining; theory and empirical results should be more clearly separated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, intuitive/adaptive algorithm, theoretical bounds, and empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: confusing notation, misleading motivation, flawed regret setup (no cost consideration), need for cost-complexity analysis, and poor writing.  
- Recommendation: Reject with request for major revisions—revise notation, clarify motivation, refine regret or provide cost-complexity analysis, and improve clarity/organization.
