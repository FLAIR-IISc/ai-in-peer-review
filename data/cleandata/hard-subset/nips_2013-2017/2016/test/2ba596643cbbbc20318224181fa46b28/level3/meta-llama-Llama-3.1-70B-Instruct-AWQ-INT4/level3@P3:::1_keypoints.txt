- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandits where cheap approximations to expensive arm outcomes are available.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a novel UCB-style algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs.
- Provides theoretical analysis: regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound.
- Empirical simulations on synthetic problems show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Strength: technically sound and novel problem setup, significant contribution to bandit literature.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation and motivation for key choices and assumptions.
- Unclear motivation for the choice of γ(m) in MF-UCB and lack of insight into its optimality.
- Paper should better compare and interpret the lower bound relative to MF-UCB’s regret bound.
- Concern about multiplicative cost penalization in the regret—additive penalization might be more natural.
- Weak broader motivation for the reward/cost model; ad-display example is not well generalized.
- Assumption 1 (constraint on ζ(m)) appears artificial and opaque; needs justification and impact analysis.
- Lacks discussion of robustness when assumptions (e.g., ζ(m) decay) are violated and algorithm performance in such cases.
- Originality: novel framing of multi-fidelity bandits but builds on existing UCB methods—needs clearer differentiation.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential applications in advertising, finance, healthcare.
- Minor issues/typos: undefined “near-optimal,” stray punctuation, and set-notation formatting errors.
