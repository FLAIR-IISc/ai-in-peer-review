- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where pulls have increasing cost with accuracy.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm adapted to multi-fidelity pulls.
- Proves upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret for the proposed setting and algorithm.
- Shows MF-UCB adapts to available approximations and costs, improving regret over naive strategies that ignore approximations.
- Simulation results on synthetic problems demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in regret.
- Main paper is clear and well-written, with a thorough theoretical analysis and clear presentation of results.
- Supplementary materials (proofs) contain many typos and small errors that may confuse readers.
- Practical applicability is unclear; MF-UCB may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity parameters (ζ) and needs discussion of mitigations.
- Use of “up-to-a-multiplicative-constant” notation is misleading because hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters.
- Key contributions: new formalism for multi-fidelity bandits, a novel MF-UCB algorithm, and theoretical characterization of its performance.
- Strengths: novel framework, thorough analysis, and strong empirical performance.
- Weaknesses: typographical/errors in proofs, unclear practical limitations, and notation issues.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix supplementary typos and clarify practical limitations and notation.
- Rating: 8/10.
