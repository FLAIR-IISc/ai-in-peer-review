- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit formulation where lower fidelities are cheaper but introduce bounded bias.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.
- Provides theoretical regret bounds for MF-UCB, though the bounds are complex and hard to interpret.
- Argues theoretically that MF-UCB often outperforms always-using highest-fidelity UCB.
- Presents simulation results claiming empirical improvements (reported ~3–4x) of MF-UCB over standard UCB.
- Strength: Novel problem formulation capturing cheaper approximations to experiments.
- Strength: Algorithm adapts UCB optimism to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Strength: Theoretical analysis offers performance guarantees for the proposed method.
- Strength: Simulations support empirical benefits of MF-UCB.
- Weakness: Modeling low fidelity as increased bias (rather than variance) is questionable and may not fit all applications.
- Weakness: Weak connection between the model and motivating applications; some examples are unconvincing.
- Weakness: Regret objective may be unclear or inappropriate for certain domains (e.g., clinical trials).
- Weakness: Simulation parameter choices are not justified, complicating interpretation of reported gains.
- Originality: Main novelty is the problem formulation; algorithmic tools are standard but adapted to this setting.
- Significance: Addresses a relevant problem with implications for advertising, finance, robotics, etc.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explanations are clear, but simulation choices need better justification.
- Quality: Technically sound analysis supported by theoretical and empirical evidence.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions requesting justification of modeling assumptions and simulation parameters and exploration of alternative models (e.g., increased variance).
