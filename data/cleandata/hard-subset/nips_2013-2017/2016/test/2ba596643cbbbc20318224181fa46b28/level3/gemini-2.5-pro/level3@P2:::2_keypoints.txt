- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit setting where arms can be pulled at M fidelities with cheaper biased/noisier low fidelities and costlier accurate high fidelities.  
- Defines objective as minimizing cost-weighted pseudo-regret under a total budget, motivated by applications like online advertising and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an intuitive extension of UCB that uses low-fidelity pulls to eliminate poor arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising ones.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: an upper bound on MF-UCB regret showing gains over single-fidelity UCB.  
- Gives a regret lower bound proving MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Main paper is well-written, clear, and organized; problem setup, algorithm, and main results are easy to follow.  
- Primary weakness: supplementary appendix contains many typos, notational inconsistencies, and minor errors that hinder verification of proofs.  
- Reviewer lists concrete appendix issues (e.g., eq.(8) set notation, H^(m)/K^(m) typo, incorrect fractions, variable mix-ups, wrong exponents/logs) requiring thorough proofreading.  
- Notation in main theorems uses vague "up-to-constant" symbols that may hide parameter dependencies (e.g., on ρ); recommends presenting explicit inequalities.  
- Practical limitation: assumes known fidelity bias bounds ζ(m); paper should discuss sensitivity to misspecification and whether ζ(m) can be learned online.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-motivated contribution with strong theory and near-optimal algorithmic results.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on substantial revision of the appendix and adding discussion on practical setting/estimation of ζ(m).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel setting, elegant algorithm, solid upper and lower bounds.  
- Arguments for rejection: appendix’s high density of errors and the strong, unaddressed assumption of known ζ(m).
