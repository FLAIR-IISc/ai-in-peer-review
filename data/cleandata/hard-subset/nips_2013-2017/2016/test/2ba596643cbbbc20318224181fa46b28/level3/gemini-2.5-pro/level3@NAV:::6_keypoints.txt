- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem where arms can be pulled at M fidelities with higher fidelity giving more accurate but costlier rewards, motivated by applications like advertising and hyperparameter tuning.
- Proposes a new cost-aware regret metric tailored to the MF-MAB setting.
- Develops the Multi-Fidelity UCB (MF-UCB) algorithm that escalates fidelities intuitively.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including an upper regret bound and a matching lower bound, establishing near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Empirical simulations support the theoretical claims and show clear improvement over a naive UCB baseline.
- Primary weakness: the method assumes known fidelity deviation bounds ζ^(m), which is unrealistic and limits practical applicability.
- Lacks discussion or methods for estimating ζ^(m) or analyzing robustness to their misspecification; relaxing this assumption would improve relevance.
- Analysis section contains multiple minor errors/typos (e.g., wrong set notation H^(m) vs K^(m), incorrect indicator term, wrong inequality directions, incorrect superscripts/indices) that need correction.
- Overall assessment: novel and rigorous contribution with minor but addressable weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
