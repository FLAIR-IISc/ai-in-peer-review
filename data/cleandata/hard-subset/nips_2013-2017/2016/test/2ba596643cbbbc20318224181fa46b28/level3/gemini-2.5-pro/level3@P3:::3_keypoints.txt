1. Introduces the MF-MAB problem: K arms, M fidelities where lower fidelities are cheaper but produce biased estimates within known bounds ζ(m).  
2. Proposes MF-UCB: a UCB variant that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
3. Fidelity selection rule: use cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and escalate to higher fidelities for promising candidates.  
4. Provides theoretical results: regret upper bound for MF-UCB and a corresponding problem-specific lower bound.  
5. Empirical claim: simulations report 3–4× improvement over standard high-fidelity-only UCB.  
6. Strength — originality/significance: novel, practical formulation relevant to simulations, hyperparameter tuning, and partial rollouts.  
7. Strength — algorithm design: elegant, intuitive extension of UCB with a sensible fidelity escalation rule.  
8. Strength — theory: complete theoretical treatment indicating near-optimality under certain conditions.  
9. Weakness — fidelity modeling: modeling low fidelity as bounded bias (rather than increased variance) is a critical and sometimes questionable assumption.  
10. Weakness — application fit: tenuous connection to some motivating applications and mismatch between regret objective and tasks like best-arm identification.  
11. Weakness — clarity: Theorem 2’s regret bounds are complex and lack intuitive or simplified special-case analysis.  
12. Weakness — experiments: parameter choices in simulations are unjustified and no sensitivity analysis over costs λ(m) and biases ζ(m) is provided.  
13. Overall: Valuable new direction with a sensible baseline algorithm and guarantees, but weakened by modeling assumptions and insufficient experimental/practical grounding.
