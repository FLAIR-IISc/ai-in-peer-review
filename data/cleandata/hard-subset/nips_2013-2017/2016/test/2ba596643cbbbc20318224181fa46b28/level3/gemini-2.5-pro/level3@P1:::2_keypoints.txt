- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms have expensive high-fidelity rewards and cheaper biased lower-fidelity approximations with known bias bounds ζ(m); objective is cost-weighted pseudo-regret under budget Λ.

- Proposes MF-UCB, which uses low-fidelity pulls to quickly eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising candidates.

- Provides theoretical analysis: an upper regret bound for MF-UCB and a matching problem-specific lower bound showing near-optimality under certain conditions.

- Strength — originality/significance: a novel, well-motivated extension with practical relevance (advertising, HPO, simulation) and clear distinction from budgeted bandits.

- Strength — theoretical quality: intuitive, elegant algorithm with rigorous proofs demonstrating adaptation to cost-accuracy trade-offs and improvement over naive high-fidelity UCB.

- Strength — clarity of main paper: well-written, organized, consistent notation, and helpful illustrative figures.

- Weakness — appendix quality: supplementary proofs contain many typos, notational inconsistencies, incorrect references, and equation errors that hinder verification.

- Weakness — presentation of bounds: reliance on up-to-constants notation hides important dependencies (e.g., parameter ρ); exact inequalities would be more transparent.

- Weakness — practical assumption: requires known ζ(m) values but lacks discussion or methods for estimating them or analyzing sensitivity to estimation errors.

- For acceptance: novel and important contribution with strong theoretical results and clear exposition in the main paper.

- Against acceptance: appendix errors obstruct proof verification and the strong ζ(m) assumption limits practical applicability.

- Recommendation: accept subject to thorough revision of the supplementary material and addition of a discussion on estimating or handling unknown ζ(m).
