- Contribution: Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework, a cost-weighted regret measure, and the MF-UCB algorithm that adaptively switches fidelities.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides a novel formulation with tight upper and lower regret bounds demonstrating near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical strength: Synthetic experiments show MF-UCB can significantly outperform standard UCB by using cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms.  
- Clarity of main text: Main paper is clear, well-written, and accessible.  
- Appendix quality: Supplementary material contains numerous typos and mathematical errors that undermine confidence in the proofs (many specific corrections listed).  
- Notation concern: Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading because hidden constants are algorithm parameters; exact inequalities are preferred.  
- Practical applicability: MF-UCB assumes known fidelity parameters (ζ(m), λ(m)); the paper lacks discussion of practical limitations and how to handle inaccurate parameter estimates.  
- Overall assessment: Original and significant theoretical contribution, but appendix errors and missing practical discussion reduce perceived quality and impact.  
- Recommendation: Revise and thoroughly fix the appendix errors and add discussion/mitigations for unknown fidelity parameters before acceptance; with these revisions it would be a strong paper.
