- Problem: Introduces multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where each arm can be queried at M fidelities with known bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m).  
- Objective: Defines a cost-weighted pseudo-regret under capital budget Λ measured against the highest-fidelity mean regardless of played fidelity.  
- Algorithm: Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style method with fidelity-aware confidence bounds inflated by ζ(m) and a fidelity-selection rule using thresholds γ(m).  
- Key idea: Use cheap low fidelities to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserve expensive high-fidelity plays for near-optimal arms.  
- Theoretical results: Provides nonasymptotic upper bound on regret relative to Λ showing low-fidelity elimination and a complementary lower bound.  
- Near-optimality: MF-UCB is shown near-optimal for a subset of arms; a gap remains for certain arm partitions (K(m)7).  
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments show MF-UCB achieves lower regret slopes than naive highest-fidelity UCB.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on UCB optimism and standard concentration tools; distinct from budgeted bandits and related to multi-fidelity optimization/RL and labeler active learning.  
- Strength — formulation: Formalizing bias-bounded approximations and costs is clear, relevant, and well-motivated.  
- Strength — algorithmic contribution: MF-UCB cleanly augments UCB with fidelity-aware confidence sets and principled γ(m) selection.  
- Strength — theory: Upper and lower bounds give useful insight into fidelity usage and when improvements occur.  
- Strength — empirical alignment: Experiments broadly support the theoretical claims.  
- Weakness — practicality: Requires known ζ(m) and λ(m); in practice these are rarely known and may be data-dependent.  
- Weakness — assumption sensitivity: Assumption 1 (fast ζ(m) decay relative to ψ) is invoked for favorable comparisons but its practical prevalence is unclear.  
- Weakness — interpretability: Regret bounds are complex; constants and arm partitioning K(m) obscure the main takeaways.  
- Weakness — optimality gap: Lower bound shows a non-negligible gap for some arms, weakening universal optimality claims.  
- Weakness — clarity: Regret definition and choice qt = µ(M)It need more discussion; main theorems lack digestible corollaries and illustrative examples.  
- Weakness — originality nuance: Main novelty is problem formulation and metric; the algorithm is an adapted UCB and should be presented as such.  
- Weakness — empirical limitations: Experiments lack sensitivity analyses, justification for parameter choices (ζ, λ, ψ), and additional baselines.  
- Suggestions — theory: Provide simple corollaries (e.g., M=2, sub-Gaussian) that quantify improvement factors over UCB.  
- Suggestions — experiments: Add sensitivity sweeps over ζ(m), λ(m), M and ablations of γ(m); include more baselines and heuristics.  
- Suggestions — robustness: Discuss how to estimate or upper bound ζ(m) from data and the effect of misspecification.  
- Suggestions — clarity: Include a self-contained illustrative example of the arm partition K(m) in the main text and clarify the regret objective for different domains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Well-motivated new setting, clean adaptation of UCB with nontrivial analysis, and empirical gains consistent with theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practicality concerns about known biases/costs, hard-to-interpret bounds with an optimality gap, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: Quality and clarity are moderate; originality is good in formulation; significance is promising but contingent on further empirical and robustness work.
