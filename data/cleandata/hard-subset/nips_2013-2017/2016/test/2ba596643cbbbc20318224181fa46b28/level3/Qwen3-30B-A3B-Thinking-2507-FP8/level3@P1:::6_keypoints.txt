- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm has M fidelities with different costs and approximate rewards.  
- Defines a cost-weighted regret that accounts for fidelity-specific costs, differing from standard bandit regret.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively selects fidelity per arm.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB when lower fidelities are sufficiently cheap and informative.  
- Gives a matching lower bound demonstrating MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Validates the approach with synthetic experiments that show significant improvement over naive UCB strategies.  
- Situates the work within bandit and multi-fidelity optimization literature, addressing the exploration–exploitation trade-off missing from prior multi-fidelity methods.  
- Strengths: clear formalization, novel regret definition, elegant analysis, insightful partitioning of arms into K(m) sets, and thorough lower-bound construction.  
- Main weakness: assumption that ζ^(m) (deviations of fidelity means) are known; the paper does not explain how to obtain or estimate these in practice.  
- Recommendation: authors should discuss methods to estimate ζ^(m) and analyze how estimation errors affect regret.  
- Minor issues: several notation/typo errors (lines 326/357/386 K vs H, line 343 indicator term, line 345 γ^(m), lines 346–347 inequality direction, line 384 summation index, line 407 δ^(M) and κ λ^(m) corrections, lines 431/440/eq.17 l ≥ p, line 450 P(A_{n,2})).  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, well-written, original and significant contribution to bandit theory; recommend acceptance despite the practical limitation on ζ^(m).
