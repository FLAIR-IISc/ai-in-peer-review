- Paper introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with M fidelities (increasing cost, decreasing bias) and proposes MF-UCB to leverage low-fidelity proxies via bias bounds ζ(m) and escalation thresholds γ(m).

- MF-UCB builds per-fidelity UCBs on the highest-fidelity mean, adaptively selects the lowest informative fidelity, and escalates only when necessary.

- Theoretical contributions: cost-weighted regret analysis under capital Λ, upper and lower bounds, and near-optimality for a large subset of arms via fidelity-dependent partitions K(m).

- Empirical results (synthetic) show MF-UCB reduces expensive pulls and outperforms a single-fidelity UCB baseline.

- Novelty: formalizes biased low-cost proxies in stochastic MABs and extends optimism with cost-aware fidelity escalation; lower bound is a valuable addition.

- Major concern: the regret objective is questionable—it compares to an oracle always playing the highest-fidelity arm and treats low-fidelity plays as if they yielded high-fidelity reward, mixing inconsistent interpretations of proxies vs. real reward.

- This regret definition ignores cost-reward trade-offs and may hide the true cost of low-fidelity sampling; alternative objectives (reward-per-cost, costed BAI) would be more natural.

- The gap between upper and lower bounds for certain arms (K(m)7 regime) is acknowledged but not resolved; practicality and frequency of these cases need discussion.

- Experiments are limited: only synthetic data and a single baseline (top-fidelity UCB); comparisons to budgeted bandits and BAI baselines are missing.

- Notation and exposition are heavy and sometimes unclear (symbols introduced after use, dispersed definitions), harming readability.

- The advertising example is misleading because it suggests nonstationarity/time-correlation while the analysis assumes stationary i.i.d. data.

- Organization issues: regret definition, oracle baseline, and modeling choices should be motivated and presented earlier and more clearly.

- Modeling assumptions need clarification: stationarity, cross-fidelity independence, known ζ(m), and whether ζ(m) is global or arm-dependent, plus how ζ(m) is obtained in practice.

- Suggested objective revisions: treat low-fidelity pulls as real reward µ(m), adopt fixed-budget BAI for proxies, or define oracle maximizing µ(M)/cost and re-analyze accordingly.

- Suggested empirical improvements: add budgeted MAB and BAI baselines, test K(m)7 stress cases, ablate γ(m), and assess sensitivity to ζ(m) misspecification.

- Writing fixes: consolidate notation table early, move technical partitions to appendix, and replace or clarify the advertising example to fit model assumptions.

- Pros summarized: interesting and timely problem, novel and practical algorithm, solid theory, and empirically consistent trends.

- Cons summarized: misaligned objective/regret notion, clarity/notation problems, misleading example, limited empirical validation, and unresolved lower-bound gap.

- Overall recommendation: potentially impactful but requires substantial revision to align objectives with applications, improve clarity, and strengthen experiments before publication.
