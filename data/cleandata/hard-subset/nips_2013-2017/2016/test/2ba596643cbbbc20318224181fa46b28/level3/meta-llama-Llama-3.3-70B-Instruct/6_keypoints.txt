- Proposes a novel, model-free framework for graph clustering that does not assume data comes from SBM or similar models.  
- Presents a generic theorem: if one clustering fits the data well under a model, any other clustering that also fits well must be close to it.  
- Instantiates the generic theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for model-based clustering algorithms under the proposed framework.  
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction, organized technical sections, and detailed, accessible proofs.  
- Experiments demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness in delivering model-free clustering guarantees.  
- Strength: novel framework offering model-free guarantees, a significant contribution to graph clustering.  
- Strength: supplies theoretical guarantees that clarify performance of model-based clustering methods.  
- Strength: shows applicability by instantiating results for SBM and PFM.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation supports theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: framework assumes the data fits the model well, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: reliance on the Laplacian eigengap, which can be sensitive to noise and perturbations.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparisons with other clustering algorithms or frameworks.  
- Overall: significant and well-presented contribution with potential wide applicability, but needs further work on robustness and comparative evaluation.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel model-free framework, theoretical guarantees for model-based methods, and supportive experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: strong model-fit assumption, sensitivity to eigengap, and absence of comparisons to existing methods.
