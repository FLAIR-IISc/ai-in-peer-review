- Introduces Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit: arms can be sampled at multiple fidelities where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased approximations of high-fidelity rewards.  
- Motivated by practical settings (A/B testing, hyperparameter tuning) where cheap evaluations can speed exploration.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive algorithm extending UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical regret analysis and a lower bound for the proposed problem.  
- Empirical results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic benchmarks.  
- Novel and significant formulation that could impact budget-constrained sequential decision-making by pruning the action space with cheap approximations.  
- Clarity issues: writing is unwieldy, notation is confusing and inconsistently introduced (e.g., variable s defined after first use).  
- Motivating advertising example is potentially misleading, introducing issues (display time correlations, non-stationarity) not handled by the stationary model.  
- Technical concern: defining the “best arm” by highest mean independent of cost is questionable for cost-sensitive settings.  
- Regret definition compares to oracle playing highest-mean arm, which may hide exploration costs from low-fidelity sampling and mischaracterize optimality.  
- Actionable suggestion: reframe analysis as cost-complexity or sample-complexity for best-arm identification under a fixed budget to better match applications.  
- Actionable suggestion: revise the manuscript for clearer writing, streamlined notation, and alignment between motivating examples and formal assumptions.  
- Overall verdict: promising core idea but not ready for publication due to clarity and theoretical formulation concerns.
