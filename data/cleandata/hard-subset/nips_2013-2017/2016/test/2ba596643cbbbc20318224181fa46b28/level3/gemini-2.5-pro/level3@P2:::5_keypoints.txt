- Introduces and formalizes the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where each arm has multiple cost-quality fidelities providing biased/cheaper approximations.  
- Motivated by real applications (e.g., online ads, algorithm selection) where cheap approximations are available.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, extending UCB by selecting arms via tightest confidence bounds across fidelities and sequentially escalating fidelity only when needed.  
- Fidelity selection starts from cheapest and moves up if uncertainty remains, saving expensive samples for promising arms.  
- Provides a regret upper bound that scales with the cost of the cheapest fidelity needed to distinguish an arm, improving over naive high-fidelity-only UCB.  
- Delivers a matching problem-specific lower bound and shows MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Theoretical results are supported by synthetic simulations that clearly illustrate the benefits.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated problem formulation that opens a new research direction.  
- Strength: technically strong, with intuitive algorithm design and thorough theoretical analysis (upper and lower bounds).  
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-written, and uses helpful figures and consistent notation.  
- Weakness: assumes known approximation quality ζ(m), which may be hard to obtain in practice and lacks robustness analysis or online estimation methods.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (sufficient decay of ζ(m)) may be restrictive; behavior when violated needs more discussion.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to synthetic data; semi-realistic or semi-synthetic evaluations would strengthen practical claims.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant contribution, elegant algorithm, solid theory, minor practical caveats.
