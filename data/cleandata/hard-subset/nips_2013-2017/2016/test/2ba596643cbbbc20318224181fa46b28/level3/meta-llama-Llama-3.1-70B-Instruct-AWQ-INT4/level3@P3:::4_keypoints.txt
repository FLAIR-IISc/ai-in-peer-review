- Proposes a novel multi-fidelity bandit framework for exploration–exploitation with cheaper approximations.
- Introduces MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs.
- Provides significant technical contributions including regret analysis and a lower bound.
- Synthetic experiments show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem statement and proposed solution.
- Notation is confusing and inconsistently presented, harming readability (e.g., variable s defined late).
- Advertising example is misleading and references to display-time correlations/non-stationarity are inappropriate.
- Regret analysis is questionable because it treats the best arm by mean reward ignoring sampling costs.
- The approach may hide costs of low-fidelity sampling; a reward-per-cost or cost-constrained objective is more appropriate.
- A sample-complexity (cost-complexity) analysis for finding the best arm under budget constraints would be better motivated.
- Writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization.
- Related work discussion is insufficient, especially regarding multi-fidelity optimization literature.
- Not ready for publication in current form; significant revisions required.
- Recommended fixes: simplify notation, remove/mend misleading examples, revise analysis to account for costs, reorganize writing, and expand related work.
