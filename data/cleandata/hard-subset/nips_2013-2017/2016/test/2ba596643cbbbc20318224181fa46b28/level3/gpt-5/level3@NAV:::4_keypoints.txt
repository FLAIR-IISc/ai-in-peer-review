- Studies multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits where each arm can be sampled at multiple costs and lower-fidelity draws give biased approximations of high-fidelity rewards.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a cost-aware UCB-style algorithm that adaptively selects fidelity using thresholds from bias bounds ζ(m) and costs λ(m).
- Provides a regret upper bound and a lower bound; synthetic experiments show MF-UCB outperforms a naive UCB that ignores lower fidelities.
- Connects to prior work on UCB, multi-fidelity optimization/BO, weak/strong labelers in active learning, and budgeted/bandits-with-costs.
- Strength: Timely, practically motivated problem and an appealing algorithmic idea of using cheap fidelities to prune arms.
- Strength: Analysis clearly shows most capital is spent at the fidelity where arms are eliminated; lower bound helps position results.
- Weakness: Regret formulation and “optimal arm” notion are questionable—comparison to highest mean ignores costs and reward-per-cost or budgeted objectives.
- Weakness: Lacks complementary cost-complexity (PAC) analysis for best-arm identification under budget/constraints.
- Weakness: Notation is confusing and scattered; needs a compact notation block and clearer organization of assumptions (ζ(m), ψ, costs).
- Weakness: Motivating advertising example invokes nonstationarity and correlations not captured by the i.i.d. stationary model—misleading.
- Weakness: Empirics are limited—only compared to vanilla UCB; should include cost-aware baselines and sensitivity to ζ(m), λ(m), and misspecification.
- Weakness: Practicality concern about assuming known bias bounds ζ(m) should be discussed.
- Originality: The formal multi-fidelity bandit model and theoretical analysis are novel relative to existing literature.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if objectives and evaluation are aligned with realistic cost-aware decision-making.
- Overall: Interesting and promising but not ready for publication without addressing objective alignment, clarity/notation, and empirical scope.
