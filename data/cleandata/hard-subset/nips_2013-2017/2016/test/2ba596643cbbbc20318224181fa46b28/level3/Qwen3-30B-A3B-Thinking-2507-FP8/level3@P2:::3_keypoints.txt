- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where cheaper lower fidelities provide biased (bounded-bias) reward estimates.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB can outperform standard UCB by using low-fidelity observations to eliminate suboptimal arms.  
- Formal contributions: problem formalization with bounded bias, the MF-UCB algorithm, and regret analysis.  
- Builds on classic bandit literature and extends prior multi-fidelity optimization work with formal regret analysis.  
- Strength: novel, practically motivated problem formulation relevant to settings with cheap approximations.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical development and technically sound analysis.  
- Strength: empirical results on synthetic problems consistently show MF-UCB outperforming UCB.  
- Weakness: regret bounds are complex and hard to interpret, limiting understanding of when gains occur.  
- Weakness: modeling lower fidelities as biased (not noisy) is questionable for many applications and not convincingly justified.  
- Weakness: weak connection between model and motivating examples (e.g., online advertising/regret meaningfulness).  
- Weakness: simulation parameters (especially ζ^(m)) lack justification; reported 3–4x improvement may be parameter-sensitive.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly written, but complex bounds and modeling choices reduce accessibility.  
- Originality/Significance: novelty lies in the problem formulation and regret analysis; significance depends on scenarios where bias-modeling is appropriate.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify theoretical results and strengthen application-model connections.
