- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity MAB setting and a cost-regret metric combining play cost with pseudo-regret, motivated by real-world problems.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses lower fidelities to filter suboptimal arms and higher fidelities for promising ones.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, including upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, and shows near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Empirical simulations on synthetic tasks corroborate theory, showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB in regret and computation.  
- Novelty and theoretical contribution are strong, filling an underexplored and practical gap in the MAB literature.  
- Algorithm design is intuitive and aligns well with the cost-regret objective, effectively leveraging multi-fidelity structure.  
- Demonstrated advantage over naive UCB, especially when many arms exist but few are near-optimal.  
- Supplementary materials contain typos, missing terms, and incorrect symbols that hurt readability and reproducibility.  
- Minor errors and inconsistent notation appear in supplementary proofs and need correction for clarity and rigor.  
- Practical applicability is unclear when fidelity estimates (ζ) are inaccurate; handling such inaccuracies is not discussed.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" hides important parameters (e.g., ρ) that significantly affect results and should be made explicit.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-analyzed algorithm with empirical support and real-world relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: substantial revisions needed in supplementary materials and insufficient discussion of practical limitations and scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, relevant contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on fixing supplementary proofs and adding discussion of practical limitations.
