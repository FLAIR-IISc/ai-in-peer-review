- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit where lower fidelities are biased but cheaper, defines cost-weighted pseudo-regret, proposes MF-UCB, and proves upper and lower bounds with synthetic experiments.  
- Extends classical UCB, budgeted bandits, and multi-fidelity optimization/ML by handling multiple biased approximation levels with known bias bounds ζ(m).  
- Strength: well-motivated setting with a regret notion aligned to highest-fidelity objective while using low-fidelity probes.  
- Strength: principled MF-UCB algorithm that selects arms via aggregated upper bounds and chooses fidelity using uncertainty thresholds γ(m).  
- Strength: nontrivial regret upper bound showing cost savings by eliminating clearly suboptimal arms at low fidelities, plus a complementary lower bound.  
- Strength: intuitive partitioning (K(m) sets) and careful, aligned proofs; simulations support theory and show desired allocation behavior.  
- Major weakness: relies on known, monotone bias bounds ζ(m) (Assumption 1); paper lacks guidance on specification or online estimation of ζ(m).  
- Suggests sensitivity analysis to ζ(m) mis-specification and cost ratios λ(m), and methods to estimate ζ(m) on the fly with robust thresholds.  
- Experimental scope limited to synthetic problems and naive UCB baseline; stronger baselines (UCB-V, Thompson Sampling, two-stage strategies) and sensitivity plots are needed.  
- Lower-bound gap for arms in K(m)7 is identified but not addressed algorithmically; more characterization of this regime’s practical prevalence and heuristics to mitigate it would help.  
- Technical quality: development is sound, ψ-based concentration and K(m) decomposition are well-structured, and lower-bound arguments are appropriate.  
- Assumptions and regret decomposition are clearly stated; matching rates for K(m)3 support near-optimality claims.  
- Clarity: overall readable with strong intuition on γ(m) and fidelity selection; recommended to instantiate ψ and γ(m) for sub-Gaussian rewards and show numeric thresholds.  
- Originality: novel multi-fidelity bandit formulation integrating biased, cost-graded fidelities with cost-aware regret.  
- Significance: conceptually and methodologically important for domains with cheap proxies, clarifying when cost savings are achievable.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss/estimate ζ(m) in practice, add robustness results, include stronger baselines and sensitivity analyses, and consider a small real or semi-synthetic case study.  
- Suggested extensions: arm-dependent costs, non-nested fidelity hierarchies, and settings where lower fidelities yield no direct reward.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear formulation, solid algorithm and analysis, and empirical evidence consistent with theory.  
- Cons for acceptance: practical reliance on known ζ(m) and limited empirical breadth; lower-bound gap for K(m)7 not addressed.  
- Minor comments/typos: several notational slips and index/label errors (multiple line references listed by reviewer) should be corrected.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward acceptance, contingent on addressing ζ(m) practicality (discussion/robustness) and broadening empirical comparisons.
