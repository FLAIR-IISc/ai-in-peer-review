- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) setting where arms can be pulled at different fidelities with cost-accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Formalizes the MF-MAB problem with a new cost-weighted regret definition suited to the setting.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an intuitive UCB extension that uses low-fidelity pulls to filter arms and reserves high-fidelity pulls for promising arms.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: an upper bound on regret for MF-UCB and a matching problem-specific lower bound, showing near-optimality under conditions.  
- Presents synthetic simulations that validate the theory and show practical gains over a standard single-fidelity UCB baseline.  
- Motivates the problem with practical applications (online advertising, hyperparameter tuning) and distinguishes it from related bandit variants.  
- Strength: high novelty and practical relevance by leveraging cheap biased approximations in sequential decision-making.  
- Strength: technically complete and non-trivial analysis, including arm partitioning across fidelities.  
- Strength: generally clear writing and intuitive presentation of the main ideas and results.  
- Major weakness: relies on a strong assumption that fidelity bias bounds ζ^(m) are known for all fidelities and arms, which is unrealistic in practice.  
- Major concern: paper lacks discussion or methods for estimating ζ^(m) online and does not analyze robustness to misspecified bias bounds.  
- Suggestion: addressing or discussing online estimation of ζ^(m) and sensitivity to their choice would substantially strengthen practical impact.
