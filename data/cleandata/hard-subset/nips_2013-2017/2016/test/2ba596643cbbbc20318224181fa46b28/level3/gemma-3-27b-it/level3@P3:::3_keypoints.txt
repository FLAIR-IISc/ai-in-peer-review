- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem modeling cheaper low-fidelity evaluations as biased estimates.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that widens confidence intervals to account for fidelity-induced bias.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds and a lower bound to argue partial optimality.  
- Empirically shows MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB in simulations.  
- Novel formulation that explicitly models bias from low-fidelity evaluations and is relevant to real-world scenarios.  
- Conceptually sound algorithm built on established UCB principles with a fidelity-aware adjustment.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis, though results are complex.  
- Regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret, limiting practical insight and comparison.  
- Modeling low-fidelity outputs as biased (rather than noisier) is debatable and under-justified.  
- Weak linkage between the theoretical objective and some motivating applications (e.g., ad displays).  
- Simulation parameter choices (especially ζ^(m)) lack justification and sensitivity analysis, raising concerns about reported gains.  
- Incremental algorithmic novelty—main idea is an adaptation of standard optimism/UCB techniques.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, logical algorithm extension, and theoretical rigor.  
- Arguments against acceptance: opaque regret bounds, questionable bias modeling, tenuous application fit, and insufficient empirical robustness.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revisions addressing regret clarity, bias justification, and more thorough empirical analysis.
