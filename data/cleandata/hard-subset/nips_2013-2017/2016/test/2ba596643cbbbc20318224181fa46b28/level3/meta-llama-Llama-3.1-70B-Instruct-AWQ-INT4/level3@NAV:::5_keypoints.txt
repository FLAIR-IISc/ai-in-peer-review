- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity (MF) formalism for K-armed bandits where cheaper approximations to outcomes are available.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting Upper Confidence Bound strategies to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- MF-UCB uses cheap, lower-fidelity plays to eliminate suboptimal arms and reserves expensive, high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- The approach achieves better regret than naive strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds characterizing MF-UCB’s performance.  
- Presents a lower-bound analysis demonstrating the theoretical improvement of the MF approach.  
- Simulation results illustrate benefits and behavioral insights of the MF bandit method.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Paper is novel, well-motivated, and has potential impact in applications like advertising, finance, and robotics.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Main limitation: assumption that lower fidelities are cheaper and better approximations may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of MF-UCB.  
- Does not discuss potential applications in depth.  
- Overall assessment: strong candidate for acceptance; negatives are minor relative to strengths.
