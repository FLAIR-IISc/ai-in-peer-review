- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit where each arm can be sampled at M fidelities with cheaper fidelities giving biased estimates bounded by ζ(m).
- Proposes MF-UCB, which allocates cheap low-fidelity samples to eliminate clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity samples for promising arms.
- Provides theoretical analysis including an upper regret bound for MF-UCB and a problem-specific lower bound, showing potential improvement over standard UCB under certain conditions.
- Novel and relevant problem formulation addressing cost–quality trade-offs in sequential decision tasks like hyperparameter tuning and simulations.
- MF-UCB is an intuitive extension of UCB with substantial technical analysis that identifies gains mainly from discarding far-from-optimal arms.
- The main theoretical results (Theorem 2) are hard to interpret due to complex dependence on arm partitions K(m) and gaps Δ(m)k; a simplified corollary or example is needed.
- Regret definition uses a multiplicative cost λ(mt)qt, which is unusual; the paper needs stronger justification and discussion of alternative formulations.
- Assumption 1 on the decay rate of ζ(m) is restrictive and may be tailored for analysis, raising concerns about algorithm robustness to arbitrary fidelity structures.
- Lack of guidance for handling fidelities that don't satisfy Assumption 1 (e.g., selecting a valid subsequence) and sensitivity of γ(m) thresholds to fidelity configuration.
- There is a gap between upper and lower bounds for arms in certain sets (K(m)), indicating MF-UCB's switching criterion may be suboptimal in some cases.
- Pros: novel, well-motivated problem; elegant algorithm; technically solid upper and lower bounds demonstrating multi-fidelity benefit.
- Cons: unclear practical significance of theoretical improvement, thin justification for multiplicative regret model, reliance on strong assumptions, and a provable suboptimality gap.
- Minor issues: overstated "near-optimal" claim without qualification; small typographical/notation improvements suggested (period at l.126, use K^(m) vs K(m)).
