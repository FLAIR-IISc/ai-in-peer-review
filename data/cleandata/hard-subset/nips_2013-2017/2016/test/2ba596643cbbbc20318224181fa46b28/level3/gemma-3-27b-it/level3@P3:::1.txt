This paper introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem, where obtaining rewards from arms has varying costs and accuracies. The authors propose MF-UCB, an algorithm adapting the UCB approach to this setting, and provide a regret bound, a lower bound, and empirical evidence of improvement over standard UCB. The problem setup is relevant and interesting, exploring a practical trade-off between cost, information, and reward. The technical analysis appears largely sound, and the paper represents a complete piece of work.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in explaining its results and assumptions, hindering interpretation. The multiplicative cost penalization in the regret formulation feels unnatural, and the motivation based solely on the ad-display example feels limited. Several specific assumptions and algorithmic choices require further justification.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Problem Formulation:** The multi-fidelity bandit setup is a valuable contribution, modeling a realistic scenario found in various applications (advertising, algorithm selection, robotics).
* **Solid Technical Foundation:** The regret bound and lower bound analysis demonstrate a strong theoretical understanding of the problem. The connection to UCB and the identification of conditions where MF-UCB outperforms it are significant.
* **Completeness:** The paper presents a complete algorithm, analysis, and empirical validation.
* **Relevance:** The trade-offs explored are highly relevant to real-world decision-making under limited resources.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Explanation:** The paper lacks sufficient explanation of key results and assumptions. For example, the meaning of the K^(m) sets (used in Thm. 2) is not adequately clarified, making it difficult to grasp the implications of the regret bound. The dependence on Delta is also obscure.
* **Regret Formulation:** Penalizing cost multiplicatively in the regret function feels artificial. An additive cost penalty would be more intuitive and align better with standard bandit regret formulations. The authors should discuss the implications of this choice and perhaps explore alternatives.
* **Limited Motivation:** The ad-display example, while illustrative, doesn't fully capture the generality of the problem. A broader discussion of applications and scenarios where this multi-fidelity setting arises would strengthen the paper’s impact.
* **Assumption 1:** The constraint on zeta_m (Assumption 1) feels artificial and its validity is unclear. The authors should provide a stronger justification or discuss scenarios where this assumption is violated and how MF-UCB performs in such cases.
* **Robustness Concerns:** If the zeta_m values do not decay as required by Assumption 1, the paper doesn’t offer guidance on selecting a suitable subsequence of fidelities to satisfy the assumption. Addressing this would enhance the algorithm’s practicality.
* **Algorithm Dependence:** The algorithm design appears highly tailored to the specific zeta configuration. It’s unclear whether Assumption 1 is crucial for the algorithm's performance or if it still provides benefits under less restrictive conditions.
* **Lower Bound Gap:** The gap between the upper and lower bounds suggests potential for further optimization. The authors should acknowledge this and discuss possible approaches to close the gap.

**Minor Issues:**

* "near-optimal" (l.56) – this is vague; specify what is meant by near-optimal.
* Unnecessary period before "for all m < M" (l.126) – remove the period.
* Use curly braces for set notation [[k]] (l.179) – use {k} instead.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a novel and practically relevant problem.
* The technical analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights.
* The empirical results support the theoretical claims.
* The work expands the scope of bandit algorithms to handle a more complex and realistic scenario.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of clarity and explanation hinders understanding and limits the paper's impact.
* The unusual regret formulation and questionable assumption raise concerns.
* The gap between the upper and lower bounds indicates potential for improvement.



**Overall Assessment:**

While the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of bandit algorithms, its clarity issues and questionable assumptions detract from its overall quality. I recommend **rejection with major revisions**. The authors should address the clarity concerns, justify the regret formulation and Assumption 1, and investigate the lower bound gap. Improving these aspects will significantly strengthen the paper and enhance its potential impact. Addressing the robustness question regarding zeta_m would also be valuable.
