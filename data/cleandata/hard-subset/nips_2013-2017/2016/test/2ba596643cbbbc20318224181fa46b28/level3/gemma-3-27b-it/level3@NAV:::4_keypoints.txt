Introduces a multi-fidelity MAB framework where lower-fidelity, cheaper evaluations provide approximate information about high-fidelity outcomes.

Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm adapted to the multi-fidelity setting.

Provides empirical results showing MF-UCB outperforms a naive UCB baseline on synthetic problems.

Includes a lower bound aimed at demonstrating near-optimality.

Connects well to existing bandit literature and the broader problem of efficient exploration.

Notation is confusing and inconsistent (variables defined after first use and sparsely used), hurting readability.

The online advertising application example is misleading and introduces irrelevant complexities (display time correlations, non-stationarity).

Regret analysis is questionable because the “best” arm is defined by highest mean without accounting for cost of achieving that mean across fidelities.

Lacks a cost-complexity or sample-complexity analysis that would quantify resources needed under budget constraints.

Writing and presentation are unwieldy and need streamlining and reorganization.

Recommendation: Reject but encourage revise-and-resubmit after substantial revisions.

Requested revisions: clarify notation, remove irrelevant advertising complexities, revise regret analysis, and add a cost-complexity analysis.
