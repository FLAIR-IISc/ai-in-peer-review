- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic M-armed bandit with M fidelity levels per arm where higher fidelities are less biased but costlier.
- Defines a cost-weighted regret that credits true high-fidelity value while penalizing capital spent across fidelities.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that adaptively selects arm and fidelity using per-fidelity confidence bounds and thresholds γ(m).
- Proves an upper bound showing MF-UCB concentrates expensive high-fidelity plays on a small candidate set and uses cheap fidelities elsewhere.
- Provides a problem-dependent lower bound showing near-optimality except on a specific subset where the switching rule is conservative.
- Empirical simulations on synthetic problems corroborate theoretical gains, especially when low fidelities are cheap and informative.
- Situates work relative to UCB, budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization/RL, and weak/strong labeler active learning, highlighting novelty in the regret formalism.
- Technical development is careful and sound; decomposition and proof ideas (arm partitioning, play control, change-of-measure) are clear.
- Key modeling caveat: assumes known fidelity biases ζ(m), which the algorithm and analysis require to set γ(m).
- Paper lacks discussion of how ζ(m) would be obtained in practice and contains no robustness analysis to misspecified ζ(m).
- Major suggestion: discuss estimation/upper-bounding of ζ(m), provide robustness theory or empirical sensitivity, or propose an adaptive-safe variant that learns bias bounds.
- Experimental suggestions: add ablations over λ(m), ζ(m), M, mis-specified ζ(m), and compare to simple baselines and switching heuristics.
- Recommend briefly discussing regimes where low-fidelity rewards themselves matter and how regret/analysis would change.
- Presentation notes: move Assumption 1 earlier, add a running toy example, and fix numerous minor typos and notational glitches listed by the reviewer.
- Pros: well-motivated formulation, elegant unified arm-and-fidelity algorithm, strong mostly-tight theoretical guarantees, and supportive simulations.
- Cons: practicality depends on known ζ(m), experiments limited to synthetic data, and several technical typos/notation issues.
- Minor detailed typos and equation/index corrections are enumerated for fixing.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing the ζ(m) assumption (at least empirically) to strengthen the contribution.
