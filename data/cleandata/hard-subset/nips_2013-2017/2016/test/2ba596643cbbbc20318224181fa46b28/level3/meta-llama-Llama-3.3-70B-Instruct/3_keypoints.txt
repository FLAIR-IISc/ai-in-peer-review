- Introduces a novel model-free framework for clustering graphs without assuming SBM-like generative models.  
- Provides correctness and stability guarantees based on observable data statistics.  
- Instantiates the framework for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and the Preference Frame Model (PFM).  
- Supplies theoretical guarantees for clustering stability and perturbation bounds.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and organized technical sections.  
- Proofs are rigorous and easy to follow.  
- Offers a thorough discussion of related work and clearly states its contributions.  
- Assumes spectral clustering as the algorithmic choice, which may not be optimal for all problems.  
- Does not provide a thorough comparison with existing model-based clustering approaches.  
- Some technical results (e.g., perturbation bounds) are difficult to interpret and apply in practice.  
- Overall, a significant and potentially influential contribution, though some results need further refinement for wider applicability.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical stability guarantees, and clear presentation; rejection arguments: reliance on spectral clustering, insufficient comparisons, and practical interpretability concerns.
