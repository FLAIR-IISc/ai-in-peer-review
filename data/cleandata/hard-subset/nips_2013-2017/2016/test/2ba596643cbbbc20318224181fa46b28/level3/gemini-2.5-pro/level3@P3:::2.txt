### Review of "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit"

**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel and practical extension of the stochastic K-armed bandit problem, termed the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit. In this setting, each arm can be pulled at one of M different "fidelities." Pulling at a lower fidelity is cheaper but yields a more biased and/or noisy estimate of the arm's true reward, while higher fidelities are more accurate but costlier. The paper formalizes this trade-off with a cost-aware regret metric, defined as the total cost expended multiplied by the cumulative pseudo-regret.

The primary contribution is the Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB) algorithm, an elegant extension of the classic UCB algorithm. The intuition behind MF-UCB is to leverage cheap, low-fidelity pulls to quickly identify and discard grossly suboptimal arms, thereby reserving the expensive, high-fidelity budget for exploring the most promising candidates. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including an upper bound on the cumulative pseudo-regret of MF-UCB and a corresponding problem-dependent lower bound. This analysis demonstrates that MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions and can significantly outperform naive strategies that only use the highest fidelity. The paper's formulation connects to prior work in multi-fidelity optimization but is novel in its application to the exploration-exploitation trade-off central to bandit problems.

**Strengths**

*   **Originality and Significance:** The multi-fidelity bandit framework is a well-motivated and significant contribution. It captures a practical challenge present in many domains, such as A/B testing, hyperparameter optimization, and clinical trials, where preliminary, cheaper experiments can inform more expensive ones. By formalizing this problem and providing a principled algorithmic solution, the paper opens up a promising new direction for research in bandit theory and its applications.
*   **Technical Quality:** The paper's theoretical contributions are strong. The proposed MF-UCB algorithm is intuitive and well-designed. The regret analysis is solid, providing both upper and lower bounds that clearly characterize the benefits of the multi-fidelity approach. The proofs appear sound in their high-level structure and logic.
*   **Clarity:** The main body of the paper is exceptionally clear and well-written. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem, explaining the algorithm's mechanics, and interpreting the theoretical results. The connection between the algorithm's switching condition and the properties of the fidelity levels is particularly well-articulated.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

While this is a strong paper, there are several areas where it could be substantially improved. The primary concerns relate to the clarity of the supplementary material and the discussion of practical limitations.

1.  **Errors in Supplementary Material:** The appendix containing the proofs, while theoretically sound at a high level, is marred by numerous typos and minor errors. These issues make the proofs very difficult to follow and verify, undermining the paper's overall quality and reproducibility. A thorough revision is strongly recommended. A non-exhaustive list of issues includes:
    *   Page 10, eq. (8): Typo in the exponent, likely should be a function of `k` not `K`.
    *   Page 10, line 328: ν/(ρ-2) should likely be ν/2 based on standard concentration bounds.
    *   Section A1: H^(m) appears to be a typo for K^(m).
    *   Page 11, after line 343: `s` is used where `μ` is meant.
    *   Page 11, eq. (10): γ should be γ^(m).
    *   Page 11, eq. (11): Contains an extra comma.
    *   Page 12, line 375: "Lemma" should refer to "Theorem".
    *   Page 12, last line & Page 13, first equation: Parentheses are missing, affecting the order of operations.
    *   Page 13, definition of x_n: K^2 should be K.
    *   Page 13, definition of y_n: The exponent should be 1/(ρ-2).
    *   Page 13, lines 388–389: The sequence of logical implications is unclear and seems to be stated incorrectly.
    *   Page 14, line 406: N should be log(N).
    *   Page 14, equation after line 406: The inequality direction appears to be reversed (N < 2n should be N > 2n).
    *   Page 15: `p` and `m` seem to be interchanged in several places.
    *   Page 15, line 448: A union (∪) is used where an intersection (∩) is needed.
    *   Section C: "distribtuions" is misspelled.
    Addressing these and other errors would greatly enhance the reader's confidence in the results.

2.  **Clarity of Theoretical Bounds:** The paper often uses "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation (e.g., `.`, `&`). While common, this practice is misleading here because one of the key "hidden constants," `ρ`, is a tunable parameter of the algorithm. This makes it difficult to understand how the choice of `ρ` quantitatively affects performance from the stated bounds. Presenting the exact, non-asymptotic inequalities in the main theorems would make the results more transparent and practically useful.

3.  **Discussion of Practical Applicability:** The algorithm and analysis rely on the assumption that the fidelity bias parameters, `ζ(m)`, are known. In many real-world applications, these values would be unknown and need to be estimated. The paper would be significantly stronger if it included a discussion on this practical limitation. How robust is MF-UCB to misspecified `ζ(m)` values? Could these parameters be learned online? Addressing these questions would provide valuable guidance for practitioners and enhance the paper's impact.

**Conclusion**

Overall, this is a very good paper that introduces an important new problem and provides a strong initial solution and analysis. The core contribution is novel, significant, and technically sound. The main paper is a model of clarity. However, the work is let down by the lack of care in the supplementary material and a limited discussion of its practical implementation challenges. I recommend acceptance, but I strongly urge the authors to perform a thorough proofreading of the appendix and to add a discussion on the practical challenges of estimating the fidelity parameters.
