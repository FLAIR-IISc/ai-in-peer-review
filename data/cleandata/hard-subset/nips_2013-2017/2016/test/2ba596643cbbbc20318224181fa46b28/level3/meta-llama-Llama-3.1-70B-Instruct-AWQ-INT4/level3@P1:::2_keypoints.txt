- Introduces a new multi-fidelity bandit formalism where cheaper fidelities approximate a costly experiment.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, showing near-optimality under conditions.  
- Shows MF-UCB uses low fidelities for broad exploration and high fidelities to refine near-optimal arms.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of framework and algorithm.  
- Supplementary proofs contain minor errors and typos that must be corrected for clarity.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is potentially misleading since hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters.  
- Practical applicability is unclear; the paper should discuss real-world limitations and mitigations.  
- MF-UCB may be sensitive to inaccurate estimation of fidelity parameters (ζ), which could hurt performance.  
- Overall contribution is significant and proofs are solid.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix supplementary errors and expand discussion of practical implications.  
- Rating: 8/10.
