- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity bandit formalism where each arm has multiple cheaper approximation fidelities.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB that leverages fidelity sequences and costs.  
- Theoretical characterization shows MF-UCB uses low fidelities for exploration and high fidelities for near-optimal arms.  
- Provides regret upper bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound under certain conditions, claiming near-optimality.  
- Simulation results show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB on synthetic problems.  
- Strength: Addresses a relevant trade-off between information, cost, and reward in stochastic bandits.  
- Strength: Technical analysis appears solid with a thorough theoretical performance characterization.  
- Strength: Paper presentation is clear and well-organized.  
- Weakness: Results and assumptions are not explained adequately, making interpretation difficult.  
- Weakness: Regret penalizes cost multiplicatively; additive penalization is not considered and may be more natural.  
- Weakness: Ad-display example may not generalize; broader motivation for reward/cost model needed.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (decay of fidelity errors) is artificial, opaque, and of unclear general validity.  
- Weakness: Algorithm design seems tailored to specific fidelity configurations; unclear if Assumption 1 critically affects performance.  
- Weakness: Gap in the lower bound requires further investigation and may need strategy redesign.  
- Minor issues: Contains typos (e.g., "near-optimal") and inconsistent set-notation/bracketing.  
- Recommendation: Paper is a good fit for the conference but authors should improve clarity and justify/generalize Assumption 1.  
- Rating: 8/10.
