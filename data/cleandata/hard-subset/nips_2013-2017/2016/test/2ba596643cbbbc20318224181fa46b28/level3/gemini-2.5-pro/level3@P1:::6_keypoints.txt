- Introduces the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem: K arms, M fidelities where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased approximations of the true (highest-fidelity) reward.  
- Motivates the setting with real-world applications (online advertising, algorithm selection) where cheap approximations exist.  
- Formalizes a cost-aware regret notion for a fixed capital budget.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that uses low-fidelity pulls to quickly eliminate grossly suboptimal arms and save high-fidelity budget for promising arms.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: regret upper bound for MF-UCB showing improvement over standard UCB that ignores fidelities.  
- Derives a problem-specific lower bound and shows MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Supports theoretical claims with synthetic experiments demonstrating practical advantage.  
- Technical quality is high; proofs and switching criterion between fidelities are well-justified.  
- Main weakness: strong assumption that bias bounds ζ(m) are known a priori, limiting practical applicability.  
- Paper lacks discussion or analysis of how to obtain/estimate ζ(m) in practice and robustness to their mis-specification.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, with intuitive algorithm presentation and helpful analysis overview, but needs minor proofreading.  
- Originality: highly original formulation and a creative, non-trivial adaptation of UCB to multiple fidelities.  
- Significance: important and likely influential; opens new avenues for bandit research and practical efficiency gains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, elegant algorithm, comprehensive theoretical analysis (upper & lower bounds), and high potential impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: practicality limited by the assumed known ζ(m) values.  
- Reviewer asks authors to address ζ(m) practicality, sensitivity to mis-specification, and whether ζ(m) can be learned online with regret implications.  
- Minor suggested corrections (line-specific): replace "H^(m)" with "K^(m)" (lines 326,357,386); indicator condition and variable fixes around lines 343–346 (β_{k*,t} ≤ s, s = μ_*, γ → γ^(m), inequality direction); sum index correction at line 384; δ^(m) → δ^(M) and missing μ_* term at line 407; "for l ≥ m" → "for l ≥ p" at lines 431 and 440/Eq.(17); "P(A_{n,3})" → "P(A_{n,2})" at line 450.
