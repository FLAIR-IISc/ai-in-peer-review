- Introduces the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit (MF-MAB) problem: cheaper biased approximations exist alongside costly true observations.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm adapted to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with regret bounds and a matching lower bound.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data show MF-UCB outperforms a naive UCB baseline.  
- Novelty: formalizes a practically relevant problem that extends classical bandits to trade off cost and fidelity.  
- Strength: tailored algorithm and rigorous regret analysis are commendable.  
- Strength: intuitive idea to use low-fidelity data to eliminate suboptimal arms and concentrate expensive evaluations on promising ones.  
- Weakness: inconsistent and confusing notation (variables introduced before definition, unused definitions) harming readability.  
- Weakness: motivating example (online advertising) is misleading and brings in irrelevant issues like non-stationarity.  
- Weakness: regret formulation is questionable because it measures regret against an oracle that ignores evaluation costs.  
- Weakness: ignoring cost in the reward/regret makes comparisons to standard UCB less meaningful.  
- Weakness: lacks a more realistic cost- or sample-complexity analysis (how many samples/cost to identify the best arm under a budget).  
- Weakness: writing and organization are unwieldy; needs streamlining and clearer presentation.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after major revisions addressing notation, motivation, cost-complexity analysis, and clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, tailored algorithm, theoretical bounds, supportive synthetic experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor readability, misleading motivation, questionable regret framework, absence of cost-complexity analysis, and weak organization.
