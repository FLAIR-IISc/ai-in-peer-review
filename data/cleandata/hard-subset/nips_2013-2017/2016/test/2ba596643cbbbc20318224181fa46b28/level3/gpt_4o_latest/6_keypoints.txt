- Introduces multi-fidelity K-armed bandit problem where fidelities trade off accuracy and cost.  
- Proposes MF-UCB algorithm that uses low-cost fidelities to eliminate arms and high-cost fidelities for promising arms.  
- Formalizes cost-weighted regret to account for fidelity-specific costs.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms naive UCB when many arms exist and few are near-optimal.  
- Derives a regret lower bound, arguing MF-UCB is near-optimal under certain conditions.  
- Empirical results on synthetic datasets corroborate theoretical improvements over standard UCB.  
- Major limitation: assumes prior knowledge of fidelity deviations (ε^(m)/ζ(m)), which may be impractical.  
- Limited empirical validation: no real-world datasets tested.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and organized.  
- Minor issues: typos, inconsistent notation, and equation errors at several line numbers.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (address fidelity-deviation assumption and expand empirical evaluation).
