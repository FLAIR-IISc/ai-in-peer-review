- Paper studies K-armed stochastic bandits with M discrete fidelities having known bias bounds ζ_m and costs λ(m), and proposes MF-UCB to maximize net reward under budget Λ.  
- MF-UCB forms fidelity-specific UCBs B_k,t^(m)=empirical_mean+uncertainty+ζ_m, picks arm by optimism across fidelities, and chooses fidelity via thresholds γ(m).  
- Authors analyze a cost-weighted regret R(Λ)=Λμ*−Σ_t λ(m_t)μ^(M)_{I_t}, prove O(log Λ) bounds decomposed by fidelity partitions K^(m), and give a Bernoulli lower bound nearly matching MF-UCB except on a subset.  
- Connects to classical UCB, budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization/RL, and active learning with weak/strong labelers; novelty is formal multi-fidelity biased observations with costs and corresponding regret analysis.  

Strengths:
- Tackles a relevant problem trading bias, cost, and reward with clear practical motivation.  
- Principled algorithm and solid analysis showing low fidelities can cheaply eliminate suboptimal arms while reserving high-cost plays.  
- Theory and experiments indicate improvement over high-fidelity-only UCB, with explicit λ(m)/λ(M) savings for many arms.  

Weaknesses / concerns — Modeling:
- Regret formulation is modeling-dependent (multiplicative cost term); needs stronger justification and discussion of alternatives (additive penalization, scalarized utility, constrained budget knapsack-style).  
- Authors should analyze how MF-UCB and bounds change under alternative objectives.  

Weaknesses / concerns — Lower bound:
- Lower bound gap for arms in K^(m)_7 suggests MF-UCB may switch fidelities too early; needs deeper investigation or a cost-aware fidelity allocation rule to close the gap.  

Clarity issues:
- Theorem 2 is hard to parse; request a simple 2–3 fidelity corollary with explicit constants and an informal term-by-term explanation.  
- Make comparison to UCB explicit: state when and by how much MF-UCB improves (concrete corollary/formulas).  
- Assumption 1 is opaque; provide intuition, sufficient conditions (e.g., geometric decay of ζ for sub-Gaussians), and explain necessity.  
- Broader motivation is narrow (ad example); add other domains where multiplicative regret metric is appropriate and where it is not.  

Originality:
- Setup with known fidelity-dependent bias bounds and costs appears original; authors should position more explicitly against closely related cost/bias-aware bandit models.  

Significance / empirical suggestions:
- Potentially impactful if assumptions hold and K^(M) small, but empirical evaluation is limited.  
- Compare to stronger baselines: cheapest-first elimination heuristics, Thompson sampling variants, and policies that skip redundant fidelities.  
- Ablate mis-specified ζ(m) and λ(m) to test robustness.  

Specific technical questions / suggestions:
- How sensitive is MF-UCB to ζ mis-specification (loose upper bounds or underestimates); can effective bias envelopes be learned adaptively?  
- If Assumption 1 fails, propose/ analyze a procedure to select a subsequence of fidelities and state regret in terms of that subsequence.  
- Quantify how critical Assumption 1 is for practical performance with a theorem or empirical study showing degradation when it fails.  
- Provide an explicit bound/lemma for the r̃(Λ) term showing it’s negligible relative to R̃(Λ).  

Pros for acceptance:
- Timely and practical extension of bandits with a clear algorithm, nontrivial analysis, and a partially matching lower bound.  
- Conceptual contribution: principled use of biased low-cost proxies.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Presentation and assumption clarity impede accessibility.  
- Insufficient justification of the regret objective and lack of discussion of alternatives.  
- Lower bound gap indicates possible suboptimality in a meaningful regime with no mitigation.  
- Empirical evaluation limited in scope and baselines.  

Minor issues / typos:
- Inconsistent hyphenation of “near-optimal”; punctuation and notation issues (extra period, set notation, complex symbols) and suggestions to simplify J^(m)_η, K^(m), and reintroduce Δ^(m)_k where used.  

Overall:
- Technically promising but needs improved exposition (esp. Theorem 2), justification of the regret metric, deeper treatment of Assumption 1 and the lower-bound gap, and stronger empirical validation; current recommendation leans weak accept if addressed, otherwise borderline.
