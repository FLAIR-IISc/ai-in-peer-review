**Review of "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in multi-armed bandit problems where cheaper approximations to a desired experiment are available. The authors introduce a multi-fidelity bandit setting, where each arm has multiple fidelities that serve as approximations to the desired outcome. They develop a new algorithm, MF-UCB, based on upper confidence bound techniques, which uses cheap lower fidelity plays to eliminate bad arms and reserves expensive high fidelity queries for a small set of arms with high expected reward. The authors provide a regret analysis and a lower bound, demonstrating that MF-UCB is near-optimal.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and interesting framework for studying multi-armed bandit problems with cheaper approximations.
2. The authors develop a new algorithm, MF-UCB, which is shown to outperform naive strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information.
3. The regret analysis and lower bound provide a thorough understanding of the algorithm's performance.
4. The paper is well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the problem and its relevance to various applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. The notation is confusing and dispersed, which hampers readability.
2. The advertising example is misleading, and references to display time correlations and non-stationarity are not relevant to the paper's scope.
3. The regret analysis is questionable, as the optimal policy should maximize reward/cost, but the paper treats the best arm as the highest mean regardless of cost.
4. The writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

1. Revise the notation to make it more consistent and clear.
2. Replace the advertising example with a more relevant and accurate example.
3. Reconsider the regret analysis to account for the cost of each arm.
4. Improve the writing style to make it more concise and easier to follow.

**Conclusion**

While the paper has some weaknesses, it proposes an interesting and novel framework for studying multi-armed bandit problems with cheaper approximations. The authors develop a new algorithm, MF-UCB, which is shown to outperform naive strategies. With some revisions to address the weaknesses, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

**Rating**

* Quality: 7/10
* Clarity: 6/10
* Originality: 8/10
* Significance: 8/10

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper, but with revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
