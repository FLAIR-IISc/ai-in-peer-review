- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit with M fidelities, fidelity-dependent bias, and a cost-weighted regret that credits the highest-fidelity mean.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that adaptively selects arm and fidelity via thresholded confidence radii.  
- Derives an upper bound showing cheap fidelities prune clearly suboptimal arms while expensive plays are reserved for near-optimal arms.  
- Proves a complementary lower bound identifying a gap on a specific subset of arms.  
- Simulations on synthetic problems support the theoretical behavior.  
- Technical development is solid, with novel upper bound, fidelity-switching criterion, and lower bound.  
- Analysis clearly explains regimes where MF-UCB outperforms single-fidelity UCB.  
- Main weakness: assumes bias envelopes ζ(m) (ε(m) in places) are known; unclear how to obtain them and how sensitive MF-UCB is to misspecification.  
- Reviewer requests discussion of practical estimation (pilot calibration, domain bounds), robustness (safe worst-case choices), or an online method to learn/upper-bound ζ(m).  
- Requests commentary on Assumption 1: its necessity and scope.  
- Empirical validation is limited to synthetic data; recommends small-scale real or semi-synthetic experiments and comparisons to baselines (e.g., budgeted bandits, top-two-fidelity variants).  
- Clarity is generally good but notation is heavy; suggests a running example mapping ζ, λ, γ to an ad scenario.  
- Originality: setting, regret, and analysis appear novel and relevant relative to prior multi-fidelity work.  
- Significance: addresses a practical problem and should spur follow-up on learning bias envelopes and broader regret notions.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel formulation and regret, principled algorithm with near-optimal guarantees, insight into using cheap fidelities to prune arms, and a complementary lower bound.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong known-ζ(m) assumption without robustness guidance, limited empirical validation with no real data, and under-discussed technical conditions (Assumption 1).  
- Minor technical corrections: list of nine line/equation fixes (specific index/notation corrections at lines 326, 343, 345, 346–347, 357, 384, 386, 407, 431, 440/Eq.(17), 450) as detailed by the reviewer.
