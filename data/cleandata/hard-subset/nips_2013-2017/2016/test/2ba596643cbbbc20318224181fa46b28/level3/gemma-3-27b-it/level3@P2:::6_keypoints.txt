- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) setting where cheap approximations of rewards exist alongside costly high-fidelity evaluations.  
- Defines regret that accounts for both reward and fidelity cost.  
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm with theoretical analysis and a matching lower bound.  
- Provides simulations showing MF-UCB outperforms a naive UCB baseline.  
- Differentiates this work from budgeted bandits and multi-fidelity optimization literature and grounds it in practical examples (online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- Strength: clear problem formulation, novel algorithm, and strong theoretical guarantees (near-optimal regret).  
- Strength: careful treatment of biased approximations via a regularity assumption.  
- Key weakness: relies on knowing the deviation ζ(m) between fidelity means — a strong assumption with no discussion of estimation or robustness.  
- Theoretical gap: acknowledged gap between algorithm performance and lower bound for some arms that should be further understood or closed.  
- Practicality concern: numerous assumptions and complex bounds may hinder adoption.  
- Minor issues: several notation and typographical errors throughout the paper that must be corrected.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical rigor (including lower bound), potential practical impact, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments for rejection: strong ζ(m) assumption limiting applicability and overall complexity of analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on addressing ζ(m) estimation/robustness and fixing the noted typos.
