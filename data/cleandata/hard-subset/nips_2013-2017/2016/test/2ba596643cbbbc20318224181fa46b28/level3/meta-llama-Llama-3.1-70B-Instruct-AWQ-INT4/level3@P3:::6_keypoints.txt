- Introduces a multi-fidelity bandit framework where expensive true-arm outcomes can be approximated cheaply by biased lower fidelities.  
- Formalizes decision process with M fidelities, each having different costs and biased estimates of the highest fidelity.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.  
- Theoretical results show MF-UCB adapts to available approximations and costs, achieving better regret than naive approaches.  
- Provides a lower bound and shows MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, practical examples, and a thorough literature review.  
- Strengths: novel problem formulation, well-designed algorithm, thorough analysis (including lower bound), and clear exposition.  
- Weakness: several typos and notation inconsistencies throughout the manuscript (multiple line references).  
- Weakness: assumption of known ε^(m) (deviation of m-th fidelity mean) is unclear and may be unrealistic; unclear how these values are obtained or if the assumption can be relaxed.  
- Overall evaluation: strong contribution with high scores (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix typos/notation and clarify or relax the ε^(m) assumption.
