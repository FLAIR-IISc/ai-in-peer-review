- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model with arms having multiple fidelities of differing cost and accuracy.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs to reduce regret versus naive UCB.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, well-organized presentation of problem, algorithm, and analysis.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including an upper regret bound and a lower bound.  
- Simulation results empirically demonstrate MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB.  
- Theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret in practice.  
- Assumes lower fidelities introduce bounded bias, which may not hold in real-world settings.  
- Originality lies in the problem formulation and applying UCB ideas to the multi-fidelity setting, though algorithmic ideas are standard.  
- Significance: addresses practical applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) and can yield meaningful improvements.  
- Clarity concerns: notation and terminology may be unfamiliar and the problem/algorithm need more detailed explanation.  
- Simulation parameter choices are not justified and could affect results.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address interpretability, assumptions, clarity, and simulation justification.  
- Suggested revisions: expand explanations of problem and algorithm, clarify notation, justify simulation parameters, and make regret bounds more interpretable.
