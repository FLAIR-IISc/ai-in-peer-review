This paper introduces a novel framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the presence of cheaper approximations to a desired experiment, known as the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit. The authors propose an algorithm, MF-UCB, which leverages upper confidence bound techniques to adapt to the sequence of available approximations and costs, achieving better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations.

The main idea of the paper is to formalize the task of multi-fidelity bandits, where at each time step, the forecaster may choose to play an arm at any one of M fidelities, with the highest fidelity (desired outcome) expending cost λ and the m fidelity (an approximation) expending λ < λ and returning a biased estimate of the highest fidelity. The authors develop MF-UCB, a novel upper confidence bound procedure for this setting, and prove that it naturally adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs, thus attaining better regret than naive strategies.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed framework is interesting and potentially impactful. However, the reviewer finds the paper not ready for publication in its current form. The notation is confusing and dispersed, harming readability. For example, the variable s is defined after its first use and then barely used. Additionally, the advertising example is misleading, and references to display time correlations and non-stationarity are inappropriate for the paper's scope.

The regret analysis is also questionable. The optimal policy should maximize reward/cost, but the paper treats the best arm as the highest mean regardless of cost, potentially hiding costs of low-fidelity sampling. A sample-complexity (cost-complexity) analysis focused on finding the best arm with high probability under budget/constraints would be more appropriate and motivated by real-world testing scenarios.

Furthermore, the writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization. The reviewer suggests reorganizing the paper to improve readability and addressing the above concerns.
