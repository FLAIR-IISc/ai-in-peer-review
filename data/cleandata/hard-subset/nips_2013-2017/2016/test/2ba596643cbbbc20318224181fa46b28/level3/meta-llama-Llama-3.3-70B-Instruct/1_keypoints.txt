- Proposes a model-free framework for graph clustering that gives "correctness" guarantees based on data statistics rather than assuming a specific generative model.  
- Introduces a generic theorem: if a clustering fits the graph well relative to a model, any other clustering that also fits well is close to the original.  
- Instantiates the theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), yielding model-free guarantees for these models.  
- Novel approach that connects model-free clustering guarantees with existing model-based recovery results.  
- Well-organized and clear presentation, including detailed model estimation algorithms and proofs of main theorems.  
- Technically sound with well-supported claims and careful theoretical analysis.  
- High originality and significance: advances the state of the art in graph clustering.  
- Assumption 1 is introduced in an artificial and opaque manner; its necessity and impact are not clearly explained.  
- Some theoretical results and bounds are difficult to interpret and compare across models.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs more datasets and comparisons to other clustering methods.  
- Certain assumptions and proofs could be explained more clearly for broader accessibility.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should clarify Assumption 1 and expand experimental validation.
