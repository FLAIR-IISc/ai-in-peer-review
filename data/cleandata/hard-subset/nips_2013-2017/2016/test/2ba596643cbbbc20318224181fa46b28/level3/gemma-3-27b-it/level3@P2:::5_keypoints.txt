- Introduces a new multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where cheap biased approximations supplement expensive true observations.
- Clear practical motivation with applications in online advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics.
- Formalizes the problem and proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound-based algorithm tailored to multiple fidelities.
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees: regret bounds, a near-optimality result, and a matching lower bound.
- MF-UCB intuitively and effectively uses cheap approximations to eliminate suboptimal arms and expensive measurements for exploitation.
- The γ(m) fidelity-selection threshold is a key component and is well-justified in the paper.
- Analysis builds rigorously on UCB theory and extends it to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Empirical simulations concisely demonstrate MF-UCB’s performance gains over standard UCB.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to readers familiar with bandits.
- Related work discussion is good but should include more on active learning with weak/strong labelers (e.g., Zhang & Chaudhuri, NIPS 2016).
- Assumption 1 (ζ(m) decay) needs more practical guidance on realistic decay patterns in applications.
- Sensitivity of results to alternative regret definitions and choices of qt should be explored and discussed.
- Proofs are rigorous but placed in the appendix; a detailed proof sketch in the main text would improve accessibility.
- Overall: strong, novel, and well-supported contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above points.
