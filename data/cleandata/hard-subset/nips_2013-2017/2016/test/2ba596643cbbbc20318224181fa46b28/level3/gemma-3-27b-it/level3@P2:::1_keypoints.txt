- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit problem where rewards have varying fidelity levels and costs.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that adapts UCB to select fidelity levels per arm and time step.
- Provides regret analysis for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound, claiming near-optimality under certain conditions.
- Core idea: use cheap, low-fidelity approximations to quickly discard suboptimal arms and reserve high-fidelity pulls for promising ones.
- MF-UCB maintains upper confidence bounds for each fidelity and adaptively chooses fidelity to improve efficiency.
- Demonstrates improved regret versus a naive high-fidelity-only strategy.
- Builds on standard UCB literature and is a natural, practically motivated extension of bandit frameworks.
- Simulation results, though brief, support the theoretical findings.
- Strength: well-defined problem, sound technical analysis, and clear connection to related work.
- Weakness: overall clarity problems make some arguments and results hard to follow.
- Regret penalization by multiplicative cost feels unnatural; additive cost penalization should be discussed and analyzed.
- Primary motivating example (ad-display) is limited; broader application discussion and reward/cost model justification are needed.
- Assumption 1 (specific decay rate for fidelity errors zeta_m) seems artificial and may not hold generally.
- Authors should address cases where Assumption 1 fails (e.g., selecting subsequences of fidelities or analyzing weaker conditions).
- Algorithm appears tailored to specific zeta configurations, raising robustness concerns.
- Gap between theoretical lower bound and algorithm performance is notable and requires more investigation.
- Presentation of Theorem 2, dependence on Δ, and the sets K^(m) is difficult to parse and needs clearer exposition.
- The effect of the parameter ρ on performance should be analyzed and discussed.
- Quality: technically rigorous but diminished by clarity and assumption issues; paper feels incomplete on limitations and extensions.
- Originality: novel and non-trivial adaptation of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Significance: potentially impactful for cost-accuracy trade-offs in practice, but tempered by the above limitations.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses a novel problem, technically sound with regret and lower bounds, and outperforms naive baselines.
- Rejection arguments: clarity issues, artificial Assumption 1, unnatural regret model, gap to lower bound, and limited discussion of limitations.
- Overall assessment: weak accept conditional on addressing clarity, Assumption 1, theoretical gaps, and minor editorial fixes.
