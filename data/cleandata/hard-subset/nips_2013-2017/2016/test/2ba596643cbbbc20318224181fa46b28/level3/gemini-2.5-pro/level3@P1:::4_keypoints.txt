- Introduces the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit (MF-MAB) problem where each arm has multiple fidelities trading cost for biased/noisy approximations of the true reward.
- Proposes the MF-UCB algorithm that adaptively selects arms and fidelities using a UCB-based switching criterion to balance information gain and sampling cost.
- Provides theoretical upper-bound analysis showing MF-UCB uses cheap fidelities to eliminate bad arms and reserves expensive pulls for promising ones.
- Derives a lower bound for MF-MAB and argues MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.
- Validates theoretical claims with synthetic experiments where MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.
- Strength: novel, practically relevant problem formulation connecting multi-fidelity sampling to bandits.
- Strength: technically complete work with algorithm, upper bounds, and matching lower bound.
- Strength: potential impact for fields like AutoML, experimental design, and online advertising where exploration cost matters.
- Weakness: paper clarity and presentation are poor—dense notation and unclear organization hinder readability.
- Weakness: motivating example (online advertising/fidelity as display time) is misleading due to likely non-stationarity not captured by the model.
- Weakness: regret formulation is questionable—defines regret against the best high-fidelity mean and ignores reward-to-cost trade-offs that matter in practice.
- Weakness: problem may be better framed as cost/sample complexity for best-arm identification under a fixed budget rather than cumulative regret minimization.
- Overall recommendation: technically solid but not ready; major revisions needed to improve clarity, re-evaluate regret definition, and better align motivation with the model.
