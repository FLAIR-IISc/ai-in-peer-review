- Introduces and formalizes the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit (MF-MAB) problem with cost-weighted pseudo-regret and multiple fidelity evaluations per arm.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate arms and reserves expensive high-fidelity evaluations for promising arms.  
- Provides a rigorous upper-regret bound showing the algorithm adapts to the cheapest fidelity that identifies suboptimal arms.  
- Includes a problem-specific lower bound demonstrating near-optimality of MF-UCB for a large class of instances.  
- Simulations on synthetic problems validate theoretical claims and show clear benefits over standard UCB.  
- Strength: novel and practically motivated problem formulation bridging bandits and multi-fidelity methods.  
- Strength: technically strong—clear algorithm design (notably the fidelity-selection rule) and rigorous analysis (Theorem 2).  
- Strength: includes a lower-bound analysis (Theorem 4), strengthening the optimality claims.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear writing, organization, and use of arm partitions for exposition and proofs.  
- Weakness: gap between upper and lower bounds for arms whose low-fidelity means misleadingly approach the optimum (the K(m) set).  
- Weakness: reliance on Assumption 1 that bias bounds decay fast relative to cost increases, which may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Weakness: experiments are purely synthetic; lacks evaluation on realistic or semi-synthetic tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning with varied training budgets).  
- Acceptance pro: novel, important problem and elegant algorithm with strong theoretical backing.  
- Acceptance con: limited experimental realism and a minor theoretical gap for specific instances.  
- Conclusion: overall a strong, well-executed paper recommended for acceptance.
