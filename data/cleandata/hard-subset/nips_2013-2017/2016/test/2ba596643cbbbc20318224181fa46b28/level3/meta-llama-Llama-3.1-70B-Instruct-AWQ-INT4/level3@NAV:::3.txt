This paper introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit model, where each arm has multiple fidelities with varying costs and accuracy. The authors propose a UCB-based algorithm, MF-UCB, which adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs to achieve better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations.

The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-organized presentation of the problem, algorithm, and analysis. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm's performance, including a regret bound and a lower bound. The simulation results demonstrate the empirical improvement of MF-UCB over UCB.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement. The theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret, which may make it difficult to understand the algorithm's performance in practice. Additionally, the paper assumes that the lower fidelities introduce bounded bias in estimates, which may not always be the case in practice.

The paper's originality lies in its problem formulation and the adaptation of UCB to the multi-fidelity setting. While the algorithm itself uses standard optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty ideas, the application of these ideas to the multi-fidelity setting is novel.

The significance of the paper is that it addresses a practical problem in many applications, such as online advertising and algorithm selection. The results show that MF-UCB can achieve better regret than UCB in these settings, which can lead to significant improvements in practice.

However, there are some concerns about the paper's clarity. The notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to some readers, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the problem and the algorithm. Additionally, the simulation parameter choices lack justification, which may affect the reported results.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel problem formulation and algorithm for the multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides a regret bound and a lower bound for the algorithm.
* The simulation results demonstrate the empirical improvement of MF-UCB over UCB.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical regret bounds are messy and hard to interpret.
* The paper assumes that the lower fidelities introduce bounded bias in estimates, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The simulation parameter choices lack justification.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the problem and the algorithm.

**Revision Suggestions:**

* Provide a more detailed explanation of the problem and the algorithm.
* Clarify the notation and terminology.
* Justify the simulation parameter choices.
* Consider revising the theoretical analysis to make the regret bounds more interpretable.
