- Introduces a multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandit where each arm can be queried at M biased, cheaper fidelities approximating the highest-fidelity reward.  
- Defines a cost-aware pseudo-regret that compares against an oracle investing in the optimal arm at any fidelity.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: add known fidelity bias ζ(m) to confidence radii and pick the cheapest fidelity whose uncertainty exceeds a bias–cost threshold γ(m).  
- Analysis partitions arms into sets K(m) by closeness at fidelity m and shows MF-UCB concentrates budget on the lowest fidelity that can certify suboptimality.  
- Regret bound improves over naive UCB for most suboptimal arms by exploiting cheaper fidelities; a lower bound shows near-optimality on a large subset of arms.  
- Simulations corroborate theoretical predictions on fidelity usage and regret slopes.  
- Extends classical UCB and relates to budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, simulator-based RL, and weak/strong labeler settings, but differs in modeling biased approximations of a single target.  
- Technical contribution is strong: appropriate regret definition, careful handling of biased fidelities, and well-motivated partitioning and γ(m) design.  
- Assumption 1 (decay of ζ(m) relative to ψ) is reasonable for sub-Gaussian rewards but should be clarified for other regimes.  
- Upper bound degrades gracefully to standard UCB in worst case and yields substantial gains when few arms are near-optimal.  
- Identifies a specific gap where MF-UCB’s switching rule may be suboptimal and frames resolving it as an open problem.  
- Main practical concern: MF-UCB requires known fidelity biases ζ(m), which are rarely available and may be heterogeneous across arms.  
- Suggested mitigations for unknown ζ(m): online estimation via cross-fidelity disagreement tests, conservative data-driven γ(m) with inflated ζ(m) confidence terms, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Request to make hidden constants explicit (notably dependence on ρ), provide explicit inequalities, and give tuning guidance for practitioners.  
- Ask to clarify Assumption 1’s behavior when exponential decay fails or M is large and provide bounds that scale with M.  
- Experiments should include realistic case studies (e.g., ad allocation, subsampled ML model selection) and report capital-normalized regret and fidelity counts.  
- Main paper is clear and well organized; supplementary material contains many typos, notation inconsistencies, and proof-step omissions that impede verification.  
- Novelty: formalizing biased multi-cost fidelities with MF-UCB and matching upper/lower bounds is original and meaningful.  
- Significance: provides a principled approach to exploit cheap biased proxies, especially valuable when K is large and few arms are near-optimal.  
- Pros: clear formalization, simple principled algorithm, tight guarantees, and matching empirical validation.  
- Cons: reliance on known ζ(m), hidden constants, many supplement errors, and lack of real-world experiments.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with fixes—correct supplement typos, add robustness/estimation of ζ(m), clarify constants and assumptions, and strengthen experiments.
