- Novelty: Introduces a multi-fidelity extension to multi-armed bandits, addressing costly high-fidelity evaluations.  
- Algorithm design: Proposes MF-UCB, a natural UCB adaptation that adjusts confidence intervals for biased low-fidelity evaluations.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provides regret bounds and a lower bound analysis indicating benefits of the multi-fidelity approach.  
- Empirical results: Simulations report 3–4x better regret for MF-UCB versus standard UCB.  
- Theoretical complexity: Regret bounds are hard to interpret and lack intuitive clarity for practitioners.  
- Simulation concerns: Parameter choices in experiments are not well-justified, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Modeling assumptions: Modeling low fidelity as bias (rather than variance) is only partially convincing and may not fit all applications.  
- Weak real-world connections: Motivating applications (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection) are not convincingly tied to the model or practical constraints.  
- Pro arguments: Addresses an underexplored problem, presents a well-motivated algorithm, and offers rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Con arguments: Bounds are complex, empirical parameter choices are weakly justified, and real-world applicability is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on better justification of simulation parameters and stronger connections to practical applications.
