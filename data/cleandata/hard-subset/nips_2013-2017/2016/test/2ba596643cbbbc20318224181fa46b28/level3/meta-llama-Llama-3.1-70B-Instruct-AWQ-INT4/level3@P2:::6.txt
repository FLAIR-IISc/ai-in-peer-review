The paper "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit" studies a novel variant of the classical stochastic K-armed bandit problem, where observing the outcome of each arm is expensive, but cheap approximations to this outcome are available. The authors formalize this task as a multi-fidelity bandit, where at each time step, the forecaster may choose to play an arm at any one of M fidelities. The highest fidelity (desired outcome) expends cost λ, while the m fidelity (an approximation) expends λ < λ and returns a biased estimate of the highest fidelity.

The authors' main contributions are: (1) a novel formalism for studying bandit tasks when one has access to multiple fidelities for each arm, with each successive fidelity providing a better approximation to the most expensive one; (2) a new algorithm, Multi-Fidelity Upper Confidence Bound (MF-UCB), that adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategies to the multi-fidelity setting; and (3) a theoretical characterization of the performance of MF-UCB, including a lower bound on the regret.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their contributions. The theoretical analysis is novel and well-executed, and the simulations demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
