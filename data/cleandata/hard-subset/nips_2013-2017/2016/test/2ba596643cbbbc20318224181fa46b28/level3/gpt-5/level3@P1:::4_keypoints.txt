- Novel problem: formalizes multi-fidelity stochastic K-armed bandits with biased lower fidelities and fidelity-dependent costs.
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes MF-UCB, an adaptive per-arm-per-fidelity UCB with a principled switching criterion γ(m).
- Theoretical results: provides regret upper bounds showing low-fidelity queries can efficiently eliminate poor arms and concentrates high-fidelity queries on near-optimal arms.
- Lower bound: derives a matching lower bound that exposes a gap for a subset of arms where MF-UCB’s conservative switching may be suboptimal.
- Empirical evidence: synthetic experiments show MF-UCB reduces regret slope and total regret versus single-fidelity UCB.
- Relation to prior work: connects to UCB, budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization/RL, and weak/strong labeler active learning but differs by modeling biased approximations and fidelity costs.
- Key strength: well-motivated, under-explored formulation with an intuitive and clean extension of UCB.
- Key strength: careful theoretical decomposition and insightful partition-based analysis.
- Main concern: regret/objective definition optimizes true reward under cost budget, making optimal arm cost-independent and potentially misaligned with cost-sensitive applications.
- Main concern: low-fidelity plays that do not produce true reward may understate opportunity costs; alternatives (e.g., reward-per-cost) are not analyzed.
- Modeling risk: motivating examples (e.g., advertising) may imply non-stationarity or covariate shift inconsistent with the i.i.d. reward model.
- Clarity issues: heavy, scattered notation and forward references make exposition and proofs hard to follow and hurt reproducibility.
- Assumptions/robustness: Assumption 1 and known ζ(m) may be restrictive; algorithm robustness to mis-specified ζ, redundant fidelities, and choice of ρ/γ(m) is not evaluated.
- Lower-bound gap: MF-UCB can miss cost-efficient low-fidelity distinctions for some arms; no practical remedy or refined switching analysis provided.
- Limited experiments: comparisons only to single-fidelity UCB; missing stronger baselines (KL-UCB, UCB-V, Thompson), sensitivity analyses, heavy-tailed noise, and ζ mis-specification tests.
- Suggested improvements: justify or extend objective (reward-per-cost), or scope examples to informational low-fidelity settings; consider best-arm identification under budget.
- Suggested improvements: consolidate notation (notation table, define quantities early) and provide intuition-first overview of γ(m).
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments to test ζ mis-specification, λ/ζ schedules, redundant fidelities, baseline algorithms, and sensitivity to ρ/γ.
- Suggested improvements: discuss robustness to model misspecification and practical guidance for choosing ζ(m); explore switching refinements heuristically or analytically.
- Arguments for acceptance: original, well-motivated setting and algorithm with solid theoretical guarantees and corroborating experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: objective misalignment with cost-sensitive applications, clarity/notation problems, and limited empirical validation and robustness testing.
- Overall recommendation: interesting and potentially impactful but requires revision to address objective alignment, clarity, and empirical robustness before publication.
