This paper introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls, where the cost of pulling an arm increases with accuracy. The authors propose the MF-UCB algorithm, which adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs, achieving better regret than naive strategies that ignore the approximations. The paper provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, demonstrating that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow the authors' ideas and understand the contributions. The introduction provides a thorough background on the multi-armed bandit problem and its applications, as well as a clear motivation for the new setting. The authors also provide a detailed explanation of the MF-UCB algorithm and its components, making it easy to understand how the algorithm works.

The theoretical analysis is solid, and the authors provide a thorough proof of the regret bound for MF-UCB. The lower bound is also well-established, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the assumptions and the proof.

However, the supplementary materials (proofs) contain many typos and small errors that can confuse readers. Fixing these errors would greatly improve readability. Additionally, the use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading, as hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters. Presenting exact inequalities would improve clarity.

The practical applicability of MF-UCB is unclear, as the algorithm may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity ζ and needs discussion of practical limitations and mitigations. The authors should provide more insights into how to choose the parameters of the algorithm and how to handle real-world scenarios where the assumptions may not hold.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits, and the MF-UCB algorithm is an interesting and novel approach to handling variable-fidelity pulls. However, the authors should address the issues mentioned above to improve the paper's clarity and practicality.

Strengths:

* Introduces a new and interesting multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls
* Proposes a novel algorithm (MF-UCB) that adapts to the sequence of available approximations and costs
* Provides upper and lower bounds on cumulative pseudo-regret, demonstrating that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions
* Solid theoretical analysis and proof of the regret bound for MF-UCB
* Well-written and clear paper

Weaknesses:

* Supplementary materials (proofs) contain many typos and small errors that can confuse readers
* Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is misleading
* Practical applicability of MF-UCB is unclear, and the algorithm may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity ζ
* Needs discussion of practical limitations and mitigations

Originality:

* The paper introduces a new multi-armed bandit setting with variable-fidelity pulls, which is a novel and interesting contribution to the field.
* The MF-UCB algorithm is a new approach to handling variable-fidelity pulls, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its performance.

Significance:

* The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits, and the MF-UCB algorithm is an interesting and novel approach to handling variable-fidelity pulls.
* The results have potential applications in online advertising, finance, and other fields where multi-armed bandit problems arise.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but require the authors to address the issues mentioned above, such as fixing typos and small errors in the supplementary materials, presenting exact inequalities, and providing more insights into the practical applicability of MF-UCB.
