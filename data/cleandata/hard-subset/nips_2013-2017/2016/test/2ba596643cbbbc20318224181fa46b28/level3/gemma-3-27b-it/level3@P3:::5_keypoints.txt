- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity K-armed bandit formulation where cheaper approximations of rewards can be exploited.
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm tailored to the multi-fidelity setting.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including regret bounds and a lower bound showing benefits of fidelity usage.
- Empirical results demonstrate MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB and visualize arm plays across fidelities.
- Situates work well within bandit and multi-fidelity literature and cites concurrent related work.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation.
- Authors honestly discuss limitations, including potential tightness of the lower bound and assumptions made.
- Assumption 1’s necessity is claimed but its impact when violated is not fully explored.
- Simulations are convincing but limited in scope; more varied cost ratios and fidelity accuracies would strengthen empirical claims.
- The chosen regret definition focuses on highest-fidelity reward; alternative metrics deserve deeper discussion.
- Computational complexity of MF-UCB is not explicitly analyzed or compared to standard UCB.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, well-written, and empirically supported.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient exploration of Assumption 1 violations and narrower simulation scope.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
