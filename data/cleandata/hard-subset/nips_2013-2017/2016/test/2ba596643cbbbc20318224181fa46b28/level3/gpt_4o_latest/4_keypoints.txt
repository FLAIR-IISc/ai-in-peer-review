- Paper introduces Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit (MF-MAB), where arms can be sampled at varying fidelity levels with different costs, and proposes MF-UCB to use low-fidelity samples to eliminate arms and high-fidelity for promising ones.
- MF-UCB is a well-motivated algorithm that adapts UCB techniques to exploit cheaper, lower-fidelity approximations to reduce exploration cost.
- The paper provides regret analysis for MF-UCB, shows near-optimality under certain conditions, and presents a theoretical lower bound.
- Empirical simulations on synthetic tasks show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, with clear fidelity-usage and regret visualizations.
- Strength: Novel, relevant framework addressing practical cost-efficient approximation scenarios with applications in advertising, algorithm selection, and resource-constrained optimization.
- Strength: Solid algorithmic contribution—leverages lower fidelities to reduce exploration costs in a principled way.
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor with regret bounds and lower-bound derivation; discusses when MF-UCB outperforms UCB.
- Strength: Convincing empirical validation demonstrating practical benefits over UCB.
- Weakness: Poor organization and unclear notation (definitions scattered, e.g., s), hurting readability; needs dedicated notation section.
- Weakness: Motivating example (online advertising) is misleading—non-stationarity reference is irrelevant and distracts.
- Weakness: Regret analysis assumes optimal policy prefers highest-mean arm regardless of cost, which may oversimplify cost-effectiveness in real scenarios.
- Weakness: Lacks sample complexity or cost-complexity analysis, which is crucial for multi-fidelity trade-offs.
- Weakness: Writing is verbose and unwieldy in parts; exposition needs streamlining.
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions—novel and significant core idea but requires improved clarity, organization, better examples, and sample complexity analysis.
- Pro: Novel framework with practical relevance, strong theory and empirics, and potential to inspire further multi-fidelity research.
- Con: Poor clarity/organization, misleading example and oversimplified regret assumptions, and missing sample complexity analysis.
