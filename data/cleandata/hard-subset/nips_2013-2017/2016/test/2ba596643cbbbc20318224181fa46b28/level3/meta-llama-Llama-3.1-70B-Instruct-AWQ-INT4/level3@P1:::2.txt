The paper "The Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit" introduces a new framework for studying exploration-exploitation trade-offs in the presence of cheaper approximations to a desired experiment. The authors propose a novel algorithm, MF-UCB, which adapts the classical Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) strategy to the multi-fidelity setting. They provide upper and lower bounds on the cumulative pseudo-regret, demonstrating that MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1. A new formalism for studying bandit tasks with multiple fidelities for each arm, where each successive fidelity provides a better approximation to the most expensive one.
2. A new algorithm, MF-UCB, that adapts the classical UCB strategy to the multi-fidelity setting.
3. A theoretical characterization of the performance of MF-UCB, showing that it uses the lower fidelities to explore all arms and eliminates arms with low expected reward, while reserving the higher fidelity plays for arms with rewards close to the optimal value.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their framework and algorithm. However, there are some minor errors and typos in the supplementary materials (proofs) that can confuse readers. It is essential to fix these errors to improve readability.

One potential criticism of the paper is the use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation, which can be misleading because hidden constants (e.g., ρ) are algorithm parameters. Presenting exact inequalities would improve clarity.

Additionally, the practical applicability of MF-UCB is unclear, and the authors should discuss the limitations and mitigations of their algorithm in real-world scenarios. For example, MF-UCB may suffer from inaccurate estimation of fidelity ζ, which could impact its performance.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of multi-armed bandits, and the authors provide solid proofs to support their claims. However, it is essential to address the minor errors and typos in the supplementary materials and provide a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of their algorithm.
