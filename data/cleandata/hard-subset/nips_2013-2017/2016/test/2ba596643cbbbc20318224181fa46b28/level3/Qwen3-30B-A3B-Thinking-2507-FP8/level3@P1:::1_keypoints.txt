- Paper extends K-armed bandits to multi-fidelity arms with different costs and biased lower-fidelity estimates, proposing a cost-weighted regret and MF-UCB algorithm with regret bounds.  
- Technical contribution is strong: rigorous theoretical analysis, a lower bound, and synthetic experiments supporting claims.  
- Problem is relevant to practical applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection).  
- Regret formulation (multiplicative cost penalization) is unnatural, unjustified, and alternatives (e.g., additive penalties) should be considered.  
- Motivation/examples (ad-display) are weak and do not generalize to domains like clinical trials or robotics where low fidelity may yield no reward.  
- Theorem 2 and comparison with UCB are hard to parse; sets K^(m) and their dependence on Δ^(m)_k need clearer, intuitive explanation.  
- Key Assumption 1 on ζ(m) decay is artificial, opaque, and lacks practical justification or discussion of failure cases.  
- Algorithm design appears tightly coupled to the ζ(m) decay assumption and lacks guidance for selecting fidelities when the condition fails.  
- MF-UCB does not match the lower bound for certain arms (Theorem 4), and the paper does not explain or attempt to close this gap.  
- Claim of "near-optimal" is misleading since it holds only under specific conditions.  
- Paper contains typographical and notation errors (e.g., stray punctuation, inconsistent set notation).  
- Overall quality: technically sound but weakened by questionable assumptions and unclear interpretations.  
- Clarity: significant issues in presentation and explanation of main results.  
- Originality & significance: problem is novel and relevant, but impact is limited by restrictive assumptions and lack of robustness analysis.  
- Recommendation: major revision needed—revise regret formulation, clarify key results, justify Assumption 1, and address the lower-bound gap before acceptance.
