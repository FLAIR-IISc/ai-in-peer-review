- Introduces a multi-fidelity K-armed bandit setting with cheap approximate observations alongside costly precise measurements.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm designed to exploit varying costs and fidelities.  
- Problem formulation is clear, well-motivated, and relevant to advertising, algorithm selection, and robotics.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis including regret bounds and a near-optimality lower bound.  
- Shows MF-UCB adapts to available approximations and outperforms naive strategies.  
- Empirical results and illustrative visualization convincingly validate the approach.  
- Writing and presentation are excellent and the paper is well-organized.  
- Theoretical guarantees rely on a potentially restrictive regularity condition on the decay ζ(m).  
- Lacks detailed exploration of performance when the ζ(m) assumption is violated and of practical implications.  
- Lower bound analysis reveals possible gaps where the algorithm’s switching criterion may not match theoretical optimum.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper—accept with minor revisions to address the assumption and lower-bound gaps.
