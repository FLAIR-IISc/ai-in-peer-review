- Introduces multi-fidelity (MF) stochastic K-armed bandit setting with cheap, biased approximations and a cost-weighted regret objective.  
- Proposes MF-UCB algorithm that leverages low-fidelity information to eliminate suboptimal arms and conserve expensive high-fidelity evaluations.  
- Distinguishes this setting from budgeted bandits and active learning with weak labelers by focusing on hierarchical approximations within regret minimization.  
- Formalization of the MF bandit problem is original and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong: provides upper regret bounds for MF-UCB and a lower-bound analysis showing near-optimality.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and intuitively presents the algorithm and results.  
- Simulations effectively demonstrate practical improvements of MF-UCB over standard UCB.  
- Problem setting is significant for real-world tasks (hyperparameter tuning, scientific simulation, online advertising).  
- Opens a promising new research direction and provides a solid foundation for follow-up work.  
- Minor weakness: small gap remains between upper and lower bounds for a specific subset of arms.  
- Practical applicability could be strengthened by discussing estimation of fidelity bias parameters (ζ(m)).  
- Overall judgment: excellent, high-quality contribution recommended for acceptance.
