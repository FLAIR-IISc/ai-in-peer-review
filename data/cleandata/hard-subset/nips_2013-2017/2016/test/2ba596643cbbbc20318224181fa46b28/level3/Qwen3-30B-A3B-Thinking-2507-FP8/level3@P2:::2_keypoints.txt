- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity K-armed bandit setting where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased, and proposes the MF-UCB algorithm to adaptively select fidelities.  
- Provides a strong theoretical contribution: an upper bound on MF-UCB regret and a matching lower bound under certain conditions.  
- Analysis carefully handles bias and cost structure; shows MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when promising arms are few and lower fidelities are sufficiently cheaper.  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems corroborate theoretical insights.  
- Supplementary materials contain numerous typos and small errors that could confuse readers and undermine confidence in proofs.  
- Main paper is clear, well-organized, and provides good intuition and illustrative figures.  
- Use of "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation is problematic because hidden constants (e.g., ρ) directly affect performance; exact inequalities are preferable.  
- Paper lacks discussion of practical limitations, notably sensitivity to and estimation of fidelity parameters ζ(m).  
- Originality: first formal regret analysis for multi-fidelity bandits and explicit treatment of bias from lower fidelities, distinguishing it from prior optimization work.  
- Significance: results are important for applications with costly high-fidelity evaluations (e.g., advertising, algorithm selection, clinical trials).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, well-motivated algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and clear empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: appendix typos/errors, insufficient treatment of practical issues like ζ(m) estimation and hidden-constant effects.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix appendix errors and add discussion on practical limitations and parameter sensitivity.
