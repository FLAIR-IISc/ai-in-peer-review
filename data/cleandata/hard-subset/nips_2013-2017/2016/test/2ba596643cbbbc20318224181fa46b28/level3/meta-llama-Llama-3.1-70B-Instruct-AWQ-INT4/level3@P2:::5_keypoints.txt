- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity (MF) stochastic K-armed bandit setting with cheap approximations for each arm.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB that leverages cheaper fidelity evaluations.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds for MF-UCB demonstrating improved performance over naive strategies.  
- Offers a lower-bound analysis showing near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Presents simulations that illustrate practical benefits and behavior of the MF approach.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with thorough background and related work.  
- Strong balance between theoretical analysis and practical simulation results.  
- Original contribution: formalizing MF bandits and developing MF-UCB.  
- Significant because it exploits cheap approximations common in real-world problems.  
- Assumes approximations are unbiased, which may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of MF-UCB's computational complexity.  
- Simulations are limited to a few scenarios, leaving generalization to broader settings unclear.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept for publication based on technical quality, clarity, and significance.
