- Proposes a multi-fidelity K-armed bandit formalism where each arm can be played at M fidelities with different costs and biased estimates of the highest fidelity.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm adapted to leverage cheaper, lower-fidelity approximations.  
- Proves regret bounds showing MF-UCB adapts to available approximations and costs and outperforms naive UCB strategies.  
- Provides a lower-bound analysis indicating MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Shows simulations where MF-UCB outperforms traditional UCB algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the problem and algorithm behavior.  
- Main contributions: novel multi-fidelity formalism, MF-UCB algorithm, and theoretical characterization of its exploration/exploitation use of fidelities.  
- Originality: introduces a novel framework and algorithm for using cheaper approximations in bandits.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (e.g., advertising, finance, robotics) and improves on prior approaches.  
- Related work: thorough literature review and discussion of previous limitations.  
- Weakness: assumes well-behaved reward distributions, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: analysis limited to fixed number of fidelities and experiments only on synthetic data (no real-world validation).
