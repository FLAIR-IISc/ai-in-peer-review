- Introduces the Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit problem where cheaper, noisier fidelities reduce evaluation cost.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB for multi-fidelity settings.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: regret bounds and a matching lower bound (near-optimality).  
- Motivated by applications like online advertising and algorithm selection where cheap evaluations guide exploration.  
- Core idea: use low-fidelity samples to prune suboptimal arms and focus expensive evaluations on promising arms.  
- Formalizes costs and approximation quality (ζ(m)) for different fidelities—valuable contribution.  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show MF-UCB outperforming standard UCB.  
- Writing and notation are cumbersome and sometimes confusing (e.g., variable ‘s’ defined after first use).  
- The online advertising motivation is misleading: discussed non-stationarity and correlations are not modeled in the formal framework.  
- Regret definition is problematic: optimizes absolute reward without accounting for evaluation cost, ignoring reward-per-cost objectives.  
- Choice of qt = µ(M)It feels arbitrary and not inherently cost-aware.  
- Analysis lacks practical focus: missing sample-complexity / cost-complexity analysis under budget constraints.  
- Reviewer recommends major revisions: improve clarity, refine regret/objective to be cost-aware, and include budgeted sample-complexity results.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and technically strong but presentation and problem formulation issues lead to a weak reject; encourage resubmission after substantial revision.
