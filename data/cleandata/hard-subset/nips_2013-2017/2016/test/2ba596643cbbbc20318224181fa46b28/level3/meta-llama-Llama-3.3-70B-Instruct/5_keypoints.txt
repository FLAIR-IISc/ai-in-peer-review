- Proposes a novel model-free framework for clustering graphs that gives "correctness" guarantees based on data statistics.  
- Presents a generic theorem: if one clustering fits the data well under a model, any other clustering that also fits well must be close to it.  
- Instantiates the theorem for the Stochastic Block Model (SBM) and Preference Frame Model (PFM), yielding model-free guarantees for these models.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough background on graphs, clusterings, and block models.  
- Main theorems and propositions are well-explained; proofs are provided in the supplement.  
- Uses elementary, direct proof techniques that improve accessibility.  
- Experimental evaluation demonstrates effectiveness on various datasets.  
- Weakness: assumes data are represented as a graph, limiting applicability to non-graph data.  
- Weakness: model-free guarantees may be weaker than stronger model-based guarantees.  
- Weakness: lacks clear comparison to existing model-based clustering work.  
- Recommendation: accept; framework is a significant contribution with useful model-free guarantees.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
