- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting motivated by online advertising and algorithm selection.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, a UCB-based algorithm adapted to multi-fidelity feedback; algorithm is clear and implementable.  
- Provides strong theoretical results: regret upper bound and a matching lower bound, showing near-optimality under assumptions.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB, supporting practical effectiveness.  
- Problem is practically relevant for scenarios with costly accurate feedback and cheaper approximations.  
- Major weakness: assumes known deviations ζ^(m) between fidelities, a strong assumption that limits real-world applicability.  
- Assumption 1 on the decay rate of ζ^(m) may be restrictive; sensitivity to violations is not analyzed.  
- Paper is technically sound and rigorous, but the known-ζ assumption detracts from practicality.  
- Generally well-written and organized with mostly consistent notation.  
- Highly original formulation combining multi-fidelity ideas with bandit problems; nontrivial theoretical contributions.  
- Potentially significant impact for applications with expensive feedback.  
- Minor typos and notation errors are listed (various line-numbered corrections provided).  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the known-ζ limitation in discussion and exploring relaxation avenues.
