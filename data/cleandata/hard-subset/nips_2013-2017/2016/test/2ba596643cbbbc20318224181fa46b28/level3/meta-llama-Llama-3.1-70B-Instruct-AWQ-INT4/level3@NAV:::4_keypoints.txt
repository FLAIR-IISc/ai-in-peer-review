- Proposes a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework where each arm has cheaper approximations to the target experiment.  
- Introduces MF-UCB, an algorithm that uses low-fidelity plays to eliminate poor arms and reserves costly high-fidelity queries for promising arms.  
- Provides a regret analysis and a lower bound, claiming MF-UCB is near-optimal.  
- Novel and interesting framework for leveraging cheaper approximations in bandits.  
- MF-UCB outperforms naive strategies that ignore multi-fidelity information.  
- Regret analysis and lower bound give a thorough understanding of algorithm performance.  
- Well-motivated problem with clear relevance to applications.  
- Notation is confusing and inconsistently presented, harming readability.  
- The advertising example is misleading and irrelevant references (display-time correlations, non-stationarity) should be removed.  
- Regret formulation is questionable because it ignores arm costs and treats the best arm as highest mean regardless of cost.  
- Writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining and clearer organization.  
- Suggested fixes: revise and standardize notation, replace the advertising example, incorporate cost into regret analysis, and improve writing clarity.  
- Conclusion: promising paper that could make a significant contribution after revisions.  
- Ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions.
