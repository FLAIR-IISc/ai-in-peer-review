- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity K-armed bandit setting where each arm has observations at multiple costs and accuracies, with lower fidelities providing cheaper biased estimates.  
- Main contribution is the MF-UCB algorithm, an adaptation of UCB to multi-fidelity observations.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: an upper bound for MF-UCB and a matching lower bound, showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Carefully handles bias from low-fidelity observations in the regret analysis.  
- Empirical simulations show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB across multiple synthetic problems.  
- Builds on prior MAB and UCB literature while distinguishing from multi-fidelity optimization and GP-based multi-fidelity bandits.  
- Paper is technically sound and the analysis is thorough and well-motivated.  
- Writing, organization, notation, and illustrations are clear and accessible to experts.  
- The multi-fidelity bandit framework is novel and conceptually advances exploration–exploitation study in fidelity settings.  
- The method is well-motivated by applications (e.g., online advertising, algorithm selection) and broadly applicable where cheap approximations exist.  
- Strength: novel formulation with strong theoretical guarantees and comprehensive upper/lower bound analysis.  
- Strength: clear empirical validation and excellent presentation.  
- Weakness: gap remains between MF-UCB and the lower bound for certain arms (K(m)7).  
- Weakness: lacks concrete real-world case studies despite mentioning applications.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis assumes known bias/cost functions ζ(m) and ψ(·), which may be restrictive in practice.  
- Pro acceptance: novel framework, strong theory, clear writing, and empirical support.  
- Con acceptance: some theoretical gap for specific arms, limited real-world validation, and restrictive assumptions on known fidelity parameters.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper, significant contribution, recommended for acceptance to NIPS 2016.
