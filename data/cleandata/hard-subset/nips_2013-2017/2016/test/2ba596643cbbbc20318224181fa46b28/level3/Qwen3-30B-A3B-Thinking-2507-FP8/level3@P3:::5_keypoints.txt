- Introduces the multi-fidelity K-armed bandit: arms can be evaluated at multiple fidelities with different costs and biases.  
- Formalizes regret for the multi-fidelity setting and frames cost-aware regret minimization.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an adaptation of UCB to exploit cheaper, lower-fidelity evaluations.  
- Provides theoretical regret bounds showing MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB when low fidelities are sufficiently cheap.  
- Presents a lower bound analysis demonstrating MF-UCB is nearly optimal under certain conditions.  
- Proofs and analysis are technically sound, well-structured, and built on clear assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1).  
- Empirical simulations corroborate theoretical gains: lower regret and more efficient budget use across synthetic problems.  
- Clearly distinguishes this work from related literature (budgeted bandits, prior multi-fidelity work) and cites relevant comparisons.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with a precise problem formulation and accessible presentation (effective figures like Fig.1).  
- Acknowledges and discusses limitations, including alternative regret formulations and open problems.  
- Weakness: assumes known ζ(m) biases between fidelities, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: a small gap remains in the lower bound analysis for certain arms (K(m)7), noted as an open issue.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed real-world case studies to strengthen practical significance beyond synthetic experiments.  
- Overall assessment: significant, novel contribution with strong theory and empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite minor limitations.
