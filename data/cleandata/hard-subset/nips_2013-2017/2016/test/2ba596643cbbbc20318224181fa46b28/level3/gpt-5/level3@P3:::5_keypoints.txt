- Introduces multi-fidelity (MF) stochastic K-armed bandits where each arm has cheaper, biased fidelities with known costs λ(m) and bias envelopes ζ(m) toward the highest-fidelity mean.  
- Optimizes a capital-weighted regret measuring missed highest-fidelity reward per unit cost spent, accounting for low-fidelity plays.  
- Proposes MF-UCB: per-fidelity UCBs adjusted by ζ(m), selects the arm with the tightest optimistic bound, and adaptively chooses the cheapest fidelity that meaningfully reduces uncertainty via thresholds γ(m).  
- Theorem 2: MF-UCB concentrates budget on low fidelities for clearly suboptimal arms and reserves high-fidelity plays for near-optimal arms, improving over classic UCB when cheap fidelities suffice.  
- Theorem 4: Provides a lower bound for Bernoulli rewards showing MF-UCB is nearly optimal except for a specific class of arms (K(m)7) with very small low-fidelity gaps.  
- Simulations on synthetic tasks corroborate theory: lower regret and few expensive plays for suboptimal arms.  
- Situates work relative to UCB bandits, budgeted bandits, multi-fidelity optimization, RL with simulators, and weak/strong labeler active learning; highlights novelty in biased fidelities and highest-fidelity regret.  
- Strength: Clear, compelling formulation with explicit bias envelopes ζ(m) and costs λ(m).  
- Strength: Simple, principled, computationally light algorithm combining per-fidelity UCBs and cost/bias-driven switching rule.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical analysis with fidelity-wise regret decomposition and matching lower bound under standard concentration.  
- Strength: Analysis controls play counts under capital budgets and provides clear conditions when MF-UCB may be loose.  
- Strength: Clear writing, explicit assumptions, and well-sketched proofs with detailed appendix.  
- Weakness: Requires known ζ(m) and ψ (or correct sub-Gaussian tail) and assumes a regular decay condition; limited practical guidance for estimating/robustifying these.  
- Weakness: No practical heuristic to mitigate the K(m)7 suboptimality where thresholding is conservative.  
- Weakness: Experiments are synthetic only; missing baselines (e.g., low-fidelity screening + high-fidelity UCB, Thompson sampling variants) and sensitivity analyses over ζ, λ, M.  
- Weakness: Regret notion may not cover settings where low-fidelity plays have intrinsic utility; broader empirical discussion is brief.  
- Suggestions: Study robustness to ζ misspecification, data-driven upper bounds, practical ψ choices (e.g., fixed sub-Gaussian), occasional low-fidelity validation for borderline arms, and add stronger baselines and sensitivity experiments.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid, novel, and significant; recommendation to accept with high confidence.
