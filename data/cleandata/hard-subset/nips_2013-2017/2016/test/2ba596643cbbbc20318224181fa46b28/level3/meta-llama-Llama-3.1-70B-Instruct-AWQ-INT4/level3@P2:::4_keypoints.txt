- Introduces a Multi-fidelity Multi-armed Bandit framework where cheaper, biased approximations to an experiment exist.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an upper-confidence-bound algorithm that adapts to available approximations and costs.  
- Shows MF-UCB can achieve better regret than naive strategies that ignore approximations.  
- Formalizes playing an arm at one of M fidelities, with higher fidelity costing more and approximations returning biased estimates.  
- Provides regret analysis and a lower bound, with synthetic experiments where MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Notation is confusing and dispersed (e.g., variable s defined after first use and scarcely used), harming readability.  
- The advertising example is misleading; references to display time correlations and non-stationarity are inappropriate for the paper’s scope.  
- Regret analysis is questionable because the paper treats the best arm as highest mean regardless of cost, potentially ignoring low-fidelity sampling costs.  
- Reviewer recommends a sample-complexity (cost-complexity) analysis focused on finding the best arm under budget/constraints as more appropriate for real-world testing.  
- Writing is unwieldy in parts and needs streamlining, clearer organization, and improved notation.  
- Paper is novel and potentially impactful, especially for applications like online advertising and clinical trials.  
- Recommendation: significant revisions required — reorganize, clarify notation, avoid misleading examples, and add cost-complexity analysis.  
- Overall rating given: 6/10.
