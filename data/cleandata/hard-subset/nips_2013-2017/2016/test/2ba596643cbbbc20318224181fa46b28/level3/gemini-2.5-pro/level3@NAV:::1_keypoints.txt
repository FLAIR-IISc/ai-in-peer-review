- Studies multi-armed bandits with multiple fidelity levels per arm where lower fidelities are cheaper but biased; novel and practically relevant setting.
- Proposes MF-UCB, an extension of UCB that uses cheap fidelities to quickly eliminate suboptimal arms.
- Provides technically solid theoretical analysis including a regret upper bound for MF-UCB and a corresponding lower bound.
- Core idea—using low-cost approximations for initial exploration—is intuitive and significant for expensive experimentation domains.
- Presentation lacks clarity; main results (especially Theorem 2 and its comparison to standard UCB) are hard to parse due to complex dependence on sets K^(m).
- Modeling choices are questionable: multiplicative penalization of cost in the regret is unnatural and should be compared to additive cost models.
- Motivation is overly reliant on an ad-display example, leaving generalizability to other domains unclear.
- Assumption 1 (constraining decay of fidelity errors) seems artificial; authors should justify its generality or discuss selecting fidelities to satisfy it.
- Algorithm design appears tightly coupled to Assumption 1, raising concerns about performance under different conditions.
- The gap between the provided regret bound and the lower bound needs further investigation and may indicate a fundamental limitation.
- Minor issues: ambiguous use of "near-optimal" (l.56), unnecessary period (l.126), and inconsistent set notation (l.179).
- Overall: important problem and solid analysis but requires substantial revisions to improve clarity and justify modeling assumptions before acceptance.
