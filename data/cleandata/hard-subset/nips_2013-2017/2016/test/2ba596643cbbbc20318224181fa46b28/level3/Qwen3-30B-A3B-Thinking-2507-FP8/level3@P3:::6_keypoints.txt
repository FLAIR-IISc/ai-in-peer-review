- Introduces a novel multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit setting where each arm has M fidelities with higher fidelities giving better but costlier reward estimates.  
- Formalizes a new cost-aware regret definition that accounts for varying fidelity costs.  
- Proposes MF-UCB, an algorithm that adaptively uses cheaper lower fidelities to eliminate suboptimal arms before investing in expensive fidelities.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: upper bound on MF-UCB regret showing improvements over naive UCB when lower fidelities are sufficiently cheap and promising arms are few.  
- Complements the upper bound with a lower bound demonstrating near-optimality of MF-UCB under certain conditions.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic problems supports the theoretical claims.  
- Main contribution: formalization and theoretical analysis of multi-fidelity bandits, filling a gap in prior multi-fidelity work that lacked regret/BE trade-off analysis.  
- Major limitation: the algorithm assumes known deviations ε^(m) (max difference between mth-fidelity and highest-fidelity means), which is impractical and not addressed how to estimate or relax.  
- Several minor technical errors to fix (lines and corrections noted by reviewer), e.g., index/notation mistakes at lines 326, 343, 345, 346–347, 357, 384, 386, 407, 431, 440/Eq.(17), 450.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, original, and significant with strong theoretical/empirical support, but practical impact weakened by the known-ε^(m) assumption.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to correct technical errors and strengthen discussion/handling of the ε^(m) assumption.
