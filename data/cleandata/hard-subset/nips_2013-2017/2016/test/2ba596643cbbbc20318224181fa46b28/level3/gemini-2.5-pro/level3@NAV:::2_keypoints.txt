- Introduces multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit: arms can be pulled at varying fidelities with higher fidelity giving better estimates at higher cost.  
- Proposes a cost-weighted regret metric that balances pseudo-regret with capital spent.  
- Presents MF-UCB, a UCB-style algorithm that uses cheap low-fidelity pulls to eliminate suboptimal arms and expensive high-fidelity pulls for promising ones.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with upper bounds for MF-UCB and matching lower bounds, showing near-optimality under certain conditions.  
- Major strength: significant, original, and well-motivated extension of bandits with intuitive algorithmic ideas.  
- Main paper is clear and well-written, making core concepts easy to follow.  
- Weakness: appendix/proofs contain many typos and minor errors (e.g., variable swaps like H^(m)/K^(m), incorrect constants ν/(ρ-2) vs ν/2), impeding verification.  
- Theoretical bounds presentation is unclear due to "up-to-a-multiplicative-constant" notation hiding algorithm parameters (like ρ); exact inequalities are needed.  
- Practical limitation: MF-UCB requires knowledge of fidelity parameters ζ, and the paper lacks discussion on robustness to misestimated ζ and mitigation strategies.
