- Introduces a multi-fidelity multi-armed bandit framework and proposes an adaptive MF-UCB algorithm for arms sampled at varying fidelity/costs.  
- Formalizes a cost-weighted regret measure and provides theoretical guarantees including a lower bound.  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show MF-UCB outperforms standard UCB.  
- Meaningful extension of classical bandit literature to multi-fidelity/resource-constrained settings.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, technically sound analysis, and promising experimental performance.  
- Notation is confusing and poorly organized (e.g., variable 's' defined after first use and rarely used), harming readability.  
- The advertising motivating example is misleading and references irrelevant concepts like "display time correlations" and "non-stationarity."  
- Critical flaw: regret analysis treats the optimal arm as highest mean reward irrespective of cost, contradicting the cost-aware problem setup.  
- Suggestion: replace or supplement regret analysis with a sample-/cost-complexity analysis focused on finding the best arm under budget constraints (reward/cost ratio).  
- Writing is unwieldy and needs significant streamlining, clearer organization, and more precise terminology.  
- Major revisions required (revise regret formulation, clarify example, reorganize presentation) before being suitable for publication.
