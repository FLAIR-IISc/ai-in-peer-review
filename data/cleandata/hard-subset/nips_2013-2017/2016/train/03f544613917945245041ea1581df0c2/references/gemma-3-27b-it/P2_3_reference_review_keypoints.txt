- Introduces SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two SGLD chains to reduce bias and improve SG-MCMC accuracy.
- Theoretical analysis shows improved MSE convergence rate (O(K^-4/5) vs O(K^-2/3) for SGLD) with convergence and bias bounds.
- Novelty: first adaptation of RR extrapolation to the stochastic SG-MCMC setting, a significant methodological contribution.
- Practical relevance: algorithm is parallelizable (independent chains) and shows empirical gains in large-scale matrix factorization.
- Generality: RR extrapolation approach can extend to other SG-MCMC methods like SGHMC.
- Presentation: generally clear and well-organized, with proof sketches and detailed supplement.
- Weakness — implementation: lacks sufficient guidance on choosing step sizes and the correlation parameter Σ.
- Weakness — experiments: experimental setup omits detailed justification for chosen step sizes and lacks comprehensive ablation studies.
- Weakness — technical conditions: theoretical guarantees depend on several technical assumptions whose practicality is unclear.
- Weakness — limited comparisons: evaluation should include more recent SG-MCMC and variance-reduction baselines beyond SGHMC.
- Quality score: 8/10 — technically strong but needs more empirical sensitivity analysis.
- Clarity score: 7/10 — mostly clear but some implementation notation and choices need elaboration.
- Originality score: 9/10 — highly original idea.
- Significance score: 8/10 — meaningful potential impact for large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting added implementation guidance, broader experimental comparisons, and clarification of theoretical assumptions.
