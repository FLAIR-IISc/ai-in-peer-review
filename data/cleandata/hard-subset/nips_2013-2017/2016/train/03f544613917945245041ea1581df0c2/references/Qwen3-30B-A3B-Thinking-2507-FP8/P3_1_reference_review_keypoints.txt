- Introduces SGRRLD: run two parallel SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine outputs via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Uses correlated Brownian increments to control variance when extrapolating between chains.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³), supported by CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets corroborate the theoretical improvements.  
- Builds on SGLD, SG-MCMC theory, and RR extrapolation for SDEs, extending RR to stochastic-gradient settings.  
- Strength: rigorous and thorough theoretical analysis covering both bias and variance components.  
- Strength: well-motivated connection between RR extrapolation and SG-MCMC with careful treatment of variance via correlation.  
- Strength: experiments include synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization showing practical gains.  
- Weakness: does not analyze computational cost of running two chains (wall-clock time and memory) relative to standard SG-MCMC.  
- Weakness: assumption that both chains must use the same mini-batch size is restrictive and unexplored.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other bias-reduction approaches (e.g., higher-order integrators, multi-step methods).  
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions on U, f, L are not discussed in terms of practical restrictiveness.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and structured with consistent notation and clear contributions.  
- Clarity issues: some proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) are dense and need more intuition or examples.  
- Clarity issues: implementation details about how the two chains interact and parameter choices (step sizes) are under-explained.  
- Clarity issues: heavy reliance on supplementary material, which is not provided in the review.  
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and extension to SGHMC, filling a clear gap.  
- Originality caveat: insufficient discussion of related ideas like multi-level Monte Carlo or stochastic-optimization literature.  
- Significance: meaningful theoretical MSE improvement that could impact large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Significance caveat: practical gains may be offset by computational overhead, tuning difficulty, and increased memory for two chains.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel but needs more analysis of computational trade-offs, comparisons, and practical limitations.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision with requested additions on computational cost analysis, mini-batch-size impact, comparisons to other bias-reduction methods, and clearer experimental details.
