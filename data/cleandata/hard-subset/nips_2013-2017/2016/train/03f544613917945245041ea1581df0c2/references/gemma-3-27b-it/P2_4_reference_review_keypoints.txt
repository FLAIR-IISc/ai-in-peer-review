- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two Langevin chains with different step sizes and combining results to reduce SGLD bias.
- Theoretical claim: shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K^-2/3), matching higher-order integrator accuracy with a first-order integrator.
- Practical angle: method is amenable to parallel computation and demonstrated on synthetic Gaussians and large-scale matrix factorization.
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is an original extension of a numerical-analysis technique to Bayesian inference.
- Theoretical guarantees: provides rigorous convergence analysis and bias/MSE bounds supporting the improved rate.
- Generality: authors argue RR extrapolation can be applied to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written and organized, with mathematical derivations (some details in supplement).
- Complexity concern: implementation requires two chains and correlated Brownian increments, adding practical complexity.
- Step-size sensitivity: optimal performance depends on specific step-size choices, which may need careful tuning in practice.
- Experimental scope limited: empirical gains on the real-world task are modest and broader evaluation across datasets/models is lacking.
- Assumptions: theoretical results rely on smoothness and gradient-estimator properties; limitations and practical impact of these assumptions aren’t fully discussed.
- Limited baselines: comparisons against state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods are insufficient beyond SGHMC-s.
- Quality assessment: technically sound and complete, but hyperparameter sensitivity and assumptions temper confidence.
- Clarity assessment: generally clear, though the correlated Brownian increments and their intuition/implementation could be explained better.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting more discussion on step-size selection, expanded experiments, assumption limitations, and implementation guidance for correlated increments.
