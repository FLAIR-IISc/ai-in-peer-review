- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to reduce bias and variance.  
- Claims improved asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) versus SGLD’s O(γ) and O(γ²), and MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds with detailed proofs.  
- Method is parallelizable since the two chains can be run independently, beneficial for distributed hardware.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and MovieLens matrix factorization supports theoretical claims and shows accuracy gains per wall-clock time.  
- Technique is generalizable to other SG-MCMC methods (e.g., SGHMC) and evidence for SGRRHMC is provided.  
- Paper mischaracterizes computational cost: claiming similar wall-clock time is confusing without clarifying parallel resource assumptions and doubled total CPU cost.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison with higher-order SG-MCMC methods (e.g., Chen et al., 2015) that could achieve similar rates.  
- Does not discuss memory overhead of running two chains, which may be prohibitive for very large models.  
- Theoretical results rely on technical assumptions (smoothness, Poisson equation solvability) without discussion of realism for complex models like deep nets.  
- Provides theoretical step-size choices (γ ∝ K⁻¹/⁵) but offers little practical guidance for choosing γ in real applications.  
- Description of chain scheduling (Equation 5) is confusing—needs to clarify that the γ/2 chain takes 2K steps for K steps of the γ chain.  
- Justification for using perfectly correlated Brownian increments (Equation 7) is inadequately connected to the stochastic gradient setting and needs better explanation.  
- Mentions multi-level Monte Carlo as future work but fails to compare potential efficiency relative to the RR approach.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with practical implications, but computational trade-offs need clarification.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address clarification and comparison concerns.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify computational cost/wall-clock trade-offs, add comparisons with higher-order methods, discuss memory scalability, give practical step-size guidance, and clarify RR extrapolation implementation details.
