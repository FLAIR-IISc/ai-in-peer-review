- Proposes SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical claims: improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K^-2/3), with detailed analysis of Brownian motion correlations.  
- Positions work relative to prior SG-MCMC and SDE extrapolation literature, citing Talay & Tubaro and Lemaire & Pagès.  
- Novelty: the RR extrapolation idea applied to SG-MCMC is original and bridges numerical analysis with Bayesian inference.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides convergence theorems and bias/MSE bounds supporting improved rates.  
- Experiments: synthetic and real-data (matrix factorization) results show empirical gains and effective parallel use.  
- Generality: method can potentially extend to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and well-organized, with accessible equations and exposition.  
- Practical/scalability concern: running two correlated chains in parallel may incur nontrivial communication overhead not fully analyzed.  
- Step-size sensitivity: performance depends on chosen step sizes (γ and γ/2) and the paper lacks practical guidance or extensive sensitivity analyses.  
- Assumptions: theoretical results rely on technical weak error and ergodicity conditions, with limited discussion on robustness if violated.  
- Limited comparisons: evaluation omits some recent optimized SG-MCMC variants and variational methods for stronger baselines.  
- Minor clarity issue: equation defining correlated Brownian increments (Eq. 8) is somewhat opaque and needs clearer explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, convincing experiments, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about parallel overhead, step-size sensitivity, and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment & recommendation: strong paper with minor revisions requested (address scalability and step-size guidance); recommendation: Accept.
