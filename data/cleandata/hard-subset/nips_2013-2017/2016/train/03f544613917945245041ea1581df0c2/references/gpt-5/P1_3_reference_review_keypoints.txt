- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to two parallel SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to cancel leading discretization bias.
- Uses correlated Gaussian increments across chains to control variance; theory shows perfect correlation (Σ = I) minimizes asymptotic variance.
- Provides theoretical results: CLT for the extrapolated estimator, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and finite-time convergence rates.
- With first-order integrator, achieves O(γ^2) bias, O(γ^4) variance, and MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}) with optimal γ ∝ K^{-1/5}, improving over O(K^{-2/3}) for SGLD.
- Discusses decreasing step sizes with optimal decay α = 1/5.
- Demonstrates empirical gains on a Gaussian model (closed-form) and probabilistic matrix factorization (MovieLens), and shows RR applied to SGHMC (SGRRHMC).
- Builds on prior SGLD, higher-order SG-MCMC integrators, and RR extrapolation for SDEs; notes connections to MLMC.
- Strength: clear, principled idea that cancels leading discretization bias without higher-order integrators.
- Strength: solid theoretical development tailored to stochastic gradients and rate optimization.
- Strength: actionable insight on variance control via correlated noise and generality to other SG-MCMC schemes.
- Strength: practical parallel implementation can yield wall-clock gains on multicore machines.
- Experiments align with theory on synthetic data and show wall-clock RMSE improvements on MovieLens.
- Weakness: analysis relies on strong smoothness and ergodicity assumptions (e.g., g ∈ C^9, dissipativity) with limited discussion of robustness to violations.
- Weakness: unclear practical importance of aligning stochastic gradient noise (shared mini-batches) across chains; ablation missing.
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to Gaussian/posterior-like problems; lacks tests on non-log-concave or neural models (e.g., logistic regression, BNNs).
- Weakness: baselines incomplete—should compare to higher-order and variance-reduced SG-MCMC (e.g., Ma et al. 2015, pSGLD) and include SGHMC on large tasks.
- Weakness: fairness concern—wall-clock parity assumes parallel resources; need comparison under fixed total gradient budget and discussion of memory/communication overheads.
- Weakness: experiments mainly use constant step sizes despite theory on decreasing schedules; more practical tuning guidance and pseudo-code are requested.
- Minor issues: typos/formatting, notation clarity (e.g., noise scaling), and supplement reliance for conditions/proofs.
- Overall quality: technically sound with careful proofs; main claims supported but assumptions are strong.
- Clarity: well-written and organized, though some dependence on supplement; suggest adding pseudo-code and ablation on correlation choices.
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and variance control via correlated increments.
- Significance: simple, generic, parallelizable method that could meaningfully improve SG-MCMC if broadly adopted.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, simple, solid theory, practical gains, bridges RR to SG-MCMC.
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical diversity, strong assumptions, unclear single-threaded costs.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to different mini-batches across chains; performance on logistic/ non-convex models; feasibility of multi-step RR; comparison under single-threaded fixed compute.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (7/10) — strong theory and implementable idea but needs expanded empirical validation and baseline comparisons.
