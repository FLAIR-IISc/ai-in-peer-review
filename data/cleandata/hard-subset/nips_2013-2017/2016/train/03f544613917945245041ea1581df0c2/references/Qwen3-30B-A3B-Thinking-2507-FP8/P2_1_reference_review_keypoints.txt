- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to parallel SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Shows SGRRLD attains bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem (Theorem 1) and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds (Theorem 2).  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussians and large-scale matrix factorization validate theoretical claims and show accuracy gains with comparable wall-clock time.  
- Builds on SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation literature, making a novel contribution by applying RR to SG-MCMC.  
- Method is parallelizable and can be applied to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents algorithm, theory, and experiments logically.  
- Minor clarity gap: computational cost of running two chains in parallel isn’t explicitly quantified (synchronization/overhead details).  
- Technical caveat: theoretical results rely on assumptions about U, f, and L that may be hard to verify in practice.  
- Limited exploration of very high-dimensional problems beyond matrix factorization.  
- Strengths: novel idea, rigorous theory, strong empirical support, practical parallelizability, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational cost, technical assumptions, and scope on extreme high-dimensional settings.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
