- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, running two SGLD chains at γ and γ/2 with RR extrapolation and correlated Brownian noise to reduce discretization bias and variance.  
- Theoretical results: CLT under decreasing stepsizes with optimal full Brownian correlation, non-asymptotic bias O(γ2+1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ4+1/(Kγ)), yielding MSE rate O(K−4/5).  
- Experiments: Synthetic Gaussians (including SGHMC) and MovieLens matrix factorization show lower bias/MSE and better wall-clock RMSE when chains run in parallel.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SGLD/SG-MCMC and classical RR for SDEs; novelty is applying RR to stochastic-gradient setting with CLT and finite-time bounds.  
- Quality — theory: CLT and bounds plausible and consistent with ULA/RR literature, but many strong technical assumptions are deferred to the supplement (e.g., C9 Poisson solution).  
- Quality — experiments: Empirical results support theory and parallel speedups, but additional baselines and ablations are needed.  
- Clarity issues: Minor typos and needed clarifications (Eq. (5) typo, RR estimator derivation, main-text statement of core assumptions, practical implementation of Brownian correlation).  
- Originality: Novel application of RR to SG-MCMC and proof that Brownian correlation remains optimal; extension to SGHMC increases generality.  
- Significance: Shows RR can upgrade first-order SG-MCMC to match higher-order rates and offers a simple, easily parallelizable practical method.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Regularity assumptions are heavy; applicability beyond strongly log-concave/posteriors needs discussion.  
- Weakness — gradient noise: Bounds depend on gradient-noise moments; paper should discuss batch-size effects and possible mini-batch coupling across chains.  
- Weakness — fairness of timing: Need single-core comparisons matched by gradient evaluations since SGRRLD does ≈2× gradient work in serial.  
- Weakness — baselines: Real-data comparisons should include pSGLD, SGNHT/thermostats, other higher-order integrators, and optimization baselines.  
- Weakness — task diversity: Add non-Gaussian/nonconjugate tasks (e.g., logistic regression) to test robustness.  
- Minor suggestions: Add main-text pseudo-code, practical guidance for γ/α selection, explain Brownian-implementation details, and include confidence intervals in plots.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, well-motivated idea with solid theory, improved rates, broad applicability, and supporting experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: Deferred/strong assumptions, limited experimental baselines and single-core fairness, and presentation/typo issues.  
- Typos and nits: Specific typos (“variace”, “Futher”, Eq. (5) error, etc.); clarify mini-batch matching/reuse recommendations.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity, assumption discussion, and stronger experimental comparisons.
