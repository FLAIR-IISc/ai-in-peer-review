- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias and control variance in SG-MCMC.  
- Extends RR extrapolation theory to stochastic gradient settings, with careful variance control via correlated Brownian motions.  
- Theoretical results include a CLT and non-asymptotic bounds showing MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD's O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order accuracy.  
- Method is parallelizable, demonstrated to achieve similar accuracy to SGLD in roughly half the wall-clock time in large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Synthetic experiments show large bias reduction (~10⁻² to ~10⁻⁴) and order-of-magnitude MSE improvement across dimensions and step sizes.  
- Real-data MovieLens matrix factorization experiments provide practical evidence of utility.  
- Generality claim supported by adapting RR extrapolation to SGHMC (SGRRHMC).  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical contribution with well-motivated proofs extending prior RR results.  
- Strength: practical significance via parallel implementation beneficial for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: novelty needs clearer distinction between theoretical extension and practical/implementation contributions.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details on coordinating two chains, communication overhead, and justification of wall-clock time claims.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis on the chosen step-size ratio (γ vs γ/2) to justify optimality.  
- Weakness: lacks direct empirical comparison of total computational cost versus higher-order integrator methods.  
- Weakness: correlation strategy (Σ=Id) is theoretically justified but not empirically validated against alternatives.  
- Suggestions: clarify novelty, provide parallel implementation/overhead details, include step-size sensitivity analysis, and compare total computational cost with higher-order methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, strong theory and experiments, significant contribution to scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
