- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (γ and γ/2) with correlated Gaussian noise to cancel O(γ) weak bias.  
- Theoretically proves a CLT (variance minimized with perfectly correlated Brownian increments) and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) → optimal K^(-4/5) MSE rate.  
- Shows SGRRLD matches the theoretical accuracy of some higher-order integrators while using only Euler steps and is amenable to parallelism.  
- Extends the approach to SGHMC (SGRRHMC) and compares favorably to a higher-order SGHMC integrator at larger step sizes.  
- Empirical results: synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments verify theory; large-scale matrix factorization on MovieLens shows improved RMSE at matched wall-clock time.  
- Novelty: first systematic application and analysis of RR extrapolation and coupled noise for SG-MCMC/posterior sampling.  
- Strength: conceptual simplicity — minimal change yields one-order weak-bias improvement and improved finite-time MSE with clear step-schedule optimization (α = 1/5).  
- Strength: careful asymptotic and finite-time analysis, including CLT and explicit bounds; variance-minimizing coupling is well motivated.  
- Weakness: strong technical assumptions (e.g., need solution of Poisson equation g ∈ C^9) are deferred to supplement and need clearer discussion in main text.  
- Weakness: gradient-noise modeling assumes unbiased gradients with bounded moments; sensitivity to state-dependent noise, high variance, or poor conditioning is not clarified.  
- Weakness: coupling of stochastic gradients across chains (shared vs independent mini-batches) is not specified; no ablation of mini-batch sharing or correlated vs independent Gaussian noise.  
- Weakness: empirical fairness concerns — comparisons should include fixed gradient-evaluation budgets and higher-order SGLD baselines; more diverse posteriors (e.g., logistic regression, BNN) needed.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning details, burn-in, batch sizes, and code release are limited, hindering reproducibility.  
- Clarity: generally well organized, but main text should summarize key assumptions/CLT statements and include pseudocode for practitioners.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, original, and significant; recommendation: Accept (weak to clear accept); reviewer confidence: high.
