- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two Langevin chains with different step sizes and extrapolating results.
- Theoretical claim: improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K^-2/3), with CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.
- Shows SGRRLD can match higher-order integrator accuracy while using a first-order integrator.
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization supports theoretical claims and shows improved wall-clock performance.
- Positions work well in SG-MCMC literature, citing SGLD, SGHMC, Chen et al., and prior RR applications to SDEs.
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a significant contribution.
- Theoretical rigor: careful analysis, central limit theorem, and explicit convergence guarantees are strengths.
- Presentation: clear explanation of concepts and concise algorithm description.
- Parallelizability: two independent chains make the method naturally parallel-friendly.
- Generality: approach is applicable beyond SGLD to other SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Practical overhead: running two chains doubles work in principle and constant-factor costs are not fully analyzed.
- Step-size tuning: practical guidance and a broader empirical exploration of step-size choices are lacking.
- Gradient correlation: discussion of correlated stochastic gradients is superficial and effects of imperfect correlation are not well explored.
- Limited baselines: missing comparisons to variance-reduction SG-MCMC methods (e.g., SAGA, SVRG) and other recent state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Minor presentation issues: occasional notation inconsistencies should be fixed.
- Recommendation: overall strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: quantify computational overhead, investigate gradient-correlation strategies, compare to more SG-MCMC baselines, and clean up notation.
