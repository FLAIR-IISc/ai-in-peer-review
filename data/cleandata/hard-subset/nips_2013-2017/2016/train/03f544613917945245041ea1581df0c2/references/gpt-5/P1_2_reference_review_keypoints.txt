- Proposes SGRRLD: run two coupled SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and extrapolate ergodic averages using correlated Gaussian noise to cancel leading discretization bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: CLT for RR estimator with minimal variance via optimal Brownian coupling (Σ = I), asymptotic bias O(γ^2) and variance O(γ^4), and non-asymptotic bounds yielding optimal MSE rate O(K−4/5).  
- Claims to match accuracy of higher-order integrators while retaining a simple first-order integrator and enabling parallel execution.  
- Empirical results on a Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization show improved accuracy and wall-clock efficiency; SGRRHMC similarly improves over SGHMC.  
- Builds on classic SDE RR extrapolation and SG-MCMC literature, extending RR ideas to noisy-gradient settings and emphasizing optimal Gaussian increment coupling.  
- Theory is sound and follows established Poisson-equation/Taylor-expansion strategies; derived step-size exponents and rates are clear.  
- Assumptions are strong (e.g., g ∈ C9, technical smoothness/ergodicity), which may limit applicability to non-convex or deep-model posteriors.  
- Implementation details under-specified for stochastic-gradient coupling; paper notes sharing L and minibatch size but gives no concrete minibatch-coupling scheme.  
- Minor technical/notation issues: likely typo in Eq. (5) regarding half-step gradient, and inconsistency in ULA noise scaling (√γ vs √(2γ)).  
- Clarity is generally good but notation is heavy; including pseudocode and a brief “recipe” box for the estimator and coupling choices would aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation with explicit variance control in SG-MCMC; extension to SGHMC adds generality.  
- Significance: simple, generic, parallelizable method with meaningful finite-time rate improvements that are practically useful when parallel resources are available.  
- Weaknesses/questions: lack of concrete minibatch-coupling recommendations, fairness of wall-clock comparisons under matched compute, robustness under weaker assumptions, and sensitivity to constants/step-size/batch-size.  
- Suggested improvements: provide explicit minibatch-coupling guidance, fix Eq. (5) and ULA noise scaling, include main-text pseudocode and RNG-reuse tips, add compute-normalized experiments and decreasing-step-size runs, and discuss extensions/limitations.  
- Pros: simple and practical, solid theory, effective variance control via Brownian coupling, parallel-friendly, extends to SGHMC.  
- Cons: strong theoretical assumptions, missing practical minibatch-coupling guidance, fairness concerns in comparisons, minor typos.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity, implementation guidance, and experimental fairness concerns.
