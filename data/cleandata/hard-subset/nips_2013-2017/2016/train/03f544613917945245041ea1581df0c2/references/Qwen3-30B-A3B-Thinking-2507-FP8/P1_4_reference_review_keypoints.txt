- Introduces SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretical result: SGRRLD achieves MSE convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Practical algorithm: provides implementation details including correlated Brownian motions to control variance.  
- Experiments: synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization tasks validate theory; SGRRLD outperforms SGLD and SGHMC in accuracy and efficiency.  
- Parallelization: two chains can run independently, making the method suitable for distributed architectures.  
- Organization: paper is well-structured with clear definitions and sufficient supplementary technical detail.  
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is novel and extends RR SDE work to stochastic gradients.  
- Needs clearer novelty framing: should better distinguish from prior work and explain why RR matches higher-order integrators without added complexity.  
- Mini-batch constraint: requiring identical mini-batch sizes for both chains is stated but not thoroughly justified, potentially limiting applicability.  
- Computational cost analysis missing: lacks quantitative per-iteration cost comparison between SGRRLD and SGLD, despite potential doubling of resources.  
- Constant factors not discussed: paper claims matching theoretical accuracy of higher-order methods but does not compare constant factors in convergence rates.  
- Quality: technically sound, rigorous convergence analysis, and careful variance handling.  
- Clarity: well-written with clear notation; could use more intuitive explanation of RR extrapolation’s variance control via Brownian correlation.  
- Significance: convergence improvement and parallelizability are important for large-scale Bayesian inference; experiments show practical impact.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theoretical and practical contributions.
