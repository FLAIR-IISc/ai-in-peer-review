- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining outputs.
- Theoretical results: SGRRLD attains bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Rigorous analysis with clear theorems (Theorems 1 and 2) and supporting proofs.
- Some proof details are deferred to supplementary materials, making full assessment harder without the appendix.
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussians and large-scale matrix factorization show consistent practical gains and about half the time to reach SGLD accuracy.
- Notes limitation that for very small step sizes bias and MSE can increase due to the 1/(Kγ) term dominating error bounds.
- Computational overhead of running two parallel chains is mentioned as parallelizable but lacks detailed analysis.
- Well-written and organized with consistent notation; could use more intuitive explanation of RR extrapolation interaction and Brownian increment correlation (Eq. 8).
- Original and significant: first use of RR extrapolation for SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order convergence with first-order integrators and extendable to methods like SGHMC.
- Opens promising directions (recursive RR extrapolation, multi-level Monte Carlo) and has substantial practical relevance for large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance for its theoretical rigor, novelty, and empirical validation.
- Strengths: novel idea, rigorous theory, strong experiments, practical impact; Weaknesses: deferred technical details, limited overhead discussion, could compare more baselines.
