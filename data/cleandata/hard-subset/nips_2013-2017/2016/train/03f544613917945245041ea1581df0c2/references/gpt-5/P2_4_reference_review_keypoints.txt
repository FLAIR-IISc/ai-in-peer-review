- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining time-averaged estimators.  
- Controls variance via correlated Gaussian noise across chains, with perfectly correlated Brownian increments being optimal.  
- Theoretical contributions: CLT with minimal asymptotic variance under perfect correlation; finite-time bias O(γ2 + 1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ4 + 1/(Kγ)) leading to optimal MSE rate O(K−4/5); rates for decreasing step sizes and optimal exponents.  
- Experiments (Gaussian model, SGRRHMC, MovieLens) support theory and show wall-clock gains when parallelizing the two chains.  
- Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with stochastic gradients and providing asymptotic and non-asymptotic analysis is novel relative to prior SDE/RR and SG-MCMC work.  
- Analysis aligns with known weak-error expansions and yields plausible CLT and finite-time bounds, but relies on strong assumptions (high regularity Poisson solution, bounded gradient moments, ergodicity).  
- The paper should make precise (in main text) the scaling/conditions where injected Gaussian noise dominates stochastic gradient noise.  
- The result that Σ = Id (perfect correlation) minimizes asymptotic variance is consistent with multilevel/RR literature and is actionable.  
- Finite-time bias/MSE bounds and γ–K tradeoffs are practically valuable and clearly discussed.  
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted rates; MovieLens shows parallel wall-clock benefit; SGHMC extension is promising but lacks theoretical justification here.  
- Key assumptions are mainly in the supplement and should be summarized in the main text (regularity, gradient moment conditions, ergodicity).  
- Possible typo in Chain 2 updates (Eq. (5)) that appears semi-implicit; authors should clarify/correct to Euler–Maruyama form.  
- Clarify whether the two chains must use the same minibatch size and gradient estimator law L, whether minibatches are shared across substeps/chains, and how sharing affects variance/unbiasedness.  
- Core idea is original for SG-MCMC: achieve higher-order-like rates using RR without higher-order integrators.  
- Method is simple, generic, combinable with other SG-MCMC variants, and most beneficial when parallel resources are available.  
- Serial use doubles gradient work per horizon, so include serial runtime comparisons or explicitly state the method assumes extra cores.  
- Empirical claims could be strengthened by comparisons to stronger SG-MCMC baselines (e.g., pSGLD, SGNHT).  
- Strengths: principled RR adaptation, asymptotic CLT and finite-time bounds, practical variance-control advice, consistent empirical improvements, and extension to SGHMC.  
- Weaknesses: strong/hidden assumptions, Eq. (5) typo, unclear robustness when stochastic gradient noise dominates, fairness of parallel comparisons, lack of ablations (Σ correlation, minibatch sharing), and missing SGHMC theory.  
- Questions for authors: Do you share minibatches across substeps/chains? How sensitive are gains to batch size? What are memory/communication costs for large parameters on separate devices? Can RR be applied recursively without destabilizing variance?  
- Minor issues: fix Eq. (5) typo and add brief pseudocode in the main text for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, timely, and practically useful contribution—theoretically sound under standard but strong assumptions, simple to implement, empirically beneficial; recommend acceptance pending clarifications and fixes.
