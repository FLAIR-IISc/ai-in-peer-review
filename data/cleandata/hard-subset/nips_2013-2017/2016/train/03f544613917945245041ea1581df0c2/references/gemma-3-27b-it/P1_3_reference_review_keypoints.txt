- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains and correlated Brownian motions to reduce bias vs SGLD.  
- Theoretical claim: Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) compared to SGLD’s O(K^-2/3), with CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Empirical claim: Experiments on synthetic Gaussians and a matrix factorization task support improved accuracy and comparable wall-clock performance.  
- Related work: Correctly situates within SG-MCMC literature (SGLD, higher-order integrators) and connects to Richardson-Romberg extrapolation; application to SG-MCMC is novel.  
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: Provides rigorous analysis including central limit theorem and explicit convergence rates.  
- Strength — Novelty: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and use of correlated Brownian motions to control variance.  
- Strength — Generality: Claims applicability beyond SGLD (demonstrated partially with SGHMC).  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Initial experiments show performance gains on both synthetic and real-world tasks.  
- Strength — Presentation: Core algorithm and notation are mostly clear and formally defined.  
- Weakness — Technical complexity: Analysis is dense, relies on many assumptions, and may be hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness — Experimental scope: Limited datasets and models; lacks extensive evaluations and ablation on chain correlation.  
- Weakness — Computational overhead: Running two chains and maintaining correlation costs are not thoroughly analyzed or scaled.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Insufficient discussion and guidance on tuning step sizes and sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Weakness — Clarity issues: Explanation of correlated gradients and the requirement for equal mini-batch sizes needs better motivation and clarity.  
- For acceptance: Strong theoretical contribution, improved convergence rate, supportive empirical results, and promising generality.  
- Against acceptance: Dense theory may limit accessibility, experiments lack breadth, computational costs and tuning guidance are under-explored.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, novel contribution with strong theory and encouraging experiments; weaknesses are addressable and do not invalidate the contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Suggested revisions: Expand experiments and scalability tests, provide detailed computational cost analysis, add parameter tuning guidance and sensitivity studies, clarify theoretical assumptions, and better explain correlated-gradient requirements.
