- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGLD) by running two coupled chains at γ and γ/2 and combining time-averaged estimators with correlated Brownian increments.  
- Theoretically derives CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved finite-time rates (bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ))), yielding optimal K^{-2/3} bias and K^{-4/5} MSE with constant stepsizes.  
- With decreasing stepsizes γk ∝ k^{-α}, achieves optimal asymptotic MSE O(K^{-2/5}) at α=1/5, improving over SGLD’s O(K^{-1/3}).  
- Empirically validates rate predictions on a Gaussian model, shows improvements on large-scale matrix factorization, and extends method to SGHMC matching high-order integrators’ accuracy.  
- Builds on classical RR for SDEs and recent SG-MCMC analyses; RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC appears novel and variance control uses duplicated-diffusion coupling.  
- Theory (CLT and bounds) is technically plausible and aligns with SDE RR literature, though it requires strong smoothness (g ∈ C9) and Poisson-equation solvability.  
- Identifies optimal Brownian coupling (Σ = I) and shows Brownian correlation asymptotically dominates variance; stochastic gradient correlation is less critical for CLT.  
- Key technical conditions (ergodicity, smoothness, growth, moment bounds, exact meaning of “i.i.d. unbiased gradients”) are deferred to the supplement and need clearer main-text summary.  
- Analysis should clarify that gradient estimators are conditionally unbiased given θ and state dependence of their distributions; dependence of constants on gradient-noise moments is acknowledged.  
- Algorithm and coupling are clearly described conceptually but pseudocode is only in the supplement; main text should state whether using same minibatches across chains affects variance.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly validate theoretical rates; SGHMC extension is a useful sanity check.  
- Matrix factorization results show improved RMSE/time with parallel execution, but fairness depends on parallelism since two chains cost ≈2× compute if not parallelized.  
- Missing compute-equal comparisons (equal total gradient evaluations) and broader experiments (logistic regression, Bayesian neural nets) to demonstrate robustness.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized, but important assumptions and pseudocode belong in the main text for readability.  
- The phrase “i.i.d. unbiased estimators with common distribution L” is misleading; should emphasize conditional unbiasedness and independent minibatch selection.  
- Short discussion and empirical ablation on reusing the same minibatch across chains would be valuable.  
- Originality: novel application of RR to SG-MCMC and nontrivial analysis of optimal Brownian coupling in presence of stochastic gradients.  
- Significance: simple, parallel-friendly wrapper that improves finite-time and asymptotic accuracy across SG-MCMC methods while retaining first-order integrators.  
- Limitations: strong smoothness assumptions for theoretical guarantees and reliance on parallel resources for practical speedups; benefits increase total gradient cost if not parallelized.  
- Pros: principled idea with theoretical backing, novel Brownian coupling, generic and parallel-friendly, extends beyond SGLD, empirical alignment with theory.  
- Cons: key assumptions and pseudocode in supplement, confusing “i.i.d.” phrasing, empirical reliance on parallelization, limited application diversity, no minibatch-sharing ablation.  
- Suggestions: clarify gradient-estimator conditions and ergodicity/smoothness assumptions in main text; add compute-equal comparisons; test minibatch sharing empirically; include pseudocode in paper and SGHMC hyperparameters; comment on non-smooth U applicability.  
- Overall: solid, technically careful, novel, and useful contribution meeting NeurIPS standards; recommendation: accept (weak accept to accept) conditional on clarifying assumptions and adding a compute-equal comparison.
