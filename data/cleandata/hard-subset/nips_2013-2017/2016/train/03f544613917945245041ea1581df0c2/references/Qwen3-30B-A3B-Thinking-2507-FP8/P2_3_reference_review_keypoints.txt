- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with different step sizes and combine via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.
- Theoretically achieves improved MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}) versus SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}), with formal convergence analysis and CLTs.
- Rigorous technical analysis building on prior SGLD and RR work; proofs sketched in main text with full proofs in supplement.
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization (MovieLens) validate reduced bias and MSE.
- Demonstrates generality by extending the approach to SGHMC (SGRRHMC).
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and ties theory to experiments effectively.
- Practical advantage: parallel nature suits distributed computing and can yield substantial runtime savings in practice.
- Minor omission: lacks explicit quantification of computational cost/speedup from running two chains in parallel.
- Limitation acknowledged: requires identical mini-batch sizes for both chains.
- Theoretical sensitivity to step size choices is not thoroughly addressed.
- Original contribution: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order-like rates with first-order integrators.
- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical advance with strong practical implications.
