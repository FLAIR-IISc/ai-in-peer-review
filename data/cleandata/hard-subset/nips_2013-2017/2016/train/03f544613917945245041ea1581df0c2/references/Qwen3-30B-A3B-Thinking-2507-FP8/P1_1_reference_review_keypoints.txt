- Summary: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias to O(γ²) and MSE to O(K⁻⁴/⁵).

- Summary: Claims SGRRLD attains higher-order accuracy using only first-order integrators; provides CLT and non-asymptotic bounds and validates on synthetic and MovieLens experiments.

- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous CLT (Theorem 1) and non-asymptotic bounds (Theorem 2), showing improved convergence rates.

- Strength: Novel adaptation of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, addressing SG-MCMC bias.

- Strength: Practical parallelization potential since the two required chains can be generated independently.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization showing improved bias and MSE over SGLD and SGHMC.

- Strength: Clear writing, consistent notation, and well-organized presentation with reproducible supplementary proofs.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of practical computational cost and wall-clock time tradeoffs when running two chains (even if parallelizable).

- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on standard but potentially restrictive technical conditions (smoothness, gradient moment bounds) not fully discussed for high-dimensional ML.

- Weakness: Comparisons limited to SGLD and SGHMC; lacks experiments against more recent SG-MCMC or variance-reduction/adaptive-step methods.

- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity (step sizes, mini-batch size, K) in experiments.

- Weakness: Mentions recursive RR extrapolation but does not explore or quantify potential benefits.

- Evaluation (Clarity): Highly readable and well-structured with adequate supplementary detail; minor experimental implementation details could be expanded. (Excellent)

- Evaluation (Originality): Novel and meaningful application of RR to SG-MCMC, clearly differentiated from prior SDE work. (Strong)

- Evaluation (Significance): Significant improvement in convergence rate and practical parallelizability make it valuable for large-scale Bayesian inference. (Strong)
