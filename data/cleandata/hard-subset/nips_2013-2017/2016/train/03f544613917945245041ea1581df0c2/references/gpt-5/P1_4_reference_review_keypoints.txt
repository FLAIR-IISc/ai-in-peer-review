- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, which runs two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and uses Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with correlated Brownian noise to reduce discretization bias to O(γ^2).
- Theoretical: Proves a CLT for the extrapolated estimator showing minimal asymptotic variance with perfectly correlated Brownian noise.
- Non-asymptotic bounds: Shows bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) for constant steps, yielding optimal MSE rate O(K^−4/5) with γ ∝ K^−1/5.
- Generality: Method is generic and extendable to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGHMC) and suggests multilevel/recursive RR variants.
- Empirics: Validates theoretical rates on a Gaussian model, shows SGHMC variant competes with higher-order methods, and improves RMSE in large-scale MovieLens matrix factorization leveraging parallelism.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SGLD and SG-MCMC literature and adapts classical RR extrapolation for SDEs to the stochastic gradient setting, connecting to multilevel Monte Carlo ideas.
- Strength — simplicity: Conceptually simple and broadly applicable with a clear noise-correlation prescription.
- Strength — theory: Provides solid CLT, finite-time bounds, and step-size guidance (α=1/5 optimal for MSE; α=1/3 for bias).
- Strength — practical: Two-chain parallel implementation preserves wall-clock time on multi-core setups and shows empirical gains.
- Strength — extensibility: Demonstrates applicability to SGHMC and potential for recursive/multilevel extensions.
- Weakness — strong assumptions: CLT and analysis require high smoothness (Poisson solution in C9) and strong ergodicity/weak error conditions, unclear for nonconvex/deep models.
- Weakness — algorithm typo: Eq. (5) appears to have a gradient-evaluation typo for the half-step updates and needs clarification.
- Weakness — gradient-noise handling: Theory focuses on Brownian correlation; empirical role of correlating minibatches across chains is unexplored and could reduce variance in finite time.
- Weakness — computational cost: Without parallel hardware, SGRRLD costs ~3 gradient evaluations per iteration versus 1 for SGLD; fairness and FLOP-equivalent comparisons are missing.
- Weakness — empirical scope: Real-data experiments compare only to SGLD; missing comparisons to stronger SG-MCMC baselines (pSGLD, SGNHT, preconditioning) and richer posterior diagnostics beyond RMSE.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written but defers many conditions/proofs to the supplement; main-text statement of key assumptions and explicit implementation details (seed/correlation) would help.
- Quality: Theoretical development is careful and rates are consistent with CLT; empirics support scalings but broader validation is needed.
- Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with variance control via correlated Brownian motion, connecting two established areas.
- Significance: Offers a practical route to improved SG-MCMC accuracy, attractive for parallel settings and likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Questions for authors: Clarify Eq. (5) updates; state whether minibatches are reused across half-steps/chains and provide ablations; quantify sensitivity to imperfect Brownian correlation; discuss applicability to non-smooth/heavy-tailed U and deep models; add comparisons to pSGLD/SGNHT/Riemannian variants.
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general idea with clear theoretical benefits, practical parallelization, and novel connections likely to influence SG-MCMC research.
- Cons for acceptance: Strong assumptions, minor algorithm ambiguity, limited empirical baselines, and reliance on parallelism for fairness.
- Recommendation: Accept (weak).
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
