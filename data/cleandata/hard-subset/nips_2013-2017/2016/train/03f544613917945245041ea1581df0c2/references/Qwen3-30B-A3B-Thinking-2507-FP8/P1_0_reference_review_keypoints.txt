- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while keeping variance controlled.  
- Proves SGRRLD attains asymptotic bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Method is generic and extended to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGRRHMC).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with both CLT-style asymptotics and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and MovieLens matrix factorization support theoretical claims.  
- Authors address key implementation issues such as using correlated Brownian motions and equal mini-batch sizes to avoid bias.  
- Minor concern: computational cost of running two parallel chains and coordinating correlated noise in distributed settings needs more analysis.  
- Minor concern: explanation for why the γ/2 chain must run twice as many steps is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Minor concern: experimental step sizes (γ values) are not clearly justified from theory or tuning procedure.  
- Clarity issue: notation for the two chains (θ^(γ), θ^(γ/2)) is confusing and could be simplified.  
- Clarity issue: explanation for the doubled steps of the smaller-step chain should be more explicit.  
- Clarity issue: connection between theoretical optimal step size (γ ∝ K⁻¹/⁵) and chosen experimental γ should be made explicit.  
- Originality: novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, not previously explored in literature.  
- Significance: substantially improves convergence rates and is well-suited for parallel/distributed computing, with practical performance gains.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, technically sound and well-written; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Specific request 1: provide detailed analysis of computational overhead for parallelization and correlated noise coordination.  
- Specific request 2: clarify why the smaller-step chain runs twice as many iterations and the step-counting relationship.  
- Specific request 3: explain how experimental step sizes were chosen and whether based on theory or tuning.  
- Specific request 4: simplify or improve notation distinguishing the two chains.  
- Specific request 5: explicitly link the theoretical optimal γ ∝ K⁻¹/⁵ to experimental choices.  
- Specific request 6: clarify implementation details of SGHMC-s used in comparisons (details are only in supplement).  
- Strengths: novel idea, strong theory, practical experiments, good parallelization properties, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses to address: more detail on parallel cost, step-count rationale, step-size selection, notation simplification, and theory-experiment connection.
