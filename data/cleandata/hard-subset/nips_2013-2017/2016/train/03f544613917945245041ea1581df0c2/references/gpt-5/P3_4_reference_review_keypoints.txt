- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (run two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2, combine time-averaged estimates) to cancel first-order discretization bias.  
- Proves CLT for the extrapolated estimator under decreasing step sizes and gives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows improved finite-time MSE rates: O(K^-4/5) for SGRRLD versus O(K^-2/3) for first-order SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order accuracy using only first-order integrators.  
- Identifies an optimal Brownian coupling Zγ = (Zγ/2,1 + Zγ/2,2)/√2 that minimizes asymptotic variance.  
- Extends the idea to SGHMC (SGRRHMC) and empirically matches the accuracy of higher-order integrators at larger steps.  
- Builds on RR/ Talay–Tubaro expansions and duplication/coupling literature, and complements high-order integrator and MLMC approaches.  
- Theoretical development appears sound and nontrivial; finite-K bounds (bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ))) are consistent and imply optimal γ scalings.  
- Assumptions are strong (Poisson equation solution, high smoothness, dissipativity/ergodicity) and should be stated explicitly in the main text.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized but has clarity issues: likely typo in Eq. (5), key technical assumptions deferred to the supplement, and unclear dependence on mini-batch distribution L.  
- Empirical results: synthetic Gaussian and large-scale matrix factorization corroborate theory and show RMSE improvements at equal wall-clock time.  
- Caveat: wall-clock comparisons assume perfect parallelism; results should be reported for equal FLOPs/gradient evaluations or serial baselines for fairness.  
- Missing ablations: unclear whether stochastic gradient estimators are coupled across chains, impact of correlating mini-batches, and sensitivity to mini-batch size B.  
- Experiments limited mostly to Gaussian likelihoods; including non-Gaussian posteriors (e.g., logistic regression) would increase generality.  
- Practical questions remain: should practitioners use constant vs decreasing step sizes, and is γ ∝ K^-1/5 recommended for fixed budgets?  
- Minor issues: several typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., Brownian term notation in Eq. (4)); please proofread and clarify subscripts.  
- Pros: principled, simple to implement, generic across SG-MCMC variants, theoretically grounded, exploits parallelism, and shows empirical gains.  
- Cons: strong smoothness/ergodicity assumptions, experimental fairness and ablation gaps, and some implementation/details missing.  
- Recommendation: Accept (weak accept) — novel, useful technique with solid theory and promising empirical results.
