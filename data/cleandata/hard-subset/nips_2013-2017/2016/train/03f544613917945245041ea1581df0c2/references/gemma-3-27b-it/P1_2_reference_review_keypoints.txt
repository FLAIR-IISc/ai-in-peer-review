- Paper proposes SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and combining outputs to reduce bias.
- Theoretical analysis claims improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD's O(K^-2/3), with finite-time bounds and treatment of correlated Brownian motion.
- Positions work relative to SGLD/SG-MCMC literature and links RR extrapolation for SDEs (Talay & Tubaro) to Bayesian inference.
- Novelty: first adaptation of RR extrapolation to the stochastic gradient / MCMC setting.
- Theoretical rigor: provides convergence guarantees, bias/MSE bounds, and asymptotic plus finite-time analyses.
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic models and matrix factorization show consistent performance gains and comparisons with SGHMC.
- Parallelizability: two-chain structure is inherently parallel, aiding scalability.
- Generality: method can potentially be applied to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.
- Writing and presentation are clear with well-explained motivation and notation.
- Practical overhead: paper doesn’t deeply analyze the costs of managing two chains in distributed environments (communication/synchronization).
- Gradient correlation: discussion on correlated gradients is underdeveloped and lacks practical guidance on ensuring similar gradient distributions across chains.
- Choice of Σ: reliance on Σ = I is not fully justified; sensitivity to deviations from this assumption isn’t explored.
- Limited comparisons: evaluation could include more recent/alternative SG-MCMC methods (e.g., adaptive step-size approaches).
- Hyperparameter tuning: the paper lacks details on tuning procedures, affecting reproducibility.
- Pro: novel, theoretically sound, empirically supported, and impactful for large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Con: practical overhead, gradient-correlation concerns, and limited benchmarking against newer methods.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; minor weaknesses do not outweigh merits.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: analyze practical overhead of two chains (communication costs), expand on gradient correlation implications and guidance, discuss sensitivity to non-identity Σ, and briefly compare to adaptive step-size SG-MCMC methods.
