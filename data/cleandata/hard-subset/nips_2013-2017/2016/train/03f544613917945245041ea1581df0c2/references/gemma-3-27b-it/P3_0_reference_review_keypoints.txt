- Overall: Proposes SGRRLD — Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC — with strong theory and supportive experiments; worthy of acceptance despite some practical gaps.
- Summary/prior work: Applies RR extrapolation to SGLD using two chains with different step sizes, building on Talay & Tubaro and contrasting with higher-order SG-MCMC (e.g., Chen et al.).
- Theoretical strength: Provides rigorous asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence theorems, showing improved rate O(K^-4/5) vs. SGLD's O(K^-2/3) and justifying correlated Brownian motions.
- Novelty/insight: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC that improves accuracy without needing higher-order integrators.
- Experiments: Comprehensive synthetic and real-data experiments validate reduced bias and improved MSE; synthetic results strongly match theory, real-data (matrix factorization) gains are modest.
- Writing/organization: Clear, well-organized presentation with standard, easy-to-follow notation.
- Generality: Method extends to SGHMC, indicating broader applicability.
- Implementation weakness: Lacks detailed discussion on practical implementation choices (step-size schedule, maintaining correlated Brownian motions), hurting reproducibility.
- Empirical weakness: Modest performance improvement in matrix factorization not fully analyzed (mini-batch size, hyperparameters not explored).
- Gradient correlation: Discussion on achieving correlated gradient estimates in practice is limited beyond Brownian correlation.
- Computational cost: RR extrapolation doubles per-iteration cost (two chains); wall-clock trade-offs and cost-vs-accuracy analysis are missing.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically strong, experimentally supported, and potentially impactful.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient implementation/practical details, modest real-data gains, and unaddressed computational overhead.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add implementation details and a deeper discussion of computational trade-offs.
