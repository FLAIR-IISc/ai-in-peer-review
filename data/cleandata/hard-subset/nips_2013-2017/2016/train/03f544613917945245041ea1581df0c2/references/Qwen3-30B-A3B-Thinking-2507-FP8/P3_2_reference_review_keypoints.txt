- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to achieve higher-order convergence than SGLD.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence analysis (Theorems 1 and 2) showing asymptotic bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵).  
- Identifies necessity of perfectly correlated Brownian increments to control variance and justifies this choice.  
- Practical parallelism: RR approach naturally parallelizable, with matrix factorization experiments showing comparable wall-clock times to SGLD.  
- Experiments: synthetic and real-data results support theoretical claims; Gaussian experiments show bias reduction.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured with mostly consistent notation.  

- Main weakness: no direct empirical comparison to higher-order integrator methods (e.g., Chen et al. 2015), undermining the core claim.  
- Insufficiently explains how RR extrapolation for standard MCMC differs from the SG-MCMC setting with stochastic gradient noise.  
- Algorithm description (eqs. 4–6) is confusing on chain synchronization and step-size scheduling (why Chain 2 runs twice as often).  
- Theoretical results rely on strong smoothness assumptions (f, U, L) but lack discussion on practical restrictiveness and mini-batch effects.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational overhead and parallel efficiency (speedup, scalability) when running two chains.  

- Specific: Section 3 needs a concrete example/diagram to clarify the two-chain schedule and step sizes.  
- Specific: Section 4's proof sketch for Theorem 1 is too brief; needs more detail on handling stochastic gradient noise in Taylor expansion.  
- Specific: Section 5.1 comparison with SGHMC-s is insufficient; requires comparisons across multiple γ values to support the main claim.  
- Specific: Figure 2’s explanation for increased bias/MSE at very small γ (1/(Kγ) dominance) is unclear and should be explained.  
- Specific: Conclusion’s claim about SGRRLD being more efficient than SGLD needs quantification (e.g., speedup metrics).  

- Overall: technically sound and novel application of RR to SG-MCMC with rigorous analysis and supportive experiments.  
- Critical caveat: lacking empirical validation against higher-order integrators weakens the primary claim of matching higher-order accuracy.  
- Suggested revisions: add direct comparisons with higher-order methods, clarify algorithm implementation, and analyze computational overhead.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance, contingent on addressing the above weaknesses in revision.
