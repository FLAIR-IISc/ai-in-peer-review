- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains at steps γ and γ/2 and combining estimators as 2×fine − coarse with coupled Brownian increments.
- Theory: Provides a CLT under decreasing steps showing asymptotic variance minimized by perfect Brownian coupling.
- Theory: Delivers non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds with improved rates over first-order SG-MCMC (e.g., MSE O(K^{-4/5}) vs O(K^{-2/3})).
- Implementation: Controls variance via coupling where the coarse noise equals the appropriately scaled sum of the two fine noises.
- Experiments: Synthetic linear-Gaussian model, an SGHMC variant, and MovieLens matrix factorization show reduced bias/MSE and wall-clock gains when run in parallel.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SGLD, RR extrapolation for SDEs, and MLMC coupling ideas, extending variance-minimizing correlation arguments to SG-MCMC and time-averaged estimators.
- Strength: Conceptually clean and novel combination with principled variance control and applicability beyond SGLD (e.g., SGHMC).
- Strength: Solid theoretical contributions aligning CLT and non-asymptotic bounds with observed empirical rates.
- Strength: Embarrassingly parallel implementation with demonstrated practical wall-clock improvements on a large task.
- Weakness: Strong smoothness and ergodicity assumptions (e.g., Poisson solution g ∈ C^9) that may not hold for nonsmooth or weakly regular posteriors.
- Weakness: Does not explore coupling of stochastic gradients/minibatches; gradient noise can dominate in finite-K or small-batch regimes.
- Weakness: Computational fairness concerns—requires two parallel processes; on a single core per-iteration cost doubles and single-thread comparisons are missing.
- Weakness: Limited posterior diversity in experiments—no non-Gaussian examples like logistic regression or Bayesian neural nets.
- Clarity issues: Typos/indexing errors in equations (4)–(6) and possible normalization mistakes in the RR estimator need correction.
- Quality remark: Overall technically sound, but the “variance O(γ^4)” claim needs precise statement and clarification of dependence on stochastic gradient variance/minibatch size.
- Clarity suggestion: Explicitly state how to implement perfect Brownian coupling in parallel and summarize technical assumptions in the main text.
- Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation with Brownian coupling to SG-MCMC and meaningful extension of CLTs for duplicated diffusions to the stochastic-gradient setting.
- Significance: Promising practical path to higher-order accuracy without complex integrators, particularly valuable with parallel resources; broader impact depends on addressing noted limitations.
- Suggestions: Fix equation typos, clarify variance regime and minibatch dependence, try minibatch coupling experiments, add single-thread compute vs error results, include a non-Gaussian posterior, and cite Giles (2008).
- Recommendation: Weak accept — original and technically solid but needs experiments and clarifications to address practical and assumption-related concerns.
