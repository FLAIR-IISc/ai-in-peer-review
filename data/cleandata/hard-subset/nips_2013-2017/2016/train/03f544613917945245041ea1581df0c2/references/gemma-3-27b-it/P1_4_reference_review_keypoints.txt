- Proposes SGRRLD: run two Langevin chains with different step sizes and combine via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SGLD.
- Theoretically improves MSE convergence to O(K^-4/5) versus O(K^-2/3) for SGLD, with variance control via correlated Brownian motions.
- Empirical validation on synthetic models and large-scale matrix factorization shows improved accuracy and efficiency.
- Positions work within SG-MCMC literature (SGLD, SGHMC) and adapts Richardson-Talay/Tubaro extrapolation to SG-MCMC.
- Novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC.
- Strong theoretical analysis including convergence rates and CLTs (proofs in supplement).
- Well-designed experiments that support theoretical claims and show practical benefits on large datasets.
- Algorithm is inherently parallelizable by running two chains independently.
- Technique is generalizable to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.
- Paper is generally well-written with mostly consistent notation.
- Implementation and tuning are more complex than vanilla SGLD, requiring management of two chains and correlated noise.
- Practical speedup (e.g., ~2x) may be insufficient to justify added complexity for some applications.
- Details on implementing correlated Brownian motions (Eq. 8) and associated computational/numerical costs are underdeveloped.
- Comparisons to higher-order SGHMC variants are limited and could be more thorough.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated, parallelizable, and relevant.
- Arguments against acceptance: added complexity, modest practical gains, and insufficient implementation/comparison details.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions to address implementation, cost of correlation, and broader comparisons.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
