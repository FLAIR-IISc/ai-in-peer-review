- Introduces SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains and correlated Brownian motions to reduce SGLD bias.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a CLT and bias/MSE bounds, claiming improved MSE rate O(K^-4/5) vs SGLD’s O(K^-2/3).  
- Positions the work as a novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, building on SGLD/SGHMC and SDE numerical-analysis literature.  
- Claims broad applicability: authors argue SGRRLD extends to a wide class of SG-MCMC algorithms (demonstrated with SGHMC).  
- Algorithm is naturally parallelizable (two chains) and includes practical implementation details and pseudo-code.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussians, matrix factorization, and comparisons to SGHMC show improved accuracy; scaling and ablation studies included.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation and significant convergence-rate result.  
- Strength: generality of the approach across SG-MCMC methods.  
- Strength: practical implementation that leverages parallelism and clear exposition.  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements and useful supplementary experiments.  
- Weakness: practical implications and sensitivity to the choice of correlated Brownian motion matrix Σ are under-explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially step-size selection and dataset dependence.  
- Weakness: potential computational overhead from running/synchronizing two chains and managing correlated noise not fully quantified (wall-clock/time cost).  
- Weakness: limited benchmarking against the latest state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods beyond a brief SGHMC-s comparison.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address correlation matrix, hyperparameter sensitivity, and more comprehensive benchmarking.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
