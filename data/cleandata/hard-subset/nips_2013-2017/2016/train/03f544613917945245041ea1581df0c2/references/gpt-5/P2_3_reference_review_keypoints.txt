- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (mainly SGLD) using two chains (stepsizes γ and γ/2) with correlated Brownian increments to reduce discretization bias.  
- Theoretically proves a CLT and finite-time bias/MSE bounds: bias O(γ2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ4 + 1/(Kγ)), giving optimal MSE rate O(K−4/5) and optimal schedule α = 1/5 for γk ∝ k−α.  
- Empirically validates predicted scaling on a Gaussian model, shows improvements for SGHMC variants, and demonstrates wall-clock RMSE gains on large-scale matrix factorization with parallel two-chain execution.  
- Conceptually novel and simple: a generic, parallelizable bias-reduction mechanism that attains higher-order accuracy using first-order updates; applicable beyond SGLD.  
- Strong theoretical support with explicit rates and a carefully justified variance-minimizing Brownian coupling.  
- Practical relevance: achieves accuracy similar to higher-order integrators while remaining embarrassingly parallel.  
- Empirical evidence is supportive and fair (parallel wall-clock comparisons), though benchmarks could be broader.  
- Main theoretical results rely on strong smoothness/ergodicity conditions (e.g., solution g ∈ C9), limiting applicability to many practical posteriors (nonsmooth priors, heavy tails).  
- Algorithmic clarity issues: apparent typo in Chain 2 update (Eq. 5) suggesting an implicit gradient evaluation; notation ambiguities like √(2γ) vs √2 γ need correction.  
- Role of coupling stochastic gradients (e.g., reusing minibatches across chains) is underexplored; stronger gradient coupling could reduce finite-K variance and needs ablation.  
- Limited baselines: lacks comparisons to other practical SG-MCMC improvements (preconditioned SGLD, SGFS, thermostat methods) beyond SGLD/SGHMC.  
- Practical guidance is thin: more concrete tuning advice for γ and K, sensitivity analyses, and discussion of 2× cost when parallel resources are unavailable are needed.  
- Quality: technical claims are consistent with RR literature and plausible under assumptions, but many details are deferred to the supplement and common ML posterior conditions should be summarized in the main text.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but would benefit from corrected notation, explicit pseudocode in the main text, and more intuitive explanation of the estimator.  
- Originality: novel and natural application of RR extrapolation with Brownian coupling to SG-MCMC, not previously formalized.  
- Significance: practically impactful for large-scale SG-MCMC users, with compelling improved rates and potential for extensions (multilevel, recursive RR).  
- Pros: simple, general, theoretically backed method with clear bias/MSE and wall-clock improvements and useful Brownian coupling insights.  
- Cons: strong technical assumptions, minor but important typos, missing pseudocode, limited ablations/baselines, and assumed parallelism without single-core tradeoff discussion.  
- Questions for authors: Please confirm/fix Chain 2 explicit Euler updates (Eq. 5) and clarify gradient evaluation points.  
- Questions for authors: Did you try sharing minibatches across half-steps/coarse steps, and does it improve finite-K variance?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive are results to γ and coupling choices in practice, and can you provide heuristic tuning guidance?  
- Questions for authors: Can you comment on extending to recursive RR or multilevel schemes under unbiased stochastic gradients?
