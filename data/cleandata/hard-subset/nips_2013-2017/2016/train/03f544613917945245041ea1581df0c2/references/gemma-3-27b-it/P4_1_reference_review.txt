This paper introduces Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), an SG-MCMC algorithm designed to reduce bias compared to SGLD. The core idea is to apply the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation technique, running two Langevin dynamics chains in parallel with different step sizes and combining their results. The authors provide theoretical analysis showing improved convergence rates (O(K^-4/5) MSE vs. O(K^-2/3) for SGLD) and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets (Gaussian model and matrix factorization).

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the motivation, method, theoretical results, and experimental validation. It builds upon existing work in SG-MCMC, specifically SGLD and its extensions, and logically introduces RR extrapolation as a method to address the bias issue. The inclusion of both synthetic and real-world experiments strengthens the credibility of the proposed method. The connection to Hamiltonian Monte Carlo through SGRRHMC also showcases the generality of the approach.  The careful discussion of correlated Brownian motions and their impact on variance control is a strong point.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a novel contribution. While RR extrapolation is known in numerical analysis, its adaptation to stochastic gradient-based sampling is a significant step.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including convergence rates and bounds on bias and MSE. The theorems are clearly stated and well-motivated.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is standard and well-explained.
*   **Completeness:** The paper provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method, including comparisons with existing algorithms (SGLD, SGHMC, SGHMC-s) on different datasets.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The matrix factorization experiments demonstrate the potential of SGRRLD for large-scale applications. The emphasis on parallelizability is also a valuable practical consideration.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The analysis doesn’t explicitly address the sensitivity of performance to the choice of step sizes (γ) or correlation matrix (Σ). While discussed, a more detailed exploration of practical tuning strategies would be beneficial. The experiments briefly touch upon step size but could be expanded.
*   **Computational Overhead:**  While the authors mention parallelizability, the overhead of maintaining and synchronizing two chains is not thoroughly quantified. A discussion of the computational cost relative to SGLD, considering communication costs in distributed settings, would be useful.
*   **Assumptions:** The theoretical analysis relies on several assumptions about the potential energy function (U) and the noise distribution (L). A more detailed discussion of the practical implications of these assumptions and their limitations would strengthen the paper.
*  **Limited Scope of Experimentation:** The experiments are somewhat limited in scope. Exploring other datasets and models beyond the Gaussian model and matrix factorization could provide a more comprehensive evaluation.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to improve the accuracy of SG-MCMC algorithms.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
*   The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
*   The contribution is significant and advances the state-of-the-art in scalable Bayesian inference.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The practical considerations of parameter tuning and computational overhead are not fully addressed.
*   The scope of the experiments is somewhat limited, and further evaluation on diverse datasets and models would be beneficial.
*   The assumptions underlying the theoretical analysis could be better discussed and their limitations clarified.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The proposed SGRRLD algorithm offers a promising approach to reduce bias and improve convergence rates, and the theoretical analysis and experimental validation are both strong. While some practical considerations and limitations remain, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the parameter sensitivity, computational overhead and assumption discussions.
