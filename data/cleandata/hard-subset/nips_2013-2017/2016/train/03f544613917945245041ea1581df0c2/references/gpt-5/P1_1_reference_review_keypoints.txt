- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (γ and γ/2) and linearly combining them with correlated Gaussian noise to cancel bias without inflating variance.  
- Theoretical contributions: Proves a CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds; shows asymptotic bias O(γ^2) vs O(γ) and MSE O(γ^4) vs O(γ^2), giving optimal MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}).  
- Extension: Method generalized to other SG-MCMC methods; an SGHMC variant (SGRRHMC) is illustrated.  
- Relation to prior work: RR extrapolation for SDEs is classical, but its application to noisy-gradient SG-MCMC with variance control via correlated noises is novel and complements higher-order integrator analyses.  
- Theory quality: Main claims are plausible and consistent with MLMC, but several strong technical conditions (e.g., Poisson solution g ∈ C^9) are deferred to the supplement and may limit generality.  
- Correctness issue: Likely typo/implicit-update in Chain 2 first half-step (eq. (5)) that should be clarified/corrected to an explicit Euler form.  
- Variance control: Analysis that full correlation (Σ = Id) minimizes asymptotic variance aligns with MLMC/common-random-numbers intuition and is well formalized.  
- Practical gradient-coupling: Both chains must use gradient estimators with same distribution L; sharing minibatch indices across levels could further reduce variance and should be discussed/ablation-tested.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized, but notation is heavy; key assumptions and algorithm pseudocode belong in the main text and noise-scaling notation should be unified.  
- Originality: Adapting RR extrapolation to noisy-gradient sampling with correlated increments and finite-time rates is an original and useful contribution.  
- Significance: Presents a parallel-friendly way to reduce bias/MSE without higher-order integrators, with theory and practical gains that could influence SG-MCMC design.  
- Empirics — strengths: Synthetic and MovieLens experiments validate predicted scalings and show wall-clock advantages and competitive accuracy with higher-order integrators.  
- Empirics — caveats: Fairness concerns (SGRRLD runs two chains in parallel) — compare to SGLD with matched compute; need ablations for independent vs correlated noises, minibatch sharing, and baselines like pSGLD.  
- Pros: Simple, generic, theoretically backed method; clear bias/MSE improvements; parallel-friendly and practically beneficial on large problems.  
- Cons: Strong assumptions hidden in supplement, suspected update-typo, fairness and minibatch-coupling not fully addressed empirically.  
- Questions for authors: Confirm/correct Chain 2 update; report whether minibatches were shared and the impact; sensitivity to minibatch size and decreasing step sizes; compatibility with preconditioning or control variates.  
- Minor comments: Fix typos (e.g., “varice,” “Futher”), unify factors of 2 in noise scaling, and include algorithm pseudocode in main text.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and well-motivated, but would be stronger after addressing clarity and evaluation issues.
