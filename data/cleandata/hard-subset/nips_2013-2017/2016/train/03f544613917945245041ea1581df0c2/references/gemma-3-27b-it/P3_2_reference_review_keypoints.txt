- Introduces SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two Langevin chains with different step sizes to reduce bias in SG-MCMC.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: bias/variance bounds and a CLT, claiming improved MSE rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K^-2/3).
- Algorithm is parallelizable and extendable to other SG-MCMC methods; uses correlated Brownian motions to control variance.
- Empirical results on synthetic data and matrix factorization show improved accuracy over SGLD and SGHMC.
- Novelty: first adaptation of RR extrapolation to stochastic SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order accuracy with a first-order integrator.
- Theoretical rigor: solid convergence analysis and derivations, with technical details deferred to the supplement.
- Practical considerations: authors discuss parallelizability and variance control but provide limited practical tuning guidance.
- Weakness: depends on technical assumptions (smoothness, ergodicity, weak error) that need clearer explanation in the main text.
- Weakness: lacks systematic guidance or sensitivity analysis for step-size scheduling and optimal α selection.
- Weakness: running two chains introduces computational overhead; no detailed wall-clock cost vs. accuracy analysis.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is somewhat narrow; more complex/high-dimensional model tests are needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, novelty, strong empirical support, and relevance to Bayesian ML.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, insufficient practical tuning/overhead discussion, limited experimental scope.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions and minor practical issues; recommended for acceptance (8/10).
- Recommendations: clarify assumptions in main text, provide parameter-tuning guidance, analyze computational overhead, and expand experiments.
