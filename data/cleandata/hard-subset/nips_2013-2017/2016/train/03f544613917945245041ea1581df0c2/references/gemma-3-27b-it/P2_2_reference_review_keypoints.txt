- Novel algorithm: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two Langevin chains with different step sizes and combining results.  
- Theoretical improvement: proves improved MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K^-2/3).  
- Experimental validation: synthetic Gaussian and large-scale matrix factorization experiments support theoretical gains and show improvements over SGLD/SGHMC.  
- Generality: approach extends to SGHMC, indicating broader applicability.  
- Parallelizability: two-chain structure makes the method well-suited for parallel architectures.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written with helpful pseudo-code.  
- Novelty: the idea of using Richardson-Romberg in stochastic sampling is original and significant.  
- Practical overhead concern: running two chains doubles computation and the paper lacks detailed analysis of real-world cost vs. theoretical speedup.  
- Restrictive assumptions: analysis relies on correlated Brownian increments and identical mini-batch sizes, which may be unrealistic.  
- Missing sensitivity analysis: experiments do not thoroughly explore sensitivity to step sizes, mini-batch sizes, and correlation parameter Σ.  
- Comparison gap: lacks direct comparison to optimized higher-order integrators (e.g., established methods from Chen et al. 2015).  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add discussion/experiments on computational and communication overhead, effect of differing mini-batch sizes and gradient correlations, sensitivity analyses, and comparisons to state-of-the-art higher-order methods.
