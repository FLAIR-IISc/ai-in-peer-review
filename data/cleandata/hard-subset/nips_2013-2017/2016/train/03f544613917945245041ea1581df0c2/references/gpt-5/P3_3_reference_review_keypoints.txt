- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD using coupled chains with step-sizes γ and γ/2 and correlated Gaussian noise to cancel O(γ) weak bias.
- Estimator averages 2 fine-step evaluations minus 1 coarse-step evaluation over matched time horizons.
- Theory: CLT for extrapolated estimator; minimal asymptotic variance when Brownian increments are maximally correlated (Σ = I).
- Non-asymptotic bounds: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)), yielding optimal finite-time rates O(K−2/3) for bias and O(K−4/5) for MSE with constant step-size tuning.
- For decreasing steps γk ∝ k−α, optimal asymptotic rate O(K−2/5) achieved at α = 1/5.
- Experiments (Gaussian model, SGHMC extension, MovieLens matrix factorization) support reduced bias/MSE and improved wall-clock efficiency with two parallel chains.
- Novelty: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is simple, broadly applicable, and well-motivated.
- Strong theoretical contribution: extends classical RR/Euler–Maruyama results to stochastic gradient settings with careful analysis of variance and correlation effects.
- Practicality: embarrassingly parallel implementation matches modern compute and shows empirical rate confirmation and wall-clock gains at scale.
- Generality: natural extension to SGHMC and potential applicability to thermostats and preconditioned variants.
- Heavy smoothness/regularity assumptions: proofs require high smoothness (g ∈ C^9, 7th-order Taylor), with key conditions deferred to supplement — clarify and compare to Teh et al. (2016).
- Gradient noise concerns: analysis treats Gaussian noise as dominating; need discussion/ablations on high-variance minibatch regimes and behavior of constants.
- Correlation of minibatch gradients: paper couples Brownian increments but uses independent minibatches — investigate whether sharing minibatches across levels reduces finite-time variance.
- Fairness of wall-clock baselines: must compare to two independent SGLD replicas averaged and SGLD with doubled throughput to separate bias reduction from parallel resource use.
- SGHMC extension lacks theory: only empirical results shown; provide theoretical sketch or conditions for RR cancellation in second-order dynamics.
- Notational/typo issues: fix the fine-update indexing in eq. (5) and explicitly state estimator correspondence in (6).
- Related work: cite MLMC (Giles, 2008) and discuss connections to debiasing/coupling literature for (S)MCMC.
- Quality: technically sound for main claims but hinges on nontrivial regularity and moment assumptions that should be explicit in main text.
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but move key assumptions into main paper and provide practical guidance for choosing α and tuning.
- Suggestions: include baselines (two-replica SGLD, doubled batch SGLD, tuned decreasing steps), ablations on Gaussian noise correlation and minibatch sharing, and robustness tests for non-smooth posteriors.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing fairness of comparisons and clarifying assumptions.
