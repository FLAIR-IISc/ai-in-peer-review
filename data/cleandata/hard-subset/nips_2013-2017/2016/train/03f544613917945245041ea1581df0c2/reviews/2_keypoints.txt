- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise, yielding asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Proves under certain conditions SGRRLD has faster convergence rate than SGLD for the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show clear bias-reduction advantage of SGRRLD.  
- MovieLens experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement; real-data results are arguable and need stronger evidence.  
- RR extrapolation is known for SDEs; contribution is applying it to SG-MCMC and providing convergence proofs.  
- Reviewer hasn’t checked proof details and asks what conditions on U are required for Theorems 1 & 2, e.g., can U be multimodal?  
- Questions about effect of gradient-noise correlation: if gradient noises are uncorrelated, chains may decorrelate quickly—how does combining chains reduce error order?  
- Asks whether authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.  
- Concerns about computation-time claim: SGRRLD’s parallelization cost is comparable to SGLD, but SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains—need comparison to multi-chain SGLD.  
- Suggests comparing SGRRLD to SGLD run with two parallel chains (and to SGLD with step size gamma/2) to isolate benefits.  
- Recommends more and stronger real-data experiments to substantiate practical benefit.
