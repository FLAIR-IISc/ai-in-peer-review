- Proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC bias and MSE convergence rates.
- Scheme is easy to implement with minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC.
- Paper is supported by solid theoretical results and overall method/theory looks nice.
- Major concern about how the specific convergence rates are derived from the stated theorem (see "explain fatal flaws").
- Practical concern: on a single core the proposed method costs ~3× time (two chains, one with twice the steps), so Figure 5 comparisons are unfair and SGLD timing should be scaled (2/3), reducing the reported gap.
- In Figure 3 (bias plot) the best curve appears at α=0.2 (red), conflicting with the text claim of α=1/3.
- The “optimal” curve fluctuates substantially, so its empirical rate may not match α=0.2 — needs explanation.
- Question about line 256: when stepsize is very small the 1/(Kγ) term should dominate — why doesn’t this occur for SGLD?
- Minor issues: Eq.1 uses a different symbol than θ; line 94 “RR” not defined; line 131 Γ_K not defined; line 160 “smooth function f” needs precise smoothness assumptions; line 249 figure labeling error (Bias vs. time vs. dimension); vectors should be bold.
