- Problem: SG-MCMC methods are asymptotically biased; paper proposes reducing bias via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation.  
- Method: run two SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2; chain 2 runs 2× steps), correlate injected Gaussian noise as α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2, and subtract estimates to obtain O(γ^2) bias.  
- Theory: authors prove reduced bias and a corresponding reduction in MSE.  
- Strength: method is simple to understand and implement, requiring minimal changes to base SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Generality/impact: appears broadly applicable to many SG-MCMC algorithms and thus potentially highly impactful.  
- Novelty: Richardson–Romberg extrapolation is established in numerical analysis but not widely used in ML/SG-MCMC, so contribution is novel to the community.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and presentation is clear.  
- Question on applicability: can the technique be applied to all SG-MCMC methods, or only those satisfying the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2)?  
- Question on other algorithms: can this be applied to other approximate MCMC schemes (subsampling MH) or Distributed SGLD?  
- Computation/efficiency concern: running one chain 2× longer wastes compute; can using 3 chains (one γ, two γ/2 run equal steps) avoid idle time, and how does that affect variance?  
- Extension question: is there a straightforward extension to more than two step sizes?  
- Noise/minibatch correlation: need clarification on the required correlation between injected noise and effect of correlating minibatches—is using the same minibatch optimal or negligible?  
- Experiments: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to demonstrate Bayesian benefit and contextualize final errors.  
- Presentation suggestion: include an algorithm pseudocode box early to help practitioners implement the method without delving into theory.
