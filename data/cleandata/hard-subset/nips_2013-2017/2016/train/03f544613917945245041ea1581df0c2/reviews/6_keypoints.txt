- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (higher-order numerical integrator) to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis; synthetic experiments verify bias and MSE bounds and real-data matrix factorization shows practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Reviewer finds the technique and presentation strong and situates the method well among prior work.  
- Main concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analysis of numerical integrators on SGMCMC [10] and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions [23].  
- Main concern: practical applicability limited and experiments may be unfair because correlated chains require per-step communication, but communication cost is not considered or discussed.  
- Requests clarification of experimental setup and resource allocation: whether SGLD used the same compute resources as the two-chain SGRRLD, how resources are split, and how partial resources per chain affect wall-clock time given gradient cost.  
- Suggests comparing to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains as an alternative baseline.  
- Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is duplicated (lines 25 and 70).
