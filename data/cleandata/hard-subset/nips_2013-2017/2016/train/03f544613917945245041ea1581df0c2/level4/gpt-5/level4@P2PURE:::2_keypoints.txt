- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to create SGRRLD, claiming asymptotically lower bias and MSE.  
- SGRRLD requires running two parallel Markov chains and is straightforward to implement.  
- The paper transfers known RR improvements from SDE literature to SG-MCMC and proves faster convergence under certain conditions.  
- Reviewer did not verify proofs and requests precise conditions on the potential U (e.g., can U be multimodal?).  
- Questions how error order is reduced when gradient noises are uncorrelated and the two chains decorrelate quickly — mechanism unclear.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiment clearly demonstrates SGRRLD’s bias reduction over SGLD.  
- MovieLens real-data experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement and the evidence is debatable.  
- Experimental concern: SGRRLD (γ and γ/2 chains) was compared to SGLD with step size γ, but should also be compared to SGLD with step size γ/2.  
- Comparative-cost concern: with parallelization one could run multiple SGLD chains in the same time budget — need comparison to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Reviewer asks whether the authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Recommendation: include more real-data experiments to demonstrate practical bias reduction.
