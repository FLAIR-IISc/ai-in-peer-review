This paper introduces a Richardson-Romberg extrapolation scheme to enhance the performance of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods, leading to improved convergence rates for both bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). A significant advantage of the proposed technique is its implementation simplicity, requiring only minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms. The work is well-supported by rigorous theoretical analysis.

While the proposed method is conceptually elegant and the theory appears solid, I have a primary concern regarding the derivation of the specific convergence rates from the main theorems, as detailed in the "fatal flaws" section. In addition to this theoretical point, I have several practical questions and comments.

**Major Points:**

1.  **Experimental Fairness and Computational Cost:** My main practical concern is the fairness of the comparison in the matrix factorization experiment. On a single-core machine, the proposed method would require approximately three times the computational effort of standard SG-MCMC, as it involves running two chains, one of which uses a step size twice as large (implying twice the steps for the same effective length). To ensure a fair comparison based on wall-clock time, the results for the baseline SGLD in Figure 5 should have their time axis scaled by a factor of 2/3. This adjustment would likely reduce the perceived performance gap between SGRRLD and SGLD.

2.  **Discrepancy in Experimental Results:** In the bias plot of Figure 3, the best empirical convergence rate appears to be achieved by the curve corresponding to α=0.2 (the red curve). However, the text states that the optimal rate is achieved with α=1/3. Could the authors clarify this discrepancy?

3.  **Convergence Rate Fluctuation:** The curve corresponding to the empirically optimal rate in Figure 3 exhibits considerable fluctuation. This high variance seems inconsistent with a stable convergence rate of α=0.2. Could the authors provide an explanation for this phenomenon?

4.  **Asymptotic Behavior of Error Terms:** On line 256, it is stated that for very small step sizes, the `1/(Kγ)` term dominates the error for the proposed method. Could the authors elaborate on why a similar dominance effect is not observed for the standard SGLD algorithm under the same conditions?

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Eq. 1:** The notation used for the parameter in this equation is inconsistent with `θ` used throughout the rest of the paper.
*   **Line 94:** The acronym "RR" is used before it has been formally defined.
*   **Line 131:** The term `Γ_K` is not defined.
*   **Line 160:** The text mentions a "smooth function f." Please specify the precise smoothness assumptions (e.g., Lipschitz continuous, continuously differentiable).
*   **Line 249:** The caption for Figure 1(b) is described as "Bias vs. time," but the plot appears to show "Bias vs. dimension." Should this reference be to Figure 1(c) instead?
*   **General:** For improved clarity, please use boldface notation to represent all vectors consistently throughout the manuscript.
