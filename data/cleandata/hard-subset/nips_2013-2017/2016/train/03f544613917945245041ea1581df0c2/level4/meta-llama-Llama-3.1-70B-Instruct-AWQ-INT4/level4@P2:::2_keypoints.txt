- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved convergence rate over SGLD.
- Proofs for main theorems are provided in the supplementary material.
- Synthetic experiments show SGRRLD reduces bias compared to SGLD.
- Improvements on real data are less convincing and need stronger empirical support.
- Paper is well-organized and the algorithm description is clear and implementable.
- Unclear what conditions on the potential U are required for the theorems; need clarity on whether U can be multi-modal.
- Use of correlated Brownian motions is justified, but how this reduces error order is not clearly explained.
- Concern that uncorrelated gradient noise may quickly decorrelate chains despite correlated Gaussian noise; chain correlation should be investigated empirically.
- Experimental comparisons are potentially unfair: SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) is compared to SGLD at γ rather than SGLD at γ/2 or two parallel SGLD chains.
- Computation-time claim (comparable to parallelized SGLD) is questionable; a fairer baseline is running multiple SGLD chains in parallel.
- Recommend additional experiments: compare SGRRLD vs two-chain SGLD and SGLD with the same smaller step size to better assess bias reduction.
- Application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is original and promising.
- Results are potentially significant for bias reduction in large-scale Bayesian inference but need stronger real-data validation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying theoretical conditions and adding more convincing experiments on real datasets.
