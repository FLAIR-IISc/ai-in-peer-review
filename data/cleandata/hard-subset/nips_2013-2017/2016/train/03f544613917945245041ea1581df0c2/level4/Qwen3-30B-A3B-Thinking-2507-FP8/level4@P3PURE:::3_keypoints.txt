- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Runs two parallel chains: Chain 1 with step size γ, Chain 2 with step size γ/2 run for twice the steps, with correlated Gaussian noise α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2.  
- Estimator is the difference between Chain 2 and Chain 1 estimates, yielding bias O(γ^2) versus O(γ) for single chains and a corresponding MSE reduction.  
- Method is simple, requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and is broadly applicable.  
- Novelty is adapting Richardson-Romberg (a classical numerical technique) to SG-MCMC where it has been underused.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Applicability concern: technique assumes π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ^2); are there further conditions required?  
- Extension question: can this be applied to non-stochastic-gradient approximate MCMC (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) or distributed SGLD?  
- Computational efficiency concern: Chain 2’s doubled runtime creates idle time for Chain 1; would using three chains (one γ, two γ/2) with equal run lengths reduce waste and how would that affect variance?  
- Question about multi-step-size extension: is there a natural extension to more than two step sizes?  
- Noise correlation issue: is variance minimized when Chain 1’s k-th iteration uses the same minibatch as Chain 2’s (2k−1) and 2k iterations, and is the correlation’s effect negligible otherwise?  
- Experimental validation: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to demonstrate any Bayesian advantage, since Bayesian MF isn’t widely used.  
- Accessibility suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode early to help practitioners.
