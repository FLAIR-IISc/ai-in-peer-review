- Paper introduces SGRRLD: Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SGLD to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Theoretical claims: SGRRLD has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments clearly show SGRRLD reduces bias relative to SGLD.  
- MovieLens real-data results show only slight improvement in test RMSE, making practical benefit arguable.  
- RR extrapolation is known to improve SDE Monte Carlo convergence; paper adapts this to SG-MCMC and proves faster convergence under certain conditions.  
- Faster convergence comes at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains.  
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation is straightforward.  
- Reviewer requests more real-data experiments to demonstrate practical impact of the modification.  
- Reviewer did not verify proofs and questions the conditions on U required by Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., can U be multimodal?).  
- Unclear mechanism: how combining two chains reduces the error order, especially if gradient noises decorrelate quickly.  
- Asks whether the authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Experimental setup concern: comparing SGRRLD to SGLD should account for parallelization—compare SGRRLD to multiple parallel SGLD chains and to SGLD with smaller step size (γ/2) run in parallel.
