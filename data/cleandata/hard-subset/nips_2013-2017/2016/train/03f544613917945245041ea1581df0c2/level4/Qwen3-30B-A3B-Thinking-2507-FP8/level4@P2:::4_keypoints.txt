- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (run two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine) to reduce bias.
- Claims improved rates: bias O(γ²) and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(γ) and O(K⁻²/³).
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC and higher-order integrator work but offers a simpler, more practical alternative.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds under stated conditions.
- Method is practical and easy to implement with minimal code changes and preserves parallelizability for large-scale use.
- Experiments (synthetic Gaussian) show clear bias/MSE improvements, e.g., variance error reduced from ~1e-2 to ~1e-4.
- MovieLens matrix factorization experiments show better accuracy than SGLD for comparable computation time.
- Experimental fairness issue: two-chain parallel execution on single-core setups needs time-scaling adjustment (Figure 5 not accounted for).
- Discrepancy in reported optimal α: theory cites α=1/5 or 1/3 (text inconsistent), but Figure 3 shows best at α=0.2 and noisy curves—needs clarification/replication.
- Theoretical claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates for small γ conflicts with empirical SGLD behavior; explanation required.
- Notational and clarity issues: inconsistent use of θ vs. ϑ, "RR" undefined on first use, Γ_K introduced without definition.
- Theorem 1 lacks precise smoothness requirements for test function f.
- Figure labeling error: Figure 1(b) labeled "Bias vs. dimension" but actually plots bias vs. time (likely should be 1(c)).
- Strength: original, clever application of numerical analysis to SG-MCMC that avoids complex higher-order integrators.
- Strength: strong theoretical justification and generalizability to other SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Strength: method aligns with modern parallel architectures and is straightforward to integrate into existing frameworks.
- Weakness: mismatch between theoretical and observed convergence exponents and noisy empirical curves.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons need fair accounting of two-chain runtime; several theoretical points need clearer explanation.
- Recommendation: strong contribution deserving acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify theoretical claims, experimental fairness, and fix notation/figures.
