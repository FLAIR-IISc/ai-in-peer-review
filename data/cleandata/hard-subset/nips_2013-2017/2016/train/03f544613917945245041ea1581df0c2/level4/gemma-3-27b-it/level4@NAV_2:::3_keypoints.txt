- Main contribution: Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2).
- Implementation detail: Correlates Gaussian noise between chains (αk = (β2k-1 + β2k)/√2) and combines estimates to obtain the extrapolated expectation.
- Theoretical result: Shows bias reduction and corresponding MSE improvement with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Novelty/impact: Novel combination of RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC not previously explored in ML; simple and broadly applicable approach.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and well-organized, facilitating understanding of method and proofs.
- Generality concern: Needs explicit delineation of conditions beyond Section 2.2 under which the extrapolation assumption (πγ(f) = π(f) + C·γ + O(γ^2)) holds for different SG-MCMC schemes.
- Extension suggestion: Investigate applicability to other approximate MCMC methods (e.g., sub-sampling Metropolis–Hastings, distributed SGLD).
- Computational efficiency concern: Running one chain for twice the iterations may be inefficient; alternative schemes (e.g., three chains with equal lengths) should be explored and analyzed for variance.
- Multi-step extension: Potential to extend RR extrapolation to more than two step sizes is worth investigating.
- Noise-correlation question: Clarify how correlated mini-batches affect performance and whether using the same mini-batch across corresponding iterations minimizes variance.
- Experimentation weakness: Matrix factorization experiment should be run to convergence, include comparison to SGD, and demonstrate cases where Bayesian inference gives practical advantages.
- Usability suggestion: Add an algorithm box to improve accessibility for practitioners.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, theoretically justified, shows empirical gains, and is simple to implement.
- Rejection arguments: Practical applicability may be limited by computational inefficiency, unclear effects of noise correlation, and a weaker experimental evaluation on matrix factorization.
