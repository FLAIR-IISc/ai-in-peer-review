- Bridges MCMC and numerical SDE literatures by applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SGLD.  
- Proposes a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and conditions where SGLD-RRE outperforms standard SGLD.  
- Empirical results show significant gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements at scale.  
- Clearly articulates cross-field connections, promoting idea cross-pollination between MCMC and SDE communities.  
- Demonstrates RRE mitigates SGLD bias without substantially increasing computational overhead.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and accessible to both MCMC and SDE practitioners.  
- Method is a practical refinement likely to be adopted, though not a revolutionary change to MCMC practice.  
- Figure 1a should explicitly state the plotted Gaussian is an empirical approximation using posterior mean and variance.  
- Figure 2a shows counterintuitive non-monotonic bias vs. stepsize that requires brief discussion (e.g., discretization error vs. stochastic noise).  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution deserving publication pending minor clarifications.
