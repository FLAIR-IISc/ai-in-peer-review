- Paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme for SG-MCMC that improves bias and MSE convergence rates.
- Method is simple to implement, requiring only minor modifications to standard SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Theoretical foundations are strong and the methodology appears sound.
- Major concern: the derivation of the claimed specific convergence rates from the main theorem is questionable (potentially fatal flaw).
- Practical concern: in the matrix factorization experiment the method uses two chains and would take ~3× longer on a single core, so runtime comparisons (Figure 5) are misleading.
- Correcting runtime for single-core execution would require scaling SGLD time by 2/3, reducing the reported advantage of SGRRLD.
- Figure 3 (bias) appears to show the optimal curve at α=0.2 (red), not the claimed α=1/3, casting doubt on the stated convergence rate.
- The fluctuating optimal curve suggests the observed convergence rate may not be consistent with the theoretical α=0.2 or α=1/3 claim.
- The claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates when step size is too small does not seem to hold empirically for SGLD and needs explanation.
- Minor issues: Equation (1) uses a different symbol than θ without justification.
- Minor issues: abbreviation "RR" is introduced without definition (line 94).
- Minor issues: ΓK is used without explanation (line 131).
- Minor issues: the term "smooth" for function f is vague—type/degree of smoothness should be specified (line 160).
- Minor issues: line 249 references Figure 1(b) as plotting bias versus time rather than dimension—likely meant Figure 1(c).
- Presentation suggestion: use bold letters for vectors throughout to improve clarity.
