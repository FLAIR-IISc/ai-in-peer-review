- Proposal: Use Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, Chain2 runs twice as many steps, noises correlated via αk=(β2k−1+β2k)/√2) and combining estimates to reduce bias to O(γ^2) from O(γ).  
- The paper also establishes a corresponding MSE reduction.  
- Strength: Method is simple, easy to implement, and broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novelty: RR extrapolation is known in numerical analysis but is not widely used in ML/SG-MCMC, so this application is a useful contribution.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and clearly presented.  
- Applicability question: Does the technique apply to all SG-MCMC methods, and what additional assumptions beyond the bias expansion πγ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2) are required?  
- Extension question: Can the approach be adapted to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) or distributed SGLD?  
- Resource/utilization question: Because Chain2 runs twice as long, Chain1 is idle half the time—could one run three chains (one γ, two γ/2) in parallel to avoid idling, and how would that affect estimator variance?  
- Multi-level question: Is there a natural k-level Richardson–Romberg extension, and how should coefficients and computational budgets be chosen?  
- Correlation/minibatch question: How should minibatch coupling be handled—does sharing minibatches between corresponding steps minimize variance or is minibatch coupling negligible relative to noise coupling?  
- Experiments critique: Suggest running experiments (e.g., matrix factorization) to full convergence, include an SGD baseline, and report final errors for SGRRLD, SGLD, and SGD to better demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Accessibility suggestion: Include an algorithm box/pseudocode early to lower the barrier for practitioners.
