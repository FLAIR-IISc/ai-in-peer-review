This paper proposes applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE), a standard numerical integration technique, to modern MCMC methods designed for large datasets, focusing specifically on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). A key contribution of this work is bridging the literature on numerical solutions for stochastic differential equations (SDEs), where RRE has recently been applied successfully, and the MCMC literature, a connection that should prove beneficial to both communities. The authors introduce a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, establish theoretical results for its convergence, and provide arguments for when it should outperform standard SGLD. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through numerical experiments, which show impressive improvements on synthetic examples and modest but consistent gains on large-scale problems.

Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper. The writing is clear and the technical content appears sound. Its primary strengths are the valuable connection it makes between two distinct fields, the supporting theoretical analysis, and the strong empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The resulting method is a relatively straightforward, yet effective, application of RRE to SGLD. Its computational convenience suggests that it could be a practical tool for researchers. While the method is unlikely to revolutionize current practice, it represents a useful incremental improvement, and I expect practitioners will find it valuable.
