- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real (movie recommendation) data shows improved convergence over SGLD and near higher-order-integrator accuracy.  
- Positions work within SG-MCMC literature, building on SGLD and prior bias-reduction efforts.  
- Novelty: RR extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC is a new and promising idea.  
- Strong theoretical foundation supporting the algorithm’s claims.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness and improved accuracy/convergence rates.  
- Parallelizability: independent chains enable scalable implementation for large datasets.  
- Presentation is clear with well-defined connections to prior work.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to methods using higher-order integrators to substantiate accuracy claims.  
- Step size selection/consistency is mentioned but not fully resolved or clarified.  
- Minor inconsistency between Algorithm 1 (supplement) and Equation (5) (main text) needs correction.  
- Limited discussion of variance control and the effect of the correlation parameter Σ on performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically effective, and scalable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing direct higher-order comparisons and minor technical inconsistencies.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons and clarifications.
