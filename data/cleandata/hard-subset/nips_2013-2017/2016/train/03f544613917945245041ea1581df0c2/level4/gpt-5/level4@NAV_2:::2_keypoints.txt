- Introduces Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation into SGLD (SGRRLD) to mitigate bias from stochastic gradient noise, claiming asymptotically smaller bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Theoretical claims: SGRRLD attains faster convergence rate than SGLD with same stepsize, proven under stated conditions, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and appears straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiment convincingly demonstrates bias reduction and clear improvement over SGLD.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) results show only modest RMSE improvements and are open to interpretation.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on the precise assumptions on U required for Theorems 1 and 2.  
- Question whether the theorems accommodate multimodal potentials.  
- Concern about chain decorrelation: if stochastic gradients are uncorrelated, how does extrapolation still achieve the claimed error-order reduction when chains may decorrelate rapidly?  
- Asked whether authors empirically measured correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.  
- Experimental-baseline concerns: equal wall-clock via parallelization is reasonable, but one could run two SGLD chains in parallel under the same budget—does averaging two independent SGLD chains match SGRRLD’s RMSE gains?  
- Fairness of stepsize comparison: SGRRLD uses stepsizes γ and γ/2 while SGLD was run with γ—baseline should include SGLD with stepsize γ/2 (and possibly two parallel chains).  
- Recommendation: include additional real-data experiments to strengthen the empirical case for consistent bias reduction.
