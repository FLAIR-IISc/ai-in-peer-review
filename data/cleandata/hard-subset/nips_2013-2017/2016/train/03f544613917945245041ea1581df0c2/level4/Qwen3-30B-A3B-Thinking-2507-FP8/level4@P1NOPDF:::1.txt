

This paper extends Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC methods, with a specific focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The authors establish a meaningful bridge between the MCMC and numerical SDE literatures—a connection that could benefit both fields. They propose a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, establish theoretical convergence guarantees, and provide arguments for when SGLD-RRE outperforms standard SGLD. Empirical results demonstrate substantial gains on toy problems and consistent, though more modest, improvements in large-scale settings. Overall, this is a strong contribution: it offers a clear theoretical link between two communities, delivers technically sound results, and presents a well-motivated, computationally efficient method. The writing is exceptionally clear and accessible. While the core method is a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, its practical utility is significant—practitioners will likely adopt it, though it does not represent a paradigm shift in the field.  

Minor suggestions: Figure 1a is potentially misleading, as it shows a Gaussian approximation (with empirical posterior mean and variance) rather than the actual estimated posterior. This distinction should be explicitly clarified. In Figure 2a, a brief explanation would help address why bias does not necessarily decrease as stepsize diminishes—a counterintuitive observation that merits discussion.
