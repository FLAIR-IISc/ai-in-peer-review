- Proposes SGRRLD: a Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC, running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Strong theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, bounded non-asymptotic bias and MSE.
- Claims higher convergence rates than first-order SG-MCMC methods in both finite-time and asymptotic regimes.
- Empirical support on synthetic and real data; algorithm is easy to implement with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC.
- Unclear derivation: the paper does not clearly show how specific convergence rates are obtained from the theorem.
- Experimental fairness concern: matrix factorization comparison may be unfair because SGRRLD runs two chains and costs about 3x time on a single core; SGLD timings should be adjusted (×2/3).
- Figure 3 inconsistency: bias plot indicates optimal α ≈ 0.2, not 1/3 as claimed, and the optimal curve shows fluctuations that may affect rate estimates.
- Claim about 1/(Kγ) dominating for very small step sizes does not appear to hold for SGLD in the experiments.
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent θ symbols, undefined "RR", undefined Γ_K notation, unclear smoothness assumptions for f, and an incorrect figure reference (line 249).
- Overall verdict: promising contribution likely to improve SG-MCMC convergence if the above theoretical clarifications, experimental fairness adjustments, and minor fixes are addressed.
- Reviewer arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, thorough theory, experimental support, easy implementation.
- Reviewer arguments against acceptance: unclear rate derivations, potentially unfair experiments, and presentation/notation issues.
- Rating and confidence provided by reviewer: 8/10 rating, 8/10 confidence.
