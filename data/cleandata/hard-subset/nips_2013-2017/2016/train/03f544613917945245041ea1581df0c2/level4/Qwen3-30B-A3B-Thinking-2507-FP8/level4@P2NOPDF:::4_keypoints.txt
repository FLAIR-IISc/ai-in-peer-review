- Proposes SGRRLD, a Richardson-Romberg scheme improving bias and MSE for SG-MCMC with minimal implementation changes.  
- Theoretical foundation and convergence guarantees are strong but some derivations are unclear.  
- Theorem 1’s argument for improved rates lacks explicit linkage between asymptotic bias terms and stated rates.  
- Proofs need to justify how Richardson-Romberg extrapolation yields the claimed convergence gains.  
- Matrix factorization experiments (Fig. 5) use two chains and ~3× compute for SGRRLD, making time comparisons misleading.  
- Experimental results should be re-evaluated with time-normalized metrics (scale SGLD runtime by 2/3 for fairness).  
- Figure 3 contradicts the text: α=0.2 appears optimal, not α=1/3, and shows large fluctuations suggesting instability.  
- The discrepancy and fluctuations in Fig. 3 must be explained and reconciled with convergence assumptions.  
- Line 256’s claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates for small steps is not clearly observed for SGLD and needs clarification.  
- Minor fixes: consistent notation in Eq.(1) (θ vs. θ̃), define "RR" at first use, define Γ_K at line 131.  
- Specify smoothness condition for f (e.g., Lipschitz gradients) at line 160.  
- Figure 1(b) label should match 1(c) ("Bias vs. dimension") and vectors (e.g., θ) should be bolded per notation standards.  
- Overall: promising and practically accessible idea, but experimental fairness, theoretical derivations, and figure inconsistencies must be addressed to avoid overstating claims.
