- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC sampler using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Adapts RR extrapolation from SDE numerical analysis to the SG-MCMC setting, building on and extending Chen et al. [10].  
- Claims SGRRLD attains the same theoretical accuracy as higher-order integrators while using only first-order integrators, broadening applicability.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis with asymptotic and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows MSE convergence O(K^{-4/5}) for SGRRLD versus SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}), indicating superior rate among first-order methods.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets support the theoretical improvements in accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Algorithm is parallelizable and suitable for distributed computing environments.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison against higher-order integrator methods that achieve similar theoretical rates (e.g., [10]).  
- Inconsistency between the main text and Supplementary Algorithm 1 in the description of step-size sequences (γ_k vs η_k).  
- Some step-size-related claims need clearer clarification and consistent notation.  
- Insufficient discussion of practical advantages (cost/complexity trade-offs) compared to existing higher-order integrators.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, demonstrated improved convergence, solid empirical validation, parallelizable, and generalizable framework.  
- Weaknesses: step-size inconsistency, missing direct comparisons to higher-order methods, unclear practical advantage arguments.  
- Overall: valuable, well-motivated contribution likely acceptable with minor clarifications and additional comparisons.
