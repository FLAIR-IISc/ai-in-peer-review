The authors propose a novel method called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SGRRLD) to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. This method employs Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-known technique in numerical analysis, to improve the rate of convergence of SG-MCMC. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error (MSE) of the estimator.

The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SG-MCMC is interesting and proved to be effective according to the empirical results. The authors demonstrate that SGRRLD can achieve higher rates of convergence than Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) and its extensions with first-order integrators, both in finite-time and asymptotically. They also show that SGRRLD can attain the theoretical accuracy of methods based on higher-order integrators.

The empirical results are satisfactory, and the authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their method on both synthetic and real data experiments. The results show that SGRRLD can significantly improve the performance of SGLD in terms of bias and MSE, especially in large-scale applications.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, there is an inconsistent statement about the step size in the paper. The authors should clarify the relationship between the step size and the convergence rate of SGRRLD. Secondly, Algorithm 1 in the supplement is not consistent with Equation (5). The authors should ensure that the algorithm and the equation are consistent.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms. The theoretical analysis and empirical results are comprehensive and well-presented. With some minor revisions to address the inconsistencies, the paper is ready for publication.
