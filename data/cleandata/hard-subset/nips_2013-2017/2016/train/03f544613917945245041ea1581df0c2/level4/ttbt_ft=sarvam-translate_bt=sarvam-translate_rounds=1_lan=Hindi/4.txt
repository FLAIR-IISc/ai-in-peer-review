This paper improves upon existing SG-MCMC by proposing the Richardson-Romberg scheme, which improves convergence rates in the context of bias and MSE. The scheme is easy to implement, requiring minimal modification to the traditional SG-MCMC. The paper is also supported by solid theoretical results. The paper proposes a Romberg-Richardson scheme that can improve convergence rates of standard SG-MCMC algorithms; the method looks good, and the theory looks solid. The most concerning part is how specific convergence rates are obtained from the theorem (as explained in the "Clarify the Fatal Flaws" section). Furthermore, I have a practical concern about the matrix factorization experiment result. To be fair, if we run the proposed method on a core computer, it will take 3 times longer than standard SG-MCMC because it has two chains, and one of them uses twice as many steps. If we take this into account, the plots in Figure 5 will not be fair; the time for SGLD should be multiplied by a factor of 2/3, which will reduce the difference between SGLD and SGRRLD. In Figure 3, the bias plot seems to show that the best convergence is achieved by the red treatment, which corresponds to \alpha=0.2, not the 1/3 mentioned in the text; something is wrong? Furthermore, the optimal curve looks very bumpy, so the rate will not match \alpha=0.2; what are the reasons for this? Also, it is stated in line 256 that when the stepsize is very small, the term 1/K\gamma will dominate. I wonder why this does not happen for SGLD? Line 160, the smooth function f, what kind of smoothness? Line 249: Figure 1(b) plots bias versus time, not amplitude; should it be Figure 1(c)?
