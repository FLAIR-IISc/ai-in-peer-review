- Proposes accelerating SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two correlated chains with different step sizes.  
- Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis supporting bias and MSE improvements.  
- Experiments: synthetic Gaussian validates theory; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with a valid contribution and good literature positioning.  
- Major concern 1 (novelty): Contribution may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators [10] and Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23]; clearer differentiation needed.  
- Major concern 2 (practicality/experiments): Parallel implementation requires per-step communication for correlated noise, but the paper treats this as cost-free and should discuss communication overhead.  
- Major concern 2 (resources/fairness): Unclear how computational resources were allocated between SGRRLD’s two chains and baseline SGLD; fairness and wall-clock implications need clarification.  
- Major concern 2 (alternative baseline): Should compare to two independent SGLD chains run embarrassingly parallel to isolate benefits of correlation/extrapolation vs. parallelization.  
- Minor comment: The acronym "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is defined twice.
