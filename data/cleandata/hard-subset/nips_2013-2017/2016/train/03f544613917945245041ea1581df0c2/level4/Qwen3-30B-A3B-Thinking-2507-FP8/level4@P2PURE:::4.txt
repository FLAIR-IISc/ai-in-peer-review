

This paper introduces a Richardson-Romberg scheme to enhance the convergence rates of existing SG-MCMC methods, improving both bias and MSE. The approach is straightforward to implement, requiring minimal modifications to standard SG-MCMC. Theoretical results are well-supported and rigorous. However, several critical points require clarification:  

1.  **Convergence Rate Justification**: The derivation of specific convergence rates from the theorems (as referenced in the "explain fatal flaws" section) needs greater detail. How precisely are these rates obtained?  
2.  **Experimental Fairness (Figure 5)**: The matrix factorization experiment compares runtime, but the proposed method uses two chains (one with twice the steps), requiring approximately three times the computation time of standard SG-MCMC on a single core. Thus, the time for SGLD in Figure 5 should be scaled by a factor of 2/3, which would significantly reduce the observed performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD. Without this adjustment, the comparison is unfair.  
3.  **Bias Convergence (Figure 3)**: The best convergence rate in the bias plot (Figure 3) corresponds to the red curve with $\alpha = 0.2$, contradicting the text’s claim that $\alpha = 1/3$ is optimal. Additionally, the optimal curve exhibits significant fluctuations, making the claimed convergence rate inconsistent with $\alpha = 0.2$. The cause of this fluctuation requires explanation.  
4.  **Small Step Size Behavior**: Line 256 states that for very small step sizes, the term $1/(K\gamma)$ dominates. Why does this effect not manifest in SGLD?
