This paper applies Richardson-Romberg Extrapolation (RRE) to the recent "big-data MCMC" method, specifically focusing on Stochastic gradient language dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a widely used numerical integration tool that has recently seen some excellent applications in the field of numerical Stochastic Differential Equations (SDE). A notable feature of this paper is that it establishes a link between MCMC and SDE literature, which could be beneficial to both communities. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE method, showing some theoretical results on the convergence of the new algorithm (including arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves over vanilla SGLD) and some numerical results. They achieved impressive improvements in toy numerical examples and slight but consistent improvements in larger-scale examples. Overall, it is a reliable and clearly written paper. As I mentioned above, it is a strong paper, with an interesting link between two somewhat isolated fields; interesting theoretical results; and good experimental performance of the proposed new algorithm. The paper is very clear and easy to read, and also seems technically sound. The method is ultimately a simple application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I think practitioners will use these methods, although these methods will not revolutionize current practice. A few minor comments: I found Figure 1a to be somewhat confusing – here, it is not experimentally inferred posterior distribution, but rather a Gaussian with experimental posterior mean and variance. This should be clarified/emphasized for careless readers. Figure 2a – it would be helpful to add some discussion about why bias does not decrease as the step size decreases; it is somewhat counterintuitive.
