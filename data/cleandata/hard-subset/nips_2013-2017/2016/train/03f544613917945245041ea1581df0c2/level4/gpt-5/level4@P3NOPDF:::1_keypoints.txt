- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SGLD, importing a bias-reduction tool from SDE numerics into big-data MCMC.  
- Proposes SGLD-RRE: a simple, implementable modification that aims to reduce discretization bias with little extra complexity.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results and conditions showing when SGLD-RRE improves over vanilla SGLD.  
- Presents toy and large-scale experiments showing strong gains on controlled problems and modest but consistent improvements at scale.  
- Clear exposition and careful motivation make the paper easy to follow.  
- Theoretical arguments are technically sound and align with algorithmic choices.  
- Practically useful: RRE is a drop-in change with limited code/runtime overhead.  
- Empirical results credibly support the main claims via a balance of toy and real-world settings.  
- Novelty is moderate because applying RRE to SGLD is straightforward, though this simplicity aids adoption.  
- Large-scale improvements are modest, suggesting guidance is needed on when the method is most impactful.  
- Paper needs more practical guidance on choosing step-size pairs, weights, schedules, minibatch sizes, and stability considerations.  
- Would benefit from clearer cost–benefit analysis (e.g., extra gradients, wall-clock vs. error, ESS/sec or error vs. time).  
- Should position SGLD-RRE relative to other bias-reduction, multilevel/debiasing methods, and related samplers (SGHMC, preconditioned/variance-reduced variants).  
- Clarify how theoretical assumptions (smoothness, dissipativity, noise) map to experimental models and potential violations.  
- Minor: clarify Figure 1a caption that the panel uses Gaussian with empirical mean/variance, not the empirical posterior.  
- Minor: discuss non-monotonic bias behavior in Figure 2a and possible causes (higher-order terms, gradient noise, burn-in, coupling errors).  
- Minor: provide exact RRE coefficients/level combos and default hyperparameters for reproducibility.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed, practically useful paper with persuasive theory and experiments; recommend acceptance.
