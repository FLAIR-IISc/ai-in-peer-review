- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical novelty: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, improving convergence without higher-order integrators.  
- Main theoretical results: SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a CLT, with O(γ²) bias and O(γ⁴) variance.  
- MSE improvement: for fixed γ, SGRRLD achieves O(K⁻⁴/⁵) MSE vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Practical implementation: algorithm is simple, with clear pseudocode and suitable for parallel/distributed architectures.  
- Strong synthetic validation: linear Gaussian experiments clearly show reduced bias relative to SGLD.  
- Limited real-world validation: MovieLens results show only modest RMSE gains and lack compelling practical impact.  
- Incomplete experimental comparisons: did not compare against SGLD with step size γ/2 (or two parallel γ/2 chains), which would be a fairer baseline.  
- Unclear theoretical assumptions: conditions on the potential U (e.g., multi-modality) are not sufficiently specified for the theoretical claims.  
- Missing empirical evidence on chain correlation: no experiments showing the actual correlation between the two parallel chains and its effect on variance reduction.  
- Paper quality and originality: strong theoretical contribution and clear presentation, representing a meaningful advance in SG-MCMC.  
- Clarity: explanations and theory are clear, but experimental section needs strengthening.  
- Significance: moderate—practically useful approach but real-world benefits are not yet convincingly demonstrated.  
- Recommended revisions: compare against SGLD with γ/2 or two parallel chains, provide more real-world experiments, and clarify theoretical conditions and empirical chain correlations.
