To reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC), the authors propose a novel method called stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for solving stochastic differential equations – to improve accuracy. Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC runs two chains in embarrassingly parallel fashion without requiring communication between them. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of their approach, and support this with satisfactory empirical results. The idea is interesting and, as evidenced by the experimental findings, effective. It can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). While the method achieves theoretical efficiency gains through an improved convergence rate, this benefit could potentially be obtained using higher-order integrators; unfortunately, the authors do not include a comparison to such integrators in their experiments. Furthermore, there is an inconsistency in the discussion of the step size. Finally, Algorithm 1 (in the supplementary material) does not align with Equation (5) in the main text.
