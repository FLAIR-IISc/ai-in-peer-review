- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, running two chains with different step sizes.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis: proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Claims improved convergence rates over SGLD and other first-order SG-MCMC methods.  
- Novelty: using RR extrapolation in SG-MCMC is a new idea.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show the method is effective.  
- Concern: specific convergence rates from theorems are not clearly explained or derived.  
- Concern: matrix factorization experiment is unfair—SGRRLD ran on a single core so wall-clock comparisons to SGLD are misleading.  
- Concern: Figure 3’s bias plot suggests best convergence at α=0.2, contradicting the text’s claim of 1/3.  
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent symbols, undefined terms, and incorrect figure references.  
- Ratings summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective algorithm, strong theoretical support, empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear rate derivations, unfair experimental comparison, and minor editorial issues.
