This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD) that applies the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to reduce the bias of stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD). The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and achieves a higher rate of convergence than SGLD. The experimental results on synthetic data demonstrate the advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD in reducing bias. However, the improvement on real data experiments is less convincing, and further experiments are needed to fully assess the benefits of SGRRLD.

The paper is well-organized, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the proofs of Theorem 1 and 2 are provided in the supplementary document. However, it would be helpful to clarify the conditions required for the potential energy function U to satisfy both theorems. Specifically, can U be multi-modal?

The authors' decision to use correlated Brownian motions in the two chains is justified, but it is unclear how the combination of the two chains reduces the order of the error. Intuitively, when the gradient noises are not correlated, the two chains will become uncorrelated quickly, even if the Gaussian noises are correlated. It would be interesting to investigate the correlation between the two chains in real data experiments.

The experimental setup and real data experiments raise some concerns. The authors argue that the computation time is about the same as SGLD with parallelization. However, it is possible to run SGLD with multiple parallel chains within the same time. A more convincing comparison would be to evaluate the estimation from two chains of SGLD against SGRRLD. Additionally, the real data experiment compares SGRRLD with a step size gamma and gamma/2 against SGLD with a step size gamma. It would be more informative to compare SGRRLD with SGLD using the same step size gamma/2, which has a lower bias, and run two chains in parallel.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach to reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms. However, further experiments are needed to fully demonstrate the benefits of SGRRLD, particularly on real data sets.
