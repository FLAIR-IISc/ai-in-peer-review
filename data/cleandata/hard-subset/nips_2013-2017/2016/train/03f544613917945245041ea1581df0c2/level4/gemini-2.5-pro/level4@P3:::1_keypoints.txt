- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading bias.
- Emphasizes that using correlated noise between the two chains is crucial to control estimator variance.
- Provides rigorous theory: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), outperforming standard SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy.
- Empirically validates bias reduction on a synthetic Gaussian model and demonstrates practical speedups on large-scale matrix factorization.
- Method is inherently parallelizable, making it suitable for modern compute architectures and practical use.
- Strong conceptual contribution linking SG-MCMC literature with classical numerical SDE methods, encouraging cross-field ideas.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible with appropriate background material.
- Computational overhead: SGRRLD requires more gradient evaluations (≈3× in serial), relying on parallel resources to match wall-clock time.
- Lacks discussion of the computational cost versus statistical-efficiency trade-off in serial settings.
- Incremental originality: effective combination of established techniques, framed as an evolutionary rather than revolutionary advance.
- Figure 1(a) likely plots fitted Gaussians from empirical mean/variance rather than empirical distributions—caption/text should clarify.
- Figure 2’s increase in bias/MSE for very small step sizes needs explanation, likely due to the 1/(Kγ) term in Theorem 2; connect theory to this empirical behavior.
