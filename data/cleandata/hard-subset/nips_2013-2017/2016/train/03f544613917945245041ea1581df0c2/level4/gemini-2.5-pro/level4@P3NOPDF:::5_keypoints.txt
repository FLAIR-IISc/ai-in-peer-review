- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining outputs to reduce numerical integration bias.

- Novel and simple idea: repurposes a classic numerical-analysis technique for SG-MCMC in an elegant, easy-to-implement way.

- Practical and efficient: the two chains are embarrassingly parallel, enabling straightforward deployment on modern hardware.

- Theoretical support: provides a proof of asymptotic consistency showing the estimator converges to the correct target distribution.

- Empirical validation: experiments demonstrate a tangible reduction in bias compared to baseline SG-MCMC methods.

- Needs stronger theoretical analysis: should prove an improved convergence rate/higher-order accuracy to quantify efficiency gains versus baseline.

- Missing comparisons: should empirically compare against alternative high-order SDE integrators (e.g., stochastic Runge–Kutta) to clarify trade-offs in accuracy, stability, and cost.
