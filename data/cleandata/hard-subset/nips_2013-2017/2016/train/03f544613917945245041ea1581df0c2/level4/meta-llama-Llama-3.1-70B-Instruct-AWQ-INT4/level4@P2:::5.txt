This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC methods. The authors employ Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-known method in numerical analysis, to improve the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm. Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC runs two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes and combines their outputs using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation.

The paper relates the proposed method to previous work on SG-MCMC, such as Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) and Stochastic Gradient Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (SGHMC). The authors provide a formal theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, showing that it is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error of the algorithm.

The empirical results presented in the paper are satisfactory, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in reducing the bias of SG-MCMC. The authors conduct experiments on both synthetic and real data, including a large-scale matrix factorization problem. The results show that SG-RR-MCMC outperforms SGLD and SGHMC in terms of convergence rate and accuracy.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SG-MCMC is interesting and proved to be effective in reducing the bias of the algorithm.
* The theoretical analysis provided in the paper is thorough and helps to understand the behavior of the proposed algorithm.
* The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the superiority of SG-RR-MCMC over existing SG-MCMC methods.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* There are some inconsistencies in the paper, such as the statement about the step size and the discrepancy between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5).
* The authors do not provide a comparison with high-order integrators in the experiments, which would be useful to validate the efficiency of the proposed method.
* The paper could benefit from more discussions on the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for reducing the bias of SG-MCMC. While there are some weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective method for reducing the bias of SG-MCMC.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and helps to understand the behavior of the proposed algorithm.
* The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the superiority of SG-RR-MCMC over existing SG-MCMC methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

* There are some inconsistencies in the paper that need to be addressed.
* The paper could benefit from more discussions on the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed method.
