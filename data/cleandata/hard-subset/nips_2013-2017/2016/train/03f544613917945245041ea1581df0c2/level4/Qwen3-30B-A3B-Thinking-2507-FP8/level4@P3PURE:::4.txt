

This paper presents a novel Richardson-Romberg scheme to enhance the convergence rates of stochastic gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) methods, improving both bias and mean squared error (MSE) rates. The proposed approach is straightforward to implement, requiring minimal modifications to standard SG-MCMC algorithms, and is supported by rigorous theoretical analysis. The method appears well-motivated, and the theoretical results are sound.

The primary concern pertains to the derivation of convergence rates in Theorem 1 (as referenced in the "explain fatal flaws" section). Specifically, the connection between the stated rates and the theorem's conditions requires further clarification. Beyond this, a practical issue arises with the matrix factorization experiment in Figure 5. The proposed SGRRLD method runs two chains (one with double the steps), implying that on a single-core system, it would require approximately three times the runtime of standard SGLD. Consequently, the time axis for SGLD in Figure 5 should be scaled by a factor of 2/3 to account for this, which would substantially reduce the apparent performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD. Without this adjustment, the comparison is not fair.

In Figure 3, the bias plot shows the best convergence at α=0.2 (red curve), contradicting the text's claim that α=1/3 is optimal. Please clarify this discrepancy. Additionally, the optimal curve exhibits significant fluctuations, which undermines the claimed convergence rate for α=0.2. The cause of these fluctuations should be addressed. 

Regarding line 256, the text states that for very small step sizes, the term 1/(Kγ) dominates. It is unclear why this effect is not observed in SGLD, despite similar step-size scaling.
