- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Method runs two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis with convergence results and bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Paper is well-written and situates the method relative to prior work.  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussians verify the theoretical bias and MSE bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical improvements over SGLD.  
- Main concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators [10] and Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Main concern: practical limitations due to required communication between parallel chains and unaccounted communication costs.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear resource allocation—whether SGLD and SGRRLD used comparable computational resources.  
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss communication costs and resource assignment in experiments.  
- Minor comment: repeated phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" at lines 25 and 70.  
- Overall: valid contribution with strong theory and experiments, but addressing novelty and practical/experimental concerns would strengthen the paper.
