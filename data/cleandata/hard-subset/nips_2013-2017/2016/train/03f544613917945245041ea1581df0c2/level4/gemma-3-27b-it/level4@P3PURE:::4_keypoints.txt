- Strength: Proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC convergence rates for bias and MSE with strong theoretical support.  
- Strength: Method is easy to implement with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Primary theoretical concern: Derivation of the specific convergence rates in the theorem appears questionable (see “explain fatal flaws” notes).  
- Practical concern: Matrix factorization experiment comparisons may be misleading because using two chains increases single-core computation ~3×; SGLD timings should be scaled (factor 2/3) for a fair comparison.  
- Figure 3 discrepancy: Bias plot shows best convergence at α = 0.2, not α = 1/3 as claimed — this inconsistency needs clarification.  
- Figure 3 stability issue: The optimal convergence curve for α = 0.2 fluctuates significantly, calling into question the reported convergence rate for that α; an explanation is needed.  
- Asymptotic term question: Authors claim 1/(Kγ) will dominate for sufficiently small stepsize, but this behavior is not observed with SGLD — request explanation.  
- Notation issue: Equation (1) uses different notation than the established θ convention — please standardize.  
- Undefined acronym: “RR” is introduced on line 94 without prior definition.  
- Undefined symbol: Γ_K is mentioned on line 131 but not defined.  
- Smoothness assumption: Line 160 refers to a “smooth function f” without specifying the type/degree of smoothness assumed.  
- Figure labeling error: Line 249 references Figure 1(b) plotting Bias versus time, but it should reference Figure 1(c).  
- Presentation suggestion: Use boldface consistently for vectors for clarity.
