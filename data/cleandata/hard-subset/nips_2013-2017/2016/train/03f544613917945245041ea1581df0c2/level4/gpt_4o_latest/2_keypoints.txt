- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SGLD, producing SGRRLD to mitigate bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical claims: SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE and faster convergence than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- Achieves improved rate at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and easy to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiment convincingly shows SGRRLD reduces bias and outperforms SGLD.  
- MovieLens real-data experiment shows only slight, debatable RMSE improvement; empirical gains on real data are limited.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on conditions for the potential U in Theorems 1 and 2, including whether U can be multi-modal.  
- Reviewer questions the mechanism: if gradient noise becomes uncorrelated, how does combining two chains reduce error order, and are the two chains correlated in real experiments?  
- Concerns about computational-cost claim: SGRRLD’s parallelization advantage is questionable since SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains within the same budget.  
- Experimental comparison may be unfair: authors compare SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) to SGLD at γ instead of comparing to two parallel SGLD chains or SGLD with step size γ/2.  
- Recommendation: include more real-world experiments to strengthen empirical validation of the proposed method.
