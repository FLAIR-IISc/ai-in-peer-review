This paper proposes a novel method called stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC) to reduce the bias of stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC). The authors leverage Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, an off-the-shelf tool for stochastic differential equations (SDE), to improve the accuracy of SG-MCMC. Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC involves running two chains in parallel without communication, which can be embarrassingly parallelized.

The theoretical analysis provided in the paper demonstrates that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. Moreover, the empirical results presented in the paper are satisfactory, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method. The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) is intriguing and has been proven to be effective in practice.

From a theoretical perspective, the efficiency of SG-RR-MCMC can be validated by providing a better convergence rate. However, it is worth noting that a similar convergence rate can be achieved using high-order integrators. Unfortunately, the authors did not provide a comparison with high-order integrators in their experiments, which would have strengthened their claims.

There are a couple of issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, there is an inconsistent statement about the step size in the paper, which needs to be clarified. Secondly, Algorithm 1 in the supplement is not consistent with Equation (5), which may cause confusion for readers. It is essential to ensure that the algorithm and equations are consistent to maintain the clarity and accuracy of the paper.

In conclusion, the proposed SG-RR-MCMC method is an interesting approach to reducing the bias of SG-MCMC. The theoretical analysis and empirical results presented in the paper are promising, and the idea has the potential to be effective in practice. However, the authors need to address the inconsistencies and provide a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods to further strengthen their claims.
