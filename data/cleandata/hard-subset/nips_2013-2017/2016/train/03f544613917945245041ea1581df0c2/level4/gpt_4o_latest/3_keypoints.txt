- Paper proposes applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, Chain 2 twice as long) to cancel O(γ) bias.
- Correlated Gaussian noise is used with alphak = (beta_{2k-1} + beta_{2k})/√2 to align steps between the two chains.
- The extrapolated estimator reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and yields a similar reduction in MSE.
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and requires no major changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Approach appears broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC variants, potentially impactful for the community.
- Novelty is limited in numerical analysis terms but Richardson–Romberg is not widely known in ML/SG-MCMC contexts.
- Paper is well written and clearly presented.
- Question: Can the technique be applied to all SG-MCMC methods or only those satisfying the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2)?
- Question: Can the method be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., sub-sampling Metropolis–Hastings) or to Distributed SGLD?
- Concern: Running one chain twice as long wastes computation; suggestion to use three chains (one at γ and two at γ/2) run equally—question about resulting variance increase.
- Question: Can the method be generalized to more than two step sizes?
- Unclear how minibatch correlation affects variance; question whether using the same minibatch across corresponding iterations minimizes variance or if the effect is negligible.
- Experimental suggestion: Run matrix factorization experiments to convergence, include SGD baseline and more quantitative results to show practical Bayesian advantage over SGD and better contextualize SGRRLD vs SGLD.
- Presentation suggestion: Add a pseudocode/algorithm box early in the paper to aid practitioners who may skip theoretical sections.
