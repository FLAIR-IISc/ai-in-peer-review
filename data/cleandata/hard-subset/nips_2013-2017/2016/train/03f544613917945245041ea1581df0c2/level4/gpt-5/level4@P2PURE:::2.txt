This paper applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) to reduce the bias introduced by gradient estimation noise. The resulting SGRRLD algorithm has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD. A synthetic Linear Gaussian Model experiment demonstrates the advantage of the new algorithm over SGLD. On MovieLens, there is a slight improvement in test RMSE, though the evidence is debatable.

RR extrapolation is known in the SDE literature to improve the convergence rate of Monte Carlo estimators. This paper transfers that idea to SG-MCMC and proves that, under certain conditions, SGRRLD achieves a faster convergence rate than SGLD with the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains. The algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement. The synthetic experiments clearly show SGRRLD’s bias reduction on that task. It would be valuable to see more real-data experiments to demonstrate that a simple modification to SGLD can substantially reduce bias in practice.

I have not checked the proof details of Theorems 1 and 2, but I am curious about the precise conditions required of U for both theorems to hold. Can U be multimodal? Intuitively, if the gradient noises are uncorrelated, the two chains should decorrelate quickly even when the Gaussian noises are correlated. In that case, how does combining the two chains reduce the error order? In the real-data experiments, did the authors observe correlation between the two chains?

I have concerns about the experimental setup and the real-data study. The authors argue that, with parallelization, the computation time is comparable to SGLD. However, one could also run multiple SGLD chains in parallel within the same time budget. If we compare estimates from two parallel SGLD chains to SGRRLD, does the RMSE improvement persist? Another issue is that, in the real-data experiment, SGRRLD with step sizes γ and γ/2 is compared against SGLD with step size γ. We should also compare against SGLD with step size γ/2, which has lower bias, and allow two SGLD chains to run in parallel. Additionally, more real-data experiments should be included.
