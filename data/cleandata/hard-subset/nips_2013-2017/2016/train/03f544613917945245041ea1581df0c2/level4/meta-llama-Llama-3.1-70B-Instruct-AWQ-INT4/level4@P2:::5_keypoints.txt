- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: runs two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and combines outputs via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.
- Uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to improve convergence rate of SG-MCMC methods.
- Positions method relative to prior SG-MCMC work (SGLD, SGHMC).
- Provides theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem.
- Derives non-asymptotic bounds for bias and mean squared error.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data (including large-scale matrix factorization) show improved convergence and accuracy over SGLD/SGHMC.
- Strength: Novel and effective combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SG-MCMC that reduces bias.
- Strength: Thorough theoretical treatment clarifying algorithm behavior.
- Strength: Convincing empirical validation demonstrating superiority to baseline SG-MCMC methods.
- Weakness: Inconsistencies in the paper (conflicting step-size statements and mismatch between Algorithm 1 in supplement and Equation (5)).
- Weakness: No experimental comparison with high-order integrators to validate efficiency claims.
- Weakness: Lacks discussion on limitations and potential extensions of the method.
- Overall assessment: Novel, effective, and valuable contribution despite some weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel bias-reduction method, strong theoretical analysis, and convincing empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: paper inconsistencies, insufficient discussion of limitations/extensions, and missing comparisons to high-order integrators.
