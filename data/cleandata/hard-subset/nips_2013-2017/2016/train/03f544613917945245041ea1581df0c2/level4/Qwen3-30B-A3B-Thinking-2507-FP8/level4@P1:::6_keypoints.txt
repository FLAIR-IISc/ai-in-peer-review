- Introduces SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SG-MCMC chains (steps γ and γ/2) with correlated Brownian noise and extrapolating to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD achieves MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}) versus SGLD's O(K^{-2/3}), with rigorous asymptotic (CLT) and non-asymptotic (bias and MSE) analysis.
- Empirical results: synthetic Gaussian and real-world matrix factorization tasks show SGRRLD reduces bias and MSE and improves wall-clock performance versus SGLD.
- Generality: method extends beyond SGLD (demonstrated with SGHMC), indicating broader applicability to SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical development that attains higher-order convergence rates using only first-order integrators.
- Strength: thorough experimental validation aligning with theoretical claims.
- Strength: well-organized and clearly written presentation with appropriate technical depth.
- Weakness: novelty may be incremental given prior RR-for-SDE and SG-MCMC integrator analyses (Lemaire et al. 2015; Chen et al. 2015); needs clearer differentiation from prior work.
- Weakness: practical implementation glosses over communication cost—correlating Brownian motions across parallel chains requires inter-chain communication in distributed settings.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons lack clarity on computational resource allocation (were two chains run with same total resources as single-chain SGLD?), making timing claims ambiguous.
- Main contribution summary: shows RR extrapolation can elevate first-order SG-MCMC convergence to match higher-order methods while retaining parallelizability.
- Recommendation: accept — strong technical contribution with minor caveats on novelty clarification and implementation details.
