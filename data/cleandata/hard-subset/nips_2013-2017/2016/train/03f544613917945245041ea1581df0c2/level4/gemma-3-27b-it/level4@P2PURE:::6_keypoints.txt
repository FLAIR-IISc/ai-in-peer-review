- Introduces accelerating SG-MCMC via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation requiring two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for both asymptotic and non-asymptotic regimes.  
- Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate theoretical bias and MSE bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical benefits over standard SGLD.  
- Well-written paper with sound technical contribution and good literature contextualization.  
- Novelty may be incremental given prior analysis of numerical integrators for SGMCMC [10] and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Practical applicability limited: parallel implementation and correlated Brownian motion require inter-chain communication, introducing overhead not addressed.  
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge and account for the communication cost assumption.  
- Experimental resource allocation is unclear: unclear whether SGLD used equivalent resources to the two-chain SGRRLD.  
- If resources were equivalent, details are needed on how resources were partitioned between the two SGRRLD chains.  
- Given stochastic gradient evaluation dominates cost, unclear how splitting resources across two chains yields lower wall-clock time than a single fully-resourced SGLD; implementation details required.  
- Suggests comparing against two independent SGLD chains (no communication) as an additional baseline.  
- Minor: the phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" appears redundantly.
