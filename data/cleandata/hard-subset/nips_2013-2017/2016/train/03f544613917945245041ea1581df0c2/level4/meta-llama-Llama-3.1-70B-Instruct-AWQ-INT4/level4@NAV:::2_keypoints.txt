- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to mitigate gradient-estimation bias.  
- Theoretical results claim SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Requires running two parallel Markov chains; convergence rate improvement holds under specific conditions.  
- Algorithm description is clear and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) improvement in test RMSE is marginal and ambiguous.  
- Paper builds on prior RR-extrapolation work for SDEs and extends it to SG-MCMC.  
- Unclear whether target potential U satisfying Theorems 1 and 2 can be multimodal — conditions need clarification.  
- Questionable how combining two chains yields a reduced error order, especially when gradient noises may be uncorrelated.  
- Authors should report empirical correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Computational-time claim (comparable to SGLD with parallelization) is problematic since parallel SGLD chains are also feasible.  
- Recommend comparing SGRRLD to (a) two parallel SGLD chains and (b) SGLD with step size gamma/2 to isolate benefits.  
- Additional real-world experiments are needed to robustly validate the practical gains of SGRRLD.
