To mitigate the bias inherent in SG-MCMC, this paper introduces a novel approach named stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC. This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-established technique for Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs). Specifically, the implementation involves two chains running in an embarrassingly parallel manner without requiring any communication. A theoretical analysis is provided to demonstrate that the proposed method is asymptotically consistent, and the empirical results are compelling.

The core idea is interesting and its effectiveness is validated by the experimental findings. The method can be understood as a straightforward combination of the SGLD framework and Richardson-Romberg extrapolation. While the efficiency is demonstrated empirically, the theoretical claims could be strengthened by providing a superior convergence rate. It is worth noting that improved rates can also be achieved via high-order integrators, but a comparison against such methods was unfortunately omitted from the experiments.

However, there are a few points of concern. The manuscript contains an inconsistent statement regarding the step size. Additionally, there is a discrepancy between Algorithm 1 (provided in the supplement) and the formulation presented in Equation (5).
