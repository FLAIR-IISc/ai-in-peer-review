- Proposal: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, chain 2 runs twice as many steps) and subtracting estimates to cancel first-order bias.  
- Theoretical result: proves bias reduces from O(γ) to O(γ²) and shows corresponding MSE improvement.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, minimal implementation overhead, and broadly applicable across SG-MCMC variants.  
- Strength: well-written exposition and logical flow; adapts a known numerical technique to an underused area.  
- Major concern (Generality): unclear whether the bias expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²) holds for all SG-MCMC variants or if some algorithms (e.g., with non-standard noise) violate it.  
- Major concern (Broader applicability): unclear if technique extends to non-stochastic-gradient MCMC (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings or distributed SGLD).  
- Major concern (Computational efficiency): running chain 2 for twice the steps creates idle time for chain 1; asks whether a three-chain scheme (one γ, two γ/2) could mitigate idle time and how it affects variance.  
- Major concern (Multi-step-size extension): asks whether extension to more than two step sizes (γ, γ/2, γ/4, ...) is possible and how estimator construction and bias reduction would scale.  
- Major concern (Noise correlation mechanism): the required correlation via shared mini-batches is not fully specified—need clarification on whether the same mini-batches are used and what correlation structure minimizes variance.  
- Major concern (Empirical validation): matrix factorization experiment lacks an SGD baseline and convergence curves; needs comparison to SGD and trajectory plots to demonstrate practical Bayesian advantage.  
- Major suggestion (Accessibility): include a concise algorithm/pseudocode box early to enable practitioner implementation without deep theory.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, impactful, and promising; addressing generality, efficiency, and empirical validation concerns would substantially strengthen the paper.
