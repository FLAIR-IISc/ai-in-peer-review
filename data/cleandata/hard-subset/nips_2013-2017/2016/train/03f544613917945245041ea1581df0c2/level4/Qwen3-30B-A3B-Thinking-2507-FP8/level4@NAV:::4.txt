

This paper enhances existing SG-MCMC by introducing a Richardson-Romberg scheme, achieving improved convergence rates for bias and MSE. The approach is straightforward to implement, requiring minimal modifications to standard SG-MCMC, and is supported by robust theoretical results. The proposed Romberg-Richardson scheme effectively accelerates convergence rates of standard SG-MCMC, with a well-motivated method and solid theory. The primary concern lies in the derivation of specific convergence rates from the theorems (as detailed in the "Concerns" section). Additionally, a practical issue arises with the matrix factorization experiment: on a single-core system, the proposed method would take approximately 3 times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to two chains and one chain using double the steps. Consequently, the time comparisons in Figure 5 are unfair; scaling SGLD runtime by 2/3 would significantly reduce the apparent performance gap. Regarding Figure 3 (bias vs. steps), the optimal convergence appears to be achieved by the red curve (\(\alpha=0.2\)), contradicting the text's claim of \(\alpha=1/3\). Furthermore, the optimal curve exhibits excessive fluctuation, suggesting the convergence rate may not align with \(\alpha=0.2\); the cause of this behavior requires clarification.
