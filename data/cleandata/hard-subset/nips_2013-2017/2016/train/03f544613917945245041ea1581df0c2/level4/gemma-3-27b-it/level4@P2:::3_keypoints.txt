- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ²).  
- Shows a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE).  
- Method is simple and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.  
- Likely generalizable across a range of SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis with convergence guarantees and bias/MSE bounds.  
- Empirical experiments on synthetic and real datasets support theoretical claims.  
- Computational inefficiency: one chain must run twice as long, creating potential overhead and idle time.  
- Impact of correlated mini-batches on performance is not fully explored or clarified.  
- Matrix factorization experiment omits direct comparison to SGD, limiting evidence of Bayesian advantages.  
- Lack of pseudo-code reduces accessibility for practitioners unfamiliar with implementation details.  
- For acceptance: addresses a significant SG-MCMC bias problem with a simple, theoretically sound, and empirically validated approach.  
- Against acceptance: practical computational cost, incomplete SGD comparison, and insufficient discussion of mini-batch correlation.  
- Suggestion: explore alternate strategies to mitigate computational cost (e.g., three chains with equal lengths) and study variance effects.  
- Suggestion: expand and clarify analysis of mini-batch correlation and strategies to optimize it.  
- Suggestion: include an SGD baseline in the matrix factorization experiments.  
- Suggestion: add concise pseudo-code for the proposed SGRRLD algorithm.  
- Suggestion: clarify precise applicability conditions for RR extrapolation beyond the bounds in Section 2.2.  
- Suggestion: investigate multi-step or more-than-two step-size extensions.  
- Overall assessment: elegant, theoretically rigorous, and empirically validated work with practical issues to address; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
