- Proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC convergence rates for bias and MSE.  
- Method is simple to implement with minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC.  
- Paper is supported by strong, robust theoretical results.  
- Main theoretical concern: unclear how the specific convergence rates are derived from the stated theorem (see "explain fatal flaws").  
- Practical concern: matrix factorization experiment may be unfair — two-chain scheme costs ~3× on a single core; SGLD runtime in Fig.5 should be scaled by 2/3, reducing the reported advantage of SGRRLD.  
- Fig.3 inconsistency: bias plot shows best performance for α = 0.2 (red) whereas text claims α = 1/3 — possible error.  
- Fig.3 anomaly: the “optimal” curve has large fluctuations inconsistent with the claimed rate for α = 0.2 — needs explanation.  
- Question on line 256: when step size is very small the 1/(Kγ) term is said to dominate — why would this not also apply to SGLD?
