- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments clearly demonstrate SGRRLD’s bias reduction.  
- MovieLens real-data results show only marginal improvement in test RMSE.  
- Builds on prior RR work for SDEs, successfully extending the technique to SG-MCMC.  
- Theorems claim faster convergence than SGLD under certain conditions, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation appears straightforward.  
- More real-world experiments are needed to show consistent, significant practical benefits.  
- Reviewer questions the conditions on U required by Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., multimodality).  
- Curious how combining two chains reduces the error order and whether chains remain correlated in practice.  
- Main experimental concern: comparing wall-clock times is unfair since SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains; suggest comparing to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Recommend including SGLD with step size γ/2 in comparisons (and more robust real-data evaluations).
