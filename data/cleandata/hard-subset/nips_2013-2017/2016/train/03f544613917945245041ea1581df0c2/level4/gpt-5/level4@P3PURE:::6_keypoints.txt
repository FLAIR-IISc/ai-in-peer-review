- Paper proposes using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation via a higher-order integrator to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two correlated-step-size chains and provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees; experiments show theory matches on a synthetic model and improvements on real matrix factorization tasks.  
- Reviewer considers the technique well presented, theory solid, and a valid contribution but flags issues to address.  
- Novelty/positioning: clarify what is new beyond prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC and prior uses of RR extrapolation (specifically differentiate contributions from [10] and [23]).  
- Novel theoretical/practical claims: explicitly state any novel theoretical insights or practical design choices not covered by existing work.  
- Parallelism/communication: the method requires correlated Brownian motions (per-step communication); the paper must state the communication model and whether communication costs were included in reported wall-clock times.  
- Resource allocation fairness: clarify how computing resources were assigned — did SGLD get the same total resources as the two SGRRLD chains combined, and how were cores/GPUs and memory bandwidth split?  
- Wall-clock time and gradient cost: explain how splitting compute across partial chains leads to lower wall-clock time given gradient cost dominates; provide parallelization strategy, minibatch sizes per chain, synchronization frequency, and hardware details.  
- Alternative baseline: include or justify omission of the natural baseline of two independent SGLD chains run in parallel under the same total resource budget.  
- Minor: remove duplicate mentions of “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” (appears twice).
