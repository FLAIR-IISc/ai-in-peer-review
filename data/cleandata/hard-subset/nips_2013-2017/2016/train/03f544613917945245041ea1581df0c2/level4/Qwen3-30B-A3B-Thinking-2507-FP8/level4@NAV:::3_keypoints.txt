- SG-MCMC methods suffer asymptotic bias; paper proposes Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to reduce this bias.  
- Core algorithm: run two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2), correlate Gaussian noise via α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2, and combine estimates to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Result: the RR estimator achieves bias O(γ²) and similarly improved MSE versus single-chain SG-MCMC.  
- Implementation: minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, making the method easy to adopt in practice.  
- Scope/impact: broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC variants; applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is novel in ML.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Question: is the technique universally applicable or are extra conditions needed beyond the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²)?  
- Question: can the method extend to non-stochastic-gradient MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH, distributed SGLD)?  
- Efficiency concern: running Chain 1 for half the steps while Chain 2 runs wastes compute—would a 3-chain scheme (one γ, two γ/2, equal steps) reduce waste and what is the variance tradeoff?  
- Question: is there a straightforward generalization to more than two step sizes (higher-order RR)?  
- Clarification needed on noise correlation: does minimal variance require reuse of the same mini-batch pairing across corresponding iterations, or is the effect negligible?  
- Experiments: matrix factorization study should include convergence plots and an SGD baseline to better demonstrate practical advantage.  
- Usability: add algorithmic pseudocode to ease practitioner adoption without parsing theory.
