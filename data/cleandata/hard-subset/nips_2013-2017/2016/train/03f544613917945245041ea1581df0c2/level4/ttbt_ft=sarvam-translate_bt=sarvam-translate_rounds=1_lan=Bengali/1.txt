This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, with a particular focus on Stochastic gradient language dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a widely used numerical integration tool, which has recently seen some excellent applications in the field of numerical Stochastic Differential Equations (SDE). A notable feature of this paper is that it establishes a link between the MCMC and SDE literature, which could be beneficial to both communities. The authors have introduced a new SGLD-RRE method, derived some theoretical results on the convergence of the new algorithm (including arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves upon vanilla SGLD), and shown some numerical results. They have achieved impressive improvements in toy numerical examples and slight but consistent improvements in large-scale examples. Overall, this is a reliable and clearly written paper. As I mentioned above, it is a strong paper, with an interesting link between two somewhat isolated fields; interesting theoretical results; and good experimental performance of the proposed new algorithm. The paper is very clear and easy to read, and also seems technically sound. The method is ultimately a simple application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I think practitioners will use these methods, although these methods will not revolutionize current practice. A few minor comments: I found Figure 1a to be somewhat confusing – here, it is not experimentally estimated posterior distribution, but rather a Gaussian with experimental posterior mean and variance. This should be made clearer/more important for careless readers. Figure 2a – it would be helpful to add some discussion about why the bias does not decrease as the step size decreases; it is somewhat counterintuitive.
