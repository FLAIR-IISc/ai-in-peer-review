- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, creating SGRRLD to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Core algorithm runs two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and combines them via extrapolation.  
- Theoretical contributions include asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Proven faster convergence rates than SGLD, approaching accuracy of higher-order integrators.  
- Empirical results show compelling improvements on synthetic problems and consistent but modest gains on large-scale tasks.  
- The work bridges MCMC and SDE literatures, benefiting both communities.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD/SG-MCMC.  
- Theoretical rigor: comprehensive proofs and clear theoretical analysis strengthen the method’s validity.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-written, and accessible with precise notation and explanations.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and real datasets show SGRRLD outperforms SGLD and compares favorably to SGHMC in one case.  
- Parallelizability: algorithm naturally parallelizable, suitable for modern compute architectures.  
- Incremental improvement: practical gains are often modest, especially in large-scale experiments.  
- Sensitivity to step size: performance depends on step-size tuning despite theoretical guidance.  
- Figure issues: Figure 1a needs clarification that plotted curve is a Gaussian approximation, not the empirical posterior.  
- Counterintuitive behavior: Figure 2a’s non-monotonic bias vs. step-size requires further discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, rigorous theory, convincing experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact due to modest improvements and required tuning.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid, well-justified contribution likely to be adopted in practice, with only minor revisions needed.
