- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce discretization bias in SG-MCMC by combining two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2.  
- Core idea: run two parallel Markov chains and combine outputs to cancel the leading-order discretization error.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD achieves faster convergence rates for bias and MSE (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)), with CLT and non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Synthetic experiments (linear Gaussian) clearly demonstrate bias and MSE reduction consistent with theory.  
- Real-data experiments (MovieLens matrix factorization) show modest performance improvements over standard SGLD.  
- Strength — originality: novel and clever use of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation in SG-MCMC with potential wide applicability.  
- Strength — theoretical quality: provides rigorous convergence analysis, CLT, and bias/MSE bounds.  
- Strength — clarity/practicality: well-written, simple modification of SGLD and easy to implement.  
- Strength — empirical synthetic validation: controlled experiments strongly support theoretical claims.  
- Weakness — fairness of experiments: wall-clock parity claim is questionable because parallel resources could instead run stronger SGLD baselines.  
- Suggested baselines missing: (1) two independent SGLD chains with step γ averaged, and (2) two independent SGLD chains with step γ/2, both matching SGRRLD’s parallel computational budget.  
- Weakness — limited real-data evaluation: MovieLens results are modest; broader real-world experiments would strengthen practical claims.  
- Question on theory: main theorems depend on technical assumptions on potential U (in supplement); clarify scope and applicability to multimodal potentials.  
- Question on intuition: explain how extrapolation cancels error when stochastic gradient noise is independent across chains and report any measured chain correlation to justify effectiveness.
