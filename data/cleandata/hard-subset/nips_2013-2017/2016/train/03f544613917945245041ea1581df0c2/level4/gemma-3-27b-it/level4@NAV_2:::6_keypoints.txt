- Proposes SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions to reduce SGLD bias.  
- Provides rigorous theory: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and mean-squared error.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and large-scale matrix factorization show improved convergence and accuracy vs SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Strong connection to higher-order integrators and demonstrates comparable accuracy to those methods.  
- Novelty is incremental given prior work on numerical acceleration of SDEs and higher-order SG-MCMC integrators; needs clearer distinction from related work.  
- Practical concern: correlated Brownian motions require inter-chain communication, but paper lacks discussion of communication cost and performance impact.  
- Experimental setup unclear: resource allocation and whether SGLD baselines used comparable compute are not specified; wall-clock time comparisons may be unfair.  
- Suggested fairer baselines: run two independent SGLD chains or provide detailed computational cost breakdown to validate speed claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, promising empirical improvements, clear presentation, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, missing communication-cost discussion, and unclear experimental resource allocation.  
- Minor issues: duplicate mention of “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” and missing discussion of sensitivity to step-size and correlation choices.
