Summary
The paper proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson–Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SG-MCMC, focusing on SGLD. The method runs two coupled SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 to cancel leading-order discretization bias, while using correlated Gaussian increments to control variance. The authors establish consistency and a CLT, and provide non-asymptotic bounds showing improved bias and MSE over SGLD. Under appropriate step-size schedules, SGRRLD achieves O(K−4/5) MSE versus O(K−2/3) for SGLD, matching the accuracy of higher-order integrators without using them. Experiments on a linear Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization support these claims; the RR idea is also illustrated for SGHMC.

Relation to prior work
RR extrapolation is classical for SDE discretizations (e.g., Talay–Tubaro; Lemaire–Pagès), and recent SG-MCMC work analyzes bias/MSE and higher-order integrators (e.g., Teh et al.; Chen et al.). This paper appears to be the first to adapt RR to SG-MCMC and to analyze bias/variance trade-offs with correlated Brownian increments in this setting.

Strengths
- Conceptually simple and general: RR extrapolation can be layered onto many SG-MCMC methods with minimal code changes; the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.
- Sound theory: consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds are provided; the role of Brownian correlation (Σ = Id) is well-motivated and connected to prior diffusion results.
- Practical potential: achieves higher-order accuracy using first-order integrators, and parallelizes naturally across the two chains.
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias and MSE improvements; an SGHMC variant (SGRRHMC) supports generality.

Weaknesses and questions
- Scope of assumptions: Theorems require smoothness (e.g., Poisson equation solution g ∈ C^9) and ergodicity conditions. Please clarify the precise assumptions on U and L under which both Theorem 1 and 2 hold. Can U be multimodal, and what mixing/recurrence conditions are required in that case? How sensitive are the results to violations common in high-dimensional posteriors?
- Role of stochastic gradient noise: When the stochastic gradients across chains are independent (as in distinct minibatches), the state processes may decorrelate quickly even if the Gaussian noise is correlated. Intuitively, why does the RR combination still reduce the error order in SG-MCMC? Empirically, do you observe substantial cross-chain correlation over time? An ablation comparing correlated vs independent Brownian increments, and correlated vs independent/overlapping minibatches, would be illuminating.
- Fairness of real-data comparisons: The MovieLens experiments compare SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) to a single SGLD chain at step size γ under matched wall-clock time via parallelization. However, the same computational budget could run two independent SGLD chains (or SGLD at γ/2) in parallel and average their estimates, which should reduce variance and possibly bias. Please add:
  1) Two independent SGLD chains at step size γ, averaged.
  2) One or two SGLD chains at step size γ/2, with wall-clock matched to SGRRLD.
  3) If feasible, SGLD with an optimal K,γ trade-off per your theory.
  This will more directly isolate the benefit of RR extrapolation versus parallelization alone.
- Metrics and practical significance: On MovieLens, the RMSE gains appear modest and, as presented, could be within tuning variability. Consider reporting uncertainties, posterior predictive log likelihoods, or calibration diagnostics to demonstrate statistically and practically significant gains. Wall-clock matching is good; please also report throughput and any overhead from correlation management.
- Implementation details: The theory suggests Σ = Id is optimal. Is this precisely how you implement the correlated increments? Are the two chains forced to use identical minibatch sizes and sampling schemes? What happens if the batch sizes differ or minibatch indices are synchronized to increase correlation?
- Step-size schedules: The paper emphasizes optimal α (e.g., α = 1/5 for MSE). In practice, constant step sizes are used in real-data experiments. Could you include decreasing-step results on MovieLens, or justify why constant-step settings suffice?

Quality
The mathematical development appears careful and consistent with the literature, though I did not verify all proofs. The assumptions should be consolidated in the main text, and their practical implications discussed (especially multimodality and minibatch noise correlation).

Clarity
The paper is generally well written. The algorithmic description is clear; a concise, self-contained pseudocode in the main paper (not only in the supplement) would help. Please explicitly state the requirement that both chains use the same gradient estimator distribution (e.g., identical minibatch sizes).

Originality
Adapting RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with a rigorous analysis and variance control via correlated Brownian increments is, to my knowledge, novel and interesting.

Significance
If the empirical improvements persist across tasks and under strong baselines (multi-chain SGLD, smaller step sizes), this could be a simple, broadly applicable upgrade to first-order SG-MCMC methods.

Pros
- Elegant, simple idea with strong theoretical backing.
- Improves convergence rates without higher-order integrators.
- Parallel-friendly; easy to implement.
- Clear synthetic results; extension to SGHMC.

Cons
- Real-data gains are modest and comparisons may not be fully fair.
- Assumptions/conditions not fully transparent in the main text.
- Limited exploration of chain correlation and minibatch coupling effects.
- Missing baselines (multi-chain SGLD; SGLD at γ/2).

Suggestions
- Add the proposed baselines and uncertainty bands on RMSE.
- Provide an ablation on Brownian correlation and minibatch synchronization.
- Summarize assumptions on U, L, and f in the main text and discuss multimodality.
- Report additional metrics (predictive log likelihood, calibration, ESS/time).
- Include pseudocode and implementation details for correlated increments.

Overall assessment
A promising and well-motivated contribution with clean theory and compelling synthetic evidence. Strengthening the empirical section with fairer baselines and clearer assumptions would significantly improve the paper’s impact. I lean positive, contingent on addressing the experimental concerns.
