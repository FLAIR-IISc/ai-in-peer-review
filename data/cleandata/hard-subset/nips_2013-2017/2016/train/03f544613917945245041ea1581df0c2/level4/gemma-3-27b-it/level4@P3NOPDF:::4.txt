This paper presents an appealing improvement to Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods by introducing a Richardson-Romberg (RR) scheme designed to accelerate convergence in terms of both bias and mean squared error (MSE). A key strength of the proposed approach, SGRRLD, is its ease of implementation, requiring only minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms. The work is well-supported by strong theoretical analysis. 

The central idea – leveraging a Romberg-Richardson extrapolation to enhance the convergence of standard SG-MCMC – is sound, and the theoretical results appear solid. However, I have concerns regarding the derivation of the specific convergence rates presented in the theorem, as detailed below. Additionally, I have a practical concern related to the experimental evaluation, specifically the matrix factorization experiment.

My primary concern lies in the justification of the reported convergence rates. The explanation provided in the paper regarding how these rates are obtained from the theorem is unclear. A more detailed derivation or clarification in the final version would significantly strengthen the paper.

Regarding the experiments, the timing comparisons in Figure 5 appear potentially misleading. The proposed SGRRLD method inherently requires more computation than standard SG-MCMC due to its use of two chains, with one chain requiring twice the number of steps.  If the reported timings for SGLD were adjusted to reflect this increased computational cost (e.g., multiplying SGLD's time by a factor of 2/3), the observed performance difference between SGLD and SGRRLD would likely diminish.  A fairer comparison would account for the differing computational budgets.

Further scrutiny of the results reveals a potential inconsistency in Figure 3, the bias plot. The curve corresponding to α=0.2 (red curve) appears to exhibit the lowest bias, contrary to the claim in the text that optimal performance is achieved with α=1/3. Please investigate this discrepancy.  Furthermore, the optimal convergence curve appears to exhibit considerable fluctuation. This instability seems inconsistent with the stated convergence rate and warrants further investigation; the authors should provide a potential explanation for this observed behavior.

Finally, the discussion in line 256 states that when the stepsize is sufficiently small, the term 1/Kγ dominates. I am curious as to why this effect isn't observed in the standard SGLD algorithm. A clarification addressing this point would be appreciated.

I also have several minor comments:

*   **Equation 1:** The symbol used in Equation 1 differs from the standard notation for parameters, typically represented by θ. Please ensure consistency.
*   **Line 94:** The abbreviation "RR" is used without prior definition. Please define it upon first use.
*   **Line 131:** The notation Γ<sub>K</sub> is used without definition. Please define it.
*   **Line 160:** When referring to a “smooth function f,” specify the type of smoothness being assumed (e.g., Lipschitz continuous gradient).
*   **Line 249:** Figure 1(b) plots Bias vs. time, but the caption suggests it should be Figure 1(c). Please verify the figure numbering.
*   **Notation:** Please consistently use bold letters to represent vectors throughout the paper.



Overall, this paper presents a promising contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The proposed SGRRLD method is theoretically sound and potentially impactful. Addressing the concerns outlined above – particularly regarding the clarity of the convergence rate derivation, a fairer experimental comparison, and the inconsistencies observed in the bias plot – would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its confidence.
