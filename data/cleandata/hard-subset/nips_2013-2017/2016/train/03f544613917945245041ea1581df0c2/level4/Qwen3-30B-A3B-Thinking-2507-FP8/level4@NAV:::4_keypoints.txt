- Paper introduces a Richardson–Romberg scheme for SG-MCMC that improves bias and MSE convergence rates with minimal implementation changes.
- The method is well-motivated and supported by solid theoretical results.
- Main technical concern: derivation of the specific convergence rates from the presented theorems is unclear (see "Concerns" section).
- Practical concern: matrix factorization experiment on a single core would cost ~3× more time (two chains, one with double steps), making Figure 5 time comparisons unfair.
- Suggestion: scaling SGLD runtime by 2/3 would materially reduce the apparent performance gap in Figure 5.
- Figure 3 inconsistency: the red curve (α=0.2) appears to achieve the best convergence, contradicting the text claim of α=1/3.
- The optimal curve in Figure 3 shows excessive fluctuation, calling into question the claimed convergence rate for α=0.2 and needing explanation.
- Line 256 claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates for small stepsize is not observed in SGLD experiments—please clarify.
- Minor notational/clarity issues: Eq. (1) uses a non-standard symbol for the parameter.
- Minor edits needed: line 94 "RR" is undefined; line 131 Γ_K is undefined.
- Clarify assumptions: line 160 should specify the type/degree of smoothness required for f.
- Figure labeling/plot issues: line 249 references Figure 1(b) but Bias vs. time is plotted in Figure 1(b) or should reference Figure 1(c); use boldface for vectors.
