This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SGRRLD) algorithm, which aims to reduce the bias of existing Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods while maintaining a reasonable level of variance. The approach is based on the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method that involves running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes.

The paper relates the proposed method to previous work in the field, including the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm and its extensions. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of SGRRLD, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error (MSE). The theoretical results demonstrate that SGRRLD achieves higher rates of convergence than existing SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators.

The paper also presents experimental results on both synthetic and real data, including a large-scale matrix factorization problem. The results show that SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in terms of computation time and accuracy.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient algorithm for reducing the bias of SG-MCMC methods.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and well-supported by experimental results.
