This paper improves upon the existing SG-MCMC by proposing a Richardson-Romberg scheme, which enhances the convergence rate in the case of bias and MSE. This scheme is easy to implement, requiring only minor modifications to the traditional SG-MCMC. The paper is also supported by rigorous theoretical results. It proposes a Romberg-Richardson scheme that can improve the convergence rate of the standard SG-MCMC algorithm. The method looks elegant, and the theory is solid. The most concerning issue is how to obtain specific convergence rates from the theorem (as explained in the "Explain the Fatal Flaw" section). Besides this, I also have a practical concern regarding the results of the matrix factorization test. To be fair, if the proposed method is run on a single-core computer, it will take 3 times longer than the standard SG-MCMC because it has two chains and uses twice as many steps in one of them. If we consider this, the plots in Figure 5 will not be fair; the time for SGLD should be multiplied by 2/3, which will reduce the difference between SGLD and SGRRLD. In Figure 3, the bias plot seems to show that the best convergence is achieved by the red line, which corresponds to \alpha=0.2, not 1/3 as mentioned in the text; is there some mistake? Also, the optimal curve seems to fluctuate a lot, so the rate will not match \alpha=0.2; what is the reason for this? Furthermore, line 256 states that when the stepsize is very small, the 1/K\gamma term will dominate. I am wondering why this does not happen for SGLD.
