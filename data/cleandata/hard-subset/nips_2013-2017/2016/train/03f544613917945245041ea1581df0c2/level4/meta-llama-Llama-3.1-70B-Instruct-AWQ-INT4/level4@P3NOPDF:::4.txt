This paper proposes a Richardson-Romberg scheme to improve the convergence rates of existing Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms in terms of bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). The scheme is straightforward to implement, requiring minimal modifications to traditional SG-MCMC. The paper is supported by solid theoretical results, which provide a foundation for the proposed method.

The authors propose a Romberg-Richardson scheme that can improve the convergence rates of standard SG-MCMC algorithms. The method appears to be well-founded, and the theoretical results are convincing. However, there are some concerns regarding the derivation of specific convergence rates from the theorem, as explained in the "fatal flaws" section.

From a practical perspective, I have concerns about the matrix factorization experiment results. If the proposed method were run on a single-core computer, it would take approximately three times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to the use of two chains, one of which requires twice as many steps. Taking this into account, the plots in Figure 5 would not be entirely fair, as the time for SGLD should be multiplied by a factor of 2/3. This adjustment would reduce the difference between SGLD and SGRRLD.

Furthermore, I noticed some discrepancies in the plots. In Figure 3, the bias plot suggests that the best convergence is achieved by the red curve, which corresponds to α = 0.2, rather than α = 1/3 stated in the text. Additionally, the optimal curve appears to fluctuate significantly, which may not match the expected rate for α = 0.2. It would be beneficial to provide an explanation for this phenomenon.

Moreover, the text states that when the step size is too small, the term 1/Kγ would dominate. However, it is unclear why this does not occur for SGLD.

Some minor comments and suggestions for improvement:

* Equation (1) uses a different symbol than θ. It would be consistent to use the same symbol throughout the paper.
* The abbreviation "RR" is not defined on line 94. It would be helpful to provide a definition or explanation.
* The symbol Γ_K is not defined on line 131. A definition or explanation would be beneficial.
* On line 160, the text mentions a "smooth function f" but does not specify the type of smoothness. Providing more details would be helpful.
* Figure 1(b) plots Bias vs. time, not dimension, as stated in line 249. It appears that the correct figure should be Figure 1(c).
* To improve readability, it would be beneficial to use bold letters to represent vectors throughout the paper.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to improving the convergence rates of SG-MCMC algorithms. Addressing the concerns and suggestions mentioned above would strengthen the paper and provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.
