- Paper proposes Richardson-Romberg scheme for SG-MCMC that improves convergence in bias and MSE.  
- Method is simple to implement with minor changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong and the core idea is promising and well-founded.  
- Major concern about the correctness/derivation of the specific convergence rates in the main theorem (see “explain fatal flaws” section).  
- Practical concern: Figure 5’s runtime comparison may be misleading because the proposed method uses two chains (one with twice the steps), so on one core it would take ~3× longer than standard SG-MCMC.  
- Fair runtime comparison should rescale SGLD timings (reviewer suggests multiplying SGLD times by 2/3), which would likely reduce the observed performance gap.  
- In Figure 3 (bias plot), best convergence appears at α = 0.2, not the claimed α = 1/3 — discrepancy needs explanation.  
- The optimal convergence curve shows large fluctuations that seem inconsistent with a claimed α = 0.2 rate; the cause of this behavior should be clarified.  
- Question about statement (line 256) that 1/(Kγ) will dominate for small step sizes — reviewer unclear why this dominance is not observed for SGLD.  
- Minor issues: Eq.1 uses a different symbol than θ (notation inconsistency).  
- Minor issue: "RR" is not defined at line 94.  
- Minor issue: Γ_K is not defined at line 131.  
- Minor issue: "smooth function f" at line 160 should specify the type/degree of smoothness.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1(b) appears to show Bias vs. time while caption claims Figure 1(c) is Bias vs. dimension (caption/figure mismatch).  
- Minor issue: vectors should be consistently denoted in bold throughout the paper.
