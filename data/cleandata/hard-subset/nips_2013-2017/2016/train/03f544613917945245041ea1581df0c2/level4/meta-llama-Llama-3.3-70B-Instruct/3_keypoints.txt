- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias by combining two correlated chains with step sizes γ and γ/2.  
- Two-chain scheme: second chain uses half the step size and twice the steps, with correlated Gaussian noise, yielding bias O(γ^2) vs O(γ).  
- Method is simple, requires minimal changes to base SG-MCMC algorithms, and could significantly impact the SG-MCMC community.  
- Applying Richardson-Romberg in SG-MCMC is novel for the ML community despite being established in numerical analysis.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Unclear whether the technique applies to all SG-MCMC variants; need to specify conditions beyond section 2.2.  
- Worth exploring extension to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings, Distributed SGLD).  
- To avoid idle computation of the larger-step chain, consider using three chains with different step sizes run for equal steps, though this may increase variance.  
- Investigate extending the method to more than two step sizes for further bias reduction.  
- The impact of correlated mini-batches on estimator variance is not fully clarified and requires further analysis.  
- Strengthen the matrix factorization experiment by running to convergence and comparing results to SGD for a fuller performance evaluation.  
- Add an algorithm box with pseudo-code early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
