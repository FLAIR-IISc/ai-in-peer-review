- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD-like chains with steps γ and γ/2, correlate Brownian increments, and apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Shows perfect Brownian coupling (Σ = I) is optimal to avoid variance inflation.  
- Proves consistency, a CLT for the RR-extrapolated estimator, and non-asymptotic finite-time bias and MSE bounds.  
- Asymptotic bias scales as O(γ^2) and variance as O(γ^4); with K iterations MSE can achieve O(K^{−4/5}) vs O(K^{−2/3}) for first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Technique is generic (beyond SGLD), parallel-friendly (two chains), and simple to implement.  
- Empirical results validate theory on a Gaussian posterior and show practical gains in large-scale matrix factorization; also demonstrates an SGHMC variant.  
- Theoretical development is technically solid and novel in the SG-MCMC setting (optimal coupling + CLT).  
- Paper should explicitly state which results require decaying step sizes versus constant step sizes and how stochastic gradient noise is controlled (moment/variance conditions).  
- Experiments often use constant γ while some theoretical claims assume decreasing γ_k (α = 1/5); this distinction must be clarified and aligned.  
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement appears inconsistent with Eq. (5) (indices, half-steps, and time-averaging weights); reconcile pseudocode with equations and clarify minibatch reuse.  
- Notation ambiguities: define K (macro-steps vs fine steps), Γ_K, and exact time-averaging weights used in practice.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to higher-order SG-Langevin integrators (e.g., symmetric splitting) despite claims of matching higher-order accuracy.  
- Missing ablations: effect of Brownian coupling (Σ = 0 vs Σ = I), shared vs independent minibatches across half-steps, and batch size impact on variance reduction.  
- Specific request: state required moment bounds on stochastic gradients and whether minibatch sizes must match across chains to avoid bias.  
- Specific request: clarify if fine chain takes two half-steps from the same intermediate state and whether Gaussian noise/minibatches are reused or resampled.  
- Specific request: provide practical guidance on step-size selection (constant vs decreasing) and how to choose γ for a given K per Theorem 2.  
- Specific request: discuss computational cost and memory overhead of running two chains and expected behavior without parallel hardware (approx. doubling compute).  
- Pros: simple, generic, parallelizable, solid theory (consistency, CLT, finite-time bounds), and empirical alignment with theory.  
- Cons: clarity and consistency issues in theory vs experiments and pseudocode, limited comparisons to higher-order methods, and lack of practical hyperparameter/compute guidance.  
- Overall: promising and well-motivated contribution that advances SG-MCMC; address clarity issues and strengthen empirical comparisons/ablations to improve the paper.
