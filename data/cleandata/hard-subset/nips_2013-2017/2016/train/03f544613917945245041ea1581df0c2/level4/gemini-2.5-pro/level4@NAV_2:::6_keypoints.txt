- Paper proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC (SGRRLD): run two correlated SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical contributions include a CLT (Theorem 1) and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds (Theorem 2), claiming faster convergence (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Empirical validation on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization shows improved wall-clock performance over SGLD.  
- Builds on prior work on higher-order integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and RR extrapolation for SDEs [23]; application to stochastic gradients is the novel element.  
- Strength: technically sound, rigorous convergence analysis supporting improved rates.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear motivation and formal algorithm description.  
- Strength: significant practical impact by achieving higher-order accuracy with first-order updates and showing wall-clock gains on real data.  
- Weakness: originality is incremental given related prior work; clearer delineation of conceptual novelty versus [10] and [23] is needed.  
- Weakness: practical scalability concerns from required synchronization/communication for correlated noise between parallel chains are not discussed.  
- Weakness: fairness of wall-clock comparisons unclear—need clarification on resource allocation for SGLD versus two-process SGRRLD.  
- Weakness: missing baseline comparison to two independent parallel SGLD chains (no communication) to separate RR benefit from parallel resource effects.  
- Minor: ULA acronym is defined twice in the manuscript.
