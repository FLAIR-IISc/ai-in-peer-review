This paper proposes a novel method, Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to reduce the bias inherent in Stochastic Gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) methods. The proposed approach leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-established technique for solving stochastic differential equations. A key feature of SG-RR-MCMC is that it runs two chains in a parallel and communication-free manner.

The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC, and the accompanying empirical results are satisfactory. The core idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD is interesting and appears effective based on the provided experiments.

However, there are several areas that could be improved. While the paper demonstrates empirical efficiency, the theoretical justification could be strengthened by providing a formal convergence rate analysis. Improved convergence rates can also be achieved using high-order integrators, yet a significant omission in the experimental section is the lack of comparison between the proposed SG-RR-MCMC and methods based on such integrators.

Finally, the manuscript contains some inconsistencies. There appears to be a contradictory statement regarding the step size. Furthermore, Algorithm 1, as presented in the supplementary material, does not seem to be consistent with Equation (5) in the main text.
