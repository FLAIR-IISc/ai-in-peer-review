To reduce the bias of SG-MCMC, the authors propose stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), which applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation—a standard technique for SDEs. The method runs two chains in an embarrassingly parallel manner without communication. Theoretical analysis shows that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent, and the empirical results are satisfactory. The idea is interesting and empirically effective, and it can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. Theoretical efficiency is supported by an improved convergence rate; however, similar gains could also be achieved with higher-order integrators, and the paper does not include experimental comparisons against such integrators.
