- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining estimators to reduce bias.  
- Core theoretical claim: bias reduced from O(γ) to O(γ²) while keeping variance reasonable, yielding MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs O(K⁻²/³) for standard SG-MCMC.  
- Provides asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization shows significant accuracy improvements over SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Method is conceptually simple, minimally invasive to existing SG-MCMC implementations, and broadly generalizable.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and presents clear, superior convergence rates relative to first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Experimental results are consistent and convincing across multiple datasets and settings.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with strong technical exposition.  
- Does not fully specify conditions under which RR extrapolation applies to all SG-MCMC variants (insufficient generality conditions beyond Section 2.2).  
- Matrix factorization experiments lack a standard SGD baseline to contrast the Bayesian advantage.  
- Needs more discussion on practical implications of correlating Brownian increments and mini-batch sampling strategies.  
- Briefly mentions multi-step RR extrapolation but lacks comprehensive exploration or experiments on it.  
- Strong case for acceptance: addresses a fundamental SG-MCMC limitation with a theoretically sound, practical, and implementable solution supported by experiments.  
- Main reasons against acceptance: incomplete applicability conditions, missing baselines in experiments, and limited exploration of multi-step RR extensions.  
- Overall: technically sound, original application of RR to SG-MCMC, and potentially significant for large-scale Bayesian inference.
