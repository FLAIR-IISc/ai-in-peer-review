

This paper extends Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to contemporary "big-data MCMC" approaches, specifically targeting Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a well-established numerical integration technique that has recently gained traction in numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs); a notable strength of this work is its effective bridging of the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially benefiting both communities. The authors propose a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, establish theoretical convergence guarantees for the method, and provide arguments for its superiority over standard SGLD under specific conditions. Empirical results demonstrate substantial improvements on synthetic benchmarks and consistent, albeit moderate, gains in large-scale applications. Overall, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and presents a clear contribution. The connection between two previously disparate fields is compelling, the theoretical developments are interesting, and the empirical performance of the proposed method is strong. The approach constitutes a relatively straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, offering computational efficiency. While the method is unlikely to revolutionize current practice, practitioners may adopt it for its practical advantages. Minor suggestions: Figure 1a is potentially misleading, as it depicts a Gaussian approximation (using empirical posterior mean and variance) rather than the actual empirical posterior distribution; this distinction should be clarified. In Figure 2a, a brief discussion would help explain why bias does not monotonically decrease as stepsize diminishes, as this behavior is counterintuitive.
