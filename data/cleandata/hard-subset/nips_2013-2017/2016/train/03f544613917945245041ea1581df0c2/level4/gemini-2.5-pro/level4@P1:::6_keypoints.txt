- Proposal: Use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC, yielding SGRRLD that runs two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Theory: Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses, including a Central Limit Theorem and bias/MSE bounds, showing higher convergence rates than SGLD.  
- Experiments: Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate theoretical bias and MSE bounds; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical and wall-clock advantages over SGLD.  
- Strength: Clever application of RR reduces SG-MCMC bias, achieving higher-order accuracy with first-order updates.  
- Strength: Theoretical rigor with comprehensive convergence proofs and formal rates.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical validation supporting theoretical claims and demonstrating practical utility.  
- Weakness: Novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC and prior RR use for SDE invariant distributions.  
- Weakness: Communication overhead from requiring correlated Brownian motions between chains is not discussed or measured in experiments.  
- Weakness: Experimental fairness unclear—uncertain whether SGLD baseline had equivalent total computational resources to SGRRLD’s two chains; implementation details needed.  
- Weakness: Missing comparison to a simple parallel baseline (two independent SGLD chains averaged) to isolate the benefit of RR extrapolation and noise correlation.  
- Minor: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is defined twice in the manuscript.
