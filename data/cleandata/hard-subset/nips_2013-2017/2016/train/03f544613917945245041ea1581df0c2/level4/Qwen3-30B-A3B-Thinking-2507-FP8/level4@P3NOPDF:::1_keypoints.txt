- Bridges MCMC and numerical SDE communities by applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SGLD, providing a novel theoretical link.
- Proposes SGLD-RRE algorithm that is conceptually simple, practically effective, and clearly motivated.
- Core technical contribution: adapts RRE to reduce bias in SGLD without increasing computational overhead.
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis showing improved MSE rates over vanilla SGLD under standard assumptions.
- Specifies conditions for improvement (notably when bias dominates variance), giving actionable guidance for practitioners.
- Theoretical results are sound, well-justified, and appropriately modest in claims.
- Empirical results: large gains on synthetic benchmarks (≈2–3× faster convergence) and consistent but smaller gains on MNIST/CIFAR-10.
- Empirical evaluation uses multiple metrics (MSE, effective sample size) and shows SGLD-RRE requires only minor implementation changes.
- Paper excels in clarity and interdisciplinary relevance, translating numerical analysis concepts into MCMC context accessibly.
- Limitations are discussed (e.g., stepsize sensitivity) and the narrative is cohesive between theory and experiments.
- Minor requested clarifications: label Figure 1a as a Gaussian approximation using empirical mean/variance, and explain non-monotonic bias in Figure 2a due to RRE error cancellation.
- Overall assessment: technically robust, practical advance for large-scale Bayesian inference; recommend acceptance.
