- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a new contribution building on SDE literature.  
- Requires running two parallel Markov chains, trading extra chains for faster convergence with the same step size.  
- Algorithm description is clear and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly show bias reduction and improved performance of SGRRLD.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) experiments show only marginal RMSE improvement and are inconclusive.  
- Concerns about theoretical conditions: unclear if potential U can be multi-modal and how chain correlation affects error reduction.  
- Questions raised about observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Experimental setup concerns: need fair baseline comparisons (e.g., SGLD run with multiple parallel chains, SGLD at step size γ/2, and two-chain SGLD vs SGRRLD).  
- Recommendation: include more real-data experiments and comprehensive baselines to substantiate practical benefits.
