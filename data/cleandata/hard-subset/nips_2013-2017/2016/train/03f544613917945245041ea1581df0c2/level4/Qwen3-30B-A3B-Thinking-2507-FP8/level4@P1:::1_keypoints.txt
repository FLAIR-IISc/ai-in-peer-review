- Introduces SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combining outputs.
- Connects MCMC and numerical-analysis/SDE literature, showing RRE improves convergence while keeping computational efficiency.
- Proves asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.
- Shows non-asymptotic bias = O(γ²) and MSE = O(γ⁴) for fixed step size γ.
- Demonstrates SGRRLD attains higher-order convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) using only first-order integrators.
- Maintains parallelizability, important for large-scale applications.
- Provides rigorous technical arguments and proofs, including variance control via correlated Brownian increments.
- Theoretical results are well-motivated and extend prior SG-MCMC convergence analyses.
- Thorough experiments on synthetic Gaussians show large bias and MSE reductions (bias ~10⁻² → ~10⁻⁴).
- Dimensionality experiments show scalability and MSE improvements that scale quadratically relative to SGLD errors.
- Large-scale MovieLens matrix factorization experiments confirm practical speed/accuracy gains (comparable accuracy in half the time).
- Method generalizes to SGHMC, demonstrating broader applicability.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally reproducible.
- Authors acknowledge limitation that 1/(Kγ) can dominate errors for very small step sizes.
- Two minor suggested clarifications: label Figure 1(a) curves as empirical posterior estimates and explain nonmonotonic bias behavior in Figure 2(a).
- Overall assessment: significant, practical refinement to SG-MCMC with strong theoretical and empirical support; recommend acceptance.
