- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two correlated SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to reduce discretization bias.
- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Implementation is simple and requires minimal changes to SGLD, naturally parallelizable on multi-core hardware.
- Experiments on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization show empirical bias reduction and improved performance; SGHMC example shows method generality.
- Originality: novel and elegant application of Richardson-Romberg to SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order accuracy with a first-order integrator.
- Theoretical quality: strong, rigorous proofs with clear guidance on optimal step-size scheduling.
- Clarity and practicality: conceptually simple, easy to implement, and well-suited for parallel computation.
- Experimental validation: well-designed comparisons that effectively demonstrate practical benefits.
- Clarify derivation of convergence rates: the paper should more explicitly show how theorems yield the optimal rates and step-size γ ∝ K⁻¹/⁵.
- Fairness of wall-clock comparisons: parallel execution favors SGRRLD; need to acknowledge single-core trade-offs and compare using total gradient evaluations.
- Figure 3 discrepancy: text claims α=1/3 optimal but plot suggests α=0.2 performs better; authors should clarify and explain observed fluctuations.
- Behavior for small γ: SGRRLD suffers from a 1/(Kγ) variance term at very small γ causing degradation; explain why this is less apparent for SGLD in plots.
- Minor suggestion: use boldface consistently for vector notation to improve readability.
- Minor suggestion: use a consistent base symbol (e.g., θ) for continuous and discrete-time processes for clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: method is novel, theoretically sound, practical, parallelizable, and experimentally convincing.
- Arguments against acceptance: wall-clock time comparisons may be misleading without gradient-evaluation baselines; minor clarity issues remain.
- Recommendation: strong paper with minor fixable issues; recommend acceptance.
