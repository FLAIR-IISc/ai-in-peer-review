

This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard technique in numerical analysis for variance reduction in stochastic differential equations (SDEs), and the authors provide a valuable link between the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially benefiting both communities. They introduce a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, establish theoretical convergence results, and demonstrate its advantages over vanilla SGLD under specific conditions. The method yields impressive improvements on toy examples and modest but consistent gains in large-scale settings. Overall, the paper is technically sound, clearly written, and makes a solid contribution. Its key strengths include the interdisciplinary connection, rigorous theoretical analysis, and strong empirical performance. While the approach constitutes a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD—enhancing computational convenience—the method is likely to be adopted by practitioners, though it represents an incremental advance rather than a paradigm shift.  

Minor comments:  
1. Figure 1a is potentially misleading, as it depicts a Gaussian approximation using the empirical posterior mean and variance, not the empirical posterior distribution itself. This should be explicitly clarified to avoid misinterpretation by the general reader.  
2. In Figure 2a, it would be helpful to briefly discuss why the bias does not monotonically decrease as the step size decreases, as this behavior may appear counterintuitive.
