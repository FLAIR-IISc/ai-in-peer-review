This paper applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to modern “big-data” MCMC methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). One of its strengths is the explicit connection it draws between the MCMC and SDE literatures, which should be valuable to both communities. The authors introduce the SGLD-RRE algorithm, provide convergence results including conditions under which SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD, and present numerical experiments. The empirical results show large gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements on larger-scale tasks.

The paper is clearly written, technically sound, and easy to follow. The proposed method is a relatively direct application of RRE to SGLD, which keeps it computationally convenient. While the contribution is incremental rather than transformative, I expect practitioners to find it useful and adopt it in appropriate settings.

Minor comments:
- Figure 1a: The plot appears to show a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance plugged in, rather than an empirical estimate of the posterior itself. Please make this explicit to avoid confusion.
- Figure 2a: It would help to add a brief discussion of why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the step size shrinks, as this is counterintuitive. Some explanation of the mechanisms at play (e.g., the interaction between discretization error, stochastic gradient noise, and extrapolation) would clarify the observed behavior.

Overall, this is a solid paper that forges an interesting link between two somewhat disconnected fields, offers relevant theoretical insights, and demonstrates practical improvements with a method that is simple to implement.
