- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce gradient-noise bias.  
- Theoretically shows SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and faster convergence for the same step size.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and easy to implement (runs two parallel chains combined via RR extrapolation).  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction consistent with theory.  
- Real-data MovieLens results show only marginal RMSE improvements, questioning practical significance.  
- Baseline comparison is problematic: should compare SGRRLD(γ) to SGLD(γ/2) or two parallel SGLD chains (each γ) for equal compute.  
- Claim of comparable computation via parallelization needs validation by directly benchmarking two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Theoretical assumptions on potential U are underspecified—unclear if results hold for multi-modal U.  
- Assumption/impact of gradient-noise being uncorrelated across chains is unclear; authors should report observed chain correlations.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks rigor and breadth; additional real-world experiments (larger/complex datasets like CIFAR-10) needed.  
- Authors should clarify theoretical conditions, provide empirical chain-correlation analysis, and detail computational trade-offs (MSE gain vs doubled chain overhead).  
- Overall: idea is promising and theoretically sound, but current empirical validation is insufficient to claim broad practical advantage.
