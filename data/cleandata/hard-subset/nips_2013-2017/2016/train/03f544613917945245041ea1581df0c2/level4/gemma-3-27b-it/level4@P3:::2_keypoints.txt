- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bias/MSE bounds showing improved convergence rates.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and appears straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction relative to SGLD.  
- Inherent parallelism (two simultaneous chains) is a practical advantage for modern hardware.  
- Positions work within SG-MCMC and numerical-analysis literature; novelty is adapting RR to stochastic gradients.  
- Real-data experiments (MovieLens) are limited and show only marginal improvements, making conclusions tentative.  
- Experimental comparison is potentially unfair: SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) compared to SGLD (γ) doesn’t isolate RR’s benefit—need SGLD with γ/2 and parallel runs.  
- Correlation between the two chains is discussed theoretically but not quantified empirically in real-data experiments.  
- Technical conditions on the potential U for Theorems 1 & 2 are not stated explicitly; applicability to multi-modal U is unclear.  
- Paper lacks an intuitive explanation of how RR extrapolation reduces error given rapid decorrelation from noisy gradients.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, strong theory, synthetic validation, and parallelism.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unconvincing real-data evaluation, insufficient practical analysis of chain correlation, and unclear technical assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on adding SGLD (γ/2) baselines, quantifying chain correlation, and clarifying U’s technical conditions.
