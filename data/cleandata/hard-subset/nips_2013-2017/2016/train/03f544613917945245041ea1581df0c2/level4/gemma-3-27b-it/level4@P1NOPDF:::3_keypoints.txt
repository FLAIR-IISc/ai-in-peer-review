- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias in posterior estimates.  
- Method runs two SG-MCMC chains: one with step size γ and one with γ/2 run for twice the steps.  
- Injected Gaussian noise is correlated across chains via α_k = (β_{2k-1} + β_{2k})/√2.  
- Combining estimates (Chain2 − Chain1) yields bias reduction from O(γ) to O(γ^2).  
- Authors also prove a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE).  
- Method is simple to implement and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Broad applicability across many SG-MCMC algorithms suggests significant potential impact.  
- Novelty: Richardson-Romberg is well-known in numerical analysis but underused in ML/SG-MCMC.  
- Presentation and writing are clear.  
- Question: Is the technique universally applicable to all SG-MCMC methods, or are additional conditions beyond π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ^2) required?  
- Question: Can the approach be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH) or Distributed SGLD?  
- Concern: Running one chain twice as long is computationally inefficient; could two γ/2 chains run in parallel with one γ chain improve utilization and what effect on variance?  
- Question: Is there a generalization using more than two step sizes?  
- Question: How does correlation between mini-batches affect variance; is using the same batch across corresponding iterations optimal or negligible?  
- Experiment critique: Matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include a comparison to standard SGD to demonstrate practical Bayesian advantage.  
- Suggestion: Add a pseudocode/algorithm box early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
