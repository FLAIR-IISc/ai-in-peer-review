- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with two correlated SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to cancel O(γ) bias and achieve O(γ²) bias.  
- Theoretical claim: Shows asymptotic bias reduced from O(γ) to O(γ²), improving MSE convergence from O(K⁻²/³) to O(K⁻⁴/⁵).  
- Implementation: Simple, practical, easy to integrate into existing SG-MCMC workflows and amenable to parallel architectures.  
- Generality: Method appears applicable beyond SGLD (demonstrated on SGHMC), suggesting broad impact across SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novelty: Novel application of classical Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC as an alternative to higher-order integrators.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear presentation and solid connections to prior SDE literature.  
- Applicability concern: Needs clarification on precise conditions required (beyond a weak error expansion) and applicability to other approximate or distributed MCMC schemes.  
- Computational efficiency: Parallel scheme wastes computation (larger-step chain idle half the time); alternative resource-utilization schemes (e.g., three-chain variants) should be considered.  
- Multi-level extensions: Briefly mentioned but requires discussion of practical trade-offs for recursive/multi-level RR extrapolation.  
- Gradient correlation: Analysis focuses on Gaussian noise correlation; impact and optimal strategies for correlating stochastic gradient/mini-batch sampling are unclear and should be investigated.  
- Experiments: Matrix factorization results are promising but should run to convergence and include non-Bayesian baselines (e.g., SGD) for proper context.  
- Presentation: Add an early pseudo-code algorithm box to improve accessibility and ease of adoption for practitioners.
