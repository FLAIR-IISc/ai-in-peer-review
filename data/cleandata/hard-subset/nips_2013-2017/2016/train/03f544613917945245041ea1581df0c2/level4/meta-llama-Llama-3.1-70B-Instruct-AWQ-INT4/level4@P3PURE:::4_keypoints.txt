- Paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC bias and MSE convergence, is easy to implement, and has solid theoretical backing.
- Major theoretical concern: derivation of the specific convergence rates from the main theorem appears incorrect or insufficiently justified.
- Practical/experimental concern: matrix factorization experiment results are questionable and need clarification.
- Runtime fairness issue: the proposed method runs ~3× slower on a single core (two chains, one with twice the steps), so Figure 5 comparisons are unfair and SGLD curves should be rescaled (reviewer suggests a 2/3 factor) which would reduce the reported gap.
- Figure 3 inconsistency: bias plot indicates optimal α≈0.2, not α=1/3 as claimed, and the optimal curve shows large fluctuations that contradict claimed rates—needs explanation.
- Unclear claim about small step sizes: authors state 1/(Kγ) would dominate when γ is tiny (line 256), but it’s not clear why this effect doesn’t appear for SGLD.
- Minor notation issues: Equation (1) uses a different symbol than θ without clarification.
- Minor notation issue: the abbreviation “RR” is used but not defined (line 94).
- Minor notation issue: the symbol Γ_K is introduced but not defined (line 131).
- Minor clarity issue: description of the smooth function f (line 160) should specify what kind of smoothness (e.g., Lipschitz, differentiability order).
- Figure labeling error: Figure 1(b) is said to plot Bias vs. dimension but actually shows Bias vs. time; likely the intended reference is Figure 1(c).
- Readability suggestion: use boldface for vectors to improve clarity.
