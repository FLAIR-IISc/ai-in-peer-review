- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs; provides CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds and shows MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³); evaluated on a synthetic Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization.

- Novelty/elegance: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC; conceptually simple and elegant.

- Strong theoretical contribution: Rigorous analysis with CLT and non-asymptotic bounds, showing first-order scheme attains higher-order accuracy.

- Clarity and organization: Well-written, clearly motivated, and connections to prior work are well explained.

- Practicality and parallelism: Method is inherently parallel (two independent chains), enabling comparable wall-clock time to single SGLD on multi-core hardware.

- Experimental fairness concern: Comparisons may be unfair—SGRRLD (two chains) is compared to a single SGLD chain without matching computational resources.

- Missing baselines: Should compare to two parallel SGLD chains averaged and to SGLD run with the smaller step size γ/2 to isolate RR benefits.

- Limited real-data evidence: Improvements on MovieLens are modest; practical significance is not fully established and more real-world experiments are needed.

- Assumption scope question: Request clarification on assumptions about potential U and applicability to multimodal posteriors.

- RR intuition/variance question: Ask for more intuition on how RR cancels bias given uncorrelated stochastic gradient noise and the role of correlated Brownian terms.

- Further evaluation suggestion: Test whether bias reduction extends to other posterior moments and more complex models.
