- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGR RLD) to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Shows SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- The algorithm requires running two parallel Markov chains and is straightforward to implement.  
- Theoretical results claim faster convergence for SGRRLD versus SGLD for the same step size; proofs not fully verified by reviewer.  
- Clarification requested on the conditions for the potential U in Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., can U be multi-modal?).  
- Reviewer questions how uncorrelated gradient noise affects chain correlation and error-reduction intuition.  
- Asks whether the two chains in SGRRLD showed any empirical correlation in real-data experiments.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments clearly demonstrate bias reduction in that setting.  
- MovieLens real-data experiments show only marginal RMSE improvement; significance needs further investigation.  
- Concern that running two parallel SGLD chains could offer comparable gains, so baseline comparison is insufficient.  
- Experimental comparison should include SGLD with step size γ/2 run in two parallel chains for fair computational-cost matching.  
- More real-data experiments are needed to establish general applicability and practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles important bias problem, theoretically sound, clear synthetic results, easy parallel implementation, builds on established RR extrapolation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest real-data gains, inadequate experimental baselines, unclear theoretical conditions for U, and insufficient investigation of chain correlations.
