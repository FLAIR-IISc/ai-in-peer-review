- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC methods, focusing on SGLD.  
- Provides a useful link between MCMC and SDE literatures that could benefit both communities.  
- Proposes a new SGLD-RRE method and derives theoretical convergence results.  
- Gives conditions and arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves over vanilla SGLD.  
- Empirical results: large gains on toy examples and modest but consistent improvements on large-scale problems.  
- Paper is clear, technically sound, and the method is a straightforward, computationally convenient application of RRE.  
- Practical impact: likely to be used by practitioners but not expected to revolutionize practice.  
- Minor critique: Figure 1a is misleading—it plots a Gaussian with empirical mean/variance, not the empirical posterior; clarify this.  
- Minor critique: Figure 2a needs discussion on why bias does not necessarily decrease as step size decreases, which is counterintuitive.
