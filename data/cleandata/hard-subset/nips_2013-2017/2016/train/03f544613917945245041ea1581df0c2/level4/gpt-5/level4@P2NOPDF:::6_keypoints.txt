- Introduces Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC: runs two correlated SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic guarantees; experiments show empirical gains.

- Main concern 1 — Novelty: contribution seems incremental; needs clear differentiation from prior work (e.g., [10], [23])—specify novel coupling constructions, new non-asymptotic rates under gradient noise, or unique implementation insights.

- Main concern 2 — Practicality and communication: method requires per-iteration communication to correlate Brownian increments but the paper does not discuss or measure communication overhead.

- Experimental resource accounting unclear: clarify whether SGLD and SGRRLD used equivalent total hardware and how comparisons were made to ensure fairness.

- If budgets were matched, report how resources were split between the two SGRRLD chains (cores/GPUs per chain, minibatch sizes, memory bandwidth) and justify how splitting reduces wall-clock time given gradient-cost dominance.

- Provide detailed implementation/reporting: hardware, parallelization strategy, synchronization frequency for correlated noise, communication mechanism and latency, and whether results are normalized by wall-clock time, gradient evaluations, or both.

- Suggested baseline: include two independent SGLD chains run embarrassingly parallel and averaged to separate RR extrapolation benefits from parallelism/variance reduction; compare under fixed wall-clock and fixed total gradient evaluations and test sensitivity to communication latency.

- Minor: remove duplicate introduction of “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” (lines 25 and 70).

- Overall view: promising, well-presented and theoretically careful work; clarifying novelty and strengthening resource-aware experiments would improve impact.
