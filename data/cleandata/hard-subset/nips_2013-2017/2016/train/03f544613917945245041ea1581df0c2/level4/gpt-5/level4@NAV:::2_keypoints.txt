- Proposal: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation within stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGRRLD) to mitigate bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains asymptotically smaller bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Method tradeoff: achieves faster convergence using two parallel Markov chains (computational cost argued comparable via parallelization).  
- Algorithmic clarity: description is clear and implementation is straightforward.  
- Synthetic results: Linear Gaussian experiment clearly demonstrates SGRRLD’s bias reduction over SGLD for that problem.  
- Real-data results: MovieLens shows only a slight test RMSE improvement, making practical benefit debatable.  
- Missing validation: reviewer requests more real-data experiments to establish broad practical effectiveness.  
- Theoretical question: what assumptions on U are required (e.g., can U be multimodal)?  
- Mechanistic question: how does combining two chains reduce error order when gradient noises may decorrelate quickly?  
- Empirical question: do the authors observe correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments?  
- Comparison concerns: need to compare SGRRLD to two parallel SGLD chains and to SGLD with step size gamma/2 (and two chains) to ensure fair RMSE/compute comparisons.
