This paper proposes a novel method called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SGRRLD) to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The authors employ Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a technique commonly used for stochastic differential equations (SDEs), to improve the convergence rate of SG-MCMC. The key idea is to run two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes and then combine their estimates using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, including a central limit theorem and bounds on the non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error. The authors also conduct experiments on both synthetic and real data to demonstrate the effectiveness of their method. The results show that SGRRLD achieves higher rates of convergence than Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) and its extensions with first-order integrators.

The main strength of this paper is its innovative use of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to improve the convergence rate of SG-MCMC. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their method, and their theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported. The experimental results are also convincing, demonstrating the practical benefits of SGRRLD.

However, there are a few weaknesses and areas for improvement. Firstly, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, particularly those using high-order integrators. While the authors mention that SGRRLD can achieve the same accuracy as these methods, they do not provide a direct comparison in their experiments. Secondly, there are some minor inconsistencies in the paper, such as the statement about the step size in Algorithm 1 (in the supplement) not matching Equation (5). Finally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational cost and parallelization of SGRRLD, as this is an important consideration for large-scale applications.
