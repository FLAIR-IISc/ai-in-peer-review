This paper presents an improvement to Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) through the application of a Richardson-Romberg scheme, demonstrably improving convergence rates in terms of both bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). The proposed scheme is straightforward to implement, requiring only minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and is supported by strong theoretical analysis. The core idea—a Romberg-Richardson scheme to accelerate standard SG-MCMC—appears promising and the theoretical foundation seems solid. 

My primary concern lies with the derivation of the specific convergence rates presented in the theorem, as detailed in the “explain fatal flaws” section. Beyond this, I have a practical concern regarding the matrix factorization experiment results (Figure 5). Running the proposed method on a single core would inherently take approximately three times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to the use of two chains, one of which requires twice the number of steps. Therefore, the comparison in Figure 5 may be misleading; a fair comparison would necessitate multiplying the SGLD timings by a factor of 2/3, which would likely reduce the observed performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD. 

Furthermore, examining Figure 3 (bias plot), it appears that the best convergence is achieved with α = 0.2, rather than α = 1/3 as stated in the text. Could this discrepancy be explained? Additionally, the optimal convergence curve exhibits considerable fluctuation, which seems inconsistent with a rate corresponding to α = 0.2. What accounts for this behavior? Finally, the paper notes (line 256) that when the step size is sufficiently small, the term 1/Kγ will dominate. I am unclear why this behavior is not observed with SGLD.

Several minor points require attention: Eq. 1 uses a symbol different from θ; RR is not defined at line 94; Γ_K is not defined at line 131; line 160 mentions a “smooth function f” without specifying the type of smoothness; and the plot in Figure 1(b) appears to depict Bias vs. time, while the caption suggests it should be Figure 1(c) showing Bias vs. dimension. Lastly, please consistently use bold letters to denote vectors.
