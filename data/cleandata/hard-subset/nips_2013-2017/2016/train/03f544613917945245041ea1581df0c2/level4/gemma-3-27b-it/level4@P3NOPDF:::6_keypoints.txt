- Proposes RR-extrapolation acceleration for SG-MCMC requiring two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.
- Provides theoretical asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees, with derived bias and MSE bounds.
- Experiments validate the theoretical bounds on a synthetic Gaussian model and show improvements over SGLD on a large-scale matrix factorization task.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and well-contextualized within SGMCMC literature.
- Concern: Novelty may be incremental—prior work analyzed integrator effects [10] and used RR for invariant distributions [23]; clearer discussion of how this extends or departs from prior work is needed.
- Concern: Practical applicability limited by communication costs—the required correlated Brownian motion implies per-iteration communication between chains, which the paper does not address.
- Concern: Experimental comparison fairness is unclear—no details on how computational resources were allocated between SGRRLD and SGLD, creating potential bias.
- Concern: Splitting resources between two chains may not reduce wall-clock time since gradient evaluation is the bottleneck; authors should justify any claimed runtime gains.
- Suggestion: Provide implementation details (resource allocation, communication infrastructure) and compare to two independent SGLD chains (no communication) as an alternative baseline.
- Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” appears redundantly (lines 25 and 70).
