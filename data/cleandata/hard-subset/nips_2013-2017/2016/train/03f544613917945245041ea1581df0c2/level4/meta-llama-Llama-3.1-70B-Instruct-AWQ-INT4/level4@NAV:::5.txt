The authors introduce a novel approach to mitigate the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) by proposing the stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC method. This approach leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a readily available technique for stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Notably, the SG-RR-MCMC method utilizes two parallel chains without communication, demonstrating its efficiency. The authors provide a theoretical analysis, confirming the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC. The empirical results also yield satisfactory outcomes, showcasing the method's effectiveness. The idea, which can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD), is intriguing and validated by empirical results. Theoretical validation of the method's efficiency is possible through improved convergence rates, which can also be achieved using high-order integrators (although a comparative analysis with integrators in experiments is noticeably absent). However, there is an inconsistency in the statement regarding the step size, as previously mentioned. Furthermore, a discrepancy exists between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5), which requires clarification.
