- Proposal: Use Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2), correlating noises α_k=(β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2, and subtracting estimates to reduce bias to O(γ^2) and lower MSE.  
- Strength: Very simple to understand and implement with no major changes to base SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Strength: Likely broadly applicable across a large class of SG-MCMC methods, so potentially impactful.  
- Novelty: Applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation in the ML/SG-MCMC context is novel and useful.  
- Clarity: The paper is well written and clearly presented.  
- Applicability question: Can the technique be applied to all SG-MCMC methods, and what conditions beyond π_γ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2) are required?  
- Beyond SG-MCMC: Can the method be used for other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) or distributed SGLD?  
- Computational efficiency concern: One chain is idle while the other runs twice as long—would running three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2) better use compute, and how would that affect variance?  
- Extension question: Is there a natural extension to more than two step sizes?  
- Correlations/mini-batches: Unclear how mini-batch correlation affects variance—would reusing the same mini-batches across corresponding iterations minimize variance or is the effect negligible?  
- Experiments: Recommend running matrix factorization experiments to full convergence, include SGD as a baseline, and present concrete numbers to better demonstrate practical advantage over SGLD.  
- Presentation suggestion: Add an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to make the method more accessible to practitioners.
