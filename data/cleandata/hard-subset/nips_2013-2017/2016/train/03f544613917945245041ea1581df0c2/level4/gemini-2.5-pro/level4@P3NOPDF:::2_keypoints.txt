- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorems 1 & 2) claims SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- Algorithm is clearly described, simple, and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly show bias reduction in a controlled setting.  
- Empirical validation on real-world data is limited and shows only marginal RMSE improvement on MovieLens, weakening practical claims.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: authors compare SGRRLD (two coupled chains) to single SGLD, overlooking the baseline of two independent parallel SGLD chains averaged.  
- Missing comparison to single SGLD with smaller step size (gamma/2), which could reduce bias similarly and should be included.  
- Stronger fair baseline would be two parallel SGLD chains both using step size gamma/2 to match computational cost.  
- Theory questions: unclear precise conditions on the potential U (e.g., applicability to multi-modal targets).  
- Mechanistic questions: need more intuition on how bias cancellation works when stochastic gradient noise can decorrelate chains, and whether chain correlation was measured empirically.  
- Overall: interesting and theoretically motivated idea, but practical utility not yet established; stronger, fairer experiments and clarification of theoretical conditions are needed.
