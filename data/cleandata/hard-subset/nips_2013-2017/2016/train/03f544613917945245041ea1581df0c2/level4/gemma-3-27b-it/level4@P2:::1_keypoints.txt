- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to accelerate convergence in SG-MCMC.
- Bridges MCMC and SDE literatures, adapting a numerical-analysis technique to large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Method is simple and efficient: runs two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combines them via RRE.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Empirically validated on synthetic Gaussian models and a large-scale matrix factorization (movie recommendation) task.
- Shows consistent improvements over SGLD and, in some cases, SGHMC in accuracy and convergence rate.
- Algorithm is computationally straightforward and naturally suited to parallel and distributed architectures.
- Strength—novelty: first application of RRE to SG-MCMC, offering an insightful cross-disciplinary idea.
- Strength—theoretical rigor: solid analysis with convergence proofs and error bounds.
- Strength—clarity: well-written and accessible to both MCMC and SDE communities.
- Strength—empirical validation: experiments convincingly demonstrate benefits in multiple settings.
- Weakness—modest gains in large-scale experiments: improvements are consistent but relatively small in the real-world task.
- Weakness—Figure 1a needs clarification: currently shows a Gaussian fitted to mean/variance, not the empirical posterior.
- Weakness—step-size vs. bias: counterintuitive observation that bias doesn't always decrease with smaller step size needs brief explanation.
- Acceptance argument: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically supported, and well-presented—recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Rejection argument: limited practical gains in large-scale settings and minor clarity/explanatory issues that should be addressed.
