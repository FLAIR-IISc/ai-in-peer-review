- Applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian perturbations to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE; instantiates method on SGLD (SGRRLD).  
- Experiments: synthetic Gaussian validates theoretical bias/MSE rates; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Overall assessment: solid technique and presentation, a legitimate contribution to SG-MCMC.  
- Novelty concern: paper should more explicitly discuss the incremental nature of the advance and clarify technical/conceptual differences from prior work on higher-order integrators [10] and RR for invariant measures [23].  
- Practicality/fairness concern: correlated Brownian increments imply per-iteration communication between chains; the experiments seem to assume negligible communication overhead—this should be stated and quantified.  
- Resource-allocation concern: clarify whether the single SGLD baseline received the same aggregate computational resources as the two SGRRLD chains and how resources were split, since gradient costs dominate and affect wall-clock fairness.  
- Baseline suggestion: compare to two independent parallel SGLD chains (no communication) combined post hoc as a natural alternative.  
- Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is introduced twice; remove the duplicate.
