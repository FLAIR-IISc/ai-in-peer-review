- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- SGRRLD is shown to have asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments clearly demonstrate SGRRLD’s bias reduction.  
- MovieLens experiments show only slight, marginal improvement in test RMSE.  
- Technique leverages known RR improvements for SDEs and successfully extends them to SG-MCMC.  
- Theorems claim faster convergence for SGRRLD than SGLD at the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.  
- More real-data experiments are needed to demonstrate practical bias reduction.  
- Reviewer did not verify proofs and questions conditions on matrix U required by Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., can U be multi-modal?).  
- Questions about how two chains reduce error order, especially if gradient noises become uncorrelated; asks whether chain correlation is observed in real experiments.  
- Concern about the claim of comparable compute time via parallelization, since SGLD could also run multiple parallel chains in the same time.  
- Suggests comparing SGRRLD to SGLD run with step size γ/2 (and two parallel SGLD chains), since that would reduce bias as well.  
- Strong encouragement to include more experiments on real-world datasets.
