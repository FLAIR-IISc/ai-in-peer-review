This paper investigates the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent “big-data MCMC” methods, specifically Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE, a well-established numerical integration technique, has found recent and promising applications in the context of numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A key contribution of this work is bridging the MCMC and SDE literatures, which could prove valuable to both communities. The authors propose a novel SGLD-RRE method, accompanied by theoretical analysis demonstrating its convergence properties – including arguments for its advantages over standard SGLD. Their empirical evaluation reveals substantial improvements on toy examples and more modest, yet consistent, gains on large-scale problems. Overall, this is a strong paper, characterized by its clarity and insightful connections between two relatively distinct fields; its interesting theoretical results; and the good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The paper is technically sound and easy to follow, and the resulting method represents a computationally efficient application of RRE to SGLD. While unlikely to fundamentally change current practice, I anticipate practitioners will find these methods useful. Minor comments: Figure 1a may be misleading as it presents a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirical posterior distribution itself; this should be clarified for the reader.  Regarding Figure 2a, it would be beneficial to discuss the reason why bias does not consistently decrease with decreasing stepsize, as this behavior is not immediately intuitive.
