- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, producing SGRRLD with asymptotically lower bias and MSE.
- Proves (under certain conditions) SGRRLD converges faster than SGLD for the same step size but requires running two parallel chains.
- Algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show a clear bias-reduction advantage for SGRRLD.
- MovieLens experiments show only slight, inconclusive improvement in test RMSE.
- Builds on prior RR extrapolation results for SDEs and extends them to stochastic-gradient MCMC.
- Reviewer did not verify proofs for Theorems 1 and 2 and requests clarification of conditions on matrix U, including whether U can be multi-modal.
- Asks for explanation and empirical evidence of how combining two (potentially correlated) chains reduces the error order and whether the chains decorrelate quickly in practice.
- Questions the computation-time claim: SGLD could also run multiple parallel chains, so is SGRRLD’s advantage retained if SGLD is similarly parallelized?
- Notes an unfair experimental comparison: should include SGLD with step size gamma/2 (and potentially two chains), which may reduce bias.
- Recommends additional experiments on diverse real-world datasets to establish practical significance.
