- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining estimates to reduce bias.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Shows improved convergence rates over SGLD: bias O(K^-2/5) and MSE O(K^-4/5) for K iterations.
- Synthetic experiments demonstrate SGRRLD reduces bias and MSE relative to SGLD.
- Real-data experiment (large-scale matrix factorization) shows only slight improvement in test RMSE.
- Strength: novel and interesting application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results that clarify algorithm properties.
- Strength: experimental results support potential benefits.
- Weakness: limited real-data experiments; broader empirical validation needed.
- Weakness: comparison may be unfair—SGLD could be run with multiple parallel chains for comparable compute.
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed discussion of conditions required for theoretical guarantees, especially assumptions on potential U.
- Originality: novel application and new theoretical/experimental insights.
- Significance: potential impact on scalable Bayesian inference and SG-MCMC methods.
- Clarity/quality: well-written, technically sound, and experiments are clearly presented.
- Acceptance arguments: novel algorithm, strong theory, and promising empirical results.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient real-data evaluation, questionable comparison fairness, and need for clearer theoretical conditions.
