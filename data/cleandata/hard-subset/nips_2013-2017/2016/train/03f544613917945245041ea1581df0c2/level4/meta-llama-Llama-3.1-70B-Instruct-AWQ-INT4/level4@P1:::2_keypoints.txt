- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias while keeping variance reasonable.
- Method runs two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and combines them via RR extrapolation.
- Theoretical results: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, bounded non-asymptotic bias and MSE.
- Shows faster convergence rates than SGLD in finite-time and asymptotic regimes, matching accuracy of higher-order integrators under conditions.
- Algorithm description is clear, well-organized, and easy to implement.
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show significant bias reduction for SGRRLD.
- Real-data (MovieLens) experiments show only slight, arguable RMSE improvement.
- Unclear theoretical conditions: applicability when U is multimodal is not well addressed.
- Unclear why combining two chains reduces error order when gradient noises may be uncorrelated.
- Experimental concerns: claim of equal computation time with parallelization is not fully justified.
- Missing comparisons: no experiment comparing SGRRLD to SGLD with step size gamma/2 (lower bias).
- Recommendation to run more diverse real-data experiments to validate practical effectiveness.
- Strengths: novel RR application to SG-MCMC, formal analysis, clear implementation, strong synthetic results.
- Weaknesses: limited real-data validation, unclear theorem conditions, experimental design and comparisons need improvement.
- Review scores/recommendation: originality 7/10, significance 8/10, quality 8/10, clarity 9/10; accept with minor revisions.
