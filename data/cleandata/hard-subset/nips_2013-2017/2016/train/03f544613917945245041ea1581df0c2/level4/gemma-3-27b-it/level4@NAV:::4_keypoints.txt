- Paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC convergence (bias and MSE) with simple implementation and rigorous theory.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound but needs clarification on how the stated convergence rates follow from the presented theorem.  
- Practical cost concern: method uses two chains (one with double steps) and would take ~3× runtime on a single core, so time comparisons (Fig. 5) may be misleading.  
- Suggest rescaling SGLD timings (e.g., by 2/3) because current comparisons likely overstate SGRRLD’s advantage.  
- Figure 3’s bias plot indicates optimal α≈0.2 rather than the claimed α=1/3; the optimal curve is highly fluctuating and requires further investigation.  
- Clarify why the 1/(Kγ) term is said to dominate with small step sizes (line 256) and why similar behavior isn’t observed for SGLD.  
- Notational and presentation fixes: Equation 1 uses nonstandard θ notation and vectors should be bolded for clarity.  
- Define the abbreviation “RR” before its first use (line 94).  
- Define Γ_K where it appears (line 131).  
- Specify the type/degree of smoothness assumed for function f (line 160).  
- Fix the incorrect figure reference in line 249: Fig. 1(b) is Bias vs. time; the text likely meant Fig. 1(c).
