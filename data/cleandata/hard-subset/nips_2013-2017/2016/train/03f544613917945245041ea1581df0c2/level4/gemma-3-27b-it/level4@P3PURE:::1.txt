This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent “big-data MCMC” methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard numerical integration tool that has recently found compelling applications to numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs); a key strength of this paper is that it bridges the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially benefiting both communities. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical results concerning the convergence of the algorithm (including arguments for when SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD), and present supporting numerical results. They demonstrate impressive improvements on toy examples and modest, yet consistent, gains in large-scale applications. Overall, this is a solid and clearly written paper. As mentioned previously, it provides interesting connections between two relatively disconnected fields; features interesting theoretical results; and exhibits good empirical performance for the proposed algorithm. The paper is technically sound, readily accessible, and the method represents a computationally convenient application of RRE to SGLD. I anticipate practitioners will find these methods useful, although they are unlikely to fundamentally alter current practice. 

Minor comments: Figure 1a is slightly misleading as it depicts a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirically estimated posterior distribution itself. This should be clarified or more strongly emphasized for readers unfamiliar with the detail. Regarding Figure 2a, it would be helpful to include a discussion of why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the stepsize decreases; this observation is somewhat counterintuitive.
