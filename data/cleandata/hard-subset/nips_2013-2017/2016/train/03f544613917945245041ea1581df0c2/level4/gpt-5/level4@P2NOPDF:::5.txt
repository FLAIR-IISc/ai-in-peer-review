This paper proposes stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC) to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC methods. The approach applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation, a standard technique from SDE numerics, to combine two discretizations and cancel leading-order bias. Practically, it runs two independent SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes in parallel and combines their outputs, requiring no communication between chains. The authors provide a theoretical analysis establishing asymptotic consistency and report solid empirical performance.

Strengths:
- Conceptual simplicity: the method can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with SGLD, making it easy to implement.
- Parallelizability: the two-chain setup is trivially parallel, which is attractive for modern hardware.
- Theory: the paper presents a clear consistency result and argues for improved convergence rates via bias cancellation.
- Empirics: experiments suggest the approach is effective in practice.

Concerns and suggestions:
- Scope of novelty: while the idea is elegant, it largely repurposes a known extrapolation technique within SG-MCMC. The paper would benefit from a sharper discussion of when SG-RR-MCMC provides advantages beyond existing higher-order discretizations or integrators.
- Missing comparisons: the theoretical efficiency claims would be more convincing with experimental comparisons against high-order SG-MCMC integrators that also improve convergence rates. Without such comparisons, it is difficult to assess the practical trade-offs (e.g., variance amplification, computational overhead) relative to alternative bias-reduction strategies.
- Step-size description: there appears to be an inconsistency in the presentation of the step-size schedule. Please clarify the exact schedules used for both chains, how they are coupled for extrapolation, and the conditions assumed in the theory versus those used in experiments (e.g., decreasing vs. constant step sizes).
- Algorithmic mismatch: Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not align with Equation (5). This discrepancy should be resolved by either correcting the algorithm or updating the equation, and by providing a brief derivation to show their equivalence if that was intended.
- Practical guidance: it would be helpful to include guidance on choosing step-size pairs and combining weights, and to discuss sensitivity to minibatch noise, since Richardson–Romberg can increase variance.

Overall, this is an interesting and practical contribution that leverages a well-established SDE tool to reduce bias in SG-MCMC. The theoretical guarantees and empirical results are promising. Addressing the step-size inconsistency, fixing the Algorithm 1 vs. Equation (5) mismatch, and adding comparisons to higher-order integrators would significantly strengthen the paper and clarify its practical benefits.
