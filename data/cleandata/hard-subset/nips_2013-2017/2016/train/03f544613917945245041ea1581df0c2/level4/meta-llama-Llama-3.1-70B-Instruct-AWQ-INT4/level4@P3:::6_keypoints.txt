- Proposes SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation applied to SGLD, running two correlated SGLD chains with different step sizes.  
- Theoretical contributions: thorough asymptotic and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing higher convergence rates than first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments verify theoretical bias/MSE bounds.  
- Practical experiments: large-scale matrix factorization shows SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in computation time.  
- Main novelty concern: incremental contribution given prior analyses of integrators in SG-MCMC [10] and RR extrapolation for invariant distributions [23].  
- Main practicality concern: parallel implementation requires per-step communication between chains, but communication costs were ignored and not analyzed.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear resource allocation—whether SGLD used the same compute resources as the two SGRRLD chains, and how resources were split for gradient computation.  
- Missing implementation details: how partial resources per-chain for gradient computation could yield lower wall-clock time than full-resource SGLD.  
- Suggested additional experiment: compare against two embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains (no communication) to better isolate benefits of RR extrapolation.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—authors should analyze communication costs, clarify resource allocation and implementation setup, and better position novelty relative to prior work.  
- Minor comment: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated (lines 25 and 70).  
- Strengths summary: novel bias-reducing SG-MCMC, solid theoretical analysis, supportive experimental results.  
- Weaknesses summary: possibly incremental novelty, limited practical applicability, and insufficient discussion of communication and experimental fairness.
