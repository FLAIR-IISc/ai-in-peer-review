

This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard numerical integration technique that has recently seen notable applications in numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A key contribution of this work is establishing a connection between the MCMC and SDE literatures, which may benefit both communities. The authors introduce a novel SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical convergence results for the new algorithm—including arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves upon vanilla SGLD—and present numerical results. The method yields impressive gains on toy examples and modest but consistent improvements in large-scale experiments. Overall, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented. It offers interesting theoretical insights, bridges two somewhat disconnected fields, and demonstrates strong empirical performance for the proposed algorithm. The method constitutes a straightforward adaptation of RRE to SGLD, making it computationally convenient. While practitioners will likely adopt these techniques, they are unlikely to revolutionize current practice. Minor comments: Figure 1a is potentially misleading, as it shows a Gaussian approximation using the empirical posterior mean and variance rather than the empirical posterior distribution; this should be clarified. For Figure 2a, a brief discussion explaining why bias does not necessarily decrease as stepsize decreases would be helpful, as this behavior is counterintuitive.
