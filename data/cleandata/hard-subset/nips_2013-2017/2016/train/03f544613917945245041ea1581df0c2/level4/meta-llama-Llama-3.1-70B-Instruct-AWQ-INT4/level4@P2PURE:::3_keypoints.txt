- Contribution: Proposes Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated-noise chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second chain double length) and subtracting estimates to reduce bias to O(γ^2) and improve MSE.
- Simplicity and impact: Method is simple, general, requires little modification to base SG-MCMC, and could substantially influence the SG-MCMC community.
- Novelty: Applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation from numerical analysis to SG-MCMC is novel in the ML/MCMC literature.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Applicability question: Is the technique directly applicable to all SG-MCMC methods or are additional conditions beyond the bound in Section 2.2 required?
- Extensions question: Can the method be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings, distributed SGLD)?
- Computational cost/variance question: Since one chain must run twice as long, can using three chains (γ, γ/2, γ/2) run for the same number of steps avoid wasted computation, and would that increase estimator variance?
- Multi-step-size question: Is there a straightforward extension to more than two step sizes?
- Correlation question: How does correlation between injected Gaussian noise and between minibatches affect variance—are same minibatches for Chain 1 kth and Chain 2 (2k−1,2k) iterations optimal or negligible?
- Experimental suggestion: Run the matrix factorization experiment to convergence and include an SGD baseline to demonstrate scenarios where Bayesian inference (SGRRLD) beats SGD, not just outperforming SGLD.
- Presentation suggestion: Add an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility and ease of implementation for readers unfamiliar with the area.
