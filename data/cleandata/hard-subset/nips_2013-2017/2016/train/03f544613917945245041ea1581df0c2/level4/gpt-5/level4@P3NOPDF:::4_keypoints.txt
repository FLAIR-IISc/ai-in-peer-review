- Summary: Proposes a Richardson–Romberg variance-reduction scheme for SG-MCMC that cancels leading discretization bias and improves bias/MSE rates with simple implementation and solid theory.

- Strength — Conceptual clarity: Adapting RR to SG-MCMC is natural, nontrivial, and well-articulated.

- Strength — Practicality: Integrates into existing SG-MCMC with minimal code overhead.

- Strength — Theory: Theoretical bounds and claimed rate improvements appear well-founded under standard assumptions.

- Strength — Empirics: Experiments generally support the theoretical improvements.

- Main concern 1 — Derivation of rates: The paper must explicitly show how the theorem yields the stated bias/MSE rates (decompose bias/MSE into stochastic-gradient noise, discretization error, finite-time terms and show which orders RR cancels).

- Main concern 1 — Regularity assumptions: Specify precise assumptions (Lipschitz gradient, dissipativity, Lipschitz Hessian, Poisson-equation regularity) and how they imply local weak error orders and global bias orders after RR.

- Main concern 1 — Range and optimality of α: Show admissible α range, derive why α = 1/3 is optimal from bounds (including constants).

- Main concern 2 — Fairness of matrix factorization experiment: Account for higher compute (coupled chains ≈3× gradient cost); report results under equal computational budgets (gradient-evaluations or wall-clock) and/or rescale curves.

- Main concern 2 — Hardware and parallelism: Report hardware, cores/GPUs, and whether chains were run in parallel.

- Main concern 3 — Figure 3 discrepancy: Explain why α = 0.2 appears best in bias plot despite claim α = 1/3; investigate finite-sample effects or constants.

- Main concern 3 — Variability in optimal curve: Add more repetitions, error bars, or smoothing to show predicted rates and identify whether extrapolation/minibatch coupling increases variance.

- Main concern 4 — Small step-size regime: Explain why 1/(Kγ) dominance is not observed for SGLD in plots; consider an ablation increasing K at very small γ.

- Main concern 5 — Coupling implementation: Specify whether chains share minibatches/seeds synchronously to reduce variance and enable theoretical cancellation.

- Minor editorial — Notation and definitions: Use consistent parameter notation (θ), define “RR” at first use, define Γ_K when first used, and state required smoothness for f (e.g., C^2, Lipschitz gradient/Hessian).

- Minor editorial — Figure reference and readability: Correct figure reference (Figure 1(b)/(c)) and use bold font for vectors/matrices.

- Suggestion — Theory-to-rates bridge: Add a short subsection mapping the theorem to final complexity/rate statements with explicit bias/MSE decomposition showing RR's role.

- Suggestion — Compute-normalized comparisons: Include wall-clock-time or gradient-evaluation-normalized comparisons for all baselines and report per-iteration and per-gradient-evaluation metrics.

- Suggestion — Uncertainty and stability: Report confidence intervals/shaded standard errors, increase independent runs, and show stability of claimed rates.

- Suggestion — Practical trade-off discussion: Discuss per-iteration cost vs. asymptotic gains and indicate regimes (dataset size, model dimension, target accuracy) where RR-SG-MCMC yields net wall-clock benefits.

- Suggestion — Additional baselines: If feasible, compare to well-tuned SGHMC/SGRLD or other advanced SG-MCMC variants under equal compute.

- Overall evaluation: Solid, well-motivated, and technically strong contribution; acceptance is favored pending clearer derivation of rates and fair, compute-normalized empirical comparisons.
