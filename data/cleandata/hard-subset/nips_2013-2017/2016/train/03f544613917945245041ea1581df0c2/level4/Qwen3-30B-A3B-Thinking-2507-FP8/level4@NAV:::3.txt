

Stochastic Gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) methods offer an efficient approach to Bayesian posterior inference on large datasets and have garnered significant research interest recently. A key limitation is their asymptotic bias, preventing exact convergence to the posterior distribution. This paper demonstrates that Richardson-Romberg extrapolation can mitigate this bias. The core approach involves running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel: Chain 2 uses half the step size of Chain 1 and executes twice as many iterations. The Gaussian noise injected into the chains is correlated via αₖ = (β_{2k-1} + β_{2k})/√2, where αₖ and βₖ denote the noise at time step k for Chains 1 and 2, respectively. For estimating the expectation of a function f under the target distribution, the method subtracts the Chain 1 estimate from the Chain 2 estimate. This yields an estimate with reduced bias O(γ²) compared to O(γ) from either chain alone (γ being the step size), and similarly lower MSE. The simplicity of implementation—requiring minimal modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms—and broad applicability across a wide class of SG-MCMC methods are notable strengths, suggesting significant potential impact. Novelty: While Richardson-Romberg extrapolation is established in numerical analysis, its application to SG-MCMC is novel within machine learning. Clarity: The paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
Comments/questions:  
- Is the technique universally applicable to all SG-MCMC methods, or are additional conditions required beyond the bound in Section 2.2 (π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ²))?  
- Can it extend to non-stochastic gradient approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., sub-sampling Metropolis-Hastings, Distributed SGLD)?  
- Running Chain 1 for half the steps of Chain 2 leads to computational inefficiency (Chain 1 idle during Chain 2’s execution). Would using three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2, all running equal steps) reduce this waste? Would variance increase?  
- Is there a generalization to more than two step sizes?  
- The noise correlation requirement needs clarification: Is the minimal variance achieved when the same mini-batch is used for Chain 1’s k-th iteration and Chains 2’s (2k-1)-th and 2k-th iterations, or is the correlation effect negligible?  
- The matrix factorization experiment (Figure 5) should be extended to convergence and include SGD for comparison. Current results only show superiority over SGLD; demonstrating advantage over SGD (where Bayesian inference is beneficial) would strengthen the case, especially given Bayesian matrix factorization’s limited practical adoption.  
- An algorithmic pseudocode box would enhance accessibility for practitioners, enabling immediate implementation without delving into theoretical details.
