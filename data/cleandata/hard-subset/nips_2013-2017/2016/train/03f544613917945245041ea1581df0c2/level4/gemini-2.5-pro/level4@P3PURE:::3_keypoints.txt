- Proposes reducing asymptotic bias in SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two correlated chains with step sizes γ and γ/2.  
- Correlates injected Gaussian noise via α_k = (β_{2k-1} + β_{2k})/√2 and combines chain estimates to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- The combined estimator reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and yields a corresponding MSE reduction (theoretically proved).  
- Method is simple to implement and requires minimal modification to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Approach appears broadly applicable to a wide class of SG-MCMC methods, suggesting potential high impact.  
- Novel application of a classical Richardson-Romberg technique to SG-MCMC; paper is well-written and clear.  
- Question about generality: does the technique require only the bias expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) or additional conditions?  
- Query about scope: can the method extend to other approximate MCMC (e.g., subsampled MH) or distributed variants like Distributed SGLD?  
- Computational-efficiency concern: one chain must idle while the other runs, leading to wasted computation; asks about running three chains (one γ, two γ/2) instead and impact on variance.  
- Asks about higher-order extrapolation: can more than two step sizes be used for further bias correction?  
- Unclear role of mini-batch correlation: is using the same mini-batch across corresponding iterations optimal for variance reduction or negligible?  
- Experimental concerns: matrix factorization experiments should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to contextualize Bayesian gains.  
- Accessibility suggestion: include an algorithm box or pseudocode early to aid practitioners in implementation.
