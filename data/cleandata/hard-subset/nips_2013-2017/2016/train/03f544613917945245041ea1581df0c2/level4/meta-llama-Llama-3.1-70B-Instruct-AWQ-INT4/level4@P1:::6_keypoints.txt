- Proposes SGRRLD: a SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Algorithm runs two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence, bias and MSE bounds, and improved convergence rates over first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization showing practical benefits.  
- Contribution is valid and well-situated in prior literature.  
- Concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators ([10]) and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions ([23]).  
- Practical concern: parallel implementation assumes zero communication cost and does not discuss implications of required per-step communication.  
- Experimental fairness unclear: how computing resources are allocated between SGRRLD (two chains) and SGLD (single chain) is not specified.  
- Unclear whether SGRRLD’s partial-resource gradient computations yield better wall-clock time than full-resource SGLD; setup details missing.  
- Suggested comparison missing: two embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains (no communication) versus the proposed communicating SGRRLD.  
- Minor issue: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" duplicated in the text.  
- Strengths summarized: novel algorithm with better rates, solid theoretical analysis, and supportive experiments.  
- Weaknesses summarized: potentially incremental novelty, limited practical applicability, and omission of communication cost considerations.  
- Recommendations: discuss communication cost implications, clarify experiment resource allocation and implementation details, and compare to two independent SGLD chains.
