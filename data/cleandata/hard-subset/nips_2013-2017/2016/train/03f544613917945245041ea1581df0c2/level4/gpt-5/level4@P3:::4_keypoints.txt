- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining them to cancel leading discretization bias.  
- Uses correlated Brownian increments to control variance, focusing on SGLD with an extension sketch for SGHMC.  
- Theoretical contributions: CLT for the RR estimator with variance-minimizing correlation, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and improved rates (e.g., bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) for constant steps).  
- Claims SGRRLD attains higher-order integrator accuracy while using only first-order updates; experiments on linear-Gaussian and MovieLens support this.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple, easy to implement, and parallelizable (two chains run in parallel).  
- Strength: Solid theoretical development—consistency, CLT, non-asymptotic bounds, and identification of optimal Gaussian noise correlation.  
- Strength: Empirical improvements over SGLD and initial evidence of benefits for SGHMC.  
- Strength: Provides a practical path to higher accuracy without designing new higher-order integrators.  
- Weakness: Main text lacks transparent derivations of optimal rates (e.g., α=1/5 from Theorem 1 and γ scalings from Theorem 2).  
- Weakness: Experimental fairness—wall-clock comparisons use parallelism for SGRRLD while SGLD is sequential; needs equivalent-sequential compute (or FLOP) comparisons.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 and claimed optimal α mismatch (text says α=1/3 but α=0.2 looks best); labeling, estimated constant dependence, and variability need checking and error bars.  
- Weakness: Small-γ regime explanation missing—need to explain why small-γ deterioration predicted by theory is not visible for SGLD or adjust K/γ ranges.  
- Weakness: Strong assumptions in CLT (e.g., solution g∈C^9); precise assumptions and discussion of stochastic gradient noise dominance should be moved to main text and justified.  
- Quality: Analysis is technically sound and well-aligned with prior RR/SDE and SG-MCMC theory, though some steps and assumptions should be clearer in the main text.  
- Clarity: Generally well written but some notational/editorial issues and lack of algorithm box reduce readability.  
- Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with a variance-minimizing coupling; tailored bounds and CLT are original.  
- Significance: Simple, practical, and potentially impactful—many SG-MCMC samplers could adopt RR to reduce bias without redesigning integrators.  
- Pro for acceptance: Simple, broadly applicable idea with theoretical guarantees and clear finite-time and asymptotic improvements.  
- Con for acceptance: Compute-fairness issues in experiments, buried derivations/assumptions, and minor inconsistencies in figures/text need correction.  
- Specific comment: Inconsistent notation (θ vs ϑ); use a single symbol consistently.  
- Specific comment: Define Richardson–Romberg (RR) at first mention and define ΓK and Γ(n)K before use.  
- Specific comment: State exact smoothness class for test functions (e.g., f∈C^p with polynomial growth).  
- Specific comment: Correct figure reference (bias vs time vs dimension) and use consistent vector formatting.  
- Specific comment: Clarify minibatch distribution requirements and whether minibatches are shared or independent across chains in experiments.  
- Specific comment: Briefly explain practical implementation of correlated Brownian increments with PRNGs.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong submission leaning toward acceptance, contingent on clarifying rate derivations, fixing compute-fairness in experiments, and addressing noted inconsistencies.
