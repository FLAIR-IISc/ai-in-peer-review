- Addresses SG-MCMC asymptotic bias by using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Method: run two SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2 with twice the steps) and correlate noise via α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2.  
- Estimator 2·E₂[f] − E₁[f] achieves bias O(γ²) vs O(γ) for single chains, reducing MSE.  
- Minimal modification to existing SG-MCMC algorithms and broadly applicable across the SG-MCMC family.  
- Novelty: adapts Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC where it hasn’t been widely used.  
- Strength: simplicity and generality make the approach easy to implement and practically appealing.  
- Strength: theoretical rigor—bias and MSE reductions are proven under standard SG-MCMC assumptions.  
- Strength: high potential impact due to broad applicability to SG-MCMC variants.  
- Concern: scope of applicability unclear—need to state exact conditions beyond π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²) (e.g., noise structure, step-size schedules).  
- Concern: computational inefficiency—Chain 2’s doubled steps cause idle time for Chain 1; suggest exploring a three-chain scheme and its effect on variance.  
- Concern: unclear extensibility to more than two step sizes (γ, γ/2, γ/4) and what prevents such extensions.  
- Concern: need clarification on how batch-level noise correlation affects variance and whether using identical mini-batches minimizes variance.  
- Experimental concern: matrix factorization experiments should compare SG-MCMC (SGLD, SGRRLD) to SGD to demonstrate practical benefits and relevance.  
- Accessibility suggestion: include a concise algorithm box/pseudocode early to aid practitioners.  
- Minor notes: explicitly list applicable SG-MCMC examples (e.g., SGLD, SGHMC) and use consistent notation for noise correlation.  
- Overall: elegant, sound, and adoptable method that merits publication after minor revisions addressing applicability, efficiency, and experimental comparisons.
