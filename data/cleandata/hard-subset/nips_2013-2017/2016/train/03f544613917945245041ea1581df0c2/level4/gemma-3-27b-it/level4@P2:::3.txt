Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods have gained prominence for Bayesian posterior inference in large-scale datasets. A key limitation of these methods is their inherent bias, preventing exact convergence to the posterior distribution. This paper introduces a novel approach, leveraging Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, to mitigate this bias. The core idea involves running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel: one with step size γ and another with step size γ/2, with the latter running for twice the iterations. Correlated Gaussian noise is injected into the chains, and the final estimator is constructed by combining the outputs of the two chains. This results in a reduced bias of O(γ²) compared to the O(γ) bias typically observed with standard SG-MCMC. The authors also demonstrate a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE). 

This work builds upon the well-established RR extrapolation technique from numerical analysis and applies it to the context of SG-MCMC. The simplicity of the method – requiring minimal modification to existing SG-MCMC algorithms – is a notable strength. Its potential generality across various SG-MCMC algorithms also suggests a potentially significant impact on the field. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear and intuitive idea:** The proposed method is conceptually straightforward and easy to understand.
*   **Minimal implementation effort:** RR extrapolation requires only minor changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.
*   **Generality:** The technique appears broadly applicable to a range of SG-MCMC algorithms.
*   **Strong theoretical grounding:** The paper provides formal theoretical analysis, including convergence guarantees and bounds on bias and MSE.
*   **Empirical validation:** Experiments on both synthetic and real datasets support the theoretical findings.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational inefficiency:** Running one chain for twice as long as the other introduces potential computational overhead, as one chain spends time idle.
*   **Correlation of mini-batches:** The impact of correlated mini-batches on performance is not fully explored and could be clarified.
*   **Limited comparison to SGD:** While demonstrating improvement over SGLD, the matrix factorization experiment lacks a direct comparison to Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD), limiting the ability to showcase the benefits of the Bayesian approach.
*   **Lack of pseudo-code:** The absence of a concise algorithmic description (e.g., a pseudo-code box) hinders accessibility for practitioners unfamiliar with the details of the method.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant challenge in SG-MCMC – bias reduction – with a conceptually simple and theoretically sound approach.
*   The theoretical results are rigorous and provide valuable insights into the convergence properties of the proposed method.
*   The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of RR extrapolation in improving the accuracy of SG-MCMC.
*   The method’s generality and ease of implementation increase its potential impact.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The computational inefficiency introduced by the unequal chain lengths is a practical concern.
*   The comparison to SGD in the matrix factorization experiment is incomplete, limiting the demonstration of the benefits of the Bayesian approach.
*   The discussion regarding the correlation of mini-batches could be more thorough.



**Suggestions for improvement:**

*   **Address computational inefficiency:** Explore alternative strategies to mitigate the computational cost of running chains with different lengths, such as utilizing three chains with step sizes γ, γ/2, and γ/2, run for the same number of iterations. Investigate the impact on variance.
*   **Expand mini-batch correlation discussion:** Provide a more detailed explanation of how the correlation of mini-batches impacts performance, and clarify whether specific strategies optimize this correlation.
*   **Include SGD comparison:** Add a comparison to SGD in the matrix factorization experiment to better highlight the advantages of the Bayesian approach.
*   **Provide pseudo-code:** Include a concise algorithmic description of SGRRLD (e.g., a pseudo-code box) to enhance accessibility.
*   **Clarify applicability conditions:** Elaborate on the precise conditions required for the RR extrapolation to be applicable to various SG-MCMC methods beyond those satisfying the bounds in Section 2.2.
*   **Investigate multi-step extension:** Explore the potential for extending the method to utilize more than two step sizes.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The proposed method is elegant, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. While some practical considerations and points for clarification remain, the overall quality and potential impact of the work justify acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper, with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
