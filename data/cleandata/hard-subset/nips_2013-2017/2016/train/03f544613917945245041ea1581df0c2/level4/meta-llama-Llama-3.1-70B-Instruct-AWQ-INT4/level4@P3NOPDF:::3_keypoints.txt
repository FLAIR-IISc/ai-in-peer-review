1. Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias in SG-MCMC.  
2. Method runs two parallel chains: one with step size γ, one with γ/2 run for twice as many steps.  
3. Injected Gaussian noises are correlated via αk = (β2k-1 + β2k)/√2.  
4. Combined estimator cancels O(γ) bias, achieving O(γ^2) bias and similar MSE reduction.  
5. Method is simple, easy to implement, and broadly applicable to SG-MCMC algorithms.  
6. Novel application of a classical numerical-analysis technique to ML/SG-MCMC.  
7. Paper is well-written and accessible.  
8. Question: Does the technique apply to all SG-MCMC methods or only when πγ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) holds?  
9. Question: Can this extrapolation be used for other approximate MCMC schemes (subsampling MH, distributed SGLD)?  
10. Question: Can a three-chain scheme (one γ, two γ/2 run equal steps) avoid wasted computation, and would it increase variance?  
11. Question: Is there an extension to more than two step sizes?  
12. Question: How important is minibatch correlation—should the same batch be used across paired iterations to minimize variance?  
13. Suggestion: Run matrix factorization experiments to full convergence and compare to SGD for a more complete evaluation.  
14. Suggestion: Add an early algorithm box/pseudocode to improve practitioner accessibility.  
15. Overall: Valuable, simple method with strong potential impact if open questions are addressed.
