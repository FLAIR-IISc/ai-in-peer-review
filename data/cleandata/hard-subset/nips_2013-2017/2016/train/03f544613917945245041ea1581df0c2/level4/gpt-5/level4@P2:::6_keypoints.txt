- Introduces SGRRLD: run two SGLD-like chains at γ and γ/2, combine via Richardson–Romberg with correlated Gaussian noise to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretical contributions: consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds; explicit optimal step-size schedules and rates.  
- Achieves improved MSE scaling O(K^−4/5), matching higher-order integrators using only a first-order discretization.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic linear Gaussian experiments match theory; matrix factorization shows wall-clock gains over SGLD.  
- Extends RR idea to SGHMC (SGRRHMC) and claims accuracy comparable to higher-order SGHMC integrators.  
- Strength: clear, simple, broadly applicable idea that is easy to implement and parallelize.  
- Strength: solid, careful theoretical analysis with justified correlated Brownian increments.  
- Strength: empirical results support theoretical scaling and practical benefits.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—RR and integrator-order effects are known for SDEs and SG-MCMC.  
- Weakness: two-chain design requires correlated noise and coordination; communication/synchronization overheads are not quantified.  
- Weakness: unclear whether wall-clock comparisons used matched compute budgets, cores, or throughput between methods.  
- Weakness: missing important baselines—two independent parallel SGLD chains and broader higher-order SG-MCMC comparisons on real data.  
- Quality: theory appears technically sound; proofs deferred to supplement.  
- Quality: experimental methodology solid for synthetic tests but large-scale experiments need more compute/communication detail.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized; estimator and coupling choices are clearly presented.  
- Clarify how correlated Gaussians are generated in parallel and whether minibatches are shared or independent across chains.  
- Originality: conceptually modest; novelty is in adapting RR to stochastic-gradient setting and proving CLTs/non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Significance: potentially widely applicable if computational benefits hold under fair resource accounting and communication costs.  
- Suggested improvements: report hardware/resource allocation, quantify communication overhead, and explain RNG/coupling implementation.  
- Suggested improvements: add baselines—(a) two independent parallel SGLD chains with same hardware, (b) higher-order SG-MCMC methods, (c) tuned SGLD under same time budget.  
- Suggested improvements: provide sensitivity analyses for minibatch size, step-size schedules, and coupling strength (Σ).  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general method with strong theory, practical implementation, and extension to SGHMC.  
- Cons for acceptance: incremental relative to prior work and experimental comparisons may not reflect equal resources or include necessary baselines.  
- Minor comments: remove duplicate ULA definition and fix several typos/grammatical errors.
