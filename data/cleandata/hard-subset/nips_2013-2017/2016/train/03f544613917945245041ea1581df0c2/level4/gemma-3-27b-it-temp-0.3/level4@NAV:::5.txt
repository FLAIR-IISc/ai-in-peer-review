The authors present stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), a new method designed to mitigate bias in stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. Their approach leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for stochastic differential equations – and notably achieves parallelization through the independent execution of two chains without inter-chain communication. The method’s asymptotic consistency is supported by theoretical analysis, and the empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness. The core idea represents a straightforward yet promising combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). While the theoretical analysis indicates an improved convergence rate, a comparison to higher-order integrators would strengthen this claim (and was absent from the experimental evaluation). A discrepancy exists regarding the discussion of step size selection. Additionally, the provided Algorithm 1 (in the supplementary material) does not align with the formulation presented in Equation (5).
