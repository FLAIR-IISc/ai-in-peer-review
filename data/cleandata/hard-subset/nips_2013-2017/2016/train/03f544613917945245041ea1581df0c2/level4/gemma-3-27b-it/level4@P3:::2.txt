This paper introduces SGRRLD, a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm designed to reduce bias compared to standard SGLD. The core idea is to apply Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation – a numerical sequence acceleration technique – by running two SGLD chains in parallel with differing step sizes. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating improved convergence rates and error bounds for SGRRLD, and support these findings with experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The paper builds upon the growing body of work in SG-MCMC, particularly extending the foundational SGLD approach [Welling & Teh, 2011] and recent explorations of higher-order integrators [Chen et al., 2015; Teh et al., 2016]. The application of RR extrapolation to SDEs, as highlighted by references to Talay & Tubaro [1990] and Lemaire & Pagès [2015], positions this work within a well-established numerical analysis framework. The novelty lies in adapting this technique specifically to the stochastic gradient setting common in large-scale Bayesian inference.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a formal theoretical analysis, including asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE. This grounding in theory is a significant strength, lending credibility to the proposed algorithm.
*   **Clear Algorithm Description:** The SGRRLD algorithm is clearly described, making it relatively straightforward to understand and potentially implement.
*   **Synthetic Experiment Validation:** The synthetic experiments on a Gaussian model effectively demonstrate the bias reduction achieved by SGRRLD compared to SGLD.
*   **Potential for Parallelization:** The inherent parallelism of running two chains simultaneously is a practical advantage, particularly for modern computing architectures.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** The paper appropriately acknowledges and builds upon related work in both SG-MCMC and numerical analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Real-Data Experiments:** While real-data experiments are presented, the conclusions drawn from the MovieLens datasets are somewhat tentative. The improvement observed isn't substantial, and the experimental setup could be more comprehensive.
*   **Experimental Setup Concerns:** The comparison in the real-data experiments isn't entirely fair. Comparing SGRRLD (with step sizes γ and γ/2) to SGLD (with step size γ) doesn't isolate the benefit of RR extrapolation. A comparison with SGLD using a step size of γ/2 (and potentially running multiple parallel chains) is needed to properly assess the advantage of the proposed method.
*   **Correlation of Chains:** The paper discusses correlated Brownian motions, but doesn’t fully explore the degree of correlation observed *in practice* between the two chains during the real-data experiments. Quantifying this correlation would be valuable.
*   **Technical Condition Clarification:**  The conditions on the potential energy function *U* required for Theorems 1 & 2 could be stated more explicitly.  Is the method applicable to multi-modal *U*?
*   **Intuition for Error Reduction:** The paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of *how* RR extrapolation reduces the order of the error, given the potential for rapid decorrelation of the chains due to noisy gradients.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded algorithm with the potential to improve the efficiency of SG-MCMC.
*   The theoretical analysis is strong and provides valuable insights into the convergence properties of SGRRLD.
*   The synthetic experiments provide convincing evidence of bias reduction.
*   The algorithm’s inherent parallelism is a practical advantage.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The real-data experiments are not entirely convincing and lack a comprehensive comparison against appropriately configured baselines.
*   The paper does not fully explore the practical aspects of chain correlation.
*   The technical conditions on the potential energy function *U* are not fully clarified.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The theoretical results are solid, and the proposed algorithm shows promise. However, the limited and somewhat flawed real-data experiments raise concerns about the practical impact of SGRRLD. I believe the paper is currently borderline.

**Recommendation:**

**Weak Accept.** I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors addressing the concerns raised regarding the real-data experiments. Specifically, they should: 

1.  Include a comparison with SGLD using a step size of γ/2 and multiple parallel chains.
2.  Quantify the correlation observed between the two chains in the real-data experiments.
3.  Clarify the technical conditions on the potential energy function *U* required for the theorems to hold.

Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase confidence in the practical value of SGRRLD.
