- Paper shows Richardson-Romberg extrapolation can reduce SG-MCMC asymptotic bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) by running two correlated chains with step sizes γ and γ/2.  
- Core implementation: Chain 2 runs twice as many steps with half step size, using correlated Gaussian noise α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2.  
- Authors prove a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE) for the extrapolated estimator.  
- Method is simple, easy to implement, requires minimal modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Technique appears broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC methods, potentially impactful for the community.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is novel in the ML/MCMC context.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Question: Can the technique be universally applied to all SG-MCMC methods, or are there extra conditions beyond the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2)?  
- Question: Is the method adaptable to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) and to distributed SGLD?  
- Efficiency concern: running one chain for twice the iterations may underutilize resources—would running three chains (γ, γ/2, γ/2) in parallel be more efficient and how would that affect variance?  
- Question: Is there a natural extension to use more than two step sizes for higher-order bias cancellation?  
- Question: How important is correlation of mini-batches between the chains—does using the same minibatch for corresponding iterations materially reduce variance?  
- Experimental critique: matrix factorization experiment should be run to convergence and include a standard SGD baseline to assess practical advantage and generalization gains.  
- Presentation suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
