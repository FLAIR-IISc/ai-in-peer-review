This paper adapts Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to scalable “big-data” MCMC, with a primary focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a classical sequence-acceleration technique from numerical analysis that has seen productive use for numerical SDEs; a notable contribution here is the explicit bridge it builds between the SG-MCMC and SDE literatures. The authors propose an RRE-enhanced SGLD (SGLD-RRE), develop convergence theory (including conditions under which it improves over standard SGLD), and present empirical results. The method yields striking gains on controlled toy problems and smaller but steady gains on large-scale tasks. Overall, this is a strong, clearly presented submission.

As noted above, the paper is solid: it forges useful connections across two relatively separate areas, provides meaningful theoretical results, and demonstrates good empirical performance for the new algorithm. The exposition is clear and easy to follow, and the work appears technically sound. The approach is ultimately a fairly direct application of RRE to SGLD, which makes it computationally attractive. I expect practitioners will find it useful in practice, though it is unlikely to be transformative on its own.

Minor comments:
- Figure 1a is potentially misleading: it plots a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance rather than the empirical posterior itself. Please make this explicit to avoid confusion.
- Figure 2a: it would help to discuss why the bias need not decrease monotonically as the step size shrinks, since this can be counterintuitive.
