- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGLD) by running two chains with stepsizes γ and γ/2, using correlated Gaussian noise to cancel leading discretization bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: consistency, CLT with variance-minimizing correlated increments, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing O(γ^2) bias, O(γ^4) variance, and optimal finite-time MSE O(K^−4/5) with γ_k ∝ k^−1/5.  
- Achieves higher-order accuracy without higher-order integrators; parallels classical RR results and prior higher-order SG-MCMC work but requires only first-order discretization plus correlated noise.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, broadly applicable, easy to implement and naturally parallelizable.  
- Strength: solid theoretical development and plausible, carefully stated claims (consistency, CLT, finite-time improvements).  
- Strength: synthetic experiments show reduced bias and improved MSE; SGHMC variant behaves as predicted.  
- Strength: real-data experiments demonstrate wall-clock advantages by exploiting parallelism.  
- Weakness: main assumptions on U, test functions f, and gradient noise law L are deferred to the supplement and not clearly stated in main text.  
- Weakness/question: unclear whether theorems hold for multimodal/non-convex U; need clarity on Lyapunov/ergodicity conditions and their realism.  
- Weakness/question: analysis relies on correlated Gaussian increments, but gradient noise across chains may be uncorrelated in practice—robustness to weak/absent gradient correlation is unclear.  
- Weakness/question: authors did not report empirical inter-chain correlations (states or test function values) in real-data runs.  
- Experimental fairness concern: need baselines running two independent SGLD chains in parallel (2×SGLD) and SGLD with stepsize γ/2 (possibly two chains) to match wall-clock and bias comparisons.  
- Empirical concern: real-data gains are modest; more datasets/tasks would strengthen practical claims and robustness.  
- Practical guidance missing: how to tune stepsizes (constant vs. decaying), batch sizes, and when RR extrapolation is most beneficial in practice.  
- Presentation issues: scattered typos/formatting; main assumptions should be consolidated in the main text for clarity.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and well-motivated by RR literature, but exact conditions on U for theorems need clarification.  
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written and clear algorithm, but key assumptions buried in supplement and intuition on correlated increments should be expanded.  
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC; achieves higher accuracy with first-order integrators plus extrapolation.  
- Significance: simple, general, and parallel-friendly method with potential practical impact if empirical gains scale, but current real-data evidence is modest.  
- Suggestions: state precise assumptions in main text; report inter-chain correlations and sensitivity to minibatch sharing; add baselines (2×SGLD, SGLD with γ/2); include more real datasets; provide tuning guidance.  
- Pros summary: simple, general, improves bias/MSE without higher-order integrators, parallel-friendly, convincing synthetic results and SGHMC extension.  
- Cons summary: unclear assumptions/applicability, modest real-data gains, missing stronger baselines and empirical analysis of inter-chain correlation.  
- Overall: solid, elegant contribution with strong theory and promising experiments; would be stronger with clearer assumptions and more comprehensive real-data comparisons, so reviewer leans positive.
