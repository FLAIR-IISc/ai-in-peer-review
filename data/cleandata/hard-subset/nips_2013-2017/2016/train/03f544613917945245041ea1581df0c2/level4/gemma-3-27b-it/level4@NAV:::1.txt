This paper investigates the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to modern “big-data MCMC” techniques, specifically Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE, a well-established numerical integration method, has recently found successful applications in the numerical solution of stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A key strength of this work is its bridging of the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially offering benefits to both fields. The authors propose a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, accompanied by theoretical convergence analysis – including justification for performance gains over standard SGLD – and supporting numerical demonstrations. Results show substantial improvements on test problems, and consistent, albeit more modest, gains on larger-scale applications. In summary, this is a strong and clearly written contribution. It establishes interesting connections between two traditionally separate areas, presents compelling theoretical findings, and demonstrates good empirical performance for the proposed algorithm. The method represents a readily implementable application of RRE to SGLD, and is computationally efficient. While not transformative, it is likely to be adopted by practitioners.

This distinction should be more clearly stated to avoid misinterpretation.
