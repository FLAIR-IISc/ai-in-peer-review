- Problem: Addresses high bias in SG-MCMC and proposes reducing it via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation.
- Method: Proposes SGRRLD—run two parallel SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2, combine outputs linearly, and correlate Brownian increments to control variance.
- Theoretical results: Provides CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, showing improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Novelty/significance: Novel and potentially high-impact application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, extensible to other SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Technical quality: Rigorous analysis with asymptotic consistency and clear theoretical advantages over first-order methods.
- Clarity and simplicity: Well-written paper; method is conceptually simple and easy to implement, supported by synthetic and real-data experiments.
- Experimental limitation: Matrix factorization results lack comparisons to strong optimization baselines (e.g., SGD) and should include runs to full convergence.
- Applicability scope: Needs clearer, formal conditions for compatibility (e.g., beyond existence of weak error expansion) and discussion for MH corrections or non-Gaussian noise.
- Implementation guidance: More practical discussion needed on correlating mini-batches vs independent batches and finite-sample variance trade-offs.
- Computational efficiency: Parallel scheme wastes a core while one chain waits; authors should acknowledge this and consider alternatives (e.g., running a third independent chain).
- Presentation: Include a main-text pseudocode box to improve accessibility and ease of implementation.
