- Introduces SGRRLD: a Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical results: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets shows improved convergence vs SGLD and comparable performance to higher-order integrators.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, with a brief extension to HMC.  
- Clear presentation and well-established connection to existing SG-MCMC literature.  
- Demonstrates practical benefit in a matrix factorization experiment.  
- Inherent parallelizability due to independent chain generation.  
- Weakness: derivation of specific convergence rates (e.g., O(K^-4/5)) is not fully explained in the text.  
- Weakness: experimental fairness — running two chains increases computation; comparisons should account for this extra cost.  
- Weakness: discrepancy in Figure 3 — reported optimal α (1/3) contradicts plotted optimal value (~0.2); fluctuating behavior needs explanation.  
- Weakness: several minor technical/notation issues (undefined "RR", Γ_K used before definition, inconsistent vector notation, Eq.1 symbol mismatch, unspecified smoothness of f, Figure 1(b) label error).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method with strong empirical support and useful parallel properties.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear convergence-rate derivation, potentially biased experimental comparison, and minor technical inconsistencies.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: (1) clarify convergence-rate derivation, (2) address computational-cost fairness in experiments, (3) reconcile/clarify Figure 3 and its fluctuations, (4) fix notation and figure-label issues.
