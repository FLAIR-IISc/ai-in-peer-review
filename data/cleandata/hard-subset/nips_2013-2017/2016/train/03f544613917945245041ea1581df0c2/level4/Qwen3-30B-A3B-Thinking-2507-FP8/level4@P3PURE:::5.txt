

The authors propose SG-RR-MCMC, a novel method for reducing bias in stochastic gradient MCMC via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SDEs. The approach runs two chains in an embarrassingly parallel manner without communication. Theoretical analysis establishes asymptotic consistency, and empirical results demonstrate effectiveness. The method represents a straightforward integration of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD, yielding improved convergence rates theoretically. However, the paper does not compare against high-order integrators in experiments, despite the convergence rate improvement being achievable with such methods. Critically, the paper contains inconsistent statements regarding the step size schedule. Additionally, Algorithm 1 (in the supplement) is inconsistent with Equation (5). Overall, the method is both theoretically sound and empirically effective, though these technical issues require resolution.
