

This paper proposes a Richardson-Romberg scheme to improve convergence rates (bias and MSE) for stochastic gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC), with minimal implementation changes to existing methods. The theoretical results are solid, and the approach is well-motivated. However, several points require clarification or correction to ensure the claims are fully supported.

First, the derivation of the convergence rates (as referenced in the theoretical justification section) needs more detail. The specific steps linking the theorems to the stated rates should be explicitly explained to validate the claims.

Second, the computational efficiency claim in the matrix factorization experiment (Section 5.2) appears misleading. Running the proposed method on a single core would take approximately three times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to two parallel chains and doubled steps per chain. Consequently, the time in Figure 5 should be adjusted: multiplying SGLD’s runtime by 2/3 would significantly reduce the observed performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD. Without this adjustment, the comparison is unfair.

Third, Figure 3 (bias vs. steps) shows the best convergence at α=0.2 (red curve), contradicting the text’s assertion that α=1/3 is optimal. Additionally, the optimal curve exhibits high oscillation, making the convergence rate estimation unreliable. Please clarify both the α discrepancy and the source of these fluctuations.

Fourth, the text states that the term 1/(Kγ) dominates for small step sizes (line 256), but this behavior is not evident in SGLD’s results. Could you explain why SGLD does not exhibit this effect?

Minor technical points:  
- Equation (1): The symbol θ̃ (or similar) is used instead of θ; clarify consistency.  
- Line 94: "RR" (Richardson-Romberg) should be defined at first use.  
- Line 131: Γ_K is undefined; specify its meaning.  
- Line 160: The smoothness class for function f (e.g., Lipschitz, twice differentiable) should be specified.  
- Line 249: Figure 1(b) plots "Bias vs. time," not "Bias vs. dimension" as stated; this likely refers to Figure 1(c).  
- Throughout: Use bold for vectors (e.g., **θ** instead of θ).  

The paper makes a valuable contribution with a theoretically sound extension to SG-MCMC. Addressing these points will strengthen the presentation and ensure the experimental and theoretical claims align precisely.
