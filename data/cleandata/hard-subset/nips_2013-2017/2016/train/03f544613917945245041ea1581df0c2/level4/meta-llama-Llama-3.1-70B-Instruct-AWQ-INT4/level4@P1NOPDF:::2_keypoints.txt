- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SGLD, yielding SGRRLD to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Claims SGRRLD has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show a clear advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD.  
- MovieLens (real-data) results show only slight RMSE improvement and are debatable.  
- Extends established RR extrapolation for SDEs to SG-MCMC and proves faster convergence rate for SGRRLD with same step size.  
- Improvement requires running two parallel Markov chains (computational trade-off).  
- Algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.  
- Reviewer did not verify proofs and asks about required conditions on U (e.g., can U be multimodal?).  
- Questions how combining two chains reduces the error order, especially if gradient noises decorrelate chains quickly.  
- Asks whether the authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Raises experimental/comparison concerns: SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains within same compute budget.  
- Recommends comparing SGRRLD to two parallel SGLD chains and to SGLD with step size γ/2 (lower bias).  
- Suggests additional real-data experiments to better evaluate SGRRLD’s practical benefit.
