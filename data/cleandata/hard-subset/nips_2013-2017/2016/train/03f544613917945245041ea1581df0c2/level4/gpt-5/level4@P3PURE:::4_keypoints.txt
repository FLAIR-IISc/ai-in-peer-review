- Proposal: Introduces a Richardson–Romberg (RR) augmentation for SG-MCMC to reduce bias and improve MSE with minimal implementation changes.
- Practicality: Method is easy to incorporate into existing SG-MCMC implementations.
- Theory: Paper provides non-asymptotic theoretical results supporting claimed improvements.
- Main concern — rates: Unclear how the specific convergence rates in the text follow from the stated theorems; need explicit derivation showing how terms are balanced and which assumptions are used.
- Empirical fairness — runtime: Matrix factorization experiment is unfairly compared — running two chains (one twice as long) makes the proposed method ~3× slower on a single core, so SGLD timings should be adjusted (e.g., multiply SGLD time by 2/3).
- Empirical fairness — Figure 5: After correcting for runtime, the performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD likely shrinks; current comparison is misleading.
- Figure 3 inconsistency: The figure shows best convergence at α = 0.2 (red), not α = 1/3 as claimed; also the “optimal” curve fluctuates unexpectedly and needs explanation.
- Step-size remark (line 256): Claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates for very small γ — explain why this does not similarly affect SGLD.
- Notation inconsistency: Equation (1) uses a different symbol than θ for parameters; maintain consistent parameter notation.
- Undefined acronyms/terms: RR is used before being defined (line 94); Γ_K is introduced without definition (line 131).
- Smoothness assumption: Specify the smoothness class for f (e.g., Lipschitz gradient, bounded Hessian) instead of just “smooth” (line 160).
- Figure/caption mismatch: References to Figure 1(b) plotting Bias vs. time seem incorrect — likely should be Figure 1(c) (line 249 and figure refs).
- Vector notation: Use boldface consistently for vector-valued quantities.
- Overall: Idea is appealing and theoretically promising, but requires clarification on rate derivations and correction of empirical comparison and notation/inconsistencies to strengthen the paper.
