- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC, focusing on SGLD.  
- Introduces SGRRLD: runs two SGLD chains with different step sizes and combines them via RRE.  
- Provides theoretical results: asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Shows SGRRLD converges faster than SGLD and attains accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators.  
- Empirical results: large improvements on synthetic examples and modest but consistent gains on large-scale datasets.  
- Strength: connects numerical SDE techniques (RRE) with SG-MCMC, benefiting both communities.  
- Strength: solid, well-motivated theoretical analysis and clear, well-organized writing.  
- Strength: algorithm is parallelizable and computationally convenient relative to some alternatives.  
- Weakness: method is a fairly straightforward application of RRE—novelty is moderate.  
- Weakness: implementation is more complex than standard SGLD, which may hinder adoption.  
- Weakness: practical performance gains may be scenario-dependent and not universally large.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is extensive and could be condensed for readability.  
- Minor comment: clarify Figure 1(a) as plotting a Gaussian with empirical mean/variance rather than the empirical posterior.  
- Minor comment: discuss why bias may not decrease with smaller step sizes in Figure 2(a).  
- Acceptance arguments: strong theory, empirical improvements, clear presentation, practical impact potential, novel cross-field link.  
- Rejection arguments: added implementation complexity, scenario-dependent benefits, and lengthy theory.
