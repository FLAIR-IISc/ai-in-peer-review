- Paper proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, with correlated Gaussian noise) and combining estimators to reduce bias.

- Authors prove bias reduction from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and show a similar improvement in mean squared error (MSE).

- Method is simple to implement, requires minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and is broadly applicable in principle.

- Applying Richardson-Romberg to SG-MCMC is novel though the extrapolation idea is classical; the paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Key theoretical assumption: the bias admits an expansion π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ^2); question whether this is the only required condition or if other implicit assumptions exist.

- Question about applicability to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampled Metropolis-Hastings) and variants like Distributed SGLD.

- Computational inefficiency: the larger-step chain is idle half the time; suggests running three simultaneous chains (one γ, two γ/2) and asks how that would affect estimator variance.

- Possibility of extending to higher-order bias correction using more than two step sizes (full Richardson-Romberg) is raised.

- Emphasis on correlated injected Gaussian noise between chains; unclear role and recommended practice regarding correlation of mini-batches between corresponding steps to minimize variance.

- Experimental concerns: matrix factorization experiments should be run until apparent convergence to fairly compare final performance.

- Experiments should include a standard non-Bayesian baseline (e.g., SGD) to demonstrate clear practical advantages of the Bayesian approach over point estimates.

- Suggestion to include clear pseudocode/algorithm box early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners who may not read technical derivations.
