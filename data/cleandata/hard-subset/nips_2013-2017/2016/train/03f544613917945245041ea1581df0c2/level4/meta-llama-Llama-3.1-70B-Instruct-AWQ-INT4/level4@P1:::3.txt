**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper proposes a novel technique to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods, specifically Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The technique, called Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, involves running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions. The authors demonstrate that this approach can significantly reduce the bias and mean squared error (MSE) of the estimates.

The paper is well-motivated, as SG-MCMC methods have become increasingly popular for Bayesian inference in large-scale applications. However, their performance is often limited by their bias. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the proposed solution.

The theoretical results, including the central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and MSE, are well-established and convincing. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the assumptions and conditions required for the results to hold.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of the results. The writing is clear, and the notation is well-defined.
