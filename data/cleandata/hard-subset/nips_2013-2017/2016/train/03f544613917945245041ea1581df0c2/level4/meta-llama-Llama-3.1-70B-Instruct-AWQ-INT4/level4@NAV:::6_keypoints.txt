1. Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes and correlated noise, proving asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence.  
2. Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate the proposed bias and MSE bounds; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical gains over SGLD.  
3. Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and makes a valid contribution to SG-MCMC methods.  
4. Related work is well-contextualized, but novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
5. Practical applicability is questionable because correlated Brownian motion requires per-step communication between chains, a cost the paper seems to ignore.  
6. Experimental comparisons may be unfair due to unclear allocation of computing resources between the two-chain SGRRLD and single-chain SGLD setups.  
7. It is unclear how partial resource use in SGRRLD could yield reduced wall-clock time versus full-resource SGLD; implementation and resource-splitting details are needed.  
8. Recommend comparing to two embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains (no communication) as an alternative baseline.  
9. Minor issue: repetition of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" on lines 25 and 70.
