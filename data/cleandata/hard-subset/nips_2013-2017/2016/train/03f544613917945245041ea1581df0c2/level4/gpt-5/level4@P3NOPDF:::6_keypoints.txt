- Proposes accelerating SG-MCMC convergence by applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation via two coupled chains with different step sizes and correlated noise, with asymptotic and non-asymptotic guarantees and experiments on synthetic and matrix-factorization tasks.
- Strength: Novel, conceptually appealing application of RR extrapolation for bias/variance reduction in SG-MCMC.
- Strength: Provides both asymptotic and finite-time theoretical results and empirical checks on toy problems.
- Strength: Empirical demonstration on a real matrix-factorization task shows potential practical gains.
- Strength: Clear exposition and good positioning relative to related work.
- Concern (novelty): Contribution may be incremental given prior analyses of integrators and RR for invariant measures; needs clearer statement of new technical challenges specific to stochastic gradients/minibatching.
- Concern (theory): Clarify whether and how the theory handles gradient noise and minibatching beyond prior work and whether new coupling/bias-cancellation arguments are required.
- Concern (practicality): Two coupled chains require synchronization/communication for correlated noise; communication cost appears omitted from wall-clock/throughput comparisons and should be quantified or discussed.
- Concern (fairness): Resource allocation between RR-SG-MCMC (two chains) and baseline SGLD is unclear; comparisons should be under fixed budgets (wall-clock, gradient evaluations, FLOPs, or hardware).
- Concern (implementation): Provide details on how minibatches, per-step costs, and resource splits are handled if hardware is shared, and explain why two chains would reduce wall-clock time versus dedicating resources to one chain.
- Suggested baseline: Compare to two independent SGLD chains run embarrassingly parallel and averaged to isolate RR benefit from parallelism.
- Ablation suggestions: Report sensitivity to step-size ratio and gradient-noise strength (minibatch size), robustness to imperfect/delayed noise correlation, and metrics like effective sample size per second and autocorrelation.
- Scope question: Discuss applicability or caveats of RR extrapolation for momentum-based SG-MCMC methods (e.g., SGHMC, SGNHT).
- Clarity: State assumptions in theory explicitly (smoothness, dissipativity, bounded gradient variance) and show which differ from prior works; add pseudocode/diagram for two-chain coupling.
- Minor: Remove duplicate ULA definition, fix typos/wording, and harmonize consistent method notation (RR-SGLD vs RR-SG-MCMC).
- Overall: Well-executed and clearly presented with sound theory and suggestive empirical gains, but novelty seems incremental and practical value is unclear without fair, resource-matched comparisons and stronger baselines.
