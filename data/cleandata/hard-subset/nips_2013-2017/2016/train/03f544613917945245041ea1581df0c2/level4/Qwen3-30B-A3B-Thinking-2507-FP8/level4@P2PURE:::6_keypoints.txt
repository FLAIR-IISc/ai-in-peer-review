- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC using two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Requires running two correlated chains (correlated Brownian motion) to implement the extrapolation.  
- Provides rigorous asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses, including bias and MSE bounds.  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussians validate the theoretical bias/MSE bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Paper is well-executed, technically solid, and well contextualized relative to prior work.  
- Novelty appears incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC ([10]) and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions ([23]).  
- Practical applicability concern: required per-step communication for correlated noise is unaddressed and authors assume zero communication cost.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear how computational resources were allocated between the two SGRRLD chains versus SGLD, making comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Performance concern: since stochastic gradient computation dominates runtime, splitting resources across two chains may not reduce wall-clock time; authors should explain allocation.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed implementation/setup and include a comparison to two embarrassingly parallel (uncorrelated) SGLD chains.  
- Minor comment: duplicate mention of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" at lines 25 and 70.
