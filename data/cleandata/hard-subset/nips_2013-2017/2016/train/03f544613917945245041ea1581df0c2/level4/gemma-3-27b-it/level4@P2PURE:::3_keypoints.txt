- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC: run two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second chain twice as many iterations) with correlated Gaussian noise to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Shows bias reduction from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and proves a corresponding reduction in MSE for estimates.  
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and minimally invasive to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Broad applicability across SG-MCMC algorithms suggests potential high impact within the community.  
- Novelty: Richardson-Romberg is established in numerical analysis but appears underexplored in ML/SG-MCMC.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Question: Does the technique generalize to all SG-MCMC methods, or are there additional requirements beyond the expansion condition in Section 2.2?  
- Question: Can the approach be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes not based on stochastic gradients (e.g., subsampling Metropolis-Hastings, distributed SGLD)?  
- Efficiency concern: one chain idles while the other runs twice as long—could using three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2) run in parallel be more resource-efficient, and would that increase variance?  
- Question: Have the authors considered extrapolation using more than two step sizes (higher-order Richardson extrapolation)?  
- Question: Impact of correlated injected Gaussian noise vs. correlation between mini-batches is unclear—does using the same mini-batch across the paired iterations minimize variance or is the effect negligible?  
- Experimental suggestion: In matrix factorization experiment, run until convergence and include comparison to standard SGD to better assess practical advantage of Bayesian approach.  
- Experimental suggestion: Provide quantitative results and an SGD control plot to contextualize final errors of SGRRLD and SGLD.  
- Presentation suggestion: Include an algorithm box / pseudo-code early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
