- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias.  
- Novel adaptation of RR extrapolation to stochastic gradient MCMC.  
- Algorithm is clearly presented and easy to implement.  
- Theoretical results claim asymptotically lower bias and MSE and faster convergence than SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiment shows clear bias reduction versus SGLD.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak and does not establish practical utility on real-world tasks.  
- Incomplete baselines: should compare to two independent SGLD chains run in parallel and averaged.  
- Missing comparison to SGLD with the smaller step size (γ/2) and parallelized equivalents to match computational cost.  
- Limited real-data validation: MovieLens matrix factorization shows only marginal RMSE gains; broader real-world benchmarks needed.  
- Request to clarify assumptions on the potential U for Theorems 1–2, including applicability to multi-modal potentials.  
- Need intuition and evidence on how RR extrapolation remains effective despite independent mini-batch gradient noise and whether chain-state correlations were measured.  
- Conclusion: theoretically interesting but practical significance unproven; would reconsider if stronger, fairer experiments across diverse tasks are provided.
