- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides comprehensive asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses.  
- Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate theoretical bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments on real data show improvements over standard SGLD.  
- Paper is well-structured and situates the method within existing SG-MCMC literature; contribution appears valid and meaningful.  
- Novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Practical applicability and fairness of experimental comparisons are unclear.  
- Authors neglect the communication cost of the two chains that must communicate every step and should discuss this overhead.  
- Experimental setup lacks clarity on resource allocation between SGRRLD and SGLD and how partial resource use affects wall-clock time.  
- Suggestion to compare against two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains to better assess practical benefits.  
- Minor issue: duplicate mention of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" on lines 25 and 70.
