- Paper proposes using Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2 with twice the steps) and coupling Gaussian noise to reduce asymptotic bias.
- Coupling scheme αk = (β2k−1 + β2k)/√2 and extrapolated estimator yield bias O(γ^2) versus O(γ) for single chains and provide corresponding MSE improvement.
- Method is simple to implement, requires minimal modification to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and appears broadly applicable across SG-MCMC variants.
- Novelty: RR extrapolation is classical but not widely used in ML or SG-MCMC, so the application is novel in this context.
- Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Question on applicability: Does the method apply to all SG-MCMC methods, and what additional conditions beyond a πγ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) expansion are required?
- Scope question: Can the RR idea transfer to other approximate MCMC methods (e.g., subsampling MH) or distributed SGLD?
- Parallelization/work-allocation concern: In synchronous settings the finer-step chain may cause idle time; could running one γ chain and two γ/2 chains in parallel change variance properties?
- Extension question: Is there a natural multi-level RR extension using more than two step sizes for SG-MCMC?
- Mini-batch correlation: Unclear whether correlating stochastic gradients (using same mini-batches across coupled iterations) meaningfully reduces variance beyond Gaussian-noise coupling.
- Experimental evaluation suggestion: For matrix factorization, run to convergence and compare against SGD to assess whether the Bayesian approach and acceleration outperform strong non-Bayesian baselines.
- Presentation suggestion: Add an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
