- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias while maintaining reasonable variance.  
- Novel extension of Talay & Tubaro RR extrapolation to the stochastic-gradient/SG-MCMC setting.  
- Theoretical results: asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) versus SGLD's O(γ) and O(γ²).  
- Shows MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), an improvement over SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides rigorous analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Clear algorithm description with precise implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Synthetic experiments (Gaussian models) demonstrate large bias reduction (~100×) consistent with theory.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) experiments show comparable or better RMSE than SGLD at roughly half the compute by running two chains in parallel.  
- Effective use of parallelism to implement the RR extrapolation with minimal overhead.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation and principled, elegant approach to bias reduction for SG-MCMC.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to SGLD with smaller step size (γ/2) in real-data experiments, which would be a fairer baseline.  
- Weakness: does not provide empirical evidence that the required correlation between the two parallel chains is maintained in practice.  
- Weakness: insufficiently summarized assumptions on the potential U (smoothness, growth, multimodality) in the main text.  
- Weakness: claim that the method is “virtually applicable to all SG-MCMC algorithms” is overstated and needs stronger justification.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical scenarios where the bias reduction provides meaningful real-world benefits.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed theoretical contribution that merits acceptance if experimental comparisons and practical clarifications are addressed.
