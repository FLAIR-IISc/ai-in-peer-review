- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes.  
- Main insight: RR extrapolation reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ²) while controlling variance via correlated Brownian increments.  
- Shows optimal variance control achieved with perfectly correlated Brownian increments (Equation 7).  
- Theoretically attains MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Demonstrates that a first-order integrator + RR extrapolation can match higher-order methods without their computational overhead.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Derives optimal step-size schedule with α = 1/5 for best convergence.  
- Empirical validation on an analytic Gaussian model shows significant bias reduction compared to SGLD.  
- Large-scale experiments (MovieLens matrix factorization) show comparable or better accuracy than SGLD in the same wall-clock time.  
- Method is naturally parallelizable since the two chains can be run independently, suited for distributed architectures.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical justification, thorough experiments, solid connection to prior work, practical implementation details, and well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparison to higher-order integrator methods despite theoretical claims of matching them.  
- Weakness: inconsistency in step-size notation between text and equations (η₂ₖ₋₁ = η₂ₖ = γₖ/2 vs Equation (5)).  
- Weakness: supplementary Algorithm 1 appears inconsistent with the formal algorithm description in Equation (5), risking implementation confusion.  
- Overall: solid, novel contribution with minor revision suggestions; recommended for acceptance pending fixes and added comparisons.
