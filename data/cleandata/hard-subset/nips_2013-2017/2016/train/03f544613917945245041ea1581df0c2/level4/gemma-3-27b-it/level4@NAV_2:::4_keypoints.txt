- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias and improve convergence over SGLD.  
- Implements SGRRLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes; requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC code.  
- Provides robust theoretical analysis with asymptotic and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K−4/5) compared to SGLD’s O(K−2/3).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization demonstrates bias reduction and practical applicability.  
- Discusses using correlated Brownian increments to control variance, building on prior work (Talay & Tubaro, Lemaire et al.).  
- Needs clearer derivation linking theorems to the stated convergence rates, particularly the choice of α.  
- Timing comparison may be unfair: SGRRLD incurs extra computation for dual chains and should be compared by computational cost, not just wall-clock time.  
- Figure 3 shows a discrepancy: plotted bias suggests optimal α≈0.2, contradicting the claimed α=1/3; requires investigation.  
- Fluctuations in the optimal curve in Figure 3 need further explanation.  
- The paper’s definition of “smoothness” for the test function f is imprecise and should be made precise.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid theory and experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantees, clear algorithmic presentation, supportive empirical results, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor theoretical ambiguities (α), insufficient consideration of computational cost, and unclear smoothness requirements.  
- Minor fixes: use consistent parameter notation (θ vs ϑ); define “RR” (line 94) and ΓK (line 131); clarify Figure 1(b) caption; use boldface for vectors.
