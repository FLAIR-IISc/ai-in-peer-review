This paper proposes applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data" MCMC methods, with a specific focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). A notable strength of this work is that it bridges the MCMC and numerical stochastic differential equation (SDE) literature, a connection that is likely to benefit both research communities. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE algorithm, support it with theoretical convergence results that delineate its advantages over standard SGLD, and demonstrate its performance through numerical experiments. The empirical results show impressive gains on smaller, illustrative examples and more modest, yet consistent, improvements on large-scale problems.

Overall, this is a strong and well-written paper that appears to be technically sound. The contribution is valuable, presenting interesting theoretical results and demonstrating good empirical performance for the novel algorithm. While the core method is a relatively straightforward, yet computationally convenient, application of RRE to SGLD, it is effective. The proposed techniques are likely to be adopted by practitioners, even if they do not represent a revolutionary shift in the field.

Minor Comments:
*   Figure 1a could be misinterpreted. The plot shows a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, not the empirical posterior distribution itself. The caption or text should clarify this to avoid confusion.
*   In Figure 2a, it is counter-intuitive that the bias does not monotonically decrease with the step size. The authors should add a brief discussion explaining this phenomenon.
