- Problem: SG-MCMC methods suffer asymptotic bias and fail to converge exactly to the posterior.  
- Proposal: Apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Method: Run two correlated SG-MCMC chains (chain2 uses half step size and twice the steps) and combine their estimates by subtraction.  
- Noise coupling: Inject Gaussian noise with specific correlation alphak = (beta{2k-1}+beta{2k})/sqrt(2) between chains.  
- Theoretical result: Bias reduced from O(gamma) to O(gamma^2) and analogous MSE reduction proven.  
- Strength: Method is simple, general, easy to implement, and minimally invasive to base SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novelty: Richardson–Romberg is classical in numerics but novel in the SG-MCMC/machine-learning context.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Question: Does the technique require conditions like pi_gamma(f)=pi(f)+C*gamma+O(gamma^2) to apply broadly?  
- Extension: Can the method be applied to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings, Distributed SGLD)?  
- Alternative design: Would using three chains (gamma, gamma/2, gamma/2) with equal steps be possible, and how would it affect variance?  
- Generalization: Is extension to more than two step sizes feasible?  
- Correlation concerns: The role of minibatch correlation is unclear—does using the same batch for corresponding iterations minimize variance?  
- Experiments: Matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include a comparison with SGD to better assess practical benefits.  
- Usability: Include pseudocode/algorithm box to make the method more accessible to non-expert readers.
