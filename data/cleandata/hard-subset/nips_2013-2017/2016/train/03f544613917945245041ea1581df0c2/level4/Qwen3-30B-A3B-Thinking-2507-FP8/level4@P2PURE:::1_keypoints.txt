- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD).  
- Connects MCMC and SDE literatures, offering cross-disciplinary benefits and insights.  
- Proposes a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm that is a straightforward, computationally convenient adaptation of RRE to SGLD.  
- Develops theoretical convergence results and conditions under which SGLD-RRE improves over vanilla SGLD.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements in large-scale experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presented.  
- Practical impact: likely to be adopted by practitioners but not expected to revolutionize current practice.  
- Minor critique: Figure 1a is potentially misleading—uses Gaussian approximation from empirical mean/variance rather than full empirical posterior and needs clarification.  
- Minor critique: Figure 2a needs brief discussion on why bias does not necessarily decrease with smaller stepsize, since this is counterintuitive.
