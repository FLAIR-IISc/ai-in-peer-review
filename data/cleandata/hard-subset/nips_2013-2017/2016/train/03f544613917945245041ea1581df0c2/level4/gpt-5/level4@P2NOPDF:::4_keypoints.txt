- Proposal: RR extrapolation for SG-MCMC that improves bias and MSE, simple to implement, with solid theoretical support.
- Main technical concern: mapping from the main theorem to the specific claimed convergence rates is unclear.
- Request: clarify which error terms are canceled by the RR construction.
- Request: explain how rates depend on step-size schedule and the assumed order of smoothness for the bias expansion.
- Request: explain how stochastic gradient noise and finite minibatches affect constants and rates.
- Request: state whether the presented rates are finite-time (depend on K and γ) or only asymptotic.
- Practical concern: matrix factorization experiments use ~3× more computation for the RR method, so wall-clock comparisons are unfair; provide runtime-normalized plots or per-iteration comparisons.
- Figure 3 concern: visual best curve (α=0.2) conflicts with claim that α=1/3 is optimal; explain this mismatch and the source of large oscillations.
- Line 256 question: why the 1/(Kγ) term allegedly dominating at small step sizes does not appear to dominate the SGLD baseline under the same regime; provide discussion or a comparison of term magnitudes.
- Minor comments: unify notation (use θ consistently), define “RR” at first use, define Γ_K before use, specify precise smoothness class for f required, fix Figure 1 caption (bias vs. time vs. dimension), and use boldface for vectors/consistent notation.
- Summary: method is neat and practical with promising theory; clarifying rate derivations and providing compute-normalized experiments would substantially strengthen the paper.
