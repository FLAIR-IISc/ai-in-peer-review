- Proposes SGRRLD: an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias relative to SGLD.  
- Leverages Talay & Tubaro (1990) ideas for accelerating SDE convergence and applies them to SG-MCMC.  
- Method requires running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining them via RR extrapolation.  
- Theoretical and empirical results claim lower asymptotic bias and mean squared error than SGLD.  
- Paper is clearly written and the algorithm is straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic experiments on a linear Gaussian model effectively illustrate bias reduction.  
- Properly contextualized within SG-MCMC and numerical SDE literature.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: a central limit theorem (Theorem 1) and non-asymptotic error bounds (Theorem 2).  
- Needs clarification of precise assumptions on the potential U for Theorems 1 and 2, particularly for multi-modal U.  
- Raises concern about RR effectiveness under gradient noise—uncorrelated gradient noise between chains could reduce benefits; chain correlation should be investigated.  
- Real-data MovieLens experiments show only modest RMSE improvements and the experimental setup has shortcomings.  
- Comparison to SGLD is not fully controlled—should include two parallel SGLD chains baseline and SGLD with step size γ/2.  
- Recommends more diverse real-world datasets and more rigorous statistical analysis to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically strong and novel with clear presentation; leans toward acceptance if authors address experimental and theoretical clarifications.
