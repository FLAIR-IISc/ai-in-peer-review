This paper applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) to mitigate bias from stochastic gradient noise. The proposed method (SGRRLD) is shown to have asymptotically lower bias and mean squared error (MSE) than SGLD. On a synthetic linear Gaussian model, SGRRLD clearly outperforms SGLD. On MovieLens, it yields a small improvement in test RMSE, though the practical significance is debatable.

RR extrapolation is known to accelerate Monte Carlo convergence for SDEs. This work adapts that idea to SG-MCMC and proves that, under certain assumptions, SGRRLD converges faster than SGLD with the same step size, at the cost of running two coupled Markov chains. The algorithmic description is clear and the method appears straightforward to implement. The synthetic experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction for that setup. It would be valuable to include more real-data studies to show that this simple modification reliably reduces bias in practical settings.

I have not verified the technical details of Theorems 1 and 2. I would appreciate clarification on the precise conditions on the potential U under which both results hold. In particular:
- Do the theorems allow multimodal U, or do they require global convexity/strong dissipativity?
- Intuitively, if the stochastic gradient noises are (approximately) uncorrelated, the two chains may decorrelate rapidly even if the Gaussian noises are correlated. In that case, how does the RR combination still achieve cancellation of the leading-order error? A more detailed intuition or a finite-time bound quantifying the required cross-chain correlation would help.
- In the real-data experiments, did the authors measure and report the empirical correlation between the two chains over time?

I have concerns about the experimental setup and fairness of comparisons:
- The paper argues that SGRRLD has similar wall-clock time to SGLD due to parallelization. However, the same hardware could also run multiple SGLD chains in parallel. A fair comparison should match wall-clock time or total compute and compare SGRRLD against an estimator obtained by averaging across two (or more) independent SGLD chains. Would the RMSE improvements persist under this control?
- In the real-data experiments, SGRRLD with step sizes γ and γ/2 is compared against SGLD with step size γ. For fairness, please also include SGLD with γ/2, which should have lower bias, and consider averaging across two parallel SGLD chains at γ/2 if parallel resources are assumed. This would help isolate the benefit of RR extrapolation from simple step-size tuning.
- Beyond MovieLens, additional real-data tasks would strengthen the empirical case. It would be helpful to report:
  - Sensitivity to step size schedules (constant vs. decreasing), mini-batch size, and gradient variance.
  - How performance changes with different strengths of cross-chain coupling/correlated noise.
  - Bias estimates for functionals of interest (not only RMSE) to highlight the claimed bias reduction.

Overall, the contribution is conceptually simple and potentially impactful: leveraging RR extrapolation within SG-MCMC is a natural and elegant idea, and the synthetic results are compelling. However, the real-data evidence is limited, and the experimental comparisons should better control for parallelism and step size. Clarifying the assumptions on U and providing intuition (or diagnostics) for the required cross-chain correlation would significantly strengthen the paper.
