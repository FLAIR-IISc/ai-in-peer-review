- Introduces SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC sampler using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Bridges MCMC and numerical SDE communities by adapting a standard numerical technique (Richardson-Romberg) to stochastic-gradient settings.  
- Main theoretical result: SGRRLD achieves O(γ²) asymptotic bias, O(γ⁴) variance, and a non-asymptotic MSE bound O(K⁻⁴/⁵), outperforming SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) rate.  
- Provides strong technical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and detailed non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds with full proofs in the supplement.  
- Correctly situates work relative to prior literature (e.g., Chen et al., 2015), showing similar accuracy to higher-order integrators without complex discretizations.  
- Original contribution: first adaptation of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC with correlated Brownian motion, enabling higher accuracy using first-order integrators.  
- Method is generically applicable to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD, an important theoretical plus.  
- Significant practical benefits: parallelizable, computationally efficient, and empirically faster (matrix factorization experiments show similar accuracy in ~half the wall-clock time).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets shows consistent improvements across problem sizes.  
- Minor clarity issues: Figure 1a should emphasize it shows a Gaussian approximation from empirical moments, not the empirical posterior; needs clearer caption.  
- Minor theoretical clarification requested: explain why bias may not decrease with smaller γ for fixed iterations (1/(Kγ) term dominates for small γ).  
- Caveat: practical impact may be limited to domains where bias reduction is critical, though the paper remains valuable for theory and applied Bayesian ML.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, convincing empirical and theoretical contributions; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
