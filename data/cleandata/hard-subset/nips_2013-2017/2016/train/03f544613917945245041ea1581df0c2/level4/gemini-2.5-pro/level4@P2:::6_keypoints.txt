- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motion.
- Core technical idea: use correlated noise between chains to control variance of the combined RR estimator.
- Theoretical contributions: provides a CLT and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing improved convergence rates (MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).
- Connects and builds on prior work: higher-order integrators for SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation for SDE invariant measures.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and explains the RR adaptation to SG-MCMC effectively.
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments verify bias/MSE improvements and a large-scale matrix factorization task shows practical utility and efficiency.
- Originality concern: main novelty is combining known techniques (RR extrapolation + SG-MCMC), which may be viewed as incremental.
- Practicality concern: correlated Brownian increments imply per-iteration communication/synchronization between chains, which could be costly in distributed settings.
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear resource allocation—SGRRLD may use two cores, so wall-clock comparisons with single-chain SGLD may be misleading.
- Missing baseline: should compare to running two independent SGLD chains in parallel and averaging to isolate benefits of RR and correlated noise.
- Requests for clarification: authors should detail communication overhead, implementation, and exact resource usage in experiments.
- Overall judgment: strong theoretical and empirical contribution and well-presented, recommended for acceptance subject to clarifying positioning and experimental setup.
- Minor comment: duplicated definition of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)"—remove one instance.
