The authors present stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), a new method designed to mitigate bias in SG-MCMC by leveraging Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for solving stochastic differential equations. Notably, SG-RR-MCMC utilizes two independently and parallelizable chains, requiring no inter-chain communication. A theoretical analysis demonstrates the asymptotic consistency of the proposed method, and the empirical results presented support its effectiveness. The approach represents an intuitive and successful combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). While the theoretical analysis suggests improved convergence, a comparison to higher-order integrators would strengthen this claim (and was absent from the experimental evaluation). A discrepancy exists in the discussion of step size selection. Additionally, there is an inconsistency between Algorithm 1 (in the supplementary material) and Equation (5) in the main text.
