- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, running two coupled SG-MCMC chains at step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining via Richardson–Romberg to reduce bias while controlling variance, with CLT and finite-time bounds.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and widely applicable—requires minimal code changes and acts as a generic wrapper for SG-MCMC methods.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical analysis—proves consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds and justifies optimal noise correlation (Σ = Id).  
- Strength: Practically relevant—works beyond SGLD (shown for SGHMC) and suits parallel/distributed setups.  
- Strength: Empirical support—synthetic experiments match theory and matrix factorization shows better test RMSE for comparable wall-clock time.  
- Weakness: Clarity on rate derivation—need explicit identification of dominating terms in Theorems 1 and 2 and how min/exponent combinations arise for general α.  
- Weakness: Smoothness/growth conditions—specify exact regularity assumptions on f and U and whether they differ between CLT and finite-time results.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 discrepancy—empirical fastest bias decay appears at α = 0.2, conflicting with stated theoretical optimal α = 1/3 for bias and α = 1/5 for MSE; requires reconciliation.  
- Weakness: Fluctuations in “optimal” curve—need explanation (finite-K variance, constants, mixing) and suggest more runs or confidence bands.  
- Weakness: Small-γ regime behavior—clarify why SGRRLD shows 1/(Kγ) upturn and whether SGLD exhibits similar upturn at different scales or if plotting masks it.  
- Weakness: Fairness of large-scale comparisons—SGRRLD uses three gradient computations per macro-iteration; report performance versus total gradient evaluations or CPU-hours to ensure equal-compute comparisons.  
- Weakness: Coupling of stochastic gradients—state whether minibatches are reused across chains or sampled independently and how this affects variance/constants and bias.  
- Specific comment: Notation consistency—switch from θ to ϑt is confusing; use consistent notation or explain distinction.  
- Specific comment: Define RR at first use (Line 94).  
- Specific comment: Define ΓK when first introduced and clarify Γ(n)K notation (Line 131).  
- Specific comment: Precisely specify smoothness of f/assumptions (Line 160/Theorem 1); reconcile with g ∈ C9 used in sketch.  
- Specific comment: Fix figure reference (Line 249 refers to wrong subplot).  
- Specific comment: Use boldface for vectors consistently.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel, simple extrapolation improvement with broad applicability and strong theoretical and synthetic empirical support.  
- Cons for acceptance: Ambiguity in rate derivation/choice of α and potentially unfair empirical advantage from parallelization in large-scale experiments.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and practically useful paper; recommend addressing rate clarifications and compute-fair experiments before final acceptance.
