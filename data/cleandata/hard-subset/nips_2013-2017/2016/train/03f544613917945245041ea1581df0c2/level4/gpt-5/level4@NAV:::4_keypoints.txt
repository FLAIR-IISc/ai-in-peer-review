- Proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme that improves SG-MCMC convergence rates (bias and MSE) with minimal implementation changes.  
- The method is elegant and supported by strong theoretical results.  
- Main theoretical concern: unclear how the specific convergence rates claimed are derived from the stated theorem (see “explain fatal flaws”).  
- Practical concern: matrix factorization experiment is unfair timing-wise—running two chains (one with twice the steps) makes the proposed method ~3× slower on a single core, so SGLD timings should be scaled (e.g., by 2/3), narrowing reported gaps.  
- Figure 3 (bias plot) discrepancy: the best empirical convergence appears at α=0.2, not α=1/3 as claimed; needs clarification.  
- The purported “optimal” curve fluctuates considerably, so the observed rate does not match the claimed α=0.2 — explanation required for the fluctuation/variance.  
- Line 256 claim: when stepsize is too small the 1/(Kγ) term dominates — why does this not apply to SGLD?  
- Minor notation/clarity issues: Eq.1 uses a different symbol than θ for parameters.  
- Terminology/definition omissions: Line 94 (RR) not defined; Line 131 (Γ_K) not defined.  
- Assumption clarity: Line 160 states “smooth” function f but does not specify exact smoothness conditions.
