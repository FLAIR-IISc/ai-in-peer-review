This paper proposes a novel method, Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to address and reduce the numerical integration bias inherent in standard Stochastic Gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The core idea is to apply Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a well-established technique for improving the accuracy of numerical solutions to Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs), within the SG-MCMC framework. The method involves running two parallel MCMC chains using different step sizes and then combining their outputs to produce a more accurate estimate. The authors provide a theoretical analysis to support their method, proving its asymptotic consistency, and present empirical results that demonstrate its practical effectiveness.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novelty and Simplicity:** The application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias in the SG-MCMC context is a novel and interesting contribution. The resulting algorithm is elegant in its simplicity, effectively re-purposing a classic numerical analysis tool for a modern machine learning problem.

2.  **Practicality and Efficiency:** A significant practical advantage of SG-RR-MCMC is its straightforward implementation and parallelizability. Since the two required chains run independently without any communication, the method is "embarrassingly parallel" and can be efficiently deployed on modern hardware.

3.  **Theoretical Support:** The paper is well-grounded in theory. The authors provide valuable justification for their approach by proving the asymptotic consistency of the SG-RR-MCMC estimator, which confirms that the method converges to the correct target distribution in the limit.

4.  **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are convincing and clearly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed method. The empirical evaluation shows a tangible reduction in bias when compared to baseline SG-MCMC approaches, validating the practical utility of the algorithm.

**Areas for Improvement**

1.  **Theoretical Analysis of Convergence Rate:** While the paper establishes asymptotic consistency, a more thorough theoretical analysis demonstrating an improved *convergence rate* would significantly strengthen the claims of efficiency. Proving that SG-RR-MCMC achieves a higher order of accuracy would provide a more quantitative understanding of *how much* more accurate the method is for a given computational budget compared to its baseline.

2.  **Comparison with Alternative Methods:** The contribution would be better contextualized by comparing it to other methods designed to reduce discretization error, such as those employing higher-order SDE integrators (e.g., Stochastic Runge-Kutta methods). While RR extrapolation is one way to achieve higher-order accuracy, it is not the only one. An empirical comparison against a relevant high-order integrator would help clarify the trade-offs and relative advantages of the SG-RR-MCMC approach in terms of accuracy, stability, and computational cost.

3.  **Clarity on Step Size:** There appear to be some inconsistencies in the discussion surrounding the step size selection and schedule. The authors should carefully review their description of the step sizes used for the two parallel chains and ensure the text is unambiguous and consistent throughout the manuscript.

4.  **Inconsistency between Algorithm and Equation:** A specific point of concern is the apparent discrepancy between the procedure described in Equation (5) and its implementation in Algorithm 1 (located in the supplementary material). These two descriptions of the method should be carefully checked and reconciled to ensure they are identical and to avoid confusion for the reader.

In summary, this paper presents an interesting and effective method for reducing bias in SG-MCMC methods. The approach is simple, practical, and supported by both theoretical and empirical evidence. Addressing the points above, particularly by strengthening the theoretical analysis of the convergence rate and providing a more comprehensive experimental comparison, would further enhance the impact and clarity of this work.
