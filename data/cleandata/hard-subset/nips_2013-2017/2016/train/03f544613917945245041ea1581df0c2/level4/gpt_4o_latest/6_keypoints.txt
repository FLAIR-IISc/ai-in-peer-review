- Paper proposes applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (higher-order integrator) to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.
- Authors provide both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses with synthetic Gaussian experiments validating bias and MSE bounds.
- Real-data experiments on large-scale matrix factorization show practical improvements over standard SGLD.
- Overall assessment: well-written, solid technique, clear presentation, and meaningful contribution within SG-MCMC literature.
- Primary concern 1: novelty may be limited since effects of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC were studied in [10], and Richardson-Romberg was applied to invariant distributions in [23].
- Primary concern 2: practical applicability and experimental fairness are unclear because correlated Brownian motion requires per-step communication between chains, and communication cost is ignored.
- Experimental setup lacks details on resource allocation: unclear whether single SGLD used same resources as two SGRRLD chains or how resources are split, which affects wall-clock time comparisons.
- Questioning how splitting resources between two SGRRLD chains reduces wall-clock time given stochastic gradient cost dominates.
- Suggested additional experiment: compare proposed method to two independent SGLD chains run without communication.
- Minor comment: the term "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is repeated (lines 25 and 70).
