- Contribution: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to improve bias and MSE convergence.  
- Strength: Method is easy to implement with minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Strength: Paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Major theoretical concern: Doubts about the derivation of the specific convergence rates presented in the main theorem (see reviewer’s “fatal flaws”).  
- Experimental fairness (Fig. 5): SGRRLD runs two chains (one with twice the steps), implying up to 3× single-core cost—SGLD runtimes should be scaled by 2/3 for a fair comparison.  
- Figure 3 (bias): Plot suggests optimal α ≈ 0.2 rather than stated α = 1/3, and the optimal-α curve shows unexpected large fluctuations that need explanation.  
- SGLD behavior: Claim that 1/(Kγ) dominates error for sufficiently small stepsize (line 256) is not observed in SGLD results—requires clarification.  
- Notation issue: Equation 1 uses a symbol different from θ, which is potentially confusing—standardize parameter notation.  
- Terminology: “RR” is used at line 94 without prior definition—define abbreviation on first use.  
- Undefined symbol: Γ_K appears at line 131 without definition—provide its definition.  
- Smoothness assumption: When referring to a “smooth function f” (line 160), specify the type of smoothness (e.g., Lipschitz continuous gradient).  
- Figure caption error: Line 249 references Figure 1(b) but the caption/plot appears to correspond to Figure 1(c) (Bias vs. dimension vs. time mismatch).  
- Formatting consistency: Use bold letters consistently for vectors throughout the paper.
