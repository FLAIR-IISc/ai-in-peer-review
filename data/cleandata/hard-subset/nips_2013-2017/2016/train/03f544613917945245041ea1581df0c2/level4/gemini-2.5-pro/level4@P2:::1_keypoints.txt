- Core idea: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading-order bias.
- Theoretical results: Proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds for SGRRLD.
- Improved rates: Shows SGRRLD attains MSE convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Empirical validation: Experiments on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization confirm theory and show practical speedups (similar accuracy in ~half the wall-clock time).
- Originality/significance: Novel and impactful bridge between numerical SDE techniques (RRE) and scalable SG-MCMC methods; simple and parallel-friendly to implement.
- Technical quality: Rigorous, thorough proofs that clearly establish conditions where SGRRLD outperforms SGLD.
- Clarity: Well-written, well-organized exposition with a clear connection to prior work.
- Practical utility: Method delivers modest but consistent performance improvements on a real-world recommendation task.
- Minor suggestion: Clarify Figure 1(a) caption to state the plot shows a Gaussian parameterized by empirical mean/variance (not a KDE of posterior).
- Minor suggestion: Add a brief explanation that very small γ can increase bias/MSE for fixed K due to the 1/(Kγ) term, to mitigate potential confusion.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — solid, significant contribution likely to be adopted by practitioners.
