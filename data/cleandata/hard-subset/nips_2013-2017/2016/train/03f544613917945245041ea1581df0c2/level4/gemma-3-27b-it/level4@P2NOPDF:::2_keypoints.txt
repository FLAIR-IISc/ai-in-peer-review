- Proposes SGRRLD: uses Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias in SGLD.  
- Theoretically claims SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiment strongly demonstrates bias reduction in a controlled setting.  
- MovieLens experiments show only marginal RMSE improvement, with questionable statistical significance.  
- Extends known RR extrapolation ideas from SDEs to SG-MCMC, proving faster convergence under specified conditions.  
- Speedup requires maintaining two parallel Markov chains, increasing implementation complexity.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and appears readily implementable.  
- More comprehensive real-world experiments are needed to substantiate practical bias-reduction claims.  
- Reviewer questions the theoretical constraints on matrix U and whether U can be multi-modal.  
- Needs clarification on chain decorrelation: if gradient noise is uncorrelated, how the two chains remain correlated enough for error-order reduction.  
- Authors should report observed correlations between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Experimental comparison is unfair: SGLD can be parallelized, so SGRRLD should be compared to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Recommend comparing against SGLD with reduced step size (γ/2) and two parallel chains, and expanding the dataset suite.
