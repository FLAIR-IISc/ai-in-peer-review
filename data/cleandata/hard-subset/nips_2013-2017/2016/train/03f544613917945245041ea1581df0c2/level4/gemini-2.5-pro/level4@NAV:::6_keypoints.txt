- Proposes accelerating SG-MCMC convergence using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two coupled SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides theoretical analysis covering both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence (bias and MSE) claims.  
- Validates theory on a synthetic Gaussian model and demonstrates practical gains on a large-scale matrix factorization task versus standard SGLD.  
- Paper is technically solid, well-presented, and situates the contribution clearly within SGMCMC literature.  
- Major concern: novelty may be incremental since numerical integrators for SGMCMC and Richardson-Romberg for invariant measures have been previously explored (refs [10], [23]).  
- Major concern: practical utility limited by required inter-chain communication for correlated Brownian motion, with communication overhead not accounted for in analysis.  
- Experimental details unclear: total computational budget and resource allocation between the two SGRRLD chains vs a single SGLD chain are not specified.  
- Questionable wall-clock advantage: splitting gradient computation across two chains may not beat dedicating resources to a single SGLD chain given gradients are the bottleneck.  
- Missing comparison: should benchmark against two independent SGLD chains running embarrassingly parallel (no communication cost).  
- Minor: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" acronym is defined twice (lines 25 and 70).
