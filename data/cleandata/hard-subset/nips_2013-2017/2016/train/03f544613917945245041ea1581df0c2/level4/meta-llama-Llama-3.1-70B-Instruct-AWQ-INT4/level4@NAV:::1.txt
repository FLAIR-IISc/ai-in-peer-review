This paper explores the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). Notably, RRE, a standard numerical integration tool with recent applications to numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs), is leveraged to establish a connection between the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially benefiting both fields. The authors propose a novel SGLD-RRE method, derive theoretical convergence results, and demonstrate its efficacy through numerical experiments. The results show significant improvements in toy examples and consistent, albeit modest, enhancements in large-scale examples. Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution, offering an interesting intersection of two distinct fields, compelling theoretical insights, and satisfactory empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The paper's clarity and technical soundness make it accessible and convincing. Although the application of RRE to SGLD is relatively straightforward and computationally convenient, it is likely to be adopted by practitioners, even if it does not fundamentally transform current practices. Some minor suggestions for improvement include clarifying the representation in Fig 1a, as it depicts a Gaussian approximation of the empirical posterior distribution rather than the actual estimated posterior. Additionally, a brief discussion in Fig 2a could help explain why the bias does not necessarily decrease with smaller step sizes, addressing a potentially counter-intuitive observation.
