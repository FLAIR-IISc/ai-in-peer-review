- Proposes SGRRLD: use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two correlated SG-MCMC chains with different stepsizes to reduce bias.
- Provides formal theoretical analysis showing asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.
- Demonstrates higher convergence rates than existing SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators.
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show reduced bias and mean squared error versus baselines.
- Novel and impactful idea that is simple to understand and implement.
- Well-written paper with clear presentation.
- Limited analysis of how mini-batch correlation affects the method’s performance.
- No comparisons to non-stochastic-gradient MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings).
- Method not extended or analyzed for more than two step sizes.
- Missing algorithm box or pseudo-code for easier implementation.
- Suggested to analyze mini-batch correlation effects, compare to non-SG MCMC, explore multi-step-size extension, include pseudo-code, and run matrix factorization experiment to convergence against SGD.
- Rating: 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, solid theory, empirical effectiveness, and clarity.
- Against-acceptance arguments: limited analysis on mini-batch correlation, lack of non-SG MCMC comparisons, and no multi-step-size extension.
