- Overall: Solid, well-written contribution introducing SGLD-RRE with theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance.  
- Novelty/Insight: Makes an insightful connection between MCMC and numerical SDEs, enabling RRE variance reduction for SGLD.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provides convergence analysis and conditions under which SGLD-RRE improves over standard SGLD.  
- Empirical performance: Strong improvements on toy problems and modest but consistent gains on large-scale tasks.  
- Clarity/Presentation: Exceptionally clear, well-organized, technically sound, and easy to read.  
- Weakness: Applying established RRE to SGLD is an incremental, practical improvement rather than a fundamentally new MCMC paradigm.  
- Minor comment 1: Clarify Figure 1a caption—it's a Gaussian plotted using empirical mean/variance, not an empirical posterior estimate.  
- Minor comment 2: Add discussion in Figure 2a about why bias may not decrease with step size (e.g., interaction of discretization error and stochastic gradient noise).
