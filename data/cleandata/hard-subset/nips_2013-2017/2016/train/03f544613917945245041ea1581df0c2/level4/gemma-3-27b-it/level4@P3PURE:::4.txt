This paper presents an improvement to Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) through the application of a Richardson-Romberg scheme, demonstrably improving convergence rates in terms of both bias and mean squared error. A key strength of the proposed method is its ease of implementation, requiring minimal modification to existing SG-MCMC algorithms. The paper is further supported by strong theoretical results. Specifically, the authors propose a Romberg-Richardson scheme to enhance the convergence properties of standard SG-MCMC, and the approach appears promising with a solid theoretical foundation. 

My primary concern relates to the derivation of the specific convergence rates presented in the theorem (as detailed in the “explain fatal flaws” section of my notes). Beyond this, I have a practical concern regarding the interpretation of the matrix factorization experiment results. Running the proposed method on a single-core computer requires approximately three times the computation of standard SG-MCMC due to the use of two chains, with one chain requiring twice the number of steps. Consequently, the comparisons in Figure 5 may be misleading; a fair comparison would necessitate multiplying the execution time of SGLD by a factor of 2/3, which would likely reduce the observed performance difference between SGLD and SGRRLD.

Regarding Figure 3, the bias plot indicates that the best convergence is achieved with α=0.2, rather than the α=1/3 stated in the text. Could this discrepancy be clarified? Furthermore, the optimal convergence curve appears significantly fluctuating, raising questions about the validity of the reported convergence rate for α=0.2. An explanation for this behavior would be beneficial. Finally, the authors state in line 256 that when the stepsize is sufficiently small, the term 1/Kγ will dominate. I am curious as to why this behavior is not observed with SGLD.

Several minor points require attention: In Equation 1, the notation differs from the established use of θ. Line 94 introduces “RR” without prior definition.  Line 131 mentions Γ<sub>K</sub> without defining it. Line 160 refers to a “smooth function f” without specifying the type of smoothness assumed.  On line 249, Figure 1(b) plots Bias versus time; this appears to be a mistake and should reference Figure 1(c). Lastly, for clarity, please use bold letters to consistently represent vectors.
