- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to reduce discretization bias.  
- Uses correlated noise between the two chains to control estimator variance, making RR extrapolation viable in stochastic-gradient settings.  
- Builds on SG-MCMC and SDE numerical-analysis literature to achieve higher-order accuracy using only first-order updates.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, a Central Limit Theorem, and derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows MSE improvement: SGRRLD achieves O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs. SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³), indicating faster convergence.  
- Originality: novel combination of RR extrapolation with SGLD and analysis of correlated-noise variance control.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments confirm theoretical bias/MSE and dimensionality behavior.  
- Empirical validation: experiments verify the optimal convergence rates claimed by theory.  
- Empirical validation: SGHMC experiments and comparison to SGHMC-s show SGRRLD can match higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Empirical validation: large-scale matrix factorization on real data demonstrates practical utility, computational efficiency, and parallel suitability.  
- Weakness: minor inconsistency/typo in Algorithm (Equation (5)) regarding gradient evaluation indices for Chain 2; pseudocode should be clarified and aligned.  
- Suggestion: include a direct comparison of SGRRLD to an SGLD variant using a higher-order integrator to strengthen SGLD-focused claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant practical problem, is novel and theoretically strong, parallelizable, and well-supported by empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: core components (SGLD, RR extrapolation) are not new; presentation has minor mathematical/algorithmic clarity issues.
