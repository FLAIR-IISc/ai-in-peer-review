To mitigate the bias of SG-MCMC, the authors introduce a new approach termed stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). The method leverages Richardson–Romberg extrapolation, a standard numerical acceleration technique for SDE discretizations. Concretely, it runs two chains with different step sizes in an embarrassingly parallel fashion without inter-chain communication. The paper includes theoretical analysis establishing that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. The empirical evidence is also satisfactory. The idea is appealing and, according to the experiments, effective. It can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with SGLD. Its efficiency is supported theoretically via improved convergence rates, although such rates can also be achieved by higher-order integrators (unfortunately, the experiments do not include a comparison to those integrators).
