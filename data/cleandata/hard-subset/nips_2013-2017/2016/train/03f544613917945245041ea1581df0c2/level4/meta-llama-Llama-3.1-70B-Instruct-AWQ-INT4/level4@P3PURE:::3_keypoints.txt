- Paper addresses asymptotic bias in SG-MCMC by applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.
- Method runs two correlated SG-MCMC chains (one with step size γ, one with γ/2 run twice as long) and subtracts estimates to cancel the O(γ) bias.
- The extrapolated estimator reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and yields a similar reduction in MSE.
- Method is simple to implement and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Technique appears broadly applicable to a large class of SG-MCMC algorithms, potentially impactful for the community.
- Novelty: Richardson-Romberg is known in numerical analysis but underused in ML and SG-MCMC contexts.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Question: Clarify exact conditions under which the technique applies beyond the π_γ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2) assumption.
- Question: Can the method be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis-Hastings) and to Distributed SGLD?
- Concern: Running one chain twice as long wastes computation; suggestion to use three chains (one γ, two γ/2 run equal steps) and ask whether this increases variance.
- Question: Is there an extension to more than two step sizes (higher-order extrapolation)?
- Question: Effect of correlated injected noise and minibatch correlation on variance—are identical minibatches for corresponding iterations optimal or is effect negligible?
- Experimental critique: Matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to demonstrate Bayesian advantage and contextualize SGRRLD/SGLD errors.
- Usability suggestion: Add an algorithm box or early pseudocode to help practitioners try the method without delving into theory.
