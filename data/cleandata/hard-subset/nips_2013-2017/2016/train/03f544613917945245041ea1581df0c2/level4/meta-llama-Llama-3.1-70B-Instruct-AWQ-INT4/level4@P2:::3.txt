The paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The approach is based on a numerical sequence acceleration method, namely the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, which requires running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error can be bounded.

The main idea of the paper is to apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC algorithms, which is a well-known method in numerical analysis but not widely known in the machine learning/stochastic gradient MCMC community. The authors show that this technique can be applied to a large class of SG-MCMC algorithms, making it potentially very impactful to the Stochastic Gradient MCMC community.
