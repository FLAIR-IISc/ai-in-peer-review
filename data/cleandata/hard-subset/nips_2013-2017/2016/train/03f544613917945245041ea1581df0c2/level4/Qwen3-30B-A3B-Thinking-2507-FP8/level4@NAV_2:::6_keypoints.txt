- Proposes SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation on SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions.  
- Shows asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) for the estimator.  
- Proves MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian models and real-world matrix factorization shows consistent accuracy and computational gains.  
- Technically sound with comprehensive theoretical analysis building on prior SG-MCMC and SDE extrapolation work.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with precise mathematics and effective experiments.  
- Significant practical impact: achieves higher-order accuracy using first-order integrators and is amenable to parallel/distributed setups.  
- Novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC despite prior SDE uses.  
- Main practical concern: correlated Brownian motions require per-step communication between chains, but communication costs are not analyzed.  
- Unclear resource-allocation and fairness of comparisons: experiments do not clarify whether two-chain SGRRLD used same resources as single-chain SGLD or separate resources.  
- Missing comparison to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains to evaluate practical trade-offs.  
- Minor edit: remove repeated "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" mention.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and empirical contribution, but practical implementation and resource-cost analyses need addressing.
