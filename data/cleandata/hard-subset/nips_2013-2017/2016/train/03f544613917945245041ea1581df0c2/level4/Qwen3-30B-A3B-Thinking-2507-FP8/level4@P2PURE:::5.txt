

The authors propose stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC) to reduce bias in SG-MCMC. This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a standard technique for stochastic differential equations (SDEs), by running two chains in an embarrassingly parallel manner without communication. Theoretical analysis demonstrates asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC, and empirical results are satisfactory, confirming the method's effectiveness. The approach appears as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. While the theoretical efficiency gain—evidenced by an improved convergence rate—could be achieved using high-order integrators (a comparison with which the authors omit in experiments), the method's core contribution remains valid. However, an inconsistent statement regarding the step size is present. Additionally, Algorithm 1 (supplement) is inconsistent with Equation (5).
