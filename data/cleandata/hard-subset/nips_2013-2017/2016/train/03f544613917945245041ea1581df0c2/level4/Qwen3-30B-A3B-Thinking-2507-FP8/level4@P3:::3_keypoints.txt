- Paper introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ²).

- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.

- Shows key theoretical improvement: SGRRLD achieves MSE convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).

- Method requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC code and applies broadly under mild technical conditions.

- Parallel two-chain design aligns well with distributed computing, making the method practical at scale.

- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and MovieLens matrix factorization corroborate theory and show SGRRLD matches SGLD accuracy in about half the time.

- Strength: Combines numerical-analysis RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC to produce a simple, theoretically sound, and practically effective algorithm.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to non-Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., SGD) in matrix factorization, weakening practical significance claims.

- Weakness: Paper omits clear pseudocode, reducing accessibility for practitioners.

- Weakness: Does not fully detail practical implementation of maintaining correlated Brownian increments across chains in large-scale settings.

- Arguments for acceptance: significant convergence improvement, rigorous theory, strong experiments, broad applicability, and suitability for distributed systems.

- Arguments for rejection: limited comparison to optimization baselines and absence of pseudocode harming practitioner uptake.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-written contribution that meaningfully advances scalable Bayesian inference; recommendation to accept.
