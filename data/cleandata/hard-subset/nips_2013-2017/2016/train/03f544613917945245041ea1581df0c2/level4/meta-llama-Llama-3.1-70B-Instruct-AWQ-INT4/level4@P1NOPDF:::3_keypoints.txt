- Paper proposes Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce asymptotic bias in SG-MCMC methods.
- Method: run two correlated SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 (chain with γ/2 runs twice as many steps) and combine their estimates.
- Injected Gaussian noise is correlated (αk = (β2k-1 + β2k)/√2); estimator subtracts Chain 1 from Chain 2.
- The combined estimator reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2).
- Authors also prove a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE).
- Method is simple to implement and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Technique appears broadly generalizable across a wide class of SG-MCMC methods.
- Novel contribution for the ML/SG-MCMC community despite Richardson–Romberg being classical in numerical analysis.
- Paper is well-written and the presentation is clear.
- Question: Is the method directly applicable to all SG-MCMC methods or are additional conditions beyond the bias expansion in Section 2.2 required?
- Question: Can the technique be applied to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) or Distributed SGLD?
- Concern: running one chain twice as long wastes computation; propose using three chains (one γ, two γ/2) run equally—how does that affect variance?
- Question: Is there an extension to more than two step sizes?
- Question: How important is correlation between mini-batches; is variance minimized by reusing the same batch across the specified iterations?
- Experimental critique: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence, include SGD baseline, and show scenarios where Bayesian methods outperform SGD.
- Suggestion: include an algorithm/pseudocode box early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
