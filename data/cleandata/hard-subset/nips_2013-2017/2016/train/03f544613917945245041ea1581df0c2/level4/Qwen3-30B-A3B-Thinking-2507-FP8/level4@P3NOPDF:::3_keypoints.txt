- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC: run two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, with α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2) to cancel O(γ) bias down to O(γ^2).

- Method requires no changes to base SG-MCMC, is broadly applicable (e.g., SGLD, SGHMC), easy to implement, and has minimal computational overhead.

- Provides rigorous theoretical justification for bias and MSE reduction, with clear exposition and empirical validation across benchmarks.

- Question on theoretical assumption: paper assumes π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2); clarify whether this is sufficient/necessary and whether preconditioning or adaptive step-size variants violate it.

- Scope limitation: clarify applicability to non-SG-MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) and distributed settings—can extrapolation be applied across nodes?

- Computational-efficiency concern: running the fine chain for twice as many steps may be inefficient; propose/assess alternatives (e.g., three-chain scheme) and analyze cost vs variance trade-offs.

- Higher-order extrapolation: ask whether using more step sizes (γ, γ/2, γ/4, ...) can yield higher-order bias reduction and what the computational/variance implications would be.

- Noise correlation/detail: clarify whether identical mini-batches are required for correlated noise and how mini-batch correlation affects estimator variance and implementation.

- Empirical validation critique: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence (not just 10k steps) and include SGD as a baseline to demonstrate practical Bayesian advantage.

- Accessibility: include a concise algorithm box/pseudocode in the main text (showing noise correlation) to aid practitioner implementation and reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: elegant, low-overhead, and impactful contribution with sound theory and promising results; recommended for publication after addressing clarifications.
