This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The approach is based on the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method that involves running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes.

The paper relates the proposed algorithm to previous work on SG-MCMC methods, including SGLD and its extensions. The authors provide a clear description of the algorithm and its underlying theory, including a central limit theorem and bounds for the non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD in terms of bias reduction and root mean squared error.

The paper is well-written, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement. The synthetic experiments show a clear advantage of SGRRLD in reducing the bias. However, the real data experiments could be improved by comparing SGRRLD with SGLD with the same step size and multiple parallel chains. Additionally, it would be interesting to see more real data experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

The main strengths of the paper are:

* The proposed algorithm is novel and has the potential to improve the performance of SG-MCMC methods.
* The paper provides a clear description of the algorithm and its underlying theory.
* The experiments demonstrate the advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD.

The main weaknesses of the paper are:

* The real data experiments are limited and could be improved by comparing SGRRLD with SGLD with the same step size and multiple parallel chains.
* The paper could benefit from more real data experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
