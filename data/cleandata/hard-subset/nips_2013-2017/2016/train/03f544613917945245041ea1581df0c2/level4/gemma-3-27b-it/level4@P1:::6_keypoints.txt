- Proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two correlated SG-MCMC chains (different step sizes) to accelerate convergence.  
- Provides both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses for the proposed method.  
- Validated empirically on synthetic data and a large-scale matrix factorization task, showing effectiveness.  
- Well-written paper that situates the method within existing literature and demonstrates technical soundness.  
- Novelty may be incremental since higher-order integration and RR extrapolation ideas have prior work (e.g., [10], [23]).  
- Practical concern: correlated Brownian motion requires communication between chains, but communication costs for parallel implementations are not discussed.  
- Experimental setup unclear: resource allocation between SGRRLD’s two chains and SGLD is not specified, raising fairness concerns.  
- Need clarification why splitting gradient computation across two chains would reduce wall-clock time versus one fully resourced SGLD chain.  
- Suggestion to compare SGRRLD to two independent, embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains as a baseline.  
- Minor comment: redundant mentions of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" on lines 25 and 70.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, rigorous theoretical guarantees, strong experimental results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, unaddressed practical communication costs, and insufficient experimental detail on resource comparisons.
