- SG-MCMC methods suffer asymptotic bias; the paper shows Richardson-Romberg extrapolation reduces this bias.  
- Method: run two parallel chains (step sizes γ and γ/2; second chain runs twice as many steps) with correlated Gaussian noise α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2 and subtract estimates to achieve bias O(γ²).  
- The authors prove a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE).  
- Approach is simple, requires minimal changes to base SG-MCMC, and is broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC methods.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is new for the machine learning community.  
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Open question: Is the technique universally applicable to all SG-MCMC methods or are additional conditions beyond π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²) required?  
- Open question: Can the method be extended to non-stochastic-gradient approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., sub-sampling Metropolis–Hastings, Distributed SGLD)?  
- Efficiency concern: running Chain 2 for twice as many steps wastes computation while Chain 1 idles.  
- Proposal/question: Would using three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2 run the same number of steps) improve efficiency, and how would that affect estimator variance?  
- Open question: Is there a natural extension to more than two step sizes (higher-order extrapolation)?  
- Clarification needed on noise correlation: does variance minimize when the same mini-batch is used for the correlated noise steps, or is that correlation effect negligible?  
- Experimental critique: matrix factorization experiment should be run to convergence and compared against SGD to contextualize SGRRLD/SGLD performance and demonstrate practical advantage.  
- Practical suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
