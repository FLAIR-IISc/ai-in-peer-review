- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and correlated Brownian motions.  
- Claims asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) for the estimator, yielding reduced bias while controlling variance.  
- Shows MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD vs O(K⁻²/³) for SGLD and other first-order SG-MCMC, matching higher-order integrators without extra integrator complexity.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with clear theorems, proofs, and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic Gaussian models and MovieLens matrix factorization tasks, with experiments aligning with theory.  
- Builds on prior work: SGLD (Welling & Teh), Talay & Tubaro RR extrapolation for SDEs, and convergence analyses of SG-MCMC; connects closely to RR extrapolation for invariant distributions.  
- Novelty is incremental: extends existing RR and SG-MCMC analyses and needs clearer articulation of unique contributions beyond prior work.  
- Practical implementation concerns: assumes zero communication cost between parallel chains, which is unrealistic for distributed settings.  
- Unclear resource accounting: paper does not specify whether SGRRLD uses equivalent total resources to SGLD (e.g., split vs full resources per chain).  
- Lacks experimental baseline of two independent parallel SGLD chains (without correlation) to assess practical trade-offs and communication cost impact.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, technically sound paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; recommendation: accept.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify differences from prior RR/SG-MCMC work and provide implementation details on resource allocation and communication/correlation costs in experiments.
