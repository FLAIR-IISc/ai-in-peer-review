This paper proposes a Richardson-Romberg extrapolation scheme to improve the convergence of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The method aims to achieve better convergence rates in terms of bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). The proposed scheme is straightforward to implement, requiring only minimal modifications to standard SG-MCMC frameworks, and is supported by solid theoretical results.

While the proposed method appears promising and the theory seems well-grounded, I have several significant concerns regarding the derivation of the convergence rates and the experimental evaluation that should be addressed.

**Major Concerns and Questions:**

1.  My primary concern is the derivation of the specific convergence rates from the main theorems. The paper would be strengthened if the authors could provide a more detailed and explicit explanation of how the final rates are obtained from their theoretical results.

2.  There is a practical concern regarding the fairness of the experimental comparison in the matrix factorization experiment. The proposed method runs two chains, one of which uses twice the number of steps. On a single-core processor, this would take approximately three times longer to run than standard SG-MCMC. The plots in Figure 5 do not appear to account for this computational overhead. For a fair comparison, the time for SGLD should be scaled by a factor of 2/3. Making this adjustment would likely reduce the observed performance difference between SGLD and SGRRLD.

3.  In Figure 3 (the bias plot), the best empirical convergence is achieved by the red curve, which corresponds to α=0.2. However, the text states that the optimal rate is achieved with α=1/3. Could the authors please clarify this discrepancy?

4.  Related to the previous point, the curve corresponding to the best empirical performance in Figure 3 (α=0.2) fluctuates considerably. It is unclear if this noisy behavior aligns with the claimed theoretical convergence rate. Could the authors provide a reason for these fluctuations?

5.  On line 256, it is stated that when the stepsize is too small, the error is dominated by the `1/(Kγ)` term. Why is this same behavior not observed for the standard SGLD algorithm? An explanation of this difference would be helpful.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Notation:** For clarity and readability, please consider using bold letters to represent vectors throughout the manuscript.
*   **Eq. 1:** Why is a different symbol used for the parameter instead of `θ`, which is used elsewhere?
*   **Line 94:** The acronym "RR" is used without being defined.
*   **Line 131:** The term `Γ_K` is not defined before its use.
*   **Line 160:** The paper assumes a "smooth function f". Please specify the precise smoothness conditions required (e.g., Lipschitz continuous, continuously differentiable, etc.).
*   **Line 249:** The text refers to Figure 1(b) as plotting "Bias vs. time," but the plot appears to be "Bias vs. dimension." Should the reference be to Figure 1(c) instead?
