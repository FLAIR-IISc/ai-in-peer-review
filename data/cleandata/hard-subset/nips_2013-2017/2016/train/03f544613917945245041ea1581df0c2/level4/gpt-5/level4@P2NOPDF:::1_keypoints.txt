- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to modern big-data MCMC methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD).  
- Explicitly connects the MCMC and SDE literatures, which is valuable to both communities.  
- Introduces the SGLD-RRE algorithm and provides convergence results.  
- Identifies conditions under which SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD.  
- Numerical experiments show large gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements on larger-scale tasks.  
- Paper is clearly written, technically sound, and easy to follow.  
- The method is a relatively direct, computationally convenient application of RRE to SGLD.  
- Contribution is incremental rather than transformative but likely useful and adoptable by practitioners.  
- Minor: Figure 1a seems to plot a Gaussian using empirical posterior mean/variance rather than an empirical posterior estimate—clarify.  
- Minor: Figure 2a needs discussion of why bias does not necessarily decrease with smaller step size, explaining interplay of discretization error, stochastic gradient noise, and extrapolation.
