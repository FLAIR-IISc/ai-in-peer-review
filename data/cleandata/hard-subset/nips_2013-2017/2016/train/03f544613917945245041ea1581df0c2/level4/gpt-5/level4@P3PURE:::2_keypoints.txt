- SGRRLD applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from noisy stochastic gradients, yielding asymptotically lower bias and MSE at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Synthetic experiments (Linear Gaussian Model) convincingly show bias reduction; MovieLens shows only slight test RMSE improvement, with questionable practical significance.  
- Conceptually simple and well-motivated extension of SGLD using a classical bias/variance reduction technique.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and appears straightforward to implement.  
- Theoretical results indicate faster convergence (lower bias/MSE) than SGLD under appropriate conditions with the same step size.  
- Synthetic results provide strong controlled evidence of bias reduction.  
- Real-data improvement is slight and may not translate to meaningful practical gains.  
- Computational comparison assumes parallelization parity, but a fair baseline should include averaging multiple parallel SGLD chains under the same wall-clock budget.  
- Real-data comparison is potentially unfair: SGRRLD uses step sizes γ and γ/2 while SGLD is run only with γ, omitting SGLD with γ/2 which should reduce bias.  
- Unclear what conditions on the potential U are required for Theorems 1 and 2, including whether U can be multi-modal.  
- Question about how error order reduction holds when stochastic gradient noises are uncorrelated and chains decorrelate quickly; request to quantify inter-chain correlation in experiments.  
- Suggested experiment: compare SGRRLD to an estimator averaging two independent SGLD chains run in parallel with the same wall-clock time and step sizes.  
- Suggested experiment: include SGLD with step size γ/2 (and possibly two parallel chains) in real-data comparisons to control for step-size-induced bias reductions.  
- Suggested experiment: expand real-data evaluations across diverse models and datasets to establish consistent practical gains.  
- Overall: well-presented, easy-to-implement method with appealing theory and strong synthetic evidence, but limited/mixed real-data support; fairness of baselines and broader empirical validation are needed to strengthen the paper.
