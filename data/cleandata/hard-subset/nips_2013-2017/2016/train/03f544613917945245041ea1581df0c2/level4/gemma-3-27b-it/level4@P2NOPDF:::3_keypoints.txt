- Paper proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated-noise chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second chain twice as long) to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Showing bias reduces from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and corresponding MSE improvement when combining estimates from the two chains.  
- Method is conceptually simple, easy to implement, minimally disruptive to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation in the SG-MCMC / ML context appears new.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clear.  
- Generality question: can the technique be applied to all SG-MCMC algorithms beyond satisfying π_γ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2), or are there additional required conditions?  
- Applicability beyond SG-MCMC: could the method extend to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampled Metropolis-Hastings) or distributed SGLD?  
- Computational-efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long is costly; proposing three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2 for equal lengths) as a potential alternative and asking about variance implications.  
- Higher-order extrapolation: ask whether using more than two step sizes could further reduce bias.  
- Noise correlation and mini-batches: importance of correlated injected Gaussian noise is emphasized; unclear effect of correlating mini-batch selections and whether using the same batch across paired iterations minimizes variance.  
- Experimental validation critique: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include comparison to standard SGD to demonstrate practical/generalization benefits over SGLD and encourage adoption.  
- Practical suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode to improve accessibility for practitioners who may skip theoretical sections.
