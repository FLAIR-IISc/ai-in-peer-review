- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides theoretical asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees.  
- Empirically validates derived bias and MSE bounds on a synthetic Gaussian model and shows practical gains on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and well-situated within related work.  
- Novelty concern: approach may be incremental given prior studies on numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Practicality concern: parallel implementation requires per-iteration communication for correlated Brownian motion; communication overhead is not discussed and should be explicitly stated.  
- Experimental concern: unclear resource allocation—whether a single SGLD chain used equal compute as the two SGRRLD chains; fairness of wall-clock comparisons is not justified given gradient computation costs.  
- Suggested additional experiment: compare to two embarrassingly parallel (non-communicating) SGLD chains.  
- Minor: the phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" appears redundantly.
