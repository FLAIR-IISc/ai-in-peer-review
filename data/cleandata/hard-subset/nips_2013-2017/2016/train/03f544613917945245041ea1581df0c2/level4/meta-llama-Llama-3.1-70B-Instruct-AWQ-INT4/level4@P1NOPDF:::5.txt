To mitigate the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC), the authors introduce a novel method called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a readily available tool for stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Notably, two chains are run in parallel in SG-RR-MCMC without communication. The authors provide theoretical analysis, demonstrating that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. Empirical results are also satisfactory, validating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) is intriguing and has proven effective according to empirical results. The efficiency of SG-RR-MCMC can be theoretically justified by providing a better convergence rate, although this can also be achieved using high-order integrators (unfortunately, the authors did not provide a comparison with high-order integrators in their experiments).
