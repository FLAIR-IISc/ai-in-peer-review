- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) and shows asymptotically lower bias and MSE; synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show clear advantage.  
- MovieLens real-data experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement; results are arguable.  
- Novel contribution: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC; theoretical claims of faster convergence under certain conditions.  
- Faster convergence requires running two parallel Markov chains (computational trade-off).  
- Algorithm is clearly described and easy to implement.  
- Synthetic results convincingly demonstrate bias reduction; more real-data experiments are needed to confirm practical benefit.  
- Unclear conditions on the potential U required by Theorems 1 and 2 — question whether U can be multi-modal.  
- Unclear mechanism: if gradient noises decorrelate quickly, how combining two chains reduces error order; ask whether chain correlations were measured in real-data experiments.  
- Experimental setup concern: claimed equal compute with parallelization overlooks that SGLD can also use multiple parallel chains — need comparison to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Real-data comparisons should include SGLD at step size gamma/2 (lower bias) run in two chains to fairly compare against SGRRLD.  
- Overall: novel and promising on synthetic data but requires more real-data evidence and responses to methodological/experimental concerns to be conclusive.
