- Main contribution: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, yielding SGRRLD with asymptotically lower bias and MSE.
- Novelty note: RR extrapolation for SDEs is known, but its application to SG-MCMC is a useful contribution.
- Theory claim: proves faster convergence for SGRRLD under certain conditions, at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains.
- Implementation: algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.
- Synthetic results: Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly show bias reduction for that model.
- Limited real-world validation: MovieLens results show only slight RMSE improvement; more datasets are needed to demonstrate practical gains.
- Baseline fairness: comparison is unfair—two parallel SGLD chains (same compute) should be used as a baseline.
- Step-size comparison issue: comparing SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) to SGLD (γ) is inappropriate; SGLD with γ/2 (or two γ/2 chains) should be compared.
- Theoretical clarity missing: conditions on the potential U required for Theorems 1–2 are not clearly stated (e.g., multimodality concerns).
- Lack of intuition: paper should better explain how combining two chains cancels error terms and reduces error order.
- Correlation question: need empirical/accounting of correlation between the two SGRRLD chains in practice and its effect when gradient noise is uncorrelated.
