The authors propose a novel method called stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC to reduce the bias of stochastic gradient Markov chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, an established technique for stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC runs two chains in parallel without communication, allowing for efficient computation. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC. Empirical results also show satisfactory performance. The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) is intriguing and empirically effective. The efficiency of SG-RR-MCMC can be theoretically validated through improved convergence rates, although similar performance could be achieved using high-order integrators, which the authors do not compare to in their experiments.
