This paper successfully applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). By leveraging RRE, a standard numerical integration tool with recent applications to numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs), the authors establish a valuable connection between the MCMC literature and the SDE literature, potentially benefiting both communities.

The authors introduce the new SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical results on its convergence, and provide arguments for when SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD. They demonstrate impressive improvements in toy numerical examples and modest yet consistent improvements in large-scale examples. Overall, this is a solid paper, offering interesting links between two somewhat disconnected fields, theoretical results, and good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm.

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, with sound technical aspects. The application of RRE to SGLD is computationally convenient and fairly straightforward. While the method may not revolutionize current practice, I believe practitioners will find it useful.

Minor comments: Figure 1a may be misleading, as it depicts a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance rather than the empirical estimated posterior distribution. This distinction should be clarified for the reader. In Figure 2a, it would be helpful to discuss why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the step size decreases, as this may seem counter-intuitive to some readers.
