- Introduces SGRRLD: runs two correlated SGMCMC chains with different step sizes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias and improve convergence.
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE.
- Empirical validation shows performance gains over standard SGLD on synthetic and matrix factorization datasets.
- Paper is a solid, well-presented contribution addressing bias in SG-MCMC with principled methodology.
- Novelty may be incremental given prior work on higher-order integrators ([10]) and RR extrapolation for invariant distributions ([23]).
- Needs clearer discussion on how SGRRLD distinctively builds upon and differs from those prior techniques.
- Practical overhead concern: running two chains and maintaining correlated Brownian motion may incur significant computational and communication costs.
- Paper lacks comprehensive analysis of communication and resource overheads and their impact on real-world scalability.
- Experimental fairness is unclear: must clarify whether SGLD baselines used equivalent computational resources to the two-chain SGRRLD.
- Require explanation of how gradient evaluation resources were allocated across SGRRLD chains and effects on wall-clock time.
- Suggest comparing SGRRLD to trivially parallel baselines (two independent SGLD chains) to isolate benefits of correlated noise.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RR application to SG-MCMC, strong theoretical guarantees, empirical improvements, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing practical overhead analysis, and potentially unfair experimental comparisons.
- Minor comment: redundant use of the phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" on lines 25 and 70.
