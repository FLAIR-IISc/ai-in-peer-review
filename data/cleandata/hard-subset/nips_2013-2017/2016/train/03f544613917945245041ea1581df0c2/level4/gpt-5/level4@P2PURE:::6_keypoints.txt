- Paper introduces Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC, running two chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise, and provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees; experiments validate theory and show practical gains.  
- Concern 1 (novelty): contribution may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and previous use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions [23].  
- Concern 2 (communication overhead): the parallel algorithm requires per-iteration communication to correlate Brownian motions, but the paper does not acknowledge or discuss this overhead.  
- Concern 2 (resource allocation/fairness): comparisons between SGRRLD and SGLD are unclear—does single SGLD use equal total resources as two SGRRLD chains, and how are resources split—affecting wall-clock fairness since gradient evaluations dominate cost.  
- Concern 2 (baseline): authors should compare against two independent SGLD chains run embarrassingly parallel (no communication) as a natural alternative baseline.  
- Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is duplicated in the text.
