- Proposed method: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise injections.
- Theoretical contribution: provides both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees, with bias and MSE bounds.
- Empirical results: synthetic Gaussian experiments validate theoretical bounds; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical improvement over SGLD.
- Strengths: well-crafted technique, clear exposition, and good contextualization with prior work.
- Novelty concern: contribution may be incremental since effects of numerical integrators were analyzed in [10] and Richardson-Romberg was used for invariant distributions in [23].
- Practicality/experiment fairness concern: parallel implementation requires per-step communication (correlated Brownian motion), but communication cost is ignored and not discussed.
- Resource-allocation ambiguity: unclear whether SGLD used the same compute resources as the two-chain method, how resources are split between chains, and how gradient compute costs lead to reduced wall-clock time.
- Suggested additional comparison: provide implementation details or compare to running two independent SGLD chains embarrassingly parallel (no communication).
- Minor comment: the term "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated and should be removed or rephrased.
