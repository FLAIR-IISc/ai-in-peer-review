This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent “big-data MCMC” methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard numerical integration technique that has recently found compelling applications in numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs); a key contribution of this paper is bridging the MCMC and SDE literature, which could benefit both communities. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical results concerning the convergence of the algorithm (including arguments for its improvement over vanilla SGLD), and present supporting numerical results. They demonstrate impressive improvements on toy examples and modest, yet consistent, gains on large-scale problems. Overall, this is a solid paper, clearly written and technically sound. It establishes interesting connections between two relatively disconnected fields, offers compelling theoretical results, and provides good empirical performance for the proposed algorithm. 

The method represents a relatively straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I anticipate practitioners will find these methods useful, although they are unlikely to fundamentally change current practice. 

I have a few minor comments. First, Fig 1a is slightly misleading – it depicts a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirically estimated posterior distribution itself. This should be clarified or more prominently emphasized to avoid misinterpretation. Second, in Fig 2a, it would be helpful to discuss why the bias doesn’t necessarily decrease as the stepsize decreases; this behavior is somewhat counterintuitive and warrants further explanation.
