To mitigate the bias of SG-MCMC, the authors introduce a new method called stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This approach leverages Richardson–Romberg extrapolation, a standard tool for SDEs. In particular, SG-RR-MCMC runs two chains in an embarrassingly parallel manner without communication. The theoretical analysis is provided, establishing that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. The empirical results are also satisfactory. The idea is interesting and shown to be effective empirically. It can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. The efficiency is supported theoretically via an improved convergence rate, although such gains can also be achieved with higher-order integrators (unfortunately, the authors did not provide an experimental comparison with these integrators).
