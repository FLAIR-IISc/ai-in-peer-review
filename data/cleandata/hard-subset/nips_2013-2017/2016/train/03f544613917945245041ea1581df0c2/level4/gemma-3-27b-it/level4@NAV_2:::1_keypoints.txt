- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC, focusing on SGLD (introduces SGLD-RRE).
- Connects MCMC and SDE literatures by using a standard numerical-integration tool in MCMC context.
- Provides theoretical convergence results and arguments for improved rates over standard SGLD.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on toy examples and consistent, modest gains on large-scale datasets.
- Paper is clearly written, technically sound, and easy to follow.
- Method is a straightforward, computationally convenient application of RRE to SGLD.
- Practitioners are likely to adopt the method, though it is unlikely to radically change current practice.
- Minor: Fig. 1(a) should state it shows a Gaussian with empirical posterior mean and variance, not the empirical posterior distribution.
- Minor: Fig. 2(a) needs a brief explanation why bias does not necessarily decrease with step size.
- Argument for acceptance — soundness: rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence guarantees.
- Argument for acceptance — novelty: novel use of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SGLD.
- Argument for acceptance — clarity: methodology, theory, and experiments are well explained.
- Argument for acceptance — empirical validation: experiments support theory and show practical benefits.
- Argument for acceptance — potential impact: offers a practical efficiency improvement for widely used SGLD.
- Argument against acceptance — incremental: improvements, while significant, are not dramatic on real datasets.
- Argument against acceptance — added complexity: requires running two chains in parallel, adding overhead.
- Argument against acceptance — limited experiments: evaluation is narrow; broader problems/datasets would strengthen claims.
