This paper presents an improvement to existing Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods by introducing a Richardson-Romberg scheme. This scheme enhances the convergence rates in terms of bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). The proposed method is straightforward to implement, requiring only minimal modifications to traditional SG-MCMC algorithms. Furthermore, the paper is supported by solid theoretical results.

The authors propose a Romberg-Richardson scheme that can improve the convergence rates of standard SG-MCMC algorithms. The method appears to be well-founded, and the theoretical analysis is convincing. However, my primary concern lies in the derivation of the specific convergence rates from the theorem, as explained in the "explain fatal flaws" section. Additionally, I have a practical concern regarding the matrix factorization experiment results.

To provide a fair comparison, if we were to run the proposed method on a single-core computer, it would take approximately three times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to the use of two chains, one of which requires twice as many steps. Taking this into account, the plots in Figure 5 would not accurately reflect the performance difference. To rectify this, the time for Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) should be multiplied by a factor of 2/3, which would reduce the difference between SGLD and Stochastic Gradient Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD).

In Figure 3, which plots the bias, it appears that the best convergence is achieved by the red curve, corresponding to α = 0.2, rather than α = 1/3 as stated in the text. Is there an error in the text or the figure? Moreover, the optimal curve exhibits significant fluctuations, which may not align with the expected convergence rate for α = 0.2. Could the authors provide an explanation for this discrepancy?

Furthermore, the authors state in line 256 that when the step size is too small, the term 1/Kγ would dominate. I am curious as to why this phenomenon does not occur in the case of SGLD.

Some minor comments:

* In Equation 1, the authors use a different symbol than θ. Could they explain the reason behind this choice?
* On line 94, the abbreviation "RR" is not defined. It would be helpful to provide a definition or expand the abbreviation.
* On line 131, the symbol Γ_K is not defined. A definition or explanation would be appreciated.
* On line 160, the authors refer to a "smooth function f." Could they specify the type of smoothness they are referring to?
* In line 249, the text states that Figure 1(b) plots Bias vs. time, but the figure actually plots Bias vs. dimension. Should this be corrected to Figure 1(c)?
* Finally, the authors may consider using bold letters to represent vectors for clarity and consistency.
