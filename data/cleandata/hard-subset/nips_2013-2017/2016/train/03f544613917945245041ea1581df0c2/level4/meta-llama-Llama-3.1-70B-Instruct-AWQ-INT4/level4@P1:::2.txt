This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) while maintaining a reasonable variance level. The approach is based on the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method that involves running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes. The authors provide a formal theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and has bounded non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error (MSE). The results demonstrate that SGRRLD achieves higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotic settings, and attains the theoretical accuracy of methods based on higher-order integrators.

The paper is well-organized, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement. The synthetic experiments on a Linear Gaussian Model show a significant advantage of SGRRLD in reducing bias. However, the real data experiments on the MovieLens dataset show only a slight improvement in test RMSE, and the results are somewhat arguable.

The application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC algorithms is a valuable contribution, and the proof of faster convergence rates for SGRRLD under certain conditions is a significant result. However, some questions remain regarding the conditions required for the theorems to hold, particularly with respect to the potential multi-modality of the function U. Additionally, the authors' claim that the combination of the two chains reduces the order of the error is not entirely clear, especially when the gradient noises are not correlated.

The experimental setup and real data experiments also raise some concerns. While the authors argue that the computation time is about the same as SGLD with parallelization, it is unclear whether running multiple parallel chains of SGLD within the same time would yield similar improvements in RMSE. Furthermore, the comparison of SGRRLD with SGLD using a step size of gamma/2, which has a lower bias, is not provided. More real data experiments should be conducted to demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in various settings.

Strengths:

* Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC algorithms
* Formal theoretical analysis and proof of faster convergence rates for SGRRLD
* Clear algorithm description and ease of implementation
* Synthetic experiments demonstrate significant advantage of SGRRLD in reducing bias

Weaknesses:

* Limited real data experiments and arguable results
* Unclear conditions required for theorems to hold, particularly with respect to multi-modality of U
* Experimental setup and comparison with SGLD raise some concerns
* More real data experiments are needed to demonstrate effectiveness of SGRRLD
