This paper applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) to mitigate bias introduced by gradient estimation noise. The resulting SGRRLD algorithm exhibits asymptotically lower bias and mean squared error (MSE) compared to SGLD. Experiments using a synthetic Linear Gaussian Model demonstrate a clear advantage for the proposed algorithm. While experiments on the MovieLens dataset show a slight improvement in test RMSE, these results are not conclusive. Leveraging prior work demonstrating RR extrapolation’s ability to enhance the convergence rate of Monte Carlo estimates for stochastic differential equations (SDEs), this paper applies the technique to stochastic gradient MCMC algorithms and proves, under certain conditions, that SGRRLD converges faster than SGLD with the same step size, albeit requiring the execution of two parallel Markov chains. The algorithm is clearly described and relatively straightforward to implement. The synthetic experiments effectively illustrate SGRRLD’s bias reduction capabilities in that specific setting. Further experiments with real datasets would be valuable to demonstrate the practical significance of this bias reduction. I have not verified the details of the proofs for Theorems 1 and 2, but I am interested in clarifying the conditions required for the matrix U to satisfy both theorems, specifically whether U can be multi-modal. Intuitively, if gradient noises are uncorrelated, one would expect the two chains to quickly become uncorrelated even with correlated Gaussian noise. Therefore, the paper should elaborate on how combining these chains reduces the error order. Do the authors observe the correlation between the two chains when applying the algorithm to real data? Regarding the experimental setup and real data results, the authors claim comparable computation time to SGLD due to parallelisation. However, SGLD can also be run with multiple parallel chains within the same time frame. If SGLD were similarly parallelized with two chains, would the same improvement in RMSE still be observed when compared to SGRRLD? Furthermore, the real data experiment compares SGRRLD with step sizes gamma and gamma/2 against SGLD with a step size of gamma. A fairer comparison would also include SGLD with a step size of gamma/2, which would likely exhibit reduced bias (and could also be run with two parallel chains). Finally, additional experiments on diverse real-world datasets are recommended.
