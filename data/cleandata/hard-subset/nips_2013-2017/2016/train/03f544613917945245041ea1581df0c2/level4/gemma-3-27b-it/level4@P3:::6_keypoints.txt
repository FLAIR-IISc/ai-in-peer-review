- Proposes SGRRLD: an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains and correlated Brownian increments to reduce bias and improve convergence.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: consistency, asymptotic behavior, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Empirical results show improved performance over SGLD and one experiment against SGHMC on synthetic and real datasets.
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC and RR-extrapolation work, combining ideas to achieve higher-order accuracy without complex integrators.
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Strength — Novelty: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC offering a path to reduced bias.
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: solid formal analysis distinguishing the work from purely empirical studies.
- Strength — Experimental validation: convincing improvements demonstrated on practical tasks, including large-scale matrix factorization.
- Strength — Practical relevance: performance gains indicate real-world applicability.
- Weakness — Incremental novelty: advances are closely tied to prior works [10, 23], needing clearer distinction of specific contributions.
- Weakness — Practical/communication costs: does not address the communication overhead of maintaining correlated Brownian motion in parallel implementations.
- Weakness — Fairness of comparisons: experimental setup lacks detail on resource allocation, making it unclear if SGLD received equivalent computational resources.
- Weakness — Missing implementation details: insufficient description of how resources and gradient computations were allocated between chains.
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound, theoretically justified, empirically validated, and well-written contribution to scalable Bayesian inference.
- Argument against acceptance: incremental contribution, insufficient treatment of practical limitations, and unclear experimental fairness.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses but needs addressing of practical and experimental concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify communication costs, experimental setup and resource allocation, compare to embarrassingly parallel SGLD, expand novelty discussion over [10,23], and fix repeated "ULA" mentions.
