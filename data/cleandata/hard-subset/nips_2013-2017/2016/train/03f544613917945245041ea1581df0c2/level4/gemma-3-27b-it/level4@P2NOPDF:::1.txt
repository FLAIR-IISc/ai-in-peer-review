This paper investigates the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent advancements in “big-data MCMC” methods, specifically Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE, a well-established technique for numerical integration, has recently found successful applications in the numerical solution of stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A key strength of this work lies in bridging the MCMC and SDE literatures, a connection that holds potential benefits for both fields. 

The authors introduce a novel SGLD-RRE method, accompanied by theoretical analysis demonstrating its convergence properties and providing conditions under which it outperforms standard SGLD. Empirical evaluation reveals substantial improvements on controlled numerical examples and more modest, yet consistent, gains in large-scale applications. 

Overall, this is a strong and clearly written paper. Its value stems from the insightful connection it establishes between two relatively separate areas of research, supported by compelling theoretical results and encouraging empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The approach effectively leverages RRE within the SGLD framework, resulting in a computationally efficient method. I anticipate that practitioners will find this method useful, though it is unlikely to fundamentally alter existing workflows.

I have a few minor suggestions. Figure 1a, as presented, could be misinterpreted. It depicts a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirical posterior distribution itself. This distinction should be explicitly clarified to avoid confusion. Furthermore, in Figure 2a, a discussion of why bias does not consistently decrease with diminishing step size would be valuable. This observation is non-intuitive and warrants further explanation.
