This paper applies Richardson-Rombaugh Extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big data MCMC" methods, with a particular focus on Stochastic Graded Lagouvin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard numerical integration tool that has recently seen some promising applications for Stochastic Differential Equations (SDE); a key feature of this paper is that it provides a link between the MCMC literature and the SDE literature, which could be beneficial to both communities. The authors have introduced a new SGLD-RRE method, developed some theoretical results on the convergence of the new algorithm (as well as arguments for when SGLD-RRE is better than vanilla SGLD), and shown some numerical results. They achieve impressive improvements on toy numerical examples, and modest but consistent improvements in large-scale examples. Overall, this is a solid paper, clearly written. As I said above, it is a solid paper, with interesting links between two somewhat unrelated fields; interesting theoretical results; and a good empirical demonstration of the proposed novel algorithm. The paper is very clear and easy to read, and technically sound. This method is a direct application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I believe that practitioners will use these methods, although these methods will not revolutionize current practice.
