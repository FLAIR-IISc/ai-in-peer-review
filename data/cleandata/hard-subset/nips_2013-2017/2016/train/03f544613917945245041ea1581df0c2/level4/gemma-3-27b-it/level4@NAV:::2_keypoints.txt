- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from gradient-estimation noise.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains lower asymptotic bias and MSE and faster convergence than SGLD for equal step sizes, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Synthetic (Linear Gaussian) experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) experiment shows modest RMSE improvement, but statistical significance and generalizability are unclear.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and implementation is straightforward.  
- Requests clarification of theoretical assumptions on U for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., can U be multi-modal?).  
- Seeks explanation of how error order reduces when combining two chains, given likely rapid decorrelation from uncorrelated gradient noise; asks whether chain correlations were observed in real experiments.  
- Questions the fairness of timing comparisons: parallelizing SGRRLD may match parallel SGLD chains; recommends comparing SGRRLD to two parallel SGLD chains and to SGLD with γ/2.  
- Strong recommendation for more extensive experiments on diverse real-world datasets to validate practical impact.
