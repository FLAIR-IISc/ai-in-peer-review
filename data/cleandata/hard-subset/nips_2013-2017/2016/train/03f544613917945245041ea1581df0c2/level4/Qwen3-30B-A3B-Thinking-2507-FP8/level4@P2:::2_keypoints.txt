- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to two parallel SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Builds on prior SG‑MCMC and RR extrapolation literature and extends RR to stochastic gradient settings.  
- Strength: rigorous, well-structured theoretical analysis including consistency, CLTs, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Strength: algorithm is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and amenable to distributed parallelism.  
- Strength: synthetic Gaussian experiments show strong bias reduction (variance bias ~10⁻² → ~10⁻⁴), supporting theory.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear exposition and appropriate citations.  
- Weakness: real-data MovieLens results show only marginal RMSE gains (2–3%), possibly not significant at scale.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons may be unfair — SGRRLD uses γ and γ/2 while SGLD is only run with γ; need comparisons to SGLD with γ/2 and to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Weakness: no empirical analysis of whether the two chains maintain sufficient correlation under high gradient noise, which is critical for RR to work.  
- Weakness: computational-time comparison is unclear — running two chains doubles work, and claims of "nearly identical wall-clock times" lack practical justification.  
- Pro-accept: strong, novel theoretical contribution with broad applicability to other SG‑MCMC methods.  
- Con-accept: modest empirical gains, potentially unfair baselines, and insufficient discussion of practical implementation and correlation maintenance.  
- Requested improvements: add experiments comparing against SGLD at γ/2 and two parallel SGLD chains, analyze chain correlation during sampling, include more real-world applications showing larger gains, and discuss practical distributed implementation issues.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-executed theoretically, but needs stronger empirical validation and practical analysis to justify real-world impact.
