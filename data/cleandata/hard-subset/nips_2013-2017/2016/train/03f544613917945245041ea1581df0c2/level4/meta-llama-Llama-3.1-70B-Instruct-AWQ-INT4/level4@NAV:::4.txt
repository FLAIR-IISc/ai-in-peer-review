This paper presents an enhancement to existing Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) methods by introducing a Richardson-Romberg scheme, which improves convergence rates in terms of bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). The proposed scheme is straightforward to implement, requiring only minor modifications to traditional SG-MCMC algorithms. Furthermore, the paper is supported by robust theoretical results. The authors propose a Romberg-Richardson scheme that can enhance the convergence rates of standard SG-MCMC algorithms, and the methodology appears sound with solid theoretical backing. However, the derivation of specific convergence rates from the theorem, as explained in the "fatal flaws" section, raises concerns. Additionally, there are practical concerns regarding the matrix factorization experiment results. Notably, if the proposed method is run on a single-core computer, it would take three times longer than standard SG-MCMC due to the use of two chains, one of which requires twice as many steps. This discrepancy would affect the plots in Figure 5, where the time for Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) should be adjusted by a factor of 2/3, thereby reducing the difference between SGLD and Stochastic Gradient Romberg-Richardson Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD). In Figure 3, the bias plot suggests that the optimal convergence is achieved with α=0.2, rather than the stated value of 1/3. Moreover, the optimal curve exhibits significant fluctuations, which may not align with the rate corresponding to α=0.2. It is also worth noting that the text states that when the step size is too small, the term 1/Kγ would dominate, but this phenomenon is not observed in SGLD. Some minor comments include: Equation 1 uses a different symbol than θ; the abbreviation "RR" is not defined on line 94; the notation Γ_K is not defined on line 131; the description of the smooth function f lacks specificity regarding the type of smoothness; and Figure 1(b) plots bias versus time, not dimension, which may be a mistake and should be corrected to Figure 1(c). Additionally, vectors should be represented using bold letters.
