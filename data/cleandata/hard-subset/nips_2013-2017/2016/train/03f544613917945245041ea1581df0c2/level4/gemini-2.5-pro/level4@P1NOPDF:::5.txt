The authors propose a novel method, Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to reduce the bias inherent in Stochastic Gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) methods. This approach employs Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-established technique for improving the accuracy of numerical solutions to Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs). Specifically, the algorithm runs two Markov chains in an embarrassingly parallel fashion, requiring no communication between them.

The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the method's asymptotic consistency, and the empirical results are satisfactory. The core idea is interesting and is shown to be effective in the experiments. The method can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD).

The theoretical justification for the method's efficiency could be strengthened by an analysis of its convergence rate. While the proposed method aims to achieve a better rate of convergence, so do higher-order integrators. A notable omission in the experiments is a comparison against such methods, which would be necessary to fully assess the practical benefits of the proposed approach.

However, there appears to be an inconsistency in the paper's statements regarding the step size. Furthermore, there is a discrepancy between Algorithm 1, presented in the supplement, and Equation (5) in the main text.
