This paper introduces SGRRLD, a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm leveraging Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to reduce the bias inherent in methods like SGLD. The core idea builds on established work showing RR extrapolation can accelerate convergence for stochastic differential equations (SDEs) [Talay & Tubaro, 1990], applying it to the SG-MCMC context. The authors demonstrate, both theoretically and empirically, that SGRRLD achieves lower asymptotic bias and mean squared error compared to SGLD, requiring the parallel execution of two chains with different step sizes. The paper is clearly written and the algorithm is straightforward to understand and implement. The synthetic experiments on a linear Gaussian model effectively illustrate the bias reduction achieved by SGRRLD.

The work appropriately contextualizes itself within the existing literature on SG-MCMC [Welling & Teh, 2011; Chen et al., 2014; Ding et al., 2014] and numerical methods for SDEs [Lemaire & Pagès, 2015]. The theoretical analysis, including the central limit theorem (Theorem 1) and non-asymptotic bounds (Theorem 2), provides a strong foundation for the proposed method. However, a clarification regarding the precise conditions on the potential energy function *U* required for Theorems 1 and 2 would be beneficial – specifically, whether the method extends to multi-modal *U*.  

A key question arises regarding the effectiveness of the RR extrapolation in the presence of gradient noise. While the theory suggests bias reduction, intuitively, uncorrelated gradient noise in the two chains could hinder the benefit of correlated Brownian increments. Investigating the correlation between the chains in the real data experiments, and perhaps explicitly discussing its impact, would strengthen the analysis.

The real-data experiments on the MovieLens datasets demonstrate improvements in RMSE, but the gains appear modest, and the experimental setup raises some concerns. The comparison to SGLD is not fully controlled. While the authors justify the comparable computation time through parallelization, simply running two independent SGLD chains in parallel would provide a baseline for comparison. Additionally, a fairer comparison would involve evaluating SGLD with a step size equal to half that used for SGRRLD (γ/2), as this directly addresses the bias reduction from step size alone.  More diverse real-world datasets and perhaps a more rigorous statistical analysis of the results would further support the claims.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a novel idea with the potential to improve the efficiency of Bayesian inference in large-scale settings.
*   **Theoretical soundness:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation, including convergence analysis and error bounds.
*   **Clear presentation:** The algorithm is well-described and easy to understand.
*   **Demonstrated improvement:** The synthetic experiments clearly show the bias reduction achieved by SGRRLD.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Modest real-data improvements:** The improvements on the MovieLens datasets are relatively small, and the experimental setup could be improved.
*   **Unaddressed questions:** Clarification is needed regarding the conditions on *U* for the theorems and a discussion of the impact of gradient noise correlation.
*   **Limited scope of empirical validation:** More diverse and comprehensive real-data experiments are desirable.



**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a potentially valuable contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. While the real-data results are not overwhelming, the theoretical justification and clear presentation of the method are strong. Addressing the concerns regarding the experimental setup and theoretical limitations would significantly strengthen the paper. I lean towards acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the raised points in a revision.
