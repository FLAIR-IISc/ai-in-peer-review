

This paper introduces SGRRLD, a novel stochastic gradient Markov chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm that applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) to reduce bias from gradient noise. Theoretically, SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and mean squared error (MSE) than SGLD under standard assumptions, with a faster convergence rate for the same step size—though it requires running two parallel Markov chains. The algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.  

Synthetic experiments on a Linear Gaussian Model demonstrate SGRRLD’s clear advantage in bias reduction, aligning with the theoretical claims. However, the real-data experiments on MovieLens show only a modest improvement in test RMSE, which is questionable given the computational overhead. The authors claim computation time is comparable to SGLD due to parallelization, but this overlooks a critical benchmark: running SGLD with two parallel chains (within the same time budget) could yield comparable or better results. The current comparison of SGRRLD(γ) against SGLD(γ) is misleading; a fairer evaluation would compare against SGLD(γ/2) or parallel SGLD(2 chains), as the latter would also exhibit lower bias at the same step size. The paper should explicitly address this comparison.  

The theoretical contribution is promising, building on RR’s established role in accelerating Monte Carlo convergence for SDEs (e.g., Dereich, 2013). However, the conditions on the potential function *U* for Theorems 1–2 require clarification. Specifically, can *U* be multi-modal? The authors assert that uncorrelated gradient noise causes the two chains to decorrelate rapidly, but the mechanism by which their combination reduces the error order needs further elaboration. For instance, how does the RR extrapolation interact with the correlation structure of the two chains during sampling? Real-data experiments should report the observed correlation between the two chains to validate this assumption.  

Additional concerns include the lack of broader real-data validation. More experiments on diverse datasets (e.g., image or text tasks) are needed to confirm whether SGRRLD’s bias reduction translates to significant practical gains beyond MovieLens. The current MovieLens results do not sufficiently justify the algorithm’s utility, especially given the modest RMSE improvement.  

In summary, the paper presents a theoretically sound extension of SGLD with a clear implementation path. The synthetic results are convincing, but the real-data evaluation is underwhelming and methodologically flawed due to an inadequate baseline comparison. To strengthen the contribution, the authors should: (1) compare SGRRLD against parallel SGLD(γ/2) in real experiments; (2) clarify the conditions on *U* for multi-modal targets; and (3) include additional real-world benchmarks. Addressing these points would significantly bolster the paper’s impact. The theoretical novelty is moderate but well-motivated, and the algorithm is well-suited for practical deployment. With revisions, this could be a valuable addition to SG-MCMC literature.
