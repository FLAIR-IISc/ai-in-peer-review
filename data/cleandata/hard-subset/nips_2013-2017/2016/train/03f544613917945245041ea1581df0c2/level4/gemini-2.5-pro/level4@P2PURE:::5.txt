This paper proposes a novel method, Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient MCMC. The approach applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a standard technique for solving stochastic differential equations, by running two non-communicating, embarrassingly parallel chains.

The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC. The empirical results are satisfactory, confirming the effectiveness of the proposed idea. While the method can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD, the concept is interesting and proven to be effective in practice.

However, the paper has a few weaknesses. The theoretical justification for the method's efficiency could be strengthened by providing a superior convergence rate. It should be noted that such rates can also be achieved using high-order integrators, but a significant limitation is that the authors do not provide an experimental comparison against these methods. Furthermore, the manuscript contains inconsistencies: there is a contradictory statement regarding the step size, and Algorithm 1 (in the supplement) is not consistent with Equation (5).
