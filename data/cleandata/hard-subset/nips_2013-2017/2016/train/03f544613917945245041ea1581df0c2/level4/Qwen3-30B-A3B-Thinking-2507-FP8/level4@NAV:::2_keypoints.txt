- Introduces Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SGLD, producing SGRRLD to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.
- Theoretically SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.
- Extends RR extrapolation from SDEs to SG-MCMC, proving faster convergence with the same step sizes but requiring two parallel Markov chains.
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show clear bias reduction and robust validation of SGRRLD’s advantages.
- On MovieLens, SGRRLD yields only marginal test RMSE improvement; the practical significance is questionable.
- Algorithm description is well-structured and implementation straightforward.
- Reviewer did not independently verify Theorems 1 and 2 and questions required conditions on the potential U—specifically whether U can be multi-modal.
- Reviewer asks how error order reduction holds when gradient noises are uncorrelated and chains rapidly decorrelate, and whether chain correlation is observed in real-data runs.
- Major computational concern: fair runtime comparison should be SGRRLD (one run with two chains) vs. SGLD run with two parallel chains, not vs. a single-chain SGLD.
- Real-data comparisons should also include SGLD with half the step size (γ/2) run as two chains, since that could reduce bias and isolate SGRRLD’s benefit.
- Strong recommendation for more extensive real-world experiments to establish practical utility.
