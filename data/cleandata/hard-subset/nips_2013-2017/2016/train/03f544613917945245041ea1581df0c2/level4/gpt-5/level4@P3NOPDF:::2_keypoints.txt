- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by combining two coupled step-size chains to cancel leading-order bias, with proven asymptotic bias/MSE improvements and synthetic/real-data experiments.

- Strength: Conceptually clear and well-motivated application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC.

- Strength: Provides theoretical conditions under which SGRRLD improves bias and MSE rates over matched-step SGLD.

- Strength: Algorithm is simple and clearly described, easy to implement.

- Strength: Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments convincingly show bias reduction.

- Weakness: Empirical comparisons may be unfair — SGRRLD (γ and γ/2) is compared only to SGLD with γ, favoring SGRRLD.

- Weakness: Paper does not compare to SGLD baselines that match compute (e.g., SGLD with γ/2 or two parallel SGLD chains averaged).

- Weakness: RR extrapolation can increase variance unless chains are well-coupled; the paper does not quantify induced correlation or its MSE impact.

- Weakness: Real-data improvement (MovieLens) is small and possibly within noise; limited empirical scope beyond linear-Gaussian model.

- Weakness: Assumptions for weak error expansion and bias cancellation (on U and gradient noise) are not clearly stated in the main text; applicability to multimodal U is unclear.

- Question: What precise conditions on U are required (Lipschitz gradient, dissipativity, bounded derivatives, multimodality, Lyapunov conditions)?

- Question: What moment/independence assumptions hold for stochastic gradients (unbiasedness, bounded variance, independence across steps), and are mini-batches shared across chains?

- Question: How exactly are the two chains coupled (common Gaussian noise, shared mini-batches), and what are empirical cross-correlations over time?

- Question: Can the authors provide empirical bias/variance decompositions for representative test functions to show finite-time MSE behavior?

- Question: How sensitive/stable is SGRRLD to step-size choice and does coupling constrain adaptive or preconditioned variants?

- Suggestion: Add fair baselines matching compute — SGLD with γ and γ/2, and two parallel SGLD chains averaged, report MSE under matched gradient evaluations and wall-clock.

- Suggestion: Report coupling details and ablate use of common Gaussian noise and shared mini-batches; quantify estimator correlation and its effect on variance.

- Suggestion: Broaden empirical validation to include non-Gaussian and multimodal posteriors and additional tasks/metrics (e.g., log predictive density, calibration).

- Suggestion: Clarify theory by moving precise assumptions on U and gradient noise to main text/appendix and state applicability to common SG-MCMC settings and preconditioned variants.

- Suggestion: Measure efficiency via ESS per second and per gradient evaluation, and provide confidence intervals for results like MovieLens to assess significance.

- Minor: Ensure consistent method notation (SGRRLD vs RR-SGLD) and explicitly state combination rule (e.g., 2A_{γ/2} − A_{γ}) and online computation.

- Minor: Discuss integration of RR extrapolation with variance-reduced gradients or control variates.

- Overall: Solid, implementable theoretical idea with promising synthetic results, but empirical validation and assumption clarity need strengthening to convincingly demonstrate practical benefit.
