This paper proposes a novel technique to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation. The basic idea is to run two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise, and then combine their estimates to reduce the bias. The authors provide a formal theoretical analysis and show that their approach, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem.

The paper is well-written, and the presentation is clear. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence properties of SGRRLD and show that it attains higher rates of convergence than existing SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in reducing the bias and mean squared error of the estimates.

The main contribution of this paper is the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC algorithms, which is a novel and impactful idea. The technique is simple to understand and implement, and it has the potential to be widely adopted in the Stochastic Gradient MCMC community.

Strengths:

* Novel and impactful idea
* Simple to understand and implement
* Formal theoretical analysis and convergence results
* Effective in reducing bias and mean squared error

Weaknesses:

* Limited analysis of the effect of correlation between mini-batches
* No comparison to non-stochastic gradient MCMC schemes
* No extension to more than two step sizes
* No algorithm box or pseudo-code for easy implementation

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide more analysis on the effect of correlation between mini-batches
* Compare the method to non-stochastic gradient MCMC schemes, such as sub-sampling based Metropolis-Hastings tests
* Explore the extension of the method to more than two step sizes
* Include an algorithm box or pseudo-code for easy implementation
* Run the matrix factorization experiment until convergence and compare to SGD
