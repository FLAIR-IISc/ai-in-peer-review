- Proposes SGRRLD: run two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combine outputs via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to cancel leading-order bias.  
- Theoretical guarantees: proves asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Non-asymptotic results: shows faster MSE convergence rates matching those of more complex higher-order integrators.  
- Empirical support: synthetic and large-scale experiments convincingly back the theoretical claims and show practical benefits in accuracy and wall-clock time.  
- Novelty/significance: combining SGLD with RR extrapolation is a simple, practical, and valuable contribution.  
- Technical quality: comprehensive and rigorous analysis with clear derivations of improved convergence rates.  
- Practicality: the method is embarrassingly parallel (two independent chains), making it attractive for multi-core large-scale inference.  
- Missing comparison: lacks a direct empirical comparison between SGRRLD and an SGLD variant using a higher-order integrator.  
- Clarity issue 1: guidance on choosing a single step-size schedule is not consolidated despite different optimal exponents (e.g., α=1/3 vs α=1/5).  
- Clarity issue 2: apparent inconsistency between Equation (5) and Algorithm 1 about where the Chain 2 gradient is evaluated; needs verification and clarification.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with solid theoretical and empirical contributions, offering a useful and practical advance despite minor weaknesses.
