- Proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme to improve bias and MSE convergence of SG-MCMC with minimal implementation changes.  
- Paper backed by solid theoretical results supporting the proposed method.  
- Concern about correctness/clarity of deriving specific convergence rates from the presented theorem ("fatal flaws" mentioned).  
- Practical runtime concern: using two chains (one with twice the steps) increases single-core runtime ≈3×, so Figure 5 comparisons are unfair—SGLD time should be adjusted (≈×2/3), narrowing the reported advantage.  
- Figure 3 discrepancy: bias plot indicates best performance at α=0.2 (red), not α=1/3 as claimed, and the optimal curve shows unexpected large fluctuations.  
- Unclear explanation why the 1/(Kγ) term would dominate for very small step sizes, yet this dominance is not observed for SGLD.  
- Equation (1) uses a different symbol instead of θ—symbol consistency needed.  
- Abbreviation "RR" is not defined (line 94).  
- Symbol Γ_K is not defined (line 131).  
- "Smooth function f" is referenced (line 160) without specifying the type/degree of smoothness assumed.  
- Figure labeling error: Figure 1(b) actually plots Bias vs time (not dimension); likely the intended reference is Figure 1(c).  
- Readability suggestion: use boldface for vectors throughout the paper.  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach; addressing the theoretical derivation, runtime fairness, figure discrepancies, and minor fixes would strengthen the paper.
