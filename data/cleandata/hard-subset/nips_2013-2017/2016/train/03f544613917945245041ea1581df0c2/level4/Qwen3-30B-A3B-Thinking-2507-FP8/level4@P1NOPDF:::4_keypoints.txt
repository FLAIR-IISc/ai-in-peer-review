- Proposes a Richardson-Romberg scheme to improve bias and MSE convergence for SG-MCMC with minimal implementation changes.  
- Theoretical results are solid but the derivation linking theorems to stated convergence rates needs more detailed steps.  
- Computational-efficiency claim in Section 5.2 is misleading: running SGRRLD on one core costs ~3× more than standard SG-MCMC (two parallel chains and doubled steps), so Figure 5 timings should be adjusted (SGLD runtime × 2/3).  
- Figure 3: best convergence shown at α=0.2 contradicts the text’s claim that α=1/3 is optimal; the optimal curve is highly oscillatory, making rate estimates unreliable—clarify both points.  
- The claim that the 1/(Kγ) term dominates for small step sizes (line 256) is not evident in SGLD results—explain why SGLD does not show this effect.  
- Equation (1): inconsistent use of θ̃ vs. θ—clarify notation consistency.  
- Line 94: Define "RR" (Richardson-Romberg) at first use.  
- Line 131: Γ_K is undefined—specify its definition.  
- Line 160: Specify the smoothness class assumed for f (e.g., Lipschitz, C^2).  
- Line 249 / Figure 1: Caption error—Figure 1(b) plots "Bias vs. time," not "Bias vs. dimension" (likely meant Figure 1(c)).  
- Presentation consistency: use bold for vectors (e.g., θ → **θ**) throughout.  
- Overall: paper is a valuable, theoretically sound extension to SG-MCMC but needs the above clarifications and corrections.
