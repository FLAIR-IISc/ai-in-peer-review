- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, yielding the SGRRLD algorithm to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Authors claim SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and prove faster convergence under certain conditions.  
- The claimed improvement requires running two parallel Markov chains, increasing computational cost.  
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation is straightforward.  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments clearly demonstrate SGRRLD’s bias reduction.  
- MovieLens experiments show only a slight RMSE improvement and these results are questionable.  
- More extensive real-data experiments are needed to validate practical benefits of this simple modification.  
- Theorems rely on unspecified conditions on the potential U—need clarity (e.g., can U be multi-modal?).  
- Reviewer questions the intuition/proof of error-order reduction when gradient noises are uncorrelated and chains decorrelate rapidly.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: a proper baseline is two parallel SGLD chains (same compute) or SGLD with step size γ/2 run in parallel.  
- Reviewer did not verify proof details of Theorems 1 and 2.
