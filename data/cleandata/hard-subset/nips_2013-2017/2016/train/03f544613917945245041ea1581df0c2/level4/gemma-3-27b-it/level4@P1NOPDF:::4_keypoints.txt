- Paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme for SG-MCMC that improves convergence rates for bias and MSE.
- Implementation is simple and requires minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Theoretical support for the proposed method is solid and well-developed.
- Major concern: derivation of the specific convergence rates in the main theorem may be flawed (see “explain fatal flaws”).
- Practical concern: on a single core the method costs ~3× more wall-clock time (two chains, one with twice the steps), so Figure 5 may be misleading unless SGLD time is scaled (multiply by 2/3) to equalize compute.
- Figure 3 discrepancy: the bias plot suggests optimal α≈0.2 (red) not α=1/3 as claimed—possible error.
- The optimal convergence curve in experiments fluctuates considerably, so the observed rate doesn’t clearly match α=0.2; request explanation for these fluctuations.
- Question about claim (line 256): when stepsize is very small the 1/(Kγ) term should dominate—why doesn’t this appear for SGLD in the experiments?
- Minor: Eq.1 uses a different symbol instead of θ—please clarify or standardize notation.
- Minor: Line 94 uses “RR” without prior definition—define it.
- Minor: Line 131 introduces Γ_K but does not define it—provide definition.
- Minor: Line 160 refers to a “smooth function f”—specify the smoothness assumption (e.g., Lipschitz, number of derivatives).
- Minor: Line 249/caption mismatch—Figure 1(b) vs. 1(c) labeling for Bias vs. time/dimension.
- Minor: Throughout the paper, vectors should be represented consistently in bold.
