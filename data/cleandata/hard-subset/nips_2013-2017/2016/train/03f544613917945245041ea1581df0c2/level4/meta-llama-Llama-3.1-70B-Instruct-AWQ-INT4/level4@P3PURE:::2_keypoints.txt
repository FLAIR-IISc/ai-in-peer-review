- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise, claiming lower asymptotic bias and MSE than SGLD.
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments clearly show SGRRLD reduces bias versus SGLD.
- MovieLens real-data experiment shows only marginal test RMSE improvement, making the practical benefit arguable.
- Technique extension to SG-MCMC is theoretically plausible; authors prove faster convergence under certain conditions, at cost of running two parallel chains.
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation is straightforward.
- Reviewer requests more real-data experiments to demonstrate practical significance of the simple modification.
- Unverified proofs: questions about the conditions on U required for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., can U be multimodal).
- Unclear intuition/mechanism for how combining two chains reduces error order when gradient noises become uncorrelated quickly.
- Asks whether authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.
- Experimental setup concerns: computational-time parity claim with SGLD parallelization is questionable.
- Suggested baseline comparisons missing: SGLD run with two parallel chains, and SGLD with step size γ/2 (and two chains) which could match bias reduction.
- Recommendation: conduct additional, more comprehensive real-data experiments and include the suggested comparisons to validate claims.
