- Introduces SGRRLD: a Richardson–Romberg extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC using two parallel chains to reduce bias and improve convergence over SGLD.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation within SG-MCMC.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Practicality: algorithm is easy to implement with minimal changes to existing SGLD code.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic and real datasets, including large-scale matrix factorization, show improved performance.  
- Parallelizability: the two-chain RR structure naturally maps to parallel computing architectures.  
- Strength: builds on prior SG-MCMC work and addresses known bias issues with a principled numerical technique.  
- Weakness: Figure 5 runtime comparison may be unfair because SGRRLD uses two chains (one with double steps); SGLD runtime should be adjusted (2/3 factor) for a fair comparison.  
- Weakness: Figure 3 shows optimal α≈0.2 in the bias plot, contradicting the text’s claim of α=1/3; needs investigation and explanation of fluctuations.  
- Weakness: explanation of why the 1/(Kγ) term does not dominate for small step sizes (line 256) is unclear and requires elaboration.  
- Weakness: multiple minor notational inconsistencies (different parameter symbol in Eq.1, undefined "RR" in line 94, undefined Γ_K in line 131, underspecified smoothness of f in line 160).  
- Weakness: figure/caption typo—Figure 1(b) plots bias vs time though caption claims vs dimension; likely meant Figure 1(c).  
- Weakness: vectors are not consistently boldfaced throughout the paper.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with promising empirical results and broad applicability; issues are mostly fixable.  
- Argument against acceptance: experimental fairness concerns, empirical/theoretical discrepancies (Fig.3), and an unclear technical explanation limit confidence without revisions.  
- Overall assessment: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address fair runtime comparison, clarify α discrepancy, explain the 1/(Kγ) behavior, and fix notational and figure errors.
