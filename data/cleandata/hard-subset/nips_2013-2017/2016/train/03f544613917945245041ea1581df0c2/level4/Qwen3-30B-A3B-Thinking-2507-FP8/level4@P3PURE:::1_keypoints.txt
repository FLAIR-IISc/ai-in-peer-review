- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD).  
- Provides a useful bridge between MCMC and SDE literatures, benefiting both communities.  
- Introduces a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm.  
- Establishes theoretical convergence results for the proposed method.  
- Demonstrates advantages over vanilla SGLD under specific conditions.  
- Shows large improvements on toy examples and modest but consistent gains in large-scale settings.  
- Paper is technically sound and clearly written.  
- Key strengths: interdisciplinary connection, rigorous analysis, and strong empirical performance.  
- Method is a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, improving computational convenience.  
- Likely to be adopted by practitioners but represents an incremental advance rather than a paradigm shift.  
- Minor: Figure 1a is potentially misleading—uses a Gaussian approximation from empirical mean/variance, not the empirical posterior; clarify this.  
- Minor: In Figure 2a, explain why bias does not monotonically decrease with step size, as this may seem counterintuitive.
