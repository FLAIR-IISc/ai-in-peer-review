- Proposes using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two coupled SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) with correlated Gaussian noise to cancel O(γ) bias and achieve O(γ^2) bias and reduced MSE.  
- Method is simple to implement, requires minimal modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and is broadly applicable across SG-MCMC variants.  
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation with careful noise coupling to SG-MCMC is novel for the ML/SG-MCMC literature despite RR’s origins in numerical analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is well written, motivated, and supports theoretical claims with analysis.  
- Scope question: need clarity on structural conditions beyond πγ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) (e.g., reversibility, smoothness, discretization stability, preconditioning matrices).  
- Extension question: can the approach apply to other approximate MCMC (e.g., subsampling Metropolis–Hastings) or distributed/asynchronous variants, and are there coupling pitfalls in those settings?  
- Computational-efficiency question: sequential running wastes wall-clock time; propose a three-chain design (one γ, two γ/2) for better utilization and ask how this affects variance with different coupling.  
- Higher-order question: ask about extending to multiple step sizes (γ, γ/2, γ/4, …) for O(γ^3+) bias—stability, variance trade-offs, and generalizing noise coupling.  
- Minibatch coupling: unclear role of minibatch correlation—whether using identical minibatches across coupled iterations minimizes variance or is negligible compared to Gaussian noise coupling.  
- Experiments critique: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence, include an SGD baseline, and report concrete performance numbers to contextualize gains over SGLD and practical relevance.  
- Presentation suggestion: include an algorithm box or early pseudocode to improve accessibility and adoption for practitioners.  
- Overall assessment: clear, compelling, and theoretically grounded method with potential broad impact; addressing applicability, minibatch coupling, and more comprehensive experiments would strengthen the paper.
