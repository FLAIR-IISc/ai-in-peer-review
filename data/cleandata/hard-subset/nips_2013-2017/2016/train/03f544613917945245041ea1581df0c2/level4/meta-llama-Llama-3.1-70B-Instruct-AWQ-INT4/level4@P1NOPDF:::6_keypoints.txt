- Proposal: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Theoretical results: provide asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical validation (synthetic): synthetic Gaussian experiments confirm the paper’s bias and MSE bounds.  
- Empirical validation (real data): large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Presentation: clear, well-structured exposition with connections to prior work.  
- Concern — novelty: contribution may be incremental since integrator impacts on SG-MCMC and Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions have been studied previously.  
- Concern — communication cost: parallel chains require inter-chain communication at each step, but communication overhead is not discussed.  
- Concern — experimental fairness: unclear whether computing resources for SGRRLD (two chains) and SGLD (single chain) are matched, making comparisons possibly unfair.  
- Concern — resource splitting: unclear how splitting gradient computation across partial resources for two chains yields reduced wall-clock time versus using full resources for one SGLD chain.  
- Suggestion: compare to running two independent SGLD chains in an embarrassingly parallel fashion (no communication) as an alternative baseline.  
- Minor: the term “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is repeated (lines 25 and 70).
