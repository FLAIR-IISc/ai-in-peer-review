1. SG-MCMC methods are convenient for large-scale Bayesian inference but suffer asymptotic bias (do not converge exactly to true posterior).  
2. The paper shows Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation can reduce this bias for SG-MCMC.  
3. Core method: run two coupled SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second chain twice as long) with paired Gaussian noise coupling.  
4. Estimator: subtract Chain 1 estimate from Chain 2 to obtain bias O(γ^2) instead of O(γ), with proved MSE reduction.  
5. Method is simple to understand and implement without major changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.  
6. Appears broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC variants and could have substantial community impact.  
7. Novelty: RR extrapolation is well known in numerical analysis but not widely used in ML/SG-MCMC.  
8. Clarity: paper is well written and clearly presented.  
9. Question: Is the technique directly applicable to all SG-MCMC methods, or are additional assumptions beyond pi_γ(f)=pi(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) required?  
10. Question: Can the approach extend to other approximate MCMC schemes (subsampling Metropolis–Hastings, Distributed SGLD)?  
11. Efficiency concern: one chain must run twice as long causing idle time—would running three chains (one at γ and two at γ/2) avoid idling and how would that affect variance?  
12. Question: Is there a natural extension to more than two step sizes (higher-order RR)?  
13. Question: Role of mini-batch correlation is unclear—does using the same mini-batch across coupled iterations minimize variance or is its effect negligible?  
14. Experimental concern: For matrix factorization, run until convergence, compare against SGD, and include SGD curves to show practical Bayesian advantage.  
15. Suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode early to improve accessibility for practitioners who may skip theory.
