To reduce SG-MCMC bias, the authors propose a new method called Stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC. This method uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a widely used tool for SDE. Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC allows two chains to run embarrassingly parallel without communication. Theoretical analysis is provided, showing that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. Experimental results are also satisfactory. The concept is appealing and has proven effective according to experimental results. It can be seen as a simple combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. The efficiency can be theoretically verified by providing a good convergence rate, although this can be achieved using high-order integrators (unfortunately, the authors did not provide a comparison with integrators in the experiment).
