This paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg scheme for SG-MCMC that improves convergence rates in terms of both bias and MSE. The method is straightforward to implement, requiring only minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC, and the theoretical development appears solid.

My primary concern is how the specific convergence rates are derived from the stated theorem (as noted in the “explain fatal flaws” section). Beyond that, I have a practical concern regarding the matrix factorization experiment: on a single-core machine, the proposed method runs two chains, with one chain taking twice as many steps, implying roughly 3x the wall-clock time compared to standard SG-MCMC. If this computational overhead is taken into account, the comparisons in Figure 5 are not fair as plotted. To normalize by wall-clock time, the SGLD curves should effectively be evaluated at a time axis scaled by a factor of 2/3, which would reduce the apparent performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD.

Regarding Figure 3 (bias plot), the fastest observed convergence seems to correspond to the red curve with alpha = 0.2, not alpha = 1/3 as stated in the text. Is there a discrepancy? Moreover, the “optimal” curve exhibits substantial fluctuations, which does not seem consistent with the rate expected for alpha = 0.2. Can you explain why this occurs? In addition, line 256 states that when the step size is too small, the 1/(K gamma) term dominates. Why does this dominance not appear for SGLD?
