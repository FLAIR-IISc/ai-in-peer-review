This paper proposes the application of Richardson-Romberg Extrapolation (RRE), a standard numerical integration technique, to modern large-scale MCMC methods, with a specific focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). A notable strength of this work is that it provides an explicit link between the MCMC and numerical stochastic differential equation (SDE) literature, a connection that could prove beneficial to both research communities.

The authors introduce a novel SGLD-RRE algorithm, develop theoretical results regarding its convergence, and present arguments for when this new method is expected to outperform standard SGLD. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through numerical experiments, which show impressive improvements on synthetic examples and modest but consistent gains on large-scale, practical applications.

Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper. The core idea represents an elegant and computationally convenient application of RRE to the SGLD framework. The paper is exceptionally clear, easy to read, and appears technically sound. The combination of interesting theoretical results and positive empirical performance makes a strong case for the proposed algorithm. While the method may not revolutionize current practice, it is a valuable incremental improvement that practitioners will likely find useful and easy to implement.

Minor Comments:

1.  **Figure 1a:** The caption could be slightly misleading. The plot appears to be a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior's mean and variance, rather than a direct visualization (e.g., a histogram or kernel density estimate) of the empirical posterior itself. Clarifying this distinction would be helpful for the reader.

2.  **Figure 2a:** The observation that bias does not necessarily decrease as the step size decreases is counter-intuitive. It would strengthen the paper to include a brief discussion explaining this phenomenon, perhaps touching on the interplay between discretization error and the variance from the stochastic gradients.
