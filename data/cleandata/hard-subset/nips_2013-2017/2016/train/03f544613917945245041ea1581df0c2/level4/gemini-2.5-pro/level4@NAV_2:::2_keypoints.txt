- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading-order bias.
- Novelty: first application of RR extrapolation to the stochastic-gradient MCMC setting, offering a simple route to higher-order accuracy without complex integrators.
- Theoretical results: provides rigorous analysis (CLT and non-asymptotic bounds) showing improved bias/MSE rates over SGLD, reaching O(K⁻⁴/⁵).
- Practical simplicity: algorithm is easy to implement as a modification of existing SGLD and benefits from inherent parallelism.
- Synthetic experiments: linear Gaussian experiments convincingly validate bias and MSE improvements; extension to SGHMC demonstrates framework generality.
- Main experimental concern: fairness of computational-cost comparisons—running two parallel chains in SGRRLD should be compared to two parallel SGLD chains (ensembling) to isolate RR effect from extra compute.
- Missing baseline: authors should compare against SGLD using the smaller step size γ/2 to separate benefits of RR extrapolation from simply using a smaller step size.
- Limited real-data evidence: MovieLens results show only modest RMSE gains; broader real-world experiments are needed to demonstrate practical significance.
- Clarify assumptions: request to summarize key assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., conditions on potential U such as convexity or smoothness).
- Intuition on noise correlation: need more explanation and empirical evidence why correlating only Brownian noise (but not stochastic gradient noise) suffices to control variance and maintain chain correlation.
