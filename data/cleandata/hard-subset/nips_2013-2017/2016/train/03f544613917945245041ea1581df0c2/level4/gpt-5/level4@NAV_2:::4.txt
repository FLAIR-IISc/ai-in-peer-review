This paper enhances existing SG-MCMC methods by introducing a Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation scheme that improves convergence rates in terms of bias and MSE. The approach is straightforward to implement, requiring only minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC, and it is backed by substantial theoretical analysis. Overall, the RR scheme appears promising and the theoretical development seems sound.

My main concern is how the stated convergence rates are derived from the presented theorems (as noted in the “explain fatal flaws” section). In addition, I have a practical reservation regarding the matrix factorization results. On a single-core machine, the proposed method would require roughly three times the computational effort of a standard SG-MCMC run because it maintains two chains and one of them advances with twice as many steps. Accounting for this, the comparison in Figure 5 is not entirely equitable: the wall-clock time axis for SGLD should effectively be scaled by a factor of 2/3, which would narrow the observed gap between SGLD and SGRRLD.

Regarding Figure 3 (bias), the best convergence curve appears to be the red one corresponding to α = 0.2, rather than α = 1/3 as stated in the text—is there a discrepancy? Moreover, the “optimal” curve shows substantial fluctuations, making it hard to reconcile with the α = 0.2 rate; can the authors clarify why this occurs? Also, the text (line 256) states that when the step size is very small, the 1/(Kγ) term dominates; why does the same issue not affect SGLD under those settings?
