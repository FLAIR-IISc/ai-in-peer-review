- Proposes SGRRLD: accelerate SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise; provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees and experiments on synthetic and matrix-factorization problems.  
- Major concern: novelty appears incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and RR for invariant distributions [23]; authors should better delineate what is new for stochastic-gradient methods.  
- Major concern: practicality — SGRRLD requires two chains with correlated Brownian motion, implying per-iteration communication; the paper overlooks communication overhead and must state/justify any cost-free communication assumption.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear whether SGLD baseline received the same total computational resources as the two SGRRLD chains; authors must clarify resource allocation and wall-clock comparison setup.  
- Questionable speedup: if resources were matched, explain how splitting gradient computation between two SGRRLD chains yields faster wall-clock time than a single SGLD chain, since stochastic gradients dominate cost.  
- Suggested baseline: compare to two independent, embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains averaged together to isolate benefits of RR extrapolation and the communication scheme.  
- Minor comment: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is defined twice (lines 25 and 70).
