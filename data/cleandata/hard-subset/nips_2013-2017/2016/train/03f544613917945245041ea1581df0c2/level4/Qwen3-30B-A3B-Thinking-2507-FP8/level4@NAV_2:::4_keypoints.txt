- Introduces SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel SG-MCMC chains at different step sizes to reduce bias with minimal implementation changes.

- Strong theoretical contribution: proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and improved non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds (claims MSE O(K^-4/5) vs SGLD O(K^-2/3)).

- Solid empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets and good compatibility with parallel architectures.

- Theorem 2 lacks detailed explanation of how the derived bounds translate into the claimed convergence rates.

- Matrix factorization experiment may misrepresent computational advantage: SGRRLD runs two chains (one with twice as many steps), so wall-clock time comparisons to SGLD should be adjusted (roughly a 2/3 factor), which reduces the apparent performance gap.

- Figure 3 indicates the optimal bias rate occurs at α≈0.2 (not α=1/3) and shows large fluctuations, calling the stability of the claimed rate into question.

- The claim that the 1/(Kγ) term dominates for small step sizes is not reflected in the presented SGLD results.

- Eq.1 uses a different symbol than θ (inconsistent notation).

- "RR" is not defined at first use (line 94).

- Γ_K is not defined (line 131).

- Required smoothness assumptions for function f are unspecified (line 160).

- Figure 1(b) is mislabeled: it should show bias vs. dimension, not time (line 249).

- Vector notation is inconsistent; vectors should be bolded throughout.
