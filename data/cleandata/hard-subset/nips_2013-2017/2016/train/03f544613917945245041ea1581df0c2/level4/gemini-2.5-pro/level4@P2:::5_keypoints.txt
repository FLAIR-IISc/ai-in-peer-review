- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Provides rigorous theory: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing faster rates (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Empirically validated on synthetic Gaussian and large-scale matrix factorization (MovieLens), showing better accuracy in same wall-clock time.  
- Addresses an important limitation of SG-MCMC (integrator-induced bias), offering a generally applicable bias-reduction method.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with formal guarantees that SGRRLD outperforms first-order methods.  
- Novel and simple idea: adapts classical Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to achieve higher-order accuracy using first-order integrators.  
- Practical and scalable: two chains are embarrassingly parallel, suited to multi-core architectures, with experiments demonstrating real-world efficiency.  
- Well-written and clearly organized, with good connections to prior SG-MCMC and SDE extrapolation work.  
- Contribution may be seen as incremental since it combines existing SGLD and RR techniques rather than introducing an entirely new algorithmic paradigm.  
- Technical/presentation inconsistency: Equation (5) and Algorithm 1 disagree about which state the gradient is evaluated at for the second chain and needs correction.  
- Experimental comparison could be stronger by directly comparing SGRRLD to SGLD using a higher-order integrator on the synthetic benchmark.  
- Suggestion to provide practical guidance for choosing the step-size leading constant, as α choice was insightful but constants were empirically chosen.  
- Recommend discussing the computational trade-off: SGRRLD performs about three times the gradient computations per effective iteration, which matters on a single core despite parallel advantages.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, theoretically solid, practically effective contribution that is recommendation-worthy (accept).
