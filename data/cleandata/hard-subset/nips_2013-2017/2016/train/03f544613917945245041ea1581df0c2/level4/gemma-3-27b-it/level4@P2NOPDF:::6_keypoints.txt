- Introduces SGRRLD, using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC convergence.  
- Method requires running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees.  
- Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate theoretical bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Demonstrates practical improvement on large-scale matrix factorization versus standard SGLD.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and a worthwhile contribution.  
- Novelty seems incremental given prior analyses of integrators in SG-MCMC and prior use of Richardson-Romberg; needs clearer differentiation from [10] and [23].  
- Practical utility may be limited by implementation overhead and unquantified communication costs for maintaining correlated Brownian motion across chains.  
- Experimental comparison lacks clarity on resource allocation—unclear if the single SGLD baseline used equivalent computational resources to the two SGRRLD chains.  
- If resources were shared, authors should explain how gradient computation was divided and why wall-clock time would improve, since gradients typically dominate cost.  
- Need more implementation details (hardware, software, parallelization strategy) and a comparison to two independent SGLD chains (no communication) to isolate the benefits of correlated noise and extrapolation.  
- Minor: the phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is redundantly used on lines 25 and 70.
