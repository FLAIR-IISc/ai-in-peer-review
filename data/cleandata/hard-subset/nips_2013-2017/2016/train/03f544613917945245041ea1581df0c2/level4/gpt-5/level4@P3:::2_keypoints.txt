- Proposes SGRRLD: apply RichardsonŌĆōRomberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with step sizes ╬│ and ╬│/2 and combining them to cancel leading discretization bias.  
- Couples Gaussian noises optimally (╬│ increments = average of two ╬│/2 increments) to control variance.  
- Theoretical results: proves consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bounds with asymptotic bias O(╬│^2), variance O(╬│^4), and finite-time MSE O(K^ŌłÆ4/5) under optimal scheduling.  
- Method is modular and extendable to other SG-MCMC methods (demonstrated for SGHMC).  
- Experiments (linear Gaussian, MovieLens matrix factorization) show reduced bias and modest RMSE gains at similar wall-clock time using parallel execution.  
- Novel, simple, and practical idea: RR extrapolation for SG-MCMC appears new and elegant.  
- Strong theoretical contribution clarifying when RR improves rates and showing optimality of fully correlated Brownian increments.  
- Clear presentation of estimator definition, coupling, and extrapolation.  
- Main assumptions on U, f, and stochastic gradient law L are not explicitly stated in the main text and should be made concrete (smoothness, growth, dissipativity/ergodicity).  
- Unclear how multimodal targets or heavy-tailed situations affect required drift/regularity conditions.  
- Need more intuition and empirical evidence on why RR cancels leading bias when stochastic gradients are independent across chains; correlation of gradients seems important in practice.  
- Method requires identical gradient-estimator laws (e.g., same batch size); sensitivity to slight deviations (different batch sizes/sharding) is unclear.  
- Practical guidance lacking: recommended step-size schedules, choice of ╬▒/constants for finite budgets, whether to correlate mini-batches, explicit pseudocode, and cost model per iteration.  
- Notation could better emphasize that the two chains are run to equal time horizon to interpret K and averaging weights.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: comparisons should include two independent SGLD chains averaged in wall-clock time, SGLD at ╬│/2 (and parallel chains), and higher-order SG-MCMC baselines.  
- Additional real-data tasks (e.g., logistic regression, Bayesian neural nets) would strengthen claims of practical generality.  
- Suggested experiments: ablation on mini-batch coupling (same vs independent), measure chain cross-correlation and estimator variance, and report effective sample size per second.  
- Recommend stating exact assumptions (derivative orders, moments, ergodicity conditions) in the main paper and discussing multimodality explicitly.  
- Provide finite-budget tuning advice: recommend ╬│ ŌłØ K^ŌłÆ1/3 for bias and ╬│ ŌłØ K^ŌłÆ1/5 for MSE with practical constants from pilot runs.  
- Discuss memory and communication overheads and scalability of the noise coupling in distributed settings.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, broadly applicable, solid theoretical backing, improves rates vs first-order integrators, and is parallelizable.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited empirical comparisons and deferred assumptions reduce accessibility and practical substantiation.  
- Overall: technically strong and promising, but needs stronger empirical baselines and clearer presentation of assumptions and practical guidance; reviewer judges it borderline-to-positive.
