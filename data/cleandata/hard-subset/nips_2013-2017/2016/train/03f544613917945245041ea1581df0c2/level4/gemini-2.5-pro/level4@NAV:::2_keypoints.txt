- Proposes SGRRLD: Richardson-Romberg extrapolation applied to SGLD to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Theoretically claims asymptotically lower bias and MSE than standard SGLD.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiment clearly demonstrates bias reduction.  
- On MovieLens real-data experiment, only a slight and arguably insignificant improvement in test RMSE.  
- Main contribution is adapting RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with proofs of faster convergence under certain conditions, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation appears straightforward.  
- Recommend more real-world experiments to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Ask for clarified assumptions on the potential U required by Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., multimodality).  
- Request intuition on the error-reduction mechanism and whether chain correlations were empirically measured.  
- Experimental-design concern: runtime comparison should include two independent SGLD chains run in parallel to be fair.  
- Missing baseline: SGLD with step size γ/2 (and two parallel γ/2 chains) should be compared, as it likely has lower bias.  
- Strongly encourage broadening scope of real-data experiments.
