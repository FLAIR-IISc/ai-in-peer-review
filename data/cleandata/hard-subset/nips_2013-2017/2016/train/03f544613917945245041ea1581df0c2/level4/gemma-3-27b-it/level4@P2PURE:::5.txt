To mitigate bias in Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC), the authors introduce stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), a novel method leveraging Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for solving Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs). Notably, SG-RR-MCMC utilizes two embarrassingly parallel chains without inter-chain communication. The authors support their approach with theoretical analysis demonstrating asymptotic consistency, alongside empirically satisfactory results. The core idea is appealing and demonstrably effective based on the presented experiments, representing a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). While the theoretical efficiency gains – stemming from an improved convergence rate – could potentially be achieved using higher-order integrators, a direct comparison is lacking in the experimental evaluation. Additionally, a discrepancy exists in the discussion of step size selection. Finally, Algorithm 1 (provided in the supplementary material) appears inconsistent with Equation (5).
