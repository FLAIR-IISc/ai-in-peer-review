- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Theoretical results: Proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and provides bias and MSE bounds showing improved convergence rates.  
- Empirical results: Experiments on synthetic and real datasets indicate SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in convergence.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on scalable SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation literature, with clear connections to SDE RR results.  
- Practical advantage: The two chains can be run in parallel, aligning well with modern computing architectures.  
- Generality: Approach is potentially applicable beyond SGLD (e.g., SGHMC demonstration).  
- Strengths summary: Clear presentation, rigorous theory, and supporting empirical validation.  
- Missing comparisons: No direct empirical comparison to well-implemented higher-order integrators.  
- Clarity issue: Motivation for choosing correlated Brownian motions lacks an intuitive explanation.  
- Consistency issue: Minor mismatch between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5) in the main text.  
- Practical bounds: Constant factors hidden in big-O notation are not discussed, leaving practical impact unclear.  
- Experiments: Would benefit from a more thorough ablation study to isolate the effect of RR extrapolation.  
- Recommendation: Overall positive—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address inconsistencies and expand experiments.
