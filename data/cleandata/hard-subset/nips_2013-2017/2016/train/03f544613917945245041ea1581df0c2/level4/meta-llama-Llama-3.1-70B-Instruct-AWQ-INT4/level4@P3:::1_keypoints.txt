- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC, connecting MCMC and SDE literatures.  
- Introduces SGRRLD: runs two chains with different step sizes to reduce SGLD bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on non-asymptotic bias and MSE.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness and show improvements over vanilla SGLD and favorable comparisons with SGHMC.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to readers unfamiliar with the field.  
- Theoretical results are sound and well-explained.  
- The application of RRE to SGLD is novel and original within the MCMC context.  
- Work is significant with potential practical impact for MCMC practitioners.  
- Minor issue: Fig. 1a is misleading—it's a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance, not the empirical posterior; needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: Fig. 2a needs discussion on why bias does not necessarily decrease with stepsize, which is counterintuitive.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application, clear method and theory, solid experiments, and useful cross-community linkage.  
- Arguments against acceptance: method is a relatively straightforward application of RRE and may not substantially advance SDE theory or revolutionize practice.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
