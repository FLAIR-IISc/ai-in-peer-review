- Proposes Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation applied to SGLD, yielding SGRRLD to reduce bias from noisy gradients.  
- Theoretically shows SGRRLD has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and can achieve faster convergence for the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments demonstrate SGRRLD’s bias reduction advantage over SGLD.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement; results are inconclusive.  
- Main experimental concern: fairness of comparisons—should compare SGRRLD to an ensemble of two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Baseline concern: authors should include SGLD with smaller step size (γ/2) and two-chain ensembles to fairly match computational cost.  
- More real-world experiments are needed to substantiate practical benefits of the modification.  
- Theoretical conditions need clarification: specify assumptions on the potential U (e.g., applicability to multi-modal potentials).  
- Request for more intuition on why combining two chains reduces error order and how chain correlation is maintained; ask whether inter-chain correlation was measured.
