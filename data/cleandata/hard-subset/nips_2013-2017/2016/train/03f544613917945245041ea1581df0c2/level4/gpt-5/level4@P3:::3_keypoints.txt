- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce discretization bias in SG-MCMC while controlling variance.  
- Method: Runs paired SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2, couples Gaussian noise via Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2, and forms estimator 2·π̂γ/2 − π̂γ.  
- Theory: Proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bounds for SGLD showing bias O(γ2), variance O(γ4), and finite-time MSE O(γ4 + 1/(Kγ)).  
- Rates: Finite-time MSE optimized at O(K−4/5), improving over standard first-order SG-MCMC’s O(K−2/3).  
- Optimal coupling: CLT identifies optimal Gaussian coupling (Σ = I) that minimizes asymptotic variance.  
- Relation to prior work: Matches accuracy of higher-order integrators while using only first-order updates; positioned relative to SGLD/SGHMC and high-order integrators.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and SGHMC studies show clear bias/MSE reductions; MovieLens matrix factorization demonstrates wall-clock RMSE improvements using parallelism.  
- Assumptions: Some technical conditions (e.g., g ∈ C9) are strong and need clarification about which are essential; requiring identical gradient estimator distributions (same minibatch size) is critical.  
- Clarity: Well written with clear estimator and coupling description; minor typos and notational slips; recommends adding an algorithm box in main text.  
- Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with variance control and new CLT/non-asymptotic analyses for stochastic gradients.  
- Practicality/significance: Simple, general, parallel-friendly method that can be added to existing SG-MCMC with minimal changes and provides meaningful finite-time improvements.  
- Checklist request: Ask authors to state explicit sufficient conditions (weak order-1 error, geometric ergodicity, gradient moments) for RR benefits.  
- Minibatch correlation question: Clarify whether reusing the same minibatch across coarse/fine iterations helps or harms finite-time MSE in practice.  
- Compute fairness: Request experiments controlled for total gradient evaluations (single-device setting) to quantify gains independent of parallel hardware.  
- Step-size guidance: Ask for practical recipe for choosing γ or decreasing-rate α (theory suggests α = 1/5) and constants (e.g., via pilot runs).  
- Extensions: Suggest discussion of recursive RR, multilevel strategies, and a three-chain variant (cost/variance trade-offs, stability concerns).  
- Beyond SG-MCMC: Ask whether RR extends to other approximate MCMC schemes (subsampling MH, distributed SGLD) and what fails with unequal minibatch sizes or partitions.  
- Additional experiments: Recommend adding an SGD baseline and longer runs to convergence for the matrix factorization task to contextualize Bayesian RMSE gains.  
- Pros: Simple, general, parallel-friendly; strong theoretical guarantees; convincing empirical gains.  
- Cons: Heavy technical assumptions; limited experimental baselines and compute-controlled metrics; insufficient practical guidance on minibatch/step-size choices.  
- Overall recommendation: High-quality, well-motivated contribution; recommend acceptance after minor revisions to clarify applicability, add algorithm box, and strengthen empirical comparisons.
