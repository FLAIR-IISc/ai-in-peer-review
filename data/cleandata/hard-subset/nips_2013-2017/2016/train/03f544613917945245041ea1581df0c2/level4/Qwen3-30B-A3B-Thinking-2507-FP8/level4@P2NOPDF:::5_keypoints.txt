- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: integrates Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SG-MCMC, running two independent parallel chains for embarrassingly parallel computation.  
- Theoretical analysis shows asymptotic consistency and a reduced bias/convergence rate compared to standard SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical results are competitive; method is conceptually simple and empirically effective.  
- Lacks direct experimental comparison to high-order integrators, weakening the empirical claim of superiority.  
- Theoretical efficiency claim is not fully validated without demonstrations versus high-order integrators.  
- Inconsistency in step-size analysis: paper claims step size O(1/t^{1/2}) but extrapolation discussion/Equation (3) implies different scaling (e.g., O(1/t^{1/4})), requiring clarification.  
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5) (extrapolated gradient update mismatch) and must be corrected.  
- Overall: well-motivated and technically sound contribution, but requires fixes to step-size claims, algorithm description, and additional comparisons before publication.
