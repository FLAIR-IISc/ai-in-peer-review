- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Method requires running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes for extrapolation.  
- Provides formal theoretical guarantees: asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem.  
- Derives non-asymptotic bounds on bias and mean squared error.  
- Shows the Richardson-Romberg technique can be applied to a large class of SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with detailed algorithmic and theoretical analysis.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Technically sound claims supported by theory and empirical results.  
- Original contribution: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is novel for the community.  
- Potentially significant: could substantially improve SG-MCMC performance.  
- Concern: analysis assumes SG-MCMC satisfies certain conditions that may not always hold in practice.  
- Concern: running two chains in parallel increases computational cost.  
- Concern: lacks comprehensive comparison with other approximate MCMC methods (e.g., subsampling MH, Distributed SGLD).  
- Suggestion: provide more details on conditions required for the bounds in Section 2.2.  
- Suggestion: investigate applicability to other approximate MCMC schemes and consider using three chains to cut cost.  
- Suggestion: include comprehensive comparisons with other MCMC schemes and add an algorithm box for accessibility.
