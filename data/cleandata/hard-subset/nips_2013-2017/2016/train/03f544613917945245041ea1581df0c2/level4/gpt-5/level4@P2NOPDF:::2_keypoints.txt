- Applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SGLD (called SGRRLD) to reduce bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Claims SGRRLD has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain assumptions, with proofs (Theorems 1–2).  
- Synthetic linear Gaussian experiments show clear bias reduction and superior performance of SGRRLD.  
- MovieLens experiment shows a small test RMSE improvement; practical significance is questionable.  
- Adapts known RR acceleration for SDEs to SG-MCMC; requires running two coupled Markov chains.  
- Algorithmic description is clear and implementation appears straightforward.  
- Requests clarification on precise assumptions on the potential U for the theorems (e.g., multimodality vs global convexity/strong dissipativity).  
- Questions how RR cancellation works if stochastic gradient noises across chains are (approximately) uncorrelated; asks for intuition or finite-time bounds on required cross-chain correlation.  
- Asks whether empirical cross-chain correlation was measured/reported in the real-data experiments.  
- Experimental fairness concern: claiming similar wall-clock time via parallelization ignores that parallel hardware could instead run multiple independent SGLD chains.  
- Requests comparisons matching wall-clock time or total compute, e.g., SGRRLD vs averaged independent SGLD chains.  
- Specific fairness request: include SGLD with step size γ/2 (and averaging of two γ/2 chains) since γ/2 reduces bias and may close the gap.  
- Calls for more real-data tasks to demonstrate reliable practical bias reduction beyond MovieLens.  
- Requests additional experiments on sensitivity to step-size schedules (constant vs decreasing), mini-batch size, gradient variance, and cross-chain coupling strength.  
- Suggests reporting bias estimates for relevant functionals (not just RMSE) to substantiate bias-reduction claims.  
- Overall judgment: idea is simple, natural, and potentially impactful, but needs clearer theoretical assumptions and stronger, fairer empirical evidence.
