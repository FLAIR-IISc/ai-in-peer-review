This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE), a standard numerical integration technique, to modern large-scale MCMC methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). A valuable contribution of the paper is that it bridges the literature on MCMC and numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs), a connection that should prove beneficial to both communities. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE algorithm, provide theoretical analysis of its convergence (including arguments for when it improves upon standard SGLD), and demonstrate its performance with numerical experiments. The results show impressive improvements on toy examples and modest but consistent improvements in large-scale applications.

This is a solid, technically sound, and clearly written paper. Its strengths lie in the interesting connection it establishes between two distinct fields, its sound theoretical analysis, and the good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The method itself is a computationally convenient and relatively straightforward application of RRE to SGLD. While the proposed techniques are unlikely to revolutionize current practice, they represent a valuable contribution that practitioners will likely find useful.

Minor Comments:
*   Figure 1a could be misleading. It appears to show a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirical posterior distribution itself. This distinction should be clarified or emphasized to avoid misinterpretation by the reader.
*   The result in Figure 2a is somewhat counter-intuitive, as the bias does not monotonically decrease as the step size decreases. It would be helpful for the authors to add a brief discussion explaining this phenomenon.
