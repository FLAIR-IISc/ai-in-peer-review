- Proposal: Use Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two SG-MCMC chains with correlated Gaussian noise (half step size and twice steps) to cancel O(gamma) bias.  
- Estimator property: RR combination yields bias O(gamma^2) instead of O(gamma), improving MSE.  
- Implementation: Lightweight—no change to dynamics beyond step-size schedules and correlated increments; demonstrated for SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Theoretical results: Provides consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds supporting improved rates.  
- Key insight: Correlating Brownian increments controls variance while RR cancels leading discretization error.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on a linear Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization align with theory and show wall-clock gains.  
- Clarity: Well written and accessible; suggests adding a concise algorithm box/pseudocode for practitioners.  
- Originality: Applying RR with correlated noise to SG-MCMC appears novel and potentially influential.  
- Significance: Simple, general, parallelizable method with practical relevance for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Applicability question: Need explicit conditions (ergodicity, smoothness, growth) under which the weak error expansion holds and RR applies.  
- Extension question: Could RR be applied to other biased MCMC methods (e.g., subsampling MH) or distributed SGLD variants, and what modifications are needed?  
- Parallel efficiency: Synchronous execution wastes resources; asks if multi-chain schedules (e.g., one gamma chain + two gamma/2 chains) preserve benefits and improve utilization.  
- Multilevel extension: Inquires about practical multi-step/multilevel RR and trade-offs between complexity and variance.  
- Gradient correlation: Questions the effect and potential benefit of correlating mini-batches across chains versus only correlating Gaussian noise.  
- Experimental baselines: Requests runs to convergence and inclusion of an SGD baseline to contextualize Bayesian sampler gains.  
- Presentation request: Recommends adding algorithm boxes for SGRRLD and SGHMC to improve accessibility.  
- Pros: Simple plug-in technique, solid theory, clear intuition, empirical alignment with theory, and natural parallelism.  
- Cons: Needs clearer practical guidance on assumptions and mini-batch correlation, potential synchronous inefficiency, and stronger experimental baselines.  
- Overall: Clear, well-motivated, broadly useful idea with minor clarifications and better baselines likely to increase impact.
