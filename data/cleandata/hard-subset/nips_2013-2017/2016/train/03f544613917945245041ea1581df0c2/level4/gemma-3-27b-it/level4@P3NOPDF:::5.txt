## Review of Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC

This paper introduces Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), a novel method for reducing bias in Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The core idea leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for solving Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs) – to improve the accuracy of the MCMC sampler. Specifically, the authors propose running two independent chains in parallel, without inter-chain communication, and then combining their outputs using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation. 

The paper’s strength lies in its elegant and conceptually simple approach. Combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD is a natural and potentially powerful idea, and the authors successfully demonstrate its effectiveness. The theoretical analysis provides valuable insight, establishing asymptotic consistency for the proposed method. The empirical results presented are encouraging and support the claim that SG-RR-MCMC reduces bias compared to standard SG-MCMC methods.

However, several points require attention. While the theoretical analysis demonstrates consistency, a key aspect missing is a rigorous comparison of the convergence rate with existing methods, particularly higher-order integrators. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but providing such a comparison would significantly strengthen the contribution. Demonstrating a clear advantage in convergence speed, even if requiring careful tuning, would bolster the argument for adopting SG-RR-MCMC.

**Strengths:**

*   Novel and intuitive approach combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD.
*   Theoretical guarantee of asymptotic consistency.
*   Empirically validated effectiveness in reducing bias.
*   Embarrassingly parallel implementation is a significant advantage.

I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors addressing the aforementioned concerns.
