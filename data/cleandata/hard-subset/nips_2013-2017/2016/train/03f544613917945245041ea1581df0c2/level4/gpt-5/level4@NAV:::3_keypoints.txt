- Paper proposes Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC: run two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second run twice as long) with coupled Gaussian noise to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Noise coupling uses alpha_k = (beta_{2k-1} + beta_{2k})/√2 between Chain 1 and Chain 2.  
- The extrapolated estimator reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and yields improved MSE.  
- Method is simple, requires no major changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and is easy to implement.  
- Claimed broad applicability across many SG-MCMC methods, potentially high impact for the community.  
- Novelty: Richardson–Romberg is classical in numerical analysis but under-recognized in the ML/SG-MCMC community.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and well presented.  
- Question: Can this technique be applied to all SG-MCMC methods, or are extra conditions beyond pi_γ(f)=pi(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) required?  
- Question: Can the approach extend to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH) or to distributed SGLD?  
- Efficiency concern: one chain is idle while the other runs twice as long—could running three chains (one at γ, two at γ/2) for equal iterations reduce wasted compute, and how would that affect variance?  
- Question: Is there a natural extension to more than two step sizes for higher-order bias cancellation?  
- Question: Impact of correlating mini-batches unclear—does using the same mini-batch for corresponding iterations minimize variance, or is that effect negligible?  
- Experiment suggestion: For matrix factorization, run to convergence, include SGD as a baseline, and report final numbers to show regimes where Bayesian methods outperform SGD.  
- Usability suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to help practitioners implement the method without diving into technical theory.
