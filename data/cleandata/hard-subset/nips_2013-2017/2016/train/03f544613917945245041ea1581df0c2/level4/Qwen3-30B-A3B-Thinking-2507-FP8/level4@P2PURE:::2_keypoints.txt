- Applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SGLD, producing SGRRLD to reduce bias from gradient-estimation noise.  
- Claims SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and lower MSE than SGLD (theoretical results).  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show clear bias reduction for SGRRLD.  
- MovieLens experiments show only slight test RMSE improvement and the results are questionable.  
- Extends RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and proves faster convergence than SGLD at the same step size, but requires running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.  
- Theoretical concerns about required conditions on potential U (e.g., can U be multimodal?) and unverified theorem details.  
- Question how combining two chains reduces error order if gradient noises are uncorrelated and chains decorrelate rapidly; asks whether chains are correlated in real-data experiments.  
- Experimental-design concern: claim that runtime is comparable to SGLD with parallelization is questionable because SGLD could also run multiple parallel chains.  
- Recommend comparing SGRRLD to SGLD with two parallel chains to see if RMSE improvement persists.  
- Suggest a fairer benchmark: compare SGRRLD (step size γ) to SGLD with step size γ/2 (lower bias) and two parallel chains.  
- Recommend additional real-data experiments to test whether simple SGLD modifications substantially reduce bias.
