Summary
The paper proposes a Richardson–Romberg (RR) scheme for SG-MCMC that aims to improve the convergence rates of bias and MSE. The method is simple to implement and requires only minimal modifications to standard SG-MCMC algorithms. The theoretical development appears solid overall.

Strengths
- Clear idea: augment standard SG-MCMC with an RR scheme to reduce bias and improve MSE rates.
- Practical: easy to incorporate into existing SG-MCMC implementations.
- Theory: backed by non-asymptotic results supporting the claimed improvements.

Main concerns
- Derivation of specific convergence rates: My primary concern is how the specific convergence rates are obtained from the stated theorems. As noted in my “explain fatal flaws” section, I do not see how the particular rates cited in the text follow directly from the theorem as written. Please make explicit the steps that connect the theorem to the claimed rates (i.e., how each term is balanced, and what assumptions are crucial).

Empirical and interpretability concerns
- Computational fairness in matrix factorization: On a single-core machine, the proposed method would take roughly three times longer than standard SG-MCMC because it runs two chains, and one of them uses twice as many steps. Accounting for this, the comparisons in Figure 5 do not appear fair. The time for SGLD should be multiplied by a factor of 2/3, which would shrink the observed performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD.
- Figure 3 (bias plot): The best convergence seems to be achieved by the red curve with α = 0.2, not α = 1/3 as stated in the text. Is there an inconsistency between the figure and the description? In addition, the “optimal” curve fluctuates substantially, which does not appear to match the expected behavior for α = 0.2. Can you explain why this happens?
- Step size discussion (line 256): You state that when the step size is too small, the 1/(Kγ) term will dominate. Why does this not happen for SGLD?

Minor comments and clarifications
- Equation (1): Why use a symbol different from θ for the parameter? Please keep notation consistent.
- Line 94: RR is used but not defined at first mention.
- Line 131: Γ_K is introduced without definition.
- Line 160: You refer to a “smooth” function f. Please specify the smoothness class (e.g., Lipschitz gradient, bounded Hessian, etc.).
- Line 249 and Figure references: The caption/text says Figure 1(b) plots Bias vs. time, not dimension. Should this be Figure 1(c)?
- Notation: Please use boldface for vector-valued quantities throughout.

Overall assessment
The idea is appealing and the theoretical framework appears strong. However, I need clarification on how the specific convergence rates are derived from the theorem, and I am concerned about the fairness of the empirical comparison in the matrix factorization experiment. Addressing these points, along with the noted inconsistencies and notation issues, would significantly strengthen the paper.
