This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). Notably, RRE is a standard numerical integration tool that has seen successful applications to numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs) in recent years. One of the paper's strengths is that it bridges the gap between the MCMC literature and the SDE literature, potentially benefiting both communities.

The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical results on the convergence of this algorithm, and provide arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves upon vanilla SGLD. They also present numerical results demonstrating impressive improvements on toy examples and modest yet consistent improvements on large-scale examples. Overall, this is a solid paper with clear writing, interesting links between two somewhat disconnected fields, and good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm.

The paper is well-written, easy to read, and appears to be technically sound. The method is a fairly straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I believe practitioners will adopt these methods, although they are unlikely to revolutionize current practice. The paper's contributions, including the theoretical results and empirical performance, are valuable additions to the field.

Minor comments: I found Fig 1a slightly misleading, as it does not represent the empirical estimated posterior distribution. Instead, it depicts a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance. This should be clarified or emphasized for readers who may not be aware of this distinction. Additionally, Fig 2a could benefit from a brief discussion on why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the stepsize decreases. This phenomenon may seem counter-intuitive to some readers, and a short explanation would enhance the figure's clarity.
