- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD, proposing SGRRLD to reduce bias from gradient-estimation noise.  
- Theoretical results claim SGRRLD has asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and faster convergence under certain conditions.  
- Achieves faster convergence at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains.  
- Algorithm is well-described and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments clearly show SGRRLD’s bias-reduction benefits.  
- MovieLens real-data experiment shows only marginal RMSE improvement; results are debatable.  
- More real-data experiments are needed to convincingly demonstrate practical bias reduction.  
- Questions about theorem conditions: whether potential U can be multimodal and what assumptions are required for both theorems to hold.  
- Unclear how chain correlations (especially under correlated Gaussian noise) affect error reduction; authors should investigate chain correlation in real-data runs.  
- Experimental-setup concerns: need to compare computational time fairly and compare SGRRLD to running multiple SGLD chains in parallel.  
- Comparison choices incomplete: should compare SGRRLD (γ, γ/2) against SGLD with step size γ/2 run in parallel to assess whether improvements persist.
