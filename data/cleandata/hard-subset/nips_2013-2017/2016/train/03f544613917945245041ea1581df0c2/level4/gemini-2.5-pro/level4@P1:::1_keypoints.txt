- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to large-scale SG-MCMC, focusing on SGLD.  
- Bridges MCMC and numerical SDE literatures, providing cross-disciplinary benefit.  
- Proposes the SGRRLD algorithm with theoretical guarantees (central limit theorem, non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds).  
- Method is simple to implement, computationally convenient, and naturally parallelizable.  
- Empirical results: large gains on synthetic Gaussian model and modest but consistent improvements on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Weakness: improvement is incremental—enhances existing first-order SG-MCMC rather than introducing a new paradigm.  
- Minor: Figure 1a may mislead—plots a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance, not the empirical sample distribution; clarify caption/text.  
- Minor: Figure 2’s increased bias/MSE at very small step sizes should be explained (likely due to fixed K and the O(1/(Kγ)) term from Theorem 2).
