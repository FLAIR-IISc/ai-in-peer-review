- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, running two parallel SGLD chains with step-sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to cancel leading bias; provides CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds and shows improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³), supported by experiments.

- Strength — Originality: Novel and well-motivated application of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC for reducing discretization bias.

- Strength — Technical quality: Solid theoretical development including asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds demonstrating faster convergence.

- Strength — Clarity and practicality: Method is simple to implement, naturally parallel, and experimentally efficient on multi-core architectures.

- Weakness — Fairness of computational comparison: Wall-clock plots use parallel implementation but SGRRLD performs three gradient updates per outer iteration (1 for γ, 2 for γ/2), so comparisons should also be made by total gradient computations or serialized runtime (SGLD would be ~3× faster per iteration on a single core).

- Weakness — Figure 3 discrepancy: Text claims optimal bias rate at α = 1/3, but the plot shows α = 0.2 with lowest (noisy) bias; clarify the mismatch and the source of fluctuations.

- Weakness — Error-term dominance clarity: Authors state 1/(Kγ) dominates error for small γ in SGRRLD; explain why a similar effect is not observed/discussed for baseline SGLD.

- Weakness — Derivation detail: Provide a brief derivation of the stated optimal rates (γk ∝ k⁻α leading to O(K⁻((1−α)/2)∧(2α)) and optimal α = 1/5) to make results more self-contained.

- Minor — Notation/symbols: Explain use of ϑ vs θ for continuous vs discrete processes; define RR acronym and ΓK before use; use bold for vectors to improve readability.

- Minor — Assumptions clarity: Explicitly state smoothness assumptions for f (e.g., C⁹ mentioned in sketch) up front.

- Minor — Figures/captions: Verify Figure 1(b) and line references—text mentions MSE vs dimension but figure appears to show Bias vs Time.
