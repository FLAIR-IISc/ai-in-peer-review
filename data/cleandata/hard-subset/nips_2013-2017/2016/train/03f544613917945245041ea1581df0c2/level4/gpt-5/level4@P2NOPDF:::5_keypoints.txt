- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes to cancel leading-order bias.
- Method is conceptually simple and straightforward to implement.
- Two-chain setup is trivially parallelizable for modern hardware.
- Provides theoretical analysis establishing asymptotic consistency and argues for improved convergence via bias cancellation.
- Empirical results indicate solid practical performance.
- Novelty is limited: mainly repurposes a known extrapolation technique; needs clearer discussion of when it outperforms higher-order discretizations.
- Missing experimental comparisons to high-order SG-MCMC integrators to validate efficiency and practical trade-offs (variance amplification, computational overhead).
- Inconsistency in step-size schedule presentation—clarify exact schedules, coupling for extrapolation, and theory vs. experimental assumptions (decreasing vs. constant step sizes).
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not align with Equation (5); resolve the mismatch and provide a derivation or correction.
- Lacks practical guidance on choosing step-size pairs and combining weights, and on sensitivity to minibatch noise since extrapolation can increase variance.
- Overall: interesting and practical contribution with promising theory and experiments; addressing inconsistencies and adding comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper.
