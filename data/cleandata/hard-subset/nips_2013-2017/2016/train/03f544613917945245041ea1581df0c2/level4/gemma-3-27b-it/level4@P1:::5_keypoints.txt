- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains in parallel to reduce SG-MCMC bias and enable communication-free parallelization.  
- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and provides bias and MSE bounds with faster convergence than standard SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical results: synthetic and real-data experiments support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Novelty: novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC.  
- Parallelizability: well-suited for large-scale problems due to straightforward parallel implementation.  
- Writing: generally clear and well-explained technical presentation.  
- Weakness — comparisons: lacks direct empirical comparison to higher-order integrators that can achieve similar convergence rates.  
- Weakness — step size: discussion of step size selection is unclear and appears inconsistent.  
- Weakness — consistency: minor discrepancy between Algorithm 1 (supplement) and Equation (5) (main text) needs resolution.  
- Acceptance case: novel, theoretically rigorous, empirically validated, and parallelizable—recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Rejection case: practical utility weakened by missing comparison to higher-order integrators and minor inconsistencies.
