- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs to reduce discretization bias.  
- Builds on prior RR work for SDEs and extends it to stochastic-gradient settings relevant for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Provides solid theoretical analysis: a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing faster convergence (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and large-scale matrix factorization supports theoretical claims.  
- Method is well-suited to parallel architectures since the two chains run independently, reducing wall-clock time in multi-core settings.  
- The RR scheme is general and appears applicable to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGHMC) under technical conditions.  
- Experimental cost concern: SGRRLD uses ~3× the gradient evaluations per slow-chain step, so wall-clock gains vanish on single-core systems — need performance vs gradient-evaluations comparison.  
- Clarity issue: derivation of optimal step-size exponent α (e.g., α = 1/5 for MSE) should be shown in more detail in main text or appendix.  
- Experimental inconsistency: Figure 3 suggests α = 0.2 gives lowest bias despite text claiming optimal α = 1/3; authors should clarify and explain fluctuations.  
- Small-γ behavior: when γ is very small the 1/(Kγ) term dominates the error — authors should compare this effect to standard SGLD to assess relative severity.  
- Minor fixes: define RR on first use, and introduce Γ_K before using it.  
- Minor fixes: state smoothness conditions required for test function f in theorems.  
- Minor fixes: correct Figure 1(b) caption (text vs figure mismatch), clarify use of ϑ vs θ for continuous vs discrete processes, and consider consistent vector notation (e.g., boldface).
