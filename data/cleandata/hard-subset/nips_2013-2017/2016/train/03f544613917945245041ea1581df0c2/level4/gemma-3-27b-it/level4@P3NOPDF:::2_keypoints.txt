- Paper proposes SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical and empirical claims: SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than standard SGLD.  
- Algorithm description is clear and implementation-friendly.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly show bias reduction.  
- MovieLens experiments show only marginal test RMSE improvement and unclear statistical significance.  
- More extensive real-world dataset evaluations are needed to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Theoretical results are notable: faster convergence under specific conditions but require running two parallel Markov chains.  
- Reviewer asks whether the matrix U in Theorems 1 and 2 can be multi-modal and requests clarification of required conditions.  
- Question about how gradient noise correlation and chain decorrelation affect error reduction and the role of Gaussian noise correlation.  
- Suggest reporting empirical correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments to understand behavior.  
- Computational-cost claim needs clarification: parallel SGRRLD vs. running two parallel SGLD chains should be directly compared.  
- Experimental comparisons should include SGLD with step size γ/2 (not just γ) for a fairer bias comparison.  
- Overall: promising theoretical approach with solid synthetic results, but empirical evidence on real data is currently insufficient; address theoretical and experimental questions to strengthen the paper.
