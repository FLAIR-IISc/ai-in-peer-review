To reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC), the authors propose a novel method called stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, an off-the-shelf tool for stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Specifically, two chains are run in parallel without communication in SG-RR-MCMC. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent. Empirical results are also satisfactory, showcasing the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) is interesting and yields promising empirical results. The efficiency of SG-RR-MCMC can be theoretically validated through its improved convergence rate. Notably, this enhanced convergence rate can also be achieved using high-order integrators; however, the authors do not provide a comparison with such integrators in their experiments.

Despite the method's merits, there are some inconsistencies in the manuscript. Specifically, the authors make an inconsistent statement about the step size, which warrants clarification. Additionally, Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not align with Equation (5), which may cause confusion for readers.
