- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) and proves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD under certain conditions.  
- The approach builds on RR extrapolation for SDEs and is adapted to SG-MCMC; theoretical results claim faster convergence for a given step size.  
- Improvement requires running two parallel Markov chains, increasing computational work but argued to be parallelizable.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and appears straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian Model experiments show clear bias reduction for SGRRLD over SGLD.  
- MovieLens real-data results are weak/inconclusive, showing only slight improvement in test RMSE.  
- Paper needs more extensive real-world experiments to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Reviewer did not check proofs in detail and asks what precise conditions on the potential U are required (e.g., applicability to multimodal potentials).  
- Reviewer requests more intuition on how combining two chains reduces error order despite likely fast decorrelation from uncorrelated gradient noise; asks if chain correlation was measured empirically.  
- Experimental baseline may be unfair: runtime argument ignores the stronger baseline of two independent parallel SGLD chains averaged for the same wall-clock time.  
- Comparison omits SGLD run with the smaller step size γ/2, which would lower bias; fair comparisons should include two parallel SGLD chains both at γ/2.
