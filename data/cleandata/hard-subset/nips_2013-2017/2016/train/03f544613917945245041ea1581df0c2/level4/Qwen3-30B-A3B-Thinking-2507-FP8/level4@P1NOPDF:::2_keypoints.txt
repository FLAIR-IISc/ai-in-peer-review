- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from gradient estimation noise.  
- Theoretical claims: SGRRLD achieves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and faster convergence for the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and implementation is straightforward.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments effectively demonstrate bias reduction.  
- MovieLens real-data results show only marginal RMSE improvement with questionable statistical significance.  
- Computational-cost comparison is problematic: a fair baseline is two parallel SGLD chains within the same time budget, not a single SGLD chain.  
- Current comparison to SGLD with step size γ/2 is insufficient; should compare to two parallel SGLD chains (including γ/2 run in parallel).  
- Reviewer did not verify technical proofs and raises two theoretical concerns: unspecified conditions on the potential U required by the theorems.  
- Key theoretical question: can U be multi-modal, and are the theorems valid in multi-modal settings?  
- Unclear mechanism: how RR extrapolation suppresses error order given likely rapid decorrelation of two chains and uncorrelated gradient noise.  
- Request to report whether persistent correlations between the two chains were observed in real-data experiments.  
- Strong recommendation for more extensive real-data experiments and statistically significant results; without these, practical relevance remains uncertain.  
- Concrete suggested revisions: (1) run fair comparisons against two parallel SGLD chains, (2) add more real-data experiments showing consistent improvements, (3) clarify theoretical assumptions on U (especially multi-modality).
