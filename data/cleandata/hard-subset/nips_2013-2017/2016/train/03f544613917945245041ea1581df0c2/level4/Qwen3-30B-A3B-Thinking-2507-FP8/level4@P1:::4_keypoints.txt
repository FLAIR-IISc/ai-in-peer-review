- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining outputs.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD achieves MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}), improving over SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Key insight: RR extrapolation improves convergence rates without needing higher-order integrators, building on Chen et al. (NIPS 2015).  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundations with formal convergence analysis.  
- Strength: straightforward implementation requiring minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC code.  
- Strength: empirical results show improvements on synthetic and real-world matrix factorization tasks.  
- Strength: parallel nature suits distributed architectures and extends to other SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Major weakness: inconsistency between theory and experiments in Figure 3 regarding optimal step-size exponent α (text vs plots and fluctuating curves) needing clarification.  
- Major weakness: Figure 5 wall-clock comparisons do not account for SGRRLD’s parallel implementation (true time should be ≈2/3 of SGLD for equivalent work); replotting with correct normalization required.  
- Major weakness: claim that 1/(Kγ) term dominates bias/MSE for small γ is not observed for SGLD (Figure 2); explanation needed for discrepancy.  
- Minor issues: notation and presentation errors (ϑt vs θt, RR undefined on first mention, Γ_K undefined, unspecified smoothness for f, Figure 1(b) caption mismatch, inconsistent vector bolding).  
- Overall verdict: promising and potentially strong contribution if theoretical/experimental inconsistencies and time-normalization issues are resolved.
