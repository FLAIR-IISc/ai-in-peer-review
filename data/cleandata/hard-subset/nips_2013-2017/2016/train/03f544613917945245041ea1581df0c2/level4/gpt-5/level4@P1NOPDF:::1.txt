This paper applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to “big-data MCMC” methods, focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard tool in numerical integration that has recently seen successful use for numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A notable contribution of this work is that it helps bridge the MCMC and SDE literatures, which should be beneficial to both communities.

The authors introduce an SGLD-RRE method, establish convergence results for the new algorithm, and provide arguments delineating when SGLD-RRE improves upon vanilla SGLD. They also present numerical experiments. The improvements on toy problems are striking, while on large-scale tasks the gains are modest but consistent.

Overall, this is a solid, clearly written, and technically sound paper. The proposed method is a fairly straightforward—and therefore computationally convenient—application of RRE to SGLD. I expect practitioners to adopt these techniques, even if they are unlikely to revolutionize current practice.

Minor comments:
- Figure 1a: The plotted density is not the empirical posterior distribution; it is a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance used as plug-in estimates. Please clarify and emphasize this to avoid misleading readers.
- Figure 2a: It would be helpful to discuss why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the step size shrinks. This is counterintuitive and likely reflects the interaction among stochastic gradient noise, finite-time discretization error, and the extrapolation procedure. A brief explanation would improve the reader’s understanding.
