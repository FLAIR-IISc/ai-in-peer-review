- Proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC convergence by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides comprehensive asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses.  
- Experiments on a synthetic Gaussian model validate the theoretical bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments on real data show practical gains, outperforming standard SGLD.  
- Paper is well-structured and situates the work within existing literature.  
- Concern: Novelty may be limited because numerical integrators' impact on SG-MCMC and Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions have been previously explored ([10], [23]).  
- Concern: Communication cost of synchronizing the two chains is not acknowledged, potentially limiting practical utility.  
- Concern: Experimental comparison fairness is unclear—uncertain whether SGRRLD and SGLD used equivalent computing resources or how resources were split between the two chains.  
- Concern: Given gradient computation dominates cost, it’s unclear how partial resource use per chain in SGRRLD results in reduced wall-clock time versus full-resource SGLD.  
- Suggestion: Compare to two embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains without communication as an alternative baseline.  
- Minor: Redundant mentions of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" on lines 25 and 70.
