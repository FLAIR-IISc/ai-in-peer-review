To reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC), the authors propose a novel method, stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-established technique for solving stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Specifically, SG-RR-MCMC runs two chains in embarrassingly parallel fashion, without requiring communication between them. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent, and support this with satisfactory empirical results. 

The idea is interesting and empirically effective, appearing as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The theoretical analysis suggests improved convergence rates, which would validate the efficiency of the method. However, a comparison to higher-order integrators—which could potentially achieve similar results—is missing from the experimental evaluation. 

Furthermore, there is an inconsistency regarding the step size, as noted previously. In addition, Algorithm 1 (in the supplementary material) does not align with Equation (5) in the main text.
