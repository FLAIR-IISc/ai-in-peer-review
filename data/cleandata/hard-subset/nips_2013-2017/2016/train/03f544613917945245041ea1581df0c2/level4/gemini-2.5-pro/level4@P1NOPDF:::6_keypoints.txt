- Proposes SGRRLD: use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel SG-MCMC chains (different step sizes, correlated Gaussian noise) and provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees; experiments on synthetic and real data show benefits over SGLD.

- Well-written paper that situates itself with prior work and makes a valid contribution.

- Main concern 1 — Incremental novelty: technique (effect of integrators, Richardson-Romberg for invariant measures) is known; contribution is mainly applying it to SG-MCMC and should be framed as incremental.

- Main concern 2 — Practicality and experimental fairness: certain design choices may limit practical utility and experimental comparisons need to be more rigorous.

- Communication cost: running two parallel chains with correlated Brownian motion requires per-step communication that is not accounted for and could be significant.

- Resource allocation: unclear whether experiments gave single SGLD the same total computational budget as the two SGRRLD chains; how resources were split should be clarified.

- Wall-clock time: since gradient computation dominates cost, it is unclear how two chains with divided gradient budget outperform a single full-budget SGLD in wall-clock time; implementation details needed.

- Missing baseline: experiments omit a strong baseline of two independent, embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains averaged together — this comparison is crucial.

- Minor comment: the full name "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is spelled out twice; use the acronym on the second instance.
