The authors propose a novel method, stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), to reduce the bias of stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC). This approach leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a readily available tool for stochastic differential equations (SDEs). Notably, SG-RR-MCMC can run two chains in parallel without communication, making it an efficient algorithm. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC. Empirical results also show promising performance. 

The idea of combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD) is intriguing and empirically effective. The efficiency of SG-RR-MCMC can be theoretically validated by providing a better convergence rate. However, this can also be achieved using high-order integrators, which the authors did not compare to in their experiments. 

Despite the method's potential, there are a few inconsistencies in the paper. Specifically, the statement about the step size is inconsistent. Furthermore, Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not align with Equation (5). These discrepancies should be addressed to strengthen the paper's overall quality and clarity.
