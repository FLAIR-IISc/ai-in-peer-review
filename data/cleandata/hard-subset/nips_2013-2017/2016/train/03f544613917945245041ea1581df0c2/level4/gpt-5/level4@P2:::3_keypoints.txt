- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running correlated chains at γ and γ/2 to achieve O(γ^2) bias and improved MSE, with consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bounds; experiments corroborate theory.

- Method detail: Runs two chains in parallel with Gaussian noise correlated as Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2 and forms an RR estimator from the average of small-step estimates minus the large-step estimate.

- Theoretical claim: Shows SGRRLD attains MSE O(K^−4/5) with optimal scheduling, improving on SGLD’s O(K^−2/3) and matching higher-order integrators while using first-order updates.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on SGLD/SGHMC and RR/SDE discretization literature, novelly adapting RR extrapolation and correlated increments to the stochastic-gradient setting.

- Strength: Simplicity and generality — drop-in wrapper for first-order SG-MCMC requiring minimal code changes.

- Strength: Strong theory — consistency, CLT, finite-time bounds, bias–variance trade-offs, and variance-minimizing correlation derived.

- Strength: Practicality and parallelism — naturally parallelizable with empirical gains over SGLD and parity with higher-order methods in some regimes.

- Strength: Potential impact — broadly applicable across SG-MCMC variants under mild conditions.

- Limitation: Assumptions may be strong (smoothness and Poisson equation solutions) and might not hold for non-smooth ML models.

- Limitation: Practical guidance lacking on minibatch correlation strategies and step-size tuning (fixed vs. decaying).

- Limitation: Experimental scope could be expanded — stronger baselines (e.g., SGD) and convergence-to-accuracy comparisons for matrix factorization are missing.

- Limitation: Computational overhead — one chain runs twice as often; wall-clock trade-offs and resource usage discussion could be deeper.

- Limitation: Multilevel/multi-step RR extensions are mentioned but not empirically evaluated.

- Quality: Theoretical results are clear, technically sound, and well-referenced; variance control via correlated noise is well-motivated and aligns with asymptotic rates.

- Clarity: Paper is well organized and written, but would benefit from an early algorithm box/pseudocode for SGRRLD.

- Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation with careful variance control to SG-MCMC and derivation of finite-time rates in this stochastic-gradient context.

- Significance: Provides a low-overhead route to higher accuracy in SG-MCMC, matching higher-order methods and suitable for parallel/distributed execution.

- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general, parallelizable method with strong theory and empirical evidence bridging numerical analysis and SG-MCMC.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited practical tuning exploration and potentially restrictive assumptions limiting applicability.

- Key questions: Need precise conditions for RR bias reduction across SG-MCMC variants, applicability to subsampling/approximate MH, and behavior in distributed/asynchronous settings.

- Implementation suggestions: Clarify best practices for minibatch correlation, noise/gradient synchronization, and recommended step-size schedules for finite budgets.

- Experimental suggestions: Run longer matrix factorization experiments, include SGD baseline, and evaluate multilevel RR or multi-chain strategies for resource utilization.

- Presentation suggestion: Add an algorithm box for SGRRLD and clarify implementation details for noise correlation and minibatch synchronization.

- Overall assessment: Clear, well-executed, and likely acceptable with minor additions on practical guidance and broader comparisons; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
