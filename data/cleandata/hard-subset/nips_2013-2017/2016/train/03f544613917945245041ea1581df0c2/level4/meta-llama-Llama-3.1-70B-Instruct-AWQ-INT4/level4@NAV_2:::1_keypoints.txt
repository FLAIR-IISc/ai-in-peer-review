- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SGLD to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Links MCMC and SDE literatures, providing cross-community benefit.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows SGRRLD attains higher convergence rates than SGLD and other first-order SG-MCMC methods in finite-time and asymptotic regimes.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate reduced bias and improved convergence versus SGLD.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes detailed algorithmic and theoretical explanations.  
- Strengths: novel RRE application, rigorous theory, and convincing empirical validation.  
- Discusses practical aspects: potential for parallelization and sensitivity to step-size choice.  
- Minor critique: Figure 1a should clarify it shows a Gaussian with empirical posterior mean and variance.  
- Minor critique: Figure 2a needs a brief explanation why bias may not decrease monotonically with step size.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant contribution, solid theory, clear presentation, and practical impact for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Rejection caveats: not a revolutionary advance, essentially a straightforward RRE application to SGLD, and requires minor figure/text clarifications.
