This paper proposes stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC) to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC methods. The approach leverages Richardson–Romberg extrapolation, a standard tool for SDEs, and runs two chains in embarrassingly parallel fashion without communication. Theoretical analysis is provided, showing asymptotic consistency, and the empirical results are satisfactory.

The idea is interesting and can be viewed as a simple combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with SGLD. The method appears effective empirically, and the theory suggests an improved convergence rate. However, a comparable convergence improvement can also be achieved by using higher-order integrators; the lack of experimental comparison with such integrators is a notable omission.

I have two additional concerns about clarity and correctness:
- The paper contains inconsistent statements regarding the step size. Please clarify the step-size assumptions/schedules used in the theory and in the experiments, and ensure they are consistent across the paper.
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement is not consistent with Equation (5). This discrepancy should be resolved or carefully explained.

Overall, the contribution is promising and well motivated, but the issues above should be addressed to strengthen the paper’s clarity and empirical validation.
