- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains in parallel (step-size ratio 2:1) and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Builds on SGLD (Welling & Teh 2011) and RR extrapolation analysis for SDEs (Talay & Tubaro 1991).  
- Theoretical results: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and improved non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Proven MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5), improving on SGLD’s O(K^-2/3).  
- Empirical results on synthetic Gaussian and large-scale MovieLens matrix factorization show SGRRLD outperforms SGLD for equal compute.  
- Key contribution: simple bias reduction without requiring complex higher-order integrators.  
- Parallel design makes the method practically appealing for distributed large-scale applications.  
- Method generalizes beyond SGLD (demonstrated for SGHMC as well).  
- Major weakness: no empirical comparison with higher-order integrator methods (e.g., Chen et al., 2015) to support the main claim.  
- Inconsistency in step-size descriptions (abstract vs. main text) could confuse practical implementation.  
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement appears inconsistent with Equation (5) and needs correction.  
- Some technical details (implementation of correlated Brownian motions, Eq. 8) require clarification.  
- Overall assessment: strong theory and novelty, mostly clear writing, but acceptance recommended only after major revisions to fix inconsistencies and add missing empirical comparisons.
