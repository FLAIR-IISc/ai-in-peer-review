

To mitigate bias in SG-MCMC, the authors introduce a novel approach termed stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC). This method leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation—a standard technique for stochastic differential equations—by running two chains in an embarrassingly parallel manner without inter-chain communication. Theoretical analysis establishes asymptotic consistency for SG-RR-MCMC, and empirical results demonstrate adequate performance. The core idea is interesting and empirically validated, though it can be interpreted as a straightforward combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. Theoretical efficiency is supported by an improved convergence rate, though this improvement may also be achievable via high-order integrators (the authors do not include a comparative experiment with such integrators). Additionally, an inconsistent statement regarding step size is noted. Furthermore, Algorithm 1 (supplement) is inconsistent with Equation (5).
