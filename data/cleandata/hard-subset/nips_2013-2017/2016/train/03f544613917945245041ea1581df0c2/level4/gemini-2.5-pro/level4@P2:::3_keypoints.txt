- Problem: SG-MCMC methods suffer asymptotic bias from first-order integrators and noisy gradients, preventing convergence to the true posterior.  
- Proposal: Introduce SGRRLD which uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 (two steps per one) and combining estimators.  
- Estimator: Form linear combination 2*(estimate from γ/2 chain) − (estimate from γ chain) to cancel leading-order bias.  
- Variance control: Correlating the injected Gaussian noise across chains is crucial to keep the combined estimator's variance low.  
- Theoretical results: SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a CLT, has bias O(γ²), and MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵).  
- Improvement vs SGLD: Achieves higher-order theoretical accuracy while using simple first-order updates (vs SGLD bias O(γ), MSE O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Experiments: Empirical support provided on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization for recommendations.  
- Strength — significance: Addresses an important limitation of SG-MCMC with potentially broad impact.  
- Strength — simplicity/practicality: Easy to implement, minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC, and parallel-friendly.  
- Strength — novelty: Novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC and analysis of correlated noise.  
- Strength — theoretical rigor: Thorough convergence analysis with non-asymptotic bounds and CLT.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-written and clearly organized presentation.  
- Weakness — applicability: Unclear precise conditions for applicability (e.g., need for weak error expansion) and extension to other approximate MCMC schemes.  
- Weakness — computational utilization: Parallel scheme can leave one processor idle; alternatives (e.g., additional chains) and their variance effects are not discussed.  
- Weakness — mini-batch correlation: Impact of correlating mini-batches between chains on variance is not fully analyzed or recommended.  
- Weakness — experiments: Would benefit from a non-Bayesian (SGD) baseline and longer runs to show final convergence differences.  
- Weakness — presentation: Add a pseudocode algorithm box to improve accessibility for practitioners.
