- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to stochastic gradient MCMC to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Extends Talay and Tubaro's Richardson-Romberg results to the stochastic gradient setting and connects SG-MCMC to numerical SDE methods.
- Shows theoretical MSE convergence of O(K^{-4/5}), improving on SGLD's O(K^{-2/3}).
- Provides rigorous asymptotic and non-asymptotic proofs, including Theorem 2 with superior bias and MSE bounds.
- Demonstrates empirical support on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization tasks.
- Explains the necessity of two parallel chains and correlated Brownian increments to control variance in extrapolation.
- Experimental results show dramatic gains on synthetic data and modest but significant improvements on large-scale tasks.
- Implementation and parallelization details for running the two chains are practical and well-explained.
- Paper is clear and well-structured, with straightforward presentation of methods and proofs.
- Contribution is practically significant and readily applicable to scalable Bayesian inference.
- Originality is moderate: a straightforward application of a known numerical technique but novel in SG-MCMC context with new convergence analysis.
- Main weakness is limited perceived novelty for readers familiar with numerical SDE methods or SG-MCMC.
- Minor presentation issue: figure clarity (Fig. 1a) needs a brief clarification in the main text.
- Recommendation: accept — solid technical contribution likely to be adopted by practitioners.
