- Proposes Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation via two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise to accelerate convergence.  
- Establishes both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for the method.  
- Experiments validate theoretical bias and MSE bounds on synthetic Gaussian models and show practical gains on large-scale matrix factorization versus standard SGLD.  
- Presentation is clear and situates the work within prior literature.  
- Major concern: novelty appears incremental because integrator analysis is covered in [10] and RR extrapolation for invariant distributions in [23].  
- Paper must clarify how the parallel-chain, correlated-noise implementation and non-asymptotic guarantees go beyond [10] and [23].  
- Practical viability issue: communication cost of maintaining correlated noise between chains is unaddressed and unrealistically assumed zero.  
- Authors should quantify communication costs (bandwidth, latency) and analyze impact on wall-clock time.  
- Experimental setup unclear: need to state whether SGRRLD uses double compute resources or splits resources, and how this affects fairness of comparisons.  
- Require comparison against an embarrassingly parallel baseline of two independent SGLD chains to isolate RR extrapolation benefits from communication overhead.  
- Request an implementation table specifying hardware, per-chain resource allocation, wall-clock time per iteration, and communication protocol/cost.  
- Minor issues: redundant definition of ULA and typo "pracitcal" → "practical."  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but incremental and practically unclear; recommend major revision pending clarifications on novelty and communication/experimental details.
