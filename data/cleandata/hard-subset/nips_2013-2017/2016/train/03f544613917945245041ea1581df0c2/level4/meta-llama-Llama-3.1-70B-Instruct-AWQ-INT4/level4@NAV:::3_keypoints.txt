- Paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC: run two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2 with correlated noise) and subtract estimates to reduce bias.

- Method reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and also improves MSE, with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.

- Simplicity and broad applicability to various SG-MCMC methods are emphasized as strengths.

- Novelty: RR extrapolation is standard in numerical analysis but novel for ML and SG-MCMC communities.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Question: Are there additional conditions beyond the stated bias expansion (π̂γ(f)=π(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2)) required for the technique to hold for all SG-MCMC methods?

- Question: Can the approach be extended to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH tests, distributed SGLD)?

- Question: Could using three chains (γ, γ/2, γ/2) run for equal lengths avoid wasted computation, and how would that affect estimator variance?

- Question: Is it possible to generalize the method to more than two step sizes for higher-order extrapolation?

- Question: The role of correlated injected noise and batch sharing on variance is unclear—does using the same minibatch for corresponding iterations minimize variance?

- Experimental suggestion: Run the matrix factorization experiment until convergence and compare to SGD to better demonstrate advantages.

- Practical suggestion: Include an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
