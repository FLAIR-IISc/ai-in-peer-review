This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, referred to as Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC methods. The SGRRLD algorithm applies the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm, which is a popular SG-MCMC method. The RR extrapolation involves running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and combining their estimates to reduce the bias.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the SGRRLD algorithm, including a central limit theorem and bounds on the non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error (MSE). The results show that SGRRLD achieves a faster convergence rate than SGLD, with a bias and MSE of order O(K^(-2/5)) and O(K^(-4/5)), respectively, where K is the number of iterations.

The paper also presents experimental results on both synthetic and real data sets. The synthetic experiments demonstrate the advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD in reducing the bias and MSE. The real data experiments, which involve a large-scale matrix factorization problem, show a slight improvement in the test RMSE of SGRRLD over SGLD.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The SGRRLD algorithm is a novel and interesting application of the RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC methods.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides a clear understanding of the algorithm's properties.
* The experimental results demonstrate the potential benefits of SGRRLD over SGLD.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The experimental results on real data sets are limited, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive experiments on various data sets.
* The comparison with SGLD in the real data experiments is not entirely fair, as SGLD could also be run with multiple parallel chains within the same time. It would be interesting to see a comparison of SGRRLD with SGLD run with multiple chains.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the conditions required for the theoretical results to hold, particularly with regards to the potential energy function U.
