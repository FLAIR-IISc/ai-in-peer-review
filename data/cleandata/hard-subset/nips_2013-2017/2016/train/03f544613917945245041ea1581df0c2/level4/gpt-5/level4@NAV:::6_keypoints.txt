- Proposes applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (higher-order numerical integrator) to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides both asymptotic and finite-time convergence analyses.  
- Experiments: synthetic Gaussian model validates bias and MSE bounds; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Overall technique and presentation are solid and well positioned relative to prior work.  
- Concern: Novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SGMCMC ([10]) and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions ([23]).  
- Concern: Practical utility and experimental fairness unclear—per-step communication cost of correlated Brownian motion is not discussed.  
- Concern: Unclear how compute resources were allocated between the two-chain SGRRLD and the single SGLD baseline; fairness of wall-clock/time comparisons is not demonstrated.  
- Request: Add implementation details and consider comparison to two independent SGLD chains run embarrassingly parallel (no communication).  
- Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is duplicated (lines 25 and 70).
