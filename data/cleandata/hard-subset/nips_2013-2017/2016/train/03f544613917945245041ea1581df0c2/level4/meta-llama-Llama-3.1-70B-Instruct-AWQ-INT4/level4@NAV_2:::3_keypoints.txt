- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Derives non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE for the algorithm.  
- Shows asymptotic bias = O(γ^2) and variance = O(γ^4) for SGRRLD.  
- Proves MSE convergence rate O(K−4/5), improving over SGLD’s O(K−2/3).  
- Claims SGRRLD achieves the theoretical accuracy of higher-order integrator–based methods.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data support theoretical improvements.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: novel SG-MCMC algorithm that reduces bias while controlling variance.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical treatment and finite-time/asymptotic guarantees.  
- Weakness: assumes the two required chains can be generated independently, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: missing empirical or theoretical comparison to other SG-MCMC methods using higher-order integrators.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with thorough analysis.  
- Originality assessment: novel contribution in reducing SG-MCMC bias.  
- Significance assessment: meaningful improvement in convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong theory, and improved convergence rates.  
- Rejection arguments: questionable independence assumption, no complexity analysis, no comparisons to higher-order SG-MCMC methods.
