The authors propose stochastic gradient Richardson–Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC) to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC. The method applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation, an off-the-shelf tool for SDEs. In particular, it runs two chains embarrassingly in parallel without communication. The theoretical analysis shows that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent, and the empirical results are satisfactory. The idea is interesting and appears effective based on the experiments. Conceptually, it can be viewed as a straightforward combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation and SGLD.

The efficiency gains are theoretically supported by an improved convergence rate. However, similar improvements can also be achieved with higher-order integrators; unfortunately, the experiments do not include a comparison against such integrators.
