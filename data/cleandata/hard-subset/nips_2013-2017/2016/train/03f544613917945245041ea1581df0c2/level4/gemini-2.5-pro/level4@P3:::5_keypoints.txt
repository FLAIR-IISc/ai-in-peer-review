- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining them with correlated noise to reduce bias.
- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Convergence improvement: shows MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD versus O(K⁻²/³) for standard SGLD.
- Variance control: uses correlated Brownian motions to control variance of the extrapolated estimator.
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments confirm bias/MSE and convergence-rate claims.
- Large-scale application: demonstrates significant wall-clock and accuracy gains on a matrix factorization movie-recommendation task.
- Generality: approach extends beyond SGLD (shown also for SGHMC/SGRRHMC).
- Practicality: method is simple to implement and inherently parallelizable, requiring no complex higher-order integrators.
- Originality: novel combination of RR extrapolation with stochastic-gradient MCMC, with rigorous analysis and empirical support.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear motivation and derivation.
- Minor reproducibility issue: inconsistency between Eq.(5) and Algorithm 1 regarding where the gradient for the second chain is evaluated.
- Minor guidance issue: step-size schedule discussion (constant vs. decreasing) and optimal constants (γ*b, γ*M) could be more unified for practitioners.
- Overall recommendation: strong case for acceptance due to novelty, rigorous theory, solid experiments, and practical utility.
- Main critique: only minor clarity/implementation details need fixing; no major methodological flaws.
