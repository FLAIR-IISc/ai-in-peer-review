- Proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two SG-MCMC chains (different step sizes, correlated Gaussian noise) to accelerate convergence.  
- Provides thorough asymptotic and non-asymptotic analysis and experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization that corroborate theoretical benefits over SGLD.  
- Well-motivated and well-executed work with strong literature context and connections to prior methods.  
- Novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23]; limitations should be acknowledged.  
- Practical applicability concern: parallel chains require per-step communication (correlated Brownian motion), but paper seems to ignore communication costs; this needs discussion.  
- Experimental comparison fairness unclear: allocation of computational resources between SGRRLD (two chains) and SGLD (one chain) is not specified and may bias results.  
- Unclear how splitting resources between chains (given gradient computation dominates cost) could reduce wall-clock time versus using full resources for a single SGLD chain.  
- Suggestion: add implementation details and evaluate alternative scenarios (e.g., two independent SGLD chains run in parallel without communication) to clarify performance trade-offs.  
- Minor: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is repeated and should be removed or rephrased for clarity.
