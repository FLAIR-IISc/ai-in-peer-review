- Proposes SGRRLD: use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation on two parallel SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show higher convergence rates than SGLD and first-order integrator extensions.
- Main strength: novel, well-explained use of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to improve SG-MCMC convergence.
- Writing, notation, and presentation are clear and well-organized.
- Quality of analysis and experiments is high; references are comprehensive and up-to-date.
- Lacks direct experimental comparison to methods using high-order integrators despite claims of matching their accuracy.
- Minor inconsistencies in the manuscript (e.g., step-size statement in Algorithm 1 vs. Equation (5)).
- Needs a more detailed discussion of computational cost and parallelization implications for large-scale use.
- Overall recommendation: accept, given strong originality, significance, and supporting theory/experiments.
