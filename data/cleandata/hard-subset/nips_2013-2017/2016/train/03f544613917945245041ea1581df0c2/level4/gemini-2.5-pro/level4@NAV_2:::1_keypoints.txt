- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step-size schedules and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Builds a bridge between large-scale Bayesian inference and numerical SDE RR extrapolation literature (e.g., Talay & Tubaro, Lemaire et al.).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- The theory predicts faster MSE convergence for SGRRLD (O(K⁻⁴/⁵)) versus standard SGLD (O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Empirical results on synthetic problems show substantial improvements; experiments on a large-scale matrix factorization task show consistent practical gains.  
- Method is simple, clearly described, and naturally suited to parallel execution.  
- Technical quality is high: claims are well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Significance: enables a first-order integrator to match accuracy of higher-order methods via extrapolation.  
- Originality: novel application and rigorous analysis of RR extrapolation in the SG-MCMC context, despite RR itself being classical.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental—direct application of an established numerical technique to SG-MCMC.  
- Weakness: gains on the large-scale movie recommendation task are more modest than on synthetic examples.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify Figure 1(a) caption that plotted Gaussians are parameterized by empirical mean/variance of posterior samples (not direct histograms/KDEs).  
- Minor suggestion: explicitly explain the U-shaped bias/MSE vs. step size in Figure 2 and the 1/(Kγ) trade-off where very small γ increases error for fixed K.
