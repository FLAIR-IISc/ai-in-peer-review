- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two correlated-noise chains (step γ and γ/2 with twice the steps) to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Shows bias reduction from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and corresponding MSE improvement theoretically.  
- Method is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and potentially applicable to many SG-MCMC variants.  
- Novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation in the SG-MCMC / ML context; paper is well-written and clear.  
- Question about generality: are there additional requirements beyond the π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) expansion for applicability to all SG-MCMC algorithms?  
- Ask whether the method extends to non-gradient approximate MCMC (e.g., subsampled Metropolis-Hastings) and distributed SGLD.  
- Computational efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long is wasteful; propose exploring a 3-chain equal-length setup and its variance implications.  
- Question about higher-order extrapolation: can more than two step sizes yield further bias reduction?  
- Concern about the role of noise correlation and mini-batch choices: does sharing mini-batches across corresponding iterations minimize variance?  
- Experimental concern: matrix factorization experiment needs longer runs to convergence and comparison to standard SGD to contextualize benefits.  
- Emphasize demonstrating advantage over SGD in scenarios where Bayesian treatment helps, and reporting final errors of SGRRLD/SGLD relative to SGD.  
- Suggest adding an algorithm box/pseudocode to improve accessibility and facilitate practitioner adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: promising contribution; accept with minor revisions pending clarifications and stronger empirical validation.
