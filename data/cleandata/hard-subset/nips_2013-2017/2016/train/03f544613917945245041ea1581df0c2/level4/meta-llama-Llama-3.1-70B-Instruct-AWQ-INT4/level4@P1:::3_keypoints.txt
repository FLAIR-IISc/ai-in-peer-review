- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions to reduce bias and MSE.  
- Well-motivated since SG-MCMC methods are popular but suffer from bias in large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Theoretical contributions include a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE under clear assumptions.  
- Technical analysis is sound, results convincing, and assumptions/conditions are well-explained.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and uses consistent notation.  
- Applying Richardson-Romberg to SG-MCMC is a novel and original idea despite the technique being known in numerical analysis.  
- Method has significant practical implications because it applies to a broad class of SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Suggest adding pseudo-code to improve accessibility for a wider audience.  
- Recommend more detailed analysis of how mini-batch correlation affects estimator variance.  
- Recommend comparison with other approximate MCMC methods (e.g., subsampling Metropolis-Hastings).  
- Suggest expanding discussion of potential real-world applications of the technique.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, given novelty, solid analysis, and practical relevance.
