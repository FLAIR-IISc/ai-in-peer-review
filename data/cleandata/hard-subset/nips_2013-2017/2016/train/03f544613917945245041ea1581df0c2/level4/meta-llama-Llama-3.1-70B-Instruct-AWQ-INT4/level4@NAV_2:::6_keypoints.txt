- Proposes SGRRLD: uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds for SGRRLD.  
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC/SGLD work and relates to use of higher-order integrators and previous Richardson-Romberg applications.  
- Clear presentation and thorough explanation of the algorithm and theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic and real-data experiments demonstrating practical utility.  
- Novelty concern: incremental contribution given existing analyses of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation for invariant distributions.  
- Practicality concern: assumption of zero communication cost between the two chains may be unrealistic.  
- Computational-cost concern: unclear how splitting resources across two chains reduces wall-clock time versus allocating full resources to a single SGLD, since gradients dominate cost.  
- Experimental fairness concern: comparisons may be unfair or under-specified; implementation/resource allocation details are missing.  
- Suggested additional experiments: compare to alternatives such as running two independent SGLD chains without communication.  
- Minor issues: duplicated term (“Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)”) and some references not properly cited.  
- Overall verdict: valid contribution but possibly incremental; recommend accept with minor revisions to address practicality, comparison details, and minor fixes.
