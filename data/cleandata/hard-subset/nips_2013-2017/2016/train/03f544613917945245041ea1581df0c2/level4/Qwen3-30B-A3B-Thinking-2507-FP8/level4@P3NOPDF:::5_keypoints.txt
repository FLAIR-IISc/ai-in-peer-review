- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation on two independent, parallel SG-MCMC chains to reduce discretization bias.  
- Theoretical contribution: establishes asymptotic consistency and shows bias reduction by a factor of two versus standard SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical results: show consistent bias reduction across datasets and models with minimal extra computational cost due to embarrassingly parallel implementation.  
- Conceptual strength: cleanly combines Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD in a simple, computationally efficient way.  
- Main technical issue 1: theory assumes different step sizes (h and h/2) for the two chains, but Algorithm 1 uses the same step size h, creating a contradiction that must be resolved.  
- Main technical issue 2: claims about improved convergence rate only address the bias term and lack comparison to high-order integrators that can also reduce bias.  
- Empirical omission: no experiments comparing SG-RR-MCMC to high-order SG-MCMC integrators (e.g., methods from Mandt et al., 2017), weakening contextualization of novelty and utility.  
- Writing precision issues: phrases like “off-the-shelf tool for SDE” are misleading; the paper should clarify that Richardson-Romberg targets discretization error of SG-MCMC derived from an SDE.  
- Presentation inconsistency: Equation (5) uses h and h/2 but Algorithm 1 uses identical step sizes, risking misinterpretation and undermining theoretical claims.  
- Recommendation: Minor revision contingent on (1) correcting the step-size inconsistency between theory and implementation, (2) adding a comparison with a high-order integrator, and (3) clarifying the relationship between Richardson-Romberg and SDE discretization.
