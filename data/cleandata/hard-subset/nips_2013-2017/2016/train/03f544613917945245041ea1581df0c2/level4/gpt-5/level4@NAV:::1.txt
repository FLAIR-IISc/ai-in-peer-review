This paper employs Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) in recent “big-data MCMC” methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a classical numerical integration technique that has recently seen successful applications to numerical SDEs; a nice aspect of this work is that it builds a bridge between the MCMC and SDE literatures, which may benefit both communities. The authors introduce an SGLD-RRE method, derive theoretical results on the convergence of the algorithm (including arguments for when SGLD-RRE improves on vanilla SGLD), and present numerical experiments. They show striking gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements on large-scale tasks. Overall it’s a solid, clearly written paper. As noted above, this is a strong submission, with interesting connections between two somewhat separate fields; meaningful theoretical results; and good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The paper is very clear and easy to follow and appears technically sound. The method is essentially a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, which is computationally convenient. I expect practitioners will adopt these methods, although they are unlikely to transform current practice.
