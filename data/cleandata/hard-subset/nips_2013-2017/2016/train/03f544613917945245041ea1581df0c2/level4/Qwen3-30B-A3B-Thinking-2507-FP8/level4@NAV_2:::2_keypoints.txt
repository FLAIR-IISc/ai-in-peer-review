- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Shows asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴), achieving MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) and improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Builds on prior NIPS work (Chen et al. 2015) to get higher-order accuracy without high computational cost and preserves parallelizability.  

Strengths:
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with formal proofs of asymptotic consistency, CLTs, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds (Theorems 1–2).  
- Assumptions on U are standard and well-motivated (smoothness, growth constraints).  
- Synthetic Gaussian experiments clearly validate bias reduction (variance bias from ≈1e-2 to ≈1e-4).  
- MovieLens experiments show practical efficiency gains (~2× speedup for equivalent RMSE).  
- Algorithm description and pseudo-code are exceptionally clear, including parallel chains and correlated Brownian increments.  
- Derivation of the RR extrapolation estimator is logical and well-presented.  
- Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC not previously explored at NIPS; minimal modification to SGLD and preserves parallelism.  
- Broad significance: addresses bias–variance trade-off, compatible with other SG-MCMC variants, and suitable for distributed architectures.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical results assume identical mini-batch sizes for both chains; practical impact of mini-batch correlation on bias is not empirically validated.  
- Analysis does not address multi-modal target distributions, a common SG-MCMC challenge.  
- Real-data comparison may be unfair: lacks benchmarking against two parallel SGLD chains at step size γ/2.  
- Supplementary should clarify the inter-chain correlation mechanism (Eq.7) in the main text for variance control.  
- MovieLens experiment setup (e.g., step size selection) needs clearer justification.

Pro/Con acceptance summary:
- Pro: Groundbreaking RR application to SG-MCMC, strong theory with tighter rates, clear implementation, and meaningful large-scale efficiency gains.  
- Con: Requires refined comparisons to parallel SGLD, more exploration of multi-modal targets, and clearer experimental justification; merits acceptance with minor revisions.
