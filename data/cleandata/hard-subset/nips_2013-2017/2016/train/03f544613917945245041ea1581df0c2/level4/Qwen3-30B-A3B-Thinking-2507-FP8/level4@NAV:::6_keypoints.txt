- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two correlated SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes.  
- Provides theoretical asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees (bias and MSE bounds).  
- Empirical validation on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization shows SGRRLD outperforming standard SGLD.  
- Strength: well-presented technique with solid contextualization in SGMCMC literature.  
- Concern 1 (novelty): contribution appears incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators in SG-MCMC [10] and use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions in [23].  
- Concern 2 (practicality/communication): requiring correlated Brownian noise forces per-step communication between chains, but the paper assumes zero communication cost—this undermines practical applicability.  
- Clarify resource allocation and fairness: whether SGLD uses combined resources of two SGRRLD chains, how resources are partitioned between SGRRLD chains, and how partial-resource chains reduce wall-clock time given gradient-dominated costs.  
- Recommend including an embarrassingly parallel two-chain SGLD baseline (no communication) for fair comparison.  
- Minor: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated (lines 25 and 70).
