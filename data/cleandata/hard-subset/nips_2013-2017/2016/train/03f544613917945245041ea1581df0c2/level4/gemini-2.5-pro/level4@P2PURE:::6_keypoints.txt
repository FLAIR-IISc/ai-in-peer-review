- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses with theoretical bias and MSE bounds.  
- Experiments on a synthetic Gaussian model validate theoretical bounds; large-scale matrix factorization shows practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Well-presented paper that effectively situates the work in prior literature and makes a solid contribution.  
- Concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analysis of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions [23].  
- Practicality concern: correlated noise requires per-step communication between chains, and the paper does not account for this communication cost.  
- Experimental comparison unclear: allocation of computational resources between SGRRLD’s two chains and the baseline SGLD is not specified, raising fairness questions.  
- Wall-clock time concern: since stochastic gradient computation dominates cost, splitting resources across two chains may not reduce wall-clock time; implementation details are missing.  
- Suggested baseline: compare against two independent, embarrassingly parallel SGLD chains to isolate RR extrapolation benefits from simply using twice the resources.  
- Minor comment: the full name "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is defined twice.
