- Paper proposes a Richardson-Romberg scheme to improve SG-MCMC convergence (bias and MSE) with minor implementation changes.  
- Methodology is straightforward to implement and requires only small modifications to standard SG-MCMC.  
- Strong theoretical results are presented supporting the proposed scheme.  
- Derivation of specific convergence rates from the theorem is questionable (raised as a “fatal flaw”).  
- Practical concern: on a single-core machine the method uses two chains and would take ≈3× longer than standard SG-MCMC.  
- Plots issue: Figure 5 should adjust SGLD time by factor 2/3, which would narrow the gap between SGLD and SGRRLD.  
- Matrix factorization experiment results raise practical concerns about performance and interpretation.  
- Figure 3 bias plot indicates optimal convergence at α≈0.2 (not 1/3) and shows large fluctuations inconsistent with the claimed rate.  
- Claim that 1/(Kγ) term dominates for very small step sizes is not observed empirically for SGLD.  
- Minor issues: Equation 1 uses an inconsistent symbol for θ.  
- Minor issues: Abbreviation “RR” is undefined (line 94).  
- Minor issues: Notation Γ_K is undefined (line 131).  
- Minor issues: Description of smooth function f is vague about the type of smoothness assumed.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1(b) caption/label error — bias is plotted versus time, not dimension; likely should be Figure 1(c).  
- Minor issues: Vectors should be typeset in boldface.
