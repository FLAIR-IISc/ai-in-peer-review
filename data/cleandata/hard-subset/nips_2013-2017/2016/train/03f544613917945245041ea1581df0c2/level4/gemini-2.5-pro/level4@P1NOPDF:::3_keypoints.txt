- Paper proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation on two correlated SG-MCMC chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, with correlated Gaussian noise α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2) to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Combined estimator achieves bias O(γ²) and corresponding MSE reduction versus O(γ) bias of single chains.  
- Method is simple to implement, requires no major changes to SG-MCMC, and appears broadly applicable across many SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to machine learning / SG-MCMC is new despite the technique being classical.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Question: Can the technique be applied to all SG-MCMC methods, or are additional conditions beyond π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²) required?  
- Question: Can the approach extend to other approximate MCMC schemes (non-stochastic-gradient, subsampled MH) or distributed SGLD?  
- Concern: Running one chain for twice as many iterations wastes compute; would running three equal-length chains (γ and two γ/2) be a viable alternative and how would it affect variance?  
- Question: Is there a straightforward extension to use more than two step sizes for higher-order bias correction?  
- Question: The required correlation of Gaussian noise is clear, but what is the impact of correlating mini-batches—should the same mini-batch be reused across the paired iterations to minimize variance?  
- Experiment critique: Matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to better evaluate practical advantages of the Bayesian approach.  
- Suggestion: Add an algorithm box/pseudocode early in the paper to improve accessibility for practitioners.
