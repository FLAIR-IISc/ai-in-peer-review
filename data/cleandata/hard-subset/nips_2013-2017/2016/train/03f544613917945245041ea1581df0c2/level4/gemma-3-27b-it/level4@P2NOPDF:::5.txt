This paper introduces stochastic gradient Richardson-Romberg MCMC (SG-RR-MCMC), a novel method designed to reduce bias in stochastic gradient Markov chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The core idea leverages Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-established technique for solving stochastic differential equations – to improve the accuracy of the MCMC sampling process. A key strength of SG-RR-MCMC is its simplicity and parallelizability; the method efficiently runs two independent chains in parallel without requiring communication between them.

The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the asymptotic consistency of SG-RR-MCMC. Supporting this analysis, the empirical results presented are encouraging and indicate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The method can be viewed as a straightforward yet impactful combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD). 

While the theoretical justification suggests an improved convergence rate compared to standard SGLD, this improvement stems from the use of a higher-order integration scheme. The paper lacks a direct empirical comparison to other high-order integrators, which would strengthen the claim of SG-RR-MCMC’s advantage. Such a comparison would help to establish whether the benefits observed are unique to the proposed method or simply a consequence of employing a higher-order approach.

A notable concern is an inconsistency in the discussion of step size, which requires clarification. More significantly, there is a discrepancy between Algorithm 1 (presented in the supplementary material) and Equation (5) in the main text. Resolving this inconsistency is critical for reproducibility and understanding the correct implementation of the algorithm.
