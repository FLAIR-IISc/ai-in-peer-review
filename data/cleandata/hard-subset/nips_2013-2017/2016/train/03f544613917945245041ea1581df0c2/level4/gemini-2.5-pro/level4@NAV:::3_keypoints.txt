- Paper introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Method runs two parallel chains: one with step size gamma, the other with gamma/2 run for twice the iterations, with correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Noise correlation is defined via alphak = (beta{2k-1} + beta{2k}) / sqrt(2) to couple the chains.  
- Combined estimator (Chain2 − Chain1) is proven to reduce bias to O(gamma^2) vs O(gamma) for each individual chain.  
- The authors also prove a corresponding reduction in mean squared error (MSE).  
- Strength: conceptually simple and easy to implement without changing underlying SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Strength: method appears broadly applicable to a wide class of SG-MCMC algorithms, suggesting high potential impact.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is novel in the ML context despite being a known numerical technique.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and concepts are clearly presented.  
- Question: need precise conditions for applicability beyond the stated expansion pigamma(f) = pi(f) + C * gamma + O(gamma^2).  
- Question: can the debiasing apply to non-stochastic-gradient approximate MCMC (e.g., subsampling Metropolis-Hastings, distributed SGLD)?  
- Concern: running one chain twice as long wastes computation; ask about using three simultaneous chains (one gamma, two gamma/2) to avoid idle time and effect on variance.  
- Question: is there a straightforward extension to more than two step sizes for higher-order corrections?  
- Question: unclear role of mini-batch correlation—would using the same mini-batches across paired iterations minimize variance or is it negligible?  
- Experiment critique: matrix factorization results should run to convergence and include comparison to standard SGD to better demonstrate Bayesian advantage and final error magnitudes.  
- Suggestion: include an algorithm box/pseudocode to make the method more accessible to practitioners.
