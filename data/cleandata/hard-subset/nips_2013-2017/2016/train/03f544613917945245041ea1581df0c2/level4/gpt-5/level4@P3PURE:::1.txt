This paper applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent “big-data MCMC” methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard tool in numerical integration that has recently been applied to numerical SDEs; a notable contribution here is the explicit connection it forges between the MCMC and SDE literatures, which should be valuable to both communities. The authors introduce an SGLD-RRE method, provide theoretical results on its convergence (including conditions under which SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD), and present numerical experiments. The method achieves impressive gains on toy problems and modest but consistent improvements on large-scale tasks.

Overall, this is a solid and clearly written paper with interesting cross-field links, meaningful theoretical results, and a practical algorithm whose implementation is straightforward—RRE is applied to SGLD in a way that is computationally convenient. The work appears technically sound. I expect practitioners will find SGLD-RRE useful, though it is unlikely to be transformative for current practice.

Minor comments:
- Figure 1a is slightly misleading as presented: it does not show the empirically estimated posterior distribution, but rather a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance substituted. Please clarify this explicitly to avoid confusion.
- Figure 2a: it would help to add a brief discussion of why the bias does not necessarily decrease as the step size decreases, since this behavior is counterintuitive for many readers.
