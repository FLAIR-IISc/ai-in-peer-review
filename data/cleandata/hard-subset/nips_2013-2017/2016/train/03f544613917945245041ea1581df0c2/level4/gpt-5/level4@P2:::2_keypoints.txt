- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD via two coupled chains (γ and γ/2) with correlated Gaussian increments, proving consistency, a CLT, non-asymptotic bounds, and improved MSE (O(K−4/5) vs O(K−2/3)); experiments on Gaussian model and MovieLens support claims and RR is illustrated for SGHMC.

- Relation to prior work: RR extrapolation is classical for SDEs; novelty is adapting RR to SG-MCMC and analyzing bias/variance trade-offs with correlated Brownian increments.

- Strength — simplicity/generalizability: Conceptually simple RR layering that is easy to implement and general across SG-MCMC methods.

- Strength — theoretical guarantees: Provides consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds; Brownian correlation rationale is well-motivated.

- Strength — practical potential: Achieves higher-order accuracy using first-order integrators and parallelizes naturally across the two chains.

- Strength — empirical validation: Synthetic Gaussian experiments and an SGHMC variant demonstrate bias and MSE improvements.

- Weakness — assumption scope: Theorems require strong smoothness (e.g., g ∈ C^9) and ergodicity; precise assumptions on U and L and implications for multimodality/high-dimensional posteriors need clarification.

- Weakness — stochastic gradient noise: Independent minibatch gradients may decorrelate chains; need intuition and empirical evidence for why RR still reduces error order and whether cross-chain correlation persists.

- Weakness — fairness of real-data baselines: MovieLens comparisons may be unfair; request added baselines: two independent SGLD chains at γ (averaged), one/two SGLD chains at γ/2 (wall-clock matched), and SGLD tuned per the theory.

- Weakness — metrics/practical significance: RMSE gains on MovieLens are modest and could be within tuning variability; ask for uncertainty bands, predictive log-likelihoods, calibration diagnostics, throughput, and overhead from correlation management.

- Weakness — implementation details: Clarify whether Σ = Id is used in implementation, whether minibatch sizes/ sampling schemes are identical across chains, and effects of differing batch sizes or synchronized minibatch indices.

- Weakness — step-size schedules: Theory motivates decreasing steps (α ≈ 1/5) but experiments use constant steps; request decreasing-step experiments or justification for constant-step choices.

- Quality: Mathematical development appears careful and consistent with literature, but assumptions should be consolidated in the main text and practical implications discussed.

- Clarity: Generally well written with clear algorithmic description; recommend concise pseudocode in the main paper and explicit statement that both chains use the same gradient estimator distribution.

- Originality: Novel and interesting adaptation of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with rigorous analysis and variance control via correlated increments.

- Significance: Could be a simple, broadly applicable upgrade to first-order SG-MCMC if empirical improvements hold across tasks and against strong baselines.

- Pros: Elegant, simple idea with strong theoretical backing; improves convergence rates without higher-order integrators; parallel-friendly and easy to implement; convincing synthetic results and SGHMC extension.

- Cons: Real-data gains modest and comparisons may be unfair; assumptions/conditions not fully transparent; limited exploration of chain correlation and minibatch coupling; missing baselines.

- Suggestions — experiments: Add proposed baselines, perform ablations on Brownian correlation and minibatch synchronization, and include uncertainty bands on RMSE.

- Suggestions — reporting: Summarize assumptions on U, L, and f in the main text; report predictive log-likelihood, calibration, ESS/time, throughput, and correlation overhead.

- Suggestions — implementation: Include pseudocode and implementation details for generating correlated increments and for minibatch handling.

- Overall assessment: Promising, well-motivated contribution with clean theory and compelling synthetic evidence; reviewer leans positive but requests stronger, fairer empirical validation and clearer assumptions before final acceptance.
