- Proposes SGRRLD: run two parallel SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine them via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- RR extrapolation reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ²) while controlling variance through correlated Brownian increments.  
- Uses correlated increments (Z^(γ) = (Z^(γ/2)_2k-1 + Z^(γ/2)_2k)/√2) to minimize variance, extending Talay–Tubaro analysis to SG-MCMC.  
- Achieves MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³), matching higher-order integrator accuracy with only first-order discretizations.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Specifies technical conditions for applicability, including π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ²) and ergodicity/weak error assumptions.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussians and large-scale matrix factorization shows notably lower bias and MSE than SGLD.  
- Matrix factorization experiment demonstrates roughly half the wall-clock time to reach SGLD accuracy by exploiting parallelism.  
- Strength: simple, minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.  
- Strength: method is general and applicable beyond SGLD to other SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees supported by empirical results.  
- Strength: practical computational efficiency via parallel implementation.  
- Clarity: excellent exposition, rigorous mathematics, and useful implementation notes.  
- Minor suggestions: more precise characterization of applicability conditions to other SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Minor suggestions: include experimental comparison with SGD in matrix factorization to strengthen practical claims.  
- Minor suggestions: investigate alternative parallelization schemes to avoid running one chain twice as long (e.g., three-chain strategies).  
- Novelty: meaningful and novel application of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC.  
- Significance: provides a practical route to higher-order accuracy without higher-order integrators, well-suited to distributed ML settings.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
