- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (γ and γ/2) with correlated noise to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Proves consistency and a CLT, and gives non-asymptotic bounds: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) for constant step sizes.  
- Optimizing bounds yields improved K-dependent rates: bias O(K^{-2/3}) and MSE O(K^{-4/5}), improving over first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical validation on Gaussian models, SGHMC variants, and MovieLens matrix factorization supports theoretical claims and shows better accuracy or wall-clock performance.  
- Builds on SGLD and SG-MCMC literature and adapts classical RR extrapolation (Talay & Tubaro) to stochastic-gradient settings with correlated Brownian increments.  
- Conceptually simple and broadly applicable with minimal code changes, and naturally parallelizable.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: consistency, CLT, and sharper non-asymptotic bounds than first-order methods.  
- Achieves higher-order accuracy without higher-order integrators and can exploit parallel hardware.  
- Empirical results are promising on both synthetic and large-scale datasets.  
- Rate-derivation steps (transition from Γ^(n)_K conditions to K-rates) are only sketched and need a concise derivation in the main text.  
- Wall-clock comparisons may be unfair: SGRRLD does three Euler steps per macro-iteration, so normalize by stochastic gradient evaluations or report single-core results.  
- MovieLens plots compare parallel SGRRLD to sequential SGLD without clear normalization; authors should rescale or report single-core performance.  
- Figure 3 appears inconsistent: reported optimal α differs from plotted best curve, and fluctuations make rate reading difficult; add error bars or smoothing and fix legend/captions.  
- Small-step-size regime issue: explain why 1/(Kγ) dominates for SGRRLD but an analogous effect isn't visible for SGLD in the same plot.  
- Clarify smoothness and growth assumptions on f, dissipativity/Lipschitz conditions on U, and gradient-noise law L required for CLT and bounds.  
- State explicitly that both stochastic-gradient sequences must share the same distribution (e.g., minibatch size) and report whether correlated minibatches were used experimentally.  
- Confirm whether the optimal Brownian coupling (Z^γ = (Z^{γ/2}_{2k-1}+Z^{γ/2}_{2k})/sqrt(2)) was used in all experiments.  
- Theoretical framework is technically sound and grounded in SDE literature; some proof details are deferred to the supplement and need brief intuition in the main text.  
- Empirical methodology is reasonable but requires addressing computational fairness and figure inconsistencies before publication.  
- Paper is generally clear and organized, but connections between theorem statements and practical step-size schedules need clarification.  
- Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with variance control via coupling is novel and distinct from higher-order integrator approaches.  
- The method is practical, generic, and parallel-friendly; could become a standard variance/bias reduction trick if advantages hold broadly.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel simple idea, strong theory, demonstrable finite-time and asymptotic gains, easy implementation, and good large-scale empirical results.  
- Cons against acceptance: hardware-dependent speedups, ambiguous rate derivations, figure/legend inconsistencies, and missing definitions/notation.  
- Minor fixes: consistent symbol for θ, define RR and Γ_K at first mention, specify smoothness class for f, fix figure caption mismatches, and use boldface for vectors.  
- Overall recommendation: promising contribution with a lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying rate derivations, fixing Figure 3, and adding hardware-agnostic fairness analyses.
