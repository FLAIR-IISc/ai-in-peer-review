- Introduces Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to accelerate convergence in SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for the proposed method.  
- Synthetic experiments validate the theoretical bias and MSE bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization on real data shows practical improvements over standard SGLD.  
- Overall assessment: solid technical contribution with clear execution and contextualization in SG-MCMC literature.  
- Major concern: novelty appears incremental given prior work on numerical integrators in SG-MCMC ([10]) and use of RR for invariant distribution approximation ([23]).  
- Paper should more explicitly differentiate its technical novelty from these prior works.  
- Major practical concern: implementation requires synchronized communication between chains due to correlated noise, but authors assume zero communication cost.  
- Experimental setup lacks clarity on hardware and resource allocation—unclear if SGRRLD splits resources across two chains or SGLD uses full resources, which affects wall-clock fairness.  
- Authors should report whether communication costs were measured and compare against an embarrassingly parallel two-chain SGLD baseline (no communication).  
- Minor issue: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is redundantly mentioned and should be consolidated.
