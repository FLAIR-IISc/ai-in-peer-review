- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2, combine empirical averages via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to cancel discretization bias.  
- Correlates Gaussian increments across chains using Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2 to control variance inflation.  
- Theoretical results: consistency, CLT showing optimal coupling minimizes limiting variance, non-asymptotic bounds with O(γ2) bias and O(γ4) variance, and MSE O(K−4/5) with optimal schedules.  
- Builds on prior work: SGLD/SG-MCMC, higher-order integrator analyses, and RR extrapolation for SDEs; novelty is adapting RR to stochastic-gradient setting with correlated noise.  
- Implementation is simple and parallelizable: two chains, linear combination, and shared randomness.  
- Strong theoretical analysis supporting the method’s claims and optimal coupling choice.  
- Demonstrated practical gains on synthetic Gaussian models and a large-scale matrix factorization task.  
- Insight: in asymptotics Gaussian noise can dominate stochastic-gradient noise, making Brownian correlation key for variance control.  
- Novelty is moderately incremental given existing RR for ULA and higher-order SG-MCMC integrator analyses.  
- Experiments lack clarity about practical overhead: coupling, shared randomness, and mini-batch matching across workers need explicit discussion.  
- Need clarification on compute-budget fairness and wall-clock parity between SGRRLD and baselines (cores, threads, hardware assignment).  
- Missing comparisons to simple baselines: two independent parallel SGLD chains averaged, or time-matched SGLD with doubled iterations.  
- Sensitivity to mismatched mini-batch sizes, data partitions, or sampler differences is unexplored and should be ablated.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos in Chain 2 updates, duplicated ULA definition, and pseudo-code relegated to supplement; include a short algorithm box in main text.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound analysis with supported claims, but some experimental details are absent for reproducibility.  
- Clarity assessment: generally well written but would benefit from main-text pseudo-code and clearer computational model.  
- Originality assessment: moderate — meaningful combination of RR and stochastic gradients but incremental relative to prior art.  
- Significance assessment: potentially impactful due to simplicity and broad applicability; wider empirical validation would strengthen claim.  
- Suggested improvements: detail hardware/parallelization, enforce wall-clock fairness, and specify synchronization/communication costs for shared randomness.  
- Suggested improvements: add baselines (independent averaged SGLD, time-matched SGLD, higher-order integrators) and ablation studies on coupling and batch mismatches.  
- Suggested improvements: report diagnostics (bias/variance decomposition, ESS proxies) for real tasks and move concise pseudo-code to main paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general technique with solid theory that attains higher-order accuracy using first-order integrators and shows empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, unclear experimental fairness/overhead, and limited empirical scope relative to necessary baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying/strengthening experimental methodology, baselines, and fixing presentation issues.
