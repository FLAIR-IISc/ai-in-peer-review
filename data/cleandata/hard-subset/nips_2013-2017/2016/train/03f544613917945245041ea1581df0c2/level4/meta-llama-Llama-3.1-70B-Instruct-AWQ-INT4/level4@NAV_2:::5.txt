The authors propose a novel method called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SGRRLD) to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. This method employs Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a well-known technique in numerical analysis, to improve the rate of convergence of SG-MCMC algorithms. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The authors provide a detailed description of the SGRRLD algorithm and its components, including the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation technique. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-presented, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the assumptions and conditions under which the theorems hold.

The empirical results presented in the paper are also satisfactory, demonstrating the effectiveness of the SGRRLD algorithm in reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms. The authors provide a comparison of SGRRLD with Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) on several synthetic and real-world datasets, showing that SGRRLD achieves higher rates of convergence and better accuracy than SGLD.

However, there are a few issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, there is an inconsistent statement about the step size in the paper. The authors mention that the step size should be chosen such that γk = γ1k−α for α ∈ (0, 1], but later they mention that the optimal choice for the exponent α is 1/5, which implies a different choice for the step size. This inconsistency needs to be clarified.

Secondly, the pseudo-code of the SGRRLD algorithm provided in the supplementary document is not consistent with Equation (5) in the main text. This needs to be corrected to ensure that the algorithm is correctly implemented.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are satisfactory. However, the authors need to address the issues mentioned above to ensure the clarity and correctness of the paper.
