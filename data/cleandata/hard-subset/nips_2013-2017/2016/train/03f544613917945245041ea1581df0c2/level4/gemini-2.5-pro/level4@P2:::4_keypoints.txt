- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to cancel leading-order discretization bias.  
- Uses correlated noise between chains to control variance of the extrapolated estimator.  
- Theoretical results: asymptotic consistency and a Central Limit Theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Non-asymptotic bounds: bias and MSE analyses showing faster convergence than SGLD.  
- Proven MSE rate improvement: with optimal step size SGRRLD achieves O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Practical claim: first-order integrator with RR extrapolation attains accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators.  
- Method is simple to implement and naturally parallelizable (runs two chains in parallel).  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments and large-scale matrix factorization show bias/MSE reduction and wall-clock speed gains.  
- Concern: fairness of computational-cost comparison—SGRRLD uses three gradient evaluations per iteration vs one for SGLD; authors should state total gradient evaluations and discuss single-core performance.  
- Question: apparent discrepancy in Figure 3 where α=0.2 seems to outperform α=1/3 despite theory predicting α=1/3; needs clarification (constants or finite-iteration effects?).  
- Question: high variance in convergence plots (especially for α=0.2) complicates visual confirmation of rates; explanation requested.  
- Request: make key smoothness assumptions explicit in the main text (e.g., solution to Poisson equation must be C^9).  
- Minor fixes: consistently bold vectors/matrices, define “RR” at first use, clarify notation distinction between continuous ϑ and discrete θ.  
- Suggestion: comment on and compare the 1/(Kγ) term’s behavior and onset in SGLD versus SGRRLD as seen in plots.
