This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE is a standard numerical integration tool that has recently seen applications to numerical stochastic differential equations (SDEs); a benefit of this work is its connection between the MCMC and SDE literature. The authors introduce a new SGLD-RRE method, develop theoretical convergence results (along with arguments for improvement over SGLD), and demonstrate numerical results. They achieve improvements on toy examples, and consistent, though modest, improvements in large-scale examples. Overall it's a solid paper, clearly written. This is a strong contribution with interesting links between two fields; compelling theoretical results; and good empirical performance of the proposed algorithm. The paper is clear, easy to read, and appears technically sound. The method is a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, offering computational convenience. Practitioners are likely to use these methods, though they are unlikely to revolutionize current practice. 

Minor comments: Fig 1(a) could be clarified – it depicts a Gaussian distribution with the empirical posterior mean and variance, not the empirical estimated posterior distribution itself. This should be explicitly stated for clarity. Fig 2(a) – a brief discussion of why the bias does not necessarily decrease with the step size would be helpful, as this is not immediately intuitive.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Soundness:** The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, providing convergence guarantees and demonstrating the potential for improved convergence rates compared to standard SGLD.
*   **Novelty:** Applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD is a novel approach, effectively leveraging techniques from numerical analysis to improve MCMC performance.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology, theoretical results, and experimental setup.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments, both synthetic and on real datasets, support the theoretical findings and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed method.
*   **Potential Impact:** The method offers a practical way to improve the efficiency of SGLD, a widely used algorithm for large-scale Bayesian inference.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Incremental Improvement:** While the improvements over SGLD are statistically significant, they are not always dramatic, particularly on the real-world datasets.
*   **Complexity:** Although relatively straightforward, implementing SGRRLD requires running two chains in parallel, adding a minor overhead compared to standard SGLD.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments primarily focus on a few specific models and datasets. Expanding the evaluation to a wider range of problems would strengthen the conclusions.
