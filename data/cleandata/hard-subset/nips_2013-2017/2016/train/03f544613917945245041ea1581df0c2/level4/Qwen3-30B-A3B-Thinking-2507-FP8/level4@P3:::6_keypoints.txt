- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions and combining outputs.  
- Theoretical result: proves SGRRLD achieves MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides formal asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence proofs with clear bias and MSE bounds.  
- Experimental validation: synthetic Gaussian and large-scale matrix factorization tasks show improved accuracy over SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Practical speedup: SGRRLD attains comparable accuracy to SGLD in roughly half the wall-clock time in matrix factorization experiments.  
- Novelty: RR extrapolation is known, but applying it to SG-MCMC to mimic higher-order integrator accuracy with first-order methods is a meaningful adaptation.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured and mathematically clear; minor repetition of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" noted.  
- Strength: strong theoretical justification and rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: demonstrates practical advantages for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Strength: effectively extends numerical SDE techniques to SG-MCMC with clear motivation.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of implementation details, especially communication costs in distributed settings.  
- Weakness: unclear experimental resource allocation—whether SGRRLD’s two chains shared or split computational resources versus SGLD.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to an embarrassingly parallel baseline of two independent SGLD chains (no-communication).  
- Pro: significant theoretical and practical contribution with strong empirical support.  
- Con: practical implementation and experimental fairness issues may limit immediate applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add discussion of communication costs, clarify resource allocation in experiments, and consider adding the two-SGLD baseline.
