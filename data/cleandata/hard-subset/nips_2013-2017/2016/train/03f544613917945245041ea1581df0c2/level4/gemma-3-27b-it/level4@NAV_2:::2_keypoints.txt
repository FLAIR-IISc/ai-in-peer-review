- Introduces SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias present in methods like SGLD.  
- Core idea: apply RR acceleration to lower bias while controlling variance, motivated by prior RR/SDE and higher-order integrator work.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds with improved convergence rates vs SGLD.  
- Experiments: evaluated on a synthetic linear Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization tasks.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation supporting the method’s improved convergence.  
- Strength: novel application of RR extrapolation to the SG-MCMC setting.  
- Strength: clear algorithm description and discussion of parallelizability.  
- Strength: synthetic experiments show clear benefits of bias reduction.  
- Weakness: real-data experiments are limited in scope and depth.  
- Weakness: comparison to SGLD is potentially imbalanced—should include multiple parallel SGLD chains with matched step sizes (e.g., γ/2).  
- Weakness: empirical impact of correlated Brownian increments between chains is not demonstrated.  
- Weakness: conditions on the potential U in Theorems 1 and 2 need clarification, especially regarding multimodality.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition/explanation for why combining potentially decorrelated chains reduces error order.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theoretical support, clear algorithm, and promising initial empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-data evaluation, incomplete empirical/theoretical treatment of correlations and assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experiments and clarify theoretical assumptions and intuition.
