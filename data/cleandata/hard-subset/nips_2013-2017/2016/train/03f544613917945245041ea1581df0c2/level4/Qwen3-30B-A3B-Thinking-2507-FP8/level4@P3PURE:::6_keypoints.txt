- Proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (higher-order integrator) to accelerate SG-MCMC via two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.
- Provides theoretical results: both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence (bias and MSE) guarantees.
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models validate the bias and MSE bounds from the theory.
- Large-scale matrix factorization on real data shows practical gains of the SGRRLD algorithm over standard SGLD.
- Paper is well-written, technically clear, and well situated within SGMCMC literature; contribution is meaningful.
- Concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analysis of numerical integrators in SG-MCMC ([10]) and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions ([23]).
- Concern: practical applicability limited because method requires correlated parallel chains, implying per-step communication not accounted for in the paper.
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear whether the single SGLD baseline used the same computational resources as the two SGRRLD chains—comparison may be unfair.
- Need clarification on resource allocation: if SGRRLD used shared resources, how were they split and how could it reduce wall-clock time given gradient evaluation dominates cost?
- Recommendation: explicitly acknowledge communication overhead and provide detailed implementation/setup specifications (hardware, parallelization, wall-clock measures).
- Recommendation: compare to running two independent SGLD chains (no communication) as an alternative practical baseline.
- Minor: phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated in the text.
