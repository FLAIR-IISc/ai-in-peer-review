- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD (SGRRLD) to reduce bias from noisy gradients, claiming asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD.
- Clear algorithm description, easy to implement, requires running two parallel Markov chains.
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian Model shows convincing bias reduction and SGRRLD outperforms SGLD.
- MovieLens real-data result shows only slight RMSE improvement, with unclear statistical significance.
- Theoretical claims (Theorems 1–2) are plausible but not fully verified by the reviewer; conditions and proofs need scrutiny.
- Questions about required assumptions on the potential U, especially applicability to multi-modal targets.
- Concern about role of inter-chain correlation: how uncorrelated stochastic gradients affect extrapolation error reduction and whether theory assumes measurable correlation structure.
- Request for empirical measurement of correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments and how it evolves over time.
- Fairness concern: wall-clock parity via parallelization is not equivalent to comparing against multiple parallel SGLD chains run within the same compute budget.
- Suggestion to include baselines: SGLD with step size γ/2 and multi-chain SGLD (matched compute) to isolate RR benefits from smaller steps or extra samples.
- Main weaknesses: limited real-data evidence and absence of stronger, compute-matched baselines; addressing these would strengthen the empirical case.
- Overall: conceptually appealing with solid theory and strong synthetic results, but needs expanded real-data experiments and clearer assumptions/empirical analyses.
