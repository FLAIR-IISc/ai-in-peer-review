This paper applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to recent "big-data MCMC" methods, with a focus on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD), thereby bridging the gap between the MCMC literature and the SDE literature, a connection that may benefit both communities. The authors propose a new SGLD-RRE method, derive theoretical results on its convergence, and provide arguments for when SGLD-RRE outperforms vanilla SGLD. They also present numerical results, demonstrating impressive improvements on toy examples and modest yet consistent gains on large-scale examples. Overall, this is a solid paper, featuring a clear and well-written exposition of interesting links between two somewhat disconnected fields, compelling theoretical results, and good empirical performance of the novel algorithm. The paper appears technically sound, and the method, being a straightforward application of RRE to SGLD, is computationally convenient. While the proposed method is unlikely to revolutionize current practice, I believe practitioners will find it useful.

Minor comments: Figure 1a may be misleading, as it depicts a Gaussian with the empirical posterior mean and variance, rather than the empirical estimated posterior distribution. This distinction should be clarified for the reader. Additionally, Figure 2a would benefit from a brief discussion on why the bias does not necessarily decrease with the stepsize, as this may be counter-intuitive for some readers.
