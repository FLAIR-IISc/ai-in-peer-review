- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains (step-sizes γ and γ/2) and combining estimates to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Uses correlated Gaussian increments (optimal: shared Brownian motion, Σ = Id) to mitigate variance inflation from extrapolation.
- Theoretical contributions: consistency, central limit theorem, non-asymptotic bounds, asymptotic bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), and finite-time MSE O(K^-4/5).
- Claims matching accuracy of higher-order integrators while using only first-order updates and achieves improved rates over first-order SG-MCMC (O(K^-4/5) vs O(K^-2/3)).
- Method is generic and extendable to other SG-MCMC schemes; SGHMC variant is illustrated.
- Experiments on a Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization show reduced bias/MSE and improved RMSE at equal wall-clock time using parallel chains.
- Strength: simple, general idea leveraging classical extrapolation with minimal implementation burden.
- Strength: parallel-friendly — two chains can run independently, enabling practical wall-clock speedups.
- Strength: solid theoretical development with clear optimal step-size schedules and variance-control analysis.
- Strength: empirical results are broadly consistent with theory and demonstrate practical benefits.
- Weakness: inconsistent description of step-size schedules across text, equations, and experiments, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not match Equation (5); pseudo-code must match the update scheme and correlation structure.
- Weakness: no empirical comparison to higher-order integrators to substantiate claims of matching their accuracy.
- Weakness: requires identical minibatch distributions across chains, which may limit distributed implementations; robustness to mismatched gradients or partial correlation is not tested.
- Quality assessment: technically sound and builds on prior RR/SDE work, but relies on strong standard assumptions and needs clarity fixes.
- Clarity assessment: generally well organized, but contains typos and important inconsistencies; should explicitly state Σ = Id and implementation requirements early.
- Originality assessment: novel and well-motivated application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, with natural extension to SGHMC.
- Significance assessment: broadly applicable, easy to implement, and practically attractive due to parallelism and improved convergence rates.
- Suggestion: clarify and consistently present step-size schedules, initialization, updates, and burn-in handling for both chains.
- Suggestion: fix Algorithm 1 vs Eq. (5 mismatch and provide precise pseudo-code enforcing two γ/2 steps per γ step and correlated increments.
- Suggestion: add experiments comparing to representative higher-order integrators at matched wall-clock budgets.
- Suggestion: include ablations on correlation structure (Σ = 0 vs Σ = Id) and matched vs mismatched minibatch sizes to assess robustness.
- Suggestion: report both wall-clock time and number of gradient evaluations to separate algorithmic efficiency from parallelization gains.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general, practical idea with strong theory, empirical benefits, and good parallel properties.
- Cons against acceptance: reproducibility-harming inconsistencies, missing higher-order baselines, and limited ablation studies on key implementation choices.
