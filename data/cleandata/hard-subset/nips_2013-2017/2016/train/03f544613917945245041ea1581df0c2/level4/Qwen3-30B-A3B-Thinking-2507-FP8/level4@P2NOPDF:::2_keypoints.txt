- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias from stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical claims: SGRRLD attains asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD and faster convergence for the same step size, at the cost of running two parallel chains.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction, matching theory.  
- MovieLens real-data results show only modest RMSE improvement, questioning practical benefit given overhead.  
- Authors claim comparable runtime due to parallelization but fail to benchmark against running two SGLD chains within the same time budget.  
- Current comparison SGRRLD(γ) vs SGLD(γ) is misleading; a fairer baseline is SGLD(γ/2) or two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Theoretical assumptions on the potential U need clarification—specifically whether U can be multi-modal.  
- The mechanism by which RR extrapolation reduces error order amid chain correlations is under-explained; need analysis of how extrapolation interacts with chain correlation.  
- Experiments should report observed correlation between the two chains to validate decorrelation assumptions.  
- Lack of broader real-data validation—more diverse datasets (e.g., image, text) are needed to confirm practical gains beyond MovieLens.  
- Recommendations: compare against parallel SGLD(γ/2), clarify conditions on U for multi-modality, and include additional real-world benchmarks; overall moderate novelty with promising theory but insufficient empirical validation.
