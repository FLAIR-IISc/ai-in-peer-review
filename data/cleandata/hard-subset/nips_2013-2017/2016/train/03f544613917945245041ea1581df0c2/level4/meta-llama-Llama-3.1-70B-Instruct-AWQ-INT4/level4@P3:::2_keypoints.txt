- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm to reduce SGLD bias while keeping variance reasonable.
- Uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes.
- Connects to prior SG-MCMC work and provides clear algorithm description and implementation details.
- Provides theoretical results including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Synthetic and real-data experiments show SGRRLD reduces bias and improves RMSE over SGLD.
- Paper is well-written and algorithm description is easy to implement.
- Weakness: real-data experiments are limited and need more comparisons to SGLD with the same step size and multiple parallel chains.
- Suggestion: include more real-data experiments to better demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Strengths summary: novel algorithm, clear theory, demonstrated experimental advantage.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, solid theoretical analysis, empirical improvements.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient real-data validation and comparisons.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with suggestion to improve real-data experiments and comparisons.
