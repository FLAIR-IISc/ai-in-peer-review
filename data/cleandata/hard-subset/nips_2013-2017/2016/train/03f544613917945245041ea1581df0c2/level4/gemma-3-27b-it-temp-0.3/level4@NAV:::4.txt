This paper presents a valuable improvement to Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) through the introduction of a Richardson-Romberg scheme, demonstrably enhancing convergence rates in terms of both bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE). A key strength of the proposed method is its ease of implementation, requiring only minor modifications to existing SG-MCMC algorithms. The work is further strengthened by robust theoretical foundations. Specifically, the authors propose a Romberg-Richardson extrapolation scheme to accelerate the convergence of standard SG-MCMC, and the theoretical analysis appears sound. 

My primary concern relates to the derivation of the specific convergence rates presented in the theorem (as detailed in the “explain fatal flaws” section). Beyond this, I have a practical concern regarding the interpretation of the matrix factorization experiment results presented in Figure 5. Considering the method utilizes two chains, with one requiring twice the number of steps compared to standard SG-MCMC, a single-core implementation would inherently take approximately three times longer.  Adjusting the timing for SGLD in Figure 5 by a factor of 2/3 to reflect this computational cost would likely reduce the observed performance gap between SGLD and SGRRLD. 

Furthermore, examining Figure 3, the bias plot suggests that the optimal convergence is achieved with α=0.2, rather than the stated value of 1/3.  The optimal convergence curve also exhibits considerable fluctuation, which appears inconsistent with the expected rate for α=0.2; clarification on the cause of this behavior would be appreciated.  Finally, the manuscript states that when the stepsize is sufficiently small, the term 1/Kγ should dominate. I am curious as to why this effect is not observed in the results for SGLD.

Several minor points require attention: In Equation 1, the notation differs from the established symbol for the parameter. The abbreviation “RR” is used on line 94 without prior definition.  On line 131, Γ<sub>K</sub> is used without definition. Line 160 mentions a “smooth function f” without specifying the degree or type of smoothness.  On line 249, Figure 1(b) appears to plot Bias versus time, while the text suggests it should be Figure 1(c) plotting Bias versus dimension. Finally, please consistently use bold letters to denote vectors throughout the manuscript.
