- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC, combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD to reduce bias via two independent parallel chains.  
- Theoretical analysis claims asymptotic consistency and superior convergence rate over standard SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical results show compelling performance improvements relative to baseline methods.  
- Core idea is straightforward and essentially combines known Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD.  
- Main strength: theoretical efficiency gain (better convergence rate).  
- Comparable rate improvements could be achieved by high-order integrators (e.g., Chen et al., 2015), but no direct comparisons are provided.  
- Omission of comparisons to high-order integrators weakens the claim that SG-RR-MCMC is uniquely efficient.  
- Critical inconsistency: theory assumes a diminishing step size schedule (Eq. 5), while Algorithm 1 in the supplement uses a fixed step size.  
- Direct contradiction: Eq. 5 specifies η_t = η_0 / t^α (α > 0) but Algorithm 1 uses constant η, undermining convergence guarantees.  
- Required fixes: reconcile or revise the step size treatment and include explicit comparisons with high-order integrators to demonstrate unique contribution; otherwise the method may be seen as a minor variation.
