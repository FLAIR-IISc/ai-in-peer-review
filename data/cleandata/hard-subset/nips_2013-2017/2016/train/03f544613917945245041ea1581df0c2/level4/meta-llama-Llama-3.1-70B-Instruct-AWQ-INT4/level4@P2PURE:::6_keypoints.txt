- Proposes using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses (bias and MSE bounds).  
- Experiments on a synthetic Gaussian model validate the theoretical bias and MSE bounds.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Paper is well-written and situates the method within existing SG-MCMC literature.  
- Main concern: novelty may be incremental given prior analysis of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Main concern: practical applicability is questionable because the method requires two chains to communicate each step, but communication cost is ignored.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair or unclear: resource allocation between the two-chain SGRRLD and single-chain SGLD is not specified.  
- Unclear how splitting gradient computation across two partial-resource chains could reduce wall-clock time versus using full resources for SGLD.  
- Suggested clarifications/experiments: discuss communication cost, detail implementation/resource splitting, and compare to two independent parallel SGLD chains without communication.  
- Minor comment: the term "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is repeated unnecessarily.
