- Proposal: Use Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (higher-order integrator) to accelerate SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Theory: Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence guarantees; bias and MSE bounds established and validated on a synthetic Gaussian model.  
- Empirics: Real-data large-scale matrix factorization experiments show practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Overall judgment: Strong, well-presented paper that makes a valid contribution and situates itself well in prior work.  
- Novelty concern: Contribution may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of Richardson-Romberg for invariant distributions [23].  
- Practicality/fairness concern: Parallel implementation uses correlated Brownian motion requiring per-step communication between chains, but the communication cost is not accounted for and should be acknowledged.  
- Resource-allocation clarity: Experimental setup lacks detail on whether SGLD used the same computational resources as the two-chain method and how resources were split for SGRRLD; unclear how splitting gradient computation can yield faster wall-clock time.  
- Baseline suggestion: Should compare to running two independent SGLD chains in parallel (no communication) as an alternative baseline.  
- Minor: The phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated (lines 25 and 70).
