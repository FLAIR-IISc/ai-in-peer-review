This paper proposes applying a Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation scheme to improve the convergence of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The method aims to reduce both bias and Mean Squared Error (MSE), achieving better theoretical convergence rates than standard SG-MCMC. A key advantage of the proposed algorithm is its simplicity, as it requires only minimal modifications to existing implementations. The claims are supported by a solid theoretical analysis.

However, I have several major concerns:

1.  My primary concern is the derivation of the specific convergence rates from the theorems presented. The connection is not clearly explained, and it is difficult to verify how the final rates are obtained. This point requires significant clarification.

2.  The experimental comparison regarding computational time appears unfair. The proposed method involves running two chains, with one taking twice as many steps, resulting in a total computational cost roughly three times that of a single standard SG-MCMC chain. When plotting performance against wall-clock time on a single-core machine (as in Figure 5), this overhead must be considered. A fair comparison would require scaling the time axis for the SGLD baseline by a factor of 2/3. Such an adjustment would likely shrink the observed performance gap between SGLD and the proposed SGRRLD.

Additionally, I have the following questions and requests for clarification:

*   In the bias plot in Figure 3, the best empirical performance appears to be achieved by the red curve, which corresponds to α=0.2. This contradicts the text, which states that the optimal rate is achieved with α=1/3. Could the authors please address this discrepancy?
*   The same curve in Figure 3 (for α=0.2) exhibits significant fluctuations, which seems inconsistent with a stable convergence rate. Is there a theoretical or practical reason for this behavior?
*   Line 256 states that when the stepsize is too small, the `1/(Kγ)` term dominates the error for the proposed method. Why does this same issue not affect the standard SGLD algorithm?
