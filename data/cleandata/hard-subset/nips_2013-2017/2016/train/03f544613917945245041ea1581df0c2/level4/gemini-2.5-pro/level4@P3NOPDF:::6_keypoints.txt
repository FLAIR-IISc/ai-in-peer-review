1. Proposes SGRRLD: Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC using two parallel chains coupled by correlated Gaussian noise.  
2. Provides theoretical results: asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses with bias and MSE bounds.  
3. Experiments: synthetic Gaussian verifies theory; large-scale matrix factorization shows improvements over SGLD.  
4. Paper is well-written and situates the contribution within existing literature.  
5. Major concern — incremental novelty: related work has studied integrators for SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation in non-stochastic settings; need clearer articulation of new technical challenges from stochastic gradients.  
6. Major concern — communication overhead: coupled chains require per-iteration exchange (correlated Brownian motion), which may negate wall-clock gains but is not discussed or measured.  
7. Major concern — resource/fairness of comparison: unclear whether total computational budget (gradient evaluations or CPU time) was equal between SGRRLD and SGLD.  
8. Major concern — ambiguity on resource split: if SGRRLD used double resources its advantage is expected; if budget was split between chains it’s unclear how halving per-chain gradient work yields faster convergence.  
9. Major concern — missing baseline: need comparison to two independent parallel SGLD chains combined at the end to match resources without communication overhead.  
10. Minor comment: ULA acronym is defined twice; remove redundant definition.
