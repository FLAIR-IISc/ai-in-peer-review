This paper leverages the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation technique to mitigate the bias introduced by gradient estimation noise in stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD), resulting in the SGRRLD algorithm. Theoretical analysis reveals that SGRRLD achieves an asymptotically lower bias and mean squared error (MSE) compared to SGLD. Experimental results on a synthetic Linear Gaussian Model demonstrate the superiority of SGRRLD over SGLD. However, the improvement in test RMSE on the MovieLens dataset is marginal and open to interpretation. Building upon existing literature that showcases the effectiveness of RR extrapolation in enhancing the convergence rate of Monte Carlo estimates for stochastic differential equations (SDEs), this work applies this technique to stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The authors prove that, under specific conditions, the convergence rate of SGRRLD surpasses that of SGLD with the same step size, albeit at the cost of running two parallel Markov chains. The algorithm description is clear and straightforward to implement. Synthetic experiments convincingly demonstrate the bias reduction capabilities of SGRRLD. Further experiments using real-world data would be beneficial in validating the efficacy of this simple modification to SGLD in significantly reducing bias. Without verifying the proof details of Theorem 1 and 2, it would be interesting to explore the conditions required for U to satisfy both theorems, particularly whether U can be multi-modal. Intuitively, when gradient noises are uncorrelated, the two chains are likely to become uncorrelated rapidly, even if the Gaussian noises are correlated. It is unclear how combining the two chains reduces the error order. In real-data experiments, the authors should investigate whether they observe correlation between the two chains. Concerns arise regarding the experimental setup and real-data experiments, as the authors claim comparable computation time to SGLD with parallelization. However, running multiple parallel chains of SGLD within the same timeframe is also feasible. A comparison between the estimates from two chains of SGLD and SGRRLD would be valuable in determining whether the observed improvement in RMSE persists. Furthermore, the real-data experiment should also compare SGRRLD with SGLD using a step size of gamma/2, which is expected to have lower bias, and consider running two chains in parallel. Additional real-data experiments would be beneficial in reinforcing the findings.
