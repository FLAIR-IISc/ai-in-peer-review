- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to big-data MCMC, focusing on SGLD; bridges MCMC and SDE literatures.  
- Introduces SGLD-RRE algorithm with theoretical convergence results and conditions when it outperforms vanilla SGLD.  
- Empirical results show impressive improvements on toy problems and modest but consistent gains on large-scale examples.  
- Method is a straightforward, computationally convenient application of RRE likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and balances theory and experiments effectively.  
- Use of correlated Brownian increments is a beneficial variance-control technique.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1a is misleading—plots Gaussian with empirical mean/variance rather than the empirical posterior distribution; needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2a shows bias not necessarily decreasing with step size; requires additional discussion.  
- Quality rating 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RRE application to SGLD, convincing theory, effective experiments, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: method not revolutionary and some minor presentation/analysis issues.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify Figure 1a, discuss the bias behavior in Figure 2a, and consider adding more numerical experiments to demonstrate robustness.
