- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias.  
- Core method: run two parallel SG-MCMC chains (second uses half step size, twice iterations) with correlated Gaussian noise and subtract estimates to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and lower MSE.  
- Method is conceptually simple, easy to implement, minimally intrusive to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, and broadly applicable.  
- Novelty is moderate: Richardson-Romberg is established in numerical analysis but underexplored in ML/SG-MCMC.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible.  
- Clarification needed on applicability: are conditions beyond the expansion pi_gamma(f)=pi(f)+C·γ+O(γ^2) required for the technique to hold for all SG-MCMC methods?  
- Could the approach extend to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampled Metropolis–Hastings) or to distributed SGLD?  
- Efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long is wasteful; would running three concurrent chains (one at γ, two at γ/2) be a better variance/efficiency tradeoff?  
- Is there a straightforward generalization to more than two step sizes for higher-order extrapolation?  
- Unclear impact of minibatch correlation: does reusing the same minibatch across paired iterations minimize variance or is that effect negligible?  
- Empirical critique: matrix factorization experiment should be run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to meaningfully assess practical benefits of the Bayesian approach.  
- Practical suggestion: include pseudocode/algorithm box to improve accessibility for practitioners.
