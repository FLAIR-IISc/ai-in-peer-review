This paper investigates the application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to modern “big-data MCMC” techniques, specifically focusing on Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). RRE, a well-established numerical integration method, has recently found successful applications in the numerical solution of stochastic differential equations (SDEs). A key strength of this work lies in its bridging of the MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially offering benefits to both fields. The authors present a novel SGLD-RRE method, accompanied by theoretical convergence analysis – including justifications for performance gains over standard SGLD – and supporting numerical demonstrations. The results demonstrate substantial improvements on test problems and consistent, albeit more modest, gains in large-scale applications. In summary, this is a strong and clearly written contribution. It establishes compelling connections between two relatively separate areas, delivers interesting theoretical findings, and showcases promising empirical performance for the proposed algorithm. The methodology represents a computationally efficient and direct application of RRE to SGLD, suggesting practical utility for researchers and practitioners, though likely as an incremental rather than transformative advance.
