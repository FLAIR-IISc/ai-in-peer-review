- Proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD): run two SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2, correlate Gaussian noise, and subtract estimates to reduce bias to O(γ^2).
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds for the proposed method.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data shows effectiveness of bias reduction.
- Strength: simple, general method that can be implemented with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Strength: novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, importing a known numerical-analysis tool to ML.
- Concern: unclear whether the method applies to all SG-MCMC algorithms or only those with expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2).
- Concern: running one chain twice as long may waste compute; alternative three-chain scheduling (one γ, two γ/2) might avoid idle time but could affect variance.
- Concern: ambiguity about required correlation of injected noise and whether correlating minibatches (using same minibatch) is optimal or negligible.
- Experimental critique: matrix factorization experiment should be run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to contextualize Bayesian benefits and final errors.
- Suggestion: include an algorithm pseudocode box early to aid practitioners in implementation without deep theory.
- Suggestion: clarify the precise level/type of noise and minibatch correlation needed for theoretical guarantees and practical performance.
- Originality: significant contribution by introducing Richardson-Romberg extrapolation into SG-MCMC literature.
- Significance: potential to improve accuracy of widely used SG-MCMC methods with a simple, broadly applicable technique.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address applicability, efficiency, experimental completeness, and clarification of noise/minibatch correlation.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
