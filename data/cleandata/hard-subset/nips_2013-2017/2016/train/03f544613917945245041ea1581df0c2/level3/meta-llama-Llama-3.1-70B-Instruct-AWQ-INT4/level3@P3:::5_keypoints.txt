- Proposal of SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias while controlling variance.  
- Method detail: runs two chains in parallel with different step sizes and uses sequence acceleration (Richardson-Romberg).  
- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Convergence/accuracy: shows higher rates of convergence than SGLD and other first-order SG-MCMC integrators.  
- Non-asymptotic analysis: provides bounds for bias and mean squared error.  
- Empirical results: experiments on synthetic and real data (including large-scale matrix factorization) show improved computation-time performance over SGLD.  
- Presentation quality: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and notation is well-defined; pseudo-code provided in supplement.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis including detailed CLT proof and discussion of step-size choice and Brownian motion correlation.  
- Weakness: no detailed empirical or theoretical comparison with other SG-MCMC methods using high-order integrators.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and concrete discussion of computational cost despite claiming parallelizability.  
- Weakness: minor errors and inconsistencies (e.g., Algorithm 1 in supplement inconsistent with Equation (5); inconsistent statement about step size).  
- Overall assessment: novel and significant contribution with strong theory and satisfactory experiments, but needs more comparisons and cost analysis.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theoretical analysis, satisfactory empirical performance.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing comparisons to high-order integrators, no computational cost analysis, and a few minor errors.
