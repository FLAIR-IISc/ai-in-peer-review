- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to two parallel SGLD chains to reduce SG-MCMC bias while controlling variance.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds, showing higher convergence rates than known first-order SG-MCMC methods.  
- Paper is well-written and situates the method clearly among prior work.  
- Experiments (synthetic and real, including large-scale matrix factorization) strongly support SGRRLD’s effectiveness.  
- Theoretical analysis is a major strength, offering thorough convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental design and results are a clear strength, demonstrating improved convergence over SGLD.  
- Novelty concern: RR extrapolation in SG-MCMC is new, but RR extrapolation itself is a well-known idea, so overall novelty is unclear.  
- Lacks clear comparison to other SG-MCMC methods that use higher-order integrators.  
- Practical applicability concern: running two parallel chains with different step sizes may be computationally expensive; computational cost is not clearly discussed.  
- Significance: results could impact many applications, but practicality questions temper this significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contributions, solid experiments, potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty relative to known extrapolation ideas, unclear practicality and computational cost.  
- Minor comments: duplicated phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" appears twice; recommend clearer comparisons to higher-order integrators and a discussion of computational cost.
