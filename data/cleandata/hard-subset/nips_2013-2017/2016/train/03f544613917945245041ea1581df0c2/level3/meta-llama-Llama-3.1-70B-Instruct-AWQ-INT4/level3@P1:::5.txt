This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-RR-MCMC), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm is based on the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method that requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared-error can be bounded. The results show that SG-RR-MCMC attains higher rates of convergence than Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) in both finite-time and asymptotically.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The empirical results are satisfactory and show that the idea is effective in reducing the bias of SG-MCMC. The concept is simple and combines Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD, making it easy to implement and understand.

One of the strengths of the paper is its theoretical analysis, which provides a rigorous foundation for the proposed algorithm. The authors show that SG-RR-MCMC is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem, which is essential for establishing the validity of the algorithm. The non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared-error bounds are also derived, providing a clear understanding of the algorithm's performance.

The empirical results are also satisfactory, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in reducing the bias of SG-MCMC. The experiments on synthetic and real data show that SG-RR-MCMC outperforms SGLD in terms of bias and mean squared-error.

However, there are a few limitations and inconsistencies in the paper. Firstly, the statement about the step size in the paper is inconsistent.
