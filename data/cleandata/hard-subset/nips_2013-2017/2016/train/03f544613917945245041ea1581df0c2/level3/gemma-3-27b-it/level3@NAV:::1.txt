## Review of “Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo”

This paper presents a novel method, Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) for large-scale Bayesian inference. The core idea is to apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-known numerical acceleration technique – to SGLD by running two chains in parallel with different step sizes. This builds a useful bridge between the MCMC and Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE) literature, potentially benefiting both communities. The authors provide theoretical convergence results, demonstrating conditions under which SGRRLD outperforms vanilla SGLD, and validate these findings with experiments on both toy examples and a large-scale matrix factorization problem.

The strength of this work lies in its elegant simplicity and theoretical grounding. Applying RRE to SGLD is a natural and straightforward extension, and the authors convincingly demonstrate its potential for reducing bias. The theoretical analysis, including the central limit theorem and bounds on bias and MSE, provides a solid foundation for understanding the method's behavior. Empirical results, while not transformative, consistently show improvements over SGLD, particularly in scenarios where reducing bias is crucial. The clarity of the writing is also commendable; the paper is well-organized and the technical details are presented in a comprehensible manner.

However, the improvement observed in the large-scale experiments is modest. While consistent, the gains are not dramatic, suggesting that the practical impact might be limited in certain applications. This is not necessarily a weakness, but it’s important to acknowledge. Furthermore, Fig. 1a is misleading. The plot shows a Gaussian distribution parameterized by the empirical mean and variance, rather than the empirical posterior itself. Clarifying this is crucial for accurate interpretation. Similarly, Fig. 2a warrants a discussion of why the bias doesn’t consistently decrease with decreasing step size, as this is counterintuitive and potentially obscures the method's behavior.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Solid theoretical foundation and clear presentation.
*   Provides a practical and easy-to-implement improvement to SGLD.
*   Demonstrates potential for reducing bias in MCMC sampling.
*   Builds a useful connection between MCMC and SDE literatures.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Modest improvements observed in large-scale experiments.
*   Fig. 1a is misleading and requires clarification.
*   Lack of explanation for the counterintuitive behavior in Fig. 2a.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of large-scale MCMC. While the improvements are not groundbreaking, the method is theoretically sound, practically relevant, and clearly explained. Addressing the minor concerns regarding the figures and providing a more in-depth discussion of the observed phenomena would strengthen the paper further. I recommend acceptance.
