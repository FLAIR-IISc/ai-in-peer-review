- Presents SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two correlated SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and matrix factorization datasets show faster convergence and improved accuracy versus SGLD (and SGHMC in one experiment).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and situates the work within SG-MCMC literature.  
- Includes useful pseudocode and references to key prior work on Langevin dynamics and numerical integration.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation with potentially significant practical benefits for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Strength: compelling experimental validation, particularly on matrix factorization.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty concern given prior work on higher-order integrators and RR extrapolation.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of how SGRRLD advances beyond closely related prior efforts.  
- Practical concern: communication and computational overhead of maintaining correlated Brownian increments across parallel chains is not addressed.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear resource allocation—comparison may be unfair if SGLD used fewer cores than SGRRLD.  
- Request: authors should detail experimental setup, explain wall-clock impact of correlation, and justify synchronization costs.  
- Suggested baseline: compare SGRRLD to two independent parallel SGLD chains to isolate benefits of correlation.  
- Minor issue: duplicated definition of Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA).  
- Overall: valuable contribution with acceptance recommended conditional on addressing experimental setup, communication costs, and related-work clarification.
