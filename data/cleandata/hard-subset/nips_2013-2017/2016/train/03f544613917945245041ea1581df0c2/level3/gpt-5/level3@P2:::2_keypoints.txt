- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGLD) by running coupled chains at step sizes γ and γ/2 to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Estimator & theory: time-weighted combo of chains; proved CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds with asymptotic bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), finite-time MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)), yielding optimal K^−4/5 rates.  
- Novelty: adapts classical RR extrapolation to the stochastic-gradient setting with analysis of correlated noise — meaningful extension of existing SDE RR work.  
- Main technical caveat: key assumptions (regularity/dissipativity of U, gradient-noise moments/independence) are deferred to supplement and should be stated in the main text.  
- Applicability concern: unclear whether results hold for non-convex or multimodal posteriors common in deep Bayesian models or require strong log-concavity.  
- Gradient-noise dependence: unclear how bounds rely on Gaussian injected noise vs. minibatch variance and what minibatch-variance regimes preserve the claimed rates.  
- Coupling implementation: practical details missing — how Brownian increments are coupled, whether RNG streams or minibatches were shared, and the relative variance reduction from Brownian vs. minibatch coupling.  
- Experiments (synthetic): synthetic Gaussian tests strongly confirm bias/MSE reductions and predicted scalings.  
- Experiments (real): matrix factorization (MovieLens) shows only modest RMSE gains; practical benefit on large-scale data remains weak.  
- Parallelization claim: statement that SGRRLD benefits from parallelization “whereas SGLD cannot” is misleading — independent SGLD chains can be run in parallel and averaged.  
- Required baselines: paper should compare to (i) two parallel SGLD chains with matched compute, (ii) single SGLD with γ/2 and matched compute, and (iii) higher-order methods (e.g., SGHMC-s) on real data.  
- Ablations requested: effect of Brownian coupling on/off and sharing vs. independent minibatches; quantify sensitivity to gradient-noise level.  
- Clarity suggestions: move concise pseudocode into main text and state core theorem assumptions inline.  
- Practical guidance lacking: provide tuning advice for step-size schedules (α = 1/5 vs α = 1/3 trade-offs), fixed-K tuning, and minibatch coupling strategies.  
- Experimental reporting: make compute-matching precise (report total gradient evaluations and FLOPs alongside wall-clock).  
- Positive points: simple, low-overhead method; solid theoretical development (CLT and bounds); synthetic validation and extension to SGHMC shown.  
- Negative points: limited real-data gains, potentially unfair experimental comparisons, assumptions tucked in supplement, and missing coupling/minibatch ablations.  
- Open questions for authors: exact assumptions on U/f/L for Theorems 1–2, whether analysis covers multimodal/non-convex U, how Brownian increments and minibatches were coupled in practice, empirical chain correlation diagnostics, and results for compute-matched baselines including SGHMC-s.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject — strong theory and clean idea but needs stronger, fairer experiments, clear assumptions, and coupling ablations to justify acceptance.
