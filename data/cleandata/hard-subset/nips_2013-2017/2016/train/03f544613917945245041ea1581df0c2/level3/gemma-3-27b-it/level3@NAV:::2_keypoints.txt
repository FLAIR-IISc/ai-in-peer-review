- Proposes SGRRLD: run two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combine via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Novelty: clever application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, building on SGLD and RR for SDEs.  
- Theory: provides convergence proofs and claims improved rates over standard SGLD.  
- Clarity and implementation: algorithm is clearly described and straightforward to implement.  
- Synthetic results: convincingly demonstrate bias reduction.  
- Real-data performance: only marginal RMSE improvement on MovieLens, weakening practical significance.  
- Unclear theoretical conditions: assumptions on U (e.g., multimodality vs convexity) for Theorems 1 & 2 are not fully specified.  
- Gradient noise correlation: impact not fully explored; uncorrelated gradient noise may negate benefits and reported chain correlations are missing.  
- Computational-cost claim: claiming comparable time to SGLD is potentially misleading; need direct comparison to multi-chain SGLD (e.g., two SGLD chains with γ/2) to isolate RR advantage.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; authors should clarify theoretical assumptions, analyze gradient-noise effects, justify computation claims, and provide stronger real-data experiments.
