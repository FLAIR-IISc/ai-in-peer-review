- Proposes SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias in large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees, including consistency and a central limit theorem.  
- Empirically demonstrates improvements over SGLD on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD/SGMCMC and extension to SGHMC.  
- Technically sound: rigorous mathematical presentation and correct-looking derivations tied to SDE theory.  
- Clear and well-organized exposition that situates the work within MCMC and SDE literature.  
- Practical: simple to implement (two parallel SGLD chains) and amenable to distributed architectures.  
- Strong connection to prior work (SGLD, SGHMC, Talay & Tubaro, Lemaire & Pagès) providing theoretical justification.  
- Empirical gains are statistically significant but modest on large-scale datasets (e.g., MovieLens).  
- Figure 1a is misleading: the overlaid Gaussian is not clearly stated as using empirical mean/variance and needs caption clarification.  
- Figure 2a shows non-monotonic bias vs. step size; requires deeper, more intuitive discussion (beyond the 1/(Kγ) term).  
- Limited experimental exploration of correlated Brownian increments (theory allows correlations but experiments use Σ = I).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, empirically validated, and practically usable contribution to scalable MCMC.  
- Arguments against acceptance: only modest improvements on large-scale problems and minor clarity issues in figures.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Figure 1a, expand discussion of bias non-monotonicity in Figure 2a, and briefly discuss effects of different correlation matrices.
