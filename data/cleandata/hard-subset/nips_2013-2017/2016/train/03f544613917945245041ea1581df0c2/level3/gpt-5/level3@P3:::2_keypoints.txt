- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to cancel O(γ) weak discretization bias.  
- Proves CLT for the extrapolated estimator with optimal Gaussian coupling (Σ = I) minimizing asymptotic variance.  
- Provides non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)).  
- Shows improved finite-time rates: bias K−2/3 and MSE K−4/5 versus SGLD’s K−1/2 and K−2/3.  
- Synthetic experiments confirm theoretical bias and MSE reductions; SGHMC extension appears natural.  
- MovieLens matrix factorization yields modest RMSE gains at matched wall-clock when two chains are parallelized.  
- Main novelty: transfer classical RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with correlated Brownian increments and provide convergence proofs.  
- Algorithm is simple and easy to implement, requiring only two chains with prescribed step-size relation and Gaussian coupling.  
- Theory is solid and separates fluctuation versus bias terms, showing how RR cancels the leading O(γ) term.  
- Key assumptions are deferred to the supplement and require g ∈ C9 solving a Poisson equation with polynomial growth.  
- Need clearer statement of conditions on U, f, and L in main text, especially regarding dissipativity, Lipschitzness, and multimodality.  
- Unclear whether multimodal targets are allowed or what ergodicity/regularity conditions guarantee existence of g.  
- Role of gradient-noise correlation is insufficiently explained: how RR cancels O(γ) if mini-batch noises across chains are independent.  
- Recommend clarifying which weak error terms are unaffected by cross-chain gradient-noise decorrelation and guidance on sharing mini-batches.  
- Empirical chain cross-correlation (e.g., for f) was not reported; measuring it would clarify RR behavior with uncorrelated mini-batch noise.  
- Compute-time comparisons may be unfair: need baselines of two independent SGLD chains averaged, SGLD with γ/2, and SGLD with Gaussian coupling, all compute-matched.  
- Real-data gains are small and error bars not shown; additional tasks and sensitivity studies (mini-batch size, step-size schedules) are needed.  
- RR reduces discretization bias but does not improve mixing across modes; limitations for multimodal targets should be discussed.  
- Algorithm clarity is good; highlight Gaussian coupling (Σ = I) in main algorithm box and fix minor notational typos.  
- Overall judgment: strong theoretical significance and simple practical recipe, but practical impact limited without clearer assumptions and compute-matched baselines — recommendation: weak accept pending revisions.
