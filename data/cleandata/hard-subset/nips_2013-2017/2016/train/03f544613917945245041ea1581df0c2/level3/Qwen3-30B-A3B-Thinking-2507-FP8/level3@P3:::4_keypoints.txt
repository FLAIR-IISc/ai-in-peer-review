- Paper proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Theoretical strength: claims improved MSE convergence rate O(K^(-4/5)), outperforming SGLD's O(K^(-2/3)).
- Implementation strength: method is simple to implement, requiring only parallel running of two chains with different step sizes.
- Theoretical analysis strength: provides asymptotic consistency, CLTs, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds with clear proofs.
- Empirical support: experiments on synthetic and real data show improved performance over SGLD and SGHMC.
- Major theoretical concern: apparent contradiction between Theorem 1 (rate O(K^(-(1-α)/2 ∧ 2α))) and Theorem 2 (rate O(K^(-(4α)∧(1-α)))), which both claim different optimal rates at α=1/5 vs α=1/3 and need clarification.
- Unfair computational comparison: Figure 5 does not account for SGRRLD’s ~3× computational cost (two chains, one with twice as many steps), so timing comparisons are misleading.
- Empirical/theory mismatch: Figure 3 shows best bias at α=0.2 while the paper claims optimal α=1/3; the authors must explain this discrepancy.
- Small-step behavior unclear: paper asserts SGLD bias is O(γ + 1/(Kγ)) but does not explain why the 1/(Kγ) term does not dominate for small γ in practice.
- Minor issues to fix: typos and notation errors (ϑt vs θ, undefined "RR" and Γ_K), imprecise smoothness assumptions for f, labeling error in Figure 5, and non-bold vectors.
- Overall assessment: novel and theoretically solid contribution with practical relevance, but key discrepancies (α and timing) and minor fixes must be addressed.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying the α/rate contradictions, correcting computational comparisons, and fixing noted typos and presentation issues.
