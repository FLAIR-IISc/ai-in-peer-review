- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and extrapolating to reduce bias.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds, and faster convergence rates than SGLD.  
- Pseudocode is clear and algorithm is easy to implement.  
- Synthetic linear Gaussian experiments show clear bias reduction of SGRRLD over SGLD.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) results show only slight RMSE improvement, weakening evidence of practical benefit.  
- Reviewer did not verify proof details but notes novelty is applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with convergence proofs.  
- Questions about assumptions: unclear what conditions on U are required for Theorems 1 and 2 and whether U can be multimodal.  
- Concerns about gradient-noise correlation: if gradient noises are uncorrelated, how does combining chains improve error order and do chains remain correlated in practice?  
- Requests empirical observation of correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.  
- Computation-time claim is weak: need comparison to running multiple parallel SGLD chains (e.g., two chains or SGLD with step size γ/2) to isolate benefits.  
- Calls for more and stronger real-data experiments to substantiate practical significance.  
- Presentation is well-written but recommends improving presentation of real-data results.  
- Strengths: novel combination of RR extrapolation with SGLD and solid theoretical support.  
- Weaknesses: limited practical evidence and insufficient computational comparisons; recommends revision before acceptance.
