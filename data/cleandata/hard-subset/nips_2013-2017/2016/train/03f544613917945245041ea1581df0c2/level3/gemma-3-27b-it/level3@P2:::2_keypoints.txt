- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes and combining results to reduce bias.
- Provides theoretical analysis: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved convergence rates versus SGLD.
- Novelty: adapting Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC with convergence proofs is a significant contribution.
- Theoretical rigor: Theorems 1 & 2 give detailed bias and MSE bounds that clarify algorithm behavior.
- Clarity: Algorithm and notation are clearly presented, with helpful pseudo-code in the supplement.
- Synthetic experiments: Linear Gaussian results strongly support the claimed bias reduction.
- Parallelizability: Inherent parallelism is a practical advantage for modern computing architectures.
- Real-data results: MovieLens experiments show only modest RMSE gains and unclear computational efficiency improvements.
- Conditions on U: Theorem assumptions on the potential U are not explicitly stated, leaving applicability to non-convex or multimodal U ambiguous.
- Gradient noise correlation: The impact and mechanism of correlating Brownian increments are insufficiently explored and explained.
- Comparison to multi-chain SGLD: Lacks direct comparisons to running multiple parallel SGLD chains (including SGLD with halved step size), making efficiency claims inconclusive.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound method with convincing synthetic validation and useful parallelism for large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak real-data evidence, unclear assumptions on U, incomplete explanation of noise correlation effects, and insufficient benchmarking against parallel SGLD alternatives.
- Overall assessment: Technically strong and promising but requires addressing empirical and theoretical ambiguities before full endorsement.
- Recommendation and required revisions: Weak accept with major revisions—clarify conditions on U, strengthen real-data analyses (including multi-chain SGLD comparisons), elaborate on gradient noise correlation effects with experiment evidence, and explicitly compare computational cost to parallel SGLD.
