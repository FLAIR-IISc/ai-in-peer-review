- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to two SGLD chains (steps γ and γ/2) with correlated Gaussian noise to cancel leading discretization bias.
- Shows perfect correlation of noise (Σ = Id) minimizes asymptotic variance for the extrapolated estimator.
- Proves a CLT for the extrapolated estimator and non-asymptotic bounds: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)), giving optimal finite-time rates bias O(K−2/3) and MSE O(K−4/5).
- For decreasing steps γk ∝ k−α the CLT is optimized at α = 1/5, yielding O(K−2/5) weak error, matching higher-order integrator accuracy.
- Connects to prior RR work for diffusions [23] and higher-order integrator analyses [10], extending RR to stochastic-gradient MCMC.
- Synthetic experiments validate predicted bias/MSE scalings convincingly.
- MovieLens matrix factorization experiment shows improved test RMSE vs. SGLD at matched wall-clock time.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated idea bridging numerical analysis (RR) and scalable Bayesian sampling.
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis using Poisson equation techniques and explicit finite-time rates.
- Strength: generic framework applicable to multiple SG-MCMC methods, with an SGHMC variant matching higher-order accuracy.
- Weakness: incremental novelty—combines known ideas from [10] and [23] with modest conceptual leap.
- Weakness: practical concerns about implementing the required coupled Gaussian noise—potential synchronization/communication overhead not measured.
- Weakness: fairness of wall-clock comparisons if SGRRLD uses two cores while SGLD uses one is unclear.
- Weakness: missing baseline of two independent SGLD chains run in parallel (same hardware) or single SGLD with doubled mini-batch size.
- Weakness: strong theoretical assumptions (high smoothness/ergodicity) are deferred to supplement and need clearer discussion for real models.
- Suggestion: summarize key theoretical assumptions and empirically check the claim that Gaussian noise dominates stochastic gradient noise.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-organized but would benefit from pseudo-code and a schematic of the noise coupling in the main text.
- Requests: clarify how correlated Gaussian increments are implemented in parallel, and quantify any overhead.
- Requests: report resource allocation in MovieLens experiments (core counts, CPU utilization) and whether SGLD had equivalent compute.
- Requests: specify how mini-batches are selected across chains and if their correlation affects performance.
- Requests: confirm whether step sizes were tuned per method and report robustness to tuning choices.
- Suggested experiments: add baseline of two independent SGLD chains with same hardware, single SGLD with doubled batch size, and an SGLD higher-order integrator variant.
- Suggested experiments: report wall-clock breakdown including synchronization time, and sensitivity to batch size and problem dimension.
- Suggested extension: explore recursive/multi-level RR to assess further gains or limits.
- Pros: simple, elegant technique achieving higher-order accuracy without complex integrators and well-situated in literature.
- Cons: moderate novelty, unclear practical overhead, and missing parallel baseline.
- Overall: solid, rigorous paper with promising results but needs clarification of resource fairness and noise-coupling overhead; reviewer leans positive pending those fixes.
