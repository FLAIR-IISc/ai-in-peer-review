- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs.  
- Novel theoretical contribution showing asymptotically lower bias and faster MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Algorithm is clearly described and simple to implement, increasing potential impact.  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments convincingly demonstrate bias reduction, supporting the theory.  
- Limited empirical evidence on real-world data; marginal RMSE improvement on MovieLens questions practical utility.  
- Computational comparison is potentially unfair: two-chain parallel SGRRLD compared to single-chain SGLD.  
- Recommends comparing to two parallel SGLD chains or single SGLD with γ/2 (matched gradient evaluations) to isolate RR benefits.  
- Theoretical conditions need clarification (assumptions on potential U, applicability to multimodal posteriors).  
- Lacks intuitive explanation of error cancellation under stochastic gradient noise and possible chain decorrelation.  
- Requests report on observed correlation between the two chains in real-data experiments.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and elegant method, but practical significance not yet convincingly demonstrated; stronger experiments required.
