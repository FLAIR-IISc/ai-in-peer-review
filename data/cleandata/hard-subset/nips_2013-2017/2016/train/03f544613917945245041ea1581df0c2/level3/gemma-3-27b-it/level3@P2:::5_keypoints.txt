- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing asymptotic consistency, bias and MSE bounds, and improved convergence rates versus SGLD.
- Presents a central limit theorem result and non-asymptotic error bounds supporting theoretical soundness.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real (matrix factorization) datasets shows improvements over SGLD and sometimes matches higher-order integrators.
- Novel contribution: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, connecting to known numerical-analysis techniques.
- Algorithm is easily parallelizable since the two chains can run independently, making it practical for large-scale use.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains methods and theory.
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art higher-order integrators on large-scale tasks weakens claims about matching their accuracy/cost.
- Step-size selection discussion is inconsistent/confusing, with different optimal choices for bias vs. MSE.
- Minor discrepancy between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5) in the main text needs clarification.
- Claim that Σ=I always minimizes variance (Eq. 8) is asserted too strongly and needs more justification.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically rigorous, practical, parallelizable, and empirically promising.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comprehensive comparisons and a few minor inconsistencies reduce polish.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify step-size selection, fix the algorithmic inconsistency, and ideally expand experimental comparisons.
