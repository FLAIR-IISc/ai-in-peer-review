- Addresses asymptotic bias in SG-MCMC by applying Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to cancel leading-order discretization error.  
- Runs two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 (γ/2 run for 2K steps) and combines estimators as 2*est(γ/2) − est(γ) to remove O(γ) bias.  
- Introduces correlation between injected Gaussian noises in the two chains to control variance of the combined estimator.  
- Proposes SGRRLD (Stochastic Gradient Richardson–Romberg Langevin Dynamics) as application to SGLD.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD versus O(K⁻²/³) for standard SGLD, matching higher-order integrator accuracy using a first-order integrator.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and a large-scale matrix factorization task supports theoretical claims.  
- Strength: tackles an important practical problem with rigorous theory and a simple, easily implementable method.  
- Strength: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with likely broad impact and generalizability (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Strength: clear writing and simple algorithmic change that facilitates adoption.  
- Weakness: theoretical reliance on an error expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²); unclear which SG-MCMC variants satisfy this and what conditions are required.  
- Weakness: computational inefficiency when running chains in parallel (one process idle); question about alternative schemes like two independent γ/2 chains and averaging.  
- Weakness: unclear role of minibatch/stochastic gradient correlation—need discussion whether using the same minibatch across chains helps variance reduction.  
- Weakness: experiments could be stronger (run until convergence, include non-Bayesian baselines like SGD for context).  
- Weakness: paper needs a clear pseudocode box for SGRRLD early to aid practitioners.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, impactful paper with minor clarifications requested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
