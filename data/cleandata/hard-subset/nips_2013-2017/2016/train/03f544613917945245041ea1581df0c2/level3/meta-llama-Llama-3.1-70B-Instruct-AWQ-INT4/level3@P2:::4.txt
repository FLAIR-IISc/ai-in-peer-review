This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC methods while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm applies the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm, which is a popular SG-MCMC method. The RR extrapolation is a numerical sequence acceleration method that requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and the mean squared error (MSE). They show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically, and that it achieves the theoretical accuracy of methods that are based on higher-order integrators.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The theoretical results are solid and well-supported by the provided proofs. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in reducing the bias and MSE of the estimator.

However, there are some concerns about the derivation of the specific convergence rates from the stated theorem. The authors should provide more details on how they obtain the convergence rates, as the current explanation is not sufficient.

Another practical concern is that the proposed method requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel, which can be computationally expensive. The authors should provide a fair comparison with SGLD by scaling the timing of SGLD accordingly.

In Figure 3, the best curve appears at α=0.2, which conflicts with the text claim of α=1/3. The authors should explain this discrepancy and provide more details on how they obtain the empirical rate.

Additionally, the authors should address the question about line 256, where the 1/(Kγ) term should dominate when the step size is very small. They should explain why this does not occur for SGLD.

Minor issues include the use of different symbols for θ, the lack of definition for "RR" and Γ_K, and the need for precise smoothness assumptions for the function f.

Overall, the paper presents a promising new algorithm for SG-MCMC, but some issues need to be addressed to make the paper more solid.

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm that improves the bias and MSE convergence rates of existing SG-MCMC methods.
* The theoretical results are solid and well-supported by the provided proofs.
* The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
