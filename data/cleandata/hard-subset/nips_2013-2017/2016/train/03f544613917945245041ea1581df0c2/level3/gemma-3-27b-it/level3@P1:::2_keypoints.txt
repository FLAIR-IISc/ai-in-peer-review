- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias in SG-MCMC.

- Theoretical contributions include asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.

- Novelty: explicit application of Richardson-Romberg to SG-MCMC with derived convergence guarantees, building on prior SDE and SG-MCMC work.

- Empirical results: synthetic experiments convincingly show bias reduction; MovieLens (real-data) shows only modest improvements.

- Strengths: technically sound, rigorous analysis, clear presentation, easy to implement, and amenable to parallelization.

- Weaknesses: practical gains on real datasets appear modest and their significance is unclear.

- Missing baseline: no direct comparison to running multiple independent SGLD chains (standard variance-reduction baseline).

- Unclear assumptions: the paper does not clearly state required conditions on the potential U (e.g., multimodality, smoothness, convexity) for the theorems.

- Correlated Brownian motion: justification provided, but the effect of the degree of correlation on performance is not analyzed.

- Concern about decorrelation: if gradient noise is largely uncorrelated, chains may decorrelate and diminish RR benefits—this is not investigated.

- Empirical question: unclear whether the authors observed significant inter-chain correlation on real data and how it evolved over time.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel RR application to SG-MCMC, strong theoretical guarantees, clear algorithm, and demonstrated synthetic bias reduction.

- Arguments against acceptance: modest real-world gains, lack of multi-chain SGLD comparison, unclear U constraints, and insufficient discussion of gradient-noise correlation.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: include direct comparison to two parallel SGLD chains, investigate effect of gradient-noise correlation, clarify assumptions on U, and run more extensive real-world experiments.
