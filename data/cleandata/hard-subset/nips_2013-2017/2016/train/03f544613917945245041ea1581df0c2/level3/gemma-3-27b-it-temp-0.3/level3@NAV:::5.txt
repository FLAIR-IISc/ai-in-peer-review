## Review of “Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo”

This paper proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-RR-MCMC), a novel sampling algorithm designed to reduce the bias inherent in Stochastic Gradient MCMC (SG-MCMC) methods. The core idea is elegantly simple: leverage Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes. This allows for a bias reduction without introducing communication overhead between the chains. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating asymptotic consistency and derive bounds on bias and mean squared error. Empirical results on both synthetic and real datasets (matrix factorization) confirm the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing improved convergence rates compared to standard SGLD.

The work builds upon the established foundation of SG-MCMC, particularly SGLD [Welling & Teh, 2011], and extends it by incorporating a well-known numerical technique (Richardson-Romberg) from the numerical analysis literature [Talay & Tubaro, 1990]. The theoretical analysis is a significant contribution, providing a rigorous justification for the method’s improved performance. The parallel nature of the algorithm is also a notable strength, making it well-suited for modern computing architectures. 

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the authors claim improved convergence rates, they do not provide a formal proof of an improved rate, only stating it is comparable to methods using higher-order integrators. A direct comparison to higher-order integrators in experiments would strengthen this claim. Furthermore, there's an inconsistency regarding the step size definition within the paper, which needs clarification. More critically, Algorithm 1 in the supplementary material appears inconsistent with Equation (5), creating confusion for readers attempting to implement the method.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and conceptually simple approach to bias reduction in SG-MCMC.
*   Solid theoretical foundation with proofs of consistency and error bounds.
*   Empirically validated effectiveness on both synthetic and real datasets.
*   Algorithm is easily parallelizable.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of a formal proof of improved convergence rate.
*   Missing experimental comparison with higher-order integrators.
*   Inconsistency in step size definition and algorithm implementation.



**Overall:**

Despite the minor flaws, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically supported. Addressing the inconsistencies and providing a more comprehensive experimental evaluation would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
