This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), that aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation on the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) estimator by considering two SGLD chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive bounds for its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error, and show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than all known SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators in both finite-time and asymptotically.
