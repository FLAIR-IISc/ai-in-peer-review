- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD attains MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}) versus SGLD O(K^{-2/3}); asymptotic bias O(γ^2) and variance O(γ^4).  
- Rigorous analysis with proofs of asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Method generalizes to other SG-MCMC algorithms and preserves parallelizability.  
- Practical: requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real tasks show SGRRLD outperforming SGLD for equal computation time.  
- Major concern: Figure 5 wall-clock comparisons ignore SGRRLD’s parallelism; fair comparison requires time scaling (SGRRLD should be compared at 2/3 the wall-clock), so reported gains are overstated by ~50%.  
- Figure 3 inconsistency: text states optimal α=1/3 for bias but plot indicates α≈0.2; authors must explain this discrepancy and noisy curve behavior.  
- Unexplained empirical behavior: SGLD does not show expected bias growth at very small step sizes (contradicts Theorem 2) and needs clarification.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation (θ vs ϑ), undefined acronyms (RR), undefined terms (Γ_K), imprecise smoothness assumptions for f, and inconsistent vector notation.  
- Figure 2 label is incorrect and should read “Bias vs. step size.”  
- Overall: strong theoretical and methodological contribution and originality, but experimental evaluation must be revised to account for two-chain computational cost.  
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to correct experimental time-scaling and to resolve theoretical–empirical discrepancies; current results overstate efficiency if not fixed.
