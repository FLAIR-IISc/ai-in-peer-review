**Review of "Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and achieves higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically. The paper also presents experimental results on synthetic and real data, demonstrating the effectiveness of SGRRLD.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that addresses the bias issue in SGLD, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error.
3. The experimental results on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in reducing bias and improving convergence rates.
4. The paper is well-written, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the two sequences of Gaussian random variables and stochastic gradients are correlated, but it is not clear how this correlation affects the performance of SGRRLD.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of SGRRLD, which is an important consideration in practice.
3. The real-data experiment on the MovieLens dataset shows only a slight improvement in RMSE, which may not be significant enough to justify the use of SGRRLD over SGLD.
4. The paper does not provide a comparison with other SG-MCMC algorithms, such as Stochastic Gradient Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (SGHMC), which may be more effective in certain scenarios.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the authors could provide more details on the conditions required for Theorems 1 and 2, such as the assumptions on the potential energy function U.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement. However, some of the theoretical derivations could be improved for better readability.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that addresses the bias issue in SGLD, which is a significant contribution to the field. However, the idea of applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is not entirely new, as it has been explored in other contexts.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant issue in SG-MCMC, namely the bias issue in SGLD, and proposes a novel algorithm that achieves higher rates of convergence. The results have the potential to impact the field of Bayesian machine learning and Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods.

**Recommendations**

1. Provide more details on the conditions required for Theorems 1 and 2.
2. Analyze the computational cost of SGRRLD and compare it with other SG-MCMC algorithms.
3. Provide more experimental results on real data, including comparisons with other SG-MCMC algorithms.
4. Consider exploring the use of multi-level Monte Carlo approaches in the framework of SGRRLD.

**Acceptance**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend accepting the paper for publication in NIPS, with minor revisions to address the recommendations above.
