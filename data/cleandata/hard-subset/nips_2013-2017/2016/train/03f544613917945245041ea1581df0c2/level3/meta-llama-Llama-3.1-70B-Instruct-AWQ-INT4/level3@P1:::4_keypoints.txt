- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Provides formal theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Claims improved convergence rates over existing SG-MCMC methods, including SGLD.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on synthetic and real data showing reduced bias and improved convergence.
- Algorithm is relatively easy to implement with minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC.
- Derivation of convergence rates is unclear and may contain fatal flaws according to the reviewer.
- Comparison of computation time with SGLD is unfair: SGRRLD requires roughly three times the computation time.
- Some empirical results (notably Figure 3) are inconsistent with the theoretical claims.
- Paper is a significant and original contribution to SG-MCMC if theoretical issues are resolved.
- Overall well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of theory and experiments.
- Minor issues with notation, formatting, and figure labeling need correction.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify convergence derivations, correct runtime comparisons, and reconcile empirical results with theory.
