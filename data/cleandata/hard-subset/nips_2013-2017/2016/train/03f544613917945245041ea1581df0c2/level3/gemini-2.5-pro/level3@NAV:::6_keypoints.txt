- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce discretization bias.  
- Variance control: Uses correlated Gaussian noise between chains to control estimator variance.  
- Positioning: Builds on prior analyses of higher-order integrators and RR extrapolation for SDEs.  
- Presentation & quality: Paper is clearly written and technically strong.  
- Theory: Provides thorough asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analyses showing improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Experiments (synthetic): Synthetic experiments convincingly verify bias and MSE reduction claims.  
- Experiments (application): Matrix factorization results show tangible wall-clock improvements over SGLD, indicating practical promise.  
- Originality concern: Contribution may be incremental since it combines two well-established techniques.  
- Practicality/fairness concern: Correlated-noise requirement implies per-step synchronization/communication overhead not discussed or accounted for in wall-clock comparisons.  
- Experimental clarification requested: Authors should state whether single-chain SGLD baseline had the same total computational resources as two-chain SGRRLD.  
- Suggested baseline: Recommend comparing SGRRLD to two independent SGLD chains running in parallel to isolate benefits of RR extrapolation from extra compute.  
- Minor: The term “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is duplicated on lines 25 and 70.
