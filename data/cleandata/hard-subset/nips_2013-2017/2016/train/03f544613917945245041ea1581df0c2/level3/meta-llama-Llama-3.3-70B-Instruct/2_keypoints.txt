- Proposes a novel semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to incorporate domain knowledge.  
- Introduces the γ-margin property as a new notion of clusterability characterizing cluster separability.  
- Provides an efficient clustering algorithm that succeeds w.h.p. for inputs satisfying γ-margin with γ > 1.  
- Algorithm makes O(k log n + k^2 log k) same-cluster queries and runs in O(kn log n) time.  
- Proves k-means is NP-hard even when the optimal solution satisfies the γ-margin property for γ = √3.4.  
- Shows that access to a limited number of queries can make the NP-hard problem efficiently solvable.  
- Establishes a lower bound of Ω(log k + log n) queries required to make the problem feasible.  
- Strengths: novel framework, efficient algorithm, and thorough computational and query complexity analysis.  
- Strengths: paper is well-written with clear and concise presentation of results.  
- Weakness: assumes the oracle follows a center-based clustering with margin, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on real-world applicability of the framework.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient experimental evaluation to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Originality: combines partial supervision and computational complexity themes and introduces a distinct clusterability notion.  
- Significance: potential applications in image segmentation, customer segmentation, gene expression analysis, and motivates further research.  
- Recommendation: overall acceptance suggested, with revisions to address applicability and empirical validation.
