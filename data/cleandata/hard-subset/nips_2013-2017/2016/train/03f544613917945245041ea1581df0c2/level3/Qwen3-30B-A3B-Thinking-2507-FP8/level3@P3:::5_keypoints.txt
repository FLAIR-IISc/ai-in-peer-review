- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining results (applied to SGLD → SGRRLD).  
- Theoretical analysis shows improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Builds on Welling & Teh (2011) and Talay & Tubaro (1990); novel application to stochastic-gradient setting and related to Chen et al. (2015).  
- Conceptually simple, effective, and embarrassingly parallel — suitable for distributed computation.  
- Method is general and could extend to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (including large-scale matrix factorization) support theoretical claims.  
- Strong technical soundness with rigorous theoretical proofs in supplementary material.  
- Inconsistent statement about the step size between Section 1 and Theorem 2.  
- Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5) in the main text, harming reproducibility.  
- Claims parity with higher-order integrator accuracy but lacks experimental comparison to those methods.  
- Suggestion that high-order integrators could yield similar rates is not experimentally validated.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but reduced by step-size ambiguity and algorithm inconsistencies; supplementary material required for full understanding.  
- Originality: novel and well-contextualized combination of known techniques.  
- Significance: practical way to improve convergence without complex higher-order integrators; valuable for scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix algorithm inconsistencies and clarify step-size statements; consider adding comparisons to higher-order integrators.  
- Overall: strong contribution with convincing theory and experiments; main weaknesses are missing experimental comparisons and typographical/algorithm-description errors likely fixable.
