- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes and combining them to reduce bias.  
- Extends RR extrapolation from SDE numerical analysis to the stochastic gradient setting.  
- Theoretical contributions: asymptotic consistency, CLT guarantees, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds claiming SGRRLD achieves O(K^-4/5) MSE vs SGLD's O(K^-2/3).  
- Practical strengths: conceptually simple, easy to implement, inherently parallel, and applicable to other SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Empirical results: synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization experiments show improved accuracy with comparable time when parallelized.  
- Main theoretical concern: derivation of O(K^-4/5) rate appears problematic—claim that α=1/5 is optimal conflicts with empirical Figure 3 (best performance at α=0.2) and unexplained fluctuations.  
- Runtime comparison issue: Figure 5 is unfair since SGRRLD runs two chains (~3× single-core cost); SGLD runtime should be scaled for a fair comparison, which would reduce the reported gains.  
- Unexplained small-step behavior: paper claims 1/(Kγ) term dominates for small γ, but experiments (Figure 2) show bias/MSE increase for very small γ without explanation.  
- Minor technical issues: RR not defined at first mention, Γ_K used without definition, vague smoothness assumptions for f, Figure 2 labeling error, and inconsistent parameter symbol (ϑ vs θ).  
- Overall assessment: promising and mostly rigorous work with novel, practical idea, but discrepancies between theory and experiments and unfair timing comparisons undermine the contribution.  
- Recommendation: major revision — clarify theoretical derivations, reconcile theory with empirical results, correct runtime comparisons, and fix technical/notation errors.
