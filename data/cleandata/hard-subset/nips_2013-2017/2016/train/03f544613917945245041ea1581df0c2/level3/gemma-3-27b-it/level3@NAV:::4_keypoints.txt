- Proposes SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two chains with different step sizes and combining outputs.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and improved MSE bounds.  
- Method is simple, parallelizable, and easy to implement.  
- Originality: first application of RR extrapolation to the SG-MCMC context.  
- Potential significance: addresses bias in first-order integrators for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Major concern: derivation of the K^{-4/5} MSE rate from Theorem 1 is unclear; the link from the Taylor expansion to this rate needs substantial clarification.  
- Timing comparison flaw: SGRRLD uses roughly twice the computation (two chains, one with twice the steps), so reported performance gap should be reduced by ~33%.  
- Empirical inconsistency: Figure 3 shows best bias reduction at α=0.2, contradicting the text's α=1/3 claim; the “optimal” curve fluctuates and requires further investigation.  
- Unresolved theoretical question (line 256): why doesn't the 1/(Kγ) term dominate for SGLD when the stepsize is very small?  
- Minor issues on notation and presentation: Eq.1 uses a different symbol than θ; “RR” not defined (line 94); Γ_K not defined (line 131); “smooth function f” needs precise smoothness assumptions (line 160); figure labeling error (line 249); vectors should be bold.  
- Acceptance pros: novelty of RR extrapolation, solid theory, potential performance gains, and implementability.  
- Acceptance cons: unclear convergence-rate derivation, unfair timing comparison, empirical-theory discrepancies, and the unresolved line-256 issue.
