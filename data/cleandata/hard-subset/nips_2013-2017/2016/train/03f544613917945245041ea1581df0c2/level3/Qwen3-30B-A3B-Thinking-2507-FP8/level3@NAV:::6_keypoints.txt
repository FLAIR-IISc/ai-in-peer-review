- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC via two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Theoretical result: Proves asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence with MSE O(K^{-4/5}), improving over SGLD's O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-data (large-scale matrix factorization) experiments show improved accuracy over SGLD.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical contributions building on prior numerical integrator and RR extrapolation work, with clear literature positioning.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and thorough experimental validation across synthetic and large-scale tasks.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental, directly building on prior works [10,23] rather than offering a breakthrough.  
- Weakness: Practical applicability limited by unaddressed per-step communication costs for correlated chains in distributed settings.  
- Experimental concern: Unclear whether SGLD and SGRRLD had equivalent compute resources; wall-clock time claim lacks resource-allocation details.  
- Baseline issue: Comparison should include two independent SGLD chains as an embarrassingly parallel baseline.  
- Minor issue: Duplication of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" in manuscript (lines 25 and 70) needs fixing.  
- Review summary: Quality strong theoretically but weakened by practical concerns; Clarity generally good with minor edits; Originality incremental; Significance present but limited by communication considerations.  
- Recommendation: Solid technical contribution that would be stronger with clarifications on experimental setup and communication costs.
