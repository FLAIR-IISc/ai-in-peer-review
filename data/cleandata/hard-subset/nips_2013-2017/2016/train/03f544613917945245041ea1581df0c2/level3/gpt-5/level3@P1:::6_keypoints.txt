- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (γ and γ/2) and combining them to reduce discretization bias.  
- Correlates Gaussian noises across chains (ideal coupling Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2) to minimize asymptotic variance of the RR estimator.  
- Provides asymptotic and non-asymptotic analysis: consistency, CLT, bias O(γ2), MSE O(γ4) for constant steps, and finite-time MSE O(K−4/5) with optimal α = 1/5.  
- Shows improvement over first-order SG-MCMC (SGLD) rates (O(K−4/5) vs O(K−2/3)).  
- Approach is generic and extends to SGHMC, with SGHMC variant matching higher-order integrators using first-order updates.  
- Synthetic experiments (linear-Gaussian) validate predicted bias/MSE scalings and γ–K tradeoffs (1/(Kγ) term).  
- MovieLens matrix factorization experiments show wall-clock improvements over SGLD.  
- Builds on prior work: SGLD, SG-MCMC integrator theory, and RR extrapolation for SDE invariant measures.  
- Novelty: adapting RR and variance-control coupling to stochastic-gradient setting is a meaningful, implementable contribution.  
- Theory quality: CLT and non-asymptotic bounds are carefully stated and consistent with SDE literature adapted to SG noise.  
- Assumptions: require strong smoothness/Poisson-equation conditions (C9 and technical U/L conditions); standard but strong for this line of work.  
- Empirics: synthetic validation convincing; real-data results compelling but fairness/resource issues remain.  
- Clarity: well-organized presentation; role of correlated noise and same minibatch-size distribution requirement are clearly explained; minor typos noted.  
- Originality assessment: incremental relative to RR in exact-gradient settings and higher-order integrator literature, but provides new finite-time analysis and practical algorithmic recipe.  
- Significance: useful for reducing bias without higher-order integrators, practical if parallel resources available, and potentially transferable to other SG-MCMC samplers.  
- Main weakness — computational budget fairness: need baselines matching resources (two parallel SGLD chains, SGLD with twice iterations or gradient evaluations).  
- Main weakness — communication/coupling overhead: quantify synchronization and minibatch-sharing costs, high-dimensional communication, and whether minibatches are shared or independent.  
- Main weakness — sensitivity to step-size tuning: include decreasing-step experiments on real data and provide practical tuning guidelines (α = 1/5 vs 1/3 considerations).  
- Main weakness — comparison to higher-order baselines: include comparisons to second-order/higher-order integrator baselines for SGLD to support cost-accuracy claims.  
- Main weakness — scope/limitations: discuss behavior when strong convexity/dissipativity fail and in multimodal or heavy-tailed posterior settings.  
- Suggested ablations: (i) no correlation vs perfect correlation of Gaussian noise; (ii) shared vs independent minibatches across chains; (iii) two independent SGLD chains in parallel.  
- Suggested implementation clarifications: detail RNG synchronization, minibatch coordination, exact communication costs, and report results matched by total gradient computations and core counts.  
- Minor fixes: remove duplicate ULA definition and correct small notational slips.  
- Recommendation: generally positive and leans toward acceptance, contingent on addressing resource-fairness, coupling overhead, ablations, and clarity on assumptions and tuning.
