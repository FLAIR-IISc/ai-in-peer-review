- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while maintaining variance.
- Theoretically, SGRRLD attains MSE O(K^{-4/5}) versus SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}); formal proofs given under specified conditions.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data support theory, showing large bias/MSE reductions (e.g., two orders of magnitude on a Gaussian) and modest gains on matrix factorization.
- Method requires correlated Brownian increments to control variance, a noted limitation.
- Paper is technically sound with careful theoretical analysis and appropriate experiments.
- Well-written and clearly organized; exposition of RRE in SG-MCMC is accessible and bridges communities.
- Figure 1a is misleading (plots Gaussian with empirical mean/variance, not empirical posterior).
- Figure 2a lacks sufficient explanation for counter-intuitive bias behavior as step size decreases.
- Applying RRE to SG-MCMC is novel and generalizable to other SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Contribution is a clever application of an existing numerical technique rather than a transformative breakthrough.
- Practically significant for large-scale Bayesian inference: better accuracy, natural parallelization, and easy integration into existing workflows.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application with strong theory, empirical validation, and practical utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution with limited theoretical novelty.
- Overall assessment: solid, useful addition to SG-MCMC literature likely to be adopted by practitioners, though not revolutionary.
