- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with different stepsizes and correlated Brownian motions using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.
- Provides rigorous theory: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on non-asymptotic bias and MSE.
- Empirical validation on synthetic data and large-scale matrix factorization shows improved convergence vs SGLD and sometimes SGHMC.
- Method offers bias reduction without increasing integrator order, building on SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation prior work.
- Technique is broadly applicable to other SG-MCMC algorithms beyond SGLD.
- Exploits parallelizability by running two chains, potentially benefiting multi-core architectures.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and logically organized.
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation with valuable convergence insights.
- Weakness: contribution may be considered incremental relative to higher-order integrators and prior RR-for-SDEs work.
- Weakness: communication overhead of generating correlated Brownian motions (communication at each step) is not analyzed or included in experiments.
- Weakness: experimental setup lacks clarity on resource allocation—unclear if SGLD and SGRRLD had equal cores/wall-clock time and accounted gradient costs.
- Weakness: missing baseline comparison to two independent ("embarrassingly parallel") SGLD chains to isolate RR benefits from parallelism.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address communication costs, clarify experiments, add independent-SGLD baseline, and fix duplicated ULA mention.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, significant theoretical results, promising empirical support, and wide applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty, unaddressed practical communication costs, and insufficient experimental detail and baselines.
