- Introduces SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading discretization error.  
- First application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with rigorous analysis accounting for discretization error and stochastic gradient noise.  
- Theoretical guarantees: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Key technical insight: correlating Brownian motions between chains is crucial to control variance of the combined estimator.  
- Algorithm is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and clearly presented.  
- Synthetic (linear Gaussian) experiments convincingly validate the theoretical bias reduction and convergence-rate claims.  
- Real-data (MovieLens) results show only marginal RMSE improvement, making practical utility questionable given ~2× computation.  
- Wall-clock comparison is flawed: paper compares parallelized SGRRLD to sequential SGLD rather than to two parallel SGLD chains.  
- Missing baseline: a single SGLD run with step size γ/2 for twice the iterations to match compute and isolate the benefit of RR extrapolation.  
- Request to state key technical conditions for theorems in the main paper (e.g., whether strong convexity is assumed or multimodal posteriors are allowed).  
- Concern about impact of mini-batch gradient-noise correlation: unclear if uncorrelated subsampling breaks RR cancellation; empirical chain correlation not reported.  
- Asks authors to justify experimental comparison choices or provide fair comparisons against two parallel SGLD chains (γ or γ/2).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and clearly written with significant theoretical contribution.  
- Practical significance limited by weak real-data evidence; reviewer is on the fence but leans toward acceptance pending improved empirical baselines and comparisons.
