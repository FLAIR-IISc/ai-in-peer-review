- Paper proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with stepsizes γ and γ/2 over the same horizon with correlated Gaussian increments and extrapolate (Richardson–Romberg).

- Theory: provides a CLT, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and claims improved rates vs first-order SG-MCMC (e.g., MSE O(K^-4/5) under optimal constant stepsize).

- Method is simple to implement and extensible beyond SGLD (example: SGHMC).

- Connects SG-MCMC literature to classical weak-error expansions for SDEs (Talay–Tubaro) and RR/duplicated diffusions (Lemaire–Pagès–Panloup).

- Main theorems are well-motivated and support bias/MSE improvements if assumptions hold.

- Major concern: unclear derivation of claimed convergence rates from Theorems 1–2 — mapping from conditions on ΓK and Γ(3)K to K-exponents and the transition between decreasing-stepsize (Thm 1) and fixed-stepsize (Thm 2) regimes needs to be made explicit with constants.

- Require explicit assumptions and constants on f, U, and L in the main text to support theoretical claims.

- Empirical clarity issue: explain why the 1/(Kγ) term appears to dominate for SGRRLD at small γ in Fig.2 but a similar rise is not seen for SGLD (possible causes: different K, burn-in, averaging).

- Notational and presentation issues: inconsistent use of ϑ vs θ, “RR” not defined at first use, ΓK referenced before definition, vague “smooth function f” without class conditions, inconsistent figure labels (bias/time/dimension), vectors not bolded.

- Experimental fairness: SGRRLD costs ~3× per unit horizon on one core (two chains, one double-step); Fig.5 wall-clock comparison uses two cores — SGLD should be scaled by 2/3 for equal compute, which would reduce reported gap.

- Figure 3 concerns: best curve seems α=0.2 conflicting with claimed α=1/3, the “optimal” curve fluctuates—request explanation, confidence bands, or longer runs.

- Pros: simple, general, theoretically grounded, promising empirical results.

- Cons: unclear rate derivations, fairness of timing comparisons, figure inconsistencies, notational issues.

- Recommendation: borderline accept contingent on clarifying rate derivations and providing fair compute-normalized experimental comparisons.
