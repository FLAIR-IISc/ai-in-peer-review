- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD.
- Provides rigorous theory: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and bounds on non-asymptotic bias and MSE.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show SGRRLD attains higher convergence rates than SGLD and first-order integrators.
- Strength: novel and interesting approach to reducing SG-MCMC bias.
- Strength: thorough and rigorous theoretical analysis clarifying SGRRLD’s properties.
- Strength: empirical evidence supports improved convergence.
- Weakness: experiments assume a simple Gaussian target; unclear behavior on more complex distributions.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods.
- Weakness: implementation requires running two chains in parallel, which may be impractical in some settings.
- Quality: technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments; evaluation is careful and honest.
- Clarity: well written and organized; sufficient detail for expert reproduction.
- Originality: novel combination of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with SGLD, clearly distinct from prior work.
- Significance: provides a meaningful theoretical and practical advance for bias reduction in SG-MCMC.
- For acceptance: novel method, rigorous analysis, and strong empirical support.
- Against acceptance: limited experimental scope and practical concerns about dual-chain implementation.
- Minor: Figure 1a is misleading—uses Gaussian with empirical mean/variance rather than empirical posterior; clarify caption.
- Minor: Figure 2a needs explanation for why bias may not decrease as step size decreases.
