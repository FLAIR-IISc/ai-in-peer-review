This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The authors apply the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method, to the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm. This approach involves running two parallel chains with different step sizes and extrapolating the estimators to construct a new one.

The paper provides a clear and easy-to-implement algorithm description. The authors also provide formal theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error (MSE) can be bounded. The results show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically.

The authors support their findings using both synthetic and real data experiments. The synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show a clear bias-reduction advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD. However, the MovieLens experiment shows only a slight root mean squared error (RMSE) improvement, and the real-data results are arguable and need stronger evidence.

The application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a novel contribution, and the authors provide convergence proofs. However, the reviewer hasn't checked the proof details and asks what conditions on U are required for Theorems 1 and 2. For example, can U be multimodal?

The reviewer also questions the effect of gradient-noise correlation: if gradient noises are uncorrelated, chains may decorrelate quickly—how does combining chains reduce error order? The authors should also investigate whether they observed correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.

The computation-time claim is also a concern. While SGRRLD's parallelization cost is comparable to SGLD, SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains. A comparison to multi-chain SGLD is needed to isolate the benefits of SGRRLD.

The reviewer suggests comparing SGRRLD to SGLD run with two parallel chains (and to SGLD with step size gamma/2) to isolate the benefits. More and stronger real-data experiments are also recommended to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the algorithm is clearly described. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the experiments provide some evidence for the benefits of SGRRLD. However, more work is needed to fully understand the behavior of SGRRLD and to demonstrate its practical benefits.
