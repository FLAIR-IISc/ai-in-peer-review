- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (focus on SGLD) by running two chains at step sizes γ and γ/2 and extrapolating to cancel leading discretization bias.  
- Mechanism: Uses correlated Brownian increments to control variance so the extrapolated estimator attains higher-order bias cancellation.  
- Theoretical results: Proves consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), and finite-time MSE O(K^{-4/5}), improving over SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Optimal schedules: Derives step-size schedules (α=1/5 for overall rate, α=1/3 for bias-only) consistent with theory.  
- Generality & parallelism: Claims method is generic to other SG-MCMC schemes (e.g., SGHMC) and parallelizable by running two chains concurrently.  
- Experiments (synthetic): Synthetic linear-Gaussian and SGHMC variants strongly validate theoretical bias/MSE rates and dimensional scaling.  
- Experiments (real data): Matrix factorization on MovieLens shows only modest RMSE improvements at comparable wall-clock time.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Talay–Tubaro and Lemaire/Pages RR literature for SDEs and prior SG-MCMC analyses (Teh et al., Vollmer et al.); novelty is adapting RR to stochastic-gradient setting with correlated noise analysis.  
- Theory quality concern: Main text defers many technical conditions to the supplement—assumptions on U, f, and gradient noise (smoothness, dissipativity, Lipschitzness, moments/sub-Gaussianity) should be stated upfront.  
- Nonconvexity question: Unclear whether multimodal/nonconvex posteriors satisfy the ergodicity and Poisson-equation assumptions used (e.g., existence of C9 solution).  
- Variance-control critique: Correlated Brownian increments rationale is sound and Theorem 1 favors Σ=I, but treatment of gradient-noise correlation (common vs independent mini-batches) is cursory and needs empirical/theoretical clarity.  
- Sensitivity to gradient noise: If mini-batches are independent across chains, chains may decorrelate quickly—robustness of RR gains to this is unclear.  
- Clarity/implementation: Algorithm is simple and clearly described but paper should add main assumptions to body, inline pseudo-code, discrete-time correlation implementation details, burn-in/thinning handling, and explicit cost accounting (one SGRRLD iter = one γ step + two γ/2 steps).  
- Notation/UI: Notation is heavy; a schematic of time-grid alignment for the two chains would help.  
- Originality: Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with a complete convergence analysis is novel and the correlated-noise extension/useful.  
- Significance: Theoretical advance (second-order bias cancellation without higher-order integrators) is meaningful; practical benefits are strong in synthetic cases but modest on real data.  
- Fairness of empirical comparisons: Wall-clock parallelism argument needs stronger baselines—compare to two parallel SGLD chains averaged, SGLD with γ/2 or tuned schedules, and higher-order SG-MCMC on real data.  
- Empirical suggestions: Report chain cross-correlations, experiments with shared vs independent mini-batches, and ablations over Σ (Id vs 0 vs intermediate) to validate variance analysis.  
- Questions for authors: Precisely state assumptions on U, f, and gradient noise; clarify admissibility of nonconvex/multimodal targets; robustness to independent gradient noise; whether mini-batches/common RNG were used; computational/memory overhead; Σ ablation results.  
- Pros: Simple, general, easy to implement; solid theoretical backing; clear synthetic validation; extends to SGHMC and parallel design.  
- Cons: Modest practical gains on real data; heavy technical assumptions buried in supplement; incomplete fairness in wall-clock/parallel comparisons; sensitivity to gradient-noise correlation not fully explored.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject—technically sound and elegant but empirical evidence on large-scale practical gains and fairness of comparisons is insufficient.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
