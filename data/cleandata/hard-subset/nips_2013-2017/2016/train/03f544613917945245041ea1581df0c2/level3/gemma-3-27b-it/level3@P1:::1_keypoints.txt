- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias in large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Bridges SGLD and SDE literatures, enabling potential benefits for both stochastic optimization and numerical SDE methods.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.
- Derives non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE, showing higher convergence rates than standard SGLD in finite-time and asymptotic regimes.
- Claims computational efficiency and suitability for parallel/distributed architectures due to independent parallel chains.
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian data validate bias reduction and improved convergence rates.
- Real-world experiments on MovieLens matrix factorization show consistent RMSE improvements over SGLD, with comparisons to SGHMC.
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation with valuable non-asymptotic bounds.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and thorough experimental setup.
- Strength: novel application of Richardson-Romberg to SGLD addressing bias in first-order integrators.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: method is generalizable to other SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Weakness: Figure 1a may be misleading—uses Gaussian parameterized by empirical mean/variance rather than empirical posterior.
- Weakness: Figure 2a shows non-monotonic bias vs step size without sufficient explanation (term 1/(Kγ) mentioned but needs detail).
- Weakness: modest gains in real-world experiments; sensitivity to hyperparameters/data not fully explored.
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational overhead from running two chains despite parallelization claim.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses an important scalable Bayesian inference problem with strong theory and validated experiments.
- Argument against acceptance: modest empirical improvements and minor figure clarity issues.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify Figure 1a and expand explanation for Figure 2a behavior.
