- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Claims SGRRLD attains faster convergence rates than SGLD and matches accuracy of higher-order integrator-based methods.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with solid proofs supporting theoretical claims.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show reduced bias and MSE for the proposed method.  
- Major concern: derivation of the specific convergence rates from the stated theorem is unclear and needs detailed explanation.  
- Practical concern: running two chains in parallel is computationally expensive; requires fair timing comparison by scaling SGLD.  
- Empirical issue: Figure 3 shows best performance at α=0.2, conflicting with claimed α=1/3 — authors should explain and detail how empirical rate was obtained.  
- Theoretical question at line 256: why the 1/(Kγ) term does not dominate for very small step sizes in SGLD needs clarification.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation for θ, undefined terms ("RR", Γ_K), and missing precise smoothness assumptions for f.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theoretical results, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear rate derivation, extra computational cost from two chains, and potentially unfair SGLD comparisons.
