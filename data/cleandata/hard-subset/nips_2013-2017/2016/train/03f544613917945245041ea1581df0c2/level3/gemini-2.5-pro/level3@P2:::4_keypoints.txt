- Novelty: Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (SGRRLD) to reduce discretization bias in SG-MCMC by combining two SGLD chains with different step sizes.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, claiming improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Practicality: Method is simple to implement and naturally parallelizable by running two chains in parallel.  
- Major experimental concern — computational budget: Experimental comparisons are unfair — SGRRLD uses 3 gradient evaluations per iteration and was given ~50% more gradient evaluations than SGLD.  
- Major experimental concern — inconsistent results: Figure 3 contradicts the theory on the optimal stepsize decay (text claims α=1/3 but α=0.2 shows lower bias in plots).  
- Major experimental concern — small stepsize sensitivity: SGRRLD shows larger bias/MSE increases for very small γ than SGLD, suggesting sensitivity not fully explained.  
- Acceptance argument: Novel, elegant combination of classical RR extrapolation with scalable MCMC and broad applicability.  
- Acceptance argument: Strong theoretical contributions demonstrating significantly improved convergence rates.  
- Acceptance argument: Low implementation overhead and good fit for multi-core architectures.  
- Rejection argument: Unequal computational budget in experiments undermines claimed practical gains.  
- Rejection argument: Contradiction between theoretical claims and plotted experimental results reduces confidence.  
- Rejection argument: Combined experimental issues question the paper's rigor and practical significance.  
- Required revision: Authors should rerun experiments under a fixed total-gradient-evaluations budget and resolve Figure 3 inconsistency.  
- Minor comments: Inconsistent notation (ϑ vs θ), late definition/use of acronyms (RR), ΓK used before definition, clarify smoothness assumptions on f in main text, and consider bold vectors for readability.
