This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC methods while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method, to Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The authors provide formal theoretical analysis, showing that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error can be bounded.

The paper is well-written, and the presentation is clear. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, including the limitations of existing SG-MCMC methods and the motivation for their approach. The proposed algorithm is described in detail, along with its theoretical analysis and experimental results.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed algorithm is simple to understand and implement, requiring minimal changes to base SG-MCMC algorithms.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including a central limit theorem and bounds for the non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in reducing bias and improving convergence rates.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed algorithm can be applied to other SG-MCMC methods, beyond SGLD.
* The experimental results are limited to a few specific examples, and it is unclear how well the proposed algorithm will perform in more general settings.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of SG-MCMC, and the proposed algorithm has the potential to improve the accuracy and efficiency of Bayesian inference in large-scale applications. However, further research is needed to fully explore the capabilities and limitations of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and theoretically sound algorithm for reducing bias in SG-MCMC methods.

Arguments against acceptance:
