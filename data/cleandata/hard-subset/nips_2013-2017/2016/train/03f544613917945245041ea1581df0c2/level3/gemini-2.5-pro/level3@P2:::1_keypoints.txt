- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by combining two SG-MCMC chains (steps γ and γ/2) to cancel leading-order discretization bias.  
- Focuses on adapting RR extrapolation to SGLD and analyzing its behavior with stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical contributions: rigorous asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved rates (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Demonstrates that combining two first-order integrators can match accuracy of more complex higher-order schemes.  
- Significance: bridges numerical SDE literature and SG-MCMC, offering a simple, practical, and parallelizable bias-correction technique.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, motivations, algorithm, and theory are clearly presented and well-situated in prior work.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments and MovieLens matrix factorization show reduced bias/MSE and better wall-clock performance; extension to SGHMC shown.  
- Weakness 1: Figure 1(a) caption is ambiguous—plots appear to be Gaussian PDFs from sample mean/variance rather than kernel density estimates and should be clarified.  
- Weakness 2: The counterintuitive increase in bias/MSE at very small step sizes (due to the 1/(Kγ) term) needs more explicit discussion alongside Figure 2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important discretization-bias problem, offers an easy-to-implement, parallelizable fix, provides strong theoretical guarantees, and shows convincing empirical gains.  
- Argument against acceptance: RR extrapolation itself is a classical technique; the novelty lies mainly in its application and analysis within SG-MCMC.  
- Overall recommendation: strong endorsement for acceptance based on solid theory, practical utility, and empirical support.
