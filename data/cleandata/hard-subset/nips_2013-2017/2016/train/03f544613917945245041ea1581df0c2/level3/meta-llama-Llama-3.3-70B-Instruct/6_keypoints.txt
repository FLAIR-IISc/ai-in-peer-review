- Proposes a novel semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to a domain expert.
- Introduces a γ-margin clusterability assumption and uses it to enable efficient clustering with queries.
- Presents an algorithm that succeeds under the γ-margin assumption and analyzes its correctness.
- Proves a lower bound on the number of queries required to make NP-hard clustering problems feasible.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and well-organized technical sections.
- Proofs are thorough, easy to follow, and technically sound.
- Provides detailed analysis of query complexity and computational complexity.
- Demonstrates practical effectiveness via experiments on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: balances theoretical rigor and practical validation.
- Raises important questions about the trade-off between query complexity and computational complexity.
- Limitation: assumes the domain expert follows center-based clustering with a margin, which may not hold in practice.
- Limitation: requires a significant number of queries, potentially impractical in some scenarios.
- Suggested improvements: add implementation details and quantify communication cost between algorithm and expert.
- Suggested extensions: explore other query types or supervision methods and apply the framework to other clustering tasks or domains.
- Novelty: approach builds on prior work in semi-supervised clustering and active learning and may not be entirely surprising.
- Overall: significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; has potential value for researchers and practitioners.
