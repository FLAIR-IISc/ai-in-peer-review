- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with different step sizes and apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis showing asymptotic consistency, a CLT, and improved finite-time and asymptotic convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Algorithm is described clearly and is straightforward to implement.  
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a new contribution (RR known for SDEs but not SG-MCMC).  
- Strength: Thorough derivation of bias and MSE bounds supporting faster convergence rates.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and high overall paper quality.  
- Weakness: Only one real-data experiment (MovieLens), limiting empirical validation of practical benefits.  
- Weakness: Unclear assumptions on the potential U required for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., smoothness, convexity, multimodality).  
- Weakness: Unclear how combining chains reduces error order when gradient-noise between chains is uncorrelated.  
- Weakness: Questionable computation-time claim—SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains, so cost parity is unclear.  
- Significance: Potentially important for Bayesian ML by reducing SGLD bias and accelerating convergence.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and easy to follow, with well-explained theory.  
- Quality: Solid theoretical contributions and presentation, but limited empirical breadth.  
- For acceptance: Novel algorithm with rigorous theory and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient real-data experiments and unclear technical conditions and noise-correlation effects.  
- Recommendation: Add more real-data experiments to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Recommendation: Explicitly state and justify the conditions on U for main theorems.  
- Recommendation: Analyze or empirically study the effect of gradient-noise correlation between chains.  
- Recommendation: Compare SGRRLD to SGLD run with two parallel chains to isolate the RR extrapolation advantage.
