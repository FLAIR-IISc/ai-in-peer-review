- Introduces SG-RR-MCMC, using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias of SG-MCMC while controlling variance by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.  
- Specializes the idea to SGLD, yielding SGRRLD, and demonstrates practical implementation.  
- Theoretical analysis establishes asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved MSE convergence rates (O(K^-4/5)).  
- Provides non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and large-scale matrix factorization show reduced bias, lower MSE, and faster convergence versus SGLD.  
- Method is simple, novel, and generic, potentially extendable to other SG-MCMC variants (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Parallel nature makes the algorithm well-suited for distributed and large-scale applications.  
- Lacks direct experimental comparisons with higher-order integrators (e.g., SGHMC-s), leaving relative performance unclear.  
- Presentation issues: discrepancies between Algorithm 1 and Eq. (5) and unclear step-size descriptions that may hinder reproducibility.  
- Limited empirical exploration of variance control strategies (e.g., correlated Brownian motions) and their practical impact.  
- Experimental scope is narrow, focusing mainly on SGLD and matrix factorization rather than diverse SG-MCMC variants or broader Bayesian tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—fix presentation inconsistencies and add comparisons with high-order integrators to strengthen the paper.
