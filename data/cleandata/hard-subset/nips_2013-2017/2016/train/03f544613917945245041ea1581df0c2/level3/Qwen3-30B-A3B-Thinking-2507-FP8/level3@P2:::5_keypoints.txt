- Proposes SGRRLD: runs two SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC (first time applied to SGLD context).  
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Improved convergence rate: MSE O(K^-4/5) for SGRRLD versus O(K^-2/3) for SGLD.  
- Matches accuracy of higher-order integrator methods in theory while using only a first-order integrator.  
- Embarrassingly parallel—two chains can run independently, aiding large-scale/distributed use.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and matrix factorization show clear accuracy improvements over SGLD.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Strength: technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and validated experiments.  
- Weakness: no experimental comparison with existing higher-order integrator SG-MCMC methods despite claiming comparable accuracy.  
- Weakness: inconsistent statements about step-size schedule (conflict between section and earlier assumptions).  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 in the supplement conflicts with Equation (5), hindering implementation.  
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is limited (straightforward application of RR extrapolation), possibly insufficient for top-tier novelty standards.  
- Significance: practically useful and parallelizable, but impact reduced by missing comparisons to high-order methods and technical inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add comparisons and fix technical inconsistencies.
