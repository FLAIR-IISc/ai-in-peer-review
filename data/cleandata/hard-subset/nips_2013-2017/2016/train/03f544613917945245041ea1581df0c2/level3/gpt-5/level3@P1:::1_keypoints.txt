- Proposes Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC (SGLD): run chains at γ and γ/2, combine outputs to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Uses correlated Brownian increments (ideally perfectly correlated) to control variance between the two chains.  
- Proves consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bounds: bias = O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE = O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)).  
- For fixed γ, optimal finite-time rates are bias O(K−2/3) and MSE O(K−4/5); with decreasing steps γk ∝ k−α, optimal α = 1/5 gives MSE O(K−2/5), improving on SGLD’s O(K−1/3).  
- Method matches accuracies of higher-order integrators without implementing them and extends to other SG-MCMC schemes (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Empirically shows large gains on synthetic Gaussian models and modest, consistent gains on large-scale matrix factorization; parallel runs keep wall-clock comparable to SGLD.  
- Simple, generic idea with minimal code changes and natural parallelizability.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: explicit role of Brownian correlation, consistency, CLT, and clear non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds with step-size schedules.  
- Conceptually valuable bridging of SDE numerical analysis and SG-MCMC literature.  
- Empirical validation includes toy problems with ground truth and a realistic large-scale application with fair parallel compute-time comparisons.  
- Practical benefits depend on parallel resources; without parallelism, two-chain cost may negate wall-clock gains and needs quantification (e.g., ESS/time).  
- Some assumptions are strong (e.g., solution g ∈ C9, technical L/ergodicity conditions); applicability to nonconvex or heavy-tailed posteriors is unclear.  
- Empirical scope is limited: only linear-Gaussian and one matrix factorization example; lacks logistic regression, deep models, and comparisons to higher-order SGLD variants.  
- Sensitivity to mini-batch correlation and sharing mini-batch indices across chains is not explored experimentally.  
- Minor clarity/implementation issues: some figures confusing and correlated-noise implementation details deferred to supplement.  
- Methodology and analysis are technically sound; variance-control via correlated Brownian increments is well-justified, but constants in bounds are opaque and depend on gradient-moment assumptions.  
- Suggestion to include SGRRLD pseudocode in main text and explicitly state requirement to use same mini-batch size/indices for both chains.  
- Ask authors about: sharing mini-batch indices for variance reduction, sensitivity to imperfect Gaussian correlation in multi-process settings, practical guidance for adaptive/decaying step sizes, ESS/time or calibration metrics on non-Gaussian posteriors, and impact when parallelism is unavailable.  
- Originality: novel application of Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC and non-trivial stochastic-gradient analysis.  
- Significance: practical, broadly applicable improvement likely to be adopted in parallel settings though not transformative.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general method with strong theory and positive empirical results; bridges research communities.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical breadth, reliance on parallel hardware, strong technical assumptions, and missing ablations.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium–high.
