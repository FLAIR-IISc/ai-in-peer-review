This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error can be bounded. The paper also presents empirical results on both synthetic and real data experiments, demonstrating that SGRRLD achieves higher rates of convergence than SGLD and its extensions with first-order integrators.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting approach to reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a clear understanding of the properties of SGRRLD.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in improving the convergence rate of SG-MCMC algorithms.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a relatively simple setting, where the target distribution is a Gaussian distribution. It would be interesting to see how SGRRLD performs in more complex settings.
* The paper does not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC algorithms.
* The implementation of SGRRLD requires running two chains in parallel, which may not be feasible in all scenarios.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

Originality: The paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, namely Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD. The approach is new and interesting, and the paper provides a clear explanation of how it differs from previous contributions.

Significance: The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The paper provides a unique theoretical and pragmatic approach to reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting approach to reducing the bias of SG-MCMC algorithms.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a clear understanding of the properties of SGRRLD.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in improving the convergence rate of SG-MCMC algorithms.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes a relatively simple setting, which may not be representative of more complex scenarios.
* The implementation of SGRRLD requires running two chains in parallel, which may not be feasible in all scenarios.
