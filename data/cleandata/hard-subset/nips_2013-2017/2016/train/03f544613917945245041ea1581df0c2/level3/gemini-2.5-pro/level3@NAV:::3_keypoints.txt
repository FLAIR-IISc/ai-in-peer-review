- Core idea: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading bias.

- Theoretical claim: method reduces leading-order bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and improves MSE convergence, with solid proofs.

- Strength — generality/impact: can act as a wrapper to improve a wide range of SG-MCMC algorithms, giving substantial potential impact.

- Strength — originality: novel application of RR extrapolation in the SG-MCMC context.

- Strength — quality: technically sound, elegant, and supported by rigorous analysis.

- Strength — clarity: paper is well written and presents core concepts clearly.

- Weakness — practical efficiency: current implementation leaves compute idle (γ/2 chain runs twice as many steps); need a practical parallel scheme (e.g., one γ chain + two γ/2 chains) and analysis of its variance impact.

- Weakness — scope/applicability: clarify whether the method requires a specific bias expansion (π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2)) and whether it extends to other approximate MCMC methods like subsampling MH.

- Weakness — experiments: matrix factorization runs should be extended to convergence and include a standard SGD baseline to contextualize RMSE and Bayesian benefits.

- Weakness — presentation: include algorithm pseudocode early in the paper for practitioner use.

- Overall: high-quality, novel contribution with practical and experimental issues that are addressable in revision.
