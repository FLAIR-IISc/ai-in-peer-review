- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Builds a bridge between MCMC and SDE literatures, potentially benefiting both communities.  
- Provides theoretical convergence results (CLT, bias and MSE bounds) showing conditions where SGRRLD outperforms SGLD.  
- Empirical validation on toy examples and large-scale matrix factorization shows consistent improvements, though modest in scale.  
- Method is elegant, simple, practical, and easy to implement with a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with comprehensible technical details.  
- Improvements in large-scale experiments are modest, which may limit practical impact in some applications.  
- Fig. 1a is misleading because it plots a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance instead of the empirical posterior—needs clarification.  
- Fig. 2a shows counterintuitive bias behavior with decreasing step size that is unexplained and requires discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, provided the authors address figure clarifications and discuss the observed counterintuitive phenomena.
