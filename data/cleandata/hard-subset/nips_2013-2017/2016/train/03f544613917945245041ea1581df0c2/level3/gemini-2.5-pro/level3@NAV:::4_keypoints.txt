- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel chains with different step sizes to reduce discretization bias and accelerate convergence.
- Novel synthesis building on SGLD and RR for SDEs, aimed at large-scale Bayesian inference.
- Strength: elegant, simple, easy to implement with minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC.
- Strength: theoretical analysis includes a Central Limit Theorem and non-asymptotic bounds suggesting improved convergence rates over first-order methods.
- Major weakness: paper does not clearly derive the claimed optimal convergence rates (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for α=1/5) from the main theorems, undermining the main theoretical claim.
- Experimental flaw: wall-clock time comparison is unfair because SGRRLD uses ~3× single-core computation versus SGLD; baseline runtime should be scaled for a fair comparison.
- Inconsistency between text and figures (e.g., Figure 3 shows optimal bias at α=0.2 while text claims α=1/3).
- Missing explanation for the behavior of the 1/(Kγ) error term at small step sizes.
- Recommendation: provide rigorous derivation of claimed rates and fair computational comparisons to substantiate the contribution.
