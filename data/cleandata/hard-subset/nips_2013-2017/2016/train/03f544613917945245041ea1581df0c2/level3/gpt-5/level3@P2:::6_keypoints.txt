- Proposes SGRRLD: Richardson–Romberg extrapolation applied to SG-MCMC (mainly SGLD) using two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and correlated Gaussian increments.  
- Shows Σ = Id coupling minimizes asymptotic variance and provides consistency, a CLT with bias/variance decomposition, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Claims improved rates over SGLD: asymptotic O(K−2/5) (averaged estimator) and non-asymptotic O(K−4/5) MSE with optimal tuning, vs O(K−1/3) and O(K−2/3) for SGLD.  
- Empirically demonstrates lower bias/MSE on a Gaussian model and wall-clock RMSE gains on large-scale matrix factorization; SGRRHMC improves over SGHMC.  
- Situates work relative to Talay–Tubaro/RR literature and recent high-order SG-MCMC analyses; novelty is adapting RR to stochastic gradients and proving noise-dominance and variance control.  
- Theory is solid and convincing (CLT, bounds, rate calculations), but relies on strong smoothness and Poisson-equation assumptions that may exclude nonsmooth or heavy-tailed posteriors.  
- Central variance-control mechanism (correlated Brownian increments) is well-justified, but implementation details for perfect correlation in parallel runs are missing.  
- Possible typo/implicitness in Eq. (5): gradient appears evaluated implicitly; needs correction/clarification to match Euler–Maruyama.  
- Experiments support theory but fairness/resource accounting and communication/synchronization overheads are unclear.  
- Paper is well written and organized with clear estimator and coupling descriptions; minor typos and presentation fixes suggested.  
- Originality judged moderate: RR ideas are classical, but adapting them to SG-MCMC with stochastic gradients and providing non-asymptotic analysis is nontrivial.  
- Potentially impactful as a simple, general wrapper for SGLD/SGHMC that gives higher-order accuracy and is parallelizable, but requires double resources and mainly helps expectation estimation.  
- Pros: simple/elegant technique, solid theoretical analysis, demonstrated empirical improvements, and clear variance-control insight.  
- Cons: incremental relative to prior RR/high-order work, strong assumptions limiting applicability, experimental fairness and overhead questions, missing baselines, and limited guidance for sample-based downstream tasks.  
- Request 1: Clarify computational model—hardware, cores, resource allocation, and how SGLD baseline was afforded compute relative to SGRRLD.  
- Request 2: Describe how perfectly correlated Gaussian increments were enforced across processes and quantify any per-iteration communication overhead.  
- Request 3: Add baselines: two independent SGLD chains with equal gradient budget, pSGLD/variance-reduced SG-MCMC, and higher-order SGLD variants.  
- Request 4: Report whether minibatch indices were shared across chains and empirically compare independent vs shared minibatches (gradient coupling impact).  
- Request 5: Discuss applicability/extension of analysis to nonsmooth U or heavy-tailed priors and possible relaxations (e.g., locally Lipschitz dissipative drifts).  
- Request 6: Explain how the RR estimator is used for downstream prediction (test RMSE) and whether predictive intervals/quantiles benefit.  
- Minor comments: remove duplicated “ULA” mention, fix Eq. (5) gradient evaluation, and include brief pseudocode in the main text.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on addressing fairness/resource and coupling-overhead concerns and adding suggested baselines.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
