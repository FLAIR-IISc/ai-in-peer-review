This paper introduces Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), a novel SG-MCMC algorithm aiming to reduce bias compared to standard SGLD. The core idea is to apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation – a well-known numerical acceleration technique – to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining their results. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating improved convergence rates and support their claims with both synthetic and real-world experiments.

The paper builds on a substantial body of work in SG-MCMC, notably Welling & Teh’s (2011) foundational SGLD paper and subsequent extensions like SGHMC (Chen et al., 2014). The recent focus on bias reduction within SG-MCMC, as highlighted by Chen et al. (2015) and Teh et al. (2016), provides direct context for this work. The application of RR extrapolation to SDEs, while established (Talay & Tubaro, 1990; Lemaire et al., 2015), is novel in the context of SG-MCMC, and the convergence proofs represent a significant contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a genuinely new idea with potential benefits for reducing bias.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper presents a strong theoretical analysis, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds. The convergence proofs, if correct, are a substantial contribution.
* **Clear Algorithm Description:** The SGRRLD algorithm is described clearly and appears readily implementable.
* **Synthetic Experiment Results:** The synthetic Gaussian model experiments clearly demonstrate the bias-reduction advantage of SGRRLD.
* **Potential for Parallelism:**  The algorithm’s inherent parallelism is a significant advantage, especially for large-scale applications.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Real-Data Results are Inconclusive:** The improvement on the MovieLens dataset is slight and doesn’t strongly substantiate the theoretical claims. The authors rightly acknowledge this, but stronger evidence is needed.
* **Unverified Proofs:** As a reviewer, I haven't thoroughly verified the details of the proofs. Clarification regarding the conditions on *U* in Theorems 1 & 2 is crucial. Specifically, can *U* be multimodal? A more explicit statement of the regularity assumptions on *U* would be beneficial.
* **Gradient Noise Correlation:** The paper touches upon correlated Brownian motion but doesn’t fully address the impact of gradient noise correlation. If the gradient noises are uncorrelated, the chains might decorrelate quickly.  How does combining these potentially decorrelated chains effectively reduce the error order? A more detailed discussion of this point is needed.
* **Computational Cost Comparison:** The claim about comparable computation time is not fully convincing. While SGRRLD’s parallelization cost *is* comparable, SGLD can *also* be run with multiple parallel chains. A direct comparison to multi-chain SGLD (e.g., SGLD with two chains) is essential.  Furthermore, comparison with SGLD using a step size of gamma/2, to isolate the RR benefit, would be highly valuable.
* **Limited Experimentation:**  The experiments are somewhat limited. More diverse real-world datasets and more extensive hyperparameter tuning would strengthen the conclusions.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel application of a well-established numerical technique (RR extrapolation) to SG-MCMC.
* Strong theoretical foundation with convergence guarantees (pending verification of proofs).
* Clear and accessible algorithm description.
* Demonstrated bias reduction in synthetic experiments.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Weak evidence of practical improvement on real-world data.
* Unverified proof details.
* Incomplete discussion of gradient noise correlation and computational cost.
* Limited scope of experiments.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising approach to reducing bias in SG-MCMC. The theoretical contributions are significant, and the algorithm is clearly described. However, the inconclusive real-world results and the need for proof verification raise concerns.  

**Recommendations:**

1. **Strengthen Real-Data Experiments:** Conduct more extensive experiments on diverse datasets and carefully tune hyperparameters.
2. **Clarify Proof Conditions:** Provide a more precise statement of the assumptions on *U* in Theorems 1 & 2, specifically addressing multimodality.
3. **Address Gradient Noise Correlation:**  Discuss in detail the effect of gradient noise correlation and its impact on the performance of SGRRLD.
4. **Provide Comparative Benchmarks:** Include a comparison to multi-chain SGLD and SGLD with a reduced step size (gamma/2).
5. **Consider Multi-level RR:** The authors mention multi-level RR extrapolation in the conclusion; exploring this in future work would be valuable.

I recommend a *weak accept* contingent on the authors addressing the concerns outlined above, particularly regarding the real-data experiments and the clarification of proof conditions. If these issues are not addressed, a rejection is warranted.
