- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SGLD chains (step sizes γk and γk/2) with correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Theoretically establishes an asymptotic CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing faster convergence (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Demonstrates that higher-order accuracy can be achieved using only the first-order Euler–Maruyama scheme.  
- Synthetic experiments validate the predicted bias and MSE convergence rates with respect to K and γ.  
- Large-scale matrix factorization experiments show improved predictive performance and reported similar wall-clock time to SGLD.  
- Strength: technically sound and clearly written, with thorough analysis and clear exposition of variance control via correlated noise.  
- Strength: original and significant idea—novelly combines RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC to improve accuracy.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation and extension to SGHMC demonstrating method generality.  
- Weakness: correlated-noise coupling requires synchronization/communication between chains; overhead is not analyzed or measured, questioning practicality in distributed settings.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons may be unfair since SGRRLD uses twice the computational processes; should compare to two independent SGLD chains and clarify gradient/FLOP or wall-clock matching.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty—builds on prior analyses of higher-order integrators and RR for SDEs, seen as a logical extension rather than a radical departure.  
- Minor: "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is defined twice in the introduction.  
- Recommendation: lean to accept, but authors should address communication costs and stronger experimental baselines in rebuttal/camera-ready.
