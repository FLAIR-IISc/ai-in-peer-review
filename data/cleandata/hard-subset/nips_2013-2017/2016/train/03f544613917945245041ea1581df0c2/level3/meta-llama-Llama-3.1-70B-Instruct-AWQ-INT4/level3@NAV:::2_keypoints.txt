- Proposes SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias, with theoretical analysis and experiments.

- Strength: Addresses the bias issue in SGLD, providing a significant contribution to SG-MCMC methods.

- Strength: Provides thorough theoretical analysis including CLT and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE.

- Strength: Experimental results on synthetic and real data show improved bias reduction and convergence rates.

- Strength: Paper is well-written and the algorithm description is clear and implementable.

- Weakness: Assumes correlation between two sequences of Gaussian noises and stochastic gradients without clarifying how this affects performance.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational cost of SGRRLD, important for practical use.

- Weakness: MovieLens real-data experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement, questioning practical benefit over SGLD.

- Weakness: No comparison with other SG-MCMC methods (e.g., SGHMC), leaving relative performance unclear.

- Quality: Technically sound and claims well-supported, but needs more details on conditions/assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., on potential U).

- Clarity: Generally well-written and clear algorithmically, but some theoretical derivations need improved readability.

- Originality: Novel SG-MCMC application of RR extrapolation, though the extrapolation idea has prior uses elsewhere.

- Significance: Tackles a significant bias problem in SG-MCMC and achieves higher convergence rates, with potential impact on Bayesian ML and MCMC.

- Recommendations: Add theorem condition details; analyze computational cost and compare with other SG-MCMC; run more real-data experiments including other methods; consider multi-level Monte Carlo extensions.

- Acceptance: Recommend acceptance to NIPS with minor revisions addressing the above recommendations.
