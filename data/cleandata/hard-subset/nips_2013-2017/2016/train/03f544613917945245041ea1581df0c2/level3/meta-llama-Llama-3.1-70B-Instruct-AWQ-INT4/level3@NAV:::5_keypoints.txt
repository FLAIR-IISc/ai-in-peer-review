- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias while controlling variance.  
- Uses Richardson-Romberg numerical extrapolation to accelerate convergence of SGLD.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows SGRRLD achieves higher convergence rates than SGLD and other first-order SG-MCMC methods, both finite-time and asymptotically.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data demonstrate reduced bias and improved convergence.  
- Strength: tackles the significant bias problem of SGLD in Bayesian inference.  
- Strength: strong, well-presented theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: convincing empirical validation.  
- Weakness: assumes the two SGRRLD chains can be run independently, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with SG-MCMC methods that use higher-order integrators.  
- Weakness: contains an inconsistent statement about the step size that could cause confusion.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5), potentially impacting implementation.  
- Originality: novel combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD and new application of extrapolation in SG-MCMC.  
- Significance: addresses an important Bayesian inference problem and could improve SG-MCMC accuracy/efficiency in large-scale settings.  
- Quality and clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear notation, figures, and tables.  
- Acceptance argument: novel method + thorough theory + strong empirical results.  
- Rejection argument: practical independence assumption and methodological inconsistencies (step size, Algorithm 1 vs Eq.5) and missing higher-order comparisons.
