- Proposes a novel semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to incorporate domain knowledge.  
- Introduces the γ-margin property as a clusterability condition that characterizes ease of clustering.  
- Gives a probabilistic polynomial-time clustering algorithm that succeeds when the input satisfies γ-margin with γ > 1.  
- Algorithmic costs: O(k log n + k^2 log k) same-cluster queries and O(kn log n) runtime.  
- Proves a lower bound of Ω(log k + log n) queries required to overcome k-means NP-hardness.  
- Demonstrates that a limited number of queries can convert an NP-hard clustering instance into a tractable one, revealing a query–computational complexity trade-off.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated framework addressing an important clustering problem.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of both computational and query complexity, including matching lower bounds.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured, and accessible presentation of algorithm and analysis.  
- Weakness: inconsistent notation and some unclear term definitions.  
- Weakness: needs more intuition and motivation for the γ-margin property and its relation to other clusterability notions.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—lacks comparisons to other algorithms and sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall: significant contribution that lays groundwork for further research and practical algorithm design.
