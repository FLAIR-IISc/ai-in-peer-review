**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis, including asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm through synthetic and real-data experiments.

**Strengths**

1. **Strong theoretical foundation**: The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation method and its application to SG-MCMC.
2. **Well-situated among prior work**: The authors provide a comprehensive review of prior work on SG-MCMC and numerical integrators, situating their method well within the existing literature.
3. **Effective experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a range of experiments, including synthetic data experiments and a real-data matrix factorization application, which demonstrate the practical gains of the proposed algorithm over standard SGLD.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Incremental novelty**: The main concern is that the novelty of the proposed algorithm may be incremental, given prior analysis of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC [10] and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions [23].
2. **Limited practical applicability**: The experiments may be unfair because correlated chains require per-step communication, but communication cost is not considered or discussed. This may limit the practical applicability of the proposed algorithm.
3. **Lack of clarity on experimental setup**: The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the experimental setup, including whether SGLD used the same compute resources as the two-chain SGRRLD, how resources are split, and how partial resources per chain affect wall-clock time given gradient cost.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

1. **Compare to two independent SGLD chains**: Consider comparing the proposed algorithm to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains as an alternative baseline.
2. **Discuss communication cost**: Provide a discussion on the communication cost associated with the proposed algorithm and how it may affect practical applicability.
3. **Clarify experimental setup**: Provide a clearer explanation of the experimental setup, including resource allocation and how it affects wall-clock time.

**Originality**

The proposed algorithm combines Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SG-MCMC, which is a novel contribution. However, the novelty may be incremental, given prior work on numerical integrators and RR extrapolation.

**Significance**

The proposed algorithm has the potential to improve the accuracy of SG-MCMC methods, which are widely used in Bayesian machine learning. The paper demonstrates practical gains over standard SGLD in a real-data matrix factorization application.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. However, some minor issues, such as the duplicated definition of the Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA), could be improved.

**Quality**

The paper is of high quality, with a strong theoretical foundation, effective experimental evaluation, and a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm. However, the incremental novelty and limited practical applicability may affect its overall impact.
