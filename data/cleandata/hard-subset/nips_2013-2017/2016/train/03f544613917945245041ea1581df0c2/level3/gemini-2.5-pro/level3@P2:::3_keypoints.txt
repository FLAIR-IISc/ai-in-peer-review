- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining estimators to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Runs the smaller-step chain for twice as many iterations and uses correlated Gaussian noise to control variance of the combined estimator.  
- Connects classical SDE numerical RR techniques to large-scale stochastic-gradient Bayesian inference.  
- Achieves improved theoretical convergence: SGRRLD has MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides rigorous analysis: consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Demonstrates empirical support with synthetic and large-scale real-data experiments corroborating theory.  
- Method is simple, general, and potentially high-impact for improving many SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Novel application of RR to stochastic-gradient settings and non-trivial handling of gradient noise.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and clearly motivated.  
- Question on practical parallel implementation since the γ-chain is idle half the time; asks about running one γ-chain and two γ/2-chains and its variance implications.  
- Requests precise conditions for applicability (e.g., requirement of a weak error expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²)) and extension to other samplers or subsampling MH.  
- Suggests running experiments until full convergence and including non-Bayesian baselines (e.g., SGD) for better context.  
- Asks whether correlating minibatches across chains could further reduce variance beyond Gaussian-noise correlation.  
- Recommends adding a pseudocode algorithm box for SGRRLD and briefly discussing multi-level RR extrapolation (γ, γ/2, γ/4) and trade-offs.
