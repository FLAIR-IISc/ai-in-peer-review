- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: run two SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Theoretically shows O(γ²) bias, O(γ⁴) variance, and MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Method is embarrassingly parallel, suitable for distributed architectures.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: applies RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, not previously used in this context.  
- Builds on prior SGLD and higher-order integrator theory (Welling & Teh 2011; Chen et al. 2015).  
- Empirical results on Gaussian models and matrix factorization show consistent bias and MSE improvements over SGLD.  
- Strengths: elegant theoretical analysis, clear exposition, compelling empirical validation, and practical parallel advantages.  
- Weakness: no direct experimental or theoretical comparison with existing high-order integrator methods (e.g., Chen et al.).  
- Weakness: inconsistencies in step-size description and Algorithm 1 in the supplement contradict Equation (5).  
- Weakness: claims improved rates but does not prove strict superiority over existing high-order methods—shows similar theoretical accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written but should resolve inconsistencies and add the missing high-order method comparison.  
- Significance judged moderate: incremental novelty but valuable practical simplicity and parallelism; overall technically sound but novelty claim weakened by lack of comprehensive comparison.
