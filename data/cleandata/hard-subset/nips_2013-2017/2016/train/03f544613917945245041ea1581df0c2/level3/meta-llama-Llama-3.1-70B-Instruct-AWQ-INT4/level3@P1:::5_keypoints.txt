- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, establishes a central limit theorem, and derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Claims higher convergence rates than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotic regimes.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show reduced bias and lower MSE compared to SGLD.  
- Concept is simple and easy to implement, combining Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with SGLD.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation supporting algorithm validity and performance.  
- Strength: non-asymptotic bounds give practical performance guarantees.  
- Limitation: inconsistent statement about optimal step size—empirical optimal does not always minimize bias/variance as claimed.  
- Limitation: Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5) regarding how often the second chain is run.  
- Limitation: lacks experimental comparison with high-order integrators to validate convergence-rate claims.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify/fix step-size statement and Algorithm 1, and add experiments comparing to high-order integrators.
