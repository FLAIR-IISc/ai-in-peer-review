This paper proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson–Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and carefully correlated Gaussian noise. The authors prove asymptotic consistency and a CLT, derive non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds, and show that SGRRLD achieves O(γ2) bias and O(γ4) variance, with optimal finite-time MSE rate O(K−4/5), matching higher-order integrator accuracy while using first-order discretization. Synthetic Gaussian experiments validate the predicted bias/MSE scaling; a matrix factorization case study reports better RMSE vs wall-clock time than SGLD. The method is positioned relative to SGLD/SGHMC and high-order SG-MCMC analyses [10], and to RR extrapolation for invariant measures in SDEs [23].

Quality: The theory appears careful and technically solid; the asymptotic and non-asymptotic analyses extend prior work in a coherent way, and the empirical results align with the bounds. Some assumptions (smoothness/Poisson equation) are strong; a brief discussion of how they map to common nonconvex posteriors would be helpful.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized, with good context and pointers to the supplement. Minor: “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” is duplicated; please remove the repetition.

Originality: The combination of RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC and the variance control via correlated noise is well executed, but the conceptual novelty may be incremental given (i) prior analysis of high-order integrators for SG-MCMC [10] and (ii) RR for invariant distributions and duplicated diffusions [23]. The paper would benefit from a sharper contrast to these works.

Significance: Potentially impactful if the claimed wall-clock gains translate to realistic parallel settings; the technique is generic and simple to implement.

Main concerns and requests:
- Practicality and fairness: The two correlated chains require per-step communication/synchronization to share Gaussian noise (and possibly gradient correlation). Please quantify this cost and report wall-clock with and without communication.
- Experimental resources: Clarify whether SGLD had access to the same total compute as the two-chain SGRRLD. How were cores/threads split? When gradients dominate runtime, how does halving iterations per chain but doubling chains affect throughput?
- Baselines: Add “two independent SGLD chains” (embarrassingly parallel) with the same total compute and wall-clock budget; aggregate by averaging estimators. This isolates the marginal benefit of RR correlation/extrapolation from pure parallelism.
- Implementation details: Explain how correlated Gaussians were generated across processes, whether stochastic gradients were correlated, and mini-batch choices kept identical.

Pros:
- Strong theory with matching experiments
- Clear presentation; broadly applicable idea

Cons:
- Incremental novelty relative to [10, 23]
- Practical speedups may diminish once communication is accounted for
- Missing parallel baselines and compute accounting
