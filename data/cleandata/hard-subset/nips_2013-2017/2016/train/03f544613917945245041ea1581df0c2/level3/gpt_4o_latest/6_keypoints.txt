- Introduces SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains and correlated noise to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.

- Theoretical contribution: provides asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved convergence rates with rigorous bias and MSE bounds.

- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments show reduced bias/MSE and a large-scale matrix factorization task shows practical wall-clock efficiency gains.

- Generality and scalability: method can extend to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGHMC) and fits parallel/distributed architectures.

- Contextualization: situates work within SG-MCMC literature and references prior higher-order integrator and extrapolation methods.

- Incremental novelty concern: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC may be perceived as incremental given prior work on numerical integrators and extrapolation.

- Practical limitation: reliance on two parallel chains introduces communication overhead that the paper does not address.

- Experimental fairness concern: unclear how computational resources are split for SGRRLD and whether comparisons to two independent SGLD chains were considered.

- Clarity issues: minor redundancies (e.g., repeated mention of ULA) and some unclear sections detract from presentation.

- Suggestion: address communication costs between parallel chains and propose mitigation strategies.

- Suggestion: clarify resource allocation between the two chains and include comparisons to two independent SGLD chains.

- Suggestion: streamline the manuscript by removing redundancies and improving clarity.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing experimental fairness and communication-cost concerns.
