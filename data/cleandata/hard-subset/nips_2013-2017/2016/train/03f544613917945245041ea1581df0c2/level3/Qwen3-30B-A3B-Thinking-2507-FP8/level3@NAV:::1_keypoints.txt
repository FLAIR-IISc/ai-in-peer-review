- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias in SGLD for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Bridges MCMC and SDE literatures by adapting RRE to SG-MCMC, extending prior numerical acceleration work to stochastic gradients.  
- Theoretical guarantees: O(γ²) asymptotic bias, O(γ⁴) variance, and non-asymptotic MSE bound O(K⁻⁴/⁵).  
- Shows theoretical improvement over SGLD’s convergence rate (SGLD: O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Provides technically sound, precise convergence analyses.  
- Empirical validation is thorough: experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets including large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Implementation is computationally efficient and parallelizable, offering practical bias reduction with little overhead.  
- Well-motivated and likely useful in practice for reducing SGLD bias without complex higher-order integrators.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1a is misleading—plots a Gaussian with empirical mean/variance instead of the empirical posterior and needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2a shows counter-intuitive bias behavior vs. stepsize that lacks explanation.  
- Evaluation: high quality, clear, and significant; originality is incremental (clever application of established RRE) but contribution is solid and relevant.
