- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that reduces SGLD bias while controlling variance.  
- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and extrapolating estimators.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows SGRRLD attains higher convergence rates than SGLD in finite-time and asymptotically, matching higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data demonstrates large gains on toy problems and modest consistent improvements on large-scale tasks.  
- Implementation details: authors implemented SGRRLD in C and used the GNU Scientific Library for efficient matrix computations.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; technical proofs are appropriately placed in the supplement.  
- Nicely bridges MCMC and SDE/numerical analysis literature by importing RRE to improve SGLD.  
- Figure issues: Figure 1a is misleading (plots Gaussian with empirical moments instead of empirical posterior) and needs caption clarification.  
- Figure 2a needs discussion to explain why bias may not decrease with smaller step size, which is counter-intuitive.  
- Strengths: thorough theory, solid experiments, practical and computationally convenient method likely useful in practice.  
- Weaknesses: method is a straightforward application of RRE and not transformative; RRE-for-MCMC idea not entirely new.  
- Potential limitation: may have limited interest outside Bayesian machine learning niche.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10; balanced arguments for and against acceptance.
