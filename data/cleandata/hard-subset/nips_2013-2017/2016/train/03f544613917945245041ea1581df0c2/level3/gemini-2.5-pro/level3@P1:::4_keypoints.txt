- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to cancel leading discretization error.  
- Provides theoretical results: consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds claiming improved rates (MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs standard O(K⁻²/³)).  
- Method is simple to implement and parallelizable; experiments (including MovieLens) show practical benefits.  
- Major theoretical gap: paper omits the explicit derivation optimizing step-size decay α from Theorem 2 to obtain the claimed O(K⁻²/³) bias and O(K⁻⁴/⁵) MSE rates.  
- Experimental fairness concern: wall-clock comparisons favor SGRRLD's parallelism but neglect that it requires ~1.5× more gradient computations; single-core comparisons or matched computational budgets are needed.  
- Inconsistency between text and experiments: text claims optimal α=1/3 for bias but Figure 3 shows α=0.2 performs best.  
- Unclear experimental phenomena: bias/MSE increase for very small γ (esp. why more pronounced for SGRRLD) and large fluctuations for α=0.2 lack sufficient explanation.  
- Requested action: include full derivation of optimal rates from Theorem 2 to close the logical gap.  
- Requested action: report total computational cost (e.g., gradient evaluations) in experiments alongside wall-clock time.  
- Requested action: resolve and explain the discrepancy about optimal α between text and figure.  
- Minor issues to fix: define "RR" before use; define ΓK before referencing; state precise smoothness assumptions for f; use consistent θ/ϑ notation; correct Figure 1(b) y-axis label; consider boldface for vectors.  
- Overall verdict: promising and potentially impactful work, but major theoretical and experimental issues must be addressed before strong acceptance.
