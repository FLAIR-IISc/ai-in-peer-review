- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC method that applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.
- Implements RR by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combining them.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE.
- Shows SGRRLD attains higher convergence rates than first-order SG-MCMC methods in finite-time and asymptotically.
- Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in bias and MSE and reaches equal accuracy faster.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible for expert readers.
- Novel use of RR extrapolation is a new contribution to the SG-MCMC community with potential high impact in Bayesian ML.
- Unclear whether the method applies to all SG-MCMC algorithms or only to those meeting specific conditions.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational efficiency and scalability for large-scale applications.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on clarifying applicability to other SG-MCMC methods and adding computational-efficiency analysis.
