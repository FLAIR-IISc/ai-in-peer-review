- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by combining two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ²).  
- Strong theoretical contribution: proofs that SGRRLD achieves lower asymptotic bias and MSE than SGLD with convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/₃).  
- Claims SGRRLD attains higher-order integrator accuracy while using a first-order integrator.  
- Algorithm is clearly described with accessible pseudo-code, facilitating implementation.  
- Synthetic experiments (linear Gaussian) clearly demonstrate significant bias reduction (variance estimate bias from ~1e-2 to ~1e-4).  
- Real-data MovieLens results show only modest RMSE improvements and lack evidence of statistical significance or practical meaningfulness.  
- Missing comparisons: authors do not compare SGRRLD to running multiple parallel SGLD chains or SGLD at γ/2 to isolate RR benefits.  
- The paper does not specify sufficient conditions on the potential U (e.g., multimodality) required for theoretical results to hold.  
- Unaddressed issue of gradient noise correlation and whether the two chains remain correlated enough to undermine RR extrapolation in practice.  
- No analysis or reporting of chain correlation behavior in real-data experiments as claimed in theory.  
- Parallelization/compute-cost claims are problematic without direct comparison to multi-chain SGLD baselines.  
- Strengths summarized: novel RR application to SG-MCMC, rigorous theory, clear algorithm, demonstrable synthetic bias reduction, and generalizability to other SG-MCMC methods.  
- Weaknesses summarized: weak real-data evidence and statistical testing, incomplete theoretical condition specification, lacking analysis of noise correlation, and insufficient cost/baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound and theoretically promising, but practical significance remains uncertain pending stronger experiments, theoretical condition clarification, and proper baseline comparisons.
