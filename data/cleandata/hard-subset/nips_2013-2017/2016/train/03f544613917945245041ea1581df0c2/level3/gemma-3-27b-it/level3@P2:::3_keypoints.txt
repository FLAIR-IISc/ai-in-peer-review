- Proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine outputs via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SG-MCMC bias.
- The approach is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and builds on existing SGLD with minimal modification (suggest adding an early pseudocode box).
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE demonstrating improved convergence rates.
- Demonstrates that SGRRLD can match higher-order integrator accuracy while using only a first-order scheme.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data support the theoretical benefits.
- Unclear applicability: method relies on expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2), and it’s not shown whether all SG-MCMC algorithms satisfy this condition.
- Needs discussion on extending extrapolation to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH) or distributed SGLD.
- Potential computational inefficiency: one chain must run twice as long, causing idle time despite parallelizability.
- Suggests investigating three-chain setups (or more step sizes) to balance runtime and variance, and possible extensions to further improve rates.
- Unclear treatment of noise/minibatch correlation: paper specifies Brownian increment correlation but doesn’t analyze whether sharing minibatches between chains is optimal.
- Experimental shortcomings: matrix factorization experiment should run to convergence, include an SGD baseline, and report additional metrics like log-likelihood.
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel contribution with solid theory and empirical support; recommend acceptance if authors address applicability, computational-efficiency, and minibatch correlation concerns.
