- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and correlated Brownian motions to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretical analysis shows SGRRLD attains MSE rate O(K^(-4/5)) vs SGLD's O(K^(-2/3)), matching higher-order integrator accuracy despite first-order discretization.  
- Provides clear asymptotic and non-asymptotic bounds, with careful bias, variance, and convergence rate analysis under appropriate conditions.  
- Empirical results on synthetic Gaussian models and MovieLens matrix factorization show improved accuracy and comparable (often reduced) wall-clock time.  
- Novelty: extends Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to stochastic-gradient MCMC, which is new relative to prior RR work on standard SDE discretizations.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical foundations, clear algorithm presentation, compelling synthetic and real-data experiments, and natural parallelizability for distributed settings.  
- Main novelty concern: contribution appears incremental relative to prior work (e.g., Chen et al. 2015) on higher-order integrators; distinction from using higher-order integrators is insufficiently clarified.  
- Practicality concern: paper omits discussion of communication/coordination costs between parallel chains in distributed environments.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear whether SGLD was given equal compute resources as SGRRLD (SGRRLD runs two chains), making wall-clock comparisons potentially misleading.  
- Baseline issue: should compare against two independent parallel SGLD chains to fairly assess the benefit of RR extrapolation versus simply running two chains.  
- Minor issues: duplicate definition of ULA and some unclear notation in theoretical sections.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to clarify novelty vs higher-order integrators, address experimental resource/communication details, and include fairer baselines.
