- Proposes SGRRLD: Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC using two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) to cancel O(γ) bias and achieve O(γ^2) bias.
- Correlates Gaussian noise via Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2 so the extrapolated estimator reduces variance while removing leading weak error.
- Theoretical contributions: CLT, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and finite-time convergence rate O(K^−4/5), matching higher-order integrators while using a first-order scheme.
- Experiments on a Gaussian model and matrix factorization show improved accuracy over SGLD and SGHMC.
- Places RR extrapolation in context: standard in numerical SDEs (Talay–Tubaro; Lemaire–Pagès) but novel application to SG-MCMC in ML, related to Ma et al. and Chen et al.
- Theory is careful and sound under clearly stated (though strong) smoothness and Poisson equation assumptions; variance control via noise correlation is well justified.
- Paper is well-written and organized; adding a concise algorithm pseudocode early would aid practitioners.
- Originality: transferring RR with explicit noise-correlation to SG-MCMC is novel and non-trivial for the community.
- Significance: method is simple to implement, broadly compatible with SG-MCMC variants, and potentially impactful due to generality and ease of adoption.
- Applicability question: does the approach require the expansion πγ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2), and which SG-MCMC schemes (e.g., state-dependent preconditioning, thermostats) satisfy this?
- Generalization question: can the method extend beyond Langevin to approximate Metropolis methods (subsampling MH) or asynchronous/distributed SGLD?
- Compute-efficiency concern: one chain runs 2× more steps—can a 3-chain design or other scheduling preserve variance gains without idle resources?
- Extension question: is multi-level/multi-step RR practical here, and what are the variance–cost trade-offs?
- Noise/minibatch correlation: Gaussian coupling is shown optimal, but what is the effect of correlating minibatches/gradient noise (e.g., identical minibatches) in practice?
- Experimental weaknesses: matrix factorization experiments should run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to contextualize RMSE and demonstrate Bayesian advantages.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general, theoretically grounded, novel to ML, and potentially high impact.
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental scope and need for practical guidance on applicability and resource efficiency.
