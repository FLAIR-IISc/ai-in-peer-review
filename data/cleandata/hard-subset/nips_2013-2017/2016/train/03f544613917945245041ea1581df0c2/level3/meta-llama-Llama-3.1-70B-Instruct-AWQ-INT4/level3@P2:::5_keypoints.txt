- Proposes SGRRLD: an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation that runs two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on non-asymptotic bias and mean-squared error.  
- Relates to SGLD and other SG-MCMC methods, claiming SGRRLD can reach accuracy comparable to higher-order integrator–based methods.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with detailed derivation of the algorithm and properties.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Strength: empirical validation on synthetic and real data showing effective bias reduction.  
- Weakness: no rigorous proof of an improved convergence rate is provided.  
- Weakness: similar rate improvements might be achievable with high-order integrators, but no experimental comparison to those methods is shown.  
- Weakness: inconsistent statements about the step size across the paper.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5).  
- Originality: novel combination of RR extrapolation and SGLD, but concept of RR for SG-MCMC is not entirely new.  
- Significance: potential to improve accuracy of SG-MCMC and suitability for parallel/distributed architectures makes it promising for large-scale applications.  
- Overall assessment: good quality but not excellent due to theoretical gaps and inconsistencies.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel/significant contribution, solid theoretical analysis, and satisfactory empirical results.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: lacks convergence-rate proof, missing comparisons to high-order integrators, and contains inconsistencies to be corrected.  
- Recommended improvements: provide rigorous convergence-rate proof, compare experimentally to other SG-MCMC/high-order integrators, fix step-size and algorithm inconsistencies, and add a more detailed discussion of limitations.
