- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step size γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to cancel O(γ) bias.  
- Novelty: first to combine RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC for bias reduction in scalable Bayesian inference; simple and easy to implement.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a Central Limit Theorem and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved MSE rate O(K^{-4/5}) vs SGLD's O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Empirical validation (synthetic): synthetic Gaussian experiments clearly demonstrate bias reduction and validate convergence rates in γ and K.  
- Empirical validation (real-world): large-scale matrix factorization on three datasets shows better prediction accuracy than SGLD for comparable wall-clock time.  
- Practicality: two chains run fully in parallel with no communication overhead, making the method suited to multi-core architectures and mitigating doubled computation.  
- Generality: approach extends to other SG-MCMC methods (demonstrated for SGHMC), indicating broader applicability.  
- Experimental limitation: lacks a direct experimental comparison of SGRRLD against SGLD with higher-order integrators, weakening claims about matching higher-order methods.  
- Computational-cost clarity: paper should more explicitly discuss the trade-off between doubled gradient evaluations and statistical efficiency, beyond relying on parallel hardware assumptions.  
- Minor inconsistencies/typos: possible typo in Chain 2 update in Eq. (5) and mismatch between Eq. (5) and Algorithm 1 in supplement—authors should verify.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, strong non-trivial theoretical analysis, convincing experiments, practical parallelizability, and generality to other samplers.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete experimental comparison with higher-accuracy alternatives and a few clarity/typographical issues to fix.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution with minor addressable issues; recommend acceptance.
