- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs, with theoretical CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved convergence (e.g., MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³)), validated on a synthetic Gaussian and MovieLens.

- Novelty: Innovative and simple application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, offering a principled way to reduce SGLD discretization bias.

- Theory: Strong, rigorous theoretical support including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds demonstrating faster convergence rates.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents the algorithm in an accessible, reproducible way.

- Synthetic experiments: Convincing validation on linear Gaussian models and extension to SGHMC, matching theoretical rates and showing bias/MSE reduction.

- Real-world evidence weak: MovieLens results show only marginal RMSE improvement; final performance gain is limited, questioning practical benefit.

- Unfair comparisons: Efficiency claims rely on parallelism; authors should compare to fair baselines (e.g., two parallel SGLD chains or a single SGLD with γ/2 run twice) to isolate RR benefits from extra compute.

- Correlation concerns: Practical effectiveness may be reduced by stochastic gradient noise if mini-batches are independent; paper lacks discussion/clarity on mini-batch sampling and chain correlation in experiments.

- Theory assumptions unclear: Main text defers key conditions on U, f, L to supplement; need brief summary (e.g., whether strong convexity is required) since this affects applicability to non-convex/multimodal posteriors.

- Experimental baseline request: Add fairer baselines (two parallel SGLD chains; single chain with γ/2 for twice iterations) to validate RR extrapolation benefits.

- Real-world impact request: Test on other large-scale models where bias matters (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, topic models) to strengthen practical claims.

- Clarification request on correlation and assumptions: Specify how mini-batches were sampled for the two chains, report observed chain correlation, and briefly state the assumptions on U required for the theorems (and whether non-convex/multimodal cases are covered).
