- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining results to reduce bias.  
- Theoretically proves asymptotically lower bias O(γ²) vs SGLD O(γ) and MSE O(γ⁴) vs O(γ²), with convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Algorithm is clearly described and easily implementable.  
- Rigorous convergence analysis is a clear theoretical strength.  
- Unclear required conditions on U (e.g., whether multimodal potentials are allowed).  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments convincingly show large bias and MSE reductions.  
- MovieLens real-data results show only marginal RMSE improvements, questioning practical significance.  
- Claim that parallelization cost is comparable to SGLD is questionable since SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains.  
- Insufficient comparison to multi-chain SGLD and lack of experiments addressing gradient-noise and chain correlation in practice.  
- Contribution is novel in applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and advances theoretical understanding, but practical impact is limited.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient empirical evidence of practical advantage over existing methods.
