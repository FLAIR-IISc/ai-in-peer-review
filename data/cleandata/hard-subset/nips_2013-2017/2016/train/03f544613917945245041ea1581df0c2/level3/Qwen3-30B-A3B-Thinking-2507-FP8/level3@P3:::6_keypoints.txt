- Paper proposes SGRRLD: run two correlated SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD yields bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴), and MSE convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: provides asymptotic CLT and non-asymptotic bounds with clear bias/MSE analysis.  
- Strength — Practical validation: synthetic and real matrix factorization experiments support theoretical gains and show consistent accuracy improvements.  
- Strength — Clear presentation: well-organized exposition and motivated connection between RR extrapolation and SG-MCMC.  
- Strength — Technical contribution: extends RR extrapolation to stochastic-gradient settings with appropriate correlation of Brownian increments.  
- Weakness — Novelty concern: approach appears incremental over prior work [10] (integrator analysis) and [23] (RR for invariant distributions); clearer differentiation needed.  
- Weakness — Practical applicability: does not address communication cost for correlating two parallel chains or justify wall-clock time/effective compute comparisons.  
- Weakness — Baseline fairness: lacks comparison to two independent parallel SGLD chains as a baseline to assess benefit of RR versus simple parallelism.  
- Weakness — Minor editorial: duplicated definition of "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" to be fixed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to clarify novelty, detail experimental resource/communication setup, add independent-chains baseline, and fix the duplicated ULA definition.
