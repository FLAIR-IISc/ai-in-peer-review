- Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SGLD to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Implements RRE by running two parallel SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2, second run for twice the steps) and linearly combining estimators to cancel leading bias.  
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC/SGLD work and bridges SDE numerical-analysis RRE literature with large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Empirical validation on a synthetic Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization supports theoretical claims.  
- Method is simple to implement, inherently parallelizable, and practically effective (≈ half the wall-clock time to reach same accuracy in experiments).  
- Offers a practical alternative to higher-order integrators while retaining Euler-Maruyama simplicity.  
- Originality caveat: RRE itself is not new; contribution is novel mainly in its application to SG-MCMC.  
- Minor clarity issues: Figure 1(a) caption could mislead about plotted density; Figure 2 needs clearer discussion of bias/MSE increase at very small step sizes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective method, strong theoretical contributions, practical relevance, clear presentation, and bridges two literatures.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived as somewhat incremental since it adapts an established technique.  
- Overall assessment: very strong paper whose theoretical, practical, and expository strengths outweigh the minor weaknesses.
