This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient Markov chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), that aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The approach is based on Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, which requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis, including asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis, and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through synthetic and real-data experiments.

The paper relates to previous work on SG-MCMC, including the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm, and provides a clear overview of the limitations of existing methods. The authors also situate their work within the broader context of numerical analysis, citing relevant work on Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and its application to stochastic differential equations.

The reviewer finds the technique and presentation strong, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method. However, the main concern is that the novelty of the approach may be incremental, given prior analysis of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful to evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, and the paper provides a complete piece of work.

Regarding clarity, the paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow for an expert reader. The authors provide enough information for the reader to reproduce the results, and the paper includes a clear description of the proposed algorithm and its implementation.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, and the application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is new. However, as mentioned earlier, the novelty of the approach may be incremental, given prior work in the area.

Regarding significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in Bayesian inference, and the proposed method has the potential to improve the accuracy and efficiency of SG-MCMC algorithms. The experimental results demonstrate the practical gains of the proposed approach over standard SGLD, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of the method.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that addresses a difficult problem in Bayesian inference.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis.
* The experimental results demonstrate the practical gains of the proposed approach over standard SGLD.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow for an expert reader.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The novelty of the approach may be incremental, given prior analysis of numerical integrators on SG-MCMC and prior use of RR extrapolation for invariant distributions.
* The practical applicability of the method may be limited, given the requirement for correlated Gaussian noise and the potential communication cost of running two parallel chains.
* The experimental setup and resource allocation could be clarified, including whether SGLD used the same compute resources as the two-chain SGRRLD, how resources are split, and how partial resources per chain affect wall-clock time given gradient cost.

Minor comments:

* The phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" is duplicated on lines 25 and 70.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains as an alternative baseline.
