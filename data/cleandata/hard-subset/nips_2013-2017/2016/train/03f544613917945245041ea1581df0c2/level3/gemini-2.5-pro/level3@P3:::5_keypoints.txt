- Proposed method (SG-RR-MCMC / SGRRLD): apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains (step γk and two steps γk/2) and combining their outputs.  
- Main theoretical claim: SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and achieves improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Theoretical contributions: provides a Central Limit Theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds for the estimator.  
- Practical advantage: method is embarrassingly parallel (two independent chains), enabling halved wall-clock time for same gradient evaluations on two cores.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments show reduced bias/MSE; MovieLens matrix factorization shows better accuracy than SGLD for same wall-clock time.  
- Originality/significance: novel and useful combination of existing techniques, addressing an important practical bias problem in SG-MCMC.  
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis that supports the improved convergence rate and estimator behavior.  
- Major clarity/implementation issue: Equation (5) appears to use an implicit update (gradient evaluated at the new state), contradicting explicit Euler-Maruyama and likely a critical typo.  
- Inconsistency concern: Algorithm 1 in the supplement reportedly contradicts Eq.(5), indicating imprecision that harms reproducibility and reader confidence.  
- Limited experimental comparison: lacks comprehensive comparisons to higher-order SGLD integrators across synthetic and real-data tasks, weakening claims about practical parity with higher-order integrators.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, simple, effective method with strong theory, parallel practicality, and supportive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: significant clarity/errors in core algorithmic equations and insufficient experimental benchmarking against alternative bias-reduction methods.
