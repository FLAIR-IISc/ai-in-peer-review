This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The algorithm uses Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method, to improve the rate of convergence of SGLD.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error. The results show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than SGLD and other SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators, both in finite-time and asymptotically. The paper also presents empirical results on synthetic and real data experiments, demonstrating the effectiveness of SGRRLD in reducing bias and improving convergence rates.

* The paper proposes a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that addresses the bias issue of SGLD, which is a significant problem in Bayesian inference.
