The paper "Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo" proposes a novel sampling algorithm that aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The approach is based on the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method that requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.

The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that the proposed algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive bounds for its non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared error, and show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than all known SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators in both finite-time and asymptotically.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The experimental results on both synthetic and real data experiments support the theoretical findings and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel sampling algorithm that addresses the bias issue in SG-MCMC.
* The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and derive bounds for the non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared error.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with SG-MCMC and Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, which may not be the case for all readers.
* The theoretical analysis is based on certain assumptions, and it is not clear how these assumptions affect the results.
* The experimental results are limited to a few datasets, and it is not clear how the proposed approach performs on other datasets.
