- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC: combine two SGLD chains with different step sizes via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Conceptually simple and embarrassingly parallel, so it can improve accuracy without large extra computational cost.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency and provides non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and a matrix factorization task support the theoretical claims.  
- Draws a useful connection to higher-order integrators, suggesting comparable accuracy with simpler components.  
- Claims of efficiency gains lack a rigorous convergence rate analysis to substantiate faster convergence.  
- Missing empirical and theoretical comparisons to other bias-reduction methods or higher-order integrators (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Manuscript contains inconsistencies regarding step size and a discrepancy between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5).  
- Authors acknowledge some gaps; they should fix inconsistencies and add discussion/comparison to alternatives.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing the noted issues.
