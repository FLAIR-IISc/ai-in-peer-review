- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Provides theoretical analysis claiming improved convergence rates, including a CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC and RR-for-SDE literature; novelty is applying RR to SG-MCMC.
- Strength: novel idea with clear potential to reduce bias in SG-MCMC.
- Strength: strong theoretical development and convergence proofs (subject to verification).
- Strength: algorithm is clearly described and implementable.
- Strength: synthetic Gaussian experiments show clear bias reduction.
- Strength: algorithm supports inherent parallelism, useful for large-scale applications.
- Weakness: real-data improvement (MovieLens) is slight and inconclusive.
- Weakness: proofs not fully verified by reviewer; assumptions on U (regularity, multimodality) need clarification.
- Weakness: insufficient treatment of gradient noise correlation and how decorrelated chains still yield error-order reduction.
- Weakness: computational cost comparison is incomplete—need direct comparison to multi-chain SGLD and SGLD with step size gamma/2.
- Weakness: experiments are limited in scope; more datasets and hyperparameter tuning required.
- For acceptance: novel technique, solid theoretical foundation, clear algorithm, synthetic gains.
- Against acceptance: weak practical evidence, unverified proofs, incomplete discussion of noise correlation and cost, limited experiments.
- Recommendation: strengthen real-data experiments, clarify proof conditions (esp. multimodality), analyze gradient-noise correlation, add benchmarks to multi-chain and smaller-step SGLD, and explore multi-level RR in future work.
- Final stance: weak accept contingent on addressing the above concerns; otherwise recommend rejection.
