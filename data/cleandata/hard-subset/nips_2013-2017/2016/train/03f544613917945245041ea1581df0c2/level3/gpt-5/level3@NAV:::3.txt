This paper proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson–Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), bringing Richardson–Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SG-MCMC to reduce asymptotic bias. The method runs two SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 (the latter taking twice as many steps), correlates Gaussian noise via Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2, and forms an extrapolated estimator that cancels the O(γ) weak error, yielding O(γ^2) bias. The authors prove a CLT, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and a finite-time rate O(K^−4/5), matching the accuracy of higher-order integrators (e.g., Chen et al., NIPS’15) while using a first-order scheme. Experiments on a Gaussian model and matrix factorization show improved accuracy over SGLD and SGHMC. RR extrapolation is standard in numerical SDEs (Talay–Tubaro; Lemaire–Pagès), but its application to SG-MCMC appears new to the ML community, relating to NIPS work on SG-MCMC foundations and high-order integrators (Ma et al.; Chen et al.).

Quality: The theory is careful and sound under clearly stated (though strong) smoothness/Poisson equation assumptions, and the variance control via correlated Brownian noise is well justified. Empirical results are positive but could be strengthened (see below).

Clarity: The paper is well-written and organized. A concise algorithm pseudocode box early in the main text would substantially aid practitioners.

Originality: The transfer of RR to SG-MCMC with explicit variance control via noise correlation is novel and non-trivial for this community.

Significance: The idea is simple to implement, broadly compatible with many SG-MCMC variants, and potentially impactful due to its generality and ease of adoption.

Questions/weaknesses and suggestions:
- Applicability: Does the approach require the expansion πγ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2)? Which SG-MCMC schemes satisfy this, especially with state-dependent preconditioning or thermostats?
- Beyond Langevin: Can this extend to approximate Metropolis methods (e.g., subsampling MH) or distributed SGLD where gradients/minibatches are asynchronous?
- Compute efficiency: One chain runs 2× longer. Can a 3-chain design (one γ, two γ/2 with equal wall-time) eliminate idle resources while preserving the variance benefits of noise coupling?
- Extensions: Is multi-level/multi-step RR (more than two step sizes) practical here, and what is the variance–cost trade-off?
- Noise/minibatch correlation: You show Gaussian coupling is optimal. What is the effect of correlating minibatches/gradient noise (e.g., using identical minibatches across chains)? Is it beneficial or negligible in practice?
- Experiments: For matrix factorization, please run to convergence and include an SGD baseline to contextualize RMSE and demonstrate a Bayesian advantage (e.g., uncertainty-aware metrics).

Pros for acceptance:
- Simple, general, theoretically grounded method with clear gains; novel to ML; strong potential impact.

Cons for acceptance:
- Experimental scope limited; practical guidance on applicability and resource efficiency needs elaboration.
