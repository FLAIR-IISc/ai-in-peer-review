- Paper introduces SG-RR-MCMC (SGRRLD), applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and combining results to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Theoretical analysis shows asymptotic consistency and derives bias and MSE bounds, with improved convergence rate (O(K^-4/5) vs O(K^-2/3) for SGLD).  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and matrix factorization tasks demonstrate practical effectiveness, often outperforming SGLD and matching higher-order integrators in some cases.  
- Method is embarrassingly parallel (two independent chains) and conceptually simple to implement on existing SGLD codebases.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a new and useful idea in this context.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding supporting the bias-reduction claim.  
- Strength: empirical validation supports theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: step size notation/description is inconsistent between introduction and later γ_k notation and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 in the supplement appears inconsistent with Equation (5); the update steps for the halved-step chain do not match the RR scheme and must be corrected.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison to higher-order integrators (e.g., SGHMC) is limited and should be broadened to substantiate accuracy claims.  
- Weakness: discussion and experimental analysis of variance control and correlated Brownian increments (choice of Σ) is underdeveloped and sensitivity to correlation structures is not explored.  
- For acceptance: paper is novel, theoretically sound, empirically promising, parallelizable, and addresses a known SG-MCMC limitation.  
- Against acceptance: unresolved algorithm/notation inconsistencies, limited integrator comparisons, and incomplete variance/correlation analysis weaken the manuscript.  
- Overall: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix algorithm/step-size inconsistencies, expand comparisons to higher-order methods, and clarify/experimentally investigate variance control and correlated increments.
