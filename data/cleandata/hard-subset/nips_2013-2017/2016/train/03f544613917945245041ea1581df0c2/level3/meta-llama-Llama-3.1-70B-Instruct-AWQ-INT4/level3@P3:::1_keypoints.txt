- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SGLD to improve convergence rate.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and MSE.  
- Shows SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and attains higher convergence rates than SGLD (finite-time and asymptotic).  
- Demonstrates SGRRLD reaches theoretical accuracy of methods using higher-order integrators.  
- Method is straightforward, computationally convenient, clearly written, and appears technically sound.  
- Improvements over SGLD are modest but consistent; not a transformative advance.  
- Empirical results: large improvements on toy examples and modest but consistent gains on large-scale problems.  
- Experiments show reduced bias and MSE in variance estimates and faster or better accuracy in matrix factorization tasks.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1a is misleading—uses a Gaussian with empirical mean/variance instead of the empirical posterior; caption should clarify.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2a needs discussion explaining why bias may not decrease as step size decreases.  
- Originality rated moderate (8/10): novel application of RR to SGLD but RR extrapolation itself is not new.  
- Quality (9/10), Clarity (9/10), Significance (9/10) — overall strong evaluation supporting acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theory, and supportive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty/transformative impact, modest improvements, and minor figure issues.
