- Introduces SGRRLD: a novel SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel chains to reduce bias and MSE, supported by theory and experiments.

- Strength — Theoretical contributions: provides asymptotic consistency, CLT, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and improved convergence rates (e.g., O(K^-4/5) vs SGLD O(K^-2/3)).

- Strength — Practical implementation: simple to implement with minimal changes and compatible with parallel/distributed architectures.

- Strength — Experimental validation: synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization experiments show reduced bias/MSE and computational efficiency gains over SGLD.

- Strength — Generality: method extends to other SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGHMC).

- Weakness — Convergence rate derivations: lack of clarity in deriving specific rates (α = 0.2 vs α = 1/3) and discrepancies in Figure 3.

- Weakness — Experimental fairness: matrix factorization comparisons may be unfair; SGLD runtime should be adjusted to account for SGRRLD's two-chain overhead.

- Weakness — Result fluctuations: significant fluctuations in the optimal curve (Figure 3) may not align with theoretical rates and need clarification.

- Weakness — Unexplained assumptions: unclear why the 1/(Kγ) term doesn't dominate for SGLD at small step sizes (Figure 2).

- Weakness — Minor issues: undefined symbols (RR, Γ_K), unclear smoothness assumptions for f, incorrect figure references, and formatting/readability problems.

- For acceptance: addresses bias limitation in SG-MCMC with a novel, theoretically sound, and practical method that shows empirical benefits.

- Against acceptance: concerns about experimental fairness and clarity of theoretical derivations, plus numerous presentation issues.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — significant contribution but needs clearer convergence derivations, corrected experimental comparisons, and improved clarity/formatting.
