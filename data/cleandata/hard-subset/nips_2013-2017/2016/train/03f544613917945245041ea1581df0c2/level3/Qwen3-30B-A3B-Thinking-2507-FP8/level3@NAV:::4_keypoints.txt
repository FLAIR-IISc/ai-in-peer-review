- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel SG-MCMC chains to reduce bias and MSE.
- Claim: SGRRLD achieves O(K^{-4/5}) MSE convergence versus SGLD's O(K^{-2/3}) while remaining simple to implement.
- Key idea: Run two chains with different step sizes and combine their outputs via extrapolation.
- Theoretical strength: Solid convergence analysis supporting the claimed rates and extension of RR extrapolation to stochastic gradient setting.
- Relation to prior work: Effectively connects to SG-MCMC literature and RR extrapolation for SDEs.
- Practical appeal: Minimal code changes and compatibility with parallel architectures.
- Major practical concern: Method requires ~3× computation on a single core (two chains, one with twice the steps), making Figure 5 timing comparisons misleading.
- When runtime is properly scaled (2/3 of SGLD time), the empirical performance advantage substantially diminishes.
- Experimental inconsistencies: Fig.3 shows optimal bias at α=0.2, contradicting the text’s α=1/3 claim.
- Empirical instability: The “optimal” curve fluctuates substantially, questioning agreement with theoretical rates.
- Unexplained observation: Line 256’s claim that SGLD isn’t dominated by the 1/(Kγ) term at small stepsizes lacks explanation.
- Presentation issues: Multiple minor notation problems (e.g., Eq.1, Γ_K, undefined "RR", vector formatting).
- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical contribution but practical implementation and experimental discrepancies weaken the paper; recommend rejection unless timing and experimental issues are addressed.
