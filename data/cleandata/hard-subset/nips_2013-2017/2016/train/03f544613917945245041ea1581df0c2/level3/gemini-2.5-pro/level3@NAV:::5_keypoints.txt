- Core idea: Proposes SG-RR-MCMC, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (demonstrated with SGLD) by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading discretization error.
- Theoretical strength: Proves asymptotic consistency and an improved non-asymptotic MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving on SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Practicality: Two-chain design is embarrassingly parallel with no communication overhead, fitting multi-core architectures.
- Empirical support: Synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization experiments show SGRRLD outperforms standard SGLD.
- Originality concern: Method is a clever synthesis of existing techniques (RR extrapolation + SG-MCMC) rather than a fundamentally new concept.
- Experimental gap: Missing a direct comparison to SGLD implemented with higher-order integrators to substantiate claims of matching higher-order accuracy.
- Clarity and technical issues: Manuscript has minor but important errors—an inconsistent step-size schedule statement and Algorithm 1 in the supplement appears inconsistent with Equation (5).
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed, and practical contribution with strong theory and convincing empirical results.
