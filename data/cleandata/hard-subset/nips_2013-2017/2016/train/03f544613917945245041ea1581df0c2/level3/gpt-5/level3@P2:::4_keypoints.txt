- Paper proposes SGRRLD: run two SGLD chains with stepsizes γ and γ/2, combine time-averaged estimates via Richardson–Romberg to cancel first-order bias while using correlated Brownian noise to control variance.  
- Theoretical contributions: consistency, CLT with optimal variance at fully correlated Brownian increments (Σ = Id), and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Asymptotic rates: for γk ∝ k−α, SGRRLD achieves O(K−2/5) at α=1/5, improving over SGLD’s O(K−1/3).  
- Fixed-stepsize finite-K rates: optimal choices give bias O(K−2/3) and MSE O(K−4/5), again improving over SGLD (bias O(K−1/2), MSE O(K−2/3)).  
- Experiments on a Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization support the theoretical improvements.  
- Novelty: applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC with optimal Brownian coupling appears new and simpler than designing higher-order integrators.  
- Theory quality: CLT and finite-time bounds are valuable, but derivations of rate scalings (e.g., behavior of Γ(3)K/√ΓK for γk ∝ k−α) are only sketched and should be shown explicitly in the main text.  
- Assumptions: smoothness/regularity conditions on U, f, gradient noise law L, and Poisson equation requirements are insufficiently stated in the main text.  
- Coupling of stochastic gradients across chains is under-discussed; minibatch coupling could further reduce variance and needs ablation/discussion.  
- Experiments: synthetic results informative, but matrix factorization comparisons are not compute-normalized and fairness is questionable.  
- Notational and presentation issues: inconsistent use of ϑ vs θ, RR used before definition, ΓK used before defining Γ(n)K, and imprecise statement “smooth function f.”  
- Figure issues: Figure 3 caption/axes mix “bias vs time vs dimension” and appear inconsistent; labeling and panels (bias vs MSE) need correction and clarification.  
- Clarity suggestions: use boldface for vectors, include minimal pseudocode in main text, and define all quantities upfront.  
- Major concern 1: request explicit derivation from Theorem 1 showing ΓK ≍ K1−α, Γ(3)K ≍ K1−3α, and resulting error tradeoff leading to α=1/5.  
- Major concern 2: Figure 3 inconsistency—text claims α=1/3 optimal for Theorem 2 but plots peak at α=0.2; increase repeats, add confidence bands, and reconcile finite-K effects.  
- Major concern 3: small-γ regime—1/(Kγ) term should dominate for both SGRRLD and SGLD at small γ; clarify why this isn’t observed for SGLD in Fig. 2.  
- Major concern 4: fairness in matrix factorization—each SGRRLD outer step uses ~3 gradient evaluations; provide results normalized by gradient evaluations/FLOPs, single-core CPU-time plots, and baseline with two parallel SGLD chains averaged.  
- Additional suggestions: include ablation on Brownian coupling (Σ=Id vs Σ=0), study minibatch coupling effects, sensitivity to minibatch size and stepsize schedules, and compute-normalized comparison to higher-order SGHMC.  
- Pros: simple and generic method, solid theoretical guarantees, clear asymptotic and finite-time improvements, and empirical gains on synthetic and large tasks.  
- Cons: experimental compute-fairness issues, missing main-text derivations and assumptions, notation/figure inconsistencies, and limited ablations on coupling/minibatch choices.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying rate derivations, fixing experimental fairness, and addressing noted inconsistencies.  
- Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
