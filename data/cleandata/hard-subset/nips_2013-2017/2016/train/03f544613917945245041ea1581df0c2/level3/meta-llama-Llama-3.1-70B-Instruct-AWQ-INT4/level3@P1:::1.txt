This paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) technique to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD). The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including its asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have done an excellent job in explaining the technical details of the algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The use of RRE in the context of SG-MCMC is a novel and interesting idea, and the authors have successfully bridged the gap between the MCMC and stochastic differential equations (SDEs) literatures.

The empirical results presented in the paper are impressive, showing large improvements over vanilla SGLD on toy examples and modest but consistent gains on large-scale problems. The experiments on synthetic data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in reducing the bias and mean squared error, and the results on real-world data show its potential in large-scale applications.

The paper is solid, and the method is a straightforward, computationally convenient application of RRE to SGLD. While the method may not be transformative, it is likely to be useful in practice, especially in situations where reducing bias is crucial.

One minor issue with the paper is the misleading plot in Figure 1a, which shows a Gaussian with empirical mean and variance plugged in, rather than the empirical posterior. This should be clarified in the caption.

Another minor issue is the lack of discussion on why the bias may not decrease as the step size decreases in Figure 2a. This is counter-intuitive and requires some explanation.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The proposed algorithm is a valuable addition to the toolkit of Bayesian practitioners, and the theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for understanding its properties.

**Strengths:**

* Novel application of RRE to SG-MCMC
* Thorough theoretical analysis, including asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds
* Impressive empirical results on toy examples and large-scale problems
* Well-written paper with clear explanations of technical details

**Weaknesses:**

* Minor issues with plots (Figure 1a and Figure 2a)
* Method may not be transformative, but rather a useful addition to existing toolkit
