- Proposes a novel semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to incorporate domain knowledge.
- Introduces the γ-margin clusterability condition and an efficient clustering algorithm that succeeds for γ > 1.
- Algorithmic complexity: O(k log n + k^2 log k) same-cluster queries and O(kn log n) runtime.
- Proves a lower bound showing at least Ω(log k + log n) queries are necessary to make NP-hard clustering problems tractable.
- Demonstrates that limited query access can make clustering feasible under conditions that are NP-hard without queries.
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, significance, and rigorous theoretical analysis.
- Raises interesting open questions about extending the technique to other clustering methods and more complex query types.
- Main weakness: assumes oracle answers conform to center-based clustering with a margin, which may not hold in practice.
- Performance may degrade if the oracle’s answers are noisy or inconsistent.
- Lacks sufficient experimental evaluation to demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Overall recommendation: accept, given the paper’s significant and well-presented contribution.
