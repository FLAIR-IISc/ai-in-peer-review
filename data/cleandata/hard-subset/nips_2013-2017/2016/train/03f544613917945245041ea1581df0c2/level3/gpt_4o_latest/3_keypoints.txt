- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Reduces bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and achieves improved MSE, supported by theory and experiments.  
- Novel and general approach, applicable to multiple SG-MCMC algorithms (e.g., SGLD, SGHMC).  
- Theoretical analysis convincingly demonstrates bias and MSE improvements.  
- Empirical results (synthetic and large-scale matrix factorization) corroborate theoretical gains.  
- Simple to implement and compatible with parallel/distributed architectures.  
- Paper is well-written with detailed theory, experiments, and strong supplementary material.  
- Computational inefficiency: two-chain scheme can leave one chain idle; possible improvements (e.g., three chains) not explored.  
- Insufficient exploration of the effects of noise correlation between mini-batches in practice.  
- Experiments: matrix factorization should be run to convergence and compared to SGD to better illustrate Bayesian advantages.  
- Accessibility: adding pseudo-code or an algorithm box early would aid practitioners.  
- Question: Can the method be extended to more than two step sizes for further bias reduction?  
- Question: How does SGRRLD perform with other approximate MCMC schemes or in distributed mini-batch settings?  
- Suggestion: Investigate adaptive step sizes to further optimize performance.  
- Recommendation: Significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing efficiency and experimental scope.
