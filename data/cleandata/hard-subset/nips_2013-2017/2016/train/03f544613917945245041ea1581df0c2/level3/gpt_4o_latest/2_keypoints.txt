- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce gradient estimation bias via two parallel chains with different step sizes.  
- Theoretical claims: Proves asymptotically lower bias and MSE than SGLD, with convergence O(K^{-4/5}) vs O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Empirical summary: Synthetic experiments validate bias/MSE gains; real-world MovieLens experiments show only modest RMSE improvements.  

Strengths:
- Rigorous theory: Establishes asymptotic consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds with clear derivations.  
- Novelty: First to apply RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, linking numerical acceleration and stochastic sampling.  
- Empirical support: Synthetic experiments convincingly match theoretical predictions on bias and MSE reduction.  
- Practicality: Algorithm is simple to implement and parallelizable, suitable for large-scale use.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited real-world validation: MovieLens results are modest; broader dataset evaluation is needed to demonstrate practical value.  
- Incomplete baselines: No comparison to SGLD with smaller step sizes or multiple parallel chains as fair computational baselines.  
- Theorem assumptions: Conditions for main theorems (e.g., multi-modality of U) are not fully explored, limiting generality.  
- Chain correlation concern: Assumption of correlated Brownian motions to control variance may not hold in practice; robustness unclear.  

Suggestions:
- Run additional experiments on diverse real-world datasets to assess practical impact.  
- Include baselines like SGLD with halved step sizes and multiple parallel chains for fairer comparisons.  
- Clarify and discuss assumptions in Theorems 1–2, especially regarding multi-modal potentials and chain correlation.  
- Provide detailed analysis of computational overhead and scalability on larger datasets.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing real-world applicability and computational baseline concerns.
