- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias in SG-MCMC.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Well-written and clearly explains technical details, bridging MCMC and SDE literatures.  
- Empirical results show large improvements on toy problems and modest but consistent gains on large-scale tasks.  
- Synthetic experiments demonstrate effective bias and MSE reduction; real-data experiments show practical potential.  
- Method is straightforward and computationally convenient as an application of RRE to SGLD.  
- Likely useful in practice where bias reduction is important, though not transformative for the broader field.  
- Minor issue: Figure 1a is misleading—plots a Gaussian with empirical mean/variance rather than the empirical posterior; caption needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2a lacks discussion on why bias may not decrease with smaller step sizes, which is counter-intuitive.  
- Strength: novel application of RRE to SG-MCMC.  
- Strength: impressive empirical performance and solid theoretical backing.  
- Weakness: mostly an incremental/toolkit contribution rather than a major breakthrough.  
- Ratings: Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Quality 9/10.
