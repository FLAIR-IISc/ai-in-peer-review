- Proposes SGRRLD: run two parallel chains with different step sizes and correlated Gaussian noise using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and matrix factorization validate theory and show practical gains over standard SGLD.  
- Extends the method to SGHMC, demonstrating generality of the approach.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-presented, and situates itself within related work on higher-order integrators and RR extrapolation.  
- Novelty appears incremental given prior work on higher-order numerical integration for SG-MCMC.  
- Major practical concern: per-step communication between correlated chains could be a significant scalability bottleneck not discussed.  
- Experimental setup lacks detail on resource allocation: unclear whether two SGRRLD chains used the same compute as one SGLD chain and how resources were split.  
- Need to report wall-clock time implications, since gradient computation dominates and partial allocation per chain may alter speedups.  
- Missing strong baseline: comparison to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains to separate benefits of correlation vs. parallelization.  
- Minor editorial issue: duplicate phrase “Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)” on lines 25 and 70.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear presentation, positive empirical results, and demonstrated generality to SGHMC.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, practical communication costs, and insufficient experimental detail.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability concerns and strengthen the novelty argument.
