This paper proposes a simple yet effective method for reducing the asymptotic bias inherent in Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithms. The core idea is to apply Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a classical technique from numerical analysis, to the estimates produced by SG-MCMC. This is achieved by running two parallel chains with different step sizes (γ and γ/2), correlating their injected noise to control variance, and linearly combining their outputs. The authors provide a solid theoretical analysis demonstrating that this approach reduces the leading-order bias from O(γ) to O(γ^2) and consequently improves the Mean Squared Error (MSE) convergence rate. This work builds upon the extensive literature on SG-MCMC methods (e.g., Welling & Teh, 2011; Chen et al., 2014) by introducing a novel, general-purpose enhancement rather than a new base algorithm.

**Strengths:**
*   **Significance and Impact:** The method's primary strength is its generality. It can potentially be applied as a "wrapper" to improve a wide range of existing and future SG-MCMC algorithms, making the potential impact substantial.
*   **Originality:** While RR extrapolation is not new, its application in the context of SG-MCMC for bias reduction is a novel and valuable contribution to the machine learning community.
*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with theoretical claims of reduced bias and MSE being well-supported by proofs. The method itself is elegant and simple to understand.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and the core concepts are presented clearly.

**Weaknesses and Questions:**
*   **Practical Efficiency:** The proposed implementation, where the γ/2 chain runs for twice as many steps, leaves one computational unit idle for half the time. A discussion on a more practical parallel scheme—for instance, running one chain at step size γ and two separate chains at γ/2 for the same number of steps—is needed. How would this alternative affect the variance of the final estimator?
*   **Scope of Applicability:** The authors should clarify whether the technique is applicable to all SG-MCMC methods or only those whose bias admits a specific expansion (π_γ(f) = π(f) + Cγ + O(γ^2)). Expanding on its potential use for other approximate MCMC schemes, like subsampling Metropolis-Hastings, would also strengthen the paper.
*   **Experimental Evaluation:** The matrix factorization experiment is compelling but could be improved. The runs should be extended to convergence to better assess the final performance gain. Furthermore, including a standard SGD baseline would help contextualize the reported RMSE values and highlight the specific benefits of the Bayesian approach.
*   **Presentation:** For practitioners, the inclusion of an algorithm pseudocode box early in the paper would be immensely helpful for implementation.

In summary, this is a high-quality paper with a strong, novel idea. The weaknesses are largely related to practical implementation details and experimental thoroughness, which can be addressed in a revision.
