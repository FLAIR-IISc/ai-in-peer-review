- Proposal: SG-RR-MCMC (SGRRLD) applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining estimators 2·(small-step average) − (large-step average).

- Variance control via correlated Brownian increments with optimal coupling Z(γ)k = (Z(γ/2)2k−1 + Z(γ/2)2k)/√2.

- Theoretical results: CLT/bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), and MSE O(K−4/5) at optimal decaying steps γk ∝ k−1/5, improving over SGLD rates.

- Non-asymptotic bounds are provided under smoothness and Poisson-equation solvability assumptions.

- Important requirement: stochastic-gradient estimators for both chains must have the same law (e.g., same mini-batch size) to avoid reintroducing O(γ) bias.

- Experiments (linear Gaussian, SGHMC variant, large-scale matrix factorization) show reduced bias/MSE and improved RMSE at matched wall-clock time.

- Relation to prior work: extends RR extrapolation from exact-gradient SDEs to stochastic-gradient MCMC and complements prior higher-order integrator approaches.

- Practical advantages: conceptually simple, embarrassingly parallel (two independent chains), minimal code changes, and applicable in principle to SGHMC and other SG-MCMC schemes.

- Clarity issues: inconsistent treatment of step-size schedules (constant vs decreasing) and inconsistent notation (γ vs η) across main text and supplement.

- Algorithm mismatch: Algorithm 1 in the supplement does not match Equation (5); half-step updates, time-horizon synchronization, and Brownian coupling must be reconciled.

- Missing practical detail: need explicit construction of Brownian coupling and guidance on whether to share mini-batch indices between half-steps.

- Compute-budget fairness: SGRRLD doubles gradient evaluations per unit time; paper should report both wall-clock time and number of gradient evaluations for fair comparison.

- Limited baselines: experiments lack direct comparisons to higher-order overdamped integrators (e.g., schemes from Chen et al., 2015) for the SGLD/overdamped case.

- Suggested experiments: ablation on Brownian coupling (Σ = 0 vs Σ = I), coupling stochastic gradients (shared mini-batches), sensitivity to mini-batch size and step-size hyperparameters, and tests on non-convex/multimodal posteriors.

- Pros: simple and broadly applicable idea, strong theoretical guarantees (consistency, CLT, non-asymptotic bounds), clear optimal coupling and step-size guidance, and empirical gains including SGHMC extension.

- Cons: clarity and internal inconsistencies, limited experimental breadth vs higher-order integrators, unclear compute-budget reporting, and possibly restrictive smoothness/Poisson assumptions.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and well-motivated with solid analysis and promising empirical results, but needs fixes for inconsistencies and expanded comparisons.
