- Introduces SGRRLD, using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by combining two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce SG-MCMC bias.  
- Theoretical analysis shows reduced bias and improved mean squared error (MSE) compared to standard SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical validation provided on synthetic data and matrix factorization tasks demonstrating efficacy.  
- Problem motivation is strong: addressing bias in scalable Bayesian inference is important.  
- Method is simple and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.  
- Connection to higher-order integrators and potential generality to other SG-MCMC algorithms is insightful.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, aiding reproducibility and understanding.  
- Unclear whether the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2) is necessary for the technique—needs clarification.  
- Applicability to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., Distributed SGLD) should be explored to broaden impact.  
- Computational efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long is inefficient; a three-chain equal-step scheme might be better.  
- Need to address the interaction between injected noise and mini-batch correlation and whether using the same mini-batch is optimal.  
- Matrix factorization experiment should be run to full convergence and include a standard SGD baseline for better comparison and to showcase Bayesian benefits.  
- Include an algorithm pseudocode box early in the paper to improve practitioner accessibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel use of Richardson-Romberg for SG-MCMC, strong theoretical guarantees, clear presentation, and promising experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved generality/applicability questions, computational-efficiency issues, and less robust experimental validation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to novelty, theoretical soundness, and potential impact.
