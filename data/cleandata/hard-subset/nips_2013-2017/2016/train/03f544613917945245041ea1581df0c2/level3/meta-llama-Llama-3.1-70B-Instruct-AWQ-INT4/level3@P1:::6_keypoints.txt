- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD via two parallel correlated-step-size chains to reduce bias, with theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation with both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence analysis and clear RR explanation.
- Strength: Well-situated within prior work, giving a comprehensive review of related SG-MCMC and integrator literature.
- Strength: Effective experimental evaluation (synthetic and real-data matrix factorization) showing practical gains over standard SGLD.
- Weakness: Novelty may be incremental given prior analyses of numerical integrators and prior use of RR for invariant distributions.
- Weakness: Practical applicability concerns because correlated per-step chains require communication, but communication cost is not analyzed.
- Weakness: Experimental setup lacks clarity on compute/resource allocation, whether SGLD used equivalent resources, and wall-clock implications of splitting resources.
- Suggestion: Compare to two independent (embarrassingly parallel) SGLD chains as an additional baseline.
- Suggestion: Discuss and analyze the communication cost and practical overhead of the correlated-chain implementation.
- Suggestion: Clarify experimental setup details, including resource splitting, compute per chain, and wall-clock timing.
- Originality: Novel combination of RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC, but potentially incremental relative to prior work.
- Significance: Potential to improve SG-MCMC accuracy; demonstrated benefits in a real-data application.
- Clarity: Well-written and clear overall, with minor issues (e.g., duplicated ULA definition) to fix.
- Quality: High-quality theoretical and experimental work, though overall impact may be tempered by incremental novelty and practicality concerns.
