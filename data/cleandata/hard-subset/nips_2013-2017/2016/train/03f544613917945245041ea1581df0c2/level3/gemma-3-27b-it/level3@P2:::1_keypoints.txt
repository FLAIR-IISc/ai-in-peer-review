- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes.  
- Strong theoretical analysis: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on non-asymptotic bias and MSE.  
- Proposes variance control via a correlation scheme for Brownian increments.  
- Empirical results (synthetic and matrix factorization) show faster convergence than SGLD and accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators.  
- Bridges MCMC and SDE literatures, offering a practical improvement for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation accessible to researchers in MCMC and stochastic optimization.  
- Computationally efficient due to the parallelizability of the two chains.  
- Figure 1(a) is potentially misleading—plots a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance rather than the empirical posterior; needs caption or adjustment.  
- Figure 2(a) lacks sufficient explanation for the counterintuitive non-decreasing bias with smaller step sizes.  
- Limited exploration of step-size schedule sensitivity beyond constant step sizes.  
- Real-world performance gains are consistent but modest; more detailed computational cost and scalability analysis versus other SG-MCMC methods is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, empirical improvements, practicality, clarity, and novel application of Richardson-Romberg.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest real-world gains, minor figure clarifications required, and limited step-size exploration.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with minor weaknesses addressable in revision; recommend acceptance.
