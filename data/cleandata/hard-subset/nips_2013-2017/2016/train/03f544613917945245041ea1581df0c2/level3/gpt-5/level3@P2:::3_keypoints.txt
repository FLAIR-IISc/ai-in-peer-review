- Proposes Richardson–Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by coupling γ and γ/2 chains to cancel first-order weak error and produce SGRRLD.  
- Theoretical contributions: CLT for RR estimator showing minimal variance with perfectly correlated Brownian increments.  
- Provides non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds: bias O(γ2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ4 + 1/(Kγ)), and optimal K-rates (bias O(K−2/3), MSE O(K−4/5)).  
- Derives optimal step-size schedule γk ∝ k−1/5 from CLT, improving over first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Empirical validation on linear-Gaussian model, SGHMC instantiation, and MovieLens matrix factorization showing reduced bias/MSE and competitive wall-clock efficiency via parallelism.  
- Builds on classical RR for SDEs and recent SG-MCMC theory; RR application to SG-MCMC appears novel.  
- Theory is technically sound, using weak error expansions and Poisson equation methods, and justifies noise correlation.  
- Non-asymptotic bounds align with the CLT and are informative for finite-time behavior.  
- Suggests achieving higher accuracy with only first-order updates, matching higher-order integrators in practice.  
- Paper is clear and well organized; main idea is simple and assumptions are discussed (details in supplement).  
- Recommends moving algorithm pseudocode (and noise coupling) into the main text for practitioner use.  
- Strengths: simple implementation, generality beyond SGLD (e.g., SGHMC), solid theoretical results, and practical guidance on step sizes and noise coupling.  
- Weaknesses: unclear scope—need clarification which SG-MCMC variants satisfy required weak error expansions and common counterexamples.  
- Question on minibatch correlation: need ablation comparing independent vs shared minibatches and impact on variance.  
- Computational efficiency concern: on a single core SGRRLD doubles computation; need discussion for limited parallel resources and batched scheduling effects.  
- Extensions: request outline and pitfalls for recursive/multilevel RR and practical coupling across more than two levels.  
- Generalization: ask whether approach applies to subsampling MH or distributed SGLD and what extra conditions are required.  
- Experimental suggestions: broader benchmarks (e.g., logistic regression, non-Gaussian posteriors), SGD baseline for RMSE context, sensitivity to step/minibatch sizes, and training curves to convergence.  
- Manuscript improvements: add concise pseudocode in main text, empirical ablation on noise/minibatch correlations, clarify regularity assumptions, expand guidance for other SG-MCMC variants, and provide computational cost analysis under different parallelism budgets.  
- Acceptance arguments: simple, general, strong theory bridging numerical analysis and scalable Bayesian sampling with practical gains; against: empirical breadth and practical guidance on minibatch correlation and multilevel extensions are limited, and assumptions may be restrictive.
