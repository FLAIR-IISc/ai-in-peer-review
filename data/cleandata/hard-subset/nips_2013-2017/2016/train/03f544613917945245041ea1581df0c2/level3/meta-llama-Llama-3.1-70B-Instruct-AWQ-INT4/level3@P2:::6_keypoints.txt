- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel and correlated Gaussian noise.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: both asymptotic and non-asymptotic convergence results.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness through synthetic and real-data experiments showing gains over standard SGLD.  
- Situates work within SG-MCMC and numerical analysis literature, citing prior RR extrapolation and integrator analyses.  
- Technique and presentation are strong; paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported.  
- Well-written and organized; includes enough implementation detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality is moderate: novel combination/application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC but possibly incremental given prior work.  
- Significant potential impact on Bayesian inference accuracy and efficiency for SG-MCMC methods.  
- Main novelty concern: incremental relative to prior numerical integrator analyses and RR use for invariant distributions.  
- Practicality concerns: requirement for correlated Gaussian noise and communication cost of two parallel chains may limit applicability.  
- Experimental clarity concerns: unclear whether SGLD used equivalent compute/resources, how resources are split, and effects on wall-clock time.  
- Minor issues: duplicated phrase "Unadjusted Langevin Algorithm (ULA)" and need for comparison to two independent parallel SGLD chains as a baseline.
