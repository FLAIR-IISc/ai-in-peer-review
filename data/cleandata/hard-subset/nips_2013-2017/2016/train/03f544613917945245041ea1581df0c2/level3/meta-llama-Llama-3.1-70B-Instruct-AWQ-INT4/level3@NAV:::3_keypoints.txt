- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Provides formal theory: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Algorithm is simple and easy to implement with minimal changes to base SG-MCMC methods.  
- Experimental results show reduced bias and improved convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough problem motivation.  
- Unclear how the method generalizes or can be applied to other SG-MCMC variants beyond SGLD.  
- Experiments are limited to a few specific examples, leaving general performance uncertain.  
- Lacks detailed empirical comparison with other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods.  
- Overall, the paper is a significant, potentially impactful contribution to scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, effective empirically, and clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalization explanation, narrow experimental validation, and missing strong baselines.  
- Recommended improvements: clarify applicability to other SG-MCMC methods, expand experimental scope, and include detailed comparisons with strong baselines.
