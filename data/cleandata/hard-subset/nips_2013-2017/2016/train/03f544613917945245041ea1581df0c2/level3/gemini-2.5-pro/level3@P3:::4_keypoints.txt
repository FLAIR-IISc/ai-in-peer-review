- Proposes Richardson-Romberg extrapolation for SG-MCMC by running two chains with step sizes γ_k and γ_k/2 and combining outputs (SGRRLD) to cancel leading Euler-Maruyama bias.  
- Provides theoretical results: a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds claiming improved MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD O(K⁻²/³).  
- Novel application of RR to stochastic-gradient MCMC and demonstrated generality (e.g., extension to SGHMC).  
- Method is simple, practical, easy to implement, and requires minimal changes to existing SGLD code.  
- Major concern: derivation of optimal convergence rates (e.g., γ ∝ K⁻¹/⁵ → MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵)) is presented too tersely and needs a more careful, rigorous explanation.  
- Major concern: experimental comparisons may be unfair—SGRRLD’s computational cost is higher (≈1.5×) and wall-clock comparisons conflate hardware parallelism with algorithmic efficiency.  
- Major concern: contradiction in Figure 3 where empirical optimal step-size decay (α=0.2) conflicts with theoretical claim (α=1/3), and the “optimal” curves show high fluctuation.  
- Major concern: unexplained behavior in Figure 2—SGRRLD shows a U-shaped bias/MSE attributed to 1/(Kγ), yet SGLD (which also has 1/(Kγ)) is monotonic in the plot, requiring clarification.  
- Minor: inconsistent notation (ϑ vs θ) and non-bolded vectors reduce clarity.  
- Minor: acronyms/quantities (e.g., “RR”, Γ_K) are used before definition.  
- Minor: theorem assumptions are vague—proof sketch mentions C⁹ smoothness, so precise smoothness and other conditions should be stated in main theorems.  
- Minor: possible figure labeling/caption errors (e.g., “Bias vs. time vs. dimension”) need checking.  
- Recommendation: promising and significant idea but not ready for publication until major theoretical clarifications and experimental fairness/consistency issues are addressed; reviewer may reconsider after revisions.
