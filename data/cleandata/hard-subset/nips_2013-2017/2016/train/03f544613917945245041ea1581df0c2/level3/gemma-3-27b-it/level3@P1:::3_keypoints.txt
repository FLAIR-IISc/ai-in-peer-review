- Introduces SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Core idea: run two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine estimates to achieve O(γ^2) bias.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: consistency, central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to stochastic-gradient MCMC methods.  
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC literature and connects to Talay & Tubaro (1990) extrapolation theory.  
- Method is conceptually simple and requires minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC implementations.  
- Theoretical guarantees are strong and the presentation is clear with careful notation.  
- Demonstrates generality by applying the approach to both SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Claim of broad applicability depends on expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2); conditions for this expansion are not fully specified.  
- Should discuss applicability to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH, distributed SGLD).  
- Computational efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long may cause idle time and inefficiency.  
- Suggests exploring alternatives (e.g., three-chain schemes or more than two step sizes) and analyzing variance implications.  
- Needs clarification on effect of correlating mini-batches versus using identical mini-batches for variance control.  
- Experiments are convincing but would be stronger with an SGD baseline and runs to full convergence rather than fixed wall-clock time.  
- Practical usability would improve with a clear pseudocode box early in the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, generality, clear presentation, and supportive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear scope of applicability, potential computational inefficiency, and incomplete experimental setup.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor revisions requested; recommend acceptance.
