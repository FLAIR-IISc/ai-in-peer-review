- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Algorithm description is clear and easy to implement.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, CLT, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Claims improved convergence rates over SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotic regimes.  
- Synthetic linear-Gaussian experiments show clear bias-reduction advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD.  
- MovieLens (real-data) experiment shows only slight RMSE improvement; real-data evidence is weak and arguable.  
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is new, but using multiple chains is not a novel idea by itself.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on conditions for Theorems 1 and 2 (e.g., assumptions on U and multimodality).  
- Reviewer questions proof details and notes they have not been fully checked.  
- Raises concern about gradient-noise correlation: if gradient noises are uncorrelated, chains may decorrelate—how does extrapolation improve error order?  
- Requests investigation and reporting of empirical correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.  
- Computation-time claim is contested: SGLD can also be parallelized and run multiple chains, so a fair comparison is needed.  
- Recommends experiments comparing SGRRLD to multi-chain SGLD and to SGLD with step size gamma/2 to isolate benefits.  
- Calls for more and stronger real-data experiments to substantiate practical benefits.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, clear algorithm, thorough theoretical analysis, but practical behavior and benefits need more validation.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm reducing bias, solid theoretical support, clear implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unchecked proofs, insufficient understanding of behavior, and weak real-data evidence.
