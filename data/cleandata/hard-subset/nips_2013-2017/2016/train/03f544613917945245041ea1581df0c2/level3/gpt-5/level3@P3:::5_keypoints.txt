- Proposes SG-RR-MCMC (SGRRLD) using Richardson–Romberg extrapolation: run two SGLD chains at γ and γ/2, combine ergodic averages to reduce discretization bias.
- Correlates Gaussian increments across levels to control variance and enables embarrassingly parallel implementation with no inter-process communication.
- Provides theoretical results: consistency, a CLT with optimal variance under perfect correlation, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing improved rates (MSE O(γ4 + 1/(Kγ)) → optimal O(K−4/5)).
- Empirical results on synthetic Gaussians and a large-scale matrix factorization task align with theory and show improvements over SGLD and competitiveness with higher-order SGHMC.
- Novelty lies in adapting RR extrapolation and Brownian coupling to the stochastic-gradient MCMC setting and proving asymptotic and finite-time guarantees.
- Clear, simple implementation idea that bridges numerical analysis and scalable Bayesian sampling.
- Inconsistencies in step-size statements (constant vs decreasing) across the paper; needs consolidated assumptions next to each result.
- Algorithm 1 pseudocode conflicts with Equation (5)/(6): fine chain stepping, noise scaling, and time-weighted estimator must be corrected to match equations and Brownian coupling.
- Unclear whether mini-batches are coupled across levels; recommend discussion and ablation on mini-batch coupling to avoid reintroducing first-order bias.
- Claims of efficiency would be strengthened by reporting constants or empirical slopes for CLT/MSE scaling and systematic wall-clock comparisons to state-of-the-art higher-order SG-MCMC.
- Missing direct comparisons of SGRRLD to higher-order SGLD variants (e.g., symmetric splittings/preconditioned methods); such comparisons would substantiate the claim that RR matches higher-order accuracy.
- Analysis relies on strong regularity assumptions (e.g., g ∈ C9 and moment conditions); discuss sensitivity to weaker regularity and practical impact.
- Paper is technically careful and largely sound; key expressions (σR2, μR) and important constants should be moved from supplement into main text for readability.
- Generally well-written and organized, but highlight time-weighting in the algorithm and explicitly state step-size schedules per theorem and experiment.
- Specific suggested fixes: correct Algorithm 1, clarify mini-batch coupling, unify step-size discussions, report empirical MSE slopes with CIs, add high-order SGLD comparisons, and discuss constant factors’ dependence on gradient noise and batch size.
- Pros: simple, general, parallelizable method with solid theory and supporting experiments; extensible to other SG-MCMC methods.
- Cons: clarity inconsistencies, limited comparisons to higher-order SGLD and variance-reduced methods, strong technical assumptions, and moderate conceptual novelty.
- Recommendation: lean accept, pending fixes to clarity and additional comparisons to fully solidify the contribution.
