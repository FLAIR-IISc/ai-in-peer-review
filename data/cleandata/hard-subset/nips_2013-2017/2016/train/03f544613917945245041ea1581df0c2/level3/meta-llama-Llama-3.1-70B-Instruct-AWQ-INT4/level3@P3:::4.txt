The authors propose a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed algorithm applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation on SGLD by considering two SGLD chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error (MSE) can be bounded.

The authors' approach is based on a numerical sequence acceleration method, which is a novel combination of familiar techniques. The use of RR extrapolation in SG-MCMC algorithms has not been explored before, and the authors' work contributes to the field by providing a new perspective on how to improve the performance of SG-MCMC methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and the underlying theory. The experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD in reducing bias and improving MSE compared to SGLD.

However, there are some concerns regarding the practicality of the proposed method. The authors mention that the proposed method requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel, which may increase the computational cost. Indeed, the authors report that the proposed method costs around 3 times more than SGLD on a single core, which may be a significant drawback in practice.
