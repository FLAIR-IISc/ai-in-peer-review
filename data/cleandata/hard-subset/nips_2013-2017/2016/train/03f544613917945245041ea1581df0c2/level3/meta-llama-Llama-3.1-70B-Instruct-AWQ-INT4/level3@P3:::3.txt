This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC methods. The algorithm applies the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, a numerical sequence acceleration method, to the Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) algorithm. The RR extrapolation involves running two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes, which are then combined to reduce the bias of the estimator.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bounds for the bias and mean squared error (MSE). The results show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than existing SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators in both finite-time and asymptotically.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The presentation is clear, and the authors provide sufficient information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

The proposed algorithm is novel and has the potential to be highly impactful in the field of Bayesian machine learning. The use of RR extrapolation is a new contribution to the SG-MCMC community, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in reducing the bias of the estimator.

The experiments conducted by the authors are comprehensive and demonstrate the performance of SGRRLD on both synthetic and real data. The results show that SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in terms of bias and MSE, and that it can achieve the same accuracy as SGLD in shorter computation time.

One potential limitation of the paper is that the authors do not provide a clear answer to the question of whether the proposed algorithm can be applied to all SG-MCMC methods or only those that satisfy certain conditions. Additionally, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, which may be a concern for large-scale applications.
