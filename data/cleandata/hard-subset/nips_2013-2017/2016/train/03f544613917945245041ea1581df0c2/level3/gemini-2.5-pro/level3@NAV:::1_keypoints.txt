- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes and combining outputs to reduce discretization bias.  
- Connects SDE approximation techniques with scalable Bayesian inference and offers an alternative to higher-order integrators.  
- Paper is high quality, clear, well-organized, and technically sound.  
- Theoretical contributions include formal convergence results, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic error bounds specifying when SGRRLD improves over SGLD.  
- Originality: first known application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation in the SG-MCMC context.  
- Practical utility: parallelizability makes the method computationally convenient.  
- Empirical results: large gains on synthetic data and modest but consistent improvements on large-scale matrix factorization tasks.  
- Main weakness: contribution may be viewed as incremental rather than transformative; modest gains on large-scale experiments may limit immediate practical impact.  
- Minor revision 1: clarify Figure 1a caption—it appears to plot a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance, not the empirical posterior density.  
- Minor revision 2: explicitly explain the counter-intuitive bias increase for very small step sizes in Figure 2a as an O(1/(Kγ)) artifact when K is fixed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
