- Proposal: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGRRLD) by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining estimates to cancel leading bias.  
- Theoretical results: rigorous analysis shows asymptotic consistency, a CLT, bias O(γ²) and MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(γ) bias and O(K⁻²/³) MSE.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on synthetic data and large-scale matrix factorization (MovieLens) support theory and show similar/better accuracy in about half the wall-clock time.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written and organized; a pseudocode box for SGRRLD would further aid practitioners.  
- Originality: novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, achieving higher-order accuracy using first-order methods.  
- Significance: simple to implement, broadly applicable to many SG-MCMC algorithms, and likely to have wide impact in scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Strengths: theoretical rigor, implementation simplicity, broad applicability, and strong experimental backing.  
- Weakness: clarify whether the technique applies to all SG-MCMC methods or only those with π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²).  
- Weakness: discuss applicability to other approximate MCMC schemes (e.g., subsampling MH) and distributed implementations.  
- Weakness: computational-efficiency concern—running one chain twice as long may waste compute; consider a 3-chain approach to avoid idle time.  
- Weakness: clarify required correlation between injected noise and the effect/optimality of correlating minibatches.  
- Weakness: include an SGD baseline in matrix factorization experiments to contextualize Bayesian benefits and final errors.  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance—novel, theoretically sound, practical—subject to minor clarifications on applicability and efficiency.
