This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) by applying Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive bounds for its non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared-error, and demonstrate that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically.

The main idea of SGRRLD is to run two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and then extrapolate the two estimators to construct a new one. The authors show that this approach can reduce the bias of SGLD while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. They also provide a pseudo-code of SGRRLD and demonstrate its performance on synthetic and real data experiments.

The paper is well-written, and the algorithm description is clear and easy to implement. The synthetic Linear Gaussian experiments show a clear bias-reduction advantage of SGRRLD over SGLD. However, the real-data results, specifically the MovieLens experiment, show only a slight RMSE improvement, and the evidence is not strong enough to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.

The reviewer has not checked the proof details, but the application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and the provision of convergence proofs are the main contributions of this paper. However, there are some concerns and questions that need to be addressed. For example, what conditions on U are required for Theorems 1 and 2? Can U be multimodal? The reviewer also questions the effect of gradient-noise correlation: if gradient noises are uncorrelated, chains may decorrelate quickly—how does combining chains reduce error order? Additionally, the reviewer asks whether the authors observed correlation between the two chains in real-data runs.

Another concern is about the computation-time claim: SGRRLD's parallelization cost is comparable to SGLD, but SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains—need comparison to multi-chain SGLD. The reviewer suggests comparing SGRRLD to SGLD run with two parallel chains (and to SGLD with step size gamma/2) to isolate benefits. Furthermore, the reviewer recommends more and stronger real-data experiments to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the reviewer questions the significance of the results, especially in the context of real-data experiments. The paper is well-written, and the organization is clear, but the reviewer suggests improving the presentation of the results, especially in the real-data experiments.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel algorithm that combines RR extrapolation with SGLD, which is a new contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. However, the reviewer notes that RR extrapolation is known for SDEs, and the main contribution is applying it to SG-MCMC and providing convergence proofs.

In terms of significance, the paper provides a new algorithm that can potentially improve the performance of SG-MCMC methods. However, the reviewer questions the practical benefit of SGRRLD, especially in the context of real-data experiments. More and stronger real-data experiments are needed to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm that combines RR extrapolation with SGLD, which is a new contribution to the field of SG-MCMC.
* The paper provides formal theoretical analysis and shows that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem.
* The paper demonstrates the performance of SGRRLD on synthetic and real data experiments.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The real-data results, specifically the MovieLens experiment, show only a slight RMSE improvement, and the evidence is not strong enough to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.
* The computation-time claim is not well-supported, and the reviewer suggests comparing SGRRLD to SGLD run with two parallel chains (and to SGLD with step size gamma/2) to isolate benefits.
* More and stronger real-data experiments are needed to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.

Recommendation: The reviewer recommends revising the paper to address the concerns and questions raised above, especially in terms of providing more and stronger real-data experiments to substantiate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.
