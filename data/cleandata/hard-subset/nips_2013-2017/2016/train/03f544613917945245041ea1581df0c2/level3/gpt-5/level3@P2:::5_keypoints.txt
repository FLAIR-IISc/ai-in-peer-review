- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (step-sizes γ and γ/2) with correlated Gaussian increments to reduce bias and variance.  
- Theoretically proves consistency, a CLT with minimized asymptotic variance under optimal coupling, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing improved rates over SGLD.  
- Connects to prior SG-MCMC and RR/MLMC SDE literature, showing RR correction matches higher-order integrator rates using only first-order schemes.  

Strengths:
- Simple, low-overhead implementation: two parallel SGLD chains with a specific noise coupling.  
- Provable improvements: asymptotic bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) and optimal rate O(K−4/5) versus SGLD’s O(K−2/3).  
- Generic approach applicable to multiple SG-MCMC methods under regularity/ergodicity assumptions.  
- Empirical gains shown on synthetic Gaussian tasks and large-scale matrix factorization with matched wall-clock time using parallelism.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Theory depends on strong smoothness/regularity assumptions (e.g., g ∈ C^9); move/clarify assumptions in main text and discuss plausibility and failure cases.  
- Clarify practical coupling/parallelization: explain how to implement optimal correlated noise without run-time communication and whether minibatch indices must be coupled; include ablations for noise coupling and minibatch matching.  
- Missing experimental comparison to higher-order SGLD integrators; add head-to-head baselines with equal compute.  
- Provide practical guidance on choosing γ and decay exponent α and include sensitivity analyses or recommended defaults.  
- Expand experiments to non-convex posteriors (e.g., Bayesian neural nets or logistic regression) to test robustness.  
- Fix clarity/inconsistency issues: unify constant vs. decreasing step-size exposition and separate regimes in theorems.  
- Correct Algorithm 1 pseudocode so it matches Eq. (5) and Eq. (7), including indices, Brownian coupling, and minibatch handling.  
- Improve notation: distinguish γ (constant) from γk (sequence) and define cumulative notation (Γ(n)K) once.  

Quality:
- Technically sound and grounded in RR/MLMC SDE analysis; proofs appropriate but assumptions should be surfaced and discussed more explicitly.  

Clarity:

Originality:

Arguments against acceptance:
