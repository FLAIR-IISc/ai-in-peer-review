- Proposes a novel semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to a domain expert.  
- Introduces the γ-margin clusterability condition and uses it to characterize when queries make clustering tractable.  
- Shows that limited query access can convert an NP-hard clustering problem into a feasible one under γ > 1.  
- Presents a probabilistic polynomial-time clustering algorithm that succeeds when the input satisfies the γ-margin condition.  
- Algorithm query complexity: O(k log n + k^2 log k) same-cluster queries.  
- Algorithm time complexity: O(k n log n).  
- Proves a lower bound that at least Ω(log k + log n) queries are necessary to overcome NP-hardness for k-means.  
- Demonstrates the algorithm recovers the target clustering with high probability and provides explicit time and query bounds.  
- Strengths: novel approach, thorough theoretical analysis, clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Experimental results support the effectiveness of the proposed method.  
- Consistency issues: unclear statement about step size and a discrepancy between Algorithm 1 and Equation (5).  
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of practical applications and potential limitations of the framework.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality; technically sound and well-supported claims.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing inconsistencies and expanding discussion of applications and limitations.
