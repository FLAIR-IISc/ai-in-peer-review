- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD for scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Aims to reduce bias inherent in SG-MCMC while maintaining reasonable variance for big-data MCMC.  
- Implements two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combines them via RRE.  
- Claims improved convergence rates matching higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Bridges MCMC and SDE literatures, offering cross-disciplinary insights.  
- Theoretical results: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and improved bias/MSE rates.  
- Provides non-asymptotic bounds showing SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in finite-time and asymptotic regimes.  
- Empirical validation: strong gains on synthetic/tiny examples and consistent but modest improvements on large-scale tasks (e.g., matrix factorization).  
- Exploits parallelizability for enhanced computational efficiency.  
- Strengths: clear writing, thorough theoretical grounding, practical relevance, and likely practitioner adoption.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1a interpretation unclear and bias behavior in Figure 2a requires more discussion.  
- Limitation: practical gains on large-scale applications are incremental and not transformative.  
- Overall conclusion: meaningful, well-grounded contribution suitable for acceptance despite not being groundbreaking.
