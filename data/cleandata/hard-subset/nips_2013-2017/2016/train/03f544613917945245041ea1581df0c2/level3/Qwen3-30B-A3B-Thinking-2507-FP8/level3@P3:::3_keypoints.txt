- Introduces SGRRLD: run two correlated SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine them via Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias to O(γ²).

- Theoretical results: proves asymptotic bias O(γ²), MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵), and derives a CLT and non-asymptotic bounds, improving over SGLD's bias O(γ) and MSE O(K⁻²/³).

- Experiments on synthetic and matrix factorization tasks support theoretical claims.

- Applicability concern: method requires π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²), which may not hold for all SG-MCMC variants and needs clarification.

- Clarity: paper is very well written and motivates connections between numerical analysis and MCMC; adding an early pseudocode box would aid practitioners.

- Originality: novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, despite roots in numerical analysis.

- Significance: simple to implement, parallelizable, minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC code, and likely widely adoptable.

- Key strength: proven theoretical improvement in convergence rates with clear experimental validation.

- Efficiency concern: running one chain twice as long may waste computation; alternatives (e.g., three-chain schemes) are suggested but unanalyzed.

- Extension concern: recursive RR extrapolation to more than two step sizes is mentioned but no clear extension path is provided.

- Noise correlation concern: optimal correlation for minibatch noise set as Σ=I, but paper does not clarify if using the same minibatch is equivalent or optimal.

- Experimental robustness concern: matrix factorization experiments should include runs to steady state and an SGD baseline to show Bayesian benefit.

- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to clarify applicability, noise-correlation choices, and computational-efficiency tradeoffs.

- Acceptance pros/cons: pros—novelty, theoretical gains, simplicity, strong experiments; cons—limited discussion of applicability conditions, missing pseudocode, some computational-efficiency questions.
