This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, referred to as Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), that aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The proposed approach is based on a numerical sequence acceleration method, namely the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation, which requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent, satisfies a central limit theorem, and its non-asymptotic bias and the mean squared-error can be bounded.

The paper relates to previous work on SG-MCMC, particularly Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD), and extends it by applying RR extrapolation to reduce the bias. The authors also provide a comparison with other SG-MCMC methods, such as Stochastic Gradient Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (SGHMC), and show that SGRRLD can achieve the theoretical accuracy of methods that are based on higher-order integrators.

The strengths of the paper include its clear and well-organized presentation, as well as its thorough theoretical analysis. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the SGRRLD algorithm and its properties, and support their findings using both synthetic and real data experiments. The empirical results are satisfactory and show that the idea is effective in reducing the bias of SG-MCMC.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. One of the main limitations is that the efficiency of SGRRLD could be further validated by proving an improved convergence rate. While the authors provide some theoretical analysis, they do not provide a rigorous proof of the convergence rate. Additionally, similar improved rates could be obtained using high-order integrators, but no experimental comparison was provided. Furthermore, there is an inconsistent statement about the step size in the paper, and Algorithm 1 in the supplement is inconsistent with Equation (5).

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel combination of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation and SGLD, which is a significant contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. However, the idea of using RR extrapolation to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC is not entirely new, and similar approaches have been proposed in the literature.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to improve the accuracy of SG-MCMC methods, which are widely used in Bayesian machine learning. The proposed approach is also well-suited for parallel and distributed architectures, which makes it a promising solution for large-scale applications.

Overall, I would rate the quality of the paper as good, but not excellent. The paper provides a clear and well-organized presentation, and the theoretical analysis is thorough. However, the lack of a rigorous proof of the convergence rate and the inconsistent statements about the step size are notable weaknesses. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other SG-MCMC methods and a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach.
