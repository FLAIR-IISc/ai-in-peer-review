**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD). SGRRLD aims to reduce the bias of Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) by applying the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation method. The algorithm runs two SGLD chains in parallel with different step sizes and uses the RR extrapolation to combine the outputs. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis and show that SGRRLD is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem. They also derive bounds for its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error, and show that SGRRLD attains higher rates of convergence than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel application of RR extrapolation**: The paper applies the RR extrapolation method to SG-MCMC, which is a new contribution to the field.
2. **Formal theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, including convergence proofs and bounds for its bias and mean squared error.
3. **Improved convergence rates**: SGRRLD is shown to have faster convergence rates than SGLD in both finite-time and asymptotically.
4. **Clear algorithm description**: The algorithm is described in a clear and easy-to-implement manner.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited real-data experiments**: The paper only presents results on a single real-data experiment (MovieLens), which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the practical benefit of SGRRLD.
2. **Unclear conditions on U**: The reviewer asks what conditions on U are required for Theorems 1 and 2 to hold, and whether U can be multimodal.
3. **Effect of gradient-noise correlation**: The reviewer questions how combining chains reduces error order when gradient noises are uncorrelated.
4. **Computation-time claim**: The paper claims that SGRRLD has a similar computation time to SGLD, but this may not be accurate, as SGLD can also run multiple parallel chains.

**Originality**

The paper applies the RR extrapolation method to SG-MCMC, which is a new contribution to the field. While RR extrapolation is known for SDEs, its application to SG-MCMC is novel.
