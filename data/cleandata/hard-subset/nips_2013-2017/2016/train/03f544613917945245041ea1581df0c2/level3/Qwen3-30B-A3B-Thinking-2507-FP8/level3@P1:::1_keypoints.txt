- Proposes applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) to SG-MCMC, introducing SGRRLD that runs two SGLD chains with different step sizes and combines them to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Bridges numerical analysis of SDEs with MCMC methodology by extending RRE to stochastic gradient settings previously unexplored.
- Theoretical results: SGRRLD achieves bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) versus SGLD’s O(γ) and O(γ²).
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD compared to SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).
- Provides a central limit theorem and consistency proofs under mild conditions, strengthening theoretical soundness.
- Method attains first-order integrator accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators without their computational complexity.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically rigorous yet accessible.
- Approach is generalizable to other SG-MCMC algorithms satisfying technical conditions, not limited to SGLD.
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussians show dramatic bias reduction in variance estimation (~10⁻² to ~10⁻⁴).
- Empirical results confirm theoretical MSE improvements across dimensions and step sizes and validate optimal step-size schedules.
- Practical application: matrix factorization on MovieLens shows comparable accuracy to SGLD in half the wall-clock time due to parallelism.
- Extension to SGHMC (SGRRHMC) demonstrates generality across SG-MCMC methods.
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, clear presentation, practical relevance, easy implementation, and parallel-friendly.
- Minor issues to address: Figure 1(a) misleadingly shows a Gaussian fit instead of the empirical posterior and needs clarification.
- Minor issue: Figure 2(a) shows bias increase for small γ; authors should discuss the 1/(Kγ) term dominating bias when K is fixed.
- Significance: meaningful contribution to scalable Bayesian inference, likely to be adopted by practitioners needing computationally efficient, less-biased SG-MCMC.
- Technically sound with rigorous proofs and appropriate acknowledgment of related deterministic RRE work; novelty is in the SG-MCMC context.
- Overall recommendation: strong NIPS submission that meets quality criteria and is recommended for acceptance.
