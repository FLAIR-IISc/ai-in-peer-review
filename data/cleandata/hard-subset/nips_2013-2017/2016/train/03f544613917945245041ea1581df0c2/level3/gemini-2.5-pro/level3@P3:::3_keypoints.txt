- Proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD): run two correlated-noise SG-MCMC chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine them to cancel O(γ) bias, leaving O(γ²) bias.  
- Novel application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation from numerical analysis to SG-MCMC.  
- Simple and practical: minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC code—run algorithm twice and linearly combine outputs.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds; improves MSE rate from O(K⁻²/³) to O(K⁻⁴/⁵), matching higher-order integrators.  
- Broadly applicable in principle to SG-MCMC methods whose bias admits a Taylor-like expansion in step size.  
- Clear and well-written presentation with good literature context.  
- Question on applicability: need clarification which SG-MCMC and approximate-MCMC schemes satisfy the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²).  
- Computational-efficiency concern: running γ/2 chain twice as often leaves the γ chain idle; suggest alternate parallelization (e.g., one γ and two γ/2 chains) and ask how that affects variance.  
- Unclear role of minibatch correlation: theory suggests gradient subsample correlation may not affect asymptotic variance—request practical guidance on whether to share minibatches across chains.  
- Experimental suggestions: extend matrix factorization runs to full convergence and include a non-Bayesian baseline (e.g., SGD) to contextualize RMSE and justify Bayesian complexity.  
- Ask about recursive RR extrapolation: feasibility, trade-offs, and increased variance/control complexity when using more step sizes (γ, γ/2, γ/4, ...).  
- Presentation suggestion: include a clear pseudocode algorithm with noise-correlation details in the main paper for practitioner use.
