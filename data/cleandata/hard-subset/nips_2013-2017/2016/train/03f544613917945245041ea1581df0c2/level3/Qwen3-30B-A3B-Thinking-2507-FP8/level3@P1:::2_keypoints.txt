- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Key insight: run two SGLD chains with different step sizes and correlated Brownian motions to cancel leading bias terms.  
- Asymptotic improvements: bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) for SGRRLD vs SGLD's O(γ) and O(γ²), yielding MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs O(K⁻²/³).  
- Strong theoretical contributions: Theorems 1–2 give CLT, asymptotic consistency, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Technical conditions are carefully addressed but need clarification about applicability to multimodal target distributions.  
- Algorithm description and pseudocode are clear and implementable.  
- Synthetic experiments: substantial bias reduction and MSE improvements consistent with theory (e.g., posterior variance error drop from ~1e-2 to ~1e-4).  
- Real-data experiments: only modest RMSE improvements (e.g., ML-1M achieves SGLD accuracy in half the time), less convincing than synthetic results.  
- Missing baselines: should compare to SGLD with two parallel chains or SGLD with step size γ/2 to isolate RR extrapolation benefits.  
- Correlated gradient-noise assumption: unclear performance when gradient noise is independent (e.g., independent mini-batches); authors should investigate or enforce correlation.  
- Claims about computational efficiency are questionable since two parallel chains have similar wall-clock cost to standard parallel SGLD baselines.  
- Significance: strong theoretical advance extending RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC and naturally parallelizable, but practical impact needs stronger empirical evidence.  
- Quality and clarity: rigorous theory and well-written presentation, but stronger linkage between theory and practical implementation is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept subject to addressing real-data performance concerns and clarifying technical conditions; overall a solid theoretical contribution.
