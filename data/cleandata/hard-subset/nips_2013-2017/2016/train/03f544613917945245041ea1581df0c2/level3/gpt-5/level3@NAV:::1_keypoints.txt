- Applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, creating SGRRLD by running two SGLD chains with stepsizes γ and γ/2 and carefully correlated Gaussian increments.  
- Uses coupling of Gaussian noise to cancel first-order weak discretization error while controlling variance.  
- Proves a CLT and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds under standard smoothness, Poisson equation solvability, and ergodicity assumptions.  
- Identifies optimal error schedules: bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), and MSE O(K^−4/5) versus O(K^−2/3) for first-order SG-MCMC.  
- Empirically shows large accuracy improvements on Gaussian toy problems and consistent, modest gains on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Demonstrates that parallelism nearly equalizes wall-clock cost between SGRRLD and baseline SGLD.  
- Connects SG-MCMC practice to SDE weak-error literature (Talay–Tubaro; Lemaire–Pagès–Panloup) and prior higher-order integrator work.  
- Analysis is technically careful and sound; role of Brownian correlation is well-motivated and tied to minimizing asymptotic variance.  
- Comparisons to high-order integrators (e.g., on SGHMC) are informative and supportive.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; proofs and conditions are adequately summarized though deferred.  
- Minor clarity issues: Fig. 1a misleadingly overlays a Gaussian using empirical mean/variance rather than the empirical posterior histogram.  
- Minor clarity issue: Fig. 2a shows non-monotone bias vs stepsize—needs more explicit discussion beyond the 1/(Kγ) term.  
- Originality: novel application of RRE to SG-MCMC; coupling and full convergence analysis are nontrivial despite conceptual simplicity.  
- Significance: practically useful, simple to implement, parallel-friendly alternative to higher-order integrators, though not transformative.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical contributions, simple and parallelizable method, consistent empirical gains, and clear bridging of literatures.  
- Cons / suggestions: large-scale gains are modest; include ablations on Brownian and gradient coupling, discuss single-threaded practicality and time/accuracy trade-offs, and clarify minibatch matching and figures.
