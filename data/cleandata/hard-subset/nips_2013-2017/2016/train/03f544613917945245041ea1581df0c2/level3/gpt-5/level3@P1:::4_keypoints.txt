- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and correlated Brownian increments.
- Theoretical results: Proves a CLT under decreasing step sizes and derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds for constant and decreasing schedules.
- Improved rates: For fixed γ after K iterations, bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)) and MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)) giving O(K−2/3) bias and O(K−4/5) MSE; for γk ∝ k−α, best asymptotic rate O(K−2/5) at α = 1/5.
- Empirical findings: Shows accuracy improvements on a Gaussian model, SGHMC variant, and large-scale matrix factorization; wall-clock competitive when chains run in parallel.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SGLD and SG-MCMC theory and imports RR ideas from SDE literature, offering similar accuracy gains to higher-order integrators with simpler first-order updates.
- Main theoretical contribution: Nontrivial extension of RR analysis to SG-MCMC, including CLT with explicit regimes and variance control via perfectly correlated Brownian increments.
- Strong assumptions: CLT relies on high-order smoothness (g ∈ C^9), smoothness/growth of U and f, and unbiased i.i.d. gradient estimators with bounded moments; these should be clearly stated.
- Clarity needed on rates: The manuscript blurs constant-γ and decreasing-γ regimes; derivations connecting Theorems 1 and 2 to reported convergence rates and optimal α should be explicitly shown in the main text.
- Bias/variance balance: Needs explicit quantification of constants and conditions under which gradient noise is dominated by Brownian noise to justify MSE claims.
- Variance reduction mechanism: Key variance reduction uses perfectly correlated Gaussian increments across levels; treatment of stochastic gradient noise correlation is underexplored and should be experimentally ablated.
- Practical guidance gap: Authors should discuss whether mini-batch indices are correlated across chains and empirically evaluate same vs different mini-batches and Brownian correlation.
- Computational fairness: Timing comparisons may be unfair—SGRRLD requires ~3× work per iteration on one core; wall-clock parity depends on parallel execution and should be normalized by gradient evaluations.
- Figure inconsistencies: Figure 3’s bias plot conflicts with text claims about optimal α; legends/labels and plotted quantities (bias vs MSE) need reconciliation and explanation of fluctuations.
- Small-γ behavior: Clarify why SGRRLD shows a U-shaped behavior in Figure 2 but SGLD does not, given similar theoretical bounds in the 1/(Kγ) regime.
- Derivation transparency: The transition from CLT regimes to explicit convergence rates and choice α = 1/5 for MSE should be derived in the main text, including growth rates of ΓK and Γ(3)K.
- Clarity and notation: Algorithm and high-level idea are clear, but notation/definitions (RR, ΓK, smoothness order, vector/matrix bolding, πγ vs π) need polishing and consistency.
- Empirical claims: Some figures and claims conflict with theory; empirical sections readable but require fixes to plots, legends, and experimental normalization.
- Practical sensitivity: Authors should report sensitivity to step-size tuning and provide heuristics beyond theoretical guidance.
- Multi-level RR: Question whether recursive/multi-level RR is practical in SG-MCMC and what empirical gains it yields.
- Pros summary: Method is simple, generic, implementable, extends to SGHMC, has strong theory, and offers parallelization advantages with good empirical gains.
- Cons summary: Concerns over fairness of timing comparisons, figure inconsistencies, unclear derivations of optimal rates, and incomplete treatment of stochastic gradient correlation.
- Minor fixes: Proofread for typos/grammar, fix Eq. (1) symbols, define terms at first use, clarify figures/axes/legends, and ensure consistent notation for identity matrices and distributions.
- Recommendation: Strong and timely paper with solid analysis and promising results; leans toward accept if the authors address comparisons, figure issues, and clarify derivations of optimal rates.
