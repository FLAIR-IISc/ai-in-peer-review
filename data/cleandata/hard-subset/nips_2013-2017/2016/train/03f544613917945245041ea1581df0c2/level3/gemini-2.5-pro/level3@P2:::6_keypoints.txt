- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two correlated SGLD chains with step sizes γk and γk/2 to reduce discretization bias.  
- Theoretical contribution: detailed CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing SGRRLD achieves O(K^-4/5) MSE vs SGLD's O(K^-2/3).  
- Variance control relies on correlating Gaussian noise across the two chains (key implementation detail, Eq. 7).  
- Empirical support: synthetic Gaussian and large-scale matrix factorization experiments show improved accuracy and faster wall-clock convergence.  
- Main strength: rigorous, high-quality theoretical analysis that convincingly establishes improved convergence rates.  
- Main practical weakness: communication/synchronization overhead from correlating noise between chains is unaddressed and may invalidate wall-clock gains in distributed settings.  
- Experimental fairness concern: SGRRLD used two parallel chains vs one SGLD chain, effectively giving SGRRLD twice the compute; missing comparison to two independent parallel SGLD chains pooled as a baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; minor typo—ULA acronym defined twice.  
- Originality: novel combination of RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC but incremental since related ideas exist for SDEs and higher-order integrators.  
- Significance: offers a black-box way to get higher-order benefits without complex integrators, with potential real-world impact if practical costs are addressed.  
- Suggestion: quantify communication cost and synchronization impact, especially for multi-node deployments.  
- Suggestion: include a fair baseline comparing SGRRLD to two independent parallel SGLD chains using equal resources.  
- Suggestion: discuss applicability and potential challenges of applying RR extrapolation to other SG-MCMC variants (adaptive preconditioning, thermostats).  
- Acceptance pros: strong theory, elegant method, solid synthetic validation.  
- Acceptance cons: incremental novelty and insufficient practical evaluation regarding communication overhead and fair baselines.
