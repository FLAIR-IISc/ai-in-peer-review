**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of SG-MCMC while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The algorithm is based on the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation method, which involves running two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes. The authors provide formal theoretical analysis, including a central limit theorem and bounds for the non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error (MSE) of the algorithm. The paper also presents experimental results on synthetic and real data, demonstrating the effectiveness of SGRRLD in reducing bias and improving convergence rates compared to existing SG-MCMC methods.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Theoretical contributions**: The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the SGRRLD algorithm, including a central limit theorem and bounds for the non-asymptotic bias and MSE. This is a significant contribution to the field of SG-MCMC.
2. **Improved convergence rates**: The authors demonstrate that SGRRLD achieves higher convergence rates than existing SG-MCMC methods, including Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD).
3. **Easy to implement**: The SGRRLD algorithm is relatively easy to implement, requiring minimal changes to standard SG-MCMC algorithms.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Derivation of convergence rates**: The paper's derivation of convergence rates is not entirely clear, and the reviewer has raised concerns about the fatal flaws in the derivation.
2. **Practical concerns**: The paper's comparison of SGRRLD with SGLD in terms of computation time is unfair, as SGRRLD requires approximately three times the computation time of SGLD.
3. **Empirical results**: The paper's empirical results, particularly in Figure 3, are not entirely consistent with the theoretical claims made in the paper.
