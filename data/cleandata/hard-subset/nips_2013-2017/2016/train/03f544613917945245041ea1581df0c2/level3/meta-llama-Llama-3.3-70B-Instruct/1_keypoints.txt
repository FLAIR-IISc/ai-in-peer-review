- Proposes a semi-supervised active clustering (SSAC) framework using same-cluster queries to a domain expert.
- Introduces a novel clusterability notion, the γ-margin property.
- Provides an efficient clustering algorithm that succeeds under the γ-margin assumption.
- Establishes a lower bound on the number of queries required to render NP-hard clustering problems feasible.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Offers a thorough analysis of both computational and query complexity for SSAC.
- The proposed algorithm is simple, efficient, and practical for real-world tasks.
- Bridges the clustering and query-complexity literatures.
- Demonstrates that a limited number of same-cluster queries can significantly improve computational efficiency, even when problems are NP-hard without queries.
- Identifies a sharp phase transition/trade-off between regimes where clustering is NP-hard and where it becomes efficiently solvable with queries.
- Minor issues: a potentially misleading figure and insufficient discussion of bias in one result.
- Meets conference guidelines, is technically sound, and situates well within prior work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, thorough complexity analysis, and important trade-off insights.
- Arguments against acceptance: technical contributions may be incremental and the paper lacks discussion on practical implications and applications.
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
