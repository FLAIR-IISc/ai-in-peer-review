- Proposes SGRRLD: an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation on two parallel SGLD chains to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Novelty: first application of RR extrapolation in SG-MCMC, combining numerical sequence acceleration with SGLD.  
- Theoretical contributions: shows asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Empirical results: experiments on synthetic and real data indicate reduced bias and improved MSE versus SGLD.  
- Practicality concern: requires running two chains in parallel, reported cost ~3× SGLD on a single core, raising scalability issues.  
- Fairness issue: comparison to SGLD (Figure 5) is unfair because SGLD runtime was not scaled to account for SGRRLD’s higher computational cost.  
- Minor technical issues: inconsistent notation in Equation 1 and undefined symbols (e.g., "RR", "Γ_K").  
- Missing detail: need more precise smoothness assumptions for the objective f around line 160.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explains approach and theory clearly overall.  
- Originality caveat: approach is novel but would benefit from a deeper discussion contrasting it with prior SG-MCMC work.  
- Significance: potential positive impact for Bayesian ML by improving SG-MCMC bias/MSE in practice.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel algorithm, solid theory, and experimental improvements support acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: increased computational cost, unfair runtime comparison, and minor presentation/assumption issues argue against acceptance.  
- Recommendations: address computational-cost practicality, rescale SGLD baselines for fair comparison, fix notation/definitions, and clarify smoothness assumptions and relation to prior work.
