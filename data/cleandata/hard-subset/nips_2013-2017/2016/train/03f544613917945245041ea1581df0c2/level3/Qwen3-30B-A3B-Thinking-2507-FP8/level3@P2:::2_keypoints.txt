- Introduces SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining results to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical claims: asymptotic bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴) for SGRRLD versus SGLD's O(γ) and O(γ²).  
- Proves CLT and MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD, improving on SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Provides clear algorithmic description and emphasizes correct correlation structure of Brownian increments (Eq. 8) to control variance.  
- Extends approach beyond SGLD by applying RR extrapolation to SGHMC (SGRRHMC).  
- Strengths: theoretical rigor, clear exposition, simple-to-implement algorithm, and grounding in numerical-analysis literature.  
- Synthetic experiments (Gaussian model) show strong bias reduction (variance estimation bias reduced ~10⁻² to ~10⁻⁴).  
- Real-data MovieLens experiments show only modest RMSE improvements (similar accuracy to SGLD in half the time), weakening practical significance.  
- Theoretical conditions for Theorems 1–2 are not fully specified; unclear if results hold for multimodal target U.  
- Questionable claim on parallel/distributed suitability since SGLD can also run multiple chains in parallel; need comparison to multi-chain SGLD baseline.  
- Missing empirical report on whether the two chains maintained required correlation in practice (critical for theoretical variance bounds).  
- Originality is strong (novel RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC) but comparison to recent SG-MCMC acceleration work (e.g., higher-order integrators) is limited.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address multimodal theoretical conditions, stronger real-data validation, and clearer baseline/parallelization comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contribution, clear algorithm, strong synthetic results, provable improvement over SGLD.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest real-data gains, insufficient baseline comparisons (multi-chain SGLD), unclear multimodal theory, limited discussion of practical implementation challenges.
