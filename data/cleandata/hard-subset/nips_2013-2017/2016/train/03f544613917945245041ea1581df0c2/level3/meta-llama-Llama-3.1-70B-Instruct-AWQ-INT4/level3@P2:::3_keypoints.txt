- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two correlated SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.
- Provides formal theory: shows asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Experiments: well-designed empirical results demonstrating reduced bias and MSE.
- Simplicity and implementability: minimal changes to base SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Generality: demonstrated extension to another SG-MCMC method (SGHMC).
- Novelty: Richardson-Romberg extrapolation is established in numerical analysis but novel for ML/SG-MCMC.
- Unclear applicability: whether the technique applies to all SG-MCMC methods or only those with expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ^2).
- Open extensions: unclear if method applies to other approximate MCMC schemes (subsampling MH) or Distributed SGLD.
- Computational efficiency concern: running one chain 2× longer wastes compute; possible 3-chain scheme (one γ, two γ/2) and its effect on variance not analyzed.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clear overall, but some notation (e.g., γ_k) needs clarification.
- Significance and impact: theoretically and empirically significant with potential impact on Bayesian ML.
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper recommended for acceptance, subject to addressing the above questions.
- Review score and confidence: 8/10 rating, confidence 8/10.
