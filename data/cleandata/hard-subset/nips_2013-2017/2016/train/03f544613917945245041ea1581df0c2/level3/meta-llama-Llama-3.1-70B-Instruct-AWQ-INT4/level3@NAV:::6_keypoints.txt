- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance.
- Provides formal theoretical analysis including asymptotic CLT and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Demonstrates effectiveness via synthetic and real-data experiments.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates the method among prior work.
- Novelty: applying RR extrapolation to improve SGLD convergence rate.
- Technical strength: rigorous, well-organized proofs with clear assumptions and conditions.
- Clarity: accessible explanations with helpful examples and illustrations.
- Originality concern: sequence-acceleration ideas for MCMC are not entirely new.
- Practicality concern: running two parallel chains may require significant computational resources and may not scale to large problems.
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
- Minor suggestions: add details on computational resource requirements, include more examples/illustrations, and clarify the relation between the two Gaussian noise sequences.
- Significance: meaningful contribution to SG-MCMC and potentially useful to broader MCMC research.
