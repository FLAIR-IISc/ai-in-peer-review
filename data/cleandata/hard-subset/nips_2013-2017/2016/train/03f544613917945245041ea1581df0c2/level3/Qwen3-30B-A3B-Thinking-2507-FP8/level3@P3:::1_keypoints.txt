- Introduces Richardson-Romberg extrapolation (RRE) applied to SG-MCMC, creating SGRRLD.  
- SGRRLD runs two SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combines them to reduce bias.  
- Bridges MCMC and SDE literatures by bringing numerical sequence acceleration to SG-MCMC.  
- Theoretical result: SGRRLD attains bias O(γ²) and variance O(γ⁴).  
- MSE convergence improved to O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Shows higher-order accuracy can be achieved using only a first-order integrator.  
- Method is naturally parallelizable since the two chains run independently.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous and supported by comprehensive experiments.  
- Synthetic experiments show large bias/variance reductions (e.g., variance ~10⁻⁴) and order-of-magnitude MSE improvement.  
- Real-data (MovieLens matrix factorization) experiments achieve comparable/better accuracy in half the time.  
- Writing is clear and organized; supplementary material includes pseudo-code for reproducibility.  
- Adequately references prior SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation work.  
- Main strengths: theoretical novelty, convergence analysis, practical and easy-to-implement method.  
- Weaknesses: Figure 1a misleading (uses fitted Gaussian rather than empirical posterior).  
- Weaknesses: Figure 2a shows unexplained bias increase for smaller step sizes.  
- Weaknesses: Method is an application of an existing numerical technique and not transformative.  
- NIPS criteria: high quality and clarity, novel application though technique is established, practical significance for large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear empirical validation, practical utility and ease of implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, minor figure issues, not a fundamental breakthrough.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution, recommend acceptance.
