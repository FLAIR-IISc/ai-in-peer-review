- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading bias.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: consistency, a Central Limit Theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Demonstrates improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD versus O(K⁻²/³) for SGLD.  
- Empirical results: large gains on synthetic data with known posterior and consistent but more modest improvements on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Implementation is simple, uses correlated noise to control variance, and is naturally parallelizable (two chains).  
- Connects ML SG-MCMC literature with numerical SDE methods, bringing RRE from numerical analysis into scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Paper is technically high-quality with detailed proofs and well-designed experiments that fairly compare wall-clock time.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well organized; theoretical results are stated clearly with technical details in the supplement.  
- Originality lies in applying a classical RRE technique to SG-MCMC rather than introducing a new integrator.  
- Significance: practical, easy-to-implement "black-box" improvement for SGLD that can halve time-to-accuracy in some large-scale tasks.  
- Potential weakness: viewed as incremental rather than transformative, and empirical gains may be modest relative to the doubled computational cost (mitigated by parallelism).  
- Figure 1a critique: plotting a Gaussian fit to samples can mislead; caption/text should clarify it's a Gaussian fit, not a KDE of the empirical posterior.  
- Figure 2a critique: for very small γ error increases due to the 1/(Kγ) term; main text should more explicitly explain the K–γ trade-off and limited time horizon exploration.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; solid, useful contribution likely to be accepted.
