- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and combining them to reduce bias; chains are embarrassingly parallel.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: proves asymptotic consistency and derives non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows faster convergence rates (asymptotic and finite-time), claiming accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators.  
- Builds on prior SG-MCMC and numerical analysis work (e.g., Welling & Teh 2011; Talay & Tubaro 1990; Teh et al. 2016).  
- Empirical validation on synthetic data convincingly demonstrates bias reduction and improved convergence.  
- Real-world experiments (matrix factorization/movie recommendation) show practical gains and similar computational cost due to parallelism.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, effective, and well-suited for distributed large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: theoretical exposition is dense and somewhat hard to follow; clearer connection to prior convergence results needed.  
- Weakness: lacks direct experimental comparison to higher-order integrators despite claiming comparable accuracy.  
- Weakness: slight inconsistency in step-size selection that needs clarification.  
- Weakness: inconsistency between Algorithm 1 in the supplement and Equation (5) in the main text, harming reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing algorithm description inconsistencies and preferably adding comparisons to higher-order integrators.
