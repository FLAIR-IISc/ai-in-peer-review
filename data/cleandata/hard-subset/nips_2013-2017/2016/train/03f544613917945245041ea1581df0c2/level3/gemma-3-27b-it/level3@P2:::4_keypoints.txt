- Proposes SGRRLD using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes.  
- Theoretical contributions: establishes asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and claimed improved convergence rates over standard SGLD.  
- Variance controlled via correlated Brownian motions; analysis appears generally sound.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and matrix factorization datasets support the method but are limited in scope.  
- Primary weakness: insufficiently detailed derivation linking Theorem 1 to the claimed MSE rate O(K^-4/5); step-size sequence connection is unclear.  
- Empirical-theory discrepancy: Figure 3 shows α=0.2 yielding lowest error while theory predicts α=1/3; requires reconciliation and deeper analysis.  
- Figure 3’s fluctuations cast doubt on the reliability of the reported empirical rate and need further investigation.  
- Practical concern: running two chains increases computational cost; Figure 5’s timing comparison is unfair unless SGLD timing is scaled (e.g., by 2/3).  
- Several presentation/notation issues: ϑ vs θ inconsistency, undefined "RR", Γ_K not introduced, vague “smooth function f” assumption, figure labeling error, and inconsistent vector boldface.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying convergence-rate derivation, reconciling empirical/theoretical discrepancies, and providing a fair computational comparison.
