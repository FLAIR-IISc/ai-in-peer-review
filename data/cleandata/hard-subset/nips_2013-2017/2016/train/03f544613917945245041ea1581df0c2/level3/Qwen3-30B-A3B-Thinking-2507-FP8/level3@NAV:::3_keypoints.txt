- Proposes applying Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2), correlating noise, and subtracting estimates to reduce bias from O(γ) to O(γ²).  
- Implements noise correlation via α_k = (β_{2k-1}+β_{2k})/√2 and doubling steps for the γ/2 chain.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis showing reduced bias/MSE and faster convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Novel application of a classical numerical-analysis technique to ML/SG-MCMC where it’s not widely used.  
- Method is simple to implement with minimal changes to existing SG-MCMC algorithms, making it broadly applicable.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally validated on synthetic data and a large-scale matrix factorization task with significant gains.  
- Unclear applicability to SG-MCMC variants that don’t satisfy the expansion π_γ(f)=π(f)+Cγ+O(γ²); generality needs clarification.  
- Computational-efficiency concern: running the γ/2 chain twice wastes compute; alternative three-chain schemes might be better but aren’t analyzed for variance impact.  
- Details on noise correlation require clarification—specifically whether using the same minibatch across chains is optimal.  
- Matrix factorization experiment should include an SGD baseline and be run to convergence to contextualize Bayesian gains and final errors.  
- Recommend adding an early pseudocode box to aid practitioners; overall assessment: strong contribution with sound theory and convincing experiments despite minor adoption concerns.
