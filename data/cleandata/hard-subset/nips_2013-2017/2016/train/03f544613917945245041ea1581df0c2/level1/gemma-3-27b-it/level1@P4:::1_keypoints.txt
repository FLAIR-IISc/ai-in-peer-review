- Introduces SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation with two parallel SGLD chains to reduce bias.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bias/MSE bounds.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real data show improved convergence rates over SGLD (and one SGHMC comparison).  
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and builds on prior SG-MCMC and Richardson-Romberg SDE literature.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, offering bias reduction without major variance increase.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant and carefully derived.  
- Algorithm is inherently parallelizable, beneficial for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: step-size tuning (relationship between α and K) is sensitive and needs more practical guidance.  
- Weakness: effect of correlated gradients across the two chains and differing mini-batch sizes is underexplored.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to higher-order integrators (e.g., SGHMC-s) and no computational cost analysis.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited to a few datasets, reducing confidence in generalizability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach addressing an important Bayesian inference problem with strong theory and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical adoption concerns from tuning sensitivity and insufficient experimental/comparative evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address tuning, correlation assumptions, and broader experiments.
