- Theoretical contribution: introduces SGRRLD with O(γ²) bias and O(γ⁴) MSE, giving convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³), supported by CLT and non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Builds on Talay & Tubaro (1990) and extends higher-order analysis to SG-MCMC, showing similar accuracy to higher-order integrators with simpler implementation.  
- Practical implementation: parallel two-chain design suits distributed systems and is straightforward to implement.  
- Demonstrated wall-clock advantage in matrix factorization experiments (≈ same accuracy in roughly half the time).  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian and ML-1M/10M/20M experiments support theory; MSE behaviour and time improvements shown.  
- Clarity: paper is very well-written with clear notation, precise algorithm description, and detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Weakness — correlated Brownian motions: practical details for implementing perfect correlation in distributed settings are vague.  
- Weakness — lack of direct comparison with higher-order methods (e.g., Chen et al., 2015 or SGHMC-s) weakens empirical positioning.  
- Weakness — computational cost per iteration not analyzed; running two chains may increase per-iteration cost and affect wall-clock efficiency in some setups.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and rigorous contribution that improves SG-MCMC bias/variance trade-offs and is practically useful.  
- Recommendation: minor weaknesses do not outweigh benefits — recommend acceptance for NIPS 2016.
