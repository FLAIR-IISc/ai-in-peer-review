- Introduces SGRRLD: applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading-order bias.  
- Connects SG-MCMC literature with classical numerical SDE analysis (Talay & Tubaro) and adapts RR extrapolation to stochastic gradients.  
- Key technical insight: control variance via correlated noise when combining two stochastic chains.  
- Presents a rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Claims and proves improved convergence rates: MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Validates theory with comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates practical wall-clock time improvements on large-scale matrix factorization tasks.  
- Shows method generality by applying the approach to SGHMC as well.  
- Method is simple, practical, and parallelizable, offering an alternative to higher-order integrators for bias correction.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: doubled computational cost from running two chains; needs explicit comparison to a single SGLD with equivalent total gradient evaluations.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant contribution likely to be adopted by researchers and practitioners.
