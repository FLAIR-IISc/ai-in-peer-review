- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGLD) by running two coupled chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and using a time-weighted estimator 2*fine − 1*coarse with correlated Gaussian increments.

- Proves CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing leading weak bias reduced to O(γ^2) and optimal MSE rate O(K^−4/5) (vs O(K^−2/3) for first-order SG-MCMC).

- Shows variance control via coupling; choosing coarse increments as the average of fine increments (Σ = I) minimizes asymptotic variance.

- Novelty: imports RR extrapolation into SG-MCMC, building on Talay & Tubaro and duplicated-diffusion results, and appears new relative to existing SG-MCMC literature.

- Theoretical analysis is careful and consistent with RR rationale; optimal α for decreasing step sizes and rate trade-offs are derived.

- Assumptions are strong: requires high smoothness (Poisson solution g ∈ C^9), ergodicity/weak error conditions, and identical-law stochastic gradient estimators (same mini-batch size).

- The claim that Gaussian noise dominates stochastic gradient noise depends on scaling assumptions and may not hold with large or state-dependent mini-batch variance.

- Practicality: doubles gradient computations but is trivially parallelizable; empirical wall-clock parity achieved by parallel runs, but single-thread cost doubles.

- Experiments show empirical improvements on a linear-Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization; SGRRHMC matches higher-order SGHMC for suitable step sizes.

- Missing stronger empirical baselines: lacks head-to-head comparisons with higher-order SG-MCMC (e.g., Chen et al., 2015) and control-variate/variance-reduction SG-MCMC on real data.

- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and well organized; estimator and coupling exposition is good, but pseudocode should be in the main text and Eq. (5) likely has a typo (implicit vs explicit Euler).

- Suggest positioning relative to MLMC explicitly, since SGRRLD resembles a two-level MLMC-style estimator, and discuss feasibility of multi-level (L > 2) RR in SG-MCMC.

- Pros for acceptance: conceptually simple, general, solid theoretical support, matches higher-order accuracy without complex integrators, parallel-friendly, and extends to SGHMC.

- Cons/weaknesses: strong smoothness and identical-gradient assumptions, limited empirical baselines, reliance on parallel execution for fairness, and minor clarity/notation issues.

- Requests/suggestions: confirm Eq. (5) indices; analyze sensitivity to stochastic-gradient variance; test common mini-batches/common random numbers; report fixed gradient-evaluation budget (single-thread) results; compare to high-order SG-MCMC.

- Minor comments: add Giles (2008) for MLMC, clarify whether “variance O(γ^4)” refers to finite-K MSE or asymptotic CLT variance, and fix typographical/notation inconsistencies.

- Overall assessment: technically solid, practically relevant contribution that advances SG-MCMC via RR extrapolation; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
