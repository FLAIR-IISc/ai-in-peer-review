- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains at step sizes γ and γ/2, correlating Gaussian increments, and forming a weighted difference of time-averages to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: CLT and non-asymptotic bounds showing asymptotic bias O(γ^2), variance O(γ^4), and finite-time MSE O(K−4/5) with optimal step schedules, improving over prior O(K−2/3).  
- Novelty: First to apply RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with stochastic gradients and analyze the coupled noise.  
- Theory quality: Uses Poisson-equation and high-order Taylor expansions; variance-minimizing coupling Σ = Id is well justified, but relies on strong smoothness/ergodicity assumptions.  
- Practical requirement: Two chains must use gradient estimators with the same law (e.g., same minibatch size); authors discuss bias otherwise.  
- Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments confirm rates; SGHMC variant attains accuracy similar to higher-order integrators; matrix factorization shows wall-clock gains via parallelism.  
- Empirical limitations: Limited comparisons to other SG-MCMC baselines (pSGLD, SGFS, SGNHT) and narrow task scope (no logistic regression or Bayesian neural nets).  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized; coupling construction and correlated Brownian increments are clearly explained.  
- Minor clarity issues: Suspected typo in Equation (5), unclear whether minibatch indices are reused across chains, and full pseudocode is relegated to the supplement.  
- Originality/impact: Simple, broadly applicable method that bridges RR for SDEs and SG-MCMC, offering practical bias/MSE improvements without designing higher-order integrators.  
- Significance: Provable improvements and embarrassingly parallel implementation make it attractive to practitioners with modest parallel resources; potential for extensions (e.g., multilevel).  
- Pros for acceptance: Clear theoretical guarantees, careful variance control, solid synthetic and large-scale experiments, and novel bridging of literatures.  
- Cons/weaknesses: Strong assumptions (smoothness/ergodicity), limited empirical comparisons, limited tuning guidance for α and γ, reliance on exact shared Gaussian noise coupling, and doubled computation if no parallelism.  
- Suggested improvements: Fix Eq.(5), include main-text pseudocode, give practical coupling/RNG/minibatch-reuse guidance, add logistic regression and nonconvex benchmarks with comparisons, and provide step-size tuning diagnostics and single-core trade-off discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
