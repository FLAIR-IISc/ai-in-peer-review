- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance, with theoretical and empirical validation.
- Relation to previous work: Extends SGLD and applies RR extrapolation from numerical analysis to SG-MCMC, presenting a novel extension.
- Strength — Theoretical analysis: Provides asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Strength — Improved convergence rate: Claims higher convergence rate than first-order-integrator SG-MCMC methods.
- Strength — Experiments: Shows improved performance on synthetic and real datasets compared to existing SG-MCMC algorithms.
- Weakness — Computational complexity: Requires running two parallel chains, increasing computational cost.
- Weakness — Step-size selection: Relies on specific step-size choices for RR extrapolation that may be problem-dependent and require tuning.
- Weakness — Limited applicability: Focuses on a specific class of SG-MCMC algorithms and may not generalize to other MCMC types.
- Argument for acceptance — Originality: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a significant contribution.
- Argument for acceptance — Theory: Strong theoretical backing is essential and well-developed.
- Argument for acceptance — Empirical evidence: Experimental results support claimed improvements.
- Argument against acceptance — Cost: Potentially more expensive than alternatives due to dual-chain requirement.
- Argument against acceptance — Generality concerns: Limited scope may reduce practical impact across other MCMC methods.
- Argument against acceptance — Practicality: Step-size tuning requirements may hinder real-world implementation.
- Quality and clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible to readers familiar with MCMC and numerical analysis.
- Originality and significance: Novel method with potential for meaningful improvements in SG-MCMC convergence and performance.
