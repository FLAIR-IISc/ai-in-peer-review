- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains (γ and γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading discretization error.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: consistency, CLT (Theorem 1), and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds (Theorem 2).
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³), with optimal step-size decay α = 1/5.
- Empirical validation on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale real datasets corroborates theoretical gains in accuracy and efficiency.
- Demonstrates generality by applying RR extrapolation to SGHMC as well.
- Algorithm is simple, practical, and “drop-in” for existing SG-MCMC methods, requiring only two runs with different step sizes.
- Highly parallelizable, enabling better wall-clock performance despite increased total work.
- Matches accuracy of more complex higher-order integrators while using only a first-order scheme.
- Experimental design is careful and reproducible, with clear figures and parameter studies (step size, dimensionality).
- Main technical assumptions and pseudocode are placed in the supplement; a brief summary and inclusion of pseudocode in the main text would improve self-containment.
- Method increases total computational cost (~3× FLOPs for same effective K), relying on parallel hardware to remain competitive in wall-clock time.
- Contribution is a novel and elegant combination of existing techniques applied to the stochastic-gradient setting, not a wholly new paradigm.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and likely to have strong practical impact on scalable Bayesian inference.
- Reviewer recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
