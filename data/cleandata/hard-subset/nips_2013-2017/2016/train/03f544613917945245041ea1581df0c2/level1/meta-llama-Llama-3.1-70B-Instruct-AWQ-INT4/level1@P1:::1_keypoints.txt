- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm that reduces bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two parallel chains with different step sizes.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows via CLT that Gaussian noise dominates stochastic gradient noise for the estimator π̂RK(f).  
- Uses non-asymptotic bounds to analyze convergence rates; empirically achieves higher rates and matches higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Strong experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized with clear introduction, algorithm description, and analysis.  
- Main weakness: assumes perfect correlation between the two Gaussian noise sequences, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Analysis is limited to the perfectly correlated case, though the authors discuss handling non-perfect correlation.  
- Overall assessment: high scores (9/10 across quality, clarity, originality, significance); arguments for acceptance include novelty, thorough theory, and experiments; primary argument against is the correlation assumption.
