- Proposes SGRRLD, a new SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Implements RR by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes and extrapolating their estimates.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show reduced bias and improved convergence versus SGLD.  
- Builds on prior SGLD work and adapts Richardson-Romberg extrapolation from numerical analysis.  
- Technically sound with rigorous proofs supporting theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, organized proofs, and well-described experiments.  
- Original contribution: first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC methods.  
- Significant potential impact for Bayesian inference in large-scale applications.  
- Pro: novel approach, thorough theory, and empirical validation.  
- Con: may be limited to SGLD-style methods and adds computational cost by running two chains.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported contribution and good candidate for acceptance.
