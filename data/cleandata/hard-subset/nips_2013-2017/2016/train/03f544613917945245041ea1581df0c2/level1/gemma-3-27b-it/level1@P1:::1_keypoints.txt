- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, which applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias and achieve higher-order convergence.  
- Theoretical analysis: Proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and provides non-asymptotic bounds on bias and MSE.  
- Empirical results: Synthetic and real-world (matrix factorization) experiments show SGRRLD outperforms SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Novelty: Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a novel and well-motivated idea.  
- Literature positioning: Well-situated within SG-MCMC literature and acknowledges related higher-order integrator work.  
- Simplicity vs. accuracy: Demonstrates comparable accuracy to higher-order integrators while using a simpler first-order integrator.  
- Generality: Method generalizes beyond SGLD (demonstrated with SGHMC).  
- Parallelism: Inherent parallelism of the RR approach suits modern hardware.  
- Implementation complexity: Requires careful handling of Brownian motion correlation (Eq. 8), which may be difficult in practice.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance depends on step sizes (γ and γ/2) and correlation matrix Σ; robustness needs more study.  
- Limited baselines: Lacks comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods that address bias reduction.  
- Step-size guidance: Practical application of theoretical optimal step sizes (γ = K^{-1/3} for bias, γ = K^{-0.2} for MSE) and sensitivity to deviations need clearer guidance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorous, empirically strong, clearly written, and broadly applicable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical adoption concerns (implementation and hyperparameter sensitivity) and limited benchmarking.  
- Recommendation: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity, step-size guidance, and discussion of related recent methods.
