This paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm, called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which aims to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC algorithms while keeping the variance at a reasonable level. The approach is based on a numerical sequence acceleration method, namely the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation, which involves running two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes in parallel.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, including its asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and bounds for its non-asymptotic bias and mean squared error. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of SGRRLD through experiments on both synthetic and real data.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do a good job of explaining the technical details of their approach. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experiments are well-designed and informative.
