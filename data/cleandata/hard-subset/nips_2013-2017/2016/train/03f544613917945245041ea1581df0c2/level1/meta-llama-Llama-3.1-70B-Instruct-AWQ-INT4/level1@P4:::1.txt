**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel Stochastic Gradient Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SG-MCMC) algorithm called Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD). SGRRLD aims to reduce the bias of existing SG-MCMC algorithms while maintaining a reasonable variance. The approach is based on the Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation method, which involves running two chains with different step sizes in parallel. The authors provide a formal theoretical analysis of SGRRLD, showing its asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bounds for bias and mean squared error (MSE). The paper also presents experimental results on synthetic and real data, demonstrating the improved performance of SGRRLD over existing SG-MCMC algorithms.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on SG-MCMC algorithms, particularly Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD) [1]. The authors also draw inspiration from the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation method, which has been used in numerical analysis to improve the convergence rate of Monte Carlo estimates [21]. The paper extends the RR extrapolation method to SG-MCMC algorithms and provides a novel application of this technique.

**Strengths**

2. **Improved convergence rate**: The authors show that SGRRLD achieves a higher convergence rate than existing SG-MCMC algorithms with first-order integrators.

**Weaknesses**

3. **Limited applicability**: The paper focuses on a specific class of SG-MCMC algorithms and may not be directly applicable to other types of MCMC algorithms.
