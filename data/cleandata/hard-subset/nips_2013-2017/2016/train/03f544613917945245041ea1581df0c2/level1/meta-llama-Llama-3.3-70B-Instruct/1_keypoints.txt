- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
- Method runs two chains in parallel with different step sizes to achieve bias reduction while maintaining reasonable variance.  
- Provides formal theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and detailed theoretical exposition.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show SGRRLD reduces bias and improves convergence over existing SG-MCMC methods.  
- Strength: novel algorithm addressing a significant bias issue in SG-MCMC.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical treatment covering asymptotic and non-asymptotic properties.  
- Strength: empirical evaluations supporting claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: requires running two parallel chains, increasing computational cost.  
- Weakness: step-size and mini-batch size selection may need careful tuning in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient discussion of potential applications and limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, and empirically effective method.  
- Arguments against acceptance: increased computational expense, tuning challenges, and limited discussion of applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage authors to discuss applications/limitations and explore ways to reduce computational costs and improve practicality.
