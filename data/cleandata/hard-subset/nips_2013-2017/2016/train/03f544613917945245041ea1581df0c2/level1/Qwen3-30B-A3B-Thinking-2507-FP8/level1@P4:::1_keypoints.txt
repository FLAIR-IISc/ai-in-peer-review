- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two chains with different step sizes to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Theoretically achieves MSE convergence rate O(K^-4/5), improving over SGLD's O(K^-2/3) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy using only first-order methods.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Variance control is achieved via correlated Brownian increments, with justification from prior RR/SDE work.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the connection between numerical analysis techniques and MCMC.  
- Demonstrates generality: method extends beyond SGLD (e.g., to SGHMC).  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization show superiority over SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Core technique (RR extrapolation) is borrowed from numerical analysis, so novelty is primarily in its application to SG-MCMC.  
- SGRRLD requires running two chains in parallel, raising potential memory overhead not fully analyzed.  
- Experimental scope is limited; lacks tests on more complex models like deep Bayesian neural networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, practical improvements, clear writing, simple implementation, and meaningful impact on scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of the core technique, unquantified memory costs, and narrower experimental validation.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and practically significant contribution with convincing theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance.
