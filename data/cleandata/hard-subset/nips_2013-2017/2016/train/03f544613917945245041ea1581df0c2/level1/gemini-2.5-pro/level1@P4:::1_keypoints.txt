- Introduces SGRRLD: a Richardson-Romberg extrapolated SG-MCMC running two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2 with two fine steps per coarse) and estimator 2*avg(fine) − avg(coarse).  
- Uses correlated noise (derive coarse noise from fine) to control variance; shows optimal correlation structure.  
- Theoretically: proves consistency, a Central Limit Theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Shows improved MSE convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³), matching higher-order integrators while using Euler-Maruyama.  
- Empirically validated on a synthetic Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization, demonstrating practical gains and extension to SGHMC (SGRRHMC).  
- Strength: novel and significant application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC enabling higher accuracy with a simple first-order scheme.  
- Strength: rigorous technical analysis identifying optimal noise correlation and providing CLT and MSE bounds.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and solid connection to prior work.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments showing controlled verification and large-scale practical improvements.  
- Weakness: increased computational cost—three gradient evaluations per coarse iteration (≈1.5× wall-clock with two cores); more explicit cost vs accuracy analysis desirable.  
- Weakness: theoretical results are worked out for SGLD only; generality claims to other SG-MCMC samplers lack formal extension.  
- Weakness: practical effects of correlating stochastic gradient estimators (e.g., reusing minibatches) are not fully explored experimentally.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with strong theory, practical significance, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: notable computational overhead and limited formal generality beyond SGLD.
