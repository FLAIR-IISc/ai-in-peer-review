- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Theoretical contributions: proves consistency, a central limit theorem, and provides bias and MSE bounds with claimed improved convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Experiments: evaluates on synthetic data and matrix factorization, showing improvements over SGLD and competitiveness with SGHMC.  
- Novelty: first application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC, leveraging classical numerical analysis ideas.  
- Motivation and positioning: well-motivated within SG-MCMC literature and compares conceptually to higher-order integrators while using first-order methods.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: rigorous analysis and convergence-rate results are significant contributions.  
- Strength — empirical validation: experiments support theoretical claims in demonstrated settings.  
- Weakness — strong assumptions: theoretical results rely on potentially restrictive assumptions that are not fully discussed.  
- Weakness — dense presentation: theoretical sections are dense and need more intuition about why convergence improves.  
- Weakness — limited experimental comparisons: lacks broader benchmarks (other SG-MCMC variants, different step schedules, preconditioning).  
- Weakness — computational cost: running two chains increases overhead, but the paper lacks a rigorous analysis of this cost and scalability impact.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: code is not released, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness — generality claim under-justified: claim of applicability to other SG-MCMC algorithms is not fully substantiated beyond one SGHMC example.  
- Recommendations: clarify and discuss assumptions, expand experiments (more algorithms and step schedules), analyze computational overhead of two chains, and release code.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and novel work with rigorous theory and promising experiments, but currently borderline — leans toward acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above issues.
