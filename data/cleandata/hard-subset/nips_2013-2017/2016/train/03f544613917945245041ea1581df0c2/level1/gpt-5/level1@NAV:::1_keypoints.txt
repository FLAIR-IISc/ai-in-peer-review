- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (instantiated on SGLD) by running two coupled chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and forming an extrapolated estimator.  
- Variance is controlled by correlating Gaussian increments; ideal coupling uses the same Brownian path (Σ = I).  
- The authors prove consistency and a CLT with minimal asymptotic variance under perfect correlation.  
- Non-asymptotic bounds: bias O(γ^2) and MSE O(γ^4).  
- For fixed K, optimal MSE rate O(K−4/5), improving over first-order SG-MCMC’s O(K−2/3) and matching higher-order integrators’ accuracy.  
- Method is simple, broadly applicable beyond SGLD (demonstrated on SGHMC) and parallel-friendly.  
- Empirical results (Gaussian model, SGHMC variants, MovieLens matrix factorization) show improved accuracy at equal wall-clock using parallel chains.  
- Theoretical development is careful and technically solid, adapting Talay–Tubaro and related SDE tools to the stochastic-gradient setting.  
- Proof techniques use Poisson equations and high-order Taylor expansions; assumptions are reasonable but strong (smoothness up to C9 and moment bounds).  
- Clarity: paper is well written, but main assumptions, precise coupling, pseudocode, and practical tuning guidance are relegated to the supplement.  
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with explicit variance control via common random numbers.  
- Significance: likely to influence practitioners seeking better accuracy without complex integrators.  
- Pros: simple/generic idea with strong theory, improved rates, practical and parallelizable, empirical gains on real data.  
- Cons: strong assumptions and unspecified constants, limited analysis of gradient-noise/minibatch coupling, experiments limited in scope and sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; reviewer recommends acceptance.
