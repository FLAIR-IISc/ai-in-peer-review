- Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias while keeping variance, with theory and experiments showing improved bias, MSE, and computational efficiency.

- Strength — Technical Soundness: Provides rigorous asymptotic and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds and a central limit theorem that align with experiments.

- Strength — Novelty: Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC that reduces bias without higher-order integrators.

- Strength — Practical Relevance: Algorithm is computationally efficient and well-suited for parallel/distributed large-scale Bayesian inference.

- Strength — Experimental Validation: Thorough experiments, including large-scale matrix factorization, show clear improvements over SGLD.

- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear algorithm, theory, and experimental descriptions.

- Weakness — Limited Comparisons: Lacks comprehensive comparisons to other advanced SG-MCMC methods (e.g., adaptive step sizes, preconditioning).

- Weakness — Computational Overhead: Running two parallel chains may introduce extra overhead not fully quantified.

- Weakness — Strong Assumptions: Theoretical guarantees rely on technical assumptions (smoothness, ergodicity) that may limit applicability.

- Weakness — Supplementary Dependence: Key proofs and experimental details are relegated to supplementary material, reducing accessibility.

- Arg for Acceptance: Addresses SG-MCMC bias with a novel, theoretically sound, and practically scalable method supported by experiments.

- Arg Against Acceptance: Limited empirical comparisons and unexplored computational trade-offs leave open questions about broader competitiveness and cost.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance; contributions are significant despite minor shortcomings.

- Score: 8/10
