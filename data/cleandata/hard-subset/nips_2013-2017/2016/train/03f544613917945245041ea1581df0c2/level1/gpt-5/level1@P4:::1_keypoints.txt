- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining to cancel the O(γ) bias.
- Controls variance by coupling Gaussian noise via shared Brownian increments; proves this coupling minimizes asymptotic variance among linear couplings (Σ=Id).
- Theoretical contributions: consistency, a CLT with explicit minimal variance, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Proven rates: with constant steps bias O(K−2/3) and MSE O(K−4/5); with decreasing steps (α=1/5) asymptotic MSE O(K−4/5), improving over first-order SG-MCMC.
- Extends to SGHMC (SGRRHMC) and is parallelizable by running the two chains concurrently.
- Experiments on a Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization corroborate theoretical scalings and show improved estimation and test RMSE.
- Novelty: bridges RR extrapolation for invariant measures with SG-MCMC, applying RR to stochastic gradients with rigorous analysis.
- Assumptions: requires strong smoothness (Poisson solution in C^9), dissipativity/Lipschitz conditions, and moment bounds on gradient estimators (mainly in supplement).
- Analysis assumes unbiased stochastic gradients with identical law across chains; shows Gaussian noise dominates gradient noise in the CLT.
- Variance-minimizing Brownian coupling aligns with multilevel Monte Carlo intuition and is appropriate for the method.
- Empirics show SGRRLD matches higher-order splitting integrators at larger γ for SGHMC and scales with dimension as predicted.
- Concern: exact regularity, ergodicity, and moment assumptions are relegated to supplement and need explicit statement and practical discussion.
- Concern: impact of sharing mini-batch indices across chains (correlated vs independent gradient noise) is not studied and lacks ablation.
- Concern: wall-clock comparisons rely on parallel execution, so single-core compute-budget trade-offs are unclear.
- Concern: empirical baselines omit several competitive methods (higher-order overdamped integrators, pSGLD, thermostat variants).
- Concern: real-data metrics (test RMSE) assess prediction but not posterior sampling quality; lacks MCMC diagnostics like ESS and uncertainty calibration.
- Clarity suggestions: summarize core assumptions in main text, provide pseudocode/schematic for RR estimator, and clarify Chain 2 indexing and noise scaling notation.
- Strengths: simple, broadly applicable bias-reduction; solid theoretical guarantees; empirical validation; minimal implementation overhead; parallel-friendly.
- Weaknesses: strong smoothness/ergodicity assumptions, limited ablations on gradient-noise coupling and mini-batch sensitivity, incomplete empirical baselines, and missing sampling diagnostics.
- Requests to authors: state explicit assumptions on U and gradient estimator in main text and comment on dependence of constants on dimension and batch size.
- Requests to authors: include ablation on shared vs independent mini-batches and add a single-core runtime comparison.
- Requests to authors: report additional sampling diagnostics (e.g., ESS) for matrix factorization experiments.
- Recommendation rationale: technically solid, novel, and practically useful with pros outweighing cons, but some theoretical assumptions and empirical comparisons/details should be clarified or expanded.
