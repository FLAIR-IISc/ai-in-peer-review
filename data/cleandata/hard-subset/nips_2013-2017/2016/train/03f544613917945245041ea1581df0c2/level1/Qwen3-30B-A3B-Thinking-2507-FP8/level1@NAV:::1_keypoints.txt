- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains at different step sizes and combining outputs.
- Achieves improved MSE convergence rate O(K^{-4/5}) versus SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}).
- Extends Talay & Tubaro numerical analysis techniques to the stochastic gradient setting, building on SGLD and Chen et al. (2015).
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including central limit theorems and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization, outperforming SGLD and SGHMC.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with solid support for theoretical claims.
- Methodology is original in applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, though parallel-chain ideas are not entirely novel.
- High significance: addresses SG-MCMC bias without requiring higher-order integrators and preserves parallelizability.
- Weaknesses: technical proofs may be hard for some readers; paper mainly focuses on SGLD with limited exploration of other SG-MCMC variants.
- Overall verdict: substantial, practically effective contribution to scalable Bayesian inference; recommend acceptance.
