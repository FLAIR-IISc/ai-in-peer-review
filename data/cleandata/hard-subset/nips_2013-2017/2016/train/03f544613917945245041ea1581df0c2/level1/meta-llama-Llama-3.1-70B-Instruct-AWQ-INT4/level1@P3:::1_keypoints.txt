- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SGLD bias while controlling variance.  
- RR extrapolation requires running two chains with different step sizes in parallel to improve SGLD convergence rate.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly introduces background and related work.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated with thorough theoretical analysis (asymptotic consistency, CLT, non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds).  
- Main contribution: novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data demonstrate the method’s effectiveness; results are clear and well-presented.  
- Strength: novel idea that improves SG-MCMC convergence.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical treatment of algorithm properties.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments and clear presentation of results.  
- Weakness: assumes high background knowledge in SG-MCMC and RR extrapolation, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and real-world application guidance.  
- Originality: the RR extrapolation application to SG-MCMC is new and interesting.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to SG-MCMC and Bayesian inference with practical efficiency implications.  
- Quality and clarity: well-organized, thoroughly analyzed, and clearly presented.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with solid theory and experiments despite accessibility/practical-discussion gaps.
