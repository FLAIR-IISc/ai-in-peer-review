- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation by running two SGLD chains with different step sizes and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical results: consistency, a central limit theorem, and bias/MSE bounds showing improved convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real (matrix factorization) data support improved performance and competitiveness with higher-order integrators.  
- Builds on and properly cites SG-MCMC literature and connections to higher-order integrators and Richardson-Romberg theory.  
- Novelty: first application of RR extrapolation to the SG-MCMC setting.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and rigorous with both asymptotic and non-asymptotic guarantees.  
- Empirical results are convincing and show practical improvements; explores SGRRHMC to demonstrate generality.  
- Addresses increased variance via correlated Brownian motions, showing thoughtful variance control.  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy, making the paper difficult to follow in places.  
- Insufficient discussion of computational cost, parallelization details, and scalability considerations.  
- Experimental hyperparameter choices (e.g., step sizes) are not fully justified and appear to rely on trial-and-error.  
- Lacks comparisons to other SG-MCMC-specific bias-reduction techniques.  
- Overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand computational discussion.
