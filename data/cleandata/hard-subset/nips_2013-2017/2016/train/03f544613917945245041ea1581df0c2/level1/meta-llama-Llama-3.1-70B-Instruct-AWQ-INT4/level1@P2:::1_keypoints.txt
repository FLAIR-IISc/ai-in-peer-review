- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while maintaining reasonable variance.  
- Method runs two SG-MCMC chains in parallel with different step sizes and extrapolates to accelerate numerical convergence.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Empirically validates SGRRLD on synthetic and real datasets, including large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness in practice.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theory and experiments.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with well-defined notation and good background exposition.  
- Original use of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation in SG-MCMC is a novel contribution.  
- Results are significant and potentially widely applicable in practice.  
- Pro: Novel, significant contribution with rigorous theory and informative experiments.  
- Pro: Approach has practical potential demonstrated on a common large-scale application.  
- Con: Paper’s technical nature may be hard for some readers to follow.  
- Con: Lacks deeper intuition on why Richardson-Romberg effectively reduces SG-MCMC bias.  
- Con: Could include more comparisons to other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant, well-written, and technically strong contribution.
