- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two correlated SGLD chains (step γ and two steps γ/2) and linearly combining outputs to cancel leading bias.  
- Provides rigorous theory: CLT and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds showing MSE improvement from O(K^-2/3) (SGLD) to O(K^-4/5) and identifies optimal step-size decay and need for correlated noise.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic Gaussian experiments verify theoretical bias/MSE behavior; large-scale matrix factorization shows improved RMSE vs SGLD for comparable wall-clock time.  
- Connects fields: novel combination of SG-MCMC and classical RR extrapolation, extending prior SDE extrapolation results to stochastic-gradient settings.  
- Generality: method extends to SGHMC and acts as a broadly applicable "wrapper" to improve various SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Clarity and presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written, logically organized, and clearly motivates and explains results.  
- Practical significance: addresses bias as a key limiter in SG-MCMC, is parallelizable, and likely to influence scalable Bayesian inference.  
- Computational-cost weakness: requires ~3× gradient evaluations sequentially (≈2× if parallel), so users without parallel hardware face substantial slowdowns.  
- Experimental suggestion: should include comparisons under equal computational budget (e.g., SGRRLD at K vs SGLD at 3K) to clarify efficiency gains.  
- Assumptions caveat: theoretical guarantees rely on smoothness and gradient-noise conditions; applicability to highly nonconvex/deep learning problems is not fully discussed.  
- Minor clarification: statement about SGLD not benefiting from parallelism is misleading—multiple independent SGLD chains can run in parallel, but SGRRLD uses correlated chains for principled bias reduction.  
- Acceptance case: novel, elegant, theoretically strong, practical and general approach with strong empirical support.  
- Rejection case: increased computational resource requirements could be a practical barrier in some applications.
