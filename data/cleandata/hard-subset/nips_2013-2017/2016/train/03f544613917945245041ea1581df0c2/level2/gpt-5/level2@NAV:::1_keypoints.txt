- Proposal: SGRRLD applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC (SGLD, SGHMC) using coupled discretizations with step sizes γ and γ/2 and correlated Brownian increments.  
- Core claim: extrapolation reduces bias to O(γ^2), variance to O(γ^4), and improves finite-time MSE to O(K^-4/5), matching high-order integrators while using first-order updates.  
- Theory: provides consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Empirics: on a Gaussian model and MovieLens matrix factorization, SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in bias/MSE and test RMSE at matched wall-clock via parallel chains.  
- Prior work: builds on classic RR for diffusions and recent SG-MCMC analyses; distinct from prior high-order integrator designs.  
- Analysis quality: careful and clear with principled variance control (optimal Σ = I); relies on standard but strong smoothness/Poisson-equation assumptions.  
- Clarity: well organized overall; proofs/pseudocode in supplement—would benefit from an algorithm box and practical coupling guidance.  
- Originality: novel application of RR extrapolation with explicit coupling strategy in the SG-MCMC community.  
- Significance: offers a simple, parallelizable method to improve SG-MCMC accuracy that is likely to be adopted and extended.  
- Practical coupling gap: needs guidance and ablation studies on correlating mini-batch gradients (e.g., shared indices across chains).  
- Resource-fairness concern: equal wall-clock comparisons assume two workers; discuss single-core settings and provide CPU-time-normalized results.  
- Scope limitation: experiments are limited to Gaussian/posterior-linear and matrix factorization; should include non-convex posteriors (e.g., Bayesian neural nets) and decreasing step-size (α=1/5) tests to validate CLT behavior.  
- Missing comparisons: should compare to preconditioned/thermostat methods (pSGLD, SGNHT) under RR and include ablations with/without coupling (Σ ≠ I).  
- Multilevel RR: either empirically explore recursive/multilevel RR or clarify when single-level RR suffices.  
- Reproducibility: release code and more implementation details.  
- Verdict: strong, novel, and well-supported contribution; recommended for acceptance after minor clarifications.
