- Proposes SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes in parallel.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows faster convergence rates than existing first-order SG-MCMC methods (finite-time and asymptotic).  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data; SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in computation time on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Paper is technically sound with well-organized, easy-to-follow proofs and consistent notation.  
- Writing and explanations are clear, making the algorithm and theory understandable for knowledgeable readers.  
- Novelty: first use of RR extrapolation to improve SG-MCMC accuracy.  
- Significance: potential to improve accuracy and efficiency of SG-MCMC in large-scale Bayesian inference.  
- For acceptance: novel method, strong theoretical guarantees, and convincing empirical improvement.  
- Against acceptance: assumes strong prior knowledge of SG-MCMC and RR, which may hinder accessibility.
