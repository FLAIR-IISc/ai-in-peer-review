- Proposal: Introduces SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC method that applies Richardson–Romberg extrapolation by running two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and forming a specific linear combination of their outputs.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provides a Central Limit Theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds showing improved convergence.  
- Convergence rates: Shows SGRRLD achieves MSE O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
- Variance control: Reduces variance of the combined estimator by correlating the noise terms of the two chains.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on synthetic and large-scale real data support the theoretical claims on bias and MSE as functions of step size.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC, extending RR analysis for SDEs to the stochastic-gradient setting.  
- Practical significance: Achieves higher-order accuracy using first-order updates and is parallelizable, improving statistical efficiency without increasing wall-clock time (demonstrated in matrix factorization).  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides necessary background and experimental detail.  
- Computational cost caveat: Requires running two chains (roughly double operations); parallelization mitigates this but single-core or memory-limited scenarios may be problematic.  
- Generality claim caveat: The claim that RR extrapolation is “virtually applicable” to all SG-MCMC methods should be qualified with discussion of required error-expansion properties.  
- Experimental recommendation: Suggests including the SGHMC with higher-order splitting (SGHMC-s) comparison in a main figure to better support claims about matching higher-order integrators.
