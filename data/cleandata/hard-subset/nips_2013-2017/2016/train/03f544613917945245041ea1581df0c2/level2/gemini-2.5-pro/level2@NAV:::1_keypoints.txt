- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias in SGLD.  
- Core idea: run two parallel chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine estimators to cancel leading-order discretization error.  
- Builds on scalable Bayesian inference and RR variance-reduction methods from SDE discretization literature.  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis including a central limit theorem and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Key theoretical result: SGRRLD attains MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic (ground-truth) and large-scale real-world matrix factorization tasks supports theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically structured, making complex ideas accessible.  
- Main weakness: roughly threefold computational overhead per effective iteration compared to a single SGLD chain.  
- Authors mitigate cost by noting parallelizability and faster convergence, but lack a direct comparison to running SGLD for three times as many iterations.  
- Suggestion: include a brief high-level summary of technical assumptions in the main text for better self-containedness.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution with strong theory and experiments; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses and the work is likely to be influential.
