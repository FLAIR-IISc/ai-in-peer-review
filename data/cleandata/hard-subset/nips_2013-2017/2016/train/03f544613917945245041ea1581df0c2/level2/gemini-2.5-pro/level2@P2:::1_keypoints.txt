- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two correlated chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining outputs to cancel leading-order bias.
- Uses correlated noise between chains to control variance inflation from extrapolation.
- Novelty/significance: a simple, general-purpose wrapper to get higher-order accuracy without designing complex integrators, and easily parallelizable.
- Theoretical contributions: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.
- Performance guarantee: improves optimal MSE rate from O(K⁻²/³) for SGLD to O(K⁻⁴/⁵) for SGRRLD.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear algorithm description and explanation of step-size decay choices.
- Experiments (synthetic): linear Gaussian tests validate bias/MSE reduction and derived convergence rates.
- Experiments (algorithm generality): successfully applies RR extrapolation to SGHMC and competes with higher-order splitting integrators.
- Experiments (real data): MovieLens matrix factorization shows faster wall-clock convergence than SGLD, demonstrating practical utility.
- Main weakness—computational cost: SGRRLD requires three gradient evaluations per γ-step (coarse + two fine), increasing total compute despite parallelizability.
- Suggestion: provide explicit comparisons of SGRRLD vs SGLD with more iterations (e.g., 2K or 3K) to clarify compute vs statistical efficiency.
- Presentation caveat: key technical assumptions are deferred to the supplement; include a brief summary of assumptions in the main text for clarity.
- Minor point: only uses γ and γ/2; a short discussion of other step-size ratios (e.g., γ/3) and implications would improve completeness.
