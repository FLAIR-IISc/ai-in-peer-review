## Review of "Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Markov Chain Monte Carlo"

This paper introduces Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), a novel SG-MCMC algorithm designed to reduce bias compared to standard SGLD. The core idea is to apply the Richardson-Romberg extrapolation technique – running two parallel chains with different step sizes – to the SGLD framework. The authors provide theoretical analysis demonstrating asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE, claiming improved convergence rates over SGLD. These theoretical results are supported by experiments on both synthetic Gaussian models and a real-world matrix factorization task.

The paper addresses a significant challenge in SG-MCMC: bias reduction. The proposed approach is well-motivated, building on established numerical analysis techniques (Richardson-Romberg) and adapting them to the stochastic gradient setting. The theoretical analysis is a key strength, providing rigorous justification for the expected improvements. The experiments are well-designed, showcasing the benefits of SGRRLD in both controlled synthetic scenarios and a more complex, practical application. The inclusion of comparisons to SGHMC and its RR extrapolation variant further strengthens the evaluation.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of the correlated Brownian motion requirement (equation 8). While the authors mention it, the computational cost and implementation complexity of enforcing this correlation aren't fully addressed.  Furthermore, the sensitivity of performance to the choice of step sizes (γ) could be explored in more detail, particularly in the context of adaptive step size strategies. The paper also assumes a relatively standard setting for the mini-batch size, and exploring the impact of varying this parameter alongside the step size would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a novel contribution.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The rigorous theoretical analysis provides strong support for the proposed method.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Experiments demonstrate improved performance compared to SGLD and other baselines.
*   **Significance:** Reducing bias in SG-MCMC is a crucial step towards more accurate Bayesian inference in large-scale applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Implementation Complexity:** The correlated Brownian motion requirement adds complexity.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance may be sensitive to the choice of step sizes, requiring careful tuning.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of SG-MCMC. The theoretical and empirical results are compelling. While the implementation complexity and parameter sensitivity warrant consideration, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above. The authors should elaborate on the practical considerations of implementing the correlated Brownian motion and provide further insights into the sensitivity of the algorithm to parameter choices.
