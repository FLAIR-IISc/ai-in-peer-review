- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel SG-MCMC algorithm applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while controlling variance.  
- Implements SGRRLD by running two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes in parallel and combining their outputs.  
- Theoretical analysis shows asymptotic consistency and a central limit theorem for SGRRLD.  
- Derives bias O(γ²) and mean squared error O(γ⁴) bounds for the method.  
- Demonstrates convergence rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵), improving over SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³) and matching higher-order integrator accuracy with first-order schemes.  
- Provides formal proofs and thorough theoretical treatment in the supplementary material.  
- Empirical results on synthetic Gaussian models and large-scale matrix factorization (MovieLens) support theoretical claims and outperform SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and presents precise mathematical notation and algorithm descriptions (Eqs. 4–6).  
- Supplementary material includes implementation details that aid reproducibility.  
- Main strength is theoretical bias reduction without needing more complex integrators, with a parallel design suited to distributed computing.  
- Clearly differentiates from related work (e.g., Chen et al. 2015) by achieving comparable accuracy with simpler implementation.  
- Improvement needed: clarify computational trade-offs and provide wall-clock time comparisons to higher-order methods for practical assessment.  
- Improvement needed: strengthen the “generic” claim by detailing technical conditions (ergodicity, weak error) required to apply the technique to other SG-MCMC algorithms.  
- Overall significance: technically sound, practically useful for large-scale Bayesian inference, and well-positioned for NIPS 2016.
