Summary: Proposes SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD to reduce bias while controlling variance; provides asymptotic consistency, CLT, non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds, and improved convergence rates.

Novelty: Introduces a new SG-MCMC algorithm targeting bias reduction not previously explored.

Theoretical analysis: Provides thorough theory including consistency, a central limit theorem, and bias/MSE bounds.

Experimental results: Empirically demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real datasets.

Scalability: Algorithm is designed to scale to large-scale problems.

Complexity: Algorithm and theoretical analysis are complex and may be hard for non-experts to follow.

Computational cost: Requires running two chains in parallel, increasing computational burden.

Hyperparameter tuning: Involves several hyperparameters that require time-consuming tuning.

Originality: First application of Richardson-Romberg extrapolation in the SG-MCMC/SGLD context.

Significance: Advances efficient and scalable SG-MCMC methods for large-scale Bayesian inference.

Quality: Well-written paper with solid theoretical and experimental support.

Clarity: Well-organized and clearly explained, though complexity may limit accessibility.

Argument for acceptance — novelty: Presents a novel method addressing a key bias issue.

Argument for acceptance — validation: Backed by strong theoretical results and empirical evidence.

Argument for acceptance — applicability: Designed to be scalable to real-world large problems.

Argument against acceptance — accessibility: High complexity may limit uptake by practitioners.

Argument against acceptance — cost: Parallel-chain requirement raises computational expenses.

Argument against acceptance — scope: Paper could use a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential applications.
