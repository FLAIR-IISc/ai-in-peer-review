- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias vs. standard SGLD.
- Core idea: run two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes and combine results; conceptually sound and builds on numerical analysis.
- Provides rigorous theoretical results: consistency, a central limit theorem, and bounds on bias and MSE.
- Extends prior SGLD and RR extrapolation work (Welling & Teh; Talay & Tubaro) into the SG-MCMC setting.
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic Gaussians and MovieLens matrix factorization, showing consistent improvements over SGLD.
- In some cases achieves accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators with potential reduced cost via parallelization.
- Presentation suffers from dense notation that sometimes obscures derivations and readability.
- Paper could better connect theoretical convergence rates to the practical experimental gains observed.
- Experiments lack deeper sensitivity analysis (step-size selection and the impact of correlation parameter Σ).
- No quantitative scalability or speedup analysis comparing SGRRLD to SGLD on larger compute infrastructures.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand sensitivity experiments, and include scalability analysis.
