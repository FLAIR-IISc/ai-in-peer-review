- Proposes SGRRLD, a novel sampling algorithm using Richardson-Romberg extrapolation that runs two SG-MCMC chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Aims to reduce bias of SG-MCMC while keeping variance at reasonable levels.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds.  
- Shows SGRRLD attains higher convergence rates than SG-MCMC methods with first-order integrators, both finite-time and asymptotically.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real data (linear Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization) comparing to SGLD and SGHMC.  
- Experiments demonstrate reduced bias and improved convergence rates for SGRRLD.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated introduction to the bias problem in SG-MCMC.  
- Strength: rigorous and comprehensive theoretical treatment.  
- Strength: empirical results supporting theoretical claims.  
- Strength: clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in SG-MCMC and MCMC theory, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: some theoretical results (e.g., CLT) are stated without proof.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to a few specific examples, leaving generalization uncertain.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-motivated algorithm with strong theory and supporting experiments and potential high impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited accessibility, incomplete presentation of proofs, and narrow experimental scope.
