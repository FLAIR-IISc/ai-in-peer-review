1. Introduces SGRRLD: run two parallel SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combine via Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias.  
2. Theoretical claims: asymptotic bias O(γ²), variance O(γ⁴); non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds yield convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD’s O(K⁻²/³).  
3. Method is naturally parallelizable since the two chains can be executed independently.  
4. Empirical validation: synthetic and matrix factorization experiments show practical advantages over SGLD and SGHMC.  
5. Novelty: clever, apparently first application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC.  
6. Theory quality: rigorous CLT and non-asymptotic bounds, well-derived and motivated.  
7. Presentation: clear writing, good organization, and sufficient supplementary material for reproducibility.  
8. Weakness — comparisons: lacks direct comparison to actual higher-order integrators or their theoretical performance limits.  
9. Weakness — mini-batch constraint: no clear theoretical justification for requiring identical mini-batch sizes across the two chains.  
10. Weakness — correlation claim: insufficient intuitive explanation why perfect correlation between the two sequences minimizes variance (only cites prior work).  
11. Weakness — apparent paradox: needs clarification how a first-order integrator attains higher convergence rates than SGLD when higher-order integrators are known to improve rates.  
12. Weakness — parallel efficiency: no theoretical analysis of speedup from parallelizing the two chains for large-scale distributed systems.  
13. Overall recommendation: significant and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above issues.
