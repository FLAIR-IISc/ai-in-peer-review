- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson–Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two SGLD chains (step sizes γ and γ/2) and combining ergodic averages.  
- Uses correlated Brownian increments and proves perfect correlation minimizes asymptotic variance.  
- Theoretical contributions: consistency and CLT for the RR estimator; improved non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds versus SGLD; optimal step-size schedules.  
- Constant step-size rates: bias O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)), MSE O(γ^4 + 1/(Kγ)), improving on SGLD’s O(γ + 1/(Kγ)) and O(γ^2 + 1/(Kγ)).  
- Decreasing step-size rates: optimal MSE O(K^{-4/5}) at α=1/5 for SGRRLD versus O(K^{-2/3}) for SGLD.  
- Empirical results: improved posterior variance estimation on linear Gaussian model, matches higher-order integrators for SGHMC, better test RMSE/time on MovieLens matrix factorization.  
- Method is simple, generic, parallelizable, and attains higher-order accuracy with first-order updates.  
- Novelty: first adaptation of RR extrapolation to stochastic-gradient MCMC with rigorous treatment of correlated noise.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis (including variance characterization) that aligns with experiments.  
- Weakness: relies on strong smoothness/regularity assumptions (e.g., high-order C^9 conditions and Poisson equation existence) with limited practical guidance.  
- Weakness: requires gradient estimators with identical distributions across chains; impact of differing minibatch sizes or shared vs independent indices not explored.  
- Weakness: variance control hinges on perfect noise correlation; no empirical ablation comparing independent vs correlated Brownian increments.  
- Weakness: computational-budget fairness concerns—parallelization assumption may not hold in single-core or memory-bound settings; no sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: baseline scope limited—missing comparisons to higher-order Langevin discretizations and variance-reduced SG-MCMC methods.  
- Clarity: manuscript is generally clear but has minor notational typos and could better explain RR weighting and how to construct correlated Gaussian increments.  
- Essential suggestions: clarify precise assumptions for Poisson/CLT (with concrete examples), add correlated vs independent noise ablation, and specify minibatch selection practice.  
- Nice-to-have suggestions: single-core wall-clock experiments, add higher-order/variance-reduced baselines, and provide step-size tuning guidance and pseudocode.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general technique with theoretical improvements, strong theory–experiment match, and easy parallel implementation.  
- Cons against acceptance: strong assumptions, limited analysis of minibatch/noise choices and single-core scenarios, and narrower baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, carefully analyzed contribution likely of interest to the SG-MCMC community; recommend acceptance pending clarified assumptions and small empirical ablations.
