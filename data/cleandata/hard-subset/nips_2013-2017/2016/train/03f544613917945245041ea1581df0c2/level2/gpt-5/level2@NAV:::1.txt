Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes Stochastic Gradient Richardson-Romberg Langevin Dynamics (SGRRLD), which applies Richardson-Romberg (RR) extrapolation to SG-MCMC, instantiated on SGLD and SGHMC. The core claim is that extrapolating two coupled discretizations with step sizes γ and γ/2—and using correlated Brownian increments—reduces bias to O(γ^2), variance to O(γ^4), and improves finite-time MSE rate to O(K^-4/5), matching high-order integrators (e.g., Chen et al., NIPS’15) while using only first-order updates. Theoretical results include consistency, a CLT, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds. Empirically, on a Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization (MovieLens), SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in bias/MSE and test RMSE at matched wall-clock via parallel chains. The approach builds on classical RR for diffusions (Talay & Tubaro; Lemaire et al.) and recent SG-MCMC analyses (Teh et al.; Ma et al.; Chen et al.).

Quality: The analysis is careful, with clear statements of asymptotic and non-asymptotic results and a principled variance-control via correlated noise (optimal Σ = I). Assumptions (smoothness, Poisson equation solution) are strong but standard in this line of work. Empirical support is convincing for the tested settings, though primarily Gaussian/posterior-linear and matrix factorization.

Clarity: The exposition is generally clear and well organized. The reliance on supplementary material for proofs/pseudocode is acceptable for NIPS, though the paper would benefit from a concise algorithm box and a brief practical guide on coupling (Gaussian and gradient) choices.

Originality: Applying RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC with an explicit coupling strategy is novel in this community and distinct from prior high-order integrator designs.

Significance: If broadly applicable, the method offers a simple, parallelizable route to improved accuracy in SG-MCMC without complex integrators. This is likely to be adopted and extended.

Limitations and suggestions:
- Practical coupling: while Brownian correlation is specified, guidance and ablations on correlating mini-batch gradients (e.g., shared indices across chains) would strengthen the variance story.
- Resource fairness: equal wall-clock assumes two workers; discuss single-core settings and report CPU-time-normalized results.
- Scope: add experiments on non-convex posteriors (e.g., Bayesian neural nets) and with decreasing step sizes at α=1/5 to validate the CLT regime.
- Compare to preconditioned/thermostat methods (e.g., pSGLD, SGNHT) under RR, and include ablations with/without coupling (Σ≠I).
- Discuss recursive/multilevel RR empirically, or clarify when single-level suffices.
- Release code for reproducibility.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually simple, broadly applicable idea with solid theory.
- Clear gains over SGLD; matches high-order performance with first-order schemes.
- Parallel-friendly and well-situated within NIPS SG-MCMC literature.

Cons:
- Limited diversity of models; strong smoothness assumptions.
- Coupling and computational cost trade-offs not fully explored.
- Reproducibility would benefit from code and more implementation detail.

Overall: A strong, novel, and well-supported contribution; recommended for acceptance after minor clarifications.
