- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Key insight: dual-chain RR extrapolation lowers bias while keeping variance reasonable using only first-order integrators.  
- Theoretical claim: SGRRLD achieves O(K^{-4/5}) MSE convergence vs SGLD’s O(K^{-2/3}).  
- Theorem 1: establishes a central limit theorem and identifies minimal variance achieved with correlated Brownian increments.  
- Theorem 2: provides non-asymptotic bias and MSE bounds and analyzes optimal step-size choices.  
- Analysis builds on prior SG-MCMC and RR work (e.g., Chen et al. 2015; Talay & Tubaro 1990) and is technically sound.  
- Experiments on synthetic Gaussian models validate improved bias and MSE and match predicted rates.  
- Extensions: SGRRHMC and large-scale matrix factorization on MovieLens show practical utility.  
- Parallelization: dual-chain structure is naturally parallelizable, keeping wall-clock cost similar despite two chains.  
- Novelty and impact: clever application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC, improving accuracy without higher-order integrators.  
- Practicality: method is simple to implement and applicable to SG-MCMC algorithms meeting technical conditions.  
- Minor concerns: relies on smoothness and gradient-distribution assumptions, requires equal mini-batch sizes for both chains, and the two-chain overhead could be quantified more precisely.  
- Overall verdict: rigorous theory, strong experiments, practical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
