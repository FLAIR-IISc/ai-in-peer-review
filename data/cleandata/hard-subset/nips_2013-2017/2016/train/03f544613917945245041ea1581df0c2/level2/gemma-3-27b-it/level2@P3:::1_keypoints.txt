- Introduces SGRRLD: applies Richardson-Romberg extrapolation using two parallel SGLD chains with different step sizes to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical analysis: consistency, central limit theorem, bias and MSE bounds, and claimed improved convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on synthetic Gaussian models and real matrix factorization tasks showing performance gains.  
- Compares to related methods: includes comparisons to SGHMC and a higher-order integrator variant (SGRRHMC).  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and strong connection to established Richardson-Romberg technique.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical development establishing key algorithmic properties.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Practical concern: limited discussion of implementation trade-offs for RR extrapolation in SG-MCMC, especially regarding correlated Brownian motions.  
- Parallelization concern: unclear whether maintaining correlated Brownian motions introduces significant communication overhead in distributed settings.  
- Theoretical limitation: analysis depends on smoothness and gradient-estimator assumptions; robustness to assumption violations is not discussed.  
- Theorem clarity issue: constant C in Theorem 2 is not characterized practically; needs bounds in problem-specific terms.  
- Experimental limitation: step-size selection appears arbitrary and lacks sensitivity analysis over different schedules.  
- Reproducibility/detail issue: details of the SGHMC-s higher-order integrator are insufficient for fair comparison.  
- Experimental gap: mini-batch size B is largely fixed; its influence on SGRRLD performance is not explored.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel application of Richardson-Romberg to SG-MCMC, rigorous theory, strong experiments, and potential large-scale impact.  
- Rejection arguments: limited practical discussion, potentially restrictive theoretical assumptions, and incomplete experimental exploration.  
- Overall: significant, well-motivated contribution justified for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing practical implementation details and expanded experimental analyses.
