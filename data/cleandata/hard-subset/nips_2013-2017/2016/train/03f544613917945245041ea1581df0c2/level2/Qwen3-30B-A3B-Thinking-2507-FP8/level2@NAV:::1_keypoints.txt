- Proposes SGRRLD, an SG-MCMC extension applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce bias while keeping variance reasonable.  
- Claims improved MSE convergence O(K⁻⁴/⁵) versus SGLD's O(K⁻²/³) by running two parallel chains with different step sizes.  
- Uses correlated Brownian motion to minimize variance, extending RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis: asymptotic consistency, central limit theorem, and non-asymptotic bias/MSE bounds.  
- Correctly situates work within SG-MCMC literature and cites relevant prior art (Welling & Teh, Chen et al., Talay & Tubaro).  
- Experimental validation on synthetic Gaussian model and real-world matrix factorization (ML-1M) supports theory.  
- Empirical results show SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in bias and MSE with comparable computation; ML-1M achieves SGLD accuracy in half the time.  
- Novel contribution: achieves theoretical accuracy of higher-order integrators using only first-order methods via RR extrapolation.  
- Paper is well-organized, reproducible, and candid about limitations (e.g., dominating 1/(Kγ) term for tiny step sizes).  
- Main weakness: limited direct experimental comparison to higher-order integrator methods (e.g., SGHMC-s).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, convincing results, significant contribution warranting acceptance.
