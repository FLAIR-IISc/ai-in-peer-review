- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SG-MCMC by running two parallel SGLD chains with step sizes γ and γ/2 and combining outputs to cancel leading-order bias.  
- Adapts RR extrapolation to stochastic-gradient setting, addressing extra gradient noise complexity.  
- Theoretical results: estimator is asymptotically consistent and satisfies a central limit theorem (Theorem 1).  
- Non-asymptotic bounds: bias and MSE analyzed (Theorem 2), showing improved MSE rate O(K⁻⁴/⁵) vs SGLD's O(K⁻²/³).  
- Achieves higher accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators while using only Euler-Maruyama updates.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic linear Gaussian model and large-scale matrix factorization demonstrates practical gains and applicability to other SG-MCMC methods (e.g., SGHMC).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides precise algorithm description with proofs in the supplement.  
- Novelty: first to combine RR extrapolation with SG-MCMC and to analyze noise correlation between chains to control variance.  
- Significance: simple, implementable, inherently parallel method that can match higher-order integrator accuracy without added integrator complexity.  
- For acceptance: method is elegant, theoretically strong, empirically validated, and practically useful.  
- Main weakness: roughly double computational cost compared to a single SGLD chain (one γ step + two γ/2 steps), relying on parallel resources to match wall-clock time.  
- Suggested experiment: compare against a single SGLD chain with equivalent total computational budget (e.g., twice the iterations) to clarify serial-performance tradeoffs.  
- Additional concern: increased memory footprint from maintaining two simultaneous chains could matter for very large models.
