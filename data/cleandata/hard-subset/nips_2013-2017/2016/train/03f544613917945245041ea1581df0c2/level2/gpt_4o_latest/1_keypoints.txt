- Introduces SGRRLD, applying Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to reduce SG-MCMC bias while maintaining reasonable variance.  
- Theoretically: proves asymptotic consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved bias and MSE convergence rates (asymptotic and non-asymptotic bounds).  
- Achieves accuracy comparable to higher-order integrators using only first-order methods.  
- Designed for parallel/distributed architectures; shows computational advantages on large-scale matrix factorization.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets: SGRRLD outperforms SGLD in bias, MSE, and efficiency.  
- Implementation complexity: may be challenging for practitioners unfamiliar with RR extrapolation or correlated Brownian motions; needs more implementation detail.  
- Limited empirical comparisons: lacks evaluations against other state-of-the-art SG-MCMC methods, especially higher-order integrators.  
- Scalability analysis on distributed systems is insufficient for extremely large datasets.  
- Paper does not explicitly discuss limitations such as hyperparameter sensitivity or overhead from running two chains.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address implementation guidance and broader comparisons.  
- Pro: novel, theoretically rigorous, practically impactful method with comprehensive validation.  
- Con: increased implementation complexity and limited comparisons to advanced SG-MCMC approaches.  
- Overall rating: 8/10 (Strong Accept).
