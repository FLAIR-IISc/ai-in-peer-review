- Proposes SGRRLD: apply Richardson-Romberg extrapolation to SGLD by running two chains with different step sizes and combining outputs to reduce bias.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees including consistency, a central limit theorem, and improved asymptotic and finite-time convergence rates over SGLD.  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and a real-world matrix factorization task demonstrate improved performance and scalability.  
- Novel application of RR extrapolation to SG-MCMC is a significant and original contribution.  
- Theoretical analysis is extensive and appears rigorous, offering a strong foundation for claims.  
- Framing relation to higher-order integrators is insightful, showing higher accuracy with a simpler first-order method.  
- Lacks comparisons to other recent SG-MCMC variants (e.g., SGHMC, variance-reduction methods), limiting practical assessment.  
- Discussion of the chosen correlation structure (Σ = Id) is insufficient and the effect of deviating from it is not fully explored.  
- Practical implementation details and computational overhead of generating correlated Brownian increments are not discussed.  
- Experimental setup omits justification for parameter choices (e.g., step size) and lacks sensitivity analyses.  
- Frequent references to an inaccessible supplementary document prevent full verification of proofs and experiments.  
- Presentation sometimes uses dense mathematical notation without enough intuitive explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method with encouraging experiments and good parallelization potential.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited comparisons to related work, inaccessible supplementary material, and unclear implementation/analysis details.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept—strong idea and theory but needs broader comparisons, available supplementary material, and clearer practical guidance.
