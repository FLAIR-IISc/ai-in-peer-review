This paper tackles a fundamental question regarding the EM algorithm for Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs): are there bad local optima? It provides a negative answer to Srebro’s conjecture, demonstrating the existence of such optima, and, crucially, shows that the EM algorithm, with a seemingly reasonable random initialization, converges to these suboptimal solutions with high probability. This is a bold and significant result. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, and the construction of the problematic GMM is clever. 

**Summary:** The authors demonstrate the existence of bad local optima in the likelihood of GMMs, contradicting a long-standing conjecture. They further prove that standard initialization schemes for the EM algorithm lead to convergence to these suboptimal solutions with high probability, even with well-separated Gaussians.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** This is a highly novel contribution. The existence of bad local optima was an open question, and the analysis showing the EM algorithm’s susceptibility to them is a strong result. The work directly challenges common assumptions about the behavior of EM.
*   **Significance:** The findings are significant. They explain why EM can fail in practice and provide theoretical justification for the need for careful initialization strategies. The high probability of converging to bad optima is particularly alarming.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proofs appear rigorous, and the arguments are well-structured. The use of recent results from gradient descent analysis adds to the credibility.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The motivation is well-explained, and the technical details are presented in a comprehensible manner. The introduction carefully builds the context and explains the importance of the problem.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Practical Implications - Limited Exploration:** While the paper highlights the failure of random initialization, it doesn’t deeply explore *which* initialization schemes are effective. Mentioning existing work on moment-based initialization is good, but a more detailed discussion of their potential and limitations would be beneficial.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The paper is heavily theoretical. While the theoretical results are compelling, some empirical validation—even on synthetic data—to demonstrate the observed behavior of EM and first-order EM would strengthen the paper. Showing the fraction of runs converging to a bad local optimum with different initializations would be insightful.
* **Scope of Applicability:** The analysis focuses on uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussian mixtures. While simplifying assumptions, the extent to which the results generalize to other GMM configurations (e.g., unequal weights, non-spherical covariances) isn’t fully discussed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Highly Novel & Significant Result:** The existence of bad local optima and the analysis of EM's convergence behavior are major theoretical contributions.
*   **Rigorous Analysis:** The proofs are well-developed and supported by relevant literature.
*   **Addresses a Fundamental Problem:** The paper tackles a core issue in GMM estimation that has remained open for years.
*   **Bold Approach:** The authors directly confront a widely held belief, providing a strong counterexample and a compelling analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Lack of Empirical Validation:** The absence of experimental results weakens the impact of the theoretical findings.
*   **Limited Discussion of Mitigation Strategies:** While identifying the problem is crucial, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of potential solutions beyond simply suggesting “careful initialization.”



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a highly original and significant contribution. While the lack of empirical validation and a more detailed discussion of practical implications are weaknesses, the novelty and theoretical rigor of the work are compelling. I believe the paper's strengths far outweigh its weaknesses. The authors have identified a fundamental issue with a widely used algorithm and provided a rigorous analysis of its behavior.

**Recommendation:** Accept. Despite the minor shortcomings, the paper’s novelty and significance warrant acceptance. The program committee should encourage the authors to include empirical results and expand the discussion of practical initialization strategies in a future version.
