- Paper proves existence of bad local maxima in GMM likelihoods and refutes Srebro’s conjecture in the infinite-sample limit.  
- Shows EM and first-order EM, with data-point random initialization, converge to those suboptimal maxima with high probability.  
- Strong theoretical contribution resolving an open problem and challenging a widely held belief.  
- Connects theoretical landscape results to practical algorithmic failure, highlighting importance of initialization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and logically structured.  
- Proofs are rigorous and leverage recent gradient-descent analysis to show the issue is true local maxima, not saddles.  
- Work is highly relevant to the NIPS audience, bridging statistical modeling and optimization.  
- Results are restricted to uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussians, limiting immediate generality.  
- Initialization assumption (drawing initial centers from data) may affect results; other initialization schemes need examination.  
- Practical mitigation strategies (k-means, spectral, moment methods) are only briefly discussed and need expansion.  
- Proofs are complex and require significant background; a high-level intuitive explanation would aid readers.  
- Original and novel construction demonstrating algorithmic failure under practical initialization.  
- Significant implications: explains persistent EM failures and motivates better initialization/alternative methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important theoretical and practical implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves open problem, explains EM failures, links theory to practice, and provides rigorous analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope, scant practical mitigation discussion, and potentially inaccessible proofs.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).  
- Recommendations: discuss generalization to varied weights/covariances, expand analysis of initialization strategies, add intuitive proof overview, and explore alternative initialization schemes.
