- Paper proves existence of bad local maxima in GMM likelihoods, contradicting Srebro's conjecture.  
- Constructs a 3-component isotropic, uniformly weighted Gaussian mixture that has suboptimal local maxima.  
- Shows EM with common random initialization likely converges to these bad local optima.  
- Demonstrates that achieving a good solution may require exponentially many independent EM runs in the number of components.  
- Proves first-order EM does not converge to strict saddle points.  
- Results are technically rigorous with carefully constructed proofs.  
- Novel and significant contribution challenging common assumptions about GMM likelihood landscapes.  
- Algorithmic implications highlight practical limitations of local-search methods without careful initialization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates results within related work.  
- Primary limitation: analysis restricted to uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussians.  
- Unclear practical relevance: frequency of such pathological cases in real datasets is not shown.  
- Theorem 3 requires a somewhat strong stepsize restriction, potentially limiting applicability.  
- Some proofs, especially EM convergence analyses, are dense and could use more intuition or intermediate steps.  
- Reviewer asks whether similar constructions can exist for M=2 and whether exponential dependence on M is improvable.  
- Suggests elaborating how the saddle-point result differs from other high-dimensional optimization work.  
- Recommends discussing strategies to mitigate bad local optima in practice (e.g., better initialization).  
- Requested minor revisions: discuss generalizability, practical impact, and improve proof clarity for Theorems 2–3.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend accept with minor revisions; score 8.5/10.
