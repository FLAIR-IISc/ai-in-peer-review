- Summary: Resolves Srebro's 2007 conjecture by showing population GMM likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3 well-separated components, and EM/first-order methods with random init converge to them with high probability.

- Technical Quality: Paper is technically rigorous with elegant, detailed constructions and solid proofs for Theorems 1–4.

- Originality: Novel counterexample construction that settles a long-standing open question about bad local maxima in GMMs.

- Significance: Results explain practical need for careful initialization and justify method-of-moments approaches, with exponential failure probability for random restarts in M.

- Clarity: Well-written and well-organized; provides strong intuition and accessible proofs for experts.

- Limited scope: Counterexample focuses on uniformly weighted, spherical, unit-variance GMMs; extension to non-uniform or non-spherical cases is not explored in depth.

- Practical implications: Lacks concrete discussion on how large the "arbitrarily bad" gap is in finite-sample settings and required sample sizes.

- Comparison to related work: Could more explicitly contrast the infinite-sample results with finite-sample unbounded local maxima shown by Améndola et al. (2015).

- Overall Assessment: Major theoretical contribution, surprising and well-supported results that advance understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes.

- Recommendation: Strong accept—high-quality, rigorous, and impactful work deserving publication.

- Suggestion 1: Add brief discussion about manifestation of the bad-gap in practical finite-sample scenarios.

- Suggestion 2: In the introduction, more explicitly contrast the infinite-sample results with Améndola et al. (2015)'s finite-sample findings.

- Suggestion 3: Briefly mention potential extensions to non-spherical Gaussians, even if left for future work.
