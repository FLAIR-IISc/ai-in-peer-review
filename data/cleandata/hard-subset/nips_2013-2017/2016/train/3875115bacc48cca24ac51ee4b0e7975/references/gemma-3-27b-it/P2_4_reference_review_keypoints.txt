- Paper disproves Srebro’s conjecture by constructing a 3-component GMM with bad local maxima in the likelihood.
- Constructed bad local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Randomly initialized EM converges to these suboptimal points with high probability.
- First-order EM does not converge to strict saddle points, implying bad local optima are the main barrier.
- Strong theoretical contribution resolving a long-standing open problem about GMM likelihood landscapes.
- Results have direct algorithmic implications, highlighting the need for better initialization strategies for EM.
- Proofs are rigorous and well-structured, connecting likelihood geometry to EM behavior.
- Paper is well-motivated within the GMM literature and generally clearly written.
- Results are limited to uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussian mixtures, restricting generality.
- The counterexample construction is complex and would benefit from more intuitive explanation and visualizations.
- The practical severity of bad optima is not quantified, and empirical validation is lacking.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 10/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical breakthrough, rigorous analysis, practical implications, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scope and lack of empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestions to extend scope and add empirical validation in future work.
