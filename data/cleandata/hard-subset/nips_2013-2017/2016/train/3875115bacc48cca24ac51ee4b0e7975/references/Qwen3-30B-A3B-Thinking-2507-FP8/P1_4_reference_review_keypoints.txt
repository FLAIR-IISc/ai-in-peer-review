- Paper resolves Srebro's conjecture by constructing 3-component 1D GMMs with arbitrarily bad local maxima of the population likelihood.
- Authors show EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these suboptimal points with exponentially high probability.
- First-order EM provably avoids strict saddle points, indicating failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Technical contributions are rigorous with constructive proofs and strong probabilistic analysis.
- The recursive clustering construction and proof techniques are novel and elegantly address an open problem.
- Results have significant practical implications: random initialization for EM is insufficient even for well-separated mixtures.
- The paper is well-written, organized, and provides helpful geometric intuition for the main construction.
- Scope is limited to uniform mixtures of isotropic Gaussians with fixed variance, leaving extension to non-uniform or full-covariance cases unaddressed.
- Analysis is at the population level; finite-sample behavior and how failure probability scales with sample size are not detailed.
- The paper focuses on EM and first-order EM and does not analyze whether other optimizers (e.g., SGD) might avoid bad local maxima.
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper makes a significant theoretical contribution with clear algorithmic consequences.
- Suggested improvements: briefly discuss extensions to non-uniform/non-isotropic GMMs and relate results to finite-sample behavior and alternative optimization methods.
