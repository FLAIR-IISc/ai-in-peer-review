- Resolves Srebro's (2007) conjecture by constructing a counterexample: uniform mixture of M ≥ 3 well-separated isotropic Gaussians has population log-likelihood with arbitrarily bad local maxima.
- Shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with exponentially high probability in M.
- Demonstrates failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points; first-order EM avoids strict saddles with probability one.
- The counterexample and proofs are rigorous, technically sound, and employ novel techniques potentially useful for other non-convex problems.
- Theoretical significance is high: settles a long-standing open question in GMM theory.
- Algorithmic insight is strong: random initialization is insufficient even in well-specified, idealized settings.
- Paper treats structural likelihood properties, EM convergence behavior, and nature of critical points comprehensively and coherently.
- Well-organized and clearly written; main text accessible with technical details in the appendix.
- Original contribution: novel construction for M ≥ 3 and original analysis of EM behavior under random initialization.
- Significant implications for both theory and practice in statistical learning and non-convex optimization.
- Limitation: focuses only on uniform, isotropic mixtures with fixed variance, leaving extension to more general GMMs open.
- Limitation: counterexample constructed in d = 1 and paper does not fully address scaling with dimension.
- Limitation: lacks concrete, detailed alternatives to random initialization (e.g., implementation/cost of proposed pilot estimators).
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; paper is a high-quality, important theoretical contribution.
