- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture: shows population log-likelihood for uniformly weighted spherical GMMs can have spurious local maxima even in benign settings.
- Theorem 1: for any M ≥ 3 in d=1 there are well-separated unit-variance GMMs with local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Theorems 2–3: EM and first-order EM with random initialization (samples from the mixture) converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−cM unless repeated exponentially many times.
- Theorem 4: first-order EM with constant stepsize a.s. avoids strict saddles, implying failures stem from poor local maxima rather than saddle points.
- Technique: constructs hierarchical cluster mixtures that create basins of attraction for suboptimal configurations and uses dynamical-systems arguments to rule out saddle convergence.
- Relation to prior work: complements positive guarantees (e.g., Dasgupta–Schulman) by showing naive random init fails at the population level and connects to strict-saddle literature (Lee et al., Panageas–Piliouras).
- Strength: clear, decisive negative result resolving a long-standing conjecture with a simple d=1, M≥3 construction.
- Strength: strong algorithmic relevance—gives exponential failure probability for common initialization schemes and an elegant recursive hierarchical explanation.
- Strength: clear distinction between population and sample settings and precise claim statements with high-level proof ideas.
- Broader implication: underscores critical role of initialization for likelihood methods and motivates hybrid pipelines (e.g., MoM + EM).
- Weakness: no finite-sample rates provided—needs a theorem quantifying n required (as function of M, separation, d) to preserve bad local maxima.
- Weakness: “well-separated” defined as Ω(√d) but paper should quantify how the likelihood gap Cgap scales with separations and state minimal pairwise separation needed.
- Weakness: focuses on equal weights and known unit variance—unclear which parts extend to unequal/unknown variances or weights.
- Weakness: Theorem 4 omits precise technical conditions—need explicit assumptions on measurability, absolute continuity of init, invertibility/Lipschitz of update, and how stepsize s∈(0,1) relates to L’s curvature.
- Weakness: M=2 case left open—either a partial positive result or clearer discussion of obstacles would be valuable.
- Weakness: lacks practical validation—small 1D/2D experiments demonstrating convergence to suboptimal basins would concretize algorithmic consequences.
- Questions: request explicit finite-sample bounds and dependencies on n, M, separation, and d.
- Questions: ask sensitivity of Theorems 2–3 to initialization distribution (e.g., k-means++ seeding, perturbations, uniform box).
- Questions: ask if hierarchical construction yields similar failures in higher dimensions with grid or tree-structured center arrangements.
- Questions: ask whether diminishing stepsize for first-order EM changes strict-saddle non-convergence or failure outcomes.
- Minor comments: add a figure for M=3 construction and basins, standardize references, define “bad critical point” and handle permutation symmetries, and state equivalence constant in eq. (6).
- Significance: original and significant—shifts understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes and limits of EM with random initialization.
- Recommendation: strong accept, pending clarifications on finite-sample discussion and Theorem 4 technical conditions.
