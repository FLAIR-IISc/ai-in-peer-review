- Paper studies population log-likelihood landscape of equal-weight, spherical GMMs and EM/first-order EM under random initialization.
- Resolves Srebro (2007) by constructing 1D, well-separated GMMs with arbitrarily bad suboptimal local maxima (Theorem 1).
- Shows EM and first-order EM with i.i.d. center initialization converge to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^(−cM) (Theorems 2–3).
- Proves first-order EM avoids strict saddles w.p.1, attributing failures to bad local maxima rather than saddles (Theorem 4).
- Complements positive local-convergence and alternative-method results in literature and connects to strict-saddle avoidance work.
- Strength: clean, low-dimensional equal-weight isotropic construction demonstrating bad population-level local maxima.
- Strength: strong, quantified algorithmic consequences—exponentially many random restarts needed to succeed with constant probability.
- Strength: intuitive hierarchical clustering and trapping argument with convincing occupancy (balls-into-bins) calculation.
- Strength: timely strict-saddle avoidance result that sharpens failure-mode understanding.
- Strength: generally clear writing with helpful M = 3 intuition.
- Weakness: results limited to equal weights and equal, isotropic covariances with known variance; robustness to unequal weights is unclear.
- Weakness: no analysis for mildly anisotropic or unequal (but known) covariances.
- Weakness: higher-dimensional mean configurations are only treated by embedding the 1D construction; broader high-D cases need discussion.
- Weakness: “well-separated” defined qualitatively; explicit quantitative separation constants and scaling should be stated.
- Weakness: finite-sample claims deferred to standard arguments; lacks a concrete finite-sample perturbation theorem relating n, d, M, separation.
- Weakness: Theorem 4 needs clarification on step-size conditions, diffeomorphism/local invertibility, and regularity assumptions used.
- Weakness: practical implications limited by focusing on one random initialization; impact of common seedings (k-means++, moment-based) is not analyzed.
- Weakness: no empirical illustrations demonstrating trapping or exponential dependence on M.
- Quality: technically plausible but would benefit from precise quantitative bounds on likelihood gaps and explicit trapping inequalities.
- Clarity: well-organized but needs fixed citations, explicit separation requirements, precise neighborhood definitions, and upfront assumptions for Theorem 4.
- Originality: novel and important—overturns a conjecture and provides sharp algorithmic consequences.
- Significance: high; informs theory and practice about EM failures even in idealized settings and motivates better initialization/global methods.
- Suggestions: add quantitative constants for separations and likelihood gaps.
- Suggestions: provide a finite-sample theorem with explicit n and probability bounds.
- Suggestions: discuss robustness to unequal weights/covariances and broader high-D constructions.
- Suggestions: clarify technical underpinnings of Theorem 4 (invertibility, regularity) and include brief synthetic experiments.
- Pros: resolves conjecture, strong quantitative implications, simple construction, advances landscape understanding.
- Cons: narrow assumptions, finite-sample and practical seeding gaps, technical details for Theorem 4 need sharpening, no experiments.
- Recommendation: Accept (strong). Confidence: High.
