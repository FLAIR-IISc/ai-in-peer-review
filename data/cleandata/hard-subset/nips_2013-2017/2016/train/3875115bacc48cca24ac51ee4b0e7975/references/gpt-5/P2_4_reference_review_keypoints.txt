- Settles Srebro’s conjecture by constructing well-separated, uniformly weighted spherical GMMs with spurious local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum (works in 1D for M ≥ 3).
- Shows EM and first-order EM with standard random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−cM}, so exponentially many restarts are needed for constant success probability.
- Uses stable-manifold/dynamical-systems tools to prove first-order EM a.s. avoids strict saddles, indicating failures come from bad local maxima rather than saddle points.
- Complements and contrasts prior positive EM analyses by demonstrating random initialization often fails even in well-separated, equal-weight, unit-variance settings.
- Adapts insights from nonconvex landscape and saddle-point literature to the GMM likelihood setting.
- Constructs a recursive hierarchical “hard instance” making the exponentially-small success probability sharp and intuitive.
- Theoretical claims are clearly stated at the population level with precise probability statements; Theorem 1’s construction and Theorems 2–3’s trapping arguments are well motivated and rigorous.
- Theorem 4’s strict-saddle avoidance is carefully scoped to first-order EM with step size s ∈ (0,1); extension to EM is noted as conjectural.
- Limitations: analysis is population-level only; assumes equal weights and identical covariances; main construction in 1D; M = 2 case unresolved.
- Finite-sample analogues are asserted to follow via standard empirical-process arguments but need a precise outline of convergence mode and sample complexity.
- Paper is well organized with consistent notation and good intuition, though “well-separated” constant ranges and some corrupted citations should be fixed.
- Recommended additions: a concrete parameterized example, a small finite-sample simulation of EM trajectories, and clarification of how data-based random initialization maps to the population setting.
- Original contribution: simple 1D counterexample for M ≥ 3 that resolves a long-standing open question.
- Novel algorithmic negative result: natural random restarts for EM can fail with exponentially small success probability on a natural family of mixtures.
- Tailored strict-saddle non-convergence argument is a novel application of dynamical-systems ideas to GMM likelihoods.
- Significant impact: alters community understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes and provides rigorous justification for careful initialization methods.
- Techniques for hard-instance construction and basin analysis are likely to influence future nonconvex likelihood and initialization research.
- Pros: resolves an open problem, provides strong negative algorithmic results with explicit probabilities, clarifies saddle vs local maxima role, and offers clear exposition.
- Cons: population focus, no empirical experiments, limited model scope (equal weights/unit covariances), and unresolved M = 2 landscape.
- Recommendation: Accept; paper is strong theoretically though adding finite-sample discussion and an illustrative experiment would strengthen it further (reviewer confidence: high).
