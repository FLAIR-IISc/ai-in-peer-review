- Paper shows existence of bad local maxima in GMM likelihoods, even for well-separated, isotropic, uniformly weighted components.
- Disproves Srebro (2007) conjecture about absence of bad local maxima.
- Standard EM and its first-order variant with common initialization schemes are likely to converge to these suboptimal points.
- Theoretical analysis focuses on the population log-likelihood and introduces techniques useful for future work.
- Important problem with strong algorithmic and practical implications for GMM estimation.
- Negative result is significant: local optima can be problematic in seemingly simple GMM settings.
- Strong theoretical foundation and seemingly rigorous, well-structured proofs.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with good contextualization in prior work.
- Analysis is limited to uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussian mixtures, raising questions about generality.
- The constructed bad local maximum may be contrived and its representativeness for real data is unclear.
- Theorem 2 shows exponential dependence on number of components M, which may limit practical relevance.
- Paper is primarily theoretical with limited empirical validation or visualizations to build intuition.
- Quality rated 9/10, clarity 8/10, originality 9/10, significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: disproves key conjecture, explains algorithmic difficulties, rigorous analysis, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow configuration studied, potential lack of representativeness, and sparse empirical support.
- Overall assessment: strong and significant paper; recommendation: Strong Accept with suggested empirical validation and discussion of extensions.
