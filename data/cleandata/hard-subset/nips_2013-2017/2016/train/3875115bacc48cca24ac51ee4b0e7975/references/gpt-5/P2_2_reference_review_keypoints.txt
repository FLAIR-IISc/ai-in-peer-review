- Summary: Constructs 1D GMMs (M≥3) with arbitrarily bad population log-likelihood local maxima, shows EM/first-order EM with random M-point initialization fail with prob. ≥1−e^{-cM}, and that first-order EM avoids strict saddles, implicating bad local maxima as the main failure.

- Strength: Resolves Srebro’s conjecture with explicit counterexamples showing bad local maxima even for equal-weight, spherical, well-separated GMMs.

- Strength: Clear algorithmic consequence that random initialization plus EM/gradient EM fails with exponentially high probability in M.

- Strength: Carefully separates saddle vs. local-max issues, proving first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles.

- Strength: Intuitive, recursive cluster/mini-cluster constructions and proof ideas likely useful beyond GMMs.

- Weakness: Analysis is population-level only; no explicit finite-sample bounds or sample complexity are provided.

- Weakness: Main counterexample is 1D and requires M≥3; implications for M=2 and practical prevalence of such adversarial structures are not demonstrated.

- Weakness: Negative results target only the “pick M data points uniformly” initialization; alternative practical initializations are not analyzed.

- Weakness: Technical conditions (precise “well-separated” scaling/constants and dimension dependence of failure probability) are not clearly stated in the main text.

- Quality: Theoretical claims are coherent and proofs appear sound (deferred to appendix); main quality gap is lack of finite-sample versions and empirical validation.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized with good intuition (e.g., M=3 picture); minor reference formatting issues and could benefit from a figure and finite-sample corollary.

- Originality: High — novel explicit counterexamples resolving a known conjecture and new exponential-failure result for random initialization.

- Significance: High theoretical impact for understanding EM’s landscape; practical impact is indirect but highlights the importance of initialization.

- Suggestion: Add a finite-sample corollary specifying sample-size conditions for empirical likelihood to inherit bad local maxima.

- Suggestion: Analyze whether alternative initializations (k-means++, spectral/moment-based, overfitting+pruning) avoid the constructed traps.

- Suggestion: State explicit constants/rates for “well-separated” and discuss extension to higher dimensions without inflating separation.

- Suggestion: Include empirical simulations demonstrating EM converging to the constructed bad local maxima and success-probability dependence on M.

- Arguments for acceptance: Resolves a known conjecture, gives strong algorithmic consequences for EM, and advances conceptual understanding of nonconvex likelihood landscapes.

- Arguments against acceptance: Population-only results with no finite-sample theorems or experiments; adversarial 1D constructions with unknown practical prevalence; limited discussion of mitigating initializations.

- Overall recommendation: Lean accept.
