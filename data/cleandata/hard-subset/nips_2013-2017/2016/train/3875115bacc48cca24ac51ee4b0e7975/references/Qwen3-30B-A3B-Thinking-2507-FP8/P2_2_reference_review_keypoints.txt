- Paper disproves Srebro (2007) by constructing a counterexample: a uniform mixture of three well-separated isotropic Gaussians with arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood.
- Shows EM and its first-order variant with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with exponentially high probability for M ≥ 3.
- Technical contributions are rigorous: detailed construction and proofs for Theorems 1–4 are sound and carefully developed.
- Establishes a clear link between structural properties of the likelihood surface (bad local maxima) and algorithmic failure of random-initialized EM.
- Distinguishes failure due to bad local maxima from saddle-point issues, supported by theoretical results (Theorem 4).
- Novel resolution of a decade-old open question; construction and analysis require sophisticated reasoning beyond prior work.
- Results have strong practical implications, justifying use of smarter initialization (e.g., method of moments) over random restarts.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful geometric intuition for the M=3 construction.
- Would benefit from visualizations of likelihood surfaces to aid clarity.
- Scope is limited: focuses on uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussians, so generalization to unequal weights or covariances is unclear.
- Lacks empirical validation; even small-scale experiments would strengthen and illustrate the theoretical claims.
- Leaves open whether the conjecture holds for M=2 and provides limited insight into why the construction requires M ≥ 3.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution with important algorithmic implications.
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; results are important for both theory and practice.
