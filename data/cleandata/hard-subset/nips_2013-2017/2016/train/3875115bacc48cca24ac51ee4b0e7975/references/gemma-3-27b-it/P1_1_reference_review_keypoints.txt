- Paper shows existence of bad local maxima in GMM likelihoods, even for well-separated three-component mixtures, contradicting Srebro (2007).
- Demonstrates that EM and first-order EM, with high probability and random sample-based initialization, converge to suboptimal solutions.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis using tools from statistical learning and recent gradient-descent landscape results to rule out saddle-point issues.
- Positions the contribution within identifiability, convergence-rate, and nonconvex optimization literature and cites relevant prior work.
- Focuses on uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussians to enable a focused, rigorous treatment of the problem.
- Major strength: surprising and significant theoretical result with clear algorithmic implications for initialization strategies.
- Major strength: thorough, sophisticated proofs and insightful use of recent analytic techniques.
- Major strength: generally clear organization, definitions, and motivation.
- Weakness: paper is highly technical and may be inaccessible to a broad NIPS audience without more intuition.
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate practical impact of identified local maxima.
- Weakness: results are limited to uniformly weighted, isotropic GMMs, leaving generalizability to varied weights/covariances unclear.
- Weakness: initialization scheme description (Section 2.3) could be clearer by presenting the population equivalent earlier.
- Minor issues: several typographical and notation inconsistencies.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add simulations and clarify technical explanations; reviewer confidence 8/10.
