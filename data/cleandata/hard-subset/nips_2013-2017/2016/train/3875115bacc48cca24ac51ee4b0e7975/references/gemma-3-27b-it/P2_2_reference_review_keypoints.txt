- Paper disproves Srebro’s conjectence by constructing a 1D, 3-component GMM with bad local maxima in the population likelihood.
- Shows EM and first-order EM with random data-based initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with high probability.
- Proves achieving reasonable likelihood requires an exponential number of random restarts.
- Argues failures are due to bad local optima rather than saddle points.
- Important theoretical contribution resolving a longstanding open question about GMM likelihood surfaces.
- Rigorous mathematical analysis with detailed proofs and use of recent dynamical-systems results.
- Clear articulation of algorithmic implications, highlighting EM’s sensitivity to initialization.
- Paper is generally well-written, well-organized, and situates results within existing literature.
- Counterexample is narrowly scoped (1D, 3 components), limiting broader generality.
- Analysis assumes uniformly weighted, isotropic Gaussians, reducing applicability to realistic mixtures.
- Proofs are complex and may be inaccessible without strong optimization/statistics background.
- Entirely theoretical work with no empirical experiments to validate practical prevalence of failures.
- Quality rated excellent; technical claims are well-supported.
- Clarity rated good, though proofs are dense and could use more intuition or intermediate steps.
- Originality and significance rated excellent; results likely to influence future GMM research.
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggestion to include empirical validation in future work.
