- Main result: Constructs 1D, well-separated, uniform spherical GMMs (M ≥ 3) whose population likelihood has arbitrarily bad spurious local maxima, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Algorithmic consequence: EM and first-order EM with i.i.d. random initialization converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − exp(−cM) unless exponentially many restarts are used.
- Dynamics insight: First-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures are driven by bad local maxima rather than saddle points.
- Novelty/significance: Negative resolution of a long-standing conjecture in a favorable population setting is new and important for nonconvex MLE landscapes.
- Strength: Clear, sharp structural theorem and an elegant recursive clustering construction yielding exponentially small success probability.
- Strength: Conceptual clarity distinguishing saddle avoidance from attraction to poor local optima.
- Strength: Results are practically relevant, warning against naive random restarts and motivating careful initializations or moment-based pilots.
- Potential generality: Proof techniques may extend to other latent-variable likelihoods and nonconvex objectives.
- Dimensionality concern: Theorem 1 is stated in d = 1; extension to d > 1 and separation scaling (e.g., Ω(√d)) should be stated explicitly.
- Pathology realism: The hard instances use hierarchical multi-scale grouping; authors should discuss how atypical these are in practice or quantify prevalence under generic configurations.
- Population vs sample: Results are at the population level; concrete finite-sample guarantees (n as function of M, separation, d) are missing and would strengthen claims.
- Convergence assumptions: Theorem 4’s claim of convergence for constant step size lacks standard regularity assumptions; more detail is needed on monotonicity or dynamical-system conditions used.
- Initialization scope: Negative results focus on i.i.d. mixture or uniform-from-data inits; practical schemes (k-means++, moment-based) may behave differently and merit analysis or experiments.
- Empirics lacking: No simulations or visualizations are provided; small 1D/2D experiments would aid intuition and corroborate trapping behavior.
- Technical clarity: Some proofs are deferred; the main text should state key lemmas (e.g., trapping lemma) and clarify step-size requirements and regularity.
- Presentation issues: References formatting, typos, and notation inconsistencies (e.g., µ meaning) should be fixed; define “bad local maximum” formally at first use.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—important theoretical contribution but needs finite-sample/empirical support and clearer technical assumptions.
- Suggested improvements: add finite-sample corollary with rates, a figure and small experiments, clarify d > 1 extension and separation scaling, state key lemmas in main body, and discuss practical initializations.
