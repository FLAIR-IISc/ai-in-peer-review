- Studies population log-likelihood landscape for equal-weight, spherical GMMs and behavior of EM and gradient-EM under random initialization.
- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture: for M ≥ 3 there exist spurious local maxima in the population likelihood that can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Shows EM and gradient-EM initialized by sampling centers from the data converge to suboptimal critical points with probability 1 − e−Ω(M), requiring exponentially many restarts for constant success.
- Proves gradient-EM (fixed step size s ∈ (0,1)) converges to critical points and almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are due to bad local maxima.
- Main 1D construction: two true centers close and a third far yields a strict local maximum where two estimates co-locate at the far component and one sits between the close pair.
- Hierarchical “cluster-of-clusters” construction over Θ(log M) levels produces the e−Ω(M) failure probability via likely misallocation of centers to subclusters.
- Strict-saddle avoidance argument leverages dynamical systems and recent nonconvex optimization results while avoiding global smoothness assumptions on L.
- Strength: clean, rigorous resolution of a well-known open conjecture about mixture likelihood landscapes.
- Strength: strong algorithmic consequences showing practical EM/gradient-EM failure under common random initialization.
- Strength: conceptually clear constructions and intuitive geometric explanations for trapping behavior.
- Strength: distinguishes failure due to local maxima from saddle issues; population results plausibly extend to finite samples.
- Strength: good coverage of related work on EM analysis, identifiability, moment methods, and saddle theory.
- Weakness: main structural example is in d = 1 and higher-dimensional formalization is not explicitly stated.
- Weakness: practical initializers like k-means++ are not analyzed; unclear if they also fail under the hierarchical construction.
- Weakness: lacks explicit finite-sample guarantees, sample complexity scaling in M, separation, and d.
- Weakness: quantitative separation thresholds and dependencies of constants (e.g., ξ vs. d,M) are not precisely stated.
- Weakness: characterization of local maxima lacks multiplicative/KL gap description and clarity on isolation vs plateaus.
- Weakness: minimal regularity conditions for strict-saddle avoidance and extensions to varying step sizes/line-search are not fully specified.
- Weakness: no experiments demonstrating the constructions or empirical failure probability scaling with M.
- Quality: technically sound and well-motivated proofs; would benefit from explicit constants and finite-sample translations.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and organized; recommend defining “well-separated” precisely at each usage and noting construction dimension.
- Originality: provides novel negative results overturning a conjecture and introduces a new hierarchical construction and tailored saddle-avoidance argument.
- Significance: important practical and theoretical caution against naive random initialization and motivator for principled initializers or global methods.
- Suggestions: add finite-sample theorems, analyze k-means++ behavior, provide quantitative thresholds, include experiments, and clarify extensions to unequal weights/variances.
- Pros for acceptance: resolves an open problem, strong implications, clear exposition, and likely to stimulate follow-up work.
- Cons against acceptance: limited discussion of practical initializers and finite-sample instantiations, mainly 1D constructions, and no empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept (confidence 4/5).
