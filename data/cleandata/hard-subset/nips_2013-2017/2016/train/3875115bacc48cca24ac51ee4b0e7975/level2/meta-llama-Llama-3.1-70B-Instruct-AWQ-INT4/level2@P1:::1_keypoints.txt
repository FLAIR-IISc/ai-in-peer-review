- Paper analyzes population log-likelihood of Gaussian mixture models and EM behavior in that setting.  
- Main theoretical result: arbitrarily bad local maxima exist in the population GMM log-likelihood, even for uniformly weighted, spherical, well-separated components.  
- Main algorithmic result: randomly initialized EM converges to a bad critical point with high probability.  
- Paper resolves an open question of Srebro (2007) negatively.  
- Writing and organization are clear; introduction and technical sections are easy to follow.  
- Literature review is thorough, covering identifiability, EM convergence, and MLE properties.  
- Technical proofs are rigorous and accompanied by intuitive explanations.  
- Helpful examples and illustrations improve accessibility and understanding.  
- Significant practical implication: highlights the importance of careful initialization for EM.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on practical implications and guidance for initialization strategies.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with alternative GMM estimation methods (e.g., method of moments).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance.
