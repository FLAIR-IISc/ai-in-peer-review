- Main contribution: Proves bad local maxima exist for well-separated spherical GMMs, contradicting Srebro (2007).
- EM behavior: Shows EM and a first-order variant with random initialization often converge to suboptimal local maxima with high probability.
- Saddle point result: First-order EM does not converge to strict saddle points, implying failures are due to local maxima rather than saddles.
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous proofs leveraging recent optimization results (e.g., Lee et al. 2016; Panageas & Piliouras 2016).
- Practical implication: Highlights the need for careful initialization strategies in EM for GMMs.
- Context and novelty: Addresses a natural open question about population likelihood landscapes and connects to prior work in nonconvex estimation.
- Clear presentation: Writing and organization generally make technical content accessible.
- Literature connection: Well-situated within related work on GMMs and nonconvex optimization, with useful contrasts to other problems.
- Weakness — initialization discussion: Limited treatment of alternative initialization schemes and their theoretical/practical trade-offs.
- Weakness — model assumptions: Results are restricted to uniformly weighted, spherical Gaussians, limiting generalizability to other GMM settings.
- Weakness — proof complexity: Proofs are involved and could be clarified or moved to the appendix for readability.
- Minor issues: Several small typographical and grammatical errors need fixing.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a fundamental problem, provides technically sound and surprising results with clear practical relevance.
- Arguments for rejection: Restricted setting and heavy proof complexity may limit broader impact and accessibility.
- Recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand initialization discussion and correct typos.
