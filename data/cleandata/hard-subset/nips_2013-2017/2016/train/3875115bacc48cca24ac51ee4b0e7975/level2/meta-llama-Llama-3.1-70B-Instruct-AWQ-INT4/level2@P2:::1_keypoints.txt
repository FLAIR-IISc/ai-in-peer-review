- Paper proves existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood of equally-weighted, well-separated spherical GMMs for M ≥ 3.  
- Shows log-likelihood of these bad maxima can be arbitrarily worse than any global optimum, resolving Srebro (2007).  
- Proves EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).  
- Establishes the first-order EM variant almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, attributing failure to bad local maxima.  
- Results emphasize necessity of careful initialization for EM even in highly favorable GMM settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear problem setup, results, and discussion of implications.  
- Technical contributions introduce new techniques for reasoning about population log-likelihood structure in non-convex optimization.  
- Reviewer rates quality, clarity, originality, and significance highly (9/10 each in comments).  
- Weakness: lacks a comprehensive comparison with existing GMM literature.  
- Weakness: some technical details and proofs are deferred to appendices, reducing self-contained readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contributions, important practical implications for EM, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparison to prior work and portions of proofs not in the main text.
