- Proves that the population log-likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated Gaussians, refuting Srebro (2007).  
- Shows EM and first-order EM variants, under random initialization, converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability exponentially high in M.  
- Resolves a decade-old open question, giving a definitive characterization of the GMM likelihood surface.  
- Explains the practical necessity of careful initialization or multiple restarts for EM.  
- Provides rigorous, technically sound, and elegant constructions and proofs.  
- Identifies that failures stem from local maxima (not saddle points), connecting to non-convex optimization literature.  
- Is highly original: first to prove arbitrarily bad population-level local optima and quantify high-probability EM failure under standard initialization.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized, with useful geometric intuition preceding formal arguments.  
- Minor weakness: the finite-sample extension is only briefly mentioned; more detail or a specific reference would strengthen the connection.
