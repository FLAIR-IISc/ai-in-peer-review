- Resolves Srebro's 2007 conjecture: shows population GMM likelihood can have bad local maxima for M ≥ 3.  
- Main theoretical result: equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians can produce local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Elegant constructive proof for M=3 demonstrating two centers collapsing at a midpoint and one at a true center forms a local maximum.  
- Shows EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to these bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.  
- Distinguishes bad local maxima from saddle points; Theorem 4 proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles.  
- Well-written, clearly organized paper with rigorous proofs and precise population vs. sample distinctions.  
- Practical implication: EM needs careful initialization beyond naive random starts; motivates better initialization methods.  
- Originality: novel resolution of a long-standing open problem and proper citation of related work.  
- Limitation: finite-sample implications are asserted but not explicitly developed via empirical process arguments.  
- Limitation: no empirical experiments validating the theoretical claims about EM’s behavior in practice.  
- Limitation: unclear if constructed bad local maxima are typical or pathological; lack of detailed geometric conditions and explanation for M=2 differences.  
- Recommendation: accept — theoretical contribution is strong and weaknesses are minor.
