- Addresses whether the population likelihood of GMMs has bad local maxima, resolving Srebro (2007).  
- Main result 1: For M ≥ 3, there exist well-separated, equally-weighted GMMs with arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Main result 2: EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^(−Ω(M)).  
- Theoretical approach is elegant and rigorous, building on recent non-convex optimization advances (e.g., Lee et al., 2016).  
- Shows poor local-search performance is due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points; first-order EM avoids strict saddles almost surely.  
- Situates contributions well relative to prior work on identifiability, EM convergence, and non-convex optimization.  
- Detailed proofs are provided in appendices and theoretically sound.  
- Practical implication: careful initialization is essential even for well-separated mixtures.  
- Limitation: analysis is in the population (infinite-sample) setting, though extendable to finite samples via standard empirical-process arguments.  
- Open question: whether M = 2 behaves differently remains unresolved and valuable to study.  
- Significance: high—resolves a long-standing theoretical question and motivates improved algorithmic initialization.  
- Overall recommendation: well-written, technically sound contribution that should be accepted.
