- Addresses Srebro (2007) open problem on existence of bad local maxima in population GMM likelihoods.  
- Theorem 1: For any M ≥ 3, constructs well-separated, equally-weighted spherical Gaussians whose population log-likelihood has arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Theorem 2 (algorithmic): EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.  
- First-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points.  
- Construction uses a recursive mixture structure yielding exponentially small probability of random-initialization success.  
- Strong connection established between structural likelihood results and algorithmic failure modes of EM.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and mathematically clear with careful exposition.  
- Distinguishes clearly between population-level and finite-sample settings; proofs contain appropriate technical detail.  
- Properly situates work in prior literature and acknowledges related results (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2015).  
- Technical soundness: rigorous proofs and well-supported claims, with careful analysis of limitations.  
- Original and significant: resolves a long-standing conjecture and has important practical implications for EM initialization.  
- Limitations: focuses on equally-weighted, spherical Gaussians with fixed variance; lacks discussion of extensions to more general GMMs, computational cost of exponential restarts, and deeper explanation of the M=2 difference.  
- Recommendation: technically strong, novel, and significant—meets criteria for acceptance at NIPS.
