- Studies local maxima in GMM likelihoods, focusing on equally-weighted, well-separated cases.  
- Proves existence of bad local maxima, resolving Srebro (2007) conjecture.  
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal local maxima with high probability.  
- Argues failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Constructs a specific 3-component GMM to demonstrate bad local optima (technical core).  
- Derives algorithmic consequences emphasizing the need for careful initialization.  
- Analyzes first-order EM and shows lack of convergence to saddle points.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and logically developed.  
- Thoroughly situates work within prior literature on mixtures, identifiability, and EM.  
- Main weakness: almost entirely theoretical with no empirical validation.  
- Limited exploration of practical initialization strategies beyond random initialization.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting illustrative simulations.
