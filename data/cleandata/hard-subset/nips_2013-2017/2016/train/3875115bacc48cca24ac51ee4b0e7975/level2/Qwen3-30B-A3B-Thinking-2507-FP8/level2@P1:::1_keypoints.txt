- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question on existence of bad local maxima in population likelihoods of GMMs with M ≥ 3.
- Main result 1: constructs equally-weighted, well-separated spherical GMMs with local maxima whose log-likelihoods are arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Main result 2: shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 − e^(−Ω(M)).
- Shows first-order EM a.s. does not converge to strict saddle points, so poor performance is due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Novel and significant theoretical contribution resolving a decade-old question about GMM likelihood landscapes.
- Proofs are careful, rigorous, and provide clear geometric intuition (especially for M = 3).
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and properly contextualized within related literature on GMMs and EM.
- Practical implication: random initialization is insufficient—exponentially many restarts may be needed, motivating careful initialization strategies.
- Suggestion: briefly discuss extensions to non-spherical Gaussians or unequal weights to clarify broader applicability.
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS 2016.
