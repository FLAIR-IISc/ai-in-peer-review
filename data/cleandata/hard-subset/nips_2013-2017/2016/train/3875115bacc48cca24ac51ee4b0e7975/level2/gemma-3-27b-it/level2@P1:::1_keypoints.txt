- Main contribution: Proves existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood for GMMs, refuting Srebro (2007).
- Algorithmic implication: Shows EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal solutions with high probability.
- Additional result: First-order EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points, pointing to bad local maxima as the main failure mode.
- Motivation and framing: Addresses a fundamental question about EM reliability and connects to identifiability, convergence rates, and alternative methods.
- Novelty/significance: Resolves a long-standing open problem and gives a theoretical basis for EM’s sensitivity to initialization.
- Presentation quality: Well-written, logically organized, and situates the work clearly in related literature.
- Rigor: Analysis is mathematically rigorous and builds on established techniques in statistical learning theory.
- Practical guidance weakness: Offers limited immediately applicable solutions or concrete initialization strategies beyond advising against random starts.
- Accessibility concern: Heavy theoretical focus may limit accessibility to practitioners; more intuition would help.
- Proof detail concern: Some proofs delegated to appendices lack sufficient detail in the main text for all readers to follow.
- Generality limitation: Results are primarily for uniformly weighted, spherical Gaussians; extensions to more general GMMs are not fully addressed.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, asking for improved practical guidance, added intuition, and expanded proof clarity.
