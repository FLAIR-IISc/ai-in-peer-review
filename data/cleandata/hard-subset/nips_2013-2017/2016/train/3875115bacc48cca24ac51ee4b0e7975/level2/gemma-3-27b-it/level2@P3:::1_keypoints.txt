- Demonstrates existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3, resolving Srebro (2007)’s conjecture.  
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to these suboptimal points with high probability.  
- Proves first-order EM does not converge to strict saddle points, attributing poor performance to bad local maxima instead.  
- Well-motivated work building on extensive prior literature on GMM identifiability, rates, and algorithms.  
- Rigorous mathematical treatment with sound, well-structured proofs (within reviewer constraints).  
- Quantifies probability of converging to suboptimal points rather than only showing possibility.  
- Appropriately acknowledges limitations, e.g., fixing scale parameters to avoid infinite likelihood.  
- Presentation is generally clear, though Section 2’s dense notation may challenge some readers.  
- Core arguments (especially Theorem 1) would benefit from more intuitive explanation alongside formal proofs.  
- Notation is consistent throughout the paper.  
- High originality: directly answers an open problem and offers novel analysis of random initialization effects.  
- Significant practical implications for EM initialization and alternative optimization strategies in GMMs.  
- Results suggest broader relevance to non-convex optimization beyond GMMs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves an important open problem, provides strong theoretical justification for EM challenges, novel convergence results, rigorous writing, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to mathematical density and unclear generalization beyond uniformly weighted, spherical Gaussians.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality theoretical contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Minor suggestions: add more intuition for proofs (Theorem 1), discuss generalization to more complex GMMs, and include a visual of the likelihood surface showing a bad local maximum.
