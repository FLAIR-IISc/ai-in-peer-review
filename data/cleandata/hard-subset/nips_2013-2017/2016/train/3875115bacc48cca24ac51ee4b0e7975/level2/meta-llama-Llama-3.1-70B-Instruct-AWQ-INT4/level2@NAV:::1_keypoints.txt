- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) open problem by showing arbitrarily bad local maxima exist for population log-likelihood of GMMs even for equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated components.
- Main claim: population log-likelihood for M ≥ 3 GMMs has bad local maxima in the idealized setting.
- Main claim: EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with exponentially high probability.
- Main claim: first-order EM with appropriate stepsize avoids strict saddle points almost surely.
- Authors construct a recursive GMM to theoretically demonstrate bad local maxima and EM failure to reach global maxima.
- Detailed analysis and proofs provided for first-order EM behavior and non-convergence to strict saddles.
- Practical implication: careful initialization is required for local search methods even in large-sample, well-behaved models.
- Highlights need to study algorithm robustness when data only approximately follows the generative model.
- Paper shows strong command of relevant literature and theoretical foundations (Srebro 2007, Balakrishnan et al. 2015, etc.).
- Novelty: gives a negative answer to Srebro’s question and introduces new structural techniques for population log-likelihood analysis.
- Paper is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility, including model and algorithm descriptions and proofs.
- Limitation: results are restricted to idealized equally-weighted, spherical, unit-variance, well-separated components.
- Limitation: necessity of at least three components for constructions remains unresolved.
- Constructive criticism: no concrete solution or clear initialization scheme is provided, though method-of-moments pilot estimators are suggested.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, thorough EM analysis, and strong grounding in prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted setting and lack of a practical initialization remedy.
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance despite limitations.
