- Paper proves two main results: population likelihood for Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3 has bad local maxima (even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians), and EM with random initialization converges to bad critical points with high probability; first-order EM avoids strict saddles almost surely.  
- Novelty: resolves the open question of Srebro (2007) and gives a negative answer.  
- Technical soundness: presents rigorous, technical analysis of the population likelihood and EM behavior.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Limited scope: focuses narrowly on a specific Gaussian mixture setting, limiting broader applicability.  
- Lack of experiments: no empirical results to support the theoretical claims.  
- Technical complexity: analysis may be hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Originality: results are original and offer new insights into GMM behavior.  
- Significance: findings impact EM initialization practice and algorithm design for GMMs.  
- Quality of writing: thorough introduction and logically presented technical material.  
- Relevance to NIPS: topic is relevant due to widespread use of GMMs and potential to inspire research.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel resolution of an open problem, implications for algorithm design, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, no experiments, and potential accessibility issues for non-experts.
