- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood for isotropic, known-scale, well-specified models and shows arbitrarily bad local modes can exist even with infinite data.  
- Considers specific configurations (centres split into well-spaced clusters) and proves random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with high probability as k increases.  
- Writing is generally good but contains occasional typos.  
- Main doubt 1: Practical relevance — how likely are the “bad” centre configurations in real problems?  
- Request to assume a distribution on true centres and show probability of bad configurations increases with k or d (Theorem 2 may not resolve this).  
- Main doubt 2: Initialization relevance — random sampling initialization may be unrealistic when data are clearly clustered; questions whether the failure cases are practically likely.  
- Example challenge: with 3 Gaussians (two close, one far) it seems unlikely random init would place centers in the problematic way described.  
- L74–81: unclear purpose of the example; does not convince that Srebro’s conjecture holds for k=2.  
- L233–235: requirement (1) is unclear or unnecessary — why is it needed and does a centre initialized between clusters not move to the correct cluster?  
- L267–273: question the practical importance of showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point versus a local optimum.  
- Clarify phrasing/notation issues: L218 “well-separated constant” is unclear; L225 what does Ω(k) mean?  
- Minor edits requested: L18 add “that the”; L38 use plural “estimates”; L60 rephrase to “under which conditions”; L138 clarify O(d^1/2)-separation meaning; L172 reword or remove redundancy; L177 “equivalent to”; L220 remove duplicate “the”; L245 “coincidence”; L249 add “that”; L308 add “that”.
