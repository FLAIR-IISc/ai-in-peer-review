1) Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known/fixed number of components, weights, and covariances.  
2) Proves three negative results: (i) local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum can exist even for large samples; (ii) randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to such suboptimal critical points; (iii) the same holds for gradient EM.  
3) Reviewer finds the question interesting, the results informative, and worthwhile for understanding EM in practice.  
4) Analysis is restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances); reviewer deems these simplifications acceptable given technicality.  
5) Paper is written for general dimension d but proofs are given for d = 1; reviewer asks authors to state whether extensions to d > 1 are possible or to discuss additional difficulties.  
6) Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too short—lines 288–306 (limits of supremums/maximums over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains) need more detailed justification, as continuity alone does not imply the claimed convergences.  
7) Reviewer did not read supplementary proofs and suggests lengthening the main proof (even at the expense of discussion) to convince readers.  
8) Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; reviewer requests more explanation of how Equation (5) is derived.
