- Paper studies the structure of local maxima of the GMM log-likelihood (equal mixing weights, identity covariance).  
- Result 1: Presents a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: Shows that with random initialization, the probability of avoiding a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k for certain mean configurations.  
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) appear sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea seems reasonable though appendix details were not checked.  
- Novelty/originality: Relates mean configuration to local maxima; the hierarchical grouping insight for k>3 is original and clever.  
- Potential impact/usefulness: Could inspire theoretical work on clustering optima and practical seeding strategies; suggests checking whether Lemma 7 extends to d>1.  
- Clarity/presentation: Results and intuition are well explained with a clear problem setup and interpretations.
