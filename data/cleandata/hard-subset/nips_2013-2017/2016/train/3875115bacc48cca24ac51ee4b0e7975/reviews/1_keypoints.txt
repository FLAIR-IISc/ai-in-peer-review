- Paper analyzes EM convergence for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and shows systematic non-convergence to global maxima.  
- Result: Even with 3 well-separated clusters and infinite samples, EM can get stuck in local optima.  
- Result: For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Result: Gradient EM typically avoids strict saddles, so bad local maxima (not saddles) are the main obstacle.  
- Overall claim: A coherent set of results demonstrating EM need not reach global maxima even with unlimited data.  
- Critique: The results and techniques are unsurprising and follow expected lines.  
- Key example: A 3-component construction (two close, one far) yields a local maximum with centers misallocated; expected when separations ≫ √d.  
- Relation to k-means: The bad examples mirror known failures of Lloyd’s algorithm; random-init failure rates match k-means, motivating strategies like picking k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.  
- Suggestion: Provide a comparison with Kumar–Kannan and contrast EM vs. Lloyd’s guarantees under large separation conditions.
