- Paper answers Srebro (2007) question in the negative by constructing a general class of counterexamples.  
- Authors outline likelihood-maximizing schemes for GMMs, including EM and gradient EM.  
- Paper addresses initialization choices and presents a common random initialization.  
- Primary theorems present the constructed counterexamples to Srebro’s question.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.  
- Provides intuition and a detailed proof for a simple case (k=3, d=1).  
- Manuscript is clear, well written, and balances space with intuitive explanation.  
- Results are important for understanding non-convex optimization even in the asymptotic limit.  
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material and requests estimates of constants in the probability bounds.  
- Knowing the constants is important to identify the phase transition and when bad-solution convergence appears for realistic k.  
- If constants are complex or unclear, supporting simulations should be provided to demonstrate the phenomenon.  
- The lack of constants/simulations does not detract from the paper’s novelty or impact.
