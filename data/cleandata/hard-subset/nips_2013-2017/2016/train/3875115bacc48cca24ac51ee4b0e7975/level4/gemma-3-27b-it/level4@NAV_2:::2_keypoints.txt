- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian-mixture means with known component count, weights, and covariances.  
- Presents three negative results: (1) with infinite samples, likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- (2) EM with random initialization converges with high probability to a suboptimal critical point.  
- (3) A first-order variant of EM exhibits the same undesirable convergence behavior.  
- The question is interesting and significant.  
- Authors focus on a simplified model (uniform weights, identity covariances, known k) to obtain rigorous proofs; this simplification is acceptable.  
- Results are insightful and potentially valuable for understanding EM’s practical behavior.  
- Although results are stated for general dimension d, proofs are mainly for d=1; authors should discuss extensions and challenges for higher dimensions.  
- Primary concern: Theorem 1’s proof is too condensed and needs more detail, especially lines 288–306.  
- Specifically, taking limits of suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains is nontrivial, and continuity alone does not justify the stated convergence.  
- The first-order EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) requires clearer explanation.
