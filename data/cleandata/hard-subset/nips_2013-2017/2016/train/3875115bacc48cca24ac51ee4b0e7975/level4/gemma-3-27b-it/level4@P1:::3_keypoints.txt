- Paper studies existence of local modes in log-likelihood for well-specified, isotropic Gaussian mixtures with known scale.
- Authors prove local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Analysis focuses on particular center configurations (centers divided into well-spaced clusters) that cause many local optima.
- Under these configurations, random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum with high probability as the number of components increases.
- Paper is well written but contains occasional typos and language issues.
- Main practical concern 1: Theorem 1 shows existence for a specific configuration — reviewer asks how likely such “bad” center configurations are in practice.
- Reviewer requests results under assumptions on the distribution of true centers to quantify probability of bad configurations as k or d grow.
- Main practical concern 2: Theorem 2 analyzes random initialization by sampling data — reviewer questions if this is a realistic initialization when data are clearly clustered.
- Reviewer gives the example of imbalanced clustered data and questions the practical relevance of initializing more centers in regions with fewer points.
- Lines 74–81: reviewer finds the example unclear and not convincing for Srebro’s conjecture when k = 2.
- Lines 233–235: reviewer understands requirement (2) but questions the necessity of requirement (1) and whether a center initialized between two clusters may still move correctly.
- Lines 267–273: reviewer questions practical importance of proving EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points versus local optima.
- Minor language/typo corrections listed (with line numbers and suggested fixes) throughout the manuscript.
