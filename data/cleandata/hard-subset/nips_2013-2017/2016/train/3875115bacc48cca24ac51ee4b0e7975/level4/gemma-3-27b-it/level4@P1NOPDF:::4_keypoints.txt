- Paper studies local maxima of GMM log-likelihoods (equal weights, identity covariances) and presents three main results.
- Result 1: Provides a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Shows that with random initialization, the probability of converging to a poor local maximum can be exponentially small in k, under a specific mean configuration.
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Main proofs (especially Theorem 1) are sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea seems reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked.
- Novelty: Establishes an original link between mean configuration and local maxima structure, with a clever hierarchical grouping idea for k>3.
- Potential impact: Results could spur theoretical work on local optima (e.g., k-means) and inform practical initialization strategies requiring sufficient points per true cluster.
- Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear intuition, setup, and interpretations.
