- Paper studies existence of local modes in Gaussian mixture log-likelihood for isotropic, known-scale, well-specified mixtures and shows arbitrarily bad local modes exist even with infinite data.  
- Focuses on particular center configurations (centers split between well-spaced clusters) and shows EM with random initialization will almost surely miss the global optimum as component count grows.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains occasional typos.  
- Main practical concern 1: Theorem 1 proves a local mode for a specific configuration—how likely is that configuration in practice?  
- Request to quantify probability of “bad” center configurations under explicit distributional assumptions and show how their likelihood scales with k or dimension.  
- Main practical concern 2: Theorem 2 uses random initialization; is random initialization relevant when data are clearly clustered (e.g., two close clusters and one far) given typical sample proportions?  
- Questioning practical plausibility that random initialization would place an inappropriate number of centers in well-separated clusters.  
- Clarify purpose of the example on lines 74–81; it does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k=2.  
- On lines 233–235: requirement (2) is understood but necessity of requirement (1) is unclear—does initializing a center between two clusters prevent it from moving to the correct cluster?  
- Why is the result that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point practically important—how problematic is saddle convergence versus local-optima convergence?  
- Minor language/notation corrections: line 18 (“show the” → “show that the”); line 38 (“estimate” → “estimates”); line 60 rephrasing; line 138 clarify O(d^{1/2})-separation meaning.  
- Other minor edits/clarifications: remove/rewrite redundant sentence at line 172; line 177 (“equivalent of” → “equivalent to”); line 218 clarify “well-separated” constant; line 220 fix duplicate “the”; line 225 clarify meaning of Ω(k); line 245 “coincident” → “coincidence”; line 249 add “that”; line 308 add “that”.
