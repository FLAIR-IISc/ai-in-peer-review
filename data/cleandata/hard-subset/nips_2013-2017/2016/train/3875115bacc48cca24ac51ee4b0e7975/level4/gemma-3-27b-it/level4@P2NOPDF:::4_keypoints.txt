- Studies local maxima landscape of GMMs with equal weights and identity covariances, presenting three interconnected results.
- Result 1 (counterexample k=3, d=1): some local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Result 2: for certain mean configurations and random initialization, the chance of escaping a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k.
- Result 3: gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Overall summary: provides a rigorous analysis illuminating factors that determine local maxima quality and gradient EM behavior.
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proof is sound; the approach for Theorem 2 seems reasonable though appendix details weren’t fully checked.
- Novelty: Introduces an original link between Gaussian mean configuration and local maxima structure, especially the hierarchical grouping insight for k>3.
- Potential theoretical impact: Could motivate further work on local optima in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means) and extensions to d>1.
- Practical usefulness: Emphasizes the need for sufficient seed points per true cluster when initializing clustering algorithms.
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear intuition, setup, and interpretations.
