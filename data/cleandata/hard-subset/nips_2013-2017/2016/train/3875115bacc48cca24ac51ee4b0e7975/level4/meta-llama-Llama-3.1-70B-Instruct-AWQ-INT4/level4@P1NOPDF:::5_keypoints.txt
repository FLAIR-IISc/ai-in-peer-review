- Paper answers Srebro (2007) open question: shows there are no local maxima in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihood via counterexamples.
- Constructs a general class of counterexamples proving the "no local maxima" result.
- Reviews EM and gradient EM methods and common initialization practices for Gaussian mixture likelihood maximization.
- Main theorems state the counterexamples and theoretical claims clearly.
- Shows that with commonly used random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Provides a detailed, intuitive proof sketch using a simple case (k=3, d=1).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing concision and intuition.
- Addresses an important question for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic limits.
- Reviewer requests inclusion of estimates for the constants in the probability term to locate the phase transition in k.
- If constants are intractable, reviewer recommends supporting simulations to demonstrate the phenomenon in practice; lack of these details doesn’t reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
