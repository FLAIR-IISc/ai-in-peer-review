- Paper studies convergence of EM and iterative MLE for mixtures of spherical Gaussians, showing failures to reach global optima.  
- Main result 1: EM can get stuck in local optima even with well-separated clusters and infinite samples.  
- Main result 2: With k Gaussians, the success probability of random initialization for EM is ≤ exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Main result 3: Gradient-based EM typically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the main optimization obstacle.  
- Strength: Cohesive presentation tying these results to show EM’s susceptibility to non-global optima in the infinite-data limit.  
- Criticism: Results are unsurprising and methods are fairly standard.  
- Illustrative example critique: The 3-Gaussian example (two close, one far) is predictable, especially when inter-cluster distance ≫ √d, reducing to a k-means-like problem.  
- Extension to k components via recursion is reasonable but feels predictable.  
- Random initialization failure mirrors k-means behavior, suggesting similar remedies (e.g., k log k centers or distance-squared sampling).  
- Overall assessment: Argument is sound but incremental and follows established reasoning.  
- Suggestion: Include a more detailed comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic and contrast with prior results (e.g., Balakrishnan et al.) and k-means guarantees (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) to better position the contribution.
