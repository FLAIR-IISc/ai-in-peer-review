- Problem studied: structure of local maxima of the GMM log-likelihood for equal mixing weights and identity covariance.
- Result 1: constructs a counterexample (M=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Result 2: shows that for a special mean configuration, random initialization has exponentially small probability (in M) of avoiding bad local maxima — highlighting initialization sensitivity.
- Result 3: proves first-order EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points, implying common suboptimal limits are bad local maxima.
- Technical quality: proofs are sound and arguments are well-interpreted.
- Novelty: original insight linking mean configuration to the existence and quality of local maxima.
- Key technical idea: Theorem 2 leverages Theorem 1 in a clever, nontrivial way.
- Theoretical impact: results can motivate study of local optima in related clustering tasks (e.g., k-means).
- Practical impact: emphasizes importance of seeding enough points per true cluster and informs algorithm design/initialization.
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations and intuition.
- Strength: original, high technical quality, clear exposition, and potential theory/practice impact.
- Weakness: analysis restricted to equal weights and identity covariance models.
- Weakness: some results may not generalize to higher dimensions (d > 1).
- Acceptance case: advances understanding of GMM optimization with strong technical contributions.
- Rejection case: limited scope and potential lack of generalization to other GMM variants or higher dimensions.
