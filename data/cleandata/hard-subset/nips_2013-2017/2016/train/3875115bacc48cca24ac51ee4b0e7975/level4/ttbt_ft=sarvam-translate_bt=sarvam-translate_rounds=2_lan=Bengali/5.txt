This paper presents an answer to a question raised by Srebro in his paper in 2007: "Is there a local maximum in the probability of an infinite-sample Gaussian mixture model?" The paper negatively answers the question by creating a class of common counterexamples. The authors begin by describing how to maximize probability in the context of Gaussian mixture models, including EM and gradient EM algorithms. The authors also highlight the starting problem and propose a general choice. Then, the authors state their primary theorems, which provide a counterexample to Srebro's [2007] question. The authors further show that even with a general choice of random starting, EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with rapidly increasing probability. Next, the authors prove a general example (k=3, d=1) and provide some ideas for their proof. This paper is very clear and well-written, and it balances the paper's space requirements well with the need to provide ideas for their theoretical results. The question answered by the authors is extremely important for a better understanding of the problems that arise with non-convex optimization, even to the infinite limit for a normal distribution. I have not thoroughly examined the additional elements, but it would be good if they provided some assumptions of constants within the probability term. This is important because I think k will never be considered too large. When we start to see convergence towards a bad solution in the infinite limit (i.e., where is the phase transition?), it would be good to see the assumptions of constants. If the constants are too complex or meaningless, the supporting simulations would be good, at least it is an event that we can observe. However, this problem does not detract from the novelty or impact of this paper.
