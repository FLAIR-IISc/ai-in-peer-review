- Paper analyzes convergence of EM (and related iterative MLE heuristics) for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and proves several negative convergence results.
- Even in the population setting with three well-separated clusters, EM-like algorithms can get trapped in local optima.
- For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization of EM succeeds with probability at most exp(−Ω(k)).
- Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, so the main obstacle to global convergence is bad local maxima.
- Overall message: EM need not converge to a global maximum even with infinite data; results are coherent but largely unsurprising and use expected techniques.
- Central counterexample: three-component mixture with two close components and one far component yields a local maximum that misallocates centers—intuitive under large separation (≈≫√d).
- The construction extends to k components via a recursive gadget, producing expected pathologies where centers overfit some regions and underfit others, similar to k-means failures.
- The exponential failure probability for random initialization parallels k-means, motivating k log k or k-means++ seeding to avoid placing too many centers in one region.
- Suggestion: provide a more explicit comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s (and to positive guarantees like Kumar–Kannan) and clarify where EM meaningfully diverges from Lloyd’s in less-separated regimes.
