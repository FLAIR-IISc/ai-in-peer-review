- Proves EM can converge to suboptimal local maxima for GMMs, even with well-separated components and infinite samples.  
- Shows existence of a non-trivial local maximum for a specific 3-component well-separated GMM.  
- Proves random initialization converges to suboptimal solutions with probability at least 1 – exp(-Ω(M)) for M components.  
- Argues first-order EM methods rarely converge to strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the main failure mode.  
- Presents a coherent, consistent set of theoretical results illustrating EM’s global-optimality challenges.  
- Counterexample construction (two close components, one distant) is intuitive and echoes known k-means failure modes.  
- Extension to k components uses a recursive construction that is reasonable though expected.  
- Results are technically solid but not highly surprising given existing clustering literature.  
- Lacks explicit comparison to k-means and Lloyd’s heuristic, which would contextualize and strengthen the contribution.  
- Suggests connection to Kumar & Kannan’s Lloyd’s convergence guarantees and to prior EM vs. Lloyd analyses remains underexplored.  
- Strength: clear presentation of challenging scenarios and rigorous mathematical proofs.  
- Strength: coherent narrative and valuable identification that saddle points are not the primary issue.  
- Weakness: limited novelty and predictability relative to prior clustering insights.  
- Weakness: practical implications and mitigation strategies (e.g., initialization methods) are underdeveloped.  
- Recommendation: solid theoretical work but borderline accept; revise to include comparisons to k-means/Lloyd and discussion of practical remedies.
