- Resolves Srebro (2007) by proving no local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for GMMs.  
- Constructs a general class of explicit counterexamples that settle the open theoretical question.  
- Shows EM and gradient-EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under standard random initialization as sample size grows.  
- Provides an accessible, intuitive proof for a simplified case (k=3, d=1) to illustrate the mechanism.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, balancing technical rigor and clear exposition; supplements contain deferred technical details.  
- Findings have broader significance for non-convex optimization and highlight risks of algorithmic convergence to suboptimal solutions in statistical learning.  
- Approach’s novelty lies in explicit counterexamples rather than probabilistic arguments, adding robustness to the conclusion.  
- Main limitation: lack of precise bounds on constants in probability estimates and absence of simulations demonstrating the phase transition.  
- Recommendation: include explicit numerical constants and supporting experiments to clarify practical relevance, though this is a minor concern.  
- Overall assessment: major theoretical advance, well-executed, and a strong candidate for publication.
