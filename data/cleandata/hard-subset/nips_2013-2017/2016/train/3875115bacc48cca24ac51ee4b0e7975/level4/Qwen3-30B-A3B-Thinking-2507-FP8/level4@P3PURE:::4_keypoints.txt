- Paper proves local maxima for equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum (constructive counterexample for k=3, d=1).
- Shows that with random initialization the chance of avoiding a poor local maximum can be exponentially small in k for certain true-mean configurations.
- Demonstrates gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Theorem 1’s proof is verified and sound; Theorem 2’s strategy is reasonable though appendix details were not checked.
- All three theorems are clearly stated, well-motivated, and interpreted appropriately.
- Novel contribution linking geometric arrangement of true means to the structure of local maxima.
- Theorem 2’s insight that hierarchical grouping of true means dictates local maxima for k>3 is novel and clever.
- Theoretical implications: could inspire deeper analyses of local optima in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means).
- Practical implications: emphasizes importance of sufficient seeding within true clusters and may guide more robust GMM initialization strategies.
- Suggests extending Lemma 7 and related results to higher dimensions (d>1) as a promising direction.
- Paper excels in clarity and presentation with unambiguous setup, effective intuition, and well-structured technical arguments.
