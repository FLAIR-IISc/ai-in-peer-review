- Main contribution: definitively answers Srebro (2007) by constructing counterexamples showing local maxima in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood do not exist.  
- Paper includes a clear exposition of standard GMM likelihood maximization methods, including EM and gradient-EM.  
- Discusses common initialization strategies used in practice.  
- Presents central theorems that directly resolve Srebro’s question.  
- Extends analysis to show EM and gradient-EM, with typical random initialization, converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an accessible proof sketch for a simplified case (k=3, d=1) to aid understanding.  
- Writing quality: exceptionally well-written with a strong balance of theoretical rigor and intuitive explanation.  
- Suggestion: provide estimates for constants in the probability bounds (or simulations) to clarify practical parameter regimes and potential phase transitions.
