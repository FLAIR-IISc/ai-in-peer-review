This paper resolves Srebro’s 2007 question “Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?” in the negative by constructing a broad class of counterexamples. The authors first review likelihood maximization for Gaussian mixture models via EM and its first-order (gradient) variant, and discuss standard random initialization. They then state their main theorems, which provide explicit counterexamples to Srebro’s conjecture, and further show that with random initialization both EM and first-order EM converge to suboptimal critical points with exponentially high probability. They also offer intuition through a simple illustrative case (M=3, d=1).

The paper is clearly written and well organized, striking a good balance between space constraints and conveying intuition behind the theoretical results. The problem addressed is important for understanding challenges in non-convex optimization, even in the population limit for seemingly benign mixture models.

I did not carefully verify the supplementary material, but it would be helpful to quantify the constants in the stated probability bounds. Since in practice M is rarely very large, explicit constants would clarify when one should expect the asymptotic bad-convergence behavior to manifest (i.e., the “phase transition”). If the constants are unwieldy or opaque, supporting simulations would be valuable to demonstrate the observability of the phenomenon. This caveat does not detract from the novelty or impact of the work.
