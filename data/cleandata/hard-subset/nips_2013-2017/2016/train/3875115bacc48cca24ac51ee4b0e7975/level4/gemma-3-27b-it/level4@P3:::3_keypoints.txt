- Paper studies likelihood landscape of isotropic GMMs and shows bad local maxima exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Demonstrates that EM with random initialization converges to these suboptimal local maxima with high probability as the number of components increases.  
- Resolves an open question posed by Srebro (2007) by rigorously proving the failure modes of EM under constructed conditions.  
- Theorem 4: first-order (gradient) EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points, supporting the claim that bad local optima, not saddles, drive failures.  
- Paper is generally well-written, technically sound, and logically structured.  
- Practical relevance is unclear because the bad configurations are specially constructed and the likelihood of encountering them in real data is not quantified.  
- Theorem 2 shows high-probability failure for those configurations but does not quantify how likely such configurations are under realistic data-generation assumptions.  
- The random initialization scheme (sampling centers uniformly from data) may be unrealistic in clustered settings; results’ sensitivity to other initialization strategies needs exploration.  
- Example on lines 74–81 is unclear in motivation; its purpose (or whether it proves Srebro’s conjecture for k=2) should be clarified.  
- Lines 233–235: the need for both initialization conditions is not fully justified; explain why initializing between clusters prevents correct convergence.  
- The practical significance of Theorem 3 (EM avoids saddle points) is not clearly articulated—how harmful are saddles vs local optima in practice?  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical results, resolution of an open problem, clarity, and relevance to initialization issues in practice.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of practical likelihood of problematic configurations, potentially unrealistic initialization scheme, and unclear motivations/justifications in parts of the paper.  
- Minor corrections requested: multiple wording and notation fixes (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308) and clarifications for terms like “well-separated” and Ω(k).  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution; recommendation of weak accept conditional on addressing practical relevance and clarifying supporting arguments.
