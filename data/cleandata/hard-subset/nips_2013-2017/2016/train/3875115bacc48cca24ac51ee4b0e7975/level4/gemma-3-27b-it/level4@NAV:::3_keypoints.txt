- Paper studies local modes in the log-likelihood of isotropic, known-scale Gaussian mixture models and shows local modes persist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- For well-spaced mixture centers, as the number of components k grows, random EM initialization will with high probability converge to suboptimal local modes.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains minor typos.  

Primary concern 1:
- The practical relevance of Theorem 1 depends on how likely the “bad” center configurations are in real data; the authors should quantify this under plausible distributions of center locations and show how that probability scales with k and dimension.  
- The claimed connection between Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 (lines 210–218) is unclear and should be made explicit.  

Primary concern 2:
- Theorem 2’s use of random initialization by sampling from the mixture seems unrealistic for clearly clustered data, where initialization is unlikely to place centers in sparse regions; this questions the practical relevance of the random-init result.  

Clarification requests:
- The example on lines 74–81 does not clearly illustrate the Sresbro conjecture for k=2 and its purpose should be clarified.  
- For lines 233–235, the necessity of requirement (1) is not apparent—do the authors worry that initializing a center between two clusters need not lead to correct cluster assignment?  
- The practical significance of showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint (lines 267–273) is unclear; clarify whether saddlepoint convergence is more or less problematic than convergence to a local optimum.
