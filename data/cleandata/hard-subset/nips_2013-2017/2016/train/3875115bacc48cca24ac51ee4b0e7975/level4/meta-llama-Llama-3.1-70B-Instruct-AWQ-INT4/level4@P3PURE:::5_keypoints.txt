- Paper answers Srebro (2007) open question, resolving it negatively by constructing a general class of counterexamples.  
- Authors review likelihood maximization schemes for GMMs, including EM and gradient EM.  
- Paper discusses initialization and uses a common random initialization scheme.  
- Main theorems show EM and gradient EM can converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provide intuition and a complete proof for a simple case (k=3, d=1).  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and balances space with necessary intuition.  
- The question tackled is important for understanding non-convex optimization even in the infinite-sample limit.  
- Reviewer did not examine supplements carefully and requests estimates of the constants in the probability bounds.  
- Knowing those constants is important to locate the phase transition (when suboptimal convergence appears); if constants are complex, supporting simulations are suggested.  
- Lack of constant estimates or simulations does not detract from the paper’s novelty or impact.
