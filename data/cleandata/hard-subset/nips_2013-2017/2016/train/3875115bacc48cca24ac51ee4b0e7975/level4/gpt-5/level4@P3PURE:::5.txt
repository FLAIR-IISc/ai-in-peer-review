This paper addresses the open question posed by Srebro (2007), “Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?” and answers it in the negative by constructing a general class of counterexamples. The authors begin by outlining how the likelihood is maximized in Gaussian mixture models, focusing on the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and they discuss initialization, highlighting a common random choice. They then present their main theorems, which provide the counterexamples to Srebro’s question, and further show that, even under this common random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To build intuition, they prove a simple illustrative case with k = 3 and d = 1.

The paper is very clear and well written, striking a good balance between space constraints and providing intuition for the theoretical results. The question the authors address is important for understanding the challenges of non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.

I did not carefully check the supplementary material, but estimates of the constants in their probability bounds would be valuable. In practice, one would rarely consider k to be very large, so concrete constants would clarify when we might start to see convergence to bad solutions in the asymptotic limit (i.e., where the phase transition lies). If the constants are too complicated or not interpretable, supporting simulations would be helpful to at least demonstrate that this phenomenon can be observed. However, this issue does not detract from the novelty or impact of the paper.
