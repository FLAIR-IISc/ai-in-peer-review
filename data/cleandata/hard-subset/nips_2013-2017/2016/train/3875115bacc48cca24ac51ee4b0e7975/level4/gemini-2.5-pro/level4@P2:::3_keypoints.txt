- Paper shows population log-likelihood for well-specified isotropic GMMs (M ≥ 3) can have arbitrarily poor local maxima, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Paper proves EM and a first-order variant, with random initialization from the mixture, can converge to suboptimal critical points with probability → 1 exponentially in M.
- Strength: original and significant theoretical contribution clarifying a fundamental optimization landscape question for GMMs.
- Strength: technically solid proofs and insightful constructions; uses dynamical-systems ideas to analyze first-order EM near saddles.
- Strength: paper is generally well written, motivated, and states results precisely.
- Main concern: unclear how prevalent the “bad” GMM configurations are in practice; need formal statements under reasonable generative assumptions for centers.
- Main concern: practical relevance of the i.i.d.-from-mixture random initialization is questionable; practitioners often use informed methods (e.g., k-means++) that may avoid the failure cases.
- Detailed question: the M=2 discussion/visualization is unclear — authors should clarify the intended conclusion for the M=2 case.
- Detailed question: in Theorem 2 proof sketch, clarify why condition (1) (each initialized center falls near exactly one true cluster) is necessary and what happens if a center lies between clusters.
- Detailed question: practical significance of Theorem 4 (first-order EM avoids strict saddles) — explain why ruling out saddle convergence matters relative to poor local maxima.
- Minor/typo suggestions: fixes include “We further show that the gradient EM algorithm” (L18), “quality of the estimates” (L38), rephrase L60, clarify Ω(√d)-separated (L138), remove redundancy L172, “equivalent to sampling” (L177), fix double “the” (L220), define Ω(M) (L225), and small wording fixes at L249.
