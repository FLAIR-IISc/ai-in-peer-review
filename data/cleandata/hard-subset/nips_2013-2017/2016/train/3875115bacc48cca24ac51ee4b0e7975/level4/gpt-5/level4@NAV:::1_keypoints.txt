- Paper shows EM and related MLE heuristics can get trapped in suboptimal local maxima even with infinite data.  
- Main hard instance: a 3-component mixture with two close components and a third far away produces a local optimum misplacing centers.  
- For well-separated Gaussians (separation ≫ sqrt(d)) the behavior mirrors k-means, so the hard instance is unsurprising.  
- EM with random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)) for k-component mixtures.  
- Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying poor local maxima (not saddles) cause failures.  
- Authors extend the 3-component example to k components via a recursive construction; technically nontrivial but intuitive.  
- Random initialization failure reasons and probabilities parallel known k-means failures (motivating k log k starts or distance-squared sampling).  
- Results are cohesive but not surprising; proof strategies largely follow expected approaches.  
- Critique/suggestion: paper should include a direct comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm (e.g., Kumar–Kannan guarantees) to clarify similarities and differences under large vs. small separation.
