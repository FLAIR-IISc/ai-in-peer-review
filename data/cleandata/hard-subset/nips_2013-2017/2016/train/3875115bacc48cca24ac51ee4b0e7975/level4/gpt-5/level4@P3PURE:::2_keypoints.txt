- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, known uniform weights, and identity covariances, and proves three negative results about EM and gradient EM.
- Negative result 1: Even with large samples, the likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Negative result 2: With random initialization, EM likely converges to a critical point whose likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Negative result 3: Gradient EM exhibits the same negative behavior as EM.
- Reviewer finds the question interesting, the results informative, and the simplifying assumptions (weights/uniform/identity covariances) acceptable given technical proofs.
- Clarification requested on ambient dimension d: proofs are carried out for d = 1, so authors should state whether extensions to arbitrary d are possible or explain extra difficulties for d > 1.
- Main concern: Proof of Theorem 1 in the main text is too compressed—lines 288–306 require more detailed justification when taking limits of suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains; continuity alone does not suffice.
- Request to expand the main-text argument for Theorem 1 (even if it shortens surrounding discussion) so readers can be convinced without relying on supplementary proofs.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; provide more explanation of the derivation of Equation (5).
