- Paper studies EM convergence for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and shows non-convergence to global maxima in several settings.  
- Main contribution 1: constructs a well-separated uniform mixture of three unit-variance Gaussians whose population log-likelihood has a bad local maximum, answering Srebro (2007) negatively.  
- Main contribution 2: proves random initialization for EM succeeds with at most exp(-Ω(k)) probability for k-component mixtures via a recursive clustered-center construction.  
- Main contribution 3: shows gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are typically the problem.  
- Reviewer: results are technically sound and paper is well-written and clear.  
- Reviewer: results and techniques are unsurprising and follow expected lines; main bad example (two close components, one far) is intuitive.  
- Observation: under very large separation (≫√d) the problem behaves like k-means/Lloyd’s, so the negative examples mirror known bad instances for k-means.  
- Recommendation: paper should include a more detailed comparison to k-means results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) and contrast with EM analyses (e.g., Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu).  
- Critique: implications of the results are not entirely clear given prior related work; originality rated lower.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10, Overall 30/40.
