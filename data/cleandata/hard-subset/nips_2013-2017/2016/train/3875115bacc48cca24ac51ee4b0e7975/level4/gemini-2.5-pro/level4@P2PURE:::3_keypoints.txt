- Paper studies spurious local modes in the GMM log-likelihood for well-specified isotropic mixtures with known scale, showing local modes can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum even with infinite data and that EM with random initialization typically fails for certain clustered center configurations as k grows.

- Main concern 1: Theorem 1 shows existence of “bad” true-center configurations, but it is unclear how likely such configurations are in practice.

- Suggestion: strengthen practical relevance by assuming a distribution over true centers and proving the probability of encountering bad configurations grows with k or dimensionality d; Theorem 2 does not clearly achieve this.

- Main concern 2: Theorem 2 analyzes random sampling initialization from the mixture, but this initialization may be impractical when the data displays clear cluster structure (e.g., imbalanced cluster sizes), making the negative EM result less relevant.

- L74-81: The purpose of the example is unclear and does not convincingly support that Srebro’s conjecture holds for k=2.

- L233-235: Requirement (2) is understandable, but the necessity of requirement (1) is not obvious—is it intended to address centers initialized between two clusters failing to move to the correct cluster?

- L267-273: What is the practical significance of showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point—how does saddle convergence compare in harm to convergence to a local optimum?
