- Paper constructs explicit counterexample (M≥3) resolving Srebro (2007): population log-likelihood for equally-weighted, spherical GMMs can have arbitrarily bad local maxima.
- Shows hierarchical configuration of true means causes EM and first-order EM with standard random initialization to converge to suboptimal critical points with exponentially high probability in M.
- Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, attributing algorithmic failure to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Originality/significance: provides a definitive negative answer to a long-standing conjecture and formalizes how geometry of means induces spurious local maxima.
- Technical soundness: main proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) are rigorous, elegant, and convincing, with clever hierarchical construction.
- Clarity/presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written, motivates the problem, and explains intuition before technical details.
- Impact: results have strong theoretical and practical implications, motivating careful initialization methods over naive random starts.
- Question: constructions are specific—unclear how prevalent such hierarchical/clustered mean structures are in practice versus being isolated pathological cases.
- Question: base counterexample presented for d=1—unclear whether the argument extends straightforwardly to higher dimensions (d>1).
- Question: saddle-avoidance result proven for first-order EM only; authors should clarify technical hurdles to extending this to standard EM.
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper, significant contributions, strongly recommended for acceptance.
