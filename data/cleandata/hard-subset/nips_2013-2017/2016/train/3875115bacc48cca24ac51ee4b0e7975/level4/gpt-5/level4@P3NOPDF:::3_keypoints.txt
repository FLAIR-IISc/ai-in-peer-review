- Paper proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood of well-specified isotropic Gaussian mixtures and shows EM with random initialization often converges to them as k grows.  
- Strength: Focuses on population (infinite-sample) likelihood, removing finite-sample noise.  
- Strength: Provides crisp negative results showing bad local maxima can occur even in simplest settings.  
- Strength: Theorems give clear intuition that grouping true centers into well-separated clusters creates spurious attraction basins for EM.  
- Strength: Writing and proofs are generally clear and careful; highlights an important limitation of common methods.  
- Concern: Unclear how likely the pathological true-center configurations are under realistic random-placement models—need probabilistic or smoothed-analysis statements.  
- Concern: Request formal quantification of the measure/asymptotic frequency of “bad” configurations (as functions of k, d, etc.), not just existence.  
- Concern: Practically, common initialization heuristics (k-means++, farthest-first, multiple restarts) may avoid the pathology—need analysis or discussion of those methods.  
- Concern: Clarify whether failure is driven by mismatch between number of true subclusters and initial seeds, and whether density-aware initialization fixes it.  
- Request: Clarify the purpose of the L74–81 example or remove/streamline it.  
- Request: Explain necessity of requirement (1) at L233–235 (provide lemma or counterexample).  
- Request: Clarify practical importance of ruling out convergence to saddle points versus bad local maxima (L267–273).  
- Suggestion: Discuss extensions/robustness to unknown variance, anisotropic covariances, non-uniform weights, and mild misspecification.  
- Suggestion: Provide explicit quantitative failure probabilities as functions of k, d, separation, and sample size when moving to finite-sample EM.  
- Suggestion: Add a small synthetic experiment measuring frequency of bad configurations and EM failures under random center placements.  
- Minor: Numerous typos and phrasing issues listed (e.g., lines L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308) and requests for consistent notation/definitions (e.g., Ω(k), separation constants, “Srebro”).  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical impossibility results, but gap remains between worst-case constructions and typical practice; adding probabilistic prevalence and robustness to practical initializations would strengthen the paper.
