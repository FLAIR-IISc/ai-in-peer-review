- Resolves Srebro (2007) open problem by constructing a broad class of counterexamples giving a negative answer.  
- Describes mechanisms for likelihood maximization in GMMs, including EM and gradient EM, and discusses common initialization choices.  
- States main theorems that furnish the counterexample to Srebro.  
- Shows that with standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an intuitive analysis of a simple case (k=3, d=1).  
- Paper is very clear and well written, balancing space constraints and theoretical intuition.  
- The question addressed is important for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic settings for simple distributions.  
- Reviewer did not examine supplements in detail; requests explicit constants in probability bounds (or simulations) to clarify practical phase-transition regimes.  
- Despite the above, the issue does not detract from the paper’s novelty or impact.
