- Paper shows existence of spurious local modes in the GMM log-likelihood even in the infinite-data limit.  
- These local modes can be arbitrarily poor compared to the global optimum.  
- Results are proved for configurations where true mixture centers form well-separated clusters.  
- For those configurations, random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum with high probability as k increases.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains some minor typos.

- Concern 1: Practical relevance of the specific center configurations is unclear; need analysis showing how likely such “bad” configurations are under reasonable priors on true centers as k or d grow.  
- Concern 2: Questioning relevance of the random-data-point initialization—practically, sampling from clustered data is unlikely to produce the problematic initializations analyzed, so the initialization choice may be unrealistic.

- Specific Q: L74–81 — role of the example is unclear and doesn’t convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k=2.  
- Specific Q: L233–235 — motivation for requirement (1) is unclear; is it to prevent a center initialized between clusters from moving correctly?  
- Specific Q: L267–273 — what is the practical significance of showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle versus to a local optimum?

- Minor typos / wording fixes: L18 add “that” after “show”; L38 change “estimate” to “estimates”; L60 rephrase to “under which conditions”; L138 clarify meaning of “O(d^1/2)-separated”; L172 avoid redundancy; L177 change “of” to “to”; L218 clarify “well-separated constant”; L220 remove duplicated “the”; L245 change “coincident” to “coincidence”; L249 insert “that” after “Recall”; L308 add “that” after “claim.”
