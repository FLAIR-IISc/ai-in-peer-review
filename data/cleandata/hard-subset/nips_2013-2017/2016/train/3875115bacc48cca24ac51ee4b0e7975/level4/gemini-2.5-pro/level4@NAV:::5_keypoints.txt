- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples that show non-optimal local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture models.  
- Formal main theorems establish the counterexample rigorously.  
- Reviews standard likelihood maximization techniques (EM, gradient EM) and a common parameter initialization approach.  
- Shows that with standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to these spurious maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an intuitive, simplified proof for the case k=3, d=1 to illustrate the main result.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing formal theory and intuition within page limits.  
- Results are a significant contribution to understanding persistent non-convex optimization difficulties, even in the infinite-sample regime.  
- Reviewer suggests strengthening the paper by estimating the constants in the probability bounds (or providing simulation evidence if analytic estimates are intractable) to show practical phase-transition thresholds.  
- Reviewer did not examine the supplementary material in detail and views the constant-estimate suggestion as minor that does not diminish the paper’s novelty or impact.
