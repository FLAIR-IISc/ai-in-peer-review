- Resolves Srebro (2007) by proving bad local maxima exist in the population likelihood of GMMs, even for well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Constructs explicit counterexamples demonstrating these bad local maxima.  
- Proves that EM and its first-order variant with random initialization are likely to converge to these suboptimal points.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, balancing rigor with intuitive explanations.  
- Addresses an important question for non-convex optimization in statistical models.  
- Provides a concise overview of GMMs, EM variants, initialization strategies, and states main theorems clearly.  
- Uses a simplified M=3, d=1 case to build intuition and illustrate core ideas.  
- Significant theoretical contribution resolving a long-standing open problem.  
- Strong algorithmic implications highlighting the need for careful initialization.  
- Clarity and presentation make the complex results accessible.  
- Well-motivated and properly situated within prior literature.  
- Weakness: does not estimate key constants (e.g., c in 1−exp(−cM)) or discuss sensitivity/phase transitions.  
- Weakness: lacks simulations or empirical illustrations of likelihood surfaces and finite-sample behavior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: central problem, rigorous proofs, clear exposition, and likely to stimulate further research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing concrete constant estimates and supporting simulations limit practical significance.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper with minor practical drawbacks; reviewer recommends acceptance.
