- Paper shows spurious local optima can exist in the population log-likelihood of isotropic Gaussian mixtures with known variance.  
- These spurious modes can be arbitrarily suboptimal in log-likelihood compared to the global optimum.  
- Theoretical constructions rely on specific true-center configurations, notably well-separated clusters.  
- For those configurations, random initialization of EM (sampling centers from the mixture) will likely fail to find the global optimum as the number of components grows.  
- Paper is clearly written but contains minor typographical errors.

Primary concern 1:
- Practical relevance unclear: how likely are the adversarial center configurations in real data?  
- Suggestion: quantify prevalence under a prior on true centers or show probability of encountering such configurations grows with k or d.  
- Note: Theorem 2 addresses initialization failure, not directly the prevalence of bad ground-truth configurations.

Primary concern 2:
- Question relevance of the analyzed initialization (sampling from the mixture): it can place too many initial centers in sparse regions, which seems implausible in practice.  
- Example: with 3 components where two are close and one distant, sampling could place one init in dense region and two in sparse region, a behavior practitioners would typically avoid.

Specific questions:
- L74-81: Purpose of the example is unclear and not convincing for Srebro’s conjecture in k=2—please clarify intent.  
- L233-235: Necessity of condition (2) is clear, but role/motivation of condition (1) is unclear—is it to handle equidistant initial centers?  
- L267-273: What is the practical significance of EM being unlikely to converge to a saddle point versus converging to a local minimum?
