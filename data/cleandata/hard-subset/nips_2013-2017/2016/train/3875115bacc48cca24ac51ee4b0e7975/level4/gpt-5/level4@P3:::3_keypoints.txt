- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) by constructing well-separated GMMs with M ≥ 3 that exhibit suboptimal local maxima with arbitrarily large likelihood gaps at the population level.
- Shows EM and gradient-EM with random initialization from the mixture/data converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).
- Proves gradient-EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Complements prior local-convergence analyses and contrasts with algorithms that obtain global guarantees under separation or moment conditions.
- Strength: provides explicit, sharp counterexamples resolving a known conjecture.
- Strength: derives strong algorithmic implications showing exponential failure probability for common random initialization heuristics.
- Strength: clarifies landscape geometry by isolating bad local maxima as the main issue.
- Strength: exposition is generally clear with intuitive constructions and discussion.
- Weakness: practical prevalence of adversarial “bad” center configurations is unquantified; need probabilistic or empirical evidence under natural priors.
- Weakness: negative results rely on sampling initial means from the mixture, while practical initializations (k-means++, clustering pre-steps) may avoid the failure mode.
- Weakness: M = 2 example is unclear—need to state whether it is purely illustrative or evidence toward a conjecture.
- Weakness: Section 3.2’s two initialization requirements are confusing; requirement (1) needs precise restatement and formal justification about centers initialized between clusters.
- Weakness: significance of Theorem 4 (avoiding strict saddles) should be better motivated and supported, e.g., with empirical Hessian spectra at EM fixed points.
- Major suggestion: provide either probabilistic bounds for occurrence of bad configurations under reasonable priors or empirical prevalence studies varying M and d.
- Major suggestion: analyze or experimentally evaluate more informed initializations (e.g., k-means++) to assess practical relevance of negative results.
- Minor suggestion: emphasize earlier that σ2 is known and weights are equal, and comment on extensions when variances or weights are unknown.
- Notation suggestion: precisely define “well-separated” (e.g., c√d-separated) and clarify hidden constants in Ω(·) and e−Ω(M).
- Minor edits: multiple line-specific wording and notation fixes (L18, L38, L60, L74–81, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308).
- Argument for acceptance: resolves a notable open question with clear theoretical and algorithmic implications.
- Argument against acceptance: practical significance is limited without quantifying prevalence of pathologies and considering stronger initialization schemes.
- Overall evaluation: strong theoretical contribution but needs strengthened discussion/experiments on practical prevalence and initialization, plus clarifications noted above.
