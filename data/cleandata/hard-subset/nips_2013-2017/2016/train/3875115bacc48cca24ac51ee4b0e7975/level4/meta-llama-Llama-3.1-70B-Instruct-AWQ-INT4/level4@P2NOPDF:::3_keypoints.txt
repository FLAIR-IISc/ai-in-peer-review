- Paper studies local modes in Gaussian-mixture log-likelihoods for isotropic components (known scale), proving local modes can exist with infinite data, can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum, and that EM with random initialization often misses the global optimum as k increases.

- Paper is well-written but contains occasional typos.

- Concern 1: Theorem 1 depends on a specific center configuration; reviewer questions how likely such “bad” configurations are in practice and requests assumptions on center distributions plus a statement showing bad configurations become more likely as k or d grows.

- Concern 2: Theorem 2’s negative EM initialization result relies on sampling initial centers that place multiple centers in one true cluster—reviewer doubts this is a realistic initialization in clearly clustered data and questions the practical relevance of random initialization here.

- Example (L74–81) is unconvincing: reviewer is not persuaded this supports Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2.

- Requirement (1) (L233–235) unclear/possibly unnecessary: initializing a center between two clusters should cause it to move to the correct cluster, so the need for this requirement is questioned.

- EM saddlepoint result (L267–273): reviewer asks why proving EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint is practically important, and whether saddlepoint convergence is more or less problematic than local-optimum convergence.
