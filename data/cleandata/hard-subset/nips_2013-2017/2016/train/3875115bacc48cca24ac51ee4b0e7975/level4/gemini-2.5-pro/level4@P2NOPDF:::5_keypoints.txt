- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples showing non-optimal local maxima exist in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.

- Demonstrates that the asymptotic likelihood landscape for GMMs can contain spurious local optima.

- Contextualizes the problem and reviews standard algorithms (EM, gradient EM) and the common random initialization strategy.

- Proves existence of a GMM whose infinite-sample likelihood has a non-optimal local maximum (main theorems).

- Shows EM and gradient EM with standard random initialization converge to the spurious maximum with exponentially high probability in the number of components k.

- Provides a clear, self-contained proof for an illustrative case (k=3, d=1) to build intuition.

- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing formal theory and intuitive explanation.

- Highlights fundamental importance: non-convex optimization challenges can persist even in the infinite-data regime.

- Reviewer did not fully verify supplementary material and suggests including estimates for constants in the high-probability bounds.

- If constants are intractable, recommends adding simulations to demonstrate the phenomenon for moderate k.

- Suggestion is minor and does not detract from the paper’s novelty or its definitive answer to the foundational question.
