- Paper proves three negative results: likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than global, EM with random init converges w.h.p. to arbitrarily bad critical points, and same holds for gradient EM.  
- Research question is important and results give striking theoretical insight into EM’s failure modes.  
- Focusing on a simplified GMM (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) is justified by proof complexity and still informative.  
- Main weakness: proof of Theorem 1 in the main text is overly compressed (lines 288–306) and omits key justifications.  
- Critical technical gap: unjustified exchange of limit and supremum over non-compact or parameter-dependent domains; continuity alone is insufficient.  
- Reviewer did not verify supplementary material, so main text needs a self-contained, rigorous argument even if it shortens other sections.  
- Claim of handling general d dimensions is misleading: provided proofs are for d=1 and the extension to d>1 is not discussed.  
- Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) lacks clarity; derivation of Equation (5) should be elaborated.  
- Recommendation: major revision required to provide fuller, more rigorous proofs (especially Theorem 1), clarify d>1 scope, and improve Gradient EM exposition.
