- Paper shows local maxima persist in the GMM log-likelihood even with infinite data, and such local modes can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Analysis is in the simple setting of isotropic, known-variance, correctly specified GMMs and focuses on geometric arrangements where component means form well-separated clusters.  
- Authors prove that with random-sample initialization, EM will almost surely fail to find the global optimum in these clustered configurations as the number of components grows.  
- Manuscript is generally well written but contains several typographical and phrasing issues.  

Primary concern 1:
- Unclear practical relevance: reviewer asks how likely the “bad” mean configurations are in practice and requests a quantitative probability statement under an explicit distribution over true centers (Theorem 2 is not convincing on this point).  

Primary concern 2:
- Question realism of random-sample initialization: when data show obvious clustering (e.g., two close components and one far), random initialization seems an unrealistic baseline and may not reflect practical initialization strategies.  

Technical/clarity questions:
- L74–81: role of the example is unclear and does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2.  
- L233–235: why is condition (1) needed in addition to condition (2); can a center initialized between two clusters not be guaranteed to drift to the correct one?  
- L267–273: showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle—why is this practically important compared to convergence to a local optimum?  

Minor points (typos and wording):
- Multiple small edits and clarifications requested (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308) including missing words, grammar fixes, and requests to clarify phrases like “well-separated constant,” Ω(k), and O(d^1/2)-separated.
