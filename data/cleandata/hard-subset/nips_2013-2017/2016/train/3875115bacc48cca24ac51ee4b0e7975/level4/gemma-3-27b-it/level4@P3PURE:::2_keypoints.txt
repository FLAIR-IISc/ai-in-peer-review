- Paper studies EM for estimating means of a Gaussian mixture with known number of components, known uniform weights, and identity covariances.  
- Proves three negative results: likelihood can have local maxima with values arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.  
- Shows random initialization of EM converges w.h.p. to critical points with arbitrarily suboptimal likelihood.  
- Shows the same negative convergence result holds for gradient EM.  
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the model simplifications acceptable given proof complexity.  
- Results are valuable for understanding EM behavior in practice.  
- Claims hold for general dimension d but proofs are presented only for d=1; authors should discuss extension to d>1 or obstacles.  
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too concise—taking limits of maxima over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains and using continuity require clearer justification.  
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) needs more explanation.
