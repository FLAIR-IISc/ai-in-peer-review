Summary
This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function for Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It presents three related results: (1) a counterexample for k = 3 and d = 1 showing the existence of local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum; (2) under a specific configuration of the component means, with random initialization the probability of avoiding convergence to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k; and (3) gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.

Technical quality
I checked the proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) and found them sound. After reading the analysis of Theorem 1, the proof strategy for Theorem 2 also appears reasonable, although I did not verify the appendix details. All three theorems are accompanied by clear interpretations following their statements.

Novelty and originality
The paper draws a direct connection between the configuration of the component means in a GMM and the local maxima of its log-likelihood. Although this connection is intuitively plausible, the formulation and rigorous treatment here are, in my view, original. I find especially clever the central idea in Theorem 2, building on Theorem 1: the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means determines the local maxima for k > 3.

Potential impact and usefulness
These results have potential impact in both theory and practice. On the theoretical side, they can inspire further study of the landscape of local optima in related clustering problems, such as k-means. It would also be interesting to examine whether the result stated in Lemma 7 extends to dimensions d > 1. On the practical side, the work highlights the importance of seeding enough initial points within each true cluster, and it may offer insights for designing more robust initialization schemes or new algorithms that avoid poor local maxima.

Clarity and presentation
The paper is well written and does an excellent job of presenting the results and the underlying intuition. The problem setup is clearly introduced, and the analysis is well motivated and interpreted throughout.
