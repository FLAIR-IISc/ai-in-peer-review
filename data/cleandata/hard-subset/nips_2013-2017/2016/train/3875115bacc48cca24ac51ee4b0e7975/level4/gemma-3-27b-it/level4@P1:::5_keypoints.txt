- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples that demonstrate the existence of problematic local maxima in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihoods.
- Reviews standard likelihood-maximization methods (EM and gradient EM) and a common random initialization strategy.
- Main theorems show EM and gradient EM can converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability under standard initialization.
- Provides intuitive proof sketches via a simplified example (M=3, d=1).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing rigor and intuition despite space constraints.
- The question addressed is important for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic/simple-distribution settings.
- Theoretical results are supported by rigorous proofs and insightful intuition.
- Practical significance depends on constant values in the probability bounds, which are not estimated in the paper.
- Reviewer suggests providing estimates of the constants or supporting simulations to demonstrate when the failure becomes likely in practice.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong technical contribution, clarity, significant implications, and rigorous analysis.
- Argument against acceptance: lack of estimates for constants limits immediate practical impact.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept; minor concern about constant estimates does not outweigh strengths.
