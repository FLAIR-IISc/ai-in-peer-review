- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing spurious local maxima in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.  
- Reviews standard likelihood maximization methods (EM, gradient EM) and common initialization strategies.  
- Presents primary theorems formalizing the counterexamples to the prior claim.  
- Proves that EM and gradient EM with common random initialization converge to these non-optimal local maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an intuitive analysis via a simple k=3, d=1 case to build understanding for the proofs.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and balances theoretical rigor with intuition; addresses an important question in non-convex optimization.  
- Reviewer did not verify supplementary material in detail; recommends providing estimates for constants in the probability bounds to clarify practical regimes (especially for moderate k).  
- If analytical constant estimates are difficult, recommends adding simulations to demonstrate the phase-transition/practical impact, though this omission does not reduce the paper’s novelty or significance.
