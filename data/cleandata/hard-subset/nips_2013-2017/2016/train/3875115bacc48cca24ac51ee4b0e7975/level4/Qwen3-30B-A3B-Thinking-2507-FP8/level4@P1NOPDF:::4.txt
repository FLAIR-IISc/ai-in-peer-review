

This paper investigates the structure of local maxima in the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It establishes three key results: (1) A counterexample demonstrating that for k=3 components and d=1 dimension, local maxima can have arbitrarily lower likelihood than the global maximum; (2) The authors show that with random initialization, the probability of avoiding a poor local maximum can be exponentially small in k when Gaussian means are configured in a specific hierarchical pattern; (3) They prove that gradient EM converges to saddle points of the log-likelihood almost surely.  

Technical Quality  
I verified the main proofs (Theorem 1), which are sound. The argument for Theorem 2 appears reasonable based on its reliance on Theorem 1, though I did not examine the Appendix details. All theorems are clearly stated and well-motivated with intuitive explanations.  

Novelty & Originality  
The paper’s core insight—linking GMM mean configurations to local maxima structure—is highly original. The observation that the hierarchical grouping of true means dictates local maxima for k > 3 (Theorem 2’s key idea) is particularly clever and non-obvious.  

Potential Impact & Usefulness  
The results offer significant theoretical value, potentially inspiring new analysis of local optima in clustering problems like k-means. Practically, the findings underscore the importance of sufficient seeding per cluster and may guide algorithm design. The paper also raises an interesting question: whether Lemma 7’s result extends to d > 1, which merits further investigation.  

Clarity & Presentation  
The paper excels in clarity. It presents the problem setup, intuition, and analysis with exceptional precision. Each result is introduced with clear motivation, and the technical arguments are well-structured and accessible. The writing consistently balances mathematical rigor with intuitive explanation, making the contributions highly digestible.  

Overall, this work provides a substantial theoretical advance in understanding GMM optimization landscapes. The results are well-supported, novel, and carry meaningful implications for both theory and practice. The paper is exceptionally well-written and presents a compelling case for its contributions. I recommend acceptance.
