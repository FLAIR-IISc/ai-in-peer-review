- Paper studies convergence of EM and related iterative MLE methods for spherical Gaussian mixtures, showing failure to reach global optima.  
- Main findings: EM can get trapped in local optima even with well-separated clusters and infinite samples.  
- Random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)) for k-component mixtures.  
- Gradient-based EM typically avoids strict saddle points, implying local maxima (not saddles) are the main obstacle.  
- Overall results consistently show EM does not guarantee global maximum convergence, even in the infinite-data limit.  
- Reviewer critique: results are largely unsurprising and techniques are standard.  
- Primary illustrative example: 3-component mixture with two nearby components and one distant, producing an obvious local maximum configuration.  
- When centers are separated >> sqrt(d), overlap is negligible and behavior reduces to k-means–like failure modes.  
- Generalization to k components via recursion is non-trivial but predictable given known k-means pathologies.  
- Failure of random initialization mirrors known k-means results and the motivations for improved initializations (e.g., k log k, distance-squared sampling).  
- Suggestion: paper should include a more direct comparison to k-means and Lloyd’s heuristic, referencing related work (e.g., Kumar & Kannan, Balakrishnan et al.).
