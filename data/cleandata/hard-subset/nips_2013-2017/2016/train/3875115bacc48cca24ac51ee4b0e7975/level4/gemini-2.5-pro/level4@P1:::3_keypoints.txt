- Main contribution: resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture by showing population log-likelihood for isotropic, well-specified GMMs can have spurious local maxima for any M ≥ 3.  
- Construction: provides explicit true-mean configurations with arbitrarily worse local maxima than the global optimum.  
- Algorithmic consequence: for these constructions, EM with centers drawn i.i.d. from the mixture converges to suboptimal critical points with probability →1 exponentially fast in M.  
- First-order EM result: gradient/first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the main obstacle.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and makes a significant theoretical contribution by resolving a long-standing conjecture.  
- Concern (practical prevalence): Theorem 1 uses clustered, well-separated center constructions; the review questions how common such "bad" configurations are in practice under reasonable priors or with increasing M/d.  
- Concern (initialization realism): The random i.i.d. initialization analyzed in Theorem 2 may be unrealistic when data shows clear cluster structure, since practitioners would likely place centers proportional to visible cluster mass.  
- Clarification requested (M=2 discussion): The purpose and intended conclusion of the M=2 example/figure are unclear and should be clarified.  
- Clarification requested (Theorem 2 sketch): Explain necessity of condition (1) — why must all initialized centers fall in cluster neighborhoods rather than just matching cluster counts?  
- Clarification requested (Theorem 4 significance): Briefly state practical implications of avoiding strict saddles and whether saddle convergence is less problematic than bad local optima.
