EM can get stuck in local optima even with infinite samples and well-separated clusters, shown by a three-cluster example.  
For k-Gaussian mixtures, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)), so success probability decays exponentially in k.  
Gradient EM generally avoids strict saddle points, implying the main failure mode is bad local maxima rather than saddles.  
The paper demonstrates EM does not generally converge to global maxima even with infinite data.  
Reviewer finds the results predictable and the methods follow anticipated lines.  
The three-component example (two close, one far) produces a natural local maximum where centers cluster incorrectly, which is intuitive.  
When separations exceed √d, the problem resembles k-means, so similar local-maxima issues are expected.  
Extending the example to k components is nontrivial but conceptually expected.  
Random initialization failure mirrors known k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic failures; a direct comparison to those results (e.g., Kumar-Kannan) would be helpful.
