- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood for isotropic known-scale, well-specified models and proves local modes persist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Analysis restricts to well-spaced centre configurations and shows that as the number of components grows, random initialization will almost surely cause EM to miss the global optimum.
- Manuscript is generally well-written but contains several minor typos.
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 shows existence of bad local modes only for a specific centre configuration — unclear how likely such configurations are in practice.
- Request: Provide assumptions on the distribution of true centres and quantify the probability of “bad” centre configurations as k or dimensionality grows (Theorem 2 is unconvincing on this point).
- Clarification: “Bad” configurations refer to positions of the true centres, not merely bad EM initializations.
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2 analyzes random initialization by sampling centres from the mixture, but this may not reflect practical situations with clear, well-separated clusters (example: 3 Gaussians with two close and one isolated).
- Questioning representativeness: Is the random initialization model appropriate when cluster sizes and separations make intuitive initial placements unlikely to fail?
- Specific issue: Lines 74–81 example does not convincingly address Sresbro’s conjecture for k=2 and its purpose is unclear.
- Specific issue: Lines 233–235 — necessity of requirement (1) is not apparent; does initializing a centre between two clusters fail to guarantee correct convergence?
- Specific issue: Lines 267–273 show EM unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint, but the practical significance is unclear — is saddlepoint convergence more or less problematic than convergence to a local optimum?
- Minor copy-editing corrections (suggested rephrasings and fixes): Line 18: add “that”; Line 38: “estimates”; Line 60: “under which conditions”; Line 138: clarify O(d^{1/2})-separated definition; Line 172: remove/rewrite redundant sentence; Line 177: “equivalent to”; Line 218: define “well-separated” constant; Line 220: fix duplicate “the”; Line 225: define \Omega(k); Line 245: “coincidence”; Line 249: “Recall that for uniformly weighted”; Line 308: “claim that”.
