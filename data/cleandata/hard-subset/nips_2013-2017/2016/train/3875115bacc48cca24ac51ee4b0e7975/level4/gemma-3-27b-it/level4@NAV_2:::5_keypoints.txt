- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples that show no local maxima-free likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.  
- Uses a general framework and standard likelihood-maximization techniques (EM and gradient EM), emphasizing a common initialization scheme.  
- Main theorems prove existence of bad local maxima and that EM/gradient EM converge to these suboptimal points with exponentially high probability under random initialization.  
- Provides an intuitive illustration via the simpler M=3, d=1 case.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, effectively balancing space and intuition.  
- The question is significant, illuminating non-convex optimization challenges even in the infinite-sample/asymptotic setting for simple distributions.  
- Reviewer didn’t fully examine the supplement; recommends estimating the constants in the probability bounds since M affects convergence probability and could reveal phase transitions.  
- If precise analytical constants are hard, the reviewer suggests adding simulations to demonstrate the phenomenon’s observability.  
- This methodological suggestion does not detract from the work’s novelty or impact.
