- Provides a definitive negative answer to Srebro’s 2007 question by constructing counterexamples showing local maxima exist in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.  
- Main theorems rigorously establish a general class of counterexamples resolving the long-standing theoretical question.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM with standard random initialization converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability as sample size grows.  
- Supplies a detailed, accessible proof for the simple case k=3, d=1 to build intuition alongside general results.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and balances conciseness with sufficient technical detail.  
- Advances fundamental understanding of non-convex optimization in the asymptotic regime with clear implications for statistical learning and algorithm design.  
- Theoretical contribution is novel, impactful, and undermines assumptions that infinite-sample landscapes are free of local maxima.  
- Minor suggestion: provide explicit estimates for the constants in the probability bounds or add small-scale empirical validation to illustrate the phase transition/sample-size thresholds.  
- Overall assessment: rigorous, significant work that requires no major revisions and is a strong candidate for NIPS.
