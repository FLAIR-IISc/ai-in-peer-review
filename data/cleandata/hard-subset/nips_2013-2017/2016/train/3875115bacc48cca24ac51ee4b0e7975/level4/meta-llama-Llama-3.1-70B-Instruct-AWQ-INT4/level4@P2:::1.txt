This paper studies the convergence of the EM algorithm and iterative heuristics for Maximum Likelihood Estimation for learning mixtures of spherical Gaussians. The main results show that these algorithms may not converge to global maxima, even with infinite samples. The authors present three key findings: (1) algorithms like EM can get stuck in local optima when the mixture has three clusters that are very far apart, (2) random initialization for EM only succeeds with at most exp(-Ω(k)) probability for mixtures of k Gaussians, and (3) gradient EM will not converge to strict saddle points generically, implying that bad local maxima are typically the issue.

The paper provides a coherent set of results that demonstrate the non-convergence of EM to global maxima. However, the reviewer finds the results not entirely surprising, and the techniques follow expected lines. The main counterexample is a mixture with three components, where two are closer to each other, and the third is very far from both. The authors show that for the log-likelihood objective, there is a local maximum with one center near the first two components and two centers near the far-off cluster. This behavior is fairly expected, particularly when the centers are separated by >> √d. Under this amount of separation, learning mixtures of spherical Gaussians becomes similar to k-means clustering.

The reviewer notes that similar examples provide bad instances of Lloyd's algorithm for k-means clustering. The extension of this example to k components with a recursive construction requires some effort but is also somewhat expected. Similarly, random initialization fails for similar reasons as for k-means clustering, with the same failure probability. The reviewer suggests that a comparison with k-means (and Lloyd's heuristic) that contrasts this result would be useful, particularly given the convergence guarantees for Lloyd's heuristic for k-means clustering under sufficient separation conditions.

Strengths of the paper include its coherent set of results and the presentation of a clear, albeit not surprising, story about the non-convergence to global optima. However, the reviewer finds the results and techniques to be somewhat expected and not particularly groundbreaking.
