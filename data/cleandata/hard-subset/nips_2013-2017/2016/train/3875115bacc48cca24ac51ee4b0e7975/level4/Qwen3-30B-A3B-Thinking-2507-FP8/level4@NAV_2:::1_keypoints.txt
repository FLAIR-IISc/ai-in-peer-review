- Addresses convergence of EM for GMMs, focusing on existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood.  
- Main result 1: Constructs a well-separated, equally-weighted 3-component spherical GMM with arbitrarily bad local maxima, disproving Srebro's 2007 conjecture.  
- Main result 2: Shows EM and a first-order variant with random initialization converge to bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)} for M ≥ 3.  
- Builds on prior work (Balakrishnan et al. 2015; Dasgupta & Schulman 2007) while filling a gap in population-level EM behavior.  
- Technical contributions are sound with rigorous proofs and clear handling of population vs finite-sample issues.  
- Limits acknowledged: results apply for M ≥ 3 and to specific equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated settings.  
- Paper is well-organized with precise statements and good geometric intuition for the construction.  
- Would benefit from more discussion of practical implications and initialization strategies beyond random sampling.  
- Novelty: resolves a longstanding open question and provides a non-trivial recursive extension from M=3 to general M.  
- Distinguishes bad local maxima from strict saddle points (Theorem 4), clarifying optimization landscape behavior.  
- Significance: provides theoretical justification for careful initialization and supports method-of-moments approaches.  
- Strengths: rigorous resolution of the open problem, clear connection to algorithmic consequences, and valuable theoretical distinctions.  
- Weaknesses: the 3-component construction may seem intuitive to experts and comparisons to k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm literature are insufficient.  
- Techniques for failure probability follow expected non-convex optimization analyses, limiting perceived novelty for some reviewers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen comparisons with k-means literature and elaborate practical implications.
