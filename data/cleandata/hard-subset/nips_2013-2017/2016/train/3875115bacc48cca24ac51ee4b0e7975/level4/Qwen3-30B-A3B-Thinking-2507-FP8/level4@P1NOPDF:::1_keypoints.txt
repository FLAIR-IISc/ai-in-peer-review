- Paper shows EM can fail to converge to global optima for spherical Gaussian mixtures via three results: a 3-component local maximum case, exponentially small success of random initialization for k components, and gradient EM avoiding strict saddles so local maxima are the main issue.  
- The three results form a coherent narrative but largely confirm established expectations rather than offering surprising new insights.  
- Central counterexample: two nearby clusters and one isolated cluster (separation > Θ(√d)) produce a local maximum where one center serves the two close clusters and two centers fall on the isolated component.  
- That counterexample is intuitive because, under strong separation, the problem reduces to k-means-like clustering with non-overlapping clusters.  
- Similar failure modes are known for k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm; the recursive extension to k components is technically sound but follows these known constructions.  
- The bound on random initialization success mirrors k-means results; known fixes like distance-squared sampling apply here too.  
- The paper’s main contribution does not substantially advance beyond existing k-means analyses (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., Kumar & Kannan).  
- Authors should explicitly compare and contrast their results with Kumar & Kannan’s convergence guarantees to clarify the relationship between EM and Lloyd’s algorithm under separation.  
- For a top venue the work needs either a regime where EM fundamentally diverges from k-means (e.g., moderate separation) or evidence that EM fails where k-means succeeds; otherwise the contribution seems incremental.  
- The technical presentation is sound, but the manuscript would be strengthened by a clearer articulation of the gap relative to k-means literature and explicit contextualization of novelty.
