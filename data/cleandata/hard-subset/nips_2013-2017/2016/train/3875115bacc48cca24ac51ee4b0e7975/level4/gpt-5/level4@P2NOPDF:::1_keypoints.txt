- EM and related iterative ML heuristics can get trapped in suboptimal local maxima even with three well-separated clusters and infinite data (population setting).
- For k-component mixtures, random initialization causes EM to succeed with probability at most exponentially small in k.
- Gradient EM typically avoids strict saddle points, so failures are mainly due to bad local maxima rather than saddle stagnation.
- Central counterexample: a 3-component mixture with two close components and one very far leads to a log-likelihood local maximum that merges the close pair and duplicates the far component.
- When true-center separation ≫ sqrt(d), spherical Gaussian learning behaves like k-means, making the counterexample intuitive and analogous to known Lloyd’s failures.
- The recursive extension to k components is nontrivial but yields qualitatively similar bad instances as in k-means literature.
- The negative result for random initialization mirrors k-means’ exponential failure probability, suggesting standard mitigations (≈k log k seeds or k-means++ sampling).
- The paper’s results are coherent and carefully executed but largely unsurprising given the k-means analogy.
- The manuscript would be strengthened by a direct, systematic comparison with Lloyd’s algorithm and prior guarantees (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) to separate EM-specific issues from k-means-like behavior.
- Overall: a unified, well-argued set of impossibility results for EM under high separation, but with limited novelty beyond existing k-means insights.
