- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known component count, known uniform weights, and identity covariances, focusing on mean estimation.  
- Proves existence of local maxima of the likelihood with values arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum, even for large samples (resolves Srebro 2007 open question).  
- Shows randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to a critical point whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the maximum.  
- Demonstrates the same high-probability failure result holds for gradient EM.  
- Uses a simplified, well-specified model (known components/weights/covariances) to make technical proofs tractable; reviewer finds this reasonable.  
- Results imply practitioners must be careful with initialization when using EM for Gaussian mixtures.  
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting, informative, and a valuable theoretical contribution on EM limitations.  
- Concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) needs more details—limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains and continuity arguments are not fully justified.  
- Suggestion: expand the proof of Theorem 1 (even at the cost of shortening other discussions) to better convince readers.  
- Concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is harder to follow; derivation of Equation (5) should be explained more clearly.  
- Overall assessment: well-written with clear/concise proofs but some gaps in detail; Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10.  
- High marks for impact: Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
