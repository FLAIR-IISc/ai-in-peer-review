- Paper studies population likelihood landscape of Gaussian Mixture Models and refutes Srebro (2007)’s conjecture that well-behaved GMMs have no spurious local maxima.
- Main theoretical result 1: For any M ≥ 3, the population log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for well-separated, spherical, uniformly weighted mixtures.
- Main theoretical result 2: Standard EM with initialization from the true data distribution converges to these suboptimal critical points with probability tending to 1 exponentially in M.
- Main theoretical result 3: A first-order variant of EM (gradient-ascent equivalent) also fails, and the failure is due to bad local maxima rather than many strict saddles.
- Contribution/strength: Highly original, significant, and counterintuitive negative answer to an important open question, with implications for initialization methods.
- Contribution/strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis in a simplified but reasonable setting (known weights, identity covariances) that yields valuable insights.
- Weakness: Proof sketch of Theorem 1 is too brief—arguments about limits of suprema and changing/unbounded domains need rigorous justification (e.g., uniform convergence or epsilon-delta).
- Weakness: Construction appears to be presented for d=1 while theorems claim general d; the paper should clarify how proofs extend to higher dimensions or discuss additional challenges.
- Weakness: The connection showing first-order EM is equivalent to gradient ascent is stated but not derived—include a brief derivation or intuition (e.g., relation between E[w_i(X)(X-μ_i)] and the gradient).
- Recommendation: Expand and clarify the central proof (even at expense of other sections) and address dimensional and algorithmic-exposition gaps to strengthen the paper’s persuasiveness.
