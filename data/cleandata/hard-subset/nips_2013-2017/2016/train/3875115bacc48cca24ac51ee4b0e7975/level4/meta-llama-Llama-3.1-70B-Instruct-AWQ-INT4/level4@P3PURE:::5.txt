The paper provides an answer to the open question posed by Srebro in his 2007 paper "Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?" and resolves it in the negative through the construction of a general class of counterexamples. The authors begin by outlining the schemes by which the likelihood is maximized in the setting of Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms. They also address the issue of initialization and present a common choice. The authors then state their primary theorems, which give a counterexample to the question posed by Srebro [2007]. Furthermore, they demonstrate that even with the common choice of random initialization, the EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To provide intuition for their proofs, the authors consider a simple case (k=3, d=1) and prove it. This paper is very clear and well-written, striking a good balance between the space requirements of the paper and the need to provide intuition for their theoretical results. The question addressed by the authors is of great importance for further understanding the issues that arise with non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.

I did not carefully examine the supplementary material, but estimates of the constants in the probability term provided would be valuable. This is important because it seems unlikely that one would consider k to be too large. Estimates of the constants would be useful in determining when we can expect to see convergence to suboptimal solutions in the asymptotic limit (i.e., where is the phase transition?). If the constants are too complicated or do not make sense, supporting simulations would be helpful in at least demonstrating that this phenomenon can be observed. However, this issue does not detract from the novelty or impact of this paper.
