- Studies convergence of EM for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and shows EM can fail to reach the global maximum even in idealized settings.

- In the infinite-sample case, EM can converge to a suboptimal local maximum for a simple mixture of three well-separated clusters.

- For mixtures of k Gaussians, standard random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)), i.e., exponentially small in k.

- Gradient-based EM variants will generally avoid strict saddle points, so convergence to poor local maxima—not saddles—is the main failure mode.

- Central counterexample: two nearby components and one distant component lead EM to place one center near the two close components and two centers on the distant one.

- This failure is intuitive when inter-center distances are ≫ sqrt(d), where component overlap is negligible.

- Under large separation, the ML problem for spherical Gaussians closely resembles the k-means objective, so EM’s failures mirror known k-means pathologies.

- The extension to k components via a recursive construction is technically correct but conceptually foreseeable given the k-means analogy.

- The analysis that random initialization fails with high probability aligns with prior k-means results that motivated methods like k-means++ or O(k log k) initializations.

- Main weakness: results feel incremental and unsurprising to the community; paper should explicitly compare to k-means literature.

- Suggested improvement: discuss and contrast high-separation vs low-separation regimes and relate results to existing work (e.g., Kumar & Kannan; Balakrishnan, Wainwright, Yu).
