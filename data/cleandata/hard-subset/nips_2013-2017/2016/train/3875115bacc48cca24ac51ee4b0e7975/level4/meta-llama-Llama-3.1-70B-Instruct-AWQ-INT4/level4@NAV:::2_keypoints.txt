- Paper studies EM for estimating means in Gaussian mixture models with known component count, weights, and covariances.
- Theoretical approach presents three negative results revealing limitations of EM.
- Result 1: Even with large sample sizes, the likelihood can have local maxima whose values are arbitrarily lower than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to a critical point with likelihood far below the maximum.
- Result 3: Similar negative convergence results hold for the gradient EM algorithm.
- Authors restrict analysis to a simplified model (known components, uniform weights, identity covariances) to enable rigorous proofs; this simplification is justified given technical difficulty.
- Analysis is presented for general dimension d, but proofs are carried out primarily for d=1.
- Reviewer requests discussion of feasiblity and challenges in extending proofs to higher dimensions.
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too concise (lines 288–306) and needs elaboration on limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.
- Continuity of the likelihood does not automatically justify the claimed convergence properties; the argument requires more detail.
- Section on Gradient EM (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation and context for Equation (5) should be clarified.
- Overall assessment: the question is intriguing, results are interesting and practically relevant, but key proofs and explanations need more thorough exposition.
