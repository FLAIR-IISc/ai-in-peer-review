- Paper analyzes EM for estimating Gaussian mixture means under known/fixed number of components, mixture weights, and covariance matrices.  
- Main theoretical contributions: three negative results about EM behavior.  
- Result 1: Local maxima of the likelihood can exist even with large sample sizes and can have arbitrarily low likelihood values.  
- Result 2: Randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to a critical point with suboptimal likelihood.  
- Result 3: The above negative results also hold for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Reviewer finds the question important and the simplifying assumptions (known components/weights, identity covariances, d unspecified) reasonable given technical complexity.  
- Proofs are presented only for d = 1 despite claims of generality; authors should discuss feasibility or obstacles for extension to higher dimensions.  
- Primary concern: Theorem 1’s proof in the main text is condensed; taking the supremum after a limit is not justified sufficiently and continuity alone may not guarantee the claimed convergence.  
- Recommendation: Expand and clarify the proof of Theorem 1 (even at the cost of shortening introductory material) to increase reader confidence.  
- Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; provide a clearer derivation and explanation of Equation (5) and the intermediate steps.  
- Overall judgment: Valuable and significant theoretical contribution; addressing the above concerns would strengthen the paper and support publication.
