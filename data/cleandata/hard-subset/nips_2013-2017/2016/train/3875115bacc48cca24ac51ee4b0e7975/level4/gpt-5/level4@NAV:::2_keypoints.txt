- Paper analyzes EM for estimating means in Gaussian mixtures with known component count, weights, and covariances, proving three negative results about EM behavior.
- Main theoretical results: (1) likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum even with large samples.
- Main theoretical results: (2) with random initialization EM converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood is arbitrarily below the maximum.
- Main theoretical results: (3) an analogous negative result holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the simplified setting (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) acceptable given technical arguments.
- Paper is stated for general dimension d but proofs are carried out for d = 1; reviewer requests clarification on extending proofs to arbitrary d or explanation of obstacles when d > 1.
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too brief—taking limits of suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains is nontrivial and needs more justification.
- Reviewer suggests expanding the Theorem 1 proof (possibly shortening other parts) to convincingly justify convergence statements.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is harder to follow and Equation (5) needs clearer derivation.
