- Main result 1: Provides a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Main result 2: Proves that with random initialization, the probability of converging to a good local maximum can be exponentially small in k for certain mean configurations.  
- Main result 3: Shows gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proof is verified as sound; Theorem 2’s proof strategy appears reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Clarity of results: Authors give clear interpretations for all three theorems following their statements.  
- Novelty: Establishes a novel formal connection between the true means’ configuration and the local maxima structure of the GMM log-likelihood.  
- Insight: Theorem 2’s key insight that hierarchical grouping of means determines local maxima for k>3 is particularly clever.  
- Potential theoretical impact: Results could inspire further study into local optima structures for related clustering tasks like k-means.  
- Suggested future work: Investigate whether Lemma 7’s result extends to dimensions d > 1.  
- Practical impact: Highlights importance of initializing with sufficient points per true cluster and may inform new algorithm designs.  
- Presentation: Paper is very well-written with strong problem setup, intuition, and helpful interpretations of analytical results.
