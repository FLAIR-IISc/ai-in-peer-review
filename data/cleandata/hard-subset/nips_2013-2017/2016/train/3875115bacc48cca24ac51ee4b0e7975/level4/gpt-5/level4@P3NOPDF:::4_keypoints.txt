- Paper analyzes geometry of population log-likelihood for equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs and proves three main results.
- Result 1: Constructs a (k=3, d=1) counterexample showing suboptimal local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Shows a mean configuration where random initialization converges to bad local maxima with high probability; success probability can decay exponentially in k.
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proof is sound; Theorem 2’s strategy is reasonable though some appendix details unverified; assumptions and arguments are coherent.
- Novelty: Formalizes how mean configuration drives local maxima, with a novel hierarchical grouping idea for k>3 and a sharp k=3 counterexample.
- Theoretical impact: Advances understanding of nonconvex likelihood landscapes and may inform frameworks for local optima in clustering models.
- Practical impact: Underscores importance of careful initialization and could guide improved seeding or continuation methods; extension to d>1 would increase relevance.
- Clarity: Well written and organized, with clear setup, intuition, and useful interpretations; more illustrative figures/examples would aid accessibility.
- Limitation: Analysis restricted to equal weights and identity covariance, limiting generality.
- Limitation: Negative results rely on specific mean configurations; robustness to perturbations is unclear.
- Limitation: The “almost sure” avoidance of saddles is expected under standard assumptions and needs clearer specification of randomness and conditions.
- Suggestions: Discuss leveraging hierarchical grouping to design initialization heuristics or multi-start strategies that avoid bad basins.
- Suggestions: Comment on extending Lemma 7 and related arguments to d>1 and mild covariance heterogeneity (e.g., spherical with different scales).
- Suggestions: Specify precisely over which randomness the “almost sure” statements hold (initialization only, data, etc.).
- Overall assessment: Clear, technically solid, and impactful work; compelling counterexample and exponential failure probability make it a strong contribution subject to minor clarifications on generality.
