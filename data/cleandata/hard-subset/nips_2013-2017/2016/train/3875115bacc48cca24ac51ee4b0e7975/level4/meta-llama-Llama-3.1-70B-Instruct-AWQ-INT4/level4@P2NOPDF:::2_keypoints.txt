- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known number of components, weights, and covariances.  
- Provides three negative theoretical results: existence of local maxima with arbitrarily low likelihood even for large samples.  
- Shows random initialization of EM converges with high probability to critical points with arbitrarily low likelihood.  
- Proves the same negative convergence result for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Commendable and interesting question; simplifications (known K, uniform weights, identity covariance) are acceptable for rigorous proofs.  
- Paper is written for general dimension d but proofs are only carried out for d=1; authors should discuss extensibility to higher dimensions or obstacles.  
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too concise and needs more detailed justification, especially limits involving suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.  
- Continuity alone does not justify the convergences claimed for maxima; more rigorous argumentation is required.  
- Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) should be clarified.  
- Overall: results are interesting and useful; addressing the above concerns would strengthen the paper.
