- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question on existence of local maxima in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihoods.
- Provides a negative answer by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing such local maxima exist.
- Reviews likelihood maximization methods for GMMs, including EM and gradient-based EM, and typical initialization strategies.
- States main theorems that establish the counterexamples to Srebro’s conjecture.
- Proves that with common random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to these non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Supplies a simplified, intuitive proof for the specific case k=3, d=1.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, balancing theoretical rigor and intuition within space limits.
- Results are important for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in asymptotic/simple distribution settings.
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material and requests estimates for constants in the probability bounds.
- Estimating those constants would help identify the sample/parameter regime (phase transition) where convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes significant.
- If closed-form constants are difficult, supporting simulations are recommended to demonstrate observability.
- Reviewer notes the request for constants/simulations does not detract from the novelty or impact of the work.
