1. Resolves Srebro (2007) by constructing arbitrarily bad population local maxima for uniform mixtures of M ≥ 3 well-separated isotropic Gaussians (Theorem 1).  
2. Shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)} (Theorem 2).  
3. Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, indicating failures are due to bad local maxima (Theorem 4).  
4. Technical approach is rigorous, with elegant construction and detailed proofs.  
5. Original contribution: first demonstration that bad population local maxima exist for M ≥ 3, overturning a long-standing conjecture.  
6. Significant implication: provides theoretical justification for sophisticated EM initialization strategies even for well-separated mixtures.  
7. Paper is well-written with clear definitions, logical flow, and thorough appendices.  
8. Practical relevance is unclear: constructed pathological configurations (e.g., clustered centers) may be rare in real data and need discussion.  
9. The M=2 example is unconvincing and its purpose requires better justification or clarification.  
10. Minor grammatical and notational fixes needed (e.g., phrasing, clarify "well-separated" constant, explain Ω(M)).  
11. Arguments for acceptance: resolves a long-standing open problem with strong theoretical contributions and implications for EM.  
12. Arguments against acceptance: unclear how commonly the pathological configurations occur in practice, weakening applicability.  
13. Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify practical relevance, fix the M=2 example, and correct minor issues.
