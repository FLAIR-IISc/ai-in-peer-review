- Paper answers Srebro (2007) question, providing a definitive negative answer via a general class of counterexamples.
- Authors review standard likelihood maximization methods for GMMs, including EM and gradient EM.
- Paper discusses initialization and uses a common, practical random initialization strategy.
- Main theorems formally refute Srebro’s conjecture.
- Shows EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.
- Includes a simplified illustrative proof for k=3, d=1 to build intuition.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written and balances space constraints with theoretical intuition.
- The question is important for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in the infinite-sample/asymptotic limit.
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material and recommends providing estimates for constants in the probability bounds.
- If closed-form constants are infeasible, reviewer suggests supporting simulations to demonstrate the phase-transition behavior when k is moderate.
- Recommendation/critique does not diminish the paper’s novelty or overall impact.
