- Paper studies convergence of EM and iterative heuristics for maximum likelihood in mixtures of spherical Gaussians, showing non-convergence to global maxima.
- EM can get stuck in local optima even with infinite samples and well-separated clusters; demonstrated with a three-cluster example.
- For k-component mixtures, the probability that random initialization leads EM to the global optimum is at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Gradient EM typically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the main failure mode.
- The results form a coherent narrative that EM need not converge to global optima even with infinite data.
- The main pathological example has two nearby components and one far away, producing a local maximum where centers misallocate to clusters.
- When cluster separations are ≫ √d, the problem resembles k-means and the behavior becomes predictable.
- Similar failure modes are known for Lloyd’s k-means; the authors extend the three-component example to k via a recursive construction, which is unsurprising.
- The random-initialization failure rate for EM is comparable to that for k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm.
- Reviewer suggests adding a direct comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s (e.g., Kumar–Kannan) and to prior EM results for weak separation (e.g., Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu).
