- Paper studies whether the population likelihood for Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) can have bad local maxima.  
- Resolves Srebro (2007) by showing even well-separated, equally-weighted spherical GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Shows EM and a first-order EM variant with random initialization converge to such suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.  
- Proves the first-order EM algorithm avoids strict saddle points almost surely.  
- Highlights the practical importance of careful initialization even in favorable settings.  
- Makes significant theoretical contributions and introduces novel proof techniques relevant to non-convex optimization.  
- Distinguishes empirically and theoretically between bad local maxima and saddle points as failure modes for EM.  
- Concern: Theorem 1 proofs are presented for d=1, and the claimed extension to higher dimensions is not substantiated in the main text.  
- Concern: Proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is brief; the use of continuity to justify convergence over unbounded domains needs more detailed justification.  
- Concern: Exposition of the first-order EM algorithm (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) is hard to follow and needs a brief clarification.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, technically sound paper that addresses an important open question; minor clarifications requested and recommendation to accept.
