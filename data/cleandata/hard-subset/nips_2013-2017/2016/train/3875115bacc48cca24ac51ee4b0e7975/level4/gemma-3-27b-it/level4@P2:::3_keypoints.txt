- Paper studies likelihood landscape of isotropic, well-specified GMMs and shows bad local maxima can exist and be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Authors prove that, for well-spaced cluster configurations, random EM initialization will often converge to suboptimal points as the number of components grows.
- Theoretical results are rigorous and the paper is mostly well-written, addressing an important non-convexity problem in GMM estimation.
- Primary concern: practical relevance is unclear because Theorem 1 focuses on a specific center configuration without showing how likely such configurations are in practice.
- Request for clearer characterization of the probability of encountering “bad” configurations under reasonable distributions of true centers (as function of k and d).
- Theorem 2 is intended to address practical likelihood but reviewer is not convinced it sufficiently establishes real-world significance.
- Questioning the realism of the random initialization scheme analyzed—may not reflect typical practice when cluster structure in data is obvious.
- The two-component example (lines 74–81) is unclear and does not convincingly support the claimed extension of Srebro’s conjecture for k=2.
- Lines 233–235: the necessity of the initialization requirement (having a center between clusters) is not justified; reviewer unsure what it implies for convergence.
- Lines 267–273: paper notes EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint but fails to explain the practical importance or how saddle convergence compares to local-optimum convergence.
- Strength: provides novel, rigorous theoretical guarantees and may inspire further work on non-convex optimization.
- Strength: problem statement is clear and the writing is mostly accessible.
- Weakness: limited practical relevance due to reliance on specific configurations and initialization assumptions.
- Weakness: unclear motivation and missing justifications for certain claims weaken impact.
- Acceptance arguments: solid mathematical analysis and insights into EM limitations.
- Rejection arguments: questionable applicability to real-world data and insufficient treatment of how commonly problematic scenarios arise.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject—interesting theory but practical concerns and unanswered questions outweigh contributions.
- Minor fixes needed: multiple wording, notation, and typo corrections at specified line numbers (e.g., L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308).
