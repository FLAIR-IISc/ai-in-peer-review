- Counterexample to Srebro (2007): shows a well-separated GMM can have arbitrarily bad local maxima (M=3, 1D).
- Hierarchical construction: proves EM with standard random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with exponentially high probability in M.
- First-order EM behavior: almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures stem from bad local maxima rather than saddle points.
- Resolves a long-standing open question, with significant theoretical and practical implications for GMM optimization and initialization.
- Practical caution: even in idealized, well-separated, infinite-sample settings, naive random initialization can fail catastrophically.
- Originality: elegant and novel constructions tying geometric mean structure to spurious local optima.
- Technical quality: main proofs (Theorem 1) are convincing; algorithmic claims (Theorem 2) are well-reasoned and plausible.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and explains geometric intuition clearly.
- Minor weakness: would benefit from a brief discussion on extending the 1D construction to higher dimensions (d > 1).
- Acceptance arguments: definitive negative resolution of Srebro’s conjecture, strong negative result on EM with random init, novel proof techniques, and excellent exposition.
