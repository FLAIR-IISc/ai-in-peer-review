- Main result: Counterexample showing arbitrarily bad local maxima exist for a 1D mixture of three Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Main result: With random initialization, EM and first-order EM converge to a bad critical point with probability that decreases exponentially in the number of components M.
- Main result: First-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying poor performance arises from bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Motivation: Builds on identifiability and convergence literature and connects to recent work on optimization landscapes in high dimensions.
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written with consistent notation and well-structured proofs and intuition.
- Theoretical strength: Provides rigorous, non-trivial theoretical guarantees about the GMM log-likelihood landscape and EM behavior.
- Novelty/insight: Draws an original connection between mean configurations and landscape structure; hierarchical construction for Theorem 2 is clever.
- Practical implication: Highlights the need for careful initialization strategies for EM and related algorithms.
- Related work: Thorough and appropriate literature review situating the contribution within prior results.
- Limitation: Results rely on strong assumptions (equal weights, identity covariance), limiting immediate generality.
- Limitation: Paper shows random initialization fails but gives limited concrete guidance on practical initialization methods beyond citing prior clustering approaches.
- Limitation: Lacks empirical validation or simulations to support population-level theoretical claims on finite samples.
- Argument for acceptance: Tackles a fundamental open problem with novel, rigorous analysis and clear presentation.
- Argument for acceptance: Findings have potential theoretical and practical impact on understanding and designing algorithms for GMMs.
- Argument against acceptance: Strong assumptions and lack of empirical results reduce practical relevance.
- Argument against acceptance: Does not deliver definitive, practical initialization strategies.
- Overall assessment: Strong, important theoretical contribution worth acceptance, with recommendation to pursue extensions to more general GMMs and practical initialization/evaluation in future work.
