- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian mixture means with known weights/covariances, focusing on uniform weights and identity covariances.
- Main theoretical contribution: three negative results showing EM (and gradient EM) can converge to local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Results are relevant and practically informative for understanding EM behavior; simplifying assumptions are reasonable given technical arguments.
- Paper is framed for general dimension d, but proofs focus on d = 1; authors should clarify whether and how results extend to d > 1.
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too compressed and needs more detailed justification.
- Specifically, the steps around lines 288–306 involve taking limits of suprema over unbounded or expanding domains, and the interchange of limit and supremum is nontrivial.
- Continuity of the likelihood alone does not justify the convergence claims; the proof should address compactness/tightness or other conditions needed for those limit arguments.
- Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; Equation (5) derivation and its underlying assumptions need clarification.
- Overall judgement: interesting and potentially impactful work that would be significantly improved with a strengthened proof of Theorem 1 and clearer exposition of gradient EM.
