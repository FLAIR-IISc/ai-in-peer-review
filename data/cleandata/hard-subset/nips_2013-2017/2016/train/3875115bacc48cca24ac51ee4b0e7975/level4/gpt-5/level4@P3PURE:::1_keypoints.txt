- Studies EM convergence for spherical Gaussian mixtures and shows non-convergence to global maxima can occur even in the population (infinite-sample) limit.
- Claim: Even with three well-separated components, EM can get stuck in local optima.
- Claim: For k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Claim: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddles, so bad local maxima are typically the main obstruction.
- Central counterexample: three-component construction with two close components and one far one produces a local maximum with one center near the pair and two near the distant component.
- This behavior is expected under very large separation (≫ sqrt(d)), where clusters barely overlap and the problem resembles k-means.
- The paper extends the example to k components via a recursive construction; the extension is clean but follows known patterns.
- The random-initialization failure parallels k-means, motivating k log k initial centers or distance-squared sampling as in k-means methods.
- Overall: coherent and technically sound but largely unsurprising; a direct, careful comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s and relevant prior work (Kumar–Kannan, Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu) would strengthen the contribution.
