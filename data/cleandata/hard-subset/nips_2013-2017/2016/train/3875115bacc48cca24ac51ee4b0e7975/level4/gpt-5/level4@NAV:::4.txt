This paper investigates the landscape of local maxima of the log-likelihood for Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixture weights and identity covariance. It establishes three related results: 1) a counterexample (for k = 3, d = 1) showing local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum; 2) with random initialization, under a special configuration of the component means, the probability of avoiding a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k; 3) gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.

Summary: The paper analyzes the structure of local maxima in the log-likelihood of GMMs with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It presents three main findings: 1) a counterexample for k = 3 and d = 1 demonstrating local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily inferior to the global maximum; 2) under a particular arrangement of the means, random initialization yields an exponentially small (in k) probability of escaping poor local maxima; 3) gradient EM almost surely avoids convergence to saddle points.

Technical quality I verified the proofs in the main text (Theorem 1) and found them sound. Based on the analysis of Theorem 1, the proof strategy for Theorem 2 appears plausible, although I did not check the Appendix details. Each of the three theorems is well contextualized and interpreted after its statement.

Novelty & Originality The paper connects the configuration of component means in a GMM to the local maxima of its log-likelihood. While intuitive, this linkage feels genuinely original. The key idea in Theorem 2, building on Theorem 1—that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means governs the local maxima for k > 3—is particularly clever.

Potential impact & Usefulness The results are likely impactful for both theory and practice: theoretically, they may inspire analyses of the local-optima structure in related clustering objectives (e.g., k-means); it is also interesting to examine whether their Lemma 7 extends to d > 1; practically, the findings underscore the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may offer insights for designing new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation The paper is clearly written, presenting the results and the intuition behind the analysis effectively. The problem setup is well introduced, and the analyses are accompanied by clear intuition and interpretation.
