- Scope: The paper theoretically analyzes EM for estimating Gaussian-mixture means assuming known/fixed number of components, mixture weights, and covariance matrices.  
- Main negative results: (1) with large samples the likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum; (2) random initialization leads EM with high probability to such poor critical points; (3) the same negative result holds for gradient EM.  
- Simplifying assumptions: analysis restricted to uniform weights and identity covariances to enable rigorous proofs; these simplifications are reasonable and results remain informative.  
- Significance: the research question is interesting and important and the findings contribute to practical understanding of EM.  
- Dimensionality clarification: paper is framed for general dimension d but proofs are presented for d=1; authors should state whether results extend to d>1 and discuss additional technical challenges.  
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 in the main text is overly condensed and needs expansion for clarity.  
- Technical critique: the passage between lines 288–306 requires rigorous justification when taking limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains; continuity alone is insufficient.
