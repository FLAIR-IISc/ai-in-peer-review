- Paper proves three negative results for EM: existence of arbitrarily suboptimal local maxima, high-probability convergence to suboptimal critical points from random init, and analogous failures for gradient EM.  
- The question is interesting and the findings are valuable for understanding EM in practice.  
- Authors restrict to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) to keep proofs rigorous; this simplification is justified but should be noted.  
- Although the paper claims general dimension d, all proofs are for d = 1; the authors should clarify extension to arbitrary d or discuss challenges for d > 1.  
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too brief (lines 288–306); steps involving limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains are nontrivial and need more detail.  
- The use of likelihood continuity does not automatically justify the stated convergence of suprema; more rigorous arguments are required to convince the reader.  
- Reviewer did not check the supplementary material, so completeness of omitted details is uncertain.  
- Minor concern: the Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) and the derivation of Equation (5) are unclear and need further explanation.
