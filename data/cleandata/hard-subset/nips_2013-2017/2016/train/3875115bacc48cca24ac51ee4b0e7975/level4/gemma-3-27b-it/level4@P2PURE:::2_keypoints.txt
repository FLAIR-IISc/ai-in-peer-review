- Paper studies EM for estimating means in Gaussian mixtures with known/fixed component count, weights, and covariances, proving three negative results.  
- Main negative results: likelihood can have local maxima with arbitrarily low value versus global maximum even with large samples.  
- Main negative results: random initialization of EM converges with high probability to sub-optimal critical points.  
- Main negative results: gradient EM exhibits the same unfavorable convergence behavior.  
- Theoretical focus uses a simplified model: known number of components, uniform weights, and identity covariances — deemed reasonable for technical tractability.  
- Results are stated for general dimension d, but all proofs presented are for d = 1.  
- Reviewer requests discussion on extending proofs to arbitrary dimensions or an explanation of the obstacles preventing such extensions.  
- Primary concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too concise and needs a more detailed, expanded presentation (lines 288–306 flagged).  
- Specific technical issue: taking limits of suprema/maxima over unbounded parameter domains is nontrivial and not fully justified in the current proof.  
- Continuity of the likelihood alone does not guarantee the claimed convergence properties; more argument is needed.  
- Minor concern: the Gradient EM discussion (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are hard to follow and should be elaborated.
