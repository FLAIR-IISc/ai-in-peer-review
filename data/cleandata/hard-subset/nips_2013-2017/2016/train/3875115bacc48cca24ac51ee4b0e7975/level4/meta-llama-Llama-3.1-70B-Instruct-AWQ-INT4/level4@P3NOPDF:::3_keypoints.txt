- Paper analyzes local modes in Gaussian mixture log-likelihoods for isotropic components with known scale, showing local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Authors study configurations where centers split between well-spaced clusters and show random initialization makes EM almost certainly miss the global optimum as the number of components grows.
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 relies on a specific center configuration; reviewer questions how likely such configurations are in practice and requests assumptions on center distributions to show bad configurations become more likely with larger k or d.
- Theorem 2 is claimed to address practical likelihood, but the reviewer is unconvinced and asks for a clear probabilistic statement about the prevalence of “bad” configurations.
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2’s random initialization scheme (sampling mixture points) is argued to be unrealistic in practice, since practitioners would avoid initializing centers in low-density regions for clearly clustered data.
- Reviewer gives a concrete skepticism: in a 3-Gaussian example with two close and one far, it is unlikely someone would initialize EM with two centers in the sparse region and one in the dense region.
- Minor conceptual concern: Example on lines 74–81 is unclear and does not convince the reviewer that Srebro’s conjecture holds for k=2.
- Minor conceptual concern: Lines 233–235 — requirement (2) is understood but the necessity of requirement (1) is unclear; reviewer questions the practical importance of proving EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points versus local optima.
