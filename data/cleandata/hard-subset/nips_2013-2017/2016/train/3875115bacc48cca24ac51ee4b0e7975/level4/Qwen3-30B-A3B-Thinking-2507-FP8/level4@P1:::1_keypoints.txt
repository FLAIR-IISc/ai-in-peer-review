- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question: population likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima.  
- Shows even well-separated, equally-weighted spherical Gaussians can admit local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.  
- Establishes first-order EM avoids strict saddles, so failures arise from bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Provides a rigorous geometric construction for M = 3: two nearby centers and one far center produce a poor local maximum.  
- Extends the M = 3 construction recursively to general M, yielding exponential failure probability for random initialization.  
- Technical strengths: precise proofs, clear exposition, and sound theoretical arguments.  
- Connects GMM likelihood landscape to k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm counterexamples, offering insightful parallels.  
- Novelty: geometric construction and explicit exponential failure probability distinguish the work from prior expectations.  
- Practical implication: underscores the necessity of careful initialization (e.g., method-of-moments) for reliable EM performance.  
- Limitation: analysis is in the population (infinite-sample) setting, though finite-sample extension is claimed straightforward.  
- Weakness: comparison with related k-means literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2004) could be more developed.  
- Overall: significant, well-written contribution with clear theoretical and practical implications.
