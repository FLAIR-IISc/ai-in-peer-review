- Resolves whether population likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima by constructing a counterexample for M = 3 showing arbitrarily worse local maxima.
- Proves that EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to such bad local maxima with probability at least 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.
- Shows first-order EM avoids strict saddle points almost surely, attributing failures to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Technical quality is strong: rigorous proofs, clear geometric intuition for the counterexample, and a well-motivated hierarchical construction.
- Proof techniques are elegant and novel, particularly the recursive mean-configuration argument used to make favorable initialization exponentially unlikely.
- Properly contextualizes results with prior work (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2015; Srebro 2007) and fills a gap in the literature.
- Original contribution: first resolution of the infinite-sample case for M ≥ 3 and a novel hierarchical approach to landscape analysis.
- Practical significance: indicates careful initialization is essential for EM even in well-separated mixtures and recommends methods like method-of-moments for reliability.
- The exponential decay of success probability with M is a striking, application-relevant finding.
- Presentation is exceptionally clear, well-organized, reproducible, and supported by effective figures and definitions.
- Strengths: resolves a significant open question, sound and well-motivated proofs, clear practical implications, excellent writing.
- Weaknesses: restricted to equal-weighted, spherical, well-separated mixtures; limited discussion of finite-sample extensions and whether exponential failure probability is tight or avoidable with better initialization.
- Pro-accept: major theoretical contribution with clear theoretical and practical implications, technically sound and well-written.
- Con-accept: limited immediate applicability beyond the specific GMM setting and incomplete treatment of finite-sample behavior.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
