- Paper analyzes EM convergence for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and shows EM can fail to reach global maxima.  
- Main technical results: with three well-separated clusters and infinite samples, EM can be trapped in local optima.  
- For k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with only exponentially small probability (≈ exp(−Ω(k))).  
- Gradient EM typically avoids strict saddles, implying problematic critical points are bad local maxima rather than saddles.  
- Overall claim: EM does not generally converge to global maxima even with infinite data.  
- Reviewer finds the results technically sound but largely unsurprising and following expected techniques.  
- Core example: a 3-component mixture with two close components and one far-away component yields an intuitive local maximum configuration.  
- When component separation ≫ √d, the problem behavior resembles k-means clustering, so the example is expected.  
- The authors generalize the example to k components via a recursive construction; this is plausible though not novel.  
- The exponential failure of random initialization parallels known k-means failures and similar failure probabilities.  
- This motivates alternative initializations (e.g., picking k log k centers or distance-squared sampling).  
- Suggestion: include a direct comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic and cite Kumar and Kannan’s convergence guarantees as a useful contrast.
