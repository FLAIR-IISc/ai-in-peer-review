- Studies local maxima structure of GMM log-likelihood under equal weights and identity covariances.
- Constructs a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Shows for certain pathological true-mean configurations the probability of converging to such bad local maxima under random initialization can be exponentially small in k.
- Proves gradient EM almost surely avoids converging to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical arguments are sound; Theorem 1 proof checked and Theorem 2’s high-level idea is reasonable.
- All three theorems are well-motivated and clearly interpreted in the paper.
- Main novelty: formal link between geometric configuration of true means and the log-likelihood landscape.
- Key insight for k>3: hierarchical grouping among true means determines spurious local maxima.
- Potential impact: could inspire similar landscape analyses (e.g., k-means) and influence theory and practice of mixture estimation.
- Open question noted: whether Lemma 7’s result extends to higher dimensions (d > 1).
- Practical takeaway: emphasizes critical importance of initialization for EM and suggests seeding initial points inside true clusters.
- Presentation is excellent—clear, well-motivated, and easy to follow.
