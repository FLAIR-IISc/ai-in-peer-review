- Paper proves existence of local maxima for GMMs (k=3, d=1) whose likelihoods can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Shows EM with random initialization can fail with exponentially small probability of avoiding spurious local maxima under a specific true-means configuration.
- Proves gradient-based EM almost surely avoids converging to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical proofs are sound; Theorem 1 verified and Theorem 2’s strategy appears reasonable though appendix details not fully checked.
- Authors provide clear immediate interpretations of all three theorems.
- Novel contribution: rigorous connection between true means configuration and local maxima structure of the likelihood.
- Theorem 2’s core idea: hierarchical grouping of true means explains local maxima for k > 3.
- Potential theoretical impact: could inspire study of local optima in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means).
- Practical implication: emphasizes careful initialization and seeding enough points per true cluster for GMMs.
- Suggestion/extension: investigate whether Lemma 7’s result generalizes to dimensions d > 1.
- Paper is very well written, organized, and uses intuition effectively to explain results.
