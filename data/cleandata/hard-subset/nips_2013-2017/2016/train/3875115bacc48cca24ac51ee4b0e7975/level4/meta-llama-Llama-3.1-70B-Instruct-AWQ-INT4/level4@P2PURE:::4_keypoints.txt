- Studies local maxima of the log-likelihood for GMMs with equal mixing weights and identity covariance.  
- Result 1: Provides a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: Shows that with random initialization, the probability of avoiding a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k for a specific mean configuration.  
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1 proofs are sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Novelty: Establishes an original connection between mean configuration and local maxima structure, with a clever hierarchical grouping idea for k>3.  
- Potential impact: Results could influence theory and practice (e.g., k-means), suggest checking Lemma 7 for d>1, and emphasize careful seeding of cluster centers.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-presented with clear intuition and interpretations for all analyses.
