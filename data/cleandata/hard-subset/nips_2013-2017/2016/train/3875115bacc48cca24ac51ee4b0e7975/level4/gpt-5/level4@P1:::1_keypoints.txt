- Analyzes population likelihood of equally weighted, spherical GMMs and behavior of EM and first-order EM under random initialization.
- Structural result: for M ≥ 3 there exist spurious local maxima even for well-separated mixtures, with arbitrarily large likelihood gaps, resolving Srebro (2007) negatively.
- Algorithmic result: standard random initialization leads EM and first-order EM to converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).
- First-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are driven by bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Complements prior work that requires good initialization for local convergence and relates to consistent clustering/moment estimators.
- Conceptual constructions: a concrete M=3 counterexample (two nearby, one far) and a recursive extension for general M, echoing k-means pathologies.
- Strength: resolves a notable open conject with rigorous, arbitrarily bad local maxima constructions.
- Strength: provides sharp, quantitative exponential failure probability for random initialization in M.
- Strength: clarifies landscape vs dynamics by showing saddle-avoidance and local maxima as failure mode.
- Strength: recursive instance construction is clean and technically competent.
- Weakness: phenomenon is similar to known Lloyd’s/k-means issues under large separation, reducing surprise.
- Weakness: proof strategies follow expected k-means-like arguments (trapping within clusters).
- Weakness: lacks explicit comparison to clustering-based guarantees and detailed discussion of EM vs Lloyd’s under separation.
- Quality: technically sound, clear population-level arguments, and consistent first-order EM analysis with step-size condition s ∈ (0,1).
- Clarity: generally well written, but would benefit from clearer comparison to k-means and dependence on dimension/separation.
- Originality: resolving Srebro’s conjecture and exponential failure probability are original, though techniques are conceptually standard.
- Significance: sharpens understanding of GMM likelihood landscape and limitations of naive random initialization; impact is primarily conceptual and cautionary.
- Suggestions: add dedicated comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s in large-separation regime and clarify when EM reduces to k-means.
- Suggestions: discuss relevance of positive guarantees (e.g., Kumar–Kannan) and contrast separation assumptions with negative EM results.
- Suggestions: comment on practical initialization schemes (k-means++, spectral/moment pilots) and required restarts versus the eΩ(M) lower bound.
- Suggestion: fix bibliography formatting and improve consistency.
