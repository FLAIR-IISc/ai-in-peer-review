- Paper shows spurious local modes can exist in the population log-likelihood of isotropic GMMs and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Core results use true mixture centers that form well-separated clusters to construct poor local modes.
- Authors prove EM with centers initialized by sampling from the mixture will almost surely fail to find the global optimum as the number of components grows.
- Paper is well-written with insightful theoretical contributions (minor typos noted).
- Concern 1: Unclear how practically likely the “bad” center configurations are; request analysis of their prevalence under reasonable distributions over true centers (dependence on k, d).
- Concern 2: The negative EM result relies on random-sample initialization; need discussion of robustness under practical initialization schemes (e.g., k-means++, density-aware methods).
- Clarification Q1 (L74-81): Purpose of the example is unclear—does not appear to resolve the Srebro conjecture for k=2; clarify intended contribution.
- Clarification Q2 (L233-235): Why is condition (1) in Lemma 3 necessary if condition (2) already ensures centers are near correct clusters?
- Clarification Q3 (L267-273): Practical significance of the result that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point—how does this compare to converging to poor local optima?
- Minor edits: numerous suggested wording/typo fixes (L18, L38, L60, L138 clarification on O(d^{1/2}) separation, L172 redundancy, L177, L218, L220, L225 define Ω(k), L245, L249, L308).
