- Studies local maxima of the log-likelihood for Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariances.  
- Shows a counterexample (k=3, d=1) where local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Proves that for certain mean configurations, the probability of escaping a bad local maximum via random initialization can be exponentially small in k.  
- Establishes that gradient EM almost surely avoids convergence to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Theorem 1’s proof appears sound; Theorem 2’s proof strategy is plausible though appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Each theorem is clearly stated and accurately interpreted in the paper.  
- Novelty: links the configuration of Gaussian means to the structure of log-likelihood local maxima; the hierarchical-means idea for k>3 is particularly original.  
- Potential impact: motivates further theoretical work on local optima (e.g., k-means) and has practical implications for initialization/seeding strategies.  
- Suggested extension: investigate whether Lemma 7 generalizes to higher dimensions (d > 1).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and conveys intuition effectively.
