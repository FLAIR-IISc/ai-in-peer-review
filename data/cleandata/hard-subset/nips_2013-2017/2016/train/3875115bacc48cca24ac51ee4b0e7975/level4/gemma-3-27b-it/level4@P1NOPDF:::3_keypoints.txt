- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood (isotropic, known scale, well-specified) and shows local modes persist with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Focuses on well-spaced center configurations and shows random initialization often causes EM to miss the global optimum as number of components increases.  
- Overall writing is good with only minor typos.  
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 proves existence only for a particular center configuration—need results quantifying how frequently such “bad” true-center configurations occur under reasonable distributions as k or d grow.  
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2 studies random initialization from the mixture—question practical relevance since clear, imbalanced clusters make such random initializations seemingly unrealistic.  
- Lines 74–81: The provided example is unpersuasive and does not convincingly support Sresbro’s conjecture for k=2.  
- Lines 233–235: Why is requirement (1) needed; does initializing a center between two clusters fail to guarantee convergence to the correct cluster?  
- Lines 267–273: Unclear why showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point is practically important—how problematic is saddle-point convergence versus local-optimum convergence?  
- Minor wording/typo fixes: line 18 (“We further show that the gradient EM algorithm…”), line 38 (“estimates”), line 60 (“under which conditions efficient algorithms…”), line 177 (“equivalent to sampling”), line 220 (“the random initialization”), etc.  
- Clarification requests: line 138 clarify O(d^{1/2})-separated meaning; line 172 redundant sentence; line 218 explain “well-separated constant”; line 225 clarify meaning of Ω(k); line 245 “coincidence”; line 249 “Recall that for uniformly weighted…”; line 308 “And claim that…”.
