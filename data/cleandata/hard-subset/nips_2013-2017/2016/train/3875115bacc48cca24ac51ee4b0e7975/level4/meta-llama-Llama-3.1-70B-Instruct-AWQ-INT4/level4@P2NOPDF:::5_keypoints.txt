- Paper answers Srebro (2007) open question about local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation, claiming the answer is negative and providing a general class of counterexamples.  
- Authors review likelihood-maximization methods for GMMs, including EM and gradient EM, and discuss common initialization choices.  
- Main theorems construct counterexamples and show EM/gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.  
- A simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1) is presented to build intuition for the proofs.  
- The paper is well-written and clear, balancing technical detail with intuitive explanation.  
- The question addressed is important for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.  
- Reviewer recommends adding estimates of the constants in the probability bounds (or, if infeasible, supporting simulations) to clarify when the phase transition occurs in practice.  
- The suggested omission (constants/simulations) does not diminish the paper’s novelty and impact.
