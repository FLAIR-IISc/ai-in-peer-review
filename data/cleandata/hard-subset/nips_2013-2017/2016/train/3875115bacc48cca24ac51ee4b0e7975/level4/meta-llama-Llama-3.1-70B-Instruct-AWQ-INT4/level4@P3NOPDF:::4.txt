This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. The authors present three related results in this vein: (1) a counter-example demonstrating the existence of local maxima (when k = 3 and d = 1) with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum; (2) a proof that, with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k when the means of Gaussians are configured in a special way; and (3) a demonstration that gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Summary:
The paper explores the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of GMMs, presenting three key results: (1) a counter-example showing the existence of local maxima with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum; (2) a proof that the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k under certain conditions; and (3) a demonstration that gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Technical Quality:
I have reviewed the proofs in the main paper, specifically Theorem 1, and believe they are sound. Although I did not thoroughly check the details in the Appendix, the proof idea for Theorem 2 appears reasonable based on my analysis of Theorem 1. The authors provide clear interpretations of all three theorems after their statements.

Novelty and Originality:
The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of GMMs and the local maxima of their log-likelihood. This observation, while intuitive, is highly original. I also find the key idea of Theorem 2, which leverages Theorem 1 to show that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true mean determines the local maxima for k > 3, to be particularly clever.

Potential Impact and Usefulness:
I believe the results of this paper could have a significant impact on both theory and practice. Theoretically, they could inspire the study of local optima structure in related clustering tasks, such as k-means problems. It would also be interesting to examine whether the result in Lemma 7 holds for d > 1. Practically, the paper highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may provide insights into designing new algorithms.

Clarity and Presentation:
The paper excels in presenting its results and explaining the intuition behind the analysis. The authors clearly introduce the problem setup and provide intuition and interpretation for all their analyses, making the paper easy to follow and understand.
