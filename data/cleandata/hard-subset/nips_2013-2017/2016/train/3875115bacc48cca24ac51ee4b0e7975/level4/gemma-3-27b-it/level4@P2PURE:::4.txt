This paper investigates the structure of local maxima in the log-likelihood function for Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance matrices. It presents three interconnected results: First, the authors provide a counterexample demonstrating the existence of local maxima (for k=3 and d=1) with arbitrarily poor likelihoods compared to the global maximum. Second, they show that, with random initialization, the probability of escaping a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in *k* when the Gaussian means are arranged in a specific configuration. Third, they prove that gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood. 

Summary: This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (with equal mixing weights and identity as covariance). It presents three related results regarding this vein: 1. It provides a counter-example showing that there exist local maxima (when k = 3 and d=1), whose likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global maximum. 2. They showed with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum could be exponentially small in k on when the means of Gaussians are configured in a special way. 3. They also showed gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Technical quality: I verified the proof of Theorem 1 and found it to be sound. Based on my understanding of the Theorem 1 analysis, the proof strategy for Theorem 2 also appears reasonable, though I did not examine the details in the Appendix. The theorems are all clearly interpreted following their statements.

Novelty & Originality: The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of a GMM and the local maxima of its log-likelihood. This observation, while intuitive, is highly original. Furthermore, the key idea underpinning Theorem 2 – that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means determines the landscape of local maxima for *k* > 3, building upon Theorem 1 – is particularly clever.

Potential impact & Usefulness: The results have the potential to significantly impact both theoretical and practical research. Theoretically, they could stimulate further investigation into the local optima structure of related clustering problems, such as k-means. It would also be interesting to explore whether the result in Lemma 7 extends to dimensions *d* > 1. Practically, the work highlights the importance of initializing with sufficient points within each true cluster and may provide valuable insights for the design of new algorithms. 

Clarity and presentation: The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly presenting the results and effectively explaining the intuition behind the analysis. The problem setup is introduced clearly, and all analyses are accompanied by helpful intuition and interpretation.
