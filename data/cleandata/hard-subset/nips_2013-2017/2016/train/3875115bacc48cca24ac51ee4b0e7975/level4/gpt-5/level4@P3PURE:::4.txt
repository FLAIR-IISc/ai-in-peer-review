This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood for Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It presents three related results:
- It provides a counterexample (for k = 3 and d = 1) showing that there exist local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than that of the global maximum.
- It shows that, under a particular configuration of the Gaussian means, random initialization can lead to bad local maxima with high probability; specifically, the probability of avoiding a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k.
- It proves that gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.

Technical quality
I checked the proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) and found them sound. Having read the analysis of Theorem 1, the proof idea for Theorem 2 also appears reasonable, although I did not verify the details in the appendix. All three theorems are well interpreted and contextualized after their statements.

Novelty and originality
The paper relates the configuration of the true means in a GMM to the local maxima of its log-likelihood. Although intuitive, this connection is presented in a way that feels genuinely original. The key idea underlying Theorem 2, building on Theorem 1—that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means determines the local maxima for k > 3—is particularly clever.

Potential impact and usefulness
These results could influence both theory and practice. On the theoretical side, they may inspire the study of local optimality structures in related clustering formulations, such as k-means. It is also interesting to examine whether the result in Lemma 7 extends to dimensions d > 1. On the practical side, the findings highlight the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may offer insights for designing new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation
The paper is clearly written, with a well-introduced problem setup and lucid explanations of the intuition behind the analysis. The statements of the results are followed by helpful interpretations that aid understanding.
