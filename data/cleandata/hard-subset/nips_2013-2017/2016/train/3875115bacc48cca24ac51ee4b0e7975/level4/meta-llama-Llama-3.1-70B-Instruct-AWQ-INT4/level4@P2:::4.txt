This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity as covariance. The authors present three related results regarding this topic.

Firstly, they provide a counter-example showing that there exist local maxima (when the number of mixture components, M, is 3 and the dimension, d, is 1), whose likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global maximum. This result resolves an open question of Srebro [2007] and highlights the challenges of maximizing the likelihood function in GMMs.

Secondly, they show that with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum could be exponentially small in M when the means of Gaussians are configured in a special way. This result demonstrates the importance of careful initialization in GMMs and provides insight into the behavior of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.

Lastly, they also show that the first-order EM algorithm does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely. This result is interesting as it suggests that the sub-optimal points to which the first-order EM algorithm frequently converges are bad local maxima, rather than saddle points.

The technical quality of the paper is high, with sound proofs and well-interpreted results. The authors' observation that the mean configuration of GMMs is tied to the local maxima of its log-likelihood is original and insightful. The key idea of Theorem 2, which is based on Theorem 1, is also clever and demonstrates a deep understanding of the problem.

The results in this paper have the potential to impact both theory and practice. Theoretically, they could inspire the study of the local optima structure of related clustering tasks, such as k-means problems. Practically, they show the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may provide insights into designing new algorithms.

The paper is well-written, with clear explanations and interpretations of the analysis. The authors do an excellent job of presenting their results and providing intuition for their analysis. Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes significant contributions to the field of machine learning.

Weaknesses:
