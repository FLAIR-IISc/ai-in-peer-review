- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixture means (known number of components, weights, covariances) and proves three negative theoretical results.
- Result 1: Even with large samples, local maxima can exist with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.
- Result 2: EM with random initialization converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than optimal.
- Result 3: The same negative behavior holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- The authors simplify the model (known uniform weights, identity covariances); reviewer finds these simplifications acceptable given technical difficulty.
- Paper is written for general dimension d, but proofs are provided only for d = 1; reviewer asks for discussion of extensions or obstacles for d > 1.
- Main concern: Proof of Theorem 1 is too concise (lines 288–306); limits involving suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains need more justification.
- Continuity remarks do not automatically justify the claimed convergences; reviewer requests more detailed arguments and a longer proof even if other discussions are shortened.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; request clearer derivation/explanation of Equation (5).
