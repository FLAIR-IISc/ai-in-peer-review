- Answers Srebro (2007) open question by showing population GMM likelihood can have local maxima; provides a negative answer.  
- Constructs explicit GMMs (M ≥ 3) with arbitrarily bad local maxima; gives a clear 1D M = 3 example.  
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with exponentially high probability in M.  
- Shows first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures stem from bad local maxima, not saddles.  
- Technical results are rigorous with detailed proofs supporting the constructions and convergence claims.  
- The 1D M = 3 construction provides strong intuition for the general-case phenomena.  
- Supplementary probability bounds lack explicit constants, limiting practical assessment for moderate M.  
- No empirical experiments provided to illustrate convergence to bad local maxima (theoretical only).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, balancing intuition and rigor.  
- Original and significant: resolves a long-standing theoretical question and highlights inherent non-convexity in population GMM likelihoods.  
- Practical implication: even for well-separated, spherical Gaussians, careful initialization is necessary when using EM.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical contribution with important implications despite minor gaps.
