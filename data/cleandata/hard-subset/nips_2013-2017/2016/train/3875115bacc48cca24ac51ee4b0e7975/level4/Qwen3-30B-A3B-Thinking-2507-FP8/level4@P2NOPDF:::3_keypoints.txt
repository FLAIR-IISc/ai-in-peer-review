Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood under isotropic known-scale, well-specified models with infinite data.  
Shows local modes can persist asymptotically and be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
Proves that for well-separated cluster center configurations, random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with high probability as k increases.  
Paper is well-written overall but contains minor typos.  
Primary concern: practical relevance — Theorem 1 demonstrates existence for a specific center configuration but does not quantify how likely such configurations are in real data.  
Authors should state assumptions on center distributions (e.g., uniform or Gaussian) and quantify probability of encountering “bad” configurations as k or d grow.  
Theorem 2 analyzes EM behavior conditional on configurations but lacks a probabilistic analysis of true center arrangements.  
Question practicality of random initialization for clearly clustered data; random init placing multiple centers in the dense cluster may be implausible under strong separation.  
The k=2 example (L74–81) does not convincingly support the Srebro conjecture and its purpose is unclear.  
Clarify necessity of requirement (1) (L233–235): a center initialized between clusters may still converge correctly, so avoiding such initialization may be unnecessary.  
Clarify practical significance of saddle-point avoidance (L267–273): is convergence to saddles more or less problematic than to local optima in practice?  
Define ambiguous terms like “well-separated constant” (L218).  
Fix wording/typos: L220 “initialized by the the random initialization” → “by random initialization”; clarify \Omega(k) asymptotic notation (L225).  
Additional typo fixes: L245 “unfortunate coincident” → “unfortunate coincidence”; L249 “Recall for uniform weighted” → “Recall that uniform weights...”.  
Other minor wording corrections: L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L308 as specified by reviewer.  
Overall assessment: theoretical results are sound but practical impact depends on showing problematic configurations are not pathological; recommend acceptance pending these clarifications.
