- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, weights, and covariances (theoretical focus).  
- Presents three negative results: existence of local maxima with arbitrarily low likelihood, random initialization converges w.h.p. to bad critical points, and the same negative result for gradient EM.  
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the results informative for understanding EM in practice.  
- Simplifying assumptions (known k, uniform weights, identity covariances) are acceptable given technical difficulty of proofs.  
- Paper claims results for general dimension d but proofs are only for d=1; authors should discuss extension to higher dimensions or additional difficulties.  
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too concise and needs more detail to be convincing.  
- Specific technical issue: taking limits of suprema over unbounded/domains depending on parameters is not justified by continuity alone and requires more explanation.  
- Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) should be clarified.  
- Overall: solid and interesting work; addressing the above points would strengthen the paper.
