- Counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing existence of local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- With a specific mean configuration, random initialization can have exponentially small (in k) probability of escaping a bad local maximum.  
- Gradient EM is proven to almost surely not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Paper investigates local maxima structure for equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs and presents the three results above.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proof is sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked; theorems are clearly explained.  
- Novelty: Connects mean configuration to local maxima structure; hierarchical grouping idea for k>3 is original and clever.  
- Potential impact: Could spur theoretical work (e.g., on k-means) and practical seeding strategies; suggests examining Lemma 7 for d>1.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear intuition, problem setup, and interpretation of results.
