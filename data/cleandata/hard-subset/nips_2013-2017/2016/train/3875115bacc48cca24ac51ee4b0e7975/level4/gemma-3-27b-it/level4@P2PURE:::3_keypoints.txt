- Paper studies existence of local modes in Gaussian mixture log-likelihoods for isotropic components with known scale in a well-specified model.  
- Authors show local modes persist even with infinite data and can have arbitrarily large log-likelihood gaps from the global optimum.  
- Analysis focuses on specific well-spaced center configurations and shows random-initialized EM will, with high probability, miss the global optimum as component count grows.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains several minor typos.  

Major concern 1:
- Theorem 1 proves existence of bad local modes only for particular center configurations; need assessment of how likely such configurations are in practice.  
- Request explicit assumptions on the distribution of true centers and a quantified probability that “bad” configurations occur as k or dimensionality increases.  
- Clarify that this probability should be over the true centers (not initializations).  
- Theorem 2 is claimed to address this but the reviewer finds it unconvincing without a clear probabilistic statement.

Major concern 2:
- Theorem 2 shows random-data initialization often leads EM to miss the global optimum under certain center configurations, but the realism of random initialization is questioned when data exhibits clear clustering.  
- Example: for three Gaussians with two close and one isolated, random initialization placing two centers where only N points belong seems counterintuitive; question whether random initialization is a realistic baseline in such cases.

Specific technical/questions:
- Lines 74–81: unclear purpose of example; does not convincingly support Sresbro’s conjecture for k=2.  
- Lines 233–235: necessity of requirement (1) is unclear given requirement (2); does initializing a center between two clusters fail to guarantee convergence to the correct cluster?  
- Lines 267–273: proof that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint is presented, but practical importance of avoiding saddlepoints versus local optima is unclear.
