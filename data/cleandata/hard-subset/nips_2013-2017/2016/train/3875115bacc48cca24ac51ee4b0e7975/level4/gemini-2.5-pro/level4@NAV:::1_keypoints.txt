- EM can be trapped in suboptimal local maxima even with infinite samples for a three-component spherical Gaussian mixture with well-separated centers.  
- For k-component mixtures, the success probability of EM with random initialization is exponentially small: exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Poor local maxima, not strict saddles, are the main obstacle to EM convergence; Gradient EM avoids strict saddles under generic conditions.  
- The paper's findings consistently argue EM's non-convergence to global optima.  
- The central negative example (two close components and one distant) yields an intuitive local maximum where centers miscluster.  
- In the high-separation regime (separation >> √d), learning GMMs closely resembles k-means, making the counterexample unsurprising.  
- Analogous counterexamples and exponential failure probabilities are already known for Lloyd’s k-means, motivating smarter initializations (e.g., k-means++).  
- The k-component extension uses an elegant recursive construction but follows predictable patterns from k-means literature.  
- Overall contribution confirms existing intuition rather than delivering fundamentally new insights.  
- Reviewer suggests a stronger, explicit comparison with k-means (e.g., Kumar-Kannan) and discussion of differences when components overlap (citing Balakrishnan, Wainwright, and Yu).
