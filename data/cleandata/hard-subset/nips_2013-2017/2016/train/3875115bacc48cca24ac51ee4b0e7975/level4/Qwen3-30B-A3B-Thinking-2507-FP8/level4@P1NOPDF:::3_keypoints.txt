- Paper shows local log-likelihood modes can exist for isotropic, well-specified Gaussian mixtures even with infinite data, and these modes can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- For certain configurations (well-separated true centers in groups), random EM initialization will almost surely miss the global optimum as the number of components increases.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains occasional typos that need correction.  
- Primary concern 1: Theorem 1 demonstrates existence under a specific true-center configuration, but the paper does not quantify how likely such configurations are in practice.  
- Recommend specifying a distribution over true centers (e.g., uniform) and proving that “bad” configurations become more probable with larger k or dimension.  
- Theorem 2 claim: analysis of EM failure under random initialization; primary concern 2: random initialization is unrealistic for clearly clustered data and may overstate practical failure risk.  
- Need to distinguish “bad configurations” of true centers from “bad initializations” and quantify probabilities for each.  
- The three-Gaussian example (two close, one separated) suggests practitioners would not initialize poorly, undermining Theorem 2’s practical significance.  
- The example/claim regarding Sresbro conjecture for k=2 (Lines 74–81) is unconvincing and its purpose is unclear.  
- Technical point: L233–235 Requirement (1) is unclear and needs explicit justification if a center between clusters prevents convergence.  
- Technical point: L267–273 states EM rarely converges to saddle points, but the practical implications (worse or better than local optima) are not explained.  
- List of minor wording/typo corrections (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308) provided for fix-up.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound contribution, but practical impact depends on showing the critical configurations are non-pathological and naturally arise under plausible data-generating assumptions.
