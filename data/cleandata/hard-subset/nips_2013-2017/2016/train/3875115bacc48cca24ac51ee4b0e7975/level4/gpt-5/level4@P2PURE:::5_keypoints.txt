- Paper answers Srebro (2007) by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing non-global local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.
- Authors review standard maximization procedures for GMMs (EM and gradient EM) and discuss a common random initialization scheme.
- Main theorems present the counterexamples formally.
- They prove that under the common random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal local maxima with exponentially high probability.
- A simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1) is worked through to build intuition.
- Paper is very clear and well written, balancing space constraints with theoretical intuition.
- Resolving Srebro’s question is important for understanding inherent challenges in non-convex optimization, even asymptotically for simple distributions.
- Reviewer did not check supplements in detail and requests estimates of the constants in the probability bounds.
- Constants matter in practice because k is typically small; they indicate where the phase transition to bad solutions occurs.
- If constants are hard to interpret, supporting simulations should be provided to show the phenomenon is observable; this does not reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
