- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) open question by proving that infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihood can have local maxima.  
- Authors construct a broad class of counterexamples demonstrating such local maxima.  
- Focus on standard maximization methods (EM and gradient EM) and commonly used random initialization.  
- Main theorems show EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal local maxima with exponentially high probability under that random initialization.  
- Paper builds intuition via a simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1) before presenting the general results.  
- Writing is clear, well organized, and balances space with theoretical intuition.  
- The question addressed is important for understanding non-convex optimization pitfalls even in the population limit.  
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material and requests estimates for constants in the probability bounds.  
- If constants are uninformative, reviewer suggests providing simulations to show the phenomenon in realistic regimes.  
- Concern about constants/simulations does not diminish the paper’s novelty or impact.
