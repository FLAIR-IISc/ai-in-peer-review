- Paper studies existence of local modes in Gaussian mixture log-likelihoods for isotropic, known-scale, well-specified GMMs.  
- Main result: local modes can exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily suboptimal versus the global optimum.  
- Key construction: “bad” configurations where true centers split among well-separated clusters cause many local modes.  
- With those configurations, random initialization of EM will almost surely miss the global optimum as component count k increases.  
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.  
- Primary concern 1: Theorem 1 relies on a specific center configuration—how likely are such configurations in practice? request for assumptions on center distribution and quantitative probability of “bad” configurations as k or d grow.  
- Primary concern 2: Theorem 2’s focus on random initialization may be less practically relevant when data shows clear clustering; question whether random init is realistic in such cases and clarification of the example in lines 74–81 (Srebro conjecture for k=2).  
- Question (L233–235): Necessity of requirement (1) is unclear—does an initialization between two clusters prevent guaranteed movement to the correct cluster?  
- Question (L267–273): Practical significance of proving EM unlikely to converge to a saddle point—why is saddle convergence more/less problematic than convergence to a local optimum?
