- Studies EM for mean estimation in Gaussian mixture models with known component count, uniform weights, and identity covariances.
- Proves population likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum, even with infinite data.
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with high probability.
- Shows gradient EM exhibits the same problematic convergence behavior.
- Research question is relevant and the results give important theoretical insights into EM’s limitations.
- Simplifying assumptions (known k, uniform weights, identity covariances) are reasonable given proof complexity.
- Theoretical contributions are significant and useful for practitioners.
- Paper claims results for general dimension d but proofs are only for d=1; authors should clarify extendability or discuss extra challenges.
- Major concern: Theorem 1 proof (lines 288–306) is too brief; justification for taking limits of suprema over unbounded domains is insufficient and needs more rigorous detail.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is unclear; derivation of Equation (5) requires more explanation.
