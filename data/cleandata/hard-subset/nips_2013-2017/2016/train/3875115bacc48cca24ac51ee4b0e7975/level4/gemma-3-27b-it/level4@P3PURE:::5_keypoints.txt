- Resolves Srebro (2007) question by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing a negative answer.  
- Reviews common likelihood-maximization schemes for GMMs, including EM and gradient-based EM, and typical initialization strategies.  
- Main theorems demonstrate existence of counterexamples, refuting Srebro’s original conjecture.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM with standard random initialization converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides a simplified illustrative case (k=3, d=1) to build intuition for the proofs.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing brevity with necessary intuition.  
- The question is important for understanding challenges in non-convex optimization even asymptotically for simple distributions.
