The paper provides a significant contribution to the field by resolving an open question posed by Srebro in 2007, regarding the existence of local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation. The authors present a thorough analysis, introducing a general class of counterexamples that demonstrate the presence of local maxima. They begin by outlining the methods used to maximize the likelihood in Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and discuss the issue of initialization, highlighting a common choice.

The authors then state their primary theorems, which provide a negative answer to Srebro's question. They further show that even with a common choice of random initialization, the EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. The paper is well-written, and the authors strike a good balance between providing intuition for their theoretical results and meeting the space requirements of the paper.

The question addressed by the authors is crucial for understanding the challenges that arise in non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions. The paper's contribution is substantial, and the results have significant implications for the field.

However, to further enhance the paper's impact, it would be beneficial to provide estimates of the constants in the probability term. This information would be valuable in understanding when the convergence to bad solutions can be observed in the asymptotic limit, effectively identifying the phase transition. If obtaining these estimates is too complicated, supporting simulations would be a useful addition, demonstrating the observability of this phenomenon.

Overall, the paper is well-structured, and the authors' writing is clear and concise. The results presented are novel and impactful, making this paper a valuable contribution to the field.
