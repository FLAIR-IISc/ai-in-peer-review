This paper investigates the landscape of local maxima for the log-likelihood of Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariances. It presents three connected contributions: 1) a concrete counterexample (for k = 3, d = 1) demonstrating the existence of local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily inferior to that of the global optimum; 2) an analysis showing that under random initialization, when the Gaussian means have a particular arrangement, the probability of avoiding convergence to a poor critical point can be exponentially small in k; and 3) a result establishing that gradient EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points of the log-likelihood.

Summary: This work examines the structure of local maxima in the log-likelihood of Gaussian Mixture Models with uniform weights and identity covariances. It establishes three related findings: 1) a counterexample (k = 3, d = 1) showing local maxima whose likelihoods can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum; 2) under random initialization and a specific configuration of the component means, the chance of not ending at a bad local solution can be exponentially small in k; and 3) gradient EM almost surely avoids convergence to strict saddle points.

Technical quality
I verified the proofs presented in the main text (notably Theorem 1) and found them sound. Based on the exposition of Theorem 1, the high-level argument for Theorem 2 also appears plausible, though I did not check the appendix in detail. The statements of all three theorems are clearly contextualized and interpreted after their presentation.

Novelty & Originality
The paper explicitly connects the arrangement of GMM means to the existence and quality of local maxima of the population log-likelihood. While intuitive in hindsight, this is a fresh and original perspective. The core idea underlying Theorem 2, building on Theorem 1 via a hierarchical clustering structure of the true means for k > 3, is particularly elegant.

Potential impact & Usefulness
These results are likely to influence both theory and practice. Theoretically, they can spur deeper analysis of local optimality structures in related clustering objectives, such as k-means; it would also be interesting to test whether their Lemma 7 extends to d > 1. Practically, the findings underscore the importance of initializing enough centers within each true cluster and may inform the design of improved initialization schemes or new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation
The paper is very well written, with clear problem setup, intuitive explanations, and thoughtful interpretation of the results. The exposition effectively communicates both the techniques and the implications.
