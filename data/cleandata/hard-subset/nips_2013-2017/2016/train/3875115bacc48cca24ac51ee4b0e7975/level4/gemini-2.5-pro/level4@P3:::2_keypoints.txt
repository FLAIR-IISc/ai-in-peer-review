- Proves that the population log-likelihood for GMMs with ≥3 components can have "bad" local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Construction shows bad local maxima exist even for well-separated Gaussians.  
- Shows standard EM with common random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with overwhelmingly high probability (≥1 - e^{-cM}), implying exponentially many restarts needed.  
- Extends negative results to a first-order (gradient-ascent equivalent) EM variant.  
- Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are due to poor local maxima, not saddle points.  
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental open question (Srebro 2007) and provides a definitive, surprising theoretical refutation.  
- Strength: Novel, technically sound results with an insightful constructive proof and a clever recursive analysis for failure probability.  
- Strength: Well-written, well-motivated paper with precise theorems and thoughtful discussion.  
- Weakness: Proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too condensed; needs rigorous justification for taking supremum limits over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether constructions and proofs generalize beyond the d=1 case; authors should clarify or state dimension limitation.  
- Weakness: Background on first-order EM (Eq. 6) is terse; include a brief intuitive derivation showing its relation to gradient steps on the Q-function.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution but needs expanded proof details and clarification on dimensional generality before acceptance.
