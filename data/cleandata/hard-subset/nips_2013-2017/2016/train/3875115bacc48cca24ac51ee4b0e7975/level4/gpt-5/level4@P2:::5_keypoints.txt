- Resolves Srebro’s 2007 open question by showing the population log-likelihood of GMMs can have spurious local maxima for equally weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians with M ≥ 3.
- Constructs counterexamples where local maxima are arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Analyzes EM and first-order (gradient) EM under a standard random initialization and proves they converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{-Ω(M)}.
- Shows random restarts require exponentially many trials to find a good solution under the studied initialization.
- Provides a one-dimensional k = 3, d = 1 example with geometric intuition to build understanding.
- Clearly outlines the algorithms and the initialization strategy under study.
- Strength: settles a notable open problem with elegant constructions and clear implications.
- Strength: gives clean algorithmic consequences demonstrating the necessity of careful initialization.
- Strength: presentation is clear, well organized, and balances rigorous results with intuition.
- Strength: results are relevant and significant for the NIPS community interested in non-convex optimization and EM behavior.
- Weakness: results are asymptotic and population-level; limited direct guidance on finite-sample behavior.
- Weakness: probability bounds are asymptotic (1 − e^{-Ω(M)}); hidden constants are not provided, limiting practical assessment.
- Suggestion: include small-scale simulations to illustrate the finite-sample “phase transition” under random initialization.
- Suggestion: provide brief summaries of key lemmas/assumptions that yield the exponential-failure bound for readability.
