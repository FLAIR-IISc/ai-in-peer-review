- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question: existence of local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.  
- Constructs a general class of counterexamples showing such local maxima do exist.  
- Provides clear overview of standard GMM optimization methods (EM and gradient EM) and common initialization.  
- Main theorems formally establish the existence of undesirable local maxima, refuting Srebro’s conjecture.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM converge to these non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under standard random initialization.  
- Includes a simplified, intuitive proof for the special case k=3, d=1 to build intuition for the general result.  
- Paper is well-written, balancing rigorous mathematics with intuitive explanations and good space management.  
- Work is of fundamental importance for understanding non-convex optimization in GMMs and advances theoretical understanding.  
- Primary suggestion: provide estimates of constants in the exponential probability bounds or empirical simulations to identify regime/phase-transition effects of k and d.  
- The lack of constant estimates is a minor issue and does not diminish the novelty or significance of the contribution.
