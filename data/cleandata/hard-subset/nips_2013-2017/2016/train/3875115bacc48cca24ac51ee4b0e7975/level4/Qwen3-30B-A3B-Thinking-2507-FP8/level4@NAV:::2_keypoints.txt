- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian mixture means with known components, weights, and covariances.
- Proves three negative results: (1) likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum; (2) randomly initialized EM likely converges to such bad critical points; (3) gradient EM shares the same negative convergence behavior.
- Topic is interesting and worthy of attention.
- Restricting to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) is justified given proof complexity and informative results.
- Results offer valuable insights into EM’s practical behavior.
- Although stated for general dimension d, all proofs are confined to d=1; authors should clarify extendability to d>1 or discuss additional challenges.
- Major concern: Theorem 1’s proof (lines 288–306) is too concise and asserts limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains without sufficient justification—pointwise limits do not imply suprema limits.
- Recommendation to expand and rigorously justify Theorem 1’s argument, even if other sections must be condensed.
- Gradient EM analysis (lines 162–172) is less accessible; derivation of Equation (5) should be clarified to improve readability.
