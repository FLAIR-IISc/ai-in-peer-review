- Resolves Srebro’s 2007 open question by proving the existence of local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation via a class of counterexamples.
- Provides main theorems showing these counterexamples and their implications for likelihood landscapes.
- Demonstrates that EM and gradient EM algorithms can converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.
- Discusses standard maximization schemes (EM, gradient EM) and typical initialization choices used in practice.
- Includes a detailed, intuitive walk-through of a simple example (k=3, d=1) to illustrate the proof ideas.
- Balances theoretical rigor with clarity; the paper is well-written and accessible.
- Highlights the broader significance for understanding non-convex optimization behavior even in asymptotic/simple distribution settings.
- Notes a weakness: lack of explicit estimates for the constants in the probability bounds, limiting practical guidance on when the phenomenon occurs.
- Suggests supporting simulations or constant estimates would clarify when increasing k makes bad convergence likely.
- Concludes that despite the above, the contribution is novel, impactful, and likely to have lasting influence.
