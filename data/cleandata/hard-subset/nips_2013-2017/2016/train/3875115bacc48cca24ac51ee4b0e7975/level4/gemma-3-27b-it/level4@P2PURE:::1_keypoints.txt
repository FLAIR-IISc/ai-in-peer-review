- Paper shows EM and other iterative MLE methods can fail to converge to global optima for mixtures of spherical Gaussians.
- Even with well-separated clusters (k=3) and infinite samples, EM can become trapped in local optima.
- For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization of EM finds a global optimum with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Gradient-based EM methods generally avoid strict saddle points, so poor local maxima—not saddles—are the main convergence obstacle.
- The results establish that EM does not guarantee global convergence even in the infinite-data limit.
- Reviewer finds the results unsurprising and the techniques fairly standard.
- Main illustrative example: two nearby components and one distant component produce a local maximum where one center sits near the first two and two centers near the distant cluster.
- This failure is predictable when centers are separated by much more than sqrt(d), making the problem qualitatively similar to k-means.
- Analogous failure examples exist for Lloyd’s algorithm for k-means; the paper’s recursive construction to generalize to k is expected.
- The observed random-initialization failure mirrors known k-means issues and motivates strategies like k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.
- A direct comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic would be valuable, especially showing similar behavior under large separations.
- Citing Kumar–Kannan’s convergence guarantees for Lloyd under separation and contrasting them with EM would strengthen the paper.
- Note that EM and Lloyd differ when separations are smaller, as discussed in Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu, and this distinction should be emphasized.
