- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing no local maxima in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.  
- Reviews optimization schemes for GMMs (EM and gradient EM) and discusses common initialization strategies.  
- Main theorems formally establish the counterexample to Srebro’s question.  
- Shows that with standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an intuitive, simplified proof for the special case k=3, d=1 to aid understanding.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear, balancing theoretical depth and accessible intuition within length constraints.  
- The result is important for understanding non-convex optimization in the asymptotic limit, even for simple distributions.  
- Supplementary material not fully verified; providing estimates for the constants in the probability bound (or supporting simulations) would strengthen the asymptotic analysis and clarify the phase-transition point.  
- This minor deficiency does not diminish the paper’s novelty or substantial impact.
