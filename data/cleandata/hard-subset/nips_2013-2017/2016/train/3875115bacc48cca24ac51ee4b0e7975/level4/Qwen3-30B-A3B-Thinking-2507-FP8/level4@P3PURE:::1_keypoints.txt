- Paper analyzes EM convergence for spherical Gaussian mixtures and shows it can fail to reach global maxima.  
- Result 1: Even with three well-separated clusters and infinite samples, EM can converge to a local optimum.  
- Result 2: For k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Result 3: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, so poor local maxima—not saddles—are the main obstacle.  
- Core counterexample: three components with two close clusters and one far cluster yield a local maximum where centers are misallocated.  
- Counterexample relies on large separation (≫√d), making the problem analogous to k-means.  
- These findings are largely unsurprising and align with existing intuition about clustering heuristics.  
- The recursive extension to k components is straightforward in pattern, though technically involved.  
- The random-init failure probability mirrors k-means behavior; approaches like distance-squared sampling or k log k initialization are relevant.  
- The paper would be strengthened by a direct comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic and known negative instances.  
- Related work: Kumar–Kannan give Lloyd’s guarantees under sufficient separation; Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu show EM differs when separation is insufficient.  
- Under the large-separation regime used, EM effectively behaves like Lloyd’s algorithm, so an explicit contrast to k-means guarantees is needed.
