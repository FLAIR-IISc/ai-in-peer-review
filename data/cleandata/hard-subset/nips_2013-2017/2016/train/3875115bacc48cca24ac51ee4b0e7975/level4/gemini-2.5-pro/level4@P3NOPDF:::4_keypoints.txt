- Paper studies the structure of local maxima in GMM log-likelihoods under equal weights and identity covariances, presenting three main theoretical results.
- Result 1: Constructs a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse log-likelihood than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Shows that for certain true-mean configurations, EM with random initialization has exponentially small probability (in k) of reaching a good local maximum.
- Result 3: Proves gradient-based EM almost surely avoids convergence to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Proofs appear sound; Theorem 1 verified by reviewer and Theorem 2’s high-level argument is plausible; strong explanatory text follows each theorem.
- Novelty: Establishes a formal, original link between the geometry of true GMM means and the optimization landscape, with Theorem 2’s hierarchical grouping insight being particularly clever.
- Theoretical impact: Results may spur further work on optimization landscapes for related clustering problems and extensions to higher dimensions (e.g., Lemma 7).
- Practical impact: Provides a theoretical explanation for EM’s sensitivity to initialization and motivates development of better initialization or optimization strategies.
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with precise setup and clear, insightful interpretations of results.
