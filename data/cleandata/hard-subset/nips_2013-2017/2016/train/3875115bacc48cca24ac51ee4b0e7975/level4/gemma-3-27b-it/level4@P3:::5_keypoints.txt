- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing a well-separated 3-Gaussian mixture with suboptimal local maxima in the population likelihood.
- Shows EM and first-order EM converge to these bad critical points with exponentially high probability under random initialization.
- Highlights significant implications for non-convex optimization in GMM estimation, even asymptotically.
- Exceptionally well-written, clearly structured, and accessible with balanced rigor and intuition.
- Provides thorough background and situates work relative to key prior literature.
- Novel and significant contribution: a counterexample to a longstanding conjecture.
- Theoretical analysis and proofs are sound and detailed.
- Results emphasize the importance of careful initialization strategies in practice.
- Strong algorithmic finding: high-probability convergence to bad local optima under random starts.
- Weakness: lacks concrete estimates for constants in probability bounds (e.g., constant c in Theorem 2) and their dependence on d and M.
- Weakness: limited empirical support or simulations to illustrate practical magnitude of the phenomena.
- Weakness: could provide a deeper discussion of practical initialization or mitigation strategies (e.g., method-of-moments approaches).
- Weakness: connection between non-convergence to strict saddles and observed convergence to bad local maxima could be more explicitly and deeply discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves significant open problem, rigorous analysis, clear presentation, and high relevance to NIPS audience.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited immediate practical impact due to missing constant estimates and insufficient mitigation discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant paper worthy of acceptance despite minor practical-exposition weaknesses.
