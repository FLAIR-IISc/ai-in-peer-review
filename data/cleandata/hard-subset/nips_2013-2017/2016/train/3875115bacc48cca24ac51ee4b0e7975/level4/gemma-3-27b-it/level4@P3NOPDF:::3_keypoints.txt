- Paper studies existence of local modes in GMM log-likelihoods and shows arbitrarily bad local optima exist even with infinite data; EM likely fails on well-spaced clusters as k grows.  
- Manuscript is generally well-written but contains several minor typos.  
- Main concern: practical relevance is unclear because the probability of encountering the “bad” center configurations in real data is not quantified.  
- Recommend adding assumptions on the distribution of true mixture centers and showing how probability of bad configurations scales with k and/or dimensionality.  
- Theorem 2 is claimed to address practical relevance but its argument is unpersuasive; a clearer, quantitative statement is needed.  
- Distinguish likelihood of bad center configurations from likelihood of bad initializations—these are different concerns and should be separately quantified.  
- Concern about initialization model: Theorem 2 analyzes EM with random data-sampling initialization, which may be unrealistic for clearly clustered or unbalanced data.  
- Provide discussion or analysis showing whether random initialization is a practically relevant baseline in typical clustered settings.  
- Example (3 Gaussians, two close/one separated) suggests random initialization placing multiple centers in dense cluster is counterintuitive; this scenario needs justification or exclusion.  
- Lines 74–81 (Sresbro’s conjecture for k=2) are unclear in purpose and should be removed or explicitly linked to the paper’s claims.  
- In Theorem 2 proof, necessity of requirement (1) is unclear—clarify why initializing between clusters might fail to move to correct clusters.  
- Statement that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint (lines 267–273) lacks practical interpretation; explain whether saddlepoint convergence is more or less harmful than local-optimum convergence.
