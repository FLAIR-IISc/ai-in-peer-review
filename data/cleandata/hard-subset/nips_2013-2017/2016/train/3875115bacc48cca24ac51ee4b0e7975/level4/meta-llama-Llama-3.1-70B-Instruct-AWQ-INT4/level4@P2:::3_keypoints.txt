- Main result: population likelihood for Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians.  
- Algorithmic consequence: EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability at least 1 − e−Ω(M).  
- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture negatively: local maxima need not be global in the infinite-sample limit.  
- First-order EM property: cannot converge to strict saddle points with probability one.  
- Implication: careful initialization is necessary in practice when using EM for GMMs.  
- Paper strengths: clear writing, readable proofs, thorough introduction and notation.  
- Technical strength: rigorous, detailed analysis of population likelihood and EM behavior.  
- Originality: builds on prior work but provides a significant negative resolution to an open conjecture.  
- Significance: results have important practical implications for algorithm design and initialization.  
- Minor clarity issues: example on lines 74–81 lacks context and is unclear.  
- Minor clarity issues: requirement (1) in Theorem 2 needs more explanation for necessity.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes a specific configuration of centers that may not represent all settings.  
- Limitation: paper does not quantify how likely “bad” configurations are under natural assumptions.  
- Limitation: no empirical or theoretical comparison with algorithms other than EM and its variants.
