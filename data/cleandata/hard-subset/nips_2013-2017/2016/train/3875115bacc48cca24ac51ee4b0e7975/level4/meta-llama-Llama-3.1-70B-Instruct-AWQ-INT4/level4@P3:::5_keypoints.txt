- Provides a negative answer to Srebro (2007) by constructing a general class of counterexamples.  
- Proves EM and gradient EM can converge to non-optimal local maxima with exponentially high probability, even with random initialization.  
- Reviews likelihood-maximization schemes for GMMs, including EM, gradient EM, and common initialization strategies.  
- Main theorems formalize the counterexample and the high-probability convergence-to-bad-maxima results.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and balances theoretical rigor with intuitive explanations.  
- Results have significant implications for non-convex optimization and practical use of EM/gradient EM, emphasizing careful initialization.  
- Improvement suggested: provide explicit estimates for the constants in the probability bounds to clarify practical impact and phase transitions.  
- Improvement suggested: include supporting simulations or numerical experiments to illustrate empirical behavior.  
- Strengths: clear resolution of an important open question, accessible exposition, and meaningful practical consequences.  
- Weaknesses: missing numerical constant estimates and empirical validation.
