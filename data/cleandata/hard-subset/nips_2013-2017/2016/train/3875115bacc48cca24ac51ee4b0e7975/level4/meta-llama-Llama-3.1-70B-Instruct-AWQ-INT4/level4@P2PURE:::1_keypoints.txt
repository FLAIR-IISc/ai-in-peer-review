- Paper shows EM and related heuristics for spherical Gaussian mixtures can fail to reach global maxima even with infinite samples and well-separated clusters (three components).  
- For k-component mixtures, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Gradient EM generally does not converge to strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima are the principal failure mode.  
- Main counterexample: three-component mixture with two close components and one far-away, producing a local maximum with one center near the pair and two centers near the distant component.  
- This failure is predictable when center separations are ≫ √d, where the problem becomes similar to k-means and Lloyd’s algorithm failures.  
- The three-component example is extended to k components via a recursive construction, which the reviewer finds expected.  
- The random-initialization failure mirrors k-means behavior, suggesting similar remedies (e.g., selecting ~k log k centers or distance-squared sampling).  
- Overall story is coherent but largely anticipated and follows expected proof techniques.  
- Comment: A direct comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic (and results like Kumar–Kannan under sufficient separation) would strengthen the paper by clarifying when EM and Lloyd are equivalent versus different.
