This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. The authors present three key results in this regard: (1) a counter-example demonstrating the existence of local maxima (for k=3 and d=1) with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum; (2) a proof that with random initialization, the probability of converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k when the means of Gaussians are specially configured; and (3) a demonstration that gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

The paper provides a comprehensive study of the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of GMMs, presenting three related results. Firstly, it provides a counter-example showing that local maxima (when k=3 and d=1) can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum. Secondly, it shows that with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k when the means of Gaussians are specially configured. Thirdly, it demonstrates that gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Technical quality
I have verified the proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) and found them to be sound. Although I did not thoroughly check the details in the Appendix, the proof idea for Theorem 2 appears reasonable after reading the analysis of Theorem 1. All three theorems are well-interpreted following their statements.

Novelty & Originality
The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of GMMs and the local maxima of their log-likelihood, an observation that, although intuitive, is highly original. The key idea of Theorem 2, based on Theorem 1, which links the hierarchical grouping structure of the true mean to the local maxima for k > 3, is also particularly clever.

Potential impact & Usefulness
I believe the results have the potential to significantly impact both theory and practice. Theoretically, they could inspire the study of local optima structure in related clustering tasks, such as k-means problems. It would also be interesting to examine whether the result in Lemma 7 holds for d > 1. Practically, the paper highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may provide insights into designing new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation
The paper excels in presenting its results and explaining the intuition behind its analysis. It clearly introduces the problem setup and provides intuition and interpretation for all its analyses, making the content easily accessible to readers.
