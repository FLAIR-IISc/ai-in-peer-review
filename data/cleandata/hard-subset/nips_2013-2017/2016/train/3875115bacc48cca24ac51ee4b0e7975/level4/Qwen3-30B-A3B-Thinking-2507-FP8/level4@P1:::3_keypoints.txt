1. Resolves Srebro (2007) by showing population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians with known scales.

2. Contribution 1: Constructs a specific GMM configuration where a local maximum’s log-likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.

3. Contribution 2: Shows EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to such suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.

4. Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with logically developed technical arguments and thorough background on GMMs and EM.

5. Builds naturally on prior work (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2015) and provides a well-motivated connection to the Srebro conjecture.

6. Practical concern 1: The bad-local-maxima construction is for a specific clustered configuration; the paper does not analyze how likely such configurations are in practice or under reasonable priors as M or d grow.

7. Practical concern 2: Theorem 2’s random-initialization failure may be irrelevant for clearly clustered real data where practitioners would not initialize poorly; relevance of random initialization in such settings should be addressed.

8. The example intended to support Srebro’s conjecture for M=2 (lines 74–81) is unclear and does not convincingly demonstrate the two-component case.

9. Technical question: It’s unclear why “all initialized centers falling within a single cluster” is necessary—centers initialized between clusters might move to correct clusters.

10. Technical question: Theorem 4 showing first-order EM avoids strict saddles is interesting, but the paper does not explain the practical significance of avoiding saddles versus avoiding local maxima.

11. Minor corrections and clarity edits recommended (specific line edits and wording/typo fixes listed: L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308).

12. Overall judgment: Technically strong, resolves a long-standing open question, has important theoretical implications for EM on GMMs, and merits acceptance though practical relevance could be strengthened.
