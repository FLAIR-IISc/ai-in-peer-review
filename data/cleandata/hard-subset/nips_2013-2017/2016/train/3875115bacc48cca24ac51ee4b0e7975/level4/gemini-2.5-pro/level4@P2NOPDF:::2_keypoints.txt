- Paper analyzes EM for estimating means of a GMM with known number of components, weights, and covariances, proving three negative results about likelihood and convergence.
- Main negative results: likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily lower than the global maximum even with large samples.
- Main negative results: EM with random initialization converges with high probability to critical points with arbitrarily low likelihood.
- Main negative results: gradient EM exhibits the same bad behavior as standard EM.
- Significance: addresses a fundamental question about EM’s practical performance and provides formal explanations for empirically observed failure modes.
- Strength: restricting to known component count, uniform weights, and identity covariances is justified given technical proof complexity, yet results remain informative.
- Major weakness: proofs are presented only for d=1 despite framing for general dimension d; authors should explicitly address extendability to higher dimensions or discuss technical barriers.
- Major weakness: proof of Theorem 1 (especially lines 288–306) is too condensed; the limit-of-suprema claims over changing/unbounded domains need much more rigorous, step-by-step justification.
- Minor issue: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and Equation (5) needs clearer derivation and context.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but would be significantly improved by clarifying the dimensionality scope and strengthening the rigor and exposition of the key proofs.
