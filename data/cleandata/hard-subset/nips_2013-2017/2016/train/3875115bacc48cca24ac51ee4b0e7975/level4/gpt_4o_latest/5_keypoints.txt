- Paper answers Srebro (2007) by constructing a broad class of counterexamples showing local maxima exist in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihoods.
- Explains mechanisms of likelihood maximization in GMMs, focusing on EM and gradient EM.
- Discusses the role of initialization and presents a commonly used initialization choice.
- States main theorems that establish the counterexamples to Srebro’s question.
- Proves that with standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Provides intuition via a simpler worked example (k=3, d=1).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing space constraints and theoretical intuition.
- The question addressed is significant for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic/simple settings.
- Reviewer requests estimates for the constants in the probability bound (or supporting simulations) to clarify the phase-transition point in practice.
- Limitation about constants/simulations does not reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
