- Negative answer to Srebro (2007): for M ≥ 3 there exist population GMMs with arbitrarily bad local maxima in the likelihood.  
- Theorem 1: explicit 1D counterexample constructing arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3.  
- Theorem 2: random initialization of EM (and a first-order variant) converges to suboptimal points with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.  
- Theorem 4: first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the failure mode.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous proofs for Theorems 1 and 4 and well-motivated arguments for Theorem 2.  
- Construction method: clever hierarchical grouping argument underpinning the counterexample and EM behavior analysis.  
- Population vs sample: authors carefully distinguish population results and note extensions to finite samples via standard arguments.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear setup, intuitive explanations, and helpful figures making results accessible.  
- Originality: first rigorous demonstration that arbitrarily bad local maxima exist for M ≥ 3 in the population setting.  
- Significance: important for theory of non-convex GMM likelihoods and practical EM algorithm behavior.  
- Practical implication: underscores the critical need for careful initialization strategies (random initialization often fails).  
- Strengths summarized: resolves open question, strong theory, practical insights, well-structured presentation.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited to equally-weighted, spherical unit-variance GMMs and limited discussion on required finite-sample sizes.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
