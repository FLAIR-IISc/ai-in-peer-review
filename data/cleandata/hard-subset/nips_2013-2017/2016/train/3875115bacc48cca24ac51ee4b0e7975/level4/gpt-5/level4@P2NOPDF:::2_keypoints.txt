- Paper studies EM for estimating component means in Gaussian mixtures with known components, weights, and covariances, giving three negative theoretical results.  
- Result 1: Even with large samples, the likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: With random initialization, EM likely converges to critical points with likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Result 3: Gradient EM exhibits the same problematic behavior.  
- Focusing on a simplified setting (known component count, uniform weights, identity covariances) is reasonable given technical difficulty and yields informative results.  
- Dimensionality is unclear: paper reads as general d but proofs address d=1; authors should state extensions or obstacles for higher dimensions.  
- Main concern: Theorem 1’s proof is too terse, especially steps around lines 288–306 involving limits of suprema over expanding/unbounded domains.  
- Continuity alone doesn’t justify passing limits through suprema; the argument needs more rigorous justification.  
- Suggested fixes for Theorem 1: precisely specify domains and compactness, justify interchanging limits and suprema (e.g., uniform convergence or ε–δ arguments), and add bounding lemmas controlling contributions outside large compacts.  
- Secondary concern: the gradient EM section (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are hard to follow and need expansion.  
- Recommendation: With a clearer, detailed proof of Theorem 1 and a more transparent derivation for gradient EM/Equation (5), the paper would make a strong theoretical contribution.
