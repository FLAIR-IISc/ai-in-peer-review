This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. The authors present three related results in this vein: 

1. They provide a counter-example showing that, for k=3 and d=1, there exist local maxima whose likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
2. They demonstrate that, with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k when the means of Gaussians are configured in a special way.
3. They also show that gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Summary:
This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance, presenting three related results. 

Technical quality:
I have checked the proofs in the main paper, specifically Theorem 1, and believe the proofs to be sound. Although I did not verify the details in the Appendix, the proof idea for Theorem 2 appears reasonable after reading the analysis of Theorem 1. All three theorems are well-interpreted following their statements.

Novelty & Originality:
The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of GMMs and the local maxima of their log-likelihood, an observation that, although intuitive, is very original. I also think the key idea of Theorem 2, based on Theorem 1, that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true mean determines the local maxima for k > 3, is very clever.

Potential impact & Usefulness:
I believe the results could have a significant impact on both theory and practice. Theoretically, they could inspire the study of the local optima structure of related clustering tasks, such as k-means problems. It would also be interesting to examine whether their result in Lemma 7 holds for d > 1. Practically, the paper highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may provide insights into designing new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation:
The paper does an excellent job of presenting its results and explaining the intuition behind its analysis. It clearly introduces the problem setup and provides intuition and interpretation for all its analyses.
