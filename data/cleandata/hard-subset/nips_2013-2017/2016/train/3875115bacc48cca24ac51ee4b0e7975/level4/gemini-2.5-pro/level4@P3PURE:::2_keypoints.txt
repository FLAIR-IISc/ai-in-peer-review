- Paper analyzes EM for estimating GMM means assuming known/fixed number of components, weights, and covariances.  
- Main contribution: three negative theoretical results showing presence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the likelihood, high-probability convergence of randomly initialized EM to such suboptimal critical points, and an analogous negative result for Gradient EM.  
- Restricting to simplified GMM setting (uniform weights, identity covariances) is well-justified to enable technical proofs and yields significant insights.  
- Primary concern: proof of Theorem 1 in the main paper is overly condensed and needs expansion for transparency and rigor.  
- Specific issue: the argument from lines 288–306 relies on taking limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains; continuity alone does not justify this convergence and requires a more detailed argument.  
- Framing concern: paper claims results for general d dimensions but presents proofs only for d=1; authors should clarify generalizability or outline technical challenges for d>1.  
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and needs a clearer derivation/explanation of Equation (5).
