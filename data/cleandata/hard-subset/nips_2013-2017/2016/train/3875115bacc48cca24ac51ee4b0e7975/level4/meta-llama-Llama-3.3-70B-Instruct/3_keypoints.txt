- Paper studies local modes in Gaussian mixture log-likelihoods for simple isotropic, known-scale, well-specified mixtures.
- Authors prove local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Analysis focuses on configurations where centers split among well-spaced clusters, showing random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with high probability as components grow.
- Paper is generally well-written, with occasional typos and formatting issues.
- Major concern 1: practical relevance — unclear how likely the “bad” center configurations are in practice; request assumptions on center distributions and probability statements showing bad configurations become more likely with more components or higher dimension.
- Major concern 2: practical relevance of random initialization — when data are clearly clustered, random sampling for EM initialization seems unrealistic and unlikely in practice to place centers in low-density regions.
- Unclear whether Theorem 2 adequately addresses the likelihood-of-bad-configurations concern.
- Minor questions: purpose of example on lines 74–81 and its relation to Sresbro’s conjecture is unclear.
- Minor questions: requirements on lines 233–235 are partially unclear, especially why requirement (1) is necessary.
- Clarify significance of the EM non-convergence-to-saddlepoint proof versus convergence to local optima.
- Numerous minor typographical and formatting corrections suggested throughout.
