- Paper studies existence of local modes in the log-likelihood of isotropic Gaussian mixture models with known scale.
- Authors prove local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily poor relative to the global optimum.
- Under specific well-spaced cluster configurations, random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum almost surely as the number of components increases.
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 uses a specific center configuration—unclear how likely this is in practice; authors should assume a distribution on centers and show bad configurations become more probable as k or dimensionality grows; Theorem 2 does not clearly state this likelihood.
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2 analyzes EM with random initialization from data, but placing multiple centers in a denser cluster seems implausible for well-separated clusters, calling into question practical relevance; L74–81 do not convincingly support the Srebro conjecture for k=2.
- L233–235: Requirement (1) is unclear—why is it necessary if a center initialized between clusters might not move toward the correct cluster?
- L267–273: Why is proving EM avoids saddlepoints practically important, and is saddlepoint convergence more or less problematic than convergence to local optima?
