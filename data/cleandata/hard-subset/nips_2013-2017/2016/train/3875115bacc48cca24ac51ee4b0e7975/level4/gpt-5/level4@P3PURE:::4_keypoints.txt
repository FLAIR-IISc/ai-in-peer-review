- Studies local maxima structure of the log-likelihood for GMMs with equal weights and identity covariance.
- Theorem 1: Presents a k=3, d=1 counterexample where a local maximum’s likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Theorem 2: Shows certain mean configurations make random initialization likely to yield bad local maxima; probability of avoiding them can be exponentially small in k.
- Theorem 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Proofs (Theorem 1 checked) are sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is plausible though appendix details not fully verified; results are well interpreted.
- Novelty: Original connection between true means configuration and local maxima, with a clever hierarchical grouping idea for k>3.
- Potential impact: Could spur theoretical work on local optimality in clustering (e.g., k-means) and practical insights on seeding enough points per true cluster; extension to d>1 is an interesting open question.
- Clarity: Clearly written with good setup, lucid intuition, and helpful interpretations after each result.
