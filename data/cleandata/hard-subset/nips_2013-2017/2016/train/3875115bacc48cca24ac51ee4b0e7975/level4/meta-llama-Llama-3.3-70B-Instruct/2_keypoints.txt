- Paper analyzes EM for Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, fixed mixture weights, and covariance matrices, focusing on mean estimation.
- Establishes three negative results: the likelihood can have local maxima with arbitrarily smaller values than the global maximum.
- Shows randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood is significantly lower than the maximum.
- Demonstrates similar negative convergence results for the gradient EM algorithm.
- The question is interesting and the model simplifications (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) are acceptable to enable rigorous proofs.
- Findings are insightful and potentially useful for understanding EM’s practical behavior.
- Although presented for general dimension d, the proofs are effectively for d=1; authors should clarify extendability to higher dimensions and associated challenges.
- Theorem 1’s proof (lines 288–306) is too concise; limits/suprema over unbounded domains and the continuity-based arguments need a more detailed, careful derivation.
- The Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is harder to follow; provide more explanation for the derivation of Equation (5).
