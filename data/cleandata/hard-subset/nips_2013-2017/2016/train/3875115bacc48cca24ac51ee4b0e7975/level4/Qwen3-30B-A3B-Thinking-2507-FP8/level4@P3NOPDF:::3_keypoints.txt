- Shows spurious local modes in isotropic GMM log-likelihood with known scale that can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum, even with infinite data, especially for well-separated center clusters.  
- Proves random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum almost surely as the number of components k grows.  
- Writing is clear and precise but contains multiple minor grammatical and notational errors that need fixing.  
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 lacks a quantitative assessment of how likely the adversarial center configurations are under realistic data-generating processes; recommend modelling center distributions (e.g., i.i.d.) and showing bad configurations become probable with increasing k or d.  
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2’s random-initialization failure may be unrealistic for clearly clustered data; need to justify realism of such initializations and evaluate whether practical initializers (e.g., k-means++) avoid the issue.  
- The example with three components (dense vs sparse cluster) and the Sresbro conjecture for k=2 are unclear/tangential and should be clarified or removed.  
- Clarify and formalize separation/definition language (e.g., what “O(d^{1/2})-separated” and “well-separated constant” precisely mean per Definition 3).  
- The necessity of requirement (1) in Theorem 2 is unclear—explain why both requirements (1) and (2) are needed for the proof.  
- Saddle point result is technically interesting but needs practical motivation (e.g., implications for model quality or extra iterations to escape saddles).  
- Provide either (i) probability bounds for encountering bad configurations under plausible assumptions or (ii) an explicit acknowledgement and discussion of the limited practical regimes where the results apply.  
- Fix listed minor wording corrections (specific line-by-line edits provided by reviewer) and ensure notational consistency (e.g., retain standard Ω(k)).  
- Overall: technically sound contribution, but practical impact depends on grounding the assumed center configurations and the realism of initialization scenarios.
