- Paper studies the population log-likelihood landscape for equally-weighted, spherical GMMs with unit variance.  
- Main result: constructs a 1D M=3 counterexample that disproves Srebro (2007) by exhibiting “bad” local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Shows even well-separated, highly favorable GMM settings can have pathological likelihood surfaces.  
- Algorithmic consequence: for a hierarchical mean configuration, EM and its first-order variant with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^(−cM).  
- Implies random restarts may require exponentially many initializations in M to find a global optimum.  
- Shows first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, indicating failures are due to poor local maxima.  
- Significance/originality: resolves a notable open question and links geometric mean configurations to bad local maxima; recursive construction for exponential failure is innovative.  
- Technical quality: rigorous proofs support claims; Theorem 1’s proof is sound and Theorem 2’s sketch is plausible.  
- Clarity/presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides clear intuition and implications.  
- Practical impact: provides formal justification for careful initialization and supports using advanced initialization methods (e.g., method of moments).  
- Weakness: analysis is in the population setting; lacks discussion of finite-sample sample complexity for when bad maxima appear.  
- Weakness: constructions are specific; unclear how widespread the phenomenon is in generic GMM configurations.  
- Question: does the structural local-maxima result extend to dimensions d > 1, and what challenges arise?  
- Question: can the analysis suggest designs for better initialization schemes beyond avoiding random sampling (e.g., partitioning at multiple scales)?  
- Acceptance pro: original, technically sound, resolves an open question, and shows practical algorithmic implications distinguishing maxima vs. saddle failures.  
- Acceptance con: negative results are demonstrated for particular, specific constructions.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
