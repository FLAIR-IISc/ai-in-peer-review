The paper resolves the open problem posed by Srebro in his 2007 work “Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?” by providing a negative answer through the construction of a broad class of counterexamples. The authors begin by describing the mechanisms by which likelihood is maximized in Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms. They also discuss initialization and present a commonly used choice. The main theorems are then stated, furnishing the counterexample to Srebro [2007]. The authors further demonstrate that, even under the standard random initialization, the EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To build intuition, they analyze a simple case (k=3, d=1). The paper is very clear and well written, striking a good balance between space constraints and providing intuition for the theoretical results. The question they address is of substantial importance for understanding the challenges of non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic setting for simple distributions. I did not examine the supplementary material in detail, but estimates of the constants in their probability bounds would be valuable. This is important because in practice one typically would not take k to be very large; explicit constants would clarify when convergence to poor solutions emerges in the asymptotic limit (i.e., where the phase transition occurs). If the constants are too intricate or not easily interpretable, supporting simulations would be helpful to show that this phenomenon is observable. Nonetheless, this issue does not detract from the novelty or impact of the paper.
