- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood for isotropic, known-variance, well-specified models and shows local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily suboptimal.

- Analysis focuses on configurations where true component means form well-separated clusters; under these, random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with (almost) certainty as k grows.

- Paper is clearly written overall but contains occasional typos.

- Main concern 1: Practical relevance — how likely are the “bad” true-center configurations in practice? The paper should quantify this probability under a distribution over true centers and show dependence on k or d.

- Theorem 2 does not convincingly quantify the likelihood of bad true-center configurations; an explicit probabilistic statement is needed.

- Main concern 2: Initialization model — the authors use pure random initialization (sampling data points) but practitioners often use heuristics (e.g., k-means++, data-dependent seeding); the paper should compare random starts to common practical schemes.

- Practical objection example: with three Gaussians (two close, one far) pure random initialization placing more centers in the sparse region seems unrealistic when clusters are well separated.

- L74–81: Unclear purpose of the example — does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2; clarify intent.

- L233–235: Clarify why requirement (1) is necessary; can a center initialized between two clusters move toward the correct cluster?

- L267–273: Explain practical importance of showing EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points and compare problems caused by convergence to saddles versus local optima.

- Multiple minor typos/wording issues to fix: L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225 (define Ω(k)), L245, L249, L308.

- Recommendation: strengthen practical relevance by (i) quantifying probability of bad true-center configurations under plausible generative assumptions and (ii) discussing failure modes under realistic initialization schemes beyond pure random seeding.
