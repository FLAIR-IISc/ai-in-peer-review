- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples showing spurious local maxima exist in the population log-likelihood for GMMs.  
- For any M ≥ 3, well-separated, spherical, uniformly-weighted GMMs can have local maxima whose likelihoods are arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- EM and a first-order EM variant with standard random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 − e^{-cM}, i.e., exponentially high in M.  
- The first-order EM variant almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima (not saddles) as the main cause of algorithmic failure.  
- Significance/originality: Provides a fundamental, broadly relevant negative result for non-convex optimization in statistical learning.  
- Quality: Strong theoretical analysis that quantifies algorithmic failure probabilities and links landscape structure to EM behavior.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear theorems and intuitive geometric explanations (notably the M=3 construction).  
- Weakness: Main implications are primarily asymptotic in M, limiting direct applicability to moderate M used in practice.  
- Suggestion: Provide an estimate or analysis of the constant c in the failure probability 1 − e^{-cM} to clarify practical scales of M.  
- Suggestion: Add targeted simulations showing randomly initialized EM fails on constructed instances for moderate M to demonstrate practical relevance and phase-transition behavior.
