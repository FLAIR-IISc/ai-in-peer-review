- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known weights/covariances and proves three negative results (bad local maxima, random-init convergence to bad critical points, same for gradient EM).  
- Results assume simplified model (known number of components, uniform weights, identity covariances) which is technically restrictive but informative.  
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the analysis thorough and useful for practice.  
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 lacks sufficient detail, especially lines 288–306 involving limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.  
- Continuity of the likelihood is cited but does not justify the specific convergences claimed in Theorem 1.  
- Unclear whether the proofs generalize to arbitrary dimension d; the paper should discuss extensions and potential difficulties.  
- Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) and link to the population likelihood need clarification.  
- Overall the paper is valuable but would be more convincing with additional technical detail and clearer exposition in the highlighted parts.  
- Strengths: addresses an important question, provides thorough analysis, and yields informative results.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient detail in Theorem 1 proof, no discussion of extension to higher dimensions, and unclear Gradient EM exposition.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
