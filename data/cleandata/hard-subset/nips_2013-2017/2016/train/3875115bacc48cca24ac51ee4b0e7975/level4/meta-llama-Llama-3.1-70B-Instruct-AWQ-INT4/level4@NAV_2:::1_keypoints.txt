- Paper analyzes EM convergence for spherical GMMs and shows EM can fail to find global maxima even with infinite samples.
- Provides a negative answer to Srebro (2007): population log-likelihood for ≥3 components can have arbitrarily bad local maxima.
- Proves this via a careful recursive construction of a specific Gaussian mixture distribution.
- Randomly initialized EM converges to bad critical points with high probability; success probability is exponentially small in the number of components.
- First-order EM (gradient-based) likewise converges to bad critical points with high probability and has exponentially small success probability.
- Shows first-order EM cannot converge to strict saddle points, indicating failures stem from bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Emphasizes the critical need for careful initialization when using EM, especially for large component counts.
- Develops new analytical techniques to study the structure of the population log-likelihood and establish bad local maxima.
- Techniques may be of broader interest for non-convex optimization beyond Gaussian mixtures.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs and supporting simulations.
- Writing and exposition are clear, with good motivation and explanation of concepts.
- Originality: while results may be expected by experts, the analysis is comprehensive and timely.
- Significance: findings have important practical implications for EM usage in high-component regimes.
- Arguments for acceptance: comprehensive convergence analysis, novel techniques, and practical importance.
- Arguments against acceptance: results might be unsurprising to experts, no comparison to other algorithms (e.g., k-means), and possibly limited scope to GMMs.
