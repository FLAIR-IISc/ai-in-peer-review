- Paper analyzes local modes in the log-likelihood of isotropic Gaussian mixture models with known scale, showing local modes exist even with infinite data.  
- Authors show these local modes can be arbitrarily bad in log-likelihood relative to the global optimum.  
- Analysis concentrates on configurations where mixture centers split between well-spaced clusters, and random initialization makes EM likely to miss the global optimum as the number of components grows.  
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.  
- Major concern 1: Theorem 1 depends on a specific center configuration; reviewer questions how likely such "bad" configurations are in practice and requests probabilistic assumptions on center distributions to show their prevalence as k or dimension increase.  
- Reviewer not convinced Theorem 2 adequately addresses the practical likelihood of "bad" center configurations.  
- Major concern 2: Theorem 2’s initialization model (sampling centers from the mixture) may be unrealistic, since practitioners typically avoid placing multiple centers inside a single well-separated cluster.  
- Minor concern: The example on lines 74–81 is unclear and does not clearly support Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2.  
- Conceptual question: Why is requirement (1) needed (lines 233–235), and is proving EM unlikely to converge to a saddle point practically important compared to convergence to a local optimum?  
- Numerous minor editorial and wording corrections suggested (specific line-by-line fixes: lines 18, 38, 60, 138, 172, 177, 218, 220, 225, 245, 249, 308).
