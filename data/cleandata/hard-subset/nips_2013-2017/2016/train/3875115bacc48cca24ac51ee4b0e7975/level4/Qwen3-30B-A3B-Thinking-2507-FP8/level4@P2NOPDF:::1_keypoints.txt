- Paper proves three non-convergence facts: EM can converge to local optima even with three well-separated clusters; random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)); gradient EM generically avoids strict saddles, so failures are due to local maxima.  
- Central counterexample: two nearby components and one distant component produce a local maximum where one center fits the two close clusters and two centers fit the distant cluster.  
- This behavior is unsurprising in the large-separation regime (≫√d), which reduces to a k-means–like, combinatorial clustering problem.  
- The constructed counterexamples closely mirror known k-means/Lloyd failure cases and follow expected patterns when clusters are non-overlapping.  
- The random-init failure rate (exp(-Ω(k))) parallels known k-means results and the need for smarter initializations (e.g., distance-squared/k log k sampling).  
- The paper lacks an explicit, critical comparison to k-means literature (Kumar–Kannan) and prior EM–k-means equivalence results (Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu).  
- Authors do not clarify the precise separation threshold required for their counterexamples (e.g., Ω(√d) vs. larger), weakening connections to existing results.  
- The manuscript does not discuss practical remedies or initialization strategies for EM analogous to k-means fixes, reducing its practical relevance.  
- Technical execution is solid and coherent, but the findings mostly restate expected phenomena rather than providing surprising new insights.  
- Without clearer contrasts to k-means theory or demonstrations of EM-specific challenges, the contribution appears incremental and of limited novelty for a top venue.
