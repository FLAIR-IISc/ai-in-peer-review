- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian-mixture means with known/fixed number of components, weights, and covariances.  
- Main theoretical contributions: three negative results about EM—existence of local likelihood maxima even with large samples, random-init EM likely converges to suboptimal critical points, and gradient EM shows same failures.  
- Research question is interesting and valuable; theoretical understanding of EM’s limitations is important.  
- The authors restrict to a simplified model (known components, uniform weights, identity covariances), which is justified and still informative.  
- Proofs are technical but provide useful insights into EM’s broader behavior.  
- Concern: paper claims results for general dimension d but presents proofs only for d = 1; authors should comment on extending proofs to higher dimensions or discuss difficulties.  
- Primary concern with Theorem 1: the presented proof (lines 288–306) is too concise regarding limits of suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains, and continuity alone may not justify the convergence claims.  
- Suggestion: expand and clarify the proof of Theorem 1 (provide more detailed arguments for the limit of the maximum of likelihoods).  
- Difficulty following the Gradient EM discussion (lines 162–172); request clearer derivation and context for Equation (5).
