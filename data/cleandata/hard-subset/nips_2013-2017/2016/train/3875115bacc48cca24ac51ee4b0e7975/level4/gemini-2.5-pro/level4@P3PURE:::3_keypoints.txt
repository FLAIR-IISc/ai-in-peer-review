- Paper studies spurious local modes in the Gaussian mixture log-likelihood for a simplified well-specified, isotropic-known-scale setting and shows arbitrarily poor local modes can exist even with infinite data; EM with random initialization fails to find the global optimum with high probability as k increases.  
- Paper is generally well-written but has occasional typographical errors.

Main concerns:
- Theorem 1 shows spurious local modes for a specific true-center configuration, but the reviewer questions how likely such configurations are in practice and asks for a probabilistic statement (e.g., assuming a distribution over centers) about their prevalence as k or d grow.  
- Theorem 2’s use of random initialization (sampling centers from the mixture) may be unrealistic: for clearly clustered data, random sampling is unlikely to place centers in the pathological arrangement required for failure, so the practical relevance of the initialization model is doubtful.

Specific questions/comments on text/arguments:
- L74–81: Example’s purpose is unclear and does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k=2.  
- L233–235: Motivation for requirement (1) is unclear— is it needed because a center initialized between two clusters cannot be guaranteed to move toward the correct one?  
- L267–273: The practical importance of proving EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points is unclear—are saddle points more or less problematic than local optima in practice?
