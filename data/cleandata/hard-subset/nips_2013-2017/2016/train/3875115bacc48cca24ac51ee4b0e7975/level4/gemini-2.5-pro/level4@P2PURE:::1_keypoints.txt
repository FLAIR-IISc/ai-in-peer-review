- Paper analyzes EM convergence for spherical Gaussian mixtures and proves failures to reach the global maximum likelihood.

- Main results: EM can get trapped at local optima even for a 3-component mixture with well-separated centers and infinite samples.

- Main results: With k components, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).

- Main results: Convergence failures are due to poor local maxima; Gradient EM will typically avoid strict saddle points.

- Key counterexample: two true components close together and a third far away produce a local maximum where one learned center sits between the close pair and two learned centers sit near the distant component.

- This outcome is expected in the large-separation regime (centers ≫ sqrt(d)), where clusters have minimal overlap and EM behavior approximates k-means.

- The recursive extension to k components is elegant but yields anticipated results analogous to known Lloyd/k-means counterexamples.

- The random-init failure parallels known k-means issues and motivates improved initializations like k-means++ or distance-squared sampling.

- Overall assessment: coherent but unsurprising results; techniques and conclusions follow expected lines.

- Suggestion: the paper should more explicitly compare to k-means literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) and delineate when EM differs from Lloyd's algorithm.
