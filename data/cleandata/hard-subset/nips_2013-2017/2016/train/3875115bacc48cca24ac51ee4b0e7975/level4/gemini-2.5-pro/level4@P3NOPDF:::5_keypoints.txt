- Provides a definitive negative answer to Srebro (2007): local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.  
- Constructs a general class of counterexamples showing spurious, non-optimal local maxima in the asymptotic limit.  
- Proves that EM and gradient EM with a common random initialization converge to these spurious maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Illustrates the mechanism with a clear, intuitive walkthrough for the toy case k=3, d=1.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, balancing rigorous theory and accessible intuition.  
- Contribution is highly significant: it settles an important open question and deepens understanding of non-convex optimization landscapes.  
- Practical relevance could be strengthened by quantifying dependence on model constants (e.g., k) and identifying a phase-transition threshold where spurious maxima dominate.  
- Recommends either analytical estimates for those constants or supporting simulations for moderate k to demonstrate the phenomenon empirically.  
- Suggestion does not detract from the core theoretical novelty; overall the paper is a substantial contribution.
