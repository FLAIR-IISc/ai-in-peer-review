- Thorough theoretical analysis showing existence of local maxima in GMM likelihoods, even with infinite data.  
- Focuses on the simplest setting: isotropic Gaussian components with known scale.  
- Demonstrates local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum in log-likelihood.  
- Clear proofs and well-written exposition for the main results.  
- Constructs particular center configurations (well-spaced clusters) where EM with random initialization likely misses the global optimum as component count increases.  
- Practical relevance unclear: unknown how likely the adversarial configurations are in real data.  
- Suggestion to assume a distribution over centers and show how frequently “bad” configurations occur as k or dimensionality grows.  
- Questionable initialization model: randomly sampling mixture centers may be unrealistic when data are clearly clustered.  
- Minor issues: typos, formatting, awkward sentences, and missing definitions for non-experts.  
- High quality and clarity, with rigorous theoretical support.  
- Limited originality—builds on existing literature.  
- Moderate significance—insightful for EM behavior but may be impactful mainly within a niche.
