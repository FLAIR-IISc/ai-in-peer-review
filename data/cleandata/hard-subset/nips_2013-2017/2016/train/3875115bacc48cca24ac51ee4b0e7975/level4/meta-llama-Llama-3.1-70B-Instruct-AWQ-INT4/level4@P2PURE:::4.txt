This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. The authors present three key results in this vein: (1) a counter-example demonstrating the existence of local maxima (for k = 3 and d = 1) with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum; (2) a proof that, with random initialization, the probability of avoiding convergence to a suboptimal local maximum can be exponentially small in k when the Gaussian means are configured in a specific way; and (3) a demonstration that gradient Expectation-Maximization (EM) does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Summary: This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (with equal mixing weights and identity as covariance). It presented three related results regarding this vein: 1. It provided a counter-example showing that there exist local maxima (when k = 3 and d=1), whose likelihood is arbitrarily worse than the global maximum. 2. They showed with random initialization, the probability of not converging to a bad local maximum could be exponentially small in k on when the means of Gaussians are configured in a special way. 3. They also showed gradient EM does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

Technical quality: I verified the proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) and found them sound. Although I did not thoroughly check the details in the Appendix, the analysis of Theorem 1 suggests that the proof idea for Theorem 2 is reasonable. All three theorems are well-interpreted following their statements.

Novelty & Originality: The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of GMMs and the local maxima of their log-likelihood. This observation, although intuitive, is very original. The key idea of Theorem 2, which builds upon Theorem 1, is also clever, as it shows that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true mean determines the local maxima for k > 3.

Potential impact & Usefulness: I believe the results could have a significant impact on both theory and practice. Theoretically, they could inspire the study of local optima structures in related clustering tasks, such as k-means problems. It would also be interesting to examine whether the result in Lemma 7 holds for d > 1. Practically, the paper highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may provide insights into designing new algorithms.

Clarity and presentation: The paper excels in presenting its results and explaining the intuition behind its analysis. It clearly introduces the problem setup and provides intuition and interpretation for all its analyses.
