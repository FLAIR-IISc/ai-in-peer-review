Paper shows EM and iterative MLE heuristics can get stuck in local optima even with three well-separated clusters and infinite samples.  
For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization of EM finds a global maximum with at most exp(-Ω(k)) probability.  
Gradient EM will not generically converge to strict saddle points, implying poor local maxima—not saddles—are the primary obstacle.  
Main illustrative example: three-component mixture with two close components and one distant component yields a local maximum where centers misallocate to the distant cluster.  
When center separation >> sqrt(d), cluster overlap is minimal and EM’s behavior becomes analogous to k-means/Lloyd’s, making the example predictable.  
The three-component construction is extended to k components via a recursive construction (technical but intuitive).  
Reviewer finds the results coherent but unsurprising and techniques largely follow expected lines.  
Recommendation: compare and contrast with k-means/Lloyd’s theoretical results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) and prior EM analyses (Balakrishnan, Wainwright, Yu).  
Suggest discussing initialization remedies used for k-means (k log k starts, distance-squared sampling) as motivation and mitigation.
