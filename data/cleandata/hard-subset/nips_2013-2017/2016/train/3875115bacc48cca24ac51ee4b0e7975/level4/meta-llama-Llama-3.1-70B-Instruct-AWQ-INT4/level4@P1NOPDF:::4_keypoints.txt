1) Main result: constructs a counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
2) Initialization risk: with random initialization, the chance of avoiding a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k for certain mean configurations.  
3) Gradient EM behavior: gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
4) Proof reliability: reviewer checked Theorem 1 proofs in main paper and found them sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea appears reasonable though appendix details were not checked.  
5) Interpretation: all three theorems are well-interpreted and clearly explained after their statements.  
6) Novelty: tying mean configuration to local maxima structure is original and insightful.  
7) Key idea: Theorem 2’s hierarchical grouping argument (based on Theorem 1) linking true-mean structure to local maxima for k>3 is clever.  
8) Theoretical impact: could inspire analysis of local optima in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means).  
9) Practical impact & suggestions: highlights importance of seeding enough points per true cluster, may guide new algorithm design, and suggests checking Lemma 7 for d>1.  
10) Presentation: paper is well-presented with clear setup, intuition, and explanations.
