- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known weights/covariances and gives three negative theoretical results about EM and gradient EM.
- Result 1: Even with large samples, likelihood can have local maxima with values arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to a critical point whose likelihood can be arbitrarily smaller than the maximum.
- Result 3: The same negative convergence result holds for gradient EM.
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the simplified model (known/ uniform weights, identity covariances) appropriate for technical proofs.
- Results are considered informative and useful for understanding EM in practice.
- Paper claims general dimension d, but proofs are only for d=1; authors should discuss extension difficulties to higher dimensions.
- Major concern: Theorem 1 proof (lines 288–306) is too short—limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains require more detailed justification beyond citing continuity.
- Suggestion to lengthen the Theorem 1 proof (even if other discussions need shortening) to convince readers of validity.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; provide more explanation of how Equation (5) is obtained.
