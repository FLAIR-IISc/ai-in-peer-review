- Paper shows local modes exist in the log-likelihood of isotropic, well-specified Gaussian mixtures even with infinite data, and these local modes can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Focuses on specific center configurations (components split between well-spaced clusters) and proves random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum with high probability as k grows.  
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.  

- Main concern 1: Theorem 1 proves existence of bad local modes for a particular center configuration, but the practical likelihood of such configurations is unclear.  
- Request: assume a distribution over true centers and show that “bad” configurations become more probable as k or dimension increases; Theorem 2 does not sufficiently address this.  
- Clarification: the reviewer means probability of bad true-center configurations, not bad initializations.  

- Main concern 2: Theorem 2’s use of random initialization is questioned as practically irrelevant when data are clearly clustered (e.g., two close Gaussians and one far), since random init placing two centers in a tiny cluster seems unlikely/practically avoidable.  

- Minor concern: Example on lines 74–81 does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2; purpose is unclear.  
- Question about assumptions: requirement (2) is understood but requirement (1) may be unnecessary—initializing a center between clusters should move it to the correct cluster.  
- Practical relevance concern: authors prove EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points, but the reviewer questions whether convergence to saddles is more or less problematic than convergence to suboptimal local minima.  

- Editorial/minor corrections: suggested wording/typo fixes at lines 18, 38, 60, 138 (clarify O(d^{1/2})-separation meaning), 172 (redundant sentence), 177, 218 (clarify “well-separated” constant), 220, 225 (clarify/avoid unfamiliar \Omega(k) notation), 245 (“coincidence”), 249 (“Recall that for uniform weighted”), and 308 (“And claim that”).
