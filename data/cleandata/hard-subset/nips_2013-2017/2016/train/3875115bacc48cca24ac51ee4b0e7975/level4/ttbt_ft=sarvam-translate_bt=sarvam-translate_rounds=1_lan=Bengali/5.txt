This paper presents an answer to a question posed by Srebro in his 2007 paper, "Does the infinite-sample Gaussian mixture model have a local maximum in probability?" The paper answers the question negatively by constructing a class of common counterexamples. The authors begin by outlining how to maximize probability in the context of the Gaussian mixture model, including EM and gradient EM algorithms. The authors also highlight the starting problem and offer a common choice. Then, the authors state their primary theorems, which provide a counterexample to Srebro's [2007] question. The authors further show that even with a common choice of random starting, EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with rapidly increasing probability. Next, the authors prove a common example (k=3, d=1) and provide some ideas for their proof. This paper is very clear and well-written, and it balances the space requirements of the paper well with the need to provide ideas for their theoretical results. The question answered by the authors is extremely important for a better understanding of the problems arising with non-convex optimization, even to the infinite limit for a normal distribution. I have not thoroughly examined the additional elements, but it would be good if they provided some assumptions of constants within the probability term. This is important because I think k will never be considered too large. When we start to see convergence towards a bad solution in the infinite limit (i.e., where is the phase transition?), it would be good to see the assumptions of constants. If the constants are too complex or meaningless, the supporting simulations would be good, at least it is an event that we can observe. However, this problem does not detract from the novelty or impact of this paper.
