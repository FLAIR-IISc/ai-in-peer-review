- Core result: EM and related iterative MLE heuristics can fail to converge to global optima for mixtures of spherical Gaussians.  
- Specific counterexample: Even with infinite data, three-component mixtures with two close components and one distant component admit bad local maxima.  
- Generalization: The three-component counterexample is extended to k components via a recursive construction.  
- Random initialization: EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)) for k-component mixtures under the studied setting.  
- First-order EM: First-order EM methods do not typically converge to strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the main failure mode.  
- Technical soundness: Analysis and proofs are rigorous and correctly presented.  
- Novelty: Results formalize expected behavior but are largely unsurprising and follow established techniques.  
- Relation to k-means: The counterexamples and random-init failure mirror known issues in k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm, especially under large inter-cluster separation.  
- Practical implication: Reinforces the need for careful initialization strategies (e.g., k log k centers or distance-squared sampling) when using EM.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with consistent notation, though more geometric intuition for the bad-optima construction would help.  
- Significance: Important as a formal confirmation of limitations, but impact is moderate since it confirms existing intuition without offering new algorithms.  
- Suggested improvement: Add a direct comparison with k-means and Lloyd’s heuristic to contextualize and contrast the results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Provides rigorous analysis of a fundamental estimation problem, clear presentation, and addresses an open convergence question.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Findings align with prior intuition and related work, limiting perceived novelty and significance.
