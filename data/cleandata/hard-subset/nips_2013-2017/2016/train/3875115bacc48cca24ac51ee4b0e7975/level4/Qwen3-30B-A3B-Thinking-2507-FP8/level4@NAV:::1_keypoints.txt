- Paper proves EM can converge to local (non-global) optima for mixtures of spherical Gaussians, even with three well-separated clusters and infinite samples.
- Shows for k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Establishes gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, so bad local maxima—not saddles—are the main obstacle.
- Results form a coherent narrative demonstrating EM’s asymptotic failure to reach global optima.
- Reviewer: findings largely align with established intuition and follow predictable technical approaches.
- Central counterexample: two closely spaced clusters and one distant cluster create a local maximum with one center near the close pair and two near the isolated cluster.
- This behavior is expected when separation exceeds √d, making the problem analogous to k-means under sufficient separation.
- The three-component example generalizes to k components via a recursive construction, but the mechanism mirrors known k-means/Lloyd failure modes.
- The random initialization failure probability parallels results in k-means literature, motivating strategies like distance-squared sampling.
- Overall assessment: paper consistently illustrates EM’s non-convergence but does not substantially advance beyond conventional expectations.
- Suggested improvement: add a comparative discussion contrasting these results with k-means/Lloyd’s convergence theory (e.g., Kumar–Kannan) to strengthen the contribution.
