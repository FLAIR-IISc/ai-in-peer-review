- Paper studies the optimization landscape of the population log-likelihood for GMMs and algorithmic consequences for EM.
- Result 1: For M ≥ 3, the population log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for uniform, well-separated spherical Gaussians, refuting Srebro (2007).
- Result 2: EM and first-order variants with initialization from the true data distribution converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1−e^{−Ω(M)} (exponentially likely failure).
- Result 3: A first-order EM variant almost surely does not converge to strict saddles, implying failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle proliferation.
- Strength: Resolves a long-standing open question and gives a definitive negative answer to Srebro’s conjecture.
- Strength: Technical contributions are rigorous; construction of arbitrarily bad local maxima and recursive extension to M components are sound.
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly links structural properties of the likelihood to EM performance.
- Strength: Quantitative exponential failure probability motivates the need for better initialization methods (e.g., method-of-moments).
- Weakness: Limited originality—the phenomena and construction strongly resemble known k-means failure modes (two close components, one far).
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of related k-means literature (e.g., k-means++ and known counterexamples), which weakens perceived novelty.
- Suggestion: Explicitly compare to k-means results and convergence/counterexample literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) to clarify what is new about the GMM likelihood analysis.
