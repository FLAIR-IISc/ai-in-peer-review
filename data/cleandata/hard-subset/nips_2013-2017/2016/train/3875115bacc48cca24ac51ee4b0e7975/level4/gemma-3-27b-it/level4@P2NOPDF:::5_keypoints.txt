- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing counterexamples showing infinite-sample likelihood for GMMs can lack local maxima.  
- Provides concise review of standard maximization methods for GMMs, including EM and gradient-EM, and discusses initialization strategies.  
- Main theorems explicitly construct a general class of counterexamples, giving a definitive negative answer.  
- Shows EM and gradient-EM with common random initialization converge to suboptimal local maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Presents a simplified illustrative proof for the case k=3, d=1 to build intuition.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing rigorous theory and intuitive explanations.  
- Results are important for non-convex optimization, highlighting persistent challenges even in asymptotic limits for simple distributions.  
- Reviewer did not deeply check supplementary material and requests estimates for constants in probability bounds to assess practical relevance.  
- Suggests that either analytic estimates or supporting simulations would help identify a potential phase transition as k grows.  
- Overall judgment: novel, impactful contribution that significantly advances understanding of optimization limits in GMMs.
