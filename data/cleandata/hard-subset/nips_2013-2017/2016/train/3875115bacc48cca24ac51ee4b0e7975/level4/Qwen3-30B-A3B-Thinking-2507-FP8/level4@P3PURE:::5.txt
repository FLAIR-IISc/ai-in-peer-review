

The paper provides a definitive answer to Srebro's (2007) open question—"Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?"—by constructing a general class of counterexamples that demonstrate the absence of such local maxima. The authors begin by outlining the optimization schemes for Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs), including the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and address initialization strategies, noting a common choice. They then present their primary theorems, which establish the counterexample to Srebro's question. Crucially, they further show that even with standard random initialization, both EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. The paper offers intuitive insight through a simplified proof for the case $k=3$, $d=1$. The exposition is exceptionally clear, effectively balancing theoretical depth with accessible intuition while adhering to the paper's length constraints. The question resolved by the authors holds significant importance for understanding non-convex optimization challenges in the asymptotic limit, even for simple distributions. While the supplementary material was not fully verified, providing estimates for the constants in the probability bound would strengthen the asymptotic analysis. Such estimates would clarify the "phase transition" point at which convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes probable in the asymptotic regime, which is critical given that $k$ is typically small in practice. If the constants prove intractable, supporting simulations would be valuable to empirically confirm this phenomenon. Nevertheless, this minor point does not diminish the paper's novelty or substantial impact.
