- Paper proves three main results: (1) EM can converge to local optima with infinite samples for a 3-cluster configuration (two close, one far); (2) random initialization succeeds with probability ≤ exp(-Ω(k)); (3) gradient EM generically avoids strict saddles, so poor local maxima are the primary barrier.

- Central k=3 counterexample: with cluster centers separated by Ω(√d), the log-likelihood has a local maximum where one center aligns with the two close clusters and two centers approximate the distant cluster.

- This failure mode is unsurprising and closely mirrors k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm failures when clusters lie near Voronoi boundaries.

- Extension to k components via recursive construction is technically sound but follows standard clustering techniques.

- The random-initialization negative result parallels known k-means results (e.g., needing ~k log k centers with distance-squared sampling), so it offers limited novelty.

- Key gap: the paper lacks direct comparison to prior clustering guarantees, notably Kumar and Kannan (2004) for Lloyd’s algorithm under Ω(√d) separation.

- When separation exceeds √d, EM and Lloyd’s algorithm become effectively equivalent for spherical Gaussians, implying the paper’s counterexamples are analogous to known k-means counterexamples.

- Authors should explicitly address (i) why EM’s failures are distinct from k-means despite similar failure modes, and (ii) how their results refine or extend existing k-means insights.

- Technical execution and exposition are rigorous and coherent, but the contributions are incremental and confirm expected behavior from clustering theory rather than introducing a new EM-specific failure mechanism.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—revise discussion to contrast with Kumar-Kannan, clarify EM vs Lloyd under separation, and emphasize any uniquely EM-specific findings.
