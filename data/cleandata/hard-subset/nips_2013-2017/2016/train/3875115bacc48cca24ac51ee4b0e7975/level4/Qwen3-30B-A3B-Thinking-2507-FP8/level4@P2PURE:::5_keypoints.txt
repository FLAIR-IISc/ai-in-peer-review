- The paper gives a definitive answer to Srebro's 2007 open question by constructing a general class of counterexamples that settle the question (negative result as stated).  
- The authors review likelihood-maximization schemes for Gaussian Mixture Models, focusing on EM and gradient EM algorithms and standard initialization choices.  
- The main theorems present a counterexample demonstrating the existence of suboptimal local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood.  
- They prove that with standard random initialization, both EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- A simplified illustrative case (k=3, d=1) is proved to build intuition for the general construction.  
- The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing space constraints while conveying intuition for the theoretical results.  
- The resolved question is significant for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.
