- EM can converge to a suboptimal local maximum even for a simple mixture of three well-separated spherical Gaussians with infinite samples.
- For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Gradient EM generically does not converge to strict saddle points, implying spurious local maxima are the main obstacle.
- The paper presents a coherent set of negative results showing EM fails to reach the global maximum in the infinite-sample setting.
- The techniques and findings are not very surprising and follow expected lines given known clustering behavior.
- Main counterexample: two close components and one distant component lead to a local maximum where one center covers the two close components and two centers cover the distant one.
- With center separation ≫ √d, clusters have minimal overlap and the problem qualitatively resembles k-means.
- The extension to k components via a recursive construction is straightforwardly obtained and expected.
- The random-init failure parallels k-means’ failure probability, motivating more sophisticated initializations like k-means++.
- The paper would be strengthened by direct comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s literature (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) to better contextualize contributions.
- Note that EM and Lloyd differ in high-overlap regimes (as in Balakrishnan, Wainwright & Yu), but behave similarly under the large-separation regime used here.
