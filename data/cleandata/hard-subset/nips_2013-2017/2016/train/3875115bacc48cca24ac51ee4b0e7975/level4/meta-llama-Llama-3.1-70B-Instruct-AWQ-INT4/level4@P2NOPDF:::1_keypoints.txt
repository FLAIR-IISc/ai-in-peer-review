- Paper studies convergence of EM and iterative heuristics for ML estimation of spherical Gaussian mixtures, showing non-convergence to global maxima.  
- Main result: EM can get stuck in local optima even with infinite samples and well-separated clusters.  
- For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)), implying high chance of suboptimal convergence.  
- Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, so bad local maxima (not saddles) are the primary failure mode.  
- Reviewer finds the results coherent but largely unsurprising and aligned with expectations.  
- Primary illustrative example: three-component mixture with two close components and one far-away, producing a local maximum with centers grouped counterintuitively.  
- When center separation ≫ √d, the problem behavior mirrors k-means clustering.  
- Similar bad instances are known for Lloyd’s algorithm (k-means), and the paper’s construction parallels those examples.  
- The authors extend the three-component example to k components via a recursive construction, which the reviewer views as expected though technically involved.  
- The poor success of random initialization for EM is attributed to the same causes as k-means failures, yielding comparable failure probabilities.  
- Suggestion: compare results explicitly to k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic and related guarantees (e.g., Kumar and Kannan) and discuss alternative initializations like k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.
