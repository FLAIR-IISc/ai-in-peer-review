- Paper shows local maxima in GMM log-likelihood persist even with infinite data for isotropic, known-scale components and can be arbitrarily far from the global optimum.
- Analysis focuses on well-spaced mixture centers and claims EM with random initialization will almost certainly fail to find the global optimum as the number of components increases.
- Manuscript is generally well-written but contains several minor typos.
- Main practical concern: Theorem 1 proves existence for a specific center configuration, but the paper lacks analysis of how likely such configurations are in real data.
- Recommend assuming a distribution on true centers and quantifying the probability that “bad” (EM-trapping) configurations occur as k or dimension grows.
- Theorem 2’s reliance on random initialization is questionable: initializing from data can be implausible when clusters are obvious (e.g., unequal cluster densities), reducing practical relevance.
- Request clarification or justification that random-data initialization realistically models practice when clusters are well-separated and imbalanced.
- Example on lines 74–81 is unclear and doesn’t convincingly support the Sresbro conjecture claim for k=2; its purpose should be clarified or removed.
- Lines 233–235: requirement (2) is clear but the need for requirement (1) is not justified—does it imply initialization between clusters may not converge to the nearest true cluster?
- Lines 267–273: unclear why showing EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint is practically important; need comparison of harms between saddlepoint vs local-optimum convergence.
- Minor edits/corrections suggested: line 18 (“that the”), line 38 (“estimates”), line 60 (rephrase), line 138 (clarify O(d^1/2)-separated meaning), line 172 (remove/revise redundant sentence), line 177 (“to”), line 218 (define “well-separated constant”), line 220 (remove duplicate “the”), line 225 (clarify meaning of Ω(k)), line 245 (fix wording), line 249 (add “that”), line 308 (add “that”).
