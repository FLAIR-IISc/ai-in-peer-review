- Study of local maxima structure of GMM log-likelihood with equal weights and identity covariance.  
- Result 1: A counterexample (k=3, d=1) shows local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: For a specific mean configuration, random initialization can have exponentially small probability (in k) of avoiding convergence to a suboptimal local maximum.  
- Result 3: Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proofs are sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea appears reasonable though appendix details weren’t fully checked.  
- Novelty: Establishes a novel connection between mean configuration and local maxima; Theorem 2’s hierarchical grouping insight is particularly original.  
- Impact: Results have significant theoretical and practical implications (e.g., for clustering and seeding strategies); raises question whether Lemma 7 extends to d>1.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-presented with clear problem setup and intuitive explanations.
