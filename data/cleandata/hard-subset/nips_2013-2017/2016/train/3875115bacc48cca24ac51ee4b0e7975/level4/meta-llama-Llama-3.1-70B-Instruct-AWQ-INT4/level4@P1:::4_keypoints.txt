- Paper studies local maxima structure of GMM log-likelihoods with equal weights and identity covariance.  
- Result 1: Constructs a counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: Shows random initialization can have exponentially small probability (in k) of avoiding bad local maxima for special mean configurations.  
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Analysis is thorough with clear intuition and interpretation of results.  
- Theorem 1 proofs in main paper appear sound; Theorem 2 proof idea seems reasonable though appendix not fully checked.  
- Novel insight: the configuration of means determines local maxima structure—an original and intuitive observation.  
- Key technique: hierarchical grouping of true means (from Theorem 1) extends to characterize local maxima for k>3.  
- Potential impact: motivates study of local optima in related clustering tasks (e.g., k-means) and may inform algorithm design.  
- Practical implication: emphasizes importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and could guide new seeding strategies.  
- Suggestion/question: investigate whether Lemma 7 (and related results) generalizes to d>1.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel, thorough, well-written, and potentially impactful.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: restricted to equal weights and identity covariance, and analysis is mainly for population log-likelihood (finite-sample generalization unclear).  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
