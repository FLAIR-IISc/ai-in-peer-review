- Answers Srebro (2007) negatively by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.  
- Analyzes likelihood maximization in GMMs with focus on EM and gradient EM algorithms and a common random initialization choice.  
- Main theorems show EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability under that common random initialization.  
- Provides a simple illustrative case (k = 3, d = 1) to build intuition for the results.  
- Paper is very clear and well written, balancing space constraints and theoretical intuition.  
- Tackles an important question for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic settings.  
- Reviewer did not thoroughly check the supplement; constants in the probability bounds are not fully estimated.  
- Concrete constants or supporting simulations would clarify when (in k) the phase transition to bad convergence occurs in practice.  
- If constants are complex or uninterpretable, simulations should be provided to demonstrate the phenomenon; this concern does not hurt the paper’s novelty or impact.
