- Paper studies local maxima of the log-likelihood for equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs and presents three main results.  
- Result 1: Counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: With random initialization and specially configured means, the probability of converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k.  
- Result 3: Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1 proofs are verified and sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable though appendices were not fully checked.  
- Novelty: Establishes an original connection between mean configuration and the structure of local maxima.  
- Key conceptual innovation: Theorem 2 leverages a hierarchical grouping of true means to explain local maxima for k>3.  
- Potential theoretical impact: Could motivate study of local optima structure in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means).  
- Practical impact: Highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may inform new algorithm design.  
- Open question: It would be interesting to examine whether Lemma 7 extends to dimensions d > 1.  
- Clarity: The paper is well-presented with clear setup, intuition, and interpretation of results.
