- Paper analyzes structure of local maxima of the GMM log-likelihood for equal weights and identity covariances.  
- Main result 1: Constructs a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum (resolves Srebro 2007).  
- Main result 2: Shows random initialization can have exponentially small probability (in k) of avoiding bad local maxima for certain mean configurations.  
- Main result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Key conceptual contribution: links true-mean configuration to the location/structure of local maxima.  
- Theorem 2 insight: hierarchical grouping of true means determines local maxima for k>3.  
- Technical quality: proofs are sound, well-organized, and intuition is clearly explained.  
- Novelty: introduces a new hierarchical grouping approach that may apply to other clustering problems (e.g., k-means).  
- Practical implication: emphasizes the need to seed enough centers per true cluster and motivates better initialization schemes.  
- Limitation 1: results are restricted to k >= 3.  
- Limitation 2: no generalization provided for k < 3.  
- Limitation 3: analysis is on the population log-likelihood; extension to sample-based log-likelihood is unclear.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant contributions with potential theoretical and practical impact.  
- Recommendation and rating: Accept, rating 9/10.
