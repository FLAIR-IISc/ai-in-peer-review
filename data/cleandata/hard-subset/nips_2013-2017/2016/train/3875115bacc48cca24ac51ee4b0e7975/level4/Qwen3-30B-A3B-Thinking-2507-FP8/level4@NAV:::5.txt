

The paper resolves the open question posed by Srebro in his 2007 work "Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?" by providing a negative answer through a general class of counterexamples. The authors first detail optimization schemes for Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and address initialization strategies with a common choice. They then state their core theorems, which constructively refute Srebro's question [2007]. Furthermore, they demonstrate that even with standard random initialization, both EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. The paper offers intuitive justification via a simplified case (k=3, d=1). It is exceptionally clear and well-structured, effectively balancing theoretical depth with accessible intuition within the paper's constraints. The resolved question holds significant importance for advancing understanding of non-convex optimization challenges, even in asymptotic regimes for simple distributions. While the supplementary material was not thoroughly examined, providing quantitative estimates of the constants in the probability bounds would be valuable. This is significant because such estimates would clarify the phase transition point where convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes prevalent in the asymptotic limit—particularly relevant as k is typically not excessively large. If the constants prove unwieldy or unclear, supporting simulations would help validate the observed phenomenon. Nevertheless, this suggestion does not diminish the paper's novelty or impact.
