- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by constructing examples that show bad local maxima exist in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.  
- Clarifies optimization schemes for GMMs, including EM and gradient-based EM, and common initialization practices.  
- Proves core theorems establishing the counterexamples and their generalization from a simple base case.  
- First analyzes a simplified setting (k=3, d=1) to build intuition, then extends results to the general case.  
- Shows that EM and gradient EM, with standard random initialization, converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, balancing theoretical rigor and accessible exposition while managing space effectively.  
- Contribution is important for understanding non-convex optimization in the asymptotic regime and advances GMM theory.  
- Minor critique: probabilistic bounds contain unspecified constants; quantifying them (or providing simulations) would clarify practical regimes and phase transitions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance.
