- Paper studies local maxima structure of the log-likelihood for equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs.
- Result 1: Constructs a counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Shows that under a particular configuration of means, random initialization can fail with probability exponentially small in k to avoid bad local maxima.
- Result 3: Proves that gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Proofs (Theorem 1 checked; Theorem 2 plausible) are sound and clearly explained; appendix details not fully verified by reviewer.
- Novelty: Connects mean configurations to local maxima structure; the hierarchical grouping idea for k>3 is an original and clever insight.
- Theoretical impact: Results may stimulate study of local optima in related clustering problems (e.g., k-means) and extensions to d>1.
- Practical impact: Highlights importance of seeding enough points per true cluster and could inspire new initialization/algorithm designs.
- Clarity: Presentation and intuitive explanations are excellent; problem setup and analyses are clearly introduced and interpreted.
