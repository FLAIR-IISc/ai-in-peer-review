- Paper studies convergence of EM-like methods for spherical Gaussian mixtures and proves negative population-level results.  
- Result 1: Even for a well-separated 3-component mixture with infinite samples, EM can converge to suboptimal local maxima.  
- Result 2: For k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Result 3: Gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so poor local maxima—not saddles—are the main obstacle.  
- Overall conclusion: EM need not reach global optima even in the infinite-sample limit.  
- Reviewer view: Findings are coherent but largely unsurprising and follow expected proof techniques.  
- Central counterexample: a 3-component mixture with two close components and one far one yields a local maximum that duplicates a center on the far cluster.  
- Under strong separation (≫ sqrt(d)), EM behavior mirrors k-means dynamics and its known pathologies.  
- Extension to k components via a recursive construction is clean and technically nontrivial but anticipated.  
- Random-init failure parallels standard k-means analyses, motivating alternatives like k log k seeding or distance-squared sampling.  
- Suggestion: paper would be strengthened by an explicit comparison to k-means/Lloyd’s (and positive guarantees such as Kumar–Kannan) and by contrasting with EM behavior under modest separation (e.g., Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu).
