- Resolves Srebro (2007): provides a definitive negative answer on existence of local maxima in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihood via a counterexample.  
- Counterexample is constructed for a general class of cases and is presented as convincing.  
- Reviews EM and gradient EM algorithms and discusses initialization issues, proposing a common initialization choice.  
- Main theorems formalize the counterexample and its consequences.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM with common random initialization converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Offers intuitive proof sketches by analyzing a simple case (k=3, d=1).  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, balancing brevity and intuitive explanation.  
- The result is important for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.  
- Reviewer recommends adding estimates for the constants in the probability term or supporting simulations to indicate where the phase transition appears.  
- Notes that the lack of these constants/simulations does not reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
