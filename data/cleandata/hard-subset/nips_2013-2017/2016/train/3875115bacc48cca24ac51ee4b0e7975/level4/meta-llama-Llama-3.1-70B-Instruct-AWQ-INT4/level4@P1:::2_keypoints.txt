- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, fixed weights, and fixed covariances (theoretical focus).  
- Proves three negative results: existence of local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than global even for large samples.  
- Shows randomly-initialized EM converges with high probability to critical points with arbitrarily suboptimal likelihood.  
- Shows the same negative convergence result holds for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Restricts model to simplified setting (known k, uniform weights, identity covariances) for tractable, rigorous proofs.  
- Reviewer finds these simplifications acceptable given technicality and informative nature of results.  
- Paper claims general-dimension results but proofs are carried out for d = 1; reviewer asks authors to clarify extension to d > 1 or explain added difficulties.  
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too short and lacks detail, especially lines 288–306 dealing with limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.  
- Reviewer questions the use of continuity to justify the convergence statements and requests more detailed arguments for maxima limits.  
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and needs clearer derivation of Equation (5).  
- Strengths: addresses an important question, produces informative negative results, and is generally well written.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient detail in Theorem 1 proof, unclear derivation of Eq. (5), and no discussion on extending proofs to arbitrary dimension.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand the proof details and clarify the Gradient EM derivation and dimensionality discussion.
