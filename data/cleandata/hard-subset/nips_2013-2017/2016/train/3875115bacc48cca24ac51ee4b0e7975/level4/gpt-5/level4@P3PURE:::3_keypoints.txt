- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihood for isotropic components with known variance, showing local modes persist in the infinite-sample limit and can be arbitrarily suboptimal.  
- Analysis targets configurations where true means form well-separated groups and argues random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with probability →1 as k grows.  
- Overall well written but contains occasional typos.  

- Main concern 1: Practical relevance — Theorem 1 constructs specific “bad” true-center configurations; how likely are these in practice? paper should posit a distribution over true centers and quantify probability of bad configurations as functions of k and d.  
- Main concern 2: Practicality of random initialization — For clearly clustered data (e.g., two close Gaussians and one far), random initialization seems an unrealistic model since practitioners would not place centers counterintuitively; clarify practical relevance of the random-init assumption.  

- Specific comment (L74–81): The example is unclear and does not convincingly support the Srebro conjecture claim for k = 2; clarify its purpose.  
- Specific comment (L233–235): Why is requirement (1) necessary in addition to requirement (2)? explain its role (e.g., centers initialized between clusters).  
- Specific comment (L267–273): Paper shows EM unlikely to converge to saddle points — explain why avoiding saddles is practically important and compare harm of saddles vs suboptimal local maxima.
