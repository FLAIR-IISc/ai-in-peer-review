- Paper studies local modes in GMM log-likelihoods for isotropic, known-scale, well-specified mixtures and shows local modes persist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily suboptimal.
- Analysis focuses on configurations where true centers form well-spaced clusters; authors claim random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with overwhelming probability as k grows.
- Writing is generally clear but contains occasional typos.

Main concerns about practical relevance:
- Theorem 1 constructs a specific “bad” center configuration; reviewer questions how likely such configurations are in practice and requests probabilistic statements under a distribution over true centers.
- Theorem 2 and its handling of initialization do not convince the reviewer that “bad” true-center configurations occur with increasing probability; they want a clear quantitative probability under distributional assumptions.
- Random initialization may be an unrealistic practical model when cluster densities differ markedly (example: two close components vs. one far component), making the problematic initialization scenarios unlikely in practice.

Comments on specific parts:
- L74–81: purpose of the example is unclear and does not convincingly support the Srebro conjecture for k=2.
- L233–235: reviewer questions necessity of requirement (1) and whether an initialization between clusters can move to the correct cluster.
- L267–273: unclear why proving EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point is practically important compared to converging to a local optimum.
