- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question: shows bad local maxima exist in population GMM likelihood for M ≥ 3 even with well-separated, equal-weight, spherical Gaussians.  
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.  
- Establishes failure is due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Provides a concrete counterexample to Srebro (2007) and contrasts with positive two-component results (Balakrishnan et al., 2015).  
- Connects to non-convex optimization literature (e.g., Ge et al., Lee et al.) by highlighting local maxima as the main obstacle for GMMs.  
- Strength: Technically rigorous analysis with well-constructed proofs and clear structural insights into the likelihood landscape.  
- Strength: Results hold in highly favorable settings, underscoring the necessity of careful initialization for EM.  
- Practical implication: Explains why random initialization can fail and motivates more sophisticated initialization methods (e.g., method of moments).  
- Originality: Highly original in addressing and resolving a long-standing theoretical question for M ≥ 3.  
- Significance: Advances theoretical understanding and has clear practical relevance for GMM estimation.  
- Clarity issue: Proof of Theorem 1 is brief and only proved for d=1 despite claims for general d; extension to higher dimensions should be clarified.  
- Technical gap: Argument in lines 288–306 about limits of supremums needs more justification; continuity alone may be insufficient.  
- Exposition issue: Derivation/explanation of the gradient EM update (Eq.5, lines 162–172) is unclear and could be made more transparent.  
- Limitation: Focuses on equally-weighted, spherical Gaussians with known covariance; extensions to unequal weights or non-spherical cases are not explored.  
- Overall judgment: Minor proof and exposition weaknesses but technically sound, original, significant work — recommendation to accept.
