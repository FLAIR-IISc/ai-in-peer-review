- Population-setting result: EM can converge to a spurious local optimum even for a simple mixture of three widely separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Random initialization failure: For k-component mixtures, the probability EM succeeds from random initialization is at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Gradient EM observation: Convergence to strict saddle points is not generic for Gradient EM, so spurious local maxima are the main obstacle.  
- Overall verdict: The paper presents a coherent set of negative results showing EM can fail to find the global maximum even in idealized settings.  
- Novelty concern: Central findings are arguably unsurprising and follow predictable analytical techniques.  
- k-means analogy: In the high-separation regime studied, the mixture likelihood approximates the k-means objective, making the EM failures analogous to known k-means issues.  
- 3-component counterexample: Two nearby components and one distant component create a spurious local maximum where one learned center sits between the nearby true components.  
- k-component extension: The paper generalizes the 3-component example to k components via a recursive construction that is elegant but conceptually similar.  
- Random-init analysis parallel: The failure-probability analysis mirrors reasoning for Lloyd’s k-means, yielding similar exponential-in-k failure.  
- Suggested improvement: Add a more detailed discussion contrasting these negative EM results with positive k-means guarantees (e.g., Kumar & Kannan 2010) and k-means++/seeding strategies to clarify the specific contribution beyond the k-means analogy.
