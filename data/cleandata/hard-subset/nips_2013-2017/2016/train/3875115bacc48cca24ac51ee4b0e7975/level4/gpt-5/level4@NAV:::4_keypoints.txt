- Paper analyzes the landscape of local maxima of the log-likelihood for GMMs with equal weights and identity covariance.  
- Theorem 1: provides a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Theorem 2: under a special arrangement of means, random initialization has exponentially small (in k) probability of avoiding bad local maxima.  
- Theorem 3: gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: main-text proofs (Theorem 1) are sound; Theorem 2’s proof strategy is plausible though appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Novelty: original linkage between component-mean configuration and local maxima; the hierarchical grouping idea for k>3 is especially clever.  
- Impact: findings are relevant theoretically and practically, suggesting extensions to related clustering objectives (e.g., k-means) and implications for algorithm design.  
- Limitations/suggestions: worth investigating whether Lemma 7 extends to d>1 and the practical need to seed enough points per true cluster.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written with effective intuition and interpretation accompanying results.
