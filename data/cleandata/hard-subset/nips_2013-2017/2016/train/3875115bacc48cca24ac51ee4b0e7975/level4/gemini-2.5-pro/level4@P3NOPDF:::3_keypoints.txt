- Paper analyzes spurious local modes in the population log-likelihood of isotropic, well-specified GMMs and shows such modes can be arbitrarily poor compared to the global optimum.
- Analysis focuses on configurations where true mixture centers form well-separated clusters and shows spurious modes persist even with infinite data.
- Theorem 2 claims random-sample initialization for EM will almost surely fail to find the global optimum as the number of components increases.
- Paper is well-written and tackles an important problem, but practical implications are questioned.
- Main reservation 1: unclear how likely the “bad” parameter configurations are in practice; requests analysis under distributional assumptions on true centers and whether probability grows with k or d.
- Main reservation 2: relevance of using simple random-sample initialization—practitioners often use smarter methods (e.g., k-means++); asks whether the failure mode extends to these practical initializations.
- Clarification needed (L74-81): purpose and takeaway of the example regarding Srebro’s conjecture for k=2 is unclear.
- Clarification needed (L233-235): why is requirement (1) (no more than one initialized center per true component) necessary in addition to at least one per cluster?
- Clarification needed (L267-273): practical importance of the result that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddle point—how problematic is saddle convergence relative to poor local optima?
- Minor language/typo suggestions: L18 “We further show gradient EM algorithm” → add “that the”; L38 “estimate” → “estimates”; L60 rephrase for clarity.
- Minor notation/clarity requests: L138 clarify meaning of O(d^1/2)-separated; L218 define “well-separated constant”; L225 define Ω(k) for readers.
