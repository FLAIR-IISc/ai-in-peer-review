This paper presents an answer to the open question posed by Srebro in his 2007 paper, "Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample probability of Gaussian mixture estimation?" The paper answers the question negatively through the construction of a general class of counterexamples. The authors begin to outline the schemes by which the probability is maximized in the setting of the Gaussian mixture model. These include EM and gradient EM algorithms. The authors also address the issue of initialization and offer a general alternative. Then the authors state their primary theorems, which exemplify the question posed by Srebro [2007]. The authors further show that even with the general alternative of random initialization, EM and gradient EM algorithms converge rapidly to non-optimal maxima with high probability. Then the authors provide some intuition for their proofs by proving a simple case (k=3, d=1). The paper is very clear and well-written, and well balances the paper's space requirements with the need to provide intuition for its theoretical result. The question answered by the authors is very important for further understanding the issues arising with non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions. I did not carefully check the supplementary material, but some estimates of the constant in the probability term given by them would be good. This is important because I think no one would consider k too large. It would be good to see estimates of the constant when we can start seeing convergence for bad solutions in the asymptotic limit (i.e. where is the step transition?). If the constants are too complex or not understood, auxiliary simulations would be good, at least it is an event that we can observe. However, this issue does not diminish the novelty or impact of this paper.
