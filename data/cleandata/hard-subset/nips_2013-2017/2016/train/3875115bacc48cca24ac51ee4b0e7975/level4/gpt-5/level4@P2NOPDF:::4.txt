Summary
This paper analyzes the landscape of the log-likelihood for Gaussian mixture models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance matrices. It establishes three main results:
- Existence of bad local maxima: For k = 3 components in one dimension (d = 1), there are local maxima whose likelihood can be made arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Random initialization can fail with high probability: For a carefully chosen configuration of component means, the probability that random initialization avoids converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k.
- Gradient EM avoids saddle points: Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.

Technical quality
I verified the proofs in the main paper for Theorem 1 and found them sound. Based on the analysis of Theorem 1, the proof strategy for Theorem 2 appears reasonable, though I did not check the appendix details. The statements of all three theorems are accompanied by clear interpretations that help connect the formal results to intuition.

Novelty and originality
The paper draws a direct connection between the configuration of the component means and the structure of local maxima in the GMM log-likelihood. Although intuitive, this perspective is presented in a novel and insightful way. In particular, the key idea underlying Theorem 2—building on Theorem 1—that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means determines the local maxima for k > 3 is especially clever and original.

Potential impact and usefulness
The results have potential impact in both theory and practice. Theoretically, they may spur further study of local optimality landscapes in related clustering problems, such as k-means. It would also be interesting to examine whether the result in Lemma 7 extends to dimensions d > 1. Practically, the findings highlight the importance of seeding enough initial points within each true cluster and could inform the design of more robust initialization schemes and algorithms for mixture models.

Clarity and presentation
The paper is clearly written and well organized. It introduces the problem setup cleanly and consistently provides intuition alongside formal analysis. The explanations following each theorem are helpful, and the overall presentation makes the contributions easy to understand.
