- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian mixture means with known/fixed number of components, weights, and covariances.
- Main contributions: three negative results showing EM can fail badly even with infinite data.
- Result 1: Likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Result 2: EM with random initialization converges with high probability to a suboptimal critical point.
- Result 3: A first-order variant of EM also suffers the same high-probability convergence to suboptimal points.
- The problem is interesting and worth investigating.
- For rigorous proofs the paper restricts to a simplified model: uniform weights and identity covariances.
- These simplifications are acceptable given technical complexity and the informative nature of the results.
- Paper states results for general dimension d but provides proofs only for d = 1.
- Authors should discuss whether extensions to higher dimensions are possible or what additional difficulties arise.
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too concise and needs more detailed exposition (especially lines 288–306).
- Specifically, limits/suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains and the continuity-based convergence claims need clearer justification.
- The first-order EM section (lines 362–372) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) requires more explanation.
- Pros: significant negative results, strong theoretical contribution, well-motivated problem, and mostly clear presentation.
- Cons: concise proof of Theorem 1, restriction to 1D without discussion of higher dimensions, and unclear exposition of the first-order EM section.
- Overall: paper is a valuable contribution and merits acceptance subject to minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
