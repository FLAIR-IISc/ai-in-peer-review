- Paper shows EM can be trapped in local optima even for 3 well-separated Gaussian components with infinite samples.  
- For k Gaussian mixtures, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima are the main obstacle to global convergence.  
- Overall conclusion: EM fails to converge to global optima even with infinite samples; results form a cohesive negative narrative.  
- Reviewer finds the results unsurprising and the techniques follow predictable lines.  
- Main illustrative example: two close components and one far component produce a log-likelihood local maximum with misassigned centers.  
- When cluster separation ≫ √d, behavior resembles k-means and non-overlapping clusters make the example expected.  
- Recursive construction extends the 3-component failure to k components; technically non-trivial but anticipated.  
- Failure of random initialization parallels k-means failure probabilities, motivating k·log k centers or distance-squared sampling.  
- Suggestion: include a detailed comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic (and Kumar & Kannan guarantees) to highlight similarities and differences.  
- Note: EM differs from Lloyd’s when Gaussians are not well-separated (per Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu), which should be acknowledged.
