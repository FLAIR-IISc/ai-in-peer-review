- Main result: Negatively answers Srebro’s 2007 question by constructing a broad class of counterexamples showing local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixtures.
- Approach: Reviews likelihood maximization for Gaussian mixture models via EM and gradient (first-order) EM, and discusses standard random initialization.
- Theorems: Provide explicit counterexamples and prove that EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with exponentially high probability.
- Illustration: Offers intuition through a simple concrete case (M=3, d=1).
- Writing and organization: Paper is clearly written, well organized, and balances space constraints with conveying intuition.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in the population limit for benign mixture models.
- Concern: Supplementary material was not carefully verified by the reviewer.
- Suggestion: Recommend quantifying constants in the probability bounds (or providing simulations) to clarify when the bad-convergence behavior becomes observable in practice; lack of this does not reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
