- Core result: For any M ≥ 3, the population log-likelihood of a well-separated, spherical, uniform GMM can have spurious local maxima.  
- Core result: The log-likelihood at these local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Core result: EM and a first-order EM variant with a common random initialization converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability → 1 exponentially fast in M.  
- Additional algorithmic finding: For the first-order EM variant, failure is due to bad local maxima rather than strict saddle points.  
- Significance: Resolves a long-standing open question (Srebro 2007) and challenges optimistic beliefs about the GMM population likelihood landscape.  
- Technical quality: The theoretical construction and proofs are rigorous and include analysis of algorithmic consequences for EM.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well written and organized, with a helpful M=3 intuition for the construction.  
- Weakness: Main results are asymptotic in M, leaving unclear at what (practical) M the phenomenon becomes relevant.  
- Suggestion: Provide analysis or bounds for the universal constant c in the 1 − e^{−cM} failure probability to identify the phase transition regime.  
- Suggestion: If analytic constants are hard, add simulations showing empirical convergence rates to suboptimal maxima as M increases.  
- Overall: Strong, novel, and impactful work that merits acceptance.
