- Resolves Srebro (2007)’s open question by constructing broad counterexamples showing spurious, suboptimal local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixtures.
- Provides clear background on likelihood optimization for GMMs, covering EM and gradient EM and practical initialization issues (including common random schemes).
- Main theorems formally exhibit these counterexamples and prove their existence at the population level.
- Proves that under standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to these suboptimal local maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Builds intuition via a simple illustrative example (k = 3, d = 1) before generalizing to the full result.
- Exposition is clear, well-structured, and balances brevity with necessary technical intuition.
- Significance: sharpens understanding of non-convex optimization challenges by showing problems persist even in the infinite-sample limit for simple distributions.
- Suggestion: provide explicit estimates of constants in the probability bounds to make quantitative implications clearer.
- If constants are complex, recommend supporting simulations to show the phenomenon in realistic regimes where k is not large.
- Overall assessment: comments are minor and do not detract from the paper’s novelty, clarity, or impact.
