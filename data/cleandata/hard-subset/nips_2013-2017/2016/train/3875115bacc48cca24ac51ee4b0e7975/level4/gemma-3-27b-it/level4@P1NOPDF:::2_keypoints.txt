- Paper analyzes EM for Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, known weights, and known covariances, proving three negative results.
- Result 1: Even with large samples, the likelihood can have local maxima with arbitrarily lower values than the global maximum.
- Result 2: Randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood is arbitrarily smaller than the maximum.
- Result 3: The same adverse behavior holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and the restrictive model assumptions (uniform weights, identity covariances, known K) acceptable given proof complexity.
- Paper states results in general dimension d but presents proofs only for d = 1; reviewer asks for comment or discussion on extending to higher dimensions.
- Primary concern: Proof of Theorem 1 is too condensed (lines 288–306) and needs more detail, especially regarding limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.
- Continuity arguments used do not suffice to justify the claimed convergence of maxima; the reviewer requests a more detailed and rigorous argument.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and Equation (5)’s derivation should be explained more clearly.
