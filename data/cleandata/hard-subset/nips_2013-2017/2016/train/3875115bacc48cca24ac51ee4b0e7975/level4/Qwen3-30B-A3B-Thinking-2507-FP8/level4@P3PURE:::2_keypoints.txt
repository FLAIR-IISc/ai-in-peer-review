- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known component count, uniform weights, and identity covariances.  
- Presents three negative theoretical results: likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Shows random initialization leads EM to converge with high probability to critical points with likelihoods arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Extends analogous negative results to the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Model simplifications (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) are justified given proof complexity and the insights provided.  
- Although claimed for general dimension d, all proofs are restricted to d = 1; the authors should clarify extension to higher dimensions or discuss additional challenges.  
- Major concern: Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) lacks rigorous justification when exchanging pointwise limits with limits of suprema over unbounded, parameter-dependent sets—continuity alone is insufficient.  
- Suggest adding rigorous arguments (e.g., uniform convergence, compactness) or more detailed steps for the limiting suprema calculations.  
- Gradient EM analysis (lines 162–172) is unclear; derivation of Equation (5) should be clarified for readability and correctness.
