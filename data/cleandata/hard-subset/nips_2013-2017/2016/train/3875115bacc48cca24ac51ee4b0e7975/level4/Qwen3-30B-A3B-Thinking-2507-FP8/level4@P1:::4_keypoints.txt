- Resolves Srebro (2007) open problem by showing bad local maxima can exist in the population likelihood of equal-weight spherical GMMs for M ≥ 3.
- Constructs 1D, well-separated, equally-weighted GMMs whose population log-likelihood has local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.
- Directly contradicts Srebro’s conjecture that all local maxima are global in the infinite-sample limit for well-separated mixtures.
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to such bad local maxima with probability at least 1 − e^(−Ω(M)).
- Shows first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, indicating failures arise from bad local maxima, not saddles.
- Theorem 1 (structural result) has a careful, well-motivated proof with clear intuition, especially for M = 3 in one dimension.
- Theorem 2 uses a clever recursive construction to demonstrate exponentially small success probability for random initialization.
- Theorem 4 (avoidance of strict saddles) is elegant, leveraging recent gradient-method results without smoothness assumptions.
- Novelty: first rigorous demonstration of arbitrarily bad population local maxima for M ≥ 3, beyond known finite-sample examples.
- The hierarchical/geometric construction linking true means’ configuration to bad local maxima is a novel insight.
- Significance: fundamentally alters understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes and non-convex optimization in statistical learning.
- Practical implication: random initialization can be catastrophically unreliable; careful initialization or many restarts may be required.
- Clarity: paper is excellently written, well-organized, and balances rigor with accessible intuition.
- Weakness: main structural result is shown in one dimension; extending to higher dimensions would strengthen the contribution.
- Weakness: lacks concrete guidance or thorough comparison of alternative initialization strategies for practitioners.
- Overall recommendation: strong, technically sound contribution with significant implications — accept.
