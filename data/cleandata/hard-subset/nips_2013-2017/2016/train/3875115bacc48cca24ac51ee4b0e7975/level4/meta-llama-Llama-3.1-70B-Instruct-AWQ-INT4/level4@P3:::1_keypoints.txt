- Paper studies EM convergence for spherical Gaussian mixtures and shows non-convergence to global maxima even with infinite samples.
- Main technical contribution: a 3-component mixture construction where a local log-likelihood maximum places one center near two close components and two centers near the distant cluster.
- The 3-component construction is extended recursively to yield bad local maxima for k components.
- Analysis shows EM with random initialization converges to a bad critical point with high probability; success probability is exponentially small in k.
- First-order EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the primary problem.
- Results align with known behaviors of k-means/Lloyd under similar separation conditions.
- Reviewer finds the constructions and conclusions coherent but not entirely surprising; the 3-component example is intuitive.
- Recommendation: add a comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic to contextualize results.
- Suggestion: discuss practical implications (initialization schemes, choosing number of components).
- Writing and proofs are clear and well-organized, though the bad-example construction could use more intuition/explanation.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the comments above.
