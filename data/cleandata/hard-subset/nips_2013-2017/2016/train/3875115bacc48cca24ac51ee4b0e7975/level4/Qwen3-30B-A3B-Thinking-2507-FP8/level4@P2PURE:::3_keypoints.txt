- Paper studies local modes in the log-likelihood of isotropic, well-specified Gaussian mixture models and shows local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.

- Analysis focuses on center configurations partitioned into well-separated clusters and shows random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum almost surely as the number of components grows.

- Paper is well-written but contains occasional typos.

- Primary concern 1: Theorem 1 proves existence for a specific center configuration—how likely are such “bad” configurations in practice? Request assuming a distribution on true centers to quantify probability as k or d grow; unclear how Theorem 2 addresses this.

- Primary concern 2: Theorem 2 analyzes random-sample initialization for EM, but its practical relevance is questioned for clearly clustered data (e.g., two close clusters and one far): seems unlikely random init would place centers so poorly; also the example for the Sresbro conjecture (L74–L81) is unconvincing.

- Technical point: L233–L235—Requirement (1) is unclear: does initializing a center between two clusters prevent guaranteeing convergence to the correct cluster?

- Technical point: L267–L273—The proof that EM is unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint is interesting, but the reviewer asks why avoiding saddlepoints matters practically and how that compares to converging to suboptimal local minima.

- Minor editorial fixes requested: L18 add “that”; L38 pluralize “estimate”; L60 rephrase; L138 clarify O(d^{1/2})-separated remark; L172 remove/rephrase redundancy; L177 “equivalent to”; L218 clarify “well-separated” constant; L220 fix duplicate “the”; L225 define Ω(k); L245 “coincidence”; L249 “Recall that for uniformly weighted”; L308 “And claim that”.
