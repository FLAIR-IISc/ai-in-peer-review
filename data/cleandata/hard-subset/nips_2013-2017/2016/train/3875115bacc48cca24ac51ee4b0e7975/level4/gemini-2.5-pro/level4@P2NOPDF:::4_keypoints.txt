- Paper shows spurious local maxima can exist in GMM log-likelihoods (constructive counterexample for k=3, d=1) with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.
- For certain geometric arrangements of true means, EM with random initialization can avoid good solutions with probability exponentially small in k.
- Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Overall, the paper links true-parameter geometry to the structure of local optima in the GMM likelihood landscape.
- Proofs appear sound; Theorem 1 was verified in detail and Theorem 2’s technique is well-motivated though appendix derivations were not fully checked.
- The hierarchical grouping construction in Theorem 2 is a novel, non-trivial insight explaining spurious maxima for k>3.
- The results suggest important theoretical directions, e.g., extending one-dimensional lemmas to higher dimensions (d>1).
- Practically, the work highlights the critical role of initialization and basin of attraction in fitting GMMs.
- The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and provides helpful intuitions and interpretations.
