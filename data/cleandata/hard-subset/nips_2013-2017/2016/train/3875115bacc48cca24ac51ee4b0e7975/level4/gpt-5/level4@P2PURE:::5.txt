The paper addresses Srebro’s 2007 open question, “Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?”, by constructing a general class of counterexamples that establish the existence of non-global local maxima. The authors begin by outlining standard procedures for maximizing the likelihood in Gaussian mixture models, including EM and gradient EM, and they discuss initialization, presenting a common random initialization choice. They then state their main theorems, which provide the counterexamples to Srebro (2007). Moreover, they show that even under this common random initialization, both EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal local maxima with exponentially high probability. To build intuition, they work through a simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1). The paper is very clear and well written, striking a good balance between space constraints and providing intuition for the theoretical results. The question they resolve is important for understanding challenges inherent in non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions.

I did not carefully check the supplementary material, but estimates of the constants in the probability bound would be valuable. This is important because, in practice, k is typically not very large, and such constants would indicate when we should expect convergence to bad solutions in the asymptotic regime (i.e., where the phase transition occurs). If the constants are unwieldy or not easily interpretable, supporting simulations would be helpful to demonstrate that the phenomenon is observable. However, this request does not detract from the novelty or impact of the paper.
