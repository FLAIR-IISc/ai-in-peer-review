This paper provides a definitive answer to the long-standing question posed by Srebro in 2007, regarding the existence of local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation. The authors' counterexample, constructed through a general class of cases, convincingly demonstrates a negative response. The manuscript commences by outlining the methods employed for maximizing likelihood in Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and discusses the initialization issue, proposing a common choice. The primary theorems are then presented, providing a counterexample to Srebro's question. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate that even with a common random initialization, the EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To facilitate understanding, the authors provide intuition for their proofs by examining a simple scenario (k=3, d=1). The paper is exceptionally well-written, striking a balance between conciseness and the need for intuitive explanations of theoretical results. The addressed question holds significant importance for advancing our understanding of the challenges associated with non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions. While the supplementary material was not thoroughly examined, inclusion of estimates for the constants in the probability term would be beneficial. Specifically, it would be valuable to determine when the convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes apparent in the asymptotic limit (i.e., the location of the phase transition). If deriving these constants proves too complex, supporting simulations would be helpful in demonstrating the observability of this phenomenon. Nevertheless, this omission does not diminish the paper's novelty or impact.
