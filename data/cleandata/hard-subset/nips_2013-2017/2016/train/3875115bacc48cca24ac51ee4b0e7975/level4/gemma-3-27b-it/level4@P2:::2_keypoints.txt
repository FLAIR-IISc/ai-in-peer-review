- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known weights and covariances, estimating means.
- Presents three negative results: existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in likelihood.
- Shows randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to suboptimal critical points.
- Demonstrates the same high-probability failure for a first-order EM variant.
- Addresses an important, practical question about EM’s reliability with rigorous theoretical focus.
- Strength: clear motivation and informative, rigorous proofs within a simplified model.
- Model simplifications (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) are reasonable for tractability.
- Proofs primarily treat d = 1; extending to higher dimensions is noted but not fully addressed.
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) needs more justification when taking limits of suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.
- Continuity of the likelihood is insufficiently argued to imply the claimed convergence of suprema.
- First-order EM section (lines 324–335) and derivation of Equation (5) are hard to follow and need clearer justification from the gradient of Q(θ′ | θ).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand Theorem 1 proof and clarify the first-order EM derivation.
- Overall assessment: novel, significant, and largely sound contribution despite some terse proofs and limited dimensional discussion.
