- Paper studies local maxima of GMM log-likelihoods with equal weights and identity covariances and presents three main theoretical results.  
- Result 1: Provides a counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima can have arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: Shows that with random initialization, the probability of avoiding a poor local maximum can be exponentially small in k for a specific means configuration.  
- Result 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Proofs in the main paper (especially Theorem 1) are sound; Theorem 2’s approach seems reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Novelty: The connection between true-mean configurations and local maxima structure is highly original; the hierarchical grouping insight driving Theorem 2 is particularly clever.  
- Potential impact: Results are theoretically and practically significant, suggesting further study (e.g., extension of Lemma 7 to d>1) and implications for initializer design.  
- Clarity: The paper is well-written, with clear setup, explanations, and interpretations of results.
