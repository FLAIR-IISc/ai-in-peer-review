- Paper studies local maxima of GMM log-likelihoods for equal weights and identity covariances, proving three main results about poor local optima and optimization behavior.
- Theorem 1: For k=3 and d=1, there exist local maxima with likelihoods arbitrarily close to zero compared to the global optimum.
- Theorem 2: For certain mean configurations (and hierarchical groupings for k>3), random initialization has exponentially small (in k) probability of avoiding bad local maxima.
- Theorem 3: Gradient-based EM almost surely avoids convergence to saddle points of the log-likelihood.
- Technical quality: Proofs (notably Theorem 1) are rigorous and sound; Theorem 2 is well-motivated by Theorem 1 and statements are clearly justified.
- Novelty: Linking true-mean geometry to existence/properties of local maxima and the hierarchical grouping insight for k>3 is original and significant.
- Impact/usefulness: Results have theoretical and practical implications (e.g., seeding strategies, extensions to k-means and possibly d>1), guiding algorithm design to avoid poor local maxima.
- Clarity/presentation: Exposition is excellent; setup, intuition, proofs, and implications are clearly presented and accessible to theory and applied audiences.
- Overall: Rigorous, novel, and impactful work with a strong case for acceptance.
