- Provides a negative answer to Srebro (2007) by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing local maxima exist in the infinite-sample GMM likelihood.
- Reviews methods for GMM likelihood maximization, including EM and gradient EM, and discusses a common initialization choice.
- States primary theorems that serve as the counterexamples to Srebro’s question.
- Proves that EM and gradient EM with common random initialization converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Gives intuition and proof sketch via analysis of a simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1).
- Paper is well-written, clear, concise, and insightful about non-convex optimization even in the asymptotic limit.
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material and recommends including estimates of the constants in the probability bounds because practical k is typically small.
- If deriving constants is impractical, recommends adding simulations to demonstrate observability and identify the phase transition point.
- Notes that the absence of constants/simulations does not detract from the paper’s novelty or impact.
