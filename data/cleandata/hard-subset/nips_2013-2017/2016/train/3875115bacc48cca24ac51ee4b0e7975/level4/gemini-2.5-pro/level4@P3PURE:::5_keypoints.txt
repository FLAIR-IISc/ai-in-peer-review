- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by showing spurious local maxima can exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.
- Constructs a general class of counterexamples demonstrating these non-optimal maxima.
- Proves that EM and gradient EM with common random initialization converge to these bad maxima with exponentially high probability.
- Provides a clear, self-contained proof and intuition for the simpler case k=3, d=1.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and balances formal theory with intuition.
- Result is of fundamental importance for understanding non-convex optimization challenges even in simple, infinite-sample models.
- Reviewer did not verify supplementary material in detail.
- Suggests providing estimates for constants in the probability bounds or simulations for moderate k to assess practical relevance and possible phase transitions.
