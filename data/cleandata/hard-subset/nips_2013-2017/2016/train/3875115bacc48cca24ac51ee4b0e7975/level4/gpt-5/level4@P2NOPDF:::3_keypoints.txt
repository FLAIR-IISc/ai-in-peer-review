- Paper studies local maxima of the GMM log-likelihood for isotropic Gaussians with known variance and proves spurious local maxima exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Analysis focuses on geometric arrangements where true means form well-separated clusters and shows random initialization makes EM miss the global optimum with high probability as k grows.  
- Writing is clear overall, with a few typos.

Main concerns:
- Unclear how likely the “bad” true-center configurations are in practice; need a probabilistic model over centers and a theorem quantifying the prevalence of bad configurations.  
- Questionable realism of random initialization from the mixture when clusters are clearly separated; compare to practical heuristics (k-means++, multiple restarts) or justify the model experimentally/theoretically.

Clarifications requested:
- L74–81: unclear what the example demonstrates and how it supports Srebro’s conjecture for k = 2.  
- L233–235: why is requirement (1) necessary; can a center initialized between two clusters be guaranteed to move to the intended cluster?
