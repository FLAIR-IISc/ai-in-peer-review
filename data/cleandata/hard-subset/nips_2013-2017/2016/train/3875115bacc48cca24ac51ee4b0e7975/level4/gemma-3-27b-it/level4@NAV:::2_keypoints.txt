- Paper studies EM for estimating Gaussian mixture means with fixed component count, weights, and covariances, presenting theoretical analysis.
- Main contributions: three negative results—local maxima with large samples, random EM often converges to suboptimal critical points, and these issues extend to gradient EM.
- Restricting to a simplified model (known components, uniform weights, identity covariances) is reasonable and helps enable rigorous proofs.
- Results are informative and give valuable insight into practical EM behavior.
- Results and proofs are presented for the one-dimensional case (d=1), but the paper frames the problem in general dimension d.
- Authors should address generalizability to higher dimensions or explicitly acknowledge additional complexities when d > 1.
- Primary concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too concise; lines 288–306 need more detailed justification.
- Specific issues: establishing limits of suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains is not obvious, and continuity claims do not automatically imply the stated convergence properties.
- Did not check supplementary material; expanding the main proof (even at the cost of other discussion) would strengthen confidence in the theorem.
- Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) requires clearer explanation.
