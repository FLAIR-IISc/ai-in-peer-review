- Paper studies local modes in the log-likelihood of Gaussian mixture models with isotropic components and known scale.  
- Authors prove local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily suboptimal compared to the global optimum.  
- Analysis focuses on configurations where mixture centers split between well-spaced clusters, showing random initialization can cause EM to miss the global optimum with high probability as the number of components grows.  
- Paper is well-written overall but contains occasional typos.  
- Concern 1: The practical likelihood of the specific configuration in Theorem 1 is unclear; authors should consider assumptions on center distribution to show bad configurations become more probable with larger k or dimension.  
- Concern 1 cont.: Theorem 2 is claimed to address this but its relevance to practice is uncertain.  
- Concern 2: Theorem 2 studies EM initialized by sampling mixture centers, but this initialization seems unrealistic since practitioners would avoid placing centers in sparse regions.  
- Specific issue: Example on lines 74–81 does not convincingly demonstrate the Sresbro conjecture for k = 2 and its purpose is unclear.  
- Specific issue: Necessity of requirement (1) on lines 233–235 is unclear—initializing a center between clusters should intuitively move it to the correct cluster.  
- Specific issue: Practical importance of the result on lines 267–273 (EM unlikely to converge to a saddlepoint) is not apparent.  
- Minor edits: add "that the" before "gradient" (l.18); change "quality of the estimate" to "estimates" (l.38); revise sentence on l.60 to "to characterize under which conditions efficient algorithms...".  
- Minor edits cont.: Clarify definition of O(d^{1/2})-separated (l.138); remove or merge redundant sentence (l.172); explain "well-separated constant" (l.218); fix additional typos.
