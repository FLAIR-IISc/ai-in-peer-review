- Main results: (1) a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum; (2) certain mean configurations make random initialization avoid bad local maxima with probability exponentially small in k; (3) gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points.

- Proof quality: Theorem 1 proofs are checked and sound; Theorem 2’s proof strategy appears reasonable though appendix details were not fully verified; all theorems include clear interpretations.

- Novelty: Provides an original, rigorous connection between component-mean configuration and log-likelihood local maxima; Theorem 2’s hierarchical grouping idea is especially clever.

- Impact: Results could drive theoretical work on optimization landscapes (e.g., k-means) and practical improvements in initialization or new algorithms to avoid poor local maxima; suggests examining Lemma 7 for d>1.

- Presentation: Well written, clearly motivated, and interpretations are presented effectively.
