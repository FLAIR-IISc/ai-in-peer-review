The paper addresses the open question posed by Srebro in his 2007 work, "Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?" and provides a definitive negative answer through the construction of a general class of counterexamples. The authors begin by outlining common schemes for maximizing the likelihood in Gaussian Mixture Models, including the Expectation-Maximization (EM) and gradient-based EM algorithms, and discuss a typical initialization strategy. They then present their primary theorems, which demonstrate the existence of these counterexamples, thus refuting Srebro’s original conjecture [2007]. Furthermore, the authors show that even with a standard random initialization, the EM and gradient EM algorithms converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To provide intuition for their proofs, they present a simplified case (k=3, d=1). This paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, effectively balancing the constraints of paper length with the need to convey intuition for their theoretical results. The question addressed by the authors is of significant importance for advancing our understanding of the challenges inherent in non-convex optimization, even in the asymptotic limit for relatively simple distributions. 

While I did not perform a detailed check of the supplementary material, providing estimates for the constants within the probability bounds would be beneficial. This is particularly important as the practical relevance of the results depends on the magnitude of *k*. Such estimates would help determine the point at which convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes significant in the asymptotic limit – effectively, identifying a phase transition. If precise estimates prove difficult to obtain, supporting simulations demonstrating this phenomenon would be valuable. However, this point does not diminish the novelty or impact of this paper.
