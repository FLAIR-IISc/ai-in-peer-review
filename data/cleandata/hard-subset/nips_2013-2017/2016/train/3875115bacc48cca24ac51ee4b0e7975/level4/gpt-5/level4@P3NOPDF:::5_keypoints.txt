- Resolves Srebro’s 2007 question by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing spurious local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood for GMMs.  
- Clearly formulates the likelihood maximization problem for GMMs and reviews EM and gradient EM algorithms plus common initialization strategies.  
- Presents main theorems that exhibit the counterexamples and characterize non-optimal local maxima in the population likelihood.  
- Proves that under standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to these suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- Provides an intuitive, worked example (k=3, one dimension) that complements and clarifies the general theory.  
- Paper is very well written, balancing technical rigor and accessible explanations with helpful intuition.  
- Results challenge optimistic views that the population likelihood for mixtures is benign, clarifying the non-convex optimization landscape.  
- The convergence-to-bad-solutions result has important practical implications for limitations of EM-type methods.  
- Reviewer suggests adding explicit estimates of constants in the probability bounds to improve practical interpretability.  
- Specifically recommends showing how probabilities scale with k, dimension d, component separation/overlap, and weight imbalance.  
- Recommends indicating qualitative “phase transitions” in parameters or, if constants are complex, providing supporting simulations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful contribution that settles an open question; recommendation for acceptance.
