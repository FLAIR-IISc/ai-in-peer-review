- Main result: EM and related iterative MLE heuristics can get trapped in local optima for mixtures of spherical Gaussians, even with infinite samples.
- Concrete example: Even with three well-separated clusters, EM can converge to a local (non-global) maximum.
- Random initialization failure: For k-component mixtures, random initialization finds the global optimum with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Gradient EM behavior: Gradient EM does not typically converge to strict saddle points, implying poor local maxima are the usual failure mode.
- Overall claim: The results collectively show EM’s susceptibility to suboptimal solutions despite infinite data.
- Criticism: The findings are somewhat unsurprising and the techniques largely follow established reasoning.
- Illustrative construction: A 3-component instance (two close, one distant) yields an intuitive local maximum where centers misassign, resembling k-means under large separation.
- Extension to k: The recursive construction to generalize the example to k components is non-trivial but intuitively parallels known k-means failure instances.
- Random-init connection: The low success probability for random initialization mirrors k-means behavior and motivates heuristics like initializing k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.
- Suggested addition: The paper should explicitly compare to k-means/Lloyd’s heuristic (and cite Kumar & Kannan, Balakrishnan et al.) to clarify when EM and Lloyd’s are equivalent versus different.
