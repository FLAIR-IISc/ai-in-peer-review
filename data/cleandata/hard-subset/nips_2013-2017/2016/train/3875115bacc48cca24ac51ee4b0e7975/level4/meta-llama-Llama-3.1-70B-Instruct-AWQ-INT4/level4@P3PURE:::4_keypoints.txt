- Paper studies local maxima structure of log-likelihood for GMMs with equal weights and identity covariance.  
- Result 1: For k=3, d=1, there exist local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Result 2: With a specific mean configuration, random initialization has exponentially small probability (in k) of avoiding bad local maxima.  
- Result 3: Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Main proofs (especially Theorem 1) appear sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable based on Theorem 1.  
- Novelty: Original and insightful connection between mean configuration and local maxima, with a clever hierarchical-grouping idea for k>3.  
- Impact: Results could influence theory (e.g., clustering/k-means analyses) and practice; suggests importance of seeding enough points per true cluster.  
- Open/question: Worth investigating whether Lemma 7 extends to d>1.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-presented with clear problem setup, intuition, and explanations.
