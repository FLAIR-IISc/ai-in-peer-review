- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known number of components, weights, and covariances, and is theoretical in nature.  
- Presents three negative results: existence of poor local maxima in the likelihood even for large samples.  
- Shows EM with random initialization converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Shows the gradient EM algorithm exhibits the same negative behavior.  
- Uses a simplified model (known uniform weights, identity covariances) to obtain rigorous proofs; reviewer agrees simplifications are reasonable given technicality.  
- Results are interesting and potentially useful for understanding EM in practice.  
- Paper is framed for general dimension d, but all proofs are for d=1; authors should discuss extension to higher dimensions or explain obstacles.  
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too concise and needs more detail to be convincing.  
- Specific issue with lines 288–306: justification for limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains is unclear and continuity alone doesn’t imply the stated convergences.  
- Suggest expanding the proof of Theorem 1, even if it requires shortening other discussions.  
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; provide more explanation for how Equation (5) is derived.
