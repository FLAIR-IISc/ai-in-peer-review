- Studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known components, weights, and covariances (theoretical focus).
- Establishes three negative results: local maxima can have likelihoods arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum even with large samples.
- Shows randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to critical points with arbitrarily worse likelihood.
- Shows the same negative convergence result holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- Simplifications (fixed K, uniform weights, identity covariances, focus on d=1) are reasonable for technical tractability and the results are valuable.
- Limitation: proofs are restricted to d = 1 despite framing for general d; authors should clarify whether and how results extend to higher dimensions and note additional challenges.
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too concise—limits and supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains and the use of continuity need more detailed justification.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is unclear; derivation of Equation (5) requires further explanation.
