- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known uniform weights and identity covariances, proving negative results about EM's behavior.  
- Theorem/result 1: likelihood can have local maxima with values arbitrarily below the global maximum, even for large samples.  
- Theorem/result 2: random initialization of EM converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihoods are arbitrarily below the global maximum.  
- Theorem/result 3: analogous negative convergence results hold for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- The model simplifications (known weights, uniform mixing, identity covariances, d=1) are reasonable given proof complexity and yield informative conclusions.  
- Major gap: authors claim generality to arbitrary dimension d, but proofs address only d=1—need clarification or discussion of challenges for d>1.  
- Primary concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too concise; extending limit computations to suprema over unbounded parameter sets requires rigorous justification beyond continuity.  
- Technical request: explicitly justify convergence of suprema when parameters (γ, R) diverge and clarify handling of parameter sets that depend on diverging quantities.  
- Clarity issue: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) needs intermediate steps or intuitive explanation.  
- Overall: results are valuable and relevant; addressing dimensionality claims and strengthening Theorem 1’s proof would substantially improve the paper.
