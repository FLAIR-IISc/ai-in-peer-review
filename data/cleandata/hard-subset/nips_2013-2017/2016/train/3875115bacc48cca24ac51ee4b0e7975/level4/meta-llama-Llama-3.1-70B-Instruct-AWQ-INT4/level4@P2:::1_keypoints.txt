- Paper studies convergence of EM and iterative heuristics for spherical Gaussian mixtures, showing they may not reach global maxima even with infinite samples.  
- Main result 1: EM can get stuck in local optima for a three-cluster mixture when two clusters are close and the third is very far.  
- Main result 2: Random initialization for EM succeeds with at most exp(-Ω(k)) probability for mixtures of k Gaussians.  
- Main result 3: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddles, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the primary obstacle.  
- Key counterexample: a 3-component mixture where one center ends up near the two close components and two centers near the distant cluster, producing a local maximum.  
- This failure is unsurprising when cluster separation >> √d, since the problem becomes k-means–like under that separation.  
- Similar bad instances and failure probabilities are known for Lloyd’s k-means; the recursive extension to k components is expected though technically involved.  
- Random-init failure for EM mirrors the same reasons and probabilities as for k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm.  
- Reviewer suggests adding a direct comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s and contrasting known convergence guarantees under sufficient separation.  
- Strengths: coherent set of results, technically sound, well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weaknesses: results and techniques are not particularly surprising or highly original.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (add k-means comparison and more contextual discussion).
