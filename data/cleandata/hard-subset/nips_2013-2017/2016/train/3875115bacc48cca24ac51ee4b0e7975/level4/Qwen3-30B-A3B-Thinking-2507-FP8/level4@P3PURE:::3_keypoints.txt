- Problem & setting: Studies local modes in the log-likelihood of isotropic Gaussian mixture models with known scale and a well-specified model.  
- Main theoretical claim: Local modes exist even with infinite data and can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Analysis approach: Focuses on specific configurations where true centers are partitioned into well-spaced clusters.  
- EM behavior result: Under those configurations, random initialization causes EM to miss the global optimum almost surely as the number of components k increases.  
- Writing: Paper is generally well-written but contains occasional typos.  

Primary concern 1:
- Practical relevance of Theorem 1: Theorem 1 requires a specific center configuration, but the paper does not quantify how likely such configurations are in practice or under natural priors on true centers.  
- Theorem 2 claim gap: Lines L210–L218 claim Theorem 2 addresses prevalence, but it does not clearly state the probability of encountering "bad" center configurations under reasonable assumptions (as k or d grow).

Primary concern 2:
- Relevance of random initialization: Theorem 2 analyzes random-sample initialization, but in clearly clustered data (e.g., two close clusters of 2N points and one separated cluster of N points) it is implausible a practitioner would initialize poorly; the paper needs to justify why random initialization is a realistic model in such settings.

Additional concerns:
- L74–L81: The example does not convincingly support the Sresbro conjecture for k=2 and its purpose is unclear.  
- L233–L235: Requirement (1) is unjustified — the claim that initializing a center between two clusters prevents guaranteed movement toward the correct cluster needs explanation.  
- L267–L273: The result that EM avoids saddle points is stated but its practical significance is unexplained; comparison to converging to local optima is missing.
