- Paper proves EM and related heuristics can get trapped in local optima even with three widely separated clusters and infinite data.
- For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, so failures are typically due to suboptimal local maxima rather than saddles.
- Main counterexample: a three-component mixture with two close components and one far component yields a local maximum with centers misallocated.
- Behavior of the counterexample is expected when inter-center separation ≫ sqrt(d), where mixtures behave like k-means.
- Similar bad instances exist for Lloyd’s k-means algorithm; the EM failure mirrors k-means failure modes.
- Extension to k components is achieved via a clean recursive construction, which is technically involved but conceptually expected.
- Random initialization failure for EM parallels k-means, motivating strategies like choosing k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.
- Results form a coherent narrative showing EM need not converge to global maxima even in infinite-sample regimes.
- Contributions are not particularly surprising; techniques follow anticipated lines.
- Reviewer suggests adding a direct comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s (and Kumar–Kannan guarantees) to highlight contrasts when separation is large versus settings like Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu where EM and Lloyd’s differ.
