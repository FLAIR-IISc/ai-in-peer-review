- Paper studies EM for mean estimation in a Gaussian mixture with known components, uniform weights, and identity covariances, proving three negative results about EM's behavior.  
- Result 1: Likelihood can have local maxima with values arbitrarily close to zero, even for large sample sizes.  
- Result 2: EM with random initialization converges with high probability to a critical point whose likelihood is arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.  
- Result 3: Gradient EM exhibits the same deficiency as standard EM.  
- The problem is compelling and the model restrictions are justified given the technical difficulty and insightfulness of the results.  
- Concern: Authors claim generality in dimension d but core proofs are restricted to d = 1; need explicit discussion or extension to d > 1 and the challenges involved.  
- Major technical concern: Theorem 1’s proof (lines 288–306) is too concise—passing limits through suprema over unbounded, parameter-dependent sets is not justified by continuity alone and requires a more rigorous argument.  
- Gradient EM analysis (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are insufficiently explained; authors should clarify how (5) follows from the setup, the gradient’s role, and the update rule.  
- Overall judgment: Valuable, theoretically sound, practically relevant contribution; minor clarifications on dimensionality and proofs required; recommend acceptance.
