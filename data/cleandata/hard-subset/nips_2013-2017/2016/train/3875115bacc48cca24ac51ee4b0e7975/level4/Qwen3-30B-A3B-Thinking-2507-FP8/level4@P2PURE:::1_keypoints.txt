- Paper studies convergence of EM for spherical Gaussian mixtures and proves three negative results about failure to reach global maxima.
- Result 1: Even with three well-separated clusters and infinite samples, EM can converge to local optima.
- Result 2: For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization for EM succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).
- Result 3: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying problematic stationary points are poor local maxima rather than saddles.
- Core counterexample: a three-component mixture with two nearby clusters and one distant cluster yields a local maximum with one center near the close pair and two near the distant cluster.
- The counterexample is predictable under separation ≳√d, where spherical Gaussian mixtures behave like k-means; similar bad examples exist for Lloyd’s algorithm.
- Extension to k components via a recursive construction is technically sound but follows expected patterns.
- The random-initialization failure mirrors k-means results and motivates alternative initializations (e.g., using k·log k centers or distance-squared sampling).
- Overall assessment: results form a coherent narrative documenting EM’s non-convergence but largely lack significant novelty beyond established intuition for well-separated mixtures.
- Suggested improvement: include a comparative discussion with Kumar–Kannan and clarify the relationship between EM and Lloyd’s heuristic across separation thresholds (noting Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu’s distinction when separation is small).
