- Provides a definitive negative answer to Srebro (2007) by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing local maxima exist in the infinite-sample likelihood for Gaussian mixture estimation.

- Outlines standard maximization algorithms (EM and gradient EM) and common random initialization strategy.

- Proves main theorems showing EM and gradient EM converge to sub-optimal local maxima with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.

- Includes an instructive detailed proof for the simple case k=3, d=1 to build intuition.

- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, balancing formal theory and intuition within page limits.

- Results are significant for non-convex optimization theory, demonstrating persistence of optimization issues even in the asymptotic limit for simple models.

- Reviewer recommends strengthening the analysis with explicit estimates for constants in the probability bounds or supporting simulations to clarify practical regimes/phase transitions.

- Suggestion considered minor and does not detract from the paper’s novelty or impact.
