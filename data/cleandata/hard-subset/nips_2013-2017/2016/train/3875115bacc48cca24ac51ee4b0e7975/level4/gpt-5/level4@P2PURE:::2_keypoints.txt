- Paper studies EM for estimating component means in Gaussian mixtures with known number of components, weights, and covariances.  
- Proves three negative results: existence of local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum even for large samples.  
- Proves that with random initialization EM converges w.h.p. to critical points with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.  
- Proves the same negative convergence result for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Reviewer finds the question important and the simplifications (known uniform weights, identity covariances) acceptable given technical proofs.  
- Proofs are presented only for dimension d = 1; reviewer asks authors to clarify whether extensions to arbitrary d are possible or explain obstacles for d > 1.  
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too brief; limits involving suprema/maxima over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains (lines 288–306) need more justification.  
- Continuity of the likelihood alone does not suffice to justify the claimed convergence of maxima; more detailed arguments are requested even if other material is shortened.  
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and needs clearer explanation, especially derivation of Equation (5).
