- Paper studies population log-likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 equally weighted spherical components and known variance, giving two main results about bad local maxima and algorithmic convergence failures.

- First main result: constructs well-separated center configurations where the population log-likelihood has suboptimal local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, refuting Srebro (2007) for M ≥ 3.

- Second main result: shows EM and first-order (gradient) EM with random initialization converge to such bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).

- Additional algorithmic finding: first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, indicating failures are due to true bad local maxima rather than saddles.

- Strength: resolves an open question under benign assumptions (equal weights, isotropic covariances, infinite data).

- Strength: provides clear structural intuition and rigorous proofs that bad local maxima can be arbitrarily suboptimal for M ≥ 3.

- Strength: establishes exponentially high probability of EM/first-order EM failing under random initialization, highlighting limits of naive restarts.

- Strength: clarifies that first-order EM avoids strict saddles, focusing attention on local maxima as the failure mode.

- Main concern 1: The prevalence of “bad” center configurations is unclear — need quantification of how likely such configurations are under plausible generative models for centers.

- Main concern 2: Practicality of pure random initialization is questioned; common practical initializations (e.g., k-means++, preclustering) may avoid the reported failures and should be compared or discussed.

- Clarification request: role and intended purpose of the M = 2 example (L74–81) is unclear since it does not prove or disprove the conjecture for M = 2.

- Clarification request: in L233–235, why is condition (1) for “favorable” initialization necessary in addition to condition (2)? Provide intuition or examples.

- Clarification request: contextualize the practical importance of first-order EM avoiding strict saddles (L267–273) versus converging to suboptimal local maxima.

- Pro for acceptance: resolves a well-known open question with clean counterexamples and rigorous analysis.

- Pro for acceptance: provides sharp algorithmic consequences showing exponentially small success probability under random initialization.

- Pro for acceptance: introduces techniques likely applicable to other nonconvex likelihood landscapes.

- Con for acceptance: practical implications rely on adversarial center configurations; prevalence under reasonable generative assumptions is not quantified.

- Con for acceptance: the random initialization baseline may understate practitioner methods (e.g., k-means++), and alternatives are not assessed.

- Minor editorial suggestions: wording and phrasing fixes throughout (examples: L18, L38, L60, L74–81, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308).

- Overall assessment: well-written paper with significant theoretical contributions; addressing practical prevalence of bad configurations and comparisons to realistic initialization schemes would strengthen impact.
