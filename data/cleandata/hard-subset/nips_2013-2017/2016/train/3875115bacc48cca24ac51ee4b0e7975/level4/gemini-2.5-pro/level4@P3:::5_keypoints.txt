- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) open question by proving existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood of GMMs.  
- Construction applies even to equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated components and holds for any M ≥ 3.  
- Shows EM and a first-order EM variant, with practical random initialization, converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability → 1 exponentially fast in M.  
- For the first-order EM variant, failures are due to convergence to bad local maxima rather than getting stuck at strict saddle points.  
- Contribution is significant and original, overturning intuition that population likelihood landscapes for GMMs are benign.  
- Theoretical results are strong, rigorous, and include an elegant/geometric M=3 intuition extended by a recursive construction for general M.  
- Analysis of EM convergence probability is novel and establishes an exponential failure rate.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and balances formal statements with intuitive explanations.  
- Main weakness: algorithmic results are asymptotic in M, limiting immediate practical relevance when M is moderate (e.g., <20).  
- Suggestion: provide estimates (even rough) for the universal constant c in the bound 1 − e^(−cM) to gauge when failure becomes likely.  
- Suggestion: supplement theory with simulations for the constructed GMM showing failure probability vs. M and any phase transition behavior.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, impactful, and well-presented work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
