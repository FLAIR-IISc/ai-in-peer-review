- Paper proves EM and similar iterative MLE heuristics can get trapped in local optima for mixtures of spherical Gaussians, even with well-separated components and infinite data.
- Main technical results: (1) local optima exist under strong separation, (2) random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)), (3) first-order EM avoids strict saddle points so poor local maxima drive failures.
- Primary illustrative example: a 3-component mixture with two close components and one distant component yields an intuitive local maximum where centers misalign.
- The 3-component construction is extended to k components via a recursive construction, which is technically involved but conceptually straightforward.
- Under very strong separation (≫ √d), learning resembles k-means and EM behaves similarly to Lloyd’s algorithm due to negligible Gaussian overlap.
- The random-initialization failure rate for EM mirrors known failure probabilities for k-means/Lloyd’s, highlighting a close connection.
- Techniques used are largely standard and the phenomena shown are somewhat expected given prior k-means pathology.
- The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs, but the novelty and surprise value are limited.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, though some proof sections could be simplified.
- Originality: correct application to EM, but core ideas are not groundbreaking.
- Significance: provides theoretical justification for practical EM difficulties but does not open major new research directions.
- Suggestion: explicitly compare and contrast results with Kumar & Kannan and Lloyd’s heuristic analyses to strengthen the paper.
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical analysis, valuable insights into non-convex optimization challenges, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: unsurprising results building on established ideas, incomplete connection to k-means literature, and limited practical impact.
