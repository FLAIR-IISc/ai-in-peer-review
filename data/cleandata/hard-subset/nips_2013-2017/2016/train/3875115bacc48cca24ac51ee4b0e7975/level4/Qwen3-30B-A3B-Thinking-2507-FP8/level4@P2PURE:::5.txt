

The paper provides a definitive answer to Srebro's 2007 open question—"Are there local maxima in the infinite-sample likelihood of Gaussian mixture estimation?"—by constructing a general class of counterexamples that establish the negative. The authors begin by outlining the schemes for maximizing likelihood in Gaussian Mixture Models, including the EM and gradient EM algorithms, and address initialization, noting a standard choice. They then present their primary theorems, which constitute the counterexample to Srebro's question [2007]. Furthermore, they demonstrate that even with standard random initialization, both EM and gradient EM converge to non-optimal maxima with exponentially high probability. To build intuition, the authors prove a simplified case (k=3, d=1). The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, effectively balancing space constraints with the need to convey intuition for their theoretical results. The question resolved by the authors is of significant importance for understanding non-convex optimization challenges, even in the asymptotic limit for simple distributions. While I did not verify the supplementary material in detail, providing estimates of the constants in the probability term would be valuable. Such estimates would clarify the phase transition point where convergence to suboptimal solutions becomes observable in the asymptotic limit (i.e., for which k values does this phenomenon emerge?). If the constants prove intractable or unintuitive, supporting simulations would be beneficial to empirically confirm the phenomenon. Nevertheless, this minor point does not diminish the paper's novelty or impact.
