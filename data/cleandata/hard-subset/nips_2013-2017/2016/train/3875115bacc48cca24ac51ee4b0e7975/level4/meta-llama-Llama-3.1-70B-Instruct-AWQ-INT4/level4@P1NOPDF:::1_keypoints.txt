- Paper analyzes EM convergence for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and shows systematic non-convergence to global maxima.  
- Main result 1: Even with infinite samples and well-separated clusters (3 components), EM can get stuck at local optima.  
- Main result 2: For k-component mixtures, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Main result 3: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddles, so failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.  
- The paper provides a coherent set of results demonstrating EM’s failure even in the infinite-sample setting.  
- Reviewer: results are largely intuitive and the techniques follow expected lines.  
- Key counterexample: three-component mixture with two close components and one far away, yielding a local maximum with centers misallocated.  
- Behavior is expected when center separation ≫ √d, making the problem k-means–like.  
- Construction extends to k components via a recursive method, which is technically involved but unsurprising.  
- Failure of random initialization mirrors k-means failures, explaining the exp(-Ω(k)) probability.  
- Suggestion: compare to k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm results (e.g., Kumar–Kannan) and note when EM and Lloyd’s are equivalent under large separation versus different behavior under weak separation (Balakrishnan–Wainwright–Yu).
