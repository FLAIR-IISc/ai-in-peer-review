- Paper proves three negative theoretical results about EM for Gaussian mixtures: existence of local maxima with arbitrarily lower likelihood than the global maximum.
- Second result: random initialization causes EM to converge with high probability to critical points with likelihoods arbitrarily below the global maximum.
- Third result: analogous negative behavior holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- Analysis assumes known number of components, uniform mixture weights, and identity covariance matrices to enable rigorous proofs.
- The research question is important and the simplifications are reasonable; the results are interesting and shed light on EM’s practical behavior.
- Proofs are presented only for the one-dimensional case (d = 1); authors should comment on extendability to higher dimensions or discuss extra difficulties for d > 1.
- Main concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is overly concise and needs expansion, especially steps involving limits of suprema as parameters → ∞.
- The use of continuity to justify convergences in Theorem 1 is insufficient; more detailed arguments are required to be convincing.
- Reviewer did not check the supplementary material, so any relied-upon details there should be clearly referenced or included.
- Minor concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow and the derivation of Equation (5) should be explained more clearly.
