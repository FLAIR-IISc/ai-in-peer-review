- Paper studies EM convergence for spherical Gaussian mixtures and proves EM can get stuck in local optima even with infinite samples.  
- Shows random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)), and gradient-based EM avoids strict saddles, making bad local maxima the main issue.  
- Results are technically sound and the paper delivers a coherent, consistent narrative.  
- Findings feel unsurprising: core counterexample (two close components, one distant) intuitively explains local maxima.  
- When cluster separation >> √d, mixture learning resembles k-means, so poor EM behavior mirrors known k-means failure modes.  
- Recursive extension to k components is well-executed but expected given existing intuition.  
- Random-init failure probability parallels Lloyd’s algorithm; known k-means remedies (k log k centers, distance-squared sampling) may transfer.  
- Overall contribution feels incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Major suggestion: provide a deeper, detailed comparison with k-means/Lloyd’s, clarifying when and why EM diverges from Lloyd’s performance.  
- Suggestion: elaborate on connections to Kumar and Kannan’s guarantees and pinpoint where and why EM violates those guarantees.
