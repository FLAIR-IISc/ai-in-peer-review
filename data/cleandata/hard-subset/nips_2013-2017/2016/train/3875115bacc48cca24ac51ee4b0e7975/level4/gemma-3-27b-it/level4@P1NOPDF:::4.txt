This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function for Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance matrices. It presents three related results in this area: 1) The authors provide a counterexample demonstrating that, for certain configurations (k=3, d=1), local maxima can exist with arbitrarily worse likelihoods than the global maximum. 2) They show that, with random initialization, the probability of converging to a poor local maximum can be exponentially small in *k*, but only when the means of the Gaussians are configured in a specific way. 3) They also prove that gradient Expectation-Maximization (EM) almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood. 

**Summary:** This paper investigates the structure of local maxima in the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance matrices. It presents three related findings: a counterexample of arbitrarily bad local maxima, an analysis of convergence probabilities with random initialization, and a proof regarding convergence to saddle points.

**Technical Quality:** I focused my review on the proofs presented in the main paper, specifically for Theorem 1, and found them to be sound. Based on the analysis of Theorem 1, the proof idea for Theorem 2 also appears reasonable, though I did not verify the details in the Appendix. Importantly, each theorem is clearly and accurately interpreted following its statement.

**Novelty & Originality:** The paper establishes a connection between the mean configuration of a GMM and the local maxima of its log-likelihood function. While this observation is intuitively appealing, I believe it is genuinely original. Furthermore, the key idea behind Theorem 2 – building upon Theorem 1 to demonstrate that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means dictates the local maxima for *k* > 3 – is particularly clever.

**Potential Impact & Usefulness:** The results presented here could have a significant impact on both theoretical and practical research. Theoretically, it may stimulate further investigation into the local optima structure of related clustering tasks, such as *k*-means. It would also be interesting to explore whether the result in Lemma 7 generalizes to dimensions *d* > 1. Practically, the work highlights the importance of initializing with sufficient points within each true cluster, potentially offering insights for the design of new algorithms. 

**Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presents its results. The authors effectively explain the intuition behind their analysis, providing a clear introduction to the problem setup and offering insightful interpretations of their findings.
