- Paper analyzes population log-likelihood of equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs and derives three main algorithmic results.
- Result 1: Constructs a 1D, three-component, well-separated spherical GMM exhibiting suboptimal local maxima, with arbitrarily large likelihood gap to the global optimum.
- Result 2: Shows EM and gradient EM with natural random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)} for constructed mixtures, implying exponentially many restarts needed.
- Result 3: Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, attributing failures to poor local maxima rather than saddles.
- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture negatively and complements prior local-convergence and spectral/moment methods.
- Leverages recent results on gradient dynamics and saddle-avoidance to support theoretical claims.
- Theorem 1 proof is sound and well-explained; Theorem 2’s high-level strategy is plausible though some appendix details were not fully checked.
- Theorem 4 argument is consistent with existing gradient-dynamics literature and clearly stated.
- Assumptions and limitations (population setting, equal weights, identity covariance, well-separated means, specific initialization) are explicitly stated and correctly interpreted.
- Paper is well written, logically structured, and provides strong geometric intuition; bibliography formatting needs cleanup.
- Original and substantial contribution: disproves a widely held conjecture and introduces an elegant hierarchical construction driving exponential failure probability.
- Significant implications: even benign GMM settings can be treacherous for likelihood-based methods, motivating careful initialization and alternative algorithms.
- Strengths: resolves open question, strong algorithmic consequences, clarifies maxima vs saddle role, clear exposition.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: limited to population/equal-weight/identity-covariance setting, 1D counterexample lacks explicit higher-d extension, more discussion of practical initialization and finite-sample implications needed.
- Technical clarifications requested: precise step-size conditions for first-order EM and whether analogous non-convergence-to-saddles holds for standard EM.
- Acceptance arguments: notable open-problem resolution, rigorous results with practical implications, high likelihood of influencing follow-up work.
- Rejection arguments: idealized assumptions and specific constructions limit generality; some central proofs deferred and higher-dimensional extensions underdeveloped.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper, reviewer leans toward acceptance subject to minor revisions on clarity and scope.
