- Counterexample: for k = 3 and d = 1 the paper constructs local maxima with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum.  
- Random initialization: for special mean configurations the probability of avoiding bad local maxima can be exponentially small in k.  
- Gradient EM: the paper shows gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points of the log-likelihood.  
- Technical quality: Theorem 1’s proofs appear sound, Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable though appendix details were not fully checked, and theorem interpretations are clear.  
- Novelty: Establishes a novel connection between mean configuration and local maxima structure; the hierarchical grouping idea for k > 3 is particularly clever.  
- Potential impact: Results could influence theory and practice (e.g., clustering/k-means), motivate seeding strategies, and prompt extensions like checking Lemma 7 for d > 1.  
- Clarity: The paper presents results and intuition very clearly, making the analysis easy to follow.
