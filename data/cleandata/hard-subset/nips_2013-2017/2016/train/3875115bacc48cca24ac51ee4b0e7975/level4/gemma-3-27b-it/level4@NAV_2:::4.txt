This paper studies the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It presents three related results: 1) a counterexample demonstrating the existence of local maxima (for M=3 and d=1) with arbitrarily worse likelihood than the global maximum; 2) a result showing that, with random initialization, the probability of *not* converging to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in M under a specific configuration of Gaussian means; and 3) a demonstration that first-order gradient EM does not converge to a strict saddle point of the log-likelihood almost surely.

The paper’s technical quality appears high. I verified the proofs presented in the main body (specifically for Theorem 1) and found them to be sound. Based on the analysis outlined for Theorem 1, the proposed proof strategy for Theorem 2 also seems plausible, though I did not examine the details in the Appendix. The theorems are clearly stated and well-interpreted following their presentation.

The work exhibits strong originality. Connecting the mean configuration of a GMM to the structure of local maxima in its log-likelihood is an insightful and novel observation. The core idea behind Theorem 2 – leveraging the hierarchical grouping structure of true means to understand the landscape of local maxima for M > 3 – is particularly clever. This builds upon Srebro’s conjecture and definitively resolves it with a construction that is insightful.

The results have the potential for significant impact in both theory and practice. Theoretically, this work could motivate further investigation into the local optima structure of related clustering tasks, such as k-means. It would be interesting to explore whether the result in Lemma 7 extends to higher dimensions (d > 1). Practically, the findings underscore the importance of appropriate initialization strategies – specifically, ensuring sufficient seeding of points within each true cluster – and may provide guidance for designing improved algorithms.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presents its results. The problem setup is introduced effectively, and the intuition behind the analysis is explained thoroughly. The authors provide clear interpretations of their analyses and findings. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical contribution:** The paper resolves an open question in the field and provides a rigorous analysis of the local optima landscape of GMM likelihoods.
*   **Originality and Insight:** The connection between GMM mean configurations and local maxima is novel and insightful.
*   **Potential impact:** The results could influence both theoretical research in clustering and the design of practical GMM estimation algorithms.
*   **Clear and well-structured presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Sound technical quality:** The proofs presented in the main body are correct, and the outlined approach for other theorems seems reasonable.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   None significant. The focus on a specific GMM configuration (equal weights, identity covariance) is a limitation, but the core insights are likely to generalize and the paper clearly states this scope.



Overall, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. It presents a significant theoretical advance with potential practical implications, and it is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.
