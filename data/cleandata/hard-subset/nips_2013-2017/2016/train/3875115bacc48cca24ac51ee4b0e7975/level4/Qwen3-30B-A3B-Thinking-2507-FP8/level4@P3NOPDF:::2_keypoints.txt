- Paper gives a rigorous analysis of EM for Gaussian mixtures under known K, uniform weights, and identity covariances.  
- Main negative results: likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, even with large samples.  
- Random initialization of EM converges with high probability to such suboptimal critical points.  
- The same negative convergence behavior holds for the gradient EM variant.  
- Simplifying assumptions (uniform weights, identity covariances) are justified to isolate core EM convergence issues.  
- Concern: claim for general dimension d is unclear because Theorem 1’s proof is given only for d = 1; authors should clarify extension to d > 1 or discuss additional hurdles.  
- Major technical gap in Theorem 1 proof: unjustified interchange of limits and suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains needs rigorous justification (e.g., uniform convergence or dominated convergence).  
- Gradient EM analysis is under-explained: derivation of Eq. (5) and the gradient computation steps should be made explicit.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and informative contribution; minor revisions to clarify proofs and derivations would make it suitable for acceptance.
