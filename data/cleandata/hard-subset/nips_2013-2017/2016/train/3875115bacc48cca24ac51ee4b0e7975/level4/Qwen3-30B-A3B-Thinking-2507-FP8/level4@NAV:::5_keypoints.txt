- The paper answers Srebro (2007) negatively by constructing a general class of counterexamples showing local maxima in infinite-sample Gaussian mixture likelihoods.  
- Authors present and analyze optimization schemes for GMMs, including EM and gradient EM, and discuss common initialization strategies.  
- Core theorems constructively refute Srebro’s open question.  
- They prove that with standard random initialization, EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal maxima with exponentially high probability.  
- An intuitive, simplified example (k=3, d=1) illustrates the phenomenon.  
- The manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-structured, balancing theory and intuition.  
- The result is important for understanding non-convex optimization even in asymptotic settings for simple distributions.  
- Supplementary material was not fully checked; quantitative estimates of constants in the probability bounds are requested.  
- If constants are large or unclear, supporting simulations should be provided to validate the phenomenon.  
- These suggestions are intended for clarification and do not reduce the paper’s novelty or impact.
