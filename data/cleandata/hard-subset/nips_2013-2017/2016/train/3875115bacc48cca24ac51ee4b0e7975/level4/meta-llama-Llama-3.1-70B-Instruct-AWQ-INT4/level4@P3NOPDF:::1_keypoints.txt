- Main result: EM can get stuck in local optima even with infinite samples for a 3-component spherical Gaussian mixture when one component is very far from the other two.  
- Main result: For mixtures of k Gaussians, random initialization succeeds with probability at most exp(-Ω(k)).  
- Main result: Gradient EM generically avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima (not saddles) are the primary failure mode.  
- Reviewer view: The results form a coherent story showing EM need not reach global maxima even with infinite data.  
- Reviewer view: The findings are unsurprising and follow expected proof techniques.  
- Construction detail: The key counterexample uses two close components and one distant component, yielding a local maximum with centers clustered around the distant component—behavior akin to k-means when separations ≫ √d.  
- Relation to prior work: Similar bad instances exist for Lloyd’s k-means; the authors’ recursive extension to k components is natural though technically nontrivial.  
- Initialization comment: The poor success probability for random initialization mirrors k-means failures and motivates using ~k log k centers or distance-squared (k-means++) sampling.
