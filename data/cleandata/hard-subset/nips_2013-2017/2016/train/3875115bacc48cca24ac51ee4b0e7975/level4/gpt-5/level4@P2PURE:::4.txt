This paper investigates the structure of local maxima of the log-likelihood for Gaussian Mixture Models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance. It presents three related results:
1. A counterexample (for k = 3 and d = 1) showing that local maxima can have likelihood arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
2. With random initialization, under a particular configuration of the component means, the probability of avoiding convergence to a bad local maximum can be exponentially small in k.
3. Gradient EM almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.

Technical quality
I checked the proofs in the main paper (Theorem 1) and find them sound. Based on the analysis provided for Theorem 1, the proof idea for Theorem 2 also appears reasonable, although I did not verify the appendix details. All three theorems are clearly explained and interpreted following their statements.

Novelty & Originality
The paper connects the configuration of the GMM means to the local maxima of its log-likelihood. Although intuitive, this insight is genuinely original. The key idea in Theorem 2, building on Theorem 1—that the hierarchical grouping structure of the true means determines the local maxima for k > 3—is particularly clever.

Potential impact & Usefulness
The results could influence both theory and practice. Theoretically, they may spur investigation into the local optima structure of related clustering problems, such as k-means, and it would be interesting to test whether the result in Lemma 7 extends to d > 1. Practically, the work highlights the importance of seeding enough points in each true cluster and may inspire new algorithmic designs.

Clarity and presentation
The paper does an excellent job presenting the results and explaining the underlying intuition. It clearly introduces the problem setup and provides intuitive explanations and interpretations for all analyses.
