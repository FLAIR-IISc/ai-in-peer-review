- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a GMM with known/fixed component count, weights, and covariances, proving three negative theoretical results.
- Main theoretical findings: (1) likelihood can have local maxima with values arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum even for large samples.
- Main theoretical findings: (2) random initialization causes EM to converge with high probability to critical points with likelihoods arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum.
- Main theoretical findings: (3) the same negative convergence result holds for the gradient EM algorithm.
- The problem is relevant and significant; the authors’ simplifying assumptions (uniform weights, identity covariances, known K) are justified for tractability.
- Clarification requested: although presented for general dimension d, formal proofs are for d=1 — the authors should comment on extending proofs to arbitrary d or discuss added difficulties.
- Primary concern: proof of Theorem 1 is overly condensed and should be expanded (esp. lines 288–306) to justify limits of supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.
- Technical gap noted: continuity of the likelihood alone does not justify the asserted convergence results; more detailed derivations are needed to substantiate the limits and maxima arguments.
- Reviewer did not check supplementary-material proofs, so completeness of supplemental arguments is unknown.
- Minor concern: exposition of the Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is unclear; the derivation of Equation (5) needs more explanation for reader comprehension.
