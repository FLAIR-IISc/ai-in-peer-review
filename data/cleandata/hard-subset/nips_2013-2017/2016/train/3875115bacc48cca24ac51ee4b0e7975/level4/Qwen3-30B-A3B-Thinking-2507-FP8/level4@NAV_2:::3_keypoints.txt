- Paper studies existence of poor local maxima in the population log-likelihood of isotropic, unit-variance, equally-weighted, well-separated GMMs with M ≥ 3 components.
- Main result 1: constructs examples where population likelihood has local maxima with log-likelihood arbitrarily worse than global optima, resolving Srebro (2007)’s open question.
- Main result 2: shows random initialization (sampling centers from the data) leads EM and first-order EM to converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − exp(−Ω(M)), implying bad local maxima—not saddles—cause poor performance.
- Technical contribution is significant and novel; proofs are sound and the presentation is generally well-structured.
- Theoretical significance: demonstrates fundamental limitations of local search methods for GMMs even under favorable, idealized settings.
- Practical relevance concern 1: Theorem 1 relies on specific center configurations (e.g., clustered centers); the paper does not quantify how likely such “bad” configurations are under natural center distributions or how probability scales with M or dimension.
- Practical relevance concern 2: The random-initialization model (centers drawn uniformly from data) may be unrealistic when data shows clear clustering and practitioners use informed initialization; paper should clarify relevance in such cases.
- Specific technical/clarity issues: the k=2 discussion (L74–81) is unclear and its purpose is not convincing or well-motivated.
- Specific technical/clarity issues: requirement that all initialized centers fall in the neighborhood of exactly one cluster (L233–235) seems unnecessary and should be reconsidered.
- Specific technical/clarity issues: the practical importance of the EM-avoids-strict-saddles result (L267–273) versus convergence to local maxima should be better explained.
- Many minor editorial and wording errors throughout the manuscript (examples at L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L218, L220, L225, L245, L249, L308) that should be corrected for clarity.
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and suitable for NIPS due to resolving an open theoretical question, but practical impact would be strengthened by addressing the likelihood of problematic configurations and the realism of the initialization model.
