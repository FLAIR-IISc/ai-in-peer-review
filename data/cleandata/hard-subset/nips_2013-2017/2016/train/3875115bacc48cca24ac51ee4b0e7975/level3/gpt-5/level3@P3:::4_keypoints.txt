- Summary: Proves population GMM log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3 under equal weights and identity covariances, and shows EM/gradient EM with random initialization often converges to these suboptimal points.  
- Result 1 (existence): Constructs well-separated means (M=3, d=1 and generalizable) exhibiting a suboptimal local maximum with likelihood arbitrarily below the global optimum.  
- Result 2 (random-init failure): Shows random i.i.d. initialization has success probability e−Ω(M) for a hierarchical cluster construction, so EM/gradient EM need exponentially many restarts to succeed with constant probability.  
- Result 3 (saddles): Proves gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are due to bad local maxima, not saddles.  
- Relation to prior work: Complements results showing local convergence with good initialization and spectral/moment-based algorithms, and resolves Srebro’s 2007 conjecture in this setting.  
- Theorem quality: Theorem 1 is technically sound and persuasive; Theorem 2’s hierarchical/probability argument is compelling though some appendix details were not fully checked.  
- Theorem 3/4 quality: Theorem 3 follows naturally; Theorem 4’s avoidance of strong smoothness assumptions is plausible and well-motivated.  
- Clarity: Setup, geometric intuition for M=3, and recursive construction are clearly presented and well explained.  
- Originality: The hierarchical grouping linking mean configuration to bad local maxima and the recursive e−Ω(M) construction are novel and insightful.  
- Significance: Definitively closes a long-standing conjecture, with concrete algorithmic implications that naive random seeding can be provably unreliable.  
- Scope weakness: Results assume equal weights and identity covariance; extensions to unequal weights or non-identity covariances are not shown.  
- Dimensionality weakness: Core bad-local-max construction is 1D; extensions/implications for d>1 need clearer justification.  
- Quantitative weakness: “Well-separated” is stated qualitatively; explicit separation constants and trade-offs are lacking.  
- Practical guidance weakness: Paper offers limited actionable alternatives to random initialization or diagnostics for trapped configurations.  
- Appendix/verification weakness: Some technical details (especially in Theorem 2) are dense and not fully verified by reviewer; a concise probability lemma would help.  
- Suggestions: Clarify extension of no-strict-saddle result to standard EM, adapt construction to weight/covariance variations, provide finite-sample translation and sample-size regimes, and include small numerical illustrations.  
- Pros: Resolves important open question, clever hierarchical construction, rigorous proofs, and clear separation of local-maxima vs saddle issues.  
- Cons: Limited to equal-weight/identity-covariance setting, core 1D example, dense appendices, and limited practical recommendations.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Confidence: High on assessment of Theorems 1 and 4 and overall interpretation; moderate on full technical details of Theorem 2.
