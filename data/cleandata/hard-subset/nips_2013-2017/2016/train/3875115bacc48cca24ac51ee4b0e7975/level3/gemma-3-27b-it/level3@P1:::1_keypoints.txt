- Studies EM convergence for Gaussian mixture models, focusing on the problem of local optima.  
- Shows EM can converge to suboptimal solutions even for uniformly weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Resolves an open question of Srebro (2007) by providing a counterexample.  
- Constructs a specific 3-component GMM (two close components, one well-separated) with bad local maxima in the population likelihood.  
- Proves random initialization leads EM to suboptimal critical points with an exponential dependence on the number of components M.  
- Shows the poor performance arises from bad local maxima rather than saddle points; results extend to first-order EM.  
- Situates the work in the broader literature on identifiability, convergence rates, clustering algorithms, and method-of-moments approaches.  
- Strength: rigorous mathematical analysis and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Strength: disentangles effects of local maxima versus saddle points.  
- Strength/concern: exponential dependence on M is a strong and notable finding for high-dimensional mixture modeling.  
- Weakness: construction and intuition are relatively straightforward and echo known k-means phenomena.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to k-means theoretical results (e.g., Kumar & Kannan) and discussion of methodological links.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implications and existing initialization/mitigation strategies beyond moment matching.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing comparisons to k-means and expanding practical implications/mitigation discussion.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that resolves a long-standing question, with requested revisions for greater impact.
