This paper makes significant contributions to our understanding of the structure of local maxima in the likelihood function of Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs). The authors present a thorough analysis of the population likelihood function, providing new insights into the behavior of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm and its variants.

The main results of the paper are summarized as follows: (1) the authors construct a counterexample showing that, even in the simplest case of a GMM with three components in one dimension, the population log-likelihood function can have local maxima that are arbitrarily worse than the global maximum; (2) they demonstrate that the probability of avoiding a bad local maximum using random initialization can be exponentially small in the number of components for certain mean configurations; and (3) they prove that the gradient EM algorithm almost surely does not converge to a saddle point of the log-likelihood.

The technical quality of the paper is high, with sound proofs presented in the main text. The proof idea for Theorem 2 seems reasonable, although the details in the appendix were not thoroughly checked. The novelty and originality of the paper lie in its relation of mean configuration to local maxima and the hierarchical grouping insight for k>3, which is both clever and original.

The potential impact and usefulness of this paper are significant. It could inspire theoretical work on clustering optima and practical seeding strategies. Furthermore, it suggests checking whether Lemma 7 extends to d>1, which could lead to new insights into the behavior of GMMs in higher dimensions.

The clarity and presentation of the paper are excellent, with well-explained results and intuition. The problem setup and interpretations are clear, making the paper accessible to a wide range of readers.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1. The paper presents a significant contribution to our understanding of the structure of local maxima in the likelihood function of GMMs.

3. The novelty and originality of the paper lie in its relation of mean configuration to local maxima and the hierarchical grouping insight for k>3.
4. The potential impact and usefulness of this paper are significant, with potential applications in theoretical work on clustering optima and practical seeding strategies.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

1. The paper focuses on a specific case of GMMs with equal mixing weights and identity covariance, which may limit its generalizability.
2. The proof details in the appendix were not thoroughly checked, which may raise concerns about the technical quality of the paper.

**Rating:** 8/10

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should be asked to provide additional details on the proof of Theorem 2 and to discuss the potential limitations of their results.
