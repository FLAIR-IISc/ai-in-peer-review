- Paper analyzes population log-likelihood of equally weighted isotropic GMMs (known variance) and: (i) disproves Srebro’s 2007 conjecture by constructing arbitrarily bad local maxima for any M ≥ 3; (ii) shows EM (and first-order EM) with random initialization fails with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M) on hierarchical instances; (iii) proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures come from bad local maxima.

- Connects to prior work on mixture identifiability, EM theory, and algorithmic alternatives, and sharpens finite-sample negative constructions with population-level results.

- Technical soundness: constructions and hierarchical instance are careful and convincing; strict-saddle avoidance aligns with recent dynamical analyses.

- Completeness: theory is complete for the stated initialization/model class, but practical impact depends on how typical the adversarial center configurations and initialization are.

- Clarity: generally clear and well organized, though several phrases and notational points need clarification.

- Originality: novel and important negative resolution of Srebro’s conjecture at the population level and the exponential failure probability claim.

- Significance: changes understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes and motivates robust initialization, but practical prevalence of bad instances is unclear.

- Major request 1 — practical prevalence: quantify probability of bad center configurations under natural priors (e.g., centers i.i.d. N(0, τ^2 I), uniform in [0,1]^d, or Poisson point processes), analyze multiscale cluster-tree events as M and d grow, and clarify scaling of well-separation thresholds with d.

- Major request 2 — initialization realism: test failure persistence under realistic seeding (k-means++, farthest-first, PCA-based, oversampling+prune) both in population and finite-sample settings; provide simulations for small M (e.g., M=3) to show if the “two-close-one-far” pattern yields the claimed failures.

- Major request 3 — M = 2 example: either provide a formal partial result ruling out bad local maxima under stated conditions for M=2 or explicitly mark the example as illustrative only.

- Major request 4 — clarify requirement (1) (L233–235): explain the intention behind “all initialized centers fall in the neighborhood of exactly one cluster,” and discuss dynamics for centers initialized between clusters and why they wouldn’t move to correct clusters under EM.

- Major request 5 — saddle vs maxima framing: reframe Theorem 4 as supportive evidence that bad local maxima (not saddles) drive failures, and discuss whether an analogous statement might hold for EM proper and what technical obstacles exist.

- Notation/phrasing clarifications: explicitly define “well-separated constant” (e.g., ξ = Ω(√d) with constant c), standardize use of Ω(k) versus Ω(log M), and make dimension-dependence explicit.

- Minor edits: a list of small wording/typo fixes (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L220, L245, L249, L308) as noted by the reviewer.

- Pros summary: resolves an open conjecture, provides strong and clear constructions with tight exponential bounds, and carefully distinguishes local maxima from strict saddles for first-order EM.

- Cons summary: prevalence of bad instances under natural priors is unquantified, initialization model may be unrealistic and lacks experiments for k-means++/oversampling or finite-sample EM, and some phrasing/typos cause minor confusion.

- Recommendation: important, well-executed theoretical paper; lean accept if authors address practical relevance by quantifying bad-configuration probabilities under reasonable priors and evaluating more realistic initialization schemes.
