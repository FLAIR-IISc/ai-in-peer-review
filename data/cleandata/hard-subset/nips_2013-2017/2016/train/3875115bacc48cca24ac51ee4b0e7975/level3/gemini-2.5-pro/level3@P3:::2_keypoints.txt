- Paper studies population log-likelihood of GMMs with known/fixed components, weights, and covariances, proving negative results about EM behavior.
- Main contribution: for M ≥ 3, the population likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, resolving Srebro (2007) negatively.
- Shows standard EM and first-order EM, under random initialization from the true mixture, converge to these bad critical points with probability approaching 1 exponentially in M.
- Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, indicating algorithmic failure stems from bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Strength: significant and original resolution of a longstanding conjecture, with surprising and impactful negative results about EM sensitivity to initialization.
- Strength: results are informative for practitioners and theorists, motivating advanced initialization methods even in idealized settings.
- Strength: simplifying assumptions (known M, uniform weights, identity covariances) are reasonable to isolate the core issue.
- Major weakness: proof of Theorem 1 is too brief and appears to make unjustified limit/suprema interchanges; needs a more detailed, rigorous presentation in the main text.
- Weakness: proofs are given only for d = 1, with no clear extension or sketch for higher dimensions despite claims of generality.
- Weakness: derivation/clarity of the population gradient EM update (Eq. 6) is terse and should include a brief derivation sketch to be more self-contained.
- Recommendation: high potential impact but acceptance should be conditional on a rigorous expansion of Theorem 1’s proof (move detailed proof into main paper).
