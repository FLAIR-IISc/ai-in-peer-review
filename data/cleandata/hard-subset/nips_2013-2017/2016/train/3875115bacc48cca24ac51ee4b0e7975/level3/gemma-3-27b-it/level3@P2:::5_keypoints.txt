- Resolves Srebro’s conjecture by providing a counterexample showing the population GMM likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Shows EM and first-order EM converge to these bad critical points with exponentially high probability under random initialization.  
- The work significantly advances understanding of non-convex optimization landscapes for GMMs and practical implications for EM initialization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and a helpful geometric illustration for M=3.  
- High originality: novel explicit demonstration of bad local maxima and analysis of EM’s behavior under natural initialization.  
- Theoretical results are rigorous, build on prior work, and are technically well-structured.  
- Important insight: failures of first-order methods are driven more by bad local maxima than by saddle points (Theorems 3 & 4).  
- Practical significance: highlights pitfalls of relying on local search methods even for well-separated Gaussians and large samples.  
- Strength: definitively answers a long-standing open problem in GMM literature.  
- Strength: provides quantifiable bounds on EM convergence behavior and clear exposition.  
- Weakness: probability bounds in Theorems 2 and 3 contain unspecified constants, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Weakness: lacks supporting simulations to demonstrate empirical convergence rates and practical impact for realistic M.  
- Argument for acceptance: fundamental problem, novel rigorous theory, clear exposition, and significant theoretical/practical impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: missing explicit constants and empirical validation reduce practical relevance (considered minor).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with suggestion to add simulations or estimates for the omitted constants in a future revision.
