- Proves existence of bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood for isotropic, known-scale GMMs, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Shows EM with random initialization is likely to converge to suboptimal points as the number of components grows.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and clear presentation as major strengths.
- Analyzes first-order EM and shows it’s unlikely to converge to saddle points, adding nuance to EM behavior.
- Practical relevance is questionable because the bad-center configurations used appear contrived and may be rare in real data.
- A stronger result would assume a distribution on true centers and show the probability of bad configurations grows with k or d.
- The random initialization scheme used feels unrealistic for many practical clustering scenarios where initialization is informed by data.
- The k=2 example (L74-81) intended to support Srebro’s conjecture is unconvincing.
- The necessity of requirement (1) (L233-235) is unclear and should be justified.
- The practical significance of distinguishing convergence to a saddle point versus a local optimum (L267-273) is debatable.
- There are minor phrasing issues and typos (e.g., “well-separated constant” L218, Ω(k) L225) that need fixing.
- Acceptance arguments: solid theoretical contribution, clear writing, addresses EM limitations.
- Rejection arguments: limited practical relevance, unrealistic initialization assumption, and minor clarity/technical issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, particularly adding discussion or results about the likelihood of bad configurations under a distribution over true centers.
