- Main result: For M ≥ 3 equally-weighted spherical Gaussians, the population log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima, even with well-separated components.
- Algorithmic consequence: EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization from the data distribution converges to suboptimal critical points with probability exponentially high in M; for first-order EM these are local maxima, not strict saddles.

- Strength — Significance: Resolves a long-standing conjecture and explains EM's strong sensitivity to initialization with a powerful negative result.
- Strength — Originality: Novel construction and proof techniques showing existence of bad local maxima and exponential failure probability for random initialization.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written and motivated; main results and implications are clearly communicated.

- Weakness — Technical soundness: Proof of Theorem 1 is only a high-level sketch relying on limit/sup operations over parameter-dependent domains without rigorous justification (may require uniform convergence).
- Weakness — Presentation of proof: Central theorem's proof is insufficiently detailed in the main text and should be expanded rather than deferred to appendices.
- Weakness — Generality: Theorems are stated for general dimension d but constructions/proofs appear specific to d=1; extension to d>1 is not demonstrated or discussed.
- Weakness — Derivation clarity: Derivation of the first-order EM update (Eq. 6) omits intermediate steps and should be made more self-contained.

- Pros for acceptance: Resolves an important open question; results are strong, surprising, and have significant theoretical/practical implications; paper is well-motivated and communicates main ideas clearly.
- Cons against acceptance: Main theorem's proof is currently incomplete/unconvincing; mismatch between claimed generality (all d) and presented one-dimensional analysis.

- Overall conclusion: Important and interesting results but major technical concerns (rigorous proof of Theorem 1 and clarification of dimensionality) currently prevent recommending acceptance.
