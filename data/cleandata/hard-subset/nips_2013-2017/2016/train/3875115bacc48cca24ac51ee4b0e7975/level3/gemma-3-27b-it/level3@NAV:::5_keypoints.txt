- Answers Srebro (2007): constructs counterexamples showing bad local maxima exist in the population likelihood of GMMs, even for well-separated spherical mixtures.  
- Proves EM with common random initialization converges to these suboptimal points with exponentially high probability.  
- Builds on and situates results within prior work on GMM identifiability, convergence rates, EM improvements, and saddle-point analyses.  
- Specifically shows saddle points are not the main impediment; bad local maxima are the cause of EM failure.  
- Technical contribution: rigorous guarantees and clear, technically deep proofs with strong intuitive exposition.  
- Main weakness: Theorem 2 lacks concrete constants in probability bounds, hindering assessment of practical significance and phase transition for realistic k.  
- Suggestion: authors should provide estimates of constants or simulations across k to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept—novel, impactful work with the caveat to address constant estimation or add supporting simulations in revision.
