- Studies the population log-likelihood landscape for GMMs with equal weights and identity covariances.  
- Provides a negative resolution to Srebro (2007)’s conjecture: “bad” local maxima can exist.  
- Constructs an explicit counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Shows that for certain mean configurations, random EM initialization has exponentially high probability (in k) of converging to a bad critical point.  
- Proves a gradient-based EM variant almost surely avoids strict saddle points, leaving bad local maxima as the main obstacle.  
- Technical quality is high: proofs appear sound and geometric constructions are clever and well-explained.  
- Resolves a past open problem and offers original insights linking hierarchical grouping of means to bad optima.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior EM analyses by characterizing suboptimal critical points rather than just local convergence around the global optimum.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation and lucid interpretation of results.  
- Results have significant practical implications, formally justifying the importance of initialization for GMMs and challenging the “benign” non-convexity view.  
- Analysis is limited to the population setting and a simplified GMM model, which is appropriate but a restriction.  
- Theorem 2’s recursive construction is complex and placed in the appendix; more intuition in the main text would help.
