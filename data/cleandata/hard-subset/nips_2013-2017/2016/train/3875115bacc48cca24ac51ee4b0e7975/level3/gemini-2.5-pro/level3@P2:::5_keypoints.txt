- Main contribution: Proves that for any M ≥ 3 there exist well-separated spherical GMMs whose population log-likelihood has spurious local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Construction: Provides an intuitive, clever construction (detailed geometrically for M=3) yielding a general class of counterexamples.
- EM consequences: Shows standard EM and a first-order EM/gradient-ascent variant, with common random initialization, converge to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 - exp(-cM).
- Saddles vs. maxima: Uses recent gradient-method results to argue first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures are due to poor local maxima, not saddles.
- Technical quality: Rigorous, complete proofs and sound analysis resolving a long-standing open problem.
- Clarity: Exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized manuscript balancing technical detail and intuition.
- Originality: Highly original resolution of a decade-old COLT conjecture and novel linkage to saddle-escape literature.
- Theoretical significance: Fundamental characterization of the optimization landscape for a canonical non-convex problem in the infinite-sample limit.
- Practical significance: Provides theoretical justification that naive random initialization for EM can fail catastrophically, motivating better initialization methods (moments/spectral).
- Suggested improvement: Clarify the magnitude of constant c in the probability bound or include a small simulation (e.g., M=5 or M=10) to show practical onset of the failure mode.
- Argument for acceptance: Definitive resolution of an important open problem, strong technical novelty, and major implications for theory and practice.
- Argument against acceptance: No major objections; only minor missing discussion on constants linking asymptotic theory to practice.
- Overall recommendation: Excellent, significant work that should be accepted.
