- Paper studies EM for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture with known/fixed parameters and proves three negative results.  
- Proves existence of local maxima of the population likelihood function.  
- Shows EM converges to critical points with low likelihood values.  
- Establishes that with random initialization EM converges with high probability to critical points whose likelihood is much smaller than the global maximum.  
- Demonstrates the same negative convergence behavior for the gradient EM algorithm.  
- Simplifying assumptions (known number of components, uniform weights) are acceptable given the technical nature and informativeness of the results.  
- Concern: Proof of Theorem 1 is too short and needs more detail, particularly in computing limits of supremums of likelihoods.  
- Concern: Gradient EM section is difficult to follow; Equation (5) and related explanations require clarification.  
- Overall quality is good: claims are well-supported, the structure is clear, and authors are careful and honest about strengths and weaknesses.  
- Clarity is generally good and results are reproducible for expert readers, but some parts need more detailed explanations and clearer notation.  
- Originality is high, providing new significant results on the population likelihood of Gaussian mixture models.  
- Significance is high: results highlight the need for careful initialization of EM in practice and have important ML/statistics implications.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address proofs, explanations, and notation issues.
