This paper provides two fundamental results on the population likelihood function of Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) with M ≥ 3 components. The first main result shows that the population likelihood function has bad local maxima even in the special case of equally-weighted mixtures of well-separated and spherical Gaussians, resolving an open question of Srebro [2007]. The second main result shows that the EM algorithm (or a first-order variant of it) with random initialization will converge to bad critical points with probability at least 1− e−Ω(M). 

The paper is well-written, and the authors do a great job of balancing space with intuitive explanation. The results are important for understanding non-convex optimization even in the asymptotic limit. The authors also provide a detailed proof for a simple case (k=3, d=1), which helps to build intuition about the problem.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the reviewer did not fully check the supplementary material, and the authors do not provide estimates of constants in the probability bounds. Knowing the constants is important to identify the phase transition and when bad-solution convergence appears for realistic k. If the constants are complex or unclear, supporting simulations should be provided to demonstrate the phenomenon. However, the lack of constants/simulations does not detract from the paper's novelty or impact.
