- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question on existence of local maxima in infinite-sample GMM likelihoods.  
- Provides negative resolution by constructing counterexamples showing bad local maxima exist.  
- Shows bad local maxima occur even for equally weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians.  
- Proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population likelihood for k ≥ 3 components.  
- Demonstrates EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with high probability.  
- Gives intuition via a simple k=3, d=1 case and extends results to higher dimensions and more components.  
- Highlights the crucial role of initialization for EM-based methods, even asymptotically.  
- Paper is well-written, with rigorous proofs and clear, intuitive explanations.  
- Suggests adding estimates of constants/probability bounds or simulations to observe phase transitions empirically.  
- Strengths: resolves a significant open question, constructs general counterexamples, and offers original insights into non-convex optimization for GMMs.  
- Weaknesses: lacks empirical validation and leaves unresolved the conjecture that three components are necessary for bad local maxima.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution with recommended acceptance and a suggestion to include empirical validation in future work.
