- Rigorous analysis of the population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 showing bad local maxima exist even for equally weighted, spherical, well-separated mixtures.
- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by proving arbitrarily bad local maxima can occur.
- Proves EM with random initialization converges to bad critical points with high probability; first-order EM exhibits the same issue.
- Uses a clever center configuration (split into well-spaced clusters) to construct the bad maxima; Theorem 1 proof is clear and intuitive.
- Paper is well-written with clear background and logical presentation of results.
- Main contribution is significant for practical understanding and use of EM in GMM estimation.
- Practical relevance questioned: the “bad” center configurations and random initialization assumption may be unrealistic since real data are often clearly clustered.
- The three-Gaussian example (two close, one far) suggests the problematic initialization may be unlikely in practice.
- The distinction between convergence to a saddle point versus a local optimum for first-order EM may be of limited practical importance.
- Requests minor clarifications/edits (e.g., meaning of "well-separated constant", notation Ω(k), clarity of L74-81 and requirement (1) L233-235).
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, original construction, important implications for EM.
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about practical relevance of configurations and initialization; saddle vs local-opt distinction possibly minor.
