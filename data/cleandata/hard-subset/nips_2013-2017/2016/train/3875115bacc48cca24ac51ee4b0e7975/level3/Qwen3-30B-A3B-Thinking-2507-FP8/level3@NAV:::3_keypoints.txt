- Resolves Srebro's conjecture by proving arbitrarily bad local maxima exist in the population GMM likelihood for M ≥ 3 well-separated isotropic Gaussians, even with infinite data.
- Proves random initialization for EM (and first-order EM) converges to these bad critical points with high probability (≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}).
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs; the construction via grouping centers into well-separated clusters is elegant and well-motivated.
- Important distinction made between convergence to bad local maxima versus saddle points (Theorem 4).
- Practical relevance is unclear: the paper does not show whether the constructed bad configurations are likely under natural distributions of true centers.
- Theorem 2 does not fully resolve practical occurrence; a probabilistic analysis showing how the probability of bad configurations scales with k or d is needed.
- The assumption of random initialization from the data distribution may be unrealistic when data exhibit clear clustering; problematic configurations may be rare in practice.
- Several minor issues need fixing: L74–81 example (k=2) is unconvincing; L233–235 requirement (1) is unclear; clarify L218 "well-separated constant"; define Ω(k) at L225; and address numerous small phrasing errors.
- Overall: technically strong and novel, but practical impact for the community is limited until real-world relevance is better justified.
