- Problem: analyzes the population log-likelihood landscape for GMMs (equal weights, identity covariances) and addresses Srebro (2007)’s open question about bad local maxima.  
- Contribution 1: constructs a d=1 GMM (for any M ≥ 3) with a local maximum arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Contribution 2: shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−cM}, so success probability is exponentially small in M.  
- Contribution 3: proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, indicating failure is due to poor local maxima rather than saddles.  
- Strength: significance and originality — resolves a long-standing open problem and introduces a novel hierarchical construction to create pathological landscapes.  
- Strength: technical quality — sound, clear proofs for existence of bad maxima and high-probability failure of random initialization.  
- Strength: clarity and presentation — well-written, well-motivated, and logically organized exposition.  
- Weakness: generality — hard instances use specific hierarchical configurations; unclear how representative these are of typical problems.  
- Weakness: higher dimensions — main counterexample is d=1; extension to d>1 and geometric complications are not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: finite-sample regime — claims of extension via empirical process arguments are plausible but would benefit from a brief sketch or citations.  
- Summary: significant, original, and technically solid contribution that clarifies fundamental limits of likelihood-based GMM estimation.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.  
- Argument for acceptance: resolves Srebro’s question, demonstrates severe EM limitations, and offers novel proof techniques of broad interest.  
- Argument against acceptance: negative results rely on specific mean configurations, so broader generality could be better addressed.
