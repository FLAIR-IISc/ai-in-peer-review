- Paper studies population log-likelihood of GMMs (uniform weights, identity covariances) and EM performance for estimating means.  
- Main contribution: constructs GMMs (M ≥ 3) with bad local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, refuting Srebro’s 2007 conjecture.  
- Result holds even for well-separated mixtures.  
- Algorithmic consequence: randomized initialization leads EM and first-order EM (gradient-ascent equivalent) to converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e⁻ᶜᴹ.  
- First-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implying failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle proliferation.  
- Strength: resolves a long-standing open question and provides significant theoretical insight into EM’s limitations.  
- Strength: novel link between existence of bad maxima and exponential failure probability of random initialization, motivating better initialization methods.  
- Strength: clear, logical narrative from structural result to algorithmic implications and saddle vs. maxima distinction.  
- Major weakness: proof of Theorem 1 (central result) is too condensed and contains a critical unjustified step about limits/suprema over parameter-dependent domains.  
- Scope ambiguity: claims stated for general dimension d, but constructions/proofs appear specific to d = 1; need clarification or restriction.  
- Minor clarity issue: presentation of first-order EM update is hard to parse and needs a brief derivation/sketch for self-containment.  
- Recommendation: expand and rigorously justify Theorem 1 in the main text (even if it requires trimming other material); resolve dimensionality ambiguity to make claims precise.
