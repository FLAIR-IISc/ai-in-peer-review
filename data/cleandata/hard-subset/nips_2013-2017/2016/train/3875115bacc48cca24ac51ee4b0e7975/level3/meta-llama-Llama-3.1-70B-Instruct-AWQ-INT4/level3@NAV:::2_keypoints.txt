- Paper studies EM for Gaussian mixtures with known K, weights, and covariances, proving three negative results about suboptimal critical points and convergence.
- Main theoretical claims: (i) local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than global maximum even with large samples.
- Main theoretical claims: (ii) randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to such suboptimal critical points.
- Main theoretical claims: (iii) gradient EM also converges to suboptimal critical points with high probability.
- Contribution is interesting, informative, and provides important practical insights about EM and initialization.
- Analysis restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances); reviewer accepts this but requests discussion of extensions to more general settings.
- Paper is well-written for general dimension d, but proofs are provided only for d = 1; reviewer asks for clarification or discussion of extending proofs to d > 1.
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too short and relies on unjustified limit/supremum convergence claims; needs stronger justification.
- Reviewer suggests lengthening the main proof (even at expense of discussion) to make arguments convincing; did not check supplementary proofs.
- Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; request clearer derivation of Equation (5).
- Quality: technically sound overall but Theorem 1 proof requires more detail.
- Clarity: generally good, but specific sections (gradient EM, multi-d extension) need more explanation.
- Originality: problems and approaches are new and present a novel combination of techniques.
- Significance: results are important and advance understanding of EM behavior in practice.
- Acceptance arguments: provides important, well-supported theoretical insights and addresses a difficult problem better than prior work.
- Rejection/weakness arguments: gaps in Theorem 1 proof and unclear parts of gradient EM section need to be addressed.
