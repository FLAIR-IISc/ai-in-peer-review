- Paper studies EM on GMMs with fixed K, known weights, and fixed covariances, proving existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood even for well-separated Gaussians (resolves Srebro 2007 conjecture).  
- Shows random initialization of EM converges with high probability to suboptimal critical points; a first-order (gradient) EM variant suffers the same problem.  
- Identifies failures due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points, adding to theoretical limitations of EM.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into non-convex optimization behavior for EM.  
- Clear presentation and appropriate connections to prior work (Dasgupta & Schulman 2007, Balakrishnan et al. 2015, Loh & Wainwright 2013).  
- Main weakness: analysis is restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances), limiting applicability to general GMMs.  
- Extension to higher dimensions (d > 1) is not developed; authors only suggest it might be possible without detailing challenges.  
- Major technical concern: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) relies on limits/suprema and a continuity argument that is insufficiently detailed and needs a more careful neighborhood analysis.  
- Reviewer recommends expanding the main proof (even if it reduces other discussion) and ensuring supplementary proofs support critical steps.  
- Minor concern: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are hard to follow; need explicit derivation showing how gradient ascent on Q yields Equation (6).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental question, produces surprising negative results, rigorous analysis, and broad relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted model, insufficient detail in Theorem 1 proof, and unclear gradient EM derivation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — expand Theorem 1 proof, clarify Eq.(5) derivation, and discuss challenges/extensions to higher dimensions.
