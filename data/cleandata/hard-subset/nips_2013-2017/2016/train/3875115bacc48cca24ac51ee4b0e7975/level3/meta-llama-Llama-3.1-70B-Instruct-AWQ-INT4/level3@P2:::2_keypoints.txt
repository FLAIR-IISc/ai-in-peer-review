- Paper analyzes EM and first-order (gradient) EM for Gaussian mixture mean estimation with known K, weights, and covariances, proving three negative results about suboptimal critical points.  
- Main theoretical results: (i) population log-likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- (ii) Randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to such suboptimal critical points.  
- (iii) Gradient EM exhibits the same high-probability convergence to bad critical points.  
- Reviewer finds the question interesting and results informative, and accepts the model simplifications (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) as reasonable.  
- Paper is written for general dimension d, but proofs are provided for d = 1; reviewer asks authors to state extendability to d > 1 or discuss challenges.  
- Theorem 1’s proof is concise but needs more justification (esp. handling limits/suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains in lines 288–306).  
- Reviewer recommends expanding the main proof in the paper and moving key supplementary proofs into the main text for completeness.  
- Implication for practice: results highlight significant need for careful initialization since EM/gradient EM can reliably find bad critical points.  
- Minor clarity issue: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) should be explained more clearly.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clearly written, logically organized, original, and significant.  
- Decision recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen proof details and improve clarity in the gradient EM section.
