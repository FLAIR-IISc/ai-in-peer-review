- Paper shows population log-likelihood for GMMs can have arbitrarily bad local maxima and constructs a counterexample to Srebro (2007).  
- Proves EM and a first-order variant with random initialization converge to these bad local maxima with high probability (≥ 1 − e^(−Ω(M))).  
- Builds on identifiability, convergence-rate, and non-convex optimization literature and challenges the intuition that well-separated GMMs are benign.  
- Strength: Resolves a long-standing open question with an elegant construction and novel proof techniques.  
- Strength: Significant algorithmic result demonstrating exponential failure probability of random initialization.  
- Strength: Distinguishes failures due to bad local maxima from saddle points (Theorem 4).  
- Weakness: Practical relevance unclear — no analysis of how likely these bad configurations are under natural distributions of true centers.  
- Weakness: Random initialization scheme (sampling from data) is arguably unrealistic for clearly clustered data and may not produce problematic configurations in practice.  
- Clarification needed: k=2 example (L74-81) does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture and should be explained.  
- Clarification needed: L233-235 — why require centers initialized between clusters and why wouldn’t such centers move to correct clusters?  
- Clarification needed: L267-273 — practical importance of distinguishing saddle-point convergence vs local maxima convergence is unclear.  
- Notation and phrasing issues: unclear “well-separated constant” (L218), undefined Ω(k) (L225), and other minor wording problems.  
- Minor writing issues and typos throughout (redundancies, unclear “equivalent to”, duplicate words) that need revision.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on analyses of practical relevance, clearer justification of the initialization scheme, and fixing clarity/notation/writing issues.
