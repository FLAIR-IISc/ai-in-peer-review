- Shows local maxima in GMM log-likelihood can exist even with infinite data and be arbitrarily poor.
- Demonstrates random initialization causes EM to almost surely miss the global optimum as component count grows.
- Provides valuable theoretical insights into EM behavior, but practical relevance is questionable.
- Configurations producing bad local modes may be unlikely in real-world data where clusters are clear.
- Theorem 2 is unclear about the probability of encountering “bad” configurations under realistic assumptions.
- Some parts are unclear or redundant (example lines 74–81; sentence on line 172).
- Paper needs minor edits: typos corrected and several sentences rephrased for clarity.
- Overall quality is good: claims are well-supported and the paper is well-structured.
- Originality is limited, as the work builds on existing GMM and EM literature.
- Significant contribution: gives a negative answer to Srebro (2007) and emphasizes careful initialization.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical contribution, clarity, and resolution of an open question.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact, unclear theorem statement, and some redundant/unclear exposition.
