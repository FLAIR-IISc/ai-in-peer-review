- Paper proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Shows these bad local maxima can occur even for well-separated, isotropic Gaussians.
- Demonstrates EM with random initialization often converges to these suboptimal points, with convergence probability scaling exponentially in M.
- Establishes first-order EM is unlikely to converge to saddle points, implying local maxima are the main bottleneck.
- Builds on and appropriately positions itself within prior work on identifiability and mixture estimation.
- Theoretical proofs are rigorous and provide significant negative results about the GMM optimization landscape.
- Analysis gives concrete algorithmic implications explaining EM’s potential failures in seemingly benign settings.
- Results are novel and challenge common assumptions about EM behavior.
- Major practical concern: constructed pathological configurations may not reflect real-world data frequency.
- Suggestion: analyze probability of encountering “bad” configurations under a realistic distribution on true centers (e.g., uniform on a sphere).
- Concern that the random initialization model is unrealistic; results may not generalize to informed schemes like k-means++.
- The three-Gaussian illustrative example (two close, one far) may in practice make the problematic initialization unlikely.
- Example for M=2 (L74-81) is unclear and weakly connected to the main argument.
- Technical clarity issues: unclear requirement (L233-235) and misplaced discussion about saddle points (L267-273).
- Multiple minor phrasing and notation issues throughout the paper that need proofreading.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, insight into EM limitations, clear motivation and writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical relevance, reliance on random initialization, minor clarity issues.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing practical relevance and initialization-generalizability concerns.
