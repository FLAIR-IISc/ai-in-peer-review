- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question about existence of spurious local maxima in the population likelihood for GMMs.  
- Constructs a general class of counterexamples showing spurious local maxima exist for mixtures with three or more components.  
- Demonstrates these “bad” local maxima can have likelihoods arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Extends structural results to algorithmic consequences: EM and gradient EM with common random initialization converge to bad solutions with exponentially high probability in number of components.  
- Originality and significance: resolves a decade-old conjecture and deepens understanding of non-convex optimization landscapes.  
- Practical implication: cautions against blind faith in local search heuristics like EM even in the infinite-sample limit.  
- Technical quality: high, with clear intuition and a detailed proof for the simple case k=3, d=1.  
- Presentation: exceptionally well-written and organized, balancing formal statements and accessible explanations.  
- Main weakness: probability bound uses 1 - e^{-cM} but constant c is not estimated, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Recommendation: authors should provide estimates for the constant or include supporting simulations (e.g., convergence frequency vs M).  
- Overall verdict: minor weakness only; important, complete work that merits acceptance.
