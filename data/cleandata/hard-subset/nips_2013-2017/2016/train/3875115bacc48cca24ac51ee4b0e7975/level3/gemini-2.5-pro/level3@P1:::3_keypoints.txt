- Paper shows population log-likelihood for equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated GMMs can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3, refuting Srebro (2007).
- Proves EM and a first-order EM variant with standard random initialization converge to these bad critical points with probability → 1 exponentially fast in M.
- Shows first-order EM’s failures are due to convergence to bad local maxima rather than strict saddle points.
- Strength: resolves a long-standing open question and clarifies the non-benign nature of the GMM likelihood landscape even in idealized settings.
- Strength: technically strong and convincing constructions/proofs, including an exponential (1 − e−cM) failure probability bound.
- Strength: provides rigorous justification for careful initialization of EM and links results to broader non-convex optimization literature.
- Main weakness: practical relevance of the constructed “bad” GMMs is unclear — the paper gives a worst-case instance but not average-case likelihood of such configurations.
- Question: how likely are these bad configurations under simple priors on true centers (e.g., i.i.d. Gaussian) and how do M and d affect that probability?
- Main weakness: practical relevance of the initialization failure — random data-based initialization may be naive and might not place EM in the bad basins as often as analysis suggests.
- Suggestion: include simulations to estimate how frequently these failure modes occur in practice under realistic data/initialization schemes.
- Clarity issue: L74–81 (M=2 example) is unclear about whether Srebro’s conjecture holds for M=2 and what the visualization intends to convey.
- Clarity issue: L233–235 (requirement that initialized centers fall near exactly one true cluster) needs clarification or justification.
- Clarity issue: L267–273 (Theorem 4) should better motivate the practical importance of ruling out convergence to strict saddles versus local maxima.
- Notational/phrasing fixes requested: clarify “well-separated constant,” the meaning of e^Ω(M) times, and several wording/typo lines called out.
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution deserving acceptance, but would be strengthened by addressing practical relevance and adding empirical or probabilistic analyses of the bad instances.
- Minor typos: various small wording and punctuation errors listed (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L220, L245, L249, L308) needing correction.
