- Study of local maxima of the GMM population log-likelihood for equally-weighted, spherical, unit-variance components (M ≥ 3).
- Theorem 1: proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima such that L(µ′) ≤ L(µ∗) − Cgap for some well-separated GMM.
- Resolves the open question posed by Srebro (2007).
- Theorem 2: EM (or first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.
- Theorem 4: first-order EM with suitable stepsize converges to critical points and avoids strict saddles with probability 1.
- Technical proofs are sound and the problem setup and interpretations are clearly explained.
- High novelty and originality, notably the hierarchical grouping insight for k > 3.
- Results have significant potential impact for theoretical clustering work and practical seeding strategies.
- Weakness: analysis is restricted to an idealized equal-weight, spherical, unit-variance setting, limiting realism.
- Weakness: lack of empirical experiments to validate theoretical findings.

- Recommendation: Accept.
