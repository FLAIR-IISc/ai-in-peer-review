- Paper studies convergence of EM for GMMs and shows bad local optima exist even with infinite data, refuting Srebro (2007).
- Provides bounds on the probability that EM converges to these bad local maxima.
- Shows first-order EM variants avoid saddle points, so local maxima—not saddles—are the main obstacle.
- Builds on prior theoretical work on EM and GMMs (Dasgupta & Schulman, Balakrishnan et al., Arora & Kannan, etc.).
- Technically sound with logically structured arguments; proofs are referenced though not fully detailed in-paper.
- Authors clearly acknowledge idealized assumptions (spherical Gaussians, equal weights) and their implications.
- Analysis covers both population likelihood and algorithmic behavior; Theorem 4 strengthens the saddle-point avoidance claim.
- Clarity is generally good with standard notation, but some parts (appendix proofs) are dense and need more intuition.
- Originality: definitively answers a long-standing question about bad local optima in GMM likelihoods.
- Significance: moderate — highlights a fundamental EM limitation and the importance of initialization despite idealized setting.
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous analysis, definitive result, motivates better initialization methods, well-written overall.
- Cons against acceptance: results may be unsurprising to experts, limited real-world applicability due to idealized setting, shallow treatment of k-means connection.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
- Specific suggestions: expand intuition for the recursive construction in Theorem 2, compare to Kumar–Kannan and contrast EM vs. Lloyd’s guarantees, and elaborate on adapting k-means-style initializations (k-log-k, distance-squared) for EM.
