- Paper proves Srebro’s conjecture is false: for any M ≥ 3, the population log-likelihood for isotropic, known-variance, uniformly weighted GMMs can have arbitrarily bad local maxima, even with well-separated components.
- Constructs hierarchical true-center configurations showing EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability approaching 1 exponentially in M.
- Shows a first-order EM variant almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying failures stem from attractive bad local optima rather than saddles.
- Strength: Original and significant theoretical contribution resolving a long-standing open question about GMM non-convexity.
- Strength: Strong, technically sound proofs (Theorem 1 construction and Theorem 2 analysis) linking landscape structure to EM failure.
- Strength: Generally clear writing, good organization, and intuitive proof sketches.
- Weakness: Practical relevance unclear because negative results rely on specific, constructed center configurations that may be rare in real data.
- Weakness: Analysis shows EM fails for a given bad configuration but does not quantify probability of such configurations under reasonable priors over true centers.
- Weakness: The random-initialization failure mode may be low-probability in practice (e.g., likely initial centers land in denser clusters), needing more nuanced discussion.
- Request clarification: The M=2 example (L74–81) is confusing—its intended role (illustration vs. evidence) should be clarified.
- Request clarification: The claim that only very restrictive initializations are favorable (L233–235) seems too strong; explain why intermediate initializations cannot be rescued by EM updates.
- Request clarification: Practical importance of distinguishing strict saddles from bad local optima (L267–273) should be explained for practitioners/algorithm designers.
- Request clarification: Replace vague phrase “well-separated constant” with explicit “sufficiently large separation constant ξ” (L218).
- Request clarification: Explicitly state that e^Ω(M) means required repetitions grow exponentially in M (L225).
- Request clarification: Define O(d^1/2)-separation precisely (e.g., distance ≥ c·sqrt(d)) (L138).
- Minor edits: Multiple small wording and phrasing fixes suggested across lines L18, L38, L60, L172, L177, L220, L245, L249, L308 for clarity and grammar.
