- Paper studies local maxima structure in population log-likelihood of equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs and EM behavior.
- Well-motivated and situated within prior work on GMM identifiability, convergence rates, and EM analysis.
- Main result 1: counterexample (k=3, d=1) showing arbitrarily bad local maxima exist.
- Main result 2: probabilistic analysis showing exponentially small probability of avoiding bad local maxima with random initialization.
- Main result 3: proof that gradient EM almost surely does not converge to saddle points.
- Theorem 1’s proof appears sound; Theorem 2’s proof idea is reasonable though appendix details weren’t checked.
- Original contribution: links mean configuration to existence/quality of local maxima and introduces a hierarchical grouping insight for k>3.
- Suggestion for future work: extend Lemma 7 to dimensions d>1.
- Writing and presentation are clear and intuition is well-explained.
- Potential impact: informs theoretical understanding of clustering optima and practical EM seeding strategies.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a fundamental open problem with novel, technically strong results and clear presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: limited to equally weighted, identity-covariance GMMs and lacks empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
