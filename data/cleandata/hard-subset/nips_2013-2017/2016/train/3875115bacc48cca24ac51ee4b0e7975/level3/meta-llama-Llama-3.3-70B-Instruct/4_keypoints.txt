- Proposes a novel unsupervised method for learning similarities among many exemplars using CNNs, addressing single-positive exemplar, exemplar/negative imbalance, and inconsistent SGD labels.  
- Formulates an optimization to extract batches with mutually consistent relations for CNN training.  
- Evaluated on Olympic Sports, Leeds Sports, and PASCAL VOC 2007, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear problem setup, intuition, and analysis.  
- Technical proofs (Theorem 1 and Theorem 2) are sound and provide a solid foundation.  
- Theorem 2 introduces a clever, novel idea.  
- Results have potential impact on theory (local optima/clustering) and practical algorithm design.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, sound theory, competitive benchmarks, and potential impact.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of the optimization problem and its solution.  
- Weaknesses: no comparison with alternative optimization methods.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational cost and scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations and future work.
