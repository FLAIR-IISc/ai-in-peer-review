- Studies the population likelihood landscape for mixtures of spherical Gaussians and provides theoretical results on EM convergence.
- Shows that, contrary to Srebro (2007), the likelihood can have bad local maxima even for well-separated components with infinite data.
- Presents a construction with M=3 components that yields arbitrarily poor local optima.
- Proves random initialization fails to find the global optimum with probability approaching one exponentially in the number of components M.
- Argues that for gradient EM the main barrier to convergence is bad local maxima rather than strict saddle points.
- Strength: rigorous, clear presentation that formally resolves an open question and quantifies failure probability of random initialization.
- Strength: technical analysis appears sound and the insight about local maxima vs. saddles is significant.
- Weakness: results and construction are not surprising; they mirror standard failure modes (e.g., placing two centers close and one far).
- Weakness: the exponential failure rate for random initialization is analogous to known k-means results, motivating common initialization strategies.
- Suggestion: explicitly discuss parallels with k-means and contrast guarantees/failure conditions for EM versus Lloyd’s algorithm under large separation.
- Suggestion: relate results to prior analyses of algorithms with provable guarantees under separation (e.g., Kumar and Kannan) to clarify unique contributions.
