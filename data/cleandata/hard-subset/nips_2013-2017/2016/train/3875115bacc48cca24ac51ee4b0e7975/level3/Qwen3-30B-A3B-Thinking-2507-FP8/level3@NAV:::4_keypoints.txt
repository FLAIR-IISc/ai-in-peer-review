Paper resolves Srebro (2007) by showing for M ≥ 3 equal-weight, identity-covariance GMMs the log-likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum (Theorem 1).

With random initialization, EM and first-order EM converge to these bad local maxima with probability at least 1 − e^{−Ω(M)} (Theorem 2).

Gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points (Theorem 4).

Technical quality is high: proofs (especially Theorems 1 and 2) appear sound and well-motivated.

Novelty: a hierarchical grouping construction for k>3 components provides a clever, original way to produce exponential failure probability.

Insight: links between mean configuration and local-maxima behavior are original and informative.

Clarity: exposition, problem setup, and intuitive interpretations of counterexamples are excellent.

Significance: results have substantial practical implications—random initialization can fail even in idealized settings, motivating better seeding strategies.

Limitation: counterexample is confined to d=1; extension to higher dimensions is left open (Lemma 7 suggested).

Limitation: requires M ≥ 3, so the two-component case remains unresolved.

Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with practical relevance; recommend acceptance.
