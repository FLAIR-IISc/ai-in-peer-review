- Main contribution: Shows population GMM likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3 even with equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Algorithmic result: Proves EM with random initialization converges to these bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 - e^(-Ω(M)).
- Literature context: Builds on identifiability, convergence, and EM analyses and connects to non-convex optimization and clustering theory.
- Strength: Resolves a long-standing open question with a clear theoretical construction of bad local maxima.
- Strength: Elegant construction (two centers clustered, one misplaced) showing separations of Ω(√d) produce the phenomenon.
- Strength: Provides a concrete exponential failure probability for random-initialized EM, quantifying practical risk.
- Strength: Theorem distinguishing bad local maxima from strict saddles for first-order EM is a valuable algorithmic insight.
- Weakness: Techniques and constructions follow expected patterns in the literature and do not introduce novel theoretical tools.
- Weakness: Limited practical guidance—no concrete improved initialization strategies or empirical comparisons to methods like Kumar-Kannan.
- Weakness: Narrow focus on spherical, equal-weight mixtures; limited discussion of non-spherical covariances or non-uniform weights.
- Significance: Demonstrates EM sensitivity to initialization even in idealized infinite-sample, well-separated settings, explaining empirical failures.
- Quality/Clarity: Technically rigorous and well-written, though appendices contain dense technical details that may challenge some readers.
- Originality: Resolves Srebro’s open conjecture with a novel construction in this context, but overall approach aligns with recent non-convex optimization trends.
- Acceptance case: Important resolution of an open question, strong theoretical EM failure guarantees, and clear distinction between failure modes.
- Rejection case: Incremental techniques, limited practical impact without better initialization proposals, and incomplete positioning against broader GMM variants.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, suggesting minor improvements to better connect to practical initialization strategies.
