- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) open question: shows bad local maxima can exist in the population likelihood for GMMs.  
- Three main contributions: constructs counterexample for M ≥ 3, shows random EM/gradient-EM likely converges to suboptimal critical points, and attributes failures to bad local maxima not saddles.  
- Builds on prior work (Dasgupta & Schulman; Balakrishnan et al.) that showed nicer behavior for two-component mixtures.  
- Connects to non-convex optimization results (Lee et al., Panageas & Piliouras) by showing a different failure mode for GMMs.  
- Strength: clear demonstration that even idealized GMM settings permit spurious local maxima, explaining practical need for careful initialization.  
- Strength: technical results are sound and provide important theoretical insights into limitations of local search methods.  
- Strength: simplifying assumptions (uniform weights, identity covariances, known k) are reasonable as a “best-case” setting.  
- Strength: focus on population setting is appropriate and extendable to finite samples via standard arguments.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 proof (lines 288–306) lacks sufficient justification for convergence of supremums over unbounded domains; needs a more rigorous argument.  
- Weakness: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are unclear and require clearer exposition.  
- Weakness: Paper does not address generalization of constructions/results to dimensions d > 1 despite claiming general d.  
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality theoretical contribution with fixable technical issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the proof detail, gradient-EM derivation, and discussion of higher dimensions.
