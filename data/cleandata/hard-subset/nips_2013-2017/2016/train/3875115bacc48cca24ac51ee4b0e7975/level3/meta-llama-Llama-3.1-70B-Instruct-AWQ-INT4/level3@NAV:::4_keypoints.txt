- Paper studies local maxima of GMM population likelihood and answers an open question from Srebro (2007) negatively.
- Shows population likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians.
- Well-written with clear problem setup, interpretations, and organization.
- Provides a thorough analysis of the population log-likelihood structure and introduces new proof techniques for non-convex optimization behavior.
- Main contribution 1: constructs examples of arbitrarily bad local maxima in otherwise well-behaved GMMs.
- Main contribution 2: proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with exponentially high probability.
- Main contribution 3: shows first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the failure mode.
- Technical quality is high and proofs appear sound and detailed.
- Novelty is significant; relates mean configuration to local maxima in an insightful and useful way.
- Potential impact is high: results imply careful initialization is required and that repeated random restarts may not suffice in practice.
- Reviewer recommendation: rates the paper as excellent and a significant contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: comprehensive study, original results, strong technical quality, and practical implications.
- Arguments against acceptance: none.
