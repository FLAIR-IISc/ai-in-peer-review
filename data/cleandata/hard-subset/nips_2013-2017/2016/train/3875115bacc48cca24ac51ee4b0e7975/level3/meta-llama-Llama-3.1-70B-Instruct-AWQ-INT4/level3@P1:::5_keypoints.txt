- Constructs a counterexample showing the infinite-sample GMM log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for equally weighted, spherical, well-separated Gaussians.  
- Resolves an open question from Srebro (2007) by demonstrating existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood.  
- Proves EM and its first-order variant converge to bad critical points with exponentially high probability under common random initialization.  
- Shows first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides thorough background and related work.  
- Provides a detailed, intuition-building proof for the simple case k=3, d=1.  
- Technical arguments and proofs are sound and easy to follow.  
- Supplementary material was not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Reviewer requests estimates for constants in the probability bounds to clarify phase transitions and practical k values.  
- No simulations are provided to empirically demonstrate the phenomenon.  
- Significance: advances understanding of non-convex optimization behavior in GMMs and highlights need for careful EM initialization.  
- Suggestions: include constant estimates, add simulations, and discuss practical implications for EM more thoroughly.
