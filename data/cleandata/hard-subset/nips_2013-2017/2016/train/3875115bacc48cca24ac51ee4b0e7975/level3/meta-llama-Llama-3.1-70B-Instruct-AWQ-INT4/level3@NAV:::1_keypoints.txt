- Main contribution: Rigorous analysis showing EM can get stuck in local optima for equally weighted, spherical, well-separated GMMs, with global-convergence probability decaying exponentially in the number of components.  
- Writing and organization: Well-written and clearly organized, with an accessible introduction and background.  
- Presentation of results: Main results are presented clearly, focusing on structural properties of the population log-likelihood and algorithmic consequences.  
- Algorithms analyzed: Detailed convergence analysis of EM and its variants (including first-order EM), showing they can converge to suboptimal points.  
- Theoretical techniques: Uses probability theory and dynamical systems theory to derive the results.  
- Practical implications: Highlights need for careful initialization and potential benefit of alternative algorithms.  
- Originality: Offers a new perspective on EM convergence and structural properties of the likelihood, while building on prior work.  
- Literature and appendices: Well-supported by related work and technical appendices with mathematical details.  
- Significance: Results have important implications for clustering, density estimation, and model selection.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address possible technical issues.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Pro-accept arguments: Thorough analysis of a widely used algorithm, broad applicability of results, and clear exposition.  
- Con-accept arguments: Limited appeal to non-EM/GMM readers, results may be unsurprising to experts, and analysis is restricted to idealized mixture settings.
