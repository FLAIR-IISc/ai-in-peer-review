- Problem: Analyzes local maxima in GMM likelihoods for equal mixing weights and identity covariances.  
- Main result 1: Constructs a counterexample showing arbitrarily bad local maxima even with well-separated Gaussians.  
- Main result 2: Shows randomly initialized EM likely converges to these suboptimal points with probability decaying exponentially in number of components.  
- Main result 3: Proves first-order EM avoids strict saddle points with high probability, implicating local maxima as the primary issue.  
- Motivation/context: Well-situated within literature on GMM estimation, non-convex optimization, and prior conjectures (e.g., Srebro 2007).  
- Strength: Elegant, insightful construction countering Srebro’s conjecture and clarifying likelihood landscape.  
- Strength: Strong probabilistic analysis demonstrating practical implications for EM initialization.  
- Strength: Analysis of first-order EM adds depth by separating saddle-related issues from local maxima problems.  
- Strength: Clear writing, good organization, helpful figures (e.g., Figure 1) and two-component discussion.  
- Weakness: Technical proofs (especially Theorem 2) are detailed in appendices and were not fully verified by reviewer.  
- Weakness: Results restricted to equal weights and identity covariance matrices, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: Extension to higher dimensions (Lemma 7 for d>1) is not developed and only suggested as future work.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, significant, technically sound (based on main text), and clearly presented.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited scope and reliance on appendices for full verification.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—ask authors to discuss generalization to unequal weights/non-identity covariances and potential extension of Lemma 7 to d>1.
