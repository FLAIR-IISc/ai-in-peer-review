- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture: shows population GMM likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3 even under ideal conditions (equal weights, well-separated, spherical, unit variance).  
- Proves random initialization of EM and its first-order variant converges to these suboptimal critical points with high probability (≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}).  
- Shows the failure is caused by bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Distinguishes behavior by component count: M=2 has no bad local maxima, M≥3 does.  
- Analysis in the population (infinite-sample) setting yields clean theoretical insights unclouded by finite-sample effects.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the empirical need for careful EM initialization and supports use of multiple restarts or method-of-moments initialization.  
- Strength: resolves an important open theoretical question and links structural results to algorithmic consequences.  
- Weakness: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too brief; limits over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains need more rigorous justification.  
- Weakness: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) and derivation of Equation (5) are hard to follow and need clearer exposition.  
- Weakness: results are proved for d=1 but the paper does not clarify extension or complications for d > 1.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and significant but requires minor revisions to strengthen proofs and clarify exposition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions (expand Theorem 1 proof, clarify gradient EM derivation, discuss higher-dimensional extension).
