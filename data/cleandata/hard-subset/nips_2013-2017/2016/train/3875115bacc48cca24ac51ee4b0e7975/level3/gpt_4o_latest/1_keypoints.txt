- Studies convergence of EM for GMMs on the population likelihood in the infinite-sample setting.  
- Proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima for mixtures of three or more well-separated spherical Gaussians, refuting Srebro (2007).  
- Shows EM with random initialization has high probability of converging to suboptimal critical points.  
- Demonstrates gradient-based EM escapes strict saddles but still converges to poor local maxima, so bad local optima are the main bottleneck.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs and explicit constructions of bad local maxima.  
- Extends the construction recursively to larger mixtures and gives probabilistic failure rates for random initialization.  
- Clear exposition and well-organized presentation with sufficient background.  
- Lacks surprising insights or novel techniques beyond expected nonconvex optimization behavior.  
- Does not deeply explore alternative initialization strategies or practical remedies for EM’s failures.  
- Insufficient empirical results and limited discussion of practical implications for GMM applications.  
- Needs more explicit comparison with related algorithms (e.g., k-means convergence guarantees by Kumar and Kannan).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address practical implications, remedies, and broader comparisons.
