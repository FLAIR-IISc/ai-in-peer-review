- Paper studies population likelihood of Gaussian mixtures (known M, equal weights, identity covariances) and constructs well-separated mixtures with arbitrarily bad local maxima, resolving Srebro (2007) for M ≥ 3.
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M); same for gradient EM, while gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddles.
- Complements prior EM local-convergence analyses and contrasts algorithmic global guarantees from clustering/moments methods.
- Constructions and high-level arguments are insightful and potentially important if proofs are correct.
- Major concern: proof of Theorem 1 is too brief—limits of suprema/maxima taken over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains are unjustified by continuity alone.
- Reviewer requests a rigorous fix (e.g., show coercivity and restrict to compact sublevel sets, prove uniform convergence there, or use argmax/epi-convergence) and to move key proofs into the main text.
- Clarity issue: gradient EM discussion (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; expand derivation of Equation (5) and state stepsize/smoothness assumptions.
- Originality: high — provides novel structural negative results at the population level and resolves a long-standing conjecture.
- Significance: high — sharpens understanding of EM failures and motivates principled initializers.
- Scope/limitations: analysis assumes known M, uniform weights, identity covariances; proofs are in d = 1 despite claiming general d — authors should state possible extensions and obstacles for d > 1.
- Pros for acceptance: resolves open question, strong algorithmic consequences, ties to modern nonconvex optimization theory.
- Cons for acceptance: Theorem 1 needs more detail, gradient EM derivation unclear, and dimensionality/generalization to d > 1 is left unclear.
