- Paper disproves Srebro (2007) conjecture by showing population likelihood for M ≥ 3 well-separated spherical Gaussians can have arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 - exp(-Ω(M)).  
- Proves gradient-based variants avoid strict saddles, indicating bad local maxima—not saddles—are the main obstacle.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation establishing existence of bad local maxima.  
- Strength: clear 3-component counterexample construction (two close centers, one far away).  
- Strength: connects EM failures to known k-means pathologies and gives practical guidance on initialization.  
- Weakness: results are largely unsurprising given existing non-convex optimization literature.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited; core insights follow expected lines for non-convex problems.  
- Suggestion: include stronger comparison to Kumar–Kannan GMM results and contrast with k-means guarantees under large separation.  
- Quality/Clarity: technically sound and clearly written.  
- Originality: modest — contributions mirror known failures of k-means and non-convex analyses.  
- Significance: meaningful for practitioners as concrete justification for careful EM initialization even with infinite data.  
- Acceptance arguments: resolves a long-standing conjecture, gives rigorous negative result, and offers implementation insights.  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty for NIPS audience since examples and conclusions echo known phenomena.
