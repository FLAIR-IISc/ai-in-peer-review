- Resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture: proves bad local maxima exist for M ≥ 3 even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Main results: (i) arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3; (ii) EM with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}; (iii) first-order EM shows the same behavior.  
- Provides theoretical justification that careful initialization is necessary for EM on GMMs, even under favorable conditions.  
- Advances understanding of the non-convex optimization landscape for GMMs and extends prior two-component analyses.  
- Demonstrates failures arise from bad local maxima rather than saddle points; Theorem 4 shows first-order EM avoids strict saddles almost surely.  
- Simplified model assumptions (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances) are reasonable for analyzing structural properties.  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, significant and well-motivated results, careful distinction between maxima and saddles.  
- Weakness: Proof of Theorem 1 is insufficiently detailed—claims about convergence of limits and supremums over unbounded/parameter-dependent sets need stronger justification.  
- Weakness: Gradient EM derivation (Eq. 5) is hard to follow; intermediate steps relating the update to the population likelihood should be added.  
- Dimensionality issue: results stated for general d but proofs provided only for d=1; authors should discuss extension to d>1 or explain additional difficulties.  
- Overall assessment: contributions are substantial and mostly technically sound, with Theorem 1 needing strengthening.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address proof details and clarify derivations.
