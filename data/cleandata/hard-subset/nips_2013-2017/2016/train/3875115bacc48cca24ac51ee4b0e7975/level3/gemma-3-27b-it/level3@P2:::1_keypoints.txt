- Paper proves existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood for GMMs, even for well-separated spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Shows random initialization of EM converges to suboptimal local maxima with high probability, with failure probability scaling exponentially in number of components M.
- Proves first-order EM avoids strict saddle points, implying bad local maxima are the primary failure mode.
- Builds on extensive prior work on GMM identifiability, convergence rates, and non-convex optimization, situating results in the literature.
- Strength: rigorous mathematical treatment and sound proofs addressing a fundamental estimation problem.
- Strength: resolution of Srebro’s conjecture and clear presentation of exponential dependence on M are significant contributions.
- Strength: clear writing and comprehensive citations linking to related algorithms and theory.
- Weakness: novelty feels incremental since bad local maxima and random-init failures are known in non-convex settings.
- Weakness: the specific construction (two close components and one far away) is intuitive and reminiscent of k-means failure modes.
- Weakness: paper offers little in terms of concrete practical solutions or improved initialization methods.
- Suggestion: expand comparison with Lloyd’s algorithm/k-means and discuss results under large-separation conditions (e.g., Kumar and Kannan).
- Suggestion: provide more discussion of practical implications and possible robust initialization strategies beyond pure random sampling.
- Suggestion: analyze influence of dimensionality d and the separation requirement ξ = Ω(√d) in greater depth.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand related-work discussion, practical implications, k-means connection, and limitations/future directions.
