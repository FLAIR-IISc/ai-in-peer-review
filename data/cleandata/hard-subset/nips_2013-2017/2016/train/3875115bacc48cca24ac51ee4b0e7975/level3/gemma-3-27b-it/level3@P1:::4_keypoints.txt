- Problem: studies local optima in GMM likelihoods for equal weights and identity covariances.
- Main result 1: constructs a 1D three-component GMM counterexample showing arbitrarily bad local maxima, refuting Srebro’s conjecture.
- Main result 2: proves EM with random initialization likely converges to bad local maxima, with success probability decaying exponentially in number of components M.
- Main result 3: shows first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, attributing failures to bad local maxima.
- Significance: demonstrates that even simple, well-separated GMMs have a rugged likelihood landscape impacting EM.
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis linking likelihood geometry to practical EM behavior.
- Proof technique: elegant construction and hierarchical grouping idea used in Theorem 2.
- Strength: clear presentation and logical organization from background to results and implications.
- Strength: addresses a long-standing open problem and situates work well within prior literature.
- Practical implication: warns against naive random initialization for EM and motivates better initialization strategies.
- Limitation: key proof details are deferred to the appendix, requiring further verification.
- Limitation: Lemma 7’s extension to higher dimensions (d>1) is unresolved and suggested as future work.
- Limitation: paper does not provide concrete methods for obtaining good initializations.
- Limitation: results are limited to equal weights and identity covariances, reducing generality.
- Originality: offers a new perspective linking mean configuration to bad local maxima and advances theoretical understanding of GMMs.
- Impact: likely relevant to clustering, density estimation, and non-convex optimization research.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical results, novelty, clear writing, solves an open problem, and high potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on appendix for proof verification, lack of concrete initialization solutions, and restricted setting.
- Recommendation: overall high-quality work; reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS 2016.
