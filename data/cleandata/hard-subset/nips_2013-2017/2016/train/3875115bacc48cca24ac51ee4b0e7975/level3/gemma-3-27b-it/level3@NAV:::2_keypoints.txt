- Paper shows existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in GMM likelihoods even for well-separated mixtures with known covariance and equal weights.
- Authors prove EM and first-order (gradient) EM converge with high probability to these suboptimal points under random initialization.
- Builds on Srebro (2007) and addresses an important theoretical and practical question about EM’s reliability.
- Strength: clear identification and rigorous proof of EM’s susceptibility to poor local optima in a favorable setting.
- Strength: analysis covers both EM and gradient EM and shows failures are not just due to saddle points.
- Weakness: main proofs are restricted to d = 1, limiting generality.
- Weakness: Proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is compressed and lacks sufficient justification, particularly regarding limits over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains.
- Suggestion: expand and clarify the core proof (Theorem 1), even at the expense of other sections.
- Weakness: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is unclear and Equation (5) is introduced without adequate derivation or explanation.
- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental question, presents rigorous negative results, and offers valuable insight into the likelihood surface.
- Against acceptance: limited to one dimension, insufficient detail in Theorem 1, and unclear gradient EM exposition.
- Overall: recommend acceptance conditional on addressing Theorem 1 clarity, clarifying gradient EM/Equation (5), and discussing difficulties in extending results to d > 1.
