This paper studies the population log-likelihood of Gaussian mixture models with equal mixing weights and identity covariance and provides structural and algorithmic insights into its nonconvex landscape. It settles Srebro’s 2007 conjecture by constructing, for k ≥ 3, d = 1, well-separated mixtures that admit bad local maxima whose likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum. It further shows that EM (and gradient/first-order EM) with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability 1 − e^{-Ω(k)} for certain mean configurations, via a clever hierarchical grouping argument that forces components to remain trapped in incorrect clusters. Finally, building on ideas related to stable-manifold analyses (e.g., Lee et al., 2016), the paper proves gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, indicating failures are due to poor local maxima rather than saddles. These results connect to a rich literature on EM and mixtures (Dasgupta & Schulman, Balakrishnan et al., Hsu & Kakade, Moitra & Valiant) and complement finite-sample observations of many local optima (Améndola et al.).

Quality: The main structural theorem (k = 3, d = 1 counterexample) is clean and the argument appears sound. The probabilistic failure of random initialization is plausible and well motivated by the recursive clustering construction; I did not verify every appendix detail but the proof idea is compelling. The saddle-point avoidance result aligns with recent gradient-dynamics theory; the step-size conditions and required smoothness are explained.

Clarity: The paper is very well written, with clear setup, intuition, and interpretations. The hierarchical construction and its consequences are explained crisply.

Originality: High. Relating specific mean configurations to the existence and prevalence of bad local maxima and the hierarchical grouping argument for k > 3 are novel and insightful.

Significance: High. The results clarify when EM-style methods fail, motivating principled seeding. The work should spur theory on clustering optima and practical initialization strategies. It would be valuable to explore whether Lemma 7 and the structural arguments extend to d > 1, unequal weights, and non-spherical covariances.

Suggestions: (i) Quantify separation/gap requirements and constants where possible; (ii) discuss two-component case more (conjectured benign); (iii) provide finite-sample illustrations or simulations comparing random vs. informed seeding (e.g., k-means++, spectral/moments).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Resolves a notable open question; strong conceptual contribution.
- Original hierarchical construction; clear algorithmic implications.
- High clarity and relevance to NIPS community.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Some proofs deferred; limited verification of appendix details.
- Scope restricted to equal weights/identity covariance; d = 1 for the core counterexample.
- Lack of empirical demonstrations to complement population results.
