- Resolves Srebro (2007) negatively by constructing counterexamples showing bad local maxima exist in the population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3.
- Demonstrates that even well-separated, equally-weighted, spherical GMMs can have arbitrarily worse local maxima than the global optimum.
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.
- Shows first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, so failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Provides a detailed, intuitive k=3, d=1 proof that effectively conveys the core construction and generalizes to broader cases.
- Introduces novel proof techniques that may be valuable for future non-convex optimization research.
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and balances mathematical rigor with intuitive explanation.
- Theoretical results are rigorous, reproducible by experts, and technically sound.
- Offers a definitive theoretical advance in GMM literature and addresses a long-standing open question.
- Has significant practical implications, showing that random initialization EM can fail even in favorable GMM settings and motivating careful initialization.
- Weakness: does not provide explicit constants in the probability bounds (1 - e^{-cM}), limiting finite-M interpretability.
- Weakness: lacks supporting simulations to illustrate the phenomenon for concrete parameter values.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — important, novel, and well-executed contribution despite minor gaps.
