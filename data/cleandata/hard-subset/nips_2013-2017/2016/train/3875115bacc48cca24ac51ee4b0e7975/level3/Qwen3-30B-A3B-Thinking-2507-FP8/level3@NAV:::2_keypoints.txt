- Addresses whether the population likelihood for GMMs can have "bad" local maxima and resolves an open question of Srebro (2007).
- Constructs well-separated, equally-weighted, spherical GMM examples (M ≥ 3) whose population log-likelihood has local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.
- Proves standard EM and gradient EM with random initialization converge to these suboptimal critical points with high probability (≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}).
- Shows first-order EM avoids strict saddle points.
- Delivers an important theoretical contribution highlighting fundamental limitations of local-search methods for GMMs, even in favorable settings.
- Complements prior EM convergence analyses and provides practical insight about the necessity of careful initialization.
- Analysis is restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances); authors should clarify extension to higher dimensions.
- Paper is generally well-written and technically sound.
- Major concern: Proof of Theorem 1 lacks sufficient justification for claimed convergence of supremums over unbounded domains—continuity alone does not suffice.
- Major concern: Gradient EM derivation (particularly Equation (5)) is hard to follow and needs clearer explanation.
- Overall: high-quality and significant work, but the above issues should be addressed before acceptance; with revisions it would be a valuable contribution.
