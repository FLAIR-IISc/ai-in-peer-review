- Paper proves that for M ≥ 3 well-separated spherical Gaussians, the population GMM likelihood has bad local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum (resolves Srebro 2007 open question).  
- Shows EM and a first-order variant with random initialization converge to such suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 − exp(−Ω(M)).  
- Demonstrates failure is due to local maxima rather than strict saddle points; first-order EM avoids strict saddles almost surely (Theorem 4).  
- Results are technically sound, coherent, and the paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- The work gives a rigorous, definitive negative answer to a notable open question and provides a complete narrative about GMM likelihood challenges.  
- Theorem 4 connects to modern non-convex optimization literature and timely distinguishes local optima from saddle issues.  
- Main weakness: limited originality when placed in the broader clustering literature — phenomena mirror well-known k-means/Lloyd’s algorithm failures.  
- The bad-local-max construction (two close centers and a distant third) is a classic k-means failure scenario; intuition is not novel.  
- Exponential failure probability for random initialization parallels known results for k-means and motivates advanced initializations like k-means++ and over-seeding.  
- Paper should acknowledge and discuss the strong connection to k-means literature to better position its contribution.  
- Suggested revisions: add a section comparing to k-means analyses (e.g., Kumar and Kannan) and reference initialization fixes like k-means++.  
- Recommend discussing whether EM failure conditions (separation requirements) are stronger or weaker than Lloyd’s to clarify differences between likelihood- and centroid-based challenges.  
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves Srebro’s open question, coherent theorem set, modern insight on saddles, and theoretical justification for careful initialization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty relative to k-means literature and missed opportunity to contextualize findings within that body of work.
