- Resolves Srebro (2007) open problem: shows arbitrarily bad local maxima exist for population log-likelihood of GMMs even with uniformly weighted, spherical, unit-variance, well-separated components.
- Constructs general counterexamples: for any M ≥ 3 there exists a well-separated uniform mixture of M unit-variance spherical Gaussians and a local maximum µ′ with L(µ′) ≤ L(µ∗) − Cgap.
- Shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to bad critical points with exponentially high probability.
- Provides detailed proof for a simple illustrative case (k=3, d=1) to build intuition.
- Paper is well-written, clear, with good balance between technical detail and intuitive explanation.
- Notation and definitions are clear; manuscript is well-organized.
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Results are original and significant for understanding non-convex optimization and for algorithm/initialization design.
- Reviewer concern: supplementary material not fully checked and constants in probability bounds are not provided.
- Recommendation to authors: provide estimates of constants in probability bounds or add supporting simulations to demonstrate practical phase transitions for realistic k.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 9/10.
