- Addresses Srebro's conjecture: whether population likelihood for well-separated, spherical GMMs has only global maxima.  
- Main contribution 1: Constructs a counterexample for M ≥ 3 showing arbitrarily bad local maxima exist even with infinite samples.  
- Main contribution 2: Proves random initialization for EM and first-order EM fails with probability at least 1 − e^(−Ω(M)) to reach global maxima while avoiding strict saddles.  
- Builds on prior GMM and EM work (Teicher; Genovese & Wasserman; Dasgupta & Schulman; Balakrishnan et al.) and relates to recent saddle-avoidance results (Lee et al.).  
- Connects to k-means/Lloyd’s failures, suggesting k-means initialization strategies (e.g., k log k centers or distance-squared sampling) might help EM.  
- Counterexample for M=3 (two close components, one far) is elegantly constructed and clearly explained.  
- Proof techniques are rigorous and technically sound, with novel contributions to non-convex optimization analysis.  
- Insightfully distinguishes failure due to bad local maxima from failure due to strict saddle points.  
- Results are somewhat unsurprising and follow expected lines, limiting perceived novelty.  
- The M=3 counterexample is somewhat artificial and the necessity of three components is left as an open question.  
- Important omission: no comparison with Kumar-Kannan (2005) or detailed contrast between EM and Lloyd’s guarantees under large separation.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and careful about stating limitations (population setting, M ≥ 3).  
- Practical implication: even with unlimited data and well-separated clusters, random EM initialization can fail with high probability—careful initialization is critical.  
- Significance: moderate—resolves an open theoretical question but provides no new algorithms; overall recommendation: accept.  
- Evaluation summary: Quality = High; Clarity = High; Originality = Moderate; Significance = Moderate.
