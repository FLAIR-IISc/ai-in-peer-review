- Proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima for GMMs with M ≥ 3, resolving Srebro (2007).  
- Shows these bad maxima occur even in well-separated, isotropic settings with infinite data.  
- Demonstrates EM and a first-order variant converge to suboptimal critical points with high probability under random initialization, with failure probability scaling exponentially in M.  
- Identifies that failure of first-order methods is due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Distinguishes this work by rigorously characterizing the likelihood surface structure rather than focusing on rates or algorithm tweaks.  
- Technical arguments and proofs appear sound; paper is generally well written.  
- Major theoretical contribution: a significant negative result clarifying limitations of local search methods for GMMs.  
- Practical relevance of the constructed “bad” configurations is unclear and may be pathological rather than typical.  
- Recommends analyzing a distribution over true centers (e.g., uniform in a hypercube) to show how frequently bad configurations arise with larger M or d.  
- Questions whether random initialization is realistic; unclear if results hold under more informed initialization strategies used in practice.  
- Example in lines 74–81 about two components feels disconnected and needs clearer connection to Srebro’s conjecture.  
- Justification for requirement (1) (lines 233–235) is unclear—why wouldn’t a center initialized between clusters move toward the nearest cluster under EM?  
- Argues that proving EM avoids saddle points is interesting but less practically important than demonstrating convergence to local optima.  
- Lists several minor phrasing and notation issues at specific line numbers to be corrected.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, rigorous resolution of an open problem with important insights into EM limitations.  
- Rejection arguments: questionable practical relevance, uncertain applicability to realistic initializations, limited discussion of alternative algorithms or initializations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept contingent on addressing generalizability/practicality concerns and fixing clarity/notation issues.
