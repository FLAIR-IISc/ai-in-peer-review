- Paper refutes Srebro (2007) by constructing mixtures of spherical Gaussians where the population likelihood has bad local maxima that are arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Even for a well-separated three-component spherical GMM, the population likelihood can have spurious local maxima.  
- EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization from the mixture distribution converges to a suboptimal critical point with probability ≥ 1 - exp(-Ω(M)).  
- A first-order EM variant (gradient ascent) almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying failures are due to poor local maxima rather than saddles.  
- The results show EM can systematically fail to find the global MLE even with infinite data and favorable settings.  
- Strength: Addresses a fundamental open question and provides a definitive theoretical negative resolution, increasing understanding of GMM optimization landscapes.  
- Strength: Technically rigorous with a clear progression from structural to algorithmic results, and modern connection to non-convex optimization literature.  
- Strength: Well-written and organized, with clear motivation, formal setting, and intuitive theorem statements.  
- Weakness: The negative phenomena resemble well-known k-means failure modes (e.g., two close components and one far), a parallel the paper should explicitly discuss.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct comparison to provable algorithms (e.g., spectral methods, Kumar & Kannan) that succeed under similar separation regimes, which would contextualize EM’s failures.  
- Weakness: Constructions rely on very large separations; need discussion on whether bad local maxima persist under moderate, practically relevant separations.
