- Disproves Srebro (2007): local maxima in population likelihood for well-specified isotropic GMMs need not be global.
- Constructs explicit counterexamples showing arbitrarily bad local maxima exist (Theorem 1).
- Proves random initialization of EM is highly likely to converge to suboptimal critical points as component count increases (Theorem 2).
- Shows first-order EM avoids strict saddle points, so failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Emphasizes the need for careful initialization of EM even with infinite data and known scales.
- Strength: resolves a long-standing open problem with rigorous proofs and novel constructions.
- Strength: provides important insights into the geometry of GMM likelihood surfaces and EM dynamics.
- Weakness: practical relevance is unclear because the prevalence of the “bad” configurations in real data is not quantified.
- Weakness: analysis assumes random initialization, while practitioners often use data-driven inits (e.g., k-means) that may mitigate issues.
- Weakness: the example on lines 74–81 is unclear and does not convincingly support Srebro’s conjecture.
- Weakness: the necessity of requirement (1) in L233–235 is not justified and needs explanation.
- Weakness: several minor typos and phrasing issues reduce clarity.
