- Paper studies EM for estimating means in simplified GMMs (fixed components, uniform weights, identity covariances).
- Presents three negative theoretical results: existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima, high-probability convergence to suboptimal critical points from random init, and same issues for gradient EM.
- Refutes Srebro (2007) conjecture that population log-likelihood for GMMs lacks spurious local maxima, even in favorable settings.
- Main contribution: rigorous theoretical analysis resolving an open question and showing need for careful EM initialization.
- Authors justify simplified 1D setting due to technical proof complexity, but generalization to higher dimensions is not addressed.
- Scope limitation: results restricted to one-dimensional cases; unclear if they extend to higher dimensions.
- Proofs are mathematically dense and insightful but sometimes too brief or under-justified.
- Theorem 1 explanation is insufficient, particularly about likelihood supremums over unbounded domains.
- Claim that continuity of the likelihood implies certain convergence types needs more rigorous justification.
- Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; Equation (5) requires further clarification.
- Strengths: originality, significance, resolution of a longstanding open problem, and new proof techniques.
- Impact: findings likely to influence research on initialization strategies and alternative non-convex optimization methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical insights, practical importance, and introduction of useful proof methods.
- Arguments against acceptance: 1D restriction with inadequate generalization discussion, incomplete proof details (esp. Theorem 1), and unclear exposition in parts like gradient EM.
