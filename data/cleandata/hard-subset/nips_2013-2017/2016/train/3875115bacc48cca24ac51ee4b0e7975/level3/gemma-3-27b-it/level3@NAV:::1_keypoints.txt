- Main contribution: Constructive proof that EM can converge to suboptimal local maxima for GMMs even with well-separated components and infinite data, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Probabilistic result: Random initialization leads to bad critical points with probability exponentially small in the number of components.
- Nature of failures: Convergence failures are due to local maxima rather than saddle points.
- Theoretical grounding: Builds on and cites key prior work on identifiability, convergence rates, and EM analysis.
- Connection to clustering: Relates EM pitfalls to analogous issues in k-means and mentions k-log-k initialization strategies.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis and a clear counterexample to the conjecture.
- Strength: Demonstrates that saddle points are not the main obstacle for EM convergence.
- Weakness: Results are largely unsurprising to experts familiar with non-convex optimization and GMM estimation.
- Weakness: Analysis and constructions largely follow expected properties, limiting perceived novelty.
- Suggested improvement: Provide a direct comparison to the Kumar–Kannan framework to contrast EM guarantees with Lloyd’s algorithm under similar separation.
- Acceptance arguments: Resolves an open question, offers rigorous EM convergence analysis, and emphasizes importance of initialization.
- Rejection arguments: Findings are expected and do not substantially advance beyond confirming anticipated behavior.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept with a strong suggestion to address the Kumar–Kannan comparison in rebuttal.
