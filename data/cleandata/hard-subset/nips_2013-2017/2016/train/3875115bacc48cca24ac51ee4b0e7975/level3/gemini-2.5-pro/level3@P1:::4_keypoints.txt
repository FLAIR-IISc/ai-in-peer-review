- Paper challenges Srebro (2007) by studying population log-likelihood for equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated GMMs and shows the landscape can have bad local maxima.  
- Contribution 1: Constructs a simple 1D, three-component GMM with local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum, refuting the conjecture.  
- Contribution 2: Shows EM with standard random initialization can converge to such bad critical points with probability exponentially high in the number of components via a hierarchical mean construction.  
- Contribution 3: Proves a first-order EM (gradient-ascent equivalent) almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying failures stem from poor local maxima rather than saddles.  
- Significance: Resolves a long-standing open question, changing the view that infinite-data likelihoods are free of spurious local optima and motivating better initialization methods.  
- Originality/Technical depth: Introduces elegant geometric proofs, a clever hierarchical construction, and connects to recent results on gradient methods avoiding saddles.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and accessible with clear intuition before formal proofs.  
- Weakness 1: Main negative constructions are in one dimension (d=1), leaving open how they generalize to higher dimensions.  
- Weakness 2: The saddle-avoidance result applies only to first-order EM, not standard EM; extension barriers are not fully detailed.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—contributions are fundamental, technically novel, and the weaknesses are minor relative to the impact.
