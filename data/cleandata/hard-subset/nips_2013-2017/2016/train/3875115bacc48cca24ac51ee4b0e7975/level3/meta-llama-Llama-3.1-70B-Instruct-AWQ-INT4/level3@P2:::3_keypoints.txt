- Studies local maxima in GMM likelihood for M ≥ 3 and proves two main population-level results: bad local maxima exist even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians; and EM with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1−e^{−Ω(M)}.  
- Establishes that a first-order variant of EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points.  
- Resolves Srebro (2007) open question by showing bad local maxima can exist in idealized favorable settings.  
- Shows the probability of EM success with random initialization is exponentially small in M, requiring e^{Ω(M)} restarts for constant success probability.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction; technical proofs are delegated to appendices for readability.  
- Authors provide a detailed discussion of implications and related work.  
- Practical relevance is questioned because results rely on specific center configurations (well-spaced clusters), and it’s unclear how common these are in real data.  
- Reviewer requests analysis assuming a distribution on true centers to quantify how likely “bad” configurations are as k or d grow.  
- Concern that random-sampling initialization may be unrealistic for clearly clustered data; asks for discussion/analysis of other initialization schemes.  
- Some example and justification points are unclear (L74–81, L233–235) and may be unnecessary or need clarification.  
- Practical importance of the claim about EM not converging to saddle points is unclear (L267–273).  
- Notation and phrasing issues to fix: clarify “well-separated constant”, meaning of Ω(k), O(d^{1/2})-separation, and several minor wording/typo edits.  
- Pros for acceptance: resolves an open problem, clear exposition, readable main body, thorough discussion of implications.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear practical relevance, questionable initialization assumption, and minor writing/notation issues.  
- Overall rating 7/10 with recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
