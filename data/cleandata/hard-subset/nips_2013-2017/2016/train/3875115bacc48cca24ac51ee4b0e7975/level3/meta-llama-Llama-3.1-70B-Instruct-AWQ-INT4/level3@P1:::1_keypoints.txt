- Paper analyzes convergence of EM for Gaussian Mixture Models, focusing on the population likelihood function.  
- Shows population likelihood can have bad local maxima even for well-separated, spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007) open question.  
- Proves randomly initialized EM converges to a bad critical point with high probability; success probability is exponentially small in number of components.  
- Shows first-order EM also converges to bad local maxima with high probability and cannot converge to strict saddle points.  
- Implication: careful initialization is required for local-search methods even with infinite data and well-behaved mixtures.  
- Technical analysis is sound and thorough, with detailed study of the population likelihood landscape.  
- Results and techniques are not particularly surprising and follow expected lines.  
- Key example is a 3-component construction (two close clusters, one far) that yields expected misallocated local maxima.  
- Results relate to known failures of Lloyd’s k-means; authors suggest strategies like picking k log k centers or distance-squared sampling.  
- Suggestion for future work: compare EM to Kumar-Kannan algorithm and contrast EM vs Lloyd guarantees under large separation.  
- Overall conclusion: coherent demonstration that EM need not reach global maxima even with unlimited data.  
- Meta assessments: technically sound (quality), well-written (clarity), limited novelty (originality), but practically significant (significance).  
- Pros for acceptance: thorough analysis, clear exposition, significant practical implications.  
- Cons against acceptance: unsurprising results, non-novel key example, lacks comparison to alternative algorithms.
