- Main result: Proves that for M ≥ 3 equal-weight spherical GMMs the population likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima even for well-separated mixtures, resolving Srebro’s conjecture negatively.  
- Algorithmic consequence: Shows EM and a first-order (gradient) EM with natural random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).  
- Gradient EM dynamics: Proves gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures stem from bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Relation to prior work: Complements local-convergence analyses and alternative provable algorithms (clustering/moments) and connects to literature on nonconvex landscapes and saddle-avoidance dynamics.  
- Strength: Resolves a well-known open question and gives sharp, quantitative algorithmic failure probabilities for random initialization.  
- Strength: Clarifies that principled initialization is necessary even in favorable population settings.  
- Major weakness: Theorem 1 proof (lines 288–306) lacks rigor in exchanging limits/suprema over unbounded/parameter-dependent domains and needs compactness/continuity justification.  
- Requested fix (Theorem 1): Restrict to a compact neighborhood and apply the extreme value theorem, prove a strict likelihood gap on the boundary, then deduce an interior local maximum by continuity.  
- Requested fix (Theorem 1 alternatives): If using parameter-dependent feasible sets, justify continuity via Berge’s maximum theorem or establish coercivity/uniform convergence or provide dominated/epi-convergence arguments with measurability and compactness conditions.  
- Requested detail: Spell out exact neighborhoods, boundaries, and the continuity/compactness lemmas used in the proof.  
- Scope concern: Constructions/proofs appear in d = 1; explicitly state whether and how they extend to d > 1 (e.g., embedding along an axis or product constructions) and note any new issues (separation scaling, coordinate interactions).  
- Clarify gradient-EM exposition: Provide a self-contained derivation of Eq.(5), show how E-step weights yield the population M-step, and prove the first-order update µ ← µ + s E[wi(X)(X − µi)] is gradient ascent on the population likelihood (with step-size conditions).  
- Presentation request: Expand the full proof of Theorem 1 in the main text rather than relegating crucial details to the supplement.  
- Quality note: Technical direction is strong; the recursive clustering argument for e−Ω(M) failure probability is compelling but needs a slightly longer main-text sketch for verification.  
- Regularity note: State precise smoothness/regularity conditions verifying gradient-EM’s saddle-avoidance claim for the population objective.  
- Clarity suggestions: Improve exposition of first-order EM, add an M = 3 illustration figure, and fix reference/notation inconsistencies.  
- Originality: High — provides a new negative structural result and quantitative failure probability, resolving a known conjecture.  
- Significance: High — important theoretical and practical implications about EM and initialization strategies, with argued extensions to finite samples.  
- Pros for acceptance: Resolves an open problem, offers clear negative results with practical implications, and advances understanding beyond prior EM analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: Key proof steps in Theorem 1 need rigorous justification, dimension-generalization is unclear, and gradient-EM exposition has gaps.  
- Questions for authors: Provide a concise rigorous argument handling the limit-of-maxima steps (e.g., via compactness/Berge), state whether constructions/probability bounds extend to d > 1 and quantify separation requirements, and include a finite-sample corollary with explicit n-scaling.  
- Overall assessment: Support for acceptance contingent on strengthening Theorem 1’s proof and clarifying the gradient-EM exposition.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
