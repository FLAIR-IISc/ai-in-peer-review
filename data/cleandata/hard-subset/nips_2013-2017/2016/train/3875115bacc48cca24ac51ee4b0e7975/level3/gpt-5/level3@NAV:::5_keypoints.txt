- Main result: Constructs population-level counterexamples showing Srebro’s 2007 conjecture is false for GMMs with M ≥ 3 equal-weight spherical components.
- Methods analyzed: Studies likelihood-maximization via EM and gradient/first-order EM, with a practical “sample-from-data” random initialization.
- Failure probability: Proves EM and gradient EM converge to suboptimal critical points with probability 1 − e−Ω(M) under the analyzed random initialization.
- Saddle vs. bad maxima: Shows first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, implicating genuinely bad local maxima as the failure mode.
- Proof detail: Provides clear geometric intuition and a full, detailed proof in the simple case k = 3, d = 1.
- Technical approach: Uses a crisp recursive construction that underpins the exponentially small success probability.
- Relation to literature: Positions results relative to EM local-convergence analyses (e.g., Balakrishnan et al., 2015) and saddle-avoidance work (Pascanu et al., 2014; Lee et al., 2016).
- Quality assessment: Theoretical development appears careful and technically sound at the population level, though supplementary proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized; the M = 3, d = 1 geometric intuition is especially helpful.
- Suggested clarity improvement: Add a schematic of the recursive clustering used in Theorem 2 to aid comprehension.
- Originality/significance: Novelly resolves a long-standing open question and separates saddle points from bad local maxima, with clear implications for non-convex optimization and EM practice.
- Practical implications: Motivates principled initializations (e.g., method-of-moments or spectral pilots) over naive random starts.
- Requested additions: Provide estimates or explicit dependencies for constants (e.g., c in 1 − e−cM, separation parameters, Cgap), or include simulations illustrating finite-sample behavior if constants are loose.
- Requested analysis: Discuss performance under alternative initializations (e.g., k-means++) and comment on the open M = 2 case.
- Cons/limitations: Lacks concrete constant estimates and empirical illustration; results are population-level and rely on adversarial constructions, so guidance for finite-sample, practical M would help.
