- Paper studies EM for estimating means in Gaussian mixture models with known K, weights, and covariances.  
- Theorem 1: population log-likelihood can have local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum, resolving an open question of Srebro (2007).  
- Theorem 2: EM with random initialization converges to a bad critical point with high probability, even for well-specified models and large samples.  
- Theorem 3: First-order (gradient) EM also converges to a bad critical point with high probability under random initialization.  
- Analysis restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances), which the reviewer finds acceptable given technical difficulty.  
- Paper is well-written and results are informative, rigorous, and significant for understanding EM limitations.  
- Concern: Proof of Theorem 1 is too short; steps involving limits/suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains (lines 288–306) need stronger justification beyond continuity.  
- Reviewer suggests lengthening the main proof (even at expense of discussion) to convince readers.  
- Concern: Gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) needs clearer explanation.  
- Quality score: 8/10.  
- Clarity score: 8/10.  
- Originality score: 9/10.  
- Significance score: 9/10.  
- Pros for acceptance: significant insights into EM behavior, important limitations identified, generally rigorous and well-written.  
- Cons for acceptance: gaps in Theorem 1 proof, unclear gradient EM exposition, limited generality due to simplified model.
