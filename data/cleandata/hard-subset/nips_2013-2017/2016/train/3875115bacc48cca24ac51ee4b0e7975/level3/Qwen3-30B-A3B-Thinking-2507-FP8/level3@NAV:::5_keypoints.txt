- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question by showing bad local maxima exist for GMMs with M ≥ 3 components.  
- Constructs general counterexamples where local maxima can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Proves structural properties of GMM likelihood surfaces establishing existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima.  
- Shows EM and gradient EM with random initialization converge to these bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 - e^(-Ω(M)).  
- Demonstrates first-order EM almost surely does not converge to strict saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the primary failure mode.  
- Provides clear, well-organized exposition with an instructive detailed proof for the k=3, d=1 case.  
- Results are significant for understanding non-convex optimization in the population/asymptotic regime and impact practical use of EM.  
- Main weakness: no concrete estimates for constants in the probability bounds or supporting simulations to assess finite-M behavior.  
- Technically sound, original, well-contextualized within prior work (Srebro 2007; Balakrishnan et al. 2015).  
- Recommendation: accept.
