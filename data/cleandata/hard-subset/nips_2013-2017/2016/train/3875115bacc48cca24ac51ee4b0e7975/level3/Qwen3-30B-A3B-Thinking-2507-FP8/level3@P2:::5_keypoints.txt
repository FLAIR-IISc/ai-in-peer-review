- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture negatively: population likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima even for equal-weight, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, with arbitrarily worse log-likelihoods than global optima.
- Main contributions: (1) explicit counterexamples to Srebro’s conjecture for M ≥ 3; (2) proof that EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to these bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.
- First-order EM is shown to avoid strict saddle points almost surely, so poor convergence is due to bad local maxima rather than saddles.
- Work is well-motivated and resolves a long-standing open question, building on prior identifiability and EM analyses.
- Strength: provides clear, rigorous, technically sound proofs and intuitive geometric constructions (notably M=3, d=1).
- Strength: makes an important conceptual distinction between bad local maxima and saddle points with practical implications for initialization strategies.
- Weakness: probability bound constants (in 1 - e^{-cM}) are unspecified, limiting practical interpretation.
- Weakness: connection to finite-sample settings is mentioned but lacks concrete discussion or bounds.
- Weakness: no empirical validation or simulations to illustrate the theoretical phenomena for specific parameters.
- Quality: technically excellent, complete, and not merely a position paper.
- Clarity: exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible for expert readers.
- Originality: novel counterexamples and a significant conceptual contribution separating maxima from saddles.
- Significance: substantial impact on theory and practice of GMM estimation and non-convex optimization, affecting initialization design.
- Acceptance verdict: recommended for acceptance; minor limitations do not undermine core contributions.
