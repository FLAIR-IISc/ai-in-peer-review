- Addresses Srebro (2007) open question and resolves it negatively: well-separated GMMs can have bad local maxima.  
- Result 1: Constructs a 3-component, well-separated spherical GMM with local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Result 2: Shows EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to such bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)} for M ≥ 3.  
- Counterexample is intuitive (two close centers, one far) and mirrors known k-means failure modes.  
- Structural proofs are careful and rigorous, demonstrating bad maxima even for equally-weighted, spherical, well-separated mixtures.  
- First-order EM avoids strict saddle points almost surely, so failures stem from bad local maxima rather than saddles.  
- Algorithmic implication: random initialization fails with exponentially high probability, requiring exponentially many restarts to succeed with constant probability.  
- Highlights practical necessity of careful initialization and supports interest in alternative estimators (moment-based methods).  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and connects appropriately to prior work (Dasgupta & Schulman; Balakrishnan et al.).  
- Technical details are thorough and deferred to the appendix where appropriate.  
- Limitation: results and techniques are largely expected given non-convex optimization literature and not highly novel.  
- Recommendation: should include more explicit comparisons to Kumar-Kannan (2007) and Vempala-Wang (2002) and contrast with Lloyd's algorithm guarantees under strong separation.  
- Significance: provides rigorous, practically relevant limits on EM even with infinite data and favorable conditions.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with stronger contextualization within prior algorithmic GMM work.
