- Paper studies local maxima structure of the GMM log-likelihood (equal weights, identity covariance) and EM convergence, addressing Srebro [2007].
- Main contribution 1: Constructs a k=3, d=1 counterexample showing arbitrarily worse local maxima exist, disproving Srebro’s conjecture.
- Main contribution 2: Shows for certain mean configurations random initialization yields exponentially small probability (in k) of converging to a bad local maximum.
- Main contribution 3: Proves gradient EM almost surely avoids saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the failure mode for first-order methods.
- Strength: Technical soundness — proofs (notably Theorem 1) are rigorous and well-structured.
- Strength: Originality — novel link between mean configuration and existence of bad local maxima; hierarchical grouping for k>3 is clever.
- Strength: Significance — results advance theoretical understanding of clustering optima and may guide better EM initialization strategies.
- Strength: Clarity — paper is well-written, organized, and situates itself effectively in related work.
- Weakness: Counterexample limited to d=1, leaving extension to higher dimensions open and applicability uncertain.
- Weakness: Analysis of a specific random initialization scheme may not reflect the most common practical strategies.
- Weakness: Focus on the population setting; finite-sample/empirical process implications are underdeveloped.
- Accept arguments: Resolves an important open problem, technically strong, novel insights, and broad accessibility.
- Reject arguments: Limited dimensionality of counterexample and restricted initialization analysis may limit generalizability and practical impact.
- Overall: Strong paper with significant theoretical contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.
- Minor suggestions: Discuss implications for other optimization algorithms, comment on computational complexity of finding global optima, and clarify step size 's' role in first-order EM convergence.
