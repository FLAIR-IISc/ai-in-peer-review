- Resolves Srebro [2007] open problem by showing the population log-likelihood of a GMM can have bad local maxima (negative answer).
- Constructs a counterexample: a well-separated mixture of three spherical unit-variance Gaussians with a bad local maximum.
- Shows EM and its first-order variant can converge to bad critical points with exponentially high probability under random initialization.
- Implies any local search algorithm (including EM) cannot guarantee global convergence without careful initialization.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and balances technical detail with intuitive explanations.
- Provides thorough background on GMMs, EM, and first-order EM, making the work accessible.
- Main contributions: (1) negative answer to Srebro’s question, (2) rigorous analysis of EM’s failure modes.
- Detailed proofs and a geometric description of the M=3 construction are provided.
- High significance: results affect practical use of GMMs and motivate better initialization strategies and new algorithms.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques and an innovative counterexample.
- Weakness: lacks estimates of constants in probability bounds, limiting insight into practical phase transitions.
- Weakness: no supporting simulations presented to demonstrate the phenomenon (authors acknowledge this).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental question, thorough analysis, clarity, and significant implications.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing constant bounds and supporting simulations.
- Reviewer rating 9/10 with confidence 8/10.
