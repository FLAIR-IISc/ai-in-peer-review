- Resolves Srebro (2007) by showing the population log-likelihood for GMMs can have bad local maxima in well-behaved settings.  
- Main contribution: constructive proof that for any M ≥ 3 there exist well-separated, spherical, uniform GMMs with local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Algorithmic consequence: for the constructed GMMs, EM and gradient EM with random initialization from the true mixture converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e⁻ᶜᴹ.  
- Significance: a major theoretical result changing understanding of the optimization landscape for one of the foundational models in ML/statistics.  
- Quality: rigorous, technically strong analysis with a general class of counterexamples and an elegant link between bad maxima and EM failure.  
- Additional technical strength: shows gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, isolating bad local maxima as the main obstacle.  
- Clarity: manuscript is very well written and organized, with good balance of formal theorems and high-level intuition and an effective M=3, d=1 illustrative case.  
- Practical caveat: the asymptotic failure probability depends on an unspecified universal constant c, so practical relevance for finite M is unclear.  
- Recommendation: authors should clarify or provide bounds on c in the main text.  
- Alternative suggestion: if c is hard to derive, include simulations plotting failure probability vs M for the constructed GMMs to demonstrate practical impact.
