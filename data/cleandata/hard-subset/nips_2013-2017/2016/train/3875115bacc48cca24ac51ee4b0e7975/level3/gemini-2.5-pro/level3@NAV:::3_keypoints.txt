- Studies the population log-likelihood for isotropic GMMs with known variance in the well-specified setting.  
- Main contribution: negative resolution of Srebro (2007) conjecture — GMM population likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima for M≥3, even when components are well-separated.  
- Algorithmic consequence: shows EM with random initialization can converge to these suboptimal critical points with probability →1 as M increases for certain GMMs.  
- Strength: significant theoretical advance resolving an open conjecture with technically sound analysis and clear construction of bad instances.  
- Strength: writing is generally clear and the proofs/arguments are well-organized.  
- Important implication: underscores the fundamental need for careful initialization for EM and challenges the idea that large samples alone overcome nonconvexity.  
- Reservation: the constructed bad GMMs are highly specific (hierarchically separated clusters), so practical prevalence is unclear.  
- Suggestion: quantify the probability of such bad configurations under a reasonable prior on centers (e.g., dependence on M or dimension d).  
- Reservation: analysis assumes random initialization, which may not reflect practical initializations (e.g., k-means++), potentially overstating empirical failure modes.  
- Example concern: configurations like two close components and one far component seem unlikely to be problematic under typical data-based initialization.  
- Clarification needed: purpose of the k=2 example (L74–81) is unclear since it doesn’t appear to resolve the conjecture for k=2.  
- Clarification needed: in the proof sketch (L233–235), why is requirement (1) necessary—why wouldn’t a center initialized between clusters be pulled to the correct cluster?  
- Clarification needed: better motivate the practical importance of distinguishing convergence to a saddle point versus a local optimum (L267–273).  
- Minor issues: fix phrasing and notation (e.g., “well-separated constant” L218, definition of Ω(k) L225) and address listed typos.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution, but algorithmic/practical claims should be more carefully contextualized.
