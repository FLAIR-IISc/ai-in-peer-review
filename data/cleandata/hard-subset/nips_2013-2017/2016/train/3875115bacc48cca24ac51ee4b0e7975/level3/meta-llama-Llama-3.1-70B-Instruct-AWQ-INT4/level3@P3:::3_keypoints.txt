- Main contribution: proves arbitrarily bad local maxima can exist in well-specified isotropic GMMs (resolving Srebro 2007).
- Shows a constructed GMM where EM with random initialization misses the global optimum with high probability as components increase.
- Writing and organization are generally good; introduction, related work, and technical sections are clear.
- Proofs are clear, concise, and the paper is technically sound.
- Occasional typos and minor notation/phrasing issues (e.g., unclear "well-separated constant", undefined Ω(k)).
- Practical relevance is uncertain because results rely on a specific center configuration that may not reflect real-world data.
- Random initialization assumption may be unrealistic; results may not hold under other initialization schemes.
- The distinction between convergence to saddle points versus local optima is discussed but its practical importance is not clearly justified.
- Originality: provides a novel negative result with important theoretical implications for EM on GMMs.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with implications for EM usage, but applicability to practical settings is in question.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, interesting, well-written, and technically solid.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical relevance, strong assumptions on configuration and initialization, and minor presentation issues.
- Suggested improvements: generalize to more realistic settings, analyze other initialization schemes, clarify saddle vs local optimum relevance, and fix notation/typos.
