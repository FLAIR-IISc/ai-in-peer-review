- Paper constructs k ≥ 3, d = 1 Gaussian mixtures that settle Srebro’s 2007 conjecture by exhibiting arbitrarily bad local maxima of the population log-likelihood.
- Shows EM (and gradient/first-order EM) with random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability 1 − e^{-Ω(k)} for certain mean configurations via a hierarchical grouping argument.
- Proves gradient EM almost surely avoids strict saddle points, implying failures are due to poor local maxima rather than saddles.
- Main structural theorem (k = 3, d = 1 counterexample) is clean and argument appears sound; probabilistic failure result is plausible and well motivated.
- Step-size conditions and smoothness for the saddle-avoidance result are explained and align with recent gradient-dynamics theory.
- Paper is very well written with clear setup, intuition, and crisp explanation of the hierarchical construction.
- Originality is high: novel connection between specific mean configurations and prevalence of bad local maxima, and the hierarchical grouping construction for k > 3.
- Significance is high: clarifies when EM-style methods fail and motivates principled seeding and further theory on clustering optima.
- Suggestion: quantify separation/gap requirements and constants where possible.
- Suggestion: discuss the two-component case more fully (conjectured benign).
- Suggestion: provide finite-sample simulations comparing random vs. informed seeding (e.g., k-means++, spectral/moments).
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves notable open question, offers original construction and clear algorithmic implications, and is highly relevant to the community.
- Arguments against acceptance: some proofs deferred to appendix limiting immediate verification.
- Arguments against acceptance: scope restricted to equal weights/identity covariance and core counterexample in d = 1.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks empirical demonstrations to complement population-level results.
