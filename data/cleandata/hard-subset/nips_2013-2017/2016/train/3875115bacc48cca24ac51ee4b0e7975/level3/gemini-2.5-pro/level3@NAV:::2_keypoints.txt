- Paper studies population likelihood for GMMs with known components/weights/covariances and disproves Srebro (2007)’s conjecture.  
- Main contributions: (i) existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima even for well-separated components.  
- Main contributions: (ii) standard EM with random initialization can converge to these suboptimal critical points with high probability.  
- Main contributions: (iii) extension of the negative result to gradient-based EM.  
- Strength: addresses a fundamental question about EM with strong, counter-intuitive theoretical results that deepen understanding.  
- Simplification (e.g., identity covariances) is acceptable given technical depth and has practical implications for initialization strategies.  
- Major weakness: proof of Theorem 1 (lines 288–306) is too brief; limits/suprema over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains need much more rigorous justification.  
- Reviewer did not check supplementary material; main paper should be self-contained and Theorem 1 must be convincing there.  
- Issue: paper is written for general dimension d but proofs appear to be for d=1; authors should clarify extension to d>1 or discuss added technical difficulties.  
- Clarity issue: gradient EM section (lines 162–172) is hard to follow; derivation of Equation (5) needs clearer explanation.  
- Recommendation: expand the Theorem 1 argument even at the expense of other discussions.
