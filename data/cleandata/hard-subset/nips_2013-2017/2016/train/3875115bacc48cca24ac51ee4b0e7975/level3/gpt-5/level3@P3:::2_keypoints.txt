- Paper studies EM and gradient-EM for estimating means of equally-weighted, spherical GMMs and resolves Srebro (2007) by showing bad local maxima even for well-separated mixtures; random initialization leads to suboptimal convergence with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M), and gradient-EM almost surely avoids strict saddles so failures are due to bad local maxima.  
- Structural contribution: For M ≥ 3 the population log-likelihood can have local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global maximum.  
- Algorithmic contribution: With random initialization (sampling means from the mixture), EM and gradient-EM converge to suboptimal critical points w.h.p.; gradient-EM avoids strict saddles.  
- Practical implication: Succeeding from random initialization requires exponentially many restarts, highlighting the necessity of careful initialization even under favorable assumptions.  
- Strengths: Resolves a long-standing open question, uses a clean recursive construction yielding the e−Ω(M) failure probability, and clarifies that failures are due to bad maxima rather than saddles.  
- Scope limitation: Analysis assumes known M, uniform weights, identity covariances, and the main structural proof is carried out in one dimension; extensions to d>1 are not fully clarified.  
- Major technical concern: Theorem 1 proof (lines 288–306) relies on unjustified limit/suprema interchange over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains—continuity alone is insufficient; needs uniformity/compactness or explicit tail/control lemmas.  
- Clarity issue: Gradient-EM derivation (lines 162–172) is terse; the paper should explicitly derive Equation (5) from Q(θ′|θ) and its gradient.  
- Quality assessment: Results are important and largely sound, but the main text must include rigorous details for Theorem 1 (key lemmas) so readers need not consult the supplement.  
- Requests to authors: State whether the 1D construction extends to d>1 and note any new issues; provide explicit uniformity/compactness or epi-convergence arguments for Theorem 1; expand the gradient-EM derivation; discuss the necessity of M ≥ 3.  
- Recommendation: Accept if the authors substantially strengthen Theorem 1’s proof and clarify the gradient-EM derivation; overall high originality and significance.
