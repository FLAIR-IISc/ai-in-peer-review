This paper studies the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for estimating means in a Gaussian mixture model with known and fixed number of components, weights, and covariances. The authors provide three negative results: (i) local maxima with likelihood arbitrarily smaller than the global maximum can exist even for large samples; (ii) randomly initialized EM converges with high probability to such suboptimal critical points; and (iii) the same holds for gradient EM.

I find the question interesting, and the results informative and worthwhile for understanding EM in practice. The analysis is restricted to a simplified model (known K, uniform weights, identity covariances), which I deem acceptable given the technicality of the problem. However, I would like the authors to state whether extensions to more general cases are possible or to discuss additional difficulties that may arise.

The paper is well-written for general dimension d, but the proofs are given for d = 1. I request the authors to provide more details on whether extensions to d > 1 are possible or to discuss additional difficulties that may arise.

My major concern is that the proof of Theorem 1 is too short, and lines 288-306 need more detailed justification. The authors claim that the limits of supremums/maximums over unbounded or parameter-dependent domains converge, but continuity alone does not imply the claimed convergences. I suggest lengthening the main proof, even at the expense of discussion, to convince readers. I did not read the supplementary proofs, and I believe that including more details in the main proof would strengthen the paper.

A minor concern is that the gradient EM section (lines 162-172) is hard to follow, and I request more explanation of how Equation (5) is derived.

Overall, I believe that this paper provides important insights into the behavior of EM in practice and highlights the necessity of careful initialization. However, I would like to see more detailed proofs and explanations to strengthen the results.
