- Main contribution: Shows arbitrarily bad local maxima can exist for isotropic, known-scale, well-specified GMMs even in the infinite-sample limit, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Initialization result: Proves EM with random initialization converges to such suboptimal critical points with high probability as k grows.
- Technical quality: Theoretical results and proofs are rigorous and well-motivated, with strong analysis of first-order EM convergence.
- Clarity issues: Presentation occasionally unclear, some examples’ purposes are not immediately evident, and several steps need more elaboration.
- Originality: Novel resolution of a longstanding open question and new insights on random initialization’s impact and saddle-vs-local-maxima behavior.
- Significance: Raises important doubts about unconditional reliability of EM and emphasizes the critical role of initialization in GMM estimation.
- Practical relevance concern: Unclear whether the "bad" configurations occur commonly in real-world data; authors should quantify probability under a distribution on true centers.
- Initialization realism concern: Random initialization may be unrealistic; authors should analyze more practical schemes (e.g., k-means) and their susceptibility to failure.
- Example clarity concern (L74-81): The 1-D example’s purpose is unclear and does not convincingly settle Srebro’s conjecture for k=2.
- Requirement clarity (L233-235): Condition (1) is unclear and potentially unnecessary; authors should justify why centers initialized between clusters wouldn’t move correctly under EM.
- Saddle vs local optimum importance (L267-273): The practical implications of distinguishing saddle convergence from local optimum convergence are not well justified.
- Theoretical vs practical link: The connection between constructed theoretical examples and practical settings is not fully established and needs stronger motivation.
- Request for probabilistic result: Ask authors to show that probability of bad configurations increases with k or d under a natural prior on centers.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses open problem, rigorous theory, highlights initialization importance, deepens understanding of EM limitations.
- Arguments against acceptance: Questionable practical relevance, unrealistic random initialization assumption, some examples lack motivation/explanation.
- Phrasing/notation fixes: Multiple minor edits requested (L18, L38, L60, L138, L172, L177, L220, L245, L249, L308) to improve clarity and correctness.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying practical relevance, initialization discussion, and addressing the listed clarifications.
