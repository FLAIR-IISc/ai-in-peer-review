- Main contributions: proves (1) population likelihood has bad local maxima for M ≥ 3 even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians; (2) randomly initialized EM converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M).
- Result interpretation: findings are coherent but not surprising—behavior aligns with k-means-like behavior when clusters are well separated.
- Suggested comparison: paper should contrast results with k-means and Lloyd’s heuristic to contextualize EM’s limitations.
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis and variants study of EM with clear presentation.
- Strength: detailed, helpful proofs that support the theorems.
- Weakness: limited scope—only treats Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3.
- Weakness: lacks empirical or theoretical comparison to other clustering algorithms.
- Quality: technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written; claims are well-supported.
- Originality: presents new population-likelihood results but builds on existing EM literature.
- Significance: important practical implication that EM needs careful initialization even in large-sample settings.
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address scope and comparison limitations in future work.
