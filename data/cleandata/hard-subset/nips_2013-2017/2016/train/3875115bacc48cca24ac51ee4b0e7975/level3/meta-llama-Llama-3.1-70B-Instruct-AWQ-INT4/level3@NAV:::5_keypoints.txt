- Paper proves two fundamental results for population likelihood of GMMs with M≥3: existence of bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Paper shows EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1−e−Ω(M).  
- Resolves an open question of Srebro (2007), providing a negative answer with theoretical guarantees.  
- Paper is well-written and balances space with intuitive explanations and a detailed k=3, d=1 example to build intuition.  
- Results are important for understanding non-convex optimization in the asymptotic limit.  
- Technical contributions are sound, claims are supported by theoretical analysis, and related work is adequately cited.  
- Authors provide enough detail for an expert reader to reproduce results.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques and significant departure from previous contributions.  
- Reviewer did not fully check supplementary material, leaving some verification incomplete.  
- Paper lacks explicit estimates of constants in probability bounds, making practical phase-transition thresholds unclear.  
- Absence of simulations to illustrate phenomenon and validate constants could weaken practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept, as contributions are important and weaknesses can be addressed in the final version.
