- Proves Srebro’s conjecture: for M ≥ 3 there exist well-separated 1D spherical, equal-weight GMMs with arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood.
- Shows EM and gradient EM with random center initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^−Ω(M) via a recursive clustering construction.
- Proves gradient EM (with suitable step size) almost surely avoids strict saddles, implicating bad local maxima rather than saddles as the primary failure mode.
- Complements prior positive local-convergence and method-of-moments results by demonstrating fundamental global landscape obstructions even in benign settings.
- Strength: resolves a visible open problem and sharpens understanding of GMM likelihood landscapes in the spherical, equal-weight case.
- Strength: provides a clean, insightful M = 3 construction (two close centers, one far) and a robust proof strategy excluding the global optimum from a neighborhood.
- Strength: clear algorithmic implication that random-initialized EM/gradient EM fail with exponentially high probability in M.
- Strength: conceptually important distinction between saddle avoidance and attraction to bad local maxima for first-order EM methods.
- Strength: population-level results likely transfer to large finite samples via standard empirical-process techniques.
- Weakness: lacks explicit finite-sample (population-to-sample) uniform deviation bounds to quantify sample size n needed for the failures to manifest with high probability.
- Weakness: insufficient quantification of how the suboptimality gap scales with separation and dimension; needs explicit center choices and gap dependence (Cgap).
- Weakness: does not analyze common practical initializations (k-means++, spectral/moment pilots, pruning/over-seeding) to see if they mitigate the recursive failure.
- Weakness: needs clarification of regularity conditions for the almost-sure saddle-avoidance claim and whether the update map is a diffeomorphism.
- Weakness: scope limited to equal weights and identity covariances; discussion on extension to unequal weights/diagonal/full covariances is brief.
- Weakness: lacks numerical experiments/figures (e.g., 1D M=3 likelihood surface and EM trajectories) to aid intuition.
- Quality: theorems are clear and well-motivated, but would benefit from explicit constants/parameters and finite-sample translations.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with helpful geometric intuition; needs more explicit constructions/figures and step-size/smoothness clarifications.
- Originality: high — answers an open question and introduces a novel recursive failure construction for EM.
- Significance: high — shows that random-initialized EM can fail even in benign regimes, with practical and theoretical implications.
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves a notable conjecture, provides strong negative results, and clarifies the role of local maxima vs. saddles.
- Arguments against acceptance: results are population-level without full finite-sample instantiation and omit analysis of practical initialization schemes and experiments.
- Minor: clarify coexistence of “well-separated” with a close pair of centers; state constants (e.g., c in 1 − e^−cM) and dependence on separation; fix reference/citation formatting.
