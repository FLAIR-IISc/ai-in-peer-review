- Main result 1: Shows population log-likelihood for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical GMMs (M ≥ 3) has bad local maxima whose log-likelihood can be arbitrarily worse than any global optimum, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Main result 2: Proves EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.
- Paper also shows first-order EM cannot converge to strict saddle points and that suboptimal fixed points persist unless step-size is very large.
- Theoretical proofs are technical and require deep understanding of the population likelihood and EM algorithm.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized with a thorough related-work review.
- No empirical experiments are provided to demonstrate practical implications of the theoretical results.
- Clarity: 9/10 — exposition is concise and readable.
- Quality: 8/10 — claims are technically sound and well-supported, but proofs are complex.
- Originality: 8/10 — meaningful contribution but builds on existing work rather than introducing entirely new ideas.
- Significance: 9/10 — results have important practical implications for using EM on GMMs.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, interesting/important results, strong exposition.
- Arguments for rejection: highly technical proofs that may be hard to follow, lack of empirical validation, and limited novelty.
