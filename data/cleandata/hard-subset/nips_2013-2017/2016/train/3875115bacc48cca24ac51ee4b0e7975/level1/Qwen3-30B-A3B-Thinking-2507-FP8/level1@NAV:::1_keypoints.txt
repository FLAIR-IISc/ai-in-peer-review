- Resolves Srebro (2007) by constructing counterexamples showing spherical GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population likelihood.  
- Shows EM and its first-order variant with random initialization converge to such bad local maxima with probability ≥ 1 - e^{-Ω(M)}.  
- Distinguishes bad local maxima from saddle points; Theorem 4 proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles.  
- Technical quality is high, with rigorous proofs for both structural and algorithmic claims.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with enough mathematical detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality is strong: extends prior work beyond two-component mixtures to resolve a longstanding open question.  
- Significance is high due to direct implications for practitioners using EM on GMMs.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical contribution, clear maxima vs. saddle analysis, and practical relevance.  
- Weakness: analysis restricted to idealized spherical Gaussians with fixed variances, limiting broader applicability.  
- Could better relate findings to recent non-convex optimization literature (e.g., Lee et al., 2016).  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper that meets NIPS criteria and should be accepted.
