- Studies population likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 equally weighted spherical components and presents two main theoretical results.  
- Main result 1: Disproves Srebro’s 2007 conjecture by constructing well-separated mixtures whose population log-likelihood has spurious local maxima with arbitrarily large suboptimality gaps.  
- Main result 2: Shows EM and a first-order/gradient-ascent variant with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^{-Ω(M)}.  
- Shows first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, indicating bad local maxima—not saddles—are the main algorithmic obstacle.  
- Situates work within prior NIPS literature on nonconvex optimization, EM convergence, separation-based and moment methods, and gradient dynamics.
