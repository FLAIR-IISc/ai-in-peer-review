- Main result 1: The population likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 components has bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007)’s open question.  
- Main result 2: EM (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability at least 1−e^{−Ω(M)}, so random restarts/local search can fail with high probability.  
- Practical implication: Results emphasize the necessity of careful initialization when using EM in practice.  
- Contribution: Provides a novel construction of a GMM with bad local maxima and rigorous structural results about the likelihood landscape.  
- Theoretical significance: Advances understanding of GMMs and algorithmic consequences for EM and its variants.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, with clear background, logical presentation, and detailed proofs.  
- Originality: Results are novel and not a straightforward extension of prior work.  
- Weakness: Analysis is limited to the population setting; finite-sample generalization is unclear.  
- Mitigation: Authors discuss possible extensions to finite samples via empirical process arguments, but more detail would help.  
- Additional critique: Could provide more discussion on practical implications for using EM in real-world settings.  
- Overall verdict: Excellent paper, significant contribution and recommended for acceptance.
