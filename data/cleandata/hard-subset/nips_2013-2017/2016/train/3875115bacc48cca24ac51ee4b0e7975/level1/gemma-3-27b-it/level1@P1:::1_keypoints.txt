- Main result: proves existence of bad local maxima in the GMM likelihood for M ≥ 3, refuting Srebro (2007).
- EM convergence: shows EM with random initialization is likely to converge to these suboptimal points.
- Theoretical strength: results are mathematically rigorous with detailed proofs.
- Significance: resolves an important open problem about the GMM likelihood landscape.
- Practical implication: cautions against naive use of EM and random initialization in practice.
- Literature positioning: builds on identifiability, convergence-rate, and EM-analysis literature and connects to non-convex optimization.
- Novelty: introduces new techniques for analyzing the population log-likelihood relevant to other non-convex problems.
- Presentation: well-written, well-organized, and effectively motivated.

- Strength — algorithmic: clear implications for algorithm behavior and initialization concerns.
