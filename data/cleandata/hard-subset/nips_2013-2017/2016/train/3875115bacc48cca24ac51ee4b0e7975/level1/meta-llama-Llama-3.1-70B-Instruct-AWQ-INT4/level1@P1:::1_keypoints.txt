- Proves population likelihood for Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical components.  
- Shows EM (and first-order variants) with random initialization can converge to bad critical points with high probability.  
- Highlights practical implication: careful initialization of EM is necessary even in favorable settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and relates results to prior work.  
- Provides detailed, well-organized proofs and technical explanations in the appendix.  
- Resolves an open question of Srebro (2007) by giving a negative answer about existence of bad local maxima.  
- Offers clear intuition and detailed analysis of EM behavior and its variants.  
- Limited empirical validation: only some simulations in the appendix and lacks extensive experiments.  
- Model assumptions are restrictive (equally-weighted components, spherical covariances), raising questions about generalization.  
- Technically sound and likely to interest researchers in machine learning and statistics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, important practical implications, clear exposition, and strong proofs.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient empirical support, restrictive modeling assumptions, and uncertain generalizability to more complex settings.
