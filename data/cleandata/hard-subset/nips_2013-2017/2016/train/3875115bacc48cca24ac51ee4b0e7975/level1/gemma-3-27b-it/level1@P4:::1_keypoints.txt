- Paper disproves Srebro’s conjecture: population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima.
- Shows EM with random initialization converges to these suboptimal local maxima with high probability.
- Proves first-order EM avoids saddle points, implicating bad local maxima as the main failure mode.
- Focuses analysis on equally-weighted, spherical Gaussians (specific model setting).
- Technical contributions are novel, rigorous, and well-positioned within prior GMM literature.
- Theorems and proofs are clearly stated and mathematically well-supported.
- Results have practical implications: EM requires careful initialization to avoid poor solutions.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation.
- Limitation: restricted model scope may limit generalizability to unequal weights, anisotropic covariances, or higher dimensions.
- The construction used in Theorem 2 feels contrived; real-world likelihood of such configurations is unclear.
- Theorem 3’s step-size requirement (0,1) for first-order EM may be overly restrictive in practice.
- Paper lacks discussion and comparison of practical initialization strategies (e.g., k-means++).
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves a long-standing open problem, technically strong, practically relevant, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited model scope and uncertain direct impact on real-world performance without practical initialization work.
- Overall assessment: strong paper deserving acceptance, with recommended revisions to discuss generalizability and practical initialization avenues.
