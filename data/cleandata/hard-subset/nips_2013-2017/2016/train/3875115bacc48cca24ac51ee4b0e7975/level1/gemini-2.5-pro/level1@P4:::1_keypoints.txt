- Paper resolves Srebro (2007) conjecture about the absence of bad local maxima in well-behaved GMMs by giving a definitive negative answer.  
- Theorem 1: For any M ≥ 3, there exist well-separated, uniform, spherical GMMs whose population log-likelihood has local maxima arbitrarily worse than the global optimum.  
- Construction for Theorem 1 uses a clustered configuration of true Gaussian means and analysis on the boundary of a specific neighborhood.  
- Theorems 2 & 3: EM and a first-order (gradient-ascent) variant with standard random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability at least 1 − e^(−cM).  
- Implication: Exponentially many random restarts are required to find the global optimum, making naive random initialization computationally intractable for large M.  
- The exponential failure probability is established via a clever recursive construction of the true GMM centers.  
- Theorem 4: First-order EM converges to strict saddle points with probability zero, so failures are due to poor local maxima rather than saddle proliferation.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Proofs are well-motivated, rigorous, and link structural and algorithmic results coherently.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written; main results are precisely stated with intuitive proof sketches.  
- Strength — Originality: Resolves a decade-old open problem and introduces novel constructions and analyses.  
- Significance — Theory: Alters fundamental understanding of the GMM likelihood surface, showing it can be treacherous even in idealized settings.  
- Significance — Practice: Provides rigorous evidence that naive EM initialization is insufficient, motivating advanced initialization methods.  
- Weakness: Analysis is on the population likelihood; formal finite-sample extensions are claimed but not detailed.  
- Weakness: Constructions are stylized; the prevalence of such pathological landscapes in naturally occurring GMMs is not addressed.  
- Recommendation summary: Landmark, high-impact theoretical paper with minor omissions; strengths strongly favor acceptance despite stylized constructions.
