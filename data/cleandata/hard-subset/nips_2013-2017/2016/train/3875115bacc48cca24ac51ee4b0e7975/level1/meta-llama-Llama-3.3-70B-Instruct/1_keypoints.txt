- Main result 1: Population likelihood of GMMs with ≥3 components can have bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007).
- Main result 2: EM and its first-order variant with random initialization converge to bad critical points with high probability, emphasizing need for careful initialization.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough introduction and motivation.
- Technical contribution: detailed analysis of the population log-likelihood with new proof techniques for non-convex structure.
- Strength: Resolves an open theoretical question with significant implications for EM in practice.
- Strength: Introduces broadly applicable new techniques for analyzing population log-likelihood.
- Strength: Clear and thorough presentation makes results accessible to many readers.
- Weakness: Assumes equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Weakness: No experimental validation provided to support theoretical findings.
- Evaluation: High marks for quality, originality, and significance; lower score on clarity due to technical difficulty for non-experts.
- Recommendation: Accept for publication based on significant and original contributions.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: resolution of open question; new analytical techniques; clear presentation.
- Con-acceptance arguments: restrictive modeling assumptions; lack of experimental results.
