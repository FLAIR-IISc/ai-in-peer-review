- Resolves Srebro [2007] open problem by showing population likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have bad local maxima even for equally weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Proves EM and first-order EM with random initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with high probability, highlighting the need for careful initialization.  
- Shows existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima for M ≥ 3, contradicting the conjecture that population local maxima are global.  
- Introduces novel analytical techniques for studying the structure of the population likelihood and recursive constructions.  
- Demonstrates exponential difficulty of achieving global convergence from random initializations, complementing prior initialization research.  
- Theorems are clearly stated and implications for theory and practice are well-articulated.  
- Findings have broader relevance to understanding non-convex optimization landscapes beyond GMMs.  
- Lacks empirical experiments to illustrate practical impact and finite-sample failure probabilities.  
- Analysis is restricted to uniformly weighted, spherical Gaussians; generalization to non-uniform or anisotropic mixtures is unclear.  
- Emphasizes need for better initialization but does not propose or evaluate alternative initialization schemes.  
- Some proofs (notably Theorem 2’s recursive arguments) are highly technical and may be inaccessible to a broader audience.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, relevance to ML/statistics, and practical insights on EM limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical validation, limited scope, and no concrete mitigation strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to add empirical results and discuss extensions to broader GMM settings in future work.
