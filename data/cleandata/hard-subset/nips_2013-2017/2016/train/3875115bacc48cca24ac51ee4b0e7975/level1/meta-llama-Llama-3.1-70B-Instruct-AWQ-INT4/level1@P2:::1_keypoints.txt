- Main theoretical result 1: Population log-likelihood for Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3 has bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, and these can be arbitrarily worse than the global optimum (resolves Srebro 2007 open question).

- Main theoretical result 2: The EM algorithm (and a first-order variant) with random initialization converges to bad critical points with probability at least 1 − e^{−Ω(M)}.

- Significance: Results show fundamental limitations of EM for GMMs even in idealized settings, with important implications for practitioners.

- Technical quality: Results are technically sound and rigorously proven; proofs are provided (relegated to appendices).

- Presentation: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible in the main body; complex arguments are broken down effectively.

- Accessibility caveat: The paper assumes substantial technical background in ML/statistics, which may limit accessibility for some readers.

- Missing discussion: Could benefit from more discussion of practical implications and how the results affect EM usage in other contexts.

- Contribution: Provides significant new insights into EM behavior and resolves a longstanding open problem.
