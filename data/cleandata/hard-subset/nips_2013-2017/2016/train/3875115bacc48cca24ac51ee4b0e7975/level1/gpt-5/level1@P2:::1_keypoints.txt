- Proves existence of arbitrarily bad local maxima in the population log-likelihood of GMMs with M ≥ 3 equal-weight spherical components, resolving Srebro’s conjecture.  
- Construction is explicit in 1D: place components to create neighborhoods with strictly lower boundary likelihood, guaranteeing a suboptimal local maximum.  
- Shows EM and first-order EM with random-from-data initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e^−Ω(M) on the constructed instances.  
- Proves first-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Complements prior analyses that require good initialization and strengthens finite-sample constructions by working at the population level.  
- Theorems are clearly stated and proof strategies (neighborhood argument, recursive multi-level clustering) are plausible and well-motivated.  
- Probability bound uses an exponential number of favorable allocation events and a trapping argument under large separation consistent with EM’s soft assignments.  
- Adapts dynamical-systems/saddle-avoidance results while avoiding a blanket smoothness assumption via EM gradient structure.  
- Results assume population setting, equal weights, known identical covariances, and well-separated means; finite-sample implications are claimed but not quantified.  
- Bad-local-maxima construction is presented in 1D; embedding into higher dimensions is mentioned but not explicitly detailed.  
- Gradient-EM step-size restriction s ∈ (0,1) is used; sensitivity to larger step sizes (e.g., overshooting) is not discussed.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized; suggests adding a figure for the hierarchical cluster construction and explicit separation scales for reproducibility.  
- Originality is high: resolves a notable conjecture and provides new lower bounds on success probability of common heuristics.  
- Conceptual significance is strong: demonstrates EM can fail badly even in favorable population settings, motivating principled initializers.  
- Pros: resolves conjecture, gives sharp quantitative failure probability, clarifies saddle vs local-max failure modes, and has clear exposition.  
- Cons/suggestions: provide finite-sample theorems, explicit multidimensional embedding details, discussion of alternative initializers (k-means++, spectral/moment), and more on the M=2 case.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggested minor improvements on finite-sample and dimensionality issues.
