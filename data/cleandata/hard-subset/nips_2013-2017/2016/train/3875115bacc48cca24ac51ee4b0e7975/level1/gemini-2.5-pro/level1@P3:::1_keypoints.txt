- Resolves Srebro’s conjecture negatively: for M ≥ 3, the population GMM log-likelihood can have arbitrarily bad local maxima, even for well-separated, spherical, uniform Gaussians.  
- Algorithmic consequence: EM and first-order EM with standard random initialization will hit sub-optimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M), implying exponentially many restarts are needed.  
- Nature of failures: First-order EM almost surely avoids strict saddles, indicating entrapment in genuine bad local maxima rather than saddle slowdowns.  
- Significance/impact: Provides a definitive negative answer to a fundamental open question and challenges the belief that large data resolves GMM estimation issues.  
- Originality/technical quality: Novel, insightful proofs and a clever geometric/recursive construction; leverages dynamical systems results effectively.  
- Clarity/organization: Exceptionally well-written with clear exposition, intuition, and logical structure.  
- Population vs finite-sample: Analysis is population-only; the paper lacks detailed discussion or bounds on sample complexity n for these phenomena to appear in practice.  
- Generality of construction: The counterexample is specific, leaving unclear how prevalent such pathological likelihood landscapes are.  
- Gap between EM and first-order EM: Theorem 4 holds for gradient ascent only; the analogous claim for standard EM is conjectured but unproven and should be stated explicitly.  
- M=2 case unresolved: The M=2 setting is left open; the paper should provide intuition why the M ≥ 3 construction fails for two components.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: Technically strong and important work with minor clarifications needed; strongly recommended for acceptance.
