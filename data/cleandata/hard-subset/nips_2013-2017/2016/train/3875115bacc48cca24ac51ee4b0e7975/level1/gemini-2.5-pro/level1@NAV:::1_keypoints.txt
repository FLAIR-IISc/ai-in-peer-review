- Presents fundamental analysis of the population log-likelihood surface for Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs).  
- Shows that for M ≥ 3 components, the likelihood contains bad local maxima even for well-separated spherical Gaussians, resolving Srebro (2007) conjecture.  
- Proves EM with a common random initialization converges to suboptimal critical points with probability approaching 1 exponentially in M.  
- Builds on prior local-convergence EM analyses and contrasts with methods offering global guarantees.  
- Significance: solves an open problem and clarifies the GMM likelihood landscape.  
- Originality: constructive counterexamples and proofs demonstrating pathological instances are insightful.  
- Quality: technically sound results with clear proof sketches and strong supporting arguments.  
- Additional insight: a first-order EM variant avoids strict saddle points, implying poor local maxima are the primary algorithmic barrier.  
- Weakness: analysis is limited to the population setting; a brief discussion of finite-sample/sample complexity effects would help.
