- Addresses whether bad local maxima exist in the population likelihood for GMMs and shows they do, countering Srebro (2007).  
- Demonstrates bad local maxima can occur even for well-separated mixtures.  
- Proves EM, with random initialization, is likely to converge to these suboptimal points.  
- Provides a clever, rigorous construction of a 3-component GMM exhibiting a bad local maximum.  
- Shows EM typically does not converge to strict saddle points, isolating bad local maxima as the main issue.  
- Uses dynamical systems techniques (notably in Theorem 4) to support convergence claims.  
- Situates the work well in the literature with a comprehensive related-work section.  
- Mathematical arguments and proofs are sound and rigorous.  
- Presentation is dense and notation can be cumbersome, making the paper hard to follow.  
- Lacks numerical experiments or visualizations to build intuition and empirically validate claims.  
- Restricts to equally weighted, isotropic Gaussians, limiting generality to real-world settings with unequal weights/covariances.  
- The claim that success probability is exponentially small in M is strong; the dependence constant c is not quantified.  
- Offers little concrete guidance or discussion of practical remedies or alternative initialization strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on improving clarity, adding empirical validation, and discussing extensions/remedies.
