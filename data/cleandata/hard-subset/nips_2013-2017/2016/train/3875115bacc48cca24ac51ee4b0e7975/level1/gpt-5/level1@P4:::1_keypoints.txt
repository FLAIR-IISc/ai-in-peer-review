- Main result: Population log-likelihood for GMMs with M ≥ 3 can have arbitrarily bad suboptimal local maxima, refuting Srebro (2007).
- Construction: Explicit d=1 example (M=3) merges two close centers and duplicates a far one to create a strict local maximum with large likelihood gap.
- Algorithmic consequence: EM and gradient EM with random i.i.d. initialization converge to suboptimal critical points with probability ≥ 1 − e−Ω(M) on constructed instances.
- Proof idea for algorithms: Recursive hierarchical clustering of means causes imbalance in random seed assignments at some level, trapping dynamics in bad basins; success needs exponentially unlikely perfectly balanced seeds.
- Saddle vs maxima: Gradient EM with constant stepsize s∈(0,1) almost surely avoids strict saddles, so failures come from bad local maxima.
- Contrast with prior work: Results differ from settings showing no poor local optima and complement analyses that require good initialization methods.
- Focus/scope: Results are for the population likelihood under equal weights and known spherical covariances, isolating geometric nonconvexity.
- Technique novelty: Uses barrier neighborhood arguments and hierarchical constructions that may transfer to other nonconvex likelihoods.
- Limitation: Restricted to population setting, d=1 constructions, equal weights, and known covariance; finite-sample extensions are not detailed.
- Weakness: “Well-separated” conditions and exact constants/scaling with M and d are not fully specified; practical prevalence of bad gaps is unquantified.
- Weakness: Negative instances are carefully designed; paper does not delineate regimes where random initialization still succeeds (e.g., M=2 or other geometries).
- Empirical gap: No simulations or empirical illustrations to demonstrate EM behavior on the constructed instances.
- Proof/assumption note: Avoidance of strict saddles is plausible from smoothness/analyticity of L; paper could simplify by citing analyticity.
- Quality: Theorems appear well-motivated and technically sound; proof strategies (barrier, hierarchical trapping, dynamical systems) are appropriate.
- Suggested clarity improvements: State precise separation constants, explicit compact parameter set for finite-sample transfer, and separate initialization/sample randomness.
- Presentation fixes: Add a figure for the hierarchical construction and fix minor reference formatting.
- Originality: Novel resolution of a longstanding conjecture and new exponential failure probability result for random initialization.
- Significance: High — clarifies fundamental failure modes of EM and motivates principled initialization methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: Resolves open problem, provides quantitative algorithmic consequences, and separates saddle vs maxima failure modes.
- Arguments against acceptance: Population-level and 1D focus, lack of finite-sample detail and empirical validation, and instance-specific negative results limiting practical guidance.
- Suggested improvements: Add simulations of EM trajectories, provide explicit constants/separation requirements, outline finite-sample extension with compactness and uniform convergence, and discuss higher-dimensional/general-M=2 status.
