- Investigates continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics and generalizes Accelerated Mirror Descent via two weight functions w and η.
- Provides sufficient conditions and a Lyapunov function to guarantee a 1/r(t) convergence rate for a user-defined rate r(t).
- Presents a primal formulation and applies the framework to constrained sets, deriving an accelerated replicator dynamic on the simplex.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that monitors the Lyapunov derivative to preserve convergence rate while improving practical performance.
- Supports the heuristic with preliminary numerical experiments showing improvements over existing methods.
- Directly builds on Su et al. and Krichene et al. by expanding the family of ODEs linked to Nesterov acceleration.
- Extends existing restarting heuristics with a new adaptive strategy and provides theoretical guarantees absent in prior work.
- Notes connections to replicator dynamics and viability theory, situating the work in an interdisciplinary context.
- Strength: meaningful generalization of the AMD framework offering greater flexibility and potential power.
- Strength: solid Lyapunov-based theoretical analysis with clearly stated sufficient conditions for convergence.
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is a significant, principled contribution with theoretical rate preservation.
- Strength: primal representation and concrete examples (e.g., simplex) increase practical relevance.
- Strength: numerical validation and generally clear, well-organized writing.
- Weakness: numerical experiments are limited in scope and need broader datasets, problem types, and rigorous statistical baselines.
- Weakness: discretization details for the adaptive heuristic (Algorithm 1), and sensitivity to step size s and parameter β, lack sufficient justification and analysis.
- Weakness: potential implementation complexity vs. simpler methods (e.g., fixed-step restarts) is not fully discussed.
- Weakness: convergence guarantees tied to Lyapunov decrease do not ensure convergence to a minimizer in multi-solution cases; discussion is limited.
- Weakness: limited discussion of relationships and differences with other adaptive methods like Adagrad and Adam, and interaction with momentum-based adaptivity.
- For acceptance: novel generalization, promising adaptive heuristic, clear exposition, and interesting replicator dynamics link.
- Against acceptance: insufficient empirical evaluation, possible implementation overhead, and need for more detailed comparisons to adaptive optimizers.
- Overall: strong paper with solid theory and a promising heuristic; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand numerical evaluation and statistical comparisons; justify discretization and analyze sensitivity of s and β; discuss implementation complexity and trade-offs; elaborate on differences/synergies with Adagrad, Adam, and other adaptive methods.
