- Studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with a two-parameter family AMD_{w,η} where primal is an average of a dual trajectory; analyzes via Lyapunov L_r and energy E_r.  
- Gives simple sufficient conditions on w, η to ensure d/dt L_r ≤ 0 and hence f(X(t))−f* = O(1/r(t)); E_r also shown to decrease.  
- Generalizes prior ODE views on Nesterov acceleration and recovers classical t^2 and t^p (p≥1) rates with explicit w, η choices.  
- Derives equivalent primal forms and examples (positive orthant, simplex), producing an “accelerated replicator” linking acceleration to evolutionary dynamics.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic (choose a(t)=w/W) motivated by minimizing the Lyapunov derivative; offers a discrete Algorithm 1 and experiments.  
- Theory quality: continuous-time derivations, Lyapunov argument, and existence/uniqueness proofs are sound; E_r is a useful invariant.  
- Generality vs checkability: one key condition is trajectory-dependent and hard to verify a priori; corollary provides a convenient, checkable choice (η = r a and a ≥ r′/r).  
- Discretization concerns: adaptive averaging justified in continuous time but discrete algorithm’s theoretical guarantees are deferred to supplement and Algorithm 1 has specification ambiguities.  
- Empirics: clean visualizations and multiple convex objectives, but limited to low-dimensional simplex tasks; step-size/parameter choice and sensitivity not discussed; baselines limited.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and organized; primal forms and examples aid intuition.  
- Notational/typo issues: Example 1 typo (a(t) should be β/t), λ_k formula in Algorithm 1 is incorrect, γ undefined in Step 4, role/assumptions on R unclear, several incomplete references.  
- Mapping gap: discrete heuristic’s connection to continuous-time condition ⟨∇f(X),Ẋ⟩(r−ηW/w)≤0 is discussed qualitatively but not made explicit in discrete form.  
- Originality: generalized AMD family and arbitrary-r Lyapunov template are a useful synthesis; adaptive averaging appears novel in AMD context and distinct from restarts/Adagrad/Adam.  
- Novel perspectives: accelerated replicator dynamics and energy interpretation link optimization with evolutionary dynamics.  
- Significance: unifying framework and practical adaptive heuristic with monotone Lyapunov decrease; broader impact depends on further empirical validation (higher dimensions, other constraint sets, stochastic settings).  
- Pros: clean, general Lyapunov-based O(1/r(t)) analysis; conceptually grounded adaptive averaging; concrete primal examples; monotone energy; empirical gains vs restarts.  
- Cons: missing discrete-time theory in main text; ambiguous Algorithm 1; limited experimental scope and baselines; trajectory-dependent key condition; typos/notation issues.  
- Requested additions: state discrete Lyapunov and convergence rate for Algorithm 1 in main paper; clarify assumptions on ψ, R, step sizes; bound discrete analogue of ⟨∇f,Δx⟩; discuss sensitivity to β and s and report computational costs; extend experiments and baselines.  
- Minor: replace/mention “Cauchy-Lipschitz” with Picard–Lindelöf and complete all references.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on ODE/optimization aspects; moderate regarding completeness of discrete-time claims.
