- Introduces AMDw,η: a family of continuous-time accelerated mirror descent dynamics parameterized by averaging w(t) and accumulation η(t), analyzed via an r(t)-indexed Lyapunov Lr to get f(X)−f* ≤ const/r(t).

- Corollary provides constructive weight schedules (via a(t)=w/W) achieving O(1/r(t)) rates for broad r (e.g., t^p).

- Presents a second energy Er, derives primal dynamics, and gives concrete examples on the positive orthant and simplex, yielding a novel accelerated replicator dynamics.

- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t) to decrease Lr and provides a discrete-time Algorithm 1 for r(t)=t^2.

- Experiments on low-dimensional simplex tasks show adaptive averaging compares favorably to gradient and speed restarts and yields monotone Lyapunov behavior.

- Extends prior continuous-time acceleration and AMD literature by generalizing averaging schedules and providing Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for arbitrary r(t).

- Strength: clean, unifying Lyapunov framework indexed by r(t) with transparent sufficient conditions.

- Strength: constructive recipes simplify synthesizing weights for desired continuous-time rates.

- Strength: interpretable primal forms and the novel accelerated replicator example.

- Strength: adaptive averaging is simple, intuitive, and directly tied to Lyapunov decrease.

- Weakness: Theorem 2’s condition depends on the trajectory term ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩, making it hard to verify/enforce a priori; main generic guarantee relies on more restrictive Corollary 1.

- Weakness: Discretization gap—main text lacks discrete-time guarantees (supplement only; only r(t)=t^2 treated).

- Weakness: Experiments are limited (mostly 3D simplex), missing higher-dimensional/real ML tasks, comparisons to tuned FISTA/adaptive restarts, and stochastic/noisy settings.

- Weakness: Limited practical guidance for choosing r(t), tuning β and step-size s, selecting ψ and R, and robustness/stability under misspecification.

- Weakness: Algorithm 1 contains notational/possible-typo issues and the role of the extra regularizer R and projection handling is unclear.

- Weakness: Assumptions (Lipschitzness of ∇ψ*, twice differentiability of ψ*) need clearer norm/constant statements, especially for entropy/softmax cases.

- Quality: Theoretical development and Lyapunov analysis are sound and existence/uniqueness are carefully discussed, but discrete-time theory and experimental scope are insufficient in the main text.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well organized, but needs fixes to Algorithm 1, explicit norms/Lipschitz constants, and more intuition for choosing r, w, η and adaptivity thresholds.

- Originality: Generalized averaging framework, adaptive averaging heuristic, and accelerated replicator dynamics are novel contributions.

- Significance: Advances understanding of acceleration via averaging and proposes a practical adaptive mechanism; impact would grow with stronger discrete-time results and broader experiments.

- Pros for acceptance: elegant general theory, novel adaptive heuristic, interesting constrained applications, and clear relation to prior work.

- Cons for acceptance: limited discrete-time/main experimental results, trajectory-dependent condition limits applicability, and some presentation ambiguities.

- Key suggestions: add a main-text convergence theorem for Algorithm 1 with explicit assumptions/constants, broaden experiments (higher-dimensional/real tasks, compare to tuned baselines), fix Algorithm 1 typos/definitions, and discuss strong-convexity and stochastic extensions.

- Overall: Solid, insightful contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on addressing discrete-time analysis, empirical breadth, and clarity issues.
