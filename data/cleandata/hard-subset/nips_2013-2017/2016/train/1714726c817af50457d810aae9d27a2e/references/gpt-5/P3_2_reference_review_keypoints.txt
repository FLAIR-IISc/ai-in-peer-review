- Studies a family of continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by averaging w(t) and gradient accumulation η(t), using Lyapunov Lr to obtain f(X(t)) − f* ≤ const/r(t).
- Introduces decreasing energy Er(t) = f(X(t)) + (1/r(t)) Dψ*(Z,X̌(t)) likened to potential plus kinetic energy.
- Provides primal formulation, worked examples on positive orthant and simplex, and derives an “accelerated replicator” ODE.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t)=w/W online to decrease the Lyapunov function in continuous time.
- Gives a discretization for r(t)=t^2 (Algorithm 1) and empirically compares it to gradient/speed restart heuristics on small simplex problems.
- Empirical results visualize trajectories, energy, and Lyapunov and show favorable behavior for adaptive averaging versus restarts.
- Connects closely to Bregman Lagrangian / time-scaled ODE literature (Wibisono et al., Su et al., Attouch et al.), recovering known 1/r(t) rates under η=(w r)/W and w/W≥r′/r.
- Links the averaging interpretation to Flammarion & Bach and continuous-time views of Nesterov’s method.
- Main novel contribution is the adaptive averaging heuristic and its continuous-time monotone Lyapunov decrease; accelerated replicator ODE is a useful byproduct.
- Strength: unified Lyapunov framework that transparently recovers known accelerated rates and highlights averaging weights’ role.
- Strength: simple sufficient conditions (Theorem 2, Corollary 1) easy to instantiate for r(t)=t^p.
- Strength: primal reformulation and concrete geometry examples that clarify behavior on orthant and simplex.
- Strength: adaptive averaging targets Lyapunov derivative directly and preserves continuous-time rate.
- Weakness: theoretical novelty over the Bregman Lagrangian framework is limited; much recapitulates known parametrizations in different notation.
- Weakness: adaptive guarantee is only in continuous time; discrete algorithm’s rate guarantee is deferred to supplement and not clearly stated in main text.
- Weakness: condition (2) in Theorem 2 depends on the trajectory; Corollary 1 removes dependence but reduces to known parametrization.
- Weakness: feasibility and guidance for choosing η and enforcing constraints for general r(t) (beyond r=t^2) are inadequate.
- Weakness: smoothness and geometry assumptions (norms, Lipschitz constants, compatibility with Bregman geometry) are not fully specified.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 uses ψ and an extra regularizer R without clear motivation; parameters (γ, β, s) and sensitivity are unexplained.
- Weakness: adaptive discrete rule uses f(x̃(k+1))≤f(x̃(k)) as a proxy for ⟨∇f,Ẋ⟩ sign, which may be conservative/fragile; typos in ak definition.
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale (R^3 simplex), deterministic, and miss modern baselines (monotone FISTA, line-search, stochastic); timing and robustness absent.
- Weakness: strongly convex case conjecture lacks theoretical support; either add partial results or tone down claims.
- Suggestion: include a succinct main-text theorem proving O(1/k^2) for the discrete adaptive method with explicit constants and step-size conditions.
- Suggestion: clarify differences vs. [23], emphasizing what cannot be derived from Bregman Lagrangian and what adaptive averaging achieves over restarts.
- Suggestion: specify norms and smoothness/strong-convexity assumptions and discuss ∇ψ* Lipschitz for common choices (entropy, Euclidean).
- Suggestion: fix/clarify Algorithm 1 notation, define all parameters, explain role of R vs. ψ, and how γ,β,s are chosen.
- Suggestion: expand experiments to higher dimensions, other geometries, noisy gradients, and compare to monotone/line-search FISTA; report wall-clock and sensitivity.
- Pros: elegant, unified continuous-time analysis; adaptive idea grounded in Lyapunov decrease; explicit accelerated replicator dynamics.
- Cons: modest theoretical novelty over existing ODE frameworks; discrete-time guarantees missing in main text; some assumptions and algorithmic details imprecise.
- Recommendation: borderline accept—interesting and promising adaptive averaging but needs stronger discrete-time guarantees and broader experiments.
- Confidence: high on continuous-time literature relation; medium on discrete algorithm guarantees since they are in the supplement.
