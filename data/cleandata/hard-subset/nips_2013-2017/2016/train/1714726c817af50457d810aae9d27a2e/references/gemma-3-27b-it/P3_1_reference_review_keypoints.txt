- Summary: Generalizes Accelerated Mirror Descent by introducing weight functions w and η for adaptive averaging, provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions guaranteeing 1/r(t) convergence, and proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the rate.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates the framework with examples (including accelerated replicator dynamics) and shows empirical gains, especially in strongly convex settings.  
- Related work/context: Builds on continuous-time analyses linking Nesterov acceleration to ODEs and distinguishes from adaptive methods like Adagrad/Adam and restarting heuristics.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous Lyapunov analysis with clear, well-motivated sufficient conditions on w and η.  
- Framework generality: The generalized AMD allows flexible averaging schemes beyond prior proximal AMD work.  
- Key novelty: Adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights to preserve convergence rate, outperforming restarting heuristics in theory.  
- Applicability: Connection to replicator dynamics shows relevance to game theory and broader domains.  
- Empirical support: Numerical experiments validate theoretical claims and show effectiveness in strongly convex problems.  
- Writing/clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and logically presented.  
- Empirical limitation: Experiments are limited to simple scenarios and lack demonstration across non-convex problems, large datasets, or varied constraints.  
- Implementation gaps: Algorithm 1 is described at a high level; practical details (line search, parameter tuning) are missing for reproducibility.  
- Complexity concern: Computational cost of the adaptive step (updating a(t)) is not analyzed or quantified.  
- Unexplored extension: Paper does not investigate whether adaptive averaging can improve convergence rates in non-strongly convex settings.  
- Regularizer issue: Role and impact of the regularizer R in Algorithm 1 are not fully explained.  
- Acceptance case: Novel, theoretically sound approach with significant adaptive contribution and clear presentation merits acceptance.  
- Rejection case: Limited empirical validation and missing implementation/complexity details temper practical impact.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify implementation details.
