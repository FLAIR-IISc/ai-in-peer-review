## Peer Review for "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling theoretical analysis of a generalized family of accelerated mirror descent dynamics, offering a nuanced understanding of the interplay between averaging and acceleration. The work is technically sound, clearly written, and offers a significant contribution to the theoretical foundations of accelerated optimization. While the practical implications are demonstrated through experiments, further investigation into real-world applications would strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper investigates continuous-time dynamics for accelerated first-order optimization, building on the connection established in prior works (Su et al., 2016; Krichene et al., 2016) between Nesterov’s acceleration and ODEs. It generalizes the Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics studied previously by introducing a broad family of dynamics parameterized by two weight functions governing averaging and gradient accumulation.  The core contribution lies in providing sufficient conditions – through a Lyapunov analysis – for convergence at a rate of 1/r(t), where r(t) is a user-defined convergence rate function.  Importantly, the authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts these weights, aiming to preserve the convergence rate even with restarts, a problem often lacking theoretical guarantees in existing heuristics (e.g., O’Donoghue & Candès, 2016). The connection to replicator dynamics, and its acceleration, is a particularly interesting result.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The Lyapunov analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the conditions required for accelerated convergence. The introduction of the Lyapunov function Lr is a clever way to unify the analysis.
* **Generalization of Existing Work:** The framework generalizes previous AMD dynamics, offering a more flexible and comprehensive understanding of acceleration mechanisms.
* **Adaptive Averaging Heuristic:** The proposed heuristic is well-motivated and theoretically sound, offering a significant advantage over existing restart strategies that often lack convergence guarantees.  The preservation of the 1/r(t) convergence rate is a key strength.
* **Clear and Well-Organized Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured, making it easy to follow the complex theoretical arguments.
* **Connection to Replicator Dynamics:** Deriving an accelerated version of the replicator dynamics is a significant and unexpected result, linking the optimization work to evolutionary game theory.
* **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:**  The experiments are thoughtfully designed and demonstrate the performance of the adaptive averaging heuristic compared to existing methods.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Impact:** While the experiments demonstrate the heuristic’s effectiveness, the practical implications beyond the tested scenarios are not thoroughly discussed. Further investigation on more diverse and real-world datasets would strengthen the paper.
* **Complexity of Implementation:**  The adaptive averaging heuristic, while theoretically elegant, might be more complex to implement than simpler restart strategies. A discussion of the computational overhead would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** While the authors acknowledge the challenges of applying the conditions a priori, a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the proposed framework, particularly in scenarios where the gradient is noisy or the objective function is non-convex, would be valuable.
* **Dependency on Smoothness:** The analysis relies heavily on the smoothness of the objective function and the mirror map. The robustness of the method to non-smooth functions is not addressed.



**4. Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically excellent. The proofs are rigorous, and the analysis is well-motivated. The connection between the continuous-time dynamics and discrete algorithms is clearly established. The Lyapunov analysis and convergence rate guarantees are strong.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written. The authors use precise mathematical notation and provide intuitive explanations of complex concepts. The organization is logical, and the presentation is easy to follow.
* **Originality:** The generalization of the AMD dynamics and the introduction of the adaptive averaging heuristic are original contributions. The connection to the replicator dynamics is a novel and insightful observation. The authors effectively build upon existing work while pushing the boundaries of the field.
* **Significance:** The results are significant, as they provide a deeper understanding of the theoretical underpinnings of accelerated optimization. The adaptive averaging heuristic has the potential to improve the performance of existing algorithms, and the connection to replicator dynamics opens up new avenues for research.  This work will likely be influential for researchers working on accelerated optimization methods.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and rigorous analysis.
* Novel and original contributions to the field.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Potential for significant impact on the development of accelerated optimization algorithms.
* Demonstrated effectiveness through experiments.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited discussion of practical limitations and potential computational overhead.
*  Could benefit from more extensive experimental evaluation on real-world datasets.



**6. Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest the authors:

*   Expand the discussion of the practical implications and potential limitations of the adaptive averaging heuristic.
*   Include a brief analysis of the computational overhead associated with the heuristic.
*   Consider adding a short discussion of the method's performance under noisy gradient conditions.
