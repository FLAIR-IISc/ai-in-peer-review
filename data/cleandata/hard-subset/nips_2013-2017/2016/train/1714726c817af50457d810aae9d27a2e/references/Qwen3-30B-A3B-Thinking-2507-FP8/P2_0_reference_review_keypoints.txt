- Generalizes Accelerated Mirror Descent to a family of continuous-time ODEs parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-function analysis to establish convergence rates under conditions on the weight functions.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights while preserving Lyapunov guarantees.
- Demonstrates the framework on examples, including an accelerated replicator dynamics for simplex constraints.
- Builds on Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2015), extending fixed-weight schemes to a more flexible, adaptive framework.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, rigorous, and well-structured with clear definitions, theorems, and proofs.
- Main strength is the unified framework that generalizes previous accelerated dynamics via flexible weight functions.
- Adaptive averaging is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, preserving convergence properties.
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics, notably on strongly convex problems.
- Experiments are limited (only a few examples in R^3); broader, higher-dimensional validation is needed.
- Connection to discrete-time algorithms is brief; more explicit translation to practical discrete methods would be valuable.
- Paper is clearly written and organized; notation is consistent and examples effectively illustrate concepts.
- Some proofs (e.g., Theorem 1) are dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanation of Lyapunov choices.
- Originality: significant contribution via novel adaptive averaging and the replicator-dynamics connection.
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of acceleration and offers a practical heuristic with promising empirical results.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with minor empirical and exposition limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept.
