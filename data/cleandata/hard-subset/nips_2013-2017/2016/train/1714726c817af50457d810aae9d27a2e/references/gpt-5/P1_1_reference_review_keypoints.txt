- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated first-order dynamics over convex sets via a two-weight averaging/dual-accumulation ODE family and a Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*).  
- If η and w satisfy simple conditions, Lr decreases and f(X(t))−f* = O(1/r(t)), recovering 1/t^2 and enabling polynomial rates r(t)=t^p, p≥1.  
- A second energy function (potential vs kinetic) and a primal representation clarify mirror metric effects; examples include accelerated replicator dynamics for the simplex and positive orthant.  
- The authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t)=w/W to decrease d/dt Lr in continuous time and present a discrete Algorithm 1 for r(t)=t^2 with empirical comparisons.  

- Connects to Su–Boyd–Candès and Attouch–Peypouquet inertial ODEs, the authors’ prior AMD averaging view (NIPS 2015), and Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan’s Bregman Lagrangian variational framework.  
- Novel angle: generalized averaging weights, simple Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions, accelerated replicator ODE, and adaptive averaging with continuous-time Lyapunov decrease.  

- Clear, elegant continuous-time Lyapunov framework with transparent sufficient conditions (η≥r′ and a sign condition involving ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩).  
- Constructive recipe (Corollary 1) to choose w and η for any polynomial rate r(t)=t^p, recovering known weightings.  
- Additional energy function gives physical intuition and helps interpret restart vs adaptive averaging behavior.  
- Primal formulation elucidates metric structure; accelerated replicator ODE is novel and relevant beyond optimization.  
- Adaptive averaging is simple, Lyapunov-grounded in continuous time, and empirically competitive with restarts.  
- Existence/uniqueness and feasibility preservation are discussed (initialization, Lipschitz assumptions).  

- Relation to Wibisono et al. is underdeveloped; Corollary 1 appears closely related and needs clearer delineation of novelty.  
- Key condition in Theorem 2 depends on unknown trajectory; adaptive rule requires ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩ which is impractical in discrete time and the discrete surrogate is heuristic.  
- Discrete-time convergence theory is relegated to the supplement; main-text theorem and proof sketch are missing.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to very low-dimensional (n=3) problems, lacks large-scale, wall-clock, stochastic-gradient, and FISTA+restart baselines.  
- Algorithm 1 notation and roles of parameters (R, γ, λk, β, step size s) are unclear and not well motivated.  
- Strongly convex case is mentioned but no theory or experiments (e.g., r(t)=e^{ct}) are provided to demonstrate linear rates.  
- Theorem 1’s global existence relies on global Lipschitzness; assumptions on ψ and f should be clarified.  

- Technically sound and Lyapunov derivations are correct and informative.  
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but notation is heavy and Algorithm 1 needs streamlining; references/bibliography have issues.  

- Originality is moderate: generalized averaging/Lyapunov view and accelerated replicator ODE are meaningful contributions, but connection to variational ODE frameworks blurs novelty.  
- Potential significance for designing accelerated methods is good but limited by weak empirical scope and lacking discrete guarantees.  

- Pros: unified Lyapunov analysis, flexible rates, new accelerated replicator ODE, and a simple adaptive averaging heuristic with continuous-time Lyapunov monotonicity.  
- Cons: limited novelty relative to variational formulations, discrete-time theory offloaded to supplement, small-scale experiments, and clarity/notation problems.  

- Suggest adding a main-text discrete-time convergence theorem with sketch and precise conditions.  
- Expand experiments to higher-dimensional, real datasets (e.g., logistic regression with simplex/orthant constraints), include wall-clock timings, stochastic gradients, and FISTA+adaptive-restart baselines.  
- Clarify relationship to Wibisono et al. and other inertial/Hessian-damped ODEs, highlighting unique contributions.  
- Clean up and fully define Algorithm 1 notation (λk, R, γ, β, s) and complete citations.  
- Discuss strongly convex case and whether r(t)=e^{ct} yields linear rates in continuous and discrete time.  

- Recommendation: Weak accept — interesting and technically clean work that would benefit from stronger discrete-time analysis and broader experiments.
