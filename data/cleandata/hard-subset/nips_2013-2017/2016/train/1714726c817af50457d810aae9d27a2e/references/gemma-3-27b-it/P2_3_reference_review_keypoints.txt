- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent with a generalized averaging scheme parameterized by weight functions w and η.
- Authors derive sufficient conditions on w, η, and a target rate r(t) to guarantee a Lyapunov function and convergence at rate 1/r(t).
- An adaptive heuristic for w(t) is proposed to preserve the convergence rate by reducing the Lyapunov derivative.
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive approach performs comparably or better than existing restarting heuristics.
- Strong theoretical foundation connecting discrete acceleration to continuous ODEs with rigorous Lyapunov analysis.
- Novel exploration of a broad family of averaging schemes and a promising adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Clear connections to prior work (accelerated methods, replicator dynamics, restarting heuristics) and good writing/organization.
- Practical relevance demonstrated, including an application to replicator dynamics beyond standard optimization.
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope, focusing on simple problems and the simplex constraint set.
- The adaptive heuristic increases algorithmic complexity (tuning parameters, derivative calculations) without detailed overhead analysis.
- Theoretical guarantees preserve but do not improve convergence rates; no provable acceleration beyond existing methods.
- Discussion of relationship to existing adaptive optimizers (Adagrad, Adam) is insufficiently nuanced.
- Overall technically sound and well-presented (Quality 8/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7.5/10).
- Recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions based on solid theory and promising heuristic.
- Suggested revisions: expand numerical experiments across diverse problems, analyze computational overhead of the adaptive rule, and more fully compare/contrast with adaptive methods like Adagrad/Adam.
