- Overall assessment: Valuable theoretical contribution generalizing accelerated gradient dynamics with an adaptive averaging heuristic; recommend accept with minor revisions to strengthen experiments and clarify presentation.  
- Summary: Generalizes Accelerated Mirror Descent via two weight functions w(t), η(t), derives a Lyapunov function and convergence conditions, and proposes an adaptive averaging rule that preserves theoretical rates.  
- Strength: Theoretical rigor — solid Lyapunov-based analysis and genuine extension of prior continuous-time accelerated dynamics.  
- Strength: Novel adaptive averaging heuristic that provably preserves the convergence rate versus prior heuristics lacking guarantees.  
- Strength: Insightful connection to replicator dynamics for simplex-constrained problems, linking optimization to game/evolutionary dynamics.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-organized, readable presentation.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental evaluation — few examples, small dimensions (R3), and insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art solvers to substantiate claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: Primal representation introduces a Hessian term (∇²ψ* ◦ ∇ψ) that increases computational complexity and may hinder practical implementation.  
- Weakness: Lack of sensitivity analysis regarding initial weight function a(t) and discrete parameters (β, s) in Algorithm 1.  
- Weakness: Some notation is dense or unclear (e.g., λk in Algorithm 1 needs clarification).  
- Originality: Novel generalization of AMD dynamics and adaptive averaging with theoretical guarantees; connecting adaptive weights to accelerated methods is new.  
- Significance: Potentially significant theoretical and practical impact if validated with broader experiments; replicator connection could enable broader applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory, novel guaranteed adaptive heuristic, relevant cross-disciplinary connections, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient experimental validation, less practical primal form, and missing sensitivity discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, specifically expanding experiments, adding sensitivity analysis, clarifying notation (λk), and fixing minor typographical/clarity issues.
