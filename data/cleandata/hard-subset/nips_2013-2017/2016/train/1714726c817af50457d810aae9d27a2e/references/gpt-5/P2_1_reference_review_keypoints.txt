- Paper introduces AMDw,η: a family of continuous-time accelerated mirror descent ODEs with separate averaging w(t) and gradient-accumulation η(t), analyzed via Lyapunov Lr to obtain f(X(t))−f* = O(1/r(t)).  
- Derives simple sufficient conditions (e.g., η ≥ r′ and interaction of r − ηW/w with ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩) for Lr decrease and defines a decreasing energy Er.  
- Provides a primal-only formulation, applies framework to positive orthant and simplex, and identifies an “accelerated replicator” dynamic.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts w(t) along the trajectory with guarantees to preserve the Lyapunov decrease and a discrete algorithm sketch aimed at preserving rates.  
- Theoretical derivations (d/dt Lr, bounds, existence-uniqueness) are sound and transparently presented; corollary recovers O(1/t^2) and allows r(t)=t^p.  
- Notational inconsistencies and typos (notably a(t)=w/W vs its use as 1/a, wrong parameter forms in Example 1, dimensional inconsistencies) risk confusing readers and reproducibility.  
- Discrete algorithm is only sketched in the main text with key elements deferred to the supplement, making rate-preservation and reimplementation hard to verify.  
- Experiments are illustrative but limited (small-dimensional, synthetic simplex problems); they show monotonicity of Lr and improvements over restarts but need broader validation.  
- Originality: novel decoupling of averaging and accumulation schedules, unified Lyapunov design, accelerated replicator dynamics, and a provable adaptive averaging heuristic distinct from standard restarts.  
- Significance: framework is a clean generalization that can inform new discrete methods; adaptive averaging is a practical contribution likely of interest to optimization researchers.  
- Pros: general Lyapunov framework, flexible w/η design knobs, concrete constrained examples, accelerated replicator, and a provable adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Cons: critical notational errors, non–self-contained discretization and guarantees, limited experimental scope, and possibly restrictive assumptions (global Lipschitz ∇ψ*).  
- Key requested fixes: correct a(t)/η(t) inconsistencies and Example 1, state explicit condition to zero (r−ηW/w), and fix reference formatting.  
- Suggested additions: make Algorithm 1 and its convergence proof self-contained in main text, discuss β selection and sensitivity, expand experiments (higher dimensions, other constraints, standard ML tasks), and clarify domain/Lipschitz assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (medium reviewer confidence).
