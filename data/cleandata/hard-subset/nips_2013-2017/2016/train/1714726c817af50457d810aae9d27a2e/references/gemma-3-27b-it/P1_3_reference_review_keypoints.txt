- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with generalized averaging weights and derives sufficient conditions for quadratic convergence.  
- Main contribution is an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights during trajectories while provably preserving the original convergence rate.  
- Provides theoretical analysis using Lyapunov methods and connects discrete accelerated methods to ODEs (building on Su et al. and Krichene et al.).  
- Demonstrates a notable application by accelerating replicator dynamics for simplex-constrained optimization.  
- Positions work relative to adaptive optimization (Adagrad/Adam) and restart heuristics, highlighting distinct goals and providing guarantees lacking in restarts.  
- Strength: general, rigorous framework with clear sufficient conditions and standard, effective Lyapunov proofs.  
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is significant because it comes with formal convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: insightful connection to replicator dynamics linking theory to evolutionary game theory and viability theory.  
- Strength: generally clear presentation and notation is mostly standard.  
- Strength: numerical experiments support theoretical claims and show practical benefits.  
- Weakness: theoretical framework is dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with convex analysis and ODEs.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited in scope; more diverse objectives and matrix conditions (esp. strongly convex cases) are needed.  
- Weakness: discretization/details of Algorithm 1 are under-explained; impact on discrete convergence requires more discussion and links to prior discretization work.  
- Minor issue: notation reuse of X(t) for both variable and trajectory is confusing and should be clarified (e.g., x(t)).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound framework; provable adaptive heuristic; practical relevance via replicator dynamics; experiments align with theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to complexity, insufficient experimental breadth, and incomplete discretization details.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focusing on clearer intuition, expanded experiments, fuller discretization explanation, and notation clarification.
