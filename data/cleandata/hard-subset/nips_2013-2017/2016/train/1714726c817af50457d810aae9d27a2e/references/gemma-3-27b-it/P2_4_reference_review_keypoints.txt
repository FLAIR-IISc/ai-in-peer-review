- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD), introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic, derives Lyapunov stability conditions, proves a 1/r(t) convergence rate, and presents supportive numerical experiments.  
- Theoretical rigor: solid derivations and clear sufficient conditions for convergence.  
- Generalization: flexible AMD framework parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t), enabling many averaging schemes.  
- Novel heuristic: adaptive averaging that monitors the Lyapunov decrease and preserves convergence rate, filling a gap in existing heuristics.  
- Replicator connection: insightful application to simplex constraints linking AMD to replicator dynamics.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show the heuristic is competitive and often superior to restarting methods.  
- Complexity: heavy mathematical formalism may be hard to follow for non-experts; more intuition needed.  
- Practicality: discrete algorithm provided but lacks detailed guidance on tuning parameters (e.g., β, s).  
- Experimental scope: limited set of objectives and constraints; broader problem classes and datasets needed.  
- Baselines: missing comparisons to modern adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adam) and recent Nesterov variants for non-strongly convex problems.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with convincing theoretical claims, but practical discussion and experiments could be expanded.  
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written but key derivations (Lyapunov/primal representation) need more intuitive explanation.  
- Originality assessment: novel adaptive averaging and generalization of AMD; replicator link adds novelty.  
- Significance assessment: potentially important for accelerating methods with theoretical guarantees and practical promise.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theory, novel provable heuristic, empirical support, and general framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility issues due to complexity, limited experiments, and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add intuitive explanations (Lyapunov/primal), expand experiments and baselines, and provide concrete parameter-tuning guidance.
