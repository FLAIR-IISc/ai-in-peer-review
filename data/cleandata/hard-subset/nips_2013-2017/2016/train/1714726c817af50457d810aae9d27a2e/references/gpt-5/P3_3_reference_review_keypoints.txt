- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent via a two-parameter family AMD_{w,η} and generalized averaging.  
- Introduces Lyapunov L_r(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+D_{ψ*}(Z,z*) and gives sufficient conditions ensuring L_r non-increasing, yielding f(X(t))−f* ≤ const./r(t).  
- Shows how to construct w(t) and η(t) to attain target rates r(t) (e.g., t^2).  
- Introduces a second energy Er that decreases under similar conditions and offers a potential+kinetic interpretation.  
- Provides a primal representation that includes replicator dynamics on the simplex and generalizes prior ODE families.  
- Proposes a principled adaptive averaging heuristic that drives down d/dt L_r and preserves continuous-time rate; presents a discrete algorithm with experiments.  
- Builds on Su-Boyd-Candès, Wibisono-Wilson-Jordan, and Krichene-Bayen-Bartlett, with novelty in parametrizing acceleration via generalized averaging and an adaptive rule.  
- Conceptual unification: AMD_{w,η} + Lyapunov ties target rates r(t) to weight schedules via a(t)=w/W.  
- Constructive conditions (Theorem 2, Corollary 1) give explicit recipes (η = a r, a ≥ r'/r) to ensure Lyapunov decrease and rate 1/r(t).  
- Primal examples (orthant, simplex) are clear; accelerated replicator dynamics are relevant to evolutionary game theory and constrained optimization.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is principled and the discrete scheme is reasonably compared to standard restarts.  
- Energy Er appears robust empirically and gives additional dynamical insight.  
- Notational inconsistencies and typos hinder understanding (wrong a(t) form in examples, contradictory statements about a vs η, proof consistency with a=w/W, unclear λ_k in Algorithm 1).  
- Main-text discrete-time guarantees are weak/missing; a concise discrete theorem for the adaptive algorithm is needed.  
- The trajectory-dependent condition in Theorem 2 is only alleviated by η=a r; the discrete test f(˜x_{k+1})≤f(˜x_k) needs justification linking it to ⟨∇f,Ẋ⟩ and discrete Lyapunov decrease.  
- Experiments are limited to low-dimensional simplex problems and qualitative plots; require quantitative metrics, sensitivity analyses, higher-dimensional tests, and hyperparameter-tuning fairness.  
- A strongly convex / linear-rate example or discussion on recovering linear rates in discrete time would strengthen claims about robustness and rates.  
- Need explicit mapping to the variational/Bregman Lagrangian perspective (map a,r,η to α,β and whether the adaptive rule has a variational interpretation).  
- Clarify technical assumptions: existence/uniqueness requirements (smoothness/strong convexity of ψ, handling boundaries) should be stated precisely.  
- Discuss dependence on choice of z*: D_{ψ*}(Z,z*) and implications for multiple minimizers and discrete diagnostics.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theoretical framework linking averaging schedules to accelerated rates, novel adaptive averaging heuristic, connections to replicator dynamics, and reusable analysis.  
- Cons for acceptance: notational errors that obscure conditions, discrete-time guarantees not prominent, limited experiments, and incremental novelty centered on adaptive averaging.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept contingent on fixing notation, adding a succinct discrete-time theorem, and strengthening experiments.
