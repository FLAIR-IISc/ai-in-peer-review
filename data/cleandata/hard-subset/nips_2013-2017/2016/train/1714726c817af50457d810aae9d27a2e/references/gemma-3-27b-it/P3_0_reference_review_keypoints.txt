- Proposes a generalized continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) framework parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t) and gives conditions for quadratic convergence.  
- Introduces an adaptive heuristic that dynamically chooses w(t) from the Lyapunov derivative to preserve the convergence rate.  
- Derives primal representations and gives examples for constrained sets (positive orthant, simplex).  
- Positions the work within ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and connects to prior continuous-time optimization literature.  
- Links the dynamics to replicator equations and viability theory, highlighting broader relevance.  
- Provides rigorous Lyapunov-based theoretical analysis and sufficient conditions guaranteeing quadratic rate.  
- Generalizes AMD to arbitrary weight functions, increasing flexibility for different averaging schemes.  
- Offers an adaptive averaging method with theoretical guarantees, unlike many existing heuristics.  
- Includes comprehensive experiments within chosen settings, with trajectory and Lyapunov visualizations.  
- Writing is clear and the paper is well organized.  
- Analysis depends on strong assumptions (Lipschitz gradients, differentiable mirror maps) limiting generality.  
- The adaptive heuristic may be computationally expensive due to multiple objective evaluations per iteration.  
- Experiments lack direct comparison to state-of-the-art discrete-time accelerated methods (e.g., tuned Nesterov variants).  
- Practical guidance on choosing/tuning weight functions and sensitivity to parameter β in Algorithm 1 is insufficient.  
- Minor wording issue: requiring weights to be “positive, continuous” may be overly restrictive; non-negativity and weaker regularity might suffice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound generalization; adaptive heuristic with guarantees; broader connections and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions, practical complexity, and limited comparison to discrete-time baselines.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with promising experiments; recommended for acceptance with suggested revisions on practical considerations and comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept (Strongly Consider).
