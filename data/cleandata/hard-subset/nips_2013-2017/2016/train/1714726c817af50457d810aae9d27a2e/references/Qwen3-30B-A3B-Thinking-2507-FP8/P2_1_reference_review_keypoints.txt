- Paper studies a family of continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η, extending prior work.  
- Unified Lyapunov-based analysis establishes sufficient conditions for achieving convergence rate 1/r(t).  
- Authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically sets weights while preserving the convergence rate.  
- Provide applications to simplex-constrained problems, including an accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging outperforms existing restart heuristics across several problem types.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous with appropriate proof techniques (Theorem 2, Corollary 1).  
- One limitation: the second condition in Theorem 2 depends on the solution trajectory and may be hard to check a priori.  
- The discrete version of the adaptive heuristic lacks a full formal analysis in the main paper (only a bound in supplement).  
- Paper is well-written and organized with clear definitions and consistent notation.  
- The link between continuous-time analysis and discrete algorithm design is not clearly explained and needs a brief exposition.  
- The paper would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why the adaptive averaging preserves convergence.  
- The generalization of averaging schemes and unified conditions for rates is a novel contribution.  
- Adaptive averaging is a significant practical advance over restart heuristics, offering theoretical guarantees for general convex problems.  
- Application to replicator dynamics connects optimization with evolutionary game theory and is an interesting contribution.  
- Results have both theoretical and practical significance and could inspire further research and better implementations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, and well-written — recommendation: accept.  
- Strengths: rigorous analysis, novel adaptive heuristic, replicator dynamics application, strong empirical results, clear relation to prior continuous-time work.  
- Weaknesses: unclear continuous-to-discrete transition, need more intuition for adaptive rule, insufficient discrete analysis in main text.
