- Studies a two-parameter family of accelerated mirror descent ODEs (AMDw,η) that decouples gradient accumulation η(t) and primal averaging weight w(t), with Lyapunov-based convergence f(X(t))−f* ≤ O(1/r(t)).  
- Provides constructive rules to build w, η from a desired rate r(t) and introduces a decreasing “energy” Er(t) in addition to the Lyapunov Lr.  
- Lyapunov analysis and existence/uniqueness arguments are sound and align with prior ODE-based acceleration work.  
- Corollary 1 contains a critical typo: η(t) should equal (w(t)/W(t)) r(t) (not w(t) r(t) W(t)) to zero the relevant coefficient.  
- Examples 1–2 contain typos: a(t) should be β/t (β≥2 or β≥p) for r(t)=t^p, not β t; subsequent w(t), η(t) expressions suggest the intended 1/t dependence.  
- Discrete adaptive scheme is plausible and a discrete Lyapunov rate is stated in the supplement, but main-text guarantees for Algorithm 1 are only sketched.  
- The practical trigger used (monotonicity of f(x̃)) is a surrogate for ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩; more discussion is needed on when this surrogate ensures Lyapunov decrease.  
- Experiments are clear but limited to low-dimensional (R^3) simplex problems and a few objectives; claims about outperforming restarts are supported only in these settings.  
- Broader validation (higher dimensions, different ψ, unconstrained Euclidean cases, stochastic gradients, wall-clock cost) is needed to establish practical significance.  
- Notation is somewhat heavy and could benefit from a short notation table to improve readability.  
- The relation to prior ODE families (e.g., Wibisono et al.) is noted and useful; expanding differences in admissible rates and discretizations would improve context.  
- Novelty lies mainly in the generalized weighted-averaging formulation with a simple Lyapunov certificate and the adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves continuous-time rates.  
- Contribution is incremental relative to prior variational/ODE acceleration literature but offers a meaningful, principled adaptive averaging idea.  
- Pros: clean unifying Lyapunov analysis, constructive w/η design, decreasing energy function, novel adaptive averaging heuristic, illustrative primal examples (including accelerated replicator dynamics).  
- Cons: crucial formula typos, discrete-time adaptive analysis deferred, surrogate trigger may not always reflect continuous criterion, limited experimental scope, moderate novelty.  
- Key suggestions: correct Corollary 1 and Examples 1–2 typos; add a succinct discrete-time theorem for Algorithm 1 in main text; expand experiments (dimensions, ψ, stochastic, runtimes); clarify links to Bregman Lagrangian and restarts.  
- Overall: technically solid and well-motivated with moderate novelty; leans toward weak accept if typos are fixed and discrete-time results are clarified, otherwise borderline.
