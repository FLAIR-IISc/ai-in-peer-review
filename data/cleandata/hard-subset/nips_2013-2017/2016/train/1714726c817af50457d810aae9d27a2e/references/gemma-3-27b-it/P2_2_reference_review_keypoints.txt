- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) and generalizes AMD dynamics by allowing arbitrary averaging weight functions.
- Provides sufficient conditions on averaging weights and a rate function r(t) to guarantee convergence at rate 1/r(t).
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights based on the Lyapunov function’s derivative to maintain the convergence rate.
- Uses Lyapunov function arguments and a solid mathematical framework to derive theoretical results.
- The adaptive heuristic is novel and comes with a guarantee of maintaining the convergence rate, unlike many existing heuristics.
- Clearly situates the work within existing literature on Nesterov acceleration, continuous-time optimization, and restarting heuristics.
- Offers insightful examples for positive-orthant and simplex-constrained problems, including connections to the replicator equation.
- Provides numerical experiments that show initial empirical gains of the adaptive averaging heuristic over existing restarting methods.
- Experiments are limited in scope, focusing mainly on simplex-constrained problems with an entropy function.
- Paper lacks discussion of the computational overhead and complexity of computing/applying the adaptive averaging weights.
- Theoretical analysis is primarily in continuous time, with limited translation and guarantees for discrete-time algorithms.
- The algorithm in Section 4 is a good start but needs a more thorough convergence analysis in discrete time.
- Mathematical notation is sometimes dense, which may impede readers unfamiliar with the field.
- Quality: technically sound and rigorous, but missing complexity analysis reduces completeness.
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, though notation can be dense.
- Originality: the AMD generalization and adaptive averaging heuristic are novel and interesting.
- Significance: potentially significant if empirical benefits generalize to broader problem classes.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel heuristic with guarantees, clear connections to prior work, and illustrative examples with initial empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow experiments, missing complexity discussion, and heavy emphasis on continuous-time results without full discrete-time translation.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on expanding experiments, discussing computational complexity, and providing stronger discrete-time convergence analysis.
