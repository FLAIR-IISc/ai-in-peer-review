

# Peer Review: "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

## Summary
This paper studies a family of continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics for convex optimization, extending previous work on Nesterov's accelerated methods and their continuous-time interpretations. The authors introduce a broad framework for Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η, providing a unified Lyapunov function approach to guarantee convergence rates. The key contributions include a sufficient condition for the Lyapunov function to ensure convergence at a given rate, an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence rates while improving empirical performance, and connections to replicator dynamics for simplex constraints. The paper builds on foundational work by Su et al. (2016) and Krichene et al. (2016), but significantly generalizes their framework with adaptive averaging.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors provide clear theorems with complete proofs (Theorems 1-3), establishing conditions for convergence rates and energy decay. The Lyapunov function approach is well-justified and properly applied. The numerical experiments are well-designed, comparing the adaptive averaging heuristic against existing restarting heuristics across multiple objective functions (strongly convex quadratic, weakly convex quadratic, linear, and KL divergence) on simplex constraints. The paper acknowledges limitations appropriately, such as the dependence of one condition on the solution trajectory, and provides a special case where this condition is trivially satisfied. The theoretical results are well-supported by experimental findings, particularly the demonstration that adaptive averaging preserves the decreasing property of the Lyapunov function.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined, with appropriate references to convex analysis concepts. The connection to previous work is established early, making it easy to understand how this paper extends the literature. The examples for specific constraint sets (positive-orthant and simplex) are particularly helpful for illustrating the theory. The adaptive averaging heuristic is explained with both continuous and discrete formulations, making it accessible for practical implementation. The supplementary material (referenced throughout) appears to provide sufficient detail for reproducibility. The paper effectively balances theoretical depth with practical insights.

## Originality
This paper makes several significant original contributions. The generalization of accelerated descent dynamics to include adaptive averaging represents a meaningful extension of previous work. The adaptive averaging heuristic is novel in its approach to preserving convergence rates while improving performance. The connection to replicator dynamics for simplex constraints is particularly innovative, demonstrating how accelerated methods can be applied to evolutionary game theory. While the paper builds on foundational work by Su et al. (2016) and Krichene et al. (2016), it provides a substantially broader framework with clear theoretical guarantees. The paper adequately references related work, though it could potentially cite more recent work on adaptive optimization methods.

## Significance
The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the theoretical understanding of accelerated optimization methods with clear practical implications. The adaptive averaging heuristic has direct relevance for practitioners developing optimization algorithms in machine learning and related fields. The connection to replicator dynamics opens up new applications in evolutionary game theory, potentially attracting interest from researchers in that domain. The numerical experiments convincingly demonstrate that adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics across all tested examples, with particularly significant improvements for strongly convex problems. The paper advances the state of the art in accelerated methods by providing a theoretically sound approach that maintains convergence guarantees while improving empirical performance.

## Strengths
- Rigorous theoretical framework with well-justified theorems and proofs
- Novel adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence rates while improving performance
- Interesting connection to replicator dynamics for simplex constraints
- Well-designed numerical experiments showing clear advantages over existing methods
- Excellent balance between theoretical contribution and practical implementation

## Weaknesses
- The second condition in Theorem 2 depends on the solution trajectory, which may be difficult to check a priori (though the paper provides a special case where this is trivial)
- The paper focuses primarily on convex optimization, limiting its applicability to non-convex settings (though this is beyond the paper's scope)
- The connection to practical machine learning applications could be strengthened with more specific examples

## Arguments for Acceptance
- The paper provides a significant theoretical extension to accelerated methods with clear guarantees
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is both theoretically sound and empirically superior
- The connection to replicator dynamics is novel and potentially impactful for evolutionary game theory
- The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized
- The numerical experiments convincingly demonstrate the method's advantages across multiple problem types

## Arguments against Acceptance
- The continuous-time focus may limit immediate applicability to practitioners who primarily use discrete algorithms
- The paper doesn't directly address how adaptive averaging might extend to non-convex optimization (though this is beyond the scope)
- The connection to machine learning applications could be more explicitly developed

## Conclusion
This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of accelerated optimization methods. The adaptive averaging heuristic is both novel and effective, with clear theoretical guarantees and strong empirical performance. The connection to replicator dynamics is particularly interesting and demonstrates the broad applicability of the framework. The paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a balanced view of both theoretical and practical aspects. While the focus on convex optimization limits the scope somewhat, this remains a core topic at NIPS with broad relevance to machine learning practitioners. The paper advances the state of the art in accelerated methods by providing a theoretically sound approach that maintains convergence guarantees while improving empirical performance.

**Rating: Strong Accept** (9.5/10)
