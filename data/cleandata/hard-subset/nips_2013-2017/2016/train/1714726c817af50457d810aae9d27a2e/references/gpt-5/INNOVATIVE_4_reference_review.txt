Summary and context
This paper proposes a generalized continuous-time framework for accelerated mirror descent (AMD) based on adaptive averaging. The authors introduce a two-parameter family of ODEs, AMDw,η, where w(t) controls averaging and η(t) controls gradient accumulation in the dual variable. A central contribution is a Lyapunov function Lr(X,Z,t) = r(t)(f(X) − f*) + Dψ*(Z,z*) parameterized by a desired rate r(t), with simple sufficient conditions on (w, η, r) ensuring Lr is nonincreasing and hence f(X(t)) − f* = O(1/r(t)). The framework recovers and generalizes prior continuous-time views of Nesterov/AMD and also yields an alternative “energy” function Er that is guaranteed to decrease.

The paper develops a primal-only representation, illustrates examples for the positive orthant and the simplex (leading to an “accelerated replicator dynamics”), and proposes a simple adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t) = w/W online to reduce the Lyapunov derivative, thereby preserving the target rate while improving practical performance. A discrete algorithm is given, with a rate bound deferred to the supplement. Empirical comparisons on low-dimensional simplex-constrained problems show that adaptive averaging compares favorably to gradient- and speed-restart heuristics.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on the ODE interpretations of accelerated methods (Su–Boyd–Candès) and accelerated mirror descent (Krichene–Bayen–Bartlett), and connects to the variational perspective (Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan). It relates to averaging-based interpretations of acceleration (Flammarion–Bach) and to practical restart heuristics (O’Donoghue–Candès; Su–Boyd–Candès). The adaptive averaging idea is positioned as complementary to Adagrad/Adam-style adaptivity (which target step-sizes rather than averaging for acceleration). The replicator dynamics connection is classical (Weibull; Sigmund), here extended with acceleration.

Strengths
- Novelty and unification: A clean, parameterized ODE family with a Lyapunov generator r(t) and simple constructive conditions on w and η. This gives a unifying lens on multiple accelerated flows and suggests new ones (including faster target rates r(t) = t^p).
- Adaptive averaging: A conceptually simple yet principled heuristic that modulates averaging weights by decreasing the Lyapunov derivative, with guarantees that the target rate is preserved in continuous time. This is an appealing alternative to restarts, which lack such guarantees in general convex settings.
- New connections: Acceleration of replicator dynamics is interesting and likely to stimulate cross-fertilization between optimization and evolutionary dynamics.
- Clarity of the continuous-time analysis: The Lyapunov derivations are crisp; the existence/uniqueness and feasibility results are straightforward and helpful. The energy function Er adds interpretability.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Discrete-time theory is deferred: The core novelty (adaptive averaging) is most useful in discrete algorithms, but the main text moves the convergence analysis of Algorithm 1 to the supplement and limits experiments to small problems. Bringing at least a concise discrete-time theorem (with conditions, rate, and proof sketch) into the main paper would strengthen the contribution.
- Limited empirical scope: Experiments are mostly 3D simplex cases. It would help to (i) test higher-dimensional problems and other mirror maps (e.g., ℓ1 ball, box constraints), (ii) include standard baselines like FISTA with restarts, and (iii) report sensitivity to β, step-size s, and the regularizer R. Measuring overhead and the evolution of ak would illuminate the mechanism.
- Verifiability and practicality of conditions: Condition (2) in Theorem 2 depends on the unknown trajectory; the heuristic addresses this, but the paper could better explain the computational cost of evaluating the trigger and how robust it is with noisy gradients.
- Minor technical issues/typos: In Example 1, the text says a(t) = β t but the subsequent calculations correspond to a(t) = β/t (the latter is consistent with r′/r = 2/t and yields w(t) ∝ t^{β−1} and η(t) = β t). Please correct for clarity. Some notation could be streamlined (e.g., consistent use of t0 and initialization).

Quality
Technically sound in continuous time with clear proofs and plausible discretization. The adaptive rule is well-motivated via Lyapunov descent; however, the discrete-time analysis and broader experiments should be strengthened. The replicator example is correctly derived and insightful.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. The exposition of the Lyapunov framework and constructive design of w, η is clear. A brief “design recipe” summarizing how to choose r, then a, then w and η, would help practitioners. Move key discrete-time results from the supplement if space allows.

Originality
High. The “rate-parameterized Lyapunov” view with generalized averaging and the adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the target rate are fresh and likely to spur new methods. The accelerated replicator dynamics connection is a nice conceptual addition.

Significance
Potentially impactful. Provides a simple, principled alternative to restarts with guarantees and a unifying lens on accelerated flows. With stronger discrete-time evidence on larger problems, this could influence practical accelerated mirror-descent variants.

Pros
- Conceptually novel and unifying framework for accelerated dynamics with generalized averaging.
- Simple adaptive averaging heuristic with Lyapunov-based guarantees.
- New accelerated replicator dynamics example.
- Clear continuous-time analysis; interpretable energy function.

Cons
- Discrete-time theory and experiments are limited in the main paper.
- Empirics small-scale; missing comparisons to some standard baselines.
- Some conditions are trajectory-dependent; more guidance for practice is needed.
- Minor typos/notation inconsistencies.

Recommendation
Accept (borderline strong). The paper introduces a significant and elegant new direction—adaptive averaging for acceleration with a rate-generating Lyapunov framework—that outweighs the relatively modest empirical scope. Strengthening the discrete-time analysis in the main text and expanding experiments would further bolster the contribution, but the core ideas are original, bold, and promising.

Reviewer confidence: high on theory; medium on empirical assessment.
