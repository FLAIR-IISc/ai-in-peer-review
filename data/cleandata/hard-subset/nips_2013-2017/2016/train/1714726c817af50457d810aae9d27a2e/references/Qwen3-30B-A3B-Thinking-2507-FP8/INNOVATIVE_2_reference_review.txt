

# Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

This paper introduces a novel generalization of accelerated mirror descent dynamics through a flexible averaging scheme parameterized by weight functions w and η. The authors provide a unified Lyapunov analysis that establishes sufficient conditions for achieving a specified convergence rate 1/r(t), extending prior work by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) on continuous-time accelerated methods. The key contribution is the adaptive averaging heuristic, which dynamically computes the weight functions along solution trajectories to preserve the convergence rate while improving practical performance.

The paper builds upon the established connection between Nesterov's accelerated methods and continuous-time ODEs (Su et al., NIPS 2014; Krichene et al., NIPS 2015), but significantly generalizes the framework by allowing for arbitrary averaging schemes rather than the fixed polynomial weights previously considered. The authors derive sufficient conditions for Lyapunov functions to guarantee convergence rates, and their Corollary 1 provides a constructive method for designing weight functions that achieve desired rates. The adaptive averaging heuristic (Section 4) is particularly compelling—it dynamically adjusts the weights to maximize progress while preserving theoretical guarantees, which is a significant improvement over existing restarting heuristics.

The theoretical development is sound, with clear theorems and proofs. The connection to replicator dynamics (Section 3) is especially elegant and demonstrates the broad applicability of their framework—showing that the replicator ODE from evolutionary game theory can be accelerated through their generalized averaging scheme. The numerical experiments in Section 5 convincingly demonstrate that adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics across multiple problem types (strongly convex, weakly convex, linear, and KL divergence), with significant improvements in the strongly convex case.

This paper's main strength lies in its conceptual novelty and the elegance of its adaptive heuristic, which bridges theoretical guarantees with practical performance improvements. While the paper is primarily theoretical, the numerical validation provides sufficient evidence that the approach has practical value. The connection to replicator dynamics is particularly noteworthy as it demonstrates how the framework can be applied to established ODEs in other fields, potentially opening new research directions.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a logical progression from the general framework to specific examples and applications. The authors carefully contextualize their work within the existing literature on continuous-time optimization dynamics.

Minor weaknesses include limited empirical validation on non-simplex constrained problems and some technical complexity in the continuous-time analysis. However, these do not detract from the paper's core contribution. The adaptive averaging heuristic is conceptually simple yet powerful, and its ability to preserve theoretical convergence rates while improving practical performance is a significant advance.

This work makes a strong theoretical contribution to the understanding of accelerated optimization methods, with the adaptive heuristic offering a practical approach to improve performance without sacrificing convergence guarantees. The paper demonstrates how a simple conceptual change (adaptive averaging) can lead to meaningful improvements in optimization algorithms. The connection to replicator dynamics provides an elegant application that demonstrates the framework's generality.

In terms of significance, this paper advances the state of the art by providing a unified framework for analyzing accelerated dynamics with generalized averaging, and by introducing a practical heuristic that can be applied to a wide range of optimization problems. The authors successfully demonstrate both theoretical novelty and practical relevance.

The paper is clearly within NIPS scope, addressing a fundamental problem in optimization with novel theoretical insights that have potential applications in machine learning and beyond. The work stands out from previous NIPS papers on continuous-time optimization by introducing a general framework with a practical adaptive heuristic, rather than merely analyzing a specific algorithm.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field with a novel conceptual framework and a practical heuristic. The theoretical foundation is strong, the adaptive averaging idea is bold and well-motivated, and the results are compelling. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings, offering both theoretical insights and practical improvements for accelerated optimization methods.

**Recommendation:** Accept
