## Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates continuous-time dynamics for accelerated mirror descent (AMD), building upon prior work connecting Nesterov's acceleration to ODEs. The authors generalize a previously studied AMD dynamic by introducing a broad family of dynamics parameterized by weight functions *w* and *η*, representing averaging and gradient accumulation rates, respectively. They derive sufficient conditions on *w* and *η* to guarantee a 1/r(t) convergence rate, where r(t) is a desired rate function.  Crucially, they propose a novel adaptive heuristic for computing *w(t)* that aims to preserve this convergence rate by decreasing the Lyapunov function derivative whenever possible. Numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of this adaptive averaging scheme, showing comparable or improved performance against existing restarting heuristics.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical analysis of a generalized AMD framework. The Lyapunov function approach is well-justified and provides clear convergence guarantees under defined conditions.
* **Generality and Flexibility:**  The parameterized AMD dynamics (AMD<sub>w,η</sub>) offer a flexible framework for exploring different averaging schemes.  This generality allows for the recovery of existing methods and the potential design of new ones.
* **Novel Adaptive Heuristic:** The proposed adaptive averaging heuristic is a significant contribution.  The theoretical guarantee of preserving the convergence rate is valuable, especially in contrast to existing heuristics that often lack such guarantees. The intuition behind the heuristic – maximizing progress by adjusting averaging weights – is sound.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper effectively connects its work to prior research on accelerated methods, continuous-time dynamics, and replicator dynamics, providing appropriate context.
* **Compelling Experimental Validation:** The numerical experiments demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the adaptive averaging heuristic, comparing it favorably to established techniques. The inclusion of visualizations of the trajectories is helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Theoretical Analysis:** While rigorous, the theoretical analysis can be dense and challenging to follow for readers not deeply familiar with convex analysis and differential equations.  More intuitive explanations would be helpful.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are promising, they are limited in scope. Evaluating the method on a wider range of problem instances and datasets would strengthen the conclusions.  The comparison to other adaptive methods beyond restarting heuristics (e.g., Adam) would be beneficial.
* **Practical Considerations of Implementation:** The paper focuses primarily on the theoretical and continuous-time aspects. Further discussion of practical considerations for discretizing and implementing the adaptive heuristic in a discrete setting (as briefly touched upon in Algorithm 1) would be valuable. Specifically, the sensitivity to the step size *s* could be explored.
* **Potential for Triviality:** The condition η(t) >= r'(t) is necessary, but it feels somewhat restrictive and potentially leading to trivial solutions if not carefully tuned. The paper could discuss this in more detail.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are supported by rigorous analysis and experimental results. It presents a complete piece of work, although further refinement of the practical aspects could be beneficial. The authors are honest about the limitations (e.g., the need for careful parameter tuning).
* **Clarity:** The writing is generally clear, but the theoretical sections are dense and require significant background knowledge.  The presentation could be improved with more intuitive explanations and intermediate steps.
* **Originality:** The generalization of AMD dynamics and the proposed adaptive averaging heuristic are novel contributions. The paper effectively differentiates itself from previous work. The connection to replicator dynamics provides a unique perspective.
* **Significance:** The results are significant. The adaptive averaging heuristic offers a principled approach to improving the convergence of accelerated methods, potentially impacting a broad range of applications. The theoretical guarantees are a strong point.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Solid theoretical foundation with convergence guarantees.
* Novel and potentially impactful adaptive averaging heuristic.
* Compelling experimental results.
* Clear connection to and advancement of existing work.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Theoretical complexity may limit accessibility.
* Limited scope of experiments.
*  Need for further discussion of practical implementation details.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of optimization. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the proposed adaptive heuristic is novel and well-motivated, and the experimental results are promising. While the paper could benefit from some improvements in clarity and scope, the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above, particularly expanding the experiments and clarifying the practical considerations of implementation. The paper has the potential to be highly influential in the development of accelerated optimization algorithms.
