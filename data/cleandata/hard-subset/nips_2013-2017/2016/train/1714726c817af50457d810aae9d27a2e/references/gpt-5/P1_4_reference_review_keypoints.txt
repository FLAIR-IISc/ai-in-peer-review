- Paper studies continuous-time first-order dynamics for constrained smooth convex minimization via generalized accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with two weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Main contribution: Lyapunov function Lr = r(t)(f(X)−f*) + Dψ*(Z,z*) and simple sufficient conditions on (w, η, r) ensuring Lr nonincreasing and f(X)−f* = O(1/r(t)).
- Corollary: choosing η(t)=w(t)r(t)/W(t) with W(t)=∫w recovers known O(1/t^2) rates and extends to r(t)=t^p rates.
- Defines an “energy” Er that also decreases under the same conditions.
- Provides a primal representation and illustrative examples: positive orthant (entropy → diagonal scaling) and simplex (entropy → replicator equation).
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t)=w/W to decrease the Lyapunov derivative while preserving continuous-time rates.
- Presents a discrete adaptive algorithm (variant of AMD) and empirical comparisons to gradient/speed restart heuristics on small simplex problems.
- Situates work relative to Su-Boyd-Candès (2016), Krichene-Bayen-Bartlett (2015), Wibisono-Wilson-Jordan (2016), and connects restarts and adaptive first-order methods conceptually.
- Strength: conceptual clarity and unification via generalized AMD and Lyapunov template with a constructive corollary.
- Strength: simple, theoretically grounded adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves continuous-time rates.
- Strength: illuminating primal examples and connections to evolutionary/replicator dynamics.
- Strength: technically sound continuous-time analysis (existence/uniqueness, Lyapunov, energy).
- Weakness: discrete-time guarantees for the adaptive scheme are omitted from main text; no main-theorem showing O(1/k^2) for Algorithm 1.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional (R^3) simplex problems; lacks large-scale, noisy, or ill-conditioned benchmarks and broader method comparisons.
- Weakness: trajectory-dependent condition ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩ used in theory vs. discrete trigger f(x̃(k+1))≤f(x̃(k)) lacks formal justification in main text.
- Weakness: assumptions on ψ, norm choices, and domain smoothness (e.g., entropy on simplex interior) need clearer statement.
- Weakness: relation to variational/Bregman Lagrangian framework should better delineate novelty and overlap.
- Weakness: presentation issues—typos/incomplete references, unclear choices in Algorithm 1 (R, γ, prox setup, β, s), and missing per-iteration complexity discussion.
- Quality: continuous-time theory is solid; discrete-time analysis should be highlighted in main paper for practical impact.
- Clarity: generally well written but heavy notation; adding discrete theorem and clarifying assumptions would improve readability.
- Originality: moderately high — generalized averaging and adaptive Lyapunov-based rule are novel; acceleration of replicator dynamics is a nice contribution.
- Significance: potentially impactful as a unifying design perspective and practical heuristic, but significance hinges on stronger discrete theory and broader experiments.
- Acceptance pros: clean Lyapunov framework, theoretically grounded adaptive heuristic, and interesting mirror/replicator connections.
- Acceptance cons: missing discrete-time convergence theorem in main text, limited experiments, and some implicit technical assumptions.
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on adding a clear discrete-time convergence guarantee and expanding experiments.
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
