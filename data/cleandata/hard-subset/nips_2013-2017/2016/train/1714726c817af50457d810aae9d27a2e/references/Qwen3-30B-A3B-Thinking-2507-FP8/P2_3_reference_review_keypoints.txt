- Summary: Extends accelerated mirror descent to a family of continuous-time dynamics parameterized by weight functions, gives Lyapunov-based convergence conditions, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic with applications and experiments.  
- Theoretical quality: Technically rigorous with correct existence/uniqueness (Thm.1) and Lyapunov convergence analysis (Thm.2).  
- Practical experiments: Numerical results show adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics.  
- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written; notation consistent and supplemental material links continuous and discrete views, though some technical background may challenge non-experts.  
- Originality: Novel generalization of prior NIPS work and a new adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence rates; interesting application to replicator dynamics.  
- Significance: Strong impact—unifies continuous-time accelerated methods, offers a practically useful adaptive heuristic, and broadens applications (e.g., evolutionary game theory).  
- Strengths: Rigorous proofs, clear continuous-discrete connection, novel rate-preserving heuristic, comprehensive experiments, and an illustrative replicator-dynamics example.  
- Weaknesses: Adaptive heuristic depends on solution trajectory (Ẋ(t)) and may be costly to compute; computational complexity of the heuristic not fully explored; links to discrete accelerated methods beyond quadratic cases are limited.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—meets NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) despite practical-cost caveats.
