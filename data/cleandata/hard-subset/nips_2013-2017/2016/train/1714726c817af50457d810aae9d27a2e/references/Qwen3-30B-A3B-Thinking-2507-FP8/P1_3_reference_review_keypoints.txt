- Introduces a family of continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Provides a unified Lyapunov function framework giving sufficient conditions for convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves theoretical convergence rates and improves empirical performance.  
- Establishes connections between the AMD framework and replicator dynamics for simplex constraints.  
- Presents rigorous theoretical results with complete proofs (Theorems 1–3) and energy decay guarantees.  
- Numerical experiments compare adaptive averaging against restarting heuristics across multiple objective types and constraints.  
- Experiments show adaptive averaging outperforms existing methods, especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Clarifies continuous and discrete formulations of adaptive averaging for practical implementation.  
- Supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Novel generalization of accelerated descent dynamics and an innovative adaptive averaging method.  
- Extends prior work (Su et al., Krichene et al.) into a broader, theoretically grounded framework.  
- Theoretical novelty includes applying accelerated methods to evolutionary game theory via replicator dynamics.  
- Main weakness: one condition in Theorem 2 depends on the solution trajectory and may be hard to verify a priori.  
- Scope limited to convex optimization, so applicability to non-convex settings is not addressed.  
- Could strengthen ties to practical machine learning applications with more specific examples.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong theoretical extension, novel and effective heuristic, clear empirical gains, and excellent presentation.  
- Counterarguments: continuous-time focus may limit immediate discrete-practical impact and non-convex extensions are not covered.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, well-written paper with significant theoretical and empirical contributions; recommendation Strong Accept (9.5/10).
