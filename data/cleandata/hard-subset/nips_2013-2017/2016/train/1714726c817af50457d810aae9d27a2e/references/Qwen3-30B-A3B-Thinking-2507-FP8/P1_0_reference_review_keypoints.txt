- Proposes a generalized family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η, enabling a flexible averaging scheme.
- Provides Lyapunov-based analysis establishing convergence rates under conditions on the weight functions.
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the theoretical convergence rate and can improve empirical performance.
- Connects the continuous-time dynamics to replicator dynamics for simplex-constrained problems, linking to evolutionary game theory applications.
- Builds on prior ODE-based acceleration work (e.g., Su et al., Krichene et al.) and extends those results to a broader averaging framework.
- Theoretical results (Theorems 2, 3, Corollary 1) are rigorous and well-supported by proofs.
- Authors carefully derive sufficient conditions for the Lyapunov function to decrease, underpinning convergence guarantees.
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and show adaptive averaging outperforming existing restarting heuristics across multiple problem types.
- Authors transparently acknowledge limitations, noting the need for specific conditions for Lyapunov decrease and that the heuristic preserves but does not necessarily improve rates.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and uses consistent, well-defined mathematical notation.
- The link between continuous-time dynamics and discrete optimization algorithms is clearly explained, aiding practical relevance.
- Algorithm 1 (discrete implementation) is presented but lacks clarity without supplementary material.
- Examples (positive-orthant and simplex-constrained dynamics) effectively illustrate the framework; the replicator connection is particularly compelling.
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is a novel, principled alternative to restarting heuristics, offering convergence guarantees they lack.
- Work has practical significance for designing new optimization algorithms and improving accelerated methods in practice.
- Strengths: elegant theoretical framework, rigorous Lyapunov analysis, novel heuristic, convincing experiments, and replicator dynamics connection.
- Weaknesses: unclear discrete implementation details, limited discussion of computational complexity, less explicit ties to specific discrete methods like Nesterov’s, analysis restricted to convex problems, and no discussion of scenarios where adaptive averaging may underperform.
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-written, and a meaningful contribution to accelerated optimization.
- Specific suggestions: clarify Algorithm 1’s discrete implementation, relate dynamics to discrete accelerated methods, discuss computational cost of the heuristic, note potential drawback scenarios, mention the supplementary bound in the main text, and comment on possible non-convex extensions.
