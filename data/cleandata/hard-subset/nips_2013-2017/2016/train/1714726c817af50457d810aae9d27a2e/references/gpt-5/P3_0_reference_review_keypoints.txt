- Summary: Paper analyzes a two-weight continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) family, gives a Lyapunov-based sufficient condition for O(1/r(t)) convergence, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic with a discrete Algorithm 1 tested on simplex problems.

- Main theoretical contribution: Lyapunov framework Lr yields simple sufficient conditions (Theorem 2) for nonincreasing energy and recovers known accelerated schedules for r(t)=t^p.

- Novelty: Introduces adaptive averaging that preserves the Lyapunov decrease in continuous time and derives an accelerated replicator ODE from the primal form.

- Quality of analysis: Lyapunov proofs are clean; existence/uniqueness (Theorem 1) is standard; constructive Corollary 1 links trajectory condition to a(t)=w/W.

- Technical caveat — a(t) typo: The correct relation is η(t)=a(t) r(t) (so a(t)=η(t)/r(t)); text incorrectly states a(t)=η(t) r(t) in Sec.4.

- Technical caveat — Example 1 typos: For r(t)=t^2 one needs a(t)=β/t (not β t), and the w(t) expression should follow Eq.(4) yielding w(t)=(t/t0)^{β−1} and η(t)=β t.

- Technical caveat — linkage clarity: Theorem 3 uses a:=w/W and needs explicit statement that Theorem 2’s condition holds when η=ar.

- Discretization concerns: Algorithm 1 and its convergence proof are in the supplement; a succinct main-text theorem (assumptions, rate, step-size/β ranges) is needed.

- Discretization robustness: Discuss stability/robustness to misspecification of β and step-size s, and how ak interacts with composite structure (ψ, R).

- Clarity: Paper is well organized with helpful examples, but key typos (a,w,η) and unclear ψ assumptions should be fixed.

- Clarity suggestion: Add a “design recipe” mapping desired r(t) to valid (a,w,η) and list 2–3 canonical schedules.

- Assumptions on ψ: Theorem 1/3 should explicitly state when ∇ψ* is Lipschitz, when ψ is smooth (so ∇ψ=(∇ψ*)^{-1}), and treatment of indicator functions for constrained sets.

- Smoothness/extendability: Clarify whether Theorem 3 requires twice-differentiable ψ and whether results extend to non-smooth ψ via monotone operator theory.

- Adaptive heuristic condition: Continuous-time preservation of rate requires η(t) ≥ r′(t); explain how this is enforced in the discrete algorithm and what discrete analogue of r′ is used.

- Experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited to low-dimensional simplex problems; need higher-dimensional tests, tuned restart baselines, adaptive methods comparisons, and quantitative metrics (suboptimality vs iterations/time).

- Experimental details: Report how step sizes and β were chosen/tuned, whether restarts were tuned for fairness, and include sensitivity/ablation studies for the adaptive rule.

- Relation to prior work: Provide a more direct mapping to Wibisono-Wilson-Jordan parameters to show the adaptive choice corresponds to a valid time-varying metric/friction in their Lagrangian framework.

- Pros: Clear, flexible Lyapunov framework that unifies prior ODE views; novel adaptive averaging idea; insightful primal forms and accelerated replicator dynamics.

- Cons: Distracting typos/notation errors in core formulas (a,w,η); discrete algorithm theory deferred to supplement; empirical validation is small-scale and trajectory-dependent condition (2) is not checkable a priori.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on fixing the noted errors, adding a concise discrete-time guarantee in the main text, and strengthening experiments and robustness analysis.
