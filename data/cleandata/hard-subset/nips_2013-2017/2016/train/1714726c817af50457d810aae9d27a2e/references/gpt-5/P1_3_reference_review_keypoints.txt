- Studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent family AMDw,η with dual accumulation rate η(t) and primal averaging weight w(t).
- Main theoretical contribution: unified Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*) and simple sufficient conditions on (w,η,r) to ensure Lr nonincreasing and f(X)−f*≤const/r(t).
- Reduces a trajectory-dependent inequality to a practical constructive corollary that recovers known accelerated schedules (e.g., t^2 rate).
- Introduces an energy function Er(t)=f(X(t))+(1/r(t))Dψ*(Z,∇ψ(X)) giving a potential+kinetic interpretation and monotonicity under the same condition.
- Derives primal representations for constraints (orthant, simplex) and presents an accelerated replicator dynamics as an explicit example.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that modulates weights online to further decrease the Lyapunov while preserving target rate, plus a discrete algorithm for r(t)=t^2.
- Empirical evaluation on low-dimensional simplex problems shows adaptive averaging is competitive or better than gradient and speed restarts and consistently decreases the Lyapunov.
- Theoretical analysis is sound: Lyapunov framework is unified, and existence/uniqueness arguments are careful.
- Novelty: averaging-centric two-parameter (w,η) parameterization and adaptive averaging principle appear original; accelerated replicator ODE is of independent interest.
- The energy function complements prior variational perspectives and aids physical intuition.
- Discrete algorithmic guarantees are deferred to the supplement and practical guidance on step sizes and hyperparameters is limited in the main text.
- Experiments are clean but small-scale and deterministic; no large-scale, stochastic, or broader baseline comparisons.
- Notational and presentation issues: Examples 1–2 typo (a(t) should be β/t), and Algorithm 1 lacks clear definitions/assumptions for γ, R, and step size s.
- Main sufficient condition is trajectory-dependent; the corollary is constructive but restricts choices.
- Suggestions: fix typos, clarify Algorithm 1 (define γ, R, state discrete Lyapunov/convergence in main text), provide hyperparameter selection guidance, and add larger-scale and stochastic experiments.
- Recommendation: solid, novel, and useful contribution; lean toward acceptance after minor revisions focusing on clarity and improved discrete-time discussion.
