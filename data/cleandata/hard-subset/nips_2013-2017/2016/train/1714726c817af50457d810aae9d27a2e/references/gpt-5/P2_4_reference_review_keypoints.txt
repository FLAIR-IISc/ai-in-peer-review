- Generalizes accelerated mirror descent via a family of continuous-time ODEs parameterized by averaging weight w(t) and gradient accumulation η(t), using Lyapunov Lr to get f(X(t)) − f* ≤ const/r(t), recovering O(1/t^2) and r(t)=t^p.  
- Provides primal forms and examples (positive orthant, simplex) and derives an “accelerated replicator” ODE.  
- Introduces a secondary energy Er that also decreases and offers additional interpretation.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that decreases the Lyapunov and preserves the continuous-time rate; presents a discrete Algorithm 1 for r(t)=t^2.  
- Experiments on R^3 simplex show consistent improvements over restart heuristics and monotone Lyapunov/energy behavior, but are small-scale.  
- Positions work relative to Su/Wibisono and mirror-descent literature and clarifies differences from adaptive step-size methods like AdaGrad/Adam.  
- Continuous-time theory is technically sound: existence/uniqueness, clean Lyapunov derivative, and constructive recipe for w,η (Corollary 1).  
- Er monotonicity via monotonicity of ∇ψ* is plausible and useful for interpretation.  
- Main theoretical gap: discrete-time guarantees for the adaptive rule are deferred to the supplement; the main text lacks a full discrete convergence proof.  
- The adaptive discrete rule depends on checking f(x̃) decreases, which is only indirectly tied to the continuous inner-product sign condition—this link is heuristic.  
- Discretization and adaptive rule in the main text lack explicit assumptions and detailed discrete-time analysis.  
- Experiments are empirically sound but limited: low dimension, few tasks, no large-scale/stochastic tests, and no sensitivity/ablation studies.  
- Clarity is generally good; derivations and role of a(t)=w/W are clear, but notation (e.g., reuse of X) and some implementation details (step size, β, γ) need cleanup.  
- Original contributions: novel generalized AMD via arbitrary averaging, adaptive averaging heuristic tied to Lyapunov decrease, and the accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Significance is moderate: strong continuous-time theory and promising adaptivity, but impact limited until discrete-time guarantees and broader experiments are provided.  
- Pros: unifying ODE framework, simple sufficient conditions for rates, adaptive heuristic preserving continuous rate, secondary energy function, empirical gains over restarts.  
- Cons: incomplete discrete-time theory in main text, small-scale experiments, heuristic continuous-to-discrete connection, missing comparisons to baselines like FISTA/OGM and adaptive acceleration schemes.  
- Suggested improvements: move key discrete-time analysis into main paper, clarify discrete test and design a discrete Lyapunov surrogate, expand experiments and baselines, add implementation details and code, and streamline notation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid and novel continuous-time contributions but needs stronger discrete-time theory and broader empirical validation.
