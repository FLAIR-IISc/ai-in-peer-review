- Paper introduces a generalized accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODE family parameterized by averaging w(t), accumulation η(t), and target rate r(t), unifying prior accelerated ODEs.  
- Constructs Lyapunov Lr = r(t)(f(X)−f*) + Dψ*(Z,z*) and gives sufficient conditions on (w, η, r) for Lr nonincreasing, yielding f(X(t))−f* ≤ const./r(t).  
- Presents a constructive choice w via a(t)=w/W ≥ r′/r and η = a r that recovers known accelerated schedules (e.g., polynomial r=t^p).  
- Derives primal forms of AMD for common constraints, including an “accelerated replicator” ODE for the simplex/positive orthant.  
- Introduces a second energy Er that is also nonincreasing, offering a potential/kinetic-energy interpretation.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves continuous-time rate and decreases the Lyapunov derivative; provides a discrete heuristic for r(t)=t^2.  
- Lyapunov analysis is clear, technically sound, and isolates rate and averaging-induced terms neatly.  
- Novel contributions include unified energy/Lyapunov perspective, accelerated replicator dynamics, and the adaptive averaging rule beyond prior work.  
- Discrete-time results are sketched (r=t^2) with discrete Lyapunov in supplement, but main-text discrete guarantees are limited.  
- The discrete adaptive heuristic is plausible and simple but lacks a self-contained convergence guarantee and practical tuning guidance (β, s, regularizer R).  
- Experiments compare adaptive averaging to gradient/speed restarts on simplex problems and visualize trajectories, Lyapunov and energy metrics.  
- Empirical evaluation is low-dimensional (mostly R^3), leaving scalability to high-dimensional ML tasks unclear.  
- Run-time/iteration tradeoffs and sensitivity to β, step-size, and R are not explored in the main text.  
- Condition (2) of Theorem 2 depends on the trajectory and is hard to verify a priori; Corollary 1 and the adaptive rule address this but should be highlighted earlier.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; continuous-time arguments and connections to prior ODEs are clearly explained.  
- References have missing bibliographic details and need tightening.  
- Significance: conceptually strong (clarifies averaging-acceleration interaction) and practically promising, but impact limited by discrete-time theory and empirical scope.  
- Strengths: unified continuous-time analysis, principled adaptive heuristic, new replicator dynamics, consistent empirical improvements in shown examples.  
- Weaknesses: limited discrete-time theory in main text, narrow experimental scope, unclear parameter sensitivity and computational cost, trajectory-dependent condition needs clearer bridging to discrete setting.  
- Suggestions: add a concise discrete-time theorem and proof sketch, provide tuning guidance and ablations for β/step-size/R, include higher-dimensional ML experiments, and clarify computation/approximation of trajectory condition; fix bibliography.  
- Overall assessment: good quality, clarity, and originality; moderate-to-good significance; recommendation: lean accept (medium confidence).
