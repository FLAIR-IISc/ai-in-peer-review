- Summary: Introduces a generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) continuous-time framework with weight functions, proves Lyapunov stability and 1/r(t) convergence, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation linking discrete acceleration to continuous ODEs with rigorous Lyapunov analysis.  
- Strength: Generalization via weight functions w and η offers flexible averaging and gradient-accumulation schemes beyond prior fixed methods.  
- Strength: Adaptive averaging heuristic dynamically adjusts weights to preserve convergence rate and intuitively maximize progress.  
- Strength: Provides concrete applications, including accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex, connecting to evolutionary game theory.  
- Strength: Empirical results show initial evidence that adaptive averaging can outperform existing restart heuristics.  
- Strength: Clear writing and organization that make theoretical developments accessible.  
- Weakness: Numerical experiments are limited in scope, focusing mainly on simplex constraints and few objectives.  
- Weakness: The adaptive heuristic is justified but not proven optimal or shown to outperform other adaptive schemes theoretically.  
- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of connections and differences with existing adaptive methods like AdaGrad and Adam.  
- Weakness: Implementation complexity and computational overhead of the adaptive scheme are not discussed.  
- Weakness: No analysis of parameter sensitivity (e.g., initial weights, β) and its impact on performance.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound generalization of AMD and a promising adaptive averaging approach with convergence guarantees.  
- For acceptance: Interesting interdisciplinary connections (replicator dynamics/evolutionary game theory) broaden impact.  
- Against acceptance: Empirical validation is not comprehensive enough to fully support claims.  
- Against acceptance: Theoretical rationale for the specific adaptive choice could be strengthened.  
- Against acceptance: Practical issues (complexity, parameter sensitivity) are under-addressed.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution; adaptive averaging is promising but needs more empirical and practical analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and discuss parameter sensitivity and links to other adaptive methods.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10, tempered by limited experimental scope.
