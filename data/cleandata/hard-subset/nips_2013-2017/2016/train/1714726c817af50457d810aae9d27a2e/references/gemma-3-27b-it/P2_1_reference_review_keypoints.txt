- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) and generalizes it via weight functions w and η.
- Authors derive sufficient conditions on w and η guaranteeing a 1/r(t) convergence rate.
- A novel adaptive heuristic for computing w(t) is proposed to preserve the convergence rate by decreasing the Lyapunov derivative.
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive averaging performs comparably or better than existing restarting heuristics and include helpful trajectory visualizations.
- Strong theoretical foundation: rigorous Lyapunov-based analysis with clear convergence guarantees.
- General and flexible framework (AMD_{w,η}) that recovers existing methods and enables new averaging schemes.
- The adaptive heuristic is novel and comes with theoretical guarantees, not common among existing heuristics.
- Clear and well-motivated connections to prior work on accelerated methods, continuous dynamics, and replicator dynamics.
- Compelling experimental validation, though limited in scope.
- Theoretical analysis is dense and may be hard to follow without strong background in convex analysis and ODEs.
- Experiments are limited in problem variety and lack comparison to broader adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adam).
- Practical discretization and implementation details are underdeveloped; sensitivity to step size s needs more study.
- The requirement η(t) ≥ r'(t) might be restrictive and could lead to trivial or poorly tuned solutions; needs further discussion.
- Overall technically sound, original, and potentially significant for accelerated optimization.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand experiments and clarify practical implementation considerations.
