- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated optimization dynamics with a generalized averaging scheme parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Introduces a unified Lyapunov function approach to prove sufficient conditions for achieving a target convergence rate 1/r(t).  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights while preserving convergence guarantees.  
- Connects the generalized averaging framework to replicator dynamics on the simplex, extending applicability beyond standard optimization.  
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution—Theorem 2 and Corollary 1 give clear sufficient conditions for desired rates.  
- Strength: rigorous mathematical analysis using Lyapunov techniques and a grounded continuous-to-discrete perspective.  
- Strength: novel application to replicator dynamics, potentially impactful for evolutionary game theory.  
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and provides a theoretically grounded alternative to restart heuristics.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to R^3 simplex problems and do not demonstrate performance on larger, real-world ML tasks.  
- Weakness: the connection and justification from continuous-time dynamics to the discrete Algorithm 1 are not clearly articulated in the main text.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with recent adaptive acceleration methods (e.g., refs [16, 23]), weakening novelty claims.  
- Weakness: no analysis of computational overhead of the adaptive heuristic versus simpler restart methods.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of continuous-time acceleration literature and significant by generalizing averaging and proposing adaptive averaging.  
- Practical significance is moderate; empirical improvements are shown but not large enough to clearly outperform existing methods.  
- Specific comment: need more detail on how the adaptive heuristic (Eq. 8) is discretized into Algorithm 1.  
- Specific comment: authors claim discrete-time convergence is preserved but do not provide an explicit discrete-time rate bound for Algorithm 1.  
- Specific comment: comparison with restart heuristics is limited—should include more recent adaptive methods.  
- Specific comment: supplementary contains key results (e.g., discrete-time bounds) that should be referenced and highlighted in the main text.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept / borderline for NIPS—solid theoretical work but requires stronger experiments and clearer discrete-time guarantees.  
- Suggested improvements: larger-scale experiments, clearer continuous-to-discrete theoretical connection, computational-cost analysis, and stronger comparisons with recent adaptive methods.  
- Final assessment scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 6/10; Overall: Weak Accept.
