This paper investigates continuous-time dynamics for accelerated convex optimization, specifically focusing on a generalization of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) with adaptive averaging. The authors build upon their previous work [11, 20] connecting Nesterov's accelerated methods to ODEs and present a broad family of AMD dynamics parameterized by weight functions. They establish sufficient conditions for convergence and propose a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the convergence rate. The paper is well-motivated, connects to a rich body of work in optimization and dynamical systems, and presents interesting theoretical and empirical results.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous analysis of the AMD dynamics, establishing Lyapunov conditions for convergence. The connection to Bregman divergences and mirror maps provides a solid mathematical framework.
* **Novel Adaptive Averaging Heuristic:** The proposed heuristic is a significant contribution. It’s conceptually elegant, grounded in Lyapunov stability analysis, and, importantly, *guarantees* preservation of the convergence rate—a notable achievement.
* **Connection to Replicator Dynamics:** Deriving an accelerated version of the replicator dynamics is a compelling application, demonstrating the broader applicability of the proposed framework. This links the work to evolutionary game theory and viability theory.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The numerical experiments are well-designed, comparing the adaptive averaging heuristic to established restarting methods across various objective functions and highlighting its advantages, particularly in the strongly convex case.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized.  The mathematical derivations are presented with sufficient detail, and the connection to previous work is clearly articulated.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide an extensive and relevant list of references, demonstrating a strong understanding of the field.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Theory:** While rigorous, the theoretical analysis is quite involved, potentially making it inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with convex analysis and dynamical systems. A more intuitive explanation of the Lyapunov function and its role in guaranteeing convergence would be helpful.
* **Practicality of the Heuristic:** While the heuristic guarantees convergence rate preservation, its practical implementation might be challenging.  The algorithm requires computing derivatives and evaluating conditions at each step, which could introduce overhead. A discussion of the computational cost compared to existing methods would be valuable.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are comprehensive for the chosen setup (simplex-constrained problems with the entropy function), it would be beneficial to see results on a wider range of problems and constraint sets.  For example, testing on large-scale machine learning datasets could further demonstrate the practical impact of the method.
* **Discretization Details:**  The transition from continuous-time analysis to the discrete Algorithm 1 is somewhat abrupt. A more detailed discussion of the discretization process and its potential impact on the convergence rate would strengthen the paper.  The choice of step size 's' and its influence are not clearly explained.
* **Comparison with other Adaptive Methods:**  The paper briefly mentions Adagrad and Adam but doesn't provide a direct comparison.  Including these adaptive methods in the experimental evaluation would give a more complete picture of the performance landscape.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to accelerated optimization.
* The adaptive averaging heuristic is a significant contribution with a strong guarantee on convergence rate preservation.
* The connection to replicator dynamics demonstrates the broader applicability of the framework.
* The experimental results support the theoretical findings and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The theoretical complexity may limit the paper's accessibility.
* The practical implementation of the heuristic could be challenging.
* The scope of the experiments is somewhat limited.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field of accelerated optimization. While the theoretical complexity and potential implementation challenges are valid concerns, the novelty of the adaptive averaging heuristic and its theoretical guarantees outweigh these drawbacks. The paper meets the criteria for NIPS acceptance, especially in terms of quality, originality, and significance.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Simplify Theoretical Explanation:** Provide a more intuitive explanation of the Lyapunov function and its role in convergence.
* **Discuss Computational Cost:** Analyze the computational cost of the adaptive averaging heuristic compared to existing methods.
* **Expand Experimental Evaluation:**  Include results on a wider range of problems and constraint sets, and consider comparisons with Adagrad and Adam.
* **Elaborate on Discretization:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the discretization process and its impact on convergence.
* **Consider a "Practical Guide" Section:** A section outlining practical considerations for implementing the heuristic (e.g., step size selection) would be beneficial.



**Recommendation:** Accept.
