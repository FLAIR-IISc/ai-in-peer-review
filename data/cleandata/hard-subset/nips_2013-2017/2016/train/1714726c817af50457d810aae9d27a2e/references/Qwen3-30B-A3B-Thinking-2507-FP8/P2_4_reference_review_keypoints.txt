- Paper introduces a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent dynamics parameterized by weight functions and gives a unified Lyapunov analysis for convergence rates.  
- Authors propose a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that computes weights along solution trajectories while preserving convergence guarantees.  
- Theoretical contributions include existence/uniqueness, Lyapunov decrease conditions, primal representations, and clear theorems with rigorous proofs (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Empirical evaluation compares the adaptive heuristic to restarting methods across multiple objectives and shows superior performance, especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Experiments confirm the Lyapunov function decreases under the proposed heuristic and are comprehensive across problem types.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, logically organized, and uses consistent notation; supplementary material provides reproducibility details.  
- Provides clear connections to prior continuous-time ODE-based accelerated methods and extends prior work by generalizing the averaging scheme.  
- The adaptive heuristic is novel in dynamically computing weights and preserving convergence where existing restarts may fail for non-strongly convex problems.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of acceleration and offers a practical discrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) that bridges to implementation.  
- Strength: rigorous analysis, novel heuristic, comprehensive experiments, and helpful examples linking theory to specific constraint sets.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity of the adaptive heuristic.  
- Weakness: emphasis on continuous-time dynamics may reduce immediate practical impact; more large-scale experiments would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: could better articulate connections to specific machine learning applications.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, significant theoretical contribution with strong empirical validation and minor practical-discussion gaps.
