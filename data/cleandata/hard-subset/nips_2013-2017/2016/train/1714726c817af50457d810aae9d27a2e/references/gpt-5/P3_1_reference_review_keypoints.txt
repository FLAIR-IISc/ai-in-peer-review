- Introduces a generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) ODE family with time-varying averaging w(t) and gradient accumulation η(t).
- Central Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t) = r(t)(f(X) − f*) + Dψ*(Z, z*) yields f(X(t)) − f* ≤ const / r(t) under simple conditions.
- Key sufficient condition: η(t) ≥ r′(t) and r(t) = η(t)W(t)/w(t) with W(t)=∫0^t w(τ)dτ, equivalently a(t)=w/W ≥ r′/r and η = a r.
- Presents an energy function Er(t) = f(X(t)) + (1/r(t))Dψ*(Z,X̌) giving complementary intuition.
- Provides primal-space representations including an accelerated replicator ODE on the simplex.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that targets the Lyapunov derivative sign to reduce oscillations while preserving continuous-time rates.
- Gives a discrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) and experiments comparing adaptive averaging against gradient and speed restarts.
- Properly situates work in relation to Su-Boyd-Candès, Krichene-Bayen-Bartlett, Wibisono-Wilson-Jordan, Attouch et al., and replicator dynamics literature.
- Observation that continuous-time rates beyond 1/t^2 need higher-order discretizations is correctly noted.
- Conceptual clarity: a(t)=w/W reparameterization and Lyapunov computations clearly separate averaging vs gradient accumulation roles.
- Framework is general across mirror geometries and constrained sets, with concrete examples (positive orthant, simplex).
- Energy interpretation (Er) ties to potential/kinetic analogies and aids intuition.
- Adaptive heuristic is a plausible mechanism to reduce oscillations by directly targeting the Lyapunov derivative.
- Continuous-time analysis is clear on existence/uniqueness, feasibility preservation, and initialization at t0>0.
- Theorem 2 uses a trajectory-dependent condition ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩(r−ηW/w) ≤ 0, which is hard to check in practice.
- The discrete surrogate test f(x̃(k+1)) ≤ f(x̃(k)) in Algorithm 1 is only an indirect proxy for the continuous inner-product condition, weakening algorithmic claims.
- Discrete-time analysis is underdeveloped: main text lacks theorems, step-size/L-smoothness conditions, and explicit constants for Algorithm 1.
- For r(t)=t^2 the supplement contains a bound, but the main paper omits discrete guarantees; p>2 requires higher-order methods for provable rates.
- Lipschitz assumptions on ∇f and ∇ψ* need explicit norms and constants; global vs local Lipschitzness (especially for entropy maps) is unclear.
- Lyapunov Lr depends on unknown x* (via z*), complicating empirical reporting when minimizers are non-unique.
- Experiments are small-scale (R^3 simplex, entropy mirror); performance on higher-dimensional or Euclidean problems is not shown.
- Hyperparameter tuning details (β, s, γ, R) and fairness of baseline tuning (restart thresholds) are not provided.
- Algorithm 1 requires two proximal-like steps per iteration; computational cost, choice of R, and practical guidance are not discussed.
- Suggested improvements: move discrete-time convergence theorem to main text, analyze a discrete inner-product test, state Lipschitz norms/constants, run larger-scale experiments, explore strongly convex/exponential r(t) cases, and clarify computational costs.
- Pros: clean, general Lyapunov framework; novel adaptive averaging; interesting accelerated replicator dynamics; well-situated literature review.
- Cons: discrete algorithm lacks in-text guarantees; limited experiments and unclear tuning; some assumptions and practical checks need clarification; dependence on unknown x* complicates empirical interpretation.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — strong continuous-time theory and an interesting adaptive idea, but needs stronger discrete analysis and expanded experiments to improve impact.
