

This work builds upon the ODE framework for Nesterov's acceleration introduced by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to develop and empirically assess a straightforward adaptive reweighting strategy for the algorithm's iterates, accelerating convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging approaches, this method provably dominates standard fixed-schedule averaging. Empirical results demonstrate significant acceleration across a broad class of functions. The paper is a pleasure to read: the mathematical exposition is clear and accessible, particularly for readers familiar with Krichene et al.'s work, while the empirical findings yield compelling speedups. Although the technical contributions extend Krichene et al.'s framework, this paper holds significant potential impact, as Nesterov's acceleration is ubiquitous in practice. Frequently, new adaptive methods are benchmarked against Nesterov acceleration using a fixed weight schedule, yet this work shows that adapting Nesterov's method requires only minor adjustments and can yield substantial performance gains. To strengthen the technical contribution, I recommend including a convergence rate analysis for the discretized algorithm to confirm rate preservation, which should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, exploring potential links between this adaptive averaging technique and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization would be valuable.
