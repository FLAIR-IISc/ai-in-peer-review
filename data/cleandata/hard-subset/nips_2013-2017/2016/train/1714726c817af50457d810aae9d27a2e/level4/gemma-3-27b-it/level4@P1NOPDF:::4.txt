This paper studies a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) with a generalized averaging scheme. The authors provide sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and the weights to guarantee a desired convergence rate, using a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, they employ an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are included. There is a substantial body of work focused on empirically accelerating the convergence of accelerated methods, and this paper makes a key observation – via the lens of ODEs – that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can also lead to speedups. I found this approach to be very interesting.

The main strength of this work lies in its theoretical analysis. The Lyapunov-based convergence proof is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed method. The conditions presented, while perhaps restrictive, are clearly stated and justified. The connection made between adaptive averaging and the ODE perspective is novel and provides a fresh outlook on established acceleration techniques. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the derived conditions. While the theory is sound, it's not immediately obvious how easily these conditions can be satisfied in practice, or what guidance they offer for selecting appropriate parameters. Expanding on this point, with examples or recommendations, would significantly enhance the paper’s utility.

The numerical experiments are well-conducted and demonstrate the effectiveness of the adaptive averaging scheme. However, the comparison to existing accelerated methods could be more comprehensive. Including results on a wider range of problem instances, particularly those where the proposed method might be less competitive, would provide a more balanced assessment. Specifically, exploring the performance on highly ill-conditioned problems would be valuable.

Regarding clarity, the notation is generally consistent, but some sections could be more concise. For example, the derivation of certain inequalities feels somewhat verbose and could be streamlined without sacrificing rigor. 

Minor comments:

*   Consider clarifying the specific type of ODEs being considered. A more precise definition early in the paper would be helpful.
*   The term "dual learning rate" is used frequently. Briefly explaining its role and connection to the averaging scheme would improve readability for a broader audience.
*   In the discussion of the adaptive heuristic, it would be beneficial to explain the intuition behind its design choices.

Overall, this is a strong paper with a compelling theoretical contribution. The proposed method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated. Addressing the suggestions outlined above – particularly regarding the practical implications of the theoretical results and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation – would elevate the paper further. I recommend acceptance, contingent on minor revisions.
