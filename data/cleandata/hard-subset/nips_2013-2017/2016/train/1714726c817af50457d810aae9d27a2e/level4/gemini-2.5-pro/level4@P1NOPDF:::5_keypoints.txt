- Proposes accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using a Lyapunov-function framework.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function and improve convergence speed.  
- Provides theoretical analysis establishing the desired convergence rate via the Lyapunov approach.  
- Experiments show the adaptive averaging scheme performs on par with, and sometimes better than, adaptive restarting.  
- Reviewer not an expert in continuous-time dynamics but finds the central idea interesting and promising.  
- Quality: high, with solid theory and experimental support against a relevant baseline.  
- Novelty: main contribution is the specific adaptive averaging technique applied to continuous-time constrained optimization.  
- Impact: potentially significant if the method proves robust and broadly applicable to practitioners.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and core ideas are clearly presented.
