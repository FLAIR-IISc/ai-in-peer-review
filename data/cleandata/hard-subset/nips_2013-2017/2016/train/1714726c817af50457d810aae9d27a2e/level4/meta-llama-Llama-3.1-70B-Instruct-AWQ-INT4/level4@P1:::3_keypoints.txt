- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending authors' prior work [11].
- Introduces and analyzes more general averaging schemes where the primal trajectory is an average of the mirror of the dual.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under conditions on averaging coefficients; in continuous dynamics shows rates faster than 1/t^2.
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection with provable convergence and compares it experimentally to restarting techniques.
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not provided.
- It is unclear whether the improved continuous convergence rates persist after discretization.
- Analysis in the continuous setting is rigorous, the paper is well-written, and notation is clear and consistent.
- Main contributions include new averaging schemes that can achieve faster-than-1/t^2 rates and an adaptive-weight method.
- Reviewer suggests citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to include theoretical analysis of the discrete version in a future revision.
