The paper proposes an adaptive reweighting heuristic for Nesterov's accelerated gradient method, building upon the ODE interpretation introduced by Krichene et al. in NIPS 2015. Unlike previous adaptive averaging techniques, this approach is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed method achieves significant speed-ups on a broad class of functions.

I found the paper to be an enjoyable read, with a clear and concise mathematical argument that is easily accessible to those familiar with Krichene et al.'s work. The empirical results are also promising, showcasing notable improvements in convergence rates. Although the technical development is a natural extension of Krichene et al.'s work, I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact due to the widespread use of Nesterov's acceleration in various applications. Often, new methods are compared to Nesterov's acceleration with a fixed weight sequence, even in empirical evaluations. However, this paper confirms that rendering Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a minor modification, leading to substantial improvements.

To further strengthen the paper from a technical standpoint, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate that the convergence rate is preserved. This analysis should be relatively straightforward, if I am correct. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization could provide valuable insights.

Overall, the paper presents a simple yet effective heuristic for improving the convergence rate of Nesterov's acceleration. The proposed method is theoretically grounded, and its empirical performance is promising. With some additional technical analysis and exploration of connections to other optimization methods, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
