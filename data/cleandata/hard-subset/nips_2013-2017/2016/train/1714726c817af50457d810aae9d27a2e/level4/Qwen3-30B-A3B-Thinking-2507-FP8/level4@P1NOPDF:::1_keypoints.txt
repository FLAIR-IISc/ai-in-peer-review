- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic that accelerates convergence.  
- Unlike prior adaptive averaging, the method provably dominates standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results demonstrate significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Paper is well-written and mathematically accessible, particularly for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Core argument is clear and the empirical validation is strong.  
- High practical impact: a minor adjustment to Nesterov yields dramatic performance gains and addresses benchmarking gaps.  
- Recommendation: provide a discrete-time analysis proving the adaptive reweighting preserves the convergence rate to solidify theoretical claims.  
- Recommendation: explore potential connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with a simple, effective heuristic; minor theoretical extensions would enhance rigor and impact.
