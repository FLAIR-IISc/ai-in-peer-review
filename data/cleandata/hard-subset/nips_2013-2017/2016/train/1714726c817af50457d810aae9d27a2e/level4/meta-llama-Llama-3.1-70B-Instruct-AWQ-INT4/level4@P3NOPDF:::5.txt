This paper explores accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, leveraging Lyapunov functions to achieve a desired convergence rate. The authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic, which is shown to enhance the convergence rate of the Lyapunov function. Empirical results demonstrate that this adaptive averaging approach performs comparably to, or even surpasses, adaptive restarting methods. Although I am not an expert in this field, the concept of utilizing adaptive averaging to attain a faster convergence rate for constrained convex optimization appears intriguing and promising.

The paper's quality, novelty, impact, and clarity are all satisfactory, and the authors present their ideas in a coherent and well-structured manner. However, as a non-expert reviewer, I am unable to provide in-depth comments on the technical aspects of the paper.

One potential area for improvement could be providing more context or background information on the current state of accelerated descent dynamics and the significance of the proposed adaptive averaging heuristic. This would enable readers who are not familiar with the field to better appreciate the contributions of the paper.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to constrained convex optimization, and the authors' use of Lyapunov functions and adaptive averaging is a valuable contribution to the field.
