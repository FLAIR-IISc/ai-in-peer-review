The paper proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated mirror descent, which leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration. The authors provide a unified study of a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, parameterized by two positive, continuous weight functions w and η. They give sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate and propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The authors also provide guarantees on adaptive averaging in continuous-time and prove that it preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods in discrete-time. The numerical experiments indicate that adaptive averaging performs at least as well as adaptive restarting, with significant improvements in some cases.

The empirical results show very promising speed-ups, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method. The connection to previous work, such as Krichene et al., is clearly established, and the authors provide a detailed discussion of the related literature.
