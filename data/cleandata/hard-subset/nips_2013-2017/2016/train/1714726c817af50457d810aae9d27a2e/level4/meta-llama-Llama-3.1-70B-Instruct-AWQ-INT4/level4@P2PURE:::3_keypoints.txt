- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, building on Krichene et al.'s ODE primal–dual discretization framework.
- Extends to more general averaging schemes where the primal is an average of the mirror of the dual.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under specific conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- Shows continuous-time convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2).
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with proven convergence guarantees.
- Applies the adaptive scheme in experiments and compares it to restarting techniques used in practice.
- Does not provide theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm.
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Overall: interesting and well-written with relevant new results, but open question whether improved continuous rates persist after discretization.
