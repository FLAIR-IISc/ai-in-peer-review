This paper studies fast first-order methods for perturbed convex optimization in continuous and discrete environments. The authors extend the study initiated by Kreichen et al.[7], where Nesterov's method is shown as a suitable discretization of an ODE system, combining a primitive trajectory with a double trajectory. Specifically, the primitive trajectory is obtained by taking a specific average of the double-variable mirror. In the present paper, more general average schemes are studied. Theorem 2 then achieves convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the average coefficients. In a continuous environment, convergence rates w,r,too are obtained faster than 1/t^2. An adaptive option of weighting with convergence guarantees is also studied and then used in the experimental section. Such an option is compared to restart techniques, which are known to improve the convergence of fast methods in practice. The theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included. I think this is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new average scheme for fast methods is presented in a continuous environment and is rigorous. Even though the main ideas of the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well-established in a series of papers, there are still new contributions and results. In this regard, I think the work of Atouch, Pepoux, and co-authors should be mentioned. The main theorem, showing that there are average schemes with convergence rates faster than 1/t^2, is relevant, even if it is interesting to understand whether this better rate is preserved when going through a different process.
