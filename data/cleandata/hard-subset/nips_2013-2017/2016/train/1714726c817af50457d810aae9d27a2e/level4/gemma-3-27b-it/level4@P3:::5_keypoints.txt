- Introduces a framework for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization via coupling a dual variable with a weighted-averaged primal variable.  
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to formalize accelerated convergence and provides sufficient conditions (Theorem 2).  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic to dynamically adjust weights and speed Lyapunov decrease.  
- Shows the heuristic preserves the quadratic convergence rate.  
- Empirical results indicate the adaptive heuristic matches or sometimes outperforms adaptive restarting.  
- Formalizes and generalizes prior continuous-time ODE connections to Nesterov-style acceleration and averaging.  
- Derives a primal representation and illustrates dynamics with examples (e.g., replicator equation).  
- Experiments are primarily limited to simplex-constrained problems and a small set of objectives.  
- Discretization of the adaptive averaging heuristic (Algorithm 1) needs clearer motivation and explanation.  
- Missing comparisons to more recent adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) applied to accelerated methods.  
- Some proofs (notably those using Cauchy-Lipschitz) lack intuitive exposition and could be made more accessible.  
- Overall: well-written, theoretically solid, and promising empirically.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel heuristic, clear ties to prior work, and practical potential.  
- Arguments against: limited experiments, clarity issues in discretization and technical details, and missing comparisons to modern adaptive methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify heuristic discretization and add brief discussion/comparisons to recent adaptive methods; broader experiments are desirable but not required.
