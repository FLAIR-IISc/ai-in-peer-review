Building upon an early work that connects accelerated Mirror Descent and continuous-time ODE, the authors extend the ODE dynamics with a more general averaging scheme and propose a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one of its discretizations. In some cases, the new variant of AMD with adaptive averaging outperforms existing AMD algorithms with restarting. The proposed adaptive averaging strategy is interesting and comes with a good intuition. While the paper is well-presented overall, concerns regarding its originality and usefulness remain.

Most elements, such as the Lyapunov function and discretization, directly follow from an early NIPS paper [7]. The extension from simple averaging to generalized averaging is relatively straightforward and may not be particularly useful, as the authors only consider the case with quadratic rate; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable. Some results, such as the interpretation of the energy function, reformulation into primal form, and reduction to replicator dynamics, promote a better understanding of the accelerated Mirror Descent algorithm but are not significantly important to advance machine learning.

Although the adaptive averaging is introduced to satisfy the conditions sufficient to guarantee convergence of the ODE, it is not fully theoretically grounded due to the approximation during discretization. Moreover, no explicit convergent result is provided, making it a heuristic approach, as acknowledged by the authors. Furthermore, there exist various averaging schemes for Nesterov's accelerated algorithms, and without comparing to other alternatives, it is unclear whether this adaptive variant offers significant advantages.

The experiment on a toy example with only quadratic/linear objectives is not particularly convincing in demonstrating the advantage of adaptive averaging, especially for the general case with strong convexity.

== Post-rebuttal answer ==

I have read the authors' rebuttal, in which they promise to add the following in the revision: additional higher-dimensional and non-quadratic examples, a theoretical proof of convergence of adaptive averaging, implementation of adaptive averaging on Nesterov's cubic-regularized Newton method, and a discussion comparing to other adaptive averaging schemes. I have updated my scores accordingly.
