- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, generalizing Krichene et al.’s ODE-based primal–dual averaging by exploring a broader class of averaging schemes.
- Theorem 2 proves convergence rates for these general averaging schemes under certain conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- In continuous-time, the authors derive convergence rates that improve on the classical O(1/t^2) rate.
- The paper proposes an adaptive scheme to select averaging weights and provides convergence guarantees for it.
- The adaptive scheme is empirically evaluated and benchmarked against standard restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods.
- The analysis in the continuous-time setting is rigorous, the presentation is clear, and the contributions are novel and interesting.
- The paper should cite and discuss related continuous-time work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors to better contextualize the results.
- A major limitation is that the theoretical analysis is confined to continuous-time; no formal discrete-time analysis is provided.
- An important open question remains whether the improved continuous-time convergence rates persist after discretization, which is needed to confirm practical impact.
