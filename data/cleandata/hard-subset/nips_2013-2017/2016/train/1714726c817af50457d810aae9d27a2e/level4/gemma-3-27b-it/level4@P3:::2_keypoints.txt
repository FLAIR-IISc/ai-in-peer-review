- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via continuous-time ODEs and generalizes previous ODE formulations with a broader averaging scheme.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for discretization that adjusts weights based on the Lyapunov function’s decrease rate.  
- Provides sufficient conditions for continuous-time convergence using Lyapunov stability analysis.  
- Demonstrates the replicator dynamics as a special case and presents a primal formulation.  
- Numerical experiments on simple quadratic and linear problems show the adaptive averaging often matches or outperforms existing adaptive restarting heuristics.  
- The adaptive averaging idea is intuitive and a logical extension of prior acceleration and averaging work.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid but focuses on sufficient (not necessary) conditions and stays in continuous time.  
- Discretization of the adaptive heuristic lacks a complete theoretical convergence proof and remains heuristic in practice.  
- Originality is limited because many components follow directly from the authors’ prior work (e.g., their NIPS 2016 paper).  
- Focus on achieving quadratic convergence restricts broader applicability; faster rates typically need higher-order methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: limited to simple, low-dimensional problems and not robustly comprehensive.  
- Insufficient comparison to other existing adaptive averaging and adaptive acceleration methods in the literature.  
- Overall assessment: a worthwhile but incremental contribution that would be strengthened by expanded experiments and a rigorous discretization analysis.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing experimental breadth, theoretical discretization guarantees, and comparisons in the rebuttal.
