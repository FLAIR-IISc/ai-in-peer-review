- Paper analyzes accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs parameterized by η(t) and w(t) via a Lyapunov Lr, giving sufficient conditions for Lr decrease and f(X)−f* ≤ C/r(t), unifying prior continuous-time acceleration results.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic (a(t)=w/W) that maximizes Lyapunov decrease while preserving rates, plus a complementary energy Er; proposes a discrete algorithm for r(t)=t^2 and compares empirically to restarts.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: extends averaging view of acceleration with a clean, verifiable Lyapunov framework.  
- Strength — generality: AMD family subsumes known ODEs and can attain rates faster than 1/t^2 in continuous time with suitable r(t).  
- Strength — new example: derives an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Strength — adaptive averaging: principled, simple heuristic with competitive empirical performance.  
- Strength — diagnostics: energy Er offers useful intuition and monitoring besides Lr.  
- Weakness — discrete-time theory: main-text guarantees are limited; need explicit theorem/sketch showing O(1/k^2) preservation with precise assumptions on ψ, R, and step sizes; note p>2 needs higher-order discretization.  
- Weakness — verifiability: Theorem 2’s condition (2) is trajectory-dependent/uncheckable a priori; need practical guidance on choosing η, a, t0, and w scaling and sensitivity.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: empirical study is small-scale (R^3 visuals, simplex); lacks high-dimensional, non-simplex benchmarks and sensitivity analyses to ψ, R, and step sizes.  
- Weakness — comparison details: hyperparameter tuning and computational overhead (including cost of computing/adjusting a(t)/ak) are not specified, hindering fair comparisons.  
- Weakness — literature coverage: should explicitly discuss and cite inertial/vanishing-viscosity work (e.g., Attouch–Peypouquet–Redont) given shared Lyapunov/inertial structure.  
- Quality — continuous-time analysis appears technically sound with existence/uniqueness and Lyapunov decreases; adaptive averaging is well-motivated and rate-preserving under stated conditions.  
- Quality — discrete-time algorithm is plausible and aligns with known AMD schemes, but theoretical treatment in main text is minimal and needs a clearer statement or pointer to proofs.  
- Clarity — well-written overall but notation is heavy; recommend a short “recipe” for selecting r, a, w, η and fixing minor typos and reference clarifications.  
- Originality — generalized averaging framework and adaptive averaging heuristic are novel; accelerated replicator is an impactful new instantiation.  
- Significance — advances understanding of acceleration via averaging and could inform new algorithms; impact would grow with stronger discrete-time guarantees and broader experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance — unifying continuous-time Lyapunov analysis, novel adaptive heuristic with theory and empirical gains, meaningful new examples and diagnostics.  
- Arguments against acceptance — incomplete discrete-time guarantees and limited empirical evaluation/tuning details.  
- Overall — interesting, carefully executed paper; recommend acceptance conditional on clearer discrete-time guarantees, expanded experiments, and citing related inertial dynamics literature.
