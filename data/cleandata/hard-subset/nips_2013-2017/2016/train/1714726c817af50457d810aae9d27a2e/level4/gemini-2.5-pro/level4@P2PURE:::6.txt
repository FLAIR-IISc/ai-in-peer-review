This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization. The method is guaranteed to preserve the original convergence rate for objective functions that are not strongly convex, which presents a notable advantage over existing restarting heuristics. The authors substantiate this claim with both theoretical analysis and experimental results.

However, the paper's presentation could be improved for clarity and accessibility. While the terminology and abbreviations may be familiar to experts in this specific subfield, including additional background information would make the work accessible to a broader audience. In particular, the equations between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from a more intuitive explanation to aid the reader's understanding.

Furthermore, the paper would be significantly strengthened by a discussion of the proposed method's relationship to popular adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] and Adam [2].

Finally, questions remain about the practical significance of this work. The experiments were conducted in R^3, which is a low-dimensional toy setting. It is unclear how the proposed method, and related work, scales to the high-dimensional problems typically encountered in machine learning. An evaluation in a higher-dimensional space is needed to demonstrate broader applicability.
