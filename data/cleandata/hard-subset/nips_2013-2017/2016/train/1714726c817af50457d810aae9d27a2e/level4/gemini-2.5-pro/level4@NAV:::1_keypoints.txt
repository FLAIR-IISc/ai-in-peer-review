- Builds on ODE interpretation of Nesterov to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates that is provably better than fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show significant convergence speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical argument is elegant, accessible, and well written.  
- Technical contribution is a direct extension of Krichene et al., but has substantial practical impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Demonstrates that many adaptive methods have been unfairly compared to fixed-schedule Nesterov and that a simple modification can yield dramatic improvements.  
- Recommendation: add a convergence analysis for the discretized algorithm to formally show preserved convergence rates.  
- Recommendation: discuss potential connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient, especially for strongly convex quadratics.
