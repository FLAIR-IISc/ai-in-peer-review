The paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. It continues the line of work initiated by Krichene et al. [7], where Nesterov’s method is shown to be an appropriate discretization of an ODE system coupling a primal trajectory with a dual one. In particular, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. This paper investigates more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the authors derive rates faster than 1/t^2. They also propose an adaptive choice of the weights with convergence guarantees, which is then used in the experimental section and compared with restarting techniques known to improve the practical performance of accelerated methods. The theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included.

I find this an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods, presented in the continuous setting, is rigorous. Although the main ideas behind the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well established across a series of papers, there are new contributions and results here. In this respect, the work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be cited. The main theorem, showing that some averaging schemes achieve a convergence rate faster than 1/t^2, is significant, though it would be interesting to determine whether this improved rate is preserved in the discrete procedure.
