- Paper extends AMD-ODE connections with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes an adaptive averaging strategy for discretization.
- Core contribution is the adaptive averaging strategy, which is well-motivated and sometimes outperforms AMD with restarting.
- Heavy reliance on prior NIPS work [7]; many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) directly stem from that paper.
- Generalization from simple to generalized averaging is incremental and focus on quadratic convergence limits impact.
- Some theoretical insights (energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, replicator dynamics reduction) are interesting but not groundbreaking.
- The adaptive averaging lacks a formal convergence guarantee after discretization and is treated as a heuristic.
- No comparisons to other averaging schemes for Nesterov’s methods, making practical advantage unclear.
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear examples and do not demonstrate benefits on complex or strongly convex problems.
- Post-rebuttal, authors committed to more experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other schemes.
- Reviewer updated scores positively conditional on the promised revisions but remains concerned about fundamental originality and final impact.
