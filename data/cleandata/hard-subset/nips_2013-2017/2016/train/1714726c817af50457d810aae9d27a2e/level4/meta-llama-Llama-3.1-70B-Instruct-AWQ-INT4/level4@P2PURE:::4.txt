This paper investigates a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) that incorporate a generalized averaging scheme. The authors derive sufficient conditions for the dual learning rate and weights to achieve a desired convergence rate, leveraging a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, an adaptive averaging heuristic is employed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function, providing both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons. This work contributes to a long line of research focused on empirically accelerating the convergence of accelerated methods. Notably, the authors make a crucial observation using ODEs, demonstrating that adaptively averaging the weights along solution trajectories can also yield a speedup. I found this novel method to be particularly interesting.
