- Extends prior AMD–ODE connection by introducing generalized averaging in ODEs and a novel adaptive averaging discretization.
- Adaptive AMD shows performance gains over existing AMD with restarting in some cases.
- Overall originality and practical impact are questionable; contributions are incremental.
- Generalization from simple to generalized averaging provides limited additional benefit and largely follows prior NIPS work.
- Paper focuses only on quadratic convergence rates; faster rates would require higher-order methods not broadly applicable.
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, link to replicator dynamics) deepen understanding but aren’t a major advancement.
- Adaptive averaging is motivated to meet ODE convergence conditions, but discretization approximations weaken the theoretical foundation.
- No explicit convergence proof for the adaptive scheme is presented, making it largely heuristic.
- Lacks comparison to existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods, so relative value is unclear.
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear problems and insufficient to demonstrate benefits on general strongly convex or non-quadratic tasks.
- Post-rebuttal, authors commit to adding higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, an implementation in cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes.
- Reviewer adjusted scores upward pending these substantial revisions.
