- Paper generalizes Krichene et al.'s ODE-based view of Nesterov by studying more general averaging schemes for accelerated first-order methods in constrained convex optimization (continuous and discrete settings).
- Theorem 2 provides convergence rates under conditions on averaging coefficients, demonstrating accelerated behavior (order ~1/t^2) in the continuous setting and claims existence of schemes with faster rates.
- An adaptive choice of averaging weights is proposed, proven convergent, and evaluated experimentally; it is compared against restarting techniques.
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete-time version is not included, which the reviewer views as a significant omission and limits practical applicability.
- The continuous analysis is rigorous and the paper is well-written, but related work (notably Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors) should be cited.
- Experimental results support the proposed approach despite the lack of discrete theoretic guarantees.
