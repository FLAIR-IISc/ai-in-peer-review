- Introduces a generalized continuous-time framework "Accelerated Mirror Descent" (AMD) parameterized by weight functions w and η for constrained convex optimization.  
- Provides a unified Lyapunov analysis giving sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates, generalizing Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2015).  
- Shows the framework can accelerate replicator dynamics via an appropriate averaging scheme.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively sets w(t) to accelerate Lyapunov decrease while preserving convergence guarantees.  
- Gives theoretical guarantees in continuous-time and shows the adaptive method preserves the quadratic convergence rate of discrete accelerated methods.  
- Empirical results compare adaptive averaging to heuristics (e.g., adaptive restarting) and show superior performance, especially on strongly convex problems.  
- Strength: unified theoretical framework covering many accelerated methods and offering new insights into averaging’s role.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded adaptive averaging heuristic directly targeting Lyapunov decrease.  
- Strength: strong experimental evidence and clear connection between continuous-time analysis and discrete algorithms.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is primarily continuous-time; discrete-time adaptive convergence analysis is limited.  
- Weakness: experiments are focused mainly on simplex-constrained problems; broader constraint sets would strengthen applicability.  
- Originality/significance: meaningful extension of continuous-time accelerated methods with a practical, theoretically sound alternative to restarting; recommendation: Accept.
