This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization, offering a guarantee that it preserves the original convergence rate when the objective function is not strongly convex. This advantage over existing restarting heuristics is demonstrated both theoretically and experimentally. 

However, as the paper falls outside my area of expertise, I found it challenging to follow the content. I assume that the employed abbreviations and terms are familiar to the target audience, but including background information would enhance the paper's accessibility. 

The equations between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from more intuitive explanations to facilitate comprehension. Additionally, I would appreciate a discussion on the relation between the proposed adaptive averaging method and those used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. 

Furthermore, I question the practical significance of this work, as the experiments were conducted in R^3, which appears to be a simplistic setup. It would be valuable to know how the proposed method (and related work) scales to much higher dimensions. 

A minor comment is that the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous; using a dotted line or colors to illustrate the trajectory of the primal variable would improve clarity.

[2] Kingma, Diederik, and Jimmy Ba. "Adam: A method for stochastic optimization." International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR). (2014).
