- Generalized averaging is a minor, largely trivial extension building on prior NIPS work; Lyapunov functions, discretization, and convergence are mostly inherited.  
- The extension is only meaningfully applicable in the quadratic-rate regime, which existing AMD variants already cover.  
- The paper fails to address non-quadratic objectives or settings needing higher-order methods, limiting practical significance.  
- Theoretical justification for adaptive averaging is incomplete: it’s shown at the ODE level but lacks a discrete convergence proof and leaves discretization gaps.  
- The authors do not compare the adaptive scheme to existing adaptive/averaging methods for Nesterov-type algorithms.  
- Experimental validation is weak: only quadratic/toy examples are presented, with no non-quadratic, high-dimensional, or strongly convex problems.  
- The reformulation (energy function/replicator dynamics) offers incremental, descriptive insight but no new algorithmic or practical advances.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors promise to add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a discrete convergence proof, cubic-regularized Newton implementation, and comparisons to alternatives.  
- Recommendation: current version lacks novelty and theoretical rigor; suggest weak acceptance contingent on delivering the promised revisions.
