This paper discusses a class of generalized non-linear equations (ODE) with a common average method. A Lyapunov argument provides sufficient conditions for achieving the desired convergence rate through dual learning rates and weights. Furthermore, the authors have used an adaptive average heuristic to accelerate the decay of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are provided. This is a long-term method aimed at accelerating the convergence of an empirically accelerated method. This paper makes an important observation through non-linear equations that the speed can also be increased by averaging the weights along the solution path. I found this new method very interesting.
