- Proposes adaptive reweighting of iterates to accelerate Nesterov’s method, building on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation.
- Leverages the ODE framework to design a heuristic that empirically outperforms fixed-schedule averaging and has a theoretical dominance guarantee.
- Core contribution is a simple, effective adaptive averaging scheme that modifies Nesterov’s method.
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across a range of objective functions.
- Paper is well-written with clear, accessible mathematical arguments, especially for readers familiar with prior ODE work.
- Strong combination of theoretical justification and empirical performance.
- Practically relevant: minor adaptation can substantially improve Nesterov’s performance and impact benchmarking practices.
- Suggest strengthening theory by analyzing the discretized process to confirm preservation of convergence rate.
- Recommend exploring connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics to gain further insight and improvements.
