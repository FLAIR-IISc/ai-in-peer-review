- Extends link between accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) and ODEs by generalizing averaging and proposing an adaptive averaging discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging is intuitively appealing and can outperform existing AMD with restarting in some cases.  
- Many core elements (Lyapunov function, discretization, framework) closely follow prior NIPS work [11], reducing originality.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is incremental and of limited impact, especially since focus is on the quadratic convergence case.  
- Achieving faster-than-quadratic rates would require higher-order methods, which are not generally applicable.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but are not major ML advances.  
- Theoretical foundation is incomplete: discretization approximations are unresolved and the paper lacks an explicit convergence proof.  
- Without a convergence proof the method remains heuristic, as acknowledged by the authors.  
- No comparison to many alternative adaptive averaging schemes for Nesterov-type algorithms, so practical advantage is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits in general or strongly convex settings.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof, implement adaptive averaging on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and discuss comparisons to other schemes.  
- Reviewer adjusted scores based on the authors’ promised revisions.  
- Summary pros: novel adaptive averaging, potential performance gains in specific cases, added insights into AMD dynamics.  
- Summary cons: limited originality, weak theoretical justification, narrow experimental scope, insufficient comparisons.
