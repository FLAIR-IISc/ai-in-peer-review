This paper builds on the ODE-based view of Nesterov-style acceleration introduced by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to propose and evaluate a natural heuristic that adaptively reweights algorithm iterates to speed up convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics, the proposed scheme is provably no worse than the standard fixed-schedule averaging and, in particular, is guaranteed to dominate it. The experimental results indicate consistent speed-ups across a broad class of objective functions. I found the paper clear and enjoyable. The mathematical development is concise and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.’s framework, and the empirical gains are compelling. Although the technical contribution is a fairly direct extension of Krichene et al., I expect substantial impact given how ubiquitous Nesterov’s acceleration is in applications. All too often—even in empirical comparisons—new, highly adaptive methods are benchmarked against Nesterov’s scheme with a fixed weight schedule; this paper shows that making Nesterov adaptive requires only a minor modification and can yield substantial improvements. To strengthen the technical contribution, I recommend adding an analysis of the discrete-time algorithm demonstrating preservation of the convergence rate; to my eye, this should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in exploring connections between this adaptive averaging of Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient algorithm for strongly convex quadratic problems.
