This paper investigates a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) employing a generalized averaging scheme. The authors provide sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and averaging weights to guarantee a specific convergence rate, substantiated by a Lyapunov analysis. Furthermore, they introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical results and numerical comparisons are presented to support their claims. A significant body of work focuses on empirically accelerating the convergence of optimization methods; this paper offers a key insight through the lens of ODEs, demonstrating that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can also yield performance improvements. I found this novel approach to be highly compelling.
