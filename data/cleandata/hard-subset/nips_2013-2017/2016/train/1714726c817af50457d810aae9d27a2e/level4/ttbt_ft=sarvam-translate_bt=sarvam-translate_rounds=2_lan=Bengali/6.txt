This paper presents an adaptive mean heuristic to accelerate bounded convex optimization. The presented method ensures that it preserves the original convergence rate even when the objective function is not strongly convex. This is an advantage compared to existing restart heuristics, and this paper demonstrates this theoretically and experimentally. It was difficult for me to follow the content of this paper because it is outside my field of expertise. I assume that the abbreviations and terms used are familiar to the readers addressed by this paper, but I would suggest including background information. Equations between lines 33, 34, and 46, 47 require more intuitive explanations. Furthermore, I would like to know the relationship with adaptive mean methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. Finally, I am interested in knowing the practical significance of this work. The experiments were conducted in R^3, which is clearly a toy setup. How does your method (and related work) scale to much larger dimensions? Small comment: The mention of hard lines in Figure 1 is unclear.
