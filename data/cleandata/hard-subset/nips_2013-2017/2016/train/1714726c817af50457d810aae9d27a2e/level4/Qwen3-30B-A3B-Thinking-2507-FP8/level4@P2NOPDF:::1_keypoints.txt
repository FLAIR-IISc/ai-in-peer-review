- Proposes an adaptive reweighting heuristic for Nesterov’s acceleration based on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation.  
- Method adaptively adjusts iterate weights to accelerate convergence.  
- Provides a guaranteed improvement over standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive averaging approaches.  
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions, including non-strongly convex settings.  
- Mathematical exposition is elegant, accessible, and builds cleanly on prior ODE-based analyses.  
- Empirical validation is compelling and robust.  
- Practical impact is high: minor adaptive modification can dramatically improve the ubiquitous Nesterov baseline.  
- Core theoretical claim: adaptive reweighting is guaranteed to outperform fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Suggest adding a discretization error analysis to prove convergence rates are preserved under discretization (Euler or higher-order).  
- Recommend exploring connections between the adaptive averaging mechanism and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with sound theory and practice; minor technical additions would elevate it — recommend acceptance.
