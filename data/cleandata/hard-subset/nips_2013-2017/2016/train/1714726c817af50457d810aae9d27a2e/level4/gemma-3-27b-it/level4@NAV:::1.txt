This paper introduces a novel and intuitive heuristic for accelerating Nesterov’s accelerated gradient descent by adaptively reweighting iterates, drawing inspiration from the ODE perspective established by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015). A key strength of this approach is its theoretical guarantee to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging schemes. The empirical results demonstrate consistent speed-ups across a broad range of functions. 

The paper is well-written and the mathematical exposition is clear and accessible, particularly for those familiar with the foundational work of Krichene et al. The promising empirical improvements, coupled with the relatively simple technical development – building directly upon existing results – suggest this work has significant potential impact. Nesterov’s acceleration is a ubiquitous optimization technique, and this paper convincingly demonstrates that a modest adaptation can yield substantial performance gains, a point often overlooked in comparisons with more complex, adaptive methods.

To further strengthen the contribution, I recommend an analysis of the discretized process to confirm the preservation of convergence rates. This appears to be a feasible extension of the current work. Additionally, exploring potential links between this adaptive averaging technique and the Conjugate Gradient method in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization would be a valuable addition.
