- Proposes a generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) framework parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Uses Lyapunov arguments to derive sufficient continuous-time conditions for achieving target convergence rates.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically sets weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease while preserving quadratic convergence.  
- Applies the framework to replicator dynamics on the simplex, connecting acceleration to evolutionary game theory.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a solid continuous-time foundation for understanding discrete accelerated methods.  
- Novel extension of prior accelerated mirror descent via generalized averaging and adaptive weights.  
- Adaptive averaging is a principled alternative to ad-hoc heuristics and retains theoretical guarantees.  
- Paper is very well organized with clear and accessible mathematical exposition for experts.  
- Numerical experiments are promising but lack diversity in problem instances and comparisons with more baselines.  
- Discrete-time implementation of the adaptive heuristic is presented briefly and needs more practical detail.  
- Connection to adaptive first-order methods (e.g., AdaGrad, Adam) is not clearly distinguished and should be clarified.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that outperforms existing heuristics in tested scenarios, though practical applicability needs expansion.
