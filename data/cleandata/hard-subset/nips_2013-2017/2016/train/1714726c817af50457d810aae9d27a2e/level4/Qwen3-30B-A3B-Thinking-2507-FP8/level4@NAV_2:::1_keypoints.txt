- Extends ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration to derive an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated mirror descent (AMD).  
- Formalizes a broad family of AMD dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η, with sufficient convergence conditions via a Lyapunov argument.  
- Shows how the framework accelerates replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate convergence while preserving quadratic convergence rate in continuous time.  
- Builds on continuous-time analyses of Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2015).  
- Mathematical development is elegant and well-motivated, with a clear Lyapunov-based intuition for acceleration.  
- Connection to and acceleration of replicator dynamics is compelling and well-executed.  
- Empirical results are thorough and consistently improve over existing heuristics (adaptive restarting) across several objective types (strong/weak convex quadratics, linear, KL).  
- Important practical contribution showing simple adaptive modifications to Nesterov’s method yield significant improvements.  
- Main weakness: lacks a formal, rigorous analysis of the discretized algorithm to prove discrete-time convergence-rate preservation.  
- Supplementary material gives a bound, but a more detailed discrete-time analysis should be included in the main text.  
- Scores highly on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Recommendation: accept, with camera-ready requirement to strengthen discrete-time convergence analysis.
