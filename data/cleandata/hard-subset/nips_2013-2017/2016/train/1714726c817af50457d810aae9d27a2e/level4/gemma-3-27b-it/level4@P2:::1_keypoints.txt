- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s method to propose a novel adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Core idea: dynamically reweight iterates to accelerate convergence.
- Theoretical guarantee: adaptive averaging will not perform worse than standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across diverse test functions.
- Paper is well-written with clear, accessible mathematical arguments.
- Insightful connection to replicator dynamics when applied to simplex constraints.
- Correctly highlights a literature gap on convergence guarantees for heuristics like adaptive restarting.
- Recommendation: analyze the discretized process to show preservation of the continuous-time convergence rate.
- Suggestion: explore connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, promising empirical results, practical significance, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: contribution is somewhat incremental.
- Arguments against acceptance (cont.): lack of theoretical analysis for the discrete-time implementation is a minor gap.
- Overall assessment: strong, useful incremental contribution with potential impact; recommends acceptance.
