- Paper studies accelerated descent methods for constrained convex optimization using a Lyapunov function framework.
- Authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to improve the Lyapunov-function convergence rate.
- Experimental results show adaptive averaging performs comparably to, and sometimes better than, adaptive restarting.
- Reviewer’s expertise in this subarea is limited but finds the core idea interesting and potentially impactful.
- Theoretical foundation appears sound based on the Lyapunov analysis, though reviewer cannot fully verify technical depth.
- Results provide preliminary evidence supporting the efficacy of the proposed method.
- Novelty is the specific application of adaptive averaging within Lyapunov-based accelerated descent for constrained problems.
- Potential impact includes more efficient optimization algorithms for large-scale convex problems in ML and related fields.
- Paper is generally clearly presented.
- Reviewer requests an intuitive explanation for why adaptive averaging improves the Lyapunov convergence beyond the math.
- Reviewer recommends broader experimental comparisons with more baselines to strengthen claims.
- Reviewer asks for information on the computational cost of the adaptive averaging heuristic relative to other methods.
