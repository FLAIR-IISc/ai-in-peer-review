- Paper extends ODE dynamics with generalized averaging and proposes an adaptive averaging discretization that outperforms some AMD variants with restarting in certain cases.  
- The adaptive averaging strategy is novel and well-motivated.  
- Originality and practical usefulness are concerns because many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from prior NIPS work [7].  
- The move from simple to generalized averaging is incremental and of limited benefit since the paper focuses only on the quadratic-rate case; faster rates would require higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function), primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics improve understanding but aren’t a substantial advancement for ML.  
- Theoretical foundation for the adaptive averaging is weak due to discretization approximations and the absence of an explicit convergence proof, leaving the method largely heuristic.  
- No comparison to other averaging schemes for Nesterov’s algorithms, so it’s unclear if the adaptive variant offers meaningful improvement.  
- Experiments use only quadratic/linear objectives and do not compellingly demonstrate advantages for general strongly convex functions.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
