- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, extending Krichene et al. to more general averaging schemes in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Uses a unified ODE analysis with Lyapunov function L_r(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+D_{ψ*}(Z,z*).  
- Provides sufficient conditions on η, w, and r for L_r to be a Lyapunov function and shows f(X(t)) converges at rate 1/r(t).  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic and proves convergence-rate guarantees for the continuous dynamics.  
- Main theoretical result: there exist averaging schemes achieving faster-than-1/t^2 convergence in continuous time.  
- Experimental comparison shows the adaptive averaging heuristic outperforms restarting techniques, especially in the strongly convex case.  
- Analysis is rigorous and the paper is well-written with thorough explanations of methods and heuristics.  
- Strengths: rigorous new averaging analysis, provable adaptive heuristic, thorough empirical comparison with restarting.  
- Weaknesses: lacks theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm and unclear whether improved continuous rates carry over to discretization.  
- Originality: introduces a novel averaging scheme and adaptive heuristic that are significant contributions.  
- Significance: makes a meaningful contribution to optimization with potential practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to include theoretical discrete analysis in a future revision.
