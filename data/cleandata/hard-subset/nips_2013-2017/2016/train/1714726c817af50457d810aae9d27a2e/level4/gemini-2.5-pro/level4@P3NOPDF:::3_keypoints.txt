- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending the ODE interpretation of Nesterov-style methods.  
- Builds on Krichene et al.'s framework where the primal trajectory is an averaging of the mirror of the dual variable.  
- Main contribution: generalizes the averaging scheme used to form the primal trajectory.  
- Theorem 2: derives convergence rates for these generalized averaging schemes under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- Shows existence of continuous-time averaging schemes achieving convergence rates faster than the classical O(1/t^2).  
- Proposes an adaptive weight-selection scheme and proves convergence guarantees for it.  
- Empirical section uses the adaptive scheme and compares performance to standard restarting techniques.  
- Analysis is rigorous and contributes valuable new insights to continuous-time accelerated dynamics.  
- Major limitation: no theoretical analysis for the discrete (algorithmic) version, leaving practical impact and rate preservation after discretization unclear.  
- Paper should acknowledge and discuss related continuous-time dynamics work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Key open question: whether the faster continuous-time rates can be translated into a provably superior discrete algorithm.
