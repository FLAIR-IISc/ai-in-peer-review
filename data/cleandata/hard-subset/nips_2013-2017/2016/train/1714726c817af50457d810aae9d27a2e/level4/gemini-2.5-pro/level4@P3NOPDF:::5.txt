This paper presents an analysis of accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. By constructing a suitable Lyapunov function, the authors formally establish the desired O(1/k^2) convergence rate for their proposed continuous-time dynamics. A key contribution of this work is the introduction of an adaptive averaging heuristic, which serves as a practical discretization of the continuous-time analysis. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed adaptive averaging scheme performs comparably to, and in some cases surpasses, the well-established adaptive restarting technique.

While my expertise is not primarily in the analysis of continuous-time dynamics for optimization, the paper's core idea of using an adaptive averaging scheme to achieve acceleration for constrained problems is both interesting and promising. The following is a detailed assessment of the work.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Theoretical Grounding:** The use of a Lyapunov function to analyze the convergence properties of the proposed dynamics provides a rigorous foundation for the paper's claims. This approach is standard and well-regarded in the field of dynamics and control, and its application here lends significant credibility to the method's theoretical underpinnings.

2.  **Novel Heuristic:** The primary contribution, the adaptive averaging scheme, presents a compelling alternative to adaptive restarting. Restarting schemes can sometimes be aggressive, discarding valuable information from previous iterations. An averaging approach is intuitively appealing as it seeks to smooth the trajectory and retain past information, which could lead to more stable and robust performance, as suggested by the experiments.

3.  **Empirical Validation:** The authors provide experimental results that compare their proposed method against adaptive restarting. The findings, which show that adaptive averaging performs on par with or better than this strong baseline, effectively validate the practical utility of the proposed approach.

**Areas for Improvement / Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Clarity and Intuition:** While the theoretical analysis is central to the paper, a more detailed intuitive explanation of the adaptive averaging mechanism would be beneficial for a broader audience. For instance, could the authors elaborate on the specific conditions or problem geometries where averaging is expected to outperform restarting? Providing more intuition behind the mechanism would strengthen the paper's impact and accessibility.

2.  **Scope of Experiments:** The experimental section could be strengthened by including a wider variety of constrained convex problems. It would be valuable to understand the sensitivity of the adaptive averaging scheme to different types of constraints (e.g., simple box constraints vs. more complex linear or second-order cone constraints). Furthermore, an analysis of the method's sensitivity to its own hyperparameters would be a welcome addition.

3.  **Discussion of Limitations:** The paper presents the method in a positive light, which is understandable. However, a discussion of its potential limitations would make the work more complete. Are there classes of functions or constraints for which this method might be less effective? How does the computational overhead of the adaptive averaging scheme compare to that of adaptive restarting?

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that introduces a novel and well-motivated heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization. The approach is theoretically sound and supported by promising empirical evidence. The core idea of using adaptive averaging instead of restarting is compelling and could have a notable impact on the field. The paper would be further improved by providing deeper intuition, expanding the experimental evaluation, and discussing the method's potential limitations. Nevertheless, the work represents a valuable contribution to the optimization literature.
