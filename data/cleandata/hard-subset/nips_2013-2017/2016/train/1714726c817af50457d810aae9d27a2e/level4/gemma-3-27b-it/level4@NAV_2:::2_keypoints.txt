- Paper extends prior ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent to a more general averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms existing AMD algorithms with restarting; strategy is intuitively appealing and novel.  
- Originality and broader impact are concerns because the work builds heavily on prior results (Lyapunov function, discretization, framework largely follow [11]).  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is incremental and limited in impact, especially since the paper focuses on quadratic-rate cases.  
- Achieving faster-than-quadratic rates would require higher-order methods that are not generally applicable.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal form, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but do not substantially advance ML.  
- Adaptive averaging lacks full theoretical grounding due to discretization approximations and provides no explicit convergence proof, making it effectively a heuristic.  
- Paper does not compare to numerous other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving benefits of the adaptive variant unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to simple quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate advantages or address strong-convexity scenarios.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof, implement on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and compare to other adaptive averaging schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
