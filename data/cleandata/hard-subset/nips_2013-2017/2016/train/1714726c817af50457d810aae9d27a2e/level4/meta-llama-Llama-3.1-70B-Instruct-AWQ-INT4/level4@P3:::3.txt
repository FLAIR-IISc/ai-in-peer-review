The paper "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics" presents a thorough study of accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors extend their previous work, initiated in [11], where they showed that Nesterov's method can be interpreted as a discretization of a continuous-time ODE system, coupling a primal trajectory with a dual one through a specific averaging scheme. This paper generalizes this approach by exploring more versatile averaging schemes.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. Theorem 2 provides a unified framework for analyzing these convergence rates in the continuous setting. Notably, the authors demonstrate that convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 can be achieved under certain conditions.

One of the paper's strengths is its rigorous analysis of the continuous dynamics, which builds upon the well-established ideas presented in a series of papers on this topic. However, it would be beneficial to acknowledge the work of Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors in this area.

The main theorem, showing the existence of averaging schemes with convergence rates faster than 1/t^2, is a significant contribution. Nevertheless, it would be interesting to investigate whether this improved rate is preserved when transitioning to the discrete procedure.

The paper also proposes an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees, which is then compared to restarting techniques known to enhance the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. Although the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included, the experimental section provides valuable insights into the performance of the proposed adaptive averaging scheme.

In conclusion, this is a well-written paper that presents interesting and rigorous analysis of new averaging schemes for accelerated methods. While some aspects, such as the preservation of the improved convergence rate in the discrete setting, remain to be explored, the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of convex optimization.
