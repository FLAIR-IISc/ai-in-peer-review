- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using Lyapunov function analysis to establish convergence guarantees.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic intended to accelerate the Lyapunov-based method.  
- Experiments show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms adaptive restarting on benchmark problems.  
- Core idea—adaptive averaging for accelerated convergence—is novel and promising.  
- Lyapunov framework provides a rigorous, well-motivated foundation for the analysis.  
- The heuristic is clearly defined, simple to integrate, and yields measurable improvements.  
- Heuristic adapts to problem structure without requiring prior knowledge of problem parameters, unlike restarting.  
- Experimental section is thorough, using standard test cases and clear convergence metrics that support claims.  
- Minor concern: limited discussion of the computational overhead introduced by adaptive averaging.  
- Minor concern: insufficient exploration of theoretical bounds under non-smooth constraints.  
- Writing is generally clear, though some technical passages need more detail on the heuristic’s adaptation logic.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, impactful; suitable for publication with minor revisions.
