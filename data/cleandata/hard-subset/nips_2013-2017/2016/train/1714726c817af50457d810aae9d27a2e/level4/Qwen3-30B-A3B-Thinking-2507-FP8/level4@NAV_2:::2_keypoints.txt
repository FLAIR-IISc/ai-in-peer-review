- Extends accelerated mirror descent ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging empirically outperforms existing restarting heuristics in some cases, notably for strongly convex objectives.  
- Theoretical analysis using Lyapunov functions is technically sound and provides clear convergence conditions linked to prior work.  
- Connection to replicator dynamics for simplex constraints is an interesting application in evolutionary game theory.  
- Numerical experiments indicate practical benefits but are limited in scope.  
- Core theoretical contributions heavily build on the authors' NIPS 2016 work, reducing apparent novelty.  
- The generalized averaging extension is somewhat trivial in practice since the paper focuses on the well-known quadratic rate case.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic lacks rigorous discrete-time convergence guarantees; only continuous-time results are provided.  
- Experiments are limited to simple quadratic and linear objectives on the simplex in R^3, failing to show performance on complex or high-dimensional problems.  
- The paper does not sufficiently compare the adaptive averaging to other existing adaptive schemes for accelerated methods.  
- Originality is limited: the generalized scheme and heuristic are incremental refinements rather than substantial new insights.  
- Significance is modest: provides a useful perspective but does not markedly advance ML practice; the replicator link is interesting but not transformative.  
- Overall quality: continuous-time analysis is strong, discrete-time implementation lacks rigor; presentation is clear and well-organized.  
- Arguments for acceptance: coherent extension of continuous-time dynamics with a novel heuristic and promising numerical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient discrete-time theory, and inadequate experimental validation and comparisons.
