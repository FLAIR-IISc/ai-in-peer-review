- Builds on Krichene et al.'s NIPS 2015 ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration.
- Proposes a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting algorithm iterates to accelerate convergence.
- Method guarantees dominance over standard fixed-schedule averaging in convergence rate, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Paper is well-written, enjoyable, and mathematically accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Technical development is a direct extension of Krichene et al., but has substantial potential impact given Nesterov's widespread use.
- Highlights that many prior empirical evaluations incorrectly compare adaptive methods against Nesterov with fixed weights; this work shows a minor modification yields dramatic gains.
- Reviewer recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate the convergence rate is preserved.
- Reviewer suggests exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
