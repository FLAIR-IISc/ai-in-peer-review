- Extends Krichene et al. (2015) to propose an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization via AMD dynamics parameterized by η(t) and w(t).  
- Formalizes sufficient conditions for quadratic (1/t²) convergence using Lyapunov analysis (Theorems 2–3).  
- Shows the adaptive averaging rule dynamically minimizes the Lyapunov derivative and preserves the 1/t² rate.  
- Empirically outperforms existing restarting heuristics across simplex-constrained problems, including accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Continuous-time analysis is technically sound and rigorous under minimal assumptions.  
- The adaptive heuristic is novel and provides theoretical guarantees where restarting often lacks them for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate superior performance on strongly convex quadratics, linear functions, and KL divergence.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear, with logical development from continuous-time dynamics to primal algorithms and examples; Algorithm 1 is well-justified.  
- The replicator dynamics acceleration is a fresh, significant application for evolutionary game theory.  
- The work is practically significant: a simple modification with broad applicability and tangible gains for practitioners.  
- Main technical gap: no discrete-time convergence rate proof; practical impact depends on preserving 1/t² in discrete implementations.  
- Recommendation: add a concise discrete Lyapunov argument to confirm discrete-time 1/t² preservation.  
- Underdeveloped comparison to Conjugate Gradient—should clarify differences or remove the reference to avoid misleading readers.  
- Suggestion: better theoretically link Lyapunov decay to the absence of trajectory oscillations to explain why adaptive averaging outperforms restarting.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful paper; recommended for acceptance pending the discrete-rate analysis addition.
