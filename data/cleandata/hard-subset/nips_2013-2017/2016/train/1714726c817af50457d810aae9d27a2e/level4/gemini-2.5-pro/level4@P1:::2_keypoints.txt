- Paper generalizes AMD ODEs with a flexible averaging scheme and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for discrete-time AMD to speed Lyapunov decrease.  
- Novelty: the adaptive averaging strategy is new and motivated by accelerating the continuous-time Lyapunov function decrease.  
- Insightful interpretations: provides energy function analogy, primal-space reformulation, and connection to replicator dynamics for deeper understanding.  
- Originality concern: core framework (Lyapunov function, discretization) is largely borrowed from prior work [11], and the averaging generalization is incremental.  
- Practical impact limited: analysis and experiments focus on achieving quadratic convergence rate, with faster rates requiring higher-order methods that are often impractical.  
- Significance unclear: broader impact on advancing machine learning methods from the proposed interpretations is not well established.  
- Theoretical gap: adaptive heuristic is motivated in continuous time but lacks a formal convergence proof in discrete time, leaving Algorithm 1 as a heuristic.  
- Comparative baseline shortfall: paper does not compare or contrast with other adaptive schemes for Nesterov acceleration.  
- Experimental limitations: empirical evaluation is confined to low-dimensional toy problems (quadratic and linear), insufficient to demonstrate benefits for large-scale or general strongly convex problems.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will provide a theoretical convergence proof for the adaptive averaging algorithm.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will implement adaptive averaging for Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add a discussion comparing their method to other adaptive schemes.
