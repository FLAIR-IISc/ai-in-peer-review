- Leverages Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.
- Method is guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical evaluation shows significant speed-ups across a general class of functions.
- Mathematical argument is straightforward and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Empirical results are promising and showcase dramatic improvements from a minor modification.
- Technical development is a close extension of prior work but has substantial potential impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.
- Suggests analyzing the discretized algorithm to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Recommends exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
