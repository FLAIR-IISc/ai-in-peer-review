- Presents a clean, general continuous-time framework for accelerated mirror descent using Lyapunov analysis.  
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function.  
- Provides valuable theoretical insights, including a primal-space formulation and acceleration of the replicator dynamics.  
- Shows promising empirical results on low-dimensional, simplex-constrained problems, often outperforming restarting heuristics.  
- Largely incremental over prior NIPS work, reusing the core Lyapunov structure and focusing on the same quadratic rate.  
- The adaptive heuristic lacks rigorous discrete-time convergence guarantees in the main paper.  
- Empirical validation is limited to low-dimensional synthetic tasks, leaving scalability and real-world effectiveness unclear.  
- Comparison is narrow, evaluating only against restarting heuristics rather than other adaptive optimizers or averaging variants.  
- Authors commit to adding higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments.  
- Authors plan to provide a formal convergence proof for the discrete-time adaptive averaging algorithm.  
- Authors will implement the adaptive averaging idea for higher-order methods (e.g., Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton).  
- Authors will expand the discussion and comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes.
