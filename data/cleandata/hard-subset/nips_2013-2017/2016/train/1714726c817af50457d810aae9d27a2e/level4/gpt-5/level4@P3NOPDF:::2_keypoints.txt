- Paper extends accelerated mirror descent (AMD) via generalized averaging and proposes a novel, simple adaptive averaging strategy with some empirical gains.
- Presents clear continuous-time ODE perspective, Lyapunov analysis, and interpretive reformulations (primal form, replicator dynamics).
- Adaptive averaging is intuitively motivated and easy to implement.
- Continuous-time analysis and Lyapunov construction are well presented and pedagogically useful.
- Reformulations may be valuable for community understanding.
- In some experiments, adaptive averaging outperforms restart-based AMD.
- Main originality concern: many technical components closely follow prior work [7], making the contribution feel incremental.
- Generalizing averaging is straightforward and its practical value is limited when focusing on quadratic-rate behavior.
- Adaptive scheme lacks rigorous discrete-time convergence or rate guarantees and is currently heuristic due to uncontrolled discretization approximations.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to low-dimensional toy problems (quadratic/linear), insufficient to demonstrate consistent benefits or robustness.
- Missing comparisons to existing averaging and restart strategies, leaving unclear when adaptive averaging is preferable.
- Interpretive results improve understanding but have modest practical impact unless paired with stronger theory and broader experiments.
- Requested improvements: provide discrete-time convergence/rate analysis and clarify preserved continuous-time conditions and step-size requirements.
- Requested empirical expansions: higher-dimensional/non-quadratic/strongly convex tasks, composite objectives, multiple baselines, wall-clock time, hyperparameter sensitivity, and ablations.
- Clarify scope/limitations: identify problem structures where generalized/adaptive averaging helps or hurts, and discuss integration with higher-order methods if faster rates are out of scope.
- Presentation suggestions: reduce overlap with [7], separate continuous- vs discrete-time claims, and improve notation consistency and assumption gathering.
- Post-rebuttal: authors committed to added experiments, a convergence proof, implementation in cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons; reviewer updated scores conditionally.
- Overall: originality moderate, current quality limited by lack of discrete-time guarantees and narrow experiments, clarity good, and significance uncertain pending promised revisions.
