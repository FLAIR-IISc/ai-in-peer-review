- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE framework for Nesterov's acceleration.  
- Proposes a simple adaptive reweighting strategy for iterates that accelerates convergence.  
- Method provably dominates standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical exposition is clear and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Paper has high potential impact because Nesterov acceleration is ubiquitous in practice.  
- Demonstrates that only minor adjustments to Nesterov's method yield substantial performance gains versus fixed weights.  
- Reviewer recommends adding a convergence rate analysis for the discretized algorithm to confirm rate preservation.  
- Reviewer suggests investigating connections to Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics.
