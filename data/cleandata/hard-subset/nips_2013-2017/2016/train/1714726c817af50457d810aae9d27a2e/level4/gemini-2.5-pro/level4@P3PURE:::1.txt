This paper leverages the ordinary differential equation (ODE) interpretation of Nesterov's accelerated method, as presented by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015), to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the algorithm's iterates to accelerate convergence. In contrast to previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging scheme. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed method yields significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.

I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, particularly for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. At the same time, the empirical results show very promising speed-ups. While the technical development is a direct extension of the work by Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact due to the pervasive use of Nesterov's accelerated method in numerous applications. In many empirical evaluations, new and highly adaptive methods are often compared against Nesterov's acceleration using a fixed weight sequence. This paper provides valuable confirmation that a simple modification is all that is required to make Nesterov's method adaptive, leading to dramatic performance improvements.

To further strengthen the paper from a technical standpoint, I would recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to show that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I am not mistaken, this analysis should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in a discussion of the possible connections between this adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method, particularly in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization.
