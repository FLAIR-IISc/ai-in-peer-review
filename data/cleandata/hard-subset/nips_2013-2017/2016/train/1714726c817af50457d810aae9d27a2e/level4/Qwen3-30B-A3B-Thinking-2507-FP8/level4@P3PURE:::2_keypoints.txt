- Paper proposes generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging strategy for discretizations of accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD).  
- Reviewer questions the paper's originality and overall significance.  
- Core technical pieces (Lyapunov function, discretization, generalized averaging) largely derive from prior NIPS paper [7].  
- The generalized averaging extension appears trivial and is ultimately analyzed only for quadratic convergence rates.  
- Achieving faster convergence would require higher-order methods, which are not generally applicable.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but offer limited ML impact.  
- Adaptive averaging is motivated by ODE convergence conditions but lacks full theoretical grounding after discretization.  
- No explicit convergence guarantee is provided for the discrete adaptive algorithm, making it heuristic.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to alternative averaging schemes for Nesterov-style accelerations.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy example with quadratic/linear objectives and do not demonstrate benefits for general strongly convex problems.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors promise additional experiments, a theoretical convergence proof, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparative discussion; reviewer updated score accordingly.
