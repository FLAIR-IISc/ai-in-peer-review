- Studies a class of ODEs for accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with a generalized averaging scheme over constrained convex problems.
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and averaging weights for quadratic convergence.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to speed the decrease of the Lyapunov function, with theoretical guarantees in continuous and discrete time.
- Presents a primal representation of the dynamics and an illustrative application to replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging performs comparably to or better than adaptive restarting heuristics.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and the Lyapunov-based approach is well-motivated.
- Key conceptual contribution: demonstrating via ODEs that adaptive averaging along trajectories can achieve acceleration.
- Paper is well-structured, reproducible, and situates itself relative to adaptive methods like Adagrad/Adam while noting differing focus.
- Convergence conditions given may be restrictive in practice; robustness to violations is not fully explored.
- Numerical evaluation is limited to a few examples; broader experiments would strengthen empirical claims.
- Discussion of related work could be expanded to include recent advances (e.g., Attouch et al.) and make links more explicit.
- More detailed discussion is needed on the choice of r(t) and its implications for discretization schemes.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with novel, theoretically grounded contributions and promising results—recommend acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
