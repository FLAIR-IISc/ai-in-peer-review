- Introduces a novel adaptive heuristic for reweighting iterates based on the ODE perspective of Nesterov’s acceleration (authors' NIPS 2015).
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the adaptive averaging outperforms standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups across a range of functions with compelling experimental results.
- Writing and mathematical exposition are clear and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Technical development is a natural extension of prior work but has significant potential impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.
- Demonstrates that a small modification adapting Nesterov’s method can yield substantial practical improvements.
- Recommends further analysis of the discretized process to show preservation of the convergence rate (feasible extension).
- Suggests exploring connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- Quality: technically sound; claims well-supported by analysis and experiments.
- Clarity: well-organized and provides sufficient detail for expert reproduction.
- Originality: novel problem formulation and meaningful build on existing techniques with proper citations.
- Significance: practical, likely to be adopted, advances state-of-the-art by giving guaranteed adaptation.
- For acceptance: theoretically and empirically validated improvement, well-motivated heuristic, continuous-time insights, convincing experiments.
- Against acceptance: lacks guarantees for the discretized process and limited exploration of links to other optimization methods.
- Overall verdict: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommendation to accept.
