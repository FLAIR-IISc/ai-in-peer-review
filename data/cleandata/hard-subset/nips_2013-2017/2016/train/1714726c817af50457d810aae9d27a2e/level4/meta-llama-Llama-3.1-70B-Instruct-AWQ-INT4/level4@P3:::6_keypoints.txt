- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves original convergence rates for non-strongly convex objectives, improving over restarting heuristics.  
- Theoretical and experimental results both support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper with a strong introduction and motivation for continuous-time first-order dynamics.  
- Main contribution: a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics generalizing accelerated mirror descent.  
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function and gives sufficient conditions on weight functions to guarantee continuous-time convergence rates.  
- Demonstrates that the replicator ODE can be accelerated by averaging and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence.  
- Experiments show the adaptive averaging heuristic matches or outperforms existing restarting heuristics (experiments conducted in R^3).  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in convex optimization and dynamical systems; could be more accessible with additional background material.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion connecting to adaptive averaging methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional (R^3) settings, leaving scalability to higher dimensions unclear.  
- Minor comment: Figure 1’s solid-line reference is ambiguous—use dotted lines or colors to clarify the primal trajectory.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; reviewer rating 8/10.
