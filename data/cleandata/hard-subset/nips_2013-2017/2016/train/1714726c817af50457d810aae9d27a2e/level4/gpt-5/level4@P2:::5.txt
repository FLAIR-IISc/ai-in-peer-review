This paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The authors model the dynamics as a coupling between a dual variable that accumulates gradients at a time-varying rate and a primal variable obtained by weighted averaging of the mirrored dual trajectory. Using a Lyapunov argument, they derive sufficient conditions on the accumulation schedule and averaging weights to guarantee a target convergence rate. They further introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts the weights along the trajectory to decrease the Lyapunov function more rapidly. Empirical results indicate that adaptive averaging performs at least as well as adaptive restarting and can yield notable improvements in some cases.
