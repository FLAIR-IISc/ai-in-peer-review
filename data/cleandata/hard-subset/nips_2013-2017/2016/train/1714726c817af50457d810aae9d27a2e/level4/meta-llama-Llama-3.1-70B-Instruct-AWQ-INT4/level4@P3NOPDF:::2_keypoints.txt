- Paper extends earlier ODE-based connection for accelerated Mirror Descent by introducing generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms existing AMD algorithms with restarting and is intuitively appealing.  
- Major originality/usefulness concerns: many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) largely follow prior NIPS work.  
- Generalized averaging extension is straightforward and limited to quadratic-rate cases; faster rates would need higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but are not major ML advances.  
- Adaptive averaging lacks full theoretical grounding due to discretization approximations and no explicit convergence proof, making it heuristic.  
- The paper does not compare the adaptive scheme to other existing averaging strategies for Nesterov methods, leaving its practical benefit unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate advantages for general or strongly convex cases.  
- Authors’ rebuttal promises higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other schemes, which would address some concerns.
