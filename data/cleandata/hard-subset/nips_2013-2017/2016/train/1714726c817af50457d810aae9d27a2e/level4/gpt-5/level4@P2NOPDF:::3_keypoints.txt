- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous-time and discrete settings.  
- Extends Krichene et al. by viewing Nesterov as discretization of a coupled primal–dual ODE.  
- Uses primal trajectories obtained by averaging a mirror transform of the dual variable.  
- Generalizes the framework to analyze a broader class of averaging schemes.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under suitable conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- In continuous time, derives rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive rule for choosing averaging weights and provides convergence guarantees for it.  
- Empirically evaluates the adaptive strategy and compares it to restarting techniques.  
- Does not provide a theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and offers new continuous-time insights despite established prior work.  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous, and the main theorem (rates >1/t^2) is significant.  
- Should acknowledge related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- It remains open whether improved continuous-time rates persist after discretization and under what stability/step-size conditions.
