This paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, considering both continuous and discrete settings. Building upon the framework established by Krichene et al. [7], which demonstrates the connection between Nesterov’s method and a discretization of an ODE system involving coupled primal and dual trajectories, the authors explore more generalized averaging schemes for constructing the primal trajectory from the dual variable. Theorem 2 presents convergence rate results under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. Specifically, the continuous setting yields convergence rates exceeding 1/t<sup>2</sup>. An adaptive weight selection strategy, accompanied by theoretical convergence guarantees, is proposed and evaluated in the experimental section, with comparisons made to restarting techniques commonly used to enhance the practical performance of accelerated methods. Notably, the theoretical analysis currently focuses on the continuous algorithm, and a discrete analysis is not included. 

Overall, this is a compelling and clearly written contribution. The analysis of these novel averaging schemes is rigorous and provides valuable insights into accelerated methods. While the underlying continuous dynamics analysis benefits from a growing body of related work – including that of Attouch, Peypouquet, and collaborators – the paper presents novel contributions and results. The key finding, demonstrating the existence of averaging schemes achieving convergence rates faster than 1/t<sup>2</sup>, is significant. Future work exploring the preservation of this improved rate in the discrete implementation would be of considerable interest.
