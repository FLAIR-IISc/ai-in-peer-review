

This paper investigates a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) equipped with a generalized averaging scheme. The authors establish sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weight parameters to achieve a targeted convergence rate, employing a Lyapunov argument for theoretical justification. Additionally, they propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decay of the Lyapunov function. The work provides both rigorous theoretical guarantees and comprehensive numerical comparisons. This research builds upon a substantial body of literature focused on empirically accelerating the convergence of accelerated optimization methods. The paper makes a notable contribution by demonstrating, through an ODE framework, that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can achieve similar acceleration. This novel approach represents a significant insight into the dynamics of accelerated methods.
