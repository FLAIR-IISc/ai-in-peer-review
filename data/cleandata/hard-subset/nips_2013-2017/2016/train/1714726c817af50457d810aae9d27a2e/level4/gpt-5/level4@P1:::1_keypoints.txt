- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to design an adaptive iterate-reweighting heuristic.
- Proposed adaptive averaging is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent and sometimes substantial speed-ups across a broad class of convex objectives.
- Mathematical development is simple, clear, and accessible for readers familiar with the ODE framework.
- Experiments are convincing and indicate promising practical improvements with a small modification to Nesterov’s method.
- High potential impact due to the ubiquity of Nesterov’s acceleration and the simplicity of the proposed change.
- Suggestion: provide a discrete-time analysis showing the convergence rate is preserved for the discretized algorithm.
- Suggestion: investigate connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- Argument for acceptance: clear, well-motivated extension of the ODE viewpoint on acceleration.
- Argument for acceptance: principled adaptive rule with a non-degradation guarantee relative to fixed schedules.
- Argument for acceptance: strong empirical evidence of speed-ups and practical value.
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical contribution closely follows prior work with limited novelty.
- Argument against acceptance: lacks a complete discrete-time convergence analysis to confirm rate preservation.
- Argument against acceptance: missing exploration of links to related accelerated methods (e.g., Conjugate Gradient).
- Overall: well-written, practically valuable contribution that would be strengthened by discrete-time analysis and deeper connections to established methods.
