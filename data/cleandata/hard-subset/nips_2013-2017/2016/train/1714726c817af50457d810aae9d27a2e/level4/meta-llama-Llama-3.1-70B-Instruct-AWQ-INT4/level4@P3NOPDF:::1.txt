This paper leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to design and evaluate a natural, heuristic approach to adaptively reweight the iterates of the algorithm, thereby accelerating convergence. Notably, this method is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging approach, unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that this method yields significant speed-ups on a broad class of functions.

I found this paper to be an enjoyable read, with a straightforward mathematical argument that is easy to follow, especially for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results are also promising, showcasing substantial speed-ups. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact due to the widespread application of Nesterov's acceleration. Often, in empirical evaluations, new adaptive methods are compared to Nesterov's acceleration with a fixed weight sequence, whereas this paper confirms that rendering Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a minor modification, leading to dramatic improvements.

To further strengthen the paper from a technical standpoint, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate that the rate of convergence is preserved. This analysis appears to be relatively straightforward, and its inclusion would enhance the paper's technical rigor. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization could provide valuable insights and further enrich the paper's contributions.

Overall, the paper presents a compelling approach to adaptively reweighting the iterates of Nesterov's acceleration, with a strong theoretical foundation and promising empirical results. With some additional technical analysis and exploration of connections to related methods, this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
