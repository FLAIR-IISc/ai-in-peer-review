- Extends Krichene et al.'s continuous-time framework to constrained convex optimization via broader averaging schemes of the dual mirror map.  
- Generalizes the primal trajectory construction from prior work by studying a wider class of averaging coefficients.  
- Core theoretical contribution is Theorem 2, giving convergence rates under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In continuous time, the paper claims convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2) and provides an adaptive weight selection strategy with theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical validation compares the adaptive weights against restarting techniques and shows practical viability.  
- The discrete implementation of the algorithm lacks theoretical analysis, a major omission given practical use.  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous and constitutes a meaningful theoretical advance on acceleration in constrained settings.  
- The adaptive weight choice is practically insightful, but its discretization and preservation of faster rates in practice are not addressed.  
- The authors should explicitly discuss the gap between continuous and discrete analyses and implications for practical methods.  
- The paper omits citation and discussion of related foundational work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and Redont (2016).  
- Typo/clarity issue: phrase "faster w,r,too 1/t^2" should be fixed and the precise rate improvement (o(1/t^2) vs O(1/t^{2+ε})) must be clarified.  
- Experiments support the adaptive scheme but are insufficient to fully validate theoretical claims without discrete convergence guarantees or discretization discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing citations (Attouch et al.), discrete-vs-continuous gap, rate-clarification, and brief discussion of discretizing adaptive weights.
