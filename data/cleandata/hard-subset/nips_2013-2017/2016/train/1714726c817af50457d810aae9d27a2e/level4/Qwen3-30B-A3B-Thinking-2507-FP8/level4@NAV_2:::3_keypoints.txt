- Generalizes Krichene et al.'s averaging scheme for accelerated mirror descent to constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Theorem 2: provides sufficient conditions for 1/t² convergence in continuous time via a unified Lyapunov function; includes an accelerated replicator dynamics example.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence guarantees and empirically outperforms gradient and speed restarting heuristics.  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous, well-structured, and builds on ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration (Su et al., Krichene et al.).  
- Numerical experiments are comprehensive (strong/weak convexity, linear, KL objectives) and show consistent performance improvements.  
- Strengths: Lyapunov-based theoretical framework, clear links to established literature, novel adaptive heuristic, strong empirical validation, excellent exposition.  
- Key weakness: lacks a formal discrete-time convergence analysis for the adaptive averaging method; Algorithm 1 and supplemental bound are insufficient.  
- Additional weakness: insufficient contextualization of the main theoretical result with Attouch, Peypouquet, and Redont's work on accelerated-method convergence rates.  
- Limited theoretical exploration on whether the continuous-time 1/t² rate can be preserved under discretization.  
- Pro-accept points: meaningful theoretical generalization, theory-grounded and effective heuristic, rigorous continuous-time results.  
- Con-accept points: practical impact limited by missing discrete-time proofs and need for deeper theoretical explanation of empirical gains.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with promising experiments; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing discrete-time analysis and literature connections.
