The paper "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics" presents a novel approach to adaptively reweigh the iterates of Nesterov's accelerated method to speed up convergence. The authors leverage the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration, previously introduced by Krichene et al. in NIPS 2015, to design a natural heuristic for adaptive averaging. This method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging, and the empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups on a general class of functions.

The paper is well-written, and the mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, its theoretical guarantees, and its empirical evaluation. The paper's organization is logical, and the sections are well-structured, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning.

The technical development in the paper is a close extension of Krichene et al.'s work, but the authors demonstrate a large potential impact of their proposed method. Nesterov's acceleration is a widely used technique in optimization, and the authors show that rendering it adaptive only requires a small modification, which can lead to dramatic improvements. The empirical results are promising, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method on various functions.

To improve the paper from a technical point of view, I would recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process showing that the rate of convergence is preserved. This should be relatively straightforward, given the authors' expertise in the field. Additionally, I would be interested in possible connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.

The paper's strengths include:

* A novel approach to adaptive averaging in Nesterov's accelerated method
* A clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical guarantees
* Promising empirical results on a general class of functions
* A large potential impact on the field of optimization

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al.'s work
* The analysis of the discretized process is not provided

Overall, I believe that this paper has a large potential impact on the field of optimization, and I would recommend its acceptance.
