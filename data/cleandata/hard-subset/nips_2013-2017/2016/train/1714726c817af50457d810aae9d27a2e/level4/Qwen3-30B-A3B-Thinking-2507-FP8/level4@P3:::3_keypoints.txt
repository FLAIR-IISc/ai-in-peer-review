- Studies accelerated first-order constrained convex optimization via continuous-time dynamics, extending prior accelerated mirror descent with generalized averaging.  
- Introduces two weight functions η(t) and w(t) to control dual trajectory accumulation and primal averaging.  
- Develops a unified Lyapunov analysis giving sufficient conditions for convergence rates, including the 1/t² quadratic rate.  
- Theorem 2 specifies precise conditions for Lyapunov decrease; Corollary 1 shows how to construct weight functions meeting those conditions.  
- Applies the framework to replicator dynamics on the simplex, showing acceleration in an evolutionary game theory example.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively sets weights to maintain Lyapunov decrease while preserving convergence rates.  
- Empirical results: adaptive averaging consistently outperforms or matches restarting heuristics, with notable gains on strongly convex problems; includes trajectory and energy visualizations.  
- High technical quality: rigorous, well-justified Lyapunov analysis and clear, reproducible exposition.  
- Originality: extends continuous-time analyses to a broader family of dynamics and introduces a novel practical adaptive averaging method.  
- Significance: provides a unified continuous-time perspective that can inform discrete algorithm design and has potential broad impact.  
- Main limitation: discrete-time counterpart lacks a formal convergence analysis (Algorithm 1 only briefly mentioned).  
- Second limitation: limited discussion and analysis of the computational cost of adaptive averaging versus restarting, especially in high dimensions.  
- Minor weakness: could compare more thoroughly to related continuous-time acceleration work (e.g., Attouch, Peypouquet).  
- Overall verdict: recommend acceptance — significant theoretical contributions and thorough experiments despite discrete-time analysis gap.
