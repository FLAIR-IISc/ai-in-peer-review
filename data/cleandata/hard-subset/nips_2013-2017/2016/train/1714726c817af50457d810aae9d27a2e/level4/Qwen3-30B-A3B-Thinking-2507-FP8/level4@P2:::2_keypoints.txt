- Paper extends the accelerated mirror descent — continuous-time ODE connection by introducing generalized averaging for the dual variable and an adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Builds on Su et al. and Krichene et al. (NIPS 2016) relating Nesterov acceleration to continuous-time dynamics.  
- Provides rigorous Lyapunov analysis for continuous-time dynamics under a broad family of generalized averaging schemes and gives sufficient conditions for convergence.  
- Primal representation clarifies behavior of accelerated methods and the framework can accelerate replicator dynamics.  
- Main novelty is the adaptive averaging heuristic, but generalization is limited because the authors focus on the r(t)=t² (quadratic) case already treated in prior work.  
- The adaptive scheme largely follows the continuous-time Lyapunov conditions and is not clearly distinguished from other existing adaptive methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is narrow: only simple quadratic and linear objectives on the simplex, low-dimensional and not representative of ML tasks.  
- Claims of outperforming adaptive restarting are not fully supported; experiments lack comparisons to other adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) and non-quadratic/high-dimensional problems.  
- Theoretical justification for the discrete-time adaptive heuristic is incomplete: no formal discrete-time convergence guarantee and discretization effects are not addressed.  
- Overall: strong theoretical quality and clear exposition, but limited originality, significance, and empirical/theoretical support for the discrete-time algorithm; recommend rejection pending substantial revisions.
