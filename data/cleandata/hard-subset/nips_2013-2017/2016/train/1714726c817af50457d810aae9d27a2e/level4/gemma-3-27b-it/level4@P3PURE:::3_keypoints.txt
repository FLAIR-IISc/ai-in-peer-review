- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, extending Krichene et al. by viewing Nesterov as ODE discretization coupling primal and dual trajectories.  
- Introduces and analyzes more general averaging schemes for obtaining the primal trajectory from the dual mirror map.  
- Theorem 2 proves convergence rates under conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation.  
- Compares the adaptive scheme with restarting techniques known to empirically help acceleration.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version is missing.  
- Recommends acknowledging related work by Attouch, Peypouquet and collaborators.  
- Important open question: whether the improved continuous-rate survives discretization.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-written, and presents significant new continuous-time results.
