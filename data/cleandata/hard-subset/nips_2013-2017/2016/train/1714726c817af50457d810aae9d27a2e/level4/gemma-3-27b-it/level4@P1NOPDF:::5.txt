The paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. Leveraging a Lyapunov function, the authors demonstrate a desired convergence rate. They introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to further accelerate the convergence achieved through the Lyapunov approach. The experimental results indicate that this adaptive averaging performs comparably to, and in some cases better than, adaptive restarting techniques.

While my expertise in this specific area is limited, the core idea of employing adaptive averaging to achieve a faster convergence rate for constrained convex optimization appears both interesting and promising. The theoretical justification, based on the Lyapunov function analysis, seems sound given my understanding. 

The paper’s strengths lie in its clear presentation of the problem and the proposed solution. The connection between the Lyapunov function and the adaptive averaging heuristic is reasonably well-explained, enabling a reader – even one not deeply familiar with the field – to grasp the fundamental principles. The empirical validation adds further support to the claims.

However, a more detailed discussion of the practical considerations for implementing the adaptive averaging heuristic would be beneficial. Specifically, elaborating on the sensitivity of performance to the choice of parameters used in the averaging process could strengthen the paper. Additionally, while the comparison to adaptive restarting is valuable, including a comparison to other relevant state-of-the-art methods in constrained convex optimization would provide a broader context for evaluating the contribution.

Despite these minor points, the work presents a solid contribution to the field. The proposed technique offers a potential improvement over existing methods, and the theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for these claims. The potential impact of this work stems from the possibility of accelerating convergence in practical applications of constrained convex optimization.



**Quality:** The paper is well-written and the technical arguments are presented clearly.
**Novelty:** The adaptive averaging heuristic appears to be a novel contribution.
**Impact:** The potential impact is good, as faster convergence rates are always desirable in optimization.
**Clarity:** The paper is generally clear and easy to follow, even for readers with limited expertise in the area.
