- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- The proposed scheme adaptively reweights iterates to speed up convergence.  
- Unlike prior heuristics, the method is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging (never worse, often better).  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent speed-ups across a broad class of objective functions.  
- Paper is clear, enjoyable, and mathematically straightforward, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Empirical results are compelling and indicate substantial practical gains.  
- Technical novelty is a relatively close extension of prior work, but potential impact is high due to wide use of Nesterov’s acceleration.  
- Suggest adding analysis of the discretized algorithm to show the convergence rate is preserved under adaptive reweighting.  
- Recommend discussing connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics to explore parallels in momentum/weight adaptation.
