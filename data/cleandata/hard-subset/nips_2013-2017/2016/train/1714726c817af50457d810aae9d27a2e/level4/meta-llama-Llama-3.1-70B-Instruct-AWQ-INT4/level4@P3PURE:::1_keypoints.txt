- Uses Krichene et al.'s ODE view of Nesterov acceleration to design a natural adaptive reweighting of iterates that accelerates convergence.
- The proposed adaptive averaging is provably better than standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior heuristics.
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Paper is clearly written and mathematically accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Technical contribution is a close extension of prior work but with potentially wide impact due to Nesterov's broad use.
- Demonstrates that only a minor modification to Nesterov's scheme yields substantial empirical improvements.
- Suggests adding an analysis of the discretized algorithm to confirm preservation of the convergence rate.
- Recommends exploring links between adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics.
