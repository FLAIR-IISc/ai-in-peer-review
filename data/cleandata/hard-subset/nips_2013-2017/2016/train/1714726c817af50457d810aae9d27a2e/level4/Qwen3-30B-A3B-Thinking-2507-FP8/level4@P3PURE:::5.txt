

The paper investigates accelerated gradient dynamics for constrained convex optimization, leveraging Lyapunov function analysis to establish convergence guarantees. The authors introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the convergence rate achieved through Lyapunov-based analysis. Experimental results demonstrate that this adaptive averaging technique achieves performance comparable to, or superior to, adaptive restarting. While my expertise in this specific area is limited, the core idea of employing adaptive averaging to attain faster convergence for constrained convex optimization appears promising and merits attention.  

Quality: Well-executed within the scope of the claims.  
Novelty: Modest novelty in the context of Lyapunov-based acceleration methods.  
Impact: Potential for practical applications in constrained optimization problems.  
Clarity: Clear exposition of the adaptive averaging mechanism and its theoretical foundation.
