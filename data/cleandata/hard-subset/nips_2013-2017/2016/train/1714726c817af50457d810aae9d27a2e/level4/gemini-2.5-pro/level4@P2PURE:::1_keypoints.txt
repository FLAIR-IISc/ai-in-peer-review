- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- Proposed method is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups on a broad class of functions.  
- Paper is well-written; the mathematical argument is elegant and accessible to readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Technical development is a direct extension of prior work but could have significant practical impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Corrects a common evaluation flaw: shows a minor modification makes Nesterov adaptive and dramatically improves performance versus suboptimal fixed weights.  
- Recommendation: include analysis of the discretized process to show the theoretical convergence rate is preserved.  
- Suggestion: discuss possible connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
