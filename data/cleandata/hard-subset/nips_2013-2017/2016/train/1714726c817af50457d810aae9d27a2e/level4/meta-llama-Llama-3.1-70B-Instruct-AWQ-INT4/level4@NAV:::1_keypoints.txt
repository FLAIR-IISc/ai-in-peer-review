- Uses Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to derive an intuitive heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging method that provably outperforms the standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Presentation is engaging and the mathematical argument is straightforward, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Although technically an extension of prior work, the idea has high impact due to wide use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Highlights that small adaptive modifications to Nesterov yield substantial practical improvements over fixed-weight comparisons.  
- Suggests a technical improvement: analyze the discretized algorithm to verify the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Recommends investigating connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
