- Proposes a unified framework for accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with generalized averaging via Lyapunov-based continuous-time analysis.  
- Introduces sufficient conditions on weight functions η(t) and w(t) to achieve targeted convergence rates.  
- Main contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically computes weights to accelerate convergence while preserving theoretical guarantees.  
- Shows replicator dynamics (mirror descent on the simplex) can be accelerated using simple averaging.  
- Proves the adaptive averaging heuristic preserves quadratic convergence rate in discrete time.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate adaptive averaging outperforms gradient and speed restarting heuristics, especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Lyapunov analysis (Theorems 2–3) is rigorous and technically sound.  
- Adaptive averaging is well-motivated by minimizing the Lyapunov function derivative.  
- Experiments are limited to R³, raising concerns about scalability to high-dimensional ML problems.  
- Connection between continuous-time formulation and discrete Algorithm 1 is unclear; relationship between w(t) and discrete ak needs clarification.  
- Some technical sections are dense and lack intuitive explanations (e.g., equations 33–47, key equation (3), condition (8)).  
- Novelty: strong extension of prior continuous-time work and a novel link to replicator dynamics and evolutionary game theory.  
- Comparison to adaptive optimizers (Adagrad, Adam) is cursory; should clarify differences and complementarity.  
- Practical issues not fully addressed: scalability, computational overhead of adaptive averaging, and implementation challenges.  
- Limited comparison with adaptive methods in non-convex optimization.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability experiments, clarity of key equations, and relation to non-convex/adaptive methods.
