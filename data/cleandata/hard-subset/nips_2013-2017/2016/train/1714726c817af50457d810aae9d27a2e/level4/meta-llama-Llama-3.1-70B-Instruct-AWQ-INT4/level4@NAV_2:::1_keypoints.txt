- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated mirror descent using the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Develops a unified study of a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Provides sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging rule that adaptively computes weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.  
- Proves continuous-time guarantees and shows adaptive averaging preserves quadratic convergence in discrete-time.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging performs at least as well as adaptive restarting and yields significant speed-ups in some cases.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, simple mathematical arguments.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis and strong connections to prior work (e.g., Krichene et al.).  
- Strength: novelty of the heuristic and promising empirical results.  
- Strength: thorough analysis and clear discussion of related literature.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of the discretized process.  
- Weakness: lacks connections to other optimization methods (e.g., Conjugate Gradient).  
- Weakness: needs more insights into the heuristic’s behavior and limitations.  
- Overall: technically sound with potentially large impact given the widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- For acceptance: novel method, solid analysis, and promising experiments.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient discretization analysis, limited insight on limitations, and missing links to other methods.
