- Studies continuous-time ODEs for accelerated mirror descent with a generalized averaging scheme and provides Lyapunov-based convergence analysis.
- Derives sufficient conditions on dual accumulation rate and averaging weights to guarantee desired convergence rates.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that accelerates Lyapunov decrease with continuous-time guarantees and quadratic discrete-time convergence.
- Demonstrates empirical performance comparable or improved over adaptive restarting in numerical experiments.
- Builds on and extends known connections between Nesterov acceleration and continuous-time ODEs by exploring different averaging schemes.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and a solid Lyapunov argument underpinning the convergence guarantees.
- Shows novel connections to replicator dynamics, evolutionary game theory, and viability theory, adding interdisciplinary significance.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and situated within relevant literature on acceleration and averaging.
- Practical verification concern: conditions for Lyapunov decay (Theorem 2) may be hard to check in practice.
- Empirical concern: numerical experiments are limited in scope and lack larger-scale or more diverse problem evaluations.
- Comparison concern: discussion and empirical comparison with other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) is insufficient.
- Suggestion: include more discussion on practical implications, relaxation/approximation strategies for the decay conditions.
- Suggestion: further explore connections to cubic-regularized Newton methods and their relevance.
- Overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance with minor revisions to address practical verification, expanded experiments, and deeper comparisons.
