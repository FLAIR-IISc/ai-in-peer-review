- Presents a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated first-order methods derived from the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Generalizes accelerated mirror descent dynamics with two time-varying functions η(t) (gradient accumulation rate) and w(t) (averaging weights).  
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) for achieving target convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging rule that greedily adjusts w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function at each step.  
- Shows the adaptive heuristic preserves the O(1/t^2) convergence rate in continuous time, unlike many ad-hoc restart schemes.  
- Provides a discrete-time version of the algorithm and cites a convergence proof in the supplementary material.  
- Empirical results on constrained convex problems show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms popular adaptive restarting heuristics.  
- Experiments are well-designed with informative visualizations of trajectories and Lyapunov/energy behavior.  
- Paper is technically sound, elegant, exceptionally well-written, and logically organized.  
- Novelty is incremental relative to Krichene et al. (2015) but original in deriving a principled adaptive heuristic from the ODE framework.  
- High potential impact: a simple, implementable modification to Nesterov's method that improves robustness and should become a stronger baseline.  
- Recommendation to include a formal discrete-time theorem and brief proof sketch in the main paper to strengthen the practical claim.  
- Suggestion to discuss connections to classical methods (e.g., Conjugate Gradient) for additional insight, especially on strongly convex quadratics.  
- Overall assessment: strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
