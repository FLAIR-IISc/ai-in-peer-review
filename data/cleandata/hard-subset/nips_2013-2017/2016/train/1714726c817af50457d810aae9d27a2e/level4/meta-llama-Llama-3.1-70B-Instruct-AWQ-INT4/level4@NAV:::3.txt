This paper explores accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings, building upon the work of Krichene et al. in [7]. The authors extend the study of Nesterov's method as a suitable discretization of an ODE system, which combines a primal trajectory with a dual one through a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. The current paper investigates more general averaging schemes, with Theorem 2 establishing convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. Notably, the continuous setting yields convergence rates faster than 1/t^2. An adaptive weight selection with convergence guarantees is also presented and experimentally evaluated, with comparisons to restarting techniques known to enhance the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. Although the theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not included, the paper is well-written and presents an interesting analysis of the new averaging scheme in the continuous setting. While the core ideas of the continuous dynamics analysis are well-established in the literature, this work offers novel contributions and results. It would be beneficial to acknowledge the work of Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors in this context. The main theorem, demonstrating averaging schemes with faster convergence rates than 1/t^2, is significant; however, it would be intriguing to investigate whether this improved rate is preserved when transitioning to the discrete procedure.
