- Paper studies ODE-form optimization dynamics with generalized averaging, proving Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and weights for prescribed convergence rates, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic with empirical speedups.
- Strength: Clear ODE viewpoint and Lyapunov analysis that yield interpretable rate guarantees under generalized averaging.
- Strength: Provides checkable sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and averaging weights useful for algorithm design.
- Strength: Adaptive averaging heuristic is simple, computationally cheap, and empirically improves Lyapunov decay.
- Strength: Numerical results align with theory and show competitive performance versus baselines.
- Weakness: Problem class and assumptions (smoothness, strong convexity, constraints, etc.) are not clearly stated up front.
- Weakness: Novelty relative to prior ODE-based acceleration and averaging work is unclear and needs tighter positioning.
- Weakness: Main guarantees are at the ODE level; unclear whether they translate to discrete-time algorithms with matching rates or require step-size restrictions.
- Weakness: Adaptive averaging lacks theoretical guarantees (non-asymptotic rates or monotonic Lyapunov decrease) or conditions ensuring it does not harm base rates.
- Weakness: Sensitivity and selection of the dual learning rate and weight schedules are not addressed; unclear if they require problem constants.
- Weakness: Robustness to stochastic or inexact gradients and numerical integration error is not analyzed.
- Weakness: Experimental section lacks clarity on baselines, tuning protocols, ablations, and sensitivity analyses.
- Question: Can you formalize conditions under which the adaptive averaging heuristic preserves the base convergence rate or ensures non-increasing Lyapunov discretely?
- Question: Do your sufficient conditions recover standard O(1/k^2) or linear rates under L-smooth convex or μ-strongly convex assumptions?
- Question: Is the generalized averaging ODE the continuous-time limit of a known discrete dual averaging method, and what is the precise relationship?
- Question: Any results or empirical evidence for robustness under stochastic gradients, or obstacles to extending the Lyapunov argument to noisy settings?
- Question: How do the rate constants depend on the weight schedule and dual learning rate, and are there practical choices that optimize them?
- Suggestion: State problem setting and assumptions explicitly and present theorems mapping assumptions to rates with a Lyapunov proof sketch.
- Suggestion: Add a discretization section proposing a concrete discrete algorithm, implementable step sizes/weights, and a discrete-time convergence guarantee.
- Suggestion: Provide intuition for the adaptive rule, give simple sufficient conditions for safe adaptation, and include ablations showing its isolated effect.
- Suggestion: Expand related work to better differentiate from prior ODE analyses, dual averaging, momentum methods, and adaptive restart schemes.
- Suggestion: Strengthen experiments with broader baselines, clear tuning protocols, ablations, and sensitivity/failure cases for practitioner guidance.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and promising work; needs clearer novelty positioning, discrete-time translation, and theory for the adaptive variant to be a solid contribution.
