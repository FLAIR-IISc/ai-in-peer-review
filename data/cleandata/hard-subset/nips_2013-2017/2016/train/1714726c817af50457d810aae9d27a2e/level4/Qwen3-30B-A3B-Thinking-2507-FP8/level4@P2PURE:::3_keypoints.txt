- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al. by viewing Nesterov as a discretization of coupled primal–dual ODEs with primal from averaged mirror of dual.  
- Extends analysis to more general averaging schemes beyond the specific one in prior work.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, derives rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection strategy with convergence guarantees.  
- Empirically compares the adaptive strategy to restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods.  
- Discrete counterpart analysis is omitted (open question whether improved continuous rates transfer to discrete algorithms).  
- Paper is interesting and well-written; analysis in continuous setting is rigorous.  
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
