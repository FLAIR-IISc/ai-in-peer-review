- Studies ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme and provides sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and weights for a desired convergence rate via Lyapunov arguments.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to speed the decrease of the Lyapunov function along solution trajectories.
- Presents theoretical guarantees for the method and numerical comparisons validating performance.
- Key novel insight: adaptively averaging weights along trajectories can empirically and theoretically speed up accelerated methods.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, thorough exposition and a neat use of Lyapunov functions for analysis.
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and clearly explained.
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and offer meaningful comparisons to existing methods.
- Main weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis and discussion of how the method scales with problem size.
- Secondary weakness: could better situate the method in relation to prior work and clarify connections to existing approaches.
- Overall assessment: strong and valuable contribution with scores Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to include complexity analysis and a more detailed comparison to related methods.
