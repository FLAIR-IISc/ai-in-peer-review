- Builds on prior AMD–ODE connections and proposes a generalized averaging scheme plus a novel adaptive averaging discretization.
- Adaptive averaging is novel and well-motivated but overall originality and practical utility are questioned.
- Key technical tools (Lyapunov function, discretization) are largely inherited from prior work [7].
- Extension to generalized averaging is minor and its utility is limited because analysis only achieves the quadratic rate.
- Some theoretical insights (energy interpretation, primal reformulation, link to replicator dynamics) deepen understanding but are not a major field advance.
- Adaptive averaging lacks full theoretical grounding after discretization approximations, so no explicit convergence result is provided and the method is essentially heuristic.
- Many alternative averaging schemes exist and the paper provides no comparative analysis to show superiority.
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits for general strongly convex problems.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments, provide a formal convergence proof, implement adaptive averaging for Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and include comparisons to other adaptive averaging strategies.
