The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous-time and discrete settings. It extends the line of work of Krichene et al. [7], where Nesterov’s method is interpreted as a discretization of a coupled primal–dual ODE. In that framework, the primal trajectory is obtained by averaging a mirror transform of the dual variable. This paper generalizes that approach by analyzing a broader class of averaging schemes.

Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous-time setting, the authors derive rates faster than 1/t^2. They also propose an adaptive rule for choosing the weights, provide convergence guarantees, and employ this rule in experiments. The experimental section compares the adaptive strategy against restarting techniques, which are known empirically to enhance accelerated methods. However, a theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not provided.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-written paper. The continuous-time analysis of the new averaging schemes is rigorous. Although the main ideas underlying continuous-time analyses of accelerated methods are now well established, the paper offers new contributions and insights. In this regard, related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be acknowledged. The main theorem—showing that certain averaging schemes achieve rates faster than 1/t^2—is significant. It would be valuable to understand whether these improved rates persist after discretization and, if so, under what stability and step-size conditions.
