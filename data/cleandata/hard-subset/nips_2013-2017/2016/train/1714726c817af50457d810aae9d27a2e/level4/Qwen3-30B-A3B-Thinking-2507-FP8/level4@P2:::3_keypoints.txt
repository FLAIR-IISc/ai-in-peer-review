- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via continuous-time ODEs, extending prior work by Krichene et al. and Su et al.
- Generalizes the primal averaging scheme: different weight functions yield desired convergence rates.
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on weight functions guaranteeing continuous-time convergence.
- Interprets accelerated mirror descent as coupling primal and dual variables with the primal as a weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively chooses weights to speed Lyapunov decrease.
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the adaptive heuristic preserves quadratic convergence rate (claimed) in discrete time.
- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics (evolutionary game theory) using the proposed averaging scheme.
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive heuristic outperforms restarting techniques, especially for strongly convex problems.
- Theoretical analysis in continuous time is rigorous and proofs are sound; connection to discrete algorithms is clearly discussed.
- Presentation and clarity are excellent: precise writing, good organization, clear examples, and helpful figures.
- Originality is strong: broader family of averaging schemes and a novel adaptive averaging heuristic beyond prior work.
- Suggests additional citations/contrast with Attouch and Peypouquet’s continuous-time acceleration literature.
- Main weakness: lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of the discrete adaptive algorithm, undermining practical claims.
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contributions and practical relevance despite the discrete-time analysis omission.
