- Studies accelerated mirror descent via continuous-time ODEs, generalizing prior work connecting Nesterov acceleration to ODEs.  
- Parameterizes accelerated dynamics by gradient accumulation rate η(t) and averaging weights w(t).  
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) for desired convergence rates.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the continuous-time Lyapunov function.  
- Presents a discretization of the heuristic (Algorithm 1) and empirical comparisons against restarting heuristics.  
- Strength: the adaptive averaging heuristic is novel, intuitive, and well-motivated by the Lyapunov analysis.  
- Strength: provides theoretical insights, including a primal-space representation and connection to replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well organized, with accessible theoretical development.  
- Weakness: core analytical framework and Lyapunov form are direct extensions of prior work [11], making the contribution feel incremental.  
- Weakness: practical utility is not fully demonstrated; experiments and analysis focus on standard O(1/t^2) rates and do not explore the framework’s potential for faster rates.  
- Weakness: no formal convergence proof for the discrete-time algorithm is provided, leaving the method a heuristic without rigorous discrete-time guarantees.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to low-dimensional toy problems and simple objectives, so benefits for large-scale or strongly convex problems are inconclusive.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited to restarting heuristics and omit other adaptive averaging/acceleration methods from the literature.
