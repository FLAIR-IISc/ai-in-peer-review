- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging scheme for Nesterov's accelerated method based on the ODE view (Krichene et al.).
- Introduces an intuitive heuristic to adaptively reweight iterates to accelerate convergence.
- Theoretically guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, simple, and mathematically elegant.
- High potential impact: a small modification makes Nesterov's method adaptive, likely influencing implementation and benchmarking practices.
- Suggestion: provide a rigorous analysis of the discretized algorithm to show the adaptive scheme preserves optimal convergence rates.
- Suggestion: discuss potential connections to Conjugate Gradient methods, especially for strongly convex quadratics and Lanczos-derived coefficients.
