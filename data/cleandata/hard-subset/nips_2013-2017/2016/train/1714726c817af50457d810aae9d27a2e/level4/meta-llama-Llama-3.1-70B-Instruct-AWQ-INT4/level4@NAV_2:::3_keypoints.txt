- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, extending prior ODE-based analyses with more general averaging schemes.
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 2): derives convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 under suitable averaging-coefficient conditions.
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous and well-presented, building on established ODE techniques.
- Authors propose an adaptive choice of averaging weights with proven convergence guarantees and evaluate it experimentally.
- Experiments compare the adaptive weighting scheme to restarting techniques known to help accelerated methods in practice.
- Missing citation: should reference related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors on similar continuous dynamics.
- Key weakness: no theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version is provided.
- Open question: unclear whether the improved continuous-time rate is preserved upon discretization.
- Pro-acceptance: provides new contributions and rigorous continuous-time analysis of averaging schemes.
- Pro-acceptance: adaptive weight selection with guarantees is a practically relevant contribution.
- Con-acceptance: lack of discrete-theory limits the paper’s practical impact and theoretical completeness.
- Paper quality and clarity are high: well-written, clear introduction, and rigorous analysis.
