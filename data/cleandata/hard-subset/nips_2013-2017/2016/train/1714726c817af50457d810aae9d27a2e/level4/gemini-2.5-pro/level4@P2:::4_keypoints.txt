- Studies a general class of continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics with dual gradient accumulation η(t) and primal averaging w(t).
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis giving simple sufficient conditions on w(t) and η(t) for target rates (e.g., O(1/t^2)).
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily chooses w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function and speed up convergence.
- Shows the adaptive heuristic provably preserves the theoretical convergence rate of the non-adaptive method, unlike many restarting schemes.
- Derives a discrete-time algorithm, proves it maintains the O(1/k^2) rate, and implements it for experiments.
- Empirical results show competitive or improved performance versus standard gradient and speed restarting schemes.
- Well-situated in literature linking Nesterov acceleration to ODEs and extends prior averaging perspectives.
- Paper is technically sound, with clear proofs and an elegant unified framework explaining averaging and learning-rate effects.
- Writing and organization are strong; the replicator dynamics acceleration example is insightful.
- Practical relevance: discrete algorithm is implementable and experiments indicate real-world promise.
- Weakness: main experiments are low-dimensional (R^3); higher-dimensional/ML-relevant results should be highlighted in the main text.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison or deeper discussion versus popular adaptive optimizers (Adagrad, Adam).
- Weakness: analysis only guarantees preservation of O(1/k^2); potential faster (e.g., linear) rates in strongly convex settings are observed empirically but not theoretically explained.
- Overall assessment: novel, well-motivated, technically strong work with minor empirical/theoretical extensions suggested; recommend acceptance.
