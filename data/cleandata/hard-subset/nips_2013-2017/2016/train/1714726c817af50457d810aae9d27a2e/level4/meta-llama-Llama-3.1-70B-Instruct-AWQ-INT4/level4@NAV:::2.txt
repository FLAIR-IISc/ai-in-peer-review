The authors build upon existing work connecting accelerated Mirror Descent and continuous-time ODE, expanding the ODE dynamics with a more versatile averaging scheme and introducing a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one of its discretizations. Notably, the new variant of AMD with adaptive averaging demonstrates improved performance over existing AMD algorithms with restarting in certain cases. The proposed adaptive averaging strategy is innovative and intuitively appealing. However, upon closer examination, concerns regarding the paper's originality and usefulness arise.

Most components, including the Lyapunov function and discretization, closely follow an earlier NIPS paper [7]. The extension from simple averaging to generalized averaging appears somewhat straightforward and lacks significant impact, as the authors primarily focus on the quadratic rate; exploring faster rates would necessitate higher-order methods, which may not always be applicable.

Some results, such as the interpretation of the energy function, reformulation into primal form, and reduction to replicator dynamics, offer valuable insights into the accelerated Mirror Descent algorithm. Nevertheless, these findings do not substantially contribute to advancing machine learning. Furthermore, while the adaptive averaging strategy is introduced to satisfy convergence conditions for the ODE, its theoretical foundation is incomplete due to discretization approximations. Unfortunately, no explicit convergence result is provided, rendering it a heuristic approach, as acknowledged by the authors. Additionally, the existence of alternative averaging schemes for Nesterov's accelerated algorithms raises questions about the distinctiveness of this adaptive variant.

The experimental results on a toy example with quadratic/linear objectives are not compelling enough to demonstrate the advantages of adaptive averaging, particularly in the general case with strong convexity.

== post-rebuttal answer==

After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I have taken into account their commitments to:

- include additional higher-dimensional and non-quadratic examples in the revised manuscript
- provide a theoretical proof of convergence for adaptive averaging
- implement adaptive averaging on Nesterov's cubic-regularized Newton method
- discuss comparisons with other adaptive averaging strategies

In light of these updates, I have revised my evaluation accordingly.
