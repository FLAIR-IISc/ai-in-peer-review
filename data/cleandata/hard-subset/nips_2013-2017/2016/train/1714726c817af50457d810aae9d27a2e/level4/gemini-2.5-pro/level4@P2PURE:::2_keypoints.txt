- Paper extends AMD ODEs with a more general averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization that can outperform existing AMD with restarting in some cases.
- The adaptive averaging strategy is novel and its intuition is a key strength of the paper.
- Much technical machinery (Lyapunov function, discretization) is directly adopted from prior NIPS work [7], raising originality concerns.
- The main theoretical contribution (extension to generalized averaging) is incremental and practically limited because the paper only treats the quadratic rate; faster rates would require higher-order methods with limited applicability.
- Results like the energy-function interpretation, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics provide useful theoretical insight but limited practical impact for the broader ML community.
- The adaptive averaging is motivated by continuous-time convergence conditions, but discretization approximations weaken this grounding and the paper lacks a convergence proof for the discrete algorithm (treated as a heuristic).
- The paper omits comparisons to existing alternative averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, making it hard to assess practical advantage.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to toy quadratic and linear objectives and does not convincingly demonstrate benefits, especially for strongly convex problems.
- Post-rebuttal, authors commit to add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for the adaptive discrete algorithm, an implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons with other adaptive averaging schemes.
- Reviewer raised their score contingent on those promised additions being executed well.
