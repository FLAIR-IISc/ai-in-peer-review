## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: [Paper Title – *Please Insert Here*]

This paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization problems. The authors demonstrate convergence rate improvements by leveraging a Lyapunov function analysis and introducing a novel adaptive averaging heuristic designed to enhance the Lyapunov function’s performance. Experimental results suggest this adaptive averaging strategy achieves comparable, and in some cases superior, performance to adaptive restarting techniques.

While my expertise in this specific area of optimization is limited, the core idea of employing adaptive averaging to accelerate convergence in constrained convex optimization appears both interesting and potentially impactful. The motivation for improving upon existing adaptive methods is clear, and the use of a Lyapunov function provides a principled approach to analyzing and guaranteeing convergence properties.

**Strengths:**

The paper’s primary strength lies in its theoretically grounded approach to accelerating convergence. The use of a Lyapunov function is a strong technique for proving stability and convergence, and the authors appear to have applied it effectively. The adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and seems intuitively plausible as a means to refine the Lyapunov function’s estimates and subsequently improve convergence speed. The empirical comparison to adaptive restarting is valuable, providing initial evidence of the proposed method’s competitiveness. The authors clearly articulate the problem they are addressing and the contribution they are making. 

**Weaknesses & Questions:**

Despite the promising nature of the work, several areas could benefit from further elaboration. 

* **Technical Detail:** While the high-level idea is clear, a deeper dive into the technical details of the adaptive averaging heuristic would be beneficial. Specifically, how is the averaging performed? What parameters control the averaging process, and how are they determined adaptively? A more detailed explanation of the Lyapunov function construction and its relation to the specific constrained optimization problem being considered would also strengthen the presentation.
* **Scope of Applicability:** The paper focuses on constrained convex optimization. A discussion of the limitations of this approach – i.e., what types of constraints or objective functions might be problematic – would be valuable. It would also be helpful to briefly discuss how the proposed method might generalize to other optimization settings (e.g., non-convex problems, different constraint types).
* **Experimental Evaluation:** The empirical results, while encouraging, could be expanded.  The specific optimization problems used in the evaluation should be clearly described, including their size and characteristics. A more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art optimization algorithms, beyond adaptive restarting, would further demonstrate the method’s strengths and weaknesses. Reporting variance or confidence intervals alongside the average performance metrics would enhance the statistical significance of the results.  Were any parameter tuning strategies employed, and if so, how were they validated to prevent overfitting to the test problems?
* **Clarification of ‘Desired Convergence Rate’:** The phrase "desired convergence rate" appears several times. It would be helpful to explicitly state what convergence rate the authors are aiming for and how it relates to known bounds for other algorithms in the same setting.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a potentially significant contribution to the field of accelerated optimization. The use of a Lyapunov function in conjunction with an adaptive averaging heuristic is a novel and promising approach. While the technical details and experimental validation could be strengthened, the core idea is sound and the preliminary results are encouraging. 

**Quality:**  Solid. The paper demonstrates a good understanding of relevant optimization techniques. Further detail is needed in the technical sections.
**Novelty:**  Good. The adaptive averaging heuristic appears to be a new contribution.
**Impact:**  Potentially High. If the theoretical and empirical results hold up under further scrutiny, this work could have a meaningful impact on the development of more efficient optimization algorithms.
**Clarity:**  Good. The paper is generally well-written, but could benefit from more detailed explanations of the technical components.




**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more detailed technical description of the adaptive averaging heuristic, expanding the experimental evaluation, and clarifying the achieved convergence rate. Addressing these points will significantly strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
