This manuscript examines a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) that incorporate a generalized averaging scheme. The authors establish sufficient conditions for the dual learning rate and weights to attain a desired convergence rate, leveraging a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, an adaptive averaging heuristic is employed to expedite the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The work provides both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons. This study contributes to a substantial body of research focused on empirically accelerating the convergence of accelerated methods. Notably, the authors make a significant observation, using ODEs to demonstrate that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can also yield speedup. The proposed method is found to be particularly intriguing.
