

The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. Building on the work of Krichene et al. [7], which established Nesterov's method as a suitable discretization of an ODE system coupling primal and dual trajectories (where the primal trajectory arises via a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable), the authors extend the analysis to more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, rates faster than \(1/t^2\) are derived. An adaptive weight selection strategy with convergence guarantees is developed and applied in the experiments, where it is compared against restarting techniques—empirically known to enhance convergence of accelerated methods. The discrete counterpart analysis is omitted. This is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the novel averaging scheme in the continuous setting is rigorous. While the core ideas of continuous dynamics analysis are now well-established in recent literature, the paper presents new contributions. The work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be referenced. The main theorem, demonstrating averaging schemes achieving rates faster than \(1/t^2\), is significant; however, it would be valuable to determine whether this improved rate persists in the discrete setting.
