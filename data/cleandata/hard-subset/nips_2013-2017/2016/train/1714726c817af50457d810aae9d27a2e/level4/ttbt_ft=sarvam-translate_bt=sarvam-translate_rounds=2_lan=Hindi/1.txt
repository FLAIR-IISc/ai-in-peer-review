The NIPS 2015 paper by Krikken et al. designs and evaluates a natural heuristic method for optimally weighting algorithm iterations to accelerate convergence, leveraging Krikken et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration. Unlike previous adaptive average heuristics, this method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule average. Empirical evaluation shows that the method yields speed-up over general class functions. I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical reasoning is simple and easy to follow, especially given the knowledge of Krikken et al.'s work. Furthermore, the empirical results show very promising speed-ups. While the technical development is a close extension of Krikken et al., I believe this paper has a large potential impact, as Nesterov's acceleration is so widespread in application. Very often, even within empirical evaluation, a new, possibly highly adaptive method is compared to Nesterov's acceleration with a fixed weight sequence. However, as this paper confirms, only a small modification is required to adapt the Nesterov method, and dramatic improvements can occur. To improve the paper from a technical perspective, I would recommend providing an analysis that shows that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I'm not mistaken, it should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in the potential relationship between the adaptive average of the Nesterov method and the conjugate gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
