This paper studies a class of continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics with generalized averaging. Using a Lyapunov analysis, it provides sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and the averaging weights to achieve a desired convergence rate. The authors also introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic that accelerates the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are provided. There is a long line of work aimed at empirically speeding up accelerated methods; this paper makes the key observation, via an ODE perspective, that adaptively averaging the weights along the solution trajectory can also yield speedups. I found this method very interesting.
