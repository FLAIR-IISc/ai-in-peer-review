- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated first-order methods based on a generalized primal averaging of the mirrored dual trajectory.
- Builds on the continuous-time ODE framework from Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) and uses a Lyapunov-based adaptation rule to choose averaging weights greedily.
- Provides sufficient conditions that preserve the O(1/t^2) convergence rate in continuous time.
- Proposes a discrete-time algorithm and evaluates it empirically against standard acceleration and adaptive restarting heuristics.
- Strength: Addresses an important problem with a simple, principled method that could have wide practical impact.
- Strength: Theoretical development is sound and intuitive, using the Lyapunov derivative to guide adaptation while preserving acceleration.
- Strength: Empirical results are convincing and the manuscript is well-written and clearly motivated.
- Weakness: Technical framework is a direct, incremental extension of prior ODE-based work, limiting perceived novelty.
- Weakness: The discrete-time convergence proof is relegated to the supplement; a proof sketch or more discussion in the main paper is needed.
- Weakness: Lacks discussion connecting the method to classical optimizers (e.g., relation to Conjugate Gradient for quadratics).
- Overall: Strong significance, quality, and empirical support likely outweigh limited technical novelty.
