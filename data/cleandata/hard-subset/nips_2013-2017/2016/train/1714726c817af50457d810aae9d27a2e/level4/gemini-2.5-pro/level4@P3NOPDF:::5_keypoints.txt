- Establishes accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization and proves O(1/k^2) convergence via a Lyapunov function.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic as a practical discretization of the continuous-time analysis.  
- Empirical results show adaptive averaging performs comparably to or better than adaptive restarting.  
- Theoretical grounding is rigorous and credible through standard Lyapunov-based analysis.  
- Adaptive averaging is a compelling, less aggressive alternative to restarting that can retain past information for stability.  
- Experiments should cover a wider variety of constrained problems and report sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- The paper needs clearer intuition on when averaging outperforms restarting and which problem geometries favor it.  
- A discussion of potential limitations and the computational overhead relative to adaptive restarting is missing.
