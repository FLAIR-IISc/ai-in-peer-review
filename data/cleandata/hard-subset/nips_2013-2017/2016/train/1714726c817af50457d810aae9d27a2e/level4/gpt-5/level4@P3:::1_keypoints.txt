- Studies accelerated mirror descent ODEs parameterized by η(t) (dual aggregation rate) and w(t) (primal averaging weights) and analyzes convergence via a Lyapunov Lr = r(t)(f(X)−f⋆)+Dψ*(Z,z⋆).
- Provides simple sufficient conditions on η and w under which Lr is nonincreasing, yielding f(X(t))−f⋆ ≤ O(1/r(t)).
- Unifies and extends prior ODE views on acceleration (Su–Boyd–Candès, Krichene–Bayen–Bartlett, Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan).
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t)=w/W to decrease d/dt Lr when possible while preserving the Lyapunov-based rate.
- Introduces a second “energy” function that also decreases under the same conditions and offers additional insight.
- Derives primal dynamics for common constraint sets and presents an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex.
- Gives a discrete-time algorithm for r(t)=t^2; proof sketch is in the supplement rather than the main text.
- Experiments in R^3 show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms gradient/speed restarts and helps especially for strongly convex problems.
- Lyapunov analysis is clean; main condition η(t) ≥ r′(t) plus the trajectory-dependent term yields a clear path to rates.
- Corollary with η = w r/W and w/W ≥ r′/r provides a constructive schedule for O(1/r(t)).
- Existence/uniqueness handling is careful about initialization (t0 > 0).
- Adaptive rule is provably safe: it preserves the base rate and can strictly speed up Lr decrease.
- Discrete-time theory and full O(1/k^2) preservation proof under the adaptive rule are relegated to the supplement and should be brought into the main text.
- Experimental scope is modest (low-dimensional, deterministic); larger-scale and stochastic settings are not explored.
- Paper is well written and organized; Lyapunov argument is easy to follow for readers familiar with ODE acceleration.
- Clarity improvements suggested: make a(t) central earlier, state which discrete results are in main text vs supplement, and provide guidance on β, step size s, and t0.
- Notation/typo cleanups and consistent definitions of r, w, η across sections are recommended.
- Original contributions include the generalized averaging framework, the provable trajectory-aware adaptive weighting, and accelerated replicator dynamics.
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is simple, implementable, and distinct from restart methods.
- The method is practically relevant: a low-overhead heuristic that cannot hurt Lyapunov decrease and often improves performance.
- Pros: clear Lyapunov framework, provable adaptive averaging, concrete primal examples, empirical gains over restarts, and practical adoptability.
- Cons: discrete-time guarantees mostly in supplement, limited experimental breadth, limited hyperparameter guidance, and a trajectory-dependent condition that is not directly checkable without the adaptive rule.
- Suggested improvements: move discrete-time convergence proof to main text with constants/assumptions, add higher-dimensional and stochastic experiments, discuss composite objectives and variance, and provide implementation guidance for mirror maps and ψ*.
- Overall assessment: clear, technically sound, practically relevant work recommended for acceptance subject to stronger discrete-time presentation and modestly broader empirical evaluation.
