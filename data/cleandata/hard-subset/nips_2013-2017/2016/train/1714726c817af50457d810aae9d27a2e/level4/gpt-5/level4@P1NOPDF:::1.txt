This paper builds on the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to design and evaluate a natural heuristic that adaptively reweights the iterates to speed up convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, the proposed method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation shows consistent speedups across a broad class of functions.

I enjoyed reading the paper. The mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al. At the same time, the empirical results are compelling and indicate substantial practical gains. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., the potential impact is high because Nesterov’s acceleration is so widely used in applications. All too often—even in empirical evaluations—new, possibly highly adaptive methods are compared to Nesterov’s acceleration with a fixed weight sequence. This paper confirms that making Nesterov’s method adaptive requires only a small modification and can yield dramatic improvements.

To strengthen the paper technically, I recommend adding an analysis of the discretized process to show that the accelerated convergence rate is preserved under the proposed adaptive averaging. If I am not mistaken, this should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in potential connections between adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
