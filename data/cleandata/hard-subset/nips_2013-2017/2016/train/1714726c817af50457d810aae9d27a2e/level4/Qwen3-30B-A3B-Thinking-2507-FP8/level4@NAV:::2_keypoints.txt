- Paper extends accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes an adaptive averaging variant for one discretization; sometimes outperforms restarted AMD.  
- The adaptive averaging idea is novel and well-motivated.  
- Originality/significance are concerns because key elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) are directly derived from prior NIPS work [7].  
- Generalizing from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and offers limited utility since analysis is ultimately restricted to quadratic rates.  
- Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which aren’t generally applicable.  
- Some contributions (energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but do not meaningfully advance ML.  
- Adaptive averaging lacks full theoretical grounding after discretization; no explicit convergence guarantee is provided, making it effectively heuristic.  
- No empirical or theoretical comparison to other adaptive averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods, so benefits of this variant are unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits for the general strongly convex case.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
