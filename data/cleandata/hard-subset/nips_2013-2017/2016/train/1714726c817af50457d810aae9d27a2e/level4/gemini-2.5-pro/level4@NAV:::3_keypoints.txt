- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al. by generalizing the averaging scheme that produces the primal trajectory from the dual mirror.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates for the generalized averaging schemes under conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- Shows in continuous time one can obtain convergence rates faster than the standard 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection strategy with convergence guarantees and empirically compares it to restarting heuristics.  
- Lacks a theoretical analysis for the discrete (algorithmic) counterpart—this is a notable omission.  
- Paper is well-structured and provides rigorous continuous-time analysis, contributing beyond existing literature.  
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Key open question: whether the improved continuous-time accelerated rates survive discretization.
