This paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, considering both continuous and discrete settings. Building upon the work of Krichene et al. [7], which demonstrated that Nesterov's method can be viewed as a discretization of an ODE system coupling primal and dual trajectories—where the primal trajectory is generated by averaging the mirror map of the dual variable—this paper explores more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the authors derive convergence rates exceeding 1/t<sup>2</sup>. An adaptive weighting scheme, accompanied by convergence guarantees, is also investigated and evaluated experimentally. This adaptive scheme is compared to restarting techniques, which are commonly used to enhance the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. Notably, the theoretical analysis is currently limited to the continuous version of the algorithm, with the discrete case remaining unaddressed.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the proposed averaging scheme for accelerated methods is rigorous and presented clearly in the continuous setting. While the core analytical techniques used to study the continuous dynamics are now well-established in the literature, the paper presents novel contributions and results. In this context, related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and their collaborators should be acknowledged. The main theorem, demonstrating the existence of averaging schemes that achieve a faster convergence rate than 1/t<sup>2</sup>, is a significant result, although it would be valuable to understand whether this improved rate is maintained when transitioning to the discrete algorithm.
