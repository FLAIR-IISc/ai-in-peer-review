This paper presents an analysis of a class of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) using a generalized averaging framework. Through a Lyapunov argument, the authors establish sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to ensure a desired convergence rate. Additionally, they introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The work is substantiated with both theoretical guarantees and comparative numerical results. This research contributes to a substantial body of literature aimed at empirically improving the convergence speed of accelerated methods. The key insight of this paper, derived from the ODE analysis, is that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can also achieve such a speedup. I find this novel method to be very compelling.
