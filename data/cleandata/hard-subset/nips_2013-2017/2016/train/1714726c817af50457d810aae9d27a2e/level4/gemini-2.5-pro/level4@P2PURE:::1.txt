This paper builds upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's accelerated method from Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to propose and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the algorithm's iterates to accelerate convergence. In contrast to previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule approach. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the method yields significant speed-ups on a general class of functions.

I found this paper well-written and enjoyable to read. The mathematical argument is elegant and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results show very promising speed-ups. While the technical development is a direct extension of the work by Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact due to the pervasive use of Nesterov's acceleration in practice. Too often, novel adaptive methods are compared against Nesterov's acceleration using a suboptimal fixed weight sequence. This paper confirms that a minor modification is sufficient to make Nesterov's method adaptive, which can lead to dramatic improvements.

To strengthen the paper from a technical perspective, I would recommend including an analysis of the discretized process to show that the theoretical rate of convergence is preserved. I suspect this analysis would be relatively straightforward to derive. Additionally, I would be interested in a discussion of the potential connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method, particularly in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization.
