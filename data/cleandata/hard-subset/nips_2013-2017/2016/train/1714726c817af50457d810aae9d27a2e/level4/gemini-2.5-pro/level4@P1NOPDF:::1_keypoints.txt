- Builds on the ODE interpretation of Nesterov (Krichene et al. 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.  
- The proposed heuristic is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates significant speed-ups on a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical argument is elegant and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Although a direct extension technically, the work has high potential impact due to Nesterov's widespread practical use.  
- Notes that a small adaptive modification to Nesterov can yield dramatic improvements versus common fixed-weight comparisons.  
- Recommendation: provide analysis of the discretized process to show that convergence rate is preserved.  
- Suggestion: discuss possible connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
