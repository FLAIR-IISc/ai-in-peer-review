This paper investigates accelerated descent methods for constrained convex optimization problems. The authors demonstrate convergence rate guarantees using a Lyapunov function approach. A novel adaptive averaging heuristic is proposed to further improve the convergence speed achieved by the Lyapunov analysis. Experimental results indicate that this adaptive averaging strategy performs comparably to, and in some cases surpasses, adaptive restarting techniques. While I lack deep expertise in this area, the core idea of employing adaptive averaging to accelerate convergence in constrained convex optimization appears both interesting and potentially impactful.
