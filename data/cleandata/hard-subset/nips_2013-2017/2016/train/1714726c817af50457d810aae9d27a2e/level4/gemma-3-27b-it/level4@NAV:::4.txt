This work investigates the convergence properties of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) utilizing a generalized averaging scheme. The authors establish sufficient conditions on the learning rate and weighting parameters to ensure a specific convergence rate, substantiated through a Lyapunov-based analysis. Furthermore, an adaptive averaging heuristic is introduced to enhance the dissipation of the Lyapunov function, accelerating convergence. The paper supports its theoretical findings with numerical experiments. While numerous studies have focused on empirically accelerating convergence in accelerated methods, this paper provides a key insight – demonstrated through the lens of ODEs – that adaptive averaging of weights along solution trajectories can effectively achieve this speedup. I found this novel approach to be particularly compelling.
