- Extends ODE dynamics for accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one discretization.  
- New AMD variant with adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms existing AMD with restarting and is intuitively appealing.  
- Originality/usefulness questioned: many components (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from prior NIPS work [7].  
- Generalized averaging extension seems straightforward and limited: only quadratic rate considered; faster rates would require higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function), primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics deepen understanding but are not highly impactful for ML.  
- Theoretical foundation for adaptive averaging is incomplete due to discretization approximations; no explicit convergence results initially provided, so the strategy is heuristic.  
- Missing comparisons with other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving unclear whether the adaptive variant offers real improvements.  
- Experimental evaluation limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and not convincing for general settings (e.g., strong convexity).  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implement it on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and discuss comparisons; reviewer updated scores.
