- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in a continuous-time framework, extending Krichene et al.  
- Generalizes the averaging scheme that couples primal and dual trajectories, going beyond Nesterov as a specific ODE discretization.  
- Theorem 2 proves continuous-time convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection strategy with theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.  
- Empirical results show the adaptive weights are competitive with common restarting techniques.  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous and technically sound, yielding a meaningful advancement in faster-than-1/t^2 results.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is omitted, which is a major gap given practical interest in discrete methods.  
- Unclear whether improved continuous-time rates are preserved under discretization, weakening practical relevance.  
- Experimental gains may be hard to interpret without discrete convergence guarantees—benefits might not stem solely from continuous-time insights.  
- Paper should acknowledge related continuous-time acceleration work by Attouch, Peypouquet, et al.  
- Recommend adding discussion of potential discrete adaptations or explicitly stating theoretical limitations regarding discretization.  
- Overall: strong and novel continuous-time contribution, but acceptance contingent on addressing or clearly acknowledging the discrete-analysis gap.
