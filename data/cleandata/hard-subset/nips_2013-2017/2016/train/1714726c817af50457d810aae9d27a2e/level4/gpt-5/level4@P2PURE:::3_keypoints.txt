- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending Krichene et al.’s ODE primal-dual viewpoint.
- Builds on the idea that the primal trajectory is obtained by averaging the mirror of the dual variable.
- Investigates more general averaging schemes for constructing the primal trajectory.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with provable convergence guarantees.
- Uses the adaptive weights in experiments and compares them with restarting techniques that improve practical performance.
- Does not include theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm.
- Review finds the paper interesting, well-written, and the continuous-analysis rigorous.
- Notes the continuous-dynamics ideas are now well established but there are meaningful new contributions here.
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Points out it remains open/interesting whether the improved continuous-rate is preserved after discretization.
