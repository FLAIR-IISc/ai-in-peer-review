- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for Nesterov’s accelerated method using its ODE interpretation.  
- Method is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically clear, and logically organized.  
- Technical development closely extends Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) ODE perspective.  
- Small algorithmic modification can yield potentially dramatic practical improvements.  
- Recommends providing a discretized-process analysis to show the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Suggests exploring connections between adaptive averaging and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratics.

- Weaknesses: close dependence on prior work and missing discretized analysis.
