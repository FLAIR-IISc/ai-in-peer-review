- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending Krichene et al.  
- Frames Nesterov’s method as a discretization of an ODE combining a primal trajectory with a dual one via averaging of the mirror dual variable.  
- Proposes and analyzes more general averaging schemes for the dual variable.  
- Theorem 2 proves convergence rates under specific conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- Shows continuous-time convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Presents an adaptive weight-selection scheme with convergence guarantees.  
- Empirically evaluates the adaptive scheme and compares it to restarting techniques that improve practical acceleration.  
- Does not include a theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and offers an interesting continuous-time analysis of the new averaging scheme.  
- Acknowledges that core continuous-dynamics ideas are established, but the work contains novel contributions and results.  
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Notes an open question: whether the improved continuous-time rate carries over to the discrete procedure.
