- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, building on Krichene et al.  
- Interprets Nesterov’s method as a discretization of an ODE system coupling a primal trajectory with a dual one.  
- Shows the primal trajectory arises from a particular averaging of the mirror of the dual variable.  
- Extends to more general averaging schemes and derives convergence rates (Theorem 2) under conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, obtains convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2).  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees and uses it in experiments.  
- Compares the adaptive weighting strategy to restarting techniques that empirically improve accelerated methods.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm/implementation is absent from the paper.  
- Paper is well-written and offers rigorous analysis of the continuous averaging scheme, adding new insights to established continuous-dynamics literature.  
- Notes related prior work (e.g., Attouch and Peypouquet) and highlights the important open question whether the improved continuous rates carry over to discrete procedures.
