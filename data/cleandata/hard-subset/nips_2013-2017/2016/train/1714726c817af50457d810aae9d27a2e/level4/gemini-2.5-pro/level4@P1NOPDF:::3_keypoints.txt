- Topic: studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al.: views Nesterov’s method as a discretization of an ODE coupling primal and dual trajectories.  
- In that framework the primal trajectory is obtained by a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable.  
- Contribution: investigates more general averaging schemes beyond the specific one used previously.  
- Theorem 2: establishes convergence rates for these schemes under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- Continuous-time result: derives convergence rates faster than the classical O(1/t^2).  
- Proposes an adaptive scheme for choosing weights and provides convergence guarantees for it.  
- Experiments: evaluates the adaptive method and compares its performance with restarting techniques.  
- Limitation: no theoretical analysis is provided for the corresponding discrete-time (algorithmic) discretization.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-written, and rigorous in the continuous setting.  
- Suggestion: should cite related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Key open question: whether the improved continuous-time rates are preserved after discretization.
