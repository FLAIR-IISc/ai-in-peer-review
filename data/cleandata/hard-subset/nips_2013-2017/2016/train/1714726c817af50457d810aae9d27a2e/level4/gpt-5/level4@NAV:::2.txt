Review- Building on earlier work that links accelerated Mirror Descent and continuous-time ODEs, the authors generalize the ODE dynamics with a broader averaging scheme and introduce a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one of its discretizations. In some settings, this new AMD variant with adaptive averaging outperforms existing AMD methods with restarts. The adaptive averaging strategy is genuinely new and supported by clear intuition. While the paper is well-presented overall, I remain concerned about its originality and practical value. - Many components (e.g., Lyapunov function, discretization, etc.) closely follow an earlier NIPS paper [7]. Extending from simple averaging to generalized averaging is fairly straightforward and of limited utility, since the authors ultimately focus on the quadratic-rate case; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable. - Some results—e.g., the interpretation of the energy function, the primal reformulation, and the reduction to replicator dynamics—do improve conceptual understanding of the accelerated Mirror Descent algorithm, but they are not sufficiently significant to advance machine learning. - Although adaptive averaging is introduced to meet conditions sufficient for ODE convergence, it lacks full theoretical grounding due to discretization approximations. There is still no explicit convergence result provided, so the method remains a heuristic, as the authors themselves note. Moreover, numerous averaging schemes exist for Nesterov’s accelerated algorithms; without comparisons to these alternatives, it is unclear whether this adaptive variant yields a substantial advantage. - The experiment on a toy problem with only quadratic/linear objectives is not compelling or convincing enough to demonstrate the benefit of adaptive averaging, especially for the more general setting of strong convexity. == post-rebuttal answer== I have read the authors’ rebuttal, and they commit to adding - additional higher-dimensional and non-quadratic examples in the revision - a theoretical proof of convergence for adaptive averaging - an implementation of adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method - a discussion comparing to other adaptive averaging schemes I have updated my scores accordingly.
