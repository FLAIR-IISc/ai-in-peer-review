- Paper extends AMD-ODE with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for a discretized ODE.  
- Experiments show the adaptive averaging can sometimes outperform AMD with restarting on select problems.  
- The adaptive averaging idea is novel and well-motivated by continuous-time analysis.  
- Major originality/practical-significance concerns: core theory (Lyapunov, discretization) largely adapted from prior NIPS work.  
- The jump from simple to generalized averaging is incremental, and authors focus mainly on the quadratic-rate case.  
- Faster convergence rates would require higher-order methods, which limits broad applicability.  
- Several theoretical reinterpretations (energy function, primal form, replicator dynamics) give insight but limited impact on ML practice.  
- The adaptive averaging lacks a rigorous convergence proof in the discrete setting and is treated as a heuristic; no comparison to existing adaptive averaging schemes.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and do not convincingly demonstrate advantages for general strongly convex problems.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide discrete convergence proof, implement adaptive averaging for cubic-regularized Newton, and compare with other schemes; reviewer updated scores.
