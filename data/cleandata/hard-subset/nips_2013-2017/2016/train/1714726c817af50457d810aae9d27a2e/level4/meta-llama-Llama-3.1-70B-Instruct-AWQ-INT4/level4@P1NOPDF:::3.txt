The paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors build upon the work initiated by Krichene et al. in [7], where Nesterov's method is shown to be an appropriate discretization of an ODE system that couples a primal trajectory with a dual one. Specifically, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. In this paper, the authors investigate more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 derives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the authors obtain convergence rates that are faster than 1/t^2. An adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees is also studied and later used in the experimental section. This adaptive choice is compared to restarting techniques, which are known to improve the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. However, the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included.

I think this is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods in the continuous setting is rigorous. Although the main ideas underlying the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well-established in a series of papers, there are new contributions and results. In this regard, I believe the work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be mentioned. The main theorem, which shows that there exist averaging schemes with a faster convergence rate than 1/t^2, is a relevant one. However, it would be interesting to understand whether this improved rate is preserved when transitioning to the discrete procedure.
