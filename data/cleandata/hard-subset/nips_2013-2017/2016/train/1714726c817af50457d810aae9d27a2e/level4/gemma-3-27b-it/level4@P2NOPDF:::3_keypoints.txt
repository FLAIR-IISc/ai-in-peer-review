- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization (continuous and discrete), building on Krichene et al.’s ODE-based connection to Nesterov.  
- Generalizes averaging schemes for constructing the primal trajectory using flexible averaging tied to the dual variable’s mirror map.  
- Theorem 2 provides convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates strictly better than the standard 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weighting scheme with convergence guarantees and empirical evaluation.  
- Benchmarks the adaptive scheme against restarting techniques commonly used in practice.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is omitted.  
- Paper is well-written and offers rigorous analysis within the continuous dynamics framework, contributing novel results despite building on prior work.  
- Recommends acknowledging related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and collaborators.  
- Important open question / future work: whether the improved continuous convergence rates carry over to discrete implementations.
