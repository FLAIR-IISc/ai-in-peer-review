This paper presents an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization. The presented method provides the guarantee that it preserves the original convergence rate when the objective function is not strongly convex. This is an advantage over existing restarting heuristics, and the paper shows this both theoretically and experimentally.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear contribution to the field of optimization. However, I had some difficulties following the content as it is out of my area of expertise. I assume that the used abbreviations and terms are known to those readers that are addressed by the paper, but I would suggest including some background information to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.

The equations between lines 33 and 34, and 46 and 47, could benefit from a more intuitive explanation. The authors may consider adding a few sentences to explain the physical or geometric interpretation of these equations, which would help readers understand the underlying dynamics of the system.

Furthermore, I would like to know the relation to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. While the authors mention these methods, a more detailed comparison and discussion of the similarities and differences between these methods and the proposed adaptive averaging heuristic would be helpful.

Finally, I am wondering about the practical significance of this work. The experiments were carried out in R^3, which is obviously a toy setup. How does the method (and related work) scale to much higher dimensions? Providing some insights or results on the scalability of the method would strengthen the paper and demonstrate its potential for real-world applications.

Minor comment: The reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous. Maybe use a dotted line or colors to show the trajectory of the primal variable?

Strengths:

* The paper presents a clear contribution to the field of optimization.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a high level of background knowledge in optimization and convex analysis.
* The equations could benefit from a more intuitive explanation.
* The relation to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization is not fully explored.
* The scalability of the method to higher dimensions is not demonstrated.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, but with minor revisions to address the above comments. The authors should consider adding some background information, providing a more intuitive explanation of the equations, and discussing the relation to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization. Additionally, providing some insights or results on the scalability of the method would strengthen the paper.

References:

[2] Kingma, Diederik, and Jimmy Ba. "Adam: A method for stochastic optimization." International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR). (2014).
