This paper studies a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) with a generalized averaging scheme. The authors provide sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and the weights to achieve a desired convergence rate, leveraging a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, they employ an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are presented. A substantial body of work aims to empirically accelerate the convergence of accelerated methods; this paper makes a crucial observation, framed within the ODE perspective, that adaptively averaging the weights along solution trajectories can also yield a speedup. I found this new method to be very interesting.
