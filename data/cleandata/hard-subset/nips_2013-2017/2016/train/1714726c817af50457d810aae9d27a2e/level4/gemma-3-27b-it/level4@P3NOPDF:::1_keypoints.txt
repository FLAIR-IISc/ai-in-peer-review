- Proposes a simple, ODE-based adaptation of Nesterov’s accelerated gradient descent using adaptive reweighting of iterates.  
- Builds on Krichene et al.’s continuous-time interpretation to derive a natural averaging heuristic.  
- The method has a theoretical guarantee to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging schemes.  
- Empirical evaluation shows convincing speed-ups across a variety of functions.  
- Presentation and mathematical arguments are clear and accessible.  
- Highlights the broader significance: common benchmarks against fixed-weight Nesterov may underestimate easily attainable improvements.  
- Suggests adding an analysis of the discretized process to verify preserved convergence rates or quantify discretization effects.  
- Recommends exploring connections to Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics as future work.  
- Overall assessment: strong submission with solid theory and experiments; recommends acceptance.
