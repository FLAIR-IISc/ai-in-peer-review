This paper examines accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. It extends earlier work by Krichene et al., where Nesterov’s acceleration is interpreted as a discretization of an ODE that couples a dual trajectory with a primal one formed by averaging the mirror image of the dual variable. Here, more general averaging schemes are introduced. Theorem 2 provides convergence guarantees under suitable conditions on the averaging weights. In continuous time, the framework yields rates that can be faster than 1/t^2. The authors also propose an adaptive rule for choosing the weights with convergence guarantees, and evaluate it empirically against restarting heuristics that are known to enhance accelerated methods in practice. The discrete-time algorithmic analysis is not developed in the main text.

I find the paper interesting and well written. The analysis of the new averaging mechanisms for accelerated methods is carried out in continuous time and is rigorous. While the core ideas behind analyzing continuous-time dynamics are now standard across several works, this paper contributes new insights and results. In this context, it would be appropriate to acknowledge related contributions by Attouch, Peypouquet, and co-authors. The main result, showing that certain averaging schemes can achieve rates faster than 1/t^2, is noteworthy; it would be particularly valuable to understand whether such improved rates are maintained after discretization.
