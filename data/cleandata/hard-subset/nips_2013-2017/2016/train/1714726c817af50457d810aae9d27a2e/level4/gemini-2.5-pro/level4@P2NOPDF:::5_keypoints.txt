- Proposes accelerated continuous-time dynamics for constrained convex optimization with a Lyapunov-based convergence analysis.  
- Core contribution is a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that monitors the Lyapunov function to accelerate convergence.  
- Empirical results indicate adaptive averaging is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, adaptive restarting.  
- Strength: the adaptive averaging heuristic is novel, intuitive, and offers an appealing alternative to restarting.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical underpinning via a Lyapunov function analysis establishing convergence rates.  
- Strength: solid empirical validation using adaptive restarting as a relevant baseline.  
- Weakness: paper needs clearer intuitive explanation of why adaptive averaging is effective and how it adapts to local convergence behavior.  
- Weakness: lacks a deeper conceptual and theoretical comparison between averaging and restarting, including robustness to noise or parameter misspecification.  
- Weakness: scope is limited to constrained convex optimization; discussion is needed on dependence on constraint geometry and extensions to non-smooth or stochastic problems.
