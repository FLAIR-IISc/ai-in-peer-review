This paper explores a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) with a generalized averaging scheme, providing sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to achieve a desired convergence rate through a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, the authors employ an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The paper presents both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons to support its claims.

There exists a substantial body of work focused on empirically speeding up the convergence of accelerated methods. This paper makes a crucial observation, via ODEs, that adaptively averaging the weights along the solution trajectories can also achieve such speedup. I found this new method to be particularly interesting, as it offers a fresh perspective on accelerating convergence.

The paper's use of a generalized averaging scheme and adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and effectively analyzed. The authors provide a clear and comprehensive theoretical framework, which is supported by numerical experiments. The writing is generally clear, and the paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

One potential area for improvement is in providing more context and background information on the existing literature. While the paper does mention the line of works aiming to speed up convergence, a more detailed discussion of the current state of the field and how this work contributes to it would be beneficial. Additionally, the authors may consider providing more insights into the practical implications of their method and its potential applications.

In terms of presentation, the paper is well-structured, and the figures are helpful in illustrating the main points. However, some of the equations could be better formatted for readability. Minor errors in punctuation and formatting were noticed, but these do not detract from the overall clarity of the paper.

Overall, I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field, and its findings are likely to be of interest to researchers working on accelerating convergence. With some minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, the paper has the potential to be even stronger.
