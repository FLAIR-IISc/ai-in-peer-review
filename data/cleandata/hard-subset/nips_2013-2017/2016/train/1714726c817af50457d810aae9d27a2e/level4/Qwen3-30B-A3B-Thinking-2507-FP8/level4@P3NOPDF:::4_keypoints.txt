- Introduces an ODE-based framework to analyze and accelerate optimization via adaptive averaging.  
- Derives sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and weight parameters to guarantee a specified convergence rate using Lyapunov stability.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights along solution trajectories to accelerate Lyapunov decay and convergence without losing guarantees.  
- Bridges theory and practice by providing rigorous convergence proofs alongside empirical validation.  
- Modeling the process as a generalized ODE yields precise conditions for linear convergence and links dual learning rates and weight dynamics to performance.  
- Offers a principled alternative to ad-hoc acceleration methods, addressing acceleration (e.g., Nesterov) while preserving theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks (logistic regression, Lasso) show consistent speedups over non-adaptive baselines with clear metrics.  
- Ablation studies confirm the speedup arises from the adaptive weighting rather than implementation artifacts.  
- Novelty: the ODE perspective explains why adaptive averaging works by aligning with the optimal trajectory of the continuous-time solution.  
- Well-situated relative to prior momentum-based methods (Heavy Ball, Nesterov) and clarifies theoretical connections.  
- Minor concern: implementation details for computing adaptive weights and quantifying "trajectory information" could be more explicit.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written contribution that advances theory and practice; recommendation for acceptance.
