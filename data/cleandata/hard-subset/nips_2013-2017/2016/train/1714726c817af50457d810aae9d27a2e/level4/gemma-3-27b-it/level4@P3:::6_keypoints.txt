- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights via Lyapunov decrease to preserve quadratic convergence even without strong convexity.  
- Leverages a continuous-time ODE view linking Nesterov’s acceleration to coupled primal-dual dynamics for Lyapunov-based analysis.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees and sufficient convergence conditions.  
- Novel approach distinct from restarting heuristics, with an interesting application to replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Core idea is clearly articulated and the paper is logically organized from theory to examples.  
- Numerical experiments show competitive performance against adaptive restarting methods.  
- Experiments are limited to low dimension (R^3), raising scalability concerns.  
- Paper assumes high familiarity with optimization/dynamical systems; key definitions (mirror map, Bregman divergence) need brief explanations.  
- Some equations (notably the ODE definitions) lack intuitive explanations connecting notation to optimization behavior.  
- Related-work discussion is thin; needs clearer comparisons to adaptive methods like Adagrad/Adam and explanation of differing objectives and applicability.  
- Scalability and computational-bottleneck discussion is missing or insufficient.  
- Figure 1 is unclear due to ambiguous reference to the “solid line”; better line styles or colors recommended.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers novel, well-motivated heuristic with strong theoretical analysis and promising experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, no scalability analysis, and insufficient connection to other adaptive optimizers.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions (clarify notation, expand related work, address scalability, improve figures).
