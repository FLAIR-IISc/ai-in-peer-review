This paper analyzes a class of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) that incorporate a generalized averaging scheme. The authors derive sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to guarantee a specific convergence rate, employing a Lyapunov-based argument. Furthermore, they propose an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The work is supported by both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons.

This research contributes to the extensive body of work on empirically accelerating optimization methods. The paper's crucial contribution, derived from its ODE perspective, is the observation that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can achieve significant speedups. I found this novel approach to be compelling and a promising direction for the field.

**Summary and Contributions:**
This paper investigates a class of ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme to develop a novel acceleration method. The primary contribution is the insight, derived from an ODE analysis, that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can speed up convergence. The authors support this idea with a formal analysis, using a Lyapunov argument to provide sufficient conditions on the learning rate and weights for convergence. Building on this theory, they introduce a practical adaptive averaging heuristic. The method's efficacy is demonstrated through both theoretical guarantees and numerical experiments.

**Strengths:**
The paper's main strength lies in its novel connection between adaptive averaging and optimization speed, framed elegantly through the lens of continuous-time dynamics. This ODE-based perspective provides a fresh and insightful way to approach the well-studied problem of designing faster-accelerated methods. The proposed adaptive heuristic is intuitive and well-motivated by the goal of maximizing the decrease in the Lyapunov function. The inclusion of both theoretical analysis and empirical validation makes the paper's claims convincing. This is an interesting and well-executed piece of work.
