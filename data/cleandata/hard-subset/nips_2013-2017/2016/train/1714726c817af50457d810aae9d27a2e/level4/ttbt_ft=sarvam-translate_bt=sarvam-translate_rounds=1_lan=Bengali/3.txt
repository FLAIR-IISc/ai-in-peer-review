This paper discusses a first-order method with acceleration for bounded convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors follow the research initiated by Kryzen and others [7], where Nestorov's method was shown as a suitable discretization connecting a primary trajectory and a dual trajectory. Specifically, the primary trajectory is obtained by averaging a specific mirror of the dual variable. In the present paper, a more general averaging method is discussed. Subsequently, Theorem 2 determines the convergence rate under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the convergence rate that is faster than w,r,too 1/t^2 is determined. An adaptive choice of weights with convergence assurance is also studied and later used in the experimental section. This type of choice is compared with restart techniques, which are known to improve the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. The theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included. I think this is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging method for accelerated methods is presented in the continuous setting and is rigorous. Even though the main concepts of the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well-established in the continuous paper, there are new contributions and results. In this case, I think the work of Atauch, Pepocquette, and co-authors should be mentioned. The main theorem, which shows that there are averaging methods with convergence rates faster than 1/t^2, is relevant, and it would be interesting to understand whether this improved rate is preserved when going to the discrete method.
