- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization via a continuous-time (ODE) framework and proposes corresponding discrete algorithms.  
- Generalizes Krichene et al.'s approach by analyzing a broader class of averaging schemes coupling primal and dual trajectories.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 2) gives convergence rates under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In continuous time, shows existence of averaging schemes achieving rates faster than the classical 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive scheme to select averaging weights with provided convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results show the adaptive scheme compares favorably to standard restarting techniques.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and provides rigorous analysis and novel contributions within an established continuous-time acceleration literature.  
- Major weakness: no theoretical analysis for the discrete version, leaving open whether improved continuous-time rates survive discretization.  
- Suggests the authors should cite related foundational work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
