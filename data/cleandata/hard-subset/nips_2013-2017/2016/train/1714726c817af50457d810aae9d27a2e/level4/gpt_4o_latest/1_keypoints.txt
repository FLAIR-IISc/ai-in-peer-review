- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- Proposes and empirically evaluates a natural adaptive averaging method to accelerate convergence.  
- Approach is provably superior to standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.  
- Empirical results show notable speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical reasoning is straightforward and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Empirical findings highlight significant and promising improvements in convergence speed.  
- Technical contributions are closely tied to Krichene et al.'s framework but have substantial potential impact due to widespread use of Nesterov's method.  
- Shows that a minor modification to Nesterov's method (adaptive weighting) can yield substantial performance gains.  
- Suggestion: include an analysis of the discretized process to confirm the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Curiosity: explore connections between the proposed adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems.
