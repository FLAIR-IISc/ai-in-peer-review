- Paper studies accelerated mirror-descent ODEs for constrained convex optimization parameterized by η(t) and w(t), deriving Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for a target rate 1/r(t).
- Introduces a primal representation, derives an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that targets the Lyapunov derivative.
- Proves continuous-time guarantees (existence/uniqueness, Lyapunov decrease, energy monotonicity) and gives a discrete algorithm with O(1/k^2) rate in a specific discretization.
- Empirical results show adaptive averaging is competitive with and sometimes better than gradient/speed restarts on low-dimensional problems.
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution linking acceleration to generalized averaging with simple Lyapunov conditions, unifying prior ODE analyses.
- Strength: adaptive averaging is principled (directly minimizes Lyapunov derivative) and preserves acceleration rates without requiring strong convexity.
- Strength: provides both continuous- and discrete-time analyses and a novel accelerated replicator example with cross-disciplinary interest.
- Weakness: some derivations (around the averaging dynamics and Lyapunov derivative, lines 33–34 and 46–47) are hard to follow and need more intuition for readers unfamiliar with mirror maps/Bregman divergences.
- Weakness: empirical comparison is limited to R^3, making practical significance, robustness, and scalability unclear.
- Clarity issue: paper assumes familiarity with AMD, mirror maps, Bregman divergences, and replicator dynamics; a short background subsection is needed.
- Clarity issue: Figure 1’s reference to the “solid line” is ambiguous and trajectory styles/colors should be improved.
- Originality/question: generalized weighting and adaptive averaging appear novel but authors should more clearly state how their conditions subsume or differ from Su et al. and Wibisono et al.
- Significance question: need discussion of computational overhead and scaling (memory/compute for dual/primal variables, mirror-map evaluation, adaptive weight computation) and experiments on larger problems.
- Relation to adaptive optimizers: clarify conceptual differences from Adagrad/Adam, potential for combining per-coordinate adaptivity with adaptive averaging, and whether guarantees extend to non-convex settings or at least ensure energy decrease.
- Suggestion: add a concise background on mirror descent, mirror maps, and Bregman divergences with intuitive links to averaging and acceleration.
- Suggestion: provide schematic/high-level derivations for the key equations that cancel problematic Lyapunov terms to aid intuition.
- Suggestion: expand experiments to higher dimensions, report timing/iteration counts, sensitivity to β and stepsizes, and benchmarks on standard constrained problems.
- Suggestion: improve figure visuals (dotted lines or color) to clearly distinguish primal trajectories.
- Argument for acceptance: conceptually clean, Lyapunov-based framework with simple sufficient conditions and a principled adaptive heuristic that preserves rates.
- Argument for acceptance: promising empirical gains and an interesting extension to replicator dynamics.
- Argument against acceptance: limited empirical validation (toy-scale experiments) leaves scalability and practical impact unclear.
- Argument against acceptance: parts of the exposition are difficult to follow and would benefit from added background and clearer intuition.
- Minor comment: Figure 1 should use a dotted line or color to clearly indicate the primal trajectory.
