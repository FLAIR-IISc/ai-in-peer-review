The paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors build upon their previous work with Krichene et al. [7], which demonstrated that Nesterov’s method can be viewed as a discretization of an ODE system coupling primal and dual trajectories. Specifically, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror map of the dual variable. In this paper, they investigate more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 then establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, they derive convergence rates faster than 1/t², and also study an adaptive choice of weights with corresponding convergence guarantees. This adaptive weighting scheme is then evaluated experimentally and compared with restarting techniques, which are known to improve the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. The theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm is not included.

I find this to be an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods is rigorous and presented clearly. While the core ideas behind analyzing these continuous dynamics are now well-established in a series of publications, this work offers new contributions and results. In this regard, the related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and their co-authors should be mentioned. The main theorem, demonstrating the existence of averaging schemes with a faster convergence rate than 1/t², is a significant result. However, it would be valuable to understand whether this improved rate is maintained when transitioning to the discrete procedure.
