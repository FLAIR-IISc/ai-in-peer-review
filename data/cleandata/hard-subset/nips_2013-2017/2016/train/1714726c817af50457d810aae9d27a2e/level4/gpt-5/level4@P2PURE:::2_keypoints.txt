- Paper generalizes accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs with a broader averaging scheme and introduces an adaptive averaging strategy; adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms AMD with restarts.
- Adaptive averaging is interesting, novel, and intuitively motivated.
- Many components (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from prior NIPS [7], raising originality concerns.
- The move from simple to generalized averaging is nearly trivial and of limited practical value since the paper focuses on the quadratic-rate case; faster rates need higher-order methods.
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but are not major machine-learning advances.
- Theoretical grounding for adaptive averaging is weak: discretization approximations leave no explicit convergence guarantees and the method is essentially heuristic.
- No comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes for Nesterov’s methods, so practical improvement is unclear.
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits in general strongly convex settings.
- Post-rebuttal: authors commit to adding higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes; reviewer updated scores.
