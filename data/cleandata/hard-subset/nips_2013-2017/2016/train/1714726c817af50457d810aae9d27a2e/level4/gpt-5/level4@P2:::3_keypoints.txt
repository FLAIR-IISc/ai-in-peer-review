- Studies accelerated first-order dynamics for constrained convex optimization via a generalized averaging viewpoint.  
- Introduces Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) ODEs parameterized by η(t) (dual accumulation) and w(t) (primal averaging).  
- Uses Lyapunov Lr = r(t)(f(X)−f*) + Dψ*(Z,z*) and gives conditions on (η,w,r) ensuring Lr decreases → convergence O(1/r(t)).  
- Recovers standard O(1/t^2) and allows faster continuous-time rates by choosing r(t)=t^p, p>2.  
- Gives a primal-only representation and concrete examples, including an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Introduces a second energy Er that is also decreasing under the same conditions.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic (choose w(t) or a(t)=w/W) to further decrease the Lyapunov derivative while preserving baseline rates.  
- Presents a discrete-time algorithm (for quadratic rate) and numerical comparisons showing adaptive averaging competitive or superior to restarts.  
- Connects to prior ODE/variational work on acceleration (Su–Boyd–Candès, Krichene–Bayen–Bartlett, Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan) and suggests stronger citation of inertial systems literature (Attouch–Peypouquet).  
- Continuous-time analysis is careful and technically sound; existence/uniqueness and initialization at t0>0 are addressed.  
- Lyapunov argument is clear and gives a unified condition for O(1/r(t)) rates.  
- Er appears to align with empirical behavior across heuristics.  
- Discrete-time analysis is limited in the main text; unclear if faster-than-O(1/t^2) continuous-time rates can be preserved by first-order discretizations.  
- Notation is heavy but consistent; more intuition needed for condition (2) in Theorem 2 and practical guidance for choosing r, η, w/a(t).  
- Should clarify practical impact of initializing at t0>0 and computational overhead of adaptive averaging versus restarting.  
- Generalized averaging formulation and explicit η–w coupling are original and insightful.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is a simple, principled alternative to restart schemes.  
- Work offers a unifying lens and flexible recipe to trade off averaging and accumulation in continuous time.  
- Experiments are promising but relatively small-scale; need higher-dimensional tests and overhead/robustness analyses.  
- Recommendation: accept subject to clarifying discrete-time scope, adding pointers to related inertial dynamics, and expanding practical guidance.
