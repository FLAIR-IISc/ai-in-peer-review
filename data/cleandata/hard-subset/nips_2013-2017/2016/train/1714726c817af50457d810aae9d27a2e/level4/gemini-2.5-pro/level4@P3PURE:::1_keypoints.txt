- Uses the ODE interpretation of Nesterov (Krichene et al. 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.
- Proposed heuristic is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging scheme.
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Mathematical argument is simple and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Technical development is a direct extension but could have high impact due to pervasive use of Nesterov acceleration.
- Demonstrates that a simple modification makes Nesterov’s method adaptive and markedly improves performance, providing a stronger baseline.
- Suggestion: include analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Suggestion: discuss possible connections to Conjugate Gradient methods, particularly for strongly convex quadratic problems.
