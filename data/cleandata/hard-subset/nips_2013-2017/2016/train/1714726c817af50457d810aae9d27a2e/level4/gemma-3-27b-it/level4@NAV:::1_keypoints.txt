- Proposes a novel, intuitive heuristic that accelerates Nesterov’s accelerated gradient by adaptively reweighting iterates.  
- Method is inspired by the ODE perspective from Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015).  
- Provides a theoretical guarantee that the adaptive scheme outperforms standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across a wide range of functions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, accessible mathematical exposition.  
- Builds on existing results with a relatively simple technical development, suggesting strong practical impact.  
- Demonstrates that modest adaptations to Nesterov can yield substantial gains compared to more complex adaptive methods.  
- Recommend analyzing the discretized process to verify preserved convergence rates.  
- Suggest exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems.
