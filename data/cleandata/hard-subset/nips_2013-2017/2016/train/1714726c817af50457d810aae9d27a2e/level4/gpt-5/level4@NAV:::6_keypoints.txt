- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate constrained convex optimization.  
- Method preserves baseline convergence rate for non-strongly convex objectives, an advantage over existing restart heuristics.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments are provided to support the claims.  
- Reviewer found the paper hard to follow and recommends adding background material for non-experts.  
- Equations around lines 33–34 and 46–47 need more intuitive explanations.  
- Ask to clarify relationship to adaptive averaging methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Concern about practical significance: experiments are in R^3 (toy setting); question scalability to much higher dimensions.  
- Minor: reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is unclear—use dotted line or colors to show the primal variable trajectory.
