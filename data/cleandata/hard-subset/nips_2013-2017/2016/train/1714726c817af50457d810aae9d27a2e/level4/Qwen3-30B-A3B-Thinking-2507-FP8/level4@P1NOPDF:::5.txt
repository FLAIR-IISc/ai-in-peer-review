

The paper investigates accelerated gradient dynamics for constrained convex optimization problems. Using a Lyapunov function analysis, the authors establish the desired convergence rate. They propose an adaptive averaging scheme to accelerate convergence, demonstrating that this approach matches or exceeds the performance of adaptive restarting. While my expertise in this specific area is limited, the core idea of leveraging adaptive averaging to achieve faster convergence for constrained convex optimization appears promising and merits attention.  

Quality: The technical approach is sound, though the Lyapunov analysis could benefit from greater depth in addressing the interplay between constraint handling and acceleration mechanisms.  
Novelty: The adaptive averaging technique represents an incremental refinement rather than a fundamentally new contribution; it builds directly on established acceleration frameworks.  
Impact: The practical significance for large-scale constrained optimization remains somewhat unclear, as the paper does not sufficiently address computational overhead or real-world applicability.  
Clarity: The exposition is generally accessible but would be strengthened by a more explicit motivation for why adaptive averaging is preferable to existing techniques like adaptive restarting. The connection between the Lyapunov function design and the adaptive rule could also be clarified.
