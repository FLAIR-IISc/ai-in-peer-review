- Introduces a continuous-time ODE framework for accelerated mirror descent coupling a dual gradient-accumulator η(t) with a primal weighted average w(t).  
- Extends continuous-time analyses of Nesterov acceleration (Su et al. 2014) and averaging schemes (Krichene et al. 2015) using a Lyapunov-based derivation.  
- Derives sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) to guarantee convergence at rate 1/r(t).  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily adjusts w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function and preserves the theoretical convergence rate.  
- Provides a discrete-time implementation of the adaptive averaging heuristic (Algorithm 1).  
- Empirically demonstrates competitive or superior performance versus gradient and speed restart heuristics on simplex-constrained problems, with notable speed-ups in some cases.  
- Theoretical strength: proves O(1/t^2) rate preservation for general convex functions, a stronger guarantee than many restart schemes.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, and offers intuitive continuous-time interpretation and a primal-space example (accelerated replicator dynamics).  
- Main theoretical gap: no analysis explaining when or why the heuristic yields faster rates beyond rate preservation.  
- Experimental scope: experiments are low-dimensional (R^3) for visualization; larger-scale validation is lacking.  
- Discretization concern: the discrete update rule for ak uses heuristic choices (objective increase) whose relation to the continuous-time condition could be better justified.  
- Overall: high-quality, novel contribution with solid theory and promising empirical results; weaknesses are minor and suggest directions for future work.
