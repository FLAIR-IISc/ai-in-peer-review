- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization via continuous-time ODEs, building on prior work (e.g., Krichene et al.).

- Generalizes the averaging scheme for recovering primal trajectories from dual variables and analyzes the resulting dynamics.

- Theorem 2 provides sufficient conditions on averaging coefficients to guarantee convergence.

- Introduces an adaptive weighting scheme with theoretical convergence guarantees and compares it to restarting techniques.

- Analysis is rigorous, uses Lyapunov arguments, and extends existing continuous-time accelerated method literature.

- Key theoretical claim: existence of averaging schemes that may achieve convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.

- Empirical results show competitive performance of the adaptive averaging heuristic.

- Well-written and clearly presented, with appropriate contextualization within accelerated methods literature.

- Major limitation: lacks a discrete-time analysis and convergence guarantees for practical algorithms.

- Experiments are limited to simplex-constrained problems and may not generalize across convex optimization tasks.

- Important related work (Attouch, Peypouquet, et al.) on inertial dynamics and fast convergence is insufficiently cited and discussed.

- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing discrete analysis, broader experiments, and more comprehensive related-work discussion in future revisions.
