This paper analyzes a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) equipped with a generalized averaging scheme. Using a Lyapunov framework, it provides sufficient conditions on the choice of dual learning rate and averaging weights to guarantee a prescribed convergence rate. The authors also introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic aimed at accelerating the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are presented.

There is a substantial body of work on empirically accelerating first-order and accelerated methods. This paper makes a notable contribution: through an ODE perspective, it shows that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can likewise yield speedups. I found this approach novel and compelling.
