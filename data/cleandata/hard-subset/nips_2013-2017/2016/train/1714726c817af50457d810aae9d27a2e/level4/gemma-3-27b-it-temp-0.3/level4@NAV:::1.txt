This paper presents a compelling and practical approach to accelerating Nesterov’s acceleration by adaptively reweighting iterates, building upon the ODE interpretation introduced by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015). The proposed heuristic demonstrably improves upon standard fixed-schedule averaging, a guarantee not shared by prior adaptive averaging techniques. The empirical results consistently demonstrate performance gains across a diverse set of functions. 

The paper is well-written and the mathematical reasoning is clear and accessible, particularly for those familiar with the foundational work of Krichene et al. The observed empirical speed-ups are significant and highlight an important point: adaptive methods are often benchmarked against a static implementation of Nesterov’s acceleration, despite the potential for substantial gains through a relatively simple adaptive modification, as convincingly shown here.  Given the widespread use of Nesterov’s acceleration, this work has the potential for broad impact.

To further strengthen the contribution, I recommend including an analysis of the discretized process to formally demonstrate preservation of the convergence rate. This appears to be a tractable extension of the current work. Additionally, exploring potential connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method, particularly in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization, would be a valuable addition.
