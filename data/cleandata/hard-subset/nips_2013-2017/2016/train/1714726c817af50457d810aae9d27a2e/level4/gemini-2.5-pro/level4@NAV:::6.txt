This manuscript introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic aimed at accelerating constrained convex optimization. A key contribution is that the proposed method is guaranteed to preserve the original convergence rate for objective functions that are not strongly convex. The authors demonstrate, both theoretically and with experiments, that this represents an improvement over existing restarting heuristics.

While the technical contributions appear sound, the paper's accessibility could be improved for a broader audience, as the content may be difficult to follow for those not deeply familiar with this specific subfield. Although the terminology and abbreviations used are likely standard for experts, incorporating a brief background section would enhance the paper's readability and impact. In particular, the equations presented between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would be more easily understood if accompanied by a more intuitive explanation.

Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by a discussion on the connection between the proposed method and adaptive averaging techniques used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2].

Finally, questions remain regarding the practical significance of this work. The experiments were performed in a low-dimensional space (R^3), which may not be representative of real-world applications. A discussion on how the proposed method and related approaches are expected to scale to much higher-dimensional problems would be a valuable addition.
