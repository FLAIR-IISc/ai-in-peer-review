This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate constrained convex optimization. The method guarantees preservation of the baseline convergence rate when the objective is not strongly convex. This is an advantage over existing restart heuristics, supported by both theoretical analysis and experiments. I found the paper difficult to follow since it lies outside my main area of expertise. I assume the abbreviations and terminology are familiar to the intended audience, but I recommend adding background material. The equations around lines 33–34 and 46–47 would benefit from a more intuitive explanation. In addition, I would like clarification on the relationship to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. Finally, I have concerns about the practical significance. The experiments are conducted in R^3, which is evidently a toy setting. How does your approach (and related methods) scale to substantially higher dimensions? Minor comment: The reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is unclear.
