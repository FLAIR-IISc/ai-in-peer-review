- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, building on Krichene et al.'s ODE primal–dual coupling view.
- Generalizes the averaging schemes used to obtain the primal trajectory from the mirror of the dual variable.
- Theorem 2 derives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- Shows in the continuous setting convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees.
- Uses the adaptive weighting in experiments and compares it to restarting techniques.
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not provided.
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting, well-written, and the continuous analysis rigorous.
- Notes the main ideas are established in prior work but the paper makes new contributions and should cite Attouch, Peypouquet, et al.
- Raises the question of whether the improved continuous rates persist for the discrete procedure.
