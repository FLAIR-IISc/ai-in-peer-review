- Introduces a generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) framework parameterized by weight functions w and η enabling flexible averaging schemes.
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on weight functions that guarantee desired convergence rates.
- Generalizes prior continuous-time views of acceleration (Krichene et al. 2015, Su et al. 2014) and formalizes the link between acceleration and averaging.
- Shows the replicator dynamics on the simplex can be accelerated via simple averaging and provides relevant mirror-map context.
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically selects weights to speed Lyapunov decrease while preserving quadratic convergence.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: existence and uniqueness of solutions, Lyapunov decrease conditions, and proven convergence rates.
- Gives a clear discrete-time implementation (Algorithm 1) with theoretical guarantees for the adaptive averaging scheme.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (strongly/weakly convex quadratics, linear, KL on simplex) and shows adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting heuristics.
- Empirically verifies the Lyapunov function decreases under adaptive averaging (unlike restarting), validating theoretical claims.
- Visualizations of trajectories, objective values, and energy behavior are insightful and support conclusions.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible with useful supplementary material on mirror maps and replicator dynamics.
- Original contribution: first systematic Lyapunov-based analysis of averaging for acceleration and a provably convergent adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Significant both theoretically (unified framework for accelerated dynamics) and practically (empirical speedups, especially for strongly convex problems).
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed computational complexity comparison of adaptive averaging versus restarting methods.
- Weaknesses: could more explicitly relate the adaptive averaging heuristic to other adaptive optimizers like AdaGrad and Adam.
