- Extends continuous-time AMD ODE with generalized averaging and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for a discretization.  
- New adaptive-averaging AMD variant empirically outperforms existing AMD with restarting in some cases.  
- Adaptive averaging idea is interesting and intuitively appealing.  
- Many technical elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) largely follow prior NIPS work [7], raising originality concerns.  
- The jump from simple to generalized averaging is straightforward and may have limited usefulness.  
- Generalized averaging considered only yields a quadratic rate; faster rates would need higher-order methods not always applicable.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) aid understanding but may not meaningfully advance ML.  
- Adaptive averaging is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof, making it heuristic.  
- The paper does not compare the adaptive scheme to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods, so its practical significance is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate broad advantage, especially under strong convexity.  
- Authors promise to add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes in revision.
