This paper studies a class of ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme, providing sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and the weights to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, the authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons are provided. There is a long line of works aiming to empirically speed up the convergence of accelerated methods. This paper makes a crucial observation via ODEs that adaptively averaging the weights along the solution trajectories can also achieve the speedup. I found this new method very interesting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach. The use of Lyapunov functions to analyze the convergence rate of the proposed ODEs is a nice touch. The adaptive averaging heuristic is also well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how it works. The numerical experiments are also well-designed and provide a good comparison with existing methods.

One potential criticism of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their method. While they do provide some discussion of the computational cost, it would be helpful to have a more detailed analysis of how the method scales with the size of the problem.

Overall, I think this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of optimization. The authors have clearly done a thorough job of developing and analyzing their method, and the results are well-presented and easy to follow.
