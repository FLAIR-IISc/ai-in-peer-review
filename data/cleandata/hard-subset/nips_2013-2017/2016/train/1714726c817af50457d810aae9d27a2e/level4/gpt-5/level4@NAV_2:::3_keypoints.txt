- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.
- Extends Krichene et al. by introducing more general averaging schemes coupling a dual trajectory with a primal average.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence guarantees under specific conditions on the averaging weights.
- In continuous time, the framework can yield rates faster than 1/t^2.
- Authors propose an adaptive rule for choosing averaging weights with convergence guarantees.
- Empirical evaluation compares the adaptive rule to restarting heuristics that improve accelerated methods in practice.
- Discrete-time algorithmic analysis is not developed in the main text.
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting, well written, and the continuous-time analysis rigorous.
- Notes the core continuous-time analysis approach is now standard, though the paper offers new insights.
- Suggests acknowledging related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and co-authors.
- Raises the important open question of whether the improved continuous-time rates survive discretization.
