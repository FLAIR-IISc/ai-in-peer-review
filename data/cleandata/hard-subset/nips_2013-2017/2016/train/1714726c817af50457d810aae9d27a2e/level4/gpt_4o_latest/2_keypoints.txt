- Paper extends ODE view of accelerated Mirror Descent with a more general averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging for a discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging shows improved performance over existing AMD with restarting in some settings.  
- Adaptive averaging idea is novel and supported by intuitive reasoning.  
- Major components (Lyapunov function, discretization, etc.) are directly derived from prior NIPS work [7].  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging seems trivial and of limited utility, since faster rates would require higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) deepen understanding but offer limited impact for ML.  
- Adaptive averaging is only guaranteed for ODE convergence; discretization introduces approximations and no explicit convergence proof is provided, making it heuristic.  
- Lack of comparison to other averaging schemes for Nesterov methods leaves its advantage unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear examples and do not demonstrate benefits for broader or strongly convex cases.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide theoretical convergence proof, implement adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and discuss comparisons to alternatives.  
- Reviewer updated scores based on these commitments.
