The paper NIPS 2015 by Krichene et al. designs and evaluates a natural heuristic method to optimally re-weight algorithm iterations to accelerate convergence, leveraging the ODE interpretation of Krichene et al.'s acceleration of the nestorov. Unlike previous adaptive average heuristics, this method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule average. Empirical evaluation shows that the method gives speed-up over general class functions. I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical reasoning is simple and easy to follow, especially given the knowledge of Krichene et al.'s work. Also, the empirical results show very promising speed-ups. While the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe this paper has a large potential impact, as the acceleration of the nestorov is so widespread in application. Very often, even within empirical evaluation, a new, possibly highly adaptive method is compared to the acceleration of the nestorov with a fixed weight sequence. However, as this paper confirms, only a small modification is required to adapt the nestorov method, and dramatic improvements can occur. To improve the paper from a technical point of view, I would recommend providing an analysis that shows that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I am not mistaken, it should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in the potential connection between the adaptive average of the nestorov method and the conjugate gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
