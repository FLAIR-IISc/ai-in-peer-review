Building on prior work connecting accelerated Mirror Descent and continuous-time ODEs, the authors extend the ODE dynamics with a more general averaging scheme and propose a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one of its discretizations. This new variant of AMD with adaptive averaging outperforms existing AMD algorithms with restarting in certain cases. The proposed adaptive averaging strategy is novel and well-motivated. However, while the paper is generally well-presented, its originality and practical usefulness remain significant concerns. 

Many elements – including the Lyapunov function and discretization method – directly follow from an earlier NIPS paper [7]. The extension from simple averaging to generalized averaging feels incremental and of limited benefit, as the authors ultimately focus solely on the quadratic rate case. Achieving faster rates would necessitate higher-order methods, which are not always applicable.

Certain results, such as the interpretation of the energy function, reformulation into primal form, and reduction to replicator dynamics, offer a better understanding of accelerated Mirror Descent. However, these contributions do not represent a substantial advancement for the field of machine learning.

Although the adaptive averaging strategy is designed to satisfy conditions sufficient for ODE convergence, its theoretical foundation is somewhat weak due to the approximations introduced during discretization. Crucially, the paper does not provide an explicit convergence result, rendering it, as the authors acknowledge, largely heuristic. Furthermore, numerous other averaging schemes exist for Nesterov’s accelerated algorithms; without comparison to these alternatives, it is unclear whether this adaptive variant offers a significant improvement.

The experiment presented uses only a quadratic/linear objective function and is therefore not compelling evidence of the adaptive averaging’s advantages, particularly for the general case of strongly convex functions.
