This paper studies a class of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) that model accelerated optimization methods using a generalized averaging scheme. Through a Lyapunov argument, the authors derive sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and averaging weights required to achieve a desired convergence rate. Furthermore, the paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The claims are supported by both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons.

While there is a long line of work on empirically speeding up the convergence of accelerated methods, this paper makes a crucial observation from an ODE perspective: adaptively averaging the weights along the solution trajectories can also achieve this speedup. I find this new method and the underlying analysis very interesting.
