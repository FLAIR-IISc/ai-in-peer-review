This paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors continue the line of research initiated by Krichene et al. [7], which interpreted Nesterov's method as a specific discretization of an ordinary differential equation (ODE) system coupling primal and dual trajectories. In that work, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. The present paper extends this framework by studying more general averaging schemes.

Theorem 2 derives convergence rates for these schemes under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, this analysis leads to the key result that convergence rates faster than the canonical O(1/t²) are achievable. The authors also propose an adaptive scheme for choosing the weights, provide convergence guarantees, and use it in the experimental section. This adaptive choice is compared with restarting techniques, which are known to improve the practical convergence of accelerated methods.

This is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme is presented rigorously in the continuous setting. While the main ideas for analyzing continuous dynamics are now well-established, the paper contains new contributions and results. The main theorem, which shows the existence of averaging schemes with a convergence rate faster than O(1/t²), is a significant contribution.

However, the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included, which is a notable limitation. It would be very interesting to understand whether the improved rate is preserved when the continuous dynamics are discretized. Furthermore, while the work builds on a series of papers on continuous dynamics, the contributions of Attouch, Peypouquet, and their coauthors in this area should be mentioned.
