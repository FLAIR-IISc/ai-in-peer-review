This paper proposes an approach to accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization problems. The core of the method is the use of a Lyapunov function, which allows the authors to formally establish a desired convergence rate. A key contribution is the introduction of an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function, thereby improving the overall convergence speed. The experimental results demonstrate that this adaptive averaging scheme performs on par with, and in some cases surpasses, the well-known adaptive restarting technique.

While continuous-time optimization dynamics are outside my core area of expertise, I found the central idea of this paper to be both interesting and promising. The proposed adaptive averaging heuristic appears to be an elegant and potentially effective method for accelerating convergence in this setting.

**Quality:** The paper appears to be of high quality. The theoretical analysis is based on a solid foundation using Lyapunov functions, and the claims are supported by experimental comparisons against a relevant baseline (adaptive restarting).

**Novelty:** The main novelty appears to lie in the proposed adaptive averaging heuristic. While adaptive schemes for acceleration are not new, applying this specific averaging technique within a continuous-time dynamics framework for constrained optimization seems to be a novel contribution.

**Impact:** The work has the potential for significant impact, as accelerated methods for constrained convex optimization are of broad interest to the machine learning and optimization communities. If the proposed technique proves to be robust and broadly applicable, it could become a valuable tool for practitioners.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and the core ideas are presented clearly. The authors effectively explain the motivation for their approach and the mechanism behind the proposed adaptive averaging scheme.
