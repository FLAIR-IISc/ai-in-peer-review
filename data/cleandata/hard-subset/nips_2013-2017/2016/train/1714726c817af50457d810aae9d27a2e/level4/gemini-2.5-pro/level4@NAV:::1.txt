This work builds upon the ordinary differential equation (ODE) interpretation of Nesterov's accelerated method from Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to propose and assess a principled heuristic for adaptively reweighting the algorithm's iterates to achieve faster convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is provably guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging scheme. The empirical results demonstrate significant convergence speed-ups across a broad class of functions.

The paper is a pleasure to read. The underlying mathematical argument is elegant and accessible, particularly for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al., and the empirical results show compelling performance gains. While the technical contribution is a direct extension of Krichene et al., I believe the work has substantial potential impact due to the ubiquitous use of Nesterov's acceleration in practice. The paper compellingly demonstrates that many new adaptive methods are unfairly compared to a fixed-schedule Nesterov's method, when a simple modification to the standard algorithm can yield dramatic improvements.

To further strengthen the paper, I would recommend two technical additions. First, providing a convergence analysis for the discretized algorithm would be valuable to formally show that the convergence rate is maintained; this analysis appears to be a tractable extension. Second, I would be interested in a discussion on the potential connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method, particularly in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization.
