This paper analyzes accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. Using a Lyapunov function-based analysis, the authors establish the desired convergence rate for their method. A central contribution is an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to further accelerate convergence. The experimental results demonstrate that this adaptive averaging approach performs comparably to, or even outperforms, the adaptive restarting method.

While my familiarity with this subfield is not extensive, I find the core idea of using adaptive averaging to achieve faster convergence for constrained convex optimization to be both interesting and promising.
