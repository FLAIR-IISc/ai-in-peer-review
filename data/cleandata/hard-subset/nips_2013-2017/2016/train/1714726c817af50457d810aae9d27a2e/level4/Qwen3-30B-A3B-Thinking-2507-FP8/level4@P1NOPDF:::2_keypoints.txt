- Paper extends continuous-time AMD work by introducing a generalized averaging scheme and a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one discretization variant.  
- Adaptive averaging is intuitively appealing and shows improved empirical performance over restarted AMD in specific cases.  
- Major originality concerns: generalizing simple averaging appears straightforward and not substantially novel.  
- Analysis is ultimately restricted to quadratic rates, limiting practical significance since higher-order methods (faster convergence) are often inapplicable.  
- Key technical components (Lyapunov functions, discretization techniques) are directly derived from prior work [7]; the extension is incremental.  
- Results like the energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, and link to replicator dynamics improve understanding but do not materially advance ML methodology.  
- Theoretical foundation for the adaptive averaging is incomplete: it is designed for ODE convergence but lacks rigorous discrete-theory grounding.  
- No explicit convergence guarantee is provided for the discrete algorithm; the method remains a heuristic as acknowledged by authors.  
- Paper fails to compare the adaptive scheme against established alternatives in Nesterov-style accelerated methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and narrow (toy examples with only quadratic or linear objectives), undermining empirical claims and relevance to strongly convex problems.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic examples, provide formal convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implement it for Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and include comparative analysis vs. other adaptive schemes.  
- Based on these planned revisions, the reviewer updated their recommendation to accept.
