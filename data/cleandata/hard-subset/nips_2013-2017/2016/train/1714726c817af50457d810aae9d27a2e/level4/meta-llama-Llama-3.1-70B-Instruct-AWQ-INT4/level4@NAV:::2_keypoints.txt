- Paper extends accelerated Mirror Descent–ODE connection with generalized averaging and an adaptive averaging discretization.  
- The new adaptive AMD variant shows improved performance over existing AMD with restarting in some cases.  
- The adaptive averaging idea is novel and intuitively appealing.  
- Many components (Lyapunov function, discretization) closely follow prior NIPS [7], raising originality concerns.  
- Extending from simple to generalized averaging appears straightforward and of limited impact; focusing on quadratic rate neglects faster rates that need higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) yield insights but don't materially advance ML.  
- Theoretical foundation for adaptive averaging is incomplete due to discretization approximations; no explicit convergence result provided, so it is heuristic.  
- Alternative averaging schemes for Nesterov methods exist, questioning the distinctiveness of this adaptive variant.  
- Experiments on toy quadratic/linear objectives are unconvincing for demonstrating general benefits, especially under strong convexity.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors commit to adding higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors commit to providing a theoretical convergence proof for adaptive averaging.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors commit to implementing adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors will discuss comparisons with other adaptive averaging strategies.  
- Reviewer revised evaluation upward considering these commitments.
