- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using a Lyapunov-based analysis.  
- Proposes a general framework giving sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates via a Lyapunov function.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic intended to speed Lyapunov decrease and empirically competes with adaptive restarting.  
- Provides a theoretical link between averaging and acceleration, connecting Nesterov-type methods to continuous-time ODEs.  
- Extends prior continuous-time analyses (Su et al. and authors’ earlier work) and applies framework to accelerated replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.  
- Introduction effectively motivates continuous-time dynamics for algorithm design and analysis.  
- Strength: solid theoretical rigor through Lyapunov arguments and convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: framework is general, covering a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics via parameterized weight functions.  
- Strength: adaptive averaging is a novel, promising heuristic with competitive numerical performance.  
- Strength: paper situates itself well within existing literature and clearly presents dynamic derivations and the replicator example.  
- Weakness: perceived limited novelty—Lyapunov analysis and adaptive step ideas are not wholly new, contribution is incremental.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited in scope; larger, more diverse problems and comparisons (e.g., Adam) are missing.  
- Weakness: technical conditions in Theorems 2 and Corollary 1 are dense and lack intuitive explanation for non-experts.  
- Weakness: discretization and Algorithm 1 convergence details are relegated to the supplement, reducing accessibility.  
- Argument for acceptance: well-motivated, technically sound work with a useful heuristic and clear connections to prior art.  
- Argument against acceptance: incremental novelty, insufficient experiments, and unclear presentation of some technical details.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically competent paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—expand experiments, clarify theoretical conditions intuitively, and move key discretization details into the main text.
