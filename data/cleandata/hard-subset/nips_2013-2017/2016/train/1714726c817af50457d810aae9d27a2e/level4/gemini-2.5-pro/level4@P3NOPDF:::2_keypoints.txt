- Builds on prior work [7] linking accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) to continuous-time ODEs and generalizes the averaging scheme for those dynamics.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for a specific discretization, yielding a new AMD variant that can sometimes outperform restart-based AMD.  
- Strength: adaptive averaging is well-motivated, intuitive, and grounded in continuous-time dynamics.  
- Strength: provides useful interpretations (primal reformulation, connection to replicator dynamics, energy-function analysis) that aid understanding of AMD.  
- Weakness: limited originality—core framework (Lyapunov, discretization) heavily borrows from [7], and the generalization feels incremental.  
- Weakness: focus remains on quadratic-rate results; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods with limited applicability.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees are incomplete—no proof that the adaptive scheme’s continuous-time properties carry over to the discrete algorithm.  
- Weakness: adaptive averaging currently functions as a heuristic without formal discrete-time convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is insufficient—experiments limited to toy quadratic/linear problems and omit general strongly convex settings.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to other adaptive/heuristic averaging schemes for Nesterov-style methods, leaving practical benefits unclear.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors promise additional higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors promise a theoretical convergence proof for the discrete adaptive algorithm.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors promise application of adaptive averaging to Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors promise discussion and comparisons with other adaptive averaging schemes.
