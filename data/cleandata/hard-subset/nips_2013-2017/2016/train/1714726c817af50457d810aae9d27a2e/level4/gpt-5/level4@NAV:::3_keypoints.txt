- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Extends Krichene et al. by allowing more general averaging schemes coupling primal and dual ODE trajectories.  
- Primal trajectory obtained via specific averaging of mirror of dual variable; paper generalizes that averaging.  
- Theorem 2: establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees and uses it in experiments.  
- Compares the adaptive weighting scheme with restarting techniques known to aid accelerated methods.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not provided.  
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting, well-written, and its continuous analysis rigorous.  
- Suggests citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Notes significance of the faster-than-1/t^2 result but questions whether this improved rate holds for the discrete procedure.
