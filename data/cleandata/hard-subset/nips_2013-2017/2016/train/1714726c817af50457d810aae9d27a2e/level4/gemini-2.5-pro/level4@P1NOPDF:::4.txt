This paper analyzes a class of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) that model a generalized averaging scheme for optimization. Using a Lyapunov-based argument, the authors derive sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and averaging weights to guarantee a specific convergence rate. Furthermore, the paper introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The work is well-supported, providing both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons to validate its performance.

While there is a long line of research aiming to empirically speed up accelerated methods, this paper's key contribution is to offer a novel perspective rooted in the analysis of ODEs. The authors make the crucial observation that adaptively modifying the averaging weights along the solution trajectories provides a new mechanism for achieving this speedup. I find this novel approach to be both insightful and compelling.
