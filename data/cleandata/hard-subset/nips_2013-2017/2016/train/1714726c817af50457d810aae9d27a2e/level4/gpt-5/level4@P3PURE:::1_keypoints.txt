- Leverages Krichene et al.'s ODE view of Nesterov to design an adaptive iterate-reweighting heuristic that speeds convergence.
- Proposed method provably dominates standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Mathematical arguments are simple and easy to follow for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Technical development closely extends prior work, but impact is large due to widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.
- Demonstrates that making Nesterov adaptive requires a small modification yet yields dramatic improvements compared to fixed-weight benchmarks.
- Recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Suggests exploring connections between adaptive averaging in Nesterov and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
