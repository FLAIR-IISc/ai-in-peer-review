The paper leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to design and evaluate a natural heuristic approach to adaptively reweight the iterates of the algorithm, thereby accelerating convergence. Notably, this method is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging, distinguishing it from previous adaptive averaging heuristics. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed method yields speed-ups for a general class of functions. 

I found the paper to be an enjoyable read, with a clear and simple mathematical argument that is easily accessible, especially for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results also show promising speed-ups. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al.'s work, I believe this paper has significant potential impact due to the widespread application of Nesterov's acceleration. Often, even in empirical evaluations, new adaptive methods are compared to Nesterov's acceleration using a fixed weight sequence. However, as this paper confirms, adapting Nesterov's method requires only a minor modification and can lead to substantial improvements.

To further improve the paper from a technical standpoint, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to show that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I am not mistaken, this analysis should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, I would be interested in exploring possible connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
