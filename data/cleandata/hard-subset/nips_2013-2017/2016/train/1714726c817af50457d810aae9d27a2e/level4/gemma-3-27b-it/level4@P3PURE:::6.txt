This paper presents an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization. The method offers a theoretical guarantee of preserving the original convergence rate when applied to non-strongly convex objective functions, which represents an advantage over existing restarting heuristics. This benefit is demonstrated both theoretically and experimentally. While I found it challenging to fully grasp all the details due to the work falling somewhat outside my area of expertise, I believe the paper would benefit from the inclusion of additional background information to improve accessibility. Specifically, a more intuitive explanation of the equations presented between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would be helpful. 

Furthermore, it would be valuable to discuss the relationship of this work to adaptive averaging methods commonly used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] and Adam [2]. Finally, I am interested in understanding the practical significance of this work beyond the presented experiments. The current experimental setup, conducted in R^3, appears somewhat limited. How does the proposed method—and the related work it builds upon—scale to significantly higher dimensional problems?
