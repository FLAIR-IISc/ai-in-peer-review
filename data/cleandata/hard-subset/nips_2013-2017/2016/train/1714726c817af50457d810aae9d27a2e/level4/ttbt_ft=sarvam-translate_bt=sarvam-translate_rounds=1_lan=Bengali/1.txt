In the paper, NIPS 2015, Krichene et al. design and evaluate a natural heuristic approach for algorithm iteration using Nesterov's acceleration ODE to accelerate convergence. Unlike previous adaptive mean heuristics, this method is guaranteed to influence the standard fixed-schedule mean. Experimental evaluation shows that the method increases the speed of functions in a general class. I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical reasoning is simple and easy to follow, especially with knowledge of Krichene et al.'s work. At the same time, the experimental results show very promising speed increases. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe that this paper has a major potential impact, as Nesterov's acceleration is very widespread in applications. Often, even within experimental evaluation, a new, potentially highly adaptive method is compared to Nesterov's acceleration with a specific weight sequence. However, as this paper confirms, only a small change is needed to adapt Nesterov's method, and dramatic improvements can occur. To improve the paper from a technical point of view, I would suggest providing an analysis of a discrete process that shows that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I am not mistaken, it should be relatively simple. I would also be interested in potential connections between the adaptive mean of Nesterov's method and the conjugate gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
