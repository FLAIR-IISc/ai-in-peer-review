This paper presents an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization, providing a guarantee that it preserves the original convergence rate when the objective function is not strongly convex. This is an advantage over existing restarting heuristics, and the authors demonstrate this advantage both theoretically and experimentally.

As a reviewer outside the area of expertise, I found it challenging to follow the content of the paper. While I assume that the used abbreviations and terms are familiar to the target audience, I suggest including background information to facilitate a broader understanding. Specifically, the equations between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from a more intuitive explanation to aid comprehension.

Furthermore, I would appreciate a discussion on the relation to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. This would provide valuable context and help readers understand the positioning of the proposed method within the broader optimization landscape.

I also question the practical significance of this work, given that the experiments were conducted in R^3, which appears to be a simplistic setup. It would be beneficial to discuss how the proposed method (and related work) scales to much higher dimensions, as this is often a critical consideration in real-world optimization problems.

On a minor note, the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous. Using a dotted line or colors to illustrate the trajectory of the primal variable would enhance clarity.
