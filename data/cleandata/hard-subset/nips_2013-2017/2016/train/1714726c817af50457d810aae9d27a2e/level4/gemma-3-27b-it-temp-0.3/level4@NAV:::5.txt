This paper investigates accelerated descent methods for solving constrained convex optimization problems. The authors demonstrate convergence rate guarantees through the construction and analysis of a Lyapunov function. A novel adaptive averaging scheme is proposed to enhance the Lyapunov-based convergence speed. Empirical results indicate this adaptive averaging approach achieves performance comparable to, and in some cases exceeding, that of adaptive restarting techniques. While my expertise in this specific area is limited, the core idea of leveraging adaptive averaging to accelerate convergence in constrained convex optimization appears both intriguing and potentially significant.
