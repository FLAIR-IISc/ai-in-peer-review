This paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, providing a novel approach to achieving desired convergence rates. The authors leverage a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions for the weight functions to guarantee a given convergence rate in continuous time. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The results indicate that the adaptive averaging heuristic performs at least as well as existing heuristics, such as adaptive restarting, with significant improvements in some cases.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The use of Lyapunov functions to analyze the convergence rate of the proposed dynamics is a nice touch, providing a rigorous framework for understanding the behavior of the system. The authors also provide several examples to illustrate the applicability of their approach, including the replicator dynamics, which is a well-known ODE in evolutionary game theory.

The main contribution of the paper is the design of the adaptive averaging heuristic, which is shown to preserve the convergence rate of the original dynamics. This is a significant result, as it provides a way to improve the performance of accelerated methods without sacrificing their convergence guarantees. The authors also provide a discrete version of the heuristic, which is shown to work well in practice.

One potential limitation of the paper is that the analysis is limited to continuous-time dynamics, and it is not clear how the results would generalize to discrete-time systems. However, the authors do provide a discrete version of the heuristic, which suggests that the approach may be more widely applicable.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization, providing a new approach to achieving desired convergence rates for constrained convex optimization problems. The use of Lyapunov functions and the design of the adaptive averaging heuristic are particularly noteworthy, and the results are well-supported by theoretical analysis and numerical experiments.
