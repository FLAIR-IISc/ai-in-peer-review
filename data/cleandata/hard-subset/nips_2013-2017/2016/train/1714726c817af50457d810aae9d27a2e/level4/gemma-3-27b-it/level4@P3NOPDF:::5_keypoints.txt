- Paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using a Lyapunov analysis and an adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Authors claim improved convergence rates and show empirical competitiveness, sometimes outperforming adaptive restarting.
- Strength: The approach is theoretically grounded via a Lyapunov function for proving stability and convergence.
- Strength: The adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and intuitively plausible for improving convergence.
- Strength: Empirical comparison to adaptive restarting provides initial evidence of practical effectiveness.
- Weakness: Insufficient technical detail on how adaptive averaging is performed and which parameters control it.
- Weakness: Lyapunov function construction and its relation to the specific constrained problems need deeper explanation.
- Weakness: Scope of applicability is unclear—limitations for certain constraints or objectives and potential generalization to non-convex settings are not discussed.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited; problem instances, sizes, and characteristics are not fully described.
- Weakness: Need broader comparisons to other state-of-the-art algorithms and reporting of variance/confidence intervals for results.
- Weakness: Unclear parameter tuning methodology and whether results risk overfitting to test problems.
- Weakness: The term “desired convergence rate” is used ambiguously and should be explicitly defined relative to known bounds.
- Overall: Potentially significant and novel contribution with good clarity, but requires more technical detail and stronger experiments.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand technical exposition, experiments, and clarification of convergence claims.
