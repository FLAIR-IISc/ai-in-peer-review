- Studies convergence of a class of ODEs for optimization with a generalized averaging scheme, providing sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and averaging weights.  
- Uses a Lyapunov stability argument to guarantee a specific convergence rate.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic aimed at accelerating the decrease of the Lyapunov function and improving algorithm performance.  
- Provides both rigorous theoretical analysis and empirical validation via numerical comparisons.  
- Motivates the work by connecting empirical acceleration heuristics to a fundamental ODE-based theoretical framework.  
- Novel perspective: analyzing adaptive averaging through ODEs links practical heuristics to theoretical guarantees.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed Lyapunov analysis and quantified convergence rates distinguish it from purely heuristic work.  
- Practical contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic with demonstrated performance improvements in experiments.  
- Clear presentation and well-articulated connection to existing accelerated methods literature.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical claims and show effectiveness of the proposed method.  
- Convergence conditions may be restrictive in practice and need discussion or relaxation for practical applicability.  
- Analysis is limited to a specific class of ODEs; extensions to broader ODE classes would be valuable.  
- Numerical comparisons lack a wider set of baselines, notably state-of-the-art adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adam, RMSprop).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity of the adaptive averaging heuristic is not thoroughly explored; a sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with promising practical implications; recommended for acceptance with suggested improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address practicality of conditions and broaden empirical comparisons in future revisions.
