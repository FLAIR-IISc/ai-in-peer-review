- Paper extends accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs with generalized averaging and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization, showing occasional empirical gains.  
- Adaptive averaging strategy is novel and intuitively appealing; one discretization sometimes outperforms AMD with restarting.  
- Limited originality: key tools (Lyapunov function, discretization) are adopted from prior work [7], and the generalization is incremental, focusing mainly on quadratic-rate cases.  
- Insufficient theoretical grounding: no discrete-time convergence proof for the adaptive averaging; continuous-time justification may not carry over due to approximation errors, and no comparisons to other averaging schemes are provided.  
- Minor contributions and weak experiments: theoretical insights improve understanding but practical impact is unclear, with experiments limited to a toy quadratic/linear example and insufficient coverage of strongly convex or non-quadratic problems.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implement on Nesterov's cubic-regularized Newton, and compare to other adaptive averaging methods—reviewer updated scores accordingly.
