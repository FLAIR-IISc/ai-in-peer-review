This paper presents a method for accelerating descent dynamics in the context of constrained convex optimization. The analysis leverages a Lyapunov function to establish a desired rate of convergence. The core contribution is an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to improve this convergence rate. The empirical results demonstrate that the proposed adaptive averaging technique achieves performance that is on par with, or superior to, adaptive restarting methods.

While this is slightly outside my core area of expertise, the central idea of using adaptive averaging to achieve faster convergence for this class of optimization problems seems interesting and holds promise.
