- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates to speed convergence.
- Proposed method is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent, substantial speedups across a broad class of functions.
- Mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Although a close technical extension, the potential impact is high because Nesterov’s acceleration is widely used and adaptivity yields dramatic improvements over fixed weights.
- Suggests adding an analysis of the discretized process to prove the accelerated convergence rate is preserved under the adaptive averaging.
- Requests exploration of potential connections between adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratics.
