- Proposes an adaptive reweighting heuristic for Nesterov's accelerated gradient method based on the ODE interpretation of Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015).  
- The approach is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation shows significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions and notable improvements in convergence rates.  
- Paper is a clear, concise, and accessible mathematical exposition for readers familiar with Krichene et al.'s work.  
- Technical development is a natural extension of prior work but could have high impact due to widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Demonstrates that only a minor modification (adaptive averaging) is needed to substantially improve typical fixed-weight Nesterov implementations.  
- Reviewer recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to verify the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratic problems.  
- Overall: a simple, effective, theoretically grounded heuristic with promising empirical performance; further technical analysis would strengthen the contribution.
