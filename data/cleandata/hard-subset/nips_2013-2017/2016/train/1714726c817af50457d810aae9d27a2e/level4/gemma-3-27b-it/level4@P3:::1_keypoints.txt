- Investigates adaptive averaging in accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, building on prior ODE-based connection to Nesterov acceleration.  
- Proposes a heuristic to adaptively compute weights in a weighted average of mirrored dual trajectories to speed convergence.  
- Provides sufficient conditions and Lyapunov analysis to establish convergence guarantees for the continuous-time method.  
- Demonstrates empirically that adaptive averaging matches or outperforms existing heuristics like adaptive restarting.  
- Includes the accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex as a concrete example illustrating applicability.  
- Draws an insightful connection between averaging and acceleration within the theoretical framework.  
- The method preserves the original convergence rate, giving it a stronger theoretical footing than many adaptive heuristics.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and situates contributions in relation to key prior work ([11], [20]).  
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and provide convincing supporting evidence.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the discrete implementation’s convergence rate; discretization is only briefly mentioned.  
- Experimental scope is somewhat limited; more problem instances and higher-dimensional tests would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Suggests a potentially fruitful connection to Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics that could be explored further.  
- Overall assessment: significant, theoretically sound contribution with promising empirical results; recommended for acceptance.
