- Introduces a continuous-time AMD framework coupling a dual variable (accumulated gradients) and a primal variable (weighted average), parameterized by η(t) and w(t).
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) for guaranteed convergence rates.
- Main contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily adjusts w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function and preserve O(1/t^2) convergence.
- Provides a discrete-time version and numerical comparisons on low-dimensional, simplex-constrained problems against standard AMD and restarting heuristics.
- Strength: adaptive heuristic comes with formal convergence guarantees by ensuring the Lyapunov function is non-increasing, preserving O(1/k^2).
- Strength: unified AMDw,η formulation elegantly connects averaging schemes and acceleration, enabling principled design and analysis.
- Strength: insightful connections to prior work (recovers [11]) and shows acceleration of replicator dynamics as an illustrative example.
- Weakness: paper is dense and assumes familiarity with continuous-time dynamics, mirror descent, and convex duality; needs more intuition on primal/dual roles.
- Weakness: brief and insufficient comparison to other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam); should clarify conceptual differences and potential synergies.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional (R^3) cases and do not demonstrate scalability or practical utility on high-dimensional problems.
- Minor: Figure 1 caption is ambiguous about the "solid line"; recommend distinct line styles or colors for clarity.
