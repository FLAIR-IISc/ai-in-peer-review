This paper leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration introduced by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to design and evaluate a natural heuristic that adaptively reweights the iterates to speed up convergence. In contrast with previous adaptive averaging heuristics, the proposed method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation shows consistent speed-ups across a general class of functions.

I enjoyed reading the paper. The mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. At the same time, the empirical results demonstrate very promising gains. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., the potential impact is substantial because Nesterov’s acceleration is so widely used in practice.

Too often, even in empirical comparisons, new and highly adaptive methods are benchmarked against Nesterov’s acceleration with a fixed weight sequence. This paper confirms that rendering Nesterov’s method adaptive requires only a small modification and can yield dramatic improvements.

To strengthen the paper technically, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process showing that the convergence rate is preserved; if I am not mistaken, this should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in possible connections between the adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
