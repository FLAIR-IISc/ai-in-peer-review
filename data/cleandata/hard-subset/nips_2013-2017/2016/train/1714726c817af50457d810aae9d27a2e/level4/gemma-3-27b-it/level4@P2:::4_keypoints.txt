- Studies ODEs for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization using a generalized averaging scheme and Lyapunov analysis.  
- Establishes sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and averaging weights that guarantee quadratic convergence (Theorem 2).  
- Derives an equivalent primal representation (AMD′_{w,η}) that aids analysis and provides concrete examples.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights along solution trajectories to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that the adaptive averaging heuristic preserves the convergence rate.  
- Demonstrates applicability by deriving an accelerated replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.  
- Numerical experiments on simplex-constrained problems show comparable or improved performance versus existing heuristics.  
- Visualizations of trajectories and Lyapunov/energy values give insight into algorithm behavior.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation grounded in convex analysis and Lyapunov stability.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically justified adaptive averaging heuristic distinguishing it from empirical restarts.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with strong links to existing literature on continuous-time acceleration.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to simplex constraints; broader empirical validation is needed.  
- Weakness: discretization of the adaptive heuristic (Algorithm 1) lacks detailed justification and analysis.  
- Weakness: potential computational overhead of adaptively computing weights is not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: related work could cite more recent developments (e.g., Attouch et al.).  
- Recommendation: overall positive — strong contribution with theoretical and empirical merit; reviewer recommends acceptance despite noted empirical and implementation gaps.
