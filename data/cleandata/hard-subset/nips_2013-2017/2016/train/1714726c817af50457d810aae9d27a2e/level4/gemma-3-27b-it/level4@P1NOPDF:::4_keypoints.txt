- Studies ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes adaptive averaging along trajectories to accelerate convergence.
- Provides sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to guarantee a desired convergence rate.
- Uses a rigorous Lyapunov-based convergence proof to derive theoretical guarantees.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that accelerates the decrease of the Lyapunov function.
- Presents both theoretical results and numerical experiments validating the method.
- Novel perspective: connects adaptive averaging to acceleration via the ODE viewpoint.
- Main strength is the rigorous theoretical analysis, though the stated conditions can be somewhat restrictive.
- Practical implications are unclear: needs guidance, examples, or recommendations for satisfying/choosing the conditions in practice.
- Experiments are solid but should compare to a wider range of accelerated methods and include harder/ill-conditioned problems.
- Clarity is generally good; some derivations are verbose and could be streamlined.
- Minor suggestions: more precise definition of the ODE class early, explain the "dual learning rate" role, and provide intuition for the adaptive heuristic design.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions.
