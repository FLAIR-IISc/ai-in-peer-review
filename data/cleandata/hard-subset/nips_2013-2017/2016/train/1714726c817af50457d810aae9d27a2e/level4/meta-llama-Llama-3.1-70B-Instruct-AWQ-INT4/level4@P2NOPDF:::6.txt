This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization, offering a convergence rate guarantee when the objective function is not strongly convex. This advantage is demonstrated both theoretically and experimentally, surpassing existing restarting heuristics. However, as the content is outside my area of expertise, I found it challenging to follow. I assume the abbreviations and terms used are familiar to the target audience, but including background information would enhance the paper's accessibility.

The equations between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from more intuitive explanations to facilitate understanding. Furthermore, it would be valuable to discuss the relationship between the proposed adaptive averaging method and those used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] and Adam [2]. I also question the practical significance of this work, particularly given the experiments were conducted in R^3, a seemingly simplistic setup. It would be beneficial to explore how the proposed method and related work scale to higher dimensions.

Minorly, the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous. Using a dotted line or colors to illustrate the trajectory of the primal variable could improve clarity.
