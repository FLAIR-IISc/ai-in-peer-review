This paper studies a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) with a generalized averaging scheme, providing sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and the weights to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, the authors employ an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The paper presents both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. This work contributes to a long line of research aimed at empirically speeding up the convergence of accelerated methods. Notably, the authors make a crucial observation via ODEs that adaptively averaging the weights along the solution trajectories can also achieve speedup. I found this new method to be particularly interesting, offering a novel perspective on accelerating convergence.
