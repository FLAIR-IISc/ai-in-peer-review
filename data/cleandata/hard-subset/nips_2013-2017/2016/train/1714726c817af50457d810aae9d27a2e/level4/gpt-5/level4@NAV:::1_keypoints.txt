- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.  
- The adaptive averaging method is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical development is clear and straightforward, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Though a close extension technically, the method could have substantial impact because Nesterov acceleration is widely used.  
- Highlights that many studies compare against fixed-weight Nesterov, but a minor adaptive modification can yield dramatic improvements.  
- Recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to establish preserved convergence rates.  
- Suggests investigating connections between adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s method and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
