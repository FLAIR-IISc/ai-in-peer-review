

The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. Building upon the work of Krichene et al. [7], which established Nesterov's method as a suitable discretization of an ODE system coupling primal and dual trajectories—where the primal trajectory arises from a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable—the authors extend this framework by examining more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, convergence rates faster than \(1/t^2\) are derived. An adaptive choice of weights with theoretical convergence guarantees is also explored and applied in experiments, where it is compared to restarting techniques known to enhance practical convergence of accelerated methods. The theoretical analysis of the discrete counterpart of the algorithm is omitted. This is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the novel averaging scheme in the continuous setting is rigorous and presents meaningful contributions, even though the core analytical approach for continuous dynamics is now well-established in the literature. The work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be referenced. The main theorem demonstrating averaging schemes achieving convergence rates beyond \(1/t^2\) is significant, though it would be valuable to determine whether this improved rate persists in the discrete implementation.
