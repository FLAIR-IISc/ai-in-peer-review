This paper explores a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) incorporating a generalized averaging scheme. The authors derive sufficient conditions for the dual learning rate and weights to achieve a desired convergence rate, utilizing a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, they propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to expedite the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The paper provides both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons. There exists a substantial body of work focused on empirically accelerating the convergence of accelerated methods. This study makes a significant observation, leveraging ODEs to demonstrate that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can also yield a speedup. I find this new approach particularly intriguing.
