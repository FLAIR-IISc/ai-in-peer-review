1. Extends accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) ODE with generalized averaging and proposes an adaptive averaging discretization that sometimes outperforms AMD with restarting.  
2. The adaptive averaging strategy is interesting and accompanied by good intuition.  
3. Paper is well-presented overall, but originality and practical usefulness are questioned.  
4. Many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) directly follow from an earlier NIPS paper [7].  
5. Generalized averaging extension is straightforward and limited — only yields quadratic rate; faster rates would need higher-order methods.  
6. Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) aid understanding but likely do not significantly advance ML.  
7. Adaptive averaging is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence result (treated as heuristic).  
8. Numerous existing averaging schemes for Nesterov methods are not compared, so advantages of this adaptive variant are unclear.  
9. Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits for general strongly convex problems.  
10. Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide convergence proof, implement on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and compare to other adaptive schemes; reviewer updated scores.
