- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates that accelerates convergence.  
- Offers a principled adaptive averaging approach that guarantees improvement over standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups across a broad range of test functions.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible; mathematical arguments are clear and concise, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Empirical findings demonstrate substantial and encouraging performance gains in practice.  
- Technical contribution is a direct extension of prior ODE-based analysis but has significant practical impact given Nesterov’s widespread use.  
- Demonstrates that adapting Nesterov’s method requires only a minor modification yet yields considerable gains compared to common fixed-weight baselines.  
- Recommendation to strengthen the paper by adding a convergence rate analysis for the discretized process, which seems tractable.  
- Suggests exploring potential connections between this adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient in the strongly convex quadratic setting.
