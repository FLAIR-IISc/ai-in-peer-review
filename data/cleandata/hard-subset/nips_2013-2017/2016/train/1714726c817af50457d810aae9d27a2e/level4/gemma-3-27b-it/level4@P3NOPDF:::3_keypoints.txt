- Generalizes Krichene et al.’s ODE-based view of Nesterov by studying more general averaging schemes for primal-dual trajectories in constrained convex optimization.  
- Theorem 2: proves convergence rates under conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- Shows continuous-time convergence rates that exceed the usual 1/t^2 rate.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection scheme with theoretical guarantees and empirical evaluation.  
- Empirically compares the adaptive scheme to restarting techniques known to improve practical acceleration.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited to the continuous setting; the discrete algorithm is not analyzed.  
- Limited discussion of the computational cost of the adaptive weighting relative to simpler alternatives (e.g., restarting).  
- Well-written, rigorous, and a relevant extension of prior work; central theorem is a notable contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept, provided authors acknowledge the discrete-analysis limitation and outline future work to address it.
