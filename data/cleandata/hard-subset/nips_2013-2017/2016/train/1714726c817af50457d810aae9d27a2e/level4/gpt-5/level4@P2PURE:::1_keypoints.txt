- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.  
- Proposed adaptive averaging heuristic is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates speed-ups on a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Empirical results are promising and show dramatic improvements from a small modification.  
- Technical development is a close extension of prior work but has substantial practical impact due to Nesterov’s ubiquity.  
- Notes that many methods are compared to fixed-weight Nesterov; this paper shows adaptivity is simple and beneficial.  
- Recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Suggests exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
