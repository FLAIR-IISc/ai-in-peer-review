- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, generalizing prior averaging-scheme interpretation of Nesterov’s method.
- Main contribution: derives convergence rates under conditions on averaging coefficients, with Theorem 2 giving a unified continuous-time framework.
- Demonstrates that convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 are achievable in the continuous dynamics under certain conditions.
- Provides a rigorous analysis of the continuous dynamics, building on established literature in the area.
- Suggests acknowledging related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Proposes an adaptive choice of averaging weights with provable convergence guarantees.
- Empirically compares the adaptive averaging scheme to restarting techniques and reports valuable performance insights.
- Does not include a theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm.
- Unclear whether the improved continuous-time convergence rates are preserved after discretization.
- Novelty: offers a new generalization of averaging schemes and a new adaptive-weighting contribution.
- Significance: makes a valuable contribution to convex optimization with potential practical improvements for accelerated methods.
- Quality and clarity: well-written, rigorously analyzed, clearly organized, and consistently notated.
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
