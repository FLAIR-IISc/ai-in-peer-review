The paper leverages the ordinary differential equation (ODE) interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration, as introduced by Krichene et al. in NIPS 2015, to design and evaluate a natural heuristic method for adaptively reweighting the iterates of the algorithm to accelerate convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this approach is guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed method yields speedups for a general class of functions. 

I found this paper enjoyable to read, with a straightforward and easy-to-follow mathematical argument, particularly for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results also show promising speedups. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe this paper has significant potential impact, given the widespread application of Nesterov's acceleration. In many empirical evaluations, a new, highly adaptive method is often compared to Nesterov's acceleration with a fixed weight sequence. However, this paper confirms that making Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a small modification, leading to dramatic improvements.

To further improve the paper from a technical standpoint, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to show that the rate of convergence is preserved. If I am not mistaken, this analysis should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, I would be interested in exploring possible connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
