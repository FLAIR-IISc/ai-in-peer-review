- Paper generalizes ODE dynamics for accelerated Mirror Descent with a novel adaptive averaging discretization that sometimes outperforms AMD with restarts; adaptive averaging is new and intuitively motivated, but originality and practical value are questioned.  
- Many technical components (Lyapunov function, discretization) closely follow prior NIPS work [7], and the generalized averaging extension is straightforward with limited utility since the paper focuses on quadratic-rate cases.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve conceptual understanding but are not seen as significant advances for machine learning.  
- Adaptive averaging lacks full theoretical grounding because discretization approximations leave no explicit convergence proof, so it currently functions as a heuristic; there are many other averaging schemes that are not compared.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear problem and are not convincing evidence of benefit, especially for broader strong-convexity settings.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors commit to adding higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
