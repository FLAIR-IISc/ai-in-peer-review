- Paper extends AMD-to-ODE connections with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization, showing improved performance in some cases versus restarting AMD.  
- Adaptive averaging is well-motivated and an interesting new approach.  
- Major concerns about originality and practical utility remain.  
- Many technical components (Lyapunov functions, discretization) are directly derived from prior NIPS work [7].  
- Extension to generalized averaging is trivial and ultimately restricted to the quadratic-rate case; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods not broadly applicable.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but offer limited machine-learning advances.  
- Adaptive averaging satisfies ODE convergence conditions but lacks full theoretical grounding after discretization; no explicit convergence guarantee is provided, making it effectively a heuristic.  
- No comparisons to alternative averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving the practical benefit of the adaptive variant unclear.  
- Experimental validation is limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and fails to demonstrate benefits for general strongly convex problems.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors commit to adding higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a theoretical convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation on Nesterov's cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons with other adaptive averaging schemes.
