- Paper generalizes accelerated mirror descent via a broader averaging scheme and introduces an adaptive averaging strategy for a discretization.
- AMD with adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms AMD with restarts in the reported cases.
- Adaptive averaging is a fresh idea with clear intuition and the paper is generally well presented.
- Many technical components (Lyapunov construction, discretization) are taken directly from prior work [7], so originality is limited.
- Moving from simple to generalized averaging is straightforward and of limited utility since analysis/experiments focus on quadratic rates; faster rates would require higher-order methods.
- Energy-function interpretation, primal reformulation, and connection to replicator dynamics aid conceptual understanding but are not transformative for ML practice.
- Adaptive averaging’s theoretical basis is weakened by discretization; no explicit convergence guarantees are given, so it remains heuristic.
- Lack of comparisons to other averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods makes it unclear whether the proposed variant offers a real advantage.
- Experiments use toy quadratic/linear problems and do not convincingly demonstrate practical benefits, especially in strongly convex or general settings.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a theoretical convergence proof, an implementation within Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and a discussion comparing other adaptive averaging strategies; reviewer adjusted scores.
