This paper analyzes a class of Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) incorporating a generalized averaging scheme. Using a Lyapunov-based argument, the authors establish sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and averaging weights to guarantee a specific convergence rate. Furthermore, the paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function, supporting this method with both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons.

This work contributes to the extensive literature on empirically speeding up accelerated optimization methods. The paper's key insight, derived from an ODE perspective, is the crucial observation that adaptively averaging weights along the solution trajectories can also achieve such a speedup. I find this novel approach to be very interesting.
