- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on prior work (Krichene et al.) viewing Nesterov’s method as ODE coupling primal and dual trajectories.  
- Primal trajectory obtained via specific averaging of the mirror map of the dual variable; this paper investigates more general averaging schemes.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting they derive convergence rates faster than 1/t².  
- They propose and analyze an adaptive choice of averaging weights with corresponding convergence guarantees.  
- The adaptive weighting scheme is evaluated experimentally and compared to restarting techniques.  
- Theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm is not provided.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and presents a rigorous analysis of the new averaging scheme.  
- Reviewer recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet et al., and questions whether improved continuous rates carry over to the discrete algorithm.
