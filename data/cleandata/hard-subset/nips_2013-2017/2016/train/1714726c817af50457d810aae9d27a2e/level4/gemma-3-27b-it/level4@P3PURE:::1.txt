The paper leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration, as presented by Krichene et al. at NIPS 2015, to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the iterates of the algorithm, thereby accelerating convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is theoretically guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the method yields speed-ups across a general class of functions. I found this paper to be a compelling read; the mathematical argument is straightforward and easily accessible, particularly for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results further support the approach, exhibiting promising speed-ups. While the technical development builds closely upon Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact, given the widespread applicability of Nesterov’s acceleration. It is common, even in empirical evaluations, to compare novel, potentially highly adaptive methods against Nesterov’s acceleration employing a fixed weight sequence. However, as this paper convincingly demonstrates, adapting Nesterov’s method requires only a modest modification and can lead to substantial improvements. To further strengthen the paper technically, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to show that the convergence rate is preserved. If I am correct, this analysis should be relatively tractable. I would also be interested to see a discussion of potential connections between this adaptive averaging of Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
