This paper presents an adaptive mean heuristic that accelerates constrained convex optimization. The presented method guarantees that it preserves the original convergence rate, even if the objective function is not strongly convex. This is an advantage over existing restart heuristics, and the paper demonstrates this both theoretically and experimentally. I had difficulty understanding the content of the paper because it is outside my field of expertise. I believe the abbreviations and terms used are familiar to readers addressed by the paper, but I would suggest including background information. Equations between lines 33, 34, and 46, 47 could benefit from more intuitive explanations. Furthermore, I would like to know the relationship between adaptive mean methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. Finally, I am wondering about the practical significance of this work. The experiments were conducted in R^3, which is clearly a toy setup. How does your method (and related work) scale to very large dimensions? Minor note: The reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is unclear. Perhaps use a dotted line or colors to show the trajectories of the primitive variables? [1] Duchie, John, Elad Hazan, and Yoram Singer.
