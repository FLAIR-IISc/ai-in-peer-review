- Extends prior work connecting accelerated Mirror Descent to continuous-time ODEs by introducing generalized averaging in ODE dynamics and an adaptive averaging discretization.
- Adaptive averaging idea is intuitively appealing and novel as a heuristic.
- Heavy reliance on the authors’ prior NIPS paper [11], limiting perceived originality.
- Generalized averaging extension is mathematically straightforward and mainly focused on achieving quadratic convergence.
- Contributions (energy interpretation, primal reformulation, replicator dynamics reduction) deepen understanding but are incremental for ML.
- Discretization introduces approximations; the adaptive scheme lacks a fully rigorous theoretical foundation and explicit convergence guarantees.
- Numerous existing averaging schemes for Nesterov methods are not compared, leaving unclear advantages of the adaptive variant.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to simple quadratic and linear objectives and does not convincingly demonstrate benefits in broader strongly convex settings.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: provide a theoretical proof of convergence for the adaptive averaging scheme.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: implement adaptive averaging with Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add a discussion comparing the approach to other adaptive averaging methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel adaptive heuristic, deeper continuous-time/acceleration insights, promising initial experiments, and authors’ commitment to revisions.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental over prior work, incomplete theory (heuristic), limited experiments, and averaging generalization constrained to quadratic rate.
- Overall assessment: reasonable extension with an interesting heuristic but limited impact until stronger theory and comprehensive experiments are incorporated; recommend acceptance conditional on promised improvements.
