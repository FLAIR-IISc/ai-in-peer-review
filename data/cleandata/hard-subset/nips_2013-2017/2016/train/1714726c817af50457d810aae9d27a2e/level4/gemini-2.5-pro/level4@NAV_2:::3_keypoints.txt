- Paper studies accelerated first-order constrained convex optimization via continuous-time dynamics, generalizing Nesterov’s ODE to a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) parameterized by w(t) and η(t).  
- Models acceleration as coupling a dual gradient-accumulator and a primal variable given by a generalized weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory.  
- Theorem 2: provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on w(t) and η(t) to guarantee convergence rate 1/r(t).  
- Framework can produce continuous-time dynamics with rates faster than O(1/t^2).  
- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics as a concrete example.  
- Proposes an “adaptive averaging” heuristic that dynamically adjusts w(t) to maximize Lyapunov decrease while preserving theoretical rates.  
- Presents a discrete-time version of the adaptive heuristic and empirically compares it to adaptive restarting methods.  
- Experiments (low-dimensional) show the adaptive heuristic is competitive or superior to restarting and that the Lyapunov Lr is non-increasing.  
- Analysis is technically sound, rigorous, and unified via a Lyapunov approach.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivates and derives the main results; the replicator example is insightful.  
- Novelty: generalizing averaging to achieve variable convergence rates and the principled adaptive averaging heuristic are valuable contributions.  
- Suggests better positioning relative to inertial dynamics/Hessian-driven damping literature (e.g., Attouch, Peypouquet) for clarity on precise novelty.  
- Raises the key open question of whether faster-than-O(1/t^2) continuous rates can be realized by first-order discrete algorithms; discretization may necessitate higher-order methods.  
- Recommends including a formal discrete-time convergence theorem for Algorithm 1 in the main text rather than only the supplement.  
- Notes an empirical curiosity: the energy Er also appears non-increasing for restarting heuristics and suggests briefly addressing this observation.
