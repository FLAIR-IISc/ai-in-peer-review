- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE-based view of Nesterov-style acceleration to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- Adaptive averaging scheme is provably no worse than standard fixed-schedule averaging and guaranteed to dominate it.  
- Experimental results show consistent speed-ups across a broad class of objective functions.  
- Paper is clear, enjoyable, and mathematically concise—especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.’s framework.  
- Empirical gains are compelling despite the technical contribution being a fairly direct extension.  
- Expected substantial impact because Nesterov’s acceleration is widely used and often compared against fixed schedules.  
- Recommendation to add a discrete-time analysis showing preservation of the convergence rate.  
- Suggestion to explore connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
