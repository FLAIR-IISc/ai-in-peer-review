- Proposes a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics with a generalized averaging scheme and sufficient continuous-time weight conditions for convergence rates.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic intended to preserve the continuous-time convergence rate.
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function, plus a primal ODE representation and examples for different constraint sets.
- Builds directly on earlier NIPS work linking accelerated mirror descent to continuous-time ODEs.
- The generalized averaging extension is viewed as incremental/trivial, since the paper only targets quadratic convergence rates.
- Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which the paper does not address.
- Reformulations (energy interpretation, primal form, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but offer limited ML impact.
- The adaptive averaging heuristic lacks rigorous theoretical guarantees after discretization and is presented without explicit convergence proofs.
- Experimental evaluation is weak—only toy quadratic/linear examples and no convincing results for strongly convex cases.
- No comparisons to other averaging schemes from Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving practical advantage unclear.
- Strengths: clear, well-organized presentation; interesting adaptive averaging idea; unified Lyapunov-based ODE study.
- Weaknesses: limited originality, unclear significance, theoretical gaps in discretization, and unconvincing experiments.
- Overall recommendation: score 6/10, with concerns about originality and significance to be addressed.
