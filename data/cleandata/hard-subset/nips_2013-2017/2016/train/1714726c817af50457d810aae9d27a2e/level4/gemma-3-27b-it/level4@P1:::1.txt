This paper builds upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration, developed by Krichene et al. at NIPS 2015, to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates to accelerate convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method guarantees performance comparable to, or better than, standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates speed-ups across a range of functions. I found this paper to be well-written and enjoyable to read. The mathematical argument is clear and easy to follow, particularly for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. Moreover, the empirical results suggest promising performance gains. While the technical development is a natural extension of Krichene et al.’s work, I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact, given the widespread use of Nesterov’s acceleration. It is common to compare new methods against Nesterov’s acceleration using a fixed weight sequence, even in empirical evaluations. However, as this paper demonstrates, adapting Nesterov’s method can be achieved with a modest modification and can lead to substantial improvements. 

To further strengthen the paper technically, I recommend including an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate the preservation of the convergence rate. If I am correct, this analysis should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, I would be interested to see a discussion of potential connections between the adaptive averaging presented here and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Sound Technical Contribution:** The paper presents a technically sound extension of previous work on accelerated methods, building upon a strong theoretical foundation.
*   **Clear and Accessible Presentation:** The paper is well-written and presents complex ideas in a clear and understandable manner.
*   **Promising Empirical Results:** The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed heuristic, showing speed-ups in convergence.
*   **Potential Impact:** The work addresses a common practice in the field (using fixed schedules for Nesterov’s acceleration) and offers a simple yet effective improvement.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Incremental Advance:** The work builds directly on the authors’ previous NIPS publication and could be viewed as an incremental contribution.
*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis of Discretization:** The analysis currently focuses on continuous-time dynamics; a detailed analysis of the discretized algorithm would strengthen the paper.
