- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, building on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov's method.  
- Explores more general averaging schemes where the primal trajectory averages the mirror map of the dual variable.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under specific conditions on averaging coefficients.  
- Shows in the continuous setting convergence rates strictly better than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weighting scheme and provides convergence guarantees for it.  
- Empirically evaluates the adaptive scheme and compares it to practical restarting techniques.  
- Theoretical analysis is restricted to the continuous dynamics; the discrete algorithm is not analyzed.  
- Paper is well-written and presents rigorous continuous-time analysis, though core techniques are standard in the literature.  
- Should acknowledge related work by Attouch, Peypouquet and collaborators.  
- Key open question: whether the improved continuous-time rate carries over to the discrete algorithm.
