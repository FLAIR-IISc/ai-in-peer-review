This work investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization within both continuous and discrete frameworks. The authors build upon the research initiated by Krichene et al. in [7], which framed Nesterov's method as a discretization of a primal-dual ordinary differential equation (ODE) system. Specifically, in that system, the primal trajectory is derived from a particular averaging of the dual variable's mirror. The present paper advances this concept by examining more generalized averaging schemes.

Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates for these schemes, subject to suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. For the continuous-time dynamics, the analysis demonstrates that convergence rates exceeding the traditional 1/t^2 can be attained. The paper also introduces an adaptive weight selection strategy with convergence guarantees, which is then benchmarked against restarting techniques in the experimental section—a common heuristic for improving the practical performance of accelerated methods. A theoretical analysis for the discrete counterpart of the algorithm is notably absent.

This is an interesting and well-structured paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods is rigorously presented for the continuous-time case. Although the core analytical principles for continuous dynamics are now largely established through a body of prior work, this paper provides new contributions and results. In that vein, the work of Attouch, Peypouquet, and their coauthors should be cited. The main theorem is particularly relevant, demonstrating that averaging schemes can achieve convergence rates faster than 1/t^2. A key question that remains, however, is whether this accelerated rate is maintained when the procedure is discretized.
