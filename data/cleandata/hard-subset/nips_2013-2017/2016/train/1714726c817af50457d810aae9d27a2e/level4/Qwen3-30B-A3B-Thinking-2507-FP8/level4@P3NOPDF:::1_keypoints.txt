- Presents an adaptive reweighting heuristic for Nesterov's method, extending Krichene et al.'s ODE-based interpretation.  
- Shows the adaptive averaging provably outperforms standard fixed-schedule averaging while preserving accelerated-method guarantees.  
- Demonstrates significant practical improvements across convex problems (e.g., logistic regression, Lasso) with convincing empirical evidence.  
- Technical argument is clean, accessible, and builds directly on the established continuous-time framework.  
- Empirical evaluation shows statistically significant and practically meaningful convergence speedups on benchmarks.  
- Highlights that comparing new adaptive methods to fixed-schedule Nesterov is misleading; adaptive baseline is more appropriate.  
- The heuristic requires minimal changes to standard Nesterov and is easy to adopt in practice.  
- Potentially high impact because it addresses a widespread suboptimal implementation of Nesterov's acceleration in ML/optimization.  
- Suggests adding a rigorous analysis of the discretized (implemented) algorithm to ensure convergence rates transfer from the ODE.  
- Recommends exploring connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics to potentially align/adapt weights for faster convergence.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, empirically validated contribution that merits publication.
