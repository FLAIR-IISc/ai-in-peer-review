- Core idea: proposes adaptive averaging by coupling a gradient-accumulating dual variable with a primal variable formed as a weighted average of the dual trajectory, with adaptive weights to accelerate convergence.
- Theoretical contribution: provides sufficient conditions and a Lyapunov-based analysis proving convergence of the adaptive scheme.
- Rate guarantee: shows both theoretically and empirically that the adaptive averaging preserves quadratic convergence, outperforming restarting heuristics for non-strongly convex objectives.
- Novelty and significance: offers a principled, provable adaptive acceleration heuristic rather than an empirical-only restart method.
- Connection to prior work: builds on continuous-time ODE views of Nesterov acceleration and relates to accelerated mirror descent literature.
- Example/application: connects the method to replicator dynamics, illustrating potential relevance to evolutionary game theory.
- Empirical validation: numerical experiments in R^3 show competitive performance against adaptive restarting.
- Scalability concern: experiments limited to R^3 raise questions about performance and practical challenges in higher-dimensional settings.
- Accessibility issue: technical depth (mirror descent, Lyapunov analysis) may limit readership; foundational explanations could be expanded.
- Exposition fix: equations defining the dynamics (lines 33–34 and 46–47) need more intuitive explanations to aid understanding.
- Related-work gap: lacks a detailed comparison to adaptive methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam); differences are noted but not deeply analyzed.
- Presentation detail: Figure 1’s reference to the “solid line” is ambiguous and should use clearer line styles or colors.
- Recommendation: overall strong theoretical and novel contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance despite suggested improvements.
