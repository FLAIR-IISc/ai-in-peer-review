The paper builds upon the ODE perspective of Nesterov's acceleration, originally presented by the authors in NIPS 2015, to introduce and analyze a novel adaptive heuristic for reweighting iterates, aiming to accelerate convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging approaches, this method offers theoretical guarantees of improved performance compared to standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates promising speed-ups across a range of functions. This is a well-written paper with a clear and accessible mathematical argument, particularly for those familiar with the prior work of Krichene et al. The empirical results are also compelling. While the technical development represents a natural extension of Krichene et al., this paper has significant potential impact given the widespread use of Nesterov’s acceleration. It’s common to see new methods compared against Nesterov’s acceleration using a fixed weighting sequence; however, as this work demonstrates, adapting Nesterov’s method itself through a small modification can yield substantial improvements. To further strengthen the paper, I recommend analyzing the discretized process to demonstrate the preservation of the convergence rate. This appears to be a feasible extension. Exploring potential connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization could also be a valuable addition.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with claims well-supported by analysis and experimentation. It presents a complete piece of work, carefully evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized, providing sufficient information for an expert reader to understand the approach and reproduce the results. 
**Originality:** The problem of adaptively weighting Nesterov's acceleration is novel, and the proposed approach builds on existing techniques in a meaningful way. The paper appropriately references related work.
**Significance:** The results are important, as they offer a practical improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm. This work is likely to be adopted by researchers and practitioners looking to enhance the performance of accelerated methods. It advances the state-of-the-art by providing a guaranteed method for adaptation. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   The paper presents a theoretically sound and empirically validated improvement to Nesterov’s acceleration.
*   The adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and offers a practical approach to improving convergence speed.
*   The connection to continuous-time dynamics provides valuable insights into the behavior of the algorithm.
*   The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
*   The theoretical analysis, while rigorous, could be extended to provide guarantees for the discretized process.
*   The connection to other optimization methods (e.g., Conjugate Gradient) could be further explored.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of optimization. I recommend acceptance.
