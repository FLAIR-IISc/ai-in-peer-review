This paper investigates accelerated descent methods for solving constrained convex optimization problems. The authors demonstrate convergence rate guarantees through the construction and analysis of a Lyapunov function. Furthermore, they introduce an adaptive averaging technique intended to improve the convergence speed associated with the Lyapunov analysis. Empirical results indicate this adaptive averaging strategy achieves performance comparable to, and in some cases exceeding, that of adaptive restarting methods. While my familiarity with this specific research area is limited, the proposed approach of leveraging adaptive averaging to enhance convergence in constrained convex optimization appears both insightful and holds considerable potential.
