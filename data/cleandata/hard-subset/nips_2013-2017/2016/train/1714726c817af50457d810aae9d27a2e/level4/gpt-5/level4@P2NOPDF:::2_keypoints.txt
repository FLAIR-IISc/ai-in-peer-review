- Extends AMD ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging for one discretization; adaptive variant can outperform restarted AMD in some cases.
- Adaptive averaging mechanism is interesting, well motivated, and the paper is clearly written and technically competent.
- Originality concerns: core elements (Lyapunov/energy function, discretization) closely follow prior NIPS paper [7]; the move to generalized averaging seems incremental.
- Limited scope: analysis and experiments focus on O(1/t^2) “quadratic” rate; achieving faster rates would typically require higher-order methods.
- Conceptual contributions (energy perspective, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) aid intuition but do not substantially advance practice or applicability.
- Theoretical guarantees are weak: discretization introduces approximations that undermine ODE-based guarantees and no formal convergence proof is given for the discrete algorithm.
- Missing comparisons: paper does not empirically or theoretically compare to alternative averaging schemes used in Nesterov-style methods.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and does not demonstrate benefits for strongly convex, high-dimensional, or non-quadratic problems.
- Post-rebuttal, authors committed to higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, implementation within cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons with other schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
