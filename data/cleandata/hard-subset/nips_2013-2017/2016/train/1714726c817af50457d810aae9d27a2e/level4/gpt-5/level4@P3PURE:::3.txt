This paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. It continues the line of work initiated by Krichene et al. [7], where Nesterov’s method is interpreted as a suitable discretization of an ODE that couples primal and dual trajectories. In particular, in that framework, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. The present paper generalizes this idea by analyzing broader averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous-time setting, the authors derive rates faster than 1/t^2. They also propose an adaptive choice of the weights with provable guarantees, which is then used in the experiments and compared against restarting techniques, known to improve the practical performance of accelerated methods. The theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included.

Overall, I find this to be an interesting and well-written paper. The continuous-time analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods is rigorous. While the main ideas underlying the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well established across several papers, this work does provide new contributions and results. In this respect, the authors should also cite related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors. The main theorem—showing that there exist averaging schemes with convergence rates faster than 1/t^2—is a significant result. It would, however, be important to understand whether this improved rate is preserved under discretization, especially since the theoretical analysis for the discrete algorithm is not provided.
