- Builds on continuous-time ODE interpretation of accelerated mirror descent (Krichene et al. 2015) and introduces a generalized averaging scheme.  
- Derives sufficient conditions for a desired convergence rate using Lyapunov function analysis.  
- Proposes "adaptive averaging," a heuristic that dynamically modifies averaging weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.  
- Adaptive averaging provably preserves the original O(1/t^2) convergence rate, unlike many restarting heuristics.  
- Empirical results on constrained convex problems show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms adaptive restarting, especially for strongly convex cases.  
- Overall presentation is clear and the core idea is elegant and practically significant.  
- Theoretical argument and continuous-time analysis provide strong intuition and are easy to follow.  
- Key theoretical strength: provides guarantees for the adaptive heuristic via Lyapunov analysis.  
- Experimental validation is effective and demonstrates consistent empirical benefits.  
- Weakness: main paper focuses on continuous-time analysis while the discrete-time algorithm (Algorithm 1) proof is relegated to the supplement; a sketch/main result should be in the paper.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of connections to Conjugate Gradient for unconstrained strongly convex quadratics, which could provide deeper insight.
