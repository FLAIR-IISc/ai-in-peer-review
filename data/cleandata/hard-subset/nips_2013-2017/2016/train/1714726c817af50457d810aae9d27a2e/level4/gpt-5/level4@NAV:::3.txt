The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors extend the line of work initiated by Krichene et al. [7], where Nesterov’s method is shown to be an appropriate discretization of an ODE that couples a primal trajectory with a dual one. In particular, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. The present paper studies more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 then establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 are derived. An adaptive choice of the weights with convergence guarantees is also proposed and then used in the experimental section. This choice is compared with restarting techniques, which are known to improve the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. The theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included. I find this to be an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods is presented in the continuous setting and is rigorous. Even though the main ideas underlying the analysis of continuous dynamics are now well established across a series of papers, there are new contributions and results here. In this regard, the work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be mentioned. The main theorem, showing that there exist averaging schemes with convergence rates faster than 1/t^2, is significant, though it would be interesting to understand whether this improved rate is preserved when moving to the discrete procedure.
