- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al. by viewing Nesterov’s method as an ODE coupling a primal trajectory with a dual one.  
- Primal trajectory obtained via specific averaging of the mirror map applied to the dual variable.  
- Investigates more general averaging schemes beyond the classical choice.  
- Theorem 2: establishes convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, obtains convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of averaging weights with convergence guarantees.  
- Empirically compares the adaptive scheme with restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not provided.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and provides rigorous continuous-time analysis.  
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Notes significance of the main theorem and raises the question whether improved rates extend to the discrete procedure.
