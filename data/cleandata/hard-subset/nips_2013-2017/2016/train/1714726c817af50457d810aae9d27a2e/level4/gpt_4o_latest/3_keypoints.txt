- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.
- Builds on Krichene et al.: Nesterov’s method as an ODE coupling a primal trajectory (averaged mirror of the dual) with a dual one.
- Proposes and analyzes more general averaging schemes for the primal trajectory.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates that exceed 1/t^2.
- Develops an adaptive weight-selection scheme with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Uses the adaptive scheme in experiments and compares it to restarting techniques.
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version is missing.
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and provides rigorous continuous-time analysis.
- Suggests citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and collaborators.
- Main theorem is significant, but it's important to verify whether the faster rates carry over to the discrete setting.
