- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al. by treating Nesterov’s method as a discretization of an ODE coupling primal and dual trajectories, with the primal from averaging the mirror of the dual.  
- Extends that framework to more general averaging schemes.  
- Theorem 2 proves convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, obtains convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Introduces an adaptive choice of weights with theoretical guarantees and compares it experimentally to restarting techniques.  
- Does not provide a theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm (noted as a significant omission).  
- Paper is interesting and well-written; the continuous analysis is rigorous and contributes meaningfully, though the core continuous-dynamics approach is already established.  
- Recommends citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Open question: whether the improved continuous rates persist in the discrete implementation.
