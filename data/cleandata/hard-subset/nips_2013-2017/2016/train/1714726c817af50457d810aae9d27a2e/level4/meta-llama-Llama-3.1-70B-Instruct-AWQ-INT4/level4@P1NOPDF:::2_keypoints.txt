- Paper extends AMD ODE with generalized averaging and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization that can outperform existing AMD with restarting in some cases.  
- The adaptive averaging strategy is interesting and comes with good intuition.  
- Many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) largely follow an earlier NIPS paper [7], raising originality concerns.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is technically sound but trivial and of limited use, since only quadratic rates are considered and faster rates need higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function), primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics improve understanding but do not significantly advance ML.  
- Adaptive averaging is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof, so it remains heuristic.  
- No comparison to other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving unclear whether the adaptive variant is meaningfully better.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and are not convincing for the general strongly convex case.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors promise more experiments, a convergence proof, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other schemes; reviewer updated scores accordingly.
