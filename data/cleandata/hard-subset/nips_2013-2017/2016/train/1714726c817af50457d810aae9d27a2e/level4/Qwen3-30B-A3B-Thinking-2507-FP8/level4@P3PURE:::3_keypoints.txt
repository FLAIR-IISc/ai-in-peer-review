- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al. by treating Nesterov as a discretization of coupled primal-dual ODEs and explores more general averaging schemes.  
- Theorem 2 derives convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In continuous time, the authors establish convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight choice with convergence guarantees and evaluates it experimentally.  
- Compares the adaptive strategy with restarting techniques known to improve practical convergence.  
- Omits theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm, leaving unclear whether improved continuous rates persist after discretization.  
- The lack of discrete analysis is a significant gap given the paper’s practical implications.  
- Continuous-setting analysis is rigorous and presents novel contributions despite relying on established techniques.  
- The main theorem demonstrating averaging schemes achieving rates faster than 1/t^2 is significant.  
- Recommends citing Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors to better contextualize related continuous-time results.  
- Overall, theoretical results are compelling, but omission of discrete analysis weakens practical impact.
