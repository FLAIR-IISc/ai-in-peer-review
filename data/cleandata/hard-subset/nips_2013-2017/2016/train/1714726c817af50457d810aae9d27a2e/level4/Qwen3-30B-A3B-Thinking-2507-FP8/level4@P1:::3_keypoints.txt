- Proposes a generalized family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) continuous-time dynamics parameterized by two weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Uses Lyapunov analysis to derive sufficient conditions on weight functions guaranteeing convergence rates (e.g., 1/t^2).
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves theoretical convergence rates while improving empirical performance.
- Demonstrates application of the framework to accelerate replicator dynamics on the simplex, relevant to evolutionary game theory.
- Presents thorough numerical experiments showing adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics, especially for strongly convex problems.
- Provides a rigorous and general Lyapunov-based theoretical framework extending prior continuous-time analyses of Nesterov-style acceleration.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and well-situated within relevant literature (Su et al., Krichene et al., O’Donoghue & Candès).
- Major weakness: lacks a developed discrete-time counterpart in the main text, which is critical for practical algorithms and the NIPS audience.
- Insufficient discussion and comparison with related continuous-time work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Limited technical detail on how to discretize the adaptive averaging heuristic for implementation.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, important contributions to theory and practice, recommend acceptance.
