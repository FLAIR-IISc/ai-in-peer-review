This paper builds upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration, as presented by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015), to introduce and evaluate a principled heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates, thereby accelerating convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging techniques, this approach offers a guaranteed improvement over standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical results demonstrate speed-ups across a broad range of functions. I found the paper to be well-written and accessible; the mathematical reasoning is clear and concise, particularly for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. Furthermore, the empirical findings reveal substantial and encouraging speed-ups. While the technical contribution represents a direct extension of Krichene et al., I believe this work has significant potential impact given the widespread use of Nesterov's acceleration. It is common to compare novel, often highly adaptive methods against Nesterov's acceleration using a fixed weighting scheme; however, this paper demonstrates that adapting Nesterov's method itself requires only a minor modification and can yield considerable performance gains. To strengthen the technical analysis, I recommend including a convergence rate analysis for the discretized process, which appears to be relatively tractable. I would also be interested to see a discussion of potential connections between this adaptive averaging of Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method in the context of strongly convex quadratic optimization.
