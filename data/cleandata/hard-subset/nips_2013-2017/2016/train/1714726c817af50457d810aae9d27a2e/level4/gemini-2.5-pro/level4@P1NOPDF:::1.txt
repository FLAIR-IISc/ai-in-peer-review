This paper builds upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's accelerated method, developed by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015), to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the algorithm's iterates to accelerate convergence. A key advantage of this approach, in contrast with previous adaptive heuristics, is that it is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the method yields significant speed-ups on a general class of functions.

I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical argument is elegant and easy to follow, especially for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. At the same time, the empirical results show very promising speed-ups. While the technical development is a direct extension of the work by Krichene et al., I believe this paper has a high potential for impact because Nesterov's accelerated method is so pervasive in practice. Too often, new and highly adaptive methods are empirically compared against Nesterov's method using only a fixed weight sequence. As this paper confirms, making Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a small modification and can lead to dramatic improvements.

To improve the paper from a technical standpoint, I would recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process showing that the rate of convergence is preserved. My sense is that this should be a relatively straightforward extension. I would also be interested in seeing a discussion of the possible connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
