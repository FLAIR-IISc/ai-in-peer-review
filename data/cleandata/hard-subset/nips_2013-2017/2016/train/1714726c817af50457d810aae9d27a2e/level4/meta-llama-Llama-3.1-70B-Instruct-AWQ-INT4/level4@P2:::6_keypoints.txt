- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate constrained convex optimization.  
- Guarantees preservation of the original convergence rate when the objective is not strongly convex, unlike existing restarting heuristics.  
- Studies a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) continuous-time dynamics parameterized by two positive weight functions.  
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function and gives sufficient conditions on weights to ensure desired convergence rates.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging rule that computes weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.  
- Experiments show the adaptive heuristic outperforms existing restarting heuristics, especially in the strongly convex case.  
- Empirical results confirm the Lyapunov function is decreasing under the adaptive heuristic but not under restarting heuristics.  
- Paper is well-written and provides thorough theoretical and experimental analysis.
