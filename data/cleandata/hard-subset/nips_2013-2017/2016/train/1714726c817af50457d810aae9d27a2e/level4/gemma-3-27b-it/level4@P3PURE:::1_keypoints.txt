- Leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration (Krichene et al., NIPS 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.
- The proposed adaptive averaging method is theoretically guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Mathematical development is straightforward and accessible, especially to readers familiar with Krichene et al.
- Empirical results are promising and support the practical value of the approach.
- Though building closely on prior work, the method has potential for significant impact given the wide applicability of Nesterov acceleration.
- Demonstrates that a modest modification to Nesterov’s method (adaptive averaging) can yield substantial improvements compared to fixed-weight baselines.
- Reviewer recommends adding an analysis of the discretized process to verify the convergence rate is preserved (likely tractable).
- Reviewer suggests discussing possible connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems.
