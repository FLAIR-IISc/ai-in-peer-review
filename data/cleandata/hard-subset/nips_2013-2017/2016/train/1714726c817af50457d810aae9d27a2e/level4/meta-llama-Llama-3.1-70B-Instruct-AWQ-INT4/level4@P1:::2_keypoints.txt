- Extends connection between accelerated Mirror Descent and continuous-time ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization.
- Adaptive averaging strategy is interesting, intuitive, and empirically outperforms AMD with restarting in some cases.
- Many technical elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) largely follow an earlier NIPS paper [7], raising originality concerns.
- Generalized averaging extension is limited (only treats quadratic rate); achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods not addressed.
- Reformulations (energy interpretation, primal form, reduction to replicator dynamics) aid understanding but are not critical advances for ML.
- Adaptive averaging is introduced to meet ODE convergence conditions, but discretization approximations mean it is not fully theoretically grounded.
- No explicit convergence proof is provided; authors themselves treat the method as heuristic.
- There exist many averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods; lack of comparisons leaves the practical benefit of this adaptive variant unclear.
- Experiments use only toy quadratic/linear objectives and are insufficient to demonstrate advantage in general or strongly convex settings.
- Paper should include higher-dimensional and non-quadratic experiments to strengthen empirical claims.
- Paper should provide a theoretical convergence proof for the adaptive averaging scheme.
- Paper should include comparisons and discussion versus other averaging alternatives.
- Recommendation: revise and address theoretical gaps, broaden experiments, and compare to alternatives before acceptance.
