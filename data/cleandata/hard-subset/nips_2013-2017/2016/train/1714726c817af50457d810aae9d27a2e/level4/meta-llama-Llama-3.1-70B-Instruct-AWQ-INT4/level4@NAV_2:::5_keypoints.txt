- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization and proposes a novel approach to achieve desired convergence rates.
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on weight functions guaranteeing a given continuous-time convergence rate.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function.
- Empirically, the adaptive averaging heuristic matches or outperforms existing heuristics (e.g., adaptive restarting), with significant improvements in some cases.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the arguments easy to follow.
- Lyapunov-based analysis provides a rigorous framework for understanding the proposed dynamics.
- Includes several illustrative examples (including replicator dynamics) demonstrating applicability.
- Main contribution: adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the original dynamics’ convergence rate while improving practical performance.
- Authors present a discrete version of the heuristic that performs well in experiments.
- Limitation: analysis is primarily for continuous-time dynamics, leaving unclear the generalization to discrete-time systems.
- Potential limited interest outside the optimization research community.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting additional discussion/analysis of limitations—especially regarding discrete-time generalization.
