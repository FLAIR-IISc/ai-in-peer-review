- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration.  
- Proposes a heuristic to adaptively adjust iterate weights to accelerate convergence.  
- Guarantees improved performance over traditional fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.  
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad range of functions.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, accessible mathematical argument for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Technical development closely extends prior work, but impact is substantial given widespread use of Nesterov's acceleration.  
- Demonstrates that only a minor modification is needed to make Nesterov's method adaptive with considerable gains.  
- Suggests adding analysis of the discretized process to verify preservation of the convergence rate.  
- Recommends exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
