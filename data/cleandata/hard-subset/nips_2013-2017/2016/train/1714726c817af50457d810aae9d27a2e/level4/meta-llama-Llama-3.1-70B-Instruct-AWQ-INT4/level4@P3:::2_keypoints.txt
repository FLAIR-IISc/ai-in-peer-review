- Proposes extending accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics with a generalized averaging scheme.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging strategy for one discretization that sometimes outperforms existing AMD with restarting.
- Provides a unified framework for AMD behavior under different averaging schemes using a Lyapunov-based analysis.
- Offers a primal representation of the dynamics and a reduction to replicator dynamics, aiding understanding of AMD.
- Much of the analysis (Lyapunov function, discretization) closely follows an earlier NIPS paper, raising originality concerns.
- The generalized averaging extension is unsurprising and only analyzed for quadratic (standard) convergence rates.
- Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not addressed and may be inapplicable.
- The adaptive averaging is heuristic due to approximation in discretization and lacks an explicit convergence proof.
- Unclear whether the adaptive variant improves over many existing averaging schemes without broader comparisons.
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic and linear examples and do not convincingly demonstrate advantages for strongly convex or general cases.
- Authors should add theoretical backing for the adaptive method, compare to alternative schemes, and test on higher-dimensional and non-quadratic problems.
- Overall assessment: interesting contributions but limited originality, theoretical grounding, and empirical validation.
