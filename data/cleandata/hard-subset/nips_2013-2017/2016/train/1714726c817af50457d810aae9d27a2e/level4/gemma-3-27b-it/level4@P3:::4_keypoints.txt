- Studies continuous-time ODEs for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization with a generalized averaging scheme.  
- Provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and averaging weights to guarantee target convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate convergence, with theoretical guarantees.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over adaptive restarting, particularly in the strongly convex case.  
- Frames accelerated methods as coupling of dual and primal variables and links averaging scheme variation to performance.  
- Connects the framework to mirror descent/replicator dynamics as an illustrative example.  
- Strong theoretical foundation via Lyapunov analysis.  
- Novel contribution by introducing adaptive averaging beyond traditional restarting techniques.  
- Clear linkage to existing literature on continuous-time dynamics and Nesterov acceleration.  
- Practical relevance supported by numerical experiments showing competitive or improved performance.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Mathematical exposition is dense and may be hard to follow for readers not expert in mirror descent or Lyapunov stability.  
- Numerical experiments are limited in scope and could be expanded to more problems and methods (e.g., Adam).  
- Transition from continuous-time analysis to discrete Algorithm 1 is abrupt; discretization effects on guarantees need more discussion.  
- Minor technical attribution/detail (rate of Nesterov’s method) could be clarified.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and theoretically sound work with promising experiments; recommended for acceptance.
