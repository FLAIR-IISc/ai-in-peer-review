- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, building on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s method.  
- Generalizes the prior framework by analyzing broader averaging schemes for obtaining the primal trajectory from the dual variable.  
- Theorem 2: establishes convergence rates under specific conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In continuous time, derives convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of averaging weights with provable guarantees.  
- Uses the adaptive weights in experiments and compares them against restarting techniques known to improve practical performance.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is not provided, leaving uncertainty about discretization-preservation of improved rates.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and well-written; continuous-time analysis is rigorous and provides new contributions despite related prior work.  
- Suggests citing related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
