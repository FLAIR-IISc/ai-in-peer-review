- Paper introduces AMDw,η: a continuous-time accelerated mirror-descent family parameterized by η(t) and w(t), uses a Lyapunov Lr to derive sufficient conditions for O(1/r(t)) convergence and a discrete algorithm.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic (greedy decrease of the Lyapunov) with a proof it preserves O(1/t^2) continuous-time and discrete-time rates; shows an accelerated replicator-dynamics example and R^3 experiments comparing to restarts.  
- Strength: provides a clean, unified theoretical framework (AMDw,η) and elegant Lyapunov-based convergence analysis.  
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is theory-derived and retains worst-case O(1/t^2) guarantees, unlike many restart schemes.  
- Strength: demonstrates a compelling application by deriving accelerated replicator dynamics, linking to evolutionary game theory.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to toy, low-dimensional (R^3) simplex problems and do not show scalability to realistic high-dimensional ML tasks.  
- Weakness: manuscript is technically dense and lacks intuitive explanations of the core ODEs and the primal-dual coupling.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and comparison with adaptive methods (Adagrad/Adam), especially regarding mechanism differences and potential relations.  
- Suggestion: add experiments on higher-dimensional, realistic problems (e.g., logistic regression, LASSO, SVM) and sparse settings to assess scalability.  
- Suggestion: include more intuition and geometric explanation for the primal-dual dynamics and the mirrored weighted averaging.  
- Suggestion: expand on conceptual differences between adaptive averaging and per-coordinate adaptive learning-rate methods and comment on possible combinations.  
- Minor: clarify Figure 1 caption and use distinct line styles or colors to remove ambiguity.
