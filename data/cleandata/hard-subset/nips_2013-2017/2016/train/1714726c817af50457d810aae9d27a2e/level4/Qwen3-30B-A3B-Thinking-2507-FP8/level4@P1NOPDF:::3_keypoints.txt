- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings.
- Extends Krichene et al. by generalizing the averaging that produces the primal from the dual trajectory.
- Introduces broader averaging schemes and analyzes their continuous-time behavior.
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates under conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- Derives continuous-time rates faster than 1/t^2 and proposes an adaptive weight selection with theoretical guarantees.
- Empirically compares the adaptive weighting to restarting techniques, which improve convergence in practice.
- Omits theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm and its convergence rates.
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous and presents novel, meaningful results.
- Reviewer recommends citing related continuous-time work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Key unresolved question: whether improved continuous rates survive discretization.
- Experiments support the adaptive scheme but do not address the discrete-rate gap or theoretically justify superiority over restarting in discrete settings.
- Suggests adding at least a high-level discussion or conjecture about discrete-case challenges to strengthen practical relevance.
