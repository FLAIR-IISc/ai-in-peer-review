- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging approach for accelerated descent dynamics in constrained convex optimization.  
- Leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to design the adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function and gives sufficient conditions for target convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that is claimed to preserve the algorithm's convergence rate.  
- Numerical experiments show the method compares favorably to existing restarting heuristics and improves performance in some cases.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, easy-to-follow mathematical arguments.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing across experiments.  
- Builds heavily on Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015), so technical development may be seen as a close extension rather than a radical departure.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the discretized algorithm to rigorously show the convergence rate is preserved in practice.  
- Potentially significant due to broad applicability of Nesterov acceleration, especially for strongly convex objectives.  
- Overall quality is high: organized presentation, sufficient background, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty in adaptive averaging, solid ODE/Lyapunov analysis, and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments for rejection: dependence on prior work and missing discrete-time convergence guarantees.
