- Extends prior ODE-based AMD work with a more general averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for a discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms existing AMD with restarts; the idea is genuinely new and intuitively motivated.  
- Significant concerns about originality and practical usefulness despite clear presentation.  
- Many core elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from earlier NIPS paper [7].  
- Generalizing from simple to generalized averaging appears largely trivial and of limited value since the focus remains on the quadratic-rate case.  
- Achieving faster-than-quadratic rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable.  
- Reformulations (energy interpretation, primal form, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve conceptual understanding but offer limited practical impact for ML.  
- The discretization weakens theoretical grounding: adaptive averaging was introduced for ODE convergence but no explicit convergence guarantee is provided in the discrete algorithm.  
- By the authors’ own description the adaptive method remains heuristic, and many alternative averaging schemes exist without comparison.  
- Experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and do not convincingly demonstrate benefits in the general strongly convex setting.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors will add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, an implementation within Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive schemes.  
- Reviewer updated scores following these commitments.
