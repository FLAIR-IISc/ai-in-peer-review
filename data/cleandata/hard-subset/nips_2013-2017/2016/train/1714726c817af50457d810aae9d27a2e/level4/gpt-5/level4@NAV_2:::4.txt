The paper analyzes a family of optimization ODEs equipped with a generalized averaging mechanism. Using a Lyapunov-based argument, it derives sufficient conditions on the dual step size and the averaging weights to attain a target convergence rate. The authors further introduce an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts the weights to accelerate the decay of the Lyapunov function. Both theoretical guarantees and empirical comparisons are presented. There is a substantial line of work on heuristics that empirically speed up accelerated methods; this study adds an ODE-based perspective showing that adaptively averaging the weights along the trajectory can also deliver such improvements. I found this approach particularly interesting.
