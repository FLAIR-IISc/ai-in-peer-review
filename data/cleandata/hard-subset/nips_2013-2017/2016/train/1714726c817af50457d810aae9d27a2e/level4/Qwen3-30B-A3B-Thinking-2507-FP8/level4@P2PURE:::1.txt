

The paper builds upon Krichene et al.'s NIPS 2015 ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting algorithm iterates, aiming to accelerate convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics, this method guarantees dominance over standard fixed-schedule averaging in terms of convergence rate. Empirical results demonstrate significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions. The paper is well-written and enjoyable to read, with a simple and accessible mathematical argument, particularly for readers familiar with Krichene et al. The empirical results further showcase highly promising improvements. While the technical development constitutes a direct extension of Krichene et al., the paper holds substantial potential impact due to the pervasive use of Nesterov's acceleration in applications. Many empirical evaluations incorrectly compare new adaptive methods against Nesterov's acceleration with fixed weights, yet this work confirms that adapting Nesterov's method requires only a minor modification and yields dramatic performance gains. To strengthen the technical contribution, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate that the convergence rate is preserved; this should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization would be valuable.
