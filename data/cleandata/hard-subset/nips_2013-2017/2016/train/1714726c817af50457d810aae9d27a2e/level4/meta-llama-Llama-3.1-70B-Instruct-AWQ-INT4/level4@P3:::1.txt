This paper introduces a novel approach to adaptive averaging in accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The authors leverage the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration and design a natural heuristic to adaptively reweigh the iterates of the algorithm to speed up convergence. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a unified study of the ODE using a Lyapunov function and sufficient conditions for achieving a desired convergence rate.

The authors also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the convergence rate and demonstrate its effectiveness through numerical experiments. The results show that the proposed method compares favorably to existing restarting heuristics and yields significant improvements in some cases.

The paper is well-written, and the mathematical arguments are clear and easy to follow. The empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating the potential impact of the proposed method on a wide range of applications.
