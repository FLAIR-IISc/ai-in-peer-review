The paper builds on the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to formulate and evaluate a natural heuristic that adaptively reweights the algorithm’s iterates to accelerate convergence. In contrast to prior adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging. The experiments indicate speed-ups across a broad class of functions. I enjoyed reading this paper. The mathematical development is clear and straightforward to follow, particularly for readers familiar with the work of Krichene et al. At the same time, the empirical results exhibit very promising accelerations. Although the technical contribution is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe the paper could have substantial impact, since Nesterov’s acceleration is ubiquitous in applications. All too often, even in empirical studies, a new, possibly highly adaptive method is compared against Nesterov’s acceleration with a fixed weight sequence. However, as this paper demonstrates, making Nesterov’s method adaptive requires only a minor modification and can deliver dramatic improvements. To strengthen the paper technically, I recommend including an analysis of the discretized process establishing that the convergence rate is preserved; if I am not mistaken, this should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in potential connections between adaptive averaging in Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
