- Introduces a generalized continuous-time accelerated mirror descent framework (AMD_w,η) governed by gradient accumulation η and averaging w.  
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on η and w for guaranteed convergence rates.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function.  
- Proves the heuristic preserves the O(1/t^2) convergence rate in the non-strongly convex setting, unlike existing restarting heuristics.  
- Demonstrates method effectiveness via numerical experiments on low-dimensional, simplex-constrained problems.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation and elegant Lyapunov-based analysis providing rigorous guarantees.  
- Strength: the adaptive averaging heuristic is novel and offers a new perspective versus restarting.  
- Strength: framework is general and recovers/extends previous continuous-time accelerated dynamics (e.g., accelerated replicator dynamics).  
- Weakness: paper lacks intuition and clarity around the core dynamics and the Lyapunov function’s geometric/physical interpretation.  
- Weakness: brief and insufficient comparison with adaptive learning-rate methods (Adagrad/Adam); misses conceptual distinctions and potential complementarities.  
- Weakness: experimental validation is limited to R^3 and does not show scalability to high-dimensional, real-world machine-learning problems.  
- Minor: Figure 1 caption and line styles are ambiguous and need clearer distinction between trajectories.  
- Overall: high-quality, theoretically significant work but needs deeper experiments and broader contextualization of adaptive methods.
