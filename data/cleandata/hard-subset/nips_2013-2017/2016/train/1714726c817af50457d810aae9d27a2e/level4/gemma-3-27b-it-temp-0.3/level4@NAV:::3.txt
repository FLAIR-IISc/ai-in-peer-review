This paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, examining both continuous and discrete formulations. Building upon the work of Krichene et al. [7], which established a connection between Nesterov’s method and the discretization of an ODE system involving coupled primal and dual trajectories – with the primal trajectory derived via averaging of the mirror of the dual variable – this work generalizes the analysis to encompass a broader class of averaging schemes. Theorem 2 provides convergence rate results under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. Notably, the authors demonstrate convergence rates exceeding 1/t<sup>2</sup> in the continuous setting. An adaptive weighting scheme is proposed, accompanied by convergence guarantees, and its performance is evaluated experimentally, comparing favorably to established restarting techniques commonly used to enhance the convergence of accelerated methods. While the theoretical analysis focuses on the continuous case, the paper’s contributions are significant. The rigorous analysis of these novel averaging schemes represents a valuable addition to the field, extending established techniques for analyzing continuous dynamics – work related to that of Attouch, Peypouquet, and collaborators should be acknowledged. The key result demonstrating averaging schemes achieving faster than 1/t<sup>2</sup> convergence is a relevant contribution, and future work exploring the preservation of this improved rate in the discrete setting would be of considerable interest. Overall, this is a well-written and interesting paper.
