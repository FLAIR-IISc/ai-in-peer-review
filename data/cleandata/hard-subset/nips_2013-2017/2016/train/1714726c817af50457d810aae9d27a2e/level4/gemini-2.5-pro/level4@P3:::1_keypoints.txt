- Generalizes Krichene et al.'s ODE-based view of Nesterov acceleration to a broader Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) family parameterized by η(t) and w(t).  
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis giving simple sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) to guarantee O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that chooses weights to maximize Lyapunov decrease, preserving theoretical rates while often improving empirical speed.  
- Gives continuous-time guarantees for the adaptive heuristic and proposes a discrete-time algorithm claimed to preserve quadratic convergence.  
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive heuristic often outperforms or matches popular restarting schemes across various convex problems and provide insightful visualizations.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant, impactful problem by offering a simple, implementable adaptivity that could become a standard enhancement for accelerated methods.  
- Strength: solid, elegant technical development with a principled Lyapunov argument and formal guarantee that rates are preserved.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and logically organized, easing understanding from continuous dynamics to discretization and heuristics.  
- Strength: empirical results are convincing and clearly presented.  
- Weakness: technical framework is an incremental extension of prior work (Krichene et al. 2015), limiting originality of the theoretical formulation.  
- Suggestion: include the main theorem statement and a high-level proof sketch for the discretized Algorithm 1 in the main text to make the paper more self-contained.  
- Suggestion: discuss possible connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics and whether the adaptive weights relate to following the CG path or interpret restart conditions in that context.
