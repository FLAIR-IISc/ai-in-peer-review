- Builds on Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov to propose a novel heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates to accelerate convergence.  
- Method is theoretically guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics.  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical arguments are clear and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Paper is compelling with potential significant impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Shows that a small modification to make Nesterov adaptive yields substantial practical improvements and should affect empirical baselines.  
- Suggests adding an analysis of the discretized process to demonstrate maintained convergence rate (likely straightforward).  
- Recommends exploring connections between this adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems.
