- Studies accelerated first-order constrained convex optimization via continuous-time dynamics, extending Krichene et al. to broader averaging schemes.  
- Introduces Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by averaging weight w(t) and gradient accumulation rate η(t).  
- Theorem 2: Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on w and η to guarantee O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Key theoretical finding: continuous-time averaging schemes can yield convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2).  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function while preserving theoretical convergence rates.  
- Empirical results show the adaptive scheme is competitive with or superior to restarting heuristics and demonstrates stable, monotonic Lyapunov decrease.  
- Provides a concrete accelerated replicator dynamics example illustrating the framework's applicability.  
- Strength: rigorous, unified Lyapunov analysis offering clear insights into averaging and rate functions.  
- Strength: novel theoretical contribution demonstrating faster-than-O(1/t^2) rates in continuous time.  
- Strength: practical heuristic with stronger theoretical guarantees than existing restarting methods.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation that aids understanding.  
- Weakness: gap between continuous-time results and discrete implementations; discrete analysis appears limited to O(1/k^2).  
- Weakness: needs clearer positioning relative to prior work on inertial dynamics and Hessian-driven damping (Attouch, Peypouquet, etc.).  
- Suggestion: discuss prospects and techniques for discretizations that could preserve faster continuous-time rates (e.g., achieving O(1/k^p) for p>2).
