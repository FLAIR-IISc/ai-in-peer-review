- Extends accelerated Mirror Descent ODEs with generalized averaging and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization.
- Adaptive averaging shows empirical improvements over AMD with restarting in some scenarios and is intuitively appealing.
- Main contribution lacks originality, following closely from prior NIPS work [7], with limited benefit from the generalized averaging.
- Focus on quadratic convergence rates restricts impact; faster rates would require higher-order methods not broadly applicable.
- Interpretations (energy function, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but are not a substantial field advance.
- Adaptive averaging is motivated by ODE convergence conditions but loses rigor due to discretization approximations.
- Paper provides no explicit convergence guarantees and remains largely heuristic.
- Benefit over numerous existing averaging schemes for Nesterov methods is unclear without comparative analysis.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to toy quadratic/linear problems and insufficient to demonstrate advantages for general strongly convex settings.
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a theoretical convergence proof, implementation in Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and comparative discussion; reviewer adjusted scores accordingly.
