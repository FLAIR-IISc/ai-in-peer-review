- Adapts the ODE interpretation of Nesterov (Krichene et al. 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates to speed convergence.
- The proposed method is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical results show promising speed-ups on a broad class of functions.
- The mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, particularly given familiarity with Krichene et al.
- Technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al.
- Recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Suggest exploring connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- The paper is technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments.
- The paper is clearly written and organized, enabling reproduction by expert readers.
- The idea is novel, simple to implement, and has potentially high impact due to widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.
