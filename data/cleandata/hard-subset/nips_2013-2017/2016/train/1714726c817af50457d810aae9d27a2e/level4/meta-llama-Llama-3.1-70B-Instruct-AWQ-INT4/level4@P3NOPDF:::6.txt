This paper presents an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization. The proposed method offers a significant advantage over existing restarting heuristics, as it preserves the original convergence rate when the objective function is not strongly convex. This claim is supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results.

However, I found the content of the paper challenging to follow, likely due to my limited background in this area of expertise. I assume that the authors are writing for an audience familiar with the used abbreviations and terms. Nevertheless, including some background information would make the paper more accessible to a broader readership.

In particular, the equations on lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from more intuitive explanations. This would help readers without extensive knowledge of convex optimization to better understand the underlying concepts.

Furthermore, I would appreciate a discussion on the relation between the proposed adaptive averaging method and similar methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. How does the proposed method differ from these approaches, and what are the implications of these differences?

Additionally, I am curious about the practical significance of this work. The experiments were conducted in R^3, which appears to be a simplified setup. It would be valuable to know how the proposed method (and related work) scales to much higher dimensions, where many real-world problems reside.

On a minor note, the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is somewhat ambiguous. Consider using a dotted line or different colors to illustrate the trajectory of the primal variable more clearly.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting contribution to the field of convex optimization. With some revisions to improve clarity and accessibility, it has the potential to reach a wider audience.
