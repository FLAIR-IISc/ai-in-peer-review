This work investigates the convergence properties of a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) solved via a generalized averaging scheme. The authors establish sufficient conditions on the learning rate and weighting parameters to guarantee a specific convergence rate, substantiated by a Lyapunov stability analysis. Furthermore, an adaptive averaging heuristic is introduced to accelerate the decay of the Lyapunov function. The paper supports these theoretical findings with numerical experiments. While a significant body of research focuses on empirically accelerating convergence in optimization methods, this paper presents a key insight – demonstrated through the lens of ODEs – that adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can effectively enhance convergence speed. I found this approach to be a novel and compelling contribution.
