- Core contribution: analyzes generalized averaging schemes for accelerated constrained convex optimization, linking continuous-time ODE analyses to averaging coefficients with convergence guarantees (Theorem 2).  
- Novel result: shows existence of averaging schemes achieving faster-than-1/t² convergence in continuous time and preserves quadratic rate in discrete-time under specific conditions.  
- Adaptive method: introduces an adaptive weighting heuristic with convergence guarantees and empirical validation against restarting techniques.  
- Theoretical rigor: the mathematical analysis is sound and well-justified.  
- Practical relevance: adaptive averaging heuristic shows promising improvements in experiments.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-organized, and accessible.  
- Literature connection: effectively builds on prior work linking Nesterov’s method to primal-dual ODEs.  
- Limitation — discrete analysis: main theoretical results focus on continuous dynamics; detailed discrete convergence analysis for the adaptive algorithm is missing.  
- Limitation — related work: fails to sufficiently acknowledge recent related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and collaborators on accelerated dynamics.  
- Limitation — experiments: empirical validation could be strengthened by broader objective functions and larger problem sizes.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded contributions with significant results on faster convergence and a promising adaptive heuristic.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of comprehensive discrete-theory limits practical impact and incomplete related-work discussion weakens context.  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance, with suggested follow-ups to address discrete analysis and related-work gaps.
