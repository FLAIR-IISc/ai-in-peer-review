

Building upon prior work linking accelerated Mirror Descent to continuous-time ODEs, the authors extend the ODE dynamics via a more general averaging scheme and propose a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one discretization variant. In certain cases, this adaptive averaging variant of AMD outperforms existing restarted AMD algorithms. The proposed adaptive averaging strategy is intriguingly novel and well-motivated. However, the paper's originality and significance remain significant concerns.  
- Most elements (e.g., Lyapunov function, discretization) directly derive from an earlier NIPS paper [7]. The extension from simple to generalized averaging is relatively trivial and lacks substantial utility, as the authors ultimately restrict analysis to quadratic rates; faster rates would necessitate higher-order methods, which are not universally applicable.  
- Some results (e.g., energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) enhance understanding of accelerated Mirror Descent but do not meaningfully advance machine learning.  
- Although adaptive averaging aims to satisfy ODE convergence conditions, it lacks full theoretical grounding due to discretization approximations. Crucially, no explicit convergence guarantee is provided, rendering it effectively heuristic—as acknowledged by the authors. Furthermore, without comparison to alternative adaptive averaging schemes for Nesterov-type methods, the distinct advantage of this variant is unclear.  
- The experimental validation on a toy example with only quadratic/linear objectives is unconvincing and fails to demonstrate adaptive averaging's benefits for the general strongly convex case.  

== Post-rebuttal update ==  
Having reviewed the authors' rebuttal, I note their commitment to adding:  
- Additional higher-dimensional and non-quadratic examples in the revision  
- A theoretical proof of convergence for adaptive averaging  
- Implementation of adaptive averaging on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method  
- Comparative discussion against other adaptive averaging schemes  
I have accordingly updated my scores.
