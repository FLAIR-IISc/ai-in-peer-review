- Builds on the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration (Krichene et al. NIPS 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.  
- Method guarantees performance comparable to or better than standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation shows speed-ups across a range of functions.  
- Well-written, enjoyable, and accessible presentation; mathematical argument is clear and easy to follow.  
- Technical development is a natural extension of prior work but potentially high-impact given Nesterov’s widespread use.  
- Demonstrates that a modest modification to Nesterov’s method can yield substantial improvements over common fixed-weight practices.  
- Recommendation to include analysis of the discretized process to show preservation of convergence rate.  
- Suggested exploration of connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sound technical contribution grounded in strong theory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear and accessible presentation of complex ideas.  
- Arguments for acceptance: promising empirical results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: potential to influence common practice due to simple, effective improvement.  
- Arguments against acceptance: considered an incremental advance over the authors’ previous NIPS work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical analysis of the discretized algorithm; current focus is on continuous-time dynamics.
