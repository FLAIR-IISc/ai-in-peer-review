

This paper builds on Krichene et al.'s NIPS 2015 ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration to propose a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting algorithm iterates, accelerating convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging approaches, the method provably dominates standard fixed-schedule averaging. Empirical results demonstrate significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions. The paper is well-written and mathematically accessible, particularly for readers familiar with Krichene et al.'s work. The core argument is clear and compelling, and the empirical validation is strong. While the technical development extends Krichene et al., the work holds substantial potential impact: Nesterov's acceleration is ubiquitous in practice, yet many empirical comparisons still benchmark new adaptive methods against Nesterov with fixed weights. This paper convincingly shows that adapting Nesterov's method requires only a minor adjustment and yields dramatic performance gains.  

To strengthen the technical contribution, the authors should provide a discrete-time analysis proving that the adaptive reweighting preserves the convergence rate. This analysis should be feasible given the ODE foundation and would solidify the theoretical claims. Additionally, exploring potential connections between this adaptive averaging scheme and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratics would be valuable. The CG method shares similarities with adaptive Nesterov acceleration in its iterative structure and convergence properties, and such a connection could deepen the paper's theoretical context.  

Overall, this is a solid contribution that addresses a practical gap in applying Nesterov's acceleration. The adaptive heuristic is simple yet effective, and its empirical benefits are clear. The paper makes a meaningful step toward making Nesterov's acceleration more practically useful without sacrificing theoretical guarantees. The minor suggestions above would further enhance its technical rigor and impact.
