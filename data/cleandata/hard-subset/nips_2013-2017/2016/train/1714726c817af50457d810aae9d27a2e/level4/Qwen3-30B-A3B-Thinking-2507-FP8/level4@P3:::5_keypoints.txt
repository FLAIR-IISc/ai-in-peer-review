- Introduces a novel continuous-time framework for constrained convex optimization: generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η.

- Interprets AMD as a coupling of dual and primal variables and studies it via a Lyapunov argument.

- Derives sufficient conditions for the Lyapunov function to decrease (Theorem 2) and characterizes weight functions achieving desired convergence.

- Emphasizes achieving the quadratic convergence rate 1/t² and provides concrete examples (Corollary 1).

- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.

- Proves the adaptive heuristic preserves the 1/t² convergence rate (rigorous theoretical analysis).

- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics (mirror descent on the simplex) through a simple averaging scheme.

- Carefully distinguishes continuous-time analysis from discrete-time implementation; discrete Algorithm 1 is provided.

- Numerical experiments compare adaptive averaging with existing heuristics and show consistent performance across multiple problem types.

- Clarity is generally good with consistent notation, but Section 3’s primal representation needs more intuitive/geometric explanation.

- Originality is strong: unifies generalized averaging schemes and introduces a novel adaptive averaging approach distinct from restart heuristics.

- Significance is substantial: advances adaptive acceleration methods with theoretical guarantees and connections to replicator dynamics, relevant to ML practitioners/researchers.

- Main weaknesses: limited discussion of practical real-world ML applications and uses only the replicator example for application context.

- Main weaknesses: discrete-time implementation lacks detailed comparison to alternative discretizations (supplement addresses some of this).

- Main weaknesses: numerical experiments could include a broader variety of problem types to further validate generality.

- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-supported, and novel contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
