- Leverages ODE interpretation of Nesterov (Krichene et al.) to design an adaptive reweighting of iterates that accelerates convergence.  
- Proposed method is guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive heuristics.  
- Empirical results show significant speed-ups across a broad class of functions.  
- Paper is well-written and mathematically straightforward, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Technical development is a close extension of prior work but has high potential impact due to wide use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Demonstrates that a minor adaptive modification to Nesterov’s method yields dramatic empirical improvements over fixed weights.  
- Recommendation: include analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved, improving technical rigor.  
- Recommendation: explore connections between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems for additional insight.  
- Overall: compelling approach with strong theoretical foundation and promising empirical performance; with additional analysis and connections, it could be highly impactful.
