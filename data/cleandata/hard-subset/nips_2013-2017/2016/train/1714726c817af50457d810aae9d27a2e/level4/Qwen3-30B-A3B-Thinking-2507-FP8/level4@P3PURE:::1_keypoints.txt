- Builds on Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration to propose an adaptive reweighting heuristic.  
- Method adaptively reweights iterates to accelerate convergence.  
- Unlike prior heuristics, the method guarantees dominance over standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups across a broad class of functions.  
- Mathematical exposition is clear and accessible, especially for readers familiar with Krichene et al.  
- Empirical findings are promising and demonstrate practical value.  
- Technical development is a straightforward extension of Krichene et al.'s work.  
- The paper has substantial potential impact given the widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Addresses a common shortcoming by comparing adaptive methods to Nesterov with fixed weights and showing minor modifications yield large improvements.  
- Recommendation: provide analysis of the discretized process to verify the convergence rate is preserved.  
- Recommendation: explore connections between adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
