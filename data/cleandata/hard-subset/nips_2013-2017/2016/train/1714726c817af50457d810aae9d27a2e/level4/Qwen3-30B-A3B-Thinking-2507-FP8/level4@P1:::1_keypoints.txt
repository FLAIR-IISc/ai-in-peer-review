- Extends Krichene et al.'s continuous-time Nesterov perspective to propose an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated mirror descent, parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Provides sufficient conditions for achieving desired convergence rates via Lyapunov analysis of generalized accelerated mirror descent dynamics.
- Introduces a dynamic adaptive averaging strategy that computes weights to accelerate convergence while preserving the quadratic convergence rate.
- Shows how the adaptive heuristic can be implemented in discrete time and empirically observes the Lyapunov function remains decreasing.
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates consistent improvements over existing heuristics, including adaptive restarting, across simplex problems (strongly/weakly convex and linear objectives).
- Theoretical justification fills a gap where many empirical comparisons use fixed-weight Nesterov methods, showing adaptive weights outperform while maintaining guarantees.
- Connects the method to replicator dynamics on the simplex, providing a concrete application in evolutionary game theory and mirror descent.
- Technical development is elegant and well-structured, offering a unified Lyapunov framework for the generalized averaging scheme.
- Minor weakness: discrete-time convergence guarantees are not fully developed in the main paper (left to the supplement); more detailed discretized analysis would strengthen the contribution.
- Suggestion/interest: investigate extending the adaptive averaging principle to higher-order accelerated methods (e.g., Wibisono et al.).
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded and practically effective contribution with substantial potential impact, suitable for NIPS.
