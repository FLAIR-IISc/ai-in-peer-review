- Studies continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization with a generalized averaging scheme.
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based framework for accelerated mirror descent, extending ODE connections from prior work.
- Establishes sufficient conditions on weight functions η(t) and w(t) for desired convergence rates (Theorem 2) with practical construction (Corollary 1).
- Shows adaptive averaging of trajectory weights can improve convergence rates while preserving theoretical guarantees.
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic (Section 4) that accelerates Lyapunov decrease and preserves quadratic convergence.
- Connects simplex-constrained dynamics to replicator dynamics, linking results to evolutionary game theory (Section 3).
- Numerical experiments (Section 5) show adaptive averaging consistently matches or outperforms restarting heuristics, especially in strongly convex cases.
- Paper is theoretically rigorous, filling a gap on adaptive accelerated methods that prior heuristics lacked guarantees for.
- Exposition is clear and well-structured; primal derivation and Algorithm 1 are accessible and well-motivated.
- Originality: provides a rigorous continuous-time generalization of acceleration-by-averaging and presents a novel adaptive heuristic.
- Significance: offers theoretical and empirical advances that can inform future algorithm design and applications.
- Weakness: connection between continuous-time dynamics and discrete implementations for the adaptive case is underdeveloped.
- Weakness: some technical details (e.g., choices of Bregman divergence) need clearer explanation.
- Weakness: paper could better explain mechanistically why adaptive averaging outperforms restarting beyond empirical evidence.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen discrete-time connection and deepen discussion of the adaptive mechanism.
