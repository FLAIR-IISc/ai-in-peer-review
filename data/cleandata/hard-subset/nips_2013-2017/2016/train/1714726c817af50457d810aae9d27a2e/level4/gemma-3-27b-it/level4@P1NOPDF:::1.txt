The paper builds upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration, as presented by Krichene et al. at NIPS 2015, to design and evaluate a novel heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates and accelerating convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is theoretically guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the approach yields speed-ups across a broad class of functions. I found this paper to be a compelling read. The mathematical arguments are clear and accessible, particularly for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al., and the empirical results show very promising speed-ups. 

While the technical development is a natural extension of Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for significant impact, given the widespread use of Nesterov’s acceleration. It’s common – even in empirical evaluations – to compare new, often highly adaptive methods against Nesterov’s acceleration using a fixed weighting scheme. However, as this paper convincingly demonstrates, making Nesterov’s method adaptive requires only a small modification and can lead to substantial improvements. 

To further strengthen the paper technically, I would recommend including an analysis of the discretized process, demonstrating that the convergence rate is maintained. If I understand correctly, this analysis should be relatively straightforward. I would also be interested in exploring potential connections between this adaptive averaging of Nesterov’s method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization.
