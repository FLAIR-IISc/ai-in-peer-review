- Paper extends AMD to a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging discretization.  
- Major concern about originality and significance; contributions appear incremental.  
- Core techniques (Lyapunov functions, discretization, convergence analysis) largely derive from prior NIPS work [7].  
- Extension to generalized averaging seems trivial.  
- Analysis is restricted to the quadratic convergence-rate case, limiting practical relevance.  
- Faster rates generally require higher-order methods (e.g., cubic regularization) not addressed here.  
- Energy interpretation, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics improve understanding but lack concrete ML impact.  
- Adaptive averaging is not theoretically grounded; discretization introduces approximations and no explicit convergence proof is provided.  
- No empirical or theoretical comparison to existing adaptive averaging methods for Nesterov-type schemes.  
- Experiments are weak, using only quadratic or linear toy problems, so empirical claims are unconvincing for general strongly convex settings.  
- Authors promise to add higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a formal convergence proof, implement on cubic-regularized Newton, and include comparisons to alternatives.  
- Decision: Major Revision pending incorporation of promised revisions; current version is incremental and lacks rigorous justification.
