- Builds on prior work linking accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) to continuous-time ODEs.
- Generalizes the ODE dynamics with a broader averaging scheme.
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging strategy for discretization.
- Adaptive AMD variant outperforms existing AMD with restarting in some cases.
- Adaptive averaging is conceptually interesting.
- Overall originality and practical impact are questionable.
- Many core components (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from prior NIPS work [7].
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging feels incremental with limited benefit.
- Paper focuses only on quadratic convergence rates, not faster rates.
- Achieving faster convergence would require higher-order methods, which are not universally applicable.
- Contributions like energy-function interpretation, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics improve understanding but are not a major advance for ML.
- Theoretical foundation for adaptive averaging is incomplete due to discretization approximations.
- Paper lacks explicit convergence guarantees, making the method heuristic as acknowledged by the authors.
- Value of the adaptive variant is unclear without comparisons to other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style methods.
- Experiments are limited to a toy example with quadratic/linear objectives and are insufficient for general strongly convex problems.
- Post-rebuttal: authors promised additional higher-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a theoretical convergence proof, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other adaptive averaging methods, prompting score revision.
