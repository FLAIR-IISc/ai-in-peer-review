- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings.  
- Builds on Krichene et al.: primal trajectory obtained via averaging the mirror of the dual variable, and investigates more general averaging schemes.  
- Theorem 2 provides convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, authors obtain convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees and uses it in experiments.  
- Compares the adaptive weighting to restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods in practice.  
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is missing.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and provides a rigorous analysis for continuous dynamics.  
- Suggests adding citations to related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Raises the open question whether the improved continuous-rate persists for the discrete procedure.
