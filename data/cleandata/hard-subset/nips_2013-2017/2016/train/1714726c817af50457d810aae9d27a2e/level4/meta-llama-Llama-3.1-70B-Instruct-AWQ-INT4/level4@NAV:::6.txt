This paper introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate constrained convex optimization, boasting the preservation of the original convergence rate for non-strongly convex objective functions. This represents an improvement over existing restarting heuristics, as demonstrated through both theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, I found the content challenging to follow due to its specialization, and I assume the targeted audience is familiar with the employed abbreviations and terminology. To enhance accessibility, I recommend incorporating background information for readers less familiar with the subject matter. A more intuitive explanation of the equations on lines 33-34 and 46-47 would also be beneficial. Additionally, it would be valuable to explore the relationship between the proposed adaptive averaging method and its counterparts in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] and Adam [2]. Furthermore, I am curious about the practical implications of this work, particularly given the experiments' limitation to R^3, a seemingly simplistic setup. It would be insightful to discuss how the proposed method and related work perform in higher-dimensional spaces. On a minor note, the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is somewhat ambiguous; using a dotted line or distinct colors to illustrate the primal variable's trajectory could improve clarity.
