- Leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration (Krichene et al., NIPS 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates.
- Proposed adaptive averaging is guaranteed to outperform standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates speedups across a broad class of functions.
- Presentation is enjoyable and the mathematical argument is straightforward and easy to follow.
- Technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., but the result has significant practical impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration.
- Shows that only a small modification is needed to make Nesterov’s method highly adaptive with dramatic improvements in practice.
- Recommendation: include analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Suggestion: investigate connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
