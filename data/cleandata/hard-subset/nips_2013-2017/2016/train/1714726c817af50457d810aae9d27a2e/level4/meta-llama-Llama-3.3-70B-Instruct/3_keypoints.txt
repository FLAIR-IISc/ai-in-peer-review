- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, building on Krichene et al.  
- Interprets Nesterov’s method as an ODE with coupled primal and dual trajectories, where the primal is an average of the mirror of the dual.  
- Introduces generalized averaging schemes for the primal trajectory.  
- Theorem 2 provides convergence rates under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates that improve upon 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive weight selection with provable convergence guarantees and evaluates it experimentally.  
- Compares the adaptive scheme empirically to restarting techniques used to improve accelerated methods.  
- Does not provide theoretical analysis for the discrete algorithm version.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, well-crafted, rigorous continuous-analysis contributions.  
- Suggestion to acknowledge related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Highlights the main theorem showing averaging schemes achieving faster-than-1/t^2 rates as particularly notable.  
- Notes an open question whether the improved continuous-rate persists for the discrete procedure.
