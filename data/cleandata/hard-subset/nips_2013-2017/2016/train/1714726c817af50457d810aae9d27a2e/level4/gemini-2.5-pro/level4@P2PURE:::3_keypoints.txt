- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending Krichene et al.’s ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s method.  
- Extends the framework by analyzing more general averaging schemes for obtaining the primal trajectory from the dual variable.  
- Theorem 2 gives convergence rates for these averaging schemes under conditions on the averaging coefficients.  
- In the continuous setting, shows existence of averaging schemes achieving rates faster than the canonical O(1/t²).  
- Proposes an adaptive weight-selection scheme, provides convergence guarantees, and uses it in experiments.  
- Compares the adaptive scheme with restarting techniques known to improve practical convergence.  
- Paper is interesting, well-written, and presents a rigorous continuous-time analysis with new contributions.  
- Major limitation: no theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version, leaving open whether improved rates survive discretization.  
- Suggestion: cite and discuss related continuous-dynamics work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
