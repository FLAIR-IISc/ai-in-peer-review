- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization, built on continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics.  
- Presents a unified Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics framework parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Provides a Lyapunov-based analysis (Theorem 2) that gives sufficient conditions for achieving specified convergence rates.  
- Main contribution: adaptive averaging preserves quadratic convergence rates without requiring strong convexity, outperforming restarting heuristics.  
- Extends continuous-time analysis of accelerated methods (Su et al. 2014; Krichene et al. 2015) and generalizes prior averaging approaches.  
- Applies framework to accelerating replicator dynamics, showing interdisciplinary applicability.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging consistently outperforms gradient and speed restarting heuristics, especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Demonstrates monotonic decrease of the Lyapunov function under adaptive averaging where restarting methods fail.  
- Clarity issues: paper assumes strong familiarity with continuous-time optimization and mirror descent, making it hard for non-specialists.  
- Several key equations (lines ~33–47) need intuitive/physical explanation; primal representation and transform ∇²ψ*∘∇ψ should be better motivated.  
- Relation to adaptive nonconvex methods (Adagrad, Adam) is underdeveloped; differences in theoretical guarantees and scope are unclear.  
- Experimental validation is limited to R³; scalability to higher dimensions and real-world ML problems is not demonstrated.  
- Suggest adding more large-scale experiments or complexity analysis and a brief background section on mirror maps and continuous-time optimization.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with practical promise; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing clarity and scalability.
