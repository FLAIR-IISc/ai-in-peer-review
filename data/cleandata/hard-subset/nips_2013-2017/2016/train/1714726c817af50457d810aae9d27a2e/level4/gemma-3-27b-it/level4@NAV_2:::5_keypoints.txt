- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via coupling a dual variable and a primal variable formed by weighted averaging of the dual trajectory.  
- Presents a framework using a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on weighting functions that guarantee desired convergence rates.  
- Instantiates the framework with an accelerated version of replicator dynamics as a motivating example.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights to speed the decrease of the Lyapunov function.  
- Provides continuous-time guarantees for the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Proves preservation of quadratic convergence rates for the adaptive averaging heuristic in discrete time.  
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive averaging is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, adaptive restarting strategies.  
- Builds on recent continuous-time analyses of accelerated methods and extends the generalized framework of [11] by exploring alternative averaging schemes.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clear convergence guarantees and dual continuous/discrete treatment.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated adaptive averaging heuristic with promising empirical results.  
- Weakness: computational cost/online overhead of computing adaptive weights not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis primarily limited to the specific rate function r(t) = t^2.  
- Weakness: numerical experiments are limited in scope; broader evaluation across problems/datasets is needed.  
- Weakness: discussion of connections and differences with adaptive methods like Adagrad/Adam is brief and could be expanded.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, extend analysis, broaden experiments, and deepen related-work discussion.
