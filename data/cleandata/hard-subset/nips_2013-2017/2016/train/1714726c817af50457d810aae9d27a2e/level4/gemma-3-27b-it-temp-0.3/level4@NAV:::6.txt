This paper introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate constrained convex optimization problems. A key strength of the proposed method is its theoretical guarantee of maintaining the original convergence rate even when dealing with non-strongly convex objective functions – a clear benefit compared to existing restarting approaches, as demonstrated through both theoretical analysis and experimental results. 

While the paper’s technical content appears sound, I found aspects challenging to fully grasp due to my unfamiliarity with the specific subfield. I presume the notation and terminology are standard within the target audience; however, including some introductory background material would likely broaden accessibility. Specifically, the equations presented between lines 33-34 and 46-47 could be enhanced with more intuitive explanations to aid understanding. 

Furthermore, it would be valuable to discuss the connection between this work and adaptive averaging techniques commonly employed in the non-convex optimization landscape, such as methods like Adagrad [1] and Adam [2]. Finally, I am interested in understanding the practical implications of this research. The current experiments, conducted in R^3, represent a relatively simple scenario. Investigating the scalability of the proposed method – and related work – to higher-dimensional problems would be crucial for assessing its real-world applicability.
