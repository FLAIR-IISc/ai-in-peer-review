This paper introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate constrained convex optimization problems. A key strength of the proposed method is its theoretical guarantee of maintaining the original convergence rate even in non-strongly convex settings, offering an improvement over existing restarting techniques. This advantage is substantiated through both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.

While the paper’s technical contributions appear sound, I found aspects of the presentation challenging to follow due to the specialized nature of the field. While appropriate for the target audience, briefly including foundational background information could broaden accessibility. Specifically, a more intuitive explanation of the equations presented between lines 33-34 and 46-47 would be beneficial. 

Furthermore, it would be valuable to discuss the connection of this work to adaptive averaging techniques employed in the non-convex optimization landscape, such as methods like Adagrad [1] and Adam [2]. Finally, clarifying the practical implications of this work is important. The current experimental evaluation focuses on R^3, which represents a simplified scenario. Investigating and discussing the scalability of the method – and related approaches – to higher-dimensional problems would greatly strengthen the paper.

A minor point: The reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is somewhat ambiguous. Consider using a more visually distinct line style (e.g., dotted) or employing color to clearly depict the trajectory of the primal variable.
