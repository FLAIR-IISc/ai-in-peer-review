- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization and uses a Lyapunov argument to give sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the convergence of the Lyapunov function.
- Provides a unified analysis of a broad family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and technical sections are organized and easy to follow.
- Technical analysis is sound and supported by numerical experiments comparing the method to existing heuristics.
- Main strengths: theoretical contribution to accelerated dynamics and the promising adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Main weakness: assumes substantial background in convex optimization and Lyapunov theory, making technical sections potentially challenging for some readers.
- Suggestion: could benefit from additional numerical experiments to further demonstrate effectiveness.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with high novelty and impact (Quality 8/10, Novelty 9/10, Impact 8/10, Clarity 8/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: important contribution, interesting heuristic, clear exposition, well-organized technical content.
- Arguments against acceptance: steep prerequisite knowledge for readers and limited numerical validation.
