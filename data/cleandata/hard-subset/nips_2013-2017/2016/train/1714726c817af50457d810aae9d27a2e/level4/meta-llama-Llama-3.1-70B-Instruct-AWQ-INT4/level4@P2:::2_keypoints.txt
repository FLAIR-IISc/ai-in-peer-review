- Proposes an extension of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging strategy for one discretization.  
- Builds on prior work connecting AMD to continuous-time ODEs; paper is well-written and provides good intuition for the adaptive scheme.  
- Provides a unified study of the ODE via an appropriate Lyapunov function and gives sufficient conditions for the Lyapunov function to decrease.  
- Gives a primal representation of the ODE and studies examples, including a reduction to replicator dynamics.  
- Designs the adaptive averaging heuristic to satisfy conditions sufficient for ODE convergence.  
- Presents numerical experiments comparing the adaptive averaging heuristic to existing restarting heuristics.  
- Originality concerns: many elements (Lyapunov, discretization) directly follow an earlier NIPS paper.  
- The jump from simple to generalized averaging is limited (only quadratic rate); faster rates would require higher-order methods.  
- Interpretations (energy function, primal form, replicator reduction) improve understanding but are not major advances for ML.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks explicit convergence guarantees.  
- No comparison with other averaging schemes of Nesterov’s methods, leaving practical significance unclear.  
- Experiments are limited (toy quadratic/linear objectives) and not convincing for broader or strongly convex cases.  
- Suggested improvements: provide theoretical convergence of adaptive averaging, test on higher-order methods (e.g., cubic-regularized Newton), compare to other averaging schemes, and expand experiments.
