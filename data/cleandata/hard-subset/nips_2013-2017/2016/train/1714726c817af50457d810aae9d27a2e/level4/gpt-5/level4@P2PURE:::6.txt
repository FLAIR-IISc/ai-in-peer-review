This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate constrained convex optimization. The method guarantees that, when the objective is not strongly convex, it maintains the original convergence rate. This is an advantage over existing restarting heuristics, and the paper supports this claim both theoretically and experimentally.

I found parts of the paper difficult to follow, as it is outside my primary area of expertise. While the abbreviations and terminology may be familiar to the target audience, I recommend adding background material to make the exposition more self-contained. In particular, the derivations around the equations between lines 33–34 and 46–47 would benefit from a more intuitive explanation.

I would also like to see a discussion of how this approach relates to adaptive averaging methods used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] and Adam [2].

Regarding practical significance, the experiments are conducted in R^3, which is essentially a toy setting. How does your method—and closely related work—scale to substantially higher dimensions?

Minor comment: The reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous. Consider using a dotted line or color to indicate the trajectory of the primal variable.
