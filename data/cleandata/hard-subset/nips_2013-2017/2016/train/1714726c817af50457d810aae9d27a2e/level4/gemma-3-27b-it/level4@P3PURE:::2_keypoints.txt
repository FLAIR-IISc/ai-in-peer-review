- Paper extends ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for discretization.
- In some cases the adaptive averaging variant outperforms existing AMD algorithms that use restarting.
- The adaptive averaging idea is conceptually interesting and well-motivated.
- Concerns about overall originality and practical usefulness of the contribution.
- Many core elements (Lyapunov function, discretization approach) directly follow a prior NIPS paper [7].
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging seems incremental and practically limited by focus on quadratic convergence rates.
- Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not universally applicable.
- Results like interpretation of the energy function, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics mainly improve understanding rather than advance ML practice.
- The adaptive averaging is designed to meet ODE convergence conditions, but its theoretical foundation is incomplete due to discretization approximations.
- The paper lacks an explicit convergence proof for the adaptive scheme, making it largely heuristic as acknowledged by the authors.
- No comparisons to numerous existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, so practical improvement is unclear.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and is insufficient to demonstrate advantages on general strongly convex problems.
- Post-rebuttal, authors committed to add higher-dimensional non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof, an implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive averaging techniques; reviewer updated scores.
