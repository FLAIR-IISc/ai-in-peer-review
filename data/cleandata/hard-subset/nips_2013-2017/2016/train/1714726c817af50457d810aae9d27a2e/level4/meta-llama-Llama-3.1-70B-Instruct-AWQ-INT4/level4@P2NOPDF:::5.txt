This paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, employing a Lyapunov function to achieve the desired convergence rate. The authors design an adaptive averaging heuristic to expedite the convergence rate of the Lyapunov function. Experimental results demonstrate that the adaptive averaging method performs comparably to or even surpasses adaptive restarting in terms of convergence rate. Although I am not an expert in this field, the concept of utilizing adaptive averaging to attain a faster convergence rate for constrained convex optimization appears intriguing and promising.

Novelty: While the idea of applying adaptive averaging to accelerate convergence rates is innovative, I am not familiar enough with the field to assess the novelty of this approach accurately.

Impact: The potential impact of this research is substantial, as accelerated convergence rates can significantly enhance the efficiency of constrained convex optimization algorithms. However, a more thorough understanding of the field is required to fully appreciate the implications of this work.
