

The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. Building on the work of Krichene et al. [7], which established Nesterov's method as a suitable discretization of an ODE system coupling primal and dual trajectories (with the primal trajectory obtained via a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable), this work explores more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 derives convergence rates under appropriate conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the authors establish convergence rates faster than $1/t^2$, and an adaptive weight choice with convergence guarantees is proposed and applied in experiments. This adaptive strategy is compared with restarting techniques, which are known to enhance practical convergence of accelerated methods. However, the theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm version is omitted. This is a notable gap, as the primary contribution hinges on the continuous dynamics, and it remains unclear whether the improved rate persists in the discrete setting. Despite this, the paper is interesting and well-written. The analysis of the new averaging scheme in the continuous setting is rigorous, and while the foundational ideas for analyzing continuous dynamics are now well-established in recent literature, the paper presents novel contributions. The main theorem—which demonstrates the existence of averaging schemes achieving rates faster than $1/t^2$—is significant. The work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors should be cited to contextualize these contributions. Overall, the theoretical results are compelling, but the omission of discrete analysis weakens the practical impact of the findings.
