This paper investigates a class of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) that incorporate a generalized averaging scheme. The authors provide sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to achieve a desired convergence rate, leveraging a Lyapunov argument. Furthermore, they employ an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The paper presents both theoretical guarantees and numerical comparisons. This work contributes to a long line of research aimed at empirically speeding up the convergence of accelerated methods. A crucial observation made by the authors, via the lens of ODEs, is that adaptively averaging the weights along solution trajectories can also yield a speedup. I found this new method to be particularly interesting.
