This paper utilizes the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration, as introduced by Krichene et al. in NIPS 2015, to develop and assess an intuitive heuristic approach for adaptively reweighting the algorithm's iterates, thereby accelerating convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method guarantees improved performance over the standard fixed-schedule averaging. The empirical evaluation demonstrates the method's effectiveness in achieving speed-ups for a broad class of functions. The paper is engaging to read, with a straightforward mathematical argument that is easily comprehensible, particularly for those familiar with Krichene et al.'s work. The empirical results also exhibit promising speed-ups. Although the technical development is an extension of Krichene et al.'s work, this paper has significant potential impact due to the widespread application of Nesterov's acceleration. Often, even in empirical evaluations, new adaptive methods are compared to Nesterov's acceleration using a fixed weight sequence. However, this paper confirms that making Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a minor modification, leading to substantial improvements. To further enhance the paper technically, I recommend analyzing the discretized process to show that the convergence rate is preserved. This analysis should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization would be of interest.
