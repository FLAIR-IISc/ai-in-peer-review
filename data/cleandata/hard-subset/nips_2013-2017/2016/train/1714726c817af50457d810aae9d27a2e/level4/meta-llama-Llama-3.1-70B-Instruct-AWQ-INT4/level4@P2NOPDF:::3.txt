The paper explores accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. Building upon the work initiated by Krichene et al. in [7], the authors demonstrate that Nesterov's method can be viewed as a suitable discretization of an ODE system, which couples a primal trajectory with a dual one. Specifically, the primal trajectory is obtained through a particular averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. This paper delves into more general averaging schemes, deriving convergence rates in Theorem 2 under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.

In the continuous setting, the authors achieve convergence rates that are faster than O(1/t^2). Furthermore, they investigate an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees, which is subsequently employed in the experimental section. This adaptive choice is compared to restarting techniques, known to enhance the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. Notably, the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not included in the paper.

This is an interesting and well-written paper, presenting a rigorous analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods in the continuous setting. Although the main ideas underlying the analysis of continuous dynamics have been established in a series of papers, the authors contribute new insights and results. In this context, the work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors deserves mention. The main theorem, demonstrating the existence of averaging schemes with faster convergence rates than O(1/t^2), is a significant contribution. However, it would be intriguing to investigate whether this improved rate is preserved when transitioning to the discrete procedure.
