- Establishes sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and averaging weights for convergence of a class of ODEs using a Lyapunov stability argument.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate decay of the Lyapunov function.  
- Theoretical results are well-presented and the Lyapunov analysis appears sound.  
- Clearly connects the ODE framework to practical optimization algorithms.  
- Adaptive averaging offers a novel and compelling perspective akin to accelerated methods.  
- Convergence conditions are clear and seem more practical than many existing restrictive assumptions.  
- Numerical experiments support the theory and show improvement over standard methods.  
- Lacks a sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters in the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Missing discussion/analysis of the computational cost and whether overhead is justified by speedup.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with promising contributions; recommendation to accept.
