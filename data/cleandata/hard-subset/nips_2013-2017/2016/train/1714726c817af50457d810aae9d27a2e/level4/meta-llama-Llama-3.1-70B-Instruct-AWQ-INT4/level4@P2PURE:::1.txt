This paper leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) to design and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the iterates of the algorithm, thereby accelerating convergence. Unlike previous adaptive averaging heuristics, this method is guaranteed to outperform the standard fixed-schedule averaging approach. The empirical evaluation demonstrates the method's effectiveness in achieving speed-ups on a general class of functions.

I found the paper to be an enjoyable read, with a straightforward mathematical argument that is easy to follow, particularly for those familiar with the work of Krichene et al. The empirical results are also promising, showcasing significant speed-ups. Although the technical development is a close extension of Krichene et al., I believe this paper has the potential for substantial impact, given the widespread application of Nesterov's acceleration. In many empirical evaluations, new methods are often compared to Nesterov's acceleration using a fixed weight sequence, but as this paper confirms, making Nesterov's method adaptive requires only a minor modification, leading to dramatic improvements.

To further enhance the paper from a technical perspective, I recommend analyzing the discretized process to demonstrate that the rate of convergence is preserved. This analysis appears to be relatively straightforward. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization could provide valuable insights.
