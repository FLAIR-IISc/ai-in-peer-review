- Paper studies accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization and introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic with Lyapunov-based convergence analysis.  
- Empirical results suggest adaptive averaging matches or outperforms adaptive restarting in practice.  
- Strength: Addresses practically relevant constrained convex problems.  
- Strength: Uses a principled Lyapunov framework to justify convergence rates.  
- Strength: Proposes a simple, seemingly effective adaptive averaging mechanism.  
- Strength: Comparison to adaptive restarting is appropriate and informative.  
- Weakness: Precise assumptions (smoothness, Lipschitz, strong vs. general convexity, constraint set properties, projection/prox costs) are not clearly stated.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether results extend to composite problems with nonsmooth regularizers and proximal steps.  
- Weakness: Exact convergence rates and constants (e.g., O(1/k^2) or linear under strong convexity) are not explicitly given or compared to known accelerated methods.  
- Weakness: Relationship between Lyapunov decrease and practical metrics (objective gap, gradient mapping norm, feasibility) is not clarified.  
- Weakness: Insufficient intuition/theory on how adaptive averaging affects Lyapunov decrease—unclear if it yields provable improvements or only empirical gains.  
- Weakness: Positioning relative to existing averaging schemes and adaptive restart criteria is incomplete; novelty needs clearer differentiation.  
- Weakness: Lacks clear algorithm pseudocode specifying step sizes, momentum terms, projection/prox ops, and full adaptive averaging rule.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters, computational overhead versus restart, and per-iteration cost/memory impact are not analyzed.  
- Weakness: Robustness to inexact projections, noisy gradients, expensive constraints, and scalability to high dimensions is not addressed.  
- Weakness: Experimental details (problem types, dimensions, stopping criteria, metrics), baselines, variance across runs, and ablation studies are insufficient.  
- Quality: Lyapunov framing seems sound but paper should state assumptions, provide full proofs or clear proof outlines, and give explicit constants and tightness discussion.  
- Novelty: Potentially novel combination of acceleration, constraints, and adaptive averaging, but needs clearer contrast with prior accelerated/proximal methods, restart schemes, and averaging techniques.  
- Impact: Could be practically useful if consistent improvements hold across problems with low tuning and overhead; Lyapunov analysis may guide future adaptive designs.  
- Clarity: Paper is mostly understandable but needs better intuition for Lyapunov construction, a concise assumptions box, consistent notation, pseudocode, and clearer plots (objective gap, feasibility, Lyapunov).  
- Suggestions: Add detailed algorithm, state assumptions, provide theoretical insight into the adaptive rule, expand experiments (ablation/sensitivity), discuss composite/alternative norms, and consider releasing code.  
- Overall: Promising contribution that requires clearer assumptions, stronger positioning relative to prior work, more complete theory for the adaptive rule, and substantially expanded empirical evidence.
