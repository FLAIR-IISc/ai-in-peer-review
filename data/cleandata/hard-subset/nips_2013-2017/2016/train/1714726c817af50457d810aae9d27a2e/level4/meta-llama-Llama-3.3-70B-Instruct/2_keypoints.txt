- Introduces generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging discretization for accelerated Mirror Descent; adaptive AMD can outperform existing algorithms with restarting in some scenarios and is intuitively innovative.  
- Originality concerns: key components (Lyapunov function, discretization) are directly derived from prior NIPS work [7], making the generalization feel trivial.  
- Limited scope: only quadratic rates are considered; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods that may not apply.  
- Limited practical impact: reinterpretation of the energy function and primal reformulation deepen understanding but do not significantly advance machine learning.  
- The adaptive averaging scheme lacks full theoretical grounding—discretization approximations mean no explicit convergence guarantees are provided, rendering it heuristic.  
- Missing empirical comparisons with other averaging schemes for Nesterov-type algorithms, so the adaptive variant’s advantage is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to simple quadratic/linear toy problems and are not convincing for strongly convex cases.  
- Reviewer softened their stance after the rebuttal promising more examples, a theoretical convergence proof, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons with other adaptive averaging strategies.
