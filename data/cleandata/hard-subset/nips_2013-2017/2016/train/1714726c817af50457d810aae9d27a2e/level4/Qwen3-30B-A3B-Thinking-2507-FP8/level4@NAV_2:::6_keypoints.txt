- Paper proposes a unified Lyapunov-based framework for accelerated mirror descent with generalized averaging, giving sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.
- Builds on ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and frames acceleration as dual-primal coupling with averaging.
- Main contributions: generalized averaging scheme, application to replicator dynamics, and an adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear proofs for continuous- and discrete-time Lyapunov decrease conditions.
- Adaptive averaging heuristic provably preserves quadratic convergence rate for non-strongly convex functions.
- Empirically, the adaptive heuristic outperforms existing restarting heuristics, especially in the strongly convex case.
- Novelty: generalization of averaging in accelerated mirror descent and a distinct adaptive averaging strategy vs. restarting.
- Weak clarity: technical derivations (Sections 2–3) need more intuitive explanations for readers unfamiliar with the framework.
- Weak clarity: primal representation and adaptive averaging description (Sections 3–4) would benefit from clearer, intuitive exposition.
- Missing comparison: connection to adaptive optimizers like Adagrad/Adam is not explicitly addressed.
- Experimental limitation: numerical tests only in R^3, so scalability to high-dimensional ML problems is not demonstrated.
- Acceptance pros: solid theoretical guarantees, novel adaptive heuristic, and consistent experimental improvement.
- Acceptance cons: limited scalability experiments, some technical sections lack accessibility, and practical significance on large-scale tasks is unproven.
