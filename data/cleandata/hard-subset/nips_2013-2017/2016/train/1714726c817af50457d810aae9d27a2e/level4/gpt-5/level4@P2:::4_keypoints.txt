- Paper studies accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with generalized averaging for constrained convex optimization, coupling a dual gradient-accumulating trajectory Z and a weighted averaged primal X.  
- Introduces Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*) and gives sufficient conditions on η and w to ensure f(X(t))−f*=O(1/r(t)).  
- Provides a primal-only formulation, instantiations on the positive orthant and simplex, and an accelerated replicator dynamics example.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights to decrease the Lyapunov derivative while preserving nominal rates.  
- Presents a discrete-time scheme claimed to maintain the quadratic convergence rate of first-order accelerated methods.  
- Experiments compare adaptive averaging to adaptive restarting heuristics and show competitive or improved performance on low-dimensional tasks.  
- Connects to ODE views of acceleration (Su-Boyd-Candès; Wibisono-Wilson-Jordan) and classical mirror descent, generalizing averaging interpretations (e.g., Krichene et al.).  
- Clarifies conceptual differences with adaptive gradient methods (AdaGrad, Adam).  
- Strength: clean, general Lyapunov analysis with transparent sufficient conditions (Theorem 2) and careful existence/uniqueness statements.  
- Strength: novel adaptive averaging heuristic grounded in Lyapunov descent and an appealing accelerated replicator dynamics example.  
- Strength: unifying continuous-time framework with a practical discrete-time algorithm and potential to inspire new adaptive acceleration designs.  
- Clarity is good at high level and examples on orthant/simplex are instructive.  
- Weakness: Condition (2) of Theorem 2 depends on the unknown trajectory, making a priori verification difficult; Corollary 1 partially remedies this.  
- Weakness/typo: In Example 1, inconsistency in choice of a(t) for r(t)=t^2 (a(t)=β t vs a(t)=β/t) needs correction and standardization.  
- Weakness: Discrete-time guarantees are relegated to the supplement; a concise main-text theorem and guidance on step size s and β selection are needed.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited (mostly low-dimensional); larger-scale, higher-dimensional, more constraint sets/objectives, and wall-clock/gradient-cost reporting are recommended.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to other continuous-time inspired discretizations and momentum variants; test combinations of adaptive averaging with restarts.  
- Weakness: Heavy notation (a,w,W,η,r) and t0>0 nuance hinder readability; a summary table and intuitive guidance on choosing t0 would help.  
- Weakness: Strongly convex case should state explicit linear-rate guarantees or discuss discrete-time accelerated behavior under strong convexity.  
- Evaluation summary: strong theoretical backbone and originality; clarity and discrete-time visibility need improvement; potentially impactful.  
- Pros for acceptance: unified Lyapunov framework, principled adaptive averaging, accelerated replicator link, and empirical benefits vs restarts.  
- Cons for acceptance: trajectory-dependent verifiability, discrete-time details in supplement, narrow experiments, and minor presentation issues.  
- Overall: thoughtful, rigorous extension of AMD with principled adaptive averaging; recommend strengthening discrete-time exposition, expanding experiments, and fixing minor clarifications before acceptance.
