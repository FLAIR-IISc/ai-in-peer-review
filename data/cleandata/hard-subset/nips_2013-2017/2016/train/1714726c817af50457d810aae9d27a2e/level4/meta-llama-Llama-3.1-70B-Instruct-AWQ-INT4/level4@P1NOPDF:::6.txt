This paper presents an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating constrained convex optimization, which provides a convergence rate guarantee when the objective function is not strongly convex. This is an advantage over existing restarting heuristics, and the authors demonstrate this advantage both theoretically and experimentally.

As a reviewer outside the area of expertise, I found the content of the paper challenging to follow. I assume that the abbreviations and terms used are familiar to the target audience, but including some background information would be beneficial for readers like myself. Specifically, the equations on lines 33-34 and 46-47 would benefit from more intuitive explanations to facilitate understanding.

Additionally, I would appreciate a discussion on the relationship between the proposed adaptive averaging method and those used in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad [1] or Adam [2]. This comparison would provide valuable context and help readers understand the novelty of the approach.

I also have concerns about the practical significance of this work. The experiments were conducted in R^3, which seems to be a simplified setup. It would be helpful to know how the proposed method (and related work) scales to much higher dimensions, as this is often a critical aspect of optimization problems.

On a minor note, the reference to the solid line in Figure 1 is ambiguous. Using a dotted line or colors to illustrate the trajectory of the primal variable would improve clarity.

References:

[2] Kingma, Diederik, and Jimmy Ba. "Adam: A method for stochastic optimization." International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR). (2014).
