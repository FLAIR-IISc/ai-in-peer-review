

The paper builds upon Krichene et al.'s NIPS 2015 ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration to propose and evaluate a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting iterates, accelerating convergence. Unlike prior adaptive averaging heuristics, this method guarantees dominance over standard fixed-schedule averaging. Empirical results demonstrate significant speedups across a broad class of functions. The mathematical exposition is clear and accessible, particularly for readers familiar with Krichene et al.'s work, and the empirical findings are promising. While the technical development constitutes a straightforward extension of Krichene et al., the paper holds substantial potential impact given Nesterov's acceleration's widespread use in applications. A common shortcoming in empirical evaluations is comparing new adaptive methods against Nesterov's acceleration with fixed weights; this work demonstrates that adapting Nesterov's method requires only a minor modification and yields substantial improvements. To strengthen the technical contribution, I recommend providing an analysis of the discretized process to establish that the convergence rate is preserved—this should be relatively straightforward. Additionally, exploring potential connections between the adaptive averaging of Nesterov's method and the Conjugate Gradient method for strongly convex quadratic optimization would be valuable.
