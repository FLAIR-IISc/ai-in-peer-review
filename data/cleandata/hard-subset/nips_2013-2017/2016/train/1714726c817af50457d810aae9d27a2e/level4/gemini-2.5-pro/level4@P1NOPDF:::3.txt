This paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings. The authors continue a line of study initiated by Krichene et al. [7], where Nesterov's method is shown to be an appropriate discretization of an ODE system that couples primal and dual trajectories. In that framework, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. The present paper investigates more general averaging schemes. Theorem 2 establishes convergence rates for these schemes under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. In the continuous setting, the authors derive convergence rates faster than the classical O(1/t^2). The paper also proposes and analyzes an adaptive scheme for choosing the weights, providing convergence guarantees. This adaptive method is then evaluated in the experimental section, where its performance is compared with that of restarting techniques, which are also known to improve the practical convergence of accelerated methods. A theoretical analysis of the corresponding discrete-time algorithm is not provided.

Overall, this is an interesting and well-written paper. The analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods, presented for the continuous setting, is rigorous. While the general approach of analyzing continuous dynamics is now well-established by a line of recent work, this paper offers novel contributions and results. In this context, it would be appropriate for the authors to cite the related work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and their coauthors. The main theorem, demonstrating that certain averaging schemes can achieve convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2), is a significant contribution. A key open question, however, is whether this improved rate is preserved when the algorithm is discretized.
