The paper investigates accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in both continuous and discrete settings, building upon the work initiated by Krichene et al. in [7]. The authors extend the study of Nesterov's method as an appropriate discretization of an ODE system that couples a primal trajectory with a dual one. Specifically, the primal trajectory is obtained by performing a particular averaging of the mirror of the dual variable. This paper explores more general averaging schemes, and Theorem 2 derives convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. Notably, the paper achieves convergence rates faster than O(1/t^2) in the continuous setting. The authors also examine an adaptive choice of weights with convergence guarantees, which is then applied in the experimental section. This adaptive choice is compared to restarting techniques, known to improve the convergence of accelerated methods in practice. However, the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is not provided. 

I find this to be an interesting and well-written paper, presenting a rigorous analysis of the new averaging scheme for accelerated methods in the continuous setting. Although the main ideas underlying the analysis of continuous dynamics have been established in a series of papers, this work offers new contributions and results. In this regard, the authors should consider citing the work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors. The main theorem, demonstrating the existence of averaging schemes with faster convergence rates than O(1/t^2), is a relevant contribution. Nevertheless, it would be intriguing to investigate whether this improved rate is preserved when transitioning to the discrete procedure.
