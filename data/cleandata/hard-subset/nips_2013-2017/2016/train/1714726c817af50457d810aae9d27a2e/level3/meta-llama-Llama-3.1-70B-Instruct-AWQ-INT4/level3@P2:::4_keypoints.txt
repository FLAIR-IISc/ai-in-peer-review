- Studies ODEs with a generalized averaging scheme extending the averaging formulation of accelerated mirror descent.  
- Derives sufficient conditions on the dual learning rate and weights to guarantee target convergence via a Lyapunov argument.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that accelerates Lyapunov decrease while preserving accelerated method convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and well-organized technical sections.  
- Proofs are rigorous and easy to follow.  
- Numerical experiments are thorough and provide good comparisons to existing methods.  
- Novel insight: adaptively averaging weights along solution trajectories can produce the same empirical speedups as accelerated methods.  
- Good related-work review and discussion of limitations of existing heuristics (e.g., restarting).  
- Minor weakness: lacks broader context on the significance and applications of replicator dynamics.  
- Minor weakness: numerical experiments should include more examples and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall technically sound, significant contribution to optimization, and recommended for acceptance.
