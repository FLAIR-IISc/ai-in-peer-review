- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, emphasizing primal–dual interplay and the role of averaging.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic designed to accelerate the decrease of a Lyapunov function.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for the adaptive averaging scheme, preserving quadratic convergence rates in discrete time.  
- Builds on continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and connects those to discrete algorithms.  
- Applies the framework to replicator dynamics, offering a concrete illustrative example.  
- Uses Lyapunov analysis as a solid theoretical foundation with rigorous proofs and clearly stated assumptions.  
- Novel combination of Lyapunov analysis and adaptive averaging; the specific heuristic and guarantees are a distinct contribution.  
- Experimental results show adaptive averaging is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, adaptive restarting methods.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (low-dimensional, few problem instances) and need broader testing and quantification of claimed improvements.  
- Related-work discussion is insufficient, particularly lacking deeper comparison to adaptive methods like Adagrad and Adam.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with standard notation and readable derivations.  
- Some sections (Bregman divergences, interpretation of the energy function, intuition for averaging schemes) require more elaboration.  
- Technically sound overall, though the reviewer acknowledges limited expertise to fully verify all proofs.  
- Results are potentially significant if empirical findings generalize; the work opens avenues for adaptive optimization research.  
- Recommendation: accept, provided authors address expanded experiments, deeper related-work comparison, and clearer exposition.
