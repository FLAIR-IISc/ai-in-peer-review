Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies a broad class of continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics for constrained convex optimization, parameterized by two positive weight functions: η(t), the dual accumulation rate, and w(t), the averaging weights. The dynamics couple a dual trajectory Ż(t) = −η(t)∇f(X(t)) with a primal trajectory X(t) that is the w-weighted average of the mirrored dual path. Using a Lyapunov function Lr(X,Z,t) = r(t)(f(X)−f∗) + Dψ∗(Z, z∗) with a user-chosen nondecreasing rate function r(t), the authors provide sufficient conditions on η and w ensuring that Lr is nonincreasing and that f(X(t))−f∗ = O(1/r(t)). They also derive a primal-only representation, give concrete examples (positive orthant, simplex), and show that the replicator dynamics (mirror descent on the simplex with entropic geometry) admit an “accelerated” variant via generalized averaging.

Beyond this, the paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t) := w(t)/W(t) on-the-fly to decrease the Lyapunov derivative whenever possible. This preserves the target rate in continuous time and, for the quadratic rate r(t) = t^2, yields a discrete-time algorithm that maintains the O(1/k^2) rate (details in the supplement). An additional “energy” function Er(t) = f(X(t)) + (1/r(t))Dψ∗(Z,∇ψ(X)) is shown to be nonincreasing under the same conditions, providing an interpretable potential+kinetic decomposition. Experiments (mainly on R^3 simplex problems) compare the adaptive averaging scheme to gradient and speed restarting heuristics; adaptive averaging performs at least as well, sometimes better, and exhibits monotonic Lr in accordance with theory.

The work builds on and unifies lines of research relating accelerated methods to ODEs and variational principles (Su–Boyd–Candès; Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan), continuous-time mirror descent and its accelerated variants (Krichene–Bayen–Bartlett), and heuristic restarts for acceleration (O’Donoghue–Candès). It also connects to replicator dynamics from evolutionary game theory and to recent adaptive stepsize ideas (AdaGrad/Adam), though the goal here is to adapt the averaging rather than the learning rate.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The continuous-time analysis is clean. The Lyapunov derivative bound isolates two conditions: η(t) ≥ r′(t) and a path-dependent term ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩(r − ηW/w) ≤ 0. A constructive corollary (choosing η = a r with a ≥ r′/r) guarantees the rate. Existence/uniqueness is handled carefully via the AMD′ formulation and t0 > 0. The energy function Er is a nice addition.
- Heuristic and discrete-time guarantees: The adaptive rule chooses a(t) = ηr when ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩ > 0 and increases a(t) otherwise, ensuring nonincreasing Lr in continuous time. For r(t) = t^2, the discrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) is derived via t ≈ k√s and claims to preserve the quadratic rate; the proof is deferred to the supplement. This is plausible given the discrete Lyapunov construction but would benefit from more detail in the main text.
- Examples/experiments: The primal forms for orthant and simplex are correct and illuminating, yielding an accelerated replicator equation. Experiments support the claims but are small-scale; additional higher-dimensional results are in the supplement. Hyperparameter choices and fairness of restart baselines are not fully discussed.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written, with helpful derivations and intuition (e.g., the energy interpretation). Notation is dense at times (e.g., use of checks X̌, Ž); a compact notation table would help. Algorithm 1 could be presented with clearer definitions of all constants (β, γ, s) and a brief explanation of the prox terms Dψ and R. Minor editorial issues aside, the flow is coherent.

Originality
- Generalized averaging framework with explicit sufficient conditions linking η, w, and a target rate r(t) is a useful unification, extending prior AMD formulations.
- The adaptive averaging heuristic—choosing weights to decrease the Lyapunov derivative—appears novel and conceptually different from restart heuristics.
- The accelerated replicator instantiation and the Er energy viewpoint are nice contributions.

Significance
The work contributes theoretically (a unifying Lyapunov framework for accelerated mirror-descent-type ODEs, constructive schedules achieving desired rates) and practically (an adaptive averaging heuristic competitive with popular restarts and preserving Lyapunov monotonicity). Impact is likely among researchers designing accelerated methods for constrained problems and those exploiting continuous-time perspectives. Demonstrated empirical benefits are promising but currently limited to relatively small, structured problems; broader experiments would strengthen significance.

Pros
- Clear, modular Lyapunov analysis yielding verifiable rate conditions.
- Conceptually simple adaptive averaging rule with continuous-time guarantees.
- Discrete algorithm preserving O(1/k^2) rate (with proof in supplement).
- Insightful primal representations; accelerated replicator example.
- Empirical comparison to strong baselines (gradient/speed restarts); often better performance and smoother Lyapunov behavior.

Cons
- Key adaptive rule’s discrete-time analysis is not in the main text; practical guidance on parameters (β, s, γ) is limited.
- The critical condition (2) in Theorem 2 is path-dependent and hard to verify a priori; the heuristic circumvents this but relies on quantities that may be noisy in practice.
- Experiments are small-scale and focused on entropic/simplex geometry; broader tasks (e.g., large-scale constrained logistic regression, composite objectives with proximals) would help.
- Sensitivity analysis for β and step sizes is missing; comparison to tuned restart variants is limited.
- The relationship to variational ODE frameworks (e.g., [23]) could be emphasized further, including discretization trade-offs for p > 2.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Provide, at least in abbreviated form, the discrete Lyapunov proof for Algorithm 1 in the main paper, and clarify any step-size or smoothness conditions required.
- Offer guidance on choosing β, s, and γ, and include sensitivity plots.
- Compare against more restart variants (e.g., function/value restarts, adaptive restart with backtracking) and include higher-dimensional datasets and composite objectives.
- Discuss numerical stability when a(t) becomes large (exponentially increasing w(t) segments) and any regularization used.
- Can the energy monotonicity under restarts be formalized, given the empirical observation?
- Is there a stochastic version (e.g., mini-batch gradients) where adaptive averaging remains robust?

Broader impact and scope
The topic lies squarely within NIPS/NeurIPS optimization and theory. The results advance understanding of accelerated dynamics and propose a practical heuristic with theoretical backing.

Overall recommendation
Accept (7/10). The paper offers a solid theoretical framework, a novel and principled heuristic, and promising empirical evidence. With expanded discrete-time details and broader experiments, it could have strong impact.
