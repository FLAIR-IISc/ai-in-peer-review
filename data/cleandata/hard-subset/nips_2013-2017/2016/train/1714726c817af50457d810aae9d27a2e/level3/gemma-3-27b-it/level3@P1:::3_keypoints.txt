- Central contribution: generalized framework for accelerated mirror descent dynamics parameterized by averaging and gradient-accumulation weights.  
- Theorem 2: provides sufficient conditions on weight choices to guarantee desired convergence rates.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic: proposed with provable convergence, preserving quadratic accelerated rates in discrete-time and outperforming adaptive restarting empirically.  
- Builds on continuous-time ODE viewpoint linking Nesterov acceleration to a family of dynamics for analysis and algorithm design.  
- Derivation of accelerated replicator dynamics for simplex constraints demonstrates framework versatility.  
- Theorem 1 and discussion: rigorous treatment of existence and uniqueness of continuous-time solutions.  
- Theorem 3 and energy functions: Lyapunov-based analysis establishes convergence rates with clear technical presentation.  
- Primal representation (Section 3): offers intuition and aids analysis.  
- Strengths: novel, theoretically sound, practically relevant adaptive heuristic, clear and well-organized exposition.  
- Empirical evaluation: encouraging results but limited in scope, mostly focused on simplex-constrained problems.  
- Major weakness: limited analysis of discretization — unclear if continuous-time rate improvements carry to discrete algorithms.  
- Related work omission: fails to cite recent Attouch–Peypouquet (and coauthors) work on fast inertial dynamics and vanishing viscosity methods.  
- Discrete Algorithm 1 analysis relegated to supplementary material, which may underplay its importance.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — accept, with suggestion to address discretization analysis and include omitted related work in the final version.
