- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via a generalized averaging scheme.
- Extends continuous-time frameworks of Su et al. and Krichene et al. to a broader class of ODEs using weighted averages of mirrored dual trajectories.
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis with sufficient conditions on weight function w(t) and learning rate η(t) to guarantee convergence rates.
- Derives a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively chooses weights to maximize Lyapunov decrease while preserving theoretical convergence.
- Shows adaptive averaging yields empirical speedups comparable to accelerated methods without losing guarantees.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-constructed using Lyapunov arguments.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive on simplex-constrained problems and demonstrates strong performance, including accelerating replicator dynamics.
- Numerical comparisons show adaptive averaging performs at least as well as, and often better than, alternatives like adaptive restarting.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and supplemented with reproducible proofs and materials.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and clearly discuss scope restrictions tied to specific constraint sets.
- Main weakness: experimental validation is limited mainly to simplex constraints and should include other sets (e.g., positive orthant, ellipsoids).
- Discrete-time analysis is underdeveloped and could better bridge continuous theory to practical algorithms.
- Work is technically sound, original, and represents a complete contribution rather than a position paper.
- The adaptive averaging idea is novel and significant, with potential impact on first- and higher-order optimization methods.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept—paper advances theory and provides a practically useful heuristic for the NIPS community.
