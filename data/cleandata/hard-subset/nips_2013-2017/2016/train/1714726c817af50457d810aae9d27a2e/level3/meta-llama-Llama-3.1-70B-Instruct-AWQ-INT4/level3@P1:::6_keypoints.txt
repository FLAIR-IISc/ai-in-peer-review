- Main contribution: proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves the original convergence rate for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Evidence: provides theoretical analysis and experimental results supporting the claimed guarantees.  
- Accessibility: paper is hard to follow; needs additional background material to reach a broader audience.  
- Terminology: clarify abbreviations and terms (e.g., "AMD" is used before being defined).  
- Key derivations: add more intuitive explanations for critical equations (notably around lines 33–34 and 46–47).  
- Related work: discuss relations and comparisons to adaptive methods in non-convex settings (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Experiments/scalability: experiments are only in R^3; provide evidence of scalability to higher dimensions and real-world settings.  
- Figure clarity: improve Figure 1 (e.g., use dotted line or color) to unambiguously show the primal trajectory.  
- Originality: method is novel and distinct from existing restarting heuristics.  
- Significance: has potential to make a meaningful contribution to constrained convex optimization and applications.  
- Writing/organization: paper is generally well-organized with a clear abstract, but some sections require clearer exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: promising work that requires clarifications, additional background, comparisons, and scalability experiments to maximize impact.
