- Introduces a novel adaptive reweighting heuristic for iterates using the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration.
- The proposed method is provably to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow for readers familiar with Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015).
- Technical development is a close extension of prior work but has large potential practical impact because of Nesterov’s widespread use.
- Empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups across a broad class of functions; a small adaptive change yields dramatic improvements over fixed weights.
- Paper is well-written and clear, assuming familiarity with the referenced ODE framework.
- Overall contribution is well-motivated and technically sound.
- Strengths: novelty of the heuristic, simplicity of analysis, strong empirical results, and practical significance.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the discretized algorithm to verify preserved convergence rates.
- Weakness: does not fully explore links between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, simple, empirically effective, and practically relevant extension of prior work.
- Argument against acceptance: missing discretized-process analysis and unexplored theoretical connections that could strengthen the paper.
