This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves the original convergence rate for non-strongly convex objectives. The authors provide a unified study of a family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics with generalized averaging, and give sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through numerical experiments, comparing their method to existing restarting heuristics. The results show that the adaptive averaging heuristic performs at least as well as other heuristics, and in some cases, considerably better.

However, the reviewer found the paper hard to follow in some parts, and recommends adding background material and clarifying abbreviations/terms. Specifically, the equations between lines 33-34 and 46-47 need more intuitive explanation. Additionally, the reviewer asks for a discussion of the relation to adaptive methods in non-convex optimization, such as Adagrad and Adam.

The reviewer also questions the practical significance of the experiments, which are conducted in R^3 (a toy setup). They request evidence of scalability to higher dimensions.

Minor issues include the need to clarify the reference to the solid line in Figure 1, and to use a dotted line or colors to show the primal trajectory.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of optimization, and the proposed adaptive averaging heuristic has the potential to be widely applicable. With some revisions to address the reviewer's comments, the paper is likely to be accepted for publication.
