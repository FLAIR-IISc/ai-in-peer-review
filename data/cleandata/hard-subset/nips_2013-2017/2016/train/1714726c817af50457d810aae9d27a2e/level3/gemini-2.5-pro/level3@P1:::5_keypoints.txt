- Introduces a general family of continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by time-varying η(t) and w(t).  
- Uses a Lyapunov function combining objective and Bregman divergence to derive sufficient conditions guaranteeing O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Shows the framework generalizes prior ODE-based connections to Nesterov-style acceleration and can accelerate replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function while preserving the provable rate.  
- Presents a discrete-time version of the heuristic and proves it maintains the O(1/k^2) rate of standard accelerated methods.  
- Empirical comparisons on simplex-constrained problems show adaptive averaging is competitive with and sometimes outperforms adaptive restarting.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous Lyapunov analysis, sound derivations, and well-supported theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; main results (Theorem 2) and adaptive averaging are clearly presented.  
- Clarity could be improved: Section 3 (primal representation) is dense and needs more intuition about the ∇²ψ* ◦ ∇ψ operator.  
- Transition from continuous-time heuristic to discrete Algorithm 1 is terse; more main-text explanation of discretization and convergence proof would help.  
- Originality: novel parametrization of accelerated dynamics and the adaptive averaging heuristic with a principled Lyapunov motivation.  
- Significance: provides theoretical insights into averaging in acceleration and a practical, provably-convergent alternative to restarting.  
- Pro-acceptance points: principled novel heuristic, theoretical guarantees, unified continuous-time framework, and promising empirical results.  
- Con-acceptance points (minor): experiments limited to low-dimensional simplex problems and some key discretization/proof details relegated to the supplement.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
