- Presents adaptive averaging in accelerated descent framed as a coupled dual-primal system, extending recent ODE-based frameworks.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that empirically speeds up convergence and offers an intuitive approach to acceleration.
- Provides a Lyapunov-based theoretical analysis (Theorem 2) establishing sufficient conditions for convergence.
- Derives a primal representation (Section 3) and demonstrates applicability via an accelerated replicator equation example.
- Adaptive heuristic is well-motivated and preserves theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Numerical experiments show competitive performance against existing heuristics like adaptive restarting.
- Strong connection drawn between the averaging scheme and existing accelerated methods, offering new insights into dynamics.
- Good literature coverage, including continuous-time optimization and evolutionary game theory references.
- Lacks in-depth discussion of the computational cost and practical overhead of computing adaptive weights.
- Numerical experiments are somewhat limited in scope and should include broader problem instances and stronger baselines (e.g., second-order adaptive methods).
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address computational cost and expand experiments.
