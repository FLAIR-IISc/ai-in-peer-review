- Proposes a generalized continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) framework parameterized by time-varying η(t) (dual accumulation) and w(t) (averaging weights).  
- Provides a Lyapunov-based analysis (Theorem 2) giving simple sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) to guarantee O(1/r(t)) convergence, unifying different averaging schemes.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that uses the sign of ⟨∇f(X(t)), Ẋ(t)⟩ to increase weight on trajectory segments that make progress.  
- Gives a discrete-time implementation (Algorithm 1) and numerical experiments showing empirical improvements over fixed schedules and common restarting heuristics on constrained convex problems.  
- The adaptive heuristic is proven to preserve the O(1/t^2) convergence rate of the underlying accelerated method, offering a formal robustness guarantee absent in some other heuristics.  
- The paper is technically sound, with a simple, elegant Lyapunov argument and clear exposition, especially for readers familiar with ODE interpretations of acceleration.  
- The primal-space formulation linking the dynamics to replicator dynamics provides additional intuition.  
- Empirically effective, simple to implement, and potentially high-impact for practitioners using accelerated first-order methods.  
- Originality is incremental: a natural extension of Krichene et al. (2015) rather than a paradigm shift, though the Lyapunov-derivative-guided adaptive idea is novel.  
- Main theoretical analysis is continuous-time only; the discrete-time algorithm lacks a formal theorem/sketch in the main text, weakening the theory-practice link.  
- Suggests discussing connections to Conjugate Gradient/line-search coefficients in the strongly convex quadratic case to provide deeper insight.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, practically useful contribution with solid theory and experiments; recommended for acceptance.
