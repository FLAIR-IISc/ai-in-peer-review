This paper presents a novel approach to accelerating the convergence of first-order optimization methods by adaptively reweighting the iterates. The authors leverage the ODE interpretation of Nesterov's acceleration, as introduced by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015), to design a natural heuristic for adaptively reweighting the iterates. The proposed method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging, which is a significant improvement over prior adaptive-averaging heuristics.

The mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, given familiarity with Krichene et al.'s work. The technical development is a close extension of prior work but has large potential practical impact due to Nesterov's widespread use. The empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups across a broad class of functions, demonstrating that a small adaptive modification can yield dramatic improvements over fixed-weight Nesterov schemes.

One potential technical suggestion is to include an analysis of the discretized process to show that the convergence rate is preserved. Additionally, investigating links between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics could be a promising direction for future research.

Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and technically sound contribution to the field of optimization, with significant potential for practical impact.
