- Proposes a unified framework for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization, generalizing AMD dynamics with weight functions for dual accumulation and primal averaging.  
- Derives sufficient convergence conditions via a Lyapunov argument (Theorem 2) that are clearly stated and accessible.  
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic based on decreasing the Lyapunov function to potentially accelerate convergence.  
- Shows the adaptive averaging can match or outperform heuristics like adaptive restarting and preserves quadratic convergence in discrete time.  
- Connects the framework to replicator dynamics and evolutionary game theory, illustrating broader applicability.  
- Builds on continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and generalizes prior work (e.g., [11]).  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes a primal representation and concrete simplex-constrained examples for clarity.  
- Empirical results provide preliminary support for the adaptive averaging heuristic and comparisons with existing heuristics.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (mostly simplex problems); broader problem classes and dimensions are needed.  
- Hyperparameter selection (e.g., β, s in Algorithm 1) lacks practical guidance and discussion.  
- Limited discussion of how the proposed adaptive averaging relates to or complements adaptive methods like Adagrad and Adam.  
- Minor technical issue: reference to Attouch et al. [24] about Nesterov’s rate is outdated—best known rate is better than 1/k^2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theory, novel heuristic, clear writing, empirical validation, and interdisciplinary connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental breadth and limited hyperparameter/comparison discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments, detail hyperparameter selection, and update references.
