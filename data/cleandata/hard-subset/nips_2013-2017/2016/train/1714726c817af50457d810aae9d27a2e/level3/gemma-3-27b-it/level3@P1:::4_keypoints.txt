- Studies accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization via coupling a gradient-accumulating dual and a weighted-average primal; provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions and applies framework to replicator dynamics.  
- Generalizes connections between Nesterov-like accelerated methods and continuous-time ODEs to a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions for dual rate and averaging.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically computes weights to expedite convergence, with continuous- and discrete-time guarantees.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-motivated; Lyapunov function yields convergence guarantees and preserves quadratic discrete-time convergence rates.  
- Application to replicator dynamics links optimization with evolutionary game theory and the energy-function view (Theorem 3) gives additional insight.  
- Numerical experiments indicate adaptive averaging is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, adaptive restarting, but the empirical evaluation is limited in scope.  
- Notation is dense and may hinder accessibility for readers not steeped in accelerated-methods literature.  
- Some theoretical conditions (e.g., differentiability of weight functions) may be restrictive in practice.  
- Discussion of related adaptive methods (Adagrad, Adam) is brief and could use a more nuanced comparison on adaptation vs. acceleration.  
- Connection to Wibisono et al. is noted but could be elaborated, especially on synergies with higher-order acceleration techniques.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of the generalized AMD framework and adaptive averaging, theoretical soundness, practical potential, and cross-field applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility/notation issues, limited empirical validation, and potentially restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify discussion of related adaptive methods.
