- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent based on the ODE interpretation (Krichene et al., 2015).  
- Dynamically reweights iterates using decreases in a Lyapunov function, guaranteeing rates at least as good as fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirically improves performance, particularly where fixed-weight schemes fail.  
- Technically sound analysis extending prior work to continuous-time with sufficient convergence conditions.  
- Lyapunov argument is well-motivated and the heuristic is intuitive.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing speed-ups across multiple functions, including simplex-constrained replicator dynamics.  
- Includes useful comparisons to adaptive restarting (O’Donoghue & Candès) and situates contribution relative to Adagrad/Adam.  
- Potentially high practical impact due to wide use of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Major weakness: lacks a formal analysis of the discretized algorithm and preservation of convergence rates.  
- Theoretical presentation relies on reader familiarity with prior Krichene et al. framework.  
- Useful suggested direction: investigate links between adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address discretization analysis.
