This paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, a problem that has seen significant interest in recent years due to its applications in machine learning and other fields. The authors provide a unified study of a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, parameterized by two positive, continuous weight functions w and η. They use a Lyapunov function to give sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate.

The authors also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. They provide guarantees on adaptive averaging in continuous-time and prove that it preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods in discrete-time. The experiments indicate that adaptive averaging performs at least as well as adaptive restarting, with significant improvements in some cases.
