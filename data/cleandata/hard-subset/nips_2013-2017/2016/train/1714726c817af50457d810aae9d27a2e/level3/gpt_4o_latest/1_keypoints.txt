- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization with Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on weight functions and continuous-time convergence guarantees.  
- Claims preservation of quadratic convergence rates when discretized and demonstrates empirical speed-ups across strongly convex, weakly convex, and linear objectives.  
- Technical arguments are clear, rigorous, and build on prior ODE interpretations of Nesterov’s acceleration.  
- Uses Lyapunov functions effectively to establish convergence guarantees in a straightforward, well-motivated manner.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is novel and guarantees dominance over fixed-schedule averaging, filling a gap in adaptive methods literature.  
- Empirical results are comprehensive and show significant improvements over existing heuristics like adaptive restarting, especially for strongly convex cases.  
- High potential impact given widespread use of Nesterov acceleration; small algorithmic changes yield substantial performance gains.  
- The analysis of the discretized process is less detailed; stronger discrete-time theoretical treatment is needed to confirm rate preservation.  
- The paper does not explore potential connections to Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics, missing an avenue for broader insight.  
- Limited discussion of method limitations or scenarios where adaptive averaging may fail to outperform fixed schedules or restarting heuristics.  
- Recommendation: accept—the work provides strong theoretical and empirical contributions and is relevant to the optimization community.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed discrete-process analysis, investigate Conjugate Gradient connections, and include a discussion of potential failure cases and limitations.
