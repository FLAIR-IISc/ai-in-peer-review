- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves original convergence rates for non-strongly convex objectives and claims advantages over restarting heuristics, supported theoretically and experimentally.

- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction and a clear main contribution: adaptive weight computation in accelerated mirror descent.

- Theoretical analysis is thorough and well-supported by mathematical derivations.

- Numerical experiments demonstrate the heuristic’s effectiveness and provide comparisons with existing restarting heuristics.

- Assumes high background knowledge in convex optimization and dynamical systems; needs more background material and clarification of terms/abbreviations.

- Equations between lines 33–34 and 46–47 lack intuitive/physical explanation and need more explanatory text.

- Should discuss connections to adaptive non-convex methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) to broaden context and applications.

- Practical significance is unclear because experiments are limited to R^3; scalability to higher dimensions should be demonstrated.

- Figure 1’s reference to the solid line is ambiguous; plotting should use dotted lines or colors to clearly show the primal trajectory.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel heuristic, strong theoretical support, and supportive numerical results.

- Arguments against acceptance: high assumed background, unclear practical impact, and limited experimental scope.

- Overall: promising contribution that needs revisions; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
