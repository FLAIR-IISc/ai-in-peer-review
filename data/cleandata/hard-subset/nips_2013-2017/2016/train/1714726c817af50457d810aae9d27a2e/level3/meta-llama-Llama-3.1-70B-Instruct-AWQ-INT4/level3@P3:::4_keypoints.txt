- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate convergence of continuous-time optimization dynamics.  
- Studies a broad family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by two positive continuous weight functions.  
- Provides sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate.  
- The heuristic adaptively computes weights along solution trajectories and preserves the theoretical convergence rate.  
- Empirically performs at least as well as other heuristics for first-order accelerated methods.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and presentation of main contributions.  
- Technical presentation is sound with thorough analysis of the dynamics and heuristic.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness and include comparisons with existing heuristics.  
- Main strength: novel insight that adaptive averaging along trajectories yields similar empirical speedups as accelerated methods.  
- Main weakness: lacks detailed comparison with popular adaptive methods like Adagrad and Adam.  
- Suggestion: additional numerical experiments could strengthen empirical claims.  
- High originality and potential significance for machine learning and optimization applications.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, effective experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to other adaptive methods and need for more experiments.
