- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization using the primal-dual ODE framework and generalizes averaging schemes for the primal trajectory.
- Main theoretical contribution: convergence rates for continuous-time dynamics under specific averaging coefficients (Theorem 2), showing improvements beyond the standard 1/t^2 rate.
- Proposes an adaptive weighting scheme with provable guarantees and empirical comparisons to restarting techniques showing competitive performance.
- Rigorous, clear, and well-written analysis of continuous-time accelerated dynamics.
- Illustrates broader applicability via the connection to replicator dynamics as a concrete example.
- Significant weakness: no theoretical analysis or convergence guarantees for the discrete (algorithmic) version.
- Empirical evaluation of the discrete method is provided but lacks accompanying theoretical justification, limiting practical impact.
- Missing discussion and citations of related foundational work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and co-authors on inertial dynamics and Hessian-driven damping.
- The paper should explicitly address how it extends or differs from the cited related works.
- Important open question: whether improved continuous convergence rates persist after discretization is not resolved and should be discussed with potential approaches.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add missing citations and explicitly acknowledge the discrete-convergence open question.
