- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves original convergence rates for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Presents a unified study of a family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics with generalized averaging and gives sufficient conditions on weight functions for target convergence rates.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging rule that computes weights online to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method.  
- Numerical experiments compare the method to restarting heuristics and show equal or superior performance in some cases.  
- Some parts are hard to follow; reviewer requests more background material and clearer definitions/abbreviations.  
- Equations around lines 33–34 and 46–47 need more intuitive explanation.  
- Reviewer asks for discussion connecting the method to adaptive algorithms in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Practical significance is questioned because experiments are limited to R^3 (toy setup); scalability to higher dimensions is not demonstrated.  
- Minor presentation issues: clarify reference to the solid line in Figure 1 and use dotted line or colors to show the primal trajectory.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with potential wide applicability, likely acceptable after revisions.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
