- Core contribution: proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts dual-trajectory weights (via Lyapunov decrease) to recover primal variables and accelerate constrained convex optimization.  
- Theoretical guarantees: provides continuous-time analysis showing the heuristic preserves existing convergence guarantees and the quadratic rate for non-strongly convex problems.  
- Novelty: introduces a new adaptive averaging approach distinct from restarting methods and extends ideas to replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Connection to literature: well-situated within continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov/Attouch/Su and mirror descent frameworks.  
- Lyapunov-based analysis: solid theoretical foundation with clearly stated sufficient conditions for convergence.  
- Experimental validation: numerical results in R^3 show promising performance, comparable to or better than adaptive restarting heuristics.  
- Scalability concern: experiments are limited to low dimension (R^3), leaving open whether benefits scale to high-dimensional ML problems.  
- Clarity and accessibility: paper is dense, uses unexplained abbreviations, and assumes familiarity with advanced background, hindering readability for broader audience.  
- Intuition lacking: several key equations are presented without sufficient intuitive explanation of their role or meaning.  
- Relation to adaptive optimizers: discussion of connections to Adagrad/Adam and adaptive learning-rate methods in nonconvex optimization is brief and underdeveloped.  
- Figure issues: Figure 1’s legend/reference to the “solid line” is ambiguous; clearer plotting (dashed/different colors) recommended.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method with promising initial experiments and strong literature grounding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scalability, poor clarity, and insufficient relation to adaptive gradient methods reduce practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and valuable contribution but needs improved clarity and expanded experiments.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions—authors should add background, intuitive explanations, larger-scale experiments, and fuller discussion of adaptive-gradient connections.
