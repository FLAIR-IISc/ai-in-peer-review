- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs parameterized by η(t) and w(t) and gives Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for O(1/r(t)) convergence.
- Authors recover classical O(1/t^2) and generalize to r(t)=t^p via appropriate choices of η and w.
- Provide an equivalent primal representation, recover mirror descent as a limit, and give explicit forms for positive-orthant and simplex constraints (including an accelerated replicator).
- Construct a second decreasing energy E_r as an additional diagnostic.
- Introduce “adaptive averaging” (adjusting a(t)=w/W along the trajectory) to enforce non-increase of the Lyapunov function without losing nominal rate.
- Derive a discrete Algorithm 1 for the quadratic-rate case and sketch a proof in the supplement that O(1/k^2) is preserved.
- Experiments on low-dimensional simplex problems show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms gradient/speed restarting and keeps Lyapunov monotone.
- Builds on continuous-time acceleration, mirror descent/Bregman geometry, and variational ODE analyses, extending Krichene et al. and Wibisono et al.
- Lyapunov framework and sufficient conditions are clean and clearly derived.
- Corollary tying η and w via a(t) simplifies verifying conditions and recovers known rates for t^p.
- Limitation: the key alignment condition depends on the trajectory and is generally hard to verify a priori.
- Adaptive averaging addresses enforceability but the continuous heuristic is converted to a discrete algorithm with proofs mainly in the supplement.
- No strengthened guarantees for strongly convex objectives beyond O(1/k^2).
- Discrete adaptive method appears implementable but its analysis should be moved into the main text for clarity.
- Empirical evaluation is clear but limited: small dimension (mostly R^3), few objective classes, and limited baselines.
- Suggested baselines missing: FISTA with restarts and higher-dimensional constrained problems.
- Paper is generally well written; continuous-time development and primal/dual viewpoints are clear.
- Recommend more intuition for the alignment condition and how adaptive averaging enforces it.
- Suggest moving a concise discrete-time convergence theorem into the main text and giving practical guidance for parameter choices.
- Novelty: generalized averaging decoupling η and w and adaptive averaging heuristic appear new; accelerated replicator is an interesting application.
- Significance: consolidates and generalizes continuous-time acceleration analyses and proposes a practical heuristic with monotone Lyapunov guarantees.
- Pros: unifying Lyapunov framework, novel adaptive averaging with guarantees, insightful primal examples, and an extra decreasing energy function.
- Cons: trajectory-dependent alignment condition, discrete analysis relegated to supplement, no improved strong-convexity rates, and limited experiments and implementation detail.
- Questions for authors: conditions for improved strong-convex rates; sensitivity of Algorithm 1 to β and stepsize and adaptive strategies; computational overhead of maintaining a(t) and two prox-like steps; compatibility with stochastic gradients and remaining guarantees.
- Suggestions: add a main-text theorem and sketch proof for the discrete algorithm; run higher-dimensional and more varied experiments and baselines; include ablations and visualizations of a(t)/w(t); discuss stochastic/coordinate extensions and relations to Wibisono et al.’s variational family.
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with a novel practical heuristic—weak accept pending stronger discrete analysis and broader experiments.
