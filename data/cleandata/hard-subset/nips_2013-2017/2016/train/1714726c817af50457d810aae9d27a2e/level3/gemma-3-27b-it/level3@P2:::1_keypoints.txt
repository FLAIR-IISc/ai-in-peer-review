- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via continuous-time ODEs, extending prior work (Krichene et al. 2016).
- Presents a generalized framework parameterized by weight functions for averaging and gradient accumulation.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights to maintain or accelerate quadratic convergence.
- Theoretical analysis is based on a Lyapunov argument and provides sufficient conditions for convergence.
- Gives primal representations and example dynamics for positive-orthant and simplex constraints, including an accelerated replicator dynamic.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation and clear, accessible exposition.
- Strength: adaptive averaging has theoretical guarantees of preserving convergence rate, improving over heuristics like adaptive restarting.
- Experiments provide encouraging evidence and sometimes significant improvements over existing methods.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited in scale and concentrated on simplex-constrained problems.
- Weakness: related-work discussion could better cover recent adaptive methods using second-order information or advanced momentum techniques.
- Weakness: comparison to Adagrad and Adam is cursory and needs deeper analysis of differences and complementarities.
- Important missing piece: detailed analysis of the discretized process and discrete-time convergence-rate preservation is absent from the main text.
- Suggestion: explore connections between adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics for further theoretical insight.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theory, novelty, potential practical impact, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, missing discrete analysis, and insufficient comparison to established adaptive optimizers.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and include a more detailed discretized analysis.
