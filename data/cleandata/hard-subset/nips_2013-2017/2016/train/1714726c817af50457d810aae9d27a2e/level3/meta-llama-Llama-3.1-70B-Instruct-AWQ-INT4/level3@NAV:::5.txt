This paper presents a novel approach to accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The authors introduce a generalized averaging scheme, which allows for more flexibility in the choice of weight functions. They provide sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate in continuous time.

The main idea of the paper is to use a Lyapunov function to achieve the desired convergence rate. The authors propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. This heuristic adaptively computes the weights to minimize the time derivative of the Lyapunov function.

The paper also presents a primal representation of the accelerated mirror descent dynamics, which provides a more intuitive understanding of the algorithm. The authors show that the replicator dynamics, an ODE that plays an important role in evolutionary game theory, can be accelerated using a simple averaging scheme.

The numerical experiments presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the adaptive averaging heuristic. The results show that the heuristic performs at least as well as other heuristics for first-order accelerated methods, and in some cases considerably better.

Strengths of the paper:

* The paper presents a novel approach to accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization.
* The authors provide sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate in continuous time.
* The adaptive averaging heuristic is simple to implement and can be used with various weight functions.
* The primal representation of the accelerated mirror descent dynamics provides a more intuitive understanding of the algorithm.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The paper assumes that the objective function is convex and differentiable, which may not be the case in all applications.
* The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of the adaptive averaging heuristic.
* The numerical experiments are limited to a few examples, and more experiments are needed to fully evaluate the performance of the heuristic.
