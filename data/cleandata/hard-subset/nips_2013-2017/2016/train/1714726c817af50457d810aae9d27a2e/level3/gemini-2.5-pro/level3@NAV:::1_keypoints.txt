- Presents a novel adaptive heuristic for Nesterov’s accelerated method, building on the continuous-time ODE view of Krichene et al.
- Core idea: generalize the averaging scheme and adaptively reweight iterates to greedily decrease a Lyapunov function.
- The adaptive reweighting is designed to accelerate convergence while provably preserving the O(1/t^2) continuous-time rate.
- The method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike many prior heuristics.
- The Lyapunov-based mathematical argument is clear and accessible to readers familiar with continuous-time optimization.
- Original contribution: a small, practical adaptive change to averaging yields significant empirical improvements.
- Empirical evaluation is convincing, showing speed-ups over standard Nesterov and adaptive restarting across many convex problems.
- Main weakness: technical development is a close extension of prior work and lacks a detailed discrete-time analysis in the main text.
- Recommendation: include a formal proof sketch that the discrete algorithm preserves the O(1/k^2) rate to make the paper more self-contained.
- Future work suggestion: investigate connections between the adaptive averaging scheme and Conjugate Gradient on strongly convex quadratics.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful contribution with strong theoretical grounding and practical potential.
