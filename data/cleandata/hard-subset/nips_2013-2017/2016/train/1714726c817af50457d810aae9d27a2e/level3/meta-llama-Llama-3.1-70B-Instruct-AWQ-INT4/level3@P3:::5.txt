This paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The authors propose a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, parameterized by two positive, continuous weight functions w and η, which determine the rate at which the dual variable accumulates gradients and the averaging scheme for the primal variable, respectively. The authors provide sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function, which is shown to perform at least as well as or better than adaptive restarting in numerical experiments.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise introduction to the problem of constrained convex optimization and the motivation for studying accelerated descent dynamics. The authors provide a thorough review of previous work on the topic, including the connection between Nesterov's accelerated methods and continuous-time ODEs. The main contributions of the paper are clearly stated and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

The technical content of the paper is sound, and the authors provide a rigorous analysis of the AMD dynamics using a Lyapunov argument. The sufficient conditions for the Lyapunov function to be non-increasing are clearly stated and well-justified. The adaptive averaging heuristic is also well-motivated and shown to be effective in numerical experiments.

The numerical experiments are well-designed and provide a thorough comparison of the adaptive averaging heuristic with existing restarting heuristics. The results are clearly presented and easy to interpret, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of the results.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization and provides a new perspective on accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The adaptive averaging heuristic is a promising approach that warrants further investigation, and the authors' numerical experiments provide a good starting point for future research.
