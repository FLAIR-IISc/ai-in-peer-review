- Extends ODE-based accelerated mirror descent with a generalized averaging scheme parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Provides sufficient convergence conditions using a Lyapunov argument in continuous time.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically increases weights when the trajectory makes progress.
- Validates the approach on replicator dynamics and compares it to existing restarting heuristics.
- Strength: clear connection between continuous-time dynamics and discrete optimization algorithms.
- Strength: intuitive adaptive averaging heuristic with an energy-function interpretation (Theorem 3) giving physical intuition.
- Strength: interesting application to simplex-constrained (replicator) dynamics.
- Experimental result: adaptive averaging can outperform restarting heuristics in some cases, particularly for strongly convex problems.
- Main technical contribution criticized as a trivial extension of prior Krichene et al. (NIPS 2016) work.
- Analysis limited to quadratic convergence rate r(t)=t^2; faster rates and higher-order methods are not discussed.
- Adaptive averaging heuristic lacks theoretical grounding: no discrete-time convergence proof provided.
- No comparison or relation established to other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) or other adaptive averaging schemes.
- Experiments are limited to simple quadratic/linear objectives in R^3, reducing practical assessment and generality.
- Discretization details (Algorithm 1) lack rigorous convergence guarantees.
- Originality is questionable; the paper does not clearly show substantial improvement over existing approaches.
- Clarity is generally good, but the connection to prior work and differences from restarting strategies could be clearer.
- Significance is limited by narrow analysis scope and insufficient experimental validation.
- Authors’ post-rebuttal commitments (more experiments, convergence proof, cubic-regularized Newton implementation) would strengthen the paper but are not included.
