- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerating first-order methods in constrained convex optimization.  
- Studies a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics with sufficient conditions on weight functions for continuous-time convergence rates.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis using a Lyapunov function and an energy function to capture system behavior.  
- Adaptive averaging preserves convergence rates and empirically performs at least as well as other heuristics.  
- Numerical experiments show favorable comparison to existing restarting heuristics.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem and solution.  
- Omits theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm.  
- Could include a more detailed discussion of relationships to prior work.  
- Claims are technically sound and well-supported by theory and experiments.  
- Paper has high significance and potential impact for optimization and machine learning.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper.
