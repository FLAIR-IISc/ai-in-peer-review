- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, extending Krichene et al.'s primal-dual ODE framework.  
- Focuses on the primal trajectory obtained by averaging the mirror of the dual variable and generalizes averaging schemes.  
- Provides a unified analysis of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics using a Lyapunov argument.  
- Derives sufficient conditions on averaging coefficients to achieve desired continuous-time convergence rates.  
- Introduces a new Lyapunov function yielding continuous-time convergence rates faster than the usual 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive choice of averaging weights with provable convergence guarantees.  
- Presents an adaptive averaging heuristic that empirically outperforms or matches existing restarting techniques.  
- Continuous analysis is rigorous, the paper is well-written, and the introduction and algorithm description are clear.  
- Includes thorough related-work discussion but omits citation of prior work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness and detailed implementation of the proposed methods.  
- Main limitation: theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm is omitted.  
- Open question: whether the improved continuous-time rates persist after discretization.  
- Technically sound results, claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Original contribution: novel combination/generalization of familiar techniques that differs from prior work.  
- Significant advance likely to be useful to other researchers; overall recommendation to accept despite open discrete-analysis gap.
