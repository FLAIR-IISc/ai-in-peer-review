- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization via a primal-dual ODE approach, focusing on averaged primal trajectories and generalized averaging schemes.
- Derives a broad family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w and η, analyzed with a unified Lyapunov argument.
- Provides a sufficient condition on η, w, and target rate r that ensures the Lyapunov function decreases, enabling continuous convergence rates faster than 1/t^2.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging-weight heuristic that adaptively speeds Lyapunov decrease and is shown to preserve quadratic convergence in discrete time.
- Empirical results show the adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting heuristics, with substantial improvements in some cases.
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous; the paper is well-written with clear notation and derivations.
- Major limitation: lacks a theoretical analysis of the discrete algorithm.
- Missing citations to related prior work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors.
- Raises an important open question: do the improved continuous rates persist after discretization?
- Quality assessment: technically sound and claims supported by theory and experiments.
- Originality: presents a novel combination of known techniques and introduces a new adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Significance: results are important and likely to be built upon by other researchers.
- Recommendation: with added discrete analysis and proper citations, the paper could make a strong contribution.
