- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using a Lyapunov-function approach.  
- Generalizes Krichene et al. by introducing a family of ODEs parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Provides sufficient conditions on weight functions to guarantee desired convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically computes weights and accelerates Lyapunov decrease while preserving rates.  
- Empirical comparison to adaptive restarting shows favorable numerical performance.  
- Strength: clear theoretical development using Lyapunov arguments.  
- Strength: novel adaptive averaging heuristic with improved empirical performance.  
- Strength: interesting application to replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Builds naturally on prior continuous-time acceleration work by Su et al. and Krichene et al.  
- Novelty concern: paper could better distinguish the approach from other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound with clear sufficient conditions; empirical results support claims.  
- Writing is good but needs clearer distinction from previous adaptive approaches and more explicit discussion of practical benefits.  
- Significance: offers a theoretically grounded approach to constrained acceleration, but should clarify when it is preferable to existing methods.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty and practical impact.
