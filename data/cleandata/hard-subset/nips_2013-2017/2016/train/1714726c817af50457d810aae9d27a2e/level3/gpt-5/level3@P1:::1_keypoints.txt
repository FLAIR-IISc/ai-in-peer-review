- Proposes a generalized continuous-time AMD ODE with two degrees of freedom: gradient accumulation rate η(t) and averaging weight schedule w(t).  
- Uses a Lyapunov function Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*) to derive simple sufficient conditions on (η,w) for any target rate 1/r(t).  
- Recovers standard O(1/t^2) acceleration as a special case of the framework.  
- Provides primal representations and worked examples for constrained domains (orthant, simplex), including an "accelerated replicator" dynamics.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts instantaneous averaging a(t)=w(t)/W(t) to maximize negativity of the Lyapunov derivative while preserving rate guarantees.  
- Gives a discrete-time instantiation for r(t)=t^2 (matching first-order accelerated methods) and claims preserved O(1/k^2) rate via a discrete Lyapunov argument in the supplement.  
- Experiments on simplex-constrained problems show adaptive averaging is at least as good as restarting and sometimes substantially better.  
- Builds directly on prior AMD/ODE work (Krichene et al., Su-Boyd-Candès, Wibisono et al.) and contrasts with adaptive restarting heuristics by adapting averaging inside dynamics.  
- Connects to averaging/acceleration insights (Flammarion & Bach) and adaptive first-order methods (Adagrad, Adam) while serving a different purpose.  
- Theorem 1 (ODE well-posedness) is standard and correct under Lipschitz assumptions; Theorem 2 (Lyapunov calculation) is clean and constructive.  
- Addresses the trajectory-dependent sign condition via a constructive schedule and the adaptive rule that enforces nonpositivity of the cross-term.  
- Presents an energy function Er that decreases under the same condition, providing physical intuition.  
- Explicit construction of w via a(t)=w/W and examples for r(t)=t^p are helpful.  
- Discrete-time theory for the adaptive scheme is largely deferred to the supplement; more main-text details on ψ, R, stepsize s, and inexact prox solves are needed.  
- Experiments are limited to low-dimensional (R^3/simplex) visualizations; larger-scale, higher-dimensional quantitative tests and wall-clock metrics are missing.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and well organized; derivations and worked examples improve readability.  
- Suggest clarifying intuition behind a(t) as instantaneous averaging rate and how constant vs. ramping a(t) shape weights.  
- Originality lies in the generalized averaging parametrization and Lyapunov-guided adaptive rule, though the contribution closely extends Krichene et al.’s AMD analysis.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is practically valuable if discrete guarantees and empirical gains extend to higher dimensions and common ML objectives.  
- Pros: unified Lyapunov framework, insightful primal forms and replicator example, provable rate-preserving adaptive heuristic, and easy modular integration into existing methods.  
- Cons: discrete-time theory mainly in supplement, limited experiments, limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and computational overhead, and no strong-convexity improvements shown.  
- Suggestions: move key discrete Lyapunov proof into main text with assumptions and stepsize conditions; add higher-dimensional benchmarks, wall-clock and iteration comparisons, and adaptive-rule ablations; analyze strongly convex case and CG connection; discuss composite objectives and stochastic gradients.  
- Overall: solid extension of AMD with a useful adaptive averaging rule; recommend acceptance conditional on strengthening discrete-time analysis and experiments.
