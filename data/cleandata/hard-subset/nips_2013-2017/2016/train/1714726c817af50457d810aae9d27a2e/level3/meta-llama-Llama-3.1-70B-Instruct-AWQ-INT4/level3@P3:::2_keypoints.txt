- Proposes an extension of ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging discretization strategy.  
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis of the ODE and gives sufficient conditions on weight functions for desired convergence rates.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate the decrease of the Lyapunov function.  
- Offers clear intuition for the heuristic and demonstrates its empirical effectiveness in numerical experiments.  
- Experiments show the adaptive averaging heuristic compares favorably to existing restarting heuristics and yields significant improvements in some cases.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited practical use.  
- Analysis is restricted to the quadratic-rate case; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods not considered here.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof.  
- Paper is well-written, notation is consistent, and figures help illustrate performance.  
- Numerical experiments are thorough and informative.  
- Paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms.  
- Overall contribution is valuable and novel (adaptive heuristic), but further theoretical development and broader comparisons are needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel adaptive heuristic, clear exposition, and strong numerical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: trivial generalized averaging extension, incomplete theoretical guarantees for the heuristic, and insufficient comparisons to related averaging methods.
