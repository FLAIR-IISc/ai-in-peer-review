- Presents a novel generalized averaging approach ("Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics") to accelerate optimization convergence.
- Analyzes a class of ODEs for accelerated descent and derives sufficient conditions on dual learning rate and weights for target convergence via a Lyapunov argument.
- Provides a unified framework that encompasses existing methods, including Nesterov’s accelerated gradient.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to speed Lyapunov decrease and preserves the original convergence rate.
- Shows the heuristic performs at least as well as other heuristics for first-order accelerated methods.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous.
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed heuristic.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with consistent notation and helpful figures/tables.
- Strength: unified analytical framework and a simple, implementable heuristic that preserves convergence.
- Strength: empirical validation supporting theoretical claims.
- Weakness: requires familiarity with optimization theory and ODEs, which may limit accessibility to non-experts.
- Weakness: analysis restricted to a specific class of ODEs; generalizability to other problems is unclear.
- Originality: introduces a new generalized averaging scheme and an adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Significance: potential broad impact for optimization applications in machine learning, signal processing, and control.
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
