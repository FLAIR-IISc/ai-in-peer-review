**Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent dynamics, which is a family of continuous-time optimization algorithms. The authors extend the ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and introduce a novel adaptive averaging strategy for discretization. They provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through numerical experiments.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and detailed derivation of the proposed algorithm and its convergence guarantees. However, the paper relies heavily on previous work, and some elements, such as the Lyapunov function and discretization, follow directly from earlier papers.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed solution, and the technical sections are easy to follow. The notation is consistent, and the figures and tables are helpful in illustrating the results.

**Originality**

While the paper builds upon existing work, the adaptive averaging heuristic is a novel contribution. The idea of adaptively computing the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function is intuitive and comes with a clear explanation. However, the extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited use, and the paper relies heavily on previous work.

**Significance**

The results are important, as they provide a new perspective on accelerated descent dynamics and offer a potential improvement over existing algorithms. The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm, and the authors provide a clear discussion of the results and their implications.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on previous work, and some elements, such as the Lyapunov function and discretization, follow directly from earlier papers.
2. The extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited use.
3. The paper lacks a comparison to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, which makes it unclear whether the proposed algorithm has a practical advantage.
4. The experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and are not convincing for general or strongly convex cases.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent dynamics.
* The authors provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm.
* The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Con:

* The paper relies heavily on previous work.
* The extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited use.
* The paper lacks a comparison to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms.
* The experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective.

**Post-Rebuttal Commitments**

The authors promise to add high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes. These commitments address some of the weaknesses mentioned above and would strengthen the paper.
