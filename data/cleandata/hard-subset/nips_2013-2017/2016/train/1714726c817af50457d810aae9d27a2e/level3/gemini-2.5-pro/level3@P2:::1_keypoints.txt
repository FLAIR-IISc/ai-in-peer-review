- Paper proposes generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) continuous-time dynamics parameterized by gradient accumulation rate η(t) and averaging weight w(t).  
- Main theoretical contribution: Lyapunov-based analysis giving simple sufficient conditions on η(t), w(t) for O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function to accelerate convergence.  
- The adaptive heuristic is by construction guaranteed to preserve the baseline convergence rate (e.g., O(1/t^2)).  
- Provides a discrete-time version (Algorithm 1) of the adaptive method and claims a discrete-time rate-preservation proof in the supplement.  
- Demonstrates application to accelerating replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.  
- Empirical results show adaptive averaging matches or significantly outperforms standard acceleration and adaptive restarting across several convex problems.  
- Mathematical arguments are sound, elegant, and clearly presented; assumptions and conditions are carefully stated.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with good intuition (e.g., Bregman divergence as kinetic energy) and effective visualizations.  
- The adaptive averaging idea is novel and provides a principled alternative to restarting heuristics.  
- Potentially high practical impact due to simplicity, empirical speed-ups, and formal guarantee of no worse performance than baseline.  
- Minor weakness: theoretical framework closely extends prior work (Krichene et al., 2015).  
- Minor weakness: discrete-time analysis, crucial for practical use, is placed entirely in the supplementary material rather than main text.  
- Suggestions: include a brief sketch/key intuition of the discrete-time analysis in the main paper; discuss potential connections between adaptive averaging and conjugate gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
