This paper, "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics," presents a novel approach to accelerating first-order methods for constrained convex optimization. The authors study a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics and provide sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate in continuous time. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the convergence rate and seems to perform at least as well as other heuristics for first-order accelerated methods.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear understanding of the problem and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the AMD dynamics and the adaptive averaging heuristic, including a Lyapunov function and an energy function that captures a natural measure of energy in the system. The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it compares favorably to existing restarting heuristics.
