- Central idea: Studies accelerated descent dynamics via ODEs, relating coupled dual/primal variables with Lyapunov arguments and proposing an adaptive averaging heuristic.

- Strength — Clear intuition: Adaptive averaging is motivated by decreasing a Lyapunov function and is conceptually well explained.

- Strength — Unified framework: Provides a generalized analysis of AMD dynamics that extends prior continuous-time views of Nesterov-style acceleration.

- Strength — Novel heuristic: The adaptive averaging rule is a novel contribution within the presented framework.

- Strength — Theoretical analysis: Uses Lyapunov-function arguments to give some guarantees under certain conditions.

- Strength — Insights: Primal reformulations and an accelerated replicator-dynamics reduction deepen theoretical understanding.

- Weakness — Limited originality: Much of the framework and tools closely follow the authors’ prior NIPS 2015 work and related literature.

- Weakness — Trivial extension: Generalizing averaging appears straightforward and the analysis mainly targets the quadratic-rate case.

- Weakness — Heuristic nature: The adaptive averaging is ultimately heuristic; discretization introduces approximations and a rigorous convergence proof is lacking.

- Weakness — Lack of comparisons: No benchmarking against other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms is provided.

- Weakness — Limited experiments: Empirical evaluation is confined to toy quadratic/linear objectives, limiting generalizability to realistic problems.

- Weakness — Unfulfilled commitments: Promised post-rebuttal additions (high-dimensional experiments, convergence proof, Newton implementation, comparisons) remain unaddressed.

- Quality: Mathematically sound derivations but overall impact diminished by reliance on prior work and weak empirical evidence.

- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, though notation can be dense at times.

- Originality: Moderate — adaptive averaging is new but the core framework is largely borrowed.

- Significance: Currently limited due to heuristic nature and insufficient experimental validation.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel adaptive heuristic, unified analytical perspective on accelerated dynamics, and connection to recent ODE-based acceleration results.

- Arguments against acceptance: Heavy reliance on prior work, trivial extensions, lack of rigorous convergence proof, inadequate experiments, and missing promised results.

- Overall assessment: Borderline paper with interesting ideas but insufficient novelty, theoretical rigor, and empirical support in current form.

- Recommendation: Reject, with encouragement to resubmit after providing stronger experiments, convergence proofs, and comparisons to other averaging schemes.
