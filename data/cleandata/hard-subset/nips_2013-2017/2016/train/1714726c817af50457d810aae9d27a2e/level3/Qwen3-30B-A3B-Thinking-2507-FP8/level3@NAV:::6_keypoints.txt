- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization within continuous-time accelerated mirror descent.  
- Frames the method as a generalized averaging scheme for the primal variable.  
- Uses Lyapunov analysis to show the method preserves quadratic convergence for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Connects and generalizes prior continuous-time acceleration work (e.g., Su et al. 2016, Krichene et al. 2015).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides sufficient conditions for convergence rates.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is novel and maintains convergence guarantees while improving performance.  
- Experiments in R^3 show competitive performance versus restarting heuristics.  
- Paper suffers significant clarity issues: dense math with minimal intuition, especially around lines 33–34 and 46–47.  
- Lacks necessary background (e.g., Lyapunov functions) and definitions of abbreviations/terms.  
- Experimental setup is a toy R^3 example and provides no evidence of scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Missing discussion of connections to popular non-convex adaptive methods (Adagrad, Adam).  
- Figure 1 is ambiguous (solid line reference) and needs clearer line styles or colors.  
- Overall: technically sound and original, but poor presentation and narrow experiments reduce practical significance; needs substantial revisions to be acceptable.
