- Main contribution: Generalizes prior primal–dual continuous-time dynamics to a broad class of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by averaging weight w(t) and rate η(t).  
- Key theoretical result: Theorem 2 gives simple sufficient conditions on w(t) and η(t) guaranteeing O(1/r(t)) convergence in continuous time.  
- Lyapunov framework: Uses a generalized Lyapunov function to unify and extend analyses of accelerated methods and derive convergence conditions.  
- Recovering known rates: Framework recovers O(1/t^2) and can yield faster continuous-time rates (e.g., O(1/t^p), p>2).  
- Acceleration interpretation: Shows acceleration can be understood via specific averaging schemes of mirrored dual trajectories.  
- Replicator dynamics: Demonstrates accelerating replicator dynamics as a novel application of the averaging perspective.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic: Proposes an adaptive averaging rule that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function to dampen oscillations and speed convergence.  
- Discrete algorithm: Presents a discrete-time version (Algorithm 1) implementing the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Empirical results: Experiments show adaptive averaging performs comparably or better than popular restarting heuristics and preserves Lyapunov monotonicity.  
- Strength: Technical soundness and rigorous continuous-time analysis with elegant, unified Lyapunov-based derivations.  
- Strength: Originality and significance in generalizing averaging perspective and offering provably Lyapunov-decreasing adaptive schemes.  
- Strength: Exceptional clarity and organization, with intuitive derivations from ODEs to heuristic.  
- Weakness: Positioning could be improved by more thoroughly comparing to Attouch, Peypouquet, and related inertial/Hessian-driven damping literature.  
- Weakness: Discretization gap — faster-than-1/t^2 continuous rates are not shown to transfer to practical first-order discrete methods; need discussion of fundamental barriers and higher-order requirements.  
- Weakness: Convergence proof for Algorithm 1 is relegated to the supplement; main paper should state the discrete-time theorem and sketch the proof for self-containment.  
- Overall recommendation: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; paper is a solid, novel contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
