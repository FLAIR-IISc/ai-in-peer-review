- Contribution summary: Introduces a two-parameter accelerated mirror-descent ODE family AMD_{w,η} with a Lyapunov function L_r that yields simple sufficient conditions for accelerated O(1/r(t)) rates and unifies prior fixed-weight analyses.

- Adaptive mechanism: Proposes an online adaptive averaging rule a(t)=w(t)/W(t) that minimizes instantaneous Lyapunov decrease, provably never underperforms the fixed schedule in continuous time.

- Energy functional: Introduces a second energy Er that is nonincreasing under the same conditions, providing extra interpretability.

- Primal/algorithmic instantiation: Derives primal dynamics (including an accelerated replicator on simplex) and a discrete-time Algorithm 1 for r(t)=t^2 that empirically preserves O(1/k^2).

- Empirical results: Experiments (mainly simplex/low-dim) show consistent speed-ups over adaptive restarting and monotone Lyapunov decrease in practice.

- Strength — conceptual clarity: Single Lyapunov framework gives clean, easy-to-follow conditions for acceleration with generalized averaging.

- Strength — adaptive guarantee: The adaptive heuristic has a continuous-time domination guarantee unlike most restarting heuristics, and links naturally to Nesterov-type methods.

- Strength — practical potential: Small modification with demonstrated empirical gains and likely broad adoptability; accelerated replicator may interest other communities.

- Weakness — incremental novelty: The technical analysis closely follows prior ODE/Lyapunov work; main novelty is the adaptive weight choice.

- Weakness — discrete-time theory incomplete: Preservation of O(1/k^2) for the adaptive scheme is mainly in the supplement; main text lacks explicit step-size/β/regularizer conditions and a discrete-time domination guarantee.

- Weakness — limited experiments: Empirical scope is modest (3D simplex, some supplement results); needs higher-dimensional ML benchmarks and additional mirror maps.

- Open question — strong convexity: Unclear whether adaptive averaging improves linear-rate constants or yields provable faster rates under strong convexity.

- Terminology issue: “Quadratic convergence rate” is misleading; should use “O(1/t^2) accelerated rate.”

- Quality and clarity: Results are technically sound and well written; recommend moving core discrete-time assumptions/proofs into the main text and defining symbols at first use.

- Detailed suggested improvements: Add a main-theorem for discrete-time O(1/k^2) with precise conditions, discrete-time comparisons to fixed schedules, discretization/stability analysis (step size, β, mirror smoothness), stronger convexity/CG connections, higher-dim ML experiments, wall-clock and overhead measurements, and relate to Flammarion & Bach and Wibisono et al.

- Acceptance tradeoff: Accept because of a simple, well-motivated adaptive mechanism with empirical gains and continuous-time guarantees; reject concerns stem from incremental theory and insufficiently prominent discrete-time guarantees.

- Overall recommendation: Lean accept, conditional on strengthening and foregrounding the discrete-time analysis and expanding empirical validation.
