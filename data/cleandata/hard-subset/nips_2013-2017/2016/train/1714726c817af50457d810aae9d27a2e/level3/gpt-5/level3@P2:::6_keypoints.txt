- Paper studies accelerated mirror-descent via generalized averaging: dual Z(t) accumulates gradients at rate η(t), primal X(t) is weighted average with w(t); Lyapunov Lr gives sufficient conditions for 1/r(t) convergence and unifies prior ODE-based accelerations.  
- Provides a primal-only view, examples for positive orthant and simplex (accelerated replicator dynamics), an adaptive averaging heuristic, a discrete Algorithm 1 claimed to preserve O(1/k^2), and R^3 experiments.  
- Continuous-time analysis is sound and extends Lyapunov approaches; Theorem 2’s conditions and constructive corollary make weight/η design tangible.  
- Key discrete-time guarantee is relegated to the supplement; a concise theorem and proof sketch should be moved into the main text.  
- Condition (2) in Theorem 2 depends on the trajectory; the paper should discuss existence/measurability of the adaptive rule and robustness to discretization noise.  
- Algorithm 1 is implementable for standard mirror maps but practical guidance on choosing β, γ, and stepsize s is limited and sensitivity studies are needed.  
- Experiments in R^3 are illustrative but too small to establish practical significance; scalability to high dimensions and standard ML tasks is not demonstrated.  
- Manuscript is generally well written but notation is dense and many symbols (w, W, η, r, a, X, Z, X̌, Ž) make it hard to follow.  
- Recommend adding a short primer on mirror maps/Bregman divergence, a symbol table/diagram, and a roadmap linking continuous-time results to Algorithm 1.  
- Clarify abbreviations (AMD variants) at first use and use consistent notation for “checked” variables.  
- Key derivations (averaging → AMD′ and inequality leading to Eq. (3)) need more intuition and step-by-step explanation.  
- Figure 1’s legend is ambiguous; distinguish trajectories with clear line styles or colors.  
- The generalized averaging view and Lyapunov-based weight design appear novel and subsume prior ODE families; accelerated replicator dynamics are an interesting contribution.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is simple, original, and distinct from adaptive step-size methods like Adagrad/Adam.  
- Conceptually significant: deepens understanding of acceleration-as-averaging and offers a principled way to tune weights to maintain target rates while improving convergence.  
- To increase impact, larger-scale experiments and clearer implementation guidance (hyperparameters, computational overhead) are required.  
- Request to expand on relation to adaptive methods and nonconvex optimization: whether Er decreases in nonconvex settings and whether conditions extend beyond convex f.  
- Discrete-time theory: move a concise theorem to main text stating assumptions (s, β, γ, strong convexity, Lipschitz constants) and a proof sketch that Algorithm 1 preserves O(1/k^2).  
- Discuss per-iteration computational cost relative to FISTA/accelerated prox, redundancy when R=ψ, and sensitivity of the update rule (lines 6–8) and smoothing for a_k.  
- Address initialization/numerical stability arising from W(0)=0, discrete-time mapping, and transient behavior for large a or β.  
- Broaden scope of constraints with examples beyond simplex/orthant (e.g., l2/l∞ balls, ψ(x)=½||x||^2).  
- Experimental requests: add high-dimensional (d≥1000) tests—strongly convex logistic regression with l2 constraint, simplex-constrained softmax/KL—and compare against gradient restart, speed restart, FISTA with adaptive restart, reporting wall-clock and iterations.  
- Make the connection to Wibisono et al. [23] explicit with a short proposition mapping parameters (a(t)=eα(t), r(t)=eβ(t), η=ar) and clarify implication directions of conditions/rates.  
- Minor fixes: avoid calling O(1/k^2) “quadratic convergence rate,” and complete bibliographic entries.  
- Pros: unifying Lyapunov framework, simple adaptive averaging heuristic with guarantees, novel replicator acceleration, competitive empirical behavior.  
- Cons: experiments are small-scale, discrete-time guarantees in supplement, dense notation and some key derivations lack intuition, limited hyperparameter and overhead guidance.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with a promising heuristic; needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation to maximize impact.
