- Generalizes ODE interpretation of Nesterov accelerated mirror descent by introducing flexible weight functions w(t), η(t) and gives Lyapunov-based conditions for O(1/r(t)) convergence.
- Proposes an adaptive heuristic that greedily adjusts averaging weights to decrease the Lyapunov function while preserving the baseline convergence rate ("safe" acceleration).
- Provides a discrete-time algorithm and empirical evidence that adaptive averaging often outperforms standard acceleration and adaptive restart schemes.
- Significance: simple, intuitive, easy-to-implement modification with high practical impact potential.
- Theoretical guarantee: formal proof the adaptive method never performs worse than the baseline in Lyapunov decay.
- Clarity: well-written, elegant derivations and a clear generalization from fixed schedules to flexible functions.
- Empirical evaluation: strong experiments across various convex problems with informative trajectory and Lyapunov visualizations.
- Originality concern: framework is an incremental extension of Krichene et al. (2015); novelty mainly in the adaptive averaging idea.
- Discrete-analysis gap: main theoretical analysis is continuous-time; discrete-time analysis is deferred to the supplement and should be sketched in the main paper.
- Suggestion: investigate connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratic problems to explain strong empirical performance.
- Recommendation: accept — practical importance and strong validation outweigh limited originality.
