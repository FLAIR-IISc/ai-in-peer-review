- Proposes a novel extension of accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and adaptive averaging for a discretization of the algorithm.  
- Adaptive averaging idea is novel, intuitively clear, and a significant contribution.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough analysis including a Lyapunov function and a primal reformulation.  
- Reduction to replicator dynamics broadens potential applicability.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is relatively straightforward and limited in technical depth.  
- Analysis is restricted to the quadratic-rate case; faster rates would need higher-order methods not addressed here.  
- Energy-function and primal reformulation analyses offer limited practical advancement for ML.  
- Adaptive averaging lacks a full theoretical foundation and an explicit convergence proof, relying on discretization approximations.  
- No comparison to existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, leaving practical advantage unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and not convincing for general or strongly convex settings.  
- Authors commit (in rebuttal) to add high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation on cubic-regularized Newton, and comparisons to other schemes.  
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on authors addressing the theoretical gaps, broader experiments, and comparative evaluations.
