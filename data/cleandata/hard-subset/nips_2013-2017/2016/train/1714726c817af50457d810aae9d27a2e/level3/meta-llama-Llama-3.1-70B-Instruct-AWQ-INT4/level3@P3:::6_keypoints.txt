- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves original convergence rates for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Analyzes accelerated descent dynamics and gives sufficient conditions on weight functions to guarantee target convergence rates.  
- Provides continuous-time guarantees for adaptive averaging and proves preservation of quadratic convergence in discrete-time for accelerated first-order methods.  
- Well-written paper with clear introduction, motivation, and technically clear sections.  
- Offers a unified study of accelerated mirror descent with generalized averaging and connects well to prior work.  
- Includes several illustrative examples that clarify the theoretical results.  
- Introduces a simple, adaptive weight-computation heuristic and its discrete variant that preserve accelerated method rates.  
- Numerical experiments show the adaptive averaging heuristic outperforms existing restarting heuristics, especially in strongly convex cases.  
- Paper can be hard to follow at times due to technical terminology and concepts that may be unfamiliar to non-experts.  
- Needs more detailed explanations of some technical concepts and terms for accessibility.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of connections to adaptive methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Experiments are limited to R^3; more experiments in higher dimensions are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Overall makes important contributions to convex optimization and is likely of interest to researchers.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
