- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent dynamics to speed convergence by targeting the Lyapunov decrease rate.  
- Builds on continuous-time interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and prior coupled primal–dual dynamics work.  
- Derives conditions on averaging weights using a Lyapunov argument, providing a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Connects the method to replicator dynamics on the simplex as an illustrative application.  
- Presents clear, well-organized theoretical derivations and insights into accelerated dynamics behavior.  
- Provides a discrete implementation (Algorithm 1) for practical use.  
- Empirically competitive with, and often superior to, adaptive restarting in the presented experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and focuses on relatively simple problems.  
- Does not prove faster rates for strongly convex functions; extension to these cases is left for future work.  
- Related-work discussion could be broadened to include more recent adaptive techniques involving momentum or look-ahead strategies.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, as a promising contribution, with encouragement to expand empirical validation in future work.
