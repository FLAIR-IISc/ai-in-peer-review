- Studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMDw,η) coupling dual gradient accumulation with primal averaging via weight w(t) and rate η(t).
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on w and η to obtain a 1/r(t) convergence rate.
- Introduces "adaptive averaging" heuristic that greedily adjusts w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function.
- Proves adaptive averaging preserves the O(1/t^2) worst-case convergence rate for general convex (non-strongly convex) objectives.
- Presents a discrete-time version and numerical comparisons against accelerated mirror descent and two restarting heuristics.
- Shows an accelerated form of replicator dynamics, linking the framework to game-theoretic ODEs.
- Strength: novel, theoretically justified adaptive heuristic with a convergence-rate guarantee not typically available for restarting methods.
- Strength: provides an elegant, general continuous-time framework using Lyapunov analysis that could guide future algorithm design.
- Weakness: paper is dense and assumes significant background in mirror descent, Bregman divergences, and convex duality, hurting accessibility.
- Weakness: key derivations (e.g., inequality (3) and the second energy Er) lack intuitive explanation and clearer interpretation.
- Weakness: related work discussion is insufficient, especially regarding adaptive methods like Adagrad, RMSProp, and Adam.
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited to low-dimensional (R^3) toy problems, offering no evidence of scalability or performance on realistic high-dimensional ML tasks.
- Suggested improvements: add more intuition and explanatory text for technical derivations and energy functions.
- Suggested improvements: strengthen experiments with higher-dimensional benchmarks (e.g., logistic regression, LASSO, SVM) and clearer figure labeling.
- Conclusion: theoretically promising and novel but currently incomplete due to clarity issues and limited empirical evidence; would reconsider with stronger experiments.
