- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that reweights iterates based on a Lyapunov function’s decrease.  
- Builds on the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration (Krichene et al., 2015) using a continuous-time framework.  
- The Lyapunov argument provides a solid theoretical foundation and distinguishes this method from prior adaptive averaging heuristics.  
- The method comes with a theoretical guarantee of preserving the quadratic convergence rate.  
- Mathematical presentation is generally clear and accessible for readers familiar with prior ODE-based work.  
- Empirical results show consistent speed-ups and performance at least comparable to adaptive restarting, with improvements in some cases.  
- Includes a replicator dynamics example, showing applicability beyond standard optimization problems.  
- Analysis is largely confined to continuous-time; discrete-time (discretized process) analysis is only in the supplement and needs prominence.  
- Numerical experiments are limited in scope; larger datasets, more complex functions, and comparisons to optimizers like Adam/Adagrad are needed.  
- Connection to Conjugate Gradient is suggested but not fully explored.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of when and why the method outperforms or underperforms other adaptive methods.  
- Presentation would benefit from a figure visualizing how adaptive weights evolve during optimization.  
- Originality: adapting weights via the Lyapunov derivative is a new and well-differentiated contribution.  
- Quality: technically sound and cohesive, with well-supported theoretical claims; experiments supportive but not exhaustive.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, though some derivation sections need more intuition and explanation.  
- Significance: simple, implementable heuristic with theoretical rate guarantees; potential practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, particularly adding discrete analysis and expanding empirical validation.
