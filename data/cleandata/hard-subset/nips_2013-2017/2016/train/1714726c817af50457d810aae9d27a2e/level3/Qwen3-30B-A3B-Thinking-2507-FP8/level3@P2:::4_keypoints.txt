- Studies continuous-time accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization with a generalized averaging scheme.  
- Introduces a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t).  
- Provides a unified Lyapunov analysis yielding sufficient conditions for achieving convergence rates of the form 1/r(t).  
- Main contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to speed Lyapunov decrease while preserving theoretical guarantees.  
- Theoretical results include clear, verifiable theorems (Theorems 2 and 3) and detailed proofs.  
- Empirical evaluation compares adaptive averaging to gradient and speed restarting across multiple objective types and shows improved performance in many cases.  
- Extends prior continuous-time acceleration work (Su et al., Krichene et al.) by offering a more flexible averaging framework.  
- Novel application: accelerating replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides helpful illustrative examples (positive orthant, simplex).  
- Minor clarity issue: discrete-time version of the adaptive heuristic (Algorithm 1) should be more prominent in the main text rather than supplemental material.  
- Limitations noted: Lyapunov function may not converge to zero with multiple minimizers; focus primarily on quadratic rate r(t)=t².  
- Connections to other adaptive methods (e.g., AdaGrad, Adam) are mentioned but not deeply explored.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution with practical implications; recommends acceptance.
