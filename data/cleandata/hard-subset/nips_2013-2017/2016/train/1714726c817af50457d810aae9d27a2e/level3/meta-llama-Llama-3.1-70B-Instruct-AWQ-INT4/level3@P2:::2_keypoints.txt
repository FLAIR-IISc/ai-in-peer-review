- Proposes an extension of ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with generalized averaging and a novel adaptive averaging discretization.  
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function and derives sufficient conditions for convergence rate.  
- Demonstrates applicability by accelerating replicator dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, thorough explanations.  
- Lyapunov-based convergence analysis and derivation of sufficient conditions are solid and effective.  
- Adaptive averaging strategy is novel, intuitive, and shown to be effective in experiments.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is straightforward and adds limited new insight.  
- Analysis is restricted to the quadratic-rate case; achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods not addressed.  
- Energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics offer limited advancement for ML.  
- Adaptive averaging is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof (appears heuristic).  
- No comparison to existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, so practical advantage is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and are not convincing for general or strongly convex settings.  
- Authors commit in rebuttal to add high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof, implement on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton, and compare to other adaptive schemes.  
- Meets conference criteria of quality, clarity, and originality; recommendation: accept conditional on addressing limitations and adding analyses/experiments.
