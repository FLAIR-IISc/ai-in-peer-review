- Extends ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration to a generalized averaging scheme for accelerated mirror descent.  
- Formulates dynamics as a coupled ODE where the primal variable is a weighted average of the dual trajectory.  
- Derives sufficient conditions on weight functions via a Lyapunov argument to guarantee convergence rates.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically computes weights while preserving quadratic convergence.  
- Provides theoretical proof that the adaptive heuristic preserves the convergence rate and dominates fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Empirically validates the method across strongly/weakly convex, linear, and KL-divergence problems with consistent and sometimes dramatic speedups.  
- Visualizes solution trajectories and energy functions to clarify method behavior.  
- Connects and extends prior work (Krichene et al., Su et al., Nemirovski & Yudin) and applies to replicator dynamics in evolutionary game theory.  
- Technical development is elegant, well-structured, and accessible to readers familiar with prior ODE frameworks.  
- Lyapunov-based analysis provides a simple, intuitive framework for convergence analysis.  
- Practical impact is high due to simplicity and ease of integration into existing optimization frameworks.  
- Lacks a detailed discretization analysis to rigorously show rate preservation in discrete time.  
- Does not explore potential connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.  
- Could provide more implementation details and computational complexity analysis for Algorithm 1.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with theoretical guarantees and strong empirical support — recommendation: accept.
