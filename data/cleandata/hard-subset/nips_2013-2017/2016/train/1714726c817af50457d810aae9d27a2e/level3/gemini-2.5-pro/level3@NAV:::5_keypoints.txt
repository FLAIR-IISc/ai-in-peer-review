- Introduces continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization.  
- Uses a Lyapunov function to derive sufficient conditions on weight functions for guaranteed convergence rates.  
- Main contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function to speed convergence while preserving O(1/t^2).  
- Heuristic is novel, intuitive, and by design keeps the Lyapunov function non-increasing, offering guarantees lacking in many restarting methods.  
- Theoretical framework unifies a broad class of accelerated dynamics and links averaging/gradient schedules to convergence via Lyapunov analysis.  
- Empirical results are promising, showing competitive or superior performance versus adaptive restarting techniques.  
- Weakness: the link between continuous-time theory and the discrete-time implementation (Algorithm 1) needs clearer elaboration in the main text.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on restarting baselines; including other adaptive first-order methods (e.g., Adam) would broaden practical comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound, original contribution likely useful for large-scale convex optimization, clearly written, and a good foundation for future work.
