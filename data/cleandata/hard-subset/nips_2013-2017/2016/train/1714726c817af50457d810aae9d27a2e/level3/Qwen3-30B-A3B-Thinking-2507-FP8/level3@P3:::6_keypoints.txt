- Paper studies accelerated descent dynamics via continuous-time ODEs and proposes a generalized averaging framework preserving convergence rates for non-strongly convex objectives.
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that computes weights to accelerate convergence while preserving accelerated quadratic rates.
- Builds on ODE interpretations of Nesterov-style acceleration (Su et al., Krichene et al.) and extends to a broader family of averaging schemes.
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis with Theorem 2 giving clear sufficient conditions for convergence.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and the adaptive heuristic is rigorously shown to preserve the convergence rate.
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging can outperform restarting heuristics in some cases.
- Novelty: generalization of averaging in accelerated mirror descent and a fresh adaptive-averaging perspective distinct from restarting.
- Significance: relevant to the community; continuous-time framework helps understand and potentially improve optimization algorithms.
- Clarity issues: insufficient background and ambiguous notation make the paper hard to follow for non-experts.
- Key concepts like mirror maps and Bregman divergence are not adequately explained.
- Equations (3) and (4) lack intuitive, verbal explanations connecting them to the optimization process.
- Practical significance is weak: experiments are limited to R^3 and do not demonstrate scalability.
- Paper lacks computational complexity and runtime comparisons to establish practical relevance.
- Related-work gap: inadequate discussion of connections to adaptive methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) and why the approach is specific to accelerated convex optimization.
- Figure 1 is ambiguous about the "solid line" and needs clearer visual distinction between trajectories.
- Recommendation: Accept after major revisions to address clarity and experimental weaknesses.
- For acceptance: extends prior continuous-time optimization work, offers a novel heuristic that preserves rates, and provides a unified analysis.
- For rejection if not revised: assumes too much background, experiments are toy-scale, relation to adaptive non-convex methods is under-discussed, and practical significance is unproven.
- Specific suggested fixes: add background on mirror maps/Bregman divergence, give intuitive explanations for key equations, expand experiments to higher dimensions with runtimes, discuss relation to adaptive non-convex methods, and revise Figure 1 visuals.
