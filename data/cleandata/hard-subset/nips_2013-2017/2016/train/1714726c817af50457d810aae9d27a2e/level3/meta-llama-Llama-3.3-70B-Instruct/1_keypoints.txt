- Proposes a novel approach to obtain fast learning rates for heavy-tailed losses.  
- Introduces two new conditions: the multi-scale Bernstein's condition and integrability of the envelope function.  
- These conditions enable derivation of fast learning rates for unbounded losses.  
- Provides thorough analysis of the conditions and their implications.  
- Proofs are rigorous, well-explained, and the presentation is clear and well-structured.  
- Demonstrates feasibility and effectiveness via an application to k-means clustering with heavy-tailed source distributions.  
- Offers a detailed comparison with related work, highlighting contributions and advantages.  
- Strengths: originality, technical soundness, significance, and accessibility to a broad audience.  
- Potential weakness: the introduced conditions may be difficult to verify in practice.  
- Assumes losses satisfy the multi-scale Bernstein's condition, which may not always hold.  
- Analysis is focused on specific loss types; generalization to other losses is unclear.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to provide more guidance on verifying conditions and exploring broader applicability.
