- Introduces a generalized continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) family parameterized by η(t) and w(t).
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis yielding simple sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) for O(1/r(t)) convergence.
- Shows how existing dynamics (e.g., replicator dynamics) fit and can be accelerated within this averaging framework.
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function along trajectories.
- Adaptive averaging provably preserves the O(1/t^2) worst-case convergence rate, unlike many restart heuristics.
- Presents a discrete-time version (Algorithm 1) of the adaptive heuristic and claims the quadratic rate is preserved.
- Empirical results on low-dimensional problems show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms standard accelerated mirror descent and restarting schemes.
- Theoretical development is technically sound, elegant, and the Lyapunov Lr analysis is a strong unifying contribution.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and connects continuous-time theory to the discrete algorithm intuitively.
- Work is original: generalizing accelerated dynamics by arbitrary averaging weights and using Lyapunov control is novel.
- The method is practically appealing: simple to implement and offers stronger guarantees than popular alternatives.
- Main weakness: discrete-time theoretical guarantees are deferred to the supplementary material rather than the main text.
- Empirical limitation: experiments are on relatively low-dimensional problems; larger-scale evaluations would strengthen impact.
- Recommendation: reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on novelty, strong theory, practical relevance, clarity, and promising empirical validation.
