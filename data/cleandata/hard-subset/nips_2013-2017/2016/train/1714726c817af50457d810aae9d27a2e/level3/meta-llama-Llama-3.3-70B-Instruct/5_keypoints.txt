- Introduces the multi-scale Bernstein's condition and integrability of the envelope function to obtain fast learning rates for unbounded/heavy-tailed losses.
- Thoroughly analyzes these conditions, their implications, and how to verify the multi-scale Bernstein condition in practice.
- Applies results to k-means clustering with heavy-tailed sources, showing convergence rates arbitrarily close to O(1/n).
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a clear motivation and concise overview.
- Technical sections are detailed and build logically to provide a comprehensive analysis.
- Provides a thorough discussion of related work and situates contributions within existing literature.
- Strength: novel multi-scale Bernstein condition enabling fast rates for unbounded losses.
- Strength: practical verification path increases applicability to real-world problems.
- Strength: significant contribution through the k-means heavy-tailed application.
- Weakness: assumes the hypothesis class has an integrable envelope, which may not always hold.
- Weakness: relies on i.i.d. observations; extensions to dependent settings are not addressed.
- Weakness: lacks clear comparison to alternative approaches (e.g., robust estimators) that might require weaker conditions.
- Argument for acceptance: significant, well-motivated, carefully derived contribution with clear directions for future research.
- Argument against acceptance: restrictive assumptions (integrable envelope, i.i.d.) and insufficient empirical/comparative evaluation against other methods.
