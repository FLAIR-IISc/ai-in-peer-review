- Studies accelerated mirror descent dynamics via a continuous-time ODE framework building on Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2015).
- Introduces a generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t), with the primal as a weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory.
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to give sufficient conditions on η and w guaranteeing convergence rates of the form 1/r(t).
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically computes weights w(t) to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.
- Proves the adaptive averaging heuristic preserves quadratic convergence rates of accelerated first-order methods in discrete time.
- Demonstrates how the framework can accelerate replicator dynamics on the simplex (application to evolutionary game theory).
- Provides comprehensive numerical experiments showing adaptive averaging matches or outperforms heuristics like adaptive restarting across problem types (strongly/weakly convex, linear, KL divergence).
- Theoretical analysis is sound with clear theorems and proofs; connection between continuous dynamics and discrete algorithm is well-explained.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with helpful supplementary material and implementation guidance.
- Shows significant originality by introducing adaptive averaging and distinguishing it from methods like Adagrad/Adam.
- Claims substantial significance for practical accelerated first-order methods, with theoretical guarantees addressing limits of existing heuristics.
- Main weakness: limited discussion of the practical per-iteration computational cost of the adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Additional concerns: validation primarily on simplex-constrained problems and limited exploration of connections to other adaptive methods in the literature.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with meaningful contributions, clear presentation, and practical relevance—recommended for acceptance.
