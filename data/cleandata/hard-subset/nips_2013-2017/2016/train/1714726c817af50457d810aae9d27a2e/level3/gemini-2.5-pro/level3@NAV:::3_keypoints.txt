- Central idea: generalizes the primal averaging scheme in primal-dual ODEs to accelerate constrained convex optimization.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a unified continuous-time analysis and sufficient conditions on averaging weights and gradient accumulation to achieve a 1/r(t) convergence rate.  
- Novelty: framework can produce continuous-time convergence rates faster than the standard O(1/t^2).  
- Algorithmic contribution: proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights to speed convergence while preserving theoretical rate guarantees.  
- Empirical results: experiments show the adaptive method is competitive and often outperforms restarting heuristics, especially on strongly convex problems.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and the continuous-time analysis is rigorous and insightful.  
- Missing related work: should cite and discuss inertial dynamics literature (Attouch, Peypouquet, et al.) for broader context.  
- Major limitation: lacks a detailed discrete-time analysis in the main paper, leaving open whether faster-than-O(1/t^2) rates survive practical discretization.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and interesting work that advances understanding of acceleration, but would be strengthened by discrete-time results and broader literature contextualization.
