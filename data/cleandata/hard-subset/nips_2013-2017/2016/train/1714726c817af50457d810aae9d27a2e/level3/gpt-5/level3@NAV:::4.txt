This paper studies a broad class of accelerated mirror-descent ODEs with generalized averaging. The key idea is to decouple a dual variable Z accumulating gradients at a time-varying rate η(t) from a primal variable X formed as a weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory with weights w(t). Using a Lyapunov argument, the authors derive sufficient conditions on η and w to guarantee a target convergence rate 1/r(t); they also introduce an “energy” functional that decreases under the same conditions. The work connects and extends several lines of research: continuous-time views of acceleration (Su–Boyd–Candès), variational/Bregman Lagrangian formulations (Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan), accelerated mirror descent (Krichene–Bayen–Bartlett), and practical heuristics such as adaptive restart (O’Donoghue–Candès). A notable application is an accelerated form of the replicator dynamics on the simplex. Beyond fixed schedules, the paper proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts the averaging weights along the trajectory to further decrease the Lyapunov functional; a discrete-time version is shown to preserve the quadratic rate and compares favorably to adaptive restarting in experiments.

Quality: The continuous-time analysis is careful, with existence/uniqueness, a clear Lyapunov construction, and a useful corollary yielding implementable schedules. The adaptive heuristic is well motivated and comes with continuous-time guarantees and a discrete-time rate for r(t)=t^2. The empirical section is informative but limited in scale and scope; robustness to step sizes, noise, and high dimensions remains unclear.

Clarity: Generally well written and well organized. Some conditions (e.g., the trajectory-dependent sign condition) could use more intuition and a practical recipe for choosing η and w beyond the corollary. The discretization and its assumptions could be surfaced more prominently (currently deferred to supplementary material).

Originality: The generalized averaging perspective and the adaptive averaging heuristic provide a fresh and cohesive take on acceleration without restarts. The accelerated replicator example is a nice addition linking optimization and evolutionary dynamics.

Significance: If adopted, adaptive averaging could become a practical alternative to restart heuristics. The theoretical framework may also inform new discretizations and analyses.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, general Lyapunov framework tying averaging schedules to target rates
- Simple, novel adaptive averaging heuristic with guarantees
- Insightful connection to replicator dynamics; primal/dual formulations
- Empirical gains over adaptive restart on several problems

Cons for acceptance:
- Experiments are small-scale (mainly low-dimensional simplex)
- Limited comparison set (no broader first-order baselines beyond restarting)
- Practical guidance for choosing η, w, β, and t0 is light
- Discrete-time guarantees focus on r(t)=t^2; broader cases not shown

Overall, I find the method very interesting and recommend acceptance after strengthening empirical breadth and clarifying implementation guidance.
