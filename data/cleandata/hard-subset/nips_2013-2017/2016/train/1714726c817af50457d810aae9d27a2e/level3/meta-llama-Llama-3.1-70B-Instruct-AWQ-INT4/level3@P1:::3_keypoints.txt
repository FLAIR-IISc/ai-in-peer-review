- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, generalizes averaging schemes, and proposes adaptive averaging with provable convergence.
- Provides a rigorous and thorough continuous-time analysis, deriving rates faster than the usual 1/t^2.
- Generalizes averaging schemes used to obtain the primal trajectory, extending prior work.
- Proposes a novel adaptive weighting scheme with provable convergence guarantees.
- Empirically compares the adaptive weighting scheme to restarting techniques and reports practical insights.
- Omits theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version, a significant limitation.
- Fails to cite relevant prior work (e.g., Attouch, Peypouquet and coauthors).
- Raises an important open question whether improved continuous rates persist after discretization.
- Presents original contributions in generalizing averaging schemes and introducing adaptive weights.
- Makes a significant contribution to accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization.
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and rigorous, easy-to-follow proofs.
- Overall high-quality work with solid analysis and useful empirical comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, requiring a more thorough comparison with existing methods and addressing the discretization/rates question.
