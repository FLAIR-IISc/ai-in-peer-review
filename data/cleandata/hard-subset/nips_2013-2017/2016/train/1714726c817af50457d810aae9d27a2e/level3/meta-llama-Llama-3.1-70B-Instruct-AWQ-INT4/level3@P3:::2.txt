This paper proposes an extension to the ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent algorithm with a generalized averaging scheme and introduces a novel adaptive averaging strategy for discretization. The authors provide a unified study of the ODE using a Lyapunov function and give sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate. The paper also proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of the adaptive averaging heuristic, which is a novel approach to accelerating the convergence of Mirror Descent algorithms. The authors provide a clear intuition behind the heuristic and demonstrate its effectiveness through numerical experiments. The experiments show that the adaptive averaging heuristic compares favorably to existing restarting heuristics and provides a significant improvement in some cases.

However, the paper has some limitations. The extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited use, and the authors only consider the quadratic-rate case. Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable. Additionally, the adaptive averaging heuristic is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof, making it effectively a heuristic.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical foundations. The numerical experiments are thorough and provide a good understanding of the performance of the adaptive averaging heuristic. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, which would help to clarify the practical advantage of the proposed heuristic.

Overall, the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of optimization and provides a new perspective on accelerating the convergence of Mirror Descent algorithms. However, further research is needed to fully understand the theoretical foundations of the adaptive averaging heuristic and to explore its applications in different contexts.
