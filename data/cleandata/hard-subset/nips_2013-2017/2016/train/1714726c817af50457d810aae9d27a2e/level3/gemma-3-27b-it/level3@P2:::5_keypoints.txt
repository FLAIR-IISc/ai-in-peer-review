- Proposes a unified Lyapunov-based framework for accelerated descent dynamics in constrained convex optimization.  
- Represents accelerated methods as coupled dual and primal dynamics governed by weight functions that determine averaging.  
- Provides sufficient conditions for convergence (Theorem 2, Corollary 1) with rigorous Lyapunov analysis.  
- Introduces a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights based on Lyapunov decrease.  
- Theoretical justification for adaptive averaging and extension to a broader family of Accelerated Mirror Descent dynamics.  
- Connects to existing continuous-time ODE interpretations of acceleration (e.g., Su et al., Krichene et al.).  
- Notes a practical application/connection to replicator dynamics in the simplex-constrained case.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting heuristics across several objective types.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically clear with mostly consistent notation.  
- Experiments are limited in scope, focused mainly on the simplex-constrained case and low diversity of problem settings.  
- The adaptive heuristic requires tuning (initial 'a' and discrete update choices) and lacks detailed sensitivity analysis.  
- Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art adaptive optimizers (e.g., Adam) and more sophisticated baselines.  
- Does not discuss or quantify the computational overhead of adaptively computing weights.  
- Core framework builds on prior dual-primal coupling analyses and is not a fundamentally new perspective beyond adaptive averaging.  
- Overall assessment: solid, theoretically rigorous contribution with a promising adaptive heuristic; recommends broader experiments and comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept (6/10), leaning stronger accept if weaknesses are addressed.
