- Studies convex minimization via continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics over closed convex sets.
- Analyzes a broad family of AMD dynamics parameterized by two positive continuous weight functions.
- Provides sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate.
- Proposes a simple, natural adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the convergence rate.
- Shows the adaptive reweighting guarantees to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.
- Technical development closely extends Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) but yields practically relevant improvements.
- Paper is well-written with a simple, elegant mathematical argument accessible to readers familiar with prior work.
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates substantial speed-ups on strongly/weakly convex quadratics, linear functions, and KL divergence.
- High potential practical impact due to connection with widely used Nesterov-style methods.
- Main novelty is the adaptive averaging heuristic—simple to implement and broadly applicable.
- Major weakness: no analysis of the discretized (algorithmic) process to confirm preserved rates in practice.
- Suggested follow-ups: include discretization analysis and explore connections to Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the discretization analysis and suggested investigations.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
