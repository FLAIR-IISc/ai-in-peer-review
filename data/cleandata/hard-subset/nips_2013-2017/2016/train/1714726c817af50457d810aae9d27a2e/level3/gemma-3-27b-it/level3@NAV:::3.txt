## Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

This paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization, building upon the primal-dual ODE framework established by Krichene et al. The central contribution lies in exploring and generalizing averaging schemes for the primal trajectory obtained from the mirror of the dual variable. The authors provide theoretical convergence rates under specific conditions on the averaging coefficients (Theorem 2), demonstrating faster convergence than the standard 1/t^2 rate in the continuous setting. Furthermore, they propose an adaptive weighting scheme with provable guarantees and empirically compare it to established restarting techniques, finding competitive performance. 

The paper is well-written and clearly presents a sophisticated analysis of the accelerated dynamics. The rigorous derivation of convergence rates in the continuous setting is a key strength. The connection to replicator dynamics as a specific instance of their framework provides a concrete example and highlights the broader applicability of the proposed approach. The adaptive averaging heuristic is a sensible and promising extension, and the experiments demonstrate its potential.

However, a significant weakness is the lack of theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm. While an empirical evaluation is provided, the absence of convergence guarantees for the discrete case limits the practical impact of the work. Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work. Specifically, the authors should cite foundational contributions by Attouch, Peypouquet, and co-authors (e.g., their work on fast convex optimization via inertial dynamics with Hessian-driven damping and on fast convergence of inertial gradient-like systems) which directly address similar dynamics and convergence analyses.  The connection to these works, and how this paper extends them, should be made explicit.

An important open question remains: do the improved continuous convergence rates persist after discretization?  While the authors acknowledge this implicitly, explicitly discussing this challenge and potential avenues for addressing it would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Rigorous theoretical analysis of the continuous-time dynamics.
*   Novel generalization of averaging schemes with convergence guarantees.
*   Competitive empirical performance of the adaptive weighting scheme.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm.
*   Missing citations to relevant work by Attouch, Peypouquet, and co-authors.
*   Open question regarding the preservation of improved rates after discretization not fully addressed.



**Overall:** Despite the weaknesses, I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution to the understanding of accelerated optimization dynamics. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically addressing the missing citations and acknowledging the open question of discrete convergence more explicitly.
