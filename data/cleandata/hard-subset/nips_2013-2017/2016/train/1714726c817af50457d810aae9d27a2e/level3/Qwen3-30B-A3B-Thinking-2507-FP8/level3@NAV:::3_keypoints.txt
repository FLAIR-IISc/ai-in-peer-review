- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, extending Krichene et al.'s primal-dual ODE framework.
- Introduces a generalized averaging scheme that weights the mirror of the dual variable to form the primal trajectory.
- Provides sufficient continuous-time conditions guaranteeing convergence rates faster than the standard 1/t^2.
- Theorem 2 formalizes these improved convergence rates under appropriate averaging coefficients.
- Proposes an adaptive weighting heuristic that provably preserves the improved convergence rate.
- Empirically demonstrates the adaptive heuristic outperforms existing restarting techniques across multiple problems.
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous, well-executed, and clearly connected to prior accelerated-method literature.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and offers valuable insights into continuous-to-discrete optimization links.
- Major limitation: theoretical analysis of the discrete (algorithmic) version is omitted.
- Important prior work (Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors) is not explicitly cited and should be acknowledged.
- Open question remains whether improved continuous-time rates persist after discretization, limiting practical impact.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address discrete-time guarantees and add the relevant citations.
