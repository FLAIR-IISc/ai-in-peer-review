- Paper studies accelerated mirror descent with generalized averaging, parameterizing dynamics by w(t) and η(t), deriving Lyapunov conditions for an O(1/r(t)) rate and a primal view linked to replicator dynamics.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that locally decreases the Lyapunov derivative and, when discretized with r(t)=t^2, provably preserves the O(1/k^2) rate for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Experiments compare adaptive averaging to gradient/speed restarts and show consistent improvements on toy problems.  

Quality:
- Continuous-time analysis (ineq. (3) and Theorem 2) appears sound; the second energy function construction is insightful.  
- Several derivations are hard to parse; equations between lines 33–34 and 46–47 need intuitive/geometric explanation (role of a(t)=w/W and averaged flow).  
- In the discrete section, do not call O(1/k^2) “quadratic convergence” since that term implies superlinear Newton-like rates.  

Clarity:
- Paper is hard to follow; add concise background on mirror maps, Bregman divergences, and notation.  
- Define all abbreviations (e.g., AMD_{w,η}, MD_p, E_r, L_r) on first use and include a glossary/table of symbols.  
- Figure 1’s “solid line” reference is ambiguous—use distinct colors or dotted lines and clarify the primal trajectory.  

Originality:
- Adapting averaging weights to control Lyapunov decrease is novel relative to restart heuristics and complements ODE/variational views.  
- Relation to adaptive methods (Adagrad, Adam) is only briefly mentioned; provide deeper comparison on objectives (convex vs nonconvex), stochasticity, and per-coordinate adaptivity.  

Significance / Experiments:
- Heuristic could be impactful for constrained convex optimization if scalable; current experiments are limited to R^3 toy problems.  
- Add higher-dimensional benchmarks (e.g., n ≥ 10^3 simplex, box/L1 constraints), report wall-clock and iteration metrics, and compare against strong baselines (FISTA with adaptive restart, mirror-prox variants).  
- Discuss computational overhead of adapting a_k and sensitivity/tuning of hyperparameters (β, s).  

Pros:
- Provides a clear theoretical guarantee preserving non-strongly convex O(1/k^2) rate.  
- Conceptually simple, principled heuristic motivated by Lyapunov analysis.  
- Connects to replicator dynamics and energy-based interpretations.  

Cons:
- Major clarity and notation barriers; key derivation steps lack intuition.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and does not demonstrate scalability.  
- Shallow discussion of relations to modern adaptive optimization methods.
