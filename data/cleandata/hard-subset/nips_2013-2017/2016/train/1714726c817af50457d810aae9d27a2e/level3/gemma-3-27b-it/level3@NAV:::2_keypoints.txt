- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic within Mirror Descent that adjusts weights based on Lyapunov function decrease.  
- Provides continuous-time analysis, claims quadratic convergence in discrete time, and includes preliminary numerical experiments.  
- Situates work in prior literature connecting Nesterov acceleration to ODEs, notably Su et al. and the authors' own NIPS [11].  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is presented but appears straightforward and incremental.  
- Identifies a connection to replicator dynamics and offers energy function interpretation and a primal reformulation for deeper insight.  
- Strength: the adaptive averaging idea is novel, intuitive, and provides theoretical insight into accelerated dynamics.  
- Major originality concern: substantial parts of the theoretical framework (Lyapunov approach, discretization) closely mirror prior work [11].  
- Theoretical grounding is weak: no rigorous discrete-time convergence proof beyond continuous-time analysis, leaving the method largely heuristic.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and do not demonstrate practical advantage or generalizability, especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Missing a critical comparative analysis against established averaging schemes used with Nesterov-style algorithms.  
- Recommendation: reject, with encouragement to extend experiments, prove robust convergence in discrete time, and provide strong comparisons to related methods.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel adaptive averaging and added understanding of dynamics; rejection arguments: weak guarantees, reliance on prior work, limited experiments, and incremental contribution.
