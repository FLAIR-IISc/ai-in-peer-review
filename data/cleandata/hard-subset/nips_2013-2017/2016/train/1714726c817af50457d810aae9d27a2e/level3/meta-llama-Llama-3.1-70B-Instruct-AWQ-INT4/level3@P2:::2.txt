This paper proposes an extension of the ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and presents a novel adaptive averaging strategy for its discretization. The authors provide a unified study of the ODE using a Lyapunov function and derive sufficient conditions for the convergence rate. They also demonstrate the applicability of their approach by accelerating the replicator dynamics.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their methods and results. The use of a Lyapunov function to analyze the convergence rate is a common technique in the field, and the authors apply it effectively to their ODE. The derivation of the sufficient conditions for the convergence rate is also well-done.

One of the main strengths of the paper is the proposal of the adaptive averaging strategy, which is a novel and intuitive approach. The authors provide a clear explanation of the idea behind the strategy and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The extension from simple to generalized averaging is relatively straightforward and does not add significant new insights. The authors only consider the quadratic-rate case, and achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable. Additionally, the analysis of the energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, and reduction to replicator dynamics, while improving understanding, offers limited advancement for machine learning.

Furthermore, the adaptive averaging strategy is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof, making it effectively a heuristic. The authors do not provide a comparison to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, so the practical advantage of their approach is unclear. The experiments are also limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and are not convincing for general or strongly convex cases.

In the rebuttal, the authors commit to adding high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, providing a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementing the method on Nesterov's cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparing it to other adaptive averaging schemes. These additions would significantly strengthen the paper and address some of the limitations mentioned above.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria of quality, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their methods and results, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The adaptive averaging strategy is a novel and intuitive approach that adds to the existing literature on accelerated Mirror Descent.

However, the paper could be improved by addressing the limitations mentioned above and providing a more comprehensive analysis of the adaptive averaging strategy. The authors should also consider providing more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in different settings.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and intuitive adaptive averaging strategy for accelerated Mirror Descent.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of their methods and results, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
