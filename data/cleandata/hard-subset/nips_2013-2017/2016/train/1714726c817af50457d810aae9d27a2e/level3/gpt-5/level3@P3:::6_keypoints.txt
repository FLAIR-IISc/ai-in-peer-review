- Paper studies accelerated mirror descent via generalized averaging, decoupling dual Z accumulating gradients at rate η(t) and primal X as weighted average with weights w(t).
- Uses Lyapunov Lr = r(t)(f(X)−f*) + Dψ*(Z,z*) to derive sufficient conditions on η and w guaranteeing rate 1/r(t), unifying prior continuous-time acceleration analyses.
- Derives a primal-only form and shows replicator dynamics on the simplex can be accelerated by the averaging scheme.
- Main algorithmic contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic choosing a(t)=w/W to decrease the Lyapunov derivative and preserve rates without strong convexity.
- Provides a discrete-time realization (Algorithm 1) and empirical comparisons to adaptive restart heuristics showing consistent gains.
- Strength: conceptual unification with clean sufficient conditions recovering known rates including O(1/t^2).
- Strength: novel adaptive averaging heuristic with monotonicity guarantees that typical restart heuristics lack.
- Strength: clear links to mirror descent and replicator dynamics; useful primal representation.
- Strength: continuous-time theory is solid and a discrete scheme with preserved quadratic rate is claimed (details in supplement).
- Weakness: exposition is hard to follow in places; some steps and abbreviations lack intuition and early definitions.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional R^3 toy problems, lacking evidence of scalability to higher dimensions or practical constrained ML tasks.
- Weakness: little practical guidance on choosing β, step sizes, regularizers, and no analysis of stability under noisy/stochastic gradients.
- Quality: continuous-time analysis and Lyapunov conditions appear technically sound; discrete-time proofs are relegated to the supplement and should be in the main text.
- Quality: the proxy test in Algorithm 1 (using f(x̃(k+1)) ≤ f(x̃(k))) to infer the sign of ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩ needs justification.
- Clarity request: add background and notation table and define acronyms/variables (AMD, MD, r(t), w, η, mirror map, Bregman divergence) at first use.
- Clarity request: provide a short cheat-sheet of frequently used identities and clarify Figure 1 with distinct line styles/colors.
- Requested intuition: explanation of the Lyapunov derivative bound leading to condition (2) of Theorem 2 (provided in review).
- Requested intuition: explanation of the energy derivative in Theorem 3 and how Er non-increase follows (provided in review).
- Originality: adaptive averaging to preserve Lyapunov decrease is novel and distinct from adaptive restarts; connection to replicator dynamics is a nice byproduct.
- Significance: promising if the heuristic scales and is robust, but current evidence is limited to small-scale examples.
- Relation to adaptive methods: contrast with Adagrad/Adam—this method adapts averaging weights for Lyapunov control, whereas Adagrad/Adam adapt per-coordinate learning rates; discuss potential synergies and what noise breaks in proofs.
- Questions for authors: reliability of the discrete proxy in Algorithm 1, sensitivity/backtracking options, guidance for β/s, robustness under stochastic gradients, and per-iteration computational cost vs baselines.
- Requests to strengthen paper: add concise background/notation, include discrete-time theorem and proof sketch in main text, provide higher-dimensional experiments and wall-clock comparisons, and run ablations and sensitivity analyses.
- Pros for acceptance: clear conceptual contribution, new heuristic with theoretical guarantees beyond restarts, and appealing connections to established dynamics.
- Cons for acceptance: clarity issues, narrow experimental evidence, and missing discrete-time guarantees in the main text.
- Overall assessment: solid and original contribution; borderline accept contingent on improved exposition and stronger empirical validation at scale.
