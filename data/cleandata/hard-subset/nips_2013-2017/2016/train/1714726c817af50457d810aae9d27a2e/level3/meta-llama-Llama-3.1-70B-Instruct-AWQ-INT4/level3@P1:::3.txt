**Summary**

This paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings. The authors generalize the averaging schemes used to obtain the primal trajectory and provide convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients. They also propose an adaptive choice of averaging weights with provable convergence guarantees. The paper presents a thorough analysis of the continuous setting, deriving convergence rates faster than the usual 1/t^2. The authors then empirically compare the adaptive weighting scheme to restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Rigorous continuous analysis**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous analysis of the continuous setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Generalization of averaging schemes**: The authors generalize the averaging schemes used to obtain the primal trajectory, which is a useful extension of existing work.
3. **Adaptive weighting scheme**: The proposed adaptive weighting scheme is a novel contribution, and the authors provide provable convergence guarantees.
4. **Empirical comparison**: The empirical comparison with restarting techniques is a useful addition to the paper, providing insights into the practical performance of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Omitted theoretical analysis**: The paper omits the theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm, which is a significant limitation.
2. **Lack of prior work citation**: The reviewer notes that prior related work (Attouch, Peypouquet and coauthors) should be cited.
3. **Open question**: The paper raises the open question of whether the improved continuous rates persist after discretization, which is an important area for future research.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel generalization of averaging schemes and an adaptive weighting scheme with provable convergence guarantees. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the continuous setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Significance**

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization, particularly in the area of accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization. The proposed adaptive weighting scheme and the generalization of averaging schemes are useful extensions of existing work.
