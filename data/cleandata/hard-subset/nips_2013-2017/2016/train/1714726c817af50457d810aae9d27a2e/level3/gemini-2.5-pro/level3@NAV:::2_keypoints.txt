- Studies continuous-time dynamics for accelerated mirror descent, extending Krichene et al. (2015) with a generalized averaging scheme and Lyapunov analysis for convergence rates.
- Main contribution is a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for a discretized version of the dynamics, intended to improve over adaptive restarting.
- Heuristic intuition is clear and elegant: adapt averaging weights to accelerate decrease of the Lyapunov function.
- Preliminary experiments indicate the heuristic can outperform standard restarting techniques in some settings.
- Paper provides useful reformulations (primal representation, connection to replicator dynamics) that deepen theoretical understanding.
- Limited originality/significance beyond Krichene et al.; core tools (Lyapunov function, discretization) are directly adopted and extension feels incremental.
- Analysis focuses mainly on the established quadratic-rate case, limiting generality of theoretical results.
- Adaptive averaging lacks rigorous justification in discrete time and the paper does not provide a formal convergence proof for this central contribution.
- Empirical evaluation is narrow—confined to low-dimensional toy problems with quadratic and linear objectives—so practical efficacy on complex/high-dimensional problems is untested.
- Fails to compare the adaptive scheme against other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style methods, leaving practical advantage unclear.
- Overall: novel heuristic but incremental theoretical contribution and insufficient empirical/theoretical support make the work feel incomplete and of uncertain significance.
