- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent dynamics derived from the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration.  
- Heuristic adaptively reweights iterates and is guaranteed to dominate standard fixed-schedule averaging.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Empirical results back up the theoretical benefits of the heuristic.  
- Provides a unified ODE analysis using a Lyapunov function and gives sufficient conditions for decrease.  
- Presents a discrete version of the heuristic and compares it to restarting heuristics.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides adequate background; notation is consistent and mathematics are easy to follow.  
- Original contribution via a novel combination of convex optimization and ODE techniques.  
- Potentially significant: offers a new perspective on accelerated methods with practical utility.  
- Results advance the state of the art in convex optimization.  
- Argument for acceptance: solid theory, empirical support, novelty, clarity, and potential impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: close relation to Krichene et al., so contribution may seem incremental to some.  
- Argument against acceptance: incomplete analysis of the discretized process is a limitation.  
- Argument against acceptance: lacks comprehensive empirical comparison with other methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
