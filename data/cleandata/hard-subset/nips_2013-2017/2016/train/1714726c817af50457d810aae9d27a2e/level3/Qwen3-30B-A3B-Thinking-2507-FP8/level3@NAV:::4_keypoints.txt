- Studies a class of ODEs for accelerated descent dynamics with a generalized averaging scheme building on Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2015).
- Formulates generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w(t) and gradient accumulation rate η(t).
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on w(t) and η(t) that guarantee convergence rates, generalizing Nesterov results.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that computes weights along solution trajectories to speed up the Lyapunov decrease while preserving convergence guarantees.
- Technical analysis is rigorous; Lyapunov proofs are sound and Corollary 1 gives a constructive weight-selection method.
- Empirically validated on simplex-constrained problems (including replicator dynamics) with comprehensive numerical experiments.
- Adaptive method shows significant improvements over existing restart heuristics and preserves quadratic convergence rate while accelerating Lyapunov decrease.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail to reproduce results; connections to prior work are well-established.
- Novel insight that adaptive averaging can match empirical speedups of accelerated methods is a meaningful contribution.
- Main limitation: experiments are restricted to simplex-constrained problems with limited discussion of scalability to more complex or high-dimensional settings.
- Additional limitation: relationship between the adaptive heuristic and other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) is not explored.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution that meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance); recommendation to accept.
