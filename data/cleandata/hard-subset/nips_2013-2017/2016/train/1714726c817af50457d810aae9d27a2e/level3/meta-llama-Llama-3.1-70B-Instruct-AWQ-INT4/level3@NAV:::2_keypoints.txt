- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent dynamics, extending ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with generalized averaging and an adaptive discretization scheme.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and validates the method with numerical experiments.  
- Technically sound with clear derivations; claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation; figures and tables aid understanding.  
- Novelty lies in the adaptive averaging heuristic that speeds Lyapunov decrease; generalized averaging extension is trivial.  
- Results are potentially significant, offering a new perspective and possible improvements over existing algorithms.  
- Main weakness: heavy reliance on prior work, with Lyapunov function and discretization following earlier papers.  
- Main weakness: extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited practical use.  
- Main weakness: lacks comparison to other averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, obscuring practical advantages.  
- Main weakness: experiments limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives and not convincing for general or strongly convex cases.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel heuristic, theoretical guarantees, and supportive numerical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: heavy dependence on prior work, trivial extension, missing comparisons, and limited experiments.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: add high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, provide a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implement on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and include comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes.
