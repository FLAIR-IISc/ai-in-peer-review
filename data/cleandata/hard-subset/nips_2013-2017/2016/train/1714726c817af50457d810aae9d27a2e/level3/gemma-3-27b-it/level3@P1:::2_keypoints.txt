- Studies accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization via coupled dual/primal variables and averaging; introduces generalized Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) and an adaptive averaging heuristic for weights.  
- Provides sufficient convergence conditions via Lyapunov analysis and applies the approach to derive an accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Experiments show competitive performance against adaptive restarting heuristics but are limited in scope.  
- Builds on prior continuous-time/Nesterov ODE work and appropriately cites [20,11].  
- Much of the Lyapunov framework, AMD analysis, and discretization closely follow the authors' prior NIPS 2016 paper [11], reducing core novelty.  
- Generalizing from fixed to variable averaging is relatively straightforward, and focusing only on quadratic convergence rate limits broader applicability.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights based on Lyapunov decrease to accelerate progress.  
- Strength: primal representation analysis and the accelerated replicator dynamics derivation are valuable contributions; energy function analysis offers another perspective.  
- Weakness: theoretical grounding of the adaptive heuristic is incomplete; discretization approximations are not rigorously addressed, leaving it effectively a heuristic.  
- Weakness: no comparison to existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style methods, making practical benefits uncertain.  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale (toy quadratic/linear objectives) and do not convincingly demonstrate consistent improvement, especially in strongly convex settings.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments needed: expanded experiments, rigorous convergence proof for discretization, application to higher-order methods, and comparisons to other schemes.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage resubmission after addressing theoretical gaps and strengthening empirical evaluation.
