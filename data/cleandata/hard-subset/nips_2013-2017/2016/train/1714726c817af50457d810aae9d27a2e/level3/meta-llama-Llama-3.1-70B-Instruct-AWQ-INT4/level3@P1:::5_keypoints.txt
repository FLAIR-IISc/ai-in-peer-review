- Proposes a novel family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by two positive continuous weight functions w and η.  
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on w and η for achieving desired convergence rates.  
- Provides a thorough, clear analysis showing when the Lyapunov function decreases.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves the original algorithm’s convergence rate.  
- Empirical results indicate adaptive averaging can match or outperform adaptive restarting.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and technically clear.  
- Technical details and motivation are well-explained.  
- Novel use of a Lyapunov function and the adaptive averaging heuristic are notable contributions.  
- Claims potential for significant impact on optimization algorithms broadly.  
- Lacks a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison with related prior work.  
- May be too specialized for a general audience despite strong technical merits.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to additional analysis and broader comparisons.
