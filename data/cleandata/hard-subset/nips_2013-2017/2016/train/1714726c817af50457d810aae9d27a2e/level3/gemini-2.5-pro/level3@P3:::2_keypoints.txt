- Generalization of continuous-time AMD via gradient-accumulation η(t) and averaging weight w(t), with Lyapunov-based conditions guaranteeing O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Main contribution: an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function and has a continuous-time guarantee preserving the convergence rate.  
- Additional analyses: energy-function interpretation (potential + kinetic) and a primal-space formulation revealing an accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Empirical claim: discrete adaptive averaging can outperform standard restarting heuristics on low-dimensional problems.  
- Strength: novel, intuitive adaptive averaging grounded in Lyapunov analysis that preserves theoretical rate.  
- Strength: improved theoretical insight via the energy interpretation and replicator-accelerated connection.  
- Strength: clear writing and well-structured mathematical derivations.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty — framework and Lyapunov structure closely inherit prior NIPS work and generalization to generic w(t) seems trivial.  
- Weakness: claims of faster rates are unsupported; achieving them would require higher-order methods outside the framework.  
- Weakness: main contribution lacks a discrete-time convergence proof; Algorithm 1 is essentially a heuristic tied to discretization approximations.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional toy problems, leaving practical significance for ML unclear.  
- Weakness: missing important baseline comparisons to other adaptive schemes for Nesterov-style methods.  
- Acceptance arguments: introduces a novel heuristic, is grounded in Lyapunov analysis, and provides new theoretical connections.  
- Rejection arguments: limited originality, no discrete convergence proof, weak empirical validation, and missing baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: paper is interesting but incomplete — needs convergence proof and stronger experiments/comparisons; as submitted, weaknesses outweigh strengths.
