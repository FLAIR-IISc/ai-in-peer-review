- Paper studies continuous-time Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) coupling dual gradient accumulation η(t) with primal weighted averaging w(t).  
- Presents a Lyapunov-based analysis giving sufficient conditions on w(t), η(t) to guarantee convergence rate 1/r(t).  
- Framework recovers and generalizes prior accelerated methods, including accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Proposes a novel "adaptive averaging" heuristic that greedily chooses w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function.  
- The adaptive heuristic is proven to preserve the O(1/t^2) rate for non-strongly convex functions.  
- Provides a discrete-time version of the algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation on low-dimensional, simplex-constrained problems comparing to standard AMD and two restarting heuristics.  
- Strength: originality — adaptive averaging is a novel alternative to restarting heuristics with a unified continuous-time view.  
- Strength: strong theory — technically sound Lyapunov analysis and a meaningful convergence-rate guarantee.  
- Strength: clear motivation and useful concrete example (accelerated replicator dynamics).  
- Weakness: presentation is dense and may be hard to follow for non-experts; needs more intuition and background.  
- Weakness: specific derivations (Lyapunov Lr and inequality (3), Theorem 2) need more explanatory intuition.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to toy R^3 problems and do not establish scalability or practical significance.  
- Weakness: related-work discussion on adaptive methods (Adagrad, Adam) is brief and should contrast philosophies more deeply.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically-grounded heuristic with preserved O(1/t^2) rate and unified analysis.  
- Against acceptance: limited practical validation and clarity issues may limit impact for the conference audience.  
- Recommendation: add higher-dimensional experiments, expand intuition around Lyapunov-based derivations, and clarify relation to other adaptive optimizers.  
- Minor: figures should use distinct line styles in addition to colors for clarity and black-and-white printing.
