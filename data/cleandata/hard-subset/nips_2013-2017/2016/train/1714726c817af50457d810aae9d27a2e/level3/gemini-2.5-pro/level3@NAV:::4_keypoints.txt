- Studies generalized accelerated mirror descent as continuous-time ODEs parameterized by averaging weight w(t) and learning rate η(t).
- Main theoretical result: Lyapunov-based conditions on w(t), η(t) that guarantee a target convergence rate 1/r(t).
- Proposes an "adaptive averaging" heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function while preserving the 1/r(t) rate.
- Unified analysis provides an elegant, general Lyapunov framework for a broad class of accelerated dynamics.
- Adaptive averaging is novel and theoretically guaranteed to preserve convergence rate, unlike many restarting schemes.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and connects cleanly to prior work (e.g., Su et al., Krichene et al.).
- Numerical experiments show promising performance and favorable comparison to established restarting methods.
- Experimental validation is limited to low-dimensional, synthetic problems (simplex in R^3).
- Practical advantages are not convincingly demonstrated on larger-scale, real-world machine-learning tasks.
- The continuous-time analysis is rigorous but the discrete-time algorithm link is underdeveloped.
- Would benefit from a discrete Lyapunov derivation or more explicit discrete-time guarantees for the proposed algorithm.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and theoretically grounded contribution deserving acceptance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel unified framework, adaptive heuristic with stronger guarantees, technical soundness and clarity.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation on large-scale or realistic tasks.
