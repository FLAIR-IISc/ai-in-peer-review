- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, extending Krichene et al.'s continuous-time primal-dual ODE framework.  
- Generalizes averaging in accelerated mirror descent and gives sufficient conditions on weight functions w(t) and η(t) for target convergence rates.  
- Main theoretical contribution (Theorem 2): Lyapunov-based convergence rates showing carefully designed averaging can beat the standard 1/t^2.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically selects weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease and claims it preserves quadratic convergence in discrete-time (discrete analysis relegated to supplement).  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous, technically sound, and clearly presented.  
- The connection to replicator dynamics for simplex constraints is compelling and broadens applications to game theory and biology.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms existing restarting heuristics and yields significant improvements in some cases.  
- Major weakness: discrete-time analysis of the adaptive averaging algorithm is omitted from the main paper, a significant gap given practical relevance.  
- Related work is incomplete—fails to adequately compare to prior continuous-time accelerated methods (e.g., Attouch, Peypouquet, Redont 2016).  
- The critical question of whether improved continuous-time rates survive discretization is raised but not resolved.  
- Quality assessment: strong continuous-time contribution but weakened by lack of discrete-time results.  
- Originality/significance: novel averaging generalization and adaptive heuristic, but practical impact limited until discrete-time performance is demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: add full discrete-time analysis, expand related work comparisons, and thoroughly address discretization effects to strengthen the paper.
