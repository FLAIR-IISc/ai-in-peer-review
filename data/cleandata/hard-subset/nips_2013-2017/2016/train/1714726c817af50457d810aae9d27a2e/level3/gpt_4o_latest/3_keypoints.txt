- Paper studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, extending Krichene et al.'s ODE view of Nesterov.
- Main contribution (Theorem 2): generalized averaging scheme for the primal trajectory yielding convergence rates faster than 1/t^2 under specific averaging-coefficient conditions.
- Introduces an adaptive weighting heuristic with provable convergence guarantees.
- Empirical results show the adaptive averaging heuristic can outperform restarting techniques in some cases.
- Numerical experiments and visualizations are well-designed and illuminate method behavior.
- Theoretical analysis of the continuous-time ODE family is rigorous, using Lyapunov/energy function arguments.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and accessible despite complex mathematics.
- Work is relevant to large-scale machine learning and optimization communities.
- Lacks theoretical analysis for the discrete (practical) algorithm, limiting applicability.
- Related-work discussion is incomplete; key contributions by Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors are omitted.
- Adaptive heuristic’s empirical gains are not uniformly strong; limitations and extensions need more exploration.
- Faster rates rely on specific averaging-coefficient conditions; generality and broader applicability are unclear.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address discrete-theory gap and expand related-work discussion.
