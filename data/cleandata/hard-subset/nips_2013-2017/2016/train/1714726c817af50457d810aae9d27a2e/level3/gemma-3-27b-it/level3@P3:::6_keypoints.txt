- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent in constrained convex optimization, analyzing continuous-time primal–dual dynamics.  
- Shows via a Lyapunov argument that carefully chosen averaging can preserve the original quadratic convergence rate for non-strongly convex objectives.  
- Experiments compare the heuristic against existing restarting methods, but validation scope is limited.  
- Theoretical contribution appears sound and provides clear sufficient conditions linking averaging to convergence rate.  
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is novel and may offer advantages over restarting heuristics that lose theoretical guarantees.  
- Applying the framework to accelerate replicator dynamics demonstrates broader applicability.  
- If scalable, the method could improve first-order optimization efficiency for large-scale ML problems.  
- Presentation lacks clarity: introduction jumps into technical details without sufficient motivation or background.  
- Many abbreviations (e.g., AMD, ODE) are used without initial definitions, hindering readability.  
- Key equations (lines 33–34 and 46–47) lack intuitive and geometric interpretation, especially of the mirror map and its gradient.  
- Discussion of connections to adaptive nonconvex methods (Adagrad, Adam) is too brief and needs deeper comparison and potential synergy analysis.  
- Experiments are restricted to R^3 (toy setting); scalability to higher dimensions (e.g., R^10–R^20) is not demonstrated.  
- Figure 1’s legend/line styles are ambiguous; clearer plotting (dotted lines or distinct colors) is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: valuable Lyapunov-based theory, novel heuristic, and demonstrable broader applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, limited experimental validation, and superficial related-work discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting but incomplete; recommend rejection with revision suggestions to improve clarity, add intuition, expand experiments to higher dimensions, and deepen discussion of adaptive optimization links.
