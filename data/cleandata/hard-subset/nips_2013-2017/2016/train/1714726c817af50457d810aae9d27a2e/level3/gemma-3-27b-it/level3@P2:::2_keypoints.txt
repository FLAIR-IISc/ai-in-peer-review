- Studies accelerated mirror descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, extending prior Lyapunov-based analysis (Krichene et al. NIPS 2016).  
- Core contribution: a generalized averaging scheme parameterized by weight functions and a novel adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Provides sufficient conditions for convergence, demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics, and reports experiments suggesting competitive or superior performance to adaptive restarting.  
- Well-motivated connection to Nesterov’s accelerated methods and continuous-time ODEs, with clear literature review and rigorous mathematical treatment.  
- Lyapunov-based analysis is technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Replicator dynamics example offers a concrete illustration of the framework.  
- Contribution feels incremental; the generalization to AMDw,η appears straightforward and largely follows standard techniques.  
- Emphasis on quadratic convergence rate in discretization limits broader applicability of results.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is intuitive but remains largely heuristic due to discretization approximations and lacks a complete convergence proof.  
- Energy function analysis, primal representation, and replicator application are insightful but not substantial advances for the ML community.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (toy quadratic/linear objective in R^3) and not convincing as evidence of general advantage.  
- Missing comparisons to other averaging schemes (e.g., Flammarion & Bach) and established adaptive methods (e.g., Adam, Duchi et al.).  
- Trajectory visualizations are informative but lack quantitative performance metrics.  
- Reviewer leans toward rejection: technically competent but incremental, heuristic unproven, and experiments insufficient; revisions with proofs, broader experiments, and comparisons could change the assessment.
