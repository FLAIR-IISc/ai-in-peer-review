- Studies accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics for constrained convex optimization, parameterized by weight functions w and η controlling dual gradient accumulation and primal averaging.
- Provides sufficient conditions on w and η to guarantee desired convergence rates via a Lyapunov analysis.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate Lyapunov decrease, shown empirically to match or outperform adaptive restarting.
- Technical analysis is rigorous and the Lyapunov conditions are clearly stated and justified.
- Thorough literature review connecting Nesterov acceleration and continuous-time ODEs.
- Numerical experiments are well-designed, compare adaptive averaging to restarting heuristics, and clearly present results.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible to readers with appropriate mathematical background.
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is well-motivated and a promising direction for further research.
- Assumes high mathematical sophistication, which may limit accessibility for non-experts.
- Lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Numerical experiments are limited to a few specific examples, leaving generalizability unclear.
