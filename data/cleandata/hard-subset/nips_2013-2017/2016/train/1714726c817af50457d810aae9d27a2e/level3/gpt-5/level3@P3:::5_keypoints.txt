- Paper studies a broad family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs parameterized by η(t) and w(t), uses a Lyapunov Lr to give simple sufficient conditions yielding f(X(t))−f* = O(1/r(t)) and recovers O(1/t^2).  
- Provides an equivalent primal ODE, connects to prior continuous-time acceleration work, and shows averaging can accelerate replicator dynamics on the simplex.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that chooses a(t)=w/W to decrease the Lyapunov derivative while preserving convergence rate, and gives a discrete-time algorithm with a quadratic-rate (r=t^2) guarantee.  
- Empirically on simplex-constrained toy problems adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting heuristics and yields more monotone Lyapunov decrease.  

Strengths:
- Clean Lyapunov framework with transparent sufficient conditions and illustrative examples (e.g., r(t)=t^p).  
- General applicability to constrained convex problems via mirror maps and a primal representation that maintains feasibility.  
- Novel, principled adaptive-averaging heuristic with continuous-time guarantees and a discrete-time quadratic-rate result.  
- Practical relevance: competitive empirical performance and more stable energy/Lyapunov behavior than restarting.  
- Interesting and original link to accelerating replicator dynamics.

Weaknesses:
- Experiments are small-scale (R^3 simplex) and may not generalize to higher dimensions, other constraints, composite objectives, or real ML tasks.  
- Discrete-time analysis and key proofs are deferred to the supplement; stability and rate preservation beyond p=2 or nonsmooth f are not addressed.  
- Some algorithmic details (e.g., βmax, choices of R and Dψ, step-size policy) are terse or ambiguous, harming reproducibility.  
- Limited comparative baselines — no line-search/monotone accelerations or adaptive-step methods in constrained settings.  
- No theoretical guarantees for accelerated linear rates under strong convexity, despite empirical success.

Quality:
- Continuous-time analysis appears careful and sound with well-motivated Lyapunov/energy functions; discrete-time quadratic-rate claim is plausible but under-detailed in the main text.  
- Experiments qualitatively and quantitatively support claims but are limited in scale and variety.

Clarity:
- Generally well-written and organized with helpful derivations and examples.  
- Heavy notation and ambiguous parts in Algorithm 1; needs clearer mapping between continuous and discrete parameters and a practical “how to set η,w” recipe.

Originality:
- Adaptive averaging targeting the Lyapunov derivative and acceleration of replicator dynamics are novel contributions building on established continuous-time frameworks.

Significance:
- Framework could influence design of accelerated methods for constrained problems and offer a viable alternative to restarting, but impact depends on stronger discrete-time theory and broader empirical validation.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Expand discrete-time analysis and proofs in the main text and discuss extensions to strong convexity and composite objectives.  
- Provide practical guidance and sensitivity analysis for setting η(t), w(t), and β.  
- Scale experiments to higher dimensions and diverse constraint sets and include more baseline comparisons.  
- Clarify Algorithm 1, remove typos, define all symbols (R, Dψ, β), and detail restart baseline tuning.

Reviewer expertise:
- Limited expertise in continuous-time ODE analyses; more familiar with first-order methods and practical heuristics.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear, general Lyapunov framework and a simple adaptive-averaging heuristic with guarantees and promising empirical behavior; novel perspective likely to influence practice.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited experimental scope, incomplete discrete-time presentation in the main paper, and some clarity/parameterization issues.

Recommendation:
- Lean accept.
