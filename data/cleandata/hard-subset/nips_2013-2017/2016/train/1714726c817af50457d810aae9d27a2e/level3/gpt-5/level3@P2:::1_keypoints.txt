- Studies accelerated mirror descent via a continuous-time two-parameter family AMDw,η where dual Z accumulates gradients at rate η(t) and primal X is a weighted average with weights w(t).
- Introduces Lyapunov Lr and energy Er functions and gives simple sufficient conditions on w, η to guarantee convergence rate 1/r(t), recovering t−2 and more schedules.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that decreases the Lyapunov/energy functions while preserving the nominal convergence rate.
- Derives a primal reformulation that preserves feasibility and works out an accelerated replicator dynamics example on the simplex.
- Provides a discrete algorithm (quadratic rate claimed for r(t)=t^2) but the main discrete-time theorem and proof sketch are mostly in the supplement.
- Continuous-time analysis is clean, transparent, and generalizes known accelerated schedules; adaptive averaging dominance is precise in Lyapunov/energy sense.
- Discrete-time guarantees should be moved into the main text with conditions ensuring Lr,k monotonicity and the algorithmic rate bound.
- Assumes smooth convex f with Lipschitz gradients and smooth mirror map; lacks discussion of robustness to gradient noise or inexact mirror/prox steps.
- Experiments are fair and include restart baselines, with diagnostics supporting claims, but are mostly small-scale (R^3 simplex) and would benefit from larger-scale Euclidean tasks and wall-clock/oracle metrics.
- Writing is clear for readers familiar with ODE acceleration; figures and examples aid intuition.
- Suggested clarity fixes: move discrete theorem/algorithm into main text, clarify relationship between a(t), w(t), η(t), explain t0 initialization, and unify notation (x, x̃, ž, ψ, ψ*, Dψ).
- Originality: a nontrivial generalization of Krichene et al. with a new adaptive averaging mechanism and energy function; contributions are novel and practically relevant despite known ingredients.
- Significance: a simple, provably safe adaptive heuristic could impact practical acceleration methods; stronger discrete analysis and broader experiments would increase impact.
- Pros: unified Lyapunov analysis, simple provable adaptive averaging, accelerated replicator dynamics, empirical improvements and reduced oscillations versus restarts.
- Cons: discrete-time guarantees relegated to supplement, small-scale experiments, no treatment of stochastic/noisy gradients, unexplored relation to conjugate gradient/heavy-ball on quadratics.
- Key questions for authors: provide a main-text discrete theorem for Algorithm 1, sensitivity/tuning guidance for β and step size s, interaction with restarts, connection to Conjugate Gradient on quadratics, and behavior with stochastic/coordinate updates.
- Minor suggestions: add table linking r(t)↔a(t)↔w(t), explicitly state when Lyapunov dominance implies monotone f decrease, and tighten some references (years/venues).
- Overall recommendation: Accept (score 7) with moderate reviewer confidence (3/5).
