- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via ODEs.
- Builds on Krichene et al.'s ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights in the iterate average.
- Shows adaptive weights can be computed to ensure a Lyapunov function decreases each step.
- Proves the adaptive scheme preserves the quadratic convergence rate.
- Empirically outperforms fixed-schedule averaging and prior adaptive heuristics like restarting.
- Experiments across multiple problem classes show consistent improvements, especially for strongly convex problems.
- Practical impact: simple adaptive modification yields significant gains for widely used Nesterov methods.
- Strength: elegant, accessible mathematical argument building naturally on prior work.
- Strength: technically sound, complete experimental evaluation, and excellent clarity.
- Originality: simple yet effective extension of prior accelerated methods.
- Weakness: lacks thorough discrete-time (discretized) analysis to confirm preserved convergence rate.
- Weakness: fails to explore connections to Conjugate Gradient methods for strongly convex quadratics.
- Overall assessment: meets NIPS criteria and is recommended for acceptance despite minor gaps.
