- Extends Accelerated Mirror Descent via a primal–dual ODE using generalized averaging of the mirror dual with time-varying weights w(t) and η(t).
- Theorem 2: Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on w, η, r guaranteeing convergence rate 1/r(t).
- Choosing r(t)=t^p yields continuous-time convergence rates faster than the usual 1/t^2 for p>2.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging scheme that steepens Lyapunov decrease and has continuous-time guarantees.
- Connects the framework to prior ODE views of acceleration (Su–Boyd–Candès; Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan) and to mirror/replicator dynamics.
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous, well executed, and provides a transparent, unifying Lyapunov argument.
- Primal reformulation and examples (e.g., accelerated replicator dynamics) are illustrative and insightful.
- The adaptive weighting rule is simple and provably yields Lyapunov monotonicity in continuous time.
- Discrete-time theory is limited: no proof that faster continuous rates (p>2) survive first-order discretization.
- Open whether the adaptive scheme retains guarantees beyond the quadratic (1/k^2) regime in discrete time.
- Experiments are modest, low-dimensional, and lack sensitivity analyses and ablation studies.
- Related-work coverage should better cite and contrast Attouch–Peypouquet and other inertial/vanishing-viscosity ODE results.
- Suggestions: clarify discrete-time guarantees, attempt conditions for p>2 discretization, and expand experiments (higher dimension, step-size/β sensitivity, adaptive vs restarting ablations).
- Overall: novel and potentially impactful adaptive averaging within the AMD line, but discrete-time practicality and broader experimental validation remain open.
