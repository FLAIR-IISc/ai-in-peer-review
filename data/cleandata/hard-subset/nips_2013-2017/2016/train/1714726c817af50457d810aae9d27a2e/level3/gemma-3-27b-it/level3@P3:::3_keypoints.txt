- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization via a continuous-time ODE perspective, generalizing a primal trajectory based on averaging the mirror of the dual variable with convergence analysis under conditions on averaging coefficients.
- Introduces an adaptive weighting scheme with provable continuous-time convergence and shows comparable discrete performance to restarting techniques in experiments.
- Well-motivated connection to Nesterov’s acceleration and prior coupled primal-dual ODE formulations (e.g., Krichene et al.), using a Lyapunov approach to obtain improved rates beyond the usual 1/t^2 under certain conditions.
- Theoretical analysis in continuous time is rigorous, mathematically clear, and the derivations are well-structured.
- Empirical validation (including examples like accelerated replicator dynamics) supports the proposed adaptive averaging approach versus adaptive restarting.
- Major weakness: omission of a theoretical analysis for the discrete version of the algorithm beyond empirical results and the provided convergence rate guarantee.
- Practical concern: conditions required for the improved continuous convergence rates (notably those in Theorem 2) are difficult to verify and may limit applicability.
- Suggests adding citations to Attouch, Peypouquet, and coauthors (refs. 24–26) and linking to work on Hessian-driven damping to strengthen related work.
- Open question emphasized: whether the improved continuous-time rates persist after discretization — numerical evidence is insufficient and rigorous study is needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty (generalized averaging and adaptive weighting), theoretical rigor, clarity, empirical support, and significance to optimization.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing discrete analysis, questionable practical verifiability of conditions, and limited discussion of practical limitations.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include suggested citations and address discretization and verifiability concerns.
