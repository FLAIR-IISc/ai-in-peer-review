- Paper extends ODE dynamics for constrained convex optimization with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes an adaptive averaging strategy to improve discretization.
- Leverages a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions for convergence rates and applies the framework to accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD).
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that dynamically adjusts weights to improve Lyapunov decrease and preserves quadratic discrete-time convergence.
- Provides numerical experiments comparing the method to heuristics like adaptive restarting, showing advantages in strongly convex settings.
- Strength: offers a unified theoretical framework for generalized averaging in accelerated dynamics.
- Strength: adaptive averaging is novel, intuitive, and preserves discrete-time quadratic convergence.
- Strength: connects clearly to prior work on Nesterov-type methods and continuous-time dynamics.
- Strength: empirical comparisons indicate potential benefits in specific (strongly convex) cases.
- Weakness: originality limited — many elements are extensions or minor modifications of prior work, and practical advantages are unclear.
- Weakness: adaptive heuristic lacks rigorous theoretical guarantees beyond preserving the convergence rate; no explicit faster-rate proofs for specific cases.
- Weakness: incomplete comparative analysis — does not fully compare to other averaging schemes.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to toy/low-dimensional examples and lack real-world or high-dimensional validations.
- Weakness: some derivations (e.g., Lyapunov conditions) are dense and may be hard for non-experts; scope is narrowly theoretical.
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after adding experiments, proofs, and comparisons.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel adaptive heuristic, useful theoretical framework, and promising empirical results in specific cases.
- Arguments for rejection: limited originality/utility, insufficient theoretical guarantees, weak empirical validation, and incremental impact.
- Overall rating: 5/10 (borderline reject).
