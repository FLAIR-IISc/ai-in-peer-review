- Extends ODE-based accelerated mirror descent with a generalized averaging scheme and an adaptive averaging heuristic for discretization.  
- Establishes a continuous-time framework where dual variables accumulate gradients at rate η(t) and primal variables are weighted averages of the mirrored dual trajectory.  
- Main contribution is an adaptive heuristic that chooses weights to accelerate decrease of a Lyapunov function.  
- Strength: provides clear intuition for adaptive averaging and preserves quadratic convergence rates in continuous time.  
- Strength: unified Lyapunov framework offers useful theoretical perspective.  
- Strength: replicator dynamics example shows applicability to constrained optimization.  
- Limitation: extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and only treats the quadratic-rate case r(t)=t², already covered in prior work.  
- Limitation: adaptive averaging lacks a rigorous convergence proof after discretization, making it effectively a heuristic.  
- Limitation: experiments are limited to toy quadratic/linear objectives on the simplex and do not show practical advantage for general or strongly convex problems.  
- Limitation: no comparisons to existing adaptive averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, obscuring practical significance.  
- Originality is questionable since many elements follow directly from the authors' prior NIPS paper, with limited advancement for ML applications.  
- Focus on quadratic-rate case is restrictive; faster rates would require higher-order methods not addressed.  
- Post-rebuttal promises to add high-dimensional experiments, convergence proof, and comparisons are noted but not present in the submission.  
- Overall judgment: limited novelty, weak theoretical grounding for the key contribution, and insufficient experiments—does not meet acceptance threshold.
