- Paper studies accelerated mirror descent (AMD) ODEs parameterized by η(t) and w(t), gives Lyapunov framework with target rate r(t) and O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Introduces adaptive averaging: chooses a(t)=w/W online to maintain Lyapunov decrease and accelerate when possible.  
- Provides primal-only representation, examples (positive orthant, simplex), and derives accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Proposes a discrete-time adaptive-averaging algorithm (for r(t)=t^2) and compares empirically to restart heuristics on simplex problems.  
- Builds on continuous-time acceleration literature (Su et al., Wibisono et al.) and distinguishes adaptive averaging from restart methods.  
- Strength: clear unifying Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*) with transparent sufficient conditions.  
- Strength: general framework covering mirror maps on convex sets, with both primal/dual formulations and decreasing energy Er.  
- Strength: novel, simple adaptive averaging rule that preserves target rates and is discretizable.  
- Strength: accelerated replicator dynamics are a noteworthy application for game theory and related fields.  
- Strength: experiments show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarts, reduces oscillations, and yields monotone Lyapunov decrease.  
- Weakness: key Lyapunov condition depends on trajectory term ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩ which is hard to verify and may be sensitive to estimation noise.  
- Weakness: discrete-time guarantees for adaptive averaging are deferred to the supplement; main text lacks a succinct theorem and proof sketch with assumptions/constants.  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale (R^3 simplex), use only entropy mirror map, and miss higher-dimensional, other feasible sets, and stronger baselines.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis (β, step size s, cap on a_k) and missing ablations isolating adaptivity effects.  
- Weakness: no theoretical analysis shown for strongly convex cases (e.g., linear rates) despite empirical gains.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—some notation introduced mid-derivation, z* role not fully discussed; suggest a high-level algorithm box and parameter-selection recipe.  
- Quality: continuous-time analysis appears technically sound with careful existence/uniqueness handling and clear Lyapunov derivation.  
- Main limitation: trajectory-dependent condition and lack of robustness analysis under noise/discretization.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with helpful derivations; minor organizational fixes would improve accessibility.  
- Originality: adaptive averaging mechanism and generalized Lyapunov framework are novel contributions beyond restart-based heuristics.  
- Significance: framework could influence design/tuning of accelerated methods for constrained/mirror settings; replicator acceleration has independent interest.  
- Questions for authors: can adaptive averaging work with stochastic gradients in expectation; how to choose β, s, and a_k cap in practice; can strong convexity yield provable linear rates; how sensitive is performance to mirror map ψ?  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, general theoretical framework and simple adaptive heuristic with guarantees and promising empirical behavior.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discrete-time theory and experiments, and delicate trajectory-dependent conditions lacking robustness analysis.  
- Overall verdict: solid and promising contribution, positive leaning but contingent on clarifying discrete guarantees and expanding experiments.
