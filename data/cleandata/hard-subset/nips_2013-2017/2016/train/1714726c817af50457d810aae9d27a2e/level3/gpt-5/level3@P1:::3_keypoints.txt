- Studies accelerated first-order constrained convex optimization via a continuous-time primal–dual ODE with generalized averaging.  
- Defines dual Z integrating gradients at rate η(t) and primal X as weighted average of mirror map of Z with weights w(t).  
- Introduces Lyapunov Lr(X,Z,t)=r(t)(f(X)−f*)+Dψ*(Z,z*) and derives sufficient conditions on η and w to guarantee f(X(t))−f* ≤ O(1/r(t)).  
- Unifies and extends averaging-based views of acceleration (Krichene et al., Flammarion–Bach) and connects to inertial ODE literature (Su–Boyd–Candès; Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan).  
- Provides a primal representation of dynamics with explicit examples for the positive orthant and the simplex.  
- Shows replicator dynamics as mirror descent on the simplex and introduces an “accelerated replicator” via generalized AMD.  
- Proposes a second energy functional Er offering an interpretable potential/kinetic decomposition.  
- Key contribution: adaptive averaging heuristic that adapts a(t)=w(t)/W(t) to reduce the Lyapunov derivative while preserving continuous-time rate 1/r(t).  
- Presents a discrete-time Algorithm 1 claimed to preserve O(1/k^2) quadratic rate of accelerated schemes.  
- Empirically compares adaptive averaging to gradient and speed restarting; adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting on simplex-constrained problems, especially under strong convexity.  
- Continuous-time analysis is rigorous with clean existence/uniqueness arguments and transparent Lyapunov proofs.  
- Theorem 2’s general conditions are trajectory-dependent, but Corollary 1 gives tractable sufficient conditions and constructive recipes.  
- Framework supports rates beyond 1/t^2 (e.g., r(t)=t^p) and can in principle permit exponential r(t) when convexity allows; this point could be emphasized more.  
- Discrete-time analysis in the main text is limited; proofs are deferred to the supplement and the paper lacks a concise in-paper theorem with assumptions (step-size, L, parameter ranges, stability).  
- Experiments are illustrative but small-scale (R^3) and focused on simplex constraints; they do convincingly show monotonicity of Lr under adaptive averaging.  
- Paper is well written and organized; roles of w, η, and r are clearly explained.  
- Recommend clarifying the term “quadratic rate” (authors mean O(1/t^2)/O(1/k^2), not local superlinear convergence).  
- Suggest adding a practitioner’s section on choosing β, step size s, and diagnosing sign of ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩ to aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: generalizing averaging in AMD for prescribed rates via Lyapunov design is novel; adaptive averaging differs from standard restart triggers.  
- Accelerated replicator interpretation is original and likely of cross-disciplinary interest.  
- Significance: conceptually broadens acceleration viewpoint; practical heuristic is simple and competitive; potential to target faster continuous-time rates is intriguing.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear continuous-time theory, constructive conditions, new energy function, novel adaptive heuristic with guarantees, insightful examples, good exposition.  
- Cons for acceptance: minimal main-text discrete-time guarantees, limited-scale experiments, light practical guidance on parameter selection/robustness, limited discussion of related inertial ODE literature and strong-convexity linear rates.  
- Key suggestions/questions for authors: formalize continuous-time linear (exponential) rates under strong convexity (r(t)=e^{μt}) and whether adaptive averaging discretizes this; provide an in-paper theorem for Algorithm 1 with step-size/assumption details; expand experiments to larger scale and other constraint sets (ℓ1 ball, spectrahedron); analyze sensitivity to β and s and aim for parameter-free heuristic; discuss connections to inertial ODEs with vanishing damping (Attouch–Peypouquet–Redont).
