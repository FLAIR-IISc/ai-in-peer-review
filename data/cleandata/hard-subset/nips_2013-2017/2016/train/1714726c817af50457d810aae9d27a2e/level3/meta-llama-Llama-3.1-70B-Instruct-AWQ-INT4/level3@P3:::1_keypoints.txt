- Introduces a novel adaptive reweighting (adaptive averaging) scheme to accelerate first-order optimization methods.  
- Leverages the ODE interpretation of Nesterov acceleration (Krichene et al. 2015) to design the method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise mathematical arguments.  
- Introduces an energy function Er(t) that provides an insightful measure for analysis.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that computes weights w(t) along trajectories to preserve convergence rate while improving performance.  
- The heuristic is simple to implement and has an intuitive explanation.  
- Numerical experiments compare to restarting heuristics and show equal or improved performance in several cases.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis including sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.  
- Strengths: solid analysis, practical and significant adaptive heuristic, and well-designed experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the discretized (algorithmic) process.  
- Weakness: does not investigate connections between the heuristic and other methods (e.g., Conjugate Gradient).  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS; overall high quality (Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).
