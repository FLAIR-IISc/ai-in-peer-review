- Studies accelerated mirror-descent ODEs by decoupling a dual variable Z (accumulates gradients at rate η(t)) and a primal X (weighted average of mirrored Z with weights w(t)).  
- Uses a Lyapunov argument to derive sufficient conditions on η and w for a target convergence rate 1/r(t) and introduces an energy functional that decreases under those conditions.  
- Provides a useful corollary giving implementable scheduling schemes for η and w.  
- Connects and extends continuous-time acceleration (Su–Boyd–Candès), variational/Bregman Lagrangians (Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan), accelerated mirror descent, and restart heuristics.  
- Presents an accelerated replicator dynamics application on the simplex linking optimization and evolutionary dynamics.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights to further decrease the Lyapunov functional, with continuous-time guarantees.  
- Gives a discrete-time version that preserves the quadratic rate r(t)=t^2 and shows empirical gains over adaptive restarting.  
- Continuous-time analysis is careful (existence/uniqueness, clear Lyapunov construction) and the adaptive heuristic is well motivated.  
- Empirical section is informative but limited in scale and scope; robustness to step sizes, noise, and high-dimensional problems is unclear.  
- Clarity is generally good, but trajectory-dependent sign conditions need more intuition and practical recipes for choosing η, w, β, and t0.  
- Discretization and its assumptions are important but currently relegated to the supplementary material and should be surfaced more prominently.  
- Originality: the generalized averaging view and adaptive averaging heuristic are fresh and cohesive alternatives to restart-based acceleration.  
- Significance: adaptive averaging could become a practical alternative to restart heuristics and inform new discretizations/analyses.  
- Pros: general Lyapunov framework, simple adaptive heuristic with guarantees, replicator dynamics insight, empirical gains versus restart.  
- Cons: experiments are small-scale and limited to low-dimensional simplex, narrow baseline comparisons, light practical guidance for parameter choices, and discrete-time guarantees mainly for r(t)=t^2.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting work recommended for acceptance after expanding empirical evaluation and clarifying implementation guidance.
