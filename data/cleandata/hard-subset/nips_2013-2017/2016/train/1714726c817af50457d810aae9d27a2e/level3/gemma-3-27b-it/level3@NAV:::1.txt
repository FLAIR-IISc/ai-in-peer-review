## Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

This paper presents a novel adaptive averaging heuristic for accelerated descent methods, building upon the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration established by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015). The core idea is to dynamically reweight iterates based on the decrease of a Lyapunov function, guaranteeing convergence rates at least as good as standard fixed-schedule averaging. The paper convincingly argues that this simple adaptive modification can significantly improve performance, particularly in scenarios where fixed-weight schemes struggle.

The work is technically sound, and the mathematical analysis, while reliant on familiarity with the Krichene et al. framework, is presented clearly and concisely. The authors successfully extend prior work to a continuous-time setting and provide sufficient conditions for convergence. The Lyapunov argument is well-motivated, and the proposed heuristic is intuitive. The empirical evaluation demonstrates promising speed-ups across several functions, including a detailed analysis on the simplex-constrained case with replicator dynamics. The inclusion of comparisons against adaptive restarting (O’Donoghue & Candès, 2016) provides valuable context.

A notable strength is the clear connection to existing literature on accelerated methods and continuous-time dynamics. The authors appropriately position their contribution within the broader landscape of optimization algorithms, including references to Adagrad and Adam, highlighting the novelty of their approach. The potential practical impact is substantial, as Nesterov’s acceleration is widely employed. 

However, the paper would benefit from a deeper analysis of the *discretized* process. While the authors touch upon discretization in the context of Algorithm 1 and referencing Wibisono et al. (2016), a formal analysis demonstrating the preservation of convergence rates in the discrete setting would strengthen the theoretical foundation. Furthermore, the suggestion to investigate links between adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics is excellent and would provide further insights into the method’s behavior.  

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and well-motivated adaptive averaging heuristic.
*   Strong theoretical foundation based on Lyapunov arguments.
*   Promising empirical results across a diverse set of functions.
*   Clear writing and well-organized presentation.
*   Significant potential impact due to the widespread use of Nesterov's acceleration.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of a fully detailed analysis of the discretized process and its convergence rate.
*   The theoretical analysis heavily relies on the reader’s familiarity with prior work.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution that advances the understanding of accelerated descent methods. The proposed heuristic is a valuable addition to the toolbox of optimization techniques. Despite the minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh the concerns, and I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the requested analysis of the discretized process.
