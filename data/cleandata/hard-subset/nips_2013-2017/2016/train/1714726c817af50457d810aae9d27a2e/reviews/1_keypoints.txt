- Uses the ODE interpretation of Nesterov’s acceleration (Krichene et al., NIPS 2015) to design an adaptive reweighting of iterates.
- Proposes a natural heuristic to adaptively reweigh iterates to accelerate convergence.
- Method is guaranteed to dominate the standard fixed-schedule averaging, unlike prior adaptive-averaging heuristics.
- Empirical evaluation shows promising speed-ups across a broad class of functions.
- Mathematical argument is simple and easy to follow, given familiarity with Krichene et al.
- Technical development is a close extension of prior work but has large potential practical impact due to Nesterov’s widespread use.
- Demonstrates that a small adaptive modification can yield dramatic improvements over fixed-weight Nesterov schemes.
- Technical suggestion: include an analysis of the discretized process to show the convergence rate is preserved.
- Suggests investigating links between the adaptive averaging and Conjugate Gradient for strongly convex quadratics.
