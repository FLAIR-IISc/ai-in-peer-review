- Studies accelerated first-order methods for constrained convex optimization in continuous and discrete settings, continuing Krichene et al.’s primal–dual ODE approach.
- Focuses on primal trajectory obtained by specific averaging of the mirror of the dual variable and generalizes the averaging schemes.
- Theorem 2 provides convergence rates under suitable conditions on the averaging coefficients.
- In the continuous setting, derives convergence rates faster than the usual 1/t^2.
- Proposes an adaptive choice of averaging weights with provable convergence guarantees.
- Empirically compares the adaptive weighting scheme to restarting techniques known to improve accelerated methods.
- Theoretical analysis of the discrete version of the algorithm is omitted.
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting, well-written, and the continuous analysis rigorous.
- Notes prior related work (Attouch, Peypouquet and coauthors) should be cited.
- Raises the open question whether the improved continuous rates persist after discretization.
