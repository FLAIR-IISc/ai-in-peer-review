- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic for constrained convex optimization that preserves the original convergence rate for non-strongly convex objectives.
- Claims advantage over existing restarting heuristics and supports this both theoretically and experimentally.
- Reviewer found the paper hard to follow and recommends adding background material and clarifying abbreviations/terms.
- Equations between lines 33–34 and 46–47 need more intuitive explanation.
- Reviewer asks for a discussion of relation to adaptive methods in non-convex optimization (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).
- Questions practical significance: experiments are in R^3 (toy setup); requests evidence of scalability to higher dimensions.
- Minor: Figure 1’s reference to the solid line is ambiguous—use a dotted line or colors to show the primal trajectory.
