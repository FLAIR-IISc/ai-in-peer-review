- Paper extends ODE-based accelerated Mirror Descent with a generalized averaging scheme and proposes a novel adaptive averaging strategy for a discretization.  
- Adaptive averaging sometimes outperforms existing AMD algorithms with restarting.  
- Adaptive averaging idea is novel and comes with a clear intuition.  
- Major originality/usefulness concerns: many elements (Lyapunov function, discretization) follow directly from an earlier NIPS paper.  
- Extension from simple to generalized averaging is trivial and of limited use; only quadratic-rate case is actually considered.  
- Achieving faster rates would require higher-order methods, which are not always applicable.  
- Several analyses (energy function interpretation, primal reformulation, reduction to replicator dynamics) improve understanding but offer limited advancement for ML.  
- Adaptive averaging is not fully theoretically grounded due to discretization approximations and lacks an explicit convergence proof, making it effectively a heuristic.  
- No comparison to other existing averaging schemes for Nesterov-style algorithms, so practical advantage is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to a toy quadratic/linear objective and are not convincing for general or strongly convex cases.  
- Post-rebuttal commitments: authors promise added high-dimensional/non-quadratic experiments, a convergence proof for adaptive averaging, implementation on Nesterov’s cubic-regularized Newton method, and comparisons to other adaptive averaging schemes.
