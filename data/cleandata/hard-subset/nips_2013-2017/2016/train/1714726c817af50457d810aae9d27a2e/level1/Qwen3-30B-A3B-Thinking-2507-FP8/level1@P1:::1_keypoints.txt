- Paper extends continuous-time accelerated mirror descent by introducing AMD dynamics parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t), unifying averaging schemes and extending Su et al. and Krichene et al. frameworks.  
- Provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on w and η for desired convergence rates with rigorous proofs (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves convergence rates while improving empirical performance.  
- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics on the simplex, linking results to evolutionary game theory.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms existing heuristics (e.g., gradient/speed restarting), especially for strongly convex problems.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation and illustrative examples (positive orthant and simplex dynamics).  
- Comprehensive experimental comparisons across multiple heuristics and objective functions.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited to continuous-time dynamics; discrete-time convergence bounds are relegated to the supplement.  
- Lacks discussion of computational cost per iteration for the adaptive averaging heuristic versus simpler restarts.  
- Experiments are small-scale (mostly in R^3) and do not include large-scale or more complex constrained ML problems.  
- Limited empirical/theoretical comparison with popular adaptive optimizers like Adagrad and Adam.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, novel adaptive heuristic with guarantees, and interesting applications (replicator dynamics).  
- Against acceptance: discrete-time analysis insufficiently developed and limited articulation of practical large-scale implications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper with a novel adaptive averaging method and clear empirical gains, though discrete-time analysis and experimental scale should be expanded.
