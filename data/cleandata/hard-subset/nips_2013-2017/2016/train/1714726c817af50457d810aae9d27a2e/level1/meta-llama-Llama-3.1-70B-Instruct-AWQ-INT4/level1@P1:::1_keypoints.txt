- Introduces a new family of accelerated mirror-descent dynamics coupling a dual gradient accumulator η(t) with a primal weighted average w(t) for constrained convex optimization.
- Derives sufficient conditions on η and w to guarantee desired convergence rates via a Lyapunov argument.
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to speed up Lyapunov decrease and convergence.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and sound technical content.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the dynamics and the adaptive averaging heuristic, including Lyapunov construction and convergence-rate proofs.
- Strength: unified study of a broad family of accelerated mirror-descent dynamics, advancing optimization theory.
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is novel and potentially impactful for accelerating convergence.
- Weakness: assumes significant background in optimization and dynamical systems, limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Weakness: lacks sufficient numerical experiments to demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Weakness: does not provide clear empirical comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
