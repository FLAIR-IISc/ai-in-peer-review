- Summary: Develops a weight-based continuous-time accelerated mirror-descent (AMD) framework with Lyapunov Lr linking η(t), w(t), and rate r(t) to guarantee O(1/r(t)) convergence and recovers prior ODE-based acceleration results.
- Algorithmic novelty: Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that chooses a(t)=w/W to make ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩(r−ηW/w) ≤ 0, preserving the continuous-time rate r(t).
- Discrete implementation: Provides Algorithm 1 for r(t)=t^2 with a discrete Lyapunov argument in the supplement and empirical comparison to adaptive restarts.
- Example: Recovers replicator dynamics as mirror descent and proposes an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex.
- Strength — unification: Weight-based formulation and simple sufficient conditions cleanly encompass standard accelerated mirror descent and related ODEs.
- Strength — prior work link: Explicit mapping to Wibisono et al.’s parameters and recovery of known schedules provides clear context.
- Strength — new energy: Introduces an energy Er with monotonicity guarantees under the same conditions, offering extra insight.
- Strength — principled adaptive rule: Adaptive averaging is derived to minimize the Lyapunov derivative and thus preserves continuous-time rates by construction.
- Strength — interpretable example: Accelerated replicator dynamics is a neat, familiar-domain illustration.
- Strength — empirical: Experiments show adaptive averaging is competitive with and often outperforms adaptive restarts.
- Weakness — discrete-theory gap: Key sufficient condition depends on ⟨∇f,Ẋ⟩, but the discrete rule uses an f-monotonicity proxy without a clear theoretical link in the main text.
- Weakness — no provable speedups: Paper preserves baseline O(1/r(t)) but does not prove improved rates (e.g., linear) under additional structure like strong convexity.
- Weakness — stability of weights: Changes in a(t) imply exponential changes in w(t); stability/robustness of induced weights and discrete counterparts (especially with noise) is not analyzed.
- Weakness — limited experiments: Most tests are low-dimensional (R^3) with entropy geometry; larger-scale, other mirror maps, and broader comparisons are missing.
- Weakness — assumptions clarity: Assumptions on smoothness/Lipschitzness of ∇ψ* and handling of indicator terms via conjugacy need clearer norms and constants.
- Quality: Continuous-time analysis and Lyapunov calculus are sound; discrete-time guarantee is plausible but underspecified in the main paper.
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized, though some notational overload and the jump from the continuous heuristic to Algorithm 1 could be clearer.
- Originality: Moderate to good — unified averaging viewpoint and adaptive averaging heuristic are novel and principled.
- Significance: Potentially useful if adaptive averaging consistently matches/improves restarts without harming worst-case rates; stronger theory/scale experiments would increase impact.
- Suggestions: State a main discrete-time theorem with assumptions, analyze the strongly convex case, expand experiments to higher dimensions and other geometries, and discuss robustness to stochastic gradients.
- Arguments for acceptance: Solid continuous-time unification, principled adaptive heuristic, interesting replicator application, and empirical gains over restart heuristics.
- Arguments against acceptance: Discrete-time guarantees not prominent, limited experimental scope, and no theoretical speedup beyond preserving baseline rates.
