- Presents accelerated descent as coupled dual/primal dynamics governed by weighting functions, offering a useful lens for analysis and algorithm design.
- Strong theoretical contribution: Lyapunov-based convergence guarantees for a broad class of averaging schemes (AMDw,η).
- Proposes an intuitive, theory-justified adaptive averaging heuristic that is empirically competitive with adaptive restarting.
- Builds on and generalizes prior continuous-time optimization work (Su et al., Krichene et al.).
- Novel connection to replicator dynamics and evolutionary game theory, broadening applicability.
- Includes a primal representation (AMDpw,η) and concrete examples for constraint sets (positive orthant, simplex).
- Writing is generally clear and the problem and motivation are well articulated.
- Theoretical analysis relies on strong assumptions (Lipschitz gradients, differentiable ψ); robustness to relaxed assumptions is not explored.
- Discussion of the energy function (Er) feels somewhat disconnected and its relevance to core contributions is unclear.
- Numerical experiments are encouraging but limited in scope and breadth.
- Lacks comparison to more sophisticated adaptive methods (e.g., Adam), which could yield a more nuanced evaluation.
- Discussion of existing restarting heuristics is cursory; strengths and weaknesses beyond empirical performance are not deeply analyzed.
- Brief mention of Wibisono et al. [23] without exploring implications of higher-order discretization for adaptive averaging.
- Contains an incorrect statement about Nesterov’s method being faster than 1/k^2; this should be corrected.
- Equation (3) needs more explicit justification; the derivation from derivatives of Lr to the final inequality may be unclear.
- The intuition behind the condition a(t) = η(t) r(t) is under-explained and would benefit from clearer motivation.
- Originality: not a completely new algorithm but a principled framework for designing averaging schemes; theoretical results are original and significant.
- Significance: provides theoretical foundation for adaptive averaging, practical heuristic likely adoptable by practitioners, and potential to inform new algorithm design.
- Recommendation: acceptance, with minor fixes addressing clarity and the convergence-rate claim.
