- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic to accelerate convergence of first-order methods for convex optimization.  
- Studies a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics coupling a dual gradient accumulator and a weighted-averaged primal.  
- Provides sufficient conditions on weight functions for desired convergence rates via a Lyapunov argument.  
- Proves the adaptive averaging heuristic preserves quadratic convergence rate in discrete time.  
- Includes numerical experiments comparing the heuristic to existing methods (e.g., adaptive restarting).  
- Paper is well-written and explanations are clear and concise.  
- Novel unified Lyapunov-based analysis of AMD dynamics.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is an original and interesting contribution.  
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and provide clear comparisons.  
- Assumes strong background in convex optimization and dynamical systems, which may limit accessibility.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity of the proposed heuristic.  
- Could include a more thorough discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Quality: 8/10.  
- Clarity: 8/10.  
- Originality: 9/10.  
- Significance: 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel acceleration approach, original heuristic, strong empirical comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: steep prerequisite knowledge, missing complexity analysis, limited discussion of limitations/extensions.
