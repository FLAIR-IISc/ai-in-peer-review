- Introduces a generalized averaging scheme for accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by weight functions w(t) and η(t).
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based continuous-time analysis of AMD dynamics and sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.
- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics (mirror descent on the simplex) via the averaging framework.
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to speed convergence while preserving quadratic rates.
- Builds on and extends prior continuous-time acceleration analyses by Krichene et al. (NIPS 2015) and Su et al. (NIPS 2014) to a broader family of dynamics.
- Theoretical framework is technically sound with well-constructed Lyapunov arguments linking continuous- and discrete-time acceleration.
- Adaptive heuristic is theoretically justified and empirically outperforms existing restarting heuristics in experiments.
- Mathematical exposition is clear and well organized, with precise definitions and reasoning.
- Experimental scope is limited (only R^3 simplex examples), leaving open questions about performance on more complex constrained problems.
- The paper should more explicitly discuss connections to Flammarion & Bach (COLT 2015) to better position its novelty.
- Practical implications for large-scale machine learning are underdeveloped and would benefit from concrete examples or larger-scale experiments.
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution that advances theory and provides a practical heuristic; meets NIPS criteria and is recommended for acceptance.
