- Introduces a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by positive continuous weight functions w and η.  
- Provides sufficient conditions on η and w to guarantee a desired convergence rate via a Lyapunov analysis.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.  
- Presents a unified study of AMD dynamics using an appropriate Lyapunov function.  
- Gives a primal representation of the AMD dynamics and analyzes the replicator dynamics as a concrete example.  
- Shows that replicator dynamics on the simplex can be accelerated with a simple averaging scheme.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the adaptive averaging heuristic outperforms or matches existing restarting heuristics.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and notation is consistent throughout.  
- Authors demonstrate deep expertise and provide clear, concise theoretical explanations.  
- Technical claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and numerical results.  
- Paper is a complete piece of work with careful and honest evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Potentially significant contribution to convex optimization and dynamical systems, with applications to evolutionary game theory and viability theory.  
- Accessibility is limited: assumes strong background in convex optimization and dynamical systems.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Does not compare the proposed heuristic with other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam).  
- Recommends acceptance to NIPS 2016, with suggestions to analyze complexity and compare to other adaptive methods in future work.
