- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via coupled primal-dual variables with weighting functions.
- Derives sufficient conditions for target convergence rates using a Lyapunov argument.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves quadratic convergence and often outperforms adaptive restarting.
- Frames contribution within continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and explores different averaging schemes.
- Provides a rigorous, Lyapunov-based mathematical treatment with clear convergence guarantees.
- Presents a primal representation and an interesting connection to replicator dynamics.
- Empirical results offer initial support for the heuristic but are limited in scope.
- Strength: theoretical soundness and clear statement/justification of convergence conditions.
- Strength: novelty in the adaptive averaging heuristic and its link to Lyapunov decrease.
- Strength: clarity of writing, consistent notation overall, and helpful primal representation.
- Strength: effective connection to prior work on accelerated optimization and dynamics.
- Strength: practical relevance suggested by empirical improvements over restarting heuristics.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to simple examples with few comparisons and lack quantitative breadth.
- Weakness: discretization choices in Algorithm 1 appear ad-hoc and need deeper justification and linkage to existing schemes.
- Weakness: analysis depends on strong smoothness and convexity assumptions; extensions to broader settings are not discussed.
- Weakness: limited comparison to modern adaptive/state-of-the-art optimizers (e.g., Adam or second-order methods).
- Weakness: potentially confusing notation using both X(t) and x(t) for related concepts.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically solid approach with promising heuristic and strong Lyapunov guarantees.
- Against acceptance: empirical validation and discretization justification are insufficient; strong modeling assumptions limit generality.
- Overall: good paper deserving acceptance (reviewer recommends Strong Accept) subject to minor revisions on experiments and discretization details.
