- Paper generalizes accelerated mirror descent via adaptive averaging with weight functions w(t), η(t) and gives Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for convergence.
- Main contributions: Lyapunov analysis for generalized dynamics, connection to replicator dynamics, an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves rates, and numerical experiments showing improvements over restarting.
- Strength: Rigorous Lyapunov analysis unifying various accelerated methods and providing clear sufficient conditions (Theorem 2).
- Strength: Novel connection to replicator dynamics, enabling acceleration results relevant to evolutionary game theory.
- Strength: Adaptive averaging heuristic is theoretically grounded, preserves convergence rates, and empirically outperforms restarting heuristics in reported tests.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-organized with illustrative examples (positive orthant and simplex).
- Weakness: Discrete-time connection is underdeveloped; discrete algorithm guarantees are not rigorously justified from the continuous analysis.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited (simple R^3 simplex problems) and lack tests on higher-dimensional or non-simplex constraints and broader adaptive baselines.
- Weakness: Practical implementation details for the discrete heuristic (efficiency, computational complexity) are not addressed, limiting large-scale applicability.
- Weakness: Claims of "significant improvements" are not thoroughly quantified or shown across diverse problem types; non-convex extensions lack theoretical support.
- Pro for acceptance: Strong theoretical contribution, novel adaptive heuristic with preserved guarantees, and insightful replicator dynamics link.
- Con for acceptance: Lack of rigorous discrete-time guarantees and limited empirical evaluation weaken practical significance.
- Overall: Strong continuous-time theoretical work deserving acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen discrete-time analysis and expand experiments.
