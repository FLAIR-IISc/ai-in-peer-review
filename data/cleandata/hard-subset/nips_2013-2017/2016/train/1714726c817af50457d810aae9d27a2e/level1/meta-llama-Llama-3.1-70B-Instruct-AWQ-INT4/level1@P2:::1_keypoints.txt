- Introduces a novel method (Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics) to accelerate mirror descent for constrained convex optimization.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including sufficient conditions for target convergence rates and a Lyapunov-based proof.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized with a clear introduction and adequate literature references.  
- Effectively relates the method to existing work (Nesterov's acceleration and replicator dynamics).  
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and show performance at least as good as, often better than, existing heuristics like adaptive restarting.  
- The method is original and broadly applicable to constrained convex optimization problems.  
- The convergence-rate analysis is a significant theoretical contribution with potential high impact.  
- High overall quality of exposition and technical detail.  
- Reviewer rating: 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong convergence analysis, clarity, solid experiments, and high significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: may be too specialized for some readers, technical details could be challenging, and more numerical experiments would strengthen the paper.
