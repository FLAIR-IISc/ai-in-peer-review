- Introduces a unified continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) framework parameterized by η(t) and w(t) for constrained convex optimization.  
- Analyzes dynamics using a Lyapunov function combining objective value and a Bregman divergence to derive sufficient conditions for O(1/r(t)) convergence.  
- Generalizes prior work and connects to other continuous-time acceleration perspectives.  
- Proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts w(t) to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.  
- Gives a discrete-time version of adaptive averaging that preserves the O(1/k^2) convergence rate.  
- Theoretical guarantee ensures the adaptive heuristic does not worsen worst-case convergence (safety property).  
- Lacks a full theoretical characterization of when and why adaptive averaging yields empirical speedups beyond preserving the worst-case rate.  
- Analysis of the discrete-time algorithm is deferred to the supplementary material, reducing main-paper self-containment.  
- Paper is technically sound, exceptionally clear, well-organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Examples (including accelerated replicator dynamics) are insightful and effectively illustrate the framework.  
- Empirical results on low-dimensional constrained problems show adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting heuristics.  
- Experiments are small-scale, leaving scalability and performance on large/high-dimensional ML problems unresolved.  
- Adaptive averaging is an original contribution and offers a fresh alternative to restarting or adaptive learning-rate schemes.  
- Work is significant for researchers (unified, flexible framework) and practitioners (simple heuristic with formal guarantees).  
- Reviewer’s overall verdict: strong recommendation for acceptance.
