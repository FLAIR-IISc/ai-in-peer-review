- Summary: Introduces a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by two weight functions, gives sufficient conditions for target convergence rates, and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic validated empirically.
- Strength: Unified framework: Provides a unifying study of AMD dynamics that encompasses various acceleration methods including Nesterov's.
- Strength: Sufficient conditions: Establishes sufficient conditions for achieving desired convergence rates.
- Strength: Adaptive averaging heuristic: Proposes a simple, effective averaging heuristic that preserves convergence rates in practice.
- Strength: Numerical experiments: Thorough empirical comparison showing the heuristic outperforms existing restarting heuristics on several problems.
- Weakness: Technical complexity: High technical content may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Weakness: Limited scope: Focuses mainly on AMD dynamics, potentially limiting applicability to other methods.
- Weakness: Lack of theoretical guarantees: No theoretical performance guarantees are provided for the adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Originality: Novel formulation of AMD dynamics that contributes a new perspective to accelerated methods.
- Originality: Adaptive averaging heuristic appears original and previously unexplored.
- Significance: Contributions can impact optimization research, especially accelerated methods.
- Significance: The adaptive averaging idea could extend to other optimization methods, prompting further work.
- Pro: Significant contribution to optimization and effective, original heuristic with strong empirical backing.
- Con: Accessibility concerns due to technical depth and narrow scope confined to AMD dynamics.
- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions to improve accessibility and clarify technical exposition for non-experts.
