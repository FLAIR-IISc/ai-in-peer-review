- Studies accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization via coupled primal-dual dynamics driven by averaging, providing Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic and proves it preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods.
- Provides theoretical and empirical evidence that the heuristic often outperforms adaptive restarting schemes.
- Generalizes prior continuous-time ODE connections to a broader Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) family and links to replicator dynamics.
- Lyapunov analysis and conditions for convergence are technically sound and clearly presented.
- The derivation of the primal representation and the discrete–continuous connection are helpful and well-motivated.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and easy to follow with clear equations and exposition.
- The adaptive averaging heuristic is an original contribution with novel guarantees on convergence rate.
- Results are significant for researchers/practitioners interested in optimization acceleration and provide useful benchmarks against existing heuristics.
- Experiments are limited in scope to simple problems (quadratic, linear, KL); broader, large-scale ML evaluations are needed.
- Notation is complex with many indices and symbols, making some expressions hard to parse.
- The relationship to other adaptive methods (e.g., AdaGrad, Adam) is only briefly discussed and could be deepened.
- Discretization details in Algorithm 1, including step-size choice and update rule justification for a_k, are somewhat brief and need expansion.
- Overall assessment: strong, rigorous paper that advances AMD and adaptive averaging; strengths outweigh weaknesses and the reviewer recommends acceptance.
