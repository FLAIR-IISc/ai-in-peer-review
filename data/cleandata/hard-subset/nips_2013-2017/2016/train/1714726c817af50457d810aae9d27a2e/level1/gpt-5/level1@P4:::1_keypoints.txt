- Paper introduces a two-parameter continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMDw,η) with dual accumulation η(t) and primal averaging w(t), using Lyapunov Lr to certify rate 1/r(t).
- Provides constructive recipe via a(t)=w/W to realize polynomial rates r(t)=t^p and defines a decreasing energy Er(t).
- Derives a primal form highlighting operator ∇^2ψ*∘∇ψ and instantiates ODEs for the orthant and simplex, yielding an accelerated replicator dynamic.
- Proposes a Lyapunov-guided adaptive averaging heuristic (continuous time) and a discrete Algorithm 1 claimed to preserve 1/k^2, with experiments comparing to restart heuristics.
- Connects to prior ODE interpretations of Nesterov and AMD work, generalizing previous analyses by allowing general w,η and adding adaptive averaging.

- Strength: Clear Lyapunov framework with verifiable sufficient conditions that recover known accelerated schedules.
- Strength: Constructive weight design tying acceleration to averaging, with explicit examples and clean primal representation.
- Strength: Introduction of an energy function that decreases under the same conditions, offering extra insight.
- Strength: Adaptive averaging is principled, continuous-time guaranteed, and the discrete variant claims to maintain quadratic rate.
- Strength: Empirical results show competitive or superior performance vs restarts in small-scale tests.
- Strength: Adds a new control knob (weights) to ODE-based acceleration literature.

- Weakness: Typo in Examples 1–2: a(t) should be β/t (β≥p), not β t; derivations align with β/t.
- Weakness: Gap between continuous adaptive rule (depends on ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩) and discrete trigger (f(x̃(k+1))−f(x̃(k))>0); discrete Lyapunov bridge is not explicit in main text.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 contains an ambiguous/typoed definition of λ_k; update formulas and parameter schedules need precision for reproducibility.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited (mostly R^3); main claims need moderate-to-large scale results, sensitivity analyses, and benchmarks.
- Weakness: No theoretical acceleration beyond O(1/k^2) for general convex functions; strong-convex improvements are only empirical.
- Weakness: Assumptions (Lipschitz ∇ψ*, twice-differentiability of ψ*) are scattered; a consolidated assumptions section is missing.

- Quality: Continuous-time analysis appears technically sound and recovers known schedules; energy proof uses monotonicity appropriately.
- Quality: Discrete-time guarantees are deferred to supplement; key lemmas/results should be moved into the main text for completeness.

- Clarity: Generally well-written with instructive primal formulations and examples.
- Clarity: Need to fix typos (a(t), λ_k), make discrete adaptive rule/guarantees explicit, and resolve minor notation inconsistencies (t0, γ).

- Originality: Novel contributions include generalized averaging view, accelerated replicator instantiation, and Lyapunov-guided adaptive averaging.

- Significance: Introduces adaptive averaging as a new design dimension with continuous-time guarantees and potential interest for constrained settings, but broader impact needs stronger empirical and discrete-theory support.

- Pros for acceptance: Solid continuous-time theory, principled adaptive heuristic tied to Lyapunov decrease, clear examples, and promising empirical comparisons.
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete discrete-time theory and ambiguous algorithmic details, limited experiments, and no provable improvement beyond quadratic rate for general convex problems.

- Suggested fixes: Correct a(t) and λ_k typos; include discrete Lyapunov inequality and a theorem stating conditions for Algorithm 1 to achieve O(1/k^2).
- Suggested experiments: Expand to higher dimensions and real datasets, include sensitivity analyses for β and step size, and provide ablations on the adaptive rule.
- Suggested exposition: Consolidate assumptions, discuss applicability beyond simplex/orthant, and compare to other adaptive schemes.

- Question: Can adaptive averaging provably yield faster (e.g., linear) rates under strong convexity without restarts?
- Question: What precise discrete condition ensures the analogue of ⟨∇f(X), Ẋ⟩(r − ηW/w) ≤ 0?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to β and step-size schedules, and are parameter-free variants possible?
