The paper "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics" studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. The authors introduce a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, parameterized by two positive, continuous weight functions w and η, and provide sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the AMD dynamics. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the subject matter and provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical results. The paper is well-organized, and the notation is consistent throughout.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the AMD dynamics and the adaptive averaging heuristic. The authors provide a unified study of the AMD dynamics using an appropriate Lyapunov function and give sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate. The adaptive averaging heuristic is a novel approach that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function.

The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the replicator dynamics, which is an example of mirror descent on the simplex. The authors show that the replicator dynamics can be accelerated using a simple averaging scheme and provide a primal representation of the AMD dynamics.

The numerical experiments in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the adaptive averaging heuristic. The authors compare the performance of the adaptive averaging heuristic with existing restarting heuristics and show that the adaptive averaging heuristic performs at least as well as the other heuristics and in some cases considerably better.
