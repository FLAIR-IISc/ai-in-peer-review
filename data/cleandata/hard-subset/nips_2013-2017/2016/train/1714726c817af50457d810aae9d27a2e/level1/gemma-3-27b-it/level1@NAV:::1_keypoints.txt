- Proposes adaptive averaging in accelerated descent dynamics, coupling dual and primal variables through averaging to speed Lyapunov decrease.  
- Provides sufficient convergence conditions via a Lyapunov argument and proves adaptive averaging preserves quadratic convergence rate.  
- Generalizes prior continuous-time accelerated methods (e.g., [11]) by studying a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent dynamics parameterized by weight functions.  
- Formalizes the connection between averaging and acceleration, building on observations in prior work (e.g., [8]).  
- Novel theoretical guarantee: adaptive averaging maintains original convergence rate, unlike existing heuristics such as adaptive restarting.  
- Presents clear primal ODE representations and a replicator dynamics example illustrating framework applicability.  
- Numerical experiments show promising empirical performance, especially in the strongly convex case, but are limited in scope.  
- Theoretical applicability may be restrictive in practice due to conditions like η(t) ≥ r′(t).  
- Related work discussion is incomplete; links to online/adaptive methods (e.g., Adam) could be expanded.  
- Notation is occasionally dense and could be simplified for readability.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel theoretical framework, proven adaptive heuristic, clear ties to literature, and initial empirical support.  
- Rejection concerns: restrictive assumptions, limited experiments, and insufficient related-work coverage; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
