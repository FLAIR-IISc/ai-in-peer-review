- Paper studies continuous-time accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with dual accumulation Ż = −η∇f and primal X a weighted average, using Lyapunov Lr to obtain O(1/r(t)) decay.  
- Main technical contribution: clean Lyapunov argument reducing analysis to two verifiable conditions (η ≥ r′ and sign condition r − ηW/w), with corollary η = (w/W)r and w/W ≥ r′/r.  
- Framework unifies and extends prior accelerated ODE analyses (Su–Boyd–Candès, Wibisono–Wilson–Jordan, Attouch–Peypouquet) and earlier accelerated mirror descent work.  
- Provides primal representations (positive orthant, simplex) recovering mirror descent and introducing accelerated replicator dynamics.  
- Introduces an auxiliary energy Er that decreases under the same conditions and aligns with empirical diagnostics.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts weights to decrease d/dt Lr and gives a discrete-time instantiation compared empirically to restart heuristics.  
- Technical clarity: Lyapunov and reparameterization a(t)=w/W are convenient and lead to explicit constructions.  
- Important typographical/consistency errors: examples claim a(t)=β t but should be a(t)=β/t (and related formulas assume 1/t), confusing readers.  
- Heuristic formula mistakes: to cancel the second term one needs η = a r (i.e., a = η/r), not a = η r; units and remarks are inconsistent.  
- Discrete-time guarantee that adaptive averaging preserves quadratic rate is relegated to the supplement; main text should state a concise theorem with assumptions.  
- Empirical evaluation is careful and illustrative (objective, Lyapunov, energy, trajectories) but limited to small-scale, deterministic settings; more high-dim and wall-clock tests needed.  
- Clarity overall is good and examples are illustrative, but the noted sign/scale typos and initialization at t0>0 require correction and a schematic for choosing r, a, w, η would help.  
- Originality: novel unified averaging-weight Lyapunov analysis, new accelerated replicator dynamics, and a principled adaptive averaging heuristic distinct from restarts.  
- Significance: advances understanding of acceleration via averaging and offers a practical control knob, though no theoretical improvement beyond O(1/k^2) in discrete non-strongly-convex case.  
- Pros: general Lyapunov framework, conceptual insight into acceleration via averaging, novel replicator dynamics, simple heuristic with rate-preservation guarantees, good connections to prior work.  
- Cons: critical typographical/sign inconsistencies, discrete-time theory mostly in supplement, modest experimental scope, and limited theoretical gains in non-strongly-convex setting.  
- Suggested fixes: correct a(t)/η/r expressions and state explicitly that a(t) ≥ r′/r, w via (4), η = a r; add a short discrete-time theorem in main text; expand experiments to higher dimensions/stochastic setups; compare to Bregman Lagrangian more explicitly.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, insightful contribution likely accept after fixing formula errors and adding a crisper discrete-time summary; currently borderline but positive.
