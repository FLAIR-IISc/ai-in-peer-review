- Proposes a generalized continuous-time framework for accelerated mirror descent (AMD) parameterized by gradient accumulation η(t) and averaging weight w(t).
- Provides a Lyapunov-based analysis that yields simple, sufficient conditions on η and w to guarantee desired convergence rates.
- Introduces a novel "adaptive averaging" heuristic that dynamically adjusts averaging weights to accelerate Lyapunov decrease.
- Adaptive averaging is motivated by theory and presented as an alternative to adaptive restarting schemes.
- The heuristic preserves the O(1/t^2) convergence-rate guarantee of the underlying accelerated method, ensuring formal stability.
- Paper is elegant, technically sound, and clearly written, progressing from continuous-time theory to a discrete Algorithm 1 with examples.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to low-dimensional, simplex-constrained problems and would benefit from larger-scale ML experiments.
- Comparison to related adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) is brief and would benefit from deeper discussion of mechanisms and differences.
- Overall contribution is strong and theoretically grounded; reviewer recommends acceptance.
