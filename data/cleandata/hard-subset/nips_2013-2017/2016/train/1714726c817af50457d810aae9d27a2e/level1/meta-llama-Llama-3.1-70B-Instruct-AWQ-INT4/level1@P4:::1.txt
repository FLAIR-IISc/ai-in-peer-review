This paper proposes a novel approach to accelerate the convergence of first-order methods for convex optimization by using an adaptive averaging heuristic. The authors start by studying a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, which can be described as a coupling of a dual variable accumulating gradients at a given rate, and a primal variable obtained as the weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory. They provide sufficient conditions on the weight functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument.

The authors then propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. They prove that this heuristic preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods in discrete-time and provide numerical experiments to compare it with existing heuristics, such as adaptive restarting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of a Lyapunov argument to analyze the convergence of the AMD dynamics is a nice touch, and the adaptive averaging heuristic is an interesting contribution to the field of convex optimization.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a unified study of the AMD dynamics using a Lyapunov argument, which is a novel approach to analyze the convergence of these dynamics.
* The adaptive averaging heuristic is a new and interesting contribution to the field of convex optimization.
* The numerical experiments are well-designed and provide a clear comparison between the proposed heuristic and existing methods.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes a strong background in convex optimization and dynamical systems, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed heuristic, which is an important consideration in practice.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of the proposed approach.

Overall, the paper is a solid contribution to the field of convex optimization, and the adaptive averaging heuristic is a promising approach to accelerate the convergence of first-order methods.
