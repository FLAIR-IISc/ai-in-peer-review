- Summary: Proposes a novel framework generalizing accelerated mirror descent via adaptive averaging, uses a Lyapunov function for convergence conditions, accelerates replicator dynamics, and shows continuous/discrete-time guarantees with numerical comparisons.
- Theoretical contribution: Provides a rigorous Lyapunov-based analysis establishing sufficient conditions for accelerated convergence and introduces adaptive averaging.
- Novelty: Adaptive averaging is an original heuristic linking acceleration and averaging, extending continuous-time optimization literature.
- Practical relevance: Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging can outperform restarting heuristics, particularly on strongly convex problems.
- Clarity of results: Offers clear theoretical guarantees and detailed derivations, including both Lyapunov and energy perspectives for reproducibility.
- Broader impact: Demonstrates applicability to replicator dynamics, connecting optimization advances to evolutionary game theory.
- Clarity (weakness): Dense mathematical exposition may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with continuous-time methods or mirror descent.
- Limited experiments: Empirical validation is restricted to low-dimensional settings and specific objectives, lacking high-dimensional or real-world tests.
- Limited comparisons: Does not benchmark against other discrete-time adaptive methods (e.g., Adam, Adagrad), limiting empirical context.
- Heuristic limitations: Unclear whether adaptive averaging can achieve strictly faster rates for strongly convex functions, reducing theoretical appeal.
- Argument for acceptance: Significant theoretical generalization with rigorous guarantees and practical improvements over existing heuristics.
- Argument for acceptance: Broadens relevance by applying accelerated dynamics to replicator equations in evolutionary game theory.
- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient empirical breadth and dense exposition may limit impact and accessibility.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental evaluation.
