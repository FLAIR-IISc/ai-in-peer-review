- Studies accelerated first-order methods via continuous-time dynamics and generalizes accelerated mirror descent (AMD) with two time-varying functions η(t) and w(t).
- Provides a unified Lyapunov-based analysis yielding simple sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) for O(1/r(t)) convergence.
- Framework recovers prior accelerated ODE results as special cases.
- Derives a primal form and shows how to obtain an accelerated replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily chooses w(t) to decrease the Lyapunov function while preserving the O(1/r(t)) rate.
- Presents a discrete-time implementation (Algorithm 1) of the adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Empirical results show the adaptive method is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, popular restarting heuristics.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and well-supported by proofs and experiments.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and provides clear intuition; figures effectively illustrate dynamics and Lyapunov behavior.
- The averaging generalization and adaptive averaging heuristic are novel and significant contributions.
- The work deepens understanding of averaging’s role in acceleration and offers a practical, rate-preserving alternative to restarting.
- Experiments are limited to low-dimensional problems, weakening practical claims for high-dimensional ML tasks.
- The link between the continuous-time adaptation condition (Eq. 8) and the discrete algorithmic rule needs a more formal justification.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
