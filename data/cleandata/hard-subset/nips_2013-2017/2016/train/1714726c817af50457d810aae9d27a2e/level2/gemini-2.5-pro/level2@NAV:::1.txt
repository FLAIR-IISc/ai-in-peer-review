This paper presents a generalized continuous-time framework for accelerated mirror descent dynamics, building upon the recent line of work connecting Nesterov's methods to second-order ODEs. The authors parameterize the dynamics with a gradient accumulation rate `η(t)` and an averaging weight `w(t)`. The primary theoretical contribution is a unified Lyapunov analysis providing sufficient conditions on these functions to guarantee a desired convergence rate. From this analysis, the paper derives its main practical contribution: a novel "adaptive averaging" heuristic that adjusts `w(t)` to greedily decrease the Lyapunov function. This approach is shown to preserve the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated methods, a notable advantage over many existing heuristics. The framework is compellingly instantiated to derive an accelerated version of the replicator dynamics.

**Strengths and Weaknesses:**

The paper is well-written, theoretically sound, and presents a novel and significant contribution to the optimization literature.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   **Quality & Originality:** The Lyapunov-based analysis is elegant and provides a principled foundation for the proposed heuristic. Adaptive averaging is a novel idea that addresses a key weakness of common restarting schemes: the lack of formal guarantees. Proving that the heuristic preserves the optimal convergence rate is a strong and important result.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The progression from the general continuous-time framework to the specific adaptive algorithm is logical and easy to follow.
*   **Significance:** This work offers a new, guaranteed method for empirically speeding up accelerated optimization. The demonstration that replicator dynamics can be accelerated through this averaging framework is an interesting finding that connects two important lines of research. The proposed method has the potential to be widely used by practitioners and further studied by researchers.

**Arguments Against Acceptance / Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   **Experimental Scope:** The empirical validation is limited to low-dimensional (3D) problems, chosen for visualization. While effective for illustration, the paper's claims would be significantly bolstered by demonstrating the heuristic's performance on a higher-dimensional, standard machine learning benchmark. This would provide a more convincing comparison against the restarting baselines.
*   **Discrete-Time Analysis:** The crucial proof that the discrete-time version of the heuristic (Algorithm 1) preserves the convergence rate is deferred to the supplementary material. Including a brief sketch of this result in the main text would make the paper more self-contained and strengthen its claims.

Overall, the paper's theoretical novelty and the well-grounded nature of the proposed heuristic are compelling. The weaknesses are minor and relate mostly to the scope of the evaluation rather than the core ideas. This is a high-quality submission that advances our understanding of acceleration.
