This paper introduces a novel family of accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization problems, which can be described as a coupling of a dual variable accumulating gradients at a given rate and a primal variable obtained as the weighted average of the mirrored dual trajectory. The authors provide sufficient conditions on the weight functions to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The experiments indicate that the proposed heuristic performs at least as well as existing restarting heuristics, with significant improvements in some cases.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate and the energy function, which is a natural measure of energy in the system. The experiments are well-designed and provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with existing heuristics.

The main strengths of the paper are:

* The authors provide a novel and intuitive interpretation of accelerated descent dynamics as a coupling of a dual variable and a primal variable.
* The proposed adaptive averaging heuristic is simple and effective, and it preserves the convergence rate of the original method.
* The experiments are well-designed and provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with existing heuristics.

The main weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes that the objective function is convex and differentiable, which may not be the case in many practical applications.
* The authors do not provide any theoretical guarantees for the proposed heuristic in the case of non-convex objective functions.
* The paper does not provide any analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of optimization and provides a new perspective on accelerated descent dynamics. The proposed adaptive averaging heuristic is simple and effective, and it has the potential to be widely adopted in practice.
