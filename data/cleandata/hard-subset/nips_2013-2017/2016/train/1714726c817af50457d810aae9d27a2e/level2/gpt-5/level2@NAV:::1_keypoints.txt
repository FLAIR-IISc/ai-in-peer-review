- Studies generalized accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by η(t) and w(t), giving Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions to guarantee convergence rate 1/r(t).
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adjusts a(t)=w(t)/W(t) online to maximize Lyapunov decrease while preserving the target rate.
- Connects and extends ODE views of Nesterov/Su-Boyd-Candès and the Bregman Lagrangian framework, and relates to adaptive restarting heuristics.
- Produces an accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex plus a monotone “energy” functional in addition to the Lyapunov function.
- Theoretical development is careful: existence/uniqueness, Lyapunov bound (Theorem 2) with a constructive corollary, examples for r(t)=t^p, and energy decrement (Theorem 3).
- Discrete-time scheme (Algorithm 1) claims to preserve O(1/k^2), but key proofs and details are deferred to the supplement.
- Limited practical guidance on hyperparameters (step-size s, β) and choice of regularizer R in discrete time.
- Experiments illustrate concepts but are small-scale (R^3 simplex), lack statistical rigor, and omit large-scale/standard ML benchmarks.
- Paper is generally well written; primal form and worked examples (orthant, simplex) improve clarity.
- Expository additions needed: concise recipe for choosing r, η, w; explicit pseudocode with defaults; clearer discussion of computational overhead.
- Treating acceleration as generalized averaging and adapting averaging weights appears novel relative to prior ODE/restart analyses.
- Accelerated replicator example is a notable, concrete contribution.
- If strengthened empirically and in discrete-time, adaptive averaging could be a practical alternative or complement to restarts in constrained optimization and game dynamics.
- Pros: clear general Lyapunov framework with constructive schedules; novel adaptive averaging with continuous guarantees and preserved discrete rate; insightful primal forms and examples.
- Cons: weak empirical evaluation; discrete-time analysis mostly in supplement; no new rates beyond preserving known bounds (strongly convex improvements remain conjectural).
- Suggested fixes: include main discrete-time proofs or sharper theorem; provide code and larger benchmarks with sensitivity analyses; compare to monotone FISTA, line-search, and modern restarts; study stochastic/noisy gradients; discuss automatic tuning of β and s and combining adaptive averaging with restart.
- Overall: promising idea but borderline accept—acceptance contingent on stronger experiments and clearer discrete-time exposition.
