- Continuous-time framework couples dual Z accumulating gradients at rate η(t) and primal X as weighted average with weights w(t); Lyapunov Lr yields sufficient conditions for 1/r(t) convergence.

- Theorem 2: general sufficient conditions for Lyapunov decrease implying f(X(t))−f* ≤ const/r(t).

- Provides primal representation, examples (including accelerated replicator dynamics), an adaptive averaging heuristic, a discrete-time algorithm for r(t)=t^2, and empirical comparisons to restarting.

- Strength: clean Lyapunov-based framework that isolates roles of η, w, and r and recovers known accelerated schedules.

- Strength: connects and maps explicitly to Wibisono et al.’s variational ODE family.

- Strength: novel adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves continuous-time Lyapunov decrease and has a discrete instantiation.

- Strength: accelerated replicator dynamics on the simplex broadens applicability to game-theoretic/entropy-regularized problems.

- Strength: empirical behavior shows adaptive averaging matches or outperforms restarting with reduced oscillations and monotone Lyapunov decrease.

- Weakness: discrete-time guarantees are limited to r(t)=t^2, relegated to the supplement, and lack explicit assumptions/constants in the main text.

- Weakness: Condition (2) is trajectory-dependent; theoretical link between continuous sign checks ⟨∇f, Ẋ⟩ and Algorithm 1’s discrete surrogate is unclear.

- Weakness: experiments are low-dimensional (3D simplex); need higher-dimensional benchmarks, wall-clock comparisons, and sensitivity analyses (β, step s).

- Weakness: assumptions on ψ and ψ* (Lipschitz ∇ψ*, twice differentiable) need discussion for non-smooth mirror maps and impacts on existence/uniqueness and Lyapunov analysis.

- Weakness: strongly convex setting shows empirical gains but lacks theoretical treatment (e.g., linear rates or accelerated strong-convex analysis).

- Quality: continuous-time analysis and Lyapunov derivations are sound; discrete-time analysis should be elevated into main paper.

- Clarity: overall clear but notation is dense; recommend a symbol summary, an explicit mapping to prior ODE work, and brief explanations of Algorithm 1 steps.

- Originality: general Lyapunov framework for arbitrary weights and the adaptive averaging heuristic appear novel; replicator dynamics acceleration is a notable contribution.

- Significance: moderate—framework may enable principled, rate-preserving adaptivity, but broader experiments and stronger discrete-time theory are needed for higher impact.

- Essential improvements: bring key discrete-time lemmas/theorems (assumptions, rates, constants) into main text and clarify the discrete surrogate for ⟨∇f,Ẋ⟩ used in Algorithm 1.

- Essential improvements: expand experiments to higher-dimensional tasks, report wall-clock time, and test sensitivity to β and step size s.

- Optional improvements: discuss composite/proximal extensions, stochastic gradients, deeper mapping to Wibisono et al., and guidance on choosing ψ and verifying its smoothness assumptions.

- For acceptance: unifying Lyapunov conditions, principled adaptive heuristic preserving rates, novel replicator dynamics, and insightful continuous/discrete links.

- Against acceptance: incomplete discrete-time presentation, small-scale experimental evaluation, and missing strong/composite theoretical treatment.

- Overall: promising and well-motivated; strengthen discrete-time theory presentation and broaden experiments to make the case for acceptance stronger.
