- Paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization and introduces a family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by two weight functions w and η.
- Provides sufficient conditions on w and η to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate decrease of the Lyapunov function.
- Proves the heuristic preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods in discrete time.
- Presents numerical experiments comparing the adaptive heuristic with existing heuristics, but experiments are limited.

- Strength: Thorough, technically sound analysis using Lyapunov methods.
- Strength: Original contributions—new AMD family and an adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Strength: Clear writing and explanations of technical details and intuition.

- Weakness: Limited scope—focuses only on constrained convex optimization without discussing other problem classes.
- Weakness: Lacks a rigorous proof of optimality for the adaptive averaging heuristic.
- Weakness: Numerical experiments are narrow and not comprehensive.

- Significance: Advances the state-of-the-art by introducing new AMD dynamics and adaptive averaging.
- Significance: Heuristic could improve efficiency of optimization algorithms by adaptive weight adjustment.
- Significance: Offers new insights into AMD behavior and the role of averaging.

- Recommendation: Provide a rigorous proof of the heuristic’s optimality.
- Recommendation: Discuss or extend applicability to non-convex and non-smooth optimization.
- Recommendation: Expand numerical evaluation across a wider range of problems and compare to more state-of-the-art methods.
