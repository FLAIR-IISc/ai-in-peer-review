**Summary of the Paper**

The paper studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization. It introduces a broad family of Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) dynamics, parameterized by two positive, continuous weight functions w and η. The authors provide sufficient conditions on η and w to achieve a desired convergence rate using a Lyapunov argument. They also propose an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes the weights to speed up the decrease of the Lyapunov function. The authors prove that the heuristic preserves the quadratic convergence rate of accelerated first-order methods in discrete-time and provide numerical experiments to compare it with existing heuristics.

1. **Technical soundness**: The paper provides a thorough and rigorous analysis of the AMD dynamics and the adaptive averaging heuristic. The authors use a Lyapunov argument to establish the convergence rate of the algorithm, which is a standard technique in control theory.
2. **Originality**: The paper introduces a new family of AMD dynamics and an adaptive averaging heuristic, which are novel contributions to the field of optimization.
3. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the technical details and the intuition behind the algorithm.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**
