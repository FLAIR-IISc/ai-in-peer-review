Paper generalizes accelerated mirror descent to an AMDw,η continuous-time framework parameterized by gradient accumulation rate η(t) and averaging weight w(t).
Main theoretical contribution: Lyapunov-based analysis giving simple sufficient conditions on η(t) and w(t) to achieve a 1/r(t) convergence rate.
Framework is general enough to derive an accelerated replicator dynamics on the probability simplex.
Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that greedily decreases the Lyapunov function while provably preserving the O(1/t^2) rate.
Provides a discrete-time version of the adaptive heuristic and empirically compares it to standard AMD and restarting heuristics.
Experimental results: adaptive averaging performs at least as well as restarting and substantially better in some cases, especially for strongly convex objectives.
Technical quality is high: analysis is sound, elegant, and unified via the Lyapunov function Lr.
Key strength: adaptive averaging comes with a formal convergence-rate guarantee, unlike many restarting heuristics.
Experimental validation is solid, with clear comparisons and informative plots of Lr and Er.
Minor weakness: detailed discrete-time analysis and convergence proof are deferred to the supplementary material.
Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and mathematically clear.
Suggested clarity improvement: better connect the continuous-time adaptive condition ⟨∇f(X(t)), Ẋ(t)⟩ sign to the discrete check f(x̃(k+1)) > f(x̃(k)).
Originality: AMDw,η framework, adaptive averaging heuristic, and acceleration of replicator dynamics are novel contributions.
Significance: provides a valuable theoretical tool for designing accelerated dynamics and a practical, rate-preserving heuristic for practitioners.
Limitations: experiments are primarily low-dimensional; inclusion of higher-dimensional results would strengthen empirical claims.
Minor critique: the correspondence between the continuous and discrete adaptive strategies could be more explicitly motivated.
