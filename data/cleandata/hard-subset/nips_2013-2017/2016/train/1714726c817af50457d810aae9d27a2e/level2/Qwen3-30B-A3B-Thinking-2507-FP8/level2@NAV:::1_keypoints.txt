- Proposes a unified Accelerated Mirror Descent (AMD) framework for constrained convex optimization via a generalized averaging scheme.  
- Provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions on weight functions η(t) and w(t) to achieve desired convergence rates (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics on the simplex, linking results to evolutionary game theory.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that preserves quadratic convergence while improving empirical performance.  
- Key theoretical insight: decreasing the Lyapunov function's derivative accelerates convergence without breaking guarantees.  
- Numerical experiments show adaptive averaging outperforms existing restarting heuristics, especially for strongly convex problems, and maintain a decreasing Lyapunov function.  
- The discrete-time implementation (Algorithm 1) lacks complete theoretical justification for its convergence properties.  
- Relation to adaptive optimization methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) is under-discussed and should be clarified.  
- The distinction between the proposed adaptive heuristic and the restarting techniques compared needs clearer explanation.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical novelty and empirical support; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen discrete-time convergence analysis.
