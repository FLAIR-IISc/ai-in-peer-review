## Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

This paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, framing it as a coupling of dual and primal variables via averaging. The core contribution lies in proposing an adaptive averaging heuristic to dynamically compute weights, aiming to speed up convergence. The authors provide sufficient conditions for convergence, demonstrate acceleration of replicator dynamics as an example, and present numerical experiments comparing their method to adaptive restarting.

The paper builds upon a strong foundation of recent work connecting accelerated methods to continuous-time ODEs (e.g., Su et al. [20], Krichene et al. [11]). The formulation using a Lyapunov function is well-motivated and allows for a unified analysis of different averaging schemes. The theoretical results, particularly Theorem 2, are valuable in providing conditions for convergence. The connection to replicator dynamics is a nice illustration of the method’s applicability.

A key strength is the clear presentation and motivation for the adaptive averaging heuristic. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the need for a smooth distance-generating function, and provide a discrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) for practical implementation. The numerical experiments, while limited in scope, demonstrate competitive performance compared to existing heuristics, with improvements observed in some cases. 

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. The theoretical analysis, while sound, feels somewhat conservative. The conditions for convergence, particularly the requirement of a specific relationship between η and w, may be difficult to satisfy in practice. The numerical experiments lack detailed statistical analysis (e.g., confidence intervals) and are limited to a few examples. A more comprehensive evaluation on a wider range of problems and datasets would strengthen the claims. Furthermore, the discussion of related work, while present, could be more thorough. For example, the connection to adaptive learning rate methods like Adam [10] could be explored in greater detail.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and well-motivated approach to adaptive averaging.
*   Solid theoretical foundation with convergence guarantees.
*   Demonstrated performance comparable to, and sometimes exceeding, existing heuristics.
*   Clear presentation and connection to relevant literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Theoretical conditions for convergence may be restrictive.
*   Limited scope of numerical experiments.
*   Potential for more thorough discussion of related work.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the understanding of accelerated descent dynamics and offers a promising new heuristic for adaptive averaging. While the theoretical conditions and numerical evaluation could be strengthened, the work is technically sound, clearly written, and demonstrates originality. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more comprehensive numerical evaluation and a more detailed discussion of related work.
