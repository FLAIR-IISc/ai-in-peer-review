- Core contribution: frames accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization as coupled dual-primal dynamics and provides sufficient conditions for convergence.  
- Novel results: demonstrates acceleration of replicator dynamics via averaging and proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic with convergence guarantees.  
- Theoretical foundation: builds on continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration and generalizes previous work [11, 20].  
- Rigorous analysis: uses a Lyapunov argument to provide a solid framework for analyzing accelerated descent.  
- Extension: generalizes to broader averaging schemes beyond prior specialized cases.  
- Practical demonstration: numerical experiments show the proposed method performs comparably to or better than adaptive restarting.  
- Contextualization: connects to evolutionary game theory and existing acceleration heuristics, situating the work in prior literature.  
- Presentation strength: paper is well-structured, moving logically from theory to applications.  
- Clarity issue: the transition from continuous-time ODEs to the discrete Algorithm 1 needs clearer explanation.  
- Step-size concern: choice of discrete step size ‘s’ and its relation to continuous-time parameters is underexplained.  
- Discretization gap: how discretization preserves convergence rate is stated but not fully illuminated in the main text.  
- Experimental limitation: numerical experiments are somewhat limited in scope (need more problem dimensions and varied objective landscapes).  
- Related work gap: discussion could include more recent adaptive methods beyond Adagrad and Adam, especially those for accelerated methods.  
- Notation: dense notation; recommend defining all variables upon first use for readability.  
- Recommendation summary: strong, theoretically sound paper with significant contributions and promising experiments; addressing clarity, experimental scope, and related-work coverage would strengthen the submission.
