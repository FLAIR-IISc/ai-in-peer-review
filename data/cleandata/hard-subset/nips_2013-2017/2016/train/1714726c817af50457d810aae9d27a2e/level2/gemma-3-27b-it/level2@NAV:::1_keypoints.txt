- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization via a coupled dual-primal system.  
- Main contributions: Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for convergence and an adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Shows replicator dynamics can be accelerated and provides empirical evidence outperforming adaptive restarting.  
- Builds on continuous-time ODE interpretations of Nesterov acceleration (Su et al., Krichene et al.); Lyapunov analysis is rigorous.  
- Provides a clear derivation of the primal representation and an interesting connection to replicator dynamics.  
- Adaptive averaging heuristic is intuitive—weights adjusted by the Lyapunov decrease rate—and theoretically motivated.  
- Lacks comparisons to other adaptive methods (e.g., Adagrad, Adam) beyond adaptive restarting.  
- Numerical experiments are limited in scope (few objective functions) and parameter/initial-condition choices are not well justified.  
- Discrete algorithm (Algorithm 1) seems ad-hoc and needs further analysis linking it to the continuous-time theory.  
- Treatment of the Lyapunov function’s limit behavior with multiple minimizers is brief and should be expanded.  
- For acceptance: solid theoretical contribution and a novel, promising heuristic.  
- Against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation and insufficient connection between continuous and discrete formulations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand experiments/comparisons, justify parameters, and elaborate on Lyapunov limits.
