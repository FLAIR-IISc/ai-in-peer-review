- Core contribution: unified framework viewing accelerated descent dynamics as coupled primal-dual variables with convergence governed by averaging weights and gradient accumulation rates.
- Sufficient conditions: provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions for convergence and links weight functions to convergence rates.
- Generalization: extends prior continuous-time/ODe and accelerated mirror descent frameworks to a broader family parameterized by weight functions.
- Averaging formalized: formalizes and extends the connection between acceleration and averaging observed in prior work.
- Adaptive heuristic: proposes a novel adaptive averaging heuristic with theoretical guarantees that preserves quadratic convergence.
- Empirical validation: includes numerical experiments that provide initial support for the proposed methods but are limited in scope.
- Application breadth: demonstrates acceleration in replicator dynamics, highlighting relevance to fields like evolutionary game theory.
- Presentation: paper is well-written, organized, with consistent notation and clear equations.
- Limited experiments: numerical evaluation is narrow, lacking diverse and higher-dimensional problem tests to robustly validate practicality.
- Theoretical accessibility: analysis is rigorous but potentially hard to follow for readers not versed in Lyapunov theory and convex analysis.
- Comparisons lacking: brief and insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art adaptive methods (e.g., Adam), missing deeper discussion of pros/cons.
- Discretization gaps: discretization choices for the adaptive heuristic are mentioned but not fully justified or analyzed for convergence impact.
- Computational cost missing: no discussion or assessment of the computational overhead for computing adaptive weights and practical feasibility.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theory and novel heuristic, and suggests expanding experiments and clarifying discretization and computational cost.
