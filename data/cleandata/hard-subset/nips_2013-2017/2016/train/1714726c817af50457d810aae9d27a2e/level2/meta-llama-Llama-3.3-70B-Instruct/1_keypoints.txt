- Studies accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics with a generalized averaging scheme and provides sufficient weight-function conditions for desired continuous-time convergence rates.  
- Introduces an adaptive averaging heuristic that empirically and theoretically preserves the AMD convergence rate.  
- Offers a unified Lyapunov-based analysis supporting the main claims and providing clear, detailed proofs.  
- Demonstrates practical usefulness with numerical experiments showing the adaptive heuristic outperforms/reaches parity with restarting heuristics.  
- Novelty centers on the adaptive averaging heuristic and the detailed AMD dynamics analysis for constrained convex problems.  
- Paper is well-written, cites relevant literature, and provides sufficient algorithmic detail for reproducibility.  
- Limitation: results are currently restricted to simplex-constrained problems and may not generalize without further work.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the adaptive averaging heuristic.  
- Limitation: would benefit from additional numerical experiments to broaden empirical validation.  
- Pro-acceptance: strong theoretical analysis, practical heuristic with experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance: narrow problem class, missing complexity analysis, and limited experimental breadth.
