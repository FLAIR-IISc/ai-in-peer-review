Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies a generalized family of accelerated mirror descent (AMD) dynamics parameterized by two time-varying functions: η(t) governing dual gradient accumulation and w(t) governing a weighted averaging of the mirrored dual trajectory. Using a Lyapunov construction, the authors give simple, checkable sufficient conditions on (η, w) to guarantee a prescribed continuous-time convergence rate 1/r(t). They further introduce an “adaptive averaging” heuristic that adjusts the effective averaging rate a(t)=w(t)/W(t) online to make the Lyapunov derivative as negative as possible while preserving the target rate. The work connects to and extends the ODE viewpoints of Nesterov/Su-Boyd-Candès and the variational (Bregman Lagrangian) framework of Wibisono et al., and it relates to practical heuristics such as adaptive restarting (O’Donoghue & Candès). Notably, it yields an accelerated version of the replicator dynamics on the simplex and provides a monotone “energy” functional in addition to the Lyapunov function.

Quality: The theoretical development is careful: existence/uniqueness, the Lyapunov bound (Theorem 2) with an explicit constructive corollary, examples for r(t)=t^p, and a complementary energy decrement (Theorem 3). The discrete-time scheme (Algorithm 1) claims to preserve the O(1/k^2) rate; however, key details and proofs are deferred to the supplement, and practical guidance on step-size s, β, and regularizer R is limited. Experiments illustrate the ideas but are small-scale (R^3 simplex) and lack statistical rigor or large-scale benchmarks.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized. The primal form and worked examples (orthant and simplex) improve readability. Some expository additions would help: a succinct recipe for choosing r, η, and w; explicit pseudocode with defaults; and a clearer discussion of computational overhead.

Originality: The central idea—treating acceleration as generalized averaging and adapting the averaging weights to preserve Lyapunov decrease—appears novel relative to restarting and prior ODE analyses. The accelerated replicator instantiation is a nice, concrete contribution.

Significance: If strengthened empirically and discretely, adaptive averaging could become a practical alternative (or complement) to restarts in accelerated first-order methods, with potential impact in constrained optimization and game-theoretic dynamics.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, general Lyapunov framework with constructive schedules.
- Novel adaptive averaging heuristic with guarantees in continuous time and preserved O(1/k^2) in discrete time.
- Insightful primal forms and accelerated replicator example.

Cons for acceptance:
- Empirical evaluation is limited; no high-dimensional or standard ML tasks (e.g., logistic regression, LASSO).
- Discrete-time analysis is mostly in the supplement; limited guidance on hyperparameter selection and robustness.
- No results beyond preserving known rates (e.g., strongly convex faster rates remain conjectural).

Constructive suggestions: Include main discrete-time proofs or at least a sharper theorem statement in the paper; provide code and larger benchmarks with sensitivity analyses; compare against monotone FISTA, line-search variants, and modern adaptive restarts; study stochastic/noisy gradients; and discuss automatic tuning of β and s and combining adaptive averaging with restart. Overall: promising idea; borderline accept pending stronger experiments and discrete-time exposition.
