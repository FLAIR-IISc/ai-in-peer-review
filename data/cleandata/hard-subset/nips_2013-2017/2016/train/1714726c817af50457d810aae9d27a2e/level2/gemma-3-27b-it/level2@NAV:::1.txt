## Review of "Adaptive Averaging in Accelerated Descent Dynamics"

This paper investigates accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization, framing it as a coupled dual-primal system. The core contribution lies in providing sufficient conditions for convergence based on Lyapunov analysis and proposing an adaptive averaging heuristic for improved performance. The authors demonstrate that replicator dynamics can be accelerated using this scheme and provide empirical evidence suggesting its effectiveness compared to adaptive restarting.

The paper builds upon a recent line of work connecting Nesterov's accelerated methods to continuous-time ODEs (citing Su et al. [20] and Krichene et al. [11]). The theoretical foundation, particularly the use of a Lyapunov function to guarantee convergence, is well-established and rigorously presented. A strength of the paper is the clear derivation of the primal representation of the accelerated dynamics, offering useful insight. The connection to replicator dynamics is also interesting and provides a concrete application of the theory. The proposed adaptive averaging heuristic is intuitive – adjusting weights based on the rate of Lyapunov function decrease – and justified by the theoretical analysis. 

However, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to other adaptive methods beyond adaptive restarting (e.g., Adagrad, Adam, mentioned briefly but not tested). While the numerical results are promising, they are limited in scope (R3, a few objective functions). The justification for choosing the specific initial conditions and parameters in the experiments could be strengthened. The discrete version of the heuristic (Algorithm 1) feels somewhat ad-hoc, and further analysis relating it to the continuous-time theory would be valuable. Moreover, the discussion around the Lyapunov function’s limit when multiple minimizers exist is brief and could benefit from further elaboration.

**Arguments for Acceptance:** The paper presents a solid theoretical contribution, building upon existing work in accelerated optimization. The adaptive averaging heuristic is a novel and well-motivated approach, demonstrating potential improvements over existing methods. 

**Arguments against Acceptance:** The numerical evaluation is limited, and a more comprehensive comparison with related adaptive methods is needed.  The connection between the continuous and discrete formulations could be strengthened.

**Overall:** This is a worthwhile contribution with potential for significant impact. While not a groundbreaking advance, the theoretical results and proposed heuristic are valuable additions to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically: (1) Expand the numerical experiments to include a wider range of problems and comparisons with other adaptive methods. (2) Provide more detailed justification for parameter choices in experiments. (3) Elaborate on the limitations and behavior of the Lyapunov function in the presence of multiple minimizers.
