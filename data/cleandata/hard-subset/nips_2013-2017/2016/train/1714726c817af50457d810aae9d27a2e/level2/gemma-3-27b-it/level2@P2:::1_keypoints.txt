- Studies accelerated descent for constrained convex optimization via coupling dual/primal variables through averaging; provides Lyapunov-based sufficient conditions, an adaptive averaging heuristic, continuous/discrete guarantees, and supportive experiments.

- Principled Lyapunov formulation connects to mirror descent, replicator dynamics, and the authors’ prior ODE work.

- Adaptive averaging heuristic preserves quadratic convergence rate and empirically outperforms adaptive restarting in some cases.

- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.

- Theorem 2’s conditions for Lyapunov stability are restrictive and may be difficult to verify in practice.

- The paper lacks intuition about why those conditions are sufficient and what happens when they fail.

- Comparison to adaptive methods like Adagrad and Adam is superficial; differences and potential synergies need deeper discussion.

- Numerical experiments are limited (mainly simplex constraint), do not generalize to more complex constraints/objectives, and lack statistical rigor (no std devs/p-values).

- The discrete heuristic (Algorithm 1) omits crucial implementation details, notably how to choose parameters β and s.

- Relation to higher-order accelerated methods [23] is not clearly articulated or compared.

- The benefits and limitations of preserving the Lyapunov function are not sufficiently discussed for non-strongly convex settings.

- Strengths: principled Lyapunov approach, strong convergence guarantees, insightful connection to replicator dynamics, and clear presentation.

- Weaknesses: restrictive theoretical conditions, limited experimental scope/statistics, superficial related-work comparison, incomplete algorithm explanation, and inadequate limitations discussion.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound acceleration of mirror descent with valuable convergence guarantees and encouraging experiments.

- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions limiting practicality, weak empirical validation, and insufficient relation to existing methods and limitations.

- Overall assessment: borderline accept contingent on addressing intuition for η and w, expanding experiments and statistical analysis, clarifying Algorithm 1 parameters, and discussing limitations in non-strongly convex settings.
