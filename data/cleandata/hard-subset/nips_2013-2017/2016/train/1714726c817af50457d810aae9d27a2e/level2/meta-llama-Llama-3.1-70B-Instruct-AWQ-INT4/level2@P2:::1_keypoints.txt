- Introduces a novel family of accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization: dual variable accumulates gradients and primal is a weighted average of mirrored dual trajectory.
- Provides sufficient conditions on weight functions to achieve target convergence rates using a Lyapunov argument.
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to accelerate decrease of the Lyapunov function.
- Shows experiments where the heuristic matches or outperforms existing restarting heuristics, with significant improvements in some cases.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a thorough analysis of convergence rates and the energy function.
- Strength: offers a novel and intuitive dual–primal interpretation of accelerated descent dynamics.
- Strength: adaptive averaging heuristic is simple, effective, and preserves the original method’s convergence rate.
- Strength: experiments are well-designed and provide comprehensive comparisons to existing heuristics.
- Weakness: assumes convex, differentiable objectives, limiting applicability to many practical problems.
- Weakness: no theoretical guarantees provided for the heuristic on non-convex objectives.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; request additional computational-complexity analysis and experiments on non-convex objectives.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
