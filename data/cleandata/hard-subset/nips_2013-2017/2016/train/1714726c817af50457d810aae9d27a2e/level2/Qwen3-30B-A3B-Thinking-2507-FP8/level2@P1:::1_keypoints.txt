- Studies accelerated descent dynamics for constrained convex optimization using a continuous-time perspective.  
- Introduces a generalized averaging scheme for mirror descent parameterized by weight functions w and η.  
- Develops a Lyapunov-function analysis to derive sufficient conditions for desired convergence rates.  
- Shows replicator dynamics on the simplex can be accelerated via a simple averaging scheme.  
- Proposes an adaptive averaging heuristic that adaptively computes weights to speed Lyapunov decrease.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the adaptive heuristic in continuous time.  
- Proves the adaptive approach preserves quadratic convergence rates in discrete time.  
- Presents numerical experiments comparing adaptive averaging with heuristics like adaptive restarting.  
- Experiments cover multiple problem types (strongly/weakly convex, linear, KL divergence) and show improved convergence speed.  
- Builds on and extends the authors’ prior NIPS 2016 work and relates to Su et al. (2014) and Krichene et al. (2016).  
- Main contribution: generalizes averaging schemes and introduces a principled, theoretically grounded adaptive heuristic.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound; Lyapunov approach is well-suited and proofs are well-constructed.  
- Adaptive heuristic is well-motivated, practical, and addresses a real optimization problem.  
- Paper clarity is generally good with consistent notation; some sections (Section 2) could use more intuitive explanation for non-experts.  
- Supplementary material provides helpful additional technical context.  
- Originality is strong, with a novel heuristic and an interesting application to replicator dynamics.  
- Significance is high: unified framework for acceleration, practical heuristic with guarantees, and new applications (e.g., evolutionary game theory).  
- Weaknesses: limited explicit connection from continuous to discrete time beyond the quadratic case and experiments could include more diverse problem instances.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper meriting acceptance given theoretical foundations, empirical validation, and practical relevance.
