- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): arg-top-K sparse reads/writes + ANN lookup, LRU allocation, and rollback BPTT for O(log N) time and O(1) per-step space.  
- Empirically shows large runtime/memory savings (up to 1000×/3000×) with comparable accuracy on synthetic NTM tasks, bAbI, and Omniglot one-shot.  
- Builds on MemNN/NTM ideas by replacing dense addressing with sparse approximate content-based access; novelty lies in engineering integration for writeable differentiable memory.  
- Strong practical contribution: clear, effective recipe for scaling writeable external memory with solid asymptotic and empirical claims.  
- Well-motivated engineering: sparse writes, usage-based allocation, and reversible in-place updates for efficient BPTT.  
- Broad applicability: approach can be adapted to other MANNs (e.g., DNC).  
- Novelty is limited relative to MemNN hashing/candidate reduction and RL-NTM; needs clearer differentiation.  
- Critical weakness: arg-top-K is non-smooth — gradients don’t flow to excluded slots; requires ablations on K and comparisons to dense softmax and soft candidate softmax.  
- Must analyze discreteness effects: stability, convergence, gradient starvation, bias/brittleness at decision margins, and attempts at straight-through/soft-top-k/annealing.  
- Needs discussion of treating discrete access as fixed (no learning signal) and limits/implications versus RL-based discrete policies.  
- Reproducibility issues: provide full task generators, curricula, hyperparameters, optimizer details, normalization, thresholds, initialization, BPTT length, and release code.  
- ANN specifics missing: report index type per experiment, ANN hyperparameters (trees/hashes/table sizes), distance metrics, rebuild policy, and cost breakdown.  
- Characterize failure modes/limits: tasks needing blended attention, noisy/non-metric queries, rapidly shifting distributions, high-dimensional ANN degradation, write interference, LRU limits, and sensitivity to K/BPTT truncation.  
- Suggest removing or relocating Section 3.6; avoid calling Omniglot “non-synthetic.”  
- Overall quality: solid engineering and supported complexity claims, but weakened by missing ablations and under-acknowledged discrete gate limitation.  
- Clarity: generally clear but missing key implementation and ANN configuration details for reproduction.  
- Originality: modest conceptual novelty; valuable nontrivial integration of scalable lookup into a writeable MANN.  
- Significance: high practical impact; likely to enable training MANNs at much larger scales.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on requested revisions—ablation on K/ANN choices, discrete-gating analysis, failure characterization, removal of Section 3.6, terminology fixes, and released code/task generators.
