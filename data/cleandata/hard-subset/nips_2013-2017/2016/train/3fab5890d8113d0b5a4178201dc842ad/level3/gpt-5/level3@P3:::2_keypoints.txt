- Paper addresses scaling limits of MANNs by proposing Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that reads/writes only K locations and uses ANN for content addressing.  
- SAM introduces a reversible sparse-update log for efficient backprop that avoids storing full memory states.  
- A simple write policy (write-to-read and LRU) manages sparse writes and allocation.  
- Claims asymptotically optimal complexity (O(log N) per step for addressing, O(1) space per step) and massive speed/memory improvements vs dense NTM.  
- Empirical results show parity or improvement on NTM algorithmic tasks, strong curriculum scaling, competitive bAbI performance, and solid Omniglot one-shot results.  
- Use of ANN indices yields sublinear runtime behavior across wide memory ranges.  
- Strength: clear problem focus and a practical solution that enables very large memories and long BPTT.  
- Strength: straightforward, effective engineering (K-sparsity, ANN, reversible logs) with a neat rollback implementation.  
- Strength: empirically compelling efficiency and performance benchmarks supporting the method.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty — components (top-K sparsification, LRU allocation, ANN lookup, sparse logging) are known and mainly integrated.  
- Weakness: lack of formal analysis of approximation error from K-sparsity and ANN and its effect on gradients and capacity.  
- Weakness: efficiency comparisons may be confounded if dense baselines were not equally optimized or if ANN maintenance costs are underreported.  
- Weakness: missing ablations on sensitivity to K, ANN recall/precision, rebuild frequency, and write policy, making it unclear which components are essential.  
- Weakness: sparse reads drop most gradient signal each step; impact on learning stability and convergence is not quantified.  
- Quality: engineering appears sound and backprop rollback is well described, but complexity claims rely on assumptions (constant K, amortized rebuilds).  
- Clarity: paper is well written but should unify notation, explicitly state gradient flow for sparse reads, move key supplementary assumptions into main text, and clarify rebuild policy/overhead.  
- Originality: contribution is primarily an integration of existing techniques; technical novelty is modest.  
- Significance: could materially expand practical MANN scale and influence practitioners, but limited conceptual novelty reduces its conference impact.  
- Requests: provide ablations (K, exact vs ANN, tree vs LSH, rebuild freq, write policies) and ANN recall metrics correlated with performance.  
- Requests: clarify fairness of speedups vs optimized dense baselines (checkpointing, fused kernels, FAISS-like KNN).  
- Requests: report amortized vs worst-case costs, separate query/insert/delete/rebuild times, and behavior under heavy memory churn or LRU pathologies.  
- Recommendation: lean reject pending stronger theoretical analysis, thorough ablations, and fully fair/optimized baseline comparisons.
