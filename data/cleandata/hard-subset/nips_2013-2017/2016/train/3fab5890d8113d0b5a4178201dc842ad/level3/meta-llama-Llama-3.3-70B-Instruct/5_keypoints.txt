- Introduces BREAD, a novel method for evaluating the accuracy of approximate posterior inference in probabilistic programming.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distributions.  
- Demonstrates BREAD on WebPPL and Stan, showing it can evaluate inference accuracy and guide inference algorithm and model design.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise introduction to background and related work.  
- Provides thorough technical analysis of the method’s properties, limitations, assumptions, and potential biases.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of BREAD.  
- Strength: potential to significantly impact probabilistic programming by providing a new evaluation tool.  
- Strength: detailed discussion of assumptions and biases enhances credibility.  
- Weakness: limited to PPLs that can be viewed as importance sampling over an extended state space.  
- Weakness: requires an exact posterior sample, which can be challenging to obtain in practice.  
- Weakness: experiments focus primarily on WebPPL and Stan; broader language/model coverage is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, significant technical contributions, and supportive experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability, dependence on exact posterior samples, and narrow experimental scope.
