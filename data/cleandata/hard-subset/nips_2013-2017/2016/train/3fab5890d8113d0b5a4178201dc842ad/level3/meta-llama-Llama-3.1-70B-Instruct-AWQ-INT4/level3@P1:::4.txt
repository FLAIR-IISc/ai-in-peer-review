This paper presents a novel approach to scaling memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) by introducing a sparse read and write scheme, called Sparse Access Memory (SAM). The authors effectively address the problem of scaling MANNs, which is a pressing research direction due to its potential for compelling applications with large amounts of memory.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem is clearly presented. The authors provide a thorough background on MANNs, including Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) and Memory Networks, and highlight the limitations of these models in terms of their scalability. They then introduce the SAM architecture, which is an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that retains the representational power of the original NTM while being much more efficient to train with very large memory.

The technical contributions of the paper are strong, and the authors propose several original concepts, including a sparse read operation, a sparse write operation, and an efficient data structure for content-based read operations. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a series of experiments on various tasks, including synthetic tasks, question answering tasks, and one-shot learning tasks.

The results of the experiments are impressive, showing that SAM achieves significant speedups during training while maintaining comparable performance to the original NTM. The authors also demonstrate the ability of SAM to scale to tasks requiring large amounts of memory and long sequence lengths.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and concision. The authors present the problem and solution clearly and effectively, making it easy to follow their argument. The paper is also well-organized, with a logical flow of ideas and a clear structure.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to scaling MANNs, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the time and space complexity of their approach, demonstrating its efficiency and scalability.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to enable the training of neural networks with large amounts of memory, which is a crucial aspect of many applications, including natural language processing and computer vision.
