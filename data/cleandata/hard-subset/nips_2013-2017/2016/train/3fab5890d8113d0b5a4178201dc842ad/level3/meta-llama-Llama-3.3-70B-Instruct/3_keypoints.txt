- Introduces BREAD, a novel method for evaluating approximate posterior inference in probabilistic programming.  
- BREAD is based on bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) and gives a rigorous upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Demonstrates BREAD on multiple models/datasets (linear & logistic regression, matrix factorization, autoregressive time series, mixture-of-Gaussians).  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed methodology and thorough experiments.  
- Provides a detailed comparison with related work (MCMC convergence diagnostics, automatic MCMC configuration).  
- Strength: significant novel contribution to probabilistic programming.  
- Strength: delivers a rigorous bound on a well-established divergence metric.  
- Strength: shows practical applicability through varied experiments.  
- Weakness: requires availability of an exact posterior sample, limiting practical applicability.  
- Weakness: obtaining accurate bounds is computationally intensive, challenging for large-scale models.  
- Weakness: lacks clear discussion of hyperparameter choices and their impact.  
- Overall assessment: strong, rigorous paper but needs to address limitations and hyperparameter analysis.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, rigor, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Con-accept arguments: dependence on exact samples, high compute cost, missing hyperparameter discussion.
