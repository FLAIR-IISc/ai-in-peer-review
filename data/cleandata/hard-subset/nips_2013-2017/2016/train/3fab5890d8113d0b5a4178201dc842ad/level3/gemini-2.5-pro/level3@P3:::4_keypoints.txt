- Problem: MANNs scale linearly with external memory size per time step, making them impractical for very large memories.

- Proposed solution (SAM): restricts memory interactions to a small constant subset using (1) sparse ANN-based content reads (top-K in O(log N)), (2) sparse writes to read locations or LRU slot (constant-time), and (3) sparse BPTT rollback storing only O(1) modifications.

- Empirical claim: SAM achieves up to 1,600× speedup and 3,000× memory savings while matching or improving performance on algorithmic tasks, bAbI, and Omniglot; approach extends to DNC.

- Strength — Significance/impact: solves a major scalability bottleneck and enables MANNs on massive-memory problems; broadly applicable.

- Strength — Originality/technical merit: novel integration of ANN data structures into differentiable models and a clever rollback BPTT to cut space complexity.

- Strength — Motivation & solution quality: clear complexity analysis and well-argued claims about achieving asymptotic space/time bounds for content addressing.

- Strength — Experimental thoroughness: comprehensive benchmarks (speed/memory), curriculum scaling to 200k steps, and varied learning tasks substantiate claims.

- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce and understand contributions.

- Weakness — ANN component complexity: ANN introduces extra hyperparameters and interactions with dynamic memory; sensitivity and trade-off analysis is lacking.

- Weakness — Write mechanism limits: heuristic writes (read locations or LRU) prevent arbitrary content-based writes and may be unsuitable for some tasks.

- Weakness — Approximation error impact: no analysis of how ANN retrieval errors affect learning dynamics or final performance.

- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
