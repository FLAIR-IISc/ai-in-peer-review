- Problem: tackles scalability of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) whose dense attention is computationally prohibitive for large memories.  
- Proposal: Sparse Access Memory (SAM) — constrain reads/writes to a small sparse subset of memory slots.  
- Mechanism: integrate efficient approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) data structures for content-based reads.  
- Complexity: achieves logarithmic time and constant space per timestep, decoupling performance from memory size.  
- Efficiency: reports up to three orders of magnitude gains in runtime and memory usage.  
- Impact: enables scaling MANNs to large, real-world tasks (e.g., entire books or knowledge bases).  
- Empirical support: extensive experiments show computational advantages and that sparsity does not impair — and can improve — learning.  
- Generalization: demonstrates curriculum learning and strong long-range generalization (e.g., from 10k to 200k steps).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of why sparsity yields improved curriculum learning and long-range generalization.  
- Suggested explanation: sparsity may introduce inductive biases/regularization that prevent “smearing” of information/gradients, similar to sparse autoencoders.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, impactful work — strongly recommended for acceptance.
