- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a neural memory architecture with sparse read/write to improve MANN scalability.  
- SAM extends Neural Turing Machines by using sparse operations to enable training with large memories.  
- The paper demonstrates asymptotic space and time efficiency, achieving theoretical lower bounds.  
- Experimental results show SAM is faster and uses less memory than NTM and other MANNs while maintaining comparable task performance.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with thorough explanations of architecture and components.  
- Would benefit from a brief introductory section on memory-augmented neural networks to be more self-contained.  
- Concern: Figure 4’s comparison may be unfair—matching physical memory sizes favors SAM; comparing equal logical memory would be more informative.  
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- The sparse read/write scheme is an original and notable contribution.  
- SAM has significant potential to scale MANNs to large-scale applications.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel architecture, strong empirical and theoretical results, clear significance.  
- Rejection concerns: potentially unfair experimental comparison in Figure 4 and missing brief MANN background.
