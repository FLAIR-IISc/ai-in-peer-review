This paper proposes a novel neural memory architecture called Sparse Access Memory (SAM), which aims to address the scalability issue of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) such as Neural Turing Machines (NTMs). The authors introduce a sparse read and write scheme that allows for efficient computation and memory usage, making it possible to train models with large memories.

The paper is well-structured and easy to read, with a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its components. The authors provide a thorough background on NTMs and MANNs, but a brief introduction to the concept of memory-augmented neural networks would make the paper more self-contained.

The proposed SAM architecture is an extension of the NTM, with the key innovation being the sparse read and write operations. The authors demonstrate that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, making it an efficient solution for large-scale memory-augmented neural networks.

The experimental results show that SAM outperforms the NTM and other MANNs in terms of speed and memory usage, while achieving comparable performance on various tasks. The results are interesting, and the algorithm performs close to theoretical bounds.
