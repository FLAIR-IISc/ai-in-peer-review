- Paper introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), scaling NTM-style read/write via sparse top-K reads and ANN indices with sparse backprop storing deltas for O(1) per-step memory and O(log N) access time.
- Empirically, SAM matches or improves dense NTMs on synthetic tasks, scales to very long sequences and millions of slots, performs well on bAbI (with SDNC) and Omniglot one-shot.
- Key practical contribution: applying ANN and sparse operations to fully writable, end-to-end trained external memory, yielding large speed/memory gains.
- Strength: clear and practical scaling story with sublinear reads, constant writes, and in-place rollback for BPTT.
- Strength: strong empirical results showing up to three orders of magnitude speed/memory improvement and competitive task performance.
- Strength: generality — sparse approach extends to DNC-like temporal link memories and other MANNs.
- Strength: substantial engineering contributions with reproducible systems ideas (ANN indices, sparse data structures, LRU).
- Weakness: novelty is incremental relative to MemNN hashing/WSH and RL-based discrete addressing; needs clearer contextualization.
- Weakness: top-K selection and ANN introduce non-differentiability; the paper should discuss gradient implications, training bias/instability, and K=1 behavior.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure modes — when approximate selection errors accumulate and sensitivity to K, usage threshold, LRU, and ANN hyperparameters.
- Weakness: asymptotic optimality claims need clarified assumptions (fixed K, controller size, index properties) and limits (high-dimensional keys).
- Weakness: reproducibility issues — task curricula, hyperparameters, ANN settings, and data-structure implementation details are missing; code release requested.
- Weakness: wording issue — avoid calling Omniglot “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
- Weakness: suggestion to remove or condense Section 3.6 as it adds little.
- Quality: technically sound and well-engineered with broad empirical evaluation, but needs ablations (K, exact vs ANN, write policies) and approximation-error analysis.
- Clarity: generally clear but requires essential implementation details in main text and tighter exposition of asymptotic analysis and assumptions.
- Originality: conceptually incremental but novel in comprehensive application to writable differentiable memory with sparse BPTT and LRU allocation.
- Significance: high practical impact for training MANNs with very large memories and long sequences.
- Specific revision requests: add ablations on K and ANN parameters, analyze failure regimes, clarify differentiability claims vs. RL-based methods, expand task/curriculum details, release code, and temper Omniglot phrasing.
- Pros: strong scaling, broad experiments, general applicability; Cons: limited conceptual novelty, limited analysis of approximation/discreteness, reproducibility depends on released details.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — solid, practically important contribution.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
