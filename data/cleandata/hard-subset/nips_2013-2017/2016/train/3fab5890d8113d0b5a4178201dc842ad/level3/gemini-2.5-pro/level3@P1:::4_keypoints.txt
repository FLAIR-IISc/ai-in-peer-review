- Problem: Dense attention in MANNs causes linear time complexity and O(NT) BPTT memory, limiting scalability.  
- Contribution: Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) achieving asymptotic optimality in time and space for differentiable external memory.  
- Sparse reads: Uses an ANN index (e.g., k-d tree, LSH) to retrieve K nearest memory slots in O(log N) time.  
- Sparse writes: Writes are limited to recently read locations or the LRU slot to maintain constant-sized updates.  
- Efficient BPTT: Logs only O(1) sparse modifications and reverts them during backprop, reducing per-timestep space to O(1).  
- Empirical efficiency: Demonstrates orders-of-magnitude (e.g., ~1000x) speed and memory improvements over dense NTMs.  
- Empirical performance: Matches or outperforms dense models on algorithmic tasks, bAbI QA, and Omniglot one-shot learning.  
- Scaling: Successfully scales to very long sequences (up to 200k steps) and millions of memory slots, enabling previously infeasible tasks.  
- Significance: Addresses a major bottleneck for long-term reasoning, making the approach broadly impactful for sequence modeling and lifelong learning.  
- Originality & quality: Novel and well-executed integration of sparse attention and ANN structures into an end-to-end differentiable memory system.  
- Theoretical support: Provides well-argued claims of asymptotic optimality backed by empirical verification.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, with clear motivations, design descriptions, and effective figures.  
- Experimental thoroughness: Comprehensive benchmarks, direct dense comparisons, and curriculum-based scaling experiments validate claims.  
- Weakness — ANN interaction: Lacks deeper analysis of ANN approximation effects on learning, rebuild frequency sensitivity, and comparisons across ANN types.  
- Weakness — write mechanism: Uses a heuristic (recent reads or LRU) instead of full content-addressable writes; potential failure modes and alternatives are underexplored.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; minor weaknesses do not outweigh contributions—candidate for spotlight/oral presentation.
