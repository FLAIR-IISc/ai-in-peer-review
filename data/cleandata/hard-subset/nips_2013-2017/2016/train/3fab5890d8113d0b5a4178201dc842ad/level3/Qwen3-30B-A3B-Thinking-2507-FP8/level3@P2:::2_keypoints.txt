- Paper proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a differentiable memory scheme using sparse reads/writes and approximate nearest neighbor search to scale MANNs.
- Authors claim SAM achieves asymptotic optimality and shows up to 1,000× speedup and 3,000× memory reduction vs. dense models.
- SAM constrains reads/writes to sparse subsets and uses efficient data structures for content-based retrieval.
- Empirical evaluation covers synthetic tasks (Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort), Babi reasoning, and Omniglot one-shot classification.
- Technical approach is sound and empirical results are thorough and well-supported.
- Key techniques (sparse updates, approximate nearest neighbor search, sparse backprop) are standard and not novel.
- The paper fails to clearly distinguish its contributions from prior work that used similar sparse memory techniques.
- Clarity is high: well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproduction.
- Originality is limited: contribution mainly applies known techniques to MANNs rather than introducing new algorithms or theory.
- The asymptotic optimality claim is not rigorously proven and theoretical contribution is limited.
- Significance is limited because efficiency gains derive from standard methods and no new capabilities are demonstrated.
- Strengths: thorough experiments, large empirical efficiency gains, clear explanations, and demonstrated scalability to very large memories.
- Weaknesses: lack of technical novelty, insufficient differentiation from prior work, limited theoretical advances, and limited impact on enabling new tasks.
- Pro-accept points: practical efficiency improvements, strong experimental design, clear implementation guidance, and scalability evidence.
- Con-accept points: core ideas are straightforward applications of known techniques, limited theoretical contribution, and no clear new capabilities enabled.
- Overall recommendation: reject—interesting empirical work but lacks the significant technical novelty required for acceptance.
