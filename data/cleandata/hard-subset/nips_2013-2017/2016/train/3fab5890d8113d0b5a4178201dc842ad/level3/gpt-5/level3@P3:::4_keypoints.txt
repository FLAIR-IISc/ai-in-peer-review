- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): a memory-augmented NN with sparse top-K reads (via ANN) and sparse writes with LRU-like allocation.  
- Designs efficient BPTT that records/reverts only sparse deltas, achieving O(1) space per time step and O(T log N) time.  
- Claims O(log N) access time using approximate nearest neighbor indices (randomized k-d trees or LSH).  
- Empirically 100–1600× faster and up to 3000× less memory than dense NTMs at large N.  
- Demonstrates comparable learning on standard NTM tasks, scales to millions of slots and thousands of steps via curriculum.  
- Performs strongly on Omniglot one-shot classification and adapts to DNC to produce SDNC with strong bAbI results.  
- Advances prior MANN work by addressing the linear scaling bottleneck of dense content-based attention.  
- Keeps training fully differentiable at the controller level while touching only a sparse subset per step (unlike RL-based discrete addressing).  
- Integrates ANN within the training loop and uses a pragmatic LRU-like allocator—both presented as novel and useful.  
- Strength: clearly motivated high-impact problem (scaling MANNs to realistic memory sizes).  
- Strength: technically strong contributions (sparse read/write, ANN lookup, constant-space BPTT, simple memory management).  
- Strength: provides asymptotic complexity arguments and convincing wall-clock and memory benchmarks.  
- Strength: method is general and adaptable (e.g., SDNC), and the paper is well organized with clear implementation details.  
- Weakness: top-K selection and ANN index introduce nondifferentiable components; gradients do not flow through selection—formal analysis of bias/convergence is missing.  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies (K, ANN choice, rebuild frequency, write-policy) on accuracy vs. speed trade-offs.  
- Weakness: benchmarks are skewed toward synthetic tasks; lacks large-scale real-world sequence evaluations (e.g., long-context QA or language modeling).  
- Weakness: comparisons could include more scalable memory baselines (key-value memories, cache/pointer mechanisms).  
- Quality: technically sound with proofs in supplement and empirical support, but limited discussion of failure modes.  
- Clarity: very well written; suggests moving a complexity proof sketch and ablation table into the main text.  
- Originality: novel combination of sparse reads/writes, reversible updates for BPTT, and in-network ANN for addressing.  
- Significance: high—enables MANNs to scale to millions of slots and long time horizons with practical implications.  
- Suggestions: add ablations on K/ANN/rebuild/write policy, analyze nondifferentiable selection’s optimization effects, evaluate a large-scale long-memory dataset, release code, and discuss truncated BPTT/catastrophic forgetting.  
- Review recommendation: Accept with high confidence (0.8); arguments for and against acceptance summarized (technical impact vs. nondifferentiability and limited ablations/real tasks).
