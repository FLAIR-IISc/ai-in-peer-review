- Paper introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme for scaling memory-augmented neural networks.  
- SAM adds sparsity to read/write operations, enabling efficient computation and large memories while retaining NTM representational power.  
- Problem and solution are clearly and concisely presented.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness: speed and memory benchmarks, learning with sparse memory access, and curriculum-based scaling.  
- SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, indicating optimal scaling.  
- Strength: technically strong, novel approach addressing a high-impact scaling problem.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with neural and memory-augmented networks, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: mathematical derivations and proofs need more detailed explanations, particularly in supplementary materials.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient comparisons with other state-of-the-art scaling methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand proofs and add more method comparisons.
