- Addresses scalability limitation of Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs) by proposing Sparse Access Memory (SAM) using sparse reads/writes and efficient data structures.  
- SAM yields asymptotic improvements in space and time complexity for MANNs.  
- Builds on Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks to make MANNs practical for complex real-world tasks.  
- Technical approach is sound, with clear explanation of maintaining sparsity in forward and backward passes.  
- Experiments show substantial speedups and memory reductions versus dense models (NTM, DAM) while maintaining or improving task performance (e.g., priority sort).  
- Demonstrates scalability to large memories and long sequences, addressing the core motivation.  
- Extension to the Differentiable Neural Computer highlights the generality of the approach.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, aiding comprehension.  
- Concern: Figure 4 comparison that matches physical memory sizes may be unfair; comparing equal logical memory capacity would be more informative.  
- Concern: Limited detail on the chosen Approximate Nearest Neighbor (ANN) implementation and its performance impact.  
- Suggestion: Add a brief MANN background in the introduction to improve self-containedness for less familiar readers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant scalability gains, strong experimental validation, novel and technically sound contribution, and addresses a critical bottleneck.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential unfair experimental comparison and insufficient ANN implementation discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh minor concerns.
