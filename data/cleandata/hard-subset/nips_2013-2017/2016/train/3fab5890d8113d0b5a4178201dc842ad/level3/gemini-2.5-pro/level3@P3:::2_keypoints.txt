- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): restricts reads/writes to K locations and uses ANN for O(log N) reads plus sparse rollback for O(1) per-timestep space in backprop.
- Addresses scalability of Memory-Augmented Neural Networks by enabling training with very large memories and long sequences.
- Empirically shows up to three orders of magnitude speed/memory improvements and competitive or superior task performance.
- Demonstrates generality by applying sparse access to Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC) and strong bAbI results.
- Significance: tackles a major practical barrier for MANNs, enabling new real-world applications.
- Quality: strong, convincing experimental evaluation and thorough engineering.
- Clarity: well-written, logically explained architecture and effective figures.
- Main weakness: limited technical novelty—combines existing techniques rather than introducing a new algorithmic insight.
- ANN-based sparse read is a natural, standard application of approximate nearest neighbors, not a deep novelty.
- Sparse-update rollback for BPTT is an intuitive implementation trick given the sparse write assumption.
- Paper reads more like systems/engineering work than a fundamental ML contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important problem, outstanding empirical gains, well-executed and generalizable.
- Arguments against acceptance: core ideas are straightforward applications of known methods and may be better suited to a systems venue.
- Recommendation: lean reject—impressive engineering and results but insufficient core algorithmic novelty for NIPS.
