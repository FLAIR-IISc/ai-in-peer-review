- Problem: MANNs scale poorly due to costly dense read/write operations causing high time and memory overhead.  
- Solution: Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), an end-to-end differentiable scheme that reads/writes a sparse subset of memory locations.  
- Technical novelty: Uses sparse read/write operations, novel memory management, and approximate nearest neighbor search for logarithmic-time reads.  
- Complexity gains: Drastically reduces time and space complexity, enabling training with much larger memories.  
- Empirical results: Shows up to 1000x speedup and 3000x memory reduction, with comparable or improved data efficiency across synthetic algorithms, bAbI, and Omniglot; extends to Sparse DNC.  
- Strengths: Significant scalability improvement, technically sound and novel with theoretical bounds, clear presentation, and high potential impact.  
- Weaknesses: Lacks detailed comparative analysis of ANN alternatives (e.g., FLANN, LSH) within SAM.  
- Weaknesses: Hyperparameter choices and sensitivity are insufficiently explored.  
- Weaknesses: Needs deeper discussion of trade-offs between sparsity level and performance.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analysis and sensitivity concerns.
