- Proposes BREAD, an extension of BDMC, to bound symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.
- Applies to algorithms that can be seen as importance sampling over extended state spaces (e.g., AIS, SMC).
- Provides a clear description of BDMC and the extension to BREAD, plus a practical protocol for diagnosing inference on real data.
- Experimental results show BREAD effectively evaluates posterior inference accuracy and illuminates tradeoffs between model representations.
- Well-structured and easy-to-follow presentation with clear background and motivation.
- Strength: BREAD offers a rigorous, general approach to evaluating posterior inference accuracy.
- Strength: Detailed exposition of BDMC and how it extends to BREAD.
- Strength: Experiments substantiate BREAD’s effectiveness and provide useful model-representation insights.
- Weakness: Assumes reader familiarity with BDMC, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: Comparisons between model representations are limited to a specific example and not broadly extensive.
- Weakness: Lacks a deeper discussion of BREAD’s limitations and potential extensions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous method with clear exposition and supported experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, narrow experimental comparisons, and insufficient discussion of limitations/extensions.
- Overall assessment: Significant and useful contribution to probabilistic inference that meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
