- Addresses scalability bottleneck in memory-augmented neural networks by reducing dense read/write costs.  
- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that restricts differentiable operations to a small sparse subset per timestep.  
- Integrates approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) data structures to enable efficient end-to-end differentiable sparse access.  
- Theoretical claims that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude improvements in speed and memory while matching or exceeding dense models, scaling to sequences of hundreds of thousands.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the architecture understandable.  
- Suggests adding a brief background on MANNs or clearer explanation of dense read/write operators to improve accessibility.  
- Experimental concern: comparison in curriculum scaling (Figure 3) matches physical memory but not logical memory, which may unfairly favor SAM; recommend comparisons with equal logical memory sizes.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and empirically validated contribution with minor presentation and experimental-framing issues.
