- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): constrain reads/writes to sparse subsets plus efficient data structures for asymptotic space/time improvements.  
- Reports dramatic efficiency gains: ~1,000x speedup and ~3,000x reduction in physical memory vs dense models.  
- Demonstrates scalability to very long sequences (100,000+ timesteps), overcoming a major MANN limitation.  
- Applies sparse approach to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC), producing a much more efficient variant.  
- Strong empirical performance on NTM tasks, Babi tasks, and one-shot Omniglot, often matching or exceeding prior methods.  
- Technical analysis of space/time complexity and use of sparse matrices and ANN methods is well-motivated and sound.  
- Paper shows clear practical impact by enabling MANNs on larger datasets and complex problems.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of why the sparse approximation preserves performance and how sparsity shapes learned representations.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly sparsity-related settings (number of nonzeros, write thresholds).  
- Weakness: ANN implementation details (choice and trade-offs between FLANN vs LSH) are treated cursorily.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical bottleneck, offers substantial practical advantages, strong empirical evidence, and generalizes to DNC.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks deeper explanation of mechanisms and more thorough hyperparameter/ANN analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
