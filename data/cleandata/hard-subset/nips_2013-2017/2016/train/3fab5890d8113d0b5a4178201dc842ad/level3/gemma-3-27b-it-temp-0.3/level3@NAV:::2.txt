## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

This paper addresses the critical challenge of scaling memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs), which suffer from quadratic space and time complexity as memory size increases. The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme designed to mitigate these limitations. SAM achieves efficiency by restricting read and write operations to a sparse subset of memory locations, coupled with efficient data structures for approximate nearest neighbor search. The core claim is that SAM retains the representational power of traditional MANNs while offering significant improvements in speed (≈1000x) and memory efficiency (≈3000x).

The work builds upon the foundations laid by Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) and Memory Networks, aiming to overcome their scalability bottlenecks. It’s positioned within a growing body of research exploring external memory mechanisms for neural networks, including the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC). The authors demonstrate the adaptability of SAM by extending it to the DNC architecture, resulting in a Sparse DNC (SDNC) with substantial performance gains on the Babi tasks. 

While the experimental results are interesting – showcasing improved speed, reduced memory footprint, and comparable performance on synthetic tasks and Omniglot – I find the technical contribution insufficient for acceptance to NIPS. The core ideas behind SAM – sparse updates and approximate nearest neighbor search – feel relatively straightforward extensions of existing techniques. Efficient backpropagation through time via sparse updates is a natural optimization, and leveraging ANN data structures for content-based access is a well-established practice. The novelty lies primarily in the *combination* of these techniques within the context of MANNs, but the individual components don't represent a significant breakthrough. 

Furthermore, while the speed and memory improvements are impressive, the paper doesn’t deeply analyze the potential trade-offs introduced by sparsity. A more thorough investigation into the impact of sparsity on representational capacity and generalization performance would strengthen the contribution. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
* Demonstrates practical improvements in scalability for MANNs.
* Provides a functional implementation and empirical validation.
* Extends the approach to the DNC, showcasing generality.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
* Technical contribution is incremental rather than groundbreaking.
* Core ideas are relatively straightforward extensions of existing work.
* Limited analysis of the impact of sparsity on model performance.



Overall, this paper presents a valuable engineering effort, but lacks the substantial novelty required for publication at NIPS. The results are encouraging, but further research is needed to establish the theoretical foundations and explore the full potential of sparse memory access schemes.
