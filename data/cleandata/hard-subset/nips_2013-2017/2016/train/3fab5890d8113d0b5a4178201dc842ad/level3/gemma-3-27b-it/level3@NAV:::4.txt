## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in the development of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs): their poor scalability with increasing memory size, stemming from the computational and memory overhead of dense read/write operations. The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM), an end-to-end differentiable scheme that efficiently approximates the representational power of Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) by operating on a sparse subset of memory locations. This drastically reduces both time and space complexity, enabling training with significantly larger memories – a crucial step towards tackling real-world applications.

The work is well-motivated, building on the limitations of existing MANNs like NTMs and Memory Networks. The proposed SAM architecture, with its sparse read/write operations and efficient data structures, represents a significant technical contribution. The authors cleverly leverage approximate nearest neighbor search to achieve logarithmic time complexity for read operations, and propose a novel memory management scheme to track usage and identify available slots for writing. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of SAM, showcasing substantial speedups (up to 1000x faster, 3000x less memory) compared to dense NTMs, alongside comparable or even improved data efficiency on a variety of tasks, including synthetic algorithmic problems, Babi tasks, and one-shot learning with Omniglot. The extension to a Sparse Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC) further highlights the generality of the approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significant Scalability Improvement:** The primary contribution – efficient scaling of MANNs – is convincingly demonstrated with compelling empirical results.
*   **Technically Sound & Novel:** SAM introduces several original concepts (sparse read/write, efficient memory management) presented with theoretical justification (asymptotic complexity bounds).
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly explaining the problem, proposed solution, and experimental setup.
*   **High Impact:** Addressing scalability issues in MANNs has the potential to unlock their application in a wider range of complex, real-world problems. 

**Weaknesses:**

*   While the paper mentions ANN alternatives (FLANN, LSH), a more detailed comparative analysis of their performance within the SAM framework would be beneficial. 
*   The choice of specific hyperparameters and the sensitivity of the model to these parameters could be further explored.
*   A more in-depth discussion of the trade-offs between sparsity level and performance would be valuable. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically strong solution to a significant problem in the field of neural networks.
*   The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The clarity of the presentation makes the paper accessible and impactful.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   While minor, a more detailed analysis of ANN choices and hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the work.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that makes a substantial contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
