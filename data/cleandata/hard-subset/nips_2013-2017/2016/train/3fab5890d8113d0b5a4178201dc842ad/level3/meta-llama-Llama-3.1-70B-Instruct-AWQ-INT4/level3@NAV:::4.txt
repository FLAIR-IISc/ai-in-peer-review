This paper, "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes," introduces a novel end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while training efficiently with very large memories.

The authors propose an innovative approach to scaling memory systems for neural networks, which is a high-impact problem in the field. The paper presents the problem and solution clearly, concisely, and well-explained. The key idea is to introduce sparsity in the read and write operations of the external memory, allowing for efficient computation and memory usage.

The strengths of this paper include:

* The introduction of a novel and technically strong concept, SAM, which addresses the problem of scaling memory systems for neural networks.
* The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the problem and solution, making it easy to understand for readers.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of SAM through various experiments, including speed and memory benchmarks, learning with sparse memory access, and scaling with a curriculum.
* The paper shows that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, making it an optimal solution for scaling memory systems.

The weaknesses of this paper include:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with neural networks and memory-augmented neural networks, which may make it challenging for readers without a strong background in these areas to fully understand the paper.
* The authors could provide more detailed explanations of the mathematical derivations and proofs, especially in the supplementary materials.
* The paper could benefit from more comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods for scaling memory systems, to better evaluate the performance of SAM.

Overall, this paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a significant contribution to the field of neural networks and memory-augmented neural networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of SAM through various experiments and provide a clear explanation of the problem and solution.
