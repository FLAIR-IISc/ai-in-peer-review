**Paper Review: Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes**

**Summary**

This paper introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel neural memory architecture that enables efficient training of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) with large external memories. The authors propose a sparse read and write scheme, which reduces the computational and memory overhead of traditional MANNs, making them scalable to real-world applications. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of SAM on various tasks, including algorithmic tasks, question answering, and one-shot learning on the Omniglot dataset.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability**: The proposed SAM architecture achieves significant speedups and memory reductions compared to traditional MANNs, making it possible to train on large-scale tasks.
2. **Efficient backpropagation**: The authors propose an efficient backpropagation scheme that reduces the space complexity of MANNs, allowing for longer sequence lengths and larger memory sizes.
3. **Good performance**: SAM achieves comparable or better performance than traditional MANNs on various tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Novelty**: The idea of using sparse reads and writes is not entirely new, and similar approaches have been explored in related work (e.g., Memory Networks).
2. **Lack of analysis**: The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the failure cases and limits of the proposed method.
3. **Non-smoothness**: The use of argmax/K-argmax may lead to non-smoothness issues, which are not thoroughly discussed in the paper.

**Suggestions for improvement**

1. **Provide more details on task construction**: The authors should provide more information on how tasks were constructed and evaluated.
2. **Release experiment code**: The authors should release the experiment code to facilitate replicability and further research.
3. **Analyze failure cases**: The authors should analyze the failure cases and limits of the proposed method to better understand its strengths and weaknesses.
4. **Discuss non-smoothness**: The authors should discuss the potential issues with non-smoothness and how they can be addressed.
5. **Remove Section 3.6**: The section appears to be unnecessary and can be removed to improve the paper's clarity.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper, but with revisions and clarifications. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above and provide more details on the task construction and experiment setup. With these revisions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of neural networks and memory-augmented neural networks.
