- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): enforces fixed-K sparse reads/writes and uses ANN (k-d trees/LSH) for top-K retrieval in O(log N) with LRU-like writes and sparse BPTT rollback.
- Theoretical claim of asymptotic optimality under content-based addressing; assumptions and constants currently in the supplement.
- Empirical results show large speed and memory gains over dense NTMs/DAMs (>1000× speedups, up to 3000× memory reductions) and O(1) per-step memory cost.
- SAM matches or outperforms dense baselines on classic NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort), suggesting sparsity can reduce interference and aid early learning.
- Scales to very long sequences via curriculum and generalizes to sequences far beyond training lengths.
- Competitive performance on bAbI and one-shot Omniglot; supplement shows a sparse DNC (SDNC) variant.
- Implementation and benchmarks provided in Torch7.
- Technical soundness: complexity analysis, sparse-tensor implementation, ANN integration, LRU-inspired allocation, and rollback BPTT are well designed and justified.
- Needs clearer statement of optimality assumptions and constants in the main text rather than only in the supplement.
- Requires ablations on K, choice of ANN (kd-tree vs LSH), and ANN recall/latency trade-offs to clarify robustness and failure modes.
- Runtime comparison details (hardware, batch sizes, controller size, exact vs approximate search, rebuild frequency) should be exhaustively documented for fairness.
- Analyze the effect of non-differentiable ANN components on gradient quality and training dynamics; compare exact top-K vs ANN-approximate top-K.
- Clarity improvements suggested: move optimality result into main paper, add pseudocode for write policy and rollback, and provide a hyperparameter summary table.
- Originality: novel, practical integration of fixed-K sparsity and ANN search in differentiable MANNs; efficient rollback is a useful engineering contribution.
- Significance: enables scalable content-addressed external memory (10^6+ slots, 10^5+ time steps) with large speedups while preserving or improving learning.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, widely applicable idea with strong theory, convincing empirical evidence, and thorough engineering.
- Cons against acceptance: limited ablations, optimality claims relegated to supplement, heuristic write policy not deeply analyzed for pathological behaviors.
- Actionable suggestions: analyze interference and gradient SNR, vary K and memory size, test more ANN structures, provide exact vs ANN training comparisons, report write-policy sensitivity, and release code.
- Overall assessment: well written, technically solid, and impactful paper; minor clarifications and additional ablations would make it a strong accept.
