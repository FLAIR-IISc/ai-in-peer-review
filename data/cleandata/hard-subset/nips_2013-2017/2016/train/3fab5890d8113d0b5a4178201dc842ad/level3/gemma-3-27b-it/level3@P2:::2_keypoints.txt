- Addresses a critical scalability bottleneck in memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs).
- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) using sparse read/write operations and efficient data structures.
- Uses approximate nearest neighbor search for content-based reads to avoid linear memory scans.
- Demonstrates large empirical improvements: up to ~1000x speedups and ~3000x memory savings versus dense models.
- Maintains comparable data efficiency on algorithmic tasks (NTM), bAbI, and Omniglot, and scales to long sequences/large memories.
- Shows generality by applying the sparse approach to the Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC) with improved performance.
- Technical novelty is limited; sparse updates, ANN search, and sparse backprop are incremental and established techniques.
- Claim of asymptotic optimality is insufficiently justified.
- Efficient backprop implementation is a straightforward application of tracking sparse modifications, not a novel method.
- Integrating ANN search into MANNs is natural but not a new algorithmic contribution.
- Lacks an ablation study to separate the effects of sparse reads vs writes and different ANN implementations.
- Benchmark comparisons are somewhat uneven; SDNC results use a different supervision scheme than comparable work.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-documented with helpful supplementary material.
- Originality is moderate—the combination is useful but underlying techniques are not novel.
- Results have significant practical impact for scaling MANNs but limited theoretical significance.
- Reviewer view: solid engineering contribution but incremental; recommends rejection for NIPS and suggests a workshop or applied venue.
