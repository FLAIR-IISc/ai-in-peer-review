- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): end-to-end differentiable memory with sparse read/write enabling training with millions of memory words.
- Well-motivated and clearly explained approach, including sparse operations, memory management, and efficient backpropagation through time.
- Experimental results show significant speedups and memory savings versus the original NTM and baseline models.
- Technically sound with thorough time and space complexity analysis achieving asymptotic lower bounds.
- Original contribution: novel combination of techniques and clear distinction from prior work, with a comprehensive related-work review.
- High significance: potential impact on applications like language modeling and machine translation; demonstrated on one-shot Omniglot and bAbI QA tasks.
- Limited evaluation on real-world tasks; needs more extensive real-world benchmarks.
- Insufficient comparison to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Differentiable Neural Computer).
- Could benefit from more discussion of limitations and directions for future work.
- Overall assessment: well-written, strong contribution; rating 9/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
