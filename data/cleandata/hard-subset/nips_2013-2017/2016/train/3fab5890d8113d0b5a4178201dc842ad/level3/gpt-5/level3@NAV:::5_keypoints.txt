- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): K-sparse reads/writes, ANN indices for O(log N) access, and reversible sparse BPTT with incremental updates.  
- Claims SAM retains differentiability and is ~10^3× faster and ~10^3× more memory-efficient than dense NTMs.  
- Demonstrates matching or surpassing accuracy on classic NTM tasks, scalable curricula, generalization from ~10k to ~200k steps, strong Omniglot one-shot, and SDNC bAbI results.  
- Positions work within MANN literature (NTM, Memory Networks, DNC) and contrasts with discrete/RL addressing approaches.  
- Quality: solid asymptotic analysis, thoughtful systems design (ANN integration, LRU-like allocation, reversible BPTT), and convincing empirical evaluation.  
- Missing ablations: sensitivity to K and ANN recall not evaluated.  
- Missing ablations: impact of write policy (previously read vs least-recently-used) not studied.  
- Missing fairness checks: some comparisons may conflate architectural improvements with much larger external memory (capacity-matched baselines needed).  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but needs ANN rebuild cadence and hyperparameters specified.  
- Clarity: report K and word-size choices per task and provide pseudocode for rollback/backprop logic.  
- Reproducibility: public code recommended due to engineering complexity.  
- Originality: high—novel combination of top-K differentiable access, in-network ANN, and reversible sparse BPTT.  
- Significance: high—three-order-of-magnitude gains could make MANNs practical and widely usable.  
- Hypothesized mechanism: K-sparse reads reduce gradient dilution/interference and LRU allocation enforces episodic, low-overlap storage, acting as a sparsity prior.  
- Recommended experiments: measure retrieval overlap/interference, vary K, swap dense vs sparse reads at equal capacity, and compare LRU vs alternative allocation.  
- Pros for acceptance: dramatic speed/memory improvements, scalable reversible BPTT, strong empirical performance across tasks, and clear practical applicability.  
- Cons against acceptance: missing ablations/theoretical analysis of generalization, potential capacity-mismatch in baselines, and limited robustness analysis to ANN/K.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept contingent on adding ablations and releasing implementation details.
