- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel sparse read/write scheme to scale memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs).  
- Claims SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity.  
- Leverages approximate nearest neighbor indexes (randomized k-d trees, LSH) to enable large-memory scalability.  
- Demonstrates comparable data efficiency to existing models.  
- Strong experimental validation on synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot, and bAbI QA, showing scalability to long sequences and large memories.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes helpful figures and tables.  
- Provides detailed analysis of space/time complexity and useful comparisons to existing models.  
- Technical contributions are significant and address key challenges in scaling MANNs.  
- Weakness: does not provide a detailed analysis of limitations and trade-offs of the sparse read/write approach.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison to other proposed approaches for scaling MANNs.  
- Weakness: unclear how the approach generalizes or applies to other domains and tasks.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall recommendation: positive—paper makes a significant contribution and is argued as worthy of acceptance.
