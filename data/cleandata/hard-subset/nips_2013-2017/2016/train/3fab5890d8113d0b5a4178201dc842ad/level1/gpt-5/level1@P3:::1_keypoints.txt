- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse top-K reads, sparsified writes, ANN index for sublinear lookup, and rollback BPTT for efficiency.
- Claims asymptotically optimal time/space for content-addressed MANNs, practical speed/memory gains vs dense NTMs, and comparable data efficiency on several tasks.
- Extends approach to a sparse Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC).
- Builds directly on NTMs, Memory Networks, DNC, and MANNs for meta-learning; preserves differentiability unlike hard-attention/RL approaches.
- Uses kd-tree/LSH ANN techniques sensibly; engineering contributions (rollback, CSR sparse ops) are valuable.
- Sparse reads normalize over top-K; writes restricted to read slots and LRU slot to ensure O(1) write sparsity.
- ANN treated as non-differentiable index; gradients flow only through selected entries.
- Rollback BPTT stores O(1) sparse diffs per step, enabling O(T) memory and O(1) amortized reconstruction time.
- Complexity claims plausible but practical constants (e.g., ANN rebuild cost) matter.
- Empirical results show sparsification does not harm learning and can help; strong long-sequence curriculum generalization up to 200k steps.
- Benchmarks demonstrate substantial wall-clock and peak-memory savings.
- Comparisons control for physical memory rather than slot count, conflating capacity with computational constraints.
- Paper is generally clear with concrete algorithms; some notational issues and deferred implementation details reduce clarity.
- Novel integration of top-K sparse reads, sparse writes, ANN, and rollback BPTT into a differentiable, scalable memory system.
- If robust, contribution significantly improves practicality of content-addressed external memory for long-context tasks.
- Strengths: clear motivation, simple/sound sparse mechanisms, effective rollback, convincing benchmarks, diverse task demos, reusable engineering.
- Weakness: non-differentiability of selection and ANN may bias learning; lacks discussion/ablations on K and masking effects.
- Weakness: missing sensitivity analyses for ANN choices (kd-tree vs LSH), recall metrics, rebuild schedule, and index overhead.
- Weakness: simple LRU/previous-read write policy may be brittle; needs comparison to learned allocation and temporal links.
- Weakness: fairness concerns in baseline comparisons—controller sizes, training budgets, parameter/FLOP matching unclear.
- Weakness: theoretical optimality claims need formal assumptions and summary in main text rather than supplement.
- Weakness: lacks evaluation on large real-world sequence tasks (e.g., long-context language modeling or retrieval QA).
- Clarity fixes needed: resolve symbol overloading, define K/thresholds consistently, briefly explain CSR and rollback in main body.
- Key author questions: sensitivity to K, ANN recall of true top-K, effect of sparse content-based writes, support for multiple heads without complexity loss, and interference from erase-on-LRU.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses key MANN bottleneck, strong engineering and complexity improvements, broad applicability, likely to influence future work.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient ablations/analysis of ANN and sparsity hyperparameters, theoretical claims under-supported, few large-scale real-world benchmarks, and some comparison ambiguities.
