- Addresses scalability bottleneck in Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs), especially Neural Turing Machines (NTMs).  
- Identifies computational and memory costs from dense read/write operations with large external memories.  
- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads/writes plus efficient data structures to scale MANNs.  
- Theoretical analysis shows asymptotic improvements in time and space complexity.  
- Empirical results report large speedups (~1,000x) and memory savings (~3,000x) with little performance loss on synthetic tasks and Omniglot.  
- SAM is adaptable to other architectures (e.g., Differentiable Neural Computer) and improves performance on bAbI tasks.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and supplemented for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited investigation of how sparsity affects the learning of algorithmic solutions or introduces inductive biases.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choice of ANN implementations (k-d trees vs LSH).  
- Weakness: discussion of future work (e.g., Mondrian forests, LSH forests) is cursory and lacks concrete plans.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important scalability problem, presents a well-motivated solution, and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited exploration of sparsity’s learning effects, ANN choice justification shortfall, and underdeveloped future work discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with clear contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
