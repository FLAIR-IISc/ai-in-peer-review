## Review of “Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes”

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs): their poor scalability in both space and time complexity as memory size increases. The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme that achieves asymptotic lower bounds in both space and time by restricting read and write operations to a sparse subset of memory locations. This is a significant contribution, as it directly tackles a major limitation hindering the application of MANNs to real-world problems. The work builds upon prior research in MANNs like Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) [7] and Memory Networks [21], aiming to overcome their linear computational overhead during training. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the SAM architecture, including its efficient implementation details like approximate nearest neighbor search and in-place backpropagation. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating a 1,000x speedup and 3,000x reduction in memory usage compared to non-sparse models.  Furthermore, SAM maintains comparable data efficiency on synthetic tasks and one-shot Omniglot recognition, and successfully scales to tasks requiring large memory and long sequences. The extension to a Sparse Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC) further highlights the generality of the approach. 

**Strengths:**

* **Significant Scalability Improvement:** The core contribution – achieving asymptotic lower bounds in space and time – is a substantial advancement.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The results convincingly demonstrate the practical benefits of SAM across various tasks and scales.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is easy to follow, with a logical flow and detailed explanations.
* **Generality:**  Adaptation to the Differentiable Neural Computer demonstrates the broad applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **ANN Implementation Details:** While the use of approximate nearest neighbors is mentioned, more details on the specific ANN implementation and its impact on performance would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper could benefit from a more thorough analysis of the sensitivity of SAM’s performance to different hyperparameters, particularly the sparsity level (K).
* **Comparison to Other Scaling Approaches:**  A more detailed comparison to other recent work on scaling MANNs, such as reinforcement learning-based addressing schemes [22], would strengthen the paper's positioning.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a fundamental limitation of MANNs.
* Presents a technically sound and well-validated solution.
* Demonstrates significant improvements in scalability and efficiency.
* Has the potential to broaden the applicability of MANNs to real-world problems.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*  The impact of the ANN implementation could be more thoroughly investigated.
*  A more comprehensive comparison to alternative scaling techniques would be valuable.



**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The proposed SAM architecture offers a promising solution to the scalability problem, and the experimental results are compelling. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh its limitations, and I recommend acceptance.
