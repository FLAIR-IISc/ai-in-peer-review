- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads/writes plus ANN indexing for scalable memory-augmented networks.
- Uses top-K nearest-neighbor reads (kd-trees/LSH) and a usage-based allocator that writes to recently read slots and an LRU slot.
- Saves BPTT memory by storing sparse in-place diffs and rolling back updates during backward pass, yielding O(1) space per step and O(log N) time operations.
- Builds on NTMs, Memory Networks, and DNC, and pragmatically integrates FLANN kd-trees/LSH into a differentiable system.
- Empirically achieves large wall-clock and memory savings (up to ~1000× speed, ~3000× less memory) while matching or exceeding dense baselines on classic tasks.
- Scales to millions of memory slots and thousands of untruncated BPTT steps; strong bAbI and Omniglot one-shot results at large sequence lengths.
- Strength: Compelling scalability with theoretical asymptotics supported by careful engineering and empirical curves.
- Strength: Retains learning performance despite sparsity and approximate retrieval, generalizing to much longer sequences.
- Strength: General approach extends to DNC (SDNC) and achieves strong unsupervised bAbI results.
- Strength: Practical, simple, and reproducible design choices (LRU-like allocator, write scheme, BPTT rollback).
- Weakness: Top-K neighbor selection is non-differentiable; paper lacks analysis of gradient bias or comparisons to soft top-K approximations.
- Weakness: Limited ablations — sensitivity to K, usage threshold ε, ANN type, and rebuild frequency is not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness: Benchmark scope is narrow, focusing on synthetic tasks and Omniglot; lacks large-scale real-world sequence modeling results.
- Weakness: Key theory and implementation details (optimality assumptions, rollback pseudocode) are relegated to the supplement instead of the main text.
- Weakness: Speed comparison transparency needs more details on hardware, libraries, and ANN parameters for reproducibility.
- Evaluation — Quality: Strong algorithmic design and implementation; empirical and formal claims plausible though some theory is supplemental.
- Evaluation — Clarity: Core model is clear, but crucial implementation/theory details should be summarized in the main text.
- Evaluation — Originality: High; novel combination of sparse end-to-end training, efficient BPTT via rollback, and practical ANN integration.
- Evaluation — Significance: High; enables feasible training with millions of slots and long BPTT, opening practical MANN applications.
- Requests: Provide ablations on K and ε, detailed sensitivity to ANN rebuild frequency/parameters, and consider hybrid write schemes.
- Requests: Report end-to-end wall-clock times for large curricula, specify hardware/libraries, and release code for reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses scalability with a simple, effective solution showing strong empirical improvements and generality.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited real-world evaluations and insufficient analysis of non-differentiable neighbor selection.
- Overall assessment: Well-executed, original, and practically significant work; recommend acceptance despite missing ablations and supplemental-theory reliance.
