- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads (top-K via ANN), sparse writes (previous-read + LRU), and reversible in-place updates for O(1) BPTT space.
- Integrates ANN structures (k-d trees or LSH) to scale external memory to millions of slots with claimed O(log N) time per step.
- Extends approach to Differentiable Neural Computer, yielding a Sparse DNC (SDNC).
- Empirically 100–1600× faster and uses orders of magnitude less physical memory than dense NTMs at large N.
- Matches or outperforms dense baselines (NTM, DAM, LSTM) on synthetic NTM tasks and scales to >4000 time steps and millions of slots.
- Strong unsupervised bAbI results (especially with SDNC) and improved one-shot Omniglot performance on long episodes.
- Sparsified read/write mechanism and reversible update scheme are well-motivated; complexity claims are plausible but detailed in supplement.
- Complexity arguments need clarified assumptions (fixed small K, word-dimension regimes, periodic ANN rebalancing costs).
- Neighbor selection via ANN is non-differentiable; gradients don’t flow through the index and selection dependence on queries is ignored.
- The ANN-based selection approximation works empirically but requires discussion of stability, bias, and missed-neighbor effects.
- Speed and memory benchmarks compelling and relevant to the paper’s goals.
- Some experimental comparisons conflate capacity with algorithmic efficiency (dense baselines given tiny memories vs SAM’s huge memory).
- Ablation studies are limited: missing analyses on K, ANN choice (kd-tree vs LSH), ANN recall/latency trade-offs, and write-policy sensitivity.
- bAbI reporting should include SAM-only results across the full suite and detailed training budgets/hyperparameters for fairness.
- Reproducibility improved by supplement but main text should include pseudocode for forward/backward memory updates and concrete ANN hyperparameters (trees/hashes, rebuild cadence).
- Presentation is generally clear with good high-level narrative; some notation (I_U, thresholds) and gate roles (α_t, τ_t) need streamlining and explicit ranges.
- Read/write equations and control flow would benefit from a concise algorithmic summary in the main text.
- Novelty: combining hard sparse top-K access via external ANN in end-to-end training with reversible sparse updates is original and impactful.
- Significance: high — addresses a key scalability bottleneck for memory-augmented models and enables long-context reasoning/continual learning use cases.
- Pros: clear scalability contribution, large practical speed/memory gains, competitive learning performance, SDNC boosts bAbI, well-presented related work.
- Cons: central non-differentiable ANN selection under-discussed theoretically, limited ablations, capacity-vs-efficiency confounds, limited real-world task evaluation, missing practical ANN details.
- Suggested improvements: perform ablations (vary K, compare exact vs ANN, kd-tree vs LSH), include pseudocode and ANN hyperparameters, report SAM-only bAbI, discuss kd-tree limitations and rebuild costs, explore sparse content-based writes.
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution with minor-to-moderate missing analyses; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
