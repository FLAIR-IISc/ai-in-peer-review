- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a scalable MANN with sparse read/write (K=4–8) plus ANN indexing.  
- Achieves O(log N) time per step and O(1) space per step while remaining end-to-end differentiable.  
- Empirical validation on Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort, Babi QA, and Omniglot one-shot learning.  
- Claims up to 1,000× speedup and 3,000× memory reduction versus dense NTMs.  
- Strength — strong theoretical analysis of asymptotic optimality and rigorous empirical results.  
- Strength — sparse write scheme (least-recently-accessed) and efficient backpropagation are technically sound.  
- Strength — significant impact: enables training over >100k time steps and millions of memory slots; SDNC extension and strong Babi performance.  
- Strength — original: first end-to-end differentiable sparse-access MANN integrated with ANN indexing.  
- Weakness — lacks sufficient explanation for why sparsity improves learning on certain tasks (e.g., Priority Sort).  
- Weakness — ANN implementation choices (k-d trees vs. LSH) and tradeoffs are underexplained.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — high-impact, solid theory and experiments; weaknesses are minor and fixable.
