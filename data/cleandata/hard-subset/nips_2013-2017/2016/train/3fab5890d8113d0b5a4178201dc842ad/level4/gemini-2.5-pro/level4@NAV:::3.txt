This paper presents a novel memory access scheme, Sparse Access Memory (SAM), for use in memory-augmented neural network architectures. The work is situated within the relatively new domain of Memory-Augmented Neural Networks, a field initiated by the Neural Turing Machine (NTM), which aims to overcome the limitations of traditional models like LSTMs.

The primary contribution is a mechanism that constrains all read and write operations to a fixed-size subset of memory words, rather than the entire external memory. The authors provide theoretical proofs for the proposed methodology and its optimality. As demonstrated in Section 4.1, this approach yields significant read/write performance gains, which can be attributed to the reduced memory access footprint.

Crucially, the evaluation extends beyond performance by analyzing the learning costs associated with this induced sparsity (Section 4.2). Using standard NTM benchmark tasks (Copy, Associative Recall, and Priority Sort), the authors show that SAM achieves a lower combined cost than the standard NTM on the Associative Recall and Priority Sort tasks. The inclusion of a real-world application in Section 4.4 is a commendable addition that strengthens the paper's practical relevance. The experiments were benchmarked using the Torch7 framework.

The core idea is both novel and elegantly straightforward. The reported speedup over the baseline NTM is impressive and significant. A particularly interesting finding, detailed in Section 4.2, is that the sparse access mechanism can, in some instances, improve learning during its early stages. The paper would be further strengthened if the authors could elaborate on the conditions under which this learning improvement occurs, perhaps by exploring a generalization. Additionally, investigating the integration of SAM with different neural network controller architectures would be valuable for the community.

Overall, this is a well-written and engaging paper. The authors clearly state their assumptions, and the architectural details are thoroughly documented in the appendix. It makes for an interesting read.
