The paper introduces a Sparse Access Memory architecture for neural networks augmented with memory, such as Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, achieving remarkable run-time and memory efficiency improvements of three orders of magnitude compared to existing architectures. This architecture also demonstrates significant performance improvements in the context of curriculum learning and generalization to sequences an order of magnitude longer than those observed in training, from 10,000 to 200,000 in testing. This paper has the potential to be groundbreaking.

The field of neural networks augmented with memory is actively developing, with Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks being notable examples. While research in this area has yielded interesting results, they have not yet reached a level of practical applicability. The drastic efficiency and performance improvements reported in this paper, however, suggest that memory-augmented neural networks are now ready for practical use, which could have far-reaching implications.

One aspect that is missing from the paper is a discussion on the reasons behind the drastic performance improvements in curriculum learning and the ability to predict sequences significantly longer than the training range. Run-time and memory efficiency alone cannot account for these improvements, indicating that other factors are at play. A possible explanation could be that the Sparse Memory Access architecture plays a role similar to that of Sparse Auto-encoders in other situations. The authors' thoughts on this, even if preliminary, would be valuable and of interest to readers.
