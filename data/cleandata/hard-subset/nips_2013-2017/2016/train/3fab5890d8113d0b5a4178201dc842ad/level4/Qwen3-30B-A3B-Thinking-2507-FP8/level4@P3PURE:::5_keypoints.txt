- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) architecture for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).
- Reports ~three orders of magnitude improvements in runtime and memory efficiency versus existing memory-augmented networks.
- Shows substantial curriculum-learning gains and generalization to sequences up to 200,000 steps despite training on sequences up to 10,000.
- Claims these results make memory-augmented networks practically viable and represent a significant advancement with broad implications.
- Notes a missing analysis of the mechanisms behind the curriculum and long-sequence generalization—efficiency alone does not explain these gains.
- Recommends the authors consider whether SAM operates analogously to sparse autoencoders and include that perspective, even if preliminary.
