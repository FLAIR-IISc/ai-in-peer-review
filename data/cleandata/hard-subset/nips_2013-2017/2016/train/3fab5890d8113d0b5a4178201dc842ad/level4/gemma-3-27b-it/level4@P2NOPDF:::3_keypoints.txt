- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), constraining read/write to a fixed-size subset of memory locations in memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Provides theoretical justification and argues potential optimality of the sparse access approach.  
- Argues reduced search space from limited access plausibly improves read and write performance (Sec. 4.1).  
- Evaluates SAM on standard NTM tasks (Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort) to assess learning costs (Sec. 4.2).  
- Shows overall computational cost is lower than standard NTMs for some tasks (notably Associative Recall and Priority Sort).  
- Includes a real-world example demonstrating SAM’s applicability beyond synthetic benchmarks (Sec. 4.4).  
- Implements SAM in Torch7 and reports significant speedups compared to NTMs.  
- Observes that sparse reads/writes with a linear layer or ANN for addressing can benefit early-stage learning (Sec. 4.2).  
- Recommends further study on when SAM consistently improves learning and evaluation with different ANN addressing architectures.  
- Paper is well-written, assumptions are explicit, and architectural details are available in the appendix; overall a promising contribution.
