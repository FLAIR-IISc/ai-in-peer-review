This paper presents the Sparse Access Memory architecture, a novel approach to enhancing neural networks with memory, as exemplified by Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks. The proposed architecture yields remarkable gains in runtime and memory efficiency, boasting improvements of three orders of magnitude over existing memory-augmented neural networks. Furthermore, it demonstrates significant performance enhancements in the context of curriculum learning and generalization to sequences substantially longer than those encountered during training, with lengths increasing from 10,000 to 200,000. This work has the potential to be groundbreaking. The field of neural networks augmented with memory, initiated by Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, has been rapidly evolving, with intriguing research and experimental results. However, these advancements had not yet reached a level of practical applicability. The substantial efficiency and performance improvements reported in this paper suggest that memory-augmented neural networks are now poised for practical implementation, which may have far-reaching implications. One aspect that warrants further discussion is the underlying reason for the drastic performance improvements observed in curriculum learning and the ability to predict sequences far beyond the training range. While runtime and memory efficiency cannot solely account for these improvements, it is likely that the Sparse Memory Access architecture plays a crucial role, akin to the function of Sparse Auto-encoders in other contexts. The authors' insights, even if preliminary, would be valuable in shedding light on this phenomenon.
