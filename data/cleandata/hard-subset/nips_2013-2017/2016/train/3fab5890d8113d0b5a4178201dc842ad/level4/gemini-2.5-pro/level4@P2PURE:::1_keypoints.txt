- Proposes using fast ANN and top-K selection for attention in NTMs, showing no performance degradation and enabling larger-scale training.  
- Engineering and empirical validation are valuable contributions.  
- Conceptual novelty is limited because hashing-based scalable memory lookups have been explored in Memory Network work.  
- Novel aspect: applying these techniques to a writeable memory (NTM) appears new and practically useful.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on revisions.  
- Reproducibility: paper lacks sufficient experimental detail; must precisely describe task construction and ideally release experimental code.  
- Important context: original NTM code was never released and no standard benchmarks exist, increasing the need for full task details.  
- Analysis of limitations/failure modes: must discuss non-differentiability of argmax/K-argmax and explain why the approach works in practice (role of K, task properties, etc.).  
- Training without REINFORCE: analyze boundary conditions for success — which tasks and setups tolerate direct training of discrete actions and where it breaks down.  
- Minor suggestions: consider removing Section 3.6.  
- Minor suggestions: avoid describing Omniglot as "non-synthetic" or "real-world."
