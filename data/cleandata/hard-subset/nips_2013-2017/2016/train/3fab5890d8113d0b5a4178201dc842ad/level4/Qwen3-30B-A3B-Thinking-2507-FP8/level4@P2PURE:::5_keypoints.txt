- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for neural networks with external memory (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).
- Reports massive runtime and memory efficiency gains—about three orders of magnitude over prior memory-augmented models.
- Shows strong empirical results in curriculum learning and generalization to sequences 20× longer than training (10k → 200k tokens).
- Claims the work could be a groundbreaking advance, making memory-augmented networks practical for real-world deployment.
- Notes that prior research had not achieved this level of practical applicability until now.
- Main omission: no discussion of the underlying mechanisms driving the curriculum learning and long-sequence generalization benefits.
- Recommends the authors offer a hypothesis or perspective (e.g., analogy to sparse autoencoders) on those mechanisms, even if preliminary.
