This paper presents an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while being much more efficient to train with very large memories. It introduces several original concepts that appear technically strong. The problem it targets—scaling memory-augmented neural networks—is well motivated and effectively addressed. Given the substantial potential impact of scalable memory systems for neural networks, this work is important, and the paper presents both the problem and the solution clearly, concisely, and convincingly.
