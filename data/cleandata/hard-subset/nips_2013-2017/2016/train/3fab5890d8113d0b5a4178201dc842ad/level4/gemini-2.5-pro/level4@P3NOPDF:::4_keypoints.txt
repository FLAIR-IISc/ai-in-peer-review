- Proposes a novel end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that preserves NTM representational power while enabling much more efficient training with very large memories.  
- Significant technical innovation that addresses computational and memory overheads which have limited practical scaling of NTMs.  
- Tackles an important problem—scaling memory for long-term dependencies, one-shot learning, and algorithmic reasoning—with clear and compelling motivation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with precise architectural descriptions and informative figures that aid understanding.  
- Experiments support efficiency claims but lack rigorous scaling analysis (e.g., log-scale plots of training time and GPU memory as memory grows by orders of magnitude).  
- Requires ablation studies to disentangle the contributions of the model’s novel components to performance and efficiency.  
- Comparison is mainly to the original NTM; needs discussion or empirical comparison with other recent memory-augmented architectures.  
- Overall an excellent, timely contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance; weaknesses are minor suggestions to strengthen the work.
