This paper addresses a key limitation of most memory-augmented neural networks: their poor scaling in terms of computational and memory requirements as the size of the memory grows. To overcome this, the authors propose a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory. They demonstrate that their approach can retain the representational power of denser models while being dramatically more efficient, achieving up to a 1000x speedup in training and a 3000x reduction in memory usage.

Despite these impressive empirical results, I find the paper's technical contribution to be insufficient for acceptance. The core technical components—an efficient backpropagation method and the use of approximate nearest neighbor search for the sparse memory—are, in my assessment, straightforward applications of existing ideas. While the experiments are certainly interesting, the limited novelty of the underlying techniques weakens the overall contribution. For these reasons, I cannot recommend acceptance of the paper.
