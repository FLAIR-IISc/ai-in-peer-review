This paper introduces a novel memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) in memory-augmented neural network architecture, a research area that has gained significant attention since the invention of Neural Turing Machines. This augment allows neural networks to learn beyond the limits of traditional LSTM models. The key contribution of this paper is that all writes to and reads from external memory are constrained to a fixed size, thus a subset of the memory words, instead of unlimited size. The authors rigorously prove the methodology and its optimality, demonstrating that it achieves good read and write performance due to reduced memory size, as shown in section 4.1.

Moreover, the authors investigate the learning costs of introducing sparsity in section 4.2 using standard NTM tasks: copy, associative recall, and priority sort. Notably, the combined costs are lower than NTM for some tasks (i.e., associative recall and priority sort). The inclusion of real-world examples in section 4.4 is also commendable. The benchmark is implemented using Torch7, and the idea is straightforward and novel. The speedup is significant compared to NTM, which is impressive.

One of the most interesting findings is that sparse reads and writes, facilitated by either a linear or an approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) index, can actually benefit early-stage learning in some cases (section 4.2). It would be helpful if the authors could elaborate on how to generalize this observation to other cases or by exploring different ANN approaches with SAM. This would provide valuable insights for the audience.

Overall, the paper is well-written, with all assumptions clearly laid out and architecture details provided in the appendix. The paper is engaging to read, and the authors' enthusiasm for the subject matter is evident throughout.

Strengths:
- Novel and straightforward idea
- Significant speedup compared to NTM
- Investigation of learning costs and benefits of introducing sparsity
- Inclusion of real-world examples
- Well-written paper with clear assumptions and architecture details

Weaknesses:
- Limited exploration of generalizing the observation to other cases or different ANN approaches
- Some sections could be further expanded for clarity

Originality:
- The paper introduces a novel memory access scheme, SAM, which is a significant contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks.
- The idea of using sparse reads and writes to improve performance is novel and has not been explored before in this context.

Quality:
- The paper is technically sound, with rigorous proofs and demonstrations of the methodology's optimality.
- The authors provide a thorough investigation of the learning costs and benefits of introducing sparsity.
- The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

Significance:
- The paper has the potential to significantly impact the field of memory-augmented neural networks, enabling the training of larger and more complex models.
- The results have implications for real-world applications, such as natural language processing and machine translation.

Recommendations:
- The authors should consider exploring different ANN approaches with SAM to further generalize their findings.
- Some sections, such as section 4.2, could be expanded for clarity and to provide more insights into the benefits and limitations of introducing sparsity.
- The authors may want to consider including additional real-world examples to further demonstrate the applicability of their approach.

Acceptance:
- Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above and provide additional insights into the benefits and limitations of their approach.
