- Identifies scalability (space/time) problems in neural nets with external memory as memory grows.
- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to retain representational power while improving training efficiency.
- Claims substantial empirical gains: ~1000x speedups and ~3000x memory-efficiency improvements.
- Reviewer argues the paper lacks sufficient technical novelty for acceptance.
- Core contributions (efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest-neighbor lookup via SAM) are viewed as relatively straightforward.
- Experiments are interesting but do not compensate for perceived lack of novelty; recommendation: reject.
