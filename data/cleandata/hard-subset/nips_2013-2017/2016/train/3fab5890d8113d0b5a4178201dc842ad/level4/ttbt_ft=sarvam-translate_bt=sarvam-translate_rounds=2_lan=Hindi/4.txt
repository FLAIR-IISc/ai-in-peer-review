This research paper introduces an end-to-end differential memory access scheme that has the representational capacity of a basic NTM, but is much more efficient in training with very large memory. The paper presents several fundamental concepts that appear to be technically very sound. The problem it attempts to solve is well-motivated and well-attackable. The effect of scaling memory systems for neural networks is significant, and this paper presents a clear, concise, and well-explained method for the problem and solution.
