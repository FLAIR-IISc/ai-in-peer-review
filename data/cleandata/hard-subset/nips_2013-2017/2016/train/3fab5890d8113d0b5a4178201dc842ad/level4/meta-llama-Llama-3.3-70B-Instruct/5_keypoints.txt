- Proposes a novel Sparse Access Memory architecture for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Reports up to three orders of magnitude improvements in runtime and memory efficiency versus prior models.  
- Demonstrates substantial performance gains in curriculum learning settings.  
- Shows strong generalization to sequences much longer than training (trained to 10,000, tested to 200,000).  
- Claims the contribution could make memory-augmented neural networks practically usable and potentially be groundbreaking.  
- Notes prior work lacked practical applicability, which this paper appears to address.  
- Criticizes the paper for not explaining the underlying reasons for curriculum and long-sequence generalization improvements.  
- Suggests a plausible hypothesis that the Sparse Memory Access itself (analogous to sparse auto-encoders) may drive these gains and requests authors’ insights.
