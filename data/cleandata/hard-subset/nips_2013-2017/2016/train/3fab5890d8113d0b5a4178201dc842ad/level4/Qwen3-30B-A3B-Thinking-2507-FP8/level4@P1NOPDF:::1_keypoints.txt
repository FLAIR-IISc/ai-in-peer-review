- Uses fast approximate nearest-neighbor structures and top-K argmax to compute attention in a Neural Turing Machine.  
- Experiments show training performance is not compromised and the method enables scaling to larger problems.  
- Novelty is limited because similar ideas appear in Memory Networks (Section 3.3) and subsequent work using hashing for scalability.  
- Practical implementation for writeable memories is a valuable and previously unreported contribution.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance pending revisions.  
- Replicability is currently difficult due to insufficient task specifications; paper must provide detailed task construction procedures and commit to releasing code.  
- Paper lacks analysis of failure modes and applicability boundaries, particularly given reliance on non-smooth discrete argmax operations.  
- Need to investigate whether success depends on K=1, task characteristics, or other factors, and compare to smooth/differentiable alternatives.  
- Must discuss when the method fails and why it succeeds, since discrete choices are made during training without gradient propagation.  
- Recommend removing Section 3.6.  
- Omniglot should not be described as "non-synthetic" or "real-world"; it is synthetic by design and should be labeled appropriately.
