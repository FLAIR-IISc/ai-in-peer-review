- Proposes a Sparse-NTM with sparse read/write to reduce memory and backpropagation costs while keeping performance comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Targets a major NTM limitation that makes them impractical for real-world applications due to large physical memory and operations.
- Paper is well-structured and easy to read.
- Recommend adding a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks or a clearer explanation of the memory mechanism for self-containment.
- Clarify concepts like the ideal behavior of the write operator versus conventional memories for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- Figure 4 comparison is interesting but its fairness is debatable.
- Comparing models under equal physical-memory constraint is justifiable, but using identical memory capacity across models would offer more insight.
- Noteworthy that the algorithm’s performance approaches theoretical bounds.
- Overall promising solution to an important problem; minor revisions on clarity and comparison fairness could strengthen the paper.
