This paper introduces an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that matches the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM), while being significantly more efficient to train, particularly with very large memory systems. The authors introduce a number of original concepts of significant technical merit. The problem this work addresses—scaling memory systems for neural networks—is well-motivated and of considerable impact, and the paper tackles it effectively. The problem and the proposed solution are presented in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
