- Proposes using fast ANN search and top-K selection for attention in Neural Turing Machines to improve scalability.  
- Experiments show the approximation maintains performance and enables training with larger memory sizes.  
- Core novelty is limited since hashing-based scalable lookups have been used in Memory Networks previously.  
- Applying these techniques to writeable memories (NTMs) is less common and is a useful practical contribution.  
- Recommendation to accept contingent on addressing major concerns.  
- Reproducibility is weak: task descriptions are insufficient, and authors should release experimental code and precise task parameters.  
- Need deeper analysis of limitations and failure modes, especially due to the non-differentiable arg-top-K and why training succeeds without RL.  
- Suggest ablation studies and discussion on sensitivity to K, comparison to MemNN-WSH (K=1), and conditions where the method may fail.  
- Minor suggestions: remove Section 3.6 and avoid calling Omniglot a “non-synthetic” or “real-world” dataset.
