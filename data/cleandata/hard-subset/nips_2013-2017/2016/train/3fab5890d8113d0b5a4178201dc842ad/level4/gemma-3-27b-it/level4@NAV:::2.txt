A common limitation of neural network models incorporating external memory is their computational and memory demands, which increase significantly with memory size. This paper introduces Sparse Access Memory, a novel memory access method, and demonstrates its ability to maintain representational capacity while enabling more efficient training – achieving speedups of 1000x and memory reductions of 3000x. However, I do not believe the technical contribution currently warrants acceptance to the conference. While the experimental results are promising, the core techniques of efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor search within the Sparse Access Memory framework are relatively incremental advancements. Therefore, despite the interesting empirical findings, I recommend rejecting the paper at this time.
