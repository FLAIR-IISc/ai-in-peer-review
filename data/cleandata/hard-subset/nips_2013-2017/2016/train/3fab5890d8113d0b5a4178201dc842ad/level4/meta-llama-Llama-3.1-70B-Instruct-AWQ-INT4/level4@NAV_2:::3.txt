This paper presents a novel memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) in memory augmented neural network architecture. The research area of memory augmented neural networks is relatively recent, with the introduction of Neural Turing Machines. This architecture allows neural networks to learn beyond the limits of traditional LSTM models. The key contribution of this paper is that all writes to and reads from external memory are constrained to a fixed size, thus a subset of the memory words, instead of unlimited size. The authors prove the methodology and its optimality, achieving good read and write performance due to reduced memory size as shown in section 4.1.

The paper relates to previous work on Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, which have been successful in various tasks such as question answering and language modeling. However, these models have limited scalability due to their smooth read and write operations, which incur linear computational overhead on the number of memories stored per time step of training. The proposed SAM architecture addresses this limitation by introducing sparsity in the memory access scheme.

The authors evaluate the proposed SAM architecture on various tasks, including synthetic tasks and real-world datasets such as Omniglot. The results show that SAM achieves significant speedup compared to the Neural Turing Machine, while maintaining comparable performance on various tasks. The authors also investigate the learning costs of introducing sparsity and find that it can actually benefit early-stage learning in some cases.

The paper is well-written, and the architecture is clearly explained in the appendix. The assumptions are laid out clearly, and the experimental results are thorough. The idea of using sparse reads and writes is novel and interesting, and the fact that it can benefit early-stage learning in some cases is a valuable contribution.

Strengths:

* Novel and interesting idea of using sparse reads and writes in memory augmented neural networks
* Significant speedup compared to Neural Turing Machine
* Thorough evaluation on various tasks, including synthetic tasks and real-world datasets
* Well-written paper with clear explanations and assumptions

Weaknesses:

* Limited analysis of the conditions under which the learning costs of introducing sparsity can be improved
* Limited exploration of different ANN approaches with SAM

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide more analysis on the conditions under which the learning costs of introducing sparsity can be improved
* Explore different ANN approaches with SAM and evaluate their performance
* Consider applying the proposed SAM architecture to more real-world datasets and tasks

Acceptance:

* Yes, the paper is well-written, and the idea is novel and interesting
* The experimental results are thorough, and the speedup compared to Neural Turing Machine is significant
* The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of memory augmented neural networks

Rating: 9/10
