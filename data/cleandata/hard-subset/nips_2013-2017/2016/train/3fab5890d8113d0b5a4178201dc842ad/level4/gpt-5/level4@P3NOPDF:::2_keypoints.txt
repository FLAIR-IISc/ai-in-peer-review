- Summary: Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) using sparse reads/writes plus ANN lookups to scale differentiable neural memory, claiming up to 1000x faster training and 3000x lower memory while preserving dense representational capacity.

- Strength: Addresses a timely, important scalability bottleneck for memory-augmented models.

- Strength: The sparse+ANN combination is practical and likely easy to integrate into existing systems.

- Strength: Reported empirical speed and memory improvements are potentially impactful if measured fairly.

- Strength: Method is simple, modular, and engineering-friendly.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — ANN-based addressing and sparse backprop are known in related areas and the paper mainly combines existing ideas.

- Weakness: Technical depth is shallow; the “efficient backpropagation” treatment is described as straightforward without rigorous analysis.

- Weakness: Strong claim of “retaining representational power” is not well substantiated with formal results, ablations, or error bounds.

- Weakness: Experimental rigor/scope is unclear — unclear whether speedups include index construction/overheads or hold across memory sizes, k, ANN settings, and multiple tasks.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to strong baselines such as optimized dense attention, block-sparse kernels, and other memory-augmented models.

- Weakness: Implementation details are insufficiently clear on writes, index updates, top-k differentiation, and amortized complexity.

- Question: Need formalization/bounds on discrepancy between dense and sparse access as a function of k and ANN recall.

- Question: How are gradients to non-selected memories handled and is there mitigation for bias from top-k gating?

- Question: Which ANN method is used, how sensitive are results to its hyperparameters, and can ANN costs erode speedups?

- Question: How do training stability and final accuracy scale with larger memories/longer sequences; provide accuracy vs latency/memory trade-off curves for varying k.

- Request: Include wall-clock times, hardware, batch sizes, precision, and profiling breakdowns to substantiate 1000x/3000x claims.

- Request: Add stronger baselines and broad empirical validation across tasks/datasets.

- Request: Release code, configs, seeds, and variance to ensure reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Motivating and practical but technically incremental and under-validated; recommend rejection (confidence: moderate).
