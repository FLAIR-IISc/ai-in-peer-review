- Memory-augmented neural models struggle to scale in space and time as memory size increases.
- Authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM) as a memory access mechanism.
- SAM purportedly preserves representational capacity while enabling efficient training.
- Reported efficiency gains: ~1000× faster and ~3000× more memory-efficient.
- Reviewer finds the paper not technically strong enough for acceptance.
- Main technical components (efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor via SAM) are viewed as fairly straightforward.
- Experiments are interesting but insufficient to justify acceptance.
