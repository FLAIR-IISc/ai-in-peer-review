- Proposes a Neural Turing Machine with sparse read/write to reduce physical memory and backprop costs, making NTMs more practical.  
- Claims to maintain performance comparable to state-of-the-art and approach theoretical bounds.  
- Paper is well-organized and accessible.  
- Suggest adding a concise overview of memory-augmented neural networks or a clearer explanation of the memory mechanism for non-experts.  
- Recommend clarifying the role of the write operator relative to conventional memories.  
- Critique: Figure 4 comparison may be unfair because it evaluates models by equivalent physical memory; comparing with the same memory configuration might be more insightful.  
- Note that the authors’ comparison choice is justifiable, but alternative evaluation would strengthen the paper.
