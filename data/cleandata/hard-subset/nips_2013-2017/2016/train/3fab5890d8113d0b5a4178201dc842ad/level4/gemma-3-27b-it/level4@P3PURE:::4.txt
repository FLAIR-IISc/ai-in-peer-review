This paper introduces an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that achieves the representational power of a standard Neural Turing Machine (NTM), but with significantly improved training efficiency, particularly when utilizing very large memory sizes. The work introduces several novel concepts that demonstrate technical brilliance. The problem addressed – scaling memory systems for neural networks – is well-motivated and the proposed approach is thoughtfully executed. The impact of developing scalable memory systems for neural networks is substantial, and this paper presents both the problem and its solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
