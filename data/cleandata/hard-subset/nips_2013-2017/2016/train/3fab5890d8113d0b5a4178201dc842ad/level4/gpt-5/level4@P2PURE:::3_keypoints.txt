- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), constraining reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words in memory-augmented neural nets.  
- Provides proofs supporting SAM’s method and claims of optimality.  
- Reduces active memory footprint, yielding strong read/write efficiency (Section 4.1).  
- Quantifies learning costs of sparsity and compares to NTM on Copy, Associative Recall, and Priority Sort (Section 4.2).  
- Shows combined costs lower than NTM for some tasks (notably Associative Recall and Priority Sort).  
- Demonstrates substantial and impressive speedups over NTM, implemented in Torch7.  
- Finds sparse reads/writes via linear models or ANNs can sometimes improve early-stage learning (Section 4.2).  
- Includes a real-world example evaluation (Section 4.4).  
- Suggests authors should clarify when sparsity helps early learning and test SAM with different ANN approaches to assess generality.  
- Paper is well written with clear assumptions and detailed architecture appendix.
