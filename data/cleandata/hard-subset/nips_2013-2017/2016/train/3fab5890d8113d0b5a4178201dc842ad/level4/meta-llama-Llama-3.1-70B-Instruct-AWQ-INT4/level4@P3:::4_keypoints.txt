- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme for large memories.  
- Addresses the important problem of scaling memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs).  
- Well-motivated and clearly explains limitations of existing MANNs.  
- Key innovations: sparse reads and writes to external memory.  
- Key innovation: sparse memory management scheme.  
- Achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, optimal for large memories.  
- Outperforms prior models (NTM, Memory Networks) in speed and memory efficiency.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot, and bAbI question-answering.  
- Uses approximate nearest neighbor indexes (randomized k-d trees, LSH) as a notable contribution.  
- Provides detailed time and space complexity analysis for scalability.  
- Paper balances technical depth with clarity and concision.  
- Original contributions include sparse access memory scheme and use of ANN indexes.  
- Significant potential impact on language modeling, MT, and QA through scalable memory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scaling approach, efficiency with large memories, empirical effectiveness, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: may be too technical for non-experts, experiments could be more comprehensive, lacks detailed limitations/future work analysis.  
- Reviewer rating: 9/10; Confidence: 8/10.
