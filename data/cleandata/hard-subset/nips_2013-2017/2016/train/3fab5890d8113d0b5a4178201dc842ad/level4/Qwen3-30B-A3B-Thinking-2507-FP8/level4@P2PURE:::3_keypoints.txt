- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- SAM constrains all read/write operations to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of the full memory.
- Authors provide proofs of the methodology and claim optimality of the approach.
- Sparsity reduces memory footprint, yielding improved read and write performance (Section 4.1).
- Learning costs of sparsity are analyzed using standard NTM tasks: Copy, Associate Call, and Priority Sort (Section 4.2).
- Combined costs for Associate Call and Priority Sort are lower with SAM than with NTM.
- Sparse read/write implemented via linear or ANN approaches can benefit early-stage learning in some cases (Section 4.2).
- Paper includes a real-world example and benchmarks implemented in Torch7 (Section 4.4).
- Core idea is straightforward, novel, and achieves significant speedups relative to NTM.
- Suggestion for future work: clarify conditions under which sparse access improves learning and explore integration with other ANN approaches.
- Paper is well-written, assumptions are clearly stated, and architecture details are provided in the appendix.
- Overall contribution is engaging, insightful, and promising for memory-augmented models.
