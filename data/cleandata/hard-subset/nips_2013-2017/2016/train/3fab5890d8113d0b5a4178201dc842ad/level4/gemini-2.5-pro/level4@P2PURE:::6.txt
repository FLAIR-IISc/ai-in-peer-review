This paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, designed to overcome a major problem that makes standard NTMs impractical for real-world applications. Specifically, the prohibitively large physical memory and computational cost required for backpropagation is a key limitation that the proposed architecture aims to address, while achieving performance comparable to the state of the art.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. To make it more self-contained, especially for readers who are not experts in this area, the authors could consider adding a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks to the background section. For example, an explanation of how the write operator's function differs from that of conventional memories would improve the paper's accessibility.

Additionally, the experimental comparison in Figure 4 may not be entirely fair. By constraining the models to require the same amount of physical memory, the authors provide a justifiable comparison of performance under a limited resource budget. However, a comparison using the same logical memory size, rather than the same physical memory footprint, could be more informative about the intrinsic performance of the proposed architecture.

Nevertheless, it is very interesting to see the proposed algorithm perform so well and achieve results that are close to theoretical bounds.
