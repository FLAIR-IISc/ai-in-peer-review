Most memory-augmented neural network models scale poorly in both time and space as the memory size grows. The authors propose a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM), claiming it preserves representational power while enabling efficient training, with reported speedups of 1000× and 3000× improvements in memory efficiency.

I do not find the paper technically strong enough for acceptance. The main technical components—an efficient backpropagation scheme and an approximate nearest-neighbor mechanism within SAM—are relatively straightforward. Although the experimental results are interesting, the core methods do not appear sufficiently novel or deep to justify acceptance in their current form.

Therefore, despite the promising empirical performance, I am not in favor of acceptance.
