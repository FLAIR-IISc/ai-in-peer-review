- Introduces a Neural Turing Machine with sparse read/write access to address prohibitive memory and backpropagation costs.  
- Proposed architecture reduces physical memory and computational overhead while achieving near state-of-the-art performance.  
- Paper is well structured and easy to read.  
- Recommendation: add a brief overview of memory-augmented neural networks or a concise explanation of the memory module and the write operator for non-experts.  
- Concern: Figure 4 compares models under equal physical memory; also compare under equal logical memory capacity to isolate sparsity/implementation effects from memory size.  
- Empirical results are impressive, performing close to theoretical bounds.
