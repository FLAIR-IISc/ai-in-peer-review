A significant limitation of most memory-augmented neural networks is their poor scalability; both time and space complexity grow unfavorably as the memory size increases. To address this, the authors propose a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory. They demonstrate that their method achieves substantial efficiency gains—reportedly a 1000x speedup and a 3000x reduction in memory usage—while preserving the model's representational power.

However, I find the technical contributions of the paper to be insufficient for acceptance. The core technical components, which are an efficient backpropagation method and an approximate nearest neighbor search for the sparse memory, appear to be relatively straightforward. Consequently, although the experimental results are interesting, the limited technical novelty leads me to recommend against acceptance.
