- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented networks (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).
- Reports ~1000x improvements in runtime and memory efficiency over existing memory-augmented models.
- Shows large gains in curriculum learning and strong generalization to sequences far longer than training (10k → 200k steps).
- Claims the work could make memory-augmented networks practically viable and be a groundbreaking contribution.
- Reviewer notes runtime/memory efficiency alone cannot explain the curriculum and long-sequence generalization effects.
- Missing: a discussion/hypotheses on why SAM yields dramatic improvements in curriculum learning and extrapolation.
- Suggests sparse access may act like sparse autoencoders, promoting structured/selective representations that aid stable learning and extrapolation.
- Recommends including preliminary discussion, qualitative analysis, or pointers to specific architectural choices to support these claims.
- Overall: promising advance in scalability and relevance, but strengthened by clearer explanation of observed benefits.
