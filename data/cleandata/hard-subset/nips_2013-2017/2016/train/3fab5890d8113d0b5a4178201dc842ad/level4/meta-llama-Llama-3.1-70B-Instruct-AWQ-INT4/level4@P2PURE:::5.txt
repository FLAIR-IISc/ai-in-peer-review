The paper introduces a Sparse Access Memory architecture for neural networks augmented with memory, such as Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, achieving remarkable run-time and memory efficiency improvements of three orders of magnitude compared to existing neural nets with memory. This architecture also appears to enable significant performance improvements in curriculum learning settings and generalization to sequences an order of magnitude longer than those observed during training, from lengths of up to 10,000 during training to 200,000 during testing. This could be a groundbreaking paper. The field of neural networks augmented with memory, initiated by Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, is actively developing, with interesting research and experimental results. However, these results have not yet demonstrated practical applicability. The substantial efficiency and performance improvements reported in this paper may indicate that memory-augmented neural networks are now ready for practical use, with far-reaching implications. The paper's main limitation is the lack of discussion on the reasons behind the significant performance improvements in curriculum learning and the ability to predict sequences far beyond the training range. Run-time and memory efficiency alone cannot account for these improvements, suggesting that other factors are at play. A potential explanation could be that the Sparse Memory Access architecture plays a role similar to that of Sparse Auto-encoders in other contexts. The authors' thoughts on this, even if preliminary, would be valuable and of interest to readers.
