This paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read/write access, aimed at addressing a key limitation that makes standard NTMs impractical for many real-world applications. Specifically, conventional NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and substantial computational overhead for backpropagation; the proposed architecture seeks to mitigate these costs while achieving performance comparable to the state of the art. The paper is well structured and easy to read.

To make the work more self-contained and accessible to non-experts, it would help to include a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks or a clearer explanation of the external memory and its operators. For example, while it may be obvious to researchers in this area, some readers may not fully understand what the write operator is intended to accomplish relative to conventional memories.

I am also unsure the comparison in Figure 4 is entirely fair. Matching models by equal physical memory footprint is a reasonable and defensible way to evaluate performance under resource constraints (which you have done, and this is certainly justifiable). However, comparing them under equal logical memory capacity (i.e., using the same memory, rather than the same physical footprint) would be more informative about intrinsic performance.

Nevertheless, it is impressive to see how well this algorithm performs while remaining so close to the theoretical bounds.
