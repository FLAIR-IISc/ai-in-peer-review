- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to scale memory-augmented neural networks, claiming ~1,000x speedups and ~3,000x memory reductions, validated on synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot, large-sequence tasks, and adapted to DNC.  
- Strength: Tackles the important problem of MANN scalability.  
- Strength: Empirical results are strong, showing substantial speed and memory gains.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with useful supplementary implementation details.  
- Strength: SAM generalizes to other MANNs (e.g., DNC), suggesting broader applicability.  
- Weakness: Core technical contributions (sparse backpropagation, approximate nearest neighbor, CSR) feel incremental rather than novel.  
- Weakness: Sparse memory access idea is not new; paper needs a stronger case for novelty in its gradient-based approach.  
- Weakness: Experiments lack broader comparisons to other scaling techniques and deeper hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Theoretical claim of asymptotic optimality is underdeveloped and needs more exposition in the main text.  
- For acceptance: Presents a practical, effective solution to a critical MANN problem with compelling empirical benefits.  
- Against acceptance: Contributions are not groundbreaking and theoretical/experimental support is insufficient.  
- Overall: Valuable but incremental work; reviewer leans to reject this year and recommends stronger theoretical support and more comprehensive comparisons before resubmission.
