The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a major limitation that has hindered the adoption of NTMs in real-world applications. Specifically, NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and operations to perform backpropagation, which the proposed architecture aims to mitigate while maintaining comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

To enhance the paper's accessibility, it would be beneficial to provide a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section or an explanation of the memory component. This would facilitate understanding for readers unfamiliar with the field, as certain concepts, such as the ideal behavior of the write operator in comparison to conventional memories, may not be immediately clear. For instance, while experts in the area may find the explanation straightforward, some readers might struggle to comprehend the nuances of the write operator.

Additionally, the comparison presented in Figure 4 may not be entirely fair. By selecting memories that require the same amount of physical memory, the authors compare performance under a limited resource constraint, which is a valid approach. However, using the same memory size for all models might provide more insight into the performance differences. Nevertheless, it is intriguing to observe how well the proposed algorithm performs, especially considering its proximity to theoretical bounds.
