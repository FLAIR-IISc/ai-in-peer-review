- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks, extending beyond traditional models like LSTMs and NTMs.  
- Main contribution: restricts all read/write operations to a fixed-size subset of memory words rather than accessing full memory.  
- Provides theoretical justification and claims optimality for the sparse access methodology.  
- Empirical result (Sec. 4.1): reduced access size yields superior read/write performance.  
- Thorough experiments (Sec. 4.2): on NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort), SAM shows lower combined learning costs than standard NTM on associative recall and priority sort.  
- Finding: sparse access guided by a linear controller or ANN can improve early-stage learning in some cases.  
- Demonstrates significant speedup over standard NTM and includes a real-world application (Sec. 4.4).  
- Suggested improvements: analyze conditions where sparse access helps, generalize findings, and explore different controller architectures for SAM.  
- Overall: well-written, interesting, clear assumptions, straightforward novel idea, architecture detailed in appendix.
