- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- SAM constrains all read/write operations to a fixed-size subset of external memory rather than full access.
- Authors prove the methodology's optimality and show reduced memory access size improves read/write efficiency (Sec. 4.1).
- Evaluation on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort) demonstrates lower combined computational cost than NTM for tasks 2 and 3.
- Presents a real-world benchmark implemented in Torch7 (Sec. 4.4).
- Core idea is straightforward yet novel, yielding significant speedups over standard NTM.
- Insight that sparse access (via linear or ANN mechanisms) can benefit early-stage learning (Sec. 4.2).
- Paper is well-written with clear assumptions and detailed architecture in the appendix.
- Recommendation: authors should clarify specific conditions where sparsity improves learning performance.
- Suggestion: explore generalizations to other task domains and alternative ANN approaches combined with SAM.
