- Proposes efficient attention for NTMs using fast approximate nearest neighbors and arg-top-K operations.  
- Experiments show no loss in training performance and enable training on larger problems.  
- Novelty is limited since similar ideas exist for memory networks, but applying them to writable memories is a notable contribution.  
- Practical implementation details are worth reporting despite the lack of a conceptual breakthrough.  
- Reviewer recommends accepting the paper subject to revisions to improve clarity and impact.  
- Provide detailed task descriptions to enable reproducibility, since original NTM task code is not released.  
- Authors should commit to releasing the experimental code.  
- Discuss limitations and potential failure cases, especially the effects of non-smooth argmax/K-argmax operations.  
- Analyze sensitivity to the choice of K (e.g., K=1) and whether task specifics drive the method’s success.  
- Remove section 3.6 because it does not add substantial insights.  
- Do not label Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world”; it is still a synthetic benchmark.  
- With the suggested revisions, the paper can more comprehensively present the method’s strengths and limitations.
