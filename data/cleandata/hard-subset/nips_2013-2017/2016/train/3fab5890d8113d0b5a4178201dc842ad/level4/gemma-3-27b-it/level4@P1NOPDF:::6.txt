The paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a key limitation of NTMs that hinders their application to real-world problems. Specifically, standard NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and computational resources for backpropagation. This paper proposes an architecture aimed at resolving this issue while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods. The paper is well-structured and clearly written. 

To enhance accessibility for readers less familiar with the field – even those acquainted with neural networks but not specifically memory-augmented architectures – a brief overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section would be beneficial. Similarly, a more detailed explanation of the memory mechanisms themselves would increase the paper’s self-containment. For example, clarifying the intended function of the write operator, particularly in contrast to conventional memory operations, could be helpful for a broader audience.

Regarding the comparison presented in Figure 4, I have a concern about fairness. While comparing performance under a constraint of equal physical memory is a reasonable and justifiable approach, it may be more informative to evaluate performance using the *same* memory size, rather than the same physical memory footprint. This would better illustrate the algorithm’s efficiency given a fixed amount of information storage. 

Despite this point, the results are very promising. It is particularly noteworthy that the algorithm achieves performance so close to theoretical bounds, demonstrating its effectiveness and potential.
