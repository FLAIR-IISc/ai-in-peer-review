- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): constrain reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of entire external memory.
- Provides theoretical proofs for the SAM methodology and claims of optimality.
- Demonstrates significant read/write performance gains from reduced memory access footprint (Section 4.1).
- Analyzes learning costs induced by sparsity and reports these effects (Section 4.2).
- Shows SAM achieves lower combined cost than standard NTM on Associative Recall and Priority Sort tasks.
- Evaluates on standard NTM benchmarks (Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort) and includes a real-world application (Section 4.4).
- Experiments implemented and benchmarked using the Torch7 framework.
- Observes that sparse access can improve early-stage learning in some cases (Section 4.2).
- Recommends further study to clarify conditions under which the learning improvement occurs and to generalize findings.
- Suggests exploring SAM integration with different neural network controller architectures.
- Paper is well-written, assumptions are clearly stated, and architectural details are documented in the appendix.
