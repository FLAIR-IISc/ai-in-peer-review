- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel sparse read/write mechanism to scale memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs).
- SAM achieves significant speedups and memory reductions while maintaining comparable performance to dense state-of-the-art models.
- Paper is well-structured and accessible, but would benefit from a brief MANN background and clearer explanation of memory operations (especially the write operator).
- Evaluated on synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot, and bAbI QA, showing efficiency and scalability across tasks.
- Figure 4 comparison (same physical memory) is informative for limited-resource settings, but should also include comparisons with equal memory size.
- Provides detailed time and space complexity analysis, demonstrating the approach’s efficiency.
- Strength: clear writing, organized presentation, and thorough empirical evaluation.
- Weakness: lacks in-depth comparison with other state-of-the-art models addressing similar MANN challenges.
- Weakness: needs more detailed explanation of memory operations and their relation to conventional memories.
- Acceptance arguments: novel scaling approach, strong empirical gains, thorough evaluations, and solid complexity analysis.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient comparative analysis with related SOTA and limited experimental comparison setups.
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
