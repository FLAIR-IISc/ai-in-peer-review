- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) architecture for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., NTM, Memory Networks).
- Reports ~three orders of magnitude improvement in run-time and memory efficiency over existing memory-augmented nets.
- Demonstrates significant performance gains in curriculum learning settings.
- Shows strong generalization to sequences far longer than training (from ~10,000 during training to ~200,000 at test).
- Claims results could make memory-augmented neural networks practically applicable and have far-reaching implications.
- Situates work in an active research area initiated by Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, where prior results lacked practicality.
- Main limitation: paper lacks discussion of why SAM yields large curriculum and long-range generalization gains.
- Argues run-time/memory efficiency alone unlikely to explain the performance improvements; other mechanisms are probably involved.
- Suggests a possible explanation that SAM may act similarly to sparse autoencoders and requests authors’ preliminary thoughts on this.
