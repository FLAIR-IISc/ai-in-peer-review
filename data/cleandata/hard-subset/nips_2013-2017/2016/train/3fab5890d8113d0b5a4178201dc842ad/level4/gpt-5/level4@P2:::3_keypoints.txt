- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): top-K sparse reads/writes using ANN indices and a usage-based write policy for scalable MANNs.  
- Implements ANN (k-d trees/LSH) for content-based top-K selection and sparse backprop via stored sparse memory diffs.  
- Claims O(1) per-step memory and O(log N) per-step time with ANN, arguing asymptotic optimality for content-based addressing.  
- Empirically reports huge speed (∼1000–1600×) and memory (∼3000×) improvements over dense NTMs at large N.  
- Matches or surpasses dense baselines on NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort) and can scale via curriculum to very long sequences and large memories.  
- Extends to a sparse DNC (SDNC) with strong bAbI performance without memory supervision.  
- Shows benefit on Omniglot one-shot classification when using larger memory, outperforming dense baselines in the reported setup.  
- Strength: clear, simple mechanism combining top-K reads and read-then-LRU sparse writes enabling forward and backward sparsity.  
- Strength: solid theoretical/systems story with complexity analysis and a practical sparse BPTT rollback scheme.  
- Strength: substantial empirical gains enabling experiments infeasible for dense MANNs and broad evaluation across tasks.  
- Observes that sparsity does not harm and can sometimes improve data efficiency and early learning.  
- Weakness: “asymptotically optimal” claim relies on assumptions that should be more explicitly stated and discussed (constant factors, regimes).  
- Weakness: ANN treated as fixed/non-differentiable—needs more analysis of its effect on optimization, recall, stability under online updates, and rebuild frequency.  
- Weakness: lack of ablations across ANN types, K values, distance metrics, and index parameters to show robustness and trade-offs.  
- Weakness: non-smooth top-K selection—unclear whether straight-through or other gradient tricks are used and sensitivity to K and LRU threshold ε.  
- Weakness: comparisons could include more baselines (discrete-addressing RL NTMs, other memory networks), with matched parameter counts and memory budgets.  
- Weakness: real-world evaluation limited; Omniglot is reasonable but broader domains (language modeling, MT) or discussion of practical integration needed.  
- Weakness: SDNC details (how temporal linkage is sparsified and its complexity/performance impact) should be in the main paper, not just supplementary.  
- Quality: method is technically sound with coherent sparse read/write design and careful BPTT, though some theoretical assertions need clearer exposition.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and organized with helpful supplements; minor fixes and expanded discussions on sparsity trade-offs and ANN choices recommended.  
- Originality: novel, practical synthesis enforcing sparsity in reads/writes, integrating ANN into training, and enabling efficient backward passes; SDNC shows generality.  
- Significance: important contribution that removes a major scaling barrier for MANNs, with likely broad adoption of systems insights.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant scalable approach, orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains, comparable or better learning, versatile applicability.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient analysis of ANN design effects, optimality assumptions need clarification/validation, and limited breadth of real-world evaluation.  
- Overall: well-executed, timely, and significant work that merits acceptance after addressing ANN trade-offs and theoretical assumption clarifications.
