The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a significant limitation of NTMs that makes them unsuitable for real-world applications. Specifically, traditional NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and operations for backpropagation, which the proposed architecture aims to mitigate while maintaining comparable performance to state-of-the-art models.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. However, including a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section or providing an explanation of the memory mechanism would enhance the paper's self-containment. This addition would benefit readers unfamiliar with the field, as certain concepts, such as the ideal behavior of the write operator compared to conventional memories, may not be immediately clear.

The comparison in Figure 4 is noteworthy, but its fairness is debatable. By selecting memories that require the same amount of physical memory, the authors compare performance under a limited resource constraint, which is a justifiable approach. Nevertheless, using the same memory capacity for all models would provide more insight into their performance. Despite this, it is intriguing to see the algorithm's performance approaching theoretical bounds.

Overall, the paper tackles a significant challenge in NTMs and presents a promising solution. With some minor revisions to enhance clarity and fairness in comparisons, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
