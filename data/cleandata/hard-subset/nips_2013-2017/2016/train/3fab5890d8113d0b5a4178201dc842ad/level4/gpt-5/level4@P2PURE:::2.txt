Most neural network models augmented with external memory scale poorly in both time and space as the memory size increases. The authors introduce a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) and claim it preserves representational power while enabling efficient training, reporting up to 1000× speedups and 3000× improvements in memory efficiency. However, I do not find the technical contributions strong enough for acceptance. The primary components—efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor search via sparse memory access—are relatively straightforward. Therefore, despite the interesting experimental results, I am not in favor of acceptance.
