- Paper uses fast approximate nearest-neighbor search and arg-top-K for attention in a Neural Turing Machine, enabling training on larger problems without degrading performance.
- Core novelty is limited because similar techniques exist in memory networks and hashing-based scalable lookups, though application to writeable memories is less common.
- Implementation/practical details are worth documenting despite limited conceptual novelty.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions.
- Reproducibility is inadequate: task construction is under-specified and authors must provide detailed task descriptions and commit to releasing experimental code.
- Require a deeper analysis of model limitations, particularly the discontinuities introduced by argmax/K-argmax and their impact on training.
- Investigate whether issues stem from K=1/read-only setup, specific tasks, or other factors, noting similarity to MemNN-WSH which underperformed smooth models.
- Add discussion of failure cases and conditions for successful training given the model’s discrete actions and lack of explicit discrete-action training methods.
- Remove section 3.6 and stop characterizing Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
