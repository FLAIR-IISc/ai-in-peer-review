- Uses fast approximate-nearest-neighbors and arg-top-K for attention in an NTM; experiments show no training performance loss and allow larger-scale training.  
- Novelty is limited: prior memory network work already uses millions of memory items with hashing (see Memory Networks Sec. 3.3 and related papers).  
- The practical implementation for writeable memories appears novel but lacks a conceptual leap; its engineering contribution merits reporting.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but requires revisions.  
- Reproducibility is insufficient: provide meticulous task construction details (since original NTM tasks lack standardized definitions) and commit to releasing experimental code.  
- Paper must analyze failure modes and limitations: effects of non-smooth argmax/K-argmax vs smooth alternatives, whether performance drops for K=1, task-dependent behavior, and implications of discrete action assumptions.  
- Remove Section 3.6 and stop calling Omniglot "non-synthetic" or "real-world."
