Most neural network architectures augmented with external memory exhibit poor scaling in both time and space as memory size increases. The authors propose a memory access mechanism, Sparse Access Memory (SAM), and argue that it preserves the expressive power of prior models while enabling efficient training, reporting up to 1000× speedups and roughly 3000× reductions in memory footprint. I do not find the paper technically strong enough for acceptance. The primary technical contributions—efficient backpropagation via sparse updates and approximate nearest neighbor indexing to enable sparse reads and writes—appear relatively straightforward. Consequently, although the experiments are appealing, I am not in favor of acceptance.
