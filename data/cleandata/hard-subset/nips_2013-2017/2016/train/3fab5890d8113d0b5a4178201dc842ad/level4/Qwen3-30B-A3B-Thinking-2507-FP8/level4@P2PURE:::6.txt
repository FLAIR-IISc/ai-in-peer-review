

The paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access to address a major limitation of NTMs: their prohibitive memory requirements and computational cost during backpropagation, which hinder real-world applicability. The proposed architecture aims to resolve this issue while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-structured and clearly written. However, including a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks in the introduction—or providing a concise explanation of the memory mechanism—would enhance self-containment for non-expert readers. For instance, the intended function of the write operator (distinguishing it from conventional memory systems) is clear to specialists but may be ambiguous to others. Additionally, the comparison in Figure 4 may not be fully equitable. While constraining all models to the same physical memory capacity is a valid approach for resource-constrained scenarios, comparing models using the same memory *capacity* (rather than the same physical memory) would more directly assess performance under identical storage limitations. Despite this, the algorithm's strong performance near theoretical bounds is highly compelling.
