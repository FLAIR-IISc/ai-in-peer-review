The paper "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes" presents a novel approach to address the scalability issues of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs). The authors propose a sparse access memory (SAM) scheme that significantly reduces the computational and memory overhead of MANNs, making them more suitable for real-world applications.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, providing a clear background on MANNs, including neural Turing machines (NTMs) and memory networks. The authors effectively explain the limitations of existing MANNs, particularly the linear computational overhead and memory requirements, and introduce their SAM scheme as a solution.

The SAM scheme is based on sparse read and write operations, which reduce the computational overhead from O(N) to O(logN) time complexity. The authors also propose an efficient data structure for content-based read operations, which enables fast and sparse memory access. The write operation is modified to update only a sparse subset of memory words, reducing the memory requirements.

The authors evaluate their SAM scheme on various tasks, including synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot character recognition, and question answering on the Babi tasks. The results show that SAM achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art models while significantly reducing computational and memory overhead. Notably, SAM outperforms other models on the Omniglot task, demonstrating its ability to learn with large memories.

The paper also explores the generality of the SAM scheme by applying it to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC) architecture. The results show that the sparse version of DNC (SDNC) achieves better performance than the canonical dense variant.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and effective solution to the scalability issues of MANNs.
* The SAM scheme is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, with a clear explanation of its benefits and limitations.
* The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of SAM on various tasks.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes some background knowledge of MANNs and NTMs, which may make it less accessible to readers without prior experience in this area.
* The comparison in Figure 4 may not be entirely fair, as the authors acknowledge. Using the same memory size for all models could provide a more informative comparison.

* The SAM scheme is a novel contribution to the field of MANNs, and its application to various tasks demonstrates its potential for real-world applications.
