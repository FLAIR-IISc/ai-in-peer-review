Most neural network models augmented with memory exhibit poor scaling in both space and time as the memory capacity increases. The authors present a memory access scheme, Sparse Access Memory, and demonstrate that it maintains representational power while enabling efficient training – achieving speedups of 1000x and a 3000x reduction in memory usage. However, I do not believe the paper is technically strong enough for acceptance. While efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor lookup using sparse access memory are the core technical contributions, they are relatively straightforward. Therefore, despite the interesting experimental results, I do not recommend acceptance.
