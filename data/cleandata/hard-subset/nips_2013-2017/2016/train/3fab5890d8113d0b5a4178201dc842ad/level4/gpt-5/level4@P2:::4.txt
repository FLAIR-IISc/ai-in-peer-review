This paper proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), an end-to-end differentiable memory-access mechanism that preserves the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while enabling efficient training with very large external memories. The work presents several original, technically strong ideas. The problem it tackles—scaling memory-augmented neural networks in both space and time—is well motivated and addressed effectively. Given the importance of scalable memory systems for applications like language modeling and machine translation, the potential impact is substantial. The manuscript clearly and concisely frames both the challenge and the proposed solution, and explains them in a well-structured, accessible manner.
