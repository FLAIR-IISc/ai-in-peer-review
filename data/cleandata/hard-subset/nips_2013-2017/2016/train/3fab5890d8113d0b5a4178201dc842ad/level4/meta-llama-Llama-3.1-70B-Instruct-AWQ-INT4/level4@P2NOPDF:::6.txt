The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a major limitation that has hindered the adoption of NTMs in real-world applications. Specifically, traditional NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and operations to perform backpropagation, which the proposed architecture aims to overcome while maintaining comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

To enhance the paper's accessibility, it would be beneficial to include a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section. Providing an explanation of the memory's functionality would make the paper more self-contained, particularly for readers without expertise in this area. For instance, the role of the write operator in comparison to conventional memories might not be immediately clear to all readers.

The comparison presented in Figure 4 may be perceived as unfair. While comparing models with the same amount of physical memory is a valid approach, especially when considering limited resources, using the same memory size for all models might provide more informative insights into their performance. Nevertheless, it is intriguing to observe how well this algorithm performs, nearing theoretical bounds.
