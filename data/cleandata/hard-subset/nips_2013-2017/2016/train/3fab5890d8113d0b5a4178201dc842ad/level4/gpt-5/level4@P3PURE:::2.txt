This paper addresses the well-known issue that most memory-augmented neural networks scale poorly in both space and time as memory size grows. The authors propose a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM), which is intended to retain the representational power of prior approaches while enabling efficient training. They report substantial empirical gains, claiming up to 1000× faster training and 3000× better memory efficiency.

While the experiments are interesting, I do not find the paper technically strong enough for acceptance. The core technical components—efficient backpropagation through the sparse access mechanism and the use of approximate nearest-neighbor search within SAM—are, in my view, quite straightforward. As a result, despite the appealing empirical results, I am not in favor of acceptance.
