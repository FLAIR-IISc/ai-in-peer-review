- Problem: poor time and space scalability of memory-augmented neural networks as memory size grows.
- Proposal: Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to enable efficient training while preserving representational power.
- Results: Reported up to 1000× faster training and 3000× lower memory usage compared to dense alternatives.
- Positive: The problem is important and the empirical results are appealing.
- Critique: Technical contributions are not sufficiently strong or novel for acceptance.
- Specifics: Core components (efficient backprop through sparse operations and approximate nearest-neighbor lookups) are straightforward optimizations.
- Missing: Manuscript lacks convincing conceptual innovation and theoretical insights.
- Recommendation: Despite promising experiments, reviewer is not in favor of acceptance.
