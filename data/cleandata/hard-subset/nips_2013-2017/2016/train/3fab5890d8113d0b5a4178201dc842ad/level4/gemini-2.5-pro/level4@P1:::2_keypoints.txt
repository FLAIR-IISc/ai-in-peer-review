1. Addresses MANN scaling by proposing Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that limits reads/writes to a small constant subset.  
2. Integrates sparse access with ANN indices for content-based reads and a sparse-aware backprop scheme.  
3. Claims asymptotic time/space optimality and large empirical gains (up to ~1,000x speed and ~3,000x memory improvements).  
4. Strength: Tackles an important, timely problem enabling real-world tasks needing large memory/long histories.  
5. Strength: Strong empirical results—dramatic benchmarks, successful curriculum learning to very long sequences, and good bAbI/Omniglot performance.  
6. Weakness: Limited technical novelty; contributions largely adapt well-known techniques rather than introduce new algorithms.  
7. Weakness: Core components (ANN retrieval, exploiting sparsity, sparse backprop) are straightforward applications of existing ideas.  
8. Summary: Well-executed, highly practical system but lacking foundational novelty, leading the reviewer to lean toward rejection.
