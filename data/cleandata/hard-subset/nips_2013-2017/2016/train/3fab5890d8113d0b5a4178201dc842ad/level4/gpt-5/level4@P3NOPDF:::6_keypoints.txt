- Proposes a sparse read/write Neural Turing Machine variant to reduce memory footprint and backprop cost while retaining NTM modeling power.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and reports competitive performance against strong baselines.

Strengths:
- Addresses a practical bottleneck in NTMs/MANNs by reducing physical memory usage and backprop computational overhead.  
- Sparse-access mechanism appears effective, delivering comparable performance with significantly improved resource efficiency.  
- Empirical results are compelling, sometimes approaching theoretical limits reported in the literature.  
- Experimental section is organized and easy to follow.

Major comments / suggestions:
- Add a concise background explaining standard NTM memory interface, read/write operators, and the role of differentiability.  
- Summarize computational and memory complexity of dense read/write in NTMs and show how sparsity changes these costs (e.g., a complexity table or asymptotic comparison).  
- Complement the physical-memory-budget comparisons with evaluations under equal logical memory capacity (same number of addressable slots) to isolate modeling power.  
- Report practical efficiency metrics: wall-clock training/inference times (hardware-normalized), peak memory usage, and sensitivity to sparsity level with performance/efficiency trade-offs.  
- Analyze stability and training dynamics: convergence behavior, variance across seeds, and whether sparsity introduces brittleness or needs special regularization/initialization/curriculum.  
- Clarify claims about nearing theoretical bounds by specifying which bounds, how they were computed, and absolute/relative distances to those bounds.

Minor comments:
- Provide a short glossary or recap of key terms (addressing distribution, erase/add vectors, controller).  
- Ensure figures have unambiguous labels for axes, units, and resource constraints (physical vs logical memory) and show error bars/variance where appropriate.  
- Briefly discuss applicability to larger-scale or longer-sequence tasks and anticipated bottlenecks when scaling further.

Overall assessment:
- Timely and well-executed contribution: sparse read/write is a meaningful architectural refinement with strong empirical performance and improved resource efficiency; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying evaluation protocol and adding concise NTM background.
