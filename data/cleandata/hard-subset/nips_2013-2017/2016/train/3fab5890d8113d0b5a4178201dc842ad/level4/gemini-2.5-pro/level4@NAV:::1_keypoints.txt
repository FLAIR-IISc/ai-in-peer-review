- Proposes using fast approximate nearest-neighbor structures + arg-top-K for attention in Neural Turing Machines, enabling scalability while preserving training performance.
- Conceptual novelty is limited because hashing-based scalable memory lookups are known; novelty lies in applying these tools to writeable memories.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on addressing requested changes.
- Reproducibility is insufficient: experimental task descriptions are lacking and the authors should provide meticulous task construction details and release their code.
- Paper needs a clear discussion of limitations and failure conditions, especially where the approach will fail.
- Clarify how the non-differentiable argmax/K-argmax affects training and under what circumstances the method succeeds or fails.
- Compare and analyze whether success stems from the choice of K, the nature of tasks, or other factors; include failure-case analysis and training-setup explanations.
- Suggest removing Section 3.6 from the paper.
- Advise against describing the Omniglot dataset as "non-synthetic" or "real-world."
