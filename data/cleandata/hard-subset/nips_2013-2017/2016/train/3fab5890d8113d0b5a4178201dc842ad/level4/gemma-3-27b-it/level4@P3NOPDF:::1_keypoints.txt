- Scales Neural Turing Machines by using fast ANN search and arg-top-K for attention, enabling training on more complex tasks without degrading performance.  
- Core novelty is incremental given prior memory networks with hashing, but applying ANN to writable memories is a valuable contribution.  
- Practical implementation challenges within an NTM framework are important and should be reported.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions.  
- Major reproducibility concern: task descriptions are insufficient and authors should release experimental code.  
- Need deeper analysis of limitations and failure modes, especially regarding discrete argmax/K-argmax and cases where K=1 resembles less effective MemNN-WSH.  
- Request discussion of when the method succeeds versus struggles and the impact of training without explicit differentiability considerations.  
- Suggest removing Section 3.6 as it does not contribute substantially.  
- Re-evaluate terminology: do not describe Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”  
- Clarify ANN implementation details (e.g., LSH, HNSW), libraries used, and parameter tuning for reproducibility.  
- Provide more analysis of the effect of K>1 on performance and attention smoothness.
