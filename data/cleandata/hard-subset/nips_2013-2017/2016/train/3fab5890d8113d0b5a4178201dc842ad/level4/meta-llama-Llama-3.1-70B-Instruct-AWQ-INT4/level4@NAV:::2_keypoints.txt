- Existing neural memory models scale poorly in space and time as memory size grows.
- Paper introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to retain representational capacity while enabling efficient training.
- Authors claim dramatic improvements: ~1000x faster training and ~3000x more memory-efficient.
- Reviewer has reservations about the paper's technical merit and leans toward rejection.
- Core contributions (efficient backpropagation and nearest-neighbor approximation with SAM) are considered straightforward.
- Proposed methods lack the sophistication expected for this venue.
- Experimental results are interesting but do not sufficiently offset the perceived technical weaknesses.
