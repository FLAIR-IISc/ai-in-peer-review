

The paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) variant featuring sparse read and write operations to address a critical limitation hindering NTMs' real-world applicability: their prohibitively large memory requirements and computational overhead for Backpropagation. The proposed architecture aims to resolve this challenge while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods. The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written. To enhance accessibility for non-expert readers, a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks in the introduction—or a clearer explanation of the memory mechanism—would improve self-containment (e.g., explicitly clarifying the intended functionality of the write operator relative to conventional memory systems). Additionally, the comparison in Figure 4 may benefit from a different metric: while equating physical memory usage is a valid and justifiable approach for resource-constrained scenarios, evaluating performance under identical memory capacity (rather than identical physical memory) would provide more direct insight into algorithmic efficiency. Despite this, the results demonstrate remarkably strong performance, approaching theoretical limits.
