Most neural network models augmented with memory do not scale well in space or time as the memory size grows. The authors introduce a memory access mechanism called Sparse Access Memory, showing that it maintains representational capacity while enabling efficient training, being 1000× faster and 3000× more memory-efficient. I do not find the paper technically strong enough for acceptance. The main technical elements—efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor using Sparse Access Memory—are fairly straightforward. Therefore, although the experiments are interesting, I am not in favor of acceptance.
