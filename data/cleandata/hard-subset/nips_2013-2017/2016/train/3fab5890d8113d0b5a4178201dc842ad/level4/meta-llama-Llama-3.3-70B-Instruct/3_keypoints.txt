- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks, enabling more complex learning beyond LSTMs.  
- Core contribution: restricts all memory reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of full access.  
- Authors provide theoretical validity and optimality arguments for SAM.  
- Empirical claim: reduced memory size yields improved read and write performance (section 4.1).  
- Learning costs of introducing sparsity are evaluated on standard NTM tasks (copy, associated recall, priority sorting) in section 4.2.  
- For certain tasks (notably tasks 2 and 3), combined costs are lower than those of NTM.  
- Real-world benchmark in section 4.4 implemented in Torch7 is a useful practical demonstration.  
- SAM is simple, novel, and achieves significant speedups compared to NTM.  
- Observation: sparse reads/writes mediated by a linear/ANN can improve early-stage learning in some cases (section 4.2).  
- Suggestion: paper would benefit from elaborating how findings generalize to other scenarios and exploring different ANN approaches with SAM.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear assumptions and a detailed appendix describing the architecture.
