- Proposes scaling Neural Turing Machines by replacing full attention with fast ANN lookups followed by an arg-top-K selection for read/write to enable much larger memories.

- Experiments show the approach allows NTMs to be trained on larger problems without performance degradation.

- Contribution is practically valuable: applying hashing/ANN to a writeable, dynamic memory (NTM) is non-trivial despite related prior work.

- Overall recommendation: accept if the authors address significant concerns.

- Major concern — Reproducibility: experimental task descriptions are insufficient; authors should detail task construction, data generation, evaluation, and release code.

- Major concern — Lack of analysis of limitations and failure modes; paper implies universal applicability without evidence.

- Major concern — Misleading claim of “smoothness”: arg-top-K is discrete/non-differentiable and must be addressed explicitly.

- Major concern — Need discussion of when the method fails (e.g., K=1 or read-only cases) and why it may succeed here versus prior models like MemNN-WSH.

- Major concern — Training with discrete selection lacks explicit handling of non-differentiability (e.g., RL); authors should analyze why direct training works and under what conditions.

- Minor: remove Section 3.6 as marginal to the main thesis.

- Minor: clarify wording about Omniglot—it's a standard benchmark but not a “real-world” or non-synthetic natural-image dataset.
