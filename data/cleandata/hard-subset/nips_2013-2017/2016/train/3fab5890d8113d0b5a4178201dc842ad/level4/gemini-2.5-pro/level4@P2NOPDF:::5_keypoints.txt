- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory-augmented neural network architecture.  
- Reports dramatic run-time and memory efficiency gains—up to three orders of magnitude over existing models.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance improvements beyond efficiency gains.  
- Shows remarkable generalization: trained on length-10k sequences, successfully handles test sequences up to length 200k.  
- Reports significant benefits in curriculum learning settings.  
- Claims potential for real-world deployment by overcoming prior computational and memory barriers.  
- Notes the paper lacks a thorough explanation for why SAM improves learning and generalization.  
- Recommends the authors provide theoretical or empirical hypotheses (e.g., sparsity as a form of regularization/representation learning) to explain the observed gains.
