This paper addresses a critical limitation of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs): their poor scalability in both space and time as memory size increases. The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme designed to retain the representational power of MANNs while enabling efficient training with very large memories. The core idea is to restrict read and write operations to a sparse subset of memory locations, achieving asymptotic improvements in complexity and substantial gains in practical performance – up to 1,000x faster and 3,000x less memory usage compared to non-sparse models. The authors demonstrate SAM’s effectiveness on a range of tasks, including synthetic algorithmic problems, one-shot Omniglot character recognition, and scaled versions of established benchmarks like the Babi tasks. They also show how SAM can be adapted to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC), resulting in significant speedups.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. It effectively motivates the problem of scalability in MANNs, situating the work within the context of prior research like Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) [7] and Memory Networks [21]. The architecture is explained in a logical and detailed manner, with sufficient information for an expert reader to understand the core mechanisms. The experimental results are comprehensive and convincingly demonstrate the benefits of SAM in terms of both computational efficiency and performance. The inclusion of results on the DNC, and adaptation of the approach to a related architecture, strengthens the paper’s impact and demonstrates generality.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a significant problem:** Scalability is a major bottleneck for applying MANNs to real-world problems, and this paper offers a compelling solution.
*   **Strong theoretical grounding:** The paper claims asymptotic optimality in space and time complexity, which, if correct, is a substantial contribution.
*   **Impressive empirical results:** The reported speedups and memory savings are significant, and the performance on various tasks is competitive with or better than existing approaches.
*   **Clear and well-organized writing:** The paper is easy to follow and understand, even for readers not intimately familiar with MANNs.
*   **Generality:** Adapting SAM to the DNC shows the broad applicability of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Background could be more self-contained:** While the paper clearly explains SAM, readers unfamiliar with the specifics of MANNs might benefit from a more detailed introduction to concepts like the write operator and the ideal behavior of memory access.  A more intuitive explanation of why standard MANN operations are computationally expensive would also be valuable.
*   **Figure 4 comparison could be clarified:** The choice to compare models based on equivalent *physical* memory usage, rather than *memory size*, is justifiable but requires more explicit discussion.  While highlighting resource constraints is valuable, presenting results with a fixed memory size would provide additional insight into the inherent efficiency of SAM.
*   **Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter details are in the supplementary material, but a brief discussion in the main text regarding the sensitivity of SAM’s performance to key hyperparameters (e.g., the sparsity level, the ANN parameters) would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   This paper presents a novel and effective solution to a critical problem in the field of memory-augmented neural networks.
*   The theoretical and empirical results are strong and convincingly demonstrate the benefits of SAM.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to understand.
*   The generality of the approach, as shown by its application to the DNC, adds to its value.
*   The work has the potential to significantly advance the state-of-the-art in MANNs and enable their application to more complex real-world problems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The background could be more accessible to readers unfamiliar with the intricacies of MANNs.
*   The comparison in Figure 4, while valid, could be interpreted as potentially misleading without careful explanation.
*   A more thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the findings.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. The proposed SAM architecture addresses a crucial limitation of MANNs and offers a promising path towards more scalable and efficient memory systems. While the minor weaknesses mentioned above could be addressed, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I recommend **acceptance** of this paper to NIPS 2016.
