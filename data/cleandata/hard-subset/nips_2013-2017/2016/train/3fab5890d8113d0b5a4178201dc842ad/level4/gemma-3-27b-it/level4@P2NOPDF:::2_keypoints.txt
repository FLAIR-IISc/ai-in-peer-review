- SAM addresses neural-memory scalability and reportedly achieves large efficiency gains (≈1000x speedup, ≈3000x memory reduction).  
- Reviewer feels the paper does not meet NeurIPS technical bar due to limited novelty.  
- The efficient backpropagation through SAM is an adaptation of established techniques, not a fundamentally new gradient method.  
- The approximate nearest neighbor component relies on existing algorithms and is not a substantial ANN algorithmic contribution.  
- The work is a significant engineering achievement but lacks novel algorithmic insights or deeper theoretical understanding.  
- The paper should provide theoretical analysis explaining why SAM yields such efficiency (e.g., analysis of sparsity patterns).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and should be broadened to more memory-augmented architectures and tasks.  
- More investigation is needed into hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly regarding the sparsity pattern.
