The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, aiming to overcome one of the major limitations of NTMs that make them unsuitable for real-world applications. The problem is that NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and operations for backpropagation, which the proposed architecture hopes to solve while maintaining comparable performance to the state of the art. 

The paper is well-structured and easy to read. However, providing a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section or an explanation of the memory would make the paper more self-contained, helping readers who are not experts in the field. For instance, although it is clear to those who have worked in this area, some readers might not understand what the write operator ideally is supposed to do compared to conventional memories. 

The comparison in Figure 4 may not be entirely fair. By choosing the memories such that they require the same amount of physical memory, the authors compare performance given a limited resource, which is a justifiable approach. Nevertheless, comparing performance using the same memory (not just the same physical memory) would be more informative about the performance differences. Nevertheless, it is very interesting to see how well this algorithm performs while being close to the theoretical bounds.
