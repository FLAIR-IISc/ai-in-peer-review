- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., NTMs, Memory Networks).
- Reports run-time and memory efficiency improvements of ~three orders of magnitude over prior memory-augmented models.
- Shows substantial performance gains in curriculum learning settings.
- Demonstrates strong generalization to much longer sequences (training up to 10k, testing up to 200k).
- Claims the results could make memory-augmented networks practically applicable with far-reaching impact.
- Main concern: paper lacks discussion explaining the causes of these drastic improvements.
- Efficiency alone seems insufficient to explain gains in curriculum learning and long-sequence extrapolation.
- Reviewer’s intuition: SAM may act analogously to sparse autoencoders; authors should provide even preliminary thoughts on this.
