- Proposes a Neural Turing Machine with sparse read/write access to reduce memory and backpropagation costs limiting NTM applicability.  
- Aims to retain performance comparable to state-of-the-art despite sparsity.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Recommends adding brief background on memory-augmented neural networks or a concise explanation of the memory mechanism for non-experts.  
- Suggests clarifying the write operator's intended function, as it may be ambiguous to non-specialists.  
- Questions fairness of Figure 4’s comparison: constraining physical memory differs from comparing equal memory capacity and could bias results.  
- Notes the algorithm shows strong performance close to theoretical bounds.
