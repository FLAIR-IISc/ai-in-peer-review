The authors address the poor scalability of many memory-augmented neural networks, which suffer from high space and time complexity as memory size increases. This paper introduces a memory access scheme, Sparse Access Memory, which is shown to retain the model's representational power while enabling significantly more efficient training. The authors report that their method is up to 1000x faster and 3000x more memory-efficient than previous approaches.

Despite these impressive empirical results, I find the technical contribution of the paper to be insufficient for acceptance. The main technical components—an efficient backpropagation algorithm and an approximate nearest neighbor search enabled by the sparse memory—are rather straightforward. Therefore, while the experiments are interesting, the limited novelty of the core method leads me to recommend against acceptance.
