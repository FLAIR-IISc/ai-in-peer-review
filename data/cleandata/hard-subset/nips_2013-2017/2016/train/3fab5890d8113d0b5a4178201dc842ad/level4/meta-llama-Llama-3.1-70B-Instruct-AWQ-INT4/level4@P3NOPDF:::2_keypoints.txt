- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to improve scalability of memory-augmented neural networks.
- Claims SAM retains representational power while being ~1000× faster and ~3000× more memory-efficient.
- Main technical contributions: efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbors with SAM.
- Reviewer finds these technical contributions straightforward and not particularly strong.
- Experimental results are interesting but deemed insufficient for acceptance on technical merit alone.
- Paper's primary value is empirical evaluation showing SAM's efficiency benefits.
- Reviewer requests deeper theoretical analysis and a more comprehensive evaluation of limitations and applications.
- Overall appreciation for addressing scalability, but recommendation for stronger technical contributions before acceptance.
