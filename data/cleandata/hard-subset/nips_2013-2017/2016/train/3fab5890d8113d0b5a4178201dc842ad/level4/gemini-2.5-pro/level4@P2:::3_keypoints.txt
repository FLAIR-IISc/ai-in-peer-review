- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): constrains reads/writes to K sparse memory locations using ANN for content-based reads and sparse writes to recently read or LRU slots.  
- Achieves asymptotic optimality: O(log N) time for reads via ANN and O(1) space per step with a rollback-style BPTT.  
- Empirically much faster and more memory-efficient than NTMs (up to 1600x speed, 3000x memory savings) while matching or improving task performance.  
- Significance: addresses a major scalability bottleneck for memory-augmented neural networks, enabling large-memory applications.  
- Novelty/technical quality: novel integration of ANN structures into end-to-end differentiable models and a clever sparse rollback BPTT.  
- Experimental validation: comprehensive benchmarks across NTM algorithmic tasks, long-sequence curriculum learning, bAbI, and Omniglot showing strong results and scalability to 200k steps.  
- Clarity: well-written, well-organized paper with useful supplementary material and diagrams.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of why SAM sometimes outperforms dense models (e.g., whether sparsity acts as regularization).  
- Weakness: needs deeper discussion of practical challenges/trade-offs integrating ANN structures (e.g., k-d tree imbalance, rebuild overhead, online-friendly alternatives).  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant, well-executed contribution with minor suggestions for deeper analysis.
