- Introduces an NTM with sparse read/write access to address scalability limits of conventional NTMs.
- Targets the prohibitive physical memory and computational costs of backpropagation in standard NTMs.
- Claims comparable performance to state-of-the-art while improving scalability.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Suggest adding brief background on memory-augmented neural networks for non-expert readers.
- Recommend clearer explanation of the memory mechanism, especially how the 'write' operator differs from conventional computer memory.
- Critiques experimental comparison: authors constrain models by equal physical memory, which may obscure intrinsic advantages.
- Suggest comparing models with equal logical memory size (same number of memory slots) to better isolate performance benefits.
- Notes results are impressive and approach theoretical bounds.
