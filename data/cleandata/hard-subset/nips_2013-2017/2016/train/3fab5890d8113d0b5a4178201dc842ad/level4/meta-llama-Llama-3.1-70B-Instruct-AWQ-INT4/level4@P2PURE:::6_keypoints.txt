- Proposes an NTM variant with sparse read/write to drastically reduce required physical memory and backprop operations while achieving near state-of-the-art performance.  
- Targets a key practical NTM limitation: prohibitive memory size and computational cost for real-world applications.  
- Paper is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Recommend adding a brief background or explanation of memory-augmented neural networks and the memory component, and clarify the intended/ideal behavior of the write operator versus conventional memories.  
- Figure 4 comparison may be misleading—better to compare models under the same memory capacity rather than the current setup; nevertheless, the algorithm’s performance is intriguingly close to theoretical bounds.
