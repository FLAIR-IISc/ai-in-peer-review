The proposed neural network models with memory augmentation typically exhibit poor scalability in both space and time as the memory size increases. The authors introduce a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory, demonstrating that it maintains representational power while allowing efficient training, achieving significant improvements of 1000x in speed and 3000x in memory efficiency. However, I do not believe the paper possesses sufficient technical merit for acceptance. The primary technical contributions, including efficient backpropagation and approximation of nearest neighbors using sparse access memory, are somewhat straightforward and lack complexity. Although the experiments presented appear interesting, I am not inclined to support acceptance.
