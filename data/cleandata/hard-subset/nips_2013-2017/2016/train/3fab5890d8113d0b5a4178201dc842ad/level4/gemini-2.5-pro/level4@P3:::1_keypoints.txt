- Addresses scalability bottleneck in MANNs by limiting memory interactions to a small constant-sized subset (Sparse Access Memory, SAM).  
- Sparse reads: use ANN to retrieve K nearest memory words and apply soft attention over that subset, reducing read time from O(N) to O(log N).  
- Sparse writes: restrict writes to recently read locations plus the least-recently-used slot to keep updates sparse.  
- Efficient BPTT that tracks only memory modifications yields O(log N) time and O(1) space per timestep w.r.t. memory size.  
- Empirical results show orders-of-magnitude speed and memory improvements and comparable task performance (algorithmic tasks, bAbI, Omniglot).  
- Demonstrated generality by applying SAM ideas to the DNC and achieving strong bAbI results.  
- Technical quality is high; experiments are thorough and support the performance and scalability claims.  
- Significance is high: enables training on much larger memories and extremely long sequences (up to 200k steps).  
- Reproducibility concern: algorithmic task descriptions are brief and code release is needed for replication.  
- Technical nuance: ANN-based top-K selection is a discrete, non-differentiable step (hard attention); the paper should acknowledge and discuss this.  
- Originality: scalable reads are not entirely new, but integrating them into writable memory with sparse writes and efficient BPTT is a novel, practical contribution.  
- Suggestion: analyze why the hard-attention approximation trains successfully without RL and identify potential failure cases or task dependencies.  
- Suggestion: clarify positioning relative to prior scalable Memory Networks and explicitly state the novelty (scalable read-write system).  
- Suggestion: rephrase Omniglot characterization to avoid overstating it as "real-world" data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important practical problem, delivers effective and scalable solution, and provides extensive empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental conceptual novelty, insufficient analysis of non-differentiable selection implications, and lack of experimental reproducibility details.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing reproducibility and deeper discussion of the gradient/approximation issues.
