This paper introduces an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while being much more efficient to train with very large memory. It presents several original concepts that exhibit technical brilliance. The problem it addresses is well motivated and convincingly tackled. Given the importance of scaling memory systems for neural networks, the potential impact is significant. The paper introduces the problem and the solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
