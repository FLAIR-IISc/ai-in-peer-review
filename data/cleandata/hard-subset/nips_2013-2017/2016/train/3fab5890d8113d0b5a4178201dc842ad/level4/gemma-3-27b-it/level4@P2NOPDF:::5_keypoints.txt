- Proposes a Sparse Access Memory (SAM) architecture for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Reports up to three orders of magnitude (≈1000×) improvements in runtime and memory efficiency.  
- Demonstrates improved performance in curriculum learning settings.  
- Shows strong generalization to much longer sequences (tested up to 200,000 vs trained on up to 10,000).  
- Argues the work could enable practical, real-world adoption of memory-augmented networks.  
- Paper lacks a thorough discussion of the mechanisms driving curriculum and long-sequence generalization gains.  
- Notes that efficiency improvements alone do not fully explain the observed performance boost.  
- Suggests the sparse access may act like regularization or feature selection (analogous to sparse autoencoders).  
- Recommends preliminary exploration or author insights into why the architecture improves generalization to strengthen the paper.
