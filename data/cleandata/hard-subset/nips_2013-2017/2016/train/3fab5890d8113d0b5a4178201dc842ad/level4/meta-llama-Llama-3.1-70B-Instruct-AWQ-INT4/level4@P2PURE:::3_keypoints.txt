- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel scheme constraining all reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words.
- Positions SAM within memory-augmented neural networks, building on Neural Turing Machines (NTMs).
- Main contribution: formal proof of SAM’s methodology and optimality, showing reduced memory size yields good read/write performance (Sec. 4.1).
- Evaluates learning costs of introducing sparsity (Sec. 4.2) on standard NTM tasks: copy, associative recall, and priority sort.
- Empirical results show combined costs lower than NTMs for some tasks (notably tasks 2 and 3).
- Includes appreciated real-world examples demonstrating applicability (Sec. 4.4).
- Benchmarks implemented in Torch7; approach is straightforward, novel, and achieves significant speedups over NTMs.
- Notes that sparse reads/writes, enabled by a linear or ANN, can improve early-stage learning in some cases (Sec. 4.2).
- Recommends expanding discussion on generalization conditions and experimenting with different ANN variants combined with SAM.
- Paper is well-written with clear assumptions and detailed architecture described in the appendix; overall engaging.
