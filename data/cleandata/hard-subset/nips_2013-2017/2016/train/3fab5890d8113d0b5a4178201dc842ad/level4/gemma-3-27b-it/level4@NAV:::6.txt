This paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) variant, termed the MTN, designed to address a key limitation of NTMs – their substantial memory and computational requirements for backpropagation – hindering their practical application. The proposed architecture aims to achieve comparable performance to current state-of-the-art methods while significantly reducing these demands. The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. To enhance accessibility for readers less familiar with the field, a concise overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the introduction, or a more detailed explanation of the memory mechanisms (specifically, clarifying the function of the write operator in relation to conventional memory systems), would be beneficial. Regarding the experimental comparison in Figure 4, while the chosen methodology of equating physical memory usage provides a valid assessment under resource constraints, presenting results with identical memory capacity (rather than equivalent physical memory) might offer a more direct insight into performance scaling. Despite this point, the algorithm's performance, approaching theoretical limits, is highly promising and makes for a compelling contribution.
