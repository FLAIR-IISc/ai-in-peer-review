- Method: Uses fast approximate nearest neighbor search and arg-top-K within a Neural Turing Machine to compute attention, maintaining training performance and enabling scaling to larger instances.  
- Novelty: Idea is incremental—similar techniques exist in Memory Networks and large-memory work—but application to writable memories is less common; practical implementation merits publication.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on revisions.  
- Reproducibility: Model and task descriptions are insufficient for replication; authors should detail task construction and commit to releasing experimental code.  
- Limitations analysis: Must address discrete nature of argmax/K-argmax vs claimed smoothness, investigate K=1 behavior (similarity to MemNN-WSH), analyze failure cases and conditions for training success with discrete action selection.
