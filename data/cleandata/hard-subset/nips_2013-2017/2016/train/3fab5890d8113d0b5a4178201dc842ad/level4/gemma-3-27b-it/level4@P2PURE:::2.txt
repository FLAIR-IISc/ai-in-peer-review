Most neural network models incorporating external memory exhibit poor scalability in both space and time as memory size increases. The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory, a memory access scheme designed to maintain representational power while enabling efficient training – achieving speedups of 1000x and 3000x improvements in memory efficiency. However, I do not believe the paper presents sufficient technical novelty to warrant acceptance. While the experiments are interesting, the core technical contributions – efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor lookup using sparse access memory – appear relatively straightforward. Consequently, I do not recommend acceptance.
