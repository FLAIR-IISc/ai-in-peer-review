- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): constrains all reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words.  
- Demonstrates SAM’s optimality and improved read/write performance from reduced memory size (Section 4.1).  
- Evaluates learning costs of sparsity on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort); combined costs lower than NTMs on tasks 2 and 3 (Section 4.2).  
- Shows significant speedup over NTMs; sparse reads/writes via linear or ANN methods can aid early-stage learning (Section 4.2).  
- Includes real-world examples and benchmarks implemented in Torch7 (Section 4.4).  
- Suggests expanding discussion on generalizing SAM and exploring alternative ANN approaches to improve learning.  
- Paper is well-written with clear assumptions and a detailed architecture description in the appendix.
