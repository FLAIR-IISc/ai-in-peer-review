- Proposal: use fast approximate nearest-neighbor structures and arg-top-K for computing attention in Neural Turing Machines, enabling training on larger problems without harming performance.  
- Novelty: limited relative to prior memory-network work, but applying these techniques to writable memories is a notable contribution.  
- Practical value: implementation and engineering considerations are useful and worth reporting.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions.  
- Reproducibility: provide much more detailed task and experimental descriptions and commit to releasing code, since no standard NTM code/tasks exist.  
- Limitations/failure modes: discuss and analyze failure cases and limitations of the approach, especially where it may not work.  
- Non-smoothness concern: argmax/K-argmax are inherently non-smooth; explain implications (e.g., K=1 similarity to MemNN-WSH and its poorer performance).  
- Analysis needed: clarify whether observed issues stem from K=1 vs. task specifics and characterize tasks/setups that permit successful training.  
- Training dynamics: address that discrete actions are trained without modeling underlying dynamics and compare limits to methods that do (e.g., related work).  
- Paper edits: remove section 3.6 as it adds little value.  
- Terminology: avoid calling Omniglot “non-synthetic” or “real-world,” as those terms are misleading.
