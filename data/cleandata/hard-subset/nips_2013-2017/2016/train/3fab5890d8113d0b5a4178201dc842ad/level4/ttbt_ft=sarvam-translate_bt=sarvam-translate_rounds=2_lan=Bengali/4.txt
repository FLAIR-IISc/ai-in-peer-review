This paper presents an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that has the representational capacity of an original NTM, but is much more efficient for training with very large memory. The paper introduces many fundamental concepts that appear to be technically very promising. The problem it attempts to solve is well-motivated and well-attacked. The impact of memory system scaling for neural networks is significant, and this paper clearly, concisely, and well-explained presents the problem and solution.
