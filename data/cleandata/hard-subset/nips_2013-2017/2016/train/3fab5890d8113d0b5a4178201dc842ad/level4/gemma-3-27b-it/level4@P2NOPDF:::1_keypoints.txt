- Applies fast approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) search and arg-top-K to scale Neural Turing Machines (NTMs), showing no negative impact on training and enabling larger, more complex tasks.  
- Conceptually incremental—ANNs in memory networks are known—but applying them to writable memories is less common and the practical engineering/results are valuable.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the reviewer’s concerns.  
- Reproducibility is insufficient: task definitions lack crucial detail, and the authors should provide comprehensive task construction descriptions and release experimental code.  
- Need a deeper critical analysis of model limitations: the argmax/K-argmax introduces discontinuities that may affect training and resemble prior MemNN-WSH failures.  
- Investigate why the method succeeds: is it due to K>1, task choice, or other factors; report failure cases and characterize tasks/conditions where it works or fails.  
- Training does not appear to account explicitly for the model’s discrete decisions (unlike reinforcement/relaxation-based approaches); discuss implications and alternatives.  
- Editorial suggestions: remove section 3.6 as marginal, and correct the misleading characterization of Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
