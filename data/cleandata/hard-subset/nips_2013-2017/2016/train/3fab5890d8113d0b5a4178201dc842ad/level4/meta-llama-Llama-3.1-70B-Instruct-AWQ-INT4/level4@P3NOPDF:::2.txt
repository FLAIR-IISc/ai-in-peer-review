This paper proposes a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to address the scalability issues of neural network models augmented with memory. The authors demonstrate that SAM retains the representational power of the original model while achieving significant improvements in training efficiency, being 1000 times faster and 3000 times more memory-efficient. 

The main technical contributions of this paper are the development of efficient backpropagation and approximation of nearest neighbors using SAM. However, I found these contributions to be relatively straightforward and not particularly technically strong. While the experimental results presented in the paper are interesting, I do not believe that the paper is technically strong enough to warrant acceptance.

In my opinion, the paper's primary value lies in its empirical evaluation, which demonstrates the potential benefits of SAM in improving the efficiency of neural network models with memory. However, I would have liked to see a more in-depth analysis of the theoretical foundations of SAM and a more comprehensive evaluation of its limitations and potential applications.

Overall, I appreciate the authors' efforts to address the scalability issues of neural network models with memory, but I believe that the paper requires more significant technical contributions to be considered for acceptance.
