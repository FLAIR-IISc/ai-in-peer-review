Most proposed neural network models augmented with memory scale poorly in terms of both space and time complexity as the amount of memory grows. The authors present a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory, demonstrating that it retains representational power while allowing for efficient training, achieving a 1000x speedup and 3000x memory efficiency. However, I do not believe the paper is technically strong enough to warrant acceptance. Although the main technical contributions, efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor search using sparse access memory, are sound, they are relatively straightforward and do not offer significant novelty. While the experiments appear interesting, I am inclined to reject the paper.
