- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), constraining reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words in memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Situates SAM in the lineage of Neural Turing Machines and aims to extend capabilities beyond LSTMs.  
- Main contribution: formal proof of the SAM methodology and demonstration of its optimality.  
- Constrained access reduces effective memory size, plausibly improving read/write performance (section 4.1).  
- Authors analyze learning costs introduced by sparsity and evaluate on standard NTM tasks: Copy, Associative Recall, and Priority Sort.  
- Overall cost (learning + access) is lower than standard NTM for Associative Recall and Priority Sort.  
- Includes a real-world example with a Torch7 implementation showing significant speedup over NTM (section 4.4).  
- Observation that sparse reads/writes (via linear layer or ANN) can benefit early-stage learning in some cases (section 4.2).  
- Suggests strengthening the paper by exploring when benefits generalize and testing SAM with different ANN architectures.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly states assumptions, provides detailed architectural descriptions in the appendix, and is a valuable contribution.
