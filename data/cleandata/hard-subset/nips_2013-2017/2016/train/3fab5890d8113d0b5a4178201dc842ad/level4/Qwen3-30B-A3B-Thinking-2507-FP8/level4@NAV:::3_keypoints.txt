- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- SAM restricts all external memory reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of the full memory.
- Authors provide rigorous proofs of the methodology and claim of optimality.
- Reduced memory footprint yields improved read/write performance (demonstrated in Section 4.1).
- Section 4.2 analyzes the learning cost of sparsity on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sorting).
- Combined training cost is lower than NTM for certain tasks (specifically tasks 2 and 3).
- Inclusion of a real-world example (Section 4.4) and Torch7 benchmarking are commendable.
- Core idea is straightforward, novel, and achieves significant speedups over NTM.
- Notable finding: sparse reads/writes via linear or ANN mechanisms can improve early-stage learning in some scenarios.
- Suggested improvement: clarify under which conditions SAM improves learning and explore integration with diverse ANN architectures.
- Paper is well-written, states clear assumptions, and provides a detailed architecture in the appendix.
