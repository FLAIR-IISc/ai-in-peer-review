- Proposes a Neural Turing Machine with sparse read/write access to reduce memory and computational cost for backpropagation.  
- Claims to overcome a key NTM limitation and to match state-of-the-art performance.  
- Paper is well-structured and easy to read.  
- Recommend adding a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks for self-containment, especially explaining the write operator vs. conventional memory.  
- Experimental comparison in Figure 4 may be unfair: normalizing by physical memory footprint should be supplemented by comparisons using the same number of memory locations to isolate sparse-access effects.  
- Results are impressive, with performance close to theoretical bounds.
