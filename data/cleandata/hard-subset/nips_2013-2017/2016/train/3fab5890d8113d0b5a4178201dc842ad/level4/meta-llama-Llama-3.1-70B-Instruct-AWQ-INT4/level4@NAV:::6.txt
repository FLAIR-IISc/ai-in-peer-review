This paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a major limitation of NTMs that hinders their applicability in real-world scenarios. Specifically, the proposed architecture aims to mitigate the need for excessively large physical memories and computationally expensive backpropagation operations, while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-organized and accessible.

To enhance the paper's self-contained nature, consider incorporating a concise overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section or providing a detailed explanation of the memory component. This would facilitate understanding for non-expert readers, who may be unfamiliar with the nuances of memory operations in NTMs, such as the intended behavior of the write operator in comparison to conventional memory systems.
