- Introduces a sparse-read/write variant of the Neural Turing Machine to reduce large physical memory and dense backpropagation costs.  
- Aims to retain performance comparable to state-of-the-art while improving scalability and efficiency.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; core contribution of sparse memory access is significant and practical.  
- Accessibility could be improved with a brief MANN background and clearer explanation of NTM memory/write mechanics for non-experts.  
- Experimental critique: normalizing by physical memory footprint is valid, but comparing models at the same logical memory size would better show scaling behavior.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution that achieves near-theoretical performance while addressing a key practical limitation.
