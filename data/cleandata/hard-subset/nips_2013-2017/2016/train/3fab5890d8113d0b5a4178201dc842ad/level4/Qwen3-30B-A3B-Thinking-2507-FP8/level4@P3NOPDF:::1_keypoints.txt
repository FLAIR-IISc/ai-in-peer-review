- Paper proposes using fast ANN structures and arg-top-K to compute attention in NTMs, enabling scalable attention for writeable memories.  
- Practical adaptation to writeable memories is a valuable engineering contribution, though novelty is incremental relative to prior ANN-based memory work.  
- Replicability is insufficient: task construction is underspecified and authors must provide detailed task generation protocols and public code/training scripts.  
- Failure analysis is missing: the paper overlooks limitations of discrete arg-top-K (non-differentiable for K=1) and must analyze where it fails versus smooth alternatives.  
- Authors should clarify when and why their approach succeeds (e.g., task characteristics, signal-to-noise) and whether K=1 is essential or higher K generalizes better.  
- Terminology corrections required: remove claim that Omniglot is "non-synthetic" (Section 3.6) and replace vague term "smooth" with precise phrasing like "non-differentiable discrete read operation."  
- Paper risks overstating impact by not distinguishing its contribution from existing ANN-based memory networks and ignoring discrete vs. smooth optimization trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on addressing reproducibility, failure analysis, terminology fixes, and code release.  
- Specific required revisions: add explicit task generation subsection, include comparative failure-analysis table (arg-top-K vs. smooth) on multiple tasks, remove Section 3.6, and commit to releasing code and task generators.
