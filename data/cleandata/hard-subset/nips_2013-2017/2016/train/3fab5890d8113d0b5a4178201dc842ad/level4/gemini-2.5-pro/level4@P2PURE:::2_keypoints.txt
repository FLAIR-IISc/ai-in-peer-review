- Memory-augmented neural networks suffer poor scalability: time and space complexity grow unfavorably with memory size.
- Authors propose "Sparse Access Memory" (SAM) as a memory access scheme to address scalability.
- Reported efficiency gains: ~1000x speedup and ~3000x reduction in memory usage while preserving representational power.
- Core technical components are an efficient backpropagation method and an approximate nearest neighbor search for sparse memory.
- Reviewer judges these core components to be relatively straightforward, offering limited technical novelty.
- Despite interesting experimental results, the limited novelty leads the reviewer to recommend against acceptance.
