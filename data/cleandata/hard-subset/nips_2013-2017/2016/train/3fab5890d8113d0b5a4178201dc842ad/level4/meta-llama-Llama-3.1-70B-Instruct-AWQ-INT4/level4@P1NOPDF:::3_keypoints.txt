- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- Constrains all reads and writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of allowing unlimited memory access.
- Demonstrates optimality of the methodology and improved read/write performance due to reduced memory size (Sec. 4.1).
- Evaluates learning costs of sparsity on standard NTM tasks (copy, associated recall, priority sort) (Sec. 4.2).
- Finds combined learning costs are lower than NTM for certain tasks (tasks 2 and 3).
- Includes a real-world benchmark implemented in Torch7 (Sec. 4.4).
- Shows significant speedup compared to NTM; idea is straightforward and novel.
- Observes that sparse reads/writes mediated by a linear or ANN can benefit early-stage learning in some cases (Sec. 4.2).
- Recommends elaboration on generalizing SAM, conditions for improved learning, and exploring different ANN integrations.
- Paper is well-written with clear assumptions and architecture details in the appendix.
