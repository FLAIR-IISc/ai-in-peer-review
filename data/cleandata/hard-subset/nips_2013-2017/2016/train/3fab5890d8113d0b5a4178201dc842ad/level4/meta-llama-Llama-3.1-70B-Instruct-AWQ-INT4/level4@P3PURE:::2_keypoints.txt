- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to scale neural-network memory access efficiently, claiming retention of representational power.
- Reports large empirical gains: ~1000× speedup and ~3000× memory efficiency during training.
- Core technical contributions (efficient backpropagation and nearest-neighbor approximation via sparse access) are viewed as straightforward.
- Reviewer finds the methods lack the novelty/complexity expected for a top-tier conference.
- Experiments are interesting but insufficient to convince the reviewer; not recommending acceptance.
