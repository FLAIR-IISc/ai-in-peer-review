The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a major limitation of NTMs that hinders their suitability for real-world applications. Specifically, NTMs require prohibitively large physical memories and operations to perform backpropagation, which the proposed architecture aims to overcome while achieving comparable performance to state-of-the-art models. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

To enhance the paper's self-containment, it would be beneficial to include a brief description of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section or provide an explanation of the memory component. This would assist readers unfamiliar with the field, as certain concepts, such as the ideal behavior of the write operator compared to conventional memories, may not be immediately clear.

The comparison presented in Figure 4 may be perceived as unfair. While comparing models with the same amount of physical memory is a valid approach, given the limited resources, it would be more informative to compare models using the same memory. This would provide a more accurate representation of the performance differences between the models. Nevertheless, it is intriguing to observe the algorithm's performance, which is remarkably close to the theoretical bounds.
