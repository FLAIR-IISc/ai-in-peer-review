- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- Key contribution: restricts all reads and writes to a fixed-size subset of memory locations instead of addressing entire memory.
- Provides theoretical justification and optimality results for the sparse-access approach.
- Demonstrates improved read/write performance and reduced active memory footprint (results in Section 4.1).
- Evaluates learning costs on standard NTM tasks (Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort); combined costs are lower than NTM for some tasks (notably Associative Recall and Priority Sort).
- Includes a real-world example (Section 4.4) and implements benchmarks in Torch7.
- Finds that sparse reads/writes can sometimes benefit early-stage learning for both linear controllers and ANNs (Section 4.2).
- Requests clearer discussion of when and why sparsity aids learning and how SAM generalizes to other controllers or ANN approaches.
- Paper is well written with clear assumptions and architectural details in the appendix; offers significant and impressive speedups over NTM.
