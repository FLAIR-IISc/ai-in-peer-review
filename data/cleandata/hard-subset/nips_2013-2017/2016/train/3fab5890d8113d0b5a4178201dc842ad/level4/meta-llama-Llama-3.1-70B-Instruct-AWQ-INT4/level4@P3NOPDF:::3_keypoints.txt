- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Main contribution: constrain reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of unlimited access.  
- Authors present methodology and argue optimality of the sparse access approach.  
- Reduced memory size leads to improved read/write performance (Section 4.1).  
- Investigates learning costs of sparsity on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort) in Section 4.2.  
- Combined learning costs are lower than NTM for associative recall and priority sort (tasks 2 and 3).  
- Includes a valuable real-world example demonstrating SAM (Section 4.4).  
- Benchmarks performed in Torch7; the idea is straightforward and novel.  
- SAM achieves significant speedup compared to NTM, which is impressive.  
- Sparse reads/writes via linear/ANN controllers can benefit early-stage learning in some cases.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on generalizing SAM and exploring other ANN/controller approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, assumptions are clear, architecture detailed in appendix, and writing is engaging.
