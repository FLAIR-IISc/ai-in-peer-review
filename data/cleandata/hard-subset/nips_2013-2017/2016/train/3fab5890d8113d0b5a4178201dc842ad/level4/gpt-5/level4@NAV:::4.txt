This paper proposes an end-to-end differentiable memory addressing mechanism that preserves the representational capacity of the original NTM while being far more efficient to train with very large memories. It introduces numerous novel concepts that exhibit clear technical ingenuity. The problem it addresses is well motivated and effectively tackled. Given the substantial importance of scaling memory systems for neural networks, the paper presents both the problem and its solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
