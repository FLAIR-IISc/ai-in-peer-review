- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) architecture for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., NTMs, Memory Networks).
- SAM yields up to three orders of magnitude improvement in run-time and memory efficiency over prior memory-augmented networks.
- SAM produces significant gains in curriculum learning and generalizes to sequences far longer than training (from ~10,000 to ~200,000).
- These results could be groundbreaking and make memory-augmented networks practically viable with broad implications.
- Field context: work builds on NTMs and Memory Networks; prior results were interesting but not yet practically applicable.
- Efficiency alone likely does not explain the large curriculum/generalization gains—other factors are at play.
- Reviewer requests authors' intuition or analysis (e.g., SAM acting like sparse auto-encoders) to explain these phenomena.
