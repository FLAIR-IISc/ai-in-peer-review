- Method: uses fast approximate-nearest-neighbors and argmax-top-K to compute attention in a Neural Turing Machine.  
- Empirical result: approach does not degrade training performance and enables training on larger-scale problems.  
- Novelty concern: limited conceptual novelty since prior Memory Network work already used hashing and millions of memory items.  
- Contribution: meaningful practical advance in applying these techniques to writable memories, where they were not previously used.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing two major revisions.  
- Revision 1 (reproducibility): provide meticulous task construction details and commit to releasing the experimental code.  
- Revision 2 (analysis): add analysis of failure modes and smoothness—argmax-top-K is non-smooth, K=1 resembles MemNN-WSH which underperformed, and it’s unclear whether K, task design, or discreteness drive results.  
- Miscellaneous fixes: remove Section 3.6 and do not label the Omniglot dataset as "non-synthetic."
