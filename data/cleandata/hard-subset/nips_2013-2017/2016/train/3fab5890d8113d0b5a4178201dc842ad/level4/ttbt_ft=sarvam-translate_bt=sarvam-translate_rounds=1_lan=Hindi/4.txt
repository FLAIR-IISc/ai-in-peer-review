This research paper introduces an end-to-end differential memory access scheme that has the representational capacity of a basic NTM, but is much more efficient in training with very large memory. The paper introduces several fundamental concepts that sound technically very good. The problem it attempts to solve is well-motivated and well-attackable. The impact of scaling memory systems for neural networks is significant, and this paper presents the problem and solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
