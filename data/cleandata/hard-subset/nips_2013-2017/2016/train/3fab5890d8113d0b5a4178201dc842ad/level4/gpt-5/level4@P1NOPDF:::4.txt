This paper introduces an end-to-end differentiable memory access scheme that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while being substantially more efficient to train with very large memories. It presents several original concepts that exhibit clear technical sophistication. The problem is well motivated and addressed effectively. Given the importance of scaling memory systems for neural networks, the contribution is significant. The paper presents both the problem and its solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
