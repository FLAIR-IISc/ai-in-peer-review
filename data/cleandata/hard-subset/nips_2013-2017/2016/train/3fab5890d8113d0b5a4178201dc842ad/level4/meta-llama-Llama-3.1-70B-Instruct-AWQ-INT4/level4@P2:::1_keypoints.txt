- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads/writes to scale memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Uses approximate nearest neighbor search and K-argmax attention to limit memory accesses.  
- Introduces sparse memory management to track usage and reuse unused memory blocks.  
- Achieves significant speedups and large reductions in memory usage compared to dense models.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on algorithmic, QA, and one-shot Omniglot tasks with comparable data efficiency.  
- Shows SAM can scale to large memories and long sequence lengths while maintaining performance.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains method and advantages.  
- Lacks detailed descriptions of task construction, hyperparameters, and training procedures for reproducibility.  
- Non-smooth argmax/K-argmax operations may cause training difficulties; more failure-case analysis is needed.  
- Recommends removing Section 3.6 as unnecessary and potentially distracting.  
- Advises against calling Omniglot "non-synthetic" or "real-world" to avoid misleading terminology.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to add experimental details, analyze limitations, and clarify/remove problematic sections.
