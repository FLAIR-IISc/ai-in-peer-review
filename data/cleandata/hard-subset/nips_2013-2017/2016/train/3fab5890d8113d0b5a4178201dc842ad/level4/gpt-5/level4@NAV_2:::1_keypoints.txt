- Uses fast ANN indexing + top-K selection for attention in a Neural Turing Machine, enabling scalable training without apparent harm to learning.  
- Novelty is limited compared to prior Memory Network work that scales via hashing, though applying these tools to writable external memories is a valuable engineering contribution.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on revisions.  
- Reproducibility concern: task descriptions are sparse; paper must precisely specify task construction and commit to releasing experiment code.  
- Need analysis of limits and failure modes: under what conditions does the approach break and is it robust across tasks?  
- Argmax/top-K are non-smooth; must explain how discrete selection (especially K=1) affects training and performance.  
- K=1 resembles MemNN-WSH (reported to underperform smooth models); clarify whether gaps are due to K, task choice, or other factors.  
- Suggest removing Section 3.6 and avoid describing Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
