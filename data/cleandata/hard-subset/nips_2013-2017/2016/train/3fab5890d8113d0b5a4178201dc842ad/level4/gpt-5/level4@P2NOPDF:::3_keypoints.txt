- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Restricts reads and writes to a fixed-size subset of memory locations instead of dense operations.  
- Provides theoretical justification and optimality results for the sparse design.  
- Shows constrained access yields strong read/write performance by reducing memory operations (Section 4.1).  
- Evaluates learning costs of sparsity and reports better combined costs than NTM on associative recall and priority sort (Section 4.2).  
- Finds sparse reads/writes can improve early-stage learning for both linear and neural controllers.  
- Includes a real-world example and Torch7 implementation of experiments (Section 4.4).  
- Suggests strengthening the paper by analyzing when/why sparsity helps and testing SAM with a broader range of controllers/architectures.  
- Paper is clearly written with explicit assumptions and detailed architectural appendices.
