- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to improve scalability of memory-augmented neural networks.  
- SAM retains representational power while enabling efficient training with very large memories.  
- Key innovations are sparse reads and writes that reduce computation and memory usage.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly describes limitations of existing approaches.  
- Technical contributions (efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest-neighbor for SAM) are well-explained.  
- Experiments show significant speedups, memory efficiency gains, and comparable data efficiency on synthetic tasks and Omniglot.  
- Reviewer doubts the novelty: using sparse matrices and ANN is not particularly new or groundbreaking.  
- Concern that the paper may not fit NIPS due to insufficiently significant technical contributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel memory-access scheme, strong empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited technical novelty and potential misfit with NIPS standards.  
- Overall verdict: solid, well-executed work but likely not technically strong enough for NIPS acceptance.
