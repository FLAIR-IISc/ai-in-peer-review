The paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (MTN) with sparse read and write access to address a key limitation that makes MTNs impractical for real-world applications. Specifically, MTNs require prohibitively large physical memory and substantial computation for backpropagation, which the proposed architecture aims to mitigate while achieving performance comparable to the state of the art. The paper is well structured and easy to follow. Including a brief overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section—or an explanation of the memory component—to make the paper more self-contained would help readers who are not experts (e.g., while it is clear to those working in this area, some may not understand what the write operator is intended to do relative to conventional memories). I am also unsure that the comparison in Figure 4 is entirely fair. Equalizing physical memory allows a comparison under limited resources (which you have done and is certainly defensible), but I think matching the same logical memory rather than the same physical memory would be more informative about performance. Nevertheless, it is very interesting to see how well this algorithm performs while staying so close to theoretical bounds.
