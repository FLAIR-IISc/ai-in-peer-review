- Memory-augmented neural networks struggle with scalability (space and time) as memory size increases.
- The paper introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme.
- SAM purportedly maintains representational power while enabling efficient training.
- Reported empirical improvements: ~1000x speedup and ~3000x memory reduction.
- Reviewer questions the technical merit and is hesitant to recommend acceptance.
- Key technical contributions (efficient backpropagation and nearest-neighbor approximation via sparsity) are viewed as straightforward.
- The contributions lack the complexity/depth expected for NIPS, and while experiments are intriguing, they are not deemed strong enough for acceptance.
