- Uses fast approximate nearest neighbors and arg-top-K for attention in a Neural Turing Machine, enabling larger-instance training without degrading performance.  
- Novelty is incremental; related to prior memory-network hashing work (Weston et al.), though writable-memory application is less common.  
- Practical implementation details are valuable and justify publication, so recommend acceptance contingent on revisions.  
- Replicability is currently poor: task descriptions are insufficient and authors should thoroughly describe task construction and release experimental code.  
- Require a critical analysis of method limitations, especially regarding the non-smoothness of argmax/K-argmax operations.  
- With K=1 the model closely resembles MemNN-WSH (Sukhbaatar et al.), which previously underperformed smooth alternatives; authors should investigate whether K=1, task choices, or other factors explain differences.  
- Paper should analyze failure cases and clarify the task characteristics and settings under which the approach succeeds or fails.  
- The model makes discrete action choices but training does not explicitly address discreteness (unlike Graves et al.); this discrepancy needs discussion.  
- Recommend removing section 3.6 and correcting the inaccurate characterization of Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
