- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., NTMs, Memory Networks).
- Reports dramatic runtime and memory savings—up to three orders of magnitude—compared to dense-access methods.
- Demonstrates substantial gains under curriculum training.
- Shows strong long-range extrapolation: trained on sequences up to 10,000 and evaluated successfully on sequences of length 200,000.
- Claims these efficiency and performance gains could make memory-augmented models practical for real-world use.
- Positions the work as a potentially landmark contribution to a field that has struggled with practical applicability.
- Notes the primary missing element is an explanation for why curriculum performance and long-range extrapolation improve so dramatically.
- Argues pure runtime/memory efficiency cannot fully explain the extrapolation and curriculum effects.
- Suggests sparse access might act like sparse autoencoders—improving representation and credit assignment—and requests at least a preliminary analysis from the authors.
