- Claims comparable performance to state-of-the-art while significantly lowering resource requirements.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Suggests adding a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks for self-containment and clarifying the write operator’s intended role for non-experts.  
- Notes Figure 4’s hardware-aware comparison (identical physical memory) is justified but recommends also comparing fixed memory capacity (same number of slots) to assess algorithmic memory utilization.  
- Experimental results show strong performance near theoretical efficiency bounds, supporting the main claims.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and the core technical argument—that sparse access enables practical scalability—is well supported.  
- Represents a meaningful step toward making NTMs viable for real-world deployment and is effectively communicated.
