- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a memory access mechanism for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Positions work in context of Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) and limitations of LSTMs.  
- Main contribution: restricts reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words (sparsity).  
- Provides theoretical validation and claims of optimality for the sparse access scheme.  
- Section 4.1: reduced effective memory size yields improved read/write performance.  
- Section 4.2: analyzes learning costs of sparsity using standard NTM tasks (Copy, Associative Recall, Priority Sort).  
- Empirical result: combined costs lower than NTMs for Associative Recall and Priority Sort.  
- Includes a real-world example (section 4.4) and benchmarks implemented in Torch7.  
- Method is simple, novel, and delivers significant speedup over NTMs.  
- Observation: sparse reads/writes via linear models or ANNs can improve early-stage learning in some settings.  
- Recommendation: authors should explore generalization conditions and test SAM with alternative ANN architectures.  
- Paper quality: well-written, assumptions clear, architecture detailed in the appendix, engaging and insightful.
