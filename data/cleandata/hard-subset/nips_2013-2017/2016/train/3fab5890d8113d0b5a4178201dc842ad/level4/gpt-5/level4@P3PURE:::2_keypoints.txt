- Addresses scaling problems of memory-augmented neural networks and proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM) to enable efficient training.  
- Claims substantial empirical improvements (up to 1000× faster training and 3000× better memory efficiency).  
- Experiments are interesting and show large gains.  
- Core technical contributions (efficient backprop through sparse access; use of approximate nearest-neighbor search) are viewed as straightforward.  
- Reviewer’s verdict: not technically strong enough for acceptance despite appealing empirical results.
