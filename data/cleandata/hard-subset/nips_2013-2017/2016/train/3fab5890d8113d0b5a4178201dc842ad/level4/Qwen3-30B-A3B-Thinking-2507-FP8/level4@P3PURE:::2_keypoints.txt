- Memory-augmented neural networks scale poorly in space and time as memory size increases.  
- Authors propose Sparse Access Memory (SAM) as a memory access scheme to address scalability.  
- SAM is claimed to preserve representational power while enabling efficient training.  
- Reported empirical gains: ~1000× speedup and ~3000× memory efficiency over prior methods.  
- Experimental results are interesting and compelling.  
- Technical contributions (efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor search) are relatively straightforward.  
- Innovations lack significant novelty, making the paper's technical contribution insufficient for acceptance.  
- Reviewer cannot recommend acceptance despite the experimental claims.
