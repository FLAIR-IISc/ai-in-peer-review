Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), an end-to-end differentiable memory-access mechanism preserving the representational power of Neural Turing Machines.  
Enables efficient training with very large external memories, addressing scalability in both space and time.  
Contains original, technically strong ideas.  
Well-motivated problem with substantial potential impact for applications like language modeling and machine translation.  
Manuscript is clearly written, well-structured, and explains the challenge and solution accessibly.
