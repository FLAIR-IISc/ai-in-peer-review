## Review of Neural Turing Machine with Sparse Read and Write Access

This paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) variant designed to address a key limitation of standard NTMs: their substantial memory and computational requirements, particularly for backpropagation. The authors propose a method employing sparse read and write access, aiming to achieve comparable performance to state-of-the-art NTM implementations while significantly reducing resource demands. Overall, the paper is well-structured and clearly written. 

The core contribution lies in mitigating the prohibitively large memory footprint and computational cost traditionally associated with NTMs, making them more viable for real-world applications. The proposed sparse access mechanism represents a significant step towards practical NTM implementations. 

To enhance the paper’s accessibility, particularly for readers less familiar with memory-augmented neural networks, I recommend including a concise overview of the broader landscape of memory-augmented networks in the background section. While the concepts are likely clear to researchers actively working in this area, a brief explanation of the fundamental principles and motivations behind using external memory in neural networks would be beneficial. Specifically, clarifying the intended function of the write operator – distinguishing it from conventional memory write operations – would improve understanding for a wider audience. Detailing *how* the write operator differs and *why* those differences are important would be particularly helpful.

The experimental results are promising, demonstrating performance close to theoretical bounds. However, the comparison presented in Figure 4 warrants further discussion. The authors rightly justify comparing performance under a fixed physical memory constraint, which is a valuable and defensible approach. However, a complementary comparison utilizing the *same* memory capacity (rather than the same physical memory size) could provide additional insight. While the current approach focuses on efficient resource allocation, a comparison based on identical memory sizes would better illustrate the performance gains achieved by the proposed sparse access method, independent of memory size limitations.  This would reveal how the algorithm performs when given a larger ‘sandbox’ to operate within, potentially highlighting any trade-offs between sparsity and raw capacity.

Despite this suggestion for further analysis, the reported results are compelling. The fact that the algorithm achieves near-optimal performance while addressing a fundamental scalability issue is noteworthy. The paper convincingly demonstrates the potential of sparse access mechanisms for improving the practicality of NTMs. 

**Strengths:**

* Clear and well-structured presentation.
* Addresses a significant limitation of NTMs – scalability.
* Promising experimental results with performance close to theoretical bounds.
* Justifiable experimental design focusing on fixed resource constraints.

**Weaknesses:**

* Limited background information on memory-augmented neural networks for readers unfamiliar with the field.
* Comparison in Figure 4 could benefit from a complementary analysis using identical memory capacity rather than identical physical memory size.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that tackles an important problem in the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The proposed solution is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by compelling experimental results. Addressing the minor suggestions regarding background information and the comparison in Figure 4 would further strengthen the contribution. I recommend acceptance.
