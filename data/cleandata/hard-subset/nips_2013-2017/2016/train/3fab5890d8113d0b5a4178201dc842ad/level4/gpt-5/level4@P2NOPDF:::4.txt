This paper presents an end-to-end differentiable memory access mechanism that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while training far more efficiently with very large external memories. It introduces several original concepts that demonstrate strong technical insight. The problem is well motivated and the proposed solution is thoughtfully designed and executed. Given the importance of scaling memory-augmented neural networks, the potential impact is substantial. Overall, the paper articulates the challenge and its solution clearly, concisely, and effectively.
