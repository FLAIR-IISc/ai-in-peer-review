- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).  
- Reports up to three orders of magnitude improvements in run-time and memory efficiency over existing models.  
- Shows dramatic performance gains in curriculum learning and strong generalization from training on ≤10,000-step sequences to testing on 200,000-step sequences.  
- Claims practical viability of memory-augmented neural networks with potentially far-reaching impact.  
- Main weakness: paper lacks analysis of why the large performance and generalization gains occur—efficiency alone is insufficient explanation.  
- Suggests SAM may provide a powerful inductive bias (analogous to sparsity in Sparse Auto-encoders); authors should discuss this hypothesis even if preliminary.
