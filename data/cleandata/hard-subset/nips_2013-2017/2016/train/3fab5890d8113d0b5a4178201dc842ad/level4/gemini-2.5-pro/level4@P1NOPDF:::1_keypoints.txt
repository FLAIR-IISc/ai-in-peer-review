- Proposes scaling Neural Turing Machines using fast approximate nearest-neighbor structures and top-K selection for attention, allowing larger-scale training without degrading performance.

- Experiments show the approach maintains performance on standard tasks while enabling training on bigger problems.

- Practical engineering implementation is valuable; applying hashing to writable memory is novel, but core novelty is limited given prior work on hashing for memory lookups.

- Recommendation: accept the paper.

- Reproducibility concern: experimental task descriptions lack detail; replication will be difficult without thorough descriptions and release of experimental code.

- Need analysis of limitations/failure modes: clarify why discrete top-K selection (non-differentiable) trained with backprop works here, especially compared to MemNN-WSH and RL-based approaches.

- Request investigation into the effects of K, task properties, and other factors that enable the discrete training procedure to succeed.

- Minor suggestion: consider removing Section 3.6 as tangential to the main contribution.

- Minor suggestion: do not describe Omniglot as "real-world" or "non-synthetic"; revise wording for precision.
