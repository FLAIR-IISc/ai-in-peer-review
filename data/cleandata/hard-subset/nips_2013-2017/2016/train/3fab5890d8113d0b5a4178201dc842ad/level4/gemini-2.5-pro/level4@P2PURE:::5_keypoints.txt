- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).  
- Reports runtime and memory efficiency gains of about three orders of magnitude over existing models.  
- Demonstrates strong improvements in curriculum learning performance.  
- Shows generalization to sequences an order of magnitude longer than training (from 10,000 to 200,000).  
- Claims potential for making memory-augmented networks practically deployable with far-reaching implications.  
- Paper lacks analysis of why SAM yields drastic performance gains, especially for curriculum learning and long-sequence generalization.  
- Suggests SAM might act analogously to sparse auto-encoders, and the authors should provide at least preliminary insight into this mechanism.
