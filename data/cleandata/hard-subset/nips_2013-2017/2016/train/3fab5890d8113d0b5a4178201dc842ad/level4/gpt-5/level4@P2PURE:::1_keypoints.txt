- Contribution: Replace standard NTM attention with fast approximate nearest-neighbor structures and arg-top-K, showing no training degradation and enabling larger-scale problems.  
- Novelty: Conceptually limited due to prior memory-network and hashing work, but applying these techniques to writable memories and the engineering to make it work is a useful contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions addressing major concerns.  
- Reproducibility: Current task specifications are under-described; authors must provide detailed task construction details and commit to releasing code.  
- Limits/failure cases: Paper lacks analysis of when the approach fails and whether it always works; needs experiments or discussion of failure modes.  
- Non-smoothness concern: The argmax/K-argmax steps are discrete/non-smooth and contradict claims of smoothness; this needs explanation and analysis.  
- K=1 issue and prior comparison: With K=1 the model resembles MemNN-WSH (which performed worse than smooth models); authors should clarify if issues arise from K choice, task type, or other factors.  
- Training with discrete actions: The model takes discrete actions but training ignores this (unlike prior work that accounts for discreteness); authors should discuss implications and limits.  
- Minor editorial: Remove section 3.6 and avoid labeling Omniglot as “non-synthetic” or “real-world.”
