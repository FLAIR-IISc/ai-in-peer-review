The paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) with sparse read and write access, addressing a major limitation that has made NTMs impractical for real-world applications: they require prohibitively large physical memory and computational overhead for backpropagation. The proposed architecture aims to mitigate these costs while achieving performance comparable to the state of the art. The paper is well structured and easy to read.

To make the paper more self-contained and accessible to non-experts, I recommend adding a brief overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section, or at least a concise explanation of the memory module. For example, although it may be obvious to researchers in this area, some readers might not be clear on what the write operator is intended to do compared with conventional memories.

I also have a concern about the fairness of the comparison in Figure 4. By choosing memory configurations such that they require the same amount of physical memory, you compare performance under a fixed resource budget, which is a defensible and meaningful choice. However, I believe it would be more informative to also compare models using the same logical memory capacity (i.e., the same memory content/size exposed to the controller), not just the same physical footprint. This would help isolate the effect of sparsity and implementation efficiency from the effect of memory size.

Nevertheless, it is impressive how well the proposed algorithm performs, especially given how close it is to theoretical bounds.
