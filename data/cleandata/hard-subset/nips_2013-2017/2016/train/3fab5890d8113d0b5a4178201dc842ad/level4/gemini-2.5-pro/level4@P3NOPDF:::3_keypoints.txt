- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel sparse memory access scheme for MANNs.  
- Enforces sparsity by restricting reads/writes to a small fixed subset vs dense attention in NTMs.  
- Provides theoretical justification and analysis of the sparse access properties.  
- Empirically demonstrates substantial computational speedups over standard NTM (Section 4.1).  
- Shows lower combined learning and computational costs on algorithmic tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort), notably for associative recall and priority sort (Section 4.2).  
- Finds the sparse inductive bias can act as regularization, improving early-stage learning and easing credit assignment.  
- Validates practical viability with a real-world task (Section 4.4); experiments are thorough and implemented in Torch7.  
- Paper is well-written, assumptions clear, evaluation convincing, and includes detailed architectural appendix.  
- Recommends deeper discussion of when sparsity benefits learning and exploration of interactions with different controller architectures.  
- Overall: a conceptually simple, novel, effective, and valuable contribution to memory-augmented neural networks.
