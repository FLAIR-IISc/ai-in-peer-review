

Most memory-augmented neural network models exhibit poor scalability in both space and time as memory size increases. The authors propose a memory access scheme termed Sparse Access Memory (SAM), claiming it maintains representational power while enabling efficient training—achieving a 1000× speedup and 3000× memory efficiency gain over prior methods. While the experimental results appear interesting, the technical contributions are insufficiently strong for acceptance. The core innovations—efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor search using SAM—are relatively straightforward and lack significant novelty. Consequently, despite the compelling experimental claims, I cannot recommend acceptance.
