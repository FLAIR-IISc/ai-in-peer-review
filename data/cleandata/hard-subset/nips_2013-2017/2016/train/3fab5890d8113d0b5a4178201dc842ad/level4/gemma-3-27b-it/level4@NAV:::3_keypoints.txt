- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Key idea: constrain read/write operations to a fixed-size subset of external memory rather than the whole memory.  
- The authors present the methodology and argue optimality of the sparse access approach.  
- Section 4.1 results suggest improved read/write performance due to reduced memory footprint.  
- Analyzes learning costs of sparsity and evaluates SAM on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort).  
- Shows combined computational/learning costs lower than NTM for certain tasks (Section 4.2).  
- Notes that sparse reads/writes mediated by a linear layer or ANN can improve early-stage learning in some cases.  
- Includes a real-world example (Section 4.4), enhancing practical relevance.  
- Implementation in Torch7; approach is conceptually simple, innovative, and yields impressive speedups versus NTM.  
- Recommends further study of generalizability of learning improvements and testing SAM with alternative ANN architectures.  
- Paper is well-written, assumptions are explicit, architectural details provided in appendix, and overall is a compelling contribution.
