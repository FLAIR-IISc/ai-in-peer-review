This paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) that utilizes sparse read and write access to address a significant limitation hindering the practical application of NTMs. The proposed architecture aims to resolve the issue of excessive physical memory and computational overhead required for backpropagation, while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art models. The manuscript is well-structured and easy to follow.

To make the paper more self-contained, particularly for readers who are not experts in this area, the authors might consider adding a brief overview of memory-augmented neural networks to the background section. For instance, explaining the intended function of the write operator in contrast to conventional memory could enhance clarity for a broader audience.

Furthermore, the experimental comparison in Figure 4, while justifiable, could be complemented. Comparing models constrained by the same amount of physical memory is a valid way to assess performance under limited resources. However, a comparison using identical logical memory sizes might offer more direct insight into the performance characteristics of the proposed access mechanism itself.

Despite these minor points, the results are very compelling. It is impressive to see the algorithm perform so effectively while operating close to theoretical bounds.
