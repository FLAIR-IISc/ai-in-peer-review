- Proposes an efficient method for computing NTM attention using approximate nearest neighbors and arg-top-K operations.  
- Experiments show the method preserves training performance and enables scaling to larger problems.  
- Novelty is limited since similar ideas exist in memory networks, but applying them to writable memories is a noteworthy contribution.  
- Practical implementation details are valuable and worth reporting despite limited conceptual novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept the paper, but require revisions.  
- Major revision: provide detailed descriptions of tasks and experimental setups to enable replication.  
- Require authors to commit to releasing the code for experiments to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Discuss limitations and potential failure cases, especially the non-smooth nature of argmax/K-argmax versus the claimed “smooth” model behavior.  
- Analyze whether suboptimal performance (e.g., with K=1) stems from the choice of K or the task, referencing similarity to MemNN-WSH.  
- Consider implications of training models that make discrete actions and compare to approaches that account for discreteness (e.g., reinforcement-learning-style treatments).  
- Suggest removal of section 3.6 as it adds little value.  
- Advise avoiding terms like “non-synthetic” or “real-world” when describing the Omniglot data to prevent misleading characterization.
