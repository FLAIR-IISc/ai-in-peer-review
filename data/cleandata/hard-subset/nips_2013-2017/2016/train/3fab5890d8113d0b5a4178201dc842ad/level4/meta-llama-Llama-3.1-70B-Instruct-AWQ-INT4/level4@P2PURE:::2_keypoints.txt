- Memory-augmented neural networks scale poorly in both space and time as memory size increases.
- The authors introduce a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM).
- SAM is claimed to maintain representational power while enabling efficient training.
- Reported empirical gains include ~1000× speedup and ~3000× memory efficiency improvements.
- Reviewer believes the paper lacks sufficient technical merit for acceptance.
- Main technical contributions (efficient backpropagation and sparse nearest-neighbor approximation) are viewed as straightforward and lacking complexity.
- Experiments are interesting but not persuasive enough to warrant acceptance.
