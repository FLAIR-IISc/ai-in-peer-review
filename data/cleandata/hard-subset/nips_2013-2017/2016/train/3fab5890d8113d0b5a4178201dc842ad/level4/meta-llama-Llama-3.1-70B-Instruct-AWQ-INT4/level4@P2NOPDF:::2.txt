The proposed neural network models with augmented memory often struggle with scalability, both in terms of space and time, as the memory size increases. The authors introduce a novel memory access scheme, called Sparse Access Memory, which demonstrates the ability to maintain representational power while facilitating efficient training. Notably, this approach achieves a significant speedup of 1000x and a substantial memory reduction of 3000x.

Upon reviewing the paper, I have reservations about its technical merit, which makes me hesitant to recommend acceptance. The primary technical contributions of the paper, namely efficient backpropagation and nearest neighbor approximation using sparse access memory, are somewhat straightforward and lack the complexity and depth typically expected in a NIPS paper. Although the experiments presented appear intriguing, I do not feel that the paper is strong enough to warrant acceptance.
