- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., NTM, Memory Networks).
- Reports dramatic run-time and memory efficiency improvements—about three orders of magnitude over prior models.
- Shows strong performance gains, especially with curriculum learning and generalization to sequences an order of magnitude longer (train up to 10k, test to 200k).
- Claims these improvements could make memory-augmented models practically useful, which would be a major advance for the field.
- Notes that runtime/memory gains alone do not explain the observed improvements in learning and generalization.
- Criticizes the paper for lacking analysis of the underlying mechanisms behind the performance gains.
- Suggests the authors hypothesize/explain whether SAM acts analogously to sparse auto-encoders or other mechanisms, even if preliminary.
