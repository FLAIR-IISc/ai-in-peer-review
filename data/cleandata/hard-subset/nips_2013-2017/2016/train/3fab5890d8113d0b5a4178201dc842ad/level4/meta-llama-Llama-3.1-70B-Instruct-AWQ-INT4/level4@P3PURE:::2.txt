The proposed neural network models augmented with memory typically scale poorly in both space and time as the amount of memory grows. The authors present a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory, demonstrating that it retains the representational power while enabling efficient training, boasting a 1000-fold speedup and 3000-fold memory efficiency. However, I do not believe the paper is technically strong enough for acceptance. The primary technical components, efficient backpropagation and nearest neighbour approximation using sparse access memory, are somewhat straightforward and lack the complexity and innovation typically expected in a top-tier conference. Although the experiments appear interesting, I am not in favor of acceptance.
