Most proposed neural network models, when augmented with memory, scale poorly in both space and time as the amount of memory increases. The authors have presented a memory access scheme called sparse access memory, and shown that it maintains representation capacity while training efficiently, being 1000 times faster and 3000 times more memory efficient. I don't think this paper is technically strong enough for acceptance. Efficient backpropagation and approximation using sparse access memory form the main technical part, but are quite straightforward. Therefore, even though the experiments seem interesting, I am not in favor of acceptance.
