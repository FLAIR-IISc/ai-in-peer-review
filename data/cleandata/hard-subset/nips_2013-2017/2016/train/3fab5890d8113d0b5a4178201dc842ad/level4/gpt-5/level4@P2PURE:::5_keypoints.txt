- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for neural networks with external memory (e.g., NTMs, Memory Networks).  
- Reports ~three orders of magnitude improvements in run-time and memory efficiency over prior memory-augmented models.  
- Demonstrates substantial gains in curriculum learning performance.  
- Enables generalization to sequences far longer than training (trained to ~10k, tested to ~200k).  
- Claims the work could make memory-augmented networks practical with wide implications.  
- Main omission: lacks analysis of why curriculum learning and extreme generalization improve so dramatically.  
- Notes that efficiency gains alone don’t explain the effects; suggests SAM might act analogously to sparse autoencoders.  
- Recommends authors include preliminary hypotheses or discussion of the underlying mechanisms.
