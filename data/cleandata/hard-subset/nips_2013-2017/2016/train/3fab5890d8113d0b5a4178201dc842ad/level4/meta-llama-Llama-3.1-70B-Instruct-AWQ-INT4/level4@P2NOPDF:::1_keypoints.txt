- Proposal: use fast approximate-nearest-neighbors and arg-top-K to compute attention weights in Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) efficiently.
- Experimental finding: approach preserves training performance and enables training on larger problems.
- Novelty: limited, since similar ideas appear in prior memory network work (e.g., Sukhbaatar et al. section 3.3).
- Contribution: notable application of these techniques to writable memories.
- Recommendation: paper is worth accepting based on practical utility and experiments.
- Replication request: provide detailed task descriptions (no standard NTM tasks/code) and commit to releasing experiment code.
- Limitations: discuss failure cases and limitations, especially effects of non-smooth argmax/K-argmax operations on training.
- Comparative analysis: investigate why prior similar methods (e.g., MemNN-WSH) underperformed — role of K, task differences, or other factors.
- Discrete actions: consider implications of training with discrete selection operations and whether discrete-action-aware methods are needed.
- Editorial changes: remove section 3.6 as it adds little value.
- Terminology: avoid calling Omniglot "non-synthetic" or "real-world" to prevent misleading descriptions.
