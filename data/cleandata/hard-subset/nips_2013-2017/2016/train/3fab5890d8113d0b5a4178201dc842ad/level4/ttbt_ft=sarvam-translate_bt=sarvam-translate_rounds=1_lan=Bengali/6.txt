This paper presents a Neural Turing Machine (MTN) with sparse read and write access. This is one of the main problems with MTNs, making them unsuitable for real-world applications. The problem is that MTNs require extremely large amounts of physical memory and operations to backprop them. The architecture presented in this paper aims to solve this problem while maintaining performance comparable to state-of-the-art technology. The paper is well-structured and easy to read. If a brief overview of memory augmented neural networks is provided in the background section, or an explanation of memory is provided to make the paper more self-contained, it will help readers who are not experts in the field (although this may be obvious to those who have worked in the field, some readers may not be clear what the write operator should ideally do compared to conventional memory). Also, the comparison in Figure 4 may not be fair. By selecting the memories in such a way that they require the same amount of physical memory, you can compare performance using limited resources (which you have done and is certainly fair!), but I think it is more informative about performance to use the same memory and not to use the same physical memory. Nevertheless, it is very interesting to see how well this algorithm performs from very close to the theoretical limit.
