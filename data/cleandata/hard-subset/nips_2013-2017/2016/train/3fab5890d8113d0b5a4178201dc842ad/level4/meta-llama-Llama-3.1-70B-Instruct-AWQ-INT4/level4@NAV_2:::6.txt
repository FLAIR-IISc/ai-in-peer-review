The paper "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes" presents a novel approach to scaling memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) by introducing sparse read and write operations. The authors propose a new neural memory architecture, called Sparse Access Memory (SAM), which achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity while retaining the representational power of the original approaches.

The main idea of the paper is to constrain the write and read operations to a sparse subset of the memory words, allowing for efficient computation and memory usage. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through various experiments, including speed and memory benchmarks, learning with sparse memory access, scaling with a curriculum, and question answering on the Babi tasks.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the background and related work. However, it would be beneficial to include a brief description of MANNs and the memory architecture in the background section to make the paper more self-contained for readers who are not experts in the field.

One potential issue with the paper is the comparison in Figure 4, where the authors compare the performance of different models with the same physical memory usage. While this comparison is informative, it would be more insightful to compare the performance of the models with the same memory size, as this would provide a more direct measure of the effectiveness of the sparse access memory.
