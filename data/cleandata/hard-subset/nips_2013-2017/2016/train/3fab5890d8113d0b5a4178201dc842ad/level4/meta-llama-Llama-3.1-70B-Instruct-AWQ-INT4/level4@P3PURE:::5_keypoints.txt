- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).  
- Demonstrates ~three-order-of-magnitude improvements in runtime and memory efficiency over prior memory-augmented models.  
- Shows large performance gains in curriculum learning and generalization to much longer sequences (training up to 10,000, testing up to 200,000).  
- Claims these results could be groundbreaking and move memory-augmented networks toward practical applicability.  
- Notes the field is active but previous methods lacked practical utility; SAM may change that.  
- Criticizes the paper for lacking analysis of why curriculum and extreme-length generalization improvements occur—efficiency alone is insufficient explanation.  
- Suggests a preliminary intuition that SAM may play a role analogous to sparse auto-encoders and requests the authors’ thoughts, even if preliminary.
