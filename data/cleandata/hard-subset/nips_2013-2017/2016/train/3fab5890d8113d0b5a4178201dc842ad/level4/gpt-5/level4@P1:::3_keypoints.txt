- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads/writes to external memory with ANN indices and efficient BPTT rollback, achieving O(log N) time and O(1) per-step space.
- SAM decouples parameter count from memory size, using a fixed small number of memory slots per step independent of total memory.
- Content-based reads use ANN (randomized k-d trees or LSH); writes target read locations or the least-recently-used (LRU) slot.
- Efficient backprop stores only sparse updates and rolls back memory, giving O(T) total memory for BPTT.
- Theoretical claims of asymptotic optimality under reasonable assumptions.
- Empirical speed/memory gains are dramatic (up to ~1000–1600× speedups and large memory reductions vs NTM).
- Matches or outperforms dense baselines on canonical NTM tasks, notably better on associative recall and priority sort.
- Scales via curriculum to millions of memory slots and >4000 time steps; generalizes to much longer sequences than seen in training.
- Competitive bAbI performance without supervised memory retrieval; SDNC variant achieves best reported unsupervised bAbI result known to authors.
- Strong one-shot learning on Omniglot, plausibly due to larger memory capacity.
- Builds on NTMs/Memory Networks and discretized-address RL work but remains fully differentiable; SDNC extension shows generality.
- Technical contribution is clear and practical; integrating ANN indices inside the network is well motivated.
- Clarity: assumptions, architecture, and data structures are well explained; appendices are substantive.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of when sparsity helps or hurts learning; needs ablations over K and access thresholds.
- Weakness: ANN design choices and trade-offs (accuracy vs speed, rebuild frequency, online stability) are underexplored.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation on large-scale real-world sequence tasks (e.g., language modeling, MT) beyond Omniglot and bAbI.
- Weakness: LRU write policy needs more analysis under heavy reuse or rapidly changing contexts and comparison to alternatives.
- Weakness: Reproducibility could be improved with explicit hyperparameter sensitivities (access thresholds, ANN rebuild cadence).
- Assessment — Quality: Methodologically sound with solid theoretical and empirical support; some analyses should be expanded.
- Assessment — Clarity: Generally well written; minor inconsistencies in section references.
- Assessment — Originality: Novel combination of sparse memory access, ANN addressing, and efficient backward pass.
- Assessment — Significance: Addresses a key scaling bottleneck in MANNs and is likely to influence future designs.
- Pros for acceptance: strong conceptual/practical advance, impressive empirical gains, comprehensive experiments, generalizable approach.
- Cons against acceptance: limited analysis of sparsity/hyperparameter sensitivity, incomplete ANN online exploration, lack of large-scale real-world tasks.
- Suggestions: add theoretical/empirical conditions for sparsity benefits and ablations on K/thresholds; evaluate alternative ANN methods and online behavior; include larger real-world sequence experiments and more implementation details.
- Overall recommendation: Well-executed, novel, and impactful paper — recommend acceptance.
