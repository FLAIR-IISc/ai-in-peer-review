- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks (e.g., Neural Turing Machines, Memory Networks).  
- Reports ~three orders of magnitude (~1000x) improvements in runtime and memory efficiency over prior memory-augmented models.  
- Demonstrates significant gains in curriculum learning performance.  
- Shows strong generalization to sequences far longer than training (training up to ~10k → testing up to ~200k).  
- Claims these advances could make memory-augmented networks practical for real-world use—a potentially groundbreaking contribution.  
- Primary reviewer question: why do curriculum and long-sequence generalization improvements occur beyond mere efficiency gains?  
- Reviewer intuition: SAM may act analogously to sparse autoencoders; requests authors' insights or analysis to explain the mechanism.
