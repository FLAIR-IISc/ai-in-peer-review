- Proposal: Use ANN + arg top-K for NTM attention to produce sparse reads/writes, reducing compute and memory while preserving training quality and scaling to larger problems.

- Novelty: Conceptually incremental (prior memory-networks used hashing/ANN) but novel and nontrivial in applying ANN and hard top-K to a writeable external memory.

- Strengths: Clear motivation (dense attention bottleneck), useful engineering for scalable reads/writes, and empirical evidence that training quality is maintained and larger instances become feasible.

- Major concern 1 — Reproducibility/task specification: Tasks and data-generation are underspecified; provide exact task specs, full training details (architecture, memory size, K, ANN parameters, optimizer/hyperparams, seeds), and release code/data/scripts.

- Major concern 2 — Limits and failure modes: Argmax/top-K is non-smooth—need analysis of when discrete selection succeeds/fails (vary K, compare to soft/relaxed methods, exact vs approximate neighbors), include negative/stress tests, and discuss task properties enabling success; contrast with methods that handle discrete choices during training.

- Major concern 3 — Read/write mechanics with ANN: Clarify key definition/updating for written slots, index rebuild/update frequency and amortized cost, handling of stale keys after gradient updates, read/write complexity/latency, and collision/tie-breaking/gradient routing for near-duplicates.

- Major concern 4 — Evaluation breadth and fairness: Compare to dense differentiable baselines under matched compute/memory, report wall-clock timing/peak memory/scaling curves, and run ablations isolating sparsity vs approximation (dense exact, dense approximate, sparse exact, sparse approximate).

- Major concern 5 — Clarity and presentation: Add concise algorithmic pseudocode for sparse attention (lookup, top-K, gradient handling, index maintenance), precisely define symbols/operations (e.g., “arg top-K”), and state whether surrogate/straight-through gradients are used.

- Major concern 6 — Dataset terminology and scope: Don’t label Omniglot “non-synthetic/real-world”; use neutral terms (e.g., “image-based benchmark”); consider removing Section 3.6 as low value.

- Minor comments: Expand related work on large-memory retrieval with hashing/ANN and clarify read-only vs writeable memory contributions; provide intuition for robustness of discrete writes (e.g., reduced interference); include hyperparameter and seed sensitivity analyses.

- Recommendation: Accept conditional on substantial revisions improving reproducibility (code release, exact task/train details) and deeper analysis of failure modes, K/ANN parameter ablations, and fair baseline comparisons.
