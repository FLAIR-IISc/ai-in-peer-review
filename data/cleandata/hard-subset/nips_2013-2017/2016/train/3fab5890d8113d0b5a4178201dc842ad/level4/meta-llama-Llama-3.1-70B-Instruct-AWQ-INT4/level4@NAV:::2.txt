The majority of existing neural network models that incorporate memory suffer from significant scalability issues, both in terms of space and time complexity, as the memory size increases. The authors introduce a novel memory access scheme, termed Sparse Access Memory, which purportedly maintains the representational capacity of these models while facilitating efficient training. Specifically, they demonstrate a substantial improvement in training speed (1000x) and memory efficiency (3000x). However, upon careful examination, I have reservations regarding the technical merit of this paper, which leads me to lean against acceptance. The primary technical contributions, namely efficient backpropagation and nearest neighbor approximation using sparse access memory, are somewhat straightforward and lack the sophistication typically expected in a paper of this caliber. Although the experimental results appear intriguing, they do not sufficiently compensate for the paper's overall technical shortcomings.
