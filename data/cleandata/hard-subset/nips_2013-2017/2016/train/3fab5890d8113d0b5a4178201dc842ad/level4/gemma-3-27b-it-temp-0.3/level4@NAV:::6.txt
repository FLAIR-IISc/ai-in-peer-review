This paper introduces a Neural Turing Machine (NTM) variant, termed the MTN, designed to address a key limitation of NTMs – their substantial memory and computational requirements for backpropagation, hindering practical application. The proposed architecture aims to mitigate this issue while maintaining performance competitive with current state-of-the-art methods. The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. To enhance accessibility for readers less familiar with the field, a concise overview of memory-augmented neural networks in the background section, or a more detailed explanation of the memory mechanisms (particularly clarifying the function of the write operator relative to conventional memory), would be beneficial. Regarding the comparison presented in Figure 4, while the chosen methodology of equating physical memory usage provides a valid assessment under resource constraints, presenting results with identical memory sizes (rather than identical physical memory allocations) could offer a more insightful comparison of performance characteristics. Despite this point, the algorithm’s strong performance, approaching theoretical limits, is highly encouraging and represents a significant contribution.
