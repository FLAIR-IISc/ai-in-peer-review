This paper presents an end-to-end differentiable memory access mechanism that retains the representational power of the original Neural Turing Machine (NTM) while being far more efficient to train with very large memories. It introduces several original concepts that demonstrate strong technical ingenuity. The problem it addresses is well motivated and effectively tackled. Given the substantial importance of scaling memory systems for neural networks, the paper articulates the challenge and its solution in a clear, concise, and well-explained manner.
