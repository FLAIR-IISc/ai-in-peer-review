- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): constrain reads/writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words in memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Theoretical results: authors provide rigorous proofs of the methodology and claim optimality for reduced-size read/write operations.  
- Empirical performance: SAM yields good read/write performance and significant speedup compared to Neural Turing Machines (NTM).  
- Task evaluation: on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort) combined learning costs are lower than NTM for some tasks.  
- Early learning benefit: sparse reads/writes (using linear or ANN indices) can improve early-stage learning in some cases.  
- Implementation and examples: Torch7 implementation and inclusion of real-world examples demonstrate applicability.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear assumptions and architecture details provided in the appendix.  
- Strengths summarized: novel and straightforward idea, significant speedup, rigorous analysis, and empirical investigation of sparsity costs/benefits.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited exploration of generalizing findings to other cases or ANN methods; some sections (notably 4.2) need more clarity/expansion.  
- Recommendations: authors should explore different ANN approaches with SAM, expand section 4.2, and add more real-world examples.  
- Verdict: recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
