- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory-augmented neural networks.  
- Claims up to three orders of magnitude improvements in runtime and memory efficiency.  
- Demonstrates strong performance in curriculum learning and generalization to sequences far longer than training (e.g., 10k → 200k).  
- Suggests SAM may make memory-augmented networks practical with far-reaching implications.  
- Paper is well-written and balances technical detail with high-level intuition.  
- Clear explanation of SAM and its benefits, making it accessible.  
- Impressive experimental results across synthetic tasks, one-shot Omniglot, and bAbI QA.  
- Strength: thorough analysis and demonstration of generalization to new tasks/settings.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of why SAM yields drastic curriculum-learning and long-sequence generalization gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but request additional experiments/analysis or discussion to explain underlying mechanisms behind the improvements.
