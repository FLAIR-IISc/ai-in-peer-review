- Problem: Memory-augmented neural networks scale poorly in time and space as memory size grows.  
- Proposal: Authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM) for memory access.  
- Claim: SAM preserves representational power while enabling efficient training.  
- Reported results: Authors report up to 1000× speedups and 3000× improvements in memory efficiency.  
- Technical assessment: Core components (efficient backpropagation scheme and approximate nearest-neighbor mechanism) are relatively straightforward.  
- Verdict: Experimental results are interesting, but methods lack sufficient novelty/depth; reviewer does not support acceptance.
