- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- Constrains all reads and writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words instead of unbounded memory.
- Provides rigorous proofs and claims the methodology is optimal.
- Shows improved read/write performance due to reduced active memory (section 4.1).
- Evaluates learning costs of sparsity (section 4.2) on standard NTM tasks (copy, associative recall, priority sort).
- Reports combined costs lower than NTM for certain tasks (notably tasks 2 and 3).
- Demonstrates substantial speedup compared to NTM with real-world benchmarks implemented in Torch7 (section 4.4).
- Finds sparse reads/writes using linear or ANN approaches can benefit early-stage learning in some cases.
- Recommends extending SAM to other scenarios and exploring different ANN methods to leverage it.
- Paper is well-written with clear assumptions and a detailed architecture presented in the appendix.
