The paper introduces a Sparse Access Memory architecture for neural nets augmented with memory, such as Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks. This innovation achieves remarkable run-time and memory efficiency improvements, with a three-order-of-magnitude reduction compared to existing neural nets augmented with memory. Furthermore, this architecture appears to enable significant performance improvements in the setting of curriculum learning and in the ability to generalize to sequences that are an order of magnitude longer than those observed in training, from lengths of up to 10,000 in training to sequences of length 200,000 in testing. These results have the potential to be groundbreaking.

The field of neural networks augmented with memory, initiated by Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, is actively developing. Although previous research in this area has yielded interesting results, these advancements have not yet reached a level of practical applicability. However, the drastic efficiency and performance improvements reported in the present paper suggest that memory-augmented neural networks are now poised for practical use. This development is likely to have far-reaching implications.

One aspect that is missing from the present paper is a discussion of the reasons behind the drastic performance improvements observed in curriculum learning and in the ability to predict sequences with lengths far beyond the training range. While run-time and memory efficiency cannot account for these improvements, it is clear that other factors are at play. A preliminary intuition is that the Sparse Memory Access architecture may play a similar role to that of Sparse Auto-encoders in other situations. The authors' thoughts on this topic, even if preliminary, would be valuable and enlighten readers.
