- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse reads/writes to external memory using ANN for content-based addressing, with in-place sparse write logging and rollback for BPTT.
- Claims asymptotically optimal time/space: O(log N) addressing and O(1) per-step space via sparse logging under reasonable assumptions.
- Demonstrates large empirical speed and memory savings over dense models (NTM, DAM) and scalability to millions of slots and >100k-step sequences.
- Shows comparable data efficiency and accuracy to dense models on synthetic algorithmic tasks, bAbi QA, and Omniglot one-shot classification.
- Extends approach to DNC (Sparse DNC) with strong bAbi results.
- Clearly situates work relative to NTMs/DNC, RL-based discrete addressing, and dense Memory Networks; aligns with LRU-style allocation concepts.
- Technical contributions are sound; sparse write + rollback is an elegant solution to O(NT) memory bottleneck.
- Integrating ANN indices into training is novel; gradients bypass the index and hard top-K induces zero gradients for non-selected rows but remains empirically trainable.
- Empirical claims are largely substantiated with speed/memory benchmarks, scaling experiments, and comparisons to dense baselines.
- Experimental analysis could be deeper on sensitivity to K, ANN recall errors vs exact KNN, and training stability/variance across seeds.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; key mechanisms and complexity claims are clearly described.
- A compact pseudo-code summary of forward/backward memory update and rollback in the main text would improve readability.
- Some hyperparameters, ANN configuration, and rebuild policies are deferred to the supplement, hampering reproducibility.
- Combining sparse read/write, efficient backpropagation, and ANN indices in an end-to-end MANN is novel and impactful.
- Work addresses a major scalability bottleneck for MANNs enabling previously infeasible regimes (millions of slots, multi-thousand-step BPTT).
- Strengths: principled approach, asymptotically optimal complexity, 100x–1600x speedups, dramatic memory reductions, and successful scaling/generalization.
- Strengths: applicable to multiple memory architectures, simple constant-time allocation policy, and good bAbi performance without supervised retrieval.
- Weakness: hard top-K causes biased gradients; theoretical implications for convergence are not fully addressed and annealing/soft-to-hard schemes are not discussed.
- Weakness: limited ablations on ANN choices (FLANN vs LSH), rebuild frequency, and impact of ANN recall rate.
- Weakness: allocation policy simplicity raises questions about temporal-chaining tasks compared to DNC’s allocation and temporal links; SDNC details are mostly in the supplement.
- Weakness: fairness of baselines in scaling experiments needs discussion (capacity vs sparsity effects).
- Weakness: robustness/variance across random seeds and index non-stationarity effects are not fully reported.
- Minor suggestions: include pseudo-code for updates/rollback, report K sensitivity and heuristics, clarify multi-head cost scaling, document usage-tracking thresholds, and provide per-step amortized complexity including rebuilds.
- Recommend releasing code and minimal examples to aid reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — substantial, timely contribution with strong empirical benefits despite some missing robustness/theoretical analyses.
