This paper introduces a novel neural memory architecture called Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that scales memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) efficiently. The authors propose a sparse read and write scheme that uses efficient data structures to reduce computational and memory overhead. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, architecture, and experimental results.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of SAM, which addresses the scalability issue of MANNs. The authors demonstrate that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity and show that it can be trained efficiently with very large memories. The experimental results show that SAM outperforms existing models in terms of speed and memory usage, while maintaining comparable data efficiency and performance.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the SAM architecture, including the sparse read and write operations, and the efficient backpropagation through time algorithm. The experimental results are also well-presented, with clear comparisons to existing models.

The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical and easy to follow. The authors provide sufficient background information on MANNs and the motivation for proposing SAM. The figures and tables are well-presented and help to illustrate the key points.
