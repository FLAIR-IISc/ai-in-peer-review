- Proposal: Sparse Access Memory (SAM) — differentiable memory with sparse top-K reads via ANN and writes limited to read locations + least-recently-accessed (LRA), plus O(1)-per-timestep rollback for BPTT.  
- Systems contribution: Integrates ANN indices (e.g., randomized k-d trees, LSH) into the training loop for sublinear content lookup.  
- Rollback: Stores and reverts only sparse modifications to enable O(1) per-step backprop memory.  
- Theoretical claims: Argues asymptotic time/space optimality under content-based addressing, supported by algorithms and supplements.  
- Empirical speed/memory: Reports 100–1600× runtime speedups and dramatic memory reductions vs dense NTMs at large N.  
- Task performance: Matches or exceeds dense baselines on synthetic algorithmic tasks, curriculum scaling, bAbI QA, and Omniglot one-shot.  
- SDNC result: Sparse DNC (SDNC) achieves near state-of-the-art on bAbI without supervised memory access.  
- Technical soundness: Read/write scheme, LRA allocation, and rollback BPTT are well specified; ANN treated as a fixed index with gradients through selected rows.  
- Scaling evidence: Curriculum experiments show >4000 BPTT steps and generalization to 200k steps (associative recall).  
- Ablation needs: Missing sensitivity analyses for K, exact vs approximate nearest neighbor, write-policy interpolation, and thresholding (w>ε).  
- ANN error impact: Lack of deep analysis on how ANN recall errors affect gradients and learning stability.  
- Fairness concerns: Dense baselines use far fewer memory slots (e.g., 64) vs SAM’s millions; need accuracy vs wall-clock under matched compute/memory budgets and accuracy vs memory-size curves.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; equations, usage stats, and rollback described precisely.  
- Reproducibility suggestions: Provide concise pseudocode for forward pass, write policy, index maintenance, and rollback.  
- Implementation details requested: Specify ANN rebuild policy (frequency/amortized cost), batching/GPU considerations, and assumptions behind asymptotic optimality with an intuitive proof sketch.  
- Originality: Novel combination of differentiable sparse access, O(1) rollback BPTT, and ANN integration into the training loop.  
- Significance: Enables end-to-end training of MANNs with million-scale memories and long contexts — relevant to QA, language modeling, and one-shot learning.  
- Pros condensed: Tackles an important scaling problem with elegant, practical algorithms, solid asymptotic/empirical gains, and broad empirical validation.  
- Cons condensed: Limited ablations, potential gradient mismatch from approximate search, resource-comparison fairness issues, and narrow real-world evaluation.  
- Essential fixes: Add ablations (K, exact vs ANN, write policies), report ANN recall@K and its correlation with training, detail ANN maintenance and batching, and include resource-normalized comparisons.  
- Optional extensions: Try learned allocation/erase policies, broader real-data experiments (large QA / long-context LM), and release code/implementation notes.  
- Verdict: Strong, well-executed, and impactful contribution suitable for NIPS, pending additional ablations and clearer resource-normalized comparisons.
