- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a sparse read/write neural memory architecture to scale MANNs to large external memories.  
- SAM’s sparse access scheme yields asymptotically optimal space and time complexity for large-scale memory use.  
- Builds on Neural Turing Machines and Memory Networks, addressing their smooth read/write overhead and using ideas from approximate nearest neighbor search.  
- Novel architecture specifically designed to handle large external memories efficiently.  
- Demonstrates efficient computation and claims to achieve asymptotic lower bounds in complexity.  
- Presents extensive experiments: speed and memory benchmarks, learning curves, and comparisons with other models.  
- Provides theoretical analysis, including proofs of SAM’s optimality and efficiency.  
- Theoretical guarantees are limited and depend on specific assumptions about memory access patterns.  
- Evaluation is primarily on synthetic tasks and one-shot learning, lacking extensive real-world application tests.  
- Comparisons to other MANNs are included but not comprehensive across all existing models.  
- Paper has potential impact for NLP, computer vision, and robotics applications.  
- The manuscript is technically sound, clearly written, and provides a thorough analysis.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for novelty, soundness, and impact potential, while urging more real-world experiments and broader comparisons.
