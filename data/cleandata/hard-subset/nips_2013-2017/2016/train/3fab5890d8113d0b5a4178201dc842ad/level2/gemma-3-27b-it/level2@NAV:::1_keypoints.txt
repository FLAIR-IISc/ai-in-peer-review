- Addresses scalability bottleneck of memory-augmented neural networks (quadratic time/space) by proposing Sparse Access Memory (SAM).
- SAM sparsifies reads and writes to a subset of memory locations, achieving asymptotic lower bounds in time and space complexity.
- Empirical gains: reported ~1,000x speedup and ~3,000x memory reduction versus dense models.
- Maintains comparable data efficiency on synthetic NTM tasks and one-shot Omniglot recognition while scaling to much larger sequences and memory.
- Extends SAM to a sparse Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC) and achieves state-of-the-art results on the bAbI tasks, demonstrating generality.
- Provides rigorous analysis of time and space complexity and discusses approximate nearest neighbor search and efficient backpropagation.
- Core idea is well-motivated and effectively implemented, directly addressing a key practical limitation of MANNs.
- Limited exploration of the sparsity hyperparameter K (only K=4 or 8 mentioned) and its impact on performance/task complexity.
- Comparisons to other scalability approaches (e.g., quantization, pruning) are lacking and would contextualize the contribution further.
- Presentation in Section 3 is dense and would benefit from clearer exposition and visual aids.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant contributions to MANN scalability; recommend acceptance.
