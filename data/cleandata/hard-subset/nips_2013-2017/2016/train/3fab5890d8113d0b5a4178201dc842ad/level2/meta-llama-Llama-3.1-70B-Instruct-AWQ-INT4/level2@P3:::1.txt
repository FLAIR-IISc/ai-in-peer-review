**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a novel neural memory architecture called Sparse Access Memory (SAM), which is designed to scale memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) to large external memories. The key innovation of SAM is its sparse read and write scheme, which allows for efficient computation and memory usage. The authors demonstrate that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, making it optimal for large-scale memory applications.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors build upon previous work on MANNs, such as Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) and Memory Networks. They address the limitations of these models, which suffer from high computational and memory overhead due to their smooth read and write operations. The authors also draw inspiration from work on approximate nearest neighbor search and efficient data structures.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The paper introduces a new neural memory architecture, SAM, which is designed to scale MANNs to large external memories.
2. **Efficient Computation**: The authors demonstrate that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, making it optimal for large-scale memory applications.
3. **Experimental Results**: The paper presents extensive experimental results, including speed and memory benchmarks, learning curves, and comparisons with other models.
4. **Theoretical Analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of SAM, including proofs of its optimality and efficiency.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited Theoretical Guarantees**: While the authors provide some theoretical guarantees, they acknowledge that their analysis is limited to specific assumptions about the memory access patterns.
2. **Lack of Real-World Applications**: The paper primarily focuses on synthetic tasks and one-shot learning, which may not be representative of real-world applications.
3. **Comparison to Other Models**: While the authors compare SAM to other models, they do not provide a comprehensive comparison to all existing MANNs.

**Originality and Significance**

1. **Novel Architecture**: SAM is a novel architecture that addresses the limitations of existing MANNs.
2. **Efficient Computation**: The paper demonstrates that SAM achieves asymptotic lower bounds in space and time complexity, making it a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Potential Impact**: The paper has the potential to impact various applications, including natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics.

**Quality of the Paper**

1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper is technically sound, with a thorough analysis of SAM and its properties.
2. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the architecture and experimental results.
3. **Completeness**: The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of SAM, including theoretical and experimental results.
