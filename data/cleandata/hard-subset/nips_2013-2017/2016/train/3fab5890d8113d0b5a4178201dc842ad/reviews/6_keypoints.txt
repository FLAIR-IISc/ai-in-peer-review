- Proposes a Neural Turing/Machine (MTN) with sparse read and write to reduce the huge physical memory and backprop operations that make MTNs impractical.  
- Claims to solve the scalability issue while achieving performance comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well structured and easy to read.  
- Suggests adding a brief background on memory-augmented neural networks or a clearer explanation of the memory and write operator to make the paper more self-contained.  
- Raises concern that the comparison in Figure 4 may be unfair—matching physical memory sizes is one choice, but comparing using the same logical memory (not the same physical memory) would be more informative.  
- Notes the results are interesting and the algorithm performs close to theoretical bounds.
