- Memory-augmented neural networks typically scale poorly in space and time as memory grows.  
- The paper introduces a memory access scheme called Sparse Access Memory (SAM).  
- Authors claim SAM retains representational power while being much more efficient (≈1000× faster, ≈3000× memory efficient).  
- The reviewer finds the technical contribution insufficient for acceptance.  
- Main technical components (efficient backpropagation and approximate nearest neighbor via sparse access) are viewed as straightforward.  
- Experiments are interesting, but do not outweigh the perceived lack of technical novelty.
