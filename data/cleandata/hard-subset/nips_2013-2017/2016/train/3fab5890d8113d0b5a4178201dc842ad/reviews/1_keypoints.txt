- Uses fast approximate nearest-neighbors and arg-top-K for attention in an NTM, enabling training on larger problems without degrading performance.
- Novelty is thin; related work (e.g., Memory Networks section 3.3) and other papers already use large memories with hashing for scalable lookups.
- Practical contribution: applying these tools to writeable memories is less explored and the engineering/practical results are worth reporting.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but requests revisions and clarifications.
- Replicability concern: task descriptions are insufficient and authors must provide careful task construction details and release experiment code.
- Request analysis of failure cases and limits: when does the method fail, does it always work, and under what task/setup conditions.
- Concern about non-smoothness of argmax/K-argmax: need discussion of effects (e.g., K=1) and comparison to fully smooth models and MemNN-WSH behavior.
- Note that training makes discrete actions without accounting for discreteness (unlike some prior work); discuss implications and limits.
- Suggest removing section 3.6.
- Suggest not referring to Omniglot as "non-synthetic" or "real-world."
