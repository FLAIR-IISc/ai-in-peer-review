- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM), a novel memory access scheme for memory-augmented neural networks.
- Builds on recent work like Neural Turing Machines, enabling learning beyond traditional LSTM limits.
- Main contribution: constrains reads and writes to a fixed-size subset of memory words rather than unlimited memory.
- Authors provide theoretical justification and claim optimality of the methodology.
- Empirically achieves improved read/write performance due to reduced memory size (Section 4.1).
- Evaluates learning costs of introducing sparsity (Section 4.2) using standard NTM tasks: copy, associative recall, and priority sort.
- Combined costs are lower than NTM for some tasks (associative recall and priority sort).
- Includes a real-world example (Section 4.4) and benchmarks implemented in Torch7.
- The idea is straightforward, novel, and yields significant speedups compared to NTM.
- Sparse reads/writes via linear models or ANNs can benefit early-stage learning in some cases (Section 4.2).
- Reviewer suggests authors should elaborate on when sparsity improves learning and explore other ANN integrations with SAM.
- Overall assessment: well written, clear assumptions, architecture details in appendix, and an interesting read.
