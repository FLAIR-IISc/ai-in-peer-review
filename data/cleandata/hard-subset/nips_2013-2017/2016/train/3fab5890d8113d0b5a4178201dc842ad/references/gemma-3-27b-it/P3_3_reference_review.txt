## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and technically sound approach to addressing a critical bottleneck in Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs) – the computational and memory cost associated with large external memories. The authors introduce SAM, a MANN with sparse read and write operations, demonstrating significant improvements in both speed and memory efficiency without sacrificing performance. The results across a range of tasks, including scaling to longer sequences and achieving state-of-the-art performance on the Babi tasks with the SDNC extension, are highly encouraging.  I believe this is a strong submission deserving of acceptance.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles a well-recognized limitation of MANNs – their quadratic scaling in parameter count with memory size, hindering their application to problems requiring large, long-term memories.  Prior work like NTMs and Memory Networks have decoupled capacity from parameters, but still suffer from linear overhead per memory location during training due to full memory duplication for BPTT. SAM elegantly addresses this by restricting memory access to a sparse subset, leveraging efficient data structures (ANNs & CSR) for content-based reads.  The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of MANNs, explicitly acknowledging the limitations of existing approaches and clearly differentiating SAM through its focus on sparsity and efficiency. The connection to recent work like the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC) is also well-established, and the extension to a Sparse DNC (SDNC) further demonstrates the generality of their approach.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Efficiency Gains:** The reported speedups (up to three orders of magnitude) and memory reductions are substantial and directly address a major practical obstacle for MANNs. The benchmarks are convincing and clearly demonstrate the benefits of sparsity.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors briefly outline the asymptotic optimality in space and time complexity of SAM, providing a solid theoretical foundation for their approach (further details in the supplementary material).
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The results across diverse tasks (algorithmic tasks, Babi reasoning, Omniglot one-shot learning) are impressive. The ability to scale to longer sequences via curriculum learning is particularly noteworthy, showcasing SAM's potential for tackling more complex problems.
* **Generality and Extensibility:** The successful adaptation of SAM to the DNC architecture (SDNC) demonstrates the broader applicability of the proposed sparse access mechanism.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core concepts of SAM. The supplementary material provides valuable details on implementation and experimental setup.
* **Novelty:**  While sparsity isn’t entirely new, its application *specifically* to MANNs with a focus on efficient BPTT and utilizing ANNs in this context is a significant contribution.



**3. Weaknesses:**

* **ANN Choice & Sensitivity:**  The paper mentions using both FLANN and LSH for approximate nearest neighbor search but doesn’t provide a comprehensive comparison of their performance across different tasks and memory sizes.  A more in-depth analysis of the impact of ANN choice on both speed and accuracy would be beneficial. The selection of ANN parameters (e.g., tree depth for FLANN, hash table size for LSH) is also not fully detailed.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While hyperparameters are listed in the supplementary material, a discussion of their sensitivity and the rationale behind the chosen values would strengthen the paper.  Were extensive hyperparameter sweeps performed, or were default values used for certain parameters?
* **Limited Ablation Study:** While results are shown with and without sparsity, a more granular ablation study exploring the contribution of each component (sparse reads *vs.* sparse writes, ANN *vs.* linear search) would provide deeper insights into the effectiveness of SAM.  For example, how much performance is lost by approximating the nearest neighbors?
* **Controller Complexity:** While the LSTM controller is standard, it’s not clear how the controller's complexity scales with memory size. Could a simpler controller suffice with the reduced computational burden of sparse access?


**4. Originality & Significance:**

The work is highly original in its application of sparse access mechanisms to MANNs. While the *idea* of sparsity isn't new, the specific implementation details, coupled with the efficient BPTT strategy and the integration of ANNs, represent a significant advancement. The significance of this work is substantial. Scaling MANNs is crucial for tackling increasingly complex tasks requiring long-term memory. SAM provides a practical and effective solution to this problem, opening up new possibilities for applying MANNs to real-world applications like processing large documents, reasoning over knowledge bases, and lifelong learning. The SDNC result on the Babi tasks is particularly significant, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance without supervised memory access.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Solves a critical bottleneck in MANNs.
* Achieves impressive speed and memory efficiency gains.
* Strong empirical validation across diverse tasks.
* Well-written and clearly explained.
* Demonstrates generalizability to other MANN architectures (SDNC).

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited analysis of ANN performance and sensitivity.
* Some lack of detail regarding hyperparameter tuning.
* Relatively limited ablation study.



**Final Recommendation:**

**Accept.** 

Despite the minor weaknesses outlined above, the strengths of this paper far outweigh its drawbacks. SAM represents a significant contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks, offering a practical and efficient solution to a critical scaling problem. The strong empirical results and clear presentation make this a valuable addition to the NIPS program. I recommend acceptance with the suggestion that the authors address the comments regarding ANN analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity in the camera-ready version.
