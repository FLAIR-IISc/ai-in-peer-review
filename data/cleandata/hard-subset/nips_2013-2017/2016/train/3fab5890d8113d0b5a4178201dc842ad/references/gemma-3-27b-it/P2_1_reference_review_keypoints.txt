- Introduces SAM: a MANN using sparse reads/writes and efficient data structures to overcome computational and memory bottlenecks.  
- Demonstrates up to three orders of magnitude speedup and reduced memory vs. vanilla NTM while maintaining competitive task performance.  
- Validated on diverse tasks (algorithmic, Babi, Omniglot one-shot) and scaled to longer sequences with curriculum learning.  
- Adapted SAM to SDNC, showing improved performance on Babi tasks and indicating generalizability.  
- Addresses a critical practical limitation of MANNs, enabling larger-scale applications.  
- The sparsity + data-structure combination effectively reduces time and space complexity with strong empirical evidence.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and supplemented with useful detailed material.  
- Provides a brief theoretical discussion arguing asymptotic optimality in space/time.  
- Individual components (sparse ops, approximate nearest neighbors) are not novel; novelty lies in their holistic integration for MANNs.  
- Performance may be sensitive to hyperparameters (K, discount, usage definition); needs more comprehensive sensitivity analysis.  
- Choice of ANN implementation (FLANN vs LSH) appears arbitrary and needs clearer justification.  
- Lacks comparison to other scaling approaches for MANNs (e.g., RL-based or discrete addressing methods).  
- Needs deeper discussion of limitations, such as potential information loss due to sparsity.  
- Qualitatively: technically sound, clear, moderately original, and potentially significant for long-term memory/reasoning tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves scalability, substantial speed/memory gains, thorough experiments, and generalizability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of constituent parts, hyperparameter sensitivity concerns, and missing alternative benchmarks.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept, with suggested minor revisions to address sensitivity, ANN choices, and limitation discussion.
