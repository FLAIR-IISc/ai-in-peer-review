- Proposes SAM: a sparse-access memory-augmented neural network achieving O(log N) time and O(1) per-step space via ANN structures and sparse reads/writes.
- Addresses scalability bottleneck of prior MANNs (e.g., NTMs) that incur O(N) time/space per step.
- Maintains differentiability while using sparse addressing, unlike prior RL-based discrete addressing approaches.
- Strong empirical validation on synthetic NTM tasks, bAbI QA (state-of-the-art without supervision), and Omniglot one-shot learning.
- Demonstrates extreme scalability: 1600× speedup over NTM at 1M memory words and curriculum generalization to sequences of 200k tokens.
- Theoretical claim of asymptotic optimality (Supplementary A) with empirical verification of complexity gains.
- Clear problem identification and practical, implementable solution that generalizes to other MANNs (e.g., SDNC).
- Lacks ablation on sparsity level K (uses K=4 or 8) with no justification or optimality analysis across tasks.
- Limited direct comparison to alternative sparse-scaling approaches (e.g., RL-based discrete addressing) for gradient/training quality and stability.
- Potential issues with chosen ANN structures (k-d trees, LSH) in high-dimensional or varied data distributions not fully explored.
- Theoretical claims need strengthening regarding constant factors and completeness of the asymptotic optimality argument.
- Missing analysis on why sparsity does not hurt learning and whether sparsity aids structured memory usage or reduces noise.
- Implementation details (custom data-structure for tracking memory usage) are insufficient for reproducibility.
- Overall significant and practical contribution to MANN scalability; reviewer recommendation: Accept.
