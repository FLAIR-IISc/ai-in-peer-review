## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant and well-executed contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The core idea of leveraging sparsity in read and write operations to overcome the memory and computational bottlenecks of traditional MANNs is both insightful and highly effective. The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating substantial improvements in speed and memory usage while maintaining (and sometimes even improving) performance on a variety of tasks, including scaling to much larger memory sizes and sequence lengths. I believe this paper is a strong candidate for acceptance to NIPS.

**1. Summary and Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper addresses a critical limitation of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) – their quadratic scaling in parameter count with memory size and the resulting computational burden during training.  Prior work, including Neural Turing Machines (NTMs) and Memory Networks, decoupled memory capacity from parameter count but still suffered from linear overhead in read/write operations and memory duplication for backpropagation.  The authors introduce Sparse Access Memory (SAM), which restricts read and write operations to a sparse subset of memory locations, drastically reducing computational complexity and memory requirements. This is coupled with an efficient memory management scheme. They demonstrate SAM’s performance on standard benchmarks (algorithmic tasks from NTMs, Babi tasks, Omniglot) and crucially, show its ability to scale to significantly larger memories and sequence lengths than previous methods.  They also demonstrate the generalizability of their approach by applying it to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC).

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Practical Impact:** The reduction in memory and computational cost is *substantial* – up to three orders of magnitude improvement in runtime for large memories. This makes training MANNs with large capacity feasible, unlocking potential applications previously intractable.
* **Sound Technical Approach:** The combination of sparse reads/writes and efficient data structures (compressed sparse rows, approximate nearest neighbors) is well-motivated and effectively implemented. The explanation of how to efficiently handle backpropagation with sparse updates is clear and convincing.
* **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation across a diverse set of tasks, including both synthetic and real-world datasets. They convincingly demonstrate that SAM maintains performance comparable to dense MANNs, even while achieving substantial speed and memory gains. The scaling experiments, particularly the curriculum learning results, are particularly impressive.
* **Generality of the Approach:** Demonstrating the applicability of their method to the DNC further strengthens the contribution, suggesting that the sparsity technique is broadly applicable to various MANN architectures.
* **Clear and Well-Written Paper:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the technical details of SAM. The supplementary material provides additional helpful information.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Choice of Sparsity Scheme:** While the “previously read/least recently accessed” write scheme is simple and effective, the paper doesn't extensively explore alternative sparsity strategies. A more detailed investigation of different write schemes and their impact on performance would be valuable.
* **ANN Implementation Details:** The paper mentions using both FLANN and LSH for approximate nearest neighbor search but provides limited details on the configuration and performance trade-offs of each. More discussion of these choices and their influence on results would be helpful.
* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** The paper mentions asymptotic optimality but could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the computational complexity and memory usage of SAM compared to other MANN architectures.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  While the authors provide hyperparameter details in the supplement, it’s unclear how sensitive the performance is to the choice of these parameters. A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the robustness of the results.



**4.  Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a critical bottleneck in MANN research.
* Offers a technically sound and practically effective solution.
* Demonstrates compelling empirical results on a variety of tasks.
* Shows scalability to large memory sizes and sequence lengths.
* Generalizable to other MANN architectures.

**Con:**

* Limited exploration of alternative sparsity schemes.
* Lack of in-depth analysis of ANN implementation details.
* Could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis.
* Some hyperparameter sensitivity might exist, but not thoroughly investigated.


**5.  Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by experimental results. It represents a complete and substantial piece of work. The authors are careful about discussing the limitations of their approach.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, some sections (e.g., ANN implementation) could benefit from more detailed explanations.
* **Originality:** The idea of leveraging sparsity for efficient MANNs is novel and represents a significant advance over previous work. The combination of sparsity with efficient data structures and memory management is also original.
* **Significance:** The results are highly significant, as they address a major obstacle to scaling MANNs to real-world applications. The potential impact of this work is substantial, and it is likely to inspire further research in this area.



**Overall Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS. The work is technically sound, well-executed, and addresses a significant problem in the field. The practical impact of the proposed approach is substantial, and the authors have provided a compelling demonstration of its effectiveness. While there are minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the paper.  I believe this paper will be of great interest to the NIPS community and will stimulate further research on scalable memory-augmented neural networks.
