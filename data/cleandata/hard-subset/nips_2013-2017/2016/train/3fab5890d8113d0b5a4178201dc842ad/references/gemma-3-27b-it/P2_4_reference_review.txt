## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs): the computational and memory cost associated with scaling memory size. The authors introduce SAM (Sparse Access Memory), a MANN architecture that utilizes sparse read and write operations, coupled with efficient data structures, to significantly reduce these costs while maintaining performance. They demonstrate substantial speedups (up to three orders of magnitude) and memory savings compared to a standard NTM, alongside comparable or improved results on a variety of tasks including algorithmic tasks, Babi reasoning, and Omniglot one-shot classification – even when scaling to significantly longer sequences. They also show how their approach can be applied to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC), achieving substantial performance improvements.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant bottleneck:** The scalability of MANNs is a known limitation hindering their broader application. This paper directly tackles this issue with a clever and well-motivated approach.
* **Strong empirical results:** The experimental results are compelling, showcasing significant improvements in both speed and memory usage without sacrificing performance. The scaling experiments, particularly with curriculum learning, are very convincing.
* **Theoretical justification:** The paper provides a complexity analysis (in the supplementary material) demonstrating the asymptotic optimality of SAM in terms of both space and time.
* **Generality:** The demonstration of applying the sparse access mechanism to the DNC highlights the broader applicability of the proposed approach.
* **Clear writing and organization:**  The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making it easy to follow the key ideas.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty in individual components:** While the *combination* of sparse operations and efficient data structures is novel and effective, individual components (sparse operations, approximate nearest neighbors) are not entirely new.  The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t fully emphasize the contribution lies in the synergistic effect.
* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:**  The paper touches upon hyperparameter tuning, but further discussion on the sensitivity of performance to parameters like the sparsity level (K), the discount factor, and the threshold for usage statistics would be valuable.
* **ANN implementation details:** While the use of ANN is promising, the specific choice of ANN (FLANN or LSH) and the criteria for switching between them aren’t thoroughly justified. A more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between these ANN options would be beneficial.
* **Comparison to other scaling approaches:**  The paper briefly mentions reinforcement learning-based scaling methods but a more detailed comparison, even a qualitative one, to other existing approaches would provide better context.




**Review Criteria Assessment:**

* **Quality (4/5):** The paper is technically sound, and the results are well-supported by experiments. The theoretical analysis is a valuable addition. The authors demonstrate careful evaluation, acknowledging the limitations. However, the novelty lies more in the combination of techniques than a groundbreaking single contribution.
* **Clarity (4/5):** The paper is well-written and organized. The use of figures and supplementary material helps to clarify complex concepts. A minor improvement would be to provide more detailed explanations of the ANN implementation.
* **Originality (3/5):** The approach of combining sparse access with efficient data structures is novel. However, individual components are established techniques. The originality primarily lies in the synergistic combination and its application to MANNs.
* **Significance (4/5):** The results are significant because they address a major bottleneck in the development and deployment of MANNs.  The demonstrated improvements in scalability open up new possibilities for applying these models to more complex tasks.  The potential impact on fields requiring long-term memory and reasoning is considerable.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper solves a crucial problem in the field of MANNs.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements in speed, memory usage, and performance.
* The approach is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and generally applicable.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The individual components are not entirely novel.
* A more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and the ANN implementation would be valuable.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  Despite some minor weaknesses, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The demonstrated improvements in scalability are substantial and have the potential to unlock new applications for these powerful models. The clarity of writing and the strong empirical evidence make this a valuable addition to the NIPS conference. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and ANN details.
