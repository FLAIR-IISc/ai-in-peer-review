- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): top-K content reads via ANN, sparse writes to read locations + LRU slot, and sparse rollback for O(1) per-step BPTT memory.  
- Claims asymptotic costs of O(log N) time per step and O(1) space per step (w.r.t. memory size) while remaining end-to-end trainable.  
- Builds on NTMs/DNCs and memory-network ideas but replaces dense attention with ANN-backed sparse addressing and write sparsity.  
- Technical implementation (sparse rollback, usage-based LRU) is sound and practically motivated.  
- Using ANN to restrict scoring to K candidates reduces O(N) bottleneck; ANN maintenance and periodic rebuild costs are addressed (amortized O(log N)).  
- Empirically shows large speed/space gains, maintained or improved data efficiency on canonical NTM tasks, scaling to millions of slots, competitive bAbI, and improved one-shot Omniglot.  
- Differentiability/bias concern: top-K plus approximate KNN creates biased, discontinuous attention; gradients ignore selection changes and need quantification of approximation error and its effect on convergence.  
- “Optimality” claims rely on supplemental assumptions; these assumptions and amortized rebuild costs should be explicitly stated in the main text.  
- ANN maintenance concerns: k-d trees can imbalance and LSH suffers in high dimensions—need empirical scaling vs memory word dimension M and comparisons across ANN methods.  
- Write policy is heuristic (mix of update-read and LRU); requires ablations on write gate/interpolation (αt, τt), thresholding, and frequency of catastrophic overwrites.  
- Fairness of comparisons: curriculum experiments match physical memory but not slot counts—should separate algorithmic data efficiency from capacity-enabled performance and include same-slot controls.  
- Reproducibility: core implementation details (data structures, rollback, ANN settings) are in the supplement; include concise algorithmic pseudocode in the main text.  
- Clarity: overall clear and well-organized, but Eq. (5), roles of τt/αt, and cached items for rollback need more intuitive explanation in main text.  
- Originality: novel and pragmatic 2016 contribution by integrating ANN indices with differentiable MANNs and sparse BPTT rollback.  
- Significance: important scaling of MANNs to millions of slots, enabling long-horizon BPTT and broader applicability; SDNC results generalize approach.  
- Specific suggested experiments: ablate K and exact vs ANN, quantify gradient bias, report amortized rebuild costs, analyze performance vs M (k-d vs LSH), report variance across seeds, and compare to discrete addressing baselines.  
- Pros: substantial scalability gains, maintains end-to-end training, strong empirical performance, and clear complexity articulation.  
- Cons: limited theoretical treatment of approximation-induced bias, heuristic write allocation lacking ablation, potentially unfair capacity comparisons, and underexplored ANN maintenance/dimensionality effects.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on stronger empirical ablations and clearer approximation/assumption analysis.
