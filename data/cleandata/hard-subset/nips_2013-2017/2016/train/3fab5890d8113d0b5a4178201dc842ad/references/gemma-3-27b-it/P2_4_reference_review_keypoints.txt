- Proposes SAM, a memory-augmented neural network using sparse reads/writes and efficient data structures to reduce compute and memory costs when scaling memory.  
- Demonstrates up to three orders of magnitude speedup and large memory savings over a standard NTM while maintaining or improving task performance.  
- Shows strong empirical results on algorithmic tasks, bAbI reasoning, and Omniglot one-shot classification, including longer-sequence scaling with curriculum learning.  
- Provides a complexity analysis in the supplement asserting asymptotic optimality in time and space.  
- Extends the sparse access mechanism to the DNC, showing broad applicability and substantial improvements.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Main novelty is in the combination of techniques (sparse operations + data structures), while individual components (sparsity, ANN) are not new.  
- Performance may be sensitive to hyperparameters (K, discount factor, usage thresholds); more analysis of sensitivity is needed.  
- ANN implementation choices (FLANN vs. LSH) and switching criteria are under-justified; a deeper trade-off analysis would help.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison (even qualitative) to other scaling approaches such as RL-based methods.  
- Quality rated 4/5: technically sound with strong experiments and useful theory, though novelty is composite.  
- Clarity rated 4/5: generally clear, but more ANN implementation detail would improve clarity.  
- Originality rated 3/5: novel synergistic combination, but constituent methods are established.  
- Significance rated 4/5: addresses a major bottleneck and enables applying MANNs to more complex, long-term-memory tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves crucial scalability problem, compelling empirical/theoretical evidence, broadly applicable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of components and insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and ANN choices.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity and ANN implementation details.
