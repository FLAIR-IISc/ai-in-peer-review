- Proposal: Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) — sparse reads via ANN top-K and writes to read locations + LRU, plus sparse rollback BPTT to reduce costs.  
- Complexity claim: States O(log N) per-step time and O(1) per-step space (after O(N) init) under the stated model.  
- Empirical results: Shows large speed/memory gains vs NTMs/dense variants, trains with millions of slots and long sequences, strong bAbI and Omniglot performance; SDNC variant also effective.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on NTMs/Memory Networks and ANN search (FLANN/LSH); differs by enforcing sparsity and integrating ANN for content addressing.  
- Differentiability concern: ANN neighbor selection and top-K truncation are discrete and not differentiable, creating biased gradients relative to dense soft attention.  
- Normalization clarity missing: Paper should state how truncated weights w̃ are normalized after top-K selection.  
- Ablation gap: Lacks analysis/ablation quantifying effects of K, exact vs approximate neighbors, and truncation bias on learning stability and accuracy.  
- Write policy risk: Heuristic write strategy (read locations + LRU) is simple but unexamined for failure modes (rapid context switches, aliasing, thrashing) and sensitivity to epsilon/K.  
- Optimality caveat: Asymptotic optimality argument is relegated to the supplement and relies on specific assumptions (constant K, data structure costs); needs clearer statement in main text.  
- Baseline fairness: Comparisons should include stronger baselines (dense DNC, key-value Memory Networks, RL hard attention) and explicitly discuss memory-capacity trade-offs in experiments.  
- Reproducibility issues: ANN performance depends on hyperparameters and rebuild schedules; more implementation details or released code are needed.  
- Clarity issues: Require precise definitions for w̃, gradients when neighbor set changes, ANN settings, sparse BPTT data structures, and erase behavior on LRU writes.  
- Originality: Integration of sparse reads/writes, ANN-based addressing, and efficient sparse backward pass is novel and practically significant.  
- Significance: High impact — advances scalable differentiable memory enabling long-context reasoning and large-scale one-shot learning.  
- Pros summary: Practical, well-engineered solution addressing key MANN bottlenecks with broad applicability and strong empirical validation.  
- Cons summary: Limited analysis of approximation effects, untested write-policy sensitivity, weaker exposition of optimality, and baseline/capacity fairness concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept, provided the authors add clarity, ablations, and more reproducibility/baseline details.
