## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs): the computational and memory cost associated with large external memories. The authors introduce SAM (Sparse Access Memory), a MANN architecture that leverages sparse read and write operations, coupled with efficient data structures, to significantly reduce both time and space complexity while maintaining performance. They demonstrate substantial speedups and memory savings compared to standard NTM implementations, alongside promising results on a variety of tasks, including algorithmic problems, question answering (Babi tasks), and one-shot learning (Omniglot).  They also show how to adapt their approach to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC).

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a crucial problem:** The scalability limitations of MANNs are a major obstacle to their wider adoption. This paper directly tackles this issue with a well-motivated and practical approach.
* **Significant performance gains:** The reported speedups (up to three orders of magnitude) and memory savings are substantial and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed sparse access scheme. The experimental results clearly validate the claim of improved efficiency.
* **Strong empirical validation:** The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation across a diverse set of tasks (algorithmic, QA, one-shot learning) and demonstrate comparable or improved performance to existing methods, even with significantly larger memory capacities. The scaling experiments using curriculum learning are particularly compelling.
* **Theoretical justification:** The paper includes a discussion of the time and space complexity of SAM, arguing for its optimality under reasonable assumptions.
* **Generality:** The demonstration of applying the sparse access approach to the DNC highlights the potential for wider applicability.
* **Clear writing and organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured, making it relatively easy to follow the core ideas.  Supplementary material is extensive and useful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty of individual components:** While the *combination* of sparse access and efficient data structures is effective, the individual components (sparse operations, approximate nearest neighbor search) are not entirely novel. The key contribution lies in the synergistic integration of these elements *within* a MANN framework.  This does somewhat temper the “originality” score.
* **ANN details could be more thorough:** The description of the ANN implementation (specifically the choice between k-d trees and LSH) feels a bit brief. More details on the tuning and performance characteristics of these components would be beneficial. The authors acknowledge room for improvement in this area in their discussion.
* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** Although a table of hyperparameters is provided in the supplement, exploring the sensitivity of performance to the sparsity level (K) and other key parameters would strengthen the analysis.
* **Comparison to recent work:** While references to relevant work are given, recent advances in memory compression techniques for neural networks are not explicitly discussed.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (4.5/5):** The paper is technically sound, with strong empirical evidence supporting its claims. The analysis of time and space complexity adds to the rigor.  The work is complete in the sense that it demonstrates a functional system with significant benefits.
* **Clarity (4/5):** The paper is mostly clear and well-organized. The supplementary material is very helpful. The notation could be slightly more consistent in places.
* **Originality (3.5/5):** The core idea of applying sparsity to MANNs is conceptually simple, and some of the components are borrowed from other areas. However, the effective integration and the resulting performance gains are novel and significant.  It’s a clever combination of techniques.
* **Significance (4.5/5):** This work has the potential to significantly impact the field of MANNs by removing a major barrier to scaling.  The results suggest that much larger and more complex memory systems are now feasible, opening up new possibilities for tackling challenging sequence learning problems.



**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Addresses a critical bottleneck in MANNs.
* Demonstrates substantial performance and memory improvements.
* Strong empirical validation across a diverse set of tasks.
* Potential to enable the development of more powerful and scalable memory-based models.

**Against:**

* Limited novelty of individual components.
* Some details of ANN implementation could be more thorough.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.** 

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem in a practical and effective manner. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination and the resulting benefits are significant. The paper is well-written, well-supported by experimental results, and has the potential to advance the field of MANNs.  I recommend acceptance to NIPS.
