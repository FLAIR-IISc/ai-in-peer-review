- Overall: Strong, well-executed contribution addressing scalability of MANNs; strong candidate for acceptance.  
- Core idea: Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM) that restricts reads/writes to a sparse subset of memory to cut compute and memory costs.  
- Scalability: Demonstrates up to three orders of magnitude speedups and large memory reductions without performance loss.  
- Theoretical support: Provides asymptotic optimality arguments for SAM’s space/time complexity (detailed in supplement).  
- Empirical breadth: Validated across algorithmic tasks (NTM), reasoning (bAbI), and one-shot learning (Omniglot), including longer sequence scaling.  
- Novelty: Combines sparse read/write scheme with usage tracking and efficient data structures—novel and insightful.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on MANN literature and connects well to approximate nearest neighbor (ANN) methods.  
- Generality: Extended to a sparse Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC), showing method applicability beyond SAM.  
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with comprehensive supplementary material.  
- Strength: Sparsity can improve learning in some tasks (e.g., priority sort).  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity (sparsity K, threshold ε) not thoroughly explored; needs ablation.  
- Weakness: Choice between FLANN and LSH feels ad hoc; lacks systematic ANN comparisons.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion/comparison with alternative scaling approaches (e.g., RL-based methods).  
- Weakness: Controller remains a simple LSTM; more powerful controllers not explored.  
- Qualitative: Technically sound, claims well-supported, and presents both strengths and limitations.  
- Originality & significance: High—addresses a critical practical bottleneck and enables larger memories/sequence lengths.  
- Pro acceptance: Solves scalability, novel effective solution, strong empirical validation, major speed/memory gains.  
- Con acceptance: Needs further hyperparameter analysis and broader comparative discussion.  
- Recommendation: Strongly recommended for acceptance.
