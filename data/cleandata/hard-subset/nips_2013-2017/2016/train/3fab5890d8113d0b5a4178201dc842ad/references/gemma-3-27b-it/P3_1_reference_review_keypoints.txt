- Introduces SAM (Sparse Access Memory), using sparse reads/writes to scale memory-augmented neural networks.
- Demonstrates substantial speedups and memory savings without harming performance; sometimes improves learning.
- Solves MANNs’ quadratic scaling bottleneck by limiting accesses to a small subset and using ANNs for fast nearest-neighbor search.
- Builds on NTMs, Memory Networks, and DNCs, and presents a Sparse DNC (SDNC) to show generality.
- Reports up to three orders of magnitude runtime improvement for large memories (e.g., 64,000 entries).
- Provides theoretical justification for asymptotic optimality in time and space (detailed in supplement).
- Strong empirical validation across algorithmic NTM benchmarks, bAbI reasoning tasks, and Omniglot one-shot learning.
- Curriculum learning experiments show robust generalization to sequences far longer than training.
- SDNC extension yields substantial performance gains over the dense DNC, demonstrating versatility.
- Paper is clear and well-written with a comprehensible architecture and experimental setup.
- Weakness: performance may be sensitive to choice and parameters of the ANN method; needs ablation.
- Weakness: the usage statistic U(2)T for memory eviction seems arbitrary and needs further study.
- Weakness: limited detail on hyperparameter tuning, affecting reproducibility.
- Weakness: lacks comparison/discussion with other sparsity techniques (weight pruning, sparse activations).
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, with suggested revisions to address ANN sensitivity and hyperparameter details.
