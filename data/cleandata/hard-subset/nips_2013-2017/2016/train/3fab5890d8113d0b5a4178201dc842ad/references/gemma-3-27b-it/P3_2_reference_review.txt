## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant and well-executed contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The authors effectively address a critical bottleneck in MANN training – the computational and memory cost associated with dense memory access – by introducing Sparse Access Memory (SAM). The results are compelling, demonstrating substantial speedups and memory savings without sacrificing performance on a diverse set of tasks, including scaling to impressively long sequences. I believe this work is highly relevant to NIPS and recommend acceptance.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the scaling limitations of MANNs (like NTMs and Memory Networks) which, despite their theoretical advantages, struggle in practice due to the quadratic complexity of dense memory operations and backpropagation.  The core idea of SAM is to restrict read/write operations to a *sparse* subset of memory locations, dramatically reducing computational overhead.  This builds directly upon the foundations laid by Weston et al. (Memory Networks) and Graves et al. (NTM), addressing a known major impediment to their wider adoption.  The use of approximate nearest neighbor search to further optimize sparse access is a clever addition, drawing from the broader ANN literature (e.g., Arya et al., Muja & Lowe). The application to the Differentiable Neural Computer (DNC) further solidifies the generality of the proposed approach. 

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Practical Impact:** The reported speedups (up to three orders of magnitude) and memory savings are substantial and directly address a key barrier to using MANNs on real-world problems.  The demonstration of scaling to longer sequence lengths is particularly encouraging.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The authors thoroughly evaluate SAM on a diverse set of tasks: algorithmic tasks from NTM, Babi reasoning, and Omniglot one-shot classification. This demonstrates the versatility of the approach.
* **Theoretical Justification:**  The paper outlines the asymptotic optimality of SAM in both time and space complexity (detailed in the supplementary material), providing a solid theoretical grounding for the empirical results.
* **Clever Implementation Details:** The efficient backpropagation scheme (avoiding caching the entire memory state) and the use of sparse matrix formats are crucial for realizing the performance gains.  The choice of a usage-based write scheme, coupled with approximate nearest neighbors, appears well-motivated and effective.
* **Generalizability:** Demonstrating the application to the Sparse DNC (SDNC) highlights the broad applicability of the proposed sparsity technique beyond the specific SAM architecture.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally clearly written and well-organized. The supplementary material provides helpful details regarding implementation and experimental setup.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the results are strong, a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the choice of *K* (the number of non-zero entries in sparse vectors) and the threshold parameter  in the usage statistic, would be beneficial.  How do these parameters affect performance and stability?
* **ANN Implementation Details:** The choice between FLANN and LSH for the approximate nearest neighbor search seems somewhat arbitrary.  A more detailed comparison of these approaches, and their respective impact on performance, would be valuable.  Furthermore, the cost of periodically rebuilding the ANN isn’t fully accounted for in the runtime analysis.
* **Limited Comparison with Other Scaling Techniques:** While the comparison with NTM and DAM is informative, the paper could benefit from a brief discussion of how SAM compares to other recent works aiming to scale MANNs (e.g., those employing reinforcement learning for discrete addressing as mentioned in the discussion section).
* **Curriculum Learning Details:** While the curriculum learning approach is effective, the specific rules for increasing difficulty could be presented with more clarity.



**4. Originality:**

The core idea of imposing sparsity on memory access is not entirely novel; similar approaches have been explored in other contexts. However, the *specific* implementation – the combination of sparse read/write operations, efficient backpropagation, and the incorporation of approximate nearest neighbor search – is a novel and effective solution to the scaling problem.  The demonstration of scaling to significantly longer sequences than previously achievable is a noteworthy contribution.

**5. Significance:**

This work has the potential to significantly advance the field of memory-augmented neural networks.  By addressing the critical scaling limitations, SAM opens up new possibilities for applying MANNs to more complex and realistic problems, such as processing large text corpora, reasoning about long-term dependencies, and learning complex algorithms. The demonstrated success with longer sequences and the potential for further optimization make this a significant contribution.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a major bottleneck in MANN research.
* Demonstrates significant performance improvements in terms of both speed and memory usage.
* Thoroughly validated on a diverse set of tasks.
* Presents a novel and effective combination of techniques.
* Has the potential to broaden the applicability of MANNs to real-world problems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Hyperparameter sensitivity could be further explored.
* More detailed analysis of the ANN implementation is needed.
* Limited comparison with other scaling techniques.




**Overall:** I strongly recommend accepting this paper.  It represents a significant advance in the field and has the potential to unlock the full potential of memory-augmented neural networks. The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality and impact of the work.  The authors have demonstrated a clear understanding of the problem, a well-designed solution, and a compelling empirical validation.
