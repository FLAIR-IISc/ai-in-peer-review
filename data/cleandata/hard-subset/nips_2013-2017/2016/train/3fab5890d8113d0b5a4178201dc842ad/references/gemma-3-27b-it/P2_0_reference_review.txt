## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in Memory-Augmented Neural Networks (MANNs): the computational and memory cost associated with scaling to large memory sizes. The authors introduce SAM (Sparse Access Memory), a MANN architecture employing sparse read and write operations coupled with efficient data structures (specifically, approximate nearest neighbors and a custom memory usage tracker).  SAM significantly reduces both time and space complexity compared to standard MANNs like the NTM, while maintaining comparable or improved performance on a range of tasks including algorithmic problems, question answering (Babi), and one-shot learning (Omniglot). They also demonstrate the applicability of their approach by adapting the Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC).

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Significant Problem:** Scaling MANNs is crucial for unlocking their potential in real-world applications.  The authors clearly articulate this problem and its implications.
* **Strong Technical Contribution:** The proposed sparse read/write mechanism, combined with the efficient data structures, represents a substantial technical advancement.  The claimed asymptotic optimality is a strong point, though further scrutiny in related work is warranted (see weaknesses).
* **Compelling Empirical Results:** The speed and memory benchmarks (Figure 1) are impressive, demonstrating up to three orders of magnitude improvement over the NTM.  The results on the diverse set of tasks (NTM tasks, Babi, Omniglot) show that sparsity doesn't significantly degrade performance and, in some cases, even improves learning.  Scaling the tasks via curriculum learning further validates the approach.
* **Generality Demonstrated:** Applying the sparsity approach to the DNC (SDNC) and achieving superior results on Babi tasks highlights the broader applicability of the technique.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it relatively easy to follow the proposed architecture and experimental setup. The supplementary material is extensive and helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Related Work on Sparsity:** While the paper acknowledges MANNs and related architectures, it lacks a comprehensive discussion of other work addressing sparsity in neural networks, especially those attempting to reduce computational cost.  There's a missed opportunity to position SAM within a broader landscape of sparse neural network techniques.  For example, the paper could benefit from discussing structured sparsity methods.
* **Asymptotic Optimality Claims Need Further Justification:** The claim of "asymptotically optimal" space and time complexity is strong. The supplementary material provides *some* justification, but it would be strengthened by a more rigorous theoretical comparison with other approaches.  It’s unclear what other architectures are being compared against in this claim.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions several tuning parameters (e.g., discount factor in usage definition, sparsity threshold), and it's unclear how sensitive the performance is to these choices.  A more detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning would be beneficial.
* **ANN Implementation Details:** While the authors mention using both FLANN and LSH, the rationale for choosing one over the other based on word size could be clarified.  A more detailed discussion of the ANN's impact on performance, beyond just speedup, would be helpful.
* **Curriculum Learning Details:** The curriculum learning approach is promising, but the criteria for increasing the curriculum level (“average training loss dropped below a threshold”) feels somewhat vague.  More details about the threshold and its impact would be useful.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are generally supported by experiments, and the methodology appears rigorous.  However, the asymptotic optimality claim needs stronger justification.
* **Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized, though some sections could benefit from more detail (see weaknesses).
* **Originality:** The combination of sparse read/write operations with efficient data structures in the context of MANNs is novel and represents a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** The results are important because they address a key limitation of MANNs, paving the way for their application to more complex, real-world problems. The demonstrated improvements in speed and memory usage are substantial.  The successful application to diverse tasks strengthens the impact.




**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a critical scaling issue in MANNs.
* Strong technical contribution with significant speed and memory improvements.
* Compelling empirical results on a diverse set of tasks.
* Demonstrates generality through SDNC implementation.

**Con:**

* Limited discussion of related work on sparsity in neural networks.
* Asymptotic optimality claims need further substantiation.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity not fully explored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The proposed SAM architecture effectively addresses the scaling challenges that have hindered the widespread adoption of MANNs. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh them.  I **recommend acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above (particularly expanding the related work section and providing more justification for the asymptotic optimality claims).
