## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant and well-executed contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The core idea of leveraging sparsity to address the computational and memory bottlenecks of traditional MANNs is both clever and highly effective. The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating substantial speedups and memory savings without sacrificing performance, and even showing improved learning in some cases. I believe this paper is a strong candidate for acceptance to NIPS.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The authors tackle a critical limitation of memory-augmented neural networks (MANNs) – their quadratic scaling with memory size, hindering practical application to complex tasks. Existing MANNs, like NTMs and Memory Networks, decouple memory capacity from model parameters but suffer from high computational cost due to dense read/write operations and the need to store memory states for backpropagation. This paper introduces SAM (Sparse Access Memory), a MANN that achieves scalability through sparse memory access (limiting read/write operations to a small subset of memory locations) and efficient data structures (ANNs for fast approximate nearest neighbor search).

The work builds directly upon the foundations laid by NTMs [7] and Memory Networks [21], and correctly identifies the key bottlenecks in these architectures. It also acknowledges and builds upon recent work in differentiable neural computers [8], demonstrating the generality of the proposed sparse approach through the construction of a Sparse DNC (SDNC). The authors appropriately position their work within the broader context of scaling neural networks and efficient memory management.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Scalability:** The core contribution is a demonstrable improvement in both time and space complexity compared to standard MANNs, particularly for large memory sizes. The reported 3 orders of magnitude improvement in runtime for 64,000 memories is impressive.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a concise argument for the asymptotic optimality of SAM in terms of time and space complexity (detailed in the supplementary material).
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The paper presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation on a variety of tasks – algorithmic tasks (NTM benchmarks), bAbI reasoning tasks, and Omniglot one-shot learning – demonstrating that sparsity does not significantly hinder performance and can even improve learning.
* **Curriculum Learning & Generalization:** Scaling tasks through curriculum learning provides a rigorous test of SAM’s ability to handle long sequences and large memories, and the results are very positive. The demonstrated generalization to sequences far exceeding training length is particularly encouraging.
* **SDNC Implementation:** The extension of the sparse approach to the Differentiable Neural Computer demonstrates the versatility of the method and offers a substantial performance boost over the dense variant.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The architecture is clearly explained, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **ANN Sensitivity:** While the use of approximate nearest neighbor search is crucial for scalability, the performance can be sensitive to the choice of ANN algorithm and its parameters. The paper briefly mentions FLANN and LSH, but a more detailed ablation study exploring the impact of different ANN configurations would be beneficial.
* **Usage Statistic Definition:** The choice of the usage statistic U(2)T for determining least recently accessed memory locations appears somewhat arbitrary.  A more thorough investigation into the effect of different usage statistic definitions on performance could provide further insights.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions extensive hyperparameter tuning, but provides limited details in the main text.  More information on the tuning process would enhance reproducibility.
* **Comparison to other Sparsity Techniques:**  The paper focuses on sparse *access* to memory. It would be helpful to briefly discuss and contrast this approach with other sparsity techniques commonly used in neural networks (e.g., weight pruning, sparse activations).



**4. Originality & Significance:**

The idea of leveraging sparsity to address the scaling issues of MANNs is original and significant. While sparse representations are common in other areas of deep learning, applying this principle to memory access within MANNs is a novel approach. The demonstrated speedups and memory savings are substantial, potentially enabling the application of MANNs to more complex and real-world problems.  The SDNC result is also a notable contribution, offering a practical improvement to a recent, state-of-the-art architecture.  This work directly addresses a crucial bottleneck in the development of powerful, scalable sequence learning models.

**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a fundamental challenge in MANN research (scalability).
* Presents a technically sound and well-motivated solution.
* Demonstrates strong empirical results across diverse tasks.
* Offers a practical and significant improvement over existing approaches.
*  The SDNC result adds further value and demonstrates the generality of the approach.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* ANN performance sensitivity requires further investigation.
* Usage statistic definition could benefit from more rigorous analysis.
* Limited details on hyperparameter tuning.

**6. Final Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS. The presented work is a significant contribution to the field, offering a practical and effective solution to a critical scalability challenge in memory-augmented neural networks.  The strengths of the paper far outweigh the minor weaknesses, and the potential impact of this work is substantial.  I would suggest the authors address the reviewer concerns regarding ANN sensitivity and hyperparameter details in a revised version.
