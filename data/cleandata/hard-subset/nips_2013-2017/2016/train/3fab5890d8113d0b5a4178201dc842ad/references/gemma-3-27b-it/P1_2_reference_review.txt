## Review of "Scaling Memory-Augmented Neural Networks with Sparse Reads and Writes"

**Summary:**

This paper presents SAM (Sparse Access Memory), a novel memory-augmented neural network (MANN) architecture designed to address the scalability limitations of existing MANNs like NTMs and Memory Networks. The core idea is to constrain read and write operations to a sparse subset of the memory, dramatically reducing computational and memory overhead. The authors demonstrate substantial speedups (up to three orders of magnitude) and memory savings, while maintaining competitive performance on a variety of tasks including algorithmic tasks, Babi reasoning, and Omniglot one-shot classification. They also showcase the adaptability of their approach by presenting a sparse version of the Differentiable Neural Computer (SDNC).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds directly on the foundation of MANNs established by works like NTMs [Graves et al., 2014] and Memory Networks [Weston et al., 2015]. It directly addresses the scalability issues highlighted in these works, particularly the quadratic dependency of parameters on memory size. The use of sparse operations draws connections to research on sparse neural networks, though the focus here is specifically on the memory access component of MANNs. The use of approximate nearest neighbor search leverages existing techniques from information retrieval and database indexing.  The SDNC extension relates to recent work on Differentiable Neural Computers [Graves et al., 2016].

**Strengths:**

* **Significant Scalability Improvement:** The paper convincingly demonstrates substantial improvements in both time and space complexity compared to standard MANNs.  The reported speedups and memory savings are impressive and address a critical bottleneck in the field.
* **Maintained Performance:** Importantly, the authors demonstrate that the sparsity introduced does *not* significantly degrade performance. The results on various tasks show that SAM can achieve comparable or even superior results to dense MANNs.
* **Thorough Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are comprehensive, covering both synthetic and real-world datasets.  The inclusion of curriculum learning to scale tasks is a strong point.
* **Generality:** The ability to apply the sparsity technique to other memory architectures (e.g., SDNC) demonstrates the broader applicability of the proposed approach. The impressive performance of the SDNC is a significant achievement.
* **Efficient Implementation:** The use of efficient data structures (CSR format for sparse matrices) and approximate nearest neighbor search contribute to the practical viability of the approach.
* **Clear Writing and Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the key ideas and results readily accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **ANN Details:** While the use of ANN is mentioned, the specific ANN implementations (FLANN vs. LSH) and their parameter choices are somewhat buried in the text. A more detailed discussion of the ANN tradeoffs (accuracy vs. speed) would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but the sensitivity of performance to different hyperparameters (especially regarding the sparsity level 'K' and the usage definition parameter ' ') isn’t fully explored.
* **Limited Comparison to Alternative Sparsity Methods:** The paper primarily compares to dense MANNs. A comparison to other approaches for scaling MANNs, such as those relying on reinforcement learning for discrete addressing [Zaremba & Sutskever, 2015], would strengthen the contribution.
* **Theoretical Optimality Claims:** While the authors claim asymptotic optimality, the assumptions underlying these claims are not fully elaborated within the main text (referenced in Supplementary A). This makes it difficult to fully evaluate the theoretical foundations of the approach.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses a critical bottleneck in the field of MANNs.
* Demonstrates significant practical improvements in scalability.
* Maintains competitive performance on a range of tasks.
* Offers a broadly applicable approach for sparsifying memory access.
* Well-written and thoroughly evaluated.

**Con:**

* ANN implementation details could be more comprehensive.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity could be better analyzed.
* Limited comparison to alternative scalability methods.
* Theoretical optimality claims require further clarification.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of memory-augmented neural networks. The proposed SAM architecture addresses a significant practical limitation of existing models and offers a promising path toward scaling MANNs to larger and more complex problems. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper far outweigh them.  I believe this paper is **highly suitable for acceptance** to NIPS.



**Confidence:** 9/10.



**Additional Notes/Suggestions for Authors:**

*  Please provide a more detailed discussion of the ANN implementations used, including parameter settings and a comparison of their performance.
* Consider adding a section discussing the sensitivity of performance to key hyperparameters, such as the sparsity level 'K' and the usage definition parameter ' '.
* Briefly discuss related work on alternative scalability approaches for MANNs and highlight the advantages of your method.
* Expand upon the theoretical optimality claims in the main text or provide a more detailed explanation in the supplementary material.
