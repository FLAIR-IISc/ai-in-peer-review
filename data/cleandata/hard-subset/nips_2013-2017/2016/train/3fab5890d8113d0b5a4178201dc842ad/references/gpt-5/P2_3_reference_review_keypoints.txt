- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse top-K content-based reads via ANN and sparse writes (read locations + LRU) to scale MANNs efficiently.
- Implements ANN (kd-trees/LSH) for top-K search yielding O(log N) read time and O(1) per-step writes with sparse update logs and rollback for O(T) BPTT memory.
- Memory management uses a time-since-access usage statistic and LRU-based allocation/erase.
- Empirically shows large speed/memory improvements (100–1600×) versus dense NTMs and scales to long sequences, with competitive results on synthetic tasks, bAbI, and Omniglot.
- Strength: practical engineering — sparse update log and rollback cleanly solve the O(NT) memory bottleneck with clear complexity accounting.
- Strength: internally consistent sparse read/write scheme with natural update/allocation mechanics and good constant factors.
- Strength: broad empirical validation across tasks and a demonstrated extension to DNC (SDNC).
- Weakness: non-differentiable top-K/ANN selection introduces gradient bias; no analysis of its effect or failure cases (near-ties, unstable supports).
- Weakness: theoretical optimality claims are informal in the main text and rely on supplement assumptions; dynamic ANN maintenance and amortized costs are not rigorously treated.
- Weakness: comparisons sometimes conflate capacity advantages (huge N) with algorithmic improvements; more matched-capacity ablations are needed.
- Weakness: limited ablations on K, ANN choice, and usage thresholds; performance sensitivity to these is underexplored.
- Weakness: real-world evaluation is modest — no large-scale text/long-document experiments despite stated motivation.
- Clarity: generally well-organized and explicit complexity claims, but some implementation-critical details and differentiability caveats are relegated to the supplement.
- Originality: novel combination of sparse attention, ANN integration, and BPTT rollback; offers a deterministic, low-variance alternative to discrete addressing.
- Significance: high practical impact by enabling million-scale memories and long untruncated sequences, likely to inspire follow-ups.
- Pros for acceptance: impactful scalability advance with strong empirical speed/memory gains and competitive task performance.
- Cons against acceptance: missing analyses on gradient bias, informal theoretical claims, and limited real-world validation.
- Suggestions: add ablations on K and exact vs ANN retrieval, empirically analyze gradient/selection instability, quantify ANN rebuild overheads, and include a larger long-context task.
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution that addresses a key MANN bottleneck and merits acceptance despite some missing analyses; reviewer leans toward accept.
