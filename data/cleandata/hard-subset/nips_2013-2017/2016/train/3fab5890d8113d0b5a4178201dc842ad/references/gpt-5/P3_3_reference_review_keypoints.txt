- Introduces Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse top-K reads via ANN and sparse writes to read locations or LRU, with sparse rollback BPTT for memory-efficient backpropagation.  
- Demonstrated on NTM-style algorithmic tasks, bAbI, and Omniglot one-shot learning; sparsified DNC (SDNC) yields large speedups and strong bAbI results.  
- Extends prior MANNs by enforcing sparsity to avoid linear-time/space attention, supporting online writes and retaining gradient-based training despite non-differentiable address selection.  
- Technical novelty: combining sparse read/write, ANN index in the training loop, and rollback BPTT is elegant and practically meaningful for scaling external memory.  
- Provides convincing complexity claims and implementation details (O(log N) time per step, O(1) per-step memory, CSR, ANN indices, LRU tracking), valuable to practitioners.  
- Strong empirical evidence: up to ~1600× faster than NTM, orders-of-magnitude memory savings, long-horizon BPTT (>4000 steps) and generalization up to length 200k.  
- Generality: approach applies beyond NTM; SDNC demonstrates broader applicability.  
- Major weakness: non-differentiable top-K/ANN selection yields biased gradients; paper lacks analysis/ablations on K, gradient bias, and optimization stability.  
- Write policy limitations: restricting writes to previously read locations plus an LRU slot with full erase may reduce expressivity; content-based sparse write is mentioned but unevaluated.  
- Baselines and comparisons are incomplete: optimized dense alternatives, kernelized/block-sparse attention, and contemporary memory models (MemN2N, key-value MemNets) are not fully explored.  
- ANN specifics underexplored: index rebuild costs, inclusion in timing, sensitivity to ANN hyperparameters, failure modes, and systematic FLANN vs LSH comparisons are missing.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many engineering details are in the supplement; unclear if code/data structures will be released; some notation inconsistencies.  
- Quality: technically sound with plausible asymptotic claims, though assumptions (constant K, amortized rebuild) and formal lower bounds need clearer statement.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-structured, but needs more explanation of gradient flow under top-K truncation, effects of index rebuilds, and consistent notation.  
- Originality: novel integration of sparse ANN retrieval and rollback BPTT to scale differentiable memory; addresses a major bottleneck.  
- Significance: high — substantial speed/memory improvements and generality likely to influence future MANN and large-memory architectures.  
- Suggested experiments: ablations on K and erase policy, wall-clock timings including ANN maintenance, analysis of incorrect ANN returns, and comparisons to differentiable sparse-attention alternatives.  
- Recommendation: Accept (score 8).
