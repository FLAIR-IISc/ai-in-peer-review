- Summary: Introduces SAM, a Memory-Augmented Neural Network with sparse reads/writes and efficient data structures to reduce time/space cost while maintaining or improving performance.  
- Summary: Demonstrates large speed/memory improvements and good task performance (algorithmic tasks, Babi, Omniglot) and extends approach to a sparse DNC (SDNC).  

- Strength: Clearly addresses the important problem of scaling MANNs for real-world use.  
- Strength: Strong technical contribution: sparse read/write mechanism combined with ANN structures and memory tracker, claiming asymptotic optimality.  
- Strength: Compelling empirical results showing up to ~1000x improvements in speed/memory and maintained or improved task performance.  
- Strength: Generality shown by applying sparsity approach to DNC and improving Babi results.  
- Strength: Well-written, well-organized paper with useful supplementary material.  

- Weakness: Related work on sparsity is under-discussed; paper should better position SAM within broader sparse NN literature and structured sparsity methods.  
- Weakness: The "asymptotically optimal" space/time claim needs stronger theoretical justification and clearer comparison baselines.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity (usage discount, sparsity threshold, etc.) is not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Weakness: ANN implementation choices (FLANN vs LSH) and their impact beyond speed are not fully explained.  
- Weakness: Curriculum learning schedule/thresholds are vaguely described and need more detail.  

- Quality: Methodology and experiments are generally rigorous, but theoretical claims need bolstering.  
- Clarity: Paper is clear overall but some sections lack detail (related work, ANN/curriculum specifics).  
- Originality: Novel combination of sparse access with efficient data structures in MANNs.  
- Significance: Substantial practical improvements that broaden applicability of MANNs to larger problems.  

- Pro: Addresses critical scaling issue, strong technical and empirical contributions, and demonstrates generality.  
- Con: Insufficient related work on sparsity, weak theoretical substantiation of optimality claims, and unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity.  

- Overall: Strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand related work and justify asymptotic claims.
