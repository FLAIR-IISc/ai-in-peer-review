- Proposes Sparse Access Memory (SAM): sparse top-K reads via ANN, sparse writes (read locations + LRU), and rollback-based sparse BPTT to scale MANNs to very large memories.
- Claims asymptotic optimality (O(log N) time, O(1) space per step) and implements ANN indices (k-d trees/LSH) inside the network.
- Empirical gains: orders-of-magnitude speed and memory savings vs NTM/Dense variants, scalability to millions of slots, strong curriculum generalization, good bAbI and Omniglot results.
- Extends prior MANNs (NTM, DNC, Memory Networks) by decoupling memory size from compute using sparse access and internal ANN indices.
- Strength: clear, pragmatic engineering — top-K reads, sparse writes, and rollback BPTT enabling constant per-step memory.
- Strength: theoretical/systems contributions with complexity analysis and practical BPTT scheme avoiding O(NT) duplication.
- Strength: significant empirical speedups (100–1600×), large memory reductions, and algorithmic generalization beyond training lengths.
- Strength: generality demonstrated by Sparse DNC (SDNC) with state-of-the-art bAbI without supervised memory access.
- Strength: careful comparisons to NTM, DAM, and LSTM showing sparsity can aid learning on some tasks.
- Weakness: nondifferentiability of top-K/ANN and zero gradients to non-selected slots — needs discussion on gradient bias/variance, stability, and convergence.
- Weakness: sensitivity to ANN choice, K, and word dimensionality not quantified; needs ablations on K, M, ANN type, and sensitivity to index drift.
- Weakness: simple LRU allocation may overwrite rare but relevant items; need ablations vs discounted usage and content-based writes and sensitivity to erase thresholds.
- Weakness: key quantitative results and many implementation details are relegated to the supplement (e.g., bAbI per-task numbers, Omniglot baselines, hardware/seed/hyperparams).
- Weakness: asymptotic optimality claim is deferred to supplement; should state formal assumptions and brief statement in main text.
- Weakness: limited discussion of GPU-friendliness; ANN/sparse ops may be CPU-bound and need wall-clock breakdowns for GPU pipelines.
- Quality: technically sound with solid systems design and broad experimental suite, but main methodological risks need more explicit treatment and ablations.
- Clarity: well-organized and readable, but several implementation details (usage tracking, rollback, ANN schedule) belong in supplement; pseudocode and complexity table would help.
- Originality: novel combination of ANN-based sparse access, constant-space BPTT, and simple LRU allocation within differentiable MANNs.
- Significance: high — addresses core MANN scalability bottleneck with practical speedups and applicability to reasoning and meta-learning tasks.
- Pros for acceptance: practical scalable differentiable memory with convincing empirical validation and generalizable approach.
- Cons for acceptance: limited analysis of gradient implications, missing ablations on K/ANN/allocation, and omitted main-text quantitative/implementation details.
- Requested clarifications: sensitivity analyses for K/epsilon/M, ANN maintenance/rebuild policy and costs, empirical scaling with M/N, and a formal optimality statement.
- Recommendation: Accept — impactful, well-executed work that would benefit from the requested clarifications and additional ablations.
