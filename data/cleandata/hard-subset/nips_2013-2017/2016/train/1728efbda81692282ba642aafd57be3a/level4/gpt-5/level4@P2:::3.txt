Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Koopman spectral analysis in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). It extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by lifting data via a kernel feature map and computing Ritz values and modes from the projection of the Koopman (composition) operator onto a finite-dimensional Krylov subspace in RKHS. A robust variant projects onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to mitigate ill-conditioning. The method is positioned as “kernel DMD,” akin in spirit to extended DMD (EDMD) and kernelized EDMD, with applications to prediction via pre-images, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition. Empirical examples include a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map, with additional demonstrations for motion data and change detection.

Strengths
- Clarity and organization: The paper is generally well structured and readable. The algorithmic steps are explicit, and the connection to DMD/Koopman theory is clearly motivated.
- Technical interest: Lifting DMD into RKHS to obtain richer (nonlinear) approximations of Koopman eigenfunctions is compelling. The robustification via kernel PCA is practically valuable.
- Potential significance: The approach can serve as a feature extractor for nonlinear dynamics and appears useful for downstream tasks (prediction, recognition, change detection).
- Empirical demonstrations: Although limited, the examples are informative and show that the method can recover meaningful spectra and provide useful features.

Main concerns and requests for clarification
1) Definition of the operator in Section 3 and Eq. (7). As written, KH is described as a linear operator on H, while ϕ: M → H is a feature map. Then (KH ϕ)(x) = ϕ(f(x)) maps M → H, i.e., it acts on the feature map rather than on elements of H. This is conceptually different from the standard Koopman composition operator defined on scalar observables h ∈ H by (Kh)(x) = h(f(x)). Please clarify:
- Is H an RKHS of scalar-valued functions on M with canonical feature map ψ(x) = k(x, ·) ∈ H, and is the operator the usual composition operator C_f: H → H defined by (C_f h)(x) = h(f(x))? If so, please consistently use h ∈ H, and avoid treating the feature map ϕ as an element of H.
- Alternatively, if the analysis is restricted to the finite-dimensional subspace Φ := span{ϕ(x_t)} with the induced inner product, then KH should be defined only on Φ by KH ϕ(x_t) = ϕ(x_{t+1}). Please make the domain/codomain precise and state conditions under which composition preserves H (boundedness/invariance).

2) Theorem 1 (and the phrase “apply KH to both sides”). The proof manipulates expressions where both sides are scalar or lie outside H, yet KH: H → H is invoked. Please revise the proof to ensure all objects are in the operator’s domain and the adjoint/projection operators are well defined. Also, make explicit the assumptions needed for the Arnoldi/companion-matrix argument in RKHS:
- Linear independence of {ϕ(x0), …, ϕ(xτ−1)} and existence of a QR factorization in the induced inner product.
- Distinct Ritz values and diagonalizability of the companion matrix.
- Invariance or the residual term ψ; when ψ = 0 (ϕ(xτ) ∈ span{ϕ(x0), …, ϕ(xτ−1)}), recovery of eigenfunctions is exact on the data.
Spell out these assumptions before the theorem rather than introducing additional ones mid-proof.

3) Relation to EDMD and kernel EDMD. The proposed approach is very close to EDMD with a kernel-induced dictionary. Please clarify the precise relationship to Williams et al. (2015) and to “exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting” DMD variants. A direct empirical comparison (e.g., spectra and mode reconstruction error) would strengthen the paper.

4) Robustification via kernel PCA (Section 4). This section is useful, but a few details need clarification:
- Centering consistency for Mτ and M+; whether nonstationarity biases the centered Gram matrix.
- Truncation criteria (energy threshold or scree) and sensitivity to p.
- Computational complexity and numerical stability (conditioning of S̄, use of Tikhonov regularization if needed).

5) Applications. The prediction via pre-image is interesting but under-specified. Please provide:
- The exact pre-image algorithm used, its initialization, and convergence properties.
- Quantitative prediction errors vs. baselines (e.g., EDMD, local linear models).
- Sensitivity to kernel choice and bandwidth.

6) Double-blind policy. The manuscript includes a publicly identifying code link. This compromises anonymity and should be removed for review. A statement that code will be released upon acceptance is sufficient.

Quality
- The central idea is sound and potentially impactful. However, the operator-theoretic definitions and Theorem 1 proof require careful revision to ensure mathematical correctness. Experiments are promising but relatively small and lack comparisons.

Clarity
- Generally clear, but Section 3 needs precise functional-analytic definitions (what H is; domain/codomain of KH; adjoints; conditions for composition to remain in H). Make assumptions explicit and avoid ambiguous phrases like “apply KH to both sides” when scalars are involved.

Originality
- The kernelization of DMD is natural and relates closely to EDMD with kernel dictionaries. The paper’s contribution appears to be a particular derivation via RKHS/Krylov subspaces and a robust kernel-PCA formulation. Please sharpen the novelty claims relative to prior kernel/EDMD work.

Significance
- If the theoretical clarifications are addressed and comparisons added, the method could be a useful addition to the Koopman/DMD toolbox, particularly for feature extraction in nonlinear dynamics.

Pros for acceptance
- Interesting and practically relevant extension of DMD into RKHS.
- Clear algorithmic pipeline with a robust variant.
- Demonstrated utility for downstream tasks.

Cons for acceptance
- Ambiguities in operator definitions and proof correctness in Section 3.
- Limited empirical comparisons to strong baselines (EDMD, exact/optimized DMD).
- Some implementation details (pre-image, truncation, complexity) under-specified.
- Double-blind violation in the current draft.

Minor comments and typos
- Line 55: “the the” → “the”.
- Line 153: κj/λj (indices).
- Define Q∗ as the adjoint; clarify Hermitian transpose usage in complex RKHS.
- Discuss handling of repeated/near-repeated eigenvalues and noisy data.
- Consider reporting confidence/robustness of estimated eigenvalues (e.g., bootstrapping).

Overall, I find the paper promising, but I recommend revision to fix the operator-theoretic foundations in Section 3, make assumptions explicit, and add comparisons to EDMD/kernel EDMD.
