Referee’s report on: “Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels”

This manuscript extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), yielding a kernelized DMD (KDMD) algorithm, and demonstrates it on several toy problems. By allowing feature maps to define the kernel, the approach links DMD to specific features of interest, making it a powerful generalization of standard DMD.

Overall, however, the paper is very difficult to read. Even for simple concepts, the exposition leans heavily on formal mathematics where plain, direct prose would suffice, while other parts are verbose without adding clarity. As written, the paper may be hard to follow even for specialists. In its current form, it is too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. That said, I strongly encourage the authors to revise it with a more pedagogical presentation; the contribution is significant and, with a clearer write-up, the paper could be highly cited.

I. Major remarks

1) Exposition and concision
- The presentation is often verbose and could be significantly streamlined without loss of rigor. For example, the sentence:
  “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point.”
  could be replaced by a much shorter, clearer statement conveying the same idea. Throughout, prefer precise, concise sentences over dense, multi-clause formulations when the concept is simple.

2) Section 4
- In a short paper, Section 4 adds little beyond what is already (partially) covered in the introduction. Consider removing it or folding only the essential points into the introduction to improve flow.

3) Section 5
- The material in Section 5 should be illustrated (e.g., with a small example or schematic) to aid intuition. If space is tight, at least summarize its key takeaways in the conclusion so readers understand the practical implications.

4) Section 6
- Section 6 is overly condensed. It would benefit from being developed with the level of detail and clarity used in Section 5, including more explicit guidance on implementation choices and their consequences.

II. Minor remarks

1) Possible typo in the “toy NL” case
- The “NL” toy example appears to be linear in the provided description. Please check for a typo or clarify the nonlinearity.

2) Nature of the first two problems and where KDMD should excel
- The first two problems are polynomial. This is precisely the setting where KDMD should excel—when an appropriate kernel can be guessed (as in item 1). Please provide more details and discussion, similar to the level given in Section 5, to substantiate this advantage.

Reference
- Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, “Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control,” arXiv:1510.03007v2 (2015).
