- Proposes a spectral approach using the Koopman operator applied to the second iterate x(t+2)=f(f(x(t))) for linear representation of nonlinear composition.
- Method uses eigen-decomposition of the Koopman operator with parallels to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Demonstrated for embedding and recognition of dynamical systems with reported strong empirical performance.
- Core novelty framed as representing function composition linearly and applying spectral decomposition.
- Technical contribution appears limited to kernelizing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), an established method.
- Paper does not clearly explain how the kernelized variant advances beyond prior DMD-based work.
- Applications lack compelling justification or demonstration of uniqueness/superiority over simpler alternatives like RNNs or change-point detection.
- Empirical evaluation misses key analyses: scalability with system complexity and comparisons on interpretability, sample efficiency, and computational cost versus RNNs.
- Practical relevance is unclear due to insufficient baseline comparisons and lack of clarified theoretical/practical advantages.
- Contribution judged incremental; would be strengthened by rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines and clearer exposition of advantages.
