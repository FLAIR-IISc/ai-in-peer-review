- Introduces a kernelized DMD method for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, validated on synthetic systems and motion-capture data.  
- Novelty: natural and sound extension of DMD into the kernel-methods framework, with clear theoretical derivations.  
- Clarity: well-written, good educational introduction to Koopman operator theory and DMD for ML readers.  
- Cross-disciplinary value: bridges physics (fluid dynamics) and machine learning, potentially fostering new research directions.  
- Weak ML motivation: unclear why this analysis is needed or advantageous for common ML problems, especially stochastic settings (RL, robotics, noisy time series).  
- Predictability: kernelizing a linear-operator method follows a familiar kernel-pattern and may feel unsurprising to kernel-method-savvy readers.  
- Missing baseline: experiments should include standard linear DMD comparisons on toy systems and the Hénon map to demonstrate practical gains.  
- Real-data results preliminary: locomotion experiments lack clear quantitative/qualitative comparison to simpler alternatives (e.g., PCA or kernel PCA).  
- Suggestion: discuss applicability to stochastic systems, concrete ML use-cases where kernel DMD outperforms existing methods, and any limitations.  
- Question: clarify whether the algorithm operates solely via Gram matrices (no explicit feature maps) or requires explicit feature-space representations and the implications.
