- Paper proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to compute it from measurement data.  
- Demonstrations on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA yield ambiguous results.  
- Work is an interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition and aligns with prior efforts to “kernelize” linear-operator techniques.  
- Experiments are primarily limited to artificial problems, weakening practical impact.  
- Paper is well-written and educational for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts; valuable cross-field introduction.  
- Relevance to mainstream machine learning is unclear because the setup is deterministic, whereas ML often models stochastic dynamical systems.  
- Authors should better justify practical utility and potential use cases, and demonstrate competitiveness with state-of-the-art (e.g., in robotics).  
- The theoretical derivations are correct but unsurprising to readers familiar with kernel PCA/ICA and related kernel methods.  
- Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a limitation versus kernel PCA and should be highlighted.  
- Recommend showing results of standard DMD on the toy examples and the Henon map to better illustrate differences.
