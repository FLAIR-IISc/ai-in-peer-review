- Proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS by lifting data with ϕ and computing a finite-dimensional Koopman approximation via Krylov matrices, orthogonalization, and eigendecomposition.  
- Includes a numerically robust POD/kernel-PCA variant and demonstrations on prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition with synthetic and motion-capture data.  
- Strength: Clear motivation for using RKHS features to capture richer nonlinear Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Strength: Well-structured derivations with a sensible Krylov/Arnoldi connection.  
- Strength: POD-based robustification addresses conditioning issues common in DMD.  
- Strength: Broad applications showcased beyond physics (prediction, detection, recognition).  
- Major weakness: Domain/codomain ambiguity for the “Koopman operator in H” — KH as defined maps H-valued functions, not H→H, causing inconsistent use of operators/adjoints and invalid scalar operations.  
- Major weakness: Theorems lack explicit assumptions (e.g., distinct Ritz values, full-rank Krylov matrix, companion-matrix well-posedness); proofs and operator actions need rigorous reformulation (suggest scalar-observable viewpoint g_w(x)=⟨ϕ(x),w⟩).  
- Major weakness: Notational confusion between eigenfunctions φj and vectors κj in H; must adopt a consistent assumption φj(x)=⟨ϕ(x),κj⟩ and align notation.  
- Major weakness: Method appears very close to kernel EDMD (Williams et al., 2015); paper must clarify differences, theoretical guarantees, computational tradeoffs, and include direct empirical comparisons.  
- Major weakness: Incorrect general claim that Koopman eigenvalues lie on the unit circle for attractor systems — must be qualified for measure-preserving/unitary cases; dissipative systems differ.  
- Major weakness: Experiments are largely qualitative; need quantitative metrics (prediction/reconstruction error, eigenvalue stability, noise robustness) and baselines (EDMD/KDMD, exact/optimized DMD).  
- Experimental detail gap: Preimage heuristic for prediction lacks specifics (neighbor count, kernel width sensitivity) and comparisons to regression/preimage methods; motion-recognition/change-point tasks need stronger baselines and metrics (accuracy, AUC, ablations).  
- Quality: Promising central idea but requires rigorous operator-theoretic formulation and clarified proofs; robustification is practically useful.  
- Clarity: Generally well written but Section 3 needs fixes for domain/codomain and overloaded notation; make scalar-observable viewpoint and theorem assumptions explicit.  
- Originality: Kernelization of Koopman analysis is known; QR/Krylov framing and robust kernel-POD provide perspective but novelty over kernel EDMD must be demonstrated.  
- Significance: Could be useful with clarified theory and stronger empirical baselines, but currently appears incremental without clear advantages.  
- Pros for acceptance: Conceptually appealing RKHS extension of DMD, practical robustification, broad demos, and solid Krylov/Arnoldi connection.  
- Cons for acceptance: Operator-theoretic ambiguities, limited distinction from kernel EDMD, qualitative experiments with few baselines, and an inaccurate theoretical claim.  
- Minor issues: Fix Eq. (7) domain/codomain wording, assorted typos/refs, report computational complexity and memory, and give guidance on kernel/truncation p.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject unless operator definitions/proofs are clarified and quantitative comparisons to EDMD/KDMD and exact/optimized DMD are added; could be solid after revisions.
