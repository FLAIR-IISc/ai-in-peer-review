The manuscript, entitled "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis" extended the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The resulting algorithm is then applied to a few toy problems, including a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map. It hence allows to use features map as definition of the kernel, in order to link more the DMD to specific features of interest; it is then a powerful generalization of DMD.

The paper provides a spectral analysis method with the Koopman operator in RKHSs, and develops algorithms to perform the analysis using a finite-length data sequence from a nonlinear dynamical system. The algorithm is essentially reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases of the Krylov matrix in RKHSs and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The authors also consider applications using estimated Koopman spectra with the proposed analysis, which were empirically illustrated using synthetic and real-world data.

The strengths of the paper include the extension of DMD to RKHSs, which allows for the use of nonlinear basis functions, and the development of a robustified variant of the algorithm using POD bases. The paper also provides a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical background and the algorithm.

However, my main concern is that the paper is really hard to read. Even for a few simple notions, the authors emphasize on the mathematical writings when short sentences would have been more than enough. Some sections, on the other hand, are actually verbose. It might make it difficult to understand even for specialists, and a few points should be clarified.

Specifically, I found Section 4 to be partially redundant with the introduction, and Section 5 could have been summarized in the conclusion. Section 6 was condensed too much and could have been developed further.

Minor remarks include a possible typo in the description of the toy NL case, and the fact that the first two problems are actually polynomial.

Overall, I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite the paper in a more pedagogic way, as the resulting article could be highly cited.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper provides a novel extension of DMD to RKHSs.
* The algorithm is clearly explained and the theoretical background is well-provided.
* The paper provides empirical illustrations of the proposed analysis using synthetic and real-world data.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper is hard to read due to an overemphasis on mathematical writings.
* Some sections are redundant or could be summarized.
* The paper could benefit from further development of certain sections.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Simplify the writing style to make it more accessible to a broader audience.
* Remove redundant sections or summarize them in the conclusion.
* Develop Section 6 further to provide more details on the applications of the proposed analysis.
