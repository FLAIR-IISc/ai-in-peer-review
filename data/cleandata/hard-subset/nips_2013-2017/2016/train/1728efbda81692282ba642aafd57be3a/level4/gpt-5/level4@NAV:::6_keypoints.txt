- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform this analysis from measurement data.
- Demonstrates method on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map (Henon), and real-world locomotion data; compares to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA but results are somewhat unclear.
- Positions as an interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition, reminiscent of prior “kernelizing” of linear-operator methods.
- Empirical evaluation is mainly on artificial problems; more evidence is needed to convince ML readers of practical utility.
- The paper should articulate concrete ML use cases (e.g., robotics) and whether Koopman analysis can compete with state-of-the-art methods in those domains.
- The theoretical derivations are well executed but not surprising to readers familiar with kernel PCA/ICA and related kernel methods.
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a limitation versus kernel PCA and must be stated.
- Recommend showing standard dynamic mode decomposition results on the toy example and Henon map to demonstrate differences.
- Paper is clearly written and educational for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts; reviewer is generally supportive of cross-disciplinary introductions.
- Notes that typical ML dynamical-system setups include stochastic components (noise), and the paper should address relevance to or extension for stochastic systems.
