- Proposes kernel DMD: extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition into an RKHS via the Koopman operator to better capture nonlinear dynamics.

- Develops two algorithms: a direct RKHS Krylov/Gram–Schmidt approach and a more robust kernel-PCA–based projection method.

- Shows utility of extracted Koopman modes as features for short-term prediction, embedding/recognition, and sequential change-point detection on synthetic and real data.

- Strength: novel synthesis of Koopman operator theory and kernel methods enabling a rich, implicit nonlinear basis.

- Strength: practical, robust algorithmic design using kernel PCA to mitigate numerical instability.

- Strength: broad applicability bridging dynamical-systems and machine-learning communities.

- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and presents theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) lucidly.

- Weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparisons to baselines such as standard DMD and Extended DMD (eDMD).

- Weakness: insufficient discussion and analysis of kernel selection and hyperparameter sensitivity.

- Weakness: application evaluations are largely qualitative; need quantitative metrics (e.g., RMSE, detection delay/false positive rate, classification accuracy).

- Quality assessment: technically strong and theoretically sound, but experimental validation is not rigorous enough.

- Clarity assessment: outstanding exposition and organization.

- Originality assessment: highly original—principled RKHS-based Koopman analysis is a novel contribution.

- Significance assessment: high potential impact for nonlinear feature extraction, system ID, and time-series analysis.

- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically grounded, elegant method, addresses DMD limitations, demonstrates broad applicability and clarity.

- Arguments for rejection: empirical evaluation missing relevant baselines; kernel choice/hyperparameter issues not addressed.

- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths substantially outweigh experimental weaknesses that can be remedied in a final version.
