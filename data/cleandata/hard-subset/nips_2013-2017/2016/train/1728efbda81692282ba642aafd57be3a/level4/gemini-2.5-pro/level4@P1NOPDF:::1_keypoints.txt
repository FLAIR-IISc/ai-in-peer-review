- Proposes a kernel-based Koopman operator in an RKHS for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems; eigendecomposition shows efficiency gains and the approach is well-motivated and experimentally validated.

- Novelty unclear relative to Williams et al. [35]; authors must clarify key differences and novel contributions (suggest integrating into Section 4).

- Related work is incomplete; recommend citing Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf, and others.

- Applicability to stochastic systems is not fully addressed; needs discussion of expected performance in stochastic settings and ideally a toy stochastic experiment.

- Data-assumption clarity needed: method uses pairs of consecutive observables—elaborate implications for multidimensional time series, weak pointwise relationships between components, benefits of the kernel construction, and underlying causal assumptions.

- Experimental evaluation is limited: pre-imaging prediction is standard; embedding/recognition should connect to time-series clustering and compare to kernel k-means/subspace-kernel methods; change-point detection requires comparisons to state-of-the-art online kernel methods (current baseline is weak).

- Minor issues: check index consistency (τ-1 vs. τ) and correct kernel argument punctuation to k(xi, xj).
