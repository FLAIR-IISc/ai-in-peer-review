- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm using measurement data from dynamical systems.  
- Authors claim relevance to machine learning and test on damped linear 1D dynamics, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data.  
- Locomotion-data results are presented but not clearly interpretable.  
- Work is an interesting extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) and aligns with prior kernelized linear-operator techniques.  
- Experimental evaluation is primarily on artificial/toy problems.  
- Paper is clearly written and educational for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Reviewer supports cross-disciplinary introductions of such techniques to ML.  
- Method focuses on deterministic M→M function decompositions, excluding stochastic components.  
- Reviewer questions prevalence of purely deterministic setups in ML, where stochasticity/noise is common.  
- Authors need to strengthen practical ML utility by: (1) identifying concrete use cases, (2) demonstrating competitive performance vs state-of-the-art, and (3) specifying domain advantages (e.g., robotics).  
- Theoretical derivations are sound but unsurprising given standard RKHS/kernel-method properties leading to eigendecomposition algorithms.  
- Critical technical uncertainty: whether the modal-decomposition algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a limitation that must be addressed.  
- Reviewer recommends including standard DMD results on the toy examples and Henon map to clarify methodological differences.
