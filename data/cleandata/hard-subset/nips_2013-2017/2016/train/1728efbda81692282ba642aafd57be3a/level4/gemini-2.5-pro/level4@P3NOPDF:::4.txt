This paper presents a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis by incorporating it into the framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The proposed methodology is validated through a series of experiments on both simulated and real-world datasets.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, with a clear and logical presentation of its theoretical contributions. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem, detailing their proposed method, and structuring the overall narrative. This clarity significantly enhances the reader's ability to understand the novel concepts presented.

While the submission is strong, its impact could be further enhanced by addressing two key aspects in the experimental evaluation:

1.  **Comparison with Baseline Methods:** The evaluation could be strengthened by including a direct comparison against traditional Koopman spectral analysis methods that do not operate within an RKHS framework. Such a comparison, particularly within the simulation section, would be invaluable for empirically demonstrating the specific advantages (e.g., improved accuracy, stability, or efficiency) conferred by the kernel-based approach. This would help to clearly quantify the benefits of the proposed extension over existing, non-kernelized techniques.

2.  **Analysis of Kernel Choice:** A second area for improvement relates to the choice of kernel function. The performance of kernel methods is often highly dependent on the selection of an appropriate kernel and its associated hyperparameters. The paper would benefit greatly from a more thorough discussion or empirical exploration of this aspect. For instance, an ablation study demonstrating how different kernel choices (e.g., RBF, polynomial, linear) impact the results would provide valuable insights and practical guidance for users of this method. A discussion on the sensitivity of the results to kernel parameters would also be a welcome addition.

In summary, this is a strong contribution that introduces a compelling and well-motivated extension to Koopman spectral analysis. My primary suggestions for improvement center on strengthening the empirical evaluation by including a baseline comparison to non-RKHS methods and by investigating the influence of kernel selection on the method's performance. Addressing these points would significantly enhance the paper's impact and solidify its contributions to the field.
