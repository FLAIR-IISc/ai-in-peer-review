- Proposes spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems by defining the Koopman operator in an RKHS and develops kernel-based empirical methods, demonstrated on synthetic and real data.  
- Primary contribution: a novel, well-motivated kernel construction supported by experiments.  
- Concern about novelty: unclear distinction from Williams et al. [35]; needs detailed comparison and clarification of novel contributions (esp. in Section 4).  
- Missing citations: omit several relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamics works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Stochastic settings: Koopman is for deterministic systems; require deeper discussion of performance under stochasticity and a toy experiment to illustrate behavior.  
- Data requirements and causality: clarify reliance on collections of consecutive observable pairs vs contiguous time series, advantages for multidimensional series with weak pointwise relationships, and explicit causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation weaknesses: prediction via pre-imaging is standard; embedding/recognition should compare to time-series clustering (kernel k-means, subspace kernels); change-point detection should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel methods—overall need more ambitious experiments.  
- Minor issues: paper is clear; fix indexing (tau-1 vs tau) and correct k(xi.xj) to k(xi,xj).
