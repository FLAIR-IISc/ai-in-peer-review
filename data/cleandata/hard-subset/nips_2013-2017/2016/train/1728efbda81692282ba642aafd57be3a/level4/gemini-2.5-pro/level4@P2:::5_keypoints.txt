- Summary: Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS, deriving QR- and KPCA-based algorithms and demonstrating feature-extraction applications (prediction, recognition, change detection).

- Originality & significance: Novel and principled extension of DMD to RKHS that connects dynamical-systems and kernel-methods communities and enables richer implicit basis functions.

- Potential impact: High potential across time-series and ML tasks; frames KDMD as a general feature-extraction pipeline likely to attract broad interest.

- Technical soundness: Theoretical development appears correct; the KPCA/projection variant addresses known numerical stability issues in DMD.

- Clarity & readability: Paper is difficult to read, overly dense with formalism; needs more high-level intuition and pedagogical exposition before proofs.

- Organization & focus: Structure is imbalanced—practical stabilization (Section 4) should be presented more concisely and examples/applications (Sections 6–7) should be better integrated, e.g., use a running toy example.

- Experimental evaluation: Lacks important baselines (notably EDMD), over-relies on polynomial examples and kernels, and should include non-polynomial cases (e.g., RBF) and relate to recent Koopman-invariant-subspace work.

- Conclusion: Strong, promising contribution but requires substantial revision—improved clarity, restructured narrative, and stronger experiments—to reach NIPS-quality.
