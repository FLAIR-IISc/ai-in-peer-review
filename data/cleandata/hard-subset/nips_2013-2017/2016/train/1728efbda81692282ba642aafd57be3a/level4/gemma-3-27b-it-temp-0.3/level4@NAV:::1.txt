This paper presents a spectral analysis framework leveraging the Koopman operator within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, and subsequently develops kernel-based methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems from empirical data. Eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator yields efficiency improvements demonstrated on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The work introduces a novel kernel construction with the potential to significantly advance the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, and the contribution is well-motivated and supported by experimental validation. 

However, I have several points for the authors to consider. The relationship between this work and prior research, specifically [35] M.O. Williams, I.G. Kevrekidis, and C.W. Rowley, *A data-driven approximation of the Koopman operator: Extending dynamic mode decomposition. Journal of Nonlinear Science, 25:1307–1346, 2015*, which already utilizes Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system analysis, is not sufficiently clear. I would appreciate a more detailed discussion clarifying the key distinctions between the presented approach and existing methods, along with an expanded discussion in Section 4. 

The field of kernel-based nonlinear dynamical system analysis is extensive, and the current bibliography appears incomplete. I recommend the authors consider including relevant works by L. Ralaivola and F. d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, and M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf. Furthermore, while the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, I request the authors address its performance in stochastic environments, which are often more representative of real-world applications. A more in-depth discussion, potentially supplemented by a simple experiment, would be beneficial.

The authors’ clarification regarding the estimation procedure (line 216, page 5) – that it requires a collection of consecutive observable pairs rather than a strict sequence – raises a question about the benefits of the proposed kernel construction when applied to multidimensional time series exhibiting weak pointwise relationships. A discussion of this implication would be valuable. Relatedly, elaborating on the causal assumptions inherent in the framework would strengthen the presentation.

Regarding the experimental evaluation, I find the current settings somewhat limited in ambition. The prediction task via preimaging follows a standard approach. The “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” subsection lacks discussion connecting the proposed method to the extensive literature on time series clustering (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods), and a comparative analysis would be insightful. Similarly, the sequential change-point detection experiment would be more compelling with a comparison to state-of-the-art online change detection methods utilizing kernels (given the known limitations of the current method in complex dynamics). More robust experimental results are needed to fully support the claims.

*Minor comment:* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. I noted two minor typographical errors: please verify the indices on τ-1 versus τ, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation on page 7).
