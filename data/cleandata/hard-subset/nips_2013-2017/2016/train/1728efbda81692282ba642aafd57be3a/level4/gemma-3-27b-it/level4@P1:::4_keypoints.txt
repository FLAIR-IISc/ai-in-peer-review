- Extends Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs).  
- Proposes a modal decomposition algorithm using orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Theoretical development is sound and connections to standard DMD are clearly articulated.  
- Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 are helpful for understanding the underlying principles.  
- Includes a robustification strategy using POD bases (Section 4).  
- Demonstrates practical applications: prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection (Section 7).  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Empirical results are illustrative but would be strengthened by direct comparison to non-RKHS/standard DMD (e.g., linear monomials) on synthetic data.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion and justification of kernel selection; sensitivity to kernel type and parameters is not explored.  
- Novel contribution with meaningful potential impact on Koopman-based analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, minor weaknesses do not detract; suitable for acceptance with suggested improvements.
