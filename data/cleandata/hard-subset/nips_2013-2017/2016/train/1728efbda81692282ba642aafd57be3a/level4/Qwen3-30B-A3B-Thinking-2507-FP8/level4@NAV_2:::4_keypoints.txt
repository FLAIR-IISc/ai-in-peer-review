- Extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS and proposes a kernel-based modal decomposition algorithm.
- Uses kernel-based orthogonal bases for the Krylov subspace and eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator.
- Generalizes DMD by replacing linear monomial bases with nonlinear kernel representations to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions beyond local linearization.
- Demonstrates method on synthetic systems (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (state prediction, change-point detection, locomotion dynamics recognition).
- Technical presentation is sound, with clear exposition of the RKHS-based DMD framework.
- Implementation is robust via POD-based projection/regularization (Section 4).
- Empirical validation is thorough across multiple domains, with effective figures and examples.
- Prior work connections (extended DMD, POD, subspace ID) are well contextualized (Section 5).
- Strengths: elegant mathematical formulation, practical robustness, and compelling feature-extraction applications.
- Writing quality is high, with well-organized derivations and clear figures.
- Significance: provides a more flexible spectral analysis framework that generalizes standard DMD while remaining computationally feasible.
- Minor suggestions: include direct empirical comparisons to non-kernel Koopman methods (standard/extended DMD).
- Minor suggestions: explore sensitivity to kernel choice (e.g., polynomial vs Gaussian) and its effect on eigenvalue estimation.
- Overall recommendation: paper is well-suited for NIPS given its significance, quality, and originality.
