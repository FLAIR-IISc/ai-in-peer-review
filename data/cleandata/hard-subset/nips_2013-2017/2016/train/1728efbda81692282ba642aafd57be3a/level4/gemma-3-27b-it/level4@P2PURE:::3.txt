This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator within Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for performing this analysis. The authors also address robustness and validate their approach through several numerical experiments. The overall framework can be viewed as an extension of dynamical mode decomposition leveraging the kernel trick. A key strength of this work is its clear and well-organized presentation. The authors explain the theoretical concepts underpinning their method effectively, and the approach appears promising. The paper is also technically sound. While a detailed comparison to state-of-the-art methods is absent, the numerical experiments adequately demonstrate the utility of the proposed technique. 

However, several points require clarification. The primary concern lies within Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1, which I find difficult to follow. Equation (7) closely resembles Equation (2); the authors should clarify its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as K<sub>H</sub> is a linear operator mapping H to itself, while φ is a function from M to H, and therefore not an element of H. Detailed explanation is needed to resolve this issue. This inconsistency casts doubt on the validity of the proof presented in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the meaning of "by applying the operator K<sub>H</sub> to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, given that both sides of the equation are scalar values and K<sub>H</sub> operates on elements within H. The assumptions stated in Theorem 1 appear strong and lack precise definition. The authors should provide a more thorough explanation of these assumptions. Moreover, additional, unstated assumptions emerge during the proof of the theorem; the authors should explicitly state these and justify their plausibility.
