This paper studies the spectral properties of the Koopman (composition) operator within RKHSs and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm to carry out this analysis from finite data. The authors also describe a stabilization strategy and provide several numerical examples demonstrating the method’s utility. Conceptually, the framework is a kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition. Strengths: The manuscript is clearly written and well structured. The theoretical ingredients underlying the method are explained in a way that makes the approach appealing, and the technical development appears sound. Although the comparisons to contemporary baselines are not extensive, the experiments adequately showcase the method’s value; in my view this is sufficient. That said, a few issues are concerning, detailed below. Major concerns: The main issue for me lies in Section 3.1, especially Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which I find difficult to reconcile. First, Eq. (7) seems to restate Eq. (2); what additional role does it play? Second, Eq. (7) appears inconsistent: KH is presented as a linear operator H → H, while ϕ is a map M → H and thus not an element of H. Clarification is needed. This also casts doubt on the argument on Lines 144–157. Moreover, what precisely is meant by “applying the operator KH to both sides” (Line 149)? If both sides are scalars, KH: H → H would not act on them. The assumptions underpinning Theorem 1 also seem strong and not fully spelled out; could the authors make these explicit? In addition, further assumptions appear within the proof—why are these introduced there, and are they reasonably satisfied in practice? Typos: - Line 55: “the the” - Line 153: κj/λj instead of κi/λi
