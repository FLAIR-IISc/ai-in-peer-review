This paper introduces a novel approach to Koopman spectral analysis by leveraging reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The core idea is to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer, nonlinear basis functions facilitated by kernel methods, potentially overcoming limitations of traditional approaches relying on linear monomials. The authors present a modal decomposition algorithm, theoretically grounded in the Krylov subspace and eigendecomposition, and demonstrate its efficacy through both synthetic and real-world datasets. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The authors effectively connect their work to existing literature on dynamical systems modeling, including proper orthogonal decomposition (POD), dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), and spectral methods. They appropriately position their contribution as extending DMD by offering a more flexible functional basis via RKHS. The mathematical derivations are rigorous, and the explanations are accessible. The inclusion of empirical results with both a toy system and the Henon map provides initial validation of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS is a worthwhile contribution, offering a potentially powerful approach for capturing nonlinear dynamics.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The algorithm is well-motivated and supported by a clear mathematical framework. The connection to DMD and Krylov subspace methods is effectively established.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, making complex concepts accessible. Organization is logical and enhances understanding.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The inclusion of results on both a synthetic example and a chaotic map demonstrates initial applicability and provides some confidence in the method.
*   **Applications:** Consideration of downstream applications like prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection suggests potential impact beyond theoretical analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Comparative Analysis:** A direct comparison to traditional (non-RKHS) Koopman spectral analysis or DMD on the same datasets would strengthen the paper. Quantifying the improvement achieved through the use of kernels would be valuable.
*   **Kernel Selection:** The paper lacks a discussion of kernel selection and its potential impact on results. Exploring the sensitivity of the method to different kernel parameters (e.g., bandwidth in the Gaussian kernel) or kernel types would be beneficial. A justification for the chosen kernels would be helpful.
*   **Computational Cost:** The computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly concerning the kernel matrix calculations, is not thoroughly addressed.
*   **POD Robustification:** While acknowledging potential ill-conditioning, the practical impact of the POD-based robustification step isn't fully investigated. A more detailed analysis of its performance in different scenarios would be insightful.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound extension of Koopman spectral analysis with clear potential benefits for modeling nonlinear dynamics.
*   The writing quality is exceptional, demonstrating a strong understanding of the field and a commitment to clear communication.
*   The empirical results, while preliminary, are encouraging and suggest the method’s viability.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a direct comparison to existing methods limits the assessment of the proposed method's advantages.
*   The discussion of kernel selection and computational cost is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. The core idea is novel, the mathematics are solid, and the writing is excellent. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly through a more comprehensive comparative analysis and a discussion of kernel selection, would substantially strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance, contingent on minor revisions addressing these points.
