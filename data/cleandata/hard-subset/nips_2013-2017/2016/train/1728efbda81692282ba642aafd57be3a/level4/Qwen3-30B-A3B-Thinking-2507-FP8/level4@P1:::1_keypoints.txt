- Paper proposes a novel kernel-RKHS extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions and computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS for modal decomposition.

- Authors present an algorithm performing eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection and demonstrate applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.

- Work is well-motivated, builds on established Koopman spectral analysis, and provides a more expressive approximation of eigenfunctions than linear DMD approaches.

- Theoretical development is sound and experiments (including the Hénon map) show promising estimation of Koopman eigenvalues and useful feature extraction.

- Major concern: insufficient differentiation from Williams et al. (2015) extended DMD — need to clearly state how the RKHS/kernel approach differs in basis expressiveness and mathematical properties of eigenfunction approximations.

- Literature review is incomplete; missing relevant kernel-based dynamical systems works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2011; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2013; Mouattamid & Schaback 2008) that should be cited and discussed.

- The paper briefly mentions stochastic systems but lacks substantive discussion or experiments on stochastic dynamics; authors should analyze or demonstrate performance in stochastic settings.

- Clarify the statement that estimation does not require a sequence but only pairs of consecutive observables, and discuss implications for multidimensional time series and non-pointwise relationships.

- Explicitly discuss and justify the causal assumption x_{t+1} = f(x_t) and how the kernel construction handles violations or weak pointwise relationships.

- Experimental validation is limited: prediction via preimage uses standard methodology without comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

- For embedding/recognition and change-point detection, include comparisons to established time-series/clustering and online kernel change detection methods to substantiate claimed advantages.

- Minor technical fixes: verify τ-1 versus τ indexing in equations and correct kernel notation k(x_i, x_j) (replace dot with comma).
