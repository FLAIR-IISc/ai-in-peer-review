

This paper develops a spectral analysis framework for the Koopman composition operator within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs), introducing a modal decomposition algorithm for this purpose. The authors further propose a robustification technique and validate their approach through numerical experiments. The overall methodology extends dynamical mode decomposition via the kernel trick.  

Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured. Theoretical concepts are presented with clarity, and the technical contributions appear sound. While the comparison with state-of-the-art methods is limited, the numerical experiments effectively demonstrate the method's utility.  

Major concerns: The most significant issue lies in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears redundant as it resembles Equation (2), raising questions about its purpose. More critically, Equation (7) seems inconsistent: KH is a linear operator mapping H to itself, yet φ (a function from M to H) is not an element of H. This undermines the derivation. The proof (Lines 144–157) requires clarification on two points:  
1. The phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is problematic. Since both sides of the equation are scalars, applying KH (which acts on elements of H, not scalars) is undefined.  
2. The assumptions of Theorem 1 are strong and insufficiently detailed. The proof introduces additional implicit assumptions not stated in the theorem. The authors must justify both the necessity of these assumptions and their plausibility under typical settings.  

Minor issues:  
- Line 55: "the the" → "the"  
- Line 153: "κ_j / λ_j" (not "κ_i / λ_i")  

The technical core of the paper remains promising, but the unresolved ambiguities in Section 3.1 and the theorem's assumptions significantly impact the validity of the theoretical foundation. The authors should address these points thoroughly before publication.
