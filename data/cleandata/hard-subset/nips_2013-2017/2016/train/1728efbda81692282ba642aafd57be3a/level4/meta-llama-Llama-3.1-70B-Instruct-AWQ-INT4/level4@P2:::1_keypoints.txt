- Introduces a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in an RKHS (DMD-RK) for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- DMD-RK extends traditional DMD by using kernel functions to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions and capture nonlinear dynamics.  
- Shows empirical efficiency gains on synthetic and real data; contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated.  
- Main concern: insufficient differentiation from prior work, particularly Williams et al. [35]; needs clearer comparison.  
- Literature review incomplete — missing relevant kernel/nonlinear dynamics papers (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Stochastic settings are only briefly discussed; requires more detailed analysis and experimental validation (suggested toy experiments).  
- The claim that estimation needs only pairs of consecutive observables (not sequences) is interesting but its implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations are unclear.  
- Experimental design issues: pre-image prediction uses a standard method; embedding/recognition section lacks connections to clustering/time-series literature.  
- Recommends stronger experiments, e.g., comparisons with state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection.  
- Minor issues: typos in indices (tau-1 vs. tau) and kernel notation (dot replaced by comma).  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but needs clearer literature placement, expanded stochastic experiments, improved experimental comparisons — rated 7/10.
