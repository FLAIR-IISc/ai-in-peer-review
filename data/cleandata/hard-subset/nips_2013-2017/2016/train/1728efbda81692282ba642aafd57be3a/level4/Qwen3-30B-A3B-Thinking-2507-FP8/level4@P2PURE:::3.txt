

This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for this analysis. The authors further propose a robustifying technique and present numerical experiments demonstrating the approach's efficacy. The overall framework extends dynamical mode decomposition via the kernel trick. Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly present the theoretical concepts underpinning the method, which is technically sound and compelling. While the paper lacks deep comparisons with state-of-the-art approaches, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method's usefulness. This suffices for my evaluation. However, several concerns require clarification. Major flaws: The primary concern lies in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears similar to Equation (2); its purpose is unclear. Moreover, Equation (7) appears inconsistent: \(K_H\) is a linear operator mapping \(H\) to itself, yet \(\phi\) is a function mapping \(M\) to \(H\) (not an element of \(H\)). How can Equation (7) hold? This casts doubt on the proof (Lines 144–157). Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator \(K_H\) to both sides" (Line 149) is problematic: both sides are scalar quantities, and \(K_H: H \to H\) is undefined for scalars. The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and insufficiently explicit. The authors should clarify these assumptions. Furthermore, additional assumptions emerge in the theorem's proof—why? Do they hold readily? Typos: - Line 55: "the the" → "the" - Line 153: "\(\kappaj / \lambdaj\)" → "\(\kappa_j / \lambda_j\)"
