- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamics, specifically via the composition x(t+2)=f(f(x(t))).
- Approximates the Koopman operator from collected trajectories using eigen-decomposition, leveraging ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems and demonstrates empirical effectiveness.
- For readers familiar with kernel methods, the main novelty is treating function composition as a linear operator and applying decompositions to it.
- Novelty is limited because the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists; the paper largely kernelizes an existing approach.
- Competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks, change-detection methods) exist, and the applications shown do not convincingly demonstrate unique advantages.
