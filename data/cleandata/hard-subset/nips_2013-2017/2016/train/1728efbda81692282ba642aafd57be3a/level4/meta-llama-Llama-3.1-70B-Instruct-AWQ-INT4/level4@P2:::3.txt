This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences generated from a nonlinear system. The algorithm is an extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) using the kernel trick.

The paper is well-written and organized, presenting clear theoretical concepts used in the derivation of the method. The authors also discuss a robustifying method and perform several numerical experiments to demonstrate the usefulness of their approach.

The main strength of the paper lies in its technical solidity and the clarity of the presentation. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the theoretical concepts, making it easy to follow their derivation. The numerical experiments are also well-presented, illustrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are some concerns regarding the proof of Theorem 1 in Section 3.1. Specifically, Equation (7) seems inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator from H to itself, but ϕ is a function from M to H. The authors should provide further clarification on this point. Additionally, the assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and not very explicit, and additional assumptions are made in the proof. The authors should explain these assumptions and provide justification for why they are necessary.

Minor typos were found on Lines 55 and 153.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria of quality, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method, and the technical derivation is sound. The paper also presents a novel extension of DMD using the kernel trick, making it an original contribution to the field.

However, the significance of the paper could be improved by providing more comparisons with state-of-the-art approaches and discussing the potential impact of the proposed method on the field. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the robustifying method and its effectiveness in practice.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel extension of DMD using the kernel trick.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method.
* The technical derivation is sound, and the numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The proof of Theorem 1 in Section 3.1 requires further clarification.
* The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and not very explicit.
* The paper could benefit from more comparisons with state-of-the-art approaches and a discussion of the potential impact of the proposed method on the field.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
