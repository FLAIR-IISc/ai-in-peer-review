The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," presents an extension of the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the Koopman DMD (KDMD) algorithm. The authors apply KDMD to several toy problems, demonstrating its ability to utilize feature maps as kernel definitions, thereby linking DMD to specific features of interest. This represents a powerful generalization of DMD.

However, my primary concern is that the paper is challenging to read. The authors often prioritize mathematical notation over concise explanations, even for simple concepts. Some sections are verbose, which may hinder understanding, even for specialists. A few points require clarification, making the manuscript too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite the paper in a more pedagogical style, as the resulting article has the potential to be highly cited.

I. MAJOR REMARKS

1. The presentation is overly verbose in some areas and could be simplified. For example, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be condensed into a single, straightforward sentence.

2. Section 4 is unnecessary in a short paper and partially repeats the introduction.

3. Section 5 should be illustrated or summarized in the conclusion to provide a clearer understanding of the results.

4. Section 6 should be expanded, similar to Section 5, as it feels condensed.

II. MINOR REMARKS

1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy NL case, as it appears to be linear.

2. The first two problems are actually polynomial, which is where KDMD should excel, particularly when the kernel can be guessed, as in Reference 1. More details should be provided, similar to Section 5.

REFERENCE
1. Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, "Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control," http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.03007v2, (2015)
