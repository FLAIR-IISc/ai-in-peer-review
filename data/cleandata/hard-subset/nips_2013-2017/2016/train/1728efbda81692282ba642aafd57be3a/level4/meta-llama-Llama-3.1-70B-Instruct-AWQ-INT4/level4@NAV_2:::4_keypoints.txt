- Proposes a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in RKHS with a modal decomposition algorithm.  
- Algorithm reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Returns a finite-mode decomposition serving as feature extraction for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Relates the approach to prior methods such as DMD and POD, highlighting conceptual connections.  
- Claims advantages: captures nonlinear dynamics and better represents Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Evaluated on simulated and real data: toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, and CMU motion-capture locomotion data.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effective estimation of Koopman modes and eigenvalues.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, logically organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Technically sound with theoretical support for the claims.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of Koopman operator and reproducing kernels for spectral analysis.  
- Significant potential applicability across physics, engineering, and machine learning.  
- Suggested improvement: include comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in simulations.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss and analyze the impact of kernel choice on results (empirically or theoretically).  
- Overall recommendation: significant and worthy of acceptance, but would benefit from the two suggested comparisons/discussions.
