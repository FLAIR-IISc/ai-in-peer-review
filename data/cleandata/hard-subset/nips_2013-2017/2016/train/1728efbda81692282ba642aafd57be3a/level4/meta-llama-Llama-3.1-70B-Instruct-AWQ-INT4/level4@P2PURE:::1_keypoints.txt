- Positive: introduces Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel methods, showing efficiency gains and validated on synthetic and real data.  
- Main concern: unclear differences from Williams et al. (2015); request a clear comparison and improved discussion (Section 4).  
- Missing literature: several relevant kernel-dynamical-systems references are omitted (Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Stochastic systems: Koopman is defined for deterministic systems—ask for discussion of performance in stochastic settings and a toy experiment.  
- Data assumption: clarify implications of using unordered pairs of consecutive observables, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations, and discuss the causal assumption.  
- Experiments: settings are not ambitious; prediction via premising is standard and needs stronger validation.  
- Clustering/embedding: lack of discussion and experimental comparison with time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Change-point detection: sequential detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel change detection methods (current baseline may fail on complex dynamics).  
- Minor issues: fix typos—index on tau-1 vs. tau and replace dot with comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on p.7).
