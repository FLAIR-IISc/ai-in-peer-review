- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform this analysis from dynamical-system data.  
- Demonstrates method on a 1D damped linear system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data; compares to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA though results are ambiguous.  
- Provides an interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition consistent with other kernelized linear-operator methods.  
- Well-written and educational for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Experimental validation is mainly synthetic; needs a stronger case for practical utility in ML where systems are often stochastic.  
- Requests clearer articulation of specific use cases and comparison to state-of-the-art methods in target domains (e.g., robotics).  
- Theoretical derivations are competent but not conceptually surprising to readers familiar with kernel methods.  
- Needs clarification on whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations (potential limitation vs. kernel PCA).  
- Suggests adding standard DMD baselines on the toy example and the Henon map for direct comparison.
