- Introduces a kernel-based method for spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS, with an algorithm to estimate eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes from data.  
- Demonstrated on two synthetic systems (damped linear, Hénon map) and one real-world locomotion dataset; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA, but real-data results are inconclusive.

Strengths:
- Presents a well-founded extension of DMD to nonlinear systems with clear parallels to established kernel methods.  
- Well-written and educational, effectively bridging dynamical systems/fluid dynamics and machine learning communities.  
- Theoretical derivations appear sound and are clearly presented.

Weaknesses / Areas for improvement:
- Insufficient motivation for machine learning applications; paper focuses on deterministic systems while many ML problems involve stochasticity and noise.  
- Novelty is perceived as incremental because "kernelizing" linear operators is a common pattern in ML; core idea may not be seen as a major theoretical advance.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: lacks direct DMD baseline for synthetic examples and provides ambiguous results on the locomotion data.

Questions / Suggestions:
- Clarify whether the algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations or can be implemented entirely via kernel matrices (kernel trick); if explicit features are needed, discuss computational limitations.  
- Explain how the framework could be extended to stochastic dynamical systems to increase relevance for ML applications.

Overall summary:
- Interesting and clear connection between Koopman theory and kernel methods, but impact would be increased by stronger ML motivation and more convincing experiments.
