- Proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs).  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm based on orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and Koopman projection eigendecomposition.  
- Evaluated the algorithm on simulated and real-world data, showing effective feature extraction for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Authors discuss connections to existing methods such as DMD and POD.  
- Empirical examples effectively illustrate the algorithm’s capabilities.  
- Lacks comparison with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in simulations.  
- Choice of kernels and their impact on results is not explored in depth.  
- Original contribution: novel RKHS extension of Koopman analysis.  
- Significance: provides a new approach applicable to nonlinear dynamical systems with demonstrated empirical performance.  
- Strengths: novelty, clear exposition, and useful empirical demonstrations.  
- Weaknesses/grounds for rejection: missing baseline comparisons and insufficient discussion of kernel selection.
