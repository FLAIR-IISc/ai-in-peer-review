

The paper introduces a spectral approach for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, which provides a linear representation of function composition. Specifically, the Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the double-step forward dynamics \(x(t+2) = f(f(x(t)))\). Leveraging collected system trajectories, the method performs an eigen-decomposition of the operator via techniques analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The approach is then demonstrated for embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, with the authors reporting strong empirical performance. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the novel contribution lies in representing function composition as a linear operator and applying meaningful decompositions to such compositions. As dynamical systems inherently involve functional composition, this constitutes a compelling application. However, since dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists in dynamical systems literature, the paper's novelty appears primarily confined to kernelizing DMD. Furthermore, alternative methods for dynamical system recognition and change detection—such as recurrent neural networks—are well-established. Consequently, the applications presented lack sufficient force to convincingly establish the method's distinct advantages or superiority.
