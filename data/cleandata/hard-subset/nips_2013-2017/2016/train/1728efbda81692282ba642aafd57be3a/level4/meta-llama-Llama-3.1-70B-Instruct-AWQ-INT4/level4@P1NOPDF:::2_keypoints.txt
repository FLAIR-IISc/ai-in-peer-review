- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems via a linear representation of function composition.  
- Applies the approach to forward dynamics of the form x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))) and uses collected trajectories for analysis.  
- Performs eigen-decomposition of the operator, leveraging ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks and shows empirical effectiveness.  
- Novelty for kernel-method readers is framing function composition as a linear operator enabling decompositions.  
- Limitation: dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists in the dynamical-systems literature, so novelty is primarily in kernelizing DMD.  
- Competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) exist for recognition and change detection, weakening the paper’s practical novelty.  
- Overall, the presented applications are not particularly compelling in showing unique advantages of the method.
