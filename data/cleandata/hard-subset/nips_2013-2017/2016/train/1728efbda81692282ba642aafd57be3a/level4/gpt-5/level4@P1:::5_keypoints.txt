- Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD): extend DMD to RKHS by operating in feature space with kernels, computing Krylov bases, and projecting Koopman operator for modal decomposition.  
- Connects to Koopman/DMD literature and is conceptually related to extended DMD but derived directly in RKHS using Gram matrices and kernel PCA.

Strengths:
- Timely, potentially impactful generalization leveraging kernels to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear bases.  
- Clear conceptual link between Krylov subspaces in feature space and Koopman spectra; theorems relate Ritz values/vectors to eigenvalues/eigenfunctions.  
- Practical robustification via projection onto kernel PCA/POD addresses DMD ill-conditioning.  
- Presents useful applications: short-term prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Work could be broadly useful to ML and applied dynamics communities if presented clearly.

Weaknesses:
- Exposition is overly formal and verbose, burying intuition and algorithmic steps.  
- Paper structure has redundant/misplaced sections and compressed parts that obscure meaning.  
- Empirical evaluation is minimal, largely qualitative, with no quantitative baselines, robustness analyses, or kernel-sensitivity studies.  
- Notational issues and typos reduce confidence in implementation details.

Quality:
- Theoretical framing and RKHS formulation are sound; robust variant is appropriate.  
- Proofs and algorithmic pipeline lack clarity for reproducibility; need concise pseudocode and explicit assumptions.  
- Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate KDMD advantages; require quantitative metrics and comparisons to DMD variants.

Clarity:
- Paper is hard to read due to excessive mathematical prose and occasional overly terse parts.  
- Recommend high-level algorithm overview first, move proofs to appendix, consolidate notation, and provide a running toy example.  
- Replace verbose passages with succinct statements and clearer pedagogical presentation.

Originality:
- Meaningful and timely extension of DMD to RKHS with a distinct RKHS-based derivation and robust formulation, despite relation to extended DMD.

Significance:
- High potential impact if made accessible and validated; applications suggest broad utility beyond classical DMD domains.

Major remarks:
- Simplify exposition, state results/intuition succinctly, and include concise pseudocode for basic and robust KDMD.  
- Condense or integrate Section 4; current length/placement is unnecessary for a short paper.  
- Better connect related work (Section 5) to empirical sections, including illustrative comparisons.  
- Expand Section 6 experiments with quantitative metrics, baselines, and kernel-choice studies showing where kernels help.

Minor remarks:
- Likely typo in the toy nonlinear system description; it currently appears linear—please correct.  
- Emphasize how kernel choice affects performance, especially for polynomial-like systems where KDMD should excel.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually strong, potentially influential extension of DMD to RKHS.  
- Theoretical connections to Koopman spectra are carefully argued.  
- Broad and relevant application domains.

Cons for acceptance:
- Presentation is cryptic and verbose for conference proceedings.  
- Insufficient empirical validation and lack of strong baseline comparisons.  
- Structural imbalance across sections and notation/typo issues.

Recommendation:
- Reject in current form; strongly encourage a pedagogical rewrite and expanded experiments to realize the paper's potential.
