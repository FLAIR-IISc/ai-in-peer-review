This paper proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis, which is reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using synthetic and real-world data. The results show that the proposed method can accurately estimate the Koopman modes and eigenvalues, which can be used for various applications such as prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

The strengths of the paper include its novelty, clarity, and empirical evaluation. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and related work, making it easy to follow for readers who are not familiar with Koopman spectral analysis. The paper also provides a detailed explanation of the proposed algorithm and its implementation.

However, there are a few weaknesses and potential areas for improvement. Firstly, the paper could benefit from a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. This would help to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed method and demonstrate its advantages over existing approaches.

Secondly, the paper could explore the choice of kernels and how it affects the results. The authors use a polynomial kernel of degree three for the toy system and a Gaussian kernel with width 1 for the Hénon map, but they do not discuss the rationale behind these choices or how different kernels might impact the results.

Lastly, while the paper provides some empirical examples of applications using extracted features, it would be beneficial to provide more in-depth analysis and discussion of these applications. For example, the authors could provide more details on how the proposed method can be used for prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, and how it compares to existing methods in these areas.

In conclusion, this is a well-written paper that proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHSs. While it has some weaknesses, it provides a solid foundation for future research and has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
