- Contribution: Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (KDMD), enabling feature-map–based kernels and generalizing DMD.  
- Significance: Using reproducing kernels is a notable and valuable contribution to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Formulation: KDMD algorithm is well-formulated mathematically and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Provides a finite modal decomposition functioning as feature extraction for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrated effectively on several synthetic and real-world examples.  
- Writing: Introduction and mathematical context are clear and concise overall.  
- Presentation issue: Paper is sometimes verbose; Sections 1 (DMD explanation) and 5 (related work) could be shortened.  
- Clarification needed: The “toy nonlinear” case in Section 6 is actually linear and should be clarified.  
- Detail request: The first two problems in Section 6 are polynomial (KDMD’s strong case) and need more detailed treatment, similar to Section 5.  
- Recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions to improve presentation and clarify the noted points.
