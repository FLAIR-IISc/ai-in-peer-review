- Proposes kernelized DMD (KDMD) in an RKHS: build RKHS Krylov subspace from time-shifted feature maps and estimate Koopman spectra via Gram-matrix eigendecomposition.  
- Introduces a robustified KDMD that truncates to kernel PCA/POD directions to mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Theorems relate empirical Ritz pairs in RKHS to true Koopman spectra under assumptions (distinct eigenvalues; eigenfunctions in closure of RKHS span; κj in span(U)).  
- Demonstrates applications: pre-image-based short-term prediction, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection with a CUSUM-like score.  
- Strength: conceptually clean kernelization that unifies Koopman spectral estimation with RKHS machinery; Gram-matrix formulation is practical.  
- Strength: POD-truncated (robustified) procedure is sensible and mirrors standard DMD practice.  
- Strength: Theoretical results clarify when empirical RKHS Ritz pairs recover Koopman spectra.  
- Strength: Applications show broader ML relevance beyond fluid dynamics.  
- Strength: Code is provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Exposition is overly dense and algebra-heavy; high-level intuition (when kernels help; truncation bias/variance) is buried.  
- Weakness: Theorems rely on strong assumptions whose practicality, failure modes, and implications are not discussed.  
- Weakness: Lacks clear complexity/memory analysis (Gram matrix O(τ^2) memory, O(τ^3) time) and guidance for large τ; alternatives (Nyström, randomized SVD) not considered.  
- Weakness: Relation to EDMD and DMD variants is underdeveloped; no explicit empirical baseline comparisons to EDMD with polynomial/RBF dictionaries.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited and mostly qualitative; missing noise robustness, hyperparameter selection, and quantitative metrics (spectral recovery error, prediction error, detection rates).  
- Weakness: Pre-image and change-point methods lack algorithmic details, complexity analysis, empirical justification for approximations, and comparisons to kernel CUSUM/MMD baselines.  
- Weakness: Notation is inconsistent/overloaded and there are apparent typos (e.g., toy system specification, “0.92”).  
- Quality: Core idea is technically sound and aligns with kernel and Koopman theory, but presentation and experimental justification are inadequate.  
- Clarity: Needs major revisions—add intuitive overview, concise algorithm boxes (with complexity), move proofs to appendix, and unify notation.  
- Originality: Moderately original — a natural but valuable generalization; novelty is mainly in RKHS framing and POD truncation rather than entirely new concepts.  
- Significance: Potentially significant if validated, but current empirical evidence is insufficient to demonstrate consistent advantages over EDMD/DMD.  
- Key suggested experiments: head-to-head comparisons with DMD/EDMD variants on spectral recovery, prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection using quantitative metrics.  
- Practical suggestions: discuss kernel/truncation selection and sensitivity, scalability options (Nyström, random features, streaming), numerical conditioning, and data/centering requirements.  
- Acceptance arguments: compelling RKHS-based framework, informative theoretical links, broad potential impact, and available code.  
- Rejection arguments: unclear exposition, limited empirical validation, and underdeveloped practical considerations.  
- Overall assessment: Not recommended for acceptance in current form; major revisions to clarity, experiments, and practical details are required.
