- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick.  
- Authors propose a robustness-improvement method and validate the approach with several numerical experiments.  
- Work effectively extends dynamical mode decomposition by leveraging kernel methods.  
- Major strengths: excellent clarity, organization, and accessible presentation of theoretical concepts.  
- Method appears promising and technical contributions are sound; numerical results demonstrate utility despite limited baseline comparisons.  
- Significant concern: Section 3.1, Equation (7) is unclear in purpose and closely resembles Equation (2); needs explanation.  
- Mathematical inconsistency: K_H is an operator H→H while φ is a function M→H (not an element of H), so Eq. (7) appears ill-posed.  
- Proof of Theorem 1 (Lines 144–157) is doubtful: applying K_H to both sides is problematic since both sides are scalar values and K_H cannot act on scalars.  
- Assumptions for Theorem 1 are strong, insufficiently detailed, and the proof introduces unstated assumptions that must be explicitly stated and justified.  
- Authors should clarify under what conditions the assumptions hold to assess the theorem’s applicability.  
- Minor typos: Line 55 “the the” → “the”; Line 153 “\kappaj / \lambdaj” → “\kappai / \lambda_i”.
