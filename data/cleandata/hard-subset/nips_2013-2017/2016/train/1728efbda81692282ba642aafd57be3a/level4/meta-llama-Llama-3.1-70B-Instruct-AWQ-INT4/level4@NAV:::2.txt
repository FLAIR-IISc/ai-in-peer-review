This paper introduces a spectral approach to analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems by leveraging the Koopman operator, which represents function composition as an operator. Specifically, the authors apply Koopman operator decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). By utilizing collected system trajectories, they perform an eigen-decomposition of the operator, drawing inspiration from kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The proposed method is then applied to dynamical system embedding and recognition, yielding promising results. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the novelty of this work lies in representing function composition as a linear operator, enabling interesting decompositions. The application of this concept to dynamical systems, a specific type of function composition, is a suitable demonstration of its potential. However, it is worth noting that the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, which may limit the paper's novelty to kernelizing existing methods. Furthermore, alternative approaches, such as recurrent neural networks, are available for recognizing and detecting changes in dynamical systems. As a result, the paper's applications, while adequate, do not strongly justify the method's uniqueness and advantages.
