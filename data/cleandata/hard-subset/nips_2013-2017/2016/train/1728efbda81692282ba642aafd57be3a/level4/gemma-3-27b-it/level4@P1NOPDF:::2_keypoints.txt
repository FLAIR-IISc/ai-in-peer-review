- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamics, modeling x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).
- Performs eigen-decomposition of this operator from observed trajectories, borrowing ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, showing promising empirical performance.
- Main novelty is treating function composition as a linear operator and applying decomposition techniques to it.
- Dynamical systems are a natural instance of function composition, making the approach well-suited to the problem.
- Novelty appears limited because the method is largely a kernelized variant of existing Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
- Competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks, change-detection methods) already address recognition and change detection in dynamical systems.
- The presented applications do not clearly demonstrate why this method is superior to existing techniques; results are encouraging but not conclusively compelling.
