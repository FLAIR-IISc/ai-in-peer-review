This paper presents a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis, which is reduced to calculating a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The algorithm returns a decomposition of the dynamics into a finite number of modes, making it a feature extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamical systems.

The paper relates the proposed method to previous work, including dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and proper orthogonal decomposition (POD). The authors highlight the advantages of their approach, including the ability to capture nonlinear dynamics and the provision of a more accurate representation of the Koopman eigenfunctions.

The method is evaluated on simulated and real data, including a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data from the CMU Graphics Lab Motion Capture Database. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relationship to previous work. The evaluation of the method on simulated and real data is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

However, there are a few aspects that could be improved. Firstly, it would be beneficial to include a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method and help to highlight its advantages over existing approaches.

Secondly, the paper could benefit from a discussion or exploration of the choice of kernels and how that could affect the results. This could be explored in simulations or through a theoretical analysis. A deeper understanding of the impact of kernel choice on the proposed method would be valuable for practitioners seeking to apply this approach to their own problems.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is clearly written, and the organization is logical and easy to follow. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of the Koopman operator and reproducing kernels for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The approach is distinct from existing methods, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how their method differs from previous contributions.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable in various fields, including physics, engineering, and machine learning. The results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues, which could be useful in understanding and predicting the behavior of complex systems.
