- Introduces a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) enabling nonlinear basis functions for modal decomposition.  
- Proposes an algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performs eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection.  
- Frames the method as a feature extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamical systems using kernel-based representations.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous and well-motivated, including a key result (Theorem 1) extending DMD principles to RKHS.  
- Includes a practical robustification step using kernel PCA to improve numerical stability.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, testing synthetic systems (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (locomotion, change-point detection).  
- Results demonstrate accurate estimation of Koopman eigenvalues and modes and useful downstream applications.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman/DMD methods on the same datasets to quantify benefits.  
- Provides insufficient discussion and systematic study of kernel selection and its effect on decomposition quality.  
- Theoretical claims (e.g., Theorem 2) rely on assumptions about data richness and kernel PCA approximation that are not thoroughly validated empirically.  
- Presentation quality is high: clear writing, solid experiments, and strong originality and significance for nonlinear dynamics modeling.  
- Main limitation: omissions in comparative analysis and kernel-selection exploration prevent fully establishing the relative advantages of the proposed approach.
