1. Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to compute it from measurements.  
2. Theoretical derivation is technically sound and clearly written, building on RKHS and linear-operator frameworks.  
3. Method kernelizes DMD, drawing parallels to kernel PCA/ICA, but novelty over existing kernelized DMD variants is unclear.  
4. Core contribution’s significance to ML is underdeveloped and requires stronger justification.  
5. Experiments (two synthetic systems and locomotion data) are insufficiently compelling and lack clear superiority over baselines.  
6. Synthetic results correctly identify spectra but do not show consistent improvement over standard DMD; DMD baseline missing for toy examples.  
7. Locomotion experiments produce unclear results with no metrics demonstrating advantage over PCA/kernel PCA or ML-specific baselines.  
8. The paper assumes deterministic dynamics, but ML applications often involve stochastic/noisy systems—this mismatch is not addressed.  
9. Authors must provide concrete ML use cases (e.g., robotics) and explain why Koopman analysis outperforms methods like GPs or RNNs for those tasks.  
10. Important technical gap: unclear whether algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations (hurting scalability) or can use kernel tricks—needs clarification.  
11. Experimental suggestions: include standard DMD baseline, quantify reconstruction/spectral errors for Henon, and benchmark against LSTM/Kalman filters for locomotion tasks.  
12. Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions—needs ML-focused use cases, rigorous empirical validation, and clarity on computational limitations.
