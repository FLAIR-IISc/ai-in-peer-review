The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," presents an extension of the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the KDMD algorithm. This approach allows for the use of feature maps as kernel definitions, enabling a stronger connection between DMD and specific features of interest. Consequently, KDMD serves as a powerful generalization of DMD.

My primary concern with the manuscript is its readability. The authors often prioritize mathematical notation over clear, concise language, making the text difficult to follow, even for specialists. Certain sections are verbose, which may hinder understanding and require clarification. While the content has the potential to be highly cited, the current presentation is not suitable for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. I strongly encourage the authors to revise the manuscript in a more pedagogical manner.

**Major Remarks**

1. The presentation can be simplified in several areas. For instance, the statement "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" can be condensed into a single, straightforward sentence.
2. Section 4 is unnecessary in a short paper, as it partially repeats information from the introduction.
3. Section 5 would benefit from illustrations or a summary in the conclusion to enhance comprehension.
4. Section 6 should be expanded, similar to Section 5, as it feels overly condensed.

**Minor Remarks**

1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy NL case, as it appears to be linear.
2. The first two problems are actually polynomial, which is where KDMD should excel, particularly when the kernel can be guessed, as in the case of the first problem. More details, similar to those in Section 5, would be beneficial.

**Reference**

1. Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, "Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control," http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.03007v2, (2015)
