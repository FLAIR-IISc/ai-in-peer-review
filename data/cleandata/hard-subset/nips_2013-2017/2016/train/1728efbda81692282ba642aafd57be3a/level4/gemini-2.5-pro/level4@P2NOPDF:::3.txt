This paper presents a method for the spectral analysis of the Koopman operator within the framework of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs). The authors derive a modal decomposition algorithm based on this analysis, effectively extending the popular "dynamic mode decomposition" (DMD) method via the kernel trick. The work also includes a discussion on robustifying the approach and provides several numerical experiments to validate its utility.

**Strengths:**

The paper's primary strengths lie in its clear presentation and technical soundness. The authors have done an excellent job of organizing the material and lucidly explaining the theoretical concepts underpinning their method. The proposed framework is interesting and appears to be technically correct. While a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is not provided, the numerical experiments are well-designed and sufficiently demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed algorithm.

**Major Weaknesses:**

Despite the strengths, there are several points of confusion, primarily concentrated in Section 3.1, that prevent a full assessment of the paper's correctness.

1.  **Clarity and Consistency of Equation (7):** The main concern revolves around Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1.
    *   The purpose of Equation (7) is not immediately obvious, given its similarity to Equation (2). A clarification on its distinct role in the derivation would be beneficial.
    *   More critically, the formulation of Equation (7) appears inconsistent. The operator Kʜ is defined as a linear operator from H to H. However, in the equation, it is applied to φ(x). As φ is the feature map from M to H, φ(x) is an element of H, but φ itself is not. This raises questions about the interpretation of the equation. Can the authors provide a more detailed explanation of this formulation?

2.  **Validity of the Proof of Theorem 1:** The potential inconsistency in Equation (7) casts doubt on the proof of Theorem 1 (lines 144-157).
    *   Specifically, the statement on line 149, "by applying the operator Kʜ to both sides," is confusing. The quantities on both sides of the preceding equation appear to be scalars (resulting from inner products). It is therefore unclear how an operator defined on the Hilbert space H can be applied to them. The authors must clarify this crucial step in the derivation.

3.  **Assumptions for Theorem 1:** The assumptions for Theorem 1 are not fully explicated and appear quite strong.
    *   The authors should provide more intuition and context for these assumptions to help the reader understand their implications.
    *   Furthermore, it appears that additional, unstated assumptions are introduced during the proof of the theorem. These should be formally stated in the theorem itself. The authors should also discuss how readily these assumptions might be satisfied in practice.

**Typos:**

-   Line 55: "the the" should be "the".
-   Line 153: The indices appear to be mismatched. It should likely be κⱼ/λⱼ instead of κᵢ/λᵢ.
