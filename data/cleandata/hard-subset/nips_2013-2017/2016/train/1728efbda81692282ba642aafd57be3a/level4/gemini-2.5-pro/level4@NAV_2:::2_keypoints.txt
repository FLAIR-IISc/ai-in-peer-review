- Paper proposes extending DMD to RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions via kernel-defined feature maps, performing eigendecomposition on a Krylov subspace and offering a kernel-PCA robust variant.
- Strength: Elegant, theoretically sound nonparametric extension that avoids manual basis selection.
- Strength: Clear derivation with theorems connecting computed Ritz values/vectors to true Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions.
- Strength: Robustification via projection onto kernel principal components mitigates numerical sensitivity of Arnoldi-type methods.
- Strength: Shows learned Koopman modes are useful for state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics embedding/recognition.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of novelty relative to Extended DMD (eDMD); kernel DMD can be viewed as eDMD with an implicit dictionary and needs explicit comparison and claimed advantages.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation lacks rigorous comparisons to modern nonlinear time-series baselines (e.g., Gaussian Process Dynamical Models, RNNs) for prediction.
- Weakness: Change-point detection is compared to only a single method and should be benchmarked against a broader set of modern sequential detection algorithms.
- Weakness: Insufficient practical implementation details on kernel choice and hyperparameter selection; no sensitivity analysis or guidance for reproducibility.
