This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for performing this analysis. The authors also discuss a method for robustifying the approach and provide several numerical experiments to demonstrate its usefulness. The overall framework is an extension of "dynamical mode decomposition" using the kernel trick.

The paper is well-written and organized, with clear presentations of the theoretical concepts used in deriving the method. The technical solidity of the paper is also a notable strength. Although a deep comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is lacking, the numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method's usefulness, which is sufficient in my view. However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed.

The major flaw of this paper is Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which are not entirely clear to me. Firstly, Equation (7) bears resemblance to Equation (2), raising the question of its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas \phi is a function from M to H, and thus not in H. I would appreciate it if the authors could provide further clarification on this point, as it raises doubts about the proof in Lines 144-157. Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as both sides are scalar numbers, and the operator KH: H → H does not apply in this context.

The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong and not entirely explicit. I would like the authors to provide a clearer explanation of these assumptions. Moreover, additional assumptions appear in the proof of the theorem, which is concerning. Could the authors explain why these assumptions are necessary and whether they can be easily satisfied?

Lastly, there are a couple of typos that need correction: "the the" on Line 55 should be corrected, and "\kappaj / \lambdaj" on Line 153 should be replaced with "\kappai / \lambda_i".
