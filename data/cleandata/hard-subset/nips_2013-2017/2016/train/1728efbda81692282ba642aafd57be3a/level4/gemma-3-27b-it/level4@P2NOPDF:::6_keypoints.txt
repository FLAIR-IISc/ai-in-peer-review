- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform it from dynamical data.  
- Connects concepts from fluid dynamics and machine learning, positioning Koopman operator as a useful ML tool.  
- Demonstrates method on a damped linear 1D system, the Henon map, and a real-world locomotion dataset; compares to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.  
- Results on the locomotion dataset are inconclusive.  
- Provides a principled, kernel-style extension of DMD, aligning with prior "kernelization" trends (kernel PCA, ICA, etc.).  
- Theoretical development is sound but largely anticipated given existing kernel methods.  
- Paper is well-written and serves as a clear introduction to Koopman methods for ML researchers.  
- Lacks a compelling articulation of practical relevance to machine learning, especially given focus on deterministic (noise-free) systems.  
- Authors should explicitly address applicability to stochastic/noisy settings common in ML.  
- Recommend highlighting concrete use cases (e.g., robotics) and clarifying specific benefits over existing approaches.  
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation; if so, this is a disadvantage vs kernel PCA and should be stated.  
- Suggest including standard DMD results on the toy and Henon examples to better demonstrate improvements.
