- Proposes kernel DMD: extending DMD/Koopman analysis by lifting states into an RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Presents two algorithm variants: direct Gram–Schmidt feature-space method and a numerically robust kernel-PCA-projected version.  
- Demonstrates applications: short-term prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamical embedding/recognition.  
- Strength: principled, theoretically grounded extension of DMD that models highly nonlinear dynamics nonparametrically.  
- Strength: novel and relevant connection between Koopman/DMD and kernel methods for the machine learning community.  
- Strength: thoughtful algorithm design addressing numerical instability via projection onto kernel principal components.  
- Weakness: core idea is largely a straightforward "kernelization" and lacks substantial novelty beyond applying the kernel trick.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of distinctions and advantages relative to Extended DMD.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is unconvincing and missing important baselines.  
- Weakness (prediction): no comparisons to standard time-series models (RNNs, LSTMs, GP dynamical models).  
- Weakness (change-point): compared to only one kernel method, lacking broader or state-of-the-art change-point baselines.  
- Weakness (recognition): dynamics embedding/recognition not compared to standard time-series classification approaches.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns from Gram-matrix reliance and lack of computational-complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: little guidance or sensitivity analysis for kernel choice and hyperparameter selection.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly clarify relationship to Extended DMD and whether kernel DMD is a special case.  
- Suggested improvements: strengthen empirical validation by adding comparisons to RNNs/GPs and other baselines.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss computational complexity and propose scaling strategies (e.g., random Fourier features, Nyström); include kernel-parameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and well-written but needs stronger empirical evidence and practical analysis to demonstrate significance.
