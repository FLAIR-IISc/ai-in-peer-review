This paper introduces a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator defined in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), providing a novel approach to analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data. The authors follow a standard pathway to define a set of kernel-based approaches for this analysis, resulting in an efficiency gain in both synthetic and real data analysis problems. The contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated.

The paper's main strength lies in its introduction of a significant kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed method, Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels (DMD-RK), extends the traditional Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by utilizing the expressiveness of kernel functions to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions. This allows for a more accurate representation of the nonlinear dynamics, particularly in situations where the eigenfunctions cannot be accurately approximated using linear monomials.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the paper's contribution is not clearly differentiated from existing work, particularly the paper by Williams et al. [35], which also introduced the Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for analyzing nonlinear system dynamics. A more detailed discussion on the differences between these approaches would be beneficial.

Additionally, the field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is vast, and some relevant references are missing. The authors could consider including papers by L. Ralaivola and F. d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al, Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, or M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf to provide a more comprehensive overview of the existing literature.

Furthermore, the paper briefly touches upon the performance of the proposed method in stochastic settings, but a more detailed discussion and experimental validation would be welcome. The authors could consider including a toy experiment to demonstrate the method's performance in realistic scenarios.

The remark on page 5, line 216, regarding the estimation procedure not requiring a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, is intriguing. However, the implications of this assumption in multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations are not clear. The authors could provide more insight into what is gained with the proposed kernel construction in such scenarios.

The experimental settings could also be improved. The prediction via pre-image approach follows a standard well-known method, and the "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" subsection lacks a discussion on the connections and experimental comparison with the literature on clustering time series. A more convincing experiment, such as a comparison with state-of-the-art online change detection with kernels, would be beneficial to support the development.

Minor issues include two typos: the indices on tau-1 vs. tau and the replacement of the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).

**Arguments Pro:**

* The paper introduces a novel kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The proposed method extends traditional DMD and provides a more accurate representation of nonlinear dynamics.
* The contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated.

**Arguments Con:**

* The paper's contribution is not clearly differentiated from existing work.
* Some relevant references are missing from the literature review.
* The performance of the proposed method in stochastic settings is not thoroughly discussed or experimentally validated.
* The experimental settings could be improved to provide more convincing results.

**Rating:** 7/10

**Recommendation:** The paper has potential, but the authors need to address the concerns mentioned above to strengthen their contribution. With revisions to the literature review, experimental settings, and discussion on stochastic settings, this paper could make a more significant impact in the field.
