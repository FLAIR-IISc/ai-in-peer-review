- Paper develops Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and an algorithm estimating Koopman spectra from finite data using a Krylov subspace, Gram–Schmidt QR, and eigendecomposition; provides a numerically stable kernel-PCA variant and application sketches.

- Positions method as a kernelized extension of DMD/EDMD that replaces fixed dictionaries with an implicit RKHS basis computed via Gram matrices.

- Strength: Clear exposition bridging Koopman/DMD ideas and kernel methods for ML readers unfamiliar with dynamical-systems literature.

- Strength: Mathematically consistent extension with a principled Krylov-subspace view and stabilization via kernel PCA.

- Strength: Algorithmic steps are concrete and kernelizable; demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets (toy system, Hénon map, locomotion).

- Strength: Applications section is thought-provoking, suggesting uses in feature extraction, prediction, change detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Main novelty unclear: need a sharper delineation from EDMD and prior kernelized Koopman approaches beyond “using kernels.”

- Empirical validation is limited and sometimes unclear; real-data experiments (locomotion) lack quantitative metrics and strong baseline comparisons.

- Unclear whether modal decomposition is fully expressible via Gram matrices or requires explicit feature-map computations; clarify which steps are kernelized and give complexity/memory bounds (e.g., O(τ^2) Gram storage).

- Practical benefit for ML tasks not convincingly demonstrated, especially under stochasticity; robustness to process/measurement noise is insufficiently tested.

- Prediction via preimage reconstruction uses an MDS-like approach that can be unstable and kernel-dependent; needs more details, ablations, and error metrics.

- Scalability and conditioning concerns: unclear limits on sequence length τ and effective rank p, and how POD truncation affects accuracy/stability.

- Many results are qualitative; paper should report numerical eigenvalue/eigenfunction/mode errors, reconstruction errors, and classification accuracies with confidence intervals.

- Suggestion: add direct comparisons with standard DMD and EDMD on toy and Hénon systems, reporting eigen/mode errors, reconstruction error, and noise robustness.

- Suggestion: provide quantitative locomotion results (e.g., classification accuracy via kernel principal angles) and compare to DMD/PCA/kernel PCA baselines.

- Suggestion: fully clarify algorithmic pipeline—exact kernelized steps, Gram-matrix usage, regularization/conditioning, runtime and memory complexity, and sensitivity to kernel choice/width.

- Suggestion: discuss concrete ML use cases where Koopman spectral features offer practical advantages over state-of-the-art (robotics, control, anomaly detection).

- Suggestion: expand positioning relative to EDMD and prior kernel Koopman work (e.g., a short comparison table or structured paragraph).

- Suggestion: for change-point detection, provide clearer derivation, justify approximations, and show detection-delay/false-alarm trade-offs versus strong kernel baselines.

- Evaluation summary: derivations are sound and exposition good, but originality feels incremental, empirical support is modest, and significance for ML practice remains unproven.

- Pros for acceptance: pedagogical connection between Koopman/DMD and RKHS, conceptually appealing kernelized algorithm with PCA stabilization, interesting applications, and available code.

- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty vs EDMD/kernel operators, limited and partly qualitative experiments, missing strong baselines, and unclear scalability/robustness.
