The paper proposes a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) for a nonlinear dynamical system, which extends the concept of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD). The authors present a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences generated from a nonlinear system.

The main idea of the paper is to apply the kernel trick to DMD, which allows the authors to work with nonlinear basis functions due to the expressiveness of kernel functions. The algorithm is reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The authors also discuss a robustifying method by projecting data onto the principal orthogonal directions (POD) to address potential numerical issues.

The paper is well-written and organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the theoretical concepts used in the derivation of the method. The numerical experiments demonstrate the usefulness of the approach, and the authors discuss several applications using extracted features with the proposed analysis.

However, there are some points that need clarification. The definition of the Koopman operator in Equation (7) is not entirely clear, and the proof of Theorem 1 seems to rely on strong assumptions that are not fully explained. The authors should provide more details on the purpose of Equation (7) and the consistency of the Koopman operator with the function phi. Additionally, the assumptions in Theorem 1 and the proof of the theorem should be explicitly stated and justified.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of the method. However, the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is a limitation. The numerical experiments are sufficient to illustrate the usefulness of the method, but a more comprehensive evaluation would strengthen the paper.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, but some points, such as the definition of the Koopman operator and the proof of Theorem 1, require more explanation. The authors should provide more details on the theoretical concepts and the assumptions used in the derivation of the method.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a new approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the kernel trick. The extension of DMD to RKHS is a novel contribution, and the authors demonstrate the potential of this approach through numerical experiments.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to impact the field of nonlinear dynamical systems and machine learning. The proposed method can be used for feature extraction, prediction, and change-point detection, among other applications. However, the paper would benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the method and a comparison with state-of-the-art approaches.
