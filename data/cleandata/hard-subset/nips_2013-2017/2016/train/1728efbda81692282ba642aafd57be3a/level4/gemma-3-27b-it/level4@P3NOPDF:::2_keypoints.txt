- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator and kernel methods to analyze nonlinear dynamics via eigen-decomposition of the composition f∘f (x(t+2)=f(f(x(t)))) for embedding and recognition.
- Key insight: function composition can be represented as a linear operator, enabling kernel PCA/subspace techniques to study nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Contribution appears incremental—essentially a kernelized version of existing Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
- Applications (embedding and recognition) succeed empirically but do not convincingly outperform alternatives.
- Paper does not clearly demonstrate advantages over recurrent neural networks or change-detection methods for dynamical systems.
- Lacks detailed comparative analysis to relevant baselines (DMD, RNNs), weakening claims of superiority.
- Strength: elegant and clear theoretical framing linking Koopman operators and kernel methods.
- Strength: methodology is mathematically sound and well-motivated.
- Strength: empirical results show the method performs well on chosen tasks.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior work in DMD and kernel methods.
- Weakness: selected applications are not sufficiently compelling or distinctive.
- Weakness: insufficient comparative experiments to quantify strengths and weaknesses versus existing techniques.
- Question: which specific scenarios or types of dynamical systems benefit most from this Koopman-based approach versus DMD or RNNs?
- Question: what is the computational complexity as a function of kernel choice, operator size, state dimensionality, and trajectory length?
- Question: are there theoretical guarantees on convergence or accuracy, and under what conditions do they hold?
- Overall: technically sound and interesting but of limited impact as presented; recommend rejection with encouragement to add comparisons and more challenging, distinctive applications.
