The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. Specifically, the authors apply the Koopman operator decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics of a system, given by x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). By leveraging ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis, they perform a type of eigen-decomposition on the operator using collected trajectories from the system. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, demonstrating its effectiveness.

For an audience familiar with kernel methods, the paper's key contribution lies in representing the composition of functions as a linear operator and applying interesting decompositions to function compositions. Dynamical systems, being a particular composition of functions, provide a suitable application for this approach. However, since the algorithm, dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), has been previously proposed in the dynamical system literature, the novelty of this paper appears limited to kernelizing it.

While the proposed method performs well in the given experiments, its uniqueness and advantages over existing approaches are not thoroughly demonstrated. Other methods, such as recurrent neural networks, have been used for recognizing dynamical systems and detecting changes. Therefore, the applications presented in the paper do not compellingly justify why this method is superior or unique. To strengthen the paper, the authors could provide more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods and highlight the specific benefits of their approach.
