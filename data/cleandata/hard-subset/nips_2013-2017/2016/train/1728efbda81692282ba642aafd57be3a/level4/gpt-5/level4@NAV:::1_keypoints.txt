- Proposes a spectral analysis framework using the Koopman operator in an RKHS and develops kernel methods for nonlinear dynamical systems, with empirical efficiency gains.  
- Contribution is well motivated and experiments support the claims.  
- Clarify and carefully compare this work to Williams et al. (2015) [35], highlighting substantial differences and strengthening Section 4.  
- Add missing citations and discussion of related kernel methods literature (Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Discuss performance and applicability in stochastic settings and ideally include a small illustrative experiment.  
- Clarify the claim that estimation can use collections of consecutive observable pairs rather than sequences, and explain implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Elaborate on the causal assumption implicit in the setup.  
- Strengthen experiments: prediction via pre-imaging is standard; embedding/recognition lacks comparison to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels).  
- For sequential change-point detection, compare against state-of-the-art kernel-based online approaches, since the current method may struggle with complex dynamics.  
- Overall call for stronger experiments to substantiate the proposed developments.  
- Minor issues: two typos—indices τ−1 vs. τ, and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) on p.7.
