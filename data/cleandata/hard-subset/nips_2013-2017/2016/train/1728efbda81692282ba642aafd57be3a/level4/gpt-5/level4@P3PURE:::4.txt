This paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to a reproducing-kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) framework and evaluates the approach on both simulated and real datasets. The manuscript is very well written and clear.

Strengths
- Clear and careful exposition of how Koopman spectral analysis can be carried out in an RKHS.
- Empirical evaluation includes both simulated and real data, lending credibility to the practical utility of the method.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a direct comparison in the simulation studies to standard (non-RKHS) Koopman spectral analysis methods. This would help quantify the empirical benefits and trade-offs introduced by the RKHS formulation.
- Include a discussion and, if possible, experimental exploration of kernel selection. In particular, analyze how different kernel choices (and associated hyperparameters) affect spectral estimates and downstream performance. A sensitivity study in simulations would be especially informative.

Overall, this is a strong and well-presented paper. Addressing the two points above would significantly enhance the empirical and practical understanding of the proposed approach.
