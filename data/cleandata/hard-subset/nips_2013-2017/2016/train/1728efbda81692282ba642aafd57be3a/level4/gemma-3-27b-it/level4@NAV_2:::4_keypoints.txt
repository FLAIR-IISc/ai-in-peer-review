- Introduces a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), leveraging kernel expressiveness to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.
- Proposes a modal decomposition algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS followed by eigendecomposition.
- Establishes a clear theoretical foundation and effectively connects the method to dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).
- Adds a robustification technique using POD bases to mitigate ill-conditioning in computations.
- Demonstrates feasibility on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing utility for feature extraction.
- Applies the extracted features to prediction, dynamics recognition/embedding, and change-point detection, highlighting practical relevance.
- Paper is clearly presented, well-motivated, and bridges Koopman analysis with kernel methods in a potentially powerful way.
- Empirical results provide initial validation but lack direct comparisons to traditional DMD or other Koopman-based techniques.
- Missing discussion and guidelines on kernel choice and sensitivity, including how kernel parameters (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth) affect results.
- Limited analysis of computational cost, implementation details, and scalability to high-dimensional data despite provided code.
- Strengths support acceptance: novelty, solid theory, practical applications, and a pragmatic robustification step.
- Weaknesses argue caution: insufficient comparative benchmarks, kernel selection guidance, and scalability/complexity discussion.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant potential; accept with minor revisions to address comparative analysis, kernel discussion, and scalability details.
