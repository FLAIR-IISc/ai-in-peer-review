This paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, which offers a linear representation of function composition. Using trajectories collected from a system, the authors perform an eigen-decomposition of the Koopman operator associated with the system's forward dynamics, drawing on ideas similar to those in kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The resulting method is then applied to the tasks of embedding and recognizing dynamical systems, where the authors demonstrate strong performance.

For an audience familiar with kernel methods, the primary novelty is the framing of function composition as a linear operator amenable to spectral decomposition, with a dynamical system serving as a natural application. However, the proposed algorithm is closely related to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD), a method already established in the dynamical systems literature. Consequently, the paper's main contribution appears to be the kernelization of this existing framework, which may limit its novelty.

Furthermore, the applications presented are not entirely compelling in demonstrating the unique advantages of this method. The paper does not provide a comparison against other established techniques for dynamical system recognition or change detection, such as recurrent neural networks. Without such a comparison, it is difficult to assess the practical benefits and uniqueness of the proposed approach for these tasks.
