This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a corresponding modal decomposition algorithm. The authors also propose a method for robustification and provide several numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The framework can be seen as an extension of "dynamic mode decomposition" using the kernel trick.

**Strengths**

The paper is well-written, organized, and technically solid. The theoretical concepts underlying the method's derivation are presented with clarity, making the novel aspects of the work easy to follow. While a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art approaches is not included, the numerical experiments are sufficient to illustrate the method's utility.

**Major Weaknesses**

The primary weakness of the paper lies in Section 3.1, particularly concerning Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which require significant clarification.

First, Equation (7) is very similar to Equation (2), and its distinct purpose is not immediately clear. More importantly, Equation (7) appears inconsistent. The operator K_H is defined as a linear operator from H to H. However, it is applied to \phi, which is a map from M to H, not an element of H itself. Applying K_H to \phi would seem to be a type error, and this point needs to be explained in much greater detail.

This apparent inconsistency raises concerns about the validity of the proof in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the step in Line 149, "by applying the operator K_H to both sides," is confusing. The preceding equation equates two scalar quantities for any given x. It is not obvious how K_H, an operator on the space H, can be applied to scalars.

Furthermore, the assumptions for Theorem 1 seem strong and could be stated more explicitly. The authors should elaborate on these assumptions and the conditions under which they are expected to hold. This is especially important as additional assumptions appear to be introduced during the proof of the theorem itself. The motivation for introducing them at this stage, rather than in the theorem statement, is not provided.

**Typos**

-   Line 55: "the the" should be "the".
-   Line 153: The indices appear to be incorrect. It should likely be \kappa_j / \lambda_j instead of \kappa_i / \lambda_i.
