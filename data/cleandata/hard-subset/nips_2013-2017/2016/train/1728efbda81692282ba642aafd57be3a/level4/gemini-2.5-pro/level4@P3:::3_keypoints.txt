- Proposes "kernel DMD": extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS to analyze Koopman spectral properties using the kernel trick.  
- Computes an orthogonal Krylov-basis in feature space and performs eigendecomposition to approximate Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions.  
- Introduces a robust variant that projects data onto kernel PCA principal components to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Frames Koopman modes as features for downstream ML tasks and demonstrates utility on synthetic and real datasets (prediction, change-point detection, pattern recognition).  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with a logical progression from theory to algorithms to applications.  
- Novel and significant synthesis of Koopman spectral analysis with kernel methods, enabling richer nonlinear basis functions than standard DMD.  
- Technical development is solid and algorithms are well-motivated; experiments illustrate capabilities across diverse tasks.  
- Ambiguity in the definition of the Koopman operator KH in RKHS: unclear domain/codomain and whether KH maps H to H or H-valued functions to H-valued functions.  
- Theorem 2 relies on a strong assumption that Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the span of kernel principal components; authors should discuss when this holds and limitations if violated.  
- Proof of Theorem 2 needs clearer, more detailed algebraic steps translating pointwise eigenfunction equations to the matrix eigenproblem.  
- Insufficient comparison to related methods like extended DMD (eDMD); should contrast explicit dictionaries vs implicit kernel feature spaces and provide direct comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, elegant method; well-written with solid theoretical foundation; demonstrates practical utility and community relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: key theoretical definitions and assumptions lack clarity and justification; deeper comparison to eDMD is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify operator definition, expand discussion of Theorem 2 assumptions/proof, and strengthen comparison to related work.
