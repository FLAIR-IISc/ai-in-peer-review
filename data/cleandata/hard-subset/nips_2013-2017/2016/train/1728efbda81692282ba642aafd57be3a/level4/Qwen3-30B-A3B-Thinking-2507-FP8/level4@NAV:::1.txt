

This paper presents a spectral analysis framework leveraging the Koopman operator within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, followed by standard kernel-based approaches for nonlinear dynamical system analysis using empirical data. The eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection yields efficiency gains across synthetic and real datasets. The contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated. However, several concerns require clarification:  

First, it remains unclear whether the differences between this work and Williams et al. (2015) [35]—which introduced Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear dynamics analysis—are substantial. The authors should explicitly discuss and clarify the key distinctions, particularly in Section 4.  

Second, the literature review omits significant prior work in kernel-based nonlinear dynamics analysis, including contributions by Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, and Franz & Schölkopf. These should be incorporated.  

Third, while the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, the authors should elaborate on performance in stochastic settings (which are more realistic), including a brief toy experiment to supplement their current remarks.  

Fourth, the clarification requested in line 216 (page 5) regarding the assumption that "the estimation procedure requires a collection of consecutive observable pairs, each nonlinearly pointwise related" is needed. Specifically, the implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations and the causal assumptions inherent in the framework should be addressed.  

Regarding experiments, the settings lack ambition: prediction via preimage follows standard practice. The "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" section omits discussion and comparison with established time series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods). For sequential change-point detection, a comparison with state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection (e.g., methods known to fail in complex dynamics) is essential for credibility.  

*Minor*: The paper is well-written. Please correct two typos: (1) verify indices for τ-1 vs. τ, and (2) replace the dot with a comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation, page 7).
