The paper introduces a spectral approach for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The Koopman operator provides an operator-based representation of function composition. In this work, the Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given trajectories collected from the system, one can perform an eigen-decomposition of the operator using ideas similar to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, the method is applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the authors show that it performs well. For an audience familiar with kernel methods, the new contribution is that compositions of functions can be represented as a linear operator, allowing interesting decompositions to be carried out on function compositions. A dynamical system is a particular instance of such a composition, making it a natural application. Since the algorithm, dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, the novelty of the paper appears limited to its kernelization. There are other methods for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, and for detecting changes. In this sense, the applications presented are not especially compelling in demonstrating why the method is both effective and unique.
