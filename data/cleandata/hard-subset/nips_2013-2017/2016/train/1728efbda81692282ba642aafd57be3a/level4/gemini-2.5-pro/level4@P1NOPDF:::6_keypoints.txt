- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a data-driven algorithm to compute it.  
- Demonstrated on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map (Hénon), and real-world locomotion data.  
- Comparison to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA yields inconclusive advantages.  
- Presents an interesting kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition, akin to kernel PCA/ICA approaches.  
- Experiments are primarily artificial; real-data results do not clearly show superiority.  
- Paper is clearly written and has educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Main weakness: weak motivation for the ML community because the method targets deterministic systems while ML often handles stochastic dynamics (process noise, measurement error).  
- Authors need to demonstrate practical utility with concrete use cases and comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., benefits for robotics).  
- Theoretical derivations are correct but unsurprising to those familiar with kernelized operator methods.  
- Suggestion 1: clarify whether the modal decomposition requires an explicit feature-space representation (a potential major limitation).  
- Suggestion 2: include standard DMD results on the toy example and Hénon map to provide a clearer baseline.
