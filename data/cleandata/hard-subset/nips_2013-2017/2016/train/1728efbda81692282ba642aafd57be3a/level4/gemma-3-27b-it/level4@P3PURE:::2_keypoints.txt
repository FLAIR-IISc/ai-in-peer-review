- Proposes a spectral method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator (function composition as a linear operator).
- Applies Koopman decomposition to forward dynamics defined by x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).
- Performs eigen-decomposition of this operator using kernel PCA–inspired and subspace analysis techniques.
- Demonstrates method on embedding and recognition of dynamical systems with promising empirical performance.
- Key contribution is representing function composition linearly and applying decomposition techniques, especially familiar to kernel-method readers.
- Dynamical systems form a natural and compelling application domain for this idea.
- Novelty appears limited because the approach is essentially a kernelized version of existing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).
- Alternative approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and existing change-detection methods reduce the apparent novelty and impact.
- Presented applications do not clearly highlight the method’s unique advantages over existing techniques.
