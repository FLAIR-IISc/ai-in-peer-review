- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform this analysis from measurement data.
- Demonstrations on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA are inconclusive.
- Work is an interesting extension of DMD and conceptually aligns with existing “kernelized” machine learning techniques.
- Experimental validation is primarily on artificial problems.
- Paper is clearly written and provides educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid dynamics concepts.
- Technique focuses on deterministic dynamical systems without stochastic components.
- Reviewer questions prevalence of such deterministic systems in ML and requests stronger demonstration of practical utility for ML audiences.
- Authors should specify potential applications and whether the method can compete with state-of-the-art approaches.
- Robotics suggested as a relevant domain; reviewer asks what concrete advantages Koopman analysis would offer to roboticists or other fields.
- Theoretical derivations are solid but unsurprising given similarity to kernel PCA, kernel ICA, and kernel-based methods.
- Core method essentially reduces to eigendecomposition on finite-dimensional matrices representing a linear operator in RKHS.
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation; if so, this is a limitation relative to kernel PCA and should be acknowledged.
- Recommend including performance of standard DMD on the toy example and Henon map to clearly demonstrate method differences.
