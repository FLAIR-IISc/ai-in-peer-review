- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to estimate it from measurement data.  
- Demonstrated on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map (Henon), and real-world locomotion data.  
- Comparison with DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA yields somewhat inconclusive results.  
- Interesting extension of DMD, reminiscent of prior “kernelizing” of linear-operator methods (kernel PCA, kernel ICA, etc.).  
- Experiments focus largely on artificial problems; real-world utility for ML practitioners is not convincingly shown.  
- Need clearer articulation of practical use cases and whether the method can compete with state-of-the-art in relevant fields.  
- Suggest highlighting advantages for robotics or another concrete application domain where deterministic dynamical models are useful.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound but unsurprising given parallels to existing kernel methods.  
- Concern that the approach assumes deterministic (non-stochastic) dynamics, which may limit relevance to ML settings with noise/innovations.  
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a limitation that should be acknowledged.  
- Recommend reporting standard DMD results on the toy example and Henon map for clearer baseline comparison.  
- Paper is clearly written and provides educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
