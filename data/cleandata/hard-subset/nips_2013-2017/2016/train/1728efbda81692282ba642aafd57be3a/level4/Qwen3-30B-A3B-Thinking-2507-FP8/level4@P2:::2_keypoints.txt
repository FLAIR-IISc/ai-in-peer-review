- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Introduces an RKHS modal decomposition algorithm using orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and Koopman operator projection.  
- Presents a robustified POD-based variant to mitigate numerical instability common in DMD implementations.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with sound proofs linking RKHS-DMD to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized and mathematically clear with consistent notation.  
- Demonstrates applications to dynamics recognition, state prediction, and change-point detection, showing potential practical utility.  
- Builds on prior work in Koopman theory, DMD variants, and kernel methods; the kernel extension is a natural but non-novel progression.  
- Contribution is viewed as incremental rather than a substantial novelty over existing kernel- and DMD-based methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (mostly synthetic and small real examples) and does not convincingly show superiority over alternatives.  
- Lacks thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (standard DMD, RNNs, and other kernel approaches).  
- Paper does not sufficiently explain how kernel choice affects performance or when kernel-DMD is preferred.  
- Robustified algorithm addressing numerical issues is a practical and valuable technical contribution.  
- Overall: technically sound and relevant but limited significance due to incremental novelty and weak empirical validation.
