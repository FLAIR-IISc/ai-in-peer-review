- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and develops an algorithm applied to a damped linear system, the chaotic Henon map, and locomotion data, compared against DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.  
- Core contribution: kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition to capture more complex nonlinear dynamics via RKHS.  
- Well-written and accessible introduction to Koopman operator theory for ML researchers.  
- Strength: Clear exposition that helps readers unfamiliar with Koopman theory.  
- Strength: Novel extension of DMD using kernel methods to potentially model richer nonlinearities.  
- Strength: Theoretical derivations are sound and build on established kernel-method concepts.  
- Strength: Effectively bridges fluid dynamics and machine learning communities.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to simple synthetic systems; locomotion results are unclear and not convincingly superior.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks a compelling articulation of practical ML use cases (robotics, control, forecasting) and their benefits.  
- Weakness: Does not show standard DMD performance on the same datasets, making improvement claims hard to assess.  
- Weakness: Requirement to represent data in feature space may limit scalability and practicality; this limitation is underdiscussed.  
- Argument for acceptance: Theoretically sound, novel, and educational contribution that bridges disciplines.  
- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient empirical evidence of practical advantage and unclear real-world utility, with limitations not fully addressed.  
- Overall: Solid theoretical contribution but weak on practical significance; recommendation is weak reject.  
- Suggested improvements: stronger practical focus, more thorough empirical evaluation including baseline DMD comparisons, and clearer discussion of scalability/feature-space limitations.
