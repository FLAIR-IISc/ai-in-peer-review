This paper introduces a spectral approach to analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator, treating function composition as a linear operator on observables. The authors instantiate this in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space and develop a kernelized dynamic mode decomposition that computes an eigendecomposition of the operator using ideas akin to kernel PCA and subspace methods. With trajectories collected from a system, they form an operator approximation and extract modes, then apply the resulting features to tasks such as embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, reporting favorable results. For readers versed in kernel methods, the key takeaway is that compositions of functions admit a linear-operator view that enables spectral decompositions; dynamical systems provide a natural and useful testbed for this perspective.

Given that dynamic mode decomposition already exists in the dynamical-systems literature, the contribution appears primarily to be its kernel-based extension. Moreover, alternative techniques for recognizing dynamical behavior (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and for change detection are available. Consequently, while the applications are illustrative, they are not especially compelling in demonstrating a unique advantage of the proposed method.
