- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Core contribution: modal decomposition algorithm computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.
- Enables extraction of a finite set of modes for applications like state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Well-situated in prior literature (Koopman theory, DMD, extended DMD, kernel PCA) and clearly builds on these foundations.
- Theoretical foundation is solid with clear mathematical formulations and proofs (Theorems 1 and 2).
- Robustification via POD bases addresses practical implementation issues.
- Paper is well-structured and clear; empirical examples demonstrate recovery of eigenvalues for synthetic and chaotic systems.
- Applications show practical utility (state prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection); locomotion data example is compelling for classification.
- Novelty is limited: kernelizing DMD is incremental and not clearly distinguished from extended DMD and other kernel-based methods.
- Comparative analysis is weak: lacks comparisons to RNNs/deep learning for dynamics recognition and to stronger baselines for change-point detection.
- Practical impact unclear: empirical results don’t quantify improvements over standard DMD or discuss computational complexity and scalability.
- Pro acceptance points: rigorous theory, clear writing, and meaningful extension addressing linear-basis limitations.
- Con acceptance points: limited novelty, weak comparative analysis, and insufficient evidence of superiority over established methods.
- Recommendation: marginal accept contingent on clarifying distinctions from extended DMD and strengthening empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
