- Introduces a spectral method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator to linearize function composition.  
- Applies Koopman decomposition to forward dynamics modeled as x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).  
- Performs eigen-decomposition from trajectories using techniques analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks and reports strong empirical performance.  
- Main conceptual contribution: treating function composition as a linear operator amenable to spectral decomposition.  
- For kernel-method experts, dynamical systems are a natural and compelling application of that concept.  
- Novelty appears limited because the algorithm closely relates to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).  
- Primary advance seems to be the kernelization of an existing technique rather than a fundamentally new method.  
- Lacks comparisons to other relevant approaches (e.g., RNNs, change-detection algorithms).  
- Applications shown are not sufficiently compelling to prove unique advantages over existing methods.
