- Proposes spectral analysis for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.  
- Core method: modal decomposition from finite-length data via orthogonal bases for a Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition.  
- Kernelizes Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to approximate richer, nonlinear Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Positions work within spectral analysis literature (POD, DMD) and leverages Koopman operator theory.  
- Appropriately cites relevant prior work across dynamical systems, ML, and kernel methods.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation and clear connection between kernel methods, Koopman operator, and DMD.  
- Strength: logical extension of DMD to RKHSs with potential to capture more complex dynamics.  
- Strength: algorithm is well-defined, mathematically justified, and derivations are clearly presented.  
- Strength: includes preliminary practical applications and addresses numerical issues (ill-conditioning) with POD-based robustification.  
- Weakness: novelty appears incremental—primarily a kernelization of existing DMD techniques.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation using only toy examples and the Henon map; lacks complex, realistic datasets.  
- Weakness: application section is cursory and lacks thorough benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods (prediction, change detection, recognition).  
- Weakness: related work coverage could be expanded (e.g., kernel subspace ID, Gaussian process dynamical models, neural Koopman/operator learning).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting more comprehensive comparisons to existing methods and stronger empirical validation on complex datasets.
