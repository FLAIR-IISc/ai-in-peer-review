- Proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition: spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS to obtain modal decompositions from finite data.  
- Algorithm builds an orthonormal basis for the RKHS Krylov subspace and eigendecomposes the Koopman operator projected onto that subspace.  
- Uses kernel-induced nonlinear observables to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, unlike standard DMD’s linear observables.  
- Demonstrated on a simple nonlinear system, the chaotic Hénon map, and real motion-capture tasks (prediction and clustering).  
- Strength: elegant, theoretically sound extension linking Koopman operator theory with kernel methods.  
- Strength: clearly written and accessible, with good exposition of Koopman concepts for ML readers.  
- Strength: provides valuable interdisciplinary bridge between fluid dynamics and machine learning communities.  
- Weakness: limited ML relevance as presented—analysis assumes deterministic dynamics and does not address stochastic/process or observation noise common in ML applications.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is shallow—lacks direct comparisons to standard DMD on synthetic/chaotic examples and to state-of-the-art ML baselines on applications.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is moderate—kernelizing linear-operator eigendecompositions is familiar from Kernel PCA/CCA/ICA, so contribution is mainly problem-specific.  
- Detailed comment: need clarification whether the algorithm can be implemented entirely with Gram matrices or requires explicit feature-space representations.  
- Detailed comment: strengthen the robotics motivation by detailing a concrete problem where Koopman analysis would outperform current system-identification or control methods.
