- Introduces spectral analysis using the Koopman operator in an RKHS and follows standard kernel-based approaches for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Proposes modal decomposition via orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection (an RKHS extension of DMD using Gram–Schmidt QR).  
- Shows empirical efficiency gains in synthetic and real-data analyses and introduces a significant kernel construction for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Contribution is well motivated, clearly written, and experimentally validated.  
- Relation to prior work (especially Williams et al. [35]) is not clearly delineated; needs a more detailed comparison and improved discussion in Section 4.  
- Missing relevant literature—suggested authors/papers: Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf.  
- Stochastic setting treatment is brief; further discussion and a toy experiment would strengthen the manuscript.  
- Clarify the claim that estimation only needs pairs of consecutive observables and discuss implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Experimental evaluation could be more ambitious; prediction via pre-image is standard and needs stronger novelty justification.  
- “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” lacks discussion and experimental comparisons to clustering/time-series methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods.  
- Minor issues: some typos and index notation (tau-1 vs. tau) need fixing.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with clear exposition; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
