- Presents a kernel-based spectral analysis using the Koopman operator in an RKHS with a notable kernel construction and demonstrated efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Unclear novelty relative to prior work (e.g., Williams, Kevrekidis & Rowley 2015); needs detailed comparison and clearer distinctions.  
- Omits several relevant kernel–dynamical-systems references (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf); literature review should be expanded.  
- Koopman operator is defined for deterministic systems but the paper lacks an in-depth discussion and empirical assessment of performance in stochastic/realistic settings; consider elaboration and a toy experiment.  
- The claim that only pairs of consecutive observables are needed (not full sequences) needs clarification regarding implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations and how the proposed kernel helps.  
- The causal assumption implicit in using consecutive-pair data should be discussed and its consequences made explicit.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and largely standard; more convincing experiments and comparisons are needed to substantiate claims.  
- "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" should connect to and compare with time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means and subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods, since the current baseline may fail on complex dynamics.  
- Minor typographical issues: check indices tau-1 vs. tau and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
