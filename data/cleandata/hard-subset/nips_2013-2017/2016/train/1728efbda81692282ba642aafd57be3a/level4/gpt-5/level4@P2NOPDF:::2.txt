This paper proposes a spectral approach to analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator that represents function composition. Specifically, the authors apply a Koopman decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given observed trajectories, they construct an empirical operator and perform an eigendecomposition using ideas analogous to kernel PCA and subspace methods. The approach is then used for embedding and recognizing dynamical systems, and the reported results are positive.

For readers familiar with kernel methods, the main contribution is the observation that compositions of functions can be represented as linear operators and thus admit meaningful spectral decompositions. Dynamical systems provide a natural example of function composition, making them an appropriate application domain.

That said, dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and its variants have already been introduced in the dynamical systems literature and are closely tied to Koopman operator theory. Consequently, the novelty here appears to lie primarily in a kernelized formulation of these ideas. Furthermore, there exist other established methods for modeling and recognizing dynamical systems, including recurrent neural networks and techniques for change detection. In this light, the empirical applications presented do not fully demonstrate a clear, unique advantage of the proposed method.
