- Introduces Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), extending DMD to RKHS to approximate the Koopman operator for nonlinear systems.  
- Core idea is significant and promising, with potential for wide impact if presented clearly.  
- Main weakness: manuscript presentation is poor and exceptionally difficult to read, even for specialists.  
- Inconsistent pacing: overly dense mathematical formalism in places and unnecessary verbosity in others, hindering comprehension.  
- Paper structure is ineffective: Section 4 (Related Work) is largely redundant and could be condensed.  
- Section 5 (Kernel Selection) is too abstract and needs concrete examples or visualizations showing kernel effects.  
- Section 6 (Experiments) is overly condensed and should be expanded to link theoretical kernel choices to empirical results.  
- Minor issue: probable typo in the "NL case" toy problem where dynamics appear linear.  
- Missed opportunity: experiments use polynomial systems but do not explicitly justify or discuss kernel choices (e.g., polynomial kernels) linking theory to practice.  
- Recommendation: substantial revision focused on pedagogical clarity and logical flow; a clearer exposition would likely yield a highly impactful paper.
