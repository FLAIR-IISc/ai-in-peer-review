The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," presents a valuable extension of dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in a novel algorithm (KDMD). This work effectively enables the use of feature maps to define the kernel, thereby strengthening the connection between DMD and specific features of interest – a powerful generalization of the original method. 

However, the paper’s current presentation significantly hinders its accessibility. The authors frequently employ overly complex mathematical formulations where concise explanations would suffice, while other sections are unnecessarily verbose. This combination creates a challenging reading experience, even for researchers familiar with the field, and obscures key concepts. As such, the manuscript is not currently suitable for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings due to its lack of clarity. 

Despite this, I strongly encourage the authors to revise the manuscript with a focus on pedagogical clarity. A more accessible presentation would likely result in a highly cited and impactful publication.

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**

1. The presentation often lacks conciseness. For example, the statement: "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point," could be conveyed with a much simpler sentence.
2. Section 4 is redundant given the paper’s length and largely repeats information already presented in the introduction.
3. The results in Section 5 would benefit from illustrative examples or a concise summary within the conclusion.
4. Section 6 feels underdeveloped and would be improved by expanding upon it in a manner similar to Section 5. The current condensation obscures important details.

**II. MINOR REMARKS**

1. There appears to be a typographical error in the description of the nonlinear toy problem, as it appears to be a linear system.
2. The first two test problems are polynomial, representing a scenario where KDMD should excel – particularly when the kernel can be appropriately chosen, as in problem 1. Further details and analysis, similar to those found in Section 5, would be beneficial.
