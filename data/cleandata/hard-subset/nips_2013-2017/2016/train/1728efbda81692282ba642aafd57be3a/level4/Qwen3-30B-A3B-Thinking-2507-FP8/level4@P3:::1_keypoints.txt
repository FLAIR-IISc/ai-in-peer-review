- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm that approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel-based nonlinear basis functions.  
- Uses orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Builds on Williams et al. (2015) extended DMD but replaces pre-determined linear bases with kernel methods to capture more complex nonlinearities.  
- Mathematical derivations are correct and the algorithmic procedure is clearly presented.  
- Empirical results show improved eigenvalue estimation versus standard DMD on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map.  
- Demonstrates applications to prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection, showing utility of extracted features.  
- Originality is conceptually novel but needs clearer comparison to Williams et al. (2015) to delineate differences and improvements.  
- Missing several relevant references in kernel-based dynamical systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al.).  
- Experimental validation is underpowered — prediction via preimage follows standard kernel methods and lacks clear advantage over existing techniques.  
- Dynamics recognition experiments lack comparisons to established time-series clustering methods (kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Change-point detection should be compared against state-of-the-art kernel-based online change detection methods.  
- Section 4 (robustification with POD bases) needs more detailed discussion of when it offers significant benefits over the basic algorithm.  
- Brief mention of stochastic settings is insufficient; the method's extension to stochastic systems requires more discussion and a toy experiment.  
- Specific requested revisions: clarify differences from Williams et al., add missing kernel-dynamics references, expand stochastic discussion with example, include comparative experiments for clustering/change-point detection, and fix minor notation issues.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-organized, and novel, but significance would be substantially enhanced by deeper comparisons and stronger experiments; recommend weak acceptance pending revisions.
