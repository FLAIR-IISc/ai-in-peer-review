- Presents a spectral analysis framework using the Koopman operator in an RKHS and a suite of kernel-based methods for nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Provides a notable kernel construction and reports efficiency gains from eigendecomposition on synthetic and real-world data, with experimental validation.
- Unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015); requests a detailed comparison and expanded discussion in Section 4.
- Several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems references are missing (Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).
- Needs a more thorough discussion (and possibly a toy experiment) on applying the Koopman operator to stochastic systems common in real-world settings.
- The claim that estimation can use pairs of consecutive observables needs clarification, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations; explain advantages of the kernel and causal assumptions.
- Experimental scope is limited: prediction via pre-imaging is standard; embedding/recognition should connect to time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels).
- Sequential change-point detection should be compared against state-of-the-art online kernel change-detection methods; overall experiments should be more ambitious to better support claims.
- Minor typographical issues: verify indices for τ-1 versus τ and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi, xj) (last equation on page 7).
