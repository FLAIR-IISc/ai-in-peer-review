- Positive summary: Proposes a Koopman-operator spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel-based estimators and eigendecomposition that yield efficiency gains and empirical validation.  
- Relation to prior work (Williams et al., 2015): Unclear what the substantive differences are—request explicit comparison of assumptions, kernel construction, projection/eigendecomposition methodology, and theoretical/empirical consequences (add this in Section 4).  
- Missing literature: Several relevant kernel-for-dynamical-systems works (e.g., Ralaivola, d’Alché-Buc, Tuia, Rojo-Álvarez, Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf) should be cited and the contribution positioned relative to their goals and techniques.  
- Stochastic settings: Needs a deeper discussion of behavior under measurement/process noise or stochastic transitions and, if possible, a toy experiment contrasting deterministic vs stochastic regimes.  
- Estimation setup and causal assumptions: Clarify implications of using pairs of consecutive observables for multivariate time series, handling weak/heterogeneous instantaneous dependence, and assumptions about directionality, stationarity, or Markovianity affecting identifiability and performance.  
- Experimental evaluation: Experiments are limited—pre-imaging is standard; include comparisons to time-series kernel clustering methods and state-of-the-art kernel-based online change-point detection methods to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Minor fixes: Paper is clear but check indices τ−1 vs τ and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi, xj).
