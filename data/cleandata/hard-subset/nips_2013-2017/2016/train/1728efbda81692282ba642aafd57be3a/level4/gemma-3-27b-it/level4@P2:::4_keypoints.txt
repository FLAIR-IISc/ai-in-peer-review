- Proposes extending Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS and kernel methods to approximate richer, nonlinear eigenfunction bases.  
- Presents a modal decomposition algorithm grounded in Krylov subspace theory and eigendecomposition, validated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly presented, and well-connected to existing literature (POD, DMD, spectral methods).  

Strengths:
- Novel extension of Koopman analysis to RKHS, enabling capture of more nonlinear dynamics.  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous and effectively links to DMD and Krylov methods.  
- Explanations and organization make complex concepts accessible.  
- Empirical validation on a toy system and the Henon map demonstrates initial applicability.  
- Discusses potential downstream applications (prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection).  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks direct quantitative comparison to traditional Koopman/DMD on the same datasets.  
- No discussion or sensitivity analysis of kernel selection and parameter choices.  
- Computational complexity, especially kernel matrix costs, is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Practical impact of the POD-based robustification is not fully investigated.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, theoretically sound contribution with potential impact on nonlinear dynamics modeling.  
- Excellent writing and encouraging preliminary empirical results.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient direct comparisons to existing methods and limited discussion of kernel choice and computational cost.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing comparative analysis and kernel/complexity discussions.
