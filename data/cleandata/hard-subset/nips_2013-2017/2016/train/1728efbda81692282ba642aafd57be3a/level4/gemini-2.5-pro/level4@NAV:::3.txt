This paper develops a spectral analysis of the composition (Koopman) operator within Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces an accompanying modal decomposition algorithm. The authors also propose a method for enhancing robustness and provide several numerical experiments to validate their framework, which can be viewed as a kernel-based extension of Dynamical Mode Decomposition (DMD).

**Strengths:**
The manuscript is exceptionally well-structured and clearly written. The authors do an excellent job of elucidating the underlying theoretical concepts that form the basis of their method, which I find compelling. The work is technically sound. While a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is not included, the provided numerical experiments are sufficient to effectively demonstrate the utility of the proposed technique.

**Major Flaws:**
My primary concerns lie with Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which I found difficult to follow.

First, Equation (7) bears a strong resemblance to Equation (2). Could the authors clarify the distinct purpose of introducing Equation (7)? Second, there appears to be a consistency issue in Equation (7). The operator KH is defined as a linear operator from H to itself, but the function \phi maps from M to H, meaning it does not belong to the domain of KH. A more detailed explanation of this formulation is needed. These ambiguities raise questions about the subsequent proof in Lines 144-157.

Furthermore, on Line 149, the text states "by applying the operator KH to both sides." It is unclear how this is possible, as both sides of the equation in question are scalars, while KH is an operator on the space H. This step requires clarification.

The assumptions for Theorem 1 seem rather strong and are not explicitly detailed. Could the authors provide more context and intuition for these conditions? I also noted that additional assumptions are introduced within the proof of the theorem. It would be helpful if these were stated upfront in the theorem itself, along with a discussion of how readily they might be satisfied in practice.

**Typos:**
*   Line 55: "the the" should be corrected to "the".
*   Line 153: There appears to be a subscript mismatch. It seems "\kappaj / \lambdaj" should be "\kappai / \lambda_i".
