

This paper develops a spectral analysis framework for the Koopman operator within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs), introducing a modal decomposition algorithm and a robustifying technique. The approach extends dynamical mode decomposition via the kernel trick, with numerical experiments demonstrating its utility. The paper is well-written, logically structured, and technically sound, with clear exposition of theoretical foundations. While the numerical results adequately illustrate the method's value, the absence of deep comparisons with state-of-the-art methods is acceptable given the focus on the proposed framework.

**Strengths:** The theoretical contributions are presented with clarity and rigor. The derivation of the modal decomposition algorithm is well-motivated and accessible, making the core ideas compelling. The technical content is robust, and the experimental validation supports the method's practical relevance without overclaiming.

**Major Concerns:** The primary issue lies in Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1 (Lines 144–157). The purpose of Equation (7) is unclear, as it appears structurally identical to Equation (2). More critically, Equation (7) contains a fundamental inconsistency: the Koopman operator \(K_H\) maps \(\mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}\), yet the equation applies \(K_H\) to \(\phi\) (a function from the state space \(M\) to \(\mathcal{H}\)), which is not an element of \(\mathcal{H}\). This invalidates the step "by applying the operator \(K_H\) to both sides" (Line 149), as both sides of the equation are scalars, and \(K_H\) cannot act on scalars. The assumptions underpinning Theorem 1 are also problematic. They are stated vaguely (e.g., "sufficiently regular") and appear incomplete, as additional unstated assumptions arise during the proof. The authors must clarify: (1) why Equation (7) is necessary given Equation (2), (2) the precise domain of \(K_H\) in the context of Equation (7), and (3) the conditions under which the theorem's assumptions hold. Without resolution, the theoretical foundation lacks rigor.

**Minor Issues:**  
- Line 55: "the the" → "the"  
- Line 153: "\(\kappaj / \lambdaj\)" → "\(\kappa_j / \lambda_j\)" (consistent indexing)  

**Overall Assessment:** The paper presents an interesting extension of dynamical mode decomposition with a clear experimental validation. However, the unresolved mathematical inconsistencies in Section 3.1 undermine confidence in the core theoretical claims. Addressing these concerns—particularly the domain error in Equation (7) and the explicit justification of Theorem 1's assumptions—is essential for the work to be considered sound. The technical contributions have potential, but the current presentation requires significant revision to ensure mathematical correctness. The minor typos should also be corrected.
