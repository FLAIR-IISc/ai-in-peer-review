- Paper proposes extending Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS, using kernel-defined features and Krylov/POD projections to compute Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions.  
- Also introduces a robust POD-based algorithm and shows applications for prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection on synthetic and real data.

- Strength: Novel, principled extension of DMD/Koopman theory into RKHS with solid theoretical results (Theorems 1–2).  
- Strength: Clear, well-written exposition and easy-to-follow algorithm descriptions.  
- Strength: Significant idea—using Koopman eigenfunctions as feature extraction bridging modal decomposition and machine learning.

- Weakness: Insufficient distinction from Extended DMD (eDMD); need clearer conceptual and algorithmic differences despite the implicit kernel dictionary.  
- Weakness: Experimental validation is limited—needs comparisons to established baselines in time-series clustering/classification and recent online change-point detection methods.  
- Weakness: Prediction via pre-imaging is standard in kernel methods and not novel.  
- Weakness: Paper focuses on deterministic systems; lacks analysis and experiments on stochastic/noisy settings which are important in practice.  
- Weakness: Limited positioning within broader kernel methods literature—several relevant prior works are not discussed.  
- Weakness: The remark that the method works with non-contiguous (x_t, x_{t+1}) pairs is underexplored; implications and causal assumptions need clarification.

- Conclusion: Method is well-motivated and technically sound but would be stronger with clearer novelty vs eDMD, more comprehensive empirical comparisons, and robustness analysis to stochasticity.

- Minor: Manuscript overall excellent; fix indexing inconsistencies (τ vs τ-1) and a kernel notation typo k(xj, xi).
