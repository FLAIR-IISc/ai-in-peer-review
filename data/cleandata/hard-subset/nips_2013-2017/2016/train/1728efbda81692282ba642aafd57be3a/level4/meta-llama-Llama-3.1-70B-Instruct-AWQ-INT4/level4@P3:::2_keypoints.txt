- Proposes DMD-RK: a spectral method using Koopman operator in RKHS to kernelize Dynamic Mode Decomposition for richer Koopman eigenfunction approximation.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction to the Koopman operator and its role in analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Includes a thorough review of related work, covering DMD variants and other spectral analysis methods.  
- Method is well-motivated and clearly explained, emphasizing the expressive power of kernel functions.  
- Presents a robust algorithm variant that handles ill-conditioned data via projection onto principal orthogonal directions.  
- Empirical examples show DMD-RK can accurately estimate Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions and support prediction, embedding, and recognition tasks.  
- Strength: clarity and presentation, including motivation, method description, and empirical demonstrations.  
- Weakness: novelty may be limited, essentially kernelizing existing DMD rather than introducing fundamentally new theory.  
- Weakness: presented applications (embedding, recognition) may not compellingly demonstrate unique advantages of the method.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art spectral analysis methods for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Conference scoring: strong on quality, clarity, and originality, but significance is limited without stronger applications/comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to add more compelling applications and comparative evaluations against baselines.
