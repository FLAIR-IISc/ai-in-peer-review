- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes an algorithm to perform this from measurement data.  
- Experiments on a damped 1D linear system, a chaotic map, and locomotion data are presented, but empirical results are somewhat unclear.  
- Conceptually an interesting kernelized extension of DMD; paper is well written and educational for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics tools.  
- Method targets deterministic maps (no stochastic components), which may limit relevance to mainstream ML that often models noise and randomness.  
- Paper needs to better justify practical utility and provide concrete use cases where the approach is advantageous and competitive.  
- Ask for clarification of benefits to robotics practitioners or, if not robotics, identification of an application domain where assumptions match practice.  
- Theoretical derivations are careful but expected given connections to kernel PCA/ICA and other kernel methods.  
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a limitation versus kernel PCA and should be discussed.  
- Empirical section should include standard DMD on the toy linear system and Hénon map to clarify contributions of the kernelized Koopman approach.  
- Recommendations: sharpen empirical comparisons, articulate compelling application domains and advantages over stochastic ML methods, and clarify implementation/feature-space details.
