- Presents a method for Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS, generalizing DMD to nonlinear function spaces via kernels.  
- Computes modal decomposition by eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projected onto an RKHS Krylov subspace.  
- Introduces a numerically robust variant that projects data onto kernel PCA (POD) directions before decomposition.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to DMD variants, POD, and subspace identification methods.  
- Technically sound with rigorous theoretical development; Theorem 1 supports the direct kernel DMD method.  
- Theorem 2 justifies the robustified (kernel-PCA) algorithm, addressing practical implementation issues.  
- Experimental results are illustrative on synthetic and real-world problems, though not exhaustive.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically organized with transparent derivations and algorithms.  
- Original and well-motivated extension of DMD to RKHSs, distinct from extended DMD approaches.  
- Potentially significant impact as a feature-extraction tool for complex nonlinear dynamics across ML and scientific domains.  
- Strengths: elegant theory, practical robustness via kernel PCA, thorough presentation, and diverse application demos.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: lacks direct empirical comparisons to baselines (standard/extended DMD) to quantify gains.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: missing detailed discussion and sensitivity analysis on kernel choice and hyperparameter selection.
