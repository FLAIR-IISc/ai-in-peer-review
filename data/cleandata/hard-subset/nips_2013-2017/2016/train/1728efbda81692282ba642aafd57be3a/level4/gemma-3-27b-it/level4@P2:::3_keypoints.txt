- Introduces a spectral analysis method for the Koopman operator in RKHS and a corresponding modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick.  
- Extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to RKHS, allowing richer nonlinear basis functions via kernels.  
- Situates work within Koopman/DMD literature and connects to spectral methods, POD, system identification, and kernel PCA.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and accessible.  
- Technical development appears sound and logically presented.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the method’s utility for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Novel contribution: extending DMD to RKHS is original and promising for nonlinear systems.  
- Potentially significant for extracting meaningful features from nonlinear data and broad applicability.  
- Major theoretical concern: Equation (7) is unclear and seems redundant with Equation (2).  
- Critical issue in Theorem 1 proof: application of operator KH to scalars is questionable and may be invalid.  
- Theorem 1 relies on strong, insufficiently explained assumptions and introduces unstated assumptions during the proof.  
- Need clearer explanation of the feature map ϕ and its relationship to the RKHS and implementation.  
- Connection between theoretical framework and practical implementation needs strengthening.  
- Limited comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods; potential numerical instability remains despite robustness enhancements.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to clarify Equation (7), fix Theorem 1 proof/assumptions, and add comparative analysis.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors (Line 55: "the the"; Line 153: notation typo "\kappaj / \lambdaj" → "\kappai / \lambda_i").
