- Proposes kernelized Koopman spectral analysis: project Koopman operator into RKHS using a Krylov/QR construction with kernel evaluations to compute empirical eigenvalues and modes (DMD analogue).
- Introduces a robust variant projecting data onto top kernel-PCA directions before estimating the reduced operator and proves recovery under strong assumptions.
- Positions work within Koopman/DMD and kernel literature; notes close relation to EDMD where the kernel implicitly defines the dictionary.
- Empirical demonstrations on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map show qualitative eigenvalue recovery and sketch applications: preimage-based prediction, pattern recognition, and CUSUM-style change detection in RKHS.
- Theoretical soundness: derivations are clear and correct under stated assumptions, but key assumptions (eigenfunctions lying in feature spans) are strong and lack approximation/error analysis.
- Numerical stability: correctly identifies Gram–Schmidt ill-conditioning and uses kernel-PCA projection, but needs explicit discussion of regularization (Tikhonov/truncated SVD) and quantification of truncation effects.
- Empirical support is limited: no quantitative benchmarks versus DMD/EDMD/optimized DMD, no sensitivity analysis to noise or kernel hyperparameters, and application metrics are missing.
- Clarity: generally well written with minor editorial and notational issues; should better explain the QR-to-companion-matrix link and provide time/memory complexity analysis for scalability.
- Originality: a natural but nontrivial kernel extension; the robustified method closely resembles EDMD and requires clearer distinction in estimator, assumptions, and behavior plus empirical comparison.
- Significance: potentially impactful if augmented with stronger experiments, scalability strategies (Nyström/random features), and guidance on kernel choice and hyperparameter tuning.
- Specific suggested additions: quantitative baselines and RMSE/metric evaluations, ablation of robustification/truncation, kernel sensitivity and selection guidance, complexity/memory analysis, noise experiments, and details on the preimage algorithm and its runtime/accuracy.
- Pros: principled RKHS extension of Koopman/DMD, solid theoretical ties to Arnoldi/DMD and kernel PCA, broad potential applications, readable presentation, and available code.
- Cons: weak empirical evaluation and comparisons, insufficient kernel/hyperparameter discussion, strong unaddressed theoretical assumptions, and limited discussion of scalability and preimage details.
- Recommendation: lean weak accept conditional on adding quantitative DMD/EDMD comparisons, kernel robustness analysis, and clearer scalability/implementation details.
