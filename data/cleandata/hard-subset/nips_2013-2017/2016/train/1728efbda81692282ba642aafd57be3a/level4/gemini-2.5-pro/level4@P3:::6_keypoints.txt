- Paper introduces a kernelized method for Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS, extending DMD to nonlinear settings via the kernel trick.  
- Core contributions: a theoretically-grounded RKHS Gram-Schmidt algorithm and a practical kernel-PCA-projected variant.  
- Demonstrations on synthetic systems (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and real-world motion-capture tasks (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition).

- Strength: Well-written, clear derivations connecting DMD, Krylov subspaces, and the Koopman operator with strong pedagogical value.  
- Strength: Novel and natural synthesis of Koopman spectral analysis with kernel methods, bringing nonlinear representational power to DMD.  
- Strength: Successfully bridges fluid-dynamics tools and ML, potentially inspiring cross-disciplinary work.

- Weakness: Limited motivation and justification for mainstream ML, as the method assumes deterministic dynamics while many ML approaches model stochasticity.  
- Weakness: Conceptual novelty is incremental—kernelizing linear methods follows a well-trodden pattern in ML.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is weak and unconvincing.

- Experimental weakness: No baseline comparison to standard DMD on synthetic tasks to quantify the kernel method's advantage.  
- Experimental weakness: Real-world applications lack quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art methods in change-point detection and time-series classification.

- Detailed question: Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space basis representations instead of operating only via Gram matrices—needs clarification.  
- Detailed comment: Real-world applications feel disconnected from domain state-of-the-art; the paper should show specific advantages over established models in robotics or forecasting.

- For acceptance: Technically sound kernel extension of DMD with clear exposition and pedagogical contribution.  
- For acceptance: Offers a principled approach for nonlinear feature extraction from dynamical systems.

- Against acceptance: Insufficient motivation/significance for ML audiences given common stochastic assumptions in dynamical modeling.  
- Against acceptance: Weak experimental support lacking direct DMD baselines and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Against acceptance: Perceived as incremental due to the familiar kernelization pattern.
