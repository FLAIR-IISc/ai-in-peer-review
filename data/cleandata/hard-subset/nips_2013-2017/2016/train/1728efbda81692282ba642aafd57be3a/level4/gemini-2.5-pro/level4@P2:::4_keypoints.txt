- Introduces kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, extending DMD with the kernel trick; proposes two algorithms (feature-space Gram–Schmidt and POD-based robust variant) and demonstrates on prediction, change-point detection, and motion-capture recognition.

- Originality/significance: elegant fusion of Koopman operator theory and kernel methods, framing the approach as a general feature extractor for dynamical systems beyond fluid dynamics.

- Clarity/organization: paper is exceptionally well-written with clear theoretical development and a well-motivated POD-based robustness fix.

- Technical quality: derivations appear sound; kernel PCA–based robust algorithm addresses numerical stability and applications show versatility of Koopman modes as features.

- Weakness: lacks direct empirical comparisons to standard DMD and variants (e.g., Extended DMD) and quantitative benchmarks (e.g., on chaotic systems) to validate superiority.

- Weakness: kernel selection and hyperparameter sensitivity are not addressed—choices are unexplained and no sensitivity analysis or selection strategy is provided.

- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and potentially impactful work with solid theory and interesting applications; experiments need strengthening but paper is a strong candidate for acceptance.
