- Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD), extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition into an RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions nonparametrically using kernel features.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded algorithm with Theorems 1 and 2 linking computed quantities to true Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.  
- Introduces a practical, numerically robust variant based on projection onto kernel principal components (kernel PCA).  
- Demonstrates utility across tasks including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novel and significant: elegantly connects Koopman operator theory with kernel methods and could become a widely used tool for nonlinear dynamics analysis.  
- Technically sound: solid theoretical development and thoughtful treatment of numerical stability.  
- Broad applicability: extracted Koopman modes serve as versatile features across diverse ML tasks.  
- Main weakness is clarity: writing is dense and overly formal, needing more intuitive, high-level explanations to aid comprehension.  
- Experimental depth is limited: core empirical example (Section 6) is too brief and lacks detailed analysis (e.g., rationale for kernel choice).  
- Paper organization could be rebalanced: Related Works is too long while experimental validation is underdeveloped.  
- Suggestions: thoroughly revise for readability, simplify complex sentences, and add explanatory motivation for derivations.  
- Suggestions: expand experiments—connect kernel choices (e.g., polynomial) to known eigenfunction structure and provide practical guidance on kernel selection.  
- Suggestion: clarify description of the toy nonlinear system to avoid possible reader confusion about its nonlinearity.  
- Overall: highly original and valuable contribution that, with clearer presentation and expanded experiments, could be influential and highly cited.
