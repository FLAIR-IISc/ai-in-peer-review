- Contribution: Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD), extending DMD to RKHS via the kernel trick to implicitly define feature maps — a powerful generalization demonstrated on toy problems.

- Overall judgment: Core ideas are excellent and impactful but the manuscript is not readable enough and is not ready for NIPS; a major rewrite focused on pedagogical clarity is strongly encouraged.

- Presentation/clarity (Major): Writing is often opaque and verbose with dense formalism; many passages should be streamlined and explained more intuitively.

- Section 4 (Major): Background material is redundant with the introduction and should be removed or significantly condensed to save space.

- Section 5 (Major): Introduces important conceptual points but lacks illustrations, examples, or figures to make the ideas compelling; either add examples or move key insights to the conclusion.

- Section 6 (Major): Overly condensed compared to other sections; key results need more detailed development and clearer exposition.

- Typo (Minor): The nonlinear (NL) toy problem appears to be described incorrectly and is actually linear.

- Experiments/discussion (Minor): First two tasks use polynomial dynamics where KDMD should excel; provide more detailed analysis and discussion (e.g., matching kernels to structure, as in Brunton et al.) to highlight strengths.
