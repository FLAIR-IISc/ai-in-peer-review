

The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), introducing the kernel DMD (KDMD) algorithm. KDMD leverages feature maps to define the kernel, enabling explicit alignment with user-specified features of interest. This represents a meaningful generalization of DMD, though the paper's presentation significantly impedes comprehension. The writing is unnecessarily dense: simple concepts are obscured by excessive mathematical formalism, while other sections are verbose and repetitive. For instance, the explanation of DMD as approximating Koopman eigenfunctions via linear monomials (Section 1) could be stated concisely. Such opacity risks confusing even specialists and undermines the paper’s technical merit. While the core idea holds promise, the current draft is too inaccessible for NeurIPS publication. I strongly encourage the authors to substantially revise the manuscript for clarity and pedagogy; the resulting work would likely be highly cited.  

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**  
1. **Excessive verbosity**: The paper overcomplicates straightforward concepts. Example: *“Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point”* could be simplified to: *“DMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear observables, analogous to a first-order Taylor expansion.”*  
2. **Redundant section**: Section 4 duplicates material from the introduction and offers no new insight for a short paper.  
3. **Insufficient illustration**: Section 5 lacks concrete examples or visualizations, weakening its contribution. This content should be illustrated or summarized in the conclusion.  
4. **Underdeveloped analysis**: Section 6 is overly condensed, mirroring Section 5’s shortcomings. It requires expansion to clarify the algorithm’s practical application.  

**II. MINOR REMARKS**  
1. Section 3.1 (nonlinear toy example) appears to describe a linear system, suggesting a possible typo.  
2. The first two test problems (polynomial systems) are ideal for showcasing KDMD’s strengths—particularly when the kernel aligns with the problem structure, as noted in Section 5. The paper should elaborate on this connection, providing specific details about kernel selection and performance gains.
