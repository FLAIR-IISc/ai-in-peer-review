

The manuscript, entitled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extends the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the kernel DMD (KDMD) algorithm. This approach leverages feature maps to define the kernel, thereby explicitly linking DMD to specific features of interest and constituting a powerful generalization of DMD. My primary concern is that the paper is difficult to follow due to excessive verbosity and overly complex mathematical notation for straightforward concepts. Short, clear sentences would significantly improve readability. While some sections are unnecessarily verbose, others lack sufficient detail, potentially hindering comprehension even for specialists. The level of technical density is excessive for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings; however, I strongly encourage the authors to substantially rewrite the paper with greater pedagogical clarity. The resulting work, if revised effectively, would merit high citation impact.

I. MAJOR REMARKS  
1.  The presentation is overly verbose in places where simplification is possible. For example, the statement "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" can be concisely rephrased in one clear sentence.  
2.  Section 4 is redundant with the introduction and should be removed or significantly condensed for a short paper.  
3.  Section 5 lacks illustrative examples or a summary in the conclusion, diminishing its impact.  
4.  Section 6 requires development analogous to Section 5; it is currently condensed to the point of being insufficiently detailed.  

II. MINOR REMARKS  
1.  The description of the nonlinear toy case likely contains a typo, as the system appears linear.  
2.  The first two problems are polynomial in nature—precisely where KDMD should demonstrate its advantage when the kernel can be reasonably inferred (as noted in Section 1). More details on this aspect, similar to Section 5, are needed.
