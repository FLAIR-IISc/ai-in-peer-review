The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. This approach is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of a system, where x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given collected trajectories from the system, the authors perform an eigen-decomposition of the operator, drawing on ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness.

For readers familiar with kernel methods, the novelty of the paper lies in the representation of function composition as a linear operator, enabling interesting decompositions of function compositions. Dynamical systems, being a particular type of function composition, provide a suitable application of this approach. However, the algorithm, dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), has already been proposed in the dynamical system literature, which limits the paper's novelty to kernelizing this existing method. Furthermore, other methods, such as recurrent neural networks, are available for recognizing dynamical systems and detecting changes. In this context, the applications presented in the paper are not particularly compelling in demonstrating the method's uniqueness and advantages.
