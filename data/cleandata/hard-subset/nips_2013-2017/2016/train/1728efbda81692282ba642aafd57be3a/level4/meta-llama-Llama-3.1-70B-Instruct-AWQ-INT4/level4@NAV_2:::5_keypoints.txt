- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Algorithm reduces to computing orthogonal bases of the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Enables use of feature maps/kernels to incorporate nonlinear basis functions and link DMD to specific features.  
- Introduces a robustified variant using POD bases.  
- Theoretical background and algorithm are well explained and mathematically thorough.  
- Empirical validation on toy nonlinear systems (including the Hénon map) and real-world data.  
- Main concern: paper is hard to read due to overemphasis on dense mathematical exposition.  
- Writing is sometimes verbose; short sentences would improve clarity.  
- Section 4 is partially redundant with the introduction.  
- Section 5 could be condensed or moved to the conclusion.  
- Section 6 is overly condensed and needs further development and detail.  
- Minor issues: possible typo in the toy nonlinear case description; first two test problems are actually polynomial.  
- Recommendation: rewrite in a more pedagogic, accessible style to broaden readership and impact.
