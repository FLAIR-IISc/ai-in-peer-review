- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS and an algorithm to perform this analysis from measurement data.
- Demonstrates method on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data, and compares to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA with results that are somewhat unclear.
- Offers an interesting extension of DMD akin to prior “kernelizing” techniques and provides educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Experimental evaluation is largely on artificial problems; paper is clearly written and pedagogically useful.
- Reviewer supportive of cross-disciplinary exposure but notes ML typically treats stochastic dynamics, raising questions about relevance to common ML setups.
- Authors need to better convince ML readers of practical usefulness with concrete use cases and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods in relevant domains (e.g., robotics).
- Theoretical derivations are solid but not surprising given similarities to kernel PCA/ICA and RKHS linear-operator formulations.
- Clarification needed on whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations—a potential limitation relative to kernel PCA.
- Recommends including standard DMD results on the toy example and the Henon map to illustrate differences more clearly.
- Overall: interesting DMD extension but needs more context, stronger comparisons, and clearer demonstrations of practical benefit.
