- Presents a spectral analysis framework using the Koopman operator in an RKHS with kernel-based empirical methods and demonstrates efficiency gains via Koopman eigendecomposition.  
- Contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated; paper is well-written.  
- Unclear novelty compared to Williams et al. (2015) — authors must explicitly state key distinctions, especially in Section 4.  
- Literature review misses several relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamics works (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf) and should cite/incorporate them.  
- Koopman operator is typically deterministic; need discussion and experimental assessment of performance in stochastic settings (suggest a toy experiment).  
- Clarify the assumption (line 216) that estimation requires consecutive observable pairs pointwise related; discuss implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations and causal assumptions.  
- Experimental settings lack ambition: prediction via preimage is standard practice and needs stronger novelty.  
- "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" should compare against established time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection requires comparison with state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods for credibility.  
- Minor fixes: verify indices for τ-1 vs. τ, and correct k(xi.xj) to k(xi,xj).
