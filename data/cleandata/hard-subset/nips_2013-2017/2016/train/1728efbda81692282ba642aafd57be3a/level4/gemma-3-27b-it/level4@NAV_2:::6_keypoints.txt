- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a modal decomposition algorithm for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by using RKHS/kernels to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Demonstrated on a damped linear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data.  
- Builds on established spectral methods (POD, DMD) and connects to kernel methods in machine learning.  
- Core contribution: leveraging kernel expressiveness to potentially improve Koopman eigenfunction approximation over linear monomials.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear, accessible introduction to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Theoretical framework appears solid and includes a validating theorem.  
- Novel and logical extension of DMD via reproducing kernels.  
- Effectively relates the approach to existing literature in dynamical systems and ML.  
- Empirical validation is limited in scope and lacks direct comparison to standard DMD on toy examples.  
- Unclear practical relevance and advantages for mainstream machine learning tasks.  
- Computational costs of operating in RKHS (e.g., explicit feature map requirements) are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Performance comparisons with related methods are only shown on locomotion data, not on synthetic examples.  
- For acceptance: theoretically sound, bridges dynamical systems and kernel methods, and is clearly presented.  
- Against acceptance: weak empirical demonstration of practical benefits, limited ML applicability evidence, and insufficient treatment of computational implications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requiring DMD comparisons on toy examples, clearer discussion of ML applications, and addressing RKHS computational considerations.
