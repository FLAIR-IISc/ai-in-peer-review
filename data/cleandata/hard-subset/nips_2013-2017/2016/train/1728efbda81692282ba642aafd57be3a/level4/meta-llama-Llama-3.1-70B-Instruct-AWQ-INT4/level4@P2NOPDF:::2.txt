The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. This approach involves applying the Koopman operator decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics of a system, specifically x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). By leveraging ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis, the authors perform an eigen-decomposition of the operator using collected system trajectories. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, demonstrating promising results.

For readers familiar with kernel methods, the paper's novelty lies in representing the composition of functions as a linear operator, enabling interesting decompositions of function compositions. Dynamical systems, being a specific type of function composition, serve as a suitable application of this concept. However, the algorithm's foundation in dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) – already established in dynamical system literature – limits the paper's novelty to its kernelized approach.

In comparison to other methods for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, and change detection techniques, the paper's applications do not strongly demonstrate the method's uniqueness and advantages. While the results are encouraging, the paper could benefit from more compelling examples to showcase the method's strengths and differentiate it from existing approaches.
