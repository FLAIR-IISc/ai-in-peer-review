- Introduces a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, computing orthogonal Krylov bases and projecting the Koopman operator for finite-mode nonlinear decompositions.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with experiments on toy systems, the Hénon map, and locomotion data.  
- Strong theoretical foundation (Theorems 1–2) and clear mathematical exposition tying to Koopman theory and DMD.  
- Robust practical implementation using POD-based projection (Section 4) to mitigate DMD numerical instability.  
- Clear algorithmic description and reproducible empirical examples (Figures 1–4).  
- Aligns with and extends recent NIPS-relevant Koopman/DMD work.  
- Novelty is limited—method is a natural kernelization/generalization of prior extended DMD (Williams et al. [35]) rather than a major advance.  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from existing kernel-based spectral methods [13, 35] or justify why kernelization is essential over extended DMD.  
- Empirical applications lack strong comparative evidence—no quantitative baselines against extended DMD or RNN-based methods for prediction/change detection.  
- Locomotion recognition uses standard kernel principal angle distance but does not show superiority over simpler methods like PCA.  
- Overall: technically sound and well written, but originality and significance are marginal for NIPS; needs stronger comparative experiments and clearer motivation for kernelization.  
- Recommendation: solid methodological contribution but not convincing enough for acceptance without additional comparisons and clearer articulation of novelty.
