

This paper introduces a spectral analysis framework based on the Koopman operator within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors follow the standard approach to define a set of kernel-based methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using empirical data. The eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator yields significant efficiency gains in both synthetic and real-world data analysis tasks. The proposed kernel construction represents a meaningful contribution to the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, with well-motivated theoretical foundations and experimental validation. However, several concerns require clarification:  
1. The distinction between this work and Williams et al. [35] (Journal of Nonlinear Science, 2015), which already introduced Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system analysis, is unclear. The authors should explicitly highlight and discuss the key differences, particularly regarding the kernel construction, and strengthen the discussion in Section 4.  
2. The literature review omits several relevant works in kernel-based nonlinear dynamical systems analysis, including those by Ralaivola and d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid and Schaback, and Franz and Schölkopf. These should be incorporated.  
3. While the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, the paper lacks discussion of performance in stochastic settings—critical for realistic applications. The brief mention should be expanded with additional analysis and a toy experiment.  
4. The remark at line 216 (page 5) states that "the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is supposed to be nonlinear pointwise related." The implications of this assumption for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships are unclear. The authors should clarify what advantages the proposed kernel construction offers in such scenarios. Additionally, the causal assumptions underlying the framework require explicit elaboration.  
5. The experimental evaluation lacks ambition. The prediction via preimaging follows a standard approach. Under "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics," there is insufficient discussion of connections (and experimental comparisons) with established time series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods). For sequential change-point detection, the comparison should include state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods (noting that the authors' approach is known to fail in complex dynamics). More compelling experiments are needed to substantiate the contribution.  
*Minor: The paper is well-written. Please correct two typos: (1) Verify indices for τ-1 vs. τ; (2) Replace the dot with a comma in k(x_i, x_j) (last equation on page 7).
