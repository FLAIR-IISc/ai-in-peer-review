- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to RKHS via a kernel-based KDMD algorithm for Koopman spectral analysis.
- Strong technical contribution with a sound theoretical foundation and clear links to prior Koopman/DMD work.
- KDMD provides a more expressive feature representation and can capture nonlinear dynamics better than traditional DMD in examples.
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.
- Key conceptual insight: the method yields a modal decomposition that functions as a feature-extraction procedure (approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions).
- Writing is overly verbose and mathematically dense, obscuring main ideas and reducing accessibility for specialists.
- Paper needs a more pedagogic structure: present intuitive conceptual explanations before detailed mathematical formalism.
- Several sections are redundant or misplaced (notably Section 4 on POD robustification and Section 5 on related work) and should be condensed or integrated.
- Empirical examples (Section 6) are presented too tersely and require richer explanation and interpretation of results.
- Technical descriptions (e.g., Krylov subspace and Gram–Schmidt in RKHS) lack intuitive exposition prior to formal development.
- Minor technical error: the toy “nonlinear” system appears linear and should be clarified or corrected.
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage substantial revision emphasizing clarity and conceptual presentation.
- High potential impact if revised—kernel extension of DMD for Koopman analysis could be influential with clearer exposition.
