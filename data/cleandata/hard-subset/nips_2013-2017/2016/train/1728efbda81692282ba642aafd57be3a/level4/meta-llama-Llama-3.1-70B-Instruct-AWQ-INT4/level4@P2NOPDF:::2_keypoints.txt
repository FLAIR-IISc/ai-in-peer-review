- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems via linearization of function composition.  
- Specifically applies the Koopman decomposition to forward dynamics of the form x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).  
- Method leverages kernel PCA and subspace analysis to perform eigen-decomposition from collected system trajectories.  
- Demonstrates applications to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems with promising empirical results.  
- Core novelty is representing function composition as a linear operator, which is of interest to readers familiar with kernel methods.  
- Uses dynamical systems as a natural application of the composition-decomposition viewpoint.  
- Limited novelty relative to existing literature because the algorithm is fundamentally based on dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).  
- Main contribution is essentially a kernelized variant of DMD rather than a wholly new framework.  
- Comparisons to other recognition methods (e.g., RNNs) and change-detection techniques are weak, so advantages are not clearly demonstrated.  
- Paper would benefit from more compelling examples to highlight strengths and differentiate from existing approaches.
