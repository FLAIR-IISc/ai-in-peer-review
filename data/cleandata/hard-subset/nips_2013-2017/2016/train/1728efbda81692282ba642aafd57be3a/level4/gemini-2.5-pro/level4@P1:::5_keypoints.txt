- Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD): extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- KDMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using richer nonlinear feature spaces induced by kernels.  
- Presents two formulations: a Gram–Schmidt–based KDMD and a more robust kernel PCA–projected variant.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets for state prediction, sequential change‑point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical generalization linking Koopman operator theory with kernel methods.  
- Strength: can capture complex nonlinear dynamics beyond standard linear DMD; potentially high impact.  
- Major weakness: clarity and presentation are poor—writing is dense and often cryptic.  
- Suggestion: adopt a more pedagogical exposition and simplify dense mathematical passages.  
- Structural issue: Related Works section feels disconnected and should be integrated into the introduction.  
- Empirical issue: Examples and applications are overly condensed and need expanded experimental setup and discussion.  
- Detailed comment: clarify the toy example by explicitly stating where the nonlinearity resides (y dynamics vs. x).  
- Detailed comment: discuss kernel choice—polynomial dynamics suit polynomial kernels—and cite related work (e.g., Brunton et al. 2015).  
- Conclusion: solid theoretical contribution but not yet ready for publication; revise for clarity and fuller empirical exposition.
