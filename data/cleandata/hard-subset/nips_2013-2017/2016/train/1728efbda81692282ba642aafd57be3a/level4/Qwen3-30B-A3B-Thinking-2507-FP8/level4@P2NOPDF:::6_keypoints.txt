1. Develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a measurement-based computation algorithm.  
2. Connection to machine learning is asserted but underdeveloped and weakly justified.  
3. Validated on two toy systems and locomotion data, but locomotion comparisons are ambiguous and don't show clear superiority.  
4. Theoretical contribution is incremental—aligns with existing kernelized methods (kernel PCA/ICA) and offers little novel insight.  
5. Derivations are mathematically sound and the exposition is rigorous.  
6. Paper has educational value for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid dynamics.  
7. Fails to articulate compelling ML use cases; focuses on deterministic dynamics while ML typically handles stochastic settings.  
8. Authors should explain why deterministic Koopman analysis matters for ML and where it outperforms state-of-the-art methods.  
9. Robotics suggested as a natural application; need concrete comparisons to RNNs, Gaussian processes, etc., on tasks like motion prediction or control.  
10. Experimental gaps: missing comparisons to standard DMD on the Henon map and damped dynamics.  
11. Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a significant limitation versus implicit kernel methods.  
12. Paper should clarify feature-space dependency and discuss scalability and trade-offs with kernel methods.  
13. Recommendations: refocus motivation on a specific ML domain, demonstrate concrete gains over SOTA, and clarify feature-space/scalability issues.  
14. Overall: technically competent but not compelling for ML audiences; better positioned as a cross-disciplinary educational contribution unless revised.
