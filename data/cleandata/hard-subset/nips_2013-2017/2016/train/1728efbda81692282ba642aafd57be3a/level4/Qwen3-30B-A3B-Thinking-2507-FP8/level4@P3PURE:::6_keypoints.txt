- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm using measurement data from dynamical systems.
- Demonstrations include a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic Henon map, and real-world locomotion data.
- Results are ambiguous, especially on the real-world application, and do not clearly outperform baselines.
- The approach extends DMD in a kernel-like way and is framed via linear operators in RKHS, connecting to kernel PCA/ICA and other kernel methods.
- Theoretical derivations are technically sound and the paper is clearly written with educational value.
- Practical relevance to machine learning is underdeveloped and not well justified.
- Method focuses on deterministic dynamical systems, which contrasts with noisy/stochastic settings common in ML.
- Reviewer asks for explicit ML use cases (e.g., robotics) where Koopman analysis gives tangible advantages over existing techniques.
- The paper does not demonstrate significant novelty or competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods in relevant domains.
- Technical concern: unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations, which could be a limitation versus kernel PCA.
- Technical concern: lack of direct comparison to standard DMD on the toy examples to illustrate algorithmic differences.
- Overall: reasonable contribution bridging fluid dynamics and ML, but needs stronger justification of ML relevance and clearer experimental validation against benchmarks.
