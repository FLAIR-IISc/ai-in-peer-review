- Paper extends DMD to RKHS (KDMD), tying DMD to kernel-defined feature maps and offering a powerful generalization demonstrated on toy problems.
- Principal concern: readability—heavy formalism and inconsistent verbosity make the manuscript hard to follow and too opaque for NeurIPS as written.
- Major remark 1: Exposition is often more elaborate than necessary; many statements could be conveyed in plain, concise prose.
- Major remark 2: Section 4 appears unnecessary for a short paper and overlaps material in the introduction.
- Major remark 3: Section 5 needs concrete illustrations or should be condensed and folded into the conclusion.
- Major remark 4: Section 6 is overly compressed and should be elaborated similarly to Section 5.
- Minor remark 1: Possible typo in the “toy nonlinear” example, which reads as linear.
- Minor remark 2: The first two examples are polynomial systems—where KDMD should excel—so provide more detailed treatment similar to Section 5.
- Recommendation: Strongly encourage a substantial, more pedagogical rewrite; with improved clarity the paper could be highly influential.
