The paper presents a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems based on the Koopman operator, which provides an operator representation of function composition. Specifically, the authors apply a Koopman operator decomposition to the repeated application of the nonlinear dynamics, formulated as x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given trajectories collected from the system, they perform an eigen-decomposition of this operator, leveraging concepts from kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The resulting method is demonstrated on embedding and recognition tasks, achieving promising performance. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the key contribution lies in representing function composition as a linear operator and applying decomposition techniques to these compositions; dynamical systems provide a natural application of this framework. While dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists within the dynamical systems literature, the novelty of this work appears to be the kernelization of that approach. Several alternative methods exist for dynamical system recognition, including recurrent neural networks, and for change detection. Consequently, the presented applications do not strongly demonstrate a compelling advantage or uniqueness for this method.
