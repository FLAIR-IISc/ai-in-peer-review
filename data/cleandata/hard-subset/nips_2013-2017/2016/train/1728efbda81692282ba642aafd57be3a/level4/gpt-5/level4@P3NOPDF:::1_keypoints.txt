- Paper proposes Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel-based algorithms and shows empirical efficiency on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: Conceptual RKHS formulation enabling spectral analysis with nonlinear observables.  
- Strength: Empirical results indicate computational and practical benefits.  
- Strength: Clear exposition and generally well written.  

- Major concern: Unclear substantive differences from Williams et al. (2015)/EDMD—clarify if the kernel is just an implicit/infinite dictionary or if there are theoretical/computational distinctions (consistency, regularization, spectral convergence, stability).  
- Major concern: Clarify whether efficiency gains come from kernelization, a different estimator, or new numerical eigendecomposition techniques.  
- Major concern: Explicitly state what in Section 4 is new relative to EDMD and kernel-DMD variants.  

- Missing positioning: Situate work within broader kernel/dynamics literature and cite relevant prior work (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché-Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Suggestion: Provide a taxonomy of kernel approaches (system ID, time-series embeddings, operator-theoretic methods) and contrast your method.  

- Deterministic vs stochastic: Clarify which operator the estimator converges to under stochastic dynamics (stochastic Koopman, transfer operator, or expectation).  
- Deterministic vs stochastic: Discuss sensitivity of spectral estimates to observation/process noise and possible regularization or denoising strategies tied to the kernel.  
- Deterministic vs stochastic: Recommend a small noisy synthetic experiment to illustrate robustness and limitations.  

- Data pairing/causality: Clarify limits of the paired-sample assumption when dynamics depend on unobserved state or history and when temporal context (lags/embeddings) is necessary.  
- Data pairing/causality: State causal/identifiability assumptions (e.g., x_{t+1} depends only on x_t) and discuss effects of hidden confounders or partial observability.  
- Data pairing/causality: Provide guidance on when to use paired samples versus embedded sequences.  

- Experiments/baselines: Discuss pre-imaging choices, stability of pre-image step, and scaling with kernel/regularization parameters.  
- Experiments/baselines: Compare “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” against kernel time-series clustering baselines (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) or analytically contrast methods.  
- Experiments/baselines: For change-point detection, compare to stronger state-of-the-art online kernel change detection methods rather than weak baselines.  
- Experiments/baselines: Add ablations (kernel choice, dictionary size, regularization) and report computational costs to substantiate efficiency claims.  

- Clarification: Provide guidance on kernel selection and hyperparameter tuning linked to Koopman spectral properties if possible.  
- Clarification: Discuss sample complexity and numerical stability of the eigendecomposition, especially for large datasets and near-degenerate spectra.  
- Clarification: Add theoretical guarantees or cite existing results for consistency/error bounds of the projected Koopman operator in RKHS.  

- Minor comments: Fix index typos involving τ−1 versus τ, replace dot with comma in k(x_i, x_j) on page 7, and correct typographical errors (e.g., “preimaing” → “pre-imaging”).  

- Overall: Promising, well-motivated contribution applying RKHS to Koopman analysis, but needs clearer novelty relative to EDMD/kernel methods, expanded discussion of stochastic and causal issues, and stronger, more comparative experiments to establish impact.
