- Clear introduction of the Koopman operator to the ML community, framing function composition/evolution as a linear operator.
- Strong connection between Koopman spectral analysis and kernel methods by formulating the problem in an RKHS.
- Applied to embedding and classification of dynamical systems with promising empirical results.
- Limited novelty: mainly a kernelized extension of established Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) and needs better contextualization with DMD literature.
- Insufficient experimental comparison: lacks benchmarks against strong baselines (e.g., RNNs, state-space models) for time-series and system ID tasks.
- Weak motivation for chosen applications: paper does not clearly explain why Koopman spectral embeddings are superior or link theoretical properties to practical advantages.
