This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a corresponding modal decomposition algorithm. The authors extend the Dynamic Mode Decomposition framework using the kernel trick, discuss a method for robustification, and provide several numerical experiments to demonstrate the approach's utility.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written and organized. The theoretical concepts underpinning the method are presented with clarity and appear technically solid. While the paper lacks a deep comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, the numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method's usefulness, which is sufficient for this work.

**Major Flaws:**
I have several significant concerns, primarily regarding Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1, which I found difficult to follow.

*   Equation (7) appears very similar to Equation (2); could the authors clarify its distinct purpose?
*   More critically, Equation (7) seems inconsistent. The operator K_H maps from H to H, yet φ is a function from M to H and thus not an element of H to which K_H can be applied. Could the authors explain this point in more detail? This apparent inconsistency casts doubt on the validity of the proof in Lines 144-157.
*   Relatedly, the statement "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is confusing, as both sides of the preceding equation are scalars, while K_H is an operator on H.
*   The assumptions for Theorem 1 seem strong and are not fully explicated. Could the authors provide more intuition and detail regarding these assumptions? Furthermore, additional assumptions appear during the proof itself. Why are these not stated in the theorem, and under what conditions can they be expected to hold in practice?

**Typos:**
*   Line 55: "the the"
*   Line 153: `\kappai / \lambda_i` should likely be `\kappaj / \lambdaj`
