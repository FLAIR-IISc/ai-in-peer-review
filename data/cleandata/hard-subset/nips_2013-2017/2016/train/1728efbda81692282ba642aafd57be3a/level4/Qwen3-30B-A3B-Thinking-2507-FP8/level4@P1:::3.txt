

This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs), developing a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences from nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors extend the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) framework using kernel methods, provide a robust implementation through projection onto POD directions, and demonstrate the approach through several applications including state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts. The technical content is sound, and the method is theoretically grounded. The numerical experiments appropriately illustrate the practical utility of the proposed approach. While the paper doesn't include extensive comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, the experimental results sufficiently demonstrate the method's effectiveness.

Major concerns: The primary concern lies in Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears to be a restatement of Equation (2) with notation changes, raising questions about its purpose. More critically, Equation (7) seems inconsistent: KH is defined as a linear operator from H to H, but ϕ is a function from M to H, not an element of H. This makes the expression (KHϕ)(x) = ϕ∘f(x) ambiguous, as KH should act on elements of H, not on functions mapping to H.

Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear. Since both sides of the equation are scalar values (not elements of H), applying KH (which maps H to H) does not make sense in this context.

The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and are not clearly stated. Furthermore, additional assumptions are introduced in the proof that are not mentioned in the theorem statement. The authors should clarify why these assumptions are necessary and whether they are likely to hold in practical scenarios.

Typos:
- Line 55: "the the" should be "the"
- Line 153: "\kappaj / \lambdaj" should be "\kappaj / \lambda_j"

Overall, the paper presents an interesting extension of DMD using kernel methods, but the theoretical foundation requires clarification to establish the validity of the approach. The core idea is promising, but the mathematical formulation needs to be more precise to convince the reader of its soundness.
