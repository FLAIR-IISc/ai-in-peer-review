

This paper develops a spectral analysis framework for the Koopman (composition) operator within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs), introducing a modal decomposition algorithm for this analysis. The authors further propose a robustification technique and validate their approach through comprehensive numerical experiments. The overall methodology extends dynamical mode decomposition by incorporating the kernel trick.  

Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured. The theoretical foundations are clearly presented and appear highly promising. The technical contributions are sound and rigorous. While the comparison with state-of-the-art methods is limited, the numerical experiments adequately demonstrate the method's practical utility.  

Major concerns: The primary issue lies in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears redundant with Equation (2); its purpose is unclear. Furthermore, Equation (7) lacks consistency: since $K_H$ is a linear operator from $H$ to $H$, and $\phi$ is a function mapping $M$ to $H$ (not an element of $H$), the expression is mathematically questionable. This casts doubt on the proof (Lines 144–157). Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator $K_H$ to both sides" (Line 149) is ambiguous, as both sides are scalar values, and $K_H$ operates on elements of $H$, not scalars. The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and insufficiently explicit; the proof also introduces additional assumptions not stated in the theorem. The authors must clarify the validity and practical feasibility of these assumptions.
