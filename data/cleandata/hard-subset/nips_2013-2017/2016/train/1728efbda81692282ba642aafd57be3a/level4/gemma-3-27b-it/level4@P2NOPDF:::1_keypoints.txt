- Presents a Koopman+RKHS spectral analysis framework and kernel-based methods with efficient eigendecomposition, supported by experiments.  
- Work is well-motivated, clearly written, and experimentally validated.  
- Major novelty concern: clarify differences from Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley [35] and explicitly state advantages over that prior Koopman formulation.  
- Broaden related work to include kernel/dynamical-system contributions (e.g., Ralaivola, d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez, Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Address stochastic dynamics: discuss applicability to noisy/stochastic systems and include a toy experiment showing performance with noisy data.  
- Clarify data assumptions: explain implications of requiring pointwise consecutive observables (not strict time sequence) and discuss benefits of the kernel construction when pointwise relationships are weak.  
- Explicitly state and discuss underlying causal assumptions of the framework.  
- Experimental scope is limited: predictive modeling uses standard approaches and needs stronger baselines.  
- Embedding/recognition experiments should connect to and compare against time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Change-point detection experiment needs comparison to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods.  
- Minor fixes: check indices τ−1 versus τ and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation, p.7).
