The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," presents an extension of the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the KDMD algorithm. This approach allows for the use of feature maps as kernel definitions, linking DMD to specific features of interest and providing a powerful generalization of DMD. The authors demonstrate the KDMD algorithm on several toy problems.

While the manuscript showcases a valuable contribution to the field, my primary concern is its readability. The authors often prioritize mathematical formalism over clear and concise explanations, making it challenging to follow even simple concepts. Some sections are overly verbose, which may hinder understanding, even for specialists. A few points require clarification to ensure the manuscript is accessible to a broader audience.

Given the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings' emphasis on clear communication, I strongly encourage the authors to revise the manuscript in a more pedagogical style. A rewritten article has the potential to be highly cited and widely influential.

**Major Remarks**

1. The presentation is often verbose and could be simplified. For example, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be condensed into a single, straightforward sentence.
2. Section 4 is unnecessary in a short paper and partially repeats the introduction. The authors should consider removing or integrating this section into the introduction.
3. Section 5 lacks illustrations and would benefit from a summary in the conclusion to provide a clearer overview of the results.
4. Section 6 is overly condensed and should be expanded, similar to Section 5, to provide a more comprehensive explanation of the results.

**Minor Remarks**

1. There appears to be a typo in the description of the toy NL case, which is actually linear.
2. The first two problems are polynomial, which is an area where KDMD should excel, particularly when the kernel can be guessed. More details, similar to those provided in Section 5, would be beneficial.

**Reference**

1. Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, "Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control," http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.03007v2, (2015)
