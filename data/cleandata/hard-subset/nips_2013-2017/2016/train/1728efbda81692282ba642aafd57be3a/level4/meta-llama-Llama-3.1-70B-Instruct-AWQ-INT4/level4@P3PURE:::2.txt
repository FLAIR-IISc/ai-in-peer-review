The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. Specifically, the Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given collected trajectories from the system, the operator can be approximated using an eigen-decomposition, drawing on similar ideas from kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the author demonstrates its effectiveness. For an audience familiar with kernel methods, the novelty of the paper lies in representing the composition of functions as a linear operator and applying interesting decompositions to function compositions. Dynamical systems, being particular compositions of functions, provide a suitable application of this approach. However, since the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, the paper's novelty appears limited to kernelizing this existing method. Furthermore, other methods for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, and detecting changes exist. In this sense, the paper's applications do not compellingly demonstrate the method's uniqueness and advantages.
