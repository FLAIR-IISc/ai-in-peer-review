- Main contribution: extends DMD to RKHS as Kernel DMD (KDMD) and demonstrates it on toy problems; significant and promising.
- Overall concern: paper is difficult to read—heavy formalism in places and unnecessary verbosity in others—making it cryptic for NIPS.
- Recommendation: strongly encourage a rewrite focused on pedagogical clarity to make the work accessible and influential.
- Major 1: presentation is verbose where concise prose would suffice; many statements could be much shorter and clearer.
- Major 2: Section 4 appears redundant with the introduction and could be removed or condensed for a conference paper.
- Major 3: Section 5 lacks concrete examples or visual illustrations; its key takeaways should be summarized or illustrated.
- Major 4: Section 6 is overly condensed and needs a more developed treatment.
- Minor 1: possible typo in the NL toy example—system described appears linear, not nonlinear.
- Minor 2: first two examples are polynomial systems where KDMD should excel; this point and kernel selection merits more detailed discussion (see Ref. [1]).
