This paper presents an extension of Koopman spectral analysis to the framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The methodology is evaluated using both simulated and real-world datasets. Overall, this is a clearly written and well-presented contribution. 

The core strength of the work lies in its elegant generalization of Koopman analysis, broadening its applicability to a wider range of nonlinear systems through the use of kernel methods. The experimental validation provides a reasonable demonstration of the approach's effectiveness. However, I believe the paper would benefit from two additional investigations.

First, in the simulation studies, a direct comparison to standard, non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis would be highly valuable. This would help to quantify the advantages offered by the kernelized approach – for example, in terms of accuracy, computational cost, or ability to handle specific system characteristics. Understanding where the kernelized method demonstrably outperforms (or underperforms) its non-kernelized counterpart is crucial for guiding practical applications.

Second, the paper would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of kernel selection and its impact on the results. The choice of kernel is a critical aspect of any RKHS-based method, and exploring the sensitivity of the proposed approach to different kernel parameters (or even different kernel functions altogether) would be insightful. This analysis could be conducted within the simulation framework, systematically varying kernel parameters and observing their effect on performance metrics. A discussion of the theoretical considerations informing kernel choice in this context would also be beneficial. 

Addressing these points would further enhance the paper’s contribution and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method’s capabilities and limitations. Despite these suggestions, the paper is currently strong and represents a worthwhile addition to the literature.
