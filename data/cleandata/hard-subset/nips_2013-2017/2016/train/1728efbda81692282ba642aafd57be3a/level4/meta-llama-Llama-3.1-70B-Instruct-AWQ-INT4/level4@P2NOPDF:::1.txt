This paper presents a novel spectral analysis approach using the Koopman operator defined in a proper reproducing kernel Hilbert space. The authors follow a standard pathway to develop a set of kernel-based methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data. The eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator yields efficiency gains in both synthetic and real data analysis problems. This work introduces a significant kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems, with well-motivated contributions that are experimentally validated.

However, there are some concerns that the authors should address. The main differences between this work and the paper by M.O. Williams et al. (2015) [35] are unclear, as the latter already introduced Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system dynamics analysis. A discussion clarifying these differences would be beneficial. Furthermore, the authors could improve the discussion in Section 4.

The field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is vast, and some relevant references are missing. The authors should consider citing papers by L. Ralaivola and F. d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, or M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf. Although the Koopman operator is generally defined for deterministic systems, the authors should provide more comments on its performance in stochastic settings, which are more realistic and important. A brief discussion and a toy experiment would be welcome.

The authors should also clarify the implications of the assumption that the estimation procedure requires a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is nonlinearly pointwise related. This assumption's impact on multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations is unclear, and the benefits of the proposed kernel construction in such cases should be discussed. Additionally, the authors should elaborate on the causal assumption inherent in the setting.

Regarding the experimental settings, they seem unambitious. The prediction via premapping follows a standard approach, and the "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" subsection lacks a discussion on connections and experimental comparisons with the literature on clustering time series (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods). The sequential change-point detection experiment would be more convincing with a comparison to state-of-the-art online change detection with kernels. More convincing experiments are expected to support the development.

Minor corrections: the paper is well-written and clear, but there are two minor typos. The authors should check the indices on tau-1 vs. tau and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
