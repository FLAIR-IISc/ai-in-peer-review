- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to compute it from dynamical-system measurements.
- Interesting extension/kernelization of dynamic mode decomposition in the spirit of kernel PCA/ICA and related methods.
- Theoretical development is careful, well executed, and educational for ML readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Writing is generally clear and the paper has pedagogical value.
- Practical relevance to machine learning is not convincingly established since the framework focuses on deterministic maps without stochastic noise common in ML time-series.
- Empirical results are hard to interpret, especially on the locomotion dataset, and it is unclear what is gained versus existing methods.
- The derivations are unsurprising to readers familiar with kernel methods—once cast in RKHS, finite-dimensional eigendecompositions follow naturally.
- Clarification requested: does the modal decomposition require explicit feature-space representations or can it be implemented purely with kernels?
- Suggestion: provide concrete ML use cases (e.g., robotics) where Koopman-RKHS analysis outperforms or complements current methods.
- Suggestion: include standard DMD baselines on the toy and Henon-map experiments to make differences explicit.
- Suggestion: clarify specific benefits or insights of RKHS Koopman analysis over DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA on the locomotion data.
- Recommendation: strengthen empirical evidence, present clearer baselines, discuss computational/feature-map limitations, and better articulate compelling ML use cases.
