- Paper studies spectral properties of the Koopman operator in RKHS, proposing a kernelized dynamic mode decomposition, a stabilization strategy, and numerical examples.

- Strength: manuscript is clearly written, well structured, and the theoretical development appears sound and appealing.

- Strength: experiments adequately showcase the method’s value, though comparisons to contemporary baselines are limited.

- Major concern: In Section 3.1, Equation (7) appears to simply restate Equation (2); its additional role is unclear.

- Major concern: Equation (7) seems inconsistent—KH is treated as an operator H → H while ϕ is a map M → H (not an element of H); this needs clarification.

- Major concern: Argument on Lines 144–157 is doubtful—“applying the operator KH to both sides” is unclear if both sides are scalars, since KH: H → H would not act on scalars.

- Major concern: Theorem 1’s assumptions appear strong and are not fully spelled out; the authors should state them explicitly.

- Major concern: Further assumptions are introduced inside the proof without justification—why are they needed and are they reasonably satisfied in practice?

- Minor: Typos to fix—Line 55 “the the”; Line 153 should read κi/λi instead of κj/λj.
