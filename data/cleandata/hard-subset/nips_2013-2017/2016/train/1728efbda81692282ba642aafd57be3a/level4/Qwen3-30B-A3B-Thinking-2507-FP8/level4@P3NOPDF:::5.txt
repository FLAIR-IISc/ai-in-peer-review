

The manuscript proposes an extension of Dynamical Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), termed Kernel DMD (KDMD). This approach leverages feature maps to define the kernel, enabling the explicit incorporation of domain-specific features into the Koopman spectral analysis. The resulting framework represents a significant generalization of DMD, potentially offering enhanced interpretability for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors demonstrate KDMD on several toy problems, including a polynomial system and a linear example mistakenly labeled as nonlinear.

**Strengths:** The core idea—using RKHS to generalize DMD and align spectral analysis with interpretable features—is theoretically sound and addresses a meaningful gap in Koopman operator methods. The potential for KDMD to bridge the gap between abstract spectral decomposition and feature-specific dynamics is promising, particularly for applications where domain knowledge suggests relevant observables.

**Major Concerns:** The manuscript suffers from severe readability issues that undermine its technical contribution. The presentation is overly dense with excessive mathematical formalism where simple explanations suffice, while simultaneously being verbose in other sections. This creates a barrier for even specialist readers. Key examples include:

1.  **Unnecessary Complexity:** The conceptual explanation of DMD (e.g., "Conceputally, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation...") is unnecessarily convoluted. A concise statement like "DMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear observables, akin to a first-order Taylor expansion" would be clearer and more direct.
2.  **Redundant Content:** Section 4 (on RKHS properties) largely repeats material introduced in the introduction and is not essential for the main contribution. This section should be removed or significantly condensed to avoid repetition.
3.  **Insufficient Illustration:** Section 5 (on kernel selection) lacks concrete examples or visualizations, making its practical relevance unclear. The results should be summarized or illustrated to demonstrate *how* kernel choice impacts analysis.
4.  **Underdeveloped Methodology:** Section 6 (on the nonlinear example) is critically underdeveloped compared to Section 5. It requires expansion to match the level of detail and justification provided earlier, particularly regarding why the chosen kernel is appropriate.

**Minor Concerns:**
1.  **Typo in Example:** The "nonlinear" toy example in Section 3.1 is actually linear (the dynamics are `x_{t+1} = A x_t` with `A` diagonal), contradicting the description. This should be corrected.
2.  **Underutilized Strengths:** The initial examples (Sections 3.1-3.2) are polynomial systems—precisely where KDMD's feature-based kernel selection should excel. The manuscript fails to leverage this opportunity to showcase KDMD's advantage over standard DMD. More detailed explanation of kernel choice for these cases (as referenced in Section 5) is needed.

**Recommendation:** While the underlying technical contribution is valuable and holds significant potential for impact in dynamical systems and machine learning, the current manuscript is **not suitable for publication in NIPS proceedings** due to its excessive density and lack of clarity. The writing style is overly technical without sufficient pedagogical guidance, making it difficult for the intended audience to grasp the core ideas and their significance.

**Critical Revision Required:** The authors must undertake a substantial rewrite focused on clarity and accessibility. Key steps include:
*   Replacing verbose mathematical exposition with concise, plain-language explanations of concepts (e.g., the DMD-Koopman link).
*   Removing redundant sections (like Section 4) and integrating essential RKHS background smoothly into the introduction or methodology.
*   Adding illustrative examples or visualizations to Sections 5 and 6 to demonstrate the practical impact of kernel choice and the method's application.
*   Correcting the linear/nonlinear example and explicitly demonstrating KDMD's advantage on the polynomial cases.
*   Ensuring all sections build logically towards the contribution, with a clear narrative flow.

**Conclusion:** This work addresses an important problem with a promising theoretical approach. However, its current presentation obscures its value. With a thorough revision prioritizing reader comprehension—replacing excessive formalism with clear exposition, removing redundancy, and adding illustrative examples—the paper could become a highly influential contribution to the field. I strongly encourage the authors to make these revisions before resubmission. The potential for this work to be widely cited is significant, but only if the clarity issues are resolved.
