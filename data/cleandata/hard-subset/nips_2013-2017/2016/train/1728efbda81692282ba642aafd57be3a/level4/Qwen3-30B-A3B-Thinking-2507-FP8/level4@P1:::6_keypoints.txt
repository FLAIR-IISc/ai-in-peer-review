- Proposes a spectral analysis method for the Koopman operator in RKHS, extending DMD with kernel-based nonlinear representations.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm that uses kernel functions to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer bases than traditional DMD.  
- Mathematical derivations are clear, technically sound, and well-structured, with appropriate links to kernel PCA and standard DMD.  
- Provides a detailed algorithm and implementation, including a POD-based robustification step to address numerical instability.  
- The paper’s significance to the machine learning community is unclear; presented applications are generic and do not convincingly show ML value.  
- Empirical comparisons (synthetic, locomotion, Hénon map) do not demonstrate clear advantages over standard DMD, PCA, or kernel PCA.  
- Novelty is somewhat overstated: kernelizing Koopman is a natural extension of existing kernel methods and lacks substantial new theoretical insight.  
- The method appears to require explicit feature-space representations (e.g., Gram–Schmidt QR in RKHS), unlike kernel PCA’s kernel trick, posing practical and computational limitations.  
- Authors should include direct comparisons with standard DMD on toy problems and clarify computational trade-offs and concrete ML application benefits.  
- Overall: technically solid extension but limited demonstrated ML impact, overstated novelty, and insufficient discussion of practical constraints and comparative advantages.
