- Introduces a spectral approach using the Koopman operator to obtain a linear representation of nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Applies Koopman operator decomposition specifically to the double-step forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).
- Learns the operator from collected trajectories via an eigen-decomposition analogous to kernel PCA and subspace methods.
- Demonstrates the method for embedding and recognition of dynamical systems and reports strong empirical performance.
- Novelty highlighted as representing function composition as a linear operator and applying kernel-style decompositions—appealing to readers familiar with kernel methods.
- Criticism that dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already covers similar ground, so the main novelty is essentially kernelizing DMD.
- Notes that alternative approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) are well-established for recognition and change detection.
- Concludes the applications shown are not sufficiently persuasive to demonstrate clear advantages or superiority of the proposed method.
