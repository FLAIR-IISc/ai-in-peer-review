- Paper proposes a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, extending DMD to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonparametric kernel bases.  
- Algorithm computes eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projected onto an RKHS subspace and includes a robust variant via projection onto POD/kernel PCA bases.  
- Demonstrates applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  

Strengths:
- The approach is theoretically sound and provides a principled, nonparametric framework for analyzing nonlinear dynamics.  
- Clear connection to the DMD framework; the method is a natural kernelization of existing concepts.  
- Robustification via projection onto kernel PCA directions is a valuable practical contribution addressing numerical instability.  
- Paper is well written and presents core technical ideas clearly.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Novelty vs EDMD is unclear: need a detailed discussion clarifying differences and specific advantages over Extended DMD (Williams et al.).  
- Literature review is incomplete; should cite and discuss additional kernel-based dynamical-systems work (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Paper focuses on deterministic systems; lacks discussion or experiments on stochastic systems and how the method handles noise or requires adaptation.  
- The claim that only pairs of consecutive points are needed raises practical questions for multidimensional time series with weak/delayed dependencies and about implicit causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently rigorous:
  - Prediction: unclear whether proposed features outperform established nonlinear time-series predictors.  
  - Embedding/recognition: lacks quantitative comparisons to time-series clustering/classification baselines (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace methods).  
  - Change-point detection: comparisons use a weak baseline; should include stronger, state-of-the-art online kernel-based detection methods.  

Minor points:
- Check consistency of indices involving τ and τ-1 throughout the text.  
- Correct kernel notation in last equation on page 7: likely k(xj, xi) instead of k(xi.xj).
