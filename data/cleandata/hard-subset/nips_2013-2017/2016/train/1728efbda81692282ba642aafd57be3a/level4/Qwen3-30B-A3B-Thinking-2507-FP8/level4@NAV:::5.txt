

The manuscript, titled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), yielding the kernel-based DMD (KDMD) algorithm. Applied to toy problems, KDMD enables feature maps to define the kernel, thereby explicitly linking DMD to user-specified features of interest and constituting a powerful generalization of DMD. However, the paper suffers from excessive verbosity in exposition. Simple concepts are unnecessarily obscured by overly formal mathematical phrasing, while other sections are unnecessarily long. This impedes comprehension even for specialists and requires critical clarification. While the method holds significant promise, the current presentation is too cryptic for NeurIPS publication; I strongly recommend a major rewrite prioritizing pedagogical clarity, which would position the work for high citation impact.  

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**  
1.  **Excessive verbosity:** Simplify where possible. Example: The statement "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" requires no more than one concise sentence.  
2.  **Redundant section:** Section 4 is largely repetitive of the introduction and adds no value in a concise paper.  
3.  **Insufficient illustration:** Section 5 lacks concrete examples or should be summarized in the conclusion.  
4.  **Underdeveloped section:** Section 6 requires expansion comparable to Section 5, as it is currently condensed to the point of being unclear.  

**II. MINOR REMARKS**  
1.  Section 3 likely contains a typo: the described nonlinear (NL) toy case appears linear.  
2.  The first two toy problems are polynomial; this is precisely where KDMD should excel (when the kernel is identifiable, as in Section 1). More detail on this alignment is needed, as referenced in Section 5.
