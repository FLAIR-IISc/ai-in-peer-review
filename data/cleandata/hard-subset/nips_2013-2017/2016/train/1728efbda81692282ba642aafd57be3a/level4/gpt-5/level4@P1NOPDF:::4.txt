This paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to the reproducing-kernel setting (RKHS) and evaluates the method on both simulated and real datasets. The manuscript is very well written and easy to follow.

I would have liked to see two additional elements to strengthen the contribution:
1) A direct comparison with the standard, non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulation studies. Such a baseline would clarify the benefits and trade-offs of the RKHS formulation and quantify any performance gains.
2) A discussion—and ideally an empirical exploration, which could also be carried out in simulations—of how the choice of kernel (and possibly its hyperparameters) affects the results. Because kernel choice is central in RKHS methods, understanding its influence on the outcomes would be valuable for practitioners.

Overall, this is a strong paper; addressing these points would make it even more compelling.
