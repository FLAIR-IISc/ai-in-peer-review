- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS, extending dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to nonlinear systems.  
- Applies the kernel trick to DMD to work with nonlinear basis functions via kernel functions.  
- Algorithm reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection.  
- Proposes POD-based projection to improve numerical robustness.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains theoretical derivations clearly.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the method’s usefulness and potential applications (feature extraction, prediction, change-point detection).  
- Main theoretical concerns: definition of the Koopman operator in Equation (7) is unclear and its consistency with phi needs clarification.  
- Theorem 1’s proof appears to rely on strong, unstated assumptions that must be explicitly stated and justified.  
- Lacks comparison with state-of-the-art methods, limiting evaluation of relative performance.  
- Numerical experiments are illustrative but not comprehensive; a more thorough evaluation is recommended.  
- Recommendations: clarify Equation (7), explicitly state/justify assumptions in Theorem 1, and add comprehensive comparisons to baselines.  
- Overall rating: 7/10.
