- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS plus a data-driven algorithm for doing this analysis.
- Bridges fluid-dynamics concepts with ML and validates on toy dynamical systems and real locomotion data, comparing to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA, but results are not decisive.
- Core contribution resembles prior “kernelization” of linear operators (e.g., kernel PCA, kernel ICA, GP bandits) — an extension of DMD through kernel methods.
- Theoretical development is sound and the paper is well-written, serving as a useful intro to fluid-dynamics ideas for ML researchers.
- Experimental evaluation is focused on constructed examples and lacks clear demonstration of practical advantages.
- Authors need to better articulate practical utility and identify concrete application domains (e.g., robotics) where Koopman analysis offers value.
- Must demonstrate competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods in relevant tasks to convince an ML audience.
- Theoretical results are somewhat expected given RKHS properties leading naturally to eigendecomposition-based algorithms.
- It’s unclear whether the proposed modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation, a potential limitation versus kernel PCA that should be addressed.
- Recommend adding a standard DMD baseline on the toy example and Henon map to clarify empirical benefits of the proposed method.
