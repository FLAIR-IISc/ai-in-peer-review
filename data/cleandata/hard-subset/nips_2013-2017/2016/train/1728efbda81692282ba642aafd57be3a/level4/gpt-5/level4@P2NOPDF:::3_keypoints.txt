- Contribution: Develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS, proposes a modal decomposition algorithm with a robustness enhancement, and demonstrates utility via numerical experiments; extends DMD using the kernel trick.  
- Strength: Paper is very well written and structured; theoretical components are clearly presented and the methodology appears technically sound; experiments are adequate despite limited SOTA comparison.  
- Major concern (Section 3.1): Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear.  
- Eq. (7) issue: Eq. (7) appears essentially the same as Eq. (2); unclear what new insight Eq. (7) provides.  
- Eq. (7) inconsistency: KH is an operator H→H while φ is a mapping M→H, so it’s unclear in what sense KH is applied to φ; spaces and whether the identity is operator-level or pointwise must be specified.  
- Proof issue (Lines 144–157): “Applying KH to both sides” is ill-posed when both sides are scalars; any implicit identification (e.g., representers or Riesz isomorphism) must be made explicit and justified.  
- Theorem 1 assumptions: Assumptions are strong and not stated explicitly—request listing all assumptions (boundedness/compactness, injectivity, domain/codomain, spectral properties), avoid introducing assumptions only in the proof, and discuss practical validity and verifiability.  
- Overall judgment: Direction is promising and the technical development is compelling once the above clarifications are addressed.  
- Typos/minor fixes: Line 55 “the the”; Line 153 use κ_i/λ_i instead of κ_j/λ_j.
