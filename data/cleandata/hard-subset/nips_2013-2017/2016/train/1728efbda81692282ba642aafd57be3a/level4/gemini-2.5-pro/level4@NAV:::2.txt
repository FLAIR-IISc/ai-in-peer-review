This paper introduces a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The approach is centered on the Koopman operator, which offers a linear operator representation for the composition of functions. In this work, Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward dynamics of a system, represented as x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Based on collected system trajectories, an eigen-decomposition of this operator is performed using techniques analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, the method is applied to the tasks of embedding and recognizing dynamical systems, with the authors reporting strong performance.

For an audience well-versed in kernel methods, the principal contribution is the conceptualization of function composition as a linear operator to which spectral decomposition can be applied. The study of dynamical systems, being a specific instance of function composition, serves as an appropriate and compelling application for this concept.

However, the novelty of the paper appears to be limited. The core algorithm is highly related to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD), which is already an established method in the dynamical systems literature. The main contribution, therefore, seems to be the "kernelization" of this existing technique. Furthermore, the paper lacks a comparison to other relevant methods for dynamical system recognition, such as Recurrent Neural Networks or change-detection algorithms. Consequently, the presented applications are not sufficiently compelling to demonstrate the unique advantages and necessity of the proposed method over existing alternatives.
