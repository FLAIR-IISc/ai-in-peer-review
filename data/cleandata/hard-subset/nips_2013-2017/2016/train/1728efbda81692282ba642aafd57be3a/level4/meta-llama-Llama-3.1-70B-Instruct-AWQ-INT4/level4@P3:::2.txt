This paper proposes a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The method, termed Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels (DMD-RK), extends the traditional Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by utilizing the expressive power of kernel functions to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the Koopman operator and its application to nonlinear dynamical systems. They also present a thorough review of related works, including DMD and its variants, as well as other methods for spectral analysis of dynamical systems.

The proposed method, DMD-RK, is well-motivated and clearly explained. The use of kernel functions allows for a more flexible and expressive representation of the Koopman eigenfunctions, which can be beneficial for analyzing complex nonlinear systems. The authors also provide a robust version of the algorithm, which can handle ill-conditioned data by projecting it onto the principal orthogonal directions.

The paper includes several empirical examples that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The results show that DMD-RK can accurately estimate the Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions, and can be used for various applications such as prediction, embedding, and recognition of dynamical systems.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A clear and concise introduction to the Koopman operator and its application to nonlinear dynamical systems.
* A thorough review of related works, including DMD and its variants.
* A well-motivated and clearly explained proposed method, DMD-RK.
* Empirical examples that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are some weaknesses and limitations to the paper. For example:

* The novelty of the paper is limited to kernelizing the existing DMD algorithm, which may not be considered a significant contribution by some readers.
* The applications presented in the paper, such as embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, may not be compelling enough to demonstrate the uniqueness and advantages of the proposed method.
* There is no comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, which would be beneficial for evaluating the performance of the proposed method.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper scores well on quality, clarity, and originality. However, the significance of the paper could be improved by providing more compelling applications and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel method for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.
* The method is well-motivated and clearly explained, and the empirical examples demonstrate its effectiveness.
* The paper provides a thorough review of related works and clearly explains the contributions of the proposed method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The novelty of the paper is limited to kernelizing the existing DMD algorithm.
* The applications presented in the paper may not be compelling enough to demonstrate the uniqueness and advantages of the proposed method.
* There is no comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the weaknesses and limitations mentioned above.
