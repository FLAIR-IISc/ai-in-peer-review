This paper derives a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and presents a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis. The authors also discuss a method for improving robustness and demonstrate the usefulness of their approach through several numerical experiments. The overall framework extends “dynamical mode decomposition” using the kernel trick. 

A key strength of this paper is its clear and well-organized presentation. The authors explain the theoretical concepts underlying their method in a lucid manner, and the approach itself appears very interesting. Furthermore, the paper is technically sound. While a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is lacking, the numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method’s utility, which I find sufficient for this submission. However, I have several concerns, detailed below.

My primary concern lies with Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1, which I found difficult to follow. First, Equation (7) closely resembles Equation (2); the authors should clarify its purpose and added value. Second, Equation (7) appears inconsistent. Given that *K<sub>H</sub>* is a linear operator from *H* to itself, and *ϕ* is a function mapping from the manifold *M* to *H*, applying *K<sub>H</sub>* to *ϕ* seems problematic. Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of this step? This ambiguity casts doubt on the validity of the proof presented in Lines 144-157. 

Specifically, the phrase “by applying the operator *K<sub>H</sub>* to both sides” (Line 149) is unclear. Since both sides of the equation are scalar values, the operator *K<sub>H</sub>*: *H* → *H* cannot be directly applied. Furthermore, the assumptions stipulated in Theorem 1 seem strong and lack explicit definition. The authors should clarify these assumptions and their implications. Additionally, further assumptions emerge within the proof itself; it’s important to justify these added conditions and discuss how readily they hold in practice.

Finally, I noted a few minor typos:

*   Line 55: “the the” should be “the”
*   Line 153: “\kappaj / \lambdaj” should be “\kappai / \lambda_i”
