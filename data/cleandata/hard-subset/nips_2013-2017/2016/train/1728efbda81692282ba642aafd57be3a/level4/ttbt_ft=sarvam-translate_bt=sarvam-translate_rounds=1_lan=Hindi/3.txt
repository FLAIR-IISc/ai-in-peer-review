This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Kupman (structure) operator in RKHS and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for spectral analysis. The authors also discuss a consolidation method and conduct several numerical experiments to demonstrate the usefulness of their approach. The overall framework is an extension of "dynamical mode decomposition" using kernel tricks. Strengths of the paper: The paper is very well-written and organized. The authors clearly present the various theoretical concepts used in the derivation of the method, which I find very interesting. Furthermore, it is technically sound. There is no in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, but the numerical experiments accurately demonstrate the usefulness of the method. This is sufficient in my opinion. However, some troubling points are mentioned below. Major shortcomings: As far as I am concerned, the biggest shortcoming of this paper is the proof of Section 3.1 and especially Equation (7) as well as Theorem 1, which I do not fully understand. On the one hand, Equation (7) is similar to Equation (2), so what is its purpose? On the other hand, Equation (7) does not seem consistent to me because KH is a linear operator from H to itself and \phi is certainly not in H (it is a function from M to H). Can the authors explain this point in more detail? This gives me some doubts about the proof lines 144-157. Furthermore, what does "applying the operator KH to both sides" mean (line 149)? Since both sides are scalar numbers, KH : H \to H does not apply. The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong (and not very clear). Can the authors explain these assumptions? Furthermore, additional assumptions appear in the proof of the theorem. Why? Can they be easily explained? Typo: - Line 55: The The - Line 153: \kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i
