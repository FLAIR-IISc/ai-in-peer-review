- Introduces "kernel DMD": extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS for improved Koopman spectral analysis, plus a PCA-like robustified variant.
- Demonstrates the method on state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using synthetic and real-world data.
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically structured with accessible mathematical derivations.
- Strength — Technical quality: a natural, principled generalization of DMD with theoretical justification (Theorems 1 and 2).
- Strength — Significance: provides a powerful data-driven tool for extracting Koopman modes and features from nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Weakness — Lack of baselines: experiments omit direct comparisons to standard DMD and modern variants (e.g., Extended DMD), so advantages are not clearly demonstrated.
- Weakness — Kernel selection: no discussion or sensitivity analysis of kernel choice and hyperparameters, leaving practical guidance and reproducibility lacking.
- Suggested fix: include baseline comparisons and empirical or theoretical guidance on kernel/hyperparameter selection to strengthen empirical claims.
