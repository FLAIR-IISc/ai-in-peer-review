- Extends DMD into RKHS to produce kernelized DMD (KDMD), allowing problem-specific observables and generalizing DMD; demonstrated on toy problems.  
- Manuscript is hard to read: heavy mathematical formalism, verbose exposition, and cryptic presentation; needs a substantial, pedagogical rewrite.  

Major remarks:
- Presentation is overly verbose and should be simplified with clearer, concise statements.  
- Section 4 is redundant with the introduction and should be removed or integrated.  
- Section 5 needs at least one concrete example or figure, or a concise summary of key insights in the conclusion.  
- Section 6 is too compressed and should be expanded with explicit links to algorithmic and empirical implications.  

Minor remarks:
- The “toy NL” example appears misdescribed: the system given is actually linear.  
- The first two examples are polynomial systems; the paper should detail how kernel choice matches problem structure, effects of kernel degree and regularization, and comparisons to standard DMD/polynomial observables.  
- Fix wording/typos (e.g., “features map” → “feature map”) and avoid awkward phrases like “emphasize on.”  
- Improve readability by introducing ideas in plain language before formalism, moving lengthy derivations to an appendix, and adding a concise KDMD algorithm box (inputs, outputs, complexity).  

- Recommendation: Promising and potentially influential work but requires a thorough rewrite to improve clarity, remove redundancy, and better illustrate the method.
