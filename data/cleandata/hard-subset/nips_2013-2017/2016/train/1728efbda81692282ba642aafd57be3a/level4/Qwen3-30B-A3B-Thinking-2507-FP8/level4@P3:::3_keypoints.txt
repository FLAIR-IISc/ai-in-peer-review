- Paper proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm in RKHS to extract Koopman eigenfunctions and eigenvalues from finite-length nonlinear system data.  
- Replaces linear monomial bases with kernel-induced nonlinear representations to allow more expressive Koopman eigenfunction approximations.  
- Develops a robust variant using kernel PCA to mitigate numerical instability common in DMD.  
- Positions work relative to prior research, noting conceptual similarity to extended DMD (Williams et al.) but distinct use of RKHS for nonlinear bases.  
- Connects to kernel PCA and other spectral methods for dynamical systems analysis.  
- Strength: clear exposition, well-organized structure, and mostly rigorous mathematical derivations linking the method to Koopman theory.  
- Strength: empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets showing usefulness for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Strength: provides implementation details and public code, improving reproducibility.  
- Writing and presentation are excellent, with logical section flow, precise notation, and informative figures.  
- Originality is moderate but meaningful: novel combination of kernel methods with Koopman spectral analysis and clear distinctions from extended DMD.  
- Significance demonstrated via multiple applications and accurate eigenvalue estimation on toy systems and the Hénon map.  
- Major technical concern: Equation (7) and Section 3.1 confuse the Koopman operator acting on functions with action on the feature map, creating a fundamental conceptual error.  
- Proof of Theorem 1 contains logical gaps and nonsensical steps (e.g., applying KH to scalars), and required assumptions are neither clearly stated nor justified.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods (extended DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD) in accuracy, efficiency, and applicability.  
- Pro for acceptance: technically motivated kernel extension, clear exposition, and promising empirical applications.  
- Con for acceptance: core theoretical inconsistencies, flawed theorem proof, unjustified assumptions, and insufficient comparisons undermine the paper’s validity.  
- Recommendation: authors must clarify Koopman vs. feature-map relationship, provide a rigorous proof with explicit assumptions, and demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods.
