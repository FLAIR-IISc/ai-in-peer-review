- Presents a novel kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition for spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS.  
- Proposes a practical implementation that projects data onto POD/kernel PCA directions to improve numerical stability.  
- Demonstrates utility of extracted Koopman modes on state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with synthetic and real data.  
- Strong originality and significance by bridging Koopman operator theory and kernel methods to capture nonlinear dynamics.  
- Technical quality: well-motivated algorithm and practical robustness via POD projection; links between empirical Ritz values/vectors and true Koopman spectra are established.  
- Clarity and organization: paper is well-written, provides useful background, and presents diverse applications effectively.  
- Main weakness: ambiguous definition of the Koopman operator in feature space (KH) — unclear domain and how it acts on RKHS elements.  
- Theoretical proofs lack rigor due to the operator-definition ambiguity; derivations for Theorems 1 and 2 need more detailed, step-by-step exposition.  
- Assumptions are not explicit enough, notably the strong assumption that true Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the kernel principal component subspace.  
- Paper needs discussion of conditions under which the kernel-PCA subspace assumption holds and potential method limitations when it fails.  
- Recommendation: promising and significant contribution if theoretical ambiguities (operator definition and proofs) are clarified and assumptions are better justified.
