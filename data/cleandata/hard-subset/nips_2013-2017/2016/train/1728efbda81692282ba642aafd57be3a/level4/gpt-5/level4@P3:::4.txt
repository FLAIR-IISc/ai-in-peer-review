Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a kernelized approach to Koopman spectral analysis, extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors define a Koopman operator KH acting on a feature map ϕ and construct a Krylov subspace in the feature space based on a finite data sequence. Using a Gram–Schmidt QR factorization (implemented via kernel evaluations), they project KH onto the span of the feature-space Krylov vectors and compute its eigendecomposition. The resulting eigenvalues and eigenvectors play the role of empirical Koopman eigenvalues and modes, with an analogue of the classical DMD expansion. To improve numerical stability, they propose a robust variant that projects the data onto the top kernel principal components (POD directions in feature space) before estimating the reduced operator, and they provide a theorem elucidating when the true Koopman eigenfunctions are recovered by this reduced procedure.

The paper situates itself within the Koopman/DMD literature (Schmid; Rowley et al.; Tu et al.; Jovanović et al.; Chen et al.) and connects to kernel methods (Schölkopf et al.; Shawe-Taylor and Cristianini) and spectral learning of dynamical systems. The robustified kernel procedure is closely related to “extended DMD” (Williams et al.), which estimates Koopman spectra by lifting with a user-chosen dictionary; here the dictionary is implicitly defined by a kernel, and the algorithm uses a Krylov/QR perspective.

Empirically, the method is illustrated on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map, where eigenvalues are recovered qualitatively. The paper also sketches three applications that use the extracted features: short-term prediction via preimage estimation, dynamic pattern recognition via kernel principal angles between Koopman subspaces, and sequential change-point detection using a CUSUM-type criterion with a nonparametric exponential family in RKHS.

Quality:
- Theoretical soundness: The derivations are clear and technically correct under the stated assumptions (e.g., distinct empirical Ritz values, Koopman eigenfunctions lying in the span of the feature map or the kernel-PCA subspace). The connections to DMD via the Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint are well made. However, the assumptions in Theorem 2 (eigenfunctions in Φ and within the span of U) are strong; a discussion of approximation error when these are only approximately satisfied would strengthen the theoretical claims.
- Numerical stability and implementation: The paper rightly identifies ill-conditioning in Gram–Schmidt and proposes a kernel-PCA projection. It would help to discuss regularization (e.g., Tikhonov or truncated SVD) explicitly, and to quantify the impact of truncation on eigenvalue/mode estimates.
- Empirical support: The demonstrations are suggestive but limited. There is no quantitative comparison to non-kernel DMD, exact DMD, optimized DMD, or EDMD, nor is there a study of sensitivity to noise or to kernel hyperparameters. The application sections are useful as use cases but would benefit from metrics (prediction error, recognition accuracy, change detection delay/false alarm rates).

Clarity:
- The paper is generally well written and organized, with helpful pointers between sections. A few editorial issues remain (e.g., “robustly it” in Section 1; “almost of the theoretical claims” in Section 3), and some notation could be tightened (define Φ and H once, be consistent about centered vs. uncentered features, and explicitly state matrix dimensions in key steps). The link between the QR-based estimator and the companion matrix form could be explained more intuitively for readers less familiar with Arnoldi/companion matrices.
- The algorithmic steps are clear, and the kernel trick is used cleanly to avoid explicit feature computations. Providing a brief complexity analysis (time and memory in τ and p) would improve reproducibility and help readers assess scalability.

Originality:
- Kernelizing Koopman spectral analysis via a Krylov/QR construction is a natural but nontrivial extension. The robust variant that projects onto kernel-PCA directions closely resembles EDMD with a kernel-induced dictionary; the paper acknowledges this connection but should clarify the precise differences in estimator, assumptions, and practical behavior. In particular, an empirical comparison to EDMD (and exact/optimized DMD) would help situate the contribution and demonstrate advantages of the proposed formulation.

Significance:
- If supported by stronger experiments, this approach could be impactful: kernels provide a flexible, nonlinear dictionary without manual feature engineering, and the framework opens doors to feature-based tasks (recognition, change detection) with Koopman spectra. The current experiments show promise but do not yet establish clear performance advantages or robustness across settings. Addressing scalability (e.g., via Nyström approximations or random features) and providing guidance on kernel choice would increase practical significance.

Specific suggestions:
- Add quantitative benchmarks against DMD, exact DMD, optimized DMD, and EDMD on synthetic systems with known spectra (e.g., eigenvalue/mode errors), and on prediction tasks (RMSE vs. horizon).
- Provide an ablation study of the robustification (with/without kernel-PCA projection; impact of truncation level).
- Discuss kernel selection and hyperparameter tuning (e.g., bandwidth heuristics vs. cross-validation), and analyze sensitivity to kernel choice on the toy and Hénon systems.
- Include a brief complexity and memory analysis; comment on scalability to long trajectories and many trajectories; consider Nyström or random features to reduce O(τ^2) memory.
- Elaborate on handling noise and stochasticity (beyond the brief mention), possibly with experiments under added noise.
- For the preimage step, specify the algorithm used (e.g., Kwok and Tsang) and report its effect on predictive accuracy and runtime.

Pros:
- Clear, principled extension of Koopman/DMD to RKHS with kernelized Krylov and a well-motivated robust variant.
- Solid theoretical development with connections to Arnoldi/DMD and kernel PCA.
- Broad applicability and interesting downstream tasks (prediction, recognition, change detection).
- Generally well written; code availability is a plus.

Cons:
- Limited empirical evaluation; no quantitative comparisons to existing DMD/EDMD variants.
- Insufficient discussion of kernel choice, hyperparameter selection, and sensitivity.
- Strong assumptions in theorems without approximation/error analysis.
- Scalability and computational complexity not discussed in depth; preimage step lacks detail.

Recommendation:
This is a promising and well-presented contribution that could be of interest to the NIPS community working on dynamical systems, spectral methods, and kernels. I lean toward a weak accept provided the authors add quantitative comparisons to DMD/EDMD baselines, analyze kernel choices and robustness, and clarify scalability and implementation details. These additions would substantially strengthen both the scientific rigor and the practical value of the paper.
