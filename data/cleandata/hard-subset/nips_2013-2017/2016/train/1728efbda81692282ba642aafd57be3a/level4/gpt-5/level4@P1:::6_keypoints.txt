- Paper develops Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and proposes a kernelized DMD algorithm using a feature-space Krylov subspace and projected eigendecomposition.  
- Introduces a robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions to mitigate numerical ill-conditioning.  
- Demonstrated on a toy 2D nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and applications: short-term prediction (pre-image), change-point detection, and locomotion dynamics recognition.  
- Clear, largely self-contained exposition useful for ML readers unfamiliar with Koopman/DMD.  
- Principled extension of DMD to RKHS, enabling richer nonlinear basis functions via kernels.  
- Sensible numerical stabilization via kernel PCA projection, paralleling DMD/POD best practices.  
- Illustrative examples show potential downstream uses of Koopman features.  
- Practical usefulness for ML is not well established; most development and experiments are deterministic despite common stochastic ML settings.  
- Relation to prior work (EDMD, exact/optimized DMD) is underdeveloped; paper must better position its novelty and provide empirical comparisons.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, small-scale, and lacks systematic baselines (standard DMD, EDMD, exact/optimized DMD).  
- Unclear whether the entire algorithm is fully kernelized via Gram matrices or requires explicit feature maps; needs explicit clarification.  
- Computational complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice, bandwidth, truncation) are not discussed.  
- Pre-image step for prediction is ill-posed; MDS-style approach may be fragile and needs comparison to alternative pre-image methods.  
- Empirical claims lack quantitative metrics and statistical variability (eigenvalue error, RMSE, detection delays, classification accuracy).  
- Derivations appear correct but are unsurprising to readers familiar with kernel PCA/operator-theoretic kernel methods.  
- Originality is incremental: specific RKHS formulation and numerical procedure, but similar ideas exist in EDMD and kernel operator methods.  
- Significance is potential but not yet convincing for ML practitioners due to limited empirical validation on realistic/noisy tasks.  
- Suggested additions: direct comparisons to DMD/EDMD, explicit kernelization proof, sensitivity analyses, complexity/runtime reporting, stochastic/noisy experiments, and quantitative application evaluations.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear exposition, principled RKHS extension, practical stabilization, and cross-disciplinary appeal.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical validation, unclear gains over existing methods, scalability/stochasticity concerns, and incremental novelty.
