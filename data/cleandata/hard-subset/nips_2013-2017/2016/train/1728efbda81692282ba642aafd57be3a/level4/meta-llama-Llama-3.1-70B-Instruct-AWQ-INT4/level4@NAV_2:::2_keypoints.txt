- Presents a spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHS.
- Proposes a modal decomposition algorithm that extends DMD via Gram–Schmidt QR decomposition in feature space.
- Algorithm yields a finite-mode decomposition, acting as a feature-extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamics.
- Paper introduces Koopman operator and DMD, then extends the analysis using reproducing kernels.
- Mathematical development is correct but the algorithm may be ill-conditioned in practice.
- Proposes a POD-based modification to improve numerical robustness.
- Discusses related work: spectral analysis methods, DMD, subspace identification, and links to eigensystem realization.
- Notes that the relationship between DMD and other system-identification methods remains an open problem.
- Empirical evaluation uses synthetic examples, the Hénon map, and CMU motion-capture locomotion data.
- Demonstrated applications include prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.
- Strengths: clear, well-organized presentation, thorough related-work discussion, and empirical examples.
- Weakness: novelty appears limited to kernelizing DMD, which has prior presence in the literature.
- Weakness: applications are not sufficiently compelling to showcase unique advantages of the method.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons with other approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) for dynamics recognition and change detection.
- Evaluation: scores well on clarity and originality but could improve on quality and significance with stronger applications and comparisons.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear presentation, novel RKHS extension of DMD, and empirical demonstrations.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unimpressive applications, and missing comparative evaluations.
