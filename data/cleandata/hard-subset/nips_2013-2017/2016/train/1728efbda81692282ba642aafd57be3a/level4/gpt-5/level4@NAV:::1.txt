This manuscript proposes a spectral analysis framework based on the Koopman operator defined in an appropriate reproducing kernel Hilbert space. It then follows the standard route to construct a suite of kernel methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems from empirical data. The eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator is shown to provide efficiency gains on both synthetic and real-world tasks. Overall, the kernel construction for nonlinear dynamical systems is significant, and the contribution is well motivated and supported by experiments.

There are, however, several issues I would like the authors to address: I do not clearly see the (hopefully substantial) differences between this work and [35] M.O. Williams, I.G. Kevrekidis, and C.W. Rowley. A data-driven approximation of the Koopman operator: Extending dynamic mode decomposition. Journal of Nonlinear Science, 25:1307–1346, 2015, where Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes were already leveraged to study nonlinear dynamics. Please include comments and a careful comparison highlighting the main distinctions, and strengthen the discussion in Section 4 accordingly. The literature on kernel methods for nonlinear dynamical system analysis is extensive, and some pertinent citations are missing; please consider works by L. Ralaivola and F. d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, and M.O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf. While the Koopman operator is typically formulated for deterministic systems, I would appreciate comments on performance in stochastic settings, which are often the most relevant in practice. The manuscript briefly mentions this, but further discussion—and ideally a small illustrative experiment—would be welcome. Please clarify the remark (line 216, page 5) that “the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is supposed to be” nonlinearly pointwise related. I am interested in the implications of this assumption for multidimensional time series with possibly weak pointwise relations: what benefit does the proposed kernel construction provide in such cases? Relatedly, please elaborate on the causal assumption implicit in the setup. Regarding the experiments, the settings feel insufficiently ambitious: the prediction via pre-imaging follows a well-known template; under the “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” subsection, there is little discussion (and no experimental comparison) to the broad literature on time-series clustering (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods); and for the sequential change-point detection, a comparison with state-of-the-art kernel-based online approaches would be more compelling (the method used here is known to struggle with complex dynamics). Stronger experiments are needed to substantiate the proposed developments.

Minor: the paper is very clear and well written. Two small typos: please check indices τ−1 vs. τ, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation on p. 7).
