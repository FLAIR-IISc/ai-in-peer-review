- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze compositions of dynamics (x(t+2)=f(f(x(t)))) via eigen-decomposition on trajectory data.  
- Implements the decomposition with techniques akin to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems and reports good empirical performance.  
- Key claimed novelty: observing that function composition acts as a linear operator on observables, enabling spectral decompositions.  
- For kernel-method readers, the contribution largely amounts to kernelizing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).  
- DMD already exists in the dynamical-systems literature, so novelty appears limited.  
- Other established approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) exist for system recognition/change detection, undermining uniqueness claims.  
- The presented applications are not sufficiently compelling to demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods.
