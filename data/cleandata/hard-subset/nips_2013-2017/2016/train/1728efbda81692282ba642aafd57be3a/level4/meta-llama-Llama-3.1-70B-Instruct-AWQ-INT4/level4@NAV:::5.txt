Review: 
The manuscript "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" presents a novel extension of dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), yielding the KDMD algorithm. This approach enables the incorporation of feature maps as kernel definitions, thereby strengthening the connection between DMD and specific features of interest. Consequently, KDMD emerges as a powerful generalization of DMD, with promising applications to toy problems.

However, my primary concern lies in the manuscript's readability. The authors' tendency to prioritize mathematical notation over concise explanations may hinder comprehension, even for specialists. Certain sections are excessively verbose, while others lack clarity. To enhance the manuscript's accessibility and impact, I strongly recommend a pedagogical rewrite. A revised version could potentially garner significant citations.

Referee's report on: Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels

The manuscript "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" presents a novel extension of dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), yielding the KDMD algorithm. This approach enables the incorporation of feature maps as kernel definitions, thereby strengthening the connection between DMD and specific features of interest. Consequently, KDMD emerges as a powerful generalization of DMD, with promising applications to toy problems.

However, my primary concern lies in the manuscript's readability. The authors' tendency to prioritize mathematical notation over concise explanations may hinder comprehension, even for specialists. Certain sections are excessively verbose, while others lack clarity. To enhance the manuscript's accessibility and impact, I strongly recommend a pedagogical rewrite. A revised version could potentially garner significant citations.

I. MAJOR REMARKS

1. The presentation can be simplified by condensing complex ideas into concise sentences. For instance, the concept of DMD approximating Koopman eigenfunctions can be summarized more succinctly.
2. Section 4 appears redundant and could be omitted or merged with the introduction.
3. Section 5 would benefit from illustrations or a summary in the conclusion to enhance clarity.
4. Section 6 requires expansion, similar to Section 5, to provide a more comprehensive discussion.

II. MINOR REMARKS

1. A potential typo exists in the description of the toy NL case, which appears to be linear.
2. The first two problems are polynomial and could be used to demonstrate the strengths of KDMD when the kernel can be guessed, as in the case of Section 5. Additional details would be beneficial.
