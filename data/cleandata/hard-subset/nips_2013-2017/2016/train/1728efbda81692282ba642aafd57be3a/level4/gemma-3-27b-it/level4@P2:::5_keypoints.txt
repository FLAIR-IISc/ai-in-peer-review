- Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD): extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition into RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer nonlinear features.  
- Core idea is promising: leveraging kernel methods generalizes DMD and could improve spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Mathematical formulation is rigorous and connects to Arnoldi method and POD.  
- Robustification via POD is a practical attempt to address ill-conditioning.  
- Demonstrates potential applications: state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Writing is overly dense and verbose, making the paper hard to follow even for experts.  
- Balance between rigor and clarity is poor; the paper needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Some sections are repetitive or contain unnecessary detail (e.g., DMD description).  
- Applications section (Section 7) is underdeveloped with insufficient experimental detail and analysis.  
- Section 4 (POD addition) feels disconnected and not well integrated into the main narrative.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited, focusing on relatively simple systems rather than challenging, high-dimensional datasets.  
- Related work discussion is shallow; missing deeper coverage of kernel-based spectral methods and relevant citations (e.g., Brunton et al. 2015).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension with practical potential and an attempt to handle ill-conditioning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, weak application details, limited empirical scope, and the paper feels incomplete without substantial revision.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially valuable work but requires major rewriting for clarity, expanded experiments, and stronger integration of POD and related work before acceptance.
