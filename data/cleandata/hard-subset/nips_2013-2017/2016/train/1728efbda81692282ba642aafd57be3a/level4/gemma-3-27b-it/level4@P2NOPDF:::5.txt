## Review of “Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels”

This manuscript presents an extension of Dynamical Mode Decomposition (DMD) to the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in a new algorithm termed KDMD. The core strength of this work lies in its ability to leverage feature maps as kernel definitions, effectively linking DMD to specific features of interest – a powerful generalization of the original method. However, despite the potential significance of the contribution, the paper’s current presentation significantly hinders its accessibility.

My primary concern is the difficulty in understanding the material. The authors frequently employ unnecessarily complex mathematical phrasing when simpler, more concise language would suffice. Conversely, other sections are excessively verbose, creating a frustrating imbalance. This combination makes the paper challenging to follow, even for researchers familiar with the field. Consequently, the work, in its current form, is not suitable for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings.

However, I strongly believe that a substantial rewrite focused on pedagogical clarity could transform this manuscript into a highly influential piece of work. The underlying ideas are valuable, and a more accessible presentation would greatly enhance its impact.

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**

1. **Presentation & Verbosity:** The paper consistently suffers from unnecessarily complex presentation. For example, the statement “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point” could be conveyed far more effectively with a single, simpler sentence. Streamlining such passages throughout the paper would greatly improve readability.

2. **Section 4:** Section 4 appears largely redundant given its content is partially presented in the introduction and doesn't contribute significantly to a concise paper. Its removal or substantial condensation would be beneficial.

3. **Section 5:** The results presented in Section 5 require further elaboration. Either detailed illustrations should be included within the section itself, or a comprehensive summary of the key findings should be provided in the conclusion. The current treatment feels incomplete.

4. **Section 6:** Section 6 feels underdeveloped in comparison to Section 5. The authors have condensed the discussion too much, leaving important aspects underexplored. Expanding this section to resemble the detail provided in Section 5 would strengthen the paper.

**II. MINOR REMARKS**

1. **Typographical Error:** The description of the nonlinear toy problem appears to contain a typographical error, as the system appears to be linear rather than nonlinear.

2. **Example Selection:** The first two example problems presented are polynomial, which are scenarios where KDMD’s strengths – particularly the ability to benefit from a well-chosen kernel – should be most apparent. Greater detail regarding the kernel selection and its impact on performance in these examples is needed, similar to the level of detail provided in Section 5.



**REFERENCE**

1. Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, "Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control," http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.03007v2, (2015)
