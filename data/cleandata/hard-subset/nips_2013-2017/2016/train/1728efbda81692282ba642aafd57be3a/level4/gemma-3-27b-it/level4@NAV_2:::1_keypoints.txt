- Introduces kernel-based Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS and algorithms for finite-length nonlinear dynamical data.  
- Reduces analysis to computing orthogonal bases of the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator.  
- Clear motivation, solid theoretical development, and a well-defined algorithmic contribution.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets with promising applications (prediction, pattern recognition, change-point detection).  
- Unclear novelty/distinction from prior work, especially Williams et al. [35]; needs explicit comparison of differences and advantages.  
- Section 4 lacks sufficient justification and detailed explanation of its steps/assumptions.  
- Missing relevant literature on kernel-based dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Alvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Brief and insufficient treatment of stochastic systems; should expand discussion and ideally add a simple illustrative experiment.  
- Key data assumption (consecutive observables are nonlinearly related) needs further discussion, especially for multidimensional time series where pointwise relations may be weak.  
- Clarify how the kernel construction handles weak pointwise relations and state inherent causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is conservative: prediction via preimage is standard and embedding/recognition lacks comparisons to time-series clustering and subspace-kernel methods.  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detectors for stronger validation.  
- Minor typographical issues: τ-1 vs. τ inconsistency and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) on page 7.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel RKHS application to Koopman analysis, clear theory/algorithm, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Rejection risks: insufficient differentiation from related work, experimental comparisons lacking rigor, assumptions and stochastic applicability underexplored, and missing citations.
