- Develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm, with experiments on a damped 1D linear system, a chaotic map, and a locomotion dataset.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured derivation extending DMD-style spectral analysis into the RKHS setting with educational exposition.  
- Strength: Formalizes the natural idea of using kernel machinery to analyze linear operators on function spaces.  
- Concern: Novelty/positioning is weak—method largely echoes established kernelization of linear operator methods and needs sharper distinction from existing RKHS/operator and Koopman approaches (e.g., EDMD, kernel Koopman).  
- Concern: Practical usefulness for ML is not demonstrated—unclear which ML-relevant problems benefit and how the approach handles stochasticity, process/measurement noise, or innovations.  
- Concern: Empirical evaluation is limited and benefits over baselines are unclear; more rigorous comparisons and metrics are needed.  
- Empirical suggestions: Include standard DMD results on toy systems, quantitative metrics with confidence intervals, ablation studies (kernel choice, regularization, sample size), and at least one ML-relevant benchmark (e.g., robotics, forecasting).  
- Algorithmic clarity: Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit features or can be done entirely via Gram matrices; need explicit out-of-sample evaluation procedure for modes/eigenfunctions.  
- Scalability concern: No complexity/memory analysis; kernel methods can be cubic in sample size—discuss mitigations (Nyström, random features, low-rank approximations).  
- Stability/identifiability: Need analysis of stability and sensitivity of estimated eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under finite samples and noise.  
- Suggestions: Articulate a concrete ML use case where the method offers clear advantages and benchmark against strong baselines (DMD/EDMD, state-space models, modern forecasting methods).  
- Suggestions: Expand experiments to include robustness tests (noise, sample size) and hyperparameter selection impact (kernel, bandwidth, regularization).  
- Technical clarifications requested: Whether the method operates entirely via kernel trick, how eigenfunctions/modes are computed, normalized and evaluated at new points, and what regularization is required.  
- Literature positioning: Provide a more explicit relationship to prior RKHS operator literature and Koopman-based ML methods, highlighting the genuinely new theoretical, algorithmic, or empirical contributions.  
- Overall: Well written and potentially useful bridge between operator-theoretic dynamics and kernel methods, but needs stronger positioning and more compelling empirical evidence to persuade an ML audience.
