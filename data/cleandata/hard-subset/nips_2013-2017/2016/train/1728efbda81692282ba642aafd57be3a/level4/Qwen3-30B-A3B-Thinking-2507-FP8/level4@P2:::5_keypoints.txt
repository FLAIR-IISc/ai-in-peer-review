- Proposes KDMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Develops a theoretical framework using kernel-based feature maps to generalize DMD beyond linear monomial bases.  
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposes the projected Koopman operator for modal decomposition.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Situates work within Koopman/DMD literature and contrasts with POD (captures dynamics via eigenvalues/modes).  
- Relates to prior methods (e.g., Williams et al.'s extended DMD) but does not clearly distinguish contributions.  
- Strength: more flexible nonlinear representations via kernel selection, allowing domain-specific features.  
- Strength: mathematically sound derivation and promising empirical utility across tasks.  
- Major weakness: poor clarity and inconsistent writing—verbose in places and cryptic in others, reducing accessibility.  
- Section 4 ("Robustifying with POD Bases") is redundant and should be removed or condensed.  
- Section 5 ("Related Works") reads like an unfocused literature review and needs clearer connection to contributions or consolidation.  
- Section 6 (empirical examples) is too condensed and should be expanded with more detail like Section 5.  
- Toy nonlinear example may actually be linear/polynomial, and the paper fails to explain why kernel choice matters for such systems.  
- Originality is moderate—the RKHS extension is reasonable but not sufficiently distinguished from extended DMD.  
- Significance is promising but undermined by presentation; manuscript needs substantial revision to realize impact.  
- Recommendations: rewrite concisely and pedagogically, remove/condense redundant sections, expand empirical details, clarify toy example and kernel selection, and better contrast with extended DMD.
