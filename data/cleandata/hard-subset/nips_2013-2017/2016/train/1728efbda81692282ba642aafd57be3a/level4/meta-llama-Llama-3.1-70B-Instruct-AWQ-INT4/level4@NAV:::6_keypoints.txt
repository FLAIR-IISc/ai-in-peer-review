- Paper develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes an algorithm for analyzing measurement data from dynamical systems.  
- Demonstrations include a damped 1D linear system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data, with comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA that yield ambiguous gains.  
- The work is an interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition and resembles prior “kernelized” linear-operator techniques (kernel PCA, kernel ICA, kernel tests).  
- Experimental evaluation focuses on largely artificial problems, limiting evidence of real-world machine-learning impact.  
- The paper is well-written and provides educational value bridging fluid-dynamics techniques to ML audiences.  
- The authors must better demonstrate practical usefulness for ML, since many ML dynamical systems include stochasticity not covered here.  
- The manuscript should explore relevant use cases and stronger comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., in robotics/inference for dynamical systems).  
- The theoretical derivations are solid but not particularly surprising given similarities to existing kernel methods.  
- A key technical question: does the modal decomposition require explicit feature-space representations (a potential limitation versus kernel PCA)?  
- The authors should include baseline results from standard dynamic mode decomposition on the toy example and the Henon map to clarify differences.
