- Paper proposes a spectral/kernal-based Koopman operator approach for nonlinear dynamics, using function composition to obtain a linear representation.  
- Method focuses on the second iterate x(t+2)=f(f(x(t))) rather than the standard first iterate, without clear justification.  
- Authors present an eigen-decomposition method inspired by kernel PCA and subspace analysis to approximate the Koopman operator from trajectory data.  
- Applied to dynamical system embedding and recognition tasks with reported strong empirical performance, but results are described vaguely.  
- Core novelty is framed as “kernelizing” DMD, which appears incremental relative to existing Koopman/DMD and kernel-method literature.  
- Paper fails to explain why kernelization is necessary or uniquely beneficial for function composition analysis.  
- Evaluation lacks comparisons to relevant, state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., RNNs, LSTMs, change-point detection), weakening empirical claims.  
- Experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate superiority, efficiency, or interpretability advantages over existing methods.  
- Technical exposition is ambiguous: connection to kernelized DMD is under-detailed and algorithmic/computational aspects are unclear.  
- Missing specifics on kernel choice, computational complexity, and scalability to high-dimensional systems.  
- Overall contribution seems incremental and insufficiently validated; needs clearer problem framing and stronger empirical/theoretical support.
