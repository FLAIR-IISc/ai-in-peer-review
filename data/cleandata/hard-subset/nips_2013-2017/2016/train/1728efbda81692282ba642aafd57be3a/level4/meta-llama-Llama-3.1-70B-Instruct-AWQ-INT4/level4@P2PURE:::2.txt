The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. This method applies the Koopman operator decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, specifically x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given collected trajectories from the system, the operator can be decomposed using an eigen-decomposition approach similar to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, demonstrating promising results. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the novelty of this paper lies in representing the composition of functions as a linear operator and applying interesting decompositions to function compositions. Dynamical systems, being a particular type of function composition, serve as a suitable application of this concept. However, since the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm has already been proposed in the dynamical system literature, the paper's novelty appears to be limited to its kernelized approach. Furthermore, other methods exist for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, and for detecting changes. Therefore, the applications presented in the paper are not entirely compelling in demonstrating the method's uniqueness and advantages.
