- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS with a data-driven algorithm, bridging fluid dynamics and ML.  
- Well-written and educational, helpful for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Core contribution is kernelizing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), analogous to prior kernelized linear methods.  
- Empirical validation includes two toy systems (damped linear 1D, Henon map) and a real locomotion dataset, but locomotion results are inconclusive.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound but not surprising to those familiar with kernel methods.  
- Paper lacks a compelling argument for practical utility in mainstream ML, especially compared to existing methods.  
- Focus on deterministic dynamical systems is limiting; stochastic settings with noise are more common in ML applications.  
- Authors should articulate concrete ML applications (e.g., robotics control/prediction, time-series forecasting, system identification) and show competitive performance or unique insights.  
- Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation; if so, this is a major limitation versus kernel PCA.  
- Add baseline comparisons of standard DMD to the proposed method on the toy examples and Henon map.  
- Improve presentation of locomotion results with rigorous quantitative comparisons and detailed discussion of observed differences.
