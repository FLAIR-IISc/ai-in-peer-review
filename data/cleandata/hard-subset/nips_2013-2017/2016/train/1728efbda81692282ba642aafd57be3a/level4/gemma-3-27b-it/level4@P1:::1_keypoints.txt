- Introduces Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with empirical eigendecomposition showing efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Novel kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems; well-motivated and experimentally validated.  
- Unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015); authors should clarify substantive differences and relate methods.  
- Section 4 needs clearer exposition and discussion.  
- Several relevant kernel-based dynamical systems references are missing and should be cited (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Limited discussion and evaluation for stochastic systems; request further analysis and possibly a toy experiment.  
- Clarify implications of using collections of consecutive observable pairs vs. sequences, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships.  
- Explain the causal assumption inherent in the method and how the proposed kernel helps in weak pointwise or multidimensional settings.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; prediction via preimage is standard and needs stronger validation.  
- Missing comparisons to time-series clustering and subspace-kernel methods (e.g., kernel k-means) for embedding/recognition tasks.  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods.  
- More convincing and broader experiments are required to support claims.  
- Minor typos: check indices on τ−1 vs. τ and replace dot with comma in k(xi, xj) (page 7).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel RKHS application to Koopman analysis, clear theory and algorithms, effective on datasets, potential for feature extraction.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient differentiation from prior work, limited experiments/comparisons, and inadequate discussion of limitations (stochasticity, causality).
