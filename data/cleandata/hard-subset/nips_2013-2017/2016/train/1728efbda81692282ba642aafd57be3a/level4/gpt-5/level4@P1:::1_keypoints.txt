- Paper proposes a kernelized Koopman/DMD method in RKHS, projecting the Koopman operator onto a Krylov subspace and computing eigenpairs via Gram-based QR and eigendecomposition.  
- Robust variant projects onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to address numerical instability and rank-deficiency.  
- Demonstrated applications: short-term prediction via preimage recovery, embedding/recognition using kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection with a CUSUM-like RKHS model.  
- Experiments conducted on toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, motion-capture sequences, and Lorenz map.  
- Method is conceptually a clean RKHS extension of Koopman spectral analysis with derivations relying only on kernel evaluations.  
- Robust kernel-POD mirrors practical DMD truncation and regularization practices.  
- Flexible approach: works with consecutive sequences or collections of pairs and supports multiple downstream tasks.  
- Paper is generally well written and algorithmic exposition is clear.  
- Insufficient discussion of differences and novelty relative to extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015); needs clarification on operator estimation, projection/truncation, numerical and theoretical distinctions.  
- Missing related-kernel literature references (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Does not explore stochastic dynamics empirically; needs discussion and experiments on noise effects on eigenvalues and modes.  
- Unclear assumptions about data and causality: robustness to weakly causal, partially observed, or exogenous-input settings should be discussed.  
- Experiments lack ambition and comparisons to strong baselines (time-series clustering, kernel subspace methods, state-of-the-art kernel change detection).  
- Originality is plausible but close to extended DMD with kernel dictionaries; novelty claims should be sharpened.  
- Significance is potential but limited by shallow empirical evaluation and incomplete comparisons.  
- Minor technical/typo issues: inconsistent τ−1 vs. τ indices and a dot should be replaced by a comma in k(xi, xj).
