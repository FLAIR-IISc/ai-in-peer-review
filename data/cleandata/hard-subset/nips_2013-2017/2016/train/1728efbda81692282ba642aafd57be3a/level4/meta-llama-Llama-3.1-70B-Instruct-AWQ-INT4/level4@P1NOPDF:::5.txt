The manuscript "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" extends the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the KDMD algorithm. The authors apply KDMD to several toy problems, demonstrating its ability to use feature maps as kernel definitions, thereby linking DMD to specific features of interest. This represents a powerful generalization of DMD.

My primary concern is that the paper is challenging to read. The authors often prioritize mathematical notation over concise language, even when explaining simple concepts. Some sections are verbose, which may hinder understanding, even for specialists. A few points require clarification. While the content is valuable, the presentation is not suitable for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite the paper in a more pedagogical style, as the resulting article has the potential to be highly cited.

I. MAJOR REMARKS

1. The presentation is overly verbose in some areas and could be simplified. For example, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be condensed into a shorter, more straightforward sentence.

2. Section 4 appears unnecessary in a short paper, as it partially reiterates the introduction.

3. Section 5 would benefit from illustrations or a summary in the conclusion to enhance clarity.

4. Section 6 feels condensed and would be more effective if developed in a similar manner to Section 5.

II. MINOR REMARKS

1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy NL case, as it appears to be linear.

2. The first two problems are actually polynomial. KDMD is expected to excel in situations where the kernel can be guessed, as in the case of the first problem. More details, similar to those provided in Section 5, would be beneficial.
