- Proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Frames the Koopman operator as an operator representation of function composition.  
- Applies Koopman operator decomposition specifically to forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).  
- Uses trajectory data to perform eigen-decomposition via ideas similar to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition of dynamical systems with reported good performance.  
- Main novelty: representing function composition as a linear operator to enable decompositions of compositions.  
- Notes that dynamical systems are a natural application of the composition-based operator viewpoint.  
- Contribution seems limited because dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists; this work appears to mainly kernelize DMD.  
- Points out competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks, change-detection methods) for recognizing dynamical systems.  
- Critiques the applications as not very compelling in showing why the method is particularly good or unique.
