- Paper studies Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS by kernelizing DMD, computing Ritz values/modes on Krylov subspaces and adding a kernel-PCA robust variant; demos include toy systems, Hénon map, motion data, and change detection.
- Strength: well-structured and readable with explicit algorithmic steps and clear connection to DMD/Koopman theory.
- Strength: technically interesting—lifting DMD into RKHS yields richer nonlinear Koopman approximations; kernel-PCA robustification is practically useful.
- Strength: method has potential significance as a feature extractor for nonlinear dynamics and is useful for downstream tasks.
- Strength: empirical examples are informative and show meaningful spectra and useful features.
- Major concern: ambiguous operator definitions in Section 3—KH is treated inconsistently (acting on feature maps vs. scalar observables); domain/codomain of the composition operator must be made precise.
- Request: clarify whether H is an RKHS of scalar functions with canonical feature map ψ(x)=k(x,·) and the operator is the usual composition C_f, or whether analysis is restricted to the finite subspace Φ and KH defined only on Φ.
- Theorem/proof issue: proofs invoke KH on objects not clearly in its domain and use adjoints/projections without explicit conditions; revise to ensure mathematical correctness.
- Request: explicitly state assumptions before Theorem 1—linear independence of {ϕ(x_t)}, existence of QR in induced inner product, distinct Ritz values/diagonalizability, and when residual ψ=0 for exact recovery.
- Relation to prior work: approach is very close to EDMD/kernel EDMD—clarify precise relationship to Williams et al. (2015) and to exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting DMD; include direct empirical comparisons (spectra, reconstruction error).
- Robustification details missing: clarify centering consistency for Mτ and M+, impact of nonstationarity on Gram matrix centering, truncation criteria for p, and sensitivity to p.
- Computational concerns: report computational complexity, numerical stability/conditioning of S̄, and whether Tikhonov regularization is needed.
- Applications under-specified: pre-image algorithm lacks details on method, initialization, convergence; provide quantitative prediction errors against baselines (EDMD, local models) and sensitivity to kernel/bandwidth.
- Double-blind violation: public code link compromises anonymity and must be removed for review; state code will be released upon acceptance.
- Quality assessment: central idea is sound and impactful but operator-theoretic foundations and Theorem 1 require careful revision; experiments are promising but small and lack baseline comparisons.
- Clarity assessment: Section 3 needs precise functional-analytic definitions (what H is, domain/codomain of KH, adjoints, composition invariance); avoid ambiguous phrases like “apply KH to both sides.”
- Originality assessment: kernelization of DMD is natural and closely related to EDMD; sharpen novelty claims relative to prior kernel/EDMD work.
- Significance assessment: method could be a useful addition to Koopman/DMD tools if theoretical clarifications and comparisons are addressed.
- Pros for acceptance: interesting/practically relevant RKHS extension of DMD, clear algorithmic pipeline, robust variant, and demonstrated downstream utility.
- Cons for acceptance: ambiguities in operator definitions and proofs, limited empirical comparisons to strong baselines, under-specified implementation details, and double-blind violation.
- Minor comments/typos: fix “the the” (Line 55), correct κj/λj indexing (Line 153), define Q* as adjoint and clarify Hermitian transpose usage, discuss repeated/near-repeated eigenvalues and noisy data handling, consider reporting eigenvalue confidence (e.g., bootstrap).
