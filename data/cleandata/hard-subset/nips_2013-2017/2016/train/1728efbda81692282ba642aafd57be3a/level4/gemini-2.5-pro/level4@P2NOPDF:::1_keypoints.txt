1. Presents an RKHS-based Koopman spectral analysis framework enabling kernel methods and computationally efficient eigendecomposition, validated on synthetic and real data.

2. Major concern: unclear novelty relative to Williams et al. [35]; authors must explicitly state theoretical and practical differences.

3. Major concern: literature review is incomplete and should discuss additional relevant works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).

4. Major concern: limited treatment of stochastic systems—need discussion of robustness to noise and preferably a small-scale stochastic experiment.

5. Major concern: clarify key assumptions—explain implications of using collections of consecutive observable pairs instead of full sequences, especially for multidimensional time series.

6. Major concern: clarify and discuss the inherent causal assumption implied by the method and its limitations for applicability.

7. Major concern: experiments could be stronger—prediction task uses standard pre-imaging (obscures Koopman benefits), embedding/recognition lacks comparisons to time-series clustering methods, and change-point detection needs stronger online kernel baselines.

8. Minor: check for consistent indexing (e.g., τ−1 vs. τ).

9. Minor: notation typo in kernel argument—use k(xi, xj) instead of k(xi.xj).
