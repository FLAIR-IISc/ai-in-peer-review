- Paper proposes KDMD: a kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition using RKHS feature maps, Krylov matrices, and finite projections to approximate Koopman spectra.
- Two algorithmic variants are presented: a direct QR-based approach and a numerically robust kernel PCA/POD projection method.
- The authors prove that empirical Ritz values/vectors from these projections correspond to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions in feature space under standard assumptions.
- Applications sketched include short-term prediction via preimage, sequential change-point detection with a kernelized CUSUM score, and dynamics recognition via principal angles between feature subspaces.
- Strength: Clear operator-theoretic generalization allowing implicit nonlinear feature dictionaries via kernels.
- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding with precise statements and a robust variant addressing ill-conditioning.
- Strength: Versatile framework useful for feature extraction in prediction, change detection, and recognition tasks.
- Strength: Practical kernelizable implementation and publicly available code.
- Weakness: Exposition is dense and often cryptic; the paper needs simpler prose and explicit algorithmic steps.
- Suggestion: Provide concise algorithm boxes for both KDMD variants with kernel-only operations highlighted.
- Suggestion: Move lengthy proofs and technical derivations to an appendix and keep intuition/usage in the main text.
- Suggestion: Unify notation and clearly state assumptions (e.g., centered vs. uncentered features, distinct eigenvalues, data provenance).
- Section feedback: Consolidate the robustification section with the main method and defer detailed derivations to the appendix.
- Section feedback: Shorten and sharpen the related work, explicitly contrasting KDMD with EDMD, exact/optimized DMD, and kernel subspace methods.
- Experimental critique: Current experiments are illustrative but insufficient; add quantitative evaluations and strong baselines.
- Experimental suggestions: Compare KDMD to exact/optimized DMD and EDMD with polynomial/RBF dictionaries on systems with known spectra, reporting eigenvalue/mode errors and predictive RMSE.
- Experimental suggestions: Perform sensitivity analyses over kernel choice, hyperparameters (RBF bandwidth, polynomial degree), and kernel-POD truncation levels.
- Experimental suggestions: Report computational complexity and scaling (time/memory) and detail numerical stabilization methods (regularization, truncation, centering).
- Application-specific needs: For prediction, include quantitative short-horizon results, clarify preimage methods, and discuss failure modes.
- Application-specific needs: For change-point detection, provide ROC/delay analyses, multiple traces, and justify likelihood approximations.
- Application-specific needs: For dynamics recognition, report classification metrics, confusion matrices, and explain kernel principal angle choices.
- Originality concern: KDMD closely relates to EDMD with kernel dictionaries; clarify precise differences, assumptions, computational steps, and added theoretical guarantees.
- Quality: Derivations seem sound but key assumptions must be stated prominently and applicability to pairs vs. trajectories clarified.
- Reproducibility: Preimage and CUSUM details are insufficient for reproducibility and robustness assessment.
- Minor: Clarify the “toy nonlinear” example, give kernel selection guidance, fix typos and figure readability, and provide pseudo-code with kernel-only operations.
- Pros for acceptance: Solid general framework linking Koopman analysis and kernel methods, theoretically grounded with code and potential broad impact.
- Cons for acceptance: Overly dense exposition, limited empirical validation and baselines, and unclear novelty relative to EDMD.
- Recommendation: Revise for clarity, explicitly position relative to EDMD/DMD variants, and substantially strengthen empirical comparisons before acceptance.
