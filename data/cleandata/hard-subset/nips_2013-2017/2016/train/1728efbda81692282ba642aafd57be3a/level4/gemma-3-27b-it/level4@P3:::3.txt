This paper derives a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and presents a modal decomposition algorithm for performing this analysis. The authors also discuss a robustifying method based on projecting onto POD directions and demonstrate the approach with several numerical experiments. The core contribution extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) by leveraging the kernel trick to potentially capture more complex nonlinear dynamics.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors present the theoretical concepts underpinning their method in a comprehensive manner, and the overall framework appears promising. The technical development seems sound, and while a deeper comparison to state-of-the-art approaches would be beneficial, the provided numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method's potential. 

However, certain aspects of the derivation require further clarification. Specifically, the motivation and justification for Equation (7) are unclear. While resembling Equation (2), the purpose of redefining the Koopman operator in this way is not immediately apparent. Moreover, the statement that *KH* is a linear operator from *H* to itself, while φ is a function from *M* to *H*, raises a concern. The authors should elaborate on this point; it’s crucial to understand how the operator acts on the functional space. This ambiguity casts doubt on the subsequent proof (Lines 144-157). The phrase “by applying the operator *KH* to both sides” (Line 149) is problematic, as applying a linear operator from *H* to *H* to scalar values is not well-defined. 

The assumptions underlying Theorem 1 appear strong and lack sufficient justification. Providing more context and explaining the necessity of these assumptions would significantly improve the paper. Additional assumptions surfacing within the proof require similar treatment – the authors should clarify whether these assumptions naturally hold or require specific conditions.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The core methodology appears mathematically robust.
*   **Novel Extension:** Extending DMD with kernel methods is a valuable contribution, potentially enabling the analysis of more complex nonlinear systems.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments provide initial evidence of the method’s effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Unclear Motivation (Equation 7):** The purpose and justification for redefining the Koopman operator are not adequately explained.
*   **Ambiguous Operator Application:** The application of *KH* to scalar values in the proof is unclear and potentially incorrect.
*   **Strong Assumptions:** The assumptions in Theorem 1 are not sufficiently justified, and additional assumptions appear within the proof without explanation.
*   **Limited Comparative Analysis:** While experiments are provided, a more thorough comparison with existing DMD variants and related techniques would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel extension to DMD that has the potential to improve the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
*   The mathematical development, despite some points requiring clarification, appears generally sound.
*   The authors demonstrate the method’s feasibility with empirical results.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The lack of clarity surrounding Equation (7) and the associated proof raises serious concerns about the validity of the theoretical foundation.
*   The strong and unexplained assumptions limit the generalizability of the results.
*   The limited comparative analysis makes it difficult to assess the true contribution of this work.

**Overall Assessment:**

I currently rate this paper as a **weak reject**. While the authors present an interesting and potentially valuable extension to DMD, the significant concerns regarding the clarity of the theoretical derivation and the lack of justification for key assumptions outweigh the strengths of the work. Addressing these issues—particularly providing a more rigorous and understandable explanation of Equation (7) and the associated proof—is essential for the paper to be considered for acceptance. A more detailed comparison to existing methods would also strengthen the contribution.
