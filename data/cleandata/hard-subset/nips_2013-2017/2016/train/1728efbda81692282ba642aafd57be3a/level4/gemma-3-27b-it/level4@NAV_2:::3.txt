This paper derives a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and presents a modal decomposition algorithm for this analysis. The authors also address robustness and demonstrate the method with several numerical experiments. The core contribution extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) through the application of kernel methods. 

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors present the theoretical concepts underpinning their method in a lucid manner, and the approach appears promising. The technical foundation seems sound, and while a detailed comparison to state-of-the-art techniques is limited, the included numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method’s potential.

However, there are points requiring clarification. Specifically, the motivation behind Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1 are not entirely convincing as presented. Equation (7) closely resembles Equation (2), raising the question of its specific purpose. Furthermore, the equation appears potentially inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator mapping between Hilbert spaces, while ϕ is a function from the manifold M to H, and is therefore not an element of H itself. Could the authors elaborate on this point? This ambiguity casts some doubt on the subsequent proof (Lines 144-157). The phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as both sides appear to be scalar values, making the application of a Hilbert space operator ill-defined. The assumptions of Theorem 1 seem strong and could benefit from further explicit justification. Additionally, implicit assumptions appear during the proof; clarifying whether these assumptions are generally valid would strengthen the argument.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a novel extension of DMD using kernel methods, which could broaden the applicability of spectral analysis techniques.
*   The theoretical development is presented clearly and the method is technically sound, assuming the clarifications requested are addressed.
*   The numerical experiments demonstrate the feasibility and potential utility of the proposed approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of a thorough comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods limits the assessment of the contribution’s significance.
*   The unclear explanations in Section 3.1, particularly surrounding Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, raise concerns about the mathematical rigor of the derivation.
*   The strong and potentially implicit assumptions underlying the theoretical results require further justification.

**Minor points:**

*   Line 55: “the the” should be “the”.
*   Line 153:  “\kappaj / \lambdaj” appears to be a typo and should likely be “\kappai / \lambda_i”.
