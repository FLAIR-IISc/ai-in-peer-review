

This paper introduces a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator within reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences from nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors further develop a robust implementation and demonstrate the method's utility through various numerical examples. Overall, the work represents an extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) utilizing kernel methods.

Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The authors clearly present the theoretical foundations underlying their method, which appears to be both novel and technically sound. The experimental validation is appropriately conducted and effectively demonstrates the method's utility. While the comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is somewhat limited, the experimental results adequately support the claims made.

Weaknesses: The most significant concern pertains to Section 3.1 and particularly Equation (7), along with the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears to be a restatement of Equation (2), raising questions about its purpose. Additionally, Equation (7) seems inconsistent: KH is defined as a linear operator mapping H to H, while φ is a function from M to H, not an element of H. This raises doubts about the mathematical foundation of the proof (lines 144-157). The phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" (line 149) is problematic because both sides of the equation are scalar values, and KH is an operator from H to H, making this operation undefined. The assumptions underlying Theorem 1 appear strong and not sufficiently explicit. The proof introduces additional assumptions not mentioned in the theorem statement - it would be helpful to clarify whether these assumptions are reasonable and easily satisfied in practice.
