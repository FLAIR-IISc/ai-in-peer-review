- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and presents an algorithm to compute it from measurement data.  
- Relevance of Koopman analysis to machine learning is not convincingly established.  
- Experiments use two simple dynamical systems (damped 1D, chaotic map) and real locomotion data; comparative results with DMD/PCA/kernel PCA are unclear.  
- The method is an interesting extension of DMD but closely resembles prior “kernelizing” approaches (kernel PCA, kernel ICA), so theory is unsurprising.  
- Experiments are largely artificial and may not demonstrate real-world applicability, though they have educational value.  
- Paper is well-written and useful for introducing ML readers to fluid-dynamics techniques.  
- Authors need stronger evidence of practical usefulness, concrete use cases, and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., in robotics).  
- Theoretical derivations are correct but offer limited novelty beyond established kernel methods.  
- Clarify whether modal decomposition requires an explicit feature-space representation and state this limitation if true.  
- Recommend demonstrating standard DMD on the toy examples and the Henon map to illustrate differences.
