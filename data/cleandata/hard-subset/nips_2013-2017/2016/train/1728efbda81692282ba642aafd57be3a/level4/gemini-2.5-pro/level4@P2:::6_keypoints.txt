- Paper proposes Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition: spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS using the kernel trick.
- Method extracts modes from nonlinear dynamical data and is framed as a feature-extraction technique for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Evaluated on a toy system, the chaotic Hénon map, and a motion-capture dataset.
- Main contribution: links Koopman/DMD theory with kernel methods, "kernelizing" spectral analysis for nonlinear dynamics.
- Strength: well-written with clear, technically sound derivations that bridge dynamical-systems and ML concepts.
- Strength: original application of the kernel trick to Koopman spectral analysis, offering educational value.
- Strength: potential for cross-disciplinary impact by introducing ML audiences to Koopman tools.
- Weakness: limited motivation for ML audiences since the approach focuses on deterministic dynamics while many ML models handle stochasticity.
- Weakness: empirical validation is insufficient—no direct comparison to standard linear DMD and real-data results are only qualitative (MDS plot).
- Weakness: perceived as incremental because kernelizing linear methods is a known idea; stronger empirical evidence is needed to justify novelty.
- Request: clarify specific ML applications where deterministic Koopman analysis outperforms stochastic models (e.g., robotics, control).
- Request: add comparisons with standard DMD and relevant baselines (e.g., Kernel PCA, time-series feature extractors) on benchmark tasks.
- Request: clarify whether the algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations or relies solely on the Gram matrix (kernel trick).
- Request: expand on extending the framework to stochastic systems and handling process/measurement noise.
- Overall: elegant and theoretically sound extension of DMD via kernels, but needs stronger ML motivation and more convincing empirical results.
