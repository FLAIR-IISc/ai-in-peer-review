- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm for finite-length data from nonlinear systems.  
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases of the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposes the Koopman projection.  
- Produces a modal decomposition that serves as a feature-extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Relates to prior work (DMD, POD), with clear, concise theoretical derivations and good writing.  
- Demonstrates on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map, and real locomotion data, with comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.  
- Experimental results are somewhat unclear and difficult to evaluate.  
- Main strength is educational value and a clear introduction to the Koopman operator for non-experts.  
- Needs stronger ML-oriented motivation: more use cases and demonstrations against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Unclear whether an explicit feature-space representation is required; if so, this is a limitation versus kernel PCA.  
- Should report standard DMD performance on the toy examples and Henon map to clarify differences.  
- Should provide computational complexity and scalability analysis for large datasets.  
- Technically sound and well-organized, but originality is limited by building on existing kernel and DMD methods.  
- Significance is limited because practical applicability to real-world ML problems is not well demonstrated.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel RKHS extension of DMD, clear Koopman exposition, and strong educational value.  
- Rejection arguments: lacks clear practical use cases, unclear results, and limited novelty.
