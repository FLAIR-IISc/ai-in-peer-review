- Introduces spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS, extending DMD to handle nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Proposes an algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and eigendecomposes the projected Koopman operator for modal decomposition in RKHS.
- Produces a finite-mode decomposition usable as a feature-extraction procedure; demonstrated on state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Builds on classic Koopman and DMD theory and connects the approach to kernel methods, enabling richer nonlinear basis functions than standard DMD.
- Mathematical derivations are technically sound and include a POD-based robustification to address numerical instability.
- Algorithmic description is clear, but the choice to use explicit feature-space representations rather than staying in kernel space is not sufficiently justified.
- Experiments include two synthetic systems and real locomotion data, but lack strong baselines and sufficient comparison to establish advantages over existing ML methods.
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, with consistent notation and effective figures (e.g., eigenvalue estimation visualization).
- Originality is moderate: the RKHS extension is non-trivial but follows common kernelization patterns; the relation to Williams et al. (2015) needs clearer differentiation.
- Main weakness is significance: the paper fails to convincingly show practical utility for machine learning practitioners.
- The locomotion example is promising but does not clearly demonstrate superiority to simpler alternatives.
- Suggested improvement: explicitly compare standard DMD and the proposed RKHS method on toy examples to show kernel advantages.
- Suggested improvement: articulate concrete ML applications (e.g., robotics) where the method would outperform current approaches.
- Suggested improvement: discuss trade-offs between explicit feature representations and implementing the kernel trick.
- Overall recommendation: technically solid and educational, recommend acceptance conditional on stronger justification of practical ML utility.
