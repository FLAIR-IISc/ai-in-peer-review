This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm to facilitate this analysis. The authors also propose a robustification method and conduct multiple numerical experiments to demonstrate the efficacy of their approach. The overall framework represents an extension of dynamical mode decomposition, leveraging the kernel trick. 

The paper's primary strength lies in its clear and organized presentation, effectively conveying the theoretical concepts underlying the method. The authors provide a technically sound derivation, and although a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is lacking, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method's usefulness. 

However, several concerns need to be addressed. A major limitation of the paper is Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which are not entirely clear. The similarity between Equation (7) and Equation (2) raises questions about the purpose of the former. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas \phi is not in H. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of this point, as it affects the validity of the proof in Lines 144-157. 

Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as KH is an operator from H to H, but both sides are scalar values. The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong and require further clarification. It is also concerning that additional assumptions are introduced in the proof of the theorem, and the authors should explain why these assumptions are necessary and whether they can be easily satisfied.

Minor errors include a typo in Line 55 ("the the") and an incorrect notation in Line 153 (\kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i).
