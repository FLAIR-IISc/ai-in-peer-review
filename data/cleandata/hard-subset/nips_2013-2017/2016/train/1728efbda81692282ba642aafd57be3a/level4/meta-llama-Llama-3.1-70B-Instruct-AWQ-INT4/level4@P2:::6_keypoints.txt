- Paper proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm using finite-length data via Krylov bases and eigen-decomposition of projected Koopman operator.  
- Clear, well-written introduction explaining the Koopman operator and its relevance to machine learning.  
- Demonstrations include a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map, and real locomotion data, with comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.  
- Presents an interesting kernelized extension of DMD, connecting to prior kernel methods.  
- Educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Experiments are largely on artificial problems and need stronger real-world validation for ML audiences.  
- Authors must better articulate practical use cases and compete with state-of-the-art in application domains (e.g., robotics).  
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; this limitation should be clarified.  
- Should include results of standard DMD on toy examples (e.g., Henon map) to highlight differences.  
- Original contribution: new RKHS-based extension of DMD and novel application of Koopman operator to ML.  
- Potential significance: could advance analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems with implications for robotics and fluid dynamics.  
- Quality: well-written, clear notation, and well-presented experiments but lacks broader comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Clarity: paper is organized, easy to follow, and consistent in notation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing comparisons, practical use cases, and clarity on feature-space requirements.
