- Proposes a spectral analysis approach for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator to represent function composition as a linear operator.  
- Applies Koopman operator decomposition to forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))) and performs eigen-decomposition on trajectory data.  
- Methodology is inspired by kernel PCA and subspace analysis, effectively "kernelizing" operator-based decompositions.  
- Demonstrates application to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems with promising empirical results.  
- Main novelty claim: representing composition as a linear operator and applying kernel-based decompositions to it.  
- Major concern: strong similarity to existing Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) methods, casting doubt on originality.  
- Practical value questioned because alternative approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) can perform system recognition/change detection, making the contribution less compelling.
