This paper introduces Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), an extension of the Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) algorithm to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS). By lifting the dynamics into an RKHS, the method seeks a finite-dimensional approximation of the Koopman operator for nonlinear systems. This generalization is powerful, as it allows the feature map defined by the kernel to be tailored to specific features of interest in the system. The proposed algorithm is evaluated on several illustrative examples.

**Major Remarks**

The core contribution of this work—extending DMD using kernel methods—is a significant and valuable idea. It offers a principled framework for applying linear analysis techniques to nonlinear systems and has the potential to become a widely used tool. If presented clearly, this work could be highly influential and frequently cited.

However, the primary weakness of the manuscript is its presentation, which currently poses a significant barrier to comprehension. The paper is exceptionally difficult to read, even for a specialist familiar with both kernel methods and dynamical systems. My main concerns are as follows:

1.  **Lack of Clarity and Inconsistent Pacing:** The authors often favor dense mathematical formalism where simpler, more intuitive explanations would be more effective and accessible. For example, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be conveyed more succinctly. In contrast, other sections of the paper are unnecessarily verbose without adding substantive insight. This inconsistent style makes the paper challenging to navigate.

2.  **Ineffective Structure:** The organization of the paper detracts from its message.
    *   **Section 4 (Related Work):** This section feels largely redundant, as much of its content is already covered or could be integrated into the introduction. In a conference paper with strict space limitations, this section could be significantly condensed.
    *   **Section 5 (Kernel Selection):** The discussion on choosing an appropriate kernel is crucial for any practical application of KDMD, yet it remains purely abstract. This section would be far more impactful if it were illustrated with concrete examples or visualizations showing how different kernel choices affect the resulting Koopman spectral analysis.
    *   **Section 6 (Experiments):** In sharp contrast to the preceding section, the experimental validation is overly condensed. This section should be the primary demonstration of the method's value. It needs to be expanded to more clearly connect the theoretical choices (i.e., the kernels discussed in Sec. 5) to the practical outcomes on the test systems.

**Minor Remarks**

1.  There appears to be a typo in the description of the "NL case" toy problem; the dynamics as written seem to be linear.
2.  The first two experimental systems are polynomial. This is an ideal scenario for KDMD to demonstrate its strength, particularly if an appropriate polynomial kernel is chosen. The paper misses an opportunity to explicitly link the theory from Section 5 to the experiments in Section 6 by discussing the rationale behind the kernel selection for these specific problems. More detail here would strengthen the paper's central argument.

**Recommendation**

While the core idea of this paper is excellent and holds great promise, the manuscript in its current form is not ready for publication. The lack of clarity and poor organization obscure this valuable contribution.

I strongly encourage the authors to undertake a substantial revision focused on improving the pedagogical clarity and logical flow of the paper. A revised version that is made more accessible to the broader neural information processing and dynamical systems communities would likely be very well-received. I believe a clearer exposition of this work would lead to a highly impactful publication.
