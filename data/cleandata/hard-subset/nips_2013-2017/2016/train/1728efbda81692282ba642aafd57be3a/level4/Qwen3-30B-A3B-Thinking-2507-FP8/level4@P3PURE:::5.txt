

The manuscript, entitled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extends the dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in the kernel DMD (KDMD) algorithm. This approach allows the use of feature maps to define the kernel, thereby linking DMD more directly to specific features of interest, representing a powerful generalization of DMD. My primary concern is the paper's poor readability. The authors overemphasize complex mathematical notation for straightforward concepts, while other sections are unnecessarily verbose. This obscurity impedes comprehension even for specialists, and several points require clarification. The current presentation is too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. However, I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite the paper with greater pedagogical clarity; the revised article would likely be highly influential.

I. MAJOR REMARKS  
1.  The presentation is excessively verbose where simplicity suffices. For instance, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be concisely rephrased as: "DMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear monomials of observables, analogous to a first-order Taylor expansion."  
2.  Section 4 is redundant with the introduction and adds little value to a concise paper.  
3.  Section 5 requires illustrative examples or a summary in the conclusion to demonstrate utility.  
4.  Section 6 should be expanded with the same level of detail as Section 5, as its current conciseness obscures key insights.  

II. MINOR REMARKS  
1.  The description of the nonlinear toy case likely contains a typo, as the example appears linear.  
2.  The first two problems are polynomial in nature—precisely where KDMD should excel, particularly when the kernel can be explicitly constructed (as noted in Section 5). The authors should provide more detailed analysis of these cases, as done in Section 5.
