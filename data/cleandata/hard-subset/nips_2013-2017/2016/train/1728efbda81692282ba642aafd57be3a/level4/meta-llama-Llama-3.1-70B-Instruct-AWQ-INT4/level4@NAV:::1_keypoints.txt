- Presents a novel spectral analysis using the Koopman operator in an RKHS with well-motivated kernel construction and empirical validation.  
- Uses standard kernel-based methodology for nonlinear dynamical systems and demonstrates efficiency gains via eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Unclear distinction from M.O. Williams et al. (2015); needs a discussion clarifying differences, especially regarding Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions/modes and an improved Section 4.  
- Missing relevant literature on kernel methods for dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Koopman operator is typically deterministic; paper should discuss performance in stochastic settings and include a deeper discussion plus a toy experiment.  
- Clarify implications of using collections of consecutive-observable pairs rather than sequences, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Explain and justify the causal assumption inherent in the setting.  
- Experimental evaluation is somewhat unambitious; prediction via premapping is standard and needs stronger baselines.  
- Embedding and recognition experiments should connect to and compare with time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods.  
- More convincing and diverse experiments are needed to support the methodological claims.  
- Minor typos: check indices tau-1 vs. tau and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
