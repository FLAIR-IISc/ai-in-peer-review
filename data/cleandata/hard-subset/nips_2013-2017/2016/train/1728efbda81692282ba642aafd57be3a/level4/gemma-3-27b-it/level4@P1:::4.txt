This paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm based on calculating orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in the RKHS and performing eigendecomposition on the projection of the Koopman operator. The method is evaluated on both simulated and real-world data. This is a well-written and clearly presented paper. 

The core idea – leveraging the expressiveness of kernel methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions – is a natural and promising extension of existing DMD techniques. The theoretical development appears sound, and the connection to standard DMD is well-articulated. The presentation of Theorem 1 and 2 is particularly helpful in understanding the underlying principles. The inclusion of both a robustification strategy using POD bases (Section 4) and applications to prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection (Section 7) demonstrates a good understanding of the potential of the proposed method.

However, while the empirical results are illustrative, a more direct comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis (e.g., using linear monomials as basis functions) on the synthetic data would strengthen the evaluation. This would help to quantify the benefits of using reproducing kernels, especially in scenarios where the underlying eigenfunctions are highly nonlinear. 

Furthermore, a discussion of kernel selection and its impact on the results would be valuable. The paper mentions using a polynomial kernel and a Gaussian kernel, but provides limited justification for these choices. Exploring the sensitivity of the method to different kernel parameters and types, and perhaps providing guidelines for kernel selection, would enhance the practical applicability of the work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Soundness:** The theoretical foundation is well-developed and builds upon established concepts in Koopman spectral analysis and DMD.
*   **Novelty:** Extending Koopman analysis to RKHSs represents a meaningful contribution to the field.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow, even for readers not intimately familiar with all the underlying concepts.
*   **Potential Impact:** The proposed method has the potential to improve the accuracy and applicability of Koopman-based approaches for nonlinear system analysis and control.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Comparative Evaluation:** The lack of a direct comparison to standard DMD limits the ability to fully assess the advantages of the proposed method.
*   **Kernel Selection Discussion:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of kernel selection and its influence on the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear and compelling contribution. The minor weaknesses outlined above do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I believe this paper is suitable for acceptance at NIPS, and the suggestions for improvement would further enhance its impact.
