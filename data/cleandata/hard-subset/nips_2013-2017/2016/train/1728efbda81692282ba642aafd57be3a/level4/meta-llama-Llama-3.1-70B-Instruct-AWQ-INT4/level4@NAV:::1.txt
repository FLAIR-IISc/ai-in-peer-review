This paper presents a novel spectral analysis approach utilizing the Koopman operator defined within a proper reproducing kernel Hilbert space. The authors adhere to the standard methodology for defining kernel-based methods to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data. The eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator yields efficiency gains in both synthetic and real data analysis problems. This work introduces a significant kernel construction for nonlinear dynamical system analysis, which is well-motivated and experimentally validated.

However, there are some concerns that require the authors' attention:

The distinction between the current work and the paper by M.O. Williams et al. (2015) is unclear, particularly regarding the introduction of Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system dynamics analysis. I would appreciate a discussion clarifying the main differences and an improved explanation in Section 4.

The field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is vast, and some relevant references are missing, including works by L. Ralaivola and F. d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, or M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf. 

Although the Koopman operator is generally defined for deterministic systems, I would appreciate a discussion on its performance in stochastic settings, which are more realistic. The authors briefly touch upon this issue, but a more in-depth discussion and a toy experiment would be beneficial.

The authors' remark on the estimation procedure not requiring a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables (line 216, page 5) raises questions about the implications of this assumption in multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations. I would like to see a discussion on the benefits of the proposed kernel construction in this context.

Furthermore, I would appreciate an elaboration on the causal assumption inherent in the setting.

Regarding the experimental settings, I find them somewhat unambitious. The prediction via premapping follows a standard approach, and the "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" subsection lacks a discussion on connections and experimental comparisons with the literature on clustering time series (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods). The sequential change-point detection experiment would benefit from a comparison to state-of-the-art online change detection with kernels.

Some more convincing experiments are expected to support the development.

On a minor note, the paper is well-written and clear, but I spotted two minor typos: please check the indices on tau-1 vs. tau, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
