The paper proposes a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, which is an operator representation of function composition. The Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, and the authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The algorithm is essentially reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases.

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the Koopman operator and dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), and the authors extend the analysis to RKHSs. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, and the use of RKHSs allows for the approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions. The authors also provide a robustified variant of the algorithm using proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) bases, which can help to improve the numerical stability of the method.

The paper includes several empirical examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, including the analysis of a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data from the CMU Graphics Lab Motion Capture Database. The authors also demonstrate the application of the method to prediction, sequential change-point detection, and the recognition of dynamic patterns.

While the paper is well-written and the proposed method is interesting, the novelty of the paper seems to be limited to the kernelization of the DMD algorithm. The algorithm itself has been proposed already in the dynamical system literature, and the applications in the paper are not particularly compelling in demonstrating the uniqueness of the method. There are other methods for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, and for detecting changes. Therefore, it would be beneficial for the authors to provide more detailed comparisons with existing methods and to highlight the advantages of their approach.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the Koopman operator and DMD, and the proposed algorithm is well-motivated. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide sufficient details about the implementation of the method. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with existing methods and a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel application of the Koopman operator to nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm to perform the analysis in RKHSs.
* The paper includes several empirical examples to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The authors demonstrate the application of the method to prediction, sequential change-point detection, and the recognition of dynamic patterns.
