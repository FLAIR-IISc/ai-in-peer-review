- Paper proposes kernelized DMD in an RKHS: orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix, eigendecomposition of projected Koopman operator, and a robust kernel-POD variant; applications include short-term prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Well written and organized with a clear narrative and technically solid derivation.

- Core idea of casting Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS and implementing kernelized DMD via Gram–Schmidt/QR and eigendecomposition is interesting and timely.

- Robustification via kernel POD is well motivated and addresses numerical weaknesses of vanilla DMD.

- Empirical demonstrations on synthetic and real data show the method’s potential and downstream utility of extracted modes.

- Major notation/type issue: Eq. (7) and the definition of KH/ϕ are confusing — ϕ is treated like a map into H but KH is presented as an operator on H; need to clarify whether H is an RKHS of scalar functions and ϕ(x)=κ_x.

- If H is instead a space of H-valued functions, the paper must explicitly define spaces/domains; as written Eq. (7) appears to have a type mismatch.

- Theorem 1 proof error: the phrase “applying KH to both sides” is invalid for the scalar/finite-dimensional identities used; the step should be rewritten using the correct linear-algebraic relation (e.g., MτF = K_H Mτ with F = R^{-1}PτR).

- Theorem 1 assumptions are strong and incompletely stated — require explicit listing (distinct eigenvalues, full column rank of Mτ, Krylov subspace dimension, decomposition in Eq. (10), invariance/diagonalizability, Vandermonde structure) and justification of how generic they are.

- Technical soundness depends on fixing operator-domain and representer vs. feature-map distinctions; the numerical kernel procedures (QR via Gram matrices, kernel POD) are standard and sound.

- Experimental section is modest but supportive; stronger comparisons to related methods (e.g., extended DMD) would strengthen the evaluation.

- Clarity: Section 3 and the proof of Theorem 1 need careful rewriting to resolve type/notation ambiguity and to make all theorem assumptions explicit.

- Originality: extension to RKHS is closely related to extended DMD and existing kernel methods — incrementally novel but sensible.

- Significance: method could be practically useful for nonlinear Koopman mode extraction and the robust variant addresses real numerical issues; applications indicate potential impact.

- Pros: clear motivation, coherent approach, practical robustification, and useful application demos.

- Cons: confusing/possibly incorrect notation around KH and ϕ, strong/unstated assumptions in Theorem 1, and limited comparison to state-of-the-art.

- Minor edits: fix typo “the the” (Line 55) and index mismatch at Line 153 (use κj/λj).
