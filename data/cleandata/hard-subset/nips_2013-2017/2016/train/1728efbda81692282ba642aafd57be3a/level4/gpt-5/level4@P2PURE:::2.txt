The paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The Koopman operator is presented as an operator representation of function composition. Here, the Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given trajectories collected from the system, one can perform an eigen-decomposition of the operator using ideas similar to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, the method is applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the authors show that it performs well.

For readers familiar with kernel methods, the main novelty claimed is that function composition can be represented as a linear operator, enabling interesting decompositions on compositions of functions. A dynamical system is a particular composition of functions, making it a natural application of this idea. However, since dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, the contribution here appears limited to kernelizing it. There are also other methods for recognizing dynamical systems, such as recurrent neural networks, as well as approaches for detecting changes. In this sense, the applications presented in the paper are not very compelling in demonstrating why the method is particularly good or unique.
