- Introduces a method for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHS.  
- Leverages linearity of the Koopman operator to perform eigen-decomposition on observed trajectories.  
- Combines kernel methods and subspace analysis to extend dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Demonstrates feasibility on tasks: dynamics embedding, recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Framed as kernelization of DMD, building on established dynamical systems and kernel literature.  
- Strength: Clear problem formulation and motivation for using the Koopman operator.  
- Strength: Sound theoretical foundation linking RKHS, DMD, and kernel PCA.  
- Strength: Kernel approach has potential to capture richer nonlinear eigenfunctions than linear methods.  
- Strength: Empirical examples illustrate basic performance and feasibility.  
- Strength: Good positioning within existing work, referencing POD and DMD.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—kernelizing DMD is not a large conceptual leap.  
- Weakness: Demonstrated applications are standard and do not show clear superiority over alternatives.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of computational cost and scalability of kernel methods.  
- Weakness: Does not adequately address sensitivity to kernel parameter selection or tuning guidance.  
- Weakness: Related work on kernelized/extended DMD variants is not thoroughly discussed.  
- For acceptance: Provides a mathematically rigorous RKHS extension of DMD and a clear, well-structured approach.  
- For rejection: Limited novelty, unimpressive applications, and insufficient treatment of practical issues.  
- Overall: Technically sound but incrementally novel; reviewer leans weak reject unless authors (1) show clear advantage on a challenging benchmark, (2) discuss scalability and parameter sensitivity, and (3) better position work among kernelized DMD methods.
