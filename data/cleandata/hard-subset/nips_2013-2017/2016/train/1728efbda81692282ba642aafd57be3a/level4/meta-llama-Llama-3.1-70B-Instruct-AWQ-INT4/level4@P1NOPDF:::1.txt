This paper introduces a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator defined in a proper reproducing kernel Hilbert space, and then follows the standard pathway to define a set of (kernel) approaches for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data. The eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator results in efficiency gains in both synthetic and real data analysis problems. This paper introduces a significant kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems, with a well-motivated contribution that is experimentally validated.

However, there are some concerns that I hope the authors will address. First, it is not clear to me what the substantial differences are between the present work and the paper by Williams et al. [35], where the Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes were already introduced to analyze nonlinear system dynamics. I would appreciate some comments and discussion clarifying the main differences. Additionally, the discussion in Section 4 could be improved. The field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is vast, and some relevant references are missing, including papers by Ralaivola and d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid and Schaback, and Franz and Schölkopf.

Although the Koopman operator is generally defined for deterministic systems, I would appreciate some comments on its performance in stochastic settings, which are more realistic and important. The authors briefly touch upon this issue, but further discussion and a toy experiment would be welcome. The authors clarify the remark on line 216, page 5, that the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is supposed to be nonlinearly pointwise related. I am curious about the implications of this assumption in multidimensional time series with potentially weak pointwise relations: what do we gain with the proposed kernel construction? Could the authors discuss this point? Related to this, I would also appreciate it if the authors could elaborate on the causal assumption inherent in the setting.

Regarding the experimental settings, I find them not very ambitious. Essentially, prediction via premapping follows a standard well-known approach. Under the "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" subsection, there is a lack of discussion on the connections (and experimental comparison) between the proposed approach and the literature on clustering time series (e.g., through kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods). When it comes to the sequential change-point detection experiment, it would be more appealing to see a comparison to state-of-the-art online change detection with kernels (e.g., the one used by the authors is known to fail in complex dynamics). Some more convincing experiments are expected to support the development.

Minorly, the paper is very well written and clear. I spotted two minor typos: please check the indices on tau-1 vs. tau, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
