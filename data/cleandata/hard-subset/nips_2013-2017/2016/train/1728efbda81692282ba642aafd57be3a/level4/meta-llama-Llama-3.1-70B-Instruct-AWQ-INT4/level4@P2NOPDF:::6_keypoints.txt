- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to compute it from measurement data.
- Evaluated on a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map (Henon), and real-world locomotion data; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA yield unclear outcomes.
- Interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition that builds on prior "kernelizing" linear-operator techniques (kernel PCA, kernel ICA, etc.).
- Paper is well-written and has educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics-based methods.
- Technique focuses on deterministic M→M dynamics; most ML dynamical models include stochastic effects, raising questions about practical relevance.
- Needs more context and concrete use cases (e.g., robotics) to convince ML audiences of practical utility and where Koopman analysis adds value.
- Theoretical derivations are solid but not surprising given existing kernel-method results—operator application reduces to finite-D eigendecomposition.
- Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, state this limitation relative to kernel PCA.
- Recommend running and reporting standard DMD on the toy examples (and Henon) to illustrate differences and advantages.
- Overall contribution is educational and interdisciplinary, but practical implications require more context, comparisons, and clarification.
