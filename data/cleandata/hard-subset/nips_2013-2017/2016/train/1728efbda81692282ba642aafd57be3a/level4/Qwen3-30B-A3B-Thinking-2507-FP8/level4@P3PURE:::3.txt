

This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman composition operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm based on this analysis. The authors extend dynamical mode decomposition via the kernel trick, incorporate a robustification technique, and validate their approach through numerical experiments. The paper is well-written and technically sound, with clear exposition of theoretical foundations. While the numerical results adequately demonstrate the method's utility, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art approaches; however, this omission is acceptable given the demonstrated effectiveness.  

The primary concern centers on Section 3.1 and Theorem 1. Specifically, Equation (7) appears redundant with Equation (2), raising questions about its purpose. More critically, Equation (7) is inconsistent: $K_H$ is a linear operator from $\mathcal{H}$ to $\mathcal{H}$, while $\phi$ (the feature map) is a function from the state space $\mathcal{M}$ to $\mathcal{H}$, meaning $\phi$ itself is not an element of $\mathcal{H}$. The authors must clarify how Equation (7) is well-defined. This ambiguity extends to the proof of Theorem 1 (Lines 144–157), particularly the phrase "by applying the operator $K_H$ to both sides" (Line 149). Since both sides of the equation are scalar quantities, applying $K_H : \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$ is ill-defined. Additionally, the assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicitly justified. The proof introduces further assumptions not stated in the theorem; the authors should explain the necessity and plausibility of these assumptions.
