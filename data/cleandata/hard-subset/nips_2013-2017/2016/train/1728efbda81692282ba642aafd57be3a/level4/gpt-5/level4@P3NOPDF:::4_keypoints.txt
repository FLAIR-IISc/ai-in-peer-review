- Summary: Extends Koopman spectral analysis into an RKHS framework with a kernel-based estimator, evaluated on simulated and real data; clearly written and well organized.

- Strength: Clear motivation and strong positioning linking Koopman theory and RKHS methods.

- Strength: Derivations are principled and technically sound, balancing theory and algorithms.

- Strength: Empirical validation on simulated and real datasets demonstrates practical utility.

- Strength: Lucid exposition and easy-to-follow results.

- Improvement: Include direct comparisons with non-kernel Koopman methods (e.g., DMD/EDMD) on the same systems to contextualize RKHS gains and costs.

- Improvement: Systematically explore kernel families (RBF, polynomial, Laplacian) and hyperparameters to show effects on spectra, eigenfunctions, and predictions.

- Improvement: Provide practical kernel selection/tuning strategies (median heuristic, cross-validation, reconstruction/prediction or spectral-stability criteria).

- Improvement: Perform sensitivity/ablation analysis on kernel parameters and regularization strength to assess robustness and trade-offs.

- Suggestion: Present explicit computational complexity and memory analysis, and discuss low-rank approximations (Nyström, random features) and their impact on spectral accuracy.

- Suggestion: Clarify theoretical conditions for consistency and stability (ergodicity, mixing, sampling) and provide convergence diagnostics or guarantees.

- Suggestion: Assess noise robustness to observation/process noise and examine whether RKHS regularization mitigates noise-induced spectral artifacts.

- Suggestion: Offer model selection guidance for choosing number of components or truncation thresholds (scree plots, spectral gaps, cross-validated reconstruction).

- Suggestion: Improve interpretability by relating learned spectral objects to domain-specific structures in real-data experiments.

- Suggestion: Fully specify experimental details (splits, horizons, preprocessing, kernel/reg parameters) and report variance across runs for reproducibility.

- Suggestion: Include quantitative comparison metrics relevant to Koopman analysis (prediction/reconstruction error across horizons, eigenvalue stability across bootstraps).

- Minor: Clarify notation when moving between operator/function-space and finite-sample/matrix representations.

- Minor: Improve figure readability (labels, font sizes, units) and add confidence intervals for spectral estimates where appropriate.

- Minor: Provide code or pseudocode to aid reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Strong, meaningful contribution that would benefit from direct non-kernel comparisons and deeper empirical kernel/hyperparameter analysis; recommend acceptance pending these additions.
