- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Applies the Koopman decomposition specifically to forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).
- Decomposes the operator via eigen-decomposition analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis on collected trajectories.
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks with promising empirical results.
- Novelty highlighted as representing function composition as a linear operator and applying kernelized decompositions.
- Notes dynamical systems are a natural and suitable application for this composition-based view.
- Novelty is limited because dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) already exists in the dynamical-systems literature.
- The main contribution seems to be the kernelized version of existing DMD-like approaches rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.
- Competing methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and existing change-detection techniques challenge the method’s distinctiveness.
- The applications presented do not fully convince that the approach offers clear, unique advantages.
