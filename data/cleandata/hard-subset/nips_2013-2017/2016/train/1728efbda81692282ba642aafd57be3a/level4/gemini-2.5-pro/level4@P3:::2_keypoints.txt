- Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD): extend DMD to RKHS to analyze the Koopman operator and approximate its eigenvalues/eigenfunctions from data via Krylov subspace methods.  
- Presents two algorithmic variants: a direct Gram–Schmidt formulation and a robust kPCA-projected version.  
- Uses learned Koopman modes as features for downstream tasks (state prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection).  
- Strength: principled, elegant fusion of Koopman spectral theory and RKHS enabling implicit nonlinear observables.  
- Strength: mathematically sound derivations (Theorems 1–2) and a sensible robustification analogous to POD truncation.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition with helpful background and logical progression.  
- Strength: versatile framing as a feature-extraction tool with diverse application demonstrations.  
- Weakness: limited originality — largely a kernelization of DMD and conceptually close to extended DMD (eDMD).  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical comparison — experiments are illustrative but lack rigorous baselines (e.g., GPs, RNNs for prediction; quantitative classifiers for recognition; broader change-point methods).  
- Weakness: missing practical guidance on kernel choice/parameter selection and scalability concerns due to Gram matrix complexity.  
- For acceptance: theoretically solid, complete algorithmic pipeline, clear presentation, and useful framing for the community.  
- Against acceptance: incremental novelty and weak experimental validation make practical impact unclear.  
- Overall verdict: borderline but leans toward rejection; recommends stronger empirical validation and clearer distinction from eDMD in a revision.
