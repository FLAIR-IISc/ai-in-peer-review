- Paper proposes a kernelized DMD in RKHS to approximate Koopman spectral objects using Gram-matrix-based Arnoldi/QR and a POD-projected robust variant.
- Situates work among DMD/EDMD and kernel methods, but novelty relative to kernelized EDMD is not fully distinguished.

Strengths:
- Presents a clear, principled extension of DMD to RKHS enabling nonlinear features without hand-designed dictionaries.
- Algorithmic pipeline is explicit in terms of Gram matrices, making the kernelization implementable.
- Kernelization increases expressiveness, bridging DMD toward EDMD-like richness while keeping a compact matrix form.
- Educational value: good introduction to Koopman analysis for non-fluid-mechanics readers and links to ML.

Weaknesses — Theory:
- Main theorems rely on strong, unverifiable assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, eigenfunctions in feature span, etc.).
- No error bounds, consistency discussion, or perturbation analysis for finite samples and noise.

Weaknesses — Numerical/Algorithmic:
- Robust variant lacks guidance on truncation choice, regularization, and conditioning.
- No comparisons with exact/optimized DMD, EDMD, or kernel EDMD to substantiate robustness claims.

Weaknesses — Empirics:
- Experiments are limited, figures hard to interpret, and Hénon results are qualitative.
- Applications (prediction, change detection, recognition) lack quantitative metrics, baselines, and ablations.

Clarity and presentation issues:
- Typos and inconsistent notation (e.g., φ used ambiguously); some incorrect or overgeneralized statements about Koopman spectrum.
- Needs self-contained pseudocode, complexity estimates (O(τ^3) time, O(τ^2) memory), and practical guidance on kernel/bandwidth/truncation.
- Some references incomplete; must explicitly cite kernel EDMD and recent DMD variants.

Originality:
- Kernelizing DMD is a natural extension; QR/Arnoldi-in-RKHS and POD-projection are well-articulated but not clearly more novel than existing kernel EDMD.

Significance:
- Promising as a dictionary-free nonlinear Koopman tool, but current empirical evidence is insufficient to demonstrate impact for ML applications.

Specific requests/suggestions:
- Clarify that computations use Gram matrices so explicit feature maps are unnecessary, and provide computational complexity.
- Add baselines: standard DMD, exact/optimized DMD, EDMD with polynomial/RBF dictionaries, and kernel EDMD.
- Discuss and empirically test robustness to process and measurement noise (stochastic Koopman operator).
- Address handling of repeated/near-degenerate eigenvalues (e.g., Schur-based methods or regularized solvers).
- Compare pre-image/prediction methods to established approaches and report quantitative short-horizon prediction errors.
- Justify and compare the change-point detection surrogate to MMD/two-sample and sequential detectors.
- For dynamics recognition, report classification metrics, confusion matrices, feature dimensionality, and kernel sensitivity.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, principled kernelized DMD formulation linking Koopman analysis and kernel methods.
- Potentially practical for nonlinear systems without manual dictionaries and educationally valuable.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited empirical validation and weak baseline comparisons.
- Strong theoretical assumptions without error/noise analysis and some clarity/notation issues.
- Applications are anecdotal and lack quantitative evaluation.

Recommendation:
- Borderline reject: method is promising but needs stronger empirical evidence, clearer positioning vs EDMD/kernel EDMD, and improved theoretical and practical guidance.
