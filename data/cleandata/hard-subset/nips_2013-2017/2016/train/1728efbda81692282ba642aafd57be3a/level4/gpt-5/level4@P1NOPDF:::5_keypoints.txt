- Paper extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition into an RKHS (KDMD), linking DMD to chosen feature maps and making a powerful generalization of standard DMD.  
- Contribution is significant and could be highly cited if the manuscript is made more readable.  
- Overall exposition is too formal, verbose, and cryptic; prefer concise, plain prose over dense multi-clause mathematical sentences.  
- Streamline presentation throughout; many passages can be shortened without losing rigor.  
- Section 4 adds little beyond the introduction and should be removed or its essentials folded into the introduction.  
- Section 5 needs an illustrative example or schematic to build intuition, or at minimum its key takeaways summarized in the conclusion.  
- Section 6 is overly condensed and should be expanded with implementation guidance and clearer detail comparable to Section 5.  
- Check the “toy NL” example for a possible typo or clarify why it is nonlinear, since the description appears linear.  
- The first two test problems are polynomial (where KDMD should excel); provide more discussion and detail to substantiate this advantage.
