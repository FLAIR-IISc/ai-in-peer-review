- Summary: Proposes kernelized DMD (KDMD) to approximate Koopman spectral objects in RKHS, connecting kernels/feature maps to DMD and demonstrating on toy problems.

- Strength: Conceptually strong—kernelizing DMD is a natural, flexible way to tailor observables and encode prior structure.

- Strength: Potentially high impact if clarified—KDMD could become a widely used tool for data-driven nonlinear dynamics.

- Strength: Practical flexibility—kernels offer a pragmatic route to encode features like polynomial observables.

- Weakness: Exposition is dense, overly formal, and often unclear; overall readability is poor.

- Weakness: Organizational problems—redundant sections and overly compressed important material.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to simple toy examples and some descriptions appear ambiguous or inconsistent.

- Major comment 1 (Exposition): Simplify exposition—introduce key objects intuitively first, move lengthy derivations to an appendix, use a running example to anchor notation.

- Major comment 2 (Section 4): Section 4 overlaps the introduction and should be removed or merged to save space and improve flow.

- Major comment 3 (Section 5): Section 5 needs concrete illustration or should be condensed and summarized; add a worked example or move conceptual content to conclusion.

- Major comment 4 (Section 6): Expand Section 6 to detail how KDMD estimates eigenvalues/eigenfunctions, reconstructs modes, clarify assumptions, algorithmic steps, and include a small illustrative example.

- Major comment 5 (Kernel choice): Make kernel–dynamics matching explicit, explain why polynomial kernels work for polynomial systems, and provide guidance on kernel selection and its effects on approximation and numerical stability.

- Minor comment 1: Verify a possible typo—“toy NL” system appears linear; correct the label or system description.

- Minor comment 2: Expand details for the polynomial examples (exact kernel, rank truncation, conditioning, comparison to standard DMD).

- Minor comment 3 (Editorial): Fix terminology and style (e.g., “feature map”), reduce redundancy, tighten sentences, and remove nonessential asides.

- Overall assessment: Conceptually strong and potentially influential, but reject in current form due to cryptic, uneven exposition; encourage a pedagogical rewrite with clearer examples and kernel-selection guidance.
