This paper introduces a spectral approach for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems based on the Koopman operator, an operator representing function composition. Specifically, the authors apply Koopman operator decomposition to the system’s forward dynamics, defined by a discrete-time evolution x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Utilizing trajectories obtained from the system, they perform an eigen-decomposition of this operator, drawing parallels with kernel Principal Component Analysis and subspace analysis techniques. The method is then demonstrated for embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, achieving promising results. For readers acquainted with kernel methods, the core contribution lies in representing function composition as a linear operator and subsequently applying decomposition techniques to these compositions – a particularly effective application within the context of dynamical systems. However, given the existing Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) algorithm in the dynamical systems literature, the novelty appears primarily focused on a kernelized extension of this concept. Furthermore, alternative methods for dynamical system recognition, such as recurrent neural networks, and change detection already exist. Consequently, the presented applications, while functional, do not strongly establish a compelling advantage or unique capability for this method.
