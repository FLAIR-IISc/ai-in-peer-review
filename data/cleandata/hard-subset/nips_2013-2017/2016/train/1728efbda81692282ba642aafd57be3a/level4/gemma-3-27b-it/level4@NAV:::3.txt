This paper investigates the spectral analysis of the Koopman operator within the framework of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs), and introduces a corresponding modal decomposition algorithm. The authors also address robustness concerns and validate their methodology through several numerical experiments. The work represents an extension of dynamical mode decomposition, leveraging the kernel trick. The paper’s strengths lie in its clarity of presentation and strong technical foundation; the theoretical concepts are explained well and the overall approach appears promising. While a comprehensive comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods is lacking, the numerical results effectively demonstrate the potential of the proposed method, which I find adequate. However, I have several significant concerns detailed below.

Major concerns center on Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1, which require clarification. The purpose of Equation (7) is unclear given its similarity to Equation (2). Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as *K<sub>H</sub>* is a linear operator mapping *H* to itself, while *ϕ* is a function from *M* to *H*, and not an element of *H*. The authors should elaborate on this point. This ambiguity casts doubt on the validity of the proof presented in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the meaning of "by applying the operator *K<sub>H</sub>* to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as *K<sub>H</sub>* operates on elements of *H*, while both sides of the equation at that point are scalar values. The assumptions outlined in Theorem 1 seem restrictive and lack sufficient detail. The authors should provide a more thorough explanation of these assumptions and their implications. Moreover, additional implicit assumptions emerge within the proof; their justification and potential for widespread applicability should be addressed.
