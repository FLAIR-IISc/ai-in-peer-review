- Proposes a spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator and eigen-decomposition via kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Frames function composition (dynamical systems) as a linear operator amenable to decomposition, extending dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) with reproducing kernels.  
- Applies the method to embedding, system recognition, and change-point detection, showing promising but limited empirical results.  
- Technically sound: builds on established dynamical-systems and kernel-method theory with a clearly outlined algorithm.  
- Theoretical contributions (RKHS extension of DMD) are well-motivated but largely incremental, combining existing concepts rather than introducing a fundamental breakthrough.  
- Empirical validation is limited and lacks comparisons to strong alternatives (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and existing change-point methods.  
- Sensitivity to kernel choice and parameter selection is not thoroughly analyzed; impact of kernel hyperparameters is unclear.  
- Mathematical derivations are consistent but could be made more accessible; Theorems 1 and 2 need more intuitive explanation.  
- Presentation issues: Sections 3–4 are slightly repetitive and could be streamlined for readability.  
- Related work coverage should expand to include more kernel-based dynamical-system approaches to better position the contribution.  
- Potential significance: could improve analysis of nonlinear systems where linear approximations fail, if validated on complex real-world datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel kernel extension of DMD, sound theory, clear algorithm, and demonstrated Koopman eigenvalue estimation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evidence, incremental originality, underwhelming applications, and writing could be more concise.
