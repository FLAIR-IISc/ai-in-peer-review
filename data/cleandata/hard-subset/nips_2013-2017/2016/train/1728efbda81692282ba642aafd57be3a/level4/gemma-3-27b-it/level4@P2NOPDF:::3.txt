This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator within the framework of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs), and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for performing this analysis. The authors further propose a method for improving robustness and validate their approach through several numerical experiments. The work effectively extends dynamical mode decomposition by leveraging the kernel trick. 

A key strength of this paper is its excellent clarity and organization. The authors present the underlying theoretical concepts in a highly accessible manner, and the proposed method appears promising. The technical contributions are sound, and while a detailed comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods is lacking, the numerical results adequately demonstrate the utility of the proposed approach.

However, I have several concerns regarding specific aspects of the theoretical development. The most significant issue lies within Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1. The purpose of Equation (7) is unclear, as it closely resembles Equation (2).  A clearer explanation of its necessity would be beneficial. Furthermore, the equation appears inconsistent; *K<sub>H</sub>* is a linear operator mapping *H* to itself, while φ is a function from the manifold *M* to *H*, and thus not an element of *H*. The authors should provide further clarification on this point. 

This inconsistency raises doubts about the validity of the proof (Lines 144-157). The phrase “by applying the operator *K<sub>H</sub>* to both sides” (Line 149) is problematic, as both sides of the equation represent scalar values. Consequently, the operator *K<sub>H</sub> : H → H* cannot be directly applied in this manner. 

The assumptions underlying Theorem 1 appear strong and lack sufficient explicit detail. A more thorough explanation of these assumptions is needed. Moreover, the proof introduces additional, unstated assumptions. The authors should explicitly state these assumptions and justify their plausibility. Understanding under what conditions these assumptions hold is crucial for assessing the applicability of the theorem.

Finally, I noted a few minor typographical errors:

*   Line 55: “the the” should be corrected to “the”.
*   Line 153: “\kappaj / \lambdaj” should be corrected to “\kappai / \lambda_i”.
