- Proposes KDMD: extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces for richer approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical formulation and Theorem 1 giving theoretical foundation for the method.  
- Includes a robustification technique using projection onto POD directions to address ill-conditioning in practice.  
- Positions the work within spectral analysis literature and connects clearly to DMD and POD variants.  
- Strength: theoretical soundness and clear derivations linking KDMD to established methods.  
- Strength: potential to broaden Koopman spectral analysis applicability to more nonlinear systems via kernels.  
- Empirical validation is preliminary: demonstrated on synthetic and real data but limited in scope.  
- Writing is dense and overly mathematical, reducing accessibility for readers unfamiliar with Koopman/RKHS concepts.  
- Lacks intuitive explanations and simpler descriptions to improve reader comprehension.  
- Section 4 (robustification) feels disjoint from the main narrative and may need better integration or removal.  
- Related works discussion is thorough but insufficiently integrated—KDMD’s distinctions from prior methods are not clearly emphasized.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks detailed comparative analysis against existing methods and more extensive application results.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after substantial revisions to clarity and empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid extension of DMD with practical robustification and good literature awareness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, limited empirical comparisons, possibly nonessential/disjoint Section 4, and insufficient application demonstration.
