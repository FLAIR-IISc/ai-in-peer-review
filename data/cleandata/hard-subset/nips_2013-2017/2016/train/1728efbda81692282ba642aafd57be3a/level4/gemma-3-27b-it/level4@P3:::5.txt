## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), aiming to improve the approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions. This is a potentially valuable contribution, as standard DMD relies on linear monomials and can struggle with highly nonlinear systems. The core idea of leveraging kernel methods to enrich the basis for approximating the Koopman operator is sound and offers a potentially powerful generalization of existing techniques. 

The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of spectral methods for dynamical systems, referencing relevant prior work in areas like proper orthogonal decomposition (POD), DMD, and related techniques in both physics and machine learning. They highlight the limitations of POD and the advantages of DMD in capturing dynamic information, and clearly articulate how their approach builds upon and extends existing DMD algorithms. The connection to the Koopman operator formalism is well-established and provides a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending DMD to RKHS is a logical and potentially significant step forward, offering a more flexible approach to approximating Koopman eigenfunctions.
* **Theoretical Grounding:** The paper provides a solid mathematical foundation for the proposed algorithm, leveraging the theory of RKHS and the Koopman operator. The theorems presented, particularly Theorem 1 and 2, provide a rigorous justification for the approach.
* **Potential Impact:** Successful application of this method could lead to improved performance in tasks like system identification, prediction, and control of nonlinear dynamical systems. 
* **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly defines the problem it addresses and motivates the need for a more flexible approach to Koopman spectral analysis.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity & Readability:** The paper suffers from a lack of clarity and is often overly dense in its mathematical presentation.  Explanations of key concepts are sometimes buried within complex equations, making it difficult to follow the logic, even for readers familiar with the field. Simplifying the language and providing more intuitive explanations would greatly improve accessibility. For example, the lengthy sentence describing DMD as a "one-term Taylor expansion" could be significantly condensed.
* **Section 4 – Limited Contribution:** Section 4, addressing robustness through POD projections, feels somewhat tacked on and doesn’t integrate seamlessly with the core contribution. While addressing a practical concern, its inclusion feels disruptive to the flow of the paper and could potentially be streamlined or omitted.
* **Insufficient Empirical Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation (Section 6) is limited. The results are presented concisely, but lack detailed discussion and analysis. The choice of test systems is relatively simple, and it would be beneficial to demonstrate the method’s performance on more challenging, high-dimensional datasets. The comparison to existing DMD methods is not sufficiently thorough.
* **Related Work – Limited Discussion:** Section 5 provides a list of references, but lacks critical discussion of how the proposed method compares to and differentiates itself from the cited works. A more in-depth analysis of related approaches would strengthen the paper’s positioning within the field.
* **Organization:** The organization could be improved. For instance, moving some of the background material from Section 2 to the introduction would help to streamline the paper.

**Specific Comments:**

* The description of the toy nonlinear system in Section 6 appears to be linear, requiring verification.
* Providing more detail about the kernel selection process and parameter tuning would be beneficial.
* The application sections (prediction, embedding, change-point detection) feel underdeveloped.  More extensive results and analysis would significantly strengthen these sections.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The proposed method represents a novel and theoretically sound extension of DMD.
* The potential impact on the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis is significant.
* The paper demonstrates a strong understanding of the underlying mathematical principles.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The paper is difficult to read and understand due to its dense mathematical presentation and lack of clarity.
* The empirical evaluation is limited and does not provide strong evidence of the method’s effectiveness.
* The overall presentation lacks polish and requires significant revision.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a potentially valuable contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. However, its impact is currently limited by its lack of clarity and insufficient empirical evaluation. I recommend **rejection** in its current form, but strongly encourage the authors to revise and resubmit. A significant rewrite focusing on clarity, more comprehensive empirical validation, and a more thorough discussion of related work would greatly enhance the paper’s quality and increase its chances of acceptance. The underlying idea is promising, and a more accessible and well-supported presentation could lead to a highly cited publication.
