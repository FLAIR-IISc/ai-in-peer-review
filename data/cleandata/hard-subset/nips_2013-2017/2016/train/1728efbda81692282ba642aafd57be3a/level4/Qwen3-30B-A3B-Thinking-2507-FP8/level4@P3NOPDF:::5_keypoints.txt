- Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD): an RKHS-based generalization of DMD to incorporate feature maps and domain-specific observables for Koopman spectral analysis.
- Theoretical contribution is sound and promising for linking spectral decomposition to interpretable, feature-specific dynamics.
- Demonstrated on toy problems (polynomial system and an example mislabeled as nonlinear).
- Manuscript has severe readability issues that obscure the technical contribution.
- Exposition is overly dense in mathematical formalism in places and verbose elsewhere, creating barriers for readers.
- Unnecessary conceptual complexity in DMD explanation; simpler, clearer statements would suffice.
- Section 4 (RKHS properties) is largely redundant and should be removed or condensed.
- Section 5 (kernel selection) lacks concrete examples or visualizations to show practical impact of kernel choice.
- Section 6 (nonlinear example) is underdeveloped and needs more detail and justification for kernel choices.
- Typo/error: the “nonlinear” toy example in Section 3.1 is actually linear and must be corrected.
- The polynomial examples are an underused opportunity to showcase KDMD’s advantages; kernel choices for these cases need clearer explanation.
- Recommendation: not suitable for publication in current form due to clarity and presentation problems.
- Critical revisions required: substantial rewrite for clarity, remove redundancy, integrate RKHS background succinctly, add illustrative examples/visualizations, correct examples, and explicitly demonstrate KDMD advantages.
- With thorough revision focused on accessibility and examples, the work could become a significant, highly cited contribution.
