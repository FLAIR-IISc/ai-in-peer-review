- Proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs).  
- Develops a modal decomposition algorithm reduced to computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of method and implementation.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world data demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Results show accurate estimation of Koopman modes and eigenvalues useful for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Strength: novelty of the RKHS extension.  
- Strength: clarity and thorough introductory/background material.  
- Strength: detailed algorithmic description and implementation.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in simulations.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration and justification of kernel choices and their effects.  
- Weakness: applications using extracted features need deeper analysis and comparison to existing methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the listed weaknesses and expand application details.
