- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform it from data.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic and real locomotion data and compares to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.  
- Presents a principled, kernel-based extension of DMD that leverages kernel expressiveness for spectral decomposition.  
- Paper is well-written and provides accessible, educational material bridging fluid dynamics and ML audiences.  
- Unclear practical applicability to common ML settings (many are stochastic); needs clearer target problems and benefits.  
- Robotics identified as a promising application domain where Koopman analysis could be useful.  
- Theoretical derivations are correct but largely expected given prior kernel-method results.  
- Unclear whether the algorithm requires explicit feature-space representation; if so, this could be a major scalability limitation.  
- Missing direct comparison to standard DMD on toy examples (including the Henon map) to quantify advantages.  
- Strengths: novel DMD extension, clear exposition, successful cross-disciplinary framing.  
- Weaknesses: limited demonstration of practical utility, lack of direct DMD comparisons, predictable theoretical contributions, potential computational limits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, potentially powerful tool, supports cross-disciplinary research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical demonstration versus existing methods, theoretical novelty limited.  
- Overall assessment: lean weak accept contingent on addressing weaknesses—add compelling real-world examples (e.g., robotics) and direct comparisons to standard DMD.
