This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in RKHSs and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm to carry out this analysis. The authors also introduce a robustification of the method and present numerical experiments to demonstrate its utility. Conceptually, the framework extends dynamical mode decomposition using the kernel trick.

Strengths
- The paper is very well written and well organized. The theoretical concepts underpinning the method are presented clearly and the approach is technically solid.
- Although there is no deep comparison with state-of-the-art alternatives, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the usefulness of the method. In my view, this level of empirical validation is sufficient for the contribution.

Major concerns
- Section 3.1, especially Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, is the main point of confusion for me.
  - Equation (7) appears very similar to Equation (2). What additional role or insight does Equation (7) provide beyond Equation (2)?
  - Equation (7) also seems inconsistent: KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas φ is not in H (φ is a function from M to H). Could the authors clarify how Equation (7) is to be interpreted so that the types match?
  - These issues cast doubt on the proof on Lines 144–157. In particular, what does “applying the operator KH to both sides” (Line 149) mean when both sides are scalar quantities? Since KH maps H to H, it does not act on scalars. Please clarify the intended operation and how it is well-defined.
- The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and are not very explicit. Please explain these assumptions more fully. In addition, additional assumptions seem to appear in the proof that are not stated in the theorem. Why are these extra assumptions needed, and under what conditions do they hold in practice?

Minor comments and typos
- Line 55: “the the” → “the”
- Line 153: κ_j / λ_j instead of κ_i / λ_i
