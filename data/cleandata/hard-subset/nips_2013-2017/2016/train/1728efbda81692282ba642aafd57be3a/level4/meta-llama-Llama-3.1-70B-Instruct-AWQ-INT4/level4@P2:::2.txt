The paper "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis" proposes a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors extend the concept of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs), allowing for the approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions. The proposed algorithm is mathematically correct, but a practical implementation may yield an ill-conditioned algorithm. To address this, the authors introduce a robustified variant of the algorithm using Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD) bases.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the Koopman operator and DMD. The extension to RKHSs is also well-motivated and clearly explained. The authors provide several empirical examples, including a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

However, the novelty of the paper seems to be limited to kernelizing DMD, which has already been proposed in the dynamical system literature. The applications presented in the paper, such as embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and sequential change-point detection, are not particularly compelling in demonstrating the uniqueness of the method. Other methods, such as recurrent neural networks, may also be applicable to these tasks.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as recurrent neural networks, to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its applications. The paper is well-organized, and the notation is consistent throughout. However, some of the sections, such as the related work section, could be more concise and focused on the most relevant literature.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a new extension of DMD to RKHSs, which is a novel contribution to the field. However, the idea of using kernel methods for spectral analysis is not new, and the paper builds on existing work in this area.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, and the proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable. However, the impact of the paper is limited by the lack of a more detailed comparison with other methods and a more compelling demonstration of the advantages of the proposed approach.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel extension of DMD to RKHSs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The proposed algorithm is mathematically correct, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the underlying theory.
* The paper includes several empirical examples demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The novelty of the paper is limited to kernelizing DMD, which has already been proposed in the dynamical system literature.

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other methods, such as recurrent neural networks, to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.
