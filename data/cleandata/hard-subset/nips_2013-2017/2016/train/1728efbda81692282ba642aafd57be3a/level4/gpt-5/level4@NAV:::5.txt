Review- The manuscript titled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The resulting method (KDMD) is demonstrated on several toy examples. This approach permits defining the kernel via a feature map to better align DMD with specific features of interest, making it a powerful generalization of DMD. My primary concern is readability. Even for straightforward concepts, the authors lean heavily on formal mathematics where brief explanatory sentences would suffice. Other sections are, paradoxically, overly wordy. As a result, the paper may be difficult to follow even for experts, and several points need clarification. In its current form, it is too opaque for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings, but I strongly encourage a more pedagogical rewrite; the revised paper could be highly influential.

Referee's report on: Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels
The manuscript titled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" generalizes DMD to RKHS. The proposed KDMD algorithm is applied to a few simple test cases. This formulation enables using a feature map to define the kernel, thereby tying DMD more directly to features of interest; it is thus a strong extension of DMD. My main concern is that the paper is difficult to read. For even basic ideas, the authors prioritize mathematical formalism where concise prose would be adequate. Conversely, some sections are unnecessarily verbose. This may hinder comprehension even for specialists, and several points require clarification. It is too cryptic for the Neural Information Processing Systems proceedings in its current form, but I strongly urge the authors to rewrite it in a more instructional style; the resulting article is likely to be widely cited.

I. MAJOR REMARKS
Please find my major comments below:
1. The exposition is unnecessarily verbose and could be streamlined. For example: “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point.” could be summarized in a short, simple sentence.
2. Section 4 adds little in a short paper and overlaps (in part) with the introduction.
3. Section 5 should be illustrated with examples, or else condensed and moved to the conclusion.
4. Section 6 should be developed in the spirit of Section 5; it has been compressed too much.

II. MINOR REMARKS
My other minor comments are:
1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy NL case, as it appears to be linear.
2. The first two problems are polynomial. This is precisely where KDMD should excel—when the kernel can be anticipated, as in 1. More details should be provided, as in Section 5.
