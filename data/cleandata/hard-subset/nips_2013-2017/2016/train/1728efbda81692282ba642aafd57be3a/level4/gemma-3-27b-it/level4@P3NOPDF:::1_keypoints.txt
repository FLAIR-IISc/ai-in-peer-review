- Presents Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel-based methods, an important kernel construction, efficient eigendecomposition, and validation on synthetic and real data.  
- Major novelty concern: strong similarity to Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley [35]; authors should explicitly state key distinctions and provide a thorough comparison.  
- Section 4 needs expansion and a deeper discussion of the presented results.  
- Bibliography is incomplete—should include relevant kernel + dynamical-systems works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Need a detailed discussion and empirical test of method behavior under stochastic dynamics; add a toy stochastic experiment.  
- Clarify the assumption (line 216) that estimation requires only consecutive observable pairs, explain kernel advantages for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships, and discuss causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: prediction task is unambitious; “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” should connect to and compare with time-series clustering approaches (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels).  
- Sequential change-point detection should be compared against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods, since the current baseline struggles with complex dynamics.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear; fix minor issues (τ-1 vs τ indices and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) in the last equation on page 7).
