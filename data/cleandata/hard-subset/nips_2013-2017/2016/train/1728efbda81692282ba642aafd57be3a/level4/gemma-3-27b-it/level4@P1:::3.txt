This paper derives a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) and presents a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis. The authors also discuss a method to improve robustness and demonstrate the usefulness of their approach through several numerical experiments. The overall framework extends dynamic mode decomposition using the kernel trick. A key strength of the paper is its clear and well-organized presentation. The authors clearly explain the theoretical concepts underlying their method, which appears promising. The paper is technically sound, and while a deep comparison with state-of-the-art approaches is lacking, the numerical experiments effectively illustrate the method’s usefulness, which is sufficient in this context. However, some aspects require further clarification, as detailed below.

**Major Concerns:**

Section 3.1 and particularly Equation (7), as well as the proof of Theorem 1, require further explanation. Equation (7) appears structurally similar to Equation (2), so its specific purpose needs to be clearly articulated. Furthermore, Equation (7) seems conceptually inconsistent; *KH* is a linear operator from *H* to itself, while *ϕ* is a function mapping from *M* to *H*, not an element of *H* itself. Could the authors elaborate on this point? This inconsistency raises concerns regarding the proof in Lines 144-157. Specifically, what is meant by “applying the operator *KH* to both sides” (Line 149)? Since both sides of the equation appear to be scalar values, it's unclear how the operator *KH*: *H* → *H* would apply. 

The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and lack explicit justification. Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of these assumptions? Additionally, the proof introduces further, unstated assumptions. Can these assumptions be readily satisfied, and if so, why weren’t they mentioned initially?

**Minor Issues:**

*   Line 55: “the the” should be “the”.
*   Line 153: The notation “\kappaj / \lambdaj” appears to be a typo and should likely be “\kappai / \lambda_i”.

**Summary of Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper presents a novel extension of dynamic mode decomposition by leveraging the power of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces, potentially enabling the analysis of more complex nonlinear systems.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical framework appears well-developed, and the derivation is presented clearly.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, making the core ideas accessible to the reader.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical experiments provide initial evidence of the method’s effectiveness.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Conceptual Clarity:** The explanation surrounding Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear and require substantial clarification to build confidence in the theoretical foundation.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumptions underlying Theorem 1 and its proof need further justification and discussion.
*   **Limited Comparison:** While not strictly required, a more comprehensive comparison with existing methods would strengthen the paper's contribution.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. However, the lack of clarity surrounding key theoretical points and the unstated assumptions raise concerns about the robustness of the method and the validity of the results. Addressing these issues, particularly clarifying the mathematical derivation and justifying the assumptions, would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend that the authors thoroughly address the concerns raised above before the paper is accepted. A revised version demonstrating a clear and mathematically rigorous presentation of the method would be a strong contender for acceptance.
