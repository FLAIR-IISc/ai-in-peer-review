- Introduces spectral analysis using the Koopman operator in an RKHS with kernel-based approaches for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator yields computational/efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Proposes a well-motivated kernel construction and provides experimental validation.  
- Unclear novelty relative to Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley (2015); request explicit comparison and clarification of main differences.  
- Section 4 needs improved discussion and clarification.  
- Missing relevant literature in kernel-based nonlinear dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Need discussion and possibly a toy experiment on performance in stochastic settings, since Koopman is usually deterministic but real systems are stochastic.  
- Clarify implications of using pairs of consecutive observables, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations, and elaborate on the causal assumption.  
- Experimental evaluation is not ambitious: prediction/premising follows standard approaches and lacks comparisons to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should compare to state-of-the-art online kernel change-detection methods (current choice may fail on complex dynamics).  
- Paper is well-written and clear overall, but contains minor typos (indices tau-1 vs. tau; replace dot with comma in k(xi,xj) on page 7).
