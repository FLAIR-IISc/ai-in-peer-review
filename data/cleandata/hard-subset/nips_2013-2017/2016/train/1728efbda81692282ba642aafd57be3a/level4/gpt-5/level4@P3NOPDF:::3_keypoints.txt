- Paper develops a kernel/RKHS spectral analysis of the Koopman operator and proposes a kernelized modal decomposition algorithm with a robustness variant and numerical experiments.
- Strength: clear motivation, coherent organization, and generally readable presentation.
- Strength: technically careful development and an understandable algorithmic pipeline.
- Strength: numerical experiments show practical relevance, though not exhaustive.
- Concern: Eq. (7) appears equivalent to Eq. (2); the paper must clarify the purpose and advance provided by Eq. (7).
- Concern: Notational/domain confusion—φ is used both as a map φ: M → H and as elements φ(x) ∈ H; KH is applied inconsistently to φ rather than to φ(x).
- Concern: Invalid operation of “applying KH to both sides” of scalar equalities; derivation must show correct order of operator action and inner products or specify test functions.
- Concern: Proof of Theorem 1 is unclear/possibly invalid due to the above notational and domain issues; needs an explicit, rigorous argument identifying object spaces and pointwise/operator actions.
- Concern: Theorem 1’s assumptions are insufficiently explicit; list all assumptions (system, kernel, RKHS, compactness/boundedness, spectral/data conditions), justify them, and elevate any implicit ones to the theorem statement.
- Concern: Empirical comparisons are weak—add baselines such as EDMD, KDMD, regularized/kernelized variants, and Hankel-based DMD to demonstrate practical advantages and costs.
- Concern: Robustification lacks formulation and detail—specify objective, regularizers, robustness target (state/output/noise), hyperparameter selection, sensitivity, and trade-offs.
- Suggestion: Improve notation hygiene (distinct symbol for map vs. elements, define KH operator-theoretically, distinguish Koopman from empirical operators, state representer restrictions).
- Suggestion: Strengthen proof structure—use explicit test functions/evaluations, cite spectral theorems and verify their preconditions (boundedness, compactness, self-adjointness/normality).
- Suggestion: Expand experimental reporting for reproducibility—kernel choice, feature dimension, regularization, rank truncation, and computational complexity.
- Minor: Fix typos and indexing inconsistencies (e.g., “the the” at Line 55, κj/λj vs κi/λi at Line 153).
- Overall: Interesting and potentially impactful work, but substantive notational and theoretical clarifications plus stronger empirical baselines are required before verification.
