- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator for nonlinear dynamics, treating function composition as a linear operator.  
- Applies Koopman decomposition to the two-step map x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))) and computes eigen-decomposition via kernel-PCA-like and subspace techniques.  
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks with reported good empirical performance.  
- Conceptual contribution: shows compositions of nonlinear functions can be handled by linear operator spectral decompositions, naturally fitting dynamical systems.  
- Contribution appears incremental relative to existing operator-based spectral methods like Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) and its variants.  
- Novelty seems largely a kernelized reformulation; paper lacks clear articulation of what is fundamentally new compared to prior DMD/kernel operator work.  
- Needs precise characterization of technical differences (algorithmic steps, assumptions, theoretical guarantees) from related methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: missing strong comparisons to baselines (e.g., recurrent neural networks, standard change-detection methods).  
- Recommends more comprehensive experiments, ablations, and analyses to show scenarios where the method outperforms or offers clear interpretability/robustness benefits.  
- Overall: clean operator-theoretic framing but limited novelty and unconvincing practical advantages in current experiments.
