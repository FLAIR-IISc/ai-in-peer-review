The manuscript, “Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels,” extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in an algorithm, KDMD, which is then applied to several toy problems. This allows the use of feature maps to define the kernel, thereby connecting DMD more directly to specific features of interest – a powerful generalization of the original method. 

My primary concern is the paper’s readability. The authors frequently employ complex mathematical writing where concise sentences would suffice, while other sections are unnecessarily verbose. This combination makes the paper difficult to follow, even for specialists in the field, and several points require clarification. As it stands, the manuscript is too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. However, I strongly encourage the authors to revise it with a greater focus on pedagogical clarity; a rewritten version has the potential to be highly cited.

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**

1. The presentation is often verbose and could benefit from simplification. For example, the statement: “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point,” could be easily summarized in a single, straightforward sentence.

2. Section 4 feels extraneous in a short paper and largely reiterates information already presented in the introduction.

3. Section 5 should either be accompanied by illustrative examples or its key findings summarized in the conclusion. 

4. Section 6 appears too condensed and should be developed with the same level of detail as Section 5.

**II. MINOR REMARKS**

1. There is likely a typo in the description of the nonlinear (NL) toy problem, as it appears to be linear in practice.

2. The first two example problems are polynomial. KDMD should perform particularly well in these scenarios – specifically, when the kernel can be readily determined, as in example 1. More detailed results and analysis, similar to those presented in Section 5, would be beneficial.
