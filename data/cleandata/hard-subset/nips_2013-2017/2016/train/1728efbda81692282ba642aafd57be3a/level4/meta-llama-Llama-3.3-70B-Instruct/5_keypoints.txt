- Contribution: Extends DMD to RKHS (KDMD), linking kernel feature maps to Koopman spectral analysis and demonstrating on toy problems.  
- Strength: Powerful generalization of DMD that can target specific features via kernel choice and may be highly cited if clarified.  
- Main concern: Paper is overly complex and difficult to read, with excessive mathematical notation and verbosity that hinder understanding.  
- Recommendation: Not suitable for NeurIPS in current form; authors should rewrite the paper in a more pedagogical, concise style.  
- Major remark 1: Presentation is unnecessarily verbose and could be greatly simplified (e.g., summarize DMD–Koopman relation succinctly).  
- Major remark 2: Section 4 is partly redundant with the introduction and could be omitted in a short paper.  
- Major remark 3: Section 5 needs illustrations or a summary in the conclusion to improve clarity.  
- Major remark 4: Section 6 is overly condensed and should be expanded with more detail, similar to Section 5.  
- Minor remark 1: Possible typo in the toy “nonlinear” example—system appears linear.  
- Minor remark 2: First two problems are polynomial (KDMD likely especially effective); more detail on kernel selection and polynomial cases (as in Section 5) would help.
