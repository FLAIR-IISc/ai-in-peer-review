This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm for this analysis. The authors also discuss a robustification method and provide several numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The overall framework extends the concept of "dynamical mode decomposition" using the kernel trick.

The paper's strengths lie in its clear and organized presentation, making it easy to follow the derivation of the method. The authors provide a solid technical foundation, and the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method's usefulness. Although the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, the experiments provide sufficient evidence of the method's value.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. The major flaw of this paper lies in Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which are not entirely clear. Equation (7) bears resemblance to Equation (2), raising questions about its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas \phi is a function from M to H. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of this point, as it raises doubts about the proof in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as both sides are scalar numbers, and the operator KH does not apply in this context.

The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong and not explicitly stated. The authors should clarify these assumptions and explain why additional assumptions are introduced in the proof. It is also essential to discuss whether these assumptions can be easily satisfied.

In addition to these concerns, there are a few typos that need to be corrected:

- Line 55: "the the" should be corrected to a single "the".
- Line 153: "\kappaj / \lambdaj" should be corrected to "\kappai / \lambda_i".
