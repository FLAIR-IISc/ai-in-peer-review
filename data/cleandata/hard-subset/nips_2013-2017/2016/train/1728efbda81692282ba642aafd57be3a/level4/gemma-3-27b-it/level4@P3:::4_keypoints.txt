- Proposal: Extends Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with kernel expressiveness beyond linear observables.
- Method: Computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposes the projected Koopman operator for modal decomposition.
- Practical fixes: Addresses ill-conditioning by projecting data onto POD directions as a robustification technique.
- Evaluation: Tested on synthetic and real-world datasets and demonstrated applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is very well-written, with clear notation and straightforward explanations.
- Strength — Theory: Strong, well-motivated theoretical foundation linking RKHS Koopman analysis to DMD.
- Strength — Practicality: Robustification and attention to implementation details show practical awareness.
- Strength — Evaluation breadth: Comprehensive experiments and discussion of potential applications.
- Strength — Related work: Thorough literature review situating the contribution in spectral analysis and dynamical systems.
- Weakness — Comparisons: Lacks direct empirical comparison to non-RKHS Koopman methods or standard DMD on the same datasets.
- Weakness — Kernel choice: Insufficient justification and analysis of kernel selection and parameter impact; limited guidance for practitioners.
- Weakness — Scalability: No explicit discussion or analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large datasets.
- Quality: Technically sound, rigorous theoretical analysis, and convincing experimental results.
- Originality: Novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS, offering a unique combination of techniques.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for modeling nonlinear dynamics across engineering, physics, and ML applications.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting strengths outweigh weaknesses but suggesting revisions to include comparisons and kernel-selection discussion.
