- Introduces kernelized DMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS using the Koopman operator to approximate eigenfunctions with kernel methods.  
- Core algorithm: compute orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and perform eigendecomposition for feature extraction.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Strong theoretical grounding and clear connection to Koopman theory and prior DMD literature.  
- Positions work relative to POD and spectral methods, highlighting advantages beyond principal directions.  
- Relevant citations to kernel methods indicate awareness of broader literature.  
- Robustification using POD directions addresses ill-conditioning and improves stability.  
- Empirical examples validate feasibility but are relatively simple and not strongly competitive.  
- Novelty appears incremental; needs deeper comparison to extended DMD and other kernelized spectral methods.  
- Connections to subspace identification methods are acknowledged but not thoroughly discussed.  
- Applications section is superficial and does not convincingly show superiority over existing approaches.  
- Pre-image computation for prediction is hand-wavy and requires more detail and justification.  
- Organization issues: overly long Related Works section that should be integrated more smoothly; some equations lack context.  
- Practical reproducibility issue: provided code link is broken.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically solid and potentially valuable, recommended weak accept contingent on stronger empirical validation, direct comparisons (e.g., extended DMD), and addressing the raised presentation and reproducibility issues.
