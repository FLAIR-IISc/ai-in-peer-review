- Introduces an RKHS-based spectral analysis using the Koopman operator for nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data.  
- Proposes a significant novel kernel construction that yields efficiency gains in synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Well-motivated contribution with experimental validation, clear writing, and good organization.  
- Unclear distinction from Williams et al. [35]; needs detailed comparison of differences in methodology and results.  
- Section 4’s discussion is weak and should be improved for clarity and depth.  
- Literature review omits relevant works (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Treatment of stochastic systems is brief; requires deeper discussion and a toy experiment to demonstrate applicability.  
- Remark that estimation can use pairs of consecutive observables needs clarification on implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Authors should elaborate on the causal assumptions inherent in the setting.  
- Experimental evaluation is not ambitious enough; more challenging or varied experiments are needed.  
- Prediction via pre-image is standard; connections to time-series clustering literature are lacking.  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods.  
- Minor typographical errors (e.g., tau-1 vs tau indices, punctuation in k(xi,xj)) need correction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel kernel construction, solid motivation and validation, generally good literature coverage.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty relative to prior work (Williams et al.), weak Section 4 discussion, and underwhelming experiments that need strengthening.
