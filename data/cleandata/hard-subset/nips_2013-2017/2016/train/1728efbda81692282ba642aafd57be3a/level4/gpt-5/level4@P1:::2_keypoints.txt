- Proposes a kernelized DMD in RKHS that builds a Krylov subspace, orthonormalizes via Gram matrices, and computes Ritz values/vectors by projecting the Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a numerically robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA (POD) directions to allow truncation when the Gram matrix is near rank-deficient.  
- Demonstrates applications: short-term prediction via a pre-image step, CUSUM-style kernel exponential-family change detection, and recognition/embedding via kernel principal angles.  
- Empirical illustrations include a toy system with known eigenfunctions, the Hénon map, locomotion sequences, and Lorenz map change detection.  
- Situates work relative to DMD/EDMD and kernel variants, noting close alignment with EDMD-style approaches.  
- Derivations explicitly connect RKHS Ritz values/vectors to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions and provide a coherent operator-theoretic framing.  
- Practical implementation reduces to Gram-matrix computations, includes truncation to mitigate ill-conditioning, and code is provided.  
- Method is versatile, demonstrated across prediction, recognition, and change-point detection tasks.  
- Novelty is incremental: main idea largely overlaps with EDMD/kernel EDMD and existing DMD stabilizations.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and mostly qualitative, lacking diverse datasets, quantitative metrics, and systematic baseline comparisons.  
- The pre-image prediction step is heuristic and potentially unstable, with no quantitative accuracy comparison to alternatives.  
- Change-detection derivation simplifies the likelihood ratio by dropping terms without analyzing the impact or comparing to established kernel detectors.  
- Practical aspects are under-addressed: kernel/hyperparameter selection, noise sensitivity, computational complexity, scalability, and validity of theorem assumptions in practice.  
- Mathematical development and theorems are generally correct and well-grounded.  
- Empirical section lacks error analysis, ablations (e.g., truncation rank), and comparisons to strong baselines (EDMD, RNNs, GPDM).  
- Writing is mostly clear but needs tightening, pseudocode, complexity analysis, and clearer practical guidance.  
- Core conceptual contribution (kernelizing DMD + POD stabilization) is useful but not substantially novel.  
- Potentially significant if paired with stronger empirical validation and clearer practical deployment guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify differences from EDMD/kernel EDMD, provide quantitative metrics and baselines, analyze complexity and robustness, and justify/compare pre-image and CUSUM approximations.  
- Acceptance arguments: solid RKHS operator-theoretic derivation, practical Gram-matrix algorithm with robust variant and code, and versatile applications.  
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty over existing methods, weak empirical validation, and insufficient treatment of scalability and hyperparameter/robustness issues.
