This paper derives a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and presents a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis. The authors also discuss a method for robustifying the approach and demonstrate its usefulness through several numerical experiments. The overall framework can be viewed as an extension of dynamical mode decomposition leveraging the kernel trick. A significant strength of the paper is its clear and well-organized presentation. The authors articulate the theoretical concepts underpinning the method effectively, and the approach appears highly interesting. Furthermore, the work is technically sound. While a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods is absent, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the value of the proposed method.

However, several points require clarification. The primary concern lies in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the associated proof of Theorem 1, which I find difficult to follow. Equation (7) closely resembles Equation (2); the authors should clarify its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as *K<sub>H</sub>* is a linear operator from *H* to itself, while *ϕ* is a function from *M* to *H* and therefore not an element of *H*. The authors should elaborate on this point. This inconsistency casts doubt on the validity of the proof in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the phrase "by applying the operator *K<sub>H</sub>* to both sides" (Line 149) is problematic, as both sides of the equation are scalar values, and thus *K<sub>H</sub>*: *H* → *H* is not applicable. 

The assumptions stated in Theorem 1 appear strong and lack explicit definition. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of these assumptions. Moreover, additional, unstated assumptions emerge within the proof of the theorem. The authors should explicitly state these assumptions and discuss their likelihood of holding in practical scenarios.

Finally, there are some minor typographical errors:

- Line 55: “the the” should be “the”
- Line 153: “\kappaj / \lambdaj” should be “\kappai / \lambda_i”
