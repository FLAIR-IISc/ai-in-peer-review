- Positive: Proposes a kernel-based Koopman spectral framework in RKHS with a novel kernel construction and demonstrated efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Major: Unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015); must explicitly state methodological and conceptual advances over DMD-based Koopman approximations and substantially revise Section 4.  
- Literature gap: Omits important kernel-dynamical-systems works (Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2012; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2014; Mouatamid & Schaback 2010; Franz & Schölkopf 2002) that should be cited and discussed.  
- Stochastic dynamics: Treatment of stochastic systems is superficial; authors should expand discussion and include a simple synthetic experiment to show robustness/limits in stochastic settings.  
- Assumption concern: The requirement that consecutive observables be nonlinearly pointwise related (Line 216) needs deeper theoretical and practical analysis, especially for multidimensional series with weak pointwise relationships.  
- Causality: The paper must clarify whether and how the kernel models temporal causality underlying observable pairs.  
- Experiments—prediction: Prediction task uses a standard preimage approach and lacks meaningful comparisons to established nonlinear time-series prediction methods.  
- Experiments—embedding/recognition: Claims of novelty in dynamics recognition ignore time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods); comparative baselines are required.  
- Experiments—change-point detection: Sequential change-point experiment fails to benchmark against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods (e.g., online kernel SVMs, recursive kernel embeddings).  
- Minor issues: Fix indexing inconsistency for τ-1 vs. τ and correct kernel notation k(x_i,x_j) (replace dot with comma).  
- Summary: Kernel construction is promising but needs stronger novelty justification versus prior work, deeper literature engagement, expanded stochastic analysis, and more rigorous experimental comparisons to substantively elevate the contribution.
