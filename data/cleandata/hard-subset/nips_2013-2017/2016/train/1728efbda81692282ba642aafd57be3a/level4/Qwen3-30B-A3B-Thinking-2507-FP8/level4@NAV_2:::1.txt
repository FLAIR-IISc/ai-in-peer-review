

This paper presents a spectral analysis approach for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm that performs spectral analysis through orthogonal basis calculation for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection. The method provides a feature extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamics, with demonstrated applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using both synthetic and real-world data. The contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated, with promising results in the empirical examples provided.

However, several concerns require clarification and improvement:
The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its approach from Williams et al. (2015) [35], which already introduced Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system analysis. A more detailed discussion of the key differences between this work and the cited paper is necessary, particularly regarding the kernel-based extension and its advantages.

The literature review omits several relevant works in kernel-based nonlinear dynamical system analysis, including contributions by Ralaivola and d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid and Schaback, and Franz and Schölkopf. These should be properly cited and discussed.

While the paper briefly mentions the extension of Koopman operators to stochastic systems, a more thorough discussion of performance in stochastic settings would be valuable, as these represent more realistic scenarios than deterministic systems. A simple toy experiment demonstrating this extension would strengthen the argument.

The remark on page 5 (line 216) regarding the estimation procedure not requiring a sequence but rather a collection of consecutive observable pairs warrants clarification. The implications of this assumption, particularly for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships, should be explicitly discussed. Additionally, the causal assumptions inherent in the framework need further elaboration.

The experimental section lacks sufficient ambition. The prediction via preimage approach follows a standard methodology without significant innovation. In the "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" subsection, there is no discussion of connections to or experimental comparisons with established time series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means or subspace kernel methods). Similarly, the sequential change-point detection experiment would be more compelling with comparisons to state-of-the-art online change detection methods using kernels, as the current implementation is known to fail in complex dynamics scenarios. More convincing experimental validation is needed to support the claimed advantages of the proposed approach.

* Minor: The paper is well-written and clear. Please correct two minor typos: verify the indices for τ-1 versus τ, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation on page 7).
