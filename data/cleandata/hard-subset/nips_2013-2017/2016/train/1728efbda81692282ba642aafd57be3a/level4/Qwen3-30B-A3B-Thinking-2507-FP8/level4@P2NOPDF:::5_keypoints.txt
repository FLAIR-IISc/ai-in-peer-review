- Paper proposes KDMD: extending DMD to RKHS via feature maps/kernels to target specific system features.  
- Core idea is promising but the manuscript’s presentation severely undermines its contribution.  
- Writing is excessively verbose and sometimes unnecessarily cryptic, obscuring key concepts.  
- Over-engineered mathematical phrasing replaces straightforward explanations and creates redundancy.  
- Section 4 largely repeats the introduction and should be condensed or removed.  
- Section 5’s theoretical analysis lacks concrete examples or visualizations to demonstrate practical relevance.  
- Section 6 is underdeveloped and omits illustrative details needed to clarify KDMD’s application.  
- Toy example typo: the NL test case appears linear and must be corrected.  
- Overemphasis on polynomial test cases; authors should justify their suitability and contrast with non-polynomial scenarios.  
- Recommendation: rewrite for clarity—use concise intuitive explanations, remove redundancies, add examples/figures for Sections 5–6, and fix errors before resubmission.
