This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in RKHSs and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm to carry out that analysis. The authors also introduce a robustness-enhancing variant and present several numerical experiments to demonstrate the approach’s utility. The overall framework extends “dynamical mode decomposition” via the kernel trick.

Strengths: The paper is very well written and organized. The theoretical concepts underlying the method are presented clearly, and the work appears technically solid. While there is no in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method’s usefulness. This is sufficient in my view.

Major concerns: My main concern is Section 3.1, especially Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which I do not fully understand. First, Equation (7) seems essentially the same as Equation (2); what additional purpose does it serve? Second, Equation (7) appears inconsistent: KH is a linear operator from H to H, whereas \phi is not in H (it is a function from M to H). Please clarify this point. This also raises doubts about the argument on Lines 144–157. In addition, what exactly does “applying the operator KH to both sides” mean (Line 149)? If both sides are scalars, then KH: H -> H does not apply.

Assumptions: The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong and not very explicit. Could the authors explain these assumptions more clearly? Moreover, additional assumptions appear in the proof—why are these needed, and are they easily satisfied?

Typos:

- Line 153: use \kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i
