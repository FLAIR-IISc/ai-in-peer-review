- Main contribution: Introduces a novel RKHS-based extension of Koopman spectral analysis that performs modal decomposition directly in function space for more expressive nonlinear eigenfunctions.  
- Core algorithmic idea: Reduces spectral analysis to computing orthogonal bases in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides clear theorems (1 and 2) establishing equivalence between the proposed kernel DMD and standard DMD principles.  
- Numerical robustness: Addresses stability issues via POD-based robustification (Section 4).  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real datasets (toy, Hénon, locomotion, Lorenz) with tasks like prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Comparative performance: Results are comparable to state-of-the-art approaches and show accurate eigenvalue estimation.  
- Relation to prior work: Properly situates the method relative to DMD, extended DMD, POD, and prior kernel approaches, highlighting novelty in operating directly in RKHS.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written, precise mathematical exposition with sufficient algorithmic and experimental detail for experts to reproduce.  
- Originality: Novel application of kernel methods to Koopman spectral analysis, distinct from extended DMD that uses pre-chosen basis functions.  
- Significance: Broad applicability across physics, engineering, and ML; meaningful for complex nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Limitations noted by reviewers: Sensitivity to kernel choice and higher computational complexity compared to linear DMD variants.  
- Missing comparisons: Lacks direct empirical comparison to non-RKHS Koopman methods (standard DMD/extended DMD) on the same datasets.  
- Kernel selection gap: Does not systematically study how different kernels affect spectral decomposition quality.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the two main weaknesses (direct comparisons and systematic kernel analysis).
