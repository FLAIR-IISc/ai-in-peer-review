- Paper develops a kernelized DMD in RKHS using Gram matrices, Krylov sequences, Gram–Schmidt, and Ritz eigenpairs.  
- Proposes a robust variant that projects onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions before eigendecomposition.  
- Applies learned modes/eigenvalues to short-term prediction (via pre-image), change-point detection (CUSUM-style), and dynamics recognition (subspace distances).  
- Theorems link RKHS Ritz values/vectors to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions, using QR/companion-form and kernel Gram matrices.  
- Robustification via kernel-PCA/POD aligns with standard DMD regularization practices and is practically important.  
- Missing explicit assumptions for finite-dimensional projection recovery (Φ properties, data richness, ergodicity, invariant measures) and no finite-sample error bounds.  
- Incorrect/unqualified claim that Koopman eigenvalues lie on the unit circle for attractor systems; needs precise conditions/citations (e.g., Mezić).  
- Numerical stability and regularization are only partially addressed; guidance on rank truncation, Tikhonov regularization, and noise/outlier handling is lacking.  
- Pre-image reconstruction for prediction is heuristic; need discussion of existence/uniqueness and alternatives like learned regression decoders.  
- Exposition is generally clear but would benefit from cleaner organization, consistent notation, and defining matrices/terms on first use.  
- Recommend adding pseudocode summaries for both basic and robust algorithms with I/O and computational costs.  
- Novelty is moderate: kernelizing DMD and POD projection is natural and overlaps significantly with EDMD/kernel EDMD literature.  
- Paper’s specific contributions are a Krylov/Ritz RKHS derivation, Gram–Schmidt/companion-form viewpoint, practical kernel-PCA robustification, and diverse empirical demos.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: lacks quantitative baselines (exact/optimized DMD, EDMD, kernel-EDMD), error metrics, sensitivity analyses, and comparisons to learning baselines.  
- Paper is potentially impactful as a scalable nonparametric Koopman tool but needs stronger empirical and theoretical support.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify relation to EDMD, add quantitative benchmarks and sensitivity analyses, provide kernel/hyperparameter guidance, discuss computational/memory complexity, and consider ridge/regularization strategies.  
- For prediction, recommend using a learned decoder (e.g., ridge regression) instead of or in addition to pre-image optimization and include ablation studies.  
- Overall assessment: solid and well-motivated with useful robustification, but limited novelty versus EDMD and weak empirical/theoretical grounding make the paper borderline (leaning toward rejection without substantial revisions).
