- Proposes a novel spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space with kernel-based tools for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator yields empirical efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Introduces a significant kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamics, with well-motivated contributions and experimental validation.  
- Main differences from M.O. Williams et al. (2015) are unclear; the paper needs a clear discussion of how it departs from or extends that work.  
- Section 4 requires improved exposition and clearer discussion of its methods/results.  
- Relevant prior work in kernel-based dynamical analysis is missing (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf) and should be cited/discussed.  
- The Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems; the paper should comment on performance in stochastic settings and preferably include a toy experiment.  
- Clarify the assumption that training requires pairs of consecutive observables and discuss implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Explain and justify the causal assumption inherent in using consecutive-observable pairs.  
- Experimental evaluation is unambitious: premapping/prediction follows standard practice and needs stronger baselines.  
- “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” lacks comparisons to time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel change-detection methods.  
- More convincing and comprehensive experiments are needed to fully support the proposed development.  
- Minor corrections: fix indices on tau-1 vs. tau and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
