- Extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS using a kernelized DMD-like procedure on Krylov subspaces with eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.
- Manuscript is very well written and easy to follow.
- Clear, coherent presentation that mirrors DMD while leveraging nonlinear kernel feature maps.
- Empirical demonstrations on synthetic and real data show feasibility.
- Framing as both modal decomposition and feature extraction for downstream tasks is compelling.
- Main suggestion 1: add direct comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman methods (e.g., DMD/EDMD) on eigenvalues, modes, reconstruction error, and robustness to noise/sample size.
- Main suggestion 2: include analysis of kernel choice and sensitivity—compare kernel families (RBF vs polynomial), hyperparameter effects (RBF width, polynomial degree), and practical tuning strategies (median heuristic, cross-validation).
- Also investigate robustness/ill-conditioning as a function of kernel and truncation thresholds and how the POD-based robustification interacts with kernel choices.
- These additional experiments could be integrated into the existing simulation framework and would strengthen practical value.
- Pros: principled conceptual extension, well-motivated applications (prediction, change detection, recognition), and strong writing.
- Cons: lack of direct non-kernel baselines leaves empirical advantage implicit; limited guidance on selecting and tuning kernels.
- Overall: strong contribution; addressing baseline comparisons and kernel-analysis would improve empirical rigor and practical guidance.
