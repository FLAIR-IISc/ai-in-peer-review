- Paper proposes KDMD: extending DMD to RKHS, enabling kernel-defined feature maps and linking DMD to specific features; applied to synthetic examples.

- Main concern: excessive mathematical formalism and verbosity makes the paper cryptic; strongly recommend a pedagogical rewrite.

- Major point: overall presentation can be greatly simplified; many explanatory passages (example given) could be condensed to clear single sentences.

- Major point: Section 4 (Robustifying with POD Bases) seems redundant with material in the introduction.

- Major point: Section 5 (Related Works) should be either illustrated or its content summarized in the conclusion.

- Major point: Section 6 (Empirical Example) is underdeveloped and should be expanded to match the detail given in Section 5.

- Minor point: Typo in the toy example — described as nonlinear but is actually linear.

- Minor point: First two examples are polynomial (where KDMD should do well); paper lacks sufficient discussion and guidance on kernel selection.

- Reviewer cites related work: Brunton et al. (2015) on Koopman invariant subspaces.
