This paper provides a spectral analysis of the Kupman (composition) operator in RKHS and presents a modal decomposition algorithm for spectral analysis. The authors also discuss a strengthening method and conduct several numerical tests to demonstrate the applicability of their method. The overall framework is an extension of "dynamical mode decomposition" using kernel techniques.

Strengths of this paper: This paper is very well written and structured. The authors clearly present the various theoretical concepts used in the origin of the method, which I found very appealing. Moreover, it is technically sound. Although it does not have a deep comparison with state-of-the-art methods, the numerical tests accurately illustrate the applicability of the method. In my opinion, this is sufficient. However, some concerns are explained below.

Major weakness: For me, the main weakness of this paper is paragraph 3.1 and especially equation (7), as well as the proof of theorem 1, which I did not fully understand. On the one hand, equation (7) is similar to equation (2), so what is its purpose? On the other hand, equation (7) does not seem consistent to me, because KH is a linear operator within H and \phi must not be within H (it is a function from M to H). Can the authors explain this problem in more detail? This creates some doubt about the proof in lines 144-157. Also, what does "applying the KH operator on both sides" (line 149) mean? Since both sides are scalar numbers, KH : H \to H is not applicable. The assumptions of theorem 1 seem strong (and not very clear). Can the authors explain these assumptions? Furthermore, there are additional assumptions in the proof of the theorem. Why? Can they easily maintain them?
