Referee’s report on: Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels

Summary:
The manuscript extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), yielding the KDMD algorithm, which is then applied to several toy problems. This approach allows the use of feature maps defined by the kernel to connect DMD to specific features of interest; it is therefore a powerful generalization of DMD.

Assessment:
My main concern is readability. Even for simple notions, the paper relies heavily on formal mathematical exposition where brief, clear sentences would suffice, while other sections are unnecessarily verbose. As written, the paper may be difficult to follow even for specialists. In its current form, it is too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. Nevertheless, I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite it in a more pedagogical manner; the resulting article could be highly cited.

I. Major remarks
1. The presentation is excessively verbose where it could be simplified. For example, “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point.” could be distilled into a short, simple statement.
2. Section 4 is unnecessary for a short paper and is (partially) treated in the introduction.
3. Section 5 should have been illustrated, or else summarized in the conclusion.
4. Section 6 should have been developed in the style of Section 5; it has been condensed too much.

II. Minor remarks
1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy NL (nonlinear) case, as it appears to be linear.
2. The first two problems are actually polynomial. This is where KDMD should shine—when the kernel can be reasonably guessed, as in [1]. More details should be provided, as in Section 5.

References
[1] Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, “Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control,” http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.03007v2 (2015)
