This paper proposes a spectral analysis framework for nonlinear dynamical systems by defining and analyzing the Koopman operator in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space. The authors then follow a standard route to develop kernel-based procedures for learning from finite empirical sequences. By eigendecomposing the projection of the Koopman operator onto RKHS Krylov subspaces, the method yields practical gains on both synthetic and real datasets. Overall, the kernelized construction for Koopman spectral analysis is substantial; the motivation is clear and the empirical results are supportive. That said, I have several concerns the authors should address:

It is not clear what the substantial differences are between the present manuscript and [35] M.O. Williams, I.G. Kevrekidis, and C.W. Rowley, “A data-driven approximation of the Koopman operator: Extending dynamic mode decomposition,” Journal of Nonlinear Science, 25:1307–1346, 2015, where Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes were already used to study nonlinear dynamics. Please highlight and clarify the key distinctions, and strengthen the discussion in Section 4 accordingly.

The literature on kernel methods for nonlinear dynamical systems is broad, and some relevant prior work appears to be missing; please consider citing, for example, L. Ralaivola and F. d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, and M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf.

Although the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, I would appreciate further commentary on the stochastic case, which is often the realistic one. The paper briefly mentions this, but an expanded discussion—and ideally a small illustrative experiment—would be valuable.

Please clarify the remark (line 216, page 5) that “the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables,” each assumed to be related by the dynamics. I am particularly interested in the implications for multivariate time series with weak pointwise dependence: what concrete advantages does the proposed kernel construction offer in this setting? Relatedly, please elaborate on the causal assumptions implicit in the formulation.

Regarding the experimental section, the scope feels modest. The preimage-based prediction follows a fairly standard approach; in “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” there is little discussion (and no empirical comparison) to the extensive literature on time-series clustering (e.g., kernel k-means and related kernel subspace methods); and for sequential change-point detection it would be more compelling to compare against state-of-the-art online kernel change detection methods (the baseline used is known to struggle with complex dynamics). Stronger and more competitive experiments would better substantiate the proposed development.

Minor: the manuscript is well written and clear. Two small issues: please check indices involving τ−1 vs. τ, and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi, xj) (last equation on p. 7).
