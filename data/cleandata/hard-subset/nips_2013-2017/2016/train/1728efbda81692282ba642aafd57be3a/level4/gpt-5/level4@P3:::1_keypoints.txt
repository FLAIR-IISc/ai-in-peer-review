- Proposes kernelized Koopman spectral analysis (kernel DMD) using RKHS feature maps, Krylov subspace orthogonalization, projected Koopman operator, and extraction of Ritz values/vectors.  
- Two derivations: RKHS Arnoldi/QR formulation and a numerically robust kernel-PCA/POD projection.  
- Demonstrates applications: short-term prediction (via preimage), dynamics recognition (subspace angles), and change-point detection; code provided.

- Needs precise positioning relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD; methods appear closely related and differences are not clearly articulated.  
- Clear mathematical framing linking Koopman analysis, Krylov methods, and RKHS with proofs relating projected operators to empirical Ritz values/vectors.  
- Robustification via kernel PCA/POD aligns with best practices for ill-conditioning in DMD.  
- Conceptually appealing use of Koopman modes as nonlinear features; writing is generally clear.

- Novelty relative to prior kernel/EDMD work is unclear and risks being a re-derivation unless distinct benefits are emphasized.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: small datasets, lack of comparisons to strong baselines, and mostly qualitative metrics.  
- No empirical treatment of stochastic/noisy dynamics, despite practical importance; theoretical remarks are brief.  
- Some assumptions are strong (distinct eigenvalues, small ψ, eigenfunctions in RKHS spanned by PCs) without discussion of practical implications.  
- Limited guidance on hyperparameter selection (kernel, bandwidth, truncation), regularization, computational complexity, and sensitivity.

- Core derivations are sound given assumptions; kernel PCA step reasonable and analogous to SVD truncation in exact DMD.  
- More details needed on numerical aspects: regularization (e.g., Tikhonov), truncation criteria, computational/memory complexity, and kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Experiments should report quantitative prediction errors, spectral approximation accuracy, and robustness to noise.

- Section 4 needs more intuitive explanation of how kernel PCA mitigates ill-conditioning and how it relates to EDMD’s Galerkin approximation; add pseudocode and complexity discussion.  
- Originality is limited since RKHS lifting and kernel-based Koopman/DMD are established, though the QR/Krylov perspective and explicit theorems add value.

- If novelty vs EDMD and empirical evidence (including noisy cases and baselines) are clarified and strengthened, the work could be impactful across ML and scientific computing.

- Request 1: Precisely compare RKHS-QR projection to EDMD’s least-squares/Galerkin approach, state equivalence conditions, and highlight advantages (stability, sample efficiency, cost).  
- Request 2: Expand on Koopman operator for random dynamical systems and include toy noisy experiments to assess spectral bias/variance and prediction robustness.  
- Request 3: Clarify implications of using unordered consecutive pairs vs single long trajectories, discuss identifiability/sample complexity, recommend time-delay embeddings, and state causal/Markov assumptions.  
- Request 4: Strengthen experiments — quantitatively compare to EDMD/kernel EDMD and nonlinear predictors for prediction, to clustering baselines for dynamics recognition, and to kernel change-point methods for detection; include ablations on kernels, bandwidth, truncation.  
- Request 5: Add related work in kernel dynamical systems and time-series (several cited authors/works).  
- Request 6: Provide practical details on truncation rank selection, Gram matrix regularization, time/memory complexity as functions of trajectory length and PCs, and preimage recovery pitfalls.

- Pros for acceptance: strong RKHS framing with stabilized algorithm, useful feature-extraction perspective, code availability, and clear writing.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficiently delineated novelty vs kernel EDMD/exact DMD, weak empirical evaluation lacking baselines and quantitative metrics, and underdeveloped noisy/stochastic analysis.

- Minor comments: fix index typos (τ−1 vs τ), punctuation in kernel notation, and a proofreading error in Section 1.
