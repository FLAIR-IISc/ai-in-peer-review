

The manuscript proposes "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extending Dynamical Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) via a novel algorithm (KDMD). The approach leverages feature maps to define kernels, enabling DMD to target specific system features—a meaningful generalization. However, the paper's current presentation severely undermines its technical contribution. The writing is excessively verbose in parts and unnecessarily cryptic in others, making comprehension challenging even for specialists. Key concepts are obscured by over-engineered mathematical phrasing, while critical sections lack clarity or redundancy. While the method shows promise, the manuscript requires substantial revision to meet NeurIPS standards for clarity and accessibility.  

**Major Concerns**  
1. **Excessive Verbiage**: The paper frequently replaces straightforward explanations with overly complex mathematical formulations. For instance, the conceptual link between DMD and Koopman eigenfunctions is stated as: *"Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point."* This could be distilled to: *"DMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear observables, akin to a first-order Taylor expansion."* Similar redundancies permeate the text.  
2. **Redundant Section 4**: This section largely repeats content from the introduction and adds no new insight. It should be condensed or omitted to prioritize novel contributions.  
3. **Insufficient Illustration in Section 5**: The theoretical analysis in Section 5 lacks concrete examples or visualizations. This section should either be illustrated or summarized in the conclusion to demonstrate practical relevance.  
4. **Underdeveloped Section 6**: Section 6 mirrors Section 5’s theoretical framework but omits the illustrative details present in Section 5. It requires expansion to clarify how KDMD applies to the described problems.  

**Minor Concerns**  
1. **Typo in Toy Example**: The nonlinear (NL) test case described in Section 3 appears linear (e.g., \(\dot{x} = -x\)), contradicting the stated nonlinearity. This must be corrected.  
2. **Overemphasis on Polynomial Cases**: The first two test problems are polynomial, yet KDMD’s strength lies in handling systems where kernels are *not* trivially guessable (e.g., Section 5). The paper should explicitly highlight *why* these cases are suitable for KDMD (e.g., kernel compatibility) and contrast them with non-polynomial scenarios.  

**Recommendation**  
The core idea is promising and could significantly advance Koopman-based dynamical systems analysis. However, the current draft’s lack of clarity and excessive verbosity renders it unsuitable for NeurIPS. The authors must rewrite the manuscript with a focus on pedagogical clarity:  
- Replace verbose mathematical descriptions with concise, intuitive explanations.  
- Remove redundant sections (e.g., Section 4).  
- Augment theoretical sections (5–6) with concrete examples or figures.  
- Correct the NL example typo and explicitly justify the polynomial cases.  

A revised version would likely become a highly cited reference in the field. The authors are strongly encouraged to address these issues before resubmission.
