- Introduces an RKHS-based Koopman spectral analysis framework with a novel kernel construction, sound theory, and empirical efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015); authors must explicitly state key differences, especially regarding the kernel construction and strengthen Section 4.  
- Literature review omits several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf) and should incorporate them.  
- Paper lacks discussion and evaluation for stochastic systems; need expanded analysis and a toy experiment to assess performance in realistic noisy settings.  
- The claim that pairs of consecutive observables suffice is ambiguous for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships; clarify advantages of the kernel construction and explicitly state causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is unambitious: preimaging prediction is standard; embedding/recognition needs comparisons to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels); change-point detection should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel methods and tested on complex dynamics.  
- Minor issues: fix typos—verify indices τ-1 vs. τ, and replace the dot with a comma in k(x_i, x_j) (page 7).
