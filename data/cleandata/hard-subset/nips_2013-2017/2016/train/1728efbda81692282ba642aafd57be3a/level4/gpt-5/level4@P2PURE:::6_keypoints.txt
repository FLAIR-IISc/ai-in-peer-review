- Derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm for dynamical systems.
- Demonstrated on a damped 1D linear system, the chaotic Hénon map, and real-world locomotion data, compared to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA; empirical results are somewhat unclear.
- Interesting extension of DMD and consistent with prior “kernelization” of linear-operator techniques.
- Experiments are primarily on artificial problems; paper is clearly written and has pedagogical value for ML readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics tools.
- Method appears tailored to deterministic dynamical systems, but ML applications often involve stochasticity—paper needs to address noise/innovation effects or justify relevance.
- Needs stronger motivation and concrete ML use cases (e.g., robotics) and evaluation against state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate practical utility.
- Theoretical derivations are careful but unsurprising to readers familiar with kernel PCA/ICA and related kernel methods.
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition algorithm requires an explicit feature-space representation; if so, this is a limitation relative to kernel PCA and should be stated.
- Request to include standard DMD results on the toy example and the Hénon map to highlight differences and improvements.
- Real-world locomotion comparisons and claimed advantages need clearer presentation and stronger empirical support.
