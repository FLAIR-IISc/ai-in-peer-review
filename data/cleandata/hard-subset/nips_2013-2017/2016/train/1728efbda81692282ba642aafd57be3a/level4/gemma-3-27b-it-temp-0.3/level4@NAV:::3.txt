This paper investigates the spectral analysis of the Koopman operator within the framework of Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs), and introduces an algorithm for performing this spectral analysis via modal decomposition. The authors further propose a method for improving robustness and validate their approach through several numerical experiments. The presented work effectively extends dynamical mode decomposition by leveraging the kernel trick. The paper’s strengths lie in its clear and well-organized presentation, and the authors effectively explain the underlying theoretical concepts, which are of significant interest. The technical development appears sound, and while a detailed comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods is limited, the numerical results adequately demonstrate the utility of the proposed approach. However, several points require clarification.

The primary concern resides in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1, which I find difficult to follow. The similarity between Equation (7) and Equation (2) is unclear – what is the motivation for introducing Equation (7)? Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent given that *K<sub>H</sub>* is a linear operator mapping *H* to itself, while *φ* is a function from *M* to *H*, and therefore not an element of *H*. A more detailed explanation of this point is needed. This ambiguity casts doubt on the validity of the proof presented in Lines 144-157. Specifically, the meaning of “by applying the operator *K<sub>H</sub>* to both sides” (Line 149) is unclear, as *K<sub>H</sub>* operates on elements of *H*, not scalar values. The assumptions stated in Theorem 1 appear restrictive and lack sufficient clarity. The authors should provide a more thorough explanation of these assumptions and their implications. Additionally, further assumptions are introduced during the proof of the theorem; the justification for these additional assumptions, and a discussion of their typical validity, is required.
