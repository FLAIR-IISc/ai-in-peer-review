The paper proposes a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems based on the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. Specifically, the authors apply a Koopman operator decomposition to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, defined by iterations of the form x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given observed trajectories, they perform an eigen-decomposition of this operator using techniques inspired by kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, demonstrating promising performance. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the key contribution lies in the representation of function composition as a linear operator, and the application of decomposition techniques to these compositions. Dynamical systems provide a natural and compelling application of this idea. However, given the existing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm in the dynamical systems literature, the novelty of this work appears limited to a kernelized version of DMD. Furthermore, alternative methods exist for dynamical system recognition, such as recurrent neural networks, and for detecting changes within them. Consequently, the applications presented do not strongly demonstrate the advantages and unique characteristics of this method.
