- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm—an extension of dynamic mode decomposition via the kernel trick.
- Well written, well organized, and technically solid with clear presentation of theoretical concepts.
- Numerical experiments demonstrate usefulness, but there is no deep comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- Major concern: Section 3.1 and Equation (7) are unclear and appear redundant with Equation (2).
- Equation (7) seems inconsistent: KH is an operator H→H while ϕ is a function M→H, raising domain/range issues.
- Proof of Theorem 1 (lines 144–157) is hard to follow; the step "applying KH to both sides" is questionable because both sides are scalars.
- Theorem 1’s assumptions are strong and not explicit; additional unstated assumptions appear in the proof and require justification.
- Typos: "the the" (Line 55) and κj/λj vs κi/λi (Line 153).
- Quality score 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: method is technically sound, original, and supported by numerical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved consistency issues in Eq.(7)/Theorem 1, strong/implicit assumptions, and lack of thorough SOTA comparison.
