- Paper extends DMD to RKHS (KDMD), enabling kernel-based feature maps for Koopman spectral analysis.
- Writing is dense and often overly verbose, reducing readability and accessibility.
- Major: many sentences can be made more concise to improve clarity.
- Major: Section 4 feels extraneous and partly repetitive of earlier material.
- Major: Section 5 should be expanded with illustrative examples or summarized in the conclusion.
- Major: Section 6 (empirical results) is too condensed and needs much more detailed development.
- Minor: Toy nonlinear system description may contain an error and appears linear.
- Minor: Initial examples are polynomial; KDMD should be tested on more complex cases where kernels matter, with more kernel-choice details.
- Theoretical contribution is solid: Section 3 and Theorem 1 provide a rigorous foundation and clear formulation.
- Robustness enhancement via POD projection and connection to kernel PCA are sensible and well-motivated.
- Mathematical notation is often presented without sufficient explanation, hindering the logical flow.
- Empirical evaluation is weak: kernel parameters are unjustified and their impact is not explored.
- Applications section reads like a list and lacks depth and rigorous performance assessment.
- Overall: promising and novel work but currently unsuitable for NeurIPS; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise for clarity and stronger empirical validation.
