- Introduces a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in RKHS with a modal decomposition algorithm for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Algorithm reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection, yielding a finite-mode feature decomposition.  
- Paper is well-motivated, experimentally validated, and clearly written.  
- Not clear how this approach differs from Williams et al. [35]; requires a detailed comparison.  
- Several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems references are missing (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Treatment of stochastic systems is brief; needs deeper discussion and preferably a toy experiment to demonstrate performance in stochastic settings.  
- Clarify the claim that only pairs of consecutive observables are required versus sequences, and discuss implications for multidimensional time series and the causal assumption.  
- Experimental evaluation should be more ambitious with additional comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, especially for sequential change-point detection.  
- Discuss links between the proposed approach and time-series clustering literature.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS-Koopman method, effective experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to prior work, limited experimental scope, and inadequate stochastic analysis.  
- Overall rating 7/10; recommend revisions addressing the above concerns to strengthen the paper for acceptance.
