This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in RKHSs and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm to carry out that analysis. The authors also introduce a robustness enhancement and conduct several numerical experiments to demonstrate the method’s utility. Overall, the framework extends “dynamical mode decomposition” via the kernel trick.

Strength of the paper: The manuscript is very well written and organized. The authors clearly present the theoretical ingredients used in deriving the method, which I find quite interesting. The work is also technically solid. While there is no in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art methods, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method’s usefulness, which I consider sufficient. However, there are some troubling points detailed below.

Major flaws: For me, the main issue lies in Section 3.1, particularly Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which I do not fully understand. First, Equation (7) appears essentially the same as Equation (2); what is its purpose? Second, Equation (7) does not seem consistent, since KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas φ is clearly not in H (it is a map from M to H). Could the authors clarify this in more detail? This point casts doubt on the argument in Lines 144–157. In addition, what does “applying the operator KH to both sides” mean (Line 149)? If both sides are scalars, then KH: H → H does not apply. The assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong (and not very explicit). Could the authors make these assumptions precise? Moreover, additional assumptions appear in the proof. Why are these needed, and are they readily satisfied?

Typos:

- Line 153: κ_j / λ_j instead of κ_i / λ_i
