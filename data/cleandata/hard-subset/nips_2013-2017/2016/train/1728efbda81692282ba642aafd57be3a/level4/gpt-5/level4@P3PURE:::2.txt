This paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems based on the Koopman operator, an operator representation of function composition. Here, the Koopman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given trajectories collected from the system, the authors perform an eigen-decomposition of this operator using ideas akin to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. The method is then applied to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the authors show that it performs well empirically.

For readers familiar with kernel methods, the main new element is the observation that function composition can be represented as a linear operator on observables, enabling interesting spectral decompositions of compositions; dynamical systems offer a natural application of this perspective. However, dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, and the novelty here appears to be largely limited to kernelizing DMD. Moreover, there are other established approaches for recognizing dynamical systems and detecting changes, such as recurrent neural networks, which weakens the claim that this method is uniquely advantageous. Consequently, the applications presented are not sufficiently compelling to demonstrate why the proposed approach is particularly good or distinctive relative to existing methods.
