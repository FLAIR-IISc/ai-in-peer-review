This paper presents a novel method for the spectral analysis of the Koopman operator by extending its application to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs). The core contribution is a modal decomposition algorithm, derived as a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD). The authors also introduce a technique for robustifying the method and validate their approach through a series of numerical experiments.

**Strengths**

The paper is commendable for its clarity and organization. The theoretical concepts underpinning the proposed method are presented lucidly, making the derivation easy to follow. The work appears technically sound and provides an interesting generalization of DMD to nonlinear systems via the kernel trick. While an extensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods is not provided, the numerical experiments effectively demonstrate the utility and potential of the proposed framework.

**Major Concerns**

Despite the paper's strengths, there are significant points of confusion in the theoretical development that undermine the core claims. The primary concern lies in Section 3.1, particularly surrounding Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. These sections require substantial clarification before the paper can be fully evaluated.

1.  **Clarity and Consistency of Equation (7):**
    The purpose of Equation (7) is not immediately obvious, especially given its similarity to Equation (2). Could the authors elaborate on the distinct role and necessity of Equation (7) in the derivation?

    More critically, there appears to be a conceptual or notational inconsistency in Equation (7). The operator K_H is defined as a linear operator from the Hilbert space H to itself (K_H: H → H). However, the feature map φ is a function from the manifold M to H (φ: M → H), meaning that φ itself is not an element of H. It is therefore unclear how the operator K_H can be validly applied to the function φ. This point is fundamental to the entire derivation and needs to be carefully explained.

2.  **Validity of the Proof of Theorem 1:**
    This apparent inconsistency in Equation (7) casts doubt on the subsequent proof of Theorem 1 (lines 144-157). The logic of the proof is difficult to follow, particularly the statement on line 149, "by applying the operator K_H to both sides." Since both sides of the preceding equation are scalar-valued inner products, it is not clear how an operator defined on H can be applied to them. A more detailed, step-by-step explanation of the operations in this proof is required to establish its correctness.

3.  **Assumptions for Theorem 1:**
    The assumptions stated in Theorem 1 seem quite strong and are not presented with sufficient intuition. Could the authors provide a more detailed explanation of what these assumptions imply and in what practical scenarios they might be expected to hold? Furthermore, additional assumptions appear to be introduced during the proof itself. These should be stated upfront in the theorem, and their necessity should be justified. The authors should clarify why these extra assumptions are needed and discuss how readily they might be satisfied in practice.

**Minor Points and Typos**

*   Line 55: There is a duplicate "the the".
*   Line 153: In the summation over `j`, the expression uses the index `i` (`κ_i / λ_i`). This should likely be `κ_j / λ_j`.
