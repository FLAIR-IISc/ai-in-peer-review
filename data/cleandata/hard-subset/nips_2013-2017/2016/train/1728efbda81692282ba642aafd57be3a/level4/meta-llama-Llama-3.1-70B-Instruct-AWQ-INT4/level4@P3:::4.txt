This paper proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors provide a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis, which is reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The algorithm is evaluated on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in extracting features from nonlinear dynamical systems.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The authors also discuss the connection between their approach and other existing methods, such as dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and proper orthogonal decomposition (POD). The empirical examples provided in the paper are helpful in illustrating the capabilities of the proposed algorithm.

One potential improvement to the paper would be to include a comparison with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. This would help to demonstrate the advantages of using RKHSs in Koopman spectral analysis and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

Another aspect that could be explored in the paper is the choice of kernels and how it affects the results. The authors mention that they used a polynomial kernel of degree three for the toy system and a Gaussian kernel with width 1 for the Hénon map, but they do not provide a detailed discussion on the choice of kernels and their impact on the results. This could be an interesting direction for future research.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and well-motivated extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHSs. The proposed algorithm is evaluated on both simulated and real-world data, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness in extracting features from nonlinear dynamical systems.
