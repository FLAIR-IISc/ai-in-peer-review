- Presents a Koopman-operator spectral analysis framework in RKHS with empirical eigendecomposition and demonstrated efficiency gains on synthetic and real data.  
- Introduces a significant kernel construction for nonlinear dynamical systems with a well-motivated contribution and experimental validation.  
- Insufficiently distinguishes this work from Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley (2015); needs clearer discussion of key differences.  
- Section 4 requires improved exposition and clarification.  
- Several relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamical-systems references are missing (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Alvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Needs a more detailed discussion and empirical assessment of method performance in stochastic settings, including a toy experiment.  
- Clarify implications of the assumption that estimation uses consecutive-observable pairs with pointwise nonlinear relationships, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relationships, and explain causal assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: prediction via preimaging is standard and lacks novelty.  
- Embedding/recognition experiments should connect to time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods) and include comparative analyses.  
- Change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods, since current approach may struggle with complex dynamics.  
- More substantial experiments are needed overall to fully support the paper’s contributions.  
- Minor: paper is very well written; fix typographical errors (indices on τ-1 vs. τ and replace dot with comma in k(xi,xj)).
