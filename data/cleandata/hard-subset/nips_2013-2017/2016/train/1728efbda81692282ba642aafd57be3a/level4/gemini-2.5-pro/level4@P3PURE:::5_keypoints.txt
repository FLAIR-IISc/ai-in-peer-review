- Introduces Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), extending DMD to RKHS via the kernel trick for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Core contribution is significant and promising, demonstrated on toy problems.  
- Primary weakness: presentation is opaque and difficult to follow; needs clearer, more intuitive explanations.  
- Strong recommendation for a major revision focused on pedagogical clarity; potential for high impact if rewritten.  
- Major remark — Clarity and exposition: simplify dense mathematical formalism and use more direct wording throughout.  
- Major remark — Paper structure/content: Section 4 seems redundant and could be removed; Section 5 lacks concrete examples and should be condensed or integrated; Section 6 (experiments) is too brief and needs substantial expansion.  
- Minor remark — Typo: the toy nonlinear (NL) case is described inconsistently (appears linear).  
- Minor remark — The first two toy problems are polynomial and particularly suited to polynomial kernels; the paper should elaborate on this connection and why these examples showcase KDMD.
