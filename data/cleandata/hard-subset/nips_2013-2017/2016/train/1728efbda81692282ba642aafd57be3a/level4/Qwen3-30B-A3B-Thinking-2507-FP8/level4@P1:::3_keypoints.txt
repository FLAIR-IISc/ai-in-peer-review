- Paper proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS with a modal decomposition algorithm using finite-length data and kernelized extension of DMD, plus POD-based projection and applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition).
- Strength: Exceptionally well-written, well-organized, with clear theoretical explanations and sound technical content.
- Strength: Method is theoretically grounded and numerical experiments demonstrate practical utility, though comparisons to state-of-the-art are limited.
- Major concern: Section 3.1, Equation (7) appears to merely restate Equation (2) with notation changes, making its purpose unclear.
- Major concern: Equation (7) seems inconsistent—KH is defined as an operator H→H but ϕ is a function M→H, so (KHϕ)(x)=ϕ∘f(x) is ambiguous because KH should act on elements of H, not such functions.
- Major concern: The phrase “by applying the operator KH to both sides” is unclear, since both sides are scalars and applying KH (mapping H→H) does not make sense in that context.
- Major concern: Theorem 1 uses strong assumptions that are not clearly stated; additional assumptions appear in the proof but are missing from the theorem statement.
- Request: Authors should clarify necessity and practical validity of the theorem assumptions and make the mathematical formulation more precise.
- Typos: Line 55 “the the” → “the”; Line 153 “\kappaj / \lambdaj” → “\kappaj / \lambda_j”.
- Overall: Core idea is promising, but the theoretical foundation and mathematical precision must be clarified to establish validity.
