- Derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and presents a corresponding data-driven algorithm.  
- Demonstrates the method on a damped linear system, a chaotic map, and real locomotion data, with comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA, but empirical results are somewhat unclear.  
- Paper is well-written and provides an interesting, educational extension of DMD that introduces Koopman concepts to the ML community.  
- Main weakness: insufficient motivation for practical utility in machine learning, especially given many ML problems involve stochastic dynamics.  
- Authors need to clarify potential use cases and domains where the method can compete with or complement state-of-the-art methods (e.g., specific advantages in robotics).  
- Theoretical derivations are correct but unsurprising to readers familiar with kernel-methods; the core idea follows established RKHS operator-eigendecomposition patterns.  
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this limits applicability compared to kernel methods that use the kernel trick.  
- Empirical comparison should include standard DMD on the toy examples (damped system and Henon map) to provide a clearer, controlled baseline.
