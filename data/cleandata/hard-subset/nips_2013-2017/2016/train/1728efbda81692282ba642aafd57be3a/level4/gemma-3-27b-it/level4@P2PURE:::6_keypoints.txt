- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to perform this analysis from dynamical-system data.
- Demonstrates method on a damped 1D linear system, a chaotic map, and real-world locomotion data; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA are somewhat inconclusive.
- Conceptually interesting extension of DMD, analogous to prior “kernelization” of linear operators (e.g., kernel PCA/ICA, independence tests, GP bandits).
- Experimental evaluation focuses mainly on relatively artificial problems.
- Paper is well-written and serves as a useful introduction of fluid-dynamics concepts to ML researchers; reviewer supportive of cross-disciplinary work.
- Method appears to handle deterministic dynamical systems without modeling stochastic/noise components, which may limit ML applicability.
- Authors need to better demonstrate practical utility for ML: clearly state use cases and benchmark against state-of-the-art methods.
- Should clarify advantages for robotics (or target another relevant application domain) given importance of inference and learning in dynamical systems.
- Theoretical derivations are solid but unsurprising given similarity to existing kernel methods; reduces to eigendecomposition of finite matrices in RKHS.
- Implementation detail unclear: does modal decomposition require explicit feature-space representations—if so, this is a limitation compared to kernel PCA and should be acknowledged.
- Recommend showing standard DMD results on the toy example and Henon map to clearly illustrate differences from the proposed method.
