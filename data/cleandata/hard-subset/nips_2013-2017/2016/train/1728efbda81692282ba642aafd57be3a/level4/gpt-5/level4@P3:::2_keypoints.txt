- Proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS to estimate Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes from trajectory data.
- Introduces a direct RKHS algorithm that builds a Krylov matrix in feature space, QR factorizes it, projects the Koopman operator, and eigendecomposes a companion matrix.
- Proposes a robust variant that projects data onto kernel PCA (POD) directions and estimates a finite-dimensional operator entirely from Gram matrices.
- Theorems show recovery of finite Koopman-like expansions under strong conditions (distinct Ritz values, ψ = 0) and connect F̂ eigenpairs to true Koopman eigenpairs when eigenfunctions lie in the PCA span.
- Sketches three applications: short-term prediction via preimage reconstruction, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection using a CUSUM-style statistic.
- Relates closely to EDMD and kernel EDMD; method is essentially a kernel realization of EDMD with standard POD truncation practices.
- Appropriately cites prior DMD/EDMD work but provides no empirical comparisons to those baselines.
- Strength: clear RKHS formulation reducing computations to Gram matrices, avoiding manual dictionary design.
- Strength: practical robustification via kernel PCA consistent with DMD/POD pipelines.
- Strength: theoretical statements linking feature-space operators to Koopman objects, and provided code.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental relative to existing EDMD/kernel methods and standard POD stabilization.
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees rely on strong assumptions and lack error, consistency, or convergence rates.
- Weakness: preimage-based prediction is heuristic without guarantees or sensitivity analysis to neighbors/kernel choices.
- Weakness: change-point detection derivation is ad hoc, with unjustified simplifications and no ablation studies.
- Weakness: experiments are minimal and qualitative, lacking quantitative comparisons, noise/stochastic robustness analyses, kernel/rank sensitivity, and scalability metrics.
- Quality: derivations are algorithmically sound but need tighter theoretical statements and a more rigorous treatment of preimage and change-point sections.
- Clarity: paper is mostly readable but requires editing for grammar, clearer definitions/assumptions, fuller figure captions, and complete references.
- Originality: moderate; mainly repackages EDMD/DMD ideas in RKHS with familiar POD stabilization; application sketches are not uniquely enabled by the method.
- Significance: potentially useful to practitioners, but limited impact due to weak empirical validation and baseline comparisons.
- Suggestions: add quantitative baseline comparisons, analyze kernel/bandwidth/truncation sensitivity, evaluate noise robustness, provide scalability and complexity analysis, strengthen preimage/change-point sections, and fix presentation issues.
- Acceptance arguments: clean RKHS formulation that bridges Koopman analysis and kernel methods and could interest ML/NIPS audiences.
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty, weak empirical validation, heuristic application sections, and presentation problems; overall borderline leaning toward rejection.
