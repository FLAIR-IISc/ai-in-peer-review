- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation that explains theoretical concepts accessibly.
- Strength: Technically sound derivations with promising methodological ideas.
- Strength: Numerical experiments effectively demonstrate the method’s usefulness.
- Major concern: Equation (7) and its role are unclear and appear structurally similar to Equation (2), needing explicit purpose.
- Major concern: Conceptual inconsistency—KH is an operator on H while ϕ maps M→H, so applying KH to ϕ as written is unclear.
- Major concern: Proof of Theorem 1 (Lines 144–157) is unclear about what it means to apply KH to both sides when equations appear scalar.
- Major concern: Assumptions in Theorem 1 seem strong, lack justification, and additional unstated assumptions appear in the proof.
- Minor issues: Typo at Line 55 (“the the” → “the”) and likely notation error at Line 153 (“\kappaj / \lambdaj” → “\kappai / \lambda_i”).
- For acceptance: Novel extension of dynamic mode decomposition to RKHS, clear math, and empirical validation support acceptance.
- Against acceptance: Conceptual clarity and justification of assumptions around Equation (7) and Theorem 1 need substantial clarification.
- Recommendation: Revise to clarify derivations, explicitly state and justify assumptions, and optionally expand comparisons with existing methods.
