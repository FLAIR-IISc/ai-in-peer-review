- Proposes kernelized DMD in an RKHS using a Krylov-subspace orthogonal basis and PCA-based robustification to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Novel and elegant extension connecting DMD, Krylov methods, and kernel techniques to capture complex nonlinear dynamics without explicit basis functions.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, with solid theoretical development and supporting theorems.  
- Using Koopman modes in RKHS as features is potentially significant for downstream ML tasks like classification and comparison of dynamical systems.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish the method from extended DMD (eDMD); need detailed comparison showing specific novelties/advantages over eDMD with kernel dictionaries.  
- Literature review is incomplete; missing important prior kernel-based dynamical system work (e.g., Ralaivola, d'Alché Buc, Tuia, Rojo-Álvarez, Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Insufficient treatment of stochastic systems and noise — needs theoretical discussion and simple experiments to show robustness under noise.  
- Ambiguity about using collections of consecutive pairs instead of full time sequences; unclear implications for multidimensional time series, causality assumptions, and temporal dependency strength.  
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing: prediction uses standard kernel preimage methods and lacks stronger forecasting claims.  
- Embedding/recognition experiments lack comparisons to established time-series clustering/classification baselines (e.g., kernel k-means, DTW, subspace kernels).  
- Change-point detection comparison is weak; should include modern state-of-the-art online kernel change detection methods.  
- Minor issues: typos and inconsistent indices (e.g., τ-1 vs τ) and a kernel notation typo `k(xj .xi)` should be `k(xj, xi)`.  
- Overall: interesting and well-formulated approach, but not clearly differentiated from prior work and lacks sufficient experimental evidence to demonstrate clear advantages.
