This paper develops a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in RKHSs and presents a modal decomposition algorithm to carry out that analysis. The authors also propose a robustification and provide numerical experiments demonstrating the method’s utility. Overall, the framework can be viewed as a kernelized extension of dynamical mode decomposition.

Strengths:
- The paper is well written and well organized. The theoretical concepts underlying the method are presented clearly and the work appears technically solid.
- While there is no deep, head-to-head comparison with state-of-the-art alternatives, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the method’s usefulness; in my view this is sufficient.

Major concerns:
- My main concern is with Section 3.1, especially Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which I do not fully understand.
  - Purpose of Equation (7): Equation (7) appears essentially the same as Equation (2). What new content or role does (7) serve beyond (2)? Please clarify its purpose in the derivation.
  - Type/space consistency in Equation (7): As written, KH is a linear operator from H to H, whereas φ is not an element of H but a map φ: M → H (the feature map). Writing KH φ is therefore ill-typed. If the intention is to apply KH to φ(x) ∈ H for each x ∈ M, or to identify φ with a particular element of H via a standard abuse of notation, this needs to be stated precisely. Please make the domains/codomains of all maps explicit and ensure notational consistency so the expressions are well defined.
  - Proof, Lines 144–157: Given the above, I have doubts about the steps in the proof. In particular, on Line 149 you state “by applying the operator KH to both sides,” but both sides at that point are scalar quantities, whereas KH: H → H cannot act on scalars. Do you instead mean to act on functions in H after invoking the reproducing property, or to apply a different operator? Please provide a rigorous justification of this step and check that each operation is type-consistent.
  - Assumptions in Theorem 1: The theorem’s assumptions seem strong and are not stated explicitly in the main text. Please list them clearly (e.g., required properties of the kernel, the RKHS, the dynamics, boundedness or invariance of H under composition, spectral properties of the Koopman operator, etc.), explain why each is needed, and discuss whether they are standard or mild. Moreover, additional assumptions appear during the proof that are not mentioned in the theorem statement. Why are these extra assumptions required? Under what practical conditions do they hold? It would help to consolidate all assumptions in one place and ensure the theorem statement matches what is used in the proof.

Minor comments and typos:
- Line 55: “the the” → “the”
- Line 153: κ_j/λ_j rather than κ_i/λ_i (check indices)

In summary, the paper presents an interesting and technically solid extension of Koopman spectral analysis using RKHSs, with a clear exposition and illustrative experiments. However, Section 3.1 (Equation (7)) and the proof of Theorem 1 require clarification and correction regarding the precise functional-analytic setting, operator domains, and the validity of certain proof steps. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper.
