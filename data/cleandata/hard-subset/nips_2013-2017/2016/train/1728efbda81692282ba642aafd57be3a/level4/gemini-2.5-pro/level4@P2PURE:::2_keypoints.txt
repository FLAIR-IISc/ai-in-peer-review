- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to obtain a linear representation of function composition for nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Uses trajectories and eigen-decomposition similar to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Applies the method to embedding and recognizing dynamical systems and reports strong empirical performance.
- Primary novelty is framing function composition as a linear operator suitable for spectral analysis, which resonates with kernel-methods audiences.
- The algorithm is closely related to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD); the main technical contribution appears to be its kernelization, limiting novelty.
- Presented applications are not fully compelling and do not clearly demonstrate unique advantages of the method.
- Paper lacks comparisons to established alternatives (e.g., recurrent neural networks) for recognition or change detection, leaving practical benefits unclear.
