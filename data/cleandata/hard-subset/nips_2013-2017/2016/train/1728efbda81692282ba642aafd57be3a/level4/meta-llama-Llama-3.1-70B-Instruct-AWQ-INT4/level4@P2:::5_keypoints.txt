- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (KDMD), enabling kernel/feature-map based spectral analysis.
- KDMD is a powerful generalization of DMD that links DMD to specific features of interest via kernels.
- Paper is well-motivated and connects clearly to prior work in physics, engineering, and NIPS literature.
- Authors provide a clear summary of main ideas and situate the method within existing nonlinear spectral analysis.
- Strength: originality and significance of the proposed algorithm.
- Strength: flexibility and power from using reproducing kernels.
- Strength: multiple demonstrated applications (prediction, sequential change detection, dynamic pattern recognition).
- Major weakness: paper is hard to read due to verbose and overly mathematical exposition; needs clearer, shorter sentences.
- Section 4 appears unnecessary and duplicates material from the introduction.
- Section 5 lacks illustration or should be condensed and moved to the conclusion.
- Section 6 is too condensed and should be developed similarly to Section 5.
- Minor issue: probable typo—claimed nonlinear toy case is actually linear.
- Minor point: first two toy problems are polynomial (where KDMD should be most effective), and more details are needed there.
- Recommendation: rewrite in a more pedagogical style; the work has high citation potential if clarified.
