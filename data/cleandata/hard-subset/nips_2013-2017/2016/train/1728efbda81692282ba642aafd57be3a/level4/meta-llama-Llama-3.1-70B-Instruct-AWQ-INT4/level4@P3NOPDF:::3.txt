This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman (composition) operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis. The authors also discuss a robustifying method and provide several numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The overall framework is an extension of "dynamical mode decomposition" using the kernel trick.

A major strength of the paper is its clear and organized presentation. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the theoretical concepts used in the derivation of their method, making it easy to follow and understand. The paper is also technically sound, with a solid foundation in the underlying mathematics. While the authors do not provide an in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art approaches, the numerical experiments presented adequately illustrate the usefulness of their method.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. The primary issue lies in Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1. Equation (7) appears to be similar to Equation (2), which raises questions about its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) seems inconsistent, as the linear operator KH maps from H to itself, whereas φ is a function from M to H. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of this point to clarify any misunderstandings. This inconsistency also casts doubt on the proof presented in Lines 144-157.

Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" in Line 149 is unclear, as both sides of the equation are scalar numbers, and the operator KH is defined as a mapping from H to H. The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear to be strong and not explicitly stated. The authors should provide a clear explanation of these assumptions and justify why they are necessary. It is also concerning that additional assumptions are introduced in the proof of the theorem without clear justification.

Minor errors were found in the text, including a typo in Line 55 ("the the") and an incorrect notation in Line 153 ("\kappaj / \lambdaj" instead of "\kappai / \lambda_i").

In conclusion, while the paper presents an interesting and well-organized framework for spectral analysis of the Koopman operator, there are some critical issues that need to be addressed to ensure the validity and clarity of the results.
