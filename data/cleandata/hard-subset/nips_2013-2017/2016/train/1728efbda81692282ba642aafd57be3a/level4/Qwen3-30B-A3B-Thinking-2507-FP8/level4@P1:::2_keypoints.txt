- Proposes kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis, computing orthogonal Krylov bases in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the projected operator.  
- Builds on Koopman operator and extended DMD, using kernel functions to provide richer nonlinear basis representations versus monomials.  
- Introduces POD-based robustification to address numerical instability in DMD implementations.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic and real-world datasets (Hénon map, locomotion) for state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides rigorous theorems and proofs for the kernelized DMD algorithm.  
- Practical implementation: POD robustification is a well-justified, effective approach to improve stability.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly explains connections between Koopman theory, DMD, and the kernel extension.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show reasonable performance across tasks.  
- Limited novelty: kernelization is an incremental extension rather than a fundamental conceptual advance.  
- Insufficient distinction from Williams et al.'s extended DMD [35], making the unique contribution unclear.  
- Fails to convincingly demonstrate substantial advantages of the kernelized approach over standard DMD or other methods.  
- Lacks thorough comparisons with state-of-the-art alternatives (e.g., recurrent neural networks).  
- Related work coverage is incomplete, particularly in differentiating from prior extended DMD approaches.  
- Technical quality is high, but the paper needs stronger experimental evidence to support performance claims.  
- Overall: technically solid and potentially useful, but limited novelty and insufficient empirical justification of superiority.
