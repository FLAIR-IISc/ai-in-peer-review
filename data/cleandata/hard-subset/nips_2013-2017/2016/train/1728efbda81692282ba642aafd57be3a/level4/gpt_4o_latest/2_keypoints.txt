- Introduces a spectral method using the Koopman operator to represent function composition as a linear operator for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Applies the Koopman decomposition specifically to forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).  
- Learns the operator from collected trajectories via eigen-decomposition, connecting to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks with reportedly strong empirical performance.  
- Novelty is primarily the observation that function compositions can be represented linearly, which is of interest to kernel-methods audiences.  
- Core algorithm (dynamic mode decomposition, DMD) is already established in dynamical-systems literature.  
- The paper’s novelty appears limited to kernelization of DMD rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Alternative approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) exist for system recognition and change detection.  
- Applications presented do not compellingly demonstrate the method’s uniqueness or advantages.
