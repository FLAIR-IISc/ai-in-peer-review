This paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The so-called Kupman operator is an operator representation of a function composition. In this case, the Kupman operator decomposition is applied to the system's forward nonlinear dynamics, x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))). Given the trajectory collected from the system, a kind of eigen-decomposition of the operator can be performed using the same idea as in PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, this method applies to embedding and identification of dynamical systems, and the authors have shown that this method works well. For viewers familiar with kernel methods, the novelty of this paper is that function composition can be represented as a linear operator, and some interesting decomposition can be applied to function composition. Dynamical systems are a special composition of functions, and it has a good application. Since the algorithm, Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD), has already been proposed in dynamical systems literature, the novelty of this paper seems limited to kernelizing it. There are other methods for identifying dynamical systems, such as periodic nerve functions, and for detecting changes. In a sense, the applications in this paper are not very compelling to demonstrate why this method is good and unique.
