The manuscript titled "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels" extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), yielding a kernel-based DMD (KDMD) method. The algorithm is demonstrated on several simple examples. By introducing a kernel-induced feature map, the approach ties DMD to specific features of interest and constitutes a powerful generalization of standard DMD. My principal concern is readability: even for straightforward ideas, the exposition leans heavily on formal mathematical notation where clear, concise prose would suffice, while other parts are overly verbose. This will make the paper hard to follow even for experts, and several points need clarification. In its current form, it is too opaque for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems proceedings, but I strongly encourage a substantial rewrite with a more pedagogical presentation; the resulting article could be highly influential.

Referee’s report on: Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels
The submission generalizes DMD to RKHS, producing the KDMD algorithm and applying it to a few toy problems. By allowing a kernel-defined feature map, the method aligns DMD with user-specified features, offering a strong extension of the original framework. My main reservation concerns the manuscript’s accessibility: the presentation often favors heavy formalism where shorter, simpler explanations would do, and some sections are unnecessarily wordy. This may hinder comprehension even for specialists. As written, the paper is too cryptic for NeurIPS, but with a clearer, more instructional rewrite, it has strong potential for impact and citations.

I. MAJOR REMARKS
1. The exposition is frequently more elaborate than necessary. For example, statements such as “Conceptually, DMD approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear monomials of observables as basis functions, akin to a first-order Taylor expansion at each point” could be conveyed in a brief, plain sentence. Similar simplifications throughout would greatly improve clarity.
2. Section 4 seems unnecessary for a short paper and partially overlaps with material already covered in the introduction.
3. Section 5 would benefit from concrete illustrations; alternatively, its content could be condensed and folded into the conclusion.
4. Section 6 should be elaborated in a manner similar to Section 5; it is currently compressed too aggressively.

II. MINOR REMARKS
1. There appears to be a typo in the description of the “toy nonlinear” example, which seems in fact to be linear as written.
2. The first two examples are polynomial systems. This is precisely where KDMD should excel—when an appropriate kernel can be anticipated—so more detail should be provided here, in the spirit of Section 5.
