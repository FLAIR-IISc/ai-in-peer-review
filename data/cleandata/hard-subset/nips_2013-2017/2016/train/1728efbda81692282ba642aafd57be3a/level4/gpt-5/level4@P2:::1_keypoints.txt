- Paper develops a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS feature maps and Krylov/Gram–Schmidt to compute Ritz values/vectors.
- Introduces a robust variant that projects onto kernel-PCA/POD directions before eigendecomposition, analogous to exact DMD’s SVD/POD projection.
- Uses resulting eigenvalues/modes for short-horizon prediction (via preimage), recognition (subspace comparison), and sequential change-point detection (kernel exponential-family view).
- Experiments on a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and motion-capture data demonstrate feasibility.

- Strong, principled derivation linking Koopman theory, Krylov methods, and RKHS with theorems connecting feature-space Ritz values/vectors to Koopman spectra.
- Kernelization avoids handcrafted dictionaries, potentially increasing expressiveness.
- Applicability to multiple downstream tasks is well motivated and preliminarily validated.
- Writing is clear and contributions are well motivated.

- Core method appears closely related to EDMD with a kernel dictionary; need to clarify algebraic equivalence and substantive differences.
- Request explicit answers on (i) algebraic equivalence to kernel-EDMD, (ii) computational/statistical advantages of the QR/Krylov approach, and (iii) relation of the robust variant to exact/optimized DMD and EDMD with regularization/truncation.
- Related work coverage is incomplete; add and position literature on kernel/time-series dynamical methods (e.g., Ralaivola, d’Alché-Buc, Tuia, Rojo-Álvarez, Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).
- Discussion of stochastic dynamics is shallow; need analysis of noise effects on the Ritz spectrum, Gram-based robustness, and whether kernel-POD mitigates noise—plus a noisy example in experiments.
- Clarify data requirements and causal assumptions: identifiability/sample-complexity in high-dimensional multivariate series, Markov assumptions, role of delay-embedding, and ability to infer directionality vs correlation.
- Empirical evaluation is limited: add quantitative prediction errors, sensitivity to kernel/width, truncation ablations, and comparisons to kernel k-means/subspace methods and stronger change-point baselines (MMD, kernel CUSUM, density-ratio).
- Compare empirically to EDMD with polynomial/RBF dictionaries, exact DMD, and optimized DMD to demonstrate RKHS advantages.

- Quality: solid math and clear algorithms, but needs deeper discussion of conditioning, complexity, stochastic robustness, and preimage predictor failure modes.
- Clarity: generally readable but Section 4 should better connect to exact DMD and detail computational steps; elaborate on pairwise-sample remark.
- Originality: kernelization is interesting but seems incremental vs EDMD; sharpen what is genuinely new (Krylov/QR link, conditioning, guarantees).
- Significance: potentially impactful, but stronger empirical evidence and comparisons needed to show practical gains.

- Pros for acceptance: principled RKHS framing of Koopman analysis, addresses numerical stability via kernel-POD, and unifies Koopman/DMD with kernel methods for useful applications.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty relative to EDMD, modest experiments lacking strong baselines, and insufficient treatment/testing under stochastic dynamics and pairwise-sample regime.

- Minor: check indices τ−1 vs τ in formulas, fix dot/comma in k(xi, xj), and provide brief complexity analysis in τ and retained components p.
