- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm applied to synthetic and locomotion data.
- Main contribution: a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) connecting Koopman operator theory with kernel methods.
- Strength: clearly written and educational, useful for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Strength: commendable interdisciplinary effort introducing fluid-dynamics techniques to the ML community.
- Primary concern: limited motivation for ML—focuses on deterministic systems while many ML applications involve stochastic/noisy dynamics.
- Needs clearer practical use-cases and domains where the method outperforms existing approaches (e.g., concrete benefits in robotics).
- Novelty appears incremental given the well-established pattern of representing linear operators in RKHS (kernel PCA/ICA); must be better situated in related work.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: real-world locomotion results are hard to interpret and do not convincingly show superiority.
- Missing baseline comparisons: should include standard DMD on the toy examples (1D linear system and Hénon map) to illustrate benefits over linear methods.
- Key technical ambiguity: unclear whether the algorithm requires explicit feature-space representations; if so, this is a practical limitation versus kernel-only methods.
- Authors should explicitly state dependence on the feature map and discuss implementation and computational implications.
- Overall: interesting theoretical link but needs stronger motivation, clearer applications, more compelling experiments, and clarification of implementation details to be valuable to ML readers.
