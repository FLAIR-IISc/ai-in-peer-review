- Paper proposes Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel-based eigendecomposition, showing efficiency gains and experimental validation.
- Contribution includes a significant kernel construction for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Unclear novelty relative to Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley (2015); authors should clarify main differences from that prior work.
- Section 4 needs deeper analysis and clarification.
- Several relevant kernel/dynamical-systems references are missing (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).
- Koopman operator is defined for deterministic systems—paper should discuss performance and applicability in stochastic settings and include a toy experiment.
- The assumption that data are pairs of consecutive observables needs discussion regarding implications for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations and the causal assumptions involved.
- Explain what benefits the proposed kernel construction offers in cases with weak pointwise relations across dimensions.
- Experimental evaluation is not ambitious: prediction method is standard and needs stronger baselines.
- Lack of discussion and experimental comparison with time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel change-detection methods.
- Minor typos: check indices tau vs. tau-1 and replace dot with comma in k(x_i, x_j) (last equation on page 7).
