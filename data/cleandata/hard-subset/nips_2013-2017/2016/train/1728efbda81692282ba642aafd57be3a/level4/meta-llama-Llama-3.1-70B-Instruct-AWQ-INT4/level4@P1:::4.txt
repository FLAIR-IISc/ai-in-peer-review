This paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernels. The method is evaluated on simulated and real data. This is a very well-written paper. I would have liked to see two additional aspects to this paper: the first being a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. The second, which could be explored in simulations as well, would be a discussion or exploration of the choice of kernels and how that could affect the results.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to Koopman spectral analysis and its extension to reproducing kernels.
- The method is well-evaluated on simulated and real data, demonstrating its potential applications.
- The writing is clear and well-organized, making the paper easy to follow.

Weaknesses:
- The paper lacks a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations, which would have provided a more comprehensive understanding of the method's performance.
- The choice of kernels and their potential impact on the results are not thoroughly discussed or explored.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful in evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors provide sufficient information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

Originality: The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, extending Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernels. The work is well-referenced, and the authors clearly explain how their work differs from previous contributions.

Significance: The results are important, and other researchers or practitioners may use these ideas or build upon them. The paper addresses a challenging problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper presents a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernels.
- The method is well-evaluated on simulated and real data, demonstrating its potential applications.
- The writing is clear and well-organized, making the paper easy to follow.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The paper lacks a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations.
- The choice of kernels and their potential impact on the results are not thoroughly discussed or explored.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, pending the inclusion of a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis and a discussion on the choice of kernels.
