

The paper introduces a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, which provides a linear operator representation of function composition. Specifically, the approach applies Koopman operator decomposition to the second-order forward dynamics \(x(t+2) = f(f(x(t)))\), leveraging collected system trajectories. The method employs an eigen-decomposition of the operator, drawing parallels to kernel PCA and subspace analysis techniques. The authors demonstrate applications in embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, reporting strong empirical performance. For readers familiar with kernel methods, the key novelty lies in representing function composition as a linear operator and applying novel decompositions to such compositions. As dynamical systems inherently involve function composition, this constitutes a relevant application. However, since Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) has been established in dynamical systems literature, the paper's novelty appears limited to kernelizing an existing framework. Furthermore, alternative methods exist for dynamical system recognition (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and change detection. Consequently, the applications presented do not sufficiently demonstrate the method's superiority or uniqueness compared to existing approaches.
