- Introduces kernel-based DMD (kDMD) in RKHS to perform Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, with an algorithm and a robust variant using kernel PCA; demonstrated on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Synthesizes DMD literature with kernel methods to estimate Koopman modes/eigenvalues for highly nonlinear systems via implicit nonlinear basis functions.

Strengths:
- Originality/significance: novel, principled extension of DMD using the kernel trick that can linearize nonlinear dynamics for downstream tasks.
- Clarity/organization: well-written and logically structured presentation that makes the theory accessible.
- Technical quality: solid theoretical development and experiments that effectively illustrate method capabilities on synthetic and real data.

Weaknesses / questions for clarification:
- Ambiguous definition of the Koopman operator in RKHS: unclear domain, codomain, and whether it acts on H elements or maps feature vectors phi(x) to phi(f(x)).
- Confusing treatment in proofs: need a precise distinction between scalar-valued Koopman eigenfunctions and their representers in H (e.g., phi_j vs. kappa_j) to validate Theorem 2.
- Strong assumption in Theorem 2 that an eigenfunction's representer lies in the subspace spanned by top p kernel PCs; requires discussion of when this holds and potential failure modes.

Minor points:
- Typo: "the the" (Line 55).
- Clarify notation for left and right eigenvectors (e.g., a_j) used in proofs.

Overall recommendation:
- Promising and impactful work; recommend acceptance conditional on rigorously clarifying operator definitions, notation, and assumptions to solidify theoretical claims.
