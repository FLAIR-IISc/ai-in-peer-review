- Paper extends DMD to RKHS (KDMD), linking DMD to user-specified feature maps and generalizing DMD.  
- Method is promising and could have high impact if presented clearly.  
- Current exposition is excessively verbose and often obscures simple concepts with overly formal phrasing.  
- Example of verbosity: long-winded explanation of DMD as approximating Koopman eigenfunctions via linear monomials could be one concise sentence.  
- Section 4 is largely redundant with the introduction and should be removed or condensed.  
- Section 5 lacks concrete examples and either needs more substance or should be summarized in the conclusion.  
- Section 6 is underdeveloped and must be expanded to match the detail of Section 5.  
- Typo likely in Section 3: the “nonlinear” toy case appears to be linear.  
- The first two toy problems are polynomial (where KDMD should excel); the paper needs more detail on kernel identifiability and how it aligns with Section 5.  
- Strong recommendation for a major rewrite prioritizing pedagogical clarity before NeurIPS submission.
