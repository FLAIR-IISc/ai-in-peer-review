This paper presents a well-written extension of Koopman spectral analysis to the framework of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors validate the proposed method on both simulated and real-world data, and the technical contributions are clearly articulated.

While the work is promising, the analysis could be made more compelling by addressing two key aspects. First, the experimental evaluation would be significantly strengthened by including a direct comparison to standard, non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis methods. Establishing such a baseline in the simulations is crucial for clearly quantifying the specific advantages offered by the proposed kernel-based formulation.

Second, the paper would benefit from a discussion and empirical exploration of kernel selection and its impact on performance. The choice of kernel is a critical hyperparameter in any RKHS-based method. An analysis of how different kernels affect the results, perhaps via a sensitivity study in the simulations, would provide valuable insights into the method's robustness and offer important practical guidance to users.
