- Proposes a kernelized Koopman operator in RKHS with eigendecomposition and empirical efficiency gains—well-motivated and theoretically grounded.  
- Major novelty concern: method closely resembles Williams et al. (2015); authors must explicitly state substantive differences and new capabilities conferred by the RKHS embedding.  
- Missing key prior work on kernel-based dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché-Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2014; Rojo-Alvarez et al. 2015; Mouatamid & Schaback 2016); include and position against these.  
- Insufficient treatment of stochastic dynamics—need discussion and experiments on noise, uncertainty, and probabilistic transitions.  
- Unjustified assumption that data are “pairs of consecutive observables” nonlinearly pointwise related; clarify robustness to high-dimensional, noisy, weakly dependent, or chaotic time series.  
- Causality assumption (x_{t+1} is caused by x_t) must be addressed—explain handling of reverse causality and latent variables.  
- Prediction experiments lack comparison to state-of-the-art nonlinear forecasting methods (e.g., LSTM, SINDy).  
- Clustering evaluation omits connections and comparisons to kernel-based time-series clustering methods (kernel k-means, subspace kernels).  
- Change-point detection claims are unsubstantiated—no benchmarks against modern online/adaptive kernel or sequential testing methods.  
- Minor fixes: clarify “nonlinear pointwise related,” replace k(xi.xj) with k(x_i, x_j), and ensure consistent use of τ-1 vs τ.  
- Overall: technically sound with promise but risks appearing incremental; recommend revise and resubmit after addressing novelty, literature, assumptions, and stronger experimental comparisons.
