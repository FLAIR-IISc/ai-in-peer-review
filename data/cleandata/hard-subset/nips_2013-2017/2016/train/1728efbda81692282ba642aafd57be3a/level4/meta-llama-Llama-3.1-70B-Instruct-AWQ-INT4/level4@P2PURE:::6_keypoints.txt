- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to estimate it from measurement data.
- Relevance and practical usefulness of Koopman analysis for machine learning are not demonstrated.
- Evaluation uses two simple dynamical systems (damped 1D and chaotic map) and a real locomotion dataset; results are somewhat inconclusive.
- The method extends DMD in an interesting, “kernelized” way, analogous to kernel PCA/ICA and other kernelized linear-operator techniques.
- Experimental validation is largely on artificial problems, limiting evidence of real-world impact.
- Paper is well-written and educational for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics-based techniques.
- The setup assumes deterministic dynamical systems without stochastic innovations, which may not match typical ML applications that include noise.
- Authors should provide concrete use cases and competitive advantages (e.g., in robotics) to convince ML audiences of practical value.
- The theoretical derivations are solid but unsurprising given prior kernel-operator literature.
- Technical clarification needed on whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations (unlike kernel PCA); if so, this limitation must be stated.
- Authors should compare their method against standard DMD on the toy examples and the Henon map to highlight differences and benefits.
- Overall contribution has pedagogical value, but practical relevance and certain technical details need stronger justification and clarification.
