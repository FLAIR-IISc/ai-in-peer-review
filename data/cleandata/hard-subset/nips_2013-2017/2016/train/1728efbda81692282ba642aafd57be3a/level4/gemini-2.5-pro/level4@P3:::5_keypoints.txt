- Introduces Kernel DMD (KDMD): extends DMD to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Proposes two algorithms: a direct RKHS Krylov/Gram-Schmidt extension and a more robust Kernel PCA–truncated projection approach.  
- Provides theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) linking empirical Ritz values/eigenvectors to true Koopman spectrum.  
- Frames KDMD as a feature-extraction method and demonstrates applications in short-term prediction, dynamic pattern recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Strength — Originality: kernelization of DMD is an elegant, natural, and potentially high-impact extension enabling richer nonlinear observables.  
- Strength — Theory: derivations are sound and grounded in Koopman and RKHS theory.  
- Strength — Applicability: KDMD has clear potential as a representation for downstream ML tasks.  
- Weakness — Clarity: manuscript is dense, overly formal, and difficult to follow; intuition and simpler explanations are needed.  
- Weakness — Experiments: empirical evaluation is superficial, qualitative, and lacks quantitative baselines and comparisons (e.g., GP forecasting, classification metrics).  
- Weakness — Imbalance: excessive space on derivations while application/empirical sections are compressed; better to focus deeply on one application.  
- Suggestion — Improve readability: present core intuition and algorithmic steps before heavy formalism and simplify notation.  
- Suggestion — Strengthen experiments: add thorough quantitative comparisons against strong baselines and more rigorous evaluation.  
- Suggestion — Discuss kernel selection and hyperparameter tuning, and performance when the kernel is not a priori well-matched.  
- Suggestion — Clarify relation to extended DMD (eDMD): contrast explicit dictionaries vs implicit/infinite RKHS dictionaries.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound and original with high potential, but poor clarity and weak empirical support; recommend rejection pending major revision.
