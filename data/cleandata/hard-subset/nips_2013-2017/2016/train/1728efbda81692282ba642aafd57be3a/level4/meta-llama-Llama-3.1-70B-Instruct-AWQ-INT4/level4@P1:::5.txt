The manuscript, entitled "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis," extends the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The resulting algorithm (KDMD) is then applied to a few toy problems. It hence allows using feature maps as definitions of the kernel, linking more the DMD to specific features of interest; it is then a powerful generalization of DMD.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the topic, explaining the context and relevance of the Koopman operator and DMD in the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The mathematical formulation of the KDMD algorithm is well-presented, and the use of reproducing kernels is a significant contribution to the field.

The paper's main strength lies in its ability to provide a modal decomposition of the dynamics into a finite number of modes, which can be thought of as a feature extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the KDMD algorithm through several empirical examples, including the analysis of synthetic and real-world data.

However, the paper's presentation is sometimes verbose, and some sections could be simplified or condensed. For instance, the explanation of DMD in Section 1 could be shortened, and the discussion of related works in Section 5 could be more concise.

Some minor remarks include:

* The description of the toy nonlinear case in Section 6 could be clarified, as it is actually a linear system.
* The first two problems presented in Section 6 are polynomial, which is where the KDMD should shine. More details should be given, as in Section 5.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. With some revisions to address the minor concerns and improve the presentation, the paper has the potential to be highly cited.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel extension of DMD to RKHS, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The KDMD algorithm is well-formulated and demonstrated through several empirical examples.
* The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the topic and explains the relevance of the Koopman operator and DMD in the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's presentation is sometimes verbose, and some sections could be simplified or condensed.
* Some minor concerns, such as the description of the toy nonlinear case and the presentation of the first two problems, need to be addressed.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, pending minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above. With some revisions to improve the presentation and clarify some minor points, the paper has the potential to be highly cited and make a significant contribution to the field.
