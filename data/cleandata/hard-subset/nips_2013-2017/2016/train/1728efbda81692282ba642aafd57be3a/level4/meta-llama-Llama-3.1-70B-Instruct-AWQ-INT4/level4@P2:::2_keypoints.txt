- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer bases.  
- Algorithm is mathematically correct but can be ill-conditioned in practical implementations.  
- Authors introduce a robustified variant using Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD) bases to mitigate conditioning issues.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of Koopman operator, DMD, and the RKHS extension.  
- Empirical examples (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, locomotion data) demonstrate the proposed method’s effectiveness.  
- Novelty is limited: kernelizing DMD has prior presence in dynamical-systems literature.  
- Applications (embedding, recognition, change-point detection) are not especially compelling or unique to this method.  
- Other approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) could also address the presented tasks; comparisons are lacking.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theory and experiments.  
- Clarity is high with consistent notation, though the related work section could be more concise and focused.  
- Originality: proposes a new DMD→RKHS formulation, but builds on existing kernel-spectral-analysis ideas.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and has potential applicability, but impact is limited by weak comparative evaluation and demonstrations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS extension, theoretical correctness, and supporting empirical examples.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unconvincing applications, and insufficient comparison to alternative methods.
