- Positive summary: Proposes a Koopman operator spectral framework in an RKHS with kernel-based, data-driven methods and efficient eigendecomposition; kernel construction is significant and empirically supported.

- Relation to prior work: Unclear how this differs from Williams et al. (2015); need explicit conceptual and algorithmic distinctions and comparison of regimes, consistency, sample complexity, and computational scaling.

- Missing related work: Paper omits relevant kernel-in-dynamical-systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf); should position the approach relative to these contributions.

- Stochastic dynamics: Requires deeper discussion and analysis of behavior under stochastic systems (bias, variance, robustness, identifiability) and an illustrative experiment to support claims.

- Use of consecutive-observable pairs: Clarify implications when pointwise relations are weak in multivariate time series, articulate advantages of the kernel construction (stability, expressivity, sample efficiency), and discuss effects of causal assumption violations (latent confounding, delays).

- Experimental scope and baselines: Experiments are limited; need stronger empirical comparisons to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection methods.

- Minor fixes: Correct typos (indices τ−1 vs. τ) and replace the dot with a comma in the kernel expression k(x_i, x_j).
