- Proposes an RKHS-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Develops a modal decomposition algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performs eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection.  
- Moves beyond linear monomial bases of traditional DMD by leveraging kernel methods to represent richer nonlinear eigenfunctions.  
- Implements a robust version using POD bases to address ill-conditioned matrices.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real datasets (Hénon map, locomotion data).  
- Theoretical framework is largely sound with clear mathematical derivations, but Section 3.1 contains problematic formulations.  
- Major mathematical concern: Equation (7) and the definition/application of the Koopman operator KH in RKHS are inconsistent with operator domains.  
- Theorem 1's proof uses strong/implicit assumptions that are not adequately stated or justified, undermining theoretical confidence.  
- Experimental results support effectiveness and show improved spectral estimates over traditional DMD for complex nonlinear systems.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-structured, and figures effectively illustrate performance.  
- Originality: meaningful and novel contribution by working directly in functional RKHS space rather than pre-selected basis functions.  
- Significance: clear practical relevance across domains (fluid dynamics, neuroscience, robotics) with empirical validation.  
- Weakness: mathematical inconsistencies in Section 3.1 are the primary issue and require substantial clarification.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify Koopman operator definition in RKHS, revise Theorem 1 proof, and add more comparisons with state-of-the-art kernel-based dynamical system methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on resolving the theoretical issues and strengthening comparative experiments.
