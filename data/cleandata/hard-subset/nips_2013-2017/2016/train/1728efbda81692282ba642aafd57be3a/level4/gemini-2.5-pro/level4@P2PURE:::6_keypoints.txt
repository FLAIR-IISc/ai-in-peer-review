- Paper develops Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm, tested on two simple systems and locomotion data; comparisons with DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA give unclear results.  
- Interesting extension of dynamic mode decomposition, akin to prior "kernelizing" of linear operators; well-written and educational for ML readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Focuses on deterministic dynamical systems, whereas many ML applications involve stochastic dynamics; authors must better justify practical usefulness and identify concrete use cases.  
- Unclear whether the method can compete with state-of-the-art in applied domains (e.g., robotics); the paper needs to articulate specific benefits of Koopman analysis for such domains.  
- Theoretical derivations are solid but not surprising to those familiar with kernel methods—representing linear operators in RKHS and reducing to finite eigendecompositions is standard.  
- Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires an explicit feature-space representation; if so, this is a notable limitation and should be made explicit.  
- Include standard DMD results on the toy example and the Hénon map as direct baselines to better demonstrate advantages of the proposed method.
