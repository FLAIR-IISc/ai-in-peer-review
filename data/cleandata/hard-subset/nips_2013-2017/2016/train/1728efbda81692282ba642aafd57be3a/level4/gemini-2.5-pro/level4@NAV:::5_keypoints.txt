- Introduces Kernel DMD (KDMD), extending DMD to RKHS and demonstrated on simple dynamical systems.  
- KDMD is a powerful generalization allowing feature-focused decompositions via kernels.  
- Primary concern: overall lack of clarity—dense notation and uneven verbosity make the manuscript hard to follow.  
- In current form the paper is too cryptic for NeurIPS proceedings, but the contribution is significant and worth revision.  
- Major remark: writing is often verbose where much simpler, more concise explanations would suffice.  
- Major remark: Section 4 appears superfluous for a short conference paper and duplicates introduction material.  
- Major remark: Section 5 needs illustrations or should have its key takeaways summarized and moved to the conclusion.  
- Major remark: Section 6 is overly condensed and should be expanded with the clearer style used in Section 5.  
- Minor remark: Typographical error—nonlinear toy problem is described as linear.  
- Minor remark: First two examples are polynomial systems where KDMD naturally performs well; this point and kernel choice implications should be discussed in more detail.
