This manuscript, entitled "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis", presents a novel extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The resulting algorithm, referred to as KDMD, is applied to several toy problems and real-world data, showcasing its potential as a powerful generalization of DMD.

The paper provides a thorough introduction to the topic, covering the background of DMD and its limitations. The authors then introduce the concept of KDMD, which allows for the use of feature maps as kernel definitions, enabling a more direct link to specific features of interest. This generalization is a significant contribution to the field, as it enables the application of DMD to a broader range of problems.

The manuscript is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the mathematical concepts underlying KDMD. However, some sections are overly verbose, and the use of mathematical notation can make the text difficult to follow at times. For instance, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be simplified to improve clarity.

The authors also provide several empirical examples to illustrate the effectiveness of KDMD. However, some of these examples are not fully developed, and the reader is left wanting more details. For instance, the application of KDMD to the Hénon map is interesting, but the results could be more thoroughly discussed.

The related works section is comprehensive, covering various techniques for spectral analysis and decomposition. However, some of these references could be more thoroughly discussed in the context of KDMD.

The paper's major strength lies in its ability to provide a new perspective on DMD by extending it to RKHS. The KDMD algorithm has the potential to be highly cited and influential in the field. However, to reach its full potential, the manuscript needs to be rewritten in a more pedagogical way, with clearer explanations and more detailed examples.
