The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," presents a valuable extension of dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in a novel algorithm (KDMD). This work allows for the incorporation of feature maps as kernel definitions, effectively bridging DMD with specific, relevant features – a significant generalization of the original method. However, the paper's clarity is a substantial concern. The authors frequently employ overly complex mathematical formulations where concise explanations would suffice, while conversely, some sections are unnecessarily detailed. This combination hinders comprehension, even for experts in the field, and requires clarification. 

Currently, the manuscript is not suitable for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings due to its opacity. Nevertheless, I strongly recommend the authors revise the paper with a greater focus on pedagogical clarity. A more accessible presentation would likely result in a highly impactful and frequently cited work. 

**I. MAJOR REMARKS**

1. The presentation is often excessively verbose and could benefit from significant simplification. For example, the statement: "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be conveyed more effectively with a single, concise sentence.
2. Section 4 is largely redundant in a concise paper and overlaps with material already presented in the introduction.
3. Section 5 lacks sufficient illustration or a summarizing discussion within the conclusion.
4. Section 6 requires more thorough development, mirroring the approach used in Section 5, as it currently feels overly condensed.

**II. MINOR REMARKS**

1. A potential typographical error exists in the description of the non-linear toy case, as its behavior appears linear.
2. The first two test problems presented are polynomial; KDMD should excel in scenarios where the appropriate kernel can be readily identified, as demonstrated in problem 1. Further details, similar to those provided in Section 5, would be beneficial.
