- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick to extend DMD.  
- Authors present a robustifying projection onto POD directions and validate the approach with numerical experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and the theoretical presentation is comprehensive.  
- Technical development appears mathematically sound and the framework is promising.  
- Core contribution: novel kernel-based extension of DMD that can capture more complex nonlinear dynamics.  
- Numerical experiments provide initial empirical evidence of the method’s feasibility.  
- Major concern: motivation and justification for Equation (7) are unclear and its purpose is not explained.  
- Critical ambiguity: statement that KH is a linear operator H->H while φ is M->H; how the operator acts on the functional space is not clarified.  
- Problematic proof step: applying KH to scalar values (Line 149) is ill-defined and undermines Lines 144–157.  
- Theorem 1 relies on strong assumptions that are not sufficiently justified or contextualized.  
- Additional assumptions appear within the proof without explanation; authors should state whether these hold generally or require special conditions.  
- Limited comparative analysis: more thorough comparison with existing DMD variants and related methods is needed.  
- Recommendation: currently a weak reject pending clarification of Eq. (7), correction of the proof and assumptions, and stronger empirical comparisons.  
- Typos to fix: Line 55 “the the” → “the”; Line 153 “\kappaj / \lambdaj” → “\kappai / \lambda_i”.
