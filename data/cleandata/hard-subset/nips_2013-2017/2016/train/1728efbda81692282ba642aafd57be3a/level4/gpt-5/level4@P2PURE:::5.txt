Referee’s report on: Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels

The manuscript, “Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels,” extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The resulting algorithm (KDMD) is applied to several toy problems. This framework allows the use of feature maps to define the kernel, thereby linking DMD to specific features of interest; it is thus a powerful generalization of DMD.

My main concern is that the paper is very hard to read. Even for simple notions, the authors overemphasize mathematical formalism where short, direct explanations would suffice. Other sections are unnecessarily verbose. As written, the paper may be difficult to follow even for specialists, and several points require clarification. In its current form, it is too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings. Nevertheless, I strongly encourage the authors to rewrite it in a more pedagogical style; the resulting article could be highly cited.

I. MAJOR REMARKS
1. The presentation is overly verbose and could be streamlined. For example, the sentence “Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point.” could be conveyed in a much shorter, simpler sentence.
2. Section 4 is unnecessary for a short paper and overlaps with material already (partially) covered in the introduction.
3. Section 5 should have included illustrative examples or, alternatively, been summarized in the conclusion.
4. Section 6 should have been developed along the lines of Section 5; it has been condensed too much.

II. MINOR REMARKS
1. There is likely a typo in the description of the toy “NL” case, as it appears to be linear.
2. The first two problems are polynomial. This is where KDMD should excel—when the kernel can be guessed, as in the first case. More details should be provided, as in Section 5.

REFERENCES
1. Steven L. Brunton, Bingni W. Brunton, Joshua L. Proctor, J. Nathan Kutz, “Koopman invariant subspaces and finite linear representations of nonlinear dynamical systems for control,” arXiv:1510.03007v2 (2015).
