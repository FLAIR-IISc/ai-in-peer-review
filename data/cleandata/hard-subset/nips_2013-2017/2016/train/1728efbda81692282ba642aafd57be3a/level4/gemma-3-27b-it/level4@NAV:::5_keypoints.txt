- Paper presents KDMD: an extension of DMD into RKHS enabling kernel-based feature maps and a meaningful generalization of DMD/Koopman analysis.  
- Manuscript has significant clarity problems: overly complex math in places and excessive detail in others, making it hard to follow even for experts.  
- Reviewer judges the paper not yet suitable for NeurIPS proceedings and requests major revisions emphasizing pedagogical clarity.  
- Major remark 1: Presentation is verbose and should be substantially simplified with clearer, more concise explanations.  
- Major remark 2: Section 4 is largely redundant and overlaps with the introduction; it should be shortened or removed.  
- Major remark 3: Section 5 needs more illustration or a summarizing discussion in the conclusion to highlight its contributions.  
- Major remark 4: Section 6 is underdeveloped and should be expanded with detail comparable to Section 5.  
- Minor remark 1: Possible typographical or description error in the nonlinear toy example, which appears to behave linearly.  
- Minor remark 2: Test problems are polynomial where KDMD naturally excels; more varied examples and detail (as in Section 5) would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Reviewer recommends revision focused on accessibility; believes a clearer presentation would make the work highly impactful and frequently cited.
