- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear systems.  
- Core idea of using kernel methods to enrich basis functions is sound and a logical generalization of existing DMD techniques.  
- Paper is well-positioned within spectral methods literature and connects clearly to Koopman operator formalism.  
- Theoretical grounding is strong; Theorems 1 and 2 provide rigorous justification for the approach.  
- Novelty: the RKHS extension of DMD is a potentially significant and useful contribution.  
- Potential impact: could improve system identification, prediction, and control for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Problem definition and motivation are clearly stated.  

- Presentation suffers from lack of clarity and overly dense mathematics, making key concepts hard to follow.  
- Section 4 on robustness via POD projections feels tacked on and disrupts the paper’s flow.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: simple test systems, insufficient analysis, and inadequate comparisons to existing DMD methods.  
- Related work section lists references but lacks critical comparison and differentiation from cited methods.  
- Organization could be improved; some background belongs earlier (e.g., move parts of Section 2 to the introduction).  

- Specific issues: the toy “nonlinear” system may actually be linear and needs verification.  
- Lacks detail on kernel selection and parameter tuning procedures.  
- Application sections (prediction, embedding, change-point detection) are underdeveloped and need more extensive results.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method with significant potential impact.  
- Arguments for rejection: hard to read, limited empirical evidence, and overall presentation needs substantial revision.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision and resubmission with clearer exposition, stronger experiments, and deeper related-work discussion.
