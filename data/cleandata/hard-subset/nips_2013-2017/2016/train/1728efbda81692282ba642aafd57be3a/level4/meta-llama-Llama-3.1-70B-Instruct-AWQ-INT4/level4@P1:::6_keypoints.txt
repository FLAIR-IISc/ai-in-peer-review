- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm to estimate it from measurement data.
- Demonstrates the method on a damped 1D linear system, a chaotic (Henon) map, and real locomotion data, comparing to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.
- Novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to RKHSs, connecting kernel methods with Koopman/operator techniques.
- Paper is clearly written, with well-defined notation and equations, and has educational value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Theoretical derivations are sound but not surprising given the authors’ background.
- Experimental results are limited and largely on synthetic problems.
- Does not sufficiently convince ML readers of practical usefulness; needs stronger use cases and domain-relevant comparisons (e.g., robotics).
- Needs clarification of limitations, notably the requirement for explicit feature-space representations versus implicit kernel methods like kernel PCA.
- Should include performance of standard DMD on the toy example and Henon map to better highlight advantages and limitations.
- Potential to spur new methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems and applications in areas like robotics if limitations are addressed.
- Recommendation: good fit for the conference but requires additional experiments and clarifications to address the above weaknesses.
