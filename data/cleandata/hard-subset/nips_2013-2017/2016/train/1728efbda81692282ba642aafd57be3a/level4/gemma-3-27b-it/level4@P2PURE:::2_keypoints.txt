Paper proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to represent nonlinear function composition linearly.  
Method specifically analyzes the two-step composition x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).  
Estimation uses eigen-decomposition of the operator applied to collected trajectories, leveraging kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
Demonstrated on embedding and recognition tasks, showing promising performance.  
Key contribution is representing function composition as a linear operator and applying kernelized decomposition techniques.  
Novelty appears mainly as a kernelized extension of existing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).  
Existing alternatives (DMD, recurrent neural networks, change-detection methods) mean the applications do not clearly demonstrate a strong advantage.
