- Proposes a spectral modal decomposition algorithm for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHS.  
- Reduces computation to orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction to Koopman operator theory and dynamic mode decomposition (DMD).  
- Extends DMD analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces, enabling richer eigenfunction approximations.  
- Proposes a robustified variant using POD bases to improve numerical stability.  
- Demonstrates method on toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, and CMU motion-capture locomotion data.  
- Shows applications to prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.  
- Well-motivated algorithm with sufficient implementation detail and good organization.  
- Main novelty appears limited to kernelizing existing DMD algorithms rather than a fundamentally new technique.  
- Algorithm closely related to prior dynamical-systems literature, reducing perceived originality.  
- Empirical applications are not particularly compelling or distinctive compared to existing approaches.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons to alternative methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks) for recognition and change detection.  
- Paper would benefit from clearer highlighting of advantages over existing methods and a discussion of limitations.  
- Recommendations: include more empirical comparisons across domains, emphasize unique strengths, and discuss limitations and future work.
