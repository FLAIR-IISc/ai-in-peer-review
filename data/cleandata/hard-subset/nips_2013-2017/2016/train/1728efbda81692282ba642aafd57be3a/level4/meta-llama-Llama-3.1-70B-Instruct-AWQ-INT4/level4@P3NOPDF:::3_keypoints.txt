- Paper develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick.  
- Authors introduce a method to robustify the approach and provide numerical experiments demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with thorough theoretical explanations.  
- Strength: technically sound with solid mathematical foundations.  
- Weakness: lacks in-depth comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (experiments are illustrative but not comparative).  
- Major issue: Equation (7) in Section 3.1 appears redundant with Equation (2) and its purpose is unclear.  
- Major issue: Equation (7) seems inconsistent because KH maps H→H while φ is a function M→H; this needs clarification.  
- Major issue: the inconsistency in Eq. (7) undermines the validity of the proof in Lines 144–157.  
- Major issue: the phrase “by applying the operator KH to both sides” (Line 149) is unclear since both sides are scalars but KH maps H→H.  
- Major issue: assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong, not explicitly stated, and additional assumptions are introduced in the proof without justification.  
- Minor errors: typo “the the” (Line 55) and incorrect notation in Line 153 ("\kappaj / \lambdaj" should be "\kappai / \lambda_i").  
- Overall: interesting and promising framework but several critical clarifications and corrections are required to validate the results.
