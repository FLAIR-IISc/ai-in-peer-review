- Paper proposes spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator in an RKHS with kernel-based empirical methods.  
- Core approach uses eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator and shows efficiency gains on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Main contribution is a well-motivated kernel construction validated experimentally.  

- Unclear novelty relative to Williams et al. [35]; need a detailed comparison and clarification of differences.  
- Related-work section omits several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems papers (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Applicability to stochastic systems is insufficiently discussed; recommend deeper analysis and a toy noisy experiment.  

- Clarify implications of using pairs of consecutive observables (vs. full sequences) for multidimensional time series and explain benefits of the kernel in weak pointwise-relation settings.  
- Elaborate on the causal assumptions implied by the data-pair estimation procedure.  

- Experimental evaluation is weak in places: prediction via pre-imaging follows standard approaches and needs stronger baselines.  
- Embedding/recognition experiments lack discussion and comparison to time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Change-point detection experiment uses a weak baseline; compare against state-of-the-art online kernel-based methods for challenging dynamics.  

- Minor: paper is clear and well-written.  
- Minor: check indices on τ-1 vs. τ.  
- Minor: typo in kernel notation k(xi.xj) should be k(xi, xj).
