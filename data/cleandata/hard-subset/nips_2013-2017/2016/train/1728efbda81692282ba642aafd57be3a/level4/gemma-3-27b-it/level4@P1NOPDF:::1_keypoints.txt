- Paper presents a spectral analysis framework using Koopman operator in an RKHS with kernel-based, data-driven eigendecomposition and validated experiments.  
- Unclear novelty: need explicit comparison and discussion of differences from Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley (2015).  
- Missing citations: several relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamical systems works (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf) should be referenced.  
- Stochastic systems: paper briefly mentions but lacks detailed discussion and/or experiments on performance in stochastic settings.  
- Data assumption clarity: need explanation of implications when using collections of consecutive observable pairs, especially for multidimensional series where pointwise relationships may be weak.  
- Causal assumptions: request elaboration on causal assumptions inherent in the method and their consequences.  
- Kernel advantages: ask for clarification of what advantages the proposed kernel construction provides in multidimensional/time-series scenarios.  
- Experiments are unchallenging: forecasting setup is standard and lacks comparisons to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels).  
- Change-point detection: sequential detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel change-detection methods.  
- Minor issues: two typos—indices on τ−1 vs. τ and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation, p.7).
