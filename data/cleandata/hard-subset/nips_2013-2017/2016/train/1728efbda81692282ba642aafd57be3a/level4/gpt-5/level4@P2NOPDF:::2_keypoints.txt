- Proposes a spectral approach using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems, applied to x(t+2)=f(f(x(t))).
- Constructs an empirical operator from observed trajectories and performs eigendecomposition using kernel-PCA–style and subspace-method ideas.
- Uses the resulting decomposition for embedding and recognizing dynamical systems with positive reported results.
- Key conceptual contribution: function compositions can be represented as linear operators admitting meaningful spectral decompositions; dynamical systems are a natural example.
- Prior work: dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and variants already connect to Koopman operator theory.
- Novelty appears mainly as a kernelized formulation of existing Koopman/DMD ideas.
- Empirical comparisons are limited — other established methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks, change-detection techniques) are not clearly outperformed, so unique advantages are not fully demonstrated.
