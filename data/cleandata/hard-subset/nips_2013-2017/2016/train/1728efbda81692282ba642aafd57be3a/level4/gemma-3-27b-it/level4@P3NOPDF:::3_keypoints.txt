- Paper presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a corresponding modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and demonstrates strong command of theory.  
- Proposed method is theoretically interesting and appears technically sound.  
- Numerical experiments illustrate practical utility but lack detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Major concern: Equation (7) in Section 3.1 is unclear in purpose and its relation to Equation (2) needs justification.  
- Inconsistency: K_H is defined as an operator H→H but φ is a function M→H (not an element of H), so application of K_H to φ is not clearly defined.  
- Proof issue: applying K_H to both sides (Line 149) is problematic because both sides are scalars at that step and K_H cannot act on scalars.  
- Theorem 1 assumptions are strong, imprecisely stated, and require explicit definitions and justification.  
- The proof appears to introduce unstated additional assumptions that should be listed and motivated.  
- Authors should discuss practical likelihood and limitations when Theorem 1 assumptions are not satisfied.  
- Minor typos: Line 55 “the the” → “the”; Line 153 “\kappaj / \lambdaj” → “\kappai / \lambda_i”.  
- Overall: promising and potentially impactful work but needs revision to address technical inconsistencies and clarify assumptions before acceptance.
