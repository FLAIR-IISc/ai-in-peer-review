- Paper proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm using measurement data.
- Evaluated on two toy systems (damped 1D, Henon map) and on real locomotion data; real-world results are not clearly compelling.
- Work extends DMD using established kernelized techniques (kernel PCA/ICA, kernel tests), building on prior approaches.
- Theoretical derivations are technically sound, clearly written, and educational for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid dynamics.
- Paper fails to convincingly establish practical relevance or concrete ML use cases; needs to explain why ML practitioners should care.
- Focus on deterministic maps (no stochasticity) contrasts with typical ML settings that emphasize noise and random innovations.
- Theoretical contributions are unsurprising given existing kernelized linear-operator literature; novelty justification is weak.
- Two specific concerns: reliance on explicit feature-space representations (unlike implicit kernel PCA) should be stated and its scalability implications discussed.
- Two specific concerns: include baseline performance metrics for standard DMD on toy examples to demonstrate relative advantage.
- Experimental section is limited; comparisons with DMD/PCA/kernel PCA lack depth, computational-cost analysis, and error metrics.
- Paper should reframe narrative around concrete ML applications (e.g., robotics) and show clear advantages over state-of-the-art methods.
- Overall: technically valid extension but needs stronger practical motivation, baseline comparisons, and explicit limitations to be compelling for ML.
