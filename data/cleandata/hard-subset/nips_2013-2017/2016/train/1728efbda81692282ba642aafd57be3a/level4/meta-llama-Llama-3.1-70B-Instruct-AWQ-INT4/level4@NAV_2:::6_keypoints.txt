- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm to perform it.
- Demonstrations include a damped linear 1D system, a chaotic map (Henon), and real-world locomotion data.
- Authors claim relevance to machine learning but do not convincingly demonstrate practical ML use cases.
- Method is an RKHS/kernel extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), similar in spirit to prior kernelized operator approaches.
- Experimental results are limited and somewhat unclear, especially comparisons on real locomotion data.
- Reviewer finds the paper clearly written and educational for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Need clearer articulation of potential application domains (e.g., robotics) and concrete benefits over existing methods.
- Recommend more detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art methods in relevant domains and to standard DMD on toy examples.
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations; this limitation should be clarified.
- Theoretical derivations are solid but unsurprising given parallels to kernel PCA/ICA and other kernel operator methods.
- Originality: novel application of Koopman operator to ML and extension of DMD using RKHS is interesting.
- Overall recommendation: promising contribution but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer practical motivation, and clarification of technical details.
