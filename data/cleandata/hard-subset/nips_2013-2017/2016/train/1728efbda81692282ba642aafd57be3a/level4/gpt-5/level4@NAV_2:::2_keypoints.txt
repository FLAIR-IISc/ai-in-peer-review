- Introduces a spectral approach to nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator, viewing function composition as a linear operator on observables.
- Instantiates this framework in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space and develops a kernelized dynamic mode decomposition.
- Computes an eigendecomposition of the operator using ideas related to kernel PCA and subspace methods.
- Forms an operator approximation from collected trajectories, extracts modes, and applies the resulting features to embedding and recognition tasks with favorable empirical results.
- Key conceptual takeaway: compositions of functions admit a linear-operator view enabling spectral decompositions, with dynamical systems as a natural testbed.
- Novelty appears primarily as a kernel-based extension of existing dynamic mode decomposition.
- Applications are illustrative but not clearly more compelling than alternative methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks or other change-detection techniques).
