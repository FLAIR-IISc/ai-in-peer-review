The manuscript, "Koopman Spectral Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems with Reproducing Kernels," extends dynamical mode decomposition (DMD) to the reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), resulting in an algorithm termed KDMD. This approach enables the use of feature maps to define the kernel, effectively linking DMD more directly to features of interest and representing a powerful generalization of the original method. However, a significant concern is the paper's readability. The authors frequently prioritize extensive mathematical formalism over concise explanations, even for straightforward concepts, while other sections are unnecessarily verbose. This combination may hinder comprehension even for specialists, and several points require clarification. While the work is currently too cryptic for publication in the Neural Information Processing Systems conference proceedings, I strongly encourage the authors to revise it with a more pedagogical approach; a clearer presentation would likely result in a highly cited article. 

I. MAJOR REMARKS

1. The presentation is often unnecessarily verbose and could benefit from simplification. For example, the sentence "Conceptually, DMD can be considered as producing an approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of linear monomials of observables as basis functions, which is analogous to a one-term Taylor expansion at each point" could be easily condensed into a single, straightforward sentence.

2. Section 4 is largely redundant in a concise paper, as its content was already partially addressed in the introduction.

3. Section 5 would be significantly improved by including illustrative examples, or at least a summary of its key findings in the conclusion.

4. Section 6 should be expanded in a manner similar to Section 5; its current treatment is overly condensed.

II. MINOR REMARKS

1. There appears to be a typographical error in the description of the nonlinear toy case, as it actually represents a linear system.

2. The first two example problems are polynomial, precisely the scenarios where KDMD should demonstrate its advantage – particularly when the kernel can be effectively chosen, as in example 1. More detailed explanation, similar to that provided in Section 5, would be beneficial.
