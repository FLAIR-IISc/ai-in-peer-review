- Proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator.  
- Uses eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator on forward dynamics viewed as function composition (e.g., f(f(x))).  
- Performs decomposition from collected trajectories via an approach analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.  
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems and reports good empirical performance.  
- Core insight: function composition can be represented as a linear operator amenable to powerful decomposition techniques.  
- Argues dynamical system evolution is a natural application of this operator-based framework.  
- Novelty is limited because Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) already exists in the literature.  
- The main contribution appears to be the kernelization of DMD rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Lacks thorough comparisons to other established methods (e.g., Recurrent Neural Networks) for recognition/change detection.  
- Applications shown are not fully compelling in demonstrating clear advantages over existing techniques.
