- Presents a kernel-based spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a modal decomposition algorithm extending DMD, with a POD-robustified variant and applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Well-written and clearly organized; theoretical concepts and connections to DMD, Koopman operator, and kernel methods are coherent and technically sound.

- The kernel-trick extension of DMD is elegant and the experiments demonstrate practical utility across several tasks, though not exhaustively compared to state-of-the-art.

- Ambiguity in the definition of the Koopman operator in RKHS (around Eq. (7)); unclear whether K_H maps ϕ(M)→H or H→H—domain and codomain need clarification.

- The proof of Theorem 2 is hard to follow; the step using K_H φ_j = λ_j φ_j to derive ⟨ϕ(f(x)), κ_j⟩ = ... is confusing and seems to apply K_H to a scalar-valued function without justification.

- Assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not stated explicitly enough (e.g., distinct Ritz values, implicit invertibility conditions); need discussion of when these hold and how restrictive they are in practice.

- Minor typographical errors (e.g., duplicated "the"); manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading.
