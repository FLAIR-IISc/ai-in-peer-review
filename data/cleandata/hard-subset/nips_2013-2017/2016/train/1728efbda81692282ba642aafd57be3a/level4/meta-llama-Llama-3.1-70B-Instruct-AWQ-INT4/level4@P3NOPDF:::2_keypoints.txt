- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator to analyze nonlinear dynamics, focusing on compositions x(t+2)=f(f(x(t))).  
- Leverages kernel PCA and subspace analysis to perform an eigen-decomposition of the composition operator from collected trajectories.  
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition tasks for dynamical systems, showing empirical effectiveness.  
- Main conceptual contribution: representing function composition as a linear operator and applying decompositions to it.  
- Novelty is limited: the algorithm is essentially dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) from prior literature, with the paper mainly kernelizing it.  
- Experimental results are promising but do not clearly demonstrate unique advantages over existing approaches.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to alternative methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks) used for system recognition and change detection.  
- Recommendation to strengthen the paper: include thorough comparisons and explicitly highlight specific benefits of the proposed approach.
