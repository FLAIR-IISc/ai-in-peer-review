- Introduces spectral analysis using the Koopman operator in an RKHS, defining kernel-based approaches and showing efficiency gains via eigendecomposition; significant kernel construction and experimental validation.
- Lacks clear explanation of substantial differences from Williams et al. [35]; authors should clarify novelty relative to that prior work.
- Section 4 discussion is weak and omits important related work (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Alvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).
- Needs discussion and a toy experiment on Koopman operator performance in stochastic settings (more realistic than deterministic-only analysis).
- Clarify implications of using collections of consecutive observable pairs (vs. full sequences), especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations, and explain what the kernel construction gains under this assumption.
- Elaborate on the causal assumptions inherent in the method.
- Experimental evaluation is not ambitious; prediction via premapping follows a standard approach and offers limited novelty.
- "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics" lacks discussion and experimental comparison with time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means and subspace kernel methods).
- Sequential change-point detection experiment should be compared to state-of-the-art online kernel change detection methods, since the used baseline can fail on complex dynamics.
- More convincing and diverse experiments are needed to robustly support the methodological contributions.
- Paper is generally well written and clear, but contains minor typos (indices tau-1 vs. tau; replace dot with comma in k(xi,xj) equation).
