This paper presents a spectral analysis framework based on the Koopman operator defined in an appropriate reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). Building on this, the authors follow a standard path to develop a family of kernel-based methods for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems from empirical data. By performing an eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator, they report efficiency gains on both synthetic and real datasets. Overall, the paper proposes a substantial kernel construction for nonlinear dynamical systems, with a well-motivated contribution and experimental validation. However, I have several concerns:

- Relation to prior work (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley, 2015): It is not clear what the substantive differences are between the present work and [35] M.O. Williams, I.G. Kevrekidis, and C.W. Rowley, “A data-driven approximation of the Koopman operator: Extending dynamic mode decomposition,” Journal of Nonlinear Science, 25:1307–1346, 2015, where Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes were already used to analyze nonlinear dynamics. Please clarify the main differences and expand the discussion in Section 4 accordingly.

- Missing references in kernel methods for nonlinear dynamical systems: The literature is extensive, and some relevant work is not cited. Please consider adding references such as L. Ralaivola and F. d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, and M.O. Franz and B. Schölkopf.

- Stochastic settings: While the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, practical applications often involve stochastic dynamics. The paper briefly touches on this, but further discussion would be valuable, along with (ideally) a small illustrative experiment to assess performance under stochasticity.

- Assumption about data pairs and causality: You state (line 216, page 5) that “the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is supposed to be” nonlinearly pointwise related. I am interested in the implications of this assumption for multidimensional time series with potentially weak pointwise relations. What benefit does the proposed kernel construction provide in such cases? Please discuss this, and also elaborate on the causal assumption implicit in the setup.

- Experimental design: The experiments are not very ambitious. Prediction via preimaging follows a well-known approach. In “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics,” there is little discussion of connections to, or comparisons with, the substantial literature on clustering time series (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods). For sequential change-point detection, it would be more compelling to compare against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change-detection methods; the method you use is known to fail on complex dynamics. Stronger and more convincing experiments are needed to support the proposed development.

Minor: The paper is very well written and clear. Two small typos: please check indices on tau-1 vs. tau, and replace the dot with a comma in k(x_i, x_j) (last equation on p. 7).
