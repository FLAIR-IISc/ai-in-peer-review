- Paper develops spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS, proposes a modal decomposition algorithm, a robustness-enhancing variant, and demonstrates utility with numerical experiments (extending DMD via the kernel trick).

- Strength: paper is very well written and organized; theoretical concepts are clear and the work appears technically solid.

- Strength: numerical experiments illustrate the method’s usefulness, though no in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art methods is provided.

- Major concern: Section 3.1 (Eq. (7) and Theorem 1 proof) is unclear—Eq. (7) seems essentially the same as Eq. (2); its purpose is not explained.

- Major concern: Eq. (7) appears inconsistent because KH is an operator H→H whereas φ is a mapping M→H (not an element of H); this raises doubts about the argument on lines 144–157.

- Major concern: unclear what “applying the operator KH to both sides” means (Line 149); if both sides are scalars, KH (H→H) cannot be applied.

- Assumptions: Theorem 1’s assumptions seem strong and not explicit; additional assumptions appear in the proof—clarify why they’re needed and whether they are easily satisfied.

- Typos: Line 55 “the the”; Line 153 use \kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i.
