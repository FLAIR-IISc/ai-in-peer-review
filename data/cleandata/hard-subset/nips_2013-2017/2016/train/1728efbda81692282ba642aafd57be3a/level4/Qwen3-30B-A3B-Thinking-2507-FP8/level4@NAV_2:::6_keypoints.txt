- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm for finite-length nonlinear dynamical data.
- Validated on simple systems (damped linear 1D, Hénon map) and real locomotion data; compared to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA.
- Theoretical development is sound, builds on existing kernel methods (kernel PCA/ICA/independence tests).
- Paper is clearly written and educational for ML researchers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
- Lacks strong justification of practical utility in machine learning; needs explicit target ML domains where it outperforms existing methods.
- Robotics suggested as a relevant application, but authors should specify concrete benefits of Koopman spectral analysis for roboticists.
- Experimental results on synthetic and artificial datasets do not convincingly demonstrate competitive advantages in practical ML scenarios.
- Not novel enough theoretically given prior kernelization of linear operators; results are unsurprising.
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation — a potential limitation compared to kernel PCA.
- Request to include a direct comparison of standard DMD performance on toy examples and the Hénon map to highlight differences.
