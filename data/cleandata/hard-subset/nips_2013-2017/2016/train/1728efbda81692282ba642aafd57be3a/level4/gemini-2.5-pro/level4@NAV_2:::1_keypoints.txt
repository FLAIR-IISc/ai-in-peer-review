- Presents kernel DMD (kDMD): a kernel/RKHS extension of the Koopman operator framework for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Derives an algorithm to compute eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projected onto a data-driven RKHS subspace, extracting modes, eigenvalues, and eigenfunctions.  
- Proposes a robust variant using projection onto kernel PCA directions.  
- Demonstrates utility on state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with synthetic and real-world experiments.  
- Novelty/elegance: natural and well-motivated extension of DMD to RKHS, with a clear theoretical connection to Koopman theory.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; theoretical development and theorems are easy to follow.  
- Potential significance: kDMD could be a valuable principled tool for nonlinear time-series feature extraction if robust and scalable.  
- Originality concern: insufficiently distinguishes from Williams et al. (extended DMD); needs explicit conceptual/practical differences and direct comparison.  
- Experimental weaknesses: prediction evaluation relies on standard pre-imaging, obscuring kDMD-specific benefits.  
- Experimental weaknesses: dynamics recognition lacks comparisons to established time-series baselines (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace methods).  
- Experimental weaknesses: change-point detection should be compared to state-of-the-art online/kernel-based change detectors.  
- Stochasticity concern: paper focuses on deterministic systems; needs deeper discussion and experiments on robustness to noise/stochastic dynamics.  
- Literature scope: literature review should include more related kernel-dynamical-systems work (several named authors/groups omitted).  
- Assumptions clarification: implications of requiring only consecutive state pairs vs. continuous sequences need elaboration, including causal/pointwise-relationship effects in high dimensions.  
- Minor issues: fix typos and indexing inconsistencies (e.g., τ vs. τ−1) and notation punctuation in kernel expressions.
