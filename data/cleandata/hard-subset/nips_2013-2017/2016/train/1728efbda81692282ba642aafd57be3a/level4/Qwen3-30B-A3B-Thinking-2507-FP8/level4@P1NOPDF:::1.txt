

This paper presents a spectral analysis framework using the Koopman operator in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), extending standard kernel-based approaches for nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. The eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator's projection yields efficiency gains in both synthetic and real-world data experiments. The contribution is well-motivated and experimentally validated, though several points require clarification:

First, it is unclear how this work differs from Williams et al. (2015) [35], which introduced Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system dynamics analysis. The authors should explicitly contrast their kernel construction with this foundational work and clarify their novel contributions in Section 4.

Second, the literature review omits key references in kernel-based dynamical systems analysis. Relevant works include Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouatamid & Schaback, and Franz & Schölkopf. Including these would strengthen the context and positioning of the paper.

Third, while the Koopman operator is typically defined for deterministic systems, the paper provides insufficient discussion on performance in stochastic settings—ultimately the more realistic and widely applicable case. A brief mention is made, but deeper analysis and a simple stochastic toy experiment would be valuable.

Fourth, the remark at line 216 (p.5) states the method requires "a collection of pairs of consecutive observables, where each pair is supposed to be nonlinear pointwise related." The implications of this assumption for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations need elaboration. Specifically, how does the proposed kernel construction provide advantages over standard approaches in such scenarios? Additionally, the causal assumptions underpinning the framework should be explicitly discussed.

Finally, the experimental evaluation lacks ambition. The prediction task via preimage reconstruction follows a standard approach. Under "Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics," the authors omit critical comparisons with established time series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel techniques), despite the method's apparent relevance. For sequential change-point detection, the comparison to state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods (e.g., those handling complex dynamics) is missing. The current experiments do not sufficiently justify the method's novelty or superiority.

*Minor corrections:*
- Verify indices in "tau-1 vs. tau" (line 216).
- Replace "k(xi.xj)" with "k(xi,xj)" (last equation, p.7).

The paper is otherwise well-written and clear. Addressing the above points would substantially strengthen the contribution and clarity.
