- Paper proposes RKHS-based spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamics using a kernelized Koopman operator and presents a novel kernel construction with promising synthetic and real-data results.  
- Major concern: insufficient distinction from Williams et al. [35]; authors must clearly state theoretical/practical novelties versus existing Koopman/DMD approaches.  
- Literature engagement is incomplete; cite and relate to broader kernel-based dynamical systems work (e.g., Ralaivola, d'Alché Buc, Tuia, Rojo-Álvarez, Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Method focuses on deterministic systems; expand discussion and test robustness/applicability to stochastic systems (simple toy experiment recommended).  
- Clarify data assumptions around using pairs of consecutive observables, implications for high-dimensional time series, and the causal assumptions implied by the pairwise model.  
- Experimental evaluation needs strengthening: more comprehensive prediction metrics for pre-imaging, comparisons to established time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means/subspace kernels), and stronger baselines for change-point detection (including modern kernel-based online methods).  
- Minor fixes: inconsistent indices τ-1 vs τ and change k(xi.xj) to k(xi, xj).
