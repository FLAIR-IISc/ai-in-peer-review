- Paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS).  
- Method evaluated on both simulated and real data; overall well-written and organized.  
- Core contribution: bridges Koopman theory with RKHS, enabling analysis of nonlinear systems via kernel methods.  
- Theoretical foundations are clearly articulated and well-justified.  
- Simulations demonstrate the method can recover underlying dynamics from noisy data.  
- Real-data application supports practical relevance.  
- Missing direct comparison to standard (non-RKHS) Koopman spectral analysis in simulations — leaves unclear when RKHS provides advantages.  
- Kernel choice is not sufficiently explored; performance sensitivity to kernel type and parameters should be discussed or tested.  
- Recommend discussing trade-offs and how kernel choice relates to smoothness assumptions of the dynamical system.  
- Writing, notation, and related-work coverage are clear and comprehensive.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with recommendation to accept; suggested additions would further strengthen impact.
