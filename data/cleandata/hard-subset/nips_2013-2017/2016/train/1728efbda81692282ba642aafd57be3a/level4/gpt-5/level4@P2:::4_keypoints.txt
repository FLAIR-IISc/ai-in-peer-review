- Proposes a kernelized Koopman/DMD formulation in RKHS with modal decomposition via Krylov bases and Koopman projection.
- Introduces a robust variant using kernel-PCA (kernel POD) to mitigate ill-conditioning, analogous to POD-truncated DMD.
- Applies method to short-term prediction (via preimage reconstruction), sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Empirical illustrations include a toy nonlinear system, the Henon map, and motion-capture sequences, showing plausible eigenvalue recovery and qualitative performance.
- Builds on Koopman theory and DMD, closely related to EDMD but uses implicit RKHS bases via kernels and Gram-matrix computations.
- Robustification via kernel PCA parallels POD-based improvements in standard DMD.
- Conceptual clarity and rigorous derivations (Theorems 1–2) linking empirical Ritz quantities to Koopman spectra.
- Practical algorithm that enables nonlinear features without explicit basis design and addresses rank deficiency.
- Broad applicability demonstrated across prediction, change detection, and recognition tasks.
- Writing and organization are generally clear with helpful step-by-step algorithms.
- Empirical evaluation is limited, small-scale, and largely qualitative without quantitative baselines.
- No systematic study of kernel choice or hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., RBF bandwidth).
- Scalability concerns: Gram-matrix costs scale with snapshots; no large-scale strategies (Nyström, random features, incremental updates) discussed.
- Theorem 2’s assumption that eigenfunctions lie in kernel principal directions is unexamined in practice (data richness, mixing, noise).
- Noise robustness is not evaluated.
- Preimage reconstruction uses an MDS-style method but lacks comparisons, stability analysis, and runtime reporting.
- Theoretical development and algorithms are sound but need quantitative metrics (eigenvalue error, RMSE, ROC/AUC, classification accuracy) and ablations to strengthen claims.
- Requests clearer statements of computational complexity, memory requirements, and explicit kernel/hyperparameter selection guidance.
- Suggests clarifying differences to EDMD and “kernel DMD” nomenclature and tightening notation/centralization details.
- Originality: a natural but nontrivial kernel extension of DMD with explicit QR/Krylov construction and kernel-POD robustification; close to EDMD.
- Significance hinges on stronger empirical evidence and baseline comparisons to demonstrate practical gains.
- Pro acceptance: principled kernel extension, practical robust variant, good exposition, and relevant applications.
- Con acceptance: lack of quantitative comparisons, sensitivity/noise studies, scalability discussion, and preimage practicality.
- Key questions for authors: quantitative comparisons to DMD/EDMD, kernel sensitivity/selection, time/memory complexity and scaling strategies, noise robustness, and reporting classification accuracy for recognition.
- Minor suggestions: include a kernel selection guide, ablation on retained kernel-POD components, and consider a sparsity-promoting RKHS variant.
