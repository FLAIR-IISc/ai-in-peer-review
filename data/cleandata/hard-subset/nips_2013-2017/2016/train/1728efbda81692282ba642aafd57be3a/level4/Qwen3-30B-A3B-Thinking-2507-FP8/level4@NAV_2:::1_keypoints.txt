- Proposes spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator in RKHS with a modal decomposition using Krylov matrix orthogonalization and Koopman projection eigendecomposition; demonstrated on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Contribution is well-motivated, experimentally validated, and the paper is clear and well-written.

- Fails to sufficiently distinguish the approach from Williams et al. (2015); needs a detailed comparison highlighting the kernel-based extension and its advantages.

- Literature review omits several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems works (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf) and should cite/discuss them.

- Insufficient treatment of stochastic systems: paper briefly mentions extensions but should discuss performance in stochastic settings and include a toy experiment.

- The claim that estimation does not require a sequence but only pairs of consecutive observables needs clarification, including implications for multidimensional series with weak pointwise relationships and explicit causal assumptions.

- Experiments lack ambition and necessary comparisons: preimage prediction uses standard methods; embedding/recognition should be compared to time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods); change-point detection needs comparisons to state-of-the-art online kernel-based methods.

- Current change-point detection implementation may fail on complex dynamics; more convincing experimental validation required to support claimed advantages.

- Minor fixes needed: correct indices τ−1 vs τ and replace dot with comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on page 7).
