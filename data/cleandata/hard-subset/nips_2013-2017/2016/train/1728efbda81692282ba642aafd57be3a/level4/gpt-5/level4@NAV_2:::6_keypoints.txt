- Develops a spectral treatment of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm to compute it.  
- Demonstrations on two simple synthetic systems (damped 1D, chaotic map) and on real-world locomotion data; comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA yield inconclusive results.  
- Conceptual contribution parallels prior “kernelized” operator methods (kernel PCA/ICA, kernel independence tests, kernel Bayes’ rule).  
- Empirical studies are largely synthetic and do not yet persuade ML readers of practical utility.  
- Method targets deterministic maps M→M; unclear applicability when stochastic effects/noise (common in ML) are present.  
- Reviewer requests concrete, compelling use cases and head-to-head comparisons with state-of-the-art in those domains (e.g., robotics).  
- Theoretical development is careful but not surprising to readers familiar with kernel methods.  
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires an explicit feature-space representation; if so, this limitation should be stated.  
- Suggest adding baseline DMD results on the toy system and the Hénon map to make differences clearer.  
- Paper is generally clearly written and provides pedagogical value for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics tools.
