- Novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS enabling modal decomposition with nonlinear kernel-based basis functions.  
- Frames method as a generalization of DMD, replacing linear monomials with expressive kernel functions.  
- Builds on Koopman operator theory, DMD, and extended DMD while introducing kernel-based nonlinear approximations.  
- Strong theoretical development: theorems establishing RKHS analysis via orthogonal Krylov bases and eigendecomposition of projected operator.  
- Robustification using POD bases addresses numerical instability common in DMD implementations.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets (Hénon map, Lorenz system, locomotion) convincingly demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with detailed mathematical derivations suitable for reproducibility.  
- Algorithmic steps are clearly described and illustrated with informative figures.  
- Original contribution: meaningful generalization beyond EDMD by using kernel functions for flexible, data-adaptive bases.  
- Theoretical connection to Koopman eigenfunctions is established but could be explained more thoroughly.  
- Significant practical impact shown through applications: short-term prediction (preimage reconstruction), dynamics recognition (kernel principal angles), and sequential change-point detection.  
- Strengths summarized: rigorous theory, practical implementation details, clear writing, multiple applications, strong theory-practice link.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to non-RKHS Koopman methods (standard DMD and EDMD).  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration and analysis of kernel choice and its effect on results.  
- Weakness: some theoretical links (RKHS approach to Koopman eigenfunctions) need deeper exposition.  
- Weakness: technical details like kernel parameter selection are not well justified.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing comparative evaluation and discussion of kernel selection and theoretical connections.
