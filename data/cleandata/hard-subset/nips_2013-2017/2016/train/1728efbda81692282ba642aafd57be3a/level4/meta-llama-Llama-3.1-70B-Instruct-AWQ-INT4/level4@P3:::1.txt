This paper introduces a spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator defined in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis, which is essentially reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The algorithm returns a decomposition of the dynamics into a finite number of modes, and thus it can be thought of as a feature extraction procedure for a nonlinear dynamical system.

The paper is well-motivated, and the contribution is experimentally validated. However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, it is not entirely clear how the proposed method differs from the paper by Williams et al. [35], where the Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes were already introduced to analyze nonlinear system dynamics. The authors should provide a more detailed discussion on the main differences between these two approaches.

Secondly, the field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is vast, and some relevant references are missing. The authors should consider including papers by L. Ralaivola and F. d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., M. Mouattamid and R. Schaback, or M. O. Franz and B. B. Schölkopf.

Thirdly, while the Koopman operator is defined for a deterministic system, the authors briefly touch upon its performance in stochastic settings. However, a more detailed discussion and eventually a toy experiment would be welcome to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in realistic scenarios.

Fourthly, the authors should clarify the remark that the estimation procedure itself does not necessarily require a sequence but rather a collection of pairs of consecutive observables. The implication of this assumption in multidimensional time series with eventually weak pointwise relations should be discussed, and the authors should elaborate on the causal assumption inherent in the setting.

Lastly, the experimental settings could be more ambitious. The authors should consider including more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, especially in the sequential change-point detection experiment. Additionally, the connection between the proposed approach and the literature on clustering time series should be discussed.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper introduces a novel spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in RKHS, which is a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis.
* The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow for readers.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks a detailed discussion on the differences between the proposed method and existing approaches, such as the paper by Williams et al. [35].
* The experimental settings could be more ambitious, and the authors should consider including more comparisons with state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on the performance of the proposed method in stochastic settings.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis. However, the authors need to address the concerns mentioned above to strengthen their work. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper could be a strong candidate for acceptance.
