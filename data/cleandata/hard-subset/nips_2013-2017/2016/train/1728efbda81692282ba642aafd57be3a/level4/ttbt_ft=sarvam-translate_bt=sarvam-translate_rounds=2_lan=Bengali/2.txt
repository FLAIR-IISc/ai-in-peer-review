This paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. It presents an operator representation of the so-called Kupman operator function composition. In this case, the Kupman operator decomposition is applied to the forward nonlinear dynamics of the system, x (t + 2) = f (f (x (t))). Given the trajectory collected from the system, some kind of eigen-decomposition of the operator can be performed using concepts similar to those in kernel PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, this method is applied for embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the author shows that the method works well. For readers familiar with the kernel method, the new aspect of this paper is that function composition can be represented as a linear operator, and some interesting decomposition can be applied to function composition. Dynamical systems are a special composition of functions, and this is a good application of it. Since the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm has already been proposed in the dynamical systems literature, the novelty of this paper seems to be limited to its kernelization. There are other methods for dynamical system recognition, such as recurrent neural works and for change detection. In a sense, the applications of this paper are not very strong, which is why it is difficult to demonstrate why this method is good and unique.
