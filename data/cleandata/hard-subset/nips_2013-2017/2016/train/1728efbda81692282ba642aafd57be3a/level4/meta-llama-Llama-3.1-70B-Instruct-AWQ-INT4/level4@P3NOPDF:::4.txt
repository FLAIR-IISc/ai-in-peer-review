This paper presents an extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernels, providing a novel framework for analyzing complex systems. The authors evaluate their method using both simulated and real data, demonstrating its efficacy in various scenarios. The paper is exceptionally well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed approach.

One potential improvement to the paper would be the inclusion of a comparative analysis with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis methods in the simulations. This would provide valuable insights into the benefits and limitations of the proposed approach, allowing readers to better understand its strengths and weaknesses. Additionally, exploring the choice of kernels and their impact on the results would be a worthwhile investigation, potentially conducted through simulations. This discussion would enhance the paper's completeness, enabling readers to make informed decisions when applying the method in practice.

Overall, the paper is well-structured and effectively conveys the authors' ideas. With the inclusion of these additional aspects, the paper would become even more comprehensive and informative, providing a more thorough understanding of the proposed extension to Koopman spectral analysis.
