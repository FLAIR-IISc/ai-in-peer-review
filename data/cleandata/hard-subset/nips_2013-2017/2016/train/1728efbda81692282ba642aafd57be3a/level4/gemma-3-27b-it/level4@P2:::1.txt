This paper introduces a spectral analysis method leveraging the Koopman operator defined within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors propose a framework for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using empirical data, achieving efficiency gains in both synthetic and real-world applications through eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator. The work offers a valuable kernel-based approach to the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, well-motivated and experimentally validated. However, several points warrant further consideration.

The primary concern is clarifying the novelty of this work relative to existing approaches, particularly Williams et al. [35], which already utilizes Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes for nonlinear system analysis. A detailed discussion highlighting the key distinctions and advantages of the presented method over [35] would significantly strengthen the contribution. The discussion in Section 4 could also benefit from expansion.

The field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis with kernels is extensive. The authors should broaden the related work section to include relevant contributions from researchers such as Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, and Franz & Schölkopf. 

While the Koopman operator is generally formulated for deterministic systems, its applicability to stochastic settings – which represent many real-world scenarios – is crucial. Expanding on the performance of the method under stochastic conditions, potentially with a dedicated toy experiment, would enhance its practical relevance. The current treatment of this topic is brief and could be substantially improved.

The authors state that the method doesn’t necessarily require a sequential dataset but a collection of pairs of consecutive observables. Clarification regarding the implications of this assumption for multidimensional time series with potentially weak pointwise relationships is needed. What benefits does the proposed kernel construction offer in such cases? Furthermore, a discussion of the underlying causal assumptions inherent in the framework would be valuable.

The experimental validation, while adequate, could be more compelling. The prediction via preimage follows established approaches. The “Embedding and Recognition of Dynamics” section lacks a detailed comparison to the extensive literature on time series clustering (e.g., kernel k-means and related subspace kernel methods). The sequential change-point detection experiment would benefit from a comparison to state-of-the-art online change detection methods using kernels, as the chosen method is known to struggle with complex dynamics. More robust and comparative experiments are needed to substantiate the claims.

**Strengths:**

*   Novel application of RKHS to Koopman spectral analysis.
*   Clear motivation and well-defined problem statement.
*   Demonstrated effectiveness through synthetic and real-world examples.
*   Well-written and generally clear presentation.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Lack of clear differentiation from existing work, specifically [35].
*   Incomplete related work section.
*   Limited discussion of stochastic settings.
*   Insufficient exploration of implications for non-sequential data.
*   Relatively unchallenging experimental setup and lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound approach with a clear theoretical foundation.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the potential of the method.
*   The work contributes to the growing field of data-driven dynamical systems analysis.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the contribution is not sufficiently established.
*   The experimental validation lacks rigor and comparative analysis.
*   The discussion of important practical considerations (stochasticity, non-sequential data) is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper has merit, but requires significant revisions to address the identified weaknesses. The core idea is promising, but the paper needs to better position itself within the existing literature, provide more thorough experimental validation, and address the practical limitations of the approach. I recommend a **weak reject** with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing these concerns.
