- Presents a spectral method using the Koopman operator and kernel methods to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems from time-series data.  
- Re-frames nonlinear dynamics as a linear operator acting on observables and uses eigendecomposition to produce embeddings.  
- Applies the decomposition to tasks like system recognition and change-point detection with promising empirical performance.  
- Main contribution is the elegant connection between Koopman operator theory and established kernel techniques.  
- Novelty is limited because the algorithm closely resembles a kernelized version of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).  
- The relationship to existing DMD work is not sufficiently discussed or positioned in the paper.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not convincingly demonstrate unique advantages of the method.  
- Lacks comparisons to strong, relevant baselines (e.g., RNNs for time-series, established change-point detection methods).  
- Results show the method works but fail to justify why it should be preferred over existing alternatives.
