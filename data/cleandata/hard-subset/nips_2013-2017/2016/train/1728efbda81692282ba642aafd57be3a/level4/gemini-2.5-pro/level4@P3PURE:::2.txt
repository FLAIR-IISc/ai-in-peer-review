This paper proposes a spectral method for the analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The approach is based on the Koopman operator, which provides a linear representation for function composition. The authors apply a Koopman operator decomposition to the forward dynamics of a system. Given collected trajectories, an eigendecomposition of the operator is performed using ideas analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis. Finally, the method is applied to the embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, and the authors show that it performs well on these tasks.

For an audience familiar with kernel methods, the novel insight is that function composition can be represented by a linear operator to which spectral decompositions can be applied. A dynamical system, which involves the repeated composition of a function, is a natural and well-suited application for this framework.

However, the novelty of the paper appears limited. The core algorithm is closely related to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD), which is an established method in the dynamical systems literature. The primary contribution of this work, therefore, seems to be the application of a kernel method to DMD.

Furthermore, the applications presented are not entirely compelling in demonstrating the unique advantages of this method. There are other established methods for recognizing dynamical systems and detecting changes, such as recurrent neural networks, that are not considered for comparison. Consequently, the experiments do not convincingly demonstrate why this particular approach is superior to or uniquely suited for the chosen tasks.
