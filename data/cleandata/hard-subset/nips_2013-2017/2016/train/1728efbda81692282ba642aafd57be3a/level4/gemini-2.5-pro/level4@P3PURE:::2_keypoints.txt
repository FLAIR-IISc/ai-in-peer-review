- Proposes a spectral method for nonlinear dynamical systems based on the Koopman operator.
- Applies a Koopman operator decomposition to forward dynamics and performs eigendecomposition using ideas analogous to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Demonstrates the method on embedding and recognition tasks and reports good empirical performance.
- Key insight: function composition can be represented by a linear operator amenable to spectral decomposition, making dynamical systems a natural application.
- Novelty appears limited because the core algorithm is closely related to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
- Main contribution is mainly the application of a kernel method to DMD.
- Experimental weaknesses: applications are not fully compelling, lack comparisons to alternatives (e.g., recurrent neural networks), and do not convincingly show superiority.
