This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs) and introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for performing this analysis. The authors also propose a robustifying method and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through several numerical experiments. The overall framework extends the concept of "dynamical mode decomposition" by leveraging the kernel trick. 

The paper is well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts underlying the method. The technical presentation appears solid, making the approach seem interesting and worthy of consideration. Although the authors do not provide an in-depth comparison with state-of-the-art methods, the numerical experiments adequately illustrate the usefulness of their approach. 

However, there are several concerns that require clarification. The major flaw in this paper is Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1, which are not entirely clear. Firstly, Equation (7) bears a resemblance to Equation (2), raising questions about its purpose. Furthermore, Equation (7) appears inconsistent, as KH is a linear operator from H to itself, whereas φ is a function from M to H, which seems to be at odds with the definition of KH. The authors should provide a detailed explanation to address this concern, as it casts doubt on the validity of the proof in Lines 144-157. 

Additionally, the phrase "by applying the operator KH to both sides" (Line 149) is unclear, as both sides of the equation are scalar values, and the operator KH is defined as a mapping from H to H. The assumptions in Theorem 1 appear strong and implicit; the authors should provide explicit explanations for these assumptions. It is also unclear why additional assumptions are introduced in the proof of the theorem, and whether these assumptions hold easily.
