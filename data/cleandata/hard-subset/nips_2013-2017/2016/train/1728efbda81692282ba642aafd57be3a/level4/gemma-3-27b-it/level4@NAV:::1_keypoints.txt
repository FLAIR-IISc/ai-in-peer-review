- Presents a Koopman-operator spectral analysis in an RKHS for nonlinear dynamical systems with empirical data, showing computational efficiency via eigendecomposition and a novel kernel construction with solid experiments.  
- Clarify the relationship to prior work, especially Williams et al. [35], and expand Section 4 to highlight distinct contributions beyond a standard Koopman framework.  
- Related work is incomplete; add and discuss relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamics papers (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Alvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Address applicability to stochastic systems: discuss limitations of Koopman in stochastic settings and include additional analysis or a simple illustrative experiment.  
- Clarify the assumption (line 216) about using collections of observable pairs related nonlinearly pointwise, and discuss performance with multidimensional time series that may have weak pointwise relationships and the framework’s causal assumptions.  
- Strengthen experiments: go beyond a standard prediction task and compare the embedding/recognition results with time-series clustering literature (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernel methods).  
- Improve change-point detection evaluation by comparing against state-of-the-art online kernel-based change detection methods, since the current method has known limitations in complex dynamics.  
- Paper is very clear and well-written; minor fixes: check τ-1 vs τ indices and replace the dot with a comma in k(xi, xj) on the last equation of page 7.
