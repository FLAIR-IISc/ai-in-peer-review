- Overall: Interesting RKHS extension of DMD with sound theory and limited empirical support; reviewer leans weak accept pending revisions.  
- Summary: Extends DMD into RKHS to better capture nonlinear dynamics and situates work among spectral methods and DMD variants.  
- Related Work Gap: Lacks detailed comparison with existing kernel-based DMD approaches (e.g., kernel PCA + DMD) to justify claimed novelty.  
- Strength — Theory: Mathematical derivation is rigorous and theorems are clearly stated and supported.  
- Strength — Addresses Limitation: Directly tackles standard DMD’s weakness in modeling highly nonlinear dynamics.  
- Strength — POD Robustification: Incorporates a POD-based projection to mitigate ill-conditioning, a practical improvement.  
- Strength — Feature Potential: Method could extract useful features for prediction, change detection, and pattern recognition.  
- Weakness — Limited Experiments: Empirical evaluation only on a toy system and the Henon map, insufficient to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Weakness — No Comparative Analysis: Missing quantitative comparisons with other kernelized DMD methods on accuracy, cost, or robustness.  
- Weakness — Superficial Applications: Applications (prediction, embedding, change-point detection) are presented briefly without detailed analysis or baselines.  
- Weakness — Clarity: Some sections, notably the POD robustification, need clearer explanation and intuitive motivation.  
- Weakness — Code Documentation: Code release is positive but documentation is insufficient for easy reproducibility.  
- Significance: Novel explicit RKHS formulation is valuable but incremental rather than a paradigm shift; impact depends on demonstrated benefits.  
- For Acceptance: Strong theoretical foundation, addresses known DMD limitations, useful POD robustification, and promising application potential.  
- Against Acceptance: Insufficient empirical validation, missing comparisons to prior kernel-based DMD, superficial applications, and limited novelty.  
- Recommendations: Expand experiments to more complex/real datasets to showcase advantages.  
- Recommendations: Conduct thorough quantitative comparisons with existing kernel-based DMD methods.  
- Recommendations: Deepen application experiments with quantitative metrics and baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendations: Clarify and better motivate the POD-based robustification step.  
- Recommendations: Improve code documentation to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence rated 7/10.
