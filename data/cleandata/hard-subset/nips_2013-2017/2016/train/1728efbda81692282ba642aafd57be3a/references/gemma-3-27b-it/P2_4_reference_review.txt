## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

**Summary:**

This paper extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to operate within Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS), aiming to improve the approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions beyond the limitations of linear monomials used in traditional DMD. The authors present an algorithm based on Krylov subspace calculations and eigendecomposition, and address practical implementation issues (ill-conditioning) with a projection onto POD directions. They demonstrate the method on toy systems and the Henon map, and explore potential applications in prediction, dynamic pattern recognition, and change-point detection.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a technically sound extension of DMD with potential benefits in scenarios where the Koopman eigenfunctions are not well-approximated by linear monomials. However, the empirical validation is limited, and the significance of the contribution, while promising, is not fully convincing given the current presentation. I lean towards a **weak accept** with major revisions. 

**Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality (7/10):** The mathematical derivation appears correct and logically presented. The extension to RKHS is a natural progression of DMD, and the inclusion of POD projection to address ill-conditioning is a practical and sensible addition.  The connection to the Krylov subspace and Ritz values is clearly explained. However, the empirical evaluation lacks depth. The toy example is straightforward and doesn’t fully showcase the benefits of the kernel approach, while the Henon map results are only *approximately* estimated, raising questions about the algorithm’s robustness and accuracy.  A more rigorous comparison against standard DMD and other Koopman approximation techniques (e.g., extended DMD) with controlled experiments is needed. The authors mention needing to find the pre-image in the prediction section, and simply reference MDS without detailing an implementation.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and provides context. The mathematical notation is mostly clear, though the many equations can be a bit dense.  A clearer explanation of *why* RKHS are expected to improve upon traditional DMD, with some illustrative examples, would be helpful. The discussion of potential applications is somewhat cursory.
* **Originality (7/10):** While the idea of applying kernel methods to Koopman analysis isn’t entirely novel (related work section acknowledges this), the specific formulation and algorithm presented here are a distinct contribution. The rigorous treatment of the algorithm and its connection to DMD are valuable.  However, the paper needs to better differentiate itself from existing kernel-based methods for system identification and Koopman approximation. The link to subspace identification methods mentioned in the related work section is intriguing but remains unexplored.
* **Significance (6/10):**  The potential applications (prediction, pattern recognition, change-point detection) are relevant and interesting. The authors demonstrate some initial results, but they lack sufficient detail and comparative analysis to establish significant advantages over existing methods. The performance gains from using RKHS need to be more convincingly demonstrated. The claim that this method "provides a feature extraction procedure" is a bit vague and needs to be substantiated with more concrete examples of downstream tasks.  

**Strengths:**

* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The mathematical development is rigorous and builds logically upon existing DMD theory.
* **Practical Improvement:** The POD projection addresses a known limitation of DMD and improves robustness.
* **Potential for Improved Accuracy:** The use of RKHS offers the potential to capture more complex dynamics than linear monomials.
* **Exploration of Applications:** The paper considers a range of potential applications.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The experiments are too limited to convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed method.  More comprehensive comparisons with other techniques are needed.
* **Vague Application Details:** The application sections are descriptive but lack detailed implementation and quantitative results.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  It's unclear how much of the observed performance is due to the RKHS extension versus the POD projection.
* **Connection to Related Work:** The connections to subspace identification and other related work are acknowledged but not explored in detail.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  Presents a theoretically sound and potentially valuable extension of DMD.
*  Addresses a practical issue (ill-conditioning) with a sensible solution.
*  Demonstrates initial proof-of-concept results on benchmark problems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  Empirical validation is weak and lacks comparative analysis.
*  Applications are not fully fleshed out and lack quantitative results.
*  Significance of the contribution is not fully established.




**Recommendations:**

**Major Revisions:**

1. **Expanded Empirical Evaluation:** Conduct a more thorough evaluation of the method on a wider range of datasets, including both synthetic and real-world data.  Compare performance against standard DMD, extended DMD, and other relevant Koopman approximation techniques.  Include metrics that quantify the accuracy of the estimated Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.
2. **Detailed Application Studies:**  Provide more detailed explanations of the applications, including specific implementation details and quantitative results.
3. **Ablation Studies:**  Investigate the individual contributions of the RKHS extension and the POD projection through ablation studies.
4. **Explore Related Work:** Expand the discussion of related work, particularly in the areas of subspace identification and kernel-based system identification.
5. **Clarify Implementation Details**: Add details surrounding the pre-image problem mentioned in the prediction section.

I believe that with these revisions, this paper could become a valuable contribution to the field of Koopman spectral analysis.
