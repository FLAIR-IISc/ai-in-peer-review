- Paper proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis using kernels and Krylov subspaces.  
- Introduces POD-based robustification to address numerical instability in DMD.  
- Demonstrates applications: state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Provides solid theoretical foundation with proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) linking the kernel approach to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Robustification with POD is a practical fix for ill-conditioned DMD algorithms.  
- Empirical validation includes toy systems, Hénon map, locomotion data, and Lorenz map.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical guarantees on approximation quality and error bounds related to kernel choice and data size.  
- Insufficient direct comparison with Extended DMD and other state-of-the-art Koopman/kernel methods; relation to [35] is vague.  
- No computational complexity or scalability analysis for the kernel-based method (potential O(n^3) issues unaddressed).  
- No guidance on kernel selection or sensitivity analysis for kernel parameters.  
- Empirical comparisons are limited and omit strong baselines (e.g., Extended DMD, ARIMA, LSTM).  
- Assumes Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the RKHS without discussing conditions or limitations of that assumption.  
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the impact of components like kernel choice and POD robustification.  
- Section 3 needs a clearer, detailed distinction from Extended DMD.  
- Section 4 would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why POD robustification works.  
- Hénon map results are ambiguous and need quantitative eigenvalue error metrics rather than only visual plots.  
- Change-point detection comparisons are unclear and should explicitly compare to existing change-point methods.  
- References miss some relevant recent kernel-based Koopman literature (e.g., work cited as [15]).  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance—technically sound and useful but lacking in comparisons, guarantees, and scalability analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed comparisons with Extended DMD and recent methods, analyze computational complexity, include ablation studies, provide kernel selection guidance, and report quantitative performance metrics.  
- Pros for acceptance: sound theory, addresses DMD ill-conditioning, useful applications, empirical validation, clear presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: no approximation guarantees, insufficient state-of-the-art comparisons, no complexity analysis, no kernel guidance, limited empirical baselines, unaddressed theoretical limitations.
