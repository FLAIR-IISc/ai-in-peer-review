- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Presents a modal decomposition algorithm in RKHS with Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization and a POD-robustified variant.  
- Applies the method to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and motion-capture data.  

- Strength: Strong mathematical foundation with clear theorems linking the method to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Strength: Addresses a relevant limitation of standard (linear) DMD by introducing more expressive kernel-based bases.  
- Strength: Paper is well organized and clearly written, connecting theory, method, and applications.  

- Weakness: Limited novelty—method is essentially a special case of extended DMD (basis from kernels) and differences are not clearly articulated.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is insufficient—limited datasets and no comparisons to standard/extended DMD or other baselines.  
- Weakness: Lacks quantitative metrics for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Weakness: No analysis of kernel hyperparameter effects or demonstration of scalability to larger datasets.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps—no approximation error bounds, convergence guarantees, or conditions where kernel bases outperform linear ones.  
- Weakness: Practical concerns—computational complexity and mitigation of ill-conditioning are not discussed in detail.  

- Related work: Fails to clearly distinguish from Williams et al. (extended DMD); kernel DMD is a specific instance of extended DMD.  
- Suggested improvement: Explicitly show how the method generalizes or differs from extended DMD and when it provides advantages.  
- Suggested improvement: Provide theoretical conditions and empirical scenarios where kernel-based bases outperform linear monomials.  
- Suggested improvement: Demonstrate concrete advantages on real-world problems where linear bases fail.  

- Significance: Important problem but paper does not sufficiently advance state-of-the-art or convincingly outperform simpler alternatives.  

- Recommendation: Major revision required to address novelty and provide comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Key revisions needed: Clarify differences from extended DMD; add comparisons with standard/extended DMD; include theoretical error/complexity analysis; provide quantitative empirical results across multiple datasets; discuss hyperparameter selection and scalability.  

- Final assessment: Theoretically sound but not novel or empirically rigorous enough for acceptance; recommendation: Reject (encouraged to resubmit after substantial revision).
