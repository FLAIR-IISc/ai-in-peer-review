- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) with reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis, plus a POD-based robust variant and applications to prediction, change detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Theoretical contribution is solid with two key theorems linking the algorithm to Koopman operator theory.  
- Algorithmic description is clear and complete for both basic and robustified methods.  
- Empirical validation on toy nonlinear systems and the Hénon map shows correct estimation of Koopman eigenvalues.  
- Authors acknowledge numerical instability and address it via POD projection, showing practical awareness.  
- Insufficient comparison to existing methods (notably Extended DMD) and lack of demonstrated advantages over them.  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic examples; no substantial real-world or large-scale validation.  
- Computational complexity and scalability (e.g., RKHS Gram-Schmidt/QR costs) are not adequately discussed.  
- No rigorous analysis of approximation error for finite-dimensional RKHS approximations.  
- Paper is well-organized with consistent notation and helpful figures, but some sections are hard for non-experts.  
- Practical implementation details (Gram-matrix reduction, preimage calculation) are under-explained and could use pseudocode.  
- Idea of RKHS-based DMD and POD robustification is novel, but theoretical novelty is incremental and relation to prior kernel/Koopman work is underdeveloped.  
- Potentially significant for modeling nonlinear dynamics, but significance is weakened by lack of empirical superiority and simple application demos.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but incremental, lacking comparisons and real-world validation; not ready for NeurIPS.  
- Recommendation: Reject; improve by adding thorough comparisons to Extended DMD, more real-world experiments, clearer distinctions from prior work, and analysis of computational complexity and approximation error.
