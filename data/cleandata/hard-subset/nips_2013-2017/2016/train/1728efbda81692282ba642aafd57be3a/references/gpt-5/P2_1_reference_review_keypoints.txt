- Core idea: perform Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS by applying DMD to feature-mapped observables ϕ(x) to get a finite-dimensional projection of the Koopman operator.  
- Algorithms: propose a kernelized DMD via Gram–Schmidt in feature space and a “robustified” variant projecting onto kernel PCA directions (analogous to truncated POD/Exact DMD).  
- Theory: present two theorems linking computed Ritz values/vectors to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions, but proofs omit residual terms and rely on strong assumptions.  
- Applications: propose short-term prediction via pre-image, dynamics recognition using kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection with a CUSUM-like score.  
- Experiments: small empirical illustrations on toy system, Hénon map, MoCap sequences, and Lorenz change detection, but largely qualitative and limited in scale.  
- Relation to prior work: closely related to EDMD and kernel EDMD (Williams et al. 2015) and follows common Exact/Optimized DMD and sparsity-promoting DMD practices.  
- Robustification novelty: projecting onto kernel PCA mirrors standard truncated SVD/POD in DMD and is not substantially novel.  
- Theoretical caveats: Theorem 1 should account for Arnoldi residuals explicitly; Theorem 2 depends on strong span assumptions that limit identifiability claims.  
- Algorithmic gaps: no complexity or numerical stability analysis, and missing guidance on kernel/regularization selection, truncation criteria, or conditioning.  
- Experimental weaknesses: lack quantitative error metrics, baselines (EDMD/KEDMD/Exact DMD), and statistical operating points; results are mainly qualitative.  
- Claims vs evidence: claim that kernelization improves eigenfunction approximation is plausible but not empirically demonstrated against competitors.  
- Clarity issues: numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, overloaded symbols, and insufficient detail on implementation (e.g., pre-image solver, hyperparameters).  
- References/status of related work: some references incomplete and kernel EDMD not clearly differentiated or sufficiently cited.  
- Originality assessment: reframing via Gram–Schmidt and the two theorems are incremental; methodological novelty is limited relative to existing kernel EDMD literature.  
- Significance for NeurIPS: conceptually relevant but needs clearer theoretical advances (consistency/stability) or strong empirical gains to be competitive.  
- Pros: clear operator-theoretic motivation, practical kernel-matrix algorithms, intuitive theorems about kernel PCA subspaces, and useful application ideas.  
- Cons: limited novelty over EDMD, shaky derivations (missing residuals/undefined inverses), qualitative/small-scale experiments, no complexity/regularization discussion, and writing/notation problems.  
- Key author actions suggested: clarify relation to EDMD/KEDMD, fix derivations to include residuals and valid inverses, add quantitative comparisons and baselines, discuss complexity/regularization/kernel choice, and clean up writing and references.  
- Overall assessment: timely and coherent RKHS framing but incremental relative to prior work and currently under-supported by theory and experiments; not ready for NeurIPS without substantial revision.
