- Overall: Sound idea extending DMD with RKHS but presentation is disjoint and empirical validation is insufficient — recommendation: weak accept with major revisions.
- Summary/Related work: Extends DMD by using RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, but related work section omits deeper engagement with EDMD and kernel-DMD literature.
- Claimed advantage: RKHS allows automatic, flexible basis construction potentially yielding more accurate Koopman eigenfunction approximations than monomial bases.
- Positioning gap: Needs clearer differentiation from EDMD and explicit connections to Kernel PCA-based DMD approaches.
- Strength — theory: Mathematical derivation is clear and includes a theorem showing equivalence to standard DMD under certain conditions.
- Strength — accuracy potential: RKHS basis functions should improve approximation for systems where linear monomials fail.
- Strength — robustness: Inclusion of POD projection to mitigate ill-conditioning is practical and important.
- Strength — applications: Demonstrates downstream uses (prediction, change-point detection, dynamic recognition), showing feature versatility.
- Weakness — clarity/organization: Paper flow is sometimes disjoint; POD motivation feels tacked on rather than integrated.
- Weakness — empirical validation: Experiments limited to a toy system and Henon map; results are inconclusive and need broader testing with noise and real data.
- Weakness — comparative analysis: Lacks rigorous comparisons to EDMD, kernelized DMD variants, and standard DMD on accuracy and downstream tasks.
- Weakness — kernel choice: Kernel selection (polynomial, Gaussian) seems arbitrary; missing sensitivity analysis on kernel parameters.
- Weakness — computational complexity: Scalability and cost (Gram matrix, high-dimensional computations) not thoroughly discussed; POD helps but more analysis needed.
- Significance/originality: Formal RKHS integration into DMD is novel and promising, but impact not demonstrated without consistent empirical advantage.
- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretically sound, relevant to nonlinear dynamical systems, and a novel RKHS application to spectral analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experiments, unclear comparative benefits, presentation issues, and missing kernel/complexity discussion.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on major revisions improving clarity and empirical/comparative rigor.
- Revision suggestions: Expand related work (EDMD, kernel-DMD), strengthen empirical evaluation across datasets/noise levels, include direct comparisons to other methods, perform kernel sensitivity analysis, analyze computational complexity and scalability, and reorganize for clearer presentation.
