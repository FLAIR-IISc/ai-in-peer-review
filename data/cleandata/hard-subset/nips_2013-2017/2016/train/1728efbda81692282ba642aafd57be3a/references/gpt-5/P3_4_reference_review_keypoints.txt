- Proposes kernelized DMD/Arnoldi in RKHS using Krylov subspaces, Gram–Schmidt, and Ritz value/vector computation for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Introduces a "robustified" variant projecting onto kernel-PCA directions and forming an operator expressed via Gram matrices for eigendecomposition.  
- Claims richer nonlinear basis functions than linear DMD and approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions in RKHS.  
- Demonstrates applications: short-term prediction via preimage, dynamics recognition via subspace distances, and change-point detection on toy systems.  
- Approach closely matches EDMD/kernel EDMD; the robust algorithm is essentially kernel EDMD/method-of-snapshots implemented with Gram matrices.  
- Novelty is mainly a RKHS/Krylov/Arnoldi re-derivation and two theorems linking kernel-PCA projection to Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Strength: clear motivation that linear DMD can be restrictive and kernels offer expressive nonlinear features.  
- Strength: conceptual unification of DMD/EDMD via an RKHS/Arnoldi perspective that clarifies projection and eigendecomposition steps.  
- Strength: provides a practical Gram-matrix recipe with rank truncation analogous to POD for numerical stability.  
- Strength: shows potential utility through several application sketches beyond basic spectral analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient novel contribution relative to prior kernel EDMD work; overlaps are underplayed and no direct empirical/conceptual comparison is provided.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 proof conflates multiple projected/companion operators and contains ill-defined expressions (e.g., claiming invertibility of mappings from C^τ to H).  
- Weakness: Strong, unexamined assumptions (invariance, diagonalizability, eigenfunctions lying in RKHS span) are required but not justified or discussed for noisy/continuous-spectrum cases.  
- Weakness: Incorrect claim that Koopman eigenvalues always lie on the unit circle for systems on an attractor; this ignores dissipative dynamics.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited and qualitative—lack quantitative metrics, sensitivity analyses, comparisons to DMD/EDMD/kernel EDMD, and scaling/noise studies.  
- Weakness: Preimage reconstruction is heuristic and unbenchmarked; effect of preimage error on prediction is not evaluated.  
- Weakness: Recognition/change-point experiments lack methodological detail (dataset sizes, kernel tuning, baselines, runtimes, error rates).  
- Weakness: Notational inconsistencies, incomplete references, and missing discussion of algorithmic complexity, numerical stabilization, and regularization.  
- Significance: Incremental given existing kernel EDMD literature; needs theoretical advances or stronger empirical evidence to justify publication.  
- Key suggestions: clarify operator algebra and precise conditions for theorems, correct incorrect expressions, and discuss relaxation under noise/approximation.  
- Key suggestions: strengthen theory by providing approximation/convergence results or conditions on kernels/dynamics when eigenfunctions lie in the RKHS.  
- Key suggestions: explicitly position and compare to EDMD/kernel EDMD, include head-to-head benchmarks, ablations, and quantitative error metrics.  
- Key suggestions: expand experiments to measure eigenvalue/mode errors, prediction horizons, robustness to noise and kernel parameters, and evaluate preimage methods.  
- Key suggestions: fix notation, complete references, and address complexity/scalability and numerical regularization in practice.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject — worthwhile idea but needs clearer positioning vs. kernel EDMD, corrected theory, and substantially stronger empirical validation.
