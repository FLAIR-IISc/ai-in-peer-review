This paper introduces a dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) method leveraging reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to enhance Koopman spectral analysis for nonlinear dynamical systems. The core idea is to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions derived from kernel methods, extending beyond the linear monomials used in traditional DMD. The authors present an algorithm based on Krylov subspace calculations and eigendecomposition, and address potential ill-conditioning through projection onto POD directions. They demonstrate the method's effectiveness on synthetic and real-world data, showcasing applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

The paper builds upon a strong foundation of related work, appropriately referencing seminal papers in spectral analysis (Koopman operator, POD, DMD) and kernel methods. The connection to existing DMD techniques is clearly articulated, emphasizing the advantage of using nonlinear basis functions for more accurate representation of complex dynamics. The authors accurately position their work within the broader landscape of nonlinear system identification and model reduction. The inclusion of related work on sparsity-promoting DMD, extended DMD, and subspace identification demonstrates awareness of the current research landscape.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The application of RKHS to DMD represents a valuable extension of the existing methodology. Using kernel functions to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions allows for capturing more complex, nonlinear dynamics compared to traditional DMD which relies on linear monomials.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the algorithm, particularly Theorem 1, provides a solid mathematical foundation. The inclusion of a robustification step via POD projection (Theorem 2) addresses a practical concern of ill-conditioning.
*   **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The explanation of the core concepts (Koopman operator, DMD, RKHS) is clear, and the algorithm is presented in a step-by-step manner.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The results on both the toy system and the Hénon map demonstrate the potential of the proposed method. The applications section, while brief, provides compelling examples of its utility in prediction, dynamic recognition, and change-point detection.
*   **Availability of Code:** Providing a link to the Matlab code promotes reproducibility and facilitates further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Empirical Evaluation:** While the initial results are promising, the empirical evaluation is somewhat limited. The toy system and Hénon map, while useful for validation, are relatively simple. Applying the method to more complex, high-dimensional datasets would strengthen the claims of its effectiveness.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The choice of kernel function and its parameters (e.g., kernel width in the Gaussian kernel) can significantly impact performance. The paper does not thoroughly address the sensitivity of the method to these parameters or provide guidance on how to select appropriate values.
*   **Computational Cost:** The Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process in the RKHS can be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. A discussion of the computational complexity and potential strategies for scalability (e.g., using Nyström methods or other kernel approximation techniques) would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to other existing methods (e.g., extended DMD, sparse DMD, kernel PCA) is not comprehensive. A more thorough comparison, using standardized datasets and evaluation metrics, would better establish the advantages of the proposed approach.
*   **Lack of Detailed Explanation in Applications:** The application sections present intriguing results but lack detailed explanations of the specific settings and parameter choices used. More in-depth descriptions of the data preprocessing, feature selection, and performance evaluation metrics would enhance the clarity and reproducibility of the results.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound extension of DMD with potential to improve the accuracy of Koopman spectral analysis.
*   The algorithm is well-defined and addresses a practical challenge (ill-conditioning) with a robustification step.
*   The empirical results, while limited, demonstrate the feasibility and promise of the proposed method.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The empirical evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive to fully validate the method's effectiveness on complex datasets.
*   The sensitivity to kernel parameters and the computational cost are not adequately addressed.
*   The comparison to state-of-the-art methods is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical system analysis. The proposed method, while requiring further validation and optimization, has the potential to advance the state of the art in Koopman spectral analysis. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above by:

1.  **Expanding the empirical evaluation:** Apply the method to more complex, high-dimensional datasets and compare its performance to state-of-the-art methods.
2.  **Addressing parameter sensitivity:** Conduct a sensitivity analysis of the kernel parameters and provide guidance on their selection.
3.  **Discussing computational complexity:** Analyze the computational cost of the algorithm and explore potential scalability improvements.
4.  **Providing detailed explanations:**  Elaborate on the experimental setup and evaluation metrics for the application sections.
5.  **Consider inclusion of running time measurements.** Comparing execution time of this method to other methods in the field will allow the readers to appreciate the benefits of the method.

Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential for acceptance in a future submission.
