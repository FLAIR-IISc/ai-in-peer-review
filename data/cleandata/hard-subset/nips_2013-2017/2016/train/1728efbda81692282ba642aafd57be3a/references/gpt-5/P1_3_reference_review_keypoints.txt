- Proposes kernelized DMD/Arnoldi in RKHS with two variants: direct kernelized Arnoldi/DMD and a robust kernel‑PCA/POD projection.  
- Theorem 1 gives a "kernel Ritz" expansion relating projected DMD eigenpairs to Koopman modes under diagonalizability and distinct eigenvalues.  
- Theorem 2 relates eigenvectors from the truncated kernel‑PCA projection to true Koopman eigenfunctions but requires strong representation assumptions.  
- Paper sketches applications: preimage-based short-term prediction, subspace-angle based dynamics recognition, and CUSUM-like change‑point detection in RKHS.  
- Empirical illustrations include a toy system, Hénon map, motion capture data, and a change‑point demo, but are mostly qualitative.  
- Work connects to DMD/Koopman literature and closely overlaps with EDMD/KDMD (kernel EDMD) and POD-based DMD variants.  
- RKHS derivation and kernelization via QR/projection are conceptually sound and consistent with Arnoldi/Ritz theory.  
- Linear algebra statements (e.g., F = Mτ^{-1}KHMτ) are informal and need clearer treatment of projections and pseudoinverses.  
- Theoretical results assume strong conditions and lack error bounds, convergence rates, or noise robustness analysis.  
- Robust kernel‑PCA projection mirrors standard DMD practice but computational complexity, numerical stability, and regularization are not addressed.  
- Scalability and approximations (Nyström, random features) are not discussed for large datasets.  
- Experiments lack quantitative metrics, ablation studies, sensitivity analyses, and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Hyperparameter choices (kernel type/width, truncation) are not justified or studied.  
- Contains typos, inconsistent notation (ϕ vs φ; ψ overload), and at least one incorrect general statement about Koopman eigenvalues on attractors.  
- Novelty is limited because kernelized EDMD/KDMD exist; contributions are a clean RKHS presentation and compact algorithms but incremental applications.  
- Significance is moderate: useful to practitioners but limited by empirical and theoretical gaps and overlap with prior work.  
- Pros: clear RKHS framing, practical kernel‑PCA robustification, links to Koopman eigenfunctions, and available code.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs EDMD, sparse/qualitative experiments, strong theoretical assumptions, missing error/noise analysis, clarity and scalability shortcomings.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify relation to EDMD/exact DMD, add numerical stability/complexity discussion and regularization, include quantitative experiments and baselines, correct linear algebra and attractor/eigenvalue statements, and analyze hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger comparisons, formal theory, and more thorough empirical validation.
