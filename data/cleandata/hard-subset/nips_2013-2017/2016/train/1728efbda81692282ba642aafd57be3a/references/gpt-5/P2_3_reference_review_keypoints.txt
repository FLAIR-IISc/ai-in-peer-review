- Paper proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using a Krylov matrix of feature maps, Gram–Schmidt in feature space, projection of the Koopman operator, and reconstruction of observables/modes.  
- The method is robustified by projecting onto kernel PCA (POD) to mitigate Gram–Schmidt ill-conditioning and uses eigendecomposition of centered Gram matrices.  
- Theorem 1 parallels Arnoldi/DMD, giving exact reconstruction within the snapshot window and a residual for the next snapshot.  
- Theorem 2 links the projected eigenpairs to true Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under strong representer assumptions (eigenfunctions lie in the RKHS and in the retained KPCA span).  
- The paper presents toy and Hénon-map experiments and sketches applications: short-term prediction via preimage, embedding/recognition via kernel principal angles, and CUSUM-like sequential change detection.  
- This work heavily overlaps with DMD/EDMD literature, especially kernelized EDMD (KDMD); the robust POD/SVD truncation is standard in that family.  
- The RKHS–Arnoldi equivalence is conceptually sound, but some derivations compress steps and conflate projected and true operator actions (projection nuance underemphasized).  
- The claim that Koopman eigenvalues “always lie on a unit circle” for attractors is factually incorrect; dissipative systems can have eigenvalues with magnitude < 1.  
- Practical algorithmic details need refinement: numerical stability, correct centering (mean vs sum), consistent notation, and explicit pseudocode.  
- Computational complexity and memory scaling (O(τ^3), O(τ^2)) and kernel hyperparameter selection are not discussed.  
- Experiments are weak: qualitative eigenvalue plots only, no quantitative metrics, sparse baselines (no direct EDMD/KDMD or optimized DMD comparisons), no ablations or noise sensitivity analysis.  
- Clarity issues: grammatical errors, overloaded notation (φ used for different objects), and imprecise statements about projections and centering.  
- Originality is limited: kernelizing DMD/Koopman methods and POD truncation closely match existing KDMD/EDMD approaches; novelty lies mainly in pedagogical RKHS/Arnoldi framing.  
- Significance is limited without stronger empirical evidence showing advantages over KDMD/EDMD or other Koopman learning methods, though code availability helps reproducibility.  
- Pros: clear operator-theoretic framing, sensible kernel-POD robustification, interesting application ideas, and provided code.  
- Cons: substantial overlap with prior work, weak empirical evaluation, technical inaccuracies and notation problems, and no analysis of complexity, hyperparameter sensitivity, or noise robustness.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly distinguish or concede equivalence to EDMD/KDMD, fix factual/notation errors, provide clear pseudocode and complexity, add quantitative comparisons (EDMD/KDMD/exact DMD), study noise and hyperparameter sensitivity, and discuss scalability (Nyström/randomized methods).  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — clear exposition and a reasonable pipeline but limited novelty and insufficient empirical validation for acceptance.
