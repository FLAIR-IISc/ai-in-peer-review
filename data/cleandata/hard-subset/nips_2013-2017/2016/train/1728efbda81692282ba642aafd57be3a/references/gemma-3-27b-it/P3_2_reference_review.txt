## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a conceptually interesting extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) leveraging the power of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors aim to overcome the limitations of standard DMD by using nonlinear kernel functions to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, potentially offering a more accurate representation of complex nonlinear dynamics. While the core idea is sound and the mathematical development is generally rigorous, the presentation lacks sufficient depth regarding practical considerations, and the empirical evaluation feels somewhat limited, particularly concerning the novelty of the results demonstrated.  I lean towards **weak accept** with significant revisions required.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper builds upon the established foundations of Koopman operator theory and DMD. It correctly identifies the limitations of standard DMD, which relies on linear monomials for approximating eigenfunctions. The proposed approach elegantly extends DMD by embedding the dynamics in an RKHS, allowing for the use of nonlinear basis functions via kernel methods. The connection to Krylov subspace methods is well-articulated. 

The related work section is adequate but could be more comprehensive.  While key papers in DMD and spectral methods are cited, the discussion lacks depth regarding kernel methods specifically applied to Koopman analysis. For example, the connection to kernel PCA, which is heavily leveraged in the robustification step (Section 4), could be more thoroughly explored.  Recent works on extended DMD and kernel-based DMD variants should be explicitly discussed and differentiated.  The authors mention the connection to subspace identification but don’t explore it further.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The mathematical development, particularly the derivation of the algorithm based on the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in RKHS and the theorems (1 & 2) providing the connection between the estimated eigenvalues/eigenvectors and the Koopman operator, are solid and well-presented.
* **Principled Extension of DMD:**  The use of RKHS provides a theoretically justifiable way to move beyond linear approximations in DMD, potentially unlocking the analysis of more complex systems.
* **Robustification Technique:**  The inclusion of POD-based projection (Section 4) to address potential ill-conditioning is a crucial practical consideration, demonstrating awareness of implementation challenges.
* **Multiple Applications:**  The exploration of prediction, embedding/recognition, and change-point detection showcases the potential versatility of the proposed method.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Empirical Results:** The empirical demonstrations (toy system and Henon map) are largely proof-of-concept and don’t showcase a significant advantage over existing DMD methods. The toy system results are expected given its simplicity, and the Henon map results, while showing some agreement, lack a quantitative comparison with alternative approaches. The claim of accurately estimating eigenvalues requires substantial justification beyond a visual inspection.
* **Kernel Selection and Parameter Tuning:** The paper lacks a discussion of kernel selection and parameter tuning (e.g., bandwidth in the Gaussian kernel). These are critical practical aspects that significantly influence performance, and their omission weakens the empirical evaluation.  How sensitive are the results to different kernel choices and parameters?
* **Computational Complexity:**  The computational cost of the proposed method, particularly the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in the high-dimensional RKHS, is not explicitly addressed.  This is a significant concern for large-scale datasets.  A discussion of scalability and potential approximations would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The performance is not compared to state-of-the-art Koopman learning techniques (e.g., sparse identification of nonlinear dynamics (SINDy) coupled with DMD, or other kernel-based DMD variants) on benchmark datasets or challenging real-world problems.
* **Clarity in Application Sections:** While the application sections outline potential uses, they are somewhat superficial. The prediction example lacks details on the pre-image recovery process. The locomotion example uses a standard kernel PCA approach for embedding and doesn't fully leverage the unique capabilities of the proposed method.

**4. Specific Comments & Questions:**

* **Theorem 2:** The assumption that “φj ∈ Φ” seems strong.  Can this be relaxed? What are the implications if this assumption doesn't hold?
* **Section 6:**  How were the initial conditions chosen for the toy system and Henon map?  Were multiple initial conditions used to assess the robustness of the results?
* **Section 7 (Change Point Detection):** The connection between the Koopman operator and the stochastic formulation is not sufficiently clear. More details on how the log-likelihood ratio score is derived and how it relates to the estimated Koopman modes would be helpful.
* **Robustification:** The choice of using POD directions seems intuitive, but a more theoretical justification or comparison with other projection techniques would be beneficial.



**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound extension of DMD with potential benefits for analyzing nonlinear dynamics.
* The inclusion of a robustification technique addresses a practical challenge.
* The exploration of multiple applications demonstrates the method’s versatility.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical evaluation is limited and doesn't provide strong evidence of a significant advantage over existing methods.
* The lack of discussion regarding kernel selection, parameter tuning, and computational complexity weakens the practical relevance of the work.
* The comparison to state-of-the-art Koopman learning techniques is missing.

**6. Recommendation:**

**Weak Accept, with Major Revisions.** The authors should significantly strengthen the empirical evaluation by:

1.  Applying the method to more challenging benchmark datasets or real-world problems.
2.  Conducting a thorough comparison with state-of-the-art Koopman learning techniques.
3.  Performing a sensitivity analysis of kernel selection and parameter tuning.
4.  Providing a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and potential scalability issues.
5.  Expanding the application sections with more concrete details and quantitative results.

Addressing these weaknesses will elevate the paper from a conceptually interesting idea to a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamics and machine learning.
