Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels (kernel DMD) to approximate Koopman spectra of nonlinear dynamical systems in RKHS. Two variants are developed:

- A direct kernelized Arnoldi/DMD: form the Krylov matrix Mτ = [ϕ(x0)…ϕ(xτ−1)] in feature space, compute a Gram–Schmidt QR, project the (feature-space) Koopman operator KH onto the span, and take eigenpairs of the projected operator. The paper proves a “kernel Ritz” expansion (Theorem 1) analogous to DMD’s relation to Koopman modes.

- A robust variant: project data onto a truncated kernel-PCA/POD subspace to regularize, and then compute eigenpairs of F̂ = U*KHU, where U are the leading kernel principal directions, with a theorem (Theorem 2) relating these eigenvectors to true Koopman eigenfunctions under representation assumptions.

The paper then sketches applications that use the extracted features: short-term prediction via preimage reconstruction, dynamics recognition using kernel principal angles between subspaces of estimated eigenfunctions, and sequential change-point detection via a CUSUM-like score derived from a nonparametric exponential family in RKHS. Empirical illustrations include a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, motion capture sequences, and a change-point detection example.

This work sits within the DMD/Koopman literature (Schmid; Rowley et al.) and connects to POD and spectral methods. It is closely related to extended DMD (EDMD) (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley), which performs DMD in a feature space induced by user-chosen dictionaries; using kernels yields an implicit infinite dictionary. The robust variant is also in the spirit of exact/optimized DMD, and the projection onto POD is standard in DMD practice. There is also strong overlap with kernel EDMD/KDMD formulations known in the Koopman community.

Quality
- Theory: The RKHS formulation and the kernelization of DMD via QR and projected operators are sound in spirit and consistent with Arnoldi/Ritz theory and prior DMD derivations. Theorem 1 relies on diagonalizability and distinct eigenvalues; the linear algebra manipulations (e.g., equalities like F = Mτ^{-1}KHMτ) are stated informally and would benefit from more precise statements about projections/pseudoinverses. Theorem 2 clarifies when the kernel-PCA subspace recovers Koopman eigenfunctions; the assumptions (φj ∈ Φ and κj ∈ span(U)) are strong and essentially require that data and kernel span the invariant subspace of interest. No error/convergence analysis or noise robustness is provided.

- Algorithms: The robust kernel-PCA projection is sensible and mirrors standard DMD practice. However, computational complexity, numerical stability, and regularization (e.g., Tikhonov, truncated SVD, damping) are not discussed; nor are approximations (Nyström, random features) for scalability.

- Experiments: Illustrations are minimal and largely qualitative. The toy example and Hénon map show plausible eigenvalues. The recognition and change-point detection demos lack quantitative metrics, ablations, or comparisons to strong baselines beyond one kernel change detector. Hyperparameters (kernel type/width, truncation) are not justified or analyzed.

- Some statements are inaccurate or overgeneral (e.g., “for a system evolving on an attractor, Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle” is not generally true for dissipative dynamics with decay).

Clarity
The paper is readable but needs significant polishing:
- Several typos/grammar issues and occasional imprecise notation (ϕ vs φ; ψ used both as function and residual).
- Linear algebra equalities mixing projections and inverses should be clarified.
- Figures are referenced but the textual descriptions are brief; axes/labels are not fully explained.
- References are incomplete (missing years/venues), and some important related works are not fully contrasted (e.g., EDMD with kernels, exact/optimized DMD).

Originality
Using RKHS to perform DMD/Koopman spectral analysis has been explored in EDMD with kernels; the robustification via kernel-PCA/POD and the observation that DMD in feature space is a kernelized Arnoldi method are natural extensions. The contribution here is a clear RKHS derivation with Ritz interpretations and a compact algorithmic presentation. The applications (preimage-based prediction, subspace-angle recognition, and CUSUM-style change detection) are interesting but incremental and not rigorously evaluated.

Significance
A kernelized DMD can be useful to practitioners seeking richer dictionaries than linear monomials, and the RKHS derivation will help bridge communities. However, the current empirical section does not demonstrate clear state-of-the-art performance, robustness, or scalability, and the overlap with EDMD reduces novelty. Without stronger experiments or theoretical guarantees, the impact is moderate.

Pros
- Conceptually clean RKHS formulation of DMD with Ritz/Arnoldi perspective.
- Practical robustification via kernel-PCA/POD is useful.
- Connections to Koopman eigenfunctions (Theorems 1–2) help interpretation.
- Code availability.

Cons
- Limited novelty relative to EDMD/kDMD; relationship could be made explicit and experimentally compared.
- Sparse and qualitative experiments; no systematic evaluation, baselines, or sensitivity analyses.
- Theoretical assumptions are strong; no error bounds, convergence, or noise analysis.
- Clarity/notation/ref formatting issues; one incorrect general statement about eigenvalues on attractors.
- Scalability and kernel/hyperparameter selection not addressed.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Please clarify the exact relationship to EDMD (with kernels) and exact/optimized DMD; under what conditions are your algorithms equivalent?
- Provide numerical stability and complexity discussion; include regularization and scalable approximations.
- Add quantitative experiments: recovery error vs noise/length, prediction RMSE vs baselines, recognition accuracy vs alternatives, change-point ROC vs strong methods.
- Correct/improve linear algebra derivations around projections/pseudoinverses, and revisit the statement about eigenvalues on attractors.
- Discuss kernel and truncation choices, and sensitivity to hyperparameters.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The idea is valuable and relevant, but novelty over existing EDMD/kDMD is limited, the theory is somewhat informal, and the empirical validation is not yet at the level expected for NeurIPS. Strengthening comparisons, experiments, and clarity could make this a solid contribution.
