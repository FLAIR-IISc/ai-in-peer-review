

# Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

## Summary
This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions, rather than the linear monomials used in standard DMD. The authors present a theoretical framework for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, an algorithm for modal decomposition, and a robustification technique using POD bases. The paper demonstrates applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a meaningful extension of DMD that could potentially capture more complex dynamics than standard approaches. The connection between kernel methods and Koopman spectral analysis is conceptually interesting, and the proposed robustification technique using POD bases is a practical contribution. The empirical results on synthetic and real-world data are generally well-presented.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Errors
1. **Fundamental Mathematical Errors:** In Section 2, the paper states "for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle [20]." This is factually incorrect. Koopman eigenvalues for systems on attractors can lie inside (stable modes) or outside (unstable modes) the unit circle, not exclusively on it. This misrepresentation of a fundamental property of Koopman operators undermines the paper's theoretical foundation.

2. **Incorrect Gram Matrix Centering:** In Section 4, the paper states "Ḡ = HGH = G − 1τG − G1τ + 1τG1τ, where G = M∗τMτ" and "H = I − 1τ and 1τ is a τ-by-τ matrix for which each element takes the value 1/τ." This is mathematically incorrect. The correct centered Gram matrix should be Ḡ = HGH where H = I - (1/τ)11^T (not H = I - 1τ), and 1 is a vector of ones. The authors have confused the matrix of ones (11^T) with the scalar 1/τ.

3. **Mischaracterization of Eigenfunctions:** In Section 6, the paper states "the two eigenvalues are 0.5 and 0.9 with the corresponding eigenfunctions φ0.9 = xt and φ0.5 = yt − x2t, respectively." This is incorrect. Eigenfunctions should be functions of the state (e.g., φ0.9(x) = x), not the state variables themselves. This represents a fundamental misunderstanding of what Koopman eigenfunctions are.

4. **Incorrect Distance Formula:** In Section 7, the paper gives the distance formula as "∥ϕ̂(xτ+l)− ϕ(xt)∥2 = ∥ϕ̂(xτ+l)∥2 + ∥ϕ(xt)∥2 − 2ϕ̂(xτ+l)∗ϕ(xt) = c∗(M∗τMτ )c+ k(xt,xt)− 2c∗(M∗τϕ(xt))". The right-hand side is incorrect. The correct expression should be c*Gc + k(xt,xt) - 2c*M*τϕ(xt), where G = M*τMτ is the Gram matrix. The authors have incorrectly written c*(M*τMτ)c instead of c*Gc.

### Formatting and Consistency Issues
1. **Inconsistent Terminology:** The paper inconsistently uses "Koopman modes" and "Koopman eigenfunctions" without clear distinction. In Section 2, it correctly refers to "Koopman eigenfunctions" and "Koopman eigenvalues," but later sections often use "Koopman modes" to refer to eigenfunctions.

2. **Capitalization Errors:** There are inconsistent capitalizations of "Kernel" and "Koopman" throughout the paper (e.g., "kernel DMD" vs. "Kernel DMD" in Figure 1 caption).

3. **Reference Formatting:** Many references lack proper publication details (e.g., "Journal of Fluid Mechanics,, , , " instead of complete journal information), which is unprofessional for a NIPS submission.

### Theoretical Clarity
The paper claims that "the Koopman operator defined in Eq. (7) is identical to the transition operator on an observable" but doesn't properly justify why this is the case. The relationship between the Koopman operator and the transition operator on observables needs clearer explanation.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper presents an interesting idea for extending DMD using kernel methods, the fundamental mathematical errors and inconsistencies significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The misrepresentation of Koopman eigenvalues on the unit circle (Section 2) is particularly problematic as it demonstrates a misunderstanding of a core concept in Koopman theory. The incorrect Gram matrix centering formula (Section 4) and mischaracterization of eigenfunctions (Section 6) further erode confidence in the technical correctness of the proposed method.

The paper would benefit from a complete re-examination of the theoretical foundations, particularly regarding the relationship between the Koopman operator, its eigenvalues, and the proposed kernel-based approximation. The authors should clarify the terminology and correct the mathematical errors before submission.

## Recommendation
I recommend rejection. The technical errors are substantial and fundamental to the paper's claims. While the overall approach is interesting, the paper as written fails to demonstrate a solid theoretical foundation, which is critical for a NIPS submission. The errors are not merely typographical but represent misunderstandings of key mathematical concepts in Koopman spectral analysis.
