- Summary: Proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using Krylov subspaces, feature-space QR projection, eigen-extraction, and a kernel-PCA “robustified” truncation with demo applications (prediction, recognition, change detection).  
- Relation to prior work: Method closely mirrors EDMD/KDMD; robustified variant is essentially KDMD with centering/truncation/regularization.  
- Relation to prior work (cont.): Substantial existing RKHS operator literature (kernel transfer operators, VAMP/TICA) is not clearly differentiated from the proposed method.  
- Strength: Clear motivation that kernelizing DMD yields richer nonlinear features than linear observables.  
- Strength: Emphasizes a Krylov/Arnoldi perspective in feature space linking DMD and subspace-ID views.  
- Strength: Kernel-PCA-based truncation addresses numerical conditioning consistent with DMD best practices.  
- Strength: Applications section suggests interesting downstream uses.  
- Weakness — novelty: Core algorithm reproduces KDMD/EDMD; differences are not made precise, limiting originality.  
- Weakness — Theorem 1: Claims that F is a companion matrix and related linear-algebra steps are not rigorous; dimensions/invariance conditions are ambiguous.  
- Weakness — Theorem 2: Assumes true Koopman eigenfunctions lie in RKHS and top kernel PCs, restating Galerkin projection without convergence/error bounds.  
- Weakness — conceptual inaccuracies: Incorrect claim that Koopman eigenvalues always lie on the unit circle; centering effects on Koopman spectra are not discussed.  
- Weakness — experiments: Limited, qualitative, no baselines, no noise/kernel sensitivity/scalability analysis; Hénon map choice and evaluation are narrow.  
- Weakness — clarity/notation: Mixing scalar eigenfunctions and vector feature maps, reused/ambiguous notation, omitted conditions, and invalid matrix inversions.  
- Quality: High-level idea sound but key derivations lack rigor and empirical validation is insufficient.  
- Clarity: Paper is readable but needs precise notation, missing technical conditions, and corrected proofs using standard Arnoldi/DMD relations.  
- Originality: Limited — largely recapitulates KDMD/EDMD with kernel-PCA truncation and no clear novel theory/algorithms.  
- Significance: Potentially useful in practice but currently incremental without stronger theory or experiments.  
- Suggestions: Precisely position relative to EDMD/KDMD, correct theoretical claims (use Arnoldi/Hessenberg form), analyze centering effects, add quantitative baselines/noise/sensitivity/scalability experiments, clarify preimage method, and fix notation/inaccuracies.  
- Pros: Motivated kernelization, clear link to subspace projections, practical truncation, interesting application ideas.  
- Cons: Substantial overlap with KDMD/EDMD, theoretical errors/overclaims, sparse qualitative experiments, conceptual and notational issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject; could become a solid contribution with corrected theory and stronger empirical comparisons.
