

# Peer Review: "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

## Summary
This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis. The authors develop a method that approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear kernel-based basis functions rather than the linear monomials used in standard DMD. The core contribution is a modal decomposition algorithm that operates in RKHS, with theoretical justification through a theorem establishing the connection to Koopman eigenvalues. The authors further propose a robust implementation by projecting data onto POD directions to address potential numerical instability. The paper demonstrates applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The work builds upon Koopman operator theory and DMD (previously used in fluid dynamics and engineering), but extends it into the kernel learning framework. It relates to Williams et al.'s extended DMD [35] which also uses pre-determined basis functions, though the kernel-based approach here represents a different theoretical foundation.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Soundness**: The paper presents a mathematically rigorous extension of DMD using RKHS. Theorems 1 and 2 establish clear theoretical connections between the proposed method and Koopman spectral analysis, which is well-motivated and properly proven.

2. **Novelty of Approach**: The use of reproducing kernels to capture nonlinear dynamics represents a meaningful extension of traditional DMD, which is limited to linear approximations. This addresses a key limitation of standard DMD that the authors correctly identify.

3. **Empirical Validation**: The paper demonstrates the method on both synthetic systems (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and real-world applications (locomotion data, change-point detection), showing reasonable results that align with theoretical expectations.

4. **Practical Implementation**: The robustification approach using POD bases (Section 4) addresses a known practical limitation of DMD, making the method more applicable to real data.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison with Related Work**: While the paper mentions Williams et al.'s extended DMD [35], it doesn't provide a thorough comparison of the kernel DMD approach with this and other kernel-based methods. A detailed comparison of accuracy, computational complexity, and applicability would strengthen the contribution.

2. **Computational Complexity**: The paper doesn't analyze the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly the cost of kernel operations and the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in RKHS. For large datasets, this could be prohibitive, and the paper doesn't address this limitation.

3. **Lack of Quantitative Comparison**: The empirical results show qualitative agreement with theoretical expectations (e.g., Fig. 1), but lack quantitative comparisons with existing methods like standard DMD, extended DMD, or other kernel-based approaches. Without such comparisons, it's difficult to assess the practical advantages of the proposed method.

4. **Overstated Significance**: The paper claims the method "could be considered as a feature extraction procedure for a nonlinear dynamical system" but doesn't clearly articulate how this differs from or improves upon existing feature extraction methods for dynamical systems.

## Arguments for Acceptance
- The theoretical contribution is novel and well-justified.
- The method addresses a clear limitation of standard DMD.
- The robustification approach is practical and well-motivated.
- The applications to prediction, change detection, and dynamics recognition are relevant and well-demonstrated.

## Arguments against Acceptance
- The paper lacks quantitative comparisons with existing methods that would establish the practical advantages of the proposed approach.
- The computational complexity is not analyzed, which is a significant concern for real-world applications.
- The connection to the broader ML community is not sufficiently established.
- The significance of the approach for machine learning practitioners is not clearly articulated.

## Overall Assessment

This is a technically sound paper that makes a theoretically interesting contribution to the Koopman spectral analysis literature. The kernel-based extension of DMD addresses a genuine limitation of standard DMD, and the robustification approach is well-motivated. However, the paper falls short in demonstrating the practical advantages of the proposed method over existing approaches through quantitative comparisons. The significance for the broader machine learning community is not sufficiently established, and the computational considerations are underdeveloped.

While the theoretical contribution is solid, the lack of comparative experiments and analysis of computational complexity limits the paper's impact. The paper would be strengthened by including a detailed comparison with existing methods (including extended DMD), quantitative performance metrics, and a more thorough discussion of computational requirements.

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak acceptance**. The paper presents a theoretically sound and novel extension of DMD that could be valuable for specific applications in dynamics analysis. However, it would benefit significantly from additional experiments comparing the proposed method with existing approaches, quantitative performance metrics, and a clearer articulation of the method's advantages for machine learning practitioners. The current version would be substantially improved with these additions.

The paper would be more suitable for a specialized journal or conference in fluid dynamics or applied mathematics, though it does contain elements of interest to the NIPS community. With the suggested revisions, it could make a stronger contribution to the NIPS audience.
