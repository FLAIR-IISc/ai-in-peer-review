Summary
The paper proposes a kernelized formulation of Koopman spectral analysis via Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD). It defines the Koopman operator acting on features in an RKHS, constructs a Krylov subspace in feature space, and derives an algorithm that computes Ritz values/vectors using only Gram matrices. A “robustified” variant projects onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to mitigate ill-conditioning. The paper proves two theorems linking these empirical Ritz objects to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under finite-data assumptions. It then sketches three applications using the extracted modes: short-term prediction via a preimage step, embedding/recognition of dynamical patterns via kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection via a CUSUM-like statistic, and shows small empirical examples (toy system, Hénon map, CMU mocap, Lorenz map).

Relation to prior work
DMD and its variants (exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting) are well-known. Extended DMD (eDMD) approximates Koopman operators with a chosen dictionary and is particularly relevant here. This paper’s RKHS formulation removes the need to select explicit dictionaries, analogous to a kernelized eDMD/KDMD. The POD projection echoes robust DMD practice. The paper cites eDMD [Williams et al.] and kernel PCA, and briefly mentions subspace identification connections.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity and unification: Casting DMD as a Krylov method in RKHS is clean and natural. The kernel view makes the “basis selection” implicit and expressive.
- Fully kernelized algorithm: All steps rely on Gram matrices; a robust, practically usable recipe is provided, including a truncation mechanism via kernel PCA/POD.
- Theoretical linkage: Theorems 1 and 2 connect the empirical Ritz values/vectors to Koopman spectra and make the analogy with standard DMD precise in the RKHS.
- Practicality: The approach can be trained from pairs (x, f(x)) and is compatible with common kernels. The applications show breadth (prediction, recognition, change detection), hinting at potential impact across areas where Koopman methods are used.
- Code availability.

Weaknesses
- Originality/positioning: The core idea is very close to eDMD with a kernel (KDMD). While the Krylov/QR perspective is elegant, the paper does not clearly differentiate its algorithm from kernelized eDMD in terms of what is actually new (mathematically or computationally). Several subsequent works already present KDMD and its properties; the paper needs to explicitly contrast to them (algorithmic steps, complexity, stability, assumptions).
- Theory assumptions and scope: The proofs hinge on strong finite-dimensional assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, φj ∈ Φ, κj in span(U), ψ=0 cases). No consistency/convergence results as data grows, nor robustness to noise/stochasticity are provided (beyond a brief remark). The claims about “full recovery” when ψ=0 are not actionable unless conditions under which ψ→0 are given.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: Mostly qualitative plots on small systems. No quantitative comparisons to exact/optimized DMD, eDMD/KDMD, or ablations (effect of kernel choice, width, truncation level). The recognition and change-detection examples are illustrative but lack baselines, metrics, and statistical rigor.
- Preimage and change-point components feel ad hoc: The preimage reconstruction is based on an MDS-like local method without evaluation against standard preimage solvers or sensitivity analysis. The change-point method defines a nonparametric exponential family density with inner products of φ∘f and φ and then simplifies the CUSUM statistic by dropping terms; both modeling choices and approximations need justification and more empirical support.
- Clarity and presentation: Notation is occasionally imprecise (e.g., adjoints in RKHS, centering matrix 1τ), and some statements could be tightened (e.g., “fully recovered” claim). Several references are incomplete or poorly formatted. More details on computational complexity and scalability (in τ and sample size) would help.

Quality
Technically sound at a high level, but the main theorems rely on restrictive assumptions and do not address consistency/noise. The robustification step is standard but sensible. Applications are promising yet under-evaluated. Overall, the work is a complete piece with clear algorithms but lacks rigorous validation and careful comparison.

Clarity
Generally readable and well-organized. The derivations are sketched clearly, but precise assumptions and linear-algebra in Hilbert spaces should be stated more carefully. Figures are informative; however, more quantitative tables/metrics would improve clarity of empirical claims. Reference formatting needs attention.

Originality
Moderate. The kernelization of Koopman/DMD is a known direction (eDMD/KDMD). The paper’s novelty seems to be: (i) explicit Krylov/QR viewpoint in RKHS with a Ritz-theoretic derivation; (ii) robustification via kernel-POD within this framework; and (iii) suite of applications. To justify NeurIPS acceptance, the authors should sharpen how this differs from and improves upon kernel eDMD/KDMD.

Significance
Potentially significant if properly positioned and validated, as Koopman/operator-theoretic methods are influential across ML and physics. The presented applications indicate breadth. However, the current empirical and comparative gaps limit the demonstrable impact.

Pros
- Clean RKHS reformulation of DMD with practical kernelized algorithm.
- Sensible robustification via kernel-POD.
- Theoretical connection to Koopman spectra is articulated.
- Broad, appealing application scenarios; code available.

Cons
- Overlap with existing eDMD/KDMD; insufficiently differentiated.
- Limited quantitative experiments and lack of baselines/metrics.
- Strong assumptions; no consistency/noise robustness analysis.
- Preimage and change-point parts feel heuristic and under-validated.
- Presentation/notation/references need polishing.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a detailed comparison to eDMD/KDMD (algorithmic differences, stability, complexity) and to exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting DMD. Include quantitative benchmarks on eigenvalue/mode accuracy and predictive performance.
- Analyze sensitivity to kernel choice and truncation; discuss model selection.
- Add theoretical results on convergence/consistency under increasing data and in the presence of noise; or at least cite pertinent results and clarify assumptions.
- Strengthen the preimage and change-point sections with baselines, metrics, and ablations.
- Tighten notation and proofs; fix references.

Recommendation
Weak reject. The paper is solid and broadly relevant, with a clean kernelized DMD formulation, but the novelty relative to prior eDMD/KDMD is not sufficiently established, and the empirical validation is too limited. With stronger positioning and quantitative evaluation, this could become a compelling contribution. Confidence: medium.
