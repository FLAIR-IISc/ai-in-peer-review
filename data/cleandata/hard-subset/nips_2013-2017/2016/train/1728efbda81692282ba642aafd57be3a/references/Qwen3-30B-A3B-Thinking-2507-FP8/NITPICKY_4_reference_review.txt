

# Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

## Summary
This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions, rather than the linear monomials used in standard DMD. The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm based on Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in RKHS and provide a robust version using kernel principal component analysis (POD). The paper demonstrates applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using synthetic and real-world data.

## Strengths
1. The paper addresses an interesting extension of DMD with theoretical justification.
2. The proposed method has clear potential applications in nonlinear dynamics analysis.
3. The empirical results on toy systems and Hénon map show reasonable performance.
4. The connection to related work (e.g., extended DMD) is appropriately discussed.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Errors
The paper contains a fundamental technical error in Section 2: "Note that, for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle [20]." This is factually incorrect. Koopman eigenvalues for systems on attractors can have magnitudes less than 1 (decaying modes), equal to 1 (neutral modes), or greater than 1 (growing modes). The unit circle is only relevant for purely periodic systems, not general attractors. This error significantly undermines the technical credibility of the paper.

### Formatting and Notation Issues
1. Equation (4): "g(x) = ∑∞ j=1φj(x)uj" should be "g(x) = ∑_{j=1}^∞ φ_j(x)u_j" for proper summation notation.
2. Equation (5): "λ l j" should be "λ_j^l" for correct exponent notation.
3. Equation (7): "KHϕ(x) = ϕ ◦ f(x)" is ambiguous; it should be "KHϕ(x) = (ϕ ∘ f)(x)" or "KHϕ(x) = ϕ(f(x))".
4. Theorem 1 proof contains inconsistent notation: "F := R−1PτR" should be "F = R^{-1}P_τR".
5. Section 4 contains multiple inconsistent notations: "S̄−1/2" should be "S̄^{-1/2}", "φ̄j" should be "φ̄_j", and "MτHB̄S̄−1/2bj" should be "M_τ H B̄ S̄^{-1/2} b_j".

### Methodological Concerns
1. The paper lacks justification for kernel choices (polynomial degree 3, Gaussian width 1) in Section 6. Why these specific parameters were chosen isn't explained.
2. The statement "our algorithm gives an approximation with a set of nonlinear basis functions due to the expressiveness of kernel functions" is vague and doesn't clearly explain how the kernel-based approach differs from standard DMD in terms of basis function expressiveness.
3. The reference [29] cited in Section 3 for the Gram-Schmidt QR decomposition isn't present in the provided reference list, suggesting an incomplete reference section.

### Reference and Formatting Issues
1. The reference list contains numerous formatting inconsistencies (e.g., "Journal of Fluid Mechanics,, , ,").
2. Several references are incomplete (e.g., "Berkooz. Turbulence, Coherent Structures, Dynamical Systems and Symmetry, P. Holmes, J.L. Lumley, , ,").
3. The paper incorrectly states "the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle" without providing the correct theoretical basis for the claim.

## Recommendation

I recommend rejection of this paper. While the core idea of extending DMD with reproducing kernels is potentially valuable, the paper contains a fundamental technical error that demonstrates a lack of understanding of the Koopman operator theory. The numerous formatting inconsistencies, inconsistent notation, and incomplete references further indicate a lack of careful proofreading and attention to detail. The paper's technical shortcomings outweigh its potential contributions.

The most serious issue is the incorrect statement about Koopman eigenvalues on attractors, which suggests the authors may not have fully grasped the theoretical foundations of the Koopman operator. This error would be immediately apparent to experts in the field and significantly undermines the paper's credibility.

While the paper has some empirical results that appear reasonable, the theoretical foundation is too shaky to warrant acceptance. The authors would need to substantially revise the paper to correct these issues, particularly the fundamental error in Section 2, before it could be considered for publication.
