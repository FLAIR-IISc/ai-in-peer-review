- Paper proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS using kernels to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions and presents an RKHS-based modal decomposition algorithm with POD-based robustification.

- Theoretical novelty: provides rigorous proofs (Theorems 1–2) extending DMD to nonlinear kernel bases and grounding the approach in Koopman theory.

- Practical robustification: addresses numerical instability in DMD by using POD bases, improving real-world applicability.

- Demonstrated utility: shows applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and locomotion data.

- Strong connection to Koopman operator theory and clear, well-structured presentation.

- Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art kernel and deep learning Koopman methods, weakening positioning in the literature.

- No computational complexity or scalability analysis for Gram matrix computation and eigendecompositions.

- Empirical evaluation is mostly on synthetic and simple real-world data; more challenging real-world tests are needed.

- Lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence or approximation error bounds for the proposed method.

- Section 4 (POD-based robustification) is somewhat dense and could be clarified, especially how POD relates to final eigenvectors.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions based on novelty, theoretical foundation, robustness fix, and demonstrated utility.

- Suggested improvements: add a comparison table with recent methods, include computational complexity discussion, expand challenging real-world experiments, clarify Section 4, and add (even partial) error/convergence guarantees.
