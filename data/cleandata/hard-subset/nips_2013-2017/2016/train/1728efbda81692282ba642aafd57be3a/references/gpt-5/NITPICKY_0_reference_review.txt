Summary
The paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) by (i) constructing a Krylov subspace of feature-mapped observations, (ii) projecting the Koopman operator onto that subspace via a Gram–Schmidt QR in feature space, and (iii) computing Ritz values/vectors as in DMD. To address numerical instability, the authors “robustify” via projecting onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions before eigendecomposition. They present small demonstrations (toy system and Hénon map) and sketch applications: short-term prediction via preimage reconstruction, recognition via subspace distances (kernel principal angles), and change-point detection via a CUSUM-style statistic using kernel builds.

Relation to prior work
Conceptually, this is the kernelized extension of DMD/EDMD: approximate the Koopman operator by projecting onto a finite dictionary and eigendecompose. This has been studied extensively as EDMD and kernel DMD (KDMD): Williams et al., J. Nonlinear Science, 2015; also kernel transfer operators/Koopman analysis (e.g., Klus, Nüske, Schütte, 2016–2018), and convergence analyses (Korda & Mezić, 2018). The robustification via POD/SVD is also standard in exact DMD and EDMD/KDMD. The applications (preimage-based prediction, dynamics recognition via principal angles, change detection) are plausible but not new in spirit.

Strengths
- Presents an RKHS/DMD viewpoint that could be pedagogically useful for some readers.
- The robustification via kernel PCA is sensible and aligns with best practice in DMD/EDMD.
- The paper attempts to connect learned eigenpairs to Koopman eigenfunctions and to outline practical applications.
- Code availability is noted.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Technical correctness (Quality)
- Theorem 1 and its proof have serious issues:
  - The matrix F := R^{-1}Q* K_H Q R is not a “companion matrix” in general; invoking Vandermonde diagonalization T with T_{ij} = λ_i^{j−1} is unjustified. Distinct eigenvalues do not imply companion form.
  - The identity M_τ F = K_H M_τ is generally false; what holds is M_τ F = Proj_{span(M_τ)}(K_H M_τ). The proof silently replaces K_H by its projection P_τ = Q* K_H Q and treats M_τ as invertible.
  - Using M_τ^{-1} is invalid since M_τ is rectangular; equalities relying on this are incorrect.
  - As a consequence, Eq. (9b) and the interpretation of Ritz vectors need to be restated with an explicit projection.
- Theorem 2 also mixes exact operator relations with empirical, centered Gram matrices. It assumes Ḡ is invertible but earlier truncates its spectrum; it uses H Ḡ^{-1} H with no conditions. The final expansion ϕ(x) = ∑ φ_j(x) φ̄_j requires projection onto span(U) and appropriate bi-orthogonality assumptions; as written it is too strong.
- Claim “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle [20]” is incorrect in general; for dissipative systems, eigenvalues typically lie inside the unit disk (discrete time) unless the dynamics are measure-preserving.
- Kernel centering: ϕ_c should be the mean (1/τ)∑ ϕ(x_j), but the paper defines ϕ_c = ∑ ϕ(x_j). This inconsistency propagates into the kernel-PCA step.
- Several steps (Gram–Schmidt in RKHS, computing R and Q* M_+ via Gram matrices) are asserted but not derived; numerical stability and complexity are not analyzed. KDMD typically forms A and G matrices and solves a (generalized) eigenproblem; that connection should be made explicit.

Clarity and organization
- The manuscript is hard to follow in places, with notational overloading (g vs ϕ), missing assumptions, and imprecise operator statements (projection vs exact action).
- Figures are minimally described; Fig. 2 is referenced but absent; Fig. 1 captions are cryptic.
- Numerous typos and grammatical errors (e.g., “to robustify it by projecting data…”, “this assumption should perform the calculation in practice”) hinder readability.
- References are incomplete (missing years, venues, authors truncated) and some key related work is missing (KDMD/EDMD’s standard formulations; kernel transfer operators).

Originality
- The main idea—Koopman spectral analysis via kernels and POD/SVD projection—is known (EDMD/KDMD). The manuscript does not clearly differentiate from Williams et al. (2015) or later kernel operator work (e.g., Klus et al., J. Nonlinear Sci. 2018; Klus et al., Physica D 2018). The “QR in RKHS” presentation is an alternative derivation but not a substantive advance.

Significance
- The experimental section is too limited to establish impact:
  - No quantitative comparisons to EDMD/KDMD/exact DMD on standard benchmarks; no error or robustness analyses; no ablation of truncation; no sensitivity to kernel choice.
  - The Hénon results are qualitative and not mapped to ground-truth linearizations at equilibria.
  - Applications are sketched without rigorous evaluation (no predictive RMSE, classification accuracy, or change-point detection metrics).
- Without clear novelty or strong empirical evidence, the likely impact is low.

Pros
- Clear motivation for kernelizing DMD to capture nonlinear observables.
- Providing code (if functional) can help reproducibility.
- The applications section may inspire practitioners.

Cons
- Substantial overlap with prior KDMD/EDMD literature; novelty not established.
- Technical inaccuracies in main theorems and proofs.
- Sparse, mostly qualitative experiments; missing baselines and metrics.
- Numerous presentation and referencing issues.

Suggestions for improvement
- Precisely position the contribution relative to EDMD and KDMD; explain what is new (if anything) beyond known kernel formulations and POD-based robustness.
- Correct Theorems 1–2: explicitly use projected operators; avoid unjustified companion-matrix/Vandermonde arguments; state and prove results under appropriate invariance/projection assumptions; align with standard finite-dimensional projected Koopman operator derivations.
- Provide a clear kernel implementation: derive A and G from Gram matrices, discuss truncation, regularization (e.g., Tikhonov), and complexity.
- Add thorough experiments with quantitative metrics and baselines (EDMD, KDMD, exact DMD, optimized DMD), across multiple systems; report sensitivity to kernel and truncation.
- Expand/verify application sections with rigorous evaluation (preimage prediction error, recognition accuracy using kernel principal angles, change-point detection ROC/delay).
- Fix references (complete bibliographic info) and numerous typos; improve figure quality and captions.

Overall recommendation: Reject (the contribution appears incremental to established KDMD/EDMD, contains technical inaccuracies, and lacks sufficient empirical validation). Confidence: High.
