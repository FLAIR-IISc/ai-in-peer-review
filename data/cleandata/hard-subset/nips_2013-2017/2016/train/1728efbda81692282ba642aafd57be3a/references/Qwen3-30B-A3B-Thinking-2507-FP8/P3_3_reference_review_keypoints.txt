- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions via orthogonal Krylov bases and eigendecomposition, with a POD-based robust variant.  
- Theoretical derivations are solid and well-structured (Theorems 1 and 2), clarifying the DMD–Koopman connection.  
- POD robustification addresses known numerical instability issues in DMD.  
- Demonstrated on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, showing potential utility.  
- Contribution appears incremental and very similar to Extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015), lacking clear distinctions.  
- Crucially omits prior kernel-based Koopman work by Korda & Mezić (2016), undermining novelty claims.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited, uses toy/Hénon examples that are not challenging, and lacks rigorous baselines (standard DMD, Extended DMD, other nonlinear methods).  
- Change-point detection comparison is unclear and lacks explanation of competing methods.  
- No analysis of computational complexity or scalability; unclear how the kernel approach scales with data versus standard DMD.  
- Kernel choices (polynomial, Gaussian) are not justified and sensitivity to kernel/parameters is not explored.  
- The RKHS extension is a straightforward application of existing DMD principles and does not offer major new Koopman-theoretic insights.  
- Recommended revisions: cite and distinguish from Korda & Mezić (2016) and Extended DMD, add rigorous comparisons, analyze complexity/scalability, and justify/explore kernel selection.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject — technically sound but insufficiently novel and empirically validated for NIPS; better suited to a specialized venue.
