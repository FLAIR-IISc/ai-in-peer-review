- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel-based functions.  
- Provides theoretical foundations with two theorems linking the method to Koopman operator theory.  
- Introduces a POD-based projection to address numerical instability in implementation.  
- Demonstrates the approach on synthetic systems (toy system, Hénon map) and applications: prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Strength: Solid mathematical derivations and clear connection to Koopman theory (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Strength: Clear problem statement that contextualizes DMD and Koopman operator literature.  
- Strength: Practical robustification via kernel-POD addressing numerical instability.  
- Strength: Relevant application examples showing potential utility.  
- Weakness: Novelty limited—method not clearly differentiated from Extended DMD and other kernel-based DMD work.  
- Weakness: Paper fails to cite or properly compare to related kernel/GP dynamical models (e.g., Wang et al. 2016, Kernel DMD by Kutz et al.).  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited and overly tailored to kernels chosen (polynomial, Gaussian).  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Extended DMD) in prediction and other tasks.  
- Weakness: Change-point and classification experiments lack quantitative metrics (F1, AUC, accuracy).  
- Weakness: No ablation studies on kernel choice, kernel parameters, or number of modes.  
- Weakness: Real-world example (locomotion) lacks classification accuracy or practical performance metrics.  
- Weakness: Theoretical gaps—no analysis of approximation error, convergence, or dependence on data/sample size.  
- Weakness: POD robustification lacks theoretical justification versus other regularization methods.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues and inconsistent terminology (e.g., “kernel DMD” vs. full method name) risk confusion.  
- Weakness: Notational/definition confusion (e.g., definition and use of M+).  
- Recommendation: Clearly differentiate the approach from Extended DMD and other kernel-DMD variants with a comparative table.  
- Recommendation: Expand experiments to include comparisons with state-of-the-art, quantitative metrics, and ablation studies on kernels and parameters.  
- Recommendation: Provide theoretical analysis of approximation error, convergence, and justify POD robustification.  
- Recommendation: Use consistent terminology and clarify connections to Extended DMD and related work.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound but incremental; novelty unclear and empirical validation insufficient for top-tier acceptance.  
- Recommendation outcome: Reject—incremental contribution, insufficient comparison and empirical evidence.  
- Pro acceptance: Strong theory, practical POD robustification, meaningful applications, well-written.  
- Con acceptance: Limited novelty vs. prior work, weak experiments, theoretical gaps, inconsistent terminology, and missing quantitative results.
