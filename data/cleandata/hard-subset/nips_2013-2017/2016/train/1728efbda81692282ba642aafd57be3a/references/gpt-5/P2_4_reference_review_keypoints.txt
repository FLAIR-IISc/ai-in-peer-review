- Summary: Proposes a kernelized DMD in RKHS using a Krylov/Gram–Schmidt QR approach and a kernel-PCA-based robust projection to estimate Koopman spectral objects and apply them to prediction, recognition, and change detection.

- Relation to prior work: Closely related to EDMD/KDMD and kernel transfer-operator methods; method is essentially EDMD with an implicit kernel dictionary and data-driven basis.

- Strength: Conceptual clarity — clean RKHS Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint and implementable Gram-matrix algorithm.

- Strength: Robustification via kernel PCA mirrors POD/exact DMD practice and yields a useful centered-Gram formulation.

- Strength: Theoretical statements adapt standard DMD results to feature space and clarify assumptions for recovering Koopman eigenfunctions.

- Strength: Broadened applications — includes preimage-based prediction, subspace-based dynamic recognition, and CUSUM-style change detection.

- Weakness: Originality/positioning — insufficient differentiation from EDMD/KDMD and kernel transfer-operator literature, risking an incremental impression.

- Weakness: Theoretical gaps — lacks approximation/error analysis, consistency results, and clear regularization conditions for pseudoinverses.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation — limited, mostly qualitative, no systematic baselines (exact DMD/EDMD/optimized DMD/KDMD) or quantitative metrics.

- Weakness: Practical aspects — scalability (O(τ^2)/O(τ^3)), hyperparameter selection, and the heuristic preimage step are under-discussed and potentially brittle.

- Quality: Core algorithm is technically sound as a kernelization of DMD/POD-DMD, but some derivations need more precision on projections, centering, and regularization.

- Clarity: Generally readable but has typos, skipped proof steps, and insufficient implementation details for reproducibility (kernel choices, truncation ranks, normalization).

- Originality: Overlaps substantially with existing kernelized EDMD/KDMD ideas; novelty mainly in the Krylov/QR perspective and packaged application recipes.

- Significance: Potentially useful if better positioned and empirically validated; current impact moderate due to overlap and limited experiments.

- Suggestions: Explicitly contrast with EDMD/KDMD and kernel transfer-operator work and add missing citations.

- Suggestions: Provide quantitative benchmarks against strong baselines, sensitivity analyses, and runtime/memory comparisons.

- Suggestions: Include regularized formulations (e.g., Tikhonov), hyperparameter guidance, and scalability remedies (online updates, Nyström/randomized methods).

- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject — valuable core idea but needs clearer positioning, stronger empirical validation, regularization, and reproducibility details.
