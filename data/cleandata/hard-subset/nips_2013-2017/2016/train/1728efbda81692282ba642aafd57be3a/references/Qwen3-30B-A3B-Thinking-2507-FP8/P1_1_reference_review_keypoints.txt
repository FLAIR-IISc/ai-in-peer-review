- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions via nonlinear kernel features.  
- Performs spectral analysis of the Koopman operator on the RKHS feature map and derives a modal decomposition algorithm via Krylov bases and projected eigendecomposition.  
- Introduces a robustified implementation using POD to mitigate ill-conditioning in traditional DMD implementations.  
- Builds on Koopman operator, DMD, and kernel methods literature and relates to extended DMD and prior kernel-based dynamical systems work.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong with clear derivations and proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) linking the method to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Practical implementation addresses ill-conditioning using POD, a known and sensible fix in the DMD literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, including toy nonlinear systems, the Hénon map, and applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Demonstrates clear connections between theory and practical applications, showing utility of extracted features.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with related kernel-based dynamical systems methods (e.g., Kawahara et al., Williams et al.).  
- Missing computational complexity and scalability analysis for kernel computations and τ×τ eigendecompositions on large datasets.  
- Robustness to noisy data is not thoroughly evaluated; experiments use clean data only.  
- The claim that the Koopman operator in Eq. (7) is identical to the transition operator on an observable is insufficiently justified for the kernel feature map.  
- Paper is well-organized with consistent mathematical notation and clear visualizations.  
- Some sections (notably Section 3) are dense with math and lack intuitive explanations for readers unfamiliar with RKHS/Koopman theory.  
- The POD-based robustification algorithm is technical and could use a more intuitive explanation for why it resolves ill-conditioning.  
- Practical guidance on selecting kernel parameters (degree, Gaussian width) and their effect on results is lacking.  
- Contribution is meaningful by extending DMD to RKHS, enabling richer nonlinear basis functions beyond linear monomials.  
- Originality is limited by prior kernel-based approaches and extended DMD, though the specific RKHS-Koopman integration and POD robustification are novel.  
- Addresses an important limitation of DMD and shows relevant practical applications, potentially inspiring further research.  
- Fails to convincingly quantify improvement over existing methods or demonstrate necessity of RKHS in presented applications.  
- Does not discuss scenarios where kernel DMD may be inappropriate or simpler methods would suffice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound extension, correct derivations, promising empirical results, and relevant applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to related work, missing scalability and noise-robustness analysis, unclear quantitative advantage over baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution but weakened by empirical/practical gaps, making it a borderline candidate for acceptance.  
- Specific recommendations: add comparisons with kernel methods, include complexity/scalability and runtime analysis, evaluate noise robustness, clarify Koopman–kernel connection, provide quantitative baseline comparisons, and discuss method limitations.
