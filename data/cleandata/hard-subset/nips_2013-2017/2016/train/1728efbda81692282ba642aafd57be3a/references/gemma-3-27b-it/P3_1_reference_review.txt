## NIPS Review: Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents an interesting extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) leveraging reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to improve the approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions. The core idea is sound, building logically upon existing work in DMD and spectral analysis. However, the presentation feels somewhat disjointed, and the empirical validation, while present, isn’t entirely convincing. While the method *could* be significant, the current presentation doesn’t fully demonstrate that potential. I lean towards **weak accept** with a strong recommendation for major revisions focusing on clarity, more rigorous empirical evaluation, and stronger connections to existing literature on kernel-based DMD variants.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper addresses the challenge of applying Koopman operator theory to nonlinear dynamical systems. It extends DMD by utilizing RKHS to represent functions, allowing for a more flexible and potentially accurate approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions compared to the traditional monomial basis functions used in DMD. The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of spectral methods, POD, and DMD, acknowledging the limitations of simpler approaches. They highlight the potential for feature extraction and subsequent applications like prediction and change-point detection.

The related work section is somewhat brief. While mentioning key papers in DMD and spectral analysis, it lacks a thorough discussion of existing kernel-based extensions of DMD. Specifically, the paper doesn't deeply engage with work like Extended DMD (EDMD) [Williams et al., 2015], which also utilizes pre-determined basis functions. A clearer differentiation of their approach from EDMD, particularly regarding the automatic construction of basis functions through RKHS, is crucial.  Similarly, the connection to Kernel PCA and its application to DMD (as briefly mentioned in section 4) needs to be expanded, providing a more complete landscape of related techniques.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The mathematical derivation of the RKHS-DMD algorithm is presented clearly and logically, building upon established Koopman operator theory. The theorem proving the equivalence to standard DMD under certain conditions is a valuable contribution.
* **Potential for Improved Accuracy:** The use of RKHS basis functions *should* allow for more accurate approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions, especially for systems where linear monomials are insufficient.  This is a compelling motivation for the work.
* **Robustification Technique:** The inclusion of the POD projection step (Section 4) to address potential ill-conditioning is a practical and important consideration, demonstrating awareness of implementation challenges.
* **Application Exploration:** The exploration of downstream applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamic recognition) showcases the versatility of the extracted features.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Organization:** The paper suffers from occasional disjointedness. The flow between sections isn't always smooth. For instance, the motivation for using POD feels somewhat tacked on rather than seamlessly integrated into the core algorithm.
* **Empirical Validation:** The empirical examples (toy system and Henon map) are limited. While the toy system demonstrates the method’s ability to recover known eigenvalues, the Henon map results are less conclusive (“seem to be approximately estimated”). A more comprehensive set of experiments with varying system complexities and noise levels is necessary.
* **Comparative Analysis:**  The paper lacks a rigorous comparison to existing DMD variants (especially EDMD and kernelized DMD approaches).  Demonstrating a clear advantage in terms of accuracy, computational efficiency, or performance on downstream tasks is essential.
* **Limited Discussion of Kernel Choice:** The selection of the kernel (polynomial and Gaussian) feels somewhat arbitrary. A discussion of the impact of kernel parameters (e.g., degree for polynomial kernel, bandwidth for Gaussian kernel) and a sensitivity analysis would be valuable.
* **Computational Complexity:**  The computational cost of the RKHS-based approach is not thoroughly discussed. While POD helps, the Gram matrix calculation in high dimensions can be prohibitive. A discussion of scalability and potential approximations is needed.



**4. Significance & Originality:**

The originality of the work lies in the formal integration of RKHS into the DMD framework. While kernel methods are commonly used *with* DMD (e.g., in kernel PCA preprocessing), this paper offers a more theoretically grounded and systematic approach.  The significance, however, is not fully demonstrated. If the method consistently outperforms existing techniques on relevant problems, it could be highly impactful.  Currently, it’s a promising idea that needs stronger validation.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  Theoretically sound extension of DMD with potential for improved accuracy.
*  Addresses a relevant problem in the field of nonlinear dynamical systems.
*  Presents a novel application of RKHS to spectral analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  Limited empirical validation and lack of comparative analysis.
*  Clarity and organization could be improved.
*  The significance of the contribution isn't fully established.
*  Missing discussion of kernel selection and computational complexity.



**Recommendation:** **Weak Accept.**

**Detailed Suggestions for Revision:**

* **Expand Related Work:**  Thoroughly discuss and differentiate from existing kernel-based DMD variants (EDMD, kernel PCA-based DMD).
* **Strengthen Empirical Evaluation:** Perform a more comprehensive evaluation with diverse datasets (including real-world systems), varying noise levels, and different kernel parameters.
* **Comparative Analysis:**  Compare performance against standard DMD, EDMD, and other relevant methods.  Quantify improvements in accuracy, efficiency, and downstream task performance.
* **Kernel Selection & Sensitivity Analysis:** Discuss the impact of kernel choice and perform a sensitivity analysis on kernel parameters.
* **Computational Complexity Analysis:** Discuss the computational cost of the algorithm and explore potential optimizations.
* **Improve Clarity:** Reorganize the paper for smoother flow and enhance clarity of explanations.
