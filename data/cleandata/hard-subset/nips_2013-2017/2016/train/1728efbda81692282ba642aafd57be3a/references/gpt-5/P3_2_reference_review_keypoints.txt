- Paper proposes a kernelized DMD/Koopman spectral analysis with two variants: a kernel Arnoldi/Krylov Gram–Schmidt method and a KPCA-truncated “robust” Galerkin projection via Gram matrices.  
- The robust/KPCA construction and resulting projected operator are essentially equivalent to kernel EDMD/KDMD and existing kernel transfer operator methods.  
- The Arnoldi/Ritz interpretation in feature space is conceptually natural but yields the same projected operator as EDMD when implemented with Gram matrices.  
- Strength: clear operator-theoretic framing that recasts DMD as spectral analysis of a projected Koopman operator and extends to RKHS.  
- Strength: KPCA-based truncation stabilizes computation and aligns with best practices (truncated SVD/regularization).  
- Strength: Theorems transparently relate projected operators and Ritz expansions, offering intuitive algebraic relations.  
- Strength: Paper outlines diverse applications (short-term prediction, dynamics recognition, change detection).  
- Weakness: Novelty limited—robust algorithm overlaps substantially with prior EDMD/KDMD and kernel transfer operator literature and omits key citations.  
- Weakness: Theoretical results are algebraic restatements of Ritz/Galerkin theory with no convergence/consistency analysis or realistic functional-analytic conditions.  
- Weakness: Strong, unverifiable assumptions (e.g., ψ=0, κj ∈ span(U)) and incorrect claims (e.g., Koopman eigenvalues always on the unit circle) undermine theory.  
- Weakness: Gram–Schmidt Arnoldi in RKHS is ill-conditioned; the stable KPCA-based method essentially reduces to known kernel EDMD.  
- Weakness: Centering (H) mixes KPCA with Koopman estimation in a nonstandard way and lacks justification.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited, qualitative, lack quantitative metrics or comparisons to DMD/EDMD/KDMD/optimized DMD, and omit sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: Preimage-based forecasting is ad hoc, unevaluated, and not compared to simpler baselines or learned inverse maps.  
- Weakness: Change-point detection derivation is heuristic, drops key likelihood terms without justification; better approaches exist using residuals/likelihoods.  
- Weakness: Scalability issues—method scales cubically in snapshot count with no discussion of streaming, Nyström, or random-feature approximations.  
- Quality: Linear-algebraic parts are competent but theoretical claims are often well-known or rest on strong assumptions; empirical support is weak.  
- Clarity: Generally readable but marred by notational collisions, typos, confusing steps (e.g., centering), and glossed infinite-dimensional proofs.  
- Originality: Limited; main contribution is a rephrasing of known estimators with a kernel Arnoldi viewpoint.  
- Significance: Potentially useful to practitioners unfamiliar with EDMD/KDMD, but incremental contribution and weak empirical evidence reduce impact.  
- Key questions for authors: clarify differences from kernel EDMD/KDMD, provide convergence/finite-sample guarantees, justify centering, give quantitative comparisons and sensitivity analyses, replace/adapt preimage step, and correct the unit-circle eigenvalue claim.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; strengthen by clarifying novelty, adding rigorous approximation/convergence results, and providing thorough quantitative experiments and ablations.
