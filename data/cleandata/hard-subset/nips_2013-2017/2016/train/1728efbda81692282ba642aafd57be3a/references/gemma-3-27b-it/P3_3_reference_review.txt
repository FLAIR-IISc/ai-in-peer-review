## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents an interesting extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by incorporating reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The motivation – overcoming the limitations of DMD’s linear basis functions – is sound. The theoretical development is largely correct and connects well with existing Koopman operator theory. However, the paper suffers from a lack of compelling results demonstrating a *significant* advantage over existing DMD variants, especially given the increased complexity.  While the applications section is broad, it feels somewhat superficial and doesn't fully showcase the benefits of the proposed method. I recommend **weak reject** with encouragement to strengthen the experimental validation and more clearly articulate the practical advantages over existing approaches.

**1. Summary of Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the established framework of Koopman operator theory and DMD. It proposes to enhance DMD by operating within an RKHS, allowing for a richer set of basis functions (beyond linear monomials) to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions. This addresses a known limitation of standard DMD, where accurate approximation is restricted to systems linearizable around a fixed point. The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of spectral methods for dynamical systems, acknowledging related techniques like POD, balanced truncation, and various DMD variants (exact DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD, extended DMD).  The connection to kernel PCA and its use for robustifying the algorithm (addressing ill-conditioning issues) is a logical and well-established approach.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The mathematical development of the RKHS-based DMD is largely sound and convincingly builds upon existing theory. Theorem 1 and 2 are well-stated and clearly demonstrate the relationship between the proposed method and the standard DMD framework.
* **Addresses a Known Limitation:** The motivation for using RKHS to overcome the linearity constraint of traditional DMD is well-justified.  The potential for improved accuracy in highly nonlinear systems is a compelling idea.
* **Robustification via POD:**  The inclusion of POD for robustifying the algorithm and mitigating ill-conditioning is a practical and important addition, aligning with best practices in DMD.
* **Broad Exploration of Applications:** The paper touches upon several potential applications of the extracted features – prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Validation:** This is the major weakness. The experimental section, while present, feels insufficient. The toy system demonstrates the algorithm *can* work, but it's too simple to reveal any substantial benefit over standard DMD. The Hénon map results are suggestive, but not definitive, and lack a quantitative comparison to other methods. There’s no discussion of parameter sensitivity or computational cost.
* **Lack of Comparative Analysis:** The paper doesn't demonstrate a clear advantage over existing DMD variants (e.g., sparsity-promoting DMD, extended DMD) in terms of accuracy, efficiency, or robustness.  A thorough comparison is crucial to justify the added complexity of the RKHS approach. Why is this method superior, and under what conditions?
* **Superficial Applications:**  The applications section feels underdeveloped.  The prediction example simply shows a graph; there's no quantification of error or comparison to other prediction methods. The change-point detection example is similarly limited.  The locomotion recognition example is interesting, but the description of the method is brief and lacks details about performance evaluation (e.g., classification accuracy).
* **Implementation Details:**  The authors mention a Matlab code is available, but providing some more implementation details in the paper itself (e.g., kernel selection, parameter tuning strategies) would enhance reproducibility.
* **Clarity in Notation:** While generally clear, the notation can be dense at times, particularly in Section 3.



**4. Originality & Significance:**

The idea of applying kernel methods to DMD is not entirely novel (extended DMD can be seen as a related approach). However, the specific formulation using RKHS and the detailed theoretical treatment presented in this paper offer a potentially valuable contribution. The significance hinges on demonstrating a real-world advantage over existing methods, which is currently missing. If the method can demonstrably improve accuracy or robustness in challenging nonlinear systems, it would be a significant advancement.

**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Arguments FOR acceptance:**

*  Solid theoretical foundation.
*  Addresses a known limitation of standard DMD.
*  Potential for improved accuracy in highly nonlinear systems.

**Arguments AGAINST acceptance:**

*  Insufficient empirical validation.
*  Lack of comparative analysis to existing DMD variants.
*  Superficial applications section.
*  Limited discussion of implementation details and computational cost.




**Recommendation:** **Weak Reject**

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Significantly Expand Empirical Validation:** Perform a thorough evaluation on a diverse set of benchmark datasets, including chaotic systems, real-world time series, and potentially fluid dynamics simulations.
* **Comparative Analysis:**  Compare the performance of the proposed method against several state-of-the-art DMD variants (sparsity-promoting DMD, extended DMD, optimized DMD) across a range of tasks and datasets.
* **Detailed Application Evaluation:**  Provide a more rigorous evaluation of the applications, including quantitative metrics (e.g., prediction error, classification accuracy, change-point detection precision) and statistical significance testing.
* **Discuss Parameter Sensitivity:** Investigate the sensitivity of the method to kernel parameters (e.g., kernel type, bandwidth) and provide guidance on parameter selection.
* **Computational Cost Analysis:** Analyze the computational complexity of the algorithm and compare it to other DMD variants.



I believe that with substantial revisions addressing these weaknesses, this paper could become a valuable contribution to the field. However, in its current form, the empirical evidence is not strong enough to justify acceptance at NIPS.
