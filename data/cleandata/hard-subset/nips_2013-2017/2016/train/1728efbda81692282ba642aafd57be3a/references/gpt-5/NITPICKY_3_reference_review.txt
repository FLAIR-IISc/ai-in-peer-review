Summary
The paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis via Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The core idea is to form a Krylov subspace in feature space using snapshots ϕ(x0),…,ϕ(xτ−1), project the (implicit) Koopman operator KH onto this subspace, and compute Ritz eigenvalues/eigenvectors using Gram-Schmidt QR on the feature-space snapshot matrix and eigendecomposition of a projected operator. A “robustified” variant projects the data onto kernel PCA (POD) directions, allowing truncation and avoiding numerical issues. The paper claims theoretical support (Theorems 1 and 2), and demonstrates applications to (i) toy and Hénon systems, (ii) pre-image based short-term prediction, (iii) embedding/recognition of motion capture sequences via subspace angles, and (iv) sequential change-point detection via a CUSUM-style score.

Relation to prior work: The approach is very close to Extended DMD (EDMD) and kernel EDMD/KDMD (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley, J. Nonlinear Science 2015), which already framed DMD in general dictionaries and provided kernelized variants computing U*KHU with Gram matrices. The proposed “robustification” via kernel PCA truncation is analogous to standard exact/optimized DMD variants that project onto POD subspaces (Tu et al. 2014; Chen et al. 2012). The paper cites [35], but does not clearly delineate what is novel beyond EDMD/kEDMD.

Strengths
- Conceptually clear motivation: move beyond linear monomial observables to richer RKHS bases.
- Derivation of projected operator U*KHU expressed via Gram matrices is useful in practice.
- The robust variant using kernel PCA truncation addresses numerical instability, a known issue in DMD.
- The paper sketches several application avenues (prediction via pre-image, subspace-based recognition, change detection), potentially broadening impact.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns

Quality/technical soundness
- Theorem 1 is problematic. The text asserts that F := R−1Q* M+ equals a “companion matrix,” leading to a Vandermonde diagonalization and φ̃j = columns of Mτ T−1. In Arnoldi/DMD, Q*KHQ is upper Hessenberg, not generally companion; there is no Vandermonde structure in general. The proof uses distinct eigenvalues and properties of companion matrices that do not apply to generic projected operators. As written, the proof is incorrect/misleading.
- The centering and kernel PCA section has several inconsistencies. ϕc is defined without the 1/τ factor (should be the mean). The centering matrix is H = I − (1/τ)11⊤, but 1τ is described as a matrix with each entry 1/τ; the notation is confusing and inconsistent. The inclusion of H on both sides in F̂ = S̄−1/2 B̄* H (Mτ* M+) H B̄ S̄−1/2 is not justified; centering the “future” snapshots M+ with the same mean as Mτ is nontrivial and needs explicit derivation or correction.
- Theorem 2 assumes Ḡ−1 exists; in practice, Ḡ is rank-deficient and needs a pseudoinverse with regularization. No regularization is discussed; numerical stability is a major omission.
- The claim “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle [20]” is false in general. Eigenvalues lie on the unit circle for measure-preserving dynamics; for dissipative systems on attractors, moduli are typically ≤1. This needs correction.
- Pre-image mapping is only sketched; the citation is mismatched (see below), and the approach relies on local MDS-like approximations without quantitative evaluation or error bounds.
- The change-point detection derivation is hand-wavy, treats p(xt) as a nonparametric exponential family with sufficient statistics ψ(xt) but then replaces ψ with ϕ◦f(xt−1); justification is thin, and the simplification of likelihood ratios by dropping terms is not validated.

Clarity
- Many grammatical errors and awkward phrasing impede readability (e.g., “a way to robustly it”; “almost of the theoretical claims”; “This theorem gives an extension of DMD via the Gram-Schmidt QR decomposition in the feature space.”).
- Notation is inconsistent: * used for Hermitian transpose and sometimes inner products; use of ⟨·,·⟩, Q∗, and ·∗ alternates. Definitions of H, 1τ, and ϕc are inconsistent with standard centering.
- Several figure references and content are missing/unclear. Fig. 2 is referenced but not included; Fig. 4 caption mentions “kDMD-SVM” without explanation. The experimental setups lack basic details (data length, noise, hyperparameters, truncation ranks).
- The proof of Theorem 1 incorrectly invokes the companion form; the argument needs a proper Arnoldi/DMD derivation.

Originality
- The core idea—Koopman operator approximation in RKHS via kernelized EDMD/DMD—is known (Williams et al. 2015; also see Tu et al. 2014 for exact DMD; Chen et al. 2012; and later KDMD variants). The paper does not articulate a clear conceptual or algorithmic differentiator beyond re-deriving kernel EDMD with a Gram-Schmidt viewpoint and a kernel PCA truncation (both standard). The novelty appears limited.

Significance
- Potentially useful if the derivations were tighter and the empirical section stronger. As is, experiments are minimal (toy system, Hénon, small MoCap vignette, a change detection toy) with no rigorous quantitative comparisons to EDMD/kEDMD, exact/optimized DMD, or KDMD baselines. No ablations on kernel choice, rank truncation, or robustness to noise. Without stronger evidence and clearer novelty, impact is limited.

Additional issues (minor but numerous)
- Double-blind violation: code URL (http://en.44nobu.net/codes/kdmd.zip) likely de-anonymizes authors; not acceptable in submission.
- References are severely incomplete and inconsistent: many lack years, venues, page numbers; names truncated (“L.S,”; “Ukeiley.”), typos (“Aohnson”); misnumbering (the pre-image method is cited as [18], but in the list it appears as [17]); some entries are malformed or ambiguous.
- Multiple typos: “robustify,” “Hénon” vs “Hénon,” “Poincaré,” “principle” vs “principal,” “∗ indicates the Hermitian transpose” but later “Q∗τ indicates the Hermitian transpose,” etc.
- Complexity/scalability not discussed: kernel methods scale as O(τ^3) in time and O(τ^2) in memory; no mention of Nystrom/approximate methods.
- Assumptions such as distinct eigenvalues and ψ=0 are unrealistic in noisy data; no discussion of noise, regularization (ridge), or conditioning.

Pros
- Addresses an active area (data-driven Koopman analysis).
- Kernel-based formulation can be powerful; expressing U*KHU via Gram matrices is practical.
- The robust truncation idea is aligned with best practices in DMD.

Cons
- Weak novelty relative to EDMD/kEDMD.
- Theoretical inaccuracies (Theorem 1; unit circle claim; centering/inversion issues).
- Poor writing, many typos, and serious reference/formatting problems.
- Minimal, largely qualitative experiments without proper baselines.
- Double-blind violation.

Questions for authors
1. Precisely distinguish your method from kernel EDMD/KDMD (Williams et al. 2015). What, concretely, is new?
2. Provide a correct derivation of the snapshot reconstruction (Eq. 9) without invoking “companion”/Vandermonde. How do you handle non-distinct Ritz values?
3. Clarify centering: define H and ϕc correctly; justify the placement of H in Eq. (11); address centering of M+.
4. Replace Ḡ−1 with a regularized pseudoinverse; discuss numerical stability and complexity.
5. Correct the claim about Koopman eigenvalues on attractors.
6. Provide quantitative comparisons to EDMD/kEDMD, exact/optimized DMD on standard benchmarks; include sensitivity to kernel width and truncation rank.
7. Detail and evaluate the pre-image and change-point procedures quantitatively.
8. Fix the anonymity breach and the reference list.
