Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors (i) define a Koopman operator KH acting on the feature map ϕ, (ii) construct a Krylov subspace in feature space spanned by ϕ(x0), …, ϕ(xτ−1), and (iii) estimate empirical Ritz values/vectors via a Gram–Schmidt QR factorization and an eigen-decomposition of the projected operator. They note this is equivalent in spirit to DMD applied in feature space. Recognizing numerical ill-conditioning, they “robustify” by projecting onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions and then eigendecompose a reduced operator F̂. They present two theorems linking the resulting eigenpairs to Koopman spectra under assumptions (distinct eigenvalues; eigenfunctions in the span of the chosen subspace). They illustrate on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map, and sketch applications to short-term prediction via preimage, dynamics recognition via subspace angles, and change-point detection via a CUSUM-style score.

This line of work is closely related to extended DMD (EDMD) and its kernelized variants, where DMD is performed on a dictionary of nonlinear observables (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley, J. Nonlinear Sci. 2015) and to kernel EDMD/KDMD formulations subsequently explored in the Koopman literature. The robustification via POD/SVD truncation is standard in DMD practice (exact/optimized DMD) and again appears in EDMD where the Gram or covariance matrices are truncated/regularized. The paper’s general idea—projecting Koopman dynamics into an RKHS via the kernel trick and estimating a finite-dimensional linear model—is therefore conceptually aligned with, and in parts overlapping with, known EDMD/KDMD approaches.

Strengths
- Clear conceptual bridge between DMD/Koopman analysis and RKHS machinery; the kernel trick avoids explicit construction of high-dimensional dictionaries.
- The QR/Arnoldi view in feature space is pedagogically helpful, and the POD-based truncation addresses well-known numerical stability issues.
- Theorems 1–2, while relying on standard linear algebra, provide a clean finite-data statement and an interpretable connection between eigenpairs of the reduced operator and Koopman spectra under assumptions.
- The applications section suggests versatility (prediction, recognition, change detection).

Weaknesses
- Novelty relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD is not convincingly established. The robustified variant largely mirrors EDMD with a kernel dictionary and SVD/POD truncation. A careful positioning and empirical/theoretical comparison to EDMD/KDMD is missing.
- Theory is limited to algebraic identities under strong assumptions (distinct eigenvalues; ψ=0; eigenfunctions in the span of kernel-PCA components). There are no consistency or error bounds as data length grows, no treatment of noise, nor stability/regularization analysis (e.g., Tikhonov, ridge).
- Empirical evaluation is very limited: small toy problems; qualitative eigenvalue plots; no quantitative metrics (eigenvalue/mode error, multi-step prediction error), no sensitivity to noise, no ablations, no comparisons to baselines (DMD, exact/optimized DMD, EDMD, KDMD, SINDy, GPDM, etc.). The application demos are anecdotal.
- The preimage step is ad hoc (MDS-style) with no guarantees and no empirical comparison to standard preimage methods or out-of-sample extension techniques.
- Computational aspects are underdeveloped: complexity and memory scaling with τ and kernel choice, conditioning of Gram matrices, and strategies for regularization are not discussed.
- Clarity/rigor issues: notation sometimes collides (ϕ vs. φ), centering operator definitions, assumptions for diagonalizability, and handling of stochastic systems are only cursorily treated. Several references are incomplete, and related work coverage omits direct KDMD literature.

Quality
Technically correct at the level of finite-dimensional linear algebra, but the main theorems are essentially restatements of Arnoldi/QR properties in feature space plus an assumption-heavy link to Koopman eigenfunctions. The practical algorithm lacks rigorous analysis (convergence/statistics/regularization). Experimental evidence is insufficient to support claims of practical advantage.

Clarity
Generally readable, but notation could be tightened (e.g., clear distinction between feature maps, Koopman eigenfunctions, and Ritz vectors; careful use of H and centering). Algorithmic steps would benefit from clear pseudocode, complexity, and numerical stability guidance (e.g., modified Gram–Schmidt vs. SVD). Some references are incomplete.

Originality
Moderate to low. Kernelizing DMD/EDMD and using POD/SVD truncation are well known. The paper would need to more clearly differentiate itself from EDMD/KDMD and demonstrate either theoretical advances (e.g., consistency/regularization) or practical superiority.

Significance
Unclear given current evidence. The framework may be useful for practitioners familiar with DMD who want an RKHS view, but without strong comparisons and quantitative results, it is hard to see impact beyond existing EDMD/KDMD practice.

Specific suggestions
- Position against EDMD/kernel EDMD explicitly; include head-to-head comparisons on standard benchmarks (Duffing, Van der Pol, cylinder wake, FHN) with quantitative metrics and noise robustness.
- Add regularization (ridge/Tikhonov) and analyze conditioning; compare MGS vs. SVD-based implementations; discuss kernel width selection and model order selection.
- Provide consistency/error analysis under sampling noise and finite data, at least for projected operators.
- For prediction, compare preimage strategies and report out-of-sample forecasting errors vs. EDMD/KDMD and GPDM.
- For recognition/change detection, include baselines and quantitative metrics (accuracy, detection delay/false alarm).
- Clarify assumptions in Theorem 2 (diagonalizability, left/right eigenvectors normalization) and provide proofs in an appendix.
- Clean up notation, add pseudocode, and report computational cost.

Pros
- Clear and coherent formulation; pedagogical value.
- Bridges DMD and kernels; flexible framework.
- Addresses numerical stability via POD truncation.

Cons
- Limited novelty vs. EDMD/KDMD; missing comparative evaluation.
- No statistical/consistency analysis; strong assumptions.
- Insufficient empirical validation; mainly qualitative.
- Preimage and applications are underdeveloped methodologically.

Recommendation
Reject. The paper’s main ideas are too close to established EDMD/KDMD without sufficient theoretical or empirical advances to justify publication at NeurIPS. With substantial additions—rigorous analysis, comprehensive comparisons, and stronger experiments—the work could be competitive.

Confidence
High.
