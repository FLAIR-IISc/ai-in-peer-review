

# Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproduce kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis. The authors present a modal decomposition algorithm that uses nonlinear basis functions derived from kernel functions, rather than the linear monomials used in traditional DMD. The method builds on the theoretical foundation of Koopman operators, which provide a linear representation of nonlinear dynamics. The paper extends DMD to RKHS by defining a kernel-based Koopman operator and developing an algorithm for its spectral analysis. The authors also address practical implementation issues by proposing a robustification method using POD bases, which is analogous to techniques used in standard DMD variants. The paper demonstrates applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using both synthetic and real-world data.

The paper relates well to previous work in the field. The authors correctly situate their contribution within the literature on Koopman operator analysis (citing [16, 20, 25, 26, 31]) and DMD variants (citing [8, 13, 33, 35]). They acknowledge the limitations of traditional DMD (which uses linear monomials as basis functions) and position their work as addressing these limitations through the expressiveness of kernel functions. The connection to Extended DMD [35] is appropriately noted, though the paper could better clarify the distinction between their kernel-based approach and that of Extended DMD.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a mathematically rigorous extension of DMD to RKHS. The theoretical development in Sections 3 and 4, including the proofs of Theorems 1 and 2, is sound and well-justified. The authors correctly identify and address the numerical instability issue common in DMD implementations by proposing a POD-based robustification approach.

2. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The theoretical development flows logically from basic concepts to the proposed algorithm. The empirical examples are clearly explained with appropriate visualizations (Figures 1-4), and the applications section demonstrates concrete utility of the method.

3. **Novelty:** The use of RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis represents a significant extension beyond traditional DMD approaches. While Extended DMD [35] uses pre-determined basis functions, this paper uses kernel functions to generate nonlinear basis functions, which is a distinct methodological contribution. The paper clearly differentiates its approach from previous work and demonstrates the value of nonlinear basis functions for systems where linear approximations are insufficient.

4. **Significance:** The applications demonstrated (state prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) showcase the practical value of the method. The ability to extract meaningful features from nonlinear dynamical systems has broad implications for scientific and engineering applications, particularly in fields where understanding the underlying dynamics is critical.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Comparative Evaluation:** While the paper demonstrates empirical results on synthetic and real-world data, it lacks a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, computational complexity, and scalability. A more thorough comparison with other DMD variants (e.g., Extended DMD, optimized DMD) would strengthen the claims of superiority.

2. **Kernel Parameter Selection:** The paper does not adequately justify the choice of kernel parameters (polynomial degree 3 for the toy system, Gaussian kernel width 1 for Hénon map). A systematic exploration of how kernel parameters affect performance would provide valuable insights for practitioners.

3. **Scalability Considerations:** The paper does not address the computational complexity of the proposed method or its scalability to large datasets. Given that kernel methods can become computationally expensive with large datasets, this is an important consideration for practical applications.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Clear mathematical extension of DMD to RKHS with rigorous theoretical foundation
- Practical robustification approach addressing numerical instability issues
- Demonstrated applications showing utility beyond theoretical analysis
- Well-written and clearly organized paper that advances the field of Koopman spectral analysis
- Novel use of kernel functions to generate nonlinear basis functions for Koopman operator approximation

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- Limited comparative evaluation with state-of-the-art methods
- Insufficient justification for kernel parameter selection
- Lack of discussion on computational complexity and scalability
- Limited connection to recent NIPS papers specifically focused on Koopman operators

Overall, this is a technically sound and well-written paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of Koopman spectral analysis. The extension of DMD to RKHS represents a novel approach that addresses a clear limitation of existing methods. While the paper could be strengthened with more comprehensive comparisons and discussion of computational considerations, these are not fatal flaws. The significance of the work is demonstrated through multiple applications, and the paper is well-positioned within the existing literature on Koopman operators and DMD. The contribution is sufficiently novel and significant for NIPS.
