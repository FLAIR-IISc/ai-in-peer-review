- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions and perform modal decomposition.
- Provides theoretical justification with Theorems 1 and 2 linking the method to Koopman spectral analysis.
- Introduces a POD-based robustification to mitigate numerical instability in practical implementations.
- Demonstrates applicability on synthetic systems (toy, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (locomotion, change-point detection).
- Strength: mathematically rigorous and well-proven theoretical contribution.
- Strength: novel use of reproducing kernels to capture nonlinear dynamics beyond standard linear DMD.
- Strength: empirical validation shows qualitative agreement with theory on multiple datasets.
- Strength: practical implementation (POD projection) addresses known DMD limitations.
- Weakness: limited comparison to related work, especially extended DMD and other kernel-based methods.
- Weakness: no analysis of computational complexity (cost of kernel operations and RKHS orthogonalization).
- Weakness: empirical results lack quantitative comparisons to standard DMD, extended DMD, or other baselines.
- Weakness: claim that the method is a feature-extraction procedure is not clearly differentiated from existing techniques.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method that addresses DMD limitations and includes practical robustification and relevant applications.
- Against acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, missing complexity analysis, weak ties to broader ML community, and unclear practical significance.
- Overall: technically solid and theoretically interesting but underdeveloped experimentally and computationally; recommend weak acceptance pending additional experiments, comparisons, and complexity discussion.
