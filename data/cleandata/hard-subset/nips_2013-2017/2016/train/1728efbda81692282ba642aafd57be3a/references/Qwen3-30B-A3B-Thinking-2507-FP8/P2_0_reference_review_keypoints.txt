- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Builds a theoretical foundation with clear theorems linking empirical Ritz values to Koopman eigenvalues (notably Theorem 1 and Theorem 2).  
- Introduces a POD-based robustification to address numerical issues in DMD implementations.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Empirical examples (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) show feasibility but are limited in scope.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., extended DMD, optimized DMD, other kernel approaches).  
- Does not discuss computational complexity or scalability, which is important for practical use.  
- Acknowledges limitations: requires rich data and full eigenfunction recovery needs sufficiently large Krylov subspace.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and effective figures.  
- Could provide more intuitive RKHS explanations for readers not versed in functional analysis.  
- Novel integration of kernel methods with DMD/Koopman analysis, though kernel use in dynamical systems is not entirely new.  
- References related work thoroughly but insufficiently distinguishes differences from extended DMD (Williams et al.).  
- Significance is promising but weakened by limited empirical validation and lack of demonstrations of practical advantages.  
- Overall recommendation: weak acceptance—technically sound and novel but needs stronger empirical validation.  
- Suggested improvements: add detailed comparisons with existing methods, analyze computational complexity/scalability, test on more real-world/benchmark datasets, and clarify distinctions from extended DMD.
