- Proposes kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis, including a POD-robustified variant and demonstrations on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Theoretical contribution: mathematically rigorous extension with Theorems 1–2 linking the kernel DMD to Koopman operator theory.

- Robustness: derives a POD-based robustified formulation to address ill-conditioning in naive kernel implementations.

- Novelty: first direct application of kernel methods to Koopman spectral analysis within the DMD framework.

- Practical utility: demonstrates useful applications on synthetic and real locomotion data, showing convincing empirical results.

- Properly situates work within DMD literature and distinguishes itself from extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015).

- Limited empirical comparison: only two synthetic and one real dataset; lacks quantitative benchmarks against state-of-the-art (e.g., extended DMD) on standard datasets.

- Missing computational analysis: no discussion of algorithmic complexity or scalability, important given kernel O(n^3) costs.

- Arbitrary kernel choices: kernel types and parameters are used without justification or sensitivity analysis.

- Incomplete limitations discussion: inadequate coverage of noise sensitivity, high-dimensional scalability, and theoretical approximation guarantees.

- Need for clearer implementation guidance: practical steps for efficient kernel matrix computation are not well explained.

- Empirical scope should expand: recommends benchmarking on common systems like Lorenz to strengthen claims.

- Better situate relative to other kernel-based dynamical methods (e.g., Gaussian process dynamical models) and clarify complementarities/differences.

- Locomotion application promising but needs comparison to standard dynamics-recognition methods in that domain.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing empirical comparisons, computational complexity, kernel selection justification, and broader limitations.

- Pro: solid theoretical foundation, novel kernel-Koopman integration, and demonstrated applications; Con: limited empirical benchmarking, scalability and kernel-selection issues, and insufficient exploration of limitations.
