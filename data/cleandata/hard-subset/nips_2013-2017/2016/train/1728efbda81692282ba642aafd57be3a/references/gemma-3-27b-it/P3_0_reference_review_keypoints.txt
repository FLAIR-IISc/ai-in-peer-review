- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better capture nonlinear dynamics; motivation is sound.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous and logical, with a clear derivation (Theorem 1) and POD-based robustification (Theorem 2).  
- Novel contribution: using kernel methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions and linking to kernel PCA for implementation.  
- Practical implementation considerations are addressed (Gram matrix reduction, POD to improve robustness).  
- Applications (prediction, embedding/recognition, change-point detection) are proposed but only superficially explored.  
- Empirical validation is limited—toy system and Henon map examples are insufficiently challenging or quantitative.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparative analysis against standard, exact, and sparsity-promoting DMD variants on benchmark datasets.  
- Does not discuss sensitivity to kernel choice or parameters (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth) and lacks a sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational cost for large datasets and whether overhead is justified by gains is not fully addressed.  
- Significance is moderate: conceptually valuable, but impact depends on stronger empirical evidence on real-world problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theory, clear presentation, and practical robustification.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support, missing comparisons, unclear kernel/parameter and computational issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on strengthened empirical validation, comparative analyses, and addressing kernel selection and computational concerns.
