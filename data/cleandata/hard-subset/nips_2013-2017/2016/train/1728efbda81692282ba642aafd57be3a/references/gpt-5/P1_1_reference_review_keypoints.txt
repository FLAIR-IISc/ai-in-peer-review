- Proposes a kernelized formulation of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by defining the Koopman operator in an RKHS and computing Ritz pairs from feature-mapped snapshots.  
- Introduces a robust variant projecting onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to produce a computable finite-dimensional operator F̂ expressed via Gram matrices.  
- Provides two theorems linking computed Ritz pairs to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under specific assumptions.  
- Demonstrates applications: short-term prediction via preimage, recognition via subspace angles, and sequential change detection using a kernel exponential-family CUSUM statistic.  
- Empirical illustrations: toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, motion-capture locomotion embeddings, and Lorenz change-detection example; code released.  
- Methodologically close to Extended DMD (EDMD); the RKHS/POD variant essentially matches EDMD with kernels and a data-adapted basis, but equivalence is not formally shown.  
- Prior kernel/EDMD convergence literature (e.g., Korda & Mezić; Klus et al.) and kernelized Koopman work are not cited.  
- Conceptual strength: clean RKHS extension that is fully kernelizable using only Gram matrices.  
- Practical strength: robust kernel-POD projection aligns with stabilized DMD practice and yields a computable operator scalable to moderate trajectory lengths τ.  
- Theoretical strength: theorems give an explicit link between Ritz pairs and Koopman spectra and the algebra for F̂ is implementable.  
- Applications breadth and code availability aid reproducibility and practical utility.  
- Main novelty limited; contribution appears incremental relative to EDMD/KDMD with POD truncation.  
- The paper fails to formally relate its derivations to EDMD’s generalized eigenproblem and lacks empirical comparisons to EDMD/exact DMD.  
- Mathematical rigor issues: some matrix expressions (e.g., involving rectangular Mτ) are ill-posed and Arnoldi/companion connections need residual handling.  
- Incorrect claim: Koopman eigenvalues do not always lie on the unit circle (dissipative systems contradict this).  
- Theorem 2 assumptions (eigenfunctions ∈ RKHS and in span(U)) are strong and lack discussion of when they hold or τ→∞ convergence.  
- Experiments are illustrative but lack quantitative evaluation, baselines, sensitivity analyses, and robustness studies (noise, kernel hyperparameters, truncation p).  
- Prediction/preimage approach is ad hoc, lacking quantitative forecasting metrics and comparisons to baselines (EDMD, GPR, AR models).  
- Recognition results are limited to visualization (MDS) with no classification accuracy or ablation studies.  
- Change-point detection shows a single example without ROC/ARL/delay vs false-alarm analysis or controlled benchmarks.  
- Presentation issues: notational overload, typos, missing dimensional details, unclear centering/residual handling, and incomplete bibliography entries.  
- Computational complexity and memory requirements are not discussed; no guidance on kernel selection or truncation strategies is provided.  
- Overall quality: technically plausible and implementable but contains informal derivations and overstated core claims.  
- Originality: moderate — natural RKHS extension closely related to existing EDMD/KDMD approaches.  
- Significance: potentially useful to practitioners but hard to assess improvement over EDMD without comparisons and stronger validation.  
- Recommended improvements: explicitly derive equivalence to EDMD, tighten theoretical claims (Arnoldi/residuals, unit-circle correction, RKHS eigenfunction conditions), and extend experiments with quantitative baselines and sensitivity analyses.  
- Additional suggestions: discuss scalability and approximations (Nyström, random features), compare preimage methods formally, and fix notation/bibliography for clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject pending clearer novelty, corrected theory, and substantially stronger empirical validation.
