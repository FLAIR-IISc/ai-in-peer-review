- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel functions.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm in RKHS based on Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization and a POD-based robustification to address numerical instability.  
- Provides clear theorems (Theorems 1 and 2) with rigorous proofs, giving a solid mathematical foundation.  
- Empirical evaluation on a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data shows accurate Koopman eigenvalue estimation and effective applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition).  
- Builds on and differentiates from Williams et al. (extended DMD) by using kernel methods instead of pre-determined basis functions.  
- Novel and meaningful contribution: rigorous RKHS extension of DMD that enables nonlinear basis functions for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and figures effectively illustrate key results.  
- Lacks detailed implementation guidance (e.g., kernel width selection) and could better connect theory to practical implementation; pseudocode would help.  
- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art alternatives, particularly regarding computational efficiency and scalability to high-dimensional data.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations: requirement for sufficiently rich data and numerical issues without POD robustification.  
- Significant potential impact for modeling complex nonlinear dynamical systems and practical applications where linear DMD falls short.  
- Strengths: strong theory, novel RKHS extension, practical robustification, convincing empirical demonstrations, adequate literature context.  
- Weaknesses: limited benchmarking against other methods, sparse implementation details, and insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept, with minor improvements mainly on discussing computational aspects and providing more implementation detail.
