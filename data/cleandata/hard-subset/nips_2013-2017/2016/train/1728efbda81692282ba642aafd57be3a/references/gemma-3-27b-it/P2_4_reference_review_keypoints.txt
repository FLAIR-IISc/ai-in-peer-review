- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.
- Presents an algorithm based on Krylov subspace computations and eigendecomposition, with POD projection to address ill-conditioning.
- Demonstrations include toy systems and the Henon map, with suggested applications in prediction, pattern recognition, and change-point detection.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising but currently under-validated; reviewer leans to weak accept with major revisions.
- Quality: solid mathematical derivation and sensible POD fix, but empirical evaluation is limited and Henon results are only approximate.
- Clarity: well-written and organized, though equations are dense and stronger intuition/examples for RKHS benefits are needed.
- Originality: a distinct formulation of kernelized DMD though related kernel-based Koopman methods exist; needs clearer differentiation.
- Significance: potentially useful for downstream tasks but lacks convincing empirical evidence and concrete examples.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation connecting DMD, RKHS, and Krylov/Ritz theory.
- Strength: POD projection improves robustness to ill-conditioning.
- Strength: RKHS extension can potentially capture more complex dynamics than linear monomials.
- Weakness: limited and insufficient empirical validation with no thorough comparisons to standard/extended DMD.
- Weakness: application sections are vague and lack detailed implementations or quantitative results.
- Weakness: no ablation studies to separate effects of RKHS versus POD projection.
- Weakness: related work connections (e.g., subspace identification) are mentioned but not explored deeply.
- For acceptance: theoretically sound, practical improvement for ill-conditioning, and initial proof-of-concept results.
- Against acceptance: weak empirical validation, underdeveloped applications, and unclear significance.
- Major revision recommendations: expand empirical evaluation and benchmarks; provide detailed application implementations and quantitative results; conduct ablation studies; deepen related work discussion (subspace/kernel-based ID); clarify pre-image and implementation details for prediction.
