Summary:
The paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). It formulates DMD in feature space by building a Krylov subspace from feature-mapped snapshots, projecting the Koopman operator onto this subspace, and computing Ritz values/vectors (Theorem 1). Because naive Gram–Schmidt in RKHS is ill-conditioned, the paper proposes a robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions (Theorem 2), yielding an algorithm akin to DMD with SVD truncation but executed via Gram matrices. It further sketches three applications enabled by the learned Koopman modes: short-term prediction via a pre-image construction, recognition of dynamical regimes via kernel principal angles between subspaces of estimated eigenfunctions, and sequential change-point detection using a kernelized exponential family model. Experiments include a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, a locomotion dataset for embedding/recognition, and a Lorenz sequence for change-point detection.

Relation to prior work:
The work sits squarely within DMD/Koopman-based system identification. It is closely related to Extended DMD (EDMD) and kernel EDMD (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley, J. Nonlinear Sci. 2015/2016), exact/optimized DMD, and subspace/Arnoldi perspectives on DMD. The proposed robust kernel approach, which computes a low-rank feature-space projection via kernel PCA and then eigendecomposes the induced finite operator, is conceptually very similar to kernel EDMD and subspace DMD carried out with kernels. The paper acknowledges EDMD [35] and the relation to “robust DMD” via POD, but does not provide a side-by-side derivation or empirical comparison to EDMD/kernel EDMD.

Strengths:
- Clear and appealing idea: leverage RKHS expressiveness to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions beyond linear monomials, with a clean Gram-matrix-based implementation.
- The Arnoldi/Krylov perspective in RKHS, and the link to Ritz values/vectors, is well-motivated and helpful for intuition.
- The robustification via kernel PCA is practical and aligns with best practices for DMD (POD/SVD truncation).
- Applications beyond pure spectral estimation (prediction via preimage, recognition via subspace angles, change detection) illustrate the potential utility of the learned Koopman features.

Weaknesses:
- Novelty relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD is limited and insufficiently clarified. The robust kernel algorithm essentially recovers kernel EDMD/subspace DMD; the paper does not delineate theoretical or empirical differences, nor compare against them.
- Theory stops short of guarantees. Theorems 1–2 are algebraic and require strong assumptions (e.g., eigenfunctions contained in the span of feature maps and within the KPCA subspace), but no approximation, consistency, or error bounds vs. true Koopman spectra are provided. Some steps (e.g., Mτ inverses/companion matrix arguments in infinite-dimensional H) would benefit from tighter, operator-theoretic justification.
- A key statement is likely incorrect: “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle” is false in general for dissipative systems; magnitudes can be less than 1 (decay) unless the dynamics is measure-preserving/unitary. This calls for correction and careful qualification.
- Empirical evaluation is thin and mostly qualitative. Plots of estimated eigenvalues are shown, but there is no quantitative error analysis, robustness study, or systematic comparison to DMD/EDMD on standard benchmarks. The applications are interesting but remain proof-of-concept; e.g., no prediction error metrics, classification accuracy, or change-detection operating characteristics (ROC, detection delay vs. false alarm).
- Practical aspects are under-discussed: choice/tuning of kernels, computational complexity (O(τ^3) for eigendecomposition with τ snapshots), memory scaling, handling noise, and out-of-sample/online updates.
- Writing/presentation issues: numerous typos/grammar problems; several references are incomplete; some notation is heavy and imprecise; figures are referenced but quantitative details are sparse. The inclusion of a code URL compromises anonymity.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The algebraic construction is reasonable and aligns with known DMD/EDMD machinery, but some derivations are sketched, assumptions are strong, and a questionable claim about eigenvalues needs fixing. No statistical guarantees or numerical stability analyses are given.
- Empirics: Demonstrations are small-scale and qualitative; lack of baselines and metrics limits evidential strength.

Clarity:
- Organization is acceptable, but text contains typos and ambiguous statements. Theorems would benefit from clearer assumptions and precise operator notation. Applications need more concrete algorithmic details (e.g., the pre-image/MDS procedure).

Originality:
- The move to RKHS and robustification via KPCA are known ideas in EDMD/kernel EDMD. The paper’s contribution seems to be presenting DMD-as-Arnoldi explicitly in RKHS and connecting to KPCA projections. Without clearer differentiation or comparisons, originality is moderate.

Significance:
- Koopman-in-RKHS is impactful; a solid, well-evaluated kernel DMD could be useful. However, given overlap with existing methods and limited experiments, current significance is modest. Strengthening theory and experiments could make it much more compelling.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Precisely relate the proposed algorithms to EDMD and kernel EDMD; include theoretical equivalences/differences and empirical comparisons on benchmarks (e.g., noisy linear systems, Duffing, cylinder wake snapshots if available).
- Provide quantitative evaluations: eigenvalue/mode errors vs. ground truth (when known), prediction errors vs. baselines (DMD/EDMD/GPDM), classification accuracy for dynamics recognition, and change-detection metrics.
- Add guidance on kernel/regularization selection, truncation criteria, and complexity. Consider regularized/TSVD variants and noise-robust formulations.
- Correct the statement about eigenvalues on the unit circle; clarify conditions (unitary/measure-preserving).
- Tighten proofs and assumptions; avoid informal inverses in infinite-dimensional settings; consider citing standard operator-theoretic results on Koopman approximations.
- Fix writing, complete references, and remove identifying links to preserve anonymity.

Arguments pro acceptance:
- Timely and relevant topic; kernelizing Koopman spectral analysis is important.
- Conceptual clarity of the RKHS/Krylov viewpoint.
- Practical robustification aligns with how DMD is used.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Overlaps substantially with EDMD/kernel EDMD; insufficient novelty and no direct comparisons.
- Limited empirical evidence and lack of quantitative metrics.
- Some technical imprecision and an incorrect claim; anonymity violation.

Overall: Promising direction but, in current form, below the bar for NeurIPS due to limited novelty vs. EDMD, weak evaluation, and presentation issues. Strengthening theory, comparisons, and experiments could make it competitive.
