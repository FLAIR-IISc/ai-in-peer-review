Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis. The main idea is to perform DMD in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) by (i) forming a Krylov subspace spanned by feature-mapped observations ϕ(x0), …, ϕ(xτ−1), (ii) projecting the Koopman operator KH: ϕ ↦ ϕ∘f onto this subspace, and (iii) extracting empirical Koopman eigenvalues and “modes” via eigendecomposition. Theorem 1 establishes an RKHS analogue of the standard DMD expansion, showing that the sequence ϕ(xt) admits a Vandermonde-type representation in terms of empirical Ritz values and vectors, up to an orthogonal residual.

The authors also present a numerically robust variant using kernel PCA (analogous to POD-projected DMD), projecting data onto the top kernel principal components before estimating the finite-dimensional representation F̂ = U*KHU. Theorem 2 connects the spectrum/eigenvectors of F̂ to true Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under assumptions that the Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the data feature span and their representers lie in span(U). The method is then illustrated on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map. The paper also sketches three applications: short-term prediction via a kernel preimage procedure, dynamics recognition using subspace distances derived from Koopman eigenfunctions, and sequential change-point detection via a CUSUM-like criterion with a kernel exponential family parametrization.

This work is closely related to DMD and its numerically robust variants (exact/optimized DMD), extended DMD (EDMD) which uses user-chosen dictionaries, and kernelized EDMD variants. The robust kernel PCA step directly parallels POD-truncation in DMD. The paper cites EDMD and notes similarity; however, a more direct comparison to kernel-EDMD/KDMD literature would be helpful.

Quality (technical soundness)
- The RKHS formulation and use of Gram matrices to implement Krylov/projection steps are sound in spirit and consistent with kernel methods and spectral approaches.
- The Arnoldi-like relation M+ = KHMτ and the construction of a finite-dimensional operator via projection are standard and well motivated.
- Theorem 1: The derivation relies on properties of the QR decomposition and the relation MτF = KHMτ. However, calling F a “companion matrix” and relying on Vandermonde diagonalization assumes a specific (Krylov/shift) basis and distinct eigenvalues; this requires clearer conditions. In generic Arnoldi/DMD, the projected operator is upper-Hessenberg, not necessarily companion. Clarifying basis choices/conditions under which F is similar to a companion matrix would improve rigor.
- Theorem 2: The result cleanly formalizes when kernel PCA–based truncation preserves Koopman spectral information. The assumptions (φj ∈ Φ and κj ∈ span(U)) are strong but reasonable as identifiability/excitation conditions. Some steps (involving HḠ−1H) assume a well-conditioned, truncated inverse; regularization should be discussed.
- The statement “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle” is not generally valid; it holds for measure-preserving dynamics, but for attracting dynamics eigenvalues typically lie inside the unit disk. This should be corrected or qualified.
- The change-point detection derivation is heuristic: the use of a kernel exponential family parametrized by KHϕ and the simplification of the likelihood-ratio omit important statistical conditions; no guarantees are provided.
- The preimage approach follows prior work but lacks analysis or stability guarantees.

Clarity
- The paper is generally readable and well organized, but several derivations are compressed and could use more precise conditions (distinct eigenvalues, basis choices, invertibility/conditioning, treatment of centering). A few typos remain.
- Pseudocode or a clearer algorithmic summary for both variants (with computational complexity) would aid reproducibility.
- The relation to EDMD and kernel-EDMD should be expanded, with explicit similarities/differences.

Originality
- Kernelizing DMD via RKHS projections is a natural extension. Similar ideas have appeared in EDMD with kernel dictionaries; explicit comparison to kernel-EDMD/KDMD is needed to assess novelty. The robust kernel PCA projection is an expected analogue of POD-truncation.
- Theoretical links (Theorems 1 and 2) are useful, though some steps require clarification.
- Applications (preimage-based prediction, subspace-based recognition, change detection) are interesting but incremental and not deeply validated.

Significance
- The approach could be valuable for nonlinear spectral analysis with richer function classes than linear observables, aligning with active interest in Koopman methods. If positioned against EDMD/kernel-EDMD, the contributions may be incremental.
- Empirical validation is limited: small examples, qualitative eigenvalue plots, no quantitative benchmarks or comparisons against EDMD/kernel-EDMD/exact DMD on reconstruction/prediction error, spectral accuracy, or robustness. The application demos lack metrics (e.g., classification accuracy, detection delay/false alarm rate).
- Scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice/width, truncation rank) are not analyzed.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a rigorous derivation of Theorem 1, clarifying when the projected operator is similar to a companion matrix; otherwise present the more general Arnoldi/Hessenberg formulation.
- Correct the statement about Koopman spectra on attractors.
- Add quantitative experiments comparing against DMD, EDMD, and kernel-EDMD on synthetic and real systems, reporting spectral/forecasting errors, robustness to noise, and computational cost.
- Discuss computational complexity, memory, and strategies for large datasets (e.g., Nyström/landmark approximations, incremental updates).
- Include regularization (e.g., Tikhonov) and analyze its effect on stability/performance.
- For applications, report metrics (prediction RMSE, classification accuracy, change detection ROC) and ablations.

Arguments for acceptance
- Clear and principled RKHS-based formulation of Koopman spectral analysis with implementable algorithms via Gram matrices.
- Theoretical connections between projected operators and Koopman spectra (Theorems 1 and 2) clarify when kernel projections recover true eigenstructure.
- Practical robustification via kernel PCA parallels established best practices in DMD.
- Interesting application avenues demonstrating broader relevance.

Arguments against acceptance
- Overlap with existing EDMD/kernel-EDMD; novelty relative to prior work is not clearly demonstrated.
- Some technical claims need clarification or correction; theoretical exposition of Theorem 1 is incomplete.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and largely qualitative; lacks comparisons and quantitative metrics.
- Scalability, hyperparameter selection, and robustness are not systematically addressed.

Overall recommendation
Given the current form—promising framework but with limited novelty positioning and insufficient quantitative validation—the paper does not yet meet the bar for NeurIPS. A strengthened version with rigorous clarifications and comprehensive experiments would be compelling. If a rating is required: weak reject.
