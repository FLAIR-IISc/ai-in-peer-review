## Peer Review: Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis

**Summary:**

This paper extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to operate within Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHSs), aiming to improve the approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions compared to traditional DMD which relies on linear monomials. The authors present an algorithm for modal decomposition based on the Krylov subspace in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  They also address potential ill-conditioning issues by incorporating a POD-based projection step. The paper demonstrates the method on synthetic and real-world data, showcasing its application in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the landscape of spectral methods for dynamical systems. It acknowledges the limitations of POD and the strengths of DMD, highlighting DMD’s ability to reveal dynamic characteristics (growth rates, frequencies).  The work builds on the existing DMD literature (e.g., exact DMD, optimized DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD) by introducing a kernel-based approach that expands the basis functions used for approximating Koopman eigenfunctions, drawing inspiration from kernel PCA. The connection to subspace identification methods is also mentioned, though acknowledged as requiring further investigation.  The referencing is generally good, though a few recent advancements in kernel-based DMD methods could be included (see weaknesses).

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Extension of DMD:** The core idea of extending DMD to RKHSs is a valuable contribution. It allows for the capture of more complex, nonlinear relationships in the data, potentially overcoming a key limitation of standard DMD.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid mathematical foundation for the proposed method, with a clear presentation of the key theorems and derivations (Theorems 1 & 2).
* **Robustness Enhancement:** The inclusion of the POD-based projection step to address potential ill-conditioning is a practical and important consideration, making the method more robust for real-world applications.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation covers both synthetic and real-world data, and explores multiple applications – prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition – demonstrating the versatility of the approach.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow the theoretical developments and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Empirical Demonstration of Improvement:** While the empirical results are encouraging, the paper lacks a strong comparative analysis demonstrating the *improvement* offered by the kernel-based DMD over existing methods (e.g., standard DMD, kernel DMD variations).  It would be valuable to quantify the performance gain in terms of prediction accuracy, change-point detection precision, or dynamics recognition rates.
* **Kernel Selection and Parameter Tuning:** The paper mentions the use of a polynomial kernel and Gaussian kernel, but lacks a discussion on how these kernels were chosen and how sensitive the results are to kernel parameters (e.g., degree for polynomial kernels, bandwidth for Gaussian kernels). A more thorough investigation of kernel selection and parameter tuning would strengthen the practical applicability of the method.
* **Computational Cost:** Kernel methods are often computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. The paper doesn't address the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, nor does it discuss any strategies for mitigating the computational burden. This is a crucial consideration for scalability.
* **Missing Related Work:** While the paper references relevant works, it misses some recent advancements in kernel-based DMD and Koopman operator learning. For example, works exploring kernel-based DMD for control or incorporating deep learning into Koopman operator estimation could be mentioned.  Specifically, papers on extended Koopman operator frameworks and kernel-based approaches for learning the observation function might be relevant.
* **Lack of Error Analysis:** The paper does not provide any theoretical error bounds or convergence analysis for the proposed algorithm. While this might be difficult, some discussion of the potential sources of error and their impact on the results would be beneficial.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound extension of DMD that has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and robustness of Koopman spectral analysis.
* The inclusion of the POD-based projection addresses a practical challenge in implementing the algorithm.
* The comprehensive empirical evaluation demonstrates the versatility of the method across multiple applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a rigorous comparative analysis makes it difficult to assess the true benefits of the proposed method over existing approaches.
* The absence of discussion on computational cost and kernel selection limits the practical applicability of the method.
* The limited error analysis leaves some questions regarding the robustness and reliability of the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising extension of DMD with the potential to advance the field of Koopman spectral analysis. While the theoretical foundation is strong and the empirical evaluation is comprehensive, the lack of a thorough comparative analysis and discussion of practical considerations (computational cost, kernel selection) weaken the overall impact.  

**Recommendation:**

**Weak Accept.**  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should:

1.  **Conduct a comparative analysis:**  Compare the performance of their method against standard DMD and other relevant kernel-based DMD techniques on the same datasets, using appropriate metrics.
2.  **Discuss kernel selection and parameter tuning:**  Provide a more detailed discussion on the choices made for the kernels and their parameters, and investigate the sensitivity of the results to these choices.
3.  **Address computational cost:**  Discuss the computational complexity of the algorithm and explore potential strategies for improving scalability.
4. **Update Related Work:** Include recent advancements in kernel-based DMD methods.
5. **Consider an error analysis:** Add a discussion on potential sources of error and their implications.
