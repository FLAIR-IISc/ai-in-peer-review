- Proposes kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis with a POD-based robustification.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 provide a solid theoretical connection between the kernel approach and Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Robustification using POD bases addresses DMD's numerical instability and improves practical applicability.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic examples (toy nonlinear system and Hénon map) and applied to prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Empirical validation is limited to simple synthetic datasets and lacks experiments on complex or real-world data.  
- No thorough comparison to Extended DMD or other kernel-based methods, leaving relative accuracy and efficiency unclear.  
- Computational complexity and scalability (e.g., O(n^3) Gram-matrix costs) are not adequately discussed.  
- Applications are underdeveloped; empirical results do not convincingly show advantages over standard DMD or baseline methods.  
- Insufficient exploration of kernel choice and hyperparameter sensitivity; kernel selection strategy is missing.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents concepts and algorithms logically.  
- Novelty is incremental: extends DMD to RKHS but needs clearer differentiation from existing kernel-based dynamical methods.  
- Potentially significant for nonlinear dynamics if validated on larger, more complex datasets.  
- Reviewer recommends weak acceptance pending stronger empirical validation, complexity discussion, and kernel-selection guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: compare with Extended DMD, include scalability/runtime analysis, provide kernel-selection guidance, add real-world experiments, and discuss limitations.
