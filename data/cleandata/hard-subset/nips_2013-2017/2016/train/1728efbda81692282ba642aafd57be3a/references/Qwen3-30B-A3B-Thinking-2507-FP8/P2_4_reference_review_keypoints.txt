- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis, using Gram–Schmidt in RKHS and POD robustification.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound (Theorems 1 and 2) and connect well to Koopman spectral theory.  
- POD robustification is a practical remedy for DMD’s known ill-conditioning.  
- Empirical results on synthetic systems (toy, Hénon) and real locomotion data demonstrate the method’s capabilities.  
- Lacks comprehensive quantitative comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Extended DMD) and standard benchmarks.  
- Implementation details (kernel parameter selection, computational complexity, scalability) are superficial, limiting reproducibility.  
- Theoretical analysis does not address how kernel choice impacts the quality of Koopman eigenfunction approximation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; mathematical notation and algorithm presentation are clear, though the pre-image problem explanation needs more detail.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension over Extended DMD by using kernel-defined, nonlinear, data-adaptive bases rather than predetermined basis functions.  
- Significance: addresses DMD’s local-linear limitation and has relevant applications, but lacks convincing evidence of outperforming existing methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and valuable contribution; recommendation—Weak Accept.  
- Requested minor revisions: add quantitative comparisons with Extended DMD and others, discuss computational complexity/scalability, provide kernel parameter selection and sensitivity analysis, and clarify connection between kernel choice and eigenfunction quality.
