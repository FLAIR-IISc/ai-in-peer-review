- Proposes a kernelized DMD variant for Koopman analysis using RKHS lifting, Krylov matrix construction, QR/Ritz eigenanalysis, and kernel-PCA truncation.
- Theorems show finite-dimensional projection exactness when Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the span of selected kernel principal directions.
- The approach largely overlaps with EDMD/KDMD; KDMD already provides a kernelized EDMD formulation addressing similar goals.
- The Arnoldi/Ritz perspective is a useful conceptual lens but does not appear to yield materially new algorithms beyond KDMD.
- Strength: clear operator-theoretic framing linking Krylov/Ritz methods to Koopman spectral analysis.
- Strength: kernelization enables expressive implicit feature maps computed via Gram matrices.
- Strength: kernel-PCA robustification is practically motivated and mitigates ill-conditioning like POD/SVD truncation in DMD.
- Strength: suggested applications include short-term prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to KDMD; the “robustified” algorithm and truncation mirror existing KDMD/EDMD approaches.
- Weakness: some claims are imprecise or overstated (e.g., eigenvalues always on unit circle; vacuous recovery statements).
- Weakness: the proposed change-point detection model (nonparametric exponential family) lacks justification, normalization details, and theoretical grounding.
- Weakness: no analysis of consistency, approximation error, sample complexity, or formal noise robustness and regularization strategies.
- Clarity issue: confusing notation—φ used for both Koopman eigenfunctions and feature-space vectors—and unclear exposition of Koopman acting on vector-valued feature maps.
- Proofs are algebraically correct but would benefit from explicit assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, finite Krylov rank, noiseless data).
- Implementation details are compressed: kernel/bandwidth choice, truncation rule p, regularization, and computational complexity are insufficiently discussed.
- Originality concern: paper should explicitly derive equivalence/differences between its estimator and standard KDMD to clarify novelty.
- Empirical evaluation is minimal and qualitative: lacks quantitative benchmarks, comparisons to EDMD/KDMD/exact DMD, noise studies, and ablations.
- Suggestion: provide precise KDMD comparison, correct dynamical assumptions, expand experiments with baselines/noise, and justify or remove the change-point model.
- Suggestion: discuss regularization for near-singular Gram matrices and provide computational complexity and scaling considerations.
- Pros for acceptance: clear operator-theoretic framing, useful exposition/unification, and potentially broad applicability.
- Cons against acceptance: limited novelty, weak empirical validation, technical inaccuracies, and missing theoretical/regularization analysis.
- Recommendation: weak reject pending clearer positioning relative to prior work, stronger theory or practical advantages, and substantially more rigorous experiments.
