- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Uses Krylov subspace-based eigendecomposition in feature space and presents two supporting theorems for the method.  
- Introduces a POD-based robustification to mitigate ill-conditioning in kernel DMD computations.  
- Demonstrates method on a toy system and the Henon map and explores prediction, dynamic recognition, and change-point detection applications.  
- Positions work relative to DMD/Koopman literature, POD, exact DMD, and kernel methods, claiming operations directly in functional space can recover full Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Novelty: applying RKHS/kernel methods to DMD addresses linear approximation limits of standard DMD.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivations and theorems provide rigorous justification for the approach.  
- Practical inclusion of POD is a valuable step toward numerical stability.  
- Applications showcase potential utility beyond theory, indicating multiple use cases.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clearly written.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simple systems; lacks complex or real-world datasets and comparative baselines.  
- Kernel selection appears arbitrary (degree-3 polynomial, Gaussian width=1) with no sensitivity analysis or guidance on tuning.  
- Computational cost and scalability for high-dimensional data and large datasets are not adequately addressed.  
- Impact of POD truncation on performance and accuracy is not explored.  
- Application sections are superficial and lack benchmarking against established methods (e.g., change-point detection baselines).  
- Insufficient discussion of how to choose kernels or how kernel parameters affect results.  
- Limited connection and comparison to existing kernel-based dynamical system methods (e.g., kernel state-space models).  
- Pro-accept: novel, theoretically sound extension with practical robustification and promising applications.  
- Con-accept: lacks convincing empirical evidence, scalability analysis, deeper application benchmarking, and kernel selection discussion.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires expanded experiments, sensitivity analyses, scalability discussion, and stronger comparisons.  
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions—expand empirical evaluation, perform kernel sensitivity and scalability analyses, deepen application benchmarking, and better situate within kernel-based methods.
