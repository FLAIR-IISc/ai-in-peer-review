

# Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors extend traditional DMD, which approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using linear monomials of observables (equivalent to a one-term Taylor expansion), by employing kernel functions to obtain richer, nonlinear basis functions. The core contribution is a modal decomposition algorithm based on the equivalent principle of DMD but operating in RKHS, with a robustified variant using POD bases to address numerical instability.

The paper relates to previous work by building upon the Koopman operator framework [16] and DMD [25, 26], while connecting to related techniques like Extended DMD [35], which also uses pre-determined basis functions. The authors correctly acknowledge that their approach shares conceptual similarities with Extended DMD but differs in its use of kernel functions to generate nonlinear basis functions rather than relying on pre-specified functional maps.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical foundations with two key theorems establishing the equivalence between the kernel-based approach and DMD in RKHS. The robustification via POD bases (Theorem 2) is well-motivated and addresses a known practical limitation of DMD. The mathematical derivations are clear and well-justified.

2. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-structured, with a logical flow from background to theory to applications. The theoretical contributions are clearly presented, and the empirical examples are well-designed to demonstrate the method's capabilities on both synthetic (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world data (locomotion patterns, change-point detection).

3. **Novelty:** While extending DMD with kernel methods is not entirely novel (kernel DMD was briefly mentioned in [7]), the specific formulation in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis provides a theoretically grounded approach that differs from previous DMD variants. The paper clearly articulates how their method differs from Extended DMD and other related techniques.

4. **Significance:** The proposed method enables more flexible approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions, which can be valuable for analyzing complex nonlinear dynamics. The applications section demonstrates practical utility in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, with empirical results showing competitive performance compared to existing methods.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Contribution:** While the theoretical extension is sound, the core approach (using kernels to generate basis functions) is conceptually similar to Extended DMD [35], which uses pre-determined basis functions. The paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison with Extended DMD and a clearer articulation of how the kernel-based approach provides advantages in specific scenarios.

2. **Empirical Comparison:** The empirical results, while demonstrating feasibility, lack comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods. For instance, the change-point detection results are compared to a kernel change detection method but not to other state-of-the-art change-point detection techniques. A more thorough comparison would strengthen the significance claim.

3. **Practical Implementation Details:** The paper mentions that the algorithm can be ill-conditioned but does not provide guidance on how to select the kernel parameters (e.g., kernel width for Gaussian kernel) in practice, which is crucial for real-world applications.

4. **Theoretical Limitations:** The theoretical guarantees assume that the data is sufficiently rich to span the relevant subspaces. The paper does not thoroughly discuss the conditions under which this assumption holds or how to verify it in practice.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Theoretical foundation is sound with clear theorems
- Well-organized and clearly written
- Demonstrates practical utility across multiple applications
- Addresses a known limitation of DMD through POD-based robustification
- Provides a theoretically grounded extension of DMD using kernel methods

*Con:*
- Core contribution is somewhat incremental compared to Extended DMD
- Limited empirical comparison with state-of-the-art methods
- Insufficient discussion of practical implementation considerations
- Theoretical guarantees depend on strong assumptions about data richness

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a technically sound extension of DMD using reproducing kernels for Koopman spectral analysis. The theoretical contributions are well-founded, and the empirical results demonstrate the method's applicability to several real-world problems. While the core idea builds upon existing work (particularly Extended DMD), the specific formulation in RKHS provides a theoretically grounded approach that could be valuable for practitioners dealing with complex nonlinear dynamics.

The paper advances the state of the art by providing a more flexible basis for Koopman eigenfunction approximation, which could be particularly useful when linear approximations are insufficient. However, the significance would be stronger with more comprehensive empirical comparisons and clearer articulation of advantages over existing methods.

The paper meets NIPS scope as it bridges machine learning (kernel methods) and dynamical systems analysis, addressing a problem of significant interest in both fields. The work is well-executed, technically sound, and demonstrates potential for practical applications, making it a suitable contribution for the NIPS conference.
