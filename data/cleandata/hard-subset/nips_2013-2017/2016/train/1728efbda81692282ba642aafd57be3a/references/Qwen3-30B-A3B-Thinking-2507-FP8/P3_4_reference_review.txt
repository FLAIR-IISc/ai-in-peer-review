

# Peer Review: Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis

## Summary
This paper proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors extend standard DMD, which uses linear monomials as basis functions, to employ reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for more expressive nonlinear basis functions. They present a modal decomposition algorithm based on Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in RKHS and provide a robustified version using POD projections. The method is applied to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and motion capture datasets.

## Strengths
1. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides solid mathematical grounding with clear theorems (Theorems 1 and 2) that establish the theoretical connections between the proposed method and Koopman spectral analysis. The mathematical formulation is precise and well-structured.

2. **Problem Relevance**: The authors correctly identify a key limitation of standard DMD (using linear monomials) and propose a more expressive approach using kernel methods, which is relevant to the growing interest in Koopman operator theory for nonlinear dynamical systems.

3. **Well-Organized Structure**: The paper is clearly written and logically organized, with proper connections between the theoretical background, proposed method, and applications.

## Weaknesses and Concerns

1. **Limited Novelty**: The proposed method is largely a special case of extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015), where the basis functions are generated by kernel functions rather than pre-specified ones. The paper mentions extended DMD in Section 5 but fails to clearly articulate how their method differs in terms of mathematical formulation or practical advantages. This significantly weakens the originality claim.

2. **Insufficient Empirical Evaluation**: The empirical validation is limited to a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and one motion capture dataset. Crucially, the paper lacks:
   - Comparison with state-of-the-art methods (including standard DMD and extended DMD)
   - Quantitative metrics for the applications (prediction accuracy, change-point detection performance)
   - Analysis of how kernel parameters affect performance
   - Demonstration of scalability to larger datasets

3. **Theoretical Gaps**: The paper lacks theoretical guarantees about:
   - Approximation error bounds compared to standard DMD
   - Convergence properties as the dataset size increases
   - Conditions under which the kernel-based approach outperforms linear basis functions

4. **Practical Concerns**: The computational complexity of the kernel-based approach is not discussed, which is a significant drawback for real-world applications. The paper mentions ill-conditioning issues but doesn't provide clear guidance on handling them in practice.

## Comparison to Related Work
The paper mentions extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015) but fails to properly distinguish it from their proposed method. In fact, the kernel DMD is a specific instance of extended DMD where the basis functions are derived from a kernel function. The authors could have strengthened their contribution by:
1. Explicitly showing how their method generalizes extended DMD
2. Providing theoretical conditions under which kernel-based bases outperform linear monomials
3. Demonstrating concrete advantages on real-world problems where linear bases fail

## Significance Assessment
While the problem of Koopman spectral analysis is important, the paper's contribution doesn't advance the state-of-the-art sufficiently. The proposed method appears to be a straightforward application of kernel methods to an existing framework (DMD), without compelling evidence that it outperforms simpler alternatives. The applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are interesting but lack rigorous evaluation against existing methods.

## Recommendations
1. **Major Revision Required**: The paper needs substantial revisions to address the novelty gap and provide comprehensive empirical validation.

2. **Key Revisions Needed**:
   - Clearly articulate how the proposed method differs from and improves upon extended DMD
   - Add comprehensive comparisons with standard DMD, extended DMD, and other relevant methods
   - Include theoretical analysis of approximation error and computational complexity
   - Provide detailed empirical results with quantitative metrics across multiple datasets
   - Discuss practical considerations for hyperparameter selection and scalability

## Final Assessment
The paper presents a theoretically sound extension of DMD using kernel methods, but its contribution is not sufficiently novel or impactful for NIPS. The empirical validation is inadequate to demonstrate clear advantages over existing approaches, and the connection to extended DMD is not properly established. While the mathematical formulation is strong, the paper lacks the empirical rigor and clear differentiation from prior work needed for acceptance at NIPS.

**Recommendation: Reject**

*Note: With significant revision addressing the weaknesses identified above, this paper could be a strong candidate for future submission.*
