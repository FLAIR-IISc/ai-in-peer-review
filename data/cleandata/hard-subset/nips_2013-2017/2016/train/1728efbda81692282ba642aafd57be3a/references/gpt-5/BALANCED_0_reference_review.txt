Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The main idea is to view the feature map ϕ(x) as the observable and to approximate the Koopman operator via a Krylov subspace in feature space. The authors give a DMD-like algorithm based on Gram–Schmidt/QR in RKHS that yields empirical Ritz values/vectors (Theorem 1), and then a numerically robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions (akin to POD truncation in DMD), leading to a finite-dimensional operator F̂ whose eigendecomposition approximates Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions (Theorem 2). They illustrate on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map, and sketch three applications: short-term prediction via pre-image reconstruction, recognition via kernel principal angles between subspaces spanned by estimated eigenfunctions, and sequential change-point detection using a CUSUM criterion built from kernelized predictions.

The paper situates itself with DMD and the Koopman operator literature, citing exact/optimized DMD and extended DMD (EDMD). However, the core algorithm appears essentially equivalent to kernel EDMD (“KDMD”) as introduced by Williams, Kevrekidis, and Rowley (J. Nonlinear Sci. 2015) and subsequent works (e.g., Klus et al., Korda & Mezić), where a kernel trick is used to form Gram matrices, truncate via SVD/POD in feature space, and compute a projected Koopman operator whose eigenpairs approximate Koopman spectra. The robust variant based on kernel PCA, the use of Gram matrices, and the ability to use non-consecutive snapshot pairs all mirror KDMD. The applications section overlaps with established uses of Koopman/DMD features (prediction, classification/recognition via subspaces, and change detection), though the specific pre-image and CUSUM constructions are useful engineering details.

Quality:
- The derivations for the RKHS-Arnoldi/DMD equivalence and the projected operator in the POD basis are sound and align with known results in EDMD/KDMD. Theorems 1 and 2 formalize standard properties (Ritz approximations; Galerkin projection yielding eigenpairs).
- Important caveats are not fully addressed: conditions under which Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the closure of the chosen RKHS/dictionary; consistency/convergence as the number of samples grows; handling of noise/regularization (e.g., Tikhonov for ill-conditioned Gram matrices); and the statement “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle” is inaccurate in general (unit-modulus holds for measure-preserving dynamics; dissipative systems typically have |λ| ≤ 1 with strict decay off periodic components).
- Experiments are limited: qualitative eigenvalue plots for toy/Hénon systems; illustrative figures for mocap embedding and a change-point score. There are no quantitative metrics, no comparisons to DMD/EDMD/KDMD baselines, no sensitivity to kernel choices, no noise/stability assessments, and no runtime/complexity discussion.

Clarity:
- The high-level motivation is clear, but the manuscript has substantial notational overload and typographical issues (e.g., using ϕ both for feature map and eigenfunction-like objects; φ̃j vs φ̄j; missing indices; unintroduced symbols like Ḡ−1 in proofs). Some steps (e.g., “companion matrix” construction and Vandermonde diagonalization) could be streamlined or referenced.
- The applications section mixes methods and results without sufficient detail to reproduce (data splits, hyperparameters, exact procedures for pre-image, change-point thresholds, etc.).
- The claim of “full recovery if the Krylov subspace is sufficiently large” should be qualified; exact recovery requires that the span of snapshots is invariant and contains the eigenfunctions of interest.

Originality:
- As stated, the main algorithmic contribution overlaps strongly with KDMD/EDMD with kernels. The robust variant via kernel PCA/POD truncation, and the operator construction F̂ from centered Gram matrices, are standard in that literature. The paper does not make a clear case for what is new beyond re-deriving KDMD from an Arnoldi perspective and collecting a few applications.
- If the novelty is the RKHS-Arnoldi/QR view, this needs to be positioned explicitly against KDMD, clarifying equivalences and any advantages.

Significance:
- Kernelizing Koopman/DMD is important and widely used, but without clear novelty and with minimal empirical validation, the paper’s contributions are currently incremental. The applications are promising but need rigorous evaluation to be impactful for the NeurIPS audience.

Pros:
- Coherent RKHS-based derivation connecting Krylov/Arnoldi to Koopman analysis.
- Practical robustification via kernel PCA/POD truncation.
- Clear articulation that non-sequential snapshot pairs can be used.
- Interesting application ideas (pre-image prediction, subspace-based recognition, change detection).
- Code availability.

Cons:
- Substantial overlap with established KDMD/EDMD; novelty is unclear.
- Limited and largely qualitative experiments; no baselines or metrics.
- Some theoretical imprecision (unit-circle claim; conditions for recovery; lack of convergence/regularization discussion).
- Clarity/notation issues and missing implementation details.
- No discussion of computational complexity, scalability, or hyperparameter selection.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Precisely position the work relative to EDMD/KDMD, explicitly derive equivalences, and state any genuine differences (e.g., computational, statistical, or theoretical).
- Add convergence/consistency results or cite and adopt existing ones (e.g., EDMD convergence analyses), and include regularization (ridge) in the operator estimation.
- Provide comprehensive experiments: quantitative eigenvalue/mode recovery on standard benchmarks (e.g., Duffing, Van der Pol, cylinder wake), robustness to noise, sensitivity to kernel width/dictionary size, runtime/memory scaling. Compare against DMD, EDMD, and KDMD.
- For applications: report prediction errors vs GPDM/SSMs/EDMD; classification accuracy with subspace angles vs baselines; change-point detection ROC/ARL metrics vs kernel CUSUM, with principled thresholding.
- Clarify notation, proofs, and correct the unit-circle statement; separate feature map ϕ from Koopman eigenfunctions; detail pre-image procedure and parameter choices.
- Discuss out-of-sample evaluation (mapping new x to Koopman coordinates), and possible accelerations (Nyström, random features, online updates).

Decision: Reject. The topic is relevant and the approach is useful, but the contribution appears largely rediscovery of KDMD with limited empirical and theoretical advancement. A revised version that clearly differentiates from prior work and includes rigorous, quantitative evaluations could be competitive.
