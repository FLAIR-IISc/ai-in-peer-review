- Proposal: Perform Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS by formulating DMD in feature space using a Krylov subspace and computing Ritz values/vectors (Theorem 1), with a KPCA-based robust variant (Theorem 2).  
- Applications: Demonstrates short-term prediction (pre-image), dynamical regime recognition (kernel principal angles), and sequential change-point detection (kernelized exponential-family model).  
- Relation to prior work: Strongly related to EDMD/kernel EDMD and subspace/Arnoldi DMD; the paper does not clearly delineate theoretical or empirical differences or provide side-by-side comparisons.  
- Strength: Clear, appealing idea leveraging RKHS expressiveness; Arnoldi/Krylov viewpoint and KPCA robustification are well-motivated and practical.  
- Strength: Applications illustrate potential utility beyond spectral estimation.  
- Weakness: Novelty limited relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD; proposed algorithm essentially recovers known methods without clear distinction.  
- Weakness: Theory is algebraic with strong assumptions and lacks approximation, consistency, or error bounds; some operator-theoretic steps need tighter justification.  
- Weakness: Incorrect/unqualified claim that Koopman eigenvalues lie on the unit circle for attractor dynamics; must be corrected.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is thin and qualitative—no quantitative error metrics, robustness studies, or baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: Practical issues under-discussed (kernel choice/tuning, computational/memory costs, noise handling, online updates).  
- Weakness: Writing and presentation problems—typos, incomplete references, heavy/imprecise notation, sparse quantitative figure details, and an anonymity-compromising code link.  
- Quality assessment: Algebraic construction aligns with known DMD machinery but derivations are sketchy and statistical/numerical guarantees are missing.  
- Clarity assessment: Organization acceptable but needs cleaner assumptions, notation, and corrected statements; applications need concrete algorithmic details.  
- Originality: Moderate—KPCA robustification and RKHS framing are known; explicit DMD-as-Arnoldi in RKHS is the main added perspective.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful if substantiated, but modest in current form due to overlap with existing methods and weak evaluation.  
- Key suggestions: Explicitly relate and compare to EDMD/kernel EDMD, add quantitative benchmarks and baselines, provide kernel/regularization guidance, correct theoretical claims, tighten proofs, and fix presentation/anonymity.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: Timely topic, clear RKHS/Krylov perspective, and practical robustification consistent with DMD practice.  
- Anti-acceptance arguments: Substantial overlap with prior work, insufficient novelty, weak empirical support, technical imprecision, and anonymity violation.  
- Overall verdict: Promising direction but currently below acceptance threshold; needs stronger theory, comparisons, and experiments.
