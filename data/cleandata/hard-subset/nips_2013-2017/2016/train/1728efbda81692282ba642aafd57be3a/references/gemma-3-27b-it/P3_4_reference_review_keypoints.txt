- Overall: Interesting RKHS extension of DMD that is technically sound but lacks strong novelty and empirical validation — reviewer recommends weak accept pending stronger results and clearer contributions.
- Core idea: Use kernels/RKHS to enrich basis functions for approximating Koopman eigenfunctions and address DMD’s inability to capture highly nonlinear dynamics.
- Positioning: Paper situates itself within Koopman/DMD/POD and kernel methods but needs a more detailed comparison to Extended DMD and subspace identification.
- Theoretical strength: Mathematical derivation is correct and a theorem linking empirical Ritz values/vectors to Koopman eigenpairs is a valuable contribution.
- Motivation strength: Clear motivation that RKHS can overcome a key limitation of traditional DMD.
- Robustification: Use of POD-based projection to mitigate ill-conditioning is a practical and useful addition.
- Presentation: Mathematical notation and exposition are generally clear and accessible to knowledgeable readers.
- Novelty concern: Combining DMD with kernel methods is not entirely new; existing kernel-DMD variants reduce perceived novelty.
- Empirical weakness: Experiments are superficial (toy system and Hénon map), lack complexity/realism, and do not convincingly demonstrate advantages over alternatives.
- Kernel choice: Kernel selections (polynomial, Gaussian) appear ad-hoc with no strong justification or comparison to other kernels.
- Applications section: Prediction, embedding, and change-point detection are underdeveloped, qualitative, and lack quantitative baselines and details.
- Pre-image recovery: The prediction/pre-image scheme is briefly described and lacks specifics (which MDS algorithm, distance metrics, practical recovery steps).
- POD truncation: No principled explanation of how POD mode count is chosen or its effect on accuracy.
- Computational cost: Paper omits any analysis of computational complexity or scalability relative to standard DMD and other kernel methods.
- Clarity issues: Some proofs and sections (e.g., proof of Theorem 1) could be streamlined for readability.
- Questions for authors: Need direct comparison to Extended DMD (basis, complexity, guarantees), kernel selection rationale, POD truncation strategy, pre-image details, and scalability assessment.
- Arguments for acceptance: Sound math, practical POD robustification, and useful theoretical results justify consideration.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited novelty, weak empirical validation, underdeveloped applications, and missing complexity analysis argue against strong acceptance.
