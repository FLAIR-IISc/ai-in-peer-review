- Proposes a kernelized DMD/Koopman spectral analysis by performing DMD in an RKHS on feature-mapped snapshots treated as a Krylov sequence.  
- Introduces a “robust” variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions (POD-style truncation) to mitigate ill-conditioning and rank deficiency.  
- Provides two theorems relating computed eigenpairs to Koopman eigenpairs under assumptions.  
- Sketches applications: short-term prediction via preimage, recognition via subspace angles, and sequential change-point detection with a kernelized exponential-family view.  
- Experimental demos include a toy polynomial system, the Henon map, MDS visualization on motion-capture data, and a qualitative Lorenz-like changepoint example.  
- Strong relation to prior work: overlaps substantially with EDMD and kernel EDMD (Williams et al. 2015) and later EDMD variants (Hankel/time-delay DMD, variational EDMD, sEDMD).  
- The paper underplays this overlap and does not clearly articulate what is fundamentally new beyond known kernel EDMD and standard DMD robustification.  
- Strength: clear motivation to enrich DMD bases using RKHS to capture nonlinear observables.  
- Strength: conceptually simple kernel-only implementation using Gram matrices without explicit basis enumeration.  
- Strength: robust variant mirrors best DMD practice (truncation) and useful algebra connecting reduced operators to Gram matrices.  
- Strength: theorems attempt to clarify when eigenpair recovery is possible.  
- Strength: interesting suggested downstream uses (prediction, recognition, change detection).  
- Weakness: novelty is limited given existing kernel EDMD literature and related EDMD variants.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 contains inaccuracies—uses an invalid inversion of a rectangular Mτ and omits explicit projected dynamics with QτQτ* M+.  
- Weakness: recovery claims (e.g., linear dependence implying full recovery) are too strong; correct recovery needs invariant subspace inclusion and mode excitation.  
- Weakness: centering (kernel PCA) changes the feature subspace and its effect on Koopman approximation/consistency is not justified; uses Ḡ−1 without addressing rank deficiency/pseudoinverses.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 assumes Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the kernel PCA subspace, a strong and practically unjustified assumption.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited, qualitative, and lack comparisons to EDMD/kernel EDMD, exact DMD, Hankel-DMD, or other baselines.  
- Weakness: no quantitative metrics for recognition or change detection (no ARL, detection delay, false alarm rates), and no classification accuracy reported.  
- Weakness: chaotic-system interpretation is problematic—Koopman spectra can be continuous, so global “equilibrium eigenvalues” are dubious.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis to kernel choice/width, truncation level p, trajectory length, or complexity analysis.  
- Clarity issues: overloaded/inconsistent notation (ϕ vs φ), conflation of Hermitian transpose and RKHS inner product, compressed derivations, and missing rank/eigenvalue/pseudoinverse conditions.  
- References are incomplete/inconsistently formatted and miss several key related works (kernel EDMD variants, variational EDMD, Hankel-DMD connections).  
- Overall quality: technically plausible but with theoretical errors, overstatements, and insufficient empirical support.  
- Originality: limited—essentially EDMD with implicit kernel mapping and POD truncation; lacks clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Significance: potentially useful in practice, but incremental given prior literature and weakened by weak experiments and theory.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical kernelized DMD recipe via Gram matrices, attempts to connect reduced operators to Koopman spectra, and topic relevance to the community.  
- Cons for acceptance: substantial overlap with prior work, theoretical inaccuracies and unjustified claims, weak qualitative experiments, and presentation/reference issues.  
- Key suggestions: explicitly position relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD, correct proofs using projected dynamics and pseudoinverses, justify or avoid centering, provide convergence/identifiability analysis, and add comprehensive quantitative experiments and baselines.  
- Recommendation: Reject (weak) — topic important but novelty, theory, and empirical evaluation are insufficient for acceptance.
