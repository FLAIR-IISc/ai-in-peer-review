- Proposes kernelized DMD in RKHS using a Krylov/Arnoldi-style derivation and Gram-matrix computations to estimate Koopman spectral objects.  
- Robustifies the method by projecting onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to mitigate ill-conditioning and gives corresponding matrix formulas.  
- Claims recovery of Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions when the feature span is rich and the subspace is large; shows toy examples and sketches applications (prediction via preimage, pattern recognition via subspace angles, change-point detection).  
- Closely related to EDMD/KDMD; the robustified algorithm is similar in spirit and computation to EDMD/KDMD with SVD truncation, with limited empirical comparison to related DMD variants.  
- Strength: Clear operator-theoretic RKHS-Krylov framing that connects DMD/Arnoldi to kernel methods.  
- Strength: Practical stabilization via kernel-PCA/POD and useful Gram-matrix implementation details.  
- Strength: Broad, actionable applications proposed and computational accessibility using only Gram matrices and eigendecompositions.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to EDMD/KDMD — mostly an alternative derivation/packaging rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on strong assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, Koopman eigenfunctions in finite feature span) with no convergence/consistency guarantees.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is minimal (two small examples, qualitative only) with no quantitative error metrics, baselines, ablations, or sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: Some inaccurate/confusing statements (e.g., “Koopman eigenvalues on attractors lie on the unit circle” is not generally true).  
- Weakness: Scalability and regularization (Nyström, streaming updates, Tikhonov/ridge) are not discussed despite cubic time / quadratic memory costs.  
- Assessment: Mathematically sound for finite projections but limited guarantees and no convincing empirical advantage; clear but dense presentation with sparse implementation/hyperparameter guidance.  
- Suggestions: Explicitly position vs EDMD/KDMD, provide quantitative comparisons and baselines, test regularization and scalability remedies, correct inaccurate claims, and evaluate preimage/prediction methods quantitatively.  
- Pros for acceptance: Unified, practical presentation of kernel DMD with stabilization and interesting downstream applications.  
- Cons for acceptance: Substantial overlap with prior work, weak empirical validation, and clarity/technical issues.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject; improve empirical validation, sharpen positioning versus prior art, and add stronger theory or demonstrable performance gains.
