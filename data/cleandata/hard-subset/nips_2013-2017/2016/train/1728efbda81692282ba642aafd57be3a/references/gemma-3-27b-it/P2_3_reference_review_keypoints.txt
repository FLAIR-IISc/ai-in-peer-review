- Summary: Extends DMD into RKHS using Krylov subspace projections and a POD step to improve Koopman spectral approximation for nonlinear dynamics, demonstrated on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: Addresses DMD’s linear limitation by using kernel methods to capture more complex dynamics.  
- Strength: Presents solid theoretical foundation connecting the RKHS extension to traditional DMD, including a supporting theorem.  
- Strength: Incorporates a POD-based projection to mitigate ill-conditioning and acknowledges practical implementation issues.  
- Strength: Demonstrates applicability in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on examples.  
- Strength: Situates the work clearly within existing literature on spectral methods, DMD, and kernels.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art nonlinear system ID methods (e.g., neural ODEs, SINDy).  
- Weakness: Does not adequately analyze sensitivity to kernel choice and kernel hyperparameters (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth).  
- Weakness: Fails to discuss computational complexity and scalability of the RKHS approach for large datasets.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s assumption that Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the POD span is restrictive and not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: No ablation study to separate the benefits of the kernel approach from the POD projection alone.  
- Qualitative: Paper is technically sound, well-written, and largely clear, but limited comparative evaluation weakens impact.  
- Originality/Significance: Novel RKHS extension of DMD with moderate potential impact if robustness and scalability are demonstrated.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel extension, strong theory, and demonstrated practical potential.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited experiments and baselines, missing discussion of complexity and kernel sensitivity, and restrictive theorem assumption.  
- Overall assessment: Borderline accept contingent on addressing empirical comparisons, kernel sensitivity analysis, and scalability discussion.
