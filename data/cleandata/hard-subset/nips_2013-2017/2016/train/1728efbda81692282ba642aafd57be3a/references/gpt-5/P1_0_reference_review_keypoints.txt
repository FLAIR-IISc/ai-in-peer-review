- Proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by lifting data to an RKHS and projecting the Koopman operator onto a Krylov subspace to compute Ritz values/vectors.
- Introduces a robust variant that projects onto truncated kernel PCA directions to avoid ill-conditioned Gram-Schmidt in RKHS, yielding a finite-dimensional operator expressed via Gram matrices.
- Provides theoretical connections (Theorems 1–2) relating the RKHS Arnoldi/QR construction to modal expansions and Koopman eigenfunctions under assumptions.
- Demonstrates the method on a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map and sketches applications: short-term prediction, dynamics recognition, and sequential change-point detection.
- Strong relation to EDMD and KDMD; many derived matrices and algebraic forms overlap with existing KDMD formulations.
- The robust kernel PCA truncation is algebraically similar to KDMD and should be positioned more explicitly relative to prior work.
- Clear motivation: kernelization addresses limitations of linear observables in DMD without handcrafting dictionaries.
- Conceptually simple: kernel trick replaces inner products and kernel PCA truncation is a natural robustification.
- Theoretical framing is useful: Ritz/vector expansions in RKHS and links to Koopman eigenfunctions help ground the approach.
- Broad potential applications beyond modal analysis, relevant to NeurIPS audience.
- Some derivations are informal or rely on strong/unclear assumptions; e.g., Arnoldi/companion matrix steps need rigorous confinement to projected subspace.
- Notation is confusing: mixing scalar Koopman eigenfunctions with feature-space elements and vector-valued “modes” needs clarification.
- Incorrect or overstated claims: e.g., Koopman eigenvalues lying on the unit circle is not generally true for dissipative systems.
- Theorem 2 assumes kernel PCA spans relevant eigenfunctions—a strong assumption lacking discussion of when it holds (ergodicity, kernel universality, sampling).
- Change-point detection probabilistic model is nonstandard, drops terms without justification, and lacks statistical guarantees.
- Experiments are limited and qualitative: no quantitative errors, no comparisons to EDMD/KDMD/DMD, no noise/robustness studies.
- Application sketches are proof-of-concept with heuristic methods (pre-image, MDS) and no performance metrics or baselines.
- Paper has clarity issues: typos, incomplete references, missing algorithmic details (regularization, rank selection, kernel choices).
- Centering via kernel PCA may remove constant modes; the effect on Koopman eigenvalue 1 and centering rationale are not discussed.
- Core contribution overlaps substantially with EDMD/KDMD; novelty claim should be narrowed and connections made precise.
- Current impact is limited by theoretical imprecision and insufficient empirical validation, though the pipeline could be useful with revisions.
- Questions for authors: clarify exact relationship to EDMD/KDMD and effect of centering.
- Questions for authors: explain truncation rank selection and regularization strategies and sensitivity to kernel parameters.
- Questions for authors: provide quantitative comparisons (eigenvalue/prediction error) against DMD/EDMD/KDMD on standard benchmarks with/without noise.
- Questions for authors: formalize the probabilistic change-detection model and provide detection metrics and baselines.
- Questions for authors: state computational complexity and memory footprint as functions of trajectory length τ and data dimension n.
- Pros for acceptance: clear problem framing, RKHS Ritz connections, practical robustification via kernel PCA, and broad applicability.
- Cons for acceptance: significant overlap with prior KDMD/EDMD, theoretical imprecision, insufficient experiments, and under-evaluated application sections.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage revision with tighter theory, clearer positioning relative to EDMD/KDMD, and stronger quantitative experiments.
- Reviewer confidence: high on connections to EDMD/KDMD; medium on specific theoretical claims due to presentation ambiguities.
