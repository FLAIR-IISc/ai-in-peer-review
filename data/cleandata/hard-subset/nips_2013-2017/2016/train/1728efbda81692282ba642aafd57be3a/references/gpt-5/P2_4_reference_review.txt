Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a kernelized variant of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis. It formulates DMD in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) by constructing a Krylov subspace from feature-mapped snapshots ϕ(x0), …, ϕ(xτ−1) and computing Ritz values/vectors via a Gram–Schmidt QR factorization in feature space and an eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator. To improve numerical stability (as in POD-projected/“exact” DMD), the paper proposes a robust version that projects data onto kernel PCA directions before forming the reduced operator. Theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) parallel standard DMD statements, showing an expansion of the feature-mapped trajectory in terms of empirical Ritz vectors and connecting the reduced operator’s eigensystem to Koopman eigenfunctions under representer assumptions. Applications include short-term prediction via a kernel pre-image heuristic, embedding/recognition of dynamics via kernel principal angles between subspaces spanned by estimated Koopman eigenfunctions, and a CUSUM-style change-point detector that uses inner products implied by the kernel model. Experiments on a toy system, the Hénon map, CMU mocap sequences, and a Lorenz-map change-detection example are presented.

This work is closely related to Extended DMD (EDMD; Williams et al., 2015), which uses prescribed dictionaries to approximate the Koopman operator, and to POD/“exact” DMD variants that project onto low-rank subspaces. The robust kernel method here is, in spirit, EDMD with an implicit (potentially very large) dictionary realized via kernels, and a data-driven reduced basis via kernel PCA. It also connects to kernel transfer operator literature (e.g., kernel Koopman/Perron–Frobenius operators and conditional mean embeddings), which similarly approximate linear operators in RKHS.

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: recasts DMD in RKHS with a clean Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint and gives an implementable kernel algorithm using only Gram matrices.
- Robustification via kernel PCA is practical and mirrors best practice in (exact/POD) DMD, now in a kernel setting; the derivation of the reduced operator in terms of centered Gram matrices is useful.
- The theoretical statements, while largely adaptations of standard DMD results, help ground the method and clarify assumptions under which Koopman eigenfunctions are recovered.
- Applications beyond pure spectral estimation (prediction via preimage, dynamic pattern recognition with subspace distances, and change detection) broaden potential impact.

Weaknesses
- Originality/positioning: The relation to EDMD and kernelized variants is underdeveloped. Using kernels to realize large (implicit) dictionaries and projecting onto data-driven bases is the core idea in KDMD/EDMD-with-kernels; closely related kernel transfer operator work is not cited or contrasted. The contribution risks appearing incremental without a clear differentiation or unifying perspective.
- Theoretical gaps: Theorems mainly restate finite-dimensional DMD properties in feature space; there is no analysis of approximation error, consistency, or conditions under which the kernel PCA subspace captures Koopman eigenfunctions (beyond strong representer assumptions). The use of inverses/pseudoinverses (e.g., Ḡ−1) needs regularization treatment and clearer conditions.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and mostly qualitative. There are no systematic comparisons against exact DMD, EDMD, optimized DMD, or kernel transfer operators on canonical benchmarks, no sensitivity analyses to kernel/hyperparameters, and no quantitative metrics (eigenvalue/mode error, prediction error vs horizon, change-detection ROC).
- Practical aspects: scalability and computational complexity (O(τ^2) memory and O(τ^3) time in τ snapshots) are not discussed; no guidance on kernel/hyperparameter selection; the preimage step is heuristic and may be brittle in high dimensions.

Quality
- The core algorithm is technically sound as a kernelization of DMD/POD-DMD; the Gram-matrix formulations are correct in spirit. Some derivations could be more precise about projections/residuals and the role of centering and regularization. Claims about “fully recovering eigenfunctions” when ψ=0 should be tempered to emphasize approximation on the data span. The applications (preimage, change detection) rely on additional assumptions/heuristics and would benefit from validation and caveats.

Clarity
- Generally readable and organized, with helpful high-level motivation. However, there are typos and occasional awkward phrasing. Proof sketches skip steps (e.g., treatment of inverses/pseudoinverses and projections). Implementation details (kernel choices, bandwidth selection, truncation ranks, normalization) are insufficient for full reproducibility. Figures are illustrative but lack quantitative evaluation.

Originality
- The kernelization of DMD and robustification via kernel PCA overlaps substantially with EDMD/KDMD ideas; the paper should explicitly position itself relative to:
  - EDMD and its kernelized/large-dictionary formulations,
  - kernel transfer operator approaches for Koopman/Perron–Frobenius operators,
  - exact/POD/optimized DMD variants.
- Novelty appears to be in the particular Krylov/QR perspective in RKHS and the collection of application recipes, rather than a fundamentally new operator-approximation framework.

Significance
- A practical, well-positioned kernel DMD could be useful to the community. However, given the overlap with existing approaches and the limited empirical evidence, the current impact is moderate. Stronger experimental comparisons and clearer positioning could raise the significance.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Please clearly differentiate your method from EDMD and kernel transfer operator methods (algorithmically and theoretically), and add the missing citations.
- Provide quantitative benchmarks vs exact DMD, EDMD (with hand-crafted and kernel dictionaries), and optimized DMD on standard nonlinear systems; report eigenvalue/mode errors, prediction errors, and runtime/memory.
- Discuss kernel/hyperparameter selection, truncation criteria, and provide regularized formulations (e.g., Tikhonov) for ill-conditioned Gram matrices.
- Clarify the preimage method’s limitations and compare against alternative preimage or direct observable prediction strategies.
- Expand on scalability (streaming/online updates, randomized/Nyström approximations).

Pros
- Clear and useful kernelized operator formulation and robust projection.
- Theoretical grounding of the reduced operator’s eigensystem.
- Interesting application ideas beyond pure spectral estimation.

Cons
- Insufficient positioning relative to EDMD/KDMD and kernel transfer operators.
- Limited, largely qualitative experiments without strong baselines.
- Some technical imprecision and missing practical guidance.

Overall recommendation
Borderline reject. The core idea is valuable, but to meet NeurIPS standards the paper should better situate its contributions within existing Koopman/EDMD/kernel operator literature and provide stronger, quantitative empirical validation with baselines, along with clearer, regularized formulations and reproducibility details.
