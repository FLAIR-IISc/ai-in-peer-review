- Proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS with two algorithms: a kernelized Arnoldi/DMD (Theorem 1) and a kPCA-projected “robustified” variant (Theorem 2).
- Claims kernels provide richer nonlinear bases than standard DMD for approximating Koopman eigenfunctions and presents applications (prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection).
- Strong relation to prior work: largely overlaps with EDMD/KDMD (Williams et al. 2015; Klus et al. 2016); robustified variant matches KDMD recipe.
- Arnoldi/Krylov framing in RKHS is a useful perspective but not substantially different in outcome from existing methods.
- Theoretical results align with standard DMD/Arnoldi derivations but rely on hand-wavy or strong assumptions that are not carefully stated.
- Key mathematical issues: invertibility and “inverse” operators in infinite-dimensional RKHS, companion/shift structure, and when assumptions (distinct Ritz values, ψ=0, φj∈Φ) hold are not justified.
- Incorrect or misleading claim: Koopman eigenvalues lying on the unit circle is true for measure-preserving systems but not for general dissipative systems.
- Kernel QR/Arnoldi approach is likely ill-conditioned; authors acknowledge this and prefer kPCA projection, which is standard.
- Practical algorithmic details are insufficient: regularization, truncation criteria, noise handling, kernel/bandwidth selection, and computational complexity are under-discussed.
- Pre-image step for prediction is treated heuristically (MDS-like) with no error analysis or comparison to alternatives (e.g., kernel ridge regression).
- Change-point detection formulation is speculative and only briefly validated.
- Experiments are minimal and qualitative: no quantitative metrics, noise/robustness studies, or comparisons to EDMD/KDMD/DMD baselines.
- Paper is readable but has grammatical issues, notational shortcuts, and unclear finite-vs-infinite-dimensional arguments; code link doesn’t compensate for methodological ambiguity.
- References are incomplete and poorly formatted; key related work (KDMD, kernel-EDMD) is missing.
- Originality is limited: core contributions overlap strongly with KDMD/EDMD; applications are incremental and under-evaluated.
- Significance is limited without stronger novelty, scalable algorithms, or compelling empirical results.
- Pros: clear operator-theoretic motivation, recognizes conditioning issues, Theorem 2 links Koopman eigenfunctions to kPCA, and shows application potential.
- Cons: substantial overlap with existing work, unclear theoretical assumptions, weak/qualitative experiments, heuristic pre-image and change-detection methods, and incomplete references.
- Suggestions: clearly position relative to EDMD/KDMD, state precise assumptions, strengthen theory, provide thorough empirical comparisons and robustness studies, evaluate pre-image strategies, and fix bibliography and writing.
- Recommendation: Reject, confidence high.
