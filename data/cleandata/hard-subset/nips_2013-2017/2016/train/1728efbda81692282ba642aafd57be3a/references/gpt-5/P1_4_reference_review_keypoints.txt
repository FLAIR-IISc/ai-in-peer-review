- Proposes kernelizing Koopman spectral analysis by applying DMD/Arnoldi in RKHS using Gram-Schmidt/QR on feature-space Krylov subspaces.  
- Introduces a robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA directions (kernel POD) analogous to truncated SVD in practical DMD.  
- Claims eigenpairs of the projected operator recover Koopman eigenpairs under strong assumptions.  
- Demonstrates method on a toy system and the Hénon map and sketches applications: preimage-based prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Method is essentially EDMD/KDMD in RKHS with an Arnoldi/QR perspective; differences from prior kernel EDMD are not clearly articulated.  
- Strength: clear, clean derivation and formalization (Theorems 1–2) of kernelized DMD via Krylov subspaces.  
- Strength: robust variant is practical, uses only Gram matrices, and scales to high-dimensional observations.  
- Strength: presents a unified view for several downstream tasks and provides explicit algorithmic steps plus code.  
- Weakness: originality is limited — largely overlaps with EDMD/KDMD; Arnoldi viewpoint is mainly a lens, not a clearly distinct algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees rely on strong, often unrealistic assumptions (e.g., eigenfunctions in Φ, distinct Ritz values, data richness) with no ergodic/consistency analysis.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is sparse and qualitative — only toy/Hénon examples, no quantitative metrics, baselines, or robustness studies.  
- Weakness: downstream applications (preimage, change detection, recognition) are underdeveloped and lack performance comparisons or rigorous evaluation.  
- Weakness: contains inaccurate/general statements (e.g., eigenvalues on attractors always on unit circle) and has dense notation, sketchy proofs, small figures, and grammar issues.  
- Quality: derivations are technically sound under assumptions, but the empirical evidence does not substantiate claimed advantages or practical guidance.  
- Clarity: readable but needs tighter notation, clearer contrast with EDMD/KDMD, complexity/stability discussion, and improved figures/captions.  
- Originality: incremental contribution — mainly a self-contained exposition and implementation rather than a novel method.  
- Significance: potentially useful as an exposition/tool, but impact limited by lack of rigorous comparisons and quantitative results.  
- Key suggestions: explicitly contrast with EDMD/KDMD, provide quantitative benchmarks and baselines, add regularization/conditioning analysis, evaluate kernel/truncation choices, and either improve preimage/change-detection methods or scope them out.  
- Pros: clear kernelized DMD reinterpretation, practical robust variant, and a unifying perspective for multiple tasks.  
- Cons: overlaps prior work, sparse/qualitative experiments, strong assumptions with limited practicality, and presentation inaccuracies.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject — promising but needs clearer differentiation from existing methods, stronger theoretical framing, and rigorous comparative experiments.
