- Overall: Conceptually interesting RKHS extension of DMD with sound theory but limited empirical validation — reviewer leans weak accept pending major revisions.  
- Summary: Extends DMD via RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear kernels; connects to Krylov subspace methods.  
- Related work gap: Related work section misses deeper discussion of kernel methods (e.g., kernel PCA) and recent kernel-based/extended DMD variants.  
- Strength — theory: Mathematical development and theorems linking estimated spectra to Koopman operator are solid.  
- Strength — principled: RKHS approach is a principled way to move beyond linear DMD for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Strength — robustness: Inclusion of POD-based projection addresses ill-conditioning in practice.  
- Strength — applications: Paper shows multiple potential uses (prediction, embedding/recognition, change-point detection).  
- Weakness — empirical novelty: Empirical examples (toy system, Hénon map) are proof-of-concept and do not convincingly outperform existing methods.  
- Weakness — comparisons: No quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art Koopman learning techniques or kernel-based DMD variants.  
- Weakness — kernel selection: Missing discussion and sensitivity analysis for kernel choice and hyperparameter tuning (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth).  
- Weakness — computational cost: Scalability and computational complexity (e.g., Gram–Schmidt in high-dim RKHS) are not addressed.  
- Weakness — applications clarity: Application sections lack detail (e.g., pre-image recovery in prediction, underuse of method’s unique capabilities in locomotion example).  
- Theorem concern: The assumption φj ∈ Φ in Theorem 2 is strong — needs relaxation or discussion of implications if it fails.  
- Experimental design question: Unclear how initial conditions were chosen and whether multiple initial conditions were tested for robustness.  
- Change-point detection: Connection between Koopman operator and stochastic formulation/log-likelihood ratio score needs clearer derivation.  
- Robustification critique: POD projection is intuitive but requires more theoretical justification or comparison with other projection methods.  
- Recommendation: Major revisions requested — strengthen empirical evaluation, add comparisons, perform kernel sensitivity analysis, analyze computational complexity, and expand application details.
