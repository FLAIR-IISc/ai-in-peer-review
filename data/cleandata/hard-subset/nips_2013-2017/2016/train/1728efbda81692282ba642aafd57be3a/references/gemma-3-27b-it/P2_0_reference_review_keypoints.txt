- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear systems.  
- Proposes a Krylov-subspace-based algorithm with POD-based robustification to address ill-conditioning.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation and a theorem formalizing the RKHS extension of DMD.  
- Demonstrates the method on synthetic examples (toy system and Hénon map) and exploratory applications (prediction, dynamic recognition, change-point detection).  
- Paper is well-written and connects clearly to existing DMD, Koopman, and kernel-method literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to two simple systems and lacks rigorous quantitative comparisons to baselines (e.g., extended DMD, sparse DMD).  
- Prediction results are qualitative and do not include clear evaluation metrics.  
- Computational cost and scalability concerns of kernel-matrix operations are not sufficiently addressed.  
- No clear guidance or sensitivity analysis for kernel choice and hyperparameter selection.  
- Applications are exploratory and do not demonstrate substantial performance gains.  
- Related-work discussion could be expanded to cover broader nonlinear system identification and ML approaches.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 6/10.  
- Overall: promising, theoretically sound contribution but limited by scarce empirical validation and practical discussion.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; encourage resubmission after more comprehensive experiments, baseline comparisons, kernel-analysis, and scalability discussion.
