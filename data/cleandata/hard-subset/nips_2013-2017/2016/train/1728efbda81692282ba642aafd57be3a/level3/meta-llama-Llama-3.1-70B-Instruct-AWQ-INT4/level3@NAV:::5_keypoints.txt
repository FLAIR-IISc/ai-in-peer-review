- Proposes KDMD: an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel-induced nonlinear bases.  
- Demonstrates KDMD on toy problems, showing it as a powerful generalization of DMD with empirical effectiveness.  
- Provides clear theoretical background on the Koopman operator and DMD and a detailed algorithm description.  
- Paper is original and significant, offering a more flexible framework for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems with applications across physics, engineering, and ML.  
- Claims are well-supported and the analysis thoroughly evaluates strengths and weaknesses of KDMD.  
- Manuscript is overly mathematical and verbose, making it difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Some sections (notably Section 4) appear redundant and could be removed or merged to streamline the text.  
- Organization, notation, and explanations need improvement for greater clarity and readability.  
- Paper would benefit from clearer, shorter explanations and more intuitive examples to aid understanding.  
- Recommend adding more visualizations and illustrative examples to demonstrate KDMD’s effectiveness.  
- Suggest discussing relations to other methods (e.g., eigensystem realization method) for broader context and comparison.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and high-quality contribution that could become highly citable after revisions to improve clarity and presentation.
