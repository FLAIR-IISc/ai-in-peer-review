

This paper proposes KDMD, a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors present a modal decomposition algorithm that approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions derived from kernel feature maps, extending DMD's linear monomial basis to more expressive nonlinear representations. The approach is mathematically rigorous and correctly generalizes DMD to RKHS, with applications demonstrated on synthetic and real-world data.

The paper demonstrates technical soundness (Quality), with a correct mathematical formulation and proper theoretical grounding. The core idea represents a meaningful extension of DMD (Originality), building on Koopman operator theory and DMD literature. However, the presentation severely undermines the paper's impact. The manuscript is overly verbose and mathematically dense, with unnecessary repetition (e.g., Section 4 is largely redundant with the introduction), making it difficult to follow. The writing lacks pedagogical clarity expected at NeurIPS, and key sections (Sections 5 and 6) are inadequately developed—Section 5 should include examples or be summarized in the conclusion, while Section 6 is overly condensed.

Notably, the "nonlinear" toy case appears linear, and the first two test problems use polynomial kernels that don't fully showcase KDMD's potential advantage over linear DMD. The authors could strengthen significance by elaborating on kernel selection strategies (as mentioned in Section 5) to demonstrate when KDMD provides clear advantages.

The paper would be highly citable with a more accessible presentation. The authors should substantially rewrite to clarify the contributions, streamline mathematical exposition, and better illustrate the method's utility. While the technical content is sound, the current presentation fails to communicate the value effectively to the NeurIPS audience.

Pro: Solid theoretical foundation, meaningful extension of DMD, relevant applications. Con: Poor clarity, excessive verbosity, underdeveloped sections, and inadequate demonstration of the method's advantages. With a pedagogical rewrite, this work would significantly advance Koopman spectral analysis in machine learning.
