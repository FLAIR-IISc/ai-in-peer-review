- Proposes KDMD, a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm that approximates Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear kernel feature maps.  
- Mathematically rigorous and correctly generalizes DMD to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing relevant applications.  
- Technically sound with correct formulation and proper theoretical grounding.  
- Represents a meaningful and original extension of DMD building on Koopman operator theory.  
- Presentation is overly verbose, mathematically dense, and contains unnecessary repetition (e.g., Section 4).  
- Lacks the pedagogical clarity expected at NeurIPS, making the manuscript difficult to follow.  
- Sections 5 and 6 are underdeveloped—Section 5 needs examples or a summary in the conclusion; Section 6 is too condensed.  
- Experiments do not convincingly showcase KDMD’s advantage: the “nonlinear” toy seems linear and early tests use polynomial kernels similar to linear DMD.  
- Needs clearer kernel selection strategies to demonstrate when KDMD outperforms linear DMD.  
- With a substantial rewrite to streamline exposition and better illustrate utility, the paper would be highly citable and could significantly advance Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Pro: solid theoretical foundation, meaningful extension, relevant applications; Con: poor clarity, excessive verbosity, underdeveloped sections, and inadequate demonstration of advantages.
