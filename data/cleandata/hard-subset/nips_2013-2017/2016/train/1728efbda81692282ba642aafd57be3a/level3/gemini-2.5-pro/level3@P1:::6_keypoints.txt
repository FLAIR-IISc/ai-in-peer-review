- Summary: Proposes a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS that generalizes DMD by lifting states to a feature space and extracting Koopman modes/eigenfunctions via eigendecomposition; demonstrates applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Strength: Paper is exceptionally clear and pedagogical, with a logical presentation linking Koopman operator, DMD, and RKHS.

- Strength: Offers a principled, theoretically sound generalization of DMD using RKHS, enabling richer approximations of Koopman eigenfunctions than linear observables.

- Strength: Presents promising applications as a feature extraction tool for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, broadening DMD’s appeal to ML.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks crucial baselines; no direct comparisons to standard DMD or Extended DMD to justify kernel complexity.

- Weakness: Applications are proof-of-concept without rigorous quantitative comparisons (e.g., against kernel PCA embeddings or state-of-the-art prediction/change-point methods).

- Weakness: Theoretical novelty is incremental—kernelizing a linearization idea follows well-known ML patterns (kernel PCA/SVM) rather than a fundamental breakthrough.

- Weakness: Practical relevance to ML is unclear: framework and experiments are deterministic despite many real-world dynamics being stochastic.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns unaddressed—construction and eigendecomposition of Gram matrices scale poorly with data size.

- Suggestion: Include direct baseline comparisons with standard DMD and Extended DMD on toy and Hénon map examples.

- Suggestion: Clarify implementation details—whether modal decomposition/prediction requires explicit feature-space vectors or can be done via kernel evaluations only.

- Suggestion: Strengthen ML relevance by focusing on one application and providing thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art methods in that domain (e.g., robotics/system identification).

- Suggestion: Discuss extensions to noisy or stochastic dynamics and outline challenges and possible approaches.

- Conclusion: Clear, interesting kernel extension of DMD with promising ideas, but hindered by limited empirical comparisons and insufficient discussion of stochasticity and scalability for ML applications.
