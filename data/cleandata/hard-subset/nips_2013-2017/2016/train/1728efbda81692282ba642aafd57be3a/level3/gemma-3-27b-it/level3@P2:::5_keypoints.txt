- Paper proposes KDMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel methods.
- Demonstrates KDMD on synthetic (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and real-world data; explores prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Properly situates work within POD/DMD/Koopman literature and connects KDMD as a DMD generalization.
- Strength — Novelty: kernel extension is conceptually sound and enables richer nonlinear feature extraction.
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: rigorous derivations and a theorem linking Ritz values to Koopman eigenvalues.
- Strength — Potential significance: could impact control, system identification, and time-series analysis.
- Strength — Applications: shows method versatility beyond spectral analysis.
- Weakness — Clarity & presentation: overly verbose, dense math, needs a pedagogical rewrite for accessibility.
- Weakness — Redundancy: Section 4 on POD bases feels unnecessary and inflates length.
- Weakness — Insufficient related-work discussion: Section 5 is too brief and should better contrast KDMD with existing approaches.
- Weakness — Limited empirical evaluation: experiments are condensed, lack kernel/parameter justification, and underdeveloped.
- Weakness — Toy problems: initial tests are simple and do not convincingly showcase KDMD’s nonlinear advantages.
- Assessment — Quality & clarity: technically sound but presentation prevents full evaluation; notation and organization need improvement.
- Assessment — Originality & significance: original and potentially important but currently limited by clarity and empirical support.
- Recommendation: Reject; encourage revision for clarity, stronger empirical validation, and deeper related-work comparison (suggest citing Brunton et al. 2015).
