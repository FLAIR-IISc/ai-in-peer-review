- Summary: Proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis, extracting nonlinear dynamics from finite-length data and shown on toy systems and locomotion data.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Presents a solid mathematical foundation for a kernel extension of DMD.

- Strength — Clarity: Writing is clear and well-organized, making methodology easy to follow.

- Strength — Pedagogical value: Provides a useful introduction to Koopman spectral analysis and fluid-dynamics concepts.

- Weakness — Originality: Theoretical results are not highly surprising given existing RKHS and kernel operator literature.

- Weakness — Practical usefulness: Insufficient demonstration of utility in noisy or stochastic machine-learning settings.

- Weakness — Comparisons: Evaluations versus DMD, PCA, and kPCA are unclear and incomplete.

- Weakness — Limitations: Deterministic-only setup limits relevance to real-world stochastic systems.

- Suggestion: Target a specific ML domain (e.g., robotics) to demonstrate practical utility.

- Suggestion: Include standard DMD performance comparisons on the toy example and the Henon map.

- Suggestion: Clarify whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations and implications.

- Suggestion: Discuss and empirically address extensions to stochastic systems and noise robustness.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel RKHS extension of DMD that can aid nonlinear dynamics analysis.

- Argument for acceptance: Clear, well-organized presentation of method and mathematics.

- Argument for acceptance: Strong pedagogical contribution introducing Koopman analysis.

- Argument against acceptance: Limited originality relative to prior kernel/operator results.

- Argument against acceptance: Practical usefulness in ML not convincingly shown.

- Argument against acceptance: Comparative evaluations are unclear and need expansion.

- Overall: Solid technical contribution but needs stronger empirical demonstrations of practical value and limitations to justify acceptance.
