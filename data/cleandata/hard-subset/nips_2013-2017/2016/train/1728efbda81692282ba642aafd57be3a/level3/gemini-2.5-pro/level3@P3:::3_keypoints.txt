- Proposal: Extend DMD to RKHS using the kernel trick to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, with a robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA components.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, logically structured, and separates the direct and robust formulations clearly.  
- Strength — Technical novelty: Kernelized DMD is an elegant, principled way to capture nonlinear observables without explicit dictionaries, connecting Koopman analysis to kernel methods.  
- Strength — Significance: Bridges fluid-dynamics DMD and ML, offering a powerful feature-extraction tool with relevant applications (prediction, recognition, change-point detection).  
- Weakness — Operator ambiguity: Definition/use of the Koopman operator KH in RKHS is unclear and appears type-inconsistent; the domain and relation to the standard Koopman operator need rigorous clarification.  
- Weakness — Faulty/unclear proof: Theorem 1’s proof contains confusing steps (e.g., ill-defined matrix inverse Mτ^{-1}) and an unexplained derivation of empirical Ritz vectors.  
- Weakness — Hidden assumptions: Theorem 1 relies on strong, unstated assumptions (distinct Ritz values, matrix ranks, data richness) that must be explicitly listed and justified.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Empirical evaluation lacks comparisons to key baselines such as Extended DMD and established methods for the presented applications.  
- Weakness — Minor errors: Contains typographical issues indicating need for proofreading.  
- Recommendation: Reject currently but encourage revision addressing rigorous operator definition, corrected proofs, explicit assumptions, and stronger baseline comparisons.
