- Contribution: Extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS, enabling kernel-based approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Algorithm: Proposes a modal decomposition using kernel functions and nonlinear basis functions to overcome DMD limitations.  
- Robustification: Introduces a POD-based strategy to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Applications: Demonstrates the method on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition tasks.  
- Empirical validation: Evaluated on synthetic systems (toy models, Hénon map) and real locomotion data, showing effectiveness.  
- Strength — Technical: Significant theoretical advancement by combining Koopman analysis with kernel methods.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, logically organized exposition with clear derivations and algorithms.  
- Strength — Practical impact: Innovative use of Koopman spectra for feature extraction in ML tasks.  
- Weakness — Comparisons: Lacks direct comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis methods.  
- Weakness — Kernel selection: No discussion or sensitivity analysis of kernel choice and parameters.  
- Weakness — Scalability: Does not address computational cost or scalability concerns for large datasets (Gram matrices, eigendecomposition).  
- Pro acceptance: Novel, technically sound, clear writing, and compelling empirical results.  
- Con acceptance: Missing comparative evaluation and discussion of kernel choice and computational scalability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add comparisons to non-RKHS methods and a discussion on kernel selection.
