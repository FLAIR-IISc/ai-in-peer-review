- Presents a modal decomposition algorithm extending DMD via the kernel trick for Koopman operator spectral analysis in RKHSs.  
- Provides a feature extraction procedure decomposing nonlinear dynamics into finite modes with applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Empirical demonstrations include toy systems, chaotic maps, and real-world locomotion data.  
- Well-written, technically solid, and logically organized with clear exposition and connection to existing DMD methods.  
- Use of RKHSs enables richer basis functions than traditional DMD; includes a POD-based robustified variant to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Lacks thorough quantitative comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (e.g., extended DMD and other kernel approaches).  
- Section 3.1, especially Equation (7) and Theorem 1, is unclear and potentially inconsistent regarding the operator K_H and the status of φ.  
- Theorem 1 appears to rely on strong implicit assumptions that are not explicitly stated, weakening theoretical rigor.  
- Major flaw: unclear assumptions and justification around K_H consistency and derivation of empirical Ritz values undermine the theoretical foundation.  
- Minor typographical and notation errors (e.g., "the the", incorrect subscripts) need correction.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally if authors clarify Section 3.1, state and justify assumptions explicitly, add detailed comparisons to existing methods, and fix typos.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS-based DMD extension, clear theory/implementation, diverse applications, and potential impact on ML and physics communities.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear/insufficient theoretical rigor in key sections, inadequate comparisons with competitors, and minor presentation issues.
