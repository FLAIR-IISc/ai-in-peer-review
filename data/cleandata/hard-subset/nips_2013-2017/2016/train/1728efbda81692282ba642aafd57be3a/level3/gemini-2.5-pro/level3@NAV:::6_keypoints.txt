- Proposes a kernelized extension of DMD for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, connecting nonlinear dynamical systems with kernel methods.
- Relates to prior kernelized linear-algebra methods (e.g., Kernel PCA/ICA) by applying similar principles to temporal decomposition operators.
- Strength: clear, elegant, and pedagogical introduction to Koopman operators and DMD for a machine-learning audience.
- Strength: principled fusion of kernel methods with DMD that approximates the Koopman spectrum using nonlinear basis functions.
- Weakness: practical significance for the ML community is unclear; experiments (two simple systems and locomotion) don’t demonstrate clear advantages over alternatives.
- Recommendation: evaluate on concrete ML tasks (e.g., robotics, complex time-series forecasting) and benchmark against relevant state-of-the-art methods.
- Theoretical results are technically sound but largely unsurprising given existing literature on linear operators in RKHS.
- Evaluation should include standard DMD as a baseline (e.g., on the toy example and Hénon map) to quantify kernelization benefits.
- Needs clarification whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations, which could be a major limitation.
- Limitation: focus on deterministic systems reduces relevance for ML problems where stochastic components are common.
