- Paper extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition to an RKHS using the kernel trick to better approximate Koopman operator spectra for nonlinear systems.
- Two algorithmic variants: an RKHS Arnoldi/Gram–Schmidt extension and a numerically robust kernel PCA–based projection with truncation for regularization.
- Positions method within decomposition techniques and links to machine learning and system identification.
- Frames method as a feature-extraction tool for nonlinear time series with applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.
- Strength: exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition with easy-to-follow mathematical derivations.
- Strength: technically sound with theorems connecting finite-dimensional projections to true Koopman spectral properties and a thoughtful robustified algorithm.
- Strength/novelty: principled RKHS formulation (versus Extended DMD) offering flexible function spaces via kernel choice and broader ML-relevant applications.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks direct comparisons to standard DMD or Extended DMD baselines, leaving practical advantages unclear.
- Weakness: no discussion or sensitivity analysis of kernel choice and hyperparameter selection (e.g., kernel width, polynomial degree).
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated, theoretically solid, clear writing, and demonstrates useful ML applications—good fit for the venue.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical baselines and missing treatment of kernel selection/hyperparameter tuning.
- Recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance provided authors add baseline comparisons and address kernel selection concerns.
