This paper presents an extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), offering a modal decomposition algorithm applicable to nonlinear dynamical systems. The core idea – leveraging the expressiveness of kernel functions to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions – is a natural and promising extension of existing DMD methods. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clearly explaining the mathematical foundations and algorithmic details. The presentation is logical and easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with all aspects of Koopman theory and RKHS. The empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real-world data further strengthens the paper’s credibility.

The work builds upon the recent surge of interest in Koopman operator theory for nonlinear system analysis (e.g., Rowley & Mezić, 2000; Schmid, 2010; Mezić, 2014) and DMD methods (Schmid, 2010; Rowley et al., 2005). It distinguishes itself by moving beyond linear monomials used in traditional DMD as basis functions, aiming for a more flexible and potentially accurate representation of the Koopman eigenfunctions via kernel methods, akin to Kernel PCA (Schölkopf et al., 1998). It correctly positions itself within related areas like subspace identification (Van Overschee & De Moor, 1996) and spectral methods for dynamical systems (Ghahramani & Roweis, 1998). The referencing is thorough and up-to-date.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear rigorous and well-justified. The extension to RKHS is a logical step, and the theorem proving the equivalence to DMD under certain conditions is a valuable contribution.
* **Clarity:**  The paper’s clarity is a major strength. The authors explain complex concepts in a readily understandable manner.
* **Originality:** Extending Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS, while building on existing work, represents a novel approach with the potential to overcome limitations of traditional DMD.
* **Significance:** The method offers a potentially more accurate and flexible way to analyze nonlinear dynamics, which could have implications for various fields, including control, prediction, and system identification. The exploration of applications such as prediction, embedding, and change-point detection demonstrates the practical relevance of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Comparison to Non-RKHS DMD:** As noted by the reviewer, a direct comparison to standard (non-RKHS) DMD in the simulations is missing.  While the paper demonstrates performance on synthetic and real-world data, it doesn’t explicitly quantify the improvement (or lack thereof) achieved by using the RKHS approach compared to traditional DMD on the same datasets. Providing such a comparison would be crucial for assessing the practical benefits of the proposed method.
* **Kernel Choice:** The discussion of kernel choice is limited. The paper utilizes a polynomial kernel for the toy system and a Gaussian kernel for the Hénon map without a detailed justification or exploration of the impact of different kernel parameters (e.g., kernel width in the Gaussian case, degree in the polynomial case).  A sensitivity analysis, potentially through simulations, demonstrating how the choice of kernel affects the estimated Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions would be highly beneficial.  The manuscript mentions the median heuristic for kernel width, but a more systematic investigation would strengthen the results.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis.
* The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.
* The empirical results are promising and demonstrate the applicability of the method.
* The work addresses an important problem in nonlinear dynamical systems analysis.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a direct comparison to standard DMD limits the assessment of the method's practical advantages.
* The limited discussion of kernel choice and its impact on results weakens the overall contribution.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and recommend acceptance, contingent on addressing the reviewer’s concerns. Specifically, the authors should include a direct comparison to non-RKHS DMD in their simulations and provide a more thorough exploration of kernel choice and its impact on the results. A brief sensitivity analysis of kernel parameters would significantly strengthen the paper and make its contribution more compelling.  Addressing these points would elevate the paper from a promising theoretical contribution to a more impactful and practically relevant work.
