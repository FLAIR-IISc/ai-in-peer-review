- Proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, yielding a kernelized DMD that computes Ritz values/vectors via Gram–Schmidt in feature space and a robust variant using kernel PCA/POD truncation.  
- Proves identities linking the projected Koopman operator to computed eigenpairs (Theorems 1–2).  
- Demonstrates applications to short-term prediction (via pre-image), change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Positions work against DMD, exact/optimized DMD, and EDMD; robust procedure closely related to kernel EDMD (Williams et al. JNS’15).  
- Derivations are careful and the RKHS Krylov/Arnoldi perspective is insightful.  
- Kernel PCA/POD-based robustification is sensible and practically important.  
- Lacks finite-sample or perturbation guarantees (noise robustness, truncation bias).  
- Provides limited guidance on regularization (rank truncation, Tikhonov) and computational scaling/complexity.  
- The pre-image step is heuristic and its impact on prediction error is not quantified.  
- Empirical results are illustrative but lack systematic comparative experiments.  
- Manuscript is well written and organized; algorithms are clearly described.  
- Some assumptions should be made more explicit (e.g., conditions for ψ=0 or κj∈span(U)).  
- Needs a brief complexity/conditioning discussion to help practitioners.  
- Originality overlaps with prior kernel EDMD; distinctive elements are explicit RKHS Krylov formulation, POD-based stabilization from kernel PCA, and pre-image applications.  
- Authors should sharpen and clarify novelty relative to kernel EDMD and cite related spectral-learning work.  
- Requested revisions: add systematic comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman methods, explore kernel choices and truncation sensitivity, discuss numerical complexity/conditioning/regularization and perform sample-size/SNR ablations, and clarify novelty/citations.  
- Pros: clear, well-motivated method with useful theory, practical robustification, and diverse applications.  
- Cons: insufficient comparative experiments, limited analysis of kernel choice and regularization, and unclear novelty relative to kernel EDMD.
