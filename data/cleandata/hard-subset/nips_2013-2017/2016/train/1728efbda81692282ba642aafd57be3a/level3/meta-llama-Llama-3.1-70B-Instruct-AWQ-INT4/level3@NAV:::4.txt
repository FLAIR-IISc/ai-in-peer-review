This paper presents a novel extension of the Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences. The algorithm is based on the equivalent principle of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) and uses the Gram-Schmidt QR decomposition in the feature space to calculate a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The use of RKHS allows for a more flexible and expressive representation of the Koopman eigenfunctions, which is a significant improvement over traditional DMD methods.

The authors evaluate the proposed method on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in extracting meaningful features from nonlinear dynamical systems. The empirical examples provided in the paper are helpful in illustrating the capabilities of the proposed method.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to connect the proposed method to previous work in the field, including DMD and other spectral analysis techniques. The authors provide a clear explanation of how their method differs from and improves upon existing approaches.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide a more detailed comparison of their method to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. This would help to further highlight the benefits of using RKHS.

Secondly, the authors could explore the impact of kernel choice on the results of the proposed method. This could involve evaluating the performance of different kernels on various datasets and providing guidance on how to select the most suitable kernel for a given problem.
