- Proposes kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) performing eigen-decomposition in an RKHS to capture nonlinear dynamics via the Koopman operator.
- Demonstrates the method on synthetic data and applies it to state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Situates work within existing spectral methods (Koopman operator, DMD variants, POD, balanced truncation) and related kernel methods like kernel PCA.
- Clear presentation of background and theoretical foundations (introduction and Section 2).
- Mathematically sound derivation of the kernelized DMD algorithm with Theorem 1 linking to standard DMD.
- Adds a practical POD-based robustification step to address numerical issues.
- Explores a broad set of applications, showing method versatility.
- Empirical results on a toy system and Hénon map indicate the method functions as intended.
- Central contribution is incremental—kernelizing DMD is a natural extension rather than a major novelty.
- Applications do not convincingly show advantages over existing methods; prediction lacks rigorous comparison.
- Dynamics recognition example is visually suggestive but lacks quantitative evaluation.
- Change-point detection is somewhat novel but compared against non–state-of-the-art baselines.
- Lacks thorough comparative analysis with competing techniques (e.g., RNNs, advanced change-detection methods).
- Implementation details are under-specified; kernel parameter choices lack justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Notation is sometimes dense; the link between Koopman formulations and discrete-time approximation could be clearer.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound extension, clear derivations, and potential broader impact via multiple applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, weak application evidence, and insufficient empirical benchmarks and sensitivity studies.
- Overall assessment: solid technical contribution but limited impact; recommended as a weak accept with strengthened empirical comparisons and clarity improvements.
