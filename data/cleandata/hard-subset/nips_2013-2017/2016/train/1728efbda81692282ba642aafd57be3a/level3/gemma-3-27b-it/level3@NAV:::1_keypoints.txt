- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Presents a modal decomposition algorithm and demonstrates feasibility on synthetic and real-world tasks: prediction, dynamic pattern recognition, and sequential change-point detection.  
- Claim of extending DMD to richer nonlinear bases is promising but not clearly distinguished from extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015).  
- Requires a detailed, point-by-point comparison showing advantages of the RKHS formulation over pre-determined basis functions.  
- Related work is incomplete: omits relevant kernel-based dynamical systems literature (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Evaluation focuses on deterministic settings; performance and limitations in stochastic scenarios are unexplored and necessary for real-world relevance.  
- Methodological choice to use pairs of consecutive observables instead of full sequences lacks justification, especially for high-dimensional time series.  
- Underlying causal assumptions for using pointwise pairs vs. sequences need explicit clarification.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited and should include time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection.  
- Results are encouraging but comparisons are basic and do not fully validate claimed advantages.  
- Minor issues: typos (indexing of τ) and punctuation errors in the kernel definition need correction.  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision and resubmission after improving clarity, related work coverage, and experimental validation.
