- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a data-driven, kernelized extension of DMD using orthogonal Krylov bases and eigendecomposition.  
- Demonstrated on two simple dynamical systems (damped 1D and chaotic map) and real locomotion data.  
- Strength: Clear, pedagogical writing accessible to readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Strength: Interesting kernelized extension of DMD related to kernel PCA/ICA and other kernel methods.  
- Strength: Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method.  
- Weakness: Demonstrations are limited to simple systems and comparisons to DMD/PCA/kPCA are unclear.  
- Weakness: Practical usefulness for ML is not established; lacks concrete ML use cases and state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Weakness: Theoretical results are unsurprising given existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature.  
- Weakness: Modal decomposition appears to require explicit feature-space representations, which may limit advantages over kernel PCA.  
- Weakness: Deterministic-only setup ignores stochasticity/noise common in ML, reducing applicability.  
- Originality: Method is novel, but its significance and impact on ML remain unclear.  
- Quality: Paper is well-written and organized, but empirical demonstrations and comparisons need improvement.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel RKHS Koopman spectral method and compelling kernelized DMD idea.  
- Con acceptance: Weak empirical validation, unclear ML relevance, and incremental theoretical contribution.  
- Recommendations: Include stronger demonstrations on complex systems, compare to standard DMD and state-of-the-art, clarify feature-space requirements, and consider extension to stochastic settings.
