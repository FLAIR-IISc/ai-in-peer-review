- Proposes a novel kernel-based RKHS extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Uses the Koopman operator to decompose nonlinear dynamics into finite modes for feature extraction and analysis.  
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS via Gram–Schmidt QR and obtains empirical Ritz values/vectors by eigendecomposition.  
- Provides a robustified variant using POD (principal orthogonal directions) to improve numerical stability.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic and real-world examples (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and tasks (prediction, sequential change-point detection, dynamics recognition).  
- Paper is mathematically correct with a clear, concise exposition of algorithmic and theoretical foundations.  
- Strengths: originality of kernel Koopman approach, clarity of presentation, and well-chosen empirical illustrations.  
- Main weakness: insufficiently distinguishes and compares the method to Williams et al. (2015); needs an explicit comparison.  
- Literature review is incomplete—missing relevant kernel-based dynamical systems work (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback).  
- Lacks discussion and experiments in stochastic settings; current evaluation is limited to deterministic cases.  
- Practical implications of using pairs of consecutive observables vs. longer sequences are unclear, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Needs to clarify causal assumptions underpinning the method and provide more convincing empirical evaluations on those grounds.  
- Experiments are not ambitious enough; recommend comparisons to time-series clustering (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing above issues.
