- Proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS).  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm for finite-length data sequences from nonlinear systems.  
- Algorithm is based on an equivalence principle with dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and yields nonlinear feature extraction.  
- Provides a clear, well-organized manuscript with a thorough review of related work (spectral analysis, DMD variants).  
- Evaluated on simulated and real-world data, showing effective estimation of Koopman eigenvalues and modes.  
- Demonstrates applications of extracted features: prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Key strength: enabling nonlinear basis functions via RKHS, improving over traditional DMD’s linear monomial bases.  
- Presents a robust variant using POD bases to handle ill-conditioned data.  
- Lacks comparisons with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis baselines in simulations.  
- Omits discussion and experiments on kernel choice and its impact on performance.  
- Would benefit from additional experimental results to further validate effectiveness.  
- Overall assessment: original and potentially significant contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions addressing baseline comparisons, kernel selection discussion, and more experiments.
