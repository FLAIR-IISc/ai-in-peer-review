- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator in RKHS and extends dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) to nonlinear RKHS bases; applied to toy system, Hénon map, and locomotion data.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the proposed method and key concepts.
- Provides a good review of related work, including DMD and other spectral methods.
- Empirical examples effectively illustrate the method’s capability to extract meaningful information from data.
- Strength: clear, accessible presentation that makes the method understandable to non-experts.
- Strength: multiple empirical demonstrations (toy system, Hénon map, locomotion) bolster practical relevance.
- Weakness: lacks direct, quantitative comparisons with other spectral/dynamical systems methods.
- Weakness: does not clearly explain the limitations or which system types the method is (un)suitable for.
- Originality: novel extension of DMD to RKHS, but core algorithmic contribution is largely an application of existing DMD.
- Significance: potentially useful for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems but fails to convincingly demonstrate advancement over the state of the art.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add explicit comparisons to other methods and clarify limitations; Rating 7/10, Confidence 8/10.
