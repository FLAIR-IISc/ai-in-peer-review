- Paper proposes Kernel DMD (kDMD): extends DMD to an RKHS via the kernel trick, with an Arnoldi-like procedure and a kernel-PCA-based robust variant, demonstrated on prediction, change-point detection, and motion-capture tasks.
- Strength: novel and significant synthesis of Koopman operator theory and kernel methods, addressing DMD’s limitations on highly nonlinear dynamics.
- Strength: solid theoretical development with clear connections between finite-dimensional approximations and true Koopman eigenvalues, and a justified numerically robust algorithm.
- Strength: manuscript is exceptionally well-written, organized, and makes a dense topic accessible with lucid derivations and precise algorithm statements.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks direct comparisons to non-RKHS baselines (standard DMD and eDMD) to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and justification of kernel choice and hyperparameters; no sensitivity analysis or guidance for practitioners.
- Weakness: application descriptions are brief and lack quantitative evaluation details (e.g., RMSE for prediction, detection delay and false-alarm rates for change-point detection).
- Suggestion: add comparative baselines (DMD, eDMD) and quantitative metrics to substantiate kDMD’s benefits.
- Suggestion: include a section on kernel selection, theoretical implications for representable eigenfunctions, and empirical sensitivity analyses of kernel parameters.
- Overall: strong theoretical and writing quality, but experiments need more rigorous comparisons and kernel discussion to elevate the paper.
