- Proposes KDMD: a kernelized extension of DMD that lifts observables to an RKHS, builds Krylov subspaces from feature snapshots, and computes Ritz eigenpairs of a projected Koopman operator.
- Presents a direct RKHS–DMD formulation via Gram–Schmidt and a numerically robust POD/kernel-PCA truncated variant.
- Illustrates KDMD on toy and canonical chaotic systems and sketches applications: short-term prediction (pre-images), change-point detection, and dynamics recognition via subspace angles.
- Situates KDMD relative to DMD and EDMD: KDMD is effectively EDMD with implicit (possibly infinite) kernel dictionaries plus a Krylov/POD realization and operator-theoretic framing.
- Conceptual strength: principled RKHS formulation that generalizes DMD beyond linear dictionaries and aligns with operator-theoretic learning.
- Algorithmic strength: clear spectral picture via Krylov subspaces and Ritz pairs; POD-regularized variant addresses known DMD numerical issues.
- Potential impact: KDMD could become a widely used tool for learning dynamics and feature extraction if presented pedagogically.
- Breadth: paper outlines several downstream ML applications demonstrating Koopman feature flexibility.
- Major clarity weakness: manuscript is overly mathematical, verbose, and cryptic for the target audience; needs a pedagogical rewrite with intuition and diagrams.
- Structural imbalance: redundant/overlong sections (Section 4 redundant with intro; Section 5 too long; Section 6 too condensed) and requests to move technical details to supplement and expand experiments.
- Empirical weakness: limited, illustrative experiments without quantitative baselines (DMD, exact/optimized DMD, EDMD/KEDMD), kernel ablations, or noise sensitivity studies.
- Practical guidance lacking: insufficient advice on kernel/feature selection or when specific kernels succeed/fail; first tests are polynomial so guidance is crucial.
- Minor concern: possible typo in the toy example (0.92 vs 0.9^2) and unclear labeling of nonlinearity—needs clarification.
- Theoretical soundness: derivations and links to Ritz values, Krylov subspaces, and kernel PCA appear correct but proofs are terse and heavy on notation.
- Empirical support insufficient: requested additions include quantitative comparisons to EDMD/DMD variants, sensitivity analyses (noise, kernel width/degree), and runtime/memory scaling experiments.
- Reproducibility: code is provided, but the paper must present kernels, hyperparameters, preprocessing, and model selection details inline.
- Suggested clarity improvements: add a high-level KDMD pipeline figure, a one-page intuitive overview of Koopman in RKHS, algorithm boxes with complexities, and move technical derivations to appendix.
- Originality: moderate — natural kernelization of EDMD/DMD but operator-centric derivation and Krylov/POD framing add value; authors must more clearly differentiate KDMD from EDMD/KEDMD and exact/optimized DMD.
- Significance: potentially high for ML and scientific computing if made accessible and empirically validated.
- Major actionable revisions requested: pedagogical rewrite, compress/move Section 4, trim Section 5, expand Section 6 with quantitative experiments and baselines, and give practical kernel-selection guidance.
- Minor actionable fixes: correct toy example typo, clarify toy’s nonlinear/linear components, provide kernel hyperparameters and normalization details, and unify notation.
- Pros for acceptance: solid operator-theoretic foundation, broad applicability, and available code.
- Cons for acceptance: hard-to-read presentation, limited empirical evidence, and insufficient practical guidance on kernel/dictionary choice.
- Key open questions for authors: quantitative comparison to EDMD/exact/optimized DMD on benchmarks; sensitivity of KDMD to kernel choice and hyperparameters; computational/memory complexity and practical limits in τ and n.
- Overall recommendation: borderline — worthwhile idea but requires clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation to be impactful.
