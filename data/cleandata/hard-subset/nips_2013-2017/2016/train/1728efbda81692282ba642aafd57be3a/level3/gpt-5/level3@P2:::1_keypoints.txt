- Paper proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS feature maps and Krylov subspaces from finite observable sequences.  
- Main algorithm orthogonalizes the feature-space Krylov matrix via Gram matrices, projects the Koopman operator, and computes Ritz values/vectors as Koopman approximations.  
- A “robustified” variant uses kernel-PCA (POD-style truncation) to mitigate ill-conditioning in a fully kernelized way.  
- Applications discussed: short-term prediction via pre-image recovery, dynamics recognition using subspace angles, and sequential change-point detection with a CUSUM-style kernel score.  
- Conceptually overlaps with Koopman methods, DMD literature, and kernel methods; closest prior work is EDMD/KDMD (Williams et al., 2015).  
- Strength: clear derivation connecting RKHS Ritz values/vectors to Koopman spectra.  
- Strength: pragmatic, fully kernelized robustification paralleling POD truncation in DMD.  
- Strength: useful application ideas with concrete strategies (pre-image, subspace angles, CUSUM score).  
- Strength: naturally handles nonlinear observables without explicit feature engineering and can recover richer eigenfunctions than linear DMD.  
- Weakness: novelty relative to EDMD/KDMD is not clearly articulated; missing precise comparison and differentiation.  
- Weakness: incomplete citation/positioning against prior kernel-based dynamical systems and RKHS operator learning literature.  
- Weakness: strong theoretical assumptions (e.g., Theorem 2) lack discussion of plausibility and impact of truncation on bias/variance.  
- Weakness: experiments are modest, deterministic, and lack quantitative head-to-head comparisons with EDMD/KDMD, exact/optimized DMD, and non-Koopman baselines.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—Gram-matrix methods cost O(τ^2) memory and O(τ^3) time—are not addressed nor are approximation strategies discussed.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of causal/statistical assumptions for using consecutive observation pairs; delay embeddings/Hankelization not evaluated.  
- Quality: technically sound at a high level, but claims about fully recovering eigenfunctions are idealized for finite/noisy data.  
- Clarity: generally well written, but needs a dedicated subsection comparing EDMD/KDMD with side-by-side equations and clearer experimental protocols/metrics.  
- Originality: contribution appears incremental—alternative RKHS/Krylov derivation and a robustification—unless substantive differences vs EDMD/KDMD are demonstrated.  
- Significance: potentially useful to practitioners, but would be strengthened by comprehensive comparisons, scalability, and noisy benchmark results (e.g., Lorenz/Rössler, fluid data).  
- Requests: provide theoretical/algorithmic EDMD/KDMD comparison and empirical head-to-heads on all tasks.  
- Requests: evaluate stochastic settings (process/measurement noise), report degradation of eigenvalues/eigenfunctions, and clarify what estimator approximates in stochastic systems.  
- Requests: state assumptions for pairwise sampling, discuss and evaluate delay embeddings/Hankelization for systems with memory.  
- Requests: add baselines—prediction (EDMD/KDMD, exact DMD, GP dynamical models, kernel autoregressors), recognition (kernel k-means, subspace-angle methods), and online detection (MMD-CUSUM, BOCPD variants) with quantitative metrics.  
- Requests: report computational complexity/wall-clock times and explore Nyström or random features and trade-offs with truncation p.  
- Requests: discuss pre-image stability/complexity, compare MDS-style pre-image to alternative methods and direct input-space regressors.  
- Pros: clear kernelized operator-projection framework, practical robustification, interpretable modal decomposition, and appealing extension of DMD.  
- Cons: conceptual/procedural overlap with EDMD/KDMD, limited deterministic experiments without strong baselines, and missing scalability/stochastic robustness analysis.  
- Minor: fix indexing typos (τ vs τ−1), kernel notation typo, standardize Hermitian transpose/conjugation notation, clarify matrix dimensions, and soften the attractor/unit-circle eigenvalue claim.  
- Overall: promising formulation but needs clearer differentiation from EDMD/KDMD, stronger empirical validation (including noisy/stochastic cases), and scalability/assumption discussions to be competitive for a top conference.
