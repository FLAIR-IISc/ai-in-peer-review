- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear systems.
- Core idea is logical and valuable: kernel expressiveness can improve Koopman spectral analysis over traditional linear approaches.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written with clear mathematical foundations and detailed algorithmic exposition.
- Strong connection to prior work (DMD, POD, Koopman theory), situating the contribution within the field.
- Theoretical development is rigorous and sound.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world data supports the method’s functionality.
- Practical relevance highlighted via potential applications: prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection.
- Major weakness: limited comparative analysis — lacks direct comparison to standard (non-RKHS) DMD on simulated data.
- Needs demonstration of advantages (or limitations) of RKHS over linear DMD, especially where linear DMD struggles.
- Kernel choice and its impact are underexplored; sensitivity analyses (e.g., varying Gaussian kernel width) are recommended.
- Questions remain on whether different kernels improve mode extraction and how kernel choice relates to underlying dynamics.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparative analysis and kernel selection discussion.
