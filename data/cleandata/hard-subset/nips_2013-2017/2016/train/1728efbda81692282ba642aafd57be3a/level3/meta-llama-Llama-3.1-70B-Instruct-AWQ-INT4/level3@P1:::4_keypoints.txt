- Proposes extending Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS with a modal decomposition algorithm for finite-length nonlinear system data.  
- Algorithm leverages equivalence with DMD and kernel expressiveness to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical formulation and practical implementation details.  
- Empirical examples on synthetic and real-world data demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- RKHS enables richer nonlinear representations, yielding more accurate modeling than traditional DMD.  
- Choice of kernel can significantly affect results, but the paper lacks guidance on kernel selection.  
- Computational complexity may be high, posing scalability concerns for large datasets.  
- Would benefit from comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in simulations.  
- Should explore and report the impact of different kernel choices to aid practitioners.  
- Original contribution is notable, though using RKHS in this context is not wholly novel; more differentiation from prior work is needed.  
- Quality: 9/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8.5/10; Significance: 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS extension, clear exposition, and convincing empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing kernel-selection discussion, potential computational cost, and limited contextualization vs existing methods.
