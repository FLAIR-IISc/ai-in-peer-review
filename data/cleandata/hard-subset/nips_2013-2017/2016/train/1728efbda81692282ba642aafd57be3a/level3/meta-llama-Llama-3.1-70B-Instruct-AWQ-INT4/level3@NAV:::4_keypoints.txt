- Novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Modal decomposition algorithm that works with finite-length data sequences.  
- Algorithm built on DMD principles using Gram–Schmidt QR in feature space to get orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix.  
- Well-written, clear, and concise exposition.  
- RKHS enables more flexible and expressive representation of Koopman eigenfunctions versus traditional DMD.  
- Empirical validation on simulated and real-world data demonstrates effective feature extraction.  
- Good connection to prior work, clearly explaining differences and improvements over existing approaches.  
- Suggested improvement: provide a more detailed comparison with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in simulations.  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate and discuss the impact of kernel choice across datasets.  
- Suggested improvement: offer practical guidance on selecting suitable kernels for given problems.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound and theoretically supported.  
- Acceptance pros: novel, significant contribution; solid theory and experiments; clear writing; broadly impactful results.  
- Acceptance cons: lacks detailed comparisons to non-RKHS methods and lacks analysis/guidance on kernel selection.
