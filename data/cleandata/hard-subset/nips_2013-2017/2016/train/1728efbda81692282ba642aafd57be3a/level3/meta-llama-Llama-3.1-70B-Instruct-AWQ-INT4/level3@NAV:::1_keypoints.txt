- Proposes a novel Koopman operator spectral analysis method in RKHS with a modal decomposition extending DMD to kernel spaces.  
- Uses kernel-based methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, enabling nonlinear basis function representation.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations and connections to prior work.  
- Significant contribution: kernelization allows approximation of nonlinear dynamics in RKHS.  
- Lacks clear distinction and explicit comparison with Williams et al. (2015).  
- Prior-work review is incomplete; missing relevant kernel-dynamical-systems papers (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Experiments are not ambitious enough and need more challenging datasets.  
- Needs comparisons to state-of-the-art time-series clustering and online kernel change-point detection methods.  
- Clarify practical implications of using pairs of consecutive observables vs. sequences, especially for multidimensional series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Discuss and make explicit the causal assumptions underlying the approach.  
- Address stochastic settings: performance, limitations, and applicability to stochastic systems are unclear.  
- Claims are not always supported by rigorous theoretical analysis or sufficient experimental evidence.  
- Some sections need improved readability and more intuitive explanations of mathematical concepts.  
- Overall originality and potential significance are high, but methodological distinctions, experiments, theoretical support, and literature coverage must be strengthened.
