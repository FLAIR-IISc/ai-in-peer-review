- Introduces Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), extending DMD via the kernel trick to approximate the Koopman operator in RKHS for spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.
- Presents a robust variant using kernel PCA and frames the Koopman eigenproblem through Gram-matrix operations.
- Strong originality: an elegant, principled synthesis of Koopman theory and kernel methods that can broaden DMD’s appeal to the ML community.
- Solid technical development: derivation appears correct and well-founded, solving the RKHS eigenproblem via kernel methods.
- High potential impact: Koopman modes as features could improve prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Primary weakness: manuscript is unclear, verbose, and overly mathematical, limiting accessibility and impact—needs a pedagogical rewrite.
- Paper organization is suboptimal and redundant; related work should be condensed or integrated into the introduction for better flow.
- Applications section is underdeveloped and high-level; at least one application should be worked out with concrete experiments and results.
- Empirical validation (Section 6) is too condensed—needs more detailed exploration of behavior, limitations, and sensitivity (e.g., to kernel choice and data size).
- Toy example description is slightly misleading; clarify that x-dynamics are linear and uncoupled.
- Lacks discussion on kernel selection and sensitivity; must address how to choose kernels when underlying dynamics are unknown.
- Missing relevant references (e.g., Brunton et al. 2015 and related DMD literature) to better situate the contribution.
- Recommendation: major revision focused on clearer presentation and more thorough experiments; with that, the paper could be a top-tier contribution.
