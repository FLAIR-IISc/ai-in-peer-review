- Proposes a novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS using kernel functions, enabling richer nonlinear basis functions than traditional DMD.
- Core contribution: modal decomposition in RKHS via orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection.
- Provides a robust POD-based variant to handle ill-conditioned problems, analogous to practical DMD improvements.
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) linking the method to Koopman spectral analysis.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized manuscript with reproducible mathematical derivations.
- Convincing empirical validation on synthetic systems (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (state prediction, change-point detection, locomotion recognition).
- Demonstrates clear improvements over traditional approaches in capturing complex nonlinear dynamics and extracting useful features.
- Originality: kernel/RKHS approach is a substantial conceptual advance over extended DMD and prior work (e.g., Williams et al. 2015).
- Significance: method has important applications in physics, engineering, machine learning, human motion analysis, and robotics.
- Weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparison to non-RKHS Koopman methods (standard DMD/extended DMD) in simulations.
- Weakness: insufficient exploration and justification of kernel choice and kernel-parameter sensitivity.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing direct comparisons to non-RKHS methods and systematic investigation of kernel effects.
