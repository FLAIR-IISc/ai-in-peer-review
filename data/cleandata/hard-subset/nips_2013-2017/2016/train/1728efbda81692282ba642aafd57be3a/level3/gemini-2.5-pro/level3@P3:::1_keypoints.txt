- Paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS via a kernelized, Arnoldi-like DMD and a robust KPCA-projected variant, applied to prediction, change-point detection, and pattern recognition.  
- Strength: Presents a novel, principled extension linking Koopman operator theory with kernel methods.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient by using the kernel trick to operate on Gram matrices in feature space.  
- Strength: Demonstrates versatility by framing Koopman eigenfunctions as features for multiple ML tasks.  
- Weakness: Contribution unclear due to inadequate comparison with prior work, especially Williams et al. (2015) Extended DMD.  
- Weakness: Overlooks substantial prior kernel-based literature on learning dynamical systems and operators in RKHS.  
- Weakness: Scope limited to deterministic systems; stochastic extension is brief and lacks rigor.  
- Weakness: Key practical assumptions (using data pairs, first-order Markov) are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is unconvincing—insufficient baselines and rigor to establish superiority.  
- Detailed: Must explicitly compare/contrast with Extended DMD—clarify whether the kernel method is a special case, generalization, and advantages.  
- Detailed: Missing and should cite substantial prior work on kernel embeddings, kernel system ID, and RKHS operator learning.  
- Detailed: Needs formal treatment and experiments for noisy/stochastic systems to assess estimator robustness.  
- Detailed: The use of only consecutive pairs implies a first-order Markov assumption; the implications and limitations must be discussed.  
- Experimental: Prediction experiments lack comparisons to established baselines (e.g., Gaussian Process Dynamical Models).  
- Experimental: Dynamics recognition results are qualitative; require quantitative comparisons to time-series classification/clustering baselines (e.g., kernel k-means, DTW-based methods).  
- Experimental: Change-point detection is compared to an outdated 2006 method, lacks benchmarking against modern online kernel-based methods, and the change-score derivation is heuristic.  
- Minor: Typographical/indexing errors in the algorithm (Krylov subspace indexing) and kernel notation typo (`k(xi.xj)` vs `k(xi, xj)`).  
- Conclusion: Promising idea but needs major revisions to clarify novelty, broaden scope to stochastic settings, and strengthen empirical validation.
