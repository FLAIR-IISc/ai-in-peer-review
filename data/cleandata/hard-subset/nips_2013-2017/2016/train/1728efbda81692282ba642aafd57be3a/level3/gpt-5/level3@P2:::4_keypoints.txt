- Extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS and develops kernel DMD algorithms for estimating eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes from finite data.  
- Section 3: forms Krylov matrix in feature space and computes empirical Ritz values/vectors via Gram–Schmidt QR in RKHS and eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator.  
- Section 4: introduces a robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA (truncated POD-like) subspace to improve conditioning, analogous to truncated/exact DMD.  
- Theorems 1–2 connect estimated eigenpairs to Koopman spectra under stated assumptions.  
- Demonstrations on a toy system and the Henon map plus applications: pre-image short-term prediction, CUSUM-style change-point detection, and dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles.  
- Situates work relative to DMD, improved DMD variants, EDMD, kernel PCA, and subspace/system identification.  
- Conceptual contribution: moves from linear monomial dictionaries to rich implicit kernel dictionaries; closely related to (kernel) EDMD but derived via a Krylov/RKHS perspective.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized operator-theoretic exposition with succinct proofs showing how Ritz pairs in feature space approximate Koopman spectra.  
- Strength: kernel PCA-based robustification is well motivated and aligns with DMD best practices (truncation for conditioning).  
- Strength: sensible downstream applications demonstrating utility of Koopman features for ML tasks.  
- Strength: algorithm uses only kernel Gram matrices, enabling flexible nonlinear feature mappings without explicit bases.  
- Strength: Theorem 2 provides a meaningful link between projected operator eigenpairs and Koopman eigenfunctions under reasonable richness conditions.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is narrow—toy and Henon examples lack quantitative comparisons to baselines.  
- Weakness: relation to EDMD/kernel EDMD is not fully clarified; need delineation of algorithmic/theoretical differences and conditions where KDMD outperforms EDMD.  
- Weakness: no systematic study of kernel choice and hyperparameters (RBF width, polynomial degree, truncation rank) or guidance on selection.  
- Weakness: scalability not discussed—Gram matrix memory O(τ^2) and compute O(τ^3) may limit long sequences; streaming/Nyström/randomized methods not considered.  
- Weakness: noise robustness not treated; method presented for deterministic systems without perturbation/error analysis or empirical stress tests.  
- Weakness: pre-image reconstruction for prediction may be ill-posed and the MDS-like heuristic could be brittle; lacks quantitative prediction metrics and comparisons.  
- Quality: technical core is sound and RKHS machinery is used correctly; proofs are concise but assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, inclusion of φ_j in Φ, alignment with kernel PCA subspace) should be emphasized.  
- Quality: numerical stability and regularization (e.g., Tikhonov) should be addressed explicitly.  
- Clarity: paper is well written; recommend adding pseudocode for both variants, explicit computational complexity, centering decisions, and guidance on truncation rank selection.  
- Originality: RKHS derivation and robust kernel PCA projection are appealing, but practical algorithm closely resembles kernel EDMD—needs sharper positioning of novelty (Krylov perspective, theorems, algorithmic differences).  
- Significance: kernelizing DMD is valuable and could impact system ID and time-series ML, but stronger empirical evidence is needed to establish state-of-the-art impact.  
- Requested: add quantitative comparisons to standard/exact/truncated DMD, EDMD with polynomial dictionaries, and kernel EDMD baselines reporting eigenvalue, reconstruction, and short-horizon prediction errors.  
- Requested: provide systematic exploration of kernel choices (RBF widths/median heuristic vs CV), polynomial degrees, truncation rank p, and noise sensitivity via ablations.  
- Requested: clarify relation to EDMD with side-by-side formulation and state when KDMD reduces to or extends EDMD.  
- Requested: discuss computational complexity and potential accelerations (Nyström, randomized SVD) and limitations for long sequences.  
- Pros: clean operator-theoretic formulation, practical and broadly useful extension of DMD, and well-written exposition.  
- Cons: limited empirical validation, unaddressed hyperparameter sensitivity, unclear novelty relative to EDMD, and missing scalability/noise analysis.  
- Minor: ensure all references complete, specify truncation criteria in Section 4, and discuss whether centering should be used when Koopman acts on uncentered observables.  
- Overall: solid and clear RKHS-based DMD contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on stronger experiments and clearer positioning vs EDMD.
