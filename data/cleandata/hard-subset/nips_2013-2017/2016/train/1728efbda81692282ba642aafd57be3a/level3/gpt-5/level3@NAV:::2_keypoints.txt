- Proposes a kernelized spectral method: perform DMD in an RKHS using a Krylov matrix of feature-mapped observations, orthonormalize via kernel Gram operations, and eigendecompose the projected Koopman operator to obtain Ritz values/vectors.  
- Introduces a robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA/POD directions to mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Uses the method for feature extraction (preimage heuristic), embedding/recognition (kernel principal angles), and sequential change detection.  
- Empirical demonstrations on a toy system, Hénon map, motion-capture sequences, and a Lorenz-map change-point task.  
- Derivations are sound and consistent with Arnoldi/DMD viewpoints; the RKHS formulation and POD-based stabilization are well presented.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees: no consistency results for eigenvalue/mode estimation, no sample-complexity bounds, and no conditions for small ψ.  
- Limited analysis of numerical stability, regularization, and kernel hyperparameter selection.  
- The preimage step is heuristic and may fail in high-dimensional settings.  
- Experimental evaluation is largely qualitative with little quantitative comparison (e.g., eigenspectrum error, prediction RMSE) or ablation studies (kernel choice, truncation rank).  
- Paper is generally clear and well written, but assumptions (ergodicity, richness/rank, noise) should be made explicit.  
- Computational complexity and memory costs of kernel Gram operations and out-of-sample evaluation are not discussed.  
- Novelty is limited: kernelization + POD stabilization is close to EDMD/KDMD and other DMD variants; clearer differentiation from prior KDMD/EDMD work is needed.  
- Significance is plausible but not demonstrated: applications do not convincingly outperform strong baselines (EDMD/KDMD, GPDM, spectral HMM/PSR methods, RNNs/LSTMs).  
- Pros: clear RKHS reformulation, practical stabilization, unifying perspective, and diverse application examples.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs. existing DMD variants, weak empirical evidence and baselines, and missing theoretical/sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: add rigorous comparisons to EDMD/KDMD and exact/optimized/sparse DMD, include quantitative metrics and ablations, analyze kernel/regularization choices, and clarify computational scaling and explicit assumptions.
