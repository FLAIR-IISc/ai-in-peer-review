- Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD): kernelizing Dynamic Mode Decomposition to approximate Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions in an RKHS from data.
- Two algorithmic variants: Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization in feature space and a more robust PCA-based projection onto kernel principal components.
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2) link the finite-dimensional projected operator eigendecomposition to Koopman spectra and appear correct.
- Paper is well-written and pedagogical, making Koopman operator theory and DMD accessible to a machine learning audience.
- Main limitation: framework developed only for deterministic systems, despite most ML applications involving stochasticity and noise.
- Experimental evaluation is preliminary and lacks strong empirical support for the claimed benefits of kDMD.
- Comparisons to baseline methods (standard DMD, extended DMD, Gaussian Processes, modern change-point detectors) are missing or unclear.
- Unclear practical usefulness for ML applications; suggested applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are not benchmarked against state-of-the-art.
- Relationship to extended DMD and existing kernel-based spectral methods should be discussed more explicitly.
- Need clearer discussion and experiments on robustness to process and measurement noise; question whether POD-based robustification helps.
- Implementation clarification required: does the robust algorithm need explicit feature-space vectors φ̄j, or can it be fully kernelized using only Gram matrices?
- Recommend including baseline comparisons for toy examples and Hénon map to demonstrate the added value of kernelization over standard DMD.
- Suggest focusing on a single ML-relevant application (e.g., robotics, RL, video) and showing concrete advantages to increase impact.
- Overall: elegant and interesting synthesis with solid theory and clarity, but limited empirical validation and incremental theoretical novelty reduce immediate ML relevance.
