- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS.  
- Derives a modal decomposition algorithm that uses finite-length data, computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the projected operator.  
- Builds on the known DMD–Koopman connection and extends DMD with nonlinear basis functions via kernel methods.  
- Demonstrates the method on two toy systems (damped 1D and Hénon map) and on real locomotion data.  
- Shows example applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Writing is clear and pedagogically valuable, especially for readers unfamiliar with fluid dynamics.  
- Strength: clear exposition of the algorithm and solid theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: practical implementation details address numerical stability using POD projection.  
- Strength: useful connection drawn between kernel methods and Koopman analysis, extending prior kernel PCA/ICA work.  
- Weakness: theoretical results are unsurprising given existing RKHS literature.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete justification of practical usefulness for machine learning.  
- Weakness: comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kPCA are unclear or insufficient.  
- Weakness: deterministic-only setup (no stochastic components) limits relevance to typical ML problems involving randomness.  
- Suggestion: identify specific ML use cases where this method has clear advantages over existing techniques.  
- Suggestion: include standard DMD performance on toy examples to highlight differences.  
- Suggestion: clarify whether explicit feature-space representations are required and justify kernel choice more strongly.  
- Overall: mathematically sound and well-presented but lacking compelling evidence of superior performance or unique ML applicability; significance for NIPS is questionable without clearer demonstrations.
