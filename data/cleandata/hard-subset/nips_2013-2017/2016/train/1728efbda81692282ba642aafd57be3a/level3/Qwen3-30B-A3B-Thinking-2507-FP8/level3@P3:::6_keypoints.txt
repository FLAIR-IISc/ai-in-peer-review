- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, computing spectral decomposition from finite data via Krylov bases.
- Builds on established DMD and kernel methods, relating to kernel PCA and prior extended DMD work.
- Mathematical derivations are sound and the theoretical foundation is solid.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and pedagogically clear, making Koopman concepts accessible to ML readers.
- Organization is logical with clear sections on background, theory, robustification, and applications.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simple dynamical systems (damped 1D, Hénon map) and locomotion data, lacking depth.
- Fails to sufficiently compare against standard DMD, PCA, and kPCA; no standard DMD baseline on toy examples shown.
- Does not clearly differentiate itself from Williams et al.'s extended DMD (2015) or other kernel approaches—novelty is limited.
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations, which could be a major limitation vs. kernel PCA.
- Deterministic-only setup ignores stochastic/noise components common in ML; robustness to noisy data is not demonstrated.
- Applications (state prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) do not convincingly show advantages over simpler methods.
- Significance is questionable without a concrete ML application demonstrating clear benefits (suggestions: robotics, complex time-series).
- Recommended improvements: more rigorous comparisons to baselines, identify and demonstrate a concrete ML application, clarify feature-space requirements, and test on noisy data.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound but incremental; recommendation to reject unless authors address the empirical and clarity shortcomings, after which the paper could be strong.
