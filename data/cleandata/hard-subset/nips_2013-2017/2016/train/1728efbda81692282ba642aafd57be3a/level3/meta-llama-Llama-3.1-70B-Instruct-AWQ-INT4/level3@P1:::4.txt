This paper proposes an extension of Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences generated from a nonlinear system. The algorithm is based on the equivalent principle with Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) and is capable of approximating the Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions due to the expressiveness of kernel functions.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method, including its mathematical formulation and practical implementation. The authors also provide empirical examples using synthetic and real-world data to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to provide a more accurate representation of nonlinear dynamical systems compared to traditional DMD methods. The use of RKHS allows for the incorporation of nonlinear relationships between variables, which can lead to more accurate predictions and better understanding of the underlying dynamics.

However, there are some limitations to this approach. For instance, the choice of kernel function can significantly impact the results, and the authors do not provide a thorough discussion on how to select the optimal kernel for a given problem. Additionally, the computational complexity of the algorithm may be high, especially for large datasets.

To further improve this work, the authors could provide a comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations to demonstrate the advantages of their approach. They could also explore the impact of kernel choice on the results through simulations, which would provide valuable insights for practitioners.
