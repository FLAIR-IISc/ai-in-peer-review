This paper presents a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a data-driven algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences generated from a nonlinear system. The algorithm is essentially reduced to the calculation of a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases.

The authors demonstrate their method on two simple dynamical systems, a damped 1D system and the chaotic Henon map, as well as real locomotion data. However, the comparisons to dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), principal component analysis (PCA), and kernel PCA (kPCA) yield unclear results. The method is an interesting kernelized extension of DMD, related to prior kernel methods such as kernel PCA, kernel independent component analysis (ICA), and kernel tests.

The paper is clearly written and pedagogically useful for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts. However, the reviewer questions the practical usefulness of the method for machine learning (ML) applications. The authors need to identify concrete use cases and compare their method to state-of-the-art techniques. Robotics, or another ML domain with dynamical-system inference, could be a target to demonstrate the utility of Koopman analysis.

The theoretical results are unsurprising given the existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature. The reviewer requests clarification on whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations. If so, this is a limitation compared to kernel PCA, which does not require explicit feature-space representations.

The reviewer also requests the inclusion of standard DMD performance on the toy example and the Henon map to highlight the differences between the proposed method and DMD. Additionally, the deterministic-only setup (no stochastic/noise components) limits the relevance of the method to ML applications where randomness is common.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the reviewer questions the practical usefulness of the method for ML applications.

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide a good introduction to the concepts of Koopman analysis and RKHS. However, the reviewer suggests that the authors could improve the clarity of the paper by providing more intuitive explanations of the technical concepts.

**Originality:** The method is an interesting kernelized extension of DMD, but the reviewer questions the originality of the approach given the existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature.

**Significance:** The paper provides a new perspective on spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, but the reviewer questions the practical significance of the method for ML applications. The authors need to identify concrete use cases and compare their method to state-of-the-art techniques.
