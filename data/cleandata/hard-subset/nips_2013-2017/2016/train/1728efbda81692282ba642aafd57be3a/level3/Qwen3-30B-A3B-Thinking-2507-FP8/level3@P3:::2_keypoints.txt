- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis by embedding data in an RKHS and performing eigen-decomposition on the Krylov matrix to project the Koopman operator onto orthogonal bases.
- Draws a clear connection between DMD and the Koopman operator; kernelization enables richer nonlinear basis functions and uses kernel PCA for numerical robustness.
- Strength: Mathematical formulation is sound with theorems linking kernelized DMD to Koopman analysis.
- Strength: Makes DMD accessible to dynamical-systems researchers while connecting to kernel methods familiar to ML audiences.
- Strength: Empirical results on synthetic and real-world tasks (locomotion recognition, change-point detection) demonstrate practical utility.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful examples illustrating the algorithm.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—main contribution is a straightforward kernelization of existing DMD rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to strong baselines (e.g., recurrent neural networks) that are relevant for modeling dynamical systems.
- Weakness: Applications do not convincingly show that the kernelized method outperforms standard DMD or other kernel-based methods.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis on when the kernelized approach would outperform alternatives.
- Quality: Technically sound with correct derivations, but evaluation needs stronger baselines and analysis of limitations.
- Clarity: Well-written and accessible for readers familiar with kernel methods and dynamical systems.
- Originality: Limited; does not sufficiently distinguish itself from prior kernel-based dynamical-systems work.
- Significance: Incremental contribution with limited evidence of advancing the state of the art.
- Final assessment: A natural but modest/incremental contribution with insufficient empirical justification of its advantages.
- Recommendation: Weak reject.
