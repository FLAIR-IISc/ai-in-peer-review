- Proposes training uRNNs by optimizing the recurrence matrix over the Stiefel manifold of unitary matrices.  
- Provides a theoretical criterion for when a unitary parameterization has restricted capacity.  
- Shows a previously proposed unitary parameterization is capacity-restricted for hidden dimensions > 7.  
- Introduces a method to directly optimize a full-capacity unitary matrix.  
- Demonstrates superior performance of the full-capacity uRNN versus restricted-capacity uRNNs and LSTMs on system identification, long-term memorization, speech prediction, and image classification.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and clearly explained.  
- Experimental results are convincing and comprehensive across tasks.  
- Provides a thorough review of related work on vanishing/exploding gradients in RNNs.  
- Balances theoretical rigor with practical experiments and raises questions about capacity vs. hidden-state dimension trade-offs.  
- Limitation: restricted-capacity uRNNs can still perform well, so practical trade-offs need further study.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed empirical comparison with other remedies for vanishing/exploding gradients (e.g., gradient clipping, orthogonal initialization).  
- Overall recommendation: acceptance based on significant contribution and strong results.
