- Proposes kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, using nonlinear kernel-derived basis functions.  
- Develops modal decomposition by computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a robustified variant using POD directions to address a known instability in conventional DMD.  
- Theoretical development is grounded in prior Koopman/DMD work and the exposition is clear and well-organized.  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic and real-world data show promising results for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods in experiments, reducing evaluation breadth.  
- Major mathematical inconsistencies in Section 3.1: Eq. (7) treats KH as H→H but subsequent proof treats φ(x) as a scalar, creating a contradiction.  
- Unclear whether φ: M→H implies φ belongs to H; this assumption is not clarified and may be invalid.  
- Proof of Theorem 1 appears to rely on unstated assumptions and lacks rigor.  
- Relationship between Equations (2) and (7) is unclear despite superficial similarity.  
- Typos and inconsistent notation (e.g., duplicated "the", index mismatch) detract from professionalism.  
- Overall significance is high, but unresolved theoretical issues undermine technical soundness and acceptance readiness.
