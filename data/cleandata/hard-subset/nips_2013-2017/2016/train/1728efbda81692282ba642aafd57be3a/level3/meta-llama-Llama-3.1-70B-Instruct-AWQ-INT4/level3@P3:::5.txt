This paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS), termed KDMD. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of KDMD in spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, showcasing its ability to capture complex dynamics and provide valuable insights into the underlying system behavior.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing DMD techniques and their limitations. The introduction of RKHS to DMD is a significant contribution, enabling the method to handle nonlinear systems more effectively. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the theoretical foundations of KDMD, including the extension of the Koopman operator to RKHS and the development of a modal decomposition algorithm.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method. The theoretical results are well-supported by experimental evaluations on toy problems and real-world data. The authors also discuss the connections between KDMD and other techniques, such as Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD) and subspace identification methods.

However, the paper suffers from poor clarity and verbosity. The writing style is overly mathematical, making it challenging for non-experts to follow. Many statements could be more concise, and the authors could benefit from providing clearer explanations and more intuitive examples. The paper would significantly benefit from a more pedagogical rewrite, making it more accessible to a broader audience.

The organization of the paper could also be improved. Section 4, which discusses the robustification of KDMD using POD bases, seems redundant and could be merged with Section 3. Section 5, which reviews related works, is well-written but could be more concise. Section 6, which presents empirical examples, is overly condensed and could be expanded to provide more insight into the performance of KDMD.

The authors provide a comprehensive list of references, including relevant works on DMD, POD, and subspace identification methods. However, some references, such as Brunton et al. (2015), could be more explicitly cited in the main text to provide a clearer connection between the proposed method and existing techniques.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. The extension of DMD to RKHS is a novel and valuable contribution, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in various applications.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to advance the state-of-the-art in nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. The proposed method could be widely applicable in various fields, including physics, engineering, and machine learning. The authors provide a clear vision for the potential applications of KDMD, including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

In conclusion, while the paper has some limitations in terms of clarity and organization, it makes a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. With some revisions to improve clarity and accessibility, the paper has the potential to be highly citable and influential in the field.
