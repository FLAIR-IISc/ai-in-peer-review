- Proposes kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in an RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Mathematical derivation is sound and clearly connected to existing DMD and Koopman literature.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real-world data for state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Extension to RKHS is a logical, well-motivated progression leveraging kernel methods for nonlinear approximation.  
- Novelty is limited—primarily an incremental kernelization of an existing algorithm rather than a fundamental advance.  
- Empirical results are promising but lack sufficient comparative analysis against strong baselines like RNNs and alternative change-detection methods.  
- Application scenarios are somewhat generic and do not clearly justify why kernelized DMD is the preferred method.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, mathematically sound, and well-motivated within Koopman/operator spectral analysis.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, insufficient benchmarking, and applications that don’t highlight unique benefits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically solid extension with potential benefits for capturing nonlinear dynamics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution and weak empirical evidence reduce suitability for a top-tier conference.  
- Overall verdict: borderline submission, leaning toward reject; recommend more thorough benchmarking and exploration of applications where kernelized DMD has a clear advantage.
