- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel methods.  
- Uses orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition to extract modes representing system behavior.  
- Demonstrates applications on synthetic and real data for state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Positions the work within existing literature on Koopman operator, POD, DMD, subspace identification, and kernel methods.  
- Provides a mathematically rigorous foundation with Theorem 1 and Theorem 2.  
- Explains concepts and algorithm steps clearly and logically, starting from background to the proposed method.  
- Supplies practical implementation details and publicly available Matlab code, aiding reproducibility.  
- Addresses numerical ill-conditioning by projecting data onto POD directions to improve robustness.  
- Novelty is limited: kernelizing DMD is seen as an incremental extension of a well-established algorithm.  
- Applications shown are standard and do not clearly outperform alternatives like recurrent neural networks or established change-detection methods.  
- Discussion of kernel choice is brief and lacks systematic analysis of kernel types or parameter effects.  
- Computational complexity and scalability, especially for Krylov matrix computations and RKHS eigendecomposition, are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparisons to alternative methods in accuracy, computational cost, and tuning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sound theory, clear exposition, available code, and practical robustness steps.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unimpressive application impact, insufficient comparisons, and missing complexity/scalability discussion.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed technical contribution with limited impact; recommendation leans toward weak accept with requests to expand applications, comparisons, scalability analysis, and kernel studies.
