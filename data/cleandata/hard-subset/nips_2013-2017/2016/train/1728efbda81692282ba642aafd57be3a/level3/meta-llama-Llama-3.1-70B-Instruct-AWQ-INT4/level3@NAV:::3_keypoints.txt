- Presents a spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.  
- Proposes a modal decomposition algorithm extending DMD via the kernel trick.  
- Algorithm is technically solid and numerical results demonstrate usefulness.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and explains theoretical concepts clearly.  
- Introduces a robustifying step projecting data onto principal orthogonal directions (POD) in feature space.  
- Empirical examples show effective estimation of Koopman modes and eigenvalues.  
- Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear and need clarification.  
- Equation (7) appears similar to Equation (2); the distinction and purpose are unclear.  
- Mathematical inconsistency: Koopman operator KH is defined H→H but φ: M→H, so φ may not belong to H — needs explanation.  
- Proof of Theorem 1 has unclear steps (lines 144–157); phrase “applying the operator KH to both sides” is confusing since both sides seem scalar.  
- Assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong, not explicit, and additional assumptions appear in the proof without justification.  
- Minor typos (e.g., “the the” and incorrect index notation \kappaj / \lambdaj vs \kappai / \lambda_i).  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques (RKHS + kernel trick to extend DMD); related work is comprehensive.  
- Significance: method could yield more accurate and robust Koopman mode/eigenvalue estimates with important applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the above clarifications and corrections.
