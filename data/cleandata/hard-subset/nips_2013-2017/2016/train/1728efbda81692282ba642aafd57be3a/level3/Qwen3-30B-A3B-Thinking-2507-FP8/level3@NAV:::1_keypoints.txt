- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using nonlinear kernel basis functions.
- Introduces an algorithm (and a POD-robustified variant) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with theoretical development.
- Theoretical foundations are solid and supported by mathematical derivations (Theorems 1 and 2).
- Validated on synthetic and real datasets with applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Experimental results show reasonable performance on benchmark systems like a toy nonlinear system and the Hénon map.
- Distinction from Williams et al. (2015) is unclear; extended DMD appears nearly identical to the robustified variant and needs explicit comparison.
- Key prior work in kernel-based dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al.) is omitted, weakening the novelty claim.
- Experiments lack ambition and omit important baselines such as time-series clustering (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and online kernel change-point detection.
- The paper does not address stochastic systems, limiting applicability to real-world noisy dynamics.
- Efficiency gains over traditional DMD are noted but the paper’s significance is reduced by the omissions and limited evaluation.
- Minor typos and notation issues (tau indexing, k(xi.xj)) reduce clarity.
- Practical implications of using consecutive observable pairs instead of full sequences are not adequately explained, especially for multidimensional time series.
