- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman operator eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Introduces an algorithm computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS followed by eigendecomposition.  
- Incorporates a projection onto POD directions to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Demonstrates the method on synthetic and real-world data for prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Situates the work within Koopman operator theory, kernel methods, and subspace identification literature.  
- Connects to recent DMD variants (optimized DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD) and aims to improve robustness and applicability of DMD.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates the approach.  
- Exposition of the RKHS extension of DMD is commendable and accessible.  
- Technical foundation appears sound and numerical results illustrate feasibility, though not exhaustive.  
- Correctly identifies and addresses numerical stability via POD, consistent with DMD literature.  
- Section 3.1 and Equation (7) lack clarity and need substantial clarification relative to the Koopman definition (Equation (2)).  
- Ambiguity whether the feature map φ resides in H and insufficient discussion of this crucial assumption.  
- Theorem 1’s proof contains ambiguous steps (e.g., “applying KH to both sides” on scalar quantities) and weakly stated/implicit assumptions.  
- These mathematical precision and rigor issues undermine confidence in the theoretical results.  
- Minor typographical errors are present and detract from polish.  
- Empirical comparisons to existing state-of-the-art DMD variants and system identification methods are limited.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel kernel-based DMD extension, clear exposition, broader applications, and stability handling via POD.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear/insufficient mathematical rigor, unjustified assumptions in proofs, limited empirical comparisons, and minor typos.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but currently borderline; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing theoretical clarity and comparative evaluation.
