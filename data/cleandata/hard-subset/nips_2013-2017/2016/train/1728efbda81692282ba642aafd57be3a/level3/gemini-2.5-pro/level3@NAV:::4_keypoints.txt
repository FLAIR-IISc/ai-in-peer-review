- Introduces a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
- Leverages the kernel trick to implicitly map states and approximate Koopman eigenfunctions nonparametrically.
- Provides two theoretically rigorous algorithms (Gram–Schmidt-based and kernel PCA–based projection).
- Clearly connects to Extended DMD and related spectral learning methods, relevant to the NIPS community.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear background and derivations.
- Demonstrates practical utility on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with synthetic and motion-capture data.
- Empirical section lacks direct quantitative baseline comparisons to standard DMD or Extended DMD.
- Insufficient analysis of kernel choice and hyperparameter sensitivity; rationale for kernel selection is limited.
- Overall a high-quality, significant contribution with experimental weaknesses that are addressable.
