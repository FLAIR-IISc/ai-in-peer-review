- Proposes KDMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Core idea: use kernel methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation and clear connection to Koopman operator and DMD.  
- Logical and potentially valuable extension that could improve nonlinear dynamical analysis.  
- Paper includes toy demonstrations and mentions potential machine learning applications.  
- Manuscript is unclear and excessively verbose, making core ideas hard to follow.  
- Section 4 (POD-based robustification) is redundant and should be condensed or integrated.  
- Related work section is cursory and lacks depth.  
- Empirical results lack sufficient context, interpretation, and experimental detail.  
- Applications in Section 7 are underexplored; conclusions should summarize results and implications.  
- Possible error: the “nonlinear” toy case appears to be linear and needs careful review.  
- Polynomial test problems are not ideal; KDMD benefits are clearer with nontrivial kernel/feature choices.  
- Need more explanation of kernel selection rationale and its impact on performance.  
- Argument for acceptance: sound underlying idea with potential impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: poor presentation and incomplete experimental validation.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form; major revision focusing on clarity, intuition, and illustrative examples required.
