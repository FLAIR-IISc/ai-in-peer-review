This paper proposes a novel approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors develop an algorithm for performing this analysis using finite-length data sequences, which is reduced to calculating a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the background and the proposed method. The use of RKHSs and kernel-based methods is a significant contribution, as it allows for the efficient analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues.

However, there are some issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors do not provide a clear distinction between their work and that of Williams et al. (2015), which also proposes a kernel-based approach to dynamic mode decomposition. A more explicit comparison and discussion of the differences between the two approaches would be helpful.

Secondly, the authors do not mention relevant prior work in kernel-based dynamical systems, such as the work of Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., and Mouattamid & Schaback. Including a discussion of these works and how they relate to the proposed method would strengthen the paper.

Thirdly, the authors only consider deterministic cases and do not discuss the performance and limitations of the proposed method in stochastic settings. It would be beneficial to include experiments and discussions on stochastic settings to demonstrate the robustness of the method.

Fourthly, the authors mention that the proposed method can work with pairs of consecutive observables instead of sequences, but they do not provide a clear discussion on the practical implications of this approach, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations. A more detailed discussion on the causal assumptions and the implications of using pairs of consecutive observables would be helpful.

Lastly, the experiments presented in the paper are not ambitious enough. The prediction task is a standard one, and the authors do not provide comparisons to time-series clustering methods (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection methods. More convincing evaluations, such as comparisons to other methods and more challenging tasks, would strengthen the paper.

Minor issues include two typos: indexing tau-1 vs. tau and replacing the dot with a comma in k(xi,xj) (last equation on p.7).

Overall, the paper proposes a novel and efficient approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs. However, addressing the above issues would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method.
