- KDMD applies DMD in an RKHS using kernels to implicitly define rich nonlinear observables.  
- Authors present an Arnoldi-based RKHS algorithm and a practical robust version using kernel PCA truncation.  
- Theoretical derivations (Theorems 1 & 2) are sound and link outputs to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions.  
- Method is demonstrated on synthetic examples and ML tasks (state prediction, embedding/recognition, change detection).  
- Paper is technically complete but overly verbose and mathematically dense, harming accessibility.  
- Needs a more pedagogical presentation for a broad NIPS audience unfamiliar with Koopman/DMD.  
- Clarify and explicitly connect the robustification to standard DMD's truncated SVD; condense/integrate those sections.  
- Application experiments are brief; expand one or two with quantitative results and baseline comparisons.  
- Novelty: kernelizing DMD is a clever, significant extension of Extended DMD.  
- Potentially high impact if presentation is improved; enables principled feature extraction for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Must discuss kernel choice in depth and offer guidance linking kernels to system properties (e.g., polynomial eigenfunctions).  
- Minor correction: the toy system is correctly identified as nonlinear and appropriately handled by a polynomial kernel.  
- Strengthen related work by emphasizing KDMD as a specific instance of Extended DMD and citing sparse-identification literature.  
- Pros: novel, theoretically solid, bridges dynamical systems and machine learning.  
- Cons: poor clarity and preliminary experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on a major revision to improve clarity and experimental depth.
