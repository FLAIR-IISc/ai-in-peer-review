- Paper extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear kernel-based observables.  
- Algorithm uses Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization in RKHS and includes a POD-robustified variant to address numerical instability.  
- Relation to Williams et al. (2015) / Extended DMD is unclear; authors must explicitly distinguish kernel-learned basis functions from fixed bases.  
- Section 4 should clarify differences between using kernel principal components versus fixed basis functions.  
- Several relevant prior works on kernel-based dynamical systems are missing and should be cited (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2011; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2010; Mouattamid & Schaback 2008; Franz & Schölkopf 2004).  
- Experiments are limited to deterministic settings despite claims about stochastic extensions; theoretical discussion and empirical validation under noise are needed.  
- Use of pairs of consecutive observables (x_t, x_{t+1}) instead of full sequences lacks justification; implications for non-Markovian or weakly causal multivariate time series must be discussed.  
- Experimental scope is narrow and unambitious; prediction tasks are standard and change-point detection lacks clear baselines and statistical significance testing.  
- Change-point comparison in Figure 4 is vague—“kernel change detection” is unspecified and benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods is missing.  
- Paper does not compare against time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels) or online kernel change-point detection methods.  
- Minor technical issues: inconsistent indexing (tau-1 vs. tau) and a misplaced dot in kernel notation k(xi.xj).  
- Strength: The RKHS extension of DMD is theoretically sound and better suited for nonlinear systems than linear DMD.  
- Strength: Algorithm description is clear and includes a robustified POD variant to improve numerical stability.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practical utility across prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Weaknesses summary: insufficient comparison to Williams et al. and other literature, missing kernel-dynamical-system references, lack of stochastic analysis, unclear observables justification, and limited experiments.  
- Pros: Solid theoretical contribution and promising empirical results; addresses numerical instability.  
- Cons: Fails to distinguish sufficiently from prior work, omits critical references, and provides inadequate experimental validation—recommend major revisions (current recommendation: reject).
