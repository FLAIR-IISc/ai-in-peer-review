- Paper proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS: build Krylov matrices in feature space, orthogonalize via Gram matrices, and eigendecompose a projected Koopman operator to get Ritz values/modes.  
- All computations are kernelized (via Gram matrices) enabling nonlinear basis functions instead of linear monomials used in classical DMD.  
- Introduces a robustness variant by projecting onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions before eigendecomposition to address numerical conditioning.  
- Theorems relate empirical eigenvalues/eigenvectors to Koopman spectra under assumptions on data span and kernel principal components.  
- Applications sketched: short-term prediction via a preimage step, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and change-point detection using an RKHS exponential-family CUSUM-like statistic.  
- Work is closely related to EDMD, kernel DMD, and RKHS operator estimation literature; emphasizes a Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint and kernel-PCA robustification.  
- Strength: clear, pedagogical exposition useful for ML readers unfamiliar with DMD/Koopman methods.  
- Strength: principled kernelization and a reasonable kernel-PCA truncation robustness step.  
- Strength: algorithmic pipeline is implementable via Gram matrices; theoretical statements mirror finite-dimensional DMD facts.  
- Strength: code provided and illustrative examples (toy, Hénon map, locomotion embeddings, change detection).  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental relative to EDMD/kernel DMD; theoretical results largely follow standard Arnoldi/DMD and kernel-PCA machinery.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited, qualitative, and lacks systematic comparisons to DMD, EDMD with comparable dictionaries, kPCA, and modern ML baselines.  
- Weakness: experiments are deterministic and noise-free; no robustness tests to stochasticity or measurement noise (no SNR sweeps).  
- Weakness: preimage-based prediction is ad hoc and not quantitatively evaluated (no NRMSE or baseline comparisons).  
- Weakness: no complexity or scalability analysis (Gram matrix memory/time costs) and no guidance on kernel/truncation selection.  
- Weakness: change-point detection derivation omits terms without empirical justification; no detection accuracy/false-alarm trade-offs reported.  
- Quality: derivations are technically sound as extensions of DMD/Arnoldi to RKHS, but lack error analysis, sample complexity, and sensitivity studies.  
- Quality: empirical illustrations are suggestive but insufficient to support practical effectiveness claims; lack quantitative metrics and head-to-head baselines.  
- Clarity: well-organized and readable; algorithms and definitions are clear; minor suggestions include consolidating implementation choices and complexity notes.  
- Originality: mainly an algorithmic packaging/exposition of known ideas; kernel principal angles link known, change-detection idea interesting but preliminary.  
- Significance: potentially useful for domains using Koopman analysis, but current experiments do not show clear advantages or novel capabilities for ML impact.  
- Requested clarifications: confirm no explicit feature maps are required (except preimage), position relative to Williams et al. (2015) and kernel DMD variants, and detail complexity/scalability.  
- Requested clarifications: explain kernel width and truncation selection, evaluate robustness to measurement/process noise, and justify neglecting terms in change-point derivation with quantitative detection metrics.  
- Suggestions: add quantitative comparisons (DMD, EDMD with polynomial/RBF dictionaries, kPCA), report eigenvalue/mode errors and prediction NRMSE, and include SNR robustness plots.  
- Suggestions: demonstrate an ML use case with baselines (GPDM, LDS/SSM, ESNs), include ablations on kernel/truncation/preimage, and provide runtime/memory scaling.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear, educational exposition unifying DMD, EDMD, and kernel PCA perspectives with an implementable algorithm.  
- Cons against acceptance: incremental novelty, insufficient quantitative empirical evaluation, and unresolved practical issues (preimage, scalability, hyperparameters).  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — solid and well-written but needs stronger empirical evidence, clearer positioning versus EDMD/kernel DMD, and noisy/practical ML demonstrations.
