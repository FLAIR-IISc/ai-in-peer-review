- Proposes a spectral method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator.  
- Extends Koopman spectral analysis with reproducing kernels to approximate eigenfunctions with richer basis functions.  
- Presents a modal decomposition algorithm based on an equivalence with Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).  
- Algorithm produces a finite-mode decomposition that can serve as feature extraction for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the Koopman operator and DMD.  
- Provides detailed algorithm description and experiments on a toy system, the Hénon map, and real-world data.  
- Empirical results show effective estimation of Koopman modes and eigenvalues.  
- Main novelty appears limited to kernelizing DMD rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Unclear whether the method outperforms alternative approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks or change-detection methods).  
- Applications (embedding, recognition, change-point detection) are not sufficiently compelling or clearly superior to existing methods.  
- Paper is technically sound but would benefit from more detailed comparisons with other approaches.  
- Clarity is generally good, but some mathematical derivations need improvement and more intuition is required.  
- Originality is limited by the primary contribution being the extension of DMD to reproducing kernels.  
- Significance is relevant but limited by lack of clear comparative advantage and demonstration of superiority.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—add detailed comparisons, demonstrate superiority, and improve derivations and intuition.
