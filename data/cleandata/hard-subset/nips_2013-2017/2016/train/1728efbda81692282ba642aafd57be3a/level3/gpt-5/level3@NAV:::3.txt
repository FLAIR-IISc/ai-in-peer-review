Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies Koopman spectral analysis in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) and proposes a “kernel DMD” algorithm that constructs orthonormal bases of a Krylov matrix in feature space and eigendecomposes the projected Koopman operator. A robust variant uses kernel PCA/POD truncation. The framework is a kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), closely related to extended DMD (EDMD) and kernel EDMD as in Williams et al., and to Arnoldi/Ritz approximations. Applications to prediction via pre-image, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition are illustrated on synthetic and real data.

Quality: The algorithmic development is technically solid at a high level, and the numerical experiments, though limited in comparison to state-of-the-art EDMD/kernel EDMD baselines, demonstrate usefulness. However, there are serious clarity/mathematical issues in Section 3.1 that need correction: (i) Equation (7) seems notationally indistinguishable from Equation (2) except that ϕ is H-valued; please explain the purpose and the distinction precisely. (ii) KH is described as an operator H→H while ϕ: M→H; as written, ϕ(x) ∈ H and (KHϕ)(x)=ϕ(f(x)) is H-valued, so the operator acts on H-valued observables (a space like L2(M;H)), not on H. You need to define the function space on which KH acts and state conditions under which composition C_f maps that space into itself (invariance, boundedness). (iii) In the proof around lines 144–157, the phrase “applying KH to both sides” appears after forming scalar equalities; operators do not act on scalars—please rewrite this step, likely by applying C_f prior to taking inner products. (iv) Theorem 1 relies on strong, partly implicit assumptions (distinct Ritz values; invertibility of R; invariance of the Krylov span under KH; ψ=0 cases). These should be made explicit and their validity discussed.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized, but notation conflates ϕ (feature map), φ (eigenfunction), and κ (representers). Please unify notation and specify domains/codomains for all operators and maps. Minor typos: Line 55 “the the”; Line 153 uses κj/λj where κi/λi is intended.

Originality: The kernelization and Krylov/QR viewpoint are valuable, but overlap with EDMD/kernel EDMD is substantial. Please clearly position novelty versus Williams et al. (JNS 2015) and exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting DMD.

Significance: If the theoretical issues are clarified and stronger empirical comparisons (against EDMD/kernel EDMD, exact/optimized DMD) are added, the work could be a useful contribution for learning Koopman spectra in RKHS.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear exposition of Koopman/DMD background; practical robustification via kernel POD.
- Useful empirical illustrations and applications.

Cons for acceptance:
- Core mathematical definitions/assumptions in Section 3.1/Theorem 1 are unclear or inconsistent.
- Limited empirical comparison to state-of-the-art kernel/extended DMD variants.
- Notational ambiguity and minor errors.

Recommendation: Borderline reject unless the operator-theoretic foundations and assumptions are clarified and comparative experiments are strengthened.
