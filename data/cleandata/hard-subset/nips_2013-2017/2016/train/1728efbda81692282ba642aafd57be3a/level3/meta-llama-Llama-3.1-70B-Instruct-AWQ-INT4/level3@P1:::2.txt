This paper proposes a spectral method for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator, which is an infinite-dimensional linear operator that represents nonlinear and finite-dimensional dynamics without linearization. The authors extend the Koopman spectral analysis with reproducing kernels to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions. They provide a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis based on the equivalent principle with Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD). The algorithm returns a decomposition of the dynamics into a finite number of modes, and thus it can be thought of as a feature extraction procedure for a nonlinear dynamical system.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the Koopman operator and DMD. They also provide a detailed description of their algorithm and its application to several examples, including a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and real-world data. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues.

However, the main novelty of the paper appears to be limited to kernelizing DMD rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach. The core algorithm (DMD) already exists in the dynamical-systems literature, and the authors' contribution is primarily in extending it to use reproducing kernels. While this extension is useful, it is not clear whether it provides significant advantages over other approaches, such as recurrent neural networks or change-detection methods, which also exist for system recognition.

The paper's applications are not very compelling in demonstrating the method's distinct advantages or uniqueness. For example, the authors apply their method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, but they do not provide a clear comparison with other methods or demonstrate the superiority of their approach. Similarly, the authors' application to sequential change-point detection is not particularly novel, as similar methods have been proposed in the literature.
