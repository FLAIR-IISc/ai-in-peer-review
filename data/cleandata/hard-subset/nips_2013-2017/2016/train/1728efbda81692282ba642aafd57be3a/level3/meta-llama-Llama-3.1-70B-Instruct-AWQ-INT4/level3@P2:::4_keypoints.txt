- Novel contribution: extends Koopman spectral analysis into RKHS for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Proposed algorithm: modal decomposition in RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Empirical evaluation: tested on simulated systems (toy system, Hénon map) and CMU motion-capture data, showing effective feature extraction.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written, with clear background, related work, and organized mathematical/algorithmic exposition.  
- Literature situating: thorough review of existing methods (POD, DMD, variants) that situates the work in context.  
- Reproducibility: provides step-by-step implementation guidance, making replication straightforward.  
- Strength: RKHS framework offers a more expressive and flexible approach for nonlinear system analysis.  
- Missing comparison: lacks direct comparison to non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis methods in simulations.  
- Kernel choice concerns: insufficient exploration of how different kernels affect accuracy and robustness.  
- Significance unclear: needs a deeper discussion of implications and concrete applications to demonstrate advancement of the state of the art.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, empirical demonstration on real and simulated data, and clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: absent baseline comparisons, insufficient kernel analysis, and limited discussion of broader significance.
