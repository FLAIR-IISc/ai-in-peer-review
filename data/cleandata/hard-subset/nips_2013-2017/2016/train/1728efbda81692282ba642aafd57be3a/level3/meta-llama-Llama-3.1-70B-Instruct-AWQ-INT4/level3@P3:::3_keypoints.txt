- Presents spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and a modal decomposition algorithm using the kernel trick (kernelized DMD).
- Main contribution: derivation of Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and a finite-data modal decomposition algorithm with a POD-based robustification.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and technically solid, with clear presentation of theoretical concepts.
- Demonstrates numerical experiments for prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.
- Numerical results are promising but comparisons to state-of-the-art methods are limited.
- Section 3.1, Equation (7) and its distinction from Equation (2) are unclear.
- Mathematical inconsistency: KH is an operator H→H while φ maps M→H, so φ may not belong to H—this needs clarification.
- Proof of Theorem 1 contains unclear steps (lines 144–157); phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" is confusing if both sides are scalar.
- Assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong, not explicitly stated, and additional assumptions appear in the proof whose validity is unclear.
- Several typos and notation errors (e.g., "the the" and \kappaj / \lambdaj vs. \kappai / \lambda_i).
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques (kernel trick + DMD) but relation to other methods (e.g., eigensystem realization) is underexplored.
- Significance: has potential as a new tool for nonlinear dynamical systems but needs broader empirical validation and discussion of limitations.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify mathematical derivations/proofs, fix typos, and provide more comparisons and discussion of limitations and applications.
