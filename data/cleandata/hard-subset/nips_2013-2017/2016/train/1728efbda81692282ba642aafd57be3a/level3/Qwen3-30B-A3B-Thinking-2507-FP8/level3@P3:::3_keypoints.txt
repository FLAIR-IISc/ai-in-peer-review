- Paper proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) by using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear kernel-based basis functions.  
- Approach builds on Rowley et al. (2009), Schölkopf et al. (1998), and is related to Williams et al.'s extended DMD but replaces explicit basis maps with kernel functions.  
- Algorithmic contribution: modal decomposition via orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator, with a POD-based robust variant.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and presents a technically sound mathematical framework (aside from noted issues).  
- Numerical experiments on synthetic and real-world data effectively demonstrate utility for state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Originality: meaningful and natural extension of DMD using kernel methods to capture richer nonlinear dynamics.  
- Main weakness: Section 3.1 has unclear mathematical foundations—KH as defined appears nearly identical to the standard Koopman operator, so the distinction is unclear.  
- Fundamental inconsistency: ϕ is defined as a map M→H but is treated as an element of H in Theorem 1, which is conceptually incorrect since H contains functions.  
- The proof of Theorem 1 contains an unclear step ("applying KH to both sides") that misapplies an operator on scalars and introduces unstated/strong assumptions.  
- Minor issues: typographical error ("the the") and inconsistent notation (\kappaj/\lambdaj vs. \kappai/\lambda_i).  
- Numerical results are convincing but lack comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendation: address and rigorously clarify the theoretical inconsistencies (distinguish KH from standard Koopman, fix ϕ treatment, and provide a correct proof of Theorem 1) before publication.
