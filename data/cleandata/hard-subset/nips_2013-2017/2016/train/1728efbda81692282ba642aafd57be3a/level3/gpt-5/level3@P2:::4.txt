Summary
This paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) and develops “kernel DMD” algorithms for estimating Koopman eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes from finite data. Section 3 shows that, by forming the Krylov matrix in feature space, one can compute empirical Ritz values/vectors via a Gram–Schmidt QR in RKHS and an eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator. Section 4 provides a robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions (a truncated, POD-like subspace) to improve conditioning—akin to the transition from “basic DMD” to exact/truncated DMD in prior work. The paper connects the resulting eigenpairs to Koopman spectra under assumptions (Theorems 1–2), then illustrates the method on a toy system and the Henon map, and demonstrates applications: short-term prediction via a pre-image procedure, sequential change-point detection via a CUSUM-style statistic, and dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles between subspaces of estimated eigenfunctions.

Relation to prior work is discussed, including DMD [25,26], its numerically improved variants [8,33], and extended DMD (EDMD) [35], as well as connections to kernel PCA and subspace/system identification. Conceptually, this work can be viewed as moving from linear monomial dictionaries to rich implicit dictionaries via kernels, with a robust implementation that mirrors SVD/POD-truncated DMD—close in spirit to (kernelized) EDMD but derived through a Krylov/RKHS lens.

Strengths
- Clear, well-organized exposition with precise operator-theoretic framing and succinct proofs that reveal how Ritz pairs in feature space approximate Koopman spectra.
- Practical robustification via kernel PCA is well motivated and aligns with best practices in DMD (truncation for conditioning).
- Sensible applications (prediction, change detection, dynamics recognition) demonstrate how Koopman features can support downstream ML tasks.
- Uses only kernel Gram matrices, enabling flexible nonlinear feature mappings without explicit bases.
- Theorem 2 provides a useful link between eigenpairs of the projected operator and Koopman eigenfunctions under reasonable richness assumptions.

Weaknesses and questions
- Empirical validation is limited in breadth and depth. The toy and Henon examples are illustrative but lack quantitative comparison to baselines.
- The relationship to EDMD (and kernel EDMD) is not fully clarified. The robust variant appears closely related to EDMD with a kernel dictionary and truncated SVD. Please delineate algorithmic and theoretical differences and discuss when KDMD offers advantages over EDMD (e.g., numerical stability, bias/variance, computational costs).
- No systematic study of kernel choice and hyperparameters (e.g., Gaussian width, polynomial degree), their impact on spectral estimation and prediction, or guidance on selection.
- Scalability concerns are not discussed: memory O(τ^2) and time O(τ^3) for Gram matrices/eigendecompositions may limit long sequences; streaming/online or Nyström approximations could be relevant.
- Noise: the method is presented primarily for deterministic systems; how robust is it under measurement/process noise? Theorems could be accompanied by perturbation/error analyses, or at least empirical stress tests.
- Pre-image reconstruction for prediction can be ill-posed; the proposed MDS-like heuristic is reasonable but may be brittle. Quantitative prediction metrics and comparisons would help.

Quality
- The technical core is sound, with correct use of RKHS machinery to express Krylov subspaces via Gram matrices and to relate spectral estimates to Koopman eigenpairs. Proof sketches are concise and appropriate for NIPS length, though assumptions (e.g., distinct eigenvalues, inclusion φj ∈ Φ and alignment with kernel PCA subspace) should be emphasized and their practical implications discussed. Numerical stability and regularization (e.g., Tikhonov) could be addressed explicitly.

Clarity
- Very well written and organized. The algorithmic steps are clear. Minor suggestions: include pseudocode for both variants; explicitly state computational complexity; clarify centering decisions and their effect on Koopman estimation (centered vs. uncentered features); and add guidance on truncation rank selection (e.g., energy threshold).

Originality
- The RKHS-based derivation of DMD and the robust kernel PCA projection are conceptually appealing. However, the practical algorithm is close to kernel EDMD/feature-space DMD using truncated SVD. The paper would benefit from a sharper positioning versus EDMD [35] and variants (including any kernelized versions), articulating what is new: e.g., the Krylov perspective in RKHS, theoretical links (Theorems 1–2), or specific algorithmic differences that improve performance.

Significance
- Extending DMD to kernels is valuable for learning nonlinear dynamics and extracting Koopman features for ML tasks. This could impact practitioners in system ID, fluid mechanics, and time-series ML. Demonstrated applications are promising, but stronger empirical evidence and comparisons are needed to convincingly establish state-of-the-art improvements.

Requested additions before acceptance
- Add quantitative comparisons on the presented simulations (toy and Henon) and at least one additional benchmark to:
  - standard/exact/truncated DMD,
  - EDMD with polynomial dictionaries,
  - and, if available, kernel EDMD baselines.
  Report eigenvalue errors (magnitude/phase), reconstruction errors, and short-horizon prediction errors.

- Provide a systematic exploration/discussion of kernel choice and hyperparameters:
  - Compare RBF widths/median heuristic vs. cross-validation,
  - polynomial degrees,
  - effect of truncation rank p,
  - sensitivity to noise.
  Simple ablations would greatly strengthen the paper.

- Clarify relation to EDMD: give a side-by-side formulation and state when KDMD reduces to/extends EDMD.

- Discuss computational complexity and potential accelerations (Nyström, randomized SVD) and any limitations for long sequences.

Pros
- Clean operator-theoretic formulation; clear algorithm and robust variant.
- Practical and potentially broadly useful extension of DMD via kernels.
- Well written with insightful connections and applications.

Cons
- Limited empirical validation and lack of comparisons to strong baselines.
- Kernel choice and hyperparameter sensitivity unaddressed.
- Novelty relative to (kernel) EDMD not crisply established.
- Scalability and noise robustness not thoroughly discussed.

Minor comments
- Ensure all references are complete (years/pages missing in the list).
- In Section 4, please specify criteria for truncation (energy threshold or scree test).
- Discuss whether centering (kernel PCA) should be used when the Koopman operator acts on uncentered observables.

Overall evaluation
A solid and clearly presented contribution that brings DMD into RKHS with a robust and practical algorithm. With the requested empirical comparisons and kernel sensitivity analysis, this paper would be a strong fit for NIPS. As is, I recommend acceptance conditional on strengthening the experimental section and clarifying the relationship to EDMD.
