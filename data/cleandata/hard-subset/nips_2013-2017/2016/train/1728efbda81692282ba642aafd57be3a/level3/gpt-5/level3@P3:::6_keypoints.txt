- Paper develops a kernelized DMD/Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS with Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint and gives direct and POD-robust algorithms.
- Method returns RKHS eigenvalues and modal vectors and is used for prediction (via preimage), dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Situates work relative to Koopman/DMD, Krylov theory, and kernel methods, closely related to EDMD and kernel-EDMD.
- Theoretical derivations are sound and consistent with known Hilbert-space operator and Ritz-value results.
- Theoretical contributions are incremental; main steps mirror existing Arnoldi/EDMD and RKHS operator literature.
- No sample-complexity, stability, or formal noise-robustness guarantees are provided.
- Algorithms are clearly described and implementable, with kernel-POD addressing ill-conditioning.
- Scalability is not analyzed; Gram matrices imply O(τ^2) memory and O(τ^3) time and no Nyström/random feature approaches are discussed.
- Experiments show toy, Henon, and locomotion demos, but comparisons to baselines are not systematic.
- Lacks quantitative metrics (spectral error, prediction RMSE, classification accuracy) and state-of-the-art baselines.
- Paper is well written, self-contained, and pedagogical with clear explanation of Arnoldi/Ritz-Koopman connection and POD motivation.
- Kernelizing DMD and projecting onto kernel PCs is a natural extension; novelty is primarily in assembling a coherent pipeline and RKHS-specific proofs.
- Practical impact for ML is unclear from current experiments; stronger use cases and comparisons are needed.
- Reviewer requests inclusion of exact/optimized DMD, EDMD/kernel-EDMD, and kPCA baselines with sensitivity/noise analyses.
- Ask authors to identify concrete ML domains where Koopman features outperform strong baselines and report task metrics and baselines.
- Highlight need to address stochasticity/noise: add noisy experiments, clarify estimator behavior, and relate to stochastic Koopman and noise-aware DMD variants.
- Question use of preimages: clarify when preimages are necessary vs. operating purely in RKHS and consider linear readouts instead of inversion.
- Request guidance and ablation on kernel choice, bandwidth, truncation rank, and bias–variance trade-offs versus EDMD with explicit bases.
- Ask for scalability analysis and discussion of approximations (Nyström, random Fourier features) and streaming updates for long sequences.
- Evaluation needs improvement: quantify locomotion recognition (e.g., kernel principal angles) and compare change-point detection to kernel tests with ROC/AUC and detection delay.
- Pros: clear pedagogy, sound algorithms, natural kernelized extension, public code and demos.
- Cons: incremental theory, limited/qualitative experiments, deterministic/noise-free focus, no scalability strategy, and preimage limitations.
- Recommendation: leaning toward weak reject; paper needs stronger empirical validation, clearer positioning vs EDMD/kernel-EDMD, treatment of noise, and demonstrated ML utility.
