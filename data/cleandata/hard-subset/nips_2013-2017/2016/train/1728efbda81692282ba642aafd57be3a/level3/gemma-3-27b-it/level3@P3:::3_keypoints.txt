- Presents a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS to capture nonlinear dynamics.  
- Core idea: use kernel methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer nonlinear bases versus standard DMD.  
- Provides a theoretical framework plus an algorithm based on Krylov subspace computations and eigendecomposition, with POD-based robustification.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real-world data, but numerical comparisons to state-of-the-art are limited.  
- Builds naturally on existing Koopman/DMD and kernel-method literature (Rowley, Schmid, Jovanović, Williams, Kernel PCA).  
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-written, and logically organized.  
- Strength: mathematical foundations appear solid and the authors show good understanding of Koopman theory, RKHS, and DMD.  
- Strength/novelty: kernel trick extension to DMD is a valuable contribution for representing nonlinear dynamics.  
- Weakness: Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are hard to follow and insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness: Purpose and distinction of defining KHϕ(x)=ϕ◦f(x) (Equation 7) and its relation to prior definitions is unclear.  
- Weakness: Mathematical inconsistency about domains—KH: H→H vs. φ: M→H—needs clarification why φ(x) can be treated as an element of H.  
- Weakness: Proof steps (lines 144–157) are vague—applying KH to scalar quantities is not justified or explained.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 relies on strong or implicit assumptions that are not explicitly stated or justified in the text.  
- Weakness: Minor typographical errors (e.g., “the the” and incorrect notation \kappaj / \lambdaj).  
- Argument for acceptance: theoretically sound, clearly written, and promising method despite limited comparisons.  
- Argument against acceptance: clarity issues in key theoretical sections, mathematical inconsistencies, unclear assumptions, and lack of thorough empirical comparison.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires major revisions addressing Section 3.1, Theorem 1 proofs/assumptions, and stronger empirical comparisons; recommendation—conditional acceptance pending major revisions.
