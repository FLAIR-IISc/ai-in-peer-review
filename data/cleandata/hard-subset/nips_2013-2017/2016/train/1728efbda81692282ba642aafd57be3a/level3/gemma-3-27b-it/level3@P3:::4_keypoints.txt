- Paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS, enabling kernel-based approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Core idea of using expressive kernel functions as bases is a natural, promising extension of DMD methods.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and presents mathematical foundations and algorithms clearly and accessibly.
- Theoretical contributions include rigorous derivations and a theorem showing equivalence to DMD under certain conditions.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets strengthens the paper’s credibility.
- Positions itself well within related literature (Koopman theory, DMD, Kernel PCA, subspace ID) with thorough, up-to-date references.
- Strength: technical soundness — derivations are rigorous and well-justified.
- Strength: originality — novel use of RKHS to overcome limitations of traditional DMD.
- Strength: significance — potential impact on control, prediction, system identification; demonstrated applications include prediction, embedding, and change-point detection.
- Weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparison to standard (non-RKHS) DMD on the same datasets.
- Weakness: limited discussion and justification of kernel choice and parameters; missing sensitivity analysis (e.g., Gaussian width, polynomial degree).
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding direct comparisons to non-RKHS DMD and a more systematic exploration/sensitivity analysis of kernel choices and parameters.
