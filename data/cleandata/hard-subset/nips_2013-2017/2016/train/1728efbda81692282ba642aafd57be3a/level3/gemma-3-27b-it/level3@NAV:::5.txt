## Review of “Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis”

This paper proposes KDMD, an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to enable a more powerful generalization for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The core idea—leveraging kernel methods to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions—is a natural and potentially valuable extension to existing DMD techniques. The authors demonstrate the method on toy problems and suggest potential applications in machine learning.

The paper’s strength lies in its theoretical foundation and the clear connection to established concepts like the Koopman operator and DMD. Extending DMD to RKHS is a logical step, and the authors correctly identify the potential benefits of using nonlinear basis functions. The inclusion of applications, while briefly discussed, hints at the broader impact of this work.  Brunton et al. (2015) provide a relevant example of the broader interest in data-driven discovery of equations and spectral analysis of dynamical systems.

However, the manuscript suffers significantly from a lack of clarity and excessive mathematical detail. The presentation is needlessly verbose, making it difficult to follow the core ideas. Section 4, concerning POD-based robustification, feels largely redundant as it reiterates concepts already established in the introduction and could be substantially condensed or integrated into the main algorithm description.  Section 5’s ‘related works’ is cursory and lacks depth, and the empirical results in Section 6 are presented without sufficient context or interpretation. The potential of the applications outlined in Section 7 remains underexplored; a summary of the results and a discussion of their implications in the conclusion would be beneficial.

Furthermore, the “nonlinear” toy case appears to be linear, requiring a careful review. The choice of polynomial test problems initially is not ideal; the advantage of KDMD truly shines when the kernel/feature map choice is non-trivial. More detail regarding the rationale behind kernel selection and its impact on performance is needed, as suggested in Section 5.

**Arguments for Acceptance:** The underlying idea is sound and represents a potentially valuable contribution to the field.

**Arguments against Acceptance:** The current presentation hinders understanding and limits the impact of the work. The manuscript feels incomplete and lacks sufficient experimental detail.

**Overall Assessment:** I recommend **rejection** in its current form. The authors should significantly revise the manuscript with a focus on clarity, conciseness, and pedagogical presentation. A rewrite emphasizing intuition and providing more illustrative examples would dramatically increase the paper’s potential for citation and impact.
