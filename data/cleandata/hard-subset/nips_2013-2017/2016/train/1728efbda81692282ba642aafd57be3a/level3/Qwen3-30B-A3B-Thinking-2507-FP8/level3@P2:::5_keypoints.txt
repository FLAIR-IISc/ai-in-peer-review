- Proposes Kernel DMD (KDMD): extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamics.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound and well-developed (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- KDMD offers a principled kernel-based approach to nonlinear Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Demonstrates applications: prediction via preimage reconstruction, dynamics recognition using kernel principal angles, and change-point detection.  
- Empirical validation provided on synthetic benchmarks (toy system and Hénon map).  
- Paper is overly mathematical and verbose; needs a more pedagogical introduction and clearer exposition of key concepts.  
- Structure issues: Section 4 (POD bases) is largely redundant and should be condensed or integrated.  
- Presentation is cryptic; many statements should be more concise and the toy example appears mischaracterized (claimed nonlinear but seems linear).  
- Evaluation is limited to synthetic data and lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and detailed analysis of when KDMD outperforms alternatives.  
- Significance unclear: needs discussion of scenarios where KDMD is particularly beneficial (e.g., when kernel/feature maps are known from domain knowledge).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after major revisions to improve clarity, remove redundancy, and expand empirical comparisons.  
- Suggested fixes: add a pedagogical intro, remove redundant sections, expand Section 6 with more examples/comparisons, clarify the toy example, and explain practical advantages of KDMD.
