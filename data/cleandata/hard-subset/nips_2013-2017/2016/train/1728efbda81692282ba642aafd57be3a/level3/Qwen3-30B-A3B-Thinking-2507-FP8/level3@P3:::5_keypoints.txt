- Proposes KDMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS using kernel feature maps to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, generalizing DMD.  
- Mathematical foundation is sound and the theory is rigorous.  
- KDMD can capture more complex nonlinear dynamics through expressive kernel functions.  
- Empirical examples (toy system and Hénon map) show KDMD can accurately estimate Koopman eigenvalues.  
- Demonstrated potential applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Primary weakness is poor presentation: overly mathematical, verbose, and repetitive, which hinders comprehension.  
- Section 4 is largely redundant with the introduction and should be removed or condensed.  
- Section 5 (Related Works) is too brief and should be expanded to better contextualize the contribution.  
- Section 6 (Empirical Example) should include illustrative examples rather than brief descriptions.  
- Section 7 (Applications) is overly condensed and needs fuller development.  
- Possible typo: the labeled “nonlinear” toy case appears polynomial/linear and could be handled by standard DMD with quadratic monomials.  
- The paper lacks a detailed discussion of when KDMD offers advantages over standard DMD; current tests are polynomial where standard DMD may suffice.  
- Needs more thorough experimental comparisons with standard and extended DMD variants to establish practical benefits.  
- Originality: novel and meaningful extension of DMD to RKHS via kernel methods, distinct from basis-function extended DMD.  
- Significance is promising but limited by presentation and underdeveloped applications.  
- Recommendation: technically strong but unpolished; reject in current form with encouragement to rewrite for clarity, remove redundancies, expand applications, and clarify advantages.
