- Proposal: Introduces Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD) — a kernelized extension of DMD/EDMD performing Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS using Krylov-sequence feature snapshots.

- Core method: Computes an orthogonal basis in feature space via Gram–Schmidt/QR using Gram matrices, then eigendecomposes the finite-dimensional Koopman projection to obtain Ritz values and modes.

- Robust variant: Projects data onto kernel-PCA (POD) directions to improve conditioning and enable rank truncation.

- Applications: Demonstrates short-term prediction (via pre-image), motion recognition (subspace angles), and sequential change-point detection (exponential-family model with Koopman features).

- Relation to prior work: Closely related to EDMD and DMD literature; leverages the kernel trick to implicitly use rich nonlinear dictionaries and mirrors POD-based robustification in DMD.

- Strength — conceptual: KDMD is a natural, powerful generalization enabling richer dictionaries without manual basis selection.

- Strength — theory/derivation: Solid linear-algebraic formulation (Arnoldi/Ritz equivalence, QR-based derivations) and justified kernel-PCA robustification.

- Strength — practicality: Computation reduces to Gram-matrix operations; truncation improves numerical stability; snapshot-pairs remark broadens applicability.

- Strength — applications: Recognition and change-point examples highlight utility beyond classical physics/fluid problems.

- Weakness — exposition: Paper is overly mathematical and verbose; needs clearer, more pedagogical presentation, pseudocode, and pipeline diagrams.

- Weakness — structure: Section organization is repetitive (kernel-PCA section), related work is too long, and empirical section is too condensed — reflow and expand empirical details.

- Weakness — experiments: Empirical validation is limited, relies on near-polynomial tests; needs non-polynomial, noisy/stochastic, and misspecified-kernel experiments plus stronger baselines.

- Weakness — comparisons: Should compare quantitatively to EDMD with hand-crafted dictionaries, exact/optimized DMD, and Gaussian-process dynamical models where appropriate.

- Weakness — model/kernel selection: Lacks guidance on kernel choice, bandwidth/degree selection, truncation level, and cross-validation procedures.

- Weakness — computational/regularization detail: Needs discussion of complexity and memory scaling with τ and p, ill-conditioning, and regularization (e.g., Tikhonov).

- Weakness — theoretical assumptions: Assumes distinct eigenvalues and eigenfunctions in kernel span; should discuss multiplicities, continuous spectra, finite-data/noise effects, and non-normal dynamics.

- Weakness — specific application details: Pre-image step needs comparison to standard pre-image methods and direct-observable prediction; change-point detection needs clearer derivation, baselines, and metrics.

- Quality: Technical content is sound and well-grounded, but empirical validation and implementation details (centering, normalization, regularizers, runtimes) are insufficient.

- Clarity: Dense notation and verbosity reduce accessibility; recommend shortening and adding high-level intuition and pseudocode.

- Originality: Meaningful, nontrivial synthesis of kernel methods with DMD/Koopman analysis; robust kernel-PCA projection and application suite add value though related ideas exist.

- Significance: Potentially impactful if clarity and empirical rigor are improved; current evidence promising but not definitive.

- Specific requests: Fix possible typos in toy system, clarify reduction to EDMD/exact DMD, add Brunton et al. (2015) citation, and provide code and runtimes.

- Recommendation: Support acceptance conditional on major clarity improvements and substantially stronger empirical validation; otherwise recommend revision.
