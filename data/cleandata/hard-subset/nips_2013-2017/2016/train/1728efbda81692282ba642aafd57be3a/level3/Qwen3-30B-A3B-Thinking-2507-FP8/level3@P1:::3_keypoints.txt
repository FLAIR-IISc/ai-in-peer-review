- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in an RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Core algorithm: compute orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecompose the Koopman operator projection; robustified via kernel PCA for numerical stability.  
- Builds on Koopman/DMD literature and extended DMD, aligning with prior kernel approaches (e.g., Kawahara, Williams).  
- Demonstrates applications: state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with experiments on synthetic and real data (Hénon map, locomotion).  
- Strengths: very well written and organized, clear algorithmic exposition, sound mathematical development, and practically useful robustification; locomotion example is compelling.  
- Fundamental mathematical inconsistency: Section 3.1 Eq.(7) defines KHφ(x)=φ∘f(x) yet KH is claimed to map H→H while φ maps M→H, creating a domain/codomain mismatch.  
- Flawed proof of Theorem 1: applying KH to both sides is ill-posed because the objects acted on appear to be scalars/vectors, not elements of H, and v_j are not defined as elements of H.  
- Theorem 1 implicitly assumes φ_j(x)=⟨φ(x), κ_j⟩ for some κ_j∈H, a property not established or justified in the paper.  
- Notational errors and typos (e.g., repeated words, inconsistent λ notation) that reduce clarity.  
- Needs a clearer, more rigorous mathematical foundation clarifying the relationship between feature map φ, RKHS H, and the Koopman operator KH, and separating functions in H from feature-space vectors.  
- Lacks sufficient empirical comparison to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Williams et al. extended DMD) in performance and computational complexity.  
- Overall significance is promising, but unresolved foundational issues make the technical contribution questionable; paper is borderline pending mathematical clarification.
