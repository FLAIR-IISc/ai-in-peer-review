- Proposes kernelized dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis, using Krylov orthogonal bases and eigendecomposition for tasks like prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Properly situates work within existing literature (Koopman operator, DMD, POD, kernel PCA) and notes links to subspace identification methods.
- Theoretical foundation and algorithm are clearly presented and mathematically sound.
- Robustification via POD bases is a useful practical addition addressing conditioning issues.
- Empirical examples demonstrate functionality but are relatively simple and not very convincing.
- Contribution is incremental—kernelizing DMD is logical but not a fundamental novelty to experts familiar with kernel methods.
- Real-world evaluation (locomotion/CMU mocap) is superficial, uses a single dataset, and lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., RNNs, other kernel classifiers).
- Change-point detection application is interesting but lacks benchmarking against existing methods.
- Limited hyperparameter tuning and absence of comparative analyses weaken empirical evidence.
- Writing is generally clear but mathematical notation is dense and more intuitive explanations and parameter discussion are needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: sound mathematics, robustification technique, good literature integration and presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, weak and narrow empirical evaluation, superficial applications.
- Overall assessment: weak accept—technically solid but needs stronger experiments and clearer practical advantages to merit higher ranking.
