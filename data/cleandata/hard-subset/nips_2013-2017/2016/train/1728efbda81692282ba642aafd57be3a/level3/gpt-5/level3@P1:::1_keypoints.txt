- Paper proposes a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using Krylov/Arnoldi ideas and computes orthogonal bases of the feature-space Krylov subspace from data.  
- Robust implementation projects onto kernel-POD directions to enable truncation and mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) link empirical Ritz values/vectors to Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under span assumptions.  
- Demonstrates three applications: short-term prediction via preimage, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and change-point detection via a CUSUM-like statistic.  
- Experiments on toy examples, Hénon map, motion capture, and synthetic change-point scenarios show feasibility but are limited in scope.  
- Method is conceptually close to DMD/EDMD; the distinction from EDMD (kernelized dictionary, operator diagonalized, bias/variance, compute cost) is under-explained and needs explicit comparison.  
- Missing related work on kernel-based dynamical modeling and time-series kernels (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché-Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Strength: clean, principled extension of DMD into RKHS with a clear Krylov/eigenspace derivation and sensible kernel-PCA robustification.  
- Strength: applications illustrate using Koopman features beyond visualization (prediction, classification, online detection).  
- Weakness: no analysis or experiments for stochastic/process or observation noise; regularization needs and behavior under noise are unaddressed.  
- Weakness: the “pairs {(x1,x2)} suffice” claim needs discussion of assumptions (stationarity, ergodicity, causality) and limitations for delays/partial observability.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks competitive baselines for dynamics recognition (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and change detection (kernel CUSUM, density-ratio detectors).  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—kernel methods’ O(τ^2) memory and O(τ^3) time—no complexity analysis or discussion of approximations (Nyström, random features).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to kernel choice, hyperparameters, truncation, and preimage failure modes are not explored.  
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions (ψ=0, φj∈Φ, κj∈span(U)) are strong; no discussion of approximation error when violated.  
- Quality: derivations are sound and robustness is well motivated, but empirical validation and practical guidance (hyperparameters, complexity) are insufficient.  
- Clarity: generally clear with helpful intuition, but needs clearer EDMD comparison, explicit algorithmic summary (inputs/outputs/complexity), and clarification of eigenvalue conditions and preimage assumptions.  
- Originality: feature-space QR/Krylov formulation is a useful perspective but novelty over EDMD is incremental without clearer differentiation.  
- Significance: potentially impactful if better positioned and validated with stronger experiments and baselines.  
- Suggestions: add explicit conceptual and empirical EDMD comparison, expand related work, include noisy/stochastic experiments, add quantitative baselines, discuss complexity and scalable approximations, and clarify pairs vs. sequences assumptions.  
- Minor comments: fix typos (τ−1 vs τ in kernel PCA setup; punctuation in k(xi,xj) equation).  
- Recommendation: borderline reject in current form; could be strong after addressing EDMD distinction, stronger empirical evidence (including stochastic settings), expanded related work, and scalability/hyperparameter analysis.
