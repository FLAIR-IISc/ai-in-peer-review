This paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS), termed KDMD, for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors provide a modal decomposition algorithm that leverages the expressiveness of kernel functions to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions. The method is applied to toy problems and demonstrates a powerful generalization of DMD.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough theoretical foundation for KDMD. The algorithm is well-defined, and the empirical examples demonstrate its effectiveness. The connection to previous work on DMD and the Koopman operator is clearly established, and the authors provide a comprehensive review of related techniques.

However, the manuscript is overly mathematical and verbose, making it challenging to follow for a non-expert audience. The text is dense with technical details, and many statements could be more concise. The authors should strive to provide clearer, shorter explanations to facilitate a broader understanding of their work.

The paper's organization could also be improved. Section 4 appears redundant, as the main ideas are already introduced in the introduction. Section 5, which discusses related work, would benefit from examples or a summary in the conclusion to provide a clearer connection to the proposed method. Section 6, which presents empirical examples, is overly condensed and should be developed in a more detailed manner.

A minor concern is the description of the "nonlinear" toy case, which appears to be linear. Additionally, the first two test problems involve polynomial kernels, but the paper could benefit from more detail on how to choose the kernel or feature map, as discussed in Section 5.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel extension of DMD to RKHS, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of KDMD on toy problems, and the method has the potential to be applied to more complex systems.

The significance of the paper lies in its ability to provide a more accurate and robust spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed method has the potential to be used in various fields, including physics, engineering, and machine learning.

In conclusion, while the paper is technically sound, it would benefit from a more pedagogical rewrite to make it more accessible to a broader audience. With clearer explanations and a more concise presentation, the paper has the potential to be highly citable and influential in the field.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel extension of DMD to RKHS, which is a significant contribution to the field.
