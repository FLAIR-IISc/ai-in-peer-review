- Proposal: extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator.
- Algorithm: kernel-based eigen-decomposition of the Koopman operator from observed trajectories using kernel PCA and subspace-analysis ideas.
- Connection to prior work: links classical DMD literature with kernel methods and relates closely to Extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015).
- Strength: elegant framing of function composition as a linear operator in RKHS and a technically sound method for extracting dynamic modes.
- Empirical: demonstrated on system embedding, recognition, and change-point detection with promising results on selected datasets.
- Weakness: originality is limited—kernelization of DMD appears incremental rather than a fundamental advance.
- Weakness: insufficiently clarifies distinctions from closely related methods such as Extended DMD.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to strong, standard baselines (e.g., RNNs and robust change-detection methods).
- Weakness: chosen applications do not compellingly demonstrate scenarios where the method uniquely outperforms established techniques.
- Overall: technically solid and interesting fusion of ideas, but limited novelty and unconvincing experiments weaken the paper’s impact.
