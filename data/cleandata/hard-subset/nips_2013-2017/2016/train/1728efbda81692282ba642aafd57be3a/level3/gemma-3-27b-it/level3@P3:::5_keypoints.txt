- Proposes KDMD: extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using kernel methods.  
- Core idea is promising and potentially powerful for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Builds on DMD and spectral methods literature; RKHS extension can capture more complex dynamics.  
- Manuscript is overly dense and mathematically verbose, hindering clarity and accessibility.  
- Presentation is repetitive and lacks intuition and pedagogical exposition for a NeurIPS audience.  
- Section 4 (POD-based robustification) is largely redundant and should be condensed or merged into Section 3.  
- Section 5 (Related Works) is inadequate: it lists papers without critically assessing differences or elaborating links to subspace identification.  
- Section 6 (empirical) is too condensed: kernel choices and parameter impacts (e.g., kernel width) are unexplained and analysis is insufficient.  
- Minor correctness/clarity issue: the “nonlinear” toy case is actually linear; first two tests are polynomial and don’t showcase KDMD’s strengths.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel RKHS extension of DMD, theoretical soundness, and promising test results.  
- Rejection arguments: poor clarity, redundancy, weak empirical evaluation, insufficient related-work discussion, and lack of pedagogical approach.  
- Recommendation: Reject with strong encouragement to rewrite—streamline notation, condense Section 4, expand/contextualize Section 5, deepen empirical evaluation, and fix the toy-case typo.
