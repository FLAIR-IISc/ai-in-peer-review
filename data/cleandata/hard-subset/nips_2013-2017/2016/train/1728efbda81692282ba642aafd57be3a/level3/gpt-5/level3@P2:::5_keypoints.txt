- Proposes KDMD: a kernelized DMD/Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using Krylov matrices of feature maps and kernel-based Gram–Schmidt, with POD/kernel PCA truncation for robustness.
- Describes reconstruction of Koopman modes/eigenfunctions in RKHS and applications to short-term prediction (pre-image), dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Empirical demonstrations include a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, motion-capture locomotion data, and a change-point example.

Strengths
- Conceptually clear idea: lifting Koopman analysis to RKHS enables rich nonlinear features with DMD-like Krylov methods.
- Technically sound derivation: feature-space Arnoldi/QR perspective and kernel trick appear correct.
- Robustification via POD/kernel PCA truncation is well motivated to address DMD conditioning issues.
- Potentially useful applications in prediction, recognition, and change-point detection.

Weaknesses
- Manuscript is overly verbose, hard to follow, and would benefit from a pedagogical rewrite, figures, and pseudocode.
- Novelty is unclear: substantial overlap with EDMD/kernel EDMD (Williams et al. 2015/2016) and insufficient contrast or new guarantees.
- Empirical evaluation is limited, largely qualitative, small-scale, and lacks rigorous comparisons to standard baselines (exact/optimized DMD, EDMD, kernel EDMD, SP-DMD).
- Minimal guidance or analysis on kernel/feature selection and sensitivity, despite kernel choice being critical.
- Some sections are redundant or poorly balanced.

Quality
- Theory: constructions are plausible but mostly repackage known DMD/Arnoldi results; missing deeper insights like approximation error bounds, recovery conditions, sample complexity, or noise stability.
- Experiments: illustrative but not comprehensive—missing quantitative metrics, ablations (truncation rank, kernel width), and baseline comparisons.
- Evaluation honesty: paper acknowledges conditioning and pre-image issues but should discuss limitations more candidly.

Clarity
- Writing is dense and notation inconsistent; needs streamlined derivations, standardized notation, and algorithm boxes/pseudocode.
- Section balance problems: Sections 3–4 could be unified, related work should be condensed, and empirical sections need expansion and clearer setups.

Originality
- Moderate: kernelizing DMD and Gram-matrix projections are known; main contributions are RKHS-Arnoldi framing and a kernel PCA robustification, which feel incremental without clearer differentiation.

Significance
- Potentially impactful if contributions are clarified and empirically validated; current overlap with prior work and weak experiments limit significance.

Detailed suggestions
- Clearly state theoretical/algorithmic differences vs. EDMD/kernel EDMD and highlight any concrete benefits (conditioning, complexity, guarantees).
- Provide a single unified algorithm with truncation options, pseudocode, complexity analysis, and move lengthy proofs to the supplement.
- Integrate key related work into intro/method and tighten the related-work section.
- Expand experiments with quantitative benchmarks (eigenvalue/mode accuracy, forecasting error, noise robustness, runtime) against standard datasets (e.g., cylinder wake, FitzHugh–Nagumo).
- Include sensitivity analyses for kernel choice (bandwidth, polynomial degree) and guidance for practitioners.
- Compare the proposed MDS-based pre-image to standard pre-image methods and quantify forecasting performance.
- Fix minor inconsistencies, clarify toy system construction, and correct typos/notation.

Arguments for acceptance
- Sound, principled RKHS extension of DMD with a reasonable robustification and timely relevance.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited novelty relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD, poor presentation, and insufficient empirical validation and practical guidance.

Overall recommendation
- Weak reject; the paper could become valuable after substantial rewrite, clearer distinction from prior work, and stronger empirical comparisons.
