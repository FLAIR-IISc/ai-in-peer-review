

# Review for NIPS 2016

This paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm that approximates the Koopman operator in RKHS, which is a significant extension of standard DMD that typically uses linear monomials of observables as basis functions. The approach is theoretically grounded in the spectral analysis of the Koopman operator, with the key insight that kernel methods provide a richer set of nonlinear basis functions compared to the linear approximation used in standard DMD.

The paper makes connections to relevant prior work, including Williams et al.'s extended DMD (2015) and the foundational work on Koopman operators by Mezić (2005) and Rowley et al. (2009). The authors correctly position their work as a generalization of DMD that uses kernel methods to capture more complex dynamics, rather than being a simple extension of existing techniques. The paper also references related work on kernel methods for dynamical systems, including Kawahara et al. (2012), which provides context for their approach.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible to readers.
2. The theoretical development is technically sound, with a clear presentation of the algorithmic steps for both the basic and robustified versions of the method.
3. The authors provide meaningful applications of their method to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, with empirical results on both synthetic and real-world data.
4. The robustification approach using POD bases is well-motivated and addresses a known limitation of standard DMD implementations.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The paper contains several mathematical inconsistencies that require clarification. In Section 3.1, Equation (7) defines KHφ(x) = φ∘f(x), but KH is stated to be an operator H→H while φ is a map M→H. This creates a fundamental confusion about the domain and codomain of the operator. The phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" in the proof of Theorem 1 (lines 144-157) is particularly problematic since both sides of the equation appear to be scalar values, not elements of H.

2. The assumptions in Theorem 1 are too strong and not clearly stated. The proof introduces additional assumptions (e.g., that φj(x) = ⟨φ(x), κj⟩) that are not explicitly mentioned in the theorem statement. These assumptions need to be clearly justified or stated as part of the theorem.

3. The paper lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods. While the authors demonstrate the approach on several examples, they do not compare against other kernel-based DMD variants or relevant spectral analysis methods for dynamical systems.

4. There are minor technical issues: "the the" on line 55, and inconsistent use of \kappaj/\lambdaj instead of \kappai/\lambda_i on line 153.

**Originality:**
The paper presents a novel combination of kernel methods and Koopman spectral analysis. While there are prior works on DMD extensions (e.g., Williams et al., 2015), the use of RKHS for this purpose appears to be original. The paper clearly differentiates its approach from previous work by emphasizing the nonlinear basis functions provided by kernel methods versus the linear monomials used in standard DMD.

**Significance:**
The proposed method has potential significance for feature extraction in nonlinear dynamical systems. The applications demonstrated (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are relevant to both machine learning and scientific computing. However, the significance would be stronger with more comprehensive comparisons to existing methods and clearer demonstration of how the approach advances the state-of-the-art.

**Recommendation:**
The paper presents an interesting extension of DMD using kernel methods for Koopman spectral analysis. While the theoretical framework is promising, the current presentation contains significant mathematical inconsistencies that need to be addressed before the paper can be considered for publication. The paper would benefit from a more precise mathematical formulation, particularly regarding the domain/codomain of the operators, and more comprehensive experimental comparisons to establish the method's advantages over existing approaches.

The authors should clarify the relationship between the Koopman operator KH and the feature map φ, explicitly state all assumptions needed for their theorems, and provide more thorough comparisons with state-of-the-art methods. With these revisions, the paper would make a valuable contribution to the field of dynamical systems analysis.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- Novel extension of DMD using kernel methods that provides richer basis functions for Koopman spectral analysis
- Well-organized and clearly written paper with good theoretical development
- Demonstrated applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with empirical results
- Robustification approach using POD bases is practical and well-motivated

*Con:*
- Significant mathematical inconsistencies in the theoretical development
- Weak experimental comparisons to state-of-the-art methods
- Assumptions for theorems are not clearly stated or justified
- The significance of the approach is not sufficiently demonstrated against existing methods

Overall, this paper has promising ideas but requires substantial revisions to address the mathematical inconsistencies before it can be recommended for acceptance.
