- Proposes spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS and a novel modal decomposition algorithm via the kernel trick.  
- Extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to richer nonlinear basis functions using RKHS.  
- Derives core algorithm and a robustified variant that projects onto principal component directions for numerical stability.  
- Demonstrates utility through numerical experiments on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Positions work relative to extended DMD and subspace identification; claims novelty in direct RKHS formulation and analysis.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, well-argued motivation, good organization, and sufficient background for DMD/kernel-aware readers.  
- Potential impact: kernelizing DMD is a natural, powerful extension with significant promise for data-driven analysis of complex systems.  
- Technical work is largely solid, but numerical results lack extensive comparison to state-of-the-art alternatives.  
- Major flaw: Section 3.1 (core theoretical development) lacks clarity, undermining the soundness of main claims.  
- Ambiguity in defining the Koopman operator in RKHS—unclear how (KHϕ)(x)=ϕ∘f(x) defines a linear operator on H and how it differs from the standard Koopman operator.  
- Mathematical inconsistency: KH is described as H→H while its eigenfunctions φj are elements of H, with unclear justification for how an operator defined via ϕ can have eigenfunctions in H.  
- Theorem 1’s proof is hard to follow; the claim MτF = KHMτ is a large, unjustified leap and the proof mixes operator and matrix algebra while implicitly assuming operator invertibility.  
- Minor issues: typos (e.g., repeated words).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but willing to reconsider if the fundamental mathematical issues are rigorously addressed in a revision.
