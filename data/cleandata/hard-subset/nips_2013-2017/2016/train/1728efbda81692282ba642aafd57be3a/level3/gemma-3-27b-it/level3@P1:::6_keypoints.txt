- Introduces a kernelized DMD method in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis using orthogonal bases of the Krylov matrix to obtain finite-dimensional dynamics representations.
- Extends standard DMD to move beyond linear monomial bases by leveraging RKHS and kernel methods.
- Provides clear theoretical exposition of Koopman operator and DMD, with well-presented mathematical derivations.
- Presents a practical algorithm for kernelized DMD with pedagogical clarity suitable for non-experts.
- Demonstrates applications (prediction, dynamic pattern recognition, sequential change-point detection) on synthetic data (toy system, Henon map) and a locomotion dataset.
- Situates the work within existing literature, citing POD, balanced modes, DMD variants, kPCA, and subspace identification appropriately.
- Strength: clear, well-written, and pedagogically valuable presentation of the method and theory.
- Strength: algorithm is clearly outlined and includes illustrative application examples.
- Weakness: limited and unclear comparative analysis—lacks standard DMD and kPCA baselines, especially on toy examples.
- Weakness: empirical validation is weak; locomotion demo lacks specific metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Originality is incremental—extension to RKHS is natural given prior RKHS and kernel literature.
- Main contribution is the specific algorithm for decomposition rather than surprising new theoretical results.
- Significance is unclear due to lack of convincing practical utility and absence of concrete target application domains.
- Deterministic setup limits applicability to noisy ML problems and broader real-world scenarios.
- Computational cost concerns: unclear complexity and potential limitations if explicit feature maps are required.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear exposition, solid theoretical foundation in RKHS and Koopman theory, potential impact on nonlinear dynamics analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons, weak empirical results, unclear practical significance, deterministic assumptions, and possible computational costs.
- Overall assessment: technically sound but under-validated empirically; reviewer recommends weak reject and asks for stronger comparative analysis, clearer application use-cases, noise handling, and computation-cost clarification before resubmission.
