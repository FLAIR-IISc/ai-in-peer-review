- Proposes a kernel-based Koopman spectral analysis: a DMD formulation in RKHS using the kernel trick to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions and obtain modal decompositions of nonlinear dynamics.

- Strength: Elegant, non-parametric RKHS formulation with sound theoretical derivations; demonstrates potential via applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Originality/positioning weak: paper does not clearly distinguish itself from prior work, notably Extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015); the robustified method appears similar and needs explicit comparison.

- Literature gap: overlooks a substantial body of kernel-based dynamical-systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2016; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2008; Mouattamid & Schaback 2021; Franz & Schölkopf 2006).

- Scope limitation: analysis restricted to deterministic systems with no discussion of stochastic settings or method limitations under noise.

- Methodological clarity lacking: unclear practical implications of using consecutive-state pairs versus longer sequences for high-dimensional time series; underlying causal assumptions are not made explicit.

- Experimental shortcomings: experiments are not ambitious and comparisons are insufficient — need benchmarking against time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means) for dynamics recognition and against state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection methods.

- Minor issues: probable indexing typo on page 4 (τ−1 vs τ in Eq. 6a).

- Minor issues: notation typo in kernel expression on page 7: `k(xi.xj)` should be `k(xi, xj)`.
