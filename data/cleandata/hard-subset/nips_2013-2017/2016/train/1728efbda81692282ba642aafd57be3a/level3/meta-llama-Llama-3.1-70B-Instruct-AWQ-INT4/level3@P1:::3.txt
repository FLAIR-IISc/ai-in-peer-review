This paper proposes a novel spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors extend the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm, which is a popular approach for estimating the Koopman eigenvalues and modes from data. The proposed method, referred to as kernel DMD, uses the kernel trick to map the data into a higher-dimensional feature space, allowing for a more accurate representation of the nonlinear dynamics.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, presenting the theoretical concepts clearly and concisely. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the Koopman operator and its relation to DMD, as well as the extension to RKHSs. The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman eigenvalues and modes, and the authors provide a robustifying method to improve the accuracy of the algorithm.

One of the major strengths of the paper is its technical solidity. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the kernel DMD algorithm and its connection to the Koopman operator. The numerical results illustrate the usefulness of the proposed method, despite limited comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms.

However, there are some concerns and questions regarding the paper. Specifically, Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear. The purpose and distinction between Equation (7) and Equation (2) are not explicitly stated. Additionally, there appears to be a mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH, which is an operator H→H, but φ is a map M→H, and φ may not belong to H. This needs further explanation.

Furthermore, the proof of Theorem 1 is unclear, particularly the phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" in lines 144-157. It is not clear what this means, as both sides of the equation appear to be scalar. Additionally, the assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicitly stated, and additional assumptions appear in the proof, whose validity is unclear.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel extension of the DMD algorithm using the kernel trick. However, the connection to other spectral analysis methods, such as proper orthogonal decomposition (POD), is not thoroughly discussed.

Regarding significance, the paper addresses an important problem in nonlinear dynamical systems, and the proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable. The numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method, and the authors provide a robustifying method to improve the accuracy of the algorithm.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically solid, but some clarifications and explanations are needed to address the concerns and questions raised above. With some revisions to address these issues, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel extension of the DMD algorithm using the kernel trick.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized, presenting the theoretical concepts clearly and concisely.
* The numerical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper addresses an important problem in nonlinear dynamical systems.

Arguments against acceptance:

* Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear.
* Mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH.
* Assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicitly stated.
* Limited comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms.

Recommendations for revision:

* Clarify the purpose and distinction between Equation (7) and Equation (2).
* Address the mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH.
* Provide a clear explanation of the proof of Theorem 1, particularly the phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides".
* Explicitly state the assumptions in Theorem 1 and provide a clear explanation of their validity.
* Consider adding more comparisons to state-of-the-art algorithms to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
