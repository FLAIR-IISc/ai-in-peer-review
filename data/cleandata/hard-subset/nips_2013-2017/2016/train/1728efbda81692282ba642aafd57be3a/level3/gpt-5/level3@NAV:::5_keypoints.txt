- Proposes KDMD: a kernel-RKHS extension of DMD that builds a Krylov subspace via kernel evaluations and computes Ritz values/vectors; includes a robust variant using kernel PCA/POD truncation.
- Theoretical contributions include theorems linking empirical Ritz objects to Koopman spectra and an Arnoldi/Gram–Schmidt perspective unifying DMD with kernel spectral methods.
- Methodologically sound, but empirical validation is thin and largely qualitative.
- Missing experimental baselines and quantitative comparisons (EDMD, exact/optimized DMD) and lacking metrics for performance evaluation.
- Insufficient analysis of sensitivity to kernel choice, truncation level, noise, and sample size.
- Manuscript is overly mathematical, verbose, and cryptic; needs a pedagogical rewrite with tighter exposition, intuition, diagrams, and pseudocode.
- Section 4 is largely redundant and could be cut; Section 5 should be condensed or integrated into the conclusion; Section 6 should be expanded with quantitative experiments.
- Originality is moderate: RKHS generalization is natural, but the Krylov/QR perspective and robustification add useful novelty.
- Potentially high significance if presented clearly; KDMD could be widely used in ML and physics.
- Minor fixes: clarify the “nonlinear” toy example (possible typo), and note that initial tests are polynomial where kernels/feature maps are obvious.
- Provide practical guidance and examples where kernels/feature maps are not obvious, as suggested in Sec. 5.
- Cite and position the work against Brunton et al. (2015) and Williams et al. (2015).
- Arguments for acceptance: principled kernel extension of DMD with solid theoretical links to Koopman theory and broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity/organization and limited, mostly qualitative experiments without comparisons or kernel/truncation guidance.
