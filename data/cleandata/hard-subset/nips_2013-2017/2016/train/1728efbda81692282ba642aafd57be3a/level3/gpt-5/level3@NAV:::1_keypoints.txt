- Proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and derives a kernelized DMD (kDMD) that builds a Krylov subspace in feature space and computes Ritz values/vectors via Gram operations.
- Introduces a robust variant using kernel PCA/POD truncation to stabilize computations.
- Provides theoretical results linking the computed eigenpairs to Koopman spectra.
- Demonstrates applications for prediction (preimage heuristic), dynamics recognition (principal angles), and change-point detection.
- Conceptually intersects DMD/Koopman analysis and kernel methods, closely related to Williams et al. (2015) EDMD and kernel EDMD variants.
- Strength: mathematically clean operator-theoretic formulation with practical algorithms that avoid explicit feature maps.
- Strength: POD-based stabilization is sensible and aligns with common DMD practice; method can be computationally attractive for moderate snapshot counts.
- Weakness: Insufficiently distinguished from Williams et al. (2015)/kernel EDMD—needs explicit algorithmic and empirical comparisons, especially for the POD/stabilized variant.
- Weakness: Related-work coverage is incomplete; should cite and position relative to kernel-based dynamical systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).
- Weakness: Treatment of stochastic settings (CUSUM) is informal—requires principled Koopman–Markov/operator discussion and experiments on stochastic dynamics.
- Weakness: Data/causal assumptions are unclear—need guidance on when consecutive-pair data suffice versus requiring lagged embeddings or sequences for multivariate/weakly coupled systems.
- Weakness: Experiments are modest; request stronger baselines (EDMD/kernel EDMD, optimized DMD, kernel k-means, subspace-kernel methods, MMD/RuLSIF/BOCPD kernel CPD) and runtime/scalability comparisons (memory O(τ^2), time O(τ^3)).
- Pros: Clear operator-theoretic framing, practical kernelized algorithm, promising feature-extraction pipeline for nonlinear dynamics.
- Cons: Insufficient novelty relative to EDMD, missing related work, limited empirical validation, and unclear stochastic/causal assumptions.
- Minor: Two typos noted (indexing τ−1 vs τ; use comma instead of dot in k(xi, xj) in equation).
