This paper presents a novel approach to Koopman spectral analysis by extending it to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS). The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences generated from a nonlinear system. The algorithm is based on the equivalent principle with dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) and provides a feature extraction procedure for nonlinear dynamical systems.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The manuscript is organized in a logical manner, making it easy to follow. The authors also provide a thorough review of related works, including spectral analysis techniques, DMD, and its variants.

The proposed algorithm is evaluated on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in estimating the Koopman eigenvalues and modes. The authors also provide several applications using extracted features, including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a novel extension of DMD to RKHS, which allows for the use of nonlinear basis functions. This is a significant improvement over traditional DMD, which relies on linear monomials of observables as basis functions. The authors also provide a robust version of the algorithm using proper orthogonal decomposition (POD) bases, which can handle ill-conditioned data.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the authors do not provide a comparison with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis in the simulations. This would be useful to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach. Secondly, the authors do not discuss the choice of kernel and its impact on the results. This is an important aspect, as the choice of kernel can significantly affect the performance of the algorithm. The authors could provide some simulations or experiments to demonstrate the effect of different kernels on the results.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to Koopman spectral analysis by extending it to RKHS. This is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a new tool for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors also provide a thorough review of related works, which demonstrates their understanding of the existing literature.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed algorithm can be used to analyze complex systems, and the extracted features can be used for various applications such as prediction, control, and recognition. The authors also provide several examples of applications, which demonstrates the practicality of the proposed approach.

In conclusion, this paper presents a novel approach to Koopman spectral analysis by extending it to RKHS. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, and the manuscript is well-organized. The proposed algorithm is evaluated on both simulated and real-world data, demonstrating its effectiveness in estimating the Koopman eigenvalues and modes. However, the authors could improve the paper by providing a comparison with non-RKHS Koopman spectral analysis and discussing the choice of kernel and its impact on the results.
