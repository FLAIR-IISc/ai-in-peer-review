- Introduces an RKHS-based DMD method for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Proposes an algorithm to estimate Koopman eigenvalues and eigenvectors from data using kernel functions instead of linear monomial bases.
- Demonstrates applicability on synthetic and real-world tasks: state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Core idea is sound and builds on Koopman operator theory to capture complex dynamics without explicit system ID.
- Framing as a DMD generalization via the equivalent principle provides a clear conceptual foundation.
- Empirical results show feasibility and initial validation of performance.
- Related-work discussion is limited and misses key kernel-based dynamical systems papers (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc 2004; Tuia et al. 2011; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2015; Mouattamid & Schaback 2017; Franz & Schölkopf 2006).
- No clear theoretical or empirical comparison with extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015); novelty claims are not substantiated against it.
- Practical implications and differences of projecting onto POD directions versus existing extended DMD formulations are not clarified.
- Method’s applicability to stochastic dynamical systems and robustness to noise is not explored.
- Use of consecutive observables (vs full sequences) may be limiting for high-dimensional time series; causal assumptions and potential information loss are not discussed.
- Experimental evaluation is modest and lacks comparisons to established baselines (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels, online kernel change-point detection).
- Locomotion data example is promising but needs deeper analysis and stronger comparative evaluation.
- Minor typographical errors need correction (e.g., “tau-1” → “τ-1”, “k(xi.xj)” → “k(xi, xj)”).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS extension of DMD, solid theoretical grounding in Koopman theory, and empirical feasibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete related work review, missing extended DMD comparison, lack of stochastic-system treatment, and insufficiently rigorous experiments.
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable contribution but current manuscript has significant gaps; reviewer recommends weak reject pending revisions addressing related work, comparisons, stochasticity, and experiments.
