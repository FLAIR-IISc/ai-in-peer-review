- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear kernels.  
- Method computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performs eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator to obtain modal decompositions.  
- Builds on existing Koopman/DMD literature (notably Williams et al.'s Extended DMD); kernelization is an instantiation of pre-determined basis approaches.  
- Mathematical derivation is technically sound, with clear theorems linking the algorithm to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Practical robustification via POD bases addresses known numerical stability issues in DMD implementations.  
- Empirical results (toy system, Hénon map, real data) demonstrate reasonable recovery of Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.  
- Strengths: clear mathematical formulation, solid theoretical grounding, numerical practicality, and multiple empirical demonstrations.  
- Novelty is incremental—primarily kernelizing an existing technique rather than introducing fundamentally new Koopman theory.  
- The paper’s own comparisons suggest the kernel approach is analogous to localized Taylor expansions and not dramatically more expressive in practice.  
- Applications are weakly motivated and do not convincingly outperform simpler alternatives like PCA or stronger baselines such as RNNs.  
- Change-point detection results are comparable to existing kernel-based methods without clear advantage.  
- Lacks sufficient comparison to state-of-the-art methods in relevant application domains, limiting assessment of relative contribution.  
- Clarity and organization are good, but connections to broader machine learning and recent deep-learning approaches for dynamical systems are underdeveloped.  
- Overall significance is modest: technically solid and useful to the Koopman/DMD community but incremental and of limited impact for the wider ML field; needs stronger empirical comparisons and articulation of unique advantages.
