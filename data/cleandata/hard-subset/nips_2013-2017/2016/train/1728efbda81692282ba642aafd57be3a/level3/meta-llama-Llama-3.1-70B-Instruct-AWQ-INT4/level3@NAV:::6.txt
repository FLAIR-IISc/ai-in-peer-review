**Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis. The authors develop a modal decomposition algorithm that can extract nonlinear dynamics from finite-length data sequences. The method is demonstrated on simple dynamical systems and real-world locomotion data.

**Strengths**

1. **Technical soundness**: The paper presents a solid mathematical foundation for the proposed algorithm, which is a kernelized extension of DMD.
2. **Clarity**: The writing is clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology.
3. **Pedagogical value**: The paper provides a useful introduction to fluid-dynamics concepts and Koopman spectral analysis, making it accessible to readers unfamiliar with these topics.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Originality**: While the paper presents a novel combination of techniques, the theoretical results are not entirely surprising, given the existing literature on RKHS and kernel linear operators.
2. **Practical usefulness**: The authors need to better demonstrate the practical usefulness of their method, particularly in machine learning applications where stochasticity and noise are common.
3. **Comparisons**: The comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kPCA yield unclear results, and the authors should provide more comprehensive evaluations.
4. **Limitations**: The deterministic-only setup limits the relevance of the method to real-world applications where randomness is inherent.

**Suggestions for improvement**

1. **Target a specific ML domain**: Demonstrate the utility of Koopman analysis in a specific machine learning domain, such as robotics, to showcase the method's practical value.
2. **Include standard DMD performance**: Provide a comparison with standard DMD performance on the toy example and the Henon map to highlight the differences.
3. **Clarify modal decomposition**: Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations and discuss the implications of this assumption.
4. **Address stochasticity**: Discuss how the method can be extended to handle stochastic systems and provide empirical results to demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Arguments for acceptance**

1. The paper presents a novel extension of DMD using RKHS, which can be useful for analyzing nonlinear dynamics.
2. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method and its underlying mathematics.
3. The paper has pedagogical value, introducing readers to Koopman spectral analysis and fluid-dynamics concepts.

**Arguments against acceptance**

1. The paper's originality is limited, as the theoretical results are not entirely surprising.
2. The method's practical usefulness is not well demonstrated, particularly in machine learning applications.
3. The comparisons to other methods are unclear, and the authors should provide more comprehensive evaluations.

Overall, the paper presents a solid technical contribution, but the authors need to better demonstrate the practical value and limitations of their method to make a stronger case for acceptance.
