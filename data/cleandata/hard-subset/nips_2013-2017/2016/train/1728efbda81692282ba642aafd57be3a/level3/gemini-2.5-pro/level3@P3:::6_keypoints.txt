- Summary: Proposes Kernel DMD (kDMD) to perform Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, with two algorithm variants (direct Gram–Schmidt and kPCA-projected) and demonstrations on prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Strength: Exceptionally clear and pedagogical exposition connecting Koopman/operator theory, DMD, Krylov methods, and kernel techniques.

- Strength: Novel and natural combination that extends DMD via kernels to capture richer nonlinear dynamics in a principled way.

- Weakness: Limited experimental comparison — no direct baseline vs. standard linear DMD on toy/Hénon examples.

- Weakness: Comparisons to related methods (e.g., kPCA) are not quantitative; lacks concrete metrics showing kDMD advantages.

- Weakness: Unclear practical ML significance — Koopman-mode features are not benchmarked against state-of-the-art ML time-series methods (e.g., GPs, LSTMs) or tied to a clear application domain.

- Weakness: Framework assumes deterministic dynamics (xt+1 = f(xt)); treatment of observation/process noise or stochastic systems is missing or only briefly mentioned.

- Weakness: Implementation ambiguity for modal decomposition — unclear whether explicit feature-space data matrices are required (potentially high memory/computational cost) versus Gram-matrix-only computation.

- Weakness: Modest theoretical novelty — mainly an application/collection of existing linear-operator and kernel-method theory rather than a fundamental theoretical advance.

- Overall recommendation: Interesting and well-written fusion but needs stronger baselines, ML benchmarks, clarification of computational requirements, and a compelling application (e.g., robotics) to demonstrate practical impact.
