- Paper proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator in RKHS to kernelize DMD for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm representing function composition as a linear operator, extending DMD with kernel methods.
- Demonstrates applications for feature extraction, embedding, and recognition of dynamics with experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Strength: Significant technical novelty by enabling nonlinear basis functions through kernelized Koopman/DMD.
- Strength: Strong mathematical rigor with clear derivations and links to kernel PCA and subspace analysis.
- Strength: Shows practical versatility across prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition tasks.
- Strength: Empirical validation on toy nonlinear systems, Henon map, and motion capture data demonstrating effectiveness.
- Weakness: Applications shown are not uniquely compelling versus existing methods (e.g., RNNs, other spectral techniques) and lack justification.
- Weakness: Scalability concerns due to kernel methods and eigendecomposition are not addressed for large datasets.
- Weakness: Presentation could be clearer and better organized for readers unfamiliar with spectral methods or RKHS.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art competing methods limits assessment of practical advantage.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical extension of DMD to RKHS, technically sound and aligned with conference goals.
- Arguments for acceptance: Versatile method with promising results across multiple applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical impact undermined by weak application justification, scalability issues, and limited comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—improve application justification, address scalability, and add comprehensive comparisons.
