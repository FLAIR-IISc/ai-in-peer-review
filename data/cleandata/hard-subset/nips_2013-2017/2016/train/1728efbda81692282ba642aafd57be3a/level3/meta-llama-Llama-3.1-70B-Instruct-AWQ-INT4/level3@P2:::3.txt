This paper proposes a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors extend the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm to work with RKHSs, allowing for a more flexible and powerful analysis of nonlinear systems. The paper is well-written and organized, presenting theoretical concepts clearly and providing numerical experiments to demonstrate the approach.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of a modal decomposition algorithm for the Koopman operator in RKHSs. The authors show that this algorithm can be used to extract features from nonlinear dynamical systems, which can then be used for tasks such as prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition. The paper also includes a robustifying method, which improves the stability and accuracy of the algorithm.

The paper is technically solid, and the numerical results illustrate the usefulness of the approach despite limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical concepts, making the paper accessible to readers with a background in machine learning and dynamical systems.

However, there are some concerns and questions that need to be addressed. In Section 3.1, Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear. Specifically, Equation (7) appears similar to Equation (2), and the purpose and distinction between the two equations are unclear. Additionally, the mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH and the map φ needs to be explained. The proof of Theorem 1 also requires clarification, particularly the phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides," which is unclear since both sides seem scalar.

Furthermore, the assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicit, and additional assumptions appear in the proof, whose validity is unclear. The authors should provide a clear explanation of the assumptions and their implications on the results.

Finally, there are some minor typos in the paper, such as "the the" on Line 55 and the inconsistent notation for kappa and lambda on Line 153.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and powerful approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The algorithm is well-motivated and technically sound.
* The numerical experiments demonstrate the usefulness of the approach.
* The paper is well-written and organized, making it accessible to readers with a background in machine learning and dynamical systems.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper requires clarification on some technical points, such as Equation (7) and the proof of Theorem 1.
* The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicit, and their implications on the results need to be explained.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, but with the condition that the authors address the technical concerns and questions raised above. The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and dynamical systems, and with some revisions, it can be even stronger.
