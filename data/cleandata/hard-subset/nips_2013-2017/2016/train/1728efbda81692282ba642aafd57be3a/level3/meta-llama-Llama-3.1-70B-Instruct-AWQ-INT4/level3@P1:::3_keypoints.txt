- Proposes kernel DMD: a novel extension of DMD using RKHS and the kernel trick for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Well-written and well-organized with clear exposition of the Koopman operator and RKHS extension.  
- Technically solid with a detailed derivation linking kernel DMD to the Koopman operator.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness in estimating Koopman eigenvalues and modes and include a robustifying method to improve accuracy.  
- Potentially significant and widely applicable to nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms; more benchmarking is needed.  
- Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear and require clarification.  
- The purpose and distinction between Equation (7) and Equation (2) are not explicitly stated.  
- Possible mathematical inconsistency: KH is defined as an operator H→H while φ maps M→H and φ may not lie in H.  
- The phrase “applying the operator KH to both sides” in the proof is ambiguous because both sides appear scalar.  
- Assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong, not explicitly stated, and additional assumptions appear only in the proof.  
- Connection to other spectral analysis methods (e.g., POD) is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Recommend clarifying Equations (2) and (7) and resolving the Koopman operator definition.  
- Recommend providing a clear, complete proof of Theorem 1 and explicitly stating/justifying all assumptions.  
- Recommend adding more comparisons to state-of-the-art algorithms to strengthen empirical validation.  
- Overall: promising and technically solid work that needs revisions on mathematical clarity and comparative evaluation before acceptance.
