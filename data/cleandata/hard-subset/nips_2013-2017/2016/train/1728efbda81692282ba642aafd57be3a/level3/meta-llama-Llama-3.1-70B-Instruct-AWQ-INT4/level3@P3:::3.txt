This paper presents a spectral analysis method with the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis using finite-length data sequences, which is an extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) using the kernel trick.

The paper is well-written and organized, presenting theoretical concepts clearly. The authors provide a robustifying method and demonstrate the approach with numerical experiments. The framework is technically solid, and the numerical results illustrate the usefulness of the method, despite limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and the proposal of a modal decomposition algorithm. The authors also provide a robustified version of the algorithm by projecting data onto the POD directions. The paper demonstrates the application of the proposed method to various tasks, including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and recognition of dynamic patterns.

However, there are some concerns and questions regarding the paper. In Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear. Equation (7) appears similar to Equation (2), and the purpose and distinction between them are unclear. There is a mathematical inconsistency in that KH is an operator H→H, but φ is a map M→H, so φ may not belong to H; this needs explanation. The proof of Theorem 1 has some unclear steps, particularly lines 144–157, where the phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" is unclear since both sides seem scalar. The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicit, and additional assumptions appear in the proof, whose validity is unclear.

Additionally, there are some typos in the paper, such as "the the" in Line 55 and the use of \kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i in Line 153.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the application of the kernel trick to DMD. However, the relation between the proposed method and other existing methods, such as the eigensystem realization method, is not fully explored.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to provide a new tool for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed method can be used for various tasks, including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and recognition of dynamic patterns. However, the paper could benefit from more extensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

In conclusion, the paper presents a technically solid and original contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems. However, it requires some clarification and correction of the mathematical derivations and proofs. With further development and comparison to existing methods, the proposed method has the potential to become a valuable tool for analyzing complex systems.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the application of the kernel trick to DMD.
* The proposed method is technically solid and demonstrates promising results in various tasks.
* The paper is well-written and organized, presenting theoretical concepts clearly.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The mathematical derivations and proofs require clarification and correction.
