- Proposes a spectral method that approximates the Koopman operator in RKHS by treating composition g → g∘f as a linear operator and adapting DMD to feature space.  
- Algorithm builds a Krylov matrix from trajectories, orthonormalizes (Gram–Schmidt), projects and eigendecomposes to obtain approximate Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions/modes.  
- Introduces a numerically robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA (POD) directions before spectral estimation to address ill-conditioning.  
- Empirical demonstrations: eigenvalue recovery on toy and Hénon-map examples.  
- Empirical demonstrations: short-horizon prediction via a pre-image step.  
- Empirical demonstrations: embedding/recognition using subspace distances (kernel principal angles).  
- Empirical demonstrations: change-point detection using a CUSUM-style statistic with kernelized predictions.  
- Strength: clear conceptual framing linking compositions, Koopman analysis, and kernel methods.  
- Strength: methodologically sound RKHS extension of DMD with clear theorems on Ritz values/vectors.  
- Strength: practical robustness from kernel PCA/POD truncation for noisy or rank-deficient data.  
- Strength: breadth of applications shown and code provided for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: originality is incremental relative to EDMD and existing kernelized Koopman approaches; positioning versus prior work is not sharply distinguished.  
- Weakness: lacks finite-sample error guarantees, sample complexity, and robustness analyses; relies on strong assumptions.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited, small-scale, and lacks comparisons to strong baselines (EDMD, GPDM, PSRs, RNNs, BOCPD, MMD tests).  
- Weakness: sparse practical guidance on kernel choice, hyperparameters, mode selection, and computational/scale issues; no Nyström/random features discussed.  
- Quality: algorithmic development is technically sound but claims are qualitative with insufficient rigorous comparisons or ablations.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; suggests unified notation table, pseudocode, complexity/memory costs, and clearer pre-image discussion.  
- Originality/significance: moderate novelty—useful to DMD practitioners but limited impact without stronger empirical evidence on high-dimensional tasks.  
- Suggestions: add head-to-head comparisons with EDMD and other baselines, quantitative metrics, sensitivity analyses, scaling approximations, and perturbation/error analyses.  
- Acceptance arguments: clean RKHS unification and practical robustification with tutorial value for ML audiences.  
- Rejection arguments: incremental novelty, weak experiments and baselines, no error/stability analysis or scalability guidance.
