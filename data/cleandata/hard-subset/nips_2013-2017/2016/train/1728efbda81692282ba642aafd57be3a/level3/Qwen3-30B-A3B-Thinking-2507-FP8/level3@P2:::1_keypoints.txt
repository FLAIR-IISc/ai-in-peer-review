- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, constructing orthogonal Krylov bases and performing Koopman eigendecomposition.  
- Theoretical development is sound and supported by theorems linking the kernel approach to Koopman analysis.  
- Algorithm is mathematically rigorous and the mathematical presentation is well-structured.  
- Fails to clearly differentiate the method from Williams et al. (2015) extended DMD; needs an explicit methodological and theoretical comparison.  
- Experimental validation is insufficient and lacks comparisons to standard time-series forecasting and kernel-based baselines.  
- Change-point detection experiments are not benchmarked against state-of-the-art kernel-based change detection methods (e.g., [7]).  
- No discussion or experiments on stochastic/noisy settings, limiting claims about real-world robustness.  
- Practical implementation details need clarification, especially using pairs of consecutive observables vs full sequences, causal assumptions, and applicability to multidimensional time series.  
- Originality is limited: mainly a technical adaptation of DMD to RKHS rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- The paper omits comparisons and citations to relevant kernel-based dynamical systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Demonstrated applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are promising but do not show clear advantages over existing methods; prediction uses a standard pre-image approach without performance gains.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, clear link between kernel methods and Koopman analysis, and demonstrated application examples.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate differentiation from prior work, missing baselines, lack of noise/stochastic analysis, limited experimental validation, and incomplete literature review.  
- Recommended fixes: provide detailed comparison with Williams et al. and other extended DMD, add benchmark experiments for prediction/change-point/dynamics recognition, discuss stochastic/noise performance, and expand the literature review.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but potentially acceptable after major revisions addressing the above concerns.
