This paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) using feature maps, which is referred to as KDMD. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of KDMD on toy problems and highlight its potential as a powerful generalization of DMD.

The paper is well-structured and provides a thorough theoretical foundation for KDMD. However, the writing style is overly mathematical and verbose, making it challenging to follow for non-experts. The reviewer suggests that the authors should strive for clearer and more concise explanations to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.

One of the major concerns is that the paper is too cryptic for a NeurIPS audience. The reviewer strongly encourages the authors to rewrite the paper in a more pedagogical style, which would make it highly citable. Additionally, the presentation is needlessly verbose, with many statements that could be more concise.

The reviewer also suggests that Section 4 is largely redundant and could be removed or merged with the introduction. Section 5 should be illustrated with examples or summarized in the conclusion, and Section 6 is overly condensed and should be developed further.

Minor remarks include a possible typo in the description of the nonlinear toy case, which appears to be linear. The reviewer also notes that the first two test problems are polynomial and suggests that more detail is needed on the choice of kernel/feature map, as discussed in Section 5.

The reviewer cites Brunton et al. (2015) as a relevant reference, which may be useful for the authors to consider in their future work.

**Quality:** 7/10
The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough theoretical foundation for KDMD. However, the writing style and presentation could be improved to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.
