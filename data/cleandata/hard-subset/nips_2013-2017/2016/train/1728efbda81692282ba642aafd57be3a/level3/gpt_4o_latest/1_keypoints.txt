- Introduces a kernel-based Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, extending DMD to richer nonlinear representations.  
- Proposes a mathematically rigorous modal decomposition algorithm using Krylov subspaces and RKHS eigendecomposition, with POD-based robustification.  
- Demonstrates utility on synthetic (e.g., Hénon map) and real-world (locomotion) datasets for prediction, clustering, and change detection.  
- Highlights versatile applications including sequential change-point detection and dynamics recognition.  

- Lacks clear comparison and differentiation from prior work, notably Williams et al. (2015).  
- Omits important related-work references on kernel methods for nonlinear dynamical systems (Ralaivola, d'Alché Buc, Tuia, Rojo-Alvarez, Mouattamid, Schaback, Franz, Schölkopf).  
- Underexplores stochastic dynamics: no experiments or detailed discussion of performance in stochastic settings.  
- Experimental evaluation is modest and does not compare against state-of-the-art time-series clustering or online change-detection methods.  
- Assumptions about pointwise relations and causality in multidimensional time series are insufficiently clarified.  
- Minor issues: two typos (indices on τ-1 vs. τ) and a misplaced dot in kernel notation k(xi, xj).  

- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound RKHS extension of Koopman analysis with promising algorithmic and experimental results.  
- Against acceptance: weak contextualization vs. prior work, limited experiments, and lack of stochastic analysis.  

- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending revisions to clarify differences from prior work, add missing references, include stochastic experiments and stronger baselines, and clarify assumptions on pointwise relations and causality.
