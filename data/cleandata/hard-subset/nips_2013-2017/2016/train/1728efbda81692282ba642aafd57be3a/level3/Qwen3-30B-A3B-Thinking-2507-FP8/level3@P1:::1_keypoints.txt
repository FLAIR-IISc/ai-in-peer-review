- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis using kernel-based nonlinear bases.  
- Develops a theoretical RKHS Koopman framework and an algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and performs eigendecomposition.  
- Introduces a POD-based robustified variant to mitigate ill-conditioning in the basic algorithm.  
- Demonstrates applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: Theoretical extension offers a more expressive representation of nonlinear dynamics compared to traditional DMD.  
- Strength: POD robustification is well-motivated and addresses a practical numerical limitation.  
- Empirical results show applicability, notably in locomotion recognition and change-point detection, but are limited in scope.  
- Major weakness: Unclear relationship to Williams et al. (2015) Extended DMD; paper does not clearly distinguish theoretical/practical differences.  
- Major weakness: Omits several relevant prior works on kernel-based dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc 2005; Tuia et al. 2011; Rojo-Álvarez et al. 2012; Mouattamid & Schaback 2013; Franz & Schölkopf 2004).  
- Major weakness: Only addresses deterministic systems and lacks discussion of extensions or limitations for stochastic dynamics.  
- Major weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art time-series prediction, online kernel change-point detection, and time-series clustering methods (e.g., kernel k-means, subspace kernels).  
- Major weakness: Uses pairs of consecutive observables rather than sequences without clarifying implications or causal assumptions for multidimensional time series.  
- Minor issues: Typo on page 7 (indexing τ-1 vs. τ) and missing comma in kernel notation k(xi.xj).  
- NIPS criteria summary: sound theoretical quality but weak experimental comparisons; generally clear writing but needs methodological clarifications; originality is questionable given related work; significance limited by evaluation gaps.  
- Recommendation: Explicitly compare to Williams et al. (2015), include omitted prior work, discuss stochastic extensions, and perform more comprehensive comparative experiments to strengthen the paper.
