- Novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS).  
- Proposes a modal decomposition algorithm that uses kernels to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer nonlinear bases.  
- Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrating feature extraction and applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamic pattern recognition).  
- Clear motivation, methodology, and results; paper is exceptionally well-written.  
- Builds on the Koopman–DMD connection and generalizes DMD by embedding observables in RKHS to capture nonlinear dynamics.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid and the algorithm is algorithmically sound.  
- Practical implementation addresses ill-conditioning via projection onto POD directions.  
- Empirical evaluation includes a toy system and the Hénon map, showing method performance.  
- Explores downstream applications, demonstrating practical utility of extracted features.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive (POD, DMD variants, kernel methods) and code is made available for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: no direct comparison to standard (non-RKHS) Koopman spectral analysis or DMD on the same datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and sensitivity analysis of kernel choice, parameters, and trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: high-quality contribution with minor revisions suggested (add direct comparisons and kernel analysis).  
- Overall score recommendation: accept with minor revisions; suggested score 8/10.
