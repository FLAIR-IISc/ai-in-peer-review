- Proposes a novel modal decomposition applying the Koopman operator in an RKHS to extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).  
- Claims the algorithm can recover nonlinear dynamics from finite-length data sequences and shows empirical examples.  
- Clear and concise exposition of the Koopman operator and thorough review of DMD and related spectral techniques.  
- Strength: technically sound method with well-explained underlying concepts.  
- Weakness: insufficiently distinguished from Williams et al. (2015); relationship should be clarified.  
- Missing citations to several relevant kernel-based dynamical-systems works (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Needs deeper discussion of practical implications of using pairs of consecutive observables vs. longer sequences, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations.  
- Should articulate the causal assumptions underlying the approach more clearly.  
- Experiments are limited; recommend more ambitious evaluations and comparisons (time-series clustering, kernel k-means, subspace kernels, online kernel change-point detection).  
- Should include evaluations for stochastic settings and clarify performance limitations.  
- Minor issues: two typos (indexing tau-1 vs. tau) and kernel notation typo (dot vs comma in k(xi,xj) on p.7).  
- Overall ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel RKHS-based approach, clear Koopman explanation, empirical demonstrations.  
- Rejection/weakness arguments: unclear novelty relative to prior work, missing prior citations, limited experimental validation.
