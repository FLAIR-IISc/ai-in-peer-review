- Paper extends Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS by formulating DMD in feature space and computing modal decomposition via Gram–Schmidt on the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of a projected operator.  
- Introduces a numerically robust variant using kernel-PCA truncation (kDMD) to produce finite-dimensional Koopman estimates for eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and modes.  
- Builds on DMD/Koopman and EDMD literature; RKHS acts as an implicit rich dictionary and kernel-PCA truncation parallels EDMD least-squares estimation.  
- Demonstrates applications in short-term prediction (via pre-image), dynamics recognition (kernel principal angles), and change-point detection on simulated and real data.  
- Strength: clean RKHS formulation with elegant theorems linking Ritz values/vectors in feature space to Koopman spectra.  
- Strength: practical, numerically robust pipeline that reduces to Gram matrix operations and is implemented in code.  
- Strength: broad applicability shown across prediction, recognition, and detection tasks with clear exposition.  
- Weakness: lacks quantitative comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman methods (standard/optimized/exact DMD, EDMD) on the same problems.  
- Weakness: no systematic study of kernel choice and truncation effects (e.g., RBF bandwidth, polynomial degree) on spectra, stability, or downstream accuracy.  
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions require eigenfunctions to lie in the RKHS span; no approximation error bounds or convergence guarantees under noise.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—Gram matrices and eigendecompositions scale poorly and large-sample remedies (Nyström, random features, incremental KPCA) are not discussed.  
- Weakness: pre-image recovery is heuristic and sensitivity to neighbors, noise, and kernel parameters is not analyzed.  
- Empirical evaluation is largely qualitative; reviewer requests eigenvalue/mode errors and multi-step prediction errors for toy/Hénon systems.  
- Requests: add baselines (standard/exact/optimized DMD, EDMD) with metrics for eigenvalue error, reconstruction error, and prediction RMSE; report MoCap classification accuracy vs baselines.  
- Requests: study kernel sensitivity (RBF bandwidth selection, polynomial degree, regularization), plot eigenvalue stability/residuals/prediction error vs kernel and truncation rank, and consider multi-kernel strategies.  
- Requests: provide computational complexity and memory analysis, and evaluate Nyström/random features/incremental KPCA for scalability.  
- Requests: robustness tests under observation noise and model misspecification, showing how POD truncation and regularization mitigate spurious eigenvalues.  
- Requests/Theory: discuss conditions for small residual ψ, effects of continuous spectra (chaos), and bounds relating approximation error to kernel PCA truncation and sample size.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant RKHS generalization of DMD, clear connection to Koopman theory and kernel PCA, practical algorithm with code.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited quantitative evaluation, no systematic kernel selection study, scalability and robustness gaps, and theoretical reliance on strong inclusion assumptions without error analysis.  
- Overall: promising, well-written contribution that needs stronger empirical comparisons, kernel analyses, scalability treatment, and theoretical/robustness clarifications to be publication-ready.
