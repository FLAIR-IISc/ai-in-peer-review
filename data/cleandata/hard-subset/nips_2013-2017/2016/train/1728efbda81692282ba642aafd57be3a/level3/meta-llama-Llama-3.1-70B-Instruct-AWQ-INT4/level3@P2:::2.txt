This paper proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The method is based on the idea of decomposing the Koopman operator, which is an infinite-dimensional linear operator that represents nonlinear and finite-dimensional dynamics without linearization. The authors apply this decomposition to model forward dynamics and use kernel PCA and subspace-analysis ideas to perform eigen-decomposition on collected trajectories.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the Koopman operator and its relevance to nonlinear dynamical systems. The proposed method is also well-explained, and the authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation.

The paper's main contribution is the application of kernel methods to the Koopman operator decomposition. The authors show that by using kernel PCA and subspace-analysis ideas, they can perform eigen-decomposition on the Koopman operator and extract meaningful information about the nonlinear dynamical system. The paper also provides several empirical examples that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, the main novelty of the paper appears to be limited to kernelizing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD), which is a well-established method in the dynamical-systems literature. While the authors do provide some new insights and applications of the method, the core algorithm is not fundamentally new.

The paper's applications are not very compelling in demonstrating the method's distinct advantages or uniqueness. The authors apply the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems, but the results are not particularly impressive, and competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and change-detection methods exist for system recognition.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its implementation. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods and a more thorough evaluation of the method's performance.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its implementation. However, some of the technical details may be difficult for non-experts to follow, and the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the kernel methods used.

In terms of originality, the paper's main contribution is the application of kernel methods to the Koopman operator decomposition. However, as mentioned earlier, the core algorithm is not fundamentally new, and the paper's novelty is limited to kernelizing DMD.

In terms of significance, the paper's results are not particularly impressive, and the method's advantages and uniqueness are not clearly demonstrated. However, the paper does provide some new insights and applications of the Koopman operator decomposition, and the method may be useful for certain types of nonlinear dynamical systems.
