- Proposes kernelized DMD (kDMD) performing Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS via Krylov projections and Gram-matrix-based Ritz extraction from snapshot trajectories.

- Introduces a robust variant that projects onto kernel PCA directions (analogous to POD truncation) to mitigate ill-conditioning.

- Provides algorithms that use only kernel evaluations and proves connections between computed spectra/modes and Koopman objects (Theorems 1–2); code is released.

- Demonstrations cover a toy system and Hénon map, short-horizon prediction via a preimage heuristic, motion-capture embedding/recognition, and sequential change-point detection.

- Paper builds on DMD/EDMD and RKHS operator literature; the kernel viewpoint and Krylov/Ritz treatment are natural but presented cohesively for ML audiences.

- Strengths: clear, pedagogical exposition; kernel-only concrete algorithms; theoretical links; multiple applications sketched; reproducible code; bridges fluid-dynamics and ML communities.

- Weaknesses: moderate novelty—closely related to EDMD/kernel EDMD; theoretical results unsurprising given RKHS projections.

- Weaknesses (experiments): empirical evaluation is limited and largely qualitative with small-scale examples and no quantitative baselines or ablations.

- Weaknesses (robustness/scalability): lacks treatment of stochasticity/noise, no regularization analysis, ad hoc preimage prediction step, and no discussion of scalability or hyperparameter selection (kernel width, truncation p).

- Quality: derivations are technically sound but rely on strong assumptions; no statistical consistency or finite-sample error analysis; empirical validation lacks quantitative rigor.

- Clarity: well written and organized with thorough background; some notation inconsistencies and figures missing quantitative metrics/confidence intervals.

- Originality: reasonable synthesis of known ideas; robust kernel PCA projection is natural but the core approach resembles EDMD and needs clearer differentiation.

- Significance: potentially useful for bringing Koopman features to ML tasks, but immediate impact is uncertain without stronger empirical evidence on noisy/large-scale ML benchmarks.

- Key questions for authors: confirm kernel-only computation, provide computational/memory complexity in τ and p and out-of-sample costs; explain hyperparameter selection/sensitivity; clarify theoretical/computational differences vs EDMD; show robustness to noise and effects of PCA truncation.

- Suggested improvements: add quantitative baselines (DMD/EDMD/POD/kPCA/Hankel-DMD/AR models), report eigenvalue errors, prediction RMSE, classification metrics, detection ROC/AUC and runtime/memory scaling; include noise/regularization experiments and kernel/truncation ablations; demonstrate a strong ML application.

- Arguments for acceptance: clear, practical kernel DMD framework with pedagogical value, community bridging, and available code.

- Arguments against acceptance: incremental relative to EDMD/kernel operator literature, incomplete positioning, and weak experimental validation limiting demonstrated practical utility.
