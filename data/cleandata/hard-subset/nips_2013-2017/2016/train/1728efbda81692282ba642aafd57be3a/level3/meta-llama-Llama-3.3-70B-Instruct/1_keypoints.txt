- Introduces a novel Koopman-operator-based method in an RKHS that yields efficiency gains for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Experimentally validated on synthetic and real data, showing effectiveness in system identification, long-term memorization, and speech prediction.  
- Contribution is well-motivated and has potential high impact on nonlinear dynamical system analysis.  
- Lacks clear discussion of differences from prior work (e.g., Williams et al.) — relation and novelty over existing methods need clarification.  
- Missing citations to relevant kernel-based nonlinear dynamical systems literature (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alché-Buc, Tuia et al., and others).  
- Paper focuses on deterministic settings; performance and behaviour in stochastic/realistic noisy settings are not discussed or evaluated.  
- The assumption of nonlinear pointwise relations in multidimensional time series and the causal assumptions require further elaboration and justification.  
- Experimental suite, while convincing, is not ambitious enough—needs broader and more challenging benchmarks.  
- Should include comparisons to state-of-the-art methods such as online kernel change detection and other relevant baselines.  
- Writing is clear, but contains minor typos and index/punctuation errors that should be corrected.  
- Strengths: novel approach, thorough validation, and strong potential impact.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient comparisons to existing work, limited experimental scope, and missing relevant references.
