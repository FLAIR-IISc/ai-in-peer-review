- Proposes Kernel DMD: extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS to approximate Koopman operator eigenvalues/eigenfunctions from data.  
- Develops a direct Gram–Schmidt feature-space algorithm and a more robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA/POD modes.  
- Frames extracted Koopman modes/eigenvalues as features for ML tasks (state prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection).  
- Elegant, principled fusion of Koopman theory with kernel methods, treating function composition as a linear operator in RKHS.  
- Bridges dynamical-systems and machine-learning communities by expressing DMD in kernel/Gram-matrix language.  
- Theoretical soundness: establishes connection between Ritz values/vectors and true Koopman spectrum (Theorems 1–2).  
- Robust POD/kernel PCA projection addresses known numerical instabilities of standard DMD/Arnoldi methods.  
- Main novelty is largely the kernelization of existing DMD and kernel PCA ideas, so originality is limited.  
- Related work (e.g., extended DMD) overlaps conceptually; contribution vs. prior methods not sharply delineated.  
- Experimental evaluation demonstrates feasibility but lacks strong baselines and broad comparisons to state-of-the-art time-series models (GPDMs, LSTMs, DTW, etc.).  
- Change-point detection derivation relies on a simplifying assumption; comparisons are limited and not comprehensive.  
- Paper may be dense for readers unfamiliar with Koopman/DMD; practical motivation for algorithmic choices (Gram–Schmidt → POD) could be emphasized more.  
- For acceptance: introduces elegant, principled framework and novel feature extraction linking two communities.  
- Against acceptance: incremental novelty and weak experimental validation limit demonstrated practical impact.
