- Paper studies Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS and proposes a kernel DMD algorithm with Krylov-based orthonormal bases and a robust kernel PCA/POD truncation variant.  
- Work is a kernelized extension of DMD, closely related to EDMD/kernel EDMD (Williams et al. 2015) and Arnoldi/Ritz methods.  
- Algorithmic development is technically solid and numerical experiments show usefulness, but experiments are limited versus state-of-the-art EDMD/kernel EDMD baselines.  
- Major mathematical/clarity problems in Section 3.1: Eq. (7) is notationally indistinguishable from Eq. (2); the distinction and purpose of ϕ must be clarified.  
- The operator KH is mischaracterized: it is described as H→H though it actually acts on H-valued observables (e.g., a space like L2(M;H)); the domain/codomain and invariance/boundedness conditions must be defined.  
- Proof step around lines 144–157 is incorrect: operators cannot act on scalars—rewrite by applying composition operator before taking inner products.  
- Theorem 1 hides strong assumptions (distinct Ritz values, R invertible, Krylov span invariance, ψ=0 cases); these must be stated explicitly and justified.  
- Notational confusion between ϕ (feature map), φ (eigenfunction), and κ (representers); unify notation and specify domains/codomains for all operators/maps.  
- Minor typos and indexing errors (e.g., “the the” on line 55; κj/λj vs κi/λi on line 153) need correction.  
- Novelty is limited due to substantial overlap with EDMD/kernel EDMD and existing DMD variants; clearly position contributions relative to Williams et al. and exact/optimized/sparsity-promoting DMD.  
- If theoretical foundations are clarified and stronger empirical comparisons added (EDMD/kernel EDMD, exact/optimized DMD), the paper could be a useful contribution.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject unless operator-theoretic assumptions/proofs are fixed and comparative experiments are strengthened.
