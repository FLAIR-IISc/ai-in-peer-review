- Introduces full-capacity unitary RNNs that optimize the recurrence matrix over all unitary matrices.  
- Provides a theoretical test for restricted capacity of unitary parameterizations and shows a recent parameterization is restricted for hidden dimension > 7.  
- Proposes an SGD method that constrains gradients to the differentiable manifold of unitary matrices for training the recurrence.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and technically solid with clear theoretical exposition.  
- Experimental evaluation (system ID, long-term memorization, speech-frame prediction, pixel-by-pixel MNIST) demonstrates full-capacity uRNNs often match or outperform LSTMs and restricted uRNNs.  
- Method addresses vanishing/exploding gradient issues by using unitary recurrence matrices to stabilize training.  
- Originality: combines known techniques (unitary matrices, manifold SGD) with a novel theoretical capacity argument; related work is adequately cited.  
- Technical concern: proof of Theorem 3.2 relies on Sard’s theorem, which may be unfamiliar to some readers.  
- Technical concern: application of the operator K_H in the proof of Theorem 3.2 is not entirely clear and needs clarification.  
- Minor issues: a few typos reported (e.g., lines 55 and 153).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the technical clarifications and typos.  
- Pro-accept points: novel/significant contribution, clear presentation, strong experimental results, potential field impact.  
- Con-accept points: reliance on Sard’s theorem, unclear operator usage in proof, and typographical errors.
