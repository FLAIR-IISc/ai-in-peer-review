- Proposes kernelized Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS using Krylov/QR/Ritz methods, plus a robustified variant projecting onto kernel-PCA directions and applications to prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

- Situates work among POD/DMD/Koopman/EDMD methods and is closest to extended DMD / kernel EDMD (Williams et al., 2015), but lacks explicit comparison.

- Technical soundness: RKHS formulation and proofs are reasonable and reduce to practical Gram-matrix computations.

- Novelty unclear: could be incremental over KDMD unless the Krylov/QR/Ritz derivation and kernel-PCA projection are shown distinct.

- Empirical support is illustrative (toy systems, Hénon map, locomotion, change detection) but not sufficiently ambitious or comparative.

- No quantitative experiments in stochastic/noisy settings despite discussion of stochastic extensions.

- Reproducibility: algorithmic detail is adequate for experts, but hyperparameter choices, runtime, and memory scaling are not reported.

- Clarity: paper is generally readable with clear Sections 3/4, but major gaps remain regarding distinction from EDMD/KDMD.

- Needs explicit clarification whether the “robustified” method is equivalent to KDMD up to centering/truncation and how the Ritz construction compares to EDMD’s least-squares operator.

- The remark about using pairs of consecutive observables requires discussion of causal/Markov assumptions, time-reversal, and when delay embeddings are preferable.

- Related work is incomplete; should discuss additional kernel-based time-series/system-identification literature (Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback, Franz & Schölkopf).

- Should compare and relate to delay-embedding Koopman methods (Hankel-DMD, subspace-DMD), GPDM, and PSR-style spectral learning.

- Significance: method could bridge Koopman theory and kernel learning, but impact limited by unclear novelty and weak empirical baselines.

- Pros: clear RKHS derivation linking Ritz values/vectors to Koopman spectra; practical kernel-PCA robustification; kernel trick yields implementable algorithms; multiple application sketches and code availability.

- Cons: unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015); small-scale qualitative experiments; no noisy/stochastic evaluation; missing related work; lack of guidance on pairs vs sequences and causal assumptions.

- Revision requests (1): explicitly compare to Williams et al. (2015) theoretically and empirically, including complexity and numerical behavior.

- Revision requests (2): expand related work to include named kernel/time-series/system-identification references and delay-embedding Koopman methods.

- Revision requests (3): include experiments with process/measurement noise and discuss robustness, bias, and regularization.

- Revision requests (4): clarify assumptions on pairs vs sequences, when delay embeddings are required, and implications for multivariate weakly coupled series.

- Revision requests (5): stronger evaluations — quantitative prediction comparisons vs EDMD/KDMD/Hankel-DMD/GPDM; clustering baselines; online change-point detection baselines and ROC/delay metrics.

- Revision requests (6): report scalability (O(τ^3) runtime, memory), kernel/truncation selection strategies, and sensitivity analyses.

- Minor issues: typos (indexing τ−1 vs τ, punctuation in kernel notation), consider anonymizing code for double-blind review.

- Overall evaluation: promising and well-presented RKHS view but incremental over KDMD with insufficient empirical validation; current recommendation weak reject with clear path to improvement.
