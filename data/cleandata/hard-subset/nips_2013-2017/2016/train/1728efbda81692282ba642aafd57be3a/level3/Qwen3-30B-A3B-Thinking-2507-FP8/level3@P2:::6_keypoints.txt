- Proposes kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) in RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Builds on the DMD–Koopman connection and uses kernel functions for nonlinear basis representations.  
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposes the Koopman projection.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Mathematical derivations are sound, well-justified, and clearly presented.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, pedagogical, and properly contextualized within prior work (kernel PCA, extended DMD).  
- Novelty is incremental; the contribution is close to existing kernel-based spectral analysis approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: missing comparisons to standard DMD on toy and Hénon examples.  
- Locomotion experiments lack comparisons to state-of-the-art motion-recognition methods.  
- Practical significance for ML is unclear; deterministic-only setup ignores noise and stochasticity common in ML tasks.  
- Key concern: unclear if modal decomposition needs explicit feature-space representations, which would hurt scalability.  
- Key concern: authors must identify concrete ML applications where the method outperforms existing approaches.  
- Recommendation: reject—technically sound and well-written but lacks empirical evidence of significance and broader ML applicability.
