- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator with kernel PCA and subspace-analysis to perform eigen-decomposition on collected trajectories.  
- Method models forward dynamics without linearization by decomposing the infinite-dimensional Koopman operator.  
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a clear explanation of the Koopman operator and the algorithm.  
- Core technical contribution is applying kernel methods (kernel PCA) to Koopman operator decomposition.  
- Empirical examples are provided and show the method can be effective.  
- Novelty is limited—primarily amounts to kernelizing dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Applications (embedding and recognition) do not strongly demonstrate distinct advantages over alternatives like RNNs or change-detection methods.  
- Paper is technically sound and implementation details are well-described.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons to existing methods and a more thorough evaluation of performance.  
- Some technical parts may be difficult for non-experts; the kernel-methods exposition could be clearer.  
- Originality and significance are limited; contributions may fit a specialized venue but are borderline for a top-tier conference.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear exposition, solid technical presentation, and empirical demonstrations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unconvincing applications, and insufficient comparative evaluation.
