- The paper extends DMD by leveraging RKHS for a more expressive, theoretically sound Koopman eigenfunction representation.
- Clear, well-written exposition with detailed derivations and algorithmic steps, offering high educational value.
- Novelty in combining Koopman operator theory with kernelized DMD, bridging fluid dynamics and machine learning.
- Demonstrated applications include prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection on synthetic and locomotion data.
- Experiments are limited to synthetic systems and a single real-world dataset, leaving broader applicability unclear.
- Comparative analysis with DMD, PCA, and kernel PCA is present but results and trade-offs are ambiguously discussed.
- Unclear whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representation, raising computational scalability concerns.
- Real-world machine learning use cases are limited, weakening the demonstrated practical significance.
- Robustness to noise and sparse data is not thoroughly evaluated despite a proposed POD-based robust variant.
- Pro: Strong theoretical foundation, clarity, novelty, and potential interdisciplinary impact.
- Con: Limited experimental validation and unclear practical impact for real-world ML tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept as a workshop paper or poster if clarity, comparative analysis, and application concerns are addressed.
