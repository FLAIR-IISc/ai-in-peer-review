- Proposes KDMD: an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS using feature maps.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and technically sound development.  
- Demonstrates KDMD on toy problems, showing its potential as a powerful generalization of DMD.  
- Writing is overly mathematical and verbose, making the paper hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Paper is cryptic for a NeurIPS audience; needs a more pedagogical presentation to increase accessibility and citation potential.  
- Many statements are needlessly verbose and could be made more concise.  
- Section 4 is largely redundant and should be removed or merged with the introduction.  
- Section 5 needs illustrative examples or should be summarized in the conclusion.  
- Section 6 is overly condensed and should be developed further.  
- Minor issue: possible typo in the nonlinear toy case description (appears linear).  
- Minor issue: first two test problems are polynomial; more detail needed on kernel/feature map choices as discussed in Section 5.  
- Suggests citing Brunton et al. (2015) as a relevant reference.  
- Quality score: 7/10 — technically sound but needs presentation improvements.  
- Clarity score: 6/10 — challenging to follow due to style.  
- Originality score: 8/10 — novel extension of DMD to RKHS.  
- Significance score: 8/10 — potential high impact in dynamical systems and ML.

- Against acceptance: overly mathematical/verbose, redundant sections, and underdeveloped Section 6.
