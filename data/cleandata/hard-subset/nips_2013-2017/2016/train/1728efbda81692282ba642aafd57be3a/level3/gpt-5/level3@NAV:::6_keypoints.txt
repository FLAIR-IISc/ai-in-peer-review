- Proposes kernelized DMD in RKHS using a Krylov matrix, orthonormal basis, and projection of the Koopman operator, with a kernel-PCA truncation for robustness.  
- Evaluations on a damped 1D system, the Hénon map, and CMU motion-capture for recognition/change detection.  
- Derivations are sound and consistent with RKHS linear-operator and spectral Galerkin/Arnoldi literature.  
- Theoretical contributions are incremental and largely expected given prior work on operators in RKHS.  
- Experiments are limited and lack quantitative comparisons against strong baselines (standard/exact/optimized/sparse DMD, EDMD/kernel EDMD, PCA/kPCA).  
- Pre-image step is heuristic and its fidelity is not evaluated; unclear when explicit feature-space vectors are required versus kernel evaluations.  
- Method is deterministic; robustness to noise and stochastic dynamics is discussed but not validated experimentally.  
- Paper is clearly written and pedagogically valuable; algorithmic steps are laid out cleanly.  
- Needs clarification on modal reconstruction requirements and reporting of computational complexity and memory scaling with trajectory length and kernel choice.  
- Originality is an incremental but useful synthesis; must more explicitly discuss relationships/differences to EDMD/kernel EDMD and kernel spectral methods.  
- Significance is potential relevance to ML/robotics/system ID, but current evidence and targeted benchmarks are insufficient.  
- Suggestions: add quantitative baselines, evaluate noisy/stochastic settings and sample complexity, provide a concrete robotics use case, and analyze kernel selection, hyperparameter sensitivity, and pre-image accuracy.  
- Pros: clear, pedagogical presentation; principled RKHS formulation; practical robustification via kernel-PCA; potentially general tool for dynamical feature extraction.  
- Cons: incremental theory, limited and unclear empirical validation, missing strong baselines and noise robustness tests, and insufficient analysis of pre-image and computational costs.
