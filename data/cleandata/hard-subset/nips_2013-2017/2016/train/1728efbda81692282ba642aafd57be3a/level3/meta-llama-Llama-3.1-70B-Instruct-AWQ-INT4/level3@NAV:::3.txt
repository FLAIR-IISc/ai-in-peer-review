This paper presents a spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm to perform this analysis, which is an extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) using the kernel trick. The algorithm is technically solid, and the numerical results illustrate its usefulness.

The paper is well-written, organized, and presents theoretical concepts clearly. The authors provide a robustifying method by projecting data onto the principal orthogonal directions (POD) in the feature space. The empirical examples demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues.

However, there are some concerns and questions regarding the paper. Specifically, Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear. Equation (7) appears similar to Equation (2), and the purpose and distinction between them are unclear. Additionally, there is a mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH, which is an operator H→H, but φ is a map M→H, so φ may not belong to H. This needs explanation.

Furthermore, the proof of Theorem 1 has some unclear steps, particularly lines 144-157, where the phrase "applying the operator KH to both sides" is unclear since both sides seem scalar. The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicit, and additional assumptions appear in the proof, whose validity is unclear.

There are also some typos in the paper, such as "the the" in Line 55 and "\kappaj / \lambdaj" instead of "\kappai / \lambda_i" in Line 153.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the use of RKHSs and the kernel trick to extend DMD. The related work section is comprehensive, and the authors adequately reference previous contributions.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to provide a more accurate and robust method for estimating the Koopman modes and eigenvalues, which is important for understanding nonlinear dynamical systems. The applications of the proposed method, such as state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, are also significant.

In conclusion, the paper is technically solid, and the numerical results are promising. However, some clarifications and explanations are needed to address the concerns and questions mentioned above. With some revisions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the use of RKHSs and the kernel trick to extend DMD.
* The algorithm is technically solid, and the numerical results illustrate its usefulness.
* The paper is well-written, organized, and presents theoretical concepts clearly.
* The applications of the proposed method are significant and have the potential to impact various fields.

Arguments con acceptance:

* Section 3.1, Equation (7), and the proof of Theorem 1 are unclear.
* There is a mathematical inconsistency in the definition of the Koopman operator KH.
* The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicit, and additional assumptions appear in the proof, whose validity is unclear.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the concerns and questions mentioned above.
