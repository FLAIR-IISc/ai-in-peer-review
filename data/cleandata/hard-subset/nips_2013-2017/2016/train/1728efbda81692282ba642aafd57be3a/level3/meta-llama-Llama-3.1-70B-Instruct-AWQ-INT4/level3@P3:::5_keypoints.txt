- Proposes KDMD: a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS).
- Demonstrates KDMD’s effectiveness for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, capturing complex dynamics and insights.
- Provides thorough theoretical foundations, extending the Koopman operator to RKHS and developing a modal decomposition algorithm.
- Empirical evaluations on toy problems and real-world data support the theoretical results.
- Discusses connections between KDMD and other techniques (POD, subspace identification).
- Original and significant contribution with broad applicability (prediction, sequential change-point detection, dynamics recognition).
- Paper suffers from poor clarity and verbosity; writing is overly mathematical and hard for non-experts.
- Needs a more pedagogical rewrite with concise statements, clearer explanations, and intuitive examples.
- Organizational issues: Section 4 (robustification using POD) seems redundant and could merge with Section 3; Section 6 (empirical examples) is overly condensed and should be expanded; Section 5 could be more concise.
- References are comprehensive but some (e.g., Brunton et al., 2015) should be cited more explicitly in the main text.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, comprehensive theoretical and experimental analysis, demonstrated effectiveness.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to clarity and organizational problems, lack of intuitive examples.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and accessibility.
- Rating: 7/10; Confidence: 8/10.
