- Proposes extending Koopman spectral analysis into RKHS and presents a modal decomposition algorithm using kernel-based basis functions.
- Claims kernel-based approach can approximate Koopman eigenfunctions more expressively than standard DMD (linear monomials).
- Positions work relative to prior Koopman/DMD literature (Rowley et al., 2009; Williams et al., 2015) and kernelized DMD approaches.
- Mathematical framework is clearly presented and well-structured.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and includes synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Demonstrated applications include state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition, showing practical utility.
- Strengths: novel theoretical extension to RKHS, excellent writing, and meaningful real-world applications.
- Major weakness: no comparative experiments against non-RKHS Koopman methods (e.g., standard DMD or extended DMD) to demonstrate advantage.
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion and exploration of kernel choice and parameter sensitivity; different kernels/parameters not systematically evaluated.
- Recommendation: acceptance with minor revisions to add comparative analyses and systematic kernel selection experiments.
