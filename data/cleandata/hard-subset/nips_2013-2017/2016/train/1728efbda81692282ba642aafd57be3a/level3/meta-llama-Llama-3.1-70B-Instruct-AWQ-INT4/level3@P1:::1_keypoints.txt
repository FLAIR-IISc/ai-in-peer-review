- Proposes a novel kernel-based spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHS and an algorithm based on Krylov bases and eigendecomposition.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear background and method exposition; RKHS/kernel approach is a significant contribution.  
- Experimental results show the method can effectively estimate Koopman modes and eigenvalues.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish the approach from Williams et al. (2015); needs explicit comparison and discussion of differences.  
- Omits discussion of related kernel-based dynamical-systems work (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez et al., Mouattamid & Schaback).  
- Only considers deterministic systems; lacks experiments and discussion on performance and limitations in stochastic settings.  
- Claims method can use pairs of consecutive observables but provides insufficient discussion of practical implications, causal assumptions, and issues for multidimensional time series.  
- Experiments are not ambitious: standard prediction task, no comparisons to time-series clustering (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) or state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection.  
- Minor typos: indexing tau-1 vs. tau and punctuation in k(xi,xj) (p.7).  
- Quality score: 7/10 — well-written but needs comparisons, broader experiments, and deeper discussion.  
- Clarity score: 8/10 — generally clear but sections on stochastic settings and causal assumptions need improvement.  
- Originality score: 6/10 — novel RKHS formulation but unclear distinction from prior kernel-based DMD work.  
- Significance score: 7/10 — promising and potentially widely applicable, but requires stronger evaluations to demonstrate impact.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel efficient RKHS Koopman method with demonstrated mode/eigenvalue estimation.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient distinction from prior work, weak/limited experiments, and missing discussion of stochastic/causal aspects.
