- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS and provides a corresponding algorithm.  
- Clear and pedagogical explanation of the Koopman operator and traditional DMD, accessible to non-experts.  
- Technically sound derivation of the kernelized DMD algorithm.  
- Potential to capture more complex dynamics than traditional monomial-based DMD.  
- Originality is incremental—results largely follow naturally from existing RKHS theory.  
- Empirical validation is limited to simple examples (damped 1D system, Henon map) and is rudimentary.  
- Comparisons to DMD, PCA, and kPCA lack sufficient detail, statistical rigor, and clear baselines.  
- Applications (prediction, embedding/recognition, change-point detection) are speculative rather than demonstrated.  
- Unclear whether modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations, which could limit advantages over kernel PCA.  
- Only addresses deterministic systems; lacks treatment of stochastic/noisy data common in ML applications.  
- Recommends including standard DMD baseline results on toy examples to clarify benefits of the kernelized approach.  
- Suggests demonstrating utility in a realistic ML domain (e.g., robotics) and comparing to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically correct but incremental with weak empirical evidence; recommends rejection pending stronger experiments and clearer ML relevance.
