- Paper proposes performing Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS by lifting data via a feature map, building a Krylov matrix in feature space, projecting the Koopman operator via QR, and computing Ritz values/modes.  
- Authors introduce a robust variant that projects onto kernel-PCA directions (POD-like truncation) and assembles a p×p operator from Gram matrices for numerics.  
- Work is closely related to DMD, EDMD, and KDMD; the Krylov/Arnoldi viewpoint and kernel-PCA robustification are positioned as the novel angle.  

Strengths:
- Clear motivation: leverages RKHS to capture nonlinear structure beyond polynomial observables.  
- Well written and organized with an easy-to-follow algorithmic flow and engaging applications.  
- Technically plausible: Gram-matrix computations make the method practical and robust variant mirrors established DMD practices.  
- Experiments show the method can recover meaningful eigenvalues/modes and support downstream tasks.  

Technical weaknesses:
- Notational/type confusion in Section 3/Theorem 1: KH is defined inconsistently (appears to act on H-valued maps but is described as an operator H→H), and function spaces/domains/codomains are unclear.  
- Proof of Theorem 1 treats non-square or infinite-dimensional objects as invertible matrices; steps like MτF = KHMτ need rigorous justification in Hilbert space.  
- Emergence of companion matrix/Vandermonde diagonalization assumes minimal polynomial/shift structure and distinct Ritz values; these assumptions are not stated.  
- Steps around “apply KH to both sides” are ambiguous about object types (scalars, H-elements, or functions) and need rewriting with precise types and inner products.  
- Assumptions for Theorem 1 (e.g., when ψ=0, convergence of Ritz to Koopman spectrum) are not explicit and no consistency/error bounds are provided.  
- Robust variant omits details on centering, small-eigenvalue handling, pseudoinverses vs truncation, and regularization to ensure numerical stability.  
- Computational complexity is unaddressed: forming Gram matrices is O(τ^2) and eigendecompositions O(τ^3); scalability strategies (Nyström, random features) are not discussed.  

Clarity suggestions:
- Fix key notation early (distinguish scalar RKHS, vector-valued observables, feature map ϕ, and the operator K/KH).  
- Either adopt a consistent scalar-RKHS framework or cite/define composition operators on Bochner spaces and state boundedness assumptions.  

Originality and empirical significance:
- The RKHS framing is closely related to EDMD/KDMD; the novelty beyond EDMD with kernel dictionaries and POD truncation is not fully articulated.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: lacks comparisons to exact DMD, EDMD/KDMD, and optimized DMD, and reports no quantitative metrics (reconstruction/prediction/classification errors).  
- Applications are interesting but rely on heuristic methods (e.g., pre-image reconstruction) and need more rigorous evaluation.  

Specific requested clarifications/suggestions:
- Explicitly define function spaces and clarify Equation (7) vs (2).  
- State all assumptions in Theorem 1 (distinct Ritz values, rank/boundedness/compactness conditions, invertibility of R) and make infinite-dimensional operations well-posed.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify when κj∈span(U) and effects of truncation (p<τ) on bias.  
- Provide empirical comparisons to EDMD/KDMD and other DMD variants with quantitative metrics.  
- Discuss kernel choice, hyperparameter selection, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Add regularization/pseudoinverse usage where matrix inverses are implied (R, S̄).  

Minor issues/typos:
- Several typos and index mistakes (e.g., “the the”; κj/λj vs κi/λi); inconsistent notation for conjugate/Hermitian transpose; unqualified claim about Koopman eigenvalues on the unit circle.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Timely and practical extension of DMD to RKHS with clear exposition and potentially useful applications.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Theoretical derivations have type/assumption ambiguities and informal steps; empirical evaluation is limited and novelty over EDMD/KDMD is under-argued.  

Overall recommendation:
- Borderline: clarify operator-theoretic setup and proofs, state/justify assumptions, and add quantitative comparisons to EDMD/KDMD and other DMD variants to make the contribution strong.
