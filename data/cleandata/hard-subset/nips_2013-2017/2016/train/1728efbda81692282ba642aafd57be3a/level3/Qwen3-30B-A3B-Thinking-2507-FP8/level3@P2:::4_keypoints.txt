- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Novel integration of Koopman operator theory with kernel methods, building on recent related work but combining them in a new way.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical development with proofs linking the RKHS-DMD formulation to the Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a practical, robustified implementation using POD bases to address numerical concerns.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic systems (toy nonlinear, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (locomotion recognition, change-point detection) shows promising performance.  
- Demonstrates utility in downstream tasks: short-term prediction via pre-image reconstruction, dynamics recognition using kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection.  
- Presents clear, well-organized writing and accessible exposition for readers familiar with DMD and kernel methods.  
- Includes helpful visualizations (e.g., eigenvalue estimation, dynamics recognition) that illustrate results effectively.  
- Highlights originality by enabling more flexible nonlinear eigenfunction approximations than traditional DMD using kernel representations.  
- Significance is evidenced by multiple practical applications and meaningful real-world clustering in locomotion recognition.  
- Primary weakness: lacks direct quantitative comparison with standard DMD or extended DMD on the same datasets.  
- Secondary weakness: insufficient analysis of how kernel choice and bandwidth affect results (no ablation studies).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution with strong clarity; recommends minor revisions to add comparisons and kernel-impact analysis for acceptance.
