- Proposes a novel spectral analysis method using the Koopman operator in RKHS by extending DMD to kernel functions.  
- Method enables approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on tasks: state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Experiments are well-designed and results are clearly presented.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and uses consistent notation; figures/tables are clear.  
- Novel contribution: kernelized extension of DMD with clear connections to prior work.  
- Method has broad potential applicability (physics, engineering, ML) and possible uses in control/optimization.  
- Strengths: novelty, effective feature extraction, clear exposition, and solid experiments.  
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks.  
- Needs more comparisons to alternative methods (e.g., recurrent neural networks).  
- Requires clearer analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large systems.  
- Overall recommendation: well-written, significant, technically sound — recommend acceptance.
