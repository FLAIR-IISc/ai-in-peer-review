- Introduces Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS to extend DMD and capture nonlinear dynamics via kernel-based eigenfunction representation.  
- Algorithm based on Krylov subspace calculations and eigendecomposition; demonstrated on synthetic and real data for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Positions itself well within POD, DMD, and spectral analysis literature; extension to RKHS is a natural and potentially valuable progression.  
- Strength: novel extension of Koopman analysis to RKHS with clear algorithmic presentation and relevant application examples.  
- Weakness: unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015) / extended DMD—needs explicit comparison of implementation, theory, and practice.  
- Weakness: Section 4 (POD-based robustification) feels disconnected and lacks clear motivation beyond ill-conditioning concerns.  
- Weakness: key prior work on kernel-based dynamical systems is omitted (Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf) and must be discussed.  
- Weakness: evaluation restricted to deterministic settings; robustness to noise and applicability to stochastic systems are unexplored.  
- Weakness: reliance on pairs of consecutive observables rather than sequences simplifies theory but raises causal assumptions (xt+1 as function of xt) and practical limitations for multidimensional series.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to time-series clustering and state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection baselines.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors (e.g., "tau-1" should be "τ-1", "k(xi.xj)" should be "k(xi, xj)").  
- Overall assessment: potentially valuable contribution but insufficient novelty claim, incomplete literature review, and limited evaluation—recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and address concerns.
