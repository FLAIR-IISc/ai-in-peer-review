This paper develops a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to perform Koopman spectral analysis in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The core idea is to form a Krylov matrix in feature space from a trajectory, compute an orthonormal basis (via Gram–Schmidt/QR), and then eigendecompose the projection of the Koopman operator onto this subspace; a robust variant projects onto kernel-PCA directions to address ill-conditioning. The authors illustrate the approach on a simple damped 1D system, the Hénon map, and CMU motion capture data for recognition/change detection. The work relates to Koopman/DMD literature and to kernel methods (kernel PCA, subspace angles) and is close in spirit to extended DMD and kernelized EDMD.

Quality: The derivations are largely sound and consistent with the RKHS linear-operator literature. However, the theoretical results are mostly expected given prior work on linear operators in RKHS and spectral Galerkin/Arnoldi approaches. The experiments are limited and lack clear, quantitative comparisons against strong baselines (standard DMD, exact/optimized/sparse DMD, PCA/kPCA, EDMD/kernel EDMD). The pre-image step is heuristic and its fidelity is not evaluated. The method is currently deterministic; robustness to noise/stochastic dynamics is only discussed, not validated.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and pedagogically valuable, especially for readers unfamiliar with Koopman/DMD from fluid dynamics. Algorithmic steps are laid out cleanly, but some points need clarification: does modal reconstruction or any downstream use require explicit feature-space vectors (as opposed to kernel evaluations), and when is a pre-image necessary? Also report computational complexity and memory scaling with trajectory length and kernel choice.

Originality: The contribution is an incremental but useful synthesis: a kernelized DMD via Krylov/Ritz in RKHS with a kernel-PCA truncation. Its relationship and differences to EDMD/kernel EDMD and kernel spectral methods should be discussed more explicitly.

Significance: Potentially useful for ML areas involving dynamics (e.g., robotics, model-based control, system ID), but current evidence is insufficient. A more compelling case needs targeted benchmarks.

Suggestions:
- Add quantitative baselines: standard/exact/optimized/sparse DMD on the toy system and Hénon map; EDMD/kernel EDMD; PCA/kPCA for recognition tasks.
- Evaluate in a noisy/stochastic setting (additive process/measurement noise), reporting stability and sample complexity.
- Provide a concrete use case in robotics (e.g., gait phase prediction, system identification, or Koopman-based MPC) to demonstrate utility.
- Detail kernel selection, hyperparameter sensitivity, and pre-image accuracy.

Pros:
- Clear, pedagogical presentation; principled RKHS formulation.
- Practical robustification via kernel-PCA truncation.
- Potentially general tool for dynamical feature extraction.

Cons:
- Incremental theory; limited, unclear empirical validation.
- Missing strong baselines and noise robustness.
- Pre-image and computational costs insufficiently analyzed.
