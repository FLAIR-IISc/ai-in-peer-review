- Paper presents a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.
- Authors propose a modal decomposition algorithm based on Krylov subspace computation and a POD-based robustification to address numerical instability.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains theoretical concepts accessibly.
- Technical approach appears sound and numerical experiments demonstrate the proposed method’s functionality.
- Comparison to state-of-the-art methods is limited in the experiments.
- Section 3.1 (Equation (7)) is unclear and appears very similar to Equation (2); the benefit of operating on the feature map φ(x) is not clarified.
- Theorem 1 proof is imprecise (e.g., the step “applying the operator KH to both sides” seems inconsistent if both sides are scalars).
- Theorem 1 relies on seemingly strong or partially unstated assumptions, and additional assumptions appear inside the proof without discussion.
- Inconsistency in mathematical spaces: KH is an operator H→H while φ maps M→H, raising whether φ lies in H and can be acted on by KH.
- Minor typos noted (e.g., “the the” on line 55; \kappaj / \lambdaj should be \kappai / \lambda_i on line 153).
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound extension, clear exposition, and demonstrated numerical functionality.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear/mathematically weak parts in Section 3.1 and Theorem 1, unstated assumptions, and limited comparisons — overall recommendation is borderline acceptance pending revisions.
