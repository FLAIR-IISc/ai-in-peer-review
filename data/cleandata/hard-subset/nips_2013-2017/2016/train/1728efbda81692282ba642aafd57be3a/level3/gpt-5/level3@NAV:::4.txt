This paper proposes a Koopman spectral analysis in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS), yielding a kernelized DMD procedure that computes Ritz values/vectors via Gram–Schmidt in feature space and a robust variant using kernel PCA/POD truncation. The authors prove identities connecting the projected Koopman operator to the computed eigenpairs (Theorems 1–2), and demonstrate applications to short-term prediction via pre-image construction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition. The work is positioned against DMD, exact/optimized DMD, and EDMD; the robust procedure is closely related to EDMD with kernel dictionaries (kernel EDMD), as discussed by Williams et al. (JNS’15).

Quality: The derivations are careful, and the feature-space Krylov/Arnoldi view is insightful. The robustification via kernel PCA is sensible and practically important. However, the paper lacks finite-sample or perturbation guarantees (e.g., noise robustness, truncation bias), and offers limited guidance on regularization (rank truncation, Tikhonov) and computational scaling. The pre-image step is heuristic and its effect on prediction error is not quantified. Empirically, results are illustrative but not yet comparative.

Clarity: The manuscript is very well written and organized. The algorithms are clearly described, with helpful links to standard linear algebra. Some assumptions could be made more explicit (e.g., conditions under which ψ=0 or κj∈span(U)), and a brief complexity discussion would help practitioners.

Originality: The kernelized extension aligns with prior EDMD/kernel EDMD. The paper’s distinctive elements are the explicit Krylov subspace formulation in RKHS, the POD-based stabilization derived from kernel PCA, and the pre-image-based applications. The authors should sharpen the delineation from kernel EDMD, clarifying what is genuinely new in theory, computation, or guarantees.

Significance: Koopman methods are influential in ML and scientific computing; a robust RKHS variant is valuable. With stronger benchmarks and guidance on kernel choices, this work could be impactful.

Requested revisions:
- Include systematic comparisons to non-RKHS Koopman analyses (exact DMD, EDMD with polynomial dictionaries), measuring eigenvalue accuracy, modal reconstruction error, and short-horizon prediction on the toy and Henon systems.
- Add an exploration of kernel choice (RBF vs polynomial; bandwidth/degree selection), and truncation levels, showing sensitivity curves and practical selection heuristics.
- Discuss numerical complexity, conditioning, and regularization under noise; small ablations on sample size and SNR would strengthen the case.
- Clarify novelty relative to kernel EDMD and cite closely related NeurIPS/NIPS spectral-learning work.

Pros:
- Clear, well-motivated method with useful theory.
- Practical robustification and diverse applications.
- In-scope and of broad interest.

Cons:
- Insufficient comparative experiments.
- Limited analysis of kernel choice and regularization.
- Novelty relative to kernel EDMD needs sharpening.
