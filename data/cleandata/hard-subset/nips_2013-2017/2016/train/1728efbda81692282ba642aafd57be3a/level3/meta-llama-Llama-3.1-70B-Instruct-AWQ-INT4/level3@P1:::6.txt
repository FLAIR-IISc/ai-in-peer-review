**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for nonlinear dynamical systems. The Koopman operator is an infinite-dimensional linear operator that represents nonlinear and finite-dimensional dynamics without linearization. The authors derive a data-driven algorithm to perform this analysis, which is an extension of dynamic mode decomposition (DMD). The algorithm uses a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The method is demonstrated on two simple dynamical systems (damped 1D and chaotic map) and real locomotion data.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper is clearly written and pedagogically useful for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.
* The method is an interesting kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition, related to prior kernel methods (kernel PCA/ICA, kernel tests, etc.).
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method.

Weaknesses:

* The demonstrations use simple dynamical systems, and the comparisons to DMD/PCA/kPCA yield unclear results.
* The practical usefulness of the method for machine learning (ML) applications is questioned, and the authors need to identify concrete use cases and compare to state-of-the-art.
* The theoretical results are unsurprising given existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature.
* The modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations, which may be a limitation compared to kernel PCA.
* The deterministic-only setup (no stochastic/noise components) limits relevance to ML applications where randomness is common.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a new method for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using RKHS, which is an original contribution to the field. However, the significance of the method is not entirely clear, and the authors need to demonstrate its practical usefulness for ML applications. The method is related to prior kernel methods, but its novelty and impact on the field of ML need to be further established.

**Quality and Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed method. However, the demonstrations and comparisons to other methods could be improved to provide clearer results. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical background.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a new method for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using RKHS, which is an original contribution to the field.
* The method is related to prior kernel methods, and its kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition is an interesting contribution.

Con:

* The demonstrations use simple dynamical systems, and the comparisons to DMD/PCA/kPCA yield unclear results.
* The practical usefulness of the method for ML applications is questioned, and the authors need to identify concrete use cases and compare to state-of-the-art.
* The theoretical results are unsurprising given existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature.

**Recommendations**

* Provide more convincing demonstrations of the method using more complex dynamical systems and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
* Identify concrete use cases for the method in ML applications and compare to state-of-the-art.
* Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations and discuss the limitations of this approach.
* Include standard DMD performance on the toy example and the Henon map to highlight differences.
* Consider extending the method to stochastic dynamical systems to increase its relevance to ML applications.
