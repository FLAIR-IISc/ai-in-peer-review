- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS to obtain nonlinear basis functions for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Technically sound with rigorous mathematics and two theorems linking kernel DMD to the Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a robustified variant projecting onto POD directions to address numerical stability.  
- Empirical results on synthetic (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world (locomotion recognition, change-point detection) tasks demonstrate applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, particularly extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015).  
- Needs quantitative comparisons to show clear advantages of the kernel approach over existing methods.  
- Well-organized and mathematically precise, but Sections 3–4 need more intuitive explanations for readers unfamiliar with RKHS/Koopman theory.  
- Algorithm descriptions are detailed, though the link between theory and practical implementation could be clearer.  
- Figures effectively illustrate results (Figure 3 is especially useful for dynamics recognition).  
- Novelty is incremental: kernel adaptation is valid but conceptually straightforward relative to established DMD.  
- Contribution not sufficiently distinguished from extended DMD and similar basis-function approaches.  
- Significance is limited: lacks evidence of outperforming simpler models (ARIMA, RNN) or existing kernel/change-point methods.  
- Pros: well-motivated, strong theoretical grounding, robust algorithm, relevant applications; Cons: incremental novelty, insufficient comparisons, limited demonstrated benefits.  
- Overall recommendation: technically solid but not sufficiently novel/significant for acceptance; recommend rejection unless stronger experimental comparisons and clearer advantage demonstrations are provided.
