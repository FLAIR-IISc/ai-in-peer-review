- Introduces kernel DMD (kDMD), extending Koopman operator theory and DMD into RKHS for richer nonlinear spectral analysis.  
- kDMD finds an orthogonal basis for the Krylov subspace in RKHS and solves an eigenproblem for the projected Koopman operator.  
- Proposes a numerically robust variant using kernel PCA (POD) to mitigate DMD instability.  
- Frames Koopman modes as features for downstream ML tasks and demonstrates utility in prediction, change-point detection, and pattern recognition.  
- Evaluated on synthetic chaotic maps (e.g., Hénon) and real-world motion-capture data.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong and elegant, centered on Theorem 1 and rigorous development.  
- Robust algorithm design thoughtfully parallels standard DMD regularization techniques and addresses practical numerical issues.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and accessible to non-experts.  
- Work is highly original: combines kernel methods with Koopman analysis, offering more flexibility than Extended DMD’s finite dictionary.  
- Significant potential impact by enabling more powerful, data-driven analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Weakness: experiments lack direct comparison to standard (non-kernel) DMD baselines to quantify benefits of kernelization.  
- Weakness: paper does not analyze sensitivity to kernel choice or hyperparameters (e.g., Gaussian width), limiting practical guidance.  
- Reviewer’s recommendation: overall excellent and recommends acceptance, with suggested additions of baseline comparisons and kernel-selection discussion.
