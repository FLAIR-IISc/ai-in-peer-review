- Proposes Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), extending DMD into RKHS to perform spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Uses feature maps to identify nonlinear basis functions, generalizing traditional DMD beyond linear observables.  
- Connects Koopman operator analysis with kernel methods from machine learning, building on prior work like Williams et al. (2015).  
- Highly original and significant; KDMD is an elegant, principled framework with strong potential for data-driven feature extraction and modeling.  
- Theoretical connection between Koopman theory and kernels is a valuable contribution for the NeurIPS audience.  
- Major weakness: paper is dense, overly mathematical, verbose, and difficult to follow for non-experts.  
- Presentation needs a pedagogical rewrite with clearer, more concise explanations to increase accessibility.  
- Organizational problems: Section 4 is largely redundant, Section 5 (Related Work) should be integrated into intro/conclusion, and Section 6’s example should be expanded into a full experimental section.  
- Minor issues: possible typo where a claimed “nonlinear” toy case appears linear.  
- Experiments use polynomial test problems where kernel choice is easy; need more discussion and guidance on kernel selection when dynamics are unknown.  
- Recommend citing broader data-driven dynamics work (e.g., Brunton et al., 2015).  
- Strong recommendation for substantial revision focusing on clarity and structure; with revisions this could become a highly impactful, citable paper.
