- Proposes KDMD: a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition into Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm leveraging kernel functions to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Demonstrates KDMD on toy problems, showing powerful generalization of standard DMD.  
- Paper is technically sound with a thorough theoretical foundation and a well-defined algorithm.  
- Empirical examples effectively illustrate the method’s capabilities.  
- Strong connection to prior DMD and Koopman literature and a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Manuscript is overly mathematical and verbose, making it difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Needs clearer, more concise explanations and a more pedagogical rewrite to broaden accessibility.  
- Organizational issues: Section 4 seems redundant; presentation could be streamlined.  
- Section 5 (related work) would benefit from examples or a summary in the conclusion to clarify connections.  
- Section 6 (empirical examples) is overly condensed and should be expanded with more details.  
- Minor concerns: the purportedly “nonlinear” toy case appears linear, and kernel/feature-map selection is underexplained.  
- Originality and significance: novel extension with potential for broader application in physics, engineering, and ML.  
- Overall recommendation balanced: strengths justify acceptance, but clarity, organization, and kernel-selection guidance need improvement; rating 7/10, confidence 8/10.
