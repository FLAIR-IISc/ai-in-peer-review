This paper proposes a Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS, deriving a kernelized DMD (kDMD) that (i) constructs a Krylov subspace in feature space and computes Ritz values/vectors via Gram operations, and (ii) introduces a robust variant using kernel PCA/POD truncation. Theoretical statements connect the resulting eigenpairs to Koopman spectra, and the method is demonstrated on toy and real data for prediction (via a preimage heuristic), dynamics recognition (principal angles), and change-point detection. Conceptually, this line sits at the intersection of DMD/Koopman analysis and kernel methods, closely related to Williams et al. (2015, extended DMD/EDMD) and kernel EDMD variants, while leveraging Gram-matrix computations for efficiency and numerical stability.

Strengths (Quality/Significance): The formulation is mathematically clean, with clear operator-theoretic grounding and practical algorithms that avoid explicit feature maps. The POD-based stabilization is sensible and commonly adopted in DMD practice. Theorems make the connection to Koopman objects explicit, and the applications outline a promising feature-extraction pipeline for nonlinear dynamics. The approach can be computationally attractive when the number of snapshots is moderate, as it scales with Gram matrices and benefits from truncation.

Weaknesses and requests (Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance):
- Distinguish more explicitly from Williams et al. (2015) and kernel EDMD: what is genuinely novel beyond using RKHS and POD in Sec. 4? Please provide side-by-side algorithmic and empirical comparisons (esp. Sec. 4).
- Related work is incomplete on kernel-based dynamical systems. Please discuss and cite, e.g., Ralaivola & d’Alché Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf, and position your contribution relative to them.
- Stochastic settings: the brief treatment around CUSUM is informal. Provide a principled discussion (Koopman–Markov operators, expectations) and experiments with stochastic dynamics to assess performance and limitations.
- Data assumptions: the “pairs of consecutive observables” remark needs sharper practical guidance. What causal assumptions are required? For multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations or multi-step dependencies, are pairs sufficient, or are lagged embeddings/sequences preferable?
- Experiments are modest. Please add stronger baselines: for prediction (EDMD/kernel EDMD, optimized DMD), for dynamics recognition (kernel k-means, subspace-kernel methods), and for change-point detection (state-of-the-art online kernel CPD: MMD-based, RuLSIF-based, BOCPD-kernel variants). Report runtime and scalability (O(τ2) memory/O(τ3) time) versus alternatives.

Pros:
- Clear operator-theoretic framing; practical, kernelized algorithm; potential impact on feature extraction for dynamics.

Cons:
- Insufficient differentiation from EDMD; missing related work; limited and unconvincing empirical comparisons; unclear stochastic and causal assumptions.

Minor: two typos—indexing τ−1 vs τ; replace dot with comma in k(xi, xj) (last equation on p.7).
