- Paper proposes RKHS-based Koopman spectral analysis using an RKHS-Krylov matrix and DMD-style projection, with a robust kernel-PCA pre-projection.  
- Connects to DMD/EDMD and kernelized EDMD approaches, offering a principled kernelized extension of DMD.  

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and well-organized presentation of core concepts (Koopman operators, Krylov subspaces, kernelization).  
- Technically sound RKHS-Krylov/projection viewpoint and a practical robust kernel-PCA step.  
- Applications (prediction, dynamics recognition, change-point detection) demonstrate potential utility.  
- Likely useful to ML practitioners wanting rich implicit dictionaries.  

Major concerns:
- Section 3.1 / Eq. (7): operator and function-space notation is ambiguous—KH appears to act on H-valued functions, not H itself; this must be explicitly stated.  
- Domain/codomain mismatch: ϕ: M→H so ϕ∉H; need correct function-space notation (e.g., calligraphic F for H-valued functions) and inner product specification.  
- Eigen-objects unclear: must distinguish scalar Koopman eigenfunctions φj: M→C, RKHS modes v∈H, and RKHS representers κj with φj(x)=⟨ϕ(x),κj⟩.  
- Phrases like “applying KH to both sides” are ambiguous when the displayed equality is scalar-valued—state precisely whether KH acts on F or parametrized vectors.  
- Theorem 1: missing/unstated strong assumptions (full column rank of Mτ, existence/distinctness of companion form eigenvalues, noise absence, Krylov invariance); proof uses implicit left inverses without justification.  
- Claim of “full recovery” is too strong; require that the invariant subspace of relevant eigenfunctions lies in the data span and is sufficiently excited—tone down and qualify.  
- Experiments are limited: need comparisons to exact/optimized DMD, EDMD with chosen dictionaries, and kernelized EDMD baselines.  
- Empirical study should include sensitivity to kernel choice/width and truncation rank, plus computational complexity and memory vs. τ and rank for scalability assessment.  

Quality and clarity:
- Core idea and robustification are valuable, but Sec. 3 needs careful rewrite to fix functional-analytic and notational ambiguities.  
- Notational overload (ϕ vs φ) harms readability; recommend consistent naming (e.g., ϕ feature map, vj modes, φj scalar eigenfunctions, κj RKHS representers).  

Specific requests for authors:
- Formalize the function space for H-valued observables and restate KH and Theorem 1 in that setting.  
- Clarify relationship between empirical Ritz vectors φ̃j∈H and Koopman modes uj, and conditions for approximation.  
- In Eq. (11), state conditions for F̂ to be a consistent estimator and discuss bias from centering/truncation.  
- For change-point detection, justify dropping the second term in the likelihood ratio and assess robustness.  

Minor issues/typos:
- Fix typos (e.g., “the the” at line 55) and index mixups (κj/λj vs κi/λi at line 153).  
- Consider renaming to reduce confusion between ϕ and φ.  

Pros:
- Clear exposition, solid algorithmic contribution, and practical robust variant.  

Cons:
- Mathematical ambiguities in Sec. 3.1, unstated strong assumptions in Theorem 1, and limited empirical comparisons.  

Recommendation:
- Weak accept conditional on clarifying operator/function-space definitions, tightening Theorem 1 (assumptions/proof), and adding minimal EDMD/exact DMD comparisons.
