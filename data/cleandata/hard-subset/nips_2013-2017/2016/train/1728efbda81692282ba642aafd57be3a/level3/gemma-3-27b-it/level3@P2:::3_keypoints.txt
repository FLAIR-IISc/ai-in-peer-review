- Presents a novel RKHS-based extension of DMD for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Core contribution: modal decomposition via orthogonal bases of the Krylov matrix in RKHS followed by eigendecomposition.
- Well-written, clearly organized paper with thorough introduction and detailed methodology.
- Technically sound formulation leveraging functional analysis and numerical linear algebra to incorporate nonlinear basis functions.
- Completeness: includes POD-based robustification and demonstrations of multiple applications.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Derivation clarity issue: Section 3.1 (Equation (7) and Theorem 1) is insufficiently explained and confusing.
- Mathematical consistency concern: unclear domain relationship between KH (H→H) and eigenfunction φ (M→H); φ may not lie in H.
- Assumptions and proof validity: underlying assumptions for Theorem 1 are not explicitly stated and appear restrictive; proof introduces unstated assumptions.
- Limited comparative evaluation: lacks benchmarks against state-of-the-art methods (e.g., extended DMD, SINDy).
- Minor typographical and notation errors need correction (e.g., duplicate "the", variable mix-up \kappaj/\lambdaj).
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically grounded novel method, broadly applicable algorithm, encouraging empirical results, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear derivations, missing comprehensive comparisons, and unresolved mathematical inconsistencies.
- Overall recommendation: potentially valuable work; recommend weak accept contingent on clarifying Theorem 1/Equation (7), justifying assumptions, and adding comparative evaluations.
