- Proposes a kernelized Koopman spectral analysis by formulating DMD in RKHS using time-shifted feature maps as a Krylov sequence and projecting the Koopman operator onto that subspace.

- Derives an RKHS Arnoldi/DMD procedure: orthonormalize Krylov snapshots, form projected operator, and extract Ritz values/vectors as Koopman eigenvalues/modes.

- Introduces a robustified algorithm projecting onto kernel-POD (kernel PCA) directions to stabilize computation in rank-deficient or ill-conditioned regimes.

- Proves theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) relating computed eigenvalues/eigenvectors to true Koopman spectra under span/richness and distinct-eigenvalue assumptions.

- Applies the method to short-term prediction (via pre-image), dynamics recognition (subspace distances), and sequential change-point detection (kernelized CUSUM statistic).

- Empirical demonstrations on toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, motion-capture locomotion, and Lorenz-map change detection show feasibility but are qualitative.

- Situates work relative to prior methods: closest to EDMD/KDMD, but framed via RKHS Krylov/Arnoldi with a QR/Ritz perspective and kernel-POD robustification.

- Linear-algebraic development is sound and clearly motivated; theoretical claims are reasonable under stated assumptions.

- Practical aspects like centering, truncation, and Gram matrices are addressed, but pre-image accuracy and computational cost may dominate in practice.

- Major empirical weaknesses: limited quantitative evaluation and missing comparisons to standard DMD, exact DMD, EDMD, and KDMD baselines.

- No complexity or memory analysis; kernel methods scale poorly (O(τ^2) memory, O(τ^3) time) and scalability is not discussed.

- Clarity is high—well written and organized—but the paper should add pseudo-code with complexity and clearer hyperparameter guidance.

- Suggests evaluating kernel choice/width/degree, truncation p, regularization, noise robustness, and providing diagnostics for theoretical assumption violations.

- Recommends comparisons of pre-image methods and reporting pre-image/prediction errors, plus exploring scalable approximations (Nyström, random features).

- Pros: clear RKHS derivation, practical robustification via kernel PCA, useful applications, and code availability.

- Cons: limited quantitative experiments and baselines, no kernel/hyperparameter or scaling analysis, unassessed pre-image errors, and strong theoretical assumptions without diagnostics.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—technically competent and useful but needs stronger empirical comparisons and kernel analyses to demonstrate advantages over existing (K)DMD methods.
