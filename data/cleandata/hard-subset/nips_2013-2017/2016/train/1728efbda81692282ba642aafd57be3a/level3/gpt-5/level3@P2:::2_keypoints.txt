- Proposes kernelized DMD in RKHS using a Krylov matrix of feature vectors and two procedures: RKHS Arnoldi/QR (Ritz) and a kernel PCA–based robust variant.
- Uses resulting Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions for short-term prediction (pre-image), recognition via kernel principal angles, and CUSUM-style change-point detection.
- Experiments on a toy system, the Hénon map, and mocap locomotion demonstrate feasibility but are limited and mostly qualitative.
- Approach is closely related to EDMD/kernel EDMD and kernel transfer operator methods; novelty is incremental and mainly algorithmic reframing.
- Theorem 1 restates Arnoldi/Ritz in feature space; Theorem 2 aligns with EDMD-style consistency under strong, restrictive assumptions (distinct eigenvalues; eigenfunctions in retained subspace).
- Key technical assumptions are restrictive and not critically discussed.
- No convergence/consistency proofs or noise-sensitivity analysis are provided.
- The kernel PCA “robustification” lacks guidance on truncation, regularization, and numerical-stability choices (e.g., Tikhonov, pivoted QR, randomized SVD).
- Change-point detection derivation contains heuristic steps (dropping likelihood terms) without theoretical guarantees.
- Pre-image recovery method is heuristic with no error analysis or specified success conditions.
- Experimental comparisons are sparse: missing direct comparisons to EDMD/kernel EDMD, kernel transfer operators, GPDM/GPSSM, Koopman RRR/OKDMD, RNNs/LSTMs, and spectral baselines.
- Computational complexity, memory scaling with time points and kernel choice, and hyperparameter selection strategies are not discussed.
- Some claims are imprecise (e.g., eigenvalues on unit circle only under measure-preserving dynamics) and notation/centering details are sometimes unclear.
- Paper lacks pseudocode for algorithms and omits details of the pre-image algorithm, harming reproducibility.
- Framing via RKHS Arnoldi/QR is a useful perspective but does not, as presented, offer clear theoretical or practical advantages over prior work.
- Potential significance exists if the method can be shown to provide clear empirical or computational benefits (accuracy, robustness, sample efficiency, scalability).
- Pros: clear RKHS formulation linked to Krylov/Arnoldi, conceptually attractive feature-extraction view, and provided code/illustrative examples.
- Cons: limited novelty versus prior kernel Koopman methods, weak theoretical guarantees, heuristic application steps, sparse/qualitative experiments, and unclear practical advantages.
- Suggested improvements: clarify novelty vs EDMD/kernel transfer operators and add direct empirical comparisons and ablations.
- Suggested improvements (cont.): provide complexity/memory analysis, hyperparameter/truncation guidance, quantitative benchmarks with noise robustness, and principled change-point metrics (detection delay/false alarms).
- Suggested clarity fixes: add concise pseudocode, fix typos/notation inconsistencies, refine theoretical statements and expand proofs or references.
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — technically competent and well-motivated but incremental with insufficient theoretical and empirical support.
