- Proposes a kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Embeds observable functions into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) to provide a more expressive basis than standard linear monomials.  
- Uses kernel PCA for robustness, then eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator to produce a modal decomposition for feature extraction.  
- Demonstrates reasonable empirical performance on synthetic and real-world tasks: dynamics recognition, state prediction, and change-point detection.  
- Mathematical derivation is technically sound and the paper is well-organized with clear theoretical presentation.  
- Novelty is limited: kernelizing DMD is an incremental adaptation rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Lacks sufficient demonstration of distinct advantages over competing methods like recurrent neural networks or established change-detection techniques.  
- Applications section does not convincingly show superior performance or unique contributions compared to existing techniques.  
- Originality is questionable given the incremental contribution.  
- Significance is limited due to insufficient evidence that the method advances the state of the art for target applications.  
- Recommends more thorough comparisons with relevant baselines and clearer articulation of the unique value proposition.
