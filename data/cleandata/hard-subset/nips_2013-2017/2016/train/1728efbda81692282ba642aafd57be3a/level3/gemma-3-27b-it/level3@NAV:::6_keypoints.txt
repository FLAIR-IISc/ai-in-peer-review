- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS for more flexible Koopman eigenfunction approximation.
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performs eigendecomposition.
- Presentation is clear and pedagogically strong, with helpful background on Koopman operators and DMD.
- Theoretical foundation is sound and builds on existing spectral, DMD, and kernel/RKHS literature.
- Empirical evaluation is weak: toy examples show the method works but do not prove clear advantages.
- Comparisons to standard DMD, PCA, and kPCA are inconclusive and fail to demonstrate superiority.
- Locomotion data example lacks quantitative comparisons and a compelling demonstration versus standard ML methods.
- Limited practical ML relevance: no strong demonstrations in application domains like robotics or broader dynamical inference tasks.
- Deterministic setup limits applicability to real-world problems where stochasticity/noise matters.
- Potential limitation if the method requires explicit feature-space representations versus implicit kernel mappings.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear exposition, theoretically grounded, and pedagogically valuable.
- Arguments against acceptance: unconvincing empirical results, limited practical impact, deterministic focus, and incremental theoretical novelty.
- Recommendations: include standard DMD comparisons on toy/Henon examples, clarify feature-space requirements, focus on a specific ML application with rigorous comparison, and incorporate noise/stochasticity in experiments.
