- Proposal: Extend Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Algorithms: Present two methods — a direct Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization in feature space and a practical robust version projecting data onto kernel-PCA (POD) bases before estimating the Koopman operator.
- Applications: Demonstrate learned Koopman modes/eigenvalues as features for state prediction, dynamics recognition, and sequential change-point detection.
- Main strength: Elegant, theoretically grounded fusion of Koopman operator theory with kernel methods, linking DMD to machine learning tools.
- Theoretical soundness: Theorems 1 and 2 are well-derived and provide solid justification for the proposed algorithms.
- Potential impact: Using Koopman eigenfunctions as ML features is a promising direction beyond system identification.
- Originality concern: The method, especially the robust version, appears highly similar to Extended DMD (eDMD) and lacks a clear theoretical or empirical distinction from Williams et al. (2015).
- Literature gap: The review misses substantial prior work on kernel methods for dynamical systems and time-series (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc, Tuia et al., Rojo-Álvarez, Franz & Schölkopf).
- Experimental weakness: Empirical evaluation is illustrative rather than rigorous and insufficient for a top-tier venue.
- Prediction task lacks baselines: No comparisons with standard time-series forecasting baselines (e.g., autoregressive models, Gaussian Process dynamical models).
- Dynamics recognition lacks quantitative evaluation: Uses MDS visualization but no comparisons to established time-series classification or clustering methods.
- Change-point detection comparison is narrow: Benchmarked only against an older kernel method; needs comparison to recent state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detectors.
- Stochastic systems not addressed: Paper focuses on deterministic systems with insufficient discussion or experiments on noise/stochasticity and algorithm robustness.
- Data-pair formulation unclear: The remark about using collections of (x_t, x_{t+1}) needs clarification on handling multidimensional time series, causality assumptions, and independence.
- Clarity and presentation: Must explicitly compare and contrast with Williams et al. [35], fix indexing inconsistencies and minor typos (e.g., indexing in Theorem 1 and kernel notation).
