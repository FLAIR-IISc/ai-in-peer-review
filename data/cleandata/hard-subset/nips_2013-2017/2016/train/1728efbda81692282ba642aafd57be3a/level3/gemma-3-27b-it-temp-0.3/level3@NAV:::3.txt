## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper presents a kernel-based extension to Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis. The core idea is to perform DMD within a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), allowing for the approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions, moving beyond the linear monomials used in traditional DMD. The authors propose an algorithm based on Krylov subspace calculations and provide numerical experiments demonstrating its effectiveness, including a robustification method using POD.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly articulated, and the theoretical concepts are presented in a logical and accessible manner. The extension of DMD using kernel methods is a natural and potentially powerful direction, building upon the established strengths of both techniques. The numerical results, while limited in comparison to state-of-the-art methods, do illustrate the usefulness of the proposed approach on both synthetic and real-world data. The inclusion of applications like state prediction and change-point detection further highlights the potential impact of this work.

However, several points require clarification. Section 3.1, specifically Equation (7), and the subsequent proof of Theorem 1 are unclear. The purpose and distinction between Equation (7) and Equation (2) are not immediately obvious, and the notation feels somewhat redundant. A key mathematical inconsistency arises from the fact that *KH* is an operator *H→H*, while *φ* is a map *M→H*, implying *φ* may not necessarily belong to *H*. This needs a more thorough explanation. Furthermore, the phrase "applying the operator *KH* to both sides" in lines 144-157 is unclear, as both sides appear to be scalars. 

The assumptions in Theorem 1 are strong and not explicitly stated, with additional assumptions appearing within the proof itself. The validity of these implicit assumptions needs to be addressed. Finally, there are minor typos – “the the” on line 55 and an inconsistency in index notation on line 153 (\kappaj / \lambdaj instead of \kappai / \lambda_i).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of DMD with strong theoretical grounding.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Demonstrated usefulness through numerical experiments.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity in Section 3.1 and the proof of Theorem 1.
*   Mathematical inconsistencies requiring clarification.
*   Limited comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods.
*   Unstated and potentially strong assumptions.



**Overall:** This paper presents a promising contribution to the field of Koopman spectral analysis. Addressing the concerns regarding clarity and mathematical rigor would significantly strengthen the manuscript and increase its impact. I recommend acceptance with major revisions.
