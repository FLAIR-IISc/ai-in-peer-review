- Proposes Kernel Dynamic Mode Decomposition (KDMD), extending DMD to RKHS using the kernel trick to recover Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear implicit features.
- Provides theoretical development and proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) plus an algorithm and a numerically robust variant via kernel PCA.
- Demonstrates KDMD on synthetic and real data for prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Novel and significant: combines Koopman operator theory, DMD, and kernel methods to overcome DMD’s linear-observable limitation.
- Technical quality is strong with sound theoretical foundations and attention to numerical robustness.
- Potentially high impact as a versatile feature-extraction tool for nonlinear time-series and dynamics analysis.
- Major weakness: paper is overly mathematical, dense, and hard to read for a broad ML audience, obscuring core intuition.
- Clarity and presentation need a pedagogical rewrite with high-level intuition before formalism (e.g., Section 3).
- Redundancy and structure issues: presenting an ill-conditioned Gram–Schmidt algorithm in full before the practical robust algorithm is inefficient.
- Empirical section (Section 6) is overly condensed and lacks detailed discussion, sensitivity analysis, and parameter studies (e.g., kernel choice).
- Experimental concerns: reliance on matched kernels (polynomial kernel for polynomial dynamics) raises questions about performance with mismatched or unknown dynamics.
- Suggest adding guidance/discussion on kernel selection and analyzing degradation under kernel mismatch.
- Related work connections could be strengthened, explicitly linking to Extended DMD and citing other data-driven discovery methods (e.g., Brunton et al., SINDy).
- Minor remark: clarify that the toy system is nonlinear due to the x_t^2 term; some dynamics appear linear but are not fully so.
- Minor remark: applications (Section 7) and pre-image computation are presented briefly and need more thorough experimental comparison and explanation.
