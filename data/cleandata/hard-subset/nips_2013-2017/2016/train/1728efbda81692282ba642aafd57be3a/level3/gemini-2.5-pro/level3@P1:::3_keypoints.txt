- Paper proposes kernelized DMD in an RKHS to approximate the Koopman operator in feature space, with a direct Gram–Schmidt algorithm and a robust kernel-PCA projection variant, demonstrated on prediction, recognition, and change‑point tasks.

- Novel and significant idea: elegant combination of Koopman operator theory and kernel methods that can extend DMD to rich nonlinear bases when good basis functions are unknown.

- Well-written and logically structured; clear background and sensible progression from exact but unstable algorithm to a practical, robust version.

- Technically plausible: kernel-PCA dimensionality reduction is a practical way to mitigate numerical instability, and experiments show the method can extract meaningful dynamic features.

- Major theoretical weakness: Section 3 is unclear—definition of the Koopman operator KH in RKHS is ambiguous (domain/codomain and the nature of eigenfunctions are not specified).

- Mathematical inconsistency: treating ϕ as both a map M→H and an element of H makes the application of KH ill-defined; the operator/feature-map relationship needs rigorous formalization.

- Theorem 1 and proofs are hard to follow and rely on unstated, strong assumptions (e.g., Krylov matrix Mτ full rank and invertible R); these assumptions must be explicitly stated and justified.

- Experimental evaluation is limited: lacks rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (other time-series models, Extended DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD, etc.), and change-point experiments compare to only one other method.

- Recommendation summary: promising and potentially impactful idea but requires clearer, rigorous theory and stronger experimental comparisons before acceptance.

- Minor issues: typo "the the" (Line 55) and possible indexing error in the proof using κj and λj (Line 153).
