- Proposes an RKHS-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for richer Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Develops an RKHS modal decomposition algorithm using orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of a projected Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a POD-based robust variant to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Demonstrates applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data.  
- Builds on prior DMD and Koopman work (Schmid 2010; Williams et al. 2015) and connects naturally to kernel PCA and spectral methods.  
- Claims improved expressiveness over traditional DMD by capturing global nonlinear Koopman eigenfunctions rather than local linearizations.  
- Presents strong mathematical rigor, clear theoretical development, and well-supported theoretical claims.  
- Provides thorough empirical validation, including toy nonlinear system and Hénon map with accurate eigenvalue estimation.  
- Shows practical utility in the locomotion dataset by distinguishing motion patterns.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality is significant: novel integration of RKHS with Koopman spectral analysis and appropriate literature positioning.  
- Significance is substantial, with potential impact across fields that analyze nonlinear dynamics (e.g., fluid dynamics, neuroscience, robotics).  
- Weakness: limited comparative experiments against non-RKHS Koopman/DMD methods across diverse datasets with varying nonlinearity.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of how kernel choice and parameter selection affect Koopman decomposition quality.  
- Weakness: some application comparisons (e.g., prediction) lack benchmarking against state-of-the-art alternatives.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to include more systematic comparisons and analysis of kernel choice effects.
