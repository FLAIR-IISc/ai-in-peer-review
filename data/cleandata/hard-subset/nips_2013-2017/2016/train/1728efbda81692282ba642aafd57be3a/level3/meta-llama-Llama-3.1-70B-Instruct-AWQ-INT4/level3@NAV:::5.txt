This paper proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS), referred to as KDMD. The authors apply this method to toy problems and demonstrate its effectiveness as a powerful generalization of DMD. The key idea is to approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions using a set of nonlinear basis functions due to the expressiveness of kernel functions.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the theoretical background, including the Koopman operator and DMD. The proposed algorithm is described in detail, and the authors provide empirical examples to illustrate its effectiveness.

However, the manuscript is overly mathematical and verbose, making it challenging to follow for non-experts. The authors could benefit from providing clearer, shorter explanations and more intuitive examples to facilitate understanding. Additionally, some sections, such as Section 4, appear redundant and could be removed or merged with other sections.

The paper's originality and significance are evident in its extension of DMD to RKHS, which provides a more flexible and powerful framework for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors demonstrate the potential applications of KDMD in various fields, including physics, engineering, and machine learning.

The paper's quality is high, with well-supported claims and a thorough analysis of the proposed method. The authors are careful in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

In terms of clarity, the paper could benefit from improvements in organization, notation, and explanation. The authors could provide more intuitive examples and visualizations to facilitate understanding.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems and machine learning. With some revisions to improve clarity and readability, it has the potential to become a highly citable paper.
