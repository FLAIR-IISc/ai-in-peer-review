- Proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Enables approximation of richer Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions in RKHS.  
- Algorithm is mathematically sound with well-done derivations.  
- Demonstrated effective at extracting meaningful dynamical information in empirical examples.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear, concise introduction to the Koopman operator and DMD.  
- Extension to RKHS is well-motivated and thoroughly explained.  
- Use of kernel PCA to robustify the algorithm is a good and clearly explained idea.  
- Empirical examples are well-chosen and illustrate the algorithm’s strengths.  
- Good discussion of related work and clear differentiation from existing methods.  
- Paper exhibits high clarity and readability despite technical content.  
- Potential weakness: assumes a high level of mathematical sophistication from readers.  
- Potential weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to nonlinear dynamical systems with high practical potential.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
