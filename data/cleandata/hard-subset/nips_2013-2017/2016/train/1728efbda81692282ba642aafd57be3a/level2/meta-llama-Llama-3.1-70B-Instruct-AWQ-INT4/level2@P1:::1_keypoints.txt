- Proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm that reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the Koopman projection.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides a concise introduction to background material.  
- Technical details and the proposed algorithm are thoroughly explained with analysis of properties and limitations.  
- Provides several empirical examples on synthetic and real-world data demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: novel and potentially more accurate/robust extension of DMD using RKHS.  
- Strength: well-organized presentation and clear exposition of technical material.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of method properties and limitations.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in dynamical systems, Koopman theory, and RKHS, which may limit accessibility.  
- Weakness: computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS can be computationally expensive for large datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art Koopman spectral methods.  
- Originality: significant contribution by combining DMD and RKHS for Koopman analysis.  
- Significance: potential impact across applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating: 8/10; Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
