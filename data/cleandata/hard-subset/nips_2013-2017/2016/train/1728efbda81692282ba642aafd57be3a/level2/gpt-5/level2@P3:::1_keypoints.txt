- Proposes a kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS lifts, computing Ritz values/vectors from a Krylov subspace via Gram-matrix Gram–Schmidt QR, with a robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA/POD directions.  
- Theorem 1: RKHS Ritz vectors reconstruct lifted observables along trajectories; Theorem 2: kernel-POD-projected operator relates to Koopman eigenstructure if eigenfunctions lie in the span of kernel principal components.  
- Work is closely related to EDMD/kernel EDMD and to stable DMD variants (exact/optimized DMD); distinctions and advantages over kernel EDMD are not clearly delineated.  
- Strength: Clear RKHS/Arnoldi formulation and constructive algorithm relying only on kernel evaluations.  
- Strength: Practical focus on numerical stability via kernel PCA/POD projection and truncation.  
- Strength: Theoretical links between computed eigenpairs and Koopman objects plus reconstruction formulas.  
- Strength: Broad application scope (prediction, change detection, motion recognition) and code availability.  
- Weakness: Novelty over (kernel) EDMD is unclear; robustified algorithm appears equivalent or very close to kernel EDMD without formal comparison.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited and mainly qualitative; lacks baselines and quantitative metrics (eigenvalue/mode errors, prediction RMSE, CPD performance).  
- Weakness: Incorrect or imprecise claim that Koopman eigenvalues always lie on the unit circle — this requires measure-preserving dynamics and fails for dissipative systems.  
- Weakness: Pre-image procedure is only sketched (MDS-style) with no algorithmic details, complexity analysis, or empirical robustness comparisons to established pre-image methods.  
- Weakness: Application experiments lack rigorous evaluation and ablations (kernel choice, truncation level, noise sensitivity, data requirements); figures/labels are sometimes unclear.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 relies on strong assumptions (eigenfunctions in span of kernel PCs); need discussion of when these hold and any consistency guarantees.  
- Quality: Linear-algebra derivations are plausible, but more numerical guidance (conditioning, regularization, pivoting, SVD implementations) is needed; change-point derivation drops terms without justification.  
- Clarity: Overall well-structured with explicit algorithm steps, but some notation/grammar issues and inconsistent figure references; implementation details need expansion.  
- Originality: Framing DMD in RKHS is natural and related to prior EDMD work; incremental novelty (Arnoldi/Krylov perspective and POD stabilization) is not clearly established.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful if differentiated and empirically validated, but current positioning and experiments are insufficient for strong venue impact.  
- Essential suggestions: provide rigorous theoretical and empirical comparison to EDMD/kernel EDMD; add quantitative experiments with ground-truth spectra and robust baselines; report standard metrics for applications.  
- Important suggestions: refine theoretical claims (unit-circle), discuss conditions for Theorem 2, and provide consistency/error bounds if possible; detail and evaluate the pre-image algorithm.  
- Nice-to-have suggestions: guidance on kernel/parameter selection, truncation criteria, ablation studies, and supplementary code for reproducibility.  
- Pros summary: clear RKHS-based framework, attention to numerical stability, broad application potential, code available.  
- Cons summary: unclear novelty vs kernel EDMD, limited quantitative validation, and some claims/derivations need refinement and empirical support.  
- Overall assessment: Promising mathematical framing but appears incremental relative to kernel EDMD and lacks the empirical rigor and comparisons needed for strong acceptance; strengthen positioning and experiments.
