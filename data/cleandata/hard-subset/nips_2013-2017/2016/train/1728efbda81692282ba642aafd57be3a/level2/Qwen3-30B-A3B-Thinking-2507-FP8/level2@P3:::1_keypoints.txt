- Proposes an RKHS extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Algorithm reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection.  
- Uses nonlinear basis functions via kernel functions, contrasting with standard DMD's linear monomials.  
- Introduces a POD-based robustification to mitigate numerical instability of standard DMD.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: Theorem 1 shows equivalence with DMD in RKHS and Theorem 2 links the method to Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Provides clear connections between mathematical formulation and practical implementation with detailed algorithm descriptions.  
- Empirical evaluation includes synthetic examples (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world tasks (locomotion recognition, change-point detection) demonstrating feature utility.  
- Limited empirical comparison to state-of-the-art methods; no quantitative benchmarks against Extended DMD or other recent approaches.  
- Applications section lacks rigorous comparisons to alternative feature extraction or classification methods.  
- Computational complexity and scalability are not thoroughly discussed, which is important for practical use.  
- Clarity is generally good but some sections need more intuitive explanations and clearer physical interpretation of Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Originality is moderate; the paper does not sufficiently differentiate or show improvements over Williams et al.'s Extended DMD and other kernel-based methods.  
- Significance is promising but not fully demonstrated—paper should show cases where the kernel approach outperforms linear DMD.  
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative comparisons on standard benchmarks, provide detailed computational complexity analysis, and more explicitly differentiate from Extended DMD and related work.
