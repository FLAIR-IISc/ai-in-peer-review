- Proposes Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS with two algorithms: a Gram–Schmidt/QR Krylov DMD in feature space and a robust kernel-PCA–projected operator F̂ = U* K_H U.  
- Theorems claim exact feature-space reconstructions on the observed window and relate F̂ eigenpairs to Koopman spectra under subspace richness assumptions.  
- Applications presented: short-term prediction via a pre-image method, recognition using kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection with RKHS exponential-family CUSUM scores.  
- Experiments: toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, motion-capture embedding, and a change-point example—mainly proof-of-concept plots.  
- Closely related to EDMD/KEDMD; construction is essentially a data-driven Galerkin projection equivalent to kernel EDMD with a data-derived dictionary.  
- Krylov/QR viewpoint usefully ties Arnoldi/DMD to kernel methods, but the practical finite operator matches standard kernel EDMD Gram-matrix forms.  
- Theoretical claims parallel standard DMD/EDMD results, but some derivations are informal and notation blurs operators in H with finite matrices (e.g., ill-posed inverses).  
- Strong assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, eigenfunction representability in the subspace) are used and not fully relaxed or justified.  
- Robust variant yields the usual projected operator F̂ = U* K_H U; connections to Koopman eigenfunctions follow standard EDMD theory.  
- Pre-image prediction is heuristic and MDS-like, lacking error analysis and clear failure modes.  
- Change-point detection derivation is terse and omits key implementation details (log-partition estimation, threshold calibration, noise handling).  
- Recognition experiment uses kernel principal angles but provides no classification metrics or quantitative evaluation.  
- Empirical evaluation is qualitative, lacking systematic metrics (prediction errors), noise-robustness tests, hyperparameter sensitivity, and comparisons to EDMD/KEDMD/Exact DMD.  
- Manuscript is readable but notation-heavy and sometimes imprecise; needs clearer separation of H-operators vs. finite matrices and correct use of pseudoinverses/projectors.  
- Recommend adding concise pseudocode for both algorithms, computational complexity, and numerical stabilization (pivoted QR/SVD) details.  
- Clarify modeling assumptions (ergodicity, attractor/measure-preserving qualifiers) and correct the misleading claim about Koopman eigenvalues on attractors.  
- Pre-image section should specify the optimization, initialization, and when the method fails (e.g., non-invertible feature maps, ambiguity).  
- Originality assessed as moderate — main novelty is the Krylov/Arnoldi re-derivation and QR-based viewpoint; method overlaps substantially with existing EDMD/KEDMD.  
- Significance limited by overlap with KEDMD and lack of strong empirical evidence showing superiority in accuracy, stability, or scalability.  
- Pros: clarifies DMD/Arnoldi–RKHS connections, proposes practical kernel-PCA truncation, outlines diverse applications, and leverages the kernel trick to avoid explicit features.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs. EDMD/KEDMD, some informal theory, qualitative experiments, and underdeveloped pre-image and change-detection components.  
- Key author questions: exact differences/conditions of equivalence with kernel EDMD; quantitative comparisons to EDMD/KEDMD/Exact DMD; sensitivity to kernel choice and truncation rank; noise handling and need for regularization; treatment of eigenvalue multiplicity and non-diagonalizable cases.  
- Minor requests: fix typos/formatting, clarify how Hénon equilibrium eigenvalues were computed, provide runtime/memory scaling with τ and p, and release reproducible code.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — paper is a useful synthesis but needs clearer theoretical distinctions or stronger quantitative experiments to demonstrate added value.
