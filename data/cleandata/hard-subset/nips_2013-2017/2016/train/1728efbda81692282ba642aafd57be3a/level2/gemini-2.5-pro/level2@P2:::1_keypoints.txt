- Summary: Proposes kernelized DMD performing spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in an RKHS to recover nonlinear Koopman eigenfunctions and modes.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Derives a DMD-in-feature-space algorithm using Krylov subspaces and Gram–Schmidt, plus a numerically robust variant projecting onto kernel principal components.  
- Applications: Demonstrates learned Koopman features for state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Strength — originality: Novel synthesis of Koopman/operator-theoretic DMD with kernel methods, offering principled nonlinear spectral decomposition.  
- Strength — technical quality: Solid theoretical development (Theorems 1–2) linking RKHS Krylov methods to Koopman eigenfunctions and justifying the robustified algorithm.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, accessible introduction and clear derivations for readers familiar with kernel methods.  
- Weakness — relation to eDMD: Insufficient differentiation from Extended DMD; robust algorithm may be equivalent to eDMD with kernel dictionary and needs clearer theoretical/empirical distinction.  
- Weakness — experimental rigor: Lacks quantitative comparisons and baselines across tasks (e.g., MSE for prediction, comparisons to standard DMD and other nonlinear models).  
- Weakness — Hénon map experiment: Results are vague—unclear which equilibrium eigenvalues are recovered and how accurately dynamics are reconstructed.  
- Weakness — embedding task: Locomotion embedding is visually appealing but lacks comparison to other manifold/time-series embedding methods.  
- Weakness — change-point detection: Needs quantitative metrics (detection delay, false alarms) and clearer baseline description; score derivation uses an unquantified heuristic.  
- Weakness — practical details: Omits hyperparameter selection (kernel choice/parameters, truncation rank p) and sensitivity analysis crucial for reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity issues: Heuristic approximation in change-point derivation should be stated/justified; important remark about using data pairs vs. contiguous trajectories should be highlighted earlier.  
- Conclusion: Strong, significant contribution likely of interest to the community, but should address eDMD relation and substantially strengthen quantitative, comparative experiments before final version.
