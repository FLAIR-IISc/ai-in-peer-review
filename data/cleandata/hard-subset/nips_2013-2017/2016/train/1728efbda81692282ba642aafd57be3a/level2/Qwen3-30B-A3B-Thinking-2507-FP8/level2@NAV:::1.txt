

This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The main claim is that their method provides a nonlinear feature extraction procedure by approximating Koopman eigenfunctions with richer basis functions than traditional DMD (which uses linear monomials), thereby capturing global dynamics without explicit knowledge of governing equations.

The paper presents a theoretically sound algorithm that reduces to calculating orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection. The authors provide two key theorems establishing the connection between their method and Koopman spectral analysis, along with a practical robustification approach using POD bases to address numerical instability. The empirical validation includes synthetic data (toy system, Hénon map) and real-world applications (locomotion dynamics recognition, sequential change-point detection using Lorenz map data).

Strengths include a strong theoretical foundation with clear mathematical derivations, meaningful novelty in extending DMD to nonlinear kernel-based features, and practical implementation considerations. The robustification method effectively addresses a key limitation of standard DMD. The applications demonstrate the method's utility in machine learning contexts.

Weaknesses include insufficient discussion of method limitations (particularly regarding kernel selection and scalability), somewhat limited scope of real-world applications, and incomplete comparison with related kernel-based dynamical system methods. The connection to recent work on kernel methods for dynamical systems could be strengthened.

The paper makes a significant contribution by advancing Koopman spectral analysis through the incorporation of kernel methods, providing a more expressive feature extraction framework than traditional DMD. The theoretical claims are well-supported by the derivation and empirical results, though the practical impact could be further demonstrated with more diverse applications.

Pro: Theoretical novelty and rigor, meaningful extension of DMD, practical robustification, clear empirical validation.
Con: Limited discussion of limitations, narrow application scope, insufficient comparison with related kernel-based methods.

This work represents a valuable advancement in Koopman spectral analysis with clear potential for broader application in machine learning and dynamical systems research. The method's theoretical soundness and practical implementation make it a strong contribution to the field.
