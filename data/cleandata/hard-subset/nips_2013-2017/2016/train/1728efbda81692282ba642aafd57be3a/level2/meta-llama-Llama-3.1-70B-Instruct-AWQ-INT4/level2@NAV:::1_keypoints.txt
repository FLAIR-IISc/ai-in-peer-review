- Proposal: kernel DMD extends DMD into RKHS to perform spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Claim: kernel DMD can accurately approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions.  
- Claim: the method can be used for feature extraction and analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Theoretical support: provides a thorough mathematical derivation including extension of DMD to RKHS and Gram–Schmidt QR decomposition.  
- Empirical support: synthetic and real-world experiments show effectiveness in estimating Koopman eigenvalues and modes.  
- Usefulness: enables feature extraction and analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Usefulness: applicable to prediction and forecasting of nonlinear systems.  
- Usefulness: suitable for sequential change-point detection in nonlinear systems.  
- Field knowledge: authors demonstrate strong familiarity with spectral analysis, DMD, and kernel methods and explain mathematics clearly.  
- Originality: novel contribution by combining kernel methods with DMD to operate in RKHS using nonlinear basis functions.  
- Completeness: thorough derivations and examples provided, but implementation and computational-complexity details are under-specified.  
- Limitations: potential for ill-conditioned algorithms and sensitivity to kernel choice and parameter tuning.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel and effective method, demonstrated empirically, with broad application potential.  
- Cons for acceptance: requires careful kernel/parameter selection, may be computationally expensive for large datasets, and may not suit all nonlinear systems.
