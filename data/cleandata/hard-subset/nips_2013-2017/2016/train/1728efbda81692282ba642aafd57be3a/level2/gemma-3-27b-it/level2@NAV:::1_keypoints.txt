- Proposes RKHS-based DMD to perform Koopman spectral analysis for nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- Extends traditional DMD by using kernels to access richer function spaces and capture more complex dynamics.  
- Algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and performs eigendecomposition.  
- Demonstrates method on synthetic and real-world data for state prediction, change-point detection, and pattern recognition.  
- Builds logically on Koopman operator theory, DMD, and POD, correctly identifying traditional DMD limitations for highly nonlinear systems.  
- Theoretical foundation (notably Theorem 1) appears sound.  
- Includes POD-based robustification to mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Empirical results on a toy system and the Hénon map show promising eigenvalue estimation.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with existing kernel-based DMD variants (e.g., Extended DMD) and other nonlinear system identification methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and missing quantitative metrics (prediction error, classification accuracy) and baseline comparisons.  
- Presentation needs clearer guidance on practical kernel selection and parameter tuning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on stronger empirical validation, comprehensive related-work comparison, and clarification of kernel selection considerations.
