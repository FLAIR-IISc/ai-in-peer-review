- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis with orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and POD-based robust implementation.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong; Theorems 1 and 2 show equivalence with DMD in RKHS and justify the robust implementation.  
- RKHS extension offers richer basis functions than standard DMD, enabling better capture of complex nonlinear dynamics.  
- Clear connections and citations to prior Koopman and DMD work (Rowley et al., Chen et al., Williams et al.) and kernel methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, and real locomotion data demonstrate eigenvalue estimation, prediction, and recognition.  
- Robust POD projection effectively mitigates ill-conditioning issues present in standard DMD implementations.  
- Demonstrated practical applications: state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Paper is well organized and clearly written; mathematical exposition is accessible overall.  
- Some parts (notably the connection to kernel PCA) could use more intuitive explanation for non-RKHS specialists.  
- Original and significant: distinguishes itself from extended DMD by using kernel methods rather than pre-determined basis functions.  
- Potentially high impact across domains (fluid mechanics, control, ML) due to improved feature extraction for nonlinear systems.  
- Weakness: insufficient guidance on how to select kernels for different systems.  
- Weakness: computational complexity analysis is limited and needs deeper comparison to standard DMD.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of relation to eigensystem realization/subspace identification; comparison could be expanded.  
- Recommendation: suitable for NIPS given the theoretical advance, robust implementation, and compelling empirical results.
