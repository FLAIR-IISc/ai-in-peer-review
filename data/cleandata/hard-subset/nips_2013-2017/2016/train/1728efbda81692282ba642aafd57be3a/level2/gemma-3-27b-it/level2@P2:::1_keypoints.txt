- Introduces DMD in RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using richer nonlinear kernels rather than monomials.  
- Presents an algorithm using orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS followed by eigendecomposition.  
- Theoretical formulation appears sound, with Theorem 1 providing a solid foundation.  
- Adds a pragmatic robustness enhancement via POD projections to mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Well-motivated link to Koopman operator theory and clear connection to existing DMD methods.  
- Mathematical notation and exposition are dense; Section 3 is particularly hard to follow and needs clearer transitions.  
- Kernel choice and hyperparameter selection (e.g., Gaussian width) are crucial but receive insufficient discussion and sensitivity analysis.  
- POD dimensionality reduction lacks a clear criterion for selecting the number of retained modes.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: toy example is basic and Hénon map does not clearly outperform existing DMD variants.  
- Applications section (Section 7) proposes ideas but lacks detailed comparative analysis against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Figures are small and poorly labeled, hindering quantitative assessment of results.  
- Related work section is incomplete—other kernel-based dynamical methods and subspace identification links are underexplored.  
- Citation of [36] (kernel principal angles) seems out of place and only briefly mentioned.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS extension of DMD, solid theory, and practical POD improvement.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity, limited empirical validation, and inadequate discussion of practical considerations.  
- Overall assessment: weak reject; recommend substantial revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and deepen comparisons with existing methods.
