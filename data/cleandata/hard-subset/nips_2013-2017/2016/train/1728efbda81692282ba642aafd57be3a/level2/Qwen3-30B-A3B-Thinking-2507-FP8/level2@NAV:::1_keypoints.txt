- Proposes kernel-based extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with richer nonlinear features.  
- Main claim: method captures global nonlinear dynamics without needing the governing equations.  
- Algorithm: reduces to computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.  
- Provides two theorems linking the method formally to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Introduces a POD-based robustification to mitigate numerical instability in practice.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic systems (toy model, Hénon map) and real tasks (locomotion recognition, Lorenz change-point detection).  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Strength: meaningful novelty by extending DMD to kernel-based nonlinear features.  
- Strength: practical implementation considerations and effective robustification strategy.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations, especially regarding kernel choice and computational scalability.  
- Weakness: narrow scope of real-world applications presented.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to related kernel-based dynamical systems literature; connections to recent work could be strengthened.  
- Overall: significant contribution that advances Koopman spectral analysis, though practical impact needs broader empirical demonstration.
