- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS) to perform spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Central claim: using the kernel trick enables approximation of Koopman eigenfunctions with richer nonlinear basis functions than standard DMD.
- Builds on Koopman operator theory and DMD and connects these to established kernel methods.
- Presents a theoretically grounded algorithm derived from the Arnoldi method in RKHS and a numerically robust variant based on kernel PCA.
- Technically solid with clear theorems that justify the proposed method.
- Addresses a key limitation of standard DMD by moving beyond linear observables, representing an original and significant contribution.
- Demonstrates utility as a feature extraction tool with applications in state prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides adequate background for readers.
- Empirical evaluation is illustrative but needs more rigorous quantitative comparisons.
- Missing direct experimental comparison to extended DMD and other relevant baselines.
- Prediction and change-point detection results lack quantitative metrics against baselines.
- No discussion of kernel choice and hyperparameter selection (e.g., kernel width), affecting performance and reproducibility.
- Scalability concerns due to Gram matrix computations for very long time series are not addressed.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and relevant applications, but experimental validation and practical considerations need strengthening.
