- Proposes a novel RKHS-based extension of Koopman spectral analysis and DMD to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical derivations, theorems, and proofs supporting the method.  
- Addresses ill-conditioning by projecting data onto POD directions to robustify the algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks: prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.  
- Assumes strong background in RKHSs, Koopman operators, and DMD, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational cost, raising concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Does not offer comprehensive empirical comparisons with existing methods to establish superiority.  
- Original contribution: novel RKHS Koopman formulation and DMD extension enabling nonlinear eigenfunction approximation.  
- Significant potential impact across physics, engineering, and machine learning for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.  
- High-quality, well-organized, and clearly written manuscript overall.  
- Clarity is good but still mathematically demanding for readers without advanced background.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication (NIPS 2016) subject to minor revisions addressing noted concerns.
