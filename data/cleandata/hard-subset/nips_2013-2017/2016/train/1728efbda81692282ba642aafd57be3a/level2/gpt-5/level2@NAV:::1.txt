Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a kernelized dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) framework for Koopman spectral analysis. It formulates DMD in an RKHS by constructing a Krylov subspace of feature maps, projecting the Koopman operator onto this subspace via Gram–Schmidt QR, and extracting eigenvalues/eigenfunctions as “modes.” A numerically robust variant projects data onto kernel PCA directions (POD in feature space). The authors illustrate use as feature extractors for prediction via pre-image, dynamics recognition (via kernel principal angles), and sequential change-point detection. This is closely related to extended DMD (EDMD) and kernel EDMD (Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley), exact/optimized DMD (Tu et al., Chen et al., Jovanović et al.), and spectral learning methods; the manuscript acknowledges EDMD but the overlap with kernel EDMD/KDMD deserves a deeper comparison.

Claims and support: The main claims are (1) a theoretically grounded RKHS formulation of DMD with proofs connecting empirical Ritz spectra to Koopman spectra; (2) a robustified algorithm via kernel PCA truncation; (3) practical usefulness of the extracted features in ML tasks. The theoretical statements (Theorems 1–2) are clear and reasonable under strong assumptions (rich dictionaries, ψ=0, φj∈Φ, κj in span(U)). Empirical support is limited to toy systems and qualitative plots; there is no quantitative evaluation, ablation, or statistical significance, and baselines (exact DMD, EDMD/KDMD) are missing.

Quality: Technically sound at the level of linear-algebraic reductions; assumptions ensuring Koopman acts on H, conditions for ψ≈0, and sample/noise robustness are not fully discussed. Numerical stability is addressed via kernel PCA, but complexity, scaling, and hyperparameter selection (kernel, τ, truncation) are not analyzed.

Clarity: The paper is generally readable, though notation is heavy. Define all symbols on first use, tighten Section 4 derivations, and fix minor grammar. Providing pseudocode, complexity analysis, and an explicit algorithm box would improve reproducibility (code link is helpful).

Originality: Incremental. The RKHS/Krylov perspective is elegant, but conceptually very close to EDMD with kernels (KDMD). The novelty would be clearer with theoretical/empirical distinctions relative to KDMD.

Significance/usefulness: Potentially useful as a unifying view and practical tool, but current experiments are too limited to assess impact. The pre-image step is nontrivial and its accuracy and stability are not quantified.

Pros:
- Clear RKHS-Krylov formulation with proofs.
- Practical stabilization via kernel PCA.
- Code release; interesting application sketches.

Cons:
- Limited experimental validation; no baselines or metrics.
- Overlap with existing KDMD not convincingly disentangled.
- Missing analysis of noise, scalability, hyperparameters, and pre-image accuracy.

Suggestions: Add quantitative benchmarks against exact DMD, EDMD/KDMD on standard systems; evaluate noise sensitivity and kernel/truncation choices; analyze computational complexity; clarify assumptions for Koopman on H; expand multi-trajectory training remark; report prediction errors and pre-image diagnostics. Overall: weak reject, with encouragement to revise; the idea has merit but needs stronger empirical and comparative evidence.
