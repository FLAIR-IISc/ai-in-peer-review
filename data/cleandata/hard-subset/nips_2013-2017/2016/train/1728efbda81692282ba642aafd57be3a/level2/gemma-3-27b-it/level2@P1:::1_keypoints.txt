- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear kernels.  
- Core idea is sound and leverages kernel expressiveness to capture more complex dynamics than linear DMD.  
- Paper is well-structured, with clear background on Koopman theory and DMD before introducing the method.  
- Mathematical derivations and Theorems 1 and 2 appear rigorous and build logically on existing theory.  
- Presentation is generally clear and accessible given the mathematical complexity.  
- Empirical validation includes a toy system and the Henon map, demonstrating reasonable agreement with expected results.  
- Includes a POD-based robustification step to address practical ill-conditioning, adding practical value.  
- Comparisons are limited; lacks comprehensive evaluation against other nonlinear system identification and spectral methods (e.g., extended DMD, SINDy).  
- Kernel selection appears ad hoc (polynomial for toy, Gaussian for Henon) with no strategy or sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Computational complexity and scalability (kernel matrix operations, high-dimensional/long time-series behavior) are not discussed.  
- Applications section is cursory and lacks in-depth analysis or strong, transformative results.  
- Related work coverage could be improved, with stronger positioning relative to kernel PCA, SINDy, and the eigensystem realization method.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, well-motivated, clear, and initially validated empirically with practical robustification.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons, inadequate kernel selection discussion, missing computational complexity analysis, and weak application depth.  
- Recommended minor revisions: expand method comparisons, discuss and analyze kernel selection, provide computational complexity and scalability discussion, strengthen application analyses with baselines, and elaborate connection to eigensystem realization method.
