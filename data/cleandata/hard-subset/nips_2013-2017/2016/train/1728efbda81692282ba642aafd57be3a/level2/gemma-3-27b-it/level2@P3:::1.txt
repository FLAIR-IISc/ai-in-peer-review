This paper introduces a dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) method extending the standard approach to operate within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The core idea is to approximate the Koopman operator's eigenfunctions with kernel-based functions, offering a potentially richer representation than linear monomials used in traditional DMD. The authors present an algorithm based on calculating orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in the RKHS and performing eigendecomposition. They demonstrate the method on synthetic and real-world data, including applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

The paper is well-motivated, placing the work within the context of spectral analysis of dynamical systems and highlighting limitations of existing techniques like POD and standard DMD. The connection to the Koopman operator is clearly articulated. The mathematical formulation is generally sound, and the proposed algorithm logically extends DMD into the RKHS framework. The inclusion of applications demonstrates the potential utility of the method. The related work section covers relevant literature, including DMD variants and kernel methods.

However, several areas could be strengthened. While the theoretical derivation is present, the practical implications and potential challenges of implementing the algorithm are not fully explored. Specifically, the choice of kernel and its impact on performance aren’t thoroughly discussed. The empirical evaluation, while demonstrating feasibility, feels somewhat limited. The toy system is a good starting point, but the application to the Henon map lacks detail regarding parameter selection and a more rigorous comparison against other methods. The application sections – prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition – are presented briefly. More detailed results and comparative analysis against state-of-the-art techniques would significantly enhance the paper.

The robustness enhancement using POD bases (Section 4) is a valuable addition, acknowledging a potential ill-conditioning issue. However, the discussion around the truncation parameter ‘p’ and its impact on performance is missing. A sensitivity analysis regarding ‘p’ would be beneficial. The discussion of the pre-image problem for prediction (Section 7) is interesting, but the connection to existing methods for solving this problem isn't clearly established.  The notation could be made more consistent throughout. For example, using a consistent font for variables like ‘x’ and ‘φ’.

**Strengths:**

*   Novel extension of DMD using RKHS, offering potentially richer representations of dynamics.
*   Clear mathematical formulation and derivation of the algorithm.
*   Practical considerations addressed with the POD-based robustness enhancement.
*   Demonstration of potential applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Limited empirical evaluation and lack of comparative analysis.
*   Insufficient discussion of kernel selection and its impact.
*   Lack of sensitivity analysis for key parameters, such as the truncation parameter in the POD enhancement.
*   Brief presentation of application sections without detailed results.
*   Notation could be improved for consistency.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of DMD.
*   The work has the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in spectral analysis of dynamical systems.

**Arguments for rejection:**

*   The empirical evaluation is limited and doesn't fully demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method.
*   The practical considerations and challenges of implementation aren't sufficiently addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a promising paper with a solid technical foundation. However, the limited empirical evaluation and lack of detailed analysis raise concerns about the practical impact of the proposed method.  A major revision addressing the weaknesses outlined above, specifically a more comprehensive empirical validation and a deeper discussion of practical considerations, is recommended before acceptance. The inclusion of a comparison against existing state-of-the-art methods within each application section is crucial.
