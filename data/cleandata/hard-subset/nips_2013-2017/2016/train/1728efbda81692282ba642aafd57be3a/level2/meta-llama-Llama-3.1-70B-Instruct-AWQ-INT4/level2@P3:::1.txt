**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The Koopman operator is an infinite-dimensional linear operator that represents nonlinear and finite-dimensional dynamics without linearization. The authors extend the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm to work with RKHSs, allowing for the approximation of the Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions. The proposed algorithm is shown to be mathematically correct and is robustified by projecting data onto the principal orthogonal directions (POD) in the feature space. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through various applications, including prediction, sequential change-point detection, and recognition of dynamic patterns.
