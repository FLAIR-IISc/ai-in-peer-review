- Central idea: Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD) to perform Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS by implicitly using rich nonlinear basis functions via the kernel trick.

- Methodology: Presents a direct RKHS Gram–Schmidt formulation and a numerically robust variant using truncated kernel PCA.

- Theoretical grounding: Provides clear theorems linking constructed matrix eigenpairs to true Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions under reasonable assumptions.

- Practical algorithmic contribution: Identifies numerical instability of naive kDMD and offers a robust truncated-kPCA approach analogous to POD/SVD truncation.

- Versatility and applications: Demonstrates kDMD as a feature-extraction tool for prediction, dynamics recognition, embedding, and change-point detection.

- Novelty and significance: Elegantly combines DMD and kernel methods to move beyond linear DMD without manual basis engineering.

- Relation to eDMD: kDMD can be seen as an implicit-data-centered instance of eDMD; the connection is noted but not deeply explored.

- Comparison gap: Lacks empirical or detailed discussion contrasting implicit kernel dictionary versus explicit basis choices in eDMD.

- Computational complexity concern: Core operations involve τ×τ eigendecompositions with O(τ^3) cost, raising scalability issues for long time series.

- Scalability suggestions missing: Paper should discuss approximations (e.g., Nystrom, random features) or other ways to scale kDMD.

- Hyperparameter selection: Paper states kernels used but omits how kernel type/parameters were chosen; needs discussion of heuristics or cross-validation for reproducibility.

- Experimental rigor: Prediction experiments lack comparison to standard nonlinear models (e.g., GPs, LSTMs); dynamics recognition needs quantitative metrics beyond MDS visuals.

- Change-point detection: Derivation uses a heuristic simplification that requires justification or empirical validation.

- Overall assessment: High-quality, theoretically grounded, and practically useful contribution that is recommendable for acceptance despite addressable weaknesses.
