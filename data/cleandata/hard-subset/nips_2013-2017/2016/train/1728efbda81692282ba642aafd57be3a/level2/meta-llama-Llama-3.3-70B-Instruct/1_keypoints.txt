- Proposes a spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.  
- Claims the method yields a finite modal decomposition usable as a feature-extraction procedure.  
- Supports the claim with a theoretical framework and empirical examples on synthetic and real data.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and balances theory and experiments.  
- Provides a thorough review of related work and frames the method as an extension of DMD via RKHS-based Koopman eigenfunction approximation.  
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of the theoretical framework.  
- Strength: comprehensive related-work discussion including DMD and other spectral methods.  
- Strength: empirical examples demonstrate the method’s effectiveness on illustrative tasks.  
- Strength: discusses potential applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamic-pattern recognition).  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in functional analysis and operator theory, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited to a few simple systems; scalability to complex systems is unclear.  
- Weakness: lacks clear comparison with other spectral methods (e.g., DMD) regarding computational efficiency and accuracy.  
- Overall: significant contribution with practical potential, but accessibility and empirical/comparative evaluation need strengthening.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, clearly presented method with promising empirical illustrations and application potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope, mathematical accessibility issues, and missing computational/accuracy comparisons.
