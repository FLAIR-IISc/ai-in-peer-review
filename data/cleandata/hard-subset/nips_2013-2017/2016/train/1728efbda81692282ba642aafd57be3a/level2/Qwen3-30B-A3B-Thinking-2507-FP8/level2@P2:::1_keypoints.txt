- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to RKHS for Koopman spectral analysis using kernel-derived nonlinear basis functions and a POD-based robust variant.  
- Theoretical contribution is interesting and sound; Theorems 1 and 2 and the connection to Koopman spectral theory are well-established.  
- Applications (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) show potential utility of the approach.  
- Related work section is inadequate and fails to systematically compare with extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015), which also uses predefined basis functions.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient: synthetic examples are small-scale and do not demonstrate clear superiority over standard DMD.  
- Results (e.g., eigenvalue estimation) lack direct comparisons with standard or extended DMD on the same datasets.  
- Applications section lacks quantitative comparisons with baseline methods, making claimed improvements unverifiable.  
- Claim of providing a “richer approximation” of Koopman eigenfunctions is unsubstantiated; no concrete scenarios showing kernel superiority over linear methods.  
- The POD-based robustification is conceptually similar to existing DMD variants (e.g., Chen et al., 2012) and the paper does not clarify differences or improvements.  
- Empirical validation of the robust variant is lacking.  
- Paper needs comprehensive comparisons with standard DMD, extended DMD, and other relevant methods on more challenging datasets.  
- Requires quantitative metrics (prediction error, change-point detection accuracy) to demonstrate advantages.  
- Should identify specific scenarios where the kernel approach outperforms linear methods and discuss computational complexity and scalability.  
- Overall verdict: promising theoretical work but insufficient empirical evidence; recommend rejection pending substantial experimental and comparative revisions.
