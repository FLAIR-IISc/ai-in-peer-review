Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a kernelized variant of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis. By lifting observables into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), the method computes Ritz values and vectors from a Krylov subspace in feature space. The core algorithm is expressed via Gram matrices using Gram–Schmidt QR in RKHS; a “robustified” variant projects data onto kernel PCA/POD directions to mitigate ill-conditioning, analogous to truncated SVD/POD variants used for standard DMD. Theoretical results (Theorem 1) show that the RKHS Ritz vectors reconstruct the lifted observables along a trajectory; Theorem 2 connects the kernel-POD-projected operator to Koopman eigenstructure under assumptions that the Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the span of the kernel principal components. The paper further explores applications: short-term prediction via feature-space time extrapolation followed by a pre-image step, recognition/embedding of dynamical patterns via subspace angles between estimated Koopman-invariant subspaces, and sequential change-point detection using a kernel exponential-family model tied to the Koopman operator.

This work is closely related to extended DMD (EDMD) [35], which approximates Koopman operators using a dictionary of basis functions. Kernel EDMD replaces finite dictionaries with implicit infinite dictionaries via kernels, enabling computations entirely through Gram matrices; the robustified variant here appears particularly close to kernel EDMD and to numerically stable DMD variants (exact/optimized DMD [33, 8]). The paper acknowledges [35] and positions the robust version as similar, but the distinctions and advantages over kernel EDMD are not fully delineated.

Strengths:
- Clear formulation of Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS with an explicit Krylov/Arnoldi perspective and a constructive algorithm using only kernel evaluations.
- Practical attention to numerical stability via kernel PCA/POD projection and truncation.
- Theorems that link the computed eigenpairs to Koopman objects and provide reconstruction formulas within the feature space.
- Broad view of potential applications, beyond fluid dynamics, including prediction, change-point detection, and motion recognition; code availability is noted.

Weaknesses and questions:
- Novelty over EDMD/kernel EDMD remains insufficiently clarified. The robustified algorithm seems equivalent or very close to kernel EDMD (estimating a finite-dimensional Galerkin/least-squares approximation in the span of kernel principal components). A formal comparison, including derivations showing when the proposed operators coincide with EDMD/kernel EDMD and when they differ, would help establish the contribution.
- Empirical evaluation is limited, largely qualitative, and lacks baselines. There are no systematic comparisons to (kernel) EDMD, exact/optimized DMD, or sparsity-promoting DMD. Quantitative metrics (e.g., eigenvalue/mode error on systems with known spectra, reconstruction/prediction error, classification accuracy, change-point detection delay/false-alarm rates) are largely absent.
- Some claims could be refined for correctness. The statement that “for a system evolving on an attractor, the Koopman eigenvalues always lie on a unit circle” generally requires measure-preserving dynamics; for dissipative systems, eigenvalues typically lie inside the unit disk.
- The pre-image procedure is sketched and relies on an MDS-style approach; algorithmic details, complexity, and quantitative accuracy are missing. The pre-image problem is known to be ill-posed; empirical robustness and comparisons to established pre-image methods (e.g., [18]) would be informative.
- Application sections present interesting directions but without rigorous evaluation or ablation: kernel choices, truncation level p, sensitivity to noise, and data requirements. Figures appear limited (e.g., references to Fig. 2 but not shown), and some labels/axes are unclear.
- Theoretical assumptions in Theorem 2 (eigenfunctions lying in the span of kernel PCs) are strong; discussion of when these hold (e.g., richness of data, universality of kernels) and any consistency guarantees would strengthen the theory.

Quality:
The algorithms are plausible and the linear-algebraic derivations are sound in spirit. However, numerical guidance (conditioning, regularization, pivoting, SVD-based implementations) should be detailed, and the empirical validation is too thin to support strong claims, particularly for the applications (prediction and change detection). The change-point detection derivation simplifies the likelihood ratio by dropping terms without theoretical justification or empirical validation.

Clarity:
The exposition is generally clear and well-structured, with algorithm steps explicitly listed. Some notational and grammatical issues remain, and a few figure references appear inconsistent. A careful editing pass and more explicit descriptions of implementation details (e.g., kernel choices, parameter tuning, truncation criteria) would help.

Originality:
Framing DMD in RKHS and operating via Gram matrices is a natural extension that has been explored under EDMD/kernel EDMD. The incremental contribution here seems to be an Arnoldi/Krylov perspective with kernel Gram–Schmidt and a particular POD-based stabilization. Without a direct comparison and precise positioning relative to kernel EDMD, the originality is unclear.

Significance:
If convincingly differentiated and empirically validated against strong baselines, a kernelized, stable Koopman spectral method could impact data-driven dynamical systems modeling in ML. In its present form, the empirical evidence and positioning are insufficient for broad NeurIPS impact.

Suggestions for improvement (prioritized):
- Essential: Provide a rigorous comparison to EDMD/kernel EDMD (derivations and experiments). Clarify equivalences/differences, computational complexity, and practical advantages (e.g., stability, accuracy, sample efficiency).
- Essential: Add quantitative experiments with ground-truth spectra (toy systems, linear systems) measuring eigenvalue/mode errors; include robustness to noise and parameter sensitivity; compare to exact/optimized DMD and kernel EDMD.
- Essential: For applications, report standard metrics and baselines: prediction RMSE vs EDMD and GP-DM; change-point detection ROC/delay vs kernel CUSUM/other CPD methods; classification accuracy for motion recognition vs reasonable baselines.
- Important: Refine theoretical statements (unit-circle eigenvalues), and discuss conditions under which Theorem 2 holds; provide any consistency or approximation-error bounds if possible.
- Important: Detail the pre-image algorithm, its computational cost, and empirical performance; compare to alternative pre-image techniques.
- Nice-to-have: Provide guidance for kernel/parameter selection, truncation criteria, and include ablations; release code in the supplementary for reproducibility.

Pros:
- Clear RKHS-based algorithmic framework for Koopman spectral analysis.
- Attention to numerical stability via kernel PCA/POD.
- Broad set of potential applications and code availability.

Cons:
- Unclear novelty over kernel EDMD; missing theoretical and empirical differentiation.
- Limited quantitative evaluation and lack of comparisons to established baselines.
- Some claims and application derivations need refinement and validation.

Overall assessment:
Promising direction with solid mathematical framing, but current contribution appears incremental relative to kernel EDMD, and the empirical validation is insufficient for NeurIPS. Strengthening the positioning and adding rigorous comparative experiments would substantially improve the work.
