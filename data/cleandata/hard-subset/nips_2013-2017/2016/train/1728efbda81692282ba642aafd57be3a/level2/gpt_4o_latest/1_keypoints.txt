- Contribution: Proposes a novel kernel-based Koopman spectral analysis in RKHS with a modal decomposition algorithm for finite-length nonlinear time series.  
- Strength: Extends DMD by incorporating nonlinear kernel basis functions, improving Koopman eigenfunction approximation.  
- Strength: Provides rigorous theoretical development with proofs and connections to existing methods.  
- Strength: Robustifies the algorithm using POD bases to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Strength: Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating utility for prediction, change-point detection, and pattern recognition.  
- Weakness: Paper is densely written and assumes strong background in Koopman theory, RKHS, and DMD, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed quantitative comparisons against existing DMD variants and other spectral methods.  
- Weakness: Scalability and computational complexity in high-dimensional RKHS are not explicitly addressed; kernel selection and parameter tuning underexplored.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of other limitations such as sensitivity to noise and dependence on kernel choice.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on improving accessibility and expanding experimental comparisons.  
- Pro: Innovative RKHS extension, rigorous theory, and broad applicability to ML and scientific problems.  
- Con: Dense presentation, limited comparative analysis, and inadequate treatment of computational/practical challenges.
