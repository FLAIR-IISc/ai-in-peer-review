## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper proposes a dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) method leveraging reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to perform Koopman spectral analysis on nonlinear dynamical systems. The core idea is to extend traditional DMD, which relies on linear monomials, to utilize the richer function space provided by kernels, potentially capturing more complex dynamics. The authors present an algorithm based on calculating orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performing eigendecomposition. They then demonstrate the method on synthetic and real-world data, showcasing applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.

The paper builds logically on prior work in spectral analysis of dynamical systems (Koopman operator theory, DMD, POD) and correctly identifies the limitations of traditional DMD in handling highly nonlinear systems. The proposed RKHS-DMD is a natural extension, and the theoretical foundation, particularly Theorem 1, appears sound. The inclusion of a robustification step via POD projection, addressing potential ill-conditioning, is a valuable addition. The empirical results on the toy system and Henon map are promising, demonstrating accurate eigenvalue estimation.  The applications section provides a good overview of potential uses for the extracted features.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of thorough comparison with existing kernel-based DMD variants (e.g., Extended DMD) and other nonlinear system identification techniques. While related works are mentioned, the discussion lacks depth regarding the specific advantages of this approach over them. The empirical evaluation, while illustrative, feels somewhat limited. The application sections would be strengthened by quantitative evaluation (e.g., prediction error metrics, classification accuracy) and a comparison with baseline methods.  Furthermore, the presentation could be improved by more clearly explaining the practical considerations of kernel selection and parameter tuning.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of DMD using RKHS provides a potentially more powerful tool for analyzing nonlinear dynamics.
*   Sound theoretical foundation with clear mathematical formulation.
*   Addresses practical issues of ill-conditioning with the POD projection.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparative evaluation against related kernel-based methods.
*   Insufficiently detailed empirical validation with quantitative metrics.
*   Lack of discussion on practical challenges of kernel selection.

**Overall:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of nonlinear dynamical systems analysis. While the current presentation lacks a thorough comparative evaluation and detailed empirical validation, the core idea is sound and the theoretical foundation is strong. I recommend acceptance with **minor revisions**, specifically focusing on strengthening the empirical evaluation and providing a more comprehensive discussion of related work. The authors should also clarify the practical considerations surrounding kernel selection.
