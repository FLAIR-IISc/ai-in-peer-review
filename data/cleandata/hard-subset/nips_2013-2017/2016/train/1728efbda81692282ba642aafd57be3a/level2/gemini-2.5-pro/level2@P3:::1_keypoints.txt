- Proposes a kernelized DMD in RKHS to perform spectral analysis of the Koopman operator from time-series data.  
- Algorithm builds an orthogonal basis for a Krylov subspace in feature space and eigendecomposes the projected operator.  
- Introduces a robust variant using kernel PCA for regularization to address numerical instability.  
- Theorem 1 generalizes the DMD decomposition to the feature space; Theorem 2 links KPCA regularization to Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Demonstrates utility of Koopman modes as features for state prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Experiments on synthetic (toy system, Hénon map) and real (motion capture) data support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with consistent notation, helpful algorithm summaries, and informative figures.  
- Kernelizing DMD is an original and significant contribution, combining Koopman theory with kernel methods.  
- Could better clarify the relationship and advantages relative to Extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015).  
- Lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability (Gram matrix is τ×τ); mention of approximations like Nyström would help.  
- Needs more on kernel/parameter selection and sensitivity analysis to improve reproducibility.  
- Should acknowledge the difficulty of the pre-image problem used in prediction and its potential limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, practically robust, strong empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to Extended DMD and missing practical details on scaling and hyperparameter choice.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality contribution whose strengths outweigh minor weaknesses that can be addressed in revision.
