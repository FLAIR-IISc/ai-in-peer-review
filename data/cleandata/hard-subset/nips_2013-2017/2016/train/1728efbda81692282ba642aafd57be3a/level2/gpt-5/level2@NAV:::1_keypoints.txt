- Proposal: Kernelized DMD in an RKHS using a Krylov subspace of feature maps, Gram–Schmidt QR projection, and extraction of eigenvalues/eigenfunctions; robust variant projects onto kernel PCA directions.  
- Applications illustrated: feature extraction for prediction via pre-image, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection.  
- Relation to prior work: Closely related to EDMD, kernel EDMD/KDMD, exact/optimized DMD, and spectral learning methods; overlap with KDMD needs deeper comparison.  
- Main claims: (1) RKHS formulation with proofs linking empirical Ritz spectra to Koopman spectra, (2) robustified algorithm via kernel PCA truncation, (3) practical usefulness of extracted features.  
- Theoretical support: Theorems are clear and reasonable but rely on strong assumptions (rich dictionaries, ψ=0, φj∈Φ, κj in span(U)).  
- Empirical support: Limited to toy systems and qualitative plots; lacks quantitative evaluation, ablations, statistical significance, and comparisons to baselines.  
- Technical quality: Linear-algebraic reductions are sound, but assumptions for Koopman acting on H, conditions for ψ≈0, and sample/noise robustness are not fully discussed.  
- Numerical stability/scaling: Kernel PCA addresses stability, but complexity, scaling, and hyperparameter selection (kernel, τ, truncation) are unanalyzed.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: Generally readable but heavy notation; needs symbol definitions, tightened derivations, pseudocode/algorithm box, and complexity analysis despite code release.  
- Originality: Incremental contribution—elegant RKHS/Krylov perspective but conceptually very close to KDMD; novelty not convincingly disentangled.  
- Significance: Potentially useful as a unifying view and tool, but limited experiments prevent assessment of impact; pre-image accuracy and stability are unquantified.  
- Pros: Clear RKHS-Krylov formulation with proofs, practical stabilization via kernel PCA, code release and interesting application sketches.  
- Cons: Limited experimental validation with no baselines/metrics, unclear separation from KDMD, and missing analyses of noise, scalability, hyperparameters, and pre-image accuracy.  
- Suggestions: Add quantitative benchmarks versus exact DMD/EDMD/KDMD, evaluate noise sensitivity and kernel/truncation choices, analyze computational complexity, clarify Koopman-on-H assumptions, expand multi-trajectory training discussion, and report prediction/pre-image errors.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject with encouragement to revise—idea has merit but requires stronger empirical and comparative evidence.
