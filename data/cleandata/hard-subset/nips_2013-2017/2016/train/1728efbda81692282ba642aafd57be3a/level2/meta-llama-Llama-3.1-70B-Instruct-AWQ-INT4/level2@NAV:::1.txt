This paper proposes a novel approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors extend the traditional dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) method to work with nonlinear basis functions, enabling the extraction of more accurate and informative features from nonlinear systems.

**Claims:**

1. The proposed method, called kernel DMD, can accurately approximate the Koopman eigenfunctions using nonlinear basis functions.
2. The method can be used for feature extraction and analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.

**Support for Claims:**

The authors provide a thorough mathematical derivation of the kernel DMD method, including the extension of DMD to RKHSs and the use of the Gram-Schmidt QR decomposition. They also provide empirical examples on synthetic and real-world data, demonstrating the effectiveness of the method in estimating Koopman eigenvalues and modes.

**Usefulness:**

The proposed method has several potential applications in machine learning, physics, and engineering, including:

1. Feature extraction and analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems.
2. Prediction and forecasting of nonlinear systems.
3. Sequential change-point detection in nonlinear systems.

**Field Knowledge:**

The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature in spectral analysis, DMD, and kernel methods. They also provide a clear and concise explanation of the underlying mathematical concepts.

**Originality:**

The proposed method is a novel extension of traditional DMD to work with nonlinear basis functions in RKHSs. While DMD has been widely used in physics and engineering, the use of kernel methods to extend DMD is a new contribution.

**Completeness:**

The paper provides a thorough mathematical derivation of the method, as well as empirical examples and applications. However, some details on the implementation and computational complexity of the method could be further clarified.

**Limitations:**

The authors acknowledge some limitations of the method, including the potential for ill-conditioned algorithms and the need for careful selection of kernel functions and parameters.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

Pro:

* The method provides a novel and effective way to analyze nonlinear dynamical systems.
* The empirical examples demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in estimating Koopman eigenvalues and modes.
* The method has several potential applications in machine learning, physics, and engineering.

Con:

* The method may require careful selection of kernel functions and parameters.
* The computational complexity of the method could be high for large datasets.
* The method may not be suitable for all types of nonlinear systems.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs. While some limitations and potential areas for improvement are noted, the method has several potential applications and is a valuable contribution to the field.
