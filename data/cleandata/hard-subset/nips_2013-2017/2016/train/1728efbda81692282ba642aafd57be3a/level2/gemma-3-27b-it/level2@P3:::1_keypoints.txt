- Introduces a kernelized DMD method in RKHS to approximate Koopman operator eigenfunctions.  
- Core algorithm computes orthogonal Krylov bases in RKHS followed by eigendecomposition.  
- Demonstrated on synthetic and real-world data for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Well-motivated with a clear connection to Koopman spectral analysis and limits of POD/standard DMD.  
- Mathematical formulation is generally sound and logically extends DMD into RKHS.  
- Related work section adequately covers DMD variants and kernel methods.  
- Practical implementation issues and the impact of kernel choice are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; toy example is fine but Henon map lacks parameter details and rigor.  
- Application sections (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are presented too briefly without detailed results.  
- POD-based robustness enhancement is a valuable addition addressing ill-conditioning concerns.  
- Missing sensitivity analysis for the POD truncation parameter ‘p’ and its performance impact.  
- Pre-image problem for prediction is noted but not well connected to existing solution methods.  
- Notation is inconsistent in places (e.g., fonts for x and φ) and should be clarified.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound extension with potential to advance the field.  
- Against acceptance: limited empirical validation and insufficient attention to practical challenges; major revision recommended.
