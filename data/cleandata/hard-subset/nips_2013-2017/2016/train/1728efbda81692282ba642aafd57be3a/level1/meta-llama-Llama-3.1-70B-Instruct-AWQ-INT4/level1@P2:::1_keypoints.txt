- Proposes a novel extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Algorithm enables computation of Koopman eigenfunctions in RKHSs, improving flexibility and approximation power.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains theoretical background and algorithmic details.  
- Thorough related-work review situates the contribution relative to DMD and other spectral techniques.  
- Empirical examples convincingly demonstrate effectiveness and potential for real-world applications.  
- Well-organized and clearly structured presentation aids readability.  
- Assumes strong background in functional analysis and operator theory, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity, raising concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Does not include comprehensive empirical or theoretical comparisons with alternative spectral analysis methods.  
- Originality: significant and non-trivial extension of DMD to RKHSs with a new algorithm for Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Significance: potential impact across physics, engineering, and machine learning for analyzing complex nonlinear systems.  
- Clarity: theoretical exposition and examples are clear and well-illustrated.  
- Quality: method is well-motivated, rigorously presented, and empirically validated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, clear exposition, and strong empirical demonstrations.  
- Arguments for rejection: accessibility issues, missing computational-complexity analysis, and insufficient comparative evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance subject to addressing computational complexity and comparative evaluation concerns.
