- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) to perform Koopman spectral analysis with nonlinear basis functions.  
- Well-motivated extension addressing limitations of traditional DMD's local linearizations.  
- Provides theoretical justification with rigorous derivations (Theorems 1 and 2) linking the method to DMD principles.  
- Empirical demonstrations on synthetic and real-world data show applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.  
- Presentation is clear, math is approachable, and a code repository is provided.  
- Incorporates POD-based robustification to mitigate ill-conditioning.  
- Novelty: RKHS-DMD potentially improves analysis of highly nonlinear systems and expands Koopman applicability.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivations appear rigorous and well-grounded.  
- Practical relevance: demonstrated utility across multiple applications strengthens impact.  
- Related work coverage is reasonably comprehensive.  
- Computational cost: paper lacks thorough discussion of kernel method complexity and scalability to high-dimensional/large datasets.  
- Kernel selection: choice of kernels and parameter settings (widths, degrees) is insufficiently justified or systematically explored.  
- Limited comparative analysis: insufficient benchmarking against other nonlinear system ID methods (e.g., neural nets, Gaussian processes).  
- Empirical validation: examples are relatively simple; need tests on more challenging, high-dimensional datasets.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: no analysis or guidance on tuning kernel and POD truncation parameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension with clear presentation and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments for rejection: unresolved concerns about scalability, limited comparisons, insufficient empirical validation, and missing hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall assessment: worthwhile contribution with solid theory but needs more work on computation, comparisons, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational complexity, broader comparative experiments, harder datasets, hyperparameter sensitivity, and kernel parameter justification.
