- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis using kernel functions.
- Core algorithm: modal decomposition computing orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection.
- Demonstrates applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using extracted features.
- Theoretical development is rigorous with well-constructed Theorems 1 and 2 supporting the approach.
- Addresses numerical instability by incorporating POD-based robustification.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world data support theoretical claims but are limited in scope.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; some sections (esp. Section 4) assume advanced RKHS knowledge and may be hard for non-experts.
- Originality: novel combination of DMD and kernel methods, differing from Extended DMD by using kernel-induced features rather than pre-set bases.
- Potential significance for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems across domains, but claimed advantages are not fully demonstrated.
- Limited empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Extended DMD and other kernel approaches).
- Insufficient discussion and evaluation of computational complexity and scalability.
- Experiments are sparse: only two synthetic systems and one real-world application.
- Paper does not clearly establish specific cases where the kernel approach outperforms linear-basis DMD.
- Strengths: rigorous theory, practical robustification, well-motivated applications, clear connection to Koopman analysis.
- Weaknesses: weak empirical baselines, missing scalability analysis, limited experimental breadth, unclear practical advantages.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound, novel extension, practical robustification, relevant applications, well-situated in literature.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate empirical validation, unaddressed complexity/scalability, unclear superiority over existing methods.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on stronger empirical validation and clearer demonstration of computational/practical advantages.
