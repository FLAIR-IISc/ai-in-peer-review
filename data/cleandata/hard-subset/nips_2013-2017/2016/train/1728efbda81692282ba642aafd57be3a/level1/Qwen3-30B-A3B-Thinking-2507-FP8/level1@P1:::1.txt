

# Peer Review: Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis

This paper proposes an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems. The authors build upon the Koopman operator framework, which provides a linear representation of nonlinear dynamics, and extend DMD (a popular method for approximating Koopman eigenvalues and modes) to work with kernel functions rather than linear basis functions. The core contribution is a modal decomposition algorithm that computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and performs eigendecomposition of the Koopman operator projection. The paper further demonstrates applications in prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition using the extracted features.

The work relates to several NIPS papers on Koopman operator analysis and DMD, including Williams et al. (2015) "A data-driven approximation of the Koopman operator" and Tu et al. (2014) "On dynamic mode decomposition: Theory and applications." It also connects to kernel methods in machine learning, particularly the use of RKHS for nonlinear feature extraction. The paper correctly cites related work on Extended DMD, which uses pre-determined basis functions, and distinguishes their approach by leveraging the expressiveness of kernel functions to provide richer nonlinear approximations.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound extension of DMD to RKHS. The theoretical development is rigorous, with well-constructed theorems (Theorems 1 and 2) establishing the mathematical foundation. The authors properly address the practical issue of numerical instability by incorporating POD for robustification, which is a well-known technique in DMD literature (as referenced in the paper). The experimental results on synthetic and real-world data support the theoretical claims, though the empirical evaluation could be strengthened with more comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical derivations are presented systematically, and the connection between the proposed method and DMD is well-explained. The application examples (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are well-motivated and described with sufficient detail. However, some sections (particularly in Section 4) assume familiarity with advanced concepts in RKHS and kernel methods, which may make the paper challenging for readers outside this specific domain.

**Originality:** The core contribution is the extension of DMD to RKHS, which represents a novel combination of Koopman spectral analysis and kernel methods. While Extended DMD (Williams et al., 2015) also uses basis functions, the authors' approach differs in that it uses kernel functions to create nonlinear basis functions directly in the feature space, rather than working with pre-determined basis functions. This is a meaningful extension that addresses a limitation of traditional DMD (which uses linear monomials as basis functions, limiting its expressiveness). The paper properly situates this work within the NIPS literature, showing how it differs from previous approaches.

**Significance:** The method could be valuable for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems across various scientific fields, particularly where traditional DMD might fail due to limited basis expressiveness. The applications demonstrated (prediction, change-point detection, dynamics recognition) are relevant and show practical utility. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently demonstrate why the kernel DMD approach is superior to existing methods for specific applications. The comparison to Extended DMD is limited, and the paper doesn't clearly establish the computational advantages or scalability of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**
- Mathematically rigorous extension of DMD to RKHS with proper theoretical grounding
- Practical robustification using POD to handle numerical instability
- Well-motivated applications demonstrating the utility of the extracted features
- Clear connection between the proposed method and Koopman spectral analysis

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited empirical comparison to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Extended DMD, other kernel-based approaches)
- Insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability
- The synthetic examples are limited to two systems, with only one real-world application
- Doesn't clearly establish the advantages of the kernel approach over linear basis functions for specific problems

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Provides a theoretically sound extension of DMD to RKHS that addresses a meaningful limitation of traditional DMD
- Offers a practical robustification technique for real-world applications
- Demonstrates several relevant applications of the extracted features
- Well-written and properly situates the work within the NIPS literature

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- The empirical evaluation doesn't sufficiently demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method
- The computational complexity and scalability of the method are not addressed
- The paper doesn't clearly establish why this approach is better than existing methods for specific applications
- The connection to practical machine learning applications could be strengthened

Overall, this is a technically sound paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of Koopman spectral analysis. While the theoretical extension is novel and well-executed, the empirical evaluation could be strengthened to better demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach. The paper would benefit from more comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and a clearer explanation of the practical advantages of the kernel approach. 

The method has potential for significant impact in fields where nonlinear dynamical systems analysis is important, but the current empirical results don't fully establish this potential. With stronger empirical validation and clearer demonstration of advantages over existing methods, this paper could make a substantial contribution to NIPS. As it stands, it's a solid technical contribution that advances the state of the art in Koopman spectral analysis, but the significance could be better established.

Recommendation: Weak Accept. The paper presents a theoretically sound and novel extension of DMD to RKHS that addresses a meaningful limitation of existing methods. However, the empirical evaluation could be strengthened to better demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach over existing methods, particularly in terms of practical performance and computational efficiency. With minor revisions addressing these points, this paper would be a strong contribution to NIPS.
