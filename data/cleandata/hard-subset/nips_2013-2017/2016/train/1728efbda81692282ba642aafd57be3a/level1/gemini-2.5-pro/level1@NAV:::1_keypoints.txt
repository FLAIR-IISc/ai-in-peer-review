- Proposes Kernel DMD (kDMD): extends Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS using the kernel trick for Koopman spectral analysis.
- Core motivation: standard DMD is limited by linear observables; kDMD implicitly uses richer nonlinear basis functions in feature space.
- Derives a principled algorithm based on the Krylov subspace in RKHS.
- Proposes a robust variant that projects data onto kernel principal components to address ill-conditioning.
- Positions work within existing DMD and kernel-based literature, relating to Extended DMD and kernel subspace methods.
- Strength: original and significant synthesis of Koopman operator theory with kernel methods.
- Strength: strong theoretical development with clear theorems and a practically robust algorithm.
- Strength: clearly written and demonstrates kDMD as a feature extraction tool for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Main weakness: empirical validation is mostly illustrative and lacks rigorous quantitative comparisons to strong baselines.
- Missing empirical comparisons: direct benchmarks against Extended DMD and standard nonlinear time-series models.
- Recommendation: accept—technical and conceptual contributions outweigh weaknesses, but final version should include stronger baseline comparisons.
