- Proposes kernelized DMD in RKHS using Krylov subspaces; computes orthogonal RKHS bases from kernel Gram matrices to form a projected Koopman operator and extract Ritz values/vectors.  
- Introduces a “robustified” variant that projects onto kernel PCA/POD directions to address ill-conditioning.  
- Provides two theorems linking the estimates to Koopman spectra, using standard kernel machinery.  
- Algorithmic derivations are sound and consistent with Arnoldi/DMD theory.  
- Theorem 2 relies on strong assumptions (eigenfunctions lie in span of kernel PCs) with no practical validation.  
- No finite-sample perturbation analysis, consistency proof, or noise-robustness guarantees are given.  
- Empirics are illustrative (toy system, Hénon) but small-scale and not rigorous.  
- Experiments lack quantitative baselines, metrics, and ablations versus EDMD/kernel EDMD or optimized/exact DMD.  
- Notation is occasionally dense (e.g., Mτ, Qτ, F̂) and clarity could be improved.  
- Complexity and storage costs for Gram matrix operations are not discussed.  
- A consolidated algorithm box and guidance on kernel/regularization choices would improve usability.  
- Originality is limited: approach closely parallels EDMD/kernel EDMD; main novelties are Arnoldi/QR framing and POD/kernel PCA robustification.  
- Overlap with prior KDMD should be acknowledged and distinctions clarified.  
- Significance is credible for nonlinear dynamics ML, but impact is limited without stronger theoretical and empirical support.  
- Pros: clear derivation connected to Koopman theory, practical robustification, useful application sketches, and released code.  
- Cons: limited novelty relative to EDMD, no quantitative evaluations or baselines, lacks finite-sample/error analysis and scalability/kernel-sensitivity discussion.
