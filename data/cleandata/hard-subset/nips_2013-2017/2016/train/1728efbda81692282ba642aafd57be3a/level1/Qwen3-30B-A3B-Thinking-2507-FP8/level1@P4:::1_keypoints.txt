- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for more expressive Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.
- Provides rigorous theoretical grounding with Theorems 1 and 2 justifying the modal decomposition algorithm.
- Uses kernel functions to generate nonlinear basis functions that generalize standard (linear/monomial) DMD.
- Introduces a robustification step via kernel PCA (POD) to address numerical instability and make the method practical.
- Empirically validated on synthetic systems (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map) and real-world data (locomotion, change-point detection).
- Demonstrates applications for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition with clear, well-structured examples and figures.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear mathematical derivations and reproducible details for experts.
- Novel contribution relative to standard DMD by operating directly in functional RKHS rather than using pre-determined basis functions.
- Shows practical utility and potential impact for practitioners in physics, engineering, and machine learning.
- Limited empirical comparison with the most similar prior work (Williams et al.'s extended DMD).
- Insufficient discussion and justification for kernel selection choices (e.g., polynomial vs. Gaussian).
- Limited analysis of computational complexity and scalability relative to standard DMD.
- The robustification via POD is conceptually similar to existing DMD techniques, reducing novelty in that component.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel kernel-based extension, strong theory, clear link to applications, and convincing empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate comparison with key prior work, limited novelty in robustification, and sparse computational discussion.
