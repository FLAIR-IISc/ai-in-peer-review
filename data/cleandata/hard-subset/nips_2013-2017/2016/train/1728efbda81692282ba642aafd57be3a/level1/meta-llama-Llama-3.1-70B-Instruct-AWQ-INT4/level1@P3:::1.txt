**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHSs). The authors extend the dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) algorithm to work with nonlinear basis functions, allowing for a more accurate representation of the system's dynamics. The proposed algorithm, called kernel DMD, uses a set of orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator onto the subspace spanned by the bases. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using synthetic and real-world data and discuss potential applications in machine learning, such as state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel extension of DMD**: The authors propose a new approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, extending the DMD algorithm to work with nonlinear basis functions.
2. **Theoretical foundations**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed algorithm, including a clear explanation of the Koopman operator and its properties.
3. **Empirical evaluation**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using synthetic and real-world data, including a toy nonlinear system, the Hénon map, and locomotion data.
4. **Potential applications**: The paper discusses potential applications of the proposed algorithm in machine learning, such as state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The proposed algorithm may be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets, due to the need to compute the Gram-Schmidt QR decomposition and the eigendecomposition of the projection of the Koopman operator.
2. **Robustness to noise**: The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the robustness of their algorithm to noise in the data, which may be a concern in practice.
3. **Comparison to existing methods**: While the authors compare their approach to existing methods, such as DMD and POD, a more thorough comparison to other spectral analysis methods would be beneficial.
4. **Limitations of the Koopman operator**: The authors acknowledge that the Koopman operator may not be applicable to all nonlinear dynamical systems, and a more thorough discussion of its limitations would be helpful.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems, extending the DMD algorithm to work with nonlinear basis functions. While the idea of using kernel methods for spectral analysis is not new, the specific application to the Koopman operator and the development of the kernel DMD algorithm are original contributions.

**Significance**

The paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of nonlinear dynamical systems, particularly in the analysis of complex systems where traditional methods may fail. The proposed algorithm may also have applications in machine learning, such as state prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clear, with a logical structure and concise explanations of the theoretical foundations and empirical evaluation. However, some of the mathematical derivations may be challenging for non-experts to follow, and additional explanations or examples may be helpful.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, with a clear explanation of the theoretical foundations and empirical evaluation. However, some of the limitations of the proposed algorithm, such as its computational complexity and robustness to noise, could be more thoroughly addressed.
