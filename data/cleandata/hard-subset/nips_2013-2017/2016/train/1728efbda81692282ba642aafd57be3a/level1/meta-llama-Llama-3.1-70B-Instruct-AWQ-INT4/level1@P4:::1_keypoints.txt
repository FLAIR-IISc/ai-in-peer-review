- Proposes a novel spectral analysis method for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.  
- Extends DMD to reproducing kernels for more flexible and accurate dynamic representations.  
- Uses Gram–Schmidt QR in feature space to build orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and projects the Koopman operator for eigendecomposition.  
- Introduces a robustified variant using POD bases to mitigate numerical issues.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction and well-explained technical development.  
- Reproducing kernels are a significant contribution, enabling richer representations than traditional DMD.  
- Empirical examples (toy system, Hénon map) show accurate Koopman eigenvalue/mode estimation.  
- Demonstrates applications in prediction, sequential change detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.  
- Lacks a detailed empirical comparison with traditional DMD and other spectral methods.  
- Insufficient discussion and analysis of the computational complexity despite Gram-matrix reduction.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear notation, diagrams, and consistent presentation.  
- Original and significant direction with potential broad impact across physics and engineering.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RKHS approach, solid technical presentation, and convincing empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to prior work, weak complexity analysis, and potentially narrow audience interest.
