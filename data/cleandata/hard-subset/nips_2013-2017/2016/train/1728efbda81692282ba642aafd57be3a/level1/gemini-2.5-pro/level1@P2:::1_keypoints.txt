- Proposes kernelized Dynamic Mode Decomposition (k-DMD) that lifts dynamics into an RKHS to approximate the Koopman operator for nonlinear systems.
- Presents two algorithms: a direct RKHS Gram–Schmidt extension (theoretically sound) and a numerically robust variant using kernel PCA for dimensionality reduction.
- Theorem 1 justifies the direct k-DMD extension; Theorem 2 justifies the robust, reduced-rank algorithm under reasonable approximation assumptions.
- Identifies numerical instability in the direct approach and remedies it via projection onto principal components (analogous to POD/SVD regularization in standard DMD).
- Demonstrates utility of Koopman eigenvalues/eigenfunctions as features for short-term prediction, dynamic pattern recognition, and sequential change-point detection.
- Experimental validation includes toy examples and the Hénon map showing recovery of known eigenvalues, plus application case studies.
- Evaluation is primarily qualitative; predictive and detection tasks lack quantitative comparisons (e.g., RMSE) against state-of-the-art methods.
- Change-point detection uses a simplified CUSUM score but its justification and quantitative validation are brief.
- Theoretical development and derivations are clear, well-organized, and accessible to readers familiar with dynamical systems or kernel methods.
- Code is provided, supporting reproducibility.
- The kernel approach is novel and elegant, implicitly using an infinite-dimensional dictionary versus explicit dictionaries in extended DMD.
- Lacks in-depth discussion comparing trade-offs between kernel-based implicit dictionaries and extended DMD’s explicit basis choices.
- Hyperparameter issues (choice of kernel and parameter selection/sensitivity) are insufficiently discussed or analyzed.
- High potential impact by bridging DMD/Koopman methods with kernel ML, offering a general tool for analyzing nonlinear dynamics.
- Reviewer’s recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, citing novelty, strong theory, practical algorithm, and significant potential.
