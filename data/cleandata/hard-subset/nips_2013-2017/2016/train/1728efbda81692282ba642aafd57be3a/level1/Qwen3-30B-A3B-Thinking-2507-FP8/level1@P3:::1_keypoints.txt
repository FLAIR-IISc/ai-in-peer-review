- Solid theoretical extension of DMD to RKHS with correct derivations linking the kernel approach to Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Practical contribution: robustification using POD bases to address ill-conditioning in DMD implementations.  
- Demonstrated potential applications in prediction, change-point detection, and motion recognition.  

- Empirical validation is very limited (only two synthetic examples) and lacks quantitative comparisons to standard DMD, extended DMD, or other kernel methods.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish or empirically compare to Williams et al.'s extended DMD, leaving the specific advantages of the kernel approach unclear.  
- Missing critical implementation details (kernel parameter selection, computational complexity) and no evidence that the kernel basis yields more accurate Koopman eigenfunctions.  
- Some claims (e.g., providing growth rates/frequencies) are overstated without showing superiority over linear DMD on non-trivial systems.  

- Overall assessment: theoretically sound but appears incremental relative to extended DMD and lacking practical significance due to weak empirical evidence.  
- POD-based robustification is useful but insufficient alone to justify the paper's main claims.  

- Required revisions: add quantitative comparisons with standard and extended DMD; evaluate on diverse/real datasets; show improved Koopman eigenfunction approximations; clarify differences from Williams et al.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form, with potential for acceptance after substantial empirical and comparative revisions.
