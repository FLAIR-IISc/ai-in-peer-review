- Proposes kernel DMD: extends DMD to nonlinear basis functions using Koopman operator in RKHS, with orthogonal Krylov bases and eigendecomposition of the projected operator.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets (toy nonlinear system, Hénon map, locomotion data).  
- Highlights potential ML applications: state prediction, sequential change-point detection, dynamics recognition.  
- Novel extension of DMD to nonlinear spectral analysis with kernel methods.  
- Provides solid theoretical foundations and clear explanation of the Koopman operator and properties.  
- Empirical evaluation shows promising results across multiple datasets.  
- Algorithm may be computationally expensive (Gram-Schmidt QR and eigendecomposition) for large datasets.  
- Robustness to noise is not thoroughly analyzed and may be a practical concern.  
- Comparisons to existing methods (beyond DMD and POD) are limited; deeper benchmarking would strengthen the paper.  
- Limitations and applicability boundaries of the Koopman operator are acknowledged but not fully discussed.  
- Original contribution: specific application of kernel methods to Koopman operator and development of kernel DMD.  
- Potentially significant impact on analysis of complex nonlinear dynamical systems and related ML tasks.  
- Paper is well-written and logically structured, but some derivations may be hard for non-experts.  
- Technically sound overall, though some limitations (complexity, noise robustness) need fuller treatment.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with revisions to address computational cost, noise robustness, comparisons, and Koopman limitations.
