- Paper studies spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems with the Koopman operator in an RKHS and derives a kernelized DMD via a Krylov/Arnoldi approach in feature space.  
- Two main theorems: (i) Arnoldi-style Ritz expansion expressing feature vectors as combinations of empirical Ritz vectors/eigenvalues; (ii) connection between reduced POD-based operator eigenpairs and Koopman eigenpairs under representability assumptions.  
- Proposes a “robust” POD-projected variant (akin to exact/optimized DMD) to mitigate ill-conditioning, implemented using only Gram matrices and accommodating non-consecutive data pairs.  
- Applications demonstrated: preimage-based prediction, dynamics recognition via kernel principal angles, and sequential change-point detection using a nonparametric exponential family and CUSUM.  
- Empirical illustrations include a toy system, the Hénon map, motion-capture embedding, and a change-point detection example.  
- Relation to prior work: substantial overlap with EDMD/kernel DMD and exact/optimized DMD; derivation via Krylov/Arnoldi is a different viewpoint but not clearly distinguished from prior methods.  
- Strength: clear, principled connection between Koopman/DMD, Krylov/Arnoldi, and kernels, with formal theorems and a practical Gram-matrix implementation.  
- Strength: robustification via kernel-PCA mirrors best practices and addresses numerical issues; code availability aids reproducibility.  
- Strength: framing DMD as feature extraction for recognition and change detection is well motivated and interesting.  
- Weakness: limited originality relative to EDMD/kDMD and exact DMD; paper underplays the degree of overlap and lacks a formal equivalence discussion.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is thin—mostly qualitative plots with no quantitative metrics or baseline comparisons (EDMD/kDMD, exact/optimized/sparse DMD).  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 assumes eigenfunctions lie in the kernel-PCA span; this is strong with no guidance on approximation error, sample complexity, or noise impact.  
- Weakness: preimage step is heuristic and brittle with no analysis or comparisons to preimage solvers or direct forecasting baselines.  
- Weakness: some proofs are sketchy, notation is overloaded and contains typos, and algorithmic complexity and practical choices (kernel width, truncation, regularization) are not discussed.  
- Quality: technically sound application of Arnoldi/Ritz and kernel trick, but theoretical novelty is modest and lacks error bounds or robustness analysis.  
- Clarity: high-level ideas understandable but notation and exposition need streamlining, explicit pseudocode, and discussion of numerical stabilization/centering.  
- Originality: substantial conceptual and algorithmic overlap with prior EDMD/kDMD; novelty mainly in derivation viewpoint and specific application recipes.  
- Significance: Koopman-based kernel features are of interest and practically useful, but impact is limited without stronger empirical/theoretical advances beyond EDMD/kDMD.  
- Suggestions: provide direct quantitative comparisons to EDMD/kDMD and DMD variants, add regularization, discuss kernel/truncation selection, and analyze approximation/error/stability.  
- Suggestions: expand preimage discussion with baselines and ablations, improve notation and include pseudocode and complexity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid, correct RKHS treatment linking Arnoldi/Ritz to kernel DMD, useful practitioner-oriented pipeline, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: significant overlap with prior work, limited theoretical novelty, and insufficient quantitative validation of heuristic components.  
- Overall: thoughtful and potentially useful paper that requires clearer positioning relative to EDMD/kDMD and substantially stronger empirical or theoretical contributions to meet conference standards.
