- Paper proposes kernelized DMD/Arnoldi in RKHS using Gram matrices to approximate Koopman spectra from trajectories.
- Presents a robust variant projecting onto kernel PCA (POD) directions to form a finite-dimensional operator using centered Gram matrices.
- Applies method to short-term prediction (via preimage), dynamics recognition (subspace angles), and sequential change-point detection (CUSUM with kernel-exp-family).
- Method is closely related to EDMD and existing RKHS transfer-operator approaches; kernel corresponds to an implicit dictionary.
- The kernel-POD truncation is analogous to numerical robustness techniques in exact/optimized DMD.
- Theoretical derivations mirror standard finite-dimensional Krylov/Arnoldi results; Theorem 1 restates known DMD relations in feature space.
- Theorem 2 links F̂ eigenstructure to Koopman eigenfunctions under strong, nontrivial assumptions (eigenfunctions must lie in span of selected kernel PCs).
- No consistency guarantees provided as data length grows or under noise; claims about full recovery of eigenfunctions are overly optimistic without conditions.
- Continuous spectrum and non-diagonalizable cases are not addressed and can invalidate conclusions.
- Algorithm scales O(τ^3) time and O(τ^2) memory due to τ×τ eigendecompositions, limiting applicability to long trajectories.
- Regularization strategies (Tikhonov/ridge), sensitivity to kernel choice/width, and truncation-level effects are not analyzed or discussed.
- Experiments are qualitative and small-scale (toy bilinear system, Henon); quantitative metrics and baselines are missing.
- No comparisons with EDMD (polynomial/RBF dictionaries), kernel-EDMD, exact/optimized DMD, or Gaussian-process baselines.
- Prediction lacks RMSE/MAE comparisons; recognition lacks classification metrics; change detection lacks thresholding, power/false-alarm analysis.
- Notation is confusing (ϕ feature map vs. φ eigenfunction); adjoints vs. conjugate transpose and H vs. C^τ should be clarified.
- Theory should state required conditions (linear independence, distinct eigenvalues, noise assumptions) for Theorems 1–2 to hold.
- Practical details missing: computational complexity, memory, suggested approximations (Nyström, randomized SVD), and kernel selection guidance.
- Incorrect statement: Koopman eigenvalues do not always lie on the unit circle for attractors (dissipative systems have decaying modes).
- Method is a useful synthesis but offers limited novelty over EDMD/kernel transfer-operator literature; clearer differentiation is needed.
- Potential significance if empirical evidence and scalability/regularization guidance are strengthened.
- Pros: clean kernelization implementable via Gram matrices; sensible kernel-POD robustification; ties to ML tasks; theoretical alignment under assumptions.
- Cons: limited novelty/positioning, strong theoretical assumptions, lack of consistency/noise analysis, weak experimental evaluation, scalability and preimage issues, clarity errors.
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative benchmarks vs. EDMD/kEDMD and DMD variants, implement regularization and scalable approximations, analyze noise robustness.
- Also suggested: correct spectral claims, discuss continuous spectra/non-normality, provide kernel/truncation selection guidance, and detail preimage/CUSUM implementations.
