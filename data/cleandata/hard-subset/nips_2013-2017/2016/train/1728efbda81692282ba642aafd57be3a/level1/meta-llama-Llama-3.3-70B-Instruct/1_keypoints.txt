- Proposes a novel spectral analysis approach for nonlinear dynamical systems using the Koopman operator in RKHSs.
- Introduces a modal decomposition algorithm based on orthogonal bases for the RKHS Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.
- Algorithm yields a finite-mode decomposition usable for feature extraction and state prediction in nonlinear dynamics.
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivates the problem and background.
- Technical sections are organized and connect the method to existing approaches like DMD.
- Empirical examples demonstrate effectiveness in estimating Koopman modes and forecasting future states.
- Strength: novel RKHS-Koopman formulation for spectral analysis.
- Strength: development of a practical modal decomposition algorithm.
- Strength: empirical demonstrations supporting the approach’s utility.
- Weakness: assumes substantial prior knowledge of Koopman operator theory and RKHSs, limiting accessibility.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparative analysis with DMD and other spectral methods.
- Weakness: some empirical examples are relatively simple and may not showcase full potential.
- Argument for acceptance: original, well-motivated method with promising applicability and empirical support.
- Argument against acceptance: paper may be overly technical for general readers and comparison/experiments are limited.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to improve accessibility, comparisons, and more challenging experiments.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
