- Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD): extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition to RKHS to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions using implicit nonlinear bases.
- Presents a direct algorithm based on Gram–Schmidt QR of the Krylov matrix in feature space, but notes numerical instability.
- Proposes a robustified algorithm that projects data via kernel PCA (KPCA) and performs eigendecomposition in a truncated subspace.
- Provides theoretical guarantees linking computed eigenvalues/eigenvectors to true Koopman spectral objects (Theorems 1 and 2).
- Frames kDMD as a feature extraction method for dynamical systems applicable to prediction, embedding/recognition, and change-point detection.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic systems (toy system, Hénon, Lorenz) and real motion-capture data; includes code release.
- Technical strength: rigorous formulation of DMD in RKHS and solid theoretical grounding.
- Technical weakness: change-point detection derivation is heuristic (nonparametric exponential family and dropped term) and needs stronger justification or validation.
- Technical weakness: prediction relies on solving the pre-image problem (MDS-based) which is briefly described and may be unreliable.
- Clarity strength: well-written, organized, with accessible introduction and clear progression from theory to robust algorithm.
- Clarity weakness: applications section is concise and lacks detailed methodology (especially pre-image and change-point specifics); minor inconsistency in caption/text ("kDMD-SVM" vs CUSUM).
- Originality strength: novel synthesis of Koopman theory and kernel methods, providing a data-driven implicit basis alternative to eDMD.
- Originality weakness: the KPCA-based robustification is a straightforward extension of POD in standard DMD (useful but not highly novel).
- Significance strength: broad potential impact for analyzing nonlinear dynamics and as a dynamical feature extractor for ML tasks.
- Significance weaknesses: scalability concerns due to τ×τ Gram matrix and sensitivity to kernel choice/hyperparameters are not deeply explored.
- Overall acceptance arguments: novel, technically sound, broadly applicable, and clearly presented; counterarguments: limited application detail and practical scalability/kernel-selection guidance.
