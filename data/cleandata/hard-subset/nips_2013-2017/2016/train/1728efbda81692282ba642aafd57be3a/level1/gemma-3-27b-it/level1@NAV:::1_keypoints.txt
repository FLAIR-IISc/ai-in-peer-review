- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) with reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis.  
- Core idea: use kernel methods instead of linear monomial bases to better approximate Koopman eigenfunctions for nonlinear dynamics.  
- Provides theoretical framework, a practical algorithm, and empirical demonstrations on synthetic and real data.  
- Positions the work within prior literature (POD, DMD, kernel PCA, Koopman analysis) and cites relevant foundational works.  
- Strength: conceptually sound extension of DMD leveraging RKHS expressiveness.  
- Strength: clear and well-motivated theoretical derivations.  
- Strength: introduces POD projection for robustness against ill-conditioning.  
- Strength: empirical results show capability to estimate eigenvalues and useful features for prediction, recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited and not comprehensive.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other DMD variants (e.g., exact DMD, sparse DMD).  
- Weakness: kernel parameter selection (polynomial degree, Gaussian width) is not well justified or discussed.  
- Weakness: applications lack rigorous quantitative benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: paper repeats established DMD/Koopman background and could be more concise.  
- Verdict: promising and theoretically solid; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to expand empirical comparisons and clarify kernel parameter selection.
