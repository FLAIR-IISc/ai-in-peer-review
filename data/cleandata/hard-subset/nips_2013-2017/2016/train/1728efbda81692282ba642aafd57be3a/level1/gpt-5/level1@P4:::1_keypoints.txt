- Proposes a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) for Koopman spectral analysis using RKHS feature maps and Gram matrices.  
- Introduces two procedures: feature-space Arnoldi (RKHS Gram–Schmidt Krylov method) and a robust kernel PCA/POD-truncated operator F̂.  
- Theorems claim Ritz-value approximations from feature-space Arnoldi and recovery/modal expansion under subspace containment.  
- Implementation operates entirely via Gram matrices; feature-space Arnoldi uses RKHS QR/orthogonalization.  
- The robust operator F̂ is algebraically very similar to EDMD/kernel EDMD’s finite operator (G−1A).  
- Paper cites EDMD but lacks a deep theoretical or empirical comparison to kernel EDMD.  
- Technical soundness is generally reasonable, aligning with Krylov/DMD theory, but some linear-algebra details need clarification.  
- Theorems omit explicit assumptions (distinct Ritz values, invertibility/independence of Krylov vectors, conditioning) that are needed for rigor.  
- Identification of F as a “companion matrix” and some constructions are ambiguous in infinite-dimensional settings.  
- The impact of centering (H) on Koopman invariance and on the robust operator is not discussed.  
- Application ideas (pre-image prediction, subspace-angle dynamics recognition, kernel-CUSUM change detection) are heuristic and under-analyzed.  
- Pre-image via MDS can be unstable; complexity and convergence are not analyzed.  
- Change-point detection formulation uses a kernel exponential family but ignores the log-partition function and normalization, effectively reducing to a kernel-similarity CUSUM without full justification.  
- Clarity is adequate at a high level but marred by notational inconsistencies, typos, sparse figure labels, and limited quantitative evaluation.  
- Originality is incremental relative to EDMD/kernel EDMD; the feature-space Arnoldi view and theorems are useful but not a major departure.  
- Significance is potential but currently limited by lack of quantitative benchmarks, error analyses, and comparisons to EDMD/optimized DMD.  
- Strengths: clean RKHS/Gram-matrix implementation, theoretical connections (Ritz expansions, recovery conditions), POD-style robustification, code availability.  
- Weaknesses: substantial overlap with prior EDMD work, missing theorem assumptions, heuristic application sections, limited baselines and reproducibility details.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly relate/compare to EDMD/kernel EDMD, state theorem assumptions, discuss centering, add quantitative experiments (noise sensitivity, kernels, truncation), and clarify pre-image and change-point method details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid RKHS formalization, practical robustification aligned with DMD practice, and potentially useful to practitioners.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental relative to EDMD, limited empirical validation and baselines, and theoretical/presentation gaps that need addressing.
