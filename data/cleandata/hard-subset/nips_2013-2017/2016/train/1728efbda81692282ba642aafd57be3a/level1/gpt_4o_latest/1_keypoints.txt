- Novel extension of Koopman spectral analysis to RKHS enabling richer nonlinear eigenfunction approximation.
- Robustified algorithm using POD bases addresses ill-conditioning and improves numerical stability.
- Demonstrated versatility with applications in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition on synthetic and real-world data.
- Clear mathematical framework with well-articulated derivations and algorithmic steps.
- Timely and relevant work for machine learning, physics, and engineering communities.
- Experimental evaluation lacks comprehensive benchmarks against state-of-the-art methods (e.g., extended DMD, other kernel approaches).
- Scalability and computational complexity for large or high-dimensional datasets are not thoroughly discussed.
- Limited real-world validation with relatively narrow dataset scope.
- Some application descriptions (prediction via preimage, change-point detection) are dense and could use clearer exposition or visuals.
- For acceptance: technically novel and sound extension, robustified method improves practicality, and demonstrated multi-domain potential.
- Against acceptance: limited experiments, lack of rigorous comparisons and scalability analysis, and narrow/unclear real-world application presentation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, address scalability, and clarify application sections.
