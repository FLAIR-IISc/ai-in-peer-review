- Summary: Introduces kDMD, a kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition that lifts data into an RKHS to approximate Koopman spectra via a data-spanned Krylov subspace and proposes a kernel-PCA–based robust variant; demonstrates prediction, recognition, embedding, and change-point detection on synthetic and real data.

- Strength: Elegant, principled generalization of DMD using kernels to handle nonlinearity without manually specifying observables; theorem connects Koopman in RKHS with an Arnoldi-like procedure.

- Strength: Practical and robust algorithm addressing numerical instability via kernel PCA truncation, implementable with Gram-matrix computations.

- Strength: Demonstrates usefulness of learned Koopman modes as features for ML tasks (dynamics recognition, change-point detection), broadening DMD relevance to ML audiences.

- Strength: Clear, well-organized exposition and clearly stated theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2).

- Weakness: Inadequate discussion of relation to Extended DMD (EDMD); kDMD is essentially EDMD with an implicit kernel dictionary and needs a deeper comparative analysis of trade-offs.

- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance on kernel and hyperparameter selection (e.g., kernel width), hindering reproducibility and applicability.

- Weakness: Theoretical assumption (Theorem 2) that Koopman eigenfunctions lie in the subspace spanned by kernel principal components is strong and its implications/limitations are not sufficiently discussed.

- Weakness: Change-point detection derivation uses heuristics (ignoring a term claimed to have "much smaller" variation) without enough justification or empirical validation.

- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound synthesis of Koopman theory and kernel methods addressing DMD’s nonlinear limitations.

- For acceptance: Robust, practical algorithm with compelling ML-relevant applications and clear writing.

- Against acceptance: Novelty is partially reduced by under-discussed connection to EDMD.

- Against acceptance: Missing crucial practical details on hyperparameter/model selection affecting reproducibility.

- Conclusion: High-quality, impactful paper recommended for acceptance provided revisions clarify EDMD connection and add guidance on hyperparameter selection.
