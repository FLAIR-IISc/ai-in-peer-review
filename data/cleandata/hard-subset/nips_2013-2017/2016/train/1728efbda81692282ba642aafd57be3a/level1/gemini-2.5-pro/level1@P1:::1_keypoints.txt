- Proposes kernel DMD (kDMD): extending DMD to RKHS to perform Koopman spectral analysis in a feature space via kernel functions.  
- Core algorithm avoids explicit feature computations by operating on Gram matrices to approximate Koopman eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.  
- Introduces a robust variant that projects data onto kernel PCA (kPCA) components before decomposition, analogous to POD in standard DMD.  
- Frames kDMD as an implicit, data-dependent dictionary approach related to extended DMD (eDMD), enabling richer nonlinear representations without hand-crafted bases.  
- Strength: original and significant synthesis of DMD/Koopman theory with kernel methods, making Koopman analysis more applicable to complex nonlinear systems.  
- Strength: solid theoretical development (Theorem 1 and Theorem 2) and clear connection between estimated modes and true Koopman eigenfunctions under reasonable assumptions.  
- Strength: experiments illustrate the method can correctly identify eigenvalues for nonlinear and chaotic systems and highlight utility for prediction, recognition, and change-point detection.  
- Strength: positions Koopman spectral analysis as a useful feature-extraction tool for time-series and machine learning tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficiently explored relation to eDMD—kDMD can be viewed as eDMD with kernel-centered dictionaries and deserves deeper comparison.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—constructing and eigendecomposing τ×τ Gram matrices is O(τ^2)–O(τ^3) and prohibitive for long time series; remedies (Nyström, random features) not discussed.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion and justification for kernel choice and hyperparameter selection; no sensitivity analysis shown.  
- Weakness: some experimental and implementation details are sparse (pre-image method, approximations in change-point score), harming reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel kernelization of DMD, principled data-driven Koopman analysis, strong bridging of fields, and promising feature-extraction potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty vs eDMD, unaddressed computational limits, ignored kernel/hyperparameter issues, and incomplete experimental details.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper recommended for acceptance with requested revisions to clarify relation to eDMD, address scalability, and improve experimental reproducibility.
