- Summary: Proposes a kernelized DMD via a Krylov/Arnoldi construction in RKHS, with a kernel-PCA (POD) robustification and applications to prediction, dynamics recognition, and change detection; demonstrated on toy systems, Hénon, locomotion, and Lorenz examples.

- Relation to prior work: Largely recovers EDMD/kernel EDMD; main framing novelty is an Arnoldi/Krylov derivation in feature space but substantial overlap with existing kernel EDMD literature.

- Theory: Theorems 1–2 are algebraically correct under strong assumptions (distinct eigenvalues, true eigenfunctions in the feature span) but provide no statistical/consistency guarantees for finite or noisy data.

- Algorithms: Uses standard QR/Gram tricks and kernel PCA; implementation details are thin—no complexity analysis, limited discussion of numerical conditioning, and no treatment of noise (e.g., Tikhonov).

- Experiments: Empirical work is qualitative and limited—lacks quantitative metrics, robustness/sensitivity analyses, baselines (EDMD/kEDMD, GPDM, RNNs), ablations, and systematic benchmarks.

- Clarity: Paper is generally readable and organized but uses heavy notation, has typos, and should better explicate algebraic equivalence to EDMD and provide concrete implementation guidance.

- Originality: Incremental—conceptual framing is cohesive but methodological novelty over kernel EDMD is modest and not clearly delineated.

- Significance: Modest in current form due to overlap with prior work and weak empirical/statistical validation; stronger theory and experiments would be needed to increase impact.

- Pros: Clear presentation of RKHS Koopman spectral analysis, practical kernel PCA robustification, Gram-matrix implementation approach, code availability, and illustrative application sketches.

- Cons: High overlap with EDMD/kernel EDMD, insufficient quantitative experiments, strong/unrealistic theorem assumptions, and sparse guidance on kernel selection, regularization, and truncation.

- Suggestions: Explicitly derive and contrast with EDMD/kEDMD, provide quantitative eigenvalue/mode recovery and prediction benchmarks, analyze noise/kernel/truncation sensitivity and regularization, and add computational complexity/scalability analysis.

- Overall assessment: Moderate quality and clarity but limited originality and empirical rigor; leans toward rejection unless strengthened with clearer differentiation, theoretical guarantees, and thorough experiments.
