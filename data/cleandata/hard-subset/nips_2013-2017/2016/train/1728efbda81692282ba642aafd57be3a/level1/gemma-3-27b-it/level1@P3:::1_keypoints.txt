- Proposes an RKHS-based DMD method for Koopman spectral analysis using kernel-induced basis functions and a Krylov subspace algorithm.  
- Positions the work well within existing literature; related work section is comprehensive and connects to control subspace identification.  
- Novelty: extends DMD expressiveness by using RKHS bases to overcome linear-monomial limitations.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivations and theorems are rigorous and clearly tied to the Krylov/DMD framework.  
- Completeness: provides a full algorithmic description and addresses ill-conditioning with a POD-based robustification.  
- Empirical validation: demonstrates method on synthetic and real data for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamic pattern recognition.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear notation and well-outlined algorithm steps.  
- Computational cost: lacks thorough discussion of complexity and scalability, especially for kernel/Gram matrix computations.  
- Kernel selection: choice of kernels seems arbitrary and the paper lacks sensitivity analysis or guidelines for kernel/parameter selection.  
- Comparative analysis: evaluations are limited and do not comprehensively compare to state-of-the-art methods for the presented tasks.  
- Parameter tuning: experiments do not explain how POD dimensions or regularization parameters were chosen.  
- Implementation details: practical aspects (Gram matrix optimization, libraries used) are under-specified despite available code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: method is novel, theoretically justified, empirically promising, and code is provided for reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability/cost issues, insufficient comparative benchmarks, and weak kernel/parameter discussion.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address complexity/scalability, kernel/parameter selection, broader comparisons, and implementation details.
