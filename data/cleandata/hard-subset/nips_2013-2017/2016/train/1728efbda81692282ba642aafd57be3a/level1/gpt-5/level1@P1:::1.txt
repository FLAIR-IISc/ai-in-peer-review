This paper studies spectral analysis of nonlinear dynamical systems via the Koopman operator in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The authors derive a kernelized dynamic mode decomposition (DMD) by constructing a Krylov subspace in feature space, performing a Gram–Schmidt QR, and computing Ritz values/vectors from the projected Koopman operator. They provide two main theorems: (i) an Arnoldi-style expansion showing that feature vectors along a trajectory can be expressed as linear combinations of empirical Ritz vectors/eigenvalues; and (ii) a connection between eigenpairs of a reduced operator built on kernel-PCA (POD) bases and Koopman eigenpairs under representability assumptions. They then propose a “robust” variant that projects onto kernel-PCA directions (akin to exact/optimized DMD’s POD projection) to mitigate ill-conditioning. Finally, they demonstrate applications as feature extraction for prediction (via a pre-image step), dynamics recognition (kernel principal angles between subspaces spanned by estimated Koopman eigenfunctions), and sequential change-point detection (CUSUM using a nonparametric exponential family built from the estimated features). Empirical illustrations include a toy system, the Hénon map, motion capture data embedding, and a change-point detection example.

Relation to prior work: Koopman/DMD is well established [25,26,31], and robust variants (exact/optimized DMD, sparsity-promoting DMD) exist [8,13,33]. Extended DMD (EDMD) and its kernelized/“kernel DMD” formulations due to Williams, Kevrekidis, Rowley [35] provide a data-driven RKHS approximation of the Koopman operator using Gram matrices and generalized eigenproblems, with theory and algorithms that permit non-consecutive pairs. The method here—QR/Arnoldi in feature space and a POD-projected variant—appears mathematically equivalent or very close in spirit to EDMD/kDMD and exact DMD, with a different derivation emphasizing Krylov/Arnoldi, plus application-oriented add-ons (preimage-based prediction, subspace-angle recognition, CUSUM). The paper cites [35] but underplays the degree of overlap.

Strengths
- Clear and principled connection between Koopman spectral analysis, Krylov/Arnoldi, and kernels. Theorems formalize the Ritz expansion in RKHS and the role of kernel-PCA projection.
- Practical algorithmic steps using only Gram matrices; accommodates non-consecutive data pairs.
- Useful perspective: DMD as feature extraction for dynamics; applications to recognition and change detection are well motivated and interesting.
- The robustification via kernel-PCA mirrors best practices in DMD, addressing numerical issues.
- Code availability improves reproducibility.

Weaknesses
- Originality is limited relative to EDMD and kernel DMD [35]. The robust variant is essentially exact/optimized DMD in feature space. The paper does not provide a crisp distinction or formal equivalence discussion.
- Empirical evaluation is thin: mostly qualitative plots; no quantitative metrics, no comparisons to baselines (EDMD/kDMD, exact/optimized/sparse DMD) on eigenvalue accuracy, mode reconstruction error, forecasting RMSE, recognition accuracy, or change-point detection delay/false-alarm rates.
- Assumptions in Theorem 2 (eigenfunctions lying in the span of kernel-PCA bases) are strong; no guidance on approximation error or impact of noise/finite data. No sample complexity or stability analysis.
- The preimage step is heuristic and potentially brittle; no analysis of when it succeeds nor comparisons to standard preimage solvers or direct forecasting baselines.
- Clarity: some proofs are sketchy; notation is occasionally overloaded (ϕ, φ, Φ) and there are typos (“robustly it”). Algorithmic complexity and practical choices (kernel width selection, truncation criteria, regularization) are not discussed.

Quality
- Technically sound in its use of Arnoldi/Ritz ideas and the kernel trick; however, several claims are standard in the DMD/Koopman literature, and theoretical novelty is modest. No error bounds or robustness analyses. Experiments are not rigorous enough to substantiate advantages.

Clarity
- The high-level idea is understandable and the step-by-step procedures are given. Notation could be streamlined; include explicit pseudocode, numerical stabilization (regularization of Gram matrices), and complexity. Improve exposition of centering in kernel-PCA vis-à-vis Koopman analysis.

Originality
- Conceptual and algorithmic overlap with EDMD/kDMD is substantial; the main novelty is the derivation via a Krylov/Gram–Schmidt viewpoint and the particular application recipes (preimage prediction, subspace-angle recognition, CUSUM). This is incremental.

Significance
- Koopman-based features are of interest to the NIPS community, and a kernelized, numerically robust pipeline is practically useful. However, without stronger empirical evidence or novel theory beyond existing EDMD/kDMD, the impact is limited.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide direct comparisons to EDMD/kDMD, exact and optimized DMD, and sparse DMD on synthetic and real datasets. Report quantitative metrics (eigenvalue errors vs. ground truth, mode reconstruction error, short-horizon prediction RMSE, recognition accuracy, change-point detection ROC/ARL).
- Clarify the relationship/equivalence to EDMD/kDMD and exact DMD; discuss when the proposed method differs or offers advantages (e.g., computational, numerical stability).
- Add regularization (e.g., Tikhonov) to handle noisy, rank-deficient Gram matrices; discuss kernel bandwidth/truncation selection (median heuristic vs. cross-validation).
- Analyze approximation error due to finite data and truncation; discuss noise and stochastic systems more rigorously.
- Expand preimage discussion with baseline comparisons and ablations.
- Improve writing/notation and include algorithmic pseudocode and complexity.

Arguments for acceptance
- Solid, correct treatment of Koopman/DMD in RKHS with a clear algorithm and useful applications.
- Offers a unifying perspective linking Arnoldi/Ritz to kernel DMD; could be useful to practitioners in ML who are new to Koopman methods.
- Code provided.

Arguments against acceptance
- Overlaps significantly with prior EDMD/kernel DMD; limited theoretical novelty.
- Insufficient quantitative evaluation and lack of strong comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Heuristic components (preimage, change detection) not rigorously validated.

Overall, this is a thoughtful and potentially useful paper, but to meet NIPS standards it needs clearer positioning relative to EDMD/kDMD and substantially stronger empirical and/or theoretical contributions.
