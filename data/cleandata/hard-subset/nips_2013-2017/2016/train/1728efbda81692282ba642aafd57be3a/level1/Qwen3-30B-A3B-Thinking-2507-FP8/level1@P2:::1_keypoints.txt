- Contribution: Extends DMD by using reproducing kernels (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis, enabling nonlinear basis functions.
- Core algorithm: Performs modal decomposition in RKHS by computing orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix and eigendecomposition of the projected Koopman operator.
- Theoretical foundation: Theorems 1 and 2 give rigorous justification for the kernel-based extension and its relation to traditional DMD.
- Numerical stability: Recognizes stability issues and employs a POD-based robustification step to improve numerical robustness.
- Empirical validation: Convincing experiments on synthetic (toy, Hénon, Lorenz) and real-world (locomotion) data, with useful comparisons to standard and equilibrium DMD.
- Missing comparisons: Does not sufficiently compare to other kernel-based system identification methods (e.g., Gaussian process dynamical models).
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with detailed derivations; minor abrupt transition when introducing the robustification step.
- Originality: Novel application of reproducing kernels to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, distinct from extended DMD and related approaches.
- Significance: Substantial — provides a more expressive nonlinear representation useful for prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition across domains.
- Strengths: Strong theoretical development, clear extension of DMD literature, solid empirical results, practical applications, and good writing.
- Weaknesses: Limited kernel-method comparisons, under-motivated robustification step, insufficient justification for kernel parameter choices, and sparse discussion of computational complexity.
- Recommendation: Pro acceptance — meaningful, well-founded contribution with convincing results, though would benefit from more comparisons and computational detail.
