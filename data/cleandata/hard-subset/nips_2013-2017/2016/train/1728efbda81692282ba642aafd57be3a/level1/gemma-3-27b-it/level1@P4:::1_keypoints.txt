- Proposes extending Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis to capture nonlinear dynamics.
- Uses kernel methods to replace linear monomials with nonlinear basis functions and presents a theoretical framework and algorithms.
- Demonstrates applications on synthetic and real-world data for prediction, sequential change-point detection, and dynamics recognition.
- Novelty: RKHS-DMD is a meaningful extension of DMD that offers more flexible basis functions for nonlinear systems.
- Technical soundness: Theoretical development (Theorems 1 and 2) is rigorous and connections to existing DMD techniques are well-motivated.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent, readable mathematical notation.
- Completeness: Presents a full algorithm with practical implementation considerations (e.g., POD for ill-conditioning) and empirical validation.
- Empirical validation is limited: examples are encouraging but lack thorough comparisons, detailed analysis, and justification of kernel parameter choices.
- Computational complexity is not addressed: RKHS-based DMD may be substantially more expensive than traditional DMD for large datasets.
- Related work discussion is insufficient: needs deeper comparison to other kernelized DMD variants and extended DMD approaches.
- Assumptions and limitations are under-discussed: conditions like φj ∈ Φ in Theorem 2 and other method limitations require more analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically justified extension to a popular technique with practical algorithm and promising results.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support, missing computational-cost analysis, incomplete related-work comparison, and under-explored assumptions.
- Recommendation: Leans toward acceptance with minor revisions—authors should add more benchmarks, parameter-tuning discussion, and complexity/related-work clarifications.
