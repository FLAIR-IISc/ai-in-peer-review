## Review of "Dynamic Mode Decomposition with Reproducing Kernels for Koopman Spectral Analysis"

This paper proposes a Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) algorithm extended to utilize reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis. The core idea is to move beyond linear monomial basis functions traditionally used in DMD, leveraging the expressiveness of kernels to approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, potentially enabling the analysis of more complex nonlinear dynamics. The authors provide a theoretical framework, a practical algorithm, and demonstrate its performance on both synthetic and real-world data, including applications in prediction, dynamics recognition, and change-point detection. 

This work builds on a substantial body of research in spectral analysis of dynamical systems, including Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD), DMD, and related methods like balanced POD. It directly addresses a limitation of standard DMD – its reliance on linear approximations – by incorporating the power of kernel methods, connecting it to work on kernel PCA and kernel subspace methods for system identification. The paper correctly positions itself within this lineage, referencing relevant works such as those by Koopman, Rowley, Schmid, and others. 

**Strengths:** The paper’s main strength lies in its conceptually sound extension of DMD using RKHS. The theoretical derivations are clear and well-motivated. The inclusion of a robustness enhancement through projection onto POD directions is a pragmatic addition, addressing a potential practical issue with ill-conditioning. The empirical evaluations, while relatively simple, demonstrate the method’s ability to estimate eigenvalues and apply the resulting features to relevant tasks. The applications section showcases the potential utility of the method beyond simply spectral estimation.

**Weaknesses:** While theoretically sound, the empirical validation feels somewhat limited. The comparison to other DMD variants (e.g., exact DMD, sparse DMD) is missing, hindering a clear assessment of the benefits of the proposed kernel-based approach. The parameter selection for the kernel (degree for polynomial, width for Gaussian) is not thoroughly discussed or justified. The applications, while interesting, lack a rigorous quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art methods in each domain. The paper also spends considerable time reiterating established concepts in DMD and Koopman analysis, which could be condensed.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of DMD with RKHS offers potential for analyzing more complex nonlinear dynamics.
*   Theoretically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Practical improvement through POD projection.
*   Demonstrates applicability in relevant tasks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited empirical validation compared to existing DMD methods.
*   Lack of detailed discussion on kernel parameter selection.
*   Applications are not comprehensively benchmarked.
*   Redundancy in background information.



**Overall:** This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of spectral analysis of dynamical systems. While the empirical validation could be strengthened, the core idea is promising and the theoretical foundation is solid. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically focusing on more comprehensive empirical comparisons and a clearer discussion of kernel parameter selection.
