- Introduces DMD in RKHS to extend basis beyond linear monomials for improved Koopman spectral analysis and applications.

Strengths:
- Novelty: Extending DMD to RKHS is a valuable idea that can capture more complex dynamics than standard DMD.
- Theoretical soundness: Mathematical derivation is rigorous and connects to standard DMD via Krylov subspace analysis.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explains motivation and technical details clearly.
- Applications: Demonstrates potential usefulness in prediction, pattern recognition, and change-point detection.

Weaknesses:
- Computational cost: RKHS computations and POD-based robustification may be expensive; scalability is not discussed.
- Kernel selection: Choice and tuning of kernels (e.g., Gaussian bandwidth) are only briefly mentioned without guidance.
- Limited empirical evaluation: Examples are simple; lacks tests on complex/high-dimensional datasets and comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Application depth: Application results (e.g., change-point detection) lack quantitative benchmarking against alternatives.
- Reference gaps: Misses discussion of related kernel-based system identification literature (e.g., kernel ridge regression).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, impactful extension addressing a key limitation of DMD.
- Sound theoretical framework with clear derivations.
- Empirical examples demonstrate potential despite limited scope.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Scalability and computational complexity inadequately addressed.
- Empirical evaluation insufficient and missing thorough comparisons.
- Applications are superficial and lack quantitative analysis.

Overall assessment:
- Solid contribution with promise, but would benefit from deeper discussion of computation, kernel selection, and stronger empirical validation.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions; authors should address computational complexity, kernel selection, expand empirical comparisons, and provide more quantitative application analysis.
