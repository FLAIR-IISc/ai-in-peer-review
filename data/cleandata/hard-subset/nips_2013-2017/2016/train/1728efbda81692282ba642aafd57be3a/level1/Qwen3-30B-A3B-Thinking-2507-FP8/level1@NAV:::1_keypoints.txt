- Proposes kernelized extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD) using reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces (RKHS) for Koopman spectral analysis of nonlinear systems.  
- Core algorithm computes orthogonal bases for the Krylov matrix in RKHS and eigendecomposes the projected Koopman operator.  
- Introduces a robustified variant using POD bases to mitigate numerical instability.  
- Provides strong theoretical grounding with proofs linking the method to Koopman operator theory.  
- Builds on and relates to prior work (standard DMD, extended DMD) while addressing linear approximation limitations.  
- Demonstrates practical utility in state prediction, change-point detection, and dynamics recognition on synthetic and real data (including CMU motion capture).  
- Strengths: clear mathematical development, thorough empirical validation, meaningful applications, and good reproducibility.  
- Robustification with POD is a practically valuable contribution for numerical stability.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and analysis of computational complexity relative to standard DMD.  
- Weakness: experimental section could better quantify performance gains over existing methods and alternatives.  
- Weakness: insufficient guidance on when the kernel-based approach is preferable to standard or extended DMD.  
- Weakness: limited comparison with other existing kernel-based approaches.
