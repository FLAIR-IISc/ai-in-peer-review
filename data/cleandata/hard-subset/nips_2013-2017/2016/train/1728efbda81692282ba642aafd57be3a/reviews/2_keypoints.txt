- Proposes a spectral method using the Koopman operator for analyzing nonlinear dynamical systems.
- Uses Koopman operator decomposition to model forward dynamics x(t+2) = f(f(x(t))).
- From collected trajectories, performs eigen-decomposition using kernel PCA and subspace-analysis ideas.
- Applies the method to embedding and recognition of dynamical systems and reports good empirical performance.
- Novelty for kernel-method readers: treats function composition as a linear operator and applies decomposition to compositions.
- Dynamical systems are a natural application since they are repeated function compositions.
- The core algorithm (dynamic mode decomposition, DMD) already exists in the dynamical-systems literature.
- Main novelty appears limited to kernelizing DMD rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.
- Competing approaches (e.g., recurrent neural networks) and change-detection methods exist for system recognition.
- The paper’s applications are not very compelling in demonstrating the method’s distinct advantages or uniqueness.
