- Paper derives spectral analysis of the Koopman operator in RKHS and proposes a data-driven algorithm.  
- Demonstrations use two simple dynamical systems (damped 1D and chaotic map) and real locomotion data, but comparisons to DMD/PCA/kPCA yield unclear results.  
- The method is an interesting kernelized extension of dynamic mode decomposition, related to prior kernel methods (kernel PCA/ICA, kernel tests, etc.).  
- Paper is clearly written and pedagogically useful for readers unfamiliar with fluid-dynamics concepts.  
- Reviewer questions the practical usefulness for ML; authors need to identify concrete use cases and compare to state-of-the-art.  
- Suggest robotics (or another ML domain with dynamical-system inference) as a target to demonstrate utility of Koopman analysis.  
- The theoretical results are unsurprising given existing RKHS/kernel linear-operator literature.  
- Clarify whether the modal decomposition requires explicit feature-space representations — if so, this is a limitation compared to kernel PCA.  
- Request to include standard DMD performance on the toy example and the Henon map to highlight differences.  
- Concern that the deterministic-only setup (no stochastic/noise components) limits relevance to ML applications where randomness is common.
