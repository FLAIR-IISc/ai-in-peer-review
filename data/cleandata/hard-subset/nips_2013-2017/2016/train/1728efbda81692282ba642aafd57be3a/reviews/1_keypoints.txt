- Paper introduces Koopman spectral analysis in an RKHS with kernel-based methods, showing efficiency gains and experimentally validated contributions.  
- Unclear distinction from Williams et al. (2015) — request explicit comparison and clearer discussion (esp. Sec. 4).  
- Missing relevant prior work in kernel-based dynamical systems (e.g., Ralaivola & d'Alche Buc; Tuia et al.; Rojo-Álvarez et al.; Mouattamid & Schaback; Franz & Schölkopf).  
- Need discussion and experiments on stochastic settings (performance, limitations), not just deterministic cases.  
- Clarify the practical implications of using pairs of consecutive observables instead of sequences, especially for multidimensional time series with weak pointwise relations, and discuss the causal assumptions.  
- Experiments are not ambitious: prediction is standard; lack of comparisons to time-series clustering methods (kernel k-means, subspace kernels) and to state-of-the-art online kernel change-point detection; more convincing evaluations requested.  
- Minor issues: two typos—indexing tau-1 vs. tau and replace dot with comma in k(xi.xj) (last equation on p.7).
