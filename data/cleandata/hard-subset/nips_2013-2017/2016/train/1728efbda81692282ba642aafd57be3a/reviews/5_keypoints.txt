- Paper proposes KDMD: extending DMD to RKHS using feature maps; applied to toy problems and is a powerful generalization of DMD.  
- Main concern: manuscript is hard to read—overly mathematical and verbose; needs clearer, shorter explanations.  
- Paper is cryptic for NeurIPS; strongly encourage a more pedagogical rewrite (would then be highly citable).  
- Major remark: presentation is needlessly verbose; many statements could be much more concise.  
- Major remark: Section 4 is largely redundant/covered in the introduction and unnecessary in a short paper.  
- Major remark: Section 5 should be illustrated with examples or summarized in the conclusion.  
- Major remark: Section 6 is overly condensed and should be developed in the manner of Section 5.  
- Minor remark: Possible typo—described “nonlinear” toy case appears to be linear.  
- Minor remark: First two test problems are polynomial; KDMD is most useful when the kernel/feature map can be guessed—more detail needed (as in Sec. 5).  
- Reviewer cites Brunton et al. (2015) as a relevant reference.
