- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method combining l1 sparsity and a Laplacian spatial penalty to learn spatially coherent, sparse brain regions at large scale.  
- Novelty: successfully integrates a spatial smoothness prior into the scalable online dictionary-learning framework, addressing a gap between structured models and computational feasibility.  
- Scalability: online algorithm scales to massive datasets (e.g., HCP), enabling practical application to large neuroimaging cohorts.  
- Empirical validation: strong experiments on HCP with qualitative and quantitative metrics (explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction) and informative learning curves showing advantages in small-to-medium data regimes.  
- Runtime: spatial prior increases runtime modestly (2–3× vs unstructured SODL) but remains substantially faster than batch methods like CanICA.  
- Code availability: authors promise to release code in Nilearn, enhancing reproducibility and impact.  
- Clarity: paper is very well-written, clearly motivated, and easy for experts to follow.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: selecting sparsity τ, code regularization α, and smoothness γ is challenging; K-fold CV is costly and more practical guidance or automated tuning is needed.  
- Choice of Laplacian vs TV: authors should justify using an l2 Laplacian penalty over l1 Total Variation, as Laplacian may oversmooth and TV better preserves sharp boundaries.  
- Predictive-performance trade-off: Smooth-SODL improves interpretability and stability but is outperformed by dense CanICA in raw behavioral prediction, suggesting structured priors may discard predictive subject-specific variance.  
- Evaluation summary: quality high, clarity excellent, originality good, significance high.  
- Recommendation: accept.
