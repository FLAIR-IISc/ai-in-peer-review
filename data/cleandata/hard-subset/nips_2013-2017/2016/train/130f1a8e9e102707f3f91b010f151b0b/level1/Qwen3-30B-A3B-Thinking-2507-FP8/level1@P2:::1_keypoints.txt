- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension that adds a Laplacian spatial smoothness penalty to learn interpretable brain regions.  
- Addresses neuroimaging challenge of principled ROI extraction without segmentation or cross-modality alignment.  
- Enforces spatial smoothness to produce localized "blobs" instead of scattered atoms while retaining online learning benefits.  
- Technical contribution: efficiently integrates Laplacian penalty into online dictionary learning with only a 2–3x runtime increase.  
- Solves a non-trivial coupling introduced by spatial structure without resorting to slow alternating optimization.  
- Evaluated on 500 HCP subjects, compared against SODL and CanICA.  
- Experimental metrics include visual inspection, explained variance, atom stability, and behavioral prediction performance.  
- Results: Smooth-SODL yields more interpretable, spatially structured regions than SODL and CanICA.  
- Learning curves show a 14× advantage for Smooth-SODL in small-data regimes, with comparable performance as dataset size grows.  
- Mathematical formulation is sound, with clear justification for the Laplacian penalty and convergence guarantees.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and reproducible, with helpful figures and supplementary technical details.  
- Originality: non-trivial extension of online dictionary learning; integrating Laplacian smoothing in this setting is novel for neuroimaging.  
- Significance: relevant to large-scale neuroimaging; improves interpretability and behavioral prediction while scaling to HCP-sized data.  
- Strengths: strong technical execution, thorough experimental validation, practical efficiency, and useful hyperparameter guidance.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of Laplacian penalty limitations for non–piecewise-smooth brain structures and lack of direct comparison with TV-ℓ1 methods.  
- Minor clarity/completeness issue: some computational complexity details (e.g., exact FISTA iterations) could be clarified.  
- Acceptance arguments: meaningful ML and neuroimaging contribution, solid results, scalability, and practical impact.  
- Rejection arguments: novelty could be strengthened with more comparisons to other structured dictionary methods and deeper analysis of Laplacian limits.
