- Summary: Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online multivariate dictionary-learning method using L1 sparsity and a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce sparse, piecewise-smooth, compact brain-image components and shows improved performance on a large public dataset.

- Relation to prior work: Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning to incorporate spatial structure and compares to CanICA and SODL.

- Strength — spatial structure: Introduces an effective way to incorporate spatial smoothness/compactness critical for brain imaging.

- Strength — interpretability: Produces more interpretable dictionary atoms useful for understanding brain organization.

- Strength — performance: Outperforms baselines on explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction in experiments.

- Weakness — runtime: Computational cost remains significantly higher than standard SODL despite claimed efficiency.

- Weakness — hyper-parameter selection: Lacks a clear strategy for choosing optimal hyper-parameters.

- Weakness — limited evaluation: Experiments are restricted to a single dataset, limiting generalizability.

- Pro acceptance — novelty: Novel and important incorporation of spatial structure for brain imaging dictionary learning.

- Pro acceptance — empirical support: Demonstrates effectiveness on a large public dataset with favorable comparisons.

- Pro acceptance — impact: Potential to improve interpretability and downstream behavioral prediction.

- Con acceptance — scalability: Higher computational complexity may hinder large-scale use.

- Con acceptance — usability: No clear hyper-parameter tuning guidance for practitioners.

- Con acceptance — generality: Single-dataset evaluation may not represent diverse brain imaging scenarios.

- Quality: Rated 8/10 — well-written with clear explanations but needs more complexity analysis and tuning details.

- Clarity: Rated 9/10 — organized and clear, though some derivations may be hard for non-experts.

- Originality: Rated 9/10 — novel spatial extension of existing methods, though building on prior work.

- Significance: Rated 9/10 — potentially impactful for brain imaging, contingent on broader evaluation.
