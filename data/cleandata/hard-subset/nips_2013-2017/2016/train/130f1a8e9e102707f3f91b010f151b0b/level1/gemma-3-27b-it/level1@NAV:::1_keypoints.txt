- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning combining L1 sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to learn spatially coherent brain-region “blobs.”  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning to handle large-scale neuroimaging and inter-subject variability.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limitations of ROI-based analyses and scalability issues of ICA/PCA on large datasets.  
- Technical contribution (adding Laplacian regularization) is reasonable and potentially valuable.  
- Demonstrates scalability with only modest runtime increase versus baseline online dictionary learning.  
- Experiments on HCP show qualitative improvements in interpretability and competitive behavioral prediction performance.  
- Provides a detailed and useful discussion of hyperparameter tuning and convergence considerations.  
- Comparison to CanICA is superficial despite CanICA’s stronger behavioral prediction performance.  
- Lacks in-depth analysis of why learned representations differ from strong baselines (e.g., representation-level comparison).  
- Visual evidence for benefits of Laplacian regularization is not fully convincing; quantitative measures of smoothness/compactness are missing.  
- Trade-off of spatial regularization in data-rich regimes is acknowledged but not thoroughly explored; learning curves are limited.  
- Related work section is comprehensive but reads like a list rather than a critical, synthetic discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, technically sound and scalable method, promising large-scale empirical results, solid practical discussion.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient baseline comparison, missing quantitative structural evaluation, unclear benefit of spatial regularization with abundant data, weak critical synthesis of related work.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept; addressing the empirical analysis and comparisons would substantially improve impact.
