- Introduces Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness in brain imaging components.  
- Produces blob-like, neuroanatomically plausible components compared to sparsity-only methods.  
- Offers a scalable, online alternative to batch structured-penalty methods (e.g., TV-l1).  
- Evaluated comprehensively on a large public dataset (HCP) against strong baselines (SODL, CanICA).  
- Experiments assess visual quality, explained variance, stability, and downstream behavioral prediction.  
- Finds the smoothness prior is especially beneficial in low-data regimes.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and organized.  
- Contribution is incremental—combines existing online DL and Laplacian regularization techniques.  
- Hyperparameter selection (especially smoothness γ) is delicate and needs clearer guidance or an adaptive scheme.  
- FFT-based dictionary update is only in the supplement; a brief main-text clarification of when it helps would improve practicality.  
- Overall a strong, technically sound, and practically useful contribution to scalable neuroimaging decomposition.
