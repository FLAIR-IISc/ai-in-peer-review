- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method with a Laplacian penalty to produce sparse, spatially smooth, interpretable brain components (extension of Mairal et al. 2010).  
- Introduces a Laplacian smoothness penalty that yields coherent, blob-like dictionary atoms versus scattered patterns from L1 sparsity alone.  
- Derives an efficient block-coordinate descent online algorithm with a FISTA-based dictionary update, adding only modest computational cost.  
- Positions the method as a scalable alternative to batch structured decomposition (e.g., TV) and a more interpretable option than PCA/ICA.  
- Formulation is technically sound: objective combines data fidelity, code penalty, atom sparsity, and Laplacian smoothness; derivations follow established framework.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: uses large HCP dataset, compares to SODL and CanICA, and measures qualitative visuals, explained variance, stability, and downstream predictive performance.  
- Learning curves (Table 1) strongly support the value of the spatial regularizer, especially in low-data regimes.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with effective figures (notably Figure 2) and helpful supplementary material; minor suggestion to expand derivation of Equation (7).  
- Originality lies in integrating Laplacian regularization into an online, scalable dictionary-learning framework, distinguishing it from prior structured and batch methods.  
- Significance is high for neuroimaging and spatio-temporal data analysis given growing dataset sizes and planned Nilearn inclusion.  
- Strengths: addresses an important practical problem, presents a novel and efficient algorithm, offers comprehensive validation, and is clearly written.  
- Weaknesses: introduces additional hyperparameter (Laplacian weight γ) requiring cross-validation; lacks discussion comparing Laplacian to other priors (e.g., Total Variation).
