- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method combining sparsity with a Laplacian spatial penalty to learn structured brain-image atoms.  
- Demonstrates improvements over L1-regularized dictionary learning and other state-of-the-art methods on a large public neuroimaging dataset.  
- Clear formulation and derivation of the optimization problem and algorithm.  
- Well-organized paper with thorough related-work discussion and potential applications.  
- Experiments are well-designed and results are clearly presented with helpful figures and tables.  
- Novelty: integrates spatial structure into online sparse dictionary learning for neuroimaging.  
- Potential significance: could improve brain-region identification and understanding of neural mechanisms.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with ML and neuroimaging, which may hinder non-experts.  
- Lacks sufficient implementation details (hyperparameter choices, computational complexity).  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of method limitations and directions for future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong experimental validation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: accessibility issues for non-experts, missing implementation/complexity details, and limited discussion of limitations.
