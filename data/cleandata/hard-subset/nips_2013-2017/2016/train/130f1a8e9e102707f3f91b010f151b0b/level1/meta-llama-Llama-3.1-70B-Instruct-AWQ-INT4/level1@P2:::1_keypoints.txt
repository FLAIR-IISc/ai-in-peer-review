- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial structure in brain-image dictionary atoms.
- Extends standard online dictionary-learning to better handle inter-subject variability in brain imaging data.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, motivating introduction and concise explanations of contributions and setup.
- Technical content is sound, including a detailed optimization algorithm and convergence guarantees.
- Introducing a Laplacian penalty is a novel idea that improves interpretability of extracted components.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using a large public dataset and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
- Results show Smooth-SODL outperforms Canonical ICA and sparse online dictionary-learning by producing structured, denoised dictionaries that capture inter-subject variability.
- Paper is well-organized with a thorough related-work discussion and clear distinction from existing methods.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, stating the paper meets NIPS criteria.
