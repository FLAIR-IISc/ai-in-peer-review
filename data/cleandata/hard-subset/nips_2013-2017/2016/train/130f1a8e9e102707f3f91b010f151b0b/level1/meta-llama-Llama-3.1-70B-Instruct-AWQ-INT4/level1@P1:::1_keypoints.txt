- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method combining sparsity and Laplacian penalties to extract structured, sparse brain image components.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over L1-regularized dictionary learning and state-of-the-art models on a large public dataset.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning; reduces to that method when Laplacian penalty is zero.  
- Novelty lies in introducing a Laplacian spatial regularizer to impose structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental support for claims.  
- Provides a concise study of hyper-parameter impacts and characterizes an optimality regime.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of method limitations and future research directions.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, but some paragraphs are long and some sentences are wordy.  
- Could better clarify distinctions from prior approaches and expand on the method’s novelty relative to past work.  
- Significant for neuroimaging due to potential improvements in interpretability and accuracy, but broader impact discussion is limited.  
- Pros: novel method, demonstrated effectiveness on large dataset, potential practical value.  
- Cons: needs more limitations/future work, clearer differentiation from prior methods, and improved readability in places.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing limitations, clarity, and comparison to previous work.
