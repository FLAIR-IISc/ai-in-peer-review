- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with l1-constrained atoms and a Laplacian/Sobolev penalty; codes obtained by ridge regression.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding Laplacian regularization in the dictionary update, solved with proximal-gradient (FISTA) and projection, claiming small overhead.  
- Empirical (HCP task fMRI, n=500, p≈260k, k=40): atoms are smoother and more blob-like than SODL.  
- Empirical: improved stability/reproducibility across splits compared to SODL and CanICA.  
- Empirical: explained variance lower than CanICA but higher than SODL.  
- Empirical: behavioral prediction better than SODL and thresholded CanICA but worse than CanICA.  
- Runtime: reported ≈3× SODL and faster than CanICA on a single CPU, but implementation/measurement details are sparse.  
- Positions method relative to prior work: builds on online DL and relates to GraphNet, structured sparse PCA, and TV-l1 neuroimaging methods.  
- Novelty: incremental but meaningful—scalable integration of Laplacian structure into online BCD with convergence guarantees.  
- Formulation: objective and per-atom subproblem are standard and convex; signed variability can be handled via signed codes despite nonnegative atom constraints.  
- Optimization: codes solved efficiently via SVD; dictionary update is smooth quadratic + Laplacian handled by proximal-gradient with projection.  
- Lipschitz bound L=1+γ̃·||L|| is reasonable but derivation assumes specific discretization/boundary conditions that need clarification.  
- Claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations suffice to reach 1e-6 accuracy is unlikely; few iterations may suffice in practice but not to that tolerance.  
- Convergence: inherits Mairal et al.’s guarantees, but unclear whether γ_t scheduling in Eq.9 is actually used in experiments.  
- Complexity: Laplacian gradient is linear in p and compatible with large p, but reported runtime improvements require details (epochs, stopping, FFT) to be credible.  
- Experiments: use appropriate metrics (EV, Hungarian stability, predictive R2) but lack confidence intervals, statistical tests, and some evaluation specifics (EV computation, tCanICA threshold, run counts).  
- Clarity: generally understandable but contains typos, section inconsistencies, and missing algorithmic details (epochs T, mini-batch size rationale, stopping criteria, schedules).  
- Hyperparameter tuning: useful section but refers to missing figures and lacks a concise reproducible protocol; “parameter-free” BCD claim is misleading.  
- Significance: method improves interpretability and stability for large-scale neuroimaging ROI discovery, though empirical scope is limited to one task contrast.  
- Pros: practical, scalable extension of online DL with modest overhead, improved interpretability/stability, and potential for widespread use if open-sourced.  
- Cons: limited experimental breadth, missing methodological and implementation details, optimistic claims (FISTA iterations, runtime), lack of uncertainty quantification and ablations, and γ scheduling inconsistencies.  
- Suggested improvements: provide full experimental details (passes, stopping, hardware, FFT), report per-epoch wall-clock times, add error bars and statistical tests, and include small-scale TV-l1/structured baseline comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify Laplacian discretization and Lipschitz derivation, correct/rephrase FISTA convergence claims, state whether γ_t/α_t schedules were used, and add ablation studies (ridge vs l1 codes, nonnegativity, k).  
- Overall: solid incremental contribution addressing a practical gap in scalable structured dictionary learning; would be stronger with improved experimental rigor and clarity.
