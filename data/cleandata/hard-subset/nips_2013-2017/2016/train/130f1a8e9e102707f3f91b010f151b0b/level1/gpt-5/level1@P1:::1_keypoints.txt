- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for multi-subject brain images with sparse atoms and Laplacian (Sobolev) spatial smoothness, codes via ridge regression.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by replacing Euclidean projection with a proximal quadratic-plus-Laplacian update solved by FISTA in a block-coordinate scheme, claiming near-linear scalability and ~2–3× runtime over vanilla SODL.  
- Situates work among TV-ℓ1/GraphNet structured neuroimaging methods and scalable online DL literature; novelty is integrating Laplacian priors into an online, large-scale DL framework with practical heuristics.  
- Empirically on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈2.6e5, k=40) shows more spatially structured and reproducible maps than SODL/CanICA, competitive explained variance and behavioral prediction, and reasonable runtimes.  
- Strength: clear, practically motivated objective to learn population-level sparse, spatially coherent, scalable ROIs.  
- Strength: modeling choice (Laplacian smoothness + nonnegativity/simplex) trades TV brittleness for simpler proximal steps and interpretability.  
- Strength: scalable algorithm that preserves online/streaming nature with simple ridge code updates and atom-wise separability.  
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation on a large public dataset with multiple metrics and a learning-curve analysis.  
- Strength: provides practical hyperparameter and implementation guidance (mini-batches, projections, tuning ranges).  
- Weakness: convergence claims with inexact inner solves are unclear—need formal conditions or tempered claims about convergence with 1–2 FISTA iterations.  
- Weakness: time-varying γ to control Lipschitz constants changes objective over time—needs clarification of limiting objective and empirical effect of annealing.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to structured baselines (TV-ℓ1/GraphNet) even on smaller subsets to justify Laplacian choice.  
- Weakness: design choice to enforce sparsity on atoms and ridge on codes departs from common practice; ablation (ℓ1 vs ridge codes, atom constraints) is needed.  
- Weakness: metric and statistical reporting lack details—matching procedure, CV protocol, confidence intervals, and statistical tests are not fully described.  
- Weakness: Lipschitz bound depends on discretization/boundary conditions; paper should specify boundary conditions, voxel spacing, masking effects.  
- Weakness: claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e-6 accuracy is implausible; provide stopping criteria and empirical optimality gaps.  
- Quality: algorithmic development is sound but convergence discussion must be tightened for inexact/time-varying updates; experiments are substantial but need stronger baselines and ablations.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized with actionable algorithmic details, but fix typos and be explicit about simplex/nonnegativity effects.  
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—main contribution is computational/practical integration of Laplacian regularization into online DL.  
- Significance: useful for neuroimaging and large-scale factorization; moderate methodological novelty but valuable empirical study and scalability.  
- Suggestions: provide formal/informal convergence conditions for inexact updates, compare vs TV-ℓ1/GraphNet on subsets, add ablations (code/atom sparsity, nonnegativity), report error bars/statistics, clarify γ schedule, and discuss memory/implementation details.  
- Pros: scalable with small overhead, more interpretable/stable brain maps, strong large-scale empirical validation, likely adoptable implementation (Nilearn).  
- Cons: limited theoretical clarity on inexact/time-varying updates, missing structured baselines and ablations, incomplete uncertainty/protocol reporting.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (reviewer confidence: medium).
