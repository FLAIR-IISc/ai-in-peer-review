**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for obtaining decompositions of brain images with structured and sparse components. The method, called Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), combines a sparsity-inducing penalty with a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a large public dataset, showing improvements over traditional `1-regularized dictionary learning and state-of-the-art models. The paper also provides a concise study of the impact of hyper-parameter selection on the problem and describes the optimality regime based on relevant criteria.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The proposed method builds upon the online dictionary-learning framework of Mairal et al. (2010), which is a special case of the Smooth-SODL model when the Laplacian regularization parameter is set to zero. The authors also compare their method to other state-of-the-art models, including Canonical ICA (CanICA) and sparse online dictionary-learning (SODL). The paper's contribution is the introduction of a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms, which is not present in previous online dictionary-learning methods.
