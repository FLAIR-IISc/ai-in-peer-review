- Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary-learning method adding a Laplacian penalty to enforce piecewise-smooth, compact atoms for brain imaging.
- Extends SODL to balance sparsity and spatial structure, improving interpretability and denoising.
- Maintains scalability for large datasets (suitable for HCP), with an acceptable ~3x slowdown vs SODL.
- Demonstrates improved interpretability, stability, and predictive power vs SODL and CanICA on HCP task-fMRI.
- Provides detailed hyperparameter tuning analysis and computational efficiency evaluation.
- Technical contribution: well-motivated Laplacian penalty that meaningfully advances dictionary learning for neuroimaging.
- Scalability: retains online efficiency, making it practical for large-scale data.
- Experimental rigor: extensive comparisons across multiple metrics (explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction).
- Interpretability: qualitative results show more structured and interpretable atoms than competing methods.
- Reproducibility: sufficient algorithmic detail and planned Nilearn integration to facilitate adoption.
- Clarity concern: paper is dense and occasionally hard to follow, especially for readers new to dictionary learning.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: method requires careful tuning of multiple hyperparameters, limiting ease of use.
- Limited comparisons: benchmarks focus on SODL and CanICA; lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art (e.g., deep learning) methods.
- Behavioral prediction issue: outperforms SODL and tCanICA but underperforms CanICA on behavioral prediction without clear explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant neuroimaging problem, shows clear empirical advantages, and is well-suited to large datasets.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity, reliance on tuning, and narrower comparative evaluation weaken the submission.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing meaningful contribution and practical impact despite noted weaknesses.
