- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, sparse brain maps scalable to large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Integrates spatial structure into Mairal et al.’s online dictionary learning framework, bridging unstructured scalable methods and structured batch methods.  
- Laplacian penalty (l2 gradient) complements l1 sparsity to encourage blob-like, interpretable components.  
- Technical derivation is sound: dictionary update framed as a GraphNet-like denoising problem solvable with FISTA.  
- Shows only a modest runtime increase (2–3×) versus standard SODL, retaining online scalability and convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: uses large HCP dataset (N=500) and compares to SODL and CanICA with multiple metrics.  
- Learning curve results convincingly show the Laplacian regularizer’s benefit in low-to-medium sample regimes.  
- Qualitative maps are visually cleaner and more anatomically plausible than competing methods.  
- Behavioral prediction: Smooth-SODL beats other interpretable models but dense CanICA achieves the best overall predictive performance, highlighting a trade-off.  
- Weakness: choice of Laplacian over TV not fully justified; TV may better encourage piecewise-constant regions but is non-smooth/slower.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter guidance is limited; data-adaptive alpha needs more justification and practical advice for selecting gamma (e.g., SNR or spatial scale heuristics).  
- Weakness: algorithmic derivation (dictionary update term) is compressed and could use clearer intuition for non-experts.  
- Weakness: FFT-based acceleration mentioned but only in supplement; main text should state when it applies and its speed benefits.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — novel, scalable, technically solid, and well-validated; minor clarifications on prior choice and trade-offs would improve the paper.
