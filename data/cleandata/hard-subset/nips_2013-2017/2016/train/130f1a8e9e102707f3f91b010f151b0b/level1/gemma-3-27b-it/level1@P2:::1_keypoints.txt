- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning for brain images combining L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness to produce blob-like brain regions.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning to handle structured sparsity for neuroimaging.  
- Demonstrates promising results on large HCP fMRI data with comparisons to CanICA and SODL.  
- Technically sound: L1 + Laplacian is reasonable and theoretically justified for spatial smoothness.  
- Computational cost increases by a factor of ~2–3 compared to baseline methods.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided, but discussion of the Lipschitz constant and its impact is brief and unclear.  
- Experiments well-designed: relevant baselines, large dataset, and a strong ablation study showing Laplacian impact.  
- Practical considerations and hyperparameter tuning guidance are detailed and helpful.  
- Clarity issues: mathematical formulations and notation may be hard for non-experts; transitions and some algorithmic explanations (e.g., BCD) are terse.  
- Figures need clearer labels and explanations; intro discussion of PCA/ICA relationships is meandering.  
- Originality is incremental—combining L1 and Laplacian is not new—but applying it in an online multivariate neuroimaging context is a valuable contribution.  
- Significance is high: scalable to large datasets, captures inter-subject variability, and shows potential for behavioral prediction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid technical extension, better performance on a large public dataset, scalability, and thorough evaluations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity could be improved, originality is not groundbreaking, and convergence discussion is limited.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid contribution with strong experiments and practical impact despite some clarity and novelty limitations.
