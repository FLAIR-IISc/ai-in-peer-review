- Proposes an online multivariate dictionary learning method combining l1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness to decompose brain images into structured “blob” atoms.  
- Extends prior online dictionary learning (e.g., Mairal et al. 2010) to handle inter-subject variability in neuroimaging.  
- Positions work between sparse decomposition and spatially structured approaches, relating to ROI, clustering, ICA/PCA, and structured sparsity literature.  
- Novelty: first efficient integration of l1 and Laplacian penalties in an online dictionary learning framework for large datasets.  
- Scalability: designed for large-scale neuroimaging data via an online learning approach.  
- Theoretical soundness: clear mathematical formulation with discussed convergence properties and reasonable algorithm derivations.  
- Experimental validation: HCP experiments show improved explained variance and predictive power over SODL and CanICA, especially early in learning and for behavioral prediction.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and clearly motivates contributions and experimental setup.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: tuning α, γ, τ is challenging; α is data-adaptive but γ requires cross-validation and the sensitivity analysis is limited.  
- Computational cost: method is ~3x slower than base SODL, likely due to the Laplacian term, without detailed bottleneck analysis.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art: comparisons (e.g., to CanICA/tCanICA) are incomplete; more thorough benchmarking against ROI and spatial clustering methods is needed.  
- Clarity of Laplacian penalty: mathematical definition present but intuitive impact on atoms needs clearer illustrative explanation.  
- Lack of ablation: no study isolating contributions of l1 vs. Laplacian penalties to demonstrate their individual effects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, scalable, experimentally promising, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: higher computational cost, complex hyperparameter tuning, and insufficient comparative analysis.  
- Recommendation and required revisions: accept with minor revisions to analyze computational cost and optimizations, expand hyperparameter sensitivity and guidelines, strengthen comparisons including thresholded CanICA, and add an ablation study.
