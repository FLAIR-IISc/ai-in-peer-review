- Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially coherent ("blob-like") atoms for brain imaging.
- The method incurs only a modest 2–3× computational slowdown, making it scalable to large datasets (e.g., HCP, 500 subjects).
- Produces more interpretable brain regions and better captures inter-subject variability across small, medium, and large datasets.
- The mathematical formulation and algorithmic derivation are technically sound and clearly presented.
- Convergence analysis is well-supported by theoretical guarantees from prior literature.
- Novel combination of online learning and spatial structure that is faster than previous structured approaches.
- Thorough experimental validation with learning curves and visualizations; implementation provided in Nilearn.
- Lacks systematic comparison with alternative spatial penalties (e.g., total variation) to justify the Laplacian choice.
- Limited analysis of method limitations and failure modes, e.g., when regions are not spatially smooth (pathologies).
- Insufficient comparison with other recent structured dictionary-learning methods (e.g., Jenatton et al., Varoquaux et al.).
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is not thoroughly explored beyond selected figures.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, computational advantage, strong experiments, practical relevance, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparative analysis, under-discussed limitations, some claims need stronger empirical support.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution likely to be useful in neuroimaging; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add comparisons and discuss limitations.
