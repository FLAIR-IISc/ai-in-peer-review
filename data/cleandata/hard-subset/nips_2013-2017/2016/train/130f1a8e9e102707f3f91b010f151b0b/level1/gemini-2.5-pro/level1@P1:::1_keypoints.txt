- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method that adds a Laplacian spatial-smoothness prior to produce sparse, blob-like brain image atoms.  
- Extends Mairal et al.'s scalable online dictionary learning by incorporating spatial regularization and solving the dictionary update as a GraphNet denoising problem.  
- Uses a block-coordinate descent scheme within the online learning paradigm and solves the convex subproblem with first-order methods like FISTA.  
- Positions the work within neuroimaging dictionary learning, addressing noisy, hard-to-interpret maps from standard sparse models.  
- Argues computational advantage over prior spatially structured methods (e.g., TV-based) that are typically slower and batch-mode.  
- Significance/practicality: makes structured dictionary learning feasible at scale (e.g., HCP) with only 2–3× slowdown versus unstructured methods.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: compares against SODL and CanICA on runtime, interpretability, held-out explained variance, stability, and downstream prediction.  
- Key experimental result: spatial regularizer yields dramatic benefits in low-data regimes (up to 14× explained-variance improvement over SODL).  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written, connects to GraphNet literature, and promises public code in Nilearn.  
- Technical novelty is incremental: core components (online BCD, Laplacian regularization, FISTA) are established; main contribution is their synthesis and implementation.  
- Insufficient discussion of regularizer choice: little analysis of Laplacian versus alternatives like Total Variation from a modeling/anatomical perspective.  
- Hyper-parameter sensitivity: model has multiple key parameters (α, γ, τ); tuning (especially τ and γ) is computationally demanding despite an adaptive α.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important domain problem, provides a scalable structured algorithm, strong empirical evidence, and will release code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: components are not novel, Laplacian choice is primarily for convenience, and performance depends on careful hyper-parameter tuning.  
- Recommendation: reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on practical impact, empirical validation, and reproducibility.
