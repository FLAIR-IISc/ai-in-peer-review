- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian/Sobolev penalty to enforce piecewise-smooth, compact spatial atoms.
- Maintains online scalability of Mairal et al. (2010) while improving interpretability and capturing inter-subject variability across dataset sizes.
- Strong technical quality: well-motivated formulation, rigorous algorithmic development, convergence guarantees, and practical implementation details including hyperparameter tuning.
- Comprehensive experiments on HCP fMRI data demonstrating improved interpretability and denoising over SODL and CanICA.
- Quantitative improvements: greater spatial structure and stability (0.4 vs. 0.1 for CanICA) with comparable predictive performance on behavioral variables.
- Clarity is excellent: well-organized manuscript, clear method/algorithm explanations, effective figures, and proper related-work contextualization.
- Originality: first integration of spatial regularization with online dictionary learning while preserving computational efficiency.
- Significance: addresses a key neuroimaging challenge—extracting interpretable, spatially coherent brain regions from large-scale data without predefined ROIs.
- Primary weakness: limited theoretical discussion of how the Laplacian penalty interacts with sparsity and noted hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Practical impact enhanced by an implementation in Nilearn and strong fit for a NIPS submission.
