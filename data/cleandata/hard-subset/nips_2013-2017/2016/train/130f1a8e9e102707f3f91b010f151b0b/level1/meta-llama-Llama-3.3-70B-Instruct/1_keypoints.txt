- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a novel multivariate online dictionary-learning method for decomposing brain images into structured and sparse components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Produces decompositions that better delineate brain structures and capture inter-subject variability than state-of-the-art models.  
- Demonstrates improved interpretability and denoising of dictionaries across small, medium, and large-scale regimes.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and description of contributions.  
- Method is thoroughly described and experiments are well-designed and extensively evaluated on a large public dataset.  
- Provides a concise and useful study of hyper-parameter selection impact for practitioners.  
- Strength: novel and effective approach addressing lack of spatial structure in existing online dictionary-learning methods.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation showing superiority over competing models.  
- Strength: practical hyper-parameter analysis.  
- Weakness: computational complexity may be a concern for very large datasets.  
- Weakness: method requires careful hyper-parameter tuning in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other spatial-structure methods (e.g., TV-1 minimization), limiting contextual evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, given novelty, strong empirical results, and useful hyper-parameter study.
