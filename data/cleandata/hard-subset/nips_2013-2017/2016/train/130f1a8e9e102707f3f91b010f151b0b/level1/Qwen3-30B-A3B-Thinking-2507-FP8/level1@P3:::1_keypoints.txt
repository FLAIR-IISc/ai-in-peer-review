- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to learn spatially coherent brain regions from inter-subject variability.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and structured dictionary learning literature, adapting structure to a scalable online framework.

Strengths:
- Technical: non-trivial algorithmic extension that preserves online nature and includes a solid convergence proof.  
- Scalability: only 2–3× slower than standard SODL, making it practical for large datasets like HCP.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments vs. CanICA and SODL across multiple metrics, with strong visual interpretability and better performance with limited data.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written paper, effective figures, and detailed supplementary materials.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical justification: lacks rigorous explanation for why the Laplacian penalty specifically yields better brain-region extraction.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: limited practical guidance for choosing (γ, τ); reliance on k-fold CV may be impractical at scale.  
- Comparisons: misses direct comparison to other spatially structured but potentially more efficient methods (e.g., Varoquaux et al. 2016).  
- Generalizability: focus is confined to neuroimaging with little discussion of applicability to other modalities or structured data.

Overall assessment:
- Quality: technically sound and empirically convincing, but theory around the penalty could be strengthened.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear and well-organized.  
- Originality: novel and meaningful adaptation of structured dictionary learning to the online setting.  
- Significance: practically important for scalable ROI extraction and interpretability in large-scale neuroimaging.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions to bolster theoretical justification and hyperparameter-selection guidance.

Pros/Cons summary:
- Pro: strong technical contribution, excellent empirical validation, practical scalability, clear presentation.  
- Con: limited theoretical rationale for Laplacian, superficial hyperparameter analysis, narrow application focus.
