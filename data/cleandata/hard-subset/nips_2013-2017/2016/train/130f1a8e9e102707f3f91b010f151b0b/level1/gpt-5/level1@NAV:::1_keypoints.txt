- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — online dictionary learning that enforces sparsity + spatial structure via l1 constraint + Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty; codes by ridge regression; dictionary updates by block-coordinate descent with FISTA.
- Goal/scale: Targets interpretable, blob-like brain components and scales to large n and p.
- Empirical claim: Produces more structured, reproducible atoms than vanilla sparse online DL and denser PCA/ICA baselines on HCP task fMRI Z-maps, with ~2–3× runtime overhead.
- Positioning: Sits between prior TV/GraphNet structured decompositions and scalable online DL, offering a practical synthesis.
- Algorithmic quality: Sound formulation, careful optimization engineering, and convergence inherits standard online DL guarantees (to local optima).
- Practical guidance: Valuable scheduling rules (α_t ~ t^-1/2 and γ scheduling) provided.
- Evaluation scope: Empirically assessed on interpretability, explained variance, stability, prediction, and runtime.
- Missing baselines: Does not compare to strong structured methods (e.g., TV-l1 DL, GraphNet factorization).
- Statistical issues: Lacks uncertainty quantification (no CIs across splits) and shows only one HCP contrast.
- Convergence concern: Claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations achieve 1e-6 accuracy seems optimistic and needs clarification/verification.
- Clarity issues: Typos and imprecisions; Laplacian boundary conditions and neighborhood system not fully specified.
- Modeling choices unmotivated: Choice of simplex/nonnegativity vs. l1-ball not justified w.r.t. negative BOLD effects.
- Originality: Modest but meaningful — combining Laplacian-regularized atoms with online DL and efficient BCD/FISTA updates; γ scheduling insight is novel.
- Significance: Useful contribution for ML neuroimaging (improved interpretability/stability at small-to-medium sample sizes); moderate impact for broader NeurIPS audience.
- Pros: Scalable practical algorithm, improved interpretability/stability with modest runtime cost, solid link to online DL literature, and useful hyperparameter scheduling.
- Cons: Missing strong structured baselines, limited dataset/task diversity and uncertainty reporting, clarity shortcomings, unverified inner-loop convergence, and only modest novelty.
- Suggestions: Add TV-l1/GraphNet baselines, report CIs over multiple splits, detail Laplacian discretization and neighborhood system, ablate simplex vs l1-ball and ridge vs lasso codes, and verify inner-loop tolerances.
