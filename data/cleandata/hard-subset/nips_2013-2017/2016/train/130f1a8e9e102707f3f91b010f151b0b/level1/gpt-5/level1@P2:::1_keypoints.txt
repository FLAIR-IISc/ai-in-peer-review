- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method adding ℓ1-type sparsity and Laplacian (Sobolev/GraphNet) smoothness on atoms with ridge (or ℓ1) coding and BCD + FISTA updates.  
- Positions work as a non-trivial extension of Mairal et al.’s online SODL to incorporate spatial structure for brain images.  
- Experiments on HCP task-fMRI (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) compare to SODL and CanICA, evaluating runtime, explained variance, stability, interpretability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Strength: per-block optimization is convex and well-posed; using accumulated sufficient statistics preserves scalability.  
- Strength: technically sound discussion of Lipschitz constants and practical guidance on γ scaling for step sizes.  
- Strength: empirical overhead is modest (~3× vs SODL) to gain spatial structure, and runtime comparisons include CanICA.  
- Strength: experiments include qualitative atom assessment, train/test EV, cross-split stability with Hungarian matching, and behavioral prediction.  
- Weakness: convergence claims rely on exact subproblem solves from prior work, but practical method uses 1–2 FISTA steps and time-varying γ without proven guarantees.  
- Weakness: Laplacian enforces smoothness but not edge preservation; no quantitative comparison to TV-regularized alternatives known to yield sharper ROIs.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection is heuristic and under-specified—α schedule, γ/τ search ranges, CV protocol, and sensitivity analyses are missing.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to one HCP contrast and k=40, lacking ablations on k, Laplacian boundary conditions, non-negativity vs signed atoms, and additional datasets.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized with explicit algorithms, though minor typos and notational slips exist.  
- Clarity: need clearer exposition of constraint set C (ℓ1 ball vs simplex), neuroscientific implications of non-negativity, and implementation/boundary conditions for 3D Laplacian.  
- Clarity: figures informative but would benefit from quantitative stability and EV curves with confidence intervals.  
- Originality: idea is incremental conceptually but the algorithmic integration of spatial smoothness into an online scalable solver is practically valuable.  
- Originality: choice of Laplacian (vs TV) is a design decision; explicit comparison to TV would clarify novelty.  
- Significance: addresses a relevant problem—interpretable, reproducible ROIs at scale—and could have substantial community impact if released (e.g., in Nilearn).  
- Significance: Smooth-SODL improves stability and interpretability vs SODL, matches asymptotic EV, and yields competitive behavioral prediction, though EV lags PCA/ICA.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical, scalable algorithm; empirical evidence of improved interpretability/stability; clear positioning and runtime benchmarking.  
- Cons for acceptance: lack of theory for inexact BCD with time-varying γ; missing TV and other structured baselines; limited experimental scope and underspecified hyperparameter search.  
- Suggestions: provide convergence conditions or temper claims, add a TV-regularized baseline (e.g., FAASTA), detail hyperparameter search/CV and Laplacian boundary conditions, run ablations on k and a second dataset/contrast, and report effects of non-negativity.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically useful, and moderately novel; reviewer leans toward weak accept pending tempered convergence claims and stronger experimental comparisons.
