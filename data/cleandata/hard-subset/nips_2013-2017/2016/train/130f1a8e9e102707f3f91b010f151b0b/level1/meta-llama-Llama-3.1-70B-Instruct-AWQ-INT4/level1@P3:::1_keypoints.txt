- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a multivariate online dictionary-learning method adding a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on brain-image dictionary atoms (extends Mairal et al. 2010).  
- Demonstrated on a large public dataset with improved interpretability and better capture of inter-subject variability versus L1-regularized dictionary learning.  
- Novel extension: introducing a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatially structured atoms.  
- Produces more interpretable dictionaries that capture inter-subject variability better than traditional L1 methods.  
- Designed for scalability to arbitrarily large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the algorithm.  
- Slower than traditional L1-regularized dictionary learning, raising computational concerns for very large datasets.  
- Hyper-parameter tuning remains difficult and no clear solution is provided.  
- Limited empirical comparison: lacks benchmarks against other state-of-the-art neuroimaging methods beyond L1 DL and CanICA.  
- Significant and potentially widely adoptable contribution for interpretable, scalable neuroimaging analysis.  
- Well-written and organized, with clear mathematical and algorithmic exposition and thorough evaluation.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, practical, theoretically grounded method with adoption potential.  
- Con acceptance: computational cost, tuning difficulties, and incomplete benchmarking weaken the submission.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, hyper-parameter tuning, and expand comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.
