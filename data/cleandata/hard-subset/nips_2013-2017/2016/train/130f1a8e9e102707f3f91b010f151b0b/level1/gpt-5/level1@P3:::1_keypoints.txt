- Proposed Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with ℓ1/simplex atom constraints and a Laplacian quadratic to produce spatially sparse, smooth neuroimaging atoms.  
- Key novelty: integrating a Laplacian prior into Mairal et al.’s online DL framework to get structured atoms with modest overhead (~2–3× SODL).  
- Sits between classic sparse DL and spatially structured decompositions (TV-ℓ1, structured sparse PCA, GraphNet), trading piecewise-constant edges for speed.  
- Empirically: improved spatial interpretability and stability vs SODL, higher explained variance especially in low-data regimes, competitive behavioral prediction, and reasonable runtime.  
- Correctness: formulation sensible and per-atom subproblem convex with simple projection; online surrogate and block-coordinate updates follow prior work.  
- Ambiguity: claim of “exact” per-atom minimization conflicts with using only 1–2 FISTA iterations—need clarification on warm starts/approximate updates and convergence implications.  
- Ambiguity: Lipschitz constant and γ schedule appear to require γ decay with t, but γ is treated as fixed in tuning—need reconciliation or principled adaptive γ_t.  
- Design choice: using simplex (nonnegativity) constraints may be inappropriate for fMRI contrasts with positive and negative lobes—justify choice and ablate signed vs nonnegative atoms.  
- Empirical fairness: CanICA was run with spatial smoothing while SODL/Smooth-SODL were not, potentially biasing EV and interpretability comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter selection: unclear how τ and γ were chosen (folds, grids, held-out data); sensitivity plots and exact CV procedure are missing.  
- Behavior prediction details missing: unspecified regression model, regularization selection, CV protocol, confound controls, and significance reporting.  
- Clarity: generally clear with helpful pseudocode, but minor typos and LaTeX issues present.  
- Clarify boundary conditions and masking for Laplacian and whether FFT-based updates assume periodic boundaries.  
- Report memory and per-iteration computational complexity (cost of applying Δ, storage of B_t ∈ R^{p×k}).  
- Clarify exact projection operator when combining Laplacian penalty with simplex/ℓ1 constraints (accelerated projected gradient is implied).  
- Originality: modest but meaningful extension—embedding Laplacian in online DL is practical though not conceptually groundbreaking.  
- Significance: practical utility for large neuroimaging cohorts if empirical evidence and fair comparisons are robust.  
- Pros: clear practical algorithm, large-scale application, improved stability/interpretability, solid engineering and implementation plan.  
- Cons: limited novelty, under-specified evaluation fairness and hyperparameter tuning, theoretical claims need tightening, and lacking key ablations.  
- Suggested ablations: isolate effects of Laplacian vs none, nonnegativity vs signed atoms, and ridge vs ℓ1 codes.  
- Suggested experiments: justify or adopt adaptive γ_t, compare to TV-regularized DL on small datasets, equalize preprocessing across methods, and include spatial ICA baselines.  
- Suggested reporting: detail hyperparameter selection, behavior-prediction protocol, confound control, and statistical uncertainty.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — incremental but practically useful conditional on clarifying optimization details and strengthening fair, rigorous evaluations.
