- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method with sparsity plus Laplacian spatial smoothness to identify brain regions using across-subject variability.  
- Extends online SODL to incorporate spatial regularization, enabling large-scale fMRI analysis.  
- Demonstrates on HCP data improved interpretability, reproducibility, and predictive power versus standard SODL and ICA/CanICA.  
- Thoroughly situates the method among prior work (dictionary learning, TV-l1, clustering, CanICA) with a comprehensive related-work discussion.  
- Novelty: first to combine online dictionary learning with Laplacian regularization for scalable brain-region discovery.  
- Scalability: online framework handles high voxel dimensionality and many subjects; runtime comparisons are encouraging.  
- Interpretability: learned atoms are spatially smoother and more interpretable than baselines, supported by visual examples.  
- Empirical validation: experiments cover qualitative assessment, explained variance, stability, and prediction with multiple baselines.  
- Theoretical grounding: links to GraphNet justify the Laplacian penalty and enable efficient optimization; discusses Lipschitz constants and tuning.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well organized.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: α, γ, τ strongly affect results; time-varying α appears ad hoc and needs systematic sensitivity analysis or cross-validation.  
- Unclear guidance on selecting Laplacian γ; the claimed “safe range” lacks concrete criteria.  
- Behavioral prediction is positive but modest (low R²); should analyze which behavioral measures map to atoms.  
- Risk of local optima due to non-convexity; paper should discuss mitigation strategies (e.g., multiple random restarts).  
- Lacks comparison to alternative spatial regularizers (e.g., total variation) to further justify Laplacian choice.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, and empirically validated contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Final recommendation/rating: Accept (minor revisions).
