- Introduces Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian penalty for spatially smooth, compact atoms and ℓ1-type sparsity constraints.  
- Codes computed via ridge regression (option for ℓ1); dictionary update solved by projected FISTA inside online BCD using sufficient statistics.  
- Targets large-scale neuroimaging (p ~ 1e5–1e6 voxels; n up to 1e5) with modest runtime overhead (~3×).  
- On HCP task fMRI (500 subjects, k=40) yields more structured, interpretable atoms and higher split stability than SODL; explained variance close to CanICA.  
- Behavioral prediction: CanICA best, but Smooth-SODL outperforms SODL and thresholded CanICA.  
- Builds on online DL (Mairal et al., Mensch et al.) and connects to GraphNet/TV-ℓ1 structured methods; novelty is integrating spatial structure into efficient online DL.  
- Missing experimental comparison to TV-ℓ1/GraphNet-based structured dictionary learning.  
- Algorithmically reasonable: per-atom subproblem is strongly convex and amenable to projected first-order updates; sufficient-statistics and mini-batching are scalable.  
- Theoretical gap: inexact dictionary updates via few FISTA iterations lack a bounded-inexactness convergence argument; 1–2 iterations to 1e-6 accuracy is implausible without clarification.  
- Theory/practice mismatch: Eq. (9) suggests γ should scale with t, but experiments use fixed γ; need reconciliation (e.g., A_t[j,j]/t stabilization or warm starts).  
- FFT-based Laplacian denoising claim unclear: FFT valid only for full grids with periodic BC; need clarity on brain masks, boundary conditions, and whether FFT is used.  
- Constraint choice matters: simplex (non-negativity + ℓ1) may be inappropriate for fMRI contrasts with positive and negative values; report ℓ1-ball results or justify positivity.  
- Empirics strong in scale and relevance but evaluation breadth limited (single dataset/task).  
- Missing comparisons to stronger structured baselines (TV-ℓ1/GraphNet) even on smaller data to isolate benefits vs cost.  
- Stability metric under-specified: number of splits, matching protocol, and variance not reported.  
- Behavioral prediction protocol unclear: ensure feature selection and hyperparameter tuning are nested within training folds and report error bars.  
- Visualizations selective; quantitative spatial-compactness measures (Gini, cluster size, perimeter/area) would strengthen claims.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing implementation details (mask/Laplacian construction, voxel spacing, projection algorithm, warm-start strategy); code release in Nilearn is promised.  
- Clarity issues: typos, incomplete references, inconsistent notation for constraint set C (simplex vs ℓ1-ball), and incomplete definitions of metrics/objectives.  
- Originality moderate: incremental novelty by integrating Laplacian regularization into online DL; Laplacian/GraphNet smoothing itself is not new.  
- Significance pragmatic for neuroimaging practitioners if released; methodological advance is incremental for core ML.  
- Pros: addresses scalable structured decompositions, elegant integration with modest overhead, strong large-scale experiment, potential high adoption via Nilearn.  
- Cons: theoretical gaps on inexact updates and γ scheduling, limited structured-baseline comparisons, single-dataset evaluation, and clarity issues.  
- Key author questions requested: details on FISTA warm-starts and stopping criteria; Laplacian definition on brain mask and use of FFT; reconciliation of Eq. (9) with fixed γ; justification for non-negativity vs ℓ1-ball; stability computation details; confirmation of nested feature selection/hyperparameter tuning; add small-scale TV-ℓ1 DL comparison.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending authors addressing convergence/stopping, γ scheduling, stronger baselines, evaluation rigor, and clarity improvements.
