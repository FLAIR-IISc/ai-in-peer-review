- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with sparse, spatially smooth atoms via ℓ1-ball/simplex constraint and Laplacian quadratic penalty.
- Algorithm follows Mairal’s online block-coordinate descent with ridge (or l1) coding and per-atom FISTA solves for projection/denoising subproblems.
- Targets large-scale neuroimaging (p ~ 1e5–1e6, n up to 1e5) and reports modest runtime overhead (~2–3x) versus standard SODL.
- Empirical claims: cleaner, blob-like, more interpretable maps than SODL/ICA, improved explained variance and cross-split stability on HCP task fMRI (n=500, k=40).
- Positions work relative to prior art: extends Mairal’10 online DL, connects to GraphNet smoothness and contrasts with slower TV-ℓ1 atlas methods.
- Strength: tackles an important problem (inter-subject variability) with a scalable structured decomposition useful for population neuroimaging.
- Strength: atom update reduces to a convex smooth quadratic plus indicator, making projected FISTA and linear-time ℓ1/simplex projection reasonable.
- Strength: practical observation that Δ has bounded spectrum (~4D) under grid discretization informs step sizes.
- Strength: reported runtimes and code release in Nilearn support adoption and reproducibility.
- Weakness: time-varying regularization γ_t that decays with t changes optimization target, lacks convergence/consistency analysis and conflicts with persistent spatial structure.
- Weakness: alternative, principled fixes for γ_t (normalizing A_t, rescaling, fixed γ with preconditioning) are not discussed.
- Weakness: claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations suffice to reach 1e-6 accuracy is not credible; iteration counts, stopping criteria, and residuals are missing.
- Weakness: need discussion of inexact BCD convergence if very few inner iterations are used.
- Weakness: constraint choice (simplex enforcing nonnegativity) is strong and unjustified; no ablation vs symmetric ℓ1-ball.
- Weakness: key derivation from global objective to per-atom surrogate is deferred and should be included; boundary conditions and masking details for ∇/Δ are unclear.
- Experimental weakness: missing direct comparisons to TV-ℓ1 structured DL / atlas methods despite citing them.
- Experimental weakness: preprocessing asymmetry—CanICA inputs are smoothed but SODL/Smooth-SODL inputs are not—complicates comparisons.
- Experimental weakness: stability metrics lack confidence intervals and matching/sign-handling protocol details.
- Experimental weakness: behavioral prediction pipeline is underspecified (model type, hyperparameter tuning, nested CV, feature scaling) and may leak if feature selection is not in-fold.
- Experimental weakness: sensitivity analyses and ablations (vary k, τ, γ, ridge vs lasso codes, simplex vs ℓ1) are missing and only one task/k reported.
- Presentation issues: typos, inconsistencies, missing figure panels, incomplete references, and unclear/loose notation (e.g., claims about “global optimum at each iteration”).
- Significance: novelty is incremental (known ingredients) but practically useful for scalable neuroimaging if made robust and reproducible.
- Questions raised: whether γ was fixed or time-varying in final runs and sensitivity to schedule.
- Questions raised: how many FISTA iterations are actually used, stopping rule, backtracking or preconditioning.
- Questions raised: what boundary conditions/masking and units are used for ∇/Δ.
- Questions raised: details of behavioral prediction (models, hyperparameters, nested tuning) and request for TV-ℓ1 baseline and simplex vs ℓ1 ablation.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying γ/inner-solver issues and strengthening experimental comparisons.
