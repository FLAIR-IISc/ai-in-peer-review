- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — online dictionary learning enforcing spatial sparsity and smoothness via an ℓ1-type atom constraint, Laplacian penalty, ridge-coded updates, and per-atom FISTA denoising.  
- Algorithmic change: Adapts Mairal et al.’s online DL framework to include a GraphNet-like smoothing step with a small number of FISTA iterations.  
- Empirical setup: Evaluated on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈2.6e5, k=40) versus SODL and CanICA for runtime, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Claimed novelty: Integration of a Laplacian smoothness penalty into an online, large-scale dictionary learning framework with practical speed/convergence.  
- Strength — practicality: Simple, scalable approach with an accessible implementation pathway (Nilearn).  
- Strength — computation: Only a 2–3× slowdown versus SODL and faster than CanICA in reported experiments.  
- Strength — empirical relevance: Uses large HCP dataset and multiple evaluation criteria (qualitative maps, variance, stability, prediction).  
- Strength — interpretability: Learned atoms are visually cleaner and more neuroanatomically plausible than SODL/ICA components.  
- Strength — stability trade-off: Laplacian regularization improves stability at modest cost to explained variance.  
- Weakness — novelty: Conceptually incremental; similar spatial regularizers (TV, GraphNet) and structured methods exist and need clearer differentiation.  
- Weakness — baselines fairness: SODL and CanICA configurations may be mismatched (code penalties, smoothing/thresholding) and stronger baselines (TV-DL, structured SPCA) are missing.  
- Weakness — objective clarity: Eq.(2) and the online surrogate need clearer definition for finite datasets and expected risk.  
- Weakness — inner solves: Claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e-6 is implausible; inexact solves break the cited convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness — Lipschitz detail: Lipschitz constant depends on discretization and boundary conditions; these implementation details are unspecified.  
- Weakness — adaptive γ schedule: Changing γt/αt over time alters the objective and may invalidate online DL convergence claims without justification.  
- Weakness — positivity ablation: The simplex (positivity + ℓ1) constraint may drive results; the role of nonnegativity is not quantified.  
- Evaluation gap — spatial metrics: Spatial contiguity/compactness not quantified (e.g., connected components, TV, dispersion).  
- Evaluation gap — hyperparams & stats: Hyperparameter grids, selection, sensitivity, error bars, and statistical tests are insufficiently reported.  
- Evaluation gap — behavioral prediction: Prediction model, nested CV, leakage controls, and full result reporting are unclear or incomplete.  
- Evaluation gap — generality: Results shown for one task contrast and one k only; robustness across tasks/k is not assessed.  
- Minor issues: Typos, notation slips, and overstated claims (e.g., global optimality for inexact inner solves).  
- Questions for authors: Clarify SODL code penalty used; exact FISTA stopping criterion and warm-starting; justification for adaptive γ/α schedules; add TV-DL and structured SPCA baselines; provide quantitative compactness and positivity ablation; detail behavioral prediction protocol and report error bars.  
- Pros for acceptance: Practical importance, scalable implementation, solid empirical signs of improved interpretability and stability, likely practitioner adoption.  
- Cons for acceptance: Modest technical novelty, insufficient/possibly unfair baselines, and methodological and evaluation issues needing substantial revision.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — valuable practical contribution but needs stronger baselines, clearer methodology/convergence discussion, and more rigorous quantitative evaluation.
