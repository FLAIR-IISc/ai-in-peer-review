- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension that adds a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for learning brain-region atoms from multi-subject fMRI.  
- Strength: Clearly motivated by limitations of anatomical and ICA/PCA ROI methods and leverages inter-subject variability for principled ROI definition.  
- Strength: Mathematically rigorous formulation with careful derivation of algorithmic extensions and discussion of convergence and complexity.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient—spatial regularization adds only a 2–3× cost compared to much larger costs for alternatives like CanICA.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments on HCP (500 subjects, 260k voxels) with multiple baselines and metrics, showing better spatial structure and performance, especially with limited data.  
- Strength: Practicality—plan to integrate into Nilearn, improving community accessibility.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to other structured dictionary learning methods (e.g., TV-L1) that directly target spatial structure in brain imaging.  
- Weakness: No theoretical analysis explaining why spatial regularization is especially beneficial with limited data—claims are only empirically supported.  
- Weakness: The term “structured” is used ambiguously without a precise definition (connectivity, functional networks, or merely spatial smoothness?).  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter selection relies on k-fold CV without discussing computational cost at large scale and the theoretical basis for the adaptive α choice is unexplained.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of Laplacian approach limitations (performance on heterogeneous populations, bias toward certain spatial shapes, scaling to 10k+ subjects).  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-executed, but would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis and broader methodological comparisons.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized overall, but the Laplacian penalty explanation and the notion of “structured” need clearer exposition; simplex choice not justified.  
- Originality: Novel extension of online dictionary learning for neuroimaging; Laplacian smoothing itself is not new and needs clearer differentiation from prior work.  
- Significance: Addresses an important neuroimaging problem with potential impact for small-sample studies and ROI definition in clinical research.  
- Suggestion: Add comparisons against TV-L1 and related structured dictionary methods to properly position the contribution.  
- Suggestion: Provide theoretical justification for why spatial regularization reduces sample complexity or effective parameter count.  
- Suggestion: Precisely define “structured” in the brain-region context and relate it to known functional organization.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost and feasibility of hyperparameter selection at large scale, and justify adaptive α theoretically.  
- Suggestion: Add a limitations paragraph addressing heterogeneous populations, spatial-shape biases, and scaling to very large cohorts.  
- Suggestion: Include visualizations illustrating the Laplacian penalty’s effect on dictionary atoms to clarify spatial smoothness.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—strong contribution but needs comparisons and stronger theoretical grounding.  
- Pro: Tackles a key ROI problem, is computationally efficient, shows clear empirical improvements, and will be integrated into Nilearn.  
- Con: Missing comparisons to other structured methods, insufficient theory on regularization benefits, ambiguous terminology, and limited limitations discussion.
