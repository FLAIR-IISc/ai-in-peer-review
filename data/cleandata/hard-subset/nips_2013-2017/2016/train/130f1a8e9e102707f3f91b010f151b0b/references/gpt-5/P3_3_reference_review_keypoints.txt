- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method enforcing spatial sparsity and Laplacian smoothness on atoms with ridge-penalized codes for neuroimaging.  
- Builds on Mairal et al.'s online DL, adding an ℓ2 Laplacian penalty on atoms to produce blob-like ROIs at scale.  
- Atom updates use block-coordinate convex quadratic solves with ℓ1-ball/simplex constraints and projected accelerated-gradient (FISTA-like) steps.  
- Codes are updated by ridge regression to promote stability while atoms are spatially structured.  
- Empirical results on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) show improved interpretability, higher split-half stability, faster early learning, and competitive explained variance.  
- Computational overhead is modest (~3× standard SODL) and runtime comparisons are favorable for large cohorts.  
- Conceptual contribution is incremental: extending online DL with Laplacian atom regularization is close to existing GraphNet/structured SPCA/TV-ℓ1 work.  
- Theoretical presentation is underdeveloped: stochastic approximation framing, finite-sample sufficient statistics, and convergence with inexact inner solves need formalization.  
- Reported inner-loop claims (1–2 FISTA iterations to 1e-6) are implausible; iteration counts, tolerances, and stopping criteria must be provided.  
- Convergence justification is weak given inexact solves and time-varying αt, γt; error-control or references are missing.  
- Guidance on γ scaling contradicts experiments: theoretical suggestion vs fixed γ used (10^3–10^4) need reconciliation.  
- Modeling choices lack ablation: effect of nonnegativity/simplex vs ℓ1-ball, ridge codes vs sparse codes, and Laplacian vs TV priors are not evaluated.  
- Evaluation fairness issues: CanICA was run on pre-smoothed data while SODL variants were not, confounding comparisons.  
- Stability/reproducibility reporting is insufficient: details on splits, atom matching, error bars, and multiple initializations are missing.  
- Prediction results are incomplete/biased: only positive R2s reported, no full distribution, significance tests, or confound control.  
- Generalization tests are limited: only one HCP task and one k value presented; broader tasks and ks needed.  
- Clarity problems: typos, formatting issues, incomplete references, and missing implementation details (mask boundary conditions, FFT on irregular domains, preprocessing).  
- Originality is modest but practically useful: integrates spatial atom structure into an online pipeline usable on laptops for neuroimaging practitioners.  
- Significance is domain-focused: useful in practice but limited ML novelty; gains mainly in interpretability/stability rather than prediction.  
- Suggested fixes: formalize optimization and convergence, report inner-loop details, justify/experiment with γ/α choices, run ablations (nonnegativity, ℓ1-coding, TV vs Laplacian), equalize smoothing across methods, expand stability analyses, detail preprocessing, and release code.  
- Pros: scalable, practical algorithm; improved interpretability and stability; solid large-scale experiments and reasonable baselines.  
- Cons: limited novelty vs prior structured methods, theoretical/methodological inconsistencies, missing optimization/hyperparameter details, fairness gaps in evaluation, and lacking TV-based comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject—practically valuable but needs stronger theoretical grounding, fairer/broader evaluation, and clearer exposition before acceptance.
