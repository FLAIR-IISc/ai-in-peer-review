- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian regularization to learn spatially coherent ROIs from population fMRI.  
- Leverages inter-subject variability to define data-driven ROIs, avoiding anatomical priors and activation-based selection.  
- Builds on online dictionary learning, sparse coding, and relates to ICA/PCA, clustering, and graph-based spatial regularization.  
- Novel contribution: combining online dictionary learning with Laplacian smoothing for spatially coherent ROI extraction.  
- Scalable to large datasets (demonstrated on HCP) due to online learning framework.  
- Produces spatially smooth, sparse atoms that are more interpretable than dense ICA components.  
- Empirically validated against CanICA and SODL across explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction metrics, showing consistent improvements.  
- Provides practical guidance on hyperparameter tuning and initialization; implemented in Nilearn.  
- Writing and technical exposition are clear.  
- Computational cost is higher than basic SODL (≈3× slower), which may limit some uses.  
- Results show sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, γ, τ), needing careful tuning and more systematic exploration.  
- Comparison to CanICA may be unfair without exploring CanICA regularization or post-processing variants.  
- Behavioral prediction improvements are modest (low R²), needing deeper analysis linking components to cognition.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited; more rigorous convergence/statistical property results would strengthen the paper.  
- Overall assessment: significant and well-validated contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, hyperparameter sensitivity, CanICA comparison, and behavioral analysis.
