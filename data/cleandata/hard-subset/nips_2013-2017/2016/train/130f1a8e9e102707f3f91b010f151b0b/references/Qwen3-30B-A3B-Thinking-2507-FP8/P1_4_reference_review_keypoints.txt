- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness and define ROIs from inter-subject variability.  
- Addresses limitations of anatomical/manual/clustering ROI methods by learning spatially structured patterns directly from data.  
- Extends sparse online dictionary learning with minimal computational overhead (≈3× slower than SODL) for large-scale neuroimaging.  
- Evaluated on 500 HCP subjects and compared against SODL, CanICA, and PCA, producing more structured and interpretable regions.  
- Uses multiple evaluation metrics (explained variance, stability, reproducibility, prediction) for comprehensive validation.  
- Shows improved prediction of behavioral variables (IQ, vocabulary) using Smooth-SODL features versus baselines.  
- Technically sound: clear formulation, justified optimization, convergence guarantees, and practical hyper-parameter discussion.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of how Laplacian regularization affects statistical properties like efficiency or consistency.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with effective visualizations and precise algorithm descriptions.  
- Minor clarity issue: insufficient explanation of why the Laplacian produces “blob”-like spatial patterns.  
- Novelty: an elegant, scalable combination of spatial structure with online dictionary learning addressing prior computational barriers.  
- Clearly distinct from previous dictionary learning work and appropriately cites related literature.  
- Significant practical contribution for principled, data-driven ROI extraction and dimensionality reduction in neuroimaging.  
- Practical implementation ready for adoption, with planned integration into the Nilearn library.  
- Strengths summarized: clear motivation, computational efficiency, comprehensive evaluation, immediate applicability, and insightful hyperparameter analysis.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited theoretical grounding, limited demonstration of neuroscientific interpretability beyond prediction, unclear hyperparameter selection details, and no comparison to other structured DL methods.  
- Recommendation: accept — high-quality, novel, empirically strong, and practically impactful despite areas for theoretical strengthening.
