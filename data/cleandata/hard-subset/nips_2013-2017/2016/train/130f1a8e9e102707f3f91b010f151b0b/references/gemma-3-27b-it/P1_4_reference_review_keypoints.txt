- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with L1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial smoothness to discover brain regions from population fMRI.  
- Leverages inter-subject variability to define ROIs, moving beyond anatomical or task-based definitions.  
- Scalable online framework handles large voxel counts and many subjects.  
- Laplacian penalty yields spatially smooth, interpretable, blob-like regions versus purely sparse atoms.  
- Empirical validation on the HCP dataset shows improvements in interpretability, stability, explained variance, and prediction versus standard SODL and CanICA.  
- Positions work relative to PCA/ICA, online dictionary learning, structured sparsity, and graph-based methods.  
- Includes practical guidance on hyperparameter tuning for α, γ, τ.  
- Well-written, clear exposition with standard notation and logical organization.  
- The main algorithmic novelty is incremental—combining known components rather than a radical new method.  
- Added Laplacian regularization increases computational cost (~3x slower than standard SODL).  
- Hyperparameter selection appears sensitive and may require extensive cross-validation; interactions between parameters are not fully explored.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent spatially regularized or deep-learning-based decomposition methods.  
- Prediction experiments use simple linear regression; more complex models might better reveal representational strengths/weaknesses.  
- The approach is principled and potentially significant for automated, reproducible ROI definition in neuroimaging.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for deeper analysis of computational trade-offs and discussion of newer related work.
