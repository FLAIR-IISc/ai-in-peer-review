- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian penalty for spatial smoothness and L1/simplex constraints for sparsity/nonnegativity.
- Retains online block-coordinate descent framework: ridge-coded updates and one-by-one atom updates.
- Atom update becomes a quadratic denoising problem solved via projected proximal-gradient (FISTA) with small extra cost versus vanilla online DL.
- Experiments on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) compare Smooth-SODL to SODL and CanICA.
- Results show improved interpretability and split-half stability over SODL, much better small-sample explained variance, and competitive behavioral prediction (CanICA best for prediction).
- Builds on Mairal’s online DL and connects to TV-ℓ1/GraphNet/structured sparse PCA literature; novelty is scaling spatial regularization to online DL.
- Technical soundness: objective is separately convex and Laplacian preserves strong convexity; projected proximal-gradient is appropriate.
- Major theoretical concern: convergence claims are not justified under inexact atom updates, time-varying αt and γt, and early-stopped FISTA.
- Numerical claim issue: assertion that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e−6 accuracy is implausible; actual duality gaps/objective decreases should be reported.
- Confusing scaling/Lipschitz notation: derivation of γ̃ and dependence on At[j,j]/t and Eq. (7) and Lt need clarification.
- Presentation issues: notational slips (max vs min), missing details in algorithms, and typos reduce clarity.
- Empirics: dataset and runtime comparisons are appropriate and informative; stability and EV analyses are useful.
- Empirical shortcomings: limited baselines (no TV-ℓ1/GraphNet decompositions, no ℓ1-coded DL, no NMF) and lack of statistical uncertainty (no error bars).
- Behavioral prediction evaluation is honest, but model selection, cross-validation, and voxel selection details are lacking.
- Clarity: high-level idea is clear and connections to GraphNet helpful, but atom-update derivation, proximal step, and role of sufficient statistics need fuller exposition.
- Originality: incremental but useful—natural combination of spatial prior with online DL rather than a fundamentally new paradigm.
- Significance: practically valuable for neuroimaging adoption (Nilearn), modest but relevant contribution to ML for scalable structured priors.
- Pros: simple scalable extension, large-scale HCP experiments, clear practical benefits (interpretability, stability, small-sample EV), implementation plan for Nilearn.
- Cons: unsupported convergence guarantees, limited baselines, narrow experimental scope (single dataset, fixed k), limited statistical analysis, clarity and numerical-claim issues.
- Suggestions: provide explicit atom-update derivation and Lipschitz scaling, report per-iteration residuals/gaps, fix or justify time-varying regularization, add ablations and baselines, expand experiments and report error bars, detail stability matching.
- Overall: practical and computationally relevant contribution with moderate originality; needs clarifications and broader experiments but is promising.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept.
