- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian regularization to discover spatially coherent brain regions from fMRI.
- Leverages population variability to define regions and encourages smooth, sparse dictionary atoms ("blobs").
- Demonstrated on a large HCP dataset, compared against CanICA and standard SODL with hyperparameter guidance.
- Builds on Mairal et al.'s online dictionary learning and connects to clustering- and ICA/PCA-based region definitions.
- Novelty: first to combine scalable online dictionary learning with spatial Laplacian regularization for neuroimaging.
- Scalability: online approach handles high dimensionality and large datasets, faster than CanICA despite some overhead vs. vanilla SODL.
- Theoretical justification: clear mathematical formulation with discussion of convergence properties.
- Empirical validation: shows improved explained variance, stability, and predictive power; includes a useful ablation on γ.
- Practical guidance: provides advice on hyperparameter selection and initialization, mitigating common dictionary learning issues.
- Writing and presentation: generally clear, well organized, and accessible notation.
- Weakness: only Laplacian regularization explored—no comparison with alternatives like total variation.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, γ, τ) still requires careful tuning and possible cross-validation.
- Weakness: limited qualitative analysis of dictionary atom functional significance and links to known brain networks.
- Weakness: algorithmic clarity could improve—roles of historical statistics A and B are under-explained.
- Weakness: assumption of compact data support is stated but impacts of violations on fMRI are not discussed.
- For acceptance: addresses a hard problem, is novel and theoretically sound, shows strong empirical results, and gives practical tips.
- Against acceptance: lacks exploration of other spatial regularizers, requires challenging hyperparameter tuning, and needs deeper functional interpretation.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable, and significant contribution to neuroimaging; suitable for acceptance.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
- Additional suggestion: add deeper functional analysis of atoms, brief experiments with alternative spatial regularizers, and clearer explanation of A and B in algorithms.
