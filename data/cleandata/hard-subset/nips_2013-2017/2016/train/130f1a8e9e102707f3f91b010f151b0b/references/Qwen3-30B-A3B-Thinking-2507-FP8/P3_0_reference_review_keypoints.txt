- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to learn spatially smooth, sparse brain regions from population neuroimaging data.  
- Addresses need for principled ROI definitions that capture inter-subject variability rather than relying on anatomical segmentation.

Strengths:
- Strong motivation: clearly argues why inter-subject variability should be used to define ROIs.  
- Technical soundness: solid mathematical formulation and clear BCD algorithm with Laplacian regularization.  
- Comprehensive experiments: large-scale HCP evaluation (500 subjects, 260k voxels) with multiple baselines and metrics.  
- Practical impact: ~3x speedup over prior structured methods and planned Nilearn integration to aid adoption.  
- Hyperparameter analysis: provides guidance and identifies a practical “safe range” for key parameters (α, γ, τ).

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Limited theoretical justification for using a Laplacian penalty specifically over other spatial regularizers.  
- Insufficient direct comparisons with other structured dictionary-learning methods (e.g., TV-ℓ1 approaches).  
- Hyperparameter selection treatment is superficial; lacks systematic analysis across datasets and linkage to theoretical requirements.  
- No statistical significance tests reported for performance differences (e.g., explained variance gaps).  
- Possible alternative interpretation: spatial regularization might capture noise patterns rather than meaningful brain structure.

Specific technical concerns:
- Convergence claims lack empirical comparison of convergence rates versus competing methods.  
- Missing quantitative comparison to TV-ℓ1 and related structured methods referenced in the literature.  
- Algorithm complexity not fully analyzed; unclear scaling impact of the Laplacian penalty.  
- Data preprocessing differences (no pre-smoothing for SODL variants vs. CanICA with pre-smoothing) may bias comparisons.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, significant contribution extending online dictionary learning to incorporate spatial structure with minimal overhead and compelling experimental results.  
- Main shortcomings are limited justification for the regularizer choice, incomplete hyperparameter analysis, and lack of comprehensive comparisons.

Recommendation:
- Accept.

Additional suggestions:
- Include direct comparisons with TV-ℓ1 dictionary-learning methods to quantify computational and performance trade-offs.  
- Report statistical significance for reported performance differences.  
- Discuss scenarios where spatial smoothness assumptions may fail and possible adaptations.  
- Add qualitative validation of atoms (e.g., comparison to known functional networks) to bolster interpretability claims.  
- Clarify the connection between the theoretical γ requirement (Eq.9) and practical γ selection in experiments.
