- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online sparse dictionary learning with a Laplacian spatial regularizer to learn blob-like brain regions.  
- Uses inter-subject variability to define ROIs and enforces spatial smoothness while maintaining sparsity for interpretability.  
- Demonstrated on HCP fMRI (500 subjects), compared to SODL and CanICA, showing more structured, stable atoms and similar or better behavioral prediction.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear optimization, convergence guarantees, and computational efficiency.  
- Comprehensive experiments evaluating visualization, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Thorough hyperparameter analysis illustrating sparsity vs. reconstruction trade-offs.  
- Claim that spatial regularization is necessary with abundant data is not fully supported; benefits are clearer with limited data.  
- CanICA comparison is unfair because CanICA was run on smoothed data while Smooth-SODL/SODL were not.  
- Paper lacks guidance on sensitivity to the Laplacian parameter γ and risks of degenerate maps when γ is too large.  
- Insufficient explanation of why the Laplacian penalty produces spatially compact “blobs” rather than other patterns.  
- Vague definition of “structured” regions and insufficient distinction from other spatial regularizers.  
- Laplacian operator implementation for 3D images (finite differences, boundary handling) is not clearly specified.  
- Insufficient differentiation from related work (e.g., TV-based methods) and limited discussion of other structured dictionary-learning approaches.  
- Does not acknowledge that some brain structures (e.g., long tracts) may not be well-represented by blob-like atoms.  
- Significance may be overstated: modest prediction gains (5–10%) and unclear practical adoption path for neuroimaging researchers.  
- Lacks quantitative biological validation linking learned regions to known functional networks.  
- Recommendation to re-run comparisons with consistent preprocessing (unsmoothed vs. smoothed) for fairness.  
- Recommendation to clarify the spatial regularization mechanism and differences from total variation.  
- Recommendation to test performance across varying dataset sizes and clarify when regularization helps most.  
- Recommendation to provide practical hyperparameter selection guidance and safe ranges for γ and τ.  
- Recommendation to add quantitative biological validation against known functional networks.  
- Overall recommendation: Major Revision — promising and well-written but requires substantial revisions before acceptance.  
- Summary scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10, Overall ~7.25/10.
