- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online sparse dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for neuroimaging ROI discovery.
- Builds on SODL and aims to improve interpretability over CanICA and standard SODL by leveraging inter-subject variability.

- Problem significance: correctly identifies principled ROI definition as an important challenge and motivates using inter-subject variability.
- Algorithmic innovation: adds Laplacian regularization to SODL with modest extra computation (≈2–3× slower) and retains online scalability.
- Experimental evaluation: comprehensive experiments on HCP fMRI comparing visual quality, explained variance, stability, and predictive performance; learning curves show benefit for small datasets.
- Clarity and presentation: well-organized, precise math, and effective visual comparisons.

- Limited theoretical justification: convergence shown but no analysis of why the Laplacian penalty improves performance or its effect on bias–variance.
- Overstates necessity of spatial regularization: advantage of Smooth-SODL shrinks with large data (e.g., HCP n=500), suggesting regularization may be less critical.
- Superficial hyperparameter analysis: lacks detail on selection procedure and sensitivity despite known tuning difficulty in dictionary learning.
- Limited biological interpretability: claims of more interpretable regions rely on visual inspection without strong ties to known functional networks or new neuroscientific insights.
- Comparative analysis gap: insufficient differentiation from other spatially structured dictionary methods (e.g., TV-ℓ1 atlas methods cited).

- Overall: technically sound, practical, and experimentally thorough, but claims about universal necessity of spatial regularization are overstated.
- Suggested improvements: clarify when spatial regularization helps (small/noisy datasets), provide theoretical bias–variance analysis, expand hyperparameter sensitivity reporting, and strengthen biological validation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
