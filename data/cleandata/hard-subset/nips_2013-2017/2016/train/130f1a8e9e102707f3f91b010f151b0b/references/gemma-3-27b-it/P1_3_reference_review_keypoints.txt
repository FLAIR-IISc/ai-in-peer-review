- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online structured sparse dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, sparse brain regions from fMRI.
- Leverages inter-subject variability to define localized, interpretable dictionary atoms.
- Extends SODL by adding spatial smoothness regularization to reduce fragmented components.
- Demonstrates scalability to large HCP dataset via an online learning approach.
- Provides clear mathematical formulation, justification for L1 and Laplacian penalties, and convergence discussion.
- Empirically outperforms CanICA and standard SODL on explained variance, stability, and predictive performance.
- Includes hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and learning curves, and plans to release implementation (Nilearn).
- Situates work well among ROI, clustering, ICA/PCA, and structured sparsity literature, and cites relevant online learning and graph-regularization methods.
- Novel combination of online dictionary learning and Laplacian regularization addresses interpretability issues in neuroimaging.
- Computational cost: Smooth-SODL is about 3× slower than vanilla SODL, suggesting room for further optimization.
- Hyperparameter tuning appears ad-hoc; more systematic or automated selection and exploration of constraint sets needed.
- Lacks comparison to recent deep learning approaches (autoencoders/VAEs) and a discussion of their relative strengths/weaknesses.
- Biological validation is limited; stronger comparison to known anatomical/functional networks would increase credibility.
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for improved hyperparameter treatment, broader comparisons, and more rigorous biological validation.
