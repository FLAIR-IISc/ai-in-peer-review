- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness and L1 sparsity for learning brain regions from inter-subject variability.  
- Addresses need for principled, data-driven ROI definitions rather than anatomical or predefined ROIs.  
- Adds spatial smoothness to dictionary atoms with minimal computational overhead (~3x slower than SODL).  
- Algorithm extends prior online dictionary learning with convergence guarantees referenced from prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: compares against SODL and CanICA on 500 HCP subjects using visual quality, explained variance, reproducibility, and behavioral prediction.  
- Ablation study on hyperparameters is valuable and informative for practitioners.  
- Demonstrates superior interpretability and reproducibility (e.g., 0.4 correlation vs 0.1 for CanICA) and improved performance in small-data regimes.  
- Implementation will be made available in Nilearn, facilitating adoption.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presents results with helpful visualizations.  
- Could better clarify intuition for why the Laplacian penalty produces biologically meaningful “blobs” in brain imaging.  
- Original combination of online learning, sparsity, and spatial smoothness is novel and computationally efficient compared to prior slow alternating methods.  
- Significance is high: method could become a standard tool for data-driven ROI definition in neuroimaging.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, novel method, thorough experiments, practical implementation, and clear advantages over baselines.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical justification for Laplacian optimality in brain imaging, some computational-complexity details lacking, and the relation to prior spatially-constrained dictionary methods could be more explicitly differentiated.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, significant, and practically useful contribution to machine learning and neuroimaging.
