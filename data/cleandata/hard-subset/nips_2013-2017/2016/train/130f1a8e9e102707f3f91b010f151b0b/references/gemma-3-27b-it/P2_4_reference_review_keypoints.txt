- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with sparsity and Laplacian smoothness to learn spatially coherent brain regions from population fMRI.
- Method is scalable via online learning, enabling application to large datasets like HCP.
- Empirical results on HCP show improved dictionary interpretability, stability, and predictive power versus SODL and CanICA.
- Mathematical formulation and algorithmic details are clear and reproducible; paper is well-written.
- Novelty: the combination of online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty is original.
- Technical quality: method is sound and claims are well-supported by experiments.
- Significance: offers a principled, data-driven, interpretable ROI definition approach with potential impact on neuroimaging and behavioral prediction.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (α, γ, τ) is considerable; heuristics exist but systematic or automated tuning is lacking.
- Computational cost is higher (Smooth-SODL 340s vs SODL 110s), which may be problematic for larger datasets or complex models.
- Behavioral prediction experiments are limited in scope (only IQ and reading); broader cognitive/behavioral measures should be explored.
- CanICA comparison is under-detailed, especially the thresholding (tCanICA) step and its sensitivity to threshold choice.
- Lacks direct comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art segmentation/clustering methods (e.g., deep learning approaches).
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter tuning, computational efficiency, and broader comparisons.
