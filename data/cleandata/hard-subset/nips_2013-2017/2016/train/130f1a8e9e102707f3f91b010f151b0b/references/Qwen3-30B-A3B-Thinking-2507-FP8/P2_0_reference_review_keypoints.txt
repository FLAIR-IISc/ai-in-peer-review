- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness/compactness of dictionary atoms for neuroimaging.  
- Models brain activity as linear mixtures of spatially structured components using inter-subject variability in statistical maps.  
- Adds Laplacian regularization to SODL (Mairal et al., 2010) with ~3× computational overhead compared to standard SODL.  
- Provides Algorithm 1, convergence analysis, and implementation details (Nilearn) enabling reproducibility.  
- Validated on HCP task fMRI (language vs. math) and compared against SODL and CanICA using explained variance, stability, reproducibility, and behavioral prediction.  
- Empirical outcome: Smooth-SODL yields more interpretable, spatially structured regions and higher stability (mean atom correlation 0.4 vs. 0.1 for SODL and CanICA).  
- Quantitative results (Fig.2, Table1) support claims of better trade-off between interpretability (sparsity) and data fidelity.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient mathematical detail for experts.  
- Authors acknowledge hyperparameter sensitivity and give nuanced guidance on selection.  
- Clarity could improve by more explicitly explaining how the Laplacian penalty enforces spatial structure and when it’s necessary given abundant data.  
- Originality: natural but meaningful extension of online dictionary learning applied to neuroimaging; application is novel.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with other spatially structured dictionary learning methods (notably TV-ℓ1 approach cited as [1]).  
- Significance limited by evaluation on a single dataset and single task despite claims across data regimes.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, solid math, efficient implementation, careful hyperparameter analysis, strong baseline comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental scope, insufficient comparisons with other structured methods, inadequate discussion of the necessity of spatial regularization beyond HCP, somewhat overstated significance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; suggested revisions include (1) detailed comparison with other spatially structured methods (e.g., TV-ℓ1), (2) clearer explanation of Laplacian benefits given data abundance, and (3) additional experiments on more datasets/tasks to bolster significance.
