- Summary: Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary-learning method that enforces sparsity and spatial smoothness via an ℓ1-type constraint and a Laplacian penalty, using ridge codes and FISTA-updated atoms.
- Summary (results): On a large HCP task-fMRI dataset, Smooth-SODL produces more interpretable, spatially compact components, higher stability across splits, competitive explained variance/prediction, and modest runtime overhead versus SODL.

- Methodological soundness: Optimization setup is sensible; atom subproblems are strongly convex and FISTA with projections is appropriate; ridge codes stabilize updates.
- Methodological limits: Claims about sufficiency of 1–2 FISTA iterations and time-varying γt scheduling are empirically motivated but lack formal analysis or robustness checks.

- Empirical rigor: Experiments are solid and comparisons relevant, but limited to one task/contrast and missing error bars/statistical tests.
- Reproducibility concerns: Critical implementation details are omitted (input preprocessing/normalization, exact constraint set, nonnegativity, behavioral prediction pipeline and CV nesting).

- Clarity: Paper is generally readable and well-structured, with appropriate links to related structured-sparsity work.
- Presentation issues: Numerous typos/grammar problems and key derivations/complexity analysis are relegated to unavailable supplementary material.

- Originality: Novelty lies in integrating Laplacian smoothness into scalable online dictionary learning for large neuroimaging datasets.
- Originality caveat: Using ridge codes is not new; contribution is primarily engineering/practical rather than deep theoretical innovation.

- Practical significance: High value for neuroimaging—interpretable, stable ROIs learned at HCP scale with modest runtime overhead (~3× SODL).
- Scientific impact: Improvements in stability and early-sample efficiency are important, though explained-variance gains over PCA/ICA are modest.
- Generalizability: Likely transferable to other modalities but demonstrated on a single contrast only.

- Strength: Well-motivated objective aligning with neuroscience needs (interpretability and stability).
- Strength: Scalable optimization strategy with proximal gradient smoothing and reasonable runtime; plan to release code.
- Strength: Large-scale experiments showing qualitative and stability improvements and useful learning-curve analysis.

- Weakness: Missing details on constraint choice (positivity), preprocessing, and prediction protocol risk unfair comparisons or leakage.
- Weakness: Limited dataset scope, absent error bars/statistical testing, and missing baselines like TV-regularized DL or spatially regularized NMF.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter schedules and inner-solver iteration claims are heuristic without ablation or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Theoretical discussion is light; no convergence guarantees under inexact inner solves.

- Questions for authors: Are atoms constrained nonnegative (simplex) and how does that affect signed fMRI maps?
- Questions for authors: Was feature selection/hyperparameter tuning for prediction nested within cross-validation to avoid leakage?
- Questions for authors: Can you provide ablations for αt/γt schedules and the number of FISTA iterations?
- Questions for authors: What exact preprocessing (masking, normalization, detrending) was applied?

- Suggestions: Clarify and justify constraint-set choice and report results with/without non-negativity.
- Suggestions: Add nested CV details, leakage safeguards, error bars, and statistical tests.
- Suggestions: Provide ablation studies for hyperparameters and inner-solver accuracy.
- Suggestions: Compare to TV-regularized offline DL or spatially regularized NMF on a subset to quantify trade-offs.
- Suggestions: Include key derivations and complexity analysis in the main text and fix typographical errors.

- Overall evaluation: Technically sound and practically valuable but missing important methodological and evaluation details.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — useful empirical/engineering contribution that needs stronger methodological detail and evaluation rigor.
