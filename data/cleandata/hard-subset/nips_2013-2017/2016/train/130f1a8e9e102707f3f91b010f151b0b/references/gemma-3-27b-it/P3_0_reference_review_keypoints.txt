- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with sparsity and Laplacian smoothness to discover spatially localized brain regions from inter-subject fMRI variability.  
- Extends prior online dictionary learning by adding a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial coherence in atoms.  
- Correctly positions work among ROI definition, dimensionality reduction, clustering, and ICA, and cites relevant prior methods.  
- Novelty: combines online learning and spatial regularization to address interpretability limits of standard dictionary learning.  
- Scalability: online framework handles large-scale neuroimaging datasets with modest additional computational cost.  
- Theoretical grounding: clear penalized formulation with discussion of convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical validation: HCP experiments show improved interpretability, stability, and predictive power over SODL and ICA.  
- Presentation: well-written, organized, and includes algorithmic details.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: tuning is ad-hoc and needs more systematic or automated strategies; Eq. (9) explanation is terse.  
- Limited comparisons: lacks benchmarking against more recent ROI methods (e.g., deep learning or graph neural network approaches).  
- Interpretation gap: learned atoms labeled as “regions” but their functional relevance and relation to known networks are insufficiently explored.  
- Sparsity choice: simplex constraint (Sp(τ)) appears arbitrary without justification versus alternatives like the ℓ1 ball.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, scalable, empirically strong, well-explained, and potentially impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: hyperparameter tuning issues, limited state-of-the-art comparisons, and inadequate functional interpretation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: elaborate hyperparameter tuning and consider automated methods; compare to more recent ROI techniques; analyze functional content of regions; justify simplex sparsity constraint.
