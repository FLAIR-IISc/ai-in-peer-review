- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to learn spatially coherent brain regions from cross-subject fMRI variability.  
- Leverages cross-subject variability to define ROIs rather than relying on predefined regions or pure clustering.  
- Extends SODL by adding spatial smoothness and compactness via a Laplacian penalty while preserving sparsity.  
- Optimization uses Block Coordinate Descent with convergence discussion and a data-adaptive learning rate for α.  
- Scalable online framework suitable for large-scale, high-dimensional neuroimaging datasets.  
- Empirical validation on HCP compares Smooth-SODL to CanICA and SODL across explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Learning curves show spatial regularization benefits, especially with limited data.  
- Paper is generally well-written, mathematically rigorous, and implementation is planned to be open-sourced.  
- Novel application: combining online dictionary learning with Laplacian spatial regularization is original for ROI definition.  
- Potentially significant impact by providing a principled, scalable, data-driven ROI construction method.  
- Hyperparameter selection (γ, τ) appears ad-hoc and sensitive; requires more systematic tuning methods beyond k-fold CV.  
- The time-varying update and role of α need clearer explanation.  
- CanICA comparison may be unfair due to thresholding; unthresholded component results would be informative.  
- Sensitivity analysis mixes τ and γ; need focused exploration of γ alone and better theoretical justification for γ scaling (eq.9).  
- Behavioral prediction results lack statistical significance reporting and comparisons to simple baselines (e.g., raw fMRI).  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important contributions; recommended accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify hyperparameter tuning, expand on γ scaling rule, include unthresholded CanICA results, and add statistical details for behavioral predictions.
