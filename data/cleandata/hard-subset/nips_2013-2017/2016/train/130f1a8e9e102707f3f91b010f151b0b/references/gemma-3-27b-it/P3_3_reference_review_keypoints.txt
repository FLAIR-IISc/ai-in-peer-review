- Proposes Smooth-SODL: structured sparse online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to define brain regions from neuroimaging data.  
- Decomposes subject-level statistical maps into spatially localized, sparse dictionary atoms representing potential brain regions.  
- Extends online dictionary learning with spatial smoothness to produce more interpretable atoms.  
- Demonstrates method on HCP dataset, comparing to CanICA (ICA) and SODL with improved interpretability, reproducibility, and predictive power.  
- Positions work well within prior literature, citing online dictionary learning, spatial regularization, and graph-based methods like GraphNet.  
- Argues online framework overcomes computational bottlenecks of prior structured decomposition methods.  
- Strength: novel combination of scalability and spatial coherence for brain region definition.  
- Strength: scalable to large datasets with reasonable runtime compared to batch methods.  
- Strength: Laplacian regularization yields blob-like, more interpretable components.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation on HCP (explained variance, reproducibility, prediction).  
- Strength: thoughtful hyperparameter analysis and discussion of sparsity–smoothness–fidelity trade-offs.  
- Strength: clear writing and detailed algorithmic exposition.  
- Weakness: sensitive to hyperparameters (narrow optimal ranges for γ and τ) requiring careful tuning.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art segmentation methods (e.g., deep learning).  
- Weakness: computational cost ~3x slower than vanilla SODL; runtime depends on γ and BCD updates need optimization.  
- Weakness: reproducibility details incomplete (preprocessing, exact HCP settings, random seeds).  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence and statistical guarantees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, addresses ROI definition, empirically effective, and scalable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: hyperparameter sensitivity, limited SOTA comparisons, and higher computational cost.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant impact potential; weaknesses are minor and addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add hyperparameter selection details/automation, compare to modern segmentation methods, discuss runtime optimizations, and improve reproducibility information.
