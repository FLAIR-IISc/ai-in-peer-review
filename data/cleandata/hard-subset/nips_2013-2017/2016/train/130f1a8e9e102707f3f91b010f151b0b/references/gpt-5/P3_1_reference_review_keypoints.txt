- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian atom smoothness and dense ridge-penalized codes to learn blob-like, interpretable brain ROIs.  
- Uses per-atom convex quadratic updates with Laplacian term solved by a few FISTA iterations within block-coordinate descent.  
- Empirical results on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) show more spatially coherent components, higher atom stability, competitive explained variance (especially in low-data regimes), and better behavioral prediction than SODL and thresholded CanICA.  
- Method is related to GraphNet/TV-regularized dictionary learning and CanICA, but adapts structured spatial regularization to an online, scalable setting.  
- Motivated design choice: enforce sparsity and smoothness on atoms (ROIs) while keeping codes dense, argued to be appropriate for ROI discovery.  
- Strength: clear practical motivation to learn reproducible, spatially compact ROIs without pre-smoothing.  
- Strength: methodological contribution integrates Laplacian regularization into online dictionary learning with practical per-atom convex updates and complexity analysis.  
- Strength: scalability demonstrated (runtime ≈3× SODL, faster than CanICA in reported setup) on large neuroimaging data.  
- Strength: empirical evidence of improved interpretability, stability across splits, and superior low-data explained variance vs. SODL.  
- Strength: sensible ablation/hyperparameter discussion (αt schedule, impact of γ and τ) and use of Hungarian matching for stability.  
- Weakness: inexact inner solves (1–2 FISTA iterations) claim of 1e-6 accuracy seems unrealistic and convergence of overall scheme with inexact solves is not justified.  
- Weakness: inconsistency/unclear γ scheduling—paper claims γ must scale with At/t but experiments use fixed large γ; need clarity on implementation and numerical stability for rarely used atoms.  
- Weakness: modeling choice (sparse atoms, dense codes) is non-standard; paper lacks ablation comparing alternative sparsity placements (sparse codes, both sparse) to quantify trade-offs.  
- Weakness: missing direct comparisons to TV-regularized dictionary learning and structured sparse PCA baselines; even small-scale comparisons would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: preprocessing fairness—CanICA was run on pre-smoothed data while SODL variants were not; results should control for smoothing.  
- Weakness: reproducibility issues—missing details on number of epochs T, stopping criteria for FISTA/BCD, CPU model, memory footprint, exact CV protocol, and learning-curve setup.  
- Weakness: Laplacian Lipschitz constant derivation would benefit from explicit discretization/boundary condition details for clarity.  
- Weakness: notation inconsistencies and imprecise statements (e.g., “converge to a global optimum at each iteration”) need correction.  
- Originality: idea is an incremental but useful engineering advance—bringing Laplacian-regularized atoms into online dictionary learning is practical though not fundamentally novel.  
- Significance: fills a real gap for scalable structured decompositions in large neuroimaging cohorts, improving interpretability and stability; limited gains in prediction vs CanICA but narrows gap.  
- Questions for authors: whether nonnegativity/simplex was used in all experiments and effect of allowing negative atoms.  
- Questions for authors: actual average FISTA iterations, stopping criteria, and any warm-start strategies used.  
- Questions for authors: number of epochs T and how explained variance and stability evolve with T.  
- Questions for authors: request for a small-scale comparison to TV-regularized dictionary learning to support the “minor extra cost” claim.  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated, scalable method with careful algorithmic design and solid empirical evaluation on relevant dataset.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical justification for inexact solves and γ scheduling, missing structured baselines, preprocessing mismatch, and reproducibility details need tightening.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending methodological clarifications and at least a small-scale comparison to TV/structured baselines.
