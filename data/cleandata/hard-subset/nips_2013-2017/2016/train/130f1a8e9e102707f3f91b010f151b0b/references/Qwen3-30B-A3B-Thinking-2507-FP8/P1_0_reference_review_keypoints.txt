- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness and sparsity for interpretable brain regions (ROIs).
- Targets the problem of principled, data-driven ROI definition that captures inter-subject variability without anatomical priors.
- Evaluated on 500-subject Human Connectome Project data and compared to SODL and CanICA.
- Novelty: combines online dictionary learning with spatial constraints at large scale for neuroimaging.
- Technical soundness: clear theoretical justification for the Laplacian penalty and BCD updates with convergence guarantees.
- Algorithmic details and implementation considerations are well described.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with multiple metrics (explained variance, stability, reproducibility, predictive performance).
- Authors analyze hyperparameter effects and provide practical guidance.
- Comparisons to relevant baselines (SODL, CanICA) are appropriate and limitations acknowledged.
- Strength: improved interpretability and capture of inter-subject variability shown qualitatively and via explained variance.
- Weakness: claim that spatial regularization is universally needed is overstated; gains diminish with larger datasets.
- Weakness: lacks formal statistical tests to support claims about improved dictionaries beyond visuals and variance metrics.
- Weakness: reproducibility analysis is limited—only mean correlations reported without variability across seeds/splits.
- Weakness: computational complexity discussion is superficial; timing claim (2–3x slower) lacks detailed complexity or large-scale scaling experiments.
- Clarity: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically precise.
- Clarity: figures effectively illustrate qualitative differences between methods.
- Clarity weakness: intuitive explanation of why the Laplacian yields "blobs" could be stronger with geometric/visual examples.
- Clarity weakness: no clear practical guideline for selecting the (γ, τ) parameter pair.
- Comparison weakness: CanICA comparison is somewhat unfair; a sparse ICA baseline would be more informative.
- Originality weakness: Laplacian spatial smoothness has prior use; the paper should better distinguish its implementation from related work.
- Significance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem and scales to large datasets, offering practical ROI extraction benefits.
- Significance weakness: overstated claim about becoming the de facto method; applicability beyond the tested HCP task and modalities is not demonstrated.
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative, and scalable contribution that merits acceptance despite some presentation and analysis gaps.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, comprehensive large-scale evaluation, improved interpretability, practical implementation details, and relevance to neuroimaging.
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated necessity of spatial regularization, unfair baseline comparisons, limited reproducibility analysis, and unclear generalizability.
- Specific suggestion: clarify when spatial regularization adds value (e.g., data-limited settings).
- Specific suggestion: report reproducibility variability across multiple random seeds or data splits.
- Specific suggestion: discuss extension to other neuroimaging modalities (diffusion/structural MRI).
- Specific suggestion: add comparison to a sparse ICA variant for fairer baseline.
- Specific suggestion: discuss limitations of the Laplacian operator across spatial resolutions and coordinate systems.
