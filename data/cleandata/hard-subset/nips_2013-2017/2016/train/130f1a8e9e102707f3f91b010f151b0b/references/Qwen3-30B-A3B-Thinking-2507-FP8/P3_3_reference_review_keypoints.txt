- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to learn spatially structured, sparse brain regions.  
- Addresses a key gap in neuroimaging by providing principled, scalable ROI extraction that leverages inter-subject variability.  
- Novel technical contribution: integration of spatial (Laplacian) regularization into online dictionary learning with algorithmic adaptation and convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental evaluation on HCP fMRI is comprehensive and shows improved interpretability, reproducibility, and prediction performance versus SODL and CanICA.  
- Learning-curve results highlight strong low-data benefits (14x gain over SODL at 17 subjects) with the advantage decreasing as data grows.  
- Computationally practical: Smooth-SODL is only ~3× slower than SODL (340s vs. 110s) while delivering substantial benefits.  
- Main omission: no direct comparison to recent related work [23] (online group-level functional brain mapping), weakening the novelty claim.  
- Lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for α, γ, and τ; only a "safe range" is reported.  
- Interpretability claims are primarily supported by visual inspection; quantitative alignment with known anatomy is missing.  
- Theoretical treatment lacks analysis of how Laplacian smoothing affects statistical properties or bias-variance trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing comparisons, hyperparameter analysis, and quantitative interpretability metrics.  
- Overall: well-written, scalable, and practically valuable method with clear potential to become a standard ROI extraction approach after suggested fixes.
