- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method that learns spatially localized brain regions using L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness penalties.  
- Novelty: first to combine online dictionary learning with both sparsity and spatial smoothness constraints.  
- Motivation: addresses limitations of ad-hoc or prior-driven ROI definitions by offering a principled, data-driven alternative.  
- Scalability: online framework well-suited to large-scale neuroimaging datasets.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive evaluation on HCP showing improved interpretability, stability, explained variance, and behavioral prediction versus SODL and ICA.  
- Theoretical grounding: clear mathematical formulation with discussion of convergence and references to relevant theory.  
- Practical considerations: provides guidance on hyperparameter tuning and initialization for practitioners.  
- Writing: paper is generally clear and well-written.  
- Computational cost: Laplacian penalty incurs ~3x slowdown versus standard SODL; trade-off analysis is needed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: results sensitive to α, γ, τ; Eq. 9 explanation is hand-wavy and requires more rigorous justification and systematic exploration.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art: lacks comparisons to more recent/advanced spatial decomposition methods (e.g., deep learning approaches).  
- Interpretation of "blobs": spatial "blob" notion is intuitive but not rigorously connected to neuroanatomy or known brain structures.  
- Ablation study: limited—should systematically remove individual regularizers to isolate their contributions.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-executed contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, scalable, clear presentation, high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: higher computational cost, hyperparameter tuning burden, limited comparisons to newer methods.  
- NIPS criteria: Quality—strong; Clarity—good; Originality—high; Significance—high.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggested revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
