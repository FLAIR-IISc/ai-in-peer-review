- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian spatial regularizer to learn smooth, compact brain regions.  
- Targets principled ROI definition by leveraging inter-subject variability in neuroimaging data.  
- Adds spatial regularization to SODL so atoms are piecewise-smooth and less scattered.  
- Theoretical foundation includes convergence guarantees and complexity analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation on HCP task fMRI shows more interpretable, reproducible regions than SODL and CanICA.  
- Demonstrates only a modest ~3× computational overhead compared to SODL.  
- Strong advantage in small-data regimes: reported 14× improvement in explained variance with 17 subjects.  
- Comprehensive experiments across visual quality, explained variance, reproducibility, and behavioral prediction.  
- Clear writing, well-organized method description, and reproducible experimental setup with illustrative figures.  
- Novelty: first adaptation of structured dictionary learning with Laplacian penalty to an online setting for neuroimaging.  
- Significant practical impact: suitable for large datasets and most neuroimaging studies that are data-limited.  
- Strengths: strong motivation, theoretical justification, thorough evaluation, and careful discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Weaknesses: limited to one dataset and one modality (HCP task fMRI); unclear why Laplacian is optimal versus other smoothness penalties.  
- Additional limitation: authors could better articulate performance under extreme noise or differing brain structures.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically sound, original, practically relevant, and likely to become a standard ROI extraction tool.
