- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method with a Laplacian penalty to learn spatially localized, interpretable brain-region “atoms.”
- Addresses inter-subject variability and ROI definition; applied to large-scale HCP task fMRI data.
- Builds on online dictionary learning [16] and structured sparse learning, connecting to GraphNet for dictionary updates.
- Novelty: combining online dictionary learning with spatial-smoothness regularization is a genuine contribution.
- Scalability: online framework handles large neuroimaging datasets and shows reasonable runtime vs CanICA.
- Theoretical grounding: provides optimization derivation and convergence discussion based on prior work.
- Empirical validation: HCP experiments show improved balance of sparsity, spatial coherence, and predictive power versus CanICA and SODL.
- Practical guidance: addresses hyperparameter tuning and initialization, offering practitioner advice.
- Writing: paper is clear, well-organized, and accessible.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends sensitively on α, γ, and τ; guidance insufficient.
- Weakness — limited comparisons: lacks benchmarks against other spatially regularized decompositions (e.g., TV-based methods).
- Weakness — computational cost: about 3× slower than SODL; dictionary update could be further optimized (FFTs suggested).
- Weakness — interpretability evaluation: mostly qualitative; needs quantitative comparisons to atlases or functional-connectivity analyses.
- Weakness — modeling assumption: Laplacian penalty assumes spatial contiguity of functional regions, which may not always hold.
- For acceptance: method is novel, scalable, well-grounded, empirically validated, clearly presented, and has high potential impact.
- Against acceptance: sensitivity to hyperparameters, incomplete comparisons, and primarily qualitative interpretability assessment.
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions on hyperparameter sensitivity, computational optimization, and interpretability evaluation.
- Review scores: Quality — Excellent; Clarity — Very Good; Originality — Good; Significance — Good.
