- Contribution: Proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.  
- Technical soundness: Model definition and algorithms follow a well-established framework and appear solid, but initial technical rating was marginally below average.  
- Empirical support weak: Paper did not clearly demonstrate empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior; Figure 2 did not quantify or clearly show in what way SSMF outperformed alternatives.  
- Request for quantitative evaluation: Reviewer asks for explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measurements showing that SSMF atoms are more structured and meaningful.  
- Rebuttal impact: Additional results provided in rebuttal were informative and raised the technical quality rating from 2 to 3; reviewer asks these be included in revision.  
- Novelty: The main original contribution is extending prior work by incorporating Sobolev priors for spatial structure.  
- Potential impact: Method could be a useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging and might be applied to other tasks like image segmentation and regional sensing.  
- Presentation quality: Manuscript has confusing descriptions, typos, and editing issues that impede understanding.  
- Specific mathematical/notation issues: Inconsistencies and possible typos in matrix decomposition transpose placement, signs and terms in Equations (6) and (8), and unclear variable references in updates.  
- Missing references: HCP dataset reference is missing.  
- Algorithmic detail requested: Reviewer asks for explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) (e.g., include derivation in appendix).  
- Figure 2 issues: Different slices shown for different methods hinder direct comparison; claim that SSMF is more “structured” is not supported visually or quantitatively.  
- Figure 3 issues: Unclear what is being predicted, whether regression uses U_i, legends and caption do not explain baselines (PCA is missing), and claims about R^2 > 0.1 are ambiguous as to which curves support this.  
- Minor typos: Multiple line-specific typos and stray comments (e.g., “Graph-Net penalty can imposes”, duplicated words, stray bold sentence) need fixing.
