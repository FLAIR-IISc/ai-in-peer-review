- Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary learning algorithm with a Laplacian penalty to produce sparse, spatially smooth, compact atoms for neuroimaging.
- Novel integration of a structured Laplacian prior into the scalable SODL framework, motivated and non-trivial.
- Aims to improve interpretability of components as functional brain regions compared to batch structured-prior methods and standard SODL.
- Strong technical quality, originality, and relevance to the neuroimaging community.
- Thorough experimental validation on a large Human Connectome Project dataset.
- Empirical comparisons against SODL and CanICA show qualitatively more interpretable and quantitatively more stable atoms across splits.
- Computational cost is modestly higher (≈3×) than SODL while delivering interpretability and stability gains.
- Limitation: derivation of the dictionary update (Eq. 7) assumes reader familiarity with prior work and should be more self-contained for reproducibility.
- Limitation: brief discussion on hyperparameter selection; more guidance or automated strategies for γ and τ would improve practical utility.
- Limitation: explained variance advantage over SODL diminishes in very large-data regimes, reducing superiority for purely predictive tasks.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant practical problem, provides a novel efficient method, and shows strong empirical evidence of improved interpretability and stability.
- Arguments for rejection: contribution seen as incremental extension of existing algorithm and benefits for some metrics shrink in the large-data limit.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
