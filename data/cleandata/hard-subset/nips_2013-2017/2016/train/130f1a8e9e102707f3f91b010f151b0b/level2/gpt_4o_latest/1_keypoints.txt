- Introduces Smooth-SODL, extending SODL with a Laplacian penalty to produce compact, piece-wise smooth dictionary atoms tailored for neuroimaging.
- Laplacian-based spatial regularization yields more interpretable and denoised atoms, addressing a key limitation of prior methods.
- Online algorithm design ensures scalability to very large neuroimaging datasets.
- Comprehensive experimental comparison against CanICA and SODL shows improved interpretability, stability, and explained variance, especially with limited data.
- Demonstrates practical utility by improving behavioral prediction from fMRI relative to several baselines.
- Visual and quantitative results are clearly presented and support the paper’s claims.
- Hyperparameter selection is challenging and lacks systematic guidelines for practitioners.
- Smooth-SODL is about three times slower than SODL, posing a computational trade-off that may limit adoption.
- Limited analysis of failure modes (e.g., degenerate maps under strong regularization) weakens understanding of method limitations.
- Reproducibility details are insufficient (e.g., initialization and parameter ranges), though code release is planned.
- Pro: Effectively combines sparsity and spatial structure, filling a notable gap in neuroimaging dictionary learning.
- Pro: Scalable, well-validated approach with clear advantages over existing methods.
- Con: Increased computational cost may deter time-sensitive applications.
- Con: Needs better treatment of hyperparameter tuning and reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Accept — the contribution is significant, well-validated, and likely to stimulate further research and applications.
