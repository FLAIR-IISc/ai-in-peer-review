- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to extract spatially structured, sparse brain components.  
- Extends online dictionary learning to enforce smoothness for more interpretable, spatially localized decompositions.  
- Evaluated on Human Connectome Project fMRI data, showing improvements over ICA and standard sparse dictionary learning.  
- Demonstrates better capture of inter-subject variability and improved behavioral score prediction.  
- Addresses an important, well-motivated problem of principled, data-driven ROI definition in neuroimaging.  
- Technical contribution of adding a Laplacian penalty to online dictionary learning is sound and non-trivial.  
- Experiments on a large public dataset with comparisons to relevant baselines (CanICA, SODL) strengthen empirical validation.  
- Ablation studies and hyperparameter exploration add robustness and practical insight.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and plans to release code for reproducibility.  
- Practical benefits of the online approach (memory, scalability) are not fully detailed or quantified.  
- Choice of Laplacian penalty needs stronger justification and discussion of alternatives (e.g., total variation).  
- Comparison to CanICA is limited by lack of detail on thresholding procedure and parameter choices.  
- Quantitative results lack statistical rigor—should report p-values or confidence intervals for behavioral prediction.  
- Convergence properties of the overall algorithm are not empirically assessed or sufficiently discussed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, promising contribution with mostly minor, addressable revisions that would increase impact.
