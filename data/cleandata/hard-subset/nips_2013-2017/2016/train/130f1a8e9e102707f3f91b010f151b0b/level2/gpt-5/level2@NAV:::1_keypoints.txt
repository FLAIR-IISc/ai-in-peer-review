- Proposal: Smooth-SODL adds a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty and sparsity/positivity constraints to online dictionary learning for sparse, piecewise-smooth, localized neuroimaging components.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to GraphNet/TV-ℓ1 structured sparsity and extends Mairal et al. (2010) to a scalable, online Laplacian-regularized setting.  
- Implementation: Uses block coordinate descent with ridge coding and FISTA-based atom updates; discusses Lipschitz constants and safe γ schedules.  
- Computational cost: Claims modest extra cost (~2–3× that of SODL).  
- Experiments: Evaluated on HCP task fMRI (n=500, k=40) against SODL and CanICA.  
- Empirical findings: Reports improved qualitative spatial structure and higher atom stability than CanICA/SODL.  
- Data-regime behavior: Shows earlier gains in explained variance for small sample sizes and competitive behavioral prediction versus dense baselines.  
- Practical relevance: Relevant to ROI discovery and modeling inter-subject variability; integrates with neuroimaging pipelines.  
- Strength: Clear, scalable method that produces interpretable decompositions with potential for adoption.  
- Strength: Technically sensible algorithmic choices and discussion of optimization details.  
- Weakness: Lacks uncertainty estimates and statistical tests (confidence intervals, permutation baselines, stability significance).  
- Weakness: Needs reproducibility quantification (split-half/bootstraps, variance across seeds/splits).  
- Weakness: Missing ablations isolating Laplacian vs sparsity vs positivity and comparing ridge vs ℓ1 coding; include “smooth-only” and “sparse-only” settings.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines—add TV-ℓ1/GraphNet decompositions, recent online matrix factorization (e.g., Mensch et al.), and nonnegative matrix factorization when positivity is used.  
- Weakness: Missing implementation details — Laplacian boundary conditions, atom normalization, exact τ/γ/α schedules, number of passes, stopping criteria, and memory footprint.  
- Weakness: Claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e-6 tolerance seems optimistic; report residuals vs runtime trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Generalization limited — evaluate on additional tasks/resting-state data and test cross-dataset transfer.  
- Weakness: Interpretability is qualitative; provide quantitative metrics (compactness, blob count, smoothness norms) or expert ratings/user study.  
- Clarity: Generally well written but algorithmic description should better distinguish constraints vs penalties and clarify “parameter free” vs FISTA step sizes.  
- Originality: Novel extension—online structured atoms with Laplacian regularization in a scalable framework.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for data-driven atlases and population analyses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Scalable, interpretable decomposition with empirical stability and early-data advantages, and clear methodological contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient statistical validation, missing ablations and baselines, incomplete implementation details, and code not yet available.
