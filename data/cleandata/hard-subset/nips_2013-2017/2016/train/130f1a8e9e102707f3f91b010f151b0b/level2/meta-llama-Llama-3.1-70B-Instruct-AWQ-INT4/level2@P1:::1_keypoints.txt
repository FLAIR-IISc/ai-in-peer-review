- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for brain images combining L1-norm sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty for piecewise-smooth, compact components.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over L1-regularized dictionary learning and state-of-the-art models on a large public neuroimaging dataset.

- Novel method addressing limitations of existing dictionary-learning approaches in neuroimaging.  
- Thorough performance analysis with experiments on a large public dataset.  
- Detailed algorithm description and implementation enabling reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates benefits of spatial regularization for neuroimaging decompositions.

- Assumes strong background in dictionary learning and neuroimaging, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.  
- Evaluation is focused primarily on a single dataset; broader validation on other datasets/tasks is missing.  
- Limited discussion of method limitations and future work directions.

- Technically sound with clear method description and well-designed experiments, but needs more on complexity and scalability.  
- Well-written and organized with informative figures/tables, yet possibly inaccessible to non-experts due to assumed background.

- Original contribution by combining L1-norm constraint with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty in dictionary learning.  
- Potentially significant impact in neuroimaging by enabling extraction of structured, sparse brain-image components.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, thorough analysis on large dataset, and potential significant impact in neuroimaging.  
- Arguments against acceptance: steep background requirements for readers, insufficient complexity/scalability analysis, and limited evaluation across datasets/tasks.
