- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension that adds spatial structure via a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty plus L1 sparsity to produce blob-like, piecewise-smooth atoms.  
- Maintains scalability: only a 2–3× runtime overhead vs. SODL and scales to large datasets (e.g., HCP 500 subjects, 260k voxels).  
- Addresses a key neuroimaging challenge: extracting interpretable, spatially coherent brain regions without predefined ROIs or cross-modality segmentation.  
- Non-trivial adaptation of Mairal et al.'s SODL using BCD with FISTA for dictionary updates and convergence guarantees derived from existing theory.  
- Laplacian penalty effectively promotes spatial coherence; qualitative results show smoother, more anatomically plausible atoms than SODL and CanICA.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive, evaluating explained variance, reproducibility, behavioral prediction, and learning curves.  
- Demonstrates a major benefit in small-data regimes (e.g., 14× higher explained variance at 17 subjects vs. SODL).  
- Strong empirical validation against relevant baselines and clear justification of design choices (ridge coding, adaptive α-tuning).  
- Practical implementation provided in Nilearn, increasing accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Advances the state of the art by bridging dictionary learning with spatial constraints while preserving online efficiency, outperforming slower alternating methods.  
- Claims about computational gains are supported (noted 5× speedup over CanICA and modest overhead relative to SODL).  
- Minor weakness: limited quantitative metrics for interpretability beyond visual assessment (no formal anatomical accuracy comparison to expert regions).  
- Minor weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity discussion is brief and could be expanded.  
- Minor weakness: comparison to very recent online structured approaches (e.g., Mensch et al., 2016) could be more explicit.  
- Overall assessment: technically rigorous, clearly written, original, and significant; meets conference criteria and recommended for acceptance.
