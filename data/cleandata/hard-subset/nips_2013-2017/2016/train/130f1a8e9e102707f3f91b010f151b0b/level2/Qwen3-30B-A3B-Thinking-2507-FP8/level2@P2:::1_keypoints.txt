- Proposes Smooth-SODL: adds a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to online dictionary learning to enforce spatial structure in brain imaging atoms.
- Motivation: structured, blob-like brain regions are more interpretable than scattered patterns and can capture inter-subject variability.
- Theoretical extension is sound with proper optimization using block coordinate descent (BCD) and FISTA.
- Method scales to large datasets and remains computationally efficient (only ~2–3× slower than SODL).
- Produces structured, sparse dictionary atoms with improved spatial coherence compared to SODL and CanICA.
- Shows improved stability across data splits (measured by mean correlation between corresponding atoms).
- Achieves competitive behavioral prediction performance without sacrificing predictive power.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction, and learning curves across dataset sizes.
- Provides useful guidance on hyperparameter selection, especially the trade-off between γ (Laplacian) and τ (sparsity).
- Lacks clear justification for using simplex constraint Sp(τ) instead of the common L1-ball Bp,1(τ); choice affects results and isn’t well motivated.
- Limited direct comparison to other spatial regularizers (e.g., total variation); no experimental benchmarking against alternatives.
- Claim of better capturing inter-subject variability is unsupported beyond stability metrics; needs quantitative links to known brain networks.
- Assertion that CanICA is "dense and noisy" is shown visually but lacks quantitative measures (e.g., non-zero counts, smoothness metrics).
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, and logically organized with appropriate experiments and implementation details.
- Main revision requested: clarify constraint choice and add a brief experimental comparison with TV-regularization to strengthen novelty claims.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
