- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online sparse dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, interpretable brain-region atoms for large-scale neuroimaging.

- Novelty/significance: Integrates spatial smoothness into scalable online dictionary learning, addressing lack of spatial coherence in prior sparse approaches.

- Technical quality: Sound formulation and efficient block-coordinate/FISTA optimization; experiments on HCP (n=500) against SODL and CanICA with comprehensive metrics.

- Clarity/presentation: Well-written, clear model/algorithm description, effective figures (esp. Fig.2) and clear finding that structure helps in low-data regimes.

- Practical impact: Reasonably fast (≈3× slower than SODL, faster than CanICA) and implementation to be released in Nilearn increases usability and community impact.

- Weakness: No comparison/discussion versus TV (l1-gradient) regularizer; should justify Laplacian choice and trade-offs between smoothness vs piecewise-constant assumptions and optimization complexity.

- Weakness: Limited guidance on hyperparameter selection (γ, τ); cross-validation recommended but practical heuristics and sensitivity analysis would be helpful.

- Weakness: Behavioral prediction results show CanICA (dense) sometimes outperforms Smooth-SODL, indicating a trade-off between interpretability and raw predictive power that needs discussion.

- Weakness: Minor reproducibility gaps—specify voxel neighborhood for discrete Laplacian (e.g., 6- vs 26-connectivity) and fix incomplete citations.

- Overall recommendation: Strong, well-executed contribution with minor clarifications suggested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
