- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce spatially smooth, blob-like dictionary atoms.  
- Motivated by inter-subject variability and the need for principled, data-driven ROI definition in neuroimaging.  
- Logical extension of Mairal et al. (2010) that sensibly enforces spatial structure.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large-scale brain imaging datasets.  
- On HCP data, extracts more interpretable dictionaries and better captures inter-subject variability than ICA and original SODL.  
- Shows notable gains in predicting behavioral variables.  
- Runtime is ~3x slower than SODL and lacks detailed computational complexity analysis.  
- Hyperparameter selection is only briefly described; sensitivity analysis is superficial.  
- Comparisons are limited (e.g., mainly CanICA); needs benchmarks against more recent spatially-aware dictionary methods.  
- Related work discussion is thin; should elaborate links to total variation regularization and its neuroimaging uses.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions focused on runtime analysis and broader method comparisons.  
- Paper has significant potential impact for ROI definition and understanding brain organization.
