- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with an l1-ball constraint and a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to learn sparse, spatially structured brain components at scale.  
- Scales to large neuroimaging datasets, demonstrated on the Human Connectome Project, and compared to SODL and CanICA.  
- Novel and significant: combines scalable online learning with a spatial prior, bridging unstructured fast methods and structured but costly batch methods.  
- Technically sound: clear derivation of block-coordinate updates, identifies constrained quadratic subproblem solvable with fast first-order methods, and discusses convergence and hyperparameter considerations.  
- Strong empirical validation: uses a large public dataset, appropriate baselines, and comprehensive metrics (compute, interpretability, explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction).  
- Learning-curve analysis shows the spatial regularizer helps substantially in low-to-medium data regimes while remaining competitive at large-scale.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with effective figures and tables.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of Laplacian vs. Total Variation — qualitative differences and neuroscientific implications of smooth vs. piecewise-constant regions are not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity — more practical guidance for choosing γ and τ (e.g., relation to voxel size or data smoothness) would improve usability.  
- Weakness: choice of l2 (Ridge) penalty on codes U lacks explicit justification or empirical comparison to l1 sparsity at the subject level.  
- Overall: high-quality, impactful work with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
