- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for brain images with ℓ1 sparsity on atoms and Laplacian/Sobolev spatial smoothing.  
- Codes computed by ridge regression; per-atom dictionary updates via block coordinate descent and projected (F)ISTA for constrained quadratics.  
- Extends online sparse DL (Mairal et al. 2010) by integrating spatial regularization on atoms while aiming to keep scalability.  
- Discusses relation to GraphNet-style smoothness and FFT-based acceleration for the Laplacian.  
- Empirically on HCP (n=500, p≈2.6×10^5) produces more interpretable blob-like atoms, improved stability, and better early-regime generalization vs vanilla SODL.  
- Competes with ICA/PCA on explained variance and behavior prediction; implementation planned for Nilearn.  

- Strength: tackles an important practical neuroimaging need for sparse, smooth, reproducible population-level atoms.  
- Strength: clear algorithmic integration of Laplacian smoothing with per-atom BCD and projected accelerated gradient; convergence discussed under known assumptions.  
- Strength: sensible hyperparameter discussion (γ vs stability/complexity) and heuristic schedules for α, guidance on τ and γ.  
- Strength: solid empirical validation on a large public dataset with runtime, EV, stability, and downstream prediction metrics.  
- Strength: small-data learning-curve analysis shows spatial regularization greatly improves sample efficiency.  
- Strength: planned open-source integration (Nilearn) increases impact and reproducibility.  

- Weakness: methodological novelty is incremental; main contribution is engineering/scalability rather than new theory.  
- Weakness: projected FISTA update described high-level; missing details on projection, backtracking, stopping criteria, and 3D Laplacian boundary/mask handling.  
- Weakness: masks break FFT assumptions — unclear when FFT acceleration is valid and what was used in main experiments.  
- Weakness: claim that “1–2 FISTA iterations suffice to reach 1e-6 accuracy” is implausible at p≈10^5 and needs quantification of residuals and impact of inexactness.  
- Weakness: γ scheduling (Eq. 9) vs practice mismatch — experiments use fixed large γ; need reconciliation and discussion of Lipschitz blow-up.  
- Weakness: baseline fairness concerns — CanICA had smoothing options but SODL variants did not, and stronger structured baselines (TV-ℓ1, GraphNet PCA/ICA) are missing.  
- Weakness: incomplete quantitative reporting (missing Table 1, precise stability numbers) and no significance testing for EV/R^2 differences.  
- Weakness: choice of ℓ2 (ridge) codes vs ℓ1 sparse codes not ablated — need comparisons to isolate source of gains.  

- Quality: technically sound overall but theoretical claims on inexact updates and γ scheduling need tighter statements or empirical ablations.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, but compact derivations; add projected FISTA pseudocode and Laplacian implementation details (masking, boundaries, normalization).  
- Originality: moderate — useful integration at scale but uses established ingredients (online DL, Laplacian smoothing, projected first-order methods).  
- Significance: high practical significance for neuroimaging workflows; likely adoption if reproducible and implemented in Nilearn.  

- Key questions for authors: specify Laplacian boundary conditions, masking, and normalization.  
- Key questions for authors: state whether projected FISTA uses fixed step or backtracking, stopping criteria, and typical iteration counts.  
- Key questions for authors: describe how γ and τ were selected (CV grid?) and provide sensitivity curves for stability/EV trade-offs.  
- Key questions for authors: provide results with ℓ1 or elastic-net codes to quantify effect of code sparsity vs atom smoothness.  
- Key questions for authors: report memory footprint scaling with k, p, and batch size and feasibility for p>10^6.  

- Suggestions: add structured baselines (batch TV-ℓ1 DL, GraphNet-regularized PCA/ICA, spatial ICA) and/or run them on subsets.  
- Suggestions: report statistical significance/confidence intervals for EV, stability, and behavior prediction.  
- Suggestions: include ablations for k, γ, τ, α schedule, number of FISTA iterations, FFT vs no-FFT, and pre-smoothing.  
- Suggestions: clarify/justify γ scheduling vs fixed γ and provide a practical recipe for practitioners.  
- Suggestions: provide full implementation details to reproduce the Laplacian on masked 3D volumes.  

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a timely problem scalably, shows interpretability and stability gains, and promises open-source release.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited methodological novelty, missing implementation/evaluation details, and weaker baselines/statistical analysis.  

- Overall verdict: valuable, well-motivated engineering contribution with practical utility, contingent on clarifying Laplacian update, parameterization, baseline fairness, and statistical reporting.
