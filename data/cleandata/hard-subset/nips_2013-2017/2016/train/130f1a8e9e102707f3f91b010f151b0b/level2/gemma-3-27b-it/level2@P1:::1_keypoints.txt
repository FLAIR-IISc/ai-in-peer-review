- Proposes an online multivariate dictionary learning method combining L1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial smoothness to decompose brain images into structured, sparse components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning to include spatial priors for blob-like atoms.  
- Addresses neuroimaging need to learn regions directly from data, handling inter-subject variability without predefined ROIs.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets and compares running time to CanICA and SODL.  
- Produces more interpretable, spatially structured dictionary atoms than ICA and standard online dictionary learning.  
- Empirical validation on HCP data shows better capture of inter-subject variability and predictive performance on behavioral variables.  
- Clear mathematical formulation and helpful pseudocode (Alg. 1 & 2); paper is generally well written.  
- Novelty: combining L1 and Laplacian penalties in an online framework is a valuable contribution.  
- Strength in scalability is important for real-world neuroimaging applications.  
- Visual interpretability of learned atoms is a compelling empirical result.  
- Thorough experiments and hyperparameter impact analysis strengthen empirical claims.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning (γ and τ) is ad-hoc and shows sensitivity, limiting practical usability.  
- Weakness: computational cost is higher (≈3× slower than vanilla SODL), which may hinder very large or real-time use.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to recent deep learning–based representation learning methods.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of convergence and statistical guarantees.  
- Weakness: some redundancy and repetitiveness in the introduction; could be more concise.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions—method is novel, scalable, and empirically strong despite noted weaknesses.  
- Suggested improvements: provide principled hyperparameter selection, optimize Laplacian computation, compare to deep learning approaches, discuss theoretical limitations, and streamline the introduction.
