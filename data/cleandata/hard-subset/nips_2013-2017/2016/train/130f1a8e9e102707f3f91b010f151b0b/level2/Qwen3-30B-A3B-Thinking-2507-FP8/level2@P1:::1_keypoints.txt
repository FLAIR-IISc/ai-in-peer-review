- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for brain imaging with a Laplacian penalty enforcing spatial smoothness while retaining sparsity.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) SODL framework and adds spatial regularization to produce blob-like brain regions.  
- Produces more spatially structured, interpretable brain maps compared to SODL and CanICA.  
- Experimental results on HCP task fMRI: higher stability (0.4 vs 0.1 for SODL and CanICA) and higher test explained variance (55% vs 49%).  
- Computationally practical: only 2–3× slower than standard SODL for large-scale neuroimaging.  
- Strength: addresses a meaningful neuroimaging problem with clear practical impact for extracting interpretable regions.  
- Strength: method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded; Laplacian penalty aligns with known spatial organization.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation against relevant baselines across multiple metrics.  
- Strength: provides practical hyperparameter guidance (time-varying α and safe range for γ).  
- Weakness: novelty claim is weak—insufficient differentiation from prior structured dictionary learning (e.g., Varoquaux et al., 2012).  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for spatial regularization benefits when large datasets (HCP) are available.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of Laplacian penalty limitations (may miss non-smooth or complex- boundary structures).  
- Weakness: connection to prior structured sparsity ML work (e.g., Jenatton et al., 2011) is underdeveloped.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and reproducible with careful algorithmic detail.  
- Recommendation: strengthen comparisons to prior work, discuss when spatial regularization helps, note Laplacian limitations, and clarify links to structured sparsity literature.  
- Overall verdict: strong, practical contribution with minor revisions suggested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
