This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for obtaining decompositions of brain images with structured and sparse components. The method, called Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), combines the standard online dictionary-learning framework with a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a large public dataset, showing improvements over traditional `1-regularized dictionary learning.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. The introduction provides a good overview of the problem and the proposed solution. The related work section is comprehensive, and the authors do a good job of positioning their work in the context of existing research.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors extend the online dictionary-learning framework to incorporate a Laplacian penalty, which allows for the imposition of spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. They also provide a detailed analysis of the optimization problem and propose an efficient algorithm for solving it.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method in extracting structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability. The comparison with state-of-the-art methods, such as SODL and CanICA, shows that the proposed method outperforms these methods in terms of explained variance and stability.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm. While the authors provide some information on the running time of the algorithm, a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity would be helpful in understanding the scalability of the method.

Another potential weakness is the lack of a clear explanation of how the hyper-parameters of the method are selected. The authors provide some information on the selection of the regularization parameters, but a more detailed explanation of how these parameters are chosen would be helpful.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the technical contributions are significant. The experimental results are impressive, and the method has the potential to be widely used in the field of neuroimaging.
