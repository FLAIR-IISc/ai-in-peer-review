- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for brain imaging with L1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial penalty on atoms.  
- Claims it yields more interpretable, structured brain regions that capture inter-subject variability with only 2–3× computational overhead.  
- Addresses an important gap by providing a principled region definition without segmentation or cross-modality alignment.  
- Experiments on HCP (500 subjects) show improved interpretability (Fig.2a), higher stability (0.4 vs 0.1 vs CanICA), and better behavioral prediction (Fig.2c).  
- Computational efficiency claim (≈3× slowdown vs SODL) is supported by runtime measurements.  
- Authors responsibly note a limitation: spatial regularization is most helpful when data are limited.  
- Novelty is unclear: insufficient distinction and comparison to prior structured dictionary learning work (e.g., Varoquaux et al., IPMI 2015).  
- Methodological detail OK, but hyperparameter selection (Section 3.2) is superficial and lacks practical guidance or sensitivity analysis for (γ, τ).  
- Overall: solid technical contribution with practical value; paper would be stronger by better contextualizing novelty and giving concrete parameter-selection guidance.
