- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to extract spatially smooth, sparse brain region components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) SODL by adding spatial regularization (Laplacian) alongside L1 sparsity.  
- Theoretical grounding connects the Laplacian regularizer to Sobolev-space smoothness.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Human Connectome Project fMRI, showing more interpretable, blob-like atoms than ICA and standard SODL.  
- Shows improved capture of inter-subject variability and better behavioral prediction performance.  
- Clear exposition, sound theoretical justification, and thorough experimental validation on a large public dataset.  
- Includes useful comparisons to CanICA and original SODL and discusses data-adaptive hyperparameter selection for α.  
- Running time is 2–3× higher than SODL but authors argue the trade-off is reasonable; runtime comparisons included.  
- Weakness: insufficient detailed computational cost analysis and lack of time breakdown across algorithm components.  
- Weakness: convergence claim “to a local optimum” is weak; convergence criteria and FISTA parameter choices need more discussion.  
- Weakness: justification for choosing the Laplacian (vs. alternatives like Total Variation) and the simplex constraint is insufficient.  
- Weakness: downplays CanICA’s superior explained variance; needs a nuanced discussion of the interpretability vs explained-variance trade-off and possible hybrid approaches.  
- Minor issue: some equations use dense notation and are hard to follow; presentation and variable explanations should be improved.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, novel contribution recommended for acceptance pending revisions addressing computational analysis, regularization justification, trade-off discussion, and clearer equations.
