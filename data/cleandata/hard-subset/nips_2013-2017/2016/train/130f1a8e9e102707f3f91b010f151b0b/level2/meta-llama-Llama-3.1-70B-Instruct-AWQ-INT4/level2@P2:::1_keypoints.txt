- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a multivariate online dictionary-learning method that adds a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large public neuroimaging dataset with improvements over traditional l1-regularized dictionary learning.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and comprehensive related work positioning.  
- Technical contribution: novel extension of online dictionary learning to include a Laplacian penalty for spatial smoothness.  
- Provides a detailed optimization analysis and an efficient algorithm for the proposed objective.  
- Experimental results show structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Outperforms state-of-the-art methods (SODL, CanICA) on explained variance and stability.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational-complexity analysis to assess scalability.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation and justification of hyperparameter selection.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: methodological novelty, strong empirical results, high potential impact in neuroimaging.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding a detailed complexity analysis and clear hyperparameter selection procedure.
