- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — an online dictionary learning method with a Laplacian penalty to produce sparse, piecewise-smooth spatial components ("blobs") for brain images.  
- Scalability: Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online framework, keeping computational efficiency while adding spatial structure at modest cost.  
- Motivation: Addresses need for scalable, interpretable, and stable ROI extraction for very large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Technical soundness: Well-formulated optimization using Block-Coordinate Descent and FISTA; algorithmic choices are appropriate and clearly described.  
- Experimental strength: Thorough evaluation on HCP with qualitative and quantitative metrics (explained variance, stability, predictive performance) and comparison to SODL and CanICA.  
- Learning-curve evidence: Table 1 compellingly shows the spatial regularizer’s benefit, especially in small-to-medium sample regimes.  
- Originality: First truly online, large-scale solver that integrates a Laplacian spatial smoothness prior for structured sparse decomposition in neuroimaging.  
- Significance: Produces more interpretable and reproducible components than existing methods and is suitable for consortium-scale data; planned Nilearn release increases impact.  
- Clarify dictionary update: Recommend adding analysis showing how the number of inner-loop FISTA iterations affects solution quality and overall runtime.  
- Discuss predictive trade-offs: Need deeper discussion of why non-sparse CanICA attains higher predictive accuracy and the trade-off between interpretability/stability and raw prediction.  
- Hyperparameter guidance: Provide more explicit practical recommendations for setting gamma (Laplacian weight) and tau (sparsity) beyond cross-validation on explained variance.
