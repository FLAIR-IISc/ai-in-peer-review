- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev and L1 regularization to extract fMRI components, aiming for spatial smoothness and sparsity.  
- Methodologically interesting twist but incremental—uses Sobolev+L1 to promote smoothness and sparsity simultaneously.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show superiority over simpler baselines; reported improvements are marginal.  
- Lacks strong quantitative comparisons with standard baselines (e.g., sparse dictionary learning, PCA); comparisons are absent or superficial.  
- Added complexity of combined regularization is not justified or shown to give meaningful practical gains.  
- Manuscript appears rushed: draft comments remain, notation is inconsistent, and exposition is unclear in places.  
- Theoretical motivation for the regularization terms is opaque, making core assumptions and necessity of the method hard to assess.  
- Recommends stronger evaluation (rigorous baselines, explicit ablation studies) and clearer explanation of the Sobolev constraint’s added value; without this, novelty and impact are limited.
