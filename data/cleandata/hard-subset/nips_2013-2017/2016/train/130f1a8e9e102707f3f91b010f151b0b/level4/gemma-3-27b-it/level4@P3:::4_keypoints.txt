- Proposes a multivariate online dictionary learning method for brain images combining L1 sparsity and a Sobolev (Laplacian) smoothness penalty to produce compact, blob-like components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning by adding spatial regularization to leverage inter-subject variability for region definition.  
- Demonstrates the method on HCP fMRI data and compares against CanICA and standard online dictionary learning (SODL).  

Strengths:
- Novelty: sensible combination of sparsity and smoothness for more interpretable, spatially localized components.  
- Scalability: online framework enables application to large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on a large public dataset (HCP) with baseline comparisons.  
- Theoretical grounding: clear mathematical formulation and algorithmic outline.  
- Implementation plan: authors intend to release code in Nilearn, aiding reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Modest improvements: gains over baselines are real but not consistently substantial, especially for explained variance.  
- Clarity and presentation: writing is dense, occasionally confusing, with organizational issues and draft comments present.  
- Insufficient comparison details: limited information on CanICA preprocessing and related trade-offs.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: analysis of α, γ, τ and guidance for tuning is superficial.  
- Limited limitations discussion: computational cost increase vs. SODL and other practical downsides are not thoroughly addressed.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Technically sound, novel approach addressing scalability and interpretability for fMRI decomposition.  
- Empirical results indicate potential benefits and planned code release supports reproducibility.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Modest empirical gains may not justify added complexity.  
- Presentation, hyperparameter analysis, and limitations discussion need substantial improvement.

Overall assessment:
- Rated weak accept; sound idea with promise but requires clearer presentation, deeper result analysis, and expanded discussion of limitations and hyperparameter sensitivity.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing clarity, providing more in-depth analyses, and expanding limitations/hyperparameter discussion.
