- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method using a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce piece-wise smooth, compact atoms.  
- Extends standard online dictionary-learning to extract structured, sparse components from brain images.  
- Main contribution: a novel regularizer that combines sparsity and smoothness to produce structured, denoised dictionaries.  
- Claims the approach better captures inter-subject variability in brain data.  
- Provides a detailed algorithmic description and discussion of convergence properties.  
- Experiments on a large public dataset show improved performance over CanICA and SODL.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and includes analysis of hyperparameter effects.  
- Strength: clear presentation, concise introduction, and well-organized experimental results.  
- Weakness: problem formulation is non-convex and the existence of a solution is not demonstrated.  
- Weakness: algorithm convergence properties are not rigorously established.  
- Weakness: writing needs minor improvements in places (e.g., line 146).  
- Overall: reviewer satisfied, recommends publication; rating 7/10 with confidence 8/10.
