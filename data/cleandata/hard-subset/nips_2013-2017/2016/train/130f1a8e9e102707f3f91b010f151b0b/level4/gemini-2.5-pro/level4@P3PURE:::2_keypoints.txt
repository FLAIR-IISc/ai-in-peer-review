- Proposes an online algorithm (SSMF) for learning structured dictionaries with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Model definition and algorithm are clear and follow the established framework of [14].  
- Main empirical weakness: paper fails to clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior over alternatives.  
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscientifically-justified criteria and quantitative measures to show SSMF’s superiority (e.g., smoother boundaries or unique identification of known connected regions).  
- Rebuttal addressed some concerns and improved the reviewer’s technical-quality score; authors should incorporate these results into the revision.  
- Novelty: primary contribution is extending [14] to include Sobolev priors for spatially structured dictionary atoms.  
- Potential impact: method is theoretically well-motivated and could be a useful, efficient tool for large-cohort neuroimaging and other applications (e.g., segmentation, regional sensing).  
- Manuscript suffers from poor editing, confusing descriptions, and numerous typographical errors that hinder readability.  
- Ambiguity in matrix decomposition equation (unclear whether transpose applies to group V^j or each V^j).  
- Missing reference for the HCP dataset (Line 73).  
- Grammar error: “The Graph-Net penalty can imposes...” (Line 83).
