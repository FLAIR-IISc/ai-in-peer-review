- Proposes Smooth-SODL: multivariate online dictionary learning combining L1 sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially structured, blob-like brain regions.  
- Tackles the neuroimaging problem of defining spatially meaningful ROIs without manual segmentation or cross-modality correspondences.  
- Extends Mairal et al.'s online dictionary learning to include spatial regularization while remaining computationally efficient (only 2–3× slower than standard online methods).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on HCP task fMRI: more interpretable dictionaries than SODL and CanICA, and better capture of inter-subject variability.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation across visual interpretability, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Shows particular advantage in small-data regimes where spatial regularization yields significant gains.  
- Algorithmic adaptation of Laplacian regularization is carefully executed and practically effective.  
- Paper lacks formal theoretical analysis: no bounds on reconstruction error or detailed study of how the Laplacian penalty affects learning dynamics.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., V vs v), unclear introduction of the Laplacian penalty (Eq. 4), and presence of an unremoved reviewer comment in the manuscript.  
- Originality is incremental: builds on existing structured dictionary learning but meaningfully adapts it to large-scale online neuroimaging.  
- Could better distinguish its contribution from prior multi-subject dictionary learning (e.g., Varoquaux [25]).  
- Hyperparameter selection discussion is thorough but could better justify why specific parameter ranges work best.  
- Significant practical impact: addresses a critical bottleneck in neuroimaging and can be useful for research and clinical applications.  
- Implementation is available in Nilearn, aiding reproducibility and adoption.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix clarity issues and strengthen theoretical/contextual discussion.
