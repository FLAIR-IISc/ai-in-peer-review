- Main contribution: Smooth-SODL adds a Laplacian regularization to online dictionary learning to enforce spatial smoothness in dictionary atoms.  
- Algorithm is scalable and efficient, reportedly only marginally slower than unstructured SODL.  
- Method evaluated on large fMRI data (Human Connectome Project) for interpretability, stability, and predictive power.  
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated, building on Mairal et al. (2010).  
- Novelty: integrating a spatial Laplacian prior into a scalable online framework is a valuable contribution for neuroimaging decompositions.  
- Primary weakness: empirical results do not convincingly show that the Laplacian penalty yields superior outcomes versus baselines.  
- Qualitative comparison (Figure 2a) is hard to assess because different anatomical slices are shown for different methods.  
- Paper lacks quantitative metrics for “structuredness” (e.g., spatial compactness, smoothness, contiguity) to support claims.  
- Should demonstrate neuroscientific value by recovering known functional networks or showing higher correspondence with established atlases.  
- Rebuttal added informative results that partially address empirical concerns; reviewer raised score and urges incorporation of these into the revision.  
- Presentation needs polishing: several typos, unclear descriptions, and leftover co-author comments noted.  
- Mathematical formalism issues: apparent inconsistency between Equation (7) and Algorithm 2; clarify signs/terms and that V refers to previous dictionary.  
- Reproducibility: provide explicit algorithm/pseudocode for solving the dictionary update sub-problem (Equation 8), e.g., FISTA, possibly in an appendix.  
- Figure 2c is unclear—clarify what is being predicted, which features are used, meaning of legends/colors, and why PCA baseline is omitted; R² claims are hard to verify from the plot.  
- Recommendation: acceptance conditional on substantial revision to strengthen empirical validation and improve clarity and presentation.
