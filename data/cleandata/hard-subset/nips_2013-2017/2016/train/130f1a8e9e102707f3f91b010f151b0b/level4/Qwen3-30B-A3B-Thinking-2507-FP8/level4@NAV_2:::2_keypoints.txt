- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm for neuroimaging, extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms.
- Method applied to empirical neuroimaging data, exploring tuning-parameter effects and comparing against two alternative methods.
- Model formulation and algorithms are clear and build on established framework, appearing technically sound.
- Empirical results do not clearly demonstrate the added benefit of the Sobolev prior; spatial contiguity and interpretability gains are not quantified.
- Figure 2 is inadequate for comparing spatial contiguity across methods because it lacks quantitative metrics and uses different slices per method.
- Reviewer requests specific, quantifiable neuroscience-related metrics to support claims that SSMF is “more structured.”
- After the authors’ rebuttal with additional results, the reviewer raised the technical rating from 2 to 3 and urges inclusion of those results in the final paper.
- Novelty: meaningful contribution by incorporating Sobolev priors into the online dictionary-learning framework.
- Potential impact: efficient online algorithm could be a useful tool in neuroscience and medical imaging and may extend to image segmentation and regional sensing.
- Presentation: overall acceptable organization but multiple editing issues and unclear descriptions hinder comprehension.
