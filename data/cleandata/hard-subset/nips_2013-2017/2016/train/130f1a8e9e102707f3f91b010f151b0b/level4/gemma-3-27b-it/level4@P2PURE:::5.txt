This paper addresses dictionary learning, specifically in the context of brain region analysis. The authors propose a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating Sobolev regularization to enforce structure. My primary concerns are as follows: 1) This work appears to primarily extend existing online dictionary learning methods (e.g., Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, I find the novelty to be limited and potentially below the threshold for acceptance at NIPS. 2) While Sobolev regularization is used to impose structure on the learned dictionary, the precise meaning of this “structure” remains unclear. If the intent is to encourage sparsity, it’s not immediately obvious why dictionary sparsity itself would yield substantial improvements in this application. The motivation for employing Sobolev regularization requires further justification. 3) I suspect a typographical error in Eq.(1); it should likely be written as [V<sup>1</sup>, V<sup>2</sup>,…, V<sup>r</sup>]<sup>T</sup>. 4) The derivation of Eq.(2) would benefit from additional explanation and analysis. Specifically, the rationale for using an l2 norm on the codes, rather than the l1 norm employed in Mairal (JMLR 2010), should be clarified. 5) Line 146 is unclear and requires attention. 6) The manuscript would benefit significantly from careful editing to address numerous typographical errors and improve overall clarity of the writing.
