- Proposal: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, contiguous “blob” atoms for neuroanatomically plausible brain regions.  
- Contribution: bridges scalable unstructured online methods and slower structured priors, enabling efficient atlas discovery on large datasets.  
- Model/algorithm: Smooth-SODL is well-formulated, adapting Block-Coordinate Descent from Mairal et al. and using FISTA for the dictionary update.  
- Complexity/convergence: authors analyze computational complexity and verify Mairal et al. assumptions to justify convergence (to stationary points implied).  
- Empirical evaluation: thorough comparisons to SODL and CanICA on the HCP dataset with both quantitative (explained variance, stability, predictive) and qualitative assessments.  
- Empirical finding: shows superior performance in low-to-medium data regimes (learning curve / Table 1).  
- Clarity issue: derivation of the dictionary update (Equation 7) is compressed and needs a more detailed, self-contained walkthrough.  
- Non-convexity discussion: should explicitly state that theoretical guarantees concern convergence to stationary points (clarification needed).  
- Hyper-parameter choice: data-adaptive schedule α_t ∼ t^{-1/2} is pragmatic and effective but lacks theoretical motivation or justification.
