- Presents a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for identifying functionally interesting brain regions from neuroimaging; timely and well-motivated application.  
- Major weakness: no theoretical analysis—no convergence guarantees, no learning-rate analysis, and no characterization of learned dictionary properties.  
- Without theory the method reads as a heuristic and its robustness and advantages over existing methods are unclear.  
- Manuscript quality is very poor with numerous grammatical issues and awkward phrasing that impede clarity.  
- Contains unacceptable leftover internal comments (e.g., "XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v"), indicating lack of proofreading.  
- Carelessness in presentation casts doubt on implementation quality and reported experimental results.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; authors should develop theoretical foundations and thoroughly rewrite the paper for clarity and professionalism before resubmission.
