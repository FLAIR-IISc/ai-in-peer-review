This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework with an additional Sobolev regularization for learning brain regions. The authors' contribution is an extension of the online dictionary learning framework (Mairal, JMLR 2010) with a Sobolev regularization term. My major comments are as follows:

1. The novelty of this paper is fairly weak, as it primarily builds upon existing work. The extension with Sobolev regularization may not be substantial enough to meet the NIPS standard.

2. The paper utilizes Sobolev regularization to impose structure constraints on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of "structure" is unclear. If the structure refers to sparsity, it is not evident why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would significantly improve the results. The motivation behind using Sobolev regularization is not well-explained.

3. There appears to be a mistake in Eq.(1). It should be [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T.

4. The construction of Eq.(2) requires more explanation and analysis. For instance, the choice of l2 norm over l1 norm for the codes, unlike in Mairal (JMLR 2010), is not justified.

5. The text does not provide enough context for line 146, making it difficult to understand its relevance.

6. The language of the paper requires refinement. There are several typos throughout the text that need correction.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should address these concerns by providing clearer explanations, justifying their design choices, and refining the writing.
