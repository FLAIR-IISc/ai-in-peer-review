This paper studies dictionary learning for delineating brain regions, proposing a large-scale online dictionary learning method augmented with a Sobolev regularizer to encode structure.

Major comments:
1) Novelty: The contribution appears to be an extension of online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010) with an added Sobolev penalty. As presented, this seems incremental and may not meet NeurIPS standards. Please clarify what is fundamentally new theoretically or algorithmically (e.g., a new optimization scheme, guarantees, or properties unique to the Sobolev term at scale).

2) Definition and motivation of “structure”: The paper does not clearly define the structural prior being enforced. Does “structure” mean spatial smoothness/continuity, sparsity, or another property (e.g., piecewise smoothness)? If the goal is sparsity, explain why sparsity in dictionary atoms—rather than (or in addition to) sparsity in codes—improves brain-region delineation. If the goal is smoothness (which a Sobolev norm typically induces), justify this choice over alternatives such as total variation or graph/Laplacian regularizers, and explain why Sobolev is particularly suitable for this data and task.

3) Possible typo in Eq. (1): It appears the expression should be [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T (stacked vertically). Please verify.

4) Construction of Eq. (2): Provide more detail on the modeling choices. In particular, why use an ℓ2 penalty on the codes instead of the ℓ1 sparsity used by Mairal et al. (2010)? Discuss the implications for sparsity, interpretability, and performance, and include empirical comparisons or ablations if possible.

5) Line 146: There seems to be a stray or unclear reference at or around line 146. Please clarify or remove.

6) Writing quality: The manuscript requires careful language editing. There are numerous grammatical errors and typos.

Overall, the topic is relevant and the focus on scalability is appealing, but the current version lacks clear novelty, a precise definition and motivation for the structural prior, and sufficient justification for key modeling decisions.
