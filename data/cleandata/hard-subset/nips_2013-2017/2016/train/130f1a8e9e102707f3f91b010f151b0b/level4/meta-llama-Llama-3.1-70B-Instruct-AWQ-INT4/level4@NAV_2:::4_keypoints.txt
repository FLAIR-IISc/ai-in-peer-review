- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev and L1 regularization to extract smooth, sparse functional components from fMRI data.  
- Introduces a multivariate online dictionary-learning algorithm applied to brain images.  
- Novelty: combining Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization within dictionary learning appears original.  
- Method is evaluated on real fMRI data and compared to state-of-the-art alternatives.  
- Strength: approach is interesting and relevant to neuroimaging applications.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation that encourages both smoothness and sparsity.  
- Weakness: empirical improvements over simpler methods are small or not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Weakness: manuscript presentation is rushed, with leftover draft comments and poor readability.  
- Weakness: explanations and methodological justifications are insufficiently detailed.  
- Clarity: writing is confusing and requires repeated reading to follow key points.  
- Significance: potentially valuable contribution, but impact is undermined by weak empirical evidence and presentation.  
- Recommendation tension: accept for novelty and real-world application vs reject/revise due to limited gains and poor manuscript quality.
