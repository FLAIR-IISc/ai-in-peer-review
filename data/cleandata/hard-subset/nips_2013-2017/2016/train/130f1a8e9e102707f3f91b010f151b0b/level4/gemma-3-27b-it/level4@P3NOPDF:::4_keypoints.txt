- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning combining Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization for fMRI component identification.  
- Central idea is potentially interesting and relevant to fMRI analysis.  
- Mathematical formulation appears correct, focusing on joint smoothness and sparsity.  
- Empirical improvements over baselines (standard dictionary learning, ICA) are modest and sometimes not statistically significant.  
- Manuscript lacks explanation of whether gains come from Sobolev, L1, or their combination; needs ablation study.  
- Choice and names of regularization parameters (lambda, Sobolev parameter) are not justified; sensitivity analysis missing.  
- Insufficient comparison to broader state-of-the-art fMRI methods (e.g., spatial clustering, advanced ICA variants).  
- Statistical reporting is weak; p-values or confidence intervals are not clearly provided.  
- Computational cost of the proposed method versus baselines is not reported.  
- Writing is dense, unclear, and contains draft comments and inconsistent notation, indicating rushed editing.  
- Biological interpretation of the Sobolev norm and its relation to fMRI spatial characteristics is vague and needs clarification.  
- Figures have poor labels/visual presentation and need improvement to convey results effectively.  
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision with stronger empirical evaluation, clearer theory, ablation studies, parameter justification, and improved presentation.
