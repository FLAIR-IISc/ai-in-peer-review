The authors extend dictionary learning models to incorporate a Sobolev prior, tackling an interesting problem. However, it is challenging to assess the impact or improvement of the newly proposed algorithm. The primary results figure relies on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified blobs on the brain, but this may not be immediately apparent to an untrained observer. Furthermore, in my experience with this type of decomposition, the data quality is often equally, if not more, important than the algorithm itself. By incorporating penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not entirely surprising that the results yield smoother blobs. This approach might be more beneficial in scenarios with limited data, but given the vast amount of data available through the Human Connectome Project, this does not appear to be a significant issue. I had expected this paper to further consolidate the differences between brain regions across subjects and utilize inter-subject differences and consistency as a means of validating the performance. Unfortunately, the validation presented in the paper seems subjective, and the benefits of the proposed method are not clearly demonstrated.
