- Initial technical quality rating raised from 2 to 3 after rebuttal; model and algorithm are clearly defined and methodologically sound within the framework of [14].  
- Main weakness: paper fails to convincingly demonstrate empirical benefits of the Sobolev prior — claims of “more structured” atoms are vague and unsupported by quantitative evidence.  
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscientifically-justified criteria and quantitative metrics (e.g., boundary smoothness, anatomical connectivity) to substantiate structure claims.  
- Rebuttal provided informative additional results; these should be incorporated into the revised manuscript with quantitative metrics and neuroscience validation.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of [14] by incorporating Sobolev priors to enforce spatial structure in dictionary atoms.  
- Potential impact: method is well-motivated and could be a valuable online tool for neuroimaging, medical imaging, and other applications like segmentation or regional sensing.  
- Manuscript needs careful copyediting: numerous typos and confusing descriptions reduce clarity.
