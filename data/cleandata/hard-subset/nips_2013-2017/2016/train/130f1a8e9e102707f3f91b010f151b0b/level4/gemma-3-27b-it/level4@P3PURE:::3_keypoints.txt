- Paper extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior — an interesting idea.
- Impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm are difficult to assess.
- Primary results rely on visual comparisons of clarity/consistency of brain regions.
- Visual results may not be clear to readers unfamiliar with the data or task.
- Data quality and preprocessing often matter as much or more than the algorithm.
- Smooth, spatially consistent regions are expected given the imposed penalties.
- Method might be more useful for sparse data, but less relevant given large datasets (e.g., HCP).
- The work misses leveraging inter-subject differences and consistency for validation.
- Validation is largely subjective rather than quantitative.
- Practical benefit and real-world advantage of the method remain unclear.
