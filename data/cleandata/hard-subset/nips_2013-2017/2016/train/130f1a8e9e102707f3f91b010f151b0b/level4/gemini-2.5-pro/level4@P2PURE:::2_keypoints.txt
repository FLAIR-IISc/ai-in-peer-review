- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm for multi-subject neuroimaging that incorporates a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity in atoms.  
- Method builds on prior framework [14] and is evaluated on empirical data with tuning-parameter sensitivity and comparisons to two baselines.  
- Initial technical assessment was below acceptance threshold, but the author rebuttal provided informative results that improved the reviewer’s score.  
- The model and optimization algorithm are clearly defined and appear technically solid.  
- Initial manuscript did not convincingly demonstrate empirical advantages of the Sobolev prior; Figure 2 made superiority claims hard to verify.  
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscientifically justified criteria and quantitative metrics to show atoms are smoother/more meaningful.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer raised technical-quality rating from 2 to 3 and urges inclusion of the new results in revision.  
- Novelty: main contribution is extending [14] with Sobolev priors to encourage spatially structured dictionary atoms — a notable contribution.  
- Potential impact: method is theoretically well-motivated and could be useful for large-scale neuroimaging and other applications (e.g., image segmentation).  
- Manuscript presentation is rushed, containing confusing descriptions, typographical errors, and leftover co-author comments.  
- Specific technical/presentation issues: possible transpose placement error in matrix decomposition, missing HCP dataset reference, grammatical errors, and a leftover bolded co-author sentence.  
- Mathematical clarity issues: sign inconsistency between Equations (6) and (8); ambiguity whether V in Eq.(6) is previous-iteration dictionary; need explicit solution to Eq.(6) (appendix).  
- Repetition and typos: duplicated phrase "a single," typo "th how," and other editing mistakes.  
- Figure issues: Figure 2 uses different slices across methods preventing direct visual comparison; Figure 3 is unclear about the prediction target, legends, missing PCA baseline, and ambiguous R^2 claims.
