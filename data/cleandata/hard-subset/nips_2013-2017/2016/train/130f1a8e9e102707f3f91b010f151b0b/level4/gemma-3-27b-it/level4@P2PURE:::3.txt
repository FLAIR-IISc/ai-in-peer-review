The authors propose extending dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior, which addresses an interesting problem. However, evaluating the impact and improvement offered by this new algorithm proves difficult. The primary results are presented through visual comparison of the clarity and consistency of identified brain regions, a comparison that may not be readily interpretable without specialized knowledge. Furthermore, based on experience with similar decomposition methods, data quality is often as crucial – if not more so – than the algorithm itself. Given the imposed penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, the resulting smoother regions are not unexpected. While this approach might be valuable for analyzing datasets with limited data, this benefit seems less relevant given the extensive data available through projects like the Human Connectome Project. I had anticipated this work would focus more on consolidating differences in brain regions *across* subjects, potentially leveraging inter-subject variations and consistency as a form of performance validation. The current validation appears subjective, and the practical benefit of the proposed approach remains unclear.
