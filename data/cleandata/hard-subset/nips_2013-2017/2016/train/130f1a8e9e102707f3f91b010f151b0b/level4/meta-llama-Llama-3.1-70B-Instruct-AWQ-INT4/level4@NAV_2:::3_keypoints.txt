- Proposal: multivariate online dictionary-learning for brain images with structured and sparse components.  
- Main novelty: incorporation of a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to enforce spatial smoothness on dictionary atoms.  
- Presentation: clear, well-structured description of algorithm and implementation.  
- Experiments: run on a large public dataset (Human Connectome Project).  
- Results: method extracts structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Quantitative comparison: outperforms SODL and CanICA on explained variance and atom stability.  
- Validation concern: evaluation relies on subjective visual comparisons that are unclear to untrained readers.  
- Method clarity issue: unclear explanation of how differences across subjects are consolidated.  
- Limitation: smoothness/spatial penalties may be unsurprising given abundant HCP data; benefits in low-data settings not shown.  
- Significance/originality: originality could be better emphasized and the significance of results more clearly explained.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Sobolev prior, effective experiments, superior metrics versus SOTA.  
- Arguments against acceptance: subjective validation, unclear consolidation mechanism, limited novelty given large dataset availability.  
- Overall: well-presented approach but validation and significance need improvement.
