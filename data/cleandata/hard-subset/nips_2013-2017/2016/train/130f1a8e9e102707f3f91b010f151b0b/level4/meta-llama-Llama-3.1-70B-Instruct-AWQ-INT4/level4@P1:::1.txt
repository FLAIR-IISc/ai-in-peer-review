This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework to find interesting brain regions. The topic itself is very interesting. However, the authors could strengthen their contribution by providing a theoretical analysis to show the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. Furthermore, it appears that the authors did not thoroughly review the draft before submission, as indicated by comments such as "146 XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v." left in the paper.

The paper's main idea is to extend the online dictionary-learning framework to learn structured regions representative of brain organization by incorporating a Laplacian penalty on the component maps. This approach is novel and has the potential to improve the interpretability of the extracted brain regions. However, the authors could provide more insight into the choice of the Laplacian penalty and its impact on the results.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods. However, the presentation of the results could be improved for better clarity and readability. The figures and tables are not clearly referenced in the text, making it difficult to follow the discussion of the results.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing methods for brain region extraction. However, they could further discuss the advantages and limitations of their approach compared to others.

The paper's quality, clarity, and originality are good, but the lack of theoretical analysis and the presence of errors in the draft detract from its overall quality. With further revisions to address these issues, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The topic is interesting and relevant to the NIPS community.
- The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to improve the interpretability of extracted brain regions.
- The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The lack of theoretical analysis to show the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
- The presence of errors in the draft, indicating a lack of thorough review before submission.
- The presentation of the results could be improved for better clarity and readability.
