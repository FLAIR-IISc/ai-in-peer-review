- Paper proposes an online dictionary-learning algorithm for multi-subject neuroimaging that extends [14] by adding a Sobolev prior to promote spatially contiguous atoms.
- Model specification and algorithmic framework are clear and built on established methods, but empirical benefits of the Sobolev prior are not convincingly demonstrated.
- Reviewer requests quantitative, neuroscience-grounded criteria to show that SSMF atoms are smoother, more structured, or recover connected regions compared to baselines.
- Rebuttal provided informative additional results that led the reviewer to raise the technical rating; the reviewer urges those results be included in the revision.
- Main novelty is the incorporation of Sobolev priors into the online dictionary-learning framework from [14].
- Potential impact is high for neuroimaging/medical imaging; method could be useful for other tasks like image segmentation and regional sensing.
- Manuscript needs better editing—confusing statements and typos hinder comprehension throughout.
- Matrix decomposition notation likely incorrect: question whether transpose on V^j applies to each individual V^j.
- Missing reference for the HCP dataset at Line 73.
- Grammar/typo issues: e.g., “The Graph-Net penalty can imposes ...”, repeated phrases “a single, a single”, and “We did an extensive study of th how”.
- Unresolved co-author comment appears as a bold sentence near Line 146.
- Equations inconsistent: sign disagreement between last lines of Eq.(6) and Eq.(8), and ambiguity whether V in Eq.(6) is the previous-iteration dictionary.
- Request to explicitly provide the mathematical/algorithmic solution to Eq.(6) (at least in an appendix).
- Figure 2 problematic: slices differ across methods so direct visual comparison is difficult and claim of “more structured” is not reconciled with the figure.
