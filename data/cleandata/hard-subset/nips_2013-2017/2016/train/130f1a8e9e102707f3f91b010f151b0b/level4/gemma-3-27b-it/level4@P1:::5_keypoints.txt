- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning for brain images combining L1 sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce smooth, compact “blob” atoms and capture inter-subject variability.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and claims scalability to large datasets with only 2–3× runtime increase.  
- Addresses an important neuroimaging problem: defining ROIs and handling inter-subject variability without biased segmentation or cross-modality alignment.  
- Combines sparsity and smoothness in a principled, model-based decomposition akin to PCA/ICA and related to clustering methods.  
- Strength: scalable online framework suitable for large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strength: mathematically principled use of sparsity and smoothness penalties for ROI definition.  
- Strength: experiments show more interpretable dictionaries and better capture of inter-subject variability vs. standard dictionary learning.  
- Strength: clear connection to existing literature and prior work.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—adding a Sobolev penalty may not meet top-tier novelty standards.  
- Weakness: definition and motivation of “structure” and choice of the Sobolev penalty are insufficiently justified.  
- Weakness: technical exposition has clarity issues, minor errors, and requires proofreading.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity acknowledged but lacks in-depth sensitivity analysis or practical guidance.  
- Specific comment: Equation (1) likely has a transpose error (Xi ≈ VUi or X = VU).  
- Specific comment: motivation for using an L2 norm on codes Ui (Eq. 2) instead of L1 is not explained.  
- Specific comment: line 146 is incomplete; manuscript contains typos and needs careful proofreading.  
- Specific comment: comparison to CanICA should include more detailed discussion of CanICA’s computational cost and limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: scalable, principled approach with promising empirical improvements and good connection to prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, weak motivation for Sobolev penalty, and insufficiently polished technical presentation.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and potentially valuable but incremental and in need of clearer motivation, better technical clarity, and proofreading — recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
