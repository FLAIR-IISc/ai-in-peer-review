- Paper proposes an online algorithm extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.
- Algorithmic framework is clear and follows an established, solid approach from [14].
- Paper does not clearly demonstrate empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior; improvements are described qualitatively but lack explicit quantitative evidence.
- Figure 2 presentation prevents direct comparison across methods (different slices used); unclear in what sense SSMF is "more structured."
- Reviewer requests neuroscientifically-justified criteria and quantitative metrics (e.g., boundary smoothness, functional connectivity recovery) to demonstrate improvements.
- Rebuttal provided informative results addressing some concerns; reviewer urges including these in the final paper.
- Novelty is primarily the incorporation of a Sobolev prior into the [14] framework.
- Potential impact is positive: an efficient online algorithm with spatial regularization could be useful for neuroscience and medical imaging and possibly other tasks (e.g., segmentation).
- Manuscript contains multiple presentation issues, typos, and unclear phrasing that hinder comprehension.
- Specific mathematical/notation issues: ambiguity whether transpose in matrix decomposition applies per V^j or to the whole set.
- Missing reference for the HCP dataset (line 73).
- Typo: "The Graph-Net penalty can imposes..." (line 83).
- Unaddressed co-author comment appears in bolded sentence (line 146).
- Possible sign disagreement and consistency issues in update equations (Eq. (6) vs Eq. (8)); clarify whether V denotes previous-iteration dictionary and the optimization variable for the j-th column.
