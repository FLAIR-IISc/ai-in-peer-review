- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an extension of online dictionary learning with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to impose spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Motivation: standard sparse dictionary learning produces scattered activations; Smooth-SODL yields compact, blob-like structures that align better with brain organization.  
- Maintains online learning property, enabling scalability to large datasets (e.g., Human Connectome Project, 500 subjects).  
- Computational cost increases only ~2–3x compared to standard SODL while producing more interpretable, spatially structured dictionaries.  
- Demonstrates better capture of inter-subject variability across small, medium, and large-scale datasets.  
- Addresses practical neuroimaging challenge of ROI definition by exploiting population-level statistical variability rather than ad hoc segmentation.  
- Aligns with prior population-level functional mapping work but needs clearer distinction from those prior methods.  
- Strength: method is well-motivated for spatially structured brain imaging patterns.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on a large-scale HCP dataset demonstrating scalability.  
- Strength: comprehensive comparative analysis with SODL and CanICA across interpretability, explained variance, stability, and prediction metrics.  
- Strength: implementation provided in Nilearn, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Strength: spatial regularization yields large gains in small-data regimes (reported 14× improvement in explained variance over SODL).  
- Technical: Laplacian penalty effectively enforces blob-like structures with sound theoretical optimization analysis and convergence guarantees.  
- Clarification needed: justify choice of Laplacian penalty versus alternatives (e.g., total variation, anisotropic diffusion).  
- Clarification needed: more precise articulation of how sparsity and spatial structure interact in the model.  
- Clarification needed: justify use of ridge (l2) for codes instead of l1 and discuss impact on interpretability/performance.  
- Minor technical issues to fix: Equation (1) should use V not V^T; line 146 contains an incomplete sentence fragment.  
- Suggestion: briefly discuss potential applications beyond brain imaging to strengthen NIPS relevance.  
- Overall assessment: solid, incremental but meaningful contribution to neuroimaging ML; technically sound and well-evaluated; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications.
