- Extends online dictionary learning to enforce spatially structured, sparse atoms using an ℓ1-type constraint and a 3D Laplacian penalty for piecewise-smooth maps.  
- Codes estimated per-sample with ridge (optionally ℓ1) penalties; optimization via online block-coordinate descent with per-atom FISTA updates.  
- Scales comparably to standard SODL with ~2–3× overhead from the Laplacian term and an optional FFT speedup.  
- Positioned against unstructured SODL (scattered atoms) and TV/GraphNet structured methods (slow, not online), and compared to multi-subject PCA/ICA (CanICA).  
- Evaluated on 500-subject HCP task fMRI (p≈260k, k=40): produces more coherent, denoised, and reproducible atoms than SODL.  
- Achieves competitive explained variance, improves early-sample predictive performance, and remains computationally practical.  
- Objective, constraints, and algorithmic details are clearly specified; practical choices for step sizes and schedules are discussed.  
- Convergence arguments adapted from Mairal et al. (2010), but a formal theorem specific to the Laplacian and compact constraints is missing.  
- Empirical scaling and stability results are convincing, but reported metrics lack confidence intervals or variability estimates.  
- Experimental comparisons are comprehensive and fair, including qualitative maps, EV, dictionary stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Reproducibility is promising: implementation will be released and hyper-parameter choices/tuning strategies are listed.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized but contains typos and some notation inconsistencies that hinder readability.  
- Suggested clarifications: state finite-sample surrogate explicitly, summarize assumptions for convergence with Laplacian, clarify ridge vs ℓ1 code choices, and detail projection mechanics for constraints.  
- Need more guidance on γ schedule and its interaction with At[j,j]/t, plus practical parameter ranges and sensitivity analyses.  
- Originality is moderate-to-strong: novel integration of Laplacian prior into online dictionary learning with efficient per-atom updates.  
- Significance is high for large-scale neuroimaging due to improved interpretability/stability while preserving scalability.  
- Strengths: scalable integration of spatial smoothness and sparsity, practical FISTA per-atom update, strong empirical evidence, comprehensive comparisons, planned open-source release.  
- Weaknesses: limited formal analysis specific to the new regularizer, missing uncertainty estimates in experiments, mixed behavioral prediction results, and minor presentation issues.  
- Recommendations: add a succinct convergence theorem/assumptions, justify γ and α schedules with sensitivity analyses, report variability across seeds/splits, and test smoothing/preprocessing effects on baselines.  
- After rebuttal: authors addressed convergence concern; reviewer requests an explicit assumptions/theorem in the final paper and raised clarity score from 2 to 3.
