- Proposes an online dictionary learning method combining L1 and Sobolev regularization to learn sparse, smooth dictionary atoms for fMRI ROI discovery.
- Novelty: non-trivial extension of Mairal et al. (2010) by incorporating spatial regularization into online dictionary learning.
- Addresses a relevant neuroimaging problem by offering a data-driven approach to define ROIs and handle inter-subject variability.
- Uses an online framework suitable for large, high-dimensional fMRI datasets, mitigating computational issues.
- Extensive evaluation on the HCP dataset with comparisons to CanICA and SODL demonstrates competitive performance.
- Improvement over existing methods is present but often marginal, especially in explained variance and as dataset size grows.
- Claims about better capturing inter-subject variability are not consistently or convincingly supported by the provided metrics.
- Manuscript presentation is rough (e.g., leftover draft comments) and the writing is sometimes dense and confusing.
- Hyperparameter tuning discussion is insufficient and lacks practical guidance for researchers.
- Related work coverage could be broadened to include more recent advances in spatially regularized dictionary learning.
- Implementation availability via Nilearn is a positive for reproducibility and adoption.
- Recommendation: technically sound and valuable contribution but should be accepted with minor revisions to improve clarity, report key results more clearly, provide hyperparameter guidance, and discuss limitations/future work.
