- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a structured sparse online dictionary learning method for brain imaging that adds a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness on dictionary atoms.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning to extract spatially coherent, interpretable brain regions outperforming SODL and CanICA.  
- Demonstrates on HCP data that Smooth-SODL yields more structured, denoised dictionaries with comparable runtime (≈3× SODL) and better inter-subject variability capture.  
- Thorough empirical validation on a large public dataset showing qualitative (blob-like structures) and quantitative (reproducibility, behavioral prediction) improvements.  
- Plans to release implementation via Nilearn, improving reproducibility.  
- Critical weakness: no theoretical analysis—missing convergence guarantees, rates, and error bounds expected for a NIPS submission.  
- Problem is acute given the paper’s novel optimization focus despite citing prior work claiming convergence.  
- Critical weakness: editorial problems (e.g., leftover placeholder comment) indicate insufficient proofreading and undermine professionalism.  
- Overall judgment: valuable empirical work but lacking theoretical grounding and polish; recommend rejection until those issues are addressed.
