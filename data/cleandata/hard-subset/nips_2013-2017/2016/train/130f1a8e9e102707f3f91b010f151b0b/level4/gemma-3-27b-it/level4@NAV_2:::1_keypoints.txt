- Proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, compact ("blob") dictionary atoms for brain imaging.  
- Core idea: learn structured population-level representations from neuroimaging data instead of using predefined ROIs.  
- Builds on prior clustering and ICA/PCA-based functional mapping work and adapts online learning for high-dimensional neuroimaging.  
- Strength: bridges scalable online learning with spatially structured representations needed in brain imaging.  
- Strength: leverages inter-subject variability to define brain regions and demonstrates results on a large HCP dataset.  
- Strength: produces more interpretable dictionaries than traditional sparse dictionary learning and compares favorably to ICA (CanICA).  
- Strength: provides running-time comparisons and scalability discussion versus existing methods (SODL, CanICA).  
- Weakness: no rigorous theoretical analysis justifying the effectiveness of the Laplacian penalty or guidance on choosing regularization parameter γ.  
- Weakness: empirical hyperparameter study is helpful but does not replace theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: writing quality issues and presence of internal comments indicate insufficient manuscript preparation.  
- Weakness: related-work discussion is incomplete; lacks broader review of spatially regularized dictionary methods (e.g., total variation).  
- Weakness: novelty is somewhat incremental and empirical improvements over existing methods are not always substantial.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem, proposes a novel scalable extension, shows promising HCP results, and offers practical scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks theoretical justification and parameter-selection guidance, has writing issues, limited novelty, and only modest empirical gains.
