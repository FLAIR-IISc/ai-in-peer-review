1. Paper proposes an online algorithm for structured dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms and compares it to two alternatives on neuroimaging data.

2. Model definition and algorithm follow a well-established framework and appear technically sound.

3. Presentation undercuts technical clarity: the paper does not clearly demonstrate empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior.

4. Figure 2 fails to show quantitative evidence (e.g., smoother boundaries or recovery of known connected regions) that SSMF outperforms alternatives.

5. Reviewer requests neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measurements to support the claim that SSMF yields more structured atoms.

6. Rebuttal provided informative additional results that improved the technical rating from 2 to 3; authors should incorporate those results in the revision.

7. Novelty: notable and original extension of [14] by incorporating Sobolev priors to encourage spatial structure.

8. Potential impact: an efficient online algorithm for Sobolev-prior dictionary learning could be valuable for neuroscience/medical imaging and other tasks like segmentation and regional sensing.

9. Paper reads like a draft and requires significant editing to fix confusing descriptions and typos.

10. Math/notation issues: unclear transpose placement in decomposition between lines 67–68 and disagreement/sign errors between Equations (6) and (8), plus ambiguity about variable V in Eq.(6).

11. Missing or incorrect references/phrasing: HCP dataset reference missing (line 73), and grammar errors like "The Graph-Net penalty can imposes ..." (line 83) and an apparent co-author comment left bold on line 146.

12. Minor textual errors: repetition of "a single" (line 157) and incomplete sentence "We did an extensive study of th how" (line 169).

13. Methodological clarity: Section 3.3 should explicitly provide the mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6), at least in the appendix.

14. Figure issues: Figure 2 uses different slice views that hinder direct comparison and does not substantiate the "more structured" claim; Figure 3 is unclear about the prediction task, unexplained legends, missing PCA baseline, and ambiguous R^2>0.1 claims.
