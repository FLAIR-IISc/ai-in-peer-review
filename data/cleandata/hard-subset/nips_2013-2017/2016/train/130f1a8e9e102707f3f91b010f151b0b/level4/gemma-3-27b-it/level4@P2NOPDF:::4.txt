This paper introduces an under-complete dictionary learning approach incorporating combined Sobolev and L1 regularization, applied to the task of identifying functional components within fMRI data. The core idea is promising; the novelty lies in the specific combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization to simultaneously promote smoothness and sparsity in the learned components. However, the reported results do not demonstrate a substantial advantage over simpler, existing methods, and the extent of any improvement is not convincingly justified. 

Furthermore, the manuscript suffers from a lack of polish, suggesting a hurried preparation. Instances of draft comments remain within the text (e.g., line 146), and the writing is frequently dense, tedious, and occasionally confusing. This hinders clear understanding of the methodology and findings. 

Specifically, while the motivation for leveraging dictionary learning for fMRI analysis is sound, the justification for *combining* Sobolev and L1 regularization requires more thorough explanation. The authors claim this combination encourages both smoothness and sparsity, but a more detailed discussion of the interplay between these two regularizers, and how they specifically benefit functional component discovery, is needed. What are the theoretical advantages of this combined approach compared to using either regularization term in isolation?

The empirical evaluation also needs strengthening. The improvements reported appear marginal, and the statistical significance of these improvements is not adequately addressed. A more comprehensive comparison to relevant baselines, including variations of dictionary learning with different regularization schemes (e.g., purely L1, purely Sobolev, other combinations), would be valuable. The choice of parameters for each method should be clearly explained and ideally justified through cross-validation. 

The presentation of results could also be improved. The figures and tables are not always clearly labeled or explained, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed method. For instance, visual comparisons of the learned components from the proposed method and the baselines would be helpful to illustrate the benefits of the combined regularization.

Finally, the manuscript would benefit from a careful revision for clarity and conciseness. Several sentences are unnecessarily complex and could be rephrased for better readability. Addressing the remaining draft comments and ensuring consistent terminology throughout the paper are also essential steps towards improving the overall quality of the submission.

**Summary of concerns:**

*   **Limited Improvement:** The empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage over simpler methods.
*   **Insufficient Justification:** The benefits of the combined Sobolev/L1 regularization are not fully explained or theoretically grounded.
*   **Lack of Polish:** The manuscript contains draft comments and suffers from clarity and readability issues.
*   **Incomplete Baseline Comparison:** The evaluation lacks a comprehensive comparison to relevant baseline methods.
*   **Presentation Issues:** Figures and tables could be improved for better understanding.
