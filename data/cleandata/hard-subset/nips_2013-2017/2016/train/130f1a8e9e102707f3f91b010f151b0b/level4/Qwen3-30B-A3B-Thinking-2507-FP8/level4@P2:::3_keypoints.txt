- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty for spatial smoothness in brain image decompositions.  
- Key innovations: (1) Laplacian regularization integrated into online dictionary learning with modest runtime overhead (~3× SODL).  
- Key innovations: (2) Theoretical convergence guarantees provided (Theorem 1).  
- Key innovations: (3) Empirical improvements in interpretability and reproducibility across dataset sizes demonstrated via learning curves.  
- Technical soundness: Laplacian penalty is well-justified and convergence/runtime analysis is convincing.  
- Scalability: Runtime (340s vs 110s for SODL) shows method is computationally practical compared to slow alternating minimization.  
- Novelty: Uniquely brings spatial regularization into online dictionary learning, bridging efficiency and spatial interpretability.  
- Significance: Addresses ROI definition in neuroimaging and captures inter-subject variability; provides a Nilearn implementation for practical use.  
- Validation weakness: Primary evidence is visual brain maps; lacks objective metrics linking spatial structure to interpretability (e.g., behavioral prediction).  
- Data-scale clarity: HCP (500 subjects) is not very large for fMRI; paper should clarify why spatial regularization helps at this scale (e.g., 50 vs 500 comparisons).  
- Comparison weakness: Limited analysis of why Smooth-SODL outperforms tCanICA (e.g., role of spatial structure vs hard-thresholding in SNR).  
- Pro/Con summary: Pros—minimal runtime cost, theory, small-data advantage, neuroimaging relevance; Cons—subjective validation, unclear benefit at medium scale, limited explanatory comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, conditional on adding quantitative interpretability metrics and clarifying the small-data advantage and comparison explanations.
