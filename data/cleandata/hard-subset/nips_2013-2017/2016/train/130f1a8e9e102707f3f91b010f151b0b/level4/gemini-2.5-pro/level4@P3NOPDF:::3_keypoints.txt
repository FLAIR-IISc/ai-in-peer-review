- Introduces a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial smoothness in dictionary learning components.  
- The direction of applying structured priors is well-motivated and interesting.  
- Primary weakness is validation methodology: relies on subjective visual comparisons of spatial components.  
- Visual inspection is hard to interpret without domain expertise and limits independent verification.  
- Producing smoother, contiguous components is an expected outcome of adding smoothness penalties, so this alone is weak evidence of superiority.  
- Lacks demonstrations that smoothness yields neuroscientifically meaningful, reproducible, or predictive improvements.  
- Using high-quality Human Connectome Project data reduces clarity on the prior’s necessity or advantage in data-rich settings.  
- Paper would be strengthened by objective quantitative validation (e.g., inter-subject consistency) to show practical benefits.  
- Overall: sound modeling idea but insufficient, mostly subjective evaluation leaves practical benefits unclear.
