The authors extend the model for learning a dictionary to incorporate Sobolev's theory. This is an interesting problem, but it is difficult to assess the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main results depend on comparing the clarity/consistency of the identified spots on the brain, but this is not obvious to the untrained eye. Also, in my experience with this type of decomposition, the algorithm is as important (if not more) as the data itself. By including penalties for smoothness and spatial stability, it is not surprising that you get smooth spots. Perhaps this is more useful for distinguishing areas with less data, but with the vast amount of accessible data with the human connectome project, this does not seem to be an issue. I hoped that this paper would do more to consolidate the differences in brain regions across subjects and perhaps use inter-subject differences/consistency as a verification of performance. The verification presented here seems very subjective and it is not clear what its benefit is.
