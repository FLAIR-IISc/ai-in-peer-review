

This paper introduces an online algorithm for learning structured dictionaries to characterize neuroimaging data across individuals, extending a well-established framework [14] by incorporating a Sobolev prior to promote spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms. The authors applied their method to empirical data, analyzed tuning parameter effects, and benchmarked performance against two alternative approaches. Technical quality: Initially rated marginally below 3, this assessment was revised to 3 following the authors' rebuttal. The model definition was clear, and the algorithm followed a solid, established framework [14]. However, the paper initially failed to clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior in the results. Specifically, Figure 2 did not quantify how SSMF outperformed the alternatives—e.g., whether spatial boundaries were smoother, or if known connected brain regions were uniquely identified. The claim that SSMF results were "more structured" lacked neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative metrics. The rebuttal provided informative results, justifying the upgrade to a 3. The authors should include these results in revision and explicitly quantify the structural improvement of SSMF atoms (using neuroscience-relevant metrics) and demonstrate their neuroscientific meaningfulness. Novelty/originality: The paper offers notable originality in extending [14] with Sobolev priors for spatially structured dictionary atoms. Potential impact/usefulness: Dictionary learning is valuable for group-level neuroimaging analysis, and this efficient online algorithm with spatial priors provides a useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging. The method could also benefit applications like image segmentation or regional sensing. Presentation: The paper’s organization is acceptable but suffers from poor editing. Key issues include: (1) Matrix decomposition (Lines 67–68): Unclear if the transpose applies to each $V^j$ or the group; (2) Line 73: Missing HCP dataset reference; (3) Line 83: "can imposes" (typo); (4) Line 146: Unfixed bolded co-author comment; (5) Equations (6) and (8): Sign inconsistency ($V^j + A_t[j,j]^{-1}(V A_t^j - B_t^j)$ vs. $A_t[j,j] V^j - (V A_t^j - B_t^j)$) and $V$’s definition in Equation (6); (6) Line 157: "a single, a single"; (7) Line 169: "We did an extensive study of th how"; (8) Section 3.3 (near Line 147): Lack of explicit solution to Equation (6); (9) Figure 2: Results shown at inconsistent slices (horizontal/sagittal), hindering direct comparison; the claim of "more structured" SSMF results lacks justification; (10) Figure 3: Unclear prediction task (e.g., regression on behavioral metrics via $U_i$?), unexplained legends, and absence of PCA baseline mentioned in caption; Line 224–227’s reference to $R^2 > 0.1$ is unclear.
