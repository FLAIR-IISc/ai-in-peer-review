This paper investigates dictionary learning within the context of brain region analysis, specifically presenting a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating Sobolev regularization. 

My primary concerns are outlined below:

1) The contribution appears to be an extension of existing online dictionary learning methods (e.g., Mairal, JMLR 2010) through the addition of a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, the novelty seems limited and may not fully meet the standards expected at NIPS.

2) While Sobolev regularization is employed to enforce structure on the learned dictionary, the precise definition of this “structure” remains unclear. If the intended structure relates to sparsity, the rationale for why dictionary sparsity specifically leads to performance gains in this application is not adequately explained. The motivation for utilizing Sobolev regularization therefore requires further clarification.

3) There appears to be a potential error in the presentation of Eq.(1); it should likely be represented as [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T.

4) The derivation and justification for Eq.(2) would benefit from more detailed explanation. Specifically, the choice of an l2 norm on the codes, as opposed to the l1 norm used in Mairal (JMLR 2010), requires further discussion.

5) Line 146 is unclear and requires attention.

6) The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading and refinement of the language; numerous typos were observed throughout the text.
