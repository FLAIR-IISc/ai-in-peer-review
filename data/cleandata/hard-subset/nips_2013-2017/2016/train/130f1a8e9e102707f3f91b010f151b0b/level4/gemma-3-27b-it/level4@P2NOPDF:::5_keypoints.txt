- Paper proposes large-scale online dictionary learning with a Sobolev regularization term for brain region analysis.  
- Main concern: novelty is limited—appears to be a direct extension of Mairal et al. (2010) and may be an incremental contribution not meeting NIPS standards.  
- Use of Sobolev regularization to impose “structure” is vague; the paper does not clearly define the structure being enforced.  
- Motivation for Sobolev regularization and how it specifically benefits brain region learning is insufficiently justified.  
- If the goal is sparsity, the rationale for achieving performance gains via dictionary “structure” versus conventional sparsity is unclear.  
- Possible error in Equation (1): the transpose likely should apply to the concatenated vector [V1, V2, …, Vr]T.  
- Equation (2) needs clarification and justification, particularly the choice of an l2 norm on codes instead of the l1 norm used by Mairal et al.; discuss trade-offs.  
- Line 146 is unclear and requires revision.  
- Manuscript needs careful editing to fix numerous typos and awkward phrasing that hinder clarity.
