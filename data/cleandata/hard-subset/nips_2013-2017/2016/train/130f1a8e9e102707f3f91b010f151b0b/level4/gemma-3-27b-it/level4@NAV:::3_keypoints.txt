- Paper proposes a Sobolev-prior extension to dictionary learning addressing a potentially valuable problem in neuroimaging decomposition.
- Practical impact and demonstrable improvement of the new algorithm are not convincingly quantified.
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of identified brain regions, which depend heavily on expert interpretation and may be opaque to non-specialists.
- The smoother, more spatially consistent results are expected given the smoothness and spatial-consistency penalties, limiting novelty of the outcome.
- Method may help in low-data settings but its utility is less convincing given large datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- The manuscript underutilizes inter-subject variability and should better leverage differences and consistencies across subjects to validate performance.
- Current validation is largely subjective, leaving the practical benefits and robustness of the method unclear.
