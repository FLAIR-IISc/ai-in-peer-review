The authors extend dictionary learning by incorporating a Sobolev prior. While this is an interesting direction, it is difficult to evaluate the impact or improvement of the proposed algorithm. The main results rely on visual comparisons of the clarity and consistency of detected brain “blobs,” which is not convincing, particularly to readers without specialized training. In my experience with this type of decomposition, the data are at least as important as the algorithm. Given the added smoothness and spatial-consistency penalties, obtaining smoother blobs is expected. This might be helpful for isolating regions when data are limited, but with the abundance of data available from the Human Connectome Project, data scarcity does not appear to be the limiting factor. I had hoped the paper would do more to consolidate brain regions across subjects and use inter-subject differences or consistency as a validation of performance. As presented, the validation is highly subjective, and the benefits of the method are not clearly demonstrated.
