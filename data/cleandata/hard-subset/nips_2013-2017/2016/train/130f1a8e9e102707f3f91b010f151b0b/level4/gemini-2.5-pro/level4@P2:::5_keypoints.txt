- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty and l1-ball atoms to produce spatially smooth brain maps.  
- Scalable and practical: builds on Mairal et al.'s online framework and adds only a 2–3x runtime overhead, enabling use on massive neuroimaging datasets.  
- Improved interpretability: Laplacian regularization yields smoother, less noisy, contiguous components that are more meaningful for neuroscientists.  
- Strong empirical validation: comprehensive experiments on HCP data comparing against SODL and CanICA across explained variance, stability, and predictive tasks, showing gains especially with limited data.  
- Incremental novelty: core technical contribution is largely the integration of a standard Laplacian regularizer into existing online dictionary learning, limiting conceptual originality.  
- Modeling choices need clearer justification: why Laplacian vs alternatives (e.g., Total Variation) and why use an l2 penalty on codes instead of l1 should be better motivated.  
- Clarity and presentation issues: manuscript has typos, grammatical errors, and readability problems that should be corrected.  
- Notational inconsistency: Xi is described as a p-dimensional row vector but used as a column vector in the objective; notation should be clarified.  
- Opaque derivation step: the underbraced term in Equation (7) is referenced to [16] without naming or briefly explaining it — make this explicit.  
- Recommended fixes: better articulate conceptual novelty, justify modeling assumptions, and thoroughly proofread and clarify technical derivations.
