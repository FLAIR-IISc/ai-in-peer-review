- Proposal: multivariate online dictionary-learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to produce spatially smooth, sparse “blob”-like dictionary atoms.  
- Motivation: exploit inter-subject variability in neuroimaging to define ROIs from functional images.  
- Novelty/positioning: extends Mairal et al. (2010) by combining sparsity and spatial smoothness, distinct from clustering, PCA, and ICA approaches.  
- Method: clear Smooth-SODL formulation with algorithmic details (BCD, FISTA) well described.  
- Scalability: inherits online learning and is demonstrated on the large HCP dataset.  
- Results presentation: relies heavily on visual comparisons of atoms; quantitative metrics (explained variance, reproducibility, behavioral prediction) included but interpretation is mixed.  
- Ablation finding: spatial prior yields clear benefits in low-data regimes (Table 1).  
- Weakness — baselines: comparison to CanICA may be unfair; needs comparison to spatially regularized ICA or other appropriate baselines.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: hyperparameter tuning discussion (and the data-adaptive α choice) is underdeveloped and needs more practical guidance.  
- Weakness — neuroanatomical validity: connection between learned atoms and known anatomy is weak; needs anatomical labeling and atlas comparisons.  
- Writing: generally good but could be more concise and reduce redundancy.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses a relevant problem, technically sound extension, scalable, and beneficial in low-data settings.  
- Rejection arguments: primary results are subjective visual assessments, quantitative gains with large data are unclear, comparisons and anatomical validation insufficient.  
- Overall: worthwhile and technically sound contribution whose significance could be strengthened with additional experiments and clearer validation.
