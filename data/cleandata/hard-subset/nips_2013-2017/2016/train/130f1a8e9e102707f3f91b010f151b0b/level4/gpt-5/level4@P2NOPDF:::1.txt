This paper presents a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for identifying salient brain regions in neuroimaging data. The problem is important and the topic is compelling.

However, the manuscript lacks theoretical analysis to support the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. There are no guarantees or discussion of convergence, complexity, identifiability, or approximation quality, nor a comparison to existing theoretical results on online dictionary learning. Without such analysis, it is difficult to assess the soundness and generality of the approach.

In addition, the presentation appears insufficiently polished. The draft does not seem to have been carefully proofread before submission. For example, an editorial note remains in the text (around line 146): “XXX: I don’t understand the switch between V and v.” This also highlights unclear or inconsistent notation that should be clarified.

Overall, the idea is promising and the application is interesting, but the paper requires (i) theoretical justification of the algorithm and its properties, and (ii) thorough editing to remove placeholders, resolve notational inconsistencies, and improve clarity.
