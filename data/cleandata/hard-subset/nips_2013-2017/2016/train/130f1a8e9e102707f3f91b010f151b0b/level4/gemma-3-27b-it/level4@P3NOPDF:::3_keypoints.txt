- Paper proposes adding a Sobolev prior to dictionary learning to improve clarity and consistency of identified brain regions.  
- The problem is interesting but the presented results make impact difficult to evaluate.  
- Primary evaluation is visual comparison of “blobs,” which is subjective and hard for non-specialist reviewers to judge.  
- Apparent improvement may simply reflect the expected effect of adding smoothness/spatial consistency regularization.  
- With large datasets like the Human Connectome Project, gains from enforcing smoothness may be limited.  
- Major concern: lack of quantitative evaluation and reliance on subjective validation.  
- The paper should demonstrate benefits beyond visual smoothness to be convincing.  
- A strong validation approach would leverage inter-subject variability and consistency across a cohort.  
- Examples of valuable quantitative checks: showing learned dictionary features are consistent across subjects or correlate with known subject characteristics.  
- Current validation does not convincingly show superiority over existing dictionary learning or standard smoothing methods.  
- Authors should analyze parameter sensitivity and compare against established methods using quantitative metrics.
