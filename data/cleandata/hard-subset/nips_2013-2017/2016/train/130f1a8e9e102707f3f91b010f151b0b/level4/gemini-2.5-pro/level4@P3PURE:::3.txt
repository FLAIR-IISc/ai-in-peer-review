This paper extends dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior. While this is an interesting direction, the paper makes it difficult to judge the impact or improvement offered by the newly proposed algorithm.

The main results figure relies on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified brain regions, but any improvement is not readily apparent to a reader without domain expertise. Moreover, the primary benefit of the model appears to be the generation of smoother, more spatially consistent components. This is an expected outcome, given that the model directly penalizes a lack of smoothness and spatial consistency. The results, therefore, do not seem surprising.

While such a prior might be useful for isolating regions in data-sparse scenarios, its necessity is less clear when applied to large-scale datasets like the Human Connectome Project, where data availability is not a primary issue. A more compelling evaluation could have focused on consolidating findings of brain regions across subjects, perhaps using inter-subject consistency as an objective performance metric.

Overall, the validation presented is highly subjective, and the practical benefits of the proposed method are not clearly demonstrated.
