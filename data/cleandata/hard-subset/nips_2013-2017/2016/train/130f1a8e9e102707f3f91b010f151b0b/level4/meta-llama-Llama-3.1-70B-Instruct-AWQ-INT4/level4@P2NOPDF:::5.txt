This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework with an additional Sobolev regularization for structure in the application of brain region learning. The authors' main contribution is the incorporation of Sobolev regularization into the online dictionary learning framework. However, I have several major concerns regarding the paper's novelty, clarity, and technical soundness.

Firstly, the paper appears to be an extension of the online dictionary learning framework (Mairal, JMLR 2010) with the addition of a Sobolev regularization term. In my opinion, this extension does not provide sufficient novelty to meet the NIPS standard. The authors should clearly explain how their work differs from existing methods and what significant improvements they offer.

Secondly, the paper utilizes Sobolev regularization to impose structure constraints on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of "structure" is not clearly defined. If the structure refers to sparsity, it is unclear why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would bring significant improvements in the problem at hand. The motivation behind the use of Sobolev regularization is not well-explained, and the authors should provide a clearer justification for their choice.

Thirdly, I suspect there might be a mistake in Eq. (1). It appears that it should be [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T. The authors should verify this equation and correct any errors.

Fourthly, the construction of Eq. (2) requires more explanation and analysis. For instance, the authors should justify why the l2 norm is imposed on the codes instead of the l1 norm, as used in (Mairal, JMLR 2010). A clear explanation of the design choices behind Eq. (2) would strengthen the technical soundness of the paper.

Fifthly, I am unclear about the meaning of line 146 and would appreciate further clarification from the authors.

Lastly, the language and writing style of the paper require refinement. I have identified several typos, and the authors should thoroughly proofread their work to ensure that it is free of errors and easy to follow.

In summary, while the paper proposes an interesting application of dictionary learning to brain region learning, it requires significant improvements in terms of novelty, clarity, and technical soundness to meet the NIPS standard.
