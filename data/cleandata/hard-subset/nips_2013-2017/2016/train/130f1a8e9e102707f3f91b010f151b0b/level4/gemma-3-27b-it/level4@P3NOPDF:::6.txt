This paper introduces a novel regularization technique for matrix factorization designed to encourage both sparsity and smoothness in the resulting low-rank representation. The core strength of this work lies in the introduction of a new regularizer within the matrix factorization optimization problem, offering a potentially valuable tool for dimensionality reduction and feature learning. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves performance superior to existing state-of-the-art approaches on the tested datasets. This suggests the approach holds considerable promise.

However, a significant concern is the lack of theoretical guarantees regarding the existence and uniqueness of solutions. While the authors present an algorithm for obtaining a solution, its convergence and optimality are not formally established. The empirical validation, while strong, serves as the sole justification for the algorithm’s efficacy. Given the authors acknowledge the non-convexity of the problem, the absence of theoretical analysis weakens the contribution. A proof of existence, or at least a discussion of conditions under which solutions are likely to exist, would significantly strengthen the paper.

The writing quality requires improvement. Specific instances, such as line 146, demonstrate a need for more careful editing and phrasing. While the current presentation is understandable, refining the language would improve clarity and impact. 

Following the rebuttal, I am satisfied with the authors’ response regarding the computational complexity and practical convergence of the algorithm. This addressed a key concern about its applicability to larger datasets. I am therefore revising my assessment of the Clarity and Presentation score upwards from 2 to 3.

**Strengths:**

*   Novel regularization technique promoting both sparsity and smoothness.
*   Strong empirical performance compared to state-of-the-art methods.
*   Potential for applications in dimensionality reduction and feature learning.
*   Thoughtful response to initial concerns during rebuttal.

**Weaknesses:**

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a promising new regularization technique for matrix factorization with encouraging experimental results. However, the lack of theoretical analysis is a significant drawback. While the empirical performance is strong, it does not fully compensate for the absence of guarantees regarding solution existence and convergence. The improved clarity following the rebuttal is appreciated.
