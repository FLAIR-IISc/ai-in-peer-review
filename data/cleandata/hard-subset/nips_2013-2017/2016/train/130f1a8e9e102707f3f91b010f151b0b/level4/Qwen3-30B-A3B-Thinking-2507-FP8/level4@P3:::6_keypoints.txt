- Introduces Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatially smooth, sparse brain activation atoms.  
- Key insight: leverage inter-subject functional variability to define meaningful, structured ROIs rather than scattered activations.  
- Methodologically novel: fuses Laplacian regularization with online SODL to obtain structured "blobs" while remaining scalable.  
- Computationally efficient: only ~2–3× slower than SODL and faster overall than CanICA on the tested dataset.  
- Evaluated on 500-subject HCP task-fMRI, showing improved interpretability, stability, and behavioral prediction vs. SODL and CanICA.  
- Strong technical contribution: Laplacian penalty enforces spatial structure without sacrificing scalability for high-dimensional neuroimaging data.  
- Convergence analysis: cites BCD convergence (Bertsekas) and addresses non-convexity concerns under standard assumptions.  
- Comprehensive experiments: quantitative (explained variance, reproducibility), qualitative maps, hyperparameter analysis, and Nilearn implementation for reproducibility.  
- Excels in small-data regimes (e.g., 17 subjects) where regularization yields substantially better explained variance than SODL.  
- Originality: novel application of structured sparsity in an online dictionary-learning framework, addressing prior slow alternating-optimization approaches.  
- Significance: enables data-driven, biologically meaningful ROI extraction and is practical for large-scale studies.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with sufficient supplementary detail; minor references (GraphNet) could be added for clarity.  
- Weakness: non-convexity could be better contextualized to emphasize convergence to local optima, not global ones.  
- Weakness: comparisons could more deeply justify why CanICA’s lack of sparsity harms predictive power beyond interpretability.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity needs broader discussion on generalization to other datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, rigorous, and practically impactful contribution to neuroimaging methods.
