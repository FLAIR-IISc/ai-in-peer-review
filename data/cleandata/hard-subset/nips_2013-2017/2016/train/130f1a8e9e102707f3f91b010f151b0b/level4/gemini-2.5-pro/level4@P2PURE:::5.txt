This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for identifying brain regions. The main contribution is the incorporation of a Sobolev regularization term to enforce structure on the learned dictionary atoms.

My major comments are as follows:

1.  The primary contribution appears to be the extension of the online dictionary learning framework (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010) with a Sobolev regularization term. This incremental improvement raises concerns about the novelty of the work, which may not meet the standards for a NIPS publication.

2.  The motivation for using Sobolev regularization is not well-justified. The paper states that this term imposes a "structure constraint," but the precise definition of "structure" is unclear. If the intended structure is sparsity, the authors should explain why sparsity on the dictionary atoms themselves is beneficial for this particular application. A clearer explanation of the role and benefit of this regularizer is needed.

3.  There may be a typo in Equation (1). It seems it should be [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T.

4.  The construction of the objective function in Equation (2) requires more detailed justification. Specifically, why is an l2 norm penalty imposed on the codes, rather than the l1 norm that is commonly used to enforce sparsity in dictionary learning (e.g., in Mairal et al., 2010)?

5.  The text on line 146 is unclear and appears to be incomplete or contain a typo.

6.  The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct numerous typos and grammatical errors.
