- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method that enforces atom sparsity via L1 and spatial smoothness via a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty, solved with BCD + FISTA and ridge-coded mini-batches.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by integrating spatial regularization into a scalable online framework, positioned against PCA/ICA and TV-L1 structured sparse models.  
- Strength: addresses a timely problem, yielding more interpretable, stable brain-image components at scale with modest runtime overhead (2–3× vs SODL).  
- Strength: methodologically simple, practical, and planned open-source release (Nilearn) increases reproducibility and impact.  
- Weakness: no theoretical analysis of how the Laplacian penalty affects convergence, stability, or statistical properties of the algorithm.  
- Weakness: manuscript has significant writing and presentation problems (typos, inconsistent notation, and an unresolved internal comment), undermining clarity.  
- Weakness: experimental evidence is preliminary and lacks deeper quantitative analysis, sensitivity studies, and robustness checks across datasets/tasks.  
- Technical soundness: optimization approach is reasonable and grounded in existing techniques but lacks guarantees specific to the modified updates.  
- Empirical support: shows improved interpretability and competitive explained variance, but limited by absence of statistical rigor (e.g., confidence intervals, hyperparameter sensitivity).  
- Clarity: difficult to read in places due to notation inconsistencies (e.g., V vs v), formula/indexing issues, and editorial oversights.  
- Originality: combining L1 and Laplacian penalties is known, but integrating this into a scalable online dictionary-learning pipeline is a useful practical contribution.  
- Significance: could be a practical tool for large-scale brain mapping if validated and polished, but current theoretical and presentation gaps reduce immediate impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important problem with a scalable method, shows promising empirical improvements, and has potential for broad adoption via open-source release.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacks theoretical analysis, has serious clarity/presentation flaws, and presents preliminary evaluation that needs strengthening.  
- Suggestions: add minimal theoretical convergence/stability analysis, thoroughly proofread and standardize notation, clarify sparsity vs Laplacian interplay and schedules, and strengthen empirical evaluation with clearer metrics, confidence intervals, and additional datasets.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful work that requires improved theoretical grounding, clearer presentation, and stronger empirical validation before publication.
